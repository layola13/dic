guerrilla:/ɡəˈrɪlə/ /ɡəˈrɪljə/ | n. | 游击队员；游击战 | A member of a small independent group taking part in irregular fighting, typically against larger regular forces. |  The guerrillas ambushed the army convoy. 游击队员伏击了军队的车队。| synonyms: partisan, freedom fighter, insurgent, rebel | antonyms: soldier, regular, loyalist | noun

guerrilla:/ɡəˈrɪlə/ /ɡəˈrɪljə/ | adj. | 游击队的；游击式的 | Relating to or characteristic of guerrillas or their activities. |  The group employed guerrilla tactics to disrupt the enemy's supply lines.  该组织采用游击战术扰乱敌人的补给线。| synonyms: unconventional, irregular, underground | antonyms: conventional, regular, aboveground | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *guerrilla*, diminutive of *guerra* "war", from Germanic *werra* "confusion, strife".  The word gained popularity during the Peninsular War (1808-1814) against Napoleon's forces in Spain.
