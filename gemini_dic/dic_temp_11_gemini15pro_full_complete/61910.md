empyreal:/ɛmˈpɪriəl/, /ɛmˈpaɪriəl/ | adj. | 天上的，天空的；崇高的，高尚的 |  Relating to the sky or heavens;lofty, sublime.  | The poet described the empyreal beauty of the sunset. 诗人描绘了日落的天空之美。 |  The eagle soared into the empyreal heights.  雄鹰翱翔于高空。| 近义词：celestial, heavenly, ethereal, sublime | 反义词：earthly, mundane, terrestrial | 词性: adjective

empyrean:/ɛmˈpɪriən/, /ɛmˈpaɪriən/ | n. | 天空，苍穹；最高天 | The highest heaven or sphere in ancient cosmology, often considered the abode of God or the blessed. |  He felt his spirit ascend to the empyrean. 他感觉自己的灵魂升入了天堂。| The souls of the righteous dwell in the empyrean. 正义之人的灵魂居住在天国。 | 近义词：heaven, firmament, sky | 反义词：earth, hell | 词性: noun


词源:
From Late Latin *empyreus, from Ancient Greek ἔμπυρος (émpuros, “fiery, burning, in fire”), from ἐν (en, “in”) + πῦρ (pûr, “fire”).
