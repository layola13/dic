deliberatenesses:/dɪˈlɪbərɪtnəsɪz/
|n.|故意；审慎；从容|The deliberateness of her movements suggested she was hiding something. 她的动作故作从容，表明她藏着什么。|例句：The deliberateness of his actions was chilling. 他故意的行为令人不寒而栗。|例句：She tied her shoelaces with deliberateness, stalling for time. 她故意慢慢地系鞋带，为了拖延时间。|近义词：intentionality, purposefulness, calculation, care, caution|反义词：hastiness, carelessness, impulsiveness|词性：名词

deliberate:/dɪˈlɪbərɪt/
|adj.|故意的；深思熟虑的|It was a deliberate attempt to mislead the public. 这是故意误导公众的行为。|例句：He spoke in a slow, deliberate manner. 他说话缓慢而从容。|例句：It was a deliberate act of sabotage. 这是一个蓄意的破坏行为。|近义词：intentional, purposeful, calculated, careful, cautious|反义词：accidental, unintentional, impulsive, careless|词性：形容词

deliberate:/dɪˈlɪbəreɪt/
|v.|仔细考虑；商议|The jury deliberated for three days before reaching a verdict. 陪审团商议了三天，才做出裁决。|例句：They deliberated on the best course of action. 他们仔细考虑了最佳行动方案。|例句：We need to deliberate further on this matter. 我们需要进一步商议此事。|近义词：consider, ponder, discuss, debate|反义词：rush, act impulsively|词性：动词

词源：From Late Latin dēlīberātus, past participle of dēlīberāre (“to weigh in mind, consider, deliberate”), from dē (“from, out of”) + lībra (“balance, scales”).
