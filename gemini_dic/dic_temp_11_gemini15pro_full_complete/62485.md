endoss:/ɪnˈdɔːs/ | vt. | 背书；认可；赞同 | To sign (a check or bill of exchange) on the back to make it payable to someone other than the stated payee or to accept responsibility for paying it. To give support or approval to someone or something. |  Example 1:  He endorsed the check and handed it to the cashier. 他在支票背面签字，然后递给了收银员。

Example 2: The mayor endorsed the candidate for city council. 市长支持这位市议员候选人。 | 近义词: approve, support, back, champion, ratify, validate | 反义词: disapprove, oppose, reject, condemn | 词性: 动词
