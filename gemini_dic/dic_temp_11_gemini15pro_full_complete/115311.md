mannerist:/ˈmænərɪst/| n. adj.|风格主义者；矫揉造作的|An artist, writer, etc., who affects an unnatural or artificial style; of or relating to a style in art and literature characterized by artificiality and affectation, especially in Italy in the late 16th century. |例句：The painting is a classic example of the Mannerist style. 这幅画是风格主义的典型例子。|近义词：affected, artificial, stylized, pretentious|反义词：natural, simple, unaffected|词性：noun, adjective

mannerist:/ˈmænərɪst/| adj.|矫揉造作的；风格主义的|Characterized by artificiality and affectation, especially in Italy in the late 16th century.  |例句：His prose is too mannerist for my taste. 他的散文对我来说太矫揉造作了。|近义词：affected, artificial, stylized, pretentious|反义词：natural, simple, unaffected|词性：adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *manierista*, from *maniera* "manner," from Latin *manus* "hand."  The original sense referred to an artist's characteristic "hand" or style.
