chasubled:/ˈtʃæzəbəld/ | adj. | 穿着祭披的；身穿祭服的 | Dressed in a chasuble, a sleeveless outer vestment worn by a priest celebrating Mass. | The priest, chasubled in green, began the service.  神父穿着绿色的祭披，开始了仪式。|  vested, robed | unvested, disrobed | adjective

Etymology:
From Old French *chasuble, from Late Latin casubula, a hooded outer garment, perhaps from casula "small hut, cottage," from casa "house."  The sense evolution suggests the garment was originally a simple, protective outer covering.
