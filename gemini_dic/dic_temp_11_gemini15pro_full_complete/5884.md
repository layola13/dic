ambergris:/ˈæmbərɡriːs/ (US), /ˈæmbəɡrɪs/ (UK)| n.|龙涎香|A waxy substance secreted by the sperm whale, often found floating at sea or washed ashore, used in perfumery as a fixative. It is formed in the digestive system of the sperm whale, possibly around irritants such as squid beaks.|Example 1: The perfumer prized the ambergris for its unique ability to enhance the longevity of fragrances. 香水师珍视龙涎香，因为它具有增强香水持久性的独特能力。

Example 2:  A beachcomber stumbled upon a lump of ambergris, a valuable find indeed. 一个海滩拾荒者偶然发现了一块龙涎香，这真是一个宝贵的发现。|Synonym:  (for its function) fixative, perfume base|Antonym:  (no direct antonym, but perhaps) synthetic fragrance ingredient|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *ambre gris (“gray amber”), from ambre (“amber”) + gris (“gray”).  Ultimately from Arabic ʿanbar (“ambergris”) and Latin griseus (“gray”).
