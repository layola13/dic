fan:/fæn/
n.|扇子；风扇；狂热爱好者|A device for creating a current of air by movement of a surface or surfaces.|She used a fan to cool herself down. 她用扇子给自己扇凉。
fan:/fæn/
n.|扇子；风扇；狂热爱好者|A person who has a strong interest in or admiration for a particular person or thing.|He's a big fan of Manchester United. 他是曼联的铁杆球迷。
fan:/fæn/
vt.|扇；煽动；激起|To move air with a fan or similar implement.|He fanned the flames with a newspaper. 他用报纸煽动火焰。
fan:/fæn/
vt.|扇；煽动；激起|To increase the strength of (a fire); cause to burn more intensely.|The wind fanned the flames. 风助长了火势。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fann (unattested), from Latin vannus (“winnowing fan, basket for winnowing grain”). Cognate with Old High German wanna, Old Norse vanna.
