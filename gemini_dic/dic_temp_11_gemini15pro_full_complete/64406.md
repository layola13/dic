ergative:/ˈɜːrɡətɪv/| adj. n.| 及物的，作格的；作格|A verb whose subject undergoes the action of the verb when it is intransitive, but whose subject performs the action when it is transitive.  A noun phrase that would be the object of a transitive verb but is the subject of an intransitive verb with the same meaning. |例句：The verb "break" can be ergative: "The window broke" (intransitive) vs. "I broke the window" (transitive). 窗户破了（不及物）vs. 我打破了窗户（及物）。

例句：Basque is an example of an ergative language. 巴斯克语是一种作格语言的例子。|近义词：transitive|反义词: intransitive|词性: adjective, noun
