colportage:/ˈkɒlpɔːtɪdʒ/ (UK) /kəˈlpɔːrtɪdʒ/ (US)| n.|兜售；传播；散播|The act of carrying from place to place and offering for sale, especially books and pamphlets, often with an underlying religious or political message.  It can also refer to the spread of ideas or information, sometimes with a negative connotation of rumor-mongering or propaganda.|The colportage of religious tracts was common in the 19th century. 19世纪兜售宗教小册子很常见。

The internet facilitates the rapid colportage of misinformation. 互联网加速了错误信息的快速传播。|近义词：distribution, dissemination, propagation, canvassing|反义词：suppression, censorship|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *colportage*, from *colporter* "to carry around one's neck," from *col* "neck" (from Latin *collum*) + *porter* "to carry" (from Latin *portare*).
