haemocyanin:/ˌhēmōˈsīənɪn/ | n. |血蓝蛋白|A type of respiratory pigment found in the blood of some invertebrates, such as mollusks and arthropods, that contains copper and carries oxygen.  It appears blue when oxygenated and colorless when deoxygenated.|例句：Haemocyanin is responsible for the blue color of horseshoe crab blood.  鲎的血呈蓝色是因为血蓝蛋白。

例句：Unlike hemoglobin, haemocyanin does not bind oxygen within red blood cells. 与血红蛋白不同，血蓝蛋白不在红细胞内结合氧。|近义词：respiratory pigment, oxygen carrier|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *haîma* (blood) + *kýanos* (dark blue) + *-in* (suffix forming names of chemical substances).
