condescending:/ˌkɑːndɪˈsendɪŋ/
adjective
居高临下的，屈尊的
showing or characterized by a patronizing or superior attitude toward others.
She spoke to me in a condescending tone. 她用居高临下的语气跟我说话。
近义词:patronizing, supercilious, haughty
反义词:humble, respectful, egalitarian
词性:adjective

condescendent (This word is rarely used and considered archaic or nonstandard)
/ˌkɑːndɪˈsendənt/
adjective
屈尊的，俯就的
showing that you believe you are more intelligent or better than other people
He gave a condescendent smile. 他带着屈尊的微笑。
近义词:patronizing, condescending
反义词:humble, respectful
词性:adjective


condescendents (This word is not standard English.  The correct plural form of the noun "condescendent" would be "condescendents", but the noun form itself is extremely rare.)
/ˌkɑːndɪˈsendənts/
noun
屈尊俯就的人 (极少使用)
People who behave in a condescending manner (very rarely used).
The condescendents in the group made the newcomers feel unwelcome.  (This sentence uses a non-standard word.)  小组里那些居高临下的人让新来的人感到不受欢迎。(这个句子使用了一个不标准的词。)
近义词:snobs, elitists
反义词: (No common direct antonym for this rare noun)
词性:noun (rare and nonstandard)



词源: From Latin "condescendĕre,"  meaning "to descend, condescend," from "com-" (together, with) + "descendĕre" (to descend).
