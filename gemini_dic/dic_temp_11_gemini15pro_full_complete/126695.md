name-dropping:/ˈneɪmˌdrɑːpɪŋ/
n.
炫耀；抬出名人以自抬身价
The practice of casually mentioning the names of famous people one knows or claims to know in order to impress others.
He's always name-dropping, trying to make himself sound important. 他总是抬出名人以自抬身价，试图让自己显得重要。
近义词：boasting, bragging
反义词：humility, modesty
词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From name + dropping, the present participle of drop (v.).  The literal sense is "letting fall a name (as if accidentally)."  First recorded in the 1920s.
