cotransport:/ˈkəʊˌtrænspɔːt/ (US) /ˈkəʊˌtrænspɔːt/ (UK)| n.|共转运|The simultaneous transport of two substances across a cell membrane by one protein, one substance moving down its electrochemical gradient and the other moving against its gradient.|Glucose is cotransported with sodium ions into the cell.|Synonyms: coupled transport, symport|Antonyms: countertransport, antiport|noun
