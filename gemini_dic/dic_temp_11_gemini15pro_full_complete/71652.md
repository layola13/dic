flattered:/ˈflætərd/| adj.|感到受宠若惊的，感到荣幸的|Pleased and honored by something, especially praise or attention.|I was flattered by her invitation to dinner. 我很荣幸她邀请我去吃晚饭。|近义词：complimented, honored, gratified|反义词：unimpressed, indifferent|adjective

flatter:/ˈflætər/| v.|奉承，恭维；使显得更漂亮|To praise someone excessively, especially in order to gain favor; to make someone look more attractive than usual.|He flattered her with compliments on her appearance. 他用恭维她外貌的话来奉承她。|近义词：compliment, praise, charm|反义词：criticize, insult|verb

词源: From Old French *flater "to stroke, caress," from Frankish *flattjan, from Proto-Germanic *flatjanan "to flatten, make smooth," from PIE root *plat- "flat." The semantic shift is from "stroking" to "soothing with pleasant words" to "praising insincerely."
