mollusks: /ˈmɒləsks/ (UK) /ˈmɑːləsks/ (US)| n. |软体动物|Any of a large phylum (Mollusca) of invertebrate animals (such as snails, clams, or squids) with a soft unsegmented body usually enclosed in a calcareous shell broadly : shellfish.|例句：Mollusks are a diverse group of animals. 软体动物是动物的一个多样化群体。

Mollusks such as oysters and clams are often eaten raw. 像牡蛎和蛤蜊这样的软体动物经常生吃。|近义词：shellfish|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps vertebrates)|词性:noun
