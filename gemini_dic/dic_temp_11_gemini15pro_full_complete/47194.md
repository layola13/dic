daydreamer:/ˈdeɪˌdriːmər/| n. |白日梦者，幻想家|A person who spends a lot of time daydreaming; someone who is lost in thought and not paying attention to what is happening around them.|例句：He's a bit of a daydreamer and often gets distracted in class. 他有点爱做白日梦，上课经常走神。

例句：She was a daydreamer, always imagining herself on adventures in faraway lands. 她是个幻想家，总是想象自己在遥远的国度冒险。|近义词：fantasist, dreamer, visionary, idealist|反义词：realist, pragmatist|词性:noun

daydreaming:/ˈdeɪˌdriːmɪŋ/| n. |做白日梦，幻想|The act of having pleasant thoughts about something you would like to happen or imagine doing; a state of being lost in thought, especially pleasant thoughts.|例句：I was daydreaming about my vacation when the boss walked in. 我正幻想着我的假期，这时老板走了进来。

例句：She spent most of the afternoon daydreaming about her future. 她一下午的大部分时间都在幻想她的未来。|近义词：fantasizing, musing, woolgathering|反义词：concentrating, focusing|词性:noun
