markedness:/ˈmɑːrkɪdnɪs/| n.|显著性，标记性|The markedness of a linguistic feature refers to its relative unusualness or complexity compared to a more basic or unmarked form.|The plural form of nouns in English (e.g., "cats") is marked by the addition of "-s," while the singular form (e.g., "cat") is unmarked.|近义词：salience, prominence, distinctiveness|反义词：unmarkedness|词性: noun
