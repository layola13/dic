Eskimos:/ˈɛskɪˌmoʊz/ | n. |爱斯基摩人（现已不被认为是合适的称呼，建议使用Inuit或Yupik）| A term formerly used to refer to the indigenous peoples inhabiting the Arctic regions of North America and parts of Greenland and Siberia.  This term is now considered outdated and often offensive, and the preferred terms are Inuit (for those in Canada and Greenland) or Yupik (for those in Alaska and Siberia). |例句：The Eskimos (Inuit) have lived in the Arctic for centuries. 爱斯基摩人（因纽特人）在北极生活了几个世纪。

例句：Eskimos (Yupik) are known for their resilience and adaptability to harsh climates. 爱斯基摩人（尤皮克人）以其在恶劣气候下的韧性和适应能力而闻名。|近义词：Inuit, Yupik |反义词: N/A |词性: noun
