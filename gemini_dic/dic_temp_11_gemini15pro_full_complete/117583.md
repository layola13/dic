megalomaniacally:/ˌmɛɡəloʊˈmeɪniækəli/ | adv. | 夸大地；狂妄自大地 | In a way that shows megalomania (an obsession with power and having delusions of grandeur). | He megalomaniacally declared himself emperor of the world. 他狂妄地宣布自己是世界皇帝。| Example: The dictator megalomaniacally boasted about his absolute power over the nation.  独裁者狂妄自大地吹嘘他对国家的绝对权力。|Synonyms:  grandiosely, pretentiously, arrogantly, pompously | Antonyms: humbly, modestly, unassumingly | Part of Speech: Adverb

megalomania: /ˌmɛɡəloʊˈmeɪniə/ | n. | 夸大狂；狂妄自大 | A mental disorder marked by delusions of grandeur, power, wealth, or omnipotence. | He suffers from megalomania and believes he is a god. 他患有夸大狂，认为自己是个神。| Example:  Her megalomania led her to believe she could control the weather. 她的夸大狂让她相信她可以控制天气。 | Synonyms:  delusions of grandeur, grandiosity | Antonyms: humility, modesty | Part of Speech: Noun

megalomaniac: /ˌmɛɡəloʊˈmeɪniæk/ | n. | 夸大狂患者 | A person who suffers from megalomania. | The megalomaniac plotted to take over the world. 这个夸大狂密谋要接管世界。| Example: The CEO, a known megalomaniac, fired anyone who dared to disagree with him. 这位首席执行官，一个著名的夸大狂，解雇了任何敢于反对他的人。| Synonyms:  egomaniac, narcissist | Antonyms:  humble person | Part of Speech: Noun
