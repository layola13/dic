oracularities:/ˌɔːrəˈkælərɪtiz/ | n. |神谕，玄妙的话，故弄玄虚的话|  Plural form of oracularity, meaning utterances that are ambiguous, obscure, or pronouncements delivered as if with divine authority. | The professor's pronouncements were filled with oracularities, leaving the students more confused than enlightened. 教授的声明充满了玄妙的话，让学生们更加困惑而不是得到启发。| The oracularities of the fortune teller were so vague that they could apply to anyone. 算命先生故弄玄虚的话语含糊不清，可以适用于任何人。 |synonyms: ambiguities, obscurities, pronouncements, enigmas | antonyms: clarities, straightforwardness, explicitness | noun
