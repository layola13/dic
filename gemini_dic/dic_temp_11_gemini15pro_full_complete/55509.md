distraughtness:/dɪˈstrɔːtnəs/
|n.|
|极度悲伤，悲痛欲绝|
|The state of being extremely worried, upset, and confused.|
|Her distraughtness was evident in her trembling voice and tear-stained face. 她的极度悲伤从她颤抖的声音和泪流满面的脸上显而易见。|
|The news of her father's death left her in a state of utter distraughtness. 她父亲去世的消息使她悲痛欲绝。|
|anguish, despair, grief, misery|
|calmness, composure, serenity, tranquility|
|noun|

ETYMOLOGY->
From distraught + -ness.  Distraught itself comes from the past participle of Middle English *distracten, from Latin distractus, past participle of distrahere "to pull apart, divide, distract," from dis- "apart" + trahere "to draw, pull."  The suffix -ness forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
