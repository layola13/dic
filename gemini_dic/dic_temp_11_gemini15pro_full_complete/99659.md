interleukins:/ˌɪntərˈluːkɪnz/| n. |白细胞介素|A group of proteins (cytokines) produced by white blood cells (leukocytes) that act as chemical messengers between different types of immune cells, regulating various aspects of the immune response, including inflammation, cell growth and differentiation.|Interleukins play a crucial role in the body's defense against infections. 白细胞介素在机体抵抗感染中起着至关重要的作用。|Synonyms: cytokines, lymphokines|Antonyms: immunosuppressants|Noun


Etymology:  From inter- + leukin, from leuko- "white" + -kin "active substance."
