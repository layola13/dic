kya:/kjɑː/ (pronunciation may vary depending on dialect and context)| pronoun, adverb | क्या (Hindi) - what | Kya is the Hindi word for "what."  It's used in the same way as "what" is used in English to ask questions.  It does not directly translate into a single English word, but functions similarly to the English interrogative pronoun and adverb "what." | Example:  Kya aap theek hain? (क्या आप ठीक हैं?) - Are you okay? |  Kya tum kal aa rahe ho? (क्या तुम कल आ रहे हो?) - Are you coming tomorrow? | Synonym: (No direct English synonym, functionally similar to "what") | Antonym: (No direct antonym) | Pronoun, Adverb

PHRASE-> (While "kya" is Hindi, these phrases combine English and Hindi and are colloquially used, especially in code-switching contexts)

What kya? = 什么“kya”？（表示不明白或惊讶）
Kya baat hai! =  太棒了！/ 真厉害！ (Expressing admiration or surprise)
Kya hua? =  怎么了？/ 发生了什么？(Asking what happened)
Achcha kya? =  真的吗？/ 是吗？ (Expressing disbelief or seeking confirmation)
Phirste kya? = 然后呢？ (Asking what's next)


Note:  "kya" itself is Hindi, not English.  Therefore, English synonyms, antonyms, and direct translations don't precisely apply. It's crucial to understand its function as an interrogative word within Hindi grammar.  The provided examples demonstrate how "kya" functions similarly to "what" in English question formation.  The phrases combine Hindi and English, reflecting common usage in multilingual conversations.
