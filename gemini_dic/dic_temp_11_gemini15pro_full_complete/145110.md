overapprehensiveness:/ˌoʊvərˌæprɪˈhɛnsɪvnɪs/| n. |过度忧虑，过分担心|Excessive anxiety or worry, often about things that are unlikely to happen.|She suffered from overapprehensiveness about her children's safety.|近义词:anxiety, worry, solicitude, apprehension|反义词:calmness, tranquility, serenity|词性:名词

Etymology:
From over- + apprehensiveness.  "Over-" signifies "excessive" or "too much". "Apprehensiveness" comes from "apprehend," which has roots in the Latin "apprehendere" meaning "to seize, grasp, or understand."  The evolution of meaning shifted from the physical act of seizing to the mental act of anticipating something, often with fear or anxiety.
