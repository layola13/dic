knotweed:/ˈnɒtwid/ (UK) /ˈnɑːtwid/ (US)| n. |虎杖，蓼科植物|A perennial weed with jointed stems, small white flowers, and large spreading roots. It is considered invasive in many parts of the world.|例句：The knotweed has taken over the entire garden. 虎杖已经蔓延到整个花园了。

例句：Controlling knotweed can be a very expensive process.  控制虎杖可能是一个非常昂贵的过程。|近义词：Japanese knotweed (specifically for the invasive species), weed|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps cultivated plant)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From knot + weed.  Referring to the swollen nodes on the plant's stems that resemble knots.
