ostentation:/ˌɑːstenˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 炫耀，卖弄 |  An exaggerated display of wealth, knowledge, or skill, intended to impress others. | He avoided all ostentation in his dress. 他衣着朴素，避免任何炫耀。 | synonyms: showiness, pretentiousness, flashiness, gaudiness | antonyms: modesty, humility, simplicity, unpretentiousness | noun

ostentatious:/ˌɑːstenˈteɪʃəs/ | adj. | 炫耀的，卖弄的 | Characterized by or given to ostentation; pretentious. |  They criticized the ostentatious lifestyle of their neighbors. 他们批评邻居们炫耀的生活方式。 | synonyms: showy, pretentious, flashy, flamboyant | antonyms: modest, humble, simple, understated | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *ostentationem (nominative ostentatio) "a showing, display," noun of action from past-participle stem of ostentare "to display, show," frequentative of ostendere "to show, exhibit," from ob "in front of, before" (see ob-) + tendere "to stretch" (from PIE root *ten- "to stretch").
