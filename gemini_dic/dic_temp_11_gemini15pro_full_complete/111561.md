lobefoot:/ˈloʊbfʊt/| n. |裂足动物；叶足动物|An animal belonging to the phylum Lobopoda, characterized by unjointed, fleshy appendages called lobopods.|The velvet worm is a well-known example of a lobefoot. 天鹅绒虫是裂足动物的一个著名例子。|近义词:onychophoran|反义词: (无明确的反义词，可考虑其他动物门类，例如arthropoda节肢动物门)|词性:noun

Etymology->
From *lobo-* "lobe" + *pod"* "foot".  The phylum name Lobopoda itself combines the Greek elements *lobós* (λοβός), meaning "lobe," and *poús* (πούς), meaning "foot." This refers to the characteristic stubby, lobe-like legs of these animals.
