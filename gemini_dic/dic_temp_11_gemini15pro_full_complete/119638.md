middle-of-the-roader:/ˌmɪdl əv ðə ˈroʊdər/ (US) /ˌmɪdl əv ðə ˈrəʊdə(r)/ (UK)
noun
中间派；温和派；中庸之道者
Someone who holds moderate political views, avoiding extreme positions.
The candidate presented himself as a middle-of-the-roader, appealing to a broad range of voters.  这位候选人把自己塑造成一个中间派，以吸引更广泛的选民。
He's a middle-of-the-roader on most issues, rarely taking a strong stance.  他在大多数问题上都是中立派，很少采取强硬立场。
近义词: moderate, centrist
反义词: extremist, radical
词性: n.


ETYMOLOGY->
Mid-19th century. From middle of the road + -er.  The literal meaning referred to someone walking in the middle of the road to avoid being hit by traffic from either direction. This evolved into the figurative meaning of avoiding extreme political positions.
