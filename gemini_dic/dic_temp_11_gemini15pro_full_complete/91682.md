hypercoagulable:/ˌhaɪpərkoʊˌæɡjələbəl/| adj.|高凝的，高凝状态的|Characterized by an abnormally increased tendency to form blood clots.|Example:  Patients with cancer are often in a hypercoagulable state. 癌症患者通常处于高凝状态。

Example:  Hereditary hypercoagulable disorders can increase the risk of thrombosis. 遗传性高凝疾病会增加血栓形成的风险。|近义词：thrombophilic|反义词：anticoagulant (as an adjective)|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
hyper- (prefix): from Greek "hyper," meaning "over, above, excessive"
coagulable: from Latin "coagulabilis," meaning "capable of being curdled or clotted"
