burrowed:/ˈbɜːroʊd/ (US) /ˈbʌrəʊd/ (UK)| v. | 挖洞；钻入；躲藏；埋头苦干 | to dig a hole or tunnel into or under something; to move oneself into a comfortable and safe position; to immerse oneself in study or research | The rabbit burrowed into the ground. 兔子钻进了地里。 | Synonyms: dig, tunnel, excavate, nestle, delve | Antonyms: emerge, surface, uncover | verb

burrowed:/ˈbɜːroʊd/ (US) /ˈbʌrəʊd/ (UK)| adj. |  挖洞的；钻入的；躲藏的 | having burrowed; being in a burrow | The burrowed animal felt safe from predators. 躲藏的动物感到安全，不会受到捕食者的攻击。| Synonyms: hidden, concealed, nestled | Antonyms: exposed, visible, uncovered | adjective
