impugnability:/ɪmˌpʌɡnəˈbɪləti/| n. |可 impugn 的状态; 可责难性; 可质疑性|The state or quality of being impugnable; the capacity of being called into question; challengeability.|The lawyer questioned the impugnability of the witness's testimony. 律师质疑证人证词的可质疑性。

The report's conclusions were met with widespread impugnability due to methodological flaws.  由于方法上的缺陷，该报告的结论受到了广泛的质疑。|近义词：challengeability, disputability, contestability|反义词：indisputability, irrefutability, unquestionability|词性:noun

impugn:/ɪmˈpjuːn/| v. |责难; 质疑; 攻击; 非难|dispute the truth, validity, or honesty of (a statement or motive); call into question.|The politician's opponent impugned his motives.  这位政治家的对手质疑他的动机。

She impugned the accuracy of the report. 她质疑报告的准确性。|近义词：challenge, question, dispute, attack, criticize|反义词：support, defend, uphold, endorse|词性:verb
