mitre:/ˈmaɪtər/ (US) /ˈmʌɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.|主教冠，斜接；斜接，斜切|A tall, pointed hat worn by bishops and some other senior clergy.  Also, a joint made by beveling two pieces to form a corner, typically at a 45-degree angle.|The bishop wore a magnificent gold mitre.|近义词:miter (US spelling),  bevel,  corner|反义词:curve|词性:noun, verb

mitre:/ˈmaɪtər/ (US) /ˈmʌɪtə(r)/ (UK)|v.|斜接，斜切|To join two pieces together by beveling the edges to form a corner, typically at a 45-degree angle.|He mitred the corners of the picture frame.|近义词:bevel, join|反义词:separate|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mitre*, from Latin mitra, from Greek *μίτρα (mítra)*, meaning "headband," "turban." Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mey-*, meaning "to bind."
