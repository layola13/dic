convolutedly:/ˌkɒnvəˈluːtɪdli/ /ˌkɑːnvəˈluːtɪdli/| adv.| 迂回地，复杂地|In a complicated and difficult to understand way.  Derived from "convolute" (meaning rolled up or twisted together), with the suffix "-ly" forming the adverb.|He explained the process convolutedly, leaving everyone confused.|近义词：intricately, complexly, tortuously|反义词：simply, straightforwardly, directly|词性: 副词

词源: From Latin *convolutus*, past participle of *convolvere* "to roll together, roll up, intertwine," from *com-* "together" + *volvere* "to roll".
