dichloride:/daɪˈklɔːraɪd/ | n. |二氯化物|A compound containing two chlorine atoms per molecule.  It refers to a chemical compound containing two chlorine atoms bonded to another element or group. |Calcium dichloride is used as a drying agent.  氯化钙用作干燥剂。|近义词: None (Specific to the chemical compound) |反义词: None (Specific to the chemical compound) |词性:noun


Etymology:
di- (prefix): two, twice (from Greek)
chloride: a binary compound of chlorine (from chlor- + -ide)
