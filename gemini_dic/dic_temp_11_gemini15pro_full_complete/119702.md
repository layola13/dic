midon: /ˈmiːdɒn/ (UK) /ˈmaɪdɑːn/ (US)| n. |米糠, 淀粉| Midon is the starchy part of grains like rice or wheat, often used in cooking and as a thickener.  It's also a by-product of milling.|  Example: The recipe calls for a tablespoon of midon to thicken the sauce.  这个食谱需要一汤匙淀粉来使酱汁变稠。|Synonym: starch, flour (in some contexts)|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps protein or fiber as a contrasting nutrient) | Noun
