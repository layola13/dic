mottes:/mɒts/| n.|城堡土墩，城堡基台，(尤指中世纪城堡的)土丘|A motte is a raised earthwork, a mound, upon which a castle or keep is built, most commonly associated with medieval castles.|The motte provided a defensive position for the wooden or stone keep built upon it.  城堡土墩为建在其上的木制或石制城堡提供了防御阵地。|The archaeological dig unearthed the remains of a motte-and-bailey castle. 考古挖掘出土了一座土丘堡垒的遗迹。|synonyms: mound, earthwork, hill,  |antonyms: ditch, moat, valley|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *motte* "clod, mound," from Vulgar Latin *mutta*, perhaps related to Latin *muttum* “mutter, murmur.”
