lithographic:/ˌlɪθəˈɡræfɪk/ | adj. |平版印刷的，石印的|Relating to or produced by lithography, a printing process that uses a flat stone or metal plate on which the image areas are worked using a greasy substance so that the ink will adhere to them by, while the non-image areas are made ink-repellent.|The museum has a collection of lithographic prints.  博物馆收藏了许多石版画。|Synonyms: planographic, offset |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but could consider "intaglio" or "relief" as contrasting printing methods) | Word type: Adjective

lithography:/lɪˈθɒɡrəfi/ | n. |平版印刷术，石版印刷术|A method of printing from a flat stone or metal plate on which the image areas are worked using a greasy substance so that the ink will adhere to them by, while the non-image areas are made ink-repellent.|Lithography was a major advance in printing technology. 平版印刷是印刷技术的一大进步。|Synonyms: planography, offset printing |Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but could consider "intaglio" or "relief" as contrasting printing methods) | Word type: Noun
