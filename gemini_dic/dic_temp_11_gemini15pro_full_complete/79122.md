gladiator:/ˈɡlædiˌeɪtər/| n. |角斗士，斗士|A Roman fighter, often a slave or captive, who was armed with a sword or other weapon and compelled to fight to the death in an arena for public entertainment.|The gladiators fought bravely in the arena. 角斗士们在竞技场上英勇作战。|Synonyms: fighter, warrior, combatant|Antonyms: pacifist, civilian|Noun
