actualistic:/ˌæktʃuəˈlɪstɪk/
adjective
现实主义的;基于现实的
Based on the study of present-day processes and conditions as a guide to understanding past events.  Relating to or characterized by actualism.
例句：Actualistic studies of modern sedimentary environments help geologists interpret ancient rocks.  现代沉积环境的现实主义研究帮助地质学家解释古代岩石。

例句：The actualistic principle suggests that the same natural laws and processes that operate in the universe now have always operated in the universe in the past and apply everywhere in the universe. 现实主义原则表明，现在在宇宙中运行的相同的自然规律和过程在过去一直都在宇宙中运行，并且适用于宇宙中的任何地方。

近义词：empirical, observational
反义词：theoretical, hypothetical
词性：adjective

词源：From "actual" + "-istic"  "Actual" comes from Late Latin "actualis," meaning "active, practical," from Latin "actus," meaning "a doing, a driving, an impulse."  "-istic" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "relating to" or "characteristic of."
