giddily:/ˈɡɪdɪli/| adv.|轻率地，头晕地|In a giddy, frivolous, or lighthearted manner.  Characterized by or showing a lighthearted, often silly, excitement or dizziness.|She laughed giddily as she spun around. 她旋转着，轻率地笑着。|Synonyms: lightheartedly, frivolously, carelessly, dizzyingly|Antonyms: seriously, solemnly, cautiously|Adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From giddy + -ly.  Giddy itself comes from Old English *gydig, meaning "possessed by a god," suggesting a state of frenzy or inspiration.  Later, it evolved to mean "dizzy" and then extended to include lighthearted excitement.
