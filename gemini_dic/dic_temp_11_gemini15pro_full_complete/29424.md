Etymology:
From Middle French *Cathayen*, from Medieval Latin *Cataya* (China), ultimately from Khitan, a nomadic people who ruled parts of Manchuria and northern China.  The term acquired negative connotations in Europe due to early trade disputes and misunderstandings.
