flute:/fluːt/| n.|长笛; 笛子|A woodwind instrument held sideways and played by blowing across a hole near one end.|She plays the flute beautifully. 她吹长笛吹得很好。|Synonyms: recorder, pipe, fife|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a musical instrument.  Could consider "silence" in a metaphorical sense.)|noun

flute:/fluːt/| v.|用笛声表达; 笛音般地吹(或唱); 脊状地做; 弄出凹槽|To play a flute; to make a sound like a flute; to make grooves in something.|The birds fluted their morning song. 鸟儿们用笛音般的歌声迎接清晨。|Synonyms: whistle, pipe, trill|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for the verb. Could consider "mute" or "silence" in a metaphorical sense.)|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "flaüte," from Old Provençal "flauta," ultimately of Germanic origin. Related to Old High German "floita."
