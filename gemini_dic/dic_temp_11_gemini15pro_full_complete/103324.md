jiggled:/ˈdʒɪɡəld/ | v. | 轻摇，抖动 | to move something up and down or back and forth quickly and slightly | She jiggled the handle to try and open the door. 她摇动把手试图打开门。| synonyms: shake, wiggle, jostle | antonyms: steady, stabilize | verb

jiggle:/ˈdʒɪɡəl/ | n. | 轻摇，抖动 | a quick, short movement up and down or back and forth | Give the bottle a jiggle before opening it. 打开瓶子前摇晃一下。| synonyms: shake, wiggle, jolt | antonyms: stillness, stability | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Likely a frequentative form of *jig*, a lively dance and the music for it, of uncertain origin, but possibly imitative.  First recorded in the 16th century.
