impermeablenesses:/ɪmˈpɜːrmiəbəlnəsɪz/
|n.|不可渗透性；不透水性； непроницаемость (Russian)|The quality or state of being impermeable; inability to be penetrated or passed through, especially by liquids or gases.  The multiple instances or degrees of this quality.|The multiple layers of clay contribute to the impermeablenesses of the landfill liner, preventing leachate from contaminating the groundwater.  The various impermeablenesses of the different rock strata affect the flow of underground water. |Near Synonyms: impenetrabilities, imperviousnesses|Antonyms: permeabilities, porosities
