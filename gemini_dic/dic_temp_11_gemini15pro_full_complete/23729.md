bratling: /ˈbrætlɪŋ/ | n. | 小孩，顽童，乳臭未干的小子 | A child, especially a mischievous or annoying one.  Often used playfully or slightly disparagingly. |  "The bratlings were running amok in the garden."  那些小家伙在花园里到处乱跑。 | Synonyms: child, kid, urchin, imp, rascal | Antonyms: adult, grown-up | noun

bratling: /ˈbrætlɪŋ/ | n. | 畜生的幼崽 | Offspring of an animal. Less common usage. | "The vixen fiercely protected her bratlings."  雌狐拼命保护她的幼崽。 | Synonyms: offspring, young, progeny | Antonyms: parent | noun

词源：From Middle English *bratling, from Old English *brædling, a diminutive of brat, meaning "offspring, child." Related to Old Norse brodd, meaning "spike, shoot."  The diminutive suffix -ling suggests smallness or youth.
