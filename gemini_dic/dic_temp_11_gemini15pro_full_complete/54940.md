dispossession:/ˌdɪspəˈzɛʃən/
n.
剥夺，驱逐，丧失
The act of taking something away from someone, especially land or property.
The dispossession of the Native Americans from their ancestral lands was a tragic chapter in history.  美国原住民被剥夺祖传土地是历史上悲惨的一章。
The family faced dispossession from their home because they couldn't pay the mortgage.  这家人因无力偿还抵押贷款而面临被驱逐的境况。

Synonyms: deprivation, expropriation, eviction, ouster
Antonyms: provision, bestowal, endowment
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *dispossession*, from Anglo-Norman *dispossession*, from *dispossesser* (“to dispossess”), from *des-* (“dis-”) + *possesser* (“to possess”), from Latin *possidēre* (“to possess, own, occupy”).
