mitre:/ˈmaɪtər/ (US) /ˈmaɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|主教冠；斜接；斜角；|A tall, pointed hat worn by bishops and some other senior clergy.  A joint made between two pieces of wood or other material at an angle, typically 45°, to form a corner.|The bishop wore a magnificent gold mitre. 主教戴着一顶华丽的金冠。|Synonyms: headdress, crown,  bevel, chamfer|Antonyms: (For the hat) None specific. (For the joint) straight joint|noun


mitres:/ˈmaɪtəz/ (US) /ˈmaɪtəz/ (UK)| n.|主教冠（复数）；斜接（复数）；斜角（复数）|Plural form of "mitre".|The mitres of the bishops gleamed in the candlelight. 主教们的冠冕在烛光下闪闪发光。|Synonyms: headdresses, crowns, bevels, chamfers|Antonyms: (For the hats) None specific. (For the joints) straight joints|noun
