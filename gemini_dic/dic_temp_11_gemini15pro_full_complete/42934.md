covenanter:/ˈkʌvənəntər/| n.|圣约者，盟约者|A person who makes a covenant or agreement, especially a solemn religious one.  Specifically, a supporter of the Scottish National Covenant of 1638 and its renewals.|例句：The covenanters fought for religious freedom. 圣约者为宗教自由而战。

例句：Many covenanters were persecuted for their beliefs. 许多圣约者因其信仰而受到迫害。|近义词：signer, signatory, subscriber, adherent|反义词：opponent, dissenter|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From covenant + -er.  Covenant comes from Old French *covenant (Modern French convenant), from convenir "to agree," from Latin convenīre "to come together, assemble, agree," from com- "together" + venīre "to come."
