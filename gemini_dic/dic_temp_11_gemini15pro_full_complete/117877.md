melodramas: /ˈmɛləˌdrɑːməz/ | n. | 情节剧；通俗剧 |  A play, film, or other narrative characterized by exaggerated emotions, stereotypical characters, and often sensational or improbable events.  It often deals with romantic or domestic situations and has a clearly defined villain and hero/heroine. |  She loves watching old melodramas on TV. 她喜欢在电视上看老式情节剧。 | synonyms: soap operas, tearjerkers, potboilers | antonyms: documentaries, realism | noun

melodramas: /ˈmɛləˌdrɑːməz/ | n. | 戏剧性事件；夸张的言行 |  A situation or series of events in real life that resembles a melodrama, especially in terms of exaggerated emotions and improbable events. | The political scandal unfolded like a real-life melodrama. 这场政治丑闻像一出真实生活中的情节剧一样展开。 | synonyms: theatrics, histrionics, dramatics | antonyms: understatement, restraint | noun
