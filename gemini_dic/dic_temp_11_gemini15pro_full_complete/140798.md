on-balance:/ˌɒn ˈbæləns/ | adv. adj.| 总的来说，总而言之 | considering all aspects of a situation; overall | On balance, the meeting was productive. 总的来说，会议很有成效。 | On balance, I think the advantages outweigh the disadvantages. 总的来说，我认为利大于弊。| 近义词: overall, all things considered, by and large, in the final analysis | 反义词: partially, incompletely | 词性: 副词，形容词

词源:  "on balance" originates from the idea of weighing things on a balance scale. The "on" refers to being "on" the scale, and "balance" refers to the process of weighing and comparing.  This figurative meaning developed from the literal meaning of balancing physical objects.
