extrinsicism:/ˌɛkˈstrɪnzɪsɪzəm/
n.
外在主义，外在性
The doctrine that value or validity are conferred by something external to the object in question.
His philosophy emphasizes extrinsicism, believing that morality comes from societal norms, not inner conscience.  他的哲学强调外在主义，认为道德来自社会规范，而不是内在良知。
近义词: externalism, objectivism
反义词: intrinsicism, subjectivism
词性: noun

extrinsic:/ɛkˈstrɪnzɪk/
adj.
外在的，外来的
Not part of the essential nature of someone or something; coming or operating from outside.
Extrinsic motivation, such as money or prizes, can be effective in the short term. 外在的激励，例如金钱或奖品，在短期内可能有效。
近义词: external, outward, outer
反义词: intrinsic, inherent, internal
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *extrinsicus, from Latin extrinsecus ("outwardly"), from extra ("outside") + secus ("beside, alongside").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *sekʷ- ("to follow").
