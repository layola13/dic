effloresce:/ˌɛfləˈrɛs/| v. |开花；繁荣；风化|To blossom or bloom; to reach a state of full development or prosperity; (of a substance) to lose water of crystallization and turn to a fine powder on exposure to air. |例句：The artist's talent effloresced during his time in Italy.  这位艺术家的才华在意大利期间得到了充分发展。

例句：The salts effloresced on the surface of the rock. 盐在岩石表面风化了。|近义词：bloom, blossom, flourish, thrive, prosper|反义词：wither, decay, decline|词性：动词

efflorescence:/ˌɛfləˈrɛsəns/| n. |开花；繁荣；风化物|The action or process of efflorescing; the state of being effloresced; a powdery deposit on the surface of a rock or wall. |例句：The efflorescence of the cherry blossoms is a beautiful sight. 樱花盛开是一道美丽的风景。

例句：The white efflorescence on the bricks was caused by salts. 砖块上的白色风化物是由盐引起的。|近义词：blooming, blossoming, flourishing, prosperity|反义词：decline, decay|词性：名词


efflower:/iˈflaʊər/| vt. |使…开花；使…繁荣；夺去…精华；玷污（尤指少女）| 1. To cause to blossom or bloom. 2. To deprive of its freshness or beauty; to deflower. | 例句：The warm spring weather efflowered the garden. 温暖的春天让花园里鲜花盛开。


例句：The experience efflowered her innocence. 这段经历夺走了她的纯真。|近义词：bloom, blossom,  deflower, spoil, taint|反义词：wither, blight, protect|词性：动词



ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *efflorēscere, from Latin ex- + florēre "to bloom, blossom," from flōs "flower."
