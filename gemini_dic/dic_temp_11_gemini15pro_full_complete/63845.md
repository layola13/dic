epiglottises:/ˌɛpɪˈɡlɒtɪsiːz/
n.
会厌（复数）
A flap of cartilage at the root of the tongue, which is depressed during swallowing to cover the opening of the windpipe.
The epiglottises prevent food from entering the trachea.  会厌防止食物进入气管。
The doctor examined the patient's epiglottises. 医生检查了病人的会厌。

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἐπίγλωττις (epíglōttis, “epiglottis”), from ἐπί (epí, “upon, on”) + γλωττίς (glōttís, “tongue, mouthpiece of a flute”).
