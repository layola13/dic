counter-mobilization:/ˌkaʊntərˌmoʊbɪləˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 反动员，对抗动员 | The act of organizing and preparing forces to oppose a mobilization of another power.  The government initiated a counter-mobilization effort in response to the enemy's military buildup. |  The government's counter-mobilization involved recalling reservists and strengthening border defenses.  A swift counter-mobilization is crucial in deterring potential aggressors. | 近义词: counter-measures, counter-offensive | 反义词: mobilization, deployment | 词性: 名词

counter-mobilize:/ˌkaʊntərˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz/ | v. | 反动员，对抗动员 | To organize and prepare forces in opposition to another power's mobilization. The country counter-mobilized its troops along the border. |  They quickly counter-mobilized to defend against the impending attack. The nation was forced to counter-mobilize its forces when the neighboring country declared war. | 近义词: counteract, oppose | 反义词: mobilize, deploy | 词性: 动词
