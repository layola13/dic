meticulosity:/ˌmɛtɪkjʊˈlɒsɪti/ /ˌmɛtɪkjʊˈlɑːsɪti/| n. |一丝不苟，细致， meticulousness|  Extreme care in the consideration or treatment of details. |  She planned the party with her usual meticulousity. 她以她一贯的细致计划了这次派对。|Synonyms: precision, exactitude, carefulness, painstakingness | Antonyms: carelessness, negligence, sloppiness | noun

meticulosities:/ˌmɛtɪkjʊˈlɒsɪtiz/ /ˌmɛtɪkjʊˈlɑːsɪtiz/| n. | 细枝末节; 谨小慎微之处 (meticulosity 的复数)| The small details or points that require extreme care and attention.|  He was known for his meticulous attention to detail, often getting lost in the meticuloussities of a project. 他以对细节一丝不苟而闻名，经常沉迷于项目的细枝末节。 | Synonyms:  minutiae, niceties, subtleties | Antonyms: generalities, approximations | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *meticulosus, from Latin metus (“fear”) +‎ -culus (diminutive suffix).  The original sense was "timid, fearful," later evolving to "overly cautious or careful," and finally to its current meaning of "extremely precise and attentive to detail."
