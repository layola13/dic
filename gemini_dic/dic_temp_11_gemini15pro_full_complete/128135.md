netherworld: /ˈnɛðərwɜːrld/ (US) /ˈneðəwɜːld/ (UK)
n.
冥界；阴间；地狱
The realm of the dead, often depicted as a dark, underground place.
He believed that upon death, his soul would journey to the netherworld. 他相信死后，他的灵魂会前往冥界。
The ancient Egyptians filled tombs with provisions for the deceased to use in the netherworld. 古埃及人在坟墓里装满了供死者在冥界使用的物品。

近义词: underworld, hades, hell, inferno
反义词: heaven, paradise
词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old English *niðerworuld (attested only in the dative plural niðerworldum), from niðer "down, downward, beneath, below" + woruld "world, age, life."  Cognate with Old Saxon nitherworald, Old High German nidarweralt, Old Norse niðarheimr (whence Old Norse Ragnarok "fate of the gods," literally "doom of the powers").
