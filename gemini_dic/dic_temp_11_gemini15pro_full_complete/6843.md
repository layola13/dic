anathematization:/əˌnæθəməˌtaɪˈzeɪʃən/
n.
正式谴责；咒逐；诅咒
The formal act of condemning or cursing someone or something, often by a religious authority.
The Pope pronounced the anathematization of the heretics. 教 pope 宣告了对异教徒的咒逐。

The council issued an anathematization of the rebel leader.  委员会发布了对叛乱头目的正式谴责。

condemnation, denunciation, excommunication, curse
blessing, praise, commendation

Etymology:
From Late Latin anathematisatio, from Late Latin anathematizare, from Ancient Greek ἀναθεματίζειν (anathematizein, “to devote to evil, curse”), from ἀνάθεμα (anathema, “a thing devoted, a curse”).
