froth:/frɒθ/ (UK), /frɔːθ/ (US)| n. vi. vt.| 泡沫，起泡沫 |A mass of small bubbles formed on or in a liquid, typically by agitation or fermentation.  A state of frivolous excitement or activity.| The beer had a thick head of froth.  啤酒上面有一层厚厚的泡沫。|Synonyms: foam, lather, spume, effervescence|Antonyms: flatness, stillness|noun

froth:/frɒθ/ (UK), /frɔːθ/ (US)| vi. vt.| 起泡沫 |(Of a liquid) form or produce a mass of small bubbles; exude or discharge (saliva) from the mouth as froth.| The waves frothed against the rocks. 海浪拍打着岩石，泛起泡沫。  The dog frothed at the mouth. 狗的嘴里吐着白沫。|Synonyms: foam, lather, bubble, fizz|Antonyms: flatten, settle|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *froþ (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *frōþaz, from Proto-Indo-European *preu- (“to flow, run”). Cognate with Old Norse froða (“froth”), Old High German froda (“froth”).
