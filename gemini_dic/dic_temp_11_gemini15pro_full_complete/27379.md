came-in-for:/keɪm ˈɪn fɔːr/ | phrasal verb | 遭受，遭到（批评）；受到（责备、惩罚等）| To receive something, typically something unpleasant, such as criticism or blame.  It can also mean to be subject to something, like punishment. | The government came in for a lot of criticism over its handling of the pandemic. 政府因其处理疫情的方式而受到很多批评。

The new policy came in for heavy criticism from the opposition party.  这项新政策受到了反对党的严厉批评。| synonyms: receive, experience, be subjected to, encounter, attract, draw, get, suffer, undergo, sustain, endure, be exposed to | antonyms: avoid, escape, evade, dodge, sidestep | verb phrase
