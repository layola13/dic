**Etymology (for both learned and learnt):**

From Middle English lernen, lerned, from Old English leornian (“to learn, study, read, think about”), from Proto-Germanic *liznōjaną (“to learn”), from Proto-Indo-European *leis- (“to follow a track, furrow, learn”). Cognate with Old Frisian lernia, Old Saxon liornian, Old High German lernēn, Old Norse læra, Gothic 𐌻𐌰𐌹𐍃𐌾𐌰𐌽 (laisjan).
