cave-in:/ˈkeɪvɪn/ (US), /ˈkeɪvɪn/ (UK)| n. |塌方；坍塌；屈服，让步|A sudden collapse or sinking, as of a roof or the ground; a yielding to pressure, argument, or demands.|The heavy rains caused a cave-in at the mine. 暴雨导致矿井塌方。|Synonyms: collapse, subsidence, surrender, concession|Antonyms:  resistance, defiance|noun

cave-in:/ˈkeɪvɪn/ (US), /ˈkeɪvɪn/ (UK)| vi. |塌陷；坍塌；屈服，让步|To collapse or sink inward; to yield to pressure, argument, or demands.|The roof of the old building caved in. 老房子的屋顶塌陷了。|Synonyms: collapse, crumble, give in, succumb|Antonyms: resist, withstand|verb
