empyreumatic:/ˌɛmpaɪruːˈmætɪk/ | adj. |焦臭的；烧焦的；熏焦的|Relating to or having a burnt smell or taste, typically from dry distillation. | The empyreumatic odor of burnt toast filled the kitchen. 烤面包的焦味弥漫了整个厨房。 | Synonyms: burnt, scorched, charred, smoky | Antonyms: fresh, unburnt, unscented | adjective


empyreuma:/ˌɛmpaɪˈruːmə/ | n. |焦臭；烧焦味|A burnt smell or taste, typically from dry distillation.|The empyreuma of burning plastic was overwhelming. 燃烧塑料的焦味令人难以忍受。|Synonyms: burnt smell, charred odor, smoky scent | Antonyms: fragrance, aroma, perfume | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin empyreumaticus, from Ancient Greek ἐμπυρεύματος (empyreúmatos, “burnt, set on fire, inflamed”), from ἐμπυρεύειν (empyreúein, “to kindle, set on fire”), from ἐν (en, “in”) + πῦρ (pûr, “fire”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *péh₂wr̥ (“fire”).
