debauchment:/dɪˈbɔːtʃmənt/| n.|放荡，堕落，腐化|The act of corrupting or seducing someone away from virtue or morality; a state of corruption or depravity.|His debauchment began with gambling and quickly spiraled into drug addiction. 他的堕落始于赌博，并迅速演变成吸毒成瘾。|Synonyms: corruption, depravity, dissipation, perversion|Antonyms: purity, virtue, morality|noun


debauch:/dɪˈbɔːtʃ/| n. v.|放荡，堕落；使放荡，使堕落| (n.) An act or occasion of excessive indulgence in pleasure, especially eating and drinking; (v.) To corrupt morally; seduce; to lead away from excellence or virtue.|The weekend was a debauch of drinking and partying.  这个周末是酗酒和聚会的放纵。|He was debauched by the city's nightlife. 他被城市的夜生活腐蚀了。|Synonyms: (n.) orgy, carousal; (v.) corrupt, pervert|Antonyms: (v.) purify, uplift|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *desbaucher* "to corrupt," from *des-* "away, from" + *bauche* "workshop, construction site."  The original meaning referred to enticing workers away from their jobs.
