blackboard:/ˈblækˌbɔːrd/ (US) /ˈblækˌbɔːd/ (UK)| n. |黑板|A dark, smooth surface, usually made of slate or a similar material, used in schools and other places for writing or drawing on with chalk.|The teacher wrote the equation on the blackboard. 老师把方程式写在黑板上。|Synonyms: chalkboard, whiteboard|Antonyms: N/A|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From black + board.  "Black" refers to the dark color of the board, and "board" refers to a flat piece of wood or other material.  The term came into use in the early 19th century.
