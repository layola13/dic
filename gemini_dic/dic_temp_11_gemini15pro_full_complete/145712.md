overcovetously: /ˌoʊvərˈkʌvɪtəsli/
adverb
过度贪婪地，过分渴望地
Excessively desirous of possessing something, especially something belonging to another.  Characterized by or arising from inordinate desire for wealth or possessions.
He overcovetously eyed his neighbor's new car.  他贪婪地盯着邻居的新车。
The king overcovetously sought to expand his territory. 国王过度贪婪地寻求扩张领土。
Synonyms: greedily, avariciously, rapaciously
Antonyms: generously, charitably, selflessly
词性: adverb (副词)


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + covetously.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessively" or "too much". "Covetously" is an adverb derived from "covetous," meaning "marked by or showing excessive desire, especially for something belonging to another."  The root of "covet" is from Old French *cuveitier*, ultimately from Latin *cupiditas* "desire, eagerness, passion, lust."
