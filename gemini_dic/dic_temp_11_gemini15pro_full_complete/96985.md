infantilistic:/ˌɪnfənˈtɪlɪstɪk/ | adj. |幼稚的，孩子气的|Exhibiting behavior or attitudes characteristic of a young child, especially when inappropriate for an adult.| His infantilistic tantrums were embarrassing to witness.  他孩子气的脾气令人尴尬。| Near synonyms: childish, immature, puerile, juvenile | Antonyms: mature, adult, grown-up |词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Latin *infantilis* "pertaining to an infant," from *infans* "infant, young child" (literally "not speaking"). The suffix *-istic* forms adjectives meaning "relating to" or "characteristic of."
