menshevik: /ˈmɛnʃɪvɪk/ | n. |孟什维克，孟什维主义者|A member of the moderate, non-revolutionary wing of the Russian Social Democratic Workers’ Party, opposed to the Bolsheviks.| The Mensheviks believed in a gradual path to socialism, unlike the Bolsheviks. 孟什维克相信逐步走向社会主义，这与布尔什维克不同。|Synonyms: moderate socialist, reformist |Antonyms: Bolshevik, revolutionary |Noun

Etymology: From Russian меньшевик (men'ševik), from меньше (men'še) 'smaller', 'lesser' (comparative of малый (malyj) 'small', 'little'), referring to their minority status after a party split in 1903.
