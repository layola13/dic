bonapartism:/ˈbəʊnəpɑːrtɪzəm/ /ˈboʊnəpɑːrtɪzəm/ | n. | 独裁统治；波拿巴主义 | The political ideology associated with Napoleon Bonaparte, characterized by a strong central government, military rule, and expansionist policies.  A system of government characterized by popular authoritarianism, often involving a charismatic leader who appeals to national sentiment and promises order and stability in exchange for curtailed civil liberties. |  Historians debate the extent to which modern France still exhibits traces of Bonapartism.  Some political analysts see echoes of Bonapartism in certain contemporary regimes. |  authoritarianism, dictatorship, despotism, tyranny | democracy, liberalism, republicanism | noun
