compensator:/ˈkɒmpənˌseɪtə(r)/ (US) /ˈkɑːmpənˌseɪtər/ (UK)| n. |补偿器；校正装置；平衡器|A device used to counteract or neutralize an undesirable effect.|The pilot used the trim compensator to adjust the aircraft's balance. 飞行员使用微调补偿器来调整飞机的平衡。|Synonyms: counterbalance, equalizer, regulator|Antonyms: amplifier, intensifier|Noun

compensate:/ˈkɒmpənˌseɪt/ (US) /ˈkɑːmpənˌseɪt/ (UK)| v. |补偿；赔偿；抵消|To make up for something unwelcome or unpleasant by exerting an opposite force or effect.|The company will compensate employees for any losses they incurred. 公司将补偿员工所遭受的任何损失。|Synonyms: offset, counterbalance, make amends for|Antonyms: aggravate, worsen|Verb

compensatory:/kəmˈpɛnsətɔːri/ (US) /kəmˈpɛnsətri/ (UK)| adj. |补偿的；赔偿的|Serving to compensate for something.|He received a compensatory payment for his injuries.  他因受伤获得赔偿金。|Synonyms: remedial, corrective, reparative|Antonyms: exacerbating, worsening|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Late Latin word *compensātor*, derived from the Latin verb *compensāre*  meaning "to weigh together, counterbalance," from *com-* "together" + *pensāre* "to weigh."
