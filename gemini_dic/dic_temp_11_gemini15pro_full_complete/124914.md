mudguards: /ˈmʌdˌɡɑːdz/ (US) /ˈmʌdˌɡɑːdz/ (UK)| n. |挡泥板；泥罩|A curved piece of metal or plastic fitted above the wheel of a vehicle to prevent mud and water from being thrown up and back.|The car's mudguards were caked in mud after the off-road drive. 汽车的挡泥板在越野行驶后沾满了泥巴。|
The cyclist's mudguards kept his clothes clean despite the wet road. 尽管路面潮湿，骑车人的挡泥板还是使他的衣服保持干净。|近义词：fenders, splash guards|反义词: None|词性: Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From mud + guard.  "Mud" comes from Old English *mudde, of unknown origin. "Guard" comes from Old North French garder, meaning "to protect," ultimately from Frankish *wardon.
