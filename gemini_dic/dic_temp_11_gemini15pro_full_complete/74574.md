fraulein:/ˈfrɔɪlaɪn/ (US), /ˈfrɔɪlʌɪn/ (UK)| n.| 小小姐；未婚女子|A young unmarried woman; Miss. (Now largely replaced by Frau, except in very formal contexts.)|例句：In older German novels, you often encounter the term "Fraulein" used to address unmarried women. 在古老的德国小说中，你经常会遇到用“Fraulein”来称呼未婚女性。

例句：While traveling in Germany in the 1950s, my grandmother was addressed as "Fraulein."  20世纪50年代，我的祖母在德国旅行时，人们称她为“Fraulein”。|近义词：Miss, Ms.|反义词: Frau (Mrs.)|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From German Fräulein, diminutive of Frau (“woman, lady, Mrs.”).

PHRASE->
(While "fraulein" itself doesn't typically form phrases in modern usage, it's more about its historical context as a form of address.)

1.  Guten Tag, Fraulein. (Good day, Miss.)
2.  Danke schön, Fraulein. (Thank you, Miss.)
3.  Entschuldigen Sie, Fraulein. (Excuse me, Miss.)
4.  Auf Wiedersehen, Fraulein. (Goodbye, Miss.)
5.  Können Sie mir helfen, Fraulein? (Can you help me, Miss?)


注意事项:  The term "Fräulein" is considered outdated and potentially offensive in modern German.  It's best avoided and replaced with "Frau" for all women regardless of marital status.  Its usage is primarily confined to historical contexts.
