neothalamic:/ˌniːoʊθəˈlæmɪk/ | adj. | 新丘脑的 | Relating to or denoting the phylogenetically newer part of the thalamus, comprising the dorsal thalamus, ventral thalamus, and epithalamus. |  The study focused on the neothalamic pathways involved in processing sensory information.  这项研究集中在新丘脑通路中参与处理感官信息的部分。| Synonym:  None (highly specific anatomical term) | Antonym:  Paleothalamic (though less commonly used) | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
neo- + thalamic
neo-:  from Greek *néos* "new, young, fresh"
thalamic: from thalamus + -ic
thalamus: from Late Latin *thalamus* "inner chamber, bridal chamber," from Greek *thálamos*  "inner chamber, store-room, bedchamber"
