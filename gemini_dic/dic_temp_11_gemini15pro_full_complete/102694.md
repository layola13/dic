jamming:/ˈdʒæmɪŋ/ (US), /ˈdʒæmɪŋ/ (UK)| n. v.|干扰，阻塞，卡住，即兴演奏|The act of deliberately interfering with radio or radar signals, or the act of playing improvised music, especially jazz or rock.|

例句：
The enemy's jamming of our radar made it difficult to track their planes. 敌人的雷达干扰使我们难以追踪他们的飞机。

Their jamming session lasted for hours. 他们的即兴演奏持续了几个小时。


近义词：interfering, blocking, obstructing, improvising|
反义词:clearing, aiding, assisting|
词性:noun, verb (present participle of jam)|

jamming:/ˈdʒæmɪŋ/ (US), /ˈdʒæmɪŋ/ (UK)| v.|干扰，阻塞，卡住，即兴演奏|To deliberately interfere with radio or radar signals, or to play improvised music, especially jazz or rock.|

例句：
The hackers were jamming the communication network. 黑客正在干扰通讯网络。

The band was jamming on a blues riff. 乐队正在即兴演奏布鲁斯重复段。


近义词：interfering with, blocking, obstructing, improvising|
反义词:clearing, aiding, assisting|
词性:verb (present participle of jam)|


词源: From the verb "jam," meaning to press or wedge tightly.  Ultimately from a Germanic root related to "kamb" (comb).  The meaning related to music arose in the 20th century.
