manan:/ˈmɑːnɑːn/ | n. | 玛那, 甘露 | Manan is a mythical, life-giving substance in Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism. It is often described as a sweet dew or nectar that falls from the heavens or is produced by certain trees.  It is believed to sustain the gods and give immortality.| The gods drank manan to maintain their immortality. 诸神饮用玛那以保持长生不老。|  The ancient texts describe the taste of manan as exquisitely sweet and satisfying. 古代文献描述玛那的味道极其甜美，令人满足。 | Synonyms: nectar, ambrosia | Antonyms: poison, venom | Noun
