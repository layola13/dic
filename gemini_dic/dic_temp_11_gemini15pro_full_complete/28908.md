carriage:/ˈkærɪdʒ/ (US) /ˈkærɪdʒ/ (UK)| n.|马车；运输；举止；（火车）客车厢|a vehicle with four wheels drawn by two or more horses, used for carrying passengers |The queen rode in a horse-drawn carriage. 女王乘坐马车出行。|Synonyms: coach, chariot, wagon|Antonyms: airplane, car|noun

carriage:/ˈkærɪdʒ/ (US) /ˈkærɪdʒ/ (UK)| n.|举止；姿态|a person's way of standing and moving, especially the way they hold their head and shoulders |She has a very elegant carriage. 她的举止非常优雅。|Synonyms: posture, bearing, demeanor|Antonyms: slouch|noun

carriage:/ˈkærɪdʒ/ (US) /ˈkærɪdʒ/ (UK)| n.|（火车）客车厢|a separate section of a train for carrying passengers |We found a seat in a first-class carriage. 我们在一节一等车厢找到了座位。|Synonyms: car, compartment, coach|Antonyms: engine|noun
