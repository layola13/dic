diploidies: /ˈdɪplɔɪdiz/ | n. |二倍体, 双倍性| The number of diploidies in a cell refers to the presence of two complete sets of chromosomes, one from each parent.  细胞中二倍体的数量指的是存在两套完整的染色体，一套来自每个亲本。 |  Many plants exhibit polyploidies, but most animals are diploidies. 很多植物表现出多倍性，但大多数动物是二倍体。| 近义词: diploids | 反义词: haploidies | 词性: 名词

diploidy: /ˈdɪplɔɪdi/ | n. |二倍性| The state of having two sets of chromosomes, one from each parent. 拥有两套染色体的状态，一套来自每个亲本。|  Diploidy is the normal state for most animal cells.  二倍性是大多数动物细胞的正常状态。| 近义词: diploid state | 反义词: haploidy | 词性: 名词

词源:  From Greek "diplóos" (double) + "-oid" (resembling) + "-y" (state or condition). 源自希腊语 "diplóos"（双倍）+ "-oid"（类似）+ "-y"（状态或条件）。
