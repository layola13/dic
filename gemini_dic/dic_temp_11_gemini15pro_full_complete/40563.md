contumaciously:/ˌkɒntjʊˈmeɪʃəsli/ | adv. | 顽固地，不服从地 | In a stubbornly disobedient manner; rebelliously. |  He contumaciously refused to follow the court's orders. 他顽固地拒绝服从法庭的命令。 |  The teenager contumaciously ignored his parents' curfew.  这个少年顽固地无视了他父母规定的宵禁时间。 | 近义词: rebelliously, defiantly, stubbornly, insolently | 反义词: obediently, respectfully, submissively | 词性: 副词

contumacious:/ˌkɒntjʊˈmeɪʃəs/ | adj. | 顽固的，不服从的 |  Stubbornly disobedient; rebellious. | The contumacious defendant was held in contempt of court.  那个顽固的被告被判藐视法庭。|  Her contumacious behavior led to her expulsion from school. 她不服从的行为导致她被学校开除。|近义词: rebellious, defiant, stubborn, insolent | 反义词: obedient, respectful, submissive | 词性: 形容词

contumacy:/ˈkɒntjʊməsi/ | n. | 顽固，不服从 | Stubborn disobedience; rebelliousness. | His contumacy resulted in a hefty fine. 他的顽固行为导致了巨额罚款。 | The judge cited the defendant's contumacy as a reason for the increased sentence. 法官引用被告的顽固作为加重判决的理由。|近义词: defiance, rebellion, stubbornness, insolence | 反义词: obedience, respect, submission | 词性: 名词


词源:  From Late Latin *contumacia, from Latin contumax (“stubborn, haughty”)  + -ly (adverbial suffix).  Ultimately from com- (“together”) + tumēre (“to swell”).  The original sense was "swelling with pride," hence "stubborn."
