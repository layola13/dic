malign:/məˈlaɪn/| adj. v. |恶意的，诽谤；污蔑，中伤|  (adj.) evil in nature or effect; malevolent;  (v.) to speak about someone in a spitefully critical way |例句：He was the victim of a malign campaign. 他是一场恶意运动的受害者。|近义词：evil, malicious, harmful, slander, defame|反义词：benevolent, benign, praise|词性:形容词，动词
malign:/məˈlaɪn/| v. |污蔑，中伤|to speak about someone in a spitefully critical way|例句：She maligned her former colleagues. 她诽谤了她的前同事。|近义词：slander, defame, vilify, traduce|反义词：praise, commend, laud|词性:动词

Etymology:
From Middle English *malignen, from Old French malignier, from Latin malignāre (“to act maliciously”), from malignus (“ill-disposed, wicked”), from male (“badly”) + genus (“kind, sort”).
