epagoge:/ɛpəˈɡoʊdʒi/ /ɛpəˈɡoʊɡiː/
n.
epagoge (induction)
A method of reasoning by which one draws a general conclusion from particular instances.  It is the opposite of deduction.
Aristotle used the term epagoge to refer to arguments that proceed from particulars to universals.
Epagoge is a crucial part of the scientific method, allowing scientists to form hypotheses based on observations.
近义词: induction, generalization
反义词: deduction, particularization
词性: noun
