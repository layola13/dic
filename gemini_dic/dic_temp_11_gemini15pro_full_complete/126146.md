mutinously:/ˈmjuːtɪnəsli/ | adv. | 反抗地，叛变地 | In a rebellious or disobedient manner, especially against authority. | The sailors mutinously refused to obey the captain's orders. 水手们反叛地拒绝服从船长的命令。 | Synonyms: rebelliously, seditiously, insubordinately | Antonyms: obediently, submissively, respectfully | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From *mutinous* + *-ly*. *Mutinous* comes from  Old French *mutin* "rebellious," from *meuten*, from Vulgar Latin *movitem*, "movement," from Latin *movere* "to move."  The sense evolution is from "stirring, restless" to "rebellious."
