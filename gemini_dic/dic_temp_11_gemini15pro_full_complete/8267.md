anti-isolationism:/ˌæntiˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪzəm/
|n.|反孤立主义|The doctrine or policy of opposing isolationism.  A political stance against the practice of a country isolating itself from the affairs of other nations.|
|Example 1:  The president's speech marked a shift towards anti-isolationism. 总统的演讲标志着向反孤立主义的转变。

|Example 2: The growth of international trade contributed to the rise of anti-isolationism. 国际贸易的增长促成了反孤立主义的兴起。|
|近义词: internationalism, globalism, engagement|
|反义词: isolationism|
|词性: noun|

词源:
"anti-" (prefix) + "isolationism"
"anti-" comes from Greek, meaning "against" or "opposite".
"isolationism" comes from "isolate" + "-ism". "Isolate" comes from Italian "isolato" (past participle of "isolare", to isolate), derived from Latin "insula" (island). "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns that denote doctrines, beliefs, or practices.
