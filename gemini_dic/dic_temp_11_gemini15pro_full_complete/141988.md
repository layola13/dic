organicism:/ɔːrˈɡænɪˌzɪzəm/ | n. |有机论，有机体论|The philosophy that emphasizes the interconnectedness and interdependence of all parts of a system, like a living organism.  It often contrasts with mechanism, which views systems as machines.|The architect's designs reflected his belief in organicism, with buildings blending seamlessly into the landscape.|近义词：holism, vitalism|反义词:mechanism, reductionism|词性:noun

Etymology:  From organic + -ism.  "Organic" traces back to the Greek word "organon," meaning "instrument" or "tool."  The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.
