loop:/luːp/ | n. v. |环，圈；循环；弯曲；打环 |A shape produced by a curve that bends round and crosses itself.  | The roller coaster went through a series of dizzying loops. 过山车穿过了一系列令人眼花缭乱的环形轨道。 |  synonyms: circle, ring, coil, spiral | antonyms: line, straight | noun

loop:/luːp/ | v. |使成环，使成圈；循环；放圈套 |To make something into a loop or loops, or to form something into a curve that bends round and crosses itself.| He looped the rope around the post. 他把绳子绕在柱子上。| synonyms: circle, enclose, encircle | antonyms: straighten, uncoil | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *loupe*, from Old French *loupe* (“magnifying glass; loop, hole”), of uncertain origin, possibly from Frankish *laufa* or a similar Germanic source akin to Old High German *loufa* (“foliage, leaves”).  More distantly related to English *leaf*. The verb sense is derived from the noun.
