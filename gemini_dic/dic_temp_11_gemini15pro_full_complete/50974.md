desinential:/ˌdɛsɪˈnɛnʃəl/
adjective
词尾的，结尾的
Relating to or forming a grammatical ending or desinence.
The desinential changes in the verb indicate tense. 动词的词尾变化表示时态。
synonyms: terminal, final, concluding
antonyms: initial, beginning, introductory
词性: adjective

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
The desinential form "-s" indicates the third-person singular present tense. 词尾形式“-s”表示第三人称单数现在时。
He walks to school every day. 他每天步行上学。

She sings beautifully. 她唱歌很好听。


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
The desinential form "-ing" indicates the present participle. 词尾形式“-ing”表示现在分词。
They are playing in the park. 他们在公园里玩耍。

He is studying for his exam. 他正在准备考试。


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
The desinential changes in the auxiliary verb "have" or "has" indicate the present perfect tense. 助动词“have”或“has”的词尾变化表示现在完成时。
She has finished her homework. 她已经完成了作业。

We have traveled to many countries. 我们去过很多国家。

The desinential form "-ing" is used in the present perfect continuous tense. 词尾形式“-ing”用于现在完成进行时。

She has been studying English for years. 她学习英语已经好几年了。


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
The desinential form "-ed" is commonly used to indicate the past tense. 词尾形式“-ed”通常用于表示过去时。
He walked to school yesterday. 他昨天步行去上学。

She baked a cake for her birthday. 她为自己的生日烤了一个蛋糕。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
The desinential form "-ing" is used in the past continuous tense. 词尾形式“-ing”用于过去进行时。
