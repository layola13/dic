doo-wop: /ˈduːwɑːp/ (American English) | n. |嘟·喔普，节奏蓝调的一种| A genre of rhythm and blues music characterized by close harmony vocals and often nonsensical syllables like "doo-wop." It was popular in the 1950s and early 1960s. |  The Penguins' "Earth Angel" is a classic example of doo-wop.  企鹅乐队的《大地天使》是嘟·喔普的经典范例。 | Synonym: rhythm and blues, R&B | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a music genre) | noun
