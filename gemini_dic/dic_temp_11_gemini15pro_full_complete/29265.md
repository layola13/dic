cassocked:/ˈkæsəkt/
adjective
穿着袈裟的，穿着法衣的
Wearing a cassock; dressed in a cassock.  A cassock is a long, close-fitting garment worn by clergy.
The cassocked priest led the procession. 穿着法衣的牧师带领着队伍。
The bishop, cassocked and mitred, addressed the congregation.  穿着法衣，戴着主教冠的主教向会众讲话。
Synonyms: robed, vested, gowned
Antonyms: unrobed, disrobed
词性: adjective


Etymology:
From the French word *casaque*, meaning a long coat, ultimately from Turkish *kazak*.
