mutism: /ˈmjuːtɪzəm/ (US) /ˈmjuːtɪzəm/ (UK)| n. | 缄默症，沉默症 | Inability or refusal to speak, often caused by psychological factors. |  She suffered from selective mutism as a child and only spoke to her family. 她小时候患有选择性缄默症，只和家人说话。

The trauma caused him to lapse into mutism.  创伤使他陷入了沉默。


|近义词：silence, speechlessness|反义词：talkativeness, loquacity|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mutismus, from Latin mutus (“mute, silent”) + -ismus.
