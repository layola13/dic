mirthsome:/ˈmɜːθsəm/ | adj. | 欢乐的；令人愉快的 | Causing or expressing mirth; merry or cheerful. |  The party was a mirthsome occasion with lots of laughter and dancing.  聚会是一个欢乐的场合，充满了欢声笑语和舞蹈。 | synonyms: merry, jovial, cheerful, joyous, blithe, festive, jolly | antonyms: somber, gloomy, morose, sad, depressing | adjective


Etymology:
From mirth + -some. Mirth itself comes from Old English *myrgþu, from Proto-Germanic *murgiþō, from Proto-Indo-European *mregh- (to be short, small, brief, to trick, deceive).
