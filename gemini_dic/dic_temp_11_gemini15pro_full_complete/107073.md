ladder:/ˈlædər/ (US), /ˈlædə(r)/ (UK)| n.|梯子; 阶梯; 途径; 途径;  (紧身裤袜或长筒袜的) 抽丝, 梯状物|A ladder is a piece of equipment used for climbing up and down, consisting of two long pieces of wood or metal joined by a series of steps or rungs.|He climbed up the ladder to the roof. 他爬上梯子到屋顶。|synonyms: steps, staircase, stairway|antonyms: descent, decline|noun

ladder:/ˈlædər/ (US), /ˈlædə(r)/ (UK)| v.|抽丝, (袜子等) 抽丝;  (尤指价格或数量) 逐步上升(或下降)|To ladder means to develop a run in tights or stockings, or to cause something to increase or decrease gradually.|Her tights laddered when she caught them on a nail. 她的紧身裤袜被钉子钩破了，抽丝了。|synonyms: run (in stockings/tights), unravel|antonyms: repair, mend|verb
