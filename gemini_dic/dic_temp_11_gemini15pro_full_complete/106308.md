koan:/ˈkoʊɑːn/| n. |公案，禅宗谜题|A paradoxical anecdote or riddle used in Zen Buddhism to demonstrate the inadequacy of logical reasoning and to provoke enlightenment. |The Zen master posed a koan to his students, hoping to guide them toward enlightenment.  禅师向他的学生提出了一个公案，希望引导他们走向顿悟。|Synonym: riddle, puzzle, paradox|Antonym: solution, explanation|noun
