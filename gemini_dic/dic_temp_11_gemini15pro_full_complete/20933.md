bleating:/ˈbliːtɪŋ/ | n. | 羊叫声，咩咩叫 | The sound a sheep or goat makes.  |  The farmer heard the bleating of the lambs in the field.  农民听到了田野里羊羔的咩咩叫声。 | synonyms: baa, baaing | antonyms: (No direct antonym for a sound) | noun

bleating:/ˈbliːtɪŋ/ | adj. |  (of a sound) like the cry of a sheep or goat; weak and complaining. | Resembling the plaintive cry of a sheep; often used figuratively to describe a weak complaint. |  He made a bleating apology for his mistake. 他为自己的错误做了一个软弱无力的道歉。 | synonyms: whining, complaining, plaintive | antonyms: assertive, confident | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bleten*, from Old English *blǣtan* (“to bleat”), from Proto-Germanic *blētąną* (“to bleat”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰléh₁- (“to blow, breathe”). Cognate with Dutch *bleten*, German *blöken*, Danish *blæke*.
