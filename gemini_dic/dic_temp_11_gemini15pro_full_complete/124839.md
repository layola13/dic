muckraker: /ˈmʌkˌreɪkər/ | n. | 揭丑记者；专门揭发丑闻的作家 | A journalist who investigates and exposes societal problems, especially corruption or injustice. |  The muckraker exposed the unsanitary conditions in the meatpacking industry.  这位揭丑记者揭露了肉类加工行业的肮脏环境。 | synonyms:  investigative journalist, whistleblower, exposer  | antonyms:  propagandist, apologist | noun

muckrake: /ˈmʌkˌreɪk/ | v. | 揭发丑闻；搜集并揭发丑闻 | To search for and expose real or alleged corruption, scandal, or the like, especially in politics. | The newspaper muckraked the mayor's corrupt dealings. 这家报纸揭露了市长的腐败交易。 | synonyms: expose, unearth, uncover | antonyms:  cover up, conceal, whitewash | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "muckrake" + "-er" (agent suffix). "Muckrake" itself comes from "muck" (manure, filth) + "rake" (a tool for gathering).  The term was popularized by President Theodore Roosevelt in a 1906 speech, where he criticized journalists who focused excessively on negative aspects of society.
