orthotropous:/ɔːrˈθɒtrəpəs/
|adj.| 直生的，正生的|Having the ovule straight, with the micropyle at the apex and the chalaza at the base. This is the most common ovule orientation in flowering plants.|The orthotropous ovule is characteristic of many plant families, including the Polygonaceae.|Synonym: atropous, straight|Antonym: anatropous, campylotropous|Adjective
