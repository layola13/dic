disconcertment:/ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrtmənt/ | n. |  disconcerted: /ˌdɪskənˈsɜːrtɪd/ (adj) | 扰乱，不安，窘迫 |  A feeling of confusion, uneasiness, or embarrassment. | The sudden change of plans caused a great deal of disconcertment among the travelers. 突然的计划改变让旅客们感到非常不安。| 近义词：unease, anxiety, perturbation, confusion | 反义词：composure, calmness, equanimity | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From French *déconcertement*, from *déconcerter* "to disconcert," from *des-* "dis-" + *concerter* "to arrange, agree upon."  Ultimately from Latin *con-* "together" + *certare* "to contend, strive." The sense evolution involves the disruption of a concerted effort or arrangement.
