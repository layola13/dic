blackmail:/ˈblækmeɪl/| vt. n.|敲诈，勒索|extort money from (someone) by threats, typically to expose a secret.  The act of demanding payment or another benefit from someone in return for not revealing compromising or damaging information about them.|He blackmailed her for years by threatening to reveal her secret. 他多年来一直以揭露她的秘密来威胁她，敲诈勒索她。|近义词：extort, coerce, threaten|反义词:  (没有明确的反义词，可以用 protect, defend 等表示保护受害者) |词性: verb, noun

blackmailed:/ˈblækmeɪld/| vt. |敲诈了，勒索了 (blackmail的过去式和过去分词)|past tense and past participle of blackmail|He blackmailed her into giving him money. 他勒索她，让她给了他钱。|近义词：extorted, coerced, threatened|反义词:  (没有明确的反义词) |词性: verb
