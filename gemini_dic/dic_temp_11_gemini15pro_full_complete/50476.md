depressibility:/dɪˌprɛsəˈbɪləti/| n. |易受压抑性，抑郁倾向|The state or quality of being easily depressed or made gloomy.  This can refer to both a physical property (like the depressibility of a button) and a psychological one (like a person's susceptibility to depression).|Her depressibility worsened after the loss of her job.  她失业后更容易抑郁了。|Synonyms: dejection, despondency, melancholy, susceptibility to depression|Antonyms: resilience, buoyancy, cheerfulness|noun

depressibility:/dɪˌprɛsəˈbɪləti/| n. |可压下性|The capacity of something to be pressed down or reduced in volume.|The depressibility of the car's suspension was tested on a bumpy road. 这辆车的悬架的可压下性在颠簸的道路上进行了测试。|Synonyms: compressibility, malleability|Antonyms: rigidity, inflexibility|noun
