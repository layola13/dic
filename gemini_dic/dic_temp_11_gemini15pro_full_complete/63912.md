epiphragm:/ˈɛpɪfræm/ | n. | 膜，隔膜，盖膜 | A membrane, or structure resembling a membrane, that covers or closes an aperture, such as the membrane closing the opening of a snail's shell or the thin layer of mucus that some snails secrete to seal their shell opening during periods of inactivity. |  Snails create an epiphragm to seal their shells and conserve moisture during dry periods.蜗牛会制造一个盖膜来封闭它们的壳，并在干燥时期保持水分。 The snail retreated into its shell and sealed the opening with an epiphragm. 蜗牛缩回壳里，用盖膜封住开口。 |Synonyms: membrane, covering, seal, operculum (in some contexts) | Antonyms: opening, aperture | noun

epiphragm:/ˈɛpɪfræm/ | n. | (解剖学)横膈膜 |  (Anatomy) A muscular partition separating the thorax from the abdomen in mammals; the diaphragm. |  The epiphragm plays a crucial role in respiration. 横膈膜在呼吸中起着至关重要的作用.  Hiccups are caused by involuntary spasms of the epiphragm. 打嗝是由横膈膜的不自主痉挛引起的。 | Synonyms: diaphragm | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps consider terms related to open passage, such as "unobstructed") | noun
