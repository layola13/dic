词源：From Middle English *bacon, *bacoun, from Anglo-Norman bacon, bacun, from Old Low Frankish *bakō (“back meat”), from Proto-Germanic *bakam (“back”). Cognate with Middle Dutch bake, bacon (“back, ham”), Old High German bahho (“back”), German Bache (“wild sow”). Doublet of back.  The sense gradually shifted to refer specifically to salted and smoked pork belly.
