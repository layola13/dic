old-Norse:/ˌoʊld ˈnɔːrs/| adj. n.|古诺尔斯语（的），古斯堪的纳维亚语（的）|A North Germanic language spoken by the inhabitants of Scandinavia and their overseas settlements from about the 8th to the 14th centuries.  It is the ancestor of the modern Scandinavian languages. |The epic poem Beowulf is written in Old Norse.|近义词：Old Scandinavian|反义词:Modern Scandinavian|词性:形容词,名词
