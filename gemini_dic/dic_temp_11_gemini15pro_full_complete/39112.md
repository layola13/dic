confrontation:/ˌkɒnfrʌnˈteɪʃn/ (US) /ˌkɑːnfrʌnˈteɪʃn/| n. |对抗，冲突，对峙|a situation in which there is angry disagreement between people or groups who have different opinions|The two groups were on the verge of a violent confrontation. 这两个团体处于暴力冲突的边缘。|例句：The confrontation between the two countries led to war. 两国之间的冲突导致了战争。|例句：He avoided a confrontation with his boss. 他避免了与老板的冲突。|近义词：conflict, clash, encounter, showdown|反义词：agreement, compromise, harmony|词性:名词

confront:/kənˈfrʌnt/ (US) /kənˈfrʌnt/| vt. |面对，遭遇，对抗|to deal with a difficult situation, problem, etc., especially because you cannot avoid it|He was confronted with a difficult decision. 他面临着一个艰难的决定。|例句：She confronted her fears and climbed the mountain. 她战胜了恐惧，爬上了山。|例句：The police confronted the suspect with the evidence. 警察用证据与嫌疑人对质。|近义词：face, encounter, challenge, oppose|反义词：avoid, evade, dodge|词性:动词
