outbreaks:/ˈaʊtbreɪks/| n. |爆发，突然发生|A sudden occurrence of something unwelcome, such as war or disease.|例句：There was an outbreak of measles in the school. 学校爆发了麻疹。|近义词：eruptions, epidemics, flares, upsurges|反义词:suppressions, containments|词性:名词

词源: From "out-" (meaning "outward, forth") + "break" (meaning "burst, erupt").  The original sense was a bursting forth or eruption.
