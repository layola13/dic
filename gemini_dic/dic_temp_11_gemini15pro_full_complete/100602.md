intimate:/ˈɪntɪmət/ (US), /ˈɪntɪmət/ (UK)| adj. n. v.|亲密的，私密的；知己；暗示，提示|Closely acquainted; familiar; private and personal.  Also, to hint or suggest something indirectly.|She shared an intimate moment with her best friend. 她和最好的朋友分享了一个亲密的时刻。|近义词：close, personal, private, confidential|反义词：distant, aloof, public, impersonal|词性: adjective

intimate:/ˈɪntɪmət/ (US), /ˈɪntɪmət/ (UK)| n.|知己，密友|A close friend or confidant.|He's one of my intimates. 他是我的一个知己。|近义词：confidant, close friend|反义词：stranger, acquaintance|词性: noun

intimate:/ˈɪntɪmeɪt/ (US), /ˈɪntɪmeɪt/ (UK)| v.|暗示，提示|To suggest something indirectly; to hint.|He intimated that he might resign. 他暗示他可能会辞职。|近义词：suggest, hint, imply|反义词：declare, state, announce|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *intimatus*, past participle of *intimare* "to make known, announce," from Latin *intimus* "inmost, innermost," superlative of *inter* "within."
