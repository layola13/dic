ETYMOLOGY->
From the legal term "grievous bodily harm," with "grievous" meaning "causing grief or suffering" and "bodily harm" referring to physical injury. "Grievous" comes from Old French "grevos" (heavy, serious), ultimately from Latin "gravis" (heavy). "Bodily" comes from Old English "bodilic" (of or relating to the body), and "harm" comes from Old English "hearm" (injury, pain).
