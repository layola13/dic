ablaut:/ˈæblaʊt/ (US), /ˈæblaʊt/ (UK)| n.|元音交替，元音变换|A systematic variation of vowels in the same root or affix, especially in Germanic languages, that indicates a grammatical distinction, such as sing, sang, sung. |The word "sing," "sang," and "sung" demonstrate ablaut.|近义词：vowel gradation, vowel mutation|反义词: (No direct antonym exists, potentially vowel stability)|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Ablaut*, from *ab* (“off, away from”) + *Laut* (“sound”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *af* (“off, away from”) + *hlautaz* (“sound”).
