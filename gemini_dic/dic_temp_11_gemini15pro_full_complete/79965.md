go-around:/ˈɡoʊ əˌraʊnd/| n.| 复飞，绕行，争论 |A maneuver in which an aircraft already on final approach to landing abandons the approach and climbs away from the runway. Also, a disagreement or argument.|The pilot had to execute a go-around due to a sudden crosswind. 由于突然的侧风，飞行员不得不复飞。|The project team had several go-arounds before reaching a final decision. 项目团队经过几次争论才最终达成决定。|synonyms: second attempt, do-over, rerun, argument, dispute|antonyms: landing, agreement, accord|noun


go-around:/ˈɡoʊ əˌraʊnd/| n.|机会，一次尝试|An opportunity or a turn.|Everyone will have a go-around on the new simulator. 每个人都将有机会在新模拟器上试一下。|The children took go-arounds riding the pony. 孩子们轮流骑小马。|synonyms: turn, opportunity, chance|antonyms: N/A|noun
