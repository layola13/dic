exaltment:/ɪɡˈzɔːltmənt/ |n.|兴奋，得意，提升|A feeling of extreme happiness or elation.  The state of being raised to a higher rank or position. |She felt a sense of exaltment upon reaching the mountain summit. 她到达山顶时感到一阵兴奋。 The exaltment of the new king was a grand ceremony.  新国王的登基是一个盛大的仪式。|近义词：elation, joy, rapture, elevation, promotion|反义词：depression, sadness, dejection, demotion, degradation|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *exaltacioun*, from Late Latin *exaltātiō*, from Latin *exaltātus*, past participle of *exaltāre* (“to exalt”), from *ex* (“out, up”) + *altus* (“high”).
