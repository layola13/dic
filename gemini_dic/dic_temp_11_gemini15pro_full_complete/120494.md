minuet:/ˌmɪnjuˈɛt/ | n. |小步舞；小步舞曲|A slow, graceful dance in triple time, popular in the 17th and 18th centuries.  A piece of music for such a dance, usually in triple time and having a stately character.|The orchestra played a beautiful minuet.  管弦乐队演奏了一首优美的小步舞曲。|Synonyms: dance, waltz, gavotte |Antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *menuet*, diminutive of *menu* (“small, slender”), from Old French. Ultimately from Latin *minutus* (“small”).
