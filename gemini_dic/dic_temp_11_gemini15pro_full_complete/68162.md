fain:/feɪn/| adj. adv.|乐意的，情愿的；不得不|Pleased or willing under the circumstances; obliged or compelled.|She was fain to admit defeat. 她不得不承认失败。|近义词：glad, willing, happy, content, inclined, disposed, obliged, compelled|反义词：reluctant, unwilling, loath|词性:形容词/副词
词源：From Old English *fǣgen (attested only in unfǣgen "unpleasant, hostile"), from Proto-Germanic *faiginaz "joyful, glad," from Proto-Indo-European *pēyg- "to decorate, adorn." Cognate with Old Frisian fein, fagen "glad," Old Saxon fagan "glad, beautiful," Dutch fijn "fine, delicate," German fein "fine, delicate, elegant," Old Norse feginn "glad," Gothic faginon "to rejoice."
