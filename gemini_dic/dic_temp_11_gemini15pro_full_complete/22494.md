bookworm:/ˈbʊkwɜːrm/| n.|书虫，酷爱读书的人|A person who spends a great deal of time reading.|She's a real bookworm and always has her nose in a book. 她是个十足的书虫，总是埋头读书。|Synonyms: bibliophile, avid reader|Antonyms: non-reader|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From book + worm.  First recorded in the late 16th century.  The "worm" part likely refers to insects that bore into books, extending figuratively to someone who "devours" books.
