character: /ˈkærɪktər/ (US) /ˈkærəktə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 性格，品质；人物，角色；字符，符号 | A person's character refers to their qualities, such as honesty, kindness, or generosity.  A character in a story is a person or other being in the narrative.  A character can also be a written or printed letter, number, or symbol. |  例句：Her generosity is a defining character trait. 她的慷慨是一个决定性的性格特征。

例句：The main character in the novel is a young woman named Alice. 小说中的主角是一个名叫爱丽丝的年轻女子。

近义词：personality, nature, individual, figure, symbol, letter| 反义词： (For personality) - nobody, cipher |词性：noun


characteristic: /ˌkærɪktəˈrɪstɪk/ (US) /ˌkærəktəˈrɪstɪk/ (UK)| adj. n. | 特有的，典型的；特征，特性 | As an adjective, characteristic describes something that is typical of a particular person, place, or thing. As a noun, it refers to a distinguishing feature or quality. | 例句：Bright colours are characteristic of this artist's work.  鲜艳的色彩是这位艺术家作品的典型特征。

例句：One of the key characteristics of this breed of dog is its loyalty.  这种狗的主要特征之一是它的忠诚。


近义词：(adj.) typical, distinctive, representative (n.) feature, trait, attribute | 反义词：(adj.) atypical, unusual |词性：adjective, noun


characterize: /ˈkærɪktəraɪz/ (US) /ˈkærəktəraɪz/ (UK) | vt. | 描绘…的特性；具有…的特征 | To characterize is to describe the distinctive nature or features of something.  It can also mean to be a typical feature of something. | 例句：The author characterizes the protagonist as a complex and conflicted individual. 作者将主人公描绘成一个复杂而矛盾的人物。

例句：Dense fog characterizes this region in winter. 浓雾是这个地区冬天的特征。
