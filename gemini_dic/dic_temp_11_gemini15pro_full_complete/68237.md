fairweather:/ˈfɛərˌwɛðər/| adj.|好天气的；晴天的；只在情况顺利时才给予支持的，靠不住的|Someone who is fairweather is only a friend or supporter when things are going well. |He's a fairweather friend - he's always there when things are good but disappears when you're having problems. 他是个酒肉朋友—— 顺境时总是在你身边，但你遇到麻烦时就消失了。|近义词：fickle, unreliable, inconstant|反义词：loyal, steadfast, true|词性:形容词

Etymology->
From fair (pleasant, agreeable) + weather.  The original sense referred literally to pleasant weather conditions. The figurative sense of "unreliable in times of difficulty" developed later.
