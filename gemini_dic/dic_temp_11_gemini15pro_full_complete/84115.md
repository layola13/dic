half-life:/ˈhæfˌlaɪf/ (US) /ˈhɑːfˌlaɪf/ (UK)| n.|半衰期|The time taken for the radioactivity of a specified isotope to fall to half its original value.|The half-life of carbon-14 is 5,730 years.  碳-14的半衰期是5730年。

half-life:/ˈhæfˌlaɪf/ (US) /ˈhɑːfˌlaɪf/ (UK)| n.|半衰期|The time it takes for something to reduce to half its original value or effectiveness (used figuratively).|The half-life of a new social media trend is about a week.  一个新的社交媒体趋势的半衰期大约是一周。

half-life:/ˈhæfˌlaɪf/ (US) /ˈhɑːfˌlaɪf/ (UK)| n.|半衰期|(figurative)  The time it takes for a feeling, influence, or effect to lose half its strength or intensity. |The half-life of my enthusiasm for the project was surprisingly short. 我对这个项目的热情衰减得惊人地快。
