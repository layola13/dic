awlwort:/ˈɔːlwɜːrt/| n. |锥子草，山芥|A European plant (Subularia aquatica) of the mustard family, growing in shallow water and having awl-shaped leaves.|The awlwort thrives in the shallows of clear lakes. 锥子草在清澈的浅湖中生长茂盛。|近义词: water awlwort, subularia|反义词: (无明确反义词)|词性:noun

Etymology: From 'awl' (referring to the shape of the leaves) + 'wort' (an Old English word for plant).  'Awl' comes from Old English *æle, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch aal and German Ahle. 'Wort' comes from Old English wyrt 'plant, root', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch wortel and German Wurzel 'root'.
