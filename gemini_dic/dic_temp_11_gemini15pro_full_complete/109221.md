lein:/liːn/ | n. | 留置权；扣押权 | A legal right to keep possession of property belonging to another person until a debt owed by that person is discharged. |  He put a lein on the property until the debt was paid.  他将该财产扣押，直到债务偿清。 | synonym: lien, charge, encumbrance | antonym: release, discharge | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *lein, from Latin *ligamen “band, tie,” from ligare "to bind."  The spelling with -ei- arose in the 16th century, probably by association with French words like seigneur.
