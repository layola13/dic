conjuncture:/ˈkʌndʒʌŋktʃər/| n. |结合; 同时发生; 特殊情况; |A combination of events or circumstances, especially one that is unusual or unexpected. |The current economic conjuncture is unfavorable for small businesses. 目前的经济形势对小型企业不利。|
conjuncture:/ˈkʌndʒʌŋktʃər/| n. |推测; 猜想; |A conclusion or opinion formed on the basis of incomplete information. |His theory is based on pure conjecture. 他的理论纯粹是基于推测。|
conjuncture:/ˈkʌndʒʌŋktʃər/| n. |(占星术中的)相位|The relative positions of planets with respect to each other and to Earth, as seen from Earth. |The conjunction of Mars and Venus is said to have a significant impact. 据说火星和金星的相位有重大影响。|
近义词:  situation, circumstance, occasion, convergence,  hypothesis, speculation, supposition|
反义词: certainty, fact, reality |
词性: noun|
词源: Late Middle English: from Latin *conjunctura, based on conjunct- ‘joined together’, from the verb conjungere.|
