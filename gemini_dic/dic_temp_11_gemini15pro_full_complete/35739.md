cochairpersons:/ˌkoʊˈtʃɛərˌpɜːrsənz/
n.|共同主席，联合主席|A group of people who share the responsibility of chairing a meeting or committee.|例句：The cochairpersons led the meeting effectively. 联合主席有效地领导了会议。|例句：The cochairpersons will announce the final decision tomorrow.  联合主席将于明天宣布最终决定。|近义词：co-chairs, joint chairs|反义词:|词性:名词


co-chairs:/ˈkoʊˌtʃɛərz/
n.|共同主席，联合主席|Shortened version of "cochairpersons."|例句：The co-chairs worked well together. 共同主席合作得很好。|例句：The conference had two co-chairs. 会议有两名共同主席。|近义词：cochairpersons, joint chairs|反义词:|词性:名词


co-chair:/ˈkoʊˌtʃɛər/
n. v.|共同主席，联合主席；共同主持|Can be both a noun (referring to the person) and a verb (referring to the action).|例句：She will co-chair the committee with her colleague. 她将与她的同事共同主持委员会。|例句：They co-chaired the meeting successfully. 他们成功地共同主持了会议。|近义词：cochairperson, joint chair|反义词:|词性:名词，动词

词源:  "co-" (together) + "chair" + "persons"  "co-" 表示“共同”，“chair”指“主席”，“persons”指“人们”。因此，"cochairpersons"表示“共同主席”。
