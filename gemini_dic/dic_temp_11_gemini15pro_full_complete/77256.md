gastronomy: /ɡæˈstrɒnəmi/ (UK), /ɡæˈstrɑːnəmi/ (US) | n. | 美食学；烹饪法；讲究的饮食 | The art or science of good eating; the practice or skill of choosing, cooking, and eating good food. |  He studied gastronomy in Paris. 他在巴黎学习美食学。|  Synonyms: culinary arts, cuisine, cookery | Antonyms:  inappetence, anorexia | noun

gastronomic: /ˌɡæstrəˈnɒmɪk/ (UK), /ˌɡæstrəˈnɑːmɪk/ (US) | adj. | 美食的；烹饪的 | Relating to gastronomy. | The city is known for its gastronomic delights. 这座城市以其美食而闻名。| Synonyms: culinary, epicurean | Antonyms: unappetizing, bland | adjective

gastronome: /ˈɡæstrənəʊm/ (UK), /ˈɡæstrənoʊm/ (US) | n. | 美食家 | A person who enjoys and knows about good food and wine.  | He's a well-known gastronome and restaurant critic. 他是一位著名的美食家和餐厅评论家。| Synonyms: gourmand, epicurean, foodie | Antonyms: glutton (although a gastronome appreciates quality, a glutton focuses on quantity) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *gastronomie*, from Ancient Greek *γαστήρ* (gastḗr, “belly, stomach”) + *νόμος* (nómos, “law, custom”).
