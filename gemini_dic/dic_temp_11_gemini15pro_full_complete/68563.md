fangled:/ˈfæŋ.ɡəld/ | adj. | 新式的，新奇的，新花样的 |  Usually used in the phrase "newfangled," meaning excessively or unnecessarily modern or innovative. Often implies a disapproval or skepticism towards the new thing. |  She doesn't like all those newfangled gadgets in the kitchen. 她不喜欢厨房里所有那些新奇的小玩意儿。 |  He prefers his old bicycle to these newfangled electric ones. 他喜欢他的旧自行车，不喜欢这些新式的电动自行车。 | 近义词: modern, innovative, novel, latest | 反义词: old-fashioned, outdated, traditional | 词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *newfangled* or *newfangle*, from *new* + *fangle* (“invention, contrivance”), from Old English *fang* (“to seize, take”) ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fahaną*.
