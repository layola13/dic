jiggered:/ˈdʒɪɡərd/| adj.| 疲惫不堪的；恼火的；惊讶的；该死的 (mildly offensive euphemism)| Exhausted, annoyed, surprised, or damned.  It's a euphemism used to avoid swearing.| "I'm absolutely jiggered after that hike." 我在那次徒步旅行后累坏了。|"I'll be jiggered!"  真是见鬼！|Near Synonyms: exhausted, tired, annoyed, flabbergasted, darned|Antonyms: energetic, refreshed, pleased, calm|adjective


jiggered:/ˈdʒɪɡərd/|  v. (past participle)| 被搞砸了；被操纵了 (often passive)|  Something has been ruined or tampered with. This usage is less common than the adjective form. |"The whole plan was jiggered from the start."  整个计划从一开始就被搞砸了。| "I think the results were jiggered." 我认为结果是被操纵的。|Near Synonyms: ruined, spoiled, manipulated, rigged|Antonyms: fixed, repaired, genuine|verb (past participle)


Etymology:
The origin is uncertain, possibly related to "jig" (a lively dance) implying being worn out from dancing. Another theory suggests a connection to "jerk" (to move suddenly), implying being startled or annoyed.
