fanning:/ˈfænɪŋ/ | v. | 扇动，煽动，激起 | Causing air to move, spreading something out like a fan, increasing something (like a flame or emotion). | She was fanning herself with a magazine. 她正用杂志给自己扇风。| synonyms: waving, airing, stirring, inciting | antonyms: smothering, suppressing | verb

fanning:/ˈfænɪŋ/ | n. | 扇风的动作; (纸牌)展开 | The act of fanning something; the spreading out of playing cards in the hand. | The fanning of the flames made the fire spread quickly. 扇动火焰使火蔓延得很快。 | synonyms: waving, spreading | antonyms: folding, gathering | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "fan," which comes from Old English "fann" (a winnowing fan), ultimately from the Proto-Germanic "*fananan."
