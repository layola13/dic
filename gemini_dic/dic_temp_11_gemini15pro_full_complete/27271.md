caltrop: /ˈkælˌtræp/ (US) /ˈkæltrəp/ (UK)| n.|蒺藜，铁蒺藜；阻碍物|A metal object with four sharp points arranged so that one point always sticks up when the object is thrown on the ground, used to impede pursuit or injure tires. |The police scattered caltrops on the road to stop the fleeing car. 警方在路上撒了铁蒺藜以阻止逃跑的车辆。|The ancient Romans used caltrops to slow down enemy cavalry. 古罗马人使用蒺藜来减缓敌方骑兵的速度。|Synonyms: spike strip, roadblock, obstacle, impediment|Antonyms: aid, assistance, help|noun

caltrop: /ˈkælˌtræp/ (US) /ˈkæltrəp/ (UK)| n.|（植物）蒺藜|A plant with a hard, spiny fruit.|Caltrops are common weeds in dry, open areas. 蒺藜是干燥、开阔地区常见的杂草。|Be careful not to step on the caltrops; their spines can be painful. 小心不要踩到蒺藜；它们的刺会很疼。|Synonyms: puncture vine, goathead|Antonyms: N/A|noun
