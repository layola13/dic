commute: /kəˈmjuːt/ (US), /kəˈmjuːt/ (UK)| n. vi.|通勤，上下班路程，通勤往返|The act of traveling regularly between one's home and place of work.|My commute takes about an hour each way. 我上下班单程大约需要一个小时。|synonyms: travel, journey|antonyms: stay, reside|noun
commute: /kəˈmjuːt/ (US), /kəˈmjuːt/ (UK)| vi.|通勤，减刑|To travel regularly between one's home and place of work.|He commutes to London every day. 他每天通勤到伦敦。|synonyms: travel, journey|antonyms: stay, reside|verb
commute: /kəˈmjuːt/ (US), /kəˈmjuːt/ (UK)| vt.|交换，减刑|To exchange one thing for another; to reduce a penalty to a less severe one.|He commuted his death sentence to life imprisonment. 他将死刑减为无期徒刑。|synonyms: exchange, substitute|antonyms: increase, lengthen|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *commuten*, from Latin *commūtāre* ("to change completely, exchange, alter"), from *com-* ("together, with") + *mūtāre* ("to change").
