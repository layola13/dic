chylific:/kaɪˈlɪfɪk/ | adj. | 乳糜的，乳糜状的 | Relating to chyle, a milky fluid consisting of lymph and emulsified fats formed in the small intestine during digestion, or conveying chyle. | The chylific vessels absorb fats from the intestines. 乳糜管从肠道吸收脂肪。 | Near Synonyms: milky, lacteal | Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, consider terms like "non-chylific" or antonyms related to function, e.g., "non-absorptive") | adjective

chylify:/ˈkaɪlɪfaɪ/ | v. |  形成乳糜 | To convert into chyle. | The digestive process chylifies the fats we eat. 消化过程将我们吃的脂肪转化为乳糜。 | Near Synonyms: emulsify | Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, consider terms related to reverse processes) | verb

Etymology: From Late Latin *chylificus, from Latin chylus (“chyle”) + -ificus (“-making”).  Latin chylus from Greek χυλός (khylós, “juice”).
