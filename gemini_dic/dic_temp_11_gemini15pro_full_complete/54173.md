disentwine:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtwaɪn/
verb
解开，分开
to separate things that have become twisted or tangled together
The branches of the two trees had become so disentwined that it was difficult to disentwine them.  两棵树的枝条缠绕在一起，很难将它们解开。
She gently disentwined her fingers from his. 她轻轻地把自己的手指从他的手中解开。
近义词:untangle, unravel, unwind, separate
反义词:entwine, tangle, intertwine
词性:v.

disentwine:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtwaɪn/
verb
理清，弄清楚
to make something that is complicated or confusing easier to understand
It took years for historians to disentwine the complex web of political intrigue.  历史学家花了数年时间才理清复杂的政治阴谋网络。
The detective worked tirelessly to disentwine the truth from the lies.  侦探不知疲倦地工作，试图从谎言中理清真相。
近义词:unravel, resolve, clarify
反义词:complicate, confuse, obscure
词性:v.
