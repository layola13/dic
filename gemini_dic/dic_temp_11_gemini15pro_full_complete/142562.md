ostentatious:/ˌɑːstənˈteɪʃəs/ | adj. | 炫耀的，卖弄的 | Characterized by vulgar or pretentious display; designed to impress or attract notice. | He drove an ostentatious sports car. 他开着一辆炫耀的跑车。|  Synonyms: showy, pretentious, flamboyant, flashy | Antonyms: modest, unassuming, understated | adjective


ostentation:/ˌɑːstənˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 炫耀，卖弄 | Pretentious and vulgar display, especially of wealth and luxury, intended to impress or attract notice. |  The ostentation of the wedding was breathtaking. 婚礼的奢华令人叹为观止。 | Synonyms: showiness, pretentiousness, flamboyance, flashiness | Antonyms: modesty, humility, understatement | noun

ostensive:/ɑːˈstɛnsɪv/ | adj. | 表面的，公开的, 外表的 | Appearing or professed to be true or real but not necessarily so.  Displayed or shown especially in an ostentatious way. | His ostensive reason for visiting was to check on his sick aunt, but he really wanted to borrow money. 他表面上的探病理由是为了看望他生病的姑姑，但他实际上是想借钱。 | Synonyms: apparent, seeming, outward, professed | Antonyms: real, genuine, actual, hidden | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *ostensivus, from Latin ostendere "to show, exhibit," from ob- "toward" (see ob-) + tendere "to stretch" (see tenet).
