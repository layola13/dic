exsudation:/ˌeksuːˈdeɪʃn/ | n. |渗出, 渗出物|The process of exuding; the oozing of a fluid, as sweat or serum, from the tissues of a plant or animal.  The substance exuded.|
例句：The exsudation of sap from the tree was a sign of disease.  树液的渗出是疾病的迹象。

例句：The wound showed signs of exsudation.  伤口显示出渗出的迹象。
|近义词：discharge, secretion, oozing, seepage|反义词: absorption, retention|词性:noun

exude:/ɪɡˈzuːd/ | v. |渗出，流出；散发|To discharge or emit (a liquid or gas, or something in a liquid or gaseous state) gradually through pores or incisions, as sweat, moisture, gum, or scent. To display conspicuously or abundantly (a quality or feeling).|
例句：The pine tree exuded resin. 松树渗出树脂。

例句：She exuded confidence. 她散发着自信。
|近义词：emit, ooze, radiate, emanate|反义词: absorb|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin exsūdātiōn- (stem of exsūdātiō ), from exsūdāre ‘to sweat out’, from ex- ‘out’ + sūdāre ‘to sweat’.
