consolidators:/kənˈsɒlɪdeɪtəz/
| n. |整合者，合并者|A person or company that consolidates something, especially by combining smaller companies or businesses into a larger one.|The airline industry has seen a wave of mergers and consolidators in recent years.|近义词：amalgamators, mergers, integrators|反义词: dividers, separators|Noun


consolidate:/kənˈsɒlɪdeɪt/
| v. |巩固，加强，合并|To make something stronger or more secure; to combine several things into a single more effective or coherent whole.|The company consolidated its position in the market by buying out its main competitor.|近义词：strengthen, reinforce, combine, merge|反义词: weaken, divide, separate|Verb


consolidated:/kənˈsɒlɪdeɪtɪd/
| adj. |合并的，统一的，巩固的|Made stronger or more secure; combined into a single more effective or coherent whole.|The company issued a consolidated financial statement for all its subsidiaries.|近义词：combined, unified, strengthened, reinforced|反义词: divided, separated, weakened|Adjective
