caitiff:/ˈkeɪtɪf/| n. adj.|懦夫，卑鄙的人；可怜的，卑怯的|A cowardly and despicable person. Can also mean wretched or miserable.|The caitiff cowered before the king, begging for mercy.|近义词: coward, villain, wretch, miscreant|反义词: hero, brave, courageous|词性:名词, 形容词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *caitif* (Modern French *chétif*), from Late Latin *captivus* "captive," from Latin *captus*, past participle of *capere* "to take, seize."  The semantic shift occurred from "captive" to "despicable" likely through association with the poor treatment of prisoners.
