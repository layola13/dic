geoglyph: /ˈdʒiːəˌɡlɪf/ | n. | 地画 | A large design or motif (generally longer than 4 metres) produced on the ground and typically formed by rocks or similarly durable elements of the landscape, such as stones, stone fragments, gravel pavements or earth mounds. |  The Nazca Lines are perhaps the most famous geoglyphs in the world. 纳斯卡线条可能是世界上最著名的地画。| synonym: earthwork, earth figure, ground drawing | antonym: N/A | noun
