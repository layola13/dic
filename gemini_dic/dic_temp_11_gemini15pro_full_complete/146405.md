overfocus: /ˌoʊvərˈfoʊkəs/| v. n.|过度专注，过度聚焦|To concentrate on something too intently or narrowly, often to the exclusion of other important things.  The act of concentrating too intently or narrowly.|He tends to overfocus on details and misses the bigger picture. 他往往过度关注细节而忽略了全局。|近义词: fixate, obsess, hyperfocus|反义词: lose focus, be distracted, neglect|词性: verb, noun

Etymology:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much") + "focus" (from Latin "focus" meaning "hearth, fireplace," later used metaphorically for a center of activity or attention)
