liberate:/ˈlɪbəreɪt/ /ˈlɪbərɪt/| v.|解放，释放|to set free, as from imprisonment or bondage; to free from any restriction or guiding influence |例句：The army liberated the prisoners of war. 军队解放了战俘。|例句：Education can liberate the mind. 教育可以解放思想。|近义词：free, release, emancipate, deliver|反义词： imprison, confine, enslave, restrict|词性:动词

liberated:/ˈlɪbəreɪtɪd/ /ˈlɪbəreɪtɪd/| adj.|解放了的，无拘无束的|freed from traditional social constraints or inhibitions, especially relating to sexual roles or behavior|例句：She felt liberated after leaving her restrictive job. 离开束缚她的工作后，她感到自由了。|例句：The liberated woman challenged societal norms.  这位思想解放的女性挑战了社会规范。|近义词：free, unrestrained, uninhibited|反义词： restricted, constrained, inhibited|词性:形容词

liberation:/ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃn/ /ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən/| n.|解放|the act of liberating or the state of being liberated|例句：The liberation of the hostages was a cause for celebration. 人质的获释是值得庆祝的事情。|例句：The women's liberation movement fought for equal rights.  妇女解放运动争取平等权利。|近义词：freedom, release, emancipation, deliverance|反义词：captivity, confinement, oppression, enslavement|词性:名词

词源：From Latin liberātus, past participle of liberāre (“to free”), from līber (“free”).
