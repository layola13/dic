nestorianism:/ˌnɛstɔːriənɪzəm/| n. |聂斯托里主义|A Christological doctrine attributed to Nestorius, Patriarch of Constantinople (died 451), asserting that the divine and human natures of Jesus Christ are separate and distinct persons united in one being, and therefore that Mary, the mother of Jesus, should be called Christotokos (Bearer of Christ) but not Theotokos (Bearer of God).|The spread of Nestorianism throughout the East was facilitated by the Silk Road.|近义词:heresy, heterodoxy|反义词:orthodoxy|noun
