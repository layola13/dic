baselining:/ˈbeɪsˌlaɪnɪŋ/
n.|基线测量；基线制定；基线化|The process of establishing a baseline, a starting point for comparisons or measurements.|例句：Baselining the current performance is crucial before implementing any changes.  制定当前性能的基线对于实施任何更改至关重要。

例句：The project manager insisted on thorough baselining before the project started. 项目经理坚持在项目开始前进行彻底的基线测量。|近义词：benchmarking, establishing a baseline, measuring initial performance|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider) ongoing measurement, continuous monitoring|词性:noun (derived from verb "baseline")

baseline:/ˈbeɪsˌlaɪn/
n.|基线；底线|A starting point or standard against which progress or comparisons can be made.|例句：We need to establish a baseline for energy consumption. 我们需要建立一个能源消耗的基线。

例句：The baseline data will help us track our progress.  基线数据将帮助我们跟踪进度。|近义词：benchmark, standard, starting point|反义词:(No direct antonym)|词性:noun

baseline:/ˈbeɪsˌlaɪn/
vt.|为…定基线；为…作对比|To establish a baseline for something.|例句：They baselined the company's performance last year. 他们以公司去年的业绩为基线。

例句：We need to baseline these figures against industry standards. 我们需要将这些数字与行业标准进行对比。|近义词：benchmark, compare, measure against a standard|反义词:(No direct antonym)|词性:verb
