chauvinist: /ˈʃoʊvɪnɪst/ | n. | 沙文主义者；盲目爱国者 | A chauvinist is someone who believes that their own group or country is superior to all others.  | He's such a chauvinist, always bragging about how much better his country is than anywhere else. 他真是个沙文主义者，总是吹嘘他的国家比其他任何地方都好。

|  例句：Many people consider him a male chauvinist. 很多人认为他是大男子主义者。

|  例句：She's a chauvinist when it comes to her hometown sports teams; she can't stand to hear any criticism of them.  说到她家乡的运动队，她就是一个沙文主义者；她不能忍受任何对他们的批评。

| 近义词: bigot, jingoist, nationalist | 反义词: cosmopolitan | 词性: noun


chauvinists: /ˈʃoʊvɪnɪsts/ | n. | 沙文主义者；盲目爱国者（复数） |  The plural form of chauvinist.  | The chauvinists started a fight in the bar. 那些沙文主义者在酒吧里挑起了争斗。

|  例句：The chauvinists refused to acknowledge any other culture as valid. 这些沙文主义者拒绝承认任何其他文化是有效的。

|  例句：Those chauvinists are always causing trouble. 那些沙文主义者总是惹麻烦。

| 近义词: bigots, jingoists, nationalists | 反义词: cosmopolitans | 词性: noun


词源: From Nicolas Chauvin, a legendary, possibly fictitious, soldier of excessive patriotism in Napoleon's army. 源于尼古拉·沙文，拿破仑军队中一位极度爱国的传奇人物，可能是虚构的。
