desolated:/ˈdesəleɪtɪd/
adjective
荒凉的，孤寂的
(of a place) deserted and lonely
例句：The desolated landscape stretched for miles.  荒凉的景色绵延数英里。

例句：She felt desolated after her husband died.  丈夫去世后，她感到十分孤寂。
近义词：deserted, barren, bleak, forlorn, desolate
反义词：populated, cheerful, lively, bustling
词性: adjective


desolate:/ˈdesələt/
adjective
荒凉的，孤寂的
(of a place) deserted and lonely
例句：The town was left desolate after the earthquake. 地震过后，这座城镇变得荒凉。

例句：He felt desolate and abandoned. 他感到孤独无助。
近义词：deserted, barren, bleak, forlorn
反义词：populated, cheerful, lively, bustling
词性: adjective


desolate:/ˈdesəleɪt/
verb
使荒凉，使孤寂
make (someone) feel utterly wretched and unhappy
例句：The war desolated the country. 战争使这个国家变得荒凉。

例句：The news of her death desolated him. 她去世的消息使他悲痛欲绝。
近义词：devastate, ruin, destroy
反义词：build, create, restore
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *desolatus, past participle of desolare (“to abandon, forsake, desert, lay waste”), from de- (“un-, de-”) + solare (“to make solitary”), from solus (“alone”).
