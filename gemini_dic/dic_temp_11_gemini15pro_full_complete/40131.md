contemplativeness:/kənˌtɛmplətɪvnəs/| n.|沉思；冥想；深思熟虑|The monk's contemplativeness was evident in his serene expression.  这位僧侣的沉思冥想从他平静的表情中可以明显看出。|Contemplativeness is a state of deep thought and reflection, often characterized by quiet introspection. 沉思是一种深度思考和反思的状态，通常以安静的内省为特征。|例句：Her contemplativeness allowed her to find a solution to the complex problem. 她的深思使她找到了解决这个复杂问题的方法。

例句：The poet's contemplativeness is reflected in his introspective verses. 诗人的沉思体现在他内省的诗句中。|近义词：thoughtfulness, pensiveness, reflection, meditation|反义词：frivolity, impulsiveness, thoughtlessness|词性:名词

contemplate:/ˈkɒntɛmpleɪt/| v.|沉思；冥想；深思熟虑|She contemplated the meaning of life. 她思考人生的意义。|To contemplate is to think deeply and seriously about something. 沉思就是深入认真地思考某事。|例句：He contemplated the consequences of his actions. 他仔细考虑了他行为的后果。

例句：She sat by the window, contemplating the beauty of the sunset. 她坐在窗边，沉思着日落的美丽。|近义词：consider, ponder, meditate, reflect|反义词：ignore, neglect, disregard|词性:动词
