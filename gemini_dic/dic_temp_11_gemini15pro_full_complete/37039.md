Comanches:/kəˈmæntʃɪz/ | n. |科曼奇人|A Native American people traditionally inhabiting the southern Great Plains, especially parts of present-day Texas, Oklahoma, Kansas, New Mexico, and Colorado.| The Comanches were renowned horsemen. 科曼奇人是著名的骑兵。| synonyms: Comanche Indians | antonyms: N/A | noun

Comanche:/kəˈmæntʃi/ | n. adj.| 科曼奇人；科曼奇语 |A member of the Comanche people; the Uto-Aztecan language of the Comanche people.| He speaks Comanche. 他会说科曼奇语。| synonyms: Comanche Indian | antonyms: N/A | noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *Comanche*, from Ute *kʉmantʃi*  "enemy, stranger".
