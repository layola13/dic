cimolite:/ˈsɪməˌlaɪt/
n.
皂土; Cimolian earth; cimolite clay
A soft, white, clayey mineral, a hydrous silicate of aluminium, found chiefly in the island of Cimolus (Kimolos) in the Aegean Sea. It is used as an absorbent, in cleaning and polishing, and as a filler in paints and pharmaceuticals.
Cimolite was used by fullers in ancient times to remove grease and dirt from cloth.  Cimolite is also used as a polishing agent for silver and other metals.

Cimolite is a rare clay mineral that is found primarily on the Greek island of Kimolos.

Synonyms:fuller's earth, bleaching clay, degreasing clay
Antonyms:N/A
word type:noun
