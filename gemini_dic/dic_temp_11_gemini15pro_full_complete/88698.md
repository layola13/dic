hobble:/ˈhɒbəl/ (UK) /ˈhɑːbl/ (US)| v. n.| 蹒跚;跛行;阻碍;困难;束缚|To walk with difficulty, typically because of an injury to the foot or leg; a rope or strap used to hobble an animal.|
The old man hobbled across the street. 老人蹒跚地穿过街道。

The injured horse hobbled back to the stable. 受伤的马一瘸一拐地回到马厩。

Government regulations are hobbling our efforts to compete. 政府法规阻碍了我们参与竞争。

The company's debt is a major hobble on its growth. 公司的债务是其发展的主要障碍。|
近义词：limp, stagger, stumble, falter, hamper, impede, restrict|
反义词：stride, sprint, run, facilitate, aid|
词性: verb, noun


hobble:/ˈhɒbəl/ (UK) /ˈhɑːbl/ (US)| n.| 蹒跚;跛行;困难;束缚|A rope or strap used to hobble an animal; a limping walk.|
The horse's hobble prevented it from running away.  马的脚绊防止它跑掉。

His injury had left him with a noticeable hobble. 他的伤使他明显跛行。

The lack of funding is a hobble to the project. 缺乏资金是该项目的障碍。|
近义词：limp, impediment, restraint|
反义词：freedom, ease|
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hoblen, probably frequentative of *hobben, of uncertain origin. Perhaps related to hop or to Icelandic *höfga (“to restrain”).
