lothness:/ˈloʊθnəs/ | n. | 不情愿，勉强 | Unwillingness or reluctance to do something. | He showed a great lothness to get involved. 他表现出极大的不情愿参与其中。|  She accepted the task with extreme lothness. 她极不情愿地接受了这项任务。 | reluctance, unwillingness, disinclination | willingness, eagerness, readiness | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lothnes*, from Old English *lāðnes*, from *lāð* ("hostile, hateful, loathsome") + *-nes* (suffix forming abstract nouns).  Cognate with Old Saxon *lēdnes* ("affliction"), Old Frisian *lēthnisse* ("hate, loathing"), Old High German *leidnessi* ("hate, distress"). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *laiþiz* ("hateful, hostile").
