coronets:/ˈkɒrənɪts/ (UK), /ˈkɔːrənɪts/ (US)| n.|小王冠；冠状物|A small crown, especially one worn by a person of noble rank less than sovereign; something resembling a small crown.|The princess wore a coronet of diamonds.|Synonyms: crown, diadem, tiara|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

coronets:/ˈkɒrənɪts/ (UK), /ˈkɔːrənɪts/ (US)| n.|花冠|The upper part of a horse's hoof where the hair meets the skin.|The farrier examined the coronets of the horse for any signs of injury.|Synonyms:  coronal band|Antonyms: N/A|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "coronete" (small crown), diminutive of "corone" (crown), from Latin "corona" (crown, garland).
