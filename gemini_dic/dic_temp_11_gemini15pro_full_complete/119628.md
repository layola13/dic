middle-agers: /ˈmɪdlˌeɪdʒərz/ | n. |中年人|People who are middle-aged, typically between 45 and 65 years old.|Middle-agers are often concerned about their health and retirement plans. 中年人通常关心他们的健康和退休计划。|Synonyms: middle-aged people, people in midlife |Antonyms: youngsters, children, seniors, elderly|noun


middle-aged: /ˌmɪdlˈeɪdʒd/ | adj. |中年的|Being in the period of life between youth and old age, typically between 45 and 65 years old.| The middle-aged couple decided to take a cruise. 这对中年夫妇决定去乘船游览。|Synonyms: midlife |Antonyms: young, youthful, elderly, old|adjective


Etymology:
"middle" + "aged"

"middle" comes from Old English "middel", meaning "mid", "equidistant from extremes".

"aged" comes from Old English "āģed", past participle of "āģian" meaning "to grow old".
