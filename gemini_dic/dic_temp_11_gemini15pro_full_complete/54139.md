disenfranchise: /ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. | 剥夺权利，使失去公民权；使丧失权利 | To deprive someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote. |  Many people were disenfranchised because of their criminal records. 许多人因为犯罪记录而被剥夺了公民权。| synonyms: dispossess, deprive, strip | antonyms: empower, enfranchise | verb

disenfranchising: /ˌdɪsɪnˈfræntʃaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 剥夺权利的，使人丧失权利的 |  Depriving of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote. | The new law is seen as a disenfranchising tactic aimed at suppressing minority votes.  这项新法律被视为旨在压制少数族裔选票的剥夺权利的策略。 | synonyms: marginalizing, oppressive, excluding | antonyms: empowering, liberating, inclusive | adjective
