eliminative:/ɪˈlɪmɪnətɪv/| adj.|消除的，排除的|Relating to or involving the elimination or removal of something, especially something unwanted or unnecessary.  Focusing on the removal of false beliefs or theories.|The scientist proposed an eliminative approach to studying the mind, discarding traditional notions of consciousness.  科学家提出了一种消除性的方法来研究心灵，摒弃了传统的意识概念。|近义词: reductive, exclusionary, purgative|反义词: additive, inclusive|词性:形容词

Etymology: From Late Latin *eliminativus, from eliminat- ‘removed, thrust out’, from the verb eliminare, from e- (variant of ex-) ‘out’ + limen, limin- ‘threshold’.
