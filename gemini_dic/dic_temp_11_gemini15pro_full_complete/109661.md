leukaemia (British English) / leukemia (American English) /ljuːˈkiːmiə/ | n. |白血病|A malignant progressive disease in which the bone marrow and other blood-forming organs produce increased numbers of immature or abnormal leucocytes. These suppress the production of normal blood cells, leading to anaemia and other symptoms.|例句1：Leukaemia is a cancer of the blood cells. 白血病是一种血细胞癌。

例句2：He was diagnosed with acute leukaemia. 他被诊断出患有急性白血病。|近义词：cancer (泛指癌症), blood cancer|反义词: health (健康), remission (缓解)|词性: noun


leucocythaemia (British English, archaic) /ˌljuːkəʊsaɪˈθiːmiə/ | n. |白血病|An archaic term for leukaemia, a malignant progressive disease in which the bone marrow and other blood-forming organs produce increased numbers of immature or abnormal leucocytes.|例句1:  The doctor used the archaic term "leucocythaemia" instead of the modern "leukaemia." 医生使用了古老的术语“leucocythaemia”而不是现代的“leukaemia”。

例句2: Leucocythaemia was a poorly understood disease in the 19th century.  在19世纪，白血病是一种鲜为人知的疾病。|近义词：leukaemia, cancer (泛指癌症), blood cancer|反义词: health (健康), remission (缓解)|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek λευκός (leukós, “white”) + κύτος (kútos, “cell”) + αἷμα (haîma, “blood”).  The term reflects the high white blood cell count often seen in this disease.
