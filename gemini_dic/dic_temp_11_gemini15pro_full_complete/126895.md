narcissuses:/nɑːrˈsɪsəsɪz/
n.
水仙花（复数）
A bulbous Eurasian plant of the amaryllis family, which typically bears bright yellow flowers with a long trumpet-shaped centre.
The narcissuses are blooming in the garden.  花园里的水仙花正在盛开。

narcissus:/nɑːrˈsɪsəs/
n.
水仙花（单数）
A bulbous Eurasian plant of the amaryllis family, which typically bears bright yellow flowers with a long trumpet-shaped centre. Also refers to a person characterized by excessive self-love.
The narcissus is a symbol of spring.  水仙花是春天的象征。


Etymology:
From Latin *narcissus, from Greek νάρκισσος (nárkissos). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *nark- (“to be numb”).  The flower is named after Narcissus, a figure in Greek mythology who fell in love with his own reflection.

Synonyms:
daffodil, jonquil

Antonyms:
(As a flower, it doesn't have a direct antonym.  As a personality trait - referring to narcissism -) humility, modesty
