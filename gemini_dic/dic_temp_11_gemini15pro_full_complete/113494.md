lysosomes:/ˈlaɪsəˌsoʊmz/| n. |溶酶体|A membrane-bound organelle found in the cytoplasm of most eukaryotic cells, containing digestive enzymes.  They break down excess or worn-out cell parts. They may be used to destroy invading viruses and bacteria.|Lysosomes are crucial for breaking down cellular waste. 溶酶体对于分解细胞废物至关重要。

The lysosomes digest unwanted materials. 溶酶体消化不需要的物质。


|近义词：None (Lysosomes are unique organelles).|反义词: None|noun
