bowdlerising: /ˈbaʊdləˌraɪzɪŋ/ | n. | 删节；净化；修订 | The act of removing or changing parts of a book, play, etc. considered offensive or unsuitable, especially to protect the morals of children. |  Bowdlerising classic literature often removes the author's original intent.  净化经典文学作品往往会抹去作者的初衷。 | Synonyms: censoring, expurgating, sanitizing | Antonyms:  unabridged, unexpurgated | noun


bowdlerise: /ˈbaʊdləˌraɪz/ | v. | 删节；净化；修订 | To remove or change parts of a book, play, etc. considered offensive or unsuitable, especially to protect the morals of children. |  The publisher bowdlerised the novel before releasing it to a younger audience. 出版商在将小说发行给年轻读者之前对其进行了删节。 | Synonyms: censor, expurgate, sanitize | Antonyms:  publish unabridged, leave unexpurgated | verb

From Thomas Bowdler (1754-1825), an English physician who published an expurgated edition of Shakespeare's works.
