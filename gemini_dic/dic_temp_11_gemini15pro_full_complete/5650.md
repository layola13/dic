altitudes:/ˈæltɪtjuːdz/
n.
海拔高度；高地；高度；高处
the height of an object or point in relation to sea level or ground level.
The plane flew at an altitude of 30,000 feet.  飞机在30,000英尺的高度飞行。

The climbers reached an altitude of 20,000 feet. 登山者到达了20,000英尺的高度。

elevation, height
depth, lowness
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: altitude, from Old French, or from Latin altitūdō ‘height’, from altus ‘high’.
