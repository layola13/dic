disjoin:/dɪsˈdʒɔɪn/ | v. | 分离，拆开 | To separate or become separated; disconnect. | The earthquake disjoined the railroad tracks. 地震使铁轨脱节了。|  The two pieces of the puzzle disjoined easily.  这两块拼图很容易分开。 | synonyms: separate, detach, disconnect, disunite, sever | antonyms: join, connect, unite, link | verb

disjoint:/dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt/ | adj. | 分离的，不连接的 | Lacking connection or consistency; separate. | The novel’s plot felt disjoint and confusing. 这部小说的情节感觉脱节且令人困惑。|  The speaker presented several disjoint arguments that were hard to follow. 演讲者提出了几个互不关联的论点，很难理解。| synonyms: disconnected, disunited, separate, fragmented, incoherent | antonyms: connected, united, joined, coherent, integrated | adjective
