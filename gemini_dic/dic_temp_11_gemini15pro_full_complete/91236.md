hydrochlorofluorocarbon:/ˌhaɪdrəˌklɔːrəˈflʊərəˌkɑːrbən/ | n. | 氢氯氟烃 | A compound containing hydrogen, chlorine, fluorine, and carbon atoms, used as refrigerants, solvents, and blowing agents.  These substances are known to deplete the ozone layer. |  The use of hydrochlorofluorocarbons has been phased out due to their ozone-depleting potential. 氢氯氟烃的使用已因其消耗臭氧层的可能性而逐步淘汰。 |  HCFCs, hydrochlorofluorocarbons |  CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons) | noun
Etymology->
hydro- (water) + chloro- (chlorine) + fluoro- (fluorine) + carbon
