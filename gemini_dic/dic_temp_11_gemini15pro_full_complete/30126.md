cedrat:/ˈsiːdræt/ | n. |香橼; 香橼树|A small, fragrant citrus fruit resembling a lemon, with a thick, bumpy rind.  It is used in perfumes and for flavoring.|The cedrat is a key ingredient in many traditional Italian liqueurs. 香橼是许多传统意大利利口酒的关键成分。|Synonyms: citron, etrog |Antonyms: N/A |Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cédrat*, from Italian *cedro*, from Latin *citrus*. Ultimately from Ancient Greek *κέδρος (kédros)* meaning "cedar, juniper". The association likely comes from the aromatic qualities shared by both cedrat and cedar wood.
