grafter:/ˈɡrɑːftər/ (UK), /ˈɡræftər/ (US)| n.|嫁接者；辛苦工作的人；贪污受贿者|A person who works hard, especially at a physically demanding job. Also, someone who uses their position or influence dishonestly for personal gain.|例句：He's a real grafter, always the first to arrive and the last to leave. 他是一个真正勤奋的人，总是第一个来最后一个走。

例句：The investigation revealed him to be a grafter who accepted bribes from contractors. 调查显示他是一个接受承包商贿赂的贪污者。|近义词：worker, toiler, hustler, swindler, crook|反义词：idler, loafer, slacker|词性: noun

graft:/ɡrɑːft/ (UK), /ɡræft/ (US)| n. v.|嫁接；努力工作；贪污|
(n.) A shoot or twig inserted into a slit on the trunk or stem of a living plant, from which it receives sap.  Also, hard work or arduous labor.  Dishonestly using one's position or influence for personal gain.
(v.) To insert (a shoot or twig) as a graft.  Also, to work hard. To acquire (money or advantage) dishonestly.|例句：The gardener grafted the apple branch onto the rootstock. 园丁将苹果枝嫁接到砧木上。

例句：She had to graft hard to get where she is today. 她必须努力工作才能达到今天的成就。|近义词：(n.) transplant, exertion, corruption; (v.) implant, toil, embezzle|反义词：(n.) idleness, honesty; (v.) laze, donate|词性: noun, verb
