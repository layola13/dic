ETYMOLOGY->
From gaudy + -ness.  "Gaudy" comes from the Anglo-French "gaudir" meaning "to rejoice," later influenced by the idea of festive attire.  The "-ness" suffix transforms the adjective "gaudy" into the noun "gaudiness."
