dodoism:/ˈdoʊdoʊɪzəm/ | n. | 愚蠢的行为；顽固不化；死板的做法 |  The practice of clinging to outdated ideas or methods, especially in the face of overwhelming evidence to the contrary.  It refers to the extinct dodo bird, known for its inability to adapt and survive. |  His dodoism in sticking to the old marketing strategy cost the company dearly. 他死守着旧的营销策略不放，这种愚蠢的行为让公司付出了高昂的代价。 | synonyms: obstinacy, rigidity, inflexibility, shortsightedness | antonyms: adaptability, flexibility, open-mindedness | noun

dodoism:/ˈdoʊdoʊɪzəm/ | n. | 故步自封；墨守成规 |  A stubborn refusal to change one's ways or adapt to new circumstances, often leading to failure or decline. | Their dodoism prevented them from seeing the need for innovation. 他们的故步自封使他们看不到创新的必要性。 | synonyms: conservatism, traditionalism (when taken to an extreme), inertia | antonyms: progressivism, innovation, adaptability | noun
