counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/| adj. n. v. |伪造的；假冒的；赝品；伪造；假冒|Made in exact imitation of something valuable or important with the intention to deceive or defraud.|例句：The police seized a large number of counterfeit banknotes. 警方查获了大量假钞。|近义词：fake, forged, spurious, bogus|反义词：genuine, authentic|词性:形容词

counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/| n. |赝品；伪造品|A fraudulent imitation of something else; a forgery.|例句：This painting is a counterfeit. 这幅画是赝品。|近义词：fake, forgery, sham|反义词：original, genuine|词性:名词

counterfeit:/ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt/| v. |伪造；假冒|To imitate fraudulently.|例句：He was accused of counterfeiting money. 他被指控伪造货币。|近义词：forge, fake, copy|反义词：verify, authenticate|词性:动词

词源:
From Old French contrefait, past participle of contrefaire "to imitate," from contre- "against" + faire "to make, to do."
