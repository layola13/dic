malpractice: /ˌmælˈpræktɪs/ | n. |渎职；玩忽职守；医疗事故|Improper, illegal, or negligent professional activity or treatment, especially by a medical practitioner, lawyer, or public official.|The doctor was sued for malpractice after the patient died during a routine operation.  这名医生在病人死于常规手术后被起诉医疗事故。| 近义词：negligence, misconduct, dereliction of duty | 反义词: due diligence, proper conduct | 词性: noun

malpractice: /ˌmælˈpræktɪs/ | n. |(尤指医生、律师等)不当行为，失职|Failure to meet the standard of care or conduct prescribed by a profession or official position.|The lawyer was accused of malpractice for failing to file the necessary paperwork on time.  该律师因未能按时提交必要的文件而被指控玩忽职守。| 近义词：misconduct, negligence | 反义词: due diligence | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From mal- (bad, wrong) + practice.  "Mal-" comes from Latin "malus" (bad). "Practice" comes from Old French "practique"  and ultimately from Latin "practica" (business, practice).
