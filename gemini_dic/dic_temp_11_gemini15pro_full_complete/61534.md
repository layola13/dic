embryonated:/ˈɛmbriəneɪtɪd/| adj.|已胚化的，含有胚胎的|Containing an embryo; fertilized and developing.|例句：The embryonated chicken eggs were carefully incubated. 已胚化的鸡蛋被小心地孵化。|例句: Scientists studied the embryonated eggs to understand early development. 科学家们研究了已胚化的卵以了解早期发育。|近义词：fertilized, developing|反义词:unfertilized, undeveloped|词性:形容词
Etymology->
From embryo + -ate + -ed.  "Embryo" ultimately comes from the Greek "embryon," meaning "something swollen," referring to a fetus in the womb. "-ate" is a suffix used to form verbs meaning "to make or become," and "-ed" is the past participle suffix.
