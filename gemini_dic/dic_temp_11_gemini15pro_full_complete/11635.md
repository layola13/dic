armed:/ɑːrmd/ | adj. | 武装的；有扶手的；有防卫器官的 | furnished with weapons; having arms or arm-like parts | The police confronted the armed robber. 警察与武装抢劫犯对峙。|  equipped, fortified, weaponed | unarmed, defenseless | adjective


armed:/ɑːrmd/ | v. | 武装；配备 | past tense and past participle of "arm" meaning to furnish with weapons |  The security guards were armed with tasers. 保安人员配备了泰瑟枪。| equip, fortify, furnish | disarm, dismantle | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *armed, *armyd, from Anglo-Norman armer, armeer, from Old French armer, from Late Latin armāre, present active infinitive of armō (“I arm, equip with weapons”), from Latin arma (“weapons, arms, tools, implements”).
