elenchus:/ɪˈlɛŋkəs/
noun
逻辑辩驳；反驳；苏格拉底式诘问法
a method of questioning someone to test the consistency and completeness of their position; a logical refutation.
Socrates used the elenchus to expose contradictions in his interlocutors' beliefs. 苏格拉底用辩驳法揭露对话者信念中的矛盾。
The philosopher employed elenchus to challenge the student's assumptions. 哲学家用辩驳法来挑战学生的假设。
近义词:refutation, cross-examination, interrogation
反义词:confirmation, affirmation
词性:n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἐλεγχος (elenkhos, “argument of disproof or refutation, cross-examination, testing”).
