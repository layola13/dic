fully:/ˈfʊli/| adv.|完全地，彻底地，充分地|Completely or entirely; to the fullest extent.|She fully understood the implications of her decision. 她完全理解她决定的含义。|completely, entirely, totally, utterly|partially, incompletely, slightly|adverb
Etymology: From Middle English *fulli*, from Old English *fullice*, from *full* (full) + *-lice* (suffix forming adverbs of manner).  The word "full" ultimately comes from Proto-Germanic *fullaz* and Proto-Indo-European *pl̥nós*.
