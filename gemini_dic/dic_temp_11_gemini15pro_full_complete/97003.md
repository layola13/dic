infarct:/ˈɪnfɑːrkt/ (US), /ˈɪnfɑːkt/ (UK)| n. |梗塞; 梗死|An area of tissue death caused by obstruction of the circulation to the area, as by a blood clot. |The patient suffered a myocardial infarct.  病人患有心肌梗塞。|Synonyms: infarction, blockage, occlusion|Antonyms: perfusion, flow|noun

infarction:/ɪnˈfɑːrkʃən/ (US), /ɪnˈfɑːkʃən/ (UK)| n. |梗塞; 梗死|The obstruction of a blood vessel, typically by a blood clot or an atheroma, preventing blood from reaching the tissues normally supplied by the vessel and causing local death of the tissues. |Pulmonary infarction is a rare but serious complication of pulmonary embolism. 肺梗塞是肺栓塞的一种罕见但严重的并发症。|Synonyms: infarct, blockage, occlusion|Antonyms: perfusion, flow|noun

infarce:/ɪnˈfɑːrs/ (US), /ɪnˈfɑːs/ (UK) | vt. | 塞入；填入; 填充|To stuff tightly; cram. (Archaic and rare usage.  In modern English, "infarct" and "infarction" are the common forms relating to medical contexts.) |He infarced the suitcase with all his clothes. 他把所有的衣服都塞进了手提箱。|Synonyms: stuff, cram, pack, fill|Antonyms: empty, unpack|verb
