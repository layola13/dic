gothism: /ˈɡɒθɪsɪzəm/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːθɪsɪzəm/ (US) | n. | 哥特主义；哥特风格 | A style in architecture prevalent in western Europe in the 12th–16th centuries, characterized by pointed arches, rib vaults, and flying buttresses, together with elaborate tracery and ornamentation. |  The cathedral is a stunning example of Gothicism. 这座大教堂是哥特式建筑的绝佳典范。| 近义词: Gothic architecture, Gothic style | 反义词: Classicism, Romanesque architecture | 词性: noun

gothism: /ˈɡɒθɪsɪzəm/ (UK) /ˈɡɑːθɪsɪzəm/ (US) | n. |  哥特文化; 哥特亚文化 | A subculture characterized by an emphasis on dark aesthetics, romanticism, and often a fascination with death, the supernatural, or the macabre. |  Her clothing and makeup reflected her interest in Gothicism. 她的服装和妆容反映了她对哥特文化的兴趣。| 近义词: Goth subculture, dark romanticism | 反义词: Mainstream culture | 词性: noun
