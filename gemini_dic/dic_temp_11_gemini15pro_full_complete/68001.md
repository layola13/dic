factitiously:/fækˈtɪʃəsli/
adverb
人为地；不自然地；虚假地
In a factitious manner; artificially.  Done in a manner that is not natural or genuine.
He coughed factitiously, hoping to draw attention to himself. 他假咳嗽，希望能引起别人的注意。
He created a factitious demand for the product by spreading rumors about its scarcity. 他通过散布产品稀缺的谣言，人为地制造了需求。
近义词: artificially, falsely, spuriously, contrivedly, feignedly
反义词: genuinely, naturally, sincerely, authentically
词性: adverb

factitious:/fækˈtɪʃəs/
adjective
人为的；不自然的；虚假的
Not natural or genuine; produced artificially.  Made; formed; done; in a manner that is not natural or genuine
The crisis was factitious and manufactured to create fear. 这场危机是人为制造的，目的是制造恐慌。
Her enthusiasm seemed rather factitious. 她的热情似乎相当虚假。
近义词: artificial, false, spurious, contrived, feigned
反义词: genuine, natural, sincere, authentic
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *facticius*, from factus (past participle of facere, "to make, to do").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁-*, "to put, to place."
