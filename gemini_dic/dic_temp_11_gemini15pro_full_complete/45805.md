custodianships:/kʌˈstoʊdiənʃɪps/| n. |监护权；保管权；托管；保管|The state of being responsible for and protecting something.  The legal right and responsibility to take care of someone, especially a child, and their property.|The museum has custodianship of a vast collection of artifacts.  博物馆负责保管大量的文物。|近义词： guardianship, care, safekeeping, charge, keeping, protection |反义词: neglect, abandonment|noun
