corporatism: /ˈkɔːrpərətɪzəm/ /ˈkɔːrpərətɪˌzəm/ | n. |社团主义，统制主义|A political ideology or system in which power is exercised through large organized groups (corporations) representing different sectors of society (such as business, labor, and agriculture), rather than through individual citizens or political parties.  These groups negotiate with the state to determine economic and social policy.|Italy under Mussolini was an example of corporatism. 墨索里尼统治下的意大利是社团主义的一个例子。

|Synonyms: syndicalism, statism, collectivism|Antonyms: individualism, liberalism, laissez-faire|noun
