discriminate:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪt/ /dɪˈskrɪmɪnət/ | v. | 区别，辨别；歧视 | to recognize a distinction; differentiate.  To treat a person or group unfairly based on prejudice. |  She can discriminate between a good wine and a cheap one. 她能分辨出好酒和廉价酒。| synonyms: differentiate, distinguish, separate, discern | antonyms: confuse, confound, mix, blend | verb

discriminate:/dɪˈskrɪmɪnɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 有识别力的；有偏见的 | having or showing refined taste or good judgment. Making or showing an unfair or prejudicial distinction between different categories of people or things. | He has a discriminating palate for wine. 他对葡萄酒有很高的鉴赏力。| synonyms: discerning, selective, judicious, prejudiced, biased | antonyms: undiscriminating, indiscriminate, unselective, impartial, unbiased | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin discriminare "to separate, divide, distinguish," from discrimen "interval, difference," from discernere "to separate, distinguish."
