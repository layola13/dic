Charon: /ˈkɛərən/ | n. | 卡戎;冥河渡神 | In Greek mythology, Charon is the ferryman of Hades who carries souls of the newly deceased across the rivers Styx and Acheron that divided the world of the living from the world of the dead. | Charon is often depicted as a sullen, cloaked old man. 卡戎通常被描绘成一个阴沉、披着斗篷的老人。 |  Example: In Dante's Inferno, Charon ferries the damned across the river Acheron. 在但丁的《神曲·地狱篇》中，卡戎渡送被诅咒的人们穿过阿cheron河。| Synonym: ferryman, boatman | Antonym: N/A | Noun
