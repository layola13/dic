long-sightedness: /ˌlɒŋˈsaɪtɪdnəs/ (British) /ˌlɔːŋˈsaɪtɪdnəs/ (American)| n.|远视|The inability to see close objects clearly. It occurs when the eye is too short or the cornea is not curved enough, causing light to focus behind the retina instead of on it.|She wears glasses to correct her long-sightedness.|近义词: hyperopia, hypermetropia|反义词: short-sightedness, myopia|词性:noun

long-sighted: /ˌlɒŋˈsaɪtɪd/ (British) /ˌlɔːŋˈsaɪtɪd/ (American)| adj.|有远见的|Having foresight or planning for the future.|His long-sighted policies benefited the company in the long run.|近义词: far-sighted, visionary|反义词: short-sighted|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "long" + "sight" + "-ed" (suffix forming adjectives) + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns).  "Long sight" referred to the ability to see distant objects clearly, which was later understood to be a condition where close objects appear blurry.
