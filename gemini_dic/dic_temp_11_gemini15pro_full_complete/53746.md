discolour:/dɪsˈkʌlər/ /dɪsˈkʌlɚ/| v. |使变色，褪色|To change the colour of something, usually to a less attractive colour, often by making it fade or become stained.|The sunlight discoloured the curtains. 阳光使窗帘褪色了。|近义词：fade, stain, bleach, tarnish|反义词: brighten, colour, dye|词性:动词

discolour:/dɪsˈkʌlər/ /dɪsˈkʌlɚ/| n. |变色，褪色|A change in the colour of something, usually to a less attractive colour.|The discolouration of the old photograph made it difficult to see the details. 旧照片的褪色使得细节难以辨认。|近义词：fading, staining, bleaching, tarnishing|反义词: brightening, colouring, dyeing|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + colour.  The prefix "dis-" signifies the opposite or absence of the base word "colour".  It implies a change from the original or natural colour, usually to something less desirable.
