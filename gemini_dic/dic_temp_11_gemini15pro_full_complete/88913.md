hold-off:/ˈhoʊld ɒf/ (US) /ˈhəʊld ɒf/ (UK)| n. |延误，推迟，暂停|A delay or postponement of an action or decision.|The general called for a hold-off on the attack until reinforcements arrived. 将军要求推迟进攻，直到援军到达。|The rain caused a hold-off in the baseball game.  下雨导致棒球比赛暂停。|近义词：delay, postponement, suspension, respite|反义词：continuation, commencement, start|词性:名词

hold off:/hoʊld ˈɔːf/ (US) /həʊld ˈɒf/ (UK)| v. |推迟，抵挡，抵抗|To delay, postpone, or resist something.|The company decided to hold off on hiring new employees until the next quarter. 公司决定推迟到下个季度再招聘新员工。|The defenders held off the enemy attack for several hours. 防守者抵挡了敌人的进攻数小时。|近义词：delay, postpone, resist, withstand, fend off|反义词：proceed, continue, advance, surrender|词性:动词

词源:  "Hold" comes from Old English "haldan," meaning "to grasp, keep, possess." "Off" comes from Old English "of," indicating separation or distance. The combination "hold off" originally referred to keeping something at a distance, and later evolved to mean delaying or postponing something.
