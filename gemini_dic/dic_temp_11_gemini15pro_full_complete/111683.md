locked-up:/ˌlɑːkt ˈʌp/ | adj. | 被锁住的；监禁的；关闭的；被抑制的 | Confined, especially in prison; also, unavailable for use or tightly secured. |  The stolen jewels were locked up in a safe. 被盗的珠宝被锁在保险箱里。

The evidence was locked up in a secure facility. 证据被锁在一个安全设施里。 | 近义词：imprisoned, incarcerated, confined, secured, closed | 反义词：free, released, open, available | 词性:形容词

locked-up:/ˌlɑːkt ˈʌp/ | n. | 监狱；监禁 |  A prison or jail. | He spent years in the locked-up. 他在监狱里待了很多年。

The movie featured a dramatic escape from the locked-up.  这部电影讲述了一个从监狱里戏剧性越狱的故事。 | 近义词：jail, prison, penitentiary | 反义词：freedom, liberty | 词性:名词


词源:  From the past participle of "lock up,"  a phrasal verb combining "lock" (from Old English *loc, a fastening device) and "up" (from Old English up, meaning upwards, higher). The original sense was to fasten something securely, often with a lock, and the meaning extended to confinement in prison.
