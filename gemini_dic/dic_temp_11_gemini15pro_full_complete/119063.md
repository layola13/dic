metonymy:/məˈtɒnɪmi/ /məˈtɑːnɪmi/| n.|转喻|A figure of speech in which a thing or concept is referred to by the name of something closely associated with that thing or concept.|The White House declined to comment. (White House refers to the US President and his administration)|synonyms: substitution, name change, transnomination|antonyms: literalism|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *metonymia*, from Ancient Greek μετωνυμία (metōnumía, “change of name”), from μετά (metá, “change”) + ὄνομα (ónoma, “name”).
