laities:/ˈleɪɪtiz/ | n. |俗人，门外汉|People who are not members of a particular profession or group, especially of the clergy. |例句：The intricacies of the legal system can be confusing to the laity. 法律体系的复杂性可能会让外行人感到困惑。

例句：The medical procedure was explained in terms the laity could understand.  医疗程序用外行人能理解的术语进行了解释。 |近义词：laypeople, nonprofessionals, amateurs |反义词：professionals, experts, clergy |词性: 名词

词源: From Old French *laieté, from Late Latin laicitas, from Latin laicus "of the people," from Greek laikos "of the people, pertaining to the people," from laos "the people."
