arguments:/ˈɑːrɡjəmənts/ | n. | 争论，辩论；论据，理由 | Reasons given in support of an idea, action or theory; a discussion involving differing points of view. |  We had a heated argument about politics. 我们就政治问题进行了激烈的争论。 | synonyms: dispute, disagreement, debate, quarrel, row, fight, contention, controversy, altercation, wrangle | antonyms: agreement, harmony, accord, concord, compromise | noun

arguments:/ˈɑːrɡjəmənts/ | n. | 参数 | A value that is passed to a function or program when it is called or executed. | The function takes two arguments: a string and an integer.  这个函数接受两个参数：一个字符串和一个整数。| synonyms: parameter, value, input | antonyms: output, result | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English argument, from Old French argument, from Latin argumentum (“reasoning, proof, argument, subject, contents”), from arguere (“to make clear, prove, declare, accuse”).
