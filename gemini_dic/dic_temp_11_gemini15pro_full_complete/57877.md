dribblet:/ˈdrɪblɪt/| n.|少量，点滴，细流|A small quantity of something, typically falling or flowing in drops.|The tap was dripping, and a dribblet of water ran down the side of the sink. 水龙头在滴水，少量的水顺着水槽边流了下来。|Example:  A dribblet of blood trickled from the wound. 一滴滴血从伤口流出。

Example:  He earned a dribblet of income from freelance work. 他从自由职业中赚取了一点收入。|Synonyms: trickle, drop, bit, trace|Antonyms: flood, torrent, deluge|Noun

dribble:/ˈdrɪbl/| v.|滴下，细流，运球|To fall or flow in small drops; to move a ball by repeated slight bounces or kicks.|Rain dribbled down the windowpane. 雨水顺着窗玻璃滴落下来。

Example: The basketball player dribbled the ball down the court. 篮球运动员运球过场。|Synonyms: trickle, drip, leak (for falling/flowing); bounce, tap (for ball handling)|Antonyms: gush, pour, stream (for falling/flowing)|Verb
