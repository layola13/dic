cabs:/kæbz/
n.
出租车；计程车（cab的复数形式）
Taxis or vehicles for hire.
The cabs lined up outside the hotel.  出租车在酒店外排成一排。
There were no cabs available at that late hour.  那么晚了，没有出租车可用。
synonyms: taxis, hackney carriages
antonyms: private cars, personal vehicles
noun


cab:/kæb/
n.
出租车；驾驶室；计程车
A taxi; the driver's compartment of a vehicle.
I took a cab to the airport. 我乘出租车去了机场。
The truck driver sat in his cab. 卡车司机坐在他的驾驶室里。
synonyms: taxi, hackney, carriage
antonyms: private car, personal vehicle
noun

cab:/kæb/
v.
乘出租车
To travel by taxi.
We cabbed it to the restaurant. 我们乘出租车去了餐馆。
They decided to cab home after the concert.  音乐会结束后，他们决定打车回家。
synonyms: take a taxi, hail a cab
antonyms: walk, drive, cycle
verb


ETYMOLOGY->
Shortened form of cabriolet, from French cabriolet, diminutive of cabriolet "light, two-wheeled, one-horse carriage," from cabrioler "to leap, caper," from Italian capriolare "to leap," from Latin capra "goat."  The sense evolution is from the light carriage's bouncing motion to any carriage for hire.
