dwindling:/ˈdwɪndəlɪŋ/
adjective
逐渐减少的，逐渐变小的
becoming steadily less; shrinking
The town's dwindling population is a cause for concern.  这个城镇不断减少的人口令人担忧。
The company's dwindling profits led to layoffs.  公司不断减少的利润导致了裁员。
synonyms: decreasing, diminishing, shrinking, declining, contracting
antonyms: increasing, growing, expanding, rising, burgeoning
词性: adjective


dwindle:/ˈdwɪndl/
verb
逐渐减少，逐渐变小
to become steadily less; to shrink
The water supply dwindled during the drought.  干旱期间，供水逐渐减少。
His enthusiasm for the project dwindled as time went on.  随着时间的推移，他对这个项目的热情逐渐减弱。
synonyms: decrease, diminish, shrink, decline, contract
antonyms: increase, grow, expand, rise, burgeon
词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dwinen, from Old English *dwīnan (“to waste away, vanish”), from Proto-Germanic *dwīnaną (“to disappear, fade, vanish”), from Proto-Indo-European *dweyh₁- (“to disappear, vanish”). Cognate with Old Saxon dwīnan (“to vanish”), Old Frisian dwīna (“to disappear”), Old High German dwīnan (“to fade, languish, pine”), Old Norse dvīna (“to dwindle, pine away”), Gothic 𝚍𝚠𝚎𝚒𝚗𝚊𝚗 (dweinans, “to die, perish”). Related also to Old English dwǣs (“foolish”).
