desolation:/ˌdesəˈleɪʃn/ | n. | 荒凉，凄凉，孤寂 | A state of complete emptiness or destruction, often causing great sadness. | The earthquake left a scene of utter desolation. 地震过后，一片 utter desolation。| synonyms: barrenness, bleakness, devastation, ruin, wasteland | antonyms: comfort, hope, joy, prosperity, abundance | noun

desolate:/ˈdesələt/ | adj. | 荒凉的，凄凉的，孤寂的 | Empty and without people, making you feel sad or lonely. |  The house stood desolate and abandoned. 这房子荒凉破败，无人居住。| synonyms: barren, bleak, deserted, forlorn, empty | antonyms: cheerful, comforting, crowded, happy, populated | adjective

desolate:/ˈdesəleɪt/ | v. | 使荒芜，使孤寂 | Make a place empty and without people, or make someone feel very sad and lonely. | The war desolated the countryside. 战争使农村变得荒芜。| synonyms: devastate, ravage, ruin, destroy, empty | antonyms: build, comfort, construct, create, populate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dēsōlātus*, past participle of dēsōlāre (“to abandon, forsake, make lonely, lay waste”), from dē- (“un-, de-”) + sōlāre (“to make alone”), from sōlus (“alone”).
