dandier:/ˈdændɪər/| adj.|更衣冠楚楚的，更纨绔子弟的|More dandy; more foppish. |He looked dandier than usual in his new suit. 他穿着新西装，看起来比平时更衣冠楚楚。|Near Synonyms: more stylish, more fashionable, more elegant, more dapper|Antonyms: shabbier, dowdier, less stylish|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From dandy + -er (comparative suffix).  "Dandy" itself originated in the late 18th century, possibly from a Scottish nickname, and referred to a man unduly devoted to style and fashion.
