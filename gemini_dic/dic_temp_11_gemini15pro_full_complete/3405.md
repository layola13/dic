aflutter:/əˈflʌtər/| adj.|激动的，兴奋的，紧张不安的|In a state of nervous excitement or agitation.|例句：She was all aflutter about her upcoming wedding. 她对即将到来的婚礼感到非常兴奋。|例句：The news of the lottery win sent the whole town aflutter. 彩票中奖的消息让全镇的人都激动不已。|近义词：excited, nervous, agitated, thrilled|反义词：calm, composed, tranquil|词性:形容词
Etymology:  From a- + flutter.  The prefix "a-" in this case indicates a state of being.  "Flutter" itself comes from Old Norse *flotra, meaning "to float, drift." The sense evolution likely moved from the physical fluttering of something (like wings) to the metaphorical fluttering of emotions.
