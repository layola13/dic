knotted:/ˈnɑːtɪd/ /ˈnɒtɪd/| adj.|打结的，纠结的|Tied in a knot or knots; tangled.  Also, showing the effects of worry or tension.|The rope was knotted tightly. 绳子紧紧地打结了。|tangled, intertwined, tied|smooth, untied|adjective

knotted:/ˈnɑːtɪd/ /ˈnɒtɪd/| v.|打结，纠结|Past participle of "knot". To tie something in a knot.|She knotted the scarf around her neck.  她把围巾在脖子上打了个结。|tie, fasten, bind|untie, loosen|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cnotta (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *knuttan, from Proto-Indo-European *genw- (“to knot, tie”).  Cognate with Old Norse knútr, Dutch knot, German Knoten, Gothic 𝑘𝑛𝑢𝑡𝑠.
