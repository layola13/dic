credulously:/ˈkrɛdʒələsli/| adv.|轻信地；易受骗地|Showing a readiness to believe things, even if they seem unlikely or improbable.  Accepting something without proper evidence or scrutiny.|He credulously accepted the stranger's story about being a prince. 他轻信了陌生人自称是王子的故事。|近义词：naively, gullibly, unsuspectingly|反义词：skeptically, cynically, suspiciously|词性:副词
Etymology: From Latin "credulus" (believing, trusting) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  "Credulus" itself comes from "credere" (to believe).
