microchip:/ˈmaɪkroʊtʃɪp/| n. |微芯片，集成电路|A tiny wafer of semiconducting material used to make integrated circuits.|例句：The microchip revolutionized the electronics industry. 微芯片彻底改变了电子工业。|例句：Modern computers rely on increasingly powerful microchips. 现代计算机依赖于功能日益强大的微芯片。|近义词：integrated circuit, chip|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "discrete component")|词性:noun

Etymology:
From micro- + chip.  "Chip," in the sense of a small piece of something, dates back to the 15th century.  The use of "chip" to refer to a piece of semiconductor material began in the mid-20th century.
