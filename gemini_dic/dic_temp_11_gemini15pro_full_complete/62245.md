/ˈɛnˌkrɪptɪd/ | adj. | 加密的，编码的 | Converted into code or cipher, especially to prevent unauthorized access.  | Example: The encrypted message was impossible to decipher without the key.  加密的消息如果没有密钥就无法解密。 | Example:  All sensitive data transmitted over the network is encrypted. 所有通过网络传输的敏感数据都被加密。 | Synonyms: coded, ciphered, scrambled | Antonyms: decrypted, deciphered, decoded | adjective

/ɪnˈkrɪpt/ | vt. | 加密，编码 | Convert (information or data) into a code, especially to prevent unauthorized access. | Example:  The software encrypts all data before storing it on the disk.  该软件在将所有数据存储到磁盘之前对其进行加密。 | Example:  They encrypted the message to ensure its confidentiality. 他们对信息进行了加密以确保其机密性。 | Synonyms: code, cipher, scramble | Antonyms: decrypt, decipher, decode | verb

/ɪnˈkrɪpʃən/ | n. | 加密，编码 | The process of converting information or data into a code, especially to prevent unauthorized access. | Example: Encryption is essential for protecting sensitive information online.  在线保护敏感信息，加密至关重要。 | Example: The strength of the encryption determines how secure the data is.  加密的强度决定了数据的安全性。 | Synonyms: coding, ciphering, scrambling | Antonyms: decryption, deciphering, decoding | noun
