cnida:/ˈnaɪdə/ | n. | 刺细胞 | A stinging cell, characteristic of cnidarians (e.g., jellyfish, corals, and sea anemones). These cells contain nematocysts, which are explosive organelles used for prey capture and defense. |  Cnidarians use their cnida to paralyze their prey. 刺胞动物用它们的刺细胞来麻痹猎物。|Synonyms: nematocyst (often used interchangeably, but technically the nematocyst is the organelle within the cnida), stinging cell| Antonyms: None (there isn't a direct opposite of a stinging cell)| Noun
