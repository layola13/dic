imbargo:/ɪmˈbɑːrɡoʊ/ (US), /ɪmˈbɑːɡəʊ/ (UK)| n. vt.|禁运，禁止|An official ban on trade or other commercial activity with a particular country.|The US government placed an embargo on trade with Cuba. 美国政府对古巴实行贸易禁运。|Synonyms: ban, prohibition, restriction, sanction|Antonyms: allowance, permission|noun, verb

imbargo:/ɪmˈbɑːrɡoʊ/ (US), /ɪmˈbɑːɡəʊ/ (UK)| vt.|禁止，阻止|To officially ban or prohibit something.|The government has embargoed the export of certain types of weapons. 政府禁止出口某些类型的武器。|Synonyms: prohibit, forbid, ban, restrict|Antonyms: permit, allow|verb

Simple Present:
The government embargoes the sale of arms to certain countries. 政府禁止向某些国家出售武器。

They sometimes embargo imports from countries with poor human rights records.  他们有时禁止从人权记录较差的国家进口商品。


Present Continuous:
The UN is currently embargoing all trade with that nation due to the conflict.  由于冲突，联合国目前正在禁止与该国的所有贸易。

Several countries are embargoing the export of key technologies.  几个国家正在禁止关键技术出口。


Present Perfect:
The country has embargoed all imports from its neighbor for several years.  该国已经禁止从邻国进口所有商品好几年了。

They have embargoed the sale of ivory to protect elephants. 他们已经禁止象牙销售以保护大象。


Present Perfect Continuous:

Simple Past:
The US embargoed trade with Japan before World War II.  美国在二战前对日本实行贸易禁运。

Last year, they embargoed the export of certain minerals. 去年，他们禁止出口某些矿物。

Past Perfect:
