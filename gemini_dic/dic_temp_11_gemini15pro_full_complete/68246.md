fairytale:/ˈfeəriˌteɪl/ (US), /ˈfɛːriˌteɪl/ (UK)| n. adj.|童话故事；童话般的；不真实的|A children's story about magical and imaginary beings and lands.  Can also be used figuratively to describe something unrealistically perfect or charming.|例句：She used to read fairytales to her children every night. 她过去每晚都给孩子们读童话故事。|近义词：story, fable, myth, legend, fantasy|反义词：reality, truth|词性:名词, 形容词

fairytale:/ˈfeəriˌteɪl/ (US), /ˈfɛːriˌteɪl/ (UK)| adj.|童话般的；不真实的|Resembling a fairytale in being magical, idealized, or improbable.|例句：Their wedding was a fairytale romance. 他们的婚礼就像童话般浪漫。|近义词：magical, enchanting, dreamlike, unreal, whimsical|反义词：realistic, mundane, ordinary|词性:形容词

词源： From *fairy tale*, from *fairy* + *tale*.  The earliest recorded usage is from 1697.  The word originally referred to a tale about fairies, but the meaning broadened over time to encompass any fantastical children's story.
