mendicant:/ˈmendɪkənt/| n. adj.|乞丐，乞讨的|A beggar; someone who lives by asking for money or food. Also used as an adjective to describe someone who begs or something related to begging.|例句：The mendicant sat on the corner, holding out a cup. 乞丐坐在街角，手里拿着一个杯子。|例句：He lived a mendicant lifestyle, travelling from town to town. 他过着乞讨的生活，从一个城镇到另一个城镇。|近义词：beggar, pauper, supplicant|反义词：philanthropist, benefactor|词性: noun, adjective


词源:
From Old French *mendicant*, present participle of *mendicanter* "to beg," from Latin *mendicare* "to beg," from *mendicus* "beggar," of uncertain origin.
