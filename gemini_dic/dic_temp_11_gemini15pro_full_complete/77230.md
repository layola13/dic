gastrodisc: /ˈɡæstrəʊdɪsk/ | n. | 胃盘，腹盘 | A flattened, disc-shaped or bowl-shaped anatomical structure relating to the stomach or abdomen of certain invertebrates. |  Many jellyfish have a prominent gastrodisc. 许多水母都有一个突出的胃盘。| synonym:  belly, abdomen (in a general sense) | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps dorsal surface could be considered an opposite in terms of location) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *gastro-* (Greek γαστήρ, gastēr, "belly, stomach") + *disc* (Latin discus, from Greek δίσκος, dískos, "disc, quoit").
