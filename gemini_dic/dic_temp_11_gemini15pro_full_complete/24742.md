broodily:/ˈbruːdɪli/| adv.|闷闷不乐地，沉思地|In a manner showing deep unhappiness or thoughtfulness, often with resentment or anger.|She stared broodily out the window, thinking about what she had lost.|近义词：sullenly, morosely, gloomily, thoughtfully, pensively|反义词：cheerfully, happily, brightly|词性:副词

词源: From the verb "brood" + "-ly" (adverbial suffix). "Brood" comes from Old English "brōd" meaning "a breeding, the young of birds hatched at once," later developing the sense of "to sit on eggs," and then figuratively "to dwell on something persistently."
