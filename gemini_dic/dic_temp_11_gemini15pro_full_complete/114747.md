ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *malodorous*, from Latin *malōdorōsus* "ill-smelling," from *mal-* "bad" + *odor* "smell" + *-ōsus*.  The ending *-ness* transforms the adjective *malodorous* into a noun indicating the state or quality of being malodorous.  The plural *malodorousnesses* refers to multiple instances or sources of this quality.
