beadroll:/ˈbiːdrəʊl/ (US) /ˈbiːdˌroʊl/| n. |念珠；祈祷时所念的圣名录；一长串名字（或物品）|A list of names, especially of people to be prayed for; a long list of anything.|The priest read from the beadroll, praying for the souls of the departed.|Synonyms:  list, roster, catalog, inventory, register|Antonyms:  None (lack of a list isn't a direct antonym)|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bede rolle*, from Old English *bed* (prayer) + *rolle* (roll, list).  The original sense referred to a roll or list of prayers or names to be prayed for.
