hocus-pocus:/ˈhoʊkəs ˈpoʊkəs/ | n.  v. | 花招，戏法；(变戏法的)咒语 |  Meaningless words used by magicians during a performance. Also used to describe deceptive or misleading talk or actions. | The salesman used a lot of hocus-pocus to convince us to buy the car. 销售员用了很多花言巧语才说服我们买了那辆车。 |  trickery, sleight of hand, magic, conjuring | deception, honesty | noun, verb

hocus-pocused:/ˌhoʊkəs ˈpoʊkəst/ | v. |  施用魔法；欺骗；蒙蔽 | Past tense and past participle of "hocus-pocus," meaning to trick or deceive someone. | We were completely hocus-pocused by the illusionist's performance. 我们完全被魔术师的表演给骗了。 | tricked, deceived, hoodwinked, bamboozled |  enlightened, informed | verb
