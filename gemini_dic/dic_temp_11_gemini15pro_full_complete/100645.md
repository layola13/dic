intolerants:/ɪnˈtɑːlərənts/ | n. |不宽容的人；偏执者| People who are unwilling to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from their own. |  The intolerants protested loudly against the new law.  不宽容的人大声抗议新法律。|近义词：bigots, fanatics, prejudiced people|反义词: tolerant people, liberals, open-minded people | noun

intolerant:/ɪnˈtɑːlərənt/ | adj. |不宽容的；偏执的| Not willing to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own. | She is intolerant of any opinion that differs from her own. 她不容忍任何与她不同的观点。|近义词：bigoted, biased, prejudiced|反义词: tolerant, accepting, open-minded | adjective


词源:

From Late Latin intolerans, present participle of intolerare (“to not endure, to be intolerant of”), from in- (“not”) + tolerare (“to bear, endure”).
