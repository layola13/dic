nervously:/ˈnɜːrvəsli/ (US) /ˈnɜːvəsli/ (UK)| adv.|紧张地，不安地|In a way that shows you are worried or frightened.|She laughed nervously. 她紧张地笑了笑。|近义词：anxiously, apprehensively, uneasily|反义词：calmly, confidently|词性:副词

Etymology:
From nerve + -ous + -ly.  "Nerve" comes from Latin "nervus" meaning "sinew, tendon, nerve." The "-ous" suffix forms adjectives meaning "full of" or "possessing the qualities of."  The "-ly" suffix forms adverbs from adjectives.
