headroom:/ˈhɛdruːm/| n. |净空；余地；安全裕度|The space between the top of a person's head and the ceiling of a car, room, etc., or between the top of a vehicle and the arch of a bridge, etc.  The amount of available capacity, especially financial capacity, in excess of what is already being used. |例句：This car has plenty of headroom. 这辆车头部空间很宽敞。

例句：The company has little financial headroom for new investments. 这家公司几乎没有新的投资资金余地。|近义词：clearance, leeway, margin, space|反义词: confinement, restriction|词性:noun

headrooms:/ˈhɛdruːmz/ | n. |净空（headroom的复数形式）|Plural form of headroom, referring to multiple instances of clearance or capacity. |例句：The architects carefully considered the headrooms in each room of the building. 建筑师仔细考虑了大楼每个房间的净空高度。

例句：Different aircraft have different headrooms requirements in hangars. 不同的飞机在机库中有不同的净空要求。|近义词：clearances, leeways, margins, spaces|反义词: confinements, restrictions|词性:noun
