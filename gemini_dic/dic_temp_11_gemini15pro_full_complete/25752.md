burble:/ˈbɜːrbl/ (US), /ˈbʌbl/ (UK)| v. n.|发出持续的低沉的声音；汩汩地流动；喋喋不休地说；低语；|To make a continuous murmuring sound like water flowing over pebbles.  To talk at length in a rambling or inconsequential way.|The brook burbled down the mountainside. 小溪沿着山坡汩汩流下。


She burbled on about her trip to Paris. 她喋喋不休地谈论她的巴黎之旅。|近义词：babble, gurgle, murmur, ramble|反义词：be quiet, be silent|词性：动词，名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *burblen, frequentative of burbeln (“to bubble”), from Old French borboler, burbuler (“to bubble”), from Latin *burbulare, frequentative of bullire (“to bubble, boil”).
