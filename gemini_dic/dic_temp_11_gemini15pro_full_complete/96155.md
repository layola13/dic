indentures:/ɪnˈdɛntʃərz/| n.|契约，合同，正式协议|A formal legal agreement, contract, or deed, typically one relating to debt, property, or an apprenticeship, often having serrated edges.|The indentures bound the apprentice to his master for seven years.|Synonyms: contract, agreement, deed, covenant, compact|Antonyms: disagreement, breach, annulment|noun


indenture:/ɪnˈdɛntʃər/| n. vt.|契约，合同；以契约约束|A formal legal agreement, contract, or deed; bind someone by an indenture.|The young man was indentured to a carpenter.|Synonyms: contract, agreement, deed, covenant, compact; bind, obligate|Antonyms: disagreement, breach, annulment; release, free|noun, verb
