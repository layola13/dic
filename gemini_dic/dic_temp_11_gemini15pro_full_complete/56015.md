do-justice:/duː ˈdʒʌstɪs/| v.|伸张正义；秉公处理；公平对待；恰当地评价；充分展现…的优点；|To treat fairly; to represent adequately, especially in a favorable way.|The photos don't do justice to the magnificent scenery. 这些照片未能充分展现壮丽的景色。|Synonyms: be fair to, treat fairly, give a fair hearing, give one's due, represent adequately, do right by |Antonyms: be unfair to, treat unfairly, misrepresent, shortchange|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *doen justice*, equivalent to *do* + *justice*. *Do* comes from Old English *dōn*, from Proto-Germanic *dōną*, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁-*. *Justice* comes from Old French *justice*, from Latin *iūstitia*, from *iūstus* ("just, righteous").
