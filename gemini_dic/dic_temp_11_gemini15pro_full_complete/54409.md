disincentivize:/ˌdɪsɪnˈsɛntɪvaɪz/
verb
使失去动力；打消积极性
To discourage someone from doing something, typically by removing an incentive or introducing a penalty.
The new tax laws disincentivize investment in small businesses. 新的税法抑制了对小型企业的投资。
近义词: deter, discourage, dissuade
反义词: incentivize, encourage, motivate
词性: verb

disincentivize:/ˌdɪsɪnˈsɛntɪvaɪz/
Etymology: dis- (not, opposite of) + incentivize (to provide with an incentive) From "incentive," which comes from Late Latin incentivus "inciting," from past participle stem of incendere "to kindle, set on fire," from in- "in" + candere "to shine."
词源: dis- (不，相反的) + incentivize (激励) 来自 "incentive"，它来源于晚期拉丁语 incentivus “煽动”，来自 incendere 的过去分词词干 “点燃，放火”，来自 in- “在...里面” + candere “发光”。
