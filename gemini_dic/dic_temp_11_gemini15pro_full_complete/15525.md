bag-of-bones:/ˌbæɡ əv ˈboʊnz/ | n. | 骨瘦如柴的人；皮包骨 |  A person or animal who is extremely thin, emaciated. | The stray cat was a real bag of bones. 那只流浪猫真是皮包骨。| synonyms:  skeleton, scarecrow, walking skeleton | antonyms:  chubby, plump, obese | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the literal image of a bag filled only with bones, emphasizing extreme thinness.  The phrase likely originated in the late 18th or early 19th century.
