edibilities:/ˌɛdɪˈbɪlɪtiz/
|n.|可食性；适口性|The edibilities of certain wild mushrooms are still debated.  某些野生蘑菇的可食性仍然存在争议。|
|英文解析:  The plural form of "edibility," referring to the quality of being fit to be eaten. It can refer to the suitability of various foods for consumption, considering factors like taste, texture, and safety.|
|例句：The edibilities of various plants were tested by the explorers. 探险家们测试了各种植物的可食性。|
|例句：Researchers are studying the edibilities of new protein sources. 研究人员正在研究新蛋白质来源的可食性。|
|近义词：palatability, consumability|
|反义词：inedibility, toxicity|
|词性:名词|

edibility:/ˌɛdɪˈbɪlɪti/
|n.|可食性；适口性|The edibility of this plant is questionable. 这种植物的可食性值得怀疑。|
|英文解析: The state or quality of being fit to be eaten.  This often relates to whether something is safe and palatable for consumption.|
|例句：The chef questioned the edibility of the slightly discolored fish. 厨师质疑那条略微变色的鱼的可食性。|
|例句：The edibility of wild berries should always be confirmed before consumption. 在食用野生浆果之前，应始终确认其可食性。|
|近义词：palatability, consumability|
|反义词：inedibility, toxicity|
|词性:名词|
