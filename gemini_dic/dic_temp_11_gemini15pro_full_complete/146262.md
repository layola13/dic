overfacile:/ˌoʊvərˈfæsɪl/ | adj. | 过于轻率的，过于简单的，肤浅的 | Too easily done or achieved; superficial.  Characterized by a ready flow of speech or writing that suggests a lack of serious thought or depth. | His overfacile solution to the complex problem was met with skepticism. 他对这个复杂问题过于轻率的解决方案遭到了质疑。| Synonyms: glib, shallow, superficial, simplistic | Antonyms: profound, thorough, complex, nuanced | word property: adjective

overfacile:/ˌoʊvərˈfæsɪl/ | adj. | 过于轻易的，过于流畅的 |  Performed or achieved too easily or readily; lacking depth or substance.  Especially used to describe speech or writing that is fluent but lacks depth of thought.  | The student's overfacile essay lacked any real analysis.  这个学生过于轻易完成的论文缺乏真正的分析。| Synonyms: slick, facile, effortless | Antonyms: labored, painstaking, profound | word property: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From "over-" (excessive) + "facile" (easy). "Facile" comes from French, ultimately from Latin "facilis" (easy), related to "facere" (to do, make).
