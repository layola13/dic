caesar:/ˈsiːzər/| n.|凯撒，皇帝，独裁者|A Roman general and statesman. Julius Caesar (100-44 BC) played a critical role in the events that led to the demise of the Roman Republic and the rise of the Roman Empire.  The title Caesar was later adopted by Roman Emperors. |Julius Caesar was assassinated on the Ides of March.  尤利乌斯·凯撒在三月十五日被暗杀。|Synonyms: emperor, ruler, dictator|Antonyms: subject, citizen|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Caesar*, the cognomen of Julius Caesar, of uncertain origin.  Possibly from Etruscan.
