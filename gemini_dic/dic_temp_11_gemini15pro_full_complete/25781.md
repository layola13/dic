bureau:/ˈbjʊəroʊ (US) / ˈbʊərəʊ (UK)| n.| 局；办事处；办公桌|A bureau is an office or department for transacting particular business.|The FBI is a federal bureau.  联邦调查局是一个联邦机构。|The tourism bureau provides information about local attractions. 旅游局提供当地景点的信息。|Synonyms: office, agency, department|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "field" in some contexts)|noun

bureau:/ˈbjʊəroʊ (US) / ˈbʊərəʊ (UK)| n.| (尤指旧式) 写字台；衣柜|A bureau is a writing desk, often with drawers and compartments.|She kept her important papers locked in her bureau. 她把重要文件锁在写字台里。|His grandmother's antique bureau was beautifully inlaid with mother-of-pearl. 他祖母的古董写字台镶嵌着美丽的珍珠母。|Synonyms: desk, writing table, chest of drawers|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *bureau*, originally meaning "baize" (a coarse woolen cloth), which was used to cover writing tables.  The meaning later shifted to the table itself, and then to an office.
