clericalism:/ˈklɛrɪkəlɪzəm/| n. |教权主义，神职人员统治|A belief in the authority of the clergy in all aspects of life, often involving the subordination of the laity to the clergy. The excessive influence or power of the clergy in secular or political affairs. |The rise of clericalism in the country led to widespread resentment among the people.| 近义词：priestcraft, hierarchism |反义词: secularism, anticlericalism |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cléricalisme*, from *clérical* (“clerical”) + *-isme* (“-ism”). *clérical* from Latin *clericus* (“cleric, clergyman”), from Ancient Greek *κληρικός* (*klērikos*, “of the clergy”), from *κλῆρος* (*klēros*, “lot, allotted portion, inheritance, clergy”).
