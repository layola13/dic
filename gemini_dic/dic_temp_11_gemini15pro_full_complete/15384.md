backwardation:/ˌbæk.wərˈdeɪ.ʃən/ | n. |  现货溢价；交割延期贴水 | A market situation where the price of a commodity for future delivery is lower than the spot price.  This typically occurs when there is immediate demand for the commodity. |  Example:  The current backwardation in the gold market suggests strong short-term demand. 黄金市场当前的现货溢价表明短期需求强劲。

Example:  Backwardation can create profitable opportunities for traders.  现货溢价可以为交易者创造获利机会。| 近义词:contango (in some contexts, as they represent opposite market conditions), premium | 反义词: contango, premium | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "backward" + "-ation" (suffix forming nouns of action or process).  The term implies a price structure that is "backward" from the normal expectation of futures prices being higher than spot prices.
