barbarian: /bɑːrˈbeəriən/ (US) /bɑːˈbɛːriən/ (UK)| n. adj.|野蛮人，未开化的人；野蛮的，粗鲁的|A person considered by those of another culture to be uncivilized, primitive, or cruel; savagely cruel or harsh.|例句：The barbarians sacked the city. 野蛮人洗劫了这座城市。|近义词：savage, vandal, brute, uncivilized|反义词: civilized, cultured, refined|词性: noun, adjective

barbarian: /bɑːrˈbeəriən/ (US) /bɑːˈbɛːriən/ (UK)| adj.|野蛮的，粗鲁的|Savagely cruel or harsh; uncivilized.|例句：The barbarian hordes swept across the land. 野蛮的游牧部落席卷了这片土地。|近义词：savage, brutal, cruel, uncivilized|反义词: civilized, humane, gentle, refined|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *barbarus*, from Greek *bárbaros* "foreign, non-Greek," originally "babbling," referring to the unintelligible speech of foreigners.  The word imitates the sound of perceived incoherent speech.
