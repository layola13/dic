diarthrosis:/ˌdaɪɑːrˈθroʊsɪs/| n. |可动关节|A freely movable articulation; a joint that allows the bones it connects to move freely in a range of directions.  It is characterized by the presence of a synovial cavity between the articulating bones.|The knee is a classic example of a diarthrosis, allowing flexion, extension, and rotation.|Synonyms: synovial joint|Antonyms: synarthrosis (immovable joint), amphiarthrosis (slightly movable joint)|Noun
