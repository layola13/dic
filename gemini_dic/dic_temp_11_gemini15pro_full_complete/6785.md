anaphoras:/əˈnæfərəz/ /əˈnæfɔːrəz/ | n. |首语重复法; 回指|The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses.  It's a rhetorical device used for emphasis or to create a sense of rhythm.|The preacher used anaphoras throughout his sermon, repeating "Let us pray" before each new petition.  牧师在他的布道中使用了首语重复法，在每个新的祈祷前重复“让我们祈祷”。|

Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream" speech is famous for its use of anaphoras. 马丁·路德·金的“我有一个梦想”演讲因其使用首语重复法而闻名。|synonyms: repetition, reiteration, alliteration (sometimes)|antonyms: N/A|noun
