jejunely:/dʒɪˈdʒuːnli/| adv.|幼稚地，空洞地|Lacking in nutritive value; lacking in interest or substance; immature, childish.|He jejunely dismissed the complex issue with a wave of his hand. 他幼稚地挥了挥手，对这个复杂的问题不屑一顾。|Synonyms: childishly, immaturely, naively, vapidly, insipidly|Antonyms: maturely, sophisticatedly, profoundly, thoughtfully|Adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *jejune*, from Latin *ieiunus* "fasting, hungry," figuratively "dry, barren, meager," from Proto-Italic *jejūnos*, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eyH₁-ūnos* "empty, fasting."
