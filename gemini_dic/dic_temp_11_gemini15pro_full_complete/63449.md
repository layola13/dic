entrenches:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃɪz/ | v. | 巩固，加强；使处于牢固地位；挖壕沟防护 |  To establish (someone or something) in a position of strength, especially one that is difficult to attack. To dig trenches for defensive purposes. | The government's policies have become further entrenched over the years. 这些年来，政府的政策已经进一步巩固。 The soldiers entrenched themselves on the hilltop. 士兵们在山顶上挖了壕沟，以防敌人进攻。 | Synonyms: fortify, strengthen, reinforce, secure, establish, embed | Antonyms: weaken, undermine, expose | verb

entrenched:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃt/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；牢固的 | Firmly established and difficult or unlikely to change; ingrained. |  The company's entrenched position in the market makes it hard for new competitors to enter.  这家公司在市场上的根深蒂固的地位使得新竞争者很难进入。  Entrenched attitudes can be difficult to overcome. 根深蒂固的态度很难克服。 | Synonyms: established, ingrained, fixed, rooted, firm | Antonyms:  weak, unstable, flexible | adjective
