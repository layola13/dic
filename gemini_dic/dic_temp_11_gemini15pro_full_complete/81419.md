grasswidowhoods: /ˈɡræsˌwɪdoʊhʊdz/ | n. | 寡妇身份（尤指丈夫长期不在身边的妻子）; 类似寡妇的处境 | The state of being a grass widow, especially the period of living as such.  A woman whose husband is absent for a prolonged period. |  She experienced several periods of grasswidowhoods while her husband was stationed overseas.  她在丈夫驻扎海外期间经历了几段类似寡妇的时期。 | 近义词: widowhood, separation | 反义词: marriage, togetherness | 词性: 名词

词源: grass + widow + -hood + -s

"grass" in this context likely refers to the idea of a wife being "put out to grass," meaning left alone or neglected.  The term originated in the 16th century.
