metaleptical:/ˌmɛtəˈlɛptɪkəl/
adjective
跳跃的，跨越的，修辞上跨越的
A figure of speech in which one thing is referred to by something else associated with it, thus creating a chain of associations.  This creates a sense of displacement or discontinuity.  Often used to create a humorous or surreal effect.
The author used a metaleptical reference to the future, mentioning a character reading a review of the very book the reader was currently holding. 作者使用了跳跃式的修辞手法来指涉未来，提到了一个角色正在阅读读者手中这本书的评论。
The film employed a metaleptical narrative, with characters aware they were in a movie.  这部电影采用了打破第四面墙的叙事方式，其中的角色意识到自己身处电影之中。
synonyms:  transumptive, far-fetched, strained, discontinuous
antonyms:  direct, literal, straightforward
