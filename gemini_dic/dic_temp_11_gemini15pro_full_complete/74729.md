Etymology:
From Middle English *fresen, from Old English frēosan (“to freeze”), from Proto-Germanic *freusaną (“to freeze”), from Proto-Indo-European *preus- (“to freeze, burn”). Cognate with West Frisian frieza (“to freeze”), Dutch vriezen (“to freeze”), German frieren (“to freeze”), Danish fryse (“to freeze”), Swedish frysa (“to freeze”), Icelandic frjósa (“to freeze”).  The sense of "to release" developed from the idea of thawing or loosening something that was frozen or stuck.
