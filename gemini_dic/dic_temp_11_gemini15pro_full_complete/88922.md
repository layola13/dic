hold your ground:/hoʊld jʊər ɡraʊnd/
|v.|坚持立场；不退缩；不屈服|To maintain one's position, especially in the face of opposition or pressure.  To refuse to yield or retreat.|The soldiers held their ground despite heavy enemy fire. 士兵们尽管遭到猛烈的敌军炮火，仍然坚守阵地。|近义词:stand firm, stand one's ground, hold fast,  stick to one's guns|反义词:retreat,  give way, back down, surrender|词性: verb phrase
