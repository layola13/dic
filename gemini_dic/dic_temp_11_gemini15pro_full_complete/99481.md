interferometer:/ˌɪntərˈfəˈrɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌɪntəˈfɪˈrɒmɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n. |干涉仪，干涉计|An instrument that uses interference of electromagnetic waves (usually light waves) to make precise measurements of wavelengths or distances.|The scientists used an interferometer to measure the minute changes in the star's position. 科学家们用干涉仪测量了恒星位置的微小变化。|近义词:interferometric apparatus|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

interfere:/ˌɪntərˈfɪər/| v. |干涉，干扰|To come into collision or opposition; to meddle|Please don't interfere in my personal affairs. 请不要干涉我的私事。|近义词: meddle, intervene, intrude|反义词: assist, aid|词性:verb

meter:/ˈmiːtər/| n. |米，计，仪表|A unit of length in the metric system; a device used for measuring|The room is five meters wide. 房间有五米宽。|近义词: gauge, measure|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

词源:  From French *interféromètre*, from interférer (“to interfere”) + -mètre (“-meter”).  Ultimately from Latin inter (“between, among”) + ferire (“to strike”) and Greek μέτρον (métron, “measure”).
