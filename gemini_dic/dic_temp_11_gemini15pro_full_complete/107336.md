lambrequin: /ˈlæmbrɪkɪn/ (US), /ˈlæmbrəkɪn/ (UK) | n. |幔帐；垂饰；窗帘盒| A short piece of drapery or fabric used to ornament a window, often covering the top of curtains or a curtain rod. | The elaborate lambrequin added a touch of elegance to the living room.  这精美的幔帐为客厅增添了一丝优雅。|  The lambrequin concealed the curtain hardware. 幔帐遮住了窗帘的五金件。 |synonyms: valance, drapery, swag, pelmet | antonyms: (No direct antonym, but consider: bare window, exposed hardware) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *lambrequin*, ultimately from Middle Dutch *lamperkijn* meaning "cover for a lamp", from *lamper* ("lamp") + *-kijn* (diminutive suffix). The meaning shifted from a lamp cover to a decorative hanging for a window or bed.
