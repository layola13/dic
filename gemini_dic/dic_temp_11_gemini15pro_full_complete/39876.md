constipate:/ˈkɑːnstɪpeɪt/ /ˈkɒnstɪpeɪt/| vt. |便秘；使迟缓；使停滞|to cause someone to be unable to empty their bowels easily|例句：A diet low in fiber can constipate you.  低纤维饮食会导致便秘。

例句：The traffic was so bad that it completely constipated the city center. 交通状况如此糟糕，以至于完全堵塞了市中心。|近义词：obstruct, clog, block|反义词: loosen, relax |词性：动词


constipated:/ˈkɑːnstɪpeɪtɪd/ /ˈkɒnstɪpeɪtɪd/| adj. |便秘的；（指思想等）保守的，僵化的|unable to empty your bowels easily; (of thinking, etc.) very unwilling to change, and therefore not able to develop new ideas|例句：I felt bloated and constipated. 我感觉肚子胀，便秘。

例句：Their thinking has become constipated and inflexible. 他们的思想变得僵化和不灵活。|近义词：blocked, bound, inflexible|反义词: open-minded, flexible |词性：形容词


constipation:/ˌkɑːnstɪˈpeɪʃn/ /ˌkɒnstɪˈpeɪʃn/| n. |便秘|the condition of being unable to empty your bowels easily|例句：Chronic constipation can be a serious problem. 慢性便秘可能是一个严重的问题。

例句：He suffered from constipation after the surgery. 他在手术后患上了便秘。|近义词：costiveness|反义词: diarrhea |词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin constipatus, past participle of constipare (“to press closely together, crowd, condense, pack, cram”), from con- (“together”) + stipare (“to press, pack”).
