fault:/fɔːlt/ (British) /fɒlt/ (American)| n. v.|缺点，错误，故障，毛病；断层|A defect or imperfection; a mistake or error; responsibility for an accident or misfortune; a fracture in the earth's crust.|The car accident was his fault. 这场车祸是他的错。|Synonyms: defect, flaw, error, mistake, blunder, shortcoming, weakness, imperfection, failing, deficiency, blemish, glitch, bug|Antonyms: merit, virtue, strength, perfection|noun

fault:/fɔːlt/ (British) /fɒlt/ (American)| v.|找出缺点，挑剔；弄错；断裂|To find a defect or imperfection in something; to criticize or blame someone for something.|He faulted her performance for lack of emotion. 他批评她的表演缺乏情感。|Synonyms: criticize, blame, censure, condemn, denounce, reprehend|Antonyms: praise, commend, laud|verb
