dizziness:/ˈdɪzinəs/| n.|头晕，眩晕|A feeling of whirling and a tendency to fall.|例句：The dizziness caused by the medication made it difficult to walk. 药物引起的眩晕使行走困难。|例句：She experienced a sudden dizziness and had to sit down. 她突然感到一阵眩晕，不得不坐下。|近义词：vertigo, lightheadedness, giddiness|反义词:stability, balance|词性:noun

dizzy:/ˈdɪzi/| adj.|头晕的，眩晕的|Having or causing a sensation of whirling and a tendency to fall.|例句：The roller coaster made me dizzy. 过山车让我头晕。|例句：I felt dizzy after standing up too quickly. 我站起来太快后感到头晕。|近义词：giddy, lightheaded, faint|反义词:steady, balanced|词性:adjective

词源: From Old English *dysig, meaning "foolish, stupid," later evolving to include the sense of "confused, bewildered" and finally "dizzy."  Related to Old High German tusic "stupid." The evolution of meaning likely stems from the disorientation associated with foolishness or confusion.
