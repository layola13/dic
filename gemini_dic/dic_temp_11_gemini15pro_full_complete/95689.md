inconcurring: /ˌɪnkənˈkɜːrɪŋ/ | adj. | 不同意的，不一致的 | Not agreeing or concurring; dissenting.  Expressing disagreement, especially in a judicial context. | The judge wrote an inconcurring opinion, disagreeing with the majority decision. 法官写了一份不同意见书，不同意多数人的决定。| The inconcurring justices believed the law was unconstitutional. 持不同意见的法官认为该法律违宪。 | 近义词: dissenting, disagreeing, opposing | 反义词: concurring, agreeing, assenting | 词性: adjective

词源: From Late Latin *inconcurrent-, from in- ‘not’ + concurrent- ‘running together’.
