hyperaccuracies:/ˌhaɪpərˈækjərəsiz/
|n.|超高精度；极高的准确性|A state of extremely high accuracy, often exceeding what is necessary or expected.  The pursuit of hyperaccuracies can sometimes be counterproductive, leading to wasted time and resources. |The team achieved hyperaccuracies in their measurements, surpassing all previous records.|近义词: precision, exactness, meticulousness|反义词: inaccuracy, imprecision, vagueness|词性:名词

Etymology:
hyper- (prefix): from Greek, meaning "over," "beyond," "excessive"
accuracy (noun): from Late Latin accuratia, from Latin accuratus "prepared with care," past participle of accurare "take care of," from ad- "to" + curare "take care of."
