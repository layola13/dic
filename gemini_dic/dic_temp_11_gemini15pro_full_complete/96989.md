infantilizations: /ɪnˌfæn.tɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃənz/ | n. | 幼化；幼稚化；婴儿化 |  The act of treating someone as if they were a child, often by restricting their independence or speaking to them in a condescending way.  It involves reducing someone, typically an adult, to a childlike state or status. This can manifest in various ways, including restricting their autonomy, diminishing their capabilities, or speaking to them in a condescending or overly simplistic manner. |  He resented the infantilizations of his elderly parents by the nursing home staff. 他憎恨养老院工作人员把他年迈的父母幼稚化。

The media's infantilization of women is often seen in advertising. 媒体在广告中经常把女性幼稚化。 |  Near synonyms: patronizing, condescending, demeaning, belittling | Antonyms: empowering, enabling, respecting, validating | Noun (plural)
