gloating:/ˈɡləʊtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈɡloʊtɪŋ/ (US)| n. adj.|幸灾乐祸，沾沾自喜|dwelling on one's own success or another's misfortune with smugness or malignant pleasure.|He couldn't resist gloating over his rival's failure. 他忍不住对竞争对手的失败幸灾乐祸。|近义词：revelling, exulting, crowing|反义词：commiserating, sympathizing|词性: noun (主要), adjective (较少用)

gloat:/ɡləʊt/ (UK) /ˈɡloʊt/ (US)| vi.|幸灾乐祸，沾沾自喜|to contemplate or dwell on one's own success or another's misfortune with smugness or malignant pleasure.|She gloated over her new expensive handbag. 她对着自己昂贵的新手提包沾沾自喜。|近义词：revel, exult, crow|反义词：commiserate, sympathize|词性: verb

词源: From Middle English *gloten, from Old Norse *gláta (“to grin, smile scornfully”), from Proto-Germanic *glōtijaną (“to look askance, leer”).
