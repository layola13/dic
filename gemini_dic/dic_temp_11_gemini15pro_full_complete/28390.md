carbolic acid:/kɑːrˈbɒlɪk ˈæsɪd/ | n. | 石炭酸；苯酚 | Phenol, a white crystalline solid with a characteristic acrid odor, used as a disinfectant and antiseptic. | Carbolic acid is toxic and should be handled with care. 石炭酸有毒，应小心处理。| Synonyms: phenol | Antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From carbo- (carbon) + -ol (alcohol) + -ic (adjectival suffix).  Ultimately from Latin "carbo" (coal, charcoal) and Arabic "al-kuḥl" (kohl, a fine powder used as eye makeup, originally made from stibnite, a sulfide of antimony).  The term "carbolic acid" was originally applied to impure phenol derived from coal tar.
