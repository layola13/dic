eavesdropping: /ˈiːvzˌdrɑːpɪŋ/ (US), /ˈiːvzˌdrɒpɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |偷听，窃听|The act of secretly listening to a private conversation.|She was accused of eavesdropping on her neighbors. 她被指控偷听邻居的谈话。|近义词：listening in, snooping, overhearing|反义词: ignoring, disregarding|词性:名词

eavesdrop: /ˈiːvzˌdrɑːp/ (US), /ˈiːvzˌdrɒp/ (UK)| v. |偷听，窃听|To secretly listen to a private conversation.|He was eavesdropping on their conversation from behind the door. 他躲在门后偷听他们的谈话。|近义词：listen in, snoop, overhear|反义词: ignore, disregard|词性:动词

词源: From "eavesdrip,"  the dripping of water from the eaves of a house, later associated with standing under the eaves to listen to conversations inside.
