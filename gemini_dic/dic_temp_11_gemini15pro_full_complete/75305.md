frontiniac: /ˌfrʌntɪˈnjæk/ | n. |  弗龙特纳克 | A red wine grape variety grown primarily in North America, particularly in Ontario, Canada.  It is known for producing wines with aromas of cherry, raspberry, and other red fruits, often with earthy or spicy notes. |  Example:  The Frontiniac wine had a distinct cherry aroma and a smooth finish.  弗龙特纳克葡萄酒有明显的樱桃香气和顺滑的口感。
Example:  Ontario is well-known for its Frontiniac grapes. 安大略省以其弗龙特纳克葡萄而闻名。 | 近义词:  (In terms of wine style) Gamay, Pinot Noir | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology:  Named after Louis de Buade de Frontenac, a governor of New France in the late 17th century.
