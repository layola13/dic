appraisee:/əˌpreɪˈziː/| n.|被评估者，被鉴定者|A person who is appraised, especially an employee whose performance is reviewed.|The appraisee was nervous about the upcoming performance review. 被评估者对即将到来的绩效评估感到紧张。|Synonym:  employee under review, subject of appraisal|Antonym: appraiser|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From appraise + -ee (suffix forming nouns denoting the person affected by or subjected to the action of the verb). Appraise ultimately comes from Old French *apreisier (compare Modern French apprécier), from Latin appretiare "to set a price to," from ad- "to" + pretium "price."
