ghetto:/ˈɡɛtəʊ/ (UK), /ˈɡɛtoʊ/ (US)| n.|贫民窟，少数民族聚居区|A part of a city, especially a slum area, occupied by a minority group or groups.|Many Jewish people were forced to live in ghettos during World War II.  二战期间，许多犹太人被迫住在贫民窟里。|slum, quarter|suburb, affluent area|noun


ghettos:/ˈɡɛtəʊz/ (UK), /ˈɡɛtoʊz/ (US)| n. |贫民窟，少数民族聚居区（复数）|Plural form of "ghetto," referring to multiple such areas.|The ghettos of many European cities were overcrowded and impoverished. 许多欧洲城市的贫民窟都人满为患，而且非常贫困。|slums, quarters|suburbs, affluent areas|noun

词源：
From Italian *getto “foundry”, later applied to the area adjacent to the foundry in Venice where Jews were compelled to reside. The word likely comes from Venetian *gèto “slag”.
来自意大利语 *getto “铸造厂”，后来被用来指威尼斯铸造厂附近的地区，犹太人被迫居住在那里。这个词可能来自威尼斯语 *gèto “矿渣”。
