hame:/heɪm/ | n. |  one of two curved pieces of wood or metal forming the collar of a draught horse, or a similar part of a plough or other implement. |  A hame is a curved piece of metal or wood that fits around a horse's collar to attach it to a cart or plow. | The farmer adjusted the hames on his workhorse.  农民调整了驮马上的颈圈。 | synonyms: collar, harness | antonyms: N/A | noun

hame:/heɪm/ | n. | home; house (Scottish, Northern English dialect) | Hame means home in some dialects of English. |  He's gannin' back to his hame in the Highlands. 他要回他在高地的家。| synonyms: home, house, abode, dwelling | antonyms: N/A | noun
