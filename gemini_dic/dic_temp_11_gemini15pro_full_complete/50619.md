derelict:/ˈderəlɪkt/ (US), /ˈdɛrəlɪkt/ (UK)| adj. n.| 被遗弃的，玩忽职守的；被遗弃的人，流浪者|Failing in or neglecting a duty or obligation; deserted or abandoned, especially by the owner or occupant.  As a noun, a person without a home, job, or property.|
The derelict building was a haven for pigeons. 这栋废弃的建筑成了鸽子的天堂。

The derelict ship lay rusting in the harbor.  这艘废弃的船停在港口里生锈。

近义词：abandoned, deserted, forsaken, neglected, ramshackle, dilapidated|
反义词：maintained, cared-for, occupied, inhabited|
词性: adjective, noun

derelict:/ˈderəlɪkt/ (US), /ˈdɛrəlɪkt/ (UK)| n.| 被遗弃的人，流浪者|A person without a home, job, or property.|
The derelicts huddled together for warmth in the doorway. 流浪汉们挤在门口取暖。

He felt like a derelict, adrift in a sea of despair. 他觉得自己像个流浪汉，漂泊在绝望的海洋里。


近义词：vagabond, tramp, bum, homeless person|
反义词：resident, citizen, homeowner|
词性: noun


词源: From Latin *derelictus*, past participle of *derelinquere*  "to abandon," from *de-* "down, completely" + *relinquere* "to leave behind."
