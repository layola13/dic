arrogations: /ˌærəˈɡeɪʃənz/
| n. | 僭越；擅取；霸占 | The act of arrogating, or that which is arrogated; an assumption of right or prerogative without just claim. |  The king's arrogations of power led to widespread unrest. 国王的权力僭越导致了广泛的动荡。| 近义词：usurpations, seizures, appropriations | 反义词: relinquishments, abdications | 词性: 名词

arrogate: /ˈærəɡeɪt/
| v. | 僭越；擅取；霸占 | To claim or seize without justification; to make undue claims to having (something). | He arrogated to himself the right to decide what everyone should do. 他僭越地认为自己有权决定每个人应该做什么。| 近义词：usurp, seize, appropriate | 反义词: relinquish, abdicate | 词性: 动词

词源:
From Latin arrogatus, past participle of arrogare (“to claim for oneself, appropriate, adopt”), from ad- (“to”) + rogare (“to ask”).  The original sense was "to claim something for oneself," later shifting to "claim without justification."
