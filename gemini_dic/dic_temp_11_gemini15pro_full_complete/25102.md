buccaneer:/ˌbʌkəˈnɪər/| n.|海盗，掠夺者|A sailor, often operating in the Caribbean, who engaged in piracy and robbery.|例句：The buccaneer plundered the Spanish galleon.  这个海盗掠夺了西班牙大帆船。

例句：Stories of buccaneers and buried treasure have captivated generations.  海盗和宝藏的故事吸引了一代又一代人。|近义词：pirate, corsair, privateer, freebooter|反义词:lawman, police|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *boucanier*, "one who smokes meat," from *boucan*, a wooden framework for smoking meat, from Carib *buccan*.  The term originally referred to hunters in the Caribbean who smoked meat on a *boucan*, and later came to refer to pirates who preyed on Spanish ships in the region.
