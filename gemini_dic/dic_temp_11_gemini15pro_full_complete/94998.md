in-tow:/ɪn ˈtoʊ/ | adv. | 被拖着；紧随其后 | Following closely behind, typically a vehicle being towed by another. | The car broke down and had to be driven in-tow. 汽车抛锚了，不得不被拖着走。 |  The little boy followed his mother in-tow. 小男孩紧跟着他的妈妈。| 近义词: towed, trailing, following | 反义词: leading, preceding | 词性: 副词

in-tow:/ɪn ˈtoʊ/ | adj. | 被拖着的；紧随其后的 | Being towed or following closely behind. |  The in-tow caravan swayed precariously. 被拖着的房车摇摇晃晃地行驶着。|  With her children in-tow, she navigated the busy market. 孩子们紧跟着她，她在繁忙的市场中穿梭。| 近义词: towed, trailing, following | 反义词: leading, preceding | 词性: 形容词


词源: "in tow"源于航海术语，指的是一艘船被另一艘船用绳索拖着走。后来，这个词语被引申用于描述任何被拖着或紧随其后的情况。
