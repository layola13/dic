clinging:/ˈklɪŋɪŋ/ | adj. | 粘着的，紧贴的；依恋的，依赖的 | tending to stick to something; holding tightly; emotionally dependent | The clinging wet clothes felt uncomfortable against her skin. 湿漉漉的衣服紧贴在她皮肤上，让她感觉不舒服。 | The child was clinging to his mother's leg.  孩子紧紧抱着妈妈的腿。 |  近义词：adhesive, tenacious, dependent | 反义词：detached, independent | 词性: adjective

clinging:/ˈklɪŋɪŋ/ | n. |  依附，固执 | the act of holding on tightly; persistent attachment |  Her clinging to old habits prevented her from moving forward. 她对旧习惯的固执阻碍了她前进。 |  His clinging to the past made it difficult for him to embrace the present. 他对过去的执着让他难以拥抱现在。| 近义词：attachment, adherence | 反义词：detachment, release | 词性: noun


词源: From Middle English *clinginge, present participle of clingen (“to adhere, stick to, cling”), from Old English clingan (“to shrink, wither, coagulate, congeal, adhere”)
