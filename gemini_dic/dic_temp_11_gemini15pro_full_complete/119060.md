metonymic:/ˌmetəˈnɪmɪk/ | adj. | 转喻的 |  A figure of speech in which a thing or concept is referred to by the name of something closely associated with that thing or concept. | The White House announced new policies. (White House refers to the President and his administration) 白宫宣布了新的政策。（白宫指代总统及其政府）

The pen is mightier than the sword. (Pen refers to written words and sword to military force) 笔比剑更强大。（笔指代文字，剑指代武力）| 近义词: figurative, symbolic | 反义词: literal | 词性: adjective

词源:  From Late Latin *metonymia, from Greek *metōnymía "change of name," from meta- "change" + onoma "name".
