escarpment:/ɪˈskɑːrpmənt/| n. |悬崖，陡坡|A long, steep slope, especially one at the edge of a plateau or separating areas of land at different heights.|The escarpment offered a breathtaking view of the valley below. 悬崖提供了下面山谷的壮丽景色。|近义词：cliff, precipice, bluff|反义词：slope, incline|词性:noun

escarp:/ɪˈskɑːrp/| n. vt. |悬崖，陡坡;使成陡坡|A steep slope or cliff; to make or become steep.|The hikers carefully navigated the rocky escarp.  徒步旅行者小心翼翼地穿过岩石陡坡。 The builders had to escarp the side of the hill to prevent landslides. 建筑工人不得不把山坡弄成陡坡以防止山体滑坡。|近义词：cliff, precipice, bluff;  steepen|反义词：slope, incline;  flatten|词性:noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French escarpe (“scarp, escarpment”), from Italian scarpa (“slope, escarpment”), from a Germanic source akin to Old High German scharf (“sharp”), ultimately from Proto-Germanic *skarpaz (“sharp”).  Related to English sharp.
