conjure:/ˈkʌndʒər/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒə(r)/ (UK)| v.|变戏法；使…变戏法般地出现；恳求；想象；想起|To make something appear as if by magic, or to bring a thought, image, or memory to mind.|The magician conjured a rabbit out of a hat. 魔术师从帽子里变出一只兔子。

She conjured up images of her childhood. 她想起了童年的景象。|近义词：summon, evoke, implore, beseech|反义词：banish, dispel|词性:动词

conjure up:/ˈkʌndʒər ʌp/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒə(r) ʌp/ (UK)| phrasal v.|使…变戏法般地出现；想象；想起|To create or bring something to mind.|He conjured up a delicious meal from the few ingredients he had. 他用仅有的几种食材变戏法似的做出一顿美味佳肴。

The smell of the ocean conjured up memories of her childhood vacations. 海水的味道让她想起了童年假期。|近义词：evoke, imagine, recall|反义词：forget, suppress|词性:动词短语


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French conjurer "to invoke (a devil or spirit) by oath," from Latin conjurare "to swear together, conspire," from com- "together" (see com-) + jurare "to swear," from jus "law, right" (see jurist). The meaning "to practice magic" is first attested c. 1500.
