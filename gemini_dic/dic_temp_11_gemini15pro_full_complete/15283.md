blacklist: /ˈblækˌlɪst/ | n. v. | 黑名单；禁止往来户；将…列入黑名单 | A list of people or things that are disapproved of and are to be avoided or excluded. |例句：The company blacklisted him after he was caught stealing.  这家公司在他被抓到偷窃后把他列入了黑名单。

例句： Several websites are blacklisted by the government. 一些网站被政府列入了黑名单。| 近义词：boycott, ban | 反义词：whitelist | 词性：名词，动词

blacklist: /ˈblækˌlɪst/ | v. | 将…列入黑名单；禁止 | To put someone or something on a blacklist. | 例句：They blacklisted several journalists for their critical reporting. 他们将几名持批评态度的记者列入黑名单。

例句：The government blacklisted the organization for its terrorist activities. 政府因其恐怖活动将该组织列入黑名单。| 近义词：ban, bar, exclude | 反义词：whitelist, approve | 词性：动词

词源: black (黑色, 不祥的) + list (名单)  - 17世纪中期开始使用，最初指记录有不良记录的人员名单。
