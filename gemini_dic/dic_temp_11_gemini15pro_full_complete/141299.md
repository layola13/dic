open-mindedly:/ˌoʊpənˈmaɪndɪdli/ | adv. |虚心地，开明地 | In an open-minded way; without prejudice. | She listened open-mindedly to his proposal, even though she initially disagreed. 她虚心地听取了他的建议，即使她最初并不同意。 |  She considered his suggestions open-mindedly, despite her initial skepticism. 尽管她最初持怀疑态度，但她还是虚心地考虑了他的建议。 | 近义词: receptively, tolerantly, impartially | 反义词: narrowly, biasedly, dogmatically | 词性: 副词

open-minded:/ˌoʊpənˈmaɪndɪd/ | adj. | 开明的，虚心的 | Willing to consider new ideas and experiences; unprejudiced. |  |  | 近义词: receptive, tolerant, impartial | 反义词: narrow-minded, biased, dogmatic | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From open +‎ minded +‎ -ly.  "Open-minded" first appeared in the early 1800s, combining "open" with "minded" to describe someone receptive to new ideas. The adverbial form, "open-mindedly," followed naturally.
