bogtrotter:/ˈbɒɡˌtrɒtər/| n.|沼泽居民，乡巴佬|A person who lives in a boggy or marshy area. Often used derisively to refer to someone considered unsophisticated or rustic.|He called them a bunch of ignorant bogtrotters.|近义词：yokel, rustic, peasant|反义词:sophisticate, cosmopolitan|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From bog + trotter.  "Bog" refers to a wet, spongy ground. "Trotter" refers to someone who trots or walks, implying someone who lives and walks in the bogs.
