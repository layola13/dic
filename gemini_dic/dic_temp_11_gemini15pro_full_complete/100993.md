invalid:/ɪnˈvælɪd/ | adj. | 无效的；病弱的；站不住脚的 | not legally or officially acceptable; (of a person) weak and ill, especially for a long time; (of an argument or reason) weak and not acceptable | Example: The ticket is invalid.  这张票无效。 Example: She became an invalid after the accident. 事故发生后，她成了残疾人。 | Near synonyms: void, null, unsound, infirm, debilitated | Antonyms: valid, sound, healthy, robust | word type: adjective

invalid:/ˈɪnvəlɪd/ | n. | 病弱者；残疾人 | a person made weak or disabled by illness or injury | Example: He cared for his invalid mother for many years. 他多年来照顾着他体弱多病的母亲。 Example: The hospital has a special ward for invalids.  这家医院有专门的病房收治残疾人。 | Near synonyms: patient, convalescent | Antonyms: none | word type: noun

invalid:/ɪnˈvælɪˌdeɪt/ | vt. | 使无效；使站不住脚 | make (something) legally or officially unacceptable;  weaken or destroy the cogency of (an argument or reason) | Example: The judge invalidated the contract. 法官宣布合同无效。 Example:  New evidence invalidated his theory. 新证据使他的理论站不住脚。 | Near synonyms: nullify, void, negate, disprove | Antonyms: validate, confirm, prove | word type: verb
