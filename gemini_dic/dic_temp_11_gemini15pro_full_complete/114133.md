maharajahs:/ˌmɑːhəˈrɑːdʒəz/
n.
土邦主，大君（复数）
A ruler in India, especially during the British Raj.
The maharajahs of India lived in opulent palaces.  印度的土邦主住在豪华的宫殿里。
The visiting maharajahs were treated like royalty.  来访的土邦主像皇室一样受到款待。
synonyms:  princes, rulers, potentates, kings
antonyms:  subjects, commoners
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Hindi महाराज (mahārāj), from Sanskrit महाराजन् (mahārājan), from महा (mahā, “great”) + राजन् (rājan, “king”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂rḗǵs (“ruler”).
