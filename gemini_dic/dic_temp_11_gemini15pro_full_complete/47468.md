dealing:/ˈdiːlɪŋ/ /ˈdilɪŋ/| n.|交易，买卖；交往，生意；待遇，对待；发牌|a way of buying or selling something; the way someone treats other people; the activity of giving playing cards to the people playing a card game|例句：He has some experience in dealing with difficult customers. 他有一些与难缠客户打交道的经验。

例句：The car dealer offered a fair dealing. 汽车经销商提供了一次公平的交易。|近义词：trade, transaction, interaction, handling, treatment|反义词:misdealing, mistreatment|词性:noun


dealing:/ˈdiːlɪŋ/ /ˈdilɪŋ/| adj.|发牌的，分配的，给予的|(usually before a noun) giving or sharing out something, especially playing cards|例句：The dealing hand paused briefly. 发牌的手短暂地停顿了一下。

例句：He was the dealing manager in that negotiation. 他是那次谈判中的主导经理。|近义词：distributing, allocating, dispensing|反义词:receiving, collecting|词性:adjective


词源: From Middle English *delen, from Old English *dǣlan (“to divide, distribute, share, deal”), from Proto-Germanic *dailjaną (“to divide, participate”), from Proto-Indo-European *dail- (“to divide, share”). Cognate with West Frisian dielje (“to share, participate”), Dutch delen (“to share, divide, distribute”), German teilen (“to divide, share, part”), Danish dele (“to share, divide”).
