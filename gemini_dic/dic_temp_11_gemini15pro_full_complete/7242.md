anglo-catholicism:/ˌæŋɡləʊˈkæθəlɪsɪzəm/| n.|盎格鲁天主教主义|A movement within the Church of England that emphasizes the Catholic heritage of the Anglican Church, often incorporating ritualistic practices and doctrines similar to those of Roman Catholicism.  It seeks to combine the Anglican tradition with elements of Roman Catholic liturgy and theology while remaining within the Church of England.|The vicar's anglo-catholicism was evident in the elaborate ceremonies he conducted.|Synonyms: High Church Anglicanism, Anglo-Papalism|Antonyms: Evangelical Anglicanism, Low Church Anglicanism|noun
