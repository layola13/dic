orchestre:/ˈɔːrkɪstrə/ (UK), /ˈɔːrˌkɛstrə/ (US)| n.|管弦乐队，乐队|A large group of musicians who play various instruments together, typically including strings, woodwind, brass, and percussion.  The orchestra played a beautiful symphony.|例句：The orchestra performed Beethoven's Fifth Symphony. 乐队演奏了贝多芬的第五交响曲。

例句：She plays the violin in the school orchestra. 她在学校管弦乐队里拉小提琴。|近义词：band, ensemble, philharmonic|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *orchestre*, from Latin *orchestra*, from Greek *orkhēstra*, meaning "a place for dancing," specifically the circular space in front of the stage in ancient Greek theatres where the chorus performed.  The modern meaning evolved because orchestras traditionally occupied this space.
