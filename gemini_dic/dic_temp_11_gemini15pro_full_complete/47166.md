dawn:/dɔːn/ (UK) /dɑːn/ (US)| n. vi.|黎明，拂晓；开端，萌芽|The beginning of something, like the start of a new day or a new era.  It can also refer to the first appearance of light in the sky before sunrise.|
The dawn of a new era. 一个新时代的黎明。
She woke at dawn. 她在黎明醒来。


近义词：sunrise, daybreak, outset, beginning|
反义词：dusk, sunset, end|
词性：noun, intransitive verb|

dawn:/dɔːn/ (UK) /dɑːn/ (US)| vi.|破晓；开始出现|To begin to appear or to become known.|
A new era is dawning. 一个新时代即将到来。
It dawned on me that I had left my keys at home. 我突然意识到我把钥匙落在家里了。


近义词：emerge, appear, begin|
反义词：disappear, fade, end|
词性：intransitive verb|

词源:
From Old English *dagian "to become day," from Proto-Germanic *dagōjaną "to become day" (from PIE root *dhegh- "to become light, bright"). The noun meaning "first appearance of daylight" is from late 12c. Related: dawning.
