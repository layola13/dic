clamber:/ˈklæmbər/ (US), /ˈklæmbə(r)/ (UK)| v. n.|攀登，爬；艰难地爬|To climb or move with difficulty, using both hands and feet.|例句：The children clambered over the rocks. 孩子们爬过岩石。|近义词：climb, scramble, scale|反义词：descend|词性：动词

clamber:/ˈklæmbər/ (US), /ˈklæmbə(r)/ (UK)| n.|攀登，爬行|A difficult climb or movement.|例句：It was a long clamber to the top of the hill. 爬到山顶是一段漫长的攀登。|近义词：climb, ascent|反义词：descent|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clambren, frequentative of climben (“to climb”), from Old English climban (“to climb”).
