incorrupted: /ˌɪnkəˈrʌptɪd/
adjective
未腐败的，未堕落的，纯洁的
Not morally corrupted; pure.  Free from moral blemish; upright.
例句：He maintained an incorrupted reputation throughout his career.  他一生都保持着清白的声誉。

例句：The incorrupted data was crucial to the success of the experiment. 原始数据对实验的成功至关重要。

近义词：untainted, undefiled, pure, uncorrupted, pristine
反义词：corrupted, tainted, defiled, polluted, contaminated
词性：adjective

incorrupt: /ˌɪnkəˈrʌpt/
adjective
廉洁的，正直的，不受腐蚀的
Not susceptible to corruption, especially bribery.  Having or showing honesty and integrity.
例句：She was known as an incorrupt judge. 她以廉洁的法官而闻名。

例句：He remained incorrupt despite the temptations of power.  尽管权力诱惑很大，他仍然保持廉洁。

近义词：honest, upright, honorable, principled, just
反义词：corrupt, dishonest, crooked, unscrupulous, venal
词性：adjective

incorrupt: /ˌɪnkəˈrʌpt/
verb
使不腐败，保持纯洁
To preserve from decay or corruption.  (Rarely used)
例句：Ancient Egyptians used elaborate methods to incorrupt the bodies of their pharaohs. 古埃及人用复杂的方法来防止法老的尸体腐烂。（注意：这个用法比较罕见，更常见的是使用preserve或mummify）

例句：They tried to incorrupt the youth with virtuous teachings. 他们试图用道德教义来保持年轻人的纯洁。（注意：这个用法也比较罕见）


近义词：preserve, protect, safeguard
反义词：corrupt, contaminate, spoil
词性：verb


词源:  From Latin *incorruptus*, from in- "not" + corruptus "corrupted," past participle of corrumpere "to destroy, spoil, corrupt."
