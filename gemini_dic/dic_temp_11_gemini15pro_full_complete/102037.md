isolation:/ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 隔离；孤立；分离；绝缘 | the state of being isolated or separated from others. |  The patient was kept in isolation to prevent the spread of infection. 病人被隔离以防止感染蔓延。

|  She lived in isolation for many years. 她多年来过着与世隔绝的生活。 | synonyms: seclusion, solitude, separation, detachment | antonyms: connection, integration, inclusion, community | noun


isolation:/ˈaɪsəleɪt/ | v. | 使隔离；使孤立；使分离；使绝缘 | to separate something from other things. |  The hospital isolated the patient with the infectious disease. 医院隔离了患有传染病的病人。

|  It's important to isolate the problem before trying to solve it. 在尝试解决问题之前，重要的是要先找出问题所在。 | synonyms: separate, segregate, detach, quarantine | antonyms: integrate, unite, connect, join | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *isolation*, from *isoler* "to isolate", from Italian *isolare* "to isolate", from *isola* "island", from Latin *insula* "island".  The sense development is from "island" to "being alone like an island" to "isolated".
