idiosyncratically:/ˌɪdiəsɪŋˈkrætɪkli/ | adv. | 特异地，独特地 | In a way that is peculiar to an individual; characteristically. | He idiosyncratically refers to his car as "The Beast". 他很独特地把他的车称为“野兽”。 | She dresses idiosyncratically, always mixing patterns and colors no one else would. 她穿着很独特，总是混搭别人不会混搭的图案和颜色。 | synonym: peculiarly, distinctively, eccentrically, uniquely | antonym: typically, conventionally, normally | Part of Speech: adverb

idiosyncrasy: /ˌɪdiəˈsɪŋkrəsi/ | n. | 特质，癖好 | A mode of behavior or way of thought peculiar to an individual. | One of his idiosyncrasies is snapping his fingers while he thinks. 他有个怪癖，思考的时候会打响指。 |  Her biggest idiosyncrasy was her insistence on wearing mismatched socks. 她最大的怪癖就是坚持穿不配套的袜子。 | synonym: peculiarity, quirk, eccentricity, mannerism | antonym: normality, conformity | Part of Speech: noun

idiosyncratic: /ˌɪdiəsɪŋˈkrætɪk/ | adj. | 特有的，独特的 | Peculiar to an individual; characteristic. | He has an idiosyncratic way of speaking, full of made-up words. 他说话的方式很独特，充满了自造词。 | The house had an idiosyncratic charm, unlike any other in the neighborhood. 这房子有一种独特的魅力，与附近任何其他房子都不一样。 | synonym: peculiar, distinctive, eccentric, unique | antonym: typical, conventional, normal | Part of Speech: adjective
