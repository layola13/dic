debilitation:/dɪˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən/| n. |衰弱，虚弱；无力|The state of being weak and infirm, often due to illness or injury.  A marked loss of strength or ability.|His long illness resulted in severe debilitation.|近义词：weakness, infirmity, feebleness, enfeeblement, impairment|反义词:strength, vigor, robustness|词性:noun

debility:/dɪˈbɪlɪti/| n. |虚弱，衰弱|Physical weakness, especially as a result of illness.|She suffered from general debility after the surgery.|近义词：weakness, frailty, infirmity|反义词:strength, health, vigor|词性:noun

debilitate:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪt/| vt. |使衰弱，使虚弱|To make someone weak and infirm.|The stroke severely debilitated him.|近义词：weaken, enfeeble, incapacitate, disable|反义词:strengthen, invigorate, empower|词性:verb
