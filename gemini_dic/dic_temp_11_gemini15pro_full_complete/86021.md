Etymology:
From Old English *hælan (unattested) or hælan "to make whole, sound; to cure," from Proto-Germanic *hailjan (source also of Old Saxon helian, Old Norse heila, Old Frisian hela, Dutch helen, German heilen "to heal"), from PIE root *kailo- "healthy, whole, uninjured." Related to Old English hal "whole, sound" (see whole).
