interest-groups:/ˈɪntrɪst ɡruːps/ | n. | 利益集团；压力集团 |  A group of people that seeks to influence public policy on the basis of a particular common interest or concern. |  Example: Lobbyists representing various interest groups try to persuade legislators to vote in their favor.  各种利益集团的游说者试图说服立法者投赞成票。

Example: Environmental interest groups are pushing for stricter regulations on pollution.  环保利益集团正在推动更严格的污染法规。| 近义词: pressure groups, lobby groups, advocacy groups | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun


interest:/ˈɪntrɪst/ | n. vt. | 兴趣；利益；利息 |  The feeling of wanting to know or learn about something or someone. | Example:  She has a keen interest in history. 她对历史有浓厚的兴趣。

Example: The bank pays 4% interest on savings accounts. 银行对储蓄账户支付4%的利息。 | 近义词:  fascination, curiosity, concern, stake, benefit | 反义词: apathy, indifference, detachment | 词性: noun, verb


group:/ɡruːp/ | n. v. | 组；团体；群 | A number of people or things that are located close together or are considered or classed together. | Example: A group of tourists gathered outside the museum. 一群游客聚集在博物馆外。
