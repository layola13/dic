antipathize:/ænˈtɪpəθaɪz/ | v. | 不喜欢；厌恶；反感 | To feel or express strong dislike or aversion towards someone or something. | She antipathizes with his political views. 她反感他的政治观点。|  He antipathized with the new management team from the start. 他从一开始就讨厌新的管理团队。| Synonyms: dislike, detest, loathe, abhor, resent | Antonyms: sympathize, empathize, like, admire | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *antipathiser*, from antipathie (“antipathy”), from Ancient Greek ἀντιπάθεια (antipátheia, “mutual aversion, reciprocal hatred”), from ἀντί (antí, “against”) + πάθος (páthos, “suffering, feeling, experience”).
