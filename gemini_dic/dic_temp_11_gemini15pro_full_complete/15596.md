bail-out: /ˈbeɪlˌaʊt/ (US) /ˈbeɪlˌaʊt/ (UK)| n.|紧急财政援助，救助；跳伞；保释|An act of giving financial assistance to a failing business or economy to save it from collapse.  The pilot had to bail out when the engine failed.|例句：The government provided a bail-out to the struggling airline. 政府向陷入困境的航空公司提供了紧急财政援助。

bail-out: /ˈbeɪlˌaʊt/ (US) /ˈbeɪlˌaʊt/ (UK)| n.|跳伞|An act of jumping out of an aircraft with a parachute.|例句：The pilot performed a successful bail-out. 飞行员成功地跳伞了。


bail-out: /ˈbeɪlˌaʊt/ (US) /ˈbeɪlˌaʊt/ (UK)| n.|保释|The act of releasing someone from custody after payment of bail.|例句：He was granted bail-out after spending a night in jail.  他在监狱里待了一晚后获得保释。



近义词：rescue, lifeline, bailout (alternative spelling), assistance, aid|反义词: refusal, denial, foreclosure|词性: noun
