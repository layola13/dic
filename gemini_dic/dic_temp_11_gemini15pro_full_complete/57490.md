dragantine:/ˈdrægənˌtiːn/ /ˈdræɡənˌtaɪn/
noun|轻帆船，一种地中海式帆船|A lateen-rigged sailing vessel, typically used in the Mediterranean.|The dragantine sailed swiftly across the Aegean Sea. 这艘轻帆船快速地航行过爱琴海。|synonyms: lateen-rigged vessel, sailing ship|antonyms: steamship, motorboat|n.

dragantine:/ˈdrægənˌtiːn/ /ˈdræɡənˌtaɪn/
adjective|轻帆船的，关于轻帆船的|Relating to or characteristic of a dragantine.|The dragantine sails billowed in the wind. 轻帆船的风帆在风中鼓起。|synonyms: lateen-rigged, sailing|antonyms: steam-powered, motor-powered|adj.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *dragantina*, diminutive of *drago* (“dragon”), from Latin *draco*, from Ancient Greek *δράκων* (*drákōn*, “dragon, serpent”).  The name likely refers to the long, slender shape of the vessel, reminiscent of a dragon or serpent.
