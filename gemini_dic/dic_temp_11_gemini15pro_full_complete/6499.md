amulet's:/ˈæmjʊləts/| n.|护身符的|The possessive form of "amulet," meaning belonging to or associated with an amulet.  It refers to something that an amulet owns or possesses, or a quality of the amulet.|The amulet's power was said to protect the wearer from evil.  据说这枚护身符的力量可以保护佩戴者免受邪恶的侵害。|Synonyms: charm's, talisman's|Antonyms: N/A (Possessive forms don't typically have antonyms)|Possessive Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *amulette*, from Late Latin *amuletum*, of uncertain origin. Possibly from Arabic *ḥamā’il* (حمایل) "anything hung around the neck," or Latin *amoliri* "to avert, turn away."
