instrumentalness:/ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəlnəs/
|n.| 器乐性，乐器演奏的程度|The degree to which a piece of music relies on instruments, rather than vocals or other sounds.  It refers to the presence and prominence of musical instruments in a recording.|This track has high instrumentalness, focusing on the guitar riff. 这首歌的器乐性很强，突出了吉他重复段。|近义词: instrumentation, musicality|反义词: vocality|词性:名词

Etymology:
From "instrumental" + "-ness."  "Instrumental" comes from Late Latin "instrumentalis,"  meaning "serving as an instrument," from Latin "instrumentum," meaning "tool, implement." "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns expressing state, quality, or condition.
