misinterpreted:/ˌmɪsɪnˈtɜːrprɪtɪd/ |v. | 误解，曲解 |  understood incorrectly, given the wrong meaning |  His silence was misinterpreted as agreement. 他的沉默被误解为同意。| synonyms: misconstrued, misunderstood | antonyms: understood, comprehended | verb (past participle)

misinterpret:/ˌmɪsɪnˈtɜːrprɪt/ |v. | 误解，曲解 | to understand something incorrectly, to give the wrong meaning |  She misinterpreted his kindness as romantic interest. 她把他的好意误解为浪漫的兴趣。| synonyms: misconstrue, misunderstand | antonyms: understand, comprehend | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (wrongly) + interpret (to explain the meaning of).  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old English "mis-", meaning "wrongly, badly, wrongly." "Interpret" comes from the Latin "interpretārī," meaning "to explain, expound, interpret."
