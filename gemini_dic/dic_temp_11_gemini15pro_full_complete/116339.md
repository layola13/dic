masterpieces:/ˈmæstərpiːsɪz/
| n. |杰作；名著|A work of outstanding artistry, skill, or workmanship.|
|例句：The museum houses many masterpieces of Renaissance art.  博物馆收藏了许多文艺复兴时期的杰作。|

|例句：Shakespeare's plays are considered masterpieces of English literature.  莎士比亚的戏剧被认为是英国文学的杰作。|
|近义词：classics, gems, works of art, chef-d'oeuvres |
|反义词：failures, disasters, mediocrities|
|词性: 名词 (plural of masterpiece)|


masterpiece:/ˈmæstərpiːs/
| n. |杰作；名著|A work of outstanding artistry, skill, or workmanship.|
|例句：The Mona Lisa is considered a masterpiece.  蒙娜丽莎被认为是一件杰作。|

|例句：The symphony is a masterpiece of musical composition.  这部交响曲是一部音乐创作的杰作。|
|近义词：classic, gem, work of art, chef-d'oeuvre |
|反义词：failure, disaster, mediocrity|
|词性: 名词|


词源:
From Middle French *maistrepiece* (modern French *chef-d'œuvre*), from *maistre* ("master") and *piece* ("piece, work").
