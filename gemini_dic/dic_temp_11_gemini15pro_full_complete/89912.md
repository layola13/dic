horrors:/ˈhɔrərz/ | n. | 恐怖，惊骇；令人恐怖的事物 |  A feeling of great fear and shock, or something that causes this feeling. Often used in the plural to express a strong feeling of shock and disapproval, sometimes in a slightly exaggerated way. |  She recoiled in horror at the sight of the snake. 她一看到蛇就吓得退缩了。

The horrors of war are indescribable. 战争的恐怖难以形容。| 近义词: terrors, atrocities, nightmares | 反义词: delights, joys, pleasures | 词性: 名词

horrors:/ˈhɔrərz/ | int. |  天哪！哎呀！| An exclamation expressing shock, dismay, or disapproval. Often used humorously or ironically. | "Horrors!" she exclaimed, "I've forgotten my keys!" “天哪！”她惊叫道，“我忘了带钥匙了！”

"Horrors! Another meeting?"  “哎呀！又要开会？”| 近义词: good heavens, oh dear, good grief | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for an interjection) | 词性: 感叹词
