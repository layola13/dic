bluffest:/ˈblʌfɪst/| adj.|最虚张声势的；最爱吓唬人的|Superlative adjective of "bluff."  It describes someone who engages in bluffing to the greatest degree. |He was the bluffest poker player I'd ever seen, constantly raising the stakes even with a weak hand. 他是我见过的最爱虚张声势的扑克玩家，即使牌很差也总是加注。|近义词：most deceitful, most pretentious|反义词：most honest, most genuine|词性:形容词

bluff:/blʌf/| n. vt. adj.|悬崖；虚张声势；吓唬；用虚张声势吓唬；愚弄|From probably Dutch *bluffen "to boast, brag."  As a noun, it refers to a steep cliff or a false display of confidence. As a verb, it means to deceive someone by pretending to be stronger or more knowledgeable than one actually is. As an adjective, it describes something abrupt or blunt, especially in manner.|He climbed up the steep bluff overlooking the ocean. 他爬上了可以俯瞰大海的陡峭悬崖。|近义词：(n.) cliff, pretense; (v.) deceive, feign; (adj.) abrupt, blunt|反义词：(n.) slope; (v.) be honest; (adj.) subtle, gradual|词性:名词;动词;形容词
