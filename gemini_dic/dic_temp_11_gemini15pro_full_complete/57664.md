drawdowns:/ˈdrɔːdaʊnz/ | n. |  资金回撤，缩水 | A decline in the value of an investment from its peak before it recovers to that peak;  a reduction in an amount of something | Example: The hedge fund experienced significant drawdowns during the financial crisis.  该对冲基金在金融危机期间经历了严重的资金回撤。

| Example:  The prolonged drought led to significant drawdowns in the reservoir's water level. 长期干旱导致水库水位大幅下降。 | Near synonyms: declines, reductions, losses, dips | Opposite: gains, increases, growth | noun
