fangs:/fæŋz/ | n. | 犬齿，尖牙；毒牙 | Long, pointed teeth, especially the canine teeth of a dog, wolf, or other carnivorous animal, or the hollow, venom-injecting teeth of a snake. | The wolf bared its fangs. 狼露出了它的尖牙。| Synonyms: teeth, canines, tusks | Antonyms: gums | noun


fangs:/fæŋz/ | n. | （尤指蛇的）毒牙 |A long, sharp tooth that animals such as snakes use to poison their prey. |The snake injected venom through its fangs. 蛇通过毒牙注射毒液。| Synonyms: venom teeth, poison teeth | Antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fang, "that which seizes," related to German *fangen and Dutch vangen, "to catch."  The original meaning referred to the act of seizing or grasping, and later narrowed to the teeth used for this purpose.
