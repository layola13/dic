gentianic:/ˌdʒɛnʃəˈnɪk/ | adj. | 龙胆的；龙胆蓝的；极苦的 | Relating to, or containing, gentian; having the properties of gentian; intensely bitter. | The gentianic elixir was known for its intense bitterness.  这种龙胆药剂以其极苦的味道而闻名。|  The gentianic blue of the lake was breathtaking.  湖水的龙胆蓝色令人叹为观止。| synonyms: bitter, acrid | antonyms: sweet, sugary | adjective

gentian:/ˈdʒɛnʃən/ | n. | 龙胆 | Any of numerous plants of the genus Gentiana, many of which have showy, usually blue flowers and intensely bitter roots and leaves. |  The gentian bloomed a vibrant blue in the alpine meadow.  龙胆在高山草甸上绽放出鲜艳的蓝色。 | synonyms:  (no direct synonym for the plant itself, but for the color) azure, sapphire | antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *gentiānus, from Latin gentiāna, from Gentius (king of Illyria, said to have discovered the medicinal properties of the gentian plant).
