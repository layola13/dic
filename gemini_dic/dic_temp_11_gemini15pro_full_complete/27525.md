camps:/kæmps/ | n. | 营地；阵营；拘留营 |A place where people stay in tents or other temporary structures, often for recreation or military purposes. Also, a group of people with shared interests or opinions, or a place where prisoners or refugees are held. |We set up our camps near the river. 我们在河边扎营。|  近义词：sites, grounds, settlements, groups, factions | 反义词：home, residence | 词性：名词


camps:/kæmps/ | v. | 露营；扎营 |The act of setting up and staying in a camp. |They camped in the mountains for a week. 他们在山里露营了一个星期。| 近义词：encamp, bivouac, lodge | 反义词：depart, leave | 词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "camps" and Latin "campus" meaning "field," "plain."
