bookworms:/ˈbʊkwɜːrmz/
noun
书虫；爱读书的人
A person who spends a lot of time reading.
She's a real bookworm and always has her nose in a book. 她是个十足的书虫，总是埋头苦读。
Synonyms: bibliophile, avid reader, voracious reader
Antonyms: non-reader


Etymology:
book + worm
"book" (n.) Old English "boc" "beech," later "book," because runes were originally carved on beech boards.
"worm" (n.) Old English "wyrm" any creeping or crawling creature.
The compound "bookworm" first appeared in the late 16th century.
