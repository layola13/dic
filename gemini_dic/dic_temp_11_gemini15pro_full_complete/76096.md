furrowing:/ˈfɜːroʊɪŋ/ (US) /ˈfʌrəʊɪŋ/ (UK)
|n.|犁沟；皱纹；(额头上的)沟|The act of making furrows, or a furrow itself.  The action of contracting the brow into wrinkles.|
|例句：The furrowing of his brow showed his concern. 他紧皱的眉头显示出他的担忧。|
|例句：The farmer was furrowing the field with his tractor.  农夫正在用拖拉机犁地。|
|近义词：creasing, wrinkling, grooving, plowing|
|反义词：smoothing, flattening|
|词性: 名词 (noun),  动名词 (gerund)|


furrow:/ˈfɜːroʊ/ (US) /ˈfʌrəʊ/ (UK)
|v.|犁地；使起皱纹|To make a long narrow cut or groove in; to wrinkle.|
|例句：He furrowed his brow in concentration. 他皱着眉头，集中注意力。|
|例句：The farmer furrowed the field ready for planting.  农民犁好了田地，准备播种。|
|近义词：crease, wrinkle, groove, plow|
|反义词：smooth, flatten|
|词性: 动词 (verb)|


furrowed:/ˈfɜːroʊd/ (US) /ˈfʌrəʊd/ (UK)
|adj.|起皱纹的；犁过的|Marked with furrows; wrinkled.|
|例句：His furrowed brow betrayed his anxiety. 他皱巴巴的眉头显露出他的焦虑。|
|例句：The farmer surveyed the newly furrowed field.  农民查看了新犁过的田地。|
|近义词：creased, wrinkled, grooved, plowed|
|反义词：smooth, flat|
|词性: 形容词 (adjective)|


词源: From Old English *furh, from Proto-Germanic *furhō ("furrow, groove"), from Proto-Indo-European *perkʷ- ("to furrow, scratch").
