motoneuron: /ˈməʊtəʊˌnjʊərɒn/ (UK) /ˈmoʊtəˌnʊrɑːn/ (US)| n. |运动神经元|A nerve cell forming part of a pathway along which impulses pass from the brain or spinal cord to a muscle or gland.|Motoneurons transmit signals from the central nervous system to the muscles, causing them to contract.| The motoneurons innervating the hand muscles are located in the spinal cord.  运动神经元支配手部肌肉，位于脊髓中。

The disease affects the motoneurons, leading to muscle weakness.  这种疾病影响运动神经元，导致肌肉无力。|近义词：motor neuron, efferent neuron|反义词: sensory neuron, afferent neuron|词性: noun

Etymology:  From *motor* + *neuron*. *Motor* comes from Latin *mōtor*, "mover," agent noun from *movēre*, "to move."  *Neuron* comes from Greek *νεῦρον* (neûron), "sinew, tendon, nerve."
