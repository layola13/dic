clasper:/ˈklɑːspər/ (US), /ˈklɑːspə(r)/ (UK)| n.|扣环，抱器，螯|A clasper is a specialized organ in some male animals, such as sharks and rays, used for internal fertilization. In insects, it's a grasping appendage.|The male shark uses its claspers to hold onto the female during mating. 雄鲨鱼用抱器在交配时抓住雌鲨鱼。

The dragonfly uses its claspers to hold the female during copulation.  蜻蜓用抱器在交配时抓住雌性。|Synonyms: appendage, grip, holdfast|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
