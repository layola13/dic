capriole:/ˈkæprɪˌoʊl/ (US), /ˈkæprɪəʊl/ (UK)| n. vi.|跳跃，腾跃；突变，反复无常|A playful leap or jump, especially one in which a horse or other quadruped rears up on its hind legs and kicks out its hind feet. Also, a sudden change or variation.|The horse performed a graceful capriole, much to the delight of the audience.  这匹马表演了一个优雅的腾跃，让观众非常高兴。|The market took a capriole, dropping sharply before recovering slightly. 市场发生了突变，急剧下跌，然后略有回升。|近义词：leap, jump, bound, caper, gambol|反义词:fall, descent|词性:noun, verb

词源: From French *capriole*, from Italian *capriola* "a somersault," from *capro* "goat," from Latin *caper* "goat."  源自法语 *capriole*，来自意大利语 *capriola* “空翻”，来自 *capro* “山羊”，来自拉丁语 *caper* “山羊”。
