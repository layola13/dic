enswathement:/ɪnˈsweɪðmənt/| n.|包裹，缠绕；覆盖|The act of wrapping or swathing something, often to the point of completely covering or concealing it.  This can refer to a literal wrapping, like a baby in a blanket, or a more figurative sense of being enveloped or surrounded by something.|The enswathement of the ancient mummy was intricate and beautiful.  古代木乃伊的裹尸布复杂而美丽。|近义词：wrapping, covering, shrouding, enveloping|反义词:uncovering, exposing|词性:noun

Etymology: From the verb "enswathe," which comes from the Old English word "beswæþan," meaning "to wrap up."  The prefix "en-" intensifies the meaning.
