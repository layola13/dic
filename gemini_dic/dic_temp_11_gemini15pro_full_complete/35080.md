clot:/klɑːt/ (US) /klɒt/ (UK)| n. v. |凝块，血块；阻塞；(使)凝结|A clot is a thick, almost solid mass formed when blood or milk dries.  As a verb, it means to form such a mass.|The blood around the wound started to clot. 伤口周围的血液开始凝结。|近义词：coagulate, thicken, congeal|反义词:dissolve, liquefy|词性:noun, verb
clot:/klɑːt/ (US) /klɒt/ (UK)| v. |(使)凝结，(使)阻塞|To clot means to become thick and partly solid.|The milk clotted when we added lemon juice. 我们加了柠檬汁后，牛奶凝结了。|近义词：coagulate, thicken, congeal|反义词:dissolve, liquefy|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *clott, akin to Middle Dutch klotte (“lump, clod”), from Proto-Germanic *klut- (“lump, ball”), from Proto-Indo-European *glew- (“to stick, glue”).
