beatniks: /ˈbiːtnɪks/ | n. |垮掉的一代；避世的一代|Members of the Beat Generation, a group of American writers and artists popular in the 1950s and early 1960s, who rejected conventional society and embraced nonconformity, often through artistic expression, drug use, and Eastern spirituality. |Jack Kerouac and Allen Ginsberg were prominent figures among the beatniks. 杰克·凯鲁亚克和艾伦·金斯堡是垮掉的一代中的杰出人物。|Synonyms: hipsters, bohemians |Antonyms: conformists |Noun
