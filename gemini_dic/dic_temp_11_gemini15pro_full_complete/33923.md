cirrus:/ˈsɪrəs/| n.|卷云，卷须|1. (meteorology) A type of cloud characterized by thin, wispy strands. 2. A tendril or hairlike appendage found in some animals and plants.|Example 1: Cirrus clouds are often seen high in the atmosphere. 卷云经常在大气层高处出现。

Example 2: The sea anemone uses its cirri to capture food. 海葵用它的卷须捕捉食物。|近义词：tendril, filament, whisker (for animal appendages), wisp (for clouds)|反义词:(No direct antonym for either meaning)|词性:noun


词源: Both "cirrate" and "cirrus" derive from the Latin word "cirrus," meaning "curl" or "tuft of hair."
