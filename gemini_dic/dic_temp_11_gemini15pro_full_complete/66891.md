expediently:/ɪkˈspiːdiəntli/
adverb
有利地，方便地，权宜之计地
In a way that is convenient and practical although possibly improper or immoral.
He acted expediently to save his own career, even though it meant sacrificing his principles. 他为了保住自己的事业而采取了权宜之计，即使这意味着牺牲他的原则。
Synonym: conveniently, pragmatically, practically
Antonym: inconveniently, impractically, inappropriately
词性: 副词


expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/
adjective
权宜的；方便的；有利的
Suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose, especially one that is immediate or self-serving.
It was expedient to avoid a confrontation. 避免冲突是权宜之计。
Synonym: advantageous, convenient, beneficial
Antonym: disadvantageous, inconvenient, detrimental
词性: 形容词


expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/
noun
权宜之计；应急手段
A means to an end; not necessarily a principled or just one.
Using a bribe was an expedient to get what he wanted. 使用贿赂是他得到想要东西的权宜之计。
Synonym: measure, resource, stratagem
Antonym: principle, scruples, ethics
词性: 名词

词源: From Latin *expediens*, present participle of expedire "to extricate, disengage, liberate," from ex- "out" + ped- "foot."  Originally meant "freeing the feet," hence "freeing from difficulties."
