assemblage:/əˈsɛmblɪdʒ/ /əˈsembləʒ/| n.|集合，组合；装配；（艺术作品的）集合体|A collection or gathering of things or people; A group of things or people considered as a whole; A work of art made by grouping found or unrelated objects.|The museum boasts a unique assemblage of antique toys. 这家博物馆以其独特的古董玩具收藏而自豪。|The artist created an assemblage of found objects. 艺术家创作了一个由现成物品组成的集合艺术品。|Synonyms: collection, group, gathering, compilation, aggregate|Antonyms: dispersal, separation, division|noun

assemblage:/əˈsɛmblɪdʒ/ /əˈsembləʒ/| n.|集合，组合；装配；（艺术作品的）集合体|An assembling or bringing together of separate parts into a single whole.|The assemblage of the new machine took several hours. 新机器的组装花了几个小时。|The watchmaker carefully completed the assemblage of the tiny gears. 钟表匠仔细地完成了微型齿轮的组装。|Synonyms: assembly, construction, fabrication|Antonyms: disassembly, deconstruction|noun
