middling:/ˈmɪdlɪŋ/ | adj. n. | 中等的；普通的；一般的；中庸,次货|Of moderate or average quality, size, ability, or status.  Also can be used as a noun to refer to goods of medium quality.|例句：He was a middling student, neither brilliant nor failing. 他是一个中等水平的学生，既不优秀也不差。

|例句：The restaurant received middling reviews. 这家餐馆得到的评价一般。|近义词：average, moderate, ordinary, mediocre |反义词：excellent, outstanding, exceptional, superior, inferior |词性:形容词,名词

middlings:/ˈmɪdlɪŋz/ | n. | 中等品；次货|Goods of medium quality, especially flour or other milled grain.|例句：The middlings were sold at a lower price. 这些次货以较低的价格出售。

|例句：The farmer used the middlings to feed his livestock. 农民用这些中等品喂养他的牲畜。 |近义词：seconds, by-products|反义词：premium, best quality |词性:名词
