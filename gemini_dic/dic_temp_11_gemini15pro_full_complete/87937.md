heist: /haɪst/
noun|抢劫, 盗窃|A robbery, especially a large or daring one.|例句：The bank heist was planned meticulously.  银行抢劫案经过了周密的策划。

例句：They pulled off the biggest art heist in history. 他们完成了历史上最大的艺术品盗窃案。|近义词：robbery, theft, burglary|反义词:return, give back|词性:n.

heist: /haɪst/
verb|抢劫, 盗窃|To rob a place or person, especially in a daring way.|例句：They heisted the jewelry store in broad daylight.  他们在光天化日之下抢劫了珠宝店。

例句：Someone heisted the painting from the museum. 有人从博物馆偷走了这幅画。|近义词：rob, steal, plunder|反义词:protect, guard|词性:v.

Etymology:  From earlier *hoist* "to lift, raise," perhaps a variant of *histe*, from Middle Dutch *hyssen*, *hissen* "to hoist," of uncertain origin.  The meaning shifted from "to lift, carry off" to "to steal" in American English slang in the early 20th century.
