launderers: /ˈlɔːndərərz/ (UK) /ˈlɑːndərərz/ (US)
n.
洗钱者；洗黑钱的人
People who engage in money laundering, the process of concealing the origins of illegally obtained money by passing it through a complex sequence of banking transfers or commercial transactions.
The launderers were arrested after a lengthy investigation.  这些洗钱者在经过长时间的调查后被逮捕了。
The government is cracking down on launderers and their complex financial schemes. 政府正在打击洗钱者及其复杂的金融计划。
Synonyms: money launderers, criminals
Antonyms: law enforcement officers
词性: 名词 (plural noun)


launderer: /ˈlɔːndərər/ (UK) /ˈlɑːndərər/ (US)
n.
洗钱者；洗黑钱的人
A person who engages in money laundering, the process of concealing the origins of illegally obtained money by passing it through a complex sequence of banking transfers or commercial transactions.
The launderer used a series of shell corporations to hide the source of the funds.  这个洗钱者利用一系列空壳公司来掩盖资金的来源。
The authorities are working to identify and prosecute the launderer. 当局正在努力识别和起诉这个洗钱者。
Synonyms: money launderer, criminal
Antonyms: law enforcement officer
词性: 名词 (singular noun)


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "to launder," which originally meant "to wash," from the Old French "laver," ultimately from the Latin "lavare" (to wash). The term "money laundering" arose by analogy to the idea of "cleaning" dirty money to make it appear legitimate.
