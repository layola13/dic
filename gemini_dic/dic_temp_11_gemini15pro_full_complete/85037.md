harborer:/ˈhɑːrbərər/ (US), /ˈhɑːbərə(r)/ (UK)| n.|窝藏者，包庇者|A person who shelters or hides someone, especially someone who is a criminal or fugitive.|The harborer of the escaped convict was arrested along with him.  窝藏逃犯的人和他一起被捕了。|近义词：protector, shelterer, concealer|反义词: betrayer, exposer|noun

harbor:/ˈhɑːrbər/ (US), /ˈhɑːbə(r)/ (UK)| n. vt.| 港口，庇护所，窝藏|n. a place on the coast where ships may moor in shelter, especially one protected from rough water by piers, jetties, and other artificial structures. vt. keep (a thought or feeling, typically a negative one) in one's mind, especially secretly.|The ship sailed into the harbor. 这艘船驶入了港口。  She harbored a secret resentment towards her boss. 她对老板怀恨在心。|近义词：(n.) port, haven, dock; (vt.) shelter, conceal, nurture|反义词: (n.) open sea; (vt.) expose, reveal|noun, verb
