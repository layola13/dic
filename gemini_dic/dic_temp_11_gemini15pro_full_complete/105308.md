kibibit:/ˈkɪbɪbɪt/| n. |千比特|A unit of information equal to 1024 bits, or 2^10 bits.  It is used to represent data storage capacity. |A typical floppy disk holds about 1440 kibibits of data.|Synonym: kilobit (often used interchangeably but technically incorrect)|Antonym: N/A|noun
