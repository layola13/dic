overabstracting: /ˌoʊvəræbˈstræktɪŋ/
|v.|过度抽象化|The process of making something too abstract, removing too many specific details, making it difficult to understand or apply in a practical context.|He was overabstracting the issue, making it seem more complicated than it really was. 他把这个问题过度抽象化了，使它看起来比实际情况更复杂。|overgeneralizing, oversimplifying|concretizing|verb

overabstract:/ˌoʊvəræbˈstrækt/
|adj.|过度抽象的|Excessively abstract; lacking in concrete details or specific examples.|His overabstract theories were difficult for the students to grasp. 他过于抽象的理论让学生难以理解。|overly abstract, too general|concrete, specific|adjective

overabstract:/ˌoʊvəræbˈstrækt/
|v.|过度抽象化|To make something excessively abstract; to remove too many specific details, making it difficult to understand or apply.|By overabstracting the data, he lost sight of the real-world implications. 通过过度抽象化数据，他忽略了现实世界的含义。|overgeneralize, oversimplify|concretize|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
"over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi "over, above") + "abstracting" (from "abstract," from Latin abstractus, past participle of abstrahere "to draw away," from ab- "away from" + trahere "to draw")
