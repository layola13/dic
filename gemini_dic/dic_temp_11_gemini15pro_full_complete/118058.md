mendacities:/mɛnˈdæsɪtiz/| n. |谎言，虚假|A plural form of "mendacity," meaning lies or falsehoods. It refers to instances of untruthfulness. |The politician's speech was filled with mendacities, designed to mislead the public. 这位政治家的演讲充满了谎言，旨在误导公众。

The historical account was riddled with mendacities, obscuring the truth of the events.  这段历史记载充满了谎言，掩盖了事件的真相。|近义词：lies, falsehoods, fabrications, untruths, deceptions|反义词：truths, honesty, veracity|词性:Noun (plural)


Etymology:

From Late Latin *mendacitas*, from Latin mendax (“lying, deceitful”), from mendum (“fault, defect”).
