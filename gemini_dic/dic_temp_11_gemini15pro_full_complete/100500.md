interventionary:/ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃəˌnɛri/
adjective|干预的，介入的|Relating to or characterized by intervention, especially by a government or other authority in the affairs of another country or organization.|The U.S. took interventionary action in the conflict.|近义词: intrusive, interfering, meddlesome|反义词: non-interventionist, non-interfering|词性: adjective

interventionary:/ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃəˌnɛri/
noun|干预者，介入者|A person who intervenes, especially in a dispute or crisis.|The UN sent interventionaries to help mediate the peace talks.|近义词: mediator, arbitrator, intercessor|反义词: bystander, onlooker|词性: noun


Etymology:
From Late Latin *interventionarius*, from Latin intervenire "to come between, interrupt," from inter- "between" + venire "to come."
