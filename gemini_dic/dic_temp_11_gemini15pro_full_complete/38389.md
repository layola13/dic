conceptualisms:/kənˈsɛptʃuəlɪzəmz/
n.
概念主义；观念艺术
A philosophy that emphasizes the importance of concepts or ideas. It often manifests in art that prioritizes the idea behind the work over its aesthetic qualities.
Conceptual art often challenges traditional notions of art and its value. For example, a pile of bricks arranged in a gallery might be considered a conceptual artwork if it represents a specific idea or commentary on society.  This piece challenges viewers to think about what constitutes art.
The conceptualisms of the late 20th century revolutionized the art world.  These new approaches questioned traditional artistic practices.

Synonyms:  concepts, ideas, notions, theories, abstractions
Antonyms:  concretions, realities, tangibles, materializations
词性: 名词 (plural noun)
