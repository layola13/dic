oversaturate:/ˌoʊvərˈsætʃəreɪt/ | v. | 过饱和，过度饱和 | to cause something to contain more of a substance than it can absorb or hold; to supply something with too much of something | The market is oversaturated with cheap products. 市场充斥着廉价产品。| The colors in the photograph were oversaturated, making it look unnatural. 照片中的颜色过饱和，看起来不自然。| 近义词: saturate, flood, glut, inundate | 反义词: undersupply, deplete | 词性: 动词

oversaturated:/ˌoʊvərˈsætʃəreɪtɪd/ | adj. | 过饱和的，过度饱和的 | containing more of a substance than can be absorbed or held | The oversaturated job market makes it difficult for recent graduates to find work. 过度饱和的就业市场让应届毕业生找工作很困难。 | The cake was oversaturated with syrup. 蛋糕的糖浆太多了。| 近义词: saturated, glutted, flooded | 反义词: unsaturated, depleted | 词性: 形形词

词源: over- (prefix meaning "excessively") + saturate (from Latin saturatus, past participle of saturare "to fill full," from satur "full")
