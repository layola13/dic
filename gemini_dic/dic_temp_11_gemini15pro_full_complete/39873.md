constellatory:/ˌkɒnstəˈleɪtəri/ /ˌkɑːnstəˈleɪtɔːri/ | adj. | 星座的；似星座的；由星座组成的 | Relating to or resembling a constellation; composed of constellations. |  The constellatory patterns in the night sky fascinated the ancient astronomers. 夜晚星空中的星座图案使古代天文学家着迷。 |  The poet used constellatory imagery in his work. 这位诗人在他的作品中使用了星座意象。 |  stellar, astral | terrestrial | adjective

constellation:/ˌkɒnstəˈleɪʃən/ /ˌkɑːnstəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 星座；一群；一系列 | A group of stars forming a recognizable pattern that is traditionally named after its apparent form or identified with a mythological figure; a group or cluster of related things. | The Big Dipper is a well-known constellation. 北斗七星是一个著名的星座。| A constellation of Hollywood stars attended the premiere. 一群好莱坞明星出席了首映式。 | cluster, collection, assemblage | scattering, dispersal | noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *constellatio, from Latin con- ‘together’ + stella ‘star’.
