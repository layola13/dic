microgyria:/ˌmaɪkroʊˈdʒaɪriə/| n. |小脑回|A condition characterized by abnormal smallness of the convolutions of the brain.|Microgyria can cause seizures and developmental delays.|Synonym: polymicrogyria (although this refers to excessive small gyri, it's often used in similar contexts)|Antonym: pachygyria (abnormally thick gyri)|noun
