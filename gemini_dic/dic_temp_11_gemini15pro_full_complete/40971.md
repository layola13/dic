coops:/kuːps/ | n. |鸡舍；合作社；警察 | a small shed or enclosure for poultry; a cooperative business or enterprise; (slang) police | The chickens were kept in coops behind the house.  鸡被关在房子后面的鸡舍里。 | synonyms: henhouse, chicken coop, cooperative, association | antonyms:  | noun


coops:/kuːps/ | n. |合作，协作 (通常指不正当的) |  Cooperation or collaboration, often implying a secret or underhanded agreement. | The two crooks worked in coops to rob the bank. 两个骗子合伙抢劫银行。 | synonyms: collusion, conspiracy, collaboration | antonyms: competition, rivalry | noun


Etymology:
"Coop" comes from Middle English *coupe*, meaning "cage or basket," borrowed from Old French *coupe*, meaning "tub, cask." The plural "coops" simply adds the regular English plural ending.  The slang meaning "police" may derive from the idea of being "cooped up" in jail.
