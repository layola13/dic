clutch:/klʌtʃ/ | v. n. adj.|抓住，紧握；离合器；掌握，控制|To grasp or hold tightly; a device for connecting and disconnecting a motor vehicle engine to its transmission system; a critical situation.|
He clutched the steering wheel tightly as the car skidded. 他在车打滑时紧紧抓住方向盘。
She held a clutch of flowers in her hand. 她手里拿着一束花。
It was a clutch play that saved the game. 这是挽救比赛的关键一击。
|近义词：grasp, grip, seize, hold, grab|反义词：release, let go, loosen|词性: 动词, 名词, 形容词


clutched:/klʌtʃt/ | adj.|抓紧的，紧握的|Past participle of "clutch," used as an adjective to describe something held tightly.|
He stood there, white-knuckled and clutched. 他站在那里，指关节发白，紧紧地抓住什么东西。
She kept her clutched purse close to her side. 她把她紧紧抓住的钱包放在身边。|近义词：gripped, grasped, held|反义词：released, loosened|词性: 形容词


词源: From Old English *clyccan "to bend, hook," related to clocca "bell."
