marques:/ˈmɑːrkeɪz/ /ˈmɑːrkeɪ/| n.|侯爵（尤指欧洲大陆的贵族）；商标；品牌；（尤指汽车的）型号，款式|A nobleman ranking below a duke and above a count/earl, especially in continental Europe. A manufacturer's mark or brand. A make or model, especially of a car.|The Marques of Queensberry rules are the standard rules for professional boxing.|近义词：brand, make, model, nobleman, peer|反义词:commoner|词性:noun

Etymology: From Old French *marcheis, from marche "frontier, border district" (see march2). Originally denoting a nobleman who ruled a border area.  The sense "manufacturer's trademark" is from the 19th century, from the practice of marking goods with a nobleman's crest.
