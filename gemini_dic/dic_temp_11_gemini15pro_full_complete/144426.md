outsulks:/ˌaʊtˈsʌlks/
verb
超过…的闷闷不乐; 比…更闷闷不乐
to sulk longer or more intensely than someone else.
She outsulked her brother after their argument.  她和兄弟争吵后，比他更闷闷不乐。
He outsulked everyone in the room, refusing to speak for hours. 他比房间里的每个人都更闷闷不乐，几个小时都拒绝说话。
synonyms: out-sulk (rarely used)
antonyms:  out-rejoice (rarely used),  be cheerful, be jovial
词性：verb (动词)

ETYMOLOGY->
"Out-" (prefix denoting exceeding or surpassing) + "sulk" (verb meaning to be silent, morose, and bad-tempered out of annoyance or disappointment).
