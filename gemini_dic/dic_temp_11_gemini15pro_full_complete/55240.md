ETYMOLOGY->

From dis- + similarity, from similar + -ity.  "Dis-" is a Latin prefix meaning "apart," "asunder," "away," expressing negation, reversal, or absence. "Similar" comes from the Latin "similis," meaning "like, resembling, of the same kind." "-ity" is a suffix used to form nouns expressing state or quality.
