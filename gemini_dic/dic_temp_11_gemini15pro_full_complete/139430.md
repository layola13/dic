obsessivenesses:/əbˈsɛsɪvnəsɪz/
|n.|痴迷，着迷；困扰|The state of being obsessive; the characteristic of being preoccupied with something to an excessive degree.|His obsessivenesses with cleanliness made it difficult for him to live with others. 他对清洁的痴迷让他很难与别人一起生活。|Synonyms: fixations, preoccupations, compulsions|Antonyms: indifference, apathy|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From obsessive + -ness.  "Obsessive" comes from the Late Latin "obsessus," past participle of "obsidere" (to besiege, blockade), from "ob-" (against, toward) + "sedere" (to sit).  The "-ness" suffix forms nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition.
