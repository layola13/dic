harum:/ˈhɑːrəm/ | adj. | pleasing to the senses, especially to the smell; fragrant |  Agreeably pungent or aromatic.  Evoking a feeling of pleasantness or delight, especially through the sense of smell. Refers to something delightful or captivating.  | The harum scent of the flowers filled the air.  花朵的芬芳弥漫在空气中。| Synonyms: fragrant, aromatic, sweet-smelling, redolent, balmy | Antonyms: foul, fetid, putrid, noxious, rank | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *harum, likely a variant of *carum (“dear, beloved”), from which also comes Latin cārus.  Related to the English word "charm."
