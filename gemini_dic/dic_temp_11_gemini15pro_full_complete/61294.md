embarrassed: /ɪmˈbærəst/ (US), /ɪmˈbærəst/ (UK)| adj.|尴尬的，窘迫的|Feeling or showing shame or awkwardness.|例句：He was embarrassed when he realized he had forgotten his wallet. 他意识到自己忘记带钱包时感到很尴尬。|例句：She felt embarrassed about her clumsy mistake. 她对自己的愚蠢错误感到很尴尬。|近义词：ashamed, mortified, humiliated, awkward|反义词：confident, comfortable, composed|词性:形容词

embarrass: /ɪmˈbærəs/ (US), /ɪmˈbærəs/ (UK)| vt.|使尴尬，使窘迫|To cause someone to feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed.|例句：Don't embarrass me in front of my friends. 不要在我的朋友面前让我难堪。|例句：His constant questions embarrassed her. 他不停地问问题让她很尴尬。|近义词：humiliate, mortify, shame|反义词：comfort, reassure|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *embarrasser*, from Spanish embarazar "to impede, perplex," from Portuguese embaraçar, from Vulgar Latin *imbarricare* "to bar, block," from Latin *in-* "in" + *barra* "bar."
