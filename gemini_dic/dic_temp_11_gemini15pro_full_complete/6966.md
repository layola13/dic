ETYMOLOGY->
From Proto-Germanic *andwaz (“without sight”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂n̥dʰh₁-o- (“blind”). Cognates include Old English and, Old High German ant, Old Norse andr, Gothic ands.  Ultimately related to Latin ante (“before, in front of”), suggesting an original meaning of “being in front of darkness”.
