anticonformist:/ˌæntiˈkənˈfɔːrmɪst/| n. adj.|反传统者，反从众者|Someone who deliberately behaves in a way that is different from the way most other people behave, especially in order to shock people.|She was an anticonformist in her youth, rejecting societal norms and expectations. 她年轻时是个反传统主义者，拒绝社会规范和期望。|Synonyms: maverick, nonconformist, rebel, iconoclast, dissident|Antonyms: conformist, traditionalist|Noun, Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- (against) + conformist (one who conforms).  The word "conformist" comes from the Latin "conformis" meaning "similar in form."
