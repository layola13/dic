nimrod:/ˈnɪmrɑːd/ (US) /ˈnɪmrɒd/ (UK)| n.|猎人；笨蛋；傻瓜|A great hunter mentioned in the Bible (Genesis 10:8-9); now used humorously or ironically to refer to a clumsy or inept person.  Originally a mighty hunter, the name Nimrod has evolved to mean someone unskilled, especially in hunting. This ironic usage stems from a misinterpretation of the biblical text.|例句：He was such a nimrod that he tripped over his own feet while trying to shoot the rabbit. 他真是个笨蛋，想射兔子的时候居然被自己的脚绊倒了。

例句：Don't be such a nimrod! You've put the tent up backwards. 别这么傻了！你把帐篷搭反了。|近义词：fool, idiot, dunce, klutz, bungler|反义词:expert, skilled hunter, genius|词性:noun
