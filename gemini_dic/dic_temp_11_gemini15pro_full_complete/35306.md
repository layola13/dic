cmet:/ˈsɪmɛt/ | n. |  循环上皮间质转化 (Circular Epithelial-to-Mesenchymal Transition) |  A process where epithelial cells transform into mesenchymal cells and then back to epithelial cells, often observed in wound healing and cancer progression.  It's a circular process distinct from the more common EMT (Epithelial-to-Mesenchymal Transition). | The researchers observed cMET in the wound healing process, where cells transitioned to a mesenchymal state for migration and then reverted back to an epithelial state.  |  近义词:  partial EMT, reversible EMT | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: noun

cmet: /ˈsɪmɛt/ |abbr.|  c-Met (also known as MET or hepatocyte growth factor receptor) | A proto-oncogene that encodes for a receptor tyrosine kinase.  c-Met is often involved in cell growth, motility, and invasion. While 'cmet' by itself isn't a formally recognized abbreviation, it might be used informally to refer to c-Met. |  Elevated cmet expression is often linked to poor prognosis in several cancers. | 近义词:  MET, HGFR | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: abbreviation
