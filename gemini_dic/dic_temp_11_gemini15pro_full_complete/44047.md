cringe:/krɪndʒ/| vi. n.|畏缩，感到尴尬不舒服|To shrink back in fear or embarrassment.  The movie was so bad it made me cringe.|例句：I cringed at his awkward attempt to dance. 我对他笨拙的舞姿感到尴尬。

例句：Seeing the accident made me cringe. 看到事故让我感到畏缩。|近义词：wince, recoil, flinch|反义词：embrace, relish|词性：动词(vi.), 名词(n.)

cringeworthy:/ˈkrɪndʒˌwɜːrði/| adj.|令人感到尴尬的|Causing feelings of embarrassment or awkwardness.|例句：His cringeworthy jokes made everyone uncomfortable. 他令人尴尬的笑话让每个人都不舒服。

例句：The talent show was full of cringeworthy performances. 才艺表演充满了令人尴尬的表演。|近义词：embarrassing, awkward, uncomfortable|反义词：impressive, admirable|词性：形容词(adj.)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cringan "to bend, yield, succumb," of Germanic origin; related to Dutch krimpen "to shrink," German kringeln "to curl." The sense of "to cower in fear" developed in Middle English.
