let-sleeping-dogs-lie:/lɛt ˈsliːpɪŋ dɒgz laɪ/ | idiom | 不要自找麻烦；别没事找事；多一事不如少一事 |  To avoid interfering in a situation that could cause trouble.  It's best to leave things as they are if they might cause problems if you try to change them. | Example: The company decided to let sleeping dogs lie and not pursue the matter any further. 这家公司决定息事宁人，不再追究此事。 | Example:  He knew he could bring up her past mistakes, but he thought it best to let sleeping dogs lie. 他知道可以提起她过去的错误，但他认为最好不要自找麻烦。 | Synonyms: leave well enough alone, don't rock the boat, avoid trouble, keep a low profile | Antonyms: stir the pot, make waves, open a can of worms, provoke, agitate | idiom
