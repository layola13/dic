acculturate: /əˈkʌltʃəreɪt/ | v. | 融入文化，同化 |  To adapt or adjust to a new culture by adopting its values, beliefs, and customs. |  Many immigrants successfully acculturate to their new countries. 许多移民成功地融入了他们的新国家。| The company provides training to help new employees acculturate quickly. 公司提供培训以帮助新员工快速融入。| assimilate, integrate, adapt | resist, segregate | verb

acculturates: /əˈkʌltʃəreɪts/ | v. | 融入文化，同化（第三人称单数形式）|  Third-person singular present tense form of "acculturate," meaning to adapt or adjust to a new culture. | He acculturates quickly to new environments. 他能很快适应新环境。 | She acculturates easily to different social settings. 她很容易适应不同的社会环境。 | assimilates, integrates, adapts | resists, segregates | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin ad- (“to, toward”) + cultura (“cultivation”).  The verb form emerged in the late 19th century.
