malignancy:/məˈlɪɡnənsi/
| n. |恶性肿瘤；癌；恶意|The malignancy spread quickly throughout his body. 恶性肿瘤迅速扩散到他的全身。|The state or presence of a malignant tumor; cancerous growth. Can also refer to a feeling of intense ill will or hatred.|The malignancy of her intentions was clear. 她恶毒的意图显而易见。|近义词：cancer, tumor, carcinoma|反义词: benignity|词性:名词


词源:
From Late Middle English malignite, from Late Latin malignitās (“malignity, wickedness”), from Latin malignus (“ill-disposed, bad, wicked”), from male (“badly”) + genus (“kind, sort, origin”). Doublet of malignity.
