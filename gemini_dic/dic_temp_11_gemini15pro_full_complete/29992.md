cavish:/ˈkeɪvɪʃ/ | adj. | 吹毛求疵的；挑剔的；难以讨好的 | Inclined to find fault or raise petty objections; excessively critical. |  She's so cavish about her food that no one enjoys dining with her. 她对食物太挑剔了，以至于没有人喜欢和她一起吃饭。|例句：He was cavish about the wording of the contract, delaying the signing for several hours. 他对合同的措辞吹毛求疵，拖延了几个小时的签字。| 近义词：critical, fastidious, picky, hypercritical, carping | 反义词：uncritical, tolerant, accepting, easygoing, undemanding |词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cavius*, perhaps from Anglo-Norman *caviller*, to cavil (from Latin *cavillārī*, frequentative of *cavillī*, "to jest, scoff").  The connection between jesting and carping criticism likely lies in the idea of raising frivolous objections.
