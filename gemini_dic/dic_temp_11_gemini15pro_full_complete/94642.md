impressionabilities:/ɪmˌprɛʃənəˈbɪlətiz/
|n.|易受影响性，可印性|The quality of being easily influenced or impressed; susceptibility.|Children's impressionabilities make them vulnerable to advertising.|近义词：suggestibility, susceptibility, plasticity, receptiveness|反义词：resistance, imperviousness|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From impressionable + -ities (suffix forming plural nouns). Impressionable is derived from impression + -able (suffix forming adjectives). Impression comes from the Late Latin impressio, meaning "a pressing in," from the verb imprimere, meaning "to press into."
