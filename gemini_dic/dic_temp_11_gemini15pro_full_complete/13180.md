at-grade:/æt ˈɡreɪd/| adj.|与地面齐平的；同层级的；|At an elevation equal to that of the surrounding ground, typically used in reference to roads and railway lines.  It means that the road or railway is at the same level as the surrounding land, not elevated or depressed.|The at-grade crossing was considered dangerous due to the high volume of traffic.|Synonyms: level, flush, ground-level|Antonyms: elevated, depressed, overhead, underground|adjective


at-grade:/æt ˈɡreɪd/| n.|同层交叉口；平面交叉；|A place where two or more roads or a road and a railway intersect at the same level.|The city planned to replace the at-grade crossing with an overpass.|Synonyms: level crossing, grade crossing, flat crossing|Antonyms: overpass, underpass, flyover|noun
