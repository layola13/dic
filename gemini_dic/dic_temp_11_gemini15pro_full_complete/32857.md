choliamb:/kəˈlaɪæmb/
noun
跛行诗; 跛行体; 抑扬格三音步转五音步
A verse or line of verse having a spondaic substitution in the final foot.
Example:  The poet experimented with choliambs, giving his work a unique rhythm.  诗人尝试了跛行诗，赋予了他的作品独特的节奏。

Example:  Choliambs were often used in satirical poetry. 跛行诗常用于讽刺诗。

Etymology:  From Latin *choliambus*, from Greek *kholiambos*, from *kholoibos* ‘lame iamb’, from *kholos* ‘lame’ + *iambos* ‘iamb’. 源于拉丁语 *choliambus*，来自希腊语 *kholiambos*，源于 *kholoibos* “跛脚的抑扬格”，来自 *kholos* “跛脚的” + *iambos* “抑扬格”。

Synonym:  skazon, hipponactean
Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but could consider regular iambic verse)
word class: noun
