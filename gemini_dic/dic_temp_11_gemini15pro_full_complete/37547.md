commonplace:/ˈkɒmənpleɪs/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmənpleɪs/ (US)| n. adj.| 平凡的事物；陈词滥调；司空见惯的 |A commonplace is something that is ordinary or unremarkable. As an adjective, it describes something as being ordinary and not interesting.|  The speaker filled his speech with commonplaces. 演讲者在他的演讲中充满了陈词滥调。|synonyms: cliché, banality, platitude, bromide, stereotype| antonyms: original, novel, unique, extraordinary, remarkable | noun, adjective
