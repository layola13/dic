niggardliness:/ˈnɪɡərdlinəs/ | n. |吝啬，小气 | Extreme unwillingness to spend money; stinginess. |  His niggardliness prevented him from donating to the charity. 他的吝啬阻止了他向慈善机构捐款。|  stinginess, parsimony, miserliness, meanness | generosity, liberality, munificence | noun

niggardly:/ˈnɪɡərdli/ | adj. |吝啬的，小气的 |  Begrudgingly or stingily given or dispensed; mean. |  He gave a niggardly tip to the waiter. 他给了服务员很少的小费。| stingy, miserly, parsimonious, mean | generous, liberal, munificent | adjective


词源:

From Middle English *nigardlinesse, derived from nigard (“niggard, miser”) +‎ -linesse, equivalent to niggard +‎ -liness.  Ultimately from Old Norse *níggr (“stingy”) (whence also Swedish njugg (“stingy”), Danish nøj (“exacting”)), from Proto-Germanic *knaukaz (“stingy”). More distantly related to Middle High German nouwe (“narrow”), Old English hnēaw (“stingy”).
