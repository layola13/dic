daltonian:/dɔːlˈtəʊniən/ /dɑːlˈtoʊniən/
daltonian| n. adj.| 色盲者；色盲的|A person who is unable to distinguish certain colors, most commonly red and green. Also used as an adjective to describe things related to color blindness.|例句：He's a daltonian and can't tell the difference between red and green. 他是个色盲，分不清红色和绿色。|近义词：color-blind|反义词:|n. & adj.
daltonian:/dɔːlˈtəʊniən/ /dɑːlˈtoʊniən/
daltonian| adj.| 色盲的|Relating to or characteristic of color blindness.|例句：Daltonian vision makes it difficult to choose matching clothes. 色盲使得很难选择搭配的衣服。|近义词：color-blind|反义词:|adj.


ETYMOLOGY->
Named after John Dalton, an English chemist and physicist who himself had color blindness and published the first scientific paper on the subject in 1798.
