alightment:/əˈlaɪtmənt/| n.|点火，点燃；启迪，顿悟|the act of setting something on fire or the state of being lit; a sudden realization or understanding|The pilot confirmed the safe alightment of the aircraft. 飞行员确认飞机安全着陆。|近义词：ignition, illumination, enlightenment, insight|反义词：extinguishing, darkness, confusion|noun

alightment:/əˈlaɪtmənt/| n.|下车，下马；到达，抵达|the act of getting out of a vehicle or the process of landing or arriving|The passengers' alightment from the train was orderly. 乘客们有序地下了火车。|近义词：disembarkation, arrival, landing|反义词：embarkation, departure|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From a- + light (verb) + -ment.  The original meaning referred to the action of lighting a fire or lamp.  The sense of dismounting or landing likely developed from the idea of "settling down" or "coming to rest."
