involution:/ˌɪnvəˈluːʃən/ | n. | 内卷，退化，卷入 |  A decline or degeneration, or the process of becoming complexly involved.  In modern usage, often refers to excessive competition and diminishing returns, particularly in the context of education and employment in East Asia. | The involution of the market led to lower profits for everyone. 市场内卷导致每个人的利润都降低了。| synonyms: regression, degeneration, complication, entanglement | antonyms: evolution, progress, simplification, disentanglement | noun

involute:/ˈɪnvəˌluːt/ | adj. | 内卷的，退化的，卷入的 |  Complexly involved, especially to the point of being difficult to understand. |  The plot of the novel was so involute that I quickly lost interest. 小说的情节太复杂了，我很快就失去了兴趣。 | synonyms: complicated, intricate, complex, entangled | antonyms: simple, straightforward, clear, disentangled | adjective
