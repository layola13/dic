nonsociably:/ˌnɒnˈsoʊʃəbli/ | adv. | 不合群地，孤僻地 |  In a manner that avoids or dislikes being with other people; unsociably. | He lived nonsociably, rarely leaving his apartment. 他过着不合群的生活，很少离开他的公寓。|  He stared nonsociably at the newcomer. 他不合群地盯着新来的人。 | Synonyms: unsociably, aloofly, reclusively | Antonyms: sociably, gregariously | adverb

Etymology:
non- (prefix) + sociable + -ly (suffix)

"non-" is a Latin prefix meaning "not."
"sociable" comes from the Late Latin "sociabilis," meaning "companionable," from "sociare" meaning "to associate," ultimately from "socius" meaning "companion."
"-ly" is a suffix used to form adverbs.
