chauffeuring: /ʃoʊˈfɜːrɪŋ/ /ʃəʊˈfɜːrɪŋ/
chauffeuring: n./v.|驾驶，当司机|The act of driving someone in a vehicle. |He spent the day chauffeuring his boss around town. 他整天开着车载着他的老板在城里转悠。|近义词：driving, transporting|反义词: riding, being driven|词性: Noun/Gerund
chauffeuring: |v.|开车接送，充当司机|Acting as a chauffeur. |She's chauffeuring her children to school this week.  这周她开车送孩子们上学。|近义词：driving, conveying|反义词: riding, being driven|词性: Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French chauffeur ("stoker, driver"), from chauffer ("to heat"), from Old French chaufer, from Vulgar Latin *calfare, from Latin calfacere ("to make warm"), from calidus ("warm") + facere ("to make, do"). The original sense referred to the operator of a steam-powered vehicle.
