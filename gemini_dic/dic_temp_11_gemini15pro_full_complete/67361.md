extensionalisms:/ɪkˌstɛnʃənəˈlɪzəmz/| n. |外延主义|The philosophical doctrine that the meaning of a term is determined solely by its extension, i.e., the set of objects to which it applies.  It emphasizes the actual objects referred to by a concept, rather than the internal meaning or intension of the concept.|例句：Extensionalisms, as opposed to intensionalisms, prioritize the objects a term refers to.  外延主义，与内涵主义相反，优先考虑一个术语所指的对象。

例句：Some philosophers argue that extensionalisms provide a clearer and more objective basis for understanding meaning. 一些哲学家认为，外延主义为理解意义提供了更清晰、更客观的依据。|近义词：extensionalism, denotationalism|反义词:intensionalism|词性:noun
