distemperate:/dɪˈstɛmpərɪt/| adj.|放纵的，无节制的， intemperate, immoderate|Lacking in moderation or self-control, especially in expressing one's feelings or opinions.|His distemperate outburst shocked everyone in the room. 他无节制的爆发让房间里的每个人都感到震惊。|
The journalist wrote a distemperate article criticizing the government.  这位记者写了一篇措辞激烈的文章批评政府。|
Synonyms: intemperate, immoderate, excessive, unrestrained, uncontrolled, inordinate, extreme, undue, extravagant, wild, rash, reckless, impetuous, impulsive, hot-headed, passionate, fiery, vehement, furious, frenzied, frantic, unbridled|
Antonyms: temperate, moderate, restrained, controlled, calm, composed, reasonable, rational, sober, level-headed|
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin distemperatus "disordered," from dis- "not, opposite of" + temperatus "temperate," past participle of temperare "to moderate."
