immobilization: /ɪˌmoʊbɪlɪˈzeɪʃən/ /ɪˌmɒbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 固定；制动；限制 |The act of making something or someone immobile or unable to move.  | The doctor recommended immobilization of the broken arm with a cast. 医生建议用石膏固定骨折的胳膊。

The immobilization of enemy troops allowed our forces to advance.  敌军的固定化使我军得以推进。| 近义词: restriction, fixation, stabilization | 反义词: mobilization, movement, release | 词性: 名词

immobilize: /ɪˈmoʊbɪˌlaɪz/ /ɪˈmɒbɪlaɪz/ | vt. | 使不动；固定；限制 | To prevent something or someone from moving. |  The paramedics immobilized the patient's neck before transporting him to the hospital.  医护人员在将病人送往医院之前固定了他的颈部。

The heavy snow immobilized traffic for several hours. 大雪使交通瘫痪了几个小时。 | 近义词: restrict, confine, restrain | 反义词: mobilize, free, release | 词性: 动词
