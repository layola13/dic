caltrop:/ˈkæltrɒp/ (US) /ˈkæltrəp/ (UK)| n.|蒺藜；铁蒺藜；(阻止车辆通行的)三角钉|A metal object with four sharp points arranged so that when it is thrown on the ground, one point always sticks up, formerly used as a weapon to delay pursuers or injure the feet of enemies; a similar device used to impede the progress of vehicles.|The retreating army scattered caltrops on the road to slow down the enemy's advance.  撤退的军队在路上撒满了铁蒺藜以延缓敌人的前进。|Synonyms: spike, jack, crow's-foot, tribulus|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "smooth surface")|noun

caltrop:/ˈkæltrɒp/ (US) /ˈkæltrəp/ (UK)| n.| (植物)蒺藜|A herbaceous plant of the family Zygophyllaceae, having spiny fruit.|Caltrops are common weeds in arid regions. 蒺藜是干旱地区的常见杂草。|Synonyms: tribulus, puncture vine|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|noun
