hippy:/ˈhɪpi/ (US) /ˈhɪpi/ (UK)| n. |嬉皮士；嬉皮族|A person, typically young, especially during the 1960s and 1970s, who rejected conventional social values and embraced a lifestyle of peace, love, and freedom, often involving communal living, psychedelic drugs, and unconventional dress.|The festival attracted thousands of hippies.|近义词：bohemian, counter-culturalist, flower child|反义词:conformist, traditionalist|词性:noun

hippie:/ˈhɪpi/ (US) /ˈhɪpi/ (UK)| adj. (informal) |嬉皮的；嬉皮士风格的|Characteristic of or relating to hippies.|She wore a hippie dress and sandals.|近义词：bohemian, counter-cultural, flower-power|反义词:conventional, traditional|词性:adjective
