launderer:/ˈlɔːndərər/ (US) /ˈlɔːndrər/ (UK)| n.|洗涤者；洗衣工；洗钱者|A person or company that launders clothes or other items.  A person who conceals the source of illegally obtained money, typically by investing it in legitimate businesses.|例句：The launderer carefully pressed the shirts. 洗衣工仔细地熨烫衬衫。


例句：The authorities are investigating the launderer's complex financial network.  当局正在调查洗钱者的复杂金融网络。

|近义词：cleaner, washerman, money launderer|反义词: (For the cleaning sense) soiler|词性: noun


launder:/ˈlɔːndər/ (US) /ˈlɔːndər/ (UK)| v.|洗涤；清洗；洗钱|To wash, dry, and iron clothes or other items. To conceal the source of illegally obtained money, typically by investing it in legitimate businesses.|例句：I need to launder my dirty clothes. 我需要洗我的脏衣服。


例句：He was accused of laundering drug money. 他被指控洗黑钱。

|近义词：wash, clean, cleanse, rinse (for cleaning); conceal, disguise (for money laundering)|反义词: soil, dirty (for cleaning)|词性: verb
