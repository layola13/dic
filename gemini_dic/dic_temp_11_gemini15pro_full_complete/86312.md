hebraism:/ˈhiːbreɪɪzəm/ | n. |希伯来语用法；希伯来风格；希伯来习语|A characteristic feature of the Hebrew language; a Hebrew idiom or expression; a mode of thought or expression characteristic of the Hebrews or their literature. |The use of parallelism in the poem is a clear hebraism. 这首诗中平行结构的使用是明显的希伯来语用法。| synonyms: Hebraic idiom, Hebrew expression | antonyms: Hellenism |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Hebraismus, from Ancient Greek Ἑβραϊσμός (Hebraïsmós, “Hebraism”), from Ἑβραῖος (Hebraîos, “Hebrew”).
