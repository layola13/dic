bereave: /bɪˈriːv/ | vt. | 使丧失（尤指亲属），剥夺 | to deprive and make desolate, especially by death; to deprive ruthlessly |  The accident bereaved her of her husband and left her to raise three young children alone.  这场事故夺走了她丈夫的生命，留下她独自抚养三个年幼的孩子。| synonyms: deprive, strip, rob, dispossess | antonyms: provide, give, endow, enrich | verb

bereaved: /bɪˈriːvd/ | adj. | 丧失亲人的 | deprived of a loved one through a profound absence, especially due to the loved one's death. | The bereaved family gathered for the funeral.  失去亲人的家庭成员聚集在一起参加葬礼。| synonyms: grieving, mourning, sorrowful | antonyms: joyful, happy, celebratory | adjective

bereavement: /bɪˈriːvmənt/ | n. | 丧亲之痛，丧失 | the state or fact of being bereaved or deprived of something or someone valued |  She experienced a profound sense of bereavement after her mother's death. 她母亲去世后，她经历了深深的丧亲之痛。| synonyms: loss, grief, mourning, deprivation | antonyms: gain, acquisition, blessing | noun
