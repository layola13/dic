lawmen: /ˈlɔːmən/ (US), /ˈlɔːmɛn/ (UK)
n.
执法人员，警官
Law enforcement officers, especially police officers or sheriffs.
The lawmen chased the bandits through the desert.  执法人员在沙漠中追捕土匪。
The town's lawmen were quick to respond to the robbery.  镇上的警察对抢劫案反应迅速。
近义词: police officers, policemen, cops, sheriffs, deputies
反义词: criminals, outlaws, lawbreakers
词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old English *lagu-mann (“law-man”), from lagu (“law”) + mann (“man”).  The word "law" ultimately derives from Proto-Germanic *lagō ("something laid down or fixed"), while "man" comes from Proto-Germanic *mann- (“man, person”).
