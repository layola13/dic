eclipsing:/ɪˈklɪpsɪŋ/| adj. |蚀的，超越的，使黯然失色的|Surpassing; exceeding; obscuring.|The athlete's performance was eclipsing, breaking all previous records.  这位运动员的表现令人叹为观止，打破了所有以前的记录。|例句：Her beauty eclipsed everyone else at the party. 她的美貌使晚会上所有的人都黯然失色。|近义词：surpassing, exceeding, overshadowing, outshining|反义词：inferior, lesser, subordinate|词性:形容词

eclipsing:/ɪˈklɪpsɪŋ/| n. |日食，月食，黯然失色|The act of obscuring or surpassing something.|The eclipsing of the sun by the moon is a rare astronomical event. 日月食是一种罕见的天文现象。|例句：The scandal resulted in the eclipsing of his political career.  这起丑闻导致了他的政治生涯的黯然失色。|近义词：obscuring, overshadowing|反义词：illumination, highlighting|词性:名词
