galloping:/ˈɡæləpɪŋ/ | adj.  v. (present participle of gallop) | 奔驰的，飞驰的；急速发展的 | moving or developing very quickly; (of a horse) running at the fastest pace, with all four feet off the ground at some points |  The horse was galloping across the field.  马在田野里飞奔。| Synonyms: racing, dashing, bolting, sprinting | Antonyms: crawling, creeping, plodding | adjective/verb

galloping:/ˈɡæləpɪŋ/ |  v. (present participle of gallop) | (马)飞奔，疾驰；快速发展 | (of a horse) to run at the fastest pace, with all four feet off the ground at some points; to increase or develop very quickly. | Inflation is galloping at an alarming rate. 通货膨胀正以惊人的速度增长。 | Synonyms: racing, dashing, bolting, sprinting | Antonyms: crawling, creeping, plodding | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *galoper, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *kalpa (“to gallop”).
