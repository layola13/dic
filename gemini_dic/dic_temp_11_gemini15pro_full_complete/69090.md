faust:/faʊst/| n.|浮士德，魔鬼，浮士德博士|A protagonist who makes a pact with the devil, typically exchanging his soul for knowledge, power, or other desires.  It originates from the legendary figure of Johann Georg Faust, a German astrologer, alchemist, and magician of the 16th century.|例句：He made a Faustian bargain for fame and fortune. 他为了名利而做了浮士德式的交易。

例句：The story of Faust is a cautionary tale about the dangers of unchecked ambition. 浮士德的故事是一个警示性的故事，讲述了不加控制的野心的危险。|近义词：mephistopheles, satan, devil|反义词:angel, god|Noun
