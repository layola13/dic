convocation:/ˌkɒnvəˈkeɪʃn/ /ˌkɑːnvəˈkeɪʃn/
n.|会议，集会；召集，召开|A large formal assembly of people.  A ceremony at a university, college, or school, during which degrees or diplomas are conferred.|例句：The university convocation was held in the grand hall. 大学毕业典礼在大会堂举行。

例句：The president called for an emergency convocation of Congress. 总统要求召开紧急国会会议。
|近义词：assembly, gathering, meeting, congress, conference|反义词：dispersal, adjournment|词性:名词

convoke:/kənˈvoʊk/
v.|召集，召开|To call together or summon (an assembly or meeting).|例句：The chairman convoked a meeting of the board of directors. 董事长召集了董事会会议。

例句：The king convoked Parliament to discuss the crisis. 国王召开议会讨论危机。
|近义词：summon, assemble, convene|反义词：dismiss, adjourn|词性:动词
