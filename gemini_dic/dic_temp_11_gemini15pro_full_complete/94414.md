implicature: /ˈɪmplɪkətʃər/ | n. | 含意; 言外之意; 暗示 | An implicature is something the speaker suggests or implies with an utterance, even though it is not literally expressed.  It's the intended meaning beyond the literal words spoken. |  A: "Are you going to John's party?" B: "I have to work." (Implicature: B is not going to the party.) | 近义词: implication, suggestion, innuendo, hint | 反义词: explicit statement, direct meaning | noun

Etymology:  Derived from the verb "imply" (from Old French *implier, from Latin implicare "to involve, entangle, enfold"), with the suffix "-ature" used to form nouns denoting an action or result.
