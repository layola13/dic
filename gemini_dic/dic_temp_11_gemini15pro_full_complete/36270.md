cohabitation:/ˌkəʊˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən/ |n.|同居，同栖|The increasing rate of cohabitation is a significant social trend. 同居率的上升是一个重要的社会趋势。  Cohabitation before marriage is becoming more common. 婚前同居变得越来越普遍。|近义词：living together, shacking up|反义词：marriage, single life|noun

cohabit:/ˌkəʊˈhæbɪt/ |v.|同居，同栖|They cohabited for five years before getting married. 他们结婚前同居了五年。 Many young couples choose to cohabit before making a long-term commitment. 许多年轻夫妇选择在做出长期承诺之前同居。|近义词：live together, shack up|反义词：separate, live apart|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *cohabitātiōn- (stem of cohabitātiō), from Latin cohabitāre "to dwell together," from com- "together" + habitāre "to dwell."
