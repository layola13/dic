airling:/ˈeərlɪŋ/| n.|小鸟，雏鸟；空中精灵|A young bird, especially one that has recently left the nest; a creature of the air, often with connotations of delicacy or ethereal beauty.|The airling took its first flight from the nest. 这只雏鸟第一次从巢中飞出。|The myth spoke of airlings who guarded the mountain peaks.  神话中讲述了守护山峰的空中精灵。|近义词：fledgling, chick, sprite, fairy|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly "land-bound creature")|词性:noun
Etymology: air + -ling (diminutive suffix). "Air" comes from Old French "air" and ultimately from Latin "aer". "-ling" is a native English suffix.
