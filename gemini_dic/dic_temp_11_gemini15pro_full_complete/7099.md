anesthetize: /əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪz/ (US) /əˈniːsθətaɪz/ (UK)| vt.|麻醉，使麻木|To make a person or an animal unable to feel pain, usually by giving them a drug before a medical operation.|The vet anesthetized the cat before the surgery. 兽医在手术前给猫麻醉了。|Synonyms: numb, desensitize, sedate|Antonyms: stimulate, arouse|Verb

anesthesia: /ˌænəsˈθiːʒə/ (US) /ˌænəsˈθiːziə/ (UK)| n.|麻醉，麻醉状态|Loss of feeling or sensation, especially the loss of pain, induced by drugs or other means.|The patient was under general anesthesia during the operation. 病人在手术期间处于全身麻醉状态。|Synonyms: numbness, insensibility|Antonyms: sensation, feeling|Noun

anesthetic: /ænəsˈθɛtɪk/ (US) /ænəsˈθɛtɪk/ (UK)| n. adj.|麻醉剂，麻醉的|A substance that causes anesthesia.|The dentist used a local anesthetic before filling the cavity. 牙医在补牙前使用了局部麻醉剂。|The anesthetic wore off after a few hours. 麻醉药几个小时后就失效了。|Synonyms: narcotic, opiate (n.); numbing, analgesic (adj.)|Antonyms: stimulant (n.); stimulating (adj.)|Noun, Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *anesthésier*, from Greek *anaisthesia* "lack of sensation," from *an-"* not + *aisthēsis* "sensation," from *aisthanomai* "I perceive."
