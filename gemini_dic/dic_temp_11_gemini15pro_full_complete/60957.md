elf:/ɛlf/ |n.|精灵，小妖精|A small, often mischievous, imaginary being with magical powers, usually depicted as human in form but with pointed ears.|She believes in elves and fairies. 她相信精灵和仙女的存在。|Synonyms: fairy, pixie, sprite, goblin|Antonyms: giant, ogre|Noun


elf:/ɛlf/ |n.|顽皮的孩子，小淘气|A mischievous child.|He's a little elf, always getting into trouble. 他是个小淘气，总是惹麻烦。|Synonyms: imp, rascal, scamp|Antonyms: angel|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ælf (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *albaz, of uncertain origin.  Cognate with German *Alp "elf, nightmare," Old Norse álfr "elf."
