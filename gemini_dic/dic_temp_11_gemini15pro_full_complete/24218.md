bridgeheads:/ˈbrɪdʒˌhɛdz/| n.|桥头堡|A fortified position established on the enemy's side of a river or other obstacle, especially as a starting point for further advances.|The troops established a bridgehead on the opposite bank.|Synonyms: foothold, lodgement|Antonyms: retreat, withdrawal|noun
bridgeheads:/ˈbrɪdʒˌhɛdz/| n.|据点;立足点|A position that has been gained and can be used as a base for further progress.|The company has established a bridgehead in the European market.|Synonyms: foothold, base, starting point|Antonyms: disadvantage, weakness|noun
