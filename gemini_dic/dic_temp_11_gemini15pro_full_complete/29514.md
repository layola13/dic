catastrophically:/ˌkætəˈstrɒfɪkəli/
| adv. |灾难性地；灾祸性地|In a way that involves or results in great suffering or damage.|The city was catastrophically damaged by the earthquake.  这座城市在地震中遭受了灾难性的破坏。|Example: The dam failed catastrophically, flooding the valley below. 大坝灾难性地溃决，淹没了下面的山谷。|Synonyms: disastrously, ruinously, devastatingly|Antonyms: beneficially, fortunately, auspiciously|词性:副词

catastrophic:/ˌkætəˈstrɒfɪk/
| adj. |灾难性的；灾祸的|Involving or causing sudden great damage or suffering.|The oil spill had a catastrophic effect on the environment. 石油泄漏对环境造成了灾难性的影响。|Example: A catastrophic fire destroyed the historic building. 一场灾难性的大火摧毁了这座历史建筑。|Synonyms: disastrous, calamitous, tragic|Antonyms: fortunate, lucky, beneficial|词性:形容词

catastrophe:/kəˈtæstrəfi/
| n. |大灾难；灾祸|An event causing great and often sudden damage or suffering; a disaster.|The hurricane was a catastrophe for the small island nation. 飓风对这个小岛国来说是一场灾难。|Example: The loss of their home in the fire was a catastrophe for the family. 在火灾中失去家园对这个家庭来说是一场灾难。|Synonyms: disaster, calamity, tragedy|Antonyms: triumph, success, blessing|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *catastrophique*, from Ancient Greek καταστροφή (katastrophḗ) meaning "overthrow, sudden turn," from κατά (katá) meaning "down, against" + στροφή (strophḗ) meaning "a turning."  最终源自希腊语，kata 意为“向下，反对”，strophe 意为“转折”。
