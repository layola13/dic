beeve:/biːv/
noun
肉用牛; 屠宰的牛
An animal, especially a cow or bull raised for its meat.
The farmer sold his prize beeve at the market. 农民在市场上卖掉了他的获奖肉牛。
synonyms: ox, steer, bullock, cow
antonyms: None (Beeve refers to a specific type of animal, not a quality with an opposite)
n.


beeve:/biːv/
verb (archaic or dialectal)
抱怨; 诉苦
To complain; to grumble.
"He beeved about the long hours and low pay."  他抱怨工作时间长、工资低。
synonyms: complain, grumble, whine, moan
antonyms: praise, commend, applaud
v.



ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *beve*, from Old English *bēof*, from Proto-Germanic *bewaz* (compare West Frisian *boef*, Dutch *boef* (“knave, rogue”), German *Bube* (“boy, knave”)), from Proto-Indo-European *gʷṓws* (“cow, ox”).  The meaning shifted from "ox" to "beef" over time.
