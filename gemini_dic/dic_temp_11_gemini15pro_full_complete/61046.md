elizabethanism: /ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθənɪzəm/
n.
伊丽莎白时代风格；伊丽莎白时代精神
The characteristics of the culture of England during the reign of Queen Elizabeth I (1558–1603).  This period is characterized by a flourishing of English literature, especially in drama (Shakespeare) and poetry (Spenser). It also saw significant developments in music, architecture, and exploration.
The Globe Theatre is a perfect example of Elizabethanism in architecture.  环球剧场是伊丽莎白时代建筑的完美典范。
近义词: Renaissance, Elizabethan era
反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider Victorianism or another specific era)
词性: noun
