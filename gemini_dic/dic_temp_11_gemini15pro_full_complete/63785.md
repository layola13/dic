epicurism: /ˈɛpɪkjʊərɪzəm/ | n. | 享乐主义, 美食主义 | The pursuit of pleasure, especially in reference to food and drink; the philosophy of Epicurus.  It often implies a refined or discriminating taste in such pleasures. | He indulged in epicurism, savoring every bite of the gourmet meal. 他沉溺于享乐主义，细细品味着每一口美食。 |  Example 2:  Her epicurism was evident in her extensive wine cellar and her love of fine dining. 她的享乐主义体现在她丰富的酒窖和对美食的热爱。| 近义词: hedonism, sensualism, luxury, indulgence | 反义词: asceticism, abstinence, self-denial | 词性: noun

Etymology: From French *épicurisme*, from Late Latin *Epicurismus*, from Greek *Epikouros* (Epicurus), the name of the Greek philosopher.
