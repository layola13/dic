grander:/ˈɡrændər/| adj.|更宏伟的，更壮丽的，更堂皇的；更重要的；更显赫的|Comparative adjective of grand.  Meaning more impressive or magnificent than something else; of greater importance, distinction, or stature.|The new concert hall is grander than the old one.  新音乐厅比旧的更宏伟。|Synonyms: more magnificent, more imposing, more stately, more impressive, loftier, more august|Antonyms: smaller, less impressive, humbler, simpler|adjective

grander:/ˈɡrændər/| n.|宏伟；壮丽；堂皇；重要；显赫|The noun form, less commonly used, referring to the state of being grander.|The grander of the new building impressed everyone. 新建筑的宏伟给每个人都留下了深刻的印象。|Synonyms: magnificence, grandeur, splendor, majesty, stateliness|Antonyms: smallness, humbleness, simplicity|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *grander*, comparative of *grand*, from Old French *grand*, from Latin *grandis* ("large, great, tall, grown up"), perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰrew- (“to grow”).
