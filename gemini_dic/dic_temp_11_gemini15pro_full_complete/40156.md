contemptuousnesses:/kənˌtɛmptʃuəsnɪsɪz/
|n.|轻蔑；傲慢；鄙视|The state of being contemptuous; disdainfulness.|His contemptuousnesses towards his colleagues created a hostile work environment. 他对同事的轻蔑造成了敌对的工作环境。|Synonyms: disdain, scorn, arrogance, haughtiness, superciliousness|Antonyms: respect, admiration, humility, deference|noun


contemptuous:/kənˈtɛmptʃuəs/
|adj.|轻蔑的；鄙视的；傲慢的|Showing contempt; scornful.|She gave him a contemptuous look. 她轻蔑地看了他一眼。|Synonyms: disdainful, scornful, sneering, disrespectful, insulting|Antonyms: respectful, admiring, humble, deferential|adjective


contempt:/kənˈtɛmpt/
|n.|轻蔑；蔑视；鄙视|The feeling that a person or a thing is beneath consideration, worthless, or deserving scorn.|He looked at the politician with contempt. 他轻蔑地看着那个政客。|Synonyms: disdain, scorn, disrespect, derision, mockery|Antonyms: respect, admiration, reverence, esteem|noun


Etymology:
From Late Latin *contemptuosus, from Latin contemptus (“contempt”), past participle of contemnere (“to despise, scorn”), from com- (“together”) + temnere (“to slight, despise”).
