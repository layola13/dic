clowned:/klaʊnd/ | v. | 扮演小丑；胡闹；嘲笑 | acted like a clown; behaved foolishly; made fun of | He clowned around in the classroom, making everyone laugh. 他在教室里胡闹，逗得大家哈哈大笑。 |  She clowned him mercilessly for his terrible singing. 她毫不留情地嘲笑他糟糕的歌声。 | synonyms: joked, fooled, mocked, ridiculed | antonyms: behaved, respected, honored | verb


clowned:/klaʊnd/ | adj. | 滑稽的；小丑般的 |  funny, clown-like (less common as an adjective) |  His clowned antics amused the children. 他滑稽的动作逗乐了孩子们。 |  He wore a clowned expression, trying to hide his disappointment. 他装出一副滑稽的表情，试图掩饰他的失望。 | synonyms: comical, ludicrous, farcical | antonyms: serious, solemn, grave | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From "clown" (n.), which comes from Middle English *cloune*, meaning "rustic, boor, peasant," of uncertain origin, perhaps Scandinavian.  The verb sense developed later, likely in the 16th century.
