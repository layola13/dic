kidnap: /ˈkɪdnæp/ (US), /ˈkɪdnæp/ (UK)| vt. n.|绑架，劫持|To unlawfully seize and detain a person, typically for ransom.|The gang kidnapped the businessman's daughter and demanded a million dollars.  这伙匪徒绑架了商人的女儿，并索要一百万美元。|近义词：abduct, seize, capture|反义词: release, free|词性: 动词, 名词

kidnapping: /ˈkɪdnæpɪŋ/ (US), /ˈkɪdnæpɪŋ/ (UK)| n.|绑架，绑架案|The act of kidnapping someone.|The kidnapping of the child shocked the entire community.  孩子的绑架案震惊了整个社区。|近义词：abduction, seizure|反义词: liberation|词性: 名词

词源:  From kid (meaning child, in slang) + nap (meaning to snatch).  源自kid（俚语，意为孩子）+ nap（意为抢夺）。

He kidnaps wealthy tourists for ransom. 他绑架富有的游客以勒索赎金。
She sometimes kidnaps pets and returns them for a reward. 她有时会绑架宠物，然后以换取奖励的方式归还它们。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
The police are kidnapping the suspect now. 警方正在抓捕嫌疑人。 (Note: This usage is less common and could be considered figurative, implying forceful apprehension.)
