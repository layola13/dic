bifurcate:/ˈbaɪfərkeɪt/ (US), /ˈbaɪfəkeɪt/ (UK)| v. adj.|分叉，分岔|to divide into two branches or forks; forked, branched|The river bifurcates into two smaller streams a few miles north of the town. 这条河在小镇以北几英里处分岔成两条小溪。|近义词：fork, divide, split, branch|反义词:merge, join, unite|词性:动词，形容词
bifurcate:/ˈbaɪfərkeɪt/ (US), /ˈbaɪfəkeɪt/ (UK)| n.|分叉，分岔|the point of forking or dividing|The bifurcation of the main road led to two distinct villages.  主干道的分叉通向两个不同的村庄。|近义词：fork, division, split, branch|反义词:merging, junction, union|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin bifurcatus, past participle of bifurcare “to divide into two forks or branches,” from bi- “two” + furca “fork.”
