cockled:/ˈkɒkəld/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkəld/ (US)| adj.|起皱的，翘曲的；(指贝壳)有凹凸的；(指表面)不平整的|Wrinkled, warped, or uneven, especially of a surface or shell.  Often used to describe the appearance of something that has been shrunk or distorted by heat or moisture. Also, can metaphorically describe something disturbed or ruffled, like emotions.|The pages of the old book were cockled with age. 这本旧书的书页因年代久远而卷曲。|The intense heat cockled the paint on the metal door.  高温使得金属门上的油漆起皱了。|The cockled surface of the desert stretched before them.  起伏不平的沙漠表面在他们面前延伸。|The child's cockled brow showed his confusion.  孩子皱起的眉头显示出他的困惑。|Synonyms: wrinkled, warped, buckled, crumpled, ruffled, uneven|Antonyms: smooth, flat, even|adjective
