coelacanths:/ˈsiːləkænθs/| n. |腔棘鱼|A rare order of fish (Coelacanthiformes) that includes two extant species of the family Latimeriidae, known as coelacanths.  They are known as "living fossils" because they were thought to be extinct until one was discovered in 1938.|The coelacanths are found in deep waters off the coast of Africa. 腔棘鱼生活在非洲海岸附近的深水区。| The discovery of the coelacanth was a major scientific event. 腔棘鱼的发现是一项重大的科学事件。|Synonyms: Latimeria (genus name), living fossil|Antonyms: None|Noun
