mere:/mɪr/| adj.|仅仅的，只不过的；纯粹的|Being a mere child, he wasn't allowed to stay up late.  只是一个孩子，他不允许熬夜。|Example: It's a mere scratch.  这只不过是擦伤。|Synonyms: simple, pure, only, sheer, bare|Antonyms: major, significant, important|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mere*, from Old English *mǣre* (“famous, glorious, excellent, splendid, beautiful, noble”), from Proto-Germanic *mērijaz* (“famous, renowned”), from Proto-Indo-European *mēryos* (“famous”). Cognate with Old Frisian *mēre* (“famous, renowned”), Old Saxon *mǣri* (“famous, renowned, celebrated”), Old High German *mâri* (“famous, renowned, celebrated, excellent”), Old Norse *mærr* (“famous, renowned”), Gothic *𐌼𐌴𐍂𐍃* (*mērs*, “famous”). More at mayor.  The meaning shifted from “pure, unmixed, sheer” to “barely, only” in Middle English.
