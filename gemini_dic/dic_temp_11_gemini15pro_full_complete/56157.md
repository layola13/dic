dodder:/ˈdɑdər/ (US) /ˈdɒdə(r)/ (UK)| n. vi.|菟丝子；蹒跚；摇晃|A leafless parasitic plant with threadlike stems that attach to and obtain nutrients from a host plant.  To tremble or totter, typically because of old age.|The dodder had completely engulfed the bush. 菟丝子完全吞没了灌木丛。|The old man doddered across the street. 老人蹒跚地穿过街道。|Synonyms: twine, entwine,  totter,  stagger|Antonyms: detach, separate, stand firm, walk steadily|noun, verb
