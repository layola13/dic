covetousnesses:/ˈkʌvɪtəsnɪsɪz/

The plural form of covetousness emphasizes multiple instances or manifestations of this desire.|His covetousnesses knew no bounds, desiring everything he saw, from his neighbor's car to the crown jewels.|近义词：greed, avarice, rapacity, cupidity|反义词：generosity, contentment, altruism|词性: noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *coveitousnes*, from Old French *coveitous*, from Late Latin *cupiditosus*, from Latin *cupidus* "eager, passionate," from *cupere* "to desire."  The "-ness" suffix transforms the adjective into a noun indicating the state or quality of being covetous.
