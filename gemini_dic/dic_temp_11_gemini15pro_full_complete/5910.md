ambiguously:/æmˈbɪɡjuəsli/ | adv. | 模棱两可地，含糊不清地 | In a way that is open to more than one interpretation; unclearly or inexactly. | She answered the question ambiguously, leaving everyone confused. 她模棱两可地回答了这个问题，让每个人都很困惑。|  The contract was worded ambiguously, leading to disputes later on.  合同措辞含糊不清，后来引起了争议。 | vaguely, unclearly, equivocally, obscurely | clearly, explicitly, definitely, precisely | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *ambiguosus, from Latin ambiguus (“doubtful, uncertain”), from ambigere (“to wander about, waver, hesitate, doubt”), from ambi- (“around, about”) + agere (“to drive, lead, do, act”).
