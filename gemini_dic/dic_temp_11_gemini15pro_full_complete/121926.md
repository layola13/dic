misshapennesses:/ˌmɪsˈʃeɪpənɪsnɪsɪz/| n. |畸形，变形|The state of being misshapen; a collection of misshapen things or features.  It refers to multiple instances of something being deformed or out of its normal shape.|The artist was fascinated by the misshapennesses of the ancient trees, finding beauty in their gnarled branches.  这位艺术家对古树的畸形感到着迷，在它们粗糙的树枝中发现了美。|Synonyms: deformities, malformations, distortions|Antonyms: symmetries, perfections|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From misshapen + -ness. Misshapen itself comes from the Old English "mis-" (meaning "wrongly" or "badly") + "shapen" (past participle of "shape").  Therefore, misshapennesses signifies the state or quality of being wrongly shaped, often occurring in multiple instances.
