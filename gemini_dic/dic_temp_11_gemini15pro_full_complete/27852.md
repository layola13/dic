canonizations: /ˌkænənɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. |册封圣徒，认可为经典|The act of officially declaring someone a saint, or recognizing something as being of high quality and lasting importance. |The canonizations of the new saints took place in a grand ceremony. 新圣徒的册封仪式隆重举行。| 近义词: sanctifications, glorifications, recognitions | 反义词: condemnations, denunciations | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Late Latin canonizatio, from canonizare "to canonize," from canon "church law; rule."
