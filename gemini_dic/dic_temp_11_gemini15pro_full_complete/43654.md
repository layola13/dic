creature-comfort:/ˈkriːtʃər ˈkʌmfərt/ (US), /ˈkriːtʃə ˈkʌmfət/ (UK)| n.|生活舒适；物质享受|Something that contributes to physical ease and well-being.|例句：After a long day's hike, a hot shower and a soft bed were pure creature comforts.  经过一天的长途跋涉，热水淋浴和柔软的床铺是纯粹的物质享受。

例句：He enjoys the creature comforts of a luxury hotel. 他喜欢豪华酒店的舒适享受。|近义词：luxury, amenity, convenience|反义词：hardship, privation|词性:名词

creature:/ˈkriːtʃər/|n.|生物；动物；人|A living being, especially an animal.|例句：All creatures great and small.  所有生物，无论大小。

例句：The Loch Ness Monster is a mythical creature. 尼斯湖水怪是一个神话生物。|近义词：animal, being, organism|反义词：-|词性:名词

comfort:/ˈkʌmfərt/|n. vt.|舒适；安慰；安慰者|A state of physical ease and freedom from pain or constraint.  The act of giving strength and hope to someone.|例句：The hotel offers every comfort. 这家酒店提供各种舒适设施。

例句：She comforted the crying child. 她安慰了哭泣的孩子。|近义词：solace, consolation, ease|反义词：discomfort, distress|词性:名词；动词
