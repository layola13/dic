filler:/ˈfɪlər/ (US), /ˈfɪlə(r)/ (UK)| n.|填充物，填料；（广播或电视节目的）填充节目；（食物中的）填充剂；（绘画、雕塑等的）填料；（织物中的）填充纱|A substance added to something else to make it thicker, larger, stronger, or heavier. |The cushion is filled with a synthetic filler.|近义词：padding, stuffing, packing, additive|反义词：emptying, removal|词性：名词

filler:/ˈfɪlər/ (US), /ˈfɪlə(r)/ (UK)| n.|（时间或空间的）填补物，补白|Something that fills a period of time or space, especially something unimportant or uninteresting. |The TV show was padded with fillers.|近义词：stopgap, placeholder|反义词：main content|词性：名词

filler:/ˈfɪlər/ (US), /ˈfɪlə(r)/ (UK)| n.|(语言中的)填充词，语气词|A word or phrase used to fill a pause in conversation or to give the speaker time to think. |"Um," "like," and "you know" are common fillers.|近义词：hesitation marker, pause filler|反义词：(None -  the absence of fillers implies fluency)|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "fill" + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting agents or things that do something).  "Fill" comes from Old English "fyllan," from Proto-Germanic "*fulljanan," related to "full."
