faintingly:/ˈfeɪntɪŋli/| adv.|微弱地，模糊地，隐约地|In a faint or slight way; indistinctly.|She smiled faintly at him. 她朝他淡淡一笑。|近义词：slightly, weakly, dimly, vaguely|反义词：strongly, clearly, distinctly|词性:副词
Etymology: From faint + -ly.  "Faint" comes from Old French *feint, past participle of feindre "to feign, pretend," from Latin fingere "to shape, form, invent, feign."  The adverbial suffix "-ly" derives from Old English "-lice," meaning "like," "having the form or manner of."
