moralists:/ˈmɔːrəlɪsts/ /ˈmɔrəlɪsts/| n. |道德家，伦理学家|A person who teaches or advocates morality.|The moralists of the Victorian era were very strict. 维多利亚时代的道德家非常严格。|近义词：ethicists, preachers|反义词:immoralists, libertines|词性:noun


Etymology->
From Middle French *moraliste*, from moralité (“morality”) + -iste (“-ist”), ultimately from Late Latin mōrālis (“moral”) + -ista (“-ist”).
