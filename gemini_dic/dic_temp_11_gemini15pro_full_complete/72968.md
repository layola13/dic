fool:/fuːl/
n.|傻瓜，愚人；受骗者|A person who acts unwisely or is easily tricked.|He was a fool to trust her. 他信任她真是个傻瓜。
fool:/fuːl/
v.|愚弄，欺骗；开玩笑|To trick or deceive someone.|She fooled him into believing she was rich. 她骗他相信她很富有。
fool:/fuːl/
adj.|愚蠢的|Foolish or silly.|That was a fool thing to do. 那是一件愚蠢的事情。

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French fol, from Late Latin follis (“bellows, windbag”), from Latin follis (“leather bag, bellows”).  The semantic development is from "inflated, puffed up" to "empty-headed, foolish."
