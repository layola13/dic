disadvantageousness:/ˌdɪsədvænˈteɪdʒəsnəs/| n.|不利，劣势|The disadvantageousness of the location hindered the business's growth.  这个位置的不利条件阻碍了业务的增长。|例句：The disadvantageousness of his position made it difficult to win the election. 他所处位置的劣势使得他很难赢得选举。

The disadvantageousness of the terrain made it hard for the army to advance.  这片地形的不利条件使军队难以推进。|近义词：unfavorableness, detrimentalness, handicap, drawback |反义词: advantageousness, benefit, asset |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From disadvantage + -ous + -ness.  Disadvantage comes from the Old French desavantage, meaning "loss, detriment."  The suffix "-ous" forms adjectives meaning "full of" or "having the qualities of," and "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives.
