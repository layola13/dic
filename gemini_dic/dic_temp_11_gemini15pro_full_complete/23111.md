bourbonisms:/ˈbɜːrbənɪzəmz/
n.|波旁主义；波旁王朝的习俗或特征|Characteristic features, customs, or principles of the Bourbon dynasty, especially as associated with Kentucky. |The Kentucky Derby is full of bourbonisms, from mint juleps to big hats.|近义词: traditions, customs, practices|反义词: modernisms, innovations|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From Bourbon + -ism.  Bourbon refers to the House of Bourbon, a European royal house.  In the US context, it's strongly associated with Kentucky and its bourbon whiskey production, leading to the term being used to describe cultural elements associated with the state and its history.
