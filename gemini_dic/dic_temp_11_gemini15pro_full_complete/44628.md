ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crowinge*, verbal noun from *crowen* (“to crow”), from Old English *crāwan* (“to crow”), from Proto-Germanic *krōaną* (“to crow”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱrewh₂- (“to croak, caw”). Cognate with Dutch *kraaien* (“to crow”), German *krähen* (“to crow”), Danish *kraae* (“to crow”).
