highfaluting: /ˌhaɪfəˈluːtɪŋ/ | adj. | 浮夸的；言过其实的；高谈阔论的 | Pompous or extravagant in language, style, or manner, especially in a way that is intended to impress.  Characterized by pretentious, inflated, or bombastic language. |  He delivered a highfaluting speech that no one understood. 他发表了一篇没有人能理解的浮夸的演讲。 |  The critic dismissed the novel as highfaluting and shallow.  评论家认为这部小说华而不实，内容肤浅。| 近义词: pompous, pretentious, bombastic, inflated, grandiose | 反义词: simple, unpretentious, modest, down-to-earth | 词性: adjective

词源:  From "high-flying" + "-ing," suggesting lofty or extravagant language.  Originally referred to high-flying birds.
