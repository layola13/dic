byzantine:/ˌbɪzənˈtaɪn/ /ˈbɪzənˌtin/ | adj. | 拜占庭式的，错综复杂的，阴谋诡计的 |  Relating to the Byzantine Empire or its highly complex and intricate art, architecture, or culture. Also used to describe something excessively complicated, devious, or secretive. | The plot of the novel was so byzantine that it was difficult to follow. 这部小说的情节错综复杂，难以理解。 | 近义词：complex, intricate, convoluted, devious, Machiavellian | 反义词：simple, straightforward, transparent | 词性: 形容词

byzant:/ˈbɪzænt/ /ˈbɪzənt/ | n. |  拜占庭金币；拜占庭 | A gold coin of the Byzantine Empire. Also, a shortened form of "Byzantine", less common than the adjective form.  |  He found an old byzant in his grandfather's collection. 他在他祖父的收藏中发现了一枚古老的拜占庭金币. | 近义词: Byzantine coin, solidus (for the coin) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but could consider modern currencies) | 词性: 名词
