glitzy: /ˈɡlɪtsi/ | adj. | 浮华的，炫目的 | Ostentatiously glamorous or showy.  Characterized by excessive showiness and glamour, often in a somewhat superficial or vulgar way. |  The actress wore a glitzy dress to the awards ceremony.  这位女演员穿着闪亮的礼服出席了颁奖典礼。|  The hotel lobby was all glitz and glamour. 酒店大堂金碧辉煌。| 近义词: glamorous, flashy, showy, ostentatious | 反义词: understated, simple, plain, modest | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:  Likely a blend of "glitter" and "ritzy," both suggesting luxury and showiness.  "Glitter" comes from Old Norse *glitra "to glitter, sparkle," while "ritzy" is derived from the name of the opulent Ritz hotels.
