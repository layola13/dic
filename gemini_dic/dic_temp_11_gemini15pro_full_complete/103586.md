join hands:/dʒɔɪn hændz/
| v. |携手；合伙；联手；握手|To clasp each other's hands, often as a symbol of unity, agreement, or support.  Also, to work together or collaborate.|The two leaders joined hands on stage, symbolizing the new partnership between their countries. 两国领导人在台上握手，象征着两国之间新的伙伴关系。

The companies joined hands to develop the new software.  这些公司携手开发新的软件。|近义词：collaborate, cooperate, unite, team up|反义词：separate, divide, disagree, part ways|词性:动词

join:/dʒɔɪn/
| v. |加入；连接；参与|To become a member of a group or organization; to connect or link things together; to take part in an activity.|She joined the gym last month. 她上个月加入了健身房。

Join the two pieces of wood with glue. 用胶水把这两块木头粘在一起。 |近义词：connect, link, attach, enter, become a member of|反义词：leave, separate, detach, quit|词性:动词

hands:/hændz/
| n. |手；帮助；掌握|The part of the body at the end of the arm; assistance or control.|Please wash your hands before dinner.  请在晚饭前洗手。

The project is in safe hands.  这个项目在可靠的人手中。|近义词：(for assistance) help, aid, support; (for control) possession, power, authority|反义词：(for assistance) neglect, abandonment; (for control) weakness, impotence|词性:名词
