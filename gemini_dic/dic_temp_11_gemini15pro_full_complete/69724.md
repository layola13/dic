feodatory:/ˈfiːdəˌtɔːri/ (US), /ˈfjuːdəˌtɔːri/ (UK)| n. adj.|封臣；附庸的|A person who holds land from a feudal lord in return for service and loyalty. Also used as an adjective to describe something related to this system.|The duke's feodatories swore allegiance to him. 公爵的封臣们向他宣誓效忠。|近义词：vassal, liegeman, subject, tributary|反义词：lord, overlord|词性: noun, adjective

feodatory:/ˈfiːdəˌtɔːri/ (US), /ˈfjuːdəˌtɔːri/ (UK)| adj.|附庸的；封建的|Relating to or characteristic of a feudal system.|The feodatory system ensured loyalty through land ownership. 封建制度通过土地所有制确保了忠诚。|近义词：feudal, vassal|反义词：independent, sovereign|词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *feudataire*, from Medieval Latin *feudatarius*, from *feudum* ("fief").
