misconstrue:/ˌmɪskənˈstruː/ | v. | 误解，曲解 | To interpret (something, such as a person's words or actions) wrongly. | She misconstrued his silence as anger. 她把他的沉默误解为生气。 | He misconstrued her friendly gesture as a romantic advance. 他误解了她友好的举动，认为是浪漫的表示。 |  misinterpret, misapprehend, misunderstand | understand correctly, interpret correctly | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- + construe.  Mis- is a prefix meaning "wrongly" or "badly." Construe comes from the Latin word construere, meaning "to construct, build, or interpret."  Therefore, misconstrue literally means "to interpret wrongly."
