cato:/ˈkeɪtoʊ/ | n. | 加图（罗马政治家或作家）| A Roman statesman or writer, especially any of the Porcii Catones.  Specifically refers to either Marcus Porcius Cato the Elder (234–149 BC), also known as Cato the Censor, or Marcus Porcius Cato the Younger (95–46 BC), also known as Cato Uticensis. | Cato the Younger was known for his staunch opposition to Julius Caesar. 小加图以坚决反对尤利乌斯·凯撒而闻名。|  近义词: statesman, politician | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps demagogue or tyrant depending on context) | 词性: noun
