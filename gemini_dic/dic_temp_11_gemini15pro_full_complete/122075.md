misthread:/ˌmɪsˈθred/
verb
错误地穿线；穿错线
to thread something incorrectly, especially through the eye of a needle or through a hole
She misthreaded the needle and had to start again. 她把针穿错了，不得不重新开始。
misthread:/ˌmɪsˈθred/
noun
穿错的线
a thread that has been threaded incorrectly
The misthread caused the machine to malfunction. 穿错的线导致机器故障。


ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- + thread.  "Mis-" is a prefix of Old English and Germanic origin meaning "wrong," "bad," or "wrongly." "Thread" comes from Old English "thræd," meaning "cord" or "filament."


SYNONYMS->
(v) mishandle, fumble, botch
(n) error, mistake, blunder


ANTONYMS->
(v) thread correctly, manage well
(n) accuracy, correctness, precision
