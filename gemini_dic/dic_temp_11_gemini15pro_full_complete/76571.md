galliard:/ˈɡæljərd/ (US), /ˈɡæliɑːd/ (UK)| n.|加利亚德舞；加利亚德舞曲|A lively, vigorous Renaissance dance in triple time, often incorporating leaps and other virtuosic movements. It was popular in Europe from the 15th to the 17th centuries.|The musicians played a lively galliard, and the courtiers danced with gusto.  音乐家们演奏了一首活泼的加利亚德舞曲， courtiers们尽情地跳舞。|Synonyms: jig, romp, frolic|Antonyms: waltz, slow dance|noun

galliard:/ˈɡæljərd/ (US), /ˈɡæliɑːd/ (UK)| adj.|（古）快活的，愉快的|Characterized by lively, spirited, or brisk movement; merry or cheerful.  (Archaic)|He had a galliard air about him that was quite infectious. 他有一种欢快的姿态，很有感染力。|Synonyms: lively, merry, cheerful, jovial, sprightly|Antonyms: somber, melancholy, morose|adjective
