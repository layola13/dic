forebode:/fɔːrˈboʊd/ /fɔːˈbəʊd/
verb
预示;预兆;预感(尤指不祥之事)
to act as an advance warning of (something bad)

例句：
The dark clouds forebode a storm.
乌云预示着暴风雨即将来临。

The captain's worried frown foreboded trouble ahead.
船长忧心忡忡的皱眉预示着前方有麻烦。


近义词：presage, portend, augur, foreshadow, predict, omen
反义词：none (lack of foreboding doesn't have a single antonym)
词性：verb

词源：
From Middle English *foreboden, from Old English forebōdian (“to forebode, predict, portend”), from fore- + bōdian (“to announce, proclaim, foretell”).  Related to German vorbedeuten (“to portend, presage”).
