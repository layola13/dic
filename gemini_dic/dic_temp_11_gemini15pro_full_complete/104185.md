junket:/ˈdʒʌŋkɪt/ | n. | 旅行，游玩；(尤指官员的)公费旅游；野餐；宴会 |  A trip or excursion, often one taken for pleasure by an official at public expense; a feast; a picnic. | The senator went on a junket to Hawaii.  参议员到夏威夷公款旅游去了。| Synonyms: excursion, trip, outing, jaunt, expedition | Antonyms: work, business | noun


junket:/ˈdʒʌŋkɪt/ | vi. |  去旅行；进行游览 | To go on a junket; to feast. | They junketted through Europe last summer. 去年夏天他们游遍了欧洲。| Synonyms: travel, tour, journey | Antonyms: stay, remain | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *jonquette "rush basket," from jonc "rush," from Latin juncus. Originally referred to a basket made of rushes, later to delicacies (especially cream cheese) served in such baskets, and then to a feast or picnic.  The sense of a pleasure trip, often at public expense, developed later.
