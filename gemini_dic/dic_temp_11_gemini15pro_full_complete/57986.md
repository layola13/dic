drivel:/ˈdrɪvəl/| n. vi.|胡说；废话；口水；流口水|Silly and meaningless talk or writing; saliva that flows from the mouth.|He was talking such drivel that I just tuned him out. 他净说些废话，我干脆不听他说了。|近义词：nonsense, rubbish, twaddle, balderdash|反义词：sense, wisdom|词性:名词,动词

drivel:/ˈdrɪvəl/| vi.|胡说八道；流口水|To talk nonsense; to let saliva flow from the mouth.|He sat there driveling about the good old days. 他坐在那里胡扯过去的好时光。|近义词：ramble, babble, drool|反义词：articulate, enunciate|词性:动词

词源: From Middle English *drevelen, frequentative of dreven (“to drive, push, urge”), from Old English drīfan (“to drive, push, pursue, compel, urge”). Related to Dutch drijven (“to drive”), German treiben (“to drive”).  The sense of "dribbling saliva" arose from the idea of saliva being "driven" or "flowing" from the mouth.
