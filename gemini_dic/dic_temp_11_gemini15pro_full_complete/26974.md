calcination:/ˌkælˈsɪˈneɪʃən/ |n.|煅烧，焙烧|The process of heating a substance to a high temperature but below the melting or fusing point, causing loss of moisture, volatile matter, or oxidation.|Limestone is subjected to calcination to produce lime. 石灰石经过煅烧生成石灰。|近义词：roasting, burning, firing|反义词：hydration|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *calcinatio, from Latin calcinare "to burn lime," from calx "lime" (see chalk).
