cuttlefish:/ˈkʌtəlˌfɪʃ/| n. |乌贼；墨鱼|A marine mollusk with ten arms, eight short and two long tentacles, and an internal shell (cuttlebone). It can eject ink to confuse predators.|例句：The cuttlefish changed color to blend in with the coral. 乌贼改变颜色以融入珊瑚。

例句：Cuttlefish are known for their intelligence and camouflage abilities. 乌贼以其智力和伪装能力而闻名。|近义词：squid, cephalopod|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

词源：
From Middle English *cuttlefishe, from Old Norse koddi (“cushion, pillow”) + fiskr (“fish”), referring to the cuttlebone.  The Old Norse koddi is related to Old English codd (“bag, pouch”).
来自中古英语 *cuttlefishe，源自古诺尔斯语 koddi（“垫子、枕头”）+ fiskr（“鱼”），指的是乌贼骨。古诺尔斯语 koddi 与古英语 codd（“袋子、小袋”）相关。
