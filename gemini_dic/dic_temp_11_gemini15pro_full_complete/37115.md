combustible:/kəmˈbʌstɪbl/ | adj. n. | 易燃的；可燃物；易动激情的 | Able to catch fire and burn easily; a substance that can be burned. |
The warehouse stored combustible materials, making it a fire hazard. 仓库储存了易燃材料，使其成为火灾隐患。
His combustible temper often led to arguments. 他易怒的脾气经常导致争吵。
| 近义词: flammable, inflammable, ignitable | 反义词: noncombustible, fireproof, inert | 词性: 形容词, 名词

combustion:/kəmˈbʌʃtʃən/ | n. | 燃烧；氧化；骚动 | The process of burning something; rapid chemical combination of a substance with oxygen, involving the production of heat and light. |
The combustion engine powers most cars. 内燃机为大多数汽车提供动力。
The combustion of fossil fuels releases harmful gases. 化石燃料的燃烧释放有害气体。
| 近义词: burning, ignition, oxidation | 反义词: extinguishment | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin combustibilis "that can be burned," from Latin combustus, past participle of comburere "to burn up," from com- "completely" + burere "to burn."
