liege:/liːdʒ/
n.|臣民；诸侯；君主|A person who owes allegiance to a ruler or lord. |The king addressed his lieges.|近义词：subject, vassal, retainer|反义词：ruler, monarch, sovereign|词性:名词

lieges:/ˈliːdʒɪz/
n.|臣民；诸侯；君主（复数）|Plural form of liege, referring to multiple people who owe allegiance to a ruler or lord. |The king's lieges gathered in the courtyard.|近义词：subjects, vassals, retainers|反义词：rulers, monarchs, sovereigns|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French liege, lige (“liege lord, vassal, subject”), from Late Latin *laegius, from Frankish *letig, from Proto-Germanic *leudigaz (“free with respect to one's people”), from *leud- (“people”) + *-igaz (adjective suffix). Cognate with Old High German liut (“people, nation”), Old English lēod (“people, nation, tribe, race”).
