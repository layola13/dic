outlaugh: /ˌaʊtˈlæf/ | v. | 比…笑得更久或更大声;  胜过; |  She outlaughed everyone in the room. 她比房间里的每个人都笑得更久更大声。 | outdo, surpass | - | verb
Etymology:  'out-' (prefix indicating exceeding or surpassing) + 'laugh' (verb).  The prefix "out-"  is of Germanic origin, meaning "outside, beyond." "Laugh" is also of Germanic origin, related to Old English *hliehhan.
