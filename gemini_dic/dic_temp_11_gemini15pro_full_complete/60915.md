elephantiasis: /ˌelɪfənˈtaɪəsɪs/
n.
象皮病，淋巴丝虫病
A chronic disease characterized by the gross enlargement of an area of the body, especially the limbs and genitals, caused by obstruction of the lymphatic vessels.  This obstruction is typically caused by parasitic worms, particularly those of the filarial species.
Elephantiasis can be a debilitating condition, causing significant swelling and disfigurement.  象皮病可能是一种使人衰弱的疾病，会导致严重的肿胀和畸形。

Elephantiasis is a serious health concern in many tropical countries. 象皮病是许多热带国家的一个严重的健康问题。


近义词: lymphedema, filariasis
