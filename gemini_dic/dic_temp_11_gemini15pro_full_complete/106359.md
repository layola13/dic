kolmogorov:/ˌkɒlməˈɡɔːrɒf/ | n. | 科尔莫戈罗夫 |  Andrey Nikolaevich Kolmogorov (1903-1987), a prominent Soviet mathematician, known for his foundational contributions to probability theory, topology, and other areas of mathematics. | The Kolmogorov-Smirnov test is a statistical test used to compare distributions. 科尔莫戈罗夫-斯米尔诺夫检验是一种用于比较分布的统计检验。 | synonyms: (no direct synonym for a person, but related to his work) probabilistic, stochastic | antonyms: N/A | proper noun

kolmogorov complexity:/ˌkɒlməˈɡɔːrɒf kəmˈpleksɪti/ | n. | 科尔莫戈罗夫复杂度 | A measure of the computational resources needed to specify an object, such as a piece of text or a string of bits. It's the length of the shortest computer program that can produce the object. | The Kolmogorov complexity of a random string is typically high. 随机字符串的科尔莫戈罗夫复杂度通常很高。 | synonyms: algorithmic complexity, descriptive complexity | antonyms: N/A | noun

kolmogorov axioms:/ˌkɒlməˈɡɔːrɒf ˈæksiəmz/ | n. | 科尔莫戈罗夫公理 |  The foundational axioms of probability theory, formulated by Andrey Kolmogorov. | Kolmogorov axioms provide a rigorous basis for probability theory. 科尔莫戈罗夫公理为概率论提供了严谨的基础。 | synonyms: probability axioms | antonyms: N/A | noun

kolmogorov equation:/ˌkɒlməˈɡɔːrɒf ɪˈkweɪʒən/ | n. | 科尔莫戈罗夫方程 | A type of differential equation used in probability theory and related fields.  | The Kolmogorov equation describes the evolution of the probability distribution of a random variable. 科尔莫戈罗夫方程描述了随机变量概率分布的演变。 | synonyms: (depending on the specific equation) Fokker-Planck equation, Chapman-Kolmogorov equation | antonyms: N/A | noun
