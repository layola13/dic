curtseyed:/ˈkɜːrtsiːd/ /ˈkɝːtsiːd/| v.|行屈膝礼|Made a curtsy, a gesture of respect or greeting typically performed by women.|She curtseyed to the queen. 她向女王行屈膝礼。|近义词：bowed, genuflected|反义词:stood erect|verb (past tense)


ETYMOLOGY->
From *curtsy* + *-ed*. *Curtsy* is a shortening of *courtesy*, from Old French *curteisie* "courtly behavior," ultimately from Latin *cohors* "courtyard, enclosure."  The shift in meaning from general courtesy to a specific gesture of respect occurred in the 17th century.
