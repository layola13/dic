lice:/laɪs/| n.|虱子|Small, wingless, parasitic insects that live on the skin of mammals and birds and suck their blood.|The dog was infested with lice.|parasites, vermin|None|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lūs (plural *lȳs), from Proto-Germanic *lūz (plural *lūsiz), from Proto-Indo-European *lew- (“louse”). Cognate with Scots louse, West Frisian luus, Dutch luis, German Laus, Danish lus, Swedish lus.
