forlese:/fɔːrˈliːs/ | v. | 失去，丧失（权利、特权等） | To lose or be deprived of (a right, privilege, etc.) by some error, fault, or negligence. | He forlesed his right to the property by failing to pay the taxes. 他因未能缴纳税款而丧失了财产权。 | forfeit, lose, | retain, keep | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *forlesen, from Old English *forlēosan (“to lose, destroy, forfeit”), from Proto-Germanic *fra-lēusaną (“to lose”), equivalent to for- + lose. Cognate with Dutch verliezen (“to lose”), German verlieren (“to lose”).
