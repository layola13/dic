co-inheritor:/ˌkoʊɪnˈherɪtər/
n.
共同继承人
A person who inherits something jointly with another person or other people.
My sister and I are co-inheritors of our grandmother's estate. 我和我的妹妹是我们祖母遗产的共同继承人。
The two brothers were named as co-inheritors of the family business. 这两兄弟被指定为家族企业的共同继承人。
Synonym: joint heir, fellow heir
Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but one could consider "disinherited")
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From co- (together) + inheritor (one who inherits).  "Inherit" comes from Old French enheriter, from Latin in- (in) + heres (heir).
