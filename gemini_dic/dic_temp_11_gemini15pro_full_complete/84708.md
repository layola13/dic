handicapping: /ˈhændɪˌkæpɪŋ/
n.
障碍，不利条件;设置障碍;预测比赛结果;让分盘
The act of assigning a handicap, especially in sports or competitions, to create a more balanced contest.  It can also refer to the process of assessing or predicting the outcome of a competition.  Sometimes used figuratively to describe something that hinders or obstructs progress.
The handicapping system in golf allows players of different abilities to compete fairly.  高尔夫球的让分系统允许不同水平的选手公平竞争。

The bookmaker was busy handicapping the horses for the next race.  博彩公司正忙着为下一场比赛的马匹设置障碍。

His lack of experience is a major handicapping factor in his job search. 他缺乏经验是他找工作的主要障碍。

近义词: hindering, obstructing, impeding, hampering
反义词: aiding, assisting, helping, facilitating
词性: noun


handicap: /ˈhændɪˌkæp/
n.
障碍，不利条件
Something that limits a person's ability to do something.
Her shyness is a handicap in social situations. 她的害羞在社交场合是一个障碍。

v.
妨碍，使不利
To limit someone's ability to do something.
Lack of funding has handicapped the project. 缺乏资金阻碍了该项目。

近义词: disadvantage, impediment, obstacle, hurdle
反义词: advantage, asset, benefit, help
词性: noun, verb


词源: From the Old English *hand in cap,* a game of chance where forfeits were placed in a cap.  The term evolved to describe a system of equalizing chances in a contest, and later broadened to include any kind of disadvantage.
