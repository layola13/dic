goose-stepped:/ˈɡuːs.stɛpt/ | adj. | 正步走的，以正步走的 |  Marched in a stiff-legged, straight-kneed fashion, reminiscent of the goose step used by some military forces. |  The soldiers goose-stepped across the parade ground. 士兵们在阅兵场上正步走。 | The troops goose-stepped past the reviewing stand. 部队正步走过检阅台。 | synonyms: marching stiffly, parading rigidly | antonyms: walking casually, strolling, ambling | adjective

goose-step:/ˈɡuːs.stɛp/ | n. vi. vt.| 正步，鹅步，以正步走 | A marching step in which the legs are swung high and kept straight.  |  The goose step is a military march. 正步是一种军队的行进方式。 |  The soldiers goose-stepped down the street. 士兵们在街上正步走。 | synonyms: march, parade | antonyms: stroll, amble | noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Gänsemarsch* (goose march) and *Gänsetritt* (goose step), literally meaning "goose step."  Likely refers to the resemblance of the high, stiff-legged gait to the way a goose walks.
