eminence:/ˈɛmɪnəns/ | n. | 卓越；显赫；高处；高地 | a position of prominence or superiority; a high or raised area |  The scientist achieved eminence in the field of physics. 这位科学家在物理学领域取得了卓越的成就。| synonyms: distinction, prominence, fame, renown, elevation, height | antonyms: insignificance, obscurity, lowliness | noun

eminences:/ˈɛmɪnənsɪz/ | n. | 卓越；显赫；高处；高地（eminence的复数形式） | plural form of eminence |  Many eminences in the medical field attended the conference. 许多医学界的杰出人士出席了这次会议。| synonyms: distinctions, prominences, fames, renowns, elevations, heights | antonyms: insignificances, obscurities, lowlinesses | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *eminence*, from Latin eminentia (“a projecting, a standing out”), from eminent- (stem of eminēns), present participle of eminēre (“to stand out, project”), from ex- (“out”) + -minēre (related to mont-, “mountain”).
