fancifulness:/ˈfænsifʊlnəs/ | n. | 幻想；奇思妙想；异想天开 | The state or quality of being fanciful; imaginative or whimsical. | Her writing is full of fancifulness and vivid imagery. 她的作品充满了奇思妙想和生动的意象。| Synonyms: imagination, whimsy, creativity, inventiveness, fantasy | Antonyms: practicality, realism, dullness, ordinariness | noun

fanciful:/ˈfænsifʊl/ | adj. | 想象的；奇特的；异想天开的 | Characterized by or showing imagination; unreal or whimsical. |  He told a fanciful tale of dragons and unicorns. 他讲述了一个关于龙和独角兽的奇幻故事。| Synonyms: imaginative, whimsical, fantastic, unreal, dreamy | Antonyms: realistic, practical, down-to-earth, mundane | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From fancy (n.) + -ful + -ness.  "Fancy" comes from Old French "fantasie," ultimately from Latin "phantasia," meaning "appearance, image, phantom."
