misnarrating: /ˌmɪsnəˈreɪtɪŋ/
|v.|错误地叙述，歪曲|Giving a false or inaccurate account of events; telling a story incorrectly. |He was misnarrating the story to make himself look better. 他为了让自己看起来更好而歪曲了故事。|The journalist was accused of deliberately misnarrating the facts.  这位记者被指控故意歪曲事实。|misrepresenting, falsifying, distorting|accurately narrating, truthfully recounting|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (prefix meaning 'wrongly' or 'badly') + narrate (from Latin narrare 'to tell') + -ing (present participle suffix).
