melancholia:/ˌmɛlənˈkəʊliə/ | n. | 忧郁，忧伤 | A feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause. |  A deep melancholia had settled over him after his wife's death.  他妻子去世后，他陷入了深深的忧郁之中。 | synonyms: sadness, depression, gloom, dejection | antonyms: happiness, joy, elation | noun


melancholy:/ˈmɛlənˌkɒli/ | adj. | 忧郁的，悲伤的 | Having a feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause. | The melancholy strains of the music filled the room.  忧郁的音乐旋律充满了整个房间。 | synonyms: sad, depressed, gloomy, dejected | antonyms: happy, joyful, elated | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin melancholia, from Greek melankholia "black bile," from melas (genitive melanos) "black" + kholē "bile."  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholia.
