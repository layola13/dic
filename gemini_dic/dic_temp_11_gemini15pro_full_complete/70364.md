fierier:/ˈfaɪəriər/ (US), /ˈfaɪəriə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更炽热的，更激烈的|Comparative adjective of fiery.  Meaning more fiery; characterized by greater intensity of emotion or heat.|The sunset was fierier than last night.  日落比昨晚更火红。|The debate became fierier as the candidates addressed the controversial issue. 随着候选人谈到这个有争议的问题，辩论变得更加激烈。|近义词：hotter, more intense, more passionate, more fervent|反义词：cooler, calmer, milder, less intense|词性:形容词

fiery:/ˈfaɪəri/ (US), /ˈfaɪəri/ (UK)| adj.|炽热的，火红的；激烈的，暴躁的|Characterized by fire or intense heat; passionate or easily angered.|The fiery sunset painted the sky in vibrant hues. 火红的日落将天空染上了鲜艳的色彩。|She delivered a fiery speech that roused the crowd. 她发表了慷慨激昂的演讲，激起了群众的热情。|近义词：blazing, burning, passionate, fervent|反义词：cool, calm, mild, impassive|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *fieri, fyrie, from Old French fier, fer, from Latin ferus (“wild, untamed”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰwer- (“wild animal; beast”).
