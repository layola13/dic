commissar:/ˈkɒmɪsɑːr/ (US) /ˈkɑːmɪsɑːr/| n.|政委；委员；专员|An official of the Communist Party, especially in the former Soviet Union, responsible for political education and organization.  A person appointed by a government to oversee an area or activity.|The commissars ensured party loyalty within the army. 政委们确保了军队内部的党性忠诚。|synonyms: official, commissioner, agent, delegate, representative|antonyms: civilian, non-official|noun


commissars:/ˈkɒmɪsɑːrz/ (US) /ˈkɑːmɪsɑːrz/| n.|政委们；委员们；专员们|Plural form of commissar.|The commissars were responsible for enforcing party discipline. 这些政委负责执行党的纪律。|synonyms: officials, commissioners, agents, delegates, representatives|antonyms: civilians, non-officials|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Russian комиссар (komissar), from French commissaire, from Latin commissarius (“commissioner”).
