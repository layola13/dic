flunkey:/ˈflʌŋki/ | n. | 仆从，走狗，马屁精 | A servant, especially a liveried footman or manservant; a person who behaves servilely to someone important. | The millionaire was surrounded by flunkeys who catered to his every whim.  这个百万富翁身边围满了为他百依百顺的仆从。| Synonyms: lackey, toady, sycophant, minion | Antonyms: master, boss, leader | noun


flunkey:/ˈflʌŋki/ | v. |  卑躬屈膝，拍马屁 | To behave servilely; to fawn. | He flunkeyed around the CEO, hoping to get a promotion. 他围着CEO拍马屁，希望能升职。| Synonyms: fawn, grovel, kowtow | Antonyms: defy, resist, challenge | verb
