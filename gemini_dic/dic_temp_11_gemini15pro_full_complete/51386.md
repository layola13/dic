determinateness:/dɪˈtɜːrmɪnɪtnəs/
n.|确定性；明确性|The quality of being determinate; definiteness.  The state of being fixed or established.|The lawyer admired the determinateness of the judge's ruling.  律师很钦佩法官裁决的确定性。|
近义词:certainty, definiteness, precision, clarity
反义词:indeterminateness, vagueness, ambiguity, uncertainty
词性:noun

determinate:/dɪˈtɜːrmɪnət/
adj.|确定的；明确的|Having exact and discernible limits or form.  Fixed or established.|The contract included a determinate completion date. 合同中包含确定的完成日期。|
近义词:fixed, definite, precise, exact
反义词:indeterminate, vague, ambiguous, uncertain
词性:adjective

determine:/dɪˈtɜːrmɪn/
v.|确定；决定；测定|1. To fix conclusively or authoritatively; decide. 2. To ascertain or establish exactly, typically as a result of research or calculation. 3. To be the decisive factor in.|1. The jury determined that the defendant was guilty. 陪审团认定被告有罪。 2. Scientists are still trying to determine the cause of the disease. 科学家们仍在努力确定这种疾病的病因。 3.  Her persistence determined her success. 她的坚持决定了她的成功。|
近义词:decide, resolve, establish, ascertain
反义词:waver, hesitate
词性:verb
