neocon:/ˈniːoʊkɑːn/ | n. | 新保守主义者 | A person who advocates a return to traditional moral values, particularly in politics, and who favors a strong national defense and an assertive foreign policy. | He's been described as a neocon, advocating for military intervention. 他被描述为新保守主义者，主张军事干预。 |  Example 2:  Many neocons supported the Iraq War. 许多新保守主义者支持伊拉克战争。 | 近义词：hawk, conservative | 反义词: dove, liberal | 词性: noun

Etymology:  A shortened form of "new conservative," first used in the 1970s to describe a group of intellectuals who were formerly liberals but became more conservative in their views.  来源于“new conservative”（新保守主义）的缩写形式，最初用于20世纪70年代，用来描述一群曾经是自由主义者但在观点上变得更加保守的知识分子。
