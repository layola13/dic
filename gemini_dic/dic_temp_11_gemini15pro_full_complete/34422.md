claudication:/ˌklɔːdɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |跛行，间歇性跛行|A condition characterized by pain and cramping in the legs, especially the calves, during exercise, typically caused by obstruction of the arteries.  This pain is relieved by rest.|Example 1:  The patient presented with claudication in his left leg after walking just a short distance.  病人左腿走了很短一段距离后就出现跛行。

Example 2:  Claudication can be a sign of peripheral artery disease. 间歇性跛行可能是外周动脉疾病的征兆。|近义词：limping, lameness|反义词: (No direct antonym, consider "free movement" or "unimpeded gait")|词性:noun
