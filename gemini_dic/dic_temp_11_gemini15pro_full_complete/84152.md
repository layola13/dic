half-orphans:/ˌhɑːf ˈɔːrfənz/ | n. | 半孤儿 | A child who has lost one parent. |  Example 1: The charity provides support for half-orphans who have lost their mothers.  这个慈善机构为失去母亲的半孤儿提供支持。

Example 2:  Many half-orphans live with their remaining parent. 许多半孤儿与他们剩下的父母住在一起。 | Synonyms: semi-orphans | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, consider "children with both parents") | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
"half-" + "orphan". "Half-" denotes one of two parts, while "orphan" comes from the Greek word "orphanós" meaning "bereft of parents".  The combination creates the meaning of "bereft of one parent".
