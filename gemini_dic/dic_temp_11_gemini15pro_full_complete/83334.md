gypsy: /ˈdʒɪpsi/ (US) /ˈdʒɪpsi/ (UK)| n. adj.|吉普赛人，流浪者；吉普赛式的，流浪的|A member of a traditionally itinerant people who originated in northern India and migrated westward to Europe in the Middle Ages, often facing prejudice and discrimination. Also used to describe a nomadic or free-spirited lifestyle.|例句：The gypsy caravan traveled across the country. 吉普赛大篷车周游全国。

例句：She lived a gypsy life, moving from place to place. 她过着吉普赛般的生活，四处漂泊。|近义词：traveler, nomad, wanderer, bohemian, Romany|反义词：settler, resident|词性: 名词，形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *Gypcian*, shortened from *Egipcien*, from Old French *Gipcyan*, ultimately from Medieval Latin *Aegyptianus* (“Egyptian”), from Late Latin *Aegyptius*, from Ancient Greek *Αἰγύπτιος* (Aigúptios).  The Romani people were mistakenly thought to have originated in Egypt.
