coquetry:/ˈkoʊkɪtri/ /ˈkɒkɪtri/| n. | 卖弄风情，调情，媚态 | The behavior or arts of a coquette; flirtation.  A deliberate attempt to attract admiration and attention, often in a playful or provocative way. | She used all her coquetry to charm the men. 她使出浑身的媚态来迷住那些男人。 |  近义词：flirtation, dalliance, teasing, allurement | 反义词：modesty, reserve, demureness | 词性: noun

coquette:/koʊˈkɛt/ /kɒˈkɛt/| n. | 妖艳的女人，卖弄风情的女人 | A woman who flirts lightheartedly with men to win their admiration. |  She's such a coquette, always batting her eyelashes at the men. 她真是个爱卖弄风情的女人，总是对男人抛媚眼。 | 近义词：flirt, tease | 反义词：prude | 词性: noun

coquettish:/koʊˈkɛtɪʃ/ /kɒˈkɛtɪʃ/| adj. | 卖弄风情的，调情的 | Behaving in such a way as to suggest a playful sexual attraction; flirtatious. |  She gave him a coquettish smile. 她给了他一个卖弄风情的微笑。 | 近义词：flirtatious, teasing, playful | 反义词：modest, reserved, demure | 词性: adjective

词源：
From French coquetterie, from coquet "flirt, male flirt," from coq "rooster." The sense evolution is from "vain, strutting" to "flirtatious."
来源于法语 coquetterie，来自 coquet “调情者，男性调情者”，来自 coq “公鸡”。意义演变是从“虚荣的，昂首阔步的”到“轻浮的”。
