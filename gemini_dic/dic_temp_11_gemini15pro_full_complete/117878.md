melodramatic:/ˌmelədrəˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 夸张的，感情过分戏剧化的 | Exaggeratedly emotional or sentimental; characteristic of melodrama. |  She reacted to the news in a melodramatic way, bursting into tears and accusing everyone of conspiring against her. 她对这个消息的反应过于戏剧化，突然大哭起来，并指责每个人都在密谋陷害她。 |例句：He flung his arms wide in a melodramatic gesture. 他夸张地张开双臂。| 近义词：histrionic, theatrical, overdramatic, exaggerated | 反义词：understated, restrained, subtle | 词性: 形容词

melodramatic:/ˌmelədrəˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 夸张的，感情过分戏剧化的 | Exaggeratedly emotional or sentimental; characteristic of melodrama. | The movie was criticized for its melodramatic plot and unconvincing acting. 这部电影因其夸张的情节和不令人信服的演技而受到批评。 |例句：It would be melodramatic to say that her life was ruined. 说她的生活被毁了未免太夸张了。| 近义词：sentimental, sensational | 反义词：realistic, understated | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mélodrame*, from Greek *melos* "song" + French *drame* "drama".  Originally referring to a play with music, it later came to describe overly emotional or sensational dramatic works.
