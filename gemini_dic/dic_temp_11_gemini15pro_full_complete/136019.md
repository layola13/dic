nonrealisms:/ˌnɒnˈriːəlɪzəmz/
noun
非现实主义，非写实主义
Theories encompassing various movements in literature, art, and philosophy that reject realism.  These theories explore subjective experience, abstract ideas, or imaginative constructs that do not necessarily conform to perceived reality.
Modern art often embraces nonrealisms, exploring abstract forms and subjective emotions. 现代艺术经常包含非现实主义，探索抽象的形式和主观的情感。
近义词:idealisms, abstractions, surrealisms
反义词:realisms, naturalisms
词性:n.

词源:
Prefix "non-" (not) + "realism" (the attitude or practice of accepting a situation as it is and being prepared to deal with it accordingly). "Realism" comes from Late Latin "realis" (relating to things) + "-ism" (a doctrine, belief, or way of doing something).
