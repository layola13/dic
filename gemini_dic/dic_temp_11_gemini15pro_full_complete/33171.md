chromid:/ˈkroʊmɪd/ | n. |染色体外DNA，染色质体 |A cytoplasmic genetic element found in many bacteria. It is a large, extrachromosomal DNA molecule that contains essential genes.  It is distinct from plasmids in its larger size and essential gene content. |Example: The chromid of Vibrio cholerae carries genes essential for virulence.  霍乱弧菌的染色体外DNA携带着对毒力至关重要的基因。

Example:  Researchers are studying the role of chromids in bacterial evolution. 研究人员正在研究染色体外DNA在细菌进化中的作用。|近义词:megaplasmid (巨质粒)|反义词:chromosome (染色体)|词性:noun (名词)
