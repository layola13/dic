livestock: /ˈlaɪvˌstɒk/ (US) /ˈlaɪvˌstɒk/ (UK) | n. |家畜；牲畜| farm animals, such as cows, sheep, and pigs, that are kept for agricultural purposes | The farmer raised livestock for meat and wool. 农民饲养牲畜以获取肉和羊毛。| synonyms: farm animals, cattle, stock | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, possibly wildlife, wild animals) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lyvestocke*, *lifstok*, from *lyf, lif* ("life, living") + *stok* ("stock").  The word originally referred to animals kept alive, as opposed to slaughtered animals.
