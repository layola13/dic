glaucomatic:/ɡlɔːkəˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 青光眼的，患青光眼的 | Relating to or characteristic of glaucoma. |  Example: Glaucoma is a glaucomatic disease that can lead to blindness. 青光眼是一种可导致失明的青光眼疾病。| Example:  Glaucomatic changes in the eye can be detected through regular checkups.  眼睛的青光眼变化可以通过定期检查发现。 | Synonym: glaucomatous | Antonym: healthy (in the context of eye health) | word property: adjective

glaucoma:/ɡlɔːˈkoʊmə/ | n. | 青光眼 | A group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, the health of which is vital for good vision. This damage is often caused by an abnormally high pressure in your eye. | Example: Glaucoma can lead to blindness if left untreated.  如果不及时治疗，青光眼会导致失明。 | Example: Regular eye exams are crucial for early detection of glaucoma. 定期眼科检查对于早期发现青光眼至关重要。 | Synonym: eye disease  | Antonym: healthy vision | word property: noun

Etymology:  From Greek *glaukōma* "cataract," literally "bluish-green swelling," from *glaukos*  "bluish-green, gray"  + *-ōma* suffix forming nouns of action or condition.  In ancient times, various eye diseases causing pupil discoloration were grouped together.  Later, the term became specifically associated with glaucoma.
