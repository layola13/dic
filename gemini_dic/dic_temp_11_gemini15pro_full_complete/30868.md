chaetognatha:/kiːˈtɒɡnəθə/| n. |毛颚动物门|A phylum of small, elongated, transparent marine worms, also known as arrow worms, characterized by a series of curved spines or hooks on each side of the head, used for capturing prey.  They are predatory marine invertebrates.|Chaetognatha are important components of the plankton. 毛颚动物是浮游生物的重要组成部分。|Near Synonyms: arrow worms|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a taxonomic group)|Noun
