inofficious:/ˌɪnəˈfɪʃəs/
|adj.|
|不友好的，不和的；(尤指遗嘱)违反自然公正的；(法律)无效的|
|Contrary to natural duty or affection, especially in making a will; unkind, disagreeable.  Legally ineffective.|
|His inofficious behavior towards his family alienated him from them.|他对他家庭不友好的行为使他与家人疏远了。

|His inofficious will left his entire estate to a stranger.|他那份违反自然公正的遗嘱把全部财产留给了一个陌生人。
|
|The court declared the inofficious contract null and void.|法院宣布这份无效的合同无效。
|
|Synonyms: unkind, uncharitable, unnatural, unjust, invalid|
|Antonyms: kind, charitable, natural, just, valid|
|Word type: Adjective|


Etymology:
From Latin *inofficiosus*, from *in-* (not) + *officiosus* (dutiful, obliging).  *Officiosus* is from *officium* (duty, service).
