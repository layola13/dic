ourselves:/ɑːrˈsɛlvz/
pron.|我们自己|reflexive pronoun; plural form of "ourself," used when the subject and the object of a verb are the same and refer to "we"|We made the cake ourselves. 我们自己做的蛋糕。|近义词: each other, one another |反义词: (No direct antonym for a reflexive pronoun) |词性: pronoun

ourself:/ɑːrˈsɛlf/
pron.|我们自己|reflexive pronoun; singular form of "ourselves," used in formal contexts or when speaking as a representative of a group|We convinced ourself that the project was feasible. 我们说服自己这个项目是可行的。|近义词: myself (when speaking for a group) |反义词: (No direct antonym for a reflexive pronoun) |词性: pronoun


词源：
Old English *ūs self/ūs sylfe (dative/accusative) “to/for ourselves”, from ūs (dative/accusative of wē “we”) + self/sylf (“self”).
