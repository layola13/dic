keepsake:/ˈkiːpseɪk/| n. |纪念品；信物|Something kept as a reminder of a person, place, or event.|She kept the ticket as a keepsake of her trip to Europe. 她把票留作她欧洲之旅的纪念品。|Example:  This ring is a keepsake from my grandmother. 这枚戒指是我祖母留给我的纪念品。|Synonyms: memento, souvenir, token, remembrance|Antonyms: N/A|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *kepesake, equivalent to keep + sake.  Originally referred to something given to be kept for the sake of the giver, as a pledge or token of friendship or love.
