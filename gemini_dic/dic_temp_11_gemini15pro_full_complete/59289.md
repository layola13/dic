eagre:/ˈiːɡər/ (US), /ˈeɪɡər/ (UK)| n.|大潮，涌潮，怒潮|A sudden rise of tide in a river, estuary, or bay, especially one caused by the funneling of a high tide into a narrow inlet.|The eagre rushed up the river, a wall of water several feet high.|近义词：tidal bore, bore, surge|反义词：ebb tide|词性:noun

Etymology: From Middle English *eger, from Old Norse *ágr, from Proto-Germanic *agō ("water, flood"), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ekʷeh₂ ("water").  Cognate with Old English ēg ("sea"), Old Frisian ā ("water"), Old Saxon ā ("river"), Middle High German aha ("river"), Gothic *𐌰𐍈𐌰 (*ahwa, "river").
