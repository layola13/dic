classically:/ˈklæsɪkli/| adv.|经典地；古典地；传统地|In a way that relates to the established styles of ancient Greece and Rome, especially in art, literature, and architecture.  Also, in a way that is typical of a high standard or of a particular style or period.|The building was classically designed with columns and a pediment. 这座建筑采用古典设计，带有柱子和山形墙。|近义词：traditionally, conventionally, typically|反义词:modernly, unconventionally|词性:副词
ETYMOLOGY->
From classical + -ly.  Classical comes from Late Latin classicus, meaning "belonging to the highest class of citizens," ultimately derived from Latin classis, meaning "class" or "division."
