frivol:/ˈfrɪvəl/| adj.|轻浮的，不严肃的|Not having any serious purpose or value.|She spent her allowance on frivolous things like makeup and magazines. 她把零用钱花在化妆品和杂志等无关紧要的东西上。|例句：He made a frivolous remark. 他说了一句轻佻的话。|近义词：flippant, silly, trifling, superficial, shallow|反义词：serious, important, significant|词性:形容词
frivolity:/frɪˈvɒləti/| n.|轻浮，轻佻|Lack of seriousness; lightheartedness.|The frivolity of the conversation surprised me.  谈话的轻浮让我感到惊讶。|例句：He wasted his time on frivolities. 他把时间浪费在无聊的事情上。|近义词：levity, silliness, triviality|反义词：seriousness, gravity, solemnity|词性:名词
frivolously:/ˈfrɪvələsli/| adv.|轻浮地，轻佻地|In a frivolous manner; not seriously.|He frivolously spent his inheritance. 他轻率地花掉了他的遗产。|例句：She dressed frivolously for the occasion. 她在那个场合穿着轻佻。|近义词：flippantly, carelessly, thoughtlessly|反义词：seriously, thoughtfully, carefully|词性:副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *frivolus, from Latin frivolus "silly, trifling, worthless," perhaps from friare "to rub, break into small pieces, crumble" (source of English friable). The sense evolution would be from "fragile" to "easily broken" to "of little value."
