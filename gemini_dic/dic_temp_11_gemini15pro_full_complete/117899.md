melopoeia:/ˌmɛləˈpiːə/ | n. | 音乐性，声调优美 | The use of words which mimic sounds; the quality of poetry that gives pleasure through its sounds.  The art or theory of producing melody in verse; prosody. |  Edgar Allan Poe explored melopoeia in "The Raven."  埃德加·爱伦·坡在《乌鸦》中探索了音乐性。 |  euphony, music, rhythm, cadence | cacophony, dissonance | noun
