hawkster:/ˈhɔːkstər/| n.|鹰派人物; 政治投机分子; 兜售者|Someone, especially a politician, who favors aggressive policies, especially in foreign affairs. Also, a person who promotes or sells something aggressively.|The senator, known as a hawkster, advocated for increased military spending.|近义词：war hawk, warmonger, pusher, salesman|反义词:dove, pacifist|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hawk" (referring to a bird of prey, and by extension, someone aggressive) + "-ster" (a suffix forming agent nouns, often with a negative connotation).
