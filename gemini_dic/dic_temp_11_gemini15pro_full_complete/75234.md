frolickers:/ˈfrɒlɪkərz/| n. |嬉戏玩耍的人；玩闹者|People who are playfully boisterous and enjoying themselves. |The beach was full of frolickers enjoying the sunshine. 海滩上满是享受阳光的嬉戏玩耍的人。|The park was alive with the sound of children frolicking. 公园里充满了孩子们嬉戏的声音。|近义词：revelers, merrymakers, partygoers|反义词：mourners, grievers|词性:Noun (plural)

frolic:/ˈfrɒlɪk/| n. vi. |嬉戏；玩闹|A playful and lively activity; to engage in playful activity.|The lambs frolicked in the meadow. 羊羔在草地上嬉戏。|They spent the afternoon frolicking on the beach. 他们下午在海滩上嬉戏玩闹。|近义词：play, romp, cavort, gambol|反义词：mope, sulk|词性:Noun, Verb (Intransitive)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *frok(e)len, from or related to Middle Dutch wrokkelen, vrokkelen (“to wriggle, rub, crumple”) and Middle Low German vrōkeln (“to enjoy oneself, boast, brag”).
