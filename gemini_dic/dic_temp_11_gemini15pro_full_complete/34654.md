clerestory:/ˈklɪərstɔːri/ (US), /ˈklɪəstɔːri/ (UK)| n. |天窗，高侧窗|A high section of wall that contains windows above eye level, admitting light to the central part of a building. |The clerestories in the cathedral bathed the nave in soft light.  教堂的天窗使中殿沐浴在柔和的光线中。| synonym: high window, skylight| antonym: low window| noun

clerestories:/ˈklɪərstɔːriz/ (US), /ˈklɪəstɔːriz/ (UK)| n. |天窗（复数）|Plural form of clerestory.|The architect designed a series of clerestories to maximize natural light.  建筑师设计了一系列天窗，以最大限度地利用自然光。| synonym: high windows, skylights| antonym: low windows| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clerestorie, from Old French clerestorie, ultimately from Latin *claristorium, a compound of clarus (“clear, bright”) and historia (“story, window”).
