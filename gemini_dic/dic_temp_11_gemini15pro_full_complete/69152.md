favours:/ˈfeɪvəz/ (British), /ˈfeɪvərz/ (American)| n.| 好感；恩惠；帮助；优待；偏袒；小礼物；(婚礼上的)小饰物；彩带结|A kind act or gesture; an act of kindness beyond what is due or expected. Also, small gifts or trinkets given as a token of goodwill or affection, particularly at weddings or other celebrations.  In British English, this is the standard spelling for the plural of "favor" (or "favour" in British English).|例句：She did me a great favour by lending me her car. 她把车借给了我，真是帮了我大忙。

例句：The wedding favours were small boxes of chocolates. 婚礼上的小礼物是一盒盒巧克力。|近义词： kindnesses, courtesies, gifts, boons, privileges|反义词： disfavors, slights, insults|noun


favours:/ˈfeɪvəz/ (British), /ˈfeɪvərz/ (American)| v.| 赞成；支持；有利于；偏爱；长得像；赠予；给予|To support or prefer one thing or person to another; to treat someone or something with partiality.  Also, to resemble or look like (a parent or relative). In British English, this is the standard spelling for the third person singular present tense form of "favor" (or "favour" in British English).|例句：The warm weather favours outdoor activities.  温暖的天气有利于户外活动。

例句：He favours his mother in appearance. 他长得像他妈妈。|近义词： prefers, supports, promotes, endorses, resembles|反义词： opposes, disfavors, hinders, impedes|verb
