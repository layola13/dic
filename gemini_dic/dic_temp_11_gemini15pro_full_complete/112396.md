lost-causes: /ˈlɒst ˈkɔːzɪz/ (British) /ˈlɔːst ˈkɑːzɪz/ (American) | n. | 失败的事业；注定要失败的事业；无望成功的人(或事物) |  A person, goal, or principle that has little chance of succeeding or gaining popular support and therefore considered a waste of time or effort to support. |  He's fighting for a lost cause – the council has already made its decision. 他在为一项注定失败的事业而奋斗——议会已经做出了决定。 |  lost hopes, forlorn hopes, hopeless cases | successful endeavors, promising ventures | noun

lost-cause: /ˈlɒst ˈkɔːz/ (British) /ˈlɔːst ˈkɑːz/ (American) | adj. |  注定要失败的；没有希望的 | Describing something that is unlikely to succeed and therefore not worth supporting. |  Supporting that political party now is a lost-cause.  现在支持那个政党是徒劳的。| hopeless, futile, doomed | promising, hopeful | adjective
