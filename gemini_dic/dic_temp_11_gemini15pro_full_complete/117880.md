melodramatics: /ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪks/ | n. |夸张的言行；装腔作势|Exaggerated behaviour or emotions intended to create a dramatic effect. | She reacted to the news with typical melodramatics, fainting on the sofa. 她对这个消息的反应是典型的装腔作势，晕倒在沙发上。

|例句：His melodramatics were embarrassing to watch. 他的装腔作势让人尴尬。|近义词：histrionics, dramatics, theatrics, tantrums|反义词：understatement, restraint|词性: 名词

melodramatic: /ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪk/ | adj. |夸张的；戏剧性的；感情用事的|Characteristic of melodrama, especially in being exaggerated, sensationalized, or overemotional.|例句：He gave a melodramatic sigh and rolled his eyes. 他夸张地叹了口气，翻了个白眼。 |近义词：histrionic, theatrical, overdramatic, exaggerated|反义词：understated, restrained, subtle|词性: 形容词
