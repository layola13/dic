casuistry: /ˈkæʒuɪstri/ | n. | 诡辩术，决疑法 |  the use of clever but unsound reasoning, especially in relation to moral questions; sophistry.  | Example 1: The lawyer's skillful casuistry confused the jury. 律师巧妙的诡辩术迷惑了陪审团。

Example 2: He resorted to casuistry to justify his actions. 他诉诸诡辩来为自己的行为辩护。 | Synonym: sophistry, equivocation, quibbling | Antonym:  directness, honesty, forthrightness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *casuista* "resolver of cases of conscience," from Latin *casus* "case, chance, accident" (from PIE root *kad-* "to fall").
