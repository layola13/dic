chicanes:/ʃɪˈkeɪnz/
chicanery:/ʃɪˈkeɪnəri/
noun
诡计，刁难；赛车弯道
a trick or deception, especially one used to achieve a political, financial, or legal purpose; an artificial obstacle on a motor-racing course
The lawyer used legal chicanery to get his client off.  律师运用法律上的诡计使他的客户脱罪。
The track has a series of challenging chicanes.  这条赛道有一系列充满挑战的弯道。
synonyms: trickery, subterfuge, deception, artifice, ruse, wile, stratagem, maneuver
antonyms: honesty, straightforwardness
n.

chicanes:/ʃɪˈkeɪnz/
noun
(赛车运动中的)减速弯道
a chicane is an artificial feature added to a racetrack, creating extra turns by deliberately narrowing the track at a particular point.
The drivers had to navigate a series of tight chicanes. 车手们必须驾驶通过一系列狭窄的减速弯道。
The new chicane on the track has made overtaking much more difficult. 赛道上新增的减速弯道使得超车变得更加困难。
synonyms: hairpin bend, S-bend, sharp turn, curve
antonyms: straight

词源：chicanery (n.)  1570s, "trickery, sophistry," from Middle French chicanerie "legal quibbling" (14c.), from chicaner "to quibble," perhaps from chique "small piece, trifle" (see chic (adj.)). Sense of "mean or petty tricks of lawyers" is from 1590s. Related: Chicanerous.  来自中古法语 chicanerie “法律上的诡辩”（14世纪），源自 chicaner “诡辩”，可能来自 chique “小块，琐事”。“律师的卑鄙或小伎俩”的含义始于1590年代。
chicanes 是 chicane 的复数形式，可以指诡计或赛车弯道。
