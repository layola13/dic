equivocate: /ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪt/ | v. | 模棱两可地说，含糊其辞 | to use unclear language especially to deceive or avoid committing oneself |  The politician equivocated when asked about his stance on the issue.  这位政治家在被问及他对这个问题的立场时含糊其辞。|  He equivocated about his intentions, leaving everyone confused. 他对自己的意图含糊其辞，让每个人都感到困惑。 | 近义词: prevaricate, hedge, evade | 反义词: be forthright, be direct, be honest | 词性: 动词

equivocation: /ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 模棱两可的话，含糊其辞 | the use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or to avoid committing oneself. | Her equivocation on the subject made it difficult to understand her true feelings. 她在这个问题上的含糊其辞让人很难理解她的真实感受。 | The witness's equivocation made the jury question his credibility. 证人在法庭上的含糊其辞使得陪审团质疑他的可信度。| 近义词: prevarication, evasion, ambiguity | 反义词: directness, honesty, clarity | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin aequivocatus, past participle of aequivocare (“to speak ambiguously”), from Latin aequivocus (“ambiguous”), from aequi- (“equal”) + vocare (“to call”).
