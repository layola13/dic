instantaneities: /ˌɪnstənteɪˈniːɪtiz/ | n. |瞬间，瞬息，即时性|The concept of instantaneities refers to events or moments of extremely brief duration, often occurring simultaneously or in rapid succession.| The physicist studied the instantaneities of the particle collisions. 物理学家研究了粒子碰撞的瞬间。|近义词: moments, flashes, ephemeralities |反义词: eternities, durations |词性: 名词

词源: From "instantaneous" + "-ity" (suffix forming nouns of quality or state). "Instantaneous" comes from Late Latin "instantaneus," from "instans" (present participle of "instare" - to stand near, be at hand, press upon) + "-aneus" (suffix forming adjectives).
