indicative:/ɪnˈdɪkətɪv/| adj. n.|指示的，表示的；陈述语气；指示词|Something indicative of a problem suggests that there is a problem. |Her expression was indicative of her true feelings. 她的表情表明了她的真实感受。|suggestive, symptomatic|concealing|adjective, noun

indicative:/ɪnˈdɪkətɪv/| n.|陈述语气|The indicative mood is used to make factual statements. |He spoke in the indicative mood, stating the facts as he knew them. 他用陈述语气说话，陈述了他所知道的事实。|declarative|interrogative, imperative, subjunctive|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *indicatif, from Old French indicatif, from Latin indicātīvus, from indicātus, past participle of indicāre (“to point out, indicate”), from in- + dicāre (“to proclaim, say”).
