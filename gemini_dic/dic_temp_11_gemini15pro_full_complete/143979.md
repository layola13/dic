outraging:/aʊtˈreɪdʒɪŋ/
|adj.|令人愤慨的；骇人的|Causing great shock, anger, or resentment. |The jury heard outraging details of the abuse. 陪审团听取了令人愤慨的虐待细节。|近义词:shocking, appalling, scandalous, disgraceful|反义词:pleasing, delightful|词性:形容词
outrage:/ˈaʊtreɪdʒ/
|n.|暴行；愤怒|An act of extreme violence or viciousness; a feeling of righteous anger. |The terrorist outrage shocked the world. 恐怖主义暴行震惊了世界。|近义词:atrocity, offense, indignation|反义词:delight, pleasure|词性:名词
outrage:/aʊtˈreɪdʒ/
|v.|激怒；使震怒|To arouse intense indignation in (someone).|The judge's decision outraged many people. 法官的判决激怒了很多人。|近义词:infuriate, enrage, incense|反义词:please, delight|词性:动词
