leashed:/liːʃt/
|adj.|被拴住的，受束缚的|restrained by a leash; controlled or restricted|例句：The dog was leashed and walked calmly beside its owner.  这只狗被拴着皮带，平静地走在主人旁边。

例句：Feeling leashed by her responsibilities, she longed for freedom.  被责任束缚着，她渴望自由。|近义词：tied, restrained, bound, tethered|反义词：free, unrestrained, loose, unleashed|词性:形容词
词源: From the noun "leash," which comes from Old French "laisse" meaning a thong or slip, ultimately from Latin "laxare" to loosen, slacken.  Ironically, a leash, though derived from a word meaning "to loosen," is used to restrain.
