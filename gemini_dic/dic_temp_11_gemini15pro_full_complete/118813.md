metamorphose:/ˌmɛtəˈmɔːrfoʊz/ (US) /ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəʊz/ (UK)| v. | 变形，变质 | to change or cause to change completely in form or nature; to transform | The caterpillar metamorphosed into a beautiful butterfly. 毛毛虫变成了美丽的蝴蝶。| synonyms: transform, convert, transmute | antonyms: stagnate, fix, remain | verb

metamorphosis: /ˌmɛtəˈmɔːrfəsɪs/ (US) /ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəsɪs/ (UK) | n. | 变形，变质 | a process of transformation, as by magic or sorcery | The metamorphosis of the tadpole into a frog is a fascinating process. 蝌蚪变成青蛙的过程令人着迷。| synonyms: transformation, conversion, transmutation | antonyms: stagnation, fixity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *métamorphoser*, from Late Latin *metamorphōsis*, from Greek *metamórphōsis*, from *metamorphoun* "to transform," from *meta-* "change" + *morphē* "form."
