dispose:/dɪˈspəʊz/ (UK) /dɪˈspoʊz/ (US)| vt. |处理，处置；使倾向于；安排|to get rid of something, especially by throwing it away; to arrange things or people in a particular way or position; to make someone feel a particular way towards someone or something|例句：The company disposed of its toxic waste illegally.  这家公司非法处置了有毒废物。

例句：He disposed his troops along the riverbank. 他沿着河岸部署了他的部队。|近义词：arrange, place, discard, throw away, get rid of|反义词：keep, retain, hoard|词性:动词


dispose of:/dɪˈspəʊz ɒv/ (UK) /dɪˈspoʊz əv/ (US)| phrasal verb |处理，处置|to get rid of something, especially by throwing it away|例句：How should I dispose of this old computer? 我应该如何处理这台旧电脑？

例句：The evidence was disposed of immediately. 证据立即被销毁了。|近义词：get rid of, throw away, discard|反义词：keep, retain|词性:动词短语


be disposed to:/bi dɪˈspəʊzd tuː/ (UK) /bi dɪˈspoʊzd tuː/ (US)| phrasal verb |倾向于，有意于|to be likely or willing to behave in a particular way|例句：I'm not disposed to argue with you. 我不想和你争论。

例句：She was disposed to be helpful. 她乐于助人。|近义词：inclined, willing, prone|反义词：disinclined, unwilling|词性:动词短语


dispose for:/dɪˈspəʊz fɔːr/ (UK) /dɪˈspoʊz fɔːr/ (US)| phrasal verb |为...做好准备|to arrange something or prepare it to be used in a particular way (less common usage)|例句：The room was disposed for a meeting. 房间已为会议做好准备。

例句：Everything was disposed for the arrival of the guests. 一切都为客人的到来做好了准备。|近义词：arrange, prepare, ready|反义词：disarrange|词性:动词短语


at one's disposal:/æt wʌnz dɪˈspəʊzəl/ (UK) /æt wʌnz dɪˈspoʊzəl/ (US)| idiom |由某人支配，供某人使用|available to be used by someone|例句：I have a car at my disposal. 我有一辆车可供使用。

例句：He had a vast library at his disposal. 他拥有一个庞大的图书馆供他使用。|近义词：available, accessible|反义词：unavailable, inaccessible|词性:习语
