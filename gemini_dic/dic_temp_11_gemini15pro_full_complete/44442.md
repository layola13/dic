cross-saddle:/ˈkrɒs ˈsædəl/ (UK), /ˈkrɔːs ˈsædəl/ (US)| adv., adj.| 侧骑, 骑马时两腿分开|Riding with legs on either side, as opposed to astride.  It can refer to the position itself or the type of saddle designed for it.  Historically, women rode side-saddle due to social conventions.|She rode cross-saddle through the fields. 她侧骑穿过田野。|近义词: sidesaddle|反义词: astride|词性: adverb, adjective

cross-saddle:/ˈkrɒs ˈsædəl/ (UK), /ˈkrɔːs ˈsædəl/ (US)| n.| 女用鞍|A saddle designed for riding cross-saddle, typically having a single pommel and a cantle designed to support the rider's right leg.|The antique cross-saddle was beautifully crafted. 这副古董女用鞍制作精美。|近义词: sidesaddle|反义词:  (no direct antonym for the noun form, perhaps "men's saddle" in a specific context)|词性: noun
