carve:/kɑːrv/ /kɑːrv/| v. |雕刻，切，开创|To cut (a hard material) in order to produce an artistic object or design; to cut (cooked meat) into slices for serving.|He carved a statue from a block of marble. 他用一块大理石雕刻了一尊雕像。|近义词：sculpt, engrave, chisel, slice, cut|反义词：assemble, construct, build|词性:动词

carve:/kɑːrv/ /kɑːrv/| n. |(尤指烤肉的)一块，一片|A slice of cooked meat.|He gave me a generous carve of the roast beef. 他给了我一大块烤牛肉。|近义词：slice, portion, piece|反义词：whole|词性:名词

词源:
From Old English *ceorfan (attested only in the past participle corefen), from Proto-Germanic *kerfaną (compare West Frisian kerfje (“to notch, carve”), Dutch kerven (“to notch”), German kerben (“to notch”)), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰerbʰ- (“to scratch, carve”).
