daresay:/ˈdeə(r)ˌseɪ/ /ˈderˌseɪ/| v.| 敢说，想必，可能| I daresay you're right. 我想你是对的。|近义词：presumably, probably, suppose, imagine|反义词:doubt|词性:动词

词源: From Middle English *dar I seien*, from *dar* (“dare”) + *I seien* (“I say”).  Essentially, "I dare say this."

用法说明:

Daresay is a somewhat old-fashioned or formal way of expressing a tentative opinion or belief. It often implies a slight reluctance to commit fully to the statement.  It can also be used to express a polite disagreement. It's typically used with the first person singular (I).


例句：

I daresay it will rain tomorrow.
我想明天会下雨。

I daresay she'll be late again.
我估计她又要迟到了。

He's quite intelligent, I daresay.
他很聪明，我敢说。

You're probably right, I daresay.
你可能是对的，我估计。
