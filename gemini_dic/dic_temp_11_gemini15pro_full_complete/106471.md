kowtow:/ˌkaʊˈtaʊ/ |vi. n.|叩头，磕头；卑躬屈膝；唯命是从|To kneel and touch the ground with the forehead in worship or submission, especially in former Chinese custom. Also, to act in an excessively subservient manner.|例句：He kowtowed to the emperor. 他向皇帝叩头。|例句：She refused to kowtow to the demands of her boss. 她拒绝向老板的要求卑躬屈膝。|近义词：grovel, fawn, prostrate oneself|反义词：defy, resist|词性：动词，名词

kowtows:/ˌkaʊˈtaʊz/ |n. |叩头，磕头（复数）|Plural form of "kowtow," referring to multiple instances of the act of kneeling and touching the ground with the forehead.|例句：The kowtows performed by the subjects demonstrated their subservience.臣民们行的叩头礼展现了他们的恭顺。|例句：He witnessed numerous kowtows during the ceremony. 在仪式中，他目睹了无数次的叩拜。|近义词：prostrations|反义词：rebellions|词性：名词
