narrow:/ˈnæroʊ/ (US), /ˈnærəʊ/ (UK)| adj. v. n.|狭窄的，勉强的；变窄，缩小；狭窄部分，隘路|Limited in extent, scope, or range; not broad or wide.  A narrow victory. |The road narrows after the bridge.  这条路过了桥就变窄了。|近义词：thin, slender, limited, restricted|反义词：wide, broad, vast, extensive|词性: adjective, verb, noun

narrow:/ˈnæroʊ/ (US), /ˈnærəʊ/ (UK)| v. |使变窄，缩小；限制|To make or become less broad or wide.  He narrowed his eyes in suspicion. |She narrowed her focus to 18th-century literature. 她将研究重点缩小到18世纪文学。|近义词：restrict, limit, contract, reduce|反义词：widen, broaden, expand, enlarge|词性: verb

narrow:/ˈnæroʊ/ (US), /ˈnærəʊ/ (UK)| n. |狭窄部分，海峡，隘路|A narrow passage, especially of water.  The boat navigated the narrow between the islands. |The narrows of the strait are treacherous. 海峡的狭窄处非常危险。|近义词：strait, channel, passage, gap|反义词：wide, expanse, breadth|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *nearu "near, close, small, confined," from Proto-Germanic *narwaz "narrow, confined," from PIE root *ner- (1) "on the left hand; below."
