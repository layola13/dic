intangibility:/ɪnˌtændʒəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 无形性；非物质性；不可捉摸性 | The state of being intangible; not able to be perceived by touch.  Something that is intangible cannot be physically touched. | The intangibility of the software makes it difficult to assess its value.  软件的无形性使得难以评估其价值。| synonyms: abstractness, impalpability, immateriality | antonyms: tangibility, concreteness, palpability | noun

intangible:/ɪnˈtændʒəbəl/ | adj. | 无形的；非物质的；难以捉摸的 |  Unable to be touched or grasped; not having physical presence.  Difficult to define or understand. |  The benefits of a good education are often intangible. 良好教育的益处通常是无形的。 | synonyms: impalpable, incorporeal, ethereal | antonyms: tangible, concrete, palpable | adjective
