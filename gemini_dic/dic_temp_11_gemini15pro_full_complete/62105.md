enchantment:/ɪnˈtʃæntmənt/ /ɛnˈtʃæntmənt/| n.|魅力；施魔法；着迷；魔法|A quality that delights or captivates, often suggesting magic or a spell.  The state of being under a spell.|例句：The enchantment of the moonlight on the water was breathtaking. 月光洒在水面上，美得令人窒息。

例句：The witch cast an enchantment on the prince. 女巫对王子施了魔法。|近义词：magic, spell, charm, fascination, allure|反义词：disenchantment, disillusionment|词性:noun

enchant:/ɪnˈtʃænt/ /ɛnˈtʃænt/| vt.|使着迷；施魔法于|To delight greatly; to put a magic spell on.|例句：The storyteller enchanted the children with his tales.  讲故事的人用他的故事迷住了孩子们。

例句：The wizard enchanted the sword with magical powers.  巫师给剑施了魔法。|近义词：captivate, charm, fascinate, bewitch|反义词：disenchant, repel|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "enchantement," from "enchanter" (to enchant), from Late Latin "incantare" (to sing a magic formula), from Latin "in-" (in, into) + "cantare" (to sing).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*kan-" (to sing).
