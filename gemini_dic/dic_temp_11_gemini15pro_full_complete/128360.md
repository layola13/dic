neutron:/ˈnjuːtrɒn/ (US) /ˈnjuːtrɒn/ (UK)| n.|中子|A subatomic particle of about the same mass as a proton but without an electric charge, present in all atomic nuclei except those of ordinary hydrogen.|Neutrons and protons make up the nucleus of an atom. 中子和质子构成原子的原子核。|synonyms: neutral particle|antonyms: proton, electron|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From neutral + -on (suffix used to form names of subatomic particles).  The word "neutral" itself comes from Late Middle English, from Old French neutre, from Latin neuter, meaning "neither."
