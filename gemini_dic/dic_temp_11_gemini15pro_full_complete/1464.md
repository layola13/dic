acerbate:/ˈæsərˌbeɪt/ | v. |加剧，使恶化；使苦恼|to make something worse, especially a bad situation or a negative feeling; to make something taste bitter or sour |The humid weather acerbated his skin condition. 潮湿的天气加剧了他的皮肤病。| Synonyms: aggravate, worsen, exacerbate, intensify, embitter | Antonyms: alleviate, improve, soothe, mitigate | verb

acerbate:/ˈæsərˌbeɪt/ | adj. |（味道）酸涩的；尖刻的|having a sour or bitter taste; sharp and critical in tone or manner |Her acerbate remarks about his presentation offended him. 她对他演讲的尖刻评论冒犯了他。| Synonyms: bitter, sour, acrid, tart, caustic, mordant | Antonyms: sweet, mild, gentle, pleasant | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *acerbatus, past participle of acerbare (“to make sour, bitter”), from Latin acerbus (“sour, bitter, harsh”).
