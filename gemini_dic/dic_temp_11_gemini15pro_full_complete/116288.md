massif: /mæˈsiːf/ (US), /ˈmæsɪf/ (UK) | n. | 山块，大型山体 | A compact group of mountains, especially one that forms a single unit. |  The climbers attempted to scale the north face of the massif.  登山者试图攀登大型山体的北坡。 | synonyms: mountain range, mountain chain, block mountain, uplands | antonyms: valley, plain, lowland | noun

massif: /mæˈsiːf/ (US), /ˈmæsɪf/ (UK) | n. | (尤指)密集的整体；大块 | A large, compact, and uniform area or group. | The data revealed a massif of evidence supporting the theory. 数据揭示了支持该理论的大量证据。 | synonyms: block, mass, bulk, chunk | antonyms: fragment, piece, bit | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *massif*, from Old French *massís* "massive, solid," past participle of *massir* "to build, construct," from Vulgar Latin *massificare*, from Latin *massa* "mass, lump."
