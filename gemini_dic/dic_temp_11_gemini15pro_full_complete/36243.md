cognizance:/ˈkɒɡnɪzəns/ /ˈkɑːɡnɪzəns/| n. |认识，认知；审理权；标志|knowledge or awareness; the right to hear and determine a legal case; a distinguishing mark or emblem|The court took cognizance of the new evidence. 法院注意到新的证据。|Synonyms: awareness, knowledge, notice, perception, understanding|Antonyms: ignorance, unawareness|noun

cognizances:/ˈkɒɡnɪzənsɪz/ /ˈkɑːɡnɪzənsɪz/| n. |认识，认知（cognizance的复数形式）|plural form of cognizance, meaning knowledge or awareness; the right to hear and determine legal cases; distinguishing marks or emblems|The judge's cognizances of the facts of the case were impressive.  法官对案件事实的了解令人印象深刻。|Synonyms: awarenesses, knowledges, notices, perceptions, understandings (though these are less common in the plural)|Antonyms: ignorances, unawarenesses (though these are less common in the plural)|noun
