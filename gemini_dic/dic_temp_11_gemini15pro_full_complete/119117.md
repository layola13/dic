metropolis:/məˈtrɒpəlɪs/ (US) /mɪˈtrɒpəlɪs/ (UK)| n. |大都市；首府|A very large and important city, often the capital of a country or region. |例句：London is a bustling metropolis. 伦敦是一个繁华的大都市。

例句：Tokyo is a sprawling metropolis with a population of over 37 million. 东京是一个人口超过3700万的庞大都市。|近义词：city, megalopolis, urban area|反义词：village, hamlet, countryside|词性:noun

metropolis:/məˈtrɒpəlɪs/ (US) /mɪˈtrɒpəlɪs/ (UK)| n. |主要都市，中心城市|The chief city of a country, state, or region. |例句：New York City is considered the metropolis of the East Coast. 纽约市被认为是东海岸的主要都市。


例句：The ancient city of Rome was a metropolis of the Roman Empire. 古罗马城是罗马帝国的主要都市。|近义词：capital, major city|反义词：province, suburb, rural area|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek μητρόπολις (mētrópolis, “mother city, capital city, metropolis”), from μήτηρ (mḗtēr, “mother”) + πόλις (pólis, “city”).  Originally referred to a city-state in ancient Greece that had established colonies.
