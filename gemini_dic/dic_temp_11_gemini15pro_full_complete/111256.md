litigative:/ˈlɪtɪɡeɪtɪv/| adj.|诉讼的，好诉讼的|Inclined to engage in lawsuits; readily resorting to litigation. |例句： The company has a reputation for being litigative.  这家公司以好诉讼而闻名。

例句：She adopted a less litigative approach to resolving the dispute. 她采取了较少诉讼的方式来解决纠纷。|近义词：contentious, disputatious, quarrelsome|反义词：conciliatory, amicable, peaceable|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *litigativus*, from litigare (“to go to law”), from lis (“lawsuit”) + agere (“to do, drive, act”).
