cribration:/kraɪˈbreɪʃən/ | n. |筛分，筛选，过滤 |The act or process of sifting or riddling.  The state of being sifted. A perforated part or structure. |The cribration of the soil allows water to drain easily. 土壤的筛分使水容易排出。|Synonyms: sifting, riddling, screening, filtration |Antonyms: combination, mixture, integration |noun

cribration:/kraɪˈbreɪʃən/ | n. |穿孔，多孔 |The state of being perforated or having many small holes. |The cribration of the bone allows for the passage of blood vessels and nerves. 骨骼的多孔性允许血管和神经通过。|Synonyms: perforation, porosity |Antonyms: solidity, impenetrability |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cribrātiō*, from cribrātus, past participle of cribrāre (“to sift”), from cribrum (“sieve”).
