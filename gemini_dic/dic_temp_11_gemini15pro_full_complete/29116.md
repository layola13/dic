cascade:/kæˈskeɪd/ | n. v. | 小瀑布，倾泻，层叠 | a small waterfall, typically one of several that fall in stages down a steep rocky slope.  To fall or hang in copious quantities. | The water cascaded down the rocks. 水从岩石上倾泻而下。 |She let her hair cascade down her back. 她让头发披散在背上。| synonyms: waterfall, cataract, torrent, flood, avalanche, deluge | antonyms: trickle, drip, dribble | noun, verb

cascade:/kæˈskeɪd/ | v. |  倾泻，层叠 |  arrange (a number of devices or objects) in a series or sequence. |  The information cascaded down through the organization. 信息自上而下地传遍了整个组织。 |  synonyms: fall, pour, gush, stream, flow | antonyms: ascend, rise, climb | verb
