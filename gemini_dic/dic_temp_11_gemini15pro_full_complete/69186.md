fazing:/ˈfeɪzɪŋ/| adj.|令人不安的，使人慌乱的|Disconcerting or unnerving; disturbing the composure of.|The loud noise was fazing to the nervous dog.|近义词:unnerving, disconcerting, unsettling, disturbing, perturbing|反义词:calming, soothing, reassuring|词性:形容词
Etymology:  From the verb "faze," which originated in the late 19th century, likely as a variant of "feeze," an obsolete word meaning "to drive away," "frighten," or "put to flight."  Ultimately, it may be related to the Old English word "fēsian" meaning "to drive away."
