determinator:/dɪˈtɜːrmɪneɪtər/ | n. |限定词|A word or a group of words that introduces a noun phrase and limits its meaning, such as articles (a, an, the), demonstratives (this, that, these, those), possessives (my, your, his, her, its, our, their), quantifiers (some, many, few, all, every, each, any, no), and numbers.|The determiner "the" specifies a particular book.|近义词:specifier, modifier|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

Etymology: From the late Middle English "determinate" (to limit, determine), derived from Latin "determinatus," the past participle of "determinare" (to limit, define), from "de-" (down, from) + "terminare" (to set bounds to), from "terminus" (boundary, limit).  The "-or" suffix denotes an agent or instrument.
