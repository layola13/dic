custom:/ˈkʌstəm/ (US), /ˈkʌstəm/ (UK)| n. adj.|风俗，习惯；(复数)海关；定制的，定做的|A traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something that is specific to a particular society, place, or time.  Also refers to the practice of frequenting a particular shop or business.  As an adjective, it means made or done to order for a particular customer.|

例句：
The custom of giving gifts at Christmas is widespread.  圣诞节送礼物的习俗很普遍。

It is a local custom to remove your shoes before entering a home.  进屋前脱鞋是当地的习俗。

近义词：tradition, practice, habit, convention, usage, bespoke, tailor-made
反义词: unconventionality, unorthodoxy
词性: noun, adjective


customs:/ˈkʌstəmz/ (US), /ˈkʌstəmz/ (UK)| n.|海关；关税|The government department responsible for collecting duties levied on goods imported and exported and for controlling the flow of goods, including animals, transports, personal effects, and hazardous items, into and out of a country.|

例句：
We had to go through customs at the airport. 我们必须在机场过海关。

The customs officials checked our luggage. 海关官员检查了我们的行李。

近义词：border control, port of entry
反义词: None
词性: noun
