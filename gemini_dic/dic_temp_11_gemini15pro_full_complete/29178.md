cashel:/ˈkæʃəl/| n.|石砌堡垒，石砌围墙|A circular stone fort, typically one of early medieval Ireland.|The ancient cashel provided a safe haven for the villagers during times of war.  古代的石砌堡垒在战争时期为村民提供了安全的避难所。|synonym: fort, fortress, stronghold|antonym: open field|noun

cashel:/ˈkæʃəl/| n.|石堆, 碎石堆|A heap of stones; rubble.|The builders left a cashel of stones and debris after finishing the wall. 建筑工人在砌完墙后留下了一堆石头和瓦砾。|synonym: heap, mound, pile|antonym: level ground|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Irish *caiseal*  meaning "stone fort, stone enclosure".  Ultimately from Proto-Celtic *kassos* meaning "twisted, ring".
