moderates:/ˈmɑːdəreɪts/ (US) /ˈmɒdərɪts/ (UK)| n. |温和派；稳健派|People who hold moderate political views, avoiding extreme positions.|The moderates in both parties are trying to find a compromise. 两党中的温和派正在试图找到一个妥协方案。|近义词：centrists, moderatists|反义词：extremists, radicals|词性:noun

moderates:/ˈmɑːdəreɪts/ (US) /ˈmɒdərɪts/ (UK)| v. |缓和；使适中|Makes something less extreme, intense, or violent.|The chairman tried to moderate the debate. 主席试图缓和辩论。|近义词：lessen, mitigate, temper|反义词：intensify, aggravate, exacerbate|词性:verb

moderates:/ˈmɑːdəreɪts/ (US) /ˈmɒdərɪts/ (UK)| adj. |温和的；适度的|Avoiding extremes; not excessive or extreme.|The weather was moderate, neither too hot nor too cold. 天气温和，既不太热也不太冷。|近义词：reasonable, temperate, mild|反义词：extreme, immoderate, excessive|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin moderatus, past participle of moderari "to regulate, restrain," from Latin modus "measure, manner."
