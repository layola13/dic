lindals:/ˈlɪndɑːlz/ | n. | 林达尔均衡 |  A theoretical concept in economics, particularly within public goods theory, relating to the efficient provision of public goods. It suggests that individuals should contribute to a public good up to the point where their marginal benefit from consuming the good equals their marginal cost of contributing to it. |  The city council discussed applying Lindals equilibrium to fund the new park. 市议会讨论了应用林达尔均衡为新公园提供资金。| synonyms: benefit taxation, Lindahl pricing | antonyms:  N/A | noun
