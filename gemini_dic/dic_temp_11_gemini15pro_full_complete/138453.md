novatian: /noʊˈveɪʃən/ | n. | 诺瓦天派信徒; 诺瓦天主义; 诺瓦天派的教义 | A follower of Novatian, a 3rd-century priest in Rome who advocated strict discipline and denied the Church's power to grant absolution to those who had lapsed in their faith during persecution. |  The Novatians believed that those who had denied their faith during persecution could not be readmitted to the Church. 诺瓦天派认为那些在迫害期间放弃信仰的人不能重新加入教会。 | 近义词: rigorist, purist | 反义词: laxist | 词性: 名词

novatian: /noʊˈveɪʃən/ | adj. | 诺瓦天派的; 诺瓦天主义的 | Relating to Novatian or his doctrines. | The Novatian schism was a major controversy in the early Church. 诺瓦天派的分裂是早期教会的一个主要争议。 | 近义词: rigoristic, puritanical | 反义词: lax, lenient | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From Latin *Novatiānus*, from *Novatiānus* (referring to Novatian), from *Novātus* (the name of Novatian's father).
