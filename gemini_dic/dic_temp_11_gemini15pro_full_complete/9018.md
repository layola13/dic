antimachinery:/ˌæntiˈməˈʃiːnəri/| adj.|反对机械化的，抵制机械化的|Opposed to the use of machinery, especially in industry or agriculture. |The Luddites were known for their antimachinery stance during the Industrial Revolution.  工业革命时期，卢德派以其反对机械化的立场而闻名。|近义词：anti-technology, anti-industrial, anti-automation|反义词: pro-technology, pro-automation|词性: adjective

Etymology:
anti- (prefix) + machinery (noun)

"anti-" comes from Greek "anti" meaning "against" or "opposite".
"machinery" comes from Middle French "machinerie" and ultimately from Latin "machina" meaning "machine, engine, device, trick, contrivance".
