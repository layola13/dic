orwellian:/ɔːrˈwɛliən/ | adj. | 奥威尔式的，反乌托邦的 | Characteristic of the totalitarian or manipulative social practices depicted in the writings of George Orwell, especially in his novel *Nineteen Eighty-Four*. |例句：The government's surveillance program felt eerily Orwellian. 政府的监控项目让人感到毛骨悚然地像奥威尔式社会。

例句：The constant propaganda and censorship created an Orwellian atmosphere.  持续不断的宣传和审查制度制造了一种奥威尔式的氛围。 | 近义词：dystopian, totalitarian, authoritarian | 反义词：utopian, democratic, libertarian | 词性: 形容词

词源:  Derived from the name of the British author George Orwell (1903-1950), whose novels *Nineteen Eighty-Four* and *Animal Farm* depict oppressive totalitarian regimes that control every aspect of people's lives through propaganda, surveillance, and censorship.
