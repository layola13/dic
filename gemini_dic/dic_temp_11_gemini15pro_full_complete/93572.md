imagists:/ˈɪmɪdʒɪsts/| n.|意象派诗人|Members of a school of poetry that flourished in England and the U.S. between 1912 and 1917.  They emphasized the use of precise imagery and clear, sharp language.|Ezra Pound was a leading figure among the Imagists.  埃兹拉·庞德是意象派诗人的领军人物。|Synonyms:  Modernists, avant-garde poets|Antonyms: Traditional poets|Noun
