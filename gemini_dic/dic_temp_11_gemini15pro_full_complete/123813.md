mopping:/ˈmɒpɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈmɑːpɪŋ/ (US)| n. v.|拖地，擦拭；（口语）彻底击败|The act of cleaning a floor with a mop.  Also (informally) a decisive defeat.|例句：I spent the morning mopping the kitchen floor. 我早上都在拖厨房的地板。

例句：Our team took a mopping in the final game. 我们队在决赛中惨败。|近义词：cleaning, wiping, swabbing, thrashing, routing (informal)|反义词:soiling, dirtying|词性：名词，动词


mopped:/mɒpt/ (UK) /mɑːpt/ (US)| v. (past tense of mop)|拖把擦拭过；（口语）筋疲力尽的|Cleaned with a mop; (informally) exhausted and dejected.|例句：The floor was freshly mopped. 地板刚拖过。

例句：After a long day at work, I felt completely mopped.  在漫长的一天工作后，我感到筋疲力尽。|近义词：cleaned, wiped, exhausted, drained|反义词:energized, invigorated|词性:动词


mops:/mɒps/ (UK) /mɑːps/ (US)| n. (plural of mop)|拖把（复数）|More than one mop.|例句：The store sells various kinds of mops. 这家商店出售各种各样的拖把。

例句：We need new mops for the cleaning crew.  我们需要为清洁人员准备新的拖把。|近义词：None (for the plural form)|反义词:None|词性:名词


词源：From the Middle English *moppen*, from Old French *mapper*, "to soak bread", from Late Latin *mappa*, meaning "napkin" or "cloth."
