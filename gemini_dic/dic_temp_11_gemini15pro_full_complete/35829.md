cockleshell:/ˈkɒkəlʃɛl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkəlʃɛl/ (US)| n. |贝壳；脆弱的小船；微不足道的东西|A small, fragile shell of a cockle, often used figuratively to represent something delicate or insignificant.  It can also refer to a small, light boat.|例句：The child held the delicate cockleshell in her hand.  孩子手里拿着一个精致的贝壳。

例句：They sailed across the ocean in a cockleshell. 他们乘着一艘小船横渡了大洋。|近义词：seashell, shell, skiff (for boat sense)|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps something large and sturdy like "battleship" for the boat sense)|词性:noun

cockleshell:/ˈkɒkəlʃɛl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːkəlʃɛl/ (US)| adj. |(rare) 由贝壳制成的|Made of cockleshells.|例句：She wore a cockleshell necklace. 她戴着一条贝壳项链。

例句：He made a cockleshell ornament. 他制作了一个贝壳装饰品。|近义词：shell-like|反义词:  (No direct antonym)|词性:adjective
