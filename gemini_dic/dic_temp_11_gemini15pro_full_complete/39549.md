conscienced:/ˈkɒnʃənst/ | adj. | 有良心的，凭良心的 |  Equipped with a conscience; influenced by conscience.  This word is less common than "conscientious". It emphasizes the *presence* of a conscience, while "conscientious" emphasizes the *action* resulting from it. | He made a conscienced decision to return the lost wallet. 他凭良心决定归还丢失的钱包。 | 近义词: conscientious, principled, moral | 反义词: unconscionable, unscrupulous, immoral | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From the noun "conscience" + "-ed" suffix.  "Conscience" comes from Old French "conscience," from Latin "conscientia," meaning "knowledge within oneself," from "consciens," present participle of "conscire"  "to be conscious," from "com-" (together) + "scire" (to know).
