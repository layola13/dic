demoralizingly:/dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪzɪŋli/
adv.
令人沮丧地
in a way that causes someone to lose confidence or hope
The team played demoralizingly, losing by a large margin.  这支球队打得很令人沮丧，输了很多分。

demoralize:/dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪz/
v.
使士气低落
to cause someone to lose confidence or hope
The constant criticism demoralized the team.  不断的批评使团队士气低落。

moral:/ˈmɔːrəl/
adj./n.
道德的；士气
relating to principles of right and wrong behavior;  the confidence, enthusiasm, and discipline of a person or group at a particular time
The moral of the story is to never give up.  这个故事的寓意是永不放弃。
His morals are questionable. 他的道德令人质疑。

-ize/-ise:/aɪz/
suffix
…化；使…
forms verbs from adjectives or nouns
This suffix turns the adjective 'moral' into the verb 'moralize' and subsequently 'demoralize'. 这个后缀将形容词“moral”变成动词“moralize”，然后变成“demoralize”。

de-:/diː/
prefix
否定；除去
indicates reversal or removal
The prefix 'de-' adds the meaning of reversal to 'moralize', creating 'demoralize'.  前缀“de-”为“moralize”添加了反转的含义，从而创建了“demoralize”。

近义词:discouragingly, dishearteningly, depressingly
反义词:encouragingly, inspiringly, upliftingly
词性:副词 (adverb)
