for-dear-life:/fɔːr dɪər laɪf/
|adv.| 拼命地，竭尽全力地|Holding on for dear life, he managed to avoid falling from the cliff.  他拼命抓住，才避免从悬崖上掉下来。|English Explanation:  Used to emphasize the desperation or intensity with which someone holds onto something or tries to avoid something bad happening.|Example: The child clung to his mother's hand for dear life as they crossed the busy street.  孩子过马路时紧紧地抓住妈妈的手。|Synonyms: desperately, frantically, with all one's might|Antonyms: calmly, leisurely, gently|Part of Speech: adverb
