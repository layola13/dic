disaffect:/ˌdɪsəˈfɛkt/| vt.| 使疏远；使不满意；使不忠 | To alienate the affection or loyalty of; to make discontented or disgruntled. | The new policy disaffected many voters. 新政策使许多选民不满。|Synonyms: estrange, alienate, antagonize | Antonyms: reconcile, appease, placate | verb

disaffected:/ˌdɪsəˈfɛktɪd/| adj.| 不满的；不忠的；叛逆的 | Discontented and resentful, especially against authority. |  Many disaffected voters switched parties. 许多不满的选民转投其他政党。| Synonyms: disgruntled, disloyal, rebellious | Antonyms: loyal, contented, satisfied | adjective
