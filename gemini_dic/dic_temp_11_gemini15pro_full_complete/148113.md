oversecularizing:/ˌoʊvərˈsɛkjələrˌaɪzɪŋ/
verb
过度世俗化
Making something excessively secular; focusing too much on worldly or non-religious aspects at the expense of spiritual or religious considerations.
The new government policies are oversecularizing the education system, removing all references to religious holidays.  新的政府政策使教育系统过度世俗化，删除了所有与宗教节日相关的参考文献。
Some critics argue that the media is oversecularizing society by constantly promoting materialistic values. 一些评论家认为，媒体通过不断宣传物质主义价值观，使社会过度世俗化。
近义词: secularizing, laicizing
反义词: sacralizing, sanctifying
词性: verb (present participle)

词源:
From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "secularize" (verb meaning "to make secular") + "-ing" (present participle suffix).  "Secularize" ultimately derives from the Latin word "saecularis" meaning "worldly" or "of an age."
