narcotically: /nɑːrˈkɑːtɪkli/ | adv. | 麻醉地；昏昏沉沉地 | In a way that relates to or produces narcosis; in a way that resembles the effects of narcotics. | The patient was breathing narcotically after the surgery. 病人在手术后呼吸微弱，像是麻醉了一样。|  The drug made him feel narcotically drowsy. 这种药让他感到昏昏欲睡，像麻醉了一样。| 近义词: drowsily, stuporously, lethargically | 反义词: alertly, energetically | 词性: 副词

narcotic: /nɑːrˈkɑːtɪk/ | n. adj. | 麻醉剂；麻醉的 |  A drug or other substance affecting mood or behavior and sold for nonmedical purposes, especially an illegal one. |  The doctor prescribed a narcotic to relieve the patient's pain. 医生开了一种麻醉药来缓解病人的疼痛。| The effects of the narcotic were immediate. 麻醉药的效果是立竿见影的。| 近义词: opiate, analgesic, anesthetic | 反义词: stimulant | 词性: 名词，形容词


词源：From French *narcotique*, from Late Latin *narcoticus*, from Greek *narkōtikos* “making numb”, from *narkaō* “to benumb”.
