crampon:/ˈkræmpɒn/ (UK), /ˈkræmpɑːn/ (US)|n.|冰爪，防滑钉|A metal frame with spikes, attached to boots for walking on ice or snow.|The climbers put on their crampons before tackling the icy slope. 登山者在攀登冰坡前穿上了冰爪。|Near Synonyms: spikes, cleats|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "smooth soles")|Noun

Etymology: From the French word "crampon," meaning "hook," ultimately from a Frankish word *krampō.
