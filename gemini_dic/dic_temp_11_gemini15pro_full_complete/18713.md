bereave: /bɪˈriːv/ | vt. | 使丧失（尤指亲属）; 使孤寂 | to deprive and make desolate, especially by death; to deprive ruthlessly |  His family was bereaved by the sudden death of his wife. 他的家人因他妻子的突然去世而悲痛欲绝。 | synonyms: deprive, strip, rob, dispossess | antonyms: provide, give, bestow | verb

bereaved: /bɪˈriːvd/ | adj. | 丧失亲人的；感到悲痛的 | deprived of a close relation or friend through their death | The bereaved family received many condolences.  这个痛失亲人的家庭收到了很多慰问。| synonyms: grieving, mourning, sorrowful | antonyms: joyful, happy, celebrating | adjective

bereavement: /bɪˈriːvmənt/ | n. | 丧亲之痛；丧失 | the state of being bereaved or deprived of something valuable |  She suffered a great bereavement when her father died. 她父亲去世后，她承受了巨大的悲痛。 | synonyms: loss, deprivation, grief | antonyms: gain, acquisition, blessing | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *berēafian, from be- (completely) + rēafian (to rob, plunder). Related to bereave (verb). Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *raubōną (to rob).
