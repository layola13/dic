mortress:/ˈmɔːr.tər/ (US) /ˈmɔː.trəs/ (UK)| n. |灰浆；迫击炮；研钵|A mixture of lime or cement, sand, and water, used in building to bond bricks or stones. A short, smooth-bore gun for firing shells at high angles.|例句：The builder mixed the mortar for the brick wall.  建筑工人混合了砌砖墙的灰浆。

例句：The mortar crew fired at the enemy position. 迫击炮小组向敌方阵地开火。|近义词：cement, grout (for building material sense); howitzer (for weapon sense)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mortier*, from Classical Latin mortārium (“mortar”), neuter of mortārius (“of pounding or crushing”), from mortarium (“mortar”), from *mort-, perfect passive participle stem of *morō (“rub, crush”); related to molō (“grind”). Doublet of mortar.
