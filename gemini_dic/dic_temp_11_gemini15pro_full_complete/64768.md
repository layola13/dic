escutcheon:/ɪˈskʌtʃən/
n.
饰板；盾形纹章；锁眼盖；名声，名誉
A decorative metal plate or shield around a keyhole, door handle, light switch, or something similar.  Also, a shield or emblem bearing a coat of arms. Can also refer to one's reputation or honor.
The escutcheon on the front door was tarnished with age. 前门的饰板因年代久远而失去光泽。
He felt that the scandal had stained his escutcheon. 他觉得这桩丑闻玷污了他的名声。
synonym: shield, crest, emblem, reputation, honor
antonym: disgrace, dishonor
noun

escutcheons:/ɪˈskʌtʃənz/
n. (plural)
饰板；盾形纹章；锁眼盖；名声，名誉 (Plural of escutcheon)
Multiple decorative metal plates or shields, or multiple coats of arms. Can also refer to the reputations or honor of multiple individuals.
The escutcheons on all the doors in the mansion were identical.  这栋豪宅所有门上的饰板都一模一样。
Their family escutcheons were proudly displayed in the hall. 他们的家族纹章骄傲地陈列在大厅里。
synonym: shields, crests, emblems, reputations, honors
antonym: disgraces, dishonors
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *escuchon*, diminutive of *escu*, "shield," from Latin *scutum*, "shield."
