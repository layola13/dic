dage:/ˈdɑːɡə/ (Danish pronunciation may vary)| n.| 老爸；老哥（丹麦语非正式）| A Danish slang term of endearment and familiarity, similar to "dude," "mate," or "bro" in English. It is used to address a male friend or acquaintance, and sometimes even one's father. | Example: Hey dage, what's up? 嘿，老哥，怎么了？| Example:  Dage, can you pass the salt? 老爸，你能把盐递给我吗？| Near synonyms: dude, mate, bro, pal, buddy, governor (UK), pop (father) | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, as it's a term of address) | noun
