drouthy:/ˈdraʊθi/| adj.|口渴的；渴望的|Thirsty; desiring drink. Also used figuratively to mean yearning or craving for something.  Derived from "drought."|The farmer felt drouthy after working in the fields all day.  那个农民整天在地里干活后感到口渴。|The poet had a drouthy mind, always seeking new knowledge.  这位诗人有着渴望知识的头脑，总是寻求新的知识。|近义词:thirsty, parched, arid|反义词:quenched, sated|词性:形容词

词源:
From Scots *drouthie*, from *drouth* ("thirst, drought"), from Old English *drugoth* (“dryness, drought”), from Proto-Germanic *druguz* (“dry”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰrewgʰ- (“dry”). Cognate with Dutch *droogte* (“drought”), German *Dürre* (“drought”), *Trockenheit* ("dryness").
