commercializes:/kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪzɪz/
|v.|商业化，使商品化|To make something commercial; to exploit or develop something for profit.|The company commercializes university research.|近义词：monetize, market, merchandise|反义词：decommercialize|词性:动词

Etymology:
From commercialize + -es (third-person singular present tense form).
Commercialize: From commercial + -ize (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become commercial").
Commercial: From Late Middle English comercial, from Medieval Latin commērciālis (“of or pertaining to trade”), from Latin commērcium (“trade, commerce”), from com- (“together”) + merx (“merchandise”).
