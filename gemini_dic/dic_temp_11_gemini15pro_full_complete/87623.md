heuristically:/ˌhjʊərˈɪstɪkli/ | adv. | 启发式地；凭经验地 |  In a way that involves using experience to make judgments and solve problems, rather than following strict rules or algorithms.  It often involves educated guesses and approximations to find solutions that are good enough, even if not perfectly optimal. |  The problem was solved heuristically, using a rule of thumb.  这个问题是用经验法则启发式地解决的。

Heuristically, we can assume that the larger the city, the higher the crime rate. 从经验来看，我们可以假设城市越大，犯罪率越高。 | 近义词: empirically, practically, intuitively | 反义词: algorithmically, systematically, rigorously | 词性: 副词 (adverb)
