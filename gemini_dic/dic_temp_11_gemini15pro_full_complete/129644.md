non-count:/ˌnɒnˈkaʊnt/ | adj. |不可数的 | Not able to be counted; used to describe nouns that cannot be used with numbers or the plural form. |  I need some advice. (我需要一些建议。)  | Synonyms: uncountable, mass | Antonyms: countable, count | Adjective

Etymology:  "non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "count" (from Old French "conter," from Latin "computare" - to count).
