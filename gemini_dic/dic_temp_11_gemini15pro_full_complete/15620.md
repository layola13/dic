bailors:/ˈbeɪlərz/| n.|委托人；寄托人|A bailor is a person who delivers goods to another person, called a bailee, for some purpose, such as safekeeping or repair, under a contract called a bailment.|The bailors entrusted their valuable artwork to the museum for safekeeping.|Synonyms: consignors, depositors|Antonyms: bailees|Noun
