condemnations:/ˌkɑːndɛmˈneɪʃənz/
n.
谴责，定罪;非难;判刑
Expressions of strong disapproval; pronouncements of guilt.

The jury's verdict led to several condemnations.  陪审团的裁决导致了几项定罪。

The government faced international condemnations for its human rights abuses.  该政府因其侵犯人权的行为而面临国际社会的谴责。

Synonyms: censures, criticisms, denunciations, reproaches, rebukes
Antonyms: approvals, praises, endorsements, commendations

Etymology:  From Late Latin *condemnationem, from Latin condemnare "to condemn, sentence, doom," from com- "together" (see com-) + damnare "to inflict loss upon, condemn," from damnum "loss, damage."
