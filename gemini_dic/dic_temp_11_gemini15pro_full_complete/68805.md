fasciated:/ˈfæʃɪeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 簇生的，扁化的，带状的 |  (of a plant or plant part) abnormally flattened and broadened, often as a result of the fusion of several stems, flowers, or fruits into one.  Also, captivated, fascinated (though this meaning is less common now). | The fasciated stem of the plant looked unusual.  这株植物的扁化茎看起来很不寻常。|  The sunflower had a fasciated head, producing a crest of seeds. 这株向日葵的花头簇生在一起，形成了一排种子。 |Synonyms: crested, banded, coalesced (for plants), captivated, enthralled (for the older meaning) | Antonyms: normal, separate, unjoined (for plants) |词性: 形容词

fasciated:/ˈfæʃieɪtɪd/ | adj. | 着迷的，入迷的 |  intensely interested or captivated. (This meaning is now less common than the botanical one). | He was completely fasciated by her story. 他完全被她的故事迷住了。 |  She was fasciated by the intricate details of the painting. 她被这幅画错综复杂的细节迷住了。 |Synonyms: captivated, enthralled, mesmerized | Antonyms: bored, disinterested, uninterested |词性: 形容词
