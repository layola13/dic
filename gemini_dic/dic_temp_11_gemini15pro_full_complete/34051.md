city-state:/ˈsɪti steɪt/ | n. | 城邦 |  A self-governing urban center and the agricultural territory under its control. |  The Italian Renaissance saw the flourishing of many city-states. 意大利文艺复兴时期见证了许多城邦的繁荣。 | synonym: republic | antonym: colony | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "city" + "state". "City" comes from Old French "cité"  (itself from Latin "civitas", meaning "citizenship" and related to "civis", "citizen"), and "state" comes from Old French "estat" (from Latin "status", meaning "condition").
