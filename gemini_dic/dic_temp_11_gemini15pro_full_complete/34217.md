clangor:/ˈklæŋər/ (US), /ˈklæŋɡə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.| 铿锵声，喧闹声；发出铿锵声|A loud, resonant, metallic sound, like that of a large bell or a clash of cymbals.  As a verb, it means to make such a sound.|
例句：The clangor of the bells filled the air. 钟声的铿锵声响彻天空。
例句：The blacksmith's hammer clangored against the anvil. 铁匠的锤子敲击着铁砧，发出铿锵的响声。
近义词：din, clamor, clang, ringing, resonance|
反义词：silence, quiet, stillness|
词性：noun, verb

clangor:/ˈklæŋər/ (US), /ˈklæŋɡə(r)/ (UK)| v.| 发出铿锵声|To make a loud, resonant, metallic sound.|
例句：The fire alarm clangored through the building. 火警警报声响彻整栋楼。
例句：The pots and pans clangored as they fell to the floor. 锅碗瓢盆掉到地上，发出铿锵的响声。
近义词：ring, resound, clang, clash|
反义词：mute, silence|
词性：verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *clangōrem, accusative singular of clangor ("a loud cry, shriek, clang"), from clangō ("to sound, resound, clang").
