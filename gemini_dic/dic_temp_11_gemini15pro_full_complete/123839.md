moralises:/ˈmɒrəlaɪzɪz/ (UK) /ˈmɔːrəlaɪzɪz/ (US)| v.| 说教；教训；评论道德| To make moral judgments, especially in a self-righteous or tedious way. | He's always moralising about the behaviour of young people.  他总是对年轻人的行为说三道四。|Synonyms: preach, sermonize, lecture, pontificate | Antonyms:  None (It's difficult to have a direct antonym for "moralizing") | verb (third-person singular simple present moralises, present participle moralising, simple past and past participle moralised)


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *moraliser*, from *moral* ("moral") + *-iser* (suffix forming verbs).  Ultimately from Latin *moralis*.
