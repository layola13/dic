meta-ethics:/ˈmɛtəˌɛθɪks/ | n. | 元伦理学 |  The branch of ethics that seeks to understand the nature of ethical properties, statements, attitudes, and judgments. It does not prescribe moral principles or values but investigates the meaning of moral language and the metaphysics of moral facts. | Meta-ethics explores questions like "What does it mean for something to be good?" rather than "Is lying wrong?". | 近义词: philosophical ethics | 反义词: normative ethics, applied ethics | 词性: noun

Etymology:  "meta-" (Greek: "after," "beyond," "with," "beside," "among," "self") + "ethics"
