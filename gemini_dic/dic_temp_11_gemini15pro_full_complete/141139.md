onye:/ˈɔːnjɛ/ | n. | 人，个人 (尤指 Igbo 族人) | A term used primarily in Igbo language to refer to a person, individual, or somebody, often with connotations of respect and belonging within the Igbo community. | Example:  Onye Igbo kwuru okwu. (An Igbo person spoke.) | Example:  Onye ọ bụla nwere ikike ikwu okwu. (Everyone has the right to speak.) | Synonym: person, individual, somebody (general terms), mmadụ (Igbo) | Antonym:  N/A (No direct antonym for "person") | Noun

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Onye a na-arụ ọrụ kwa ụbọchị. = This person works every day.
Onye ọ bụla nwere ikike ịgụ akwụkwọ. = Everyone has the right to education.

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Onye ahụ na-agụ akwụkwọ ugbu a. = That person is reading a book now.
Ha na-ekwu okwu ugbu a. = They are talking now.

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
Onye nkuzi a akụzirila ọtụtụ ụmụ akwụkwọ. = This teacher has taught many students.
Onye isi ala agwala okwu. = The president has spoken.

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
Onye ahụ anọwo na-arụ ọrụ kemgbe ụtụtụ. = That person has been working since morning.
Ha anọwo na-akparịta ụka ruo awa abụọ. = They have been talking for two hours.

一般过去时 (Simple Past):
Onye ahụ gara ụlọ akwụkwọ ụnyaahụ. = That person went to school yesterday.
Onye ọrụ ugbo ahụ kọrọ ọka n'afọ gara aga. = The farmer planted corn last year.

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
Onye ahụ na-agụ akwụkwọ mgbe m batara. = That person was reading when I came in.
Ha na-eri nri mgbe oge ruru. = They were eating when the time came.


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
Onye ahụ agụọla akwụkwọ ahụ tupu m abịa. = That person had read the book before I arrived.
Ha eriela nri tupu anyị abịa. = They had eaten before we arrived.

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
Onye ahụ anọwo na-arụ ọrụ ruo ọtụtụ awa tupu m abịa. =  That person had been working for many hours before I arrived.
Ha anọwo na-akparịta ụka ruo ogologo oge tupu mmadụ abịa. = They had been talking for a long time before anyone arrived.
