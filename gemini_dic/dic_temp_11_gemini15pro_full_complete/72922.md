fonder:/ˈfɑːndər/ (US), /ˈfɒndə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更喜欢的，更深情的|more affectionate or loving, especially over a long period of time|例句：I'm growing fonder of her every day. 我一天比一天更喜欢她。

例句：He's fonder of his dog than of his children. 他更喜欢他的狗胜过他的孩子。

近义词：more affectionate, more loving, dearer, closer|反义词：less affectionate, indifferent, hostile|词性:形容词

fonder:| n/a | n/a | n/a | n/a | n/a | n/a |词性: n/a  (fonder itself is not a noun)


词源: From Middle English *fondere, compartive of *fond (“foolish, silly; desirous”), from Old English *fundian (“to strive after, aspire to; be intent on; intend”), from Proto-Germanic *fundōną (“to try, test, tempt”). Related to Old Frisian fundia (“to intend”), Dutch vinden (“to find”), German finden (“to find”). More at find. The semantic development is from "foolishly loving" to simply "loving."
