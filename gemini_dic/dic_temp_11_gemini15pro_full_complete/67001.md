expiatist:/ɪkˈspaɪətɪst/ | n. |赎罪者，补偿者|A person who atones for sin or wrongdoing, especially in a religious context.  A person who makes amends or reparations for a wrong.|例句：The expiatist sought forgiveness for his past transgressions. 赎罪者寻求宽恕他过去的罪过。

例句：She dedicated her life to charitable work, acting as an expiatist for the excesses of her youth. 她将自己的一生都奉献给了慈善事业，以此来弥补她年轻时的放纵。|近义词：atoner, redeemer, penitent |反义词:sinner, offender |词性:noun

expiate:/ˈekspieɪt/ | vt. |赎罪，补偿|To make amends or reparation for (a sin, crime, or offense); atone for.|例句：He sought to expiate his guilt through acts of charity. 他试图通过慈善行为来赎罪。

例句：The company tried to expiate its environmental damage by investing in renewable energy. 这家公司试图通过投资可再生能源来弥补其对环境造成的损害。|近义词：atone for, redeem, make amends for |反义词:commit, offend |词性:verb

expiation:/ˌekspiˈeɪʃən/ | n. |赎罪，补偿|The act of making amends or reparation for a sin, crime, or offense; atonement.|例句：He performed acts of expiation for his sins. 他为自己的罪孽进行了赎罪。

例句：The community demanded expiation for the damage caused by the company. 社区要求公司对造成的损害进行赔偿。|近义词：atonement, redemption, reparation |反义词:sin, offense |词性:noun

词源:  From Late Latin *expiat-, past participle stem of expiare "to atone for," from ex- "out" + piare "to appease, purify, make good," from pius "dutiful, devout."
