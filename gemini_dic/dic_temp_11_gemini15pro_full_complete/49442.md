delphinine:/ˈdɛlfɪnaɪn/ | n. |飞燕草碱|A poisonous alkaloid, C24H35NO5, extracted from the seeds of *Delphinium staphisagria* (stavesacre) and other species of larkspur. It is used as a parasiticide.|Delphinine is highly toxic and can cause paralysis.|近义词: aconitine (similar toxicity and effects)|反义词:  (No direct antonym for a specific chemical compound.  Could consider "antidote" in a broader sense, but no single specific one exists.)|词性:noun
