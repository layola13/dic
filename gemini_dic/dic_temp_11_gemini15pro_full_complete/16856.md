basidiospore:/bəˈsɪdioʊspɔːr/| n. |担孢子|A reproductive spore produced by Basidiomycetes fungi, typically containing one haploid nucleus.  It is formed on a specialized cell called a basidium.|The mushroom released millions of basidiospores into the air.  蘑菇向空气中释放了数百万个担孢子。|近义词:spore|反义词:None|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From *basidium* + *spore*.  *Basidium* comes from the Greek *basis* meaning "base," referring to the club-shaped structure on which the spores are borne. *Spore* comes from the Greek *spora* meaning "seed."
