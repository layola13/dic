groggy: /ˈɡrɑːɡi/ (US) /ˈɡrɒɡi/ (UK)| adj.|昏昏沉沉的，迷糊的|Dazed, weak, or unsteady, especially from lack of sleep or drunkenness.|I felt groggy after the long flight. 长途飞行后我感到昏昏沉沉的。| I woke up feeling groggy and disoriented. 我醒来时感到昏昏沉沉，迷失方向。 |Synonyms: dazed, sleepy, lethargic, sluggish, dopey|Antonyms: alert, awake, energetic, lively, refreshed|adjective

groggy: /ˈɡrɑːɡi/ (US) /ˈɡrɒɡi/ (UK)| adj.|因酒精或药物而头昏脑胀的|Mentally and physically confused as a result of excessive alcohol or drugs|He was still groggy from the medication. 他仍然因为药物作用而头昏脑胀。|She was groggy after the party and couldn't remember how she got home.  派对结束后她昏昏沉沉的，不记得自己是怎么回家的。|Synonyms: intoxicated, inebriated, tipsy, woozy|Antonyms: sober, clear-headed|adjective
