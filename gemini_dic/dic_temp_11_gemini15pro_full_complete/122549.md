modeller:/ˈmɒdələ(r) (UK) /ˈmɑːdələr (US)| n.| 建模者，建模师|A person who creates models, often using computer software, to simulate or represent real-world systems or objects.|The modeller created a 3D representation of the proposed building. 建模师创建了拟建建筑的3D模型。|Synonyms: simulator, designer, creator|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "destroyer" in a specific context)|Noun

modeller:/ˈmɒdələ(r) (UK) /ˈmɑːdələr (US)| n.| 模特儿|A person who poses for artists or photographers, or displays clothes by wearing them.|The modeller wore the latest designs from the fashion house. 模特儿穿着时装店的最新设计。|Synonyms: model, mannequin|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "model" + "-er" (agent suffix).  "Model" ultimately comes from the Old French "modelle" and the Italian "modello," meaning "pattern" or "mold," derived from the Latin "modulus," meaning "measure" or "standard."
