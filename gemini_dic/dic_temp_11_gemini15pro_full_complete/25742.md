buoys:/bɔɪz/ | n. | 浮标，航标 | An anchored float serving as a navigation mark, to show reefs or other hazards, or for mooring. | The buoys marked the channel into the harbor.  浮标标示了进入港口的航道。| synonyms: float, marker, beacon | antonyms: anchor, sinker | noun


buoy:/bɔɪ/ | v. | 使浮起，支撑，鼓励 | To keep afloat or support; to raise the spirits of; encourage. |  The life jacket buoyed him up in the water. 救生衣使他漂浮在水中。| synonyms: uplift, encourage, support, sustain | antonyms: depress, discourage, sink | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *buie, boie (“buoy, chain, fetter”), from Middle Dutch boeie, buie (“shackle, fetter”), from Proto-Germanic *baugijō (“ring, chain”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰewg- (“to bend, curve”). Related to bow (weapon). The verb is derived from the noun.
