hoggerel:/ˈhɒɡərəl/ (US) /ˈhɑːɡərəl/ (UK)| n.|打油诗，蹩脚诗|Crude, clumsy, or poorly written verse.|Example 1: His attempts at poetry were mere hoggerel. 他尝试写的诗只不过是打油诗。

Example 2: The poem was dismissed as childish hoggerel. 这首诗被斥为幼稚的打油诗。|Synonym: doggerel, jingle, verse|Antonym: poetry, lyric, sonnet|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hogrel, from hog + -rel, diminutive suffix.  The original meaning was likely something pertaining to hogs, perhaps a song or rhyme used by swineherds, later evolving to mean any crude or poorly made verse.  First recorded use circa 1400.
