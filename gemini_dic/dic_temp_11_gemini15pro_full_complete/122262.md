mithridatic:/ˌmɪθrɪˈdætɪk/ | adj. n. | 解毒的；万灵药；解毒剂 | An antidote; a medicinal concoction believed to protect against or counteract poison.  Derived from Mithridates VI, king of Pontus, who was said to have immunized himself against poisons. | He took a mithridatic every morning, believing it would protect him from any attempts on his life. 他每天早上都服用一种解毒剂，认为这样可以保护自己免遭任何暗杀。|  Near Synonyms: antidote, counterpoison, prophylactic | Near Antonyms: poison, toxin, venom | adjective, noun
