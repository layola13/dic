emulsify: /ɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪ/ /ɪˈmʌl.sə.faɪ/ | v. | 乳化 | To combine two liquids that don't usually mix, like oil and water, into a stable mixture. |  Example:  The chef emulsified the oil and vinegar to make a vinaigrette.  厨师将油和醋乳化，制成油醋汁。 | Example: Lecithin helps to emulsify the ingredients in the cake batter. 卵磷脂有助于乳化蛋糕糊中的成分。 | Synonyms: blend, mix, homogenize, combine | Antonyms: separate, divide, disintegrate | verb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *emulsionare, from Latin emulgere "to milk out," from ex- "out" + mulgere "to milk."  The original meaning referred to the milky appearance of some emulsions.
