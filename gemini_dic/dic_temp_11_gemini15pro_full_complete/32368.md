childs:/tʃaɪldz/ | n. | 孩子们 (non-standard, archaic or dialectal) |  An archaic or dialectal plural of child, meaning more than one child.  Now considered non-standard English. |  The childs were playing in the yard. (non-standard usage) | Near synonyms: children, kids, youngsters | Antonym: adults | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *childre, *childer, from Old English ċildru, plural of ċild (child).  The standard modern English plural is "children."
