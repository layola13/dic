deadlock:/ˈdedlɒk/ (UK), /ˈdɛdlɑːk/ (US)| n. v.|僵局，死锁|A situation, typically one involving opposing parties, in which no progress can be made.|例句：The negotiations reached a deadlock. 谈判陷入了僵局。|近义词：standoff, stalemate, impasse, gridlock|反义词：agreement, breakthrough, compromise|词性:名词, 动词

deadlock:/ˈdedlɒk/ (UK), /ˈdɛdlɑːk/ (US)| v.|使陷入僵局，停顿|Cause (a situation or process) to become deadlocked.|例句：The talks were deadlocked over the issue of pay.  谈判在薪酬问题上陷入了僵局。|近义词：stall, block, obstruct|反义词：resolve, settle, mediate|词性:动词
