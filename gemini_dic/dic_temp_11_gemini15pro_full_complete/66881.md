expectorator:/ɪkˈspɛktəreɪtər/ | n. |祛痰药，促痰药|A medicine that helps to bring up phlegm from the respiratory tract.|The doctor prescribed an expectorator to help clear his lungs.|Synonyms: mucolytic, antitussive (sometimes)|Antonyms: None (no direct opposite, but a cough suppressant might be considered a functional opposite)|noun

expectorate:/ɪkˈspɛktəreɪt/ | v. |咳出，吐痰|To cough or spit out phlegm from the lungs or throat.|He expectorated the phlegm into a tissue.|Synonyms: cough up, spit out, hawk|Antonyms: swallow, ingest|verb
