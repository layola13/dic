glamorization: /ˌɡlæməraɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 魅力化；美化；渲染 | The act of making something seem more glamorous or attractive than it actually is. |  The glamorization of smoking in old movies contributed to its popularity. 老电影中对吸烟的美化促成了它的流行。

He criticized the glamorization of war in the media. 他批评了媒体对战争的美化。 | 近义词：idealization, romanticization, glorification | 反义词：vilification, denigration, disparagement | 词性: noun


词源:  From the verb "glamorize," which comes from "glamour" (influenced by "grammar" due to a folk etymology associating glamour with magic spells) + "-ize" (suffix forming verbs).  "Glamour" ultimately derives from Scottish "gramarye," meaning "magic, enchantment," from Old French "gramaire."
