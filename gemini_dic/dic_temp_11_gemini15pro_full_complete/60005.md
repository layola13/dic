editorializing:/ˌedɪˈtɔːriəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
|n./ v.|发表社论；（尤指）在新闻报道中发表个人意见|The reporter was accused of editorializing in his news story.  这名记者被指责在他的新闻报道中发表个人意见。|The anchor tried to avoid editorializing during the broadcast.  主播努力避免在广播中发表个人意见。|近义词：commenting, opining, pontificating|反义词：reporting objectively, presenting facts|词性：动名词/现在分词 (gerund/present participle)

editorialize:/ˌedɪˈtɔːriəˌlaɪz/
|v.|发表社论；（尤指）在新闻报道中发表个人意见|The newspaper editorialized against the proposed law. 这家报纸发表社论反对拟议中的法律。|He tends to editorialize even when he's supposed to be reporting the facts. 即使他应该报道事实，他也倾向于发表个人意见。|近义词：comment, opine, pontificate|反义词：report objectively, present facts|词性：动词 (verb)


ETYMOLOGY->
From editorial + -ize.  "Editorial" ultimately derives from Latin editor, meaning "publisher."  The suffix "-ize" means "to make or become."
