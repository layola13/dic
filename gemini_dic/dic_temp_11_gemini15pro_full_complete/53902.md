discountable:/dɪsˈkaʊntəbl/ | adj. |可贴现的，可打折的| Something that is discountable can be reduced in price or value, often in a financial context.  It typically refers to bills, notes, or other financial instruments that can be sold at a price less than their face value before maturity.|  The company held a large amount of discountable commercial paper. 这家公司持有大量可贴现的商业票据。| synonyms: negotiable, transferable | antonyms: non-negotiable, non-transferable | adjective

discountable:/dɪsˈkaʊntəbl/ | adj. |可忽略的，不重要的| Something that is discountable can be disregarded or treated as unimportant. | His contribution to the project was discountable. 他对这个项目的贡献微不足道。 | synonyms: negligible, insignificant, trifling | antonyms: significant, important, crucial | adjective
