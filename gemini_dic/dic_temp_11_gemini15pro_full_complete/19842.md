bind-out:/ˈbaɪnd aʊt/| n.| 契约佣工；学徒|A system where a child, typically from a poor family, was bound by indenture to work for another family, often a farmer, in exchange for food, clothing, and shelter.|例句：He was a bind-out, working on a farm from a young age. 他从小就在农场当契约佣工。

例句：Bind-outs were common in the 19th century.  契约佣工在19世纪很常见。|近义词：indentured servant, apprentice|反义词: employer, master|词性: 名词

bind-out:/ˈbaɪnd aʊt/| adj.| 被契约的；受约束的 | Bound by contract or agreement, typically to work for someone.|例句：The bind-out boy was obligated to work until he reached adulthood. 这个被契约的男孩有义务工作到成年。

例句：Bind-out children often faced harsh conditions. 被契约的孩子们经常面临恶劣的条件。|近义词：indentured, contracted|反义词: free, liberated|词性: 形容词
