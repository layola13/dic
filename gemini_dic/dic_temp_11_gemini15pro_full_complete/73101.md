fopping:/ˈfɒpɪŋ/ /ˈfɑːpɪŋ/| adj. | 浮华的，纨绔子弟的 | Characteristic of or resembling a fop; behaving in a foppish manner; vain and affected. | He struck a fopping pose, twirling his cane. 他摆出一个纨绔子弟的姿势，转动着手杖。 |  He adjusted his cravat with a fopping gesture.  他以一种纨绔子弟的姿态调整了他的领巾。 | 近义词：dandyish, pretentious, affected, ostentatious | 反义词：unpretentious, modest, simple, unaffected | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From the noun "fop," meaning "a man who is concerned with his clothes and appearance in an excessive and affected way," from the 17th century. Possibly related to "fob" (a small pocket), suggesting a man who plays with his watch chain or other trinkets.
