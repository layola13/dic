colchicine: /ˈkɒltʃɪsiːn/ (UK) /ˈkoʊltʃɪsiːn/ (US) | n. | 秋水仙碱 | An alkaloid drug, originally extracted from plants of the genus *Colchicum* (e.g., autumn crocus), used to treat gout and familial Mediterranean fever. It inhibits cell division by disrupting microtubules. | Colchicine is effective in relieving the pain of acute gout attacks. 秋水仙碱可有效缓解急性痛风发作的疼痛。 |  Colchicine is used in plant breeding to induce polyploidy. 秋水仙碱用于植物育种以诱导多倍体。 | Synonyms: (for gout treatment) allopurinol, febuxostat | Antonyms: None (no direct pharmacological opposite) | noun
