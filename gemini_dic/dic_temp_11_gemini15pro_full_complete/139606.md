occasionalism:/əˈkeɪʒənəlɪzəm/ | n. | 偶然主义；临时解释；权宜之计 |  The philosophical doctrine that minds do not perceive external objects directly but only through the medium of ideas created by God on the occasion of physical stimuli.  The term also refers to the creation of a word or phrase for a particular occasion. |  His explanation seemed like an occasionalism, invented just to get him out of a tight spot. 他的解释似乎是权宜之计，只是为了让他摆脱困境。 |  The architect's use of an unconventional material was an occasionalism dictated by budget constraints. 建筑师使用非常规材料是受预算限制的权宜之计。 | synonyms: improvisation, expedient, makeshift, stopgap | antonyms:  premeditation, planning, foresight | noun
