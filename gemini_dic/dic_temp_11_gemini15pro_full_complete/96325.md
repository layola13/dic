indignation:/ˌɪndɪɡˈneɪʃn/| n. |愤慨，义愤，愤怒|A feeling of anger or annoyance provoked by what is perceived as unfair treatment.|例句：She expressed her indignation at the unfair treatment. 她对不公平的待遇表示愤慨。

例句：He felt a surge of indignation when he heard the news. 当他听到这个消息时，他感到一阵愤慨。|近义词：anger, resentment, fury, outrage|反义词：calmness, contentment, pleasure|词性:名词

Etymology: Late Middle English: from Old French indignacion or Latin indignatio(n-), from indignari ‘deem unworthy’.
