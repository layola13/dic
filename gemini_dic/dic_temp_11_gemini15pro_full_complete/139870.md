oculinacea:/ˌɒkjʊlɪˈneɪsiə/ | n. | 有孔虫目 | An order of foraminifera, single-celled protists, characterized by a test (shell) with chambers arranged in a spiral or helical pattern. |  Foraminifera of the order Oculinacea are found in marine environments. 有孔虫目的有孔虫存在于海洋环境中。|  The Oculinacea are an important group of microfossils used in biostratigraphy. 有孔虫目是生物地层学中一组重要的微化石。 |近义词: foraminiferans (广义上的) | 反义词: 无 | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *oculus* (eye) + -inacea (suffix forming order names).  The name refers to the often eye-like appearance of the aperture (opening) in the test.
