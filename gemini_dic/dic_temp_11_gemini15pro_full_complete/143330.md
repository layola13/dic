outed:/ˈaʊtɪd/ | v. | 揭发，公开（通常指性取向或秘密）| To reveal someone's secret, especially their sexual orientation or something they want to keep hidden. |  例句：He was outed as a spy by a former colleague. 他被一名前同事揭发是间谍。| 近义词：exposed, revealed, disclosed | 反义词：concealed, hidden | 词性: 动词

词源:  Derived from the verb "out," meaning to reveal or make public.  The "-ed" suffix creates the past participle form, which can be used as an adjective (e.g., "the outed politician") or as part of a passive verb construction (e.g., "He was outed by the media").
