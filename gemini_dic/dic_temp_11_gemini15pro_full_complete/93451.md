illuminism:/ɪˈlʊːmɪnɪzəm/
n.
启蒙运动; 智识启蒙; 光照派
A philosophical movement of the 18th century characterized by belief in the power of human reason and by innovations in political, religious, and social thought.  It emphasized reason, individualism, and skepticism.
The Enlightenment, also known as the Age of Reason, was an intellectual and cultural movement of the 17th and 18th centuries that stressed reason, individualism, and human rights.
例句：The ideals of illuminism greatly influenced the American and French Revolutions. 启蒙运动的思想极大地影响了美国和法国大革命。

例句：Illuminism challenged traditional authority and emphasized the importance of individual liberty. 启蒙运动挑战了传统权威，强调个人自由的重要性。

近义词：Enlightenment, rationalism
反义词：obscurantism, mysticism
词性：noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *illuminisme*, from *illuminer* "to illuminate," from Latin *illuminare*  "to light up, brighten," from *in-* "in, on" + *luminare* "to light," from *lumen* "light."
