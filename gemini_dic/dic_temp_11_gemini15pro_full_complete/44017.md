criminologists:/ˌkrɪmɪˈnɑːlədʒɪsts/ | n. |犯罪学家|Experts who study crime, its causes, and its impact on society.  They may work in academia, law enforcement, or government agencies.| Criminologists are studying the rise in cybercrime. 犯罪学家正在研究网络犯罪的上升趋势。|  The criminologists presented their findings at the conference. 犯罪学家在会议上展示了他们的发现。|近义词：crime analysts, criminal justice experts|反义词: N/A | Noun


Etymology->
From criminology + -ist.  Criminology itself comes from the French criminologie, coined by Italian anthropologist Raffaele Garofalo in 1885.  It combines the Latin crimen ("crime") and the Greek -logia ("study of").
