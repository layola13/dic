interclavicle: /ˌɪntərˈklævɪkəl/| n. |锁间骨，叉骨|A bone situated between the clavicles, typically found in reptiles, birds, and some mammals.  It connects the two clavicles (collarbones) and provides additional support to the shoulder girdle.| The interclavicle is a prominent feature of avian skeletons, helping to brace the wings during flight. 锁间骨是鸟类骨骼的一个显著特征，在飞行过程中有助于支撑翅膀。| The fossil showed a well-preserved interclavicle. 这块化石显示出一块保存完好的锁间骨。|Synonyms: episternum, furcula (in birds)|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
