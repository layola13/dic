machiavellianism:/ˌmækiəˈvɛliənɪzəm/ | n. | 马基雅维利主义;不择手段;权术 |  The employment of cunning and duplicity in statecraft or in general conduct.  It refers to a political philosophy, or a way of acting, that prioritizes expediency and achieving power over morality and ethical considerations.  Derived from the writings of Niccolò Machiavelli, especially *The Prince*.  |  His machiavellianism made him a successful politician, but he had few real friends. 他的马基雅维利主义使他成为一名成功的政治家，但他几乎没有真正的朋友。 | Synonyms: cunning, duplicity, ruthlessness, expediency, manipulation, artifice | Antonyms:  honesty, integrity, scrupulousness, forthrightness | noun


machiavellisms: /ˌmækiəˈvɛliənɪzəmz/ | n. | 马基雅维利主义的策略或行为（复数）| Instances or examples of Machiavellian behavior or principles; acts of cunning, deception, or manipulation employed to gain power or advantage. |  The CEO's machiavellisms eventually led to his downfall.  这位首席执行官的马基雅维利主义最终导致了他的垮台。 | Synonyms:  schemes, plots, intrigues, maneuvers, tactics | Antonyms:  fair play, sportsmanship, honesty, transparency | noun
