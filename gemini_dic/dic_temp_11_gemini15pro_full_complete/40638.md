conventionalists:/kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪsts/ | n. |因循守旧者，墨守成规者；约定论者| People who adhere to conventional beliefs or practices, especially in art or philosophy; those who believe that scientific laws and principles are based on agreements within the scientific community rather than reflecting objective reality. |The conventionalists refused to accept the new artistic styles. 那些墨守成规者拒绝接受新的艺术风格。|Synonyms: traditionalists, conformists, conservatives |Antonyms: radicals, revolutionaries, innovators | Noun

conventionalists:/kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪsts/ | n. |约定论者| In philosophy of science, those who hold the view that scientific theories are chosen by convention or agreement, not solely determined by empirical evidence. | The conventionalists argued that the choice between Euclidean and non-Euclidean geometry is a matter of convention. 约定论者认为，在欧几里得几何和非欧几里得几何之间的选择是一个约定俗成的问题。 |Synonyms:  None (specific to philosophy of science) |Antonyms: realists | Noun
