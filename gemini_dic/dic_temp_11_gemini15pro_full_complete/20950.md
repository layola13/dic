bleeds:/bliːdz/
verb
流血; 出血; 渗出; 悲痛; 同情
loses a large amount of blood.
The cut on his arm was bleeding badly. 他手臂上的伤口流血很厉害。


bleeds:/bliːdz/
noun
(尤指彩色印刷时)出血位
(especially in colour printing) the part of an illustration or photograph that extends beyond the edge of the printed page.
The image bleeds off the page. 图片超出了页面边缘。

词性: v. & n.

英文解析:
As a verb:
1. To lose blood from the body, as from a wound or injury.
2. To leak or seep out a coloured liquid or substance.
3. To feel or express great sadness or sympathy.

As a noun:
1. An instance of bleeding.
2. In printing, an image that extends beyond the edge of the page.

例句：

The soldier bleeds profusely from his wounds.  这个士兵的伤口大量出血。

The dye from the fabric bled into the water.  织物上的染料渗入了水中。


My heart bleeds for the victims of the disaster.  我对灾难的受害者深感同情。


The printer messed up and there's a bleed on the edge of the poster.  打印机出了故障，海报边缘有出血位。


The doctor stopped the bleed quickly. 医生迅速止住了血。

近义词: (v.) hemorrhage, gush, leak, ooze, weep, grieve, sympathize; (n.) outflow, seepage

反义词: (v.) clot, coagulate


词源: Old English *blēdan (attested in compounds), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch bloeden and German bluten.
