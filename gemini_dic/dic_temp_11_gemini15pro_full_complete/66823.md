expansiveness:/ɪkˌspænsɪvnəs/| n. |开阔；广阔；豪爽；健谈|The quality of being expansive, especially in terms of personality, behavior, or outlook.  It often suggests generosity, open-mindedness, and a willingness to share.|例句：His expansiveness made him a popular host. 他的豪爽使他成为受欢迎的主人。

例句：The expansiveness of the landscape was breathtaking.  这片风景的广阔令人叹为观止。|近义词：breadth, generosity, open-mindedness, affability, gregariousness|反义词：reserve, reticence, introversion, parsimony, taciturnity|词性:noun

expanse:/ɪkˈspæns/| n. |广阔；广阔的区域；浩瀚|A wide and open area or surface.|例句：The vast expanse of the ocean stretched before them.  浩瀚的海洋在他们面前伸展。

例句：The starry expanse above filled her with wonder.  头顶上繁星点点的天空让她充满了惊奇。|近义词：stretch, area, sweep, vastness|反义词：limit, confinement, restriction|词性:noun

expand:/ɪkˈspænd/| v. |扩大；扩张；展开；发展|To become or make larger or more extensive.|例句：The company plans to expand its operations overseas.  公司计划将其业务扩展到海外。

例句：Heat causes metal to expand.  热量导致金属膨胀。|近义词：enlarge, grow, increase, develop, extend|反义词：contract, shrink, reduce, diminish|词性:verb

expansive:/ɪkˈspænsɪv/| adj. |广阔的；浩瀚的；豪爽的；健谈的|Covering a wide area in space or scope; (of a person) outgoing and sociable; characterized by generosity or open-mindedness.|例句：She had an expansive view of the valley from her window.  从她的窗户可以看到山谷的广阔景色。

例句：He was in an expansive mood after winning the lottery.  他中了彩票后心情非常舒畅。|近义词：wide, broad, extensive, comprehensive, outgoing|反义词：limited, restricted, narrow, reserved, introverted|词性:adjective
