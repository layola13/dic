duodecimo:/ˌduːəˈdɛsɪmoʊ/ /ˌdjuːəˈdɛsɪmoʊ/
n.|十二开本|A size of book page resulting from the folding of each printed sheet into twelve leaves (24 pages).|This rare book was a duodecimo, making it small and portable. 这本稀有的书是十二开本的，小巧便携。|octavo, quarto|folio|noun
Etymology->
From Latin *duodecimus* (twelfth), from *duodecim* (twelve). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*dwóh₁ déḱm̥* (two ten).
