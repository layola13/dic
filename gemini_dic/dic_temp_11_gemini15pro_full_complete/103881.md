jubilee:/ˈdʒuːbɪliː/
n.
禧年，周年纪念，欢乐的节日
a period of celebration or rejoicing, especially one marking a special anniversary
The town held a jubilee to celebrate the 50th anniversary of its founding.  小镇举行禧年庆典，庆祝其成立 50 周年。
近义词：celebration, anniversary, festival
反义词：mourning, grief


indulgence:/ɪnˈdʌldʒəns/
n.
放纵，纵容，沉溺；（尤指宗教的）特赦
the act of allowing oneself or another person to have or do something enjoyable, especially something that is considered wrong or unhealthy; (especially in the Roman Catholic Church) a remission of part or all of the temporal punishment due to sin
She treated herself to a weekend of indulgence at a spa. 她犒劳自己去水疗中心享受了一个周末。
近义词：pampering, gratification, luxury
反义词：abstinence, self-denial


jubilee-indulgence:/ˈdʒuːbɪliː ɪnˈdʌldʒəns/
n.
禧年特赦 (尤指天主教)
a special indulgence granted by the Roman Catholic Church during a jubilee year
The jubilee-indulgence was offered to pilgrims who visited Rome during the Holy Year. 在圣年中，前往罗马的朝圣者可获得禧年特赦。
近义词：plenary indulgence, remission of sins
反义词：penance
