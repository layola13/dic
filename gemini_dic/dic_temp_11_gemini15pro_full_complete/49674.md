demissly:/dɪˈmɪsli/| adv.|谦恭地；顺从地；卑恭屈膝地|In a humble and submissive manner, often suggesting a lack of confidence or self-assertion.  It implies a meekness that can sometimes border on timidity.|She demissly accepted the criticism, even though she knew it was unfair. 她谦恭地接受了批评，即使她知道这是不公平的。|近义词：humbly, meekly, submissively, unassertively|反义词：boldly, confidently, assertively|词性:副词
词源: From Late Latin *demissus, past participle of demittere "to lower," from de- "down" + mittere "to send."
