channel:/ˈtʃænl/ (US), /ˈtʃænəl/ (UK)| n. vt.|频道；渠道；途径；海峡；引导；输送|A channel is a passage for water or other fluids to flow through, or a band of frequencies used in radio and television broadcasting.  It can also refer to a way of communicating or achieving something, or the act of directing something along a particular route. |The ship sailed through the English Channel. 这艘船驶过英吉利海峡。|Synonyms: course, waterway, passage, route, medium, conduit |Antonyms: blockage, obstruction| noun
channel:/ˈtʃænl/ (US), /ˈtʃænəl/ (UK)| vt.|引导；输送； channeling |To channel something means to direct it along a specific path or route. |He channeled his anger into his work. 他把愤怒发泄在工作上。|Synonyms: direct, guide, convey, transmit |Antonyms: disperse, scatter| verb
