foreappoint:/ˌfɔːrəˈpɔɪnt/| vt.|预先指定；预定|to appoint or ordain beforehand; predestine|God had foreappointed him to be the savior. 上帝预定他为救世主。|例句：The prophet had foreappointed the day of his return. 先知预言了他回归的日子。

例句：The council had foreappointed the next meeting date. 理事会预先指定了下次会议日期。

|近义词：predestine, preordain, predetermine|反义词: - |词性:动词
