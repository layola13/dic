mesentery:/ˈmezənˌtɛri/ | n. | 肠系膜 | A fold of the peritoneum which attaches the stomach, small intestine, pancreas, spleen, and other organs to the posterior wall of the abdomen. | The surgeon carefully examined the patient's mesentery.  外科医生仔细检查了病人的肠系膜。 | The mesentery was inflamed due to infection. 肠系膜由于感染而发炎。 | Synonyms: peritoneum (in a broader sense) | Antonyms: None | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mesenterium, from Ancient Greek μεσέντερον (mesénteron, “mid-intestine”), from μέσος (mésos, “middle”) + ἔντερον (énteron, “intestine”).
