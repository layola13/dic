gringo:/ˈɡrɪŋɡoʊ/ (US), /ˈɡrɪŋɡəʊ/ (UK)| n. |外国人（尤指美国人或英国人），老外|A term used, often disparagingly, in Latin America and Spain to refer to a foreigner, especially one from the United States or Britain.  It often carries a connotation of being naive, culturally insensitive, or exploitative.|He was known as "gringo" in the small Mexican village.  他在那个墨西哥小村庄里被称为“老外”。|Synonyms: foreigner, outsider, alien|Antonyms: native, local|noun

gringos:/ˈɡrɪŋɡoʊz/ (US), /ˈɡrɪŋɡəʊz/ (UK)| n. |外国人（尤指美国人或英国人），老外（复数）|Plural form of "gringo."|The gringos were easily spotted in the local market.  这些老外在当地市场很容易被认出来。|Synonyms: foreigners, outsiders, aliens|Antonyms: natives, locals|noun
