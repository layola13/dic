legitimization:/ləˌdʒɪtɪmaɪˈzeɪʃn/ | n. | 合法化，正统化 |The act of making something legitimate; the process of giving legal or official status or acceptance to something. |The legitimization of gambling has led to a surge in casino construction. 赌博合法化导致赌场建设激增。|  The government's legitimization of the rebel group sparked controversy. 政府将叛军合法化的举动引发了争议。 |  validation, justification, ratification | delegitimization, invalidation | noun


legitimate: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmət/ | adj. | 合法的，正当的 | Conforming to the law or to rules. | Is his business legitimate? 他的生意合法吗？ |  It's a perfectly legitimate question. 这是一个完全合理的问题。 | lawful, legal, valid | illegitimate, illegal, invalid | adjective


legitimate: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmeɪt/ | v. | 使合法，认可 | To make something legal or acceptable. | The government legitimized the use of force. 政府认可了使用武力。 | They are trying to legitimate their claim to the land. 他们正试图使他们对这片土地的所有权合法化。 | validate, justify, authorize |  invalidate, prohibit, ban | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin legitimatio, from Latin legitimare "to make lawful," from legitimus "lawful," from lex (genitive legis) "law."
