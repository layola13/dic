impudently:/ɪmˈpjuːdəntli/ | adv. | 无礼地，放肆地 | In a manner that is not respectful and shows a lack of shame or embarrassment.  Showing bold or disrespectful behavior. | She impudently interrupted the speaker mid-sentence. 她无礼地在演讲者讲话中途打断了他。| He impudently ignored his teacher's instructions. 他厚颜无耻地无视了老师的指示。 | 近义词: insolently, brazenly, rudely, disrespectfully | 反义词: respectfully, politely, humbly, deferentially | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *impudentli*, from Anglo-Saxon *impudent* (impudent) + *-lice* (like, -ly).  The word "impudent" comes from Latin *impudentem* (nominative *impudens*), present participle of *impudere* "to be ashamed," from *in-* (not) + *pudere* "to be ashamed."
