discomfiting: /dɪsˈkʌmfɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人不安的，令人尴尬的 | Making someone feel uneasy or embarrassed. |  The silence after his joke was discomfiting. 他讲完笑话后的沉默令人尴尬。|  The news of the layoffs was discomfiting for the entire staff. 裁员的消息让全体员工感到不安。 | 近义词: unsettling, disturbing, embarrassing, disconcerting | 反义词: comforting, reassuring, encouraging | 词性: 形容词

discomfit: /dɪsˈkʌmfɪt/ | v. | 使不安，使尴尬 | To make someone feel uneasy or embarrassed. |  He was discomfited by her pointed questions. 她尖锐的问题让他感到不安。 | The unexpected question discomfited him.  突如其来的问题让他很尴尬。| 近义词: unsettle, disturb, embarrass, disconcert | 反义词: comfort, reassure, encourage | 词性: 动词


词源:  From Old French *desconfit, past participle of desconfire “to defeat, rout, undo, destroy,” from des- “expressing reversal” + con- “with, together” + faire “to do, make.”  The original meaning of "defeat utterly" evolved to "frustrate the plans of" and then to "disconcert, embarrass."
