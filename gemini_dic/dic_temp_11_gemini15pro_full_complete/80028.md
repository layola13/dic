词源:  "Go-to-it" likely originated as a colloquial expression in American English in the late 19th or early 20th century.  It's formed from the verb "go to" (meaning to begin or undertake something) and the pronoun "it," referring to the task or activity at hand.  The reduplication emphasizes the energy and enthusiasm with which the activity is approached.
