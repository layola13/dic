mystique:/mɪˈstiːk/ | n. | 神秘感，神秘气氛；奥秘；神秘主义 | An aura of mystery or fascination surrounding something or someone. |  She cultivated an air of mystique, rarely giving interviews and always appearing in public veiled. 她营造了一种神秘的氛围，很少接受采访，并且在公共场合总是戴着面纱。| aura, charisma, allure | clarity, plainness | noun


mystiques:/mɪˈstiːks/ | n. | 神秘事物；奥秘（mystique 的复数）|  Plural form of mystique, referring to multiple auras of mystery or fascination. | The mystiques surrounding ancient civilizations continue to fascinate archaeologists. 古代文明的种种奥秘继续吸引着考古学家。 | auras, mysteries, enigmas | clarities, plainnesses | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mystique*, from Late Latin *mysticus* "mystical," from Greek *mystikos* "of mysteries, mystic," from *mystes* "one initiated into mysteries," from *myein* "to close, shut" (especially the eyes or mouth), related to *myōps* "short-sighted." The sense development is from "religious mysteries" to "something obscure or secret."
