entreating:/ɪnˈtriːtɪŋ/ | adj. | 恳求的，哀求的 | Making an earnest or urgent request; pleading. | She gave him an entreating look, hoping he would change his mind. 她恳求地看着他，希望他能改变主意。|  He raised his hands in an entreating gesture. 他举起双手做了个恳求的手势。 |近义词：pleading, begging, imploring, beseeching | 反义词：demanding, commanding, ordering | 词性: adjective

entreat:/ɪnˈtriːt/ | v. | 恳求，哀求 | To ask someone earnestly or anxiously to do something. |  I entreat you to reconsider your decision. 我恳求你重新考虑你的决定。 |  She entreated him for help. 她恳求他帮忙。| 近义词：beg, implore, beseech, plead | 反义词：demand, command, order | 词性: verb


词源:
From Middle English *entreten*, from Old French *entraiter*, from *en-* + *traiter* (“to treat, handle”), from Latin *tractāre* (“to handle, manage, drag”), frequentative of *trahere* (“to pull, draw”).
