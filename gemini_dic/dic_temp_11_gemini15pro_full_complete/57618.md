draughted:/ˈdræftɪd/ | v. | 起草；征召；选派 |  selected or enrolled, especially for military service; made a preliminary version of (something, such as a document). | He was drafted into the army during the war. 他在战争期间被征召入伍。

The architect drafted the plans for the new building.  建筑师起草了新建筑的平面图。| synonyms: conscripted, recruited, enlisted, formulated, composed | antonyms: rejected, discharged, demobilized | verb

draught:/drɑːft/ | n. |  气流；通风；草稿；汇票 | a current of air (usually coming into a room or vehicle); a preliminary version of a piece of writing; a single act of drinking or inhaling; an order for payment of money, especially from one bank to another. |  There's a terrible draught in this room.  这个房间里有一股可怕的穿堂风。

She's working on the first draught of her novel. 她正在写她小说的初稿。 | synonyms: breeze, current, draft, sketch, outline | antonyms: stillness, calm | noun


draughty:/ˈdrɑːfti/ | adj. |  通风的；有穿堂风的 |  (of a room or building) cold and uncomfortable because of currents of air. | The old house was draughty and cold.  这栋老房子通风不好，很冷。| synonyms: airy, breezy, ventilated | antonyms: airtight, sealed, stuffy | adjective
