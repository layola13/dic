dicrotism: /ˈdaɪkrətɪzəm/ | n. | 双脉搏; 重搏脉 |  A condition in which two palpable beats are felt with each heartbeat, the second beat occurring in the downstroke of the arterial pulse.  It is usually due to low blood pressure, aortic regurgitation, or other cardiovascular conditions. | The patient's dicrotism was a sign of his underlying heart condition. 病人的重搏脉是他潜在心脏病的一个迹象。 |  Synonym: double pulse | Antonym:  (No direct antonym exists, consider terms like "regular pulse" or "normal pulse") | noun

dicrotic: /daɪˈkrɒtɪk/ | adj. | 双脉搏的; 重搏脉的 | Relating to or characterized by dicrotism. | The dicrotic pulse was difficult to detect. 重搏脉很难检测到。 | Synonym: double-pulsed | Antonym: (No direct antonym, consider "monocrotic" or "single-pulsed," though these are less common) | adjective
