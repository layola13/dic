meridian:/məˈrɪdiən/ | n. | 子午线；经线；顶点；最高点 | A circle of constant longitude passing through a given place on the earth's surface and the terrestrial poles. Also, a peak or highest point of something. |  例句：The prime meridian passes through Greenwich. 本初子午线穿过格林威治。

例句：The sun was at its meridian. 太阳正当中天。| 近义词：longitude, apex, zenith, culmination, acme | 反义词：nadir, bottom | 词性：noun

meridian:/məˈrɪdiən/ | adj. | 最高点的；顶峰的；子午线的 | Relating to or situated at a meridian. Of or at the highest point. | 例句：The meridian sun beat down on the desert. 顶峰的太阳照射着沙漠。

例句：Meridian lines are used to measure longitude. 子午线用来测量经度。| 近义词：culminating, peak, zenithal | 反义词：nadir, lowest | 词性：adjective
