hypostatically:/ˌhaɪpəˈstætɪkəli/ | adv. | 本体论地，位格地 | In a manner relating to hypostasis, the underlying reality or substance.  It often refers to the distinct persons of the Trinity in Christian theology. |  The Council of Chalcedon defined Christ as being hypostatically united in two natures.  迦克墩会议将基督定义为在两个属性中本体论地联合。|  The divine and human natures of Christ are hypostatically united. 基督的神性和人性是本体论地联合的。|  essentially, substantially, ontologically | N/A | adverb
