heated:/ˈhiːtɪd/ | adj. | 加热的；激烈的；兴奋的 | made hot or warm;  characterized by anger or excitement | The heated debate continued late into the night.  这场激烈的辩论一直持续到深夜。 | synonyms: hot, fervent, passionate, intense | antonyms: cold, calm, cool | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *heten, from Old English hātan (“to command, order; call, name; promise; threaten”), from Proto-Germanic *haitaną (“to call, command”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱeyd- (“to incite, order”).  Cognate with Scots heat (“to call, name”), West Frisian hjitte (“to call, be called”), Dutch heten (“to be called”), German heißen (“to be called, to bid”), Danish hedde (“to be called”).  More at hight.  The adjective meaning "hot" developed from the past participle.
