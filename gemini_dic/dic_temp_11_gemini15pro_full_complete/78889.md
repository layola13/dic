ginnier:/ˈɡɪnɪər/| adj.|更狡猾的，更奸诈的|Comparative form of "ginny," meaning more sly, cunning, or crafty.  This word is informal and possibly dialectal.|He's become ginnier as he's gotten older, always trying to get one over on people. 他随着年龄的增长变得更加狡猾，总是试图占别人的便宜。|近义词：craftier, slyer, more cunning, trickier|反义词：more honest, more forthright, more naive|词性:形容词

词源:
Likely derived from "gin," a trap or snare, which itself comes from the Old French "engin," meaning ingenuity or skill.  The evolution suggests a shift from referring to a physical trap to a more metaphorical sense of cunning and trickery.
