manila:/məˈnɪlə/ | n. |马尼拉纸，马尼拉麻, 马尼拉(菲律宾首都)|A strong, brown paper originally made from Manila hemp, used for envelopes, folders, etc. Also refers to Manila hemp itself, and to the capital city of the Philippines. |Example 1:  The important documents were shipped in a sturdy manila envelope. 重要文件装在一个结实的马尼拉纸信封里运送。

Example 2: The ropes were made of strong manila. 这些绳子是用结实的马尼拉麻制成的。|Synonym:  abaca, jute (for the fiber) |Antonym:  N/A (no direct antonym for a type of paper or fiber/city)|noun


manila:/məˈnɪlə/ | adj. |马尼拉纸制的，马尼拉麻制的|Made from manila hemp or resembling the color of manila paper. |Example 1: She preferred manila folders for their durability. 她更喜欢用马尼拉纸文件夹，因为它们很耐用。

Example 2: The rope had a distinct manila color. 这根绳子有着明显的马尼拉纸的颜色。|Synonym:  abaca, jute (for the fiber-based adjective) |Antonym:  N/A |adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Manila, the capital city of the Philippines, from where the hemp and the paper were originally exported.  The city's name is believed to be derived from *may-nilà*, a Tagalog phrase meaning "there is indigo," referring to the indigo plant that grew in the area.
