demoralizing:/dɪˈmɒrəlaɪzɪŋ/
adjective
令人沮丧的，使士气低落的
Causing discouragement; destroying or impairing morale.
The team suffered a demoralizing defeat.  这支队伍遭受了一场令人沮丧的失败。
The constant criticism was demoralizing.  不断的批评令人沮丧。
近义词:discouraging, disheartening, dispiriting
反义词:encouraging, uplifting, inspiring
词性:adjective


demoralize:/dɪˈmɒrəlaɪz/
verb
使士气低落，使沮丧
To deprive (someone) of spirit, courage, or confidence; dishearten.
The loss demoralized the entire team.  这场失利使整个团队的士气低落。
Failing the exam badly demoralized her. 考试不及格让她很沮丧。
近义词:discourage, dishearten, dispirit
反义词:encourage, hearten, inspire
词性:verb


词源:
From French démoraliser, from dé- (expressing reversal) + moraliser "to moralize," from moral "moral."  源自法语 démoraliser，由 dé-（表示反转）+ moraliser “使道德化”组成，源自 moral “道德”。
