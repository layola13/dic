illuminatism:/ɪˌluːmɪˈnɪzəm/ | n. |启蒙主义；光明主义 | A philosophical movement of the 18th century that emphasized reason and individualism rather than tradition. It advocated the use of reason to examine accepted doctrines and institutions, and often led to attacks on religious dogma. | The French Revolution was heavily influenced by the ideals of illuminatism. 法国大革命深受启蒙主义思想的影响。|  The spread of illuminatism challenged the authority of the Church. 启蒙主义的传播挑战了教会的权威。 | 近义词: enlightenment, rationalism | 反义词: obscurantism, mysticism | 词性: noun

Etymology: From French *illuminisme*, from Latin *illuminare* "to illuminate," from *in-* "in" + *lumen* "light."
