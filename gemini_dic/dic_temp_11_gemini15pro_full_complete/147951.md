overreprimanding: /ˌoʊvərˌrɛprɪˈmændɪŋ/
| adj. | 过度责备的，过分训斥的 |  Criticizing or scolding someone excessively, beyond what is necessary or appropriate. | The teacher was overreprimanding the student for a minor mistake. 老师因为一个小错误过度责备了学生。| Synonyms: overcritical, overscolding, excessively critical | Antonyms: lenient, forgiving, understanding | adjective

overreprimand: /ˌoʊvərˌrɛprɪˈmænd/
| v. | 过度责备，过分训斥 | To criticize or scold someone excessively. | The manager overreprimanded the employee in front of his colleagues. 经理在他的同事面前过度责备了这个员工。 | Synonyms: overcriticize, overscold | Antonyms: praise, commend | verb

reprimand: /ˈrɛprɪˌmænd/
| n. | 谴责，训斥 | A formal expression of disapproval. | The soldier received a reprimand for disobeying orders. 士兵因不服从命令而受到训斥。| Synonyms: rebuke, reproof | Antonyms: praise, commendation | noun
| v. | 谴责，训斥 | To express disapproval formally. | The judge reprimanded the lawyer for his disrespectful behavior. 法官因律师的不尊重行为而谴责他。 | Synonyms: rebuke, reproof | Antonyms: praise, commend | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From re- (again) + premere (to press).  The original sense was "to check, restrain."  The meaning shifted to "to rebuke, censure" in the 17th century.
