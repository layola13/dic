bound-with:/ˌbaʊnd ˈwɪð/ | adj. | 合订的，装订在一起的 |  Used to describe books or other publications that are physically bound together in a single volume, often different works by the same author or on a similar subject. |  Example: The library has a first edition of his poems bound-with his early essays.  图书馆收藏了他的诗歌和早期散文合订的第一版。 | Example: I found a bound-with copy of volumes one and two of the encyclopedia. 我找到了一本合订的百科全书第一卷和第二卷。 | 近义词: bound together, collected | 反义词: separate, unbound | 词性: 形容词

bound-with:/ˌbaʊnd ˈwɪð/ | n. | 合订本 |  A book or publication consisting of several separate works bound together.  | Example: This bound-with edition contains all three novels in the trilogy.  这本合订本包含了三部曲中的所有三部小说。 | Example: The auction house sold a rare bound-with of Shakespeare's early plays.  拍卖行出售了一本罕见的莎士比亚早期戏剧合订本。| 近义词: composite volume | 反义词: separate volumes | 词性: 名词
