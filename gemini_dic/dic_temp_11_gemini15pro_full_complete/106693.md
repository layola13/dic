queue: /kjuː/ | n. | 队列，排队 | A line or sequence of people or things awaiting their turn to be attended to or processed. |  例句：There was a long queue at the bank.  银行排了很长的队。| 近义词：line, lineup, file | 反义词: disorder, chaos | 词性: 名词

cue: /kjuː/ | n.  vt. | 提示，暗示；线索；球杆；给…提示 | A signal, such as a word, phrase, or action, that indicates the time for something to happen or be done. A hint or suggestion.  A tapering rod used to strike the balls in billiards, pool, or snooker. | 例句：The actor waited for his cue to enter the stage. 演员等待他的提示上台。|例句: He cued the band to start playing. 他示意乐队开始演奏。 | 近义词：signal, hint, prompt, clue  | 反义词: none | 词性: 名词，动词

kew: /kjuː/ | n. | 英国伦敦西南部的一个地区，以皇家植物园闻名 | A district in southwest London, England, famous for its Royal Botanic Gardens. | 例句：We visited Kew Gardens last weekend. 我们上周末参观了邱园。 | 近义词： (as a place name - no synonyms) | 反义词: none | 词性: 名词
