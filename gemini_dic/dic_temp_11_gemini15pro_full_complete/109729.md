levels:/ˈlɛvlz/ (US) /ˈlɛvəlz/ (UK)| n.|水平，等级；级别；程度；楼层|A standard or point on a scale of achievement, quantity, or quality.|The company operates at several levels of management. 这家公司在多个管理层级运作。|synonyms: stages, grades, ranks, tiers, degrees|antonyms:  none (lack of levels is not a direct opposite, it's an absence of the concept)|noun

levels:/ˈlɛvlz/ (US) /ˈlɛvəlz/ (UK)| v.|使平坦；使平等；夷平；瞄准；对准|Make (something) level or flat.|They leveled the ground before building the house. 他们在建房子之前把地弄平了。|synonyms: flatten, even, smooth, plane|antonyms:  raise, elevate, incline|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French * livel, from Latin *libellum, diminutive of libra "balance, scales."  The verb sense evolved from the noun, relating to the idea of making something as flat as the arm of a balance scale.
