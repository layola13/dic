fiatist:/ˈfaɪətɪst/ /ˈfiːətɪst/| n.|法币主义者;法定货币支持者|A person who believes in or advocates for fiat money, a currency without intrinsic value that is established as money by government regulation.|The fiatist argued that gold-backed currency was outdated.|近义词: advocate of fiat currency, supporter of fiat money|反义词: gold bug, hard money advocate|词性:noun

Etymology:
From fiat + -ist.  "Fiat" comes from the Latin word "fiat," meaning "let it be done," used in the context of a decree or authoritative command.  "-ist" is a suffix used to form nouns denoting a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system.
