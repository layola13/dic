crawfish:/ˈkrɔːfɪʃ/ (US) /ˈkrɔːfɪʃ/ (UK)| n. vi.|小龙虾；退缩；放弃；食言|A small freshwater crustacean resembling a lobster, found in streams and rivers.  To back out of a commitment or agreement; to retreat or withdraw, often in a timid or awkward manner.|例句：We caught crawfish in the creek. 我们在小溪里抓小龙虾。|近义词：crayfish, crawdad, back out, retreat|反义词:advance, proceed|词性:名词, 动词


crawfished:/ˈkrɔːfɪʃt/ (US) /ˈkrɔːfɪʃt/ (UK)| v.|退缩；放弃；食言|(past tense and past participle of crawfish)  Backed out of a commitment or agreement; retreated or withdrew, often in a timid or awkward manner.|例句：He crawfished on his promise to help. 他食言了，没有兑现帮忙的承诺。|近义词：backed out, retreated, withdrew|反义词:followed through, persevered|词性:动词
