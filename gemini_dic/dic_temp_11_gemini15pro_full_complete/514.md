abolitionary:/ˌæbəˈlɪʃəneri/
adjective
废除的；废奴的
Relating to or advocating for the abolition of a practice or institution, especially slavery.
The abolitionary movement gained momentum in the 19th century.  19世纪，废奴运动势头强劲。
The senator gave an abolitionary speech against the death penalty.  这位参议员发表了反对死刑的废除性演讲。
近义词:  anti-slavery, emancipationist
反义词:  pro-slavery, segregationist
词性: adjective
词源: From the noun "abolition" (from Latin abolitio, from abolere "to abolish") + the suffix "-ary" (forming adjectives meaning "relating to" or "connected with").
