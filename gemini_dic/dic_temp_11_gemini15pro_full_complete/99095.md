intercalary:/ɪnˈtɜːrkəleri/ (US), /ɪnˈtɜːkələri/ (UK)| adj. n.|闰的；插入的；闰日；闰月|Inserted into a calendar, especially a day or month added to make the calendar year correspond more accurately with the solar year; also, something inserted or introduced.|The Julian calendar introduced an intercalary day every four years.|近义词：inserted, interpolated, added, extra|反义词：removed, deleted|词性:形容词, 名词

intercalate:/ɪnˈtɜːrkəleɪt/ (US), /ɪnˈtɜːkəleɪt/ (UK)| v.|插入；嵌入|To insert (something) between other things; to introduce or interpolate.|They intercalated an extra day into the calendar.|近义词：insert, interpolate, interpose, introduce|反义词：remove, extract, delete|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin intercalaris ("of an intercalary day"), from intercalare ("to proclaim," "to insert"), from inter- ("between") + calare ("to proclaim"). The notion is of proclaiming a day to be inserted.
