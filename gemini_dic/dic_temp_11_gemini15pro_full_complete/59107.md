dyked:/daɪkt/
adjective
筑堤的；女同性恋的
Built with or protected by a dyke. Also, (offensive slang) used to describe a lesbian.
The dyked fields prevented the river from flooding the town.  筑堤的田野防止了河流淹没城镇。
She identified as a dyked woman. 她认为自己是一名女同性恋者。
近义词: lesbian, gay (for the slang meaning), embanked (for the literal meaning)
反义词: straight (for the slang meaning)
词性: adjective


dyked:/daɪkt/
verb (past participle of dyke)
筑堤；排斥
To furnish or protect with a dyke; or (slang) to shun or exclude, often due to perceived lesbianism.
The low-lying areas were dyked to prevent flooding. 低洼地区筑了堤坝以防洪水。
She felt dyked by her former friends after coming out.  出柜后，她感到被以前的朋友排斥了。
近义词:  embanked, excluded, ostracized
反义词: included, welcomed
词性: verb (past participle)


词源: From the noun "dyke," meaning a ditch or embankment, from Old English *dīc, from Proto-Germanic *dīkaz. The slang usage originated in the late 20th century.
