linchpin:/ˈlɪntʃpɪn/| n. |关键人物；关键事物；核心；中枢|A linchpin is someone or something that is very important to an organization, plan, or system, and without which it cannot function properly. |例句：She is the linchpin of the whole team. 她整个团队的核心人物。

例句：The free flow of information is the linchpin of a free society. 信息的自由流通是自由社会的中枢。|近义词：cornerstone, keystone, kingpin, essential element, pivotal factor |反义词: inessential component, unimportant factor |词性:noun

词源:  From Middle English *lynchpinne, from Old English *lynċpinne, from *lynċ (“latch, linch”) + *pinne (“pin”).  The word originally referred to a pin used to secure the wheel of a cart to the axle.  This essential function led to the metaphorical meaning of a key element.
