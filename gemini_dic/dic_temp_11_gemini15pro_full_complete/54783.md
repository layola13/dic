dispensablenesses:/dɪˌspɛnsəbəlˈnɛsɪz/
n.
可有可无；不必要性；可分配性
The state or quality of being dispensable; the ability to be dispensed with or done without.
Many employees were worried about the dispensablenesses of their jobs during the economic downturn.
许多员工在经济低迷时期担心自己工作岗位的可有可无。
The new software highlighted the dispensablenesses of several manual processes.
这款新软件凸显了几道人工流程的可废弃性。

近义词: unimportance, inessentiality, redundancy
反义词: indispensability, essentiality, necessity
词性: noun (plural)


Etymology:
From dispensable + -ness.  Dispensable comes from Late Middle English: from Old French dispensable or late Latin dispensabilis, from dispensare ‘dispense’ (see dispense). The suffix "-ness" is a native English suffix used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
