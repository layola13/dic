coquet:/kəʊˈkɛt/ (UK), /koʊˈkɛt/ (US)| vi. |调情，卖弄风情| to try to attract attention and admiration by being playful and charming | She coquetted with the bartender all evening. 她整晚都在和酒保调情。|近义词：flirt, tease, charm|反义词：ignore, shun|词性: 动词

coquette:/kəʊˈkɛt/ (UK), /koʊˈkɛt/ (US)| n. |卖弄风情的女人，妖妇| a woman who flirts lightheartedly | She's such a coquette, always batting her eyelashes at men. 她真是个爱卖弄风情的女人，总对着男人眨眼睛。|近义词：flirt, vamp|反义词：prude|词性: 名词

coquettish:/kəʊˈkɛtɪʃ/ (UK), /koʊˈkɛtɪʃ/ (US)| adj. |卖弄风情的，娇媚的| behaving in a way intended to attract attention and admiration by being playful and charming | She gave him a coquettish smile. 她给了他一个娇媚的微笑。|近义词：flirtatious, teasing, charming|反义词：modest, reserved|词性: 形容词

词源: From French coquette, from coq 'cock, rooster'.  Originally meaning 'little hen', the word came to be used for a flirtatious woman, perhaps because of the perceived strutting and preening of a rooster.
