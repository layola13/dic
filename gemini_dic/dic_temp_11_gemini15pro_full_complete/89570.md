honours:/ˈɒnəz/ (UK) /ˈɑːnərz/ (US)| n. |荣誉，优等学位，荣幸|A degree awarded with distinction.  It also refers to special distinction or recognition. |She graduated with first-class honours. 她以一等优异成绩毕业。|Synonyms: distinction, merit, credit, accolades, privilege |Antonyms: dishonor, disgrace, shame| noun

honour:/ˈɒnə/ (UK) /ˈɑːnər/ (US)| n. vt.|荣誉，尊敬，敬意，给…以荣誉|High respect; great esteem. As a verb, it means to show respect towards. |It was an honour to meet the Queen. 见到女王是我的荣幸。  They honoured him with a knighthood. 他们授予他骑士爵位以示敬意。|Synonyms: respect, esteem, reverence, dignity, privilege |Antonyms: dishonor, disgrace, disrespect, shame | noun, verb

honourable:/ˈɒnərəbl/ (UK) /ˈɑːnərəbl/ (US)| adj.|光荣的，可敬的，正直的|Worthy of respect; guided by integrity. |He led a long and honourable life. 他过着漫长而光荣的一生。|Synonyms: respectable, reputable, noble, upright, ethical |Antonyms: dishonourable, shameful, disreputable, corrupt | adjective
