chimera:/kaɪˈmɪərə/ /kɪˈmɪərə/
n.
嵌合体；虚构的怪物；幻想
A fire-breathing female monster with a lion's head, a goat's body, and a serpent's tail.  An illusion or fabrication of the mind; especially : an unrealizable dream.
例句：The chimera of economic stability proved elusive.  经济稳定的幻想证明是难以捉摸的。

例句：Genetic engineering has made the creation of chimeras a reality. 基因工程使创造嵌合体成为现实。

近义词：illusion, fantasy, delusion, monster, hybrid
反义词：reality, truth
词性：noun


chimera:/kaɪˈmɪərə/ /kɪˈmɪərə/
n.
嵌合体
An organism containing a mixture of genetically different tissues, formed by processes such as fusion of early embryos, grafting, or mutation.
例句：The researchers created a plant chimera by grafting different species together.  研究人员通过嫁接不同物种创造了一种植物嵌合体。

例句：Bone marrow transplants can create a chimera, where the recipient has some blood cells derived from the donor. 骨髓移植可以创造一个嵌合体，其中接受者有一些来自捐赠者的血细胞。

近义词：hybrid, mosaic
反义词：N/A
词性：noun
