hesitate:/ˈhezɪteɪt/ (US), /ˈhezɪtət/ (UK)| v. |犹豫，迟疑|To pause before doing or saying something, often because of uncertainty or nervousness.|She hesitated before answering the question. 她犹豫了一下才回答问题。| synonyms: pause, waver, falter| antonyms: decide, resolve, proceed|verb

hesitating:/ˈhezɪteɪtɪŋ/ (US), /ˈhezɪtətɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |犹豫的，迟疑的|Showing or feeling hesitation.|He gave a hesitating smile. 他迟疑地笑了笑。| synonyms: uncertain, tentative, hesitant| antonyms: decisive, confident, resolute|adjective

hesitation:/ˌhezɪˈteɪʃn/ (US), /ˌhezɪˈteɪʃn/ (UK)| n. |犹豫，迟疑|The act of hesitating or the state of being hesitant.|Without hesitation, he jumped into the river. 他毫不犹豫地跳进了河里。| synonyms: pause, indecision, doubt| antonyms: decisiveness, resolution, certainty|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *haesitare*, frequentative of haerere "to stick, adhere, hold fast"
