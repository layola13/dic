nonveritably:/ˌnɒnˈvɛrɪtəbli/
| adv. |不真实地，不确凿地|Not truly or factually; in a way that is not verifiable.  It describes something stated or presented as if it were true, but lacking actual evidence or confirmation.| He nonveritably claimed to have witnessed the event, providing no supporting details. 他不真实地声称目睹了这一事件，没有提供任何支持细节。|  The politician nonveritably accused his opponent of corruption without offering any proof.  这位政客不实地指责他的对手腐败，却没有提供任何证据。|  spuriously, falsely, untruly, deceptively | veritably, truly, genuinely, authentically | adverb
