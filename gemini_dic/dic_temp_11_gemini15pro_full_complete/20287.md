biscotin:/bɪskɒtɪn/ | n. | 小饼干; 小甜饼 | A small, crisp, twice-baked cookie or biscuit, often flavored with almonds or anise. |例句：She offered me a delicious biscotin with my tea. 她给我端了一杯茶，还有一块美味的小饼干。

|例句：The bakery is known for its delicate, almond-flavored biscotins. 这家面包店以其精致的杏仁味小饼干而闻名。

|近义词：cookie, biscuit, rusk |反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly cake or bread as a softer alternative) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *biscotin*, diminutive of *biscotu*, from Italian *biscotto* "twice-baked," from Latin *bis* "twice" + *coctus*, past participle of *coquere* "to cook."
