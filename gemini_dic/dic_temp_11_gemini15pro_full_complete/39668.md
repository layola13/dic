conservatorship:/kənˈsɜːrvətərʃɪp/
n.
监护权，保护权
A legal arrangement in which a court appoints a person to manage the affairs of another person who is deemed incapable of doing so themselves.  This can include managing finances, healthcare, and personal decisions.
Britney Spears was under a conservatorship controlled by her father. 布兰妮·斯皮尔斯曾处于她父亲控制的监护之下。

The court placed him under conservatorship due to his declining mental health. 由于他的精神健康状况下降，法院将他置于监护之下。

近义词: guardianship, custodianship, trusteeship
反义词: independence, autonomy

词性: noun

词源: From "conserve" (Latin *conservāre, from con- ‘together’ + servāre ‘to keep’) + "-ator" (agent suffix) + "-ship" (state or quality of being).
