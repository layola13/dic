mulishnesses:/ˈmjuːlɪʃnɪsɪz/| n. |固执, 倔强, 顽固|The state or quality of being mulish; stubbornness. | He displayed incredible mulishnesses in refusing to admit he was wrong. 他拒不承认错误，表现出令人难以置信的固执。|  His mulishnesses frustrated his parents. 他的固执使他的父母很沮丧。 |Synonyms: stubbornness, obstinacy, intransigence, recalcitrance | Antonyms: compliance, acquiescence, docility, amenability | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From mulish + -ness.  Mulish itself comes from mule (a hybrid offspring of a horse and a donkey known for its stubbornness) + -ish (forming adjectives).  Ultimately from Latin mulus "mule."
