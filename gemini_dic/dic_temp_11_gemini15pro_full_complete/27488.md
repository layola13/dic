campanularian: /ˌkæmpənjuˈlɛəriən/ | n. adj. | 钟形水螅，钟形水母 |  Relating to or resembling a campanula (bellflower); denoting hydrozoans of the order Campanulariae, which have a bell-shaped medusa stage. | The marine biologist studied the life cycle of the campanularian. 海洋生物学家研究了钟形水螅的生命周期。| The delicate, branching structure of the campanularian colony was fascinating to observe.  钟形水螅群体精致的分支结构令人着迷。 |  近义词: hydrozoan, hydroid | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific biological term) | 词性: noun, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin *Campanularia*, from Latin *campanula* ("little bell") + *-aria* (suffix forming nouns relating to things or places). *Campanula* is a diminutive of *campana* ("bell").
