fractiousness:/ˈfrækʃəsnəsnəs/
noun
易怒，暴躁
The state of being easily irritated or annoyed; quarrelsomeness.
The fractiousness of the crowd made it difficult for the speaker to be heard.  人群的暴躁使得演讲者很难被听到。
irritability, peevishness, cantankerousness, querulousness
agreeableness, amiability, placidity
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From fractious + -ness.  Fractious comes from the Latin word "fractus," meaning "broken," via the French word "fractieux."
