infeodation:/ɪnˌfiːəˈdeɪʃən/ | n. | 封地；授予封地；效忠 | The act of granting an estate as a fief; the state of being enfeoffed; the granting of rights or privileges in return for loyalty or service. |  The infeodation of the church to the state led to much conflict. 教会对国家的效忠导致了许多冲突。 | 近义词：enfeoffment, vassalage | 反义词: liberation, freedom | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *infeodatio, from Latin infeodare "to enfeoff," from in- "in" + feodum "fief."
