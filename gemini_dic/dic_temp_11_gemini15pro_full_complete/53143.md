diplomatic:/ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 外交的；圆滑的；策略的 |  Skilled in dealing with sensitive matters or people; tactful. |  He took a diplomatic approach to the problem, trying to avoid offending anyone. 他采取了一种外交手段来处理这个问题，试图避免冒犯任何人。|  tactful, politic, discreet, subtle |  undiplomatic, blunt, tactless, clumsy | adjective

diplomatically:/ˌdɪpləˈmætɪkli/ | adv. | 外交地；圆滑地；策略地 | In a way that is skilled in dealing with sensitive matters or people; tactfully. | He diplomatically avoided answering the controversial question. 他巧妙地避开了回答那个有争议的问题。 | tactfully, discreetly, subtly, carefully | undiplomatically, bluntly, tactlessly, clumsily | adverb

diplomatical:/ˌdɪpləˈmætɪkəl/ | adj. | 外交的；圆滑的；策略的 | This is a less common variant of "diplomatic," and generally considered archaic or nonstandard. It carries the same meaning as "diplomatic." |  He gave a diplomatical response to the journalist's probing questions.  他以外交辞令回应了记者的尖锐提问。 | tactful, politic, discreet, subtle |  undiplomatic, blunt, tactless, clumsy | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *diplomatique*, from Late Latin *diplomaticus*, from Greek *diplōma* "folded document, letter of recommendation," ultimately from *diploun* "to fold double."
