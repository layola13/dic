glad-handers:/ˈɡlædˌhændərz/
noun
热情握手者；虚情假意的人
A person who greets people in an excessively friendly and enthusiastic way, often insincerely, especially for personal gain.
He's such a glad-hander, always shaking hands and slapping backs, but I don't think he's genuinely interested in anyone. 他真是个虚情假意的人，总是握手拍背，但我认为他对谁都不是真心的。
Synonyms: flatterer, sycophant, schmoozer
Antonyms: recluse, introvert, cynic
n.

A person who greets people in an excessively friendly and enthusiastic way, often insincerely, especially for personal gain.

ETYMOLOGY->
From "glad hand" (a hearty handshake) + "-er" (suffix forming agent nouns). "Glad hand" emerged in the late 19th century in American English.
