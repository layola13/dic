gentian: /ˈdʒɛnʃən/ | n. | 龙胆; 龙胆根; 龙胆属植物 |A plant with trumpet-shaped flowers, usually blue, that grows in mountain regions. The root of some types of gentian is used to make medicine and to give a bitter flavour to drinks. |  The alpine meadows were dotted with vibrant blue gentians. 高山草甸上点缀着鲜艳的蓝色龙胆花。| synonyms:  none (specific plant)  | antonyms: none | noun


gentian: /ˈdʒɛnʃən/ | adj. | 龙胆色的; 龙胆制的 | Of or relating to gentians or their colour. | The liqueur had a distinctive gentian bitterness.  这种利口酒有一种独特的龙胆苦味。| synonyms: blue, azure (for the color) | antonyms: none | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *gentiāna, from Latin gentiāna (herba) (“Gentian (plant)”), from Gentius (c. 180 – 168 BC), an Illyrian king said to have discovered the medicinal properties of the plant.
