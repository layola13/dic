intire:/ɪnˈtaɪər/| adj.|完整的，全部的，完全的|An archaic or now chiefly poetic variant of entire.  It means whole, complete, or total.|The intire family was present at the wedding. 全家人都出席了婚礼。|近义词：entire, whole, complete, total|反义词：partial, incomplete, fragmented|词性:形容词
词源：From Middle English *entir, entyre, from Old French entier, entir (“whole, complete”), from Latin integer (“whole, untouched, complete”), from in- (“not”) + tangō (“touch”).  The spelling "intire" reflects an older pronunciation and is now less common than "entire".
