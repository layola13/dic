cavo:/ˈkɑːvoʊ/ (US), /ˈkævəʊ/ (UK)| n.|洞穴，凹处；地窖，酒窖|A hollow space in the ground, especially one that is natural or large.|The pirates hid their treasure in a secret cavo. 海盗们把他们的宝藏藏在一个秘密的洞穴里。|Cave, cavern, grotto, cellar|Hill, mound|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *cavo*, from Latin *cavus* ("hollow"). Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *ḱewh₂-* ("to swell, arch, bend, vault"). Related to English *cave*.
