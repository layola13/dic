crusader:/kruːˈseɪdər/| n.|十字军战士；拥护者；改革者|A person who campaigns vigorously for a cause, especially one involving social reform.  A warrior who engaged in any of the military expeditions undertaken by Christians of Europe in the 11th, 12th, and 13th centuries to recover the Holy Land from the Muslims.|He was a crusader for civil rights. 他是一位民权运动的斗士。|近义词：reformer, advocate, champion, campaigner|反义词：opponent, antagonist|词性:名词

crusader:/kruːˈseɪdər/| n.|十字军战士；拥护者；改革者|Referring to the historical Crusades.  A participant in any of the medieval military expeditions made by Europeans to recover the Holy Land from the Muslims.|The crusaders captured Jerusalem in 1099. 十字军于1099年占领了耶路撒冷。|近义词：knight, warrior|反义词：infidel (from the Crusader perspective)|词性:名词
