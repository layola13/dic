heliotrope:/ˈhiːliəˌtroʊp/ (US), /ˈhiːliəˌtrəʊp/ (UK)| n. adj.| 香水草，淡紫色|A plant of the borage family, with small fragrant purple or white flowers, or the purplish color of its flowers.  As an adjective, it describes something having a purplish color.|例句：The heliotrope in the garden emitted a sweet fragrance. 花园里的香水草散发着甜甜的香味。|近义词：lavender, lilac, violet|反义词: (As a color) orange, yellow|词性: noun, adjective

heliotropic:/ˌhiːliəˈtrɒpɪk/ (UK), /ˌhiːliəˈtrɑːpɪk/ (US)| adj.| 向日性的，向光的|Exhibiting or marked by heliotropism; turning or growing toward the light.|例句：The sunflowers exhibited heliotropic behavior, following the sun's path across the sky. 向日葵表现出向光性，跟随太阳的路径穿过天空。|近义词：phototropic|反义词: aphototropic|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *héliotrope*, from Latin *hēliotropium*, from Ancient Greek *ἡλιοτρόπιον (hēliotrópion)*, from *ἥλιος (hḗlios)* ‘sun’ + *τρέπω (trépō)* ‘I turn’.
