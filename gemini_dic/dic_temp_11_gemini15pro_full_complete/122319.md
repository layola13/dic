mitz:/mɪts/| n.| (犹太教)诫命，戒律；(尤指成年礼)受诫礼|A commandment of the Jewish law, especially one of the 613 enumerated in the Talmud; also, the ceremony for a Jewish boy or girl reaching religious maturity (usually 13 for boys, 12 for girls), at which they become bound to observe the commandments.|例句：He celebrated his bar mitzvah last year.  他去年庆祝了他的受诫礼。

例句：Keeping kosher is an important mitzvah.  遵守犹太教饮食戒律是一项重要的诫命。|近义词：commandment, precept|反义词: sin, transgression|词性: noun
