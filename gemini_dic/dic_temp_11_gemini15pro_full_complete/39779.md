consonant:/ˈkɑːnsənənt/ | n. adj.|辅音，辅音的|A basic speech sound in which the breath is at least partly obstructed and which can be combined with a vowel to form a syllable. |She pronounced each consonant carefully. 她仔细地发出每一个辅音。|近义词:non-vowel|反义词:vowel|词性:名词, 形容词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *consonant, *consonaunt, from Old French consonant, consonaunt (modern French consonne), from Latin consonāns, consonantis, present participle of consonāre (“to sound together, harmonize”), from con- (“together, with”) + sonāre (“to sound”).
