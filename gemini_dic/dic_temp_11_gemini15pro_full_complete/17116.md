batten-down-the-hatches:/ˈbætən daʊn ðə ˈhætʃɪz/ | idiom |  做好准备，严阵以待 | To prepare for a difficult situation, often by securing things and taking precautions.  Originates from nautical terminology, referring to securing hatches on a ship before a storm. | Example: Knowing a hurricane was coming, they battened down the hatches and stocked up on supplies.  知道飓风要来了，他们严阵以待，储备了物资。 | Synonym: prepare, brace oneself, take precautions | Antonym: be unprepared, be careless | phrase


batten:/ˈbætən/ | n. vt. | 板条，用板条固定 | A long, thin piece of wood or metal used to fasten something or as a support.  To fasten or secure something with battens. | Example: They used battens to secure the shutters on the windows. 他们用板条固定窗户上的百叶窗。 | Synonym: strip, slat, fasten, secure | Antonym: loosen, unfasten | noun, verb

down:/daʊn/ | adv. prep. adj. | 向下，沿着，下降的 |  From a higher to a lower place or position. | Example: The rain poured down. 雨倾盆而下。 | Synonym: downwards, below | Antonym: up, upwards | adverb, preposition, adjective

hatches:/ˈhætʃɪz/ | n. | 舱口，舱盖 | An opening or a cover for an opening in a ship's deck, roof, floor, etc., often one with a hinged lid. | Example: The sailors closed the hatches before the storm. 水手们在暴风雨来临前关闭了舱口。 | Synonym: opening, cover, lid | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "bottom" in some contexts) | noun
