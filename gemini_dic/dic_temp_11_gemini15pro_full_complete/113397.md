lying-in-state:/ˈlaɪɪŋ ɪn ˈsteɪt/
n.
公开瞻仰遗容；停灵
The ceremonial practice of placing the coffin of a distinguished person in a public place for viewing before burial.
The Queen's lying-in-state at Westminster Hall drew thousands of mourners. 女王的灵柩停放在威斯敏斯特大厅供公众瞻仰，吸引了成千上万的哀悼者。
lying in repose, public viewing
private burial, private funeral
noun
