aversenesses: /əˈvɜːrsnəsɪz/
| n. |厌恶，反感|A strong feeling of dislike or opposition.|His averseness to crowds led him to avoid large gatherings. 他对人群的厌恶使他避免大型聚会。|
|近义词: dislike, distaste, antipathy, repugnance, aversion |
|反义词: liking, fondness, preference, inclination, attraction |
|词性: Noun (plural) |


Etymology:
From "averse" + "-ness".  "Averse" comes from the Old French "avers" (turned away), ultimately from the Latin "aversus" (turned away from), past participle of "avertere" (to turn away).  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
