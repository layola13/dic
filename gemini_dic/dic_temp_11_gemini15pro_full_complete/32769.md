chock-full:/ˌtʃɑkˈfʊl/ | adj. | 充满的，塞满的 | Completely full; crammed. | The pantry was chock-full of canned goods. 食品储藏室里塞满了罐头食品。| The train was chock-full of commuters. 火车上挤满了通勤者。 |Synonyms: crammed, packed, jammed, overflowing, brimming | Antonyms: empty, vacant, bare | adjective

Etymology:
From "choke-full," where "choke" originally meant "cheek" (Old English *cēace), referring to the cheeks being full.  Over time, the meaning shifted to "full to the point of choking" and eventually to simply "very full."
