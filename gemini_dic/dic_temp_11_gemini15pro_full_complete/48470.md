deep:/diːp/
adjective|深的，深奥的，深刻的|extending far down from the top or surface; intense or extreme|The well is very deep. 这口井很深。|
近义词:profound, intense, extreme|反义词:shallow, superficial|词性:adjective

deep:/diːp/
adverb|深深地，深入地|far down or in; to a great extent or degree|He breathed deeply. 他深呼吸。|
近义词:profoundly, intensely, extremely|反义词:shallowly, superficially|词性:adverb

deep:/diːp/
noun|深处，深渊|the deep part or area of something|The ship sank into the deep. 船沉入了深渊。|
近义词:abyss, depths|反义词:surface, shallows|词性:noun


词源:Old English dēop "deep, profound, low," of Germanic origin; related to Old Saxon diop, Old Frisian diap, Dutch diep, Old High German tiof, German tief, Old Norse djūpr, Gothic diups.
