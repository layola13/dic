circuses: /ˈsɜːrkəsɪz/ | n. |马戏团；喧闹的场面|A company of performers who put on shows of horsemanship, acrobatics, juggling, wild animal acts, and clowning, typically in a large tent. Also, a situation or place of great activity or chaos.|例句：The children loved going to the circus. 孩子们喜欢去看马戏。

例句：The office was a complete circus on the day of the deadline. 截止日期那天，办公室里一片混乱。|近义词：carnival, fair, spectacle, commotion, pandemonium|反义词：order, calm, tranquility|词性: 名词

Etymology: From Latin *circus* "circle, ring," from Greek *kirkos* "circle, ring."  The sense of "public entertainment" developed in the late 18th century, referring to the circular arena where such performances were held.
