misappraises:/ˌmɪsəˈpreɪz/| vt.|错误地评估；低估；看错|To assess or evaluate something incorrectly, especially to underestimate its value or quality.|He misappraised the situation and made a costly mistake. 他错误地估计了形势，犯了一个代价高昂的错误。|The antique dealer misappraised the value of the vase and sold it for far less than it was worth. 古董商错误地估计了花瓶的价值，以远低于其价值的价格出售了它。|近义词：underestimate, undervalue, misjudge, miscalculate|反义词: overestimate, overvalue|词性:动词

词源:
From mis- (expressing wrongness or error) + appraise (to assess the value or quality of).  "Mis-" comes from Old English, while "appraise" comes from Old French "apreisier," meaning "to set a price."
