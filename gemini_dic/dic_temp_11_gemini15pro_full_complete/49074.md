dehydration:/ˌdiːhaɪˈdreɪʃn/
n.
脱水
The state of having lost a significant amount of bodily fluids.
The marathon runner suffered from dehydration. 马拉松运动员遭受脱水。
depletion, dryness
hydration, wetness
noun

dehydrate:/ˌdiːhaɪˈdreɪt/
v.
脱水，使…脱水
To lose or remove water from.
The hot sun dehydrated the hikers.  炎热的太阳使徒步旅行者脱水。
dry, desiccate
hydrate, moisten
verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *déshydratation*, from dés- (prefix meaning "removal, reversal") + hydrater ("to hydrate") + -ation (suffix forming nouns of action).  Hydrater itself comes from Greek *ὕδωρ (húdōr)* meaning "water".
