monitor:/ˈmɒnɪtə(r) (UK), /ˈmɑːnɪtər (US)| n. v. | 监视器；显示器；监控；班长；监听 |A device used to check or record something, or a person who observes a situation.  Also, to observe and check the progress or quality of something or someone over a period of time. |例句：I connected a second monitor to my laptop. 我给我的笔记本电脑连接了第二个显示器。|近义词：screen, display, observe, supervise, track|反义词:ignore, neglect|词性:noun, verb

monitor:/ˈmɒnɪtə(r) (UK), /ˈmɑːnɪtər (US)| n. v. | 监控；监听；班长 | To listen to or watch something secretly in order to obtain information. Or, a student appointed to assist a teacher in maintaining discipline. |例句：The police were monitoring the suspect's phone calls. 警方正在监听嫌疑人的电话。|近义词：observe, surveil, eavesdrop|反义词:ignore, disregard|词性:noun, verb
