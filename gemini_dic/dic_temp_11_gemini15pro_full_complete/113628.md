machicolated:/məˈʃɪkəleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 装有射击孔的，有垛口的 | Having machicolations; provided with projecting battlements with openings between the corbels that support them, through which missiles or burning objects could be dropped on attackers below. | The machicolated battlements of the castle offered excellent defensive positions. 城堡装有射击孔的城垛提供了极佳的防御阵地。 | Near synonyms: crenellated, embattled, fortified | Near antonyms: undefended, unprotected | adjective

machicolation:/məˌʃɪkəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 射击孔，垛口 |  (1) An opening between the corbels of a projecting parapet or battlement, used for dropping missiles or other objects on attackers below. (2) A projecting parapet or battlement with such openings. | The machicolations allowed the defenders to rain down stones and boiling oil. 垛口使得防御者可以向下倾泻石块和沸油。 | Near synonyms: battlement, parapet, embrasure | Near antonyms:  opening, breach (in a defensive structure)| noun
