benedictus:/ˌbɛnɪˈdɪktəs/| n. adj.|圣福若瑟祷文，受祝福的|A hymn of praise and thanksgiving, especially the Benedictus, a canticle traditionally included in Christian services such as Matins and the Eucharist. Also used as an adjective meaning blessed or consecrated.|例句：The choir sang the Benedictus beautifully.唱诗班优美地唱了圣福若瑟祷文。|近义词：blessed, consecrated, hallowed|反义词：cursed, unholy|词性:名词，形容词
Etymology: From Latin benedictus (“blessed”), past participle of benedicere (“to bless, speak well of”), from bene (“well”) + dicere (“to speak”).
