calls:/kɔːlz/ (British) /kɔːlz/ (American)
n.|呼叫，电话，访问|A request for something; a demand.  The act of telephoning. A brief visit.|He received many calls about the job opening.  他接到了很多关于这个职位空缺的电话。|visits, rings, summons|rejections, refusals|noun

calls:/kɔːlz/ (British) /kɔːlz/ (American)
v.|呼叫，打电话，访问|To speak to someone by telephone; to cry out; to make a short visit.|She calls her mother every Sunday. 她每个星期天都给她母亲打电话。|telephones, rings, visits|ignores, avoids|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *callen*, from Old Norse *kalla* (“to call, name, invite”), from Proto-Germanic *kalaną* (“to call”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱel-* (“to shout”).
