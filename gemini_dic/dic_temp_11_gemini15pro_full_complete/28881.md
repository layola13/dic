carpooled: /kɑːrpuːld/ | v. |拼车；合伙用车|To travel to a destination together in a single vehicle, typically to save money or reduce emissions. |Example 1: We carpooled to work to save on gas. 我们拼车上班以节省汽油。

Example 2: They carpooled to the concert to avoid parking hassles. 他们拼车去音乐会以避免停车麻烦。| 近义词:shared a ride, rode together|反义词: drove alone, drove separately |词性: 动词

carpooling: /ˈkɑːrpuːlɪŋ/ | n. |拼车；合伙用车|The act of sharing a vehicle with others to travel to a destination. | Example 1: Carpooling is a great way to reduce traffic congestion. 拼车是减少交通拥堵的好方法。

Example 2:  The company encouraged carpooling by offering designated parking spaces. 公司通过提供指定的停车位来鼓励拼车。|近义词: ride-sharing, lift-sharing |反义词: driving alone, solo driving | 词性: 名词
