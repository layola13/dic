manipulatively:/məˈnɪpjʊlətɪvli/ | adv. | 操纵地，控制地 | In a way that controls or influences someone or something unfairly or unscrupulously. | He manipulatively used her emotions to get what he wanted. 他操纵她的感情来达到自己的目的。 | Example: She manipulatively twisted the facts to make herself look innocent. 她巧妙地歪曲事实，使自己看起来很清白。 | Synonyms:  craftily, deviously, schemingly, shrewdly, artfully | Antonyms: honestly, openly, fairly, frankly, sincerely | Word type: Adverb

manipulative:/məˈnɪpjʊlətɪv/ | adj. | 操纵的，控制的 | Exercising unscrupulous control or influence over a person or situation. | He was manipulative and controlling in his relationships. 他在人际关系中控制欲强，爱耍手段。| Example:  She employed manipulative tactics to get her way. 她采用操纵策略来达到目的。 | Synonyms: controlling, scheming, exploitative, calculating | Antonyms: honest, forthright, straightforward, genuine | Word type: Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Latin word "manus" (hand) and "plere" (to fill), leading to "manipulus" (a handful, a company of soldiers) and eventually "manipulate" (to handle or manage skillfully), then to the adjective "manipulative" and finally the adverb "manipulatively".
