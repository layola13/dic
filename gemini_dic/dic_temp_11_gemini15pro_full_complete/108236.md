laughing-off: /ˈlæfɪŋ ɒf/ (UK) /ˈlæfɪŋ ɔːf/ (US)
|phrasal verb|一笑置之；付之一笑|To dismiss something unpleasant or embarrassing with humor, often to avoid dealing with it seriously.|He laughed off the accusations, claiming they were ridiculous. 他对这些指控一笑置之，声称它们很荒谬。|例句：She laughed off her mistake, pretending it didn't matter. 她对自己的错误一笑置之，假装没关系。|近义词：dismiss, disregard, shrug off, make light of|反义词：take seriously, dwell on|词性: 动词短语

laughing-off: /ˈlæfɪŋ ɒf/ (UK) /ˈlæfɪŋ ɔːf/ (US)
|n.|一笑置之|The act of dismissing something with laughter.|His laughing-off of the situation made everyone else uncomfortable. 他对这种情况一笑置之的态度让其他人感到不舒服。|例句：Her laughing-off of the criticism showed her confidence. 她对批评一笑置之表明了她的自信。|近义词：dismissal, disregard|反义词：serious consideration|词性: 名词 (由动词短语转化而来，使用较少)
