complicity:/kəmˈplɪsɪti/ | n. | 共谋，同谋，串通 |  The state of being involved with others in an illegal activity or wrongdoing. |  He denied any complicity in the robbery. 他否认参与了抢劫。| synonyms: collusion, connivance, collaboration, involvement  | antonyms: innocence, non-involvement | noun

complicity:/kəmˈplɪsɪti/ | n. | 牵连，共犯关系 | The state of being connected with or involved in something, especially something bad. | There's evidence of his complicity in the scandal. 有证据表明他与这起丑闻有牵连。| synonyms: involvement, implication, connection | antonyms: dissociation, detachment | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *complicitatem, from Latin complicit-, past participle stem of complicare "to fold together," from com- "together" + plicare "to fold."
