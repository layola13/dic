abracadabra: /ˌæbrəkəˈdæbrə/ | n. interj. |咒语，胡言乱语| A word formerly used as a charm or incantation, now used chiefly to express an air of magic or to mock pretentious or unintelligible language.  It originated from a Late Latin word believed to have Hebrew origins. Its original purpose was medicinal, written on an amulet and worn to ward off illness. | The magician waved his wand and uttered "abracadabra." 魔术师挥舞着魔杖，念着“阿布拉卡达布拉”。| synonym:  magic word, incantation, charm, spell | antonym: reality, fact | noun, interjection
