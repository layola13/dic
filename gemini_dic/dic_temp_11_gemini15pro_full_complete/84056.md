half-forgotten:/ˌhɑːf fərˈɡɒtn/ | /ˌhæf fərˈɡɑːtn/ | adj. | 半被遗忘的，几乎被遗忘的 | Partly or almost forgotten. | The old house was filled with half-forgotten memories.  这栋老房子里充满了几乎被遗忘的记忆。 | Example:  She found a half-forgotten letter in the attic.  她在阁楼里发现了一封几乎被遗忘的信。 | Synonyms: almost forgotten, dimly remembered, vaguely recalled | Antonyms: well-remembered, unforgettable, vivid | adjective

Etymology:
"half-" + "forgotten"
"half-" from Old English *healfe, from Proto-Germanic *halbaz
"forgotten" past participle of "forget" from Old English forgietan, from Proto-Germanic *fra-getan, meaning "to lose hold of, to let go"
