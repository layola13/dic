emenders:/ɪˈmɛndərz/
|n.|校订者；修改者；改善者|People who correct or improve something, especially a text or piece of legislation.|The emenders worked tirelessly to restore the original manuscript.|近义词:correctors, revisers, editors, improvers|反义词:corrupters, damagers|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *emender*, from Middle French *emendeur*, from *emendre* (“to amend, correct”), from Latin *ēmendāre* (“to free from faults, correct”), from *ex* (“out of”) + *menda* (“fault, blemish”).
