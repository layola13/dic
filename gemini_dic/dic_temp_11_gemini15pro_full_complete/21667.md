blustering:/ˈblʌstərɪŋ/ | adj. | 咆哮的，狂妄的；大吵大闹的 | characterized by strong winds and stormy weather; loud, noisy, and boastful, but lacking substance or effect | The blustering wind rattled the windows. 呼啸的大风拍打着窗户。| synonyms: boisterous, tumultuous, ranting, swaggering | antonyms: calm, gentle, quiet, meek | adjective

bluster:/ˈblʌstər/ | v. | 咆哮，怒吼；气势汹汹地说 | talk in a loud, aggressive, or indignant way with little effect |  He blustered about how he would get revenge. 他咆哮着说要如何报复。 | synonyms: rant, rave, storm, bellow | antonyms: whisper, murmur, soothe, appease | verb

bluster:/ˈblʌstər/ | n. | 咆哮，怒吼；喧闹 | loud, empty threats or protests; noisy, violent commotion |  His bluster didn't scare anyone. 他的咆哮并没有吓到任何人。| synonyms: ranting, raving, storm, commotion | antonyms: silence, stillness, peace, quiet | noun
