grandfathering:/ˈɡrændˌfɑːðərɪŋ/| n.|祖父条款；豁免条款；旧有权利保留|The act of exempting certain persons or entities from new rules or regulations, allowing them to continue operating under the previous, often more lenient, set of rules.  This allows older, established businesses or individuals to avoid complying with new requirements.|Existing businesses were grandfathered in under the new regulations.  现有的企业在新规下被保留了原有的权利。|The grandfather clause allowed older cars to remain on the road despite the new emission standards.  祖父条款允许旧车尽管有新的排放标准，仍然可以在路上行驶。|exemption, exception|restriction, prohibition|noun
