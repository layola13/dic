flatteringly: /ˈflætərɪŋli/ | adv. | 奉承地，讨好地，使人显得更漂亮地 | in a way that praises someone, often in a way that is not sincere, or makes someone look or seem better than they really are |  The portrait flattered her, making her look younger than she was. 这幅肖像画把她画得很好看，让她看起来比实际年龄更年轻。| synonyms:  complimentarily, fawningly, ingratiatingly | antonyms: unflatteringly, critically, honestly | adverb

flatter: /ˈflætər/ | v. | 奉承，讨好，使显得更漂亮 | to praise someone in a way that is not sincere, especially in order to get something from them, or to make someone look or seem better or more attractive than usual | He flattered her with compliments about her cooking. 他用恭维她厨艺的方式奉承她。| synonyms:  compliment, praise insincerely, butter up | antonyms: criticize, disparage, insult | verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *flatren*, from Old French *flater* (to stroke, caress, flatter), perhaps from Frankish *flatōn* (to flatten), from Proto-Germanic *flataną* (to flatten). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *plat- (flat, broad).
