convicted:/kənˈvɪktɪd/ | adj. | 被判有罪的；已被定罪的 |  Having been declared guilty of a criminal offense by the verdict of a jury or the decision of a judge in a court of law. | The convicted felon was sentenced to 20 years in prison.  已被定罪的重罪犯被判处20年监禁。 | Synonyms: condemned, sentenced, guilty | Antonyms: acquitted, innocent, exonerated | adjective

convicted:/kənˈvɪkt/ | vt. | 证明…有罪；使…知罪 | To prove or declare (someone) to be guilty of a criminal offense by the verdict of a jury or the decision of a judge in a court of law.  | The jury convicted him of murder. 陪审团判他谋杀罪。 | Synonyms: condemn, sentence, incriminate | Antonyms: acquit, exonerate, clear | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *convict*, from Latin *convictus*, past participle of *convincō* (“I conquer, overcome, prove wrong, refute, convict”), from *con-* (“together, with”) + *vincō* (“I conquer, overcome”).
