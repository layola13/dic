brown-bagger:/ˈbraʊnˌbæɡər/ |n.|自带午餐者；自带午餐的聚会；|Someone who brings their own lunch, typically in a brown paper bag, to work or school, often to save money or eat healthier.| Many brown-baggers eat in the park during their lunch break. 许多自带午餐者在午休时间在公园里吃饭。|Synonyms: lunch-packer, BYOLer (Bring Your Own Luncher) |Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but consider) restaurant-goer, cafeteria-patron |noun


brown-bag:/ˈbraʊnˈbæɡ/ | v. |自带午餐|To bring one's own lunch, typically in a brown paper bag, to work or school. | He brown-bags it every day to save money. 他每天自带午餐以省钱。|Synonyms: pack a lunch, bring your own lunch |Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but consider) eat out, dine out |verb


brown-bagging:/ˌbraʊnˈbæɡɪŋ/ | n. |自带午餐的行为；自带午餐的聚会|The practice of bringing one's own lunch. | Brown-bagging is becoming more popular as people try to eat healthier. 自带午餐的做法随着人们越来越注重健康饮食而变得越来越流行。|Synonyms: lunch packing, BYOL (Bring Your Own Lunch) |Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but consider) eating out, dining out |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the traditional use of brown paper bags to carry packed lunches.  The term emerged in the mid-20th century.
