asyndeton:/əˈsɪndɪtɒn/ /əˈsɪndɪtɑːn/ | n. |  省略连词 | The omission of conjunctions between words, phrases, or clauses. |  Example: "I came, I saw, I conquered." (omitting "and")  我来了，我看见了，我征服了。（省略了“和”） | Example: "He was brave, fearless, determined." (omitting "and") 他很勇敢，无畏，坚定。（省略了“和”） |  Synonym: parataxis | Antonym: polysyndeton | noun

PHRASE->
(Generally, asyndeton doesn't form set phrases.  It's a stylistic device employed within phrases and clauses.  Therefore, offering "phrases" isn't truly applicable. However, here are examples of *how* it might appear *within* a phrase-like construction):

Reduced clauses using asyndeton:
Venit, vidit, vicit (He came, he saw, he conquered)  = 他来了，他看见了，他征服了。
Eat, sleep, repeat = 吃，睡，重复
Quick, fast, in a hurry = 快，迅速，匆忙
Awake, alert, alive = 清醒，警觉，活着

No specific "phrases" with "asyndeton" exist as it's a device *used within* phrases, not a phrase itself.
