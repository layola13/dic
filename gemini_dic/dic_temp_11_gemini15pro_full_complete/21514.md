blue-chip:/ˈbluː tʃɪp/| adj. n.|蓝筹股；一流的；稳健的|A blue-chip stock is the stock of a large, well-established and financially sound company that has operated for many years. It is typically a company with a solid track record of earnings and dividend payments.  As an adjective, it describes something considered to be of high quality and reliable. |Example: Investing in blue-chip stocks is generally considered a safe bet. 投资蓝筹股通常被认为是安全的赌注。

Example:  She landed a blue-chip job right out of college. 她大学一毕业就找到了一份很棒的工作。 |近义词：top-notch, first-rate, prestigious, reliable|反义词:speculative, risky, unstable|词性:adjective, noun


blue chip:/ˈbluː tʃɪp/| n.|蓝筹股；绩优股|A blue-chip stock is the stock of a large, well-established and financially sound company. |Example:  This investment firm specializes in blue chips. 这家投资公司专门经营蓝筹股。

Example:  Blue chips are usually less volatile than smaller company stocks. 蓝筹股通常比小公司的股票波动小。|近义词：top-rated stock, high-grade stock|反义词:penny stock, speculative stock|词性:noun
