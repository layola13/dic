adjutor:/əˈdʒuːtər/| n.|助手，副官，帮手|A helper or assistant, especially a military assistant to a senior officer.|The general relied heavily on his adjutor for day-to-day tasks. 将军非常依赖他的副官处理日常事务。|Synonyms: assistant, aide, helper, deputy|Antonyms: opponent, enemy|noun

adjutor:/əˈdʒuːtər/| n.|助手，副官，帮手|Someone who helps or supports another person, especially in a practical way. |She acted as his adjutor in the project, organizing all the research materials.  她在项目中担任他的助手，整理所有的研究资料。|Synonyms: supporter, collaborator, partner|Antonyms: hinderer, obstructer|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adjutor, from Latin adjūtō ("I help"), frequentative of adjuvō ("I help"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eug- ("to increase, to strengthen").
