moorier:/ˈmʊəriər/ | adj. | 更荒凉的，更像荒野的 | More resembling or characteristic of a moor; more desolate and open. | The landscape became moorier as we climbed higher up the mountain.  我们越往上爬，景色就越荒凉。| Near synonyms: bleaker, wilder, more desolate, more barren | Near antonyms: more fertile, more cultivated, lusher | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From moor + -ier (comparative suffix).  Ultimately from Old English *mōr "moorland, marsh, bog,"  of Germanic origin.
