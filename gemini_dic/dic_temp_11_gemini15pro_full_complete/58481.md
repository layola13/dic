dudgeon:/ˈdʌdʒən/
noun
愤恨，恼怒
A feeling of indignation and resentment.
例句：He stormed off in high dudgeon. 他怒气冲冲地离开了。

例句：She left the meeting in a fit of dudgeon. 她愤然离开了会议。

近义词：resentment, indignation, anger, pique, umbrage, offense
反义词：pleasure, happiness, contentment, satisfaction
词性:n.

词源：
From Middle English *dogion, from Anglo-Norman *dogiun, *dugiun (“resentment, ill-will, indignation”), from Old French doignon, variant of deignon (“disdain, contempt, indignation”), from Late Latin *dignionem, from Latin dignatio (“esteem, dignity”), from dignus (“worthy”). More at deign, dainty.  Influenced by dungeon.
