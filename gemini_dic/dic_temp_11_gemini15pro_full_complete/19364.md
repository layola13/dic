bibliotaph: /ˈbɪbliəˌtæf/ | n. | 藏书家；爱书的人；尤指不读书只收藏书的人 | A person who collects or hoards books, especially one who does not read them. | He was a bibliophile, bordering on a bibliomaniac, but his brother was a true bibliotaph, his books pristine and untouched. 他是一个爱书的人，近乎于藏书癖，但他的兄弟是一个真正的藏书家，他的书崭新未动。 | 近义词: book collector, book hoarder | 反义词: bibliophile | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *biblion* ("book") + *taphos* ("tomb, burial").  Literally a "book burier."  The word implies that the books are hidden away and not used.
