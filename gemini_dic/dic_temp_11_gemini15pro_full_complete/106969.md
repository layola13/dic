lackeyed:/ˈlækid/| adj.|谄媚的，卑躬屈膝的|Acting in a subservient manner, like a lackey (a servant).  Showing excessive deference or attentiveness, often for personal gain.| He gave a lackeyed smile to the CEO, hoping to get a promotion.|近义词：servile, obsequious, fawning, sycophantic|反义词：independent, assertive, defiant|词性:adjective

词源:
From the noun "lackey" + "-ed".  "Lackey" comes from Old French "laquay," ultimately from Arabic "al-qa'id" meaning "the leader, governor."  The meaning shifted over time to refer to a soldier's personal attendant and then to any servant or follower.
