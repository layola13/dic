antimilitarisms:/ˌæntiˈmɪlɪtərɪzəmz/
n.
反军事主义，反军国主义
opposition to war and the maintenance of armies.
The rise of antimilitarisms after World War I reflected the widespread disillusionment with traditional justifications for armed conflict.  第一次世界大战后反军事主义的兴起反映了人们对传统武装冲突理由的普遍失望。
The various antimilitarisms movements often share common ground in their pacifist beliefs.  各种反军事主义运动通常在和平主义信仰方面有共同点。
近义词: pacifism, anti-war sentiment
反义词: militarism, pro-war sentiment
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix) : against, opposed to (from Greek anti-)
militarism (noun) : the belief or desire of a government or people that a country should maintain a strong military capability and be prepared to use it aggressively to defend or promote national interests. (from Latin militaris "of soldiers, military")
-ism (suffix): forming nouns denoting action, practice, or system
