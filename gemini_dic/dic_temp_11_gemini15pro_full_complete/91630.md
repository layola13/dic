hyperbaton:/haɪˈpɜːrbətɒn/ (UK) /haɪˈpɜːrbətɑːn/ (US)| n.| 修辞倒装法，移位修辞法| A figure of speech in which the expected word order is inverted, often for emphasis or poetic effect.  It involves rearranging the typical order of words or phrases in a sentence.| From ancient Greek ὑπέρβατον (hupérbaton) 'transposition', from ὑπερβαίνειν (huperbainein), from ὑπέρ (hupér) 'over' + βαίνειν (bainein) 'to go'.|

Example 1: "Some rise by sin, and some by virtue fall." (Shakespeare, Measure for Measure)  一些人因罪恶而升迁，一些人因美德而堕落。

Example 2:  "Object there was none. Passion there was none." (Edgar Allan Poe, The Tell-Tale Heart)  没有对象。没有激情。


近义词: inversion, anastrophe, transposition|
