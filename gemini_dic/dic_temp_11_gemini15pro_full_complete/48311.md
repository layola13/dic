decrepitudes: /dɪˈkrɛpɪtjuːdz/ /dɪˈkrɛpɪtuːdz/ | n. | 衰老；破旧（复数，较少使用）| Plural form of decrepitude, referring to multiple instances or aspects of decline due to age or wear.  | The decrepitudes of the old house were evident in the crumbling facade and leaky roof.  这栋老房子的破败从摇摇欲坠的外墙和漏水的屋顶就能看出来。|近义词：frailties, infirmities, debilities |反义词:youthfulness, vigor, robustness |词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *decrepitudo, from Latin decrepitus (“enfeebled by age”), past participle of decrepere, from de- + crepere (“to creak, rattle”).  The word suggests the creaking and rattling of old age or worn-out objects.
