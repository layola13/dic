outfroth: /ˌaʊtˈfrɒθ/ | v. | 超越，胜过（尤指在热情或兴奋方面）| to surpass or exceed in frothiness or exuberance, especially in enthusiasm or excitement |  The candidate outfrothed his opponent in the debate, whipping the crowd into a frenzy.  这位候选人在辩论中热情澎湃，胜过他的对手，使人群陷入狂热。| 近义词: outdo, surpass, excel, outperform | 反义词:  be outdone, lose to, fall behind | 词性: 动词

Etymology:  A combination of "out-" (meaning surpassing or exceeding) and "froth" (meaning foam or bubbles, often associated with excitement or agitation).  The word suggests surpassing someone in terms of displayed enthusiasm or excitement, as if producing more "froth."
