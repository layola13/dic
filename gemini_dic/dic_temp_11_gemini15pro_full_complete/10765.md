aquaculturists: /ˈæk.wə.kʌl.tʃər.ɪsts/ | n. | 水产养殖者 |  People who raise aquatic animals and plants, such as fish, shellfish, and seaweed, for food or other commercial purposes. |  The aquaculturists are experimenting with new methods to increase shrimp production. 水产养殖者正在试验新的方法来提高虾的产量。|  The aquaculturists monitored the water quality regularly to ensure the health of the fish. 水产养殖人员定期监测水质，以确保鱼类的健康。 | Synonyms: fish farmers, seafood farmers | Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "hunter-gatherers" in a broad sense) | Noun


Etymology:

* aqua- (Latin): water
* culture (Latin cultura, from colere 'to cultivate'): cultivation
* -ist (suffix): a person who practices or is concerned with something
