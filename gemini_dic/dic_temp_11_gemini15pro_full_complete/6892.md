anchoretic:/ˌæŋkəˈrɛtɪk/| adj. n.|隐居的，隐士的；隐士|Characterized by seclusion from society; of or relating to a hermit or anchorite.  A person who lives in seclusion, typically for religious reasons; a hermit.|He led an anchoretic life in the mountains, far from the distractions of the city. 他在山中过着隐居的生活，远离城市的喧嚣。|The anchoretic monk devoted his life to prayer and contemplation.  这位隐居的僧侣一生致力于祈祷和沉思。|近义词：hermitic, ascetic, reclusive, solitary|反义词：gregarious, social, outgoing|词性: adjective, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *anchorēticus*, from Greek *anachōrētēs* "one who has retired," from *anachōrein* "to retire, withdraw," from *ana- "back" + *chōrein* "to give way, withdraw."
