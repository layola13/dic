chlorhydrin: /klɔːrˈhaɪdrɪn/ | n. |氯醇| An organic compound containing both a chlorine atom and a hydroxyl group.  It is formed by the addition of hypochlorous acid to an alkene. |  Ethylene chlorohydrin is used in the production of ethylene oxide. 乙烯氯醇用于生产环氧乙烷。

Chlorohydrins are useful intermediates in organic synthesis. 氯醇是有机合成中的有用中间体。 | 近义词: halohydrin | 反义词:  (No direct antonym exists) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From chloro- + hydrin (from Greek hydōr, water).  "Chloro-" indicates the presence of chlorine, and "hydrin" refers to a hydroxyl group.
