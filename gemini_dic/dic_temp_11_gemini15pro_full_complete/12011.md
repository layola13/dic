arthropod:/ˈɑːrθrəpɒd/ (US) /ˈɑːrθrəpɑːd/| n. |节肢动物|An invertebrate animal of the large phylum Arthropoda, such as an insect, spider, or crustacean.  Characterized by a segmented body, jointed limbs, and usually a chitinous shell that undergoes moltings.|例句：Spiders, insects, and crabs are all arthropods. 蜘蛛、昆虫和螃蟹都是节肢动物。

|例句：The arthropod exoskeleton provides protection and support. 节肢动物的外骨骼提供保护和支撑。|近义词：invertebrate|反义词: vertebrate|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *arthropode*, from Ancient Greek ἄρθρον (árthron, “joint”) + πούς (poús, “foot”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰédʰ- (“to seize, take”).
