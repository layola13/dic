Choctaws: /ˈtʃɒktɔːz/ (UK) /ˈtʃɑːktɔːz/ (US) | n. | 乔克托人 |  A Native American people originally inhabiting what is now Mississippi, Alabama, and parts of Louisiana.  They were one of the Five Civilized Tribes and were forcibly relocated to Oklahoma during the Trail of Tears. | The Choctaws were known for their sophisticated agricultural practices. 乔克托人以其先进的农业实践而闻名。 |  The Choctaw language is part of the Muskogean language family. 乔克托语属于马斯科吉语系。 | synonyms: Choctaw Nation, Choctaw tribe | antonyms: N/A | noun
