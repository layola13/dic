desolators:/ˈdɛsəˌleɪtərz/
n.
破坏者，使荒芜者
Those who cause destruction or devastation, leaving a place barren or desolate.
The desolators ravaged the countryside, leaving only scorched earth and ruin.  破坏者蹂躏了乡村，只留下焦土和废墟。
The invading army acted as desolators, destroying everything in their path. 入侵的军队充当了破坏者，摧毁了他们前进道路上的一切。
近义词: destroyers, ravagers, devastators
反义词: creators, builders, protectors
词性: 名词(plural)

desolate:/ˈdɛsəlɪt/
adj.
荒凉的，孤寂的
Barren or laid waste; deserted and lonely.
The desolate landscape stretched for miles, devoid of any sign of life. 荒凉的景色绵延数英里，没有任何生命的迹象。
She felt desolate after her husband's death. 丈夫去世后，她感到十分孤寂。
近义词: barren, deserted, bleak, lonely
反义词: fertile, populated, lively, cheerful
词性: 形容词

词源:  From Latin *dēsōlātus*, past participle of *dēsōlāre* (“to abandon, forsake, lay waste”), from *dē-* (“from, away”) + *sōlus* (“alone”).
