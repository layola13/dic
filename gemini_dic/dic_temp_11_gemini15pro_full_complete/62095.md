enchainment:/ɪnˈtʃeɪnmənt/| n.|束缚，连锁，一系列事件|A situation in which someone is forced to do something or is prevented from doing something they want to do, or a series of events that happen one after the other like links in a chain.|The enchainment of events led to the inevitable conclusion. 这一系列事件导致了不可避免的结局。|近义词：bondage, captivity, chain, fetter, shackle, series, sequence, succession|反义词:freedom, liberation, release|词性:noun

enchainment:/ɪnˈtʃeɪnmənt/| n.|连锁，连接|The act of linking things together, especially abstract ideas or concepts.|The poem explores the enchainment of love and loss. 这首诗探讨了爱与失去的联系。|近义词：linking, connection, concatenation|反义词:separation, disconnection|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French enchainer, from en- (“in, into”) + chain (“chain”). Ultimately from Latin catena (“chain”).
