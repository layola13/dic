blacklisted:/ˈblækˌlɪstɪd/ | adj. | 被列入黑名单的，被禁止的 | Put on a blacklist; barred or excluded from a job, activity, or privilege.  |  Example: The actor was blacklisted after the scandal.  这位演员在丑闻之后被列入了黑名单。 | Example:  Several websites are blacklisted by the company's firewall.  公司防火墙将几个网站列入了黑名单。 | Synonyms: banned, barred, boycotted, ostracized, prohibited | Antonyms:  accepted, approved, permitted, welcomed | adjective

blacklisting:/ˈblækˌlɪstɪŋ/ | n. | 列入黑名单 | The act of putting someone or something on a blacklist. | Example: The blacklisting of the director effectively ended his career.  将这位导演列入黑名单有效地结束了他的职业生涯。 | Example: The company engaged in the blacklisting of union members.  该公司参与了将工会成员列入黑名单的活动。| Synonyms: banning, barring, boycotting | Antonyms: accepting, approving, permitting | noun
