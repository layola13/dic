glamorously: /ɡlæmərəsli/ | adv. | 迷人地，华丽地 | In a glamorous manner; attractively, excitingly, and often in a way that suggests wealth and success. | She was glamorously dressed in a sparkling evening gown. 她穿着闪耀的晚礼服，打扮得光彩照人。|  例句：The actress posed glamorously for the photographers.  这位女演员迷人地摆姿势供摄影师拍照。| 近义词：attractively, elegantly, stylishly, luxuriously | 反义词： plainly, simply, dully | 词性：副词

glamorously: /ɡlæmərəsli/ | adv. | 耀眼地，光彩夺目地 | In a way that is full of glamour and excitement. | The party was glamorously decorated with chandeliers and flowers. 派对用枝形吊灯和鲜花装饰得富丽堂皇。| 例句：The ballroom was glamorously lit for the grand occasion. 舞厅灯火辉煌，为盛大的场合增添了光彩。| 近义词：splendidly, magnificently, brilliantly | 反义词：drably, plainly, simply | 词性：副词


词源: From the word "glamour" + "-ously" (adverbial suffix). "Glamour" itself comes from the Scottish word "gramarye," meaning magic, enchantment, or spell.  This evolved from "grammar," which originally referred to any kind of learning, especially occult learning or magic.
