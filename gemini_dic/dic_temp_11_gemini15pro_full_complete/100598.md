intice:/ɪnˈtaɪs/| vt.|引诱，怂恿|To tempt someone to do something, usually something that is wrong or unwise, by offering something pleasant or exciting.|The advertisements entice customers to buy their products by promising huge discounts. 广告通过承诺巨额折扣来引诱顾客购买他们的产品。|She was enticed by the offer of a free trip to Hawaii. 她被免费去夏威夷旅行的提议所诱惑。|近义词：tempt, lure, seduce, attract|反义词：deter, discourage, repel|词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *enticer "to set on fire, excite," from Vulgar Latin *intitiare "to set on fire," from Latin in- "in" + titio "firebrand, torch."
