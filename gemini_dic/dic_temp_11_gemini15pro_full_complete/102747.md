jansenist:/ˈdʒænsənɪst/| n. adj.|詹森派教徒，詹森派的|A follower of Jansenism, a 17th-century theological movement within Catholicism emphasizing original sin, human depravity, and predestination.|例句：He was accused of being a Jansenist because of his austere lifestyle and emphasis on divine grace.  他因为其简朴的生活方式和对神恩的强调而被指责为詹森派教徒。

|例句：Jansenist beliefs were condemned by the Catholic Church. 詹森派的信仰被天主教会谴责。|近义词：Augustinian, rigorist|反义词：Jesuit, laxist|词性: noun, adjective

Etymology: From Jansenius + -ist.  Jansenius refers to Cornelius Jansen (1585-1638), Bishop of Ypres, whose theological writings inspired the movement.
