demurrage:/dɪˈmɜːrɪdʒ/ | n. |  滞期费，延误费 |  A charge payable to the owner of a chartered ship in respect of failure to load or discharge the ship within the time agreed. | The charterer had to pay a hefty demurrage for exceeding the laytime.  承租人因超过装卸时间而不得不支付巨额滞期费。 |  Synonyms: detention, delay charges | Antonyms: dispatch, expedition | noun


demur:/dɪˈmɜːr/ | v. |  表示异议，反对；犹豫，迟疑 | Raise doubts or objections or show reluctance.  | He demurred at the idea of working on a Sunday. 他反对星期天工作。 She demurred at his suggestion. 她对他的建议表示犹豫。| Synonyms: object, protest, hesitate, waver | Antonyms: agree, accept, assent, concur | verb


ETYMOLOGY->

demurrage: From Old French *demorage, from demorer "to linger, delay," from Latin demorari "to delay, linger," from de- "down, completely" + morari "to delay."
