nominalism:/ˈnɑːmɪnəlɪzəm/ | n. |唯名论|The philosophical theory that only individual things exist and that universal concepts like "redness" or "humanity" are merely names.  It asserts that abstract concepts, general terms, or universals have no independent existence but exist only as names or labels assigned to particular objects.|Example:  Nominalism denies the existence of abstract entities like "beauty" itself, arguing that the word "beauty" only describes individual beautiful things.|Synonyms:  terminism |Antonyms: realism, conceptualism |noun

nominalistic:/ˌnɑːmɪˈnəlɪstɪk/ | adj. |唯名论的|Relating to or characteristic of nominalism; adhering to the doctrine of nominalism.|Example: The philosopher presented a nominalistic interpretation of the concept of justice.|Synonyms: terministic |Antonyms: realistic, conceptualist |adjective
