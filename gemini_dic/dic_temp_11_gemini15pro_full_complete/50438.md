deprecatingly: /ˌdɛprɪˈkeɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 谦逊地；轻描淡写地；不以为然地 | Expressing disapproval of oneself, often with affected modesty.  | He deprecatingly waved away the compliments. 他谦逊地对赞美一笑置之。 |  Synonyms: modestly, humbly, self-effacingly | Antonyms: boastfully, proudly, arrogantly | word property: adverb

deprecating: /ˈdɛprɪkeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 贬低的；反对的；轻视的 | Expressing disapproval of. | She gave him a deprecating look. 她轻蔑地看了他一眼。| Synonyms: disapproving, critical, disparaging | Antonyms: approving, complimentary, praising | word property: adjective

deprecate: /ˈdɛprɪkeɪt/ | v. | 反对；轻视；贬低 | Express disapproval of. | The teacher deprecated the student's disruptive behavior. 老师批评了学生扰乱课堂秩序的行为。| Synonyms: disapprove of, criticize, condemn | Antonyms: approve of, praise, commend | word property: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *deprecatus, past participle of deprecari (“to avert by prayer, pray against, plead against”), from de- (“away”) + precari (“to pray, entreat”).
