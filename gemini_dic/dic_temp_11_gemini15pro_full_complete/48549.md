defalcator: /ˈdɪfɔːlkeɪtər/ (US), /ˈdɛfɔːlkeɪtər/ (UK)| n. | 亏空公款者；挪用公款者 | A person who misappropriates money entrusted to them, typically for personal gain. | The company discovered that one of their employees was a defalcator who had embezzled thousands of dollars. 公司发现他们的一名员工是一名挪用公款者，贪污了数千美元。| Synonyms: embezzler, peculator, misappropriator | Antonyms: benefactor, philanthropist | noun


defalcate: /ˌdɪˈfɔːlkeɪt/ (US), /ˌdɛˈfɔːlkeɪt/ (UK) | v. | 亏空；挪用（公款） | To misappropriate money entrusted to one, typically for personal gain. | The treasurer defalcated thousands of dollars from the club's funds.  财务主管挪用了俱乐部基金数千美元。 | Synonyms: embezzle, peculate, misappropriate | Antonyms: donate, contribute | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *defalcare "to cut off, diminish," from Latin de- "down, away" + falcare "to cut with a sickle," from falx "sickle." The financial sense developed from the idea of "cutting away" a portion of funds for oneself.
