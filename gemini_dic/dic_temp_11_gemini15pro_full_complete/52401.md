diddle:/ˈdɪdəl/| v.|欺骗，诈骗；拨弄，摆弄|To cheat or swindle someone out of something, typically a small amount of money. Also, to fiddle or play with something idly.|He diddled me out of five dollars. 他骗了我五块钱。|Synonyms: cheat, swindle, defraud, con, trick, deceive, hoodwink, bamboozle |Antonyms: be honest, be truthful|verb


diddle:/ˈdɪdəl/| v.|闲混，无所事事|To waste time or dawdle.|He just diddled around all day instead of working. 他整天无所事事，而不是工作。|Synonyms: dawdle, dally, loiter, linger, trifle,  mess around, fool around|Antonyms: work hard, be productive|verb
