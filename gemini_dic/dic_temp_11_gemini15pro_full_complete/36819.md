colonializing:/ˈkɒlənaɪzɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkɑːlənaɪzɪŋ/| v. |殖民化，殖民|Establishing control over a foreign territory and its people, often exploiting its resources and imposing one's own culture.|The British were colonializing many parts of Africa in the 19th century. 19世纪，英国正在殖民非洲的许多地区。|Synonyms: settling, occupying, conquering, subjugating|Antonyms: liberating, freeing, decolonizing|Verb (present participle)

colonializing:/ˈkɒlənaɪzɪŋ/ (US) /ˈkɑːlənaɪzɪŋ/| v. |殖民化，殖民|The act of establishing a colony or colonies.|European powers were actively colonializing the Americas for centuries.  几个世纪以来，欧洲列强一直在积极地殖民美洲。|Synonyms: imperializing, expanding|Antonyms: withdrawing, retreating|Verb (present participle)


ETYMOLOGY->
From colonize + -ing.  From colony + -ize. Ultimately from Latin colōnus (“farmer, cultivator, settler”).
