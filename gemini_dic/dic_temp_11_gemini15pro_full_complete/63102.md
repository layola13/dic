enshrines:/ɪnˈʃraɪnz/ | vt. | 珍藏；奉为神圣；铭记； |  To enclose in or as if in a shrine; to cherish as sacred. To preserve or protect something valuable. | The Constitution enshrines the basic rights of all citizens. 宪法 enshrines 所有公民的基本权利。|  The document enshrines the principles of democracy.  这份文件 enshrines 民主原则。 |  commemorates, preserves, sanctifies, enshrine | desecrates, profanes, violates | verb

enshrined:/ɪnˈʃraɪnd/ | adj. | 被珍藏的；被铭记的； | Preserved or cherished as sacred. |  These rights are enshrined in the constitution. 这些权利 enshrined 在宪法中。|  The memory of our fallen heroes is enshrined in our hearts. 我们逝去的英雄的记忆 enshrined 在我们心中。| cherished, hallowed, sacred | forgotten, disregarded | adjective
