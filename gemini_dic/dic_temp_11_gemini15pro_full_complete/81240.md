grandstand:/ˈɡrændstænd/ (US), /ˈɡrændstænd/ (UK)| n. |看台；哗众取宠的言行|A large, raised structure with rows of seats, typically at a racecourse or sports ground, from which spectators can watch the event. Also refers to ostentatious behavior intended to impress onlookers.|The fans cheered from the grandstands.  球迷们在看台上欢呼。|近义词：bleachers, stands, gallery |反义词: field, court| noun

grandstand:/ˈɡrændstænd/ (US), /ˈɡrændstænd/ (UK)| vi. | 哗众取宠；作秀|To behave in an ostentatious manner in order to impress onlookers.|The politician was accused of grandstanding for the cameras.  这位政治家被指责在镜头前哗众取宠。|近义词：showboat, show off |反义词: be humble, be modest| verb
