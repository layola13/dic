middle-of-the-road:/ˌmɪdl əv ðə ˈroʊd/| adj.|中间立场的，温和的，不激进的|Avoiding extremes; moderate.|例句：He has a middle-of-the-road political stance. 他持温和的政治立场。

例句：The restaurant offers a middle-of-the-road menu with something for everyone. 这家餐厅提供口味适中的菜单，适合各种口味。|近义词：moderate, centrist, average, mainstream|反义词：extreme, radical, unconventional, fringe|词性:形容词

Etymology:  This term originates from the idea of driving in the middle of the road, avoiding the ditches on either side.  It began being used figuratively in the mid-20th century to describe moderate political views.
