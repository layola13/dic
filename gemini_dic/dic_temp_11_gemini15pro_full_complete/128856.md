nihilism:/ˈnaɪɪlɪzəm/ /ˈniːɪlɪzəm/| n. |虚无主义，无政府主义|The rejection of all religious and moral principles, often in the belief that life is meaningless.|Many people found themselves turning to nihilism after the horrors of the war.  战争的恐怖使许多人转向虚无主义。|Synonyms: cynicism, pessimism, skepticism, negativity|Antonyms: optimism, faith, belief|noun


nihilisms:  (This is not a standard English word.  The word "nihilism" is typically used in the singular. While grammatically possible to pluralize it, it's rarely done and doesn't add a distinct meaning.) /ˈnaɪɪlɪzəmz/ /ˈniːɪlɪzəmz/| n. |(罕用) 虚无主义的各种形式|Different forms or instances of the rejection of all religious and moral principles.|While rarely used, "nihilisms" could theoretically refer to the various philosophical expressions of nihilistic thought throughout history.|Synonyms: (Same as nihilism, as this plural form is rarely used) cynicism, pessimism, skepticism, negativity|Antonyms: (Same as nihilism) optimism, faith, belief|noun
