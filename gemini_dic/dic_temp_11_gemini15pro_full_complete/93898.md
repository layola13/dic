immortalize:/ɪˈmɔːrtəlaɪz/ | vt. |使不朽，使永垂不朽|To make someone or something famous or remembered for a very long time.|Shakespeare immortalized the name of Hamlet. 莎士比亚使哈姆雷特的名字永垂不朽。|
immortalize:/ɪˈmɔːrtəlaɪz/ | vt. |赋予不朽的生命|To give someone or something eternal life.|The gods of ancient Greece were believed to be immortalized. 古希腊的神被认为是永生的。|
immortalize:/ɪˈmɔːrtəlaɪz/ | vt. |使永存，使不灭|To preserve something from destruction or oblivion.|These photographs immortalize the spirit of the era. 这些照片使那个时代的精髓永存。|


近义词:commemorate, celebrate, eternalize, memorialize
反义词:forget, obliterate, erase
词性:verb (动词)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *immortalizare, from Latin immortalis (“immortal”) + -izare, equivalent to in- (“not”) + mortalis (“mortal”).
