bleeder-resistor:/ˈbliːdər rɪˈzɪstər/| n. |泄放电阻|A resistor used to discharge the stored energy in a capacitor or other electrical component after the power is turned off, for safety purposes.|The bleeder resistor ensures that the capacitor is discharged quickly, preventing electric shock.|Synonyms: discharge resistor, drain resistor||noun
bleeder:/ˈbliːdər/| n. |出血的人/物|Someone or something that bleeds, often excessively. Can also refer to a person who is easily exploited or taken advantage of.|The cut was deep and he was a bleeder, so we applied pressure quickly.|Synonyms: hemorrhage, sufferer|Antonyms: coagulant||noun
resistor:/rɪˈzɪstər/| n. |电阻器|An electrical component that impedes the flow of current.|The resistor limited the current flowing through the circuit.|Synonyms: resistance||noun
