custodialism:/kʌˈstoʊdiəlɪzəm/ | n. |监护主义;监禁主义;托管主义 | A system in which the state assumes primary responsibility for the welfare of its citizens, often extending to significant control over their lives.  It can refer to government policies that prioritize control and containment, particularly of marginalized groups, over rehabilitation and integration. | The report criticizes the current system's emphasis on custodialism and its failure to address the root causes of crime.  该报告批评了当前制度对监禁主义的强调，以及未能解决犯罪的根本原因。|  The custodialism of the mentally ill in the past often led to inhumane treatment. 过去对精神病人的监护常常导致不人道的待遇。|Synonyms: paternalism, protectionism, guardianship | Antonyms: autonomy, self-reliance, independence | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "custody" (Latin: custodia "guarding, keeping, care, protection") + "-ial" (forming adjectives) + "-ism" (forming nouns indicating a doctrine, system, or practice).
