insolvent: /ɪnˈsɒlvənt/ (UK) /ɪnˈsɑːlvənt/ (US)| adj. n.|无力偿还的；破产者|Unable to pay debts owed.|例句：The company was declared insolvent after failing to secure further funding.  这家公司在未能获得进一步的资金后被宣布破产。

例句：He was declared insolvent and lost everything. 他被宣布破产，失去了一切。

|近义词：bankrupt, broke, ruined|反义词: solvent, affluent|词性:形容词

insolvent: /ɪnˈsɒlvənt/ (UK) /ɪnˈsɑːlvənt/ (US)| n.|破产者|A person who is unable to pay their debts.|例句：The insolvents were forced to sell their assets. 破产者被迫出售他们的资产。

例句：The number of insolvents has increased dramatically in recent years. 近年来，破产者的人数急剧增加。

|近义词：bankrupt, debtor|反义词: creditor|词性:名词
