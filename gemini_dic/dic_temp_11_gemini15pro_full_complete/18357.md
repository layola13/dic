bellowslike:/ˈbɛloʊzˌlaɪk/| adj.|像风箱一样的|Resembling bellows, especially in shape or function; expanding and contracting rhythmically.|The lungs work in a bellowslike manner, expanding and contracting to draw in and expel air.|近义词：expanding, contracting, pulsating, throbbing|反义词:static, rigid|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From 'bellows' + '-like'. 'Bellows' comes from Old English 'belg', meaning 'bag, skin,' related to Old High German 'balg' (bag, skin).  The '-like' suffix is a common English suffix meaning "resembling, having the characteristics of."
