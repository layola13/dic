antialbumose:/ˌæntiˌælˈbjuːmoʊs/
n.
抗白蛋白胨
A substance formed during peptic digestion of proteins, not coagulable by heat, and precipitated by saturation with ammonium sulfate.  It represents an intermediate product in the breakdown of proteins to albumoses and peptones.
Antialbumose is a product of protein digestion. 抗白蛋白胨是蛋白质消化后的产物。
Further digestion breaks down antialbumose into peptones.  进一步消化将抗白蛋白胨分解成蛋白胨。
近义词: proteose, deuteroalbumose
反义词: None (It's a specific biochemical substance, not a concept with a direct opposite.)
词性: noun
