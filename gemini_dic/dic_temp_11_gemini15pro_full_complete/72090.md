floatings:/ˈfloʊtɪŋz/| n.|漂浮物，浮游物|Things that are floating on the surface of a liquid. |例句：The floatings on the surface of the pond were mostly leaves and twigs. 池塘水面上漂浮的东西大多是树叶和树枝。

例句：Remove the floatings from the soup before serving. 上汤前把汤上的漂浮物撇掉。|近义词：driftwood, debris, flotsam|反义词:sinkings, sediment|词性:noun


floatings:/ˈfloʊtɪŋz/| adj.|漂浮的，浮动的|Describing something that is floating. This usage is less common than the noun form.  |例句：The floating islands of plastic in the ocean are a serious environmental problem. 海洋中漂浮的塑料岛屿是一个严重的环境问题。

例句：The floating dock bobbed gently in the harbor. 浮动码头在港口轻轻地摇晃。|近义词：buoyant, afloat|反义词:sunken, submerged|词性:adjective (less common)


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "to float" + "-ing" (present participle/gerund suffix) + "-s" (plural suffix).  "Float" ultimately derives from Old English *floatian, of Germanic origin.
