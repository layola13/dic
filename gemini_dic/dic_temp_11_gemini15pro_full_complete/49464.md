deluders:/dɪˈluːdərz/| n.| 欺骗者，迷惑者 | People who deceive others into believing something false.  Those who mislead or trick. | The deluders preyed on vulnerable individuals. 这些欺骗者专门捕食弱势群体。| Synonyms: deceivers, tricksters, charlatans, fraudsters | Antonyms: truth-tellers, honest people | Noun (plural)


Etymology:
From the verb "delude," from Late Latin "deludere"  (to mock, deceive), from "de-" (down, away) + "ludere" (to play).  The "-er" suffix denotes the agent or one who performs the action.
