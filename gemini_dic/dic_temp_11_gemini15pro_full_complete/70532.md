filibustered:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstərd/
verb
阻挠议事，冗长发言
to obstruct or delay legislative action, especially by making long speeches
The senator filibustered the bill for over 12 hours.  这位参议员用冗长的发言阻挠了这项法案超过12个小时。
The opposition party filibustered to prevent the bill from passing. 反对党采取了冗长辩论以阻止该法案通过。
Synonyms: delayed, obstructed, stalled, stonewalled
Antonyms: expedited, facilitated, hastened
verb

filibustering:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstərɪŋ/
noun/verb
阻挠议事，冗长发言/进行阻挠议事
the act of obstructing or delaying legislative action, especially by making long speeches / engaging in such obstruction
The filibustering went on for days, preventing a vote on the bill.  冗长辩论持续了数天，阻止了对该法案的投票。
His filibustering tactics angered members of both parties. 他的阻挠议事的策略激怒了两党成员。
The senator was filibustering the new healthcare bill.  这位参议员正在阻挠新的医疗保健法案。
They were filibustering in an attempt to delay the vote.  他们正在进行阻挠议事，试图推迟投票。
Synonyms: obstruction, delaying tactic, stonewalling
Antonyms: cooperation, expediting
noun/verb
