forswornness: /fɔrˈswɔːrnɪs/ | n. | 背信弃义，伪证 | The state of having broken an oath or vow; perfidy. | His forswornness shocked his friends, who had always considered him a man of honor. 他的背信弃义震惊了他的朋友们，他们一直认为他是一个正直的人。| Synonyms: perfidy, treachery, betrayal, faithlessness | Antonyms: loyalty, fidelity, faithfulness | Noun

forswear: /fɔrˈswer/ | vt. | 发誓放弃，作伪证 | 1. To renounce or deny under oath. 2. To formally reject or disavow a formerly held belief, usually under pressure. | He forswore all worldly possessions upon entering the monastery. 他进入修道院时放弃了所有的世俗财产。  She forswore her previous testimony and admitted to lying. 她放弃了之前的证词，承认自己撒谎了。| Synonyms: renounce, repudiate, abjure, disavow | Antonyms: affirm, uphold, maintain, aver | Verb
