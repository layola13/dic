dissociability:/dɪˌsoʊʃiəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可分离性; 分离性 | The capacity of something to be dissociated or the state of being dissociated. The ability of a mental process to operate independently of other mental processes. |  The dissociability of language and thought is a complex issue. 语言和思维的可分离性是一个复杂的问题。|  separability, detachability, disunity | unity, inseparability | noun

dissociate:/dɪˈsoʊʃieɪt/ | v. |  分离，分开；使脱离 | To separate or become separated; to disconnect or become disconnected. In psychology, to become disconnected from one's thoughts, feelings, memories, or sense of identity. | The company decided to dissociate itself from the controversial product. 公司决定与备受争议的产品脱离关系。 | separate, detach, disconnect | connect, unite, associate | verb

dissociated:/dɪˈsoʊʃieɪtɪd/ | adj. | 分离的，分裂的；脱离的 | Separated or disconnected; having become split off or detached from something else, especially one's thoughts, feelings, or sense of identity. | He felt dissociated from reality after the traumatic experience.  在经历了创伤性事件后，他感到与现实脱节。| detached, separated, disconnected | connected, united, integrated | adjective
