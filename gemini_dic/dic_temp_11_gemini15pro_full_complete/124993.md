mughal:/ˈmuːɡəl/ | adj. n. |  莫卧儿（的）;  |  a member of the Muslim dynasty of Mongol origin that ruled most of India from the 16th to the 19th century.  | He studied Mughal architecture. 他研究莫卧儿建筑。 | synonyms: Mogul, Timurid | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could be considered in opposition to other Indian empires like Maratha or Rajput) | adjective, noun

From Persian مغول‎ (muġul), from Mongolian ᠮᠣᠩᠭᠣᠯ (mongɣol, “Mongol”). Ultimately from Proto-Mongolic *mongɣol.
