bipolarizing:/ˌbaɪˈpoʊləˌraɪzɪŋ/
adjective
使两极分化的，造成分裂的
Causing something to divide into two sharply contrasting groups or sets of opinions.
The new law has been highly bipolarizing, with supporters claiming it protects freedom of speech while opponents argue it restricts it.  这项新法律造成了极大的两极分化，支持者声称它保护了言论自由，而反对者则认为它限制了言论自由。

The candidate's controversial stance on immigration is bipolarizing the electorate.  候选人关于移民问题的争议立场使选民两极分化。

近义词:divisive, polarizing, alienating
反义词:uniting, unifying, conciliatory
词性:adjective

词源: From bipolar + -ize + -ing.  来自bipolar（两极的） + -ize（使...化） + -ing（现在分词后缀）
