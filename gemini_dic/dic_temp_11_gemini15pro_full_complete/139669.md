occupies: /ˈɒkjʊpaɪz/ (UK) /ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪz/ (US)| v. |占据，占用；居住；使忙碌|fills, holds, or uses a space, area, or amount of time; resides or lives in a place; keeps someone busy or engaged|The painting occupies a prominent position on the wall. 这幅画占据了墙上的显著位置。|近义词：fills, inhabits, uses, takes up|反义词:vacates, abandons|词性:动词

Etymology:
From Middle English occupien, from Old French occuper, from Latin occupāre (“to seize, take possession of, occupy”), from ob (“towards, against”) + capiō (“to take, seize”).
