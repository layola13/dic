lisps:/lɪsps/ (plural of lisp) | n. | 咬舌，口齿不清 | A speech defect in which s and z are pronounced like th in thick and this, respectively.  It can also refer to the act of speaking with a lisp. | Example 1: He spoke with a noticeable lisp. 他说话明显口齿不清。

Example 2: Her lisp made it difficult to understand her sometimes.  她的咬舌有时让人难以理解她。| Synonym: slurs, speech impediment | Antonym: clear speech, articulate speech | noun

lisp: /lɪsp/ | v. | 咬着舌头说话，口齿不清地说 | To speak with a lisp. | Example 1:  She lisped when she said "sister." 当她说“sister”时，她咬着舌头说话。

Example 2: He lisps slightly, but it's charming. 他说话有点口齿不清，但这很迷人。 | Synonym: slur, mispronounce | Antonym: enunciate, articulate | verb
