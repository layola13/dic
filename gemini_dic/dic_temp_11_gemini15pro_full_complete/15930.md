balmer:/ˈbɑːlmər/| n.|巴尔默|Referring to the Balmer series, a series of spectral emission lines of the hydrogen atom that result from electron transitions from higher energy levels to the energy level with principal quantum number 2.  Relating to or characteristic of this series.|The Balmer lines are prominent in the spectra of many stars.|Synonyms:  (No direct synonyms, can use descriptions like "hydrogen spectral lines," "visible hydrogen emission lines")|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Named after Johann Jakob Balmer (1825-1898), a Swiss mathematician and physicist, who discovered the formula describing the wavelengths of these spectral lines.
