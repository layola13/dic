boreholes:/ˈbɔːrhəʊlz/ (UK) /ˈbɔːrhoʊlz/ (US)| n. |钻孔，井眼|A deep, narrow hole made in the ground, especially to locate water or oil.|例句：Engineers drilled boreholes to test the stability of the ground. 工程师钻了几个钻孔来测试地基的稳定性。

例句：Several boreholes were sunk to extract geothermal energy.  为了提取地热能，人们凿了几个井眼。

近义词：wells, shafts, holes|反义词: mound, hill|词性: 名词
