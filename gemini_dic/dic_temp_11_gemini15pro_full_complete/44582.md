croupier: /ˈkruːpieɪ/ (UK) /ˈkruːpiər/ (US) | n. | 荷官；赌场发牌员 |  A casino employee who collects and pays bets at a gaming table, such as roulette or blackjack. | The croupier spun the roulette wheel and waited for the ball to land. 荷官旋转轮盘，等待球落地。

The croupier dealt the cards with a practiced hand. 荷官熟练地发牌。


近义词: dealer | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "player" or "gambler") | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *croupier*, originally meaning "one who rides on the croup" (the rump of a horse), later "one who stands behind a gambler to give advice or lend money."  The term evolved to refer to the person managing the bank at a gaming table.
