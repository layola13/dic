cours:/kɔːr/ (UK), /kɔːrs/ (US)| n.| 课程; 路线; 行程;一道菜 |A series of lessons or lectures on a particular subject. Also, a direction or route taken; a sequence of events or actions; a part of a meal. |The chef prepared a five-cours meal. 厨师准备了一顿五道菜的大餐。|Synonyms: course (as in direction), path, route, curriculum, program, dish|Antonyms: detour, digression, deviation|noun


cours:/kʊər/ (rare, archaic)| v.| run or flow swiftly|To move swiftly or smoothly.  This usage is now quite rare. | The blood cours through his veins. 血液在他的血管中奔流。（古语用法，现在很少见） |Synonyms: rush, flow, stream, surge|Antonyms: stagnate, halt, stop|verb
