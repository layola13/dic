flosculous: /ˈflɒskjʊləs/ | adj. | 花哨的，华丽而空洞的 | Characterized by flowery and overly elaborate language, often lacking in substance.  |  His writing style is often criticized for being too flosculous. 他的写作风格经常因过于华丽空洞而受到批评。 | ornate, flowery, bombastic, pretentious | plain, simple, concise, unpretentious | adjective

Etymology:  Late 16th century: from Latin *flosculus ‘little flower’ (diminutive of flos floris ‘flower’) + -ous.
