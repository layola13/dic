hackney:/ˈhækni/ (UK) /ˈhækni/ (US)| n. adj. v.|出租马车；出租汽车；陈腐的；雇佣文人；出租；滥用|A hackney was originally a type of horse, later a horse-drawn carriage for hire, and now can refer to a taxicab. As an adjective, it means 'overused and unoriginal'.  As a verb, it means to make something commonplace or stale through overuse.|

例句：
The writer hackneyed the plot device of the love triangle.  这位作家滥用了三角恋的剧情设定。

例句：
We took a hackney carriage to the theatre. 我们乘出租马车去了剧院。

近义词：(n.) cab, taxi, carriage; (adj.) banal, clichéd, trite|
反义词：(adj.) original, fresh, innovative|
词性: noun, adjective, verb|


hackneyed:/ˈhæknid/ (UK) /ˈhæknid/ (US)| adj.|陈腐的；老套的|Hackneyed describes something that has been overused to the point of being unoriginal and uninteresting.|

例句：
The plot of the movie was hackneyed and predictable.  这部电影的情节老套且可预测。

例句：
His hackneyed phrases failed to impress the audience. 他陈腐的措辞未能打动观众。


近义词：banal, clichéd, trite, overused, stale|
反义词：original, fresh, innovative, novel, creative|
词性: adjective|
