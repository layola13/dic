canaanite:/ˈkeɪnənˌaɪt/ | n. adj.|迦南人；迦南人的；迦南语的|A member of a Semitic people inhabiting ancient Canaan in the second millennium BCE, or their language.  Relating to Canaan or its people.|例句：The Canaanites were known for their advanced civilization. 迦南人以其先进的文明而闻名。|例句：Canaanite culture influenced the Israelites. 迦南文化影响了以色列人。|近义词：Phoenician (sometimes)|反义词: Israelite (often presented as opposing group)|词性:noun, adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Canaanīta, from Ancient Greek Χαναναῖος (Khanaanaîos), from Biblical Hebrew כְּנַעֲנִי (kənaʿănī). Ultimately from a Northwest Semitic word meaning “lowlander”.
