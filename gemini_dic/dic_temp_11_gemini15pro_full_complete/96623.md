indulgers:/ɪnˈdʌldʒərz/
noun
沉溺者；纵容者；放纵者
People who indulge in something, especially something considered pleasurable or enjoyable.  Those who yield to their desires or whims.
The indulgers at the all-you-can-eat buffet went back for thirds and fourths.  那些自助餐的沉溺者们三番五次地回去拿食物。
synonyms: hedonists, sybarites, epicureans, bon vivants
antonyms: ascetics, abstainers
n.

indulge:/ɪnˈdʌldʒ/
verb
沉溺；放纵；纵容
Allow oneself to enjoy the pleasure of.
She indulged in a long hot bath. 她尽情享受了一个长时间的热水澡。
synonyms: revel in, luxuriate in, wallow in, bask in
antonyms: abstain from, refrain from, resist
v.

indulgence:/ɪnˈdʌldʒəns/
noun
沉溺；放纵；纵容
The act of indulging; gratification of a desire.
Chocolate is my only indulgence. 巧克力是我唯一的放纵。
synonyms: gratification, satisfaction, pleasure, enjoyment
antonyms: abstinence, self-denial, restraint
n.

indulgent:/ɪnˈdʌldʒənt/
adjective
纵容的；溺爱的
Having or showing a tendency to be overly generous to or lenient with someone.
He gave his children indulgent smiles. 他对孩子们露出溺爱的笑容。
synonyms: permissive, lenient, tolerant, forgiving
antonyms: strict, severe, harsh, austere
adj.
