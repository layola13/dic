draught:/drɑːft/| n.|通风; 气流; 一饮; 草稿|A current of air, especially one that enters a room or building and makes it cold.  A single act of drinking or swallowing. A preliminary version of a piece of writing.|例句：There's a terrible draught in here. 这里有股可怕的冷风。


例句：He took a long draught of beer. 他喝了一大口啤酒。


例句：She sent the first draught of her novel to her publisher. 她把小说的初稿寄给了出版商。|近义词：draft, breeze, gulp, sketch, outline|反义词:stillness, stuffiness|词性:名词

draught:/drɑːft/| n.| draught beer 生啤；散装啤酒|A type of beer that is served from a cask or keg, rather than from a bottle or can.|例句：I'd like a pint of draught beer, please. 请给我一品脱生啤。


例句：The pub had a wide selection of draught beers on tap. 这家酒吧有各种各样的散装啤酒。|近义词：draft beer, keg beer, cask beer|反义词:bottled beer, canned beer|词性:名词

draughts:/drɑːfts/| n.|西洋跳棋|A board game played by two people, each with 12 pieces, on a checkered board.|例句：We played a game of draughts. 我们玩了一局西洋跳棋。


例句：Draughts is a game of strategy. 西洋跳棋是一种策略游戏。|近义词：checkers (美式英语)|反义词:无|词性:名词
