lithagogue: /ˈlɪθəɡɒɡ/ (UK), /ˈlɪθəɡɑːɡ/ (US) | n. | 排石剂；利尿剂 | A medicine or other agent for dissolving or expelling calculi (stones) from the kidneys, bladder, or urinary tract. |  The doctor prescribed a lithagogue to help pass the kidney stone. 医生开了排石剂来帮助排出肾结石。| Example: The patient was given a lithagogue to help break down the stones in his kidneys.  病人被给予排石剂以帮助分解肾脏中的结石。 | Synonyms: antilithic,  calculus solvent | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps something that promotes stone formation) | noun
