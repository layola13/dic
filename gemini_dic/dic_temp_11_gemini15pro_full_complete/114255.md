maimed:/meɪmd/| adj.|残废的，伤残的|Seriously injured, usually with the result that a part of the body is permanently damaged. |例句：The soldier returned home maimed.  这名士兵残废回家。|例句：Many people were maimed in the explosion. 许多人在爆炸中致残。|近义词：crippled, disabled, injured, mutilated|反义词：healthy, uninjured, whole|词性:形容词

maim:/meɪm/| vt.|使残废，使伤残|To wound or injure someone so severely that a part of their body is permanently damaged.|例句：The accident maimed him for life. 这场事故使他终身残疾。|例句：He was maimed in a car crash. 他在一次车祸中致残。|近义词：cripple, disable, injure, mutilate|反义词：heal, cure|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *mahaigner "to wound, injure, mutilate," probably from a Vulgar Latin *mahaminare, related to Old Norse meiða "to hurt," Gothic gamaiþan "to maim."
