PHRASE->
dangle a carrot (引诱):  The company dangled a carrot of a promotion to motivate employees.  公司用升职来激励员工。
dangle something before someone (在某人面前晃来晃去): The salesman dangled the keys to the new car before the customer. 推销员在顾客面前晃动着新车的钥匙。
dangle something in front of someone (在某人面前悬挂): She dangled the toy in front of the baby.  她在婴儿面前晃动着玩具。
dangle a participle (悬垂分词): Dangling from the tree, the monkey ate a banana. (错误用法，猴子悬挂在树上，而不是香蕉)
leave something dangling (悬而未决): The author left the ending of the story dangling. 作者留下了一个悬念式的结局。


词源:  From Middle English *danglen, frequentative of dangen (“to strike, knock”), of uncertain origin.


注意事项: dangle 通常指轻微地悬挂或摆动。
