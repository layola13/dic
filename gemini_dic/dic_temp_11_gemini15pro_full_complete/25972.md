burry:/ˈbɛri/ | v. | 埋葬; 隐藏; 沉浸于 | To cover something or someone completely; to conceal something or someone; to involve oneself deeply in something |  She burried the treasure in the backyard. 她把宝藏埋在了后院。|  He burried himself in his work. 他沉浸在工作中。 |  bury, conceal, hide | uncover, reveal | verb

burry:/ˈbʌri/ | adj. | (指织物)粗糙的; 起毛的 |  Having a rough or fuzzy surface | The fabric had a burry texture. 这块布料的质感很粗糙。 | rough, fuzzy, nappy | smooth, sleek | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *byrgan, from Proto-Germanic *burjanan, meaning "to protect, conceal."  Related to German bergen "to hide, save," Dutch bergen "to save, store," and Gothic bairgan "to preserve, keep."  The sense of "to bury (a corpse)" developed later.
