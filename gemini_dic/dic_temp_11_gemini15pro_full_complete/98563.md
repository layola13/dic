institute: /ˈɪnstɪtjuːt/ (US) /ˈɪnstɪtjuːt/ (UK)| n. vt.|学院，研究所；机构；建立，制定|A place for advanced study or research; to establish or set up| The Massachusetts Institute of Technology is renowned for its scientific research. 麻省理工学院以其科学研究而闻名。| synonyms: academy, organization, establish, found | antonyms: abolish, terminate| noun

institute: /ˈɪnstɪtjuːt/ (US) /ˈɪnstɪtjuːt/ (UK)| vt.|建立，制定|To establish or set up something, such as a rule or system| The government instituted new policies to combat inflation. 政府制定了新的政策来对抗通货膨胀。| synonyms: establish, enact, implement | antonyms: abolish, repeal| verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French institut or Latin institutum ‘regulation, practice’, neuter past participle of instituere ‘set up, establish’, from in- ‘in, into’ + statuere ‘set up’.
