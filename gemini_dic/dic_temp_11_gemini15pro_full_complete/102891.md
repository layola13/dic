jaw:/dʒɔː/ | n. v. |下巴，颌；钳口；唠叨|The lower part of the face; either of the two bony structures that form the framework of the mouth.  To talk excessively, especially in a complaining or nagging way. |He clenched his jaw in anger. 他愤怒地咬紧牙关。|Synonyms: mandible, maxilla (anatomy),  chatter, gab (verb) |Antonyms:  (For verb) be quiet, listen | noun, verb
