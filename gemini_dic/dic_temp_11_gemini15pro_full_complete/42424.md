countercultural:/ˌkaʊntərˈkʌltʃərəl/ | adj. | 反文化的 | Relating to or representing a culture with values and beliefs that differ radically from or reject those of the established culture. |  The countercultural movement of the 1960s questioned traditional societal norms.  六十年代的反文化运动质疑了传统的社会规范。| synonyms:  alternative, nonconformist, unconventional, rebellious, radical | antonyms:  mainstream, conventional, traditional, orthodox, conformist | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"counter-" (prefix) + "culture" + "-al" (suffix)

"counter-" comes from the Old French "contre-" meaning "against" or "opposite".

"culture" comes from the Latin "cultura" meaning "cultivation" or "growing". It later evolved to encompass the arts, customs, and achievements of a particular group or nation.

"-al" is a suffix used to form adjectives meaning "relating to" or "characterized by".
