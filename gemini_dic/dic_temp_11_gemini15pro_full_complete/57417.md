drabber:/ˈdræbər/ | adj. | 更单调的，更乏味的 | Comparative form of drab: more drab.  Something that is drabber is duller or less interesting than something else. | The new paint made the room look drabber than before. 新油漆使房间看起来比以前更单调了。|  The weather today is even drabber than yesterday. 今天的天气比昨天更阴沉了。 | 近义词: duller, more monotonous, more tedious | 反义词: brighter, more vibrant, more interesting | 词性: 形容词

drab:/dræb/ | adj. | 单调的，乏味的；浅褐色的，土褐色的 |  Lacking brightness or interest; dull and monotonous.  Of a dull light brown colour. |  She wore a drab brown coat. 她穿着一件土褐色的单调外套。| The landscape was drab and uninteresting.  景色单调乏味。 | 近义词: dull, monotonous, dreary, lackluster, uninteresting | 反义词: bright, vibrant, colorful, interesting, exciting | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Middle English *draben, *drabben (“to be dirty, to make dirty”), probably ultimately of Scandinavian origin. Cognate with Norwegian dialectal drabba (“to drip, to drabble”), Swedish drabba (“to befall, to strike, to hit”). More at drabble.
