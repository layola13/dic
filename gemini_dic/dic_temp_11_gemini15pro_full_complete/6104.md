americas: /əˈmɛrɪkəz/
n.
美洲（尤指北美和南美）, 西半球
The Americas refers to the landmasses of North and South America, including Central America and the Caribbean.
Columbus sailed to the Americas in 1492.  哥伦布于1492年航行到美洲。
The Americas are rich in natural resources. 美洲自然资源丰富。
近义词: Western Hemisphere, New World
反义词:  Eastern Hemisphere, Old World
词性: 名词

Etymology:
From Amerigo Vespucci, an Italian explorer.  源自意大利探险家亚美利哥·韦斯普奇的名字。
Mapmakers began using variations of "America" to label the New World in the early 16th century, acknowledging Vespucci's role in demonstrating that the lands discovered by Columbus were not part of Asia.  16世纪初，制图师开始使用“America”的变体来标记新大陆，以承认韦斯普奇在证明哥伦布发现的土地并非亚洲一部分方面所起的作用。
