facecrime:/ˈfeɪskrɑɪm/| n. | 面部犯罪，表情犯罪 |  A crime committed by showing an improper facial expression, particularly in a dystopian society where emotions and expressions are strictly controlled.  This term originated in George Orwell's novel *Nineteen Eighty-Four*. |  In Oceania, any hint of unorthodox facial expressions, such as a flicker of anxiety or skepticism, could be interpreted as facecrime. 在大洋国，任何非正统的面部表情，比如一丝焦虑或怀疑，都可能被解释为面部犯罪。|Synonym: thoughtcrime | Antonym: orthodoxy, conformity | noun
