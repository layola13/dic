civilizatory:/ˌsɪvɪlɪˈzeɪtəri/ |adj.|文明的，与文明相关的|Relating to civilization, its development, or its influence.|例句：The civilizatory impact of the printing press was enormous. 印刷机对文明的影响是巨大的。

例句：They discussed the civilizatory implications of artificial intelligence. 他们讨论了人工智能对文明的影响。|近义词：cultural, societal|反义词：uncivilized, barbaric|词性: adjective

Etymology: From "civilize" + "-atory". "Civilize" comes from the French "civiliser," ultimately from the Latin "civilis" meaning "civil, relating to citizens," from "civis" meaning "citizen." The suffix "-atory" forms adjectives and nouns indicating relation or connection.
