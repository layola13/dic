outprice:/ˌaʊtˈpraɪs/
verb
定价过高；出价高于（别人）
to charge more than someone else for something, so that they are unable to compete with you; to offer a higher price than someone else for something
例句：Smaller companies are often outpriced by larger ones.  小公司常常因为大公司的定价过高而无法竞争。

例句：They were outpriced by a rival company.  他们被一家竞争对手公司出价更高而败下阵来。
近义词：overcharge, undersell
反义词：underprice
词性：v.
