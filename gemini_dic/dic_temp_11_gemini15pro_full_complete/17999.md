beggar:/ˈbeɡər/ (US), /ˈbeɡə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.|乞丐；使贫穷；难以描述|A person who lives by asking for money or food.|The beggar asked for spare change. 乞丐 meminta uang receh.|近义词：panhandler, mendicant, pauper|反义词:rich person, philanthropist|词性:noun, verb

beggar:/ˈbeɡər/ (US), /ˈbeɡə(r)/ (UK)| v.|使贫穷；难以描述；胜过|To make someone very poor; to exhaust the supply of; to defy description.|His extravagant lifestyle has almost beggared him. 他奢侈的生活方式几乎使他一贫如洗. |The beauty of the sunset beggared description. 日落的美丽难以言喻.|近义词：impoverish, deplete, exhaust|反义词:enrich, enhance|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *beggere*, from Old French *begard*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Middle Dutch *beggaert* ("mendicant friar").  Ultimately possibly from Proto-Germanic *bag* ("to fight, contend").
