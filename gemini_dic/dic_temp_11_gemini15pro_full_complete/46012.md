cyberneticist's:/ˌsaɪbərˈnɛtɪsɪsts/ | n. | 控制论学家的（所有格） |  Belonging to or associated with a cyberneticist.  A cyberneticist is a specialist in cybernetics, the science of communication and control systems in both machines and living beings. |  The cyberneticist's research focused on artificial intelligence. 控制论学家的研究集中在人工智能上。 |  The cyberneticist's laboratory was filled with complex machinery. 控制论学家的实验室里充满了复杂的机器。 | Near synonyms: cybernetician's | Antonyms: N/A (Possessive forms rarely have direct antonyms) | Possessive noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From cybernetics + -ist + -'s.  "Cybernetics" comes from the Greek κυβερνήτης (kybernētēs) meaning "steersman, governor, pilot,"  related to κυβερνᾶν (kybernan) "to steer, govern."  The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who studies or practices a particular subject.  The "-'s" indicates possessive form.
