disconsolateness:/dɪskənˈsoʊlətnəs/
|n.| 沮丧，忧郁，无法安慰的状态 |A state of being deeply unhappy and unable to be comforted. |Her disconsolateness after the loss of her dog was profound. 她失去爱犬后的沮丧心情非常深重。| synonyms: dejection, despondency, grief, melancholy, sadness | antonyms: cheerfulness, happiness, joy, elation | noun


disconsolate:/dɪsˈkɑːnsələt/
|adj.| 沮丧的，忧郁的，无法安慰的 |Deeply unhappy and unable to be comforted. |He wandered disconsolately through the streets after hearing the bad news. 听到坏消息后，他沮丧地在街上游荡。| synonyms: dejected, despondent, heartbroken, inconsolable, miserable | antonyms: cheerful, happy, joyful, elated | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *disconsolatus, from dis- "not" + consolatus "comforted," past participle of consolari "to comfort," from com- "together" + solari "to soothe."
