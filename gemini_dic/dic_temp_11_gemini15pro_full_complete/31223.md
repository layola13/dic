channel: /ˈtʃæn(ə)l/ (UK) /ˈtʃænəl/ (US) | n. vt.| 频道；渠道；途径；海峡 | A length of water wider than a strait, joining two larger areas of water, especially two seas.  A band of frequencies used in radio and television transmission, especially as used by a particular station. A way of communicating with or reaching someone or something.  To direct something into a particular course. |例句：
The English Channel separates England from France. 英吉利海峡把英格兰和法国分隔开来。

The program will be broadcast on Channel 4.  这个节目将在4频道播出。

We must find new channels for our products. 我们必须为我们的产品寻找新的销售渠道。

The government is channelling funds into education. 政府正将资金投入教育。
