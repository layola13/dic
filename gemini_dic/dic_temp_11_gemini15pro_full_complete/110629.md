limpin:/ˈlɪmpɪn/| adj.|跛行的，艰难进行的| limping is the present participle of the verb "limp," which means to walk with difficulty because of an injury to the leg or foot.  It can also be used figuratively to describe something that is progressing with difficulty.|The limping dog struggled to keep up with its owner.  这只跛脚的狗努力跟上它的主人。|The project is limping along due to lack of funding.  由于缺乏资金，该项目进展缓慢。| synonyms: halting, hobbled, struggling| antonyms:  running, sprinting, healthy| verb (present participle)


limpin:/ˈlɪmpɪn/| n.|跛行|In this less common usage, "limpin" acts as a noun, referring to the act of limping itself.|His limpin was noticeable after the fall. 他的跛行在摔倒后很明显。|The limpin of the old horse was a sad sight. 老马的跛行令人伤感。|synonyms: lameness, hobble| antonyms:  stride, gait| noun
