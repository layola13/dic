lubricate:/ˈluːbrɪkeɪt/ | v. | 润滑；使…顺利进行 | To apply a substance such as oil or grease to (something) in order to reduce friction and allow smooth movement. |  The mechanic lubricated the car's engine.  机械师给汽车引擎上了润滑油。| synonyms: oil, grease, smooth, facilitate | antonyms:  roughen, obstruct, hinder | verb

lubrication:/ˌluːbrɪˈkeɪʃn/ | n. | 润滑；润滑作用 | The action of applying a substance such as oil or grease to (something) in order to reduce friction and allow smooth movement. | Regular lubrication is essential for the proper functioning of machinery.  定期润滑对于机器的正常运转至关重要。| synonyms: oiling, greasing | antonyms: drying, friction | noun

lubricant:/ˈluːbrɪkənt/ | n. | 润滑剂 | A substance, such as oil or grease, used to reduce friction and allow smooth movement. |  This lubricant is specifically designed for high-temperature applications.  这种润滑剂是专门为高温应用设计的。| synonyms: oil, grease | antonyms: abrasive | noun
