offbeat:/ˌɒfˈbiːt/ /ˌɔːfˈbiːt/| adj.|不寻常的，另类的，反传统的|Unconventional, unusual, and often quirky or eccentric.  Characterized by a rhythm or accent that does not match the regular beat of the music.|例句：She has an offbeat sense of humor. 她有一种不寻常的幽默感。|例句：The movie had an offbeat plot that kept me guessing. 这部电影的情节不落俗套，让我一直猜测。|近义词：unconventional, quirky, eccentric, unusual, idiosyncratic|反义词：conventional, ordinary, mainstream, typical|词性:形容词
offbeat:/ˈɒfbiːt/ /ˈɔːfbiːt/| n.|弱拍，反拍|An unaccented beat, especially in music.|例句：The drummer emphasized the offbeats, creating a syncopated rhythm.  鼓手强调了弱拍，创造了一种切分音的节奏。|例句：The comedian's offbeat delivery made his jokes even funnier.  喜剧演员反传统的表达方式使他的笑话更加有趣。|近义词：upbeat (in some contexts)|反义词：downbeat (in some contexts)|词性:名词

词源:
From "off" + "beat."  Originally referring to a musical beat that is not emphasized, the word broadened its meaning to encompass anything that is unconventional or out of the ordinary.
