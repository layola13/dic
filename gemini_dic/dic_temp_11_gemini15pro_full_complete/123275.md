monoecia: /məˈniːʃə/ | n. | 雌雄同株 | A condition in plants in which the male and female reproductive organs are in separate flowers on the same plant. | The oak tree exhibits monoecia, with separate male and female flowers on the same branches. 橡树表现出雌雄同株，同一枝条上有独立的雄花和雌花。|  monoecism, dicliny | dioecy | noun

Etymology: From Ancient Greek *μόνος (mónos, “single, alone”) + *οἰκία (oikía, “house”).
