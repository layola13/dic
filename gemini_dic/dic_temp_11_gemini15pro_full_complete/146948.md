overinventoried:/ˌoʊvərˈɪnvəntɔːrid/ | adj. | 库存过剩的 | Having more inventory than is necessary or desirable.  The company was overinventoried and had to offer deep discounts to clear out the excess stock. | The company was overinventoried after the holiday season and had to offer deep discounts to move the merchandise. | overstocked, surplus | underinventoried | adjective
overinventoried:/ˌoʊvərˈɪnvəntɔːrid/ | v. |  使库存过剩 | To accumulate excessive inventory. The retailer overinventoried in anticipation of high demand, but sales were disappointing. | They overinventoried on winter coats and now they're stuck with a warehouse full of them. | overstock,  oversupply | understock | verb
