hesitate:/ˈhezɪteɪt/ (US), /ˈhezɪteɪt/ (UK)| v. |犹豫，迟疑|To pause before doing something, often because of uncertainty or fear.|She hesitated before answering the question. 她犹豫了一下才回答问题。| He hesitated to ask for help. 他犹豫要不要寻求帮助。|近义词：pause, waver, falter|反义词：decide, resolve, proceed|词性:动词

hesitation:/ˌhezɪˈteɪʃn/ (US), /ˌhezɪˈteɪʃn/ (UK)| n. |犹豫，迟疑|The act of hesitating; a pause in speech or action.|After a moment's hesitation, she agreed to go.  片刻犹豫之后，她同意去了。| He spoke without hesitation. 他毫不犹豫地说了。|近义词：pause, indecision, doubt|反义词：determination, decisiveness|词性:名词

hesitating:/ˈhezɪteɪtɪŋ/ (US), /ˈhezɪteɪtɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |犹豫的，迟疑的|Showing or feeling hesitation.|She gave him a hesitating smile. 她迟疑地对他笑了笑。| His hesitating manner betrayed his nervousness. 他犹豫不决的态度暴露了他的紧张。|近义词：uncertain, tentative, hesitant|反义词：decisive, confident, resolute|词性:形容词


词源: From Latin *haesitatus, past participle of *haesitare "to stick fast, remain fixed," frequentative of haerere "to stick, cling, adhere."
