hardfavoredness:/ˌhɑːrdˈfeɪvərdnəs/
|n.|丑陋，难看|The state or quality of being hard-favored; unattractiveness.|His hardfavoredness made it difficult for him to find a partner.|近义词: unattractiveness, ugliness, homeliness|反义词: attractiveness, beauty, handsomeness|词性: noun

Etymology:
From "hard-favored" + "-ness".  "Hard-favored" means having a coarse or unattractive face.  "Hard" in this context refers to something harsh or unpleasant, and "favored" relates to appearance.  The suffix "-ness" turns the adjective into a noun denoting a state or quality.
