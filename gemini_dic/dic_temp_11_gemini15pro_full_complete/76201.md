fustier:/ˈfʌstiər/ | adj. |  霉臭的；陈腐的；古板的 | Smelling stale, damp, and stuffy; musty. Also, old-fashioned or outdated in ideas and attitudes. | The attic was dark and fustier than I remembered. 阁楼比我记忆中更加阴暗和霉臭。 | synonyms: musty, stale, fusty, stuffy, moldy, antiquated, outdated, old-fashioned | antonyms: fresh, airy, modern, contemporary | adjective


Etymology:
From Middle English *fusti, from Old French fust "wood, tree trunk," from Late Latin fustis "cudgel, club," of uncertain origin.  The original meaning referred to the smell of decaying wood.  The figurative sense of "stale, outdated" developed later.
