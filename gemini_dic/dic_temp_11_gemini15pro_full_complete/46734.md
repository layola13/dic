dandily:/ˈdændɪli/| adv.|衣着华丽地，花哨地；漂亮地|In a dandy, or showy, manner.  Characterized by foppishness in dress and manner.|He dressed dandily in a brightly colored suit. 他穿着一套颜色鲜艳的西装，打扮得很花哨。|近义词：smartly, stylishly, nattily, jauntily|反义词：slovenly, shabbily|词性:副词

Etymology:
From dandy + -ly.  "Dandy" originates from the late 18th century, of uncertain origin, possibly a pet-form of Andrew or a shortening of the given name "Andrew". It initially referred to a man unduly devoted to style, neatness, and fashion in dress and appearance.
