antipuritan:/ˌæntiˈpjʊərɪtən/
| adj. n.| 反清教徒的，反清教徒 | Opposed to or hostile towards Puritanism or its adherents.  |  He held antipuritan views, believing their strict religious code was stifling. 他持有反清教徒的观点，认为他们严格的宗教信条令人窒息。 |  Example: The antipuritan sentiment was growing in the country. 反清教徒的情绪在该国日益增长。 | Near synonyms:  libertine, freethinker, nonconformist (in a different sense) | Antonyms: puritan, puritanical | adjective, noun


antipuritan:/ˌæntiˈpjʊərɪtən/
| n. | 反清教徒 | A person who opposes Puritanism. | Many antipuritans fled to the colonies seeking religious freedom. 许多反清教徒逃到殖民地寻求宗教自由。 | Example: He was considered an antipuritan due to his love of theatre.  由于他对戏剧的热爱，他被认为是一个反清教徒。| Near synonyms:  libertine, freethinker, nonconformist (in a different sense) | Antonyms: puritan, puritanical | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + Puritan. "Anti-" is a prefix of Greek origin meaning "against" or "opposite." "Puritan" refers to a member of a Protestant group in 16th- and 17th-century England and America seeking religious reform.
