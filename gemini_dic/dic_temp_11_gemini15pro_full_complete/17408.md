beamed:/biːmd/ | v. | 微笑；发射；播送 | To smile broadly and radiantly. To transmit (a signal, program, etc.) by means of a beam, as of radio waves, light, or particles. |  She beamed at him with pride. 她自豪地向他微笑。| synonyms: smiled, grinned, radiated, transmitted | antonyms: frowned, scowled | verb

beamed:/biːmd/ | adj. |  (of a structure) supported by beams. | The beamed ceiling gave the room a rustic feel.  横梁天花板给房间一种乡村的感觉。 | synonyms: supported, braced | antonyms: unsupported | adjective

Etymology:
From beam + -ed.  Ultimately from Old English *bēam, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch boom ‘tree, beam’ and German Baum ‘tree’.
