on-the-dole:/ɒn ðə dəʊl/ | adj. | 领取救济金的；失业的 | Receiving government benefits because of unemployment; unemployed. | He's been on the dole for six months. 他已经失业六个月，一直在领取救济金。|  He was on the dole after his factory closed down. 工厂倒闭后，他靠救济金生活。| Synonyms: unemployed, jobless, out of work | Antonyms: employed, working | adjective

Etymology:  "Dole" originally referred to a portion or share of something distributed.  It comes from Old English *dāl (meaning "portion"), from Proto-Germanic *dailiz (meaning "part, share"). The phrase "on the dole" came to be associated with unemployment benefits in the early 20th century.
