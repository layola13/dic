fail-safe:/ˈfeɪl seɪf/| adj. n.|故障安全的，保险装置|Designed to return to a safe condition in the event of a failure.  Designed or organized so that if one part stops working, the whole system does not become dangerous.|The nuclear reactor has a fail-safe mechanism to prevent meltdowns.|近义词：reliable, dependable, secure|反义词：risky, dangerous|adjective

fail-safe:/ˈfeɪl seɪf/| n.|保险装置，安全措施|A system or plan designed to prevent failure or to minimize its effects.|The engineers built in several fail-safes to ensure the bridge would withstand an earthquake.|近义词：backup, precaution, safeguard|反义词：hazard, risk|noun
