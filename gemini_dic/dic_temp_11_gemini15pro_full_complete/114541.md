malapropistic: /ˌmæləˈprɒpɪstɪk/ | adj. |  使用文字错误的；滑稽误用的 | characterized by the ludicrous misuse of words, especially by the confusion of words that are similar in sound. |  Her malapropistic use of "suppository" instead of "repository" was hilarious. 她把“repository”（仓库）误用成“suppository”（栓剂），真是滑稽。 |  inappropriate, incorrect, inaccurate, unsuitable |  appropriate, correct, accurate, suitable | adjective

malapropism: /ˈmæləˌprɒpɪzəm/ | n. | 滑稽的词语误用 | the ludicrous misuse of a word, especially by confusion with one of similar sound. |  "Dance a flamingo " (instead of flamenco) is a classic malapropism. “跳弗拉明戈舞” (而不是弗拉明戈舞)是一个经典的词语误用。 |  misusage, solecism, catachresis | correct usage | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mal à propos* "inappropriate," literally "badly to the purpose," from *mal* "badly" + *à propos* "to the purpose." Popularized by the character Mrs. Malaprop in Richard Sheridan's 1775 play *The Rivals*.
