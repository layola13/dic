ethnographies:/ɛθˈnɑːɡrəfiz/
n.
民族志学；人种志
Detailed descriptive studies of human societies. These studies are usually based on fieldwork and participant observation.
She's reading several ethnographies of Amazonian tribes for her dissertation. 她正在为她的论文阅读几部关于亚马逊部落的民族志。
The museum's collection includes fascinating ethnographies from around the world.  博物馆的收藏包括来自世界各地的引人入胜的民族志。
近义词:studies, accounts, reports
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "fictional accounts")
词性:noun (plural)

ethnography:/ɛθˈnɑːɡrəfi/
n.
民族志；人种志
The systematic study of people and cultures. It is designed to explore cultural phenomena where the researcher observes society from the point of view of the subject of the study.
Her research involves conducting ethnography in remote villages. 她的研究涉及在偏远村庄进行民族志研究。
The ethnography of the community revealed complex social structures. 对该社区的民族志研究揭示了复杂的社会结构。
近义词:anthropology, cultural study
反义词: (No direct antonym)
词性:noun (singular)
