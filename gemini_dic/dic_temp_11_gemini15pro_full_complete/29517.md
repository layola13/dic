catastrophize:/kəˈtæstrəfaɪz/
verb
使灾难化；小题大做；夸大
To imagine or exaggerate the worst possible outcome of a situation.
She tends to catastrophize every little setback. 她倾向于把每一个小挫折都夸大成灾难。
He catastrophized the situation, imagining he would lose his job and end up homeless. 他把情况灾难化了，想象他会失去工作，最终无家可归。
近义词: exaggerate, overreact, dramatize, overstate
反义词: downplay, minimize, understate
词性: verb

catastrophizing:/kəˈtæstrəfaɪzɪŋ/
noun
灾难化思维；小题大做
The act of imagining or exaggerating the worst possible outcome of a situation.
Her constant catastrophizing was making her anxious and stressed. 她不断地灾难化思维让她焦虑和压力很大。
Catastrophizing is a common cognitive distortion. 灾难化思维是一种常见的认知偏差。
近义词: exaggeration, overreaction, dramatization
反义词: minimization, understatement
词性: noun

词源: From catastrophe + -ize.  Catastrophe comes from the Greek katastrophē, meaning "overturn," from kata- "down" + strophē "a turning."
