dismarble:/dɪsˈmɑːrbl/
v.
剥夺大理石般的光泽或性质; 使失去光彩; 使黯然失色
To deprive of the qualities of marble, such as smoothness, coolness, or hardness; to make less beautiful or impressive.
The harsh wind and rain had dismarbled the once-gleaming statue.  风雨侵蚀使曾经闪亮的雕像失去了大理石般的光泽。
The years of neglect had dismarbled the grand old house.  多年的疏于照料使这座古老的大宅失去了昔日的光彩。
Synonyms: dull, tarnish, dim, fade
Antonyms: polish, brighten, enhance
verb


ETYMOLOGY->
dis- (prefix) + marble (n.)
"dis-"  表示否定或相反的意思
"marble"  大理石
