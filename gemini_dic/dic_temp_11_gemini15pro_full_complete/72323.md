flouting:/ˈflaʊtɪŋ/| n. |蔑视，藐视，無視|openly disregarding (a rule, law or convention).|例句：Their flouting of the rules resulted in severe penalties. 他们对规则的蔑视导致了严厉的惩罚。

例句：He was flouting the speed limit by driving so fast. 他以如此快的速度驾驶，藐视了速度限制。|近义词：disregarding, ignoring, defying, scorning, contravening |反义词: respecting, obeying, honoring, upholding |词性:noun (gerund or present participle of the verb "flout")

flout:/ˈflaʊt/| v. |蔑视，藐视，無視|openly disregard (a rule, law or convention).|例句：She flouted convention by wearing jeans to the formal event. 她穿着牛仔裤出席正式活动，蔑视了惯例。

例句：The company flouted environmental regulations and was heavily fined. 这家公司无视环境法规，被处以巨额罚款。|近义词：disregard, ignore, defy, scorn, contravene |反义词: respect, obey, honor, uphold |词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *flouten, *flowten, from Old Norse *flauta (“to play the flute, whistle, hiss; jeer at”), from Proto-Germanic *flautōną (“to play the flute”), from Proto-Indo-European *plew- (“to flow, float, swim”). Cognate with Norwegian flaute (“to play the flute”), Swedish flöjta (“to play the flute”), Icelandic flauta (“to play the flute, whistle”).
