cantharis:/ˈkænθərɪs/| n. |斑蝥，芫菁|A blister beetle, especially of the genus *Cantharis*, dried and used medicinally as a counterirritant and vesicant.  It secretes cantharidin, a toxic crystalline compound used as an aphrodisiac and to treat certain urogenital conditions.|Spanish fly was once believed to be derived from cantharis beetles.|Synonym: blister beetle, Spanish fly|Antonym: None (no direct opposite for a specific beetle)|noun
