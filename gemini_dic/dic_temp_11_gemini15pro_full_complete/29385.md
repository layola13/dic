casuistical: /ˌkæʒuˈɪstɪkəl/
adjective
诡辩的，伦理学上的
Relating to or involving casuistry; using subtle, often specious reasoning, especially about moral issues.
例句：
The lawyer's casuistical arguments convinced the jury, despite the weakness of the evidence.
律师诡辩的论点说服了陪审团，尽管证据不足。

He presented a casuistical justification for his actions, claiming they were for the greater good.
他为自己的行为提出了诡辩的理由，声称是为了更大的利益。

近义词：sophistic, specious, Jesuitical
反义词：straightforward, honest, principled
词性: adjective


casuistry: /ˈkæʒuɪstri/
noun
诡辩术，决疑法
The use of clever but unsound reasoning, especially in relation to moral questions; specious argument.
例句：
The politician's casuistry allowed him to avoid answering the question directly.
这位政治家的诡辩让他避免了直接回答问题。

He was accused of resorting to casuistry to defend his unethical behavior.
他被指责 resorting to 诡辩来为他的不道德行为辩护。

近义词：sophistry, quibbling, equivocation
反义词：honesty, directness, forthrightness
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *casuisticus*, from Latin casus (“case, event, chance, accident”). See case.
