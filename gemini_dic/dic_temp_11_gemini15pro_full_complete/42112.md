cottar:/ˈkɒtər (UK), ˈkɑːtər (US)/| n. |佃农，小屋农|A cottager, especially one who occupies a cottage with a small piece of land attached to it.  A peasant farmer.|The cottar and his family worked the small plot of land.|近义词：cottager, peasant, smallholder, crofter |反义词:landowner, landlord |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *coter, from Old English *cottere (“cottager”), equivalent to cot +‎ -er. Cognate with Scots cotter (“cottager”), Dutch kotter (“small boat; barge”). More at cot.
