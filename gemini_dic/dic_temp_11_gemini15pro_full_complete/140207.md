offices: /ˈɒfɪsɪz/ (British) /ˈɔːfɪsɪz/ (American)
| n. |办公室；办事处；政府机关；服务；仪式|A room, set of rooms, or building used as a place of business for non-manual work.|She works in the company's London offices.|Synonyms: bureaus, workplaces, headquarters|Antonyms: residences, homes|noun

offices: /ˈɒfɪsɪz/ (British) /ˈɔːfɪsɪz/ (American)
| n. |(尤指宗教)仪式；典礼|(especially in religion) a service or ceremony|the offices of the church|Synonyms: ceremonies, rituals, services|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "informalities" in some contexts)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French office "duty, function, service; divine service, rite; administrative or judicial position," from Latin officium "duty, service, function; ceremonial duty, rite; kindness, courtesy," from ops, opis "power, might, resources" + facere "to do, make" (from PIE root *dhe- "to set, put").
