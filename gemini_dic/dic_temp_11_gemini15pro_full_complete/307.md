aberration:/ˌæbəˈreɪʃən/| n.|异常，偏差，失常|a departure from what is normal, expected, or usual|例句：His outburst was an aberration from his usual calm demeanor. 他的爆发是他平时冷静举止的一次失常。

例句：The telescope's lens caused an aberration in the image. 望远镜的镜头导致图像出现偏差。|近义词：anomaly, deviation, irregularity, abnormality|反义词：normality, regularity, standard|词性:名词

aberration:/ˌæbəˈreɪʃən/| n.|光行差|the apparent displacement of a celestial object due to the finite speed of light and the motion of the observer|例句：Astronomers must account for aberration when calculating the position of stars. 天文学家在计算恒星位置时必须考虑光行差。

例句：The aberration of starlight can affect astronomical observations. 星光的光行差会影响天文观测。|近义词：displacement, shift|反义词: N/A|词性:名词
