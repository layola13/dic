half-flattering:/ˌhæf ˈflætərɪŋ/
adj.
半恭维的；略带奉承的
Partially complimentary, often with an underlying insincerity or backhandedness; giving a mixed impression of praise and criticism.
Her half-flattering comment about my dress made me wonder if she really liked it. 她对我衣服的半恭维的评论让我怀疑她是否真的喜欢它。
The review of the play was half-flattering, praising the acting but criticizing the script.  这部戏剧的评论半褒半贬，赞扬了表演，但批评了剧本。
Near Synonyms: backhanded, ambivalent, double-edged, faintly praising
Antonyms: genuinely complimentary, wholeheartedly praising, effusive
Word Origin:  A combination of "half" and "flattering". "Half" acts as an adverb modifying "flattering," indicating a partial or incomplete state of flattery. "Flattering" derives from the Old French "flater" meaning "to stroke, caress."
