bandog: /ˈbændɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈbændɔːɡ/ (US) | n. | 班多犬 | A type of large, powerful dog, historically bred as a guardian or catch dog. bandog: /ˈbændɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈbændɔːɡ/ (US)| n. | (古)拴链犬| A dog kept tied or chained, especially during the day. This historical meaning predates the modern usage referring to a specific breed type. |  In medieval times, a bandog was often kept chained during the day and released at night to guard the property. 在中世纪，拴链犬通常白天被拴起来，晚上放出来看守财产。| 近义词: chained dog, tethered dog | 反义词: free-roaming dog | 词性: noun
