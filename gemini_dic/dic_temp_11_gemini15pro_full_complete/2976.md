aegicrania:/ˌiːdʒɪˈkreɪniə/ | n. |偏头痛，羊癫疯|A type of headache localized to one side of the head, characterized by severe throbbing pain, often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound.  It stems from the Greek words "aix" (goat) and "kranion" (skull), perhaps alluding to the butting sensation of a goat or the intense, localized pain. |She suffered from debilitating aegicrania that made it difficult for her to function. 她患有使她难以正常活动的衰弱性偏头痛。|Synonym: migraine, cephalalgia |Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a specific type of headache) | Noun
