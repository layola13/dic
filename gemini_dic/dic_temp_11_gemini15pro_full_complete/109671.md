leucopathy:/ˌluːˈkɒpəθi/ /ˌljuːˈkɑːpəθi/
noun
白斑病；皮肤白化
A condition in which the skin loses its normal pigmentation, resulting in pale or white patches.
Leucopathy can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, autoimmune disorders, and certain medications.  皮肤白化症可能由多种因素引起，包括遗传、自身免疫性疾病和某些药物。
The patient presented with leucopathy on her hands and face. 病人的手和脸上出现了白斑。
synonyms: leukoderma, vitiligo, hypopigmentation
antonyms: hyperpigmentation, melanosis
