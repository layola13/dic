grumbling:/ˈɡrʌmblɪŋ/ (US) /ˈɡrʌmblɪŋ/ (UK)| n. v. adj.|抱怨，嘟囔，喃喃自语|The act of complaining in a low, indistinct voice; a persistent, low-pitched complaint.|He kept up a grumbling about the cold weather. 他不停地抱怨天气冷。|近义词：complaining, whining, muttering, grousing|反义词：praising, approving|词性: 主要为名词，也可作动词或形容词

grumble:/ˈɡrʌmbl/ (US) /ˈɡrʌmbl/ (UK)| v. n.|抱怨，嘟囔|To complain in a low, indistinct voice.|He grumbled about the long wait. 他抱怨等了很久。|近义词：complain, whine, mutter, grouse|反义词：praise, approve|词性: 动词，也可作名词


词源:  From Middle English *grumbelen, frequentative of grumen (“to murmur, grumble”), from Old French grommeler, from Frankish *grumōn (“to murmur”), from Proto-Germanic *grumōną (“to murmur, mutter”). Cognate with Dutch grommelen (“to grumble”), German grummeln (“to grumble”), Swedish grumla (“to be turbid”), Icelandic grumla (“to murmur”).
