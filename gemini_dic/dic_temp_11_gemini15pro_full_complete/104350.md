juxtaposing:/ˌdʒʌkstəˈpəʊzɪŋ/ | v. | 并列，并置 | placing two or more things side by side, especially for comparison or contrast | The artist juxtaposed images of war and peace in his exhibition.  这位艺术家在他的展览中并列展示了战争与和平的图像。 |  comparing, contrasting, setting side by side | separating, dividing | verb
juxtapose:/ˌdʒʌkstəˈpoʊz/ | vt. | 并列，并置 | to place close together for contrasting effect | The director juxtaposed scenes of violence with scenes of tranquility. 导演将暴力场景与宁静场景并置。 | compare, contrast, place side by side | separate, divide | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *juxtapositus*, past participle of *juxtaponere* ("to place side by side"), from *juxta* ("near, beside") + *ponere* ("to place").
