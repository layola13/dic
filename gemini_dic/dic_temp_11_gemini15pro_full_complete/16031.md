bandager:/ˈbændɪdʒər/| n.|绷带人，包扎员|A person who applies bandages; someone who bandages injuries.|The bandager quickly wrapped the wound.  绷带人迅速包扎了伤口。|Example 2:  A skilled bandager can make a real difference in emergency situations.  熟练的包扎员在紧急情况下能起到真正的作用。|近义词：dresser, medic|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "injurer" as someone who causes the need for a bandager)|noun

bandage:/ˈbændɪdʒ/| n. vt.|绷带，包扎|A strip of material used to bind a wound or injury; the act of applying a bandage.|She wrapped a bandage around her cut finger. 她用绷带包扎了她割伤的手指。|Example 2: He bandaged the wound carefully. 他仔细地包扎了伤口。|近义词：dressing, gauze|反义词: (No direct antonym)|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *bandage (Modern French bandage), from bande "strip, band," from Frankish *binda, from Proto-Germanic *bindaną "to bind," from PIE root *bhendh- "to bind, tie."
