in-grainedness:/ɪnˈɡreɪndnɪs/ | n. | 根深蒂固； ingrainedness | The ingrainedness of racism in society is a difficult problem to address.  社会中种族主义的根深蒂固是一个难以解决的问题。 | ingrainedness is the noun form of the adjective "ingrained", referring to the state or quality of being deeply fixed or established. |  His ingrainedness in his habits made it hard for him to adapt to change.  他根深蒂固的习惯使他难以适应变化。 |  deep-rootedness, fixity,  entrenchment | superficiality, transience | noun

in-grained:/ɪnˈɡreɪnd/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；深入的 |  The ingrained prejudices of the community were hard to overcome. 社区的根深蒂固的偏见很难克服。 | Ingrained describes something that is deeply embedded or firmly fixed, often referring to habits, beliefs, or attitudes.  |  His ingrained skepticism made him question everything. 他根深蒂固的怀疑态度使他对一切都提出质疑。 | deep-rooted, fixed, entrenched, inherent | superficial, temporary, acquired | adjective
