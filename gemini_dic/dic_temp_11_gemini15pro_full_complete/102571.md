jaghir:/ˈdʒɑːɡɪər/ | n. | 土地保有权，采邑 | An estate granted especially by a South Asian ruler, the revenues of which were used to maintain the grantee's troops or for other services.  A system of land tenure prevalent especially in Mughal India, whereby the collection of the land revenue of an estate and the power of government within it were assigned to an individual, usually in return for military service. | The jaghir was granted to the loyal nobleman.  这片采邑被授予了这位忠诚的贵族。 |  fief, feud, benefice |  N/A | noun
