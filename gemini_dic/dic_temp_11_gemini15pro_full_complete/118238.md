mercenaries: /ˈmɜːrsənɛriz/ | n. | 雇佣兵 | Soldiers who are paid to fight for a foreign country or group, typically motivated by financial gain rather than ideology or patriotism. | The warlord hired a band of mercenaries to protect his territory. 军阀雇佣了一群雇佣兵来保护他的领地。 |  The king relied on mercenaries to bolster his army. 国王依靠雇佣兵来增强他的军队。 | synonyms: soldiers of fortune, hired guns, condottieri | antonyms: patriots, volunteers, conscripts | noun
