gull:/ɡʌl/| n.|海鸥；易受骗的人|A large white and grey seabird with long wings. A person who is easily tricked or cheated.|例句：The gulls wheeled and cried overhead. 海鸥在头顶盘旋鸣叫。

例句：He's such a gull, he believed everything she said. 他太容易上当了，他相信她说的每一句话。|近义词：dupe, fool, chump|反义词: cynic, skeptic|词性:noun


gull:/ɡʌl/| v.|欺骗，愚弄|To trick or deceive someone.|例句：They gulled him out of his life savings. 他们骗走了他一生的积蓄。

例句：Don't be gulled by their promises. 不要被他们的承诺所欺骗。|近义词：deceive, trick, cheat, swindle|反义词: enlighten, inform|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gulle*, probably of imitative origin, referring to the bird's cry.  Related to Old Norse *gaukr* ("cuckoo").
