kickback:/ˈkɪkbæk/| n.|回扣，佣金；强烈反应；后座力|A sum of money or other payment illegally or unethically given in return for a favor or service.  A strong reaction or backlash.|例句：He received a hefty kickback for awarding the contract to that company. 他因为把合同判给那家公司而收受了巨额回扣。

例句：The new policy experienced a significant kickback from the public. 这项新政策遭到了公众的强烈反对。|近义词：bribe, payoff, commission, backlash, recoil|反义词:bonus (sometimes)|noun


kick back:/kɪk ˈbæk/| v.|轻松休息；(使)向后倾斜|To relax and enjoy oneself. To push or force something backwards.|例句：After a long week of work, I just want to kick back and watch some TV.  工作了漫长的一周后，我只想放松一下看看电视。

例句：The recoil kicked the gun back into his shoulder. 后坐力使枪向后撞击到他的肩膀上。|近义词：relax, unwind, recline,rebound|反义词:work, exert oneself|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "kick" + "back".  "Kick" meaning a strike with the foot, and "back" indicating a return motion or direction.  The noun sense developed from the idea of a portion of money being "kicked back" to someone as a bribe or commission.
