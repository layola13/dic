anti-pragmatical:/ˌæntipræɡˈmætɪkl/
adjective
反实用主义的；反务实的；不切实际的
Opposed to or averse to pragmatism; impractical or idealistic.
He has an anti-pragmatical approach to politics, focusing on ideals rather than practical solutions. 他对政治采取反实用主义的态度，注重理想而不是实际的解决方案。
His anti-pragmatical stance made it difficult for him to compromise. 他不切实际的立场使他难以妥协。
Synonyms: impractical, idealistic, unrealistic, quixotic, utopian
Antonyms: pragmatic, practical, realistic, down-to-earth
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to  From Greek anti- "against, opposite of, instead of, in return,requital".
pragmatical:  From Late Latin pragmaticus "skilled in business or law," from Greek pragmatikos "fit for business, active, businesslike," from pragma (genitive pragmatos) "a deed, act; that which has been done; a thing, matter, affair," especially an important one, from prassein/prattein "to do, act, perform."
