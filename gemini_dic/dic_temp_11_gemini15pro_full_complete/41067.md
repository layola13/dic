copped-out:/ˌkɑːpt ˈaʊt/ | adj. | 逃避的，放弃的，退缩的 |  To avoid or withdraw from something, especially a commitment or responsibility.  It describes someone who has taken the easy way out or avoided a difficult situation.  It carries a negative connotation, suggesting a lack of courage or integrity. | He copped-out of the difficult assignment. 他逃避了那项艰巨的任务。 |  avoided, shirked, backed out, ducked, weaseled out of | faced, confronted, tackled | adjective
