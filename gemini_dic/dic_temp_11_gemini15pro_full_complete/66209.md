excite:/ɪkˈsaɪt/ /ɛkˈsaɪt/ | vt.|使兴奋，激发，刺激| To cause feelings of enthusiasm and eagerness in (someone).|The news excited the children.  这消息使孩子们兴奋不已。

The band's performance excited the audience. 乐队的表演让观众兴奋不已。|近义词：stimulate, arouse, thrill, animate|反义词：calm, soothe, bore, depress|词性:动词

excitable:/ɪkˈsaɪtəbl/ /ɛkˈsaɪtəbl/ | adj.|易激动的，兴奋的|Easily excited.|He's a very excitable child. 他是一个很容易激动的孩子。

She has an excitable temperament. 她性格易激动。|近义词：nervous, agitated, restless|反义词：calm, composed, serene|词性:形容词

excitation:/ˌɛksɪˈteɪʃn/ | n.|兴奋，激动，刺激|The act of exciting or the state of being excited.|The excitation of the crowd was palpable.  人群的兴奋之情显而易见。

The discovery caused great excitation among scientists. 这一发现引起了科学家们的极大兴奋。|近义词：stimulation, arousal, thrill|反义词：calm, peace, tranquility|词性:名词

excited:/ɪkˈsaɪtɪd/ /ɛkˈsaɪtɪd/ | adj.|兴奋的，激动的|Feeling or showing enthusiasm and eagerness.|I'm excited about the trip. 我对这次旅行感到很兴奋。

The children were excited to open their presents. 孩子们兴奋地打开礼物。|近义词：thrilled, enthusiastic, elated|反义词：bored, apathetic, indifferent|词性:形容词

excitedly:/ɪkˈsaɪtɪdli/ /ɛkˈsaɪtɪdli/ | adv.|兴奋地，激动地|In an excited manner.|The children chattered excitedly. 孩子们兴奋地叽喳喳喳。

She waved excitedly to her friends. 她兴奋地向朋友们挥手。|近义词：eagerly, enthusiastically|反义词：calmly, quietly|词性:副词


词源: From Latin excitare "to rouse, stir up, call forth," frequentative of exciere "to call out, summon forth," from ex- "out" + ciere "to set in motion, rouse, call."
