ETYMOLOGY->
From "legislator" + "-ship".  "Legislator" comes from the Latin "legislator," agent noun from "legislat-" past participle stem of "legislare"  "to legislate," from "lex" (genitive "legis") "law"  + "-lat-"  frequentative suffix. "-ship" is an Old English suffix denoting state, condition, office, or skill.
