fainest:/ˈfeɪnɪst/| adj.|最好的，最精致的，最快乐的|Superlative form of *fain*.  Means pleasing, desirable, or excellent; also, happy, joyful, or glad. Archaic in most senses except "happy" or "glad," and even those are somewhat literary.|She wore her fainest gown to the ball. 她穿着她最漂亮的礼服去参加舞会。|近义词：best, finest, excellent, superb, splendid, magnificent|反义词：worst, poorest, terrible, awful|词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *faynest*, superlative of *fain*, from Old English *fægen* (“glad, joyful, fain”), from Proto-Germanic *fagainaz* (“joyful”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰǵʰei̯-*. Cognate with Old Frisian *fein*, *fagen* (“glad, joyful”), Dutch *fijn* (“fine, delicate, subtle”), German *fegen* (“to sweep, cleanse”).
