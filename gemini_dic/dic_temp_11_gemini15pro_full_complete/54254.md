disgraced:/dɪsˈɡreɪst/
adj. | 耻辱的，丢脸的 | Having lost honor or respect; in deep public shame. | The disgraced politician resigned from office.  这位名誉扫地的政治家辞去了职务。 | disgraced: shamed, dishonored, humiliated | honored, respected
词性: 形容词

disgrace:/dɪsˈɡreɪs/
n. | 耻辱，丢脸 | Loss of honor or respect; something that brings shame. | His actions brought disgrace upon his family. 他的行为给他的家庭带来了耻辱。 | disgrace: shame, dishonor, humiliation | honor, respect
词性: 名词

disgrace:/dɪsˈɡreɪs/
vt. | 使丢脸，使蒙羞 | To bring shame or dishonor upon. | He disgraced himself by cheating on the exam. 他因考试作弊而丢脸。 | disgrace: shame, dishonor, humiliate | honor, respect
词性: 动词


词源:
From Middle French *desgracier*, from des- "away from" + grace "grace, favor".  The prefix dis- adds a negative connotation.
