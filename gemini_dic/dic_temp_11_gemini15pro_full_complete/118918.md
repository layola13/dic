mete-out:/miːt ˈaʊt/| v. |给予（尤指惩罚）; 分配; |to give or order a particular punishment to someone|The judge meted out a harsh sentence. 法官判处了严厉的刑罚。|Synonyms: allot, allocate, dispense, administer, deal out, dole out|Antonyms: withhold, retain|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mǣtan* "to measure," from Proto-Germanic *mēt-. Related to German *messen* "to measure." The sense of "to distribute by measure" arose in Middle English.
