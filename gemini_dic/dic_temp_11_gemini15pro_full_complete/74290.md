fractals:/ˈfræktəlz/
n.
分形，分形体
A fractal is a curve or geometric figure, each part of which has the same statistical character as the whole. Fractals are useful in modelling structures (such as eroded coastlines or snowflakes) in which similar patterns recur at progressively smaller scales, and in describing partly random or chaotic phenomena such as crystal growth and galaxy formation.
分形是一条曲线或几何图形，其每一部分都具有与整体相同的统计特征。分形可用于模拟在逐渐缩小的尺度上重复出现相似图案的结构（如被侵蚀的海岸线或雪花），以及描述部分随机或混沌的现象，如晶体生长和星系形成。
例句：
The coastline of Norway is a good example of a natural fractal.
挪威的海岸线是天然分形的一个很好的例子。

Coastlines, mountains, and clouds are not simple geometrical shapes like triangles, squares, or spheres.  They are more complex than that – they are fractals.
海岸线、山脉和云不是简单的几何形状，如三角形、正方形或球体。它们比那更复杂——它们是分形。

近义词：
pattern, design, motif
反义词:
(No direct antonym, perhaps "smooth" or "regular" in certain contexts)

词性
