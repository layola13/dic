osteolite:/ˈɒstiəˌlaɪt/ | n. | 骨磷灰石;磷灰石|A phosphate of calcium, resembling apatite, that occurs as a fibrous or earthy concretion or as a pseudomorph after other minerals, especially organic remains. |The osteolite was found embedded in the ancient fossilized bone. 这块骨磷灰石被发现嵌在古代的骨骼化石中。| Example: The museum displayed a rare specimen of osteolite formed from a fossilized shell.  博物馆展出了一件由贝壳化石形成的罕见的骨磷灰石标本。 |Synonym: apatite, phosphorite|Antonym: (No direct antonym exists as it is a specific mineral) |noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From osteo- (bone) + -lite (stone, mineral)
