overreports:/ˌoʊvərɪˈpɔːrts/ | v. | 过度报告，夸大报告 | To report something in excess of the actual amount or frequency.  It implies exaggeration or misrepresentation, often for personal gain or to create a specific impression. | The company overreported its profits to attract investors.  该公司夸大其利润以吸引投资者。 | The witness overreported the speed of the car involved in the accident. 目击者夸大了事故车辆的速度。 | exaggerate, inflate, overstate | underreport, downplay | verb

overreport:/ˌoʊvərɪˈpɔːrt/ | n. |  过度报告，夸大报告| An instance of reporting something in excess of the actual amount or frequency. | The investigation revealed several overreports of expenses. 调查揭露了几起费用过度报告的案例。| The overreport of crime statistics caused unnecessary panic. 对犯罪统计数据的夸大报告造成了不必要的恐慌。| exaggeration, inflation, overstatement | understatement, downplay | noun
