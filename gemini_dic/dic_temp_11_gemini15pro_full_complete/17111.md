battalions:/bəˈtæljənz/
n.
营; 部队
A large body of troops ready for battle, especially an infantry unit forming part of a brigade typically commanded by a lieutenant colonel.
The general deployed three battalions to secure the bridgehead. 将军部署了三个营来确保桥头堡的安全。

battalions:/bəˈtæljənz/
n.
大群; (从事某活动的)一大批人
A large number of people or things regarded as forming a group.
Battalions of lawyers were employed by the corporation. 这家公司雇佣了大批律师。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French bataillon (modern French bataillon), from Italian battaglione "battalion," from battaglia "battle," from Vulgar Latin *battualia, from Late Latin battuere "to beat, fight," from Latin battuo, frequentative of batere "to beat."

SYNONYMS->
regiment, force, contingent, division, legion, army, troop, cohort

ANTONYMS->
individual, single, one
