limping:/ˈlɪmpɪŋ/ | adj. | 跛行的，一瘸一拐的 | walking with difficulty because of an injured leg or foot | The dog was limping after it hurt its paw. 这只狗伤了爪子后一瘸一拐地走着。 | synonyms: hobble, stagger | antonyms: running, striding | adjective

limping:/ˈlɪmpɪŋ/ | n. | 跛行 | the act of walking with difficulty because of an injured leg or foot |  His limping made it difficult for him to climb the stairs. 他的跛行使他爬楼梯很困难。| synonyms: hobble, stagger | antonyms: running, striding | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *limpen, from Old English *limpian, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Old Norse lempja (“to beat, slap”), suggesting a connection to the impact that might cause a limp.
