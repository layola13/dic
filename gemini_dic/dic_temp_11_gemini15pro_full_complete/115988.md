marshal:/ˈmɑːrʃəl/ (US) /ˈmɑːʃəl/ (UK)| v.|  整理；安排；引导；集结 |The general marshaled his troops for battle. 将军集结他的部队准备战斗。|synonyms: arrange, organize, assemble, direct, guide|antonyms: disorganize, scatter, disperse|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French mareschal, marescal "marshal" (Modern French maréchal), from Frankish *marhskalk "horse servant," from *marha- "horse" + *skalk "servant."
