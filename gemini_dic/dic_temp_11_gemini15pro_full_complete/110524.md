limbo:/ˈlɪmboʊ/ (US) /ˈlɪmbəʊ/ (UK)| n.|边缘，不确定状态，地狱边境|An uncertain period of awaiting a decision or resolution; a state of neglect or oblivion. Also, in some Christian beliefs, a place for unbaptized but innocent souls.|The project has been in limbo for months. (这个项目已经搁置好几个月了。)|Synonyms: uncertainty, purgatory, oblivion, abeyance|Antonyms: certainty, decision, resolution|noun

limbo:/ˈlɪmboʊ/ (US) /ˈlɪmbəʊ/ (UK)| n.|林波舞|A Caribbean dance in which the dancer bends backward and passes under a horizontal bar without touching it.|She won the limbo competition by passing under the bar set incredibly low. (她以令人难以置信的低杆高度赢得了林波舞比赛。)|Synonyms: dance|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin Limbus, meaning "border," "edge."  This relates to the theological concept of a "border" place between heaven and hell. The dance sense likely originated from the image of bending low to pass under the bar.
