ableisms: /ˈeɪbəlɪzəmz/
| n. |歧视行为; 歧视残疾人; |Discrimination or prejudice against individuals with disabilities based on the belief that typical abilities are superior. It often manifests as attitudes, actions, and institutional practices that limit the opportunities and full participation of people with disabilities.| Example: The new building lacked wheelchair access, a clear example of ableisms in architectural design. 新大楼缺少轮椅通道，这是建筑设计中歧视残疾人的一个明显例子。| Example:  Using terms like "crazy" or "insane" to describe eccentric behavior is a form of ableisms that perpetuates negative stereotypes about mental illness. 用“疯狂”或“精神错乱”等词语来形容古怪的行为是一种歧视行为，它延续了对精神疾病的负面刻板印象。 |Synonym: discrimination, prejudice, bigotry|Antonym: acceptance, inclusion, accessibility| noun

Etymology:
From "able" + "-ism," a suffix denoting a discriminatory or prejudicial attitude or belief system. "Able" itself comes from the Old French "hable," meaning "apt, skillful," ultimately from Latin "habilis," meaning "easy to handle, manageable, skillful."
