macaranga:/məˈkærəŋɡə/| n. | 血桐属;|A genus of plants in the family Euphorbiaceae (spurge family), comprising about 280 species of trees and shrubs native to tropical Africa, Asia, and Australia. They are characterized by their large, often peltate leaves and small, unisexual flowers.
