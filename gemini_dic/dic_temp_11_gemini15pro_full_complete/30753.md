cespitine:/ˈsɛspɪtiːn/ /ˈsɛspɪtaɪn/
n.
腐胺; 腐肉碱
A nitrogenous base, C₅H₁₄N₂, formed by the putrefaction of proteins.  A ptomaine found in decaying animal tissue.
Cespitine was isolated from cod liver oil.  从鳕鱼肝油中分离出了腐胺。
Synonyms: cadaverine, putrescine
Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a chemical compound.  One could consider "fresh", "unputrefied" as antonymic concepts, but not direct antonyms.)
