gymnophthalmata:/ˌdʒɪmnɒfˈθælmətə/
n.
裸眼蜥蜴科; 裸眼蜥蜴类
A family of spectacled tegu lizards, characterized by reduced or absent eyelids.  They are found in Central and South America.
Gymnophthalmata are a diverse group of lizards found in tropical America. 裸眼蜥蜴科是一个发现于美洲热带的多样化蜥蜴群体。
Gymnophthalmata includes several genera such as Bachia, Calyptommatus, and Gymnophthalmus. 裸眼蜥蜴科包括几个属，例如巴氏蜥蜴属、遮眼蜥蜴属和裸眼蜥蜴属。
Synonym:  Microteiids (sometimes used, but Gymnophthalmata is the preferred and more current term)
No direct antonym (since it's a taxonomic classification).  Could potentially contrast with other lizard families like Scincidae (skinks) or Teiidae (tegus) based on specific characteristics.
noun

From Ancient Greek γυμνός (gumnós, “naked, bare”) + ὀφθαλμός (ophthalmós, “eye”), referring to the reduced or absent eyelids in many species.
