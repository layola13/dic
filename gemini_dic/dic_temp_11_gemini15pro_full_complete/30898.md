chagrin:/ʃəˈɡrɪn/ /ʃəˈɡrin/| n. |懊恼，失望，沮丧|A feeling of annoyance, disappointment, or humiliation arising from failure, disappointment, or a disconcerting event.|例句：He felt deep chagrin at being rejected. 他被拒绝后感到非常懊恼。

例句：Much to her chagrin, she wasn't invited to the party. 令她懊恼的是，她没有被邀请参加聚会。|近义词：annoyance, disappointment, vexation, mortification|反义词：delight, pleasure, satisfaction|词性:noun

chagrin:/ʃəˈɡrɪn/ /ʃəˈɡrin/| vt. |使懊恼，使失望|To cause (someone) to feel chagrin.|例句：It chagrined him that he had to ask for help.  不得不求助让他感到很懊恼。

例句：She was chagrined by her failure to win the race. 她因未能赢得比赛而懊恼。|近义词：annoy, disappoint, vex, mortify|反义词：delight, please, satisfy|词性:verb

词源：From French chagrin, from Old North French chagrenier (“to grieve, sadden”), from chagre (“grief, sadness”), probably ultimately from Vulgar Latin *caricare (“to load”), from Latin carrus (“wagon”).  The original sense may have been “to make the face rough or wrinkled (as with grief)”.
