chancellors: /ˈtʃɑːnsələrz/ (US), /ˈtʃɑːnslərz/ (UK)| n. |大臣；总理；大学校长|A chancellor is a high-ranking official, often the head of a government or university.|The chancellor announced a new economic policy. 大臣宣布了一项新的经济政策。|The university chancellor conferred the degrees. 大学校长授予了学位。|synonyms: minister, premier, president, rector|antonyms: subordinate, deputy|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *chancelier, from Late Latin cancellarius "chief scribe," originally "usher who stood by the cancelli, lattice work separating a judge's seat from the rest of the court," from cancelli "lattice."
