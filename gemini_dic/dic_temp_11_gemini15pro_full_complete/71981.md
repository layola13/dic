flinch:/flɪntʃ/| vi. n.|畏缩，退缩； flinch (from something/doing something)|To make a sudden small movement because of pain or fear.| He didn't even flinch when the nurse cleaned the wound.  护士清洗伤口时，他甚至都没退缩一下。| flinch (at something)  She flinched at the sight of the blood. 看到血，她畏缩了一下。|近义词：wince, recoil, shrink, cower|反义词：brave, face, confront|词性:动词, 名词

flinch:/flɪntʃ/| n.|畏缩，退缩|A sudden small movement because of pain or fear.| There was no flinch, no tremor in his voice. 他的声音里没有一丝颤抖，没有一丝畏缩。|近义词：wince, recoil, shrinking|反义词：bravery, boldness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Old French *flenchir "to bend, turn aside," from Frankish *flankōn “to bend,” related to Old English *flanc "loin."
