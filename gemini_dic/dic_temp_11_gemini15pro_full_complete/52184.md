diarists:/ˈdaɪərɪsts/| n.|日记作者；日记体作家|People who write diaries regularly are called diarists.  经常写日记的人被称为日记作者。|The conference brought together diarists from all over the world.  这次会议汇集了来自世界各地的日记作者。|近义词：journalists, writers, chroniclers|反义词: (无直接反义词)|词性:noun
词源: From "diary" + "-ist". "Diary" comes from Old French "diarie", from Late Latin "diarium" meaning "daily allowance, daily journal,"  from Latin "dies" meaning "day."  "-ist" is a suffix indicating a person who practices or is concerned with something.  来自 "diary" + "-ist"。 "Diary" 源于古法语 "diarie"，来自晚期拉丁语 "diarium"，意思是“每日津贴，每日日志”，源自拉丁语 "dies"，意思是“日”。 "-ist" 是一个后缀，表示从事或关心某事的人。
