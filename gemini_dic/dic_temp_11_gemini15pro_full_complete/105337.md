kick-on:/ˌkɪk ˈɒn/ (UK) /ˌkɪk ˈɑːn/ (US)| phrasal verb|继续; 更进一步; 开始活跃起来|To continue or progress, often after a pause or interruption; to become more lively or exciting.|例句：After the pub closed, we decided to kick on to a club. 酒吧关门后，我们决定再去夜店玩。|近义词：continue, proceed, carry on, press on|反义词：stop, halt, cease|词性：动词短语


kick-on:/ˌkɪk ˈɒn/ (UK) /ˌkɪk ˈɑːn/ (US)| n.| (非正式)继续进行的活动；聚会| An informal gathering or continuation of an activity, especially after a main event.|例句：We're having a kick-on at my place after the concert.  音乐会结束后，我们要去我家继续聚会。|近义词：after-party, gathering|反义词：N/A|词性：名词

词源:  "Kick" comes from Middle English *kikken*, of uncertain origin. "On" is from Old English *on*. The phrasal verb meaning developed from the literal sense of kicking something to propel it further.
