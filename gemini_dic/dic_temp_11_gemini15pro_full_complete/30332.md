censures:/ˈsɛnʃərz/| n.|谴责，责难|Strong expressions of disapproval.  The politician faced censures from both sides of the aisle.|例句：The Senate passed a resolution of censure against the senator. 参议院通过了对这位参议员的谴责决议。

例句：His actions drew strong censures from the community. 他的行为受到了社区的强烈谴责。|近义词：rebukes, condemnations, criticisms, reproaches|反义词:praise, commendation, approval|noun

censure:/ˈsɛnʃər/| v.|谴责，责备|To express strong disapproval.|例句：The committee censured the mayor for his handling of the crisis. 委员会谴责市长对危机的处理方式。

例句：The teacher censured the student for cheating on the exam. 老师谴责学生考试作弊。|近义词：rebuke, condemn, criticize, reproach|反义词:praise, commend, approve|verb
