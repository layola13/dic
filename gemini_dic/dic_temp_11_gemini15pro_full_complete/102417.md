jack-in-the-box:/ˌdʒæk ɪn ðə ˈbɒks/ (US) /ˌdʒæk ɪn ðə ˈbɒks/ (UK)| n.|玩具盒里的玩偶；突然出现的人或物|A children's toy consisting of a box containing a figure on a spring that pops up when the lid is opened.  Also used figuratively to describe someone or something that appears suddenly or unexpectedly.|例句：The children were delighted with the jack-in-the-box. 孩子们对这个玩具盒里的玩偶感到很高兴。

例句：He sprang up from behind the sofa like a jack-in-the-box. 他像个玩具盒里的玩偶一样从沙发后面跳了起来。|近义词：surprise, pop-up|反义词:|词性:noun
