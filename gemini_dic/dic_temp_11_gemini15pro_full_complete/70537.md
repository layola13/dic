filibusterous:/ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstərəs/| adj.|冗长演说的，阻挠议事的|Characterized by or resembling a filibuster; obstructive, dilatory, especially in legislative proceedings.  Given to or characterized by long speeches or other delaying tactics, especially in a legislative assembly, to obstruct or delay progress. | The senator's filibusterous speech lasted for over 12 hours, effectively blocking the vote.  这位参议员冗长的演讲持续了12个多小时，有效地阻止了投票。

The filibusterous tactics employed by the opposition party prevented the bill from being passed. 反对党采取的阻挠策略阻止了该法案的通过。|近义词：obstructionist, delaying, procrastinating|反义词：cooperative, expediting, efficient|词性: adjective

词源：From filibuster + -ous.  "Filibuster" itself comes from the Dutch vrijbuiter, meaning "freebooter" or "pirate."  This term was later applied to adventurers who engaged in unauthorized military expeditions in Central America, and then further evolved to describe the political tactic of delaying legislative action.
