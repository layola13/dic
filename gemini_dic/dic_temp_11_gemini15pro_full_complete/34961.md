cloddily:/ˈklɑːdəli/ | adv. | 笨拙地，土里土气地 | In a cloddish or clumsy manner; resembling a clod. | He walked cloddily across the field, his boots sinking into the mud. 他笨拙地走过田野，靴子陷进了泥里。| Synonyms: clumsily, awkwardly, gracelessly | Antonyms: gracefully, elegantly, deftly | 词性: 副词

clod:/klɑːd/ | n. | 土块，笨蛋 | A lump of earth or clay.  A stupid person. | The farmer broke up the clods of earth with a hoe. 农民用锄头把土块打碎。| Synonyms: lump, chunk, dolt, oaf | Antonyms: genius, intellect | 词性: 名词

词源: From Middle English *clodde, from Old English clūd (“rock, hill, mass of earth or other material”), from Proto-Germanic *kludō (“lump, ball”), from Proto-Indo-European *glewd- (“to stick together, to form a ball”).
