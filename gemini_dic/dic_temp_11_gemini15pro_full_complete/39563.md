conscionably:/ˈkɒnʃənəbli/ /ˈkɑːnʃənəbli/| adv.|凭良心；合理地；道德上|In a manner informed by one's conscience; with integrity; reasonably. |He could not conscionably refuse to help. 他实在不忍心拒绝帮助。|近义词：ethically, morally, justly, reasonably, fairly|反义词：unconscionably, unjustly, unfairly|词性:副词

词源:
From Late Latin *conscionabilis (“knowing in one's conscience”), from Latin consciens (“conscious”), present participle of conscire (“to be conscious of, to know”), from con- (“together”) + scire (“to know”).
