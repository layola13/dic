endochrome:/ˈɛndəˌkroʊm/| n. |内色素，胞内色素|A coloring matter within a cell, especially of algae and other lower plants, in contrast to pigments located in the cell wall.|The endochrome of this algae species is a vibrant green.|近义词：pigment|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From  endo- (within) + -chrome (color).  Ultimately from Greek *éndon (within) and *chrôma (color).
