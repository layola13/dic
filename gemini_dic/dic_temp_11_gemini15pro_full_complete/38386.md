conceptualises:/kənˈsɛptʃuəlaɪzɪz/
|v.|概念化；将…概念化|form a concept or idea of something.|She conceptualises the problem as a conflict between two opposing forces. 她将这个问题概念化为两种对立力量之间的冲突。|Synonyms: conceive, envision, imagine, visualize|Antonyms: misunderstand, misconstrue|verb

conceptualise:/kənˈsɛptʃuəlaɪz/
|v.|概念化；将…概念化|form a concept or idea of something.|He struggled to conceptualise the sheer scale of the universe. 他难以理解宇宙的浩瀚规模。|Synonyms: conceive, envision, imagine, visualize|Antonyms: misunderstand, misconstrue|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From late 19th century: from conceptual + -ize.  The root word "concept" comes from the Latin "conceptus," past participle of "concipere" (to conceive, take in), from "com-" (together) + "capere" (to take).
