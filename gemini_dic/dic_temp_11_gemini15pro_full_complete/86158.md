heartquake:/ˈhɑːrt.kweɪk/| n. | 心震，内心震颤 | A sudden, intense emotional disturbance or upheaval, often related to love or loss.  A feeling of emotional shock, similar to a physical earthquake. |  She felt a heartquake when she heard the news. 她听到这个消息时感到内心一阵震颤。

My heartquake subsided when I realized he was safe. 当我意识到他很安全时，我的内心震颤平息了。| 近义词：pang, shock, jolt, tremor | 反义词：calm, peace, tranquility | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
"heartquake" is a combination of "heart" and "quake." "Heart" refers to the emotional center, while "quake" denotes a trembling or shaking.  The word is a metaphorical extension of the physical phenomenon of an earthquake to describe an emotional experience.  While its precise origin is difficult to pinpoint, it likely emerged in the late 19th or early 20th century.
