oratories:/ˈɔːrətɔːriz/ |n.|雄辩术；祈祷室；小礼拜堂|A place of prayer, especially one attached to a private house or a larger institution, but not a parish church; a small chapel. Also: the art or practice of formal speaking in public, especially as a religious or political activity.|The manor house contained several oratories for private worship. 这座庄园内有几个供私人祈祷的小礼拜堂。|Synonyms: chapels, shrines, sanctuaries|Antonyms: secular buildings, public spaces|noun


oratory:/ˈɔːrətɔːri/ |n.|演讲术；雄辩；雄辩的演说|The art or practice of formal speaking in public, especially as a religious or political activity; eloquence.|His oratory skills captivated the audience. 他的演讲技巧吸引了观众。|Synonyms: rhetoric, eloquence, public speaking|Antonyms: inarticulateness, silence|noun
