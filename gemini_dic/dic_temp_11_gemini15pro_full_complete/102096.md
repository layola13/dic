isospondylous:/ˌaɪsəˈspɒndɪləs/ | adj. | 等椎的 | Having vertebrae similar in structure and size, especially referring to the similar size of the centra (the main body of the vertebra).  This term is primarily used in comparative anatomy, particularly in the study of fish. |  Example: Isospondylous fish typically exhibit greater flexibility in their spine. 等椎鱼通常脊柱更灵活。

Example: The paleontologist identified the fossil as belonging to an isospondylous fish due to the uniform shape of its vertebrae.  古生物学家根据其椎骨的均匀形状，将该化石鉴定为属于一种等椎鱼类。 |  Synonym: homospondylous (somewhat interchangeable, though may imply even greater similarity) | Antonym: heterospondylous (having vertebrae that are dissimilar in size or structure) | adjective
