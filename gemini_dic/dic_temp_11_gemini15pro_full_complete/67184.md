express:/ɪkˈspres/ (US) /ɪkˈsprɛs/ (UK)| v. adj. n.|表达，表示；快速的，明确的；快递，快车|To convey (a thought or feeling) in words or by gestures and conduct.|She expressed her gratitude. 她表达了她的感激之情.|synonyms: convey, articulate, communicate, voice, utter, state, declare, pronounce, assert, affirm, aver, avow, manifest, demonstrate, show, indicate, signify, denote, reveal, disclose, divulge, intimate, imply, suggest|antonyms: repress, suppress, conceal, hide|verb

express:/ɪkˈspres/ (US) /ɪkˈsprɛs/ (UK)| adj.|快速的，明确的|Operating at high speed.|an express train  快车|synonyms: fast, rapid, quick, swift, speedy, expeditious, direct, nonstop, limited|antonyms: slow, local|adjective

express:/ɪkˈspres/ (US) /ɪkˈsprɛs/ (UK)| n.|快递，快车|A service that transports goods or mail rapidly.|I sent the package by express. 我用快递寄了包裹。|synonyms:  courier, special delivery, rapid transit|antonyms: slow mail|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *expressen*, from Old French *expresser* (modern French *exprimer*), from Latin *expressus*, past participle of *exprimō* (“press out, express”), from *ex* (“out of”) + *premō* (“press”).
