boycotts: /ˈbɔɪkɒts/ (UK) /ˈbɔɪkɑːts/ (US)| n. v.|抵制，联合抵制|An act of voluntarily abstaining from using, buying, or dealing with a person, organization, or country as an expression of protest, usually for political or social reasons.  A refusal to buy or use goods and services.|Many consumers boycotted the company's products due to their unethical labor practices. 许多消费者因该公司不道德的劳工行为而抵制其产品。|synonyms: embargo, ban, sanction| antonyms: support, patronize| noun, verb
