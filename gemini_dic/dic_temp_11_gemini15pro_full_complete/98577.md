institutionalize: /ˌɪnstɪˈtuːʃənəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使制度化；使机构化；使住院治疗 | To establish something as a convention or norm in an organization or culture; to place someone in an institution such as a hospital or prison. |  The government institutionalized new regulations for environmental protection. 政府将新的环境保护法规制度化。

The family decided to institutionalize their elderly mother as her dementia worsened.  由于母亲的痴呆症恶化，家人决定将她送进养老院。| 近义词：establish, formalize, hospitalize, commit | 反义词： abolish, informalize, release | 词性: verb


institutionalization: /ˌɪnstɪˌtuːʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 制度化；机构化；住院治疗 | The act of institutionalizing something or someone. | The institutionalization of this policy has led to significant changes in the workplace. 这项政策的制度化导致了工作场所的重大变化。

His long institutionalization made it difficult for him to adjust to life outside the hospital. 长期住院治疗使他难以适应医院外面的生活。| 近义词：establishment, formalization | 反义词： abolition | 词性: noun


词源:  From institution + -ize.  "Institution" comes from Late Latin "institutiōn-", from the verb "instituere" meaning "to set up, establish."
