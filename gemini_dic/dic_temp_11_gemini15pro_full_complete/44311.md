crooning:/ˈkruːnɪŋ/| n. v.|低吟浅唱，哼唱|A soft, sentimental style of singing, often associated with jazz and popular music.  The act of singing in this style.|例句：He was crooning a love song to his girlfriend. 他正对着他的女朋友低吟一首情歌。

例句：The crooning of the singer lulled the baby to sleep. 歌手的低吟浅唱哄宝宝入睡了。|近义词：humming, singing softly, warbling|反义词:shouting, bellowing|词性:noun, verb (present participle)

croon:/kruːn/| v. n.|低吟浅唱，哼唱|To sing in a soft, sentimental style.|例句：She crooned a lullaby to her child. 她低声哼唱一首摇篮曲给她的孩子听。

例句：He crooned his way to stardom. 他一路哼唱，直至成名。|近义词：hum, sing softly, warble|反义词:shout, bellow|词性:verb, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Scottish and northern English *crone* (“to hum, moan, lament”), of imitative origin.  Related to Dutch *kreunen* (“to groan, moan”).
