intertextual:/ˌɪntərˈtekstʃuəl/ | adj. |文本间的，互文的| Relating to or involving a relationship between texts, especially literary ones.  The relationship could be through allusion, quotation, parody, translation, or other means. | Example 1: The novel's intertextual references to classical mythology enrich its meaning. 这部小说对古典神话的互文引用丰富了它的意义。

Example 2:  The intertextual dialogue between the two poems creates a fascinating literary effect.  这两首诗之间的互文对话创造了一种引人入胜的文学效果。| Synonym: allusive, referencing | Antonym: original, independent | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From inter- + textual.  "Inter-" is a Latin prefix meaning "between" or "among." "Textual" relates to a text or texts.
