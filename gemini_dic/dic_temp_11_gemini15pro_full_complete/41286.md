cordillera:/ˌkɔːrdɪˈljɛrə/ /ˌkɔːrdɪˈljɛrə/| n. |山脉；科迪勒拉山系|A system or group of parallel mountain ranges.|The Andes cordillera stretches along the western coast of South America. 安第斯山脉沿着南美洲西海岸延伸。|The hikers traversed the rugged cordillera.  徒步旅行者穿越了崎岖的山脉。|Synonyms: mountain range, chain, sierra, massif|Antonyms: valley, plain|noun

cordillera:/ˌkɔːrdɪˈljɛrə/ /ˌkɔːrdɪˈljɛrə/| n. |科迪勒拉山系|Specifically refers to The American Cordillera, a chain of mountain ranges (cordilleras) that consists of an almost continuous sequence of mountain ranges that form the western "backbone" of North America, Central America, South America and Antarctica.|The American Cordillera is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. 美洲科迪勒拉山系拥有种类繁多的动植物。|The formation of the Cordillera was a complex geological process. 科迪勒拉山系的形成是一个复杂的地质过程。|Synonyms: The Americas, Western Hemisphere mountains|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could possibly use specific eastern lowlands of the continents)|noun
