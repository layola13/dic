khamsin:/kɑːmˈsiːn/ /ˈkæmˌsɪn/| n.|卡辛风; 五旬节;|A hot, dry, southerly wind that blows in Egypt and the surrounding areas, especially in spring.|The khamsin blew relentlessly for three days, coating everything in a fine layer of dust. 卡辛风无情地刮了三天，万物都被一层细细的灰尘覆盖。|The travellers sought shelter from the oppressive khamsin. 旅行者们寻找躲避酷热的卡辛风的地方。|近义词：simoon, sirocco, sandstorm|反义词:breeze, zephyr|词性:noun
Etymology: From Arabic خَمْسِين‎ (ḵamsīn, “fifty”), because it supposedly blows for fifty days.
