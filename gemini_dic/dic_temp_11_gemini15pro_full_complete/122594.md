modernisms:/ˈmɒdərnɪzəmz/ (US) /ˈmɑːdərnɪzəmz/| n. |现代主义（作品、风格、原则等）|Modernisms refers to the principles, styles, or practices of modernism, particularly in art, literature, and architecture, often considered as a whole.|The museum's exhibit showcased a variety of modernisms in 20th-century painting.|近义词：modernist movements, avant-garde styles|反义词：traditionalism, classicism|词性: noun


Etymology:
From modernism + -s (plural suffix)
Modernism from modern + -ism
Modern from Old French moderne, from Latin modernus "modern, recent," from modo "just now," ablative of modus "measure, manner."
