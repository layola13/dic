antinaturalism:/ˌæntiˈnætʃrəlɪzəm/
|n.|反自然主义|A philosophical viewpoint that rejects the idea that everything can be explained in terms of natural laws and physical processes. It often emphasizes the role of the supernatural, spiritual, or divine in the world.|The rise of antinaturalism in the late 19th century was a reaction against the dominance of scientific materialism.|近义词:supernaturalism, transcendentalism|反义词:naturalism, materialism|词性:名词

Etymology:
anti- (prefix meaning "against, opposed to") + naturalism (from Latin naturalis "natural") + -ism (suffix forming nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or practices)
