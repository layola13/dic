humors:/ˈhjuːmərz/ (US) /ˈhjuːməz/ (UK)| n.| 幽默，诙谐; 体液; 情绪，心情|  A person's temperament or disposition.  Also, archaic usage referring to the four bodily fluids (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile) believed to determine temperament and health.|  He has a good sense of humor. 他很有幽默感。  The doctor said the imbalance of humors was causing her illness. (archaic) 医生说她体液失衡导致了她的疾病。| synonyms: moods, temperaments, dispositions, whims, fancies (for moods); fluids (archaic) | antonyms: seriousness, solemnity (for humor); solids (archaic) | noun


humors:/ˈhjuːmərz/ (US) /ˈhjuːməz/ (UK)| v. | 迎合；迁就 | To comply with the wishes or whims of.|  She humored her grandfather by listening patiently to his long stories.  她耐心地听着祖父的长篇大论，以顺从他。 He humored the child by pretending to believe his fantastic tales. 他假装相信孩子荒诞的故事来迁就他。 | synonyms: indulge, cater to, pander to, gratify | antonyms: oppose, resist, deny | verb
