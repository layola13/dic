equimultiple:/ˌiːkwɪˈmʌltɪpl/ | adj. | 等倍数的，同倍数的 |  Having a common multiple. Two quantities are equimultiple if one is obtained by multiplying the other by an integer greater than zero. | The numbers 12 and 18 are equimultiple because they are both multiples of 6 (12 = 2*6 and 18 = 3*6). 12和18是等倍数，因为它们都是6的倍数（12=2*6，18=3*6）。|  multiple, commensurable | indivisible | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *aequimultiplex*, from *aequi-* (equal) + *multiplex* (manifold, having many folds).
