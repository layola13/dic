entrenchment:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt/ | n. | 壕沟；防御工事；根深蒂固；牢固地位 |  The act of digging trenches for defense, or the state of being firmly established or deeply ingrained. |  The soldiers dug entrenchments to protect themselves from enemy fire. 士兵们挖了壕沟来保护自己免受敌人的炮火。|  The entrenchment of corruption in the system made reform difficult.  系统中腐败的根深蒂固使得改革变得困难。 | synonyms: fortification, stronghold, establishment, rooting | antonyms: vulnerability, weakness, exposure, eradication | noun


entrenched:/ɪnˈtrɛntʃt/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；牢固的 | Firmly established and difficult to change. | His entrenched beliefs made it hard to reason with him. 他根深蒂固的信念使人很难与他讲道理。 | The company's entrenched position in the market made it difficult for new competitors to emerge.  该公司在市场上的稳固地位使新的竞争对手难以出现。 | synonyms: established, ingrained, fixed, rooted | antonyms:  weak, unstable, precarious, removable | adjective
