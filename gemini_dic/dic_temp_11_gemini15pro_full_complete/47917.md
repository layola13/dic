deception:/dɪˈsepʃn/| n.|欺骗，欺诈，诡计|The magician's performance was full of deception. 魔术师的表演充满了欺骗。|例句：He was accused of obtaining money by deception. 他被指控通过欺骗获取钱财。

deceptive:/dɪˈseptɪv/| adj.|欺骗性的，虚伪的|Appearances can be deceptive. 外表可能是骗人的。|例句：The calm surface of the water was deceptive; the current underneath was very strong. 水面上的平静是骗人的；下面的水流非常湍急。


例句：He had a deceptive smile that hid his true intentions. 他脸上挂着欺骗性的笑容，掩盖了他真正的意图。


近义词：misleading, deceitful, fraudulent|反义词:honest, truthful, genuine|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *decepcion*, from Latin *deceptionem* (nominative deceptio) "a deceiving, cheating, ensnaring," noun of action from past-participle stem of decipere "to ensnare, catch; beguile, cheat," from de- "away" + capere "to take."
