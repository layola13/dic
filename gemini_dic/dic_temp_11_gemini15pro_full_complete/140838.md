on-the-alert:/ˌɑːn ði əˈlɜːrt/ | adj. | 警惕的，警觉的 | Watchful and prepared for danger or something unexpected. | The soldiers were on-the-alert for any signs of enemy movement. 士兵们对敌人的任何动静都保持警惕。| The security guard remained on-the-alert throughout the night. 保安整夜都保持警惕。 | 近义词: vigilant, watchful, wary, observant | 反义词: inattentive, careless, oblivious | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  "On the alert" is a phrase that originated from military terminology. "Alert" itself comes from the Italian word "all'erta," meaning "on the watch" or "on the height," referring to a high place offering a good view for watching out for danger.  Over time, it evolved into the English word "alert," retaining its meaning of being watchful and ready. The preposition "on" emphasizes the state of being actively engaged in alertness.
