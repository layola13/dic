neocomian:/ˌniːəˈkəʊmiən/| adj. n.|新科曼阶的，新科曼阶|Of, relating to, or denoting the earliest stage of the Cretaceous period, between the Jurassic and Aptian periods (approximately 145 to 133 million years ago).  Also used as a noun referring to the rocks of this stage.|例句：The neocomian strata are rich in fossils.  新科曼阶地层富含化石。

例句：Neocomian fossils provide valuable insights into the early Cretaceous ecosystem. 新科曼阶化石为了解早白垩世生态系统提供了宝贵的见解。|近义词：Early Cretaceous, Berriasian, Valanginian, Hauterivian, Barremian (depending on specific usage)|反义词:N/A|词性:adjective, noun

Etymology: From Neocomum, the Latin name for Neuchâtel, Switzerland, where rocks of this age are well-exposed.
