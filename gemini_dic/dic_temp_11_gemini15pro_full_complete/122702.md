moghuls:/ˈmoʊɡʌlz/ | n. |  莫卧儿人，大亨 |  A member of the Muslim dynasty of Mongol origin which ruled most of India from the 16th to the 19th century.  Also used figuratively to refer to a powerful or influential person, especially in business or industry. | The Moghuls built magnificent monuments like the Taj Mahal. 莫卧儿人建造了像泰姬陵这样宏伟的纪念碑。

The movie moguls of Hollywood control vast fortunes. 好莱坞的电影大亨控制着巨额财富。 | 近义词：magnates, tycoons | 反义词: commoners, paupers | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Urdu and Persian *mughal*, ultimately from Mongolian *Mongol*.
