meltdowns:/ˈmɛltdaʊnz/
|n.|崩溃，熔毁|A meltdown is a situation where something fails or collapses suddenly and dramatically, often in a very public way. It can refer to a nuclear reactor accident, a financial crisis, or an emotional breakdown.|The stock market meltdown of 2008 wiped out billions of dollars in investments. 2008年的股市崩盘使数十亿美元的投资化为乌有。

The Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster involved a meltdown of three of the plant's nuclear reactors. 福岛第一核电站灾难涉及该核电站三个核反应堆的熔毁。|近义词：collapse, crisis, breakdown, debacle|反义词：recovery, breakthrough, improvement|词性:名词

meltdown:/ˈmɛltdaʊn/
|n.|（尤指儿童的）情绪失控|A meltdown is a sudden and complete loss of emotional control, especially by a child.|The child had a meltdown in the grocery store because his mother wouldn't buy him candy.  这个孩子在杂货店里情绪失控，因为他妈妈不给他买糖。

After a long and stressful day, she had a meltdown and started crying uncontrollably.  经过漫长而紧张的一天后，她情绪崩溃，开始无法控制地哭泣。|近义词：tantrum, outburst, breakdown|反义词：calm, composure, self-control|词性:名词
