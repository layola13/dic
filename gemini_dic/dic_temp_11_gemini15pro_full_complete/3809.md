agnostic:/æɡˈnɑːstɪk/ | n. adj. |不可知论者，不可知论的 |  A person who believes that nothing is known or can be known of the existence or nature of God or of anything beyond material phenomena; relating to agnosticism.  | Example: He is an agnostic and doesn't subscribe to any particular religion. 他是一个不可知论者，不信仰任何特定的宗教。| Example: Her agnostic views prevented her from fully embracing either faith or atheism. 她的不可知论观点使她无法完全接受信仰或无神论。 | Synonym: doubter, skeptic, unbeliever | Antonym: believer, devotee, faithful | noun

agnostic:/æɡˈnɑːstɪk/ | adj. |不可知论的 | Relating to the view that the existence of God, of the divine or the supernatural is unknown or unknowable. | Example:  He maintained an agnostic stance on the question of life after death. 他对死后生命的问题保持不可知论的立场。 | Example:  The philosopher presented an agnostic argument regarding the nature of reality. 这位哲学家提出了一个关于现实本质的不可知论的论点。 | Synonym: skeptical, questioning, doubtful | Antonym: believing, faithful, devout | adjective
