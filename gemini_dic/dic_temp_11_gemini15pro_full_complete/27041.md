calendar: /ˈkælɪndər/ (US) /ˈkælɪndə(r)/ (UK)| n.|日历；日程表|A system for dividing time into days, weeks, months, and years, often displayed on a chart or in a book, used for keeping track of appointments and events.|I marked the date on my calendar. 我在日历上标记了日期。|synonyms: schedule, timetable, almanac|antonyms: disorder, chaos|noun

calendar: /ˈkælɪndər/ (US) /ˈkælɪndə(r)/ (UK)| v.|安排；预定|To schedule an event or appointment.|The meeting is calendared for next Tuesday. 会议安排在下周二。|synonyms: schedule, book, arrange|antonyms: cancel, postpone|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *calendrier, from Latin calendarium "account book, register," from calendae "calends," the first day of the Roman month, on which debts fell due and accounts were settled.  The word calendae is related to calare "to proclaim, call out."
