lamellirostral:/ˌlæməˈlɪrɒstrəl/ | adj. | 薄片喙的；板状喙的 | Having a beak that is broad and flattened, often with lamellae (thin plates or ridges) along the edges. This type of beak is common in filter-feeding birds. | Example: The duck is a lamellirostral bird, using its bill to filter food from the water.  这只鸭子是板状喙鸟类，用喙从水中过滤食物。 | Example: Lamellirostral beaks are well-suited for sifting through mud and water for small invertebrates. 板状喙非常适合在泥土和水中筛选小型无脊椎动物。 | 近义词:  plate-billed | 反义词:  (No direct antonym -  Consider the opposite beak structure like hooked or pointed) | 词性: adjective

Etymology:
From Latin *lamella* ("thin plate") + *rostrum* ("beak, snout")
