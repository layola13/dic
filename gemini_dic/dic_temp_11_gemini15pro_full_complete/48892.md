defrauder:/dɪˈfrɔːdər/
n.|欺诈者，骗子|A person who defrauds someone of something.|The defrauder was eventually caught and sentenced to prison.  这个骗子最终被抓获并被判入狱。|Synonyms: cheat, swindler, con artist|Antonyms: honest person, philanthropist|noun

defraud:/dɪˈfrɔːd/
vt.|欺骗，诈骗|Illegally obtain money from (someone) by deception.|He defrauded the company of millions of dollars. 他诈骗了公司数百万美元。|Synonyms: cheat, swindle, con|Antonyms: compensate, repay|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English defrauden, from Old French defrauder, from Latin dēfraudāre (“to defraud, cheat, deceive”), from dē- (“from, down, away, off, out of”) + fraudāre (“to cheat, deceive”), from fraus (“fraud, deceit, treachery”).
