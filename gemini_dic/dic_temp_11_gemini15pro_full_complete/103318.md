jiggery-pokery:/ˈdʒɪɡəri ˈpəʊkəri/ | n. | 欺骗；诡计；花招 | Dishonest or deceitful behavior; trickery. |  He got the job through some jiggery-pokery with the selection committee. 他通过在遴选委员会耍花招得到了这份工作。

ETYMOLOGY->
"Jiggery-pokery" is of uncertain origin, but it likely arose in the 19th century, possibly from Scottish or Irish dialect.  "Jiggery" itself may be related to "jig," suggesting a quick, deceptive movement, while "pokery" might be connected to "poke," meaning to prod or meddle. The combination suggests a sense of clandestine manipulation.
