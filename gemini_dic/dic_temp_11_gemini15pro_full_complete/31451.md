charlatan:/ˈʃɑːrlətən/ | n. |江湖郎中；骗子；吹牛大王|A person falsely claiming to have a special knowledge or skill; a fraud.|例句：He was exposed as a charlatan when his "miracle cure" was found to be nothing more than sugar water.  他所谓的“神奇疗法”被发现不过是糖水后，他的骗子身份就暴露了。

例句：The charlatan promised he could cure any disease, but his methods were dubious at best.  这个江湖郎中承诺他能治好任何疾病，但他的方法充其量也是可疑的。|近义词：fraud, imposter, quack, sham, fake, swindler |反义词：expert, professional, genuine |词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *ciarlatano*, from *ciarlare* "to chatter," from Old Italian *cerretano* "inhabitant of Cerreto," a town in Tuscany known for its quacks.  The association arose because the quacks from Cerreto were known for their fast-talking sales pitches.
