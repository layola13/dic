diphthong:/ˈdɪfθɒŋ/ (UK) /ˈdɪfθɔŋ/ (US)| n.|双元音|A sound formed by the combination of two vowels in a single syllable, in which the sound begins as one vowel and moves toward another (as in coin, loud, and side).|The word "loud" has the diphthong /aʊ/.  单词“loud”包含双元音/aʊ/。|Example: The diphthong in "boy" is /ɔɪ/.  单词"boy"中的双元音是/ɔɪ/。|Near Synonyms: vowel glide, vowel combination|Antonyms: monophthong|noun
