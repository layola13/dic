cottiers:/ˈkɒtiəz/ (British), /ˈkɑːtiərz/ (American)| n. |佃农，小农场主|A cottier is a peasant farmer who occupies a small holding of land, often rented, and who typically lives in a cottage.  They are characterized by their low social and economic status, often working the land for a landlord or landowner in exchange for rent or a share of the crops.|The cottiers worked long hours in the fields but barely earned enough to survive.|Synonyms: peasant, smallholder, tenant farmer|Antonyms: landlord, landowner|Noun

cottier:/ˈkɒtiə(r)/ (British), /ˈkɑːtiər/ (American)| n. |佃农，小农场主|A cottier is a peasant farmer who occupies a small holding of land, often rented, and who typically lives in a cottage.
