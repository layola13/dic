abdicate: /ˈæbdɪkeɪt/ (US) /ˈæbdɪkeɪt/ (UK)| v. |放弃（权力、责任等）；退位|To formally give up a high office or position, especially that of king or queen. |The king abdicated the throne. 国王退位了。|  The CEO abdicated his responsibility and left the company in chaos. 首席执行官放弃了他的责任，使公司陷入混乱。 | synonyms: relinquish, renounce, resign, step down | antonyms: assume, usurp, seize, retain | verb

abdicated: /ˈæbdɪkeɪtɪd/ (US) /ˈæbdɪkeɪtɪd/ (UK) | v. (past tense and past participle of abdicate) |放弃了（权力、责任等）；退位了|Past tense and past participle form of abdicate, meaning to have formally given up a high office or position. | The emperor abdicated his throne in favor of his son.  皇帝为了他的儿子而放弃了王位。 |  She abdicated her role as team leader due to personal reasons. 由于个人原因，她放弃了团队领导者的角色。| synonyms: relinquished, renounced, resigned | antonyms: assumed, usurped, seized, retained | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *abdicatus*, past participle of *abdicare* "to disown, renounce, reject," from *ab* "away" + *dicare* "to proclaim, dedicate, devote."
