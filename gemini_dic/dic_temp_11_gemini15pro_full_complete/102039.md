isolationism:/ˈaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪzəm/
n.|孤立主义|A national policy of avoiding involvement in world affairs.|The country's isolationism prevented it from joining the international alliance. 这个国家的孤立主义使其未能加入国际联盟。|neutrality, nonintervention|interventionism, internationalism|noun

isolationisms:/ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪzəmz/
n.|孤立主义（复数）|Multiple instances or forms of the policy of national isolation.|The historian compared the isolationisms of different countries throughout the 20th century.  这位历史学家比较了20世纪不同国家的孤立主义。|neutralities, noninterventions|interventionisms, internationalisms|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "isolation" + "-ism."  "Isolation" comes from French "isolation," from "isoler" (to isolate), from Italian "isolare," from "isola" (island), from Latin "insula" (island).  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns meaning a doctrine, belief, or practice.
