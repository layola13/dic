cowled:/kaʊld/
adjective
戴着风帽的；覆盖着的
Covered with or as if with a cowl.
The figure was cowled and cloaked, making it difficult to discern their identity.  这个身影戴着风帽，披着斗篷，很难辨认出他们的身份。
The monk, cowled and silent, entered the chapel.  戴着风帽、沉默不语的僧侣走进了小教堂。
近义词: hooded, cloaked, covered
反义词: uncovered, exposed
词性: adjective

cowl:/kaʊl/
noun
风帽；罩；通风管罩
A large loose hood, especially one forming part of a monk's habit. Also, a covering for a chimney, engine, etc.
The monk pulled his cowl over his head.  僧侣把风帽拉过头顶。
A cowl protects the engine from the elements.  罩子保护发动机免受各种因素的影响。
近义词: hood, covering
反义词: (For clothing) none; (For engine) none
词性: noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *coule, from Old French coule, from Latin cucullus (“hood, cowl”), possibly related to cucumis (“cucumber”). The sense of "chimney covering" arose from the resemblance to a monk's cowl.
