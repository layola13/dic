leider:/ˈlaɪdɐ/| adv. |可惜地，遗憾地|Unfortunately, regrettably. Expressing sorrow or regret.  It's often used to introduce bad news or express disappointment. |Leider muss ich absagen. (Unfortunately, I have to cancel.)|Synonyms: unfortunately, sadly, regrettably|Antonyms: fortunately, luckily|Adverb

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Leider regnet es heute.
Unfortunately, it's raining today.

Leider ist das Museum heute geschlossen.
Unfortunately, the museum is closed today.


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Leider regnet es gerade.
Unfortunately, it is raining right now.

Leider arbeitet er gerade.
Unfortunately, he is working right now.


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
Leider hat der Zug Verspätung.
Unfortunately, the train has been delayed.

Leider habe ich meinen Schlüssel verloren.
Unfortunately, I have lost my key.


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
Leider hat es den ganzen Tag geregnet.
Unfortunately, it has been raining all day.

Leider hat er die ganze Zeit gearbeitet.
Unfortunately, he has been working all this time.


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
Leider regnete es gestern.
Unfortunately, it rained yesterday.

Leider konnte ich gestern nicht kommen.
Unfortunately, I couldn't come yesterday.


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
Leider regnete es, als wir ankamen.
Unfortunately, it was raining when we arrived.

Leider arbeitete er, als ich anrief.
Unfortunately, he was working when I called.


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
Leider hatte der Zug schon Verspätung, bevor ich am Bahnhof ankam.
Unfortunately, the train had already been delayed before I arrived at the station.

Leider hatte ich meinen Schlüssel verloren, bevor ich das Haus verließ.
Unfortunately, I had lost my key before I left the house.


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
Leider hatte es schon seit Stunden geregnet, bevor wir ankamen.
Unfortunately, it had been raining for hours before we arrived.

Leider hatte er schon den ganzen Tag gearbeitet, bevor ich ihn sah.
Unfortunately, he had been working all day before I saw him.


一般将来时 (Simple Future):

Unfortunately, it will rain tomorrow.

Unfortunately, I won't be able to come tomorrow.


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):

Unfortunately, I will be working tomorrow.

Leider wird er morgen verreisen.
Unfortunately, he will be travelling tomorrow.


将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
Leider wird der Zug Verspätung haben, wenn wir ankommen.
Unfortunately, the train will have been delayed by the time we arrive.

Leider werde ich meine Arbeit nicht beendet haben, wenn du kommst.
Unfortunately, I will not have finished my work by the time you arrive.


将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):

PHRASE->
Leider nein.
Unfortunately no.

Leider ja.
Unfortunately yes.

Das ist leider so.
That's unfortunately the case.

Leider Gottes. (More archaic/formal)
Unfortunately. / Alas.

Es tut mir leider sehr leid.
I'm very sorry, unfortunately.



Note: "Leider" doesn't have a direct opposite.  While "fortunately" or "luckily" express the opposite sentiment, they aren't grammatically opposite in the same way "happy" is to "sad."  "Leider" is primarily used to express regret or sorrow about a situation, not to describe the situation itself.  The situation itself might be described as "bad" or "unfavorable," the opposites of which would be "good" or "favorable."
