middle-brow: /ˈmɪdlˌbraʊ/ | adj. n. | 中庸的；中等文化水平的；品味一般的；中庸艺术爱好者 | Appealing to or typical of a person with conventional, middle-class tastes in literature, the arts, etc.; not highly intellectual or cultured, but not lowbrow either.  Someone who appreciates things that are neither highbrow nor lowbrow. |  She enjoys middle-brow novels with engaging plots and relatable characters. 她喜欢情节引人入胜、人物贴近生活的中庸小说。 |  He considered the exhibition rather middle-brow. 他认为这次展览相当平庸。| 近义词：conventional, unpretentious, mainstream | 反义词：highbrow, lowbrow, intellectual, sophisticated | 词性: adjective, noun


Etymology:  "Middlebrow" emerged in the early 20th century, combining "middle" with "brow," a metaphorical reference to the level of one's forehead, suggesting intellectual capacity.  "Highbrow" referred to those with supposedly high intellects, while "lowbrow" denoted those with less intellectual interests.
