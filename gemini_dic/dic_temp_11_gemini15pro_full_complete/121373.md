misguiding:/ˌmɪsˈɡaɪdɪŋ/
adjective
误导的；使人误入歧途的
leading someone to believe something that is not true
例句：The advertisement was misleading, as the product did not perform as advertised. 这则广告具有误导性，因为产品并不像广告中宣传的那样。

例句：His misguiding directions caused us to get lost. 他误导性的指示使我们迷路了。

近义词：deceptive, misleading, fallacious
反义词：accurate, truthful, helpful
词性: adjective

词源:  From mis- + guiding, present participle of guide.  Mis- is a prefix of Germanic origin meaning "wrong," "bad," "wrongly," or "badly." "Guide" comes from the Old French word "guider," meaning "to guide" or "to show the way."
