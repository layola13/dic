non-compete: /ˌnɒn kəmˈpiːt/ (UK) /ˌnɑːn kəmˈpiːt/ (US)
non-compete: adj. n.|竞业禁止（的），非竞争（的）|A non-compete clause is a clause under which one party (usually an employee) agrees not to enter into or start a similar profession or trade in competition against another party (usually the employer).|例句：He signed a non-compete agreement when he left the company. 他离开公司时签署了一份竞业禁止协议。|近义词：restrictive covenant, non-competition|反义词: open competition|词性: adjective, noun

non-compete: /ˌnɒn kəmˈpiːt/ (UK) /ˌnɑːn kəmˈpiːt/ (US)
non-compete: n.|竞业禁止协议|A legal agreement in which someone promises not to work for a competitor for a specific period of time after leaving their current employer.|例句：The non-compete prevented her from working for a rival firm. 这份竞业禁止协议阻止了她为竞争对手工作。|近义词：non-competition agreement, restrictive covenant|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider "freedom to work")|词性: noun
