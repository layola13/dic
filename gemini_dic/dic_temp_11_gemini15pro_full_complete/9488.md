antiromanticism:/ˌæntiroʊˈmæntɪsɪzəm/| n.|反浪漫主义|A movement or attitude that rejects or opposes romanticism, particularly in literature and the arts.  It emphasizes realism, objectivity, and reason over emotion, imagination, and idealism.|The stark realism of the novel was a clear example of antiromanticism in literature.|近义词:realism, classicism, cynicism|反义词:romanticism, idealism, sentimentalism|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + romanticism.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to," derived from Greek. "Romanticism" comes from the French word "romantisme," ultimately from the Old French "romanz" meaning "romance" or "narrative."
