furiously:/ˈfjʊəriəsli/| adv.|激烈地，狂暴地|in a furious manner; with great energy and intensity.  Driven by anger or uncontrolled emotion.|The wind howled furiously.  风狂暴地呼啸。|violently, intensely, wildly|calmly, gently|adverb
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *furiosli, *furiousely, equivalent to furious +‎ -ly.  The root word, "furious," comes from the Latin "furiosus," meaning "full of fury or rage."
