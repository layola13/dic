half-subdued:/ˌhæf səbˈdjuːd/ | adj. | 半抑制的；半控制的；半压制的 | Partially suppressed or controlled; not fully subdued. | Her laughter was half-subdued, a mixture of amusement and nervousness. 她笑得半带压抑，既觉得好笑又有些紧张。 |  The city lights were half-subdued by the fog. 城市的灯光被雾气半遮半掩。 | Near synonyms: partially suppressed, muted, restrained, dampened | Near antonyms: unrestrained, uninhibited, boisterous, loud | Adjective

subdue: /səbˈdjuː/ | v. | 征服；压制；减轻；克制 | Overcome, quieten, or bring under control (a feeling or person). | The fire was finally subdued by the firefighters. 消防员最终扑灭了大火。 | He struggled to subdue his anger. 他努力压制自己的愤怒。 | Near synonyms: conquer, overcome, suppress, control, vanquish | Near antonyms: release, unleash, incite, provoke | Verb
