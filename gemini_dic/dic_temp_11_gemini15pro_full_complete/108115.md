laticlave: /ˈlætɪˌkleɪv/
noun
宽紫条纹长袍; 骑士阶级; 元老阶级
A broad purple stripe on the tunic of Roman senators and knights.  It was a mark of rank.
The senator wore his laticlave with pride.  这位元老院议员自豪地穿着他的宽紫条纹长袍。
Synonyms:  purple stripe,  senatorial stripe,  toga praetexta (for magistrates), clavus
Antonyms:  angusticlave (narrow purple stripe worn by knights)
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *latus* (broad) + *clavus* (stripe, nail).
