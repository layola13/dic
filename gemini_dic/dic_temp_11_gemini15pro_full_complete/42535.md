countermarch:/ˈkaʊntərˌmɑːrtʃ/ | n. v. | 反方向行进，反行进；对策 | A maneuver in which a body of troops reverses the direction of its march.  To march back; to march in a reversed order or direction. | The soldiers performed a countermarch on the parade ground. 士兵们在阅兵场上进行了反方向行进。 | synonyms: about-face, reverse march, retreat, withdrawal | antonyms: advance, proceed, progress | noun, verb

countermarch:/ˈkaʊntərˌmɑːrtʃ/ | v. | 采取对策；对抗 | To take countermeasures against. | The company countermarched the competitor's aggressive advertising campaign with a series of humorous commercials. 这家公司用一系列幽默的广告来对抗竞争对手咄咄逼人的广告攻势。| synonyms: counteract, oppose, counter | antonyms: support, assist, aid | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *contremarche*, from contre- (“against, counter-”) + marche (“march, walk”).
