deflectionize:/dɪˈflɛkʃənaɪz/ | v. | 使偏斜，使转向 | To cause something to deflect or be deflected; to cause something to deviate from a straight course. | The magnetic field deflectionizes the electrons. 磁场使电子发生偏转。|  The curved surface deflectionizes the light rays. 曲面使光线偏转。 | synonyms: deflect, divert, deviate | antonyms: straighten, focus, direct | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "deflection" + "-ize". "Deflection" comes from the Late Latin "deflexio," from the verb "deflectere," meaning "to bend down or aside."  "-ize" is a suffix forming verbs, meaning "to make or cause to be."
