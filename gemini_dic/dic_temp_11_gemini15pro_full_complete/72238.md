florideae:/flɔːˈrɪdiˌiː/ | n. |红藻纲| A class of red algae, characterized by having no flagella at any stage in their life cycle. They are typically marine and include many economically important species, such as those used for agar and carrageenan production. | Example: Many species of Florideae are used in Asian cuisine. 许多红藻纲物种用于亚洲菜肴。| Example: Florideae play a crucial role in marine ecosystems. 红藻纲在海洋生态系统中扮演着至关重要的角色。| Synonyms: Rhodophyceae | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a taxonomic class) | noun

Etymology:  From Latin *floridus* ("blooming, flowery") + -eae (a taxonomic suffix denoting a class).
