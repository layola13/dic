discourse:/ˈdɪskɔːrs/ /ˈdɪskʊrs/| n. vi.| 论述，谈话，演讲；进行讨论 |A formal discussion of a subject in speech or writing.| The professor gave a discourse on the history of philosophy. 教授就哲学史发表了演讲。|近义词：discussion, conversation, speech, lecture, talk|反义词：silence, quiet|noun

discourse:/ˈdɪskɔːrs/ /ˈdɪskʊrs/| vi.| 演讲，谈论 |To speak or write authoritatively about a topic.| He discoursed at length on the merits of vegetarianism. 他详细论述了素食主义的好处。|近义词：speak, talk, lecture, expound|反义词：be quiet, listen|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English discours, from Old French discours, from Late Latin discursus (“running about, conversation, discourse”), from Latin discursus, past participle of discurrere (“to run about, run to and fro, digress”), from dis- (“apart, in different directions”) + currere (“to run”).
