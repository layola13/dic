dominus:/ˈdɒmɪnəs/ (Classical Latin) /ˈdoʊmɪnʊs/ (Ecclesiastical Latin)| n.|主，主人，领主，阁下|A Latin word meaning "lord," "master," or "owner." It is the origin of words like "dominate" and "domain."|The Roman dominus held absolute power over his slaves.|近义词：lord, master, ruler, owner|反义词：slave, servant|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Proto-Italic *dominos, from Proto-Indo-European *dṓm- (“house”).  Cognates include Sanskrit dám- (“house”), Ancient Greek δόμος (dómos, “house”), and Old English tūn (“enclosure”). The word evolved from referring to the master of a house to a more general term of authority and power.
