flustration: /flʌˈstreɪʃən/ | n. | 慌乱，不安，紧张 | A state of nervous excitement or agitation. |  I felt a surge of flustration as I realized I was late for the interview. 我意识到面试迟到了，感到一阵慌乱。

She experienced a moment of flustration when she couldn't find her keys. 她找不到钥匙时，感到一阵不安。 | 近义词: agitation, anxiety, nervousness, disquiet, unease | 反义词: calmness, composure, tranquility | 词性: 名词

词源:  "Flustration" likely originates as a back-formation from "flustered," which itself comes from the now obsolete verb "fluster," meaning "to make hot and red," likely related to "blush."  The addition of "-ation" forms the noun, indicating the state of being flustered.
