contrastimulant:/ˌkɒntrəˈstɪmjʊlənt/ /ˌkɑːntrəˈstɪmjələnt/| n. adj.|对抗兴奋剂，抑制兴奋的|A contrastimulant is a substance or medication that reduces or opposes the effects of a stimulant. It can diminish the physiological and psychological effects of stimulation, such as increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure, agitation, and anxiety.  In some contexts, it can also refer to something that counteracts or offsets excitement or stimulation in a broader sense, not necessarily limited to pharmacological agents.|
