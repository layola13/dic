flaxweed:/ˈflæksˌwiːd/| n. |亚麻 weeds|A weedy plant with usually blue, purple, or white flowers, especially of the genus *Linaria*.|The field was overrun with flaxweed.|近义词：toadflax, butter-and-eggs|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From *flax* + *weed*.  Flaxweed is so named because it resembles flax and is considered a weed.  *Flax* comes from Old English *fleax*, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *flahsaz*. *Weed* comes from Old English *wēod*, meaning "plant, herb."
