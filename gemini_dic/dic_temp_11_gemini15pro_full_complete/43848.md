crepuscular:/krɪˈpʌskjələr/ | adj. | 暮色昏暗的；朦胧的；黄昏的 | Relating to or resembling twilight; dim; occurring or active during twilight. |  The crepuscular light cast long shadows across the lawn. 暮色昏暗的光线在草坪上投下长长的影子。

The owl is a crepuscular bird, most active at dawn and dusk. 猫头鹰是一种暮色活动的鸟类，在黎明和黄昏最活跃。| 近义词:twilight, dusky, dim | 反义词:bright, daytime, diurnal | 词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *creperus ("doubtful, obscure") related to creper ("dark"), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *krep- (“to waver, tremble, be dark or dusky").  The same root gives us "creep" in English, referring to the dim and uncertain light in which creatures might creep unseen.
