coparcener:/ˌkoʊˈpɑːrsənər/ | n. |共同继承人；遗产共同继承人 |A coparcener is a person who inherits property jointly with others, typically under common law.  This term is most often used in relation to inheritance by daughters who inherit equally. |Example 1:  The three sisters were coparceners of their father's estate.  这三姐妹是她们父亲遗产的共同继承人。

Example 2:  Under the old laws, sons were often preferred over daughters as coparceners.  根据旧法律，儿子通常比女儿更优先成为共同继承人。 | 近义词: joint heir, coheir | 反义词: sole heir | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From Anglo-French *parcener, from parçonier "partner," from parçon "share, portion," from Latin partitio "a dividing, division, partition," from partiri "to divide, share, distribute," from pars "part, piece, share."
