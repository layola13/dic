gondorians:/ɡɒnˈdɔːriənz/
|n.|刚铎人|People of Gondor, a fictional kingdom in J.R.R. Tolkien's Middle-earth.|The Gondorians were renowned for their courage and skill in war. 刚铎人以其勇气和战争技巧而闻名。|Near Synonyms: Men of Gondor, Southrons (in a specific context)|Antonyms: Orcs, Easterlings (common enemies)|Noun

Etymology:
Derived from "Gondor" + "-ian" (suffix denoting belonging to or originating from). "Gondor" itself comes from Sindarin, one of Tolkien's invented languages, and likely means "Stone-land."
