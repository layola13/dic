capacitive:/kəˈpæsɪtɪv/| adj.|电容的，电容性的|Relating to or having capacitance; having the ability to store an electrical charge.|例句：A capacitive touchscreen responds to the electrical charge in your finger.  电容式触摸屏会对您手指中的电荷做出反应。

例句：Capacitive sensors are used in many electronic devices. 电容传感器用于许多电子设备中。|近义词：electrostatic|反义词: resistive, inductive|词性:adjective

capacitance:/kəˈpæsɪtəns/| n.|电容，电容量|The ability of a system to store an electric charge.|例句：The capacitance of the capacitor is measured in farads. 电容器的电容以法拉为单位。

例句：Increasing the area of the plates increases the capacitance. 增加极板面积会增加电容。|近义词：capacity|反义词: (No direct antonym, but low capacitance is the opposite of high capacitance) |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "capacity" + "-ive". "Capacity" comes from Late Latin "capacitas", from Latin "capax" (able to hold), related to "capere" (to take, hold).  "-ive" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "tending to; having the nature or quality of".
