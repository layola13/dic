handcuffs: /ˈhændkʌfs/ (US) /ˈhændkʌfs/ (UK)| n.| 手铐 |A pair of metal rings connected by a short chain and locked around a person's wrists, typically used by police to restrain a prisoner. |The police officer placed the suspect in handcuffs. 警察给嫌疑人戴上了手铐。|Synonyms: manacles, shackles, restraints|Antonyms: freedom, liberty|Noun

handcuff: /ˈhændkʌf/ (US) /ˈhændkʌf/ (UK)| vt.| 给…戴上手铐 |To restrain someone by fastening handcuffs on their wrists. |The officers handcuffed the suspect and took him to the police station.  警察给嫌疑犯戴上手铐，并把他带到了警察局。|Synonyms: restrain, shackle, manacle|Antonyms: release, free|Verb
