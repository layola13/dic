kidnappings:/ˈkɪdnæpɪŋz/
|n.|绑架（kidnapping的复数形式）|The act of taking someone away illegally by force, typically for ransom.|Several kidnappings have been reported in the area recently. 最近该地区报告了几起绑架案。|
|近义词：abductions, seizures|
|反义词：releases, liberations|
|词性：名词|


kidnapping:/ˈkɪdnæpɪŋ/
|n.|绑架|The act of taking someone away illegally by force, typically for ransom.|The kidnapping of the businessman made headlines. 商人的绑架事件上了头条。|
|近义词：abduction, seizure|
|反义词：release, liberation|
|词性：名词|

kidnap:/ˈkɪdnæp/
|vt.|绑架|To take (someone) away illegally by force, typically for ransom.|They kidnapped the child and demanded a ransom. 他们绑架了孩子并索要赎金。|
|近义词：abduct, seize|
|反义词：release, liberate|
|词性：动词|


ETYMOLOGY->
From kid (child) + nap (to snatch).  Originally referred to the abduction of children, but now encompasses the abduction of adults as well.
