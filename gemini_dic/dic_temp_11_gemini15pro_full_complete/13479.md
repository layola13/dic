atropia:/əˈtrəʊpiə/ (US: /əˈtroʊpiə/) | n. |阿托品|A poisonous crystalline alkaloid extracted from deadly nightshade and other related plants. It is used in medicine as an antispasmodic and to dilate the pupil of the eye. | Atropine is used to treat certain types of nerve agent and pesticide poisonings. 阿托品用于治疗某些类型的神经毒剂和农药中毒。

Atropine can be used to dilate the pupils during an eye exam.  阿托品可用于在眼科检查期间扩大瞳孔。


近义词: hyoscyamine, scopolamine | 反义词: pilocarpine (in terms of pupil constriction) | 词性: noun


Etymology:
From Modern Latin *atropia, from Atropa (the deadly nightshade plant genus, named after Atropos, one of the three Fates in Greek mythology who cuts the thread of life) + -ia (a suffix used to form nouns).
