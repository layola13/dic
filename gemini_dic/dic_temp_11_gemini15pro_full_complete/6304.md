amortised: /ˈæmərtaɪzd/ (US) /əˈmɔːtaɪzd/ (UK)| adj.|摊销的，分期偿还的| Gradually reducing or writing off the cost or value of an asset over a period of time.| The company amortised the cost of the new equipment over five years.  公司将新设备的成本在五年内摊销。| synonyms: depreciated, expensed, written off | antonyms: appreciated, accrued | adjective

amortize: /ˈæmərtaɪz/ (US) /əˈmɔːtaɪz/ (UK)| v.|摊销，分期偿还| To gradually reduce or write off the cost or value of an asset over a period of time.|  The loan will be amortised over 25 years. 这笔贷款将在 25 年内摊销。| synonyms: depreciate, expense, write off | antonyms: appreciate, accrue | verb

词源: From French *amortir*, from Vulgar Latin *admortire* "to kill, extinguish," from Latin *ad-* "to" + *mors* "death."  The financial sense developed from the idea of "killing" or extinguishing a debt.
