head-off:/ˌhɛd ˈɔːf/ | v. | 阻止，防止 | To prevent something from happening, especially something unpleasant. | The police headed off the riot before it could get out of control. 警察在骚乱失控之前阻止了它。| prevent, avert, forestall | allow, permit | verb

head-off:/ˌhɛd ˈɔːf/ | v. | 拦截，拦住 | To intercept or stop someone or something from going somewhere. | They headed off the cattle before they reached the highway. 他们在牛到达高速公路之前拦截了它们。| intercept, stop, block | release, free | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the literal sense of turning the head of someone or something away from a particular direction.  The "head" refers to the front or leading part, and "off" indicates a diversion or prevention.
