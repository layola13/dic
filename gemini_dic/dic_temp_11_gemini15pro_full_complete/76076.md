furor:/ˈfjʊərɔːr/ (US) /ˈfjʊərɔː(r)/ (UK)| n.|狂热，轰动，喧闹，激动|An outbreak of public anger or excitement; a widespread, temporary enthusiasm.|The release of the new phone caused a furor among consumers.  这款新手机的发布在消费者中引起了轰动。|Synonyms: uproar, commotion, frenzy, excitement, rage|Antonyms: calm, peace, tranquility|noun

furor:/ˈfjʊərɔːr/ (US) /ˈfjʊərɔː(r)/ (UK)| n.|狂怒，愤怒|A state of intense anger or fury.|His insensitive remarks sparked a furor among the audience.  他不敏感的言论激起了观众的愤怒。|Synonyms: rage, fury, wrath, indignation|Antonyms: calmness, composure, serenity|noun
