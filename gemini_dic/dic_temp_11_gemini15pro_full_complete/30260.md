celtiberian:/ˌkɛltɪˈbɪəriən/
adjective|凯尔特伊比利亚的；凯尔特伊比利亚人|Relating to or denoting the Celtiberians, a pre-Roman people of the Iberian Peninsula.|The Celtiberian language was influenced by both Celtic and Iberian languages.|近义词:Hispano-Celtic, Iberian|反义词: (No clear antonym, possibly Roman, Carthaginian depending on context)|词性:adjective

celtiberian:/ˌkɛltɪˈbɪəriən/
noun|凯尔特伊比利亚人|A member of an ancient people inhabiting the central-eastern part of the Iberian Peninsula.|The Celtiberians were known for their fierce resistance to Roman conquest.|近义词:Hispano-Celtic, Iberian|反义词:(No clear antonym, possibly Roman, Carthaginian depending on context)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Celt + Iberian.  Combines "Celt," referring to the Celtic influence, and "Iberian," referring to the Iberian Peninsula.
