disinclination:/dɪsˌɪnklɪˈneɪʃn/| n. |不情愿，厌恶|A feeling of not wanting to do something; reluctance.|例句：He showed a strong disinclination to get involved. 他表现出极不愿意参与。

例句：She had a disinclination for housework. 她厌恶做家务。


|近义词：reluctance, aversion, unwillingness, distaste|反义词: inclination, willingness, eagerness, desire|词性:noun

disinclinations:/dɪsˌɪnklɪˈneɪʃnz/| n. |不情愿，厌恶（复数）|Plural form of disinclination, referring to multiple feelings of not wanting to do something.|例句：His disinclinations towards exercise are well known. 他对运动的厌恶是众所周知的。


例句：Her many disinclinations prevented her from pursuing a career in finance. 她的诸多不情愿阻碍了她从事金融事业。


|近义词：reluctances, aversions, unwillingnesses, distastes|反义词: inclinations, willingnesses, eagernesses, desires|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + inclination (a feeling of wanting to do something).  The word "inclination" ultimately comes from the Latin "inclinare" meaning "to bend or lean."
