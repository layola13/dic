arbitrating: /ˈɑːrbɪtreɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 仲裁；公断 |  The process of resolving a dispute between two parties by appointing a neutral third party (an arbitrator) to make a binding decision. |  The union and management are arbitrating their contract dispute. 工会和管理层正在仲裁他们的合同纠纷。| synonyms: mediating, adjudicating, umpiring | antonyms: disputing, fighting, litigating | verb

arbitrate: /ˈɑːrbɪtreɪt/ | v. | 仲裁；公断 | To act as an arbitrator; to settle a dispute between two parties.  | They asked a judge to arbitrate the boundary dispute. 他们请一位法官来仲裁边界纠纷。| synonyms: mediate, adjudicate, umpire | antonyms: dispute, fight, litigate | verb

arbitrator: /ˈɑːrbɪtreɪtər/ | n. | 仲裁员；公断人 | A neutral third party chosen to settle a dispute. | The arbitrator's decision is final and binding. 仲裁员的决定是最终的和有约束力的。| synonyms: mediator, adjudicator, umpire | antonyms: disputant, litigant | noun


Etymology:
From Latin *arbitratus*, past participle of *arbitrari* "to witness, judge, consider," from *arbiter* "judge, witness."
