desecrate:/ˈdesɪkreɪt/| v. |亵渎，玷污|To treat (a sacred place or thing) with violent disrespect; violate.|The vandals desecrated the church by spray-painting graffiti on the altar.|近义词:profane, violate, defile|反义词:consecrate, sanctify, venerate|词性:动词
Etymology: From Latin *desecratus*, past participle of *desecrare* "to unhallow, profane," from *de-* "un-" + *sacer* "sacred."
