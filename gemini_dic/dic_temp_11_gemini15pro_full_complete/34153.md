clam-up:/klæm ˈʌp/| phrasal verb | 闭嘴，沉默不语 | To suddenly become silent; to refuse to speak. |  He clammed up when the police started asking questions. 他在警察开始提问时闭口不言。

|  She clammed up as soon as her ex-husband walked into the room.  当她的前夫走进房间时，她立刻就不说话了。 | 近义词: shut up, fall silent, go quiet, refuse to speak | 反义词: open up, speak out, be talkative | 词性: 动词短语

词源:  "Clam" originally referred to the bivalve mollusk that closes its shell tightly. The phrasal verb "clam up" extends this imagery to someone closing their mouth and refusing to speak.
