faintishness:/ˈfeɪntɪʃnəs/| n. |微弱；昏厥；虚弱|A state of feeling faint, lightheaded, or dizzy.|She experienced a brief spell of faintishness after standing up too quickly. 她站起来太快后感到一阵短暂的晕眩。|近义词：dizziness, lightheadedness, weakness, faintness|反义词: strength, vigor|词性: noun

Etymology:
From faint + -ish + -ness.  "Faint" comes from Old French *feint, past participle of feindre "to feign, pretend," from Latin fingere "to shape, form, invent, feign."  "-ish" is a suffix forming adjectives from nouns or adjectives, meaning "somewhat," "rather," "like," "characteristic of." "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
