nonnegligibility:/ˌnɒnˌnɛɡlɪdʒɪˈbɪlɪti/ | n. |不可忽略性；非可忽略性|The quality of being significant or important enough to warrant attention or consideration.  This often appears in academic contexts, particularly in statistics, computer science (specifically cryptography), and economics.|The nonnegligibility of the side effects prompted the researchers to halt the study.  副作用的不可忽略性促使研究人员停止了研究。|significance, importance, materiality, consequence, relevance|insignificance, negligibility|noun
