incorporealism:/ˌɪnkɔːrˈpɔːriəlɪzəm/
n.
非物质性；非实体性；精神性
The doctrine that there are no material substances, only minds and ideas.  The belief that material things have no real existence.
His philosophy embraced a form of incorporealism, believing that the physical world was merely an illusion. 他信奉一种非物质主义哲学，认为物质世界只是一种幻觉。

The ancient philosopher argued for incorporealism, stating that true reality existed beyond the physical realm.  这位古代哲学家主张非物质主义，声称真正的实在存在于物质领域之外。

Synonyms: immaterialism, spiritualism
Antonyms: materialism, physicalism
