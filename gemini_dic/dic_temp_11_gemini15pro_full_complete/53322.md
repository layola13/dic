disabuse:/ˌdɪsəˈbjuːz/
verb
使醒悟，纠正，打消…的错误想法
to free someone from a mistaken belief
例句：He quickly disabused me of the notion that I could get rich quickly. 他很快打消了我能快速致富的想法。

例句：The teacher disabused the students of the idea that cheating was acceptable. 老师纠正了学生们认为作弊是可以接受的错误想法。

近义词：undeceive, enlighten, correct, set straight
反义词：deceive, mislead, misinform
词性：v.

词源：From Late Latin *disabusare, from dis- ‘expressing reversal’ + abusare ‘to misuse, deceive’.
