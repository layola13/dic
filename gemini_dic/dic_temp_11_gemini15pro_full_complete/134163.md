nonimputably:/ˌnɒnɪmˈpjuːtəbli/
adjective
不可归罪地，无责任地
Not imputable; incapable of being imputed; not chargeable.  Absence of blame or fault.
His actions were nonimputably motivated by his desire to help. 他的行为是出于帮助别人的愿望，无可指责。

Synonym: blamelessly, irreproachably, unimpeachably, faultlessly
Antonym: culpably, blamably, reprehensibly

Etymology:
From non- (not) + imputable (capable of being attributed, especially of guilt or blame) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  Imputable comes from Late Latin imputabilis, from imputare "to reckon, attribute," from in- "in, on" + putare "to think, consider, reckon."
