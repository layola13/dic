deprostrate: /ˌdiːˈprɒstreɪt/ /ˌdiːˈprɑːstreɪt/
verb
使俯卧；使屈服；使衰弱
to cast (oneself) face down in humility, submission, or adoration; to reduce to extreme weakness or helplessness
He deprostrated himself before the king. 他在国王面前俯首称臣。
The illness deprostrated her.  疾病使她衰弱。
近义词: prostrate, humble, subdue, weaken
反义词: strengthen, empower, uplift
词性: verb

词源:
From Late Latin *deprostrare, from Latin de- + prostrare (“to throw down, prostrate”), from pro- + sternere (“to spread, strew, lay flat”).
