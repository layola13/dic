casualty: /ˈkæʒʊəlti/ (US) /ˈkæʒuəlti/ (UK)| n.|伤亡人员；受害者；损失；毁坏|A person killed or injured in a war or accident. Something lost or destroyed.|例句：There were dozens of casualties in the train crash. 火车相撞事故中有数十人伤亡。

例句：The hurricane caused heavy casualties and damage. 飓风造成了严重的伤亡和损失。

近义词：victim, fatality, loss|反义词: survivor|词性: noun


casualty: /ˈkæʒʊəlti/ (US) /ˈkæʒuəlti/ (UK)| n.|意外事件；事故|An accident, especially one involving loss of life or serious injury.|例句：The emergency services dealt with multiple casualties on the motorway. 紧急服务部门处理了高速公路上发生的数起事故。


例句：The storm resulted in several casualties at sea.  风暴导致海上发生了多起事故。


近义词：accident, incident, mishap|反义词: N/A|词性: noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From French *casuelté*, from Late Latin *casualitas* ("chance, accident"), from Latin *casualis* ("happening by chance"). Ultimately from Latin *cadere* ("to fall").
