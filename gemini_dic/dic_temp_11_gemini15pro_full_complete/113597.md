macer:/ˈmeɪsər/ | n. |浸渍器；浸渍者|A person or thing that macerates.  A vessel in which something is macerated. |The pharmacist used a macer to soften the herbs. 药剂师用浸渍器软化草药。|Synonym: steeper, soaker |Antonym: dryer, dehydrator | noun

macerate:/ˈmæsəreɪt/ | v. |浸软；(使)糜烂；憔悴；变瘦|To soften or break down by soaking; to become soft or separated into parts by steeping in a liquid. To make or become lean or thin, especially by fasting or other ascetic practice. |The chef macerated the fruit in wine. 厨师将水果浸泡在葡萄酒中。|Synonym: soak, steep, soften |Antonym: dry, dehydrate, harden | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *macerāre, from Latin macer (“lean, thin”), from Proto-Italic *makros, from Proto-Indo-European *mak- (“long, lean”).  Related to meager.
