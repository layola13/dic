creeper:/ˈkriːpər/| n.| 爬行者；攀缘植物；匍匐前进的人或动物|A plant that grows along the ground, walls, or other surfaces by clinging or twining.  A person or animal that moves slowly and quietly, often close to the ground.  A type of firework that burns slowly along the ground before exploding.|The ivy is a creeper that covers the entire wall.  常春藤是一种覆盖整面墙的攀缘植物。

The cat moved like a creeper, stalking the bird.  这只猫像爬行者一样移动，跟踪着那只鸟。

He lit the creeper firework and it snaked across the ground. 他点燃了爬行烟火，它在地面上蜿蜒前行。|近义词：climber, crawler, vine,  stealthy person|反义词:runner, sprinter|词性:noun

creeper:/ˈkriːpər/| adj. (informal, often disapproving)|令人毛骨悚然的人；讨厌鬼|Someone who is unpleasant or strange, especially in a way that makes you feel uncomfortable.|He's such a creeper, always staring at people. 他真是个讨厌鬼，总是盯着别人看。

I get a creeper vibe from that guy. 我从那个家伙身上感到一种令人毛骨悚然的感觉。|近义词：weird, creepy, unsettling, disturbing|反义词:pleasant, agreeable|词性:adjective
