delegitimization:/diːləˈdʒɪtɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |合法性剥夺，非合法化|The act of making something or someone seem not legitimate or valid.  The process of delegitimizing something or someone. |The delegitimization of the government led to widespread protests. 政府的合法性遭到剥夺，导致了广泛的抗议。| The media's constant attacks contributed to the delegitimization of the president. 媒体不断的攻击导致了总统的合法性遭到削弱。 |synonyms: discrediting, undermining, invalidating | antonyms: legitimizing, validating, authorizing | noun

delegitimize:/diːləˈdʒɪtɪmaɪz/ | v. |使失去合法性，剥夺合法性|To make something or someone seem not legitimate or valid. |The opposition party tried to delegitimize the election results. 反对党试图否定选举结果的合法性。|  False accusations were spread online to delegitimize the candidate. 网上散布不实指控，意图使候选人失去合法性。|synonyms: discredit, undermine, invalidate | antonyms: legitimize, validate, authorize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- (prefix meaning "remove, reverse") + legitimate + -ize (suffix forming verbs) + -ation (suffix forming nouns from verbs).  Ultimately from Latin legitimus "lawful."
