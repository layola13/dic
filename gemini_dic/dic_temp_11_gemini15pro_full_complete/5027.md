all-in:/ˌɔːlˈɪn/| adj. adv.| 全押的，孤注一掷的；包括一切的|  Completely committed to something, typically involving risk; inclusive of all costs or charges. | He went all-in with his poker hand. 他把所有的筹码都押上了。| 近义词：committed, invested, all-inclusive | 反义词: hesitant, cautious, partial | 词性: 形容词，副词

all-in:/ˌɔːlˈɪn/| n.| 全押，孤注一掷| The act of betting all of one's chips or resources in a game or venture. | His all-in bet paid off handsomely. 他的全押获得了丰厚的回报。| 近义词：gamble, wager | 反义词: caution, prudence | 词性: 名词


词源:  "All-in" originates from the gambling world, specifically poker, where it refers to betting all of one's chips. This usage then broadened to encompass any situation where someone is fully committed or has risked everything.
