infertilenesses:/ˌɪnfərˈtɪlnɪsɪz/
| n. |不孕症，不育症|The state of being infertile, inability to conceive children or produce offspring.  The plural form emphasizes multiple instances or cases of infertility.|The study examined the infertilenesses of couples exposed to certain toxins.|近义词：barrenness, sterility, unproductiveness|反义词：fertility, fecundity|词性: Noun

Etymology:
From infertile + -ness + -es.  "Infertile" comes from the Latin "in-" (not) + "fertilis" (fertile), which is related to "ferre" (to bear, carry). The suffix "-ness" forms nouns indicating a state or quality.  The "-es" creates the plural form.
