crusader:/kruːˈseɪdər/| n.|十字军战士；拥护者；改革者|A person who campaigns vigorously for political, social, or religious change; a participant in one of the religious wars between Christians and Muslims in the 11th, 12th, and 13th centuries.|例句：He was a crusader for educational reform. 他是一位教育改革的倡导者。|例句：The crusaders captured Jerusalem in 1099. 十字军于1099年占领了耶路撒冷。|近义词：campaigner, advocate, reformer, champion|反义词：opponent, antagonist|词性：名词

crusader:/kruːˈseɪdər/| n.|十字军|A series of religious wars between Christians and Muslims started primarily to secure control of holy sites considered sacred by both groups.|例句：The crusades lasted for several centuries. 十字军东征持续了几个世纪。|例句：The First Crusade began in 1096. 第一次十字军东征始于1096年。|近义词：holy war, expedition|反义词：peace, truce|词性：名词
