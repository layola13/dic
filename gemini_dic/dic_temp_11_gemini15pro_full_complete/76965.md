garbel:/ˈɡɑːrbəl/| n. vt.|垃圾，废物；筛选，挑选|Something worthless or nonsensical; to sift or sort, especially to remove impurities.|例句：The editor garbled the story, making it incomprehensible. 编辑把故事弄得乱七八糟，让人无法理解。|近义词：rubbish, trash, sift, sort|反义词:treasure, value, keep|n.


garbel:/ˈɡɑːrbəl/| vt.|筛选，挑选；歪曲，曲解|To distort or mix up (a message, text, etc.), so as to make it unintelligible.|例句：He garbled the instructions so badly that no one knew what to do. 他把指示说得乱七八糟，以至于没有人知道该怎么办。|近义词：distort, twist, muddle, confuse|反义词:clarify, explain, elucidate|v.
