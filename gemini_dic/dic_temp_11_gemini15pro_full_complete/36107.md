coerced: /koʊˈɜːrst/ (US) /kəʊˈɜːst/ (UK)| adj.|被胁迫的|persuaded or forced to do something by using threats or intimidation.| Example: The confession was coerced.  这供词是被逼的。|Synonyms: forced, compelled, pressured, intimidated|Antonyms: persuaded, willing, voluntary|Word Type: Adjective

coerce: /koʊˈɜːrs/ (US) /kəʊˈɜːs/ (UK)| vt.|胁迫，强迫|to persuade someone to do something by using force or threats.| Example: He was coerced into signing the confession. 他被迫签署了供词。|Synonyms: force, compel, pressure, intimidate|Antonyms: persuade, encourage|Word Type: Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *co-arcere "to restrain, confine," from co- "together" + arcere "to enclose, confine."
