kihi: /ˈkiːhiː/ | n. | 新西兰铃鸟 | A New Zealand honeyeater (Notiomystis cincta) with a short, slightly curved bill and a pair of distinctive fleshy wattles hanging from the gape. | Example: The kihi flitted from branch to branch, its bright yellow wattles bobbing. 新西兰铃鸟在树枝间飞来飞去，它鲜黄色的肉垂摇摇摆摆。| Example: Conservation efforts have helped increase the kihi population. 保护工作帮助增加了新西兰铃鸟的数量。 | Synonym: stitchbird, hihi | Antonym: N/A | noun
