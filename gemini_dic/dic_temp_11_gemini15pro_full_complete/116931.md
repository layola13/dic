mayweed:/ˈmeɪwiːd/| n. |五月菊，雏菊 |A common weedy plant with daisy-like flowers, typically white with a yellow center.  It belongs to the genus *Tripleurospermum* (formerly *Matricaria* *perforata* or *Anthemis cotula*).|The field was covered in mayweed.|Synonyms: dog fennel, stinking chamomile, dill weed|Antonyms: N/A (No direct antonym for a plant name)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *maythe*, from Old English *maþe* (meaning "mayweed") + *weed* (meaning "a wild plant").  The name refers to its flowering period, which often peaks in May.
