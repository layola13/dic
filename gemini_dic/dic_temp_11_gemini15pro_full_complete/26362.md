by-election:/ˌbaɪ ɪˈlɛkʃən/| n.|补选|A special election held between regular elections to fill a vacancy, typically caused by the death or resignation of an elected official.|例句：A by-election was called following the death of the MP.  由于议员去世，举行了补选。

例句：The government is expected to lose the upcoming by-election. 预计政府将在即将举行的补选中失利。|近义词：special election|反义词:general election|词性:noun

by-elections:/ˌbaɪ ɪˈlɛkʃənz/| n.|补选（复数）|Special elections held between regular elections to fill vacancies.|例句：Several by-elections were held across the country. 全国各地举行了多次补选。

例句：The results of the by-elections were a blow to the ruling party. 补选结果对执政党来说是一个打击。|近义词：special elections|反义词:general elections|词性:noun
