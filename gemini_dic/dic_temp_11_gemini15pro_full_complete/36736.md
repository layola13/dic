colloped:/ˈkɒləpt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləpt/ (US)| adj.| 切成薄片的；有皱纹的；有条纹的 | cut into thin slices; marked with wrinkles or stripes |  The chef colloped the beef for the stir-fry.  厨师把牛肉切成薄片用来炒菜。| synonyms: sliced, wrinkled, striped | antonyms: whole, smooth, unstriped | adjective

colloped:/ˈkɒləpt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːləpt/ (US)| v. (archaic) | 阉割；割下；剥夺 | castrate; cut off; deprive of |  The king colloped his enemies of their power. 国王剥夺了敌人的权力。 (archaic usage) | synonyms: castrate, geld, deprive, divest | antonyms: empower, enrich | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *colopen, possibly from Old Norse *kollauppr (“chopped meat”), from kolla (“to chop”) + upp (“up”). Related to Icelandic kolfur (“chopped meat”). The sense of "wrinkled" arose from the resemblance of wrinkles to slices of meat.
