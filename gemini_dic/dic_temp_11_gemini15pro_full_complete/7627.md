annuities:/əˈnjuːɪtiz/
n.
年金；养老金；年金保险
A fixed sum of money paid to someone each year, typically for the rest of their life.
Example: She invested her inheritance in an annuity. 她把遗产投资于年金。
Example: He receives a monthly annuity from his retirement plan. 他从退休计划中每月领取年金。
Synonyms: pension, allowance, stipend
Antonyms: lump sum, one-time payment
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *annuitates, plural of annuitatas (“yearly payment”), from Latin annuus (“yearly”).
