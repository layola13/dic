knavishly:/ˈneɪvɪʃli/| adv.| 恶作剧地；无赖地；狡猾地 | acting in a dishonest or unscrupulous way, typically in order to gain an advantage. | He knavishly stole the old woman's purse. 他狡猾地偷走了老太太的钱包。|  He knavishly avoided answering the question directly. 他狡猾地避免直接回答这个问题。| Synonyms: dishonestly, deceitfully, craftily, roguishly | Antonyms: honestly, honorably, fairly | Adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From knave + -ish + -ly.  "Knave" comes from Old English *cnafa "boy, male servant," later "rogue, villain."  The "-ish" suffix adds the meaning "like, resembling," and "-ly" transforms it into an adverb.
