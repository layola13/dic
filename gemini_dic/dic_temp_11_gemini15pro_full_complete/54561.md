dislodge:/dɪsˈlɒdʒ/ (UK), /dɪsˈlɑːdʒ/ (US)| v. |驱逐，取出，移动|To remove something or someone, often with difficulty, from a fixed position.|The earthquake dislodged several boulders from the cliff face. 地震使几块巨石从悬崖面上掉落。|近义词：remove, displace, extricate, oust|反义词：lodge, embed, fix, secure|词性:动词
dislodge:/dɪsˈlɒdʒ/ (UK), /dɪsˈlɑːdʒ/ (US)| n. |(罕用) 移位，排出物|The act of dislodging something. (This noun form is less common.)|The dislodge of the blockage caused a flood.  (Less common usage) 阻塞物的移位导致了洪水。|近义词：removal, displacement|反义词：lodging, embedding|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "deslogier," from "des-" (meaning "out of, from") + "logier" (meaning "to lodge").  Ultimately from Frankish "*lauhjan," meaning "to place, put, lay."
