ommatidium:/ˌɒməˈtɪdiəm/ (US) /ˌɒməˈtɪdɪəm/ (UK)| n. |小眼，单眼|One of the small optical units of the compound eye of arthropods such as insects and crustaceans.|Each ommatidium contains a lens and photoreceptor cells and functions as a separate visual receptor.|The dragonfly's eye has thousands of ommatidia, giving it exceptional vision.|Synonyms: facet, simple eye|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, consider "compound eye" as a contrasting structure)|Noun
