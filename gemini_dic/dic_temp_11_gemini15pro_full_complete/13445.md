atrabilarious: /ˌæt.rəˈbɪl.i.əs/
adjective
忧郁的；易怒的；坏脾气的
Affected with, characterized by, or causing melancholy, especially of a peevish or irritable kind.  Arising from or attributed to an excess of black bile.
He became increasingly atrabilarious as the rainy weather continued.  雨一直下个不停，他变得越来越忧郁易怒。
Synonyms: melancholic, morose, sullen, choleric, irascible, splenetic
Antonyms: cheerful, jovial, good-humored
词性: adjective


Etymology:
From Late Latin *atrabilārius, from Latin ātra bilis (“black bile”).  The four humors of ancient physiology were blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile.  An excess of black bile was believed to cause melancholy and irritability.
