absolutists:/ˈæbsəluːtɪsts/
n.|专制主义者；绝对主义者|A person who believes in absolutism (a political theory that absolute power should be vested in one ruler or authority).|The absolutists in the court resisted any attempts to limit the king's power.|近义词：autocrats, dictators, despots|反义词：democrats, liberals|词性:名词

Etymology:
From "absolute" + "-ist."  "Absolute" comes from Late Latin "absolutus," past participle of "absolvere" (to absolve, set free, finish), from "ab-" (from, away) + "solvere" (to loosen, solve).  "-ist" is a suffix forming nouns denoting agents or adherents of a system or principle.
