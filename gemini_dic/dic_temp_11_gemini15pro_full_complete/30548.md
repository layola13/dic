cephalopods:/ˈsefələpɒdz/ (US) /ˈkɛfələpɒdz/ (UK)| n. |头足纲动物|Marine mollusks characterized by bilateral body symmetry, a prominent head, and a set of arms or tentacles modified from the primitive molluscan foot.  They include squid, octopuses, cuttlefish, and nautiluses.|例句：Cephalopods are highly intelligent invertebrates. 头足纲动物是高智商的无脊椎动物。

cephalopod:/ˈsefələpɒd/ (US) /ˈkɛfələpɒd/ (UK)| n. |头足纲动物|A single animal belonging to the Cephalopoda class.|例句：The giant squid is a fascinating cephalopod.  大王乌贼是一种迷人的头足纲动物。

例句：This cephalopod uses ink as a defense mechanism.  这种头足纲动物用墨汁作为防御机制。|近义词：cephalopodan|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek  *κεφαλόπους (kephalópous)  meaning “head-foot”,  from  *κεφαλή (kephalḗ)  meaning “head” + *πούς (poús) meaning “foot”.
