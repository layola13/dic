joe-sixpacks:/ˌdʒoʊ sɪksˈpæks/ | n. | 普通人，蓝领工人 |  Refers to the average working-class American male, often stereotyped as enjoying beer (six-packs) and watching sports.  It can be considered slightly derogatory, implying a lack of sophistication.  The term originates from the image of a working man buying a six-pack of beer after a hard day's work. |  He's a joe-sixpack who enjoys watching football on Sundays. 他是一个喜欢在星期天看橄榄球的普通人。| Synonyms: average Joe, working man, blue-collar worker | Antonyms: elite, sophisticated, upper-class | noun
