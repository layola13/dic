evidentiality:/ˌɛvɪˌdɛnʃiˈælɪti/
n.|证据性，显在性|The linguistic marking of the source of evidence for a statement.  The way in which a speaker indicates how they know the information they are conveying. |The verb in the sentence carries evidentiality, showing that the speaker heard the information from someone else.|Synonym: epistemic modality|Antonym: N/A|noun

evidential:/ˌɛvɪˈdɛnʃəl/
adj.|证据的，基于证据的|Relating to or based on evidence.|The evidential markers in the language help listeners understand the source of the speaker's knowledge.|Synonym: probative, evidentiary|Antonym: conjectural, speculative|adjective
