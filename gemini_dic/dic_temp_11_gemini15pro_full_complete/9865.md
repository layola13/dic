apagoge:/əˈpæɡədʒi/ | n. | 反证法，归谬法 |  A method of proof by contradiction, where the falsity of a proposition is demonstrated by showing that its truth would imply the truth of a false or absurd proposition. |  The philosopher used apagoge to prove his point. 这位哲学家使用反证法来证明他的观点。 | Example: By assuming the opposite of what you want to prove and showing that it leads to a contradiction, you can demonstrate the truth of your original proposition through apagoge. 通过假设你想要证明的反面，并表明它会导致矛盾，你可以通过反证法证明你最初的命题的真实性。 | Synonym: reductio ad absurdum, indirect proof | Antonym: direct proof | Noun
