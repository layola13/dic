impetigo:/ɪmˈpɪtiɡoʊ/ /ɪmˈpɪtaɪɡəʊ/
n.|脓疱病|A highly contagious bacterial skin infection characterized by isolated pustules that become crusted and rupture.|The child had impetigo on his face.|Synonyms: infectious impetigo, pyoderma|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a disease.  One could consider "healthy skin" a contrasting state.)|noun


impetigos: /ɪmˈpɪtiɡoʊz/ /ɪmˈpɪtaɪɡəʊz/
n.|脓疱病（复数）|Plural form of impetigo, referring to multiple instances or occurrences of the skin infection.|The doctor diagnosed several cases of impetigos in the school.|Synonyms:  (Plural of synonyms above – infectious impetigoes, pyodermas – though less commonly used)|Antonyms: (Same as singular form - no direct antonym)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *impetīgin-, stem of impetīgo (“a scabby eruption”), from impetīre (“to attack, rush upon”), from in- (“in, on, upon”) + petere (“to seek, aim at, attack”).
