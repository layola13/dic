caulocarpous:/ˌkɔːloʊˈkɑːrpəs/| adj.|茎花植物的|Having flowers and fruits borne on the old wood (trunk or main stems) rather than on young shoots or branches.  This term is used in botany to describe plants that flower and fruit directly from their trunks or main branches, rather than from smaller branches or new growth.|Example: The cacao tree is a caulocarpous species, with its pods emerging directly from the trunk. 可可树是一种茎花植物，其果荚直接从树干上长出来。

Example:  Caulocarpy is a fascinating adaptation observed in certain tropical trees. 茎花是一种在某些热带树木中观察到的迷人适应性。|近义词:ramiflorous (sometimes)|反义词: (No direct antonym, but the opposite concept would be flowering and fruiting on new growth)|词性:adjective
