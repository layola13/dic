courting:/ˈkɔːrtɪŋ/ (US), /ˈkɔːtɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |求爱，追求；招致，试图获得|The process of trying to win the affection of someone with the goal of establishing a romantic relationship.  It involves wooing, attracting, and pursuing a potential partner.|He spent months courting her before she finally agreed to go out with him. 他花了几个月的时间追求她，她才终于答应和他约会。|近义词：wooing, pursuing, romancing|反义词：rejecting, spurning|词性:noun (gerund)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *courten*, from Old French *corteis*, meaning "courteous, polite, refined."  Originally, courting referred to the elaborate rituals and displays of courtesy associated with aristocratic love and marriage.  Over time, the meaning broadened to encompass any form of romantic pursuit.
