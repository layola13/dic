bono:/ˈboʊnoʊ/ (US), /ˈbɒnəʊ/ (UK)| n.|奖金；红利；额外津贴；债券|A sum of money added to a person's wages, salary, or other payment.|He received a $500 bono for his excellent work. 他因工作出色获得了500美元的奖金。|近义词：bonus, premium, perk|反义词：penalty, deduction|noun

bono:/ˈboʊnoʊ/ (US), /ˈbɒnəʊ/ (UK)| n.|债券|A certificate issued by a government or a public company promising to repay borrowed money at a fixed rate of interest at a specified time.|The company issued bonos to raise capital.  这家公司发行债券以筹集资金。|近义词：bond, debenture|反义词： (No direct antonym for a financial instrument like a bond)|noun

bono:/ˈboʊnoʊ/ (US), /ˈbɒnəʊ/ (UK)| adj.|好的；善良的 (Italian origin, often used in music)|Good, kind. (Primarily used in musical contexts)|The song had a bono melody. 这首歌的旋律优美。|近义词：good, fine, pleasant|反义词：bad, unpleasant|adjective
