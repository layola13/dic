obligers:/əˈblaɪdʒərz/ | n. | 债务人，义务人 |  A person who is obligated to do something, especially to pay a debt or perform a service. | The obligers are required to make monthly payments. 债务人必须每月付款。|  debtors, debtees | creditors | noun

obligers:/əˈblaɪdʒərz/ | n. | 施惠者，恩人 | A person who confers a benefit or favor; one who obliges another.  | He felt like an obliger to the people who had helped him. 他感觉像是对帮助过他的人的施惠者。|  benefactors, patrons |  recipients | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *obliger (Modern French obliger), from Latin obligare ("to bind, tie up, fasten; put under obligation"), from ob- ("towards, against") + ligare ("to bind").
