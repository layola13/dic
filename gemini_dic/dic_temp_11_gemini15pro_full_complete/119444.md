microlith:/ˈmaɪkrəlɪθ/ | n. | 微石器 | A small stone tool, typically a blade or flake, characteristic of the Mesolithic period. |  Archaeologists unearthed numerous microliths at the site. 考古学家在遗址中挖掘出大量的微石器。

microlith:/ˈmaɪkrəlɪθ/ | n. | 微晶 | A minute crystal, especially in a glassy matrix. | The rock sample contained various microliths.  岩石样本中包含各种微晶。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek *μικρός (mikrós, “small”) + *λίθος (líthos, “stone”)

SYNONYMS->
(for stone tool meaning): bladelet, flake, chip
(for crystal meaning): crystallite

ANTONYMS->
(for stone tool meaning): megalith (although not a direct opposite, it represents the opposite end of the size spectrum)
(for crystal meaning): macrocrystal (although not a direct opposite, it represents the opposite end of the size spectrum)
