fancy:/ˈfænsi/ | adj. n. v. | 奇特的；昂贵的；精美的；想要；爱好；想象；设想 |  From Anglo-French *fantaisie "fantasy, imagination," or directly from Old French fantasie "illusion, fancy, imagination," from Late Latin phantasia, from Greek phantasia "appearance, image, imagination," from phantazesthai "to make visible, to appear," from phainein "to show, to appear." Originally meaning "imagination, hallucination," the sense of "capricious liking" is from 1590s. As a verb, from 1560s. |  She wore a fancy dress to the party. 她穿了一件华丽的礼服去参加派对。 | Synonyms: elaborate, ornate, extravagant, whimsical, desire, imagine | Antonyms: plain, simple, ordinary, dislike | adjective
