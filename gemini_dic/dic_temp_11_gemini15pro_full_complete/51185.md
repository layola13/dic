destabilizations:/diːˌsteɪbɪləˈzeɪʃənz/| n. |不稳定，动摇；破坏稳定|The political destabilizations in the region are a cause for concern. 该地区政治上的不稳定令人担忧。|例句：The recent destabilizations in the financial markets have led to widespread panic.  最近金融市场的不稳定导致了广泛的恐慌。

The ongoing destabilizations of the ecosystem are threatening biodiversity.  持续的生态系统失衡正在威胁生物多样性。|近义词：instabilities, disruptions, upheavals|反义词:stabilizations, strengthening|词性:名词

destabilization:/diːˌsteɪbɪləˈzeɪʃən/| n. |不稳定，动摇；破坏稳定|The destabilization of the government led to chaos. 政府的不稳定导致了混乱。|例句：The rapid destabilization of the region poses a serious threat to international security. 该区域的迅速动荡对国际安全构成了严重威胁。

The economic destabilization caused by the pandemic has had far-reaching consequences. 由大流行引起的经济动荡造成了深远的影响。|近义词：instability, disruption, upheaval|反义词:stabilization, strengthening|词性:名词


词源:  From destabilize + -ation.  Destabilize comes from de- (meaning "reverse" or "remove") + stabilize (meaning "to make stable"). Stabilize ultimately derives from the Latin word "stabilis" meaning "firm" or "steadfast".
