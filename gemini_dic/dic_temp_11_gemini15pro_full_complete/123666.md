mooching:/ˈmuːtʃɪŋ/| n. vi.|蹭吃蹭喝，闲逛，乞讨|The act of getting something (especially food or money) from someone else without paying for it; loitering or wandering about with no particular purpose. |He's always mooching off his friends for food. 他总是蹭朋友的食物。|Synonyms: scrounging, sponging, cadging, begging, loitering, idling, dawdling|Antonyms: working, earning, providing, contributing|Noun, Verb (gerund or present participle)
