decadentism:/dɪˈkæd(ə)ntɪzəm/| n.|颓废主义；颓废派|A movement in literature and art, at the end of the 19th century, characterized by refined aestheticism, artifice, and the rejection of moral or social values.|The novel captures the spirit of decadentism prevalent in the late 1800s. 这部小说捕捉了19世纪末盛行的颓废主义精神。|Synonyms: degeneration, decline, decay, corruption|Antonyms:  morality, virtue, wholesomeness|noun


decadence:/ˈdɛkədəns/| n.|颓废；衰落|Moral or cultural decline as characterized by excessive indulgence in pleasure or luxury.|The Roman Empire's decadence ultimately led to its downfall. 罗马帝国的颓废最终导致了它的灭亡。|Synonyms: degeneration, decline, decay, corruption|Antonyms:  morality, virtue, wholesomeness|noun


decadent:/ˈdɛkədənt/| adj.|颓废的；衰落的|Characterized by or reflecting a state of moral or cultural decline.|The decadent lifestyle of some celebrities is often criticized. 一些名人的颓废生活方式经常受到批评。|Synonyms: corrupt, degenerate, dissipated, self-indulgent|Antonyms:  moral, virtuous, wholesome|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décadence*, from Late Latin *decadentia*, from *decadēns*, present participle of *decadere* "to decay," from *de-* "down" + *cadere* "to fall."
