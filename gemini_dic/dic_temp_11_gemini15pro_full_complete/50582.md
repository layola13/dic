derailing:/dɪˈreɪlɪŋ/
n.|脱轨，出轨，阻挠|The derailing of the peace talks was a major setback. 和平谈判的破裂是一个重大挫折。|English Explanation:  Causing something to go off track, either literally (like a train) or figuratively (like a plan).|Example: The scandal threatened to derail his campaign.  这桩丑闻可能会破坏他的竞选活动。|Synonyms: obstructing, sabotaging, hindering, disrupting|Antonyms: furthering, advancing, promoting|Noun

derailing:/dɪˈreɪlɪŋ/
adj.|脱轨的，出轨的，阻挠的|The derailing incident caused significant delays. 脱轨事件造成了严重的延误。|English Explanation:  Describing something that causes or relates to a derailment, either literal or figurative.|Example:  His derailing tactics prevented the project from moving forward. 他阻挠的策略阻止了项目的进展。|Synonyms: disruptive, obstructive, hindering|Antonyms: helpful, constructive, supportive|Adjective
