bargepole:/ˈbɑːdʒˌpoʊl/ (UK) /ˈbɑːrdʒˌpoʊl/ (US)| n. |驳船撑杆；（比喻）绝不，绝对不|A long pole used to propel or guide a barge. Figuratively used to express strong aversion or refusal.|I wouldn't touch that with a bargepole. 我绝对不会碰那个东西。|近义词:pole, staff (literal); not at all, under no circumstances (figurative)|反义词: N/A (literal); willingly, gladly (figurative)|词性:名词

bargepoles:/ˈbɑːdʒˌpoʊlz/ (UK) /ˈbɑːrdʒˌpoʊlz/ (US)| n. |驳船撑杆（复数）|Plural form of "bargepole".| We needed two bargepoles to navigate the shallow river. 我们需要两根驳船撑杆才能在浅河中航行。|近义词:poles, staffs|反义词: N/A|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From *barge* + *pole*.  "Barge" comes from Old French "barge," a type of boat. "Pole" comes from Old English "pal," meaning a stake or rod.
