gallant:/ˈɡælənt (US), ˈɡælənt (UK)|
adj.|勇敢的，英勇的；殷勤的，有礼貌的；华丽的，壮观的|
Showing courage; chivalrous and attentive to women; splendid and imposing.
例句：The gallant knight rescued the princess from the dragon. 勇敢的骑士从恶龙手中救出了公主。
例句：He was always gallant and opened doors for her. 他总是很有风度，为她开门。


近义词：brave, courageous, chivalrous, courteous, polite, stately, magnificent
反义词：cowardly, timid, rude, impolite


gallant:/ˈɡælənt (US), ˈɡælənt (UK)|
n.|英勇的人；对女士殷勤的男人|
A brave, chivalrous man; a man who is exceptionally polite and attentive to women.
例句：The gallants of the court competed for the queen's favor. 宫廷里的绅士们争相获得女王的青睐。
例句：He was considered a gallant for his heroic actions. 他因其英勇的行为被认为是一位英雄。


近义词：hero, knight, gentleman, suitor
反义词：coward, villain, cad


gallants:/ˈɡælənts (US), ˈɡælənts (UK)|
n.|（尤指旧时的）衣着华丽的男子；时髦人物|
Fashionably dressed men, especially of former times; stylish people.
例句：The gallants strolled through the park, showing off their fine clothes. 衣着华丽的男子们在公园里漫步，炫耀着他们漂亮的衣服。
例句：The gallants of the era were known for their extravagant tastes. 那个时代的花花公子以其奢侈的品味而闻名。

近义词：dandies, beaux, fops
反义词：slobs


词源：From Old French "galant," present participle of "galer" (to rejoice, make merry), from Frankish "*galan" (to sing), from Proto-Germanic "*galaną" (to sing, enchant).
