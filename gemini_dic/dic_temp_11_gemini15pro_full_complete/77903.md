genii's:/ˈdʒiːnɪaɪz/ |n. |精灵的，天才的（复数）|  Possessive form of "genii," meaning belonging to or associated with genii (plural of genius in its older sense of a spirit or supernatural being). It can also be the plural possessive of the word "genius" in its modern sense, but this usage is less common. |The genii's magic filled the air. 精灵的魔法充满了空气。|Synonyms: spirits', daemons', deities' | Antonyms: mortals', humans' |Noun
genius:/ˈdʒiːniəs/ | n. | 天才，天赋，精灵 | Exceptional intellectual or creative power or other natural ability. Also, in mythology, a spirit, often a guardian spirit, associated with a person, place, or thing. | He has a genius for mathematics. 他有数学天赋。| Synonyms: talent, aptitude, gift, intellect, brilliance, prodigy, mastermind, whiz, adept, expert | Antonyms: inability, incompetence, mediocrity, dullness | Noun
