informed: /ɪnˈfɔːrmd/ | adj. | 知情的；见多识广的 | Having or showing knowledge of a particular subject or situation. |  She made an informed decision after researching all the options. 她研究了所有选项后做出了明智的决定。|  The journalist wrote a well-informed article about the political situation. 这位记者写了一篇关于政治局势的知识渊博的文章。 |  knowledgeable, educated, aware |  ignorant, unaware, uninformed | adjective

Etymology:
From the verb "inform," which comes from the Old French "enformer" and the Latin "informare," meaning "to shape, instruct, educate." The prefix "in-" intensifies the meaning, and "-form" relates to shape or structure, thus "to give shape to the mind" or "to instruct."
