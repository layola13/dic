obscurant:/əbˈskjʊərənt/ |adj. n.| 朦胧的，模糊的；干扰者，掩盖真相者 |Something that obscures; specifically, something designed to prevent observation, as smoke.  Something that makes things unclear or difficult to understand.| The heavy fog acted as an obscurant, hiding the approaching ships. 浓雾遮蔽了驶来的船只，起到了掩盖的作用。 | The politician's speeches were full of obscurant jargon, designed to confuse the public. 这位政客的演讲充满了令人费解的专业术语，旨在迷惑公众。 | 近义词: vague, ambiguous, confusing, evasive, shadowy, nebulous | 反义词: clear, lucid, transparent, straightforward | 词性: adjective, noun

obscurant:/əbˈskjʊərənt/ | n. |干扰者，掩盖真相者 | A person or thing that obscures something.  Someone who deliberately tries to prevent something from becoming known or understood.|  He acted as an obscurant, withholding vital information from the investigators. 他隐瞒了调查人员的重要信息，扮演了一个掩盖真相者的角色。| The committee accused the government of being obscurants, deliberately hiding the truth from the public. 委员会指责政府掩盖真相，故意向公众隐瞒真相。 | 近义词: prevaricator, equivocator, dissembler | 反义词: informant, whistleblower | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin obscurans, present participle of obscurare "to darken," from Latin obscurus "dark, dim, obscure."
