borsholder:/ˈbɔːrzˌhoʊldər/| n.|居民代表；十户长|A person appointed to represent a tithing or similar small district, originally responsible for maintaining order and collecting taxes.|The borsholder was responsible for collecting taxes from the ten families in his tithing.|近义词：headman, constable, tithingman|反义词:|名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *burh-sēalde, from burh "borough" + sēalde "inhabitant."  The -s- represents a genitive singular ending.
