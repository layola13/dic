craven:/ˈkreɪvən/| adj. n.|懦弱的，胆小的；懦夫，胆小鬼|A craven person is extremely cowardly.  A craven act is one done by a coward.|He was denounced as a craven by his former colleagues. 他被他以前的同事谴责为懦夫。|近义词:cowardly, timid, fearful, pusillanimous|反义词:brave, courageous, valiant|词性:形容词, 名词
词源:From Middle English *cravant, from Old French *cravante (“defeated, vanquished”), past participle of cravanter (“to defeat, overcome”), from Vulgar Latin *crepantare (“to break”), from Latin crepare (“to crack, creak”).  The original meaning referred to someone defeated or subdued, later evolving to mean "cowardly."
