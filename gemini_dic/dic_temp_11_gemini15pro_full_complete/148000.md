overrighteousness:/ˌoʊvərˈraɪtʃəsnəs/
n.|过度正义;自以为义;伪善|The state of being excessively righteous, often to the point of hypocrisy or self-righteousness.  It implies a judgmental and often intolerant attitude towards others perceived as less moral.|He displayed a frustrating overrighteousness, constantly criticizing others' flaws while ignoring his own. 他表现出令人沮丧的自以为是，不断批评别人的缺点，却忽视了自己的缺点。|近义词：sanctimoniousness, hypocrisy, pharisaism|反义词：humility, modesty, tolerance|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + righteousness.  "Over-"  is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much."  "Righteousness" comes from the Old English *rihtwīsnes*, meaning "rightness," derived from *riht* (right) and *wīs* (wise).
