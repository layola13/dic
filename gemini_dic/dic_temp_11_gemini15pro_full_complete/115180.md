manichaeism:/ˌmænɪˈkiːɪzəm/ | n. | 摩尼教; 二元论 |A dualistic religious system with Christian, Gnostic, and pagan elements, founded in Persia in the 3rd century by Mani. It emphasized a cosmic struggle between good and evil, light and darkness. |  Manichaeism spread widely throughout the Roman Empire and beyond. 摩尼教广泛传播到罗马帝国及其他地区。

The influence of Manichaeism can be seen in various later religious movements.  摩尼教的影响可以在各种后来的宗教运动中看到。 | 近义词: dualism 二元论 | 反义词: monism 一元论 | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Manichaeismus, from Greek Μανιχαϊσμός (Manikhaïsmós), from Μάνης (Mánēs, “Mani”) + -ισμός (-ismós).  Ultimately from Aramaic ܡܐܢܝ (Mānī).
