haematitic:/ˌhiːməˈtɪtɪk/ | adj. |  血红色的，赤铁矿的 | Relating to, resembling, or containing hematite (a reddish-black iron ore). | The rocks were haematitic, stained a deep rusty red.  这些岩石是赤铁矿的，染成了深锈红色。 | The soil had a distinctive haematitic hue. 土壤有一种独特的赤铁矿色调。 | Synonyms: ferruginous, iron-rich, rusty, reddish-black | Antonyms: non-ferruginous | adjective

Etymology->
From Greek *haimatitis* (blood-like) from *haima* (blood) + *-itēs* (suffix forming adjectives).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *seyh₂-* (to pour).
