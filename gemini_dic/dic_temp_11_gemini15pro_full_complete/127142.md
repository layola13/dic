nattering:/ˈnætərɪŋ/| adj. |喋喋不休的，唠叨的|Characterized by trivial or gossipy chatter, often in a complaining or critical way.  |Example:  The nattering of the children in the backseat made it difficult to concentrate. 车后座孩子们喋喋不休的说话声让我很难集中注意力。|Example:  My aunt's nattering about the neighbors went on for hours. 我阿姨对邻居的唠叨持续了几个小时。|近义词：chatty, gossipy, chattering, rambling|反义词:quiet, silent|词性:adjective

natter:/ˈnætər/| vi. |闲聊，唠叨|To talk incessantly about unimportant things, often in a complaining or critical way.  |Example:  They nattered away for hours about nothing in particular. 他们闲聊了几个小时，也没聊什么特别的事。|Example:  He was nattering on about the price of petrol again. 他又在唠叨汽油价格了。|近义词：chat, gossip, chatter, ramble|反义词:be quiet|词性:verb

natter:/ˈnætər/| n. |闲聊，唠叨|Trivial or gossipy conversation, often complaining or critical. |Example:  The constant natter from the next room was distracting. 隔壁房间不断的闲聊声让人分心。|Example:  I couldn't hear the speaker over the natter of the crowd. 我听不到演讲者的声音，因为人群太吵闹了。|近义词：chat, gossip, chatter, rambling|反义词:silence|词性:noun
