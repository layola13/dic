abstractly: /æbˈstræktli/ | adv. | 抽象地；理论上；概括地 |  In a way that is considered apart from concrete realities, specific objects, or actual instances.  |  He thought about the problem abstractly, without considering the specific details. 他抽象地思考这个问题，没有考虑具体的细节。 |  theoretically, conceptually, generally | concretely, specifically | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *abstractē, from Latin abstractus (past participle of abstrahō, "I draw away"), from abs- ("from, away from") + trahō ("I draw, pull").
