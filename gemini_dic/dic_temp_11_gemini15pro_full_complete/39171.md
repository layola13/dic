congealablenesses:/kənˌdʒiːləˈbɛlnəsɪz/ | n. | 可凝固性；凝固能力 |  The congealablenesses of different oils vary greatly depending on their fatty acid composition.  | The congealablenesses of various substances were tested in the lab. 不同物质的可凝固性在实验室进行了测试。 |  coagulability, solidifiability | fluidity, liquidity | noun

Etymology:
From congealable + -ness.  Congealable comes from the verb "congeal," from Old French *congealer, from Latin congelare "to freeze, stiffen, thicken," from com- "together" + gelare "to freeze."  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
