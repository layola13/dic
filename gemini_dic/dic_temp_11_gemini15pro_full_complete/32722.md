chlorite:/ˈklɔːraɪt/ /ˈklɔrˌaɪt/ | n. |亚氯酸盐; 绿泥石 |A salt of chlorous acid. A dark green mineral of the mica group, consisting of hydrous silicates of aluminium, magnesium, and iron.  It is formed by the alteration of igneous rocks such as basalt. |The chlorite in the soil gives it a greenish tinge. 土壤中的绿泥石使其略带绿色。|Synonyms: berthierine, penninite (for the mineral) |Antonyms: None (specific antonyms are not applicable to chemical compounds or minerals) | noun


chlorite:/ˈklɔːraɪt/ /ˈklɔrˌaɪt/ | adj. |绿泥石的 |Containing or resembling chlorite. |The chlorite schist contained garnets. 绿泥石片岩中含有石榴石。|Synonyms: chloritic |Antonyms: None | adjective
