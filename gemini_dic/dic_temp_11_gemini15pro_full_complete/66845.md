expatriatism:/ˌɛksˈpeɪtriətɪzəm/ | n. |侨居国外; 移居国外 | The state or condition of living in a foreign country. |  Many writers explored themes of expatriatism in their works, reflecting their experiences living abroad. 许多作家在他们的作品中探讨了侨居国外(异乡情结)的主题，反映了他们在国外生活的经历。

| Expatriatism can be a challenging but rewarding experience. 移居国外可能是一项具有挑战性但回报丰厚的经历。 | 近义词： emigration, exile, migration | 反义词： repatriation, return | 词性: noun


expatriate:/ɛksˈpeɪtriət/ | n., adj., v. |移居国外者; 外侨 |  A person who lives outside their native country. (n.)  / Relating to or characteristic of expatriates. (adj.) / To banish (someone) from their native country. (v.) | She worked as an expatriate in Hong Kong for several years. 她在香港做了几年的外籍人士。

|  The expatriate community in the city is quite large.  这个城市的外籍人士群体相当庞大。

| The government expatriated the dissidents. 政府将持不同政见者驱逐出境。 | 近义词：(n.) emigrant, exile, migrant; (v.) banish, deport, exile | 反义词：(n.) native, resident; (v.) repatriate | 词性: noun, adjective, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin expatriatus, past participle of expatriare "to banish," from ex- "out of" + patria "native country," from pater "father."
