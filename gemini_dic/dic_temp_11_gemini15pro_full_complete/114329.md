maistre:/ˈmeɪstər/ (Archaic) | n. | Master, teacher, expert | A skilled craftsman or artist, a person of authority or expertise.  From Old French "maistre", derived from Latin "magister" (master, teacher).  No longer commonly used in modern English, replaced by "master". |  Example: The maistre of the guild oversaw the training of apprentices.  行业协会的师傅监督学徒的训练。| Synonym: master, expert, teacher, chief, leader | Antonym: apprentice, novice, student | noun
