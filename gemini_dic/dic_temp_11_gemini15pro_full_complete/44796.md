crunk: /krʌŋk/ | adj. | 兴奋的；狂热的；喝醉的 |  Energetic and excited, especially due to the influence of alcohol or drugs;  full of energy and enthusiasm, especially in the context of a party or hip-hop music. |  The club was crunk last night. 昨晚俱乐部气氛很high。 | Synonyms: hyped, pumped, lit, turnt | Antonyms: calm, mellow, subdued | adjective


crunk: /krʌŋk/ | n. |  Crunk music (a subgenre of hip-hop) | A style of Southern hip-hop music characterized by its heavy bass and call-and-response vocals. | That DJ plays the best crunk.  那位DJ播放最好的Crunk音乐。 | Synonyms: trap, hip-hop | Antonyms: classical, folk | noun

词源:  Crunk likely originated as a combination of "crazy" and "drunk," although some sources suggest it comes from the past participle of "crank" (meaning to start or enliven). It gained popularity in the Southern United States hip-hop scene in the 1990s.
