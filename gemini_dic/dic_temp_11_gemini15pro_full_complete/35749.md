cochleae:/ˈkɒkliːiː/| n. |耳蜗（复数）|The plural form of "cochlea," referring to the spiral cavity of the inner ear that produces nerve impulses in response to sound vibrations.|The cochleae of both ears were damaged, resulting in profound hearing loss. 双耳的耳蜗都受损了，导致严重的听力损失。|近义词: (singular) cochlea|反义词: None|词性:Noun

cochlea:/ˈkɒkliə/| n. |耳蜗|The spiral cavity of the inner ear containing the organ of Corti, which produces nerve impulses in response to sound vibrations.|Damage to the cochlea can cause hearing loss. 耳蜗损伤会导致听力损失。|近义词: inner ear (general area)|反义词: None|词性:Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cochlea*, from Ancient Greek κοχλίας (*kokhlíās*) "snail, screw, spiral," because of its coiled shape.
