distressednesses:/dɪˈstrɛsɪdnɪsɪz/
n.
极度痛苦；极度悲伤
The state of being extremely distressed; multiple instances of distress.
The refugees displayed overwhelming distressednesses from the trauma they had endured. 难民们表现出了他们所经历的创伤带来的极度痛苦。
The therapist specialized in alleviating the distressednesses of her patients.  这位治疗师擅长减轻病人的痛苦。
近义词: agonies, miseries, sufferings
反义词: comforts, joys, happinesses
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From distressed + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing a state or condition).  Distressed itself comes from distress + -ed. Distress ultimately derives from the Old French destresse, meaning "constraint, oppression, hardship," from Vulgar Latin *districtia, from Latin distringere "to draw tight, stretch apart, distract," from dis- "apart" + stringere "to draw tight."
