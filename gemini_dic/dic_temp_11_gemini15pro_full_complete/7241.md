anglo-catholic:/ˈæŋɡləʊˈkæθəlɪk/
| adj. n.|英国天主教的；英国天主教徒|A person or church observing a form of Anglicanism that incorporates some elements of Roman Catholic ritual and belief.|例句：He identified himself as an Anglo-Catholic. 他自认为是英国天主教徒。|例句：The Anglo-Catholic movement gained momentum in the 19th century. 英国天主教运动在19世纪获得了发展势头。|近义词：High Church Anglican, |反义词：Low Church Anglican, Evangelical Anglican|词性：形容词，名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From *Anglo-* (meaning English) + *Catholic*.  *Anglo-* ultimately derives from the Latin word *Anglus*, referring to the Angles, one of the Germanic peoples who settled in Britain. *Catholic* comes from the Greek word *katholikos*, meaning "universal".
