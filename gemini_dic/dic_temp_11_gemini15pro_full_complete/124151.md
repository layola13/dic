mortiser:/ˈmɔːrtaɪzər/ (US) /ˈmɔːtɪsər/ (UK)| n.|榫眼机，凿榫机|A machine tool used for cutting mortises (rectangular holes) in wood or other materials, typically for joining pieces together using tenons.|He used a mortiser to create precise mortises for the chair legs.|Synonyms: hollow chisel mortiser, chain mortiser, benchtop mortiser|Antonyms: N/A|noun

词源： From mortise + -er.  Mortise itself comes from Old French *mortaise, from mort "dead," from Latin mortuus, past participle of morī "to die."  The connection likely refers to the way a tenon fits tightly into a mortise, as if “dead” or immobile.
