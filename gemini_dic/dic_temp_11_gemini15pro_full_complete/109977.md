libertarians:/ˌlɪbərˈtɛəriənz/
|n.|自由意志主义者；自由论者
|People who believe in libertarianism, a political philosophy that emphasizes individual liberty and minimal government intervention.|
|Libertarians advocate for lower taxes and less government regulation. 自由意志主义者主张降低税收和减少政府监管。|
|Many libertarians believe in the right to own guns. 许多自由意志主义者相信拥有枪支的权利。|
|synonyms: individualists, liberals (in the classical sense)|
|antonyms: authoritarians, statists|
|noun|

libertarian:/ˌlɪbərˈtɛəriən/
|adj.|自由意志主义的；自由论的
|Relating to or characteristic of libertarianism.|
|He holds a libertarian view on drug legalization. 他对毒品合法化持自由意志主义的观点。|
|The libertarian party advocates for minimal government intervention. 自由党主张政府尽量少干预。|
|synonyms: individualistic, liberal (in the classical sense)|
|antonyms: authoritarian, statist|
|adjective|

libertarianism:/ˌlɪbərˈtɛəriənɪzəm/
|n.|自由意志主义；自由论
|A political philosophy that emphasizes individual liberty and minimal government intervention, especially in economic and social matters.|
|Libertarianism is a growing political ideology. 自由意志主义是一种日益增长的政治意识形态。|
|The core principle of libertarianism is individual freedom. 自由意志主义的核心原则是个人自由。|
|synonyms: individualism, liberalism (in the classical sense)|
|antonyms: authoritarianism, statism|
|noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *libertaire*, from Latin *liber* (free) + *-tarian* (a suffix denoting advocacy of a particular doctrine or system).
