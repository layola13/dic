hocus-pocus:/ˌhoʊkəsˈpəʊkəs/ |adj. n.| 花花把戏，骗人的把戏；胡言乱语；变戏法；|A set of words used in magic or trickery, often meaningless and intended to deceive or create a sense of mystery.  It can also refer to deceptive or misleading talk or behavior. |The magician uttered some hocus-pocus and pulled a rabbit out of the hat. 魔术师念了几句咒语，就从帽子里变出一只兔子。 |Synonyms: trickery, sleight of hand, deception, chicanery, mumbo jumbo, abracadabra | Antonyms: honesty, truth, reality, straightforwardness| adjective, noun
