chromolithographer:/ˌkroʊməlɪˈθɑːɡrəfər/
n.
彩色石版画印刷工人
A person who produces chromolithographs, a method of printing color images using multiple lithographic stones, each for a different color.
The skilled chromolithographer carefully aligned each stone to create a vibrant and detailed image.  这位熟练的彩色石版画印刷工人仔细地对齐每一块石头，以创造出一幅充满活力和细节的图像。
Chromolithographers were highly sought after in the 19th century for their ability to produce colorful illustrations.  19世纪，彩色石版画印刷工人因其制作彩色插图的能力而备受追捧。
