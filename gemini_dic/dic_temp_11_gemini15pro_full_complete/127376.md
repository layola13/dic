neanderthal:/niˈændərˌtɑːl, neɪˈændərˌtɑːl/| n. adj.|尼安德特人，尼安德特人的|A species of archaic humans that lived in Eurasia until about 40,000 years ago.  An extinct species of human that lived in ice-age Europe. Also used to describe someone as primitive or unintelligent.|例句：Neanderthals were skilled hunters. 尼安德特人是熟练的猎人。|例句：He called me a Neanderthal for not knowing how to use the new software. 他因为我不知道如何使用新软件而叫我尼安德特人。|近义词：caveman, hominid|反义词:modern human|词性:名词，形容词

{neand} itself is not a valid English word.  The closest and intended word is likely **Neanderthal**.
