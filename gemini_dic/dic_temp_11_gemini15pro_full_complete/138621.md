nuclei:/ˈnjuːkliˌaɪ/| n. |原子核; 细胞核; 核心; 中心|The plural of nucleus.  The central and most important part of an object, movement, or group, forming the basis for its activity and growth.|The nuclei of the atoms are positively charged. 原子核带正电。|Synonyms: centers, cores, hearts, kernels|Antonyms: peripheries, edges, exteriors|noun

nuclei:/ˈnjuːkliˌaɪ/| n. |(生物)细胞核|The membrane-bound organelle within a eukaryotic cell that contains the chromosomes and controls the structure and function of the cell.|The nuclei of these cells are unusually large. 这些细胞的细胞核异常大。|Synonyms: cell nucleus (technically the same, not a true synonym)|Antonyms: cytoplasm|noun
