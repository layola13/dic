joiner:/ˈdʒɔɪnər/ | n. |  连接者; 细木工人 | A person or thing that joins. Someone whose job is making and fitting wooden parts of buildings, especially doors, windows, and stairs. | The joiner is busy fitting the new kitchen cabinets.  细木工正忙着安装新的橱柜。| synonyms: carpenter, woodworker | antonyms: separator, divider | noun


joiner:/ˈdʒɔɪnər/ | n. |  连接物;  接合工具 | Something that joins or connects things. A tool or device used for joining things. |  This special joiner creates a strong bond between the two pieces of wood.  这种特殊的连接器在两块木头之间形成了牢固的连接。| synonyms: connector, coupler, fastener | antonyms: separator, divider | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *joynere, from Anglo-Norman joignour, from Old French joigneor, from joign- (“joining”), present participle stem of joindre (“to join”), from Latin jungere.
