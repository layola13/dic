gowd:/ɡoʊld/| n.|黄金；金子|A precious yellow metal, highly valued for its beauty, resistance to corrosion, and malleability.  It is used in jewelry, currency, and electronics.|She wore a beautiful gowd necklace. 她戴着一条漂亮的黄金项链。|Synonyms: gold, bullion, |Antonyms: dross,  worthlessness|noun


Etymology:
From Middle English *gowd*, from Old English *gold*, from Proto-Germanic *gulþą*, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰelh₂- (“yellow, green, bright”). Cognate with Scots gowd, West Frisian goud, Dutch goud, German Gold, Danish guld, Swedish guld. More at gold.  The spelling "gowd" reflects a Scots or Northern English dialect pronunciation.
