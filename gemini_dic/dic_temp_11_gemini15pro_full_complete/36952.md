colourism: /ˈkʌlərɪzəm/ (US) /ˈkʌlərɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |肤色歧视；颜色主义|Prejudice or discrimination against individuals with a dark skin tone, typically among people of the same ethnic or racial group.|Example: Colourism affects many communities of color, where lighter skin is often seen as more desirable.  肤色歧视影响着许多有色人种社区，在这些社区，肤色较浅通常被认为更受欢迎。

Example:  She faced colourism within her own family, being treated differently from her lighter-skinned siblings.  她在自己的家庭中面临着肤色歧视，受到的待遇与她肤色较浅的兄弟姐妹不同。|近义词：discrimination, prejudice, bigotry, racism |反义词: equality, acceptance, inclusion|词性: noun


Etymology:  A blend of "color" + "-ism" (a suffix forming nouns meaning a discriminatory practice or ideology). The term gained prominence in the late 20th century.
