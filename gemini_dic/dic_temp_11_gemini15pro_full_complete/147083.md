overliberalizing: /ˌoʊvərˈlɪbərəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
verb
过度自由化
The act of liberalizing something, such as an economy or a political system, to an excessive degree.  It implies that the deregulation or relaxation of restrictions has gone too far, often leading to negative consequences.
The government was criticized for overliberalizing the banking sector, which contributed to the financial crisis. 政府因过度放开银行业而受到批评，这导致了金融危机。
The overliberalizing of the market led to a surge in imports and damaged domestic industries. 市场过度自由化导致进口激增，损害了国内产业。
Synonyms: deregulating excessively, loosening restrictions too much
Antonyms: tightening regulations, restricting, controlling
