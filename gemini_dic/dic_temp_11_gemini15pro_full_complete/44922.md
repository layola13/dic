cryptoclimates:/ˈkrɪptoʊˌklaɪməts/ | n. | 密码气候，加密气候 |  The hidden or concealed influences of cryptography and data security on various aspects of society, technology, or the environment.  It can refer to the overall atmosphere or conditions created by the prevalence of encryption and its related technologies, including its impact on privacy, surveillance, law enforcement, and international relations. |  The increasing use of end-to-end encryption has created a complex cryptoclimate, making it difficult for law enforcement to access crucial data. 加密技术的日益使用创造了一个复杂的密码气候，使执法部门难以获取关键数据。
