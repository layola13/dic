back-handed:/ˌbækˈhændɪd/ | adj. | 间接的，反讽的，虚情假意的；(网球等)反手的 | Something that is backhanded appears to be a compliment or a friendly gesture but is actually an insult or criticism. | He gave a backhanded compliment about her outfit, saying it was "interesting." 他对她的服装给了一个虚情假意的恭维，说它“很有趣”。 |Synonyms: sarcastic, ironic, insincere, left-handed (for sports), ambiguous | Antonyms: sincere, genuine, straightforward, direct, right-handed (for sports)|adjective


backhand:/ˈbæk.hænd/ | n. | 反手击球 | A stroke played with the back of the hand facing the direction of the stroke. | His backhand is much weaker than his forehand. 他的反手比正手弱很多。 |Synonyms: backhand stroke | Antonyms: forehand|noun

backhand:/ˈbæk.hænd/ | vt. | 反手击球 | To hit (a ball) with the back of the hand facing the direction of the stroke. | He backhanded the ball across the net. 他反手将球击过网。|Synonyms: hit backhand | Antonyms: hit forehand|verb


Etymology:
From "back" + "hand," referring to the position of the hand in striking a ball. Figurative sense of indirect or insincere developed later.
