incapacitating:/ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪteɪtɪŋ/
adjective
使无能力的；使丧失能力的
Making someone unable to work or do things normally.
The injury was incapacitating, preventing him from walking for months.  这伤让他几个月都不能行走，使他丧失了行动能力。
He suffered an incapacitating back injury. 他背部受伤，丧失了行动能力。
近义词: disabling, crippling, debilitating
反义词: enabling, empowering
词性: adjective

incapacitate:/ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪteɪt/
verb
使无能力；使丧失能力
To deprive of strength or ability; to make legally incapable or ineligible.
The accident incapacitated him from working.  这起事故使他无法工作。
Her illness incapacitated her for several weeks. 她的病使她几周都不能工作。
近义词: disable, cripple, debilitate
反义词: enable, empower
词性: verb

词源: From Late Latin incapacitatus, past participle of incapacitare “to make unfit,” from in- “not” + capacitatus, past participle of capacitare “to make capable,” from Latin capax “capable.”
