enfranchisements:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənts/
|n.|授予公民权；解放；赋予特权|The act of enfranchising, or the state of being enfranchised.  The giving of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.|
例句：The 19th Amendment guaranteed the enfranchisement of women in the United States. 美国宪法第十九修正案保障了美国妇女的选举权。

例句：The enfranchisements of the serfs marked a significant step towards a more equitable society.  农奴的解放标志着向更公平社会迈出了重要一步。
|近义词：empowerment, liberation, emancipation|
|反义词：disenfranchisement, oppression, subjugation|
|词性：名词|

Since *enfranchisement* is a noun, it doesn't typically form phrases in the same way verbs or adjectives do.  It's more common to see it used within phrases describing the *process* or *result* of enfranchisement.  For example:

* **the enfranchisement of women:** 妇女的选举权
* **struggle for enfranchisement:** 为争取选举权而斗争
* **universal enfranchisement:** 普选权
* **gaining enfranchisement:** 获得选举权
* **denying enfranchisement:** 剥夺选举权
