esoterics: /ˌɛs.əˈtɛr.ɪks/ | n. | 秘传的事物；奥秘；难懂的事物 | Relating to or understood by only a small number of people with specialized knowledge or interest. |  Example: He was drawn to the esoterics of ancient religions. 他被古代宗教的奥秘所吸引。 | Example: She delved into the esoterics of quantum physics. 她钻研量子物理学的深奥之处。 | 近义词: mysteries, arcana, secrets | 反义词: exoterics, common knowledge | 词性: noun

esoteric: /ˌɛs.əˈtɛr.ɪk/ | adj. | 秘传的；深奥的；难懂的 | Intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people with specialized knowledge or interest. | Example: The professor's lectures were filled with esoteric allusions. 教授的讲座充满了深奥的典故。 | Example:  The group explored esoteric philosophical concepts.  这个小组探讨了深奥的哲学概念。| 近义词: abstruse, arcane, obscure | 反义词: exoteric, common, accessible | 词性: adjective
