overexacting:/ˌoʊvərɪɡˈzæktɪŋ/ | adj. | 过于苛求的，过于严格的 |  Making excessive demands; requiring more than is reasonable or necessary.  | He was an overexacting boss who expected perfection from his employees. 他是一位过于苛求的老板，期望他的员工做到完美。| demanding, fastidious, rigorous, stringent | lenient, tolerant, undemanding, easygoing | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + exacting.  "Over-" signifies "excessively" or "too much." "Exacting" comes from the verb "exact," which means "to demand and obtain something from someone."
