desolater:/ˈdesəleɪtər/| n.|荒芜者，使孤寂的人|Someone or something that causes a place or a person to become desolate.  |The war was a desolater of the countryside, leaving behind only ruins and sorrow. 战争使乡村荒芜，只留下废墟和悲伤。

The prolonged drought proved to be a desolater of the once fertile farmland. 长时间的干旱使曾经肥沃的农田变得荒芜。|近义词：devastor, ravager, destroyer|反义词: creator, builder, restorer|词性:名词


desolate:/ˈdesələt/| adj.|荒凉的，孤寂的|Barren or laid waste; deserted; lonely; forlorn. |The desolate landscape stretched for miles, unbroken by any sign of life. 荒凉的景色绵延数英里，没有任何生命的迹象。

She felt desolate and abandoned after her family moved away.  在她家人搬走后，她感到孤寂和被遗弃。|近义词：barren, deserted, bleak, lonely|反义词: fertile, populated, cheerful, happy|词性:形容词


词源: From Late Latin *desolatus, past participle of desolare (“to abandon, forsake, lay waste”), from Latin de- + solus (“alone”).
