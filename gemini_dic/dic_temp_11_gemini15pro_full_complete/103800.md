jovialist:/ˈdʒoʊviəlɪst/
noun
快乐主义者，乐观主义者
A person who is jovial and promotes joviality.
He was a true jovialist, always finding the bright side of any situation. 他是一个真正的快乐主义者，总能在任何情况下找到光明的一面。
Synonyms: optimist, cheerful person, life of the party
Antonyms: pessimist, cynic, grump
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From jovial + -ist.  Jovial comes from Late Middle English, from Old French jovif, from Late Latin Iovialis "of Jupiter," from Latin Iovem, accusative of Iuppiter "Jupiter."  Jupiter was considered a source of happiness and good cheer. The suffix "-ist" forms nouns denoting a person who practices or is skilled in something, or a follower of a doctrine or system.
