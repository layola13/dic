huts:/hʌts/| n. |小屋，棚屋|A small, simple dwelling, often made of wood or other natural materials.  A group of simple shelters.|The hikers found refuge in the mountain huts.  徒步旅行者在山间小屋里找到了避难所。|Synonyms: cabins, shacks, sheds|Antonyms: mansions, palaces|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hytt* (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *hudjō* (also source of Old Norse *hytja* "hut"), perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *kud-/*ked- "to cover, conceal".
