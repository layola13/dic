Appalachia: /ˌæpəˈlætʃə/ | n. | 阿巴拉契亚 | A cultural region in the Eastern United States that stretches from the Southern Tier of New York to northern Alabama and Georgia. |  Example: Coal mining has long been a major industry in Appalachia. 阿巴拉契亚地区的煤矿开采长期以来一直是一项主要产业。 | Example:  The Appalachian Mountains are known for their stunning natural beauty. 阿巴拉契亚山脉以其令人惊叹的自然美景而闻名。 | Synonym:  Appalachian Mountains, the Appalachians | Antonym:  N/A | Noun

Etymology: From the Apalachee, a Native American tribe.
