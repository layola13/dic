baroques:/bəˈrəʊks/ | adj. n. | 巴洛克式的；巴洛克风格的（作品）；| Relating to or characteristic of the Baroque style of art, architecture, and music, which flourished in Europe from the early 17th to the mid-18th century, emphasizing dramatic, often strained effects, dynamism, and elaborate ornamentation.  Also, a plural noun referring to works of art, architecture, or music in the Baroque style. | The palace is full of baroques decorations.  这座宫殿充满了巴洛克式的装饰。| The museum has a large collection of baroques. 这家博物馆收藏了大量的巴洛克风格作品。 | 近义词：ornate, elaborate, flamboyant | 反义词：simple, austere, minimalist | 词性: adjective, noun


Etymology: From French *baroque* "irregularly shaped pearl," from Portuguese *barroco*, from uncertain origin.  The term was initially used derisively to describe the perceived excesses and irregularities of the Baroque style.
