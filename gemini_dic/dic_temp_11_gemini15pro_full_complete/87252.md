heriot:/ˈhɛriət/| n. |遗产；死后交付领主的财产；|A heriot was originally an item of property, such as the best beast or piece of armour, that reverted to a feudal lord on the death of a tenant.|Upon the death of the tenant, the landlord was entitled to the best beast as a heriot.|Synonyms: tribute, due, levy|Antonyms: N/A (Heriot represents a payment or obligation, thus lacks a direct antonym.)|noun
