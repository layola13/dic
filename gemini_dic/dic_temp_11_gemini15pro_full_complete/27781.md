cannibalize:/ˈkænɪbəlaɪz/| v. |蚕食，内部竞争|To reduce the sales of one product by introducing a new product that fills a similar need, or to take parts from one machine to repair another.|The company's new phone is cannibalizing sales of its older models. 这家公司的新手机正在蚕食其旧型号的销量。|Example:  The introduction of the new sports car cannibalized sales of the company's sedan. 引进新款跑车蚕食了该公司轿车的销量。

Example:  He cannibalized parts from his old computer to build a new one. 他从旧电脑上拆下零件组装了一台新电脑。|Synonyms: undermine, erode, eat into|Antonyms: boost, enhance, support|verb

cannibalizing:/ˈkænɪbəlaɪzɪŋ/| n. |蚕食，内部竞争 (名词形式，较少使用)|The act of reducing the sales of one product by introducing a new product, or taking parts from one machine to repair another.|The cannibalizing of the older product line was expected but still significant. 旧产品线的蚕食在意料之中，但仍然很严重。|Example: The cannibalizing of the existing customer base by the new service is a concern. 新服务对现有客户群的蚕食令人担忧。

Example: Constant cannibalizing of old equipment led to a shortage of spare parts. 不断地从旧设备上拆卸零件导致了备件短缺。|Synonyms: undermining, erosion|Antonyms: boosting, enhancing|noun/gerund
