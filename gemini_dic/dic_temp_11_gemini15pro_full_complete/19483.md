biddies:/ˈbɪdiz/| n. |老妇人（尤指爱管闲事者），小鸡（尤指母鸡）|A group of older women, often used informally or dismissively, especially those perceived as gossipy or meddling. Also, young chickens, especially female ones. |The biddies at the bingo hall were gossiping about the new neighbors.|Synonyms: old ladies, hens, gossips (for people); chicks, pullets (for chickens)|Antonyms: youngsters, young men (for people); roosters, cocks (for chickens)|noun

词源：biddy的起源尚不完全确定，但据信它可能源自中古英语单词“bride”或“bid”，意思是“祈祷”或“邀请”。这可能与年轻女性在婚礼或其他社交场合的角色有关。后来，这个词的含义演变为指代年轻的女性，然后是年长的女性，通常带有贬义。
