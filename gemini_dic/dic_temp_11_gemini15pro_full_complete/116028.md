marsupials:/mɑːrˈsuːpiəlz/ | n. | 有袋动物 | Mammals whose females have a pouch (called a marsupium) containing the teats where their young are carried and suckled. | Example 1: Kangaroos and koalas are well-known marsupials.  袋鼠和考拉是著名的有袋动物。

Example 2: Marsupials are found mainly in Australia and the Americas.  有袋动物主要分布在澳大利亚和美洲。| Synonym:  pouched mammals | Antonym: placental mammals | Noun
