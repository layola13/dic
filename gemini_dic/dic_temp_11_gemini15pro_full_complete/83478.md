hack-up:/ˈhæk ʌp/ (US), /ˈhæk ʌp/ (UK)| n. | 咳出物；(代码)粗制滥造的东西 | something that is coughed up from the lungs; (informal) a piece of computer code or software that has been written quickly and carelessly |  He had a nasty hack-up after his run. 他跑步后咳出了一些难看的痰.  The website was a real hack-up – full of bugs and security flaws.  这个网站真是粗制滥造——充满了漏洞和安全缺陷。 | 近义词: cough up (v.),  spit up (v.), botch (n.), mess (n.) (for code) | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun


hack-up:/ˈhæk ʌp/ (US), /ˈhæk ʌp/ (UK)| v. | 咳出；粗制滥造(代码) | to cough something up from the lungs; (informal) to produce (computer code or software) quickly and carelessly | He was hacking up phlegm all night. 他整夜都在咳痰.  They hacked up a prototype in a few hours.  他们在几个小时内草草做出了一个原型。 | 近义词: cough up (v.),  spit up (v.), cobble together (v.),  throw together (v.) (for code)| 反义词: N/A | 词性: verb

词源:  "Hack" originally meant to cut or chop roughly.  The "up" intensifies the action.  The meaning related to coughing is literal, while the meaning related to code is figurative, implying something roughly assembled or thrown together.
