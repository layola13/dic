machicolation:/məˌtʃɪkəˈleɪʃən/
|n.|堞口，射孔，杀人孔|An opening between the corbels supporting a projecting parapet or the floor of a gallery, through which stones or other missiles could be dropped on attackers below.|The castle's machicolations allowed defenders to rain down boiling oil on the enemy.|Near synonyms: embrasure, crenel, loophole|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "solid wall")|Noun

词源: From Old French *machecoler "to pour molten matter on," from mache "hammer, mallet" (from Latin *matteuca, of uncertain origin) + coler "to strain, filter" (from Latin colare).  The original meaning referred to pouring boiling oil or molten lead through the openings.
