half-creole:/ˌhæf ˈkriːoʊl/
noun
半克里奥尔语
A language that shares some characteristics with creole languages, but retains more features of its parent languages.  It represents an intermediate stage in the development of a creole.
The language spoken on this island is considered a half-creole, showing influences from both English and the indigenous language.

(No clear antonym exists as it's a descriptive linguistic term)
