egocentrisms: /ˌiːɡoʊˈsɛntrɪzəmz/ | n. | 自我中心主义；利己主义 | The tendency to view the world from one's own perspective and to believe that one's own needs and interests are more important than those of others.  It manifests in an inability or unwillingness to understand other people's viewpoints. |  Her egocentrisms made it difficult for her to work in a team. 她的自我中心主义使她难以在团队中工作。

His constant egocentrisms alienated his friends. 他持续的自我中心让他疏远了朋友。

| 近义词：self-centeredness, narcissism, egoism | 反义词：altruism, selflessness | 词性: noun (plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From ego + center + -ism.  "Ego" comes from the Latin word for "I". "Center" derives from the Greek word "kentron" meaning "sharp point" or "center".  "-ism" is a suffix used to form nouns denoting doctrines, beliefs, or practices.
