griever:/ˈɡriːvər/| n. |悲痛者，哀悼者|A person who is grieving; someone who is deeply saddened by a loss, typically a death.|She was a griever, still raw from the recent loss of her husband.|近义词：mourner, bereaved, sorrowful person|反义词:rejoicer, celebrant|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From grieve (v.) + -er (suffix forming agent nouns).  The verb "grieve" comes from the Old French "grever" meaning "to burden, oppress, harm," ultimately from the Latin "gravare" meaning "to burden."
