machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | adj. n.| 马基雅维利主义的；权谋的；不择手段的；马基雅维利主义者|Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or advancing one's career.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian political philosopher whose book "The Prince" described how to acquire and maintain political power, often by deceitful means. |  He employed machiavellian tactics to get ahead in the company. 他在公司里运用马基雅维利式策略以获得晋升。|Synonyms: cunning, devious, manipulative, unscrupulous, artful, wily | Antonyms: honest, ethical, principled, straightforward, scrupulous | adjective

machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | n. | 马基雅维利主义者；权谋家|A person who uses machiavellian tactics. | He's a real machiavellian, always plotting and scheming. 他是一个真正的马基雅维利主义者，总是阴谋诡计。|Synonyms: manipulator, intriguer, schemer, plotter | Antonyms: moralist, idealist | noun
