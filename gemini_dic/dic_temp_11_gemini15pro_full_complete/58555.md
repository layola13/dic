duked:/djuːkt/ | v. | 躲避，推诿，假装 | to evade or avoid (a responsibility, duty, etc.) by trickery or deception | He duked his responsibilities by pretending to be sick. 他装病逃避责任。|近义词：evade, dodge, shirk, avoid |反义词: confront, face, meet |词性:动词

词源:  Likely a back-formation from "duke out," meaning to fight or settle something through physical confrontation, which in turn probably originated from "duke it out" referencing the fists as "dukes."  The sense of avoiding something likely evolved from the idea of dodging blows in a fight.
