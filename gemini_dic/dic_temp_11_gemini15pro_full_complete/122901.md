molossine:/ˈmɒləˌsaɪn/ /ˈmoʊləˌsaɪn/| adj. n.|摩洛斯式的，摩洛斯音步的；摩洛斯音步|A metrical foot consisting of three long syllables, as in “downright”.  A line or verse made up primarily of such feet.  Relating to, or consisting of molossians.|The poet skillfully used molossine rhythm to create a sense of gravity. 诗人巧妙地运用摩洛斯节奏来营造一种庄严感。|Near Synonyms: trisyllabic, triple;  Noun near synonym: molossus |Antonyms: (For the noun form - considering metrical opposites) iamb, trochee, dactyl, anapest|adjective, noun
