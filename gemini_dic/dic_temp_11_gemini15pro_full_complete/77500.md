gazette: /ɡəˈzɛt/ | n. | 公报；报纸 | An official journal or newspaper, especially one publishing official appointments, notices, etc. | The results of the election were published in the government gazette.  选举结果刊登在政府公报上。| synonyms: journal, newspaper, periodical, publication, bulletin | antonyms:  None (Gazette doesn't have a direct opposite.) | noun

gazette: /ɡəˈzɛt/ | v. | (在公报上)公布，刊登 | To publish or announce something officially in a gazette. | The company’s bankruptcy was gazetted last week. 该公司的破产上周已在公报上公布。| synonyms: announce, publish, proclaim, declare | antonyms: conceal, suppress, withhold | verb


gazettes: /ɡəˈzɛts/ | n. | 公报；报纸（gazette的复数形式）| Plural form of "gazette," referring to multiple official journals or newspapers. | The local gazettes all carried the story.  当地各家报纸都刊登了这篇报道。 | synonyms: journals, newspapers, periodicals, publications, bulletins | antonyms: None | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *gazette*, from Venetian *gazeta* ("newspaper"), perhaps from *gazzetta* ("magpie," from its chattering), or from a small Venetian coin of that name, originally the price of the newspaper.
