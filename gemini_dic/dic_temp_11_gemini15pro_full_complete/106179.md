knockabouts:/ˈnɒkəˌbaʊts/| n. |耐穿的衣服；随便穿的衣服；(尤指)儿童游戏服；乱撞的人；流浪汉；适于粗用的（小船、汽车等）|Clothes or other items designed for rough or casual wear, especially children's play clothes; a person who wanders or drifts from place to place; something, especially a boat or car, designed for rough use.|例句：He wore his old knockabouts for gardening. 他穿着他的旧工作服做园艺。|例句：The children were playing in their knockabouts. 孩子们穿着他们的游戏服在玩耍。|近义词：play clothes, casual wear, work clothes, vagrant, tramp, runabout|反义词:formal wear, best clothes|词性:名词

knockabout:/ˈnɒkəˌbaʊt/| adj. |耐穿的；随便的；适于粗用的|Designed for rough or casual use; (of a comedy, play, etc.) characterized by boisterous action and horseplay.|例句：They bought a knockabout boat for fishing trips. 他们买了一艘耐用的船去钓鱼。|例句：It was a knockabout comedy, full of slapstick humor. 这是一部闹剧喜剧，充满了滑稽的幽默。|近义词：rough-and-ready, durable, casual, slapstick|反义词:delicate, fragile, formal|词性:形容词
