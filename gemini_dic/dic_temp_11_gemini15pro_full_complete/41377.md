corm:/kɔːrm/ | n. |球茎；块茎|A rounded underground storage organ present in plants such as crocuses and gladioli, consisting of a swollen stem base covered with scale leaves.|Gladioli grow from corms.  剑兰由球茎生长而来。|The crocus corms should be planted in autumn. 番红花球茎应在秋季种植。|Synonyms: bulb, tuber |Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a plant part) |noun
