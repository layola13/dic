leptocardia:/ˌlɛptəˈkɑːrdiə/ | n. |无头类，狭心纲|A subphylum of chordates, also known as lancelets or amphioxus, characterized by a slender, translucent body and a notochord that extends the length of the body.  They are small, fish-like marine invertebrates.|Leptocardia are important for studying the evolution of vertebrates.  无头类对于研究脊椎动物的进化很重要。|Synonyms: Cephalochordata, Amphioxi|Antonyms: Vertebrata|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *leptós* (thin, slender) + *kardía* (heart)
