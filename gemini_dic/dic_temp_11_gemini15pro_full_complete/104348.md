juxtaposed:/ˌdʒʌkstəˈpoʊzd/ | adj. | 并列的，并置的 | Placed close together for contrasting effect. |  The artist juxtaposed light and shadow in the painting. 艺术家在画作中将光线和阴影并置。 | The director juxtaposed scenes of war with scenes of peace, creating a powerful emotional impact. 导演将战争场面与和平场面并置，营造出强烈的感染力。 | synonyms: compared, contrasted, placed side by side | antonyms: separated, distanced, disjoined | adjective

juxtaposition:/ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən/ | n. | 并列，并置 | The act or an instance of placing two or more things side by side often to compare or contrast or to create an interesting effect. | The juxtaposition of the two images creates a striking visual effect.  这两幅图像的并置营造出惊人的视觉效果。 | The poem explored the juxtaposition of love and loss. 这首诗探讨了爱与失去的并置。| synonyms: comparison, contrast, collocation | antonyms: separation, division, disjunction | noun
