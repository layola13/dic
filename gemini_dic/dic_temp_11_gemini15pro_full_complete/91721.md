hypercriticism:/ˌhaɪpərˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/
|n.|过度批评，苛刻的批评|Excessive or unduly severe criticism; captious fault-finding.|The author faced hypercriticism for a minor historical inaccuracy in her novel.  这位作者因为她的小说中一个小小的历史错误而面临过度批评。|
| |The teacher's hypercriticism discouraged the students from participating in class discussions. 老师的过度苛刻的批评使学生们不愿参与课堂讨论。|
| |Synonyms: faultfinding, nitpicking, carping, caviling|
| |Antonyms: praise, commendation, approval|
|noun|


Etymology:
From hyper- + criticism.  Hyper- comes from the Greek "hyper," meaning "over, beyond, excessive."
