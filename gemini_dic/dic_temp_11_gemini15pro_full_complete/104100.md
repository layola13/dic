jump-started:/ˈdʒʌmpˌstɑːrtɪd/
verb|启动;快速启动; |To start a vehicle or machine by connecting its battery to another battery using jump leads. To cause something to start quickly.  To revive or revitalize something.| I jump-started my car this morning because the battery was dead. 我今天早上启动了我的车，因为电池没电了。|The government's investment jump-started the economy. 政府的投资快速启动了经济。| synonyms: boost, kick-start, revive, revitalize| antonyms: stall, hinder, obstruct|v.

jump-start:/ˈdʒʌmpˌstɑːrt/
noun|启动;快速启动;|The act of jump-starting a vehicle or machine. A boost or impetus given to something.|The car needed a jump-start. 这辆车需要启动。|The new marketing campaign provided a jump-start to sales. 新的营销活动促进了销售。| synonyms: boost, kick-start, impetus| antonyms: hindrance, obstruction|n.

jump-start:/ˈdʒʌmpˌstɑːrt/
adjective|启动的;快速启动的;|Started quickly or suddenly, often with outside help.| The jump-start program helped many unemployed people find jobs. 快速启动计划帮助许多失业者找到了工作。|The economy received a jump-start from the government's investment. 经济因政府的投资而快速启动。| synonyms: boosted, kick-started, revived, revitalized| antonyms: stalled, hindered, obstructed|adj.
