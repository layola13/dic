ferment:/fərˈment/ (v.)  /ˈfɜːrment/ (n.)
verb|发酵，酝酿，骚动|to undergo or cause to undergo fermentation; to be in or bring into a state of agitation or excitement.|The grapes are fermenting into wine. 葡萄正在发酵成葡萄酒。|synonyms: brew, effervesce, fester, seethe|antonyms: stagnate, settle, calm|v.

ferment:/fərˈment/ (v.)  /ˈfɜːrment/ (n.)
noun|发酵，骚动，酵素|a state of agitation or excitement; a substance capable of causing fermentation.|The city was in a ferment over the new tax.  这个城市因新税而骚动。|synonyms: agitation, unrest, turmoil, enzyme, yeast|antonyms: peace, tranquility, calm|n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French ferment (noun), fermenter (verb), from Latin fermentum "leaven, yeast; drink made of fermented barley," from fervere "to boil, seethe, ferment."
