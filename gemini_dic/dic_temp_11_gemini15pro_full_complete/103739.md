jostling: /ˈdʒɒslɪŋ/ (UK), /ˈdʒɑːslɪŋ/ (US)
| n. | 拥挤；推搡；竞争 | the act of pushing or struggling with others in a crowd, especially to get somewhere or do something |  The jostling for position at the front of the stage was intense.  舞台前排的争夺十分激烈。 |  shoving, pushing, scrambling, struggling | cooperation, yielding | noun
jostle: /ˈdʒɒsl/ (UK), /ˈdʒɑːsl/ (US)
| v. | 推挤；挤开；争夺 | to push or knock against someone roughly, typically in a crowd | He was jostled by the crowd as he left the stadium. 他离开体育场时被人群推搡。 | shove, push, elbow, bump | yield, give way | verb
