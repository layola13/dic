greenspan: /ˈɡriːnspæn/ | n. | 格林斯潘；（尤指）艾伦·格林斯潘（曾任美联储主席） |  Refers to Alan Greenspan, a highly influential American economist who served as the Chairman of the Federal Reserve of the United States from 1987 to 2006.  His name is often used as shorthand for his economic policies and predictions. | The market reacted nervously to Greenspan's comments on inflation. 市场对格林斯潘关于通货膨胀的评论反应紧张。 | synonym: Fed chairman (during his tenure), economist | antonym: N/A | noun
