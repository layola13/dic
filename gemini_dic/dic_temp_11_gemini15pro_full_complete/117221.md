meatily:/ˈmiːtɪli/ | adv. | 丰盛地；多肉地；实质性地 | In a meaty or substantial way; relating to meat; (figuratively) dealing with important or substantial matters. |  He wrote a meatily argued essay on the subject. 他就这个主题写了一篇论证充分的论文。| Synonyms: substantially, solidly, significantly, heartily | Antonyms: slightly, superficially, insignificantly | Part of Speech: adverb

Etymology: From *meat* (n.) + *-ly* (adv. suffix). The word "meat" comes from Old English *mete*, meaning "food" in general.  Over time, its meaning narrowed to refer specifically to the flesh of animals.
