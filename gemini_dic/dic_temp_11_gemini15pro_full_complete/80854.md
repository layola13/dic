gourd:/ɡɔːrd/ /ɡʊərd/| n. |葫芦；葫芦科植物|A fleshy, typically large fruit with a hard skin, some types of which are edible.  The dried hard-skinned fruit of various plants, used for decoration or as vessels.|例句：She used a dried gourd as a water bottle. 她用一个干葫芦当水壶。

例句：Gourds are grown in many warm climates. 葫芦生长在许多温暖的气候中。|近义词：calabash, pumpkin, squash|反义词: N/A |词性:noun


gourdy:/ˈɡɔːrdi/ /ˈɡʊərdi/| adj. |葫芦形的；膨胀的|Resembling a gourd in shape; swollen.|例句：The old man had a gourdy belly. 老人的肚子像葫芦一样鼓鼓的。

例句：The plant produced gourdy fruits. 这种植物结出葫芦形的果实。|近义词：rounded, swollen, bulbous|反义词:slender, thin|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gourde, from Latin cucurbita, ultimately from a reconstructed Proto-Indo-European root *gʷṓr-dʰ-.
