anticonvulsive:/ˌæntiːkənˈvʌlsɪv/| adj. n.|抗惊厥的；抗惊厥药|A drug or other agent that suppresses convulsions.|The doctor prescribed an anticonvulsive medication to control her seizures. 医生给她开了抗惊厥药来控制她的癫痫发作。|Synonyms: antiepileptic, antispasmodic|Antonyms: convulsant, proconvulsant|adjective

anticonvulsive:/ˌæntiːkənˈvʌlsɪv/| n.|抗惊厥药|A drug used to prevent or reduce the severity of convulsions.|She takes an anticonvulsive every day to manage her epilepsy. 她每天服用抗惊厥药来控制她的癫痫。|Synonyms: antiepileptic, antispasmodic|Antonyms: convulsant, proconvulsant|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- (against) + convulsive (related to convulsions).  The word "convulsive" comes from the Latin "convulsus," the past participle of "convellere," meaning "to pull violently," from "com-" (together, with) + "vellere" (to pluck, tear).
