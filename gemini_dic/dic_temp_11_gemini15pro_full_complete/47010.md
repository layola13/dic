dashism:/ˈdæʃɪzəm/| n.|达达主义；达达派|An avant-garde art movement that flourished between 1916 and 1922, originating in Zurich, Switzerland. It arose as a reaction to World War I and the prevailing rationalism and nationalism of the time. Dadaist art is often nonsensical and anti-art, embracing chaos, irrationality, and chance.|例句：The dashism movement rejected all traditional artistic values. 达达主义运动反对所有传统的艺术价值观。

|例句：Her dashism artwork challenged conventional notions of beauty. 她的达达主义艺术作品挑战了传统的美的概念。|近义词：Dada, Dadaism|反义词:Traditionalism, Classicism|词性:noun
