importunate:/ɪmˈpɔːrtʃənət/ /ɪmˈpɔːrtʃʊnət/ | adj. | 缠扰不休的，再三要求的，急切的 |  Persistent, especially to the point of annoyance or intrusion; making repeated or insistent requests. |  The child's importunate demands for a new toy finally wore down his parents.  这孩子没完没了地要新玩具，最终他的父母妥协了。|  persistent, insistent, pressing, demanding |  unobtrusive, discreet, reserved, shy | adjective

importunate:/ɪmˈpɔːrtʃənət/ /ɪmˈpɔːrtʃʊnət/ | adj. | 难以摆脱的，讨厌的 |  Difficult to avoid or get rid of; troublesome. |  He was plagued by importunate creditors. 他被纠缠不休的债主所困扰。 | troublesome, bothersome, annoying, irritating |  welcome, pleasant, agreeable | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *importunus, from Latin importūnus (“unfit, inconvenient, troublesome”), from in- (“not”) + portus (“harbor, haven”).  The sense development is from "unsuitable" to "troublesome" to "insistent, pressing."
