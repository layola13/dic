gloomy:/ˈɡluːmi/| adj.| 忧郁的，阴暗的，沮丧的|Characterized by darkness, sadness, or a lack of hope.  Often used to describe weather, moods, or prospects.|The gloomy weather reflected his mood. 阴沉的天气反映了他的心情。|The future looked gloomy for the company. 这家公司的前景看起来很黯淡。|近义词：dark, depressing, dismal, somber, bleak|反义词：bright, cheerful, sunny, optimistic|词性:形容词
词源:From Middle English *gloumen, from Old English *glōm (“twilight”), from Proto-Germanic *glōmaz (“twilight”). Related to Dutch gloom (“ember”), German Glumm (“ember”), and Swedish glomma (“to glow”). The original sense was of a dying fire, hence "dusky, gloomy."
