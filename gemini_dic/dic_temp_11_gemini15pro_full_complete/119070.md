metoposcopy: /ˌmɛtəˈpɒskəpi/ /ˌmɛtəˈpɑːskəpi/
n.
面相术，相面
The study of facial features, especially the lines on the forehead, as a means of interpreting character or predicting the future.
Metoposcopy was once a popular method for determining personality traits.  面相术曾经是一种流行的确定性格特征的方法。
Metoposcopy is considered a pseudoscience by most modern scientists.  大多数现代科学家认为面相术是伪科学。
近义词: physiognomy, face reading
反义词: None
词性: noun

Etymology: From Greek *metopon* (forehead) + *-skopia* (observation).
