cab:/kæb/| n.|出租车；计程车|A taxi, especially one driven by a licensed driver and for which a fare is charged. |例句：I took a cab to the airport. 我乘出租车去了机场。

例句：We hailed a cab in the pouring rain. 我们在倾盆大雨中叫了一辆出租车。|近义词：taxi, taxicab |反义词: None |词性: Noun
cab:/kæb/| v.|乘坐出租车|To travel in a cab. |例句：We cabbed it to the theatre. 我们乘出租车去了剧院。

例句：Let's cab home, it's too late to walk.  我们打车回家吧，太晚了不适合走路。|近义词：taxi |反义词: None |词性: Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
Shortened from cabriolet, from French cabriolet, diminutive of cabriole "caper, leap," from Italian capriola "somersault," from Latin capreolus "wild goat," diminutive of caper "goat."  The connection is to the light, bouncing movement of the carriage.
