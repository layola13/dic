brant:/brænt/ | n. | 黑雁 | A small, dark goose of the northern hemisphere, related to the Canada goose. | We saw a flock of brant flying overhead. 我们看到一群黑雁从头顶飞过。|  A brant landed gracefully on the water. 一只黑雁优雅地落在水面上。 | synonyms: Brent goose, barnacle goose | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a specific animal species) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *brente*, *brant*, from Old Norse *brandgás* (“burnt goose”, literally “burnt goose”), from *brandr* (“fire, brand, torch”) + *gás* (“goose”).  So called from its dark plumage.
