daimyo:/ˈdaɪmjoʊ/
n.
大名, 诸侯
A Japanese feudal lord, especially a powerful territorial magnate.
The daimyo ruled their domains with absolute authority.  大名以绝对的权威统治他们的领地。
The shogun relied on the loyalty of the daimyo.  幕府将军依靠大名的忠诚。
近义词：lord, magnate, shogun
反义词: peasant, commoner
词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Japanese 大名 (daimyō), from 大 (dai, “large”) + 名 (myō, from 名田 (myōden), “private estate”).  源于日语大名 (daimyō)，由 大 (dai, “large”) + 名 (myō, from 名田 (myōden), “private estate”) 组成。
