non-cognate:/nɒnˈkɒɡneɪt/ | adj. | 非同源的 | Not derived from the same source; having no common ancestor.  Used in linguistics to describe words in different languages that look or sound similar but have different origins. | English "much" and Spanish "mucho" look similar but are non-cognate. 英语单词"much"和西班牙语单词"mucho"看起来很像，但它们是非同源词。 |  | synonyms: unrelated, dissimilar | antonyms: cognate, related | adjective
