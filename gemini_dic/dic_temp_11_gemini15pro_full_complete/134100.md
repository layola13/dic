nonimmanencies:/ˌnɒnɪˈmænənsiz/
|n.| 非内在性；非固有性；非内在状态 |The state or quality of not being immanent; transcendence.  A philosophical concept referring to that which is not inherent or intrinsic to a particular being or system, but rather exists independently or externally to it. |His philosophy explores the nonimmanencies of consciousness, suggesting that it's not confined to the brain. 他的哲学探讨了意识的非内在性，认为它并不局限于大脑。|近义词: transcendence, externality |反义词: immanence, inherence|词性: noun


Etymology:
"non-" (Latin prefix): not
"immanence" (from Late Latin "immanentia"):  the state of being within or inherent
"-ies" (suffix): plural form
