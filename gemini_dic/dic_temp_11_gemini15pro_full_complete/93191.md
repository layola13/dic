ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *īgnōrāmus* "we do not know," the first word of the verdict of an English grand jury when they refused to indict an accused person, taken as a noun meaning "an ignorant person."  来自拉丁语 *īgnōrāmus* “我们不知道”，这是英国大陪审团拒绝起诉被告时的裁决的第一句话，被用作名词，意思是“一个无知的人”。
