apoplexed:/ˈæpəˌplekst/ (US) /ˈæpəplekst/ (UK)| adj.| 震惊的，气得说不出话的，恼怒的|Overwhelmed with anger or shock; speechless with indignation.|例句：He was apoplexed when he heard the news. 他听到这个消息后气得说不出话来。

例句：She was absolutely apoplexed at his rudeness. 她对他的粗鲁感到非常恼火。

近义词：furious, enraged, indignant, speechless, stunned|反义词:calm, composed, tranquil|词性:形容词

apoplexy:/ˈæpəˌpleksi/ (US) /ˈæpəpleksi/ (UK)| n.| 中风，(旧) 突然发作的疾病|Sudden loss of consciousness or bodily function, especially caused by a stroke.|例句：He suffered an apoplexy and died within minutes. 他突发中风，几分钟内就去世了。

例句：In older texts, apoplexy could refer to any sudden, severe illness. 在较早的文献中，apoplexy 可以指任何突然发生的严重疾病。

近义词：stroke, seizure|反义词:health, wellness|词性:名词


词源:

From Late Latin *apoplexia, from Ancient Greek ἀποπληξία (apoplexía, “sudden disablement”), from ἀποπλήσσω (apoplēssō, “to disable by a stroke”), from ἀπό (apó, “from, away from”) + πλήσσω (plēssō, “to strike”).
