commiserators:/kəˈmɪzəˌreɪtərz/| n. |慰问者，吊唁者|People who express sympathy and sorrow to someone who is suffering or grieving.|Many commiserators attended the funeral to offer condolences to the family. 许多吊唁者参加了葬礼，向家属表示哀悼。|Synonyms: sympathizers, condolers|Antonyms: celebrants, revelers|Noun

commiserate:/kəˈmɪzəreɪt/| v. |同情，怜悯，慰问|To express or feel sympathy or pity for someone.|She commiserated with him over the loss of his job. 她对他的失业表示同情。|Synonyms: sympathize, empathize, console|Antonyms: disregard, ignore|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *commiserārī, present active infinitive of commiseror (“I pity, I compassionate”), from Latin com- (“together”) + miseror (“I lament, I pity”).
