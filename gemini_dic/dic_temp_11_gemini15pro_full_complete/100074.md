interpolator:/ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪtər/ | n. | 插值器，内插器 | A device or mathematical function that estimates values between known data points.  | Example: The interpolator smoothly connected the data points on the graph.  插值器平滑地连接了图表上的数据点。 | Example:  This software uses a cubic spline interpolator for image resizing. 这个软件使用三次样条插值器来调整图像大小。 | Synonym: estimator, approximator | Antonym: N/A | noun

interpolating:/ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 插值的 | Relating to or involving interpolation. | Example: The interpolating function accurately predicted the missing values. 插值函数准确地预测了缺失值。 | Example: Interpolating polynomials are commonly used in numerical analysis. 插值多项式常用于数值分析。 | Synonym:  interpolated | Antonym: N/A | adjective

interpolate:/ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪt/ | v. | 插值，内插；插入(额外的事) | To insert (something of a different nature) into something else. / To estimate values between known data points. | Example: He interpolated some witty remarks into his lecture. 他在讲座中穿插了一些诙谐的评论。| Example: We can interpolate the missing data using a linear function. 我们可以使用线性函数插入缺失的数据。 | Synonym: insert, interpose, extrapolate (in some contexts) | Antonym: extrapolate (in some contexts), remove | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin interpolatus, past participle of interpolare "to refurbish, alter," from inter- "between" + polare "to polish, smooth."
