hellenistic:/ˌhɛlɪˈnɪstɪk/
adj.
希腊化的；希腊文化盛行的
Of or relating to the Greek history, language, and culture of the period between the death of Alexander the Great in 323 BC and the conquest of Greece by Rome in 146 BC.
Example: The museum has a wonderful collection of Hellenistic sculptures.  这家博物馆收藏了大量的希腊化时期的雕塑。
Example:  The Hellenistic period saw a flourishing of art and philosophy. 希腊化时期见证了艺术和哲学的繁荣。
Synonym: Hellenic, Greek
Antonym: Roman, non-Greek
词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *hellénistique*, from Ancient Greek Ἑλληνιστικός (Hellēnistikós, “imitating the Greeks”), from ἑλληνίζω (hellēnízō, “speak or behave like a Greek”), from Ἕλλην (Héllēn, “Greek”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱel- (“to shout, resound”).
