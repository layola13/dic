gullish:/ˈɡʌlɪʃ/| adj.|容易受骗的；轻信的；幼稚的|Easily deceived or cheated; credulous; naive. |He was gullish enough to believe her lies. 他很容易受骗，竟然相信了她的谎言。|Synonyms: naive, credulous, gullible, unsuspecting|Antonyms: astute, shrewd, discerning, skeptical|adjective

Etymology:  From gull (noun, meaning "a person who is easily tricked") + -ish (suffix forming adjectives). The noun "gull" ultimately comes from a word referring to the bird, the gull, which was perceived as being easily caught.
