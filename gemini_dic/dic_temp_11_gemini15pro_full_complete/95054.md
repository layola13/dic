inadaptation:/ˌɪnædæpˈteɪʃən/ | n. |不适应，不适合|The inability to adjust oneself to a new environment or situation. This can manifest in physical, psychological, or social difficulties. |His inadaptation to the city life led him to move back to the countryside. 他对城市生活的不适应使他搬回了农村。|Synonyms: maladjustment, unsuitability, inability to cope, incompatibility|Antonyms: adaptation, adjustment, suitability, compatibility|noun

inadaptation:/ˌɪnædæpˈteɪʃən/ | n. |不适应，不适合|The state of being poorly suited to a particular purpose or situation.  |The design of the building showed a clear inadaptation to the local climate.  这座建筑的设计明显不适应当地气候。|Synonyms: unfitness, inappropriateness, inaptness|Antonyms: fitness, appropriateness, aptness|noun
