bellicosities: /ˌbelɪˈkɒsɪtiz/
| n. |好战性，交战状态|A plural form of bellicosity, referring to multiple instances or acts of aggression or a general atmosphere of hostility.  |The bellicosities between the two nations led to a prolonged and devastating war. 这两个国家之间的敌对状态导致了一场旷日持久的毁灭性战争。|
|The current bellicosities in the region threaten to destabilize the entire continent. 该地区目前的敌对行动有可能破坏整个大陆的稳定。|
|近义词：aggressions, hostilities, warmongering|
|反义词：peacefulness, pacifism, diplomacy|
|词性：名词|


bellicosity: /ˌbelɪˈkɒsəti/
| n. |好战性，好斗|A noun referring to a warlike or hostile attitude, a tendency to fight or provoke conflict.|His bellicosity made him a dangerous opponent. 他的好战性使他成为一个危险的对手。|
|The nation's bellicosity was evident in its aggressive foreign policy.  这个国家的好战性在其侵略性的外交政策中显而易见。|
|近义词：aggressiveness, hostility, pugnacity|
|反义词：peacefulness, pacifism, gentleness|
|词性：名词|



词源：
From Latin bellicosus (“warlike”), from bellum (“war”).
