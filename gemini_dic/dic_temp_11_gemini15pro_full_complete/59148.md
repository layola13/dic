dynast: /ˈdaɪnəst/ | n. | 君主, 王朝的统治者 | A ruler or member of a ruling family, especially one who holds power for a long time. | The Ming dynast ruled China for centuries.  明朝统治者统治了中国几个世纪。 | Synonyms: ruler, monarch, sovereign, emperor, king, queen | Antonyms: subject, commoner | Noun

dynasty: /ˈdaɪnəsti/ | n. | 王朝, 世家 | A sequence of rulers from the same family, stock, or group. | The Tang dynasty is considered a golden age in Chinese history. 唐朝被认为是中国历史上的黄金时代。 | Synonyms: reign, empire, house, lineage | Antonyms: anarchy, disorder | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dynastia, from Greek dunasteia "lordship, power, domination," from dunastēs "lord, master, prince," from dunasthai "to be able, be powerful," from dunamis "power."
