joller:/ˈdʒɒlər/ (British English) | n. | 庆祝者，狂欢者 | A person who makes merry; a reveller. |  He's a real joller, always the life of the party. 他是一个真正的狂欢者，总是聚会的焦点。| 近义词：reveller, partygoer, celebrant | 反义词：mourner, killjoy | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From the verb "joll" + "-er" (agent suffix).  "Joll" is an obsolete verb meaning "to make merry, to rejoice."  Ultimately from Middle English "jollen," of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old French "jolif" (jolly).
