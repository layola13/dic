menstruation:/ˌmen.struˈeɪ.ʃən/ (US), /ˌmen.strʊˈeɪ.ʃən/ (UK)| n. |月经；行经|The process of discharging blood and other materials from the lining of the uterus at intervals of about one lunar month from puberty until menopause, except during pregnancy.|例句：She experienced her first menstruation at age 13. 她13岁第一次来月经。

例句：Irregular menstruation can be a sign of hormonal imbalance. 月经不调可能是荷尔蒙失衡的迹象。|近义词：period, menses|反义词: N/A|词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English menstruacioun, from Late Latin menstruātiō, from Latin menstruāre (“to discharge the menses”), from menstruus (“monthly”), from mēnsis (“month”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mḗh₁n̥s (“moon”, “month”).
