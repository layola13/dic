fainted:/ˈfeɪntɪd/| adj.|昏厥的，微弱的，虚弱的|Lost consciousness briefly due to illness, shock, or exhaustion.|She felt faint from the heat. 她因天热感到头晕。|近义词：dizzy, lightheaded, weak, feeble|反义词:strong, robust, conscious|词性:形容词

faint:/feɪnt/| vi. vt. adj. n.|昏厥，微弱的，不清楚的，昏厥|To lose consciousness briefly.|He fainted from the pain. 他痛得晕了过去。|近义词：swoon, pass out, black out|反义词:revive, recover|词性:动词，形容词，名词

Etymology: From Old French *feint, past participle of feindre "to feign, pretend, disguise," from Latin fingere "to shape, form, invent, feign." The sense of "losing consciousness" developed in the late 16th century, perhaps from the idea of feigning death.
