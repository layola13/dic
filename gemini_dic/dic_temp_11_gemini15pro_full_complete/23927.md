breakthrough: /ˈbreɪkθruː/
n.
突破；重大进展；突破性
A sudden, important discovery or development that helps to improve a situation or solve a problem.
Scientists have made a major breakthrough in the treatment of cancer. 科学家们在癌症治疗方面取得了重大突破。

breakthrough: /ˈbreɪkθruː/
n.
突破点
The point at which a critical problem is overcome.
The negotiations reached a breakthrough after several days of talks.  经过几天的谈判，谈判取得了突破。


English Explanation: A significant and sudden advance, development, or achievement, especially one that removes a barrier or solves a difficult problem.  It can also refer to the act of overcoming or penetrating an obstacle.

Synonyms:  advance, development, progress, leap, discovery, innovation, revolution

Antonyms: setback, hindrance, obstacle, impasse, stalemate, retardation, regression

Etymology:  From "break" + "through."  "Break" meaning to shatter, interrupt, or overcome, and "through" indicating penetration or completion of a passage.  Therefore, "breakthrough" literally signifies breaking through a barrier.
