demedicating:/diːˈmɛdɪkeɪtɪŋ/
|v.|停止用药；逐渐减少药物剂量|The process of gradually reducing and stopping a patient's medication(s).  This is often done to assess if the medication is still needed or to minimize potential side effects.| The doctor is demedicating the patient slowly to see if her symptoms return. 医生正在慢慢地给病人停药，看看她的症状是否会复发。|  The patient was demedicated after several years of taking antidepressants.  这个病人服用抗抑郁药数年后停药了。| tapering, weaning, discontinuing| medicating, administering|verb
