insinuatingness:/ɪnˈsɪnjuːeɪtɪŋnɪs/
n.
暗示性；含蓄性；迂回曲折
The quality of being insinuating; subtly suggesting something, often something unpleasant or disapproving, without saying it directly.
Her insinuatingness made him uncomfortable; he felt she was implying something negative about him without actually saying it.
近义词：suggestiveness, implication, innuendo, indirectness
反义词：directness, forthrightness, explicitness
词性: noun

insinuate:/ɪnˈsɪnjuːeɪt/
v.
暗示；旁敲侧击地指出；使潜入；使迂回地潜入
To suggest or hint (something bad or reprehensible) in an indirect and unpleasant way.
He insinuated that she was lying, but he didn't have any proof. 他暗示她在撒谎，但他没有任何证据。
近义词：imply, suggest, hint, intimate
反义词：state, declare, assert
词性: verb
