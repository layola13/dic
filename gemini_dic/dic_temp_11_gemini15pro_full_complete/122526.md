一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Modal verbs like "can," "may," "must," and "should" are frequently used in the simple present tense to express ability, permission, or obligation.
He can speak three languages. 他会说三种语言。

She must submit her assignment by Friday. 她必须在星期五之前提交作业。


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Modal verbs are generally not used in the continuous form themselves. However, they can be used with verbs in the continuous form.
He should be studying for his exams right now. 他现在应该正在为考试学习。

She may be working late tonight. 她今晚可能要工作到很晚。


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
Modal verbs can be used with the perfect infinitive to talk about past possibilities or obligations.
He could have gone to the party, but he chose not to. 他本可以去参加聚会，但他选择不去。

She should have finished her work by now. 她现在应该已经完成工作了。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
Modal verbs are generally not used with the perfect continuous form.


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
Past forms of modal verbs, like "could," "might," "would," and "should," are used to express past abilities, possibilities, or obligations.
He could swim when he was five years old. 他五岁时就会游泳。

They might have gone to the cinema. 他们可能去看电影了。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
Similar to the present continuous, modal verbs are not used in the past continuous form themselves, but can precede verbs in the past continuous.
He might have been sleeping when I called. 我打电话时他可能正在睡觉。

She could have been working on the project. 她可能一直在做那个项目。


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
Modal verbs can be used with the past perfect infinitive to express events that should have, could have, or would have happened in the past.
He should have told me the truth earlier. 他应该早点告诉我真相。

She could have avoided the accident if she had been more careful. 如果她更小心的话，她本可以避免这场事故。


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
Modal verbs are generally not used with the past perfect continuous form.


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
Modal verbs "will," "shall," "may," "might," and "can" are used to express future possibility or prediction.
They will probably arrive tomorrow. 他们可能明天到达。

He may travel to Europe next summer. 他明年夏天可能会去欧洲旅行。


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
Modal verbs are generally not used with the future continuous form themselves but can accompany main verbs in this tense.
She will likely be working on the project tomorrow afternoon. 明天下午她可能正在做这个项目。

They might be travelling to Asia next year.  明年他们可能正在去亚洲旅行。



将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
Modal verbs can be used with the future perfect infinitive.
He should have finished the project by next week. 他应该在下周之前完成这个项目。

They will have completed the course by the end of the year. 他们将在年底前完成课程。


将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
Modal verbs are generally not used with the future perfect continuous form.


虚拟语气:
Modal verbs are often used in conditional sentences to express hypothetical situations.
If I were you, I would apologize. 如果我是你，我会道歉。

She could have won the race if she had trained harder. 如果她训练得更刻苦，她本可以赢得比赛。


被动语态:
Modal verbs can be used in passive constructions.
The problem should be solved soon. 这个问题应该很快就能解决。

The meeting might have been cancelled. 会议可能已经取消了。
