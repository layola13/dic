excitinger: /ɪkˈsaɪtɪŋər/ | adj. (non-standard) | 更令人兴奋的 |  A comparative form of "exciting," suggesting something is more exciting than something else.  This is not standard English; the correct comparative is "more exciting." |  "This rollercoaster is excitinger than the last one." (non-standard)  This sentence should be: "This rollercoaster is *more exciting* than the last one." | 近义词: more exciting, thrilling, stimulating | 反义词: less exciting, boring, dull | 词性: adjective
词源:  Derived from "exciting," which comes from the verb "excite." "Excite" traces back to the Latin "excitare," meaning "to rouse, call forth, awaken."  The "-er" suffix is a comparative ending.
