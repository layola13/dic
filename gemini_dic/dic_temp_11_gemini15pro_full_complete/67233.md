expropriationists: /ɛksˌproʊpriˈeɪʃənɪsts/
| n. | 征用者；没收者 | People who seize property, often land, from others, typically by government action. | The expropriationists seized the farmer's land without proper compensation. 征用者在没有适当补偿的情况下夺取了农民的土地。 |  The government acted as expropriationists, taking control of the privately owned oil fields. 政府充当了征用者的角色，控制了私有的油田。 | synonyms: confiscators, seizers, appropriators | antonyms:  restorers, returners | noun

expropriate: /ɛksˈproʊprieɪt/
| v. | 征用，没收 | To take possession of property, especially for public use by the right of eminent domain, often without fair compensation. | The government expropriated the land for the construction of a new highway. 政府为了修建一条新的高速公路而征用了这块土地。 | The regime expropriated the assets of the exiled businessman. 该政权没收了流亡商人的资产。| synonyms: seize, confiscate, appropriate | antonyms: return, restore | verb

expropriation: /ɛksˌproʊpriˈeɪʃən/
| n. | 征用；没收 | The act of expropriating. | The expropriation of land for public use is a controversial issue. 征用土地供公众使用是一个有争议的问题。 | The expropriation of the company's assets by the government led to international protests. 政府没收公司资产的举动引发了国际抗议。 | synonyms: seizure, confiscation, appropriation | antonyms: restitution, restoration | noun
