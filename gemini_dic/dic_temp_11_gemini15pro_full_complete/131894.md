noncontemptibleness:/ˌnɒnkənˈtɛmptɪbəlnəs/ | n. | 不可轻蔑；值得尊重；不容忽视 | The noncontemptibleness of her achievements impressed everyone. |  Her work ethic and dedication to her craft demonstrated a certain noncontemptibleness. | Near Synonyms: respectability, worthiness, merit, significance | Near Antonyms: insignificance, worthlessness, contemptibility | noun

Etymology:
From non- + contemptible + -ness.  "Contemptible" comes from Old French "contemptible" and ultimately from Latin "contemptibilis," meaning "despicable," from "contemnere" (to despise).  The prefix "non-" simply negates the meaning.
