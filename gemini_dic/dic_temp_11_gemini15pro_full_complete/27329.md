camail:/kəˈmeɪl/| n.|锁子甲罩衫，披肩甲|A piece of medieval armor, typically made of chain mail, that protected the neck and shoulders.|The knight wore a camail over his hauberk.  骑士在他的锁子甲上穿了一件锁子甲罩衫。|Synonyms: aventail, mail coif, |Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "unprotected neck/shoulders")|noun

Etymology:
From Old French *camail*, from Late Latin *capmailla*, a diminutive of *cappa* ("cape, hood"). Ultimately from Latin *cappa*.
