coercively:/koʊˈɜːrsɪvli/
adverb
强制地；胁迫地
in a way that uses force or threats to make someone do something
The government coercively enforced the new law. 政府强制执行了这项新法律。
The confession was coercively obtained. 口供是逼供得来的。
Synonyms: forcibly, compulsorily, by force, under duress
Antonyms: willingly, voluntarily, freely
词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From coerce + -ive + -ly.  Coerce comes from Latin "coercere"  (to restrain, confine, enclose), from "co-" (together) + "arcere" (to enclose, confine).
