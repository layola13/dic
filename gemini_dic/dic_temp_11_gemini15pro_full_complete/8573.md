anticivil:/ˌæntiˈsɪvəl/| adj.|不文明的，失礼的，粗鲁的|Opposing or hostile to civilized behavior; rude, uncourteous, or lacking in civility.|His anticivil behavior at the dinner party embarrassed everyone. 他在晚宴上的不文明行为让每个人都很尴尬。|The protestors’ anticivil actions, including vandalism and looting, sparked outrage. 抗议者不文明的行为，包括故意破坏和抢劫，引发了众怒。|近义词：rude, impolite, discourteous, uncivil, barbarous, boorish|反义词：civil, polite, courteous, respectful|词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + civil.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to," derived from Greek. "Civil" comes from the Latin "civilis," meaning "relating to citizens or the state," and later acquired the sense of "polite" or "courteous."
