boxes:/ˈbɒksɪz/ (UK) /ˈbɑːksɪz/ (US)| n.|盒子；箱子|A container, typically rectangular and made of cardboard or wood, used for storage or shipping.|The delivery driver left several boxes on the porch. 送货司机在门廊上留下了几个箱子。|synonyms: cartons, containers, crates|antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical object, could be considered conceptually opposite to "contents")|noun


boxes:/ˈbɒksɪz/ (UK) /ˈbɑːksɪz/ (US)| v.|拳击；打耳光|To fight with fists; to slap someone's face.|He boxes professionally. 他是职业拳击手。|synonyms: fights, punches, spars|antonyms: embraces, protects|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "boiste," diminutive of "boise," meaning "box" (made of wood), ultimately from Late Latin "buxida," from Latin "buxus," meaning "boxwood" (a type of wood).
