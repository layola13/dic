defoliation:/ˌdiːfoʊliˈeɪʃən/ | n. |落叶；去叶；落叶（现象）| The act of removing leaves from a plant or tree, either naturally (as in autumn) or artificially (often by chemicals). | The defoliation of the trees by the caterpillars was extensive.  毛毛虫对树木的落叶造成了广泛的损害。| synonyms: leaf fall, leaf shedding | antonyms: foliation, leafing | noun


defoliate: /ˌdiːˈfoʊliˌeɪt/ | vt. |使落叶；除去…的叶子| To cause the leaves of a plant or tree to fall off. | The chemical agent was used to defoliate the jungle.  这种化学制剂被用来使丛林落叶。| synonyms: strip, denude | antonyms: foliate, leaf | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *defoliatus*, past participle of *defoliare*  "to strip of leaves," from *de-* "off, down, away" + *folium* "leaf."
