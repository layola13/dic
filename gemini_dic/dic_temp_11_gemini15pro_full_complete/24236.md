bridle:/ˈbraɪdl/ (US), /ˈbraɪdl/ (UK)| n. v. |马勒; 约束; 控制|A piece of equipment used to control a horse, consisting of a headstall, bit, and reins.  To put a bridle on a horse; to show one's resentment or anger, especially by throwing up the head and drawing in the chin.|She put the bridle on the horse. 她给马套上马勒。|Synonyms: harness, restrain, curb|Antonyms: unleash, free, liberate|noun
bridle:/ˈbraɪdl/ (US), /ˈbraɪdl/ (UK)| v. |控制; 约束; 昂首表示不满|To show one's resentment or anger, especially by throwing up the head and drawing in the chin. To bring something under control; to restrain.|She bridled at his suggestion. 她对他的建议表示不满。|Synonyms: restrain, curb, check|Antonyms: unleash, free, encourage|verb
