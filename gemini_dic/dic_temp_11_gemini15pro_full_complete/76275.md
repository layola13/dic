fyke:/faɪk/
noun
鱼梁，堰,一种用柳条或网做成的锥形捕鱼陷阱
A fyke is a type of fish trap, typically made of wickerwork or netting, shaped like a cone or funnel.
Fishermen set fykes in the river to catch eels. 渔民在河里设置鱼梁来捕捞鳗鱼。
The fyke net was full of salmon. 鱼梁里满是鲑鱼。
synonyms: fish trap, weir, fish weir
antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but consider terms like "open water" or "free-flowing river" to represent the opposite of a trap.)
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fik, *fyk, *fyke, from Old English *fic, *fyc  "fish weir," related to Old Norse fíka "fishing net," Dutch fuik "fishing net," German Feige "fish weir."  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fikō "fish weir."
