animism:/ˈænɪmɪzəm/
n.|万物有灵论|The belief that natural objects, natural phenomena, and the universe itself possess souls.  The attribution of a soul to plants, inanimate objects, and natural phenomena.|Indigenous cultures often practice animism, believing that spirits inhabit trees, rocks, and rivers.|近义词:vitalism, panpsychism|反义词:materialism, mechanism|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From French *animisme*, from Latin *anima* ("soul, life, breath") + *-ismus* (suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine).
