misdeserve: /ˌmɪsdɪˈzɜːrv/ | v. | 不配; 不应得到 | To not deserve something, either good or bad. | He misdeserved the punishment he received. 他不应受到他所受到的惩罚。| She felt she misdeserved the praise she was given. 她觉得她不配得到她所受到的赞扬。 | 近义词: not deserve, be unworthy of | 反义词: deserve, be worthy of | 词性: 动词

词源:  "mis-" (badly, wrongly) + "deserve" (to be worthy of something).  "Mis-" is a prefix of Old English origin, while "deserve" comes from Old French "deservir," ultimately from Latin "deservire" (to serve well, to deserve).
