agnostic-aphasias:/æɡˈnɒstɪk əˈfeɪʒəz/ | n. | 失认性失语 | A type of aphasia where the patient has difficulty recognizing objects, sounds, smells, tastes, and other sensations, even though their sensory pathways are intact. This is not a single, universally recognized category, but rather a descriptive term for a cluster of symptoms that can occur in various aphasic syndromes.  It can involve difficulty understanding the meaning of sensory information, even though the sensation itself is perceived. |  The patient with agnostic-aphasias could feel the key in his hand, but couldn't identify it as a key.  这个失认性失语症患者能感觉到手中的钥匙，但却无法识别它是一把钥匙。

The doctor suspected agnostic-aphasias after the patient failed to recognize common household items.  医生在病人无法识别常见的家居用品后怀疑是失认性失语。 | 近义词: sensory aphasia, receptive aphasia | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it's a deficit, not a specific ability) | 词性: noun
