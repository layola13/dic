disaffirmance:/ˌdɪsəˈfɜːrməns/ | n. | 拒绝;否认;废除;撤销 |The act of disaffirming; a denial or rejection; the annulment or cancellation of something, especially a contract or agreement.  This is often used in legal contexts, particularly regarding contracts entered into by minors. |  The minor's disaffirmance of the contract rendered it void.  未成年人对合同的否认使其无效。 |  repudiation, revocation, cancellation, annulment |  affirmation, ratification, acceptance | noun

disaffirm:/ˌdɪsəˈfɜːrm/ | vt. | 拒绝;否认;废除;撤销 |To deny or reject something, especially a contract or agreement.  This term is commonly used in legal settings, particularly when a minor chooses to void a contract they entered into before reaching the age of majority. | He disaffirmed the contract as soon as he turned 18. 他一满18岁就废除了合同。 |  renounce, repudiate, revoke, cancel |  affirm, ratify, accept | verb
