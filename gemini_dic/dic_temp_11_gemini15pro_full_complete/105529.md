kilowatt: /ˈkɪləwɒt/ (UK) /ˈkɪləwɑːt/ (US)| n.|千瓦|A unit of power equal to 1,000 watts.|The power plant generates thousands of kilowatts of electricity.|近义词: megawatt|反义词: milliwatt|词性:noun

Etymology:
From kilo- + watt.  "Kilo-" is a combining form representing "one thousand" and comes from the Greek khilioi. "Watt" is named after James Watt, a Scottish inventor and mechanical engineer.
