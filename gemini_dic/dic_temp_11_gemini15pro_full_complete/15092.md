bacchanal: /ˌbækəˈnæl/ | n. adj.| 狂欢；狂欢的；喧闹的 | An occasion of wild and drunken revelry.  A wild, drunken revelry or orgy. As an adjective, characterized by or given to drunken revelry; riotously drunken. | The fraternity party was a complete bacchanal.  兄弟会的派对简直是一场狂欢。| Synonyms: orgy, revelry, carousal, debauchery, spree | Antonyms: solemnity, sobriety, temperance | noun

bacchanal: /ˌbækəˈnæl/ | adj.| 狂欢的；喧闹的 | Characterized by or given to drunken revelry; riotously drunken. | The bacchanal atmosphere of the concert was infectious.  音乐会狂欢的气氛很有感染力。| Synonyms: riotous, boisterous, unruly, wild | Antonyms: quiet, peaceful, calm | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Bacchanalia*, plural of *Bacchanale*, from *Bacchus*, the Roman god of wine (equivalent to the Greek Dionysus).
