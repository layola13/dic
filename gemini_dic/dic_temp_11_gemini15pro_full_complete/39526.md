conqueringly:/ˈkɒŋkərɪŋli/ | adv. | 征服地，胜利地 | In a manner suggestive of victory or conquest.  Derived from "conquer" with the suffix "-ingly" which transforms it into an adverb indicating the manner of the action. | He smiled conqueringly, having finally won the debate. 他胜利地微笑着，最终赢得了辩论。| triumphantly, victoriously | submissively, meekly | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From conquer + -ing + -ly. Conquer comes from Old French conquerre, ultimately from Latin conquirere, meaning “to seek out, procure, win, gain”.
