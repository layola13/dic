come-home-to-roost:/kʌm hoʊm tuː ruːst/| idiom |自作自受；(恶果)自食其果；(不好的事情)最终回到自身|This idiom refers to the consequences of one's actions eventually returning to affect them negatively, like chickens coming home to roost in the evening.  It implies a sense of inevitability and karmic retribution.|His dishonesty finally came home to roost when he was caught embezzling funds. 他的不诚实最终让他自食恶果，因为他被发现挪用了资金。|Synonyms: backfire, boomerang, rebound, catch up with|Antonyms: escape consequences, get away with it|idiomatic phrase

come-home-to-roost:/kʌm hoʊm tuː ruːst/| verb phrase |自作自受；(恶果)自食其果；(不好的事情)最终回到自身|This phrase acts as a verb phrase, describing the action of negative consequences returning to the originator.|Her early mistakes in the project came home to roost when the deadline approached.  她在项目早期的错误在截止日期临近时让她自食恶果。|Synonyms: backfire, boomerang, rebound, catch up with|Antonyms: escape consequences, get away with it|idiomatic phrase
