crazes:/ˈkreɪzɪz/
noun
狂热；风尚；短暂的流行
An enthusiasm for a particular activity or object that lasts for a short period of time.
The latest craze is for young people to dye their hair bright colours. 最新的潮流是年轻人把头发染成鲜艳的颜色。
fads, trends, fashions
---


craze:/kreɪz/
noun
狂热；风尚；短暂的流行
An enthusiasm for a particular activity or object that lasts for a short period of time.
There was a craze for wearing ripped jeans a few years ago. 几年前流行穿破洞牛仔裤。
fad, trend, fashion
---
verb
(使)发狂；(使)精神错乱
To make someone insane or behave as if they are insane.
The heat was starting to craze him. 高温开始让他发狂。
 madden, derange
sanity, rationality
词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *kreisa "to bend, twist," related to Danish krase "to crackle." The original sense in English was "to break, crack," later "to impair mentally."  The noun sense "fashion, fad" arose in the 19th century.
