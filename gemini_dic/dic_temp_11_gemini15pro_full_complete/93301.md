ill-bred:/ˌɪlˈbrɛd/ | adj. | 粗鲁的，没有教养的 | Showing a lack of good manners; rude and impolite. |  His ill-bred behavior embarrassed everyone at the dinner party. 他的粗鲁行为让宴会上每个人都感到尴尬。 |  The ill-bred child threw a tantrum in the store.  这个没有教养的孩子在商店里大发脾气。 | Synonyms: rude, impolite, unmannerly, discourteous, boorish | Antonyms: polite, courteous, well-mannered, refined | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From ill + bred (past participle of breed).  "Ill" here means "badly" or "poorly."  Therefore, "ill-bred" literally means "badly raised" or "poorly brought up."
