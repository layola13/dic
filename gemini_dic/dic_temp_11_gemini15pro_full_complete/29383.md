casuist:/ˈkæʒuɪst/| n.|诡辩家，决疑家|A person who studies and resolves moral problems by applying principles of casuistry.|The professor, a renowned casuist, skillfully navigated the complex ethical dilemma.  这位著名的决疑家巧妙地解决了这个复杂的伦理困境。|Synonyms: moralist, ethicist, theologian|Antonyms: amoralist, nihilist|noun

casuistry:/ˈkæʒuɪstri/| n.|决疑法，诡辩|The application of general ethical principles to particular cases, especially in moral theology; specious or excessively subtle reasoning intended to rationalize or justify questionable behavior.|The lawyer's arguments were dismissed as mere casuistry.  律师的论点被斥为纯粹的诡辩。|Synonyms: sophistry, quibbling, hairsplitting|Antonyms: straightforwardness, honesty|noun
