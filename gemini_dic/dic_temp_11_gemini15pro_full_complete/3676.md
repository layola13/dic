aggrandize: /əˈɡrændaɪz/ | vt. | 增大，扩张；吹捧 | To increase the power, status, or wealth of; to enhance or exaggerate the importance, power, or reputation of someone or something. | He aggrandized himself by claiming achievements that were not his own. 他通过声称不属于自己的成就来抬高自己。

The king sought to aggrandize his realm through conquest. 国王试图通过征服来扩张他的王国。

| 近义词：augment, enlarge, expand, exalt, glorify, magnify | 反义词：diminish, reduce, belittle, deprecate | 词性：动词

aggrandizement: /əˈɡrændɪzmənt/ | n. | 增大，扩张；吹捧  | The act of aggrandizing; an increase in power, status, or wealth. |  His constant boasting revealed his desire for self-aggrandizement. 他不断的吹嘘暴露了他渴望自我膨胀的欲望。

The aggrandizement of the Roman Empire took centuries. 罗马帝国的扩张花费了几个世纪。

| 近义词：augmentation, enlargement, expansion, exaltation | 反义词：diminution, reduction | 词性：名词
