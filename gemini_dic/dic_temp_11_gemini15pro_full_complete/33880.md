circumspectnesses:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈspektnɪsɪz/| n. |谨慎；小心；慎重|The quality of being circumspect; careful consideration of all circumstances and possible consequences before acting. |His circumspectnesses in making decisions is well known. 他做决定时的谨慎是众所周知的。|近义词：caution, prudence, carefulness, discretion|反义词：recklessness, impulsiveness, carelessness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Late Middle English: from Old French *circon-spect ‘looking around’, from Latin circumspectus ‘looked around, cautious’, past participle of circumspicere, from circum- ‘around’ + spicere ‘to look’. The plural form is attested from the early 17th century.
