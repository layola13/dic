anti-isolation:/ˌæntiˌaɪsəˈleɪʃən/ | adj. n.| 反隔离，反孤立 | Opposing or intended to prevent isolation or loneliness.  This can refer to policies, technologies, or social initiatives. |  The government introduced anti-isolation measures for the elderly during the pandemic. 政府在疫情期间为老年人推出了反隔离措施。

The anti-isolation policy aimed to connect people digitally.  这项反隔离政策旨在以数字方式连接人们。 | 近义词：social, connecting, integrative, inclusive | 反义词：isolating, segregating, exclusionary | 词性: adjective, noun


Etymology:
anti- : a prefix meaning "against," "opposite of," from Greek anti-.
isolation : from isolate + -ion. Isolate comes from Italian isolato, past participle of isolare “to isolate,” from isola “island,” from Latin insula “island.”
