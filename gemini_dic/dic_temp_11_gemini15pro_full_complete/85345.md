harquebuse:/ˈhɑːrkɪbʌs/ | n. | 火绳枪，钩铳 | An early type of portable firearm with a long barrel, supported on a tripod or a forked rest. It was ignited by a match applied to a touchhole. |  The soldiers advanced, their harquebuses poised and ready. 士兵们向前推进，他们的火绳枪准备就绪。

The harquebuse was a crucial weapon in the sixteenth century.  火绳枪是十六世纪的一种重要武器。 | 近义词: arquebus, handgun, firearm | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "bow and arrow" or "sword" as earlier weapons) | 词性: noun


Etymology:
From Middle French *harquebuse*, from Middle Dutch *haakbus*, literally "hook gun," from *hake* ("hook") + *bus* ("gun," "cannon").
