acyclovirs: /ˌeɪˈsaɪkloʊvɪrz/ | n. | 阿昔洛韦（复数） | An antiviral medication used for the treatment of herpes simplex virus (HSV) infections, chickenpox, and shingles.  It works by stopping the spread of the herpes virus in the body. |  The doctor prescribed acyclovirs for her shingles. 医生给她开了阿昔洛韦治疗带状疱疹。| Synonym: Zovirax (brand name) | Antonym: None | Noun

Etymology:  A blend of "acyclic" (referring to the chemical structure) + "-vir" (a suffix used in antiviral drug names).
