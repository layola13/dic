endure:/ɪnˈdjʊr/ (US), /ɪnˈdʒʊə(r)/ (UK)| v. |忍受，忍耐；持续，持久|To suffer (something painful or difficult) patiently over a long period; to remain in existence; last.|例句：She had to endure years of hardship. 她不得不忍受多年的艰辛。|近义词：tolerate, bear, withstand, last, persist|反义词: surrender, yield, succumb, perish|词性:动词
endurance:/ɪnˈdjʊərəns/ (US), /ɪnˈdʒʊərəns/ (UK)| n. |忍耐力，持久力|The ability or strength to continue or last, especially despite fatigue, stress, or other adverse conditions; the fact or power of enduring an unpleasant or difficult process or situation without giving way.|例句：The marathon is a test of endurance. 马拉松是对耐力的考验。|近义词：tolerance, stamina, resilience|反义词: weakness, fragility|词性:名词
enduring:/ɪnˈdjʊərɪŋ/ (US), /ɪnˈdʒʊərɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |持久的，持续的|Continuing or long-lasting.|例句：He has an enduring love for his wife. 他对妻子有着持久的爱。|近义词：lasting, permanent, persistent|反义词: temporary, transient, ephemeral|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French endurer, from Latin indurare "to harden, make hard," from in- "in" + durare "to harden, last," from durus "hard."
