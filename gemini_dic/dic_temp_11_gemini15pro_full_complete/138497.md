noviciates:/noʊˈvɪʃiɪts/ /nəˈvɪʃiɪts/
n.
见习期;见习僧(或尼);新手
The period during which a novice of a religious order lives in a monastery or convent before taking final vows. A person who is new to and inexperienced in a job or situation.
The young doctor still felt like a novitiate despite having completed her residency.  这位年轻的医生虽然完成了住院医师实习期，但她仍然觉得自己像个新手。
They spent a year in the novitiate before taking their vows. 他们在宣誓前在修道院度过了一年的见习期。
近义词:apprenticeship, probation, traineeship, initiation, introduction
反义词:expertise, mastery, veteran, professional
词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English novisiates, from Old French, from Medieval Latin novitiatus, from Latin novus (“new”).
