Celts: /kɛlts/ /selts/ | n. |凯尔特人|A group of peoples inhabiting much of Western and Central Europe in ancient times, including the Gauls, Britons, Irish, and others, and speaking Celtic languages.|The Celts were known for their intricate metalwork. 凯尔特人以其复杂的金属制品而闻名。|Synonyms: Gauls, Britons, Irish|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a group of people)|Noun
