notarizing: /ˈnoʊtəˌraɪzɪŋ/
Verb
公证；确认
The official act of certifying a document.
例句：The lawyer is notarizing the signature on the will. 律师正在公证遗嘱上的签名。

例句：Notarizing the document makes it legally binding.  公证文件使其具有法律约束力。

近义词：certifying, authenticating, validating
反义词：invalidating
词性：v.


notary: /ˈnoʊtəri/
Noun
公证人
A person authorized to perform certain legal formalities, especially to certify signatures or documents.
例句：I had to go to a notary public to get my affidavit notarized. 我不得不去找公证人对我的宣誓书进行公证。

例句：The notary stamped and signed the document. 公证人在文件上盖章并签字。

近义词：notary public
反义词：N/A
词性：n.

notarization: /ˌnoʊtərɪˈzeɪʃən/
Noun
公证
The act or process of notarizing.
例句：Notarization of the document is required for it to be legally valid. 该文件需要公证才能具有法律效力。

例句：The notarization ensures the authenticity of the signatures. 公证确保了签名的真实性。

近义词：certification, authentication, validation
反义词：invalidation
词性：n.


词源: From Late Latin *notāriālis, from Latin notārius (“shorthand writer, secretary”), from nota (“mark, character, letter”).
