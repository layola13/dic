nonconformists: /ˌnɒnkənˈfɔːmɪsts/ (British) /ˌnɑːnkənˈfɔːrmɪsts/ (American)
|n.|不墨守成规的人；非国教徒|People who do not conform to generally accepted beliefs, customs, or practices. Originally, in England, a Protestant who did not conform to the Church of England.|Martin Luther King Jr. was a nonconformist who challenged the status quo. 马丁·路德·金是一位挑战现状的不墨守成规者。|synonyms: maverick, individualist, dissident, rebel, iconoclast|antonyms: conformist, traditionalist|noun
nonconformist: /ˌnɒnkənˈfɔːmɪst/ (British) /ˌnɑːnkənˈfɔːrmɪst/ (American)
|adj.|不墨守成规的；非国教徒的|Not conforming to generally accepted beliefs, customs, or practices.|Her nonconformist style made her stand out from the crowd. 她不墨守成规的风格使她从人群中脱颖而出。|synonyms: unconventional, unorthodox, rebellious, independent|antonyms: conformist, conventional, orthodox, traditional|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + conformist.  "Conformist" comes from the Late Latin "conformis" (similar in form), from "com-" (together) + "formis" (form).
