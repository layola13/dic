operandi:/ˌɒpəˈrændi/ /ˌɑːpəˈrændi/ | n. |  做法，手段，行动方式 |  (Latin) Literally, "method of operating."  It usually refers to a characteristic or habitual way of working, often used in the context of criminal investigations.  It is most frequently used in the phrase *modus operandi*. | The police are trying to figure out the suspect's modus operandi. 警方正试图弄清嫌疑人的作案手法。| synonyms: method, procedure, technique, approach | antonyms: N/A | noun

modus operandi:/ˌməʊdəs ɒpəˈrændi/ /ˌmoʊdəs ɑːpəˈrændi/ | n. | 作案手法，惯用手段 | A particular way of doing something, especially one that is characteristic of a particular person or group.  It is a Latin phrase that literally translates to "mode of operating." | The burglar's modus operandi involved disabling the alarm system before entering the house.  窃贼的作案手法包括在进入房屋前先破坏报警系统。 | synonyms: method, procedure, technique, approach, operandi | antonyms: N/A | noun
