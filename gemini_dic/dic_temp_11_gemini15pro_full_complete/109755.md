levier: /ˈlɛviər/ (US), /ˈliːvə(r)/ (UK)| n.|杠杆; 手段,方法;|A rigid bar resting on a pivot, used to help move a heavy or firmly fixed load with one end when pressure is applied to the other.|She used a stick as a lever to lift the heavy rock. 她用一根棍子作为杠杆来抬起沉重的岩石。|Synonyms: crowbar, handle, tool, instrument, means, method|Antonyms: impediment, obstacle|noun

levier: /ˈlɛviər/ (US), /ˈliːvə(r)/ (UK)| n.|（金融）杠杆比率;|The ratio of a company's loan capital (debt) to the value of its common stock (equity).|The company's high leverage made it vulnerable to economic downturns. 该公司的高杠杆率使其容易受到经济衰退的影响。|Synonyms: gearing, debt-to-equity ratio|Antonyms: deleveraging|noun
