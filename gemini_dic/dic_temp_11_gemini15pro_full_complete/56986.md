double-crossing:/ˌdʌbəlˈkrɒsɪŋ/ | adj. n.| 背信弃义的，欺骗的；背叛，欺骗 | betraying someone after having agreed to help or support them |  He was furious when he found out his partner had been double-crossing him. 他发现他的伙伴一直在欺骗他后非常愤怒。 | The double-crossing led to the downfall of the organization. 这次背叛导致了该组织的垮台。| 近义词：treacherous, deceitful, betraying, two-timing | 反义词：loyal, faithful, trustworthy | 词性: adjective, noun

double-cross:/ˌdʌbəlˈkrɒs/ | v. | 背叛，欺骗 | to betray someone after having agreed to help or support them | The spy double-crossed his country by selling secrets to the enemy.  这个间谍把秘密卖给敌人，背叛了他的国家。 | She felt double-crossed when her friend revealed her secret. 当她的朋友泄露了她的秘密时，她感到被出卖了。| 近义词：betray, deceive, cheat | 反义词：support, aid, assist | 词性: verb
