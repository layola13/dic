circumstantiality:/ˌsɜːrkəmˌstænʃiˈælɪti/
n.|冗长, 啰嗦; 枝节性; 赘述;|The excessive and unnecessary detail in speech or writing, often seen in individuals with certain mental health conditions.|He answered the question with such circumstantiality that everyone lost interest. 他回答问题时如此冗长，以至于大家都失去了兴趣。|Synonyms: prolixity, verbosity, long-windedness|Antonyms: conciseness, brevity, succinctness|noun

circumstantial:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈstænʃəl/
adj.|间接的; 依照情况的; 详尽的;|Based on circumstances or inference rather than direct evidence; detailed.|The evidence against him was purely circumstantial. 对他不利的证据完全是间接的。|Synonyms: indirect, inferential, presumptive|Antonyms: direct, conclusive, definitive|adjective
