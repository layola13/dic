crimp:/krɪmp/| n. vt.|卷曲，波纹；压接；妨碍|A curl or bend (as in hair); something that crimps, curls, or kinks; to press (something, such as a metal edge or a fabric edge) into small regular folds; to cause to curl or bend; to put a check or restraint on|例句：The hairdresser used a crimping iron to style her hair.  发型师用卷发器给她做头发。

例句：The economic downturn put a crimp on their expansion plans. 经济衰退阻碍了他们的扩张计划。|近义词：curl, wave, bend, crease, restrain, hinder|反义词：straighten, flatten, encourage, promote|词性:名词,动词
crimps:/krɪmps/| n.|卷曲，波纹|Plural form of crimp|例句：Her hair was full of crimps. 她的头发全是波纹。

例句：The crimps in the metal sheet added strength to the structure. 金属板上的波纹增加了结构的强度。|近义词：curls, waves, bends, creases|反义词：straight lines, flat surfaces|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle Dutch *krimpen, frequentative of *krimmen (“to curve, bend”), from Proto-Germanic *krimpaną (“to shrink, crumple, wrinkle”). Cognate with Dutch krimpen (“to shrink, shrivel, crimp”), German krimmen (“to shrink, shrivel”).
