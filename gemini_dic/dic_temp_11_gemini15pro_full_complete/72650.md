flyweight:/ˈflaɪˌweɪt/| n.|蝇量级，轻量级选手，不重要的东西|A boxer who weighs no more than 112 pounds.  A person or thing of little importance or influence.|The new employee was treated like a flyweight and his opinions were ignored.  这个新员工被当作无足轻重的人，他的意见被忽视了。|Synonyms: lightweight, insignificant, unimportant|Antonyms: heavyweight, important, significant|noun
flyweights:/ˈflaɪˌweɪts/| n.|蝇量级选手（复数），轻量级选手（复数），不重要的东西（复数）|Plural form of flyweight, referring to multiple boxers in the flyweight category, or multiple insignificant things.|The flyweights in the tournament were all highly skilled.  锦标赛上的蝇量级选手都非常熟练。The CEO dismissed the concerns as the complaints of flyweights. 首席执行官认为这些担忧是无关紧要的人的抱怨，不予理会。|Synonyms: lightweights, insignificants, unimportants|Antonyms: heavyweights, importants, significants|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"flyweight" comes from "fly" (referring to something light or quick) + "weight" (referring to mass or importance).  The term originated in boxing to denote a weight class.
