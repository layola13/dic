nonconvertiblenesses:/ˌnɑːnkənˈvɜːrtəbəlnəsəz/ | n. | 不可兑换性（复数） | The state or quality of not being convertible, especially of currency or securities, into another form, such as gold or a foreign currency. |  The nonconvertiblenesses of several currencies contributed to the global financial crisis.  几种货币的不可兑换性导致了全球金融危机。| Synonyms: inconvertibilities, non-exchangeabilities | Antonyms: convertibilities, exchangeabilities | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + convertible + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing state or quality).  Convertible ultimately comes from the Latin verb convertere, meaning "to turn around" or "to change."
