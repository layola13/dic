grasswidowhood:/ˈɡræsˌwɪdəʊhʊd/| n. |草寡妇身份，指丈夫长期不在身边的妻子状态|The state of being a grass widow, a woman whose husband is away for a prolonged period, often for work or military service.  A woman who is temporarily separated from her husband.|She experienced a period of grasswidowhood while her husband was stationed overseas.|近义词：separation, absence|反义词: togetherness, cohabitation|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->

From "grass" + "widowhood".  "Grass" in this context alludes to a wife being left alone in a grassy field, symbolic of her husband's absence.  The term originated in the American West, referring to women whose husbands were away for extended periods working as cowboys or prospectors.
