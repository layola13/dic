high-tension:/ˌhaɪ ˈtɛnʃən/| adj.|高压的，高度紧张的|Characterized by high voltage or a state of high emotional stress or anxiety.|The high-tension wires buzzed ominously. 高压电线发出不祥的嗡嗡声。|Synonyms: high-voltage, tense, strained|Antonyms: low-tension, relaxed, calm|adjective

high-tension:/ˌhaɪ ˈtɛnʃən/| n.|高压，高度紧张|High voltage or a state of high emotional stress or anxiety.|There was a palpable high-tension in the room before the announcement.  宣布之前，房间里弥漫着明显的紧张气氛。|Synonyms: high voltage, tension, stress|Antonyms: low tension, relaxation, calmness|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "high" + "tension". "Tension" comes from the Latin "tensionem" (nominative tensio) "a stretching, tightening," noun of action from past-participle stem of tendere "to stretch, extend."
