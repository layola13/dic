burghers:/ˈbɜːrɡərz/
n.
市民，镇民
A citizen of a town or city, especially one possessing municipal privileges.
The burghers of the town gathered in the square to hear the mayor's announcement.  镇上的市民聚集在广场上听市长的公告。

The burghers elected a new council. 市民们选举了一个新的议会。

citizens, townspeople, townsfolk
peasants, countrymen, rural dwellers
noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *burgeis*, *burgeys*, from Anglo-Norman *burgeis*, *burges*, from Old French *burgeis*, *borjois*, from Late Latin *burgensis* (“inhabitant of a burg”), from *burgus* (“fortified town”). Doublet of *bourgeois*.
