ocher:/ˈoʊkər/ (US) /ˈəʊkə(r)/ (UK)| n. adj.|赭色，赭石|A moderate orange yellow.  As a noun, it refers to an earthy pigment containing hydrated iron oxide, ranging in color from yellow to deep orange or brown. As an adjective, it describes something having this color.|

例句：
The artist used ocher to paint the sunset.
艺术家用赭色颜料画日落。

The walls of the ancient city were painted a deep ocher.
古城墙被涂成了深赭色。


近义词：yellow-orange, orange-brown, sienna, rust|
反义词:blue, green|
词性:noun, adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *ochre*, from Latin *ochra*, from Ancient Greek *ὤχρα* (*ōkhra*), meaning "pale yellow."  Ultimately from a Proto-Indo-European root meaning "yellow."
