fleetingly:/ˈfliːtɪŋli/ | adv. | 短暂地，飞快地 | For a fleeting moment, I thought I saw him.  | The image flashed fleetingly across the screen. 图像在屏幕上飞快地闪过。 | briefly, shortly, momentarily | lastingly, permanently | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From fleeting + -ly.  Fleeting comes from Middle English *fletinge, present participle of fleten (“to float, fly, hasten, flee”), from Old English flēotan (“to float, swim, flow”), from Proto-Germanic *fleutaną (“to float”), from Proto-Indo-European *plewd- (“to flow, float, swim”).
