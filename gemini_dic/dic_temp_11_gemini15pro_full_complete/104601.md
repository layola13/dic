kanzu:/ˈkɑːnzuː/
noun
坎祖服，长袍
A long, white, robe-like garment worn by men in East Africa, particularly in Uganda and Tanzania.  It is often worn for formal occasions such as weddings, funerals, and religious ceremonies.
He wore a crisp white kanzu to the wedding. 他穿着清爽的白坎祖服参加婚礼。
Synonyms: robe, garment, gown
Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps casual wear)
