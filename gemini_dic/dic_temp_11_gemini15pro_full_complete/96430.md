indistinct:/ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋkt/ | adj. | 模糊的，不清楚的 | Not clearly defined or easy to perceive or understand. | The photograph was old and the faces were indistinct.  照片很旧，脸都模糊不清了。|  The sound of their voices was indistinct, lost in the wind. 他们的声音模糊不清，消失在风中。| 近义词: vague, unclear, fuzzy, blurred, faint | 反义词: clear, distinct, sharp, precise | 词性: 形容词

indistinct:/ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋkt/ | adj. | 微弱的，不明显的 |  Difficult to hear or see clearly. |  There was an indistinct murmur coming from the crowd.  人群中传来一阵微弱的低语。|  She had an indistinct feeling that something was wrong. 她隐隐约约觉得有什么地方不对劲。 | 近义词: subtle, faint, muted | 反义词: obvious, pronounced, noticeable | 词性: 形容词


词源: Late Middle English: from in- ‘not’ + distinct.
