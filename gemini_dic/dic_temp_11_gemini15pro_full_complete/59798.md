ecphasis:/ˈɛkfəsɪs/| n.|强调，着重|A distinctive emphasis; special stress laid upon some word or words in a sentence; a particular prominence given to an idea or thought in speaking or writing.|The speaker delivered the speech with marked ecphasis on the importance of education.|近义词：emphasis, stress, accent, prominence|反义词: understatement, downplaying|词性:noun
Etymology: From Ancient Greek ἔκφασις (ékphasis, “a declaration, utterance, statement, expression”), from ἐκφαίνω (ekphaínō, “I reveal, I make known”), from ἐκ (ek, “out of”) + φαίνω (phaínō, “I show, I bring to light”).
