first-foot:/ˈfɜːrst fʊt/ | n. | 开年第一客；新年初访者 | The first person to enter a house on New Year's Day, traditionally thought to bring good or bad luck depending on their characteristics.  A dark-haired man is considered lucky. |  My uncle was our first-foot this year, bringing us shortbread and whisky.  今年我叔叔是我们的开年第一客，他给我们带来了酥饼和威士忌。 | synonym: first footer | antonym: N/A | noun

first-footing: /ˈfɜːrst ˈfʊtɪŋ/ | n. |  开年第一踩；新年初访 | The custom of visiting someone's house soon after midnight on New Year's Day. | We always go first-footing at my grandparents' house. 我们总是在我祖父母家进行新年初访。| synonym: first-foot, Hogmanay visit (Scotland) | antonym: N/A | noun
