care:/kɛr/ | n. vi. vt.|关心, 照顾, 忧虑|A feeling of concern for someone or something.|She took care of her sick mother. 她照顾她生病的母亲。|近义词:concern, worry, attention, look after|反义词:neglect, indifference|noun
care:/kɛr/ | n. vi. vt.|小心, 谨慎|The process of protecting someone or something and providing what they need.|Take care when crossing the road. 过马路要小心。|近义词:caution, prudence, heed|反义词:negligence, carelessness|verb
care:/kɛr/ | n. vi. vt.|喜欢, 介意|To feel concern or interest; attach importance to something.|I don't care what you think. 我不在乎你怎么想。|近义词:mind, bother, be concerned|反义词:disregard, ignore|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *carian "to grieve, lament, care," from Proto-Germanic *karo "grief, lament" (source also of Old High German chara "lament," Gothic kara "sorrow, anxiety"). The meaning shifted from "feel grief" to "feel concern or anxiety," then to "take charge of, look after."
