disorienting:/dɪsˈɔːriˌentɪŋ/ | adj. | 使人迷失方向的，令人迷惑的 | Causing someone to lose their sense of direction or their bearings; confusing. | The flashing lights were disorienting. 闪爍的灯光令人迷失方向。| The complex plot of the movie was disorienting. 这部电影复杂的剧情令人迷惑。 | synonyms: confusing, bewildering, perplexing, unsettling | antonyms: orienting, guiding, clarifying | adjective

disorient:/dɪsˈɔːrient/ | verb | 使迷失方向，使迷惑 | To cause someone to lose their sense of direction or their bearings; to confuse. | The sudden change in scenery disoriented him.  场景的突然变化让他迷失了方向。|  The loud noise disoriented the animals. 巨大的噪音使动物们惊慌失措。| synonyms: confuse, bewilder, perplex, unsettle | antonyms: orient, guide, clarify | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation, reversal) + orient (to align or position oneself).  The word "orient" itself comes from the Latin "oriens," meaning "rising" (as in the rising sun), and was originally used to mean "to face east."
