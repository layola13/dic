buggered:/ˈbʌɡərd/ (UK) /ˈbʌɡərd/ (US)| adj.|精疲力竭的，累坏了的；（委婉语）该死的，糟糕的；损坏的|Ruined, exhausted, or broken (often used as a mild oath).  This word is considered vulgar in some contexts.|例句：I'm absolutely buggered after that hike. 那次远足之后我累坏了。

例句：The car's buggered – the engine won't start. 这车坏了——引擎发动不了了。|近义词：exhausted, tired, broken, damned (vulgar), ruined|反义词:functioning, working, fine|词性:adjective


bugger:/ˈbʌɡə(r)/ (UK) /ˈbʌɡər/ (US)| n. vt.|讨厌鬼，家伙；（粗俗语）鸡奸；烦扰，使…困惑|A nuisance or tiresome person; (vulgar) sodomite; to annoy or confound.|例句：He's a lazy bugger. 他是个懒鬼。

例句：Don't bugger about with my computer! 别乱动我的电脑！|近义词：nuisance, pest, bother (verb)|反义词:helper, assistant (for verb sense)|词性:noun, verb

PHRASE->
Bugger all = nothing at all  什么也没有
Bugger off = go away (rude)  滚开
Bugger me! = an exclamation of surprise  我的天啊！
I couldn't be buggered = I'm too tired or unmotivated  我累死了/我一点也不想动
Like buggery! = certainly not (very rude)  才怪！

词源：
From Old French *bougre, "heretic," from Medieval Latin Bulgarus, referring to a Bulgarian sect of heretics (Bogomils).  The meaning shifted over time to a term of abuse and then to its current range of meanings.
