cobweb:/ˈkɑːbwɛb/ (US) /ˈkɒbwɛb/ (UK)| n.|蜘蛛网；蛛丝；混乱；陈旧的东西|A network of thin threads made by a spider to trap insects.  Also used figuratively to describe something old, neglected, or dusty.|The attic was filled with cobwebs.|Synonyms: spiderweb, gossamer, tangle, snare|Antonyms: order, neatness|noun

cobweb:/ˈkɑːbwɛb/ (US) /ˈkɒbwɛb/ (UK)| v. (transitive, often passive) |布满蜘蛛网；使混乱；使陈旧|To cover or become covered with cobwebs. Also used figuratively to describe something becoming outdated or neglected.|The furniture was cobwebbed from years of disuse.|Synonyms: ensnare, entangle, obscure|Antonyms: clarify, organize|verb
