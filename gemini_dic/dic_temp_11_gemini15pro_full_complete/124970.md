muggard:/ˈmʌɡərd/| n.| 吝啬鬼，守财奴|A miserly or stingy person; a skinflint. | He's such a muggard, he wouldn't even buy his own mother a birthday present. 他真是个吝啬鬼，甚至连自己母亲的生日礼物都不买。|近义词：miser, skinflint, tightwad, cheapskate|反义词：spendthrift, philanthropist|词性: 名词

muggard:/ˈmʌɡərd/| adj.| 吝啬的，守财奴似的|Miserly or stingy. |She gave him a muggard look when he asked for more money. 当他要求更多钱时，她给了他一个吝啬的眼神。|近义词：miserly, stingy, tightfisted, parsimonious|反义词：generous, lavish, prodigal|词性: 形容词


词源:
The origin of "muggard" is uncertain. It likely comes from a dialectal or slang term, possibly related to "mug," meaning "face" or "expression," implying a sour or disapproving countenance associated with miserliness.  It's primarily used in Scottish and Northern English dialects.
