mody:/ˈmoʊdi/| adj.|喜怒无常的，情绪化的|Given to sudden changes of mood; temperamental.  Characterized by a gloomy or sullen mood. |例句：He's been very mody lately, snapping at everyone.  他最近情绪很不好，动不动就对人大吼大叫。

例句：The mody teenager slammed the door and stomped upstairs.  喜怒无常的少年砰地一声关上门，跺着脚上楼去了。|近义词：temperamental, changeable, erratic, moody, fickle, capricious|反义词：stable, steady, even-tempered, cheerful|词性:形容词
词源: From Middle English *modi, from Old English *mōdig (meaning "proud, arrogant, brave, high-spirited"), from Proto-Germanic *mōdigaz, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mē- (“to measure, compare”).  The meaning shifted over time from "proud" to "changeable in mood."
