cox:/kɒks/ | n. | 舵手；（尤指大学或学院赛艇队的）艇长；（小船的）艇夫 | a person who steers a boat, especially the coxswain of a racing boat (especially at a university or college), or a boatman who takes people or goods in a small boat. | The cox steered the boat expertly through the rough waters. 舵手熟练地驾驶着船穿过汹涌的水面。 | synonyms: coxswain, steersman, helmsman | antonyms: passenger, crew | noun

cox:/kɒks/ | v. | 驾驶（尤指赛艇）；指挥（赛艇队） | to steer (especially a racing boat); to be coxswain of (a racing crew) | She coxed the Cambridge crew to victory. 她指挥剑桥队取得了胜利。 | synonyms: steer, pilot, navigate, helm | antonyms: drift, follow | verb
