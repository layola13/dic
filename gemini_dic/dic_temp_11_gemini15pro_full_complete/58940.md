dust-up:/ˈdʌstʌp/| n.|争吵，吵闹，小冲突|A short fight or loud argument.|例句：There was a bit of a dust-up between the two players after the tackle.  两名球员在抢断后发生了一些小争执。

例句：They had a dust-up over who would pay the bill. 他们为了谁付账而吵了一架。|近义词：quarrel, argument, squabble, brawl, altercation|反义词：agreement, harmony, peace|词性:名词

Etymology:  Likely originated in the late 19th century American West, possibly referring to the clouds of dust kicked up during a physical altercation, like a bar fight.  The exact origin is uncertain, but the imagery is clear.
