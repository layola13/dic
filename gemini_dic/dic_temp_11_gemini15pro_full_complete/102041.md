isolationists:/ˈaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪsts/ | n. | 孤立主义者 | A person who advocates isolationism, a policy of remaining apart from the affairs or interests of other groups, especially the political affairs of other countries. |  The senator was known for his isolationist views, preferring to focus on domestic issues. 这位参议员以其孤立主义观点而闻名，他更倾向于关注国内问题。

| 近义词：separatists, noninterventionists | 反义词：internationalists, globalists | 词性：noun


isolationist:/ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪst/ | adj. | 孤立主义的 | Favoring isolationism. | The isolationist stance of the country during the war was controversial. 该国在战争期间的孤立主义立场备受争议。 | 近义词：separatist, noninterventionist | 反义词：internationalist, globalist | 词性：adjective


Etymology:  From isolation + -ist. Isolation from isolate + -ion. Isolate from French isoler, from Italian isolare, from isola "island," from Latin insula "island."
