doddering:/ˈdɒdərɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈdɑːdərɪŋ/ (US)| adj.|步履蹒跚的，老态龙钟的|Mentally or physically infirm with age; feeble and shaky, especially from old age.  Characterized by trembling, tottering movements or by mental decline.|The doddering old man shuffled slowly down the street.  那个老态龙钟的老人在街上蹒跚而行。|近义词：feeble, frail, infirm, shaky, tottering|反义词：vigorous, robust, sturdy|词性:形容词

dodder:/ˈdɒdə(r)/ (UK) /ˈdɑːdər/ (US)| vi.|蹒跚而行，脚步不稳|To tremble or totter, typically because of old age.|He doddered across the room to his chair. 他蹒跚地走过房间，来到他的椅子旁。|近义词：totter, stumble, shuffle|反义词：stride, walk briskly|词性:动词
