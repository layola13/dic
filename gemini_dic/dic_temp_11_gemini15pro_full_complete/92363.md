hypochondria:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndriə/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɑːndriə/ (US)| n. |疑病症，臆想病|A psychological disorder characterized by excessive worry about having a serious illness, even when there are no symptoms or medical evidence to support the concern.  It involves misinterpreting normal bodily sensations as signs of a serious disease.|She suffers from hypochondria and is constantly convinced she has a life-threatening illness.|近义词:health anxiety, illness anxiety disorder|反义词:robust health,  well-being|词性:noun

hypochondriac:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndriæk/ (UK) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɑːndriæk/ (US)| n. adj. |疑病症患者，疑病的|A person who suffers from hypochondria. / Exhibiting the characteristics of hypochondria; excessively worried about one's health.|He's a hypochondriac and always rushing to the doctor for minor ailments. /  She gave him a hypochondriac look.|近义词:valetudinarian|反义词:stoic|词性:noun, adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hypochondria, from Ancient Greek ὑποχόνδριος (hupochóndrios, “hypochondriac”), from ὑπό (hupó, “under”) + χόνδρος (khóndros, “cartilage”).  The hypochondria (the area below the rib cartilage) was once believed to be the seat of melancholy and hypochondria.
