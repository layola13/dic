coddle:/ˈkɒdl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːdl/ (US)| v. |溺爱；娇养；悉心照料|To treat someone with excessive care and tenderness, usually to the point of spoiling them.  To cook something gently in liquid just below boiling point.|She coddled her youngest son, always giving him what he wanted.|He coddled the eggs in simmering water.|近义词: pamper, indulge, spoil, baby|反义词: neglect, ignore|词性:动词

coddle:/ˈkɒdl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːdl/ (US)| n. |娇生惯养的人；被溺爱的人|A person who is treated with excessive care and tenderness.|He was a bit of a coddle, always complaining if things weren't exactly to his liking.|近义词: softie, weakling|反义词: tough guy, independent person|词性:名词
