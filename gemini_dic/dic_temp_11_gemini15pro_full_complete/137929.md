nonvolubleness:/ˌnɒnˌvɒljʊˈb(ə)lnəs/
n.
寡言少语；沉默寡言
The state or quality of not being talkative or voluble.
Her nonvolubleness made it difficult to know what she was thinking. 她沉默寡言，让人很难知道她在想什么。
近义词: reticence, taciturnity, reserve
反义词: volubility, loquacity, talkativeness
词性: noun

Etymology:
From non- + voluble + -ness.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Voluble" comes from the Latin "volubilis," meaning "easily rolling, fluent."  "-ness" is a suffix that forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
