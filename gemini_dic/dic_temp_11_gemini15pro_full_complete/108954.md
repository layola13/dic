leerily:/ˈlɪərɪli/| adv.|警惕地；猜疑地；不信任地|In a wary or suspicious manner, suggesting distrust or apprehension.|He looked at her leerily, unsure of her intentions. 他警惕地看着她，不确定她的意图。|The cat leerily approached the strange object. 猫咪小心翼翼地靠近那个陌生的物体。|近义词：suspiciously, warily, distrustfully|反义词：trustingly, confidently|词性:副词

词源:
From leer (v.) + -ly (adv. suffix).  The verb "leer" originates from the Middle English "leren," meaning "to look askance,"  likely influenced by the Old English "hlēor," meaning "cheek."  The modern sense of a suggestive or malicious look developed later.
