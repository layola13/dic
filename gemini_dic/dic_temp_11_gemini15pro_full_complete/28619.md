caretaken:/kɛərˈteɪkən/ | adj. | 受照顾的；被照看的 |  Taken care of; looked after.  Having been the object of care.  | The children were well caretaken by their grandmother. 孩子们被他们的祖母照顾得很好。 |  例句：The caretaken garden flourished under her attention.  受到精心照料的花园在她悉心呵护下欣欣向荣。 | 近义词：looked after, cared for, attended to | 反义词：neglected, abandoned | 词性: 形容词

词源:  "Caretaken" is the past participle of the verb "caretake," which is formed by combining "care" and "take."  "Care" comes from the Old English "caru," meaning "anxiety, grief, sorrow," and later developed the sense of "attention, heed."  "Take" comes from the Old English "tacan," meaning "to grasp, seize, lay hold of."
