dreadest:/ˈdredɪst/| adj. |最可怕的，最令人恐惧的|Superlative form of "dreadful," meaning causing great fear or anxiety.|例句：It was the dreadest experience of my life.  这是我一生中最可怕的经历。

例句：The haunted house was the dreadest place on the street.  那间鬼屋是街上最可怕的地方。|近义词：most terrifying, most frightening, most awful, worst|反义词：least frightening, best, most wonderful|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *drǣdan, from Proto-Germanic *drēdaną (“to fear, respect”), from Proto-Indo-European *tras-  (“to tremble, be afraid”).  Related to Old Norse hræða, Old Saxon drādan, Old Frisian drēda, Middle Dutch drēden, Dutch dreden, Old High German trātan, German treten (“to tread”).  The superlative ending "-est" is a standard English suffix.
