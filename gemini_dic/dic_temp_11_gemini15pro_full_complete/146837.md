overinclines:/ˌoʊvərɪnˈklaɪnz/ | v. | (使)过度倾向，(使)过分倾斜 | To incline excessively; to cause to lean or slope too much. | The architect overinclined the roof, making it susceptible to snow buildup.  建筑师使屋顶过度倾斜，使其容易积雪。|  The government overinclined towards austerity measures, harming the economy. 政府过分倾向于紧缩措施，损害了经济。 |  biases, predisposes, leans | resists, avoids | verb

overinclined:/ˌoʊvərɪnˈklaɪnd/ | adj. | 过度倾向的，过分倾斜的 |  Excessively inclined or biased. | He was overinclined to believe conspiracy theories. 他过分倾向于相信阴谋论。| The tower was overinclined and eventually collapsed. 塔过度倾斜，最终倒塌。 | biased, prejudiced, predisposed | unbiased, neutral, objective | adjective

词源: "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "incline" (from Latin "inclinare," meaning "to bend, lean").
