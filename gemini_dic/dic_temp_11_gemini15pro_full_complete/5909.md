ambiguous:/æmˈbɪɡjuəs/ | adj. | 模棱两可的，含糊不清的 | Open to more than one interpretation; having a double meaning. | The ending of the movie was ambiguous, leaving the audience to wonder what really happened. 电影的结尾模棱两可，让观众想知道真正发生了什么。| The politician gave an ambiguous answer to the reporter's question.  这位政客对记者的提问给出了一个模棱两可的答案。 | 近义词: unclear, vague, equivocal, obscure | 反义词: clear, precise, unequivocal, definite | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *ambiguus*, from Latin ambo (“both”) + agere (“to drive, lead, do”).  The original sense was "driving both ways," or "wavering, uncertain."
