lazarist:/ˈlæzərɪst/| n.|拉撒路会修士；遣使会会士|A member of the Congregation of the Mission, a Roman Catholic society of priests and brothers founded by St. Vincent de Paul.|He was a dedicated lazarist, serving the poor and sick.|近义词:Vincentian|反义词:|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Saint Lazarus, patron saint of lepers, as the Congregation of the Mission originally cared for lepers.  Ultimately from Latin Lazarus, from Greek Λάζαρος (Lazaros), from Hebrew אֶלְעָזָר (Elʿazar) meaning "God has helped".
