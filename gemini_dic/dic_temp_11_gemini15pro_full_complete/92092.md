hyperrealizing:/ˌhaɪpərˈriːəlaɪzɪŋ/
verb
超现实化，过度现实化
The act of making something seem more real than it actually is, often through heightened sensory details or exaggerated depictions.
He was hyperrealizing the dangers of the situation, imagining every possible worst-case scenario. 他在过度现实化情况的危险性，想象着每一种最坏的情况。

She found herself hyperrealizing the textures and colors of the food, as if seeing them for the first time. 她发现自己过度现实化了食物的纹理和颜色，就好像第一次看到它们一样。

Synonym: exaggerating, amplifying, intensifying
Antonym: downplaying, minimizing, understating
verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hyper-" (meaning "over, excessive") + "realize" (from Old French "realiser," meaning "to make real").
