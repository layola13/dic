aquiparous:/əˈkwɪpərəs/
adjective
卵胎生的
Producing eggs that hatch within the body, so that the young are born alive but without placental attachment, as in some snakes and fishes.
The three-toed skink is an aquiparous lizard, meaning its eggs hatch inside its body. 三趾石龙子是一种卵胎生的蜥蜴，这意味着它的卵在体内孵化。
近义词: ovoviviparous
反义词: oviparous, viviparous
词性: adjective

Etymology: From Latin *aquiparus ("producing live young from eggs"), from aqui- ("egg") + -parus ("bearing, producing").  The Latin prefix aqui- is related to ovum ("egg").
