amusette:/ˌæmjuˈzɛt/
n.
a light, diverting piece of music, a bagatelle; a small, light cannon; a small, light amusement or pastime.
A short, light musical piece, typically for piano.  A small toy or trinket. A light field gun.
She played a charming amusette on the piano.  The children were delighted with the amusettes they found in the party bags.  The army deployed a battery of amusettes.
synonyms: bagatelle, trifle, divertimento, knick-knack, plaything, toy
antonyms:  epic, masterpiece, chore, burden
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *amusette*, diminutive of *amuser* (to amuse), from Old French *amusser*, from *a-* (to) + *musser* (to hide, conceal, muse).  Ultimately from Latin *mūsa* (muse).
