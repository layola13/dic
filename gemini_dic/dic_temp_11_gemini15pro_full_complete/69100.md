fauvists:/ˈfɔːvɪsts/| n. |野兽派画家|A group of early 20th-century French artists whose works emphasized painterly qualities and strong color over the representational or realistic values retained by Impressionism.|The Fauvists, led by Matisse, revolutionized the Paris art scene. 野兽派画家，以马蒂斯为首，彻底改变了巴黎艺术界。|Near Synonyms:  Expressionists, Modernists|Antonyms:  Realists, Impressionists|Noun


fauvist:/ˈfɔːvɪst/| n. |野兽派画家|An artist belonging to the Fauvist movement.|Matisse is considered a leading Fauvist. 马蒂斯被认为是野兽派的主要代表人物。|Near Synonyms:  Expressionist, Modernist|Antonyms:  Realist, Impressionist|Noun


fauvism:/ˈfɔːvɪzəm/| n. |野兽派|A style of painting with vivid expressionistic and non-naturalistic use of color.|Fauvism was a short-lived but influential movement in art history. 野兽派是艺术史上一个短暂但有影响力的运动。|Near Synonyms:  Expressionism, Modernism|Antonyms:  Realism, Impressionism|Noun
