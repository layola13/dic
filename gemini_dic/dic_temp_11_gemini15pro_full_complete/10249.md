apothegmatize:/əˈpɒθɪɡmətaɪz/| v. |箴言化；以格言表达；|To express something concisely and forcefully, often in the form of a maxim or aphorism.  To turn something into an aphorism.|He apothegmatized his philosophy of life into a few pithy sentences. 他将自己的人生哲学精辟地概括成几句话。|近义词：epigrammatize, condense, summarize|反义词:expand, elaborate|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *apothegmaticus, from Greek apothegmatikos "of or pertaining to an aphorism," from apothegma (see aphorism).  The ending -ize comes from French -iser and ultimately from Greek -izein.
