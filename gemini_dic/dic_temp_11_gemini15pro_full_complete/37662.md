compactedness's:/kəmˈpæktɪdnəsɪz/ | n. |紧密性，紧凑性|The state of being compacted; the degree to which something is compact. |The compactedness's of the soil affected the growth of the plants. 土壤的紧实度影响了植物的生长。|Synonyms: density, solidity, firmness|Antonyms: looseness, porosity, sponginess|noun

Etymology:
From compacted + -ness + -'s (possessive)
compacted: past participle of compact (verb), from Latin compactus, past participle of compangere "to fasten together," from com- "together" + pangere "to fix, fasten."
-ness: suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
-'s: possessive suffix.
