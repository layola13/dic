inboard:/ˈɪnbɔːrd/| adj. adv.|内部的，舷内的；在船内；向内；|Located or used within the hull or fuselage of a vehicle, especially a ship or aircraft.  Positioned closer to the centreline of a vehicle.|The inboard engine provides more cabin space. 舷内发动机提供了更多的舱内空间。|The boat's inboard motor was quiet and efficient. 这艘船的舷内发动机安静高效。|近义词：internal, inside, inner|反义词：outboard, external|词性:形容词,副词

inboard:/ˈɪnbɔːrd/| adv.|向内，在内|Towards the centre or interior.|He turned the steering wheel inboard. 他把方向盘向内转。|She secured the line inboard. 她把绳子固定在船内。|近义词：inward, inwards|反义词：outboard|词性:副词

inboard:/ˈɪnbɔːrd/| n.|内部构件，舷内发动机|The internal parts of something, especially a ship or aircraft. An engine located within the hull of a vessel.|The inboard of the ship was meticulously designed.  这艘船的内部结构是经过精心设计的。|The mechanic inspected the inboard for damage. 技工检查了舷内发动机是否有损坏。|近义词：internals, inner workings|反义词：outboard motor|词性:名词

词源:  From "in" + "board," referring to placement inside the board or side of a ship.
