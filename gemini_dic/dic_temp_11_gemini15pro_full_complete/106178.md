knockabout's:/ˈnɒkəbaʊts/| n.|破旧物品；耐用物品；（尤指）旧车|A knockabout is something, especially a car, that is old and not in very good condition, but is still usable.|"He drives an old knockabout." 他开着一辆老爷破车。|Synonyms: beater, clunker|Antonyms: luxury car, new car|noun


knockabout:/ˈnɒkəbaʊt/| adj.|喧闹的；随便的；粗鲁的|Knockabout describes something, especially a play or film, that is full of noisy activity and often violence, usually intended to be funny. It can also describe someone who is boisterous and not very refined.|"It was a knockabout comedy." 这是一部闹剧喜剧。|Synonyms: boisterous, rowdy, slapstick|Antonyms: refined, sophisticated, gentle|adjective
