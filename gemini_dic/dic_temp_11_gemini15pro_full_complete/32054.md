cherry-picked: /ˈtʃɛriˌpɪkt/ | adj. | 精心挑选的；优中选优的 |  Selectively chosen; chosen with great care, often to present a misleadingly positive impression or to serve a particular purpose, while ignoring less desirable data or individuals.  The term comes from the practice of picking only the best cherries from a tree. | The company's presentation included cherry-picked data that made their performance look much better than it actually was. 这家公司的演示文稿包含了精心挑选的数据，使他们的业绩看起来比实际情况好得多。 |  The coach cherry-picked the best players from different teams to form an all-star team. 教练从不同的队伍中精心挑选了最好的球员组成了一支全明星队。 | 近义词: selective, hand-picked, choice, culled | 反义词: random, unselected, representative, comprehensive | 词性: 形容词
