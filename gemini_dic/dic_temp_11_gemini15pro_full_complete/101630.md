irredeemably:/ˌɪrɪˈdiːməbli/
adjective
无可挽回地；不可救药地
In a way that is impossible to correct, improve, or change.
His reputation was irredeemably damaged by the scandal. 他的名誉被这桩丑闻彻底毁了。

The film was irredeemably awful. 这部电影烂到无可救药。

Synonym: irretrievably, hopelessly, permanently, beyond repair
Antonym: redeemably, retrievably, salvageable
词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From in- (not) + redeemable + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Redeemable" comes from the Old French *redemable and ultimately from the Latin redemptus, past participle of redimere "to buy back, redeem," from re- "back" + emere "to buy, take."
