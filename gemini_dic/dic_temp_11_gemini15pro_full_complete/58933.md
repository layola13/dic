duskish:/ˈdʌskɪʃ/ | adj. | 微暗的，昏暗的 | Somewhat dark; shadowy. | The duskish room felt mysterious.  昏暗的房间感觉很神秘。 |  The duskish sky hinted at an approaching storm.  昏暗的天空预示着暴风雨即将来临。| synonyms: dim, shadowy, twilight, gloomy | antonyms: bright, light, luminous | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From dusk + -ish.  Dusk (Old English *dux, related to Dutch duyster "dark") refers to the period of partial darkness between day and night.  The suffix "-ish" means "somewhat" or "having the quality of."
