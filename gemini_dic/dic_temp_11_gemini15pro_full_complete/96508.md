indo-saracenic:/ˌɪndoʊˌsærəˈsɛnɪk/
| adj.| 印度-撒拉逊式的|A style of architecture that blended Indian and Islamic (especially Mughal) elements, prevalent in India in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.|The Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya in Mumbai is a beautiful example of Indo-Saracenic architecture.  孟买贾特拉帕蒂·希瓦吉·马哈拉杰博物馆是印度-撒拉逊式建筑的一个美丽典范。|Synonyms: Indo-Islamic, Mughal-Gothic|Antonyms: None|adjective
