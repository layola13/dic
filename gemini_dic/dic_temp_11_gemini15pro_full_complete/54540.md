dislike: /dɪsˈlaɪk/ | v. n. | 不喜欢，厌恶 | To have or feel distaste for; to regard with aversion or displeasure. | I dislike strong cheese. 我不喜欢味道很浓的奶酪。 |  She dislikes being told what to do. 她不喜欢别人告诉她该做什么。 | 近义词: hate, detest, abhor, loathe | 反义词: like, love, enjoy, admire | 词性:动词 & 名词

词源:
From dis- (expressing negation) + like (to be pleased with).  The prefix "dis-" signifies negation or reversal, modifying the base verb "like."  The word "like" itself has its roots in Old English *līcian, meaning "to please."
