come-in-for:/kʌm ˈɪn fɔːr/ | phrasal verb | 遭受；受到（批评或责备）；获得；得到 | to receive something, usually something unpleasant, or to be subjected to something |  The government has come in for a lot of criticism over its handling of the economy. 政府因其处理经济的方式受到了很多批评。| Synonyms: receive, experience, get, be subjected to | Antonyms: avoid, escape, evade | verb phrase

come-in-for:/kʌm ˈɪn fɔːr/ | phrasal verb |  获得；得到（奖赏、遗产等）| to receive something, usually something positive, like praise, an inheritance, or a prize | He came in for a large sum of money when his uncle died. 他叔叔去世时，他得到了一大笔钱。 | Synonyms: inherit, receive, obtain, gain | Antonyms: lose, forfeit | verb phrase

词源： "come in for" 最初与航海有关，指船只进港以获取某些东西，例如货物或补给。后来，这个短语的含义扩展到包括获得或接受任何东西，无论是好的还是坏的。
