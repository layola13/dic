ballaster:/ˈbæləstər/ | n. |压舱物，镇流器|A heavy substance, such as gravel, sand, iron, or water, placed low in a vessel to improve its stability.  A device that regulates the current in fluorescent lamps.|例句：The ship took on ballaster in port to prepare for the rough seas.  这艘船在港口装载压舱物，为应对汹涌的大海做准备。

例句：The flickering fluorescent light indicated a faulty ballaster. 闪烁的荧光灯表明镇流器有问题。|近义词：counterweight, stabilizer (for stability sense); ballast resistor (for electrical sense)|反义词: (No direct antonym exists, depends on context) |词性:noun
