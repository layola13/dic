brainwashes:/ˈbreɪnwɒʃɪz/ (British), /ˈbreɪnwɑːʃɪz/ (American)| v. (third-person singular simple present brainwashes, present participle brainwashing, simple past and past participle brainwashed)| 洗脑 |To force someone to believe something by repeatedly telling them that it is true and preventing them from hearing other points of view.| The cult leader brainwashes his followers into believing he is a god.  邪教头目给他的追随者洗脑，让他们相信他是个神。| synonyms: indoctrinates, propagandizes, conditions | antonyms: deprograms, enlightens | verb

brainwashing:/ˈbreɪnwɒʃɪŋ/ (British), /ˈbreɪnwɑːʃɪŋ/ (American)| n. | 洗脑 |The act or process of brainwashing someone.|  The government's brainwashing campaign was successful in suppressing dissent. 政府的洗脑运动成功地压制了异议。| synonyms: indoctrination, propaganda, conditioning | antonyms: deprogramming, education | noun
