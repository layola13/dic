chrismatory: /ˈkrɪzmətɔːri/ /ˈkrɪzməˌtɔri/
n.
圣油瓶；圣油室
A vessel for holding chrism (holy oil); a room or place where chrism is kept.
The priest took the chrism from the chrismatory.  神父从圣油瓶里取出圣油。
The chrismatory was located near the altar. 圣油室位于祭坛附近。
synonyms: chrismarium, ampulla
antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a physical object.  Potentially antonyms for its function could be "secular container," but this is highly contextual.)
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *chrismatorium, from Latin chrisma (“anointing oil”), from Ancient Greek χρῖσμα (khrîsma, “anointment, unguent”), from χρίω (khríō, “I anoint”).
