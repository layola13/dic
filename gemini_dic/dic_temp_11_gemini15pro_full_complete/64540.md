errantia:/ɪˈrænʃɪə/ | n. |  wandering or mobile animals; errant polychaetes | Wandering or mobile animals, especially a class of polychaete worms that move about freely. |  The errantia, unlike their sedentary counterparts, are active foragers.  Errant polychaetes are known for their diverse lifestyles, from scavenging to predation. | 近义词: mobile organisms, free-living animals | 反义词: sedentary organisms, sessile animals | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *errant- (stem of errāns), present participle of errāre (“to wander, stray, roam, err”).
