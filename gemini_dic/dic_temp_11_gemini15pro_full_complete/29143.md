casemate:/ˈkeɪsmeɪt/ | n. |炮台；装甲炮塔；防空洞；（尤指军舰上的）炮位|A fortified gun emplacement, often part of a larger fortification.  A protected structure, often underground, designed to house and protect artillery or other military equipment.| The soldiers took cover in the casemate during the bombardment.| 近义词: bunker, emplacement, pillbox | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, possibly open field) | noun

Etymology->
From French casemate, from Italian casamatta, from casa "house" + matta "dark, dull".  Ultimately from Latin casa "hut, cottage".
