desmobacteria:/ˌdɛzmoʊbækˈtɪəriə/ | n. | 厌氧细菌，脱硫细菌 | A type of anaerobic bacteria involved in the reduction of sulfur compounds, particularly sulfates, to sulfides.  They play a key role in the sulfur cycle in anaerobic environments. | Desmobacteria are crucial for the sulfur cycle in marine sediments. 脱硫细菌在海洋沉积物的硫循环中至关重要。

Desmobacteria can produce hydrogen sulfide, which has a characteristic rotten egg smell. 脱硫细菌可以产生硫化氢，它有一种特有的臭鸡蛋味。 |  Synonym: sulfate-reducing bacteria (SRB) | Antonym:  sulfur-oxidizing bacteria | Noun
