nobly:/ˈnoʊbli/| adv.|高贵地；崇高地；出色地|In a noble manner; with high moral principles, dignity, or excellence.  It describes actions or qualities that are worthy of admiration and respect.|He bore his suffering nobly. 他高贵地忍受着痛苦。|Synonyms: honorably, courageously, gallantly, virtuously, admirably|Antonyms: basely, ignobly, dishonorably, meanly|adverb

Etymology:
From Middle English *nobleliche*, equivalent to *noble* + *-ly*.  *Noble* ultimately derives from Latin *nobilis* "well-known, famous, high-born, excellent," from *noscere* "to come to know."
