fodientia:/foʊˈdɛnʃiə/ /fɒˈdɛnʃiə/
noun
啮齿目动物
Rodents; an order of mammals characterized by strong constantly growing incisors in both upper and lower jaws. These teeth are used for gnawing.
Rodents such as squirrels and mice belong to the order Fodientia.  像松鼠和老鼠这样的啮齿动物属于啮齿目。
Synonyms: Rodentia, Glires
Antonyms: None (as it's a taxonomic classification)
