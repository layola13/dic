arthropoda:/ɑːrˈθrɒpədə/ | n. |节肢动物门|A large phylum of invertebrate animals that includes insects, arachnids, crustaceans, and myriapods, characterized by a segmented body, jointed appendages, and an exoskeleton.|Insects, spiders, and crabs belong to the phylum Arthropoda. 昆虫、蜘蛛和螃蟹都属于节肢动物门。|Synonyms:  invertebrate,  |Antonyms: vertebrate |noun


Etymology->
From Ancient Greek ἄρθρον (árthron, “joint”) + πούς (poús, “foot”).
