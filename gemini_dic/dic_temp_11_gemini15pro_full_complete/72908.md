fomenter:/ˈfoʊˌmɛntər/ | n. |煽动者，教唆者 |Someone who stirs up or encourages (usually negative things like trouble or rebellion). | The fomenter of the rebellion was quickly arrested.  煽动叛乱的人很快就被逮捕了。|Synonyms: agitator, instigator, provocateur | Antonyms: peacemaker, mediator, conciliator | noun

foment:/foʊˈmɛnt/ | vt. |煽动，助长，激起 | To stir up or incite (negative things like trouble or rebellion). |  The politician was accused of fomenting unrest.  这位政客被指控煽动骚乱。|  The hot weather fomented the spread of the disease. 炎热的天气助长了疾病的传播。 | Synonyms: incite, instigate, provoke, abet | Antonyms: quell, suppress, allay, mitigate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French fomenter "to apply a warm lotion; cherish, encourage," from Late Latin fomentare "to bathe with warm lotions, cherish, encourage," from Latin fomentum "warm application, poultice," from fovere "to warm, cherish."
