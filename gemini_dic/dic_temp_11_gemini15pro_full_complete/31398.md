charged-up:/ˈtʃɑːrdʒd ʌp/ | adj. | 充满活力的，兴奋的，激动的 | Filled with energy and enthusiasm; excited. | The team was charged-up after their big win. 球队在大胜后充满了活力。|  The crowd was charged-up for the concert.  观众对这场音乐会非常兴奋。| energized, enthusiastic, excited, thrilled, pumped |  lethargic, apathetic, listless, depressed | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "charge" (v.) meaning to fill with energy + "up" (adv.) indicating completion or intensification. The verb "charge" has roots in Old French "chargier" and ultimately Latin "carricare," meaning "to load a cart."
