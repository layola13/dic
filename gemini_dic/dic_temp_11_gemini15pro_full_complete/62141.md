enclavement: /ɪnˈkleɪvmənt/ /ɛnˈklevmənt/
noun
包围，被包围的状态; 孤立
The state of being enclosed or isolated, especially politically or geographically.
The enclavement of the small nation made trade difficult.  这个小国家的被包围状态使得贸易变得困难。
The city's enclavement within enemy territory made it vulnerable. 这座城市被包围在敌方领土内，使其变得脆弱。
近义词: enclosure, isolation, confinement
反义词: freedom, liberation, openness
词性: n.


enclave: /ˈɑːŋkleɪv/ /ˈɛŋkleɪv/
noun
飞地；孤岛；被包围的领土
A portion of territory within or surrounded by a larger territory whose inhabitants are culturally or ethnically distinct.
The ethnic enclave celebrated its unique traditions. 这个少数民族聚居地庆祝其独特的传统。
The wealthy enclave was known for its exclusive shops and restaurants.  这个富人区以其高档商店和餐厅而闻名。
近义词: territory, region, district
反义词: mainstream, surrounding area
