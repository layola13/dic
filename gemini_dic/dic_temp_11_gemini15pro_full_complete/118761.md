metallicity:/mɛtəˈlɪsɪti/| n. |金屬豐度；金屬量|The proportion of matter in a star or other celestial object that is made up of chemical elements other than hydrogen and helium.  It is a measure of how rich a star is in elements heavier than hydrogen and helium.|The metallicity of the Sun is about 1.4%. 太阳的金屬豐度約為 1.4%。|The astronomers measured the metallicity of the distant galaxy. 天文學家測量了遙遠星系的金屬豐度。|Near synonyms: abundance of heavy elements, chemical composition|Near antonyms: low metallicity|noun
