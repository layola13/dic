gigs:/ɡɪɡz/| n. |演出；零工；临时工作|A job, especially one that is temporary or that has a short performance.  A live performance by a musician or group.|例句：She's got a gig playing at a club tonight. 她今晚在一个俱乐部有演出。

例句：He picked up a few gigs doing data entry to earn extra money. 他接了一些数据录入的零工来赚外快。|近义词：job, performance, concert, show|反义词: unemployment, inactivity|词性:名词

gigs:/ɡɪɡz/| v. |(使)轻快地摇摆；轻敲|To equip with a gig (a light, two-wheeled carriage).  To fish with a gig (a type of spear). (These verb senses are now archaic.)|例句：The old carriage gigged along the cobblestone street. (Archaic) 老式马车沿着鹅卵石街道轻快地行驶。

例句：He gigged the fish with a swift motion. (Archaic) 他迅速地用鱼叉叉鱼。|近义词：jiggle, bounce, tap|反义词: - |词性:动词
