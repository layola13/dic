lash:/læʃ/| n. v. | 睫毛；鞭打；猛击；捆绑 |The sudden rapid movement of something like a whip or the flexible part of something.  It can also refer to eyelashes.  As a verb, it means to strike with a whip or to criticize harshly. | The lash of the waves against the rocks was deafening. 海浪拍打岩石的声音震耳欲聋。 | synonyms: whip, flog, strike, thrash, beat,  eyelashes | antonyms: caress, soothe | noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English lasshen, from Old French lachier,  probably of Germanic origin. Related to Old Norse leka "to drip, leak."  The sense of “eyelash” is from the resemblance to the fringes of a whip.
