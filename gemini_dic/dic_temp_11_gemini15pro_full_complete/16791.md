bascule:/ˈbæskjuːl/ /bæsˈkuːl/| n. v.|桥，跷桥；倾斜；翻转|A device or structure like a seesaw, used for raising and lowering something heavy, especially a bridge section or the entire bridge.|The Tower Bridge in London uses a bascule mechanism to allow ships to pass.  伦敦塔桥使用一个跷桥机构让船只通行。|v.  To tip or tilt (something) like a bascule.|The crane basculed the container onto the truck. 起重机将集装箱翻转到卡车上。|近义词：tilt, tip, seesaw, lever|反义词: steady, fix|词性: 名词, 动词

Etymology: From French *bascule*, from Old French *basculer* "to rock, seesaw," from *basculier* "seesaw," from *bas* "low" + *cul* "bottom."
