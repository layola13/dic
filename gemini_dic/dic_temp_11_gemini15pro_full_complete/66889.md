expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/ | adj. n. | 有利的，权宜之计的；权宜之计 | (of an action) convenient and practical although possibly improper or immoral. |  It is expedient to silence the child with a sweet.  用糖果让孩子安静下来是权宜之计。 | 近义词: advantageous, convenient, practical, useful, politic, judicious | 反义词: inexpedient, disadvantageous, inappropriate | 词性: adjective, noun

expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/ | n. | 权宜之计；应急手段 | a means of attaining an end, especially one that is convenient but considered improper or immoral. |  The government resorted to various expedients to raise funds. 政府采取各种权宜之计来筹集资金。 | 近义词: measure, means, resort, resource, device, shift | 反义词:  | 词性: noun


词源:  From Late Middle English *expedient, from Anglo-Norman and Old French expedient, from Latin expediēns, expedient- (“disentangling, extricating, setting free; developing, furthering; useful, advantageous”), present participle of expediō (“I disentangle, extricate, set free; dispatch, expedite; prepare, make ready”), from ex (“out of, from”) + pēs, ped- (“foot”)
