nonlugubriously: /ˌnɒnlʊˈɡuːbriəsli/ | adv. | 不悲伤地，不忧郁地 |  In a manner that is not sad or mournful.  It describes an action or state that is free from gloom or sorrow. |  She accepted the bad news nonlugubriously, surprising everyone with her composure. 她不悲伤地接受了这个坏消息，她镇定的态度让每个人都感到惊讶。| Synonyms: cheerfully, brightly, merrily, joyfully | Antonyms: lugubriously, mournfully, sadly, gloomily | adverb

Etymology:
* non- (prefix):  Latin origin, meaning "not"
* lugubrious (adjective):  From Latin "lugubris" meaning "mournful," ultimately from "lugeo," meaning "to mourn."
* -ly (suffix):  Forms adverbs from adjectives.
