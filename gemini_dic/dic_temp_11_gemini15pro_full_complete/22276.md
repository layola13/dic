boneset:/ˈboʊnˌsɛt/| n.|  彻底骨, 藤茎泽兰; |A North American plant (Eupatorium perfoliatum) of the daisy family, having hairy leaves and clusters of small white flowers. It was traditionally used medicinally to treat fevers. |Early settlers used boneset tea to treat fevers, hence the name. 早期定居者用彻底骨茶来治疗发烧，因此得名。|Synonyms: thoroughwort, agueweed |Antonyms: N/A| noun
