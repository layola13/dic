dig-out:/ˈdɪɡ aʊt/ | n. | 挖掘；挖出的东西；住所 |A place to live, often small and basic. Also, the act of unearthing something. |例句：After the avalanche, rescuers worked tirelessly to dig out the trapped skiers. 雪崩过后，救援人员不知疲倦地工作，将被困的滑雪者挖出来。

例句：They lived in a small dig-out in the side of the mountain. 他们住在山坡上的一个小洞穴里。| 近义词：excavation, unearthing, home, shelter, abode | 反义词：burial, interment | 词性：名词

dig-out:/dɪɡ ˈaʊt/ | vt. | 挖掘；发现；搜寻出 |To remove something by digging; to unearth or uncover something. |例句：We had to dig out the car after the snowstorm. 暴风雪过后，我们不得不把车挖出来。

例句：She dug out an old photo album from the attic. 她从阁楼里翻出一本旧相册。| 近义词：excavate, unearth, uncover, find, discover | 反义词：bury, conceal | 词性：动词
