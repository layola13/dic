backdraught:/ˈbækdrɑːft/ (US) /ˈbækdrɔːft/ (UK)| n.|逆火; 反吹; 回燃|A backdraught is a rush of air into a confined space, such as a room or a chimney, caused by a sudden drop in pressure. It often occurs in fires when a door or window is opened, allowing oxygen to rush in and ignite unburnt gases, causing an explosion.|
The firefighters were injured by the backdraught. 消防员被逆火烧伤。

A sudden gust of wind caused a backdraught in the chimney. 一阵突如其来的狂风导致烟囱里发生了回燃。|
近义词：backdraft, blowback|
