moonshot:/ˈmuːnʃɒt/ (UK) /ˈmuːnʃɑːt/ (US)| n.|登月，极具挑战的目标，远大的目标|A project or undertaking, especially in the field of technology, that is ambitious and ground-breaking, but also difficult to achieve and possibly risky.  It originally referred to the Apollo 11 mission to land on the moon.  Now it's often used metaphorically to describe any ambitious and innovative project.|例句：The company's new electric car is their latest moonshot. 这家公司的新款电动汽车是他们最新的登月计划。

例句：Developing a cure for cancer is a moonshot, but researchers are making progress.  研制治疗癌症的方法是一个极具挑战性的目标，但研究人员正在取得进展。

|近义词：ambitious project, long shot, risky venture, groundbreaking project|反义词:realistic goal, achievable target|词性:名词
