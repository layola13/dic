coruscate: /ˈkɔːrəskeɪt/ (US) /ˈkɒrəskeɪt/ (UK)
verb
闪耀，闪烁，焕发
To emit flashes of light; sparkle.  To be brilliant or showy in a lively or striking way.
The diamonds coruscated in the sunlight. 钻石在阳光下闪耀。
Her wit coruscated throughout the evening. 她的才智整晚都闪耀着。
近义词: glitter, sparkle, gleam, shimmer, flash, dazzle
反义词: dull, dim
词性: verb

coruscation: /ˌkɔːrəˈskeɪʃn/ (US) /ˌkɒrəˈskeɪʃn/ (UK)
noun
闪耀，闪烁
A sudden flash of light; a sparkle. A brilliant display of wit or intelligence.
The coruscation of the fireworks lit up the night sky. 烟花的闪耀照亮了夜空。
His speech was filled with coruscations of wit. 他的演讲充满了智慧的闪光。
近义词: glitter, sparkle, gleam, shimmer, flash, dazzle
反义词: dullness, dimness
词性: noun

词源: From Latin *coruscare, frequentative of coruscus (“shaking, quivering”).
