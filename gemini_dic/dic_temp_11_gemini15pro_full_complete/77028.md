gargoyles:/ˈɡɑːrɡɔɪlz/| n. |滴水嘴兽，怪兽状雕饰|A carved stone grotesque figure, often with a spout projecting from the mouth, typically used as an ornament or a water spout on a building.|The gargoyles on the cathedral glared down at the city. 大教堂上的滴水嘴兽俯视着城市。|例句：The gargoyles on the old church were covered in moss. 老教堂上的滴水嘴兽上覆盖着苔藓。|近义词：grotesques, chimeras|反义词:None|词性:noun

gargoyle:/ˈɡɑːrɡɔɪlz/| n. |怪兽，丑陋的人|A grotesque or fantastic figure.|He looked like a gargoyle with his hunched back and sharp features. 他弓着背，五官尖锐，看起来像个怪兽。|例句：The villain in the movie was a true gargoyle. 电影里的反派是个十足的怪兽。|近义词：monster, ogre|反义词:beauty|词性:noun
