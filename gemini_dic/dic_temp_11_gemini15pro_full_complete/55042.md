disquietness:/dɪsˈkwaɪətnəs/
noun
不安，焦虑，担忧
A state of uneasiness or anxiety.
The disquietness in her voice betrayed her fear. 她声音里的不安暴露了她的恐惧。
The looming deadline filled him with a sense of disquietness. 逼近的截止日期使他充满了不安。
近义词: unease, anxiety, worry, apprehension, perturbation
反义词: calmness, tranquility, serenity, peace, composure
词性: noun

disquiet:/dɪsˈkwaɪət/
verb
使不安，使担忧
To make someone feel uneasy or anxious.
The news disquieted her. 这消息使她不安。
The strange silence disquieted the villagers.  奇怪的寂静使村民们感到不安。
近义词: worry, disturb, upset, bother, perturb
反义词: calm, soothe, reassure, comfort, pacify
词性: verb

disquiet:/dɪsˈkwaɪət/
adjective (less common)
不安的，担忧的
Feeling or showing unease or anxiety.
He had a disquiet feeling about the whole situation. 他对整个情况有一种不安的感觉。
Her disquiet expression made him wonder what was wrong. 她不安的表情让他想知道出了什么事。
近义词: uneasy, anxious, worried, apprehensive, perturbed
反义词: calm, tranquil, serene, peaceful, composed
词性: adjective


词源: From Middle English *disquiete, from Old French desqueit, from des- (expressing negation) + queit (quiet).
