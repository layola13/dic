blubbering: /ˈblʌbərɪŋ/ | n. v. adj. | 哭诉，抽泣，（因哭泣）说话含糊不清 |The act of crying noisily and uncontrollably, often with accompanying incoherent speech.  |
例句：The child was blubbering after he scraped his knee.  孩子擦伤了膝盖后哭个不停。

例句：He tried to explain, but his words were lost in his blubbering. 他想解释，但他的话被他哭泣的声音淹没了。


近义词：crying, sobbing, weeping, wailing |
反义词：laughing, giggling, rejoicing |
词性：Noun (Gerund), Verb (Present Participle), Adjective


blubber: /ˈblʌbər/ | n. v. |  鲸脂；哭诉，抽泣 |Thick layer of fat under the skin of whales and other sea mammals; to cry noisily and uncontrollably. |
例句：Whales rely on blubber for insulation. 鲸鱼依靠鲸脂保暖。

例句：Stop blubbering and tell me what happened. 别哭了，告诉我发生了什么事。


近义词：(for v.) cry, sob, weep, wail |
反义词：(for v.) laugh, giggle, rejoice |
词性：Noun, Verb
