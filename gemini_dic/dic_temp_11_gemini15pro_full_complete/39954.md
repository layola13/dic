constructivist: /kənˈstrʌktɪvɪst/ | n. adj. | 建构主义者，建构主义的 |  A constructivist is someone who believes that learning is an active process where learners construct their own understanding of the world through experiences and reflection.  As an adjective, it describes things related to this theory. |  Example: Piaget was a prominent constructivist. 皮亚杰是一位著名的建构主义者。| Synonym: experiential, constructionist | Antonym: behaviorist, objectivist | Noun, Adjective

constructivism: /kənˈstrʌktɪvɪzəm/ | n. | 建构主义 | Constructivism is a theory of learning that emphasizes the learner's active role in constructing meaning and knowledge. | Example: Constructivism has influenced modern educational practices. 建构主义影响了现代教育实践。| Synonym: constructionism | Antonym: behaviorism, objectivism | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *constructīvus* "built, piled up," ultimately from *con*- "together" + *struere* "to pile up, build."  The suffix "-ist" denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or theory.
