cavalierism:/ˌkævəˈlɪərɪzəm/ | n. | 骑士精神；傲慢态度；轻蔑 | Disdainful or offhand treatment; haughty disregard of others or of accepted standards.  A swashbuckling attitude or way of behaving. | He treated the issue with cavalierism, dismissing concerns with a wave of his hand.  他以傲慢的态度对待这个问题，挥手 dismiss 了大家的担忧。|  The cavalierism of the young knight offended the king. 年轻骑士的傲慢无礼冒犯了国王。 | synonyms: arrogance, disdain, haughtiness, superciliousness, condescension, contempt, scorn, disregard, indifference, nonchalance | antonyms: humility, modesty, respect, deference, courtesy, consideration | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cavalier*, "horseman," from Italian *cavaliere*, from Late Latin *caballarius*, from Latin *caballus*, "horse."  The sense of "disdainful" developed in the 17th century, likely associated with the perceived arrogance of some Royalist cavalry officers (Cavaliers) during the English Civil War.
