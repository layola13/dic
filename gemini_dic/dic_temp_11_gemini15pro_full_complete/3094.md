aeroplanes:/ˈɛərəpleɪnz/ (UK), /ˈɛərpleɪnz/ (US)| n. |飞机|A powered flying vehicle with fixed wings and a weight greater than that of the air it displaces.|Example:  Two aeroplanes were circling the airport, waiting to land. 两架飞机在机场上空盘旋，等待着陆。

Example:  He's always been fascinated by aeroplanes and how they work. 他一直对飞机以及它们的工作原理着迷。|近义词：airplane, aircraft, plane|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

Etymology: From Greek ἀήρ (aḗr, “air”) + Latin planus ("flat, level").  The word was first attested in English in 1866, spelled "aeroplane."  The spelling "airplane" became more common in the United States in the early 20th century.
