co-optation:/koʊˌɑːpˈteɪʃən/
|n.|拉拢；吸纳；指派；增选|The process of adding members to a select group, often by the invitation of existing members, especially to neutralize opposition or gain support.  It can also refer to the appropriation or assimilation of something, like an idea or cultural practice, by a dominant group.|The co-optation of the rebel leader into the government surprised everyone.  将反叛领导人吸纳进政府让每个人都感到惊讶。|近义词：absorption, assimilation, incorporation, appropriation|反义词：exclusion, rejection|noun

co-opt:/koʊˈɑːpt/
|vt.|拉拢；吸纳；指派；增选|To appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members.  Also, to appropriate or assimilate something, like an idea or cultural practice, for one's own use.|The company co-opted several community leaders onto its board of directors. 公司吸纳了几位社区领导加入其董事会。|近义词：enlist, recruit, incorporate|反义词：exclude, reject|verb
