miscaptioning:/ˌmɪskæpʃənɪŋ/
n.|错误的字幕，配错的标题|The act of providing an incorrect or misleading caption for an image, video, or other media. |The museum curator was embarrassed by the miscaptioning of the ancient artifact.  博物馆馆长对古代文物的错误标题感到尴尬。|incorrect captioning, wrong captioning|accurate captioning, correct captioning|noun

miscaption:/ˌmɪskæpʃən/
v.|配错标题，加错字幕|To provide an incorrect or misleading caption for. |I accidentally miscaptioned the photo on Instagram. 我不小心在Instagram上给照片配错了标题。|caption incorrectly, label wrongly|caption correctly, label accurately|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
mis- (prefix): From Old English *mis-* meaning "wrongly," "badly," or "unfavorably." Related to Old High German *miss-* and Gothic *missa-*.
caption (noun/verb): From Old French *capcion* "a taking, capture," from Latin *captionem* (nominative *captio*), "a taking, seizing," from *capere* "to take, seize."
