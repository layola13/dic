neoimpressionism:/ˌniːoʊɪmˈprɛʃənɪzəm/ | n. | 新印象派，新印象主义 | A late 19th-century movement in French painting, characterized by the systematic use of small dots of color applied to the canvas so that, when viewed from a distance, they created an effect of blended colors and shimmering light.  It is also known as Pointillism. | Example 1: Neoimpressionism, with its emphasis on scientific color theory, marked a departure from traditional Impressionism.  新印象派强调科学的色彩理论，标志着与传统印象派的背离。

Example 2: Seurat and Signac are considered the leading figures of Neoimpressionism.  修拉和西涅克被认为是新印象派的主要人物。 | 近义词:Pointillism, Divisionism | 反义词:Impressionism (although it evolved from it) | 词性:noun
