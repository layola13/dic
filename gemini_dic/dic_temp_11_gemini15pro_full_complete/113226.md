lusitanian:/ˌluːsɪˈteɪniən/| n.|葡萄牙人|A native or inhabitant of Lusitania, an ancient Roman province roughly corresponding to modern Portugal.|例句：The Lusitanians were known for their fierce resistance to Roman rule. 葡萄牙人以其对罗马统治的强烈抵抗而闻名。


|例句：Many Lusitanians emigrated to Brazil during the colonial period.  许多葡萄牙人在殖民时期移民到巴西。|近义词：Portuguese|反义词: N/A|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Lusitānus*, from *Lusitānia* (the Roman name for the region), of uncertain origin, possibly related to a pre-Roman tribe.
