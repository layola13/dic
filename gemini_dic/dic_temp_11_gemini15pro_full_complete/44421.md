cross-plough:/ˈkrɒs plaʊ/ (UK), /ˈkrɔːs plaʊ/ (US)| v. n.|交叉耕作，交叉犁地|To plough (land) in intersecting furrows, especially as a second ploughing at right angles to the first, to break up the soil more thoroughly.|The farmer decided to cross-plough the field to prepare it for planting.|Synonyms: cross-till, double-plough|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but perhaps) single-plough|verb, noun

cross-plough:/ˈkrɒs plaʊ/ (UK), /ˈkrɔːs plaʊ/ (US)| n.|交叉犁，交叉耕作|The act or process of cross-ploughing; a cross-ploughed field.|The cross-plough left the field looking neatly patterned.|Synonyms: cross-tilling|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym)|noun
