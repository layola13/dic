cavilers:/ˈkævɪlərz/| n.|吹毛求疵的人，挑剔的人|People who raise petty objections or make trivial criticisms.  Those who find fault unnecessarily.|The cavilers complained about the font size on the menu, ignoring the delicious food.|近义词：critics, nitpickers, faultfinders, carpers|反义词:supporters, admirers|词性:名词

词源: From the verb "cavil," which comes from the Old French "caviller," meaning "to mock, jest, or argue." Ultimately derived from the Latin "cavillari," meaning "to jest, banter, or quibble."
