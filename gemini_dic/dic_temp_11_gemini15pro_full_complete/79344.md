gleek: /ɡliːk/ | vi.|幸灾乐祸；取笑；傻笑；（音乐）高音；三音符组|To express joy, especially in a mischievous or mocking manner; in music, to sing in a high pitch or to perform a three-note figure. | She gleeked when she heard he'd failed the exam. 她听到他考试不及格时幸灾乐祸地笑了。| synonyms: gloat, chortle, revel | antonyms: mourn, grieve | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gleken, gleken, from Old English *glēcian, from Proto-Germanic *glikōną (“to shine, glitter”). Cognate with Old Norse glíka (“to shine, glisten”).  The musical sense developed from the sense of "to make a shrill noise."
