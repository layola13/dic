fallow's:/ˈfæloʊz/| n.|休耕地；休耕期| fallow's refers to a period of time when land is left unplanted, typically for a season or more, in order to restore its fertility or to avoid surplus production.  It can also refer to the land itself that is left unplanted.  The word "fallow's" is a possessive form, but often used informally without a following noun, implying "fallow period" or "fallow land".| The farmer left the field fallow's this year to improve the soil. 今年，农民让这块地休耕以改良土壤。| The fallow's extended for two years due to the drought. 由于干旱，休耕期延长了两年。|synonyms: uncultivated, unplanted, idle|antonyms: cultivated, planted, active|noun
