figman:/ˈfɪɡmən/| n.|无花果商人；(旧时俚语)穿着打扮整齐的人|A person who sells figs; (old slang) a smartly dressed person.|He was a figman in the market, always shouting about the quality of his fruit. 他是市场上的无花果商人，总是大声吆喝他的水果质量如何好。| The old gangster, despite his age, still looked like a figman in his tailored suit.  尽管年事已高，这位老流氓穿着定制西装，看起来仍然衣冠楚楚。|近义词：fruit seller, well-dressed man|反义词:ragamuffin|词性:名词


Etymology->
"fig" + "man". "Fig" comes from Old French *figue, from Latin ficus. "Man" comes from Old English mann.  The slang meaning of "a well-dressed man" is likely a metaphorical extension, perhaps implying someone plump and desirable like a ripe fig.
