ophiolatry: /ˌɒfiˈɒlətri/ /ˌoʊfiˈɑːlətri/ | n. | 蛇崇拜；拜蛇教 | The worship of snakes, especially as practiced in some ancient religions. |  Ancient Egyptians practiced a form of ophiolatry, viewing snakes as symbols of healing and protection. 古埃及人实行一种蛇崇拜，将蛇视为治愈和保护的象征。

Ophiolatry was common in several ancient cultures.  蛇崇拜在几个古代文化中很常见。 | 近义词: snake worship | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Greek *ophis* (“snake”) + *-latreia* (“worship”). 源于希腊语 *ophis*（“蛇”）+ *-latreia*（“崇拜”）。
