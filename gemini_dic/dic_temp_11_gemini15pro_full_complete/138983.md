nympha:/ˈnɪmfə/| n. |仙女，蛹|A beautiful young woman, or a mythological spirit of nature imagined as a beautiful maiden inhabiting rivers, woods, or other locations.  In entomology, it refers to the immature form of some insects.|The artist painted a picture of a nympha bathing in a forest stream. 艺术家画了一幅仙女在森林溪流中沐浴的图画。|Synonyms: sprite, fairy, maiden, pupa (in entomology)|Antonyms: hag, crone|Noun

nymph:/nɪmf/| n. |仙女，幼虫|A mythological spirit of nature imagined as a beautiful maiden inhabiting rivers, woods, or other locations. Also, the immature form of some insects, especially one that resembles the adult.|The dragonfly nymph clung to the reed. 蜻蜓幼虫紧紧抓住芦苇。|Synonyms: sprite, fairy, maiden, naiad, larva, pupa|Antonyms: hag, crone|Noun
