marabout:/ˈmærəbuːt/| n.| 北非穆斯林圣人，宗教领袖；秃鹳|A Muslim holy man or religious leader in North Africa; a large stork.|例句：The marabout was consulted for spiritual guidance.  这位圣人被请来提供精神指导。

例句：Marabouts are often seen wading in shallow waters.  秃鹳经常被看到在浅水中涉水。|近义词：saint, holy man (for religious leader); stork, adjutant (for bird)|反义词: sinner (for religious leader) |词性:noun

例句：He believed the marabout would bring him good luck. 他相信这个护身符会给他带来好运。|近义词：amulet, charm, talisman|反义词: curse |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *marabout*, from Arabic *murābiṭ* "one who is stationed (at a frontier post)," participle of *rābaṭa* "to tie, bind, fasten, picket (an animal), to be stationed, garrisoned." The sense shifted from "garrisoned soldier" to "holy man" in North Africa.
