Etymology:
"cross-" (prefix) from Old English "cros" and Old Norse "kross," ultimately from Latin "crux" (cross).  Indicates crossing or intersection.
"countries" (plural noun) from Old French "contree," ultimately from Latin "contra" (against, opposite) + "-ata" (past participle suffix).  Refers to a region or nation.
