jitterier:/ˈdʒɪtəriər/ | adj. | 更紧张的，更抖动的 | More jittery; more nervous or shaky. |  My hands were jitterier than usual before the presentation.  我的手在演讲前比平时更抖了。|  shakier, more nervous, more anxious, more apprehensive | calmer, steadier, more relaxed | adjective


jitteriest:/ˈdʒɪtəriɪst/ | adj. | 最紧张的，最抖动的 | Most jittery; most nervous or shaky. | That was the jitteriest I've ever felt before an exam. 那是我在考试前感觉最紧张的一次。 | shakiest, most nervous, most anxious, most apprehensive | calmest, steadiest, most relaxed | adjective


Etymology:  From "jitter" + "-y" (suffix forming adjectives meaning "characterized by" or "full of") + "-er" (comparative suffix) and "-est" (superlative suffix).  "Jitter" likely originates from a frequentative form of "chitter," imitating a trembling sound.
