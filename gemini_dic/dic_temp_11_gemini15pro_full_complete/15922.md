ballyhoo:/ˈbælihuː/
n.
喧闹；大肆宣传；哗众取宠
A noisy attention-getting demonstration or talk.
The circus came to town with a great deal of ballyhoo. 马戏团带着大量的宣传来到镇上。

v.
大肆宣传；大吹大擂
To promote or publicize showily.
They ballyhooed the new product on television. 他们在电视上大肆宣传新产品。

近义词:hype, publicity, promotion, fanfare, hoopla
反义词:silence, understatement, secrecy
词性:名词，动词


词源:
Likely from the French word "bally" (of unknown origin) + "hoo" (a shout).  Originally a nautical term referring to a clamor or shouting of sailors.
