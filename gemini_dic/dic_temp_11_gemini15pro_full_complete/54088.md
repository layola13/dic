disembarrass:/ˌdɪsɪmˈbærəs/ | vt. | 使摆脱困难；使解脱；使卸下重担 | To free from embarrassment or difficulty; to relieve or rid of something unwanted or burdensome. | He was glad to disembarrass himself of the heavy suitcase. 他很高兴能摆脱那个沉重的行李箱。

She disembarrassed him of his coat. 她帮他脱掉了外套。

| 近义词: relieve, rid, free, disencumber, extricate | 反义词: burden, encumber, hamper | 词性: 动词

disembarrassment:/ˌdɪsɪmˈbærəsmənt/ | n. | 解脱；摆脱困境 | The act of disembarrassing or the state of being disembarrassed; relief from embarrassment or difficulty. |  His disembarrassment was obvious when he finally found his keys.  当他终于找到钥匙时，如释重负的表情显而易见。

The sale of the property brought a welcome disembarrassment from debt. 出售房产使他们摆脱了债务，令人欣慰。

| 近义词: relief, liberation, freedom | 反义词: burden, encumbrance, hindrance | 词性: 名词
