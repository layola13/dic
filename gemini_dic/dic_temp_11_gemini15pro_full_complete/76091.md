furrow:/ˈfɜːroʊ/ (US) /ˈfʌrəʊ/ (UK)| n. v.| 犁沟，皱纹 | A long narrow trench made in the ground by a plow, or a deep wrinkle in the skin, especially on the forehead. |  The farmer plowed deep furrows in the field. 农民在田里犁出深深的犁沟。| synonyms: groove, trench, wrinkle, crease | antonyms: smoothness, ridge | noun
furrow:/ˈfɜːroʊ/ (US) /ˈfʌrəʊ/ (UK)| v.| 犁田，使起皱纹 | To make furrows in something, such as the ground or one's forehead. |  He furrowed his brow in concentration. 他皱着眉头全神贯注。| synonyms: plow, groove, wrinkle, crease | antonyms: smooth, flatten | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *furh, from Proto-Germanic *furχō ("furrow"), from Proto-Indo-European *perkʷ- ("to furrow, scratch"). Cognate with Old Frisian furh ("furrow"), Middle Dutch vore ("furrow"), Old High German furh ("furrow"), German Furche ("furrow"), Old Norse for ("furrow, ditch").
