corporator:/ˈkɔːrpəreɪtər/ (US) /ˈkɔːpəreɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n.|公司成员，法人，市政议员|A member of a corporation, especially a municipal corporation.  A person who is part of a corporate body.|The corporators voted to increase funding for the new project. 公司成员投票决定增加新项目的资金。|synonyms: member, representative, official, councillor|antonyms: individual, non-member|noun

Etymology->

From Late Middle English *corporatour*, from Medieval Latin *corporātor*, from Latin *corporāre* (“to form into a body”), from *corpus* (“body”).
