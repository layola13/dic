moccasin:/ˈmɒkəsɪn/ (US) /ˈmɒkəsɪn/ (UK)| n. |鹿皮鞋；软皮鞋|A soft leather shoe, originally worn by Native Americans, made of deerskin or other soft leather and stitched together at the top.|He wore a pair of comfortable moccasins.|Synonyms: slipper, loafer|Antonyms: boot, shoe|noun

moccasin:/ˈmɒkəsɪn/ (US) /ˈmɒkəsɪn/ (UK)| n. |腹蛇；水蝮蛇（一种毒蛇）|A venomous snake found in the southeastern United States.|The moccasin is a dangerous snake.|Synonyms: cottonmouth, water moccasin|Antonyms: N/A|noun
