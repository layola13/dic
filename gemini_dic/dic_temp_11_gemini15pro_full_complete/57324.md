downtrodden:/ˌdaʊnˈtrɑːdn/| adj.|被压迫的，被蹂躏的|Oppressed or treated badly by people in power.|The downtrodden people finally rose up against the dictator. 被压迫的人民最终起义反抗独裁者。|近义词：oppressed, suppressed, subjugated|反义词：privileged, empowered, liberated|词性:形容词
Etymology: From "down" + "trodden" (past participle of "tread"), literally meaning "trodden down" or "walked over."  The word first appeared in the late 16th century.
