capacitor:/kəˈpæsɪtər/| n. |电容器|A device used to store an electric charge, consisting of one or more pairs of conductors separated by an insulator. |例句：The capacitor stores energy in an electric field. 电容器将能量存储在电场中。

例句：A faulty capacitor can cause the circuit to malfunction.  一个有故障的电容器会导致电路故障。|近义词：condenser |反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps resistor in some contexts) |词性:noun

capacitors:/kəˈpæsɪtərz/| n. |电容器（复数）|Plural form of capacitor.  Multiple devices used to store electric charge. |例句：These capacitors are essential for the smooth operation of the device. 这些电容器对于设备的平稳运行至关重要。

例句：The circuit board contains several different capacitors.  电路板上包含几个不同的电容器。|近义词：condensers |反义词: (No direct antonym) |词性:noun
