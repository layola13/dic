ETYMOLOGY->
From dreary + -ment.  Dreary comes from Old English *dreorig, from *dreor "gore," related to Old High German tror "gore," from Proto-Germanic *dreuzą.  The suffix -ment forms nouns meaning "the result or product of an action" or "the means or instrument of an action."
