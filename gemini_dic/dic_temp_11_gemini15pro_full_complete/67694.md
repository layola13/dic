exulcerate:/ɛkˈsʌlsəreɪt/
verb
溃疡化；使恶化；激怒
To cause or make worse an ulcer. To irritate or make bitter.
The constant criticism exulcerated his already fragile self-esteem.  不断的批评使他原本就很脆弱的自尊心更加恶化。
The politician's inflammatory rhetoric exulcerated tensions between the two countries.  这位政客煽动性的言论加剧了两国之间的紧张关系。
近义词: aggravate, worsen, exacerbate, irritate, inflame
反义词: soothe, heal, alleviate, ameliorate
词性: verb

exulceration:/ɛkˌsʌlsəˈreɪʃən/
noun
溃疡化；恶化；激怒
The process of becoming ulcerated. The state of being ulcerated. A feeling of irritation or bitterness.
The exulceration of the wound required immediate medical attention. 伤口溃烂需要立即就医。
His constant complaints were a sign of his deep exulceration. 他不断的抱怨是他内心深处痛苦的表现。
近义词: aggravation, worsening, exacerbation, irritation, inflammation
反义词: healing, alleviation, amelioration
词性: noun


词源:  From Late Latin *exulceratus, past participle of exulcerare "to make ulcerous," from Latin ex- "out" + ulcerare "to ulcerate," from ulcus "sore, ulcer."
