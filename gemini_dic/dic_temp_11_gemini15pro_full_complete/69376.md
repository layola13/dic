feeble:/ˈfiːbl/| adj.|虚弱的，微弱的，无力的|Lacking physical strength, especially as a result of age or illness; (of a sound) faint; lacking intensity or power. |例句：His voice was feeble and shaky. 他的声音虚弱而颤抖。|例句：She made a feeble attempt to stand. 她无力地试图站起来。|近义词：weak, frail, delicate, faint, ineffective|反义词：strong, robust, powerful, effective|词性:形容词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *feble, *floible (Modern French faible), from Late Latin flebilis "lamentable," from Latin flere "to weep."  The sense evolution is from "causing tears" to "pitiful, wretched" to "weak."
