attest:/əˈtest/ | v. | 证实，证明；表明 | To provide clear proof of something. |  The numerous awards on his wall attest to his skill as a lawyer.  他的墙上挂满了各种奖项，证明了他作为一名律师的精湛技艺。| Synonyms: certify, confirm, corroborate, verify | Antonyms: deny, contradict, refute | verb

attest:/əˈtest/ | v. | 作证，宣誓作证 | To declare something to be true, usually in a formal context like a court of law. |  The witness attested that she had seen the defendant at the scene of the crime. 目击者作证说她看到被告在犯罪现场。 | Synonyms: testify, swear, vouch | Antonyms: perjure | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *attestare*, from ad- (“to, toward”) + testari (“to bear witness, to make a will”), from testis (“witness”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *treis- (“three”). The original notion is of "a third person standing by as a witness."
