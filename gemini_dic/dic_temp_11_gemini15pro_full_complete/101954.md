一般现在时 (Simple Present):
He isn't tall. 他不高。
She isn't a doctor. 她不是医生。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
She isn't sleeping right now. 她现在没在睡觉。
They aren't working on the project anymore. 他们现在没有在做这个项目了。


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
He isn't finished with his homework yet. 他还没有完成作业。
She isn't gone to the store yet. 她还没有去商店。

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
He isn't been working here for very long. 他在这里工作的时间不长。
She isn't been feeling well lately. 她最近感觉不太好。(This usage is less common and often considered grammatically incorrect.  "Hasn't been feeling" is preferred.)


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
He wasn't at home yesterday. 他昨天不在家。
She wasn't happy with the results. 她对结果不满意。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
He wasn't sleeping when I called. 我打电话时他没有睡觉。
They weren't working on the project at that time. 他们当时没有在做这个项目。


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
He hadn't finished his homework before dinner. 他晚饭前没有完成作业。
She hadn't gone to the store before I arrived. 在我到达之前她没有去商店。


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
He hadn't been working there for long before he quit. 他辞职前在那里工作的时间不长。
She hadn't been feeling well for a while before she saw the doctor.  在她去看医生之前，她已经有一段时间感觉不舒服了。(This usage is less common and often considered grammatically incorrect.  "Hadn't been feeling" is preferred.)

PHRASE->
It isn't what it seems. 这不像看起来那样。
It isn't fair! 这不公平！
It isn't my fault. 这不是我的错。
It isn't that easy. 这没那么容易。
He isn't the one. 他不是那个人。


注意事项:

"Ain't" is highly informal and generally considered grammatically incorrect in formal writing and speech.  Avoid using it in professional or academic settings.


The contraction "isn't" is primarily used in spoken and informal written English.  In formal writing, "is not" is generally preferred.
