outscolds:/ˌaʊtˈskoʊldz/
verb
责骂，大声斥责
rebukes or criticizes someone angrily and at length.
She outscolds him for his carelessness. 她因为他粗心而责骂他。
近义词: berates, reprimands, rebukes, chastises, upbraids
反义词: praises, commends, lauds
词性: verb

词源:
From out- + scold.  "Out-" here functions as an intensifier, suggesting surpassing or exceeding in scolding.  Scold itself comes from Old Norse *skald "poet," later meaning "to speak abusively."
