dictatorship:/dɪkˈteɪtərʃɪp/| n. |独裁; 专政|A form of government in which one person or a small group has absolute power and authority.|例句：The country suffered under a brutal dictatorship for decades.  这个国家在残酷的独裁统治下遭受了几十年的苦难。

例句：His dictatorship was marked by oppression and violence. 他的独裁统治以压迫和暴力为标志。|近义词：tyranny, despotism, autocracy|反义词：democracy, republic|词性: 名词

dictator:/dɪkˈteɪtər/| n. |独裁者|A ruler with total power over a country, typically one who has obtained power by force.|例句：The dictator ruled with an iron fist.  独裁者用铁腕统治。

例句：The dictator was overthrown in a popular uprising. 独裁者在人民起义中被推翻。|近义词：tyrant, autocrat, despot|反义词：democrat|词性: 名词

dictate:/dɪkˈteɪt/| v. |口述; 命令; 规定|Say or read aloud (words to be typed, written down, or recorded). / Lay down authoritatively; prescribe.|例句：He dictated a letter to his secretary. 他向秘书口述了一封信。

例句：The rules dictate that all members must attend the meeting. 规则规定所有成员必须参加会议。|近义词：command, order, prescribe|反义词：suggest, request|词性: 动词

dictation:/dɪkˈteɪʃn/| n. |口述; 听写|The action of saying words aloud to be typed, written down, or recorded.|例句：The students had a dictation test in English class. 学生们在英语课上进行了听写测试。

例句：She took dictation from her boss. 她记录了老板的口述。|近义词：transcription|反义词： (No direct antonym)|词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dictator* (a special magistrate in the Roman Republic appointed to govern the state in times of emergency), from *dictare* "to say often, prescribe," frequentative of *dicere* "to say, speak."
