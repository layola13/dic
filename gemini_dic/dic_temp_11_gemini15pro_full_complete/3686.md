aggravatingly: /ˈæɡrəveɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 恼人地，加剧地 |  In a manner that makes something worse or more annoying. | The traffic was moving aggravatingly slowly. 交通恼人地缓慢移动。| 近义词: annoyingly, irritatingly, exasperatingly | 反义词: soothingly, calmingly | 词性: 副词

aggravatingly: /ˈæɡrəveɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 令人恼火地，使病情恶化地 | Making a situation worse or more serious, especially in a way that is annoying. |  He aggravatingly left all the lights on. 他令人恼火地把所有的灯都开着。| 近义词: provokingly, infuriatingly | 反义词: helpfully, beneficially | 词性: 副词

Etymology: From the verb "aggravate," which comes from the Latin "aggravare," meaning "to make heavier." The suffix "-ly" transforms it into an adverb.
