ennoble:/ɪˈnoʊbəl/ |v. |使高贵，使崇高|To give someone a noble rank or title. To make something or someone seem better or more worthy of respect.|Her selfless acts of charity ennobled her in the eyes of the community.  她无私的慈善行为使她在社区民众眼中变得高贵。|Synonyms: elevate, exalt, dignify, uplift|Antonyms: degrade, debase, demean|verb

ennobled:/ɪˈnoʊbəld/ |v. |使高贵，使崇高 (过去式/过去分词)|Past tense and past participle of "ennoble". Refers to the action of having been given a noble rank, or having been made to seem better or more worthy of respect.|He was ennobled by the king for his bravery in battle. 他因在战斗中的英勇而被国王封为贵族。|Synonyms: elevated, exalted, dignified, uplifted|Antonyms: degraded, debased, demeaned|verb
