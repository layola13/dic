bag-of-tricks:/ˌbæɡ əv ˈtrɪks/ | n. |  锦囊妙计；一套技巧；工具包 | A collection of methods or techniques, often clever or unconventional, used to solve problems or achieve goals. | He had a whole bag of tricks for getting his way. 他有一套达到目的的技巧。 | synonyms: repertoire, arsenal, toolkit | antonyms: limitation, deficiency | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
"Bag of tricks" originates from the literal meaning of a bag containing items used by magicians or performers.  Over time, the phrase evolved to represent a collection of resources or skills used to achieve a specific purpose.
