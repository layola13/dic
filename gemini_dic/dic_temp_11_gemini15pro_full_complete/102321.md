ivory-towerisms:/ˈaɪvəri ˈtaʊərɪzəmz/
|n.|象牙塔思想；脱离实际的想法| impractical and unrealistic attitudes or beliefs often associated with academics or intellectuals;  theories or ideas that are developed or discussed without any concern for their practical application or relevance to real-world problems.|Many academics are accused of ivory-towerisms, failing to address real-world issues.|Synonyms:  intellectualism, abstraction, detachment, impracticality|Antonyms: pragmatism, realism, practicality|noun
