countersway:/ˈkaʊntərsweɪ/| n. v.|平衡力，抵消；抗衡；|An opposing weight or force; an action used to offset or oppose another action.|The tightrope walker used a long pole as a countersway to maintain his balance.  走钢丝的人用一根长杆作为平衡杆来保持平衡。|The government implemented new policies as a countersway to the economic downturn. 政府实施了新的政策来应对经济下滑。|近义词：counterbalance, offset, counteract|反义词: sway, impetus, momentum|词性:名词, 动词

Etymology:
From counter- + sway.  "Counter-" signifies "against" or "opposite," and "sway" refers to movement or influence. Thus, "countersway" literally means a sway against another sway.
