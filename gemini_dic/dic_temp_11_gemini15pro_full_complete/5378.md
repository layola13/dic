alluvion:/əˈluːviən/ | n. | 冲积土，冲积层；淤积物 |A deposit of sand, mud, etc., formed by flowing water. |  The fertile alluvion along the riverbanks made for excellent farmland. 河岸边肥沃的冲积土形成了极好的农田。| Synonyms: alluvium, deposit, sediment, silt | Antonyms: erosion, depletion | noun


alluvion:/əˈluːviən/ | n. |  (法律) 淤积地，增积地 | An increase of land along a shore or a riverbank by the action of water, whether from natural or artificial causes. | The farmer gained additional acreage due to the alluvion beside the creek.  由于小溪边的淤积，农民获得了额外的土地。| Synonyms: accretion, increment, augmentation | Antonyms: reduction, diminution | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *alluviōn- (stem of alluviō), from Latin alluere "to wash against," from ad- "to" + -luere (related to lavere "to wash").
