libel:/ˈlaɪbl/ (US), /ˈlaɪbəl/ (UK)| n. vt.|诽谤，中伤；诽谤性文字|A published false statement that is damaging to a person's reputation.|The newspaper was sued for libel after publishing the untrue story. 这家报纸因刊登不实报道而被起诉诽谤。|Synonyms: defamation, slander, calumny|Antonyms: praise, commendation|noun

libel:/ˈlaɪbl/ (US), /ˈlaɪbəl/ (UK)| vt.|诽谤，中伤|To publish a libel against.|The article libelled the politician, falsely accusing him of corruption. 这篇文章诽谤了这位政治家，诬告他腐败。|Synonyms: defame, slander, malign|Antonyms: commend, extol|verb

词源：
From Old French *libelle (noun), libeler (verb), from Latin libellus "little book, pamphlet," diminutive of liber "book."  The pejorative sense developed from the practice of using pamphlets to attack people's reputations.
来自古法语 *libelle（名词），libeler（动词），源自拉丁语 libellus “小册子，小册子”，liber “书”的缩略词。贬义的含义来自于使用小册子攻击人们的声誉的做法。
