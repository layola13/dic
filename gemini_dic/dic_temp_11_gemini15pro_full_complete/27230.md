calmer:/ˈkɑːmər/ (US), /ˈkɑːmə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更平静的，更镇定的|Comparative form of calm: more calm.  Used to describe someone or something that is less agitated, anxious, or excited than before or than something else.|The lake was calmer in the morning. 早晨湖面更平静了。|Near synonyms: more peaceful, more tranquil, quieter, more serene, more relaxed|Near antonyms: more agitated, more frantic, more turbulent, more hectic, wilder|adjective


calm:/ˈkɑːm/ (US), /ˈkɑːm/ (UK)| adj.  n.  v.|平静的；镇静的；（使）平静；（使）镇静|1. adj. not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions; 2. n. a period or condition of freedom from storms, high winds, or rough activity of water; 3. v. to make someone become quiet and relaxed; to stop feeling or showing nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions|The sea was calm after the storm.  暴风雨过后，大海平静了。|Near synonyms: peaceful, tranquil, quiet, serene, relaxed (adj.); stillness, peace, tranquility (n.); soothe, pacify, alleviate (v.)|Near antonyms: agitated, frantic, turbulent, hectic, wild (adj.); storm, turbulence (n.); agitate, incite (v.)|adjective, noun, verb
