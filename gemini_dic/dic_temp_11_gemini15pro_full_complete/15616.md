baillie:/ˈbeɪli/| n.|市政官，区长，地主代理人|A baillie is a magistrate or municipal officer in Scotland.  A baillie can also be a land steward or manager of an estate.|The baillie presided over the local court.  这位市政官主持了地方法庭。|The laird's baillie collected the rents. 地主的代理人收取了租金。|synonyms: magistrate, alderman, steward, factor|antonyms: tenant, subject|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *baillif* (modern French *bailli*), "administrator, bailiff," from Late Latin *bajulivus*, from Latin *bajulus*, "porter, carrier."
