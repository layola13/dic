gybe: /dʒaɪb/ | n. vi. | 转帆；转向；讥讽 | A sailing maneuver in which the stern of the boat passes through the wind, and the boom swings to the other side. Also, a sharp, critical remark. | The captain ordered the crew to gybe as the wind shifted. 船长命令船员随着风向的改变而改变航向。| synonyms: jibe, shift, tack, sneer, taunt | antonyms: praise, compliment | noun, verb

gybes: /dʒaɪbz/ | n. pl. vi. pl. | 转帆；转向；讥讽(复数) | Plural form of "gybe," referring to multiple instances of the sailing maneuver or sharp remarks. | The sudden gybes caught the inexperienced sailors off guard. 突如其来的转向让缺乏经验的水手措手不及。| synonyms: jibes, shifts, tacks, sneers, taunts | antonyms: praises, compliments | noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Dutch gijben, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Middle Low German giben "to give way, yield."
