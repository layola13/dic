choice:/tʃɔɪs/ | n. |选择，抉择；选择权；供选择的东西；入选者；精选品|  An act of choosing between two or more possibilities; something that can be chosen; the right, power, or opportunity to choose. |  She had to make a choice between her career and her family. 她不得不在事业和家庭之间做出选择。| synonyms: option, alternative, selection, preference | antonyms: obligation, necessity | noun

Etymology: From Old French *chois*, past participle of *choisir* "to choose, distinguish, perceive," from Vulgar Latin *causire*, alteration of Latin *capere* "to take, hold, seize."
