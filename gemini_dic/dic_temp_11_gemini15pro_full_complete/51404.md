determinism:/dɪˈtɜːmɪnɪzəm/ | n. | 决定论 | The doctrine that all events, including human action, are ultimately determined by causes external to the will. |  例句：Determinism implies that free will is an illusion. 决定论意味着自由意志是一种幻觉。

|例句：Some philosophers argue against determinism, believing in the power of choice. 一些哲学家反对决定论，相信选择的力量。 | 近义词：fatalism, predestination | 反义词：free will, indeterminism | 词性: noun

Etymology: From late Middle English: via French déterminisme from late Latin determinatio(n-), from determinare ‘to limit, determine’ (see determine).
