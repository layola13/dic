breaths:/breθs/| n.|呼吸; 气息|The act of taking air into and expelling it from the lungs.  The air inhaled or exhaled.|She took a deep breath. 她深吸了一口气。|synonyms: respiration, inhalation, exhalation, gasp, pant|antonyms: exhalation (when referring to inhalation), inhalation (when referring to exhalation)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *bræþas, nominative/accusative plural of bræþ (“breath”).  Cognate with Old High German brādam, Old Norse brað. Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *bræþaz.
