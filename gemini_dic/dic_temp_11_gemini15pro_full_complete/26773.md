caesarism:/ˈsiːzərɪzəm/
n.|凯撒主义；专制统治|A form of government characterized by a strong central ruler, often a dictator, with absolute power.  It implies a concentration of power and suppression of individual liberties, often achieved through populist appeals and military strength. |The historian argued that the country's slide into caesarism began with the erosion of democratic institutions.  这位历史学家认为，该国滑向凯撒主义始于民主制度的侵蚀。|synonyms: autocracy, dictatorship, despotism, tyranny|antonyms: democracy, republic|noun
