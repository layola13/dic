counterpointed:/ˌkaʊntərˈpɔɪntɪd/ | adj. | 对比的，对照的 |  Set in contrast or opposition; having a contrasting element.  Originating from the musical term "counterpoint," meaning a contrasting melody. |  Her vibrant optimism was counterpointed by his quiet pessimism. 她充满活力的乐观主义与他安静的悲观主义形成了对比。 | synonymous: contrasted, juxtaposed, opposing | antonymous: similar, harmonious, agreeing | adjective

counterpoint:/ˈkaʊntərˌpɔɪnt/ | n. | 对比，对照；对位法 |  A contrasting element, theme, or idea; (in music) the art or technique of setting, writing, or playing a melody or melodies in conjunction with another, according to fixed rules. | The dark colors of the painting are a counterpoint to the bright ones.  这幅画的深色与亮色形成对比。 | synonymous: contrast, juxtaposition, antithesis | antonymous: similarity, harmony, agreement | noun
counterpoint:/ˈkaʊntərˌpɔɪnt/ | vt. |  使对比，使对照 | To set in contrast; to place in opposition. | The director counterpointed the violence of the scene with the peaceful music. 导演将场景的暴力与平和的音乐进行了对比。| synonymous: contrast, juxtapose, oppose | antonymous: harmonize, agree, unify | verb
