bogie:/ˈboʊɡi/ (US), /ˈbɒɡi/ (UK)| n.|转向架，轮对组，妖怪，鬼怪|A framework with wheels or runners, especially one supporting a railway vehicle or other heavy load. Also, a source of fear or anxiety, often imaginary.|The train derailed because of a faulty bogie. 火车因为转向架故障而出轨了。|
The children were afraid of the bogie man. 孩子们害怕妖怪。|synonyms: truck (for train), bogeyman, goblin, monster|antonyms:  (no direct antonym for the mechanical sense) comfort, reassurance (for the fear sense)|noun
bogie:/ˈboʊɡi/ (US), /ˈbɒɡi/ (UK)| n.|标准杆数，柏忌|In golf, one stroke over par for a hole.|He got a bogie on the seventh hole. 他在第七洞打出了柏忌。|synonyms: (no direct synonym) |antonyms: birdie, eagle|noun
