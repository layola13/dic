misguidedly:/ˌmɪsˈɡaɪdɪdli/
adverb
误导地；错误引导地
In a misguided way; based on wrong information or bad judgment.
She misguidedly believed that he was telling the truth. 她错误地相信了他所说的是实话。
近义词: mistakenly, erroneously, wrongly, foolishly
反义词: correctly, wisely, prudently
词性: adverb

misguided:/ˌmɪsˈɡaɪdɪd/
adjective
被误导的；判断错误的
Wrong because you have understood or judged something wrongly.
He had a misguided belief that he could fly. 他有一种误导性的想法，认为自己能飞。
近义词: mistaken, erroneous, wrong, foolish
反义词: correct, wise, prudent
词性: adjective

misguide:/ˌmɪsˈɡaɪd/
verb
误导；使误入歧途
To give someone the wrong idea or impression about something, or to lead someone in the wrong direction.
The advertisement misguides the public about the product's effectiveness. 这则广告误导了公众对产品有效性的认识。
近义词: mislead, deceive, delude
反义词: guide, direct, inform
词性: verb

词源:
From mis- + guide + -ed + -ly.  The prefix "mis-" means "wrong" or "badly".  The verb "guide" means "to lead or direct".  The suffix "-ed" forms the past participle and adjective "misguided".  The suffix "-ly" forms the adverb "misguidedly".
