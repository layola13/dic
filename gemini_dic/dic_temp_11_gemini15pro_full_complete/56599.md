donatism:/ˈdɒnətɪzəm/ (US) /ˈdoʊnətɪzəm/| n. |多纳图斯主义|A schismatic movement in the early Christian church (chiefly in North Africa) which held that the validity of sacraments depended on the moral character of the minister and that the church must exclude from its membership those who had lapsed during persecution.|The Donatist schism caused significant disruption within the early Church. 多纳图斯派的分裂给早期教会造成了巨大的破坏。|Synonyms: heresy, schism|Antonyms: orthodoxy, conformity|noun
