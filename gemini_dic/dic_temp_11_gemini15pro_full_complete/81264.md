granitoid: /ˈɡrænɪtɔɪd/
noun|花岗岩类；花岗质岩石|A coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock similar to granite in composition and texture.|The geologist identified the sample as a granitoid.|Synonyms: plutonic rock, igneous rock|Antonyms: sedimentary rock, metamorphic rock|n.


granitoid: /ˈɡrænɪtɔɪd/
adjective|花岗岩状的；花岗质的|Resembling granite in composition, texture, or appearance.|The granitoid rocks formed the core of the mountain range.|Synonyms: granitic, granite-like|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but could use terms like "non-granitic" or specific names of other rock types like "basaltic")|adj.


ETYMOLOGY->
From granite + -oid.  "Granite" comes from the Italian word "granito," meaning "grained," ultimately from the Latin "granum," meaning "grain." The suffix "-oid" means "resembling" or "like."
