gazin: /ˈɡeɪzɪn/ | n. | 注视，凝视 |  Likely a misspelling or informal shortening of "gazing," the present participle of the verb "gaze."  "Gaze" means to look steadily and intently at something, typically for a prolonged period and with a fixed expression. |  "He was gazin' out the window, lost in thought." 他凝视着窗外，陷入了沉思。 | 近义词：staring, peering, ogling | 反义词：glancing, ignoring | 词性:  Derived from verb (present participle acting nominally)

词源:
From Middle English *gasen, from Old Norse *gasa (“to gape, stare”). Cognate with Swedish gasa (“to stare”). More at gaze.
