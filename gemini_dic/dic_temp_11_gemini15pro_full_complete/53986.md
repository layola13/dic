discretions:/dɪˈskreʃənz/
n.
谨慎，慎重；自行决定的自由；判断力
The ability to behave without causing embarrassment or attracting too much attention, especially by keeping information secret.  The freedom or power to decide what should be done in a particular situation.
The investigation is being conducted with the utmost discretion. 调查正在极其谨慎地进行。
I leave the matter to your discretion. 我把这件事交给你自行处理。
近义词:prudence, circumspection, caution, judgment, wisdom
反义词:indiscretion, imprudence, recklessness
词性:名词

discreet:/dɪˈskriːt/
adj.
谨慎的，慎重的；不显眼的
Careful and prudent in one's speech or actions, especially in order to keep something confidential or to avoid embarrassment.  Intentionally unobtrusive.
He was always very discreet about his love affairs. 他对自己的风流韵事总是非常谨慎。
a discreet cough  轻轻的咳嗽
近义词:careful, cautious, prudent, circumspect, tactful
反义词:indiscreet, imprudent, reckless
词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "discrētiō" meaning "separation, distinction, discernment," from "discernere"  "to separate, distinguish."
