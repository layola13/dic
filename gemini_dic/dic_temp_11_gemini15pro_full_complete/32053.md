cherry-pick: /ˈtʃɛri pɪk/ | v. | 挑选；精选 | To select the best or most desirable items from a larger group, often ignoring the less desirable ones. |  She cherry-picked the best strawberries from the basket. 她从篮子里挑了最好的草莓。

cherry-pick: /ˈtʃɛri pɪk/ | v. | 择优挑选（尤指不公平地） | To selectively choose (the most beneficial items, information, etc.) from what is available. | The company was accused of cherry-picking data to support its claims.  该公司被指责择优挑选数据来支持其主张。

cherry-pick: /ˈtʃɛri pɪk/ | n. | 精选; 择优挑选 | The act of selecting the best or most desirable items from a larger group. | The report was based on a cherry-pick of the available evidence. 这份报告是基于对现有证据的精选。


近义词: select, handpick, cull, cream off
反义词: accept indiscriminately, take everything

词性: verb, noun
