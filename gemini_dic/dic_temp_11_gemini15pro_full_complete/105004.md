kenogenesis:/ˌkɛnəˈdʒɛnɪsɪs/
n.
新生性发生，后成性发生
The development of new characters or organs in an organism through evolutionary processes not present in its ancestors.  A modified recapitulation in which certain stages in the development of an organism are accelerated or omitted due to adaptive changes.
Kenogenesis is seen in the development of the mammalian heart, which bypasses several stages found in fish.  哺乳动物心脏的发育中可以看到新生性发生，它绕过了鱼类心脏发育中的几个阶段。
neoteny, paedomorphosis
recapitulation, palingenesis
noun
