flagellum:/fləˈdʒɛləm/ (plural flagella: /fləˈdʒɛlə/) | n. |鞭毛|A slender threadlike structure, especially a microscopic whiplike appendage that enables many protozoa, bacteria, spermatozoa, etc. to swim.|The bacterium uses its flagellum to propel itself through the liquid.  这种细菌用它的鞭毛在液体中推进自己。|Synonyms: cilium, flagella |Antonyms: N/A | noun

flagellate:/ˈflædʒəleɪt/ | vt. |鞭打，鞭笞|To whip or flog; to punish by whipping.|Some cultures still flagellate people as a form of punishment.  一些文化仍然以鞭笞作为一种惩罚形式。|Synonyms: whip, flog, scourge, lash |Antonyms: soothe, comfort | verb

flagellate:/ˈflædʒəleɪt/ | adj. |鞭状的，有鞭毛的|Having flagella.| Many flagellate protozoa live in freshwater. 许多鞭毛原生动物生活在淡水中。 |Synonyms: ciliated, whiplike |Antonyms: N/A | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *flagellum* (whip, scourge), diminutive of *flagrum* (whip).
