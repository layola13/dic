copartner:/ˈkoʊˌpɑːrtnər/| n.|合伙人，共同合作者|A person who shares in a business undertaking with another or others, especially as a joint owner.|例句：The copartners decided to expand the business. 合伙人决定扩大业务。

例句：She and her copartner have been running the bakery for five years. 她和她的合伙人一起经营这家面包店已经五年了。|近义词：partner, associate, colleague|反义词: competitor, opponent|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From co- + partner. Co- comes from the Latin prefix "com-" meaning "with," "together," "jointly."  Partner comes from the Old French "parçonier" meaning "sharer," "partner," ultimately from the Latin "partiri" meaning "to divide," "to share."
