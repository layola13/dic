gaucherie:/ˈɡoʊʃəri/| n. |笨拙，不圆滑，粗鲁|Awkwardness or tactlessness in social behavior.  A lack of social grace or ease; clumsiness.|Her gaucherie at the formal dinner was evident when she spilled wine on the tablecloth.|Near Synonyms: awkwardness, clumsiness, ineptitude, tactlessness, blunder, faux pas|Antonyms: grace, poise, finesse, tact, diplomacy|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From French *gaucherie*, from *gauche* "left, awkward," from Frankish *gaut* "left."  The association of "left" with awkwardness is widespread across many cultures.
