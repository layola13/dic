approaches:/əˈproʊtʃɪz/ (US), /əˈprəʊtʃɪz/ (UK)
| n. | 方法，途径；接近，靠近 | A way of dealing with something; the act of coming nearer to something. |  The school has decided to adopt a different approach to discipline. 学校决定采取不同的纪律措施。| methods, techniques, strategies | avoidance, retreat | noun

approaches:/əˈproʊtʃɪz/ (US), /əˈprəʊtʃɪz/ (UK)
| v. | 接近，靠近；着手处理 | Come near or nearer to (someone or something) in distance or time; speak to (someone) for the first time about something, typically with a proposal or request. |  Winter is approaching. 冬天就要来了。| nears, advances, comes close to | recedes, retreats | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *aprochier (Modern French approcher) "to approach, draw near," from Late Latin appropiare "come nearer to," from Latin ad- "to" + prope "near".
