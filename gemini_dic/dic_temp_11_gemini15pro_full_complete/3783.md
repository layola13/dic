agley:/əˈɡleɪ/
adv.
awry, amiss
wrong; awry.
The best laid schemes of mice and men often go agley.  老鼠和人的最佳计划常常出错。
近义词:awry, amiss, wrong, askew
反义词:right, straight, correct
词性:adverb

Etymology: From Scots <em>agley</em>, from Middle English <em>on glay</em> (“askew”), from Old Norse <em>á gleið</em> (“awry”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic <em>*glaihwijaz</em>, from Proto-Indo-European <em>*ǵʰleyǵʰ-</em> (“to squint, leer”).
