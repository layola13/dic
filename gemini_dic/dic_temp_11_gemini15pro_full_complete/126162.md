mutters:/ˈmʌtərz/ (British) /ˈmʌtɚz/ (American)| v. | 嘀咕，咕哝 | To speak in a low, indistinct voice, often complainingly. | He muttered something about the weather and walked away. 他嘀咕了几句关于天气的事情，然后走开了。|  She muttered to herself as she worked. 她一边工作一边自言自语地嘀咕。 | mumble, murmur, grumble | shout, yell | verb


mutter:/ˈmʌtə(r)/ (British) /ˈmʌtɚ/ (American)| n. | 嘀咕，低语 | A low, indistinct utterance. |  His mutter was barely audible. 他的嘀咕声几乎听不见。| The mutter of discontent spread through the crowd.  人群中弥漫着不满的低语。| mumble, murmur, grumble | shout, yell | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mutteren*, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Dutch *motteren* (“to mumble”).
