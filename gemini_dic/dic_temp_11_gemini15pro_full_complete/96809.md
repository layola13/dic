inertia:/ɪˈnɜːʃə/ /ɪˈnɜːrʃə/| n. |惯性，惰性|The tendency of a body to resist acceleration; the property of a body that determines the acceleration it will have when acted upon by a given force.|例句：The car's inertia kept it moving forward even after the brakes were applied. 汽车的惯性使它即使在刹车后仍继续向前移动。

例句：He overcame his inertia and finally started working on the project. 他克服了惰性，终于开始做这个项目了。|近义词：inaction, inactivity, momentum, resistance|反义词：activity, energy, motion|词性:名词

inertias:/ɪˈnɜːʃəz/ /ɪˈnɜːrʃəz/| n. |惯性（复数）,惰性（复数）|Plural form of inertia, referring to multiple instances or types of inertia.|例句：The inertias of the different components of the system made it difficult to control. 系统中不同组件的惯性使其难以控制。

例句：Overcoming the inertias of traditional thinking is crucial for innovation. 克服传统思维的惯性对于创新至关重要。|近义词：inactions, inactivities, momentums, resistances|反义词：activities, energies, motions|词性:名词
