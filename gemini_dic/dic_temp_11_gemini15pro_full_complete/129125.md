NKVD:/ˌɛnˌkeɪˌviːˈdiː/ | n. (abbreviation) | 内务人民委员部 | People's Commissariat for Internal Affairs (Народный комиссариат внутренних дел), the secret police and state security agency of the Soviet Union from 1934 to 1946.  It was responsible for state security, border control, and the administration of the Gulag system of forced labor camps. | The NKVD carried out mass arrests and executions during the Great Purge.  内务人民委员部在大清洗期间进行了大规模逮捕和处决。| synonyms: Cheka, OGPU, KGB | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a secret police organization, perhaps "human rights organization" as a contrasting concept) | abbreviation
