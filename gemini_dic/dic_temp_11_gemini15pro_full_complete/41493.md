cornucopian:/ˌkɔːrnjʊˈkoʊpiən/ |adj. n.|丰饶的，富饶的；信奉丰饶论的人| Relating to or resembling a cornucopia; denoting plenty or an abundant supply.  A believer in the philosophy of cornucopianism, which asserts that technological advances will always provide enough resources to sustain a growing population.| The Thanksgiving table presented a cornucopian display of food. 感恩节的餐桌上摆满了丰盛的食物。|  The mayor's cornucopian vision for the city included new parks, affordable housing, and improved public transportation. 市长对这座城市的丰饶愿景包括新的公园、经济适用房和改善的公共交通。|Synonyms: abundant, plentiful, copious, lavish, profuse|Antonyms: scarce, meager, sparse, deficient, limited|adjective, noun

cornucopia:/ˌkɔːrnjʊˈkoʊpiə/|n.|丰饶角，象征丰饶的事物|A horn overflowing with fruit, flowers, and grain, signifying abundance; an abundant supply of good things.| The centerpiece was a cornucopia filled with autumnal fruits and vegetables.  中心装饰是一个装满秋季水果和蔬菜的丰饶角。| The harvest festival featured a giant cornucopia as a symbol of prosperity.  丰收节以一个巨大的丰饶角作为繁荣的象征。|Synonyms: abundance, bounty, profusion, plethora, wealth|Antonyms: scarcity, lack, dearth, deficiency, paucity|noun
