debutant:/ˈdebjuːtɒnt/ (UK), /ˌdeɪbjuˈtɑːnt/ (US)| n.|首次亮相的人；初入社交界的女子|A person making a first appearance in a particular field, especially a young woman making her first formal appearance in society.|例句：The debutant nervously awaited her presentation at the ball.  这位初入社交界的女子紧张地等待着在舞会上被介绍。

例句：The young author was hailed as a literary debutant of great promise.  这位年轻的作家被誉为一位非常有前途的文坛新秀。|近义词：newcomer, beginner, novice|反义词:veteran, expert|词性:noun

debutante:/ˈdebjuːtɒnt/ (UK), /ˌdeɪbjuˈtɑːnt/ (US)| n.|首次亮相的女子；初入社交界的女子|A young woman making her first formal appearance in society.|例句：She was a debutante at the Queen Charlotte's Ball. 她是夏洛特女王舞会上的初入社交界的女子。

例句：The debutantes all wore white gowns and gloves.  初入社交界的女子们都穿着白色礼服和戴着手套。|近义词：newcomer, beginner, novice|反义词:veteran, expert|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French débutant, present participle of débuter "to begin," from de- "from" + but "end, goal," from Old French bout.  Ultimately from Frankish *bōt "advantage, use, improvement."
