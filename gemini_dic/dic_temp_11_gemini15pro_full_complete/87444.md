hesitation:/ˌhezɪˈteɪʃn/| n.|犹豫，踌躇|The speaker's hesitation suggested he was unsure of his facts. 演讲者的犹豫表明他对自己的论据不确定。|She showed no hesitation in accepting the challenge. 她毫不犹豫地接受了挑战。|近义词：pause, indecision, wavering|反义词: decisiveness, determination|词性:noun
hesitate:/ˈhezɪteɪt/| v.|犹豫，踌躇|Don't hesitate to ask if you have any questions. 如果你有任何问题，请尽管问。|He hesitated before answering. 他在回答之前犹豫了一下。|近义词：pause, waver, falter|反义词: decide, resolve|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *haesitationem (nominative haesitatio) "a lingering, delay," noun of action from past-participle stem of Latin haesitare "stick fast, remain fixed; stammer, hesitate," frequentative of haerere "to stick, cling, adhere; hold fast; be fixed, remain; doubt, hesitate," from PIE root *ghaes- "to stick, hold fast."
