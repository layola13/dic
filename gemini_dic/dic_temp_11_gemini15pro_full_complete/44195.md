croaky:/ˈkroʊki/| adj.|嘶哑的，粗嘎的|Having a rough or hoarse voice, typically as a result of a sore throat or overuse.|例句：He woke up with a croaky voice after shouting at the concert.  他看完演唱会后醒来，嗓子嘶哑了。|例句：The old woman's voice was croaky with age. 老妇人的声音因年老而嘶哑。|近义词：hoarse, raspy, gravelly|反义词:clear, smooth|词性:形容词
词源:From Middle English *croken, from Old English *crācian (“to croak”), of imitative origin.
