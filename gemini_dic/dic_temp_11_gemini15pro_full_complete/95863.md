incorruptibly:/ˌɪnkəˈrʌptɪbli/
adverb
廉洁地，不可腐蚀地
in a way that is not and cannot be corrupted; in a way that is honest and morally sound.
The judge ruled incorruptibly on the case, un swayed by outside pressure.  法官廉洁地对案件进行了裁决，不受外界压力的影响。
He served the public incorruptibly for many years. 他廉洁地为公众服务了很多年。
Synonyms: honestly, ethically, uprightly, unimpeachably, irreproachably
Antonyms: corruptly, dishonestly, venally, unethically

ETYMOLOGY->
From "in-" (not) + "corrupt" (from Latin "corruptus," past participle of "corrumpere," meaning "to break entirely, ruin, destroy, bribe") + "-ible" (capable of being) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix)
