hyperscholastically:/ˌhaɪpərskəˈlæstɪkəli/ | adv. | 过度学术地，过分学究地 |  In a manner excessively focused on academic pursuits, often to the detriment of practical skills or social interaction.  Excessively scholarly; in an overly academic manner. | He approached the problem hyperscholastically, citing obscure theories but failing to offer a practical solution. 他以过分学究的方式处理这个问题，引用了一些晦涩的理论，但未能提供切实可行的解决方案。|  例句：She analyzed the poem hyperscholastically, missing its emotional impact. 她以过分学究的方式分析这首诗，忽略了它的情感冲击。| 近义词：pedantically, academically, scholastically | 反义词：pragmatically, practically | 词性：副词


词源:
From hyper- "over, excessive" + scholastically (from scholastic + -ally).  "Hyper-" comes from Greek "hyper" (over, above). "Scholastic" comes from Late Latin "scholasticus" (of the schools), from Greek "skholastikos" (learned, studious).
