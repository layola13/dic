exhorts:/ɪɡˈzɔːrts/| v.|劝告，勉励，敦促|To strongly encourage or urge someone to do something.|The coach exhorts his players to give their best effort. 教练勉励他的队员们全力以赴。|The teacher exhorted her students to study hard for the exam. 老师敦促学生们努力学习，准备考试。|近义词：encourages, urges, advises, impels|反义词：dissuades, discourages|词性：动词

exhortation:/ˌɛksɔːrˈteɪʃən/| n.|劝告，忠告，勉励|An act or instance of exhorting; urgent recommendation or advice.|The president's exhortation to the nation was to remain calm. 总统告诫全国人民保持冷静。|His constant exhortations to work harder began to annoy his colleagues. 他不断地劝告大家更加努力工作，这开始让他的同事们感到厌烦。|近义词：encouragement, advice, urging|反义词：discouragement, dissuasion|词性：名词

词源: From Middle English *exhorten*, from Old French *exhorter*, from Latin *exhortārī* (“to encourage, urge strongly”), from *ex-* (“out”) + *hortārī* (“to encourage, urge”).
