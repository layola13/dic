lollardism:/ˈlɒlərdɪzəm/ |n.|罗拉德派; 罗拉德主义|A religious movement of the late 14th and early 15th centuries in England, led by John Wycliffe.  It advocated for religious reform, including translating the Bible into English and challenging the authority of the Church.|Lollardism was a precursor to the Protestant Reformation. 罗拉德派是新教改革的先驱。|近义词:Wycliffism, heresy (from the perspective of the Catholic Church)|反义词:Catholicism, orthodoxy|词性:noun

lollard:/ˈlɒlərd/ |n.|罗拉德派信徒|A follower of Lollardism.|Many lollards were persecuted for their beliefs. 许多罗拉德派信徒因其信仰而受到迫害。|近义词:heretic (from the perspective of the Catholic Church), reformer|反义词:Catholic, orthodox believer|词性:noun
