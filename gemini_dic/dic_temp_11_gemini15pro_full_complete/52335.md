dict:/dɪkt/ | n. | 字典；词典 | A book or electronic resource that lists the words of a language (typically in alphabetical order) and gives their meaning, or gives the equivalent words in a different language, often also providing information about pronunciation, origin, and usage. | I looked up the word in the dictionary. 我在字典里查了这个词。| synonyms: lexicon, wordbook, glossary | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a physical object) | noun


dict:/dɪkt/ | v. |  口述；命令；(旧)指示 | (dated) Say or read aloud (something) to be written down or recorded. | He dictated a letter to his secretary. 他向他的秘书口述了一封信。| synonyms: pronounce, deliver, command | antonyms: transcribe, listen, obey | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dictum* (“something said”), neuter past participle of *dīcere* (“to say”).  Related to *dicō* (“I say”), *dīcō* (“I dedicate, devote”), and *index* (“pointer, sign”).
