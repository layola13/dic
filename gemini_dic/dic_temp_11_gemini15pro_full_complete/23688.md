brash:/bræʃ/| adj.|傲慢的，鲁莽的，轻率的|Self-assertive in a rude, noisy, or overbearing way.|He was a brash young man who thought he knew everything. 他是一个傲慢自大的年轻人，自以为无所不知。|例句：The brash young executive alienated his colleagues with his arrogance.  这位傲慢的年轻主管因为他的傲慢疏远了他的同事。

例句：Her brash comments offended several people at the meeting. 她轻率的评论冒犯了会议上的几个人。|近义词：impudent, arrogant, cocky, bold, reckless|反义词：humble, modest, timid, cautious|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From  Scots *brasche* (“attack, assault”),  from Middle French *brache* (“breeches, codpiece”),  from Old Frankish *brāk*.  More at breech.  The sense development is perhaps from the idea of “an ostentatious display of the crotch” (compare codpiece), hence “impudent, ostentatious”.
