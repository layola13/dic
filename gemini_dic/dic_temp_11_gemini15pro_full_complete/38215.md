compurgatorial:/kəmˌpɜːrɡəˈtɔːriəl/
adjective
中文翻译：宣誓断罪的；以宣誓断罪的
英文解析：Relating to or involving compurgation, a historical practice of clearing oneself of a charge by producing sworn witnesses who testified to one's innocence.
例句：The compurgatorial system was an important part of early medieval law.  早期中世纪法律的重要组成部分是宣誓断罪制度。

例句：Compurgatorial oaths were often used in cases where there was no other evidence.  在没有其他证据的情况下，通常使用宣誓断罪誓言。
近义词：exculpatory, vindicatory
反义词：incriminating, accusatory
词性：adjective

词源:
From Late Latin *compurgatorius, from compurgare "to purify completely," from com- "together" + purgare "to purge, cleanse."
