antitypy:/ænˈtɪtɪpi/| n.|对照，对应，对偶|A relationship or correspondence between two things based on contrast or resemblance, especially in the context of biblical typology.  It refers to the fulfillment or realization of a type or prefiguration. |The sacrifice of Isaac is seen as an antitype of the sacrifice of Christ.| 近义词: counterpart, correlate, parallel |反义词: type, prefiguration | noun

Etymology->
From Late Latin *antitypia*, from Ancient Greek ἀντίτυπος (antítypos, “a blow in return, image, copy”), from ἀντί (antí, “against, opposite, in return”) + τύπος (túpos, “impression, image, type”).
