overarticulate: /ˌoʊvərɑːrˈtɪkjʊleɪt/ | v. adj. | 过分清晰地发音；过度清晰发音的 | To pronounce words more distinctly than is natural or necessary, often to an exaggerated degree.  |

例句：
He overarticulated every word, as if speaking to a child.
他每个字都发音过分清晰，好像在跟小孩说话。

The teacher overarticulated the vowel sounds for the benefit of the non-native speakers.
老师为了非母语人士的利益，过度清晰地发音了元音。


近义词：enunciate clearly, pronounce distinctly |
反义词：mumble, slur |
词性：verb, adjective |

词源: over- + articulate.  "Over-" acts as a prefix indicating excess or going beyond the normal limit. "Articulate" comes from Latin "articulatus," past participle of "articulare" meaning "to utter distinctly," from "articulus" meaning "joint, division."
