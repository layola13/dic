attorneyism: /əˈtɜːrniɪzəm/
n.
律师主义；律师作风；过分依赖律师
Excessive reliance on attorneys or legal processes; the dominance or influence of lawyers in a particular field or situation.  A tendency to approach issues with a legalistic or litigious mindset.
The company's descent into attorneyism stifled innovation and created a risk-averse culture.  这家公司陷入律师主义，扼杀了创新，并造成了一种规避风险的文化。
The attorneyism prevalent in the government slowed down decision-making. 政府中普遍存在的律师作风减缓了决策速度。
近义词: legalism, litigiousness
反义词: informality, flexibility
词性: noun

Etymology:  From "attorney" + "-ism."  "Attorney" comes from Old French "atorné" meaning "appointed," past participle of "atorner."  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns meaning a doctrine, belief, practice, or characteristic.
