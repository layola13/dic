fissure:/ˈfɪʃər/ (US), /ˈfɪʃə(r)/ (UK)| n. |裂缝，裂隙; 分歧，不和|A long, narrow opening or crack in something, especially rock or the earth; a split or division between people or groups. |例句：A deep fissure snaked its way through the rock face. 一条深深的裂缝蜿蜒穿过岩壁。

例句：The growing fissure between the two political parties threatened the stability of the government.  两个政党之间日益加剧的分歧威胁着政府的稳定。|近义词：crack, crevice, split, rift, chasm, breach|反义词：closure, union, bridge, connection|词性:名词


fissure:/ˈfɪʃər/ (US), /ˈfɪʃə(r)/ (UK)| v. | (使)裂开，(使)分裂|To crack or split open.|例句：The ground fissured during the earthquake. 地震中地面裂开了。

例句：Their relationship began to fissure under the strain. 他们的关系在压力下开始破裂。|近义词：crack, split, divide, break|反义词：unite, join, mend, heal|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *fissūra*, from fissus (past participle of findere "to split, cleave").
