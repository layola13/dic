neotectonics:/ˌnɪːəʊtekˈtɒnɪks/ /ˌnioʊtekˈtɑːnɪks/ | n. |新构造运动；新构造学| The study of the relatively recent geologic deformations of Earth's crust and the tectonic processes that cause them, typically from the Late Tertiary Period onward.  It focuses on the movements and deformations that are currently active or have occurred in the geologically recent past. |例句：Neotectonics plays a crucial role in understanding earthquake hazards.  新构造运动在理解地震灾害方面起着至关重要的作用。

|例句：Researchers study neotectonics to assess seismic risks. 研究人员研究新构造运动以评估地震风险。|近义词：recent tectonics, active tectonics|反义词:paleotectonics|词性:noun
