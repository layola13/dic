moot:/muːt/
adjective|未决定的，无实际意义的|Subject to debate, dispute, or uncertainty, and typically not admitting of a final decision.|The question of whether to wear a tie is now moot, since the meeting has been canceled.  穿不穿领带的问题现在已经没有意义了，因为会议已经取消了。|debatable, arguable, disputable|settled, resolved, decided
adjective|提出…供讨论|Raise (a question or topic) for discussion; suggest (an idea or possibility).|The idea was first mooted as early as the 1840s.  这个想法早在19世纪40年代就被提出来了。|propose, suggest, submit|suppress, conceal, hide
verb|提出…供讨论|Raise (a question or topic) for discussion; suggest (an idea or possibility).|He mooted the idea of a merger. 他提出了合并的想法。|propose, suggest, submit|suppress, conceal, hide
noun|大会，讨论会|An assembly held for debate, especially in Anglo-Saxon and medieval times.|The matter was debated in the moot.  这件事在大会上进行了辩论。|assembly, meeting, gathering|separation, parting, dispersal
