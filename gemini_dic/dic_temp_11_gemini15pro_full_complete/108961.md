leer: /lɪr/
| v. | 淫笑，斜视 | to look at someone in a way that shows sexual interest, in an unpleasant way | He leered at her across the table. 他隔着桌子淫笑着看着她。 |  近义词: ogle, leer, stare, gawk | 反义词: glance, glimpse | verb

leer: /lɪr/
| n. | 淫笑，斜视 | an unpleasant look that shows sexual interest | He gave her a suggestive leer. 他向她投去暗示性的淫笑。| 近义词: ogle, leer, stare, gawk | 反义词: glance, glimpse | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *leren, from Old English *lēran (“to look askance, leer”), from Proto-Germanic *laizijaną (“to look askance”), from Proto-Indo-European *lois- (“furrow, track”). Cognate with Old Norse leira (“clay, mire”). Related to lore. The noun is from the verb.
