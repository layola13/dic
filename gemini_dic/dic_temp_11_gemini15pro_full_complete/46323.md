dabbling:/ˈdæblɪŋ/
dabbling| n. | 浅尝；涉猎；戏水 | The act of engaging in something superficially or intermittently. |  He's been dabbling in painting for a few years. 他几年来一直断断续续地画画。| synonyms: tinkering, pottering, experimenting | antonyms: immersing, specializing | noun
dabbling| adj. | 浅尝辄止的；涉猎的 | Engaging in something superficially or intermittently. |  He's a dabbling artist, not a professional. 他是一个浅尝辄止的艺术家，而不是专业的。| synonyms: amateur, dilettante, superficial | antonyms: professional, expert, dedicated | adjective
dabble:/ˈdæbəl/
dabble| v. | 浅尝；涉猎；戏水 | To engage in something superficially or intermittently. | She dabbles in photography as a hobby. 她把摄影作为一种爱好。| synonyms: tinker, potter, experiment | antonyms: immerse, specialize | verb
