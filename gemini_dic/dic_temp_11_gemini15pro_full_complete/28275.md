captivating:/ˈkæptɪveɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 迷人的，有魅力的 | Having the power to attract and hold someone's attention; fascinating. | Her captivating smile made everyone feel at ease. 她迷人的微笑让每个人都感到轻松自在。 | The captivating performance left the audience spellbound.  引人入胜的表演让观众着迷。| 近义词: enchanting, alluring, fascinating, mesmerizing | 反义词: dull, boring, tedious, uninteresting | 词性: adjective

captivating:/ˈkæptɪveɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 吸引人的，扣人心弦的 |  Able to capture and hold the attention; enthralling. | The book's captivating plot kept me reading all night. 这本书引人入胜的情节让我读了一整夜。| The movie had a captivating storyline.  这部电影的故事情节扣人心弦。 | 近义词: gripping, compelling, absorbing, engaging | 反义词:  unremarkable, mundane, insipid, vapid | 词性: adjective


词源:  From the verb "captivate," from Late Latin "captivatus," past participle of "captivare"  "to take captive," from Latin "captivus" "captive," from "captus," past participle of "capere" "to take, seize."
