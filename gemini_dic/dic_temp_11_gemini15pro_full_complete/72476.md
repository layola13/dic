flunlyism: /ˈflʌnliɪzəm/ | n. | 浮夸的言辞；空洞的华丽辞藻 |  The use of extravagant or bombastic language, often insincere or lacking substance.  It often involves excessive use of jargon, clichés, and overly complex sentence structures to create an impression of intelligence or sophistication, but ultimately communicates little of actual value. |  His speech was filled with flunlyism, but lacked any real substance. 他的演讲充满了浮夸的言辞，但缺乏实质内容。 |  The politician's flunlyism obscured the lack of concrete proposals in his platform.  这位政客的华丽辞藻掩盖了他政纲中缺乏具体建议的事实。 | synonyms: bombast, rhetoric, verbosity, grandiloquence | antonyms: conciseness, brevity, simplicity, directness | noun
