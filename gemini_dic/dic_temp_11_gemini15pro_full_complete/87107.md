heralds:/ˈherəldz/| n. v.|预示，预兆；传令官，使者|A person or thing that announces or signals the approach of another; something that precedes or foreshadows.|The robin is a herald of spring. 知更鸟是春天的预兆。|synonyms: announcer, messenger, precursor, harbinger|antonyms: follower, successor|noun
heralds:/ˈherəldz/| v.|预示，预报；宣布，宣告|To announce or signal the approach of; to proclaim.|The newspaper heralded the arrival of a new era. 报纸预示着新时代的到来。|synonyms: announce, proclaim, foretell, presage|antonyms: conceal, suppress|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *herald*, from Frankish *hairo* "cry, shout" + *wald* "power, rule".
