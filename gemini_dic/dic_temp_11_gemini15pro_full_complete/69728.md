feoffment: /ˈfiːfmənt/ | n. |  封地；封地授予；封地契据 | The granting of a fee, especially in land.  A feoffment was the historic method of conveying freehold land. |  The feoffment transferred ownership of the estate to the new lord.  封地契约将地产的所有权转移给了新领主。 | synonyms: enfeoffment, investiture | antonyms: dispossession, deprivation | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *feoffement*,  from *feoffer* "to enfeoff," from *fief* "fee," from Frankish *fehu* "cattle, property."
