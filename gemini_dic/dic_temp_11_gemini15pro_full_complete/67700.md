exultancy:/ɪɡˈzʌltənsi/ | n. | 狂喜；欢欣；得意 | A feeling of triumphant elation or jubilation. |  She felt a surge of exultancy as she crossed the finish line. 她冲过终点线时感到一阵狂喜。

| Example: His exultancy at winning the lottery was evident. 他中了彩票后的欣喜若狂显而易见。
| Example: The team's exultancy was infectious after their victory.  团队胜利后的喜悦很有感染力。
| Synonym: jubilation, elation, triumph, rejoicing, rapture
| Antonym: despair, despondency, gloom, sorrow, grief
| Word Class: Noun

exultant:/ɪɡˈzʌltənt/ | adj. |  欢欣鼓舞的；喜气洋洋的；得意洋洋的 | Feeling or expressing great happiness or triumph. |  He gave an exultant shout of victory. 他发出一声胜利的欢呼。

| Example:  The exultant fans cheered their team to victory.  欢欣鼓舞的球迷为他们的球队加油助威，最终取得了胜利。
| Example: Her exultant smile lit up the room. 她喜气洋洋的笑容照亮了整个房间。
| Synonym: jubilant, elated, triumphant, rejoicing, joyful
| Antonym: despondent, dejected, gloomy, sorrowful, grieving
| Word Class: Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin exultantia, from Latin exultare "to leap up, rejoice," from ex- "out" + saltare "to leap, dance" (from PIE root *sel- (1) "to jump").
