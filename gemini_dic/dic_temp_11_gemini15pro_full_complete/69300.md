febricitate:/ˈfɛbrɪˌkeɪt/| v. |捏造，虚构，伪造|To invent or concoct (something), typically with deceitful intent.|He febricated a story about being robbed.|近义词：fabricate, invent, concoct, forge, falsify|反义词:verify, confirm|词性:动词

Etymology: From Latin *febricitatus*, past participle of *febricitare* "to have a fever," from *febris* "fever."  The sense shift likely arose from the idea of feverish mental activity leading to the creation of fantasies or falsehoods.
