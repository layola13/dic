emblazoners:/ɪmˈbleɪzənərz/
noun
纹章绘制者；纹章装饰者
People who design and apply coats of arms and other heraldic devices.
The emblazoners worked diligently on the royal banners.  纹章绘制者们在皇家旗帜上辛勤地工作。
The family hired emblazoners to create a coat of arms for their newly acquired title.  这个家族雇佣了纹章绘制者来为他们新获得的头衔设计纹章。
Synonyms:  heraldists, armorists
Antonyms: N/A
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "emblazoner" (to decorate with heraldic devices), from "en-" (in, on) + "blason" (coat of arms). Ultimately from Frankish "*blaso" (horn, trumpet), referring to the heraldic tradition of announcing a knight by blowing a horn and describing his coat of arms.
