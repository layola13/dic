drags:/dræɡz/ | n. | 拖，拉；累赘；阻力；乏味的东西 | Something that is tedious and slow.  A drag can also refer to the act of dragging something, or something that creates resistance or slows progress. | The meeting was a real drag.  会议真是无聊透顶。| synonyms: bore, nuisance, burden, hindrance | antonyms: boost, help, advantage | noun

drags:/dræɡz/ | v. | 拖，拉；缓慢地移动；勉强进行 | To pull something along the ground or other surface, often with difficulty.  It can also mean to move slowly and reluctantly, or to make something last longer than necessary. | He dragged the heavy suitcase across the floor. 他吃力地把沉重的行李箱拖过地板。| synonyms: haul, tow, pull, trail | antonyms: push, propel, carry | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *draga, from Proto-Germanic *draganą, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰregʰ- (“to draw, pull”). Cognate with Old English dragan (“to draw, drag, protract”), Old Frisian draga, Middle Dutch and Dutch dragen (“to carry, wear”), Old High German tragan (German tragen (“to carry, wear, bear”)), Gothic 𐌳𐍂𐌰𐌲𐌾𐌰𐌽 (draggjan, “to draw”).
