endemic:/ɛnˈdɛmɪk/ (US) /enˈdemɪk/ (UK)| adj.|地方性的；特有的；流行的|Native to or confined to a certain region or people; characteristic of or prevalent in a particular field, area, or environment.|Malaria is endemic in many hot countries. 疟疾在许多炎热的国家很流行。|Synonyms: native, indigenous, restricted to, characteristic of, prevalent in|Antonyms: pandemic, widespread, global|adjective

endemic:/ɛnˈdɛmɪk/ (US) /enˈdemɪk/ (UK)| n.|地方病；特有物种|A disease or condition regularly found among a particular people or in a certain area; a plant or animal native or restricted to a certain country or area.|Corruption is an endemic in the system. 腐败在这个系统中根深蒂固。|Synonyms: native species, indigenous species|Antonyms: introduced species, exotic species|noun
