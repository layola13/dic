necromancer: /ˈnɛkrəˌmænsər/ | n. |死灵法师，巫师|A person who practices necromancy; someone who claims to communicate with the dead, especially in order to predict the future or to use their supposed power for magic. |The necromancer in the dark tower sought forbidden knowledge.  黑暗塔中的死灵法师寻求禁忌知识。|Near Synonyms: sorcerer, wizard, magician, conjurer|Antonyms: None (The opposite concept would be someone who opposes or fights necromancy, not a single word.)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *negromancer*, from Old French nigromancien, from Medieval Latin nigromantia, from Late Latin necromantia, from Ancient Greek νεκρομαντεία (nekromanteía, “necromancy”), from νεκρός (nekrós, “corpse, dead”) + μαντεία (manteía, “divination”).
