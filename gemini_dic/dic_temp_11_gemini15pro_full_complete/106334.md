kogin:/ˈkoʊɡɪn/| n.| 梢gin布; 梢gin刺绣; 梢gin绣|A type of Japanese embroidery, originating in the Tsugaru region of Aomori Prefecture, characterized by geometric patterns stitched onto a coarsely woven hemp fabric.|The museum displayed intricate examples of kogin.  博物馆展出了复杂的kogin作品。|Synonyms:  Japanese embroidery, Tsugaru embroidery|Antonyms: None|Noun
