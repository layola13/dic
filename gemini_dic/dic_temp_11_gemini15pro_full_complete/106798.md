labiatifloral: /ˌleɪbiˌeɪtiˈflɔːrəl/
adj.
唇形花冠的
Having a corolla resembling that of plants in the mint family (Lamiaceae), typically two-lipped or bilabiate.
The labiatifloral structure of the flower attracts pollinators like bees.  这朵唇形花冠的花吸引了像蜜蜂这样的传粉者。
近义词: bilabiate, two-lipped
反义词: (No direct antonym, could describe a different corolla shape, e.g., rotate, campanulate)
词性: adjective
词源:  From Latin *labiatus* (lipped) + *floral* (relating to flowers).  *Labiatus* is from *labium* (lip).
