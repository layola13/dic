chromoblast: /ˈkrəʊməblɑːst/ (UK) /ˈkroʊməblæst/ (US) | n. | 色素体，成色体 | An immature plastid containing pigments, especially one that develops into a chloroplast or chromoplast.  A cell or plastid that contains pigment. | Example 1: The chromoblasts within the flower petals give them their vibrant colors.  花瓣中的色素体赋予它们鲜艳的色彩。

Example 2:  Scientists are studying the development of chromoblasts in fruit ripening. 科学家正在研究果实成熟过程中色素体的发育。 | Near synonyms: plastid, chromatophore | Antonyms: leucoplast | Noun
