fast-tracks:/ˈfæstˌtræks/ | v. (fast-tracked, fast-tracking) | 快速推进，加速完成 | To expedite a process or project.  | The company fast-tracked the development of the new product to meet market demand. 公司快速推进了新产品的开发以满足市场需求。

fast track:/ˈfæstˌtræk/ | n. | 快速通道，优先途径 | A route or method that provides for more rapid results than usual. |  He's on the fast track to becoming a partner in the firm. 他正快速晋升为公司的合伙人。

fast-tracked:/ˈfæstˌtrækt/ | adj. | 快速推进的，加速完成的 | Having been expedited or accelerated. | The fast-tracked project was completed in record time.  这个快速推进的项目在创纪录的时间内完成了。


Etymology:  "Fast" (Old English *fæst) meaning firmly fixed, quick. + "Track" (Middle Dutch trek) meaning to pull, draw, a path or road.  The combined meaning refers to a path or process that allows for rapid progress.

Synonyms: expedite, accelerate, hasten, rush

Antonyms: delay, hinder, obstruct, slow down
