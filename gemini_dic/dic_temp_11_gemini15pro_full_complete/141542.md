opportunistic:/ˌɒpətʃuːˈnɪstɪk/ | adj. |机会主义的；投机的| Exploiting opportunities as they arise, often without regard for moral principles or consequences. |  He made an opportunistic grab for power. 他抓住机会攫取了权力。|  She took an opportunistic approach to her career, applying for any job that came up. 她对自己的职业生涯采取了机会主义的态度，任何工作都申请。| 近义词:exploitative, predatory, unscrupulous | 反义词: principled, scrupulous | 词性:形容词

opportunism:/ˌɒpətʃuːˈnɪzəm/ | n. |机会主义；投机主义| The taking of opportunities as and when they arise, regardless of planning or principle. | Political opportunism is common in times of crisis. 政治投机主义在危机时期很常见。| His opportunism led him to betray his friends for personal gain. 他的机会主义导致他为了个人利益背叛了他的朋友。| 近义词: exploitation, manipulation | 反义词: principle, integrity | 词性:名词
