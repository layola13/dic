distressed:/dɪˈstrɛst/| adj.|痛苦的，忧虑的，悲伤的；（经济上）拮据的；仿旧的|Suffering from anxiety, sorrow, or pain.  Also, experiencing financial difficulty or made to appear worn or aged.|例句：She was visibly distressed by the news. 她听到这个消息后明显很痛苦。

例句：The distressed furniture gave the room a rustic charm. 这件仿旧家具给房间增添了乡村的魅力。|近义词：upset, worried, anxious, troubled, suffering, impoverished, shabby, worn|反义词：calm, happy, content, wealthy, pristine|词性:形容词

词源: From Old French destresse "hardship, oppression, distress,"  from Vulgar Latin *districtia "constraint, tightness," from Latin districtus "drawn asunder, separated," past participle of distringere "draw apart, pull asunder," from dis- "apart" + stringere "to draw tight."
