disestablishmentarianism: /dɪsɪˌstæblɪʃmənˈtɛəriənɪzəm/ | n. | 政教分离主义；反对英国国教主义 | The belief that the Church of England should not be the official state church.  It refers specifically to the movement in 19th-century Britain to disestablish the Church of England as the state church. |  Disestablishmentarianism was a powerful force in 19th-century British politics.  19世纪英国的政教分离主义是一股强大的力量。

Disestablishmentarianism is a rarely used word these days.  如今，政教分离主义这个词很少使用。 | 近义词: secularism, separation of church and state | 反义词: establishmentarianism | 词性: 名词


**Etymology (词源):**

From *dis- + establish + -ment + -arian + -ism*.  Ultimately from Latin *stāre* "to stand".  The prefix *dis-* indicates reversal or negation.  The suffix *-ment* forms nouns from verbs.  *-arian* denotes a person who adheres to a particular belief or principle.  *-ism* denotes a doctrine, belief, or principle.
