nettle:/ˈnetl/ (US), /ˈnetəl/ (UK)| n. v. adj.|荨麻；激怒；刺激|A herbaceous plant which has jagged leaves covered with stinging hairs.  As a verb, it means to irritate or annoy someone.|例句：The nettles stung my legs as I walked through the long grass.  走过高高的草丛时，荨麻刺痛了我的腿。|近义词：irritate, annoy, provoke, vex, irk|反义词：soothe, calm, please, mollify|词性:noun, verb, adjective
nettly:/ˈnetl-i/ (US), /ˈnetəl-i/ (UK)| adj.|易怒的；暴躁的；过敏的|Easily irritated or annoyed.|例句：He gave a nettly reply to her question. 他对她提出的问题作出了恼怒的回答。|近义词：irritable, irascible, touchy, testy, thin-skinned|反义词：calm, patient, good-natured|词性:adjective

词源:
From Old English *netele, from Proto-Germanic *natilō, from Proto-Indo-European *nēdlā, meaning "needle". This refers to the stinging hairs of the plant, which are like tiny needles.
