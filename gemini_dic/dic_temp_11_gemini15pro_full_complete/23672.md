branks: /bræŋks/
noun
辔头，嚼子；旧时一种用于惩罚饶舌妇的铁口罩
A kind of iron framework which was formerly used to punish scolds. It enclosed the head and had a gag or bit which entered the mouth and compressed the tongue.
The branks were used to silence gossiping women in the 17th century.  17世纪，嚼子被用来让爱嚼舌根的女人闭嘴。
The museum displayed a set of branks as a reminder of the harsh punishments of the past.  博物馆展出了一套嚼子，以提醒人们过去严酷的惩罚。
Synonyms: scold's bridle, gag
Antonyms: freedom of speech
