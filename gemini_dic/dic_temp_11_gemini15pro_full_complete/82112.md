groggily:/ˈɡrɑːɡɪli/ /ˈɡrɒɡɪli/| adv.|昏昏沉沉地，迷迷糊糊地|in a groggy or dazed manner, typically as a result of being sleepy or drunk|She woke up groggily and stumbled to the bathroom. 她迷迷糊糊地醒来，跌跌撞撞地走到浴室。|近义词：drowsily, sleepily, dazedly|反义词：alertly, briskly, energetically|词性:副词

Etymology: From *groggy* + *-ly*.  *Groggy* likely originated from the nickname "Old Grog" for Admiral Vernon, who introduced diluted rum (grog) in the British Navy. The effect of the diluted rum was assumed to be the source of the term.
