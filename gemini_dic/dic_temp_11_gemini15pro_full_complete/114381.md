makavelli:/ˌmɑːkəˈvɛli/ | n. | 马基雅维利，权谋家 |  Referring to Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian diplomat, philosopher, historian, and writer, often associated with political expediency and ruthlessness.  It is used as a nickname or stage name, especially in hip-hop culture, to evoke a sense of cunning, strategy, and ambition.  The term is derived from Machiavelli's most famous work, "The Prince." | Tupac Shakur adopted the stage name Makaveli after reading "The Prince" while in prison.  This persona represented a shift in his artistic direction. |  Synonyms: cunning, strategic, ruthless, Machiavellian | Antonyms:  honest, ethical, principled | noun
