intuitionism:/ˌɪn.tuːˈɪ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ | n. | 直觉主义 |  A philosophy that emphasizes the role of intuition and direct experience in acquiring knowledge, especially in ethics and mathematics.  It holds that certain truths are self-evident and known directly without the need for reasoning or proof. |  Intuitionism in mathematics rejects the law of excluded middle.  数学中的直觉主义拒绝排中律。

Intuitionism played a significant role in the development of modern ethics. 直觉主义在现代伦理学的发展中扮演了重要角色。 | synonyms:  subjectivism, non-cognitivism | antonyms:  rationalism, empiricism | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From  intuition + -ism.  "Intuition" comes from Late Latin *intuitus*, past participle of *intuērī* "to look at, contemplate," from *in-* "in, on" + *tuērī* "to look at, watch, guard."  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns meaning a doctrine, belief, or practice.
