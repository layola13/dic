amanuenses:/əˌmænjuˈɛnsiːz/
|n.|抄写员，书记员|A person employed to write what another dictates or to copy what has been written.  Someone who writes down what another person says; a scribe.|The author thanked his amanuenses for their tireless work in transcribing his handwritten manuscript. 作者感谢他的抄写员不知疲倦地抄写他的手稿。|
|近义词：scribe, copyist, secretary, clerk|
|反义词：author, dictator|

amanuensis:/əˌmænjuˈɛnsɪs/
|n.|抄写员，书记员|A person employed to write what another dictates or to copy what has been written.  Someone who writes down what another person says; a scribe.|Many medieval monks served as amanuenses, copying religious texts. 许多中世纪的僧侣担任抄写员，抄写宗教文本。|
|近义词：scribe, copyist, secretary, clerk|
|反义词：author, dictator|
|词性：noun (singular)|


Etymology:
From Latin *amanuensis*, from *a manu*, "by hand" + *-ensis*, denoting origin or belonging.  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *men-* (hand).
