leese:/liːs/ | n. | gleanings; what is left after harvesting; the right to glean | The poor were allowed to collect the leese from the fields after the harvest. | The gleaners gathered the leese left behind by the harvesters. |近义词: gleanings, remains, residue |反义词: harvest, yield |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lēas, from Proto-Germanic *laisaz, meaning "to gather."  Related to Old Norse leysa "to loosen, redeem, gather."
