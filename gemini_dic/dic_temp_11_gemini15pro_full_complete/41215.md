coracoid: /ˈkɔːrəkɔɪd/ | adj. n. |喙突的，乌喙骨|A small beak-shaped bone extending from the scapula, to which part of the biceps muscle is attached.  Relating to or resembling a crow's beak. |The coracoid process is an important landmark in shoulder surgery. 喙突是肩部手术中的一个重要标志。| The coracoid bone provides attachment for several muscles. 喙骨为几块肌肉提供附着点。|Near synonyms: beak-like, rostral| Antonyms: N/A (anatomical term, no direct opposite)|Noun, Adjective
