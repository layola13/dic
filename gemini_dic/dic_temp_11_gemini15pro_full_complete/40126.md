contemplation:/ˌkɒntəmˈpleɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌkɑːntəmˈpleɪʃn/ (US)| n.|沉思，冥想，盘算；注视，凝视|The act of thinking deeply about something; prolonged observation.|She sat in quiet contemplation. 她静静地坐着沉思。|近义词：meditation, reflection, thought, consideration|反义词：inattention, disregard, negligence|词性:名词

contemplate:/ˈkɒntəmˌpleɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːntəmˌpleɪt/ (US)| v.|沉思，冥想，盘算；注视，凝视；打算，预期|To think profoundly and at length; to look at thoughtfully; to have in mind as a purpose.|He contemplated the meaning of life. 他思考着人生的意义。|近义词：ponder, consider, muse, meditate|反义词：ignore, neglect, dismiss|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin contemplātiō (“act of looking at, observation”), from Latin contemplor (“I contemplate”), from con- (“together”) + templum (“temple, open space for observation”).  Originally, the word referred to a space marked out for observation, especially of the heavens.
