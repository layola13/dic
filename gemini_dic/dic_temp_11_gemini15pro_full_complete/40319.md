contour:/ˈkɒntʊə(r) (UK), ˈkɑːntʊr (US)| n. |轮廓，外形；等高线|an outline representing or bounding the shape or form of something|例句：The artist sketched the contours of the model's face. 艺术家画下了模特脸部的轮廓。|例句：The map showed the contours of the land. 地图显示了地形的等高线。|近义词：outline, shape, form, profile|反义词: shapelessness| noun


contour:/kənˈtʊə(r) (UK), kənˈtʊr (US)| vt. |画轮廓，描绘|to mould or form something along particular lines|例句：The road contours the mountainside. 这条路沿着山坡蜿蜒而上。|例句：The sculptor contoured the clay into a human form. 雕塑家将黏土塑造成人形。|近义词：shape, mold, form|反义词: flatten| verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French contour, from Italian contorno "outline, circuit," from contornare "to go around, border," from Latin com- "together" + tornare "to turn on a lathe," from tornus "lathe."
