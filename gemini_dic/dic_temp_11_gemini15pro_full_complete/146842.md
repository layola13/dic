overindex:/ˌōvərˈɪndeks/| v.|过度索引，过分倚重|To invest excessively in a particular asset, sector, or market segment, often leading to an unbalanced portfolio and increased risk.  The act of giving something too much prominence or importance. |Example:  They overindexed on tech stocks, and when the bubble burst, they lost a lot of money. 他们过度投资科技股，泡沫破灭时，他们损失惨重。

Example: The media tends to overindex on negative news, creating a distorted view of reality.  媒体倾向于过分强调负面新闻，从而歪曲了现实。|近义词：overweight, overemphasize, overrepresent|反义词: underweight, underemphasize, underrepresent|词性:动词
