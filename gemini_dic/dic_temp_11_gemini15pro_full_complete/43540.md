craws:/krɔːz/| v.|爬行；匍匐前进|The baby crawls across the floor. 婴儿在地板上爬行。|例句：The snake crawls through the grass. 蛇爬过草地。  The wounded soldier crawled to safety. 受伤的士兵爬到安全的地方。|近义词：creep, slither, wriggle|反义词:walk, run|词性:动词

词源: From Middle English *crawlen, from Old Norse krafla (“to scratch, claw, scrape”), from Proto-Germanic *krabōną (“to scratch, crawl”). Cognate with Danish kravle (“to crawl”), Swedish kravla (“to crawl”), Icelandic krafla (“to paw, scrape”). Related to crave.

PHRASE->
crawl out of the woodwork =  (idiomatic) To emerge from obscurity; to appear unexpectedly, especially in large numbers.  (比喻)从默默无闻中出现；意外出现，尤指大量出现。
crawl space = a shallow unfinished space beneath the first floor of a building. 爬行空间（建筑物一层地板下的浅层未完成空间）
be crawling with = be teeming with; be full of. 充满了；到处都是。
make your skin crawl = to cause a feeling of revulsion or disgust. 让你起鸡皮疙瘩；让你感到恶心或厌恶。
crawl back to someone = to return to someone humbly, especially after having offended or deserted them. 卑微地回到某人身边，尤指在冒犯或抛弃他们之后。
