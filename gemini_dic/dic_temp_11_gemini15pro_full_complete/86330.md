hecho: /ˈetʃoʊ/ (American Spanish) /ˈeʧo/ (Castilian Spanish)
| adj. n.|做成的，事实，事件|Made; done; fact; deed; event.  A past participle of the verb "hacer" which means "to do" or "to make."  It functions as an adjective meaning "made" or "done" and as a noun meaning "fact," "deed," or "event." | The table is hecho de madera. (The table is made of wood.) | synonym:  realizado, completo (as adjective);  suceso, acontecimiento (as noun) | antonym: incompleto, inacabado (as adjective - opposite of "done");  ficción (as noun - opposite of "fact")

注意事项:
"Hecho" is primarily used in Spanish. While it can appear in English texts concerning Latin American topics, it's not a common English word.  Its primary use is as the past participle of "hacer."  Be careful not to confuse it with the English word "echo."
