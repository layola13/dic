messiah:/məˈsaɪə/| n.|弥赛亚，救世主|A leader or savior who is expected to deliver a group or people from oppression or suffering.  A figure believed to be divinely chosen to bring about salvation or an era of peace and prosperity.  Often used in a religious context, particularly in Christianity and Judaism, to refer to a promised deliverer.|例句：He is considered by some to be the messiah.  他被一些人认为是救世主。

例句：Many people awaited the coming of the messiah. 许多人都在等待救世主的到来。|近义词：savior, redeemer, deliverer|反义词: oppressor, tyrant|词性:noun
Etymology: From Late Latin Messias, from Greek Messīas, from Aramaic mšīḥā, from Hebrew māšīaḥ  "anointed,"  past participle of māšaḥ "to anoint."
