meerschaum:/ˈmɪrʃəm/| n.|海泡石；海泡石烟斗|A white mineral, hydrous magnesium silicate, used for making tobacco pipes.|He lit his meerschaum pipe and settled into his chair. 他点燃了他的海泡石烟斗，然后坐到椅子上。|Synonyms:sepiolite|Antonyms:N/A|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From German Meerschaum, literally ‘sea foam’, because the mineral is light and white and sometimes floats on water.  Ultimately from Meer (sea) + Schaum (foam).
