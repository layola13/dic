averages:/ˈævərɪdʒɪz/
n.|平均数，平均水平|A number expressing the central or typical value in a set of data, in particular the mode, median, or (most commonly) the mean, which is calculated by dividing the sum of the values in the set by their number. |The average of 3, 5, and 7 is 5.  3、5和7的平均数是5。|mean, median, mode|extreme, outlier|noun

average:/ˈævərɪdʒ/
n.|平均数，平均水平|A number expressing the central or typical value in a set of data. |The average rainfall in July is 5 inches. 七月份的平均降雨量是5英寸。|mean, median, mode|extreme, outlier|noun

average:/ˈævərɪdʒ/
adj.|平均的，普通的|Constituting or representing the result obtained by adding together several quantities and then dividing this total by the number of quantities.  |The average temperature in May is 20 degrees Celsius. 五月份的平均气温是20摄氏度。|typical, normal, usual|exceptional, unusual|adjective

average:/ˈævərɪdʒ/
v.|平均为，达到平均水平|To amount to or come to an average, typically over a period of time. |His golf scores averaged 75. 他的高尔夫平均得分为75分。|calculate the mean of, amount to|vary, deviate|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *averie ("cargo damaged at sea"), from Italian avaria ("damage to ship or cargo"), from Arabic 'awārīya  ("damaged goods"). The meaning shifted from referring to the financial loss shared by multiple parties in a sea venture to the general concept of a mean value.
