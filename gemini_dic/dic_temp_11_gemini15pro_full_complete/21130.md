blitheful: /ˈblaɪθfəl/ | adj. | 愉快的；快乐的；无忧无虑的 | Showing a casual and cheerful indifference considered to be callous or improper.  Characterized by happiness, lightheartedness, and a carefree attitude. | She walked with a blitheful step, seemingly unaware of the impending storm. 她迈着愉快的步伐，似乎没有意识到即将来临的风暴。 |  The children played blithefully in the park, oblivious to the worries of the adults. 孩子们在公园里无忧无虑地玩耍，忘记了成年人的烦恼。 | 近义词：cheerful, merry, joyous, lighthearted, carefree | 反义词：sad, unhappy, gloomy, depressed, anxious | 词性: 形容词 | 词源：From blithe + -ful.  Blithe from Old English blíðe "happy, kind, friendly, gracious, merciful, gentle," from Proto-Germanic *blithiz (source also of Old Saxon blithi, Old Norse blíðr, Old Frisian blithe, Dutch blijde "happy, glad," German blide "cheerful, gentle, kind").
