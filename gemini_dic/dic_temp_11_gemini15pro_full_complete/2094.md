adamant:/ˈæd.ə.mənt/ (US), /ˈæd.ə.mənt/ (UK)| adj. n.| 坚定的，坚决的；坚硬无比的物质 |Refusing to be persuaded or to change one's mind.|例句：He was adamant that he had not seen her. 他坚决表示他没有见过她。|近义词：inflexible, unyielding, resolute, firm, determined|反义词：flexible, yielding, amenable|词性:形容词

adamant:/ˈæd.ə.mənt/ (US), /ˈæd.ə.mənt/ (UK)| n.| 坚硬无比的物质 |A legendary rock or mineral of impenetrable hardness.|例句：In the old days, adamant was thought to be an unbreakable stone. 在过去，金刚石被认为是牢不可破的石头。|近义词：diamond, hardest substance|反义词: N/A|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *adamant, from Latin adamantem, accusative of adamas, from Ancient Greek ἀδάμας (adámas, “untamed, invincible, diamond”), from ἀ- (a-, “not”) + δαμάζω (damázō, “I tame, I subdue”).
