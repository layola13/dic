arranging: /əˈreɪndʒɪŋ/ | v. | 安排，整理 | the act of putting things in order or a suitable position |  She spent the morning arranging flowers. 她花了一上午时间插花。| synonyms: organizing, planning, ordering, sorting, systematizing | antonyms: disorganizing, disordering, messing up | verb (present participle)

arranging: /əˈreɪndʒɪŋ/ | adj. | 安排的，整理的 |  used to describe something that involves putting things in order or making plans for something |  He has arranging skills. 他有组织能力。 | synonyms: organizational, planning | antonyms: disorganized | adjective (present participle)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *arengier, from a- (from Latin ad 'to') + reng 'rank, row' (from Frankish *hring).

PHRASE->
arrange for  安排…去做…  I've arranged for a car to collect them from the airport. 我已经安排了一辆车去机场接他们。
arrange with  与…商定/安排  I've arranged with the neighbours about looking after the cat. 我已经和邻居们商量好了照顾猫的事。
arrange something with someone  与某人安排某事 I'll arrange a meeting with him. 我会安排和他见面。
make arrangements for  为…做安排  We'll make arrangements for the funeral later this week. 我们将在本周晚些时候安排葬礼。
arrange a loan/mortgage/insurance etc  安排贷款/抵押/保险等  I arranged a loan with the bank. 我和银行安排了一笔贷款。
