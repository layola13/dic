expectationist:/ɛkˌspɛkˈteɪʃənɪst/
n.
期望主义者；预期主义者
Someone who bases their actions or beliefs on expectations, often excessively or unrealistically.  This word suggests a focus on what one anticipates rather than on present realities.
He's such an expectationist; he's always planning for the future but never enjoys the present. 他真是个期望主义者；他总是计划未来，却从不享受现在。
近义词: optimist, idealist, dreamer
反义词: pessimist, realist, pragmatist
词性: noun

词源:  From "expectation" + "-ist" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person who practices or believes in a particular thing).  "Expectation" comes from Late Middle English: from late Latin expectatio(n-), from the verb expectare ‘await’.
