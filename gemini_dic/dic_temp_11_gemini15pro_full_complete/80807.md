gothicize:/ˈɡɒθɪsaɪz/ (US) /ˈɡɑːθɪsaɪz/| v. |使哥特化，使具有哥特风格|To make something gothic in style or appearance; to give something a gothic character. |The architect gothicized the building by adding pointed arches and gargoyles. 建筑师通过添加尖拱和滴水嘴使建筑哥特化。

The writer gothicized the story with elements of mystery and horror. 作者用神秘和恐怖的元素使故事哥特化。|近义词：darken, medievalize|反义词：modernize, brighten|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Gothic + -ize.  "Gothic" refers to the Goths, an East Germanic people, and later to a style of architecture prevalent in Western Europe from the 12th to the 16th centuries, characterized by pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses.  The suffix "-ize" forms verbs meaning "to make or become like," "to cause to be," or "to subject to."
