conicality:/ˌkɒnɪˈkælɪti/ /ˌkɑːnɪˈkælɪti/| n. |锥度，锥形度|The property of being conical; the degree to which something is conical.  It is usually expressed as the ratio of the difference in diameters of the two ends of a cone to its length, or as an angle.|The conicality of the drill bit was too high, causing it to bind in the hole.  钻头的锥度过高，导致其卡在孔中。|近义词：taper, slope, incline|反义词：cylindricality, parallelism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From conical + -ity.  Conical comes from Late Latin conicus, from Greek kōnikos, from kōnos "cone."
