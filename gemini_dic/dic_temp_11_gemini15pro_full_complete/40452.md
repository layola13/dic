contravallation:/ˌkɒntrəvəˈleɪʃn/ |ˌkɑːntrəvəˈleɪʃn/
n.|  防御工事，围城工事|A line of fortifications built around a besieged place to protect the besiegers from sorties by the defenders.|The Roman army built a contravallation around the city to prevent any attacks from within. 罗马军队在城市周围建造了围城工事，以防止任何来自内部的攻击。|
contravallation:|n.|
Synonyms: rampart, bulwark, fortification, entrenchment
Antonyms:  breach, gap, opening
Etymology: From Latin contravallatio, from contra- (against) + vallatio (walling, rampart), from vallāre (to wall, to fortify), from vallum (wall, rampart).
