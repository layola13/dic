mass-observationist:/ˌmæs ɒbzəˈveɪʃənɪst/
noun
大众观察员，社会观察员
A person involved in Mass-Observation, a social research organization founded in Britain in 1937 that aimed to study everyday life and public opinion.
例句：The mass-observationist meticulously recorded the reactions of ordinary people to the outbreak of war.  大众观察员一丝不苟地记录了普通人对战争爆发的反应。

例句：Mass-observationists documented the changing social landscape of Britain in the mid-20th century.  大众观察员记录了20世纪中叶英国不断变化的社会景象。

近义词：social researcher, observer, surveyor
反义词：(No direct antonym, perhaps) participant (as opposed to observer)
词性: n.
