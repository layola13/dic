kitchy: /ˈkɪtʃi/ (US), /ˈkɪtʃi/ (UK)| adj.|俗气的；庸俗的；低劣的|Characterized by excessive garishness or sentimentality, typically in an attempt to appear appealing or fashionable.  Often considered in bad taste because of excessive cuteness, superficiality, or sentimentality.  Derived from the German word "kitschen," meaning "to make cheap."|例句：The restaurant's decor was undeniably kitschy, with plastic flamingos and velvet Elvis paintings.  这家餐厅的装饰无疑是俗气的，有塑料火烈鸟和天鹅绒的猫王画像。

例句：She loves collecting kitschy souvenirs from her travels, like miniature Eiffel Towers and snow globes. 她喜欢收集旅行中俗气的纪念品，比如埃菲尔铁塔的微缩模型和雪球。|近义词：tacky, gaudy, cheesy, tasteless, vulgar|反义词：stylish, tasteful, elegant, sophisticated, refined|词性: adjective
