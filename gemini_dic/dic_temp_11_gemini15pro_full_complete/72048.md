flirtatiously: /flɜːrˈteɪʃəsli/ (US) /flɜːˈteɪʃəsli/ (UK)
| adv. | 挑逗地，调情地 | In a way that shows playful romantic or sexual interest. | She smiled flirtatiously at him across the bar. 她隔着吧台挑逗地对他笑了笑。|例句：He winked flirtatiously. 他挑逗地眨了眨眼。|近义词：playfully, teasingly, coquettishly|反义词：seriously, formally, platonically|词性:副词

flirtatious: /flɜːrˈteɪʃəs/ (US) /flɜːˈteɪʃəs/ (UK)
| adj. | 爱调情的，轻浮的 | Behaving in such a way as to suggest a playful sexual attraction to someone. | She gave him a flirtatious glance. 她轻浮地瞥了他一眼。|例句：He has a flirtatious manner. 他有一种轻浮的举止。|近义词：playful, teasing, coquettish|反义词：serious, reserved, modest|词性:形容词


flirt: /flɜːrt/ (US) /flɜːt/ (UK)
| n. | 调情，调情的人 | A person who habitually flirts. | She's a terrible flirt. 她是个很会调情的人。 |例句：He's such a flirt! 他真是个调情高手！|近义词：coquette, tease|反义词：prude|词性:名词

flirt: /flɜːrt/ (US) /flɜːt/ (UK)
| v. | 调情，玩弄 | Behave as though sexually attracted to someone, but playfully rather than with serious intentions. | She was flirting with the bartender. 她正在和酒保调情。 |例句：He flirted with danger by riding his motorcycle without a helmet. 他不戴头盔骑摩托车，这是在玩火。|近义词：toy, trifle, dally|反义词：commit|词性:动词

词源: From the Old French "fleureter" meaning "to flick or flutter about, to touch lightly," perhaps imitative of quick movements.
