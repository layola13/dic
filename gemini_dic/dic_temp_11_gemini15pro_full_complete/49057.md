dehumanised:/ˌdiːhjuːmənaɪzd/ | adj. | 非人化的，失去人性的 | Deprived of human qualities, such as individuality, compassion, or civility.  Treated as less than human. |  The refugees were dehumanised by being housed in overcrowded camps.  难民们被安置在拥挤不堪的营地里，失去了人性。| The prisoners were subjected to dehumanised treatment. 囚犯们遭受了非人的待遇。 | 近义词: inhumane, brutal, degrading | 反义词: humanized, compassionate, respectful | 词性: adjective


dehumanize:/ˌdiːhjuːmənaɪz/ | v. | 使非人化，使失去人性 | To deprive of human qualities or attributes. To treat as less than human. | War can dehumanize people, making them capable of terrible atrocities. 战争会使人非人化，使他们能够犯下可怕的暴行。 | The propaganda campaign sought to dehumanize the enemy. 这场宣传运动试图将敌人非人化。 | 近义词: brutalize, degrade, objectify | 反义词: humanize, personalize, dignify | 词性: verb

词源:
From de- + humanize, from human + -ize.  The prefix "de-" signifies removal or reversal of a quality.  "Humanize" means to make human or more humane. Therefore, "dehumanize" means to remove human qualities.
