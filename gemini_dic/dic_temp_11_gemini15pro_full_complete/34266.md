claptrap:/ˈklæptræp/| n.|废话，空洞的言辞，哗众取宠的言论|Pretentious and insincere language intended to impress others.  Empty or pretentious talk designed to attract applause.|The politician's speech was full of claptrap.|Synonyms: nonsense, drivel, balderdash, twaddle|Antonyms: sense, truth, wisdom|noun
claptrap:/ˈklæptræp/| adj.|华而不实的，哗众取宠的|Designed to attract applause; cheaply theatrical.|The movie was full of claptrap dialogue.|Synonyms:  showy, pretentious, ostentatious|Antonyms: genuine, sincere, authentic|adjective
