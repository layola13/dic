hypercriticise: /ˌhaɪpərˈkrɪtɪsaɪz/ | v. | 过度批评；苛刻批评 | To criticize excessively or harshly; to find fault with in a petty or unreasonable way. | He tends to hypercriticise everything she does, making her feel inadequate. 他总是过度批评她所做的一切，让她觉得自己不够好。 | She hypercriticised the report, focusing on minor stylistic errors rather than the overall content. 她过度批评了这份报告，把重点放在细微的文体错误上，而不是整体内容。 |  近义词: overcriticize, nitpick, cavil | 反义词: praise, commend | 词性: 动词

词源: hyper- (过度) + criticise (批评)  "hyper-" comes from the Greek "hyper" meaning "over, above, beyond."  "criticise" comes from the French "critiquer" and ultimately from the Greek "kritikos" meaning "able to judge."
