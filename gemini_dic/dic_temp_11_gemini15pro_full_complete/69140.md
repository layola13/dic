favourability:/ˈfeɪvərəbɪləti/| n. |好感；有利；赞同；有利条件|The president's favourability ratings have increased. 总统的支持率上升了。|例句：The warm weather increased the favourability of the outdoor event.  温暖的天气增加了户外活动的有利条件。

例句：Public favourability towards the new policy is high. 公众对新政策的支持率很高。|近义词：popularity, acceptance, approval, advantageousness|反义词：unpopularity, disapproval, disadvantage|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From *favourable* + *-ity*. *Favourable* comes from Old French *favorable*, from Latin *favorabilis* ("favorable, propitious"), from *favor* ("favor, goodwill"). *-ity* is a suffix used to form abstract nouns from adjectives, ultimately from Latin *-itas*.
