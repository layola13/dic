notturno:/nɒkˈtʊərnoʊ/ (US) /nɒkˈtɜːnəʊ/ (UK)| n. |夜曲|A piece of music, typically for piano, of a dreamy or melancholic character, suggestive of night.| Chopin's nocturnes are famous for their beauty. 肖邦的夜曲以其优美而闻名。| synonym: serenade, nocturne | antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "diurnal music" or "march") | noun

notturni:/nɒkˈtʊərniː/ (US) /nɒkˈtɜːniː/ (UK)| n. |夜曲（复数）|Plural form of notturno.| He played several notturni at the concert.  他在音乐会上演奏了几首夜曲。| synonym: serenades, nocturnes | antonym: (No direct antonym) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *notturno*, from *notte* "night," from Latin *nox* (genitive *noctis*) "night."
