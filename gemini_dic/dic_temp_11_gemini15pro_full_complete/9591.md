antisophist:/ˈæntiˈsɒfɪst/ (US) /ˈæntiˈsɑːfɪst/| n. | 反诡辩学者；反对诡辩的人 | One who opposes the Sophists, a group of ancient Greek teachers who focused on rhetoric and argumentation, sometimes using deceptive tactics. |  Socrates is often considered an antisophist because he sought truth through reasoned dialogue rather than persuasive rhetoric. 苏格拉底常被认为是一位反诡辩学者，因为他寻求通过理性对话而不是有说服力的修辞来获得真理。

Plato wrote dialogues featuring Socrates as the antisophist, challenging the arguments of Sophists like Protagoras. 柏拉图写的对话以苏格拉底为反诡辩学者，挑战了像普罗泰戈拉这样的诡辩学者的论点。 | 近义词: opponent of sophistry, critic of sophistry | 反义词: sophist | 词性: noun
