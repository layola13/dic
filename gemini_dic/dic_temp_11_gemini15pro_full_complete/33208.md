chronicle: /ˈkrɒnɪk(ə)l/ (US) /ˈkrɑːnɪk(ə)l/ (UK)| n. vt.| 编年史，记录；把…载入编年史|A chronicle is a factual written account of important or historical events in the order that they happened. |Example: The book chronicles the writer's life in wartime London. 这本书记录了作者在战时伦敦的生活。|近义词: record, history, annals, account|反义词: fiction, fabrication|词性: noun, verb


chronicle: /ˈkrɒnɪk(ə)l/ (US) /ˈkrɑːnɪk(ə)l/ (UK)| vt.| 把…载入编年史|To chronicle something means to record events in the order in which they happened.|Example:  He chronicled the events of the war. 他记录了战争中的事件。|近义词: document, record, register|反义词:  conceal, suppress|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin chronica, from Greek khronika (biblios) "(books) of chronicles," from khronos "time"
