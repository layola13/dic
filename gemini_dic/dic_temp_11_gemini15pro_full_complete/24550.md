broadsides:/ˈbrɔːdsaɪdz/| n.|猛烈抨击；舷侧齐射；单面印刷品|A strong verbal attack; the simultaneous firing of all the guns on one side of a warship; a sheet of paper printed on one side only, typically used for proclamations or advertisements.|The candidate launched a broadside against his opponent. 候选人对他的对手发起了猛烈抨击。|近义词：attack, barrage, salvo, denunciation|反义词:praise, commendation|noun

broadsides:/ˈbrɔːdsaɪdz/| v.|用舷侧炮齐射；猛烈抨击|To attack verbally; to fire all the guns on one side of a warship simultaneously.|The ship broadsided the enemy vessel. 这艘船用舷侧炮齐射了敌舰。|近义词：attack, bombard|反义词:defend|verb

Etymology: From "broad" (wide) + "side" (side of a ship).  Originally referred to the simultaneous firing of all guns on one side of a warship.  The metaphorical meaning of "verbal attack" developed later.
