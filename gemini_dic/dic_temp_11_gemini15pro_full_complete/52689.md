dignify:/ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪ/| vt.|使高贵，使显得尊贵，抬高...的身价|To give dignity or importance to something or someone; to make something or someone seem worthy of respect.|The mayor dignified the ceremony with his presence. 市长出席了仪式，使仪式倍增光彩。|The university president dignified the graduation ceremony by wearing his academic robes.大学校长身着学术礼服，为毕业典礼增添了庄重气氛。|近义词：ennoble, elevate, exalt, honor|反义词：degrade, demean, humiliate|词性:动词

dignified:/ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪd/| adj.|高贵的，有尊严的，庄重的|Having or showing dignity; worthy of respect.|She maintained a dignified silence throughout the ordeal. 在整个磨难过程中，她始终保持着尊严的沉默。|He delivered a dignified speech at the funeral. 他在葬礼上发表了庄严的讲话。|近义词：stately, noble, majestic, imposing|反义词：undignified, common, low|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dignificare, from Latin dignus (“worthy”) + -ficare (from facere (“to make”)).
