distastes:/dɪsˈteɪsts/
| n. |厌恶，不喜欢|A feeling of aversion or dislike; something that is disliked. |He couldn't hide his distaste for the vulgar display of wealth. 他无法掩饰自己对 vulgarly displayed wealth 的厌恶。|Synonyms: aversion, dislike, antipathy, repugnance, revulsion|Antonyms: liking, fondness, preference, relish|Noun


distaste:/dɪsˈteɪst/
| v. |使厌恶，使不喜欢|To cause someone to feel aversion or dislike. |The bitter medicine distasted the child.  这苦药让孩子感到厌恶。|Synonyms: repel, disgust, nauseate, sicken|Antonyms: attract, please, delight, appeal|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desgouster (Modern French dégoûter), from des- (expressing reversal) + gouster "to taste". Ultimately from Latin gustus "taste".
