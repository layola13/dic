leapfroggers:/ˈliːpˌfrɒɡərz/ (US) /ˈliːpˌfrɒɡəz/ (UK)| n.|跳蛙者；超越者|People or organizations that surpass others by a large margin, often by adopting new technologies or strategies.|The small startup leapfrogged its larger competitors by embracing mobile technology first. 这家小型初创公司率先采用移动技术，从而超越了规模更大的竞争对手。|
例句：
These leapfroggers are changing the landscape of the industry. 这些超越者正在改变行业的格局。

Several Asian economies leapfrogged the industrialized West in mobile adoption.  几个亚洲经济体在移动应用方面超越了西方工业化国家。

|近义词：outstrip, surpass, overtake, eclipse|反义词: lag behind, fall behind|词性:noun (plural)|

leapfrog:/ˈliːpfrɒɡ/ (US) /ˈliːpfrɒɡ/ (UK)| v.|跳蛙；超越；跳过|To surpass or overtake someone or something, often by a significant margin.|The company leapfrogged its rivals in the market. 这家公司在市场上超越了竞争对手。|
例句：
They leapfrogged several stages of development. 他们跳过了几个发展阶段。

The new technology allowed the company to leapfrog the competition. 新技术使该公司能够超越竞争对手。

|近义词：outstrip, surpass, overtake, eclipse|反义词: lag behind, fall behind|词性:verb|
