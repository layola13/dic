calk:/kɔːk/| n.| 填缝；(马蹄上的)防滑钉|A substance used for filling the joints between planks in wooden ships or the seams between bricks in a wall, or a sharp metal projection on the bottom of a horseshoe or boot to prevent slipping.|The blacksmith carefully fitted calks to the horse's shoes to provide better grip on icy roads.|近义词：sealant, filler, stud|反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 gap, opening)|词性: 名词

calk:/kɔːk/| vt.| 填塞；装防滑钉|To fill or close seams or crevices with a sealant, or to provide with calks.|He calked the cracks in the boat to make it watertight.  |近义词：seal, caulk, stud|反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 uncover, expose)|词性: 动词


词源:  From Middle English *calken, *cauken, from Old North French *cauquer, *calquer (“to press, tread down”), from Latin calcare (“to tread, trample”), from calc-, calx (“heel”).  Related to heel.
