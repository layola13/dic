humoralism:/ˈhjuːmərəlɪzəm/
n.
体液学说，体液病理说
A system of medicine detailing the makeup and workings of the human body, adopted by the Greeks and Romans and persisting through the Middle Ages and later, positing that an excess or deficiency of any of four distinct bodily fluids in a person—known as humors or humours—directly influences their temperament and health.
例句：Humoralism was the dominant medical theory for centuries.  体液学说几个世纪以来一直是占主导地位的医学理论。

例句：The doctor explained the patient's condition based on the principles of humoralism. 医生根据体液学说的原理解释了病人的病情。

近义词：humorism, four humors theory
反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps modern medicine/germ theory)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hūmorālis, from Latin hūmor (“moisture, liquid”) (from Proto-Indo-European *suH- (“sap, juice”)) + -ālis (suffix forming adjectives of relation).  Compare French humoral (“humoral”), Spanish humoral (“humoral”).  Surface analysis is humor +‎ -al +‎ -ism.
