induction:/ɪnˈdʌkʃən/ (US), /ɪnˈdʌkʃn/ (UK)| n.| 就职；入门；归纳；感应|The process of being formally introduced into a new job or position; the process of introducing someone to a new skill, subject, or activity; a method of reasoning that moves from specific instances to a general conclusion; the production of an electric or magnetic state by the proximity (without contact) of an electrified or magnetized body.|例句：The induction of new employees is crucial for their success. 新员工的入职培训对他们的成功至关重要。

例句：The induction ceremony for the new CEO was held last week. 新任首席执行官的就职典礼于上周举行。

近义词：initiation, onboarding, introduction, inference, deduction|反义词：deduction, conclusion|词性：名词
