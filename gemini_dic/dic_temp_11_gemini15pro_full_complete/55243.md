dissimilation:/dɪˌsɪməˈleɪʃən/ | n. |异化，异化作用 |The process by which one sound becomes less like another nearby sound, often to make the two sounds easier to distinguish. |  The word "february" is often pronounced "febuary" through dissimilation.| 近义词: differentiation | 反义词: assimilation | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *dissimilātiōn- (stem of dissimilātiō), from dissimilāre (“to make unlike”) + -tiō.  Dissimilāre is from dis- + similāre (“to make like”), from similis (“like”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *sem- (“one; as one”).
