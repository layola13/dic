hold-over:/ˈhəʊldˌəʊvər/ (US) /ˈhəʊldˌəʊvə(r)/ (UK)| n. adj.|遗留物，延期的事物，留任者|Something that remains from an earlier period, especially a film that continues to be shown in a cinema longer than originally intended. As an adjective, it describes something that is continuing from a previous period.|The film was a hold-over from the summer season. 这部电影是夏季档的遗留影片。|synonyms: remnant, survival, leftover, carry-over|antonyms: innovation, novelty|noun
