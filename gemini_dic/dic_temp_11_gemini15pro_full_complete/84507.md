ham-fistedly:/ˌhæmˈfɪstɪdli/
adverb

clumsily, awkwardly, ineptly, gracelessly
deftly, skillfully, adroitly, gracefully

ETYMOLOGY->
From "ham-fisted" + "-ly."  "Ham-fisted" likely originates from the image of a large, clumsy hand resembling a ham, suggesting a lack of dexterity.  "Ham" in this context refers to the thick part of a hog's leg, and "fisted" refers to having a closed hand.  The suffix "-ly" is used to form adverbs.
