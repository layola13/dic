non-inflationary:/ˌnɒnɪnˈfleɪʃənəri/ (US) /ˌnɒnɪnˈfleɪʃənri/ (UK)| adj.|非通货膨胀的；不引起通货膨胀的|Not causing or characterized by inflation, a general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money.|The government implemented non-inflationary policies to stabilize the economy. 政府实施了非通胀政策以稳定经济。|The central bank aims for non-inflationary growth. 中央银行的目标是非通胀性增长。|近义词:stable, deflationary|反义词:inflationary|词性:形容词

non-inflationary:/ˌnɒnɪnˈfleɪʃənəri/ (US) /ˌnɒnɪnˈfleɪʃənri/ (UK)| n.|非通货膨胀|The state of not causing or being characterized by inflation.  While less common as a noun, it can refer to the concept itself.  |The finance minister emphasized the importance of non-inflationary for sustained economic growth.  财政部长强调了非通货膨胀对于经济持续增长的重要性。|Maintaining non-inflationary is a key goal of monetary policy. 维持非通胀状态是货币政策的关键目标。|近义词:price stability|反义词:inflation|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
non-:  a prefix of Latin origin meaning "not," "opposite of," "lacking," "without."
inflationary: derived from "inflation," which comes from the Latin word "inflare," meaning "to blow into" or "inflate."
