nether:/ˈneðər/ (US), /ˈneðə/ (UK)| adj.|下面的；下方的；阴间的；地狱的|Situated below or underneath something else; relating to the world of the dead.|The nether regions of the earth are believed to be very hot. 地球的下层区域被认为非常热。|synonyms: lower, under, infernal, underground|antonyms: upper, above, celestial|adjective

nether:/ˈneðər/ (US), /ˈneðə/ (UK)| n.|下部；底部（常用于nethers）|The lower or under part of something, often used humorously or euphemistically to refer to the buttocks or genitals.|He caught his nethers on the barbed wire fence. 他的下体被铁丝网缠住了。|synonyms: bottom, underside, buttocks|antonyms: top, upper side|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *niþera, *neþera (comparative of *niþer, *neoþor "down, below"), from Proto-Germanic *nitheraz, from Proto-Indo-European *ni- "down, below."  Cognates include Old Frisian nithera, Old Saxon nithar, Old High German nidar, Old Norse niðri, Gothic niþar.
