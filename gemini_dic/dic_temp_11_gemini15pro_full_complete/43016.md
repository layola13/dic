cowblakes:/ˈkaʊˌbleɪks/ | n. | 恐吓; 虚张声势 | An act of intimidation or a bluff.  It suggests an empty threat or a display of false bravado. |  He tried to cowblakes his opponent, but it didn't work. 他试图恐吓他的对手，但没有成功。 | synonyms: bluff, intimidation, bluster, bravado | antonyms: appeasement, conciliation | noun

Etymology: Origin uncertain. Possibly from a dialectal variant of "cow," suggesting intimidation, and "blakes," possibly related to "bleak" or "black," adding a sense of ominousness.  The word is relatively rare and informal.
