bishoped:/ˈbɪʃəpt/
|v.| 使…成为主教;任命…为主教;用象控制（对手的王）|To appoint someone as a bishop, or in chess, to put the opponent's king in a position where it cannot move without being captured.|He was bishoped in 1998. 他于1998年被任命为主教。|The white king was quickly bishoped. 白方国王很快就被象控制住了。|appoint, consecrate|dismiss, depose|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *biscopian, from biscop "bishop" (from Late Latin episcopus, from Greek episkopos "overseer," from epi- "over" + skopos "watcher"). The chess meaning arises from the diagonal movement of the bishop piece, resembling the mitre worn by bishops.
