antipragmatism:/ˌæntiˈpræɡmətɪzəm/| n. |反实用主义|A philosophical doctrine that opposes or rejects pragmatism, emphasizing instead abstract principles, absolute truths, or a priori reasoning.|His antipragmatism led him to reject any theory that couldn't be proven with absolute certainty.|近义词: idealism, absolutism|反义词: pragmatism|词性:noun

antipragmatism:/ˌæntiˈpræɡmətɪzəm/| adj. |反实用主义的|Characterized by or relating to antipragmatism.|He took an antipragmatic stance on the issue, prioritizing philosophical purity over practical results.|近义词: idealistic, absolutist|反义词: pragmatic|词性:adjective
