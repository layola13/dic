generalizabilities:/ˌdʒɛnərələɪzəˈbɪlətiz/| n. | 可概括性，可推广性 | The extent to which research findings can be applied to settings, populations, and conditions beyond the original study. | The study's small sample size limited its generalizabilities. 这项研究的小样本量限制了它的可推广性。|  generalizability, universality, applicability | specificity, particularity | noun

generalizability:/ˌdʒɛnərələɪzəˈbɪləti/| n. | 可概括性，可推广性 | The quality of being generalizable; the extent to which research findings can be applied to other contexts. | The generalizability of the findings to other populations is questionable.  这些发现对其他人群的推广性值得怀疑。 | generalizabilities, universality, applicability | specificity, particularity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From generalize + -ability.  Generalize comes from French généraliser, from Late Latin generalizare "make general," from generalis "general."
