enmew: /ɛnˈmjuː/ | v. |  囚禁，笼罩 | To confine or enclose, especially in a restrictive or stifling manner. |  The fog enmewed the city, making it feel isolated.  浓雾笼罩着城市，使它感觉与世隔绝。 | 近义词：confine, imprison, trap, enclose | 反义词：liberate, free, release | 词性: 动词

词源:  "Enmew" is a relatively rare word, likely derived from "mew," meaning a cage for hawks, especially during molting.  This, in turn, comes from the Old French word "muer," meaning "to molt" or "to shed feathers."  The prefix "en-" adds the sense of enclosure or confinement.
