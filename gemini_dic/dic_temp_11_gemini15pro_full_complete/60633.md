eleaticism:/ˌɛliˈætɪsɪzəm/ | n. |伊利亚学派|The doctrine of the Eleatic school of philosophy, characterized by its emphasis on the One, the denial of change and motion, and the belief that the senses are deceptive.|Parmenides' poem is a classic expression of Eleaticism.  巴门尼德的诗是伊利亚学派思想的经典表达。|Near synonyms:  monism, idealism |Antonyms: pluralism, materialism|noun
