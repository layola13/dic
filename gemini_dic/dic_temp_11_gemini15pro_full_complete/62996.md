enormity:/ɪˈnɔːrmɪti/ | n. | 滔天大罪；巨大；严重性；极恶 | An act of extreme wickedness.  The immense size or scale of something. The great seriousness or gravity of something. | The enormity of the crime shocked the nation.  这起罪行的滔天罪行震惊了全国。| synonyms: atrocity, wickedness, immensity, vastness, magnitude, gravity, seriousness | antonyms: triviality, insignificance, pettiness | noun

enormities:/ɪˈnɔːrmɪtiz/ | n. |  滔天大罪（复数）；巨大（复数）；严重性（复数）；极恶（复数） | Plural form of enormity.  Refers to multiple acts of extreme wickedness or instances of immense size, scale, seriousness, or gravity. | The enormities committed during the war will never be forgotten.  战争期间犯下的滔天罪行将永远不会被忘记。| synonyms: atrocities, wickednesses, immensities, vastnesses, magnitudes, gravities, seriousnesses | antonyms: trivialities, insignificances, pettinesses | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *enormité*, from Old French *enormité*, from Latin *ēnormitās* (“deviation from the rule, irregularity”), from *ēnormis* (“irregular, enormous”), from *ē-* (variant of *ex-*, “out of, from”) + *norma* (“rule, pattern, standard”).
