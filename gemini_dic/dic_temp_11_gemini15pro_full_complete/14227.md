autocoherer:/ˌɔːtəʊkoʊˈhɪərər/ | n. |自动粉末检波器|A device used in early radio receivers to detect radio waves. It consisted of a tube containing metal filings that would clump together (cohere) when a radio wave passed through them, thus reducing the electrical resistance and allowing a current to flow.  This current could then be used to activate a relay and produce a click in a headset. |The autocoherer was a key component in Marconi's early wireless telegraph system.| Synonyms: coherer | Antonyms: None | Noun
