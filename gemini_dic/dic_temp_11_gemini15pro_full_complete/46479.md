daimyo:/daɪˈmjoʊ/| n.|大名|A feudal lord in Japan, especially during the Edo period (1603-1867).  They were powerful territorial rulers, subject to the shogun.|The daimyo ruled his province with an iron fist.  这位大名用铁腕统治他的省份。|Synonym: feudal lord, magnate|Antonym: peasant, commoner|noun

daimyo:/daɪˈmjoʊ/| n.|大名|A powerful Japanese feudal lord, especially one who ruled a substantial domain from the 10th century until the latter half of the 19th century.|Each daimyo commanded a private army of samurai. 每位大名都指挥着一支由武士组成的私人军队。|Synonym: shogun, warlord|Antonym: peasant, commoner|noun
