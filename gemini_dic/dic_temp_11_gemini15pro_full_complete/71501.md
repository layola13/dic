flange:/flændʒ/
n.
凸缘；法兰；轮缘
A projecting flat rim, collar, or rib on an object, serving to strengthen or stiffen it or to attach it to something else.
The pipe has a flange at each end.  每个管子两端都有一个法兰。

flange:/flændʒ/
n.
(车轮的)轮缘
A projecting edge on a wheel, designed to keep it from running off the rails.
The train derailed when a wheel flange broke. 火车在一个车轮的轮缘断裂后脱轨了。

flange:/flændʒ/
vt.
给…装上凸缘；给…装法兰
To provide with a flange.
The pipes were flanged together. 管道用法兰连接在一起。

flange:/flændʒ/
n.
(鸟或哺乳动物的)翼，翅
A wing or winglike part of an animal, especially a bird or mammal.
The bat flapped its leathery flanges.  蝙蝠拍打着它皮革般的翅膀。


Etymology:
From Old French *flange (“broad flat piece”), of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old French flanc (“flank”).

Synonyms:
rim, edge, lip, collar, rib, brim, ledge, shoulder, flange plate, companion flange, blind flange

Antonyms:
(For the mechanical sense)  groove, recess, depression

Near Antonyms:
center, core
