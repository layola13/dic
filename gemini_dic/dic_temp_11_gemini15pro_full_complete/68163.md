faineance:/ˈfeɪniəns/| n.|懒惰，怠惰|An indolent disposition; avoidance of exertion; laziness.|He spent his days in faineance, lounging on the sofa and reading novels. 他整天无所事事，躺在沙发上看小说。|Synonyms: laziness, indolence, idleness, sloth, shiftlessness|Antonyms: diligence, industry, assiduity|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *faineantise*, from *faignant* (“idle, lazy”), present participle of *faindre* (“to feign, pretend”), from Latin *fingere* (“to shape, form, invent, feign”).
