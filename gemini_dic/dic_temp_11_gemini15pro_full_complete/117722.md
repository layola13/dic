melancholy:/ˈmɛləŋkɑːli/ (US) /ˈmɛlənkəli/ (UK)| adj. n.|忧郁的，忧伤的；忧郁，伤感|A feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause. |She felt a deep melancholy after her friend moved away. 她朋友搬走后，她感到深深的忧郁。| The melancholy atmosphere of the abandoned house was unsettling. 这栋废弃房屋的忧郁气氛令人不安。|Synonyms: sadness, gloom, dejection, depression, wistfulness|Antonyms: happiness, joy, elation, cheerfulness|adjective/noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *mélancolie*, from Late Latin *melancholia*, from Ancient Greek *μελαγχολία* (melankholía, “black bile”), from *μέλας* (mélas, “black”) + *χολή* (kholḗ, “bile”).  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy.
