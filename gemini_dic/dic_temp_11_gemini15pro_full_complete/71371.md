flagellators:/ˈflædʒəˌleɪtərz/
n.|鞭毛虫|A microscopic organism that moves by means of one or more whip-like appendages called flagella.|Many flagellators live in freshwater environments.|Synonyms: Mastigophora, flagellate|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, could consider non-motile organisms like sporozoans)|Noun

flagella:/fləˈdʒɛlə/
n.|鞭毛|Slender, threadlike appendages that enable many protozoa, bacteria, spermatozoa, etc., to swim.|The bacterium uses its flagella to propel itself through the liquid.|Synonyms: cilium (singular), cilia (plural), flagellum (singular)|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|Noun
