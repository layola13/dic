lily-whiteness:/ˈlɪli ˈwaɪtnəs/| n.|百合般的洁白;纯洁无瑕|Extreme or unnatural paleness, often associated with purity or fragility.  It evokes the image of the white lily flower, traditionally symbolic of purity and innocence.|Her lily-whiteness contrasted sharply with the tanned faces of the other beachgoers. 她百合般的洁白与其他海滩游客晒黑的肤色形成了鲜明的对比。|Synonyms: pallor, whiteness, purity, innocence|Antonyms: tan, swarthiness, impurity|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"lily" (from Old English lilie, from Latin lilium) + "-whiteness" (from "white" (from Old English hwīt) + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality))
