overbragging: /ˌoʊvərˈbræɡɪŋ/
| n. | 过度吹嘘，过分自夸 | The act of bragging excessively, often to the point of being annoying or unbelievable.  | He lost the respect of his colleagues due to his constant overbragging about his achievements. 他因不断吹嘘自己的成就而失去了同事的尊重。| 近义词：boasting, bragging, showing off | 反义词：modesty, humility | 词性: 名词

词源:  "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "bragging" (present participle of "brag," from Middle English *braggen*, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Old Norse *braka* "crack, crash," suggesting noisy or boastful behavior).
