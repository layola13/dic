noncomprehensibly:/ˌnɒnkɒmprɪˈhensɪbli/ | adv. | 无法理解地，难以理解地 | in a way that is impossible to understand |  The instructions were written so noncomprehensibly that no one could follow them.  说明书写得让人无法理解，以至于没有人能按照它操作。 | incomprehensibly, unintelligibly | understandably, clearly | adverb

Etymology:
From non- (not) + comprehensible + -ly (adverbial suffix). Comprehensible comes from Late Latin comprehensibilis, from comprehendere (to grasp mentally), from com- (together) + prehendere (to grasp).
