frankensteins:/ˈfræŋkənˌstaɪnz/ | n. | 科学怪人（的复数）; 弗兰肯斯坦的怪物（的复数）;  | A creation that becomes destructive to its creator; something that becomes a monster or a destructive force to the one who made it. |例句：The faulty economic policies became frankensteins, wreaking havoc on the country's finances. 错误的经济政策变成了弗兰肯斯坦式的怪物，对国家的财政造成了严重破坏。

例句：His inventions, meant to help humanity, turned into frankensteins, threatening the very existence of humankind.  他的发明本意是帮助人类，却变成了弗兰肯斯坦式的怪物，威胁着人类的生存。| 近义词：monsters, abominations | 反义词: creations, inventions | 词性: 名词

词源：Frankenstein comes from the name of Victor Frankenstein, the protagonist in Mary Shelley's 1818 novel *Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus*. He creates a monster from body parts.  Over time, the name "Frankenstein" has become synonymous with the monster itself, rather than its creator.
