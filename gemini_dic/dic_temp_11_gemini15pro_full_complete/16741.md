barterer:/ˈbɑːrtərər/| n.| 以物易物者，交换者|A person who exchanges goods or services for other goods or services without using money.|  Example: In the absence of a common currency, the villagers became skillful barterers, trading their produce for tools and other necessities.  在没有共同货币的情况下，村民们变成了熟练的以物易物者，用他们的农产品交换工具和其他必需品。

Example: The barterer offered a handcrafted necklace in exchange for a week's worth of food.  这个以物易物者提供了一条手工制作的项链来交换一周的食物。|近义词：trader, exchanger, swapper|反义词: buyer, seller, purchaser|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *barteren*, from Anglo-French *barater*, *baratier*, of uncertain origin, probably from Vulgar Latin *baratāre* (“to cheat, deceive, barter”), of unknown ultimate origin. Cognate with Old French *barater* (“to cheat, deceive”), Spanish *baratar* (“to barter, exchange”), Italian *barattare* (“to barter, exchange”).  More at barter.
