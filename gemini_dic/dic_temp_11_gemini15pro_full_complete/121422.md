misinformation:/ˌmɪsɪnfərˈmeɪʃn/
n.
错误信息；虚假信息
False or inaccurate information, especially that which is deliberately intended to deceive.
The spread of misinformation online can have serious consequences. 网上错误信息的传播可能会造成严重后果。

The report was based on misinformation and deliberate distortions. 这份报告是建立在错误信息和蓄意歪曲的基础上的。


近义词：disinformation, false information, propaganda, lies, untruths
反义词：truth, accurate information, facts
词性：名词


词源：
From mis- (meaning "wrong, bad, or wrongly") + information.  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old French "mes-" from a Germanic source akin to Old English "mis-". "Information" comes from Old French "informacion" meaning "formation, instruction, education," from Late Latin "informationem" (nominative "informatio") "outline, concept, idea," noun of action from past-participle stem of "informare" "to give form to, to instruct, train, educate, teach."
