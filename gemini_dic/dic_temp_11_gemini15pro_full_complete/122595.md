modernist:/ˈmɒdənɪst/ (UK) /ˈmɑːdərɪst/ (US)| n. adj.|现代主义者，现代派的|A person who advocates or practices modernism, especially in the arts.|例句：The architect was a renowned modernist, known for his innovative designs.  这位建筑师是一位著名的现代主义者，以其创新的设计而闻名。

例句：The museum's collection includes many works by early modernists.  博物馆的藏品包括许多早期现代主义者的作品。|近义词：contemporary, avant-garde|反义词：traditionalist, conservative|词性: 名词/形容词

modernistic:/ˌmɒdəˈnɪstɪk/ (UK) /ˌmɑːdərˈnɪstɪk/ (US)| adj.|现代主义风格的|Relating to or characteristic of modernism, especially in the arts; having a consciously modern style.|例句：The building's modernistic design clashed with the surrounding historical architecture.  这座建筑的现代主义设计与周围的历史建筑格格不入。

例句：She furnished her apartment with modernistic furniture and abstract art. 她用现代主义风格的家具和抽象艺术装饰了她的公寓。|近义词：contemporary, avant-garde|反义词：traditionalist, old-fashioned|词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From modern + -ist.  Modern comes from Late Latin modernus,  meaning "of the present mode or fashion,"  from Latin modo "just now," ablative of modus "measure, manner."
