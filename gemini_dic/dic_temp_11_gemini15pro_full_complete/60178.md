effete:/ɪˈfiːt/ | adj. | 衰弱的，软弱的；缺乏活力的；（尤指男子）柔弱的，女人气的 | (of a person) no longer capable of effective action; overrefined and ineffectual; decadent or having lost character, vitality, or strength | The once vibrant city had become effete and decadent.  这座曾经充满活力的城市已经变得衰弱颓废。|  The effete aristocracy seemed incapable of decisive action.  这个衰落的贵族阶层似乎无力采取果断行动。 | 近义词: decadent, weak, enfeebled, exhausted, spent, sterile | 反义词: strong, vigorous, virile, potent | 词性: 形容词

effete:/ɪˈfiːt/ | adj. | （尤指男子）柔弱的，女人气的 | characterized by excessive refinement or delicacy; affected, effeminate |  He dismissed his critics as effete intellectuals. 他把批评他的人斥为柔弱的知识分子。 |  His effete mannerisms seemed out of place in the rugged environment.  他女人气的举止在这粗犷的环境中显得格格不入。| 近义词: effeminate, womanish, unmanly | 反义词: manly, masculine | 词性: 形容词


词源:
From French *effété*, past participle of *efféter* "to exhaust, enervate," from Latin *effetus* "that has given birth, worn out by bearing," from *ex-* "out" + *fetus* "offspring."
