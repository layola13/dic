osteoblast: /ˈɒstiəʊˌblɑːst/ (UK) /ˈɑːstioʊˌblæst/ (US)| n. |成骨细胞|A cell that secretes the matrix for bone formation.|Osteoblasts are responsible for synthesizing and depositing new bone material. 成骨细胞负责合成和沉积新的骨骼材料。|Synonyms: bone-forming cell|Antonyms: osteoclast|noun

词源:
From osteo- + -blast.  "Osteo-" comes from the Greek word "osteon" meaning "bone". "-blast" comes from the Greek word "blastos" meaning "germ" or "bud", referring to a cell that is actively producing something.
