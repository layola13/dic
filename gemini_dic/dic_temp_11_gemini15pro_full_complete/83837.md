hakkas:/ˈhækəs/ | n. |客家人；客家话|A Han Chinese subgroup originating in the mountainous regions of Guangdong, Fujian, Jiangxi, Guangxi, and other provinces of southern China, also found in Taiwan, Southeast Asia, and other parts of the world.  They speak Hakka, a distinct Sinitic language. |Many Hakkas emigrated to Southeast Asia in the 19th and 20th centuries. 许多客家人在19和20世纪移民到东南亚。|Synonyms:  Hakka people,  Hakka Chinese|Antonyms:  N/A|noun
