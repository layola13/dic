muttering:/ˈmʌtərɪŋ (US)/ /ˈmʌtərɪŋ (UK)/
| n. | 喃喃自语，嘀咕 | The act of speaking softly and indistinctly, often to oneself. |  There was a lot of muttering in the crowd after the announcement.  公告发布后，人群中一片窃窃私语。| murmur, whisper, grumble | shout, yell | noun

mutter:/ˈmʌtər (US)/ /ˈmʌtər (UK)/
| v. | 喃喃自语，嘀咕 | To speak softly and indistinctly, often to oneself. | He muttered something about the unfairness of it all. 他嘀咕着这一切的不公平。| murmur, whisper, grumble | shout, yell | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mutteren*, probably of imitative origin.  Related to Dutch *motteren* and German *murren*.
