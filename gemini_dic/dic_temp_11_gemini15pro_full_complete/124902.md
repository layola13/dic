muddle:/ˈmʌd(ə)l/| n. v. |混乱，糊涂；弄混，弄乱|A state of confusion or disorder. To make something confused or disordered. |The papers on his desk were in a complete muddle. 他的办公桌上的文件一片混乱。 He muddled the instructions and ruined the experiment. 他把指示搞混了，毁了实验。|近义词：mess, confusion, jumble, disorganize, confuse|反义词：order, organization, clarity, arrange, clarify|词性:名词,动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *modlen*, frequentative of *mud*, probably meaning "to dabble in mud," hence "to behave in a confused or clumsy manner." Related to mud.
