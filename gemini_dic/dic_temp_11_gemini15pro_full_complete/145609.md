overconsciousnesses:/ˈoʊvərˈkɒnʃəsnɪsɪz/| n. |过度意识，过分在意|Excessive awareness of oneself and one's actions, often leading to self-consciousness and anxiety.  The state of being overly aware of oneself and how one is perceived by others.|She suffered from overconsciousnesses about her appearance, constantly adjusting her clothes and hair.|近义词：self-consciousness, hyperawareness, sensitivity|反义词:unawareness, obliviousness|词性:noun

Etymology: "over-" (Old English *ofer-, from Proto-Germanic *ubar "over, above") + "conscious" (from Latin conscius "knowing, aware," from com- "with, together" + scire "to know") + "-ness" (Old English -nes(s), forming abstract nouns from adjectives).
