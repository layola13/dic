consecution:/ˌkɒnsɪˈkjuːʃn/ /ˌkɑːnsɪˈkjuːʃn/| n. |连续，接续，一连串|a following of one thing after another in time or order; succession. |The king's reign was marked by a consecution of victories. 国王的统治以一连串的胜利为标志。|The photographs documented the consecution of events leading up to the eruption. 这些照片记录了火山爆发前的一系列事件。|近义词：sequence, succession, series, progression|反义词: interruption, discontinuity|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *consecūtiōn*, from Latin consecūtus, past participle of consecūī, perfect active of consequor (“I follow, ensue”), from con- (“together, with”) + sequor (“I follow”).
