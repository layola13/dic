burlesque: /bərˈlɛsk/ (US), /bɜːˈlɛsk/ (UK) | n.  vt. adj. | 滑稽模仿；讽刺作品；滑稽歌舞表演 | A literary or dramatic work that makes fun of something by imitating it in a humorous or exaggerated way.  A theatrical entertainment of broad and earthy humor, often including striptease.  Characterized by broad humor and ludicrous exaggeration. |  The play was a burlesque of Shakespearean tragedies. 这齣戏是对莎士比亚悲剧的滑稽模仿。 | Synonyms: parody, satire, travesty, caricature, mockery | Antonyms: serious, respectful, reverent | noun
