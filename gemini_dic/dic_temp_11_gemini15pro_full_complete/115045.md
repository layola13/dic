mandore:/ˈmændɔːr/ | n. | 曼陀铃，一种乐器 | A small, lute-like instrument with four to six pairs of strings, popular in the 16th and 17th centuries.  Similar to a lute or small guitar. | Example: The mandore player skillfully plucked the strings, creating a delicate melody. 曼陀铃演奏者熟练地拨动琴弦，创造出优美的旋律。 | Example:  Composers like Denis Gaultier wrote extensively for the mandore.  像丹尼斯·高蒂尔这样的作曲家为曼陀铃创作了大量的作品。 | Synonym: lute, bandora | Antonym: (No direct antonym for a musical instrument) | noun
