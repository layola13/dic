mischaracterizing: /ˌmɪskærəktəˈraɪzɪŋ/
verb
错误地描述，歪曲
present participle of mischaracterize, meaning to describe inaccurately or unfairly.
The media is mischaracterizing the situation. 媒体正在歪曲情况。
Mischaracterizing the motives of the protesters led to further unrest. 歪曲抗议者的动机导致了进一步的动乱。
synonyms: misrepresenting, distorting, falsifying, twisting, misconstruing
antonyms: accurately representing, portraying truthfully, depicting faithfully
verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- + characterize.  Mis- is a prefix of French or Middle English origin meaning "wrong," "badly," or "wrongly." Characterize comes from the Late Latin word characterizare, meaning "to mark with a character."
