niggard:/ˈnɪɡərd/| n. |吝啬鬼；守财奴|A stingy or miserly person; someone unwilling to spend or give. |He was a niggard who refused to donate to charity. 他是个吝啬鬼，不愿向慈善机构捐款。|近义词：miser, skimp, tightwad, Scrooge|反义词：spendthrift, philanthropist|名词

niggardly:/ˈnɪɡərdli/| adj. |吝啬的；少的可怜的|Stingy; mean; miserly; given or done reluctantly or in small quantities. |He gave a niggardly tip to the waiter. 他给了服务员很少的小费.  The company's niggardly budget left no room for new hires. 公司吝啬的预算没有为新员工留下任何空间。|近义词：stingy, meager, paltry, scanty|反义词：generous, lavish, abundant|形容词

词源:
From Middle English *niggard*,  from Old Norse *hnǫggr* ("stingy"), from Proto-Germanic *knugaz* (“stingy, close, tight”).  Cognate with Swedish *njugg* ("stingy"), Danish *nøjsom* ("frugal, thrifty").  First recorded in the 14th century.
