oscillators:/ˈɒsɪleɪtəz/ (US) /ˈɒsɪleɪtəz/ (UK)| n. |振荡器；振动器|An electronic circuit that produces a continuous, repeating electronic signal, usually a sine wave or a square wave.  It converts direct current (DC) from a power supply to an alternating current (AC) signal.|The radio uses a crystal oscillator to generate the carrier wave. 这台收音机使用晶体振荡器产生载波。|synonyms: vibrator, resonator|antonyms: damper, stabilizer|noun
