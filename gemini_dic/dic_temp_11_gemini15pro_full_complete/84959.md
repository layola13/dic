haplogroup:/ˈhæpləʊɡruːp/ (US) /ˈhæpləʊɡruːp/ (UK)| n.|单倍群|A haplogroup is a genetic population group of people who share a common ancestor on the patrilineal or matrilineal line.  It is identified by specific sets of shared genetic variations in their DNA.|Scientists use haplogroups to trace the migration patterns of ancient populations. 科学家利用单倍群来追踪古代人口的迁徙模式。|The man's Y-chromosome haplogroup revealed his ancestry.  这个男人的Y染色体单倍群揭示了他的祖先。|synonyms: genetic group, lineage, clan|antonyms: N/A (Haplogroup is a scientific classification, not a concept with a direct opposite.)|noun
