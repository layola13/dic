horrifyingly:/ˈhɒrɪfaɪɪŋli/ (UK) /ˈhɔːrɪfaɪɪŋli/ (US)| adv.|骇人地，令人恐惧地|In a way that causes horror; terribly.  It describes something done in a manner that inspires horror.|The accident was horrifyingly graphic.  这场事故的画面骇人听闻。|Synonyms:  terrifyingly, dreadfully, frightfully, appallingly|Antonyms:  pleasantly, delightfully, beautifully|Word Type: Adverb

horrify:/ˈhɒrɪfaɪ/ (UK) /ˈhɔːrɪfaɪ/ (US)| v.|使恐惧，使惊骇|To fill with horror; to shock greatly.|The news of the war horrified everyone.  战争的消息吓坏了所有人。|Synonyms:  terrify, frighten, appall, shock|Antonyms:  comfort, reassure, please|Word Type: Verb

horror:/ˈhɒrə(r)/ (UK) /ˈhɔːrər/ (US)| n.|恐惧，惊骇|An intense feeling of fear, shock, or disgust.|She felt a sense of horror when she saw the blood.  看到血时，她感到一阵恐惧。|Synonyms:  terror, fear, dread, fright|Antonyms:  delight, pleasure, joy|Word Type: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *horrifyen*, from Old French *horrifier*, from Latin *horrificāre* "to make rough or bristly," from *horrificus* "causing horror," from *horror* "bristling, dread, awe," of uncertain origin.
