goodlyhead: /ˈɡʊdliˌhɛd/ | n. | 好人，正派人 (古语，现已罕用) | A person of good character or reputation, especially a man.  This word is archaic and rarely used in modern English. |  He was known throughout the village as a goodlyhead. 他在整个村子里都被认为是个好人。 | 近义词: good person, upright citizen, worthy | 反义词: scoundrel, villain, rogue | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *godlyhed, equivalent to goodly +‎ -head.  "Goodly" meaning "pleasant, attractive, excellent, virtuous" and "-head" a suffix forming nouns of quality or condition.
