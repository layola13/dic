breakouts:/ˈbreɪkaʊts/ (US) /ˈbreɪkaʊts/ (UK)| n.|爆发；突围；越狱；（粉刺）爆发|A sudden increase in the number of cases of a disease or problem. A sudden escape from prison.  The sudden appearance of acne or other skin blemishes.|例句：There was a breakout of measles in the school. 学校爆发了麻疹。

例句：The prisoners planned a daring breakout. 囚犯们计划了一次大胆的越狱。

近义词：eruption, outbreak, escape, rash|反义词: containment, imprisonment, clearing|词性:名词


breakout:/ˈbreɪkaʊt/ (US) /ˈbreɪkaʊt/ (UK)| adj.|突破性的；突出的|Something that is breakout is suddenly extremely successful and popular.|例句：Her breakout role was in a small independent film. 她的突破性角色是在一部小型独立电影中。

例句：The company had a breakout year in terms of sales. 该公司在销售方面取得了突破性的一年。

近义词：successful, popular, breakthrough|反义词: unsuccessful, unpopular|词性:形容词
