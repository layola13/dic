frisker:/ˈfrɪskər/| n.|搜身者；爱嬉戏的人或动物|A person who frisks someone or something.  Someone who is playful and lively.|The security guard, acting as a frisker, patted down everyone entering the concert.|近义词：searcher, pat-down artist, playful person, romp|反义词:stoic, serious person|noun

frisky:/ˈfrɪski/| adj.|活泼的；嬉戏的|Playful and lively.|The frisky puppy chased its tail around the garden.|近义词：playful, lively, spirited, energetic, frolicsome|反义词:lethargic, sluggish, listless|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "frisk,"  which originates from the late 16th century,  meaning "to leap playfully, dance, gambol."  The meaning "to search (a person) for concealed weapons" dates from the mid-20th century.  Likely related to similar words in other Germanic languages suggesting quick movement.
