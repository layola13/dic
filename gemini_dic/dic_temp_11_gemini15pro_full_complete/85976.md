headscarf: /ˈhɛdskɑːrf/ (US) /ˈhɛdskɑːrf/ (UK)
n.|头巾（单数）|A piece of cloth worn over the head, often for religious or cultural reasons.|She wore a colorful headscarf. 她戴着一条色彩鲜艳的头巾。|synonyms: hijab, scarf, veil|antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps uncovered hair)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "head" + "scarf". "Head" comes from Old English "hēafod". "Scarf" comes from Old North French "escherpe" (strip of cloth).
