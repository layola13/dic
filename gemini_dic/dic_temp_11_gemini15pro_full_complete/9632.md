antistrike:/ˌæntiˈstraɪk/| adj.|反罢工的，反对罢工的|Opposed to strikes; designed to prevent or discourage strikes.|The government introduced antistrike legislation. 政府引入了反罢工立法。|近义词：anti-labor, anti-union|反义词:pro-strike, pro-labor|词性:形容词

Etymology:
anti- (prefix) + strike (noun)

"anti-" comes from Greek, meaning "against," "opposite of," or "opposed to."

"strike" comes from Old English "strīcan" meaning "to go," "move," or "stroke."  Its meaning evolved to include hitting or striking something, and then later to the organized cessation of work by employees.
