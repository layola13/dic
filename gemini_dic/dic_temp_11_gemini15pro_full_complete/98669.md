insulate:/ˈɪnsəleɪt/ (US), /ˈɪnsjʊleɪt/ (UK)| v. |隔离，使绝缘，使隔热|To cover or protect something with a material that stops electricity, sound, heat, etc. from getting in or out.|They insulated the attic to reduce heat loss. 他们将阁楼隔热以减少热量散失。|The workers insulated the electrical wires with rubber. 工人们用橡胶将电线绝缘。|近义词：protect, shield, isolate|反义词：expose|词性：动词

insulation:/ˌɪnsəˈleɪʃn/ (US), /ˌɪnsjʊˈleɪʃn/ (UK)| n. |隔离，绝缘，隔热|Material used to insulate something, or the act of insulating something.|The house has good insulation, so it stays warm in winter. 这房子隔热良好，所以冬天很暖和。|Proper insulation can save you money on your energy bills. 适当的隔热可以节省你的能源账单。|近义词：protection, shielding|反义词：exposure|词性：名词

insulator:/ˈɪnsəleɪtər/ (US), /ˈɪnsjʊleɪtər/ (UK)| n. |绝缘体，隔离者|A substance or device that does not readily conduct electricity, sound, or heat.|Rubber is a good insulator. 橡胶是一种良好的绝缘体。|The electrician used an insulator to protect the wires. 电工使用绝缘体来保护电线。|近义词：nonconductor|反义词：conductor|词性：名词
