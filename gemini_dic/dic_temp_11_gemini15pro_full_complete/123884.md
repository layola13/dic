morbidness:/ˈmɔːrbɪdnəs/ /ˈmɔːrˌbɪd.nəs/| n.| 病态，不健康，阴郁|An abnormal and unhealthy interest in disturbing and unpleasant subjects, especially death and disease. |She had a morbidness about her, always dwelling on death and decay.|近义词：morbidity, ghoulishness, macabre|反义词:cheerfulness, wholesomeness|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *morbide, from Late Latin morbidus (“diseased, sickly”), from Latin morbus (“disease, sickness, illness”).  Surface analysis is morb- +‎ -id.
