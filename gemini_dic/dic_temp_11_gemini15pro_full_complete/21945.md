boggled:/ˈbɑːɡəld/ | adj. | 困惑的，感到震惊的 | feeling bewildered or amazed; overwhelmed with astonishment |  I was completely boggled by the complex instructions. 我完全被这些复杂的指令搞糊涂了。 |  Example: The sheer amount of data boggled the mind.  海量的数据令人难以置信。| Synonyms: bewildered, astonished, amazed, dumbfounded, flabbergasted | Antonyms: unfazed, unsurprised, clear-headed | adjective

boggling:/ˈbɑːɡəlɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人困惑的，难以置信的 | overwhelming; astonishing | The complexity of the universe is truly boggling. 宇宙的复杂性真是令人难以置信。 | Example:  It was a boggling experience to see the Grand Canyon for the first time.  第一次看到大峡谷的经历令人叹为观止。| Synonyms: overwhelming, astonishing, astounding, staggering | Antonyms: unimpressive, underwhelming, ordinary | adjective

boggle:/ˈbɑːɡəl/ | v. | 犹豫，畏缩不前; (因吃惊等)睁大眼睛 | To hesitate or shy away from something; to stare with wide eyes in amazement |  The horse boggled at the jump.  这匹马在跳跃前犹豫了。|  Example:  I boggle at the thought of spending so much money. 我一想到要花这么多钱就犹豫不决。| Synonyms: hesitate, waver, flinch, recoil, balk; gape, stare | Antonyms: proceed, continue, advance; ignore, disregard | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *boglen*, frequentative of a verb meaning "to shy away" (as a horse might).
