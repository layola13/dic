gallivants:/ˈɡælɪvænt/ | v. | 漫游，游荡，闲逛 | To wander or travel around for pleasure, especially in a carefree and irresponsible way. | He spent his gap year gallivanting around Europe. 他用间隔年在欧洲四处游荡。|
近义词：roam, wander, traipse, gad about | 反义词：stay, remain, settle down | 词性: 动词

gallivanting:/ˈɡælɪvæntɪŋ/ | n. | 漫游，游荡，闲逛 | The act of wandering or traveling around for pleasure. |  Her gallivanting took her to some exotic locations. 她的漫游带她去了一些异国风情的地方。 | 近义词：roaming, wandering, traipsing | 反义词：staying, remaining | 词性: 名词

词源:  From the 16th century. Possibly from obsolete French *gallivanter, of unknown origin.
