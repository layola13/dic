dimly:/ˈdɪmli/| adv.|昏暗地，模糊地|In a way that is not clearly seen or understood; faintly.|The room was dimly lit. 房间里光线昏暗。|Near Synonyms: faintly, obscurely, vaguely|Antonyms: brightly, clearly, distinctly|adverb
dimly:/ˈdɪmli/| adv.|微弱地，朦胧地|In a way that is not clearly remembered or perceived; vaguely.|I dimly remember meeting him years ago. 我依稀记得几年前见过他。|Near Synonyms: faintly, vaguely, slightly|Antonyms: clearly, vividly, distinctly|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *dimmeli*, from dim + -ly.  The root *dim* comes from Old English *dimm* meaning "dark, obscure, faint," ultimately from Proto-Germanic *dimmaz.
