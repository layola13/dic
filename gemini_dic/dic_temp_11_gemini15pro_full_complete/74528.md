fraternism:/frəˈtɜːrnɪzəm/
n.
兄弟情谊；博爱；友爱
The spirit of brotherhood and comradeship; the quality of being fraternal.
Example 1:  The fraternity house was known for its strong sense of fraternism.  兄弟会以其浓厚的兄弟情谊而闻名。
Example 2:  The principles of liberty, equality, and fraternism are central to the French Revolution. 自由、平等、博爱原则是法国大革命的核心。
近义词: brotherhood, camaraderie, fellowship
反义词: hostility, animosity, antagonism
词性: noun

fraternity:/frəˈtɜːrnəti/
n.
兄弟会；协会；友爱
A group of people sharing a common profession or interests; a male student society in a university or college.
Example 1: He joined a fraternity in his first year of college. 他大学一年级就加入了兄弟会。
Example 2: There's a strong sense of fraternity among the firefighters. 消防员之间有很强的兄弟情谊。
近义词: brotherhood, fellowship, association, guild, society
反义词: sorority (specifically for female student societies)
词性: noun

fraternal:/frəˈtɜːrnl/
adj.
兄弟般的；友爱的
Of or like a brother or brothers; showing or involving brotherly love.
Example 1:  They share a fraternal bond. 他们之间有着兄弟般的感情。
Example 2:  The fraternal twins looked very similar.  这对异卵双胞胎看起来很像。
近义词: brotherly, sisterly, friendly
反义词: unfriendly, hostile
词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *fraternitas, from Latin frater ("brother").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *bʰréh₂tēr ("brother").
