overobject:/ˈoʊvərˌɑːbdʒɪkt/ | n. | 超对象；过度客体化 | An overobject is a term used in object relations theory to describe an object that is imbued with excessive and unrealistic significance, often representing a fusion of the self with the object. It can be a person, idea, or thing.  This often stems from early childhood experiences where the boundaries between self and other are blurred. |  The child's blanket became an overobject, representing comfort and security.  孩子的小毯子变成了一个超对象，代表着舒适和安全。 | synonyms: fetish, obsession | antonyms: none | noun
