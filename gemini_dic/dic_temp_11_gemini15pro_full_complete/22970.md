bottoms-up:/ˈbɑːtəmz ʌp/ | adj. adv.|  干杯；底朝天 |  Used as a toast before drinking, meaning "drink the entire contents of your glass." Also can describe a situation where something is completely overturned or reversed. | Let's raise our glasses and say "bottoms up!" 让我们举杯，干杯！| Synonyms: cheers, down the hatch | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider "sip" or "take a small drink") | Phrase
bottoms-up:/ˈbɑːtəmz ʌp/ | adj.|  自下而上的 |  Describes a method or approach that starts with the details and works its way up to the bigger picture. | They adopted a bottoms-up approach to management, soliciting input from all employees. 他们采取自下而上的管理方法，征求所有员工的意见。 | Synonyms: grassroots, ground-up | Antonyms: top-down | Adjective
