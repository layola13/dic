abderian:/æbˈdɪəriən/ | adj. | 阿布德拉的；过度爱笑的；傻笑的 | Of or relating to Abdera, an ancient Thracian city whose inhabitants were reputed to be simple-minded and given to foolish laughter.  Characterized by excessive or incessant laughter; prone to foolish mirth. | He gave an abderian laugh at the silly joke. 他对这个愚蠢的笑话发出阿布德拉式的笑声。 | Synonym:  mirthful, jovial, giddy, fatuous | Antonym: serious, somber, grave | word type: adjective

Etymology: From Abdera (Ἄβδηρα), an ancient Greek city in Thrace, whose inhabitants were stereotyped as simple-minded and prone to laughter, perhaps due to the local climate or water.  The philosopher Democritus, known for his emphasis on cheerfulness, was from Abdera.
