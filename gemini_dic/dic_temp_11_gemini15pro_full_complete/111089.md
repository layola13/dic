lit: /lɪt/ | adj. | 点燃的，照亮的；（非正式）兴奋的，狂热的 | past tense and past participle of light, illuminated; (slang) exciting, excellent | The room was lit by candles. 房间被蜡烛照亮。| The party was lit! 派对太嗨了！| 近义词：illuminated, bright, exciting, awesome | 反义词：dark, unlit, boring, dull | 词性: 形容词


lit: /lɪt/ | adj. | (slang) Drunk or intoxicated. |  Shortened form of "lit up." | He was so lit last night. 他昨晚喝醉了。| 近义词：drunk, intoxicated, tipsy | 反义词：sober | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Middle English *litten, from Old English *lȳtan (“to shine”), from Proto-Germanic *leuhtaną (“to shine, be light”), from Proto-Indo-European *lewk- (“to shine, be light”). Cognate with Scots lit (“lighted”), West Frisian ljochtsje (“to shine”), Dutch lichten (“to shine”), German leuchten (“to shine”), Swedish lysa (“to shine”).
