blowsy:/ˈblaʊzi/ | adj. |  蓬乱的，散乱的；(尤指女子)衣着邋遢的；(花)过分艳丽的 |  Disordered and untidy in appearance, typically used to describe a woman or flowers that are overly showy and slightly unkempt. | The blowsy woman stumbled down the street.  那个衣衫不整的女人在街上踉跄而行。 |  disheveled, untidy, rumpled, sloppy, frowzy | neat, tidy, trim, groomed | adjective

blowsy:/ˈblaʊzi/ | adj. |  (因炎热或饮酒)脸色红润的，面部浮肿的 |  Having a flushed and swollen appearance, typically from heat or drinking. |  His face was blowsy after a night of heavy drinking.  他喝了一夜酒后，脸色红润浮肿。|  flushed, ruddy, red-faced | pale | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From earlier *blowze "a woman with disheveled hair," perhaps related to blaze (n.) in the sense "bright spot, distinctive mark."  The notion is perhaps of a flushed or heated face.
