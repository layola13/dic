interrogative: /ɪnˌtɛrəˈɡætɪv/ | adj. n.| 疑问的，疑问词 | Used to ask a question; a word used in asking a question. | Example: "What" is an interrogative pronoun.  “What”是一个疑问代词。 | Synonym: questioning, inquisitive | Antonym: declarative, affirmative | word type: adjective/noun

interrogative: /ɪnˌtɛrəˈɡætɪv/ |  n.| 疑问词 | A word used to ask a question. | Example:  The interrogative "who" is the subject of the sentence. 疑问词“who”是句子的主语。| Synonym: question word | Antonym:  N/A | word type: noun

USAGE->
Simple Present:
What is your favorite color? = 你最喜欢的颜色是什么？
Where do you live? = 你住在哪里？

Present Continuous:
What are you watching? = 你在看什么？
Why is he crying? = 他为什么哭？

Present Perfect:
What have you learned today? = 你今天学到了什么？
Where has she been? = 她去过哪里？

Present Perfect Continuous:
What have you been working on? = 你一直在做什么？
Why has she been studying so hard? = 她为什么一直在努力学习？

Simple Past:
What did you eat for breakfast? = 你早餐吃了什么？
Where did they go yesterday? = 他们昨天去了哪里？

Past Continuous:
What were you doing at 8 pm last night? = 你昨晚8点在做什么？
Why was she sleeping so late? = 她为什么睡到这么晚？

Past Perfect:
What had you done before I arrived? = 在我到达之前你做了什么？
Why had they left so early? = 他们为什么走得这么早？

Past Perfect Continuous:
What had you been doing before the phone rang? = 电话响之前你一直在做什么？
Why had he been waiting for so long? = 他为什么等了这么久？

Simple Future:
What will you do next weekend? = 你下周末要做什么？
Where will they go for vacation? = 他们要去哪里度假？

Future Continuous:
What will you be doing this time tomorrow? = 明天这个时候你会在做什么？
Where will she be living next year? = 她明年会住在哪里？

Future Perfect:
What will you have finished by Friday? = 到星期五你将完成什么？
Where will they have traveled by the end of the year? = 到年底他们将去过哪里旅行？

Future Perfect Continuous:
What will you have been doing for ten years by 2033? = 到2033年你将已经做了十年什么？
Where will she have been living for five years by next summer? = 到明年夏天，她将已经在哪里住了五年？

词源:
From Late Middle English *interrogatif, from Old French interrogatif, from Latin interrogātīvus, from interrogātus (past participle of interrogāre ‘to question’), from inter- ‘between’ + rogāre ‘to ask’.
