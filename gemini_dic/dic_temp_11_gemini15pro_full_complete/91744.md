hyperdicrotous:/ˌhaɪpərˈdaɪkrətəs/
|adj.|过度分枝的；多次分叉的|Having a branching pattern in which the main axis forks repeatedly, with each fork producing two equal or nearly equal branches, and further branching occurring from these secondary branches, leading to a dense, bushy appearance.  Relating to or characterized by repeated branching or bifurcation.|The hyperdicrotous branching pattern of the plant made it difficult to identify the main stem.|Near synonyms: multifid, much-branched, bushy, ramified|Antonyms: simple, unbranched|Adjective
