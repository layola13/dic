grumblings:/ˈɡrʌmblɪŋz/ | n. | 抱怨，牢骚，不满 |  An expression of discontent or protest, often muttered. |  The grumblings of the passengers grew louder as the delay continued.  乘客们随着延误时间的延长，抱怨声越来越大。 | synonyms: complaints, protests, murmurs, whines, gripes | antonyms: praise, compliments, accolades | noun

grumbling:/ˈɡrʌmblɪŋ/ | adj. | 抱怨的，爱发牢骚的 | Inclined to grumble; expressing discontent. | He had a grumbling stomach after eating too much spicy food.  吃了太多辛辣食物后，他的肚子不舒服，一直在抱怨。 | synonyms: complaining, discontented, disgruntled, querulous | antonyms: contented, satisfied, happy | adjective

Etymology: From the verb "grumble," which originated in the late Middle English period, likely imitative of a low, rumbling sound.
