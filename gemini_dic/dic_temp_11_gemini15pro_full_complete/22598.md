bootstrapper:/ˈbuːtˌstræpər/
n.|引导程序; 引导装载程序; 白手起家的人|A person, group, or organization that starts up a business or other activity with little or no outside help. Also a program or piece of code that initializes a larger system, especially an operating system.|The young entrepreneur was a true bootstrapper, building her company from the ground up with limited resources.|Synonyms: self-starter, entrepreneur, founder, initiator|Antonyms: freeloader, dependent|noun

bootstrapping:/ˈbuːtˌstræpɪŋ/
n.|自举; 引导; 启动|The process of starting up a business or other activity with little or no outside help. Also the process of a simple program loading a more complex system, especially an operating system.|Bootstrapping a new business is challenging but rewarding.|Synonyms: self-starting, self-funding, launching|Antonyms: outsourcing, crowdfunding|noun
