diddler:/ˈdɪdlər/| n. |骗子; 闲混的人; 浪费时间的人|A person who swindles or cheats, especially one who cheats or exploits others in a petty way.  Someone who wastes time or avoids work.  Can also refer to someone who fiddles or plays with something idly.|He was a known diddler, always trying to get something for nothing. 他是个有名的骗子，总是想不劳而获。|近义词：swindler, cheat, con artist, loafer, idler|反义词: honest person, hard worker|noun

diddle:/ˈdɪdəl/| v. |骗取; 闲混; 浪费时间|To swindle or cheat someone out of something, typically a small amount of money. To waste time or avoid work by engaging in trivial activities. Can also mean to fiddle or play with something idly.|He diddled the company out of a few hundred dollars. 他骗取了公司几百美元。|近义词：swindle, cheat, defraud, dawdle, trifle|反义词: work diligently, be honest|verb
