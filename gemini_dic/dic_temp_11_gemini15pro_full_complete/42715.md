countervail:/ˌkaʊntərˈveɪl/ | v. | 抵消，对抗，补偿 | To offset the effect of something by countering it with something equally forceful. |  The government will provide subsidies to countervail the rising cost of fuel. 政府将提供补贴以抵消不断上涨的燃料成本。

The company hopes its new marketing campaign will countervail the negative publicity it has received. 该公司希望其新的营销活动能够抵消其所受到的负面宣传。 | 近义词: offset, counteract, neutralize, compensate | 反义词: enhance, augment, exacerbate, worsen | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *contrevaloir, from contre- "against" + valoir "to be worth".  Ultimately from Latin contra "against" and valēre "to be strong, be worth".
