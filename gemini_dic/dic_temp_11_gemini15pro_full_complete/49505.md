demagogism: /ˈdɛməɡɒɡɪzəm/ (US) /ˈdɛməɡɒɡɪzəm/ (UK)
| n. | 煽动；煽动政治；蛊惑人心 | The practice of appealing to the emotions and prejudices of ordinary people in order to gain political power. |  His speeches were filled with demagogism, playing on people's fears and anxieties. 他的演讲充满了煽动性，利用了人们的恐惧和焦虑。|近义词：demagoguery, rabble-rousing |反义词: reason, logic |词性: noun

demagogic: /ˌdɛməˈɡɒɡɪk/ (US) /ˌdɛməˈɡɒdʒɪk/ (UK)
| adj. | 煽动性的；蛊惑人心的 |  Relating to or characteristic of a demagogue. | His demagogic rhetoric stirred up the crowd. 他煽动性的言辞激怒了人群。|近义词：inflammatory, rabble-rousing |反义词: reasoned, logical |词性: adjective

demagogue: /ˈdɛməɡɒɡ/ (US) /ˈdɛməɡɒɡ/ (UK)
| n. | 煽动者；蛊惑民心的政客 | A political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument. |  He was accused of being a demagogue who exploited people's fears for his own gain. 他被指控是一个为了自身利益而利用人们恐惧的煽动者。|近义词：rabble-rouser, firebrand |反义词: statesman |词性: noun


USAGE->

词源: From Late Latin *dēmagōgia, from Ancient Greek dēmagōgía (“leadership of the people”), from dēmos (“people”) + agōgos (“leader”).

用法说明:  Demagogism refers to the methods or practices of a demagogue, specifically the use of emotional appeals, prejudice, and misinformation to manipulate public opinion and gain power.  It is often used negatively to describe political discourse that is considered inflammatory or divisive.
