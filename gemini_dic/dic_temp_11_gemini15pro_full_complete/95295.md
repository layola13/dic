incarnate:/ˈɪnkɑːrneɪt/ (US), /ɪnˈkɑːneɪt/ (UK)| adj. vt. n.| 化身的，体现的；使…化身；化身|Embodied in flesh; personified.  To give a bodily, especially a human, form to (a spirit or abstract quality). incarnate:/ˈɪnkɑːrneɪt/ (US), /ɪnˈkɑːneɪt/ (UK)| adj.| 化身的，体现的|Existing or appearing in human form, especially of a spirit or deity. |例句：She is kindness incarnate. 她是善良的化身。|近义词：embodied, personified|反义词：disembodied|词性: adjective


incarnate:/ˈɪnkɑːrneɪt/ (US), /ɪnˈkɑːneɪt/ (UK)| vt.| 使…化身|To give a bodily, especially a human, form to (a spirit or abstract quality).|例句：The artist incarnated his vision in his paintings. 艺术家在他的画作中体现了他的愿景。|近义词：embody, personify|反义词： - |词性: verb


incarnate:/ˈɪnkɑːrneɪt/ (US), /ɪnˈkɑːneɪt/ (UK)| n.| 化身|A person who embodies in the flesh a deity, spirit, or abstract quality.|例句：He was the devil incarnate. 他是魔鬼的化身。|近义词：embodiment, personification|反义词： - |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin incarnatus, past participle of incarnare "to make flesh," from in- "in" + caro, carnem "flesh."
