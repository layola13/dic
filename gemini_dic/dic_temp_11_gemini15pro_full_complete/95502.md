inclinations: /ˌɪŋklɪˈneɪʃənz/ | n. | 倾向，爱好，意愿 | a feeling that makes you want to do something |  He has strong inclinations towards music. 他对音乐有强烈的爱好。 |  tendencies, leanings, propensities, predispositions | aversions, disinclinations | noun


inclinations: /ˌɪŋklɪˈneɪʃənz/ | n. | 斜坡，倾斜 | a slope or the angle at which something slopes | The roof has a slight inclination. 屋顶略微倾斜。 | slopes, gradients, slants | levels, flats | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *enclinaisons*, from Latin *inclinationem* (nominative *inclinatio*), noun of action from past-participle stem of *inclinare* "to lean, bend, incline," from *in-* "into, in, on, upon" + *clinare* "to lean, bend."
