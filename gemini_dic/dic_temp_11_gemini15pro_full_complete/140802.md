on-call: /ˌɑːn ˈkɔːl/ (US), /ˌɒn ˈkɔːl/ (UK) | adj. | 待命的，随叫随到的 | Available to work or provide a service at any time if needed, especially in an emergency. |  The doctor is on call tonight.  今晚医生值班。 | Example: The IT support staff are on call 24/7.  IT 支持人员 24 小时待命。 | Synonyms: available, on duty, on standby | Antonyms: off duty, unavailable | Adjective

on-call: /ˌɑːn ˈkɔːl/ (US), /ˌɒn ˈkɔːl/ (UK) | n. | 值班，待命 | The state of being available to work or provide a service at any time if needed. |  Being on call can be stressful.  值班会很有压力。 | Example:  He hates being on call during the weekends. 他讨厌周末值班。| Synonyms: duty, standby | Antonyms: off-duty time, free time | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrase "on call," which literally means "ready to be called upon." The "on" preposition indicates availability or readiness, and "call" refers to the act of summoning or requesting someone's services.
