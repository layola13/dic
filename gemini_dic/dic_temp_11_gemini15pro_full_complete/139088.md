obbligati:/ˌɒblɪˈɡɑːti/ | n. | 義務；責任；債務 (尤指道義上的) |  A bond or other document representing a debt; something to which one is bound, especially morally.  Derived from the Italian word "obbligato" (obligated). The plural form is either "obbligati" or "obbligatos." In music, it refers to an elaborate melodic passage that must be played as written, without alterations or improvisation. |  He felt an obbligato to help his family. 他觉得有义务帮助他的家人。 |Synonyms: duty, obligation, responsibility, commitment, liability | Antonyms: option, choice, freedom, exemption | noun

obbligato:/ˌɒblɪˈɡɑːto/ | adj. | (音樂)必須照寫演奏的； obbligato part: 固定樂段 | In music, played or sung exactly as written, without changes or improvisation. | The violin solo was an obbligato passage, requiring precise execution.  小提琴獨奏是一個必須照寫演奏的段落，需要精確的演奏。 | Synonyms: obligatory, compulsory, required, mandatory | Antonyms: optional, discretionary, elective, improvisational | adjective
