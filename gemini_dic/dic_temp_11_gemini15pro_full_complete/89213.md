homesteads:/ˈhoʊmstedz/| n.| 宅基地，家园|A piece of land acquired from the government, typically for farming or other agricultural purposes.  A house and surrounding land owned by a family.|Many pioneers established homesteads in the American West. 许多拓荒者在美国西部建立了家园。|synonyms: farms, ranches, estates, properties|antonyms: apartments, condos|noun

homestead:/ˈhoʊmsted/| n. v.| 宅基地，家园;  取得宅基地|A piece of land acquired from the government, typically for farming or other agricultural purposes.  To acquire or settle on a homestead. |They homesteaded 160 acres in Oklahoma. 他们在俄克拉荷马州获得了160英亩的宅基地。|synonyms: settle, colonize, farm, ranch|antonyms: abandon, leave, vacate|noun, verb
