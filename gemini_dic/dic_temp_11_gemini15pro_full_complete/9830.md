anythings:/ˈɛniθɪŋz/ |pron.|任何东西（非标准用法，通常使用anything）| "Anythings" is not standard English. The correct form is "anything." It is used to refer to any thing at all, regardless of what it might be.  While "anythings" might be heard in some dialects or informal speech, it is generally considered grammatically incorrect.|"Did you bring anythings for the picnic?" (Non-standard) "Did you bring anything for the picnic?" (Standard) | 近义词: anything | 反义词: nothing | 词性: pronoun
