enarthrosis:/ˌɛnɑːrˈθroʊsɪs/
noun
杵臼关节
A freely moving joint in which a rounded head of one bone fits into a cuplike socket of another bone, allowing movement in all directions.
The shoulder joint is an example of an enarthrosis, allowing for a wide range of motion.
Synonyms: ball-and-socket joint
Antonyms: synarthrosis (immovable joint)
