dissociable:/dɪˈsoʊʃiəbl/ /dɪˈsəʊʃiəbl/
adjective
可分离的；可解离的；不相关的
Capable of being dissociated or separated.  Able to be considered separately.
例句：The effects of the drug are dissociable from its side effects.  这种药物的疗效与其副作用是可以区分开的。

例句：The two issues are dissociable and should be treated separately. 这两个问题是可以分开的，应该单独处理。

近义词：separable, detachable, independent, unconnected
反义词：inseparable, indivisible, connected, related
词性：adjective

dissociation:/dɪˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən/ /dɪˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən/
noun
分离；解离；分裂
The act of separating or the state of being separated.  A split in consciousness, which allows some thoughts and behaviors to occur simultaneously with others.  In chemistry, the separation of a molecule into simpler molecules, atoms, radicals, or ions.
例句：The dissociation of the church from the state is complete. 教会与国家完全分离。

例句：He experienced a period of dissociation after the trauma.  创伤后，他经历了一段时间的解离。

近义词：separation, detachment, division, disconnection
反义词：connection, union, integration, association
词性：noun
