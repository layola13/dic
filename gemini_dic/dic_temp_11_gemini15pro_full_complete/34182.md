clamoring:/ˈklæmərɪŋ/ | adj. | 喧嚷的，大声要求的 | Characterized by a loud uproar, as of many voices; demanding something insistently. | The clamoring crowd demanded to see the king. 喧闹的人群要求见国王。|  The starving children were clamoring for food. 饥饿的孩子们吵着要食物。 | 近义词：demanding, insistent, vociferous | 反义词：quiet, silent | 词性: adjective

clamor:/ˈklæmər/ | n. vi. | 喧闹，大声要求 | A loud uproar, as of many voices; to make a loud uproar; to demand something insistently. | The clamor of the protesters filled the streets. 抗议者的喧闹声充满了街道。 |  They clamored for the release of the prisoners. 他们大声疾呼释放囚犯。 | 近义词：outcry, uproar, commotion | 反义词：silence, quiet | 词性: noun, verb


词源: From Middle English *clamouren, from Old French clamur, clamour (“clamor, uproar”), from Latin clamor (“shout, loud noise”), from clamō (“I shout, cry out”).
