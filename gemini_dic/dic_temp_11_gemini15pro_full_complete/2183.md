addiction:/əˈdɪkʃən/
n.|瘾; 入迷; 沉溺;|The state of being enslaved to a habit or practice or to something that is psychologically or physically habit-forming, as narcotics, to such an extent that its cessation causes severe trauma.|He is now fighting his addiction to alcohol. 他现在正努力戒酒。|
近义词:dependence, habit, compulsion, obsession|
反义词:aversion, dislike|
词性:noun


addicted:/əˈdɪktɪd/
adj.|上瘾的; 入迷的;|Physiologically or psychologically dependent on a substance, especially a narcotic drug.|He became addicted to gambling. 他赌博成瘾了。|
近义词:dependent, hooked, devoted|
反义词:indifferent, averse|
词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *addictiōn- (stem of *addictiō), from Latin addictus (past participle of addicere ‘assign, surrender’), from ad- ‘to’ + dicere ‘say’.
