gowden:/ˈɡoʊdən/ | adj. | 金色的，金黄色的 |  A Scottish and Northern English variant of "golden."  It retains the older pronunciation of the word. | The gowden rays of the setting sun illuminated the loch.  落日的金色光芒照亮了湖水。|Synonyms: golden, aureate, gilded | Antonyms: dark, dull, drab | adjective

gowden:/ˈɡoʊdən/ | n. | 金子，黄金 (poetic/archaic) |  A Scottish and Northern English variant of "golden" sometimes used poetically or archaically to refer to gold itself.  |  She dreamt of finding gowden treasure. 她梦见找到了黄金宝藏。 | Synonyms: gold, treasure | Antonyms: poverty, destitution | noun
