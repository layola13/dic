improvise:/ˈɪmprəvaɪz/ | v. | 即兴创作，即兴表演 | create and perform (music, drama, or verse) spontaneously or without preparation. |  The band improvised a new song on stage. 乐队在舞台上即兴创作了一首新歌。| She had to improvise a costume for the play. 她不得不为这部戏临时制作一套服装。| synonyms: ad-lib, extemporize, wing | antonyms: plan, rehearse, prepare | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Latin improvisus "unforeseen, unexpected," past participle of improvisare, from in- "not" + provisus "foreseen," past participle of providere "to foresee."
