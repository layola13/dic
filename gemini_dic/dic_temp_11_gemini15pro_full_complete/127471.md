necessitarian:/nəˌsesɪˈtɛəriən/
n.|必然论者; 宿命论者|A person who believes in necessitarianism.|He argued against the necessitarian view of history, claiming that individuals have free will. 他反对历史必然论的观点，声称个人拥有自由意志。|Synonyms: determinist, fatalist|Antonyms: libertarian, voluntarist|noun
necessitarian:/nəˌsesɪˈtɛəriən/
adj.|必然论的; 宿命论的|Relating to or characteristic of necessitarianism.|His necessitarian philosophy left no room for chance or free will. 他的必然论哲学没有给偶然性或自由意志留下任何空间。|Synonyms: deterministic, fatalistic|Antonyms: libertarian, voluntaristic|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *necessitarius* (meaning "necessary"), from Latin *necessitas* ("necessity") + *-arius* (suffix forming adjectives and nouns).  The philosophical sense emerged in the 18th century.
