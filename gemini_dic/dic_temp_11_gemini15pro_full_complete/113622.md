machiavellianism:/ˌmækiəˈvɛliənɪzəm/ | n. | 马基雅维利主义;权术;不择手段 | the employment of cunning and duplicity in statecraft or in general conduct |  His machiavellianism helped him climb the corporate ladder quickly. 他的马基雅维利主义帮助他很快地爬上了公司的阶梯。 | synonyms:  cunning, duplicity, artifice, guile, trickery | antonyms:  honesty, integrity, forthrightness | noun

machiavellian: /ˌmækiəˈvɛliən/ | adj. | 马基雅维利的;不择手段的;狡猾的 |  cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career. | He employed machiavellian tactics to win the election. 他采用马基雅维利式的策略赢得了选举。| synonyms:  cunning, devious, unscrupulous, artful, wily | antonyms:  honest, principled, straightforward | adjective
