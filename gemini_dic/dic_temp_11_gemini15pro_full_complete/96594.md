induction's:/ɪnˈdʌkʃənz/| n.|归纳；归纳法；感应；就职；诱导|The process of discovering a general principle from particular facts or instances.  The process by which an electric current, a magnetic field, or a magnetic charge is produced in a body by the proximity of another body having an electric current, a magnetic field, or a magnetic charge, respectively, without any physical contact.|His induction into the Hall of Fame was a great honor. 他入选名人堂是一项极大的荣誉。|Synonyms: inauguration, initiation, commencement, introduction, onboarding|Antonyms: deduction, conclusion, discharge, removal|noun
