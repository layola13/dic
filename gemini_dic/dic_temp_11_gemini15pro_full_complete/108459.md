词源:  "Lay-back" is a compound word formed from the verb "lay" and the adverb "back."  "Lay" in this context means to put or place in a particular position, and "back" indicates a reclining or relaxed posture.  The term likely originated in the mid-20th century, possibly influenced by the growing popularity of casual and relaxed lifestyles.
