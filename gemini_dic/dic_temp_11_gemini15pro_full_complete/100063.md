interpolatable:/ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪtəbl/ | adj. | 可插值的，可内插的 | Able to be interpolated; capable of being estimated or determined by interpolation. | Example 1: The data points were interpolatable, allowing us to estimate the values between them. 这些数据点是可插值的，允许我们估计它们之间的值。

Example 2: The function is smooth and interpolatable, making it suitable for numerical analysis.  这个函数是平滑且可插值的，使其适合于数值分析。 | Near Synonyms: estimable, calculable, derivable, inferable | Antonyms: non-interpolatable, incalculable | Word Type: Adjective

interpolate:/ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪt/ | v. | 插入，内插；窜改 | 1. To insert (something of a different nature) into something else. 2. To introduce (something additional or extraneous) between other things or parts. 3. Mathematics: To insert an intermediate value or term into a series by estimating or calculating it from surrounding known values. | Example 1: He interpolated a few remarks while I was speaking. 我说话时，他插了几句话。

Example 2:  She interpolated the missing data points using a cubic spline. 她使用三次样条插值法插入缺失的数据点。| Near Synonyms: insert, interject, introduce, extrapolate (in some contexts) | Antonyms: remove, delete, extract | Word Type: Verb
