live-with:/lɪv wɪð/| phrasal verb |忍受；与…同住；与…共存|to tolerate or endure something unpleasant; to share a home with someone; to coexist with something|例句：I can't live with this constant noise. 我无法忍受这持续不断的噪音。|近义词：tolerate, endure, cohabit, reside with|反义词：reject, refuse, leave|词性:动词短语

live-with:/lɪv wɪð/| phrasal verb |忍受；与…同住；与…共存|to accept something unpleasant and continue to exist with it; to share a home with someone; to coexist with something|例句：She has to live with the consequences of her actions. 她必须承担自己行为的后果。|近义词：accept, bear, share a house with, coexist|反义词：fight, oppose, move out|词性:动词短语

词源：
"live" comes from Old English "libban," meaning "to be alive." "with" comes from Old English "wiþ," meaning "against, opposite, toward, near, by, among."  The phrasal verb "live with" combines these to express the idea of existing alongside something or someone.
