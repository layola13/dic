cormack: /ˈkɔːrmæk/ | n. |科马克| A Scottish surname, derived from a Gaelic personal name meaning "chariot driver" or "raven". It can also refer to Allan MacLeod Cormack, a South African-born American physicist who shared the 1979 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his work on X-ray computed tomography (CT) scanning. | Cormack's contributions to medical imaging revolutionized diagnostic procedures. | 近义词: (For the person) scientist, physicist; (For the surname) family name, last name | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for a name) | 词性: noun
