laputan:/ləˈpjuːtən/| adj.|拉普塔的；空想的，不切实际的|Of or relating to Laputa, a fictional flying island in Jonathan Swift's *Gulliver's Travels*, or its inhabitants; therefore, impractical, unrealistic, or visionary.|例句：His laputan schemes for making money quickly never came to fruition. 他那些想快速赚钱的不切实际的计划从未实现。|近义词：visionary, impractical, unrealistic, fanciful, utopian|反义词：practical, realistic, down-to-earth|词性:形容词

词源:
From Laputa, the name of a flying island described in Jonathan Swift's *Gulliver's Travels* (1726).  The inhabitants of Laputa were preoccupied with abstract and impractical pursuits, hence the adjective's meaning.
