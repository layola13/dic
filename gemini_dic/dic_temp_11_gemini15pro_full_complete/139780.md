octane: /ˈɒkteɪn/ (UK) /ˈɑːkteɪn/ (US)| n. |辛烷|A saturated hydrocarbon found in petroleum, used as a fuel and solvent.  It is a measure of the performance of gasoline/petrol, with higher octane numbers indicating greater resistance to knocking.|High-octane gasoline is recommended for this car. 这款车推荐使用高辛烷值汽油。|Synonyms: fuel, petrol, gasoline|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a chemical compound.  One could consider impurities or substances that reduce fuel efficiency as loose antonyms.)|noun
