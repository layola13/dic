miscarry:/ˌmɪsˈkæri/ /ˌmɪsˈkeri/| v. |流产；失败；被误送|To fail to achieve the intended purpose; To end prematurely and unsuccessfully;  (of a pregnancy) to end before the fetus is developed enough to survive outside the womb.|例句：The project miscarried due to lack of funding.  该项目因缺乏资金而失败。

例句：Sadly, she miscarried in the first trimester.  不幸的是，她在妊娠的头三个月流产了。|近义词：fail, abort, collapse|反义词:succeed, thrive|词性:动词

miscarriage:/ˌmɪsˈkæɪrɪdʒ/ /ˌmɪsˈkerɪdʒ/ | n. |流产；失败；误送|The failure of a plan or project;  The spontaneous expulsion of a fetus from the womb before it is able to survive.|例句：The miscarriage of justice led to his wrongful imprisonment.  司法不公导致他被冤枉入狱。

例句：She suffered a miscarriage and was deeply saddened. 她流产了，非常难过。|近义词：failure, abortion|反义词:success, birth|词性:名词
