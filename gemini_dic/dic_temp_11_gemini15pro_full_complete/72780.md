foil:/fɔɪl/| n. vt.|箔，金属薄片；陪衬物；挫败；(击剑)花剑|A thin sheet of metal, especially used to wrap food or in decoration.  Something that serves by contrast to emphasize or highlight the qualities of another thing. To prevent (something considered wrong or undesirable) from succeeding.|例句：
The chocolate was wrapped in aluminum foil. 巧克力用铝箔包着。
He acted as a foil to her exuberance. 他衬托出她的热情洋溢。
The police foiled the robbery attempt. 警方挫败了抢劫企图。|近义词：frustrate, thwart, hinder, obstruct, defeat (v.); contrast, backdrop (n.)|反义词：aid, assist, help (v.); complement (n.)|词性:名词,动词

foil:/fɔɪl/| n.| (击剑)花剑|A light fencing sword with a flexible rectangular blade tapering to a point.|例句：
He won the gold medal in foil. 他赢得了花剑金牌。
She is a skilled foil fencer. 她是一位技术娴熟的花剑击剑手。|近义词：epee, saber|反义词：-|词性:名词
