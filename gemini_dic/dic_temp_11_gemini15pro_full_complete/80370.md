golems: /ˈɡoʊləmz/ | n. |魔像，泥人，机器人|An artificial being from Jewish folklore, magically created from inanimate matter, often clay.  A golem is typically depicted as a humanoid figure that is brought to life to serve its creator.  The term has also been extended metaphorically to refer to anything that is powerful but unintelligent or lacking in free will.|The rabbi animated the golem to protect the Jewish community.| 近义词: automaton, robot, android | 反义词: human, natural being | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Yiddish גולם‎ (goylem), from Hebrew גֹּלֶם‎ (gólem, “embryo, unformed substance”), related to גָּלַם‎ (galam, “to wrap up, envelop”).
