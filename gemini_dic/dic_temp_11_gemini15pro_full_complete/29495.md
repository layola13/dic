cataract:/ˈkætərækt (US) / ˈkætərækt (UK)| n.|白内障; 瀑布; 洪水; 大雨|A clouding of the lens in the eye which leads to a gradual loss of sight.  An eye disease in which the lens becomes progressively opaque, resulting in blurred vision.|例句：He developed cataracts in both eyes. 他双眼都得了白内障。|例句：She had to have cataract surgery. 她不得不做白内障手术。|近义词：waterfall, cascade, deluge, torrent (referring to a large waterfall)|反义词: (No direct antonym for the eye condition. For waterfall, perhaps trickle) |词性: noun
