internees:/ˌɪntɜːrˈniːz/
noun
被拘留者，被扣押者
A person who is confined as a prisoner, especially in wartime.
During World War II, many Japanese Americans were unjustly interned in camps.  二战期间，许多日裔美国人被不公正地关押在集中营里。
The internees were held without trial. 被拘留者未经审判就被关押。
近义词：prisoners, detainees, captives
反义词：freemen, citizens (in a general sense)
词性:n.


intern:/ɪnˈtɜːrn/
noun
实习生
A student or trainee who works, sometimes without pay, at a trade or profession in order to gain practical experience.
The summer intern quickly learned the ropes.  暑期实习生很快学会了窍门。
The hospital has several interns working in the emergency room. 这家医院有几名实习生在急诊室工作。

verb
拘留，扣押
Confine (someone) as a prisoner, especially for political or military reasons.
The government interned enemy aliens during the war. 政府在战争期间拘留了敌方侨民。
Several foreign journalists were interned by the authorities. 几名外国记者被当局拘留。
近义词：confine, detain, imprison
反义词：release, liberate
词性:n., v.

词源:
From French *interner*, from Latin internus "inward, internal". The noun is derived from the past participle of the verb.
