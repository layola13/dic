domestication:/dəˌmɛstɪˈkeɪʃən/ |n.|驯化，驯养|The domestication of wolves led to the development of dogs.  狼的驯化导致了狗的发展。|例句：The domestication of plants and animals was crucial to the development of agriculture. 动植物的驯化对农业的发展至关重要。

例句：The domestication of the horse changed the course of human history. 马的驯化改变了人类历史的进程。|近义词：taming, cultivation|反义词：wildness, feralness|词性:名词

domesticate:/dəˈmɛstɪkeɪt/ |v.|驯化，驯养|To domesticate an animal is to tame it and make it suitable for living with humans. 驯化动物就是驯服它，使它适合与人类一起生活。|例句：Humans domesticated the dog thousands of years ago.  人类在几千年前驯化了狗。

例句：They are attempting to domesticate wild foxes. 他们正在尝试驯化野生狐狸。|近义词：tame, train, cultivate|反义词：release, free|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin domesticātiō, from Latin domesticus (“of the house, native”), from domus (“house”). 最终源自原始印欧语 *dṓm (“house”).
