graver:/ˈɡreɪvər/ (US), /ˈɡreɪvə(r)/ (UK)| n. |雕刻师，雕刻工具|A person who carves or engraves, especially on metal or wood; a tool used for engraving.| The graver carefully etched the design onto the silver plate. 雕刻师小心翼翼地在银盘上蚀刻图案。|Synonyms: engraver, carver, sculptor, burin|Antonyms: None (lack of a direct antonym for a profession/tool)|noun

graver:/ˈɡreɪvər/ (US), /ˈɡreɪvə(r)/ (UK)| adj. |更严重的，更重要的，更严肃的|More serious or solemn; of greater importance or gravity.|The situation became graver as the storm intensified. 随着风暴的加剧，情况变得更加严峻。|Synonyms: more serious, more solemn, weightier, more important|Antonyms: less serious, less important, lighter, trivial|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gravere*, from Anglo-Norman *graveur*, from Old French *graver*, from Frankish *graban*, from Proto-Germanic *grabananą* (“to dig, carve”). Cognate with Dutch *graven* (“to dig”), German *graben* (“to dig”).
