contrarious:/kənˈtreəriəs/ | adj. | 违反的；相反的；对立的；乖戾的 | Inclined to oppose or go against; perverse or contrary.  | He had a contrarious nature and always disagreed with everyone. 他性格乖戾，总是和大家意见不合。 | The contrarious wind made sailing difficult. 逆风使得航行困难。 | 近义词: perverse, awkward, difficult, opposing, contrary,  obstinate, wayward | 反义词: agreeable, compliant, obedient, amenable | 词性: adjective

contrary:/ˈkɒntrəri/ | adj. n. | 相反的；对立的；对抗的 | Opposite in nature, direction, or meaning.  |  He took a contrary view. 他持相反的观点。 |  The evidence is contrary to his claim. 证据与他的说法相矛盾。 | 近义词: opposite, conflicting, opposing | 反义词: similar, identical, same | 词性: adjective, noun


词源:  Late Middle English: from Old French contraire, from Latin contrarius ‘opposite’, from contra ‘against’.
