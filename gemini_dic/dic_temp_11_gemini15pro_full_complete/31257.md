chaotic:/ˈkeɪɑːtɪk/ (US) /ˈkeɪɒtɪk/ (UK)| adj.|混乱的，无秩序的|In a state of complete confusion and disorder.|例句：The traffic in the city was chaotic after the heavy snow.  暴雪过后，城市交通一片混乱。

例句：His life has been chaotic since he lost his job.  自从失业后，他的生活变得一团糟。

近义词：disorderly, turbulent, tumultuous, anarchic|反义词：orderly, organized, structured|词性：形容词

chaotic:/ˈkeɪɑːtɪk/ (US) /ˈkeɪɒtɪk/ (UK)| n.|混乱|A state of complete confusion and disorder. (Noun form is less common)|例句：The chaotic state of the house made it difficult to find anything. 房子的混乱状态使得很难找到任何东西。

例句：She thrives in the chaotic environment of a busy newsroom. 她很适应繁忙新闻编辑室的混乱环境。


近义词：disorder, turmoil, turbulence|反义词：order, structure, organization|词性：名词
