inoculated:/ɪˈnɑːkjəleɪtɪd/
adjective
已接种的，已预防的
Having been treated with a vaccine to produce immunity against a disease.
例句：The inoculated children showed no signs of the disease. 已接种疫苗的儿童没有表现出任何疾病迹象。
例句：He was inoculated against typhoid before traveling abroad. 他在出国前接种了伤寒疫苗。
近义词：vaccinated, immunized
反义词：unvaccinated, unprotected
词性: adjective


inoculate:/ɪˈnɑːkjəleɪt/
verb
接种，预防
To introduce a vaccine into the body to produce immunity to a disease; to introduce something into the mind of.
例句：Doctors routinely inoculate children against measles. 医生常规地给孩子们接种麻疹疫苗。
例句：The teacher tried to inoculate her students with a love of learning. 老师试图将热爱学习的思想灌输给她的学生。
近义词：vaccinate, immunize, inject
反义词：infect, expose
词性: verb


词源: From Latin *inoculātus, past participle of inoculāre "to engraft, implant," from in- "in" + oculus "eye, bud" (figuratively, "a cutting").  Originally referring to grafting buds onto plants.  The medical sense developed later, based on the analogy of introducing a foreign substance.
