demagogic:/ˌdeməˈɡɑːɡɪk/ |adj.|煽动性的，蛊惑人心的|Appealing to emotions and prejudices rather than reason, typically to gain popular support.|The politician's demagogic rhetoric stirred up the crowd.|近义词:inflammatory, rabble-rousing, populist|反义词:reasoned, rational, dispassionate|词性:形容词

demagogue: /ˈdeməɡɑːɡ/ |n.|煽动者，蛊惑民心的政客|A political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument.|History is full of demagogues who rose to power by exploiting fear and ignorance.|近义词:rabble-rouser, agitator, firebrand|反义词:statesman, diplomat|词性:名词

demagoguery: /ˈdeməɡɒɡəri/ |n.|煽动，蛊惑人心|The practice of appealing to emotions and prejudices rather than reason, typically to gain popular support.|The candidate's demagoguery was condemned by many.|近义词:rabble-rousing, agitation, incitement|反义词:reasoning, logic, diplomacy|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek dēmagōgos (leader of the people, popular leader), from dēmos (people) + agōgos (leading, leader).
