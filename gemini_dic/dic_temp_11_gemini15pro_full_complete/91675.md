hyperclassical:/ˌhaɪpərˈklæsɪkəl/| adj.|超级古典的，极端古典主义的|Excessively or extremely classical; adhering rigidly to classical principles or styles, often to an exaggerated or affected degree.|His music is so hyperclassical it feels almost like a parody. 他的音乐过于古典，感觉就像是一种滑稽模仿。|The architecture of the building is hyperclassical, with its strict adherence to Greek proportions. 这座建筑的建筑风格非常古典，严格遵循希腊的比例。|近义词：ultraclassical, rigidly classical, neoclassical|反义词：modern, avant-garde, contemporary|词性: 形容词

词源:  "hyper-" (Greek: ὑπέρ, hupér, "over, beyond") + "classical" (from Latin classicus, "belonging to the highest class of citizens," later used to describe works of the highest quality, especially in literature and art).
