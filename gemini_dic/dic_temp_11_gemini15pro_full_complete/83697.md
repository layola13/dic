hagglers:/ˈhæɡlərz/| n. |讨价还价的人；讲价者|People who bargain aggressively, especially over the price of something.|The market was full of hagglers trying to get the best deals. 市场里到处都是讨价还价的人，都想买到最划算的商品。|The hagglers finally agreed on a price for the rug. 这些讲价者最终就地毯的价格达成了协议。|近义词：bargainers, negotiators|反义词:buyers, sellers|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From haggle + -er.  Haggle comes from the Middle English *hagelen*, frequentative of an unrecorded Old Norse word *höggva* meaning "to hew, chop," related to Old English *heawan* "to cut." The sense evolution likely went from striking a bargain to bargaining persistently.
