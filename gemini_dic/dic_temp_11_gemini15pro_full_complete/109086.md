legator:/ˈleɡeɪtər/| n.|遗赠者；立遗嘱的人|A person who makes a bequest or leaves a legacy, especially in a will.|Example: The legator bequeathed his entire estate to his only daughter.  遗赠者将他的全部财产留给了他唯一的女儿。

Example: The legator's intentions were clearly stated in the will. 立遗嘱人的意图在遗嘱中已明确说明。|近义词：testator, bequeather|反义词:beneficiary, heir|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *legator, from Latin legatus (“envoy, ambassador, legate”), past participle of legare (“to send as an ambassador, bequeath, depute, appoint”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *leǵ- (“to collect, gather”).
