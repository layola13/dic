nonveracities: /ˌnɒnvɛˈræsɪtiz/ /ˌnɑːnvɛˈræsɪtiz/
noun
不真实，虚假；谎言（复数）
The report was filled with nonveracities and misleading statements.  这份报告充满了不真实和误导性的陈述。
The politician's speech was riddled with nonveracities.  这位政治家的演讲充满了谎言。
近义词: lies, falsehoods, untruths, fabrications
反义词: truths, facts, realities
词性: noun (plural)

词源:
From non- + veracity.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "veracity" comes from the Latin "veracitas," meaning "truthfulness."
