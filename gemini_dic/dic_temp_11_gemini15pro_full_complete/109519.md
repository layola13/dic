lese:/ˈliːzə/| n.| 租赁，租约；租赁物；租赁权 |A contract by which one party conveys land, property, services, etc. to another for a specified time, usually in return for a periodic payment.|He took out a three-year lease on the apartment. 他租了那套公寓三年。| synonyms: rental, hire, charter| antonyms: purchase, buy| noun

词源: From Middle English *lesen, *lessen, from Anglo-Norman lesser, leaser, from Old French laissier (“to let, allow, leave”), from Late Latin laxare, from Latin laxus (“loose, slack”). Doublet of lease and laisser.
