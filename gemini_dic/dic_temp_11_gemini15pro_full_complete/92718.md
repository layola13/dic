ichigo:/iːtʃiːɡoʊ/ | n. |草莓; 一期一会 (ichi-go ichi-e)| From Japanese 一期一会 (ichi-go ichi-e), meaning "one time, one meeting," emphasizing the preciousness of each encounter.  While primarily referring to a Japanese cultural concept, it's often used in English to refer to strawberries, due to the similar pronunciation. | Example: This ichigo is so sweet. 这个草莓真甜. | Example:  The tea ceremony embodies the spirit of ichigo ichi-e. 茶道体现了一期一会的精神.| Synonym: strawberry (for the fruit), treasured moment, once-in-a-lifetime opportunity | Antonym: N/A (for the cultural concept), ordinary event | noun
