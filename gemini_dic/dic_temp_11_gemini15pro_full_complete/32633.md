chisel:/ˈtʃɪzl/| n. vt. vi.|凿子，雕刻；欺骗|A chisel is a tool with a sharp blade used for shaping or cutting stone, wood, or metal. As a verb, it means to shape or cut something with a chisel, or to cheat someone. |He used a chisel to carve a design into the wood. 他用凿子在木头上雕刻图案。|synonyms: gouge, carve, engrave, swindle, cheat |antonyms: blunt, dull, honest|noun, verb

chisel:/ˈtʃɪzl/| v. |欺骗，骗取|To chisel someone out of something means to cheat them out of it. |They chiseled him out of his inheritance. 他们骗取了他的遗产。|synonyms: defraud, swindle, cheat |antonyms:  be honest, give |verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French cisel, from Vulgar Latin *cisellum, from Latin caesellum, diminutive of caedere "to cut."
