antiquarian:/ˌæn.tɪˈkweə.ri.ən/
n.|古董商；古物研究者；收藏家|A person who studies or collects antiques or antiquities.|例句：The antiquarian spent years searching for the lost manuscript.  这位古董商花了数年时间寻找丢失的手稿。

例句：She is a renowned antiquarian specializing in Roman artifacts. 她是一位著名的专门研究罗马文物的古董研究者。
|近义词：collector, archaeologist, historian|反义词:modernist|词性:名词

antiquarians:/ˌæn.tɪˈkweə.ri.ənz/
n.|古董商们；古物研究者们；收藏家们|Plural form of antiquarian, referring to a group of people who study or collect antiques or antiquities.|例句：The antiquarians gathered at the auction, eager to bid on rare items.  古董商们聚集在拍卖会上，渴望竞拍稀有物品。

例句：Local antiquarians are protesting the demolition of the historic building. 当地的古物研究者们正在抗议拆除这座历史建筑。
|近义词：collectors, archaeologists, historians|反义词:modernists|词性:名词
