miserly:/ˈmaɪzərli/| adj.|吝啬的，小气的|Excessively unwilling to spend money; stingy.  Characterized by or indicative of avarice.|例句：He was so miserly that he refused to buy his children new shoes. 他太吝啬了，不肯给孩子们买新鞋。|近义词：stingy, parsimonious, mean, niggardly|反义词：generous, liberal, charitable|词性:形容词
词源: From Middle English *miserli, from Old French miserere (“to pity, have mercy”), from Latin miserērī, perfect active infinitive of miserēor (“I pity”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *mey- (“to feel concern”). The semantic shift occurred from "pitiable" to "wretched" to "stingy."
