hightail:/ˈhaɪˌteɪl/| v. | 匆忙离开，迅速逃走 | To leave somewhere quickly, often because you are frightened or embarrassed. |  The robbers hightailed it out of the bank when the alarm went off. 银行警报响起时，劫匪迅速逃离了银行。

The cat hightailed it across the road when it saw the dog. 猫看到狗后，迅速跑过马路。| 近义词：flee, escape, run away, bolt, scram | 反义词：approach, arrive, stay |词性：动词

词源：
"Hightail" originates from the behavior of animals, particularly horses and deer, raising their tails high when running away in fright.  This visual image became associated with a hasty retreat.
