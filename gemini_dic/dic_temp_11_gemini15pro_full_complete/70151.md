fiasco:/fiˈæskoʊ/ (US) /fiˈæskəʊ/ (UK)| n.|惨败；彻底失败|A complete and utter failure, often in a humiliating or embarrassing way.  A situation that was intended to be impressive but turned into a complete disaster.|The opening night of the play was a total fiasco, with actors forgetting their lines and props malfunctioning.  这出戏的首演完全是一场惨败，演员忘了台词，道具也出了故障。|近义词：debacle, disaster, failure, flop, mess|反义词：success, triumph|词性:noun

fiasco:| n.|惨败；彻底失败|Originates from the Italian word "fiasco" meaning "bottle" or "flask." It evolved to mean a theatrical flop, likely from the image of a broken bottle, before broadening to encompass any kind of disastrous failure.|例句：The product launch was a fiasco due to poor planning. 产品发布会由于计划不周而以失败告终。|近义词：failure, disaster, debacle|反义词：success, triumph|词性:noun
