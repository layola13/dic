leches:/ˈletʃɪz/| interj.|哎呀！/该死！/见鬼！|An exclamation of surprise, annoyance, or dismay, primarily used in Spanish and some dialects of American English. It is a euphemism derived from the Spanish word for "milk."|例句：¡Ay, leches! I forgot my keys. 哎呀！我忘记带钥匙了。|近义词：shoot, darn, dang, heck|反义词: (No direct antonym for an interjection)|interjection

注意事项:

The pronunciation can vary slightly depending on regional dialect.

"Leches" is generally considered a mild exclamation. Stronger variations exist, but they are often considered vulgar.  Avoid using these in formal settings.


Be mindful of your audience when using "leches," as some might find it offensive, even in its mildest form.  It's always better to err on the side of caution.
