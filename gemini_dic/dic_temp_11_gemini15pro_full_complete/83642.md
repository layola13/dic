haemolytic:/ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk/ /ˌheməˈlɪtɪk/ | adj. | 溶血的，溶血性的 | Relating to or causing haemolysis (the destruction of red blood cells). |  Example: The venom of some snakes has a haemolytic effect.  一些蛇的毒液具有溶血作用。

Example:  Haemolytic disease of the newborn can occur when the mother and baby have incompatible blood types. 新生儿溶血性疾病可能发生在母亲和婴儿血型不合的情况下。| 近义词:  hemolytic | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but consider terms like "blood-building" or "erythropoietic") | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From haemo- + -lytic.  "Haemo-" (or "hemo-") derives from the Greek word "haima" meaning "blood." "-Lytic" comes from the Greek word "lytikos," meaning "able to loosen" or "dissolving."
