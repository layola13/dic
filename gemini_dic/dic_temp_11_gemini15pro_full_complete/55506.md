distraught:/dɪˈstrɔːt/ | adj. | 心烦意乱的，极度不安的 | Extremely worried, upset, and confused. | She was distraught after hearing the news of her husband's accident. 她听到丈夫出事的消息后心烦意乱。 | The children were distraught at the thought of leaving their home. 孩子们一想到要离开家就感到非常不安。| synonyms: agitated, frantic, distressed, upset, worried | antonyms: calm, composed, serene, tranquil | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *distraught*, past participle of *distraughen*, from Old English *distract*, "to pull apart, distract," from Latin *distractus*, past participle of *distrahere*, "to pull apart, distract," from *dis-* "apart" + *trahere* "to draw, pull."
