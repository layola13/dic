lour:/laʊər/ (US), /ˈlaʊə(r)/ (UK)| v.| 皱眉；阴沉；(天空)变暗| to look angry or threatening; to appear dark and threatening, especially of the sky|  The sky loured, and rain began to fall. 天空阴沉下来，开始下雨了。|近义词：frown, scowl, darken|反义词：brighten, clear|词性:动词


Etymology:
From Middle English *louren, *lowren, from Old Norse *lūra (“to frown, scowl, leer”), from Proto-Germanic *lūrą (“to bend, lurk, peer, lie in wait”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)lewd- (“to bend, curve”). Cognate with Icelandic lúra (“to frown, scowl”), Swedish lura (“to lurk, deceive”), Danish lure (“to lurk, listen secretly”), German lauern (“to lurk, ambush”), Dutch loeren (“to leer, peer, squint”).
