greatcoats: /ˈɡreɪtˌkoʊts/ | n. | 大衣，厚外套 | A long, heavy coat, typically worn in cold weather. | The soldiers wore their greatcoats to protect themselves from the biting wind. 士兵们穿着厚外套来抵御刺骨的寒风。 |  Example:  The detective, shrouded in his greatcoat, blended into the London fog.  侦探裹在大衣里，消失在伦敦的雾中。| Synonyms: overcoat, topcoat, ulster | Antonyms:  shorts, t-shirt | Noun

Etymology:
From "great" + "coat".  "Great" in this context means large or of considerable size.  The term dates back to the 17th century.
