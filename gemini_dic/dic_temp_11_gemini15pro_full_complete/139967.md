odontopteryx:/ˌoʊˌdɑːntəˈtɛrɪks/ | n. |齿翼鸟 |An extinct genus of large, toothed seabirds from the Eocene and Oligocene epochs.  Known for their bony projections on their jaws that resembled teeth. | Example: The Odontopteryx was a formidable predator of the ancient seas. 齿翼鸟是古代海洋中令人生畏的捕食者。| Example: Fossils of Odontopteryx have been found in various locations around the world.  齿翼鸟的化石已在世界各地被发现。 |Synonym: Pelagornis, Pseudodontornis | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for an extinct genus) | Noun
