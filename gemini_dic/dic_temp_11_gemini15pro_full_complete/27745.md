cankered:/ˈkæŋkərd/ | adj. | 腐烂的；溃疡的；恶毒的；怨恨的 | Affected by canker; ulcerous. Also used figuratively to describe something corrupted or embittered. | The cankered branch had to be pruned from the tree. 这根腐烂的树枝必须从树上剪掉。 |  The cankered words of the critic poisoned the artist's mind.  评论家恶毒的话毒害了艺术家的心灵。 | 近义词:rotten, festering, malignant, bitter, resentful | 反义词:healthy, wholesome, kind, benevolent | 词性:形容词

canker:/ˈkæŋkər/ | n. | 溃疡，腐烂；弊端 | A destructive fungal disease of trees and other plants. Also used figuratively to describe something that corrodes or destroys. | The canker was spreading rapidly through the orchard. 溃疡病正在果园里迅速蔓延。| 近义词: blight, disease, corruption | 反义词: health, well-being | 词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *canker*, from Old North French *cancre*, from Late Latin *cancrum*, from Latin *cancer* ("crab, cancer"), so called because of the spreading nature of the disease.  The figurative sense of "something that corrodes or destroys" developed later.
