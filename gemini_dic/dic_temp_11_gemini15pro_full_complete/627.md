abrasively:/əˈbreɪsɪvli/ | adv. | 粗暴地，伤人地 | In a harsh and grating manner, causing irritation or annoyance. |  He spoke abrasively to the waiter, causing a scene. 他粗暴地对服务员说话，引起了一场争吵。

She responded abrasively to his criticism, making the situation worse. 她对他的批评反应激烈，使情况变得更糟。

| 近义词：harshly, roughly, rudely, gratingly | 反义词：gently, kindly, politely, smoothly | 词性: 副词

abrasive: /əˈbreɪsɪv/ | adj. | 粗糙的；磨损的；生硬粗暴的 | Showing little concern for the feelings of others; harsh.  |  Her abrasive personality made it difficult to work with her. 她粗暴的性格让人很难和她共事。

Sandpaper is an abrasive material. 砂纸是一种研磨材料。

| 近义词：harsh, rough, caustic, grating | 反义词：smooth, gentle, kind, polite | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *abrasus*, past participle of *abradere* "to scrape off," from *ab* "off, away from" + *radere* "to scrape."
