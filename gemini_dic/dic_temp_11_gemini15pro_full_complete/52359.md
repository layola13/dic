dictator:/ˈdɪkteɪtər/ /dɪkˈteɪtər/| n.|独裁者,专制者|A ruler with total power over a country, typically one who has obtained power by force. |Example 1: The dictator ruled with an iron fist, suppressing all dissent. 独裁者用铁腕统治，压制所有异议。

Example 2:  The dictator's regime was marked by human rights abuses and widespread poverty. 独裁者的政权以侵犯人权和普遍贫困为特征。|近义词：autocrat, tyrant, despot|反义词:democrat|词性:noun


dictators:/ˈdɪkteɪtərz/ /dɪkˈteɪtərz/| n.|独裁者们,专制者们|Plural form of "dictator," referring to multiple rulers with total power. |Example 1: Throughout history, many dictators have risen to power through military coups. 纵观历史，许多独裁者都是通过军事政变上台的。

Example 2: The dictators of those countries were notorious for their cruelty. 那些国家的独裁者以残暴著称。|近义词：autocrats, tyrants, despots|反义词:democrats|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dictātōrem*, accusative singular of dictātor (“dictator”), from dictāre (“to dictate”), frequentative of dicere (“to say”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *deḱ- (“to show, point out; pronounce solemnly”).
