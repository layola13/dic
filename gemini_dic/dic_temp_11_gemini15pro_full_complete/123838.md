moralised:/ˈmɒrəlaɪzd/ |ˈmɔːrəlaɪzd/| adj. | 道德化的；说教的 | Imbued with morality; made moral in character or tone. Often used with a negative connotation, suggesting excessive or tedious moralizing. | The film was criticized for its moralized tone and heavy-handed message. 这部电影因其道德化的语气和生硬的说教而受到批评。 |  The author's moralized approach to storytelling alienated many readers.  作者的说教式叙事方式疏远了许多读者。 | 近义词：preachy, didactic, sanctimonious | 反义词：amoral, unethical | 词性：形容词

moralised:/ˈmɒrəlaɪzd/ |ˈmɔːrəlaɪzd/| v. | 说教；灌输道德 | To make moral; to explain or interpret morally. Often suggests imposing one's moral views on others. |  He moralized about the dangers of drinking and driving. 他对酒后驾驶的危险进行了说教。 | She tended to moralize about everything, which annoyed her friends. 她倾向于对所有事情都进行说教，这让她朋友很恼火。 | 近义词：preach, sermonize, lecture | 反义词：-- | 词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "moralize," which comes from the Late Middle English *moralisen*, from Old French *moraliser*, from Medieval Latin *moralizare*, from Latin *moralis* ("moral").
