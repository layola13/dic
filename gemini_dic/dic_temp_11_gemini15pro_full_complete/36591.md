collation:/kəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 整理，校勘，核对；（尤指正式场合的）便餐，小吃 |  The collation of the ancient manuscripts took many years. 古籍手稿的校勘工作花费了多年时间。 | Example: The museum curator is working on the collation of a new collection of artifacts.  博物馆馆长正在整理新的文物收藏。

Example:  A light collation was served after the evening lecture. 晚间讲座结束后，提供了一些小点心。 | Synonyms: arrangement, comparison, assembly, light meal, snack | Antonyms: disorganization, scattering | noun

collation:/kəˈleɪʃən/ | n. |(computing) the process of defining and comparing data from multiple sources | The database collation ensures consistent sorting and comparison of text data. 数据库排序规则确保文本数据的一致排序和比较。 | Example: Setting the correct collation is crucial for multilingual database applications.  为多语言数据库应用程序设置正确的排序规则至关重要。

Example: The collation process identified several discrepancies between the two datasets. 校对过程发现了两个数据集之间的几个差异。| Synonyms: comparison, ordering, sorting | Antonyms: N/A | noun
