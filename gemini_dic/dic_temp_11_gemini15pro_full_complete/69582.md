felspar:/ˈfɛlspɑːr/ (US) /ˈfɛlspɑː/ (UK)| n. |长石|A group of rock-forming minerals that make up as much as 60% of the Earth's crust. They are composed of aluminum silicates of potassium, sodium, and calcium.|The granite contained large crystals of felspar.|近义词：albite, anorthite, orthoclase|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

felspath:/ˈfɛlspæθ/ (UK) /ˈfɛlspæθ/ (US)| n. |长石|(Chiefly British) alternative spelling of feldspar.|The geologist identified the rock as containing felspath.|近义词：feldspar, albite, anorthite, orthoclase|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Feldspat*, from *Feld* (field) + *Spat* (spar).  The term "spar" refers to a nonmetallic mineral with good cleavage.
