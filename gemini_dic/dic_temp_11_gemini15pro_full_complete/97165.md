infirm:/ɪnˈfɜːrm/ (US), /ɪnˈfɜːm/ (UK)| adj.| 虚弱的；不坚定的；意志薄弱的|Lacking physical or mental strength; not firm in purpose or opinion.|例句：He was too infirm to walk unaided. 他太虚弱了，无法独立行走。

例句：Her infirm grandfather now lives in a nursing home. 她体弱的祖父现在住在养老院里。

近义词：weak, frail, feeble, debilitated|反义词: strong, robust, healthy|词性:形容词


infirm:/ɪnˈfɜːrm/ (US), /ɪnˈfɜːm/ (UK)| n.| 虚弱的人；病人|A person who is weak, especially through age or illness (often used in the plural).|例句：The infirmary cares for the infirm.  疗养院照顾病人。

例句：Many of the infirm require constant care. 许多体弱者需要持续的护理。

近义词：invalid, patient|反义词: N/A|词性:名词


词源: Late Middle English: from Old French enferme, based on Latin infirmus, from in- ‘not’ + firmus ‘strong’.
