hitcher:/ˈhɪtʃər/ | n. | 搭便车的人；搭车旅行的人 | A person who travels by asking for free rides in other people's vehicles. |  A hitcher stood by the side of the road, thumbing a ride. 一个搭便车的人站在路边，竖起拇指要求搭车。| Synonyms: hitchhiker, free rider | Antonyms: driver, passenger | Noun

hitched:/hɪtʃt/ | v. | 搭便车；拴住，套住；钩住 | To travel by getting free rides in passing vehicles; to fasten or tie something. | We hitched a ride to the next town. 我们搭便车到了下一个城镇.  He hitched the horse to the wagon. 他把马套到马车上。| Synonyms: hitchhike, fasten, secure | Antonyms: unhitch, detach | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "hitch," meaning "to move with a jerk or series of jerks," from Middle English hicchen.  The sense of "getting a free ride" developed in the early 20th century.
