besieging:/bɪˈsiːdʒɪŋ/
verb
围攻，包围
surrounding and attacking a place, especially a city or castle, over a period of time.
The army was besieging the city for months.  军队围攻这座城市数月。
The fans were besieging the stage door, hoping to catch a glimpse of the band. 歌迷们围在舞台门口，希望能看到乐队一眼。
近义词:surrounding, blockading, investing, beleaguering
反义词:liberating, freeing, withdrawing
词性:verb


besieging:/bɪˈsiːdʒɪŋ/
adjective
围攻的，包围的
Relating to or characteristic of a siege.
The besieging army cut off all supplies to the city. 围城的军队切断了城市的所有补给。
The constant noise of the besieging forces was demoralizing the defenders. 围攻部队持续不断的噪音使守军士气低落。
近义词:surrounding, blockading, enclosing
反义词:liberating, freeing
词性:adjective

词源:From Old French  *asegier* (modern French *assiéger*), ultimately from Latin *assedere*  "to sit down to" (from *ad* "to" + *sedere* "to sit").  The sense evolved from "sitting down before a fortified place" to "besieging."
