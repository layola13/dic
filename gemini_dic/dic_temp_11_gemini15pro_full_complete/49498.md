demagoged: /ˈdeməɡɒɡd/ (UK) /ˈdeməɡɑːɡd/ (US) | v. | 煽动，蛊惑 |  To have treated or manipulated (a person or group) using demagoguery; to have appealed to the prejudices and emotions of (a group) in order to gain power over them. | The politician demagoged the crowd with inflammatory rhetoric.  这位政客用煽动性的言辞蛊惑民众。 |  manipulated, exploited, incited | reasoned with, educated, informed | Verb

demagogue: /ˈdeməɡɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈdeməɡɑːɡ/ (US) | n. | 煽动者，蛊惑民心的政客 | A political leader who seeks support by appealing to the desires and prejudices of ordinary people rather than by using rational argument. |  He was accused of being a demagogue who preyed on people's fears. 他被指责是一个利用人们恐惧的煽动者。| agitator, firebrand, rabble-rouser | statesman, diplomat, conciliator | Noun

demagoguery: /ˈdeməɡɒɡəri/ (UK) /ˈdeməɡɑːɡəri/ (US)| n. | 煽动，蛊惑人心的言辞 | Political activity or practices that seek support by appealing to the desires and prejudices of ordinary people rather than by using rational argument. | His demagoguery stirred up the crowd. 他的煽动性言论激怒了人群。 |  rabble-rousing, incitement, agitation |  reasoning, logic, diplomacy | Noun
