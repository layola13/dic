interventionist:/ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃənɪst/
n.|干预主义者；介入主义者|A person who favors intervention, especially government intervention, in the affairs of others, such as in the economy or in other countries.|The senator was known for his interventionist foreign policy.  这位参议员以其干预主义的外交政策而闻名。|interventionists, meddlers, interferers|isolationists, noninterventionists|noun
interventionist:/ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃənɪst/
adj.|干预主义的；介入主义的|Favoring or characterized by intervention, especially government intervention.|The government took an interventionist approach to the economic crisis. 政府对经济危机采取了干预主义的做法。|interfering, meddling, intrusive|non-interfering, laissez-faire|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From intervention + -ist.  Intervention comes from Late Latin interventio, from intervenire "to come between," from inter- "between" + venire "to come."
