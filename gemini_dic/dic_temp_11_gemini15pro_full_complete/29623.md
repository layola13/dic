catelectrotonic:/ˌkætɪˌlɛktroʊˈtɑːnɪk/
adjective
阴极电紧张的
Relating to the change in membrane potential produced in the region of a nerve or muscle cell adjacent to the cathode during the passage of an electric current.
Catelectrotonic potentials can increase the excitability of the neuron. 阴极电紧张电位可以增加神经元的兴奋性。
Near Antonyms:  anelectrotonic
Word Origin:  From Greek kata- ‘down’ + electron + -ic.
