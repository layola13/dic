dilettante:/ˌdɪləˈtænti/ |n.| 业余爱好者；浅尝辄止的人 | A person who cultivates an area of interest, such as the arts, without real commitment or knowledge. | He's a bit of a dilettante when it comes to painting; he enjoys it, but he's never seriously studied it. 他在绘画方面有点像个业余爱好者；他喜欢它，但从未认真学习过。| synonyms: amateur, dabbler, trifler | antonyms: professional, expert, connoisseur | noun

dilettanti:/ˌdɪləˈtæntiː/ |n.| 业余爱好者；浅尝辄止的人（dilettante的复数形式）| The plural form of dilettante, referring to a group of people who cultivate an area of interest without real commitment or knowledge. | The gallery was filled with dilettanti, more interested in being seen than in appreciating the art. 画廊里挤满了业余爱好者，他们更感兴趣的是被人看到，而不是欣赏艺术。 | synonyms: amateurs, dabblers, triflers | antonyms: professionals, experts, connoisseurs | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *dilettante*, present participle of *dilettare* "to delight," from Latin *delectare* "to delight, charm, please."
