a-dime-a-dozen:/əˌdaɪm ə ˈdʌzən/| adj.| 普通的，不值钱的，一毛钱一打 | Something that is a dime a dozen is very common, inexpensive, and not of very good quality.  It refers to something so readily available that its value is diminished. | Those kinds of toys are a dime a dozen.  那种玩具多得很，不值钱。| Synonyms: commonplace, ordinary, common, run-of-the-mill, cheap, plentiful, abundant | Antonyms: rare, scarce, unique, valuable, precious | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
This idiom originated in the late 19th century in the United States.  "Dime" refers to a ten-cent coin, and "dozen" refers to a group of twelve.  So literally, the phrase meant something so cheap you could get twelve of them for just ten cents.
