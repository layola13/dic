betokens:/bɪˈtoʊkənz/
verb
预示，表示
indicates; is a sign of
Her flushed cheeks betokened her embarrassment. 她涨红的脸颊表明她很尴尬。
signifies, foreshadows, portends, augurs
conceals, hides, masks
v.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bitokenen*, from Old English *betācnian* (“to signify, betoken, portend, predict, designate, represent”), from *be-* (prefix meaning ‘thoroughly, completely, about, around’) + *tācnian* (“to mark, show, indicate, signify”). Cognate with Dutch *betekenen* (“to mean, signify”), German *bezeichnen* (“to mark, denote, designate”), Swedish *beteckna* (“to signify, denote”).
