astart:/əˈstɑːrt/ | adv., adj. | 开始；惊起 |  Suddenly startled or alarmed. Can also be used adverbially to mean "in a startled manner." |  The loud bang sent the birds astart. 巨响把鸟儿惊飞了。 | synonyms: startled, alarmed, surprised, shocked, jolted | antonyms: calm, composed, relaxed, unperturbed | adverb, adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *astert, from Old English on stearte, from on + steart (“tail, rump”). Literally, "on the tail," hence "suddenly," "quickly," or "startled."  Related to the verb "start."
