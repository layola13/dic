anaptychus:/əˈnæptɪkəs/| n. |肛板，厣，尾甲|A small, calcareous or horny plate found on the posterior end of the body of certain extinct cephalopods, believed to have served as an operculum or protective covering.|The paleontologist discovered a well-preserved anaptychus alongside the ammonite fossil.|近义词：operculum|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "exposed")|词性:noun
