calambac:/ˈkæləmbæk/| n.|沉香(一种珍贵的香料)|A highly prized fragrant resinous wood, especially from certain Asian trees, used in incense and perfumes.  It is formed in the heartwood of aquilaria trees when they become infected with a type of mold.|The calambac incense filled the temple with a sweet, woody aroma. 寺庙里弥漫着沉香的甜美木质香气。|Synonyms: agarwood, aloeswood, eaglewood|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Vietnamese *trầm hương* (沉香), composed of *trầm* ("sink") and *hương* ("fragrance," "aroma").  This ultimately derives from Sino-Vietnamese, where *trầm* corresponds to Chinese *chén* (沉) and *hương* corresponds to *xiāng* (香).
