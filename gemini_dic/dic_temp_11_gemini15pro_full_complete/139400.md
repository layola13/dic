observation:/ˌɑːbzərˈveɪʃən/ | n. | 观察；观察结果；评论 |  The act of noticing or watching something or someone carefully.  A statement or remark based on something one has seen, heard, or noticed. |  Example 1: The scientist made careful observations of the animal's behavior. 科学家仔细观察了动物的行为。

Example 2:  Her observations about the meeting were insightful. 她对会议的评论很有见地。 | synonyms: remark, comment, finding, notice, perception | antonyms: ignorance, disregard, oversight | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *observacioun*, from Late Latin *observationem* (nominative *observatio*), from Latin *observare* ("to watch, observe").  *Observare* is composed of *ob-* ("toward, against") and *servare* ("to keep, preserve, watch").
