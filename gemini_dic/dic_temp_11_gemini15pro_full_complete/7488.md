ankylose:/ˈæŋkɪˌloʊz/ /ˈæŋkɪloʊs/| v. |使僵硬，强直|To become or cause to become stiff or rigid, especially as a result of disease or injury.|The arthritis had ankylosed his knee joint.  他的关节炎使他的膝关节僵硬了。|The patient's hip had ankylosed due to a prolonged period of immobility.  由于长时间不动，病人的臀部僵硬了。|stiffen, immobilize|mobilize|verb
ankylose:/ˈæŋkɪˌloʊz/ /ˈæŋkɪloʊs/| adj. |僵硬的，强直的|(of a joint) stiff or rigid.|He had an ankylosed spine.  他的脊柱僵硬了。|The ankylosed fingers made it difficult for her to hold a pen.  僵硬的手指使她难以握笔。|stiff, rigid|flexible, mobile|adjective
ankylosis:/ˌæŋkɪˈloʊsɪs/ /ˌæŋkɪloʊsɪs/| n. |僵硬，强直|The stiffening or immobility of a joint.|Ankylosis of the knee can make it difficult to walk.  膝盖僵硬会使行走困难。|The surgeon performed a procedure to correct the ankylosis in her wrist.  外科医生进行了一项手术来矫正她手腕的僵硬。|stiffness, rigidity|flexibility, mobility|noun
