kingfisher:/ˈkɪŋˌfɪʃər/ | n. |翠鸟|A brightly colored bird with a long, sharp beak, that feeds on fish. It dives into water to catch its prey. |例句：The kingfisher darted down and snatched a fish from the stream. 翠鸟俯冲下来，从溪流中叼走了一条鱼。

例句：We saw a flash of blue as a kingfisher flew past.  我们看到一只翠鸟飞过，一道蓝光闪过。


|近义词：halcyon (literary - more often refers to a mythical bird) |反义词: N/A |词性:noun

Etymology: From Old English *cyninges fiscere ("king's fisher"), perhaps because of the bird's bright, regal appearance, or because it was once thought to be the king of fishers.  This evolved into Middle English kinges fissher, then to the modern form.
