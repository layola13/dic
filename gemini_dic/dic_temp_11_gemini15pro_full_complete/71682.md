flaunters:/ˈflɔːntərz/| n.|炫耀者，卖弄者|People who display their possessions or abilities in a boastful or ostentatious way.  那些以自夸或炫耀的方式展示他们的财产或能力的人。|例句：The flaunters of their new wealth were unpopular with their neighbors.  那些炫耀新财富的人不受邻居们的欢迎。

例句：The flaunters of expensive cars often attract unwanted attention. 炫耀昂贵汽车的人经常会招来不必要的注意。|近义词：show-offs, boasters, braggarts|反义词：modest people, humble people|词性:noun


flaunt:/flɔːnt/| v.|炫耀，卖弄|To display something ostentatiously, especially in order to provoke envy or admiration or to show defiance.  炫耀地展示某物，尤指为了引起嫉妒或羡慕，或表示蔑视。|例句：She flaunted her new diamond ring. 她炫耀她的新钻戒。

例句：He flaunted his knowledge of the subject. 他炫耀他对这门学科的知识。|近义词：show off, parade, display, boast|反义词：conceal, hide, downplay|词性:verb

词源: From Middle English *flaunten, *flawnten, probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian flauta (“to play the flute”), flaut (“flute, swaggerer”); Swedish dialectal flauta (“to wander about, swagger”); possibly ultimately from Old French flaüte (“flute”).  源自中古英语 *flaunten, *flawnten，可能源自斯堪的纳维亚语；比较挪威语 flauta（“吹笛子”），flaut（“笛子，大摇大摆走路的人”）；瑞典方言 flauta（“四处游荡，大摇大摆走路”）；可能最终源自古法语 flaüte（“笛子”）。
