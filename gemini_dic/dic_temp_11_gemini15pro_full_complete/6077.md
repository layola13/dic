amercement:/əˈmɜːrsmənt/| n. |罚金，罚款|A pecuniary penalty; a fine or penalty inflicted at the discretion of a court for an offense.  An amercement, unlike a fine, was imposed arbitrarily at the discretion of the court.  It has largely been replaced by statutorily defined fines. |The judge imposed a heavy amercement on the defendant for his contempt of court. 法官因被告藐视法庭而对其处以巨额罚款。|Synonyms: fine, penalty, forfeiture, mulct|Antonyms: reward, prize, bonus|noun

amercement:/əˈmɜːrsmənt/| n. |(旧)任意罚款|Historically, an amercement was a fine imposed at the mercy of the court, unlike a fixed fine set by statute.  It was often used in medieval England.|The lord of the manor imposed an amercement on the peasant for failing to attend the court.  领主因为农民没有出席法庭而对他处以罚款。|Synonyms: arbitrary fine, discretionary penalty|Antonyms: fixed fine, statutory penalty|noun
