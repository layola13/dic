nucleoplasm: /ˈnjuːkliəˌplæzəm/ | n. | 核质 | The substance of a cell nucleus, excluding the nucleolus and chromosomes.  It is a viscous fluid containing various proteins, enzymes, and other molecules involved in gene expression and regulation. | The nucleoplasm plays a crucial role in DNA replication and transcription. 核质在DNA复制和转录中起着至关重要的作用。|Synonym: karyoplasm| Antonym: cytoplasm | noun
