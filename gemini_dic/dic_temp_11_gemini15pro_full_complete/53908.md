discounting:/ˈdɪskaʊntɪŋ/ /dɪsˈkaʊntɪŋ/| n. |贴现，折扣|The act of reducing the value of something, often referring to a reduction in price for immediate payment or a reduction in the estimated value of future payments.|Example: Discounting is common practice in retail during sales periods. 零售业在销售期间通常会打折。|Synonym: reduction, deduction, rebate|Antonym: premium, surcharge|Noun

discounting:/ˈdɪskaʊntɪŋ/ /dɪsˈkaʊntɪŋ/| v. |贴现，低估，不理会|1. Reducing the price of something. 2. Considering something to be less important or likely than it really is. 3. Ignoring something.|Example: They discounted the furniture to clear out the warehouse. 他们给家具打折以便清空仓库。|Synonym: reduce, devalue, disregard|Antonym: increase, value, regard|Verb
