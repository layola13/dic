anastomosis:/əˌnæstəˈmoʊsɪs/| n. |吻合；吻合口|A connection or opening between two things (especially cavities or passages) that are normally diverging or branching, such as between blood vessels, leaf veins, or streams.|The surgeon created an anastomosis between the two blood vessels. 外科医生在两根血管之间建立了吻合。|Synonyms: junction, connection, union, link|Antonyms: separation, division, disunion|Noun

anastomose:/əˈnæstəˌmoʊz/| v. |吻合；连接|To connect or join (blood vessels, nerves, etc.) surgically so as to form an anastomosis or to communicate directly.|The surgeon will anastomose the two sections of the intestine.  外科医生将把两段肠子吻合起来。|Synonyms: join, connect, unite, link|Antonyms: separate, divide, sever|Verb
