machicolation:/məˌʃɪkəˈleɪʃən/ |n.| 垛口，射孔 |An opening between the supporting corbels of a projecting parapet or the floor of a gallery built on the top of a castle wall, through which stones or other missiles could be dropped on attackers below.| The castle's machicolations allowed defenders to rain down boiling oil on the enemy. 城堡的垛口使得守卫者可以向敌人倾倒沸油。|Synonym:  crenel, embrasure, loophole|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "solid wall" for contrast)|Noun

machicoulis:/məˌʃɪkəˈliː/ |n.| 垛口，射孔 |A projecting gallery or parapet with openings between the corbels through which missiles could be dropped on attackers below.|  The machicoulis offered a strategic advantage to the defenders. 垛口为防御者提供了战略优势。|Synonym:  crenel, embrasure, loophole|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "solid wall" for contrast)|Noun
