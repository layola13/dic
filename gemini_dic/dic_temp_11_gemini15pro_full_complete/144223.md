outsider:/ˌaʊtˈsaɪdər/ | n. | 外地人，陌生人，局外人 |  A person who does not belong to a particular group, organization, or place; a stranger. |  She felt like an outsider in the close-knit community.  她在这个联系紧密的社区里感觉像个局外人。 | synonyms: foreigner, stranger, alien, newcomer | antonyms: insider, native, local | noun

outsider:/ˌaʊtˈsaɪdər/ | n. | （比赛中的）冷门选手 | A contestant or competitor considered unlikely to win. | He was an outsider in the race, but he surprised everyone by finishing second.  他是比赛中的冷门选手，但他以第二名的成绩让所有人大吃一惊。 | synonyms: underdog, dark horse | antonyms: favorite, front-runner | noun

outsider:/ˌaʊtˈsaɪdər/ | n. | 外行 | A person who does not have special knowledge or skill in a particular activity or area of study. | As an outsider to the world of finance, I found the jargon confusing.  作为一个金融界的外行，我发现那些术语令人困惑。 | synonyms: layman, amateur, nonprofessional | antonyms: expert, professional, specialist | noun
