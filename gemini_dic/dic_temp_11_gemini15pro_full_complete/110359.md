lightship:/ˈlaɪtʃɪp/ | n. | 灯船 | A ship equipped with a bright light, anchored at a dangerous place at sea as a warning to other ships. | The lightship marked the entrance to the harbor. 灯船标示着港口的入口。|  The lightship served as a beacon in the fog. 灯船在雾中充当灯塔。 |  Near synonyms: lightboat, beacon | Antonyms: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From light + ship.  The word "light" in this context refers to a navigation aid, and "ship" refers to the vessel carrying the light.  The term has been in use since the late 17th century.
