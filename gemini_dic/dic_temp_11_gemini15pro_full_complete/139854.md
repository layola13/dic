octosyllables:/ˌɑːktəˈsɪləbəlz/| n.|八音节字，八音步诗行|A line of verse consisting of eight syllables. |The poem was written in octosyllables. 这首诗是用八音节诗句写的。|synonyms: octosyllabic verse, eight-syllable verse|antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From octo- (eight) + syllable.  "Octo-" comes from the Greek "oktō" (eight). "Syllable" comes from the Late Latin "syllaba," from the Greek "sullabē" (something taken together, a syllable).
