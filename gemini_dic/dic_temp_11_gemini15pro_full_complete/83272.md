gymnosophy:/dʒɪmˈnɒsəfi/ | n. | 裸体修行；苦行哲学 | The practice of going naked, especially as a form of ascetic discipline, as practiced by ancient Indian philosophers. |  Ancient Greek writers described Indian gymnosophists who lived simply and contemplated deep philosophical questions. 古希腊作家描述了过着简朴生活并思考深奥哲学问题的印度裸体修行者。

gymnosophy:/dʒɪmˈnɒsəfi/ | n. | 苦行；禁欲主义 |  A philosophy or way of life characterized by self-denial and abstinence. |  Gymnosophy emphasized the rejection of material possessions and physical comforts. 裸体修行强调拒绝物质财富和肉体享受。


gymnosophy:/dʒɪmˈnɒsəfi/ | n. |  (尤指古印度哲学家的)裸体修行 | The practice of going naked, especially for philosophical or religious reasons, particularly among ancient Indian philosophers. | Some ancient Indian philosophers, known as gymnosophists, practiced extreme asceticism, including gymnosophy. 一些被称为裸体修行者的古印度哲学家奉行极端的禁欲主义，包括裸体修行。
