conjecture:/kənˈdʒɛktʃər/ | n. vt.| 推测，猜想；推测的结果 |An opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information.  A statement believed to be true but not yet proven.  To form an opinion or supposition about (something) on the basis of incomplete information.|例句：He conjectured that the company would soon go bankrupt. 他推测这家公司很快就会破产。|近义词：guess, surmise, speculate, hypothesize|反义词：fact, certainty, proof|词性:noun, verb

conjecture:/kənˈdʒɛktʃər/ | vt.| 推测，猜想 |To form an opinion or supposition about (something) on the basis of incomplete information. |例句：Scientists can only conjecture about the origin of the universe. 科学家只能推测宇宙的起源。|近义词：guess, surmise, speculate, hypothesize|反义词：know, prove, demonstrate|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *coniectura, from Latin coniectūra (“interpretation, inference, conclusion, conjecture”), from conicere (“to throw together, conclude, infer, conjecture”), from com- (“together”) + iacere (“to throw”).
