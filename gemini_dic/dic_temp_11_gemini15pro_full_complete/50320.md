deplorably:/dɪˈplɔːrəbli/
adverb
悲惨地；可叹地；糟糕地
in a way that is very bad and deserves strong disapproval
The situation was handled deplorably.  这种情况处理得很糟糕。
The building was deplorably maintained. 这座建筑维护得非常糟糕。

近义词: lamentably, regrettably, shamefully, disgracefully, woefully, terribly, awfully, appallingly
反义词: admirably, commendably, excellently


词性: adverb

词源: From Late Latin deplorabilis "lamentable," from Latin deplorare "bewail, lament" (see deplore).  Meaning "worthy of strong disapproval" is recorded from 1794.
