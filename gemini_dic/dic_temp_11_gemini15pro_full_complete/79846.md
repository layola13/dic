glyptodont:/ˈɡlɪptəˌdɑːnt/| n. |雕齿兽|An extinct, heavily armored mammal resembling an armadillo, but much larger.|The museum displayed a complete glyptodont skeleton.  博物馆展出了一具完整的雕齿兽骨架。|Synonym: none (due to its unique extinct nature - could use "prehistoric mammal" in a broad sense)|Antonym: none|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek *γλυπτός (gluptós, “carved, sculpted”) + *ὀδούς (odoús, “tooth”).  Referring to the sculpted appearance of the animal's teeth.
