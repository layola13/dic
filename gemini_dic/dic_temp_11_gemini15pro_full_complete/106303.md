ko'ing:/ˈkoʊɪŋ/ |n.| 鲤鱼旗飘动 |The sound or sight of koinobori (carp streamers) fluttering in the wind. |  The ko'ing of the brightly colored carp streamers against the blue sky was a beautiful sight.  鲤鱼旗在蓝天下的飘动是一道美丽的风景。|近义词: flapping, fluttering, waving |反义词: stillness, motionless |词性:名词

Etymology: From Japanese 鯉幟 (koinobori), meaning "carp streamer".  The "-ing" suffix transforms the noun into a gerund or present participle, describing the action of the streamers.  This usage is likely a recent development, primarily used in English contexts to describe the specific visual and auditory experience.
