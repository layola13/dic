buskin:/ˈbʌskɪn/| n.|半高靴；悲剧；悲剧风格|A thick-soled boot or half-boot, especially one worn by actors in ancient Greek tragedies. Also refers to the tragic genre itself.|The actor wore buskins for his role in the Greek play.  这位演员在希腊戏剧中扮演角色时穿了半高靴。|Synonyms: boot, cothurnus|Antonyms: sock, slipper|noun

buskin:/ˈbʌskɪn/| n.|悲剧；悲剧风格|The tragic genre or style, often associated with elevated language and serious themes.|Her play was a buskin, full of woe and suffering. 她的戏剧是一出悲剧，充满了悲哀和苦难。|Synonyms: tragedy, drama|Antonyms: comedy, farce|noun
