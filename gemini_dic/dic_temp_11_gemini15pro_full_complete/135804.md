nonprolixities:/ˌnɑːnprəˈlɪksɪtiz/ | n. | 简洁，简明 (plural) | The nonprolixities of the chairman's speech were appreciated by the busy attendees. |  The report was notable for its nonprolixities, getting straight to the point. | 近义词: conciseness, brevity, terseness | 反义词: prolixity, verbosity, wordiness | noun

词源:
From non- + prolixity.  Prolixity comes from the Latin "prolixus," meaning "extended," from "pro-" (forth) + "lixus" (fluid, flowing).  Therefore, nonprolixity refers to the absence of excessive length or wordiness.
