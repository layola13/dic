autoschediastic:/ˌɔːtəʊskɪˈdæstɪk/ | adj. | 自调度; 自动调度 |  (of a statistical model or process) characterized by the property that the variance of the random disturbances changes over time.  In other words, the variability of the errors in a model is not constant. | The researchers found that the data exhibited autoschediastic properties, requiring them to adjust their statistical model. 研究人员发现数据表现出自调度特性，需要他们调整统计模型。

The stock market's volatility often exhibits autoschediastic behavior. 股票市场的波动性通常表现出自调度行为。 | Near Synonyms: heteroscedastic, time-varying variance | Antonyms: homoscedastic, constant variance |  Adjective
