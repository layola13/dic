globalise:/ˈɡloʊbəlaɪz/ (US) /ˈɡləʊbəlaɪz/ (UK)| v. | 使全球化；全球化 | To make something worldwide in scope or application; to become worldwide in scope or application. | Many companies are trying to globalise their business. 很多公司正试图将业务全球化。| The world is becoming increasingly globalised.  世界正变得越来越全球化。| 近义词: internationalize, universalize | 反义词: localize, regionalize |词性: 动词

globalization:/ˌɡloʊbələˈzeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌɡləʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (UK)| n. | 全球化 | The process by which businesses or other organizations develop international influence or start operating on an international scale. | Globalization has led to increased economic interdependence. 全球化导致经济相互依存加深。| The globalization of media has spread Western culture worldwide. 媒体的全球化将西方文化传播到世界各地。| 近义词: internationalization, universalization | 反义词: localization, regionalization |词性: 名词

globalised:/ˈɡloʊbəlaɪzd/ (US) /ˈɡləʊbəlaɪzd/ (UK)| adj. | 全球化的 | Having been made worldwide in scope or application. | The globalised economy presents both opportunities and challenges. 全球化经济既带来了机遇，也带来了挑战。| We live in a globalised world where information travels instantly. 我们生活在一个信息即时传播的全球化世界中。| 近义词: international, worldwide | 反义词: local, regional |词性: 形容词


词源: globe (地球) + -ize (使...化)
