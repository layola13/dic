freudianly:/ˈfrɔɪdiənli/| adv.|以弗洛伊德的方式；精神分析地|In a manner relating to or influenced by the theories of Sigmund Freud, particularly those concerning the unconscious mind and the importance of early childhood experiences.|He coughed freudianly, as if to emphasize his hidden desires.|近义词：psychoanalytically|反义词:consciously|词性:副词

词源:
From Freud + -ian + -ly.  Sigmund Freud (1856–1939) was an Austrian neurologist and the founder of psychoanalysis.
