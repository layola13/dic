Lombards:/ˈlɒmbɑːdz/ | n. |伦巴第人；高利贷者|  A Germanic people who ruled most of Italy from the 6th to the 8th century. Also, historically, a term for moneylenders, often used pejoratively. | The Lombards established a kingdom in northern Italy. 伦巴第人在意大利北部建立了一个王国。|  Synonyms: moneylenders, usurers | Antonyms: borrowers, debtors | noun

Lombard:/ˈlɒmbɑːd/ | adj. |伦巴第的；伦巴第人的| Relating to the Lombards or their language. | Lombardic architecture is characterized by its use of brick and stucco. 伦巴第建筑的特点是使用砖和灰泥。| Synonyms: Lombardic | Antonyms: N/A | adjective

Etymology: From Old High German *Langobardi, meaning "long beards."

Not commonly used in phrases in modern English.  However, "Lombard Street" is often used to refer to the financial district of London, particularly associated with banking and finance.  This is due to the historical presence of Lombard moneylenders in the area.  This usage doesn't directly relate to the historical Lombards.
