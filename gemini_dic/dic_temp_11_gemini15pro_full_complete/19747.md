billet:/ˈbɪlɪt/ /ˈbɪlət/| n. v.|兵营； billets (n.) 住宿；(v.) 安排住宿|A billet is a place, usually a private home, where soldiers are given food and lodging. (n.)  To billet soldiers means to lodge them in a place like this. (v.)|The soldiers were billeted in the town. 士兵们被安置在这个镇上。|The army billeted the soldiers in local homes. 军队将士兵安置在当地居民家中。|近义词：(n.) lodging, accommodation, quarters; (v.) lodge, accommodate, quarter|反义词: N/A|词性: noun, verb

billet:/ˈbɪlɪt/ /ˈbɪlət/| n.|金属坯；坯料|A billet is a piece of metal, especially steel, that has been cast into a particular shape for further processing.|The steel billets were ready for rolling into sheets. 钢坯准备好轧制成钢板了。|近义词：blank, ingot, slab|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *billet*, diminutive of bille (“log, stick, note”), from Vulgar Latin *billia*, plural of *billius* (“log”), from Gaulish *billios* (“trunk, branch”).
