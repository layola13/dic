gloomth:/ɡluːmθ/
n.|忧郁，阴郁，悲伤|A state of sadness or depression; a gloomy atmosphere.|The room was filled with a palpable gloomth. 房间里弥漫着明显的忧郁气氛。|Synonyms: gloom, melancholy, dejection, sadness, depression|Antonyms: cheerfulness, happiness, joy, elation|Noun

Etymology:

A portmanteau word combining "gloom" and "youth."  It emerged in the late 1990s/early 2000s as an aesthetic associated with a particular online subculture.  While "gloom" has Old English roots, "gloomth" is a modern coinage.
