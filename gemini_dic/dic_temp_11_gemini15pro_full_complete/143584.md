outhumors:/ˈaʊθjuːmərz/ |n.|体液;  气质; (旧时指)人体内的四种体液|Bodily fluids, especially the four humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile) that were formerly believed to determine a person's temperament and health.  In older usage, it could also refer to one's disposition or mood.|His melancholy was attributed to an imbalance of his outhumors.|Synonym: humors, temperaments, dispositions|Antonym: (No direct antonym, but one could consider balance, equilibrium as contrasting concepts)|Noun
