mopier:/ˈmoʊpiər/ (US), /ˈməʊpiər/ (UK)| adj.|更闷闷不乐的，更忧郁的|Comparative form of mopy: more mopy.  Meaning more sad, depressed, and listless.|He seemed even mopier than yesterday after he lost his job. 他丢了工作后，今天看起来比昨天更闷闷不乐了。|近义词：sadder, gloomier, more depressed, more melancholic|反义词：happier, cheerier, more cheerful|词性:形容词

Etymology:  From mope + -y (suffix forming adjectives meaning "characterized by" or "inclined to"). "Mope" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly related to Dutch *moppen "to pout, sulk."
