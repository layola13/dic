jaculate: /ˈdʒækjʊlət/ | n. | (植物)小枝，短枝 | A short branch or shoot, especially one bearing flowers. (Botany) | The jaculate on the orchid was covered in tiny blooms.  兰花的小枝上开满了细小的花朵。| 近义词：shoot, sprig, twig | 反义词：branch, limb | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin jaculatus, past participle of jaculārī (“to hurl, throw a javelin”), from jaculum (“javelin, dart”), diminutive of jactus (“a throwing”), from jacere (“to throw”).
