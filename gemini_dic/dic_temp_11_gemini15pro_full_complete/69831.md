ferreter:/ˈferɪtər/| n.|搜查者；侦探；探索者|A person who searches persistently for something; an investigator or detective. |例句：He's a real ferreter when it comes to finding bargains.  他是个真正的讨价还价高手。

例句：The journalist ferreted out the truth behind the scandal.  这位记者发现了丑闻背后的真相。|近义词：detective, investigator, researcher, searcher|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly "obfuscator" or "concealer")|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "ferret" + "-er" (agent suffix).  The verb "ferret" comes from Old French "fureter" (to hunt with ferrets), from "furet" (ferret). Ultimately from Late Latin "furittus" (little thief), diminutive of "fur" (thief).
