overmodification:/ˌoʊvərˌmɑːdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |过度修饰；过度矫正| The act of modifying something excessively, often resulting in unnatural or artificial language. This can apply to pronunciation, grammar, or vocabulary. | Her overmodification of her accent made her sound unnatural. 她过度矫正她的口音，让她听起来很不自然。|近义词：hypercorrection, overcompensation |反义词: undercorrection, simplification | 词性: 名词

overmodification:/ˌoʊvərˌmɑːdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |过度修饰；过度矫正|  Using overly formal or complex language in a casual setting, often to appear more educated or sophisticated, but which can sound pretentious or awkward. | His overmodification of his language made him sound pretentious. 他过度修饰他的语言，让他听起来很做作。 |近义词：artificiality, affectation |反义词: naturalness, simplicity | 词性: 名词
