literally:/ˈlɪtərəli/ /ˈlɪtrəli/| adv.|字面上地；确实地；真正地；（用于加强语气）简直；实在地|In its oldest and most precise meaning, literally means "in a literal or strict sense." This means that words are being used according to their primary, most basic, or exact meaning, without metaphor or exaggeration. However, literally is also often used to emphasize a statement that is not literally true.  For example, "I'm literally dying of laughter" is an exaggeration used for emphasis, not a statement of fact. |The car literally fell apart. 这辆车真的散架了。

She was literally shaking with fear. 她真的吓得发抖。|近义词：actually, really, truly, virtually|反义词：figuratively, metaphorically|词性:副词
