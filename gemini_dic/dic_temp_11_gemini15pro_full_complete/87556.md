heteronomy:/ˌhɛtərˈɒnəmi/ | n. | 他律，异律 | The state of being under the control of another, especially in respect of moral principles; subjection to an external law.  The opposite of autonomy. |  Children's morality is initially heteronomous, dictated by rules set by adults. 孩子最初的道德是他律的，由成人制定的规则决定。| The soldier followed orders, an example of heteronomy in action. 士兵服从命令，这是他律的一个例子。 | Synonyms: subjection, dependence, subordination | Antonyms: autonomy, self-government, independence | noun
