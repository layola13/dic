marcassin:/mɑːrkæˈsɪn/ | n. |小野猪，幼野猪|A young wild boar. |例句：The hunters tracked the marcassin through the forest. 猎人追踪小野猪穿过森林。

例句：The marcassin squealed as it was separated from its mother. 小野猪与母亲分开时尖叫起来。|近义词：piglet, shoat |反义词: boar (成年野猪)| noun


Etymology->
From French *marcassin*, from Old French *marcasin*, diminutive of *marc*, from Late Latin *marcus*, of Germanic origin.  Related to English *mark* (a horse).  Originally referring to a young, marked or branded animal.
