humbles:/ˈhʌmbəlz/ | v. | 使谦卑，使感到卑微；降低…的尊严，屈辱 |  To make someone feel less important or proud; to lower someone's dignity. |  Her failure humbled her. 她的失败让她感到谦卑。 | synonyms: humiliates, abases, degrades | antonyms: elevates, exalts, honors | verb (third-person singular simple present humbles, present participle humbling, simple past and past participle humbled)

humble:/ˈhʌmbəl/ | adj. | 谦逊的；简陋的；卑微的 | Having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance; of low social, administrative, or political rank; unpretentious; not costly or luxurious. | He was humble about his achievements. 他对自己的成就很谦虚。  She came from a humble background. 她出身卑微。| synonyms: modest, unassuming, meek, simple | antonyms: arrogant, proud, haughty, grand | adjective
