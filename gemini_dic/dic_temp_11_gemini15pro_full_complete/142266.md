orpharion:/ˈɔːrfəriən/ |n.|奥法里翁|A wire-stringed musical instrument of the lute family, popular in England in the 16th and 17th centuries. It had a flat, oval-shaped soundboard and typically had 18 or more strings in six courses. |John Dowland composed many pieces for the orpharion. 约翰·道兰为奥法里翁创作了许多曲子。|Near synonyms: lute, bandora|Antonym: None (lack of a direct opposite for a specific instrument)|noun
