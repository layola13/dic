groundswell:/ˈɡraʊndˌswɛl/| n.|浪涌，群众运动，舆论的转变|a surge of feeling or support for something; a swell of opinion or sentiment that seems to rise spontaneously from the people, often influencing political or social change|例句：A groundswell of support for the candidate emerged after his powerful speech.  候选人发表了强有力的演讲后，出现了一股支持他的浪潮。

例句：The government was forced to react to the groundswell of public opinion against the new policy. 政府被迫对公众反对新政策的浪潮做出反应。|近义词：surge, upsurge, wave, swell, tide|反义词:decline, decrease, ebb|词性:noun
