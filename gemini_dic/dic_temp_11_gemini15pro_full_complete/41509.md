corollary:/ˈkɔːrəleri/ /ˈkɑːrəleri/| n. |推论，必然结果|A proposition that follows from (and is often appended to) one already proved.  A direct or natural consequence or result.|例句：A corollary of increased poverty is more crime.  贫困加剧的一个必然结果是犯罪增多。

例句：Loss of habitat is a corollary of deforestation. 栖息地丧失是森林砍伐的必然结果。|近义词：consequence, result, outcome, upshot|反义词: cause, origin|词性:noun

词源: From Late Latin *corollarium "gratuity, money paid for a garland," from Latin corolla "small garland, crown" (see corolla). The sense shift in Latin was from "garland given as a reward" to "something extra," hence "deduction, consequence."
