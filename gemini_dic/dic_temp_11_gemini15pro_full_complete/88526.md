hit-and-run: /ˌhɪt ən 'rʌn/ | n. | 肇事逃逸 | An accident in which a car hits someone or something and the driver leaves without stopping to help or to report the accident. | The police are investigating a hit-and-run. 警方正在调查一起肇事逃逸案件。| synonyms: hit-skip | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider "taking responsibility" or "reporting an accident") | noun

hit-and-run: /ˌhɪt ən 'rʌn/ | adj. | 肇事逃逸的 | Relating to or involving a hit-and-run accident. | The police are looking for the driver of the hit-and-run vehicle. 警方正在寻找肇事逃逸车辆的司机。| synonyms: hit-skip | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider "responsible" or "reported") | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
"Hit" from Old English "hittan" (to come upon, meet with, light upon). "Run" from Old Norse "rinna" (to flow, run).  The combined term "hit-and-run" gained common usage in the early 20th century with the rise of automobiles.
