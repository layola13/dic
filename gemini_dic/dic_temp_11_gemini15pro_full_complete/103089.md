jeopardize: /ˈdʒɛpərdaɪz/ | vt. | 危及，损害 | To put (something) in danger of loss, harm, or failure. |  His health was jeopardized by overwork. 他的健康因过度工作而受到损害。

The scandal jeopardized his career.  这桩丑闻危及了他的事业。


近义词: endanger, imperil, threaten, risk, compromise | 反义词: protect, safeguard, secure | 词性: verb

词源: From Old French *jeu parti, literally "divided game," referring to a chess problem, from jeu "game" + parti "divided."  The original sense was "to expose to risk or loss in a game of chance."
