electronystagmograph: /ɪˌlektrɒnɪˈstæɡməɡrɑːf/ (UK) /ɪˌlektrɑːnɪˈstæɡməɡræf/ (US)
n.
眼震电图仪，眼震描记器
An instrument used to detect and record involuntary eye movements (nystagmus), often used in diagnosing balance disorders.
The electronystagmograph revealed a significant horizontal nystagmus, suggesting an inner ear problem. 眼震电图显示明显的水平眼震，提示内耳问题。
Synonyms: ENG (abbreviation), nystagmographer
Antonyms:  None (Instruments don't typically have direct antonyms.)
noun


Etymology:
electro- (from Greek ēlektron, meaning "amber," referring to static electricity) + nystagmo- (from Greek nystagmos, meaning "drowsiness, nodding") + -graph (from Greek graphē, meaning "writing, drawing")
