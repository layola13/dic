crownpiece:/ˈkraʊnpiːs/| n.|皇冠; 五先令硬币|A former British coin worth five shillings.|He paid for the meal with a crownpiece.|Synonyms: five-shilling piece, coin|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but consider: banknote, paper money)|noun

Etymology->
From crown + piece.  The coin was originally called a "crown of the double rose" and depicted a crowned rose.  Later versions featured other crowned designs, leading to the shortened name "crownpiece".
