grovel:/ˈɡrɒvəl/ (UK), /ˈɡrɑːvəl/ (US)| v. |卑躬屈膝，匍匐；低声下气地请求|To behave in a way that shows you are very eager to please someone, especially someone who is more powerful than you, often in a way that does not seem to have any respect for yourself.|He groveled at the king's feet for mercy. 他跪在国王脚下乞求怜悯。

The salesman groveled to the customer, hoping to close the deal.  这个销售员低声下气地求顾客，希望完成交易。

|近义词：crawl, fawn, cringe, beg|反义词：assert, defy, stand up to|词性：动词
