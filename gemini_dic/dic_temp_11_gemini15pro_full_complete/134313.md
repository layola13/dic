noninstinctively:/ˌnɒnɪnˈstɪŋktɪvli/ | adv. | 非本能地，非直觉地 | Not in a way that is based on instinct; in a learned or deliberate way. | She reacted noninstinctively, pausing to think before she acted. 她没有凭直觉反应，而是在行动之前停下来思考。|  He raised his hand noninstinctively to shield his eyes from the sudden bright light.  他并非本能地举起手来遮挡突如其来的强光。 | 近义词: deliberately, consciously, rationally | 反义词: instinctively, intuitively | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
non- (prefix): from Latin, meaning "not"
instinctive: from instinct + -ive
instinct: from Late Latin instinctus "impulse, instigation," from past participle stem of instinguere "to incite, impel," from in- "in" + stinguere "to prick, incite"
