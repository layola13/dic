lark:/lɑːrk/ | n. | 云雀；嬉戏；玩笑；古怪行为 | A small brown songbird, typically found in open fields, known for its melodious song, often delivered while hovering high in the air. Also refers to a playful escapade or an amusing prank. |  The lark soared high above the fields, filling the air with its song. 云雀在田野上空翱翔，歌声充满了天空。|  synonyms:  frolic, prank, escapade, spree, game, fun, amusement, antic, jape, caper | antonyms: drudgery, work, toil | noun


lark:/lɑːrk/ | v. | 嬉戏；玩耍 | To engage in playful activity; to frolic. | We larked about in the park all afternoon. 我们整个下午都在公园里嬉戏玩耍。| synonyms:  frolic, play, sport, gambol, frisk, cavort, rollick, make merry | antonyms: work, toil, labor | verb


larks: /lɑːrks/ | n. |  嬉戏；玩闹 (plural of lark, as a noun referring to playful activity) | Playful activities or pranks. | Their weekend larks often involved exploring abandoned buildings.  他们周末的玩闹经常包括探索废弃的建筑物。| synonyms:  escapades, pranks, adventures, high jinks, shenanigans | antonyms: work, chores, responsibilities | noun
