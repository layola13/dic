bastardized:/ˈbæstərdaɪzd/ | adj. | 杂交的，劣化的，非正统的 | Changed from its original form in a way that makes it impure or of lower quality; not genuine or correct; formed from a mixture of sources. |  The movie presented a bastardized version of the classic novel, omitting key characters and plot points. 这部电影呈现了一个对经典小说的拙劣改编版本，省略了关键人物和情节。| Synonyms: corrupted, debased, adulterated, distorted, degraded | Antonyms: pure, authentic, genuine, unadulterated | Adjective


bastardize:/ˈbæstərdaɪz/ | vt. |  使…杂交，使…劣化，篡改 |  To change something from its original form in a way that makes it impure or of lower quality; to corrupt or debase. | The scriptwriter bastardized the original play by adding unnecessary love scenes.  编剧通过添加不必要的爱情场景，破坏了原著戏剧的完整性。 | Synonyms: corrupt, debase, adulterate, distort, degrade | Antonyms: purify, refine, improve, enhance | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From bastard + -ize.  "Bastard" originally referred to a child born out of wedlock, and the suffix "-ize" means "to make or cause to become."  Therefore, "bastardize" literally means "to make something like a bastard," implying a corruption or debasement of the original.
