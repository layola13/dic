alexandrine:/ˌælɪɡˈzændraɪn/| n. adj.|亚历山大诗体，亚历山大的|A line of verse having six iambic feet (iambic hexameter). Also, a poem written in such verses, usually in rhyming couplets.  It takes its name from a 12th-century French romance about Alexander the Great, written in this meter.|例句：The poem was written in alexandrines. 这首诗是用亚历山大诗体写的。

例句：The alexandrine verse form was popular in French poetry. 亚历山大诗体在法国诗歌中很流行。|近义词：hexameter|反义词:|noun, adjective
