dunfish:/ˈdʌnfɪʃ/| n. |腌鳕鱼|A cod or other fish, especially haddock, cured by being split open, lightly salted, and air-dried.|The dunfish was a staple food in many coastal communities. 腌鳕鱼是许多沿海社区的主食。|近义词：stockfish, dried fish|反义词:fresh fish|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *dunfissh*, from *dun* (“dull, dark-coloured”) + *fissh* (“fish”).  The term refers to the dull, grayish-brown color of the dried fish.
