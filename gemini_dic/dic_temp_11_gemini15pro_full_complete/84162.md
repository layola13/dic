half-playfulnesses:/ˌhæf ˈpleɪfəl.nəs.ɪz/
noun
半开玩笑; 半玩耍的状态
A state of being partly playful and not entirely serious.
Her half-playfulnesses were charming, but sometimes confusing. 她的半开玩笑的态度很迷人，但有时让人困惑。
Near synonyms:  lightheartedness, teasing, bantering
Antonyms: seriousness, solemnity
词性: 名词


Etymology:
half (Old English healf) + playfulness (playful + -ness)
half:  meaning "one of two equal parts"
playful: from play (Old English plegan "to exercise oneself, move briskly; occupy oneself, contend") + -ful (suffix meaning "full of")
-ness: suffix forming nouns from adjectives, denoting a state, quality, or condition
