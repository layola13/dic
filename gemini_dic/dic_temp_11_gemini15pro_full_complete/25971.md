burrstone:/ˈbɜːrstəʊn/ (UK), /ˈbɜːrˌstoʊn/ (US)| n.|磨石，粗砂岩|A hard, coarse-grained siliceous rock, formerly used for millstones.|The miller used a burrstone to grind the wheat into flour. 磨坊主用磨石将小麦磨成面粉。|Synonyms: millstone, grindstone|Antonyms: None|noun

burr:/bɜːr/ (UK), /bɜːr/ (US)| n.|刺果，毛刺，粗糙边缘|A rough or prickly seedcase or flower head that clings to clothing or animal fur; a whirring sound; a rough edge or ridge left on metal after cutting or casting. |The burrs stuck to my socks after my walk in the woods. 走在树林里，我的袜子上粘满了毛刺。|Synonyms: barb, prickle, bur, whir, snag|Antonyms: smoothness|noun

stone:/stəʊn/ (UK), /stoʊn/ (US)| n. vt. adj. adv.|石头，宝石，果核；向…扔石头；石头的；完全地|A hard, solid, nonmetallic mineral matter forming part of the earth's crust; a piece of rock quarried and dressed for building or other purposes; the hard central part of some fruits, containing the seed; a unit of weight equal to 14 pounds.|He threw a stone into the river. 他向河里扔了一块石头。|Synonyms: rock, pebble, gem, jewel|Antonyms: None|noun, verb, adjective, adverb
