indol:/ˈɪndɒl/ (UK), /ˈɪndoʊl/ (US)| n.|吲哚|A crystalline organic compound containing a benzene ring fused to a pyrrole ring. It occurs in coal tar and is formed in the intestines by bacterial decomposition of tryptophan. It has a fecal odor when concentrated but a flowery odor when dilute, and is used in perfumery.|The distinctive smell of jasmine is partly due to indol. 茉莉花的独特香味部分归功于吲哚。|synonyms: benzopyrrole|antonyms: N/A|noun

indolent:/ˈɪndələnt/ (UK), /ˈɪndələnt/ (US)| adj.|懒惰的，好逸恶劳的； indolent ulcers/tumours 无痛性溃疡/肿瘤|Wanting to avoid activity or exertion; lazy.|He was indolent and given to lying about in bed all day. 他很懒惰，喜欢整天躺在床上。|synonyms: lazy, idle, slothful, shiftless|antonyms: industrious, diligent, active|adjective
