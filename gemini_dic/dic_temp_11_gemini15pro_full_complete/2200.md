additively:/ˈædɪtɪvli/
adverb|加法地，累加地|In a way that involves adding something, rather than subtracting or multiplying. |The ingredients were combined additively.|近义词: cumulatively, incrementally|反义词: subtractively, divisively|词性: 副词

Etymology:
From "additive" + "-ly."  "Additive" comes from the Late Latin "additivus," from "additus," the past participle of "addere" (to add), from "ad-" (to) + "dare" (to give).
