graphite: /ˈɡræfaɪt/ (US), /ˈɡræfaɪt/ (UK)| n. |石墨|A crystalline form of carbon, naturally occurring as a soft, black, lustrous mineral, and used in pencils, lubricants, and electrodes.|Pencils contain graphite. 铅笔含有石墨。|Synonyms: plumbago, black lead|Antonyms: diamond (in terms of crystalline structure)|noun
