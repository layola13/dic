iridescent:/ˌɪrɪˈdɛsənt/ | adj. | 彩虹色的，闪光的 | Showing luminous colors that seem to change when seen from different angles. | The hummingbird's feathers were iridescent, shifting from green to blue as it moved.  蜂鸟的羽毛闪着彩虹般的光泽，随着它的移动，颜色从绿色变为蓝色。|  Example: The soap bubble floated, an iridescent sphere against the blue sky. 肥皂泡漂浮着，在蓝天下呈现出一个彩虹色的球体。| Synonyms: shimmering, opalescent, pearly, lustrous | Antonyms: dull, matte, lackluster | Word type: Adjective

iridescence:/ˌɪrɪˈdɛsəns/ | n. | 彩虹色，闪光 | The quality of showing luminous colors that seem to change when seen from different angles. | The iridescence of the oil slick on the water was mesmerizing. 水面上油污的彩虹色令人着迷。| Example: The butterfly's wings displayed a beautiful iridescence. 蝴蝶的翅膀展现出美丽的彩虹色。 | Synonyms: shimmer, opalescence, pearliness, luster | Antonyms: dullness, matteness | Word type: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *īridiscens, present participle of īridēscere “to become rainbow-colored,” from īris “rainbow” (from Greek ἶρις) + -ēscere (inceptive suffix).
