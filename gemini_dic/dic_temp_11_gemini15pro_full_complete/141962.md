oreide:/ˈɔːriˌaɪd/ | n. |山精；奥里德合金|An alloy of copper, zinc, and tin, resembling gold in appearance.  A mountain nymph.|She wore a necklace of oreide that shimmered like gold. 她戴着一条像黄金一样闪耀的奥里德合金项链。|Near synonyms: imitation gold, pinchbeck|Antonym: gold|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *oréide*, from Latin *Oreias*, from Greek *Ὀρειάς* (Oreiás, “mountain nymph”).  The alloy sense comes from the resemblance of the metal's color to gold, associated with mountain nymphs.
