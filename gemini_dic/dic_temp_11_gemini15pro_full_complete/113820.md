madcap:/ˈmædˌkæp/| adj. n.|鲁莽的，狂妄的；鲁莽的人，狂妄的人|Characterized by reckless impulsiveness or wild foolishness.  A person who acts in a reckless or impulsive way.|例句：He's always been a bit of a madcap. 他一直有点鲁莽。|近义词：reckless, impulsive, foolish, wild, harebrained|反义词: cautious, sensible, prudent|词性:形容词,名词

madcap:/ˈmædˌkæp/| n. |鲁莽的人，狂妄的人|A person who acts in a reckless or impulsive way.|例句：She's a madcap, always getting into trouble. 她是个疯丫头，总是惹麻烦。|近义词：hothead, daredevil|反义词:conservative|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *madcap*, equivalent to mad +‎ cap.  The original sense was "a crazy head," later extended to mean "a crazy person."
