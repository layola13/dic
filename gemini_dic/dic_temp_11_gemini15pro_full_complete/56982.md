double-crossed:/ˌdʌbəlˈkrɒst/ /ˌdʌbəlˈkrɑːst/ | adj. v. | 被出卖的，背叛的；欺骗；背叛 | To betray someone, especially someone who trusts you.  To deceive someone by going against a promise or agreement. |  He felt double-crossed when his partner revealed their secret plan to the competitor. 他感到被出卖了，因为他的伙伴向竞争对手透露了他们的秘密计划。 | 近义词: betrayed, cheated, deceived, backstabbed | 反义词: loyal, faithful, trustworthy | 词性: adjective, verb

词源：  "Double-cross" originates from the practice of marking a map with a double cross (an "X" over another "X") to indicate a point of betrayal or deception.  This visual representation of going back on one's word or agreement led to the metaphorical use of the term.
