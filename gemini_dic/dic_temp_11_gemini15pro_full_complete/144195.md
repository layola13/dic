outsift:/aʊtˈsɪft/ | vt. | 筛选出，区分出 | To separate or distinguish (something desirable) from something else, typically by sifting. |  例句：The historian outsifted the relevant facts from a mass of details. 历史学家从大量的细节中筛选出相关的事实。

例句：She outsifted the good apples from the bad. 她把好苹果从坏苹果中挑拣出来。 | 近义词：sift, winnow, separate, filter, cull | 反义词：combine, mix, mingle | 词性：动词

词源: out- (prefix meaning "out, beyond") + sift (verb meaning "to put through a sieve")
