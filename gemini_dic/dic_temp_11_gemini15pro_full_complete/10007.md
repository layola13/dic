apiaceous:/ˌeɪpiˈeɪʃəs/ | adj. |伞形科的，伞形的 |Belonging to or characteristic of the Apiaceae (or Umbelliferae) family, which includes plants like carrots, parsley, and celery. These plants typically have small flowers arranged in umbels (umbrella-like clusters). |Example 1:  Parsley is an apiaceous herb commonly used in cooking.  香菜是一种常用的伞形科草本植物。

Example 2: The apiaceous plant had distinctive umbels of white flowers.  这种伞形科植物有独特的白色伞形花序。 |Synonym: umbelliferous |Antonym: (No direct antonym, can use terms like "non-apiaceous" or describe a different plant family) |词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *apiaceus, from apium (“celery, parsley”) + -aceus (adjectival suffix). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ep- (“water, river”).
