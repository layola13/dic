obstructionist:/əbˈstrʌkʃənɪst/
n.|妨碍者，阻挠者|A person who deliberately delays or prevents progress, especially in political affairs.  Someone who tries to stop something from happening or succeeding.|The senator was accused of being an obstructionist by his opponents, who claimed he was blocking important legislation for purely political reasons.  这位参议员被他的对手指责为阻挠者，他们声称他纯粹出于政治原因阻挠重要的立法。|Synonyms: opponent, antagonist, resister, blocker|Antonyms: supporter, advocate, promoter, facilitator|noun

obstructionistic:/əbˌstrʌkˈʃənˈɪstɪk/
adj.|阻挠的，妨碍的|Relating to or characteristic of an obstructionist; designed or intended to obstruct or delay progress.|The committee's obstructionistic tactics prevented the bill from coming to a vote. 委员会的阻挠策略阻止了该法案进行投票。|Synonyms: hindering, impeding, delaying, stalling|Antonyms: helpful, cooperative, facilitating, expediting|adjective
