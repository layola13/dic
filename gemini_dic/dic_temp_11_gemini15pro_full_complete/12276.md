ascetic:/əˈsɛtɪk/ | adj. n. |禁欲的；苦行的；禁欲主义者；苦行者 | characterized by or practicing extreme self-denial and austerity, especially for religious reasons.  | He lived an ascetic life, devoid of luxury. 他过着苦行僧般的生活，没有任何奢侈享受。 |  adj. austere, spartan, abstinent, self-denying | adj. indulgent, hedonistic, luxurious | adjective/noun

ascetic:/əˈsɛtɪk/ | n. |禁欲主义者；苦行者 | a person who practices extreme self-denial and austerity. | The ascetic retreated to the mountains to meditate.  这位苦行者 retreated to the mountains to meditate. | n. monk, hermit, recluse | n. hedonist, sybarite | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *asceticus, from Greek askētikós (“athletic, laborious”), from askētēs (“athlete, monk”), from askeō (“I exercise, train”).  The original meaning referred to athletic training, but later evolved to denote spiritual discipline.
