epicureanism: /ˌɛpɪkjʊˈriːəˌnɪzəm/ | n. | 伊壁鸠鲁主义; 享乐主义 |  A philosophy that holds pleasure to be the chief good in life, but which emphasizes moderation and intellectual pleasures rather than sensual indulgence.  It is based on the teachings of the Greek philosopher Epicurus. |  He believed in epicureanism, seeking pleasure in simple things. 他信奉伊壁鸠鲁主义，在简单的事物中寻找快乐。|  The restaurant's opulent decor reflected the owner's epicureanism.  这家餐馆华丽的装潢体现了老板的享乐主义。 | Synonyms: hedonism, sensualism, sybaritism | Antonyms: asceticism, self-denial, stoicism | noun
