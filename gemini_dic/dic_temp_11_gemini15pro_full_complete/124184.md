mosaics:/moʊˈzeɪɪks/ /məˈzeɪɪks/| n.|马赛克，镶嵌图案|A picture or pattern produced by arranging together small colored pieces of hard material, such as stone, tile, or glass.|例句：The mosaics in the church are stunningly beautiful. 教堂里的马赛克美得令人叹为观止。

例句：She created a beautiful mosaic using broken tiles. 她用碎瓷砖创作了一幅美丽的马赛克。|近义词：inlay, tessellation|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "monochrome" for contrasting effect)|词性:noun

mosaic:/moʊˈzeɪɪk/ /məˈzeɪɪk/| adj.|马赛克的，镶嵌的|Made of or decorated with a mosaic.|例句：The mosaic floor was intricate and colorful. 马赛克地板错综复杂，色彩斑斓。


例句：He has a mosaic of different cultural influences in his background. 他的背景融合了不同的文化影响。|近义词：inlaid, tessellated|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mosaïque*, from Italian *mosaico*, ultimately from Late Latin *mosaicus* "of Muses," thus "work of art," from Greek *Mouseios* "of the Muses."  The connection with Muses may be because mosaic work was considered a high art form.
