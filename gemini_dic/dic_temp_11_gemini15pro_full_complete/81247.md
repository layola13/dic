grangerism: /ˈɡreɪndʒərɪzəm/ | n. |剽窃，抄袭（尤指从多处来源拼凑而成）| The act of compiling a work by collecting and patching together passages from other works, especially without proper acknowledgment. |  His new book was criticized for grangerism. 他的新书因剽窃而受到批评。

His academic career was ruined by accusations of grangerism.  他的学术生涯因被指控剽窃而毁于一旦。 | 近义词: plagiarism, appropriation, lifting | 反义词: originality, attribution | 词性: noun

词源: From Reverend James Granger (1723-1776), who compiled a biographical history of England (1769) with blank leaves for the insertion of engraved portraits.  People would then "grangerize" their copies by adding illustrations cut from other books.  The term later broadened to encompass any kind of textual collage or patchwork assembled from other sources.
