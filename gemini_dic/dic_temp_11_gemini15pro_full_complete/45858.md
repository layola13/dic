cut-off:/ˈkʌtˌɔːf/ (US), /ˈkʌtˌɒf/ (UK)| n. adj.|截止; 切断; 停止; 隔绝; 截止点; 截止期限; 分界线|A point or level which is a limit of something; the act of stopping the supply of something|The application cut-off date is May 1st. 申请截止日期是5月1日。|synonyms: deadline, limit, end, termination|antonyms: beginning, start, commencement|noun

cut-off:/ˈkʌtˌɔːf/ (US), /ˈkʌtˌɒf/ (UK)| adj.|切断的; 隔绝的; 停止的|Stopped or interrupted; separated or isolated|They live in a cut-off village in the mountains.  他们住在山上一个与世隔绝的村庄里。|synonyms: isolated, remote, secluded, disconnected|antonyms: connected, joined, linked|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "cut off," meaning to interrupt, stop, or discontinue something.  "Cut" comes from the Old English "cytan," and "off" from the Old English "of."
