melamine: /ˈmɛləmiːn/ | n. |三聚氰胺|A white crystalline organic base, C3H6N6, used in making resins and plastics, especially in the manufacture of tableware. It is also used illegally to adulterate food products to falsely inflate apparent protein content.  |Melamine was added to milk powder to artificially boost the protein readings. 三聚氰胺被添加到奶粉中，以人为地提高蛋白质读数。| synonyms: cyanuramide, 1,3,5-triazine-2,4,6-triamine | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a chemical compound. Could consider "nutrient" in the context of food adulteration) | noun
