goody-goody: /ˈɡʊdi ˈɡʊdi/ | n. |伪善者，故作好人的人|A person who behaves in a way intended to appear morally superior or excessively virtuous, often to the point of being annoying or insincere. |例句：She's such a goody-goody, always telling the teacher when someone breaks a rule. 她真是个伪君子，总是有人违反规则就告诉老师。

例句：He acted like a goody-goody in front of his parents, but behind their backs he was a troublemaker. 他在父母面前装作好人，但在他们背后却是个捣蛋鬼。|近义词：prig, sanctimonious person |反义词: rogue, scoundrel |词性: noun


goody-goody: /ˈɡʊdi ˈɡʊdi/ | adj. |伪善的，故作好人状的|Excessively or affectedly virtuous. |例句：Her goody-goody attitude made her unpopular with her classmates. 她那副伪善的态度让她在同学中不受欢迎。

例句：He gave me a goody-goody speech about the importance of honesty. 他就诚实的重要性对我进行了一番虚伪的说教。|近义词：self-righteous, pious, holier-than-thou |反义词: rebellious, wicked |词性: adjective


词源:  A reduplication of "goody," an archaic term for something good or pleasant, often used in children's language.  The reduplication emphasizes the excessive or affected nature of the goodness.
