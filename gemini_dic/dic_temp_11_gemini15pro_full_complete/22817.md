Bosphorus: /ˈbɒsfərəs/ (UK) /ˈbɑːsfərəs/ (US)| n. |博斯普鲁斯海峡; 伊斯坦布尔海峡|A strait connecting the Black Sea with the Sea of Marmara.|Ships pass through the Bosphorus to and from the Black Sea. 船只通过博斯普鲁斯海峡往返黑海。|Synonym: Strait of Istanbul| Antonym: N/A| noun

Etymology:
From Ancient Greek Βόσπορος (Bósporos), meaning "ox-ford", from βοῦς (boûs, “ox, cow”) + πόρος (póros, “passage, ford”).  The name alludes to the myth of Io, who was transformed into a cow and swam across the strait.
