ice-cold:/ˌaɪs ˈkoʊld/| adj.|冰冷的，极冷的|Extremely cold; as cold as ice.  It describes something that is not just cold, but very, very cold.|The ice-cold water shocked my system. 冰冷的水刺激了我的神经系统。

The ice-cold wind whipped through the streets. 冰冷的风吹过街道。|近义词：freezing, frigid, glacial|反义词:hot, warm, boiling|词性:形容词
ice:/aɪs/| n. v.|冰，结冰|Frozen water.  As a verb, it means to cover something with icing or to become covered with ice.|The lake was covered in a thick layer of ice. 湖面上覆盖着一层厚厚的冰。

The roads iced over during the night. 道路在夜间结冰了。
cold:/koʊld/| adj. n.|冷的，寒冷|Having a low temperature; feeling unpleasant because of a lack of warmth.|It's a cold day today. 今天天气很冷。

I caught a cold last week. 我上周感冒了。
