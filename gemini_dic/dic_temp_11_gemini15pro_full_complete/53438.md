disappointing:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人失望的 | Causing disappointment; failing to fulfill someone's hopes or expectations. | The movie was disappointing. 这部电影令人失望。 | The team's performance was disappointing.  这个团队的表现令人失望。 | 近义词: unsatisfactory, underwhelming, frustrating | 反义词: satisfying, pleasing, encouraging | 词性:形容词

disappoint:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt/ | vt. | 使失望 | Fail to fulfill the hopes or expectations of (someone). | I hate to disappoint you, but I can't come tonight. 我很抱歉让你失望，但我今晚不能来。| The outcome of the election disappointed many people. 选举结果让很多人失望。| 近义词: let down, dissatisfy, frustrate | 反义词: please, satisfy, encourage | 词性:动词

词源: From Old French *desapointier, from des- "away from" + apointier "to appoint, arrange." The sense shifted from "to undo an appointment" to "to thwart hopes."
