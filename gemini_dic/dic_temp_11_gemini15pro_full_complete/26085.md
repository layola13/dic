bushy-tailed:/ˈbʊʃi teɪld/| adj.|精力充沛的；充满热情的；警惕的|Having a thick, bushy tail. Often used figuratively to describe someone eager and energetic, especially someone new to a job or situation, or someone alert and watchful.  Origin: Descriptive, referring to the appearance of animals like squirrels or foxes with full, thick tails.|The bushy-tailed squirrel darted up the tree. 那只尾巴浓密的松鼠窜上了树。|The new intern arrived bright and bushy-tailed, ready to learn.  新来的实习生精神抖擞，准备学习。|近义词:eager, enthusiastic, energetic, bright-eyed, alert, watchful|反义词:lethargic, apathetic, listless, dull|词性:adjective
