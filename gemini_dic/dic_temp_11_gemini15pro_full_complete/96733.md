inelasticity:/ˌɪnɪˌlæˈstɪsɪti/ | n. |无弹性，非弹性|The inability of something to return to its original size and shape after being stretched or compressed.  The lack of responsiveness to change, especially in demand or supply, in relation to price or other factors.|The inelasticity of demand for gasoline means that even a large increase in price doesn't significantly reduce consumption.|近义词：rigidity, inflexibility|反义词: elasticity, flexibility|词性: noun

inelastic:/ˌɪnɪˈlæstɪk/ | adj. |无弹性的，非弹性的|Describing something that does not return to its original size and shape after being stretched or compressed.  Describing demand or supply that is not very responsive to changes in price or other factors.|Rubber bands are elastic, while steel rods are relatively inelastic.  The demand for insulin is inelastic because people with diabetes need it regardless of the price. |近义词：rigid, inflexible|反义词: elastic, flexible|词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + elastic + -ity (noun suffix).  "Elastic" comes from the Greek word *elastos* meaning "malleable, ductile."
