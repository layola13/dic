Etymology:
inter- (prefix): between, among, mutually. From Latin *inter*.
libel (verb): to defame or slander someone by writing or printing something untrue about them.  From Old French *libelle*, meaning "little book," ultimately from Latin *libellus*, diminutive of *liber* "book."
-ed (suffix): past participle marker.
