malthusians:/mælˈθuːziənz/ | n. | 马尔萨斯主义者；信奉马尔萨斯人口论的人 | People who believe in the theories of Thomas Robert Malthus, particularly that population will always tend to outrun the food supply and that betterment of humankind is impossible without stern limits on reproduction. |  Many environmentalists are accused of being modern-day Malthusians. 许多环保主义者被指责是现代的马尔萨斯主义者。| synonyms: neo-Malthusians | antonyms: cornucopians | noun

malthusian:/mælˈθuːziən/ | adj. | 马尔萨斯主义的；关于马尔萨斯人口论的 | Relating to the theories of Thomas Robert Malthus, especially his theory that population tends to increase faster than the means of subsistence. |  A Malthusian catastrophe is a prediction that population growth will eventually outstrip agricultural production. 马尔萨斯灾难是指人口增长最终将超过农业生产的预测。| synonyms: overpopulationist | antonyms:  optimistic (regarding population growth) | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Thomas Robert Malthus (1766–1834), English economist and demographer, + -ian.
