damosella:/ˌdæməˈzɛlə/| n.|少女，闺女|A young unmarried woman, especially one of noble birth.  Historically, it referred to a young lady attending on a more senior woman.|例句：The damosella was known for her beauty and grace. 这位少女以她的美丽和优雅而闻名。

例句：The knight pledged his service to the fair damosella. 这位骑士向美丽的少女宣誓效忠。|近义词：maiden, damsel, lass|反义词: matron, crone|词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old French *damoisele, diminutive of dame (“lady”), from Latin domina (“mistress of a household”), feminine of dominus (“lord, master”).
