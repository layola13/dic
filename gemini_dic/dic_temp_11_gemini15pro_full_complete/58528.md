duffed:/dʌft/
| adj. | 搞砸的，失败的 |  Ruined, spoiled, messed up, especially due to incompetence or a mistake.  Specifically used in informal contexts, often related to baking or cooking, but also more broadly to any task or project.  Implies a sense of clumsy failure. | I completely duffed the cake – it's burnt on the outside and raw in the middle. 我完全把蛋糕烤砸了——外面烤焦了，里面还是生的。| 近义词: botched, bungled, flubbed, messed up, screwed up | 反义词: successful, perfect, accomplished
词性:形容词


duffed:/dʌft/
| v. | 搞砸，弄糟 | Past tense and past participle of "duff," meaning to ruin, spoil, or mess something up through incompetence or a mistake. | I duffed the landing and fell flat on my face. 我落地没站稳，脸朝下摔了个跟头。| 近义词: botched, bungled, flubbed, messed up, screwed up | 反义词: succeed, achieve, accomplish
词性:动词


词源:
Likely originated in Scottish dialect, potentially related to "dowf," meaning dull or spiritless.
