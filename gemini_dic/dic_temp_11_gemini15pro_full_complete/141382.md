operculated: /ˈɒpəkjʊleɪtɪd/ (UK) /ˈɑːpərkyəleɪtɪd/ (US)
adjective
有盖的；有厣的
Having an operculum; covered by an operculum.  An operculum is a lid or cover, especially one on a container or a part of the body.
The operculated snail retreated into its shell.  这只带盖的蜗牛缩回了壳里。
The operculated eggs of some fish species are protected from predators.  一些鱼类的有盖卵可以免受捕食者的侵害。
Near Synonyms: covered, lidded, sealed
Antonyms: open, uncovered, exposed
Word Origin: From Latin *operculatus*, past participle of *operculare*, from *operculum* "lid, cover"
Word Class: Adjective
