molt:/moʊlt/ (US), /məʊlt/ (UK)| v. n.|蜕皮; 换毛; 脱落|To shed periodically part or all of a coat or an outer covering, such as feathers, cuticle, or skin, which is then replaced by a new growth.|The snake molted its skin. 蛇蜕了皮。| 近义词:shed, cast off|反义词:grow|词性:动词, 名词

molted:/ˈmoʊltɪd/ (US), /ˈməʊltɪd/ (UK)| v. adj.|蜕皮的; 换毛的; 脱落的|(past tense and past participle of molt) Having shed periodically part or all of a coat or an outer covering, such as feathers, cuticle, or skin, which is then replaced by a new growth.|The bird had molted its bright summer plumage. 这只鸟已经蜕掉了它鲜艳的夏季羽毛。|近义词:shed, cast off|反义词:grown|词性:动词(过去式/过去分词), 形容词
