demagogical:/ˌdeməˈɡɒdʒɪkəl/ (US) /ˌdeməˈɡɑːdʒɪkl/| adj.|煽动性的，蛊惑人心的| Appealing to emotions and prejudices rather than reason, typically to gain popular support.|  The politician delivered a demagogical speech, filled with inflammatory rhetoric.  这个政客发表了一场煽动性的演讲，充满了煽动性的言辞。| Example: His demagogical claims about immigrants resonated with some voters.  他关于移民的煽动性言论引起了一些选民的共鸣。| synonyms: inflammatory, rabble-rousing, manipulative, populist| antonyms: reasoned, logical, factual, objective| adjective

demagogy:/ˈdeməɡɒɡi/ (US) /ˈdeməɡɑːɡi/| n.|煽动，蛊惑人心| The practice of appealing to emotions and prejudices rather than reason, typically to gain popular support.| The rise of demagogy in the country is a worrying trend.  这个国家煽动政治的兴起是一个令人担忧的趋势。| Example:  The dictator's rule was characterized by demagogy and violence.  独裁者的统治以煽动和暴力为特征。| synonyms: rabble-rousing, manipulation, incitement| antonyms: reason, logic, statesmanship| noun

Etymology:  From Late Latin *dēmagōgicus*, from Ancient Greek dēmagōgikós (“of or pertaining to a popular leader”), from dēmagōgós (“leader of the people”).  Ultimately from dēmos (“people”) + agōgos (“leading”).
