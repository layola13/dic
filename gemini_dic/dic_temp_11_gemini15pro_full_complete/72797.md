foisty:/ˈfɔɪsti/ (US), /ˈfɔɪsti/ (UK)| adj.|发霉的，有霉味的|Smelling stale and damp, usually due to poor ventilation.|The old books in the attic smelled foisty.  阁楼里的旧书闻起来有霉味。|Synonyms: musty, moldy, damp, mildewy|Antonyms: fresh, airy, dry, ventilated|adjective

foisty:/ˈfɔɪsti/ (US), /ˈfɔɪsti/ (UK)| adj.|陈腐的，过时的|Outdated, old-fashioned.|His foisty ideas about women belonged in the last century. 他关于女性的陈腐观念属于上个世纪。|Synonyms: stale, outdated, antiquated, old-fashioned|Antonyms: modern, contemporary, current, up-to-date|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *foist*, perhaps related to Old French *fust* "wine cask," ultimately from Latin *fustis* "club, staff, wood."  The connection likely lies in the musty smell of old wine casks.
