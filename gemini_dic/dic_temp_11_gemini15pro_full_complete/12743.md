assibilation:/əˌsɪbɪˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 齿擦音化；摩擦音化 | The process by which a non-sibilant consonant becomes a sibilant, often due to the influence of a nearby sound.  | Example: The pronunciation of "did you" as "didju" is an example of assibilation.  "did you" 发成 "didju" 就是齿擦音化的一个例子。 | Example: The change from /t/ to /s/ before /i/ in some languages is a common type of assibilation.  某些语言中 /t/ 在 /i/ 前变为 /s/ 是一种常见的齿擦音化现象。 | Synonyms: palatalization (sometimes) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym exists) | noun
