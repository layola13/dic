/ˈmɪstər/ (Mister - abbreviated Mr.)| n.|先生|A polite title used for a man, placed before his family name or full name.  Derived from "master".|Example: Mr. Smith is our new teacher. 史密斯先生是我们的新老师。|Example: Please address him as Mr. John Doe. 请称呼他为约翰·多伊先生。|Synonym: sir|Antonym: N/A|noun

/ˈmæstər/| n. adj. v.|大师，主人，硕士|From Old French "maistre", ultimately from Latin "magister" (teacher, chief, director).  Can refer to a person with control or authority, someone highly skilled in a craft, or an academic degree.|

Example: He is a master of disguise. 他是伪装大师。
Example: The dog obeyed its master. 狗听从了主人的话。

Synonym: expert, proficient, chief, lord, owner
Antonym: servant, slave, apprentice, beginner|noun, adjective, verb
