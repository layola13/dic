anaesthetising: /əˈnɛsθɪˌtaɪzɪŋ/ /əˈniːsθəˌtaɪzɪŋ/
|v.|麻醉；使麻木|Administering an anesthetic to cause loss of sensation or consciousness. The process of inducing anesthesia.|The surgeon was anaesthetising the patient before the operation.  外科医生在手术前给病人麻醉。|近义词:numbing, desensitizing|反义词:stimulating, awakening|词性:动词 (present participle of anaesthetise/anesthetize)

anaesthetise: /əˈnɛsθɪˌtaɪz/ /əˈniːsθəˌtaɪz/
|v.|麻醉；使麻木|To administer an anesthetic to. To deprive of feeling or awareness.|The dentist anaesthetised the patient's gum before extracting the tooth. 牙医在拔牙前麻醉了病人的牙龈。|近义词:numb, desensitize|反义词:stimulate, awaken|词性:动词


词源: From Greek ἀναισθησία (anaisthesia), from ἀν- (an-, "not") + αἴσθησις (aisthēsis, "sensation").
