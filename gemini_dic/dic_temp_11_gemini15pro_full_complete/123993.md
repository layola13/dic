morning-glories: /ˈmɔːrnɪŋ ˈɡlɔːriz/ /ˈmɔːrnɪŋ ˈɡlɔːriz/
n.
牵牛花（复数）
A climbing plant with trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon.
The morning-glories climbed the trellis, their vibrant colors a welcome sight. 牵牛花爬上了棚架，它们鲜艳的颜色令人赏心悦目。
Morning-glories are known for their rapid growth. 牵牛花以其快速生长而闻名。
synonyms: bindweed, convolvulus
antonyms: (No direct antonym for a specific flower)
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "morning" + "glory."  "Morning" refers to the time of day the flowers typically open, and "glory" refers to the beauty of the flower.  The term likely arose from the observation that these flowers bloom in the morning and display their full beauty at that time.
