deused:/ˈdjuːzd/ | adj. | 该死的，讨厌的 (mildly objectionable) |  A euphemism for "damned," used to express annoyance or frustration. It's considered a milder form of swearing and is now somewhat archaic. |  "Deused nuisance, this broken pen!" 这支破笔真讨厌！|  近义词: darned, blasted, confounded | 反义词: blessed, delightful | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From the archaic past participle of "deuse," a verb meaning "to curse."  Ultimately from the Latin "deus" (god), likely influenced by French "dé" (dice) suggesting bad luck.
