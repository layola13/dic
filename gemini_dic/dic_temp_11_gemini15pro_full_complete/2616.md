adonai:/ˌɑːdoʊˈnaɪ/ |n.| 主，我主，上帝 |A Hebrew name for God, representing reverence and respect. It's a substitute for the Tetragrammaton (YHWH), the four-letter name of God, considered too holy to be uttered.|In Jewish tradition, Adonai is used when reading the scriptures aloud in place of YHWH. 在犹太传统中，朗读经文时用Adonai代替YHWH。|Synonyms: Lord, God, The Almighty|Antonyms: N/A |Noun
