aftershock:/ˈɑːftərʃɒk/ (UK) /ˈæftərʃɑːk/ (US)| n. |余震；后续冲击|A smaller earthquake following the main shock of a large earthquake.  An aftershock is a smaller earthquake that follows the main shock of a larger earthquake.  It occurs in the same region of the main shock and is a result of the earth's crust readjusting after the main earthquake. It can also refer to the continuing unpleasant effects of a bad event.|例句：The aftershock was almost as strong as the initial earthquake.  余震几乎和最初的地震一样强烈。

例句：The country is still dealing with the aftershocks of the war.  这个国家仍在应对战争的后续冲击。|近义词：tremor, reverberation, consequence, repercussion|反义词:  (No direct antonym, could consider "precursor" for the main shock)|词性:noun
