fougasse:/fuːˈɡæs/ /fuˈɡɑːs/| n.|地雷，坑道爆破，一种普罗旺斯面包|A mine or charge placed in a cavity and exploded to destroy enemy fortifications or personnel. Also, a type of Provençal bread.|The engineers planted a fougasse near the enemy bunker.|近义词：mine, explosive, landmine, petard|反义词: None|词性: Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fougasse*, from Occitan *fogassa*, from Late Latin *focacia*, from Latin *focus* ("hearth, fireplace").  The bread and the explosive share the etymology due to the bread's traditional baking method in the embers of a fire, resembling the placement and explosion of a fougasse.
