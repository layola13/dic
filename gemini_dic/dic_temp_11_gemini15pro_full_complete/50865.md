desensationalized: /diːˌsɛnsəˈʃənəlaɪzd/
adjective
去轰动性的，淡化了的
Deprived of sensationalism; made less exciting or shocking.
The newspaper article desensationalized the celebrity scandal by focusing on the facts. 这篇报纸文章通过关注事实淡化了名人的丑闻。
The documentary presented a desensationalized account of the war. 这部纪录片对战争进行了去轰动性的描述。
近义词: downplayed, understated, toned down, de-emphasized
反义词: sensationalized, exaggerated, hyped up
词性: adjective

词源:
From de- + sensationalize + -ed.  "De-" is a prefix meaning "remove" or "reduce". "Sensationalize" means to present something in a way that is intended to provoke public interest and excitement, often at the expense of accuracy. "-ed" is a suffix used to form the past participle of verbs, often creating adjectives.
