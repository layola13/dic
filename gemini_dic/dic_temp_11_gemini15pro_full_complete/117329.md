meddling:/ˈmɛdlɪŋ/| adj. n.|干涉的，爱管闲事的；干涉，干预|Interfering in something that is not one's concern.|例句：His meddling in other people's affairs caused a lot of trouble. 他干涉别人的事情引起了很多麻烦。|近义词：interfering, intrusive, officious|反义词:noninterfering, respectful, unintrusive|词性:adjective, noun

meddle:/ˈmɛdl/| v.|干涉，干预，管闲事|To interfere in or busy oneself unduly with something that is not one's concern.|例句：Don't meddle in my affairs. 不要干涉我的事情。|近义词：interfere, intrude, tamper|反义词: abstain, ignore, leave alone|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *medlen, from Old French medler, mesler (“to mix, mingle, meddle”), from Vulgar Latin *misculāre, from Latin miscēre (“to mix”).
