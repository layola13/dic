jollied:/ˈdʒɒlid/ (British) /ˈdʒɑːlid/ (American)| v. |哄，劝，使高兴，使快活|To encourage or persuade someone cheerfully, often in a slightly manipulative way. |He jollied her into going to the party with him. 他哄着她和他一起去参加了派对。|Synonyms: coaxed, persuaded, cajoled|Antonyms: discouraged, dissuaded|verb

jollied:/ˈdʒɒlid/ (British) /ˈdʒɑːlid/ (American)| adj. |高兴的，快活的|Feeling cheerful and happy, often due to being encouraged or persuaded.|She felt jollied after he complimented her dress.  在他称赞了她的裙子之后，她感到很高兴。|Synonyms: cheerful, happy, merry|Antonyms: sad, unhappy, gloomy|adjective
