disesteemer:/ˌdɪsɪˈstimər/ |n.|轻视者，蔑视者|Someone who holds another person or thing in low regard; someone who disesteems.  It conveys a sense of lacking respect or having a negative opinion of someone or something.|He was a disesteemer of modern art, preferring the classics.| 近义词: critic, detractor, scorner, despiser | 反义词: admirer, respecter, supporter |词性: 名词

词源:  "dis-" (prefix expressing negation) + "esteem" (from Old French "estimer," ultimately from Latin "aestimare" - to estimate, value).  The suffix "-er" denotes a person who performs the action of the verb.
