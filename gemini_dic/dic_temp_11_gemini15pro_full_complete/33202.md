chromotype: /ˈkroʊ.mə.taɪp/ | n. | 染色体型，染色体组型 | A chromotype is the chromosomal characteristic of an individual cell, or the entire chromosome complement of a species. |  Example 1: The chromotype of the cancer cells was abnormal. 癌细胞的染色体型异常。

Example 2:  Researchers are studying the chromotype of this newly discovered species. 研究人员正在研究这个新发现物种的染色体组型。 | 近义词: karyotype | 反义词:  None | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *chrōma* (color) + *typos* (type).  The term originally referred to the staining properties of chromosomes, which allowed for their visualization and classification.
