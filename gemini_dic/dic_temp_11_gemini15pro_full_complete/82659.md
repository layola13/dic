guerrilla:/ɡəˈrɪlə/ /ɡəˈrɪljə/| n. adj.|游击队员，游击战的|A member of a small independent group taking part in irregular fighting, typically against larger regular forces.  Also used to describe tactics involving small, mobile groups and surprise attacks.|The guerrillas ambushed the army convoy. 游击队员伏击了军队车队。|The group adopted guerrilla marketing tactics to promote their new product.  这个团体采用游击营销策略来推广他们的新产品。|synonyms: irregular, partisan, freedom fighter, rebel, insurgent|antonyms: soldier, regular, conventional|noun, adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *guerrilla*, diminutive of *guerra* "war," from Germanic origin, related to Old High German *werra* "confusion, strife."
