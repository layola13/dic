leakers:/ˈliːkərz/| n. |泄密者，泄露者|People who disclose confidential information. |The leakers were eventually identified and prosecuted. 泄密者最终被确认并被起诉。|The leakers remained anonymous. 泄密者仍然保持匿名。|Synonyms: whistleblowers, informants, gossipmongers|Antonyms: keepers of secrets, confidants|noun

leak:/liːk/| v. |泄露，渗漏|To allow secret information to become known. |The company leaked the news to the press. 公司向媒体泄露了这个消息。|The pipe is leaking water. 管道正在漏水。|Synonyms: divulge, disclose, reveal|Antonyms: conceal, hide, suppress|verb

leak:/liːk/| n. |泄露，漏洞|A disclosure of secret information. |There was a leak in the government. 政府内部发生了泄密事件。|The leak in the roof caused damage to the ceiling. 屋顶的漏洞导致天花板受损。|Synonyms: disclosure, revelation, divulgence|Antonyms: concealment, suppression|noun
