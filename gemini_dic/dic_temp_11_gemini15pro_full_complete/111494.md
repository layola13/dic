loaferish:/ˈloʊfərɪʃ/| adj.|懒散的，游手好闲的|Characteristic of a loafer; idle and lazy.  Spending time in an idle, lazy way.|He had a loaferish air about him, always leaning against something.|近义词：lazy, idle, indolent, shiftless|反义词：industrious, diligent, hard-working|词性:形容词
Etymology: From "loafer" (a person who avoids work) + "-ish" (suffix forming adjectives). "Loafer" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly related to Dutch "loopen" (to run) or German "laufen" (to run).
