half-willfully:/ˌhɑːfˈwɪlfʊli/ | adv. | 半故意地，有些故意地 | Done with a partial or limited intention; not entirely accidental but not fully deliberate.  It suggests a conscious choice to ignore or downplay certain factors, leading to an outcome that could have been prevented with greater attention or effort. | He half-willfully ignored the warning signs and continued down the dangerous path. 他有些故意地忽略了警告标志，继续沿着危险的道路走下去。 |  He half-willfully misheard her instructions, preferring to do things his own way. 他有点故意地听错了她的指示，宁愿按自己的方式做事。 | Synonyms:  partly deliberately, semi-intentionally, quasi-deliberately | Antonyms: unintentionally, accidentally, involuntarily | Part of Speech: Adverb
