jayhawker:/ˈdʒeɪˌhɔːkər/| n.| 杰霍克人，游击队员，强盗 |A native of Kansas; also, a supporter of the Free State cause in Kansas before the Civil War, or a Union soldier from Kansas in the Civil War, sometimes applied derogatorily to any Kansan.  Originally, a term for a band of guerilla fighters in Kansas and Missouri during the Bleeding Kansas era.|  His grandfather was a Jayhawker who fought for the Union. 他的祖父是一位为联邦作战的杰霍克人。| synonyms: Kansan, abolitionist, guerrilla, raider | antonyms: Confederate, pro-slavery | noun


jayhawker:/ˈdʒeɪˌhɔːkər/| n.| 掠夺者，强盗 |Someone who robs or plunders.| The Jayhawkers raided the Confederate supply train.  这些杰霍克人袭击了同盟军的补给列车。| synonyms: bandit, robber, looter, plunderer | antonyms: lawman, protector | noun
