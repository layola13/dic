hoodwink:/hʊdˈwɪŋk/
verb
欺骗，蒙蔽
to deceive or trick someone
例句：The salesman tried to hoodwink us into buying a used car.  推销员试图欺骗我们买一辆二手车。

例句：They were hoodwinked into believing they had won a prize. 他们被骗了，以为自己赢得了奖品。

近义词：deceive, trick, dupe, swindle, cheat, mislead
反义词：enlighten, inform, be honest
词性：verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hodwynke*, from *hod* (hood) + *wynke* (wink).  Originally referred to covering someone's eyes with a hood to blind them.  The meaning later extended to any kind of deception.
