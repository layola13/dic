interpel:/ˌɪntərˈpel/| vt.|质询，诘问，(正式地)要求…解释|To question (someone, such as a public official) formally or severely. To interrupt the order of business of (a legislative body) for the purpose of demanding an explanation. |The journalist interpelled the minister about the government's new policy. 记者就政府的新政策质询了部长。|synonyms: question, challenge, interrogate, examine, inquire|antonyms: answer, reply, respond|verb


interpel:/ˌɪntərˈpel/| vt.|打断，打扰|(Especially of a group of people) to stop someone from continuing what they are saying or doing by demanding an explanation or complaining loudly.  |Protesters interpelled the speaker, shouting slogans and waving banners. 抗议者打断演讲者，高喊口号并挥舞旗帜。|synonyms: interrupt, disturb, disrupt, heckle|antonyms: allow, permit, facilitate|verb
