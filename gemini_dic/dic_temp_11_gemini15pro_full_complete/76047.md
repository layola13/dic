furloughed:/ˈfɜːrloʊd/ (US) /ˈfɜːləʊd/ (UK)| adj. v. (past participle)|休假的，暂时解雇的|Temporarily laid off from work, especially due to economic conditions, with the expectation of being recalled later.|例句：Many workers were furloughed during the pandemic. 许多工人在疫情期间被暂时解雇了。

例句：He was furloughed from his job for three months. 他被暂时解雇了三个月。|近义词：laid off, suspended, temporarily dismissed|反义词：employed, hired, working|词性: adjective, past participle of verb 'furlough'


furlough:/ˈfɜːrloʊ/ (US) /ˈfɜːləʊ/ (UK)| n. v.|休假，暂时解雇|A temporary leave of absence from work, often due to economic reasons, with the possibility of returning later; to grant a furlough.|例句：The company offered its employees voluntary furloughs. 公司向员工提供自愿休假。

例句：She was furloughed for six weeks. 她被暂时解雇了六周。|近义词：leave of absence, layoff, suspension|反义词：employment, hiring|词性: noun, verb
