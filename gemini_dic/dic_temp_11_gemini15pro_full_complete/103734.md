jostled:/ˈdʒɒsəld/ (UK) /ˈdʒɑːsəld/ (US)| v. |推搡；挤；争夺；|To bump against or push roughly, typically in a crowd.|The passengers jostled to get off the train. 乘客们互相推搡着下火车。|例句：The photographer was jostled by the crowd as he tried to get a good shot. 摄影师试图拍一张好照片时被人群推搡。|近义词：shove, push, elbow, bump, crowd, scramble|反义词：yield, give way|verb

jostle:/ˈdʒɒsəl/ (UK) /ˈdʒɑːsəl/ (US)| n. |推挤；拥挤；竞争；|The act of jostling; a struggle or competition.|There was a jostle for position at the front of the stage. 舞台前排位置竞争激烈。|例句：The jostle of the crowd made it difficult to move. 人群的推搡使得移动困难。|近义词：shoving, pushing, crowding, scrambling|反义词：order, calmness|noun
