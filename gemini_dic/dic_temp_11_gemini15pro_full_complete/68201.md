fair-trade:/ˌfer ˈtreɪd/ | adj. n. |公平交易的；公平贸易|A way of buying and selling products that makes certain that the original producer receives a fair price.  It also often means that the product has been produced in an ethical way, for example without exploiting the workforce or damaging the environment. |  例句：She always buys fair-trade coffee. 她总是买公平贸易咖啡。

例句：Fair-trade practices help farmers in developing countries. 公平贸易措施帮助发展中国家的农民。 | 近义词：ethical, responsible, sustainable | 反义词：exploitative, unethical | 词性：形容词, 名词

fair trade:/ˌfer ˈtreɪd/ | n. |公平贸易|The system of buying and selling fair-trade products. | 例句：The fair trade movement is growing rapidly. 公平贸易运动正在迅速发展。

例句：Many consumers are now choosing to support fair trade. 许多消费者现在选择支持公平贸易。 | 近义词：ethical trade, responsible trade | 反义词：free trade (in some contexts), exploitative trade | 词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
"fair" (Old English fæger "beautiful, pleasing; fitting, proper; gentle, peaceful") + "trade" (Middle English, from Old Norse  "troð" meaning "track, footstep, course") The compound "fair trade" emerged in the mid-20th century, reflecting a growing awareness of the need for equitable trading practices.
