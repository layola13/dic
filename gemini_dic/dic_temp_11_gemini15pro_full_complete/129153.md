no-man's-land: /ˈnoʊˌmænzˌlænd/ | n. |无人区；危险地带；真空地带| An area of land between two opposing armies that is not controlled by either.  A situation or subject that is not clearly defined or controlled by anyone. |  The treaty left a large swathe of territory as no-man's-land between the two countries.  条约使得两国之间的大片领土成为无人区。 The project fell into a no-man's-land between different departments, and no one took responsibility for it.  这个项目落入了不同部门之间的真空地带，没有人对其负责。| 近义词: wasteland, wilderness, buffer zone | 反义词: safe zone, occupied territory | 词性: 名词


Etymology:  From the literal sense of land belonging to no man, dating back to the 14th century.  The military sense, referring to the area between trenches, became prominent during World War I.
