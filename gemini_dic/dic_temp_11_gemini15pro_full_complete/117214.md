Etymology: From Middle English *meten*, from Old English *mǣtan* (“to dream”), from Proto-Germanic *maitōną* (“to cut, measure, dream”), from Proto-Indo-European *méh₁tis* (“thought, consideration”).  Cognate with Old High German *meizan* (“to dream”), Old Norse *meyta* (“to linger, delay”), Gothic *𐌼𐌰𐌹𐍄𐌰𐌽* (maitan, “to cut”).  The modern English word "meat" is also distantly related.
