camp:/kæmp/| n. vi. vt.|营地，露营，阵营|A place where people stay in tents or other temporary structures, usually for recreation or military purposes.  A group of people with shared interests or beliefs, especially in politics.|We set up camp by the river. 我们在河边扎营。|近义词：encampment, campsite, bivouac|反义词：home, residence|词性:n. vi. vt.
camp:/kæmp/| vi. vt.|露营，扎营，驻扎|To stay in a tent or other temporary structure, usually for recreation or military purposes. To establish a camp.|We camped in the mountains for a week. 我们在山里露营了一周。|近义词：encamp, bivouac, pitch a tent|反义词：depart, leave|词性:vi. vt.
campy:/ˈkæmpi/| adj.|夸张的，做作的|Exaggerated, theatrical, and often intentionally humorous.|The movie was a campy take on classic horror films. 这部电影是对经典恐怖电影的夸张演绎。|近义词：theatrical, exaggerated, affected|反义词：natural, understated, genuine|词性:adj.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "camp" (noun) and "camper" (verb), from Latin "campus" meaning "field, plain".
