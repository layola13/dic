grotesquery:/ɡroʊˈtɛskəri/ | n. |怪诞，奇异，怪诞的图案|Something comically or repulsively ugly or distorted. |The gargoyles on the old church were a collection of grotesqueries.  教堂上的滴水嘴兽是一堆怪诞的雕塑。|Synonyms: absurdity, bizarreness, caricature, oddity, monstrosity |Antonyms: beauty, normality, loveliness | Noun

grotesque:/ɡroʊˈtɛsk/ | adj. |怪诞的，奇异的|Comically or repulsively ugly or distorted. |The melting clock in the painting was a grotesque image.  画中融化的时钟是一个怪诞的形象。|Synonyms: bizarre, distorted, freakish, outlandish |Antonyms: beautiful, normal, ordinary | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *grotesque*, from Italian *grottesco* "of a cave," from *grotta* "cave" (from Late Latin *grupta*, variant of Latin *crypta* "crypt").  So called from the ancient paintings and decorations found in the caves or *grottoes* of Roman ruins, which were considered bizarre and fantastic.
