dry land:/ˌdraɪ ˈlænd/ | n. |旱地；陆地|Land that is not covered by water.  |Example: The shipwrecked sailors were grateful to finally reach dry land.  遇难的水手们终于到达陆地，感激不尽。

Example:  Amphibians can live both in water and on dry land. 两栖动物既能生活在水中，也能生活在陆地上。|近义词：land, terra firma, shore |反义词: water, sea, ocean |词性: noun

Etymology: From Old English *drīgland (unattested), from drīg "dry" + land "land".  The word "dry" comes from Proto-Germanic *drugaz, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰrewgʰ- "dry". The word "land" comes from Proto-Germanic *landą, from Proto-Indo-European *lendʰ- "land, heath".
