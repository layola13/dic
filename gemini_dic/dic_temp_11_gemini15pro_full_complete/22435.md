bookends:/ˈbʊkɛndz/
n.
书挡；结束；
A pair of supports placed at the ends of a row of books to hold them upright. Also used figuratively to refer to events or items that mark the beginning and end of something.
The antique bookends, shaped like lions, added a touch of elegance to the bookshelf. 这对古董书挡，形状像狮子，为书架增添了一丝优雅。
The birth of her first child and the death of her husband bookended a period of great joy and sorrow in her life. 她第一个孩子的出生和丈夫的去世标志着她生命中一段充满喜悦和悲伤的时期的开始和结束。
近义词:supports, brackets
反义词:None
词性:noun


bookend:/ˈbʊkɛnd/
vt.
用书挡夹住；构成…的开头和结尾
To enclose or flank with bookends; to mark the beginning and end of something.
The photographs bookended the chapter, providing a visual representation of the time period. 这些照片构成了这一章的开头和结尾，提供了该时期的一种视觉呈现。
Her career was bookended by two successful Broadway shows.  她的职业生涯以两场成功的百老汇演出开始和结束。
近义词:bracket, enclose, flank
反义词:None
词性:verb

词源:  Compound of "book" and "end".  First recorded in the early 20th century.
