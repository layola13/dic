lingan: /ˈlɪŋɡən/ | n. | 林岸; 岸边; 港口;  | A place on the shore of a body of water, especially a harbor or landing place.  Often used in place names of Scandinavian origin. |  The ships docked at the lingan. 船停靠在港口。| Synonyms: harbor, port, wharf, quay | Antonyms: inland, interior | noun

Etymology: From Old Norse *lynginn, meaning "heath" or "moor," likely referring to a landing place by a heather-covered shore.
