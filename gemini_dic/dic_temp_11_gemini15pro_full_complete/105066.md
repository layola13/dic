kerber:/ˈkɜːrbər/ | n. | Kerberos;地狱三头犬 |  Kerberos is a computer network authentication protocol that works based on tickets to allow nodes communicating over a non-secure network to prove their identity to one another in a secure manner.  Also, in Greek mythology, Cerberus, often called Kerberos, is a multi-headed dog that guards the gates of the Underworld to prevent the dead from leaving. | Example 1: The network uses Kerberos for authentication.  网络使用Kerberos进行身份验证。

Example 2:  In the myth, Heracles' final labor was to capture Kerberos.  神话中，赫拉克勒斯的最后一个任务是捕获地狱三头犬。 | Synonym: (for the mythological creature) Cerberus, hellhound | Antonym:  (no direct antonym for either meaning) | noun
