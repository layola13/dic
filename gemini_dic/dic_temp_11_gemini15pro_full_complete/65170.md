ethnographically:/ˌeθnəˈɡræfɪkli/
adv.
人种志地，民族志地
in a way that relates to ethnography (the study of human cultures and customs)
The community was studied ethnographically to understand their traditions.  这个社区被人种志地研究以了解他们的传统。
The researcher ethnographically documented the rituals of the tribe. 研究人员以人种学的方式记录了该部落的仪式。
近义词: culturally, anthropologically
反义词: statistically, quantitatively
词性: 副词

词源:  From ethnography + -ally.  Ethnography comes from the Greek 'ethnos' (nation, people) + 'graphein' (to write).
