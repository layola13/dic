balsas:/ˈbɑːlsəs/ | n. | 木筏，轻木筏 | A raft made of bundles of reeds or lightweight wood, typically used in South America. | The indigenous people used balsas to navigate the river. 原住民使用木筏在河上航行。 |  The fishermen used a balsa to reach their nets. 渔民们用木筏去够他们的网。 | raft, float | boat, ship | noun

Etymology: Spanish *balsa* "raft," from Quechua *walsa*.
