fluxions:/ˈflʌkʃənz/
noun
流动；变化率；通量
The continuous change of a variable quantity.  Historically, in calculus, a fluxion was the instantaneous rate of change (the derivative) of a fluent (a time-varying quantity).
Newton's method of fluxions provided a way to calculate the area under a curve. 牛顿的流数法提供了一种计算曲线下面积的方法。

The fluxions of the stock market make it a risky investment. 股票市场的波动使其成为一项高风险投资。

Synonym: variations, changes, fluctuations, derivatives
Antonym: constants, stability, stagnation
