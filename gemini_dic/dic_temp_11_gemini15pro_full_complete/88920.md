hold-up:/ˈhəʊldʌp/ (UK) /ˈhoʊldʌp/ (US)| n.|抢劫; 阻碍; 延误|A robbery at gunpoint, or something that causes a delay.|例句：The roadworks caused a major hold-up on the highway. 道路施工导致高速公路严重堵塞。|近义词：delay, robbery, heist, stick-up|反义词:advancement, progress|noun

hold-up:/ˈhəʊldʌp/ (UK) /ˈhoʊldʌp/ (US)| n.|支持物; 支撑物|(less common) Something that supports something else.|例句：The old beams acted as a hold-up for the crumbling ceiling.  旧横梁支撑着摇摇欲坠的天花板。|近义词：support, prop|反义词:hindrance|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hold" + "up," meaning to "hold" something in a stationary or delayed position.  The sense of "robbery" likely evolved from the idea of forcing someone to stop and be "held up."
