mucksy: /ˈmʌksi/ | adj. | 肮脏的，污秽的；令人厌恶的 | Dirty, filthy, or disgusting. Often used in dialects, especially British English. | The stable was mucksy and smelled awful. 马厩里又脏又臭。 | 近义词：dirty, filthy, grimy, messy, squalid | 反义词：clean, tidy, pristine | 词性：形容词

词源：Likely a variant of "mucky," which is probably derived from "muck" (animal dung) + "-y" (suffix forming adjectives).
