geomalism: /ˈdʒiːoʊˌmeɪlɪzəm/
n.|地质主义; 地理决定论|The belief that geographical factors, such as climate and terrain, significantly influence the development of societies and cultures.|Example: Geomalism suggests that island nations are more likely to develop seafaring traditions.|Synonyms: environmental determinism, geographical determinism|Antonyms: possibilism|Noun
