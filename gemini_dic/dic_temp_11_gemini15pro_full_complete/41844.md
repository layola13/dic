corybantiasm:/ˌkɔːrɪˈbæntiæzəm/ | n. | 狂乱症，精神狂乱 | A frenzied, ecstatic, and orgiastic dance, originally performed by priests of Cybele.  It is characterized by wild, uncontrolled movements and often accompanied by loud music and shouting.  |  His corybantiasm during the concert was disturbing to those around him. 他在音乐会上的狂乱行为让周围的人感到不安。 |  frenzy, mania, delirium  |  calm, lucidity, tranquility | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *corybantiasmus*, from Ancient Greek *κορυβαντισμός* (korubantismos), from *Κορύβας* (Korubas), a Corybant, a mythical Phrygian dancer. Ultimately from Pre-Greek.
