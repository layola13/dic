fawningly:/ˈfɔːnɪŋli/ | adv. | 谄媚地，奉承地 | In a way that shows excessive admiration or flattery, often in order to gain favor. | He fawned over the boss fawningly, hoping for a promotion. 他谄媚地奉承老板，希望得到晋升。|  Example: The assistant fawningly agreed with everything the CEO said. 助理谄媚地同意CEO所说的一切。|Synonyms: obsequiously, servilely, sycophantically | Antonyms: independently, critically, frankly | 词性: 副词

fawn:/fɔːn/ | v. | 1. (of a deer) give birth to a fawn. 2. (of a person) show affection or try to please someone, typically in order to gain favor. |  Origin: Middle English: from Old French faon ‘young deer’, related to Latin fetus ‘offspring’. The verb senses date from the late 16th century. | The dog fawns on its owner. 狗向它的主人撒娇。 He fawned over the wealthy investors. 他对那些富有的投资者阿谀奉承。 | Synonyms: grovel, flatter, toady | Antonyms: criticize, challenge, ignore | 词性: 动词
