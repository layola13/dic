antiseptic:/ˌæn.tɪˈsep.tɪk/ /ˌæn.t̬ɪˈsep.tɪk/| adj. n.|抗菌的，防腐的；防腐剂，消毒剂|A substance that inhibits the growth and reproduction of microorganisms, used to prevent infection.|The doctor applied an antiseptic to the wound. 医生在伤口上涂了防腐剂。|Synonyms: disinfectant, germicide, antibacterial|Antonyms: septic, infected|adjective, noun


antiseptics:/ˌæn.tɪˈsep.tɪks/ /ˌæn.t̬ɪˈsep.tɪks/| n.|防腐剂，消毒剂|Substances that inhibit the growth and reproduction of microorganisms, used to prevent infection.  This is the plural form of "antiseptic".|The nurse brought a tray of antiseptics. 护士拿来一盘消毒剂。|Synonyms: disinfectants, germicides, antibacterials|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for the plural noun, but the opposite concept would be substances that promote infection.)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *antiseptique*, from Ancient Greek *ἀντί* (*anti*, “against”) + *σηπτικός* (*sēptikos*, “putrefying”).
