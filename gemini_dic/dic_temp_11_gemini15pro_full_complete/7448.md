anisodactyla:/ˌænɪsoʊˈdæktɪlə/ | n. |  异趾类 |A suborder of artiodactyl mammals characterized by having an even number of toes, with the weight borne equally by the third and fourth toes.  This group includes ruminants like cattle, sheep, deer, and goats, as well as hippos and camels.|  Cows and sheep belong to the suborder Anisodactyla. 牛和羊属于异趾类。|  Synonyms: Artiodactyla (sometimes used interchangeably, though Artiodactyla is the broader order), even-toed ungulates | Antonyms: Perissodactyla (odd-toed ungulates) | noun
