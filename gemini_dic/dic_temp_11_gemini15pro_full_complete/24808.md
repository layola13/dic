brouded:/ˈbruːdɪd/ | adj. | 沉思的，忧郁的，闷闷不乐的 | Deep in thought; showing concentration or sadness; brooding; appearing darkly menacing or suggestive of evil. |  He sat alone, a brouded figure lost in thought. 他独自坐着，一个沉思的身影陷入了沉思。| synonym: pensive, thoughtful, meditative, gloomy, sullen | antonym: cheerful, lighthearted, jovial | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *brouden*, from Old English *brōdan* "to breed, cherish, keep warm," from Proto-Germanic *brodjan*, from Proto-Indo-European *bhreu- "to boil, bubble, effervesce, burn."  The sense shifted from "to cherish (an emotion)" to "to ponder moodily."
