complacently:/kəmˈpleɪsntlɪ/ | adv. | 自满地，沾沾自喜地 |  In a self-satisfied way, often unaware of potential dangers or deficiencies. | He smiled complacently, clearly satisfied with his performance. 他自满地笑着，显然对自己的表现很满意。|  She complacently assumed she would get the job. 她沾沾自喜地认为自己会得到这份工作。 | 近义词: smugly, self-satisfactorily | 反义词: anxiously, apprehensively | 词性: 副词

complacent:/kəmˈpleɪsnt/ | adj. | 自满的，沾沾自喜的 | Showing smug or uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements. |  He had become complacent after years of success.  多年的成功让他变得自满了。 | A complacent attitude towards safety regulations can lead to accidents. 对安全规章制度的自满态度会导致事故发生。 | 近义词: smug, self-satisfied | 反义词: anxious, concerned | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Late Latin complacent-, from Latin complacere ‘be very pleasing’, from com- (intensive) + placere ‘please’.
