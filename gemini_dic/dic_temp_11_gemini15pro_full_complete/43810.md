crenkle:/ˈkrɪŋkəl/| v. n.|起皱，皱纹，沙沙作响|To form small wrinkles or creases; a small wrinkle or crease.  The cellophane wrapper made a satisfying crenkle as she opened the candy.|例句：The leaves crenkle and dry in the sun. 叶子在阳光下皱缩变干。|例句：The old map was covered in crenkles. 这张旧地图上满是皱纹。|近义词：wrinkle, crinkle, rumple, crumple|反义词：smooth, flatten|词性：动词，名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crinkled, frequentative of *crinken, from Old English *crincan “to bend, twist, curl,” of Germanic origin. Related to cringe.
