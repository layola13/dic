birban:/ˈbɜːrbən/ | n. | 暴徒，恶棍，流氓 | A rough, lawless person; a hooligan or villain. | He was known as a birban, always getting into fights. 他被称为暴徒，总是惹是生非。 |  He acted like a birban, smashing windows and yelling obscenities.  他表现得像个恶棍，砸碎窗户，大喊脏话。|Synonyms: ruffian, thug, hoodlum, delinquent, scoundrel | Antonyms: gentleman, law-abider, citizen | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Urdu بربن (barban), from Persian بربر (barbar), ultimately from Greek βάρβαρος (bárbaros, “foreign, non-Greek; rude, uncivilized”). Originally referring to non-Greeks, later broadened to mean "barbarian."
