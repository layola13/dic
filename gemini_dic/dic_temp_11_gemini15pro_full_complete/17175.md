battue: /bæˈtuː/ | n. | 围猎，集体搜捕，(尤指)集体打猎 | An organized hunt or slaughter of animals, especially a large-scale hunt in which animals are driven towards hunters waiting in positions to shoot them. |  The king enjoyed the annual battue of deer in the royal forest. 国王喜欢在皇家森林里一年一度的集体猎鹿活动。

The battue resulted in the deaths of hundreds of pheasants. 这次围猎导致数百只野鸡死亡。

| 近义词：hunt, slaughter, massacre, drive | 反义词: conservation, preservation | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *battue*, past participle of *battre* "to beat," from Latin *battuere* "to beat, strike, fight."  The sense of "organized hunt" arose from the practice of beating bushes and undergrowth to drive animals out into the open.
