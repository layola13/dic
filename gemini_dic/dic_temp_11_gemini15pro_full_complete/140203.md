officerly:/ˈɒfɪsərli/ | adj. | 官员般的；有官员风度的；像官员的 | Resembling or characteristic of an officer, especially in being authoritative or formal. | He spoke in an officerly tone.  他以一种官员般的口吻说话。|  authoritative, formal, commanding, dignified | informal, casual, relaxed | adjective

Etymology:
From officer + -ly.  "Officer" comes from Old French "officier," ultimately from Latin "officium" meaning "duty, service."  The suffix "-ly" is used to form adjectives and adverbs, often indicating manner or resemblance.
