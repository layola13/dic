limey: /ˈlaɪmi/ | n. |英国佬（贬义）| A derogatory slang term for a British person, especially a British sailor.  The term originated in the 19th century, referring to the practice of giving British sailors limes to prevent scurvy. | Example 1: "The old limey told tales of his adventures at sea."  那个老英国佬讲述了他海上冒险的故事。

Example 2:  "Don't call him a limey; it's offensive."  别叫他英国佬，这是冒犯性的。 | Synonym: Brit, Englishman (less offensive), Britisher | Antonym: (No direct antonym, as it's a nationality-based slur) | noun
