fieldfare:/ˈfiːldˌfeər/ | n. | 田鸫 | A large thrush, Turdus pilaris, that breeds in northern Eurasia and winters farther south, often in large flocks. It is brownish above and has a grey head and rump and an orange breast streaked with black. | Example: A flock of fieldfares descended on the berry bushes. 一群田鸫飞落到浆果丛上。

Example: The fieldfare's song is a harsh, chattering call. 田鸫的歌声是一种刺耳的，喋喋不休的叫声。 | 近义词: thrush, redwing | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for a specific bird species) | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *feldeware, from feld "field" + ware "dweller".  Literally, "field-dweller".
