indented:/ɪnˈdɛntɪd/ | adj. | 锯齿状的，缩进的，受契约约束的 |  Having an outline or edge marked by notches or teeth; set in or back from a margin. | The leaf has a deeply indented edge. 这片叶子的边缘呈锯齿状。| synonyms: notched, serrated, jagged, uneven, recessed | antonyms: smooth, even, flat | adjective

indented:/ɪnˈdɛntɪd/ | adj. | 缩进的 |  Started further in from the margin than the rest of the text. | The first line of each paragraph should be indented. 每段的第一行都应该缩进。| synonyms: recessed, set back | antonyms: protruding, flush | adjective

indented:/ɪnˈdɛntɪd/ | adj. | 受契约约束的 (主要用于英式英语) | Bound by indentures as an apprentice or servant. (chiefly British) |  He was indented to a carpenter for seven years. 他与一位木匠签订了七年的学徒契约。| synonyms: bound, contracted, apprenticed | antonyms: free, unbound | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *endenten*, from Old French *endentée* (“notched, toothed”), feminine past participle of *endenter* (“to notch, tooth”), from Latin *indentāre* (“to furnish with teeth”), from *in-* + *dens*, *dent-* (“tooth”).
