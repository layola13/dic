angina: /ænˈdʒaɪnə/ /ænˈdʒiːnə/ | n. | 心绞痛 | A condition of severe pain in the chest caused by an inadequate blood supply to the heart. |  He was rushed to the hospital complaining of angina. 他因心绞痛被紧急送往医院。

| Example: The doctor diagnosed his chest pain as angina. 医生诊断他的胸痛为心绞痛。

| Synonyms: chest pain, heart pain, cardiac pain | Antonyms:  (Angina doesn't have a direct antonym.  The opposite of having angina would be having a healthy heart and good blood flow, but there isn't one single word for that.) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *angina, from Latin angere "to strangle, choke."  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *angh- "tight, painful."
