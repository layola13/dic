landscaped: /ˈlændskeɪpt/ | adj. | 美化的，园林绿化的 |  Improved in appearance by landscape gardening.  |  Example: We have a beautifully landscaped garden. 我们有一个美丽的园林式花园。 | Example: The hotel features a landscaped pool area.  这家酒店拥有一个园林式泳池区。|  Synonyms:  gardened, cultivated, manicured | Antonyms:  barren, overgrown, wild | Adjective


land·scape: /ˈlændskeɪp/ | n. | 风景，景色；地形；山水画 | All the visible features of an area of countryside or land, often considered in terms of their aesthetic appeal. | Example:  The landscape is dotted with small villages.  这片土地上点缀着一些小村庄。 | Example:  She painted a stunning landscape of the Tuscan countryside.  她画了一幅托斯卡纳乡村的迷人风景画。 | Synonyms: scenery, view, vista, panorama | Antonyms: cityscape | Noun

land·scape: /ˈlændskeɪp/ | vt. | 进行景观美化；美化环境 | To improve the aesthetic appearance of (a piece of land) by changing its contours, adding ornamental features, or planting trees and shrubs. | Example:  They landscaped the front yard with flowers and shrubs. 他们在前院种植了鲜花和灌木，使其更加美观。 | Example:  The city is planning to landscape the park.  该市正计划美化公园。 | Synonyms:  garden, cultivate, beautify | Antonyms: neglect, spoil, ruin | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Dutch word "landschap," meaning "region, tract of land," composed of "land" (land) and "-schap" (ship, condition, quality).  The term originally referred to a painting depicting scenery, and later expanded to encompass the actual scenery itself, and then the act of modifying that scenery.
