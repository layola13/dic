counter-etch:/ˈkaʊntərˌɛtʃ/ |n.|反蚀刻；反腐蚀|A process used in printmaking, particularly etching, to reduce the printing area slightly by applying a weak acid or mordant to areas that should print white or a lighter tone than the surrounding areas. This creates a subtle relief and helps prevent unwanted ink transfer.|The artist used a counter-etch to refine the details in the sky of his print.|Synonym: stop-out (partially)|Antonym: etching|Noun

counter-etch:/ˈkaʊntərˌɛtʃ/ |v.|反蚀刻；反腐蚀|To apply a counter-etch to a printing plate.|The printer counter-etched the plate to clean up the edges of the image.|Synonym: stop-out (partially)|Antonym: etch|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
"counter-" (prefix meaning "against, opposite") + "etch" (from Dutch "etsen," meaning "to eat")
