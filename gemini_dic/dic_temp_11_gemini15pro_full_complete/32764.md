chocard:/ˈʃoʊkɑːrd/ | n. |红嘴山鸦|A crow-like bird ( *Pyrrhocorax graculus*) found in high mountains of Europe and Asia, having glossy black plumage, a red bill, and red legs. | We saw a flock of chocards circling the mountain peak. 我们看到一群红嘴山鸦在山峰上盘旋。|Synonym: Alpine chough|Antonym: N/A (No direct antonym for a specific bird species)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *chocard*,  of uncertain origin, perhaps from Provençal *caucart*, from *cau* (jackdaw) + *cart* (black). Ultimately from pre-Germanic.
