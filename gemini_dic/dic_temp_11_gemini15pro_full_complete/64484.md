ernest:/ˈɜːrnɪst/| adj.|认真的，诚挚的，严肃的|Serious and sincere; not lighthearted or playful.|He had an earnest expression on his face. 他臉上帶著認真的表情。|Synonyms: serious, sincere, solemn, grave, sober|Antonyms: frivolous, flippant, lighthearted|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *ernest, from Old English *eornost (“seriousness, zeal, earnestness”), from Proto-Germanic *arnustaz (“seriousness, struggle, battle”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂erh₁- (“to struggle, fight”). Cognate with Dutch ernst (“seriousness”), German Ernst (“seriousness, earnestness”), Old Norse ern (“battle”), Gothic 𐌰𐍂𐌽𐍃 (arns, “a lamb”). More at earn.
