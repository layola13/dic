bustler:/ˈbʌslər/| n.| 忙碌的人，奔忙的人；积极的人|A person who moves with great energy and speed; a busy, energetic person.|  The market was full of shoppers and bustlers.  市场上挤满了购物者和忙碌的人。|Synonyms: hustler, go-getter, live wire|Antonyms: loafer, idler|noun


bustle:/ˈbʌs(ə)l/| v. | 喧闹；忙碌；催促|To move with great energy and speed; to be busy and active.|She bustled around the kitchen, preparing dinner. 她忙着在厨房里准备晚餐。|Synonyms: hurry, rush, dash|Antonyms: dawdle, linger|verb


bustle:/ˈbʌs(ə)l/| n. | 喧闹；忙碌；裙撑|Excited activity and movement; a frame worn under a skirt to puff it out.|The bustle of city life can be exciting. 城市生活的喧嚣令人兴奋。|Synonyms: activity, commotion, flurry|Antonyms: quiet, stillness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bustlen*, frequentative of *busken* (“to prepare, make ready”), of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Old Norse *búask* (“to get ready, dress oneself”).
