mised: /maɪzd/ | v. (past tense of mise) | 放置，安排；舞台调度；舞台演出 | To place, arrange, or stage something, especially in a theatrical production.  "The director carefully mised the scene before filming." |导演在拍摄前仔细地布置了场景。| 近义词: staged, arranged, set, placed | 反义词: disordered, disarranged | 词性: 动词

Etymology: From Middle English *misen, from Old French mis, past participle of mettre (“to put, place”), from Latin mittere (“to send, let go”).  Related to mission, missile, and remit.

Note: The word "mised" is archaic and rarely used in modern English. "Stage," "arrange," "set," or "place" are more common alternatives.  The term "mise-en-scène" is more commonly encountered and refers to the overall visual aesthetic of a stage or film production.
