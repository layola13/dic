broadside:/ˈbrɔːdsaɪd/ (US), /ˈbrɔːdsaɪd/ (UK)| n. adj. adv.|舷侧齐射，猛烈抨击，单页印刷品|A simultaneous discharge of all the guns on one side of a warship.  A strong verbal attack. A large sheet of paper printed on one side only, formerly used for proclamations or advertisements.|The ship unleashed a devastating broadside on the enemy. 这艘船向敌人发起了毁灭性的舷侧齐射。|近义词：volley, barrage, attack, denunciation, onslaught|反义词:defense, praise, commendation|词性:noun, adjective, adverb

broadside:/ˈbrɔːdsaɪd/ (US), /ˈbrɔːdsaɪd/ (UK)| adj.|横向的，侧面的|Positioned sideways; broad; extending from side to side.|The car slid broadside into the wall. 汽车侧滑撞到了墙上。|近义词：sideways, lateral|反义词:head-on, lengthwise|词性:adjective

broadside:/ˈbrɔːdsaɪd/ (US), /ˈbrɔːdsaɪd/ (UK)| adv.|侧着，横着|With one side facing forward; sideways.|The truck hit the car broadside. 卡车侧面撞上了汽车。|近义词：sideways, laterally|反义词:head-on|词性:adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "broad" (wide) + "side" (side of a ship). Originally referred to the simultaneous firing of all guns on one side of a warship.  Later extended to mean any widespread attack or critique.
