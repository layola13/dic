convocative: /kənˈvɒkətɪv/ (UK) /kənˈvɑːkətɪv/ (US)
adjective
召集性的；召唤的；令人想起的
tending or serving to convoke; evocative
例句：The president's speech was convocative, bringing together leaders from around the world.  总统的演讲具有召集性，汇聚了来自世界各地的领导人。

例句：The museum's exhibit was convocative of a bygone era. 博物馆的展览令人回想起过去的时代。

近义词：summoning, evocative, reminiscent, redolent
反义词：dispersing, unexciting, forgettable
词性：adjective


convocation: /ˌkɒnvəˈkeɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌkɑːnvəˈkeɪʃn/ (US)
noun
召集；集会；会议
the act or process of convoking; a group of people convoked
例句：The university convocation was held in the grand hall. 大学的集会在大厅举行。

例句：The king issued a convocation of his advisors. 国王召集了他的顾问们开会。
近义词：assembly, gathering, meeting, summons
反义词：dispersal, scattering
词性：noun


convoke: /kənˈvəʊk/ (UK) /kənˈvoʊk/ (US)
verb
召集；召开
to call together or summon, especially for a formal meeting
例句：The chairman convoked a meeting of the board of directors. 董事长召集了董事会会议。

例句：The parliament was convoked to discuss the new law. 议会召开会议讨论新法律。
近义词：summon, convene, assemble, gather
反义词：dismiss, disperse, scatter
词性：verb
