bluffing:/ˈblʌfɪŋ/ | n. | 虚张声势，吓唬 | The act of deceiving someone by pretending to have more strength, knowledge, or resources than one actually possesses. | He won the game by bluffing. 他靠虚张声势赢了比赛。 |  near: feigning, pretending, posturing | antonym: honesty, truthfulness | noun

bluff:/blʌf/ | v. | 虚张声势，吓唬 | To deceive someone by pretending to have more strength, knowledge, or resources than one actually possesses. | She bluffed her way through the interview. 她靠虚张声势蒙混过关了面试。|  near: feign, pretend, posture | antonym: be honest, tell the truth | verb

bluff:/blʌf/ | adj. | 直率的，陡峭的 | Direct and outspoken, often in a rough or hearty way; rising steeply with a broad, flat front. | He had a bluff and hearty manner. 他态度直率而热情。  The ship sailed past a bluff coastline. 船驶过一段陡峭的海岸线。| near: frank, outspoken, abrupt, steep, sheer | antonym: subtle, indirect, gentle, sloping | adjective
