commorient:/kəˈmɔːriənt/
adjective
同时死亡的
Dying at the same time; dying together.  Specifically in legal contexts, referring to the simultaneous deaths of two or more persons, especially where the order of their deaths cannot be established and has legal implications, such as inheritance.
The couple were commorient in the car crash, making inheritance a complex issue. 这对夫妇在车祸中同时死亡，使得遗产继承成为一个复杂的问题。

commorient:/kəˈmɔːriənt/
noun
同时死亡的人
A person who dies at the same time as another.
The will did not account for the possibility of the testator and beneficiary being commorients.  这份遗嘱没有考虑到立遗嘱人和受益人同时死亡的可能性。

commorient:/kəˈmɔːriənt/
adverb (Rare)
同时死亡地
(Rarely used) Dying at the same time.
They perished commorient in the avalanche. 他们在雪崩中同时遇难。


Etymology:  From Latin *commorient-, from com- 'together' + morient- 'dying', present participle of morī 'to die'.

Synonyms:  codeceased, simultaneously deceased

Antonyms:  surviving
