ECMO: /ˈɛkmoʊ/ (American English) /ˈɛkməʊ/ (British English) | n. | 体外膜肺氧合 | Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation. A technique of providing prolonged cardiac and respiratory support to persons whose heart and lungs are unable to provide an adequate amount of gas exchange or perfusion to sustain life. | The patient was placed on ECMO after suffering a severe heart attack. 病人严重心脏病发作后，被使用了体外膜肺氧合。|  The doctors decided to use ECMO as a last resort. 医生决定把体外膜肺氧合作为最后的治疗手段。 | synonyms: extracorporeal life support (ECLS) | antonyms: None (ECMO is a life-saving procedure; there's no direct opposite) | noun
