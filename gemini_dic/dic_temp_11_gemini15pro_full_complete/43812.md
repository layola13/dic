crenulated: /ˈkrɛnjʊˌleɪtɪd/
adjective
成扇形的，锯齿状的，波纹状的
Having a notched or scalloped edge, like a battlement.
例句：The crenulated leaves of the plant were a striking feature.  这种植物的扇形叶子是一个显著的特征。

例句：The ancient castle walls were crenulated, providing defensive positions for archers. 古城堡的城墙呈锯齿状，为弓箭手提供了防御阵地。

近义词：scalloped, notched, serrated, indented
反义词：smooth, flat, even
词性: adjective

crenulate: /ˈkrɛnjʊˌleɪt/
verb
使成扇形，使成锯齿状
To make notches or scallops in something.
例句：The artist crenulated the edges of the paper to create a decorative border.  艺术家在纸的边缘刻出扇形，以创建一个装饰性的边框。

例句：The constant erosion of the coastline has crenulated the rocks.  海岸线的不断侵蚀使岩石形成了锯齿状。

近义词：notch, scallop, serrate, indent
反义词：smooth, flatten
词性: verb

crenulation: /ˌkrɛnjʊˈleɪʃən/
noun
扇形，锯齿状，波纹
The state of being crenulated; a notched or scalloped edge.
例句：The crenulation of the coastline provided numerous sheltered bays.  海岸线的扇形地形提供了许多避风的海湾。

例句：The microscopic crenulations on the surface of the leaf help it to retain water.  叶子表面微小的波纹有助于它保留水分。

近义词：scallop, notch, serration, indentation
反义词：smoothness, flatness
词性: noun
