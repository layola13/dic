blacklist: /ˈblækˌlɪst/ (US), /ˈblækˌlɪst/ (UK) | n. vt.| 黑名单，列入黑名单 | A list of people or things that are disapproved of and should be avoided or excluded. |  例句：The company put him on the blacklist after he was caught stealing. 这家公司在他被抓到偷窃后把他列入了黑名单。

例句：Several websites were blacklisted because they contained illegal material.  一些网站因为包含非法内容而被列入黑名单。

近义词：blocklist, ban, prohibit | 反义词: whitelist, approve, allow | 词性: noun, verb


blacklist: /ˈblækˌlɪst/ (US), /ˈblækˌlɪst/ (UK) | vt.| 将…列入黑名单 | To put someone or something on a blacklist. | 例句：They blacklisted him for his political views. 他们因为他的政治观点而把他列入黑名单。

例句：The government blacklisted several organizations suspected of terrorism. 政府将几个涉嫌恐怖主义的组织列入黑名单。

近义词：ban, bar, exclude | 反义词: whitelist, approve, allow | 词性: verb
