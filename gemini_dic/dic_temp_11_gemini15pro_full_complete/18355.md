bellows:/ˈbeləʊz/ (UK), /ˈbɛloʊz/ (US)| n. |风箱；波纹管|a device that produces a stream of air when its sides are pressed together, typically used for blowing air into a fire or a musical instrument.  It consists of two boards joined by flexible leather sides that can be expanded and contracted like an accordion.|The blacksmith worked the bellows to heat the iron. 铁匠拉动风箱加热铁块。|synonyms: air pump, blower, ventilator|antonyms: vacuum pump|noun

bellows:/ˈbeləʊz/ (UK), /ˈbɛloʊz/ (US)| v. |(使)像风箱一样运动；喘息|to move in and out or up and down like bellows; to breathe heavily.|His chest bellowed with each cough. 他每次咳嗽，胸部都像风箱一样起伏。|synonyms: heave, pant, puff|antonyms: exhale smoothly, breathe calmly|verb
