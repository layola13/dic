fledgling:/ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ/ /ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ/| n. adj.|雏鸟，新手，无经验的人|A young bird that has just fledged; a person or organization that is immature, inexperienced, or new.|例句：The fledgling company struggled to compete in the market. 这家新成立的公司难以在市场上竞争。|近义词：beginner, novice, trainee, apprentice, neophyte|反义词：expert, veteran, professional|词性：名词

fledgling:/ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ/ /ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ/| adj.|羽翼未丰的，刚开始的|Newly developed and not yet established; inexperienced.|例句：The fledgling democracy faced many challenges.  这个新生的民主国家面临许多挑战。|近义词：emerging, nascent, developing, budding|反义词：mature, established, developed|词性：形容词


词源：From Middle English *fleglinge, from Old English *flyccling, from flīegan (“to fly”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *fleuganą (“to fly”).  The "-ling" suffix denotes a diminutive or someone in a particular state.
