inurbanity:/ˌɪnɜːrˈbænɪti/ | n. | 粗鲁，无礼; 缺乏教养 | Lack of refinement, politeness, or good manners; boorishness. |  He displayed surprising inurbanity by interrupting the speaker mid-sentence. 他在演讲者讲话中途打断，表现出惊人的粗鲁。| Example 2: Her inurbanity made her an unwelcome guest at social gatherings. 她的粗鲁使她在社交聚会上不受欢迎。 | 近义词: rudeness, impoliteness, incivility, discourtesy | 反义词: politeness, courtesy, civility, refinement | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Late Latin *inurbanitas*, from Latin in- "not" + urbanitas "urbanity, refinement."  The Latin word "urbanitas" itself comes from "urbanus" meaning "of the city," implying the sophisticated manners associated with city life.
