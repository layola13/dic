bust-up: /ˈbʌst ʌp/ (US), /ˈbʌst ʌp/ (UK)
n.|争吵，打架；破裂|A serious quarrel or fight; a breakup.|例句：They had a terrible bust-up last night and aren't speaking to each other. 他们昨晚大吵了一架，现在互不理睬。

例句：The business partnership ended in a bitter bust-up.  生意伙伴关系以激烈的争吵而告终。

|近义词：quarrel, fight, argument, disagreement, altercation, falling out, row (UK)|反义词：reconciliation, agreement, accord, harmony|词性: 名词


bust-up: /ˈbʌst ʌp/ (US), /ˈbʌst ʌp/ (UK)
adj.|破裂的，失败的|(of a relationship or partnership) broken or failed.|例句：Their bust-up marriage ended after only a year.  他们破裂的婚姻仅仅一年就结束了。

例句：The bust-up project cost the company millions.  这个失败的项目让公司损失了数百万。

|近义词：broken, failed, ruined, collapsed|反义词：successful, thriving, flourishing|词性: 形容词
