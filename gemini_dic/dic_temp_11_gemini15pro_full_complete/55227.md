dissidence:/ˈdɪsɪdəns/| n. |异议，不同意见|Disagreement with the government or authorities; dissent.|例句：Political dissidence is not tolerated in totalitarian regimes.  极权主义政权不容忍政治异议。|例句：The writer was known for his dissidence and was eventually exiled. 这位作家因其异议而闻名，最终被流放。|近义词：dissent, disagreement, opposition, defiance, nonconformity|反义词：agreement, conformity, obedience, compliance|词性：名词

dissident:/ˈdɪsɪdənt/| n. adj. |持不同政见者，持异议者；持不同政见的|A person who publicly disagrees with and criticizes their government.|例句：Several dissidents were arrested after the protest.  抗议活动后，几名持不同政见者被捕。|例句：He became a dissident voice in the increasingly conformist society. 他在这个日益循规蹈矩的社会中成为了一个持不同政见的声音。|近义词：protester, objector, rebel, nonconformist|反义词：conformist, loyalist|词性：名词，形容词


词源:  From Latin dissident- ‘sitting apart, disagreeing’, from the verb dissidēre, from dis- ‘apart’ + sidēre ‘to sit’.
