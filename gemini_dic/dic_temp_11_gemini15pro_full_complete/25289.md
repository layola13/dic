bug-out:/ˈbʌɡ aʊt/ | vi.| 紧急撤退，匆忙离开 | To leave suddenly and hastily, typically in response to an emergency or threat. | We had to bug out of the city when the hurricane hit.  飓风来袭时，我们不得不匆忙离开这座城市。| 近义词: evacuate, escape, flee | 反义词: arrive, enter, stay | 词性: 动词

词源:  "Bug out" originated in American military slang during World War II.  It's thought to be derived from the image of insects fleeing a disturbed nest or hiding place.  二战期间，“bug out”起源于美式军事俚语。人们认为它源于昆虫逃离被扰乱的巢穴或藏身之处的形象。
