nuthatch:/ˈnʌthætʃ/ (US), /ˈnʌthætʃ/ (UK)| n.|旋木雀，五子雀|A small, agile songbird that climbs trees and feeds on insects and nuts, often wedging nuts into crevices and hacking them open with its strong beak.|例句：The nuthatch spiralled down the trunk of the oak tree.  旋木雀盘旋着从橡树的树干上下来。

例句：We watched a nuthatch cracking open a seed on the bird feeder. 我们看着一只旋木雀在喂鸟器上啄开一颗种子。|近义词：treecreeper, creeper|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hnut-hacce, from hnut "nut" + hacce "hatchet," referring to the bird's habit of wedging nuts into crevices and hacking them open.
