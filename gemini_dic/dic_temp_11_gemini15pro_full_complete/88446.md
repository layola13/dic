histamine:/ˈhɪstəmiːn/ | n. | 组胺 | An organic nitrogenous compound, C₅H₉N₃, derived from histidine, that is released by mast cells and basophils as part of an allergic reaction in humans and other animals. It stimulates gastric secretion and causes dilation of capillaries, bronchoconstriction, and decreased blood pressure. |  The release of histamine causes the symptoms of allergies, such as itching and sneezing. 组胺的释放会导致过敏症状，例如瘙痒和打喷嚏。| synonyms:  allergen,  mediator | antonyms: antihistamine | noun
