officerhood:/ˈɒfɪsərˌhʊd/ | n. |军官身份，军官职位|The state or quality of being an officer.  The collective body of officers.| He achieved officerhood after years of dedicated service.  他经过多年的奉献服务，终于获得了军官职位。 |近义词：officership, officialdom |反义词: (No direct antonym, consider civilian status, enlisted ranks) |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "officer" + "-hood". "Officer" comes from Old French "officier", from Medieval Latin "officiarius," from Latin "officium" meaning "duty, service." "-hood" is an Old English suffix denoting "state, condition, quality."
