babbles:/ˈbæblz/| v. n.|胡言乱语，喋喋不休；牙牙学语；（溪流）潺潺作响|To talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way; to utter sounds like a baby.|The baby babbles happily in her crib. 婴儿在婴儿床里高兴地牙牙学语。|近义词：chatters, prattles, murmurs|反义词：articulates, enunciates|词性:verb (third-person singular simple present babbles, present participle babbling, simple past and past participle babbled)

babbles:/ˈbæblz/| n.|胡言乱语；潺潺声|Foolish or meaningless talk; the sound of a babbling brook.|The politician's speech was just a bunch of babbles. 这位政客的演讲只是一堆胡言乱语。|近义词：chatter, prattle, murmur|反义词：articulation, enunciation|词性:noun (plural)

词源: From Middle English *bablen, from Old French babler, of imitative origin.
