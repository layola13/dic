cliquishly:/ˈklɪkɪʃli/| adv.|小集团地，派系地|In a manner that suggests a clique; exclusively.  Showing a tendency to form or associate in small, exclusive groups.|The girls cliquishly whispered secrets among themselves, excluding anyone outside their tight-knit group. 女孩们小集团地窃窃私语，把圈子以外的人都排除在外。|Synonym: exclusively, clannishly, factionally|Antonym: inclusively, openly, broadly|Part of Speech: Adverb

clique:/kliːk/| n.|小集团，派系|A small, exclusive group of people.|The popular girls formed a clique and wouldn't let anyone else sit with them at lunch.  受欢迎的女孩们组成了一个小团体，不让其他人和她们一起吃午饭。|Synonym: coterie, inner circle, faction|Antonym: general public, masses|Part of Speech: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *clique* "latch, handle of a door," also "group of people," perhaps from Old French *cliquer* "to click, clack, clink."  The sense of an exclusive group developed in the 19th century.
