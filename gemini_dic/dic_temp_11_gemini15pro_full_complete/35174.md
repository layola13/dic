cloy:/klɔɪ/| v. |使生腻；使过饱；使厌倦|to make someone feel that they do not want any more of something pleasant, usually something sweet|The sweetness of the dessert began to cloy after a few bites.  这道甜点吃了几口后就开始觉得腻了。|The rich food cloyed his appetite.  油腻的食物让他倒了胃口。|近义词：satiate, surfeit, glut|反义词：stimulate, excite|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cloien, *cloyen, from Anglo-Norman *cloer, *cloyer (“to nail, to stop up, to drive a nail in, to spike a gun”), from Old French cloer (“to close, to shut”), from Latin claudere (“to close, to shut”).  Doublet of include and close.
