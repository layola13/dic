demobilizing:/diːˈmoʊbəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
|v. |复员；遣散；使不再动员|The process of releasing people from military service.  The government is demobilizing thousands of soldiers.|
|例句：The army is demobilizing troops after the ceasefire. 停火后，军队正在遣散士兵。|
|例句：Demobilizing the workforce after the project's completion was a complex undertaking. 项目完成后遣散劳动力是一项复杂的工作。|
|近义词：disbanding, discharging, releasing|
|反义词：mobilizing, recruiting, enlisting|
|词性：动词|


demobilized:/diːˈmoʊbəˌlaɪzd/
|adj. |复员的；遣散的|Having been released from military service.  Many demobilized soldiers struggle to find jobs.|
|例句：The demobilized soldiers returned home to their families. 复员的士兵回到了家中。|
|例句：The government offered training programs for demobilized veterans. 政府为退伍军人提供了培训项目。|
|近义词：discharged, released|
|反义词：mobilized, enlisted|
|词性：形容词|


demobilization:/diːˌmoʊbələˈzeɪʃən/
|n. |复员；遣散|The act of demobilizing. The demobilization of the army was a long and complex process.|
|例句：The demobilization of the troops took several months. 部队的复员花了几个月的时间。|
|例句：Post-war demobilization led to a surge in the civilian workforce. 战后复员导致了平民劳动力的激增。|
|近义词：disbandment, discharge|
|反义词：mobilization, recruitment|
|词性：名词|
