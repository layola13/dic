acquit:/əˈkwɪt/| v. |宣判无罪；表现；履行（职责等）|To free someone from a criminal charge by a verdict of not guilty; to conduct oneself or perform in a specified way.|The jury acquitted the defendant of all charges. 陪审团宣判被告所有罪名不成立。|Synonyms: absolve, exonerate, clear, vindicate|Antonyms: convict, condemn, incriminate|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French aquiter, from a- (intensive prefix) + quite (free, clear), from Latin quietus (at rest, free from disturbance).
