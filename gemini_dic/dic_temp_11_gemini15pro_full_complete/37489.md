committeeism:/kəˈmɪtiːɪzəm/
n.|委员会主义|Excessive reliance on committees for decision-making, often resulting in inefficiency and inaction.|The project was bogged down by committeeism; no one could make a decision.|近义词:bureaucracy, red tape|反义词:individual initiative, decisive leadership|词性:名词

Etymology:
From committee + -ism.  "Committee" comes from the French "comité," which in turn derives from the Latin "committere," meaning "to commit" or "entrust." The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.
