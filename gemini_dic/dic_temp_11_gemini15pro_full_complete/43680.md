credentialism: /krɪˈdɛnʃəlɪzəm/
noun
文凭主义；学历主义
A social system in which academic or other formal qualifications are emphasized excessively, often at the expense of practical skills or experience.
Many employers are accused of credentialism, prioritizing degrees over actual ability. 许多雇主被指责为文凭主义，优先考虑学位而不是实际能力。
近义词：qualificationism, academicism
反义词：pragmatism, experience-based
词性: noun


credentialism: /krɪˈdɛnʃəlɪzəm/
noun
资格证书制度
A reliance on formal qualifications or certifications as a primary basis for evaluating individuals' competence or suitability for a role.
The increasing credentialism in the job market makes it harder for those without specific certifications to find work.  就业市场上日益增长的资格证书制度使得那些没有特定证书的人更难找到工作。
近义词：certificationism, formalism
反义词：meritocracy, skill-based
词性: noun
