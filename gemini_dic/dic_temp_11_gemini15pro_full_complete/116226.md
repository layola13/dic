masoret:/mɑːˈsɔːrɛt/| n. |马索拉文本，马索拉学|The Masoret refers to the system of vocalization, accentuation, and cantillation marks added to the consonantal text of the Hebrew Bible by Jewish scribes, primarily between the 6th and 10th centuries CE.  It also refers to the body of traditions transmitted along with the text itself.|The Leningrad Codex is an important source for studying the Masoret.| 近义词: Masorah, Masoretic Text | 反义词: None | 词性: noun

masoret:/mɑːˈsɔːrɛt/| n. |马索拉学|Masoret, as a field of study, involves the examination and analysis of the Masoretic Text of the Hebrew Bible, focusing on the meticulous work of the Masoretes in preserving and transmitting the text.|Scholars of Masoret have made significant contributions to our understanding of the Hebrew Bible.| 近义词: Masorah studies, Masoretic studies | 反义词: None | 词性: noun
