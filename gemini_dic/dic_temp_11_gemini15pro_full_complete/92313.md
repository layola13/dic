hypnotherapy:/ˌhɪpˈnɒθərəpi/ | n. |催眠疗法|A form of therapy that uses hypnosis to treat a variety of conditions, including anxiety, depression, and addiction.  It involves guiding a person into a relaxed state of focused attention, where they are more open to suggestions that can help them change their thoughts, feelings, and behaviors.|Example 1: Hypnotherapy helped him overcome his fear of flying. 催眠疗法帮助他克服了飞行恐惧症。

Example 2: She is considering hypnotherapy to quit smoking. 她正在考虑用催眠疗法戒烟。|Synonym: hypnosis, hypnotherapeutics |Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but potentially: conscious therapy, cognitive therapy) |noun
