nullifying:/ˈnʌlɪfaɪɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
使无效；取消；废除
making legally null and void; counteracting or overriding the effect or force of something.
The treaty was nullified after the war.  战争结束后，该条约被废除。
His efforts were nullified by the rain. 他的努力被雨水化为乌有。
近义词:invalidating, negating, voiding, canceling
反义词: validating, confirming, ratifying
词性: Verb (present participle)

nullify:/ˈnʌlɪfaɪ/
verb
使无效；取消；废除
to make legally null and void; deprive of legal force; to counteract the effect of; make of no value or consequence.
The judge nullified the contract. 法官宣布该合同无效。
Congress can nullify a treaty. 国会可以废除条约。
近义词:invalidate, negate, void, cancel
反义词:validate, confirm, ratify
词性: Verb
词源: From Late Latin nullificare, from Latin nullus (“none, not any”) + facere (“to make”).
