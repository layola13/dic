grandis:/ˈɡrændɪs/| adj.|大的，宏伟的|From Latin grandis "large, great, grand."  Used primarily in scientific names (taxonomy) to denote a larger or grander variety of a species.|Example: Abies grandis (Grand Fir) is a large evergreen tree.  巨冷杉是一种大型常绿乔木。|Synonym: large, great, big, huge, immense, vast, massive|Antonym: small, little, tiny, miniature|Etymology: Latin|
