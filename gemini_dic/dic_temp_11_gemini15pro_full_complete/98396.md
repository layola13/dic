ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *insoluble, from Latin insolubilis (“that cannot be loosened, indissoluble, insoluble”), from in- (“not”) + solubilis (“that can be loosened, dissolvable, soluble”), from solvō (“I loosen, dissolve”) + -bilis (suffix forming adjectives).  Surface analysis is in- + soluble + -ness.
