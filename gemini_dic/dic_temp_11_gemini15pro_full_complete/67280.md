exsanguinous:/ˌɛkˈsæŋɡwɪnəs/
adjective
(脸色)苍白的，血色全无的; (比喻)毫无生气的
Lacking blood; drained of blood.  Figuratively, lacking vitality or spirit.
The patient appeared exsanguinous after the surgery.  手术后，病人脸色苍白。
The long, drawn-out negotiations left both parties feeling utterly exsanguinous.  漫长而拖延的谈判让双方都感到筋疲力尽。
近义词:pale, bloodless, ashen, pallid
反义词:ruddy, flushed, sanguine
词性:adjective

exsanguinate:/ɪkˈsæŋɡwɪneɪt/
verb
使失血; 放血
To drain of blood; to cause extensive blood loss.
The hunter exsanguinated the deer. 猎人给鹿放血。
Severe injuries exsanguinated the victim quickly.  严重的伤势使受害者迅速失血过多。
近义词:bleed, drain
反义词:transfuse
词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin exsanguis (“bloodless”), from ex- (“out”) + sanguis (“blood”).  The verb form, exsanguinate, is a back-formation from exsanguinous.
