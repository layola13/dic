messiahs:/məˈsaɪəz/
|n.|弥赛亚（救世主）（复数）|A leader regarded as divinely appointed to save a people or group.|Many people saw him as a messiah who would lead them to freedom.|近义词:savior, redeemer, liberator|反义词: oppressor, tyrant|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Late Latin Messias, from Greek Messīas, from Aramaic mēšīḥā, from Hebrew māšīaḥ  "anointed," passive participle of māšaḥ "to anoint."
