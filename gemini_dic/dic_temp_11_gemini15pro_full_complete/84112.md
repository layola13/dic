half-learnedly:/ˌhɑːfˈlɜːrnɪdli/ | adv. | 一知半解地，略知皮毛地 |  In a way that suggests incomplete knowledge or understanding; superficially. | He spoke half-learnedly about the subject, clearly not grasping the nuances. 他对这个话题讲得一知半解，显然没有掌握细微之处。 |  superficially, cursorily, incompletely | thoroughly, comprehensively, expertly | adverb

half-learned:/ˌhɑːfˈlɜːrnd/ | adj. | 一知半解的，略知皮毛的 | Having only superficial or incomplete knowledge. | His half-learned pronouncements on the subject were embarrassing. 他对这个话题一知半解的言论令人尴尬。 | superficial, incomplete, sketchy | thorough, comprehensive, expert | adjective

词源:  "half-" (Old English healf) + "learned" (past participle of "learn," from Old English leornian) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).
