hoity-toityness:/ˌhɔɪtiˈtɔɪtinəs/| n. |傲慢，自命不凡|The state or quality of being hoity-toity; haughty or snobbish behavior or manner.  It describes an attitude of superiority and aloofness.|Her hoity-toityness made her unpopular with her colleagues. 她的傲慢态度让她不受同事欢迎。|近义词：haughtiness, arrogance, snobbishness, superciliousness|反义词：humility, modesty, down-to-earthness|词性:noun

hoity-toity:/ˌhɔɪtiˈtɔɪti/| adj. |傲慢的，自命不凡的|Haughty, snobbish, or pompous in manner or attitude.  It suggests a disdainful and patronizing attitude towards others considered inferior.|She gave him a hoity-toity look. 她傲慢地看了他一眼。|近义词：haughty, arrogant, snobbish, supercilious|反义词：humble, modest, down-to-earth|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
Originating in the late 18th century, "hoity-toity" is likely an imitative formation, perhaps echoing the high-stepping gait of a haughty person.  The "-ness" suffix creates the noun form, indicating the state or quality of being hoity-toity.
