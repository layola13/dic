hyperclimax:/ˌhaɪpərˈklaɪmæks/ | n. | 超高潮，层进高潮 | A figure of speech in which a series of clauses are arranged in ascending order of importance, where each clause is more exciting, forceful, or intense than the preceding one, leading to an unexpected or exaggerated conclusion.  It's a heightened form of climax. | The assignment was difficult, the exam was brutal, the final project was torture, and the graduation ceremony was the apocalypse!  | Synonym:  escalation, intensification, crescendo | Antonym: anticlimax, bathos | noun
