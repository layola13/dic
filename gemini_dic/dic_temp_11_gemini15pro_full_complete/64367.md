erebus:/ˈɛrɪbəs/| n.|黑暗; 冥界; 厄瑞玻斯|A dark, gloomy place or state; the underworld in Greek mythology, where the dead pass on their way to Hades.  It is personified as a primordial deity, representing the darkness before creation.|例句：The cave was a veritable erebus, filled with shadows and the sound of dripping water. 这个洞穴是一个真正的黑暗之地，充满了阴影和滴水的声音。

例句：His heart was an erebus of despair after losing the championship. 失去冠军后，他的内心充满了绝望的黑暗。|近义词：abyss, darkness, void, underworld, hell|反义词：heaven, paradise, light|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek Ἔρεβος (Érebos, “deep darkness, shadow”).  Erebus was a primordial deity in Greek mythology, representing the darkness that existed before the creation of the world. He was the son of Chaos and the brother of Night (Nyx).
