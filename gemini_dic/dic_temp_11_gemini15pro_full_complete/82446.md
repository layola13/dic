grumblier: /ˈɡrʌmbliər/ (US), /ˈɡrʌmbliə(r)/ (UK)| adj.|更爱抱怨的，更爱发牢骚的|Comparative form of "grumpy," meaning more inclined to complain or express dissatisfaction.|He's much grumblier in the mornings. 他早上脾气更暴躁。|The old man became grumblier as the day went on.  随着时间的推移，老人变得越来越爱抱怨。|Synonyms: more irritable, more cantankerous, more bad-tempered|Antonyms: more cheerful, more pleasant, more good-natured|词性: adjective

grumbling: /ˈɡrʌmblɪŋ/ (US), /ˈɡrʌmblɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |抱怨，牢骚|The act of complaining in a low, muttered tone.|There was a lot of grumbling about the new rules.  对于新规定，大家怨声载道。|The constant grumbling from the back of the room was distracting.  来自房间后面不断的抱怨声让人分心。|Synonyms: complaining, muttering, whining|Antonyms: praising, complimenting, applauding|词性: noun

grumble: /ˈɡrʌmbl/ (US), /ˈɡrʌmbl/ (UK)| v. |抱怨，发牢骚|To complain in a low, muttered tone.|He grumbled about the cold weather. 他抱怨天气冷。|She grumbled to herself about the long lines.  她小声抱怨排队时间太长。|Synonyms: complain, mutter, whine|Antonyms: praise, compliment, applaud|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Middle English word *grummelen*,  of uncertain origin, possibly imitative.
