lockdowns:/ˈlɒkdaʊnz/ (British), /ˈlɑːkdaʊnz/ (American)| n.|封锁，禁闭|The imposition of stringent restrictions on travel, social interaction, and access to public spaces, typically in response to an emergency such as a pandemic or a security threat.  The government imposed strict lockdowns to control the spread of the virus.|例句：The city was placed under strict lockdowns during the pandemic.  疫情期间，这座城市处于严格的封锁状态。

lockdowns:/ˈlɒkdaʊnz/ (British), /ˈlɑːkdaʊnz/ (American)| n.|封锁，禁闭|The confining of prisoners to their cells, typically in response to a disturbance or emergency within a prison.  The prison was placed on lockdown after a riot broke out. |例句：The prison went into lockdown after an attempted escape.  越狱未遂后，监狱进入了封锁状态。|近义词：closures, shutdowns, restrictions, confinements|反义词: reopenings, lifting of restrictions|词性: noun
