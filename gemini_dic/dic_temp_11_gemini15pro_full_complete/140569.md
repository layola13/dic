oligarchs:/ˈɒlɪɡɑːk/ (UK), /ˈoʊlɪɡɑːrk/ (US)| n.|寡头，寡头政治执政者|A member of a small group of people who control a country or organization.|The Russian oligarchs amassed vast fortunes during the privatization of state assets. 俄罗斯寡头在国有资产私有化期间积累了巨额财富。|Synonyms: magnate, tycoon, plutocrat|Antonyms: commoner, proletarian|Noun

oligarchic:/ˌɒlɪˈɡɑːkɪk/ (UK), /ˌoʊlɪˈɡɑːrkɪk/ (US)| adj.|寡头政治的|Relating to or characterized by oligarchy (rule by a small group of people).|The country's oligarchic system concentrated power in the hands of a few wealthy families.  这个国家的寡头政治体系将权力集中在少数富裕家庭手中。|Synonyms: elitist, plutocratic|Antonyms: democratic, egalitarian|Adjective

oligarchy:/ˈɒlɪɡɑːki/ (UK), /ˈoʊlɪɡɑːrki/ (US)| n.|寡头政治|A small group of people having control of a country or organization.|The fall of the Soviet Union led to the rise of an oligarchy in Russia. 苏联解体导致俄罗斯寡头政治的兴起。|Synonyms: plutocracy, aristocracy|Antonyms: democracy, republic|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ὀλιγαρχία (oligarkhía, “rule by a few”), from ὀλίγος (olígos, “few”) + ἄρχω (árkhō, “to rule, command”).
