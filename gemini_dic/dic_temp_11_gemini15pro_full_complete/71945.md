flier:/ˈflaɪər (US)/ /ˈflaɪə(r) (UK)| n.|飞行物，传单，飞行员|A piece of paper, often printed with an advertisement, that is given to people to read.  A person or animal that flies.|例句：They were handing out fliers for a new restaurant. 他们在散发新餐厅的传单。

例句：He saw a flier advertising a concert. 他看到一张宣传音乐会的传单。|近义词：leaflet, pamphlet, handbill, aviator, pilot|反义词:None (for the leaflet sense).  For the pilot/aviator sense, perhaps "passenger."|词性:Noun


flier:/ˈflaɪər (US)/ /ˈflaɪə(r) (UK)| n.|冒险者，投机者|Someone who takes risks, especially financial risks.|例句：He's a bit of a flier in the stock market. 他在股票市场上有点冒险。

例句：She’s known as a flier, always investing in new startups. 她以冒险而闻名，总是投资新的初创公司。|近义词：risk-taker, gambler, speculator|反义词:conservative, cautious|词性:Noun
