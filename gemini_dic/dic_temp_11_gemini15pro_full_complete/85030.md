harbinger: /ˈhɑːrbɪndʒər/ (US) /ˈhɑːbɪndʒə(r)/ (UK)| n.|先驱，预兆，前兆|a person, thing, or event that announces or signals the approach of another|The robin is a harbinger of spring. 知更鸟是春天的预兆。|近义词：precursor, forerunner, herald, omen, sign|反义词: aftermath, result, consequence|词性: noun

harbinger: /ˈhɑːrbɪndʒər/ (US) /ˈhɑːbɪndʒə(r)/ (UK)| vt.|预示，预告|to signal the approach of; to presage|Dark clouds harbinger a storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨的来临。|近义词：foreshadow, portend, augur, presage|反义词: conceal, hide|词性: verb

词源: From Old North French *herbergiere, from Frankish *heriberga "lodging, shelter", from *hari "army" + *bergaz "protection". Related to harbor. Originally a person who went ahead to arrange lodgings, later generalized to a forerunner.
