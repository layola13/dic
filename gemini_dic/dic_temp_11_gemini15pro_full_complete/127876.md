neoconservatism:/ˌniːoʊkənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/ | n. | 新保守主义 | A political movement advocating a strong military, free-market capitalism, and an assertive foreign policy. | The rise of neoconservatism in the 1970s influenced American foreign policy. 新保守主义在20世纪70年代的兴起影响了美国的外交政策。| 近义词: hawkishness, right-wing conservatism | 反义词: liberalism, left-wing politics | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  "neo-" (new) + "conservatism" (a political philosophy emphasizing traditional values and institutions).  The term emerged in the 1970s to describe a group of intellectuals, many of whom were former liberals, who became disenchanted with the leftward drift of the Democratic Party.
