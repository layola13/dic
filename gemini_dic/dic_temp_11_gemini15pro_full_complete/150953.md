##paralyzed /ˈpærəlaɪzd/ | adj. |瘫痪的，麻痹的|describes a state of being unable to move or function normally, either physically or mentally.  Physical paralysis refers to the loss of the ability to move parts of thebody due to nerve damage or other medical conditions.  Mental paralysis can refer to an inability to think clearly or make decisions, often due to fear, shock,or overwhelming stress.瘫痪了，阻止了她呼救。loss of function. The term reflects the historical understanding of paralysis as a loss of control over bodily functions. Over time, the meaning broadened to encompass mental incapacitation as well.and mental states of inactivity or inability to function.  The context generally clarifies whether it refers to physical or mental paralysis.  For example,  "paralyzed limbs" clearly indicates physical incapacitation, while "paralyzed by fear" refers to a mental state.
