catheterization:/ˌkæθɪtərɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 导管插入术 | The insertion of a catheter into a body cavity, such as the bladder, to drain fluid or inject a substance. | The patient underwent catheterization to empty his bladder. 病人接受了导管插入术以排空膀胱。|  | catheter insertion, intubation | | noun

catheterize:/ˈkæθɪtəraɪz/ | vt. | 插入导管 | To insert a catheter into (a body cavity or vessel). | The nurse catheterized the patient to collect a urine sample. 护士给病人插导管以收集尿样。| cannulate, intubate | decannulate, extubate | verb


Etymology: From Late Latin *catheterizātiō, from Latin *catheterizāre, from Ancient Greek καθετηριάζω (kathetēriázō).
