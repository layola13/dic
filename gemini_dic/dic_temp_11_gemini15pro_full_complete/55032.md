disquietedly:/dɪsˈkwaɪətɪdli/ | adv. | 不安地，焦躁地 | In a manner that shows worry, nervousness, or unease. | She paced the room disquietedly, waiting for the news. 她不安地在房间里踱来踱去，等待着消息。| She glanced disquietedly at the clock, realizing she was late. 她不安地瞥了一眼时钟，意识到自己迟到了。 | nervously, anxiously, restlessly | calmly, peacefully | adverb

Etymology:
From disquiet (verb) + -ed (suffix forming past participles and adjectives) + -ly (suffix forming adverbs).  Disquiet itself comes from the Old French *desqueit*, from des- (prefix indicating reversal) + quiet (quiet).
