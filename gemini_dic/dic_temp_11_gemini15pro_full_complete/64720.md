escheat:/ɪsˈtʃiːt/
n.
充公；归属；遗产归属权
The reversion of property to the state, or (in feudal law) to a lord, on the death of the owner without legal heirs.
The property escheated to the state because the deceased had no will or heirs.  由于死者没有遗嘱或继承人，财产充公。
synonyms: forfeit, confiscate, seize
antonyms: grant, award, bestow
noun

escheat:/ɪsˈtʃiːt/
v.
（财产）归属；充公
To revert to the state, or (in feudal law) to a lord, through escheat.
His land escheated to the crown when he died without an heir. 他死后无继承人，土地归属王室。
synonyms: revert, lapse, devolve
antonyms: inherit, receive, acquire
verb
