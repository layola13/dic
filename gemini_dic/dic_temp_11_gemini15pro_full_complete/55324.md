dissolve:/dɪˈzɑːlv/ (US), /dɪˈzɒlv/ (UK)| v.|溶解，解散，消失|To become incorporated into a liquid so as to form a solution; to break up or cause to break up into separate parts or groups; to fade away or disappear gradually.|The sugar dissolved in the hot tea. 糖在热茶中溶解了。|synonyms: melt, liquefy, disintegrate, disperse, vanish|antonyms: solidify, congeal, coalesce, assemble, appear|verb

dissolution:/ˌdɪsəˈluːʃn/ (US), /ˌdɪsəˈluːʃn/ (UK)| n.|溶解，解散，解体|The act or process of dissolving; the state of having been dissolved; the termination or extinction of an assembly, partnership, or official body.|The dissolution of the company was announced yesterday. 公司的解散是昨天宣布的。|synonyms: disintegration, breakup, separation, termination|antonyms: solidification, unification, formation, establishment|noun
