flashily:/ˈflæʃɪli/| adv.|浮华地，炫耀地|In a showy or ostentatious manner; gaudily.|She was dressed flashily in a bright pink dress and gold jewelry. 她穿着一件亮粉色的连衣裙和金首饰，打扮得花枝招展。|例句：He flashily showed off his new sports car. 他炫耀地展示他的新跑车。|近义词：ostentatiously, gaudily, showily|反义词：simply, modestly, plainly|词性:副词

flashily:/ˈflæʃɪli/| adv.|闪耀地|In a bright, dazzling manner.|The lights flashed flashily, momentarily blinding me. 灯光闪烁， momentarily blinding me.|例句：The fireflies blinked flashily in the night. 萤火虫在夜里一闪一闪地发光。|近义词：brightly, dazzlingly, glitteringly|反义词：dimly, faintly|词性:副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From flash (v.) + -ily (adverbial suffix).  "Flash" originates from Old Norse *flasa "to burst forth, gush," related to Middle Low German vlischen "to splash, flash." The suffix "-ily" derives from the Old French "-ilement," ultimately from Latin "-iliter."
