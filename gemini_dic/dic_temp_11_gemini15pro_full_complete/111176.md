lithoglyph: /ˈlɪθəɡlɪf/ | n. | 岩画，石刻 | A carving or inscription on a rock surface, especially one made in prehistoric times. |  Example 1: Archaeologists discovered a fascinating lithoglyph depicting a hunting scene. 考古学家发现了一幅描绘狩猎场景的迷人的岩画。

Example 2: The ancient lithoglyphs told stories of the people who lived there long ago.  古代的岩画讲述了很久以前居住在那里的人们的故事。 | Synonym: petroglyph, rock carving, inscription | Antonym: (No direct antonym, could consider "blank rock face") | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek *lithos* (stone) + *glyphein* (to carve).
