mutinous:/ˈmjuːtɪnəs/ | adj. | 叛变的，反抗的；不服从的 | Refusing to obey the orders of a person in authority. | The mutinous sailors took control of the ship. 叛变的水手控制了这艘船。 | The mutinous crowd began to throw rocks at the police. 愤怒的群众开始向警察投掷石块。 | rebellious, insubordinate, defiant, seditious | obedient, compliant, submissive | adjective

mutiny:/ˈmjuːtəni/ | n. | 叛乱，兵变 | An open rebellion against the proper authorities, especially by soldiers or sailors against their officers. | The mutiny was quickly suppressed by the captain. 叛乱很快被船长镇压了。 |  The soldiers staged a mutiny against their commanding officer. 士兵们发动了反对他们指挥官的兵变。 | rebellion, revolt, uprising, insurrection | obedience, submission, compliance | noun
