augustine:/ɔːˈɡʌstɪn/ /ˈɔːɡəstiːn/| n. adj.|奥古斯丁, 八月瓜|Augustine is both a masculine given name and a surname.  As a given name, it is a later form of Augustinus, derived from Augustus. The name has been borne by several notable figures, most prominently Saint Augustine of Hippo, an influential philosopher and theologian. As an adjective, it refers to anything relating to St. Augustine, his teachings, or the Augustinian religious order. |例句：Saint Augustine was a prominent figure in the early Christian church. 圣奥古斯丁是早期基督教会的重要人物。

例句：Augustinian theology emphasizes the concept of divine grace. 奥古斯丁神学强调神恩的概念。|近义词：(as a given name) Gus, Austin|反义词: N/A|词性: Noun, Adjective
