calciminer: /ˌkælˈsɪmaɪnər/ | n. | 刷白灰浆; 用白灰浆涂抹的人 | A white or tinted wash made of whiting, glue, and water, used especially for walls.  A person who calcimines. | The walls were coated with a cheap calciminer. 墙壁涂了一层廉价的白灰浆。 | Synonyms: whitewash, distemper | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "paint" in a decorative context) | noun


calcimine: /ˈkæl.səˌmaɪn/ | v. | 用白灰浆涂抹 | To coat or wash with calcimine. |  They calcimined the old barn to freshen it up.  他们用白灰浆粉刷了旧谷仓，使其焕然一新。| Synonyms: whitewash, distemper | Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "paint" in a decorative context) | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *calcinamen, from Latin calcinare "to burn lime," from calc-, calx "lime" + -in- + -are (infinitive suffix).  The English word was borrowed from French calciminer.
