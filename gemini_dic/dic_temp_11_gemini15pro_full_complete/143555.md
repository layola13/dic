outhears:/ˌaʊtˈhɪərz/ | v. | 偷听到，偶然听到 | To hear something unintentionally, especially something confidential or private. |  She outheard them talking about the surprise party. 她无意中听到他们在谈论惊喜派对。|  I outheard a snippet of their conversation as I walked past. 我路过时无意中听到他们谈话的片段。 | overhear, eavesdrop | - | verb
Etymology:  From "out-" (meaning "beyond, outside") + "hear."  The prefix "out-" suggests hearing something beyond what was intended for the listener.
