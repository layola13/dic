ditransitivities:/ˌdaɪtræn.sɪˈtɪv.ɪ.tiz/ | n. | 双及物性；双宾语性 | The property of a verb that takes both a direct object and an indirect object. |  The verb "give" exhibits ditransitivity; for example, in "I gave him the book," "book" is the direct object, and "him" is the indirect object. |  ditransitive verbs, double transitivity | intransitivities | noun
