bunker:/ˈbʌŋkər/| n.|地堡，掩体，沙坑|A fortified underground shelter, typically used in warfare. Also, a sand trap on a golf course.|The soldiers took refuge in the bunker during the bombardment. 士兵们在轰炸期间躲进了地堡。|The golfer's ball landed in a bunker.  高尔夫球手的球落入了一个沙坑。|近义词：shelter, dugout, trap (golf)|反义词: open field|noun


bunker:/ˈbʌŋkər/| v.|加燃料，(尤指船舶)装燃料|To supply a ship or other vehicle with fuel.|The ship was bunkered with low-sulfur fuel oil.  这艘船加注了低硫燃料油。|They bunkered the plane before its long flight. 他们在飞机长途飞行前给它加了油。|近义词：refuel, fuel|反义词:|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Scottish *bunker* "bench, seat," later "coal bin," of uncertain origin. The military sense emerged during World War I.
