hashes:/ˈhæʃɪz/ | n. | 哈希值，散列值 | A hash is a fixed-size string of text representing a larger piece of data, like a file or a password.  It is generated by a mathematical algorithm and acts like a fingerprint for the data. |  The program calculates the hash of the downloaded file to verify its integrity.  程序计算下载文件的哈希值以验证其完整性。 | synonyms: checksum, digest | antonyms: N/A | noun

hashes:/ˈhæʃɪz/ | v. |  把…切碎；搞砸 |  To chop (something, especially food) into small pieces. Also, to make a mess of (something). | I hashed the potatoes for the stew. 我把土豆切碎炖汤。 | synonyms: mince, chop, ruin, botch | antonyms: fix, repair | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the French "hacher," meaning "to chop."  Originally referred to chopped meat dishes.  The computing sense derives from the idea of "chopping up" data into smaller, uniform pieces.
