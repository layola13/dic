deafest:/ˈdɛfɪst/ | adj. |最聋的| Superlative form of deaf; most unable to hear. |  He was the deafest person in the room and couldn't hear a word. 他是房间里最聋的人，一句话也听不见。| 近义词: most hearing-impaired | 反义词: sharpest-hearing, most acute hearing | 词性:形容词

Etymology:
From Middle English *defe, *deve, from Old English *dēaf, from Proto-Germanic *daufaz, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰewh₂- (“to be cloudy, dusty; to become deaf”). Cognate with Dutch doof (“deaf”), German taub (“deaf”), Swedish döv (“deaf”), Latin fūmus (“smoke”), Lithuanian duslùs (“dusky, gloomy”), Sanskrit धूम (dhūma, “smoke, vapor”).
