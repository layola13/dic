disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/ | n. vt. | 失望，幻灭 | Disappointment resulting from the discovery that something is not as good as one had believed in. |  The poor election turnout reflected public disillusion with politics.  这次选举的低投票率反映了公众对政治的失望。 |  disenchantment, disappointment | hope, optimism | noun

disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/ | vt. | 使失望，使清醒 | To free from illusion or false ideas. |  He quickly became disillusioned with the job. 他很快就对这份工作感到失望。| disenchant, undeceive | encourage, inspire | verb
