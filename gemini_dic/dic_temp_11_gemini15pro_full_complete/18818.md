berthing:/ˈbɜːθɪŋ/ | n. | 停泊；靠泊；泊位 |The act of mooring a vessel to a berth.  The process of securing a ship or boat alongside a wharf, pier, or another vessel. | The berthing of the large cruise ship took longer than expected. 大型游轮的停泊时间比预期的要长。| Synonyms: mooring, docking, tying up | Antonyms: unberthing, casting off | noun

berth:/bɜːθ/ | n. | 泊位；铺位；职位 | A place where a ship or boat is moored. A sleeping accommodation on a train or ship. A job or position. | The ship secured a berth at the busy port.  这艘船在繁忙的港口获得了一个泊位。|  He booked a berth on the overnight train. 他在夜车上订了一个铺位。| Synonyms: mooring, dock, bunk, position, job | Antonyms: disembarkation, unemployment | noun

berth:/bɜːθ/ | vt. | 使停泊；使靠泊；为…提供铺位 | To moor a vessel to a berth. To provide a sleeping place for someone. | They berthed the yacht in the marina for the night. 他们把游艇停泊在码头过夜。| The cruise ship can berth over 5,000 passengers. 这艘游轮可以为5000多名乘客提供铺位。| Synonyms: moor, dock, accommodate | Antonyms: unberth, cast off | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *byrða (“burden, load”), from Proto-Germanic *burþiz (“burden, load”). Related to birth.  The original meaning referred to the space occupied by a ship's cargo, later evolving to mean the space where a ship is moored.
