mosey: /ˈmoʊzi/ (US) /ˈməʊzi/ (UK)| n.|闲逛，漫步 (less common usage)|A slow, relaxed walk or stroll.|He went for a mosey around the park. 他在公园里闲逛了一圈。|近义词：stroll, amble, wander, saunter|反义词：rush, hurry, sprint|名词


ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain, possibly from a dialectal variant of "mose" meaning "to move stealthily," or from a now obsolete verb "mosey" meaning "to dawdle." First recorded in American English in the mid-19th century.
