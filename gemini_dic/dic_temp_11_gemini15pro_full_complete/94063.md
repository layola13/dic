impassibility:/ɪmˌpæsəˈbɪləti/
|n.|
|无动于衷，冷漠，无感觉；不可能|
|The impassibility of his face made it difficult to read his emotions. 他的面无表情让人很难读懂他的情绪。|
|例句：He stared at her with an air of impassibility. 他面无表情地盯着她。|
|例句：The sheer impassibility of the mountain range was daunting.  这连绵山脉的绝对不可逾越令人畏惧。|
|近义词：apathy, indifference, insensitivity, impossibility|
|反义词：sensitivity, responsiveness, possibility|
|词性: 名词|


impassive:/ɪmˈpæsɪv/
|adj.|
|无动于衷的，冷漠的，无表情的|
|His impassive face gave nothing away. 他面无表情，什么也没透露。|
|例句：She remained impassive throughout the trial. 在整个审判过程中，她始终面无表情。|
|例句：He listened to the news with an impassive expression. 他面无表情地听着新闻。|
|近义词：apathetic, indifferent, unemotional, expressionless|
|反义词：emotional, expressive, responsive|
|词性: 形容词|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *impassibilitatem*, from impassibilis ("incapable of suffering"), from in- ("not") + passibilis ("capable of suffering"), from pati ("to suffer").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *peh₁-  ("to suffer, endure").
