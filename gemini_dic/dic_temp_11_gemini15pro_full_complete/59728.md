eclogue: /ˈɛklɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈɛklɔːɡ/ (US) | n. | 牧歌; 田园诗 | A short poem, especially one written in a pastoral style and often in the form of a dialogue or soliloquy. |  Example 1: Virgil's Eclogues are some of the most famous examples of pastoral poetry.  维吉尔的《牧歌》是田园诗歌中最著名的例子。

Example 2: The poet composed a beautiful eclogue celebrating the joys of rural life. 这位诗人创作了一首优美的牧歌，歌颂乡村生活的乐趣。 |  Synonym: pastoral, idyll, bucolic | Antonym: epic, tragedy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *eclogue*, from Latin *ecloga*, from Greek *eklogē* meaning “selection, choice extract”, from *eklegein* meaning “to pick out, select”, from *ek* “out” + *legein* “to gather, speak”.  Originally referred to a select piece of writing.
