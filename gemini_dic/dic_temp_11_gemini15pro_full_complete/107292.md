lamaism:/ˈlɑːməɪzəm/| n.|喇嘛教|A Western term, now often considered outdated and sometimes offensive, for Tibetan Buddhism, referring to the dominant religion of Tibet. It emphasizes monastic life and the authority of lamas (spiritual teachers).|The term "lamaism" was popularized in the 19th century but is now considered inaccurate and Eurocentric.|近义词:Tibetan Buddhism|反义词:|n.
