gilds: /ɡɪldz/
| n. | 同业公会，协会；金箔，镀金 |A medieval association of craftsmen or merchants, often having considerable power.  The application of a thin layer of gold to a surface. |The goldsmiths' guild was very powerful in the city.  金匠行会在该市非常有势力。 The frame was gilded with 24-karat gold.  这个框架镀上了24K金。 |近义词：associations, societies, unions, plating (for the act of gilding) |反义词: (No direct antonym for the noun form. For the verb form, "tarnish" could be considered an antonym.) |词性:Noun (plural)

gild: /ɡɪld/
| vt. | 镀金，装饰；使…光彩夺目；虚饰，掩盖 |To cover with a thin layer of gold. To give a deceptively attractive or valuable appearance to. | The setting sun gilded the clouds. 落日给云朵镀上了一层金边。  He tried to gild his humble background. 他试图掩饰自己卑微的出身。 |近义词：adorn, embellish, decorate, enhance, coat, cover, camouflage, disguise |反义词: tarnish, expose, reveal |词性:Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *gieldan (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *geldaną (“to pay, yield”), from Proto-Indo-European *gʰeldʰ- (“to pay, yield”).  Related to "yield."  The sense of covering with gold arose from the value associated with gold.
