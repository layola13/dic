bastille:/bæˈstiːl/ |n.|巴士底狱； (喻) 堡垒；压制|A fortress in Paris stormed by the populace on July 14, 1789, an event generally considered to mark the beginning of the French Revolution. |The storming of the Bastille symbolized the end of oppression. 巴士底狱的攻占象征着压迫的结束。|synonyms: fortress, citadel, stronghold, prison|antonyms:  liberation, freedom|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French Bastille, ultimately from Old French bastille (“fortification, tower, bastion”), from bastir (“to build”), from Late Latin bastire, of uncertain origin.
