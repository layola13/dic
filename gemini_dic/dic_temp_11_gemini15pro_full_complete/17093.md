batrachia: /bəˈtreɪkiə/ /bəˈtreɪʃə/
| n. |两栖动物|A class of vertebrates comprising the frogs, toads, newts, salamanders, and caecilians. They are cold-blooded and typically spend part of their life cycle in water and part on land.|Batrachia are a diverse group of amphibians found across the globe.|近义词:amphibians|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps reptiles, birds, or mammals as other vertebrate classes)|noun
