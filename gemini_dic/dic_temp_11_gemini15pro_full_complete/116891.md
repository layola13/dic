mayed: /meɪd/ | v. (archaic or dialectal) |  使...昏迷；使...惊呆 |  To be dazed or bewildered; stunned. | He was mayed by the sudden news of his father's death. 他被父亲突然去世的消息惊呆了。| 近义词: dazed, stunned, bewildered, confounded | 反义词:  alert, aware, conscious | 词性：动词


词源: From Middle English *mayen, from Old English *māgan "to be able, have power," related to German mögen "to like, may," Gothic magan "to be able." The original sense was "to be able to," which evolved to "to have the power to affect," and then to "to disable or overwhelm."
