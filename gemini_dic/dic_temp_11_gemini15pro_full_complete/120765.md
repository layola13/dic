misappointed:/ˌmɪsəˈpɔɪntɪd/ | adj. | 失望的，沮丧的 | Feeling sad or displeased because someone or something has failed to fulfill one's hopes or expectations. |  I was disappointed that the concert was cancelled. 我对音乐会被取消感到很失望。| 近义词：discouraged, disheartened, let down | 反义词：pleased, satisfied, delighted | 词性: 形容词

词源: From mis- + appoint (v.).  "Mis-" is a prefix indicating something wrong or amiss.  "Appoint" originally meant "to fix, settle, determine," thus "misappoint" meant to fail to fulfill an appointment or expectation.
