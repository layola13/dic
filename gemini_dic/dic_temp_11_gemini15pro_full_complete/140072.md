ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *of, from Old English of (also *af, *æf), from Proto-Germanic *af, from Proto-Indo-European *apo (“off, away from”). Cognate with Scots aff (“off”), West Frisian of (“of”), Dutch af (“off, down”), German ab (“off, down, from”), Swedish av (“of, off”), Icelandic af (“of, off”). More at off.
