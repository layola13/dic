dog-house:/ˈdɒɡˌhaʊs/ (UK) /ˈdɑːɡˌhaʊs/ (US)| n. |狗窝；困境；失宠|A small shelter for a dog to sleep in. Also figuratively, a state of disfavor or trouble. |The dog spent most of the day in its dog-house.  这只狗一天的大部分时间都在它的狗窝里度过。


He's in the dog-house with his wife after forgetting their anniversary.  他因为忘记了结婚纪念日而被他妻子冷落。|近义词：kennel, doghouse (informal)|反义词: (Literal) None, (Figurative) favor |名词

词源：
The term "dog-house" originated in the early 20th century, referring to a literal small shelter for a dog.  The figurative meaning, "in a state of disfavor," developed later, likely from the idea of being banished to a small, uncomfortable space like a dog-house as a form of punishment.
