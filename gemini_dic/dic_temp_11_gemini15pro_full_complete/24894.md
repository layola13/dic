bruised:/bruːzd/
adjective
擦伤的，碰伤的
Injured by being hit or knocked against something; having a bruise or bruises.
She had a bruised knee. 她膝盖擦伤了。

bruised:/bruːzd/
adjective
(of fruit) damaged by being handled roughly.
(水果)碰伤的
There were a few bruised apples in the bowl. 碗里有一些碰伤的苹果。


bruised:/bruːzd/
adjective
(of feelings) hurt or upset.
(感情)受伤害的，沮丧的
He had a bruised ego after losing the match. 输了比赛后，他感到很沮丧。

bruise:/bruːz/
noun
伤痕，淤青
An injury appearing as an area of discoloured skin on the body, caused by a blow or impact rupturing underlying blood vessels.
He had a nasty bruise on his arm. 他的胳膊上有一块严重的淤青。

bruise:/bruːz/
verb
使…出现伤痕；擦伤；碰伤
Inflict a bruise or bruises on (a part of the body).
She bruised her leg falling down the stairs. 她从楼梯上摔下来，腿上碰伤了。

bruise:/bruːz/
verb
(of a fruit) damage by handling roughly.
(水果)碰伤
Don't bruise the peaches when you pack them. 包装桃子时不要把它们碰伤。

bruise:/bruːz/
verb
hurt (someone's feelings).
伤害(某人的感情)
His pride was bruised by their harsh criticism. 他们的严厉批评伤害了他的自尊心。



ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English brusen, of uncertain origin. Related to Old French bruisier.

SYNONYMS->
(adjective) injured, hurt, damaged, scraped, sore, tender, aching, painful
(noun) contusion, discoloration, lesion, mark, spot
(verb) injure, hurt, damage, mark, blemish


ANTONYMS->
(adjective) unblemished, unmarked, healed, undamaged, healthy
(verb) heal, soothe, mend
