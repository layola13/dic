bubonic:/bjuːˈbɒnɪk/ /buːˈbɑːnɪk/| adj.|腺鼠疫的；腹股沟腺炎的|Relating to or denoting bubonic plague, a highly contagious and often fatal disease characterized by fever, vomiting, and the formation of buboes.  A form of the plague that causes swelling in the lymph nodes.|例句：The bubonic plague devastated medieval Europe. 黑死病摧毁了中世纪的欧洲。

例句：He contracted bubonic plague while traveling abroad. 他在国外旅行时感染了腺鼠疫。|近义词：pestilential, contagious|反义词: healthy, non-contagious|词性:adjective

Etymology:
From Late Latin *bubo, from Greek boubōn "groin," also "swelling in the groin."  Ultimately from PIE root *beu- "to swell."
