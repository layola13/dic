cadges:/ˈkeɪdʒɪz/| v. (third-person singular simple present)|乞讨，骗取，|To persuade someone to give you something, typically money or food, by asking repeatedly or using deception.  It implies a sense of  pettiness or lowliness in the act.|He cadges cigarettes from his friends all the time. 他总是向朋友乞讨香烟。|Synonyms: begs, sponges, mooches, scrounges|Antonyms: earns, provides, gives|verb

cadge:/kædʒ/| v.|乞讨，骗取|To persuade someone to give you something, typically money or food, by asking repeatedly or using deception. ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain, possibly from Scots *cadge, to carry, or from Shelta cadger, a carrier, peddler.
