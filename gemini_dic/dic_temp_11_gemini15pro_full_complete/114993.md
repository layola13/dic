mandamus:/mænˈdeɪməs/| n.|强制令，履行职责令|A writ issued by a superior court to compel a lower court or a government officer to perform a mandatory duty.|The lawyer filed for a mandamus to force the city to issue the building permit. 律师申请了强制令，迫使市政府签发建筑许可证。|Synonyms: order, injunction, command, decree|Antonyms: prohibition, refusal, denial|noun


mandamus:/mænˈdeɪməs/| n.|强制令，履行职责令|A judicial writ issued as a command to an inferior court or ordering a person to perform a public or statutory duty.|The citizens sought a mandamus to compel the mayor to enforce the law. 市民寻求强制令，迫使市长执行法律。|Synonyms: writ, precept, directive|Antonyms:  prohibition, injunction (when used to stop an action)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *mandamus*, from Latin *mandāmus* ("we command"), first person plural present active indicative of *mandāre* ("to command, order, entrust, commit").
