flash-out:/ˈflæʃ aʊt/ | v. | 彻底清除；冲洗；（使）突然涌出 | To remove something completely, especially by flushing with liquid; to cause something to emerge suddenly. |  The police flashed out the drug dealers from their hideout. 警察将毒贩从藏身处清除出去。

The water flashed out of the broken pipe. 水从破裂的管道中喷涌而出。 | 近义词：flush out, rinse out, expel, eject | 反义词：fill, inject | 词性：动词

flash-out:/ˈflæʃ aʊt/ | n. |  (计算机) 闪存 | A sudden burst of light or energy.  In computing, a type of memory that can be erased and reprogrammed. | The camera's flash-out momentarily blinded me. 相机的闪光灯 momentarily blinded me. (This example refers to "flash" as a noun, not "flash-out" which is primarily a verb) | 近义词：flash, burst | 反义词：N/A | 词性：名词 (rarely used, usually as "flash")
