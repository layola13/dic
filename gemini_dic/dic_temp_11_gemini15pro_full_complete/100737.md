intransigent:/ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒənt/| adj. n.|不妥协的，不让步的；不妥协的人|unwilling or refusing to change one's views or to agree about something.|例句：He was intransigent in his opposition to the plan. 他坚决反对这项计划。|例句：The intransigent attitude of both parties made a compromise impossible. 双方互不让步的态度使得妥协不可能。|近义词：uncompromising, inflexible, stubborn, obstinate|反义词：flexible, yielding, accommodating|词性:形容词/名词

intransigents:/ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒənts/| n.|不妥协的人|People who are unwilling or refusing to change their views or to agree about something.|例句：The intransigents on both sides refused to budge. 双方顽固分子都拒绝让步。|例句：Negotiations broke down because of the intransigents in the group. 谈判破裂是因为小组中有些不妥协的人。|近义词：diehards, hardliners|反义词：moderates, compromisers|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin intransigent-, intransigens, from in- ‘not’ + transigent- ‘settling’, from the verb transigere (see transact).
