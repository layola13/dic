intergrave:/ɪnˈtɜːɡreɪv/ | adj. | 雕刻的，铭刻的 | Carved or engraved into something, especially a tombstone. |  The intergrave inscription on the tombstone was barely legible. 墓碑上雕刻的铭文几乎难以辨认。 |  inscribed, engraved, etched |  plain, smooth | adjective

intergrave:/ɪnˈtɜːɡreɪv/ | v. | 雕刻，铭刻 | To carve or engrave into something, especially a tombstone.  | They intergraved the date of his birth and death on the headstone. 他们在墓碑上刻上了他的生卒年月。 | inscribe, engrave, etch | erase, obliterate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *intergravare*, a compound of *inter-* (between, among, within) and *gravare* (to burden, to weigh down, to engrave).  The sense of "engrave" likely developed from the idea of cutting into a surface, creating a burden or impression upon it.
