ETYMOLOGY->
"Out of luck" is a straightforward idiom. "Out of" indicates a lack or deprivation of something, and "luck" refers to good fortune.  Therefore, "out of luck" literally means deprived of good fortune. The phrase likely originated in the late 19th or early 20th century, emerging from common usage and informal speech.
