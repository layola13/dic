incenser:/ˈɪnsɛnsər/| n.|香炉，焚香器具；香|A container in which incense is burned.|The monk lit the incenser and the temple filled with a pleasant aroma.  僧侣点燃了香炉，寺庙里充满了令人愉悦的香气。|Near synonyms: censer, thurible|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "deodorizer" in a very loose sense)|Noun

incense:/ɪnˈsɛns/| n. vt.|香；焚香；激怒|A substance that produces a fragrant odor when burned. (n.) / To make someone very angry. (vt.)|The incense filled the air with a sweet smell. 香充满了空气，散发着甜甜的气味。|His condescending remarks incensed her. 他居高临下的言论激怒了她。|Near synonyms: (n.) fragrance, perfume, aroma; (vt.) enrage, infuriate, anger|Antonyms: (n.) stench, stink; (vt.) soothe, calm, placate|Noun, Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French encens (“incense”), from Late Latin incensum (“something burned”), neuter past participle of incendere (“to set on fire, kindle”), from in- (“in, on”) + candēre (“to shine, glow”). Doublet of enkindle.
