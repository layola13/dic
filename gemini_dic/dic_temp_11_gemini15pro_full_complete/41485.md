corns:/kɔːrnz/| n. |玉米；鸡眼|Seeds of corn, or a small, hard area of skin that can be painful, especially on the foot.|The farmer planted corns in the spring. 农民在春天种了玉米。|She has a corn on her toe. 她的脚趾上长了一个鸡眼。|近义词：(for corn as food) maize, grain; (for corn as skin condition) callus, bunion|反义词: (no direct opposite for either meaning)|词性:noun

corns:/kɔːrnz/| v. |(用玉米粒)喂；腌制(肉类)|To feed with corn; to preserve and season (meat) with salt or brine and often spices.|They corn the chickens every morning. 他们每天早上都用玉米喂鸡。|The beef was corned for several weeks before being smoked. 牛肉在熏制前用盐腌制了几周。|近义词:(for feeding) feed, nourish; (for preserving) cure, pickle|反义词:(no direct opposite for either meaning)|词性:verb
