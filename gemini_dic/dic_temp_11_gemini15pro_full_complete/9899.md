apatite: /ˈæpəˌtaɪt/ (US) /ˈæpətʌɪt/ (UK)| n. |磷灰石|A common complex mineral consisting of calcium phosphate together with fluorine, chlorine, or hydroxyl, or mixtures of these, occurring in various colours and in hexagonal crystalline form.  It is used in the manufacture of fertilizers and is the main source of phosphorus.|Apatite is a major component of animal bones and teeth. 磷灰石是动物骨骼和牙齿的主要成分。| Apatite deposits are often mined for their phosphate content. 磷灰石矿床通常因其磷酸盐含量而被开采。|Synonyms: phosphate rock, phosphorite|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *apatite*, from Greek *apatē* meaning "deceit," because it was often mistaken for other minerals.
