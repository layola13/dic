brainwashed:/ˈbreɪnwɒʃt/ (UK) /ˈbreɪnwɑːʃt/ (US)| adj.|洗脑的；被洗脑的|Having had your beliefs or opinions completely changed by being persuaded or forced to accept other beliefs or opinions.|例句：He claimed he had been brainwashed by the cult.  他声称自己被邪教洗脑了。|例句：The government is accused of brainwashing the public with propaganda. 政府被指控用宣传洗脑公众。|近义词：indoctrinated, programmed, conditioned|反义词：independent-minded, freethinking|词性：形容词

brainwash:/ˈbreɪnwɒʃ/ (UK) /ˈbreɪnwɑːʃ/ (US)| vt.|给…洗脑；对…进行思想灌输|To force someone to accept a particular set of beliefs or opinions by repeating the same information again and again.|例句：The terrorists tried to brainwash their captives. 恐怖分子试图给俘虏洗脑。|例句：Children are easily brainwashed. 孩子很容易被洗脑。|近义词：indoctrinate, program, condition|反义词：enlighten, educate|词性：动词

词源：brainwash (v.) 1950, from brain (n.) + wash (v.).  A translation of Chinese xi nao, literally "wash brain," a term from the Korean War.
