haikwan:/ˈhaɪkwɑːn/ | n. | 海关；(旧时中国的)海关官员 | A customs official in China, especially under the Manchu dynasty, or a customhouse.  Historically, the Haikwan tael was a unit of currency used in China for foreign trade. |  The haikwan collected taxes on all imported goods.  海关官员对所有进口商品征税。|  The goods were inspected at the haikwan. 货物在海关接受检查。 | synonyms: customs official, customs officer, customs agent, tax collector | antonyms: smuggler | noun

haikwan tael:/ˈhaɪkwɑːn tael/ | n. | 海关两；(旧时中国外贸用的)一种记账货币单位 | A unit of weight of silver used in China for foreign trade.  It was not a coin, but a unit of account based on the weight and fineness of silver. | The merchant paid the haikwan tael for the imported silk. 商人用海关两支付进口丝绸的费用。 | The value of the haikwan tael fluctuated with the price of silver. 海关两的价值随白银价格波动。| synonyms: tael, unit of account, currency unit (historical) | antonyms: N/A | noun
