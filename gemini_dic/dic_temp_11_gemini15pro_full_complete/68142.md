fail-safed:/ˈfeɪlˌseɪftd/ | adj. |自动防故障的，有故障保护装置的 |Designed or operating so that failure of a component will not result in catastrophic failure of the system. | The airplane has a fail-safed system for landing in case of engine failure.  飞机有一个自动防故障的着陆系统，以防发动机故障。|近义词：reliable, secure, foolproof |反义词：unsafe, unreliable |词性:形容词

fail-safe:/ˈfeɪlˌseɪf/ | n. |故障保护装置，安全装置 |A device or system that is designed to prevent failure or to minimize damage in the event of failure.| The nuclear reactor has several fail-safes to prevent a meltdown. 核反应堆有几个故障保护装置以防止熔毁。|近义词：safeguard, precaution, backup |反义词：danger, hazard |词性:名词

词源:  "fail-safe" is a compound word formed from "fail" and "safe." It emerged in the mid-20th century, likely influenced by the increasing complexity of engineering systems and the need for mechanisms to prevent catastrophic failures.
