orthogeosyncline: /ˌɔːrθoʊˈdʒiːoʊˌsɪŋklaɪn/ | n. | 正地槽 | A large linear downwarp in the Earth's crust, thought to be a major site of accumulating sediments, volcanic rocks, and tectonic activity, ultimately forming folded mountain ranges. |  The Appalachian Mountains were formed from an ancient orthogeosyncline. 阿巴拉契亚山脉是由一个古老的正地槽形成的。 |  geosyncline, basin |  --- | noun
