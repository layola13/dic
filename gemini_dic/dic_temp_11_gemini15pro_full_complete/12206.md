asafetida:/ˌæsəˈfɛtɪdə/ | n. | 阿魏，又名兴渠、阿虞、熏渠、哈昔尼|A hard, resinous gum with a strong, unpleasant odor, obtained from the roots of several species of Ferula, used in cooking and medicine.|  Example 1: A pinch of asafetida was added to the lentil soup for flavor.  少许阿魏被添加到扁豆汤中提味。

Example 2:  Asafoetida is believed to have medicinal properties in some cultures. 在一些文化中，阿魏被认为具有药用价值。 |Synonym:  hing, devil's dung, stinking gum |Antonym:  N/A (no direct antonym for a specific spice) | noun
