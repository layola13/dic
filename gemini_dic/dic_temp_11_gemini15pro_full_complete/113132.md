lungwort:/ˈlʌŋwɜːrt/ (US), /ˈlʌŋwɜːt/ (UK)| n. |肺草；肺筋草|A plant of the borage family, traditionally used to treat lung diseases.  The name comes from the spotted appearance of the leaves of some species, which resemble a diseased lung.|The lungwort in my garden is starting to bloom.  我花园里的肺草开始开花了。|Synonyms: pulmonaria, Bethlehem sage, Jerusalem cowslip|Antonyms: N/A|noun

lungwort:/ˈlʌŋwɜːrt/ (US), /ˈlʌŋwɜːt/ (UK)| n. |肺衣|A lichen that grows on trees and rocks, also sometimes called lungwort because of its supposed resemblance to lung tissue.|I found some lungwort growing on the old oak tree. 我在老橡树上发现了一些肺衣。|Synonyms: tree lungwort, lung lichen|Antonyms: N/A|noun
