eyespot:/ˈaɪˌspɒt/ (UK), /ˈaɪˌspɑːt/ (US)| n. |眼点，眼斑|A simple eye or eyespot is a form of eye, often found in invertebrates consisting of a collection of photoreceptor cells.  It can also refer to markings that resemble eyes, often used for camouflage or intimidation.|The peacock's tail feathers are covered in beautiful eyespots.  孔雀的尾羽上覆盖着漂亮的眼斑。|Synonyms: ocellus, stigma, photoreceptor|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
