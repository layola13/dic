cherry-picking: /ˈtʃɛriˌpɪkɪŋ/ | n. | 挑选；精选；断章取义 | The act of selecting only the most favorable or convenient items, data, or information from a larger set, often ignoring unfavorable or inconvenient ones. |  He was accused of cherry-picking data to support his argument. 他被指责断章取义地选择数据来支持他的论点。 | cherry-pick (v.), selective choosing | suppression of evidence, distortion of facts | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the literal practice of picking only the ripest and best cherries from a tree, leaving the less desirable ones behind.  This image then became a metaphor for selectively choosing the best or most desirable items from a larger set.
