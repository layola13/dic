immerse:/ɪˈmɜːrs/ (US) /ɪˈmɜːs/ (UK)| v. |沉浸，使专心，使浸没|To involve oneself deeply in something; to submerge something completely in a liquid.|She immersed herself in her studies. 她沉浸在学习中。|近义词：engross, absorb, submerge, plunge|反义词：distract, detach, remove|词性:动词

immerse:/ɪˈmɜːrs/ (US) /ɪˈmɜːs/ (UK)| v. |沉浸，使专心，使浸没|To dip or plunge entirely under water or other fluid.|The priest immersed the baby in the holy water.  牧师把婴儿浸入圣水中。|近义词：engross, absorb, submerge, plunge|反义词：distract, detach, remove|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French immerser, from Latin immersus ‘dipped in’, past participle of immergere, from in- ‘into’ + mergere ‘to dip’.
