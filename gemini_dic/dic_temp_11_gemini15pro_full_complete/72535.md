fluster: /ˈflʌstər/ | vt. n. | 使慌乱，使不安；慌乱，不安 | To make someone nervous and confused, often causing them to blush; a state of nervous confusion. |  The loud noise flustered the speaker. 巨大的噪音让演讲者感到不安。 | 近义词: confuse, disconcert, agitate | 反义词: calm, soothe | 词性: 动词，名词

词源:  Origin uncertain, possibly related to Old Norse *flaustr "hurry, haste."
