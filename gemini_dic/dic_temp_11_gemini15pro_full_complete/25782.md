bureaucracies: /bjʊˈrɒkrəsiːz/ (British) /bjʊˈrɑːkrəsiːz/ (American)
| n. pl.|官僚机构；官僚主义；官僚作风|A system of government in which most of the important decisions are made by officials rather than by elected representatives.  A system of administration marked by excessive paperwork and inflexible rules.|The hospital's bureaucracies are so complex that it's difficult to get anything done. 这家医院的官僚机构如此复杂，以至于很难完成任何事情。
|Synonym:  red tape, officialdom, administration|Antonym:  efficiency, streamlining, democracy|Noun (plural)

Etymology:
From French *bureaucratie*, from *bureau* (“desk” or “office”) + *-cratie* (from Greek *-kratia*, meaning "power" or "rule").
