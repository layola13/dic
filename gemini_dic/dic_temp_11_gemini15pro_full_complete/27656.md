candidates:/ˈkændɪdɪts/ (US), /ˈkændɪdeɪts/ (UK)| n.|候选人；应试者|A person who applies for a job or is nominated for election.|Many candidates applied for the marketing position. 许多候选人申请了市场营销职位。|Applicants, aspirants, contenders|Incumbents, non-candidates|Noun


candidates:/ˈkændɪdɪts/ (US), /ˈkændɪdeɪts/ (UK)| n.|报考者；投考者|A person who takes an examination.|The candidates were nervous before the exam. 考生们在考试前都很紧张。|Examinees, applicants, entrants|Non-candidates|Noun


Etymology: From Late Middle English *candidat*, from Latin *candidātus* "clothed in white," past participle of *candidāre* "to make white or bright," from *candidus* "white, shining, pure."  Roman office seekers traditionally wore white togas.
