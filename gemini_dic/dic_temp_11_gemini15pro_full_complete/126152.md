mutisms: /ˈmjuːtɪzəmz/ | n. | 缄默症，沉默症 |  A condition characterized by persistent refusal to speak in situations where speaking is expected, despite the physical ability to do so.  It can be caused by various factors, including emotional trauma, anxiety, or selective mutism. | The child's mutisms was a source of concern for his teachers.  这个孩子的缄默症令他的老师们担忧。|  silence, reticence, aphasia (when related to a physical inability to speak) |  loquaciousness, talkativeness | noun
