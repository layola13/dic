blancmanges:/bləˈmɑːnʒ/ /blɑːˈmɑːnʒ/ | n. | 杏仁豆腐，牛奶冻 |A sweet dessert, usually white or pink, made with milk or cream thickened with gelatin, cornstarch, or another setting agent, and often flavored with almonds. |  My grandmother always made blancmange for special occasions. 我祖母总是在特殊场合做杏仁豆腐。

The blancmange wobbled precariously as she carried it to the table. 她把杏仁豆腐端到桌子上时，它摇摇晃晃的，很不稳定。 | 近义词: pudding, custard, jelly | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old French *blanc mangier (“white food”), from blanc (“white”) + mangier (“to eat”).  Originally referred to a dish of white meat, often capon or chicken, cooked in almond milk. Over time, the dish evolved into the sweet dessert we know today.
