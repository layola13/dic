mosaiced:/moʊˈzeɪɪkt/ | adj. | 马赛克的，镶嵌的 | Decorated with mosaics; resembling a mosaic. |  The floor was mosaiced with colorful tiles.  地板用彩色瓷砖拼成了马赛克图案。| synonyms: inlaid, tessellated | antonyms: plain, unadorned | adjective


mosaiced:/moʊˈzeɪɪkt/ | v. (past participle) | 拼成马赛克，镶嵌 | To decorate with mosaics; to make something resemble a mosaic. | The artist mosaiced the tabletop with broken pieces of glass.  艺术家用碎玻璃片把桌面拼成了马赛克。| synonyms:  inlaid, tessellated | antonyms: dismantled, separated | verb (past participle)


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mosaïque*, from Italian *mosaico*, ultimately from Late Latin *musaicus* "of the Muses," because mosaic work was considered a fine art fit for the Muses.  The word later came to refer specifically to the art form itself.
