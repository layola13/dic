excess:/ˈekses/ (US), /ˈɛkses/ (UK)| n. adj.| 过量，过度；超过；超额 | An amount of something that is more than necessary, permitted, or desirable. |例句：There is an excess of cars on the road. 路上的车辆太多了。|近义词：surplus, overflow, abundance |反义词: lack, shortage, deficiency |词性: noun

excess:/ˈekses/ (US), /ˈɛkses/ (UK)| adj.| 过量的，额外的 | Being more than is required or sufficient. | 例句：Excess baggage will be charged. 超重行李将收费。 |近义词：extra, superfluous, surplus |反义词: insufficient, lacking, deficient |词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *exces, from Latin excessus (“a going out, departure, digression, transgression”), past participle of excedere (“to go out, depart, exceed”), from ex- (“out”) + cedere (“to go, yield”).
