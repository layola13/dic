laid-on:/ˈleɪd ˈɒn/ (UK), /ˈleɪd ˈɑːn/ (US)| adj.|安排好的，特意的，虚假的|Something that is laid-on is provided specially, especially as part of a service or entertainment.|The hotel had laid-on a buffet for the guests. 酒店为客人准备了自助餐。|Synonyms: provided, arranged, organized, staged, prepared|Antonyms: impromptu, spontaneous, unplanned, improvised|adjective

laid-on:/ˈleɪd ˈɒn/ (UK), /ˈleɪd ˈɑːn/ (US)| n.|安排好的事物，提供的服务|Something that is laid-on is provided specially, especially as part of a service or entertainment (used as a noun).|The buffet was quite a laid-on.  自助餐真是太丰盛了。|Synonyms: provision, arrangement, service|Antonyms: lack, absence, deficiency|noun
