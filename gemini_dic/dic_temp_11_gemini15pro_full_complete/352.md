abide-by:/əˈbaɪd baɪ/| phrasal verb |遵守，遵照|To accept or follow a decision, rule, agreement, etc.|例句：Both parties agreed to abide by the judge's decision. 双方同意遵守法官的决定。|例句：All members must abide by the club rules. 所有成员必须遵守俱乐部规则。|近义词：observe, follow, comply with, adhere to|反义词：disobey, violate, breach, ignore|词性：动词短语

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *abiden bi* ("to remain by, stay with"), from *abiden* ("to abide, remain") + *bi* ("by"). *Abiden* comes from Old English *ābīdan* ("to wait for, await, experience"), from *ā-* (intensive prefix) + *bīdan* ("to wait, remain").

PHRASE->
There are not many common set phrases with "abide by". It is generally used on its own followed by the rules, laws, decisions, etc. being abided by.  It's more of a formal, legalistic term.  Here are some examples of how it might be used in slightly longer phrases:

1.  We must all abide by the law of the land.  我们都必须遵守国家法律。
2.  They agreed to abide by the terms and conditions of the contract. 他们同意遵守合同的条款和条件。
3.  It is important to abide by the rules of the game.  遵守游戏规则很重要。
4.  Failure to abide by these regulations will result in penalties.  不遵守这些规定将导致处罚。
5.  She vowed to abide by the decision of the court. 她发誓要遵守法院的裁决。
