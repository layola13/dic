lurk:/lɜːrk/ (UK) /lɜːrk/ (US)| v. | 潜伏，埋伏；隐藏；暗中行动 | to wait somewhere secretly, especially because you are going to do something bad or illegal; to exist although it is not always noticeable |  The crocodile lurked in the murky water. 鳄鱼潜伏在浑浊的水中。 |  synonyms: skulk, prowl, sneak, loiter | antonyms: appear, emerge, show up | verb


lurk:/lɜːrk/ (UK) /lɜːrk/ (US)| n. | 潜伏；埋伏；隐藏；暗中行动 | the act of lurking | He gave her a lurk. 他偷偷地看了她一眼。(非正式用法，较少见) | synonyms: hiding, concealment | antonyms: exposure, revelation | noun (less common)
