displaced:/dɪsˈpleɪst/| adj.|流离失所的，被取代的|forced to leave their home or country, especially because of war or some other disaster; moved from the usual or correct place|例句：The war has displaced thousands of people. 战争使数千人流离失所。|近义词：uprooted, evicted, exiled, removed|反义词：settled, established, rooted|词性:形容词

displaced:/dɪsˈpleɪst/| v.|取代，置换|to force something or someone out of its usual or original position|例句：The new technology has displaced traditional methods. 新技术取代了传统方法。|近义词：replace, supersede, supplant|反义词：establish, reinstate|词性:动词

词源：From the Latin prefix dis- (meaning "apart, asunder") + past participle of the French verb placer (meaning "to place").  来自拉丁语前缀 dis-（意为“分开，分离”）+ 法语动词 placer（意为“放置”）的过去分词。
