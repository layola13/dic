fast-tracker:/ˈfæstˌtrækər/ | n. | 快速晋升者；快速通道 | Someone who is identified as having the potential to progress rapidly in their career, and is given special training or opportunities. |  She's a real fast-tracker and is expected to be managing her own team within a year. 她是一个真正的快速晋升者，预计一年内就能管理自己的团队。

|  The company has a fast-tracker program for promising young graduates.  这家公司为有前途的年轻毕业生设立了快速晋升计划。 | 近义词: high-flyer, rising star, go-getter | 反义词: plodder, underachiever | 词性: noun


fast-track:/ˈfæstˌtræk/ | v. |  快速推进；使…快速发展 | To accelerate the progress of something, especially a project or career. | The government is fast-tracking the new legislation. 政府正在快速推进新的立法。

|  His talent was quickly recognized, and he was fast-tracked to a senior position. 他的才能很快得到认可，并被迅速提升到高级职位。 | 近义词: expedite, accelerate, hasten | 反义词: delay, hinder, obstruct | 词性: verb


词源：
"fast track" 最早出现在20世纪中期，由 "fast"（快速的）和 "track"（轨道，路线）组合而成，最初用于赛马，指速度更快的赛道。后来，这个词被引申用于指任何快速推进的进程或项目，特别是职业发展。
