by-long-odds:/baɪ lɔːŋ ɑdz/
|adv.|毫无疑问地；绝对地；远远地|By a great margin; decisively. It indicates a significant difference or superiority.|He won the race by long odds. 他以绝对优势赢得了比赛。|Synonyms: easily, hands down, decisively, by a mile, by a wide margin|Antonyms: barely, narrowly, closely, by a hair's breadth|adverb

by-long-odds:/baɪ lɔːŋ ɑdz/
|adj.|（非正式）最有可能的；最合适的|Most likely or suitable (informal).|He's by long odds the best candidate for the job. 他无疑是这项工作的最佳人选。|Synonyms: most likely, most probable, best suited|Antonyms: least likely, unsuitable|adjective

词源：
The phrase "by long odds" originated in horse racing, where "odds" referred to the betting odds against a horse winning. "Long odds" meant a horse was considered unlikely to win, so winning "by long odds" meant winning despite the unfavorable predictions, implying a decisive victory.  该短语起源于赛马，其中“odds”指的是一匹马获胜的赔率。“Long odds”意味着这匹马被认为不太可能获胜，因此“by long odds”获胜意味着尽管预测不利，却获得了胜利，这意味着决定性的胜利。
