hippie:/ˈhɪpi/ (US), /ˈhɪpi/ (UK)| n. |嬉皮士，嬉皮|A person, typically young, associated with a subculture involving a rejection of conventional values and the taking of hallucinogenic drugs.|例句：The hippie movement of the 1960s embraced peace and love.  20世纪60年代的嬉皮士运动拥护和平与爱。

例句：He lived in a commune with other hippies. 他和其他嬉皮士一起住在公社里。|近义词：bohemian, nonconformist, counterculturalist|反义词：conformist, traditionalist|词性:noun

hippier:/ˈhɪpiər/ (US), /ˈhɪpiər/ (UK)| adj.(comparative) |更嬉皮的|More like a hippie; more unconventional or countercultural.|例句：She dressed in a hippier style than her sister. 她的穿着比她姐姐更嬉皮。

例句：The festival had a much hippier vibe this year. 今年的节日气氛更嬉皮。|近义词：more bohemian, more unconventional|反义词：more conventional, more traditional|词性:adjective
