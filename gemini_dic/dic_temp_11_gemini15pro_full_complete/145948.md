overdiscriminatingly: /ˌoʊvər dɪˈskrɪmɪˌneɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 过于挑剔地，过度歧视地 |  In an excessively discriminating manner; with too much attention to fine distinctions, often leading to unfair prejudice or exclusion. | He overdiscriminatingly rejected applicants based on minor flaws in their resumes. 他过于挑剔地根据简历中的小瑕疵拒绝了申请人。|  She overdiscriminatingly selected her groceries, examining every apple for blemishes. 她过于挑剔地挑选食品杂货，检查每个苹果是否有瑕疵。 | 近义词: hypercritically, fastidiously, excessively selectively | 反义词: indiscriminately, uncritically | 词性: 副词

词源:  The adverb "overdiscriminatingly" is derived from the verb "discriminate," which comes from the Latin "discriminare," meaning "to distinguish between."  The prefix "over-" indicates an excess or extreme degree, and the suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective "overdiscriminating" into an adverb.
