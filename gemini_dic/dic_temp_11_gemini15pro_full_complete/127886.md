neocriticism:/ˌniːoʊˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/
n.
新批评主义
A formalist approach to literary criticism that emphasizes close reading and textual analysis, rejecting biographical or historical context.
The professor's lecture focused on neocriticism and its impact on modern literature.  教授的讲座重点讨论了新批评主义及其对现代文学的影响。
近义词:formalism, close reading
反义词:historicism, biographical criticism
词性:noun

Etymology:
neo- (new) + criticism
From Greek *neos* (new) + criticism (from Greek *kritikos* - able to discern)
