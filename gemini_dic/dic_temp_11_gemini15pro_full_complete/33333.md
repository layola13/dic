词源:  "Chugger" is a relatively recent term, believed to have originated in the UK in the 1990s. It is thought to be derived from the verb "to chug," meaning to make a slow, regular, rhythmic sound, perhaps mimicking the persistent requests of street fundraisers.  "Chugger"这个词是一个相对较新的术语，据信起源于20世纪90年代的英国。它被认为源自动词 "to chug"，意思是发出缓慢、规律、有节奏的声音，也许是在模仿街头募捐者持续不断的请求。
