cannulae:/ˈkænjʊliː/
noun
插管;套管;导管
A cannula is a thin tube inserted into a vein or body cavity to administer medicine, drain fluid, or insert surgical instruments.
The nurse inserted a cannula into the patient's arm for intravenous fluids. 护士在病人的手臂上插入一根套管进行静脉输液。
cannula
catheter, tube
---
Etymology:
From Latin cannula "small reed, pipe," diminutive of canna "reed, cane."
