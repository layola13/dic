cacodyl: /ˈkækoʊdɪl/ | n. |二甲胂;  卡可基 |A highly toxic, spontaneously flammable, and unpleasant-smelling arsenic compound, tetramethyldiarsine, (CH3)2As−As(CH3)2. |Cacodyl has a distinctive, and highly unpleasant, garlicky odor. 二甲胂有一种独特且非常难闻的大蒜味。|  Cacodyl oxide was used as a chemical weapon in World War I.  氧化二甲胂在一战中被用作化学武器。 |Synonyms: tetramethyldiarsine | Antonyms: (No direct antonyms for a specific chemical compound) | noun
