magnetize:/ˈmæɡnɪtaɪz/| v. |磁化；吸引|To make something magnetic; to attract strongly, as if with a magnet.|例句：The iron filings were magnetized by the bar magnet. 铁屑被条形磁铁磁化了。

例句：His charisma magnetized everyone in the room. 他的魅力吸引了房间里的每一个人。|近义词：attract, influence, mesmerize|反义词：demagnetize, repel|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *magnétiser*, from *magnétique* (“magnetic”)  + *-iser* (suffix forming verbs).  Ultimately from Latin *magnetēs* (“magnet”), from Ancient Greek *Μαγνῆτις λίθος* (*Magnētis lithos*, literally “Magnesian stone”), referring to a region called Magnesia in Thessaly, Greece, where lodestone was found.
