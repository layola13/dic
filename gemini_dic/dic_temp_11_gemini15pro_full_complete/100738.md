intransigence:/ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒəns/| n. |不妥协，不让步，固执|The government's intransigence on the issue led to a prolonged strike. 政府在这个问题上的不妥协导致了旷日持久的罢工。|例句：His intransigence made negotiations difficult. 他的固执使得谈判变得困难。|近义词：inflexibility, stubbornness, rigidity|反义词:flexibility, compromise, yielding|词性:名词

intransigent:/ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒənt/| adj. |不妥协的，不让步的，固执的|He remained intransigent, refusing to consider any other options. 他仍然不妥协，拒绝考虑任何其他选择。|例句：The union took an intransigent position in the negotiations. 工会在谈判中采取了不妥协的立场。|近义词：uncompromising, unyielding, inflexible|反义词:flexible, compromising, yielding|词性:形容词

intransigentism:/ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒəntɪzəm/| n. |不妥协，不让步，固执|The intransigentism of both parties prevented a peaceful resolution. 双方的不妥协阻碍了和平解决。|例句：Her intransigentism ultimately cost her the election. 她的固执最终让她输掉了选举。|近义词：inflexibility, stubbornness, rigidity|反义词:flexibility, compromise, yielding|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *intransigeant*, from *in-* (not) + *transigeant* (compromising), from *transiger* (to compromise), from Latin *trans-* (across) + *agere* (to act, to do).
