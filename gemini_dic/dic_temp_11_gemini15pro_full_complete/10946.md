arawak: /ˈærəwæk/ | n. adj.| 阿拉瓦克人（的）|Arawak is a group of indigenous peoples of South America and historically of the Caribbean.  In a broader sense, the term "Arawakan" has also been used to refer to the large Arawakan language family of which their languages form a part.|The Arawak people were skilled farmers. 阿拉瓦克人都是些熟练的农民。|The museum displayed Arawak pottery. 博物馆展出了阿拉瓦克陶器。|近义词: Taino (related group), Caribbean Indian|反义词: None (as it refers to a specific ethnic group)|词性: noun, adjective

Etymology: From Spanish *Arahuaco*, from a native Caribbean name.
