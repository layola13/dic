monocline:/ˈmɑːnəˌklaɪn/ /ˈmɒnəˌklaɪn/| n. |单斜层，同斜褶曲|A geological formation in which rock strata are folded or flexed in one direction only, forming a gentle slope.|例句：The monocline gently dips towards the valley. 单斜层缓缓地向山谷倾斜。

例句：Oil and gas often accumulate in monocline structures. 石油和天然气经常聚集在单斜构造中。|近义词：homocline|反义词: anticline, syncline|词性:noun
Etymology->
From mono- + -cline, from Greek *μόνος (mónos, “single, alone”) + κλίνειν (klínein, “to lean, slope, incline”).
