non-u:/nɒnˈjuː/ | adj. | 非上流社会的；俗气的 | Not characteristic of the upper class; socially unacceptable in the eyes of the upper class.  |  She made a few non-u remarks that caused some raised eyebrows at the dinner party.  她在晚宴上说了一些不符合上流社会习惯的话，引起了一些人侧目。 |  Synonyms:  vulgar, common, low-class, unrefined, inelegant | Antonyms:  u, posh, refined, elegant, aristocratic | adjective


Etymology:  A shortening of "non-upper-class," popularized by the British linguist Alan S.C. Ross in his 1954 book *U and Non-U: An Essay in Sociological Linguistics*.  The term "U" (upper-class) and its counterpart "non-U" became widely discussed and debated in the mid-20th century.
