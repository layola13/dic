magus:/ˈmeɪɡəs/| n. |魔术师，法师，术士|A magus is a member of a priestly caste of ancient Persia.  In modern usage, it refers to a magician or sorcerer, often wise and powerful.|例句：The old magus cast a powerful spell.  老法师施放了一个强大的咒语。

例句：In the fantasy novel, the magus was a mentor to the young hero. 在这部奇幻小说中，法师是年轻英雄的导师。|近义词：sorcerer, wizard, magician|反义词:mundane, ordinary|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin magus, from Greek μάγος (magos), from Old Persian *maguš. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *magh- (“to be able, to have power”).
