determinative:/dɪˈtɜːrmɪnətɪv/ | adj. n. |限定的，决定性的；限定词 |A determinative is a word, such as *the*, *a*, *this*, *that*, *each*, *every*, *some*, or *my*, that comes before a noun to show which particular thing or things you are talking about.  A determinative limits the meaning of the noun. |例句：The cat sat on the mat.  这只猫坐在垫子上。|近义词：limiting, defining, restrictive |反义词: indefinite, nonrestrictive |词性: adjective, noun

determinative:/dɪˈtɜːrmɪnətɪv/ | n. |限定词 |A determinative is a word (such as “the,” “a,” “some,” “any,” or “my”) that comes before a noun to show whether the speaker and listener/reader both know which thing is being referred to and how many things are being referred to.|例句： "A" is an indefinite article and a type of determinative. “a”是不定冠词，是一种限定词。|近义词：article, quantifier |反义词:  (No direct antonym for a grammatical function) |词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *determinativus*, from Latin *determinare* (to determine).
