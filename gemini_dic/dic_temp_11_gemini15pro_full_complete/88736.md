hobornob: /ˈhoʊboʊˌnɒb/ | n. |  (informal) 交往，交际，与…亲密来往 | The act of socializing with someone, especially someone of higher social status. |  He enjoys hobnobbing with celebrities at parties. 他喜欢在派对上与名人交往。 | 近义词: socialize, mingle, fraternize | 反义词: isolate, avoid | 词性: 名词

词源:  "Hobnob" likely originated in the late 16th century as a contraction of "hab nab," itself probably a shortening of "have or have not."  Originally, it meant to drink together, sharing whatever one had.  Over time, the meaning evolved to encompass general socializing, often implying a degree of familiarity or intimacy.
