depreciation:/dɪˌpriːʃiˈeɪʃn/ |n.| 折旧；贬值| a reduction in the value of an asset over time, due in particular to wear and tear.|The company reported $5 million in depreciation last year.  该公司去年报告了500万美元的折旧。|The depreciation of the currency made imports more expensive. 货币贬值使得进口商品更加昂贵。| synonyms: devaluation, amortization, decline | antonyms: appreciation, increase, inflation | noun

depreciations:/dɪˌpriːʃiˈeɪʃnz/ |n.|  折旧（复数）|  reductions in the value of assets over time, often used in accounting contexts to refer to multiple assets or periods.| The accountant calculated the depreciations for all company vehicles. 会计计算了所有公司车辆的折旧。 | The annual depreciations are recorded in the company's financial statements. 年度折旧记录在公司的财务报表中。| synonyms: devaluations, amortizations, declines | antonyms: appreciations, increases, inflations | noun
