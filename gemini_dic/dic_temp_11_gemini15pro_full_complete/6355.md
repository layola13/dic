amphibians:/æmˈfɪbiənz/ | n. | 两栖动物 | Cold-blooded vertebrates (such as frogs, toads, salamanders, and newts) that are aquatic as larvae and terrestrial (or partly terrestrial) as adults. |  Amphibians are sensitive to environmental changes. 两栖动物对环境变化很敏感。 | synonyms:  herptiles, batrachians | antonyms: mammals, birds, reptiles | noun

amphibian:/æmˈfɪbiən/ | adj. | 两栖的；水陆两用的 | Relating to or characteristic of an amphibian; able to live both on land and in water. |  The frog is an amphibian creature. 青蛙是一种两栖动物。| synonyms: amphibious | antonyms: terrestrial, aquatic | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *amphibia*, neuter plural of *amphibius* (“living a double life”), from Ancient Greek ἀμφίβιος (amphíbios, “living a double life, amphibious”), from ἀμφί (amphí, “on both sides, around”) + βίος (bíos, “life”).
