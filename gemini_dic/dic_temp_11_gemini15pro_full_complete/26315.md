buy-in:/ˈbaɪɪn/ | n. |认同，支持；买进；入股；预付金|Agreement or support for an idea or plan, especially by involving people in developing it; the purchase of a company, particularly by the existing management; an amount of money paid in advance to participate in an activity or organization.| The management team secured buy-in from the employees for the restructuring plan. 管理团队获得了员工对重组计划的支持。| synonyms: agreement, acceptance, approval, support, backing, endorsement, consent, concurrence | antonyms: opposition, disagreement, dissent, rejection | noun

buy-in:/ˈbaɪɪn/ | n. | 买进; 入股 | The act of purchasing something, especially a company or a share in a company. |  There has been a significant buy-in of the company's stock by foreign investors. 外国投资者大量买入了这家公司的股票。| synonyms: purchase, acquisition, investment | antonyms: divestiture, sale | noun
