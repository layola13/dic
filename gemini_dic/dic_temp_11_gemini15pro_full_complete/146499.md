overgarrisoning: /ˌoʊvərˈɡærɪsənɪŋ/
n.
过度驻军；驻军过多
The act of stationing more troops in a location than is necessary or strategically sound.  This can strain local resources, create tension with the local population, and potentially make the garrison more vulnerable to attack due to overconcentration.
The overgarrisoning of the small island proved to be a logistical nightmare.  在小岛上过度驻军被证明是一场后勤噩梦。
The general realized the overgarrisoning was a mistake and began withdrawing troops. 将军意识到过度驻军是一个错误，于是开始撤军。

近义词: overdeployment, excessive garrisoning
反义词: undergarrisoning, insufficient garrisoning
词性: noun (名词)


词源:
From "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "garrison" (verb meaning "to station troops in a fortress or town") + "-ing" (suffix forming a noun from a verb).  The word "garrison" ultimately derives from the Old French word "garison," meaning "protection."
