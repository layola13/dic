muffling:/ˈmʌflɪŋ/ (US), /ˈmʌflɪŋ/ (UK)| n. v. adj.| 抑制，消音，裹住|The act of making a sound quieter or less distinct, or something used for this purpose.  Also, can be used as an adjective to describe something that muffles.|
The muffling of the drums made the music sound distant.  鼓声的抑制使音乐听起来很遥远。

muffle:/ˈmʌf(ə)l/ (US), /ˈmʌf(ə)l/ (UK)| v. | 抑制，消音，裹住| To make a sound quieter or less distinct; to wrap or cover for warmth or protection.|
She muffled her cough with her hand. 她用手捂住嘴咳嗽。

muffled:/ˈmʌfəld/ (US), /ˈmʌfəld/ (UK)| adj.| 沉闷的，低沉的| (of a sound) quiet and indistinct because it has been muffled.|
The muffled sound of the explosion came from far away.  远处传来沉闷的爆炸声。

近义词:stifling, suppressing, deadening, quieting, hushing
反义词:amplifying, intensifying, increasing

词性:
muffling: noun, adjective (present participle of muffle)
muffle: verb
muffled: adjective (past participle of muffle)

词源:
From Old French *moufler, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to moufle "mitten, muff".
