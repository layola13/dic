idealisms:/aɪˈdiːəlɪzəmz/
n.|理想主义|A system of thought that prioritizes ideals over practical considerations.|His idealisms led him to believe that world peace was achievable.|近义词: principles, beliefs, doctrines|反义词: pragmatism, realism, cynicism|词性:名词

Etymology:
From ideal + -ism.  "ideal" comes from Late Latin "idealis" meaning "existing in idea," from "idea."  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine, principle, etc.
