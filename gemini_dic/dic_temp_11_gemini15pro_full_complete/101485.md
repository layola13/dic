iridium:/ɪˈrɪdiəm/| n.|铱|A hard, brittle, silvery-white transition metal of the platinum group, iridium is the second densest metal (after osmium) and is highly resistant to corrosion. It is used in alloys, electrical contacts, and as a catalyst.|Iridium is used in the tips of some fountain pens.|Synonyms: none (as it's a specific element)|Antonyms: none (as it's a specific element)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Greek word *iris* meaning "rainbow," because its salts are highly colorful.
