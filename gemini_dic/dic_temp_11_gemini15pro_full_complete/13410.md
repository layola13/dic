atomized: /ˈætəˌmaɪzd/ | adj. | 原子化的，雾化的，分散的 | Reduced to atoms or a fine spray; divided into many small units or parts. |  The perfume was atomized into the air, filling the room with its scent. 香水被雾化到空气中，充满了整个房间。|  The company's workforce has become increasingly atomized, with more and more employees working remotely.  公司的员工队伍变得越来越分散，越来越多的员工远程工作。 | synonyms: fragmented, dispersed, scattered, separated, disintegrated | antonyms: unified, consolidated, integrated, centralized | adjective


atomize: /ˈætəˌmaɪz/ | v. |  原子化，雾化，分散 |  To reduce to atoms or a fine spray; to divide into many small units or parts. | The farmer atomized the pesticide over the crops. 农民将杀虫剂雾化喷洒在农作物上。 | Social media can atomize communities by creating echo chambers. 社交媒体可以通过创建回音壁来分散社区。 | synonyms: fragment, disperse, scatter, separate, disintegrate | antonyms: unify, consolidate, integrate, centralize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *atomiser*, from atome (“atom”) + -iser (suffix forming verbs).  The English word "atom" ultimately comes from the Ancient Greek ἄτομος (átomos, “indivisible”).
