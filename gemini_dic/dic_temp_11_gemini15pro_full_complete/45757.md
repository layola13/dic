cushion:/ˈkʊʃən/ (US), /ˈkʊʃn/ (UK)| n. v.|垫子，缓冲物；缓和，减轻|A soft bag filled with something such as feathers or foam, used for sitting on or resting against. To make the effect or severity of something less unpleasant.|She sat on a cushion on the floor. 她坐在地板上的一個墊子上。|Synonyms: pillow, pad, bolster|Antonyms: aggravation, intensification|noun

cushion:/ˈkʊʃən/ (US), /ˈkʊʃn/ (UK)| v. |缓和，减轻|To make the effect or severity of something less unpleasant.|The government announced measures to cushion the blow of rising fuel prices. 政府宣布了旨在緩解燃料價格上漲带来的冲击的措施。|Synonyms: soften, mitigate, alleviate|Antonyms: worsen, exacerbate, aggravate|verb
