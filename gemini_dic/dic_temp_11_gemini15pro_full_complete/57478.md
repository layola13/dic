draftsmanship: /ˈdræftsmənʃɪp/ | n. | 制图术；绘图技巧；草拟（文件）的技巧 | The skill of making drawings, plans, etc., or the quality of these drawings, plans, etc. |  The architect's draftsmanship was impeccable, capturing every detail with precision.  这位建筑师的绘图技巧无可挑剔，精确地捕捉了每一个细节。 |  draughting, drawing skill |  clumsiness, ineptitude | noun

Etymology:  From draftsman + -ship.  Draftsman comes from draft (meaning "a preliminary version of a piece of writing") + -sman (meaning "a person who does something").  Ultimately from the Old English *dragan "to draw, pull."
