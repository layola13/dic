broadcasts:/ˈbrɔːdkɑːsts/ (US), /ˈbrɔːdkɑːsts/ (UK)| n. v.|广播; 播送; 播放; 散布; 传播|A broadcast is a transmission of a radio or television programme.  A single instance of such a transmission. The act or process of transmitting such programmes.|The BBC broadcasts news throughout the world.  英国广播公司向全世界播送新闻。|synonyms: transmission, airing, program, announcement|antonyms: reception, suppression, concealment|noun

broadcasts:/ˈbrɔːdkɑːsts/ (US), /ˈbrɔːdkɑːsts/ (UK)| v.|广播; 播送; 播放; 散布; 传播|To transmit (a programme) by radio or television. To make widely known.|The game was broadcast live on ESPN. 这场比赛在 ESPN 上进行了现场直播。|synonyms: transmit, air, televise, disseminate|antonyms: suppress, conceal, withhold|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Old English words "brād" (wide) and "sāwan" (to sow), originally referring to scattering seeds widely.  The modern meaning emerged with the development of radio and television broadcasting.
