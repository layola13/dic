dispersion:/dɪˈspɜːrʒən/ |n.|分散，散布，弥散|The dispersion of seeds is important for plant reproduction.  种子的散布对植物繁殖很重要。|Example: The dispersion of light through a prism creates a rainbow. 光线通过棱镜的散射形成了彩虹。|Synonyms: scattering, dissemination, distribution, diffusion|Antonyms: concentration, gathering, accumulation|noun

dispersion:/dɪˈspɜːrʒən/ |n.|离差，差量|The statistician calculated the dispersion of the data. 统计学家计算了数据的离差。|Example:  A large dispersion of test scores indicates a wide range of abilities. 考试分数的较大离差表明能力范围很广。|Synonyms: variance, deviation, spread|Antonyms: uniformity, consistency|noun
