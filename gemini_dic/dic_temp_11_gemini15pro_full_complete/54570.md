disloyal:/dɪsˈlɔɪəl/
adjective
不忠的，背叛的
Not loyal; faithless.
She felt he had been disloyal to her by gossiping about their relationship. 她觉得他通过散布他们关系的流言蜚语背叛了她。
近义词: faithless, treacherous, unfaithful, perfidious
反义词: loyal, faithful, true, steadfast
词性: adjective

词源:  From dis- (expressing negation) + loyal (from Old French loial, from Latin legalis, "legal").  The prefix "dis-" negates the meaning of "loyal," creating the sense of "not loyal."
