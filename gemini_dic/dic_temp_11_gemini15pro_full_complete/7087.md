anesthetized:/əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪzd/ | adj. | 麻醉的；麻木的 | deprived of feeling or awareness, typically by the administration of an anesthetic. | The patient was anesthetized before the surgery. 病人在手术前被麻醉了。 | The area was anesthetized with a local anesthetic before the biopsy. 在活检前，该区域用局部麻醉剂进行了麻醉。 |  近义词: numb, insensible, unconscious | 反义词: conscious, aware, sensitive | 词性: adjective

anesthesia:/ˌænɪsˈθiːʒə/ | n. | 麻醉 | loss of feeling or awareness, especially of pain, induced by drugs or other agents. | The patient was given general anesthesia for the operation. 病人接受了全身麻醉以进行手术。 | Local anesthesia is often used for minor procedures. 局部麻醉通常用于小型手术。 | 近义词: narcosis | 反义词: sensation, awareness | 词性: noun

anesthetist:/əˈnɛsθətɪst/ | n. | 麻醉师 | a person trained to administer anesthetics. | The anesthetist carefully monitored the patient's vital signs during the surgery. 麻醉师在手术过程中仔细监测病人的生命体征。 | The anesthetist explained the risks of anesthesia to the patient. 麻醉师向病人解释了麻醉的风险。| 近义词: anesthesiologist | 反义词:  (no direct antonym) | 词性: noun

词源: From Modern Latin *anaesthetisatus, past participle of anaesthetizare, from Greek anaisthētos "without sensation," from an- "without" + aisthētos "perceptible," from aisthēsis "sensation."
