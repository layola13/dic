civilizing:/ˈsɪvɪˌlaɪzɪŋ/
adjective
使文明的；教化的；开化的
bringing culture and education to a society or group of people.
例句：The civilizing influence of education is undeniable. 教育的教化作用是不可否认的。

例句：The museum plays a civilizing role in the community.  博物馆在社区中起着教化的作用。

近义词：refining, cultivating, enlightening, educating
反义词：barbarizing, brutalizing, coarsening, degrading
词性: adjective


civilize:/ˈsɪvɪˌlaɪz/
verb
使文明；教化；开化
to bring a society or group of people to a more advanced or refined stage of social development.
例句：The Romans civilized many of the tribes they conquered. 罗马人教化了他们征服的许多部落。

例句：Education can help civilize a nation. 教育可以帮助一个国家走向文明。

近义词：refine, cultivate, enlighten, educate
反义词：barbarize, brutalize, coarsen, degrade
词性: verb

词源: From Late Middle English: from Old French *civiliser, from Latin civilis ‘relating to a citizen’ (see civil).
