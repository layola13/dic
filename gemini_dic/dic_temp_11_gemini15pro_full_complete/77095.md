garrote:/ɡəˈrɒt/ /ɡəˈrɑːt/| n. vt.|绞刑，勒死，用金属丝或绳索勒死|A thin wire or cord used for strangling someone.|The victim was garroted with a piano wire. 受害者被钢琴丝勒死了。|Synonyms: strangle, choke, asphyxiate|Antonyms: resuscitate, revive|noun, verb

garrote:/ɡəˈrɒt/ /ɡəˈrɑːt/| vt.|勒死，绞死|To strangle someone with a garrote.|He garroted the guard and escaped. 他勒死了警卫逃跑了。|Synonyms: strangle, choke, asphyxiate|Antonyms: resuscitate, revive|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *garrote* "cudgel, stick," from Vulgar Latin *garrotus*, of uncertain origin.
