dogmatical:/dɒɡˈmætɪkl/ /dɑːɡˈmætɪkl/
adjective
教条的；武断的
Asserting opinions in an arrogant manner; imperiously laying down principles as undeniably true.
He was highly dogmatic and intolerant of dissenting views. 他非常教条，不容忍异议。

He delivered his pronouncements in a dogmatic tone. 他以一种武断的语气发表了他的声明。

dogmatic:/dɒɡˈmætɪkl/ /dɑːɡˈmætɪkl/
adjective
教条主义的
Related to or expressing dogma, a set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true.
The party's dogmatic approach to economic policy alienated many voters. 该党在经济政策上的教条主义做法疏远了许多选民。

The sect's dogmatic beliefs were difficult to challenge. 该教派的教条信仰难以挑战。


dogmatic:/dɒɡˈmætɪkl/ /dɑːɡˈmætɪkl/
adjective
固执己见的
Stubbornly adhering to one's opinions, often without adequate justification.
Her dogmatic insistence on her own interpretation of events was frustrating. 她固执己见地坚持自己对事件的解释，令人沮丧。

He was too dogmatic to consider any other viewpoint. 他太固执己见，不愿考虑其他任何观点。

Synonyms: doctrinaire, opinionated, peremptory, authoritarian, dictatorial, assertive, insistent
Antonyms: open-minded, receptive, flexible, tolerant, inquiring, questioning


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dogmaticus, from Greek δογματικός (dogmatikos, “pertaining to doctrines”), from δόγμα (dogma, “opinion, tenet, doctrine”), from δοκεῖν (dokein, “to seem, think, imagine”).
