against one's grain:/əˈɡenst wʌnz ɡreɪn/
|idiomatic expression|违背意愿；格格不入；|Going against the grain means doing something that is contrary to one's natural inclinations or preferences.  It feels unnatural or difficult.|例句：It goes against the grain to have to work on a Sunday.  星期天还要工作，真让人不情愿。
|例句：Asking for help goes against the grain for him; he's always been fiercely independent.  寻求帮助与他的本性相悖；他一向非常独立。
|近义词：against one's nature, reluctantly, unwillingly|
|反义词：naturally, willingly, gladly|
|phrase|
