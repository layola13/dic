folk-rock:/ˈfoʊk rɑːk/ | n. |民谣摇滚|A genre of music that combines elements of folk music and rock music, typically featuring acoustic instruments, vocal harmonies, and lyrical themes of social or political commentary.| Bob Dylan's move to electric instruments is considered a pivotal moment in the development of folk-rock. 鲍勃·迪伦改用电子乐器被认为是民谣摇滚发展的一个关键时刻。

The Byrds were pioneers of the folk-rock sound in the 1960s.  伯兹乐队是20世纪60年代民谣摇滚的先驱。|近义词：folk-pop, roots rock|反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps genres like heavy metal or electronic music)|词性:noun
