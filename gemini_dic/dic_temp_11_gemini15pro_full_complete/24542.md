broadpiece:/ˈbrɔːdpiːs/
n.
大键琴；拨弦古钢琴
A keyboard musical instrument resembling a harpsichord but larger and with a richer tone, popular in the 16th to 18th centuries.
The broadpiece produced a louder and more resonant sound than the harpsichord. 这架大键琴比羽管键琴发出更响亮、更洪亮的声音。
Synonyms: harpsichord, clavichord, virginal
Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but could consider modern piano)
noun

Etymology:
From broad + piece, referring to the broad, flat shape of the soundboard. 源于 broad（宽阔的）+ piece（部件），指的是音板宽阔扁平的形状。
