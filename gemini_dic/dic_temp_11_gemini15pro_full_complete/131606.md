noncomplementary:/ˌnɒnkɒmplɪˈmentəri/ | /ˌnɑːnkɑːmplɪˈmentɛri/| adj. |非互补的，不互补的|Not complementary; not completing or making perfect; not supplying mutual lacks or offsetting mutual weaknesses. |The two strands of DNA are noncomplementary in this region, preventing proper base pairing.|近义词：incompatible, discordant, conflicting|反义词：complementary, harmonious, compatible|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From non- + complementary.  "Complementary" comes from the Late Latin complementarius, from complementum "that which fills up or completes,"  from complēre "to fill up," from com- "together" + plēre "to fill."
