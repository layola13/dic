clauser:/ˈklɔːzər/| n.|条款制定者；加入条款者|A person or entity that includes a clause or clauses in a contract or other legal document.  Someone who inserts or adds a clause. | John, the clauser of the contract, insisted on the inclusion of a non-compete clause.|近义词:author, drafter, stipulator|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

Etymology:  From the verb "clause" + "-er" (agent suffix).  "Clause" comes from Old French "clause" (close, conclusion), ultimately from Latin "clausus" (closed), past participle of "claudere" (to close).
