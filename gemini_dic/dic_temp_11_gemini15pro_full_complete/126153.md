mutoscope: /ˈmjuːtəˌskoʊp/ (US), /ˈmjuːtəˌskəʊp/ (UK)| n. | 活动电影放映机；西洋镜|An early motion-picture device, patented by Herman Casler in 1894, in which photographs are printed on flexible cards attached to a rotating core. The viewer looks through a lens while rotating the core and sees a short moving picture.|I saw an old mutoscope at the museum. 我在博物馆看到了一台古老的活动电影放映机。|Synonyms: kinetoscope, peep show|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
