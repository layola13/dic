carcassing:/ˈkɑːrkəsɪŋ/| n.|屠宰; 去脏; (屠宰后的)畜体; (尤指大型动物的)尸体|The process of butchering an animal to remove its internal organs and prepare it for consumption.  The carcass itself, especially of a large animal.|例句：The carcassing of the cow took place in the slaughterhouse.  牛的屠宰是在屠宰场进行的。

例句：The vultures circled above the carcassing in the desert. 秃鹫在沙漠中的动物尸体上盘旋。|近义词：butchering, dressing, slaughter, carcass|反义词: None|词性:Noun


carcass:/ˈkɑːrkəs/| n.| (屠宰后的)畜体; (尤指大型动物的)尸体|The dead body of an animal, especially a large one.|例句：The carcass of the deer was left for the scavengers. 鹿的尸体留给了食腐动物。

例句：They found the carcass of a whale washed up on the beach. 他们在海滩上发现了一具被冲上岸的鲸鱼尸体。|近义词：corpse, remains|反义词: None|词性:Noun
