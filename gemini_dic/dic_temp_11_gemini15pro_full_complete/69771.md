fermata:/fərˈmɑːtə/ | n. | 延长记号，休止符 | A symbol placed over a note or rest indicating that it is to be prolonged beyond its normal time value.  A fermata can also appear over a bar line, indicating a pause before proceeding to the next measure. | The conductor held the fermata for a dramatic effect. 指挥家延长了休止符以增强戏剧效果。 | The soprano held the high note under the fermata for what seemed like an eternity. 女高音在延长记号下保持了高音，感觉像永恒一样长。 |  pause, hold, prolongation |  continuation, shortening | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *fermata*, from *fermare* "to stop," from Late Latin *firmare* "to strengthen, make firm," from Latin *firmus* "firm, strong."
