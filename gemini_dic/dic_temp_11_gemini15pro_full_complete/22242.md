bondholder:/ˈbɑːndˌhoʊldər/ | n. | 债券持有人 | A person or entity that owns a bond issued by a government, corporation, or other entity. | Example 1: Bondholders are entitled to receive interest payments and the return of principal at maturity.  债券持有人有权获得利息支付，并在到期时收回本金。

Example 2: The company's bondholders were worried about the potential for default. 该公司的债券持有人担心可能出现违约。 | Synonym: creditor, investor | Antonym: debtor, issuer | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From bond + holder. "Bond" comes from Old English "bindan" (to bind), referring to a legal agreement.  "Holder" comes from Old English "haldan" (to hold, keep).
