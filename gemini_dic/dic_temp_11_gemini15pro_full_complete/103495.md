jockeylike: /ˈdʒɒkɪˌlaɪk/ | adj. | 像骑师的；精明的；机敏的 | Resembling or characteristic of a jockey, especially in terms of skill, agility, or competitiveness.  Often implies a shrewd or cunning quality. | He had a jockeylike ability to maneuver his business through tough competition. 他有着像骑师一样精明的能力，能够在激烈的竞争中驾驭自己的生意。 | 近义词: agile, deft, shrewd, cunning, astute | 反义词: clumsy, awkward, naive, guileless | 词性: 形容词

jockey: /ˈdʒɒki/ (noun) a person who rides horses in races, especially as a profession.  From the diminutive of the given name Jock, a Scottish form of John.  The association with horse racing developed in the 16th century.

like: /laɪk/ (suffix) forming adjectives and adverbs and meaning ‘similar to’, ‘characteristic of’, ‘in the manner of’. From Old English *līc.
