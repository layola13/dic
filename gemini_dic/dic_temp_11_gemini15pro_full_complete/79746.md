glumly:/ˈɡlʌmli/| adv.|闷闷不乐地，忧郁地|In a glum or dejected manner.  Characterized by or expressing gloom.|He stared glumly out the window. 他闷闷不乐地盯着窗外。|Synonyms: dejectedly, gloomily, sadly, morosely|Antonyms: cheerfully, happily, joyfully|adverb

Etymology:
From glum + -ly.  "Glum" comes from the Middle English  "glombe" or "gloumben," meaning "to frown" or "look sullen," likely of Scandinavian origin.
