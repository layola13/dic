edwardianisms:/ˌɛdwɔːdiənɪzəmz/
|n.|爱德华时代风格；爱德华时代特征；爱德华时代的人或事|Characteristics or features typical of the Edwardian era (1901-1910), often referring to social attitudes, fashions, or artistic styles.|The play was full of amusing Edwardianisms, like the elaborate hats and the formal language.  这出戏充满了有趣的爱德华时代风格，比如精致的帽子和正式的语言。|近义词：Edwardian characteristics, Edwardian traits|反义词:modernisms, contemporary styles|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Edward + -ian + -ism.  Edward refers to King Edward VII of the United Kingdom, who reigned from 1901 to 1910. The suffix -ian denotes "pertaining to" or "characteristic of," while -ism signifies a doctrine, belief, or practice.
