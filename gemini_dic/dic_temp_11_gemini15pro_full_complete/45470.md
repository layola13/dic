curatrix: /kjʊəˈreɪtrɪks/ | n. | 女监护人，女管理人 | A woman appointed by a court to manage the affairs of another person deemed incapable of managing them. |  She was appointed curatrix of her ailing father's estate. 她被任命为她生病父亲的遗产管理人。

The court named her as curatrix for her mentally incapacitated brother. 法院指定她为她精神失常的兄弟的监护人。 | 近义词: guardian, trustee, conservator | 反义词: ward | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *curātrīx*, feminine form of *curātor* ("caretaker, guardian"), from *cūrāre* ("to take care of"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *\*ḱeyr-* ("heart, mind").
