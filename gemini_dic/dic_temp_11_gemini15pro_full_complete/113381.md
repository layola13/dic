lycopodium:/ˌlaɪkəˈpoʊdiəm/| n. |石松，石松粉|A fine, flammable powder, consisting of the spores of club mosses (Lycopodiales), formerly used in flash photography and in medicine.|The magician used lycopodium to create a dramatic flash of fire.|Synonyms: club moss powder, vegetable sulfur|Antonyms: N/A|noun


lycopodium:/ˌlaɪkəˈpoʊdiəm/| n. |石松属植物|Any of numerous vascular plants of the genus Lycopodium, having branching stems covered with small, overlapping leaves and reproducing by spores.|The forest floor was covered with various species of lycopodium.|Synonyms: club moss|Antonyms: N/A|noun
