indan:/ˈɪndæn/
noun|茚满|A colorless, flammable liquid hydrocarbon, C9H8, derived from coal tar and used in the manufacture of synthetic resins.|Indan is a bicyclic organic compound.  It is made up of a benzene ring fused to a cyclopentane ring.|Indan derivatives are sometimes used in the production of certain plastics.|Synonym: hydrindene|Antonym: N/A|n.
