co-opting:/kəʊˈɒptɪŋ/ | v. | 吸收；拉拢；借用 |  The process of absorbing or assimilating something into a larger system or group, often without the original intent or purpose. Can also mean to enlist someone's support or participation, often in a way that exploits or manipulates them. | The company co-opted the open-source software into their commercial product.  公司将开源软件吸收到他们的商业产品中。

The cult leader co-opted the vulnerable young woman into his organization.  邪教头目拉拢了那个脆弱的年轻女子加入他的组织。 | 近义词: appropriating, adopting, incorporating, enlisting | 反义词: rejecting, excluding, releasing | 词性: 动词现在分词

词源: From the Latin "co-optare," meaning "to choose together," from "co-" (together) and "optare" (to choose).
