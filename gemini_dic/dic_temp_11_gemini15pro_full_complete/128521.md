newts:/njuːts/ /nuːts/ | n. |蝾螈|A small semiaquatic amphibian with a soft, moist skin, typically spending the breeding season in the water.|例句：Newts often return to the same pond each year to breed. 蝾螈每年都会回到同一个池塘繁殖。

例句：The children were fascinated by the newts swimming in the pond. 孩子们被池塘里游动的蝾螈迷住了。|近义词：salamander, eft|反义词: None|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *newte, from Old English *ēawte, ultimately from a pre-Germanic source.  Related to German *Molch (whence modern German Molch). The initial "n" arose from the indefinite article "an" before a vowel sound.
