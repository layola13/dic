displant:/dɪsˈplænt/ |v. | 移栽; 移植; 使离开家园; 使背井离乡 | To remove (a plant) from the ground or from a pot. To force (someone) to leave their home or country. | The refugees were displanted by the war. 这些难民因战争而背井离乡。 The gardener carefully displanted the rose bush. 园丁小心翼翼地将玫瑰丛移栽。 | 近义词: uproot, displace, relocate, transplant | 反义词: plant, settle, establish | 词性: 动词

displant:/dɪsˈplænt/ |n. | (被移栽的)植物; 背井离乡的人 | A plant that has been removed from its original location. A person who has been forced to leave their home or country. |  The displants struggled to adapt to the new climate. 这些被移栽的植物难以适应新的气候。 The displants longed for their homeland. 这些背井离乡的人渴望回到故土。 | 近义词: refugee, exile, transplant | 反义词: native, resident | 词性: 名词
