nonsubtler:/nɒnˈsʌtlər/ | adj. | 不那么微妙的，更明显的 | Not subtle; more obvious or blatant.  Derived from "non-" (not) + "subtler" (comparative form of "subtle"). "Subtle" comes from Old French "sotil," ultimately from Latin "subtilis" meaning "finely woven," hence "fine," "delicate," "acute." |  Her nonsubtler attempts at flirting were embarrassing to watch. 她不太微妙的调情举动让人尴尬。| synonyms: obvious, blatant, overt, transparent, unconcealed, conspicuous, pronounced | antonyms: subtle, nuanced, understated, delicate, implicit, inconspicuous | word type: adjective
