liquidized: /ˈlɪkwɪˌdaɪzd/ | adj. v. | 清算的，变现的; 液化的 |  Converted into cash; made into a liquid. | The company was liquidated after it went bankrupt.  公司破产后被清算了。 |  The ice cream had completely liquidated in the heat. 冰淇淋在高温下完全融化了。 | Synonyms: dissolved, melted, cashed in, realized | Antonyms: frozen, solidified, invested | verb/adjective

liquidized: /ˈlɪkwɪˌdaɪzd/ |  v. |  清算，变现；清除，消灭（尤指秘密处决）; 液化 | To convert assets into cash; to eliminate, especially by killing. | They liquidated their assets to pay off their debts. 他们清算了资产以偿还债务。 | The dictator liquidated his political opponents. 独裁者清除了他的政治对手。 |  Synonyms: eliminate, kill, dissolve, melt | Antonyms: save, protect, solidify, freeze | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin liquidatus, past participle of liquidare "to make liquid," from Latin liquidus "liquid."
