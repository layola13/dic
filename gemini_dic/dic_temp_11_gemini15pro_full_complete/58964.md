duststorms:/ˈdʌstˌstɔːrmz/ | n. | 沙尘暴 | A strong wind that carries clouds of dust over a large area, especially in a desert. |  Duststorms are common in arid regions. 沙尘暴在干旱地区很常见。 | The duststorms reduced visibility to near zero. 沙尘暴使能见度降低到接近零。 | 近义词: sandstorms, haboobs | 反义词:  (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑用calm weather, clear skies) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From dust + storm.  "Dust" comes from Old English *dust, from Proto-Germanic *dunstaz. "Storm" comes from Old English storm, from Proto-Germanic *sturmaz, meaning "tumult, uproar."
