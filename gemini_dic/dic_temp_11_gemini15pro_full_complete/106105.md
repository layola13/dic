词源:
From Middle English *knight, *knyght, *kniht, from Old English cniht (“boy, youth, servant, attendant, disciple, warrior, military follower”), from Proto-Germanic *kniχtiz (“boy, servant”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kneh₁- (“to stick, adhere, belong to”). Cognate with Scots knychtis (“knights”), West Frisian knycht (“knight”), Dutch knecht (“servant, farmhand”), German Knecht (“servant, farmhand”), Icelandic knihtur (“knight”), Norwegian knekt (“knave, jack”), Danish knægt (“boy, lad”).


注意事项:
knight 既可以指中世纪的骑士，也可以指现代英国的爵士。作为动词，knight 指的是册封某人为骑士或爵士。
