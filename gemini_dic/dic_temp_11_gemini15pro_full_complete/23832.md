bread-butters:/ˈbredˌbʌtərz/| n.|涂黄油的人；抹黄油的工具|A person or utensil that butters bread.|My kids are good bread-butters, they never make a mess. 我的孩子们很擅长涂黄油，他们从来不会弄得一团糟。|近义词: butter spreaders, butter knives |反义词: None|Noun

Etymology: Compound word from "bread" and "butter" + "-er" (agent suffix)
"Bread" comes from Old English "bread."
"Butter" comes from Old English "butere," from Latin "butyrum," from Greek "boutyron."
"-er" indicates a person or thing that performs an action.
