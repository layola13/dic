hand-to-mouth:/ˌhænd tə ˈmaʊθ/| adj.|寅吃卯粮的，勉强糊口的|Living in a hand-to-mouth way means having just enough money to buy food and other basic needs. |They lived a hand-to-mouth existence, never knowing where their next meal was coming from. 他们过着勉强糊口的日子，不知道下一顿饭在哪里。|近义词：precarious, impoverished, struggling|反义词：wealthy, affluent, prosperous|词性:形容词

Etymology:
This idiom originated in the late 16th century, literally describing the act of putting food directly from one's hand into one's mouth, implying a lack of resources to store or prepare food properly.  Over time, the meaning evolved to encompass a broader sense of financial insecurity.
