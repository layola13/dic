gypping:/ˈdʒɪpɪŋ/ | n. v. (gerund) adj. (informal, offensive) | 欺骗，敲诈，诈骗 | Cheating, swindling, or defrauding someone, especially by charging excessive prices or providing inferior goods or services.  This term is considered offensive due to its etymological association with the Romani people. | Example: I felt like I was gypping myself by not taking the deal. 我觉得不接受这笔交易就是亏待自己。| Example:  They accused the car dealer of gypping them on the price. 他们指责汽车经销商在价格上欺骗了他们。 | Synonym: cheating, swindling, defrauding, conning, ripping off | Antonym: honesty, fairness, integrity | Etymology:  Derives from "gypsy," a derogatory term for the Romani people, based on the false stereotype that they were prone to dishonesty.  Using this term perpetuates harmful stereotypes. | Word Type: noun (as gerund), verb (as gerund), adjective (informal, offensive)
