coifed:/kwɑːft/ | adj. |  梳理过的，做過髮型的 | Having a particular hairstyle, usually an elaborate one.  | Her hair was perfectly coifed for the wedding. 她的头发为婚礼做了完美的造型。 |  styled, arranged, groomed | unkempt, messy | adjective

coif:/kwɔɪf/ | n. |  头巾，发网；发型 | A close-fitting cap or hood worn under a veil; a hairstyle. | The judge wore a traditional coif.  法官戴着传统的头巾。| hairstyle, hairdo, headdress | dishevelment, mess | noun

coif:/kwɔɪf/ | v. |  梳理（头发），做（头发） | To style or arrange (someone's hair), typically in an elaborate way. | The stylist coifed her hair into an intricate updo.  造型师把她的头发梳成一个复杂的发髻。 | style, arrange, dress |  dishevel, mess up | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *coife, from Late Latin cofia "cap," of uncertain origin.  The verb sense developed later from the noun.
