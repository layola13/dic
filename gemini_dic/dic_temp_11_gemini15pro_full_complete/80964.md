graces:/ˈɡreɪsɪz/
n.
优雅,魅力;优美;恩惠,慈悲;饭前或饭后的感恩祈祷
(usually in singular) A charming or attractive trait.
She moves with grace and elegance. 她举止优雅。

graces:/ˈɡreɪsɪz/
n.
(plural) Grace's belongings.
These are Grace's graces. 这些是格雷斯的物品。

graces:/ˈɡreɪsɪz/
v.
(third-person singular simple present graces, present participle gracing, simple past and past participle graced)
使优美；使增光添彩；装饰
To make something more attractive or beautiful.
Her presence graced the occasion. 她的出席为这场活动增添了光彩。


Etymology:  From Middle English *graces, plural of grace, from Old French grace, from Latin gratia (“grace, favor, thanks”).
