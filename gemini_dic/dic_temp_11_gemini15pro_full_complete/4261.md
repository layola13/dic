airsicknesses:/ˈɛərˌsɪknɪsɪz/| n. |晕机; airsickness的复数|Nausea and vomiting induced by air travel.  It's caused by a conflict between the body's sense of balance and what the eyes see.|Many people experience airsicknesses on their first flight. 很多人第一次坐飞机都会晕机。|近义词：motion sickness, travel sickness|反义词: None|词性: Noun


Etymology:
air + sickness

air (n.): From Middle English *air, eir, from Old French air, eir (“air, atmosphere, breeze, wind”), from Latin āēr (“air, atmosphere; ether; breeze”), from Ancient Greek ἀήρ (aḗr, “air, lower atmosphere; mist, darkness”).

sickness (n.): From Middle English siknesse, seknesse, siknes, sekenes, from Old English sēocness (“sickness, illness, disease”), equivalent to sick +‎ -ness.
