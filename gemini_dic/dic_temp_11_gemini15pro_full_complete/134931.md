nonmutually:/nɒnˈmjuːtʃuəli/ | adv. | 非相互地，不相互地 | Not mutually; in a way that is not shared or reciprocal. | The two events impacted nonmutually on each other. 这两个事件相互之间没有影响。 |  The two teams competed nonmutually, each focused on their own performance.  这两个队非相互竞争，每个队都专注于自己的表现。 | 近义词: independently, separately, unilaterally | 反义词: mutually, reciprocally | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  from Latin *non < Indo-European *ne "not"
mutually: from mutual + -ly. Mutual comes from Old French mutuel, from Late Latin mūtuālis "reciprocal," from Latin mūtuus "lent, borrowed, reciprocal, mutual," from PIE root *mei- (1) "to exchange, to give."
-ly (suffix): forms adverbs from adjectives
