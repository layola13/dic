epidural:/ˌɛpɪˈdjʊərəl/| adj. n.|硬膜外的；硬膜外麻醉|An epidural is a type of regional anesthesia that blocks pain in a particular region of the body. 词源: From epi- + dura mater + -al.  "Epi-" means upon or over, "dura mater" refers to the tough outer membrane surrounding the spinal cord, and "-al" forms an adjective.  Therefore, "epidural" literally means "upon the dura mater".
