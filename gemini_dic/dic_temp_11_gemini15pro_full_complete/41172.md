copycatting:/ˈkɒpiˌkætɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpiˌkætɪŋ/ (US)
n.|模仿;抄袭;鹦鹉学舌|The act of copying someone else's actions, words, or ideas. |She was accused of copycatting her competitor's marketing campaign.| Synonyms: imitating, mimicking, emulating, aping | Antonyms: originating, innovating, pioneering | noun

copycat:/ˈkɒpiˌkæt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpiˌkæt/ (US)
n. |模仿者;抄袭者|A person who copies another's behavior, work, etc. |He's just a copycat, he has no original ideas of his own.|Synonyms: imitator, mimic |Antonyms: innovator, originator | noun
adj. |模仿的;抄袭的|Made or done in imitation of something else. |The police suspect it was a copycat crime.| Synonyms: imitative, unoriginal | Antonyms: original, innovative | adjective
