chock:/tʃɑːk/ (US) /tʃɒk/ (UK)| n. v.|楔子，块，制动，塞满，阻碍|A wedge or block placed against a wheel or other rolling object to prevent it from moving.  Also, to fill completely or overcrowd.|例句：He chocked the wheels of the car. 他用楔子塞住了汽车的轮子。

例句：The harbor was chock full of boats. 港口停满了船。|近义词：wedge, block, cram, jam, fill|反义词:empty, clear|词性:名词, 动词

chock-a-block:/ˌtʃɑːk ə ˈblɑːk/ (US) /ˌtʃɒk ə ˈblɒk/ (UK)| adj.|塞满了的，拥挤不堪的|Completely full or jammed.|例句：The streets were chock-a-block with traffic. 街上交通拥堵。

例句：The room was chock-a-block with furniture. 房间里塞满了家具。|近义词：crammed, packed, jammed|反义词:empty, spacious|词性:形容词

chock-full:/ˌtʃɑːkˈfʊl/ (US) /ˌtʃɒkˈfʊl/ (UK)| adj.|充满的，塞满了的|Completely full.|例句：The bag was chock-full of candy. 袋子里装满了糖果。

例句：The stadium was chock-full of cheering fans. 体育场挤满了欢呼的球迷。|近义词：crammed, packed, overflowing|反义词:empty, vacant|词性:形容词
