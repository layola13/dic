billot:/ˈbɪlət/| n. |短木桩，木料，坯料|A short, thick piece of wood, especially one used for fuel or as a support.|The blacksmith hammered the billot into shape. 铁匠把短木桩锤打成形。|Synonyms: block, chunk, log|Antonyms: sliver, splinter|noun

billot:/ˈbɪlət/| n. |（金属）坯，锭|A piece of metal, especially steel, that has been cast into a shape suitable for further processing.|The steel billots were transported to the rolling mill. 钢坯被运送到轧钢厂。|Synonyms: ingot, bar, slab|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *bilot*, diminutive of bille (“log”), from Gaulish *billios* (“tree trunk”), from Proto-Celtic *billis* (“log, trunk”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰel- (“to swell, bloom”). Cognate with Old Irish bile (“tree”).
