mirales: /miˈrɑːles/ (European Spanish Pronunciation)| pron.| 看看他们 (Look at them)| "Mirales" is the imperative form of the Spanish verb "mirar" (to look) conjugated for the "vosotros" form, meaning "you all" or "y'all" in informal plural. It is used to tell a group of people to look at something. | ¡Mirales! ¡Son tan lindos! (Look at them! They are so cute!) | 近义词: véanlos (more formal)| 反义词: N/A | pronoun

注意事项:

"Mirales" is specific to European Spanish, primarily used in Spain.  In Latin America, the "vosotros" form is generally not used.  The equivalent would be "mírenlos."


The imperative mood is used to give commands or instructions.  Therefore, tense usage examples are not applicable in the same way as with other verb forms.


The provided examples demonstrate how "mirales" can be used in different contexts to convey various nuances of meaning.
