insubmergible:/ˌɪnsʌbˈmɜːrdʒəbl/ | adj. | 不可沉没的；不能淹没的 | Not able to be sunk or submerged. | The Titanic was thought to be insubmergible, but tragically, it sank on its maiden voyage.  泰坦尼克号曾被认为是永不沉没的，但不幸的是，它在处女航中沉没了。

The submarine is designed to be insubmergible even at great depths. 这艘潜艇被设计成即使在很深的地方也能保持不沉。| 近义词: unsinkable, buoyant | 反义词: submergible, sinkable | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + submergible (able to be submerged).  The root "submerge" comes from the Latin "submergere," meaning "to dip under," from "sub" (under) + "mergere" (to plunge).
