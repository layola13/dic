gorgon:/ˈɡɔːrɡən/ | n. |蛇发女妖；丑陋可怕的女人；令人极不愉快的人或事物 |A mythical creature, typically female, with snakes for hair, whose gaze turns people to stone; a fierce or formidable woman; something that is extremely unpleasant or disagreeable. |例句：Medusa was the most famous Gorgon. 美杜莎是最著名的蛇发女妖。

例句：She turned into a real gorgon when she found out he had lied. 当她发现他撒谎时，她变成了一个真正的泼妇。 |近义词：monster, hag, shrew, harridan |反义词：beauty, angel |词性：名词


gorgon:/ˈɡɔːrɡən/ | adj. |(literary) 令人恐惧的, 僵硬的 |Causing fear or horror; stiff or rigid. |例句：A gorgon stare froze him in his tracks.  一个可怕的凝视让他僵在原地。

例句：The gorgon face of the statue frightened the children. 雕像可怕的面孔吓坏了孩子们。 |近义词：terrifying, frightening, petrifying |反义词：calming, soothing |词性：形容词
