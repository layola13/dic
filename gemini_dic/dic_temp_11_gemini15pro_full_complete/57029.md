doublespeaker:/ˈdʌblˌspiːkər/| n.|双重语言使用者；使用模棱两可语言的人|A person who uses language deliberately to disguise, distort, or reverse the meaning of words.|The politician was accused of being a doublespeaker, using euphemisms to hide the true cost of the war.|Synonym: propagandist, prevaricator, equivocator|Antonym: straight talker, truth-teller|noun

doublespeak:/ˈdʌblˌspiːk/| n.|双重语言；模棱两可的语言|Language deliberately used to disguise, distort, or reverse the meaning of words.|The government's doublespeak about the economic crisis fooled no one.|Synonym: jargon, gobbledygook, obfuscation|Antonym: plain language, clarity|noun
