bock: /bɑːk/ | n. | 博克啤酒; 雄鹿; 公羊; (尤指羊) 撞; | A strong dark German beer, traditionally brewed in the winter and drunk in the spring.  A male goat or ibex. |例句：I enjoyed a refreshing bock beer on a warm spring day.  我在温暖的春日里享用了一杯清爽的博克啤酒。

例句：The bock charged at the fence. 公羊猛冲围栏。| 近义词：lager (for beer), billy goat, ram | 反义词：ewe (for animal), doe | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German Bockbier, literally 'goat beer', perhaps referring to its strength, or from Einbeck, a town in Lower Saxony, Germany, known for its beer. The animal meaning comes from Middle High German boc, from Old High German boc(h).
