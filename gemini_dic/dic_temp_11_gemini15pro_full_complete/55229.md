dissidently:/dɪˈsɪdəntli/ | adv. |持不同政见地，异议地 | In a manner that expresses disagreement with established political or religious beliefs or systems. |  He dissidently refused to conform to the party line. 他持不同政见地拒绝遵从党的路线。|  She dissidently voiced her opposition to the new policy. 她持不同政见地表达了她对新政策的反对。| 近义词: rebelliously, defiantly, unconventionally | 反义词: conformingly, obediently, submissively | 词性: 副词

dissident:/ˈdɪsɪdənt/ | n. |持不同政见者 | A person who publicly disagrees with and criticizes their government. | The government imprisoned several dissidents. 政府囚禁了几名持不同政见者。|  Many dissidents were forced into exile. 许多持不同政见者被迫流亡。| 近义词: rebel, protester, nonconformist | 反义词: conformist, loyalist | 词性: 名词

dissent:/dɪˈsent/ | n. vi. |异议，不同意 |  The holding or expression of opinions at variance with those commonly or officially held. | Two members dissented from the majority decision. 两名成员对多数决定表示异议。|  There was widespread dissent within the party over the new leader. 党内对新领导人存在广泛的异议。 | 近义词: disagreement, opposition, objection | 反义词: agreement, consent, approval | 词性: 名词，动词

词源: From Latin dissentire "to disagree," from dis- "apart" + sentire "to feel."
