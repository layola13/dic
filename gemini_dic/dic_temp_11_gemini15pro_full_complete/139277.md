oblique:/əˈblɪːk/ (US), /əˈbliːk/ (UK)| adj.|倾斜的，间接的，不坦率的|Neither perpendicular nor parallel to a given line or surface; slanting; not straightforward or direct.|The painting featured a series of oblique lines. 这幅画的特点是一系列倾斜的线条。|Synonyms: slanted, diagonal, indirect, evasive|Antonyms: straight, direct, forthright|adjective

obliqueness:/əˈblɪːknəs/ (US), /əˈbliːknɪs/ (UK)| n.|倾斜，间接，不坦率|The state of being oblique; deviation from a straight line or course; indirectness.|The obliqueness of his answers made her suspicious. 他回答的含糊其辞让她起了疑心。|Synonyms: slant, indirectness, evasiveness|Antonyms: directness, straightforwardness|noun

obliquely:/əˈblɪːkli/ (US), /əˈbliːkli/ (UK)| adv.|倾斜地，间接地，不坦率地|In an oblique manner; not directly or straightforwardly.|He looked at her obliquely. 他斜眼看着她。|Synonyms: slantingly, diagonally, indirectly, evasively|Antonyms: straight, directly, forthrightly|adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *obliquus* "slanting, sideways," from *obliquare* "to slant," from *ob* "toward" + *liquis* "slanting."
