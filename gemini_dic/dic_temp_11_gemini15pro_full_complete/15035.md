babuism: /ˈbɑːbuːɪzəm/ | n. | 巴布主义；过分崇洋媚外的行为 |  The excessive mimicking or adoption of Western, especially British, manners, customs, and language by Indians, often seen as a sign of subservience or misplaced aspiration during and after the British Raj. |  His babuism was evident in his affected accent and constant quoting of Shakespeare. 他的巴布主义体现在他做作的口音和不断引用莎士比亚的语录上。 | 近义词: Anglomania, mimicry | 反义词: nationalism, traditionalism | 词性: 名词
