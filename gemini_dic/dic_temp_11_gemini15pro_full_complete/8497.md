antibody:/ˈæntibaːdi/ (US), /ˈæntibɒdi/ (UK)| n. |抗体|A blood protein produced in response to and counteracting a specific antigen. Antibodies combine chemically with substances which the body recognizes as alien, such as bacteria, viruses, and foreign substances in the blood.|例句：Antibodies help our bodies fight off infections. 抗体帮助我们的身体抵抗感染。

例句：The vaccine stimulates the production of antibodies. 疫苗刺激抗体的产生。

|近义词：immunoglobulin|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider "antigen")|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Antikörper*, from *anti-* (against) + *Körper* (body). Coined by Paul Ehrlich.
