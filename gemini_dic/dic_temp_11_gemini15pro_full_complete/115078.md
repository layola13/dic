maneuvered:/məˈnuːvərd/ (US), /məˈnuːvəd/ (UK)| v. |操纵，调遣；巧妙地移动|To guide or move skillfully or carefully, especially in a difficult or complex situation. |The captain skillfully maneuvered the ship through the narrow channel. 船长熟练地驾驶着船只通过狭窄的海峡。|The lawyer maneuvered his client into a favorable plea bargain. 律师巧妙地使他的客户达成了有利的认罪协议。| synonyms: navigate, manipulate, steer, guide, pilot| antonyms: neglect, abandon, drift|verb

maneuver:/məˈnuːvər/ (US), /məˈnuːvə(r)/ (UK)| n. |策略，调动；移动|A planned and controlled movement or series of moves.|The troops carried out a series of complex maneuvers. 部队进行了一系列复杂的军事演习。|His resignation was a clever political maneuver. 他的辞职是一个巧妙的政治策略。| synonyms: tactic, strategy, operation, movement, ploy| antonyms: inaction, stillness, stagnation|noun
