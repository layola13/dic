glimpser:/ˈɡlɪmpsər/| n.|瞥见者；一瞥者|Someone who glimpses something; one who catches a brief or incomplete view.|I was a mere glimpser of the event, seeing only a small part of it. 我只是活动的一个匆匆过客，只看到了其中一小部分。|近义词：observer, onlooker, witness|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

Etymology->
From glimpse + -er.  "Glimpse" comes from Middle English *glimsen, frequentative of *glims, related to gleam.  The suffix "-er" forms agent nouns.
