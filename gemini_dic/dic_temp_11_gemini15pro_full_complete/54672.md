disoccupy:/ˌdɪsˈɑːkjupaɪ/ /ˌdɪsˈɒkjupaɪ/ | vt. |使闲暇，使失业，腾出，空出|To cause (someone) to be unemployed; to leave (a place, such as a building or room) empty or unoccupied.| The factory closure will disoccupy hundreds of workers. 工厂关闭将使数百名工人失业。| The tenants were asked to disoccupy the apartment by the end of the month. 租户被要求在月底腾出公寓。|synonyms: vacate, evacuate, empty| antonyms: occupy, inhabit, fill |verb
