hyperconformity:/ˌhaɪpərˈkənˈfɔːrməti/| n.|过度从众，过度遵从|The tendency to conform to a greater degree than is typical or expected.  This often involves suppressing individual thoughts and behaviors in favor of fitting in with a group, even if it goes against personal beliefs or values. |The pressure to achieve perfect grades led to hyperconformity among the students. 压力要取得好成绩导致学生过度从众。|近义词：overconformity, compliance, obedience|反义词：nonconformity, individuality, rebellion|词性:noun

Etymology:
From hyper- "excessive" + conformity.  The prefix "hyper-" comes from Greek "hyper" meaning "over, beyond." "Conformity" comes from the Late Latin "conformitas," meaning "similarity, resemblance," from "conformis"  "similar in form."
