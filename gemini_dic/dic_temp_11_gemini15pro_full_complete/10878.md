arachnids:/əˈræknɪdz/| n. |蛛形纲动物|A class of joint-legged invertebrate animals (arthropods) that includes spiders, scorpions, mites, and ticks. They are characterized by having eight legs, two body sections (cephalothorax and abdomen), and no antennae.|Spiders, scorpions, mites, and ticks are all arachnids. 蜘蛛、蝎子、螨虫和蜱虫都是蛛形纲动物。|Synonyms: arachnoids|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a taxonomic class)|Noun
