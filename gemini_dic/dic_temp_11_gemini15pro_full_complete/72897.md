follow-on:/ˈfɒləʊɒn/ (UK) /ˈfɑːloʊɑːn/ (US)| adj. n.|后续的，接下来的；后续行动，后续事件|Something that is a follow-on happens or is done after something similar has already happened or been done. |The band's follow-on album was even better than their first.  这支乐队的后续专辑甚至比他们的第一张还要好。|
follow-on:/ˈfɒləʊɒn/ (UK) /ˈfɑːloʊɑːn/ (US)| adj. n.|后续的，接下来的；后续行动，后续事件|This funding is designed to support follow-on research. 这笔资金旨在支持后续研究。|近义词：subsequent, succeeding, ensuing|反义词: initial, preceding, original|词性: adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrasal verb "follow on," meaning to continue after something else has finished.  The noun form emerged later to describe something that follows on from a previous event or action.
