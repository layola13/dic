choler: /ˈkɑːlər/ (US) /ˈkɒlə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 愤怒；易怒；胆汁质 | Anger or irritability, especially as a supposed humour formerly believed to cause such emotions. |  He was a man of impetuous choler. 他是一个性情暴躁的人。|Synonyms: anger, rage, fury, wrath, ire, indignation, pique | Antonyms: calmness, tranquility, peacefulness | noun

choleric: /ˈkɑːlərɪk/ (US) /kəˈlɛrɪk/ (UK)| adj.| 易怒的；暴躁的 | Bad-tempered or irritable. | His choleric temperament led to frequent arguments. 他暴躁的脾气导致经常争吵。| Synonyms: bad-tempered, irascible, irritable, cantankerous, grumpy, surly, testy | Antonyms: good-natured, affable, amiable, even-tempered | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *colre*, from Old French *colere*, from Latin *cholera*, from Ancient Greek *kholéra* (χολέρα, “bile”), from *kholḗ* (χολή, “bile, gall”). The sense of "bile" is now obsolete except in medical contexts.
