bacchante:/bəˈkæntiː/ /bəˈkɑːntiː/
n.
狂女，女祭司；(常指古希腊罗马神话中)酒神巴克斯的女祭司或信徒
A female devotee of Bacchus; a woman who engages in unrestrained revelry.
The bacchante danced wildly, lost in the ecstatic frenzy of the festival.  狂女疯狂地跳着舞，沉浸在节日狂欢的氛围中。
The painting depicted a group of bacchantes reveling in a vineyard.  这幅画描绘了一群狂女在葡萄园里狂欢的景象。
Synonyms: maenad, reveler, partygoer
Antonyms: prude, ascetic
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French bacchante, from Latin Bacchans, from Bacchus, the Roman god of wine.
