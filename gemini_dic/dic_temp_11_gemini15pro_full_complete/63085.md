ensconcing: /ɪnˈskɒnsɪŋ/ /ɛnˈskɑːnsɪŋ/
verb
安置；使…舒适地坐定
settling comfortably and securely in position; establishing or settling (someone) in a comfortable, safe, or secret place
例句：She ensconced herself in a comfortable armchair with a good book. 她舒舒服服地坐在扶手椅上，拿着一本好书。
例句：He ensconced himself in the corner booth, away from the noise. 他把自己安置在角落的座位上，远离喧嚣。
近义词：settle, nestle, install, establish
反义词：dislodge, remove, displace
词性：v.

PHRASE->
ensconcing oneself in =  让自己沉浸在...

词源: From French *ensconcer*, from en- + sconce (“fortress, bulwark”)  (ultimately from Dutch schans ("earthwork, fortification")).  Meaning shifted from "to hide in a fortified place" to "to settle securely".
