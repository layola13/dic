discretionarily:/dɪˈskreʃəˈnɛrəli/
adverb|酌情地；自由裁量地|In a way that uses discretion; at one's discretion.  Using your own judgment about what to do in a particular situation.|The funds were distributed discretionarily by the committee. 委员会酌情分配了资金。|Synonyms: judiciously, prudently, cautiously, at one's discretion, as one sees fit|Antonyms: indiscriminately, arbitrarily, without discretion|词性:副词

discretionary:/dɪˈskreʃəˌnɛri/
adjective|酌情的；自由决定的|Available to be used when and how somebody chooses. Decided by officials and not fixed by rules.|The president has discretionary powers to release classified information. 总统有权酌情公布机密信息。|Synonyms: optional, elective, non-mandatory, open to choice, at one's discretion|Antonyms: mandatory, obligatory, compulsory, required|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *discrecionary*, from Late Latin *discrētiōnārius*, from Latin *discrētiō* (“separation, distinction, discernment”), from *discernō* (“distinguish, separate”)
