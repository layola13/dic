ghostlinesses:/ˈɡoʊstlɪnəsɪz/
noun
幽灵般的状态; 鬼魂出没; 诡异气氛
The quality of being ghostly or spectral; the atmosphere of a place supposedly haunted by a ghost.
The old house was filled with ghostlinesses, making the visitors uneasy. 这座老房子充满了诡异的气氛，让来访者感到不安。
The ghostlinesses of the abandoned asylum were palpable. 这座废弃的收容所的幽灵气氛是可以感觉到的。
synonyms: eerieness, spookiness, spectralness, otherworldliness
antonyms: normality, ordinariness, tangibility


Etymology:
From ghostly + -ness + -es (plural suffix).  "Ghostly" comes from Old English "gastlic," meaning "spiritual, pertaining to the soul."  "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition. "-es" is a plural suffix.
