blue-stocking: /ˈbluːˈstɒkɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈbluːˈstɑːkɪŋ/ (US) | n. | 蓝袜子；女学者；女才子 | A woman who is considered to be intellectual or academic, especially one with feminist or unconventional views.  Originally, a term for literary women in the 18th century who wore blue worsted stockings instead of the more fashionable black silk. |  She was a renowned blue-stocking, known for her sharp wit and extensive knowledge of literature.  她是一位著名的女学者，以其敏锐的才智和丰富的文学知识而闻名。|  Example:  Virginia Woolf was considered a blue-stocking by many of her contemporaries.  弗吉尼亚·伍尔夫被许多同时代的人视为女学者。| Synonym: intellectual, academic, bluestocking (alternative spelling), highbrow | Antonym: philistine, lowbrow | Noun
