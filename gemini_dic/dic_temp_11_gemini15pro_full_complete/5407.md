almoravids:/ˌælməˈrɑːvɪdz/ | n. | 阿尔摩拉维德王朝 |  A Berber Muslim dynasty that ruled parts of North Africa and the Iberian Peninsula in the 11th and 12th centuries. | The Almoravids conquered Morocco and parts of Spain. 阿尔摩拉维德王朝征服了摩洛哥和西班牙的部分地区。|  The Almoravid empire eventually fragmented due to internal strife. 阿尔摩拉维德帝国最终由于内部冲突而分裂。 | synonyms: dynasty, empire | antonyms: N/A | noun

Etymology: From Spanish *almorávides*, from Arabic *al-Murābiṭūn* ("the people of the *ribāṭ*"), meaning "those who garrison a frontier outpost."
