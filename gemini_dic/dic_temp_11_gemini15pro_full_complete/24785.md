broth:/brɔːθ/ /brɑːθ/
noun
肉汤
Thin soup, typically made by boiling meat or vegetables in water.
She sipped the hot chicken broth. 她喝了一口热鸡汤。
stock, bouillon
gravy, sauce
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English "broð," meaning "liquid in which something has been boiled."  Related to Old High German "brod," and Old Norse "broð."  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*bruthaz."
