fast-tracking:/ˌfæstˈtrækɪŋ/
|n.|快速通道，快速审批|The process of accelerating a project or task by overlapping phases that would normally be sequential.|The construction project was fast-tracked to meet the deadline.  该建设项目为了赶上最后期限而被快速推进。|近义词：expediting, accelerating|反义词：delaying, slowing down|noun
fast-track:/ˈfæstˌtræk/
|v.|快速推进，快速审批|To accelerate the progress of something, often by skipping some of the usual stages.|They decided to fast-track the development of the new drug. 他们决定快速推进新药的研发。|近义词：expedite, accelerate|反义词：delay, postpone|verb
fast-tracked:/ˌfæstˈtrækt/
|adj.|快速通道的，快速审批的|Having been accelerated or expedited.|The fast-tracked project was completed in record time. 快速通道项目在创纪录的时间内完成。|近义词：expedited, accelerated|反义词：delayed, postponed|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "fast" (quick) + "track" (a path or course).  The term originally referred to a physical track for faster movement, and later evolved to describe expedited processes.
