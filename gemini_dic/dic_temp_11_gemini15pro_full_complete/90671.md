humblers:/ˈhʌmblərz/| n. |使人谦卑的人或事物|People or things that cause someone to feel less proud or arrogant.  Something that humbles a person.|例句：The vastness of the universe is a great humbler.  浩瀚的宇宙令人感到自身的渺小。

例句：Experiences like this are great humblers.  这样的经历能让人更加谦逊。|近义词：levelers, equalizers|反义词：ego boosters, flatterers|词性:noun (plural)

Etymology: From the verb "humble" + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting a person or thing that performs the action of the verb).  "Humble" comes from the Old French word "humble" (modern French "humble"), derived from the Latin "humilis" meaning "low, lowly, humble," related to "humus" meaning "ground, earth."
