disabuse:/ˌdɪsəˈbjuːz/ | vt. | 使醒悟；纠正…的错误；打消…的错误念头 | To free someone from a misconception or deception. |  I had to disabuse him of the notion that he was somehow indispensable to the company. 我不得不让他清醒过来，让他明白他对公司并非不可或缺。

| 近义词: enlighten, undeceive, correct, set straight | 反义词: deceive, mislead, delude | 词性: 动词

disabuse:/ˌdɪsəˈbjuːz/ | vt. |  使醒悟；纠正…的错误；打消…的错误念头 | To persuade someone that an idea or belief is mistaken. | She gently disabused him of his romantic notions about her. 她温柔地打消了他对她的浪漫想法。

| 近义词: disillusion, disenchant | 反义词:  misinform, misguide | 词性: 动词
