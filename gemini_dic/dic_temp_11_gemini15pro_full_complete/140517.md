ETYMOLOGY->
From French *oléandre*, from Medieval Latin *oleandrum*, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Latin *olea* (olive tree), because of a superficial resemblance of the leaves, or from Greek *rhododendron* (rose tree) + *nerion*, the ancient Greek name for the plant. The *oleander* part may also come from a corruption of *lorandrum*, which would be a combination of *laurus* (laurel) and *rhododendron*.
