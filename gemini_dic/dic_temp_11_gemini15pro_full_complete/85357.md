harrow:/ˈhæroʊ/ (US), /ˈhærəʊ/ (UK)| n. vt.|耙;  折磨,使痛苦|A farm implement consisting of a heavy frame set with teeth or tines, used for breaking up and smoothing ploughed land. To cause distress to.|The farmer used a harrow to prepare the field for planting. The memories of the war continued to harrow him.|近义词: rake, distress, torment|反义词: soothe, comfort|词性: noun, verb

harrowing:/ˈhærəʊɪŋ/ (US), /ˈhærəʊɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|令人痛苦的，折磨人的|Extremely distressing; agonizing.|The harrowing experience left her deeply shaken.|近义词: distressing, upsetting, traumatic|反义词: comforting, soothing|词性: adjective
