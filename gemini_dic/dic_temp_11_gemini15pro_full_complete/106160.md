knobstick:/ˈnɒbstɪk/ (UK) /ˈnɑːbstɪk/ (US)| n.|短棍，工贼，破坏罢工的人|A short, thick stick, especially one used as a weapon; a derogatory term for a strikebreaker, someone who continues to work during a strike.|The strikers attacked the knobsticks with rocks and bottles. 罢工者用石头和瓶子袭击了工贼。|近义词：strikebreaker, blackleg, scab|反义词：striker, unionist|词性: noun

词源:  From knob + stick.  Originally referred to a short club used as a weapon.  The derogatory sense arose in the 19th century, possibly because strikebreakers were sometimes armed with such sticks.
