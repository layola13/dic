highbrowed: /ˈhaɪˌbraʊd/ | adj. | 高雅的，知识分子的，曲高和寡的 | Having or showing intellectual or cultural pretensions considered by some to be superior; overly scholarly or refined.  Characterized by highbrow tastes or interests. |  The highbrowed critic dismissed the popular novel as "drivel."  这位高雅的评论家将这本畅销小说斥为“废话”。 | Example: She frequents highbrowed art galleries and lectures. 她经常光顾高雅的艺术画廊和讲座。 | Synonyms: intellectual, sophisticated, cultured, refined, pretentious | Antonyms: lowbrow, unsophisticated, philistine | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "highbrow" + "-ed." "Highbrow" originated in the late 19th century, referring literally to a high forehead, then figuratively to intellectual superiority (based on the then-popular phrenology).  The "-ed" suffix creates the adjective form.
