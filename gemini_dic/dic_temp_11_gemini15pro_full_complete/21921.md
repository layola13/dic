bog:/bɒɡ/ (UK) /bɔːɡ/ (US)| n. v.|沼泽，泥塘；陷入困境|A bog is a wetland that accumulates peat, a deposit of dead plant material—often mosses, and in a majority of cases, sphagnum moss. |The car got stuck in the bog. 汽车陷入了泥潭。|近义词：marsh, swamp, mire|反义词：dry land|词性: 名词, 动词

bog:/bɒɡ/ (UK) /bɔːɡ/ (US)| v. |使…陷入泥潭；使…陷入困境|To bog something down means to cause it to become stuck in mud or to become impeded.|The project was bogged down by bureaucratic delays. 这个项目因官僚延误而陷入困境。|近义词：mire, entangle, stall|反义词：expedite, free|词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English bogge, from Irish or Scottish Gaelic bogach (“soft, wet”), from bog (“soft”). Ultimately from Proto-Celtic *buká (“soft, damp”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰug- (“to swell, bend”).
