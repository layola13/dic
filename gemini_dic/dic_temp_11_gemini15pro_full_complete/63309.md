enticement:/ɪnˈtaɪsmənt/ | n. |诱惑，诱因|Something that attracts or tempts someone, often in a pleasing or subtle way. |例句：The enticement of a free vacation was too much to resist. 免费度假的诱惑太难以抗拒了。

例句：The store used colorful displays as an enticement to attract customers.  这家商店用五彩缤纷的陈列来吸引顾客。

近义词：allure, attraction, temptation, bait, lure |反义词：deterrent, discouragement, repellent |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *entysement, from Old French entissement, from entiser (“to incite, instigate”), from Vulgar Latin *intitō, frequentative of Latin *intitiō, from *intiāre (“to make to go into”), from *intiō (“I go into”), from in (“in, into”) + eo (“I go”).
