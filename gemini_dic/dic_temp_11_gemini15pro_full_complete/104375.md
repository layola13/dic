kabal:/kəˈbɑːl/| n.|阴谋集团，秘密结社；犹太神秘哲学|A cabal is a small group of people who work together secretly, often to achieve something that is not approved of by other people.|例句：A cabal of ministers was plotting to overthrow the government. 一群部长密谋推翻政府。

例句：He was a member of a powerful cabal. 他是一个强大的阴谋集团的成员。|近义词：clique, faction, group, ring, conspiracy|反义词:individual|词性:名词

词源: From French cabale, from Medieval Latin cabala "Jewish mysticism," from Hebrew qabbalah "received tradition, oral transmission."
