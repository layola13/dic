held-down:/ˌhɛld ˈdaʊn/ | adj. | 被压制的，被控制的，低落的 | Suppressed, controlled, or in a low position, often referring to emotions or social status.  Originates from the literal meaning of being held in a downward position. |  He felt held-down by his demanding job.  他觉得被繁重的工作压得喘不过气。 |  She had a held-down feeling after the argument. 争吵过后，她感到情绪低落。 |  subdued, oppressed, repressed | liberated, free, empowered | adjective

held-down:/ˌhɛld ˈdaʊn/ | adj. | 低收入的，低工资的 | Having a low-paying job.  Derived from the idea of being kept at a lower economic level. |  He worked a series of held-down jobs to make ends meet. 他做了一系列低收入的工作来维持生计。 | Many people in the area are stuck in held-down positions with little chance of advancement.  该地区的许多人被困在低工资的职位上，几乎没有晋升的机会。 | low-paying, low-income, poorly paid | high-paying, well-paid, lucrative | adjective
