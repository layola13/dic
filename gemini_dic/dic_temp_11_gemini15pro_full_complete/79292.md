glaverer:/ˈɡleɪvərər/ (US), /ˈɡlævərə(r)/ (UK)| n. |奉承者，谄媚者|Someone who flatters excessively and insincerely, often to gain favor or advantage.  They use excessive praise and flattery, often insincerely, to manipulate or gain something from the person they are flattering.| He's such a glaverer, always telling the boss how brilliant he is. 他真是个马屁精，总是对老板说他有多聪明。|近义词：sycophant, flatterer, toady, yes-man|反义词：critic, detractor|词性：noun

词源:
From the verb "glaver,"  meaning "to flatter or fawn," which likely originates from a Scandinavian source, possibly related to Old Norse "glafra" meaning "to gape" or "stare foolishly." The implication is that a glaverer looks at someone with excessive admiration, often feigned.
