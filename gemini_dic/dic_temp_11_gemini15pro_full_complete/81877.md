grievance: /ˈɡriːvəns/ (US) /ˈɡriːvəns/ (UK) | n. |委屈，抱怨，不平| a real or imagined cause for complaint or protest|  Example: He filed a grievance against his employer. 他对雇主提出了申诉。| Example:  She aired her grievances at the meeting. 她在会议上表达了她的不满。 |Synonyms: complaint, injustice, wrong, beef, gripe |Antonyms: satisfaction, contentment, pleasure| Noun

grievance: /ˈɡriːvəns/ (US) /ˈɡriːvəns/ (UK) | n. |申诉，冤情| a formal statement of a complaint over something believed to be wrong or unfair| Example: The union representative helped him file a grievance. 工会代表帮他提出了申诉。| Example: Management refused to hear their grievances. 管理层拒绝听取他们的申诉。 |Synonyms: complaint, protest, objection |Antonyms: acceptance, approval, agreement| Noun
