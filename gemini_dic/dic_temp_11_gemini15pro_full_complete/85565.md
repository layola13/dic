hatefully:/ˈheɪtfəli/| adv.|充满仇恨地，可恨地|In a manner filled with hate; showing or expressing intense dislike or animosity.|She glared at him hatefully. 她充满仇恨地瞪着他。|Synonyms: spitefully, maliciously, viciously, resentfully|Antonyms: lovingly, kindly, affectionately|Part of Speech: Adverb

Etymology:
From *hate* (Old English *hatian*, from Proto-Germanic *hatōną*, from Proto-Indo-European *kad-/*ked- “to grieve, sorrow”) + *-ful* (Old English *-ful*, from Proto-Germanic *-fullaz, meaning “full of”) + *-ly* (Old English *-līċe*, from Proto-Germanic *-līkō, forming adverbs of manner).
