anti-democratic:/ˌæntiˌdɛməˈkrætɪk/
|adj.|
|反民主的；反对民主的|
|Opposed to or violating the principles of democracy.|
|The government's new policies are seen as anti-democratic by many. 政府的新政策被许多人视为反民主的。|

|His anti-democratic views led to his expulsion from the party. 他的反民主观点导致他被开除党籍。|
|undemocratic, authoritarian, totalitarian, dictatorial, tyrannical|
|democratic, liberal, egalitarian|
|adjective|


Etymology->

From anti- + democratic.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to," derived from Greek. "Democratic" comes from the Greek "dēmokratia," meaning "rule by the people."
