counsellor:/ˈkaʊnsələr/ (US) /ˈkaʊnsələ(r)/ (UK)| n.| 顾问，辅导员 | A person trained to give guidance on personal, social, or psychological problems. | Example: I spoke to a counsellor about my anxiety.  我和一位顾问谈了我的焦虑。

Example: The career counsellor helped me choose a suitable university. 职业顾问帮助我选择了一所合适的大学。 |Synonym: advisor, guide, mentor, therapist | Antonym: N/A | noun

counsel:/ˈkaʊnsl/ (US) /ˈkaʊnsl/ (UK)| n. v.|  (n.) 劝告，忠告；(v.) 劝告，忠告；建议| (n.) advice, especially that given formally. (v.) give advice to (someone). | Example (n.):  He followed his father's counsel. 他听从了父亲的忠告。

Example (v.):  She counselled him to leave the country. 她劝他离开这个国家。| Synonym: (n.) advice, guidance, recommendation; (v.) advise, guide, recommend | Antonym: N/A | noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French conseillier (modern conseiller), from Latin consiliarius (“advisor”), from consilium (“counsel, advice, deliberation”).
