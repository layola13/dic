juglans: /ˈdʒʌɡlənz/ | n. | 胡桃；胡桃树；胡桃木 | A genus of deciduous trees of the walnut family (Juglandaceae), native to North and South America and Eurasia.  The trees have pinnately compound leaves and nuts enclosed in a hard, indehiscent husk. |  The juglans nigra, or black walnut, is prized for its timber. 黑胡桃木因其木材而备受珍视。| The squirrels buried the juglans nuts for the winter. 松鼠把胡桃埋起来过冬。 | synonyms: walnut, walnut tree | antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a plant genus) | noun
