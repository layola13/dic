magistrate: /ˈmædʒɪstreɪt/ | n. | 地方法官；治安官 | A civil officer or lay judge who administers the law, especially one who conducts a court dealing with minor offenses and holds preliminary hearings for more serious ones. |  The magistrate sentenced the thief to community service.  地方法官判处小偷进行社区服务。

| 近义词: judge, justice of the peace | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps defendant, accused) | 词性: noun


magistrates: /ˈmædʒɪstreɪts/ | n. | 地方法官们；治安官们 | Plural form of magistrate.  | The magistrates heard several cases that day. 那天，治安法官们审理了几个案件。 | 近义词: judges, justices of the peace | 反义词: (No direct antonym) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *magistrat, from Latin magistrātus (“office of a magistrate, magistracy”), from magistrāre (“to act as magistrate”), from magister (“master, chief, director, teacher”), from magis (“more”).
