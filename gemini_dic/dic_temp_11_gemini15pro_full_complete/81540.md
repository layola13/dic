gravy:/ˈɡreɪvi/| n.|肉汁；意外之财；轻松的胜利|A thick sauce made from the juices of cooked meat, often flavored with flour or cornstarch, and sometimes thickened with butter or other ingredients.  Also can refer to unearned or unexpected gains or an easy victory. |The roast chicken was served with delicious gravy. 烤鸡配上美味的肉汁。 He made a lot of gravy from that real estate deal. 他从那笔房地产交易中赚了很多钱。 The game was pure gravy for the experienced team. 对于这支经验丰富的球队来说，这场比赛简直就是轻松的胜利。|近义词：sauce, jus, windfall, bonus|反义词: loss, defeat|noun

词源：From Old French *grave, from grevé "burdened, heavy," past participle of grever "to burden, aggravate," from Late Latin gravare "to burden," from Latin gravis "heavy."  The culinary sense arose from the idea of a sauce adding "weight" to a meal.
