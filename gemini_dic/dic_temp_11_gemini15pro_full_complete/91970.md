hypermutableness:/ˌhaɪpərmjuːtəˈbɪlnəs/ | n. | 超突变性；高度突变性 | The state or quality of being hypermutable, meaning having a significantly increased rate of mutation. | The hypermutableness of certain viruses makes them difficult to treat with antiviral drugs.  某些病毒的超突变性使得它们难以用抗病毒药物治疗。 | 近义词: high mutability, increased mutation rate | 反义词: stability, immutability | 词性: noun

Etymology:
hyper- (prefix): excessive, above normal (from Greek "hyper")
mutable (adjective): liable to change (from Latin "mutabilis")
-ness (suffix): forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality
