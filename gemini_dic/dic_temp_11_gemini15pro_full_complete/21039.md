blighty: /ˈblaɪti/| n.|英国（尤指士兵在海外服役时对祖国的称呼）; 伤; 受伤的地方|Britain (especially as used by soldiers abroad); a wound or injury. |例句：He was wounded in battle and sent back to blighty. 他在战斗中受伤，被送回了英国。|例句：The old soldier often reminisced about his time in blighty.  这位老兵经常回忆起他在英国的时光。|近义词：Britain, England, home, homeland|反义词:abroad, overseas|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Hindi-Urdu *bilāyatī* "foreign, European," originally "belonging to Wilayat," especially Europe or England, from Arabic *wilāya* "province, district, state."  Originally referring to Europe in general, the term became associated specifically with Britain during World War I.
