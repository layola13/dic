crabs:/kræbz/
n.|螃蟹|Decapod crustaceans with a broad carapace, short tail, and five pairs of legs, the first pair of which are modified as pincers. |We caught crabs at the beach. 我们在海滩抓螃蟹。|Synonyms: crustaceans, shellfish |Antonyms: None|noun

crabs:/kræbz/
v. (third-person singular simple present crabs, present participle crabbing, simple past and past participle crabbed)|抱怨, 挑剔; (尤指横向)移动|To complain or find fault; to move sideways. |He crabbed about the service at the restaurant. 他抱怨餐厅的服务。|Synonyms: grumble, complain, carp, criticize |Antonyms: praise, compliment|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *crabba, from Proto-Germanic *krabōną, from Proto-Indo-European *gerbʰ- (“to scratch, carve”). Cognate with Dutch krab, German Krabbe, Danish krabbe. The verb sense "to move sideways" is from the typical movement of crabs.
