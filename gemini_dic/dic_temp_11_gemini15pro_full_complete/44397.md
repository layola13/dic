cross-gartered: /ˌkrɒsˈɡɑːtəd/ (UK) /ˌkrɔːsˈɡɑːrtərd/ (US)
| adj. | 交叉绑腿的；十字绑带的 | Having garters crossed on the legs, typically below the knee. This was a fashion in some periods, notably the Elizabethan era. |  Malvolio in Shakespeare's *Twelfth Night* famously wears cross-gartered yellow stockings.  在莎士比亚的《第十二夜》中，马伏里奥穿着著名的交叉绑带的黄色长袜。 | Synonym: crisscrossed | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but perhaps "plainly gartered") | adjective
