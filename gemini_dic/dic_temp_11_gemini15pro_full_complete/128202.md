neurine:/ˈnjʊəriːn/ /ˈnʊrˌin/| n.|神经碱；胆碱；三甲基乙烯基氢氧化铵|A poisonous ptomaine, C5H13NO, found in decaying or diseased nerve and muscle tissue, and also in putrefying fish and meat. It is a syrupy, alkaline liquid, soluble in water, and having a fishy odor. Also called trimethylvinylammonium hydroxide.|Neurine is a highly toxic substance found in decaying tissue. 神经碱是一种存在于腐烂组织中的剧毒物质。|Synonyms: choline (in older usage, though now distinct), amanitine|Antonyms: None (due to its specific chemical nature)|noun
