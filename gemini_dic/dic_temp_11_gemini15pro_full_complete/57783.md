dreary:/ˈdrɪəri/ (US), /ˈdrɪəri/ (UK)| adj.| 凄凉的，阴沉的，沉闷的|Causing a feeling of sadness or gloom; dull and bleak.|The dreary weather made me feel depressed.  阴沉的天气让我感到沮丧。|Synonyms: bleak, dismal, gloomy, somber, depressing|Antonyms: cheerful, bright, sunny, lively|adjective

drearily:/ˈdrɪərɪli/ (US), /ˈdrɪərɪli/ (UK)| adv.| 凄凉地，阴沉地，沉闷地|In a dreary manner.|The rain fell drearily all day. 雨凄凉地下了一整天。|Synonyms: gloomily, dismally, bleakly|Antonyms: cheerfully, brightly, sunnily|adverb

dreariness:/ˈdrɪərɪnəs/ (US), /ˈdrɪərɪnəs/ (UK)| n.| 凄凉，阴沉，沉闷|The state or quality of being dreary.|The dreariness of the landscape made her long for home.  这片景色的凄凉使她渴望回家。|Synonyms: gloom, dismalness, bleakness|Antonyms: cheerfulness, brightness, sunniness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dreorig "sad, sorrowful," from Proto-Germanic *dreurigaz "troubled, sad," related to Old Norse dreyrigr "bloody," and ultimately from a PIE root meaning "to flow, drip" (perhaps referring to tears). The sense shifted from "bloody" to "sad" and then to "gloomy, dull."
