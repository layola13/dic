missuit:/ˌmɪsˈsuːt/ | vt. n. | 不适合，不相配 | To suit badly; to be unbecoming or inappropriate to.  A garment or style that is unbecoming or inappropriate. | That dress missuits her. 那件衣服不适合她。|  The style of the house missuits the neighborhood. 这房子的风格与周围环境不协调。| 近义词: clash, disaccord, unbecoming | 反义词: suit, befit, become | 词性: 动词, 名词

词源:  From mis- + suit.  "Mis-" is a prefix of Old English and Germanic origin meaning "wrongly," "badly," or "unsuitably." "Suit" comes from Old French "suite" meaning "following," "sequence," and later, "set of clothes."
