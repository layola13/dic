chain-smokes:/ˈtʃeɪn sməʊks/ | v. |  连续抽烟，一支接一支地抽烟 | He chain-smoked all through the meeting. 他整个会议期间一支接一支地抽烟。|  He chain-smoked nervously while waiting for the news. 他焦急地等待消息时一支接一支地抽烟。 | 近义词: smoke heavily, smoke incessantly | 反义词: abstain from smoking | 词性: 动词

Etymology: "chain" + "smokes" -  The term is a compound verb formed by combining "chain," referring to a connected series, with "smoke," the act of inhaling and exhaling the fumes of burning tobacco.  It signifies the continuous act of lighting a new cigarette immediately after finishing the previous one.
