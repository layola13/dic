fightable:/ˈfaɪtəbl/| adj.|可战斗的，值得一战的|Capable of being fought against; worth fighting against.  Suitable or ready for a fight.|例句：The opponent was formidable, but fightable. 对手很强大，但可以一战。|例句：Despite the odds, they felt the situation was fightable. 尽管胜算不大，但他们觉得这种情况值得一战。|近义词：contestable, defensible, winnable|反义词：uncontestable, indefensible, hopeless|词性:形容词

词源: fight (v.) + -able (suffix)

fight: From Old English *feohtan "to fight," from Proto-Germanic *fehtan (source also of Old Saxon fehtan, Old Frisian fiuchta, Old High German fehtan, German fechten "to fight," Old Norse berjast "to fight, contend," Gothic -fijan in andbundjan "to unbind").

-able:  From Middle English -able, -ible, from Old French -able, -ible, from Latin -abilis, -ibilis, from PIE *-to- forming verbal adjectives with the sense of "able to be," "capable of being," etc.
