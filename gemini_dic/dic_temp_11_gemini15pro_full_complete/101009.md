invaluablenesses:/ɪnˈvæljuəbəlˌnɛsɪz/
|n.| 无价性；珍贵性|The state or quality of being invaluable; extreme preciousness.|Her invaluablenesses are her kindness and wisdom. 她的无价之宝是她的善良和智慧。|近义词：pricelessness, worth, value, importance|反义词:worthlessness, unimportance|词性:名词

Etymology:
From invaluable + -ness.  "Invaluable" comes from "in-" (not) + "valuable" (having worth). "Valuable" ultimately derives from Latin "valere" (to be strong, be worth).  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition.
