mineralogically:/ˌmɪnərəˈlɒdʒɪk(ə)li/
adv. | 矿物学地；从矿物学的角度 | From the perspective of mineralogy; in terms of mineralogy.
The rock was mineralogically complex, containing several rare minerals. | 这块岩石矿物学上很复杂，包含几种稀有矿物。
Near Antofagasta, Chile, the coastal range is mineralogically rich. | 在智利安托法加斯塔附近，海岸山脉矿藏丰富。
近义词: geologically, petrologically
反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider conceptually: non-mineralogically)
词性: adverb

词源:
From mineral + -logical + -ly.  "Mineral" comes from Medieval Latin "minerale", meaning "something mined," from "minera," "mine." "-logical" comes from Greek "-logikos," meaning "pertaining to reason or speech," from "logos," "word, reason." "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs.
