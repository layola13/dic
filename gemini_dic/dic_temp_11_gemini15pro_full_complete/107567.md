landlubberish:/ˌlændˈlʌbərɪʃ/| adj.|笨手笨脚的，不谙航海的|Awkward or clumsy on land, especially in a way that suggests a lack of seafaring experience.|He walked with a landlubberish gait, stumbling over the smallest pebbles.|近义词：clumsy, awkward, ungainly, inexpert|反义词:nautical, seafaring, experienced|词性:adjective

Etymology:
From "landlubber" + "-ish."  "Landlubber" is a compound of "land" and "lubber" (a clumsy or lazy person).  The "-ish" suffix adds the meaning of "like" or "characteristic of."
