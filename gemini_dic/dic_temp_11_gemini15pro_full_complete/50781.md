decanter:/dɪˈkæntər/| n.|醒酒器；倾析器|A decorative glass bottle used for serving wine or other alcoholic drinks, typically after they have been decanted.|She poured the wine into a crystal decanter. 她把酒倒进一个水晶醒酒器里。|Synonyms: carafe, flagon|Antonyms: None|Noun

decanters:/dɪˈkæntərz/| n.|醒酒器；倾析器（复数）|Plural form of decanter, referring to more than one decanter.|They have a collection of antique decanters. 他们收藏了许多古董醒酒器。|Synonyms: carafes, flagons|Antonyms: None|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décanter*, from *dé-* (indicating removal) + *canter* (to sing, from Late Latin *cantare*).  Originally referred to the act of gently pouring liquid off a sediment. The noun for the vessel came later.
