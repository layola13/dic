all-encompassing:/ɔːlɪnˈkʌmpəsɪŋ/| adj.|包罗万象的，无所不包的|Including everything or having a very wide range.|例句：The book provides an all-encompassing overview of the history of art. 这本书对艺术史进行了全面的概述。|例句：Her all-encompassing knowledge of the subject impressed everyone. 她对这个学科无所不包的知识给每个人都留下了深刻的印象。|近义词：comprehensive, exhaustive, inclusive|反义词：limited, narrow, restricted|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From all + encompassing, present participle of encompass (“to encircle, surround, enclose; to include comprehensively”). Encompass comes from Middle English encompassen, from en- + compass (“to go around, enclose”), from Old French compasser (“to measure with compasses, to go around”), from Vulgar Latin compassāre, from Latin com- + passus (“a step, pace”).
