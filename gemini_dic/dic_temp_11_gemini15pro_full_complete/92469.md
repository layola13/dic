hypostrophe:/haɪˈpɒstrəfi/ /haɪˈpɑːstrəfi/ | n. | 省略;省略符号;呼语 |  A rhetorical device consisting of an address to an absent person or a thing.  It's a form of personification. |  "O death, where is thy sting?" is an example of hypostrophe.  死神啊，你的毒钩在哪里？| 近义词: apostrophe, address | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *hypostrophe, from Ancient Greek ὑποστροφή (hupostrophḗ, “a turning back, return, subterfuge, evasion; apostrophe”), from ὑποστρέφω (hupostréphō, “I turn back, return, turn around”), from ὑπό (hupó, “under”) + στρέφω (stréphō, “I turn”).
