headiness:/ˈhedinəs/| n.|兴奋，激动，轻率|A feeling of euphoria or lightheadedness, often caused by alcohol or success.  It can also refer to impetuousness or rashness.|After winning the lottery, she experienced a headiness that made her spend recklessly.  赢得彩票后，她感到一阵兴奋，让她花钱大手大脚。|Synonyms: exhilaration, elation, giddiness, dizziness, recklessness|Antonyms: levelheadedness, calmness, sobriety, caution|noun

headiness:/ˈhedinəs/| n.|浓烈，（酒）后劲大|The quality of being potent or intoxicating, especially of alcoholic beverages.|The headiness of the wine made him feel dizzy.  这酒的后劲让他感到头晕。|Synonyms: potency, strength, intoxicating|Antonyms: mildness, weakness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From heady + -ness.  Heady itself comes from Old English *hēadig, from hēafod "head."
