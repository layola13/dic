cochlear:/ˈkɒkliə(r)/ (UK) /ˈkoʊkliər/ (US)| adj. n.|耳蜗的；耳蜗|Relating to the cochlea (a spiral-shaped cavity forming part of the inner ear) or the sense of hearing.  Also, the cochlea itself.|The cochlear implant has significantly improved his hearing. 耳蜗植入物显著改善了他的听力。|The doctor examined the patient's cochlear nerve. 医生检查了病人的耳蜗神经。|aural, auditory|vestibular|adjective, noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cochleāris*, from *cochlea* ("snail, spiral, screw," also "a liquid measure"), from Greek κοχλίας (kokhlíās, “snail, spiral, screw”)
