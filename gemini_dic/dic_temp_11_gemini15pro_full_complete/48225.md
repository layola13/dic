deconstructing:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌktɪŋ/
|v.|解构，拆解|Analyzing (something) by taking apart or examining its constituent elements.|The professor was deconstructing the poem to reveal its hidden meanings. 教授正在解构这首诗，以揭示其隐藏的含义。|analyzing, dissecting, interpreting|synthesizing, constructing|verb

deconstruction:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkʃn/
|n.|解构，拆解|The act of analyzing something by taking it apart.|The deconstruction of the old building was completed in a week. 旧建筑的拆除在一周内完成。|analysis, dissection, interpretation|synthesis, construction|noun

Etymology:
From French *déconstruction*, from *dé-* (prefix meaning 'undoing, reversing') + *construction* ('construction'). Ultimately from Latin *dē-* + *constructiō* ('a building, constructing').
