agedly:/ˈeɪdʒɪdli/
adverb|老成地，苍老地|In a manner that suggests great age; like an aged person.|He walked agedly across the room, his steps slow and deliberate. 他老态龙钟地走过房间，步履缓慢而从容。|近义词：oldly, maturely, anciently|反义词:youthfully, childishly
词性:副词

词源: From "aged" (meaning old) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  The suffix "-ly" transforms adjectives into adverbs, indicating the manner in which something is done.  "Aged" itself comes from the Old French "aage" (age), ultimately from Latin "aetas" (age).
