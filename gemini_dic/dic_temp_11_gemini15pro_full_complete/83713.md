hagioscope:/ˈhæɡiəˌskoʊp/| n.|圣龛窥视孔, 小孔|A small opening or squint in the wall of a church, near the altar, enabling a person outside the chancel to see the elevation of the Host.|The hagioscope allowed those in the side chapel to witness the consecration of the bread and wine.|Synonym: squint, lychnoscope|Antonym: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἅγιος (hágios, “holy”) + -scope
