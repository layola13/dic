costardmonger:/ˈkɒstədˌmʌŋɡə(r)/ | n. |水果小贩；卖苹果的人|A person who sells apples or other fruit, especially from a stall or barrow.  |例句：The costardmonger called out his wares in a booming voice. 水果小贩用洪亮的声音叫卖他的货物。

例句：Shakespeare uses the term "costardmonger" in several of his plays.  莎士比亚在他的几部戏剧中都使用了“costardmonger”这个词。|近义词：fruit seller, greengrocer, vendor |反义词: buyer, customer |词性: noun

Etymology: From "costard" (a type of large apple) + "monger" (a dealer or trader). The word "costard" comes from Old French "coste" (rib, side) referring to the prominent ribs of this type of apple. "Monger" comes from Old English "mangere" from Proto-Germanic "*mangari" (merchant, trader).
