engarrison:/ɛnˈɡærɪsən/ | vt. |驻军，把…安置在要塞|To station troops in a fort or fortified place; to provide (a place) with a garrison. |The general decided to engarrison the troops in the newly built fort. 将军决定在新建造的堡垒中驻扎军队。|The city was engarrisoned with thousands of soldiers.  这座城市驻扎了数千名士兵。|近义词：garrison, fortify, defend|反义词：evacuate, withdraw|词性: 动词

Etymology->
From en- + garrison.  The prefix "en-" adds the meaning of "to put into" or "to cause to be."  "Garrison" itself comes from Old French "garison" meaning "protection, defense, healing," ultimately from Frankish "*warison," related to "ward" (to guard).
