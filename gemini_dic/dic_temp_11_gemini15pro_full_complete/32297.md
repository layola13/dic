chieftainships: /ˈtʃiːftənʃɪps/
n.
酋长之位；酋长身份；酋长统治
The position, status, or rule of a chieftain.
The book explores the complex history of Scottish chieftainships.  这本书探讨了苏格兰酋长制度的复杂历史。
The struggle for chieftainships often led to bloody conflicts. 争夺酋长之位常常导致血腥冲突。
synonyms: leadership, headship, dominion, authority
antonyms: subordination, subservience

Etymology:
From chieftain + -ship.  Chieftain comes from Old French *chevetain (Modern French capitaine), from Late Latin *capitaneus, from Latin caput (“head”).  The suffix -ship denotes status, condition, or office.
