cataplexy:/ˈkætəˌpleksi/| n. |猝倒|A sudden loss of muscle tone, often triggered by strong emotions such as laughter or excitement.  It is a common symptom of narcolepsy.|例句：The sudden laughter caused a bout of cataplexy, and she slumped to the floor.  突如其来的大笑引发了一阵猝倒，她瘫倒在地板上。

例句：He experiences cataplexy whenever he gets really excited, sometimes even just thinking about something funny can trigger it. 每当他非常兴奋的时候，他都会经历猝倒，有时甚至只是想到一些有趣的事情也会引发它。|近义词：muscle weakness, atonia|反义词: muscle strength, tonicity|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek καταπλήσσω (kataplēssō, “I strike down, astound”) +‎ -y
