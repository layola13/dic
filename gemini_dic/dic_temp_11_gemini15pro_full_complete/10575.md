apprehenders:/ˌæprɪˈhɛndərz/
|n.|逮捕者，理解者|People who apprehend; those who understand or perceive.|The apprehenders quickly surrounded the suspect. 逮捕者迅速包围了嫌疑人。|近义词：arresters, captors, graspers, perceivers|反义词：releasers, liberators|Noun


apprehender:/ˌæprɪˈhɛndər/
|n.|逮捕者，理解者|A person who apprehends, especially a law enforcement officer.|The apprehender read the suspect his rights. 逮捕者向嫌疑人宣读了他的权利。|近义词：arrester, captor|反义词：liberator|Noun


apprehend:/ˌæprɪˈhɛnd/
|vt.|逮捕，理解，担心|1. Arrest (someone) for a crime. 2. Understand or perceive. 3. Fear or dread something.|1. Police apprehended the suspect at the scene. 警察在现场逮捕了嫌疑人。

2. I apprehend your meaning. 我理解你的意思。

3. I apprehend trouble ahead. 我担心前面会有麻烦。|近义词: arrest, capture, grasp, understand, fear|反义词: release, liberate, misunderstand, ignore|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English apprehendere, from Latin apprehendere (“to seize, grasp”), from ad- (“to, toward”) + prehendere (“to grasp, seize”).
