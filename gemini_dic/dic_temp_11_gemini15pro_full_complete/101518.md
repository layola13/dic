irksomely:/ˈɜːrksəmli/ | adv. | 令人厌烦地；讨厌地 | In a tedious or annoying manner. | The fly buzzed irksomely around his head.  苍蝇在他头上嗡嗡作响，令他烦躁。 |  He coughed irksomely throughout the entire performance. 他在整个演出过程中烦人地咳嗽。 | 近义词：annoyingly, tediously, tiresomely, vexatiously | 反义词：pleasantly, agreeably, delightfully | 词性: 副词

Etymology: From irksome + -ly.  Irksome comes from Middle English irk + -some. Irk is from Old Norse yrkja "to work," related to the idea of tiring work.
