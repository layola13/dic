dogmatism: /ˈdɒɡmətɪzəm/ (US) /ˈdɔːɡmətɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |教条主义，独断论| The tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true, without consideration of evidence or the opinions of others.  The assertion or belief that a particular doctrine is true. | He was known for his dogmatism and refusal to consider other viewpoints. 他因教条主义和拒绝考虑其他观点而闻名。|  Her dogmatism on the subject made any reasonable discussion impossible.  她在这个问题上的独断论使得任何合理的讨论都不可能。| synonyms: doctrinaireism, rigidity, orthodoxy, bigotry, intolerance | antonyms: open-mindedness, flexibility, liberalism, tolerance | noun
