buffaloed:/ˈbʌfələʊd/ (US) /ˈbʌfələd/ (UK)
verb
困惑的，吓倒的
intimidated or confused to the point of inaction
例句：I was completely buffaloed by the legal jargon in the contract. 我完全被合同中的法律术语搞糊涂了。
例句：He tried to buffalo me into agreeing to his terms. 他试图吓唬我，让我同意他的条件。
近义词：intimidated, bewildered, confused, overwhelmed
反义词：confident, clear-headed, unfazed
词性：动词 (past participle)

词源: From the behavior of buffalo, which can appear confused or disoriented when faced with something new or threatening.  Originally American slang.
