gondwanaland: /ɡɒndˈwɑːnəlænd/ | n. |冈瓦纳大陆|A vast southern supercontinent that existed from the late Paleozoic Era to the early Mesozoic Era, comprising present-day South America, Africa, Australia, Antarctica, the Indian subcontinent, and Arabia.|The breakup of Gondwanaland led to the formation of the Atlantic and Indian Oceans.|Synonym: supercontinent|Antonym: None|Noun

gondwanalands: /ɡɒndˈwɑːnəlændz/ | n. |冈瓦纳大陆（复数，罕用）|  The plural form of Gondwanaland, rarely used as the supercontinent was a singular entity. | While grammatically correct, "Gondwanalands" is seldom used in geological contexts.  Scientists generally refer to "Gondwanaland" or the "fragments of Gondwanaland." | Synonym: supercontinents (in a very specific context)|Antonym: None|Noun
