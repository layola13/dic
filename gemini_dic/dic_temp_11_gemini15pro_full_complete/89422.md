homozygote:/ˌhəʊməʊˈzaɪɡəʊt/ (US) /ˌhoʊmoʊˈzaɪɡoʊt/| n. |纯合子|An individual having two identical alleles of a particular gene or genes and so breeding true for the corresponding characteristic.|The homozygote for the sickle-cell allele develops sickle-cell anemia.|Synonym: purebred|Antonym: heterozygote|noun
