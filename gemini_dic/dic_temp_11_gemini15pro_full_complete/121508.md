mislike:/mɪsˈlaɪk/
verb
不喜欢；厌恶
to dislike; to not like
He misliked the city intensely and longed for the countryside. 他非常不喜欢这座城市，渴望回到乡村。
She misliked having to work on weekends. 她不喜欢周末还要工作。

近义词:dislike, abhor, detest, loathe
反义词:like, love, admire, adore
词性:verb

词源:
From Middle English *misliken*, from Old English *mislīcian* (“to displease, mislike, offend”), from *mis-* + *līcian* (“to please”).  The prefix *mis-* signifies "wrongly" or "badly", while the root *līcian* is related to "like".
