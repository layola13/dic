metasounds:/ˈmɛtəˌsaʊndz/ | n. |元音|Units of sound that are smaller than syllables and may not have meaning on their own, but combine to form syllables and words. They are often used in the study of phonology and phonetics, particularly in describing the sounds of languages that have complex sound systems.|The linguist analyzed the metasounds of the African click language. 这位语言学家分析了非洲搭嘴音语言的元音。|近义词:phones, segments|反义词:None|Noun


metasound:/ˈmɛtəˌsaʊnd/ | n. |元音|A theoretical unit of sound proposed in some phonological theories, often smaller than a phoneme and used to explain variations in pronunciation.|The theory of metasounds helps explain how different accents can arise. 元音理论有助于解释不同口音是如何产生的。|近义词:distinctive feature|反义词:None|Noun
