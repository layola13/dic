nonrandomness:/ˌnɒnˈrændəmnəs/ /ˌnɑːnˈrændəmnəs/| n. |非随机性|The lack of randomness; the state or quality of not being random; exhibiting patterns, predictability, or design.|The nonrandomness of the data suggests a deliberate manipulation.|近义词:structure, order, pattern, regularity|反义词:randomness, chance, haphazardness|词性:名词

Etymology:
non- + randomness

randomness: from random + -ness

random: from Old French randon "impetuosity, speed, violence," from randir "to run fast," of Germanic origin.

-ness: suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being."
