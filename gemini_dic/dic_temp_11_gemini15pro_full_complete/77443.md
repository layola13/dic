gawkinesses: /ˈɡɔːkɪnɪsɪz/ | n. | 笨拙，尴尬 | The state of being gawky; awkwardness, clumsiness, especially in movement or behavior. |  His gawkinesses made him feel uncomfortable at the dance. 他的笨拙让他在舞会上感到不自在。| Synonyms: awkwardness, clumsiness, ungainliness, ineptness | Antonyms: grace, elegance, poise, dexterity | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From gawky + -ness + -es (plural suffix).  "Gawky" originated in the late 18th century, possibly from "gawk" (meaning to stare foolishly), which is of uncertain origin.
