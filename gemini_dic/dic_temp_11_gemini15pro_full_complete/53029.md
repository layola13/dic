diocletian:/ˌdaɪəˈkliːʃən/ | n. |戴克里先| Roman emperor (284-305 AD) who divided the empire into four parts (the Tetrarchy) and initiated the last great persecution of Christians. | Diocletian's reforms helped to stabilize the Roman Empire. 戴克里先的改革有助于稳定罗马帝国。|  Synonyms: emperor, ruler, Caesar | Antonyms: subject, citizen | noun

diocletian:/ˌdaɪəˈkliːʃən/ | adj. |戴克里先的；戴克里先时代的|Relating to or characteristic of the Roman emperor Diocletian or his reign. | The Diocletian persecution was a period of intense hardship for Christians. 戴克里先迫害时期是基督徒遭受严重苦难的时期。| Synonyms: imperial, Roman | Antonyms:  N/A | adjective
