orthopedist: /ˌɔːrθəˈpiːdɪst/ (US), /ˌɔːθəˈpiːdɪst/ (UK)| n. | 骨科医生 | A medical doctor who specializes in the branch of medicine concerned with the prevention or correction of injuries or disorders of the skeletal system and associated muscles, joints, and ligaments. |  I need to see an orthopedist about my knee pain. 我需要去看骨科医生看看我的膝盖疼痛。|  Synonyms: orthopaedic surgeon, bone doctor | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a profession) | noun


orthopedic: /ˌɔːrθəˈpiːdɪk/ (US), /ˌɔːθəˈpiːdɪk/ (UK)| adj. | 骨科的 | Relating to the branch of medicine dealing with the correction of deformities of bones or muscles. |  He had to wear an orthopedic brace after his surgery.  手术后他不得不戴上矫形支架。| Synonyms: orthopaedic | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym) | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From ortho- + -ped- + -ic or -ist.  Ortho- comes from Greek orthos "straight,"  -ped- comes from Greek pais "child" (originally referring to the treatment of children's skeletal deformities), and -ic and -ist are common suffixes denoting "relating to" and "a specialist in," respectively.
