newsboy:/ˈnjuːzbɔɪ/ (US), /ˈnjuːzbɔɪ/ (UK)| n. |报童，卖报的男孩|A boy who sells or delivers newspapers.|The newsboy shouted the headlines as he ran down the street. 报童一边沿街跑，一边大声喊着报纸的头条新闻。|Synonyms: paperboy, news vendor|Antonyms: N/A|Noun

newsboy:/ˈnjuːzbɔɪ/ (US), /ˈnjuːzbɔɪ/ (UK)| adj. |报童式的，报童帽的|Relating to or characteristic of a newsboy, especially their cap.|He wore a newsboy cap and a tweed jacket. 他戴着一顶报童帽，穿着一件粗花呢夹克。|Synonyms: paperboy, news vendor (for the noun form)|Antonyms: N/A|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From news + boy.  The term arose in the late 19th and early 20th centuries when young boys commonly sold newspapers on street corners.
