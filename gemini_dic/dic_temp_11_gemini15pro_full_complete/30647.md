cerinthian: /səˈrɪnθiən/
cerinthian: adj. n.|克林妥派的；克林妥派信徒|Relating to or characteristic of Cerinthus, a Gnostic teacher of the 1st century AD, or his doctrines.  A follower of Cerinthus.|例句：The Cerinthian heresy was rejected by the early church. 早期教会拒绝了克林妥派的异端邪说。|例句：He was accused of being a Cerinthian. 他被指控为克林妥派信徒。|近义词：Gnostic, heretic|反义词：orthodox|词性：adjective, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Cerinthus (name of a Gnostic leader) + -ian (suffix forming adjectives and nouns).  Cerinthus's name is of uncertain origin.
