contused:/kənˈtjuːzd/ /kənˈtuːzd/| adj.|挫伤的，青肿的|Injured by a blow but without a break in the skin; bruised.|例句：She had a contused knee after falling down the stairs. 她从楼梯上摔下来，膝盖挫伤了。

例句：The boxer's face was badly contused after the fight. 拳击手赛后脸部严重挫伤。|近义词：bruised, battered, injured|反义词：uninjured, healed|词性:形容词

contusion:/kənˈtjuːʒən/ /kənˈtuːʒən/| n.|挫伤，青肿|An injury in which the skin is not broken but there is discoloration and pain from ruptured blood vessels.|例句：He suffered a severe contusion on his head. 他的头部受到严重挫伤。

例句：The doctor diagnosed the injury as a contusion. 医生诊断该损伤为挫伤。|近义词：bruise, injury|反义词：healing|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contusus, past participle of contundere (“to bruise, crush”), from Latin con- (“together, with”) + tundere (“to beat, pound”).
