obdurate:/ˈɒbdjʊrət/ (UK) /ˈɑːbdərət/ (US)| adj.|顽固的，执拗的，冷酷无情的|Stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action, hardened in feeling; resistant to persuasion or softening influences.|He remained obdurate, refusing to admit his mistake. 他仍然顽固不化，拒绝承认自己的错误。|Synonyms: stubborn, inflexible, adamant, unyielding, intransigent|Antonyms: compliant, yielding, amenable, flexible, tractable|adjective

obdurating:/ˈɒbdjʊreɪtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈɑːbdəˌreɪtɪŋ/ (US)| adj. v.|使顽固，使冷酷无情|(acting as an adjective): making someone or something obdurate. (acting as a verb): the action of making someone obdurate.|His obdurating refusal to compromise led to the failure of the negotiations. 他顽固地拒绝妥协，导致谈判失败.  The constant criticism had an obdurating effect on him. 不断的批评使他变得冷酷无情。|Synonyms: hardening, toughening,  (as verb) making stubborn|Antonyms: softening, yielding, (as verb) making compliant|adjective, verb (present participle)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *obdūrātus*, past participle of *obdūrāre* (“to harden, to persist”), from ob- (“against, towards”) + dūrāre (“to harden, to last, to endure”), from dūrus (“hard”).
