lanchester:/ˈlæntʃɪstər/ | n. | 兰切斯特 |  Refers to F.W. Lanchester, a British engineer and applied mathematician, or his theories, particularly Lanchester's laws which are mathematical formulae for calculating the relative strengths of military forces. | The Lanchester equations are still used in military modelling. 兰切斯特方程式仍在军事建模中使用。 |  e.g.,  His work on aerodynamics led to the Lanchester wing. 他在空气动力学方面的工作促成了兰切斯特机翼的诞生。| synonym: none (specific to the person and his work) | antonym: none | noun
