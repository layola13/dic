aberration:/ˌæbəˈreɪʃn/| n.|异常，偏差，失常|a departure from what is normal, usual, or expected, typically an unwelcome one.|例句：His outburst in the meeting was an aberration from his usual calm demeanor. 他在会议上的爆发是他平时冷静举止的失常。|例句：The recent spike in crime is a temporary aberration. 最近的犯罪激增只是暂时的异常现象。|近义词：anomaly, deviation, irregularity, abnormality|反义词：norm, standard, regularity|词性:noun
aberration:/ˌæbəˈreɪʃn/| n.|光行差|the apparent displacement of a celestial object from its true position, caused by the combined effect of the motion of the observer and the finite velocity of light.|例句：Astronomers must account for aberration when calculating the precise position of stars. 天文学家在计算恒星的精确位置时必须考虑到光行差。|例句：The aberration of starlight can affect astronomical observations. 星光的光行差会影响天文观测。|近义词：displacement, shift, deviation|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *aberratio*, from Latin aberrare ("to wander away, go astray"), from ab ("away from") + errare ("to wander, err").
