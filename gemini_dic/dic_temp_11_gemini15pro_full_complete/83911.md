half-believing:/ˌhɑːf bɪˈliːvɪŋ/ | adj. | 半信半疑的 | Showing or feeling a mixture of belief and disbelief; skeptical. | He listened to her story with a half-believing expression. 他半信半疑地听着她的故事。|  She gave a half-believing nod. 她半信半疑地点了点头。 | skeptical, doubtful, dubious | convinced, certain, credulous | adjective

Etymology:
half + believing.  "Half" comes from Old English "healf," meaning "side, part." "Believing" is the present participle of the verb "believe," which comes from Old English "belīefan,"  meaning "to have confidence in, trust."
