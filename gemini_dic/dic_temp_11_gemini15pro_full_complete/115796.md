maritated: /ˈmærɪteɪtɪd/ | adj. | 已婚的（主要用于女性） |  While not standard English, "maritated" is sometimes used, particularly in older or regional dialects, to mean married, especially for women. It likely stems from the Latin "maritus" (husband) and follows a similar pattern to other past participles. However, "married" is the standard and preferred term. | She described herself as "maritated" on the form.  她在表格上将自己描述为“已婚”。 | 近义词: married, wedded | 反义词: single, unmarried, divorced | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Latin *maritatus, past participle of maritare (“to marry”), from maritus (“husband”).  Compare French mariter, Catalan maridar, Spanish maridar.
