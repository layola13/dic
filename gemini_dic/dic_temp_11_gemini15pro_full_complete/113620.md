machiavellianism: /ˌmækiəˈveliənɪzəm/ | n. | 马基雅维利主义; 权谋主义; 不择手段 |  Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian diplomat and writer, whose book "The Prince" described how rulers can gain and maintain power, often through deceit and manipulation. |  His machiavellianism helped him climb the corporate ladder quickly. 他的马基雅维利主义使他迅速晋升。| synonyms: cunning, duplicity, guile, manipulation, ruthlessness, strategy | antonyms:  honesty, integrity, forthrightness | noun
