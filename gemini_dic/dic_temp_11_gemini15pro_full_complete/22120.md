bolt-action:/ˈboʊlt ˌækʃən/ (US), /ˈbəʊlt ˌækʃən/ (UK)| adj. n.|栓动式，栓式枪机|A type of firearm action in which the bolt is operated manually by the shooter via a bolt handle, to load and unload cartridges from the chamber.  The bolt usually features locking lugs which engage corresponding recesses in the receiver to lock the bolt in place during firing.|The hunter used a bolt-action rifle. 猎人使用了一把栓动步枪。|近义词:lever-action, pump-action|反义词:semi-automatic, automatic|词性:adjective, noun


bolt:/boʊlt/ (US), /bəʊlt/ (UK)| n. v.|螺栓，门闩；快速奔跑|A metal fastener, typically consisting of a cylindrical shaft with a head on one end and a threaded portion on the other, designed to be inserted through holes in assembled parts and secured with a nut. Also, a sliding bar used to fasten a door or window; to move suddenly and rapidly.|He tightened the bolt. 他拧紧了螺栓。  The horse bolted. 马飞奔而去。|近义词:dash, sprint (for verb); screw, fastener (for noun)|反义词:unbolt, loosen (for verb)|词性:noun, verb
