outraged: /ˌaʊtˈreɪdʒd/ | adj. | 愤怒的，义愤填膺的 | Feeling or expressing strong resentment or anger at something perceived as unjust, offensive, or insulting. |  The community was outraged by the vandalism of the cemetery. 社区对公墓的破坏行为感到愤怒。 |  The outraged customer demanded a refund.  愤怒的顾客要求退款。 | synonyms: angered, furious, indignant, infuriated | antonyms: pleased, calm, delighted | adjective

outrage: /ˈaʊtreɪdʒ/ | n. | 愤怒，暴行 | An act of violence or cruelty that causes great shock or horror.  | The bombing was an outrage against humanity.  这起爆炸事件是对人类的暴行。| The outrage over the judge's decision led to protests.  法官的判决引发的愤怒导致了抗议活动。 | synonyms: atrocity, offense, affront | antonyms: kindness, respect, honor | noun

outrage: /ˈaʊtreɪdʒ/ | vt. | 激怒，使震怒 | To arouse intense anger, shock, or indignation in (someone). | The judge's lenient sentence outraged the victims' families. 法官从轻的判决激怒了受害者家属。|  The company's decision to close the factory outraged the workers. 公司关闭工厂的决定激怒了工人们。| synonyms: anger, incense, infuriate | antonyms: please, soothe, calm | verb
