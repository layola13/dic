malison:/ˈmælɪsən/| n.|诅咒，咒骂|A curse; a wish of evil upon someone. |The witch muttered a malison upon the prince, turning him into a toad.|近义词：curse, imprecation, anathema, execration|反义词：blessing, benediction|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *malison*, from *mal-* (bad) + *-ison* (suffix forming nouns of action or result), from Latin *-itionem*, accusative singular of *-itio* (action, state, result). Ultimately from the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂ey-/*h₂i- (to throw, send).
