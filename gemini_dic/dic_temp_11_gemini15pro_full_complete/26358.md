by-and-large:/ˌbaɪ ən ˈlɑːrdʒ/

PHRASE->
(This phrase doesn't typically form set phrases. It functions as a standalone adverbial. However, here are some examples of how it can be used within a sentence.)
1. The food was, by and large, pretty good.
2. By and large, the new policy has been successful.
3. He's, by and large, a reliable person.
4. By and large, I agree with your assessment.
5. The students are, by and large, happy with the course.


词源:  The phrase originates from nautical terminology. "By" refers to sailing against the wind, and "large" refers to sailing with the wind.  Therefore, "by and large" originally meant sailing in all directions, hence covering all aspects of a situation.
