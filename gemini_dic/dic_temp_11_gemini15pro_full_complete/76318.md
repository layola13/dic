gabionade:/ˌɡæbiəˈneɪd/ | n. | 防冲结构（用装满石头等的金属网箱构成的）; (尤指) 笼式防御工事 | A gabionade is a structure made of gabions, typically used for erosion control or as a defensive fortification, especially one quickly constructed using readily available materials. |  The engineers quickly constructed a gabionade to protect the bridge from the rising floodwaters. 工程师们迅速建造了一个笼式结构，以保护桥梁免受上涨的洪水的影响。

The gabionade offered a surprisingly effective defense against the enemy's artillery.  笼式防御工事出人意料地有效地抵御了敌人的炮火。 | 近义词:  revetment, embankment, fortification | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "open ground") | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *gabionnade*, from *gabion* ("gabion") + *-ade* (suffix forming nouns of action or result).  *Gabion* ultimately comes from Italian *gabbione*, augmentative of *gabbia* ("cage"), from Late Latin *cavea* ("cage, enclosure"), from Latin *cavus* ("hollow").
