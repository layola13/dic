hermitically:/hərˈmetɪkli/| adv.| 密封地，气密地 | In a way that is completely airtight; sealed so as to prevent the entry or escape of air. | The tomb was hermitically sealed. 墓穴被密封了。| Synonym: airtightly, tightly, securely | Antonym: loosely, openly | adverb

hermetic:/hərˈmetɪk/ | adj. | 密封的，气密的；深奥的 |  (of a seal) airtight;  (especially of an argument or theory) insulated from outside influence. | The jar had a hermetic seal. 罐子有一个密封的盖子。/  The group's hermetic way of life made them insular. 这个团体封闭的生活方式使他们变得狭隘。 | Synonym: airtight, sealed, closed, obscure, esoteric | Antonym: open, permeable, exoteric | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin hermeticus, from Greek hermētikos “of Hermes Trismegistus,” from Hermēs.  Hermes Trismegistus was a legendary Hellenistic figure associated with alchemy and magic, and his name is related to the Greek god Hermes. The notion of a hermetic seal comes from the alchemists' practice of sealing vessels airtight.
