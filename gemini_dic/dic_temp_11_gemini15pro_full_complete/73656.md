forge:/fɔːrdʒ/ /fɔːrdʒ/| n. vt.|锻造；伪造；建立；|A place where metal is heated and shaped by hammering.  To make or shape metal by heating it in a fire and hammering it. To create something strong, new, or successful, especially with difficulty.  To make an illegal copy of something in order to deceive.|例句：The blacksmith forged a horseshoe. 铁匠锻造了一个马蹄铁。 He was caught forging banknotes. 他因伪造钞票被捕。|近义词：create, build, fashion, fabricate, counterfeit|反义词：destroy, dismantle|词性:名词,动词

forge:/fɔːrdʒ/ /fɔːrdʒ/| v.|锻造；伪造；稳步前进；|To make or shape metal by heating it in a fire and hammering it. To create something strong, new, or successful, especially with difficulty.  To make an illegal copy of something in order to deceive. To move forward steadily, especially with effort.|例句：They forged a new alliance. 他们建立了新的联盟。 The car forged through the snow. 汽车在雪中艰难前行。|近义词：advance, proceed, progress, create, build, fashion, fabricate, counterfeit|反义词：retreat, recede, destroy, dismantle|词性:动词
