immunizations:/ˌɪmjʊnɪˈzeɪʃənz/
n.
免疫接种，疫苗接种
the act of making someone immune to infection, typically by inoculation.
例句：Childhood immunizations protect against many serious diseases. 儿童免疫接种可以预防许多严重的疾病。

例句：Ensure your immunizations are up to date before traveling abroad. 出国旅行前，请确保你的疫苗接种是最新的。

近义词：vaccinations, inoculations
反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑用 susceptibility to disease 易患疾病)
词性: noun (plural)


immunization:/ˌɪmjʊnɪˈzeɪʃən/
n.
免疫接种，疫苗接种
the act of making someone immune to infection, typically by inoculation.
例句：The immunization program has significantly reduced the incidence of measles. 免疫接种计划已大大减少了麻疹的发病率。

例句：He received an immunization against tetanus. 他接种了破伤风疫苗。

近义词：vaccination, inoculation
反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑用 susceptibility to disease 易患疾病)
词性: noun


词源:  From immunize + -ation.  Immunize comes from immune, from French immun (now immune), from Latin immunis "exempt, free, not liable," from in- "not" (from PIE root *en "in") + munis "performing services, obligated" (from PIE root *mei- (1) "to change, go, move").
