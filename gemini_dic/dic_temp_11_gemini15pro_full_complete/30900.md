chagrin:/ʃəˈɡrɪn/ /ʃəˈɡrin/| n. |懊恼，失望，恼怒|A feeling of disappointment, humiliation, or annoyance, typically arising from having one's hopes or plans frustrated.|He felt chagrin at having been rejected. 他因被拒绝而感到懊恼。|Synonyms: annoyance, vexation, irritation, displeasure, mortification|Antonyms: delight, pleasure, satisfaction|noun

chagrin:/ʃəˈɡrɪn/ /ʃəˈɡrin/| vt. |使懊恼，使失望，使恼怒|To feel or cause to feel chagrin.|It chagrined him that he had to ask for help.  他不得不请求帮助，这让他很懊恼。|Synonyms: mortify, humiliate, disconcert, abash|Antonyms: please, delight, gratify|verb
