Etymology:

"over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi "over, above, beyond") + "rigid" (from Latin rigidus "stiff, hard, inflexible," from rigēre "to be stiff") + "-ness" (Old English -nes(s), forming abstract nouns of quality or state from adjectives)
