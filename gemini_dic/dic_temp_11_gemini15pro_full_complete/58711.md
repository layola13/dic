dúnadan:/ˈduːnədɑːn/| n. |杜内丹人|A race of Men in J.R.R. Tolkien's fictional Middle-earth, descended from the Númenóreans.  They are known for their long lifespans, wisdom, and nobility.  The most famous Dúnedain are Aragorn and Elrond.|Aragorn, son of Arathorn, was a Dúnadan, heir to the throne of Gondor.  阿拉贡，阿拉松之子，是一位杜内丹人，冈多的王位继承人。

The Dúnedain of the North kept watch over the Shire for centuries. 北方的杜内丹人几个世纪以来一直守护着夏尔。|近义词：Númenórean (in later ages), Man (generally)|反义词: Orc, other evil races of Middle-earth|词性:noun
