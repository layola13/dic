interspersal:/ˌɪntərˈspɜːrsl/ | n. |散布，点缀，穿插|The act of scattering or distributing something among other things.|The author enlivens his historical narrative with the interspersal of anecdotes. 作者用穿插的轶事使他的历史叙事更加生动。|近义词：distribution, scattering, insertion, intermixture|反义词：consolidation, concentration|词性:名词

intersperse:/ˌɪntərˈspɜːrs/ | vt. |散布，点缀，穿插|To scatter or distribute something among other things.|He interspersed his speech with jokes. 他在演讲中穿插了笑话。|近义词：scatter, distribute, insert, intermix|反义词：consolidate, concentrate|词性:动词

词源: From Late Latin *intersparsus, past participle of interspargere "to scatter among," from inter- "between" + spargere "to scatter".
