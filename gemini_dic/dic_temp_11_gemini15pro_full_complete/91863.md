hyperinflated:/ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪtɪd/
adjective
过度膨胀的
(of a currency or economy) having prices rising rapidly and uncontrollably.
例句：The hyperinflated economy made it difficult for people to afford basic necessities.  过度膨胀的经济使得人们难以负担基本生活必需品。

例句：During the hyperinflated period, prices doubled every few days.  在恶性通货膨胀时期，物价每隔几天就翻一番。
近义词：inflated, overvalued
反义词：deflated, undervalued
词性: adjective


Etymology:
From hyper- + inflated, past participle of inflate.  Hyper- comes from Greek *hyper* meaning "over, beyond." Inflate comes from Latin *inflare*  meaning "to blow into, inflate."
