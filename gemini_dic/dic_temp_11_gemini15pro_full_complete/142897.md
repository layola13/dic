out-on-a-limb:/ˌaʊt ɒn ə ˈlɪm/| idiom.|处于危险境地；孤立无援；冒险|In a risky or precarious position; isolated and unsupported; taking a chance.|例句：By criticizing the mayor, the councilor went out on a limb. 这位议员批评市长，让自己处于危险境地。|例句：She's going out on a limb by investing all her savings in this new business. 她把所有的积蓄都投资到这项新业务上，这是在冒险。|近义词：vulnerable, exposed, risky, precarious|反义词：safe, secure, protected|词性:习语


ETYMOLOGY->
This idiom originates from the image of climbing a tree and venturing out onto a branch (a limb). The further out you go, the more precarious your position becomes, and the greater the risk of falling.  The metaphorical meaning developed from this physical image.
