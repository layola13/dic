gain-over:/ˈɡeɪnˌoʊvər/ | n. | 增益，盈利，优势 | An increase in amount, value, or strength.  Specifically, in finance, the profit from a transaction. | The company reported a significant gain-over on the sale of its subsidiary. 该公司报告其子公司出售获得了巨额利润。| Synonyms: profit, advantage, increase, surplus, windfall | Antonyms: loss, deficit, decrease | noun

gain-over:/ˈɡeɪnˌoʊvər/ | n. | 超越，领先 | An amount or degree by which one thing exceeds another. | The team achieved a gain-over of 10 points in the second half.  该队在下半场取得了10分的领先优势。| Synonyms: lead, edge, advantage, superiority | Antonyms: deficit, disadvantage, inferiority | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "gain" (from Old North French *gaignier, ultimately from Frankish *waidanjanan "to hunt, procure food, gain") + "over" (from Old English ofer "above, across, beyond").  The combined meaning implies gaining something "over" a previous state, cost, or competitor.
