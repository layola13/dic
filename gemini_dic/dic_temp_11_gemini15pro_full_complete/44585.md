croupiest:/ˈkruːpiɪst/| adj.|最像赌场荷官的; 最阴沉的; 最冷酷无情的 |  (Of a person) resembling or characteristic of a croupier, especially in being impassive or taciturn. |  He gave a croupiest stare, as if assessing the value of her chips. 他冷冷地盯着她，就像在评估她筹码的价值一样。 | 近义词：impassive, taciturn, poker-faced, expressionless, deadpan | 反义词： expressive, emotional, animated | 词性: adjective

词源: From *croupier* + *-est*.  The word "croupier" comes from French, originally meaning "one who sits on the croup" (the rump of a horse), referring to someone who sat behind the gambler to assist them. The suffix "-est" is used to form the superlative degree of adjectives.
