overprint:/ˌoʊvərˈprɪnt/ | /ˌəʊvəˈprɪnt/ | n. v. |套印；叠印；加印 | An overprint is something printed over something else that has already been printed.  As a verb, it means to print over something already printed. | The stamp had an overprint that increased its value. 这枚邮票有一个加印，增加了它的价值。 | The company overprinted the incorrect price with the correct one. 公司在错误的价格上套印了正确的价格。 | synonyms: superimpose, surcharge | antonyms: underprint (although not a direct opposite in meaning) | noun/verb

overprint:/ˌoʊvərˈprɪnt/ | /ˌəʊvəˈprɪnt/ | n. v. |加印；叠印；套印 |  An overprint is an additional layer of printing added on top of an existing printed image. As a verb, it refers to the act of adding this extra layer of printing.  | The commemorative stamp had an overprint marking the centenary.  这枚纪念邮票加印了百年纪念的字样。 |  They had to overprint the brochures with the new dates. 他们不得不在宣传册上加印新的日期。 | synonyms: superimprint, surprint | antonyms:  (no direct antonym, but consider "original print") | noun/verb
