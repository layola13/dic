multungulate:/ˌmʌltʌŋˈɡjʊleɪt/ | adj. n. | 多趾的，多趾动物 | Having more than the usual number of toes (typically two or three) on each foot; an animal having such feet. |  Example:  The multungulate creature left strange tracks in the mud.  多趾动物在泥里留下了奇怪的足迹。 | Example:  Ancient multungulates roamed this area millions of years ago.  数百万年前，古代多趾动物在这片区域漫游。|  Synonyms: polydactyl (for the condition),  | Antonyms:  artiodactyl, perissodactyl | adjective, noun

Etymology: From Latin *multus* "many" + ungula "hoof, claw, nail"
