latitudinarian:/ˌlætɪˌtjuːdɪˈnɛəriən/
noun.|自由主义者；（尤指宗教方面）心胸宽广的人|A person, especially one in a position of authority, who is tolerant of the views of others; broad-minded, especially in matters of religion.|The bishop was known for his latitudinarian views, welcoming people of all faiths into his diocese. 主教以其宽容的观点而闻名，欢迎所有信仰的人加入他的教区。|Synonyms: liberal, tolerant, broad-minded, open-minded, permissive, indulgent, unbiased, unprejudiced, non-dogmatic, undogmatic|Antonyms: intolerant, narrow-minded, dogmatic, bigoted, prejudiced, biased|n.

latitudinarian:/ˌlætɪˌtjuːdɪˈnɛəriən/
adjective.|自由主义的；（尤指宗教方面）心胸宽广的|Tolerant of the views of others; broad-minded, especially in matters of religion.|The university had a latitudinarian approach to admissions, accepting students from diverse backgrounds. 这所大学对招生采取了自由主义的态度，接受来自不同背景的学生。|Synonyms: liberal, tolerant, broad-minded, open-minded, permissive, indulgent, unbiased, unprejudiced, non-dogmatic, undogmatic|Antonyms: intolerant, narrow-minded, dogmatic, bigoted, prejudiced, biased|adj.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin latitudo ("breadth, width"), from Latin latus ("broad, wide").  The term originally referred to a group of 17th-century English clergymen who advocated for greater freedom of thought and interpretation within the Church of England.
