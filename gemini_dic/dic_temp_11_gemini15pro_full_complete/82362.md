grown-up: /ˈɡroʊnˌʌp/ (US) /ˈɡrəʊnˌʌp/ (UK)| adj.|成年的|Having reached full physical and mental development.|例句：They have two grown-up children. 他们有两个成年的孩子。|例句：It's a film suitable for grown-up audiences. 这是一部适合成年观众观看的电影。|近义词：adult, mature|反义词：childish, immature|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the past participle of "grow up," meaning to develop from childhood to adulthood.  "Grow" comes from Old English "growan," and "up" from Old English "up" or "uppe."
