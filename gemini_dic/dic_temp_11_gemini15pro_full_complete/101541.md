ironic:/aɪˈrɑːnɪk/ | adj. | 讽刺的，具有讽刺意味的 | Happening in the opposite way to what is expected, and typically causing amusement because of this. |  It's ironic that the fire station burned down. 消防站被烧毁了，真是讽刺。 | It is ironic that he became a teacher - he used to hate school.  他竟然成了老师，真是讽刺——他以前讨厌上学。| 近义词：sarcastic, sardonic, paradoxical | 反义词：sincere, straightforward | 词性: adjective

irony:/ˈaɪrəni/ | n. | 讽刺，反讽 | A state of affairs or an event that seems deliberately contrary to what one expects and is often amusing as a result. | The irony is that I thought I was helping him, but I actually made things worse.  讽刺的是，我以为我在帮他，实际上却把事情弄得更糟了。|  The irony of the situation was lost on him.  他没能领会到情况的讽刺之处。| 近义词：sarcasm, paradox | 反义词：sincerity, seriousness | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *ironique*, from Greek *eirōneia* "dissimulation, assumed ignorance," from *eirōn* "dissembler," literally "one who says less than he thinks," from *eirein* "to speak."
