catalepsy: /ˈkætəlɛpsi/ | n. | 僵住症，木僵 | A medical condition characterized by a trancelike state with loss of voluntary motion and failure to react to stimuli. |  The patient experienced a sudden onset of catalepsy, remaining motionless for several minutes. 这位病人突然出现了僵住症，一动不动地保持了几分钟。

Catalepsy can be a symptom of various neurological and psychiatric disorders.  僵住症可能是各种神经和精神疾病的症状。 | 近义词: trance, stupor, rigidity | 反义词: mobility, flexibility | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *catalepsis, from Ancient Greek κατάληψις (katálēpsis, “seizure”), from καταλαμβάνω (katalambánō, “I seize, take possession of”).
