backbite: /ˈbækˌbaɪt/ (US), /ˈbakˌbaɪt/ (UK)
verb
背后说坏话, 诽谤
To speak spitefully and falsely of someone who is absent.
例句：He was known to backbite his colleagues to gain favor with the boss.  他因为爱在老板面前说同事坏话而出名。

例句：She refused to backbite, even though she knew it might benefit her career.  即使她知道这可能对她的职业生涯有好处，她也拒绝背后说人坏话。


近义词：slander, defame, malign, gossip, badmouth
反义词：praise, commend, laud, compliment
词性：verb

backbite: /ˈbækˌbaɪt/ (US), /ˈbakˌbaɪt/ (UK)
noun
背后诽谤, 谗言
An act or instance of speaking spitefully and falsely about someone who is absent.
例句：The backbite spread quickly through the office, damaging her reputation. 办公室里流言蜚语迅速传播，损害了她的名誉。

例句：His constant backbites made it difficult for anyone to trust him.  他不停地说人坏话，让任何人都很难信任他。

近义词：slander, defamation, calumny
反义词：praise, commendation
词性：noun
