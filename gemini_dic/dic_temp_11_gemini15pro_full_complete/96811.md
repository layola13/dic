inertitude:/ɪˈnɜːrtɪtjuːd/ /ɪˈnɜːrtɪtuːd/| n. |惰性；迟钝；不活动|A disposition to remain inactive or inert; sluggishness; lack of energy.|His inertitude prevented him from finishing the project. 他的惰性使他无法完成这个项目。|The inertitude of the government in the face of the crisis was appalling. 政府在危机面前的迟钝令人震惊。|近义词：inertness, inactivity, lethargy, sluggishness, apathy|反义词：activity, energy, vigor, dynamism|词性:名词

Etymology:
From French *inertitude*, from Late Latin *inertitūdō*, from Latin *inert-* (stem of *iners* "inactive, unskilled") + *-itūdō* (suffix forming abstract nouns of quality).
