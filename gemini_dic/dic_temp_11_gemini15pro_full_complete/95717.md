inconscionable:/ɪnˈkɒnʃənəbl/ /ɪnˈkɑːnʃənəbl/
adjective
不合理的，过分的，昧着良心的
Not right or reasonable; unreasonably excessive or outrageous. Showing no concern for what is right.
例句：The judge deemed the contract terms inconscionable and refused to enforce them.  法官认为合同条款不合理，拒绝执行。

例句：It is inconscionable to make such a young child work so many hours.  让这么小的孩子工作这么多小时是昧着良心的。
近义词：unreasonable, excessive, outrageous, unethical, immoral, unscrupulous
反义词：reasonable, fair, just, ethical, moral, scrupulous
词性: adjective

词源:  Late Middle English: from late Latin inconscionabilis, from in- ‘not’ + conscire ‘be (mutually) aware’ (from con- ‘together’ + scire ‘know’).
