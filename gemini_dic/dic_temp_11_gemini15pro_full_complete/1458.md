acephalocystic: /ˌeɪˌsefəloʊˈsɪstɪk/ | adj. | 无头囊虫的，无头蚴的 |  Relating to or characterized by acephalocysts (sterile hydatid cysts lacking heads or scolices, typically found in the liver or lungs). These cysts are formed by the larval stage of certain tapeworms, particularly *Echinococcus granulosus*. |  Acephalocystic hydatid disease is less common than the typical hydatid disease. 无头囊虫病比典型的包虫病少见。| The doctor diagnosed an acephalocystic lesion in the patient's liver. 医生诊断出病人肝脏有无头囊肿病灶。 |  Near Synonyms: sterile hydatid cyst,  | Antonyms: fertile hydatid cyst | word type: adjective

Etymology:
From a- (without) + cephalo- (head) + cyst + -ic (adjective forming suffix).  The term combines Greek roots to describe a cyst lacking a head (scolex), which is a characteristic of the sterile form of hydatid cysts.
