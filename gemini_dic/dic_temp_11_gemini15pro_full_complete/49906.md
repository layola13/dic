demystifiers:/ˌdiːˈmɪstɪfaɪərz/
|n.|揭秘者，除魅者|Someone or something that demystifies a subject or process, making it easier to understand.|The book served as a demystifier for many complex scientific concepts.|近义词: explainer, clarifier, interpreter, illuminator|反义词: obfuscator, mystifier|词性:名词

Etymology:
de- (prefix): meaning "remove," "reverse," or "reduce" (from Latin)
mystify (verb): to confuse or puzzle someone (from Late Latin mystificare, from Latin mysticus "mystic" + -ficare "-fy")
-er (suffix): used to form nouns indicating a person or thing that performs a particular action.
