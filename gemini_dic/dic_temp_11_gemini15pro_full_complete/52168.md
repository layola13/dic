diaphragms:/ˈdaɪəfræmz/ (US), /ˈdaɪəfræmz/ (UK)| n. |隔膜；光圈；横膈膜|A thin sheet of material, especially rubber, used to prevent pregnancy or sexually transmitted infections. Also, a dome-shaped muscular partition separating the thorax from the abdomen in mammals. It plays a major role in breathing, as its contraction increases the volume of the thorax and so inflates the lungs.|The doctor advised her to use a diaphragm. 医生建议她使用子宫帽。| The diaphragm contracts and relaxes to control breathing. 横膈膜收缩和放松来控制呼吸。|Synonyms: membrane, partition, contraceptive, septum|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, depends on the specific meaning.
