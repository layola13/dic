miser:/ˈmaɪzər/| n.|守财奴，吝啬鬼|A person who hoards wealth and spends as little money as possible.|Scrooge was a miser who hated spending money. 斯克鲁奇是个守财奴，讨厌花钱。|He's such a miser, he wouldn't even buy his own mother a birthday present. 他真是个吝啬鬼，甚至连给他妈妈买生日礼物都不肯。|近义词：skinflint, cheapskate, niggard|反义词：spendthrift, philanthropist|词性: noun

miser:/ˈmaɪzər/| n.|吝啬鬼，守财奴|From Old French *misere* "wretchedness, poverty, misfortune,"  from Latin miseria "wretchedness, misery, misfortune, distress; a cause of misery," from miser "wretched, unfortunate, miserable," of uncertain origin.|She inherited a fortune but lives like a miser. 她继承了一大笔财产，却过着守财奴般的生活。|The old miser wouldn't give a penny to charity.  这个老吝啬鬼一分钱也不肯捐给慈善机构。|近义词：penny-pincher, tightwad|反义词：generous person, benefactor|词性: noun
