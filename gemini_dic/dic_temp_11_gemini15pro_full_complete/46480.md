daimyor:/daɪˈmaɪɔːr/ |n.|大名; 尊称; 阁下|(Archaic) A title of respect, similar to "lord" or "sir," used especially in addressing a Japanese feudal lord.|Daimyo ruled over vast stretches of land. | 近义词:lord, shogun, nobleman| 反义词: peasant, commoner| 词性:名词


词源: From Japanese 大名 (daimyō), from 大 (dai, “large”) + 名 (myō, from 名 (na, “name”) + suffix -myō).
