acclimation:/ˌækləˈmeɪʃən/ | n. | 适应，驯化 | The process of adapting to a new climate, environment, or situation. |  The acclimation of the new students took several weeks. 新生适应环境花了几个星期。

The plant underwent a period of acclimation in the greenhouse before being planted outside. 这株植物在被种植到室外之前在温室里经历了一段时间的适应。 | 近义词：adaptation, adjustment, accommodation | 反义词：inadaptability | 词性：名词

acclimatise:/əˈklaɪmətaɪz/ | v. | 使适应，使服水土 | To become accustomed to a new climate or environment. |  It took me a while to acclimatise to the heat. 我花了一段时间才适应炎热的天气。

The climbers needed to acclimatise to the altitude before attempting the summit. 登山者需要先适应海拔高度，然后再尝试登顶。 | 近义词：adapt, adjust, acclimate | 反义词： – | 词性：动词

acclimatize:/əˈklaɪmətaɪz/ | v. | 使适应，使服水土 | To become accustomed to a new climate or environment. (American spelling) | It took the new puppy a few days to acclimatize to its new home.  新的小狗花了几天时间才适应了它的新家。

She acclimatized quickly to the fast-paced city life. 她很快就适应了快节奏的城市生活。 | 近义词：adapt, adjust, acclimate | 反义词： – | 词性：动词
