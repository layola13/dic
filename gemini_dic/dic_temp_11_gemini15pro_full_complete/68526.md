fanciful:/ˈfænsɪfəl/ | adj. | 富于幻想的；异想天开的；不切实际的 | Characterized by imagination and fondness of inventing; whimsical; unreal; capricious.  |  She had a fanciful notion of becoming a famous actress. 她有一个成为著名女演员的幻想。 | synonyms: imaginative, whimsical, inventive, creative, visionary | antonyms: realistic, practical, down-to-earth, pragmatic | adjective

fanciful:/ˈfænsɪfəl/ | adj. | 装饰华丽的；奇特的；花哨的 | Ornamented in an unusual, elaborate, or exaggerated way. | The building had a fanciful, almost fairytale-like quality. 这栋建筑有一种奇特的，近乎童话般的气质。 | synonyms: ornate, elaborate, decorative, flamboyant, extravagant | antonyms: plain, simple, austere, unadorned | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fansiful*, from *fansie* (fancy) + *-ful* (full of).  Ultimately from Latin *phantasia* (fantasy).
