consecrator:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪtər/ (US) /ˈkɒnsɪkreɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n. |祝圣者，授予圣职者|A person who performs a consecration ceremony, especially a bishop who ordains someone into a religious office. |The consecrator laid his hands on the new priest's head. 祝圣者将手放在新神父的头上。|Synonyms: ordainer, initiator|Antonyms: None|Noun

consecrate:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/ (US) /ˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/ (UK)| v. |祝圣，奉献|To make or declare something sacred; to dedicate formally to a religious or divine purpose. |The church was consecrated in 1850. 这座教堂于1850年祝圣。|Synonyms: sanctify, hallow, dedicate|Antonyms: desecrate, profane|Verb
