addressability:/əˌdrɛsəˈbɪləti/ | n. |可寻址性，可访问性|The ability of a device or system to be individually accessed or controlled.  This often refers to the ability to locate and communicate with a specific component within a larger network or system.|The increased addressability of memory allows for more efficient data retrieval. 内存可寻址性的提高使得数据检索更加高效。|Synonyms: accessibility, reachability |Antonyms: inaccessibility |noun

addressability:/əˌdrɛsəˈbɪləti/ | n. |可寻址性，可控性|The capability of being identified and interacted with individually, particularly in the context of computer systems and networks. This implies that each element can be uniquely targeted and manipulated.|The addressability of individual pixels on the screen allows for precise image manipulation.  屏幕上单个像素的可寻址性允许精确的图像操作。|Synonyms: locatability, identifiability |Antonyms: unidentifiability |noun
