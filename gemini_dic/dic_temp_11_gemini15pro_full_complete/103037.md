jellies:/ˈdʒɛliz/| n.|果冻；胶状物；凉粉；(jelly 的复数)|A sweet, clear food made with gelatin, sugar, and fruit juice, or a similar food made with meat or fish. Also refers to substances with a gelatinous consistency.|The children love eating jellies. 孩子们喜欢吃果冻。|
jellies:/ˈdʒɛliz/| v.|结冻；凝结；(jelly 的第三人称单数)|To become or make something become a jelly.|The juice jellied when it cooled. 果汁冷却后凝结了。|
jellies:/ˈdʒɛliz/| n.|女鞋；(jelly 的复数)|(Informal) Women's shoes made of PVC plastic, typically worn in warm weather.|She bought a new pair of jellies for the summer. 她为夏天买了一双新的果冻鞋。|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gelee, from gelée, past participle of geler "to freeze," from Latin gelare "to freeze," from gelu "frost." The word acquired its association with gelatinous foods through the process of boiling and cooling ingredients to create a jelly-like consistency.
