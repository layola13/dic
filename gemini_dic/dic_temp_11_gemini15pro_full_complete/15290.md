backlogging:/ˈbæklɒɡɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈbæklɔːɡɪŋ/ (US)| n.|积压，积压的工作|The act of accumulating a backlog of work or tasks that have not been completed.  This can refer to various fields, such as software development, order fulfillment, or general administrative tasks.|例句：The team is working hard to reduce the backlogging of customer support tickets.  团队正在努力减少积压的客户支持请求。

例句：The backlogging of orders caused significant delays in shipping. 订单积压导致发货严重延误。|近义词：accumulation, backlog, pile-up|反义词：completion, clearance|词性:名词 (derived from the verb "backlog")

backlog:/ˈbæklɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈbæklɔːɡ/ (US) | n. vt.|积压，积压的工作；积压|A buildup of work or tasks that have not been completed. As a verb, it means to accumulate a backlog.|例句：The company faced a huge backlog of orders after the holiday season. 假期过后，公司面临大量的订单积压。

例句：The project manager needs to backlog the tasks that are not critical for the current sprint. 项目经理需要将当前冲刺中不重要的任务积压起来。|近义词：accumulation, arrears, queue|反义词：completion, clearance|词性:名词, 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "back" + "log". "Log" originally referred to a logbook or record of work done.  "Backlog" thus refers to work that has been logged but not yet completed, hence "behind" in the log.
