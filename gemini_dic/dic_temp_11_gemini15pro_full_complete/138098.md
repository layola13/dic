normativist:/ˈnɔːrmətɪvɪst/| n. |规范主义者；规范论者|Someone who adheres to or believes in normativism, the philosophical view that claims some things, such as moral truths, are not merely descriptive but prescribe how things ought to be.|The normativist argued that ethical principles are not subjective but grounded in objective moral truths.|近义词:prescriptivist|反义词:descriptivist|词性:noun

normativistic:/ˌnɔːrməˈtɪvɪstɪk/| adj. |规范主义的；规范论的|Relating to or characteristic of normativism.|His normativistic approach to ethics led him to believe in universal moral principles.|近义词:prescriptive|反义词:descriptive|词性:adjective
