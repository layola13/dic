larval:/ˈlɑːrvəl/ | adj. | 幼虫的；幼虫期的 | Relating to or characteristic of a larva; in the larval stage. |  The larval stage of a butterfly is called a caterpillar. 蝴蝶的幼虫阶段被称为毛毛虫。| Synonyms: immature, undeveloped, nascent | Antonyms: mature, adult, developed | adjective

larva:/ˈlɑːrvə/ | n. | 幼虫；幼体 | The immature, wingless, and often worm-like feeding form of many insects that undergo metamorphosis. | The mosquito larva lives in water. 蚊子的幼虫生活在水中。| Synonyms: grub, maggot, caterpillar | Antonyms: adult, imago | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *larva* "ghost, mask, evil spirit," of uncertain origin. The biological sense emerged in the 17th century, perhaps influenced by the notion of an insect's immature form being "masked" or disguised compared to its adult form.
