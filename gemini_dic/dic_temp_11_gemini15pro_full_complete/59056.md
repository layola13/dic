dwell:/dwɛl/ | vi.|居住；存在于；细想；详述|To live in a place; to remain or continue; to think, speak, or write at length about.|He dwelled in a small cottage by the sea. 他住在海边的一个小别墅里。|近义词：live, reside, inhabit, linger, ponder|反义词：depart, leave, vacate|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dwellan (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *dweljaną (“to be stunned, to linger, to delay”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰwelh₁- (“to become dizzy, confused”). Cognate with Old Saxon dwalōn (“to err, go astray”), Old Frisian dwala (“to err, be mistaken”), Middle Dutch dwellen (“to delay, linger”), Dutch dwalen (“to wander, err, stray”), Old High German twellan (“to hinder, lead astray”), German twellen (“to tarry”), Old Norse dvelja (“to delay, hinder”), Swedish dväljas (“to linger, tarry”).
