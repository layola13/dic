novation:/nəʊˈveɪʃən/ /noʊˈveɪʃən/ | n. | 革新；更新；取代；债务转让 | The act of replacing an old obligation with a new one. The substitution of a new contract, debt, or obligation for an existing one.|  Example 1: The company underwent a novation of its computer systems, replacing all the old machines.  公司对其计算机系统进行了革新，更换了所有旧机器。

Example 2:  The novation of the debt meant that John was no longer responsible for the payments. 债务转让意味着约翰不再负责付款。 |  Synonym: replacement, substitution, renewal, innovation | Antonym:  continuation, stagnation | noun


novation:/nəʊˈveɪʃən/ /noʊˈveɪʃən/ | vt. |  革新；更新；以新代旧；转移(债务等) | To replace something with something new; to transfer a debt or obligation. | Example 1: They novated the old contract with a new one that included updated terms. 他们用包含更新条款的新合同取代了旧合同。


Example 2: The bank novated the loan to another lender.  银行将贷款转移给了另一家贷款机构。 | Synonym: replace, substitute, transfer | Antonym: retain, maintain | verb
