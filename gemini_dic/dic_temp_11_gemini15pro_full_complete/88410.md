hirudine:/ˈhɪrʊdiːn/ /ˈhɪrudɪn/| n.|水蛭素|A naturally occurring peptide in the salivary glands of medicinal leeches (Hirudo medicinalis) that has a powerful anticoagulant effect.|Hirudin is used medically to prevent blood clotting.|Synonym: anticoagulant|Antonym: coagulant|noun

hirudin:/ˈhɪrʊdiːn/ /ˈhɪrudɪn/| n.|水蛭素|A polypeptide in the saliva of leeches, such as Hirudo medicinalis, which acts as a potent antithrombin by preventing the conversion of prothrombin to thrombin.|The doctor prescribed hirudin to the patient to prevent blood clots.|Synonym: antithrombin|Antonym: procoagulant|noun
