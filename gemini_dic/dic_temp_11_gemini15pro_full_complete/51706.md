devise:/dɪˈvaɪz/| v. n.|设计，发明，想出；遗赠|To invent or plan something, especially with cleverness or imagination; to leave (real estate) to someone by the terms of a will.|例句：He devised a new method for teaching English. 他设计了一种新的英语教学方法。|例句：She devised a plan to escape. 她想出了一个逃跑计划。|近义词：invent, create, contrive, formulate, design|反义词：copy, imitate|词性：动词

devise:/dɪˈvaɪz/| n.|遗赠条款；遗赠物|A clause in a will that leaves real estate to someone.|例句：The devise of the family estate to his nephew caused resentment among his children. 他将家族财产遗赠给侄子的条款引起了他孩子们的怨恨。|例句：The devise included the house and surrounding land.  这份遗赠包括房屋和周围的土地。|近义词：bequest, legacy|反义词：N/A|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *deviser* "to divide, distribute, arrange, direct, order," from Late Latin *divisare*, frequentative of Latin *dividere* "to divide."  The meaning shifted from "divide" to "distinguish" and then to "contrive, invent."
