carapace: /ˈkærəpeɪs/ | n. | 甲壳，背甲 |  The hard upper shell of a turtle, crustacean, or arachnid.  It serves as a protective covering. | The turtle retreated into its carapace when it sensed danger. 海龟感觉到危险时就缩进了它的壳里。 |  The crab's carapace was a vibrant blue.  螃蟹的背甲是鲜艳的蓝色。 | 近义词：shell, covering, exoskeleton | 反义词: underside, belly | 词性: 名词

词源: From Old Spanish *carapacho (unattested) or Old Catalan carapace, perhaps from Arabic قَرَابَضَ (qarabaḍa, “to stick, adhere”), or perhaps a blend of Late Latin cappa (“cape, hood”) + Latin testa (“shell”).
