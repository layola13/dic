interpose:/ˌɪntərˈpoʊz/ | v. |插入，介入，干预；提出（异议等）| To place or insert between two other things; to intervene or interfere in a situation. | The teacher interposed herself between the two arguing students. 老师介入两个争吵的学生之间。| He interposed an objection during the meeting. 他在会议期间提出了反对意见。| 近义词: intervene, mediate, intercede, insert | 反义词: withdraw, retreat, abstain | 词性: 动词

interposed:/ˌɪntərˈpoʊzd/ | adj. |插入的，介入的| Placed or inserted between two other things. | The interposed layer of insulation prevented heat loss.  插入的隔热层防止了热量损失。 |  The treaty was signed after interposed discussions between the two countries.  经过两国之间的插入性讨论，该条约得以签署。 | 近义词: inserted, intervening, intermediary | 反义词: removed, separate, disconnected | 词性: 形容词


词源: From Latin *interpos-, past participle stem of interponere ("to place between, interpose"), from inter ("between") + ponere ("to put, place").
