hutches:/ˈhʌtʃɪz/ | n. | 兔子笼; 橱柜 | A hutch is a cage with a wire or wooden front, used for keeping rabbits or other small animals. A hutch can also be a type of cabinet, usually with open shelves on top and closed storage below. | The rabbit lives in a hutch in the garden. 兔子住在花园里的笼子里。|  The kitchen hutch held her grandmother's china. 厨房里的橱柜摆放着她祖母的瓷器。 | synonyms: cage, pen, coop, cabinet, cupboard | antonyms:  (For animal cage meaning) open field, free range | noun

hutches:/ˈhʌtʃɪz/ | n. | 小屋; 棚屋 (古语或方言) | A small, simple dwelling, often temporary or makeshift. This meaning is less common in modern English. |  They lived in a hutch in the woods. 他们在树林里的小屋里生活。(古语用法) | synonyms: shack, cabin, shed | antonyms: mansion, palace | noun
