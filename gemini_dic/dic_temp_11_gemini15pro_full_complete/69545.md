fellow-feeling:/ˈfeləʊ ˌfiːlɪŋ/ (US) /ˈfeloʊ ˌfiːlɪŋ/| n. |同情；同感；共鸣|A feeling of sympathy and understanding for someone else's feelings, especially someone who is suffering.|Example:  There's a strong sense of fellow-feeling among the members of the group.  组员之间有很强的同理心。

Example:  She expressed fellow-feeling for the victims of the disaster. 她对灾难的受害者表示同情。|近义词：empathy, compassion, sympathy, commiseration|反义词：apathy, indifference, coldness|词性:名词

fellow-feeling:/ˈfeləʊ ˌfiːlɪŋ/ (US) /ˈfeloʊ ˌfiːlɪŋ/| n. |友情；伙伴之情|A feeling of friendship and loyalty among people who share a common interest or experience.|Example: Their shared experience created a deep fellow-feeling between them. 他们共同的经历在他们之间建立了深厚的友情。


Example: There's a wonderful sense of fellow-feeling among the team members. 团队成员之间有一种很棒的伙伴之情。|近义词：camaraderie, solidarity, fraternity, sisterhood|反义词：enmity, hostility, antagonism|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "fellow" (companion, comrade) + "feeling" (emotional state). The term emphasizes a shared emotional experience, stemming from a sense of connection and shared humanity.
