bluffly:/ˈblʌfli/ | adv. | 粗鲁地，唐突地；直率地 | In a blunt and abrupt manner, often appearing rude or insensitive. | He bluffly told her she wasn't qualified for the job. 他粗鲁地告诉她，她不具备这份工作的资格。|  He bluffly refused her request. 他唐突地拒绝了她的请求。 | 近义词: abruptly, bluntly, curtly, roughly | 反义词: politely, tactfully, gently | 词性: 副词

bluff:/blʌf/ | n. | 悬崖；虚张声势 | A steep cliff or bank, or an attempt to deceive someone into believing that one has more power or resources than one actually possesses. |  We hiked to the edge of the bluff and looked out at the ocean. 我们徒步走到悬崖边，眺望大海。| He was bluffing when he said he had a royal flush. 当他说他有同花大顺时，他是在虚张声势。 | 近义词: cliff, pretense, deception | 反义词: honesty, truth | 词性: 名词

bluff:/blʌf/ | v. | 虚张声势；吓唬 | To deceive someone by pretending to have more power or resources than one actually possesses. | She bluffed her way through the interview, even though she knew nothing about the company. 即使她对这家公司一无所知，她也靠虚张声势蒙混过了面试。 | He tried to bluff the police officer, but he was quickly caught. 他试图吓唬警察，但很快就被抓住了。| 近义词: deceive, pretend, feign | 反义词: be honest, tell the truth | 词性: 动词


词源:  From bluff (n.), perhaps from Dutch blaf "flat, broad," also "barking (of a dog)," of uncertain origin. The sense development might be from the image of a dog's bark being deceptively loud.
