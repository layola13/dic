druid: /ˈdruːɪd/ | n. | 德鲁伊；祭司 | A member of the priestly class among the ancient Celts of Gaul, Britain, and Ireland. |  Example 1: The druid performed ancient rituals in the sacred grove. 德鲁伊在神圣的树林里举行古老的仪式。

Example 2:  Legends say the druid could communicate with animals. 传说德鲁伊可以与动物交流。 | 近义词: priest, shaman, mystic | 反义词: None (lack of a clear single antonym) | 词性: Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Irish *druí  (cf. Irish draoi "druid, sorcerer"), from Gaulish *druis, from Proto-Celtic *dru-wid-s, perhaps meaning "oak-knower", from *deru- "oak" + *weid- "to see, know". The connection to oak trees highlights their importance in druidic practices.
