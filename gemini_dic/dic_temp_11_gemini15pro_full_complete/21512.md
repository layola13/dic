blue-blooded:/ˈbluː ˈblʌdɪd/ | adj. | 貴族的；出身名門的 | Of noble or aristocratic descent.  Having or showing the qualities or attitudes associated with high social status. |  例句：He comes from a blue-blooded family. 他来自一个贵族家庭。

例句：She has a blue-blooded disdain for manual labor. 她对体力劳动有一种贵族式的蔑视。 | 近义词：aristocratic, noble, patrician, high-born | 反义词：common, low-born, plebeian | 词性: 形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Spanish phrase "sangre azul" (blue blood), referring to the apparent blueness of the veins of people with pale skin, thus seen as a mark of aristocratic ancestry who did not perform manual labor outdoors and therefore maintained a paler complexion than commoners.
