boogey:/ˈbuːɡi/ (US), /ˈbuːɡɪ/ (UK)| n. |夜魔，妖怪，鬼怪|An imaginary monster, typically used to frighten children.  It can also refer to any source of fear or anxiety.|例句：The children were scared of the boogeyman hiding under their beds. 孩子们害怕躲在床底下的妖怪。

例句：Taxes are the boogeyman for many small business owners.  税收是许多小企业主的梦魇。|近义词：monster, goblin, bogeyman, bugbear|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "comfort", "security")|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From bogeyman, attested from 1836, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to the Middle English bogge ("goblin, scarecrow").  Also possibly influenced by bug.
