galleass: /ˈɡæliæs/ | n. | 大型桨帆船 | A large, low-built galley with sails, used chiefly in the Mediterranean in the 16th and 17th centuries.  A warship propelled by oars and sails and having a more rounded hull and higher freeboard than a galley. | The galleass, a hybrid of galley and sailing ship, played a crucial role in the Battle of Lepanto. 这艘桨帆船，结合了划桨船和帆船的特点，在勒班陀战役中发挥了至关重要的作用。 | Synonyms: warship, galleon | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "merchant ship" as a contrasting vessel type) | noun
