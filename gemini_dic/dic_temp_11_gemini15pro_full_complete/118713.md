metadiscoidal:/ˌmɛtədɪsˈkɔɪdəl/ | adj. | 盘状分裂后期的，后盘状的 |  Relating to or characteristic of a later stage of meroblastic cleavage in which the blastoderm is a disc lying on top of the yolk.  A type of incomplete cleavage that occurs in very yolky eggs, such as birds and reptiles.  The cell divisions do not extend into the yolk mass, resulting in a disc of cells, the blastodisc, sitting atop a large sphere of undivided yolk.  |  Example: The metadiscoidal cleavage pattern is typical of avian development.  鸟类的发育模式为后盘状分裂。

Example:  Reptiles also exhibit metadiscoidal cleavage. 爬行动物也表现出后盘状分裂。 | Near Synonyms: meroblastic, discoidal | Antonyms: holoblastic | Word type: Adjective
