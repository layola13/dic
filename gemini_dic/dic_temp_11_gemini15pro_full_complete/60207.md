efflorescence:/ˌɛfləˈrɛsəns/| n.|风化，开花，(尤指盐类)晶华,  繁荣,兴盛|A powdery deposit on the surface of a rock, wall, etc., especially a salt formed when water carrying it evaporates. The action or process of efflorescing. A state of flourishing or a period of greatest prosperity or productivity.|Example 1: The efflorescence on the brick walls was a sign of rising damp.  砖墙上的风化是潮气上升的迹象。

Example 2: The Renaissance was a period of great cultural efflorescence. 文艺复兴时期是一个文化繁荣的时期。|Synonyms: blooming, blossoming, flowering, flourishing, heyday, peak, prime|Antonyms: decline, decay, deterioration, withering|noun


efflorescences:/ˌɛfləˈrɛsənsiz/| n.|风化(现象), 开花(现象),  (尤指盐类)晶华(复数),  繁荣,兴盛(复数)|Plural form of efflorescence.  Refers to multiple instances of the phenomena described by "efflorescence".|Example 1: The efflorescences on the building were unsightly and indicated a problem with the mortar.  建筑物上的风化现象很难看，表明砂浆有问题。

Example 2:  The numerous cultural efflorescences during the Tang Dynasty are still admired today. 唐代的众多文化繁荣至今仍令人钦佩。|Synonyms: bloomings, blossomings, flowerings, flourishings|Antonyms: declines, decays, deteriorations, witherings|noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Late Latin *efflōrescentia*, from efflōrescēns, present participle of efflōrescere "to blossom, bloom," from ex- "out" + flōrescere "to begin to blossom," from flōs "flower."
