frondeur:/frɔ̃.dœʁ/ (French pronunciation) | n. | 投石党人；叛逆者；反对派 |  A person who is rebellious and challenges authority, especially in a political context. Originally referring to those who participated in the Fronde, a series of uprisings in France during the minority of Louis XIV.  |  The journalist acted as a frondeur, constantly criticizing the government's policies.  这位记者像个投石党人一样，不断批评政府的政策。 | Synonyms: rebel, insurgent, dissident, nonconformist | Antonyms: conformist, loyalist | noun

frondeur:/frɔ̃.dœʁ/ (French pronunciation) | adj. |  叛逆的；反对的 |  Characterized by rebellion or opposition to authority.  |  He had a frondeur attitude towards the established order.  他对既定秩序持反对态度。 | Synonyms: rebellious, defiant, recalcitrant, insubordinate | Antonyms: obedient, compliant, submissive | adjective

词源:
From French frondeur, from fronde (“sling”), referring to the Fronde, a series of uprisings in France (1648–1653) during the minority of Louis XIV, where the rebels sometimes threw stones at the windows of Cardinal Mazarin's supporters.
来自法语 frondeur，源自 fronde（“投石器”），指的是在路易十四年幼时期发生在法国的一系列起义（1648-1653 年），称为投石党运动，叛乱者有时会向马萨林红衣主教支持者的窗户扔石头。
