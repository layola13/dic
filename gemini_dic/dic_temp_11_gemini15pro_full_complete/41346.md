corinth: /ˈkɔːrɪnθ/ (US), /ˈkɒrɪnθ/ (UK)| n.|科林斯|An ancient Greek city, famous for its wealth and strategic location on the Isthmus of Corinth.| Corinth was a major trading center in ancient Greece. 科林斯是古希腊主要的贸易中心。|He visited the ruins of ancient Corinth. 他参观了古科林斯的遗址。|Synonyms: city, metropolis|Antonyms: village, countryside|noun


corinthian: /kəˈrɪnθiən/ (US), /kəˈrɪnθɪən/ (UK)| adj.|科林斯的；华丽的；放荡的|Relating to Corinth, or characteristic of its luxurious and sometimes immoral lifestyle; also, referring to a classical order of architecture characterized by ornate capitals decorated with acanthus leaves.|The Corinthian columns of the temple were impressive.  这座寺庙的科林斯式圆柱令人印象深刻。| He lived a Corinthian lifestyle, indulging in every pleasure. 他过着科林斯式的生活，沉溺于各种享乐。|Synonyms: opulent, lavish, ornate (for luxurious sense), dissolute, debauched (for immoral sense)|Antonyms: austere, simple, moral|adjective
