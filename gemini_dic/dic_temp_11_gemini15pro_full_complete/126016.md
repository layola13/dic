musket:/ˈmʌskɪt/ | n. | 火绳枪，滑膛枪 | A smoothbore firearm fired from the shoulder, typically used in the 16th to 18th centuries.  It was the primary infantry weapon before the development of the rifle. |  The soldiers were armed with muskets and bayonets. 士兵们装备了火枪和刺刀。 | synonyms: firearm, gun, rifle (in later contexts) | antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps bow and arrow or sword as earlier weapons) | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
