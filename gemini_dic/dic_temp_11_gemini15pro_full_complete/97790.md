injunctions:/ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃənz/
|n.|命令，指令，禁令|An authoritative warning or order.|The court issued an injunction to stop the construction. 法院发布了禁令，阻止施工。|synonyms: order, command, decree, mandate, directive|antonyms: allowance, permission|noun


injunction:/ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən/
|n.|（法院的）强制令，禁制令|A judicial process or order requiring the person or persons to whom it is directed to do a particular act or to refrain from doing a particular act.|He obtained an injunction restraining her from selling the property. 他获得了一项禁令，限制她出售该房产。|synonyms: restraining order, court order|antonyms: permit|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English injunccion, from Old French enjoindre, from Latin iniungere (“to enjoin, impose, inflict, attach”), from in- (“in, on, upon”) + iungere (“to join”).
