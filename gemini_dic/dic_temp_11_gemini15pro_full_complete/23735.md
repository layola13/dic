brattier: /ˈbrætiər/ (US), /ˈbrætɪər/ (UK) | adj. | 更讨厌的，更顽皮的 | Comparative form of "bratty," meaning more spoiled, badly behaved, or annoying.  It describes someone behaving in a childish, selfish, and demanding way. |  Example: He's gotten even brattier since his parents bought him that new video game. 他自从父母给他买了那个新电子游戏后变得更讨厌了。

Example:  My younger sister is much brattier than me. 我的妹妹比我顽皮多了。| Near Synonyms:  more spoiled, more mischievous, naughtier, more unruly, peskier | Antonyms:  more well-behaved, more obedient, sweeter, nicer, more polite |  Word type: Adjective
