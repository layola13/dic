mutes:/mjuːts/
n.|静音; 弱音器; 哑巴; 闭口音 (尤指钢琴、小号等乐器上的)|A device used to soften the sound of a musical instrument, especially a brass instrument.  People unable to speak. A consonant sound produced with the vocal cords not vibrating.|The trumpet player used a mute to create a softer sound. 小喇叭手用弱音器来制造更柔和的声音。

The mutes on the piano softened the melody. 钢琴上的弱音器使旋律变得柔和。

He communicated with sign language, as he was one of the mutes. 他用手语交流，因为他是个哑巴。

The letter 'p' is a mute. 字母 'p' 是一个闭口音。|近义词:silencers, dampers, dumb, speechless|反义词: voiced, speakers|词性:名词

mute:/mjuːt/
v. |使(声音)减弱; 消音; 抑制; 减轻(色彩的强度)|To reduce the sound of something, typically by covering or wrapping it. To soften or subdue the intensity of a color.|He muted the television during the phone call. 他在打电话时把电视静音了。

The artist muted the bright colors with a wash of gray.  艺术家用灰色颜料冲淡了鲜艳的颜色。|近义词:soften, subdue, dampen, quieten|反义词:amplify, intensify, brighten|词性:动词

mute:/mjuːt/
adj.|无声的; 沉默的; 哑的; 闭口音的|Silent; not speaking.  Unable to speak. Relating to a mute consonant.|She stood mute with shock. 她震惊得说不出话来。
