chortled:/ˈtʃɔːrt(ə)ld/| v.|轻声地欢笑；咯咯地笑|To laugh in a gleeful, chuckling way, often suggesting quiet satisfaction or triumph.|He chortled with delight at the news. 他听到这个消息高兴地轻声笑了。|Synonyms: chuckle, giggle, titter|Antonyms:  sob, weep, lament|verb

chortle:/ˈtʃɔːrt(ə)l/| n.|轻声的笑声；咯咯的笑声|A gleeful, chuckling laugh.|She gave a little chortle of amusement. 她轻轻地咯咯一笑，表示觉得有趣。|Synonyms: chuckle, giggle, titter|Antonyms: sob, wail, lament|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Coined by Lewis Carroll in "Through the Looking-Glass" (1871), blending "chuckle" and "snort".
