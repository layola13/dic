obstinately:/ˈɒbstɪnɪtli/ | adv. | 固执地，顽固地 | In a stubborn and unyielding manner; stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action. | He obstinately refused to admit he was wrong. 他顽固地拒绝承认自己错了。|  stubbornly, persistently, pertinaciously |  complaisantly, yieldingly | adverb

obstinate: /ˈɒbstɪnət/ | adj. | 顽固的，固执的 | Stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action, despite attempts to persuade one to do so. |  He's an obstinate child who refuses to eat vegetables. 他是个固执的孩子，不肯吃蔬菜。 | stubborn, headstrong, willful | compliant, amenable | adjective

Etymology:
From Latin *obstinatus*, past participle of *obstinare* (“to persist, stand firm”), from *ob-* (“against”) + *stinare* (“to stand”), from *stare* (“to stand”).
