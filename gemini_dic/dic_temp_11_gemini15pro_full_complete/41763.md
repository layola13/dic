corruptibilities:/kəˌrʌptəˈbɪlətiz/| n. |可腐败性；易腐败性|The quality of being corruptible; susceptibility to corruption.  This refers to the degree to which someone or something can be influenced by bribery, dishonesty, or immoral offers.|The corruptibilities of the officials led to the downfall of the government. 官员们的腐败导致了政府的垮台。|synonyms: vulnerability, susceptibility, impressionability|antonyms: incorruptibility, integrity, probity|noun

corruptible:/kəˈrʌptəbl/| adj. |可腐败的；易腐败的|Susceptible to corruption, especially by bribery.|The investigation revealed that several officials were corruptible. 调查显示有几位官员腐败。|synonyms: dishonest, venal, unscrupulous|antonyms: incorruptible, honest, upright|adjective

corrupt:/kəˈrʌpt/| adj. vt.|腐败的；堕落的；使腐败；使堕落|Having or showing a willingness to act dishonestly in return for money or personal gain; (of a text or a computer program) made unreliable by errors or alterations; cause to act dishonestly in return for money or personal gain.|The corrupt politician was eventually sent to prison.  腐败的政客最终被送进了监狱。 He tried to corrupt the police officer with a bribe. 他试图用贿赂腐蚀警察。|synonyms: dishonest, crooked, fraudulent|antonyms: honest, upright, ethical|adjective, verb


Etymology:
From Middle English *corruptible*, from Old French *corruptible*, from Latin *corruptibilis*, from *corruptus* (past participle of *corrumpere* “to destroy, spoil, corrupt”), from *com-* (“together”) + *rumpere* (“to break”).
