lilts:/lɪlts/| n. v.|轻快的曲调，轻快的节奏，轻快地唱或说|A lilt is a pleasant, gentle rising and falling of the voice when speaking, or a pleasant, gentle rhythm in music.|She spoke with a lilting Irish accent. 她说话带着轻快的爱尔兰口音。|Synonyms: cadence, rhythm, melody, intonation|Antonyms: monotone, droning|noun

lilts:/lɪlts/| v.|轻快地唱或说|To lilt is to speak or sing with a pleasant, gentle rising and falling of the voice.|The singer lilted the words of the love song. 歌手轻快地唱着情歌的歌词。|Synonyms: croon, trill, warble|Antonyms: drone, mumble|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lilten, from Old Norse *lita (“to rely on, trust”), of uncertain origin.  The musical sense is probably from the sense of “to move lightly or cheerfully,” perhaps influenced by the sense of “to rely on a regular rhythm.”
