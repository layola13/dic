hird:/hɜːrd/| n.| 畜群;牧群 |A group of animals, especially cattle or sheep, that live together or are kept together as livestock. | The shepherd led his hird across the field. 牧羊人带领他的羊群穿过田野。|  近义词:flock, herd | 反义词: None | 词性:名词

hird:/hɜːrd/| v. (hird, hirds, hirding, hirded) | 放牧;群集 | To gather or drive (animals) together.  | He hirded the sheep into the pen. 他把羊群赶进羊圈。| 近义词:herd, tend, gather | 反义词:scatter, disperse |词性:动词


词源:
From Middle English *hirde*, from Old English *hirde*, from Proto-Germanic *hirdiz* (compare West Frisian *hurd*, Dutch *herder*, German *Hirt*), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱer-dʰ- (“herd”).  Cognate with Latin *cordō* (“I collect, gather”), Greek *χόρτος (khórtos)* (“enclosed space, courtyard, fodder”).
