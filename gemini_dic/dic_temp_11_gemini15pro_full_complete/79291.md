glaver:/ˈɡleɪvər/ (US), /ˈɡlævə(r)/ (UK)| v. n.|谄媚；奉承；流口水；口水；|To speak effusively or insincerely; to flatter excessively, often with the intent to manipulate or deceive. Also, to drool or to produce saliva.|He glavered over the boss, hoping for a promotion. 他对老板阿谀奉承，希望能升职。|Synonyms: fawn, flatter, toady, drool, slobber|Antonyms: criticize, condemn, disparage|verb

glaver:/ˈɡleɪvər/ (US), /ˈɡlævə(r)/ (UK)| n.|口水；奉承|Saliva; excessive flattery.|The dog's glaver coated the toy. 狗的口水沾满了玩具。|Synonyms: saliva, drool, flattery|Antonyms:  (For saliva) dryness,  (For flattery) criticism|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *glaveren*, probably of imitative origin, related to *gleveren* "to stare foolishly."  The sense of "flatter" developed from the image of a dog fawning and drooling.
