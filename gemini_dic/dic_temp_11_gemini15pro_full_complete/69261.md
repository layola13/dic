featherbrained:/ˈfeðərbreɪnd/| adj.|轻浮的，愚蠢的，头脑简单的|Someone who is featherbrained is silly or not very intelligent.  It suggests a lack of seriousness and practicality.  The image evokes a bird brain, traditionally seen as small and incapable of deep thought.|She made a few featherbrained remarks about her trip to Europe. 她说了些关于她欧洲之行的愚蠢话。|近义词：silly, frivolous, giddy, foolish, ditzy|反义词：sensible, intelligent, wise|词性:形容词

Etymology:
feather (n.) + brain (n.) + -ed (suffix forming adjectives)
"feather" refers to the light weight of a bird's feathers, and by extension, the perceived lightness of a bird's brain.
"brain" refers to the organ of thought.
"-ed" creates an adjective meaning "having the kind of brain described."
