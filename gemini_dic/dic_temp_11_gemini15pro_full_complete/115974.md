marry-off:/ˈmæri ɒf/ (US), /ˈmæri ɒf/ (UK)| v. |嫁出，安排…结婚| To arrange for someone to get married, especially a daughter. |Her parents married her off to a wealthy businessman. 她父母把她嫁给了一个富有的商人。

|They married off their youngest daughter last year. 他们去年把最小的女儿嫁出去了。|近义词：give away (in marriage), wed|反义词: N/A |词性: verb

|The king married off his son to a princess from a neighboring kingdom. 国王把他的儿子嫁给邻国的一位公主。|近义词：unite in marriage, join in matrimony|反义词: N/A |词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From "marry" + "off".  "Marry" comes from Middle English *marien*, from Old French *marier*, from Latin *maritāre* "to marry," from *marītus* "husband." "Off" in this context signifies completion or removal, suggesting the action of giving someone away in marriage, thus removing them from the family's immediate care.
