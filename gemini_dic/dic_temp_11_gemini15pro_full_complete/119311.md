microbarom:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌbærəm/ | n. | 微气压振荡，次声波 | A low-frequency atmospheric pressure wave, typically with a period of about 5 seconds, caused by ocean waves, especially in stormy conditions. | Example 1: Microbaroms are detected by specialized sensors.  微气压振荡由专门的传感器探测到。

Example 2: The study of microbaroms can help in understanding ocean wave activity. 对微气压振荡的研究有助于了解海浪活动。 | Synonym: microseism | Antonym: N/A | Noun
