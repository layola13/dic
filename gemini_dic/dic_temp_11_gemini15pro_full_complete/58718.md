duncehood:/ˈdʌnshʊd/
n.
愚蠢，笨拙；傻瓜状态
The state of being a dunce; stupidity.
He was condemned to a lifetime of duncehood for his foolish mistakes. 他因愚蠢的错误而被判终身愚蠢。
近义词：stupidity, foolishness, ignorance, idiocy
反义词：intelligence, brilliance, wisdom, cleverness
词性：名词

duncehood:/ˈdʌnshʊd/
n.
惩罚愚蠢学生的帽子
A cone-shaped hat formerly used as a punishment for slow learners at school.
In the past, children who were slow learners were often made to wear a duncehood. 过去，学习迟缓的孩子经常被戴上傻瓜帽。
近义词：dunce cap
反义词：scholar's cap
词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From dunce + -hood.  "Dunce" itself derives from the name of John Duns Scotus, a 13th-century philosopher and theologian.  His followers, the Dunsmen or Dunses, were initially respected, but later, as their ideas fell out of favor, the term "dunce" became associated with stupidity.
