mysticisms:/ˈmɪstɪˌsɪzəmz/| n. |神秘主义，神秘思想，奥秘|beliefs, ideas, or experiences that are concerned with achieving union with God or spiritual power, especially through contemplation or direct experience; the quality of being mystical|He was drawn to the mysticisms of the East. 他被东方神秘主义所吸引。|近义词：spiritualities, occultisms, esotericism|反义词：rationalism, pragmatism|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Late Middle English *misticisme*, from Old French *misticime*, from Late Latin *mystici**, from Latin *mysticus* (“mystical”), from Ancient Greek *μυστικός (mustikós)*, from *μύστης (mústēs)* (“one initiated into mysteries”), from *μύω (múō)* (“to close, to initiate”).
