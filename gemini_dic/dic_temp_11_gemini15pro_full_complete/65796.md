evictor: /ɪˈvɪktər/
| n. |驱逐者，赶走的人|A person who evicts someone, especially a tenant from a property.|The evictor posted a notice on the door. 驱逐人员在门上贴了告示。|Synonyms: expeller, ejector, ouster|Antonyms: protector, defender|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "evict," which comes from the Latin word "evincere," meaning "to overcome, conquer, or recover."  "Evincere" is formed from "ex-" (out) + "vincere" (to conquer).  The "-or" suffix denotes the agent or person performing the action.
