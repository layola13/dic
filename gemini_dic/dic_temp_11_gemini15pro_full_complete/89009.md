hollow:/ˈhɒləʊ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːloʊ/ (US)| adj. n. v. |空的，中空的；凹陷的；空洞的；虚伪的；（声音）沉闷的；山谷，洼地；挖空，凿空| having an empty space inside; not sincere; a small valley| The tree trunk was hollow. 树干是空心的。| synonyms: empty, vacant, concave, sunken, insincere, resonant | antonyms: solid, full, convex, sincere | adjective

hollow:/ˈhɒləʊ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːloʊ/ (US)| n. |山谷，洼地| a small valley or low area surrounded by hills |  We camped in a sheltered hollow. 我们在一个避风的洼地里露营。| synonyms: valley, basin, depression | antonyms: hill, peak, summit | noun

hollow:/ˈhɒləʊ/ (UK) /ˈhɑːloʊ/ (US)| v. |挖空，凿空| to make a hole or empty space in something |  The birds hollowed out a nest in the tree. 鸟儿在树上凿了一个窝。| synonyms: excavate, gouge, scoop | antonyms: fill, pack | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *holh*, from Proto-Germanic *hulnaz* ("hollow, cave"), from Proto-Indo-European *kel- ("to hide, conceal").
