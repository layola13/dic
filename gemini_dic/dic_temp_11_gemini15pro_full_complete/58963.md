duststorm:/ˈdʌstˌstɔːrm/| n. |沙尘暴|A strong wind that carries clouds of dust over a large area, especially in a desert.|The duststorm swept across the plains, reducing visibility to almost zero. 沙尘暴席卷了平原，能见度几乎降为零。|The farm was devastated by the duststorm. 这个农场被沙尘暴摧毁了。|近义词：sandstorm, haboob|反义词:calm, breeze|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From dust + storm.  First recorded in the late 19th century. "Dust" comes from Old English "dust," and "storm" comes from Old English "storm," meaning "violent weather."
