flourishingly:/ˈflʌrɪʃɪŋli/
adverb
繁荣地，兴旺地
In a way that suggests growth, prosperity, and success.
The business is flourishingly expanding into new markets.  这家公司正蓬勃地向新市场扩张。
The trees grew flourishingly in the fertile valley.  树木在肥沃的山谷里茂盛地生长。
近义词: prosperously, thrivingly, successfully
反义词: poorly, weakly, decliningly
词性: adverb

flourish:/ˈflʌrɪʃ/
verb
繁荣，兴旺；茂盛；挥舞；炫耀
1. (of a person, animal, or other living organism) grow or develop in a healthy or vigorous way, especially as the result of a particularly favorable environment.
2. wave (something) about to attract the attention of others.
The economy is flourishing. 经济欣欣向荣。
He flourished the sword in the air. 他在空中挥舞着剑。
近义词: thrive, prosper, brandish
反义词: decline, wither, fail
词性: verb

noun
繁荣；茂盛；装饰；华丽辞藻；花彩字
A bold or extravagant gesture or action, made especially to attract attention.
A fanfare of trumpets announced the arrival of the royal flourish.  一阵喇叭声宣告了皇家仪仗队的到来。
The artist added a flourish to the painting with a final stroke of the brush.  艺术家用最后一笔为画作增添了神韵。
近义词: embellishment, ornamentation, fanfare
反义词: simplicity, plainness
词性: noun


词源: From Middle English *florisshen, *flourisshen, from Anglo-Norman floriss-, stem of florir and Old French florir, from Latin flōrēre (“to bloom, blossom, flower”), present active infinitive of flōreō (“to bloom, blossom, flower”), from flōs (“flower”).
