charismas: /kəˈrɪzməz/ | n. | 魅力，感召力 |  A special magnetic charm or appeal that can inspire devotion in others. It's often associated with leadership and the ability to influence people. |  He lacks the charisma of his predecessor. 他缺乏前任的魅力。| synonyms: allure, magnetism, presence, charm | antonyms:  repulsiveness, unattractiveness | noun

charisma: /kəˈrɪzmə/ | n. | 魅力，感召力 | A special power that some people have naturally that makes them able to influence other people and attract their attention and admiration. | The president has great personal charisma. 总统极具个人魅力。| synonyms: allure, magnetism, presence, charm | antonyms: repulsiveness, unattractiveness | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek χάρισμα (khárisma, “favor, divine gift”), from χαρίζομαι (kharízomai, “I show favor, I grant”), from χάρις (kháris, “grace, favor, kindness”).
