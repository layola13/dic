cascader:/kæˈskeɪdər/| n.|瀑布作业者；级联器；串联器|A person who descends a waterfall or steep incline, often using ropes. Also, a device or arrangement that creates a cascade, such as a series of waterfalls or a sequence of interconnected devices or stages.  |The cascader carefully checked his equipment before rappelling down the waterfall. 瀑布作业者在沿着瀑布绳降之前仔细检查了他的装备。

ETYMOLOGY->
From "cascade" + "-er".  "Cascade" comes from the Italian "cascata" (waterfall), derived from "cascare" (to fall), ultimately from the Latin "cadere" (to fall).  "-er" is a suffix denoting a person or thing that performs a specific action.
