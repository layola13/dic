chinoidine:/ˌkaɪnɔɪˈdiːn/| n. |金鸡纳碱，奎诺伊丁|A brownish amorphous alkaloid, a mixture of quinidine, cinchonine, cinchonidine, and quinine, obtained from the mother liquors after the crystallization of quinine sulfate; formerly used as a tonic and antiperiodic.|Chinoidine was once a common treatment for malaria.|Synonyms: quinoin, quinoidine|Antonyms: None|Noun
