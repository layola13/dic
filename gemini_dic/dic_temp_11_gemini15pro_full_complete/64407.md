ergatively:/ˈɜːrɡətɪvli/
adv.| 作格地，作格性地|In ergative languages, the subject of an intransitive verb is treated the same as the object of a transitive verb.|The verb "shatter" can be used ergatively: "The glass shattered" (intransitive) versus "He shattered the glass" (transitive).|近义词: (No direct synonym, depends on the specific context.  Consider terms like "absolutively" for grammatical discussions)|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性: adverb

ergative:/ˈɜːrɡətɪv/
adj.| 作格的，作格性的|Relating to or having the characteristics of an ergative verb or language.|Basque is an ergative language.|近义词: (No direct synonym, consider related grammatical terms like "absolutive")|反义词: (No direct antonym)|词性: adjective

ergative:/ˈɜːrɡətɪv/
n.| 作格语言，作格动词|A noun or pronoun that is the subject of a transitive verb.|In the sentence "The boy kicked the ball," "the boy" is the ergative.|近义词: (Grammatically related to "agent")|反义词: (Grammatically related to "patient" or "absolutive")|词性: noun
