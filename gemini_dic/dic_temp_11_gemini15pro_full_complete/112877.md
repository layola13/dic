lucubratory:/ˈluːkjʊˌbreɪtəri/ | adj. | 刻苦钻研的，勤奋学习的；在夜间工作的 | characterized by study, especially intensive study at night |  Example: He spent many lucubratory hours in the library, poring over ancient texts. 他在图书馆里度过了许多刻苦钻研的时光，仔细研读古代文献。

Example:  Her lucubratory efforts resulted in a groundbreaking dissertation. 她刻苦钻研的努力最终成就了一篇开创性的论文。 | Synonyms: studious, scholarly, diligent, assiduous | Antonyms: lazy, indolent, idle |  Etymology: From Latin *lucubratorius*, from *lucubrare* "to work by lamplight," from *lux* "light." 词源：源于拉丁语 *lucubratorius*，来自 *lucubrare* “在灯光下工作”，来自 *lux* “光”。
