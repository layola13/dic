influxion:/ɪnˈflʌkʃən/ /ˈɪnflʌkʃn/ | n. | 流入；汇集；涌入；（声音、语调的）变化；音调变化 | An influx or flowing in of something. A change in pitch or tone of the voice. | The influxion of refugees into the country strained resources.  难民涌入该国，使其资源紧张。| synonyms: influx, inflow, inrush, arrival, convergence | antonyms: outflow, efflux, exodus, departure, divergence | noun

inflection:/ɪnˈflɛkʃən/ | n. |  弯曲；变形；音调变化；词尾变化 | A bend or turn. The modulation of the voice; change in pitch or tone.  In grammar, the change in the form of a word (typically the ending) to express a grammatical function or attribute such as tense, mood, person, number, case, and gender. | The inflection in her voice betrayed her excitement. 她声音的变化暴露了她的兴奋。| synonyms: modulation, intonation, cadence, lilt, accent | antonyms: monotone | noun
