euphuistic: /juːˈfjuːɪstɪk/ | adj. | 尤孚体式的，绮丽的 | Characterized by an elaborately ornate style of writing or speaking, marked by excessive use of antithesis, alliteration, and far-fetched similes.  Derived from Euphues, the title character of John Lyly's prose romances. | Her euphuistic prose was filled with elaborate metaphors and allusions. 她尤孚体式的散文充满了精心设计的比喻和典故。 | 近义词: ornate, elaborate, flowery, bombastic, high-flown | 反义词: plain, simple, unadorned, concise | 词性: adjective
