impalpability:/ɪmˌpælpəˈbɪləti/
n.
难以捉摸；无形性；不可触知性
The quality of being difficult to perceive or grasp; intangibility.
The impalpability of the mist made it seem almost otherworldly.  这雾的难以捉摸让它看起来几乎超凡脱俗。
The concept of time can be difficult to grasp due to its impalpability. 时间的概念因其无形性而难以理解。
近义词: intangibility, incorporeality, immateriality, ethereality
反义词: palpability, tangibility, concreteness, substantiality
词性: noun

impalpable:/ɪmˈpælpəbl/
adj.
难以捉摸的；无形的；无法感知的
Unable to be felt by touch; intangible.
His fear was palpable, a thick, heavy presence in the room. 他的恐惧是明显的，一种浓厚而沉重的存在感充满了整个房间。 (This is an example of the antonym "palpable")
The tension in the air was almost impalpable, yet everyone felt it. 空气中的紧张气氛几乎难以察觉，但每个人都感觉到了。
近义词: intangible, incorporeal, imperceptible, elusive
反义词: palpable, tangible, perceptible, discernible
词性: adjective


词源:
From Late Latin *impalpabilis, from in- (not) + palpabilis (tangible), from palpare (to touch).
