anti-inductive:/ˌæntiɪnˈdʌktɪv/ | adj. | 反归纳的，非归纳的 |  Characterized by reasoning or argumentation that does not rely on induction, the process of deriving general principles from specific observations.  It often involves focusing on counterexamples or exceptions to challenge generalizations. |  His anti-inductive approach to problem-solving meant he looked for the flaws in seemingly established patterns.  他反归纳的解决问题的方法意味着他会在看似已建立的模式中寻找缺陷。 | Near Antonyms: inductive, empirical | Antonyms: deductive | Adjective
