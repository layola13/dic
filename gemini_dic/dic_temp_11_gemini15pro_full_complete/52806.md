diluting:/daɪˈluːtɪŋ/
verb
稀释; 冲淡
reducing the strength of a liquid by mixing it with water or another solvent.
The chemist is diluting the acid with water.  化学家正在用水稀释酸。
The company is diluting its brand by offering cheaper products.  该公司通过提供更便宜的产品来稀释其品牌。
近义词: weakening, thinning, attenuating
反义词: concentrating, strengthening, thickening
词性: verb (present participle of dilute)

dilute:/daɪˈluːt/
verb
稀释; 冲淡
make (a liquid) thinner or weaker by adding water or another solvent to it.
You can dilute the juice with water. 你可以用水稀释果汁。
The company's profits were diluted by the rising cost of materials.  该公司的利润因材料成本上涨而被稀释。
adjective
稀释的; 冲淡的
(of a liquid) made thinner or weaker by having had water or another solvent added to it.
The painter used a dilute solution of bleach to clean the wall.  油漆工用稀释的漂白剂溶液清洁墙壁。
The report's conclusions were somewhat dilute and lacked impact.  报告的结论有些淡薄，缺乏影响力。
近义词: weak, thin, watery (adj); weaken, thin, water down (v)
反义词: concentrated, strong, thick (adj); concentrate, strengthen, thicken (v)
词性: verb, adjective

词源: From Latin *diluere, from dis- "apart, away" + -luere "to wash".  源自拉丁语 *diluere，由 dis- “分开，离开” + -luere “洗” 组成。
