colfox: /ˈkɒlfɒks/ (British English) /ˈkoʊlfɑːks/ (American English) | n. | 狐狼, 郊狼的别称 | A colloquial or dialectal term for a fox, particularly a sly or cunning one.  This word may also be used humorously or as a playful insult.  Its usage is not common in standard English. | He's a sly old colfox, always getting his way. 他是一个狡猾的老狐狸，总是能随心所欲。| 近义词: fox, vixen (female fox), reynard | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun


Etymology:
Likely a combination of "colt" (young horse) and "fox."  The reason for this combination is uncertain, but it may refer to a fox's physical characteristics, such as its reddish-brown coat similar to some horses, or perhaps to its speed and agility.  It could also be a simple rhyming compound.  The word is primarily found in regional dialects and is not considered standard English.
