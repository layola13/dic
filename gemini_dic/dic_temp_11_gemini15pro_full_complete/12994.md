astonishers:/əˈstɒnɪʃəz/
n.
令人惊讶的人或事物
People or things that cause astonishment.
The magician, with his incredible illusions, was one of the greatest astonishers of his time.  这位魔术师以其不可思议的幻觉成为他那个时代最令人惊讶的人之一。
New technologies are constant astonishers, changing our lives in unexpected ways. 新技术不断给人带来惊喜，以意想不到的方式改变着我们的生活。
近义词:surprisers, stunners
反义词:bores, disappointments
词性:noun


astonish:/əˈstɒnɪʃ/
v.
使惊讶
To surprise or amaze greatly.
The news astonished everyone. 这消息使每个人都感到惊讶。
His sudden success astonished his family. 他的突然成功使他的家人感到惊讶。
近义词:surprise, amaze, astound
反义词:bore, tire
词性:verb

词源：From Middle English *astonen, *astonien, from Old French estoner (“to stun, daze, amaze”), from Vulgar Latin *extonāre, present active infinitive of *extonō (“I thunder”), from Latin intonō (“I thunder at, assail with thunder”), from in (“in, at”) + tonō (“I thunder”).
