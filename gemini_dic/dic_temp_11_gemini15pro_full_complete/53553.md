disbar:/dɪsˈbɑːr/
verb
取消律师资格
To expel a lawyer from the bar; to deprive (a lawyer) of the right to practice law, especially by a formal order of a court.
The lawyer was disbarred for misconduct.  这位律师因行为不端被取消了律师资格。
The state bar association disbarred him for accepting bribes. 州律师协会因他收受贿赂而取消了他的律师资格。
synonyms: expel, debar, suspend, prohibit
antonyms: admit, reinstate, authorize
verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing reversal) + bar (to exclude). Ultimately from the Late Latin barra (bar).
