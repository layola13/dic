minotaurs:/ˈmɪnəˌtɔːrz/ | n. | 牛头怪（复数） | A mythical creature with the head of a bull and the body of a man, kept in the Labyrinth of Crete. |  The minotaurs roamed the labyrinth, searching for their next victim. 牛头怪在迷宫中游荡，寻找下一个受害者。|  Myths often feature minotaurs as symbols of brute strength.  神话中经常以牛头怪作为蛮力的象征。 | synonyms: monsters, beasts | antonyms: heroes, saviors | noun

minotaur:/ˈmɪnəˌtɔːr/ | n. | 牛头怪（单数） | A mythical creature with the head of a bull and the body of a man, kept in the Labyrinth of Crete. | The minotaur was eventually slain by Theseus. 牛头怪最终被忒修斯杀死。| Legends say the minotaur demanded a yearly sacrifice of young people. 传说牛头怪要求每年献祭年轻人。 | synonyms: monster, beast | antonyms: hero, savior | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
