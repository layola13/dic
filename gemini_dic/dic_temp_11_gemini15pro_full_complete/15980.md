balustered:/ˈbæləstərd/| adj.|有栏杆的，有扶手的|Having balusters; furnished with balusters.  A baluster is a short, vertical post that supports a railing. |The grand staircase was balustered and carpeted.  宏伟的楼梯有栏杆和地毯。|The balcony was balustered and offered a stunning view. 阳台有栏杆，视野极佳。|近义词:railed, balustraded|反义词:open, unenclosed|词性:adjective

Etymology:
From baluster + -ed. Baluster comes from French *balustre, from Italian balaustro, from balaustra "wild pomegranate flower," from Late Latin balaustium, from Greek balaustion. The shape of the baluster resembled the swelling calyx of the pomegranate flower.
