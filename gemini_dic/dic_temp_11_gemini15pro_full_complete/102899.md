jawbone: /ˈdʒɔːˌboʊn/ (US) /ˈdʒɔːˌbəʊn/ (UK)| n.| 颚骨；下巴骨 |The jawbone is the bone that forms the lower jaw and holds the lower teeth. |例句：He suffered a fractured jawbone in the accident. 他在事故中下巴骨骨折。

例句：The archaeologist unearthed a jawbone from the ancient burial site. 考古学家从古代墓地挖出了一块颚骨。

近义词：mandible, lower jaw|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider cranium as part of the opposing structure)|词性: noun


jawbone: /ˈdʒɔːˌboʊn/ (US) /ˈdʒɔːˌbəʊn/ (UK)| v. |（非正式）喋喋不休地说服；唠叨 |To talk to someone persistently to persuade them to do something. |例句：He jawboned the committee into accepting his proposal. 他喋喋不休地说服委员会接受了他的提议。

例句：She jawboned him for hours about cleaning his room. 她唠叨了他好几个小时，让他打扫房间。

近义词：cajole, persuade, nag|反义词:  listen, be quiet|词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English "ceaceban," meaning "cheekbone" or "jawbone."  The "ceace" part refers to the jaw, and "ban" means "bone."  Over time, the meaning shifted to primarily refer to the lower jawbone.
