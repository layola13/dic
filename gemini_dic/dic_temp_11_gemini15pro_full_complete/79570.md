gloomful: /ˈɡluːmfʊl/ | adj. | 忧郁的，阴暗的 | Filled with or expressing gloom; depressing. |  The gloomful weather matched her mood.  阴沉的天气与她的心情相符。|  The gloomful news cast a shadow over the entire family.  这令人沮丧的消息给整个家庭蒙上了一层阴影。 |近义词：depressed, despondent, melancholy, somber, dreary | 反义词：cheerful, happy, bright, sunny | 词性: 形容词

gloom: /ɡluːm/ | n. | 忧郁，阴暗 | Partial or total darkness; a state of depression or despondency. | 词源: From Middle English *gloumen, from Old English *glōm (“twilight”), from Proto-Germanic *glōmaz (“twilight”). Cognate with Scots gloum (“twilight”), West Frisian glom (“twilight”), Dutch loom (“dull, inactive”), German Glumm (“dull, inactive”), Swedish glåmig (“pale”).
