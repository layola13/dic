excavate: /ˈekskəveɪt/ (US) /ˈɛkskəveɪt/ (UK)| v. | 挖掘，开凿，出土 | To dig out and remove earth, revealing something beneath the surface. |  Archaeologists excavated the ancient city. 考古学家挖掘了这座古城。

The workers excavated a large hole for the foundation of the building. 工人们为建筑物的地基挖了一个大洞。


近义词: dig, unearth, disinter | 反义词: bury, fill, inter | 词性: verb

excavation: /ˌekskəˈveɪʃn/ (US) /ˌɛkskəˈveɪʃn/ (UK)| n. | 挖掘，开凿，发掘 | The act or process of excavating something. | The excavation of the Roman ruins took several years.  罗马遗址的挖掘工作花了数年时间。

The excavation revealed a treasure trove of ancient artifacts.  挖掘工作揭示了一批珍贵的古代文物。


近义词: digging, unearthing | 反义词: burial, filling | 词性: noun

excavator: /ˈekskəveɪtər/ (US) /ˈɛkskəveɪtər/ (UK)| n. | 挖掘机，挖掘者 | A person or machine that excavates. |  The excavator carefully removed the dirt around the fossil.  挖掘机小心地清除化石周围的泥土。

An excavator was brought in to clear the site for construction. 一台挖掘机被带进来清理场地以便施工。

近义词: digger, earthmover | 反义词: filler | 词性: noun
