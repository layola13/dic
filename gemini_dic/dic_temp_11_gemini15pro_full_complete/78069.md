geode:/ˈdʒiːoʊd/| n.|晶洞； geodes(复数)|A hollow rock lined with crystals.|I found a beautiful geode while hiking. 我在徒步旅行时发现了一个美丽的晶洞。|Synonym: druse, vug|Antonym: solid rock|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French géode, from Latin geōdes, from Ancient Greek γεώδης (geṓdēs, “earthlike”), from γῆ (gê, “earth”).  The term suggests the earthy appearance of the outside of the rock.
