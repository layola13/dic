jobsworths: /ˈdʒɒbzwɜːθs/ | n. | 墨守成规的人；死板的人；官僚主义者 | A person who insists on sticking to rules and regulations, even when it is unnecessary or unhelpful.  This term is primarily used in British English and carries a negative connotation, suggesting the person is petty, obstructive, and enjoys wielding small amounts of power. |"The jobsworth at the gate wouldn't let me in, even though I had a valid pass."  那个门口的墨守成规的人不让我进去，即使我有有效的通行证。 | Synonyms: bureaucrat, stickler, pedant, martinet | Antonyms: flexible, accommodating, helpful | noun
