overgraciously: /ˌoʊvərˈɡreɪʃəsli/
| adv. | 过分殷勤地，过分客气地 | Excessively or insincerely gracious; too polite or kind in a way that seems unnatural or affected. | He overgraciously offered me his seat, even though there were plenty of others available. 他过分殷勤地把座位让给我，尽管还有很多空位。|Synonyms: excessively polite, overly courteous, unctuously, fulsomely | Antonyms: rudely, impolitely, curtly | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
"over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "graciously" (adverb form of "gracious," from Old French "gracieux," ultimately from Latin "gratia" meaning "grace, favor, kindness").
