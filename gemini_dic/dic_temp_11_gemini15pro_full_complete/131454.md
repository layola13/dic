noncognate: /nɒnˈkɒɡneɪt/ (British) /nɑːnˈkɑːɡneɪt/ (American)| adj. n.|非同源词|A word in one language that is not derived from the same source as a word with a similar form and meaning in another language.  A noncognate word shares no common ancestor with a similar word in another language.|例句：English "much" and Spanish "mucho" are cognates, while English "cat" and French "chat" are not noncognates, but cognates.|近义词：unrelated, dissimilar|反义词:cognate|词性:adjective, noun
