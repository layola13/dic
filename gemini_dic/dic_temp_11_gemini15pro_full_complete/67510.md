extractibilities:/ɪkˌstræktɪˈbɪlɪtiz/ | n. | 可提取性，可萃取性 | The capability of a substance to be extracted from a mixture or matrix. This refers to how readily a substance can be removed or isolated using a specific method. | The extractibilities of different components in the plant varied depending on the solvent used. 不同植物成分的可提取性取决于所使用的溶剂。|  The extractibilities of heavy metals from contaminated soil were investigated. 研究了从污染土壤中提取重金属的可提取性。 | synonyms: solubility, separability, recoverability | antonyms: insolubility, inseparability, non-recoverability | noun

extractibility:/ɪkˌstræktɪˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可提取性，可萃取性 | The capability of a substance to be extracted from a mixture or matrix.  | The extractibility of the oil from the shale was low.  从页岩中提取石油的可提取性很低。 | The study focused on the extractibility of bioactive compounds from medicinal plants. 这项研究的重点是从药用植物中提取生物活性化合物的可提取性。 | synonyms: solubility, separability, recoverability | antonyms: insolubility, inseparability, non-recoverability | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "extractible" + "-ity".  "Extractible" comes from the Late Latin "extractibilis", from Latin "extractus" (past participle of "extrahere" - to draw out), from "ex-" (out of) + "trahere" (to draw).  The suffix "-ity" forms nouns of quality or state.
