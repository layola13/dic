impudicity:/ˌɪmpjʊˈdɪsɪti/| n.|厚颜无耻，无耻，放荡|The impudicity of her behavior shocked everyone present.  她的行为的无耻震惊了所有在场的人。|Impudicity is the state or quality of being impudent or shameless. It refers to a blatant disregard for modesty, decency, or proper behavior, often involving sexual or moral impropriety.| Her impudicity was evident in her provocative clothing and suggestive remarks. 她的厚颜无耻体现在她挑逗性的服装和暗示性的言辞中。|近义词：shamelessness, audacity, brazenness, immodesty, indecency|反义词：modesty, decency, humility, shame, bashfulness|词性:noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *impudicitia, from Latin impudicus (“shameless”) + -itia (“-ity”).  Latin impudicus from in- (“not”) + pudicus (“modest, chaste”).  Pudicus from pudor (“shame, modesty”).
