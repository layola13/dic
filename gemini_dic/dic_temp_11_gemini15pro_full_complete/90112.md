hot-dogger:/ˈhɒtˌdɒɡə(r)/ (US) /ˈhɒtˌdɒɡə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 花样滑雪者；冒险家；爱炫耀的人|A person who performs stunts or takes risks, especially in skiing or other sports, often to show off.  A show-off.|He's a real hot-dogger on the slopes, always trying to impress everyone with his jumps. 他在滑雪坡上真是个爱炫耀的人，总是试图用他的跳跃给每个人留下深刻印象。|Synonyms: show-off, daredevil, stuntman, exhibitionist|Antonyms: cautious person, conservative|Noun

hot-dog:/ˈhɒtˌdɒɡ/ (US) /ˈhɒtˌdɒɡ/ (UK)| n. vi.| 熱狗；炫耀|A frankfurter, typically served hot in a long split roll. To perform stunts or show off.|I had a hot dog for lunch. 我午饭吃了個熱狗。 He was hot-dogging on his skateboard. 他在滑板上炫耀。|Synonyms: frankfurter (n.), show off (v.), grandstand (v.)|Antonyms: (for verb) be modest, be humble|Noun, Verb
