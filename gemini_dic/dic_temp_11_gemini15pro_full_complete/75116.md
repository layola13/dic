fries:/fraɪz/ | n. | 炸薯条，炸土豆条 | Thinly cut and deep-fried potatoes. | Example: I ordered a burger and fries. 我点了一个汉堡和薯条。 | Example:  She loves eating fries with ketchup. 她喜欢吃蘸番茄酱的薯条。 | Synonym: chips (UK), French fries | Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "baked potato" as a contrasting side dish) | Noun

frise:/friːz/ | n. | (1)粗毛圈; (2) (建筑)带状装饰 |  (1) A type of looped pile fabric, often used for upholstery. (2) A decorative horizontal band, especially in architecture. | Example: The sofa was covered in a plush frise. 沙发覆盖着豪华的长毛绒。 | Example: The frise above the doorway was intricately carved. 门口上方的带状装饰雕刻得很复杂。 | Synonym: (1) pile, plush, (2) molding, cornice | Antonym:  (No direct antonym) | Noun

frises: This is the plural form of both "fries" and "frise".


ETYMOLOGY->

fries (as in French fries):  Shortened from "French fried potatoes." "French" in this context likely refers to the method of cutting the potatoes into thin strips.

frise (fabric/architecture): From French "frise," ultimately from Latin "frisium," a type of cloth.
