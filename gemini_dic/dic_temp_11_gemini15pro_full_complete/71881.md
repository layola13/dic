ETYMOLOGY->

From Old English *flyċċing (unattested), from *flyċċan "to feather an arrow," from Proto-Germanic *flikkjaną "to patch, mend," from *flikką "patch, piece."  Cognate with Middle Dutch vlicken "to patch," German flicken "to darn, mend," Old Norse flikkja "to patch."  The noun is derived from the verb.
