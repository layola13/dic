high-level:/ˌhaɪ ˈlevəl/| adj.|高水平的；高级的|High-level refers to something that is complex, sophisticated, or strategic, often involving abstract thinking or overall management rather than details.  It suggests a broad perspective and focus on the bigger picture.|This project requires high-level programming skills. 这个项目需要高水平的编程技能。|The CEO made a high-level decision about the company's future. 首席执行官对公司未来做出了高层决策。|近义词：advanced, sophisticated, complex, strategic|反义词:low-level, basic, simple|词性:形容词

Etymology:
From "high" + "level." "High" traces back to Old English "hēah," meaning "tall, elevated." "Level" comes from Old French "livel," ultimately from Latin "libella," meaning "carpenter's level, balance." The combined meaning developed in the 20th century.
