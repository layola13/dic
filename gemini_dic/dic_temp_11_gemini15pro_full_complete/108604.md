leadway:/ˈliːdweɪ/ | n. |领先优势，领先地位；(尤指船只的)行动余地，回旋余地|The advantage in a competition or other endeavor; freedom of action or movement, especially for a ship. |The company has a considerable leadway over its competitors in the development of new technologies. 这家公司在新技术研发方面比竞争对手拥有相当大的领先优势。 The ship needed more leadway to avoid colliding with the pier. 这艘船需要更大的回旋余地才能避免与码头相撞。|近义词：advantage, head start, leeway |反义词：disadvantage, handicap |词性:名词

词源:  From "lead" (meaning "go first") + "way" (meaning "road" or "path").  The original meaning referred to the freedom of movement a ship has.
