glamoured: /ˈɡlæmərd/ (British) /ˈɡlæmərd/ (American)| adj.| 被施魔法的，着迷的，被深深吸引的 | Affected by glamour; charmed and fascinated. |  She was completely glamoured by the Hollywood lifestyle. 她完全被好莱坞的生活方式迷住了。|  The audience was glamoured by the singer’s performance. 观众被歌手的表演迷住了。|Synonyms: captivated, enchanted, fascinated, charmed, entranced | Antonyms: disenchanted, disillusioned, unimpressed, bored | Word type: Adjective

glamour: /ˈɡlæmə(r)/ (British) /ˈɡlæmər/ (American)| n.| 魅力，魔力，吸引力 | An exciting and charming quality that makes someone or something seem attractive and special. | The city has lost its glamour. 这座城市失去了它的魅力。|  She added a touch of glamour to the occasion by wearing a stunning dress. 她穿着一件惊艳的礼服，为这个场合增添了一丝魅力。| Synonyms: charm, allure, magic, attraction, charisma | Antonyms: dullness, ordinariness, plainness | Word type: Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Scottish *glamour*, a variant of *grammar*. Originally in the sense of "magic, enchantment," specifically by learning, influenced by the association of Latin grammatica "grammar, learning" with occult practices.
