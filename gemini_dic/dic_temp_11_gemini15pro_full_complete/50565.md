deputyship:/ˈdepjʊtɪʃɪp/ | n. | 代理；副职；代理权 | The state or position of being a deputy; the period of time during which a person is a deputy. | He held the deputyship for five years.  他担任了五年副职。 | synonyms: agency, delegation, representation, lieutenancy, substituteship | antonyms: leadership, headship | noun

deputyship:/ˈdepjʊtɪʃɪp/ | n. | 监护权（尤指对精神或身体有残疾者的） | Legal guardianship over someone who is deemed incapable of managing their own affairs. | She applied for deputyship of her elderly mother. 她申请了对她年迈母亲的监护权。| synonyms: guardianship, custodianship, conservatorship, trusteeship | antonyms: autonomy, independence | noun
