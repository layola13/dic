macrosociology:/ˌmækroʊˌsoʊsiˈɑːlədʒi/ | n. |宏观社会学|The study of large-scale social structures and processes, such as social systems, institutions, and entire societies.| Macrosociology helps us understand how societal forces shape individual lives. 宏观社会学帮助我们理解社会力量如何塑造个体生活。| Synonym: large-scale sociology | Antonym: microsociology | noun

Etymology:  "macro-" (large) + "sociology" (the study of society). "Macro" comes from the Greek "makros" meaning "long" or "large".
