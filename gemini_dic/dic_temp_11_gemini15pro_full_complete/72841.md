foliosity:/foʊliˈeɪʃən/ /ˌfəʊliˈɒsəti/| n. |叶理，片理;  |The property of rocks, such as slate and schist, that allows them to be split into thin sheets or layers.|The geologist examined the foliosity of the rock sample.|近义词：cleavage, lamination, schistosity|反义词: massiveness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *foliōsus (“leafy”), from Latin folium (“leaf”).
