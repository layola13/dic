anaesthesia:/ˌænɪsˈθiːʒə/ (UK) /ˌænɪsˈθiːziə/ (US)| n.|麻醉|A state of controlled, temporary loss of sensation or awareness induced for medical purposes.|The patient was given general anaesthesia before the surgery. 病人在手术前进行了全身麻醉。|Synonyms: numbness, unconsciousness|Antonyms: consciousness, sensation|noun

anaesthetic:/ˌænɪsˈθɛtɪk/ (UK) /ˌænɪsˈθɛtɪk/ (US)| n. adj.|麻醉剂; 麻醉的|A substance that causes anaesthesia; relating to anaesthesia.|The dentist used a local anaesthetic to numb the patient's gums. 牙医使用局部麻醉剂来麻醉病人的牙龈。  The anaesthetic wore off quickly. 麻醉药很快就失效了。|Synonyms: narcotic, analgesic (n.); numbing (adj.)|Antonyms: stimulant (n.); stimulating (adj.)|noun, adjective
