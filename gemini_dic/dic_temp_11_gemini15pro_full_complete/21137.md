blithes:/blaɪðz/| adj.|快乐的，无忧无虑的|Happy and carefree; showing a casual and cheerful indifference considered to be callous or improper.|He spoke with blithe ignorance of the true situation. 他在谈到真实情况时表现得轻松愉快，却全然不知情。|近义词：cheerful, merry, joyous, lighthearted|反义词：sad, gloomy, depressed|词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *blith, from Old English *blīþe (“blithe, happy, kind, friendly, gracious, merciful, gentle, pleasant”), from Proto-Germanic *blīþiz (“blithe, happy, friendly, kind, merciful”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰlei̯dʰ- (“mild, gentle, friendly”). Cognate with Old Saxon blīði (“blithe, happy”), Old Frisian blīthe (“happy, glad”), Old High German blīdi (“blithe, happy, kind”), Old Norse blíðr (“gentle, kind, friendly”), Gothic 𐌱𐌻𐌴𐌹𐌸𐍃 (blēiþs, “merciful, gracious”).
