commemorativeness:/kəˌmɛmərətɪvnəs/| n. |纪念性，纪念意义|The quality of serving to commemorate something or someone.  It refers to the ability of something to evoke memories or mark a significant event.|The commemorativeness of the monument was palpable, reminding all who visited of the sacrifices made. 这座纪念碑的纪念意义显而易见，提醒所有参观者铭记所作出的牺牲。|近义词：remembrance, memorialization|反义词：forgetfulness, oblivion|词性:noun

commemorate:/kəˈmɛməreɪt/| v. |纪念，庆祝|To recall and show respect for (someone or something)|They commemorated the victory with a parade. 他们举行游行来庆祝胜利。|近义词：celebrate, observe, honor|反义词：forget, ignore|词性:verb

commemorative:/kəˈmɛmərətɪv/| adj. |纪念性的，纪念的|Serving to commemorate something or someone.|The commemorative stamp marked the centenary of the event.  这枚纪念邮票是为了纪念该事件一百周年而发行的。|近义词：memorial, celebratory|反义词：ordinary, everyday|词性:adjective
