apostrophe:/əˈpɒstrəfi/| n.|撇号；省字号|A punctuation mark ( ’ ) used to indicate either possession (e.g., Harry’s book ) or the omission of letters or numbers (e.g., can’t ; he’s ; class of ’99 ).|She uses an apostrophe to show possession.|Synonym:none|Antonym:none|noun

apostrophe:/əˈpɒstrəfi/| n.|呼语；顿呼法|An exclamatory passage in a speech or poem addressed to a person (typically one who is dead or absent) or to a thing (typically one that is personified).|He made a dramatic apostrophe to the heavens.|Synonym:address, invocation|Antonym:none|noun
