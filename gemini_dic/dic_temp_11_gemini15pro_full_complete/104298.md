justified:/ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪd/ | adj. | 有正当理由的，合理的，情有可原的 | Having, or able to show, good reasons for doing something.  | The police officer felt justified in using force to arrest the suspect.  警察觉得使用武力逮捕嫌疑人是合理的。 | synonyms: legitimate, valid, warranted, reasonable, right, proper | antonyms: unjustified, unwarranted, unreasonable, wrong, improper | adjective

justified:/ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪd/ | v. | 证明...是正当的，为...辩护 | To show that something is right or reasonable. |  He tried to justify his actions by saying he was protecting himself. 他试图辩解说他是为了保护自己才这么做的。| synonyms: defend, explain, excuse, rationalize, vindicate | antonyms: condemn, criticize, denounce | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French *justifié, past participle of justifier, based on Latin justus ‘just’.
