jovial: /ˈdʒoʊviəl/ (US), /ˈdʒəʊviəl/ (UK) | adj. | 快乐的；愉快的；高兴的 |  Cheerful and friendly; characterized by good humor and conviviality. | He had a jovial personality and always had a smile on his face. 他性格开朗，脸上总是挂着笑容。|  The jovial host welcomed his guests with open arms.  热情好客的主人张开双臂欢迎他的客人。|  merry, cheerful, jolly, mirthful | gloomy, morose, glum, sullen | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *jovial*, from Late Latin *jovialis* (“of or pertaining to Jupiter”), from Latin *Jovis*, the genitive case of *Jupiter*, the chief Roman god.  Influenced by the belief that people born under the planet Jupiter (named after the god) were cheerful and optimistic.
