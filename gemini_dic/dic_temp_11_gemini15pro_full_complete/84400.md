ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *halmark*, from Old French *hale marke* "hall-mark," literally "hall mark" (mark of a hall or guildhall).  Originally referred to the official stamp used to guarantee the purity of gold and silver articles.  The meaning broadened to include any distinguishing characteristic.
