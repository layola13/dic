masses:/ˈmæsɪz/ (US), /ˈmæsɪz/ (UK)| n.|群众；民众；大量；块；团|A large number of people or things; a large quantity or amount of something. Also, a coherent, typically large body of matter with no definite shape.|The politician addressed the masses.  这位政治家向群众发表了讲话。|The ship cut through masses of ice. 这艘船破开了大块的冰。|synonyms: crowds, throngs, multitudes, hordes, lots, heaps, piles, tons, loads|antonyms: individual, few, minority|noun

masses:/ˈmæsɪz/ (US), /ˈmæsɪz/ (UK)| v. (masses, massing, massed)|聚集；集结|Gather together in large numbers; assemble.|Troops were massing on the border. 部队在边境集结。|Dark clouds massed on the horizon.  乌云聚集在地平线上。|synonyms: assemble, gather, congregate, collect, muster|antonyms: disperse, scatter, separate|verb
