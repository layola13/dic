groans:/ɡroʊnz/ (US) /ɡrəʊnz/ (UK)| n. v. |呻吟声，抱怨声；呻吟，抱怨|A deep, mournful sound uttered in pain, grief, or disapproval.  It can also be the act of making such a sound. |The injured man lay on the ground, emitting low groans. 受伤的人躺在地上，发出低低的呻吟声。|近义词：moans, sighs, complains, grumbles|反义词：cheers, laughter, applause|noun
groans:/ɡroʊnz/ (US) /ɡrəʊnz/ (UK)| v. |呻吟，抱怨|To make a deep, mournful sound expressing pain, grief, or disapproval. |He groaned as he lifted the heavy box. 他抬着沉重的箱子呻吟着。|近义词：moans, sighs, complains, grumbles|反义词：cheers, laughs, applauds|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *grōnen*, from Old English *grānian* (“to groan, lament”), from Proto-Germanic *grōnaną* (“to groan”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵrēh₁-nós* (“groaning”). Cognate with Dutch *gronen* (“to groan”), German *greinen* (“to whine, cry”), Gothic *������������* (*granjan*).
