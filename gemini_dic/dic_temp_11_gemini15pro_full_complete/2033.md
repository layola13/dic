evacuation: /ɪˌvækjuˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 撤离，疏散 | the act of removing people from a dangerous place | The evacuation of the city was completed before the hurricane hit. 城市在飓风来袭前完成了疏散。| synonyms: removal, clearance | antonyms: occupation,  influx | noun

etymology: From Late Latin evacuātiōn- (stem of evacuātiō ), from evacuātus (past participle of evacuāre ), from e- (variant of ex- ) ‘out’ + vacuāre ‘to empty’, from vacuus ‘empty’.
