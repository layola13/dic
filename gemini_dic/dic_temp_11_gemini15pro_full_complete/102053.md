isomerism: /aɪˈsɒmərɪzəm/ (UK) /aɪˈsɑːmərɪzəm/ (US) | n. | 同分异构现象 | The existence of molecules that have the same number and types of atoms (i.e., the same molecular formula) but different structural or spatial arrangements of those atoms, leading to different physical and chemical properties. | Glucose and fructose are isomers, sharing the formula C6H12O6 but having different structures. 葡萄糖和果糖是同分异构体，它们具有相同的分子式 C6H12O6，但结构不同。|  isomery, isomeric state | antonym: N/A (This is a phenomenon, not a characteristic that has a direct opposite.) | noun
