coronavirus: /kəˈrəʊnəˌvaɪrəs/ (British) /kəˈroʊnəˌvaɪrəs/ (American)| n. |冠状病毒|A type of virus that can cause diseases in humans and animals.  The name comes from the Latin word "corona," meaning "crown," because of the crown-like spikes on the surface of the virus.|The novel coronavirus is highly contagious.|近义词: virus, germ, pathogen|反义词: None|词性: noun

词源: From Latin "corona" (crown) + virus.  Refers to the crown-like appearance of the virus under a microscope.
