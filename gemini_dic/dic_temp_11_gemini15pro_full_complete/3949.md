agroforestries:/ˌæɡroʊˈfɔːrɪstriːz/ |n.|农林复合系统; 农林业|  A land use management system in which trees or shrubs are grown around or among crops or pastureland. This diversifies production, provides income, and creates a more sustainable and resilient agricultural landscape.| Example: Agroforestries are gaining popularity as a way to combat climate change and improve food security. 农林复合系统作为一种应对气候变化和改善粮食安全的方法越来越受欢迎。

Example:  Many developing countries are implementing agroforestries to improve soil health and biodiversity. 许多发展中国家正在实施农林复合系统以改善土壤健康和生物多样性。| Near Synonyms: agroforestry,  integrated farming, sustainable agriculture | Antonym: monoculture | Noun


Etymology:  "agro-" from Latin *ager* (field) + "forestry" from Old French *forestrie*
