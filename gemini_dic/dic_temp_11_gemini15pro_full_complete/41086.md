coppices:/ˈkɒpɪsɪz/ |n.|矮林，萌生林；|An area of woodland where trees or shrubs are regularly cut down to ground level to stimulate growth of new shoots.|The farmer managed the coppices for firewood.|Synonyms: thicket, brushwood, undergrowth|Antonyms: grassland, meadow|Noun

coppice:/ˈkɒpɪs/ |n.|矮林，萌生林；|An area of woodland where trees or shrubs are regularly cut down to ground level to stimulate growth of new shoots.|The coppice provided a habitat for many small animals.|Synonyms: thicket, brushwood, undergrowth|Antonyms: grassland, meadow|Noun

coppice:/ˈkɒpɪs/ |v.|将…矮林作业；修剪萌生；|To cut down (trees or shrubs) to ground level to stimulate growth of new shoots.|They coppiced the hazel trees every few years.|Synonyms: pollard, trim, prune|Antonyms: plant, grow|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *copeiz, from Vulgar Latin *colpare, "to cut, chop," from Latin colaphus, "a blow, cuff."  Related to the word "coup."
