disobliging:/ˌdɪsəˈblaɪdʒɪŋ/ | adj. | 不友好的，不乐于助人的，不殷勤的 | Unwilling to help or do a favor; unhelpful. Showing a lack of courtesy.  | Example: He was disobliging and refused to lend me his notes. 他很不友好，拒绝把笔记借给我。

Example: The disobliging shopkeeper refused to accept a return.  那个不和气的店主拒绝接受退货。

| 近义词：unhelpful, uncooperative, rude, impolite, churlish | 反义词：helpful, obliging, cooperative, courteous, polite | 词性: adjective


disoblige:/ˌdɪsəˈblaɪdʒ/ | vt. | 怠慢，冷落 | To refuse to do a favor for (someone); slight. | Example: I wouldn't want to disoblige you. 我不想怠慢你。

Example:  She felt disobliged by his refusal to help. 她觉得他拒绝帮忙是一种怠慢。 | 近义词：offend, slight, snub | 反义词：oblige, accommodate, assist | 词性: verb


词源:

Late Middle English: from dis- (expressing reversal) + oblige.
