fathom:/ˈfæðəm/ | n. | 英寻（长度单位，等于6英尺或1.8288米）; 理解，了解 | A unit of length equal to six feet (approximately 1.8 meters), chiefly used in reference to the depth of water.  To understand something fully; to penetrate the meaning or nature of something. | The ship sank in over 100 fathoms of water. 这艘船沉没在100多英寻深的水中。

He couldn't fathom her sudden change in behavior. 他无法理解她突然改变行为的原因。 | synonyms: understand, comprehend, grasp, gauge, plumb, divine | antonyms: misunderstand, misinterpret | noun

fathom:/ˈfæðəm/ | v. | 测量(水的深度);  理解，了解 | To measure the depth of (water). To understand (a difficult problem or an enigmatic person) after much thought. | They tried to fathom the depth of the lake. 他们试图测量湖的深度。

She couldn't fathom his motives. 她无法理解他的动机。| synonyms: understand, comprehend, grasp, gauge, plumb, divine | antonyms: misunderstand, misinterpret | verb
