overinclusion:/ˌoʊvərɪnˈkluːʒən/| n.|过度概括，过度包容|The tendency to include objects or concepts within a category based on superficial similarities, even if they don't truly belong.  It's often seen in children and individuals with cognitive impairments.|A child with overinclusion might call all four-legged animals "dogs".  一个过度概括的孩子可能会把所有四条腿的动物都叫做“狗”。|近义词：overgeneralization|反义词: underinclusion|noun

overinclusion:/ˌoʊvərɪnˈkluːʒən/| n.|过度包容|The act of including too much or too many things, especially when inappropriate or irrelevant. In psychology, it refers to the cognitive error of broadening the scope of a concept to include elements that don't belong.|His essay suffered from overinclusion of tangential topics. 他的文章因包含了太多无关紧要的话题而显得冗长。|近义词：overgeneralization|反义词: underinclusion|noun
