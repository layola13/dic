estheticizing:/ɛsˈθɛtɪsaɪzɪŋ/
verb
美化；审美化
The act of making something aesthetically pleasing, or focusing on the aesthetic qualities of something.
The museum was accused of estheticizing violence in its new exhibition.  博物馆被指责在其新展览中美化暴力。
The author was criticized for estheticizing poverty in his latest novel.  这位作家因在其最新小说中美化贫困而受到批评。
近义词: beautifying, embellishing, glorifying, romanticizing
反义词: uglify, deface, spoil
词性: verb (present participle)

estheticize:/ɛsˈθɛtɪsaɪz/
verb
美化；审美化
To represent something as being beautiful or artistically pleasing, sometimes in a way that masks its true nature.
Some photographers estheticize war, focusing on the dramatic visuals rather than the human cost. 一些摄影师美化战争，关注戏剧性的视觉效果，而不是战争带来的人员伤亡。
They were accused of estheticizing poverty by portraying it as a quaint and picturesque lifestyle.  他们被指责美化贫困，将其描绘成一种古雅而如画的生活方式。
近义词: beautify, embellish, glorify, romanticize
反义词: uglify, deface, spoil
词性: verb

aesthetic:/ɛsˈθɛtɪk/
adjective/noun
美学的；审美的 / 美学
Concerning or characterized by an appreciation of beauty or good taste / A set of principles underlying and guiding the work of a particular artist or artistic movement.
The building's aesthetic appeal is undeniable. 这座建筑的美学吸引力是不可否认的。
The artist's aesthetic was heavily influenced by Japanese minimalism.  这位艺术家的美学深受日本极简主义的影响。
近义词: artistic, beautiful, tasteful
反义词: ugly, distasteful, unattractive
词性: adjective/noun


词源: From the Greek "aisthetikos," meaning "perceptive, sensitive," from "aisthanesthai," meaning "to perceive."
