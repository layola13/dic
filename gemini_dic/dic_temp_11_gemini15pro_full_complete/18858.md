beseechingnesses:/bɪˈsiːtʃɪŋnɪsɪz/
|n.|恳求，乞求; 央求|The quality or state of being beseeching; earnest entreaty.  The intensity or fervor of a plea or supplication.|His voice was full of beseechingnesses as he begged for another chance.  他的声音充满了恳求，乞求再给他一次机会。|近义词: supplications, entreaties, pleas, appeals|反义词: demands, commands, assertions|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From beseeching + -ness.  Beseeching comes from the Middle English *besechen*, from Old English *besēcan* "to seek, implore, beseech," from *be-* "completely" + *sēcan* "to seek."  The suffix *-ness* forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
