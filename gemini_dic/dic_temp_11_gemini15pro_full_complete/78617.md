gibbosity:/ɡɪˈbɒsɪti/ | n. |驼背，凸起，隆起|A rounded swelling or protuberance.  A condition of being humpbacked. |The old man's gibbosity was pronounced, a testament to years of hard labor.  这位老人的驼背很明显，证明了他多年辛苦劳作。|Synonyms: hump, hunch, protuberance, swelling, prominence|Antonyms: concavity, hollow, depression|noun

gibbous:/ˈɡɪbəs/ | adj. |驼背的，凸起的，隆起的|Swelling out on one side; protuberant; humpbacked.  (of the moon) more than half but less than fully illuminated. |The gibbous moon shone brightly in the night sky. 凸月在夜空中闪耀。|Synonyms: convex, protuberant, bulging, rounded, humped|Antonyms: concave, hollow, flat|adjective

词源:
Gibbosity comes from the Late Latin gibbositas, from gibbosus "humpbacked," from gibbus "hump."  The adjective "gibbous" shares this same root.
