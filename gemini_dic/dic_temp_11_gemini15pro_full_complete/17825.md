bedstraw:/ˈbedˌstrɔː/ /ˈbedˌstrɑː/| n. |床草，拉拉藤|A plant of the genus Galium, several species of which have small, hooked bristles on their stems and leaves, formerly used for stuffing mattresses.|The field was covered in sweet-smelling bedstraw.|近义词：cleavers, goosegrass|反义词: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *beddstrēaw, from bedd (bed) + strēaw (straw).  The plant was traditionally used as mattress stuffing.
