obiter:/ˈɒbɪtə(r)/ (US) /ˈoʊbɪtər/| adj. adv.|顺便提及的，附带的|Incidental; said in passing.  Relating to or containing incidental remarks (usually, legal).|The judge made some obiter remarks about the defendant's character. 法官顺便提及了被告的性格。|Synonyms: incidental, parenthetical, aside, digressive|Antonyms: essential, crucial, central, germane|adjective/adverb

obiter dictum:/ˈɒbɪtər ˈdɪktəm/ (US) /ˈoʊbɪtər ˈdɪktəm/| n.|附带意见，顺便提及|A judge's incidental expression of opinion, not essential to the decision and not establishing precedent.|The judge's comments on the witness's attire were mere obiter dicta. 法官对证人着装的评论只是附带意见。|Synonyms: incidental remark, passing comment, aside|Antonyms:  ruling, decision, judgment|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *ob iter*  "by the way," from *ob* "on account of, in front of, towards, down, before, about, concerning" + *iter*  "a going, a journey, a way, road, march, passage."
