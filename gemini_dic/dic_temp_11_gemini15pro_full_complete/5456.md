almug:/ˈælmʌɡ/| n.|红木，檀香木|A reddish wood mentioned in the Old Testament, used in the construction of Solomon's temple and musical instruments.  It is believed to be sandalwood or a similar fragrant wood.|The almug was prized for its beauty and fragrance.  红木因其美丽和芬芳而备受珍视。|The ark of the covenant was made of almug wood.  约柜是用红木做的。|Synonyms: sandalwood, algum|Antonyms: N/A|noun
