houses:/ˈhaʊzɪz/| n.|房屋，住宅|A building for human habitation, especially one that consists of a ground floor and one or more upper storeys.|They built new houses near the river. 他们在河边建造了新房子。|近义词：homes, dwellings, residences, accommodations|反义词:None|Noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *hous*, from Old English *hūs*, from Proto-West Germanic *hūs*, from Proto-Germanic *hūsan* (compare West Frisian *hûs*, Dutch *huis*, German *Haus*), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱewsóm* (“house, dwelling”). Cognate with Latin *cāsa* (“hut, cottage”).
