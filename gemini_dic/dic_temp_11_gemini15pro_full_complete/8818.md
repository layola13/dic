antiessentialist:/ˌæntiɪˈsɛnʃəlɪst/ | adj. | 反本质主义的 |  Rejecting the idea that there is a single, fixed, and inherent essence or nature to things, particularly social categories like gender, race, or sexuality.  It emphasizes the fluidity, context-dependence, and social construction of such categories. |  "Her antiessentialist approach to gender studies challenged traditional notions of masculinity and femininity." 她反本质主义的性别研究方法挑战了传统的男性气质和女性气质的概念。 |  constructivist, non-essentialist | essentialist | adjective

antiessentialism:/ˌæntiɪˈsɛnʃəlɪzəm/ | n. | 反本质主义 |  The philosophical view that rejects essentialism, arguing against fixed definitions or inherent natures, especially regarding identity categories. | "Antiessentialism emphasizes the importance of cultural and historical context in shaping identity." 反本质主义强调文化和历史背景在塑造身份方面的重要性。 | constructivism, non-essentialism | essentialism | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (from Greek _anti_)
essentialist: from essence + -ist (denoting a follower of a doctrine or belief)
essence: the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something (from Latin _essentia_)
