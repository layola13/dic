Crookes: /krʊks/ | n. | 克鲁克斯 |  Sir William Crookes (1832-1919), British chemist and physicist, known for his work with cathode rays and the invention of the Crookes tube (阴极射线管).  It often refers to the Crookes tube itself. | The discovery of the electron is credited to experiments using Crookes tubes. 电子是由使用克鲁克斯管的实验发现的。 |  Crookes demonstrated the properties of cathode rays. 克鲁克斯展示了阴极射线的特性。 | synonyms: none (as a proper noun referring to the person or his specific invention) | antonyms: none | proper noun

Crookes: /krʊks/ | adj. | 克鲁克斯的 | Relating to Sir William Crookes or his work, especially the Crookes tube. | The Crookes radiometer is a fascinating device. 克鲁克斯辐射计是一个引人入胜的装置。| The Crookes tube was crucial in the discovery of X-rays.  克鲁克斯管在X射线的发现中至关重要。| synonyms: none  | antonyms: none | adjective
