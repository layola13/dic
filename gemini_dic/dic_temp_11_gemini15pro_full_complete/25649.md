bundled-up:/ˈbʌndəld ˈʌp/| adj.|包裹严实的，穿得暖和的|Dressed warmly in many layers of clothing, typically to protect oneself from cold weather.|例句：The children were all bundled up in coats and scarves. 孩子们都裹着外套和围巾。|例句：She was bundled up against the cold wind. 她裹得严严实实以抵御寒风。|近义词：wrapped up, snug, cozy|反义词：exposed, uncovered|词性:形容词

bundle:/ˈbʌndl/| n. v.|捆，束，包；捆扎，打包|n. A collection of things tied or wrapped up together. v. To tie or wrap things together.|例句：He carried a bundle of newspapers under his arm. 他腋下夹着一捆报纸。|例句：She bundled the clothes into the washing machine. 她把衣服塞进洗衣机里。|近义词：package, bunch, pile|反义词：separate, untie|词性:名词,动词

up:/ʌp/| adv. prep. adj.|向上，起来；在上面；高兴的|adv. Towards a higher place or position. prep. To or in a higher place or position. adj.  In a higher position; raised.|例句：Prices are going up. 价格在上涨。|例句：He climbed up the stairs. 他爬上楼梯。|近义词：above, higher|反义词：down, below|词性:副词,介词,形容词

词源： "bundle" comes from Middle English "bundel," probably of Low German origin. "up" comes from Old English "up," "upp," of Germanic origin.  "bundled up" is a phrasal verb combining "bundle" and "up" to describe the action of dressing warmly.
