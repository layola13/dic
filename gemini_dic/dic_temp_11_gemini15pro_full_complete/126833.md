naphthalene:/ˈnæfθəˌlin/ | n. |萘，卫生球|A white crystalline strong-smelling hydrocarbon made from coal tar and used in mothballs and in making dyes, resins, and other chemicals.|Mothballs are made of naphthalene.|Synonyms:  mothballs, camphor |Antonyms: None | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Naphthalin*, from *Naphtha* (itself from Latin and Greek *naphtha*, meaning "petroleum") + *-in* (a chemical suffix). Ultimately from Persian *naft* ("petroleum").
