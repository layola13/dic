confoundedness:/kənˈfaʊndɪdnəs/ | n. | 困惑，混乱，糊涂 |The state of being confounded; confusion.  A feeling of bewilderment or disorientation. |His confoundedness at the sudden change of plans was evident. 他对计划的突然改变感到明显的困惑。| 近义词: confusion, bewilderment, perplexity, disorientation | 反义词: clarity, understanding, certainty | 词性: 名词

confound:/kənˈfaʊnd/ | v. | 使困惑，使糊涂，混淆 | To cause surprise or confusion in (someone), especially by acting against their expectations. To mix up (something) with something else so that the individual elements become difficult to distinguish. | The magician confounded the audience with his illusions. 魔术师用他的幻象迷惑了观众。| 近义词: confuse, bewilder, perplex, baffle | 反义词: clarify, enlighten, explain | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *confounden, from Old French confondre, from Latin confundere (“to pour together, mix, mingle, confuse”), from con- (“together”) + fundere (“to pour”).
