fobbing:/ˈfɒbɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈfɑːbɪŋ/ (US)| v. (present participle)|欺骗；搪塞；用…应付；| Deceiving or misleading someone, often by giving them something inferior or by distracting them from the truth.  It also means to put off (someone) with an excuse or a trivial substitute.|  He tried fobbing me off with some story about the train being late. 他试图用火车晚点的借口搪塞我。|  He fobbed the customer off with a faulty product.  他用一件次品打发了顾客。|Synonyms: deceiving, cheating, tricking, misleading, palming off,  |Antonyms: being honest, truthful, forthright| Verb (present participle)

Also, a chain attached to a watch carried in such a pocket. |He kept his watch in his fob. 他把表放在表袋里。 |He pulled out a gold watch on a fob. 他掏出一块带表链的金表。|Synonyms: watch pocket, watch chain|Antonyms: N/A| Noun


fob:/fɒb/ (UK) /fɑːb/ (US) | v. |欺骗；搪塞；用…应付|To deceive or mislead someone, often by giving them something inferior or by distracting them from the truth.|He fobbed me off with a fake watch. 他用一块假表骗了我。| She fobbed him off with a flimsy excuse. 她用一个站不住脚的借口搪塞他。|Synonyms: deceive, cheat, trick|Antonyms: be honest, be truthful| Verb

词源:  "fob" (verb) comes from the German "foppen" meaning "to mock or fool." The sense of "fob off" developed from the idea of palming something off on someone as genuine when it isn't.
