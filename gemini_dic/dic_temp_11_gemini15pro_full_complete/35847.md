cockneyishly:/ˈkɒkniɪʃli/ (US) /ˈkɑːkniɪʃli/| adv.|伦敦东区口音地，像伦敦东区人一样|In a manner characteristic of a Cockney, especially in speech.|He spoke cockneyishly, dropping his aitches and using rhyming slang.|Near synonyms: Cockney-like, in a Cockney fashion|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, consider "formally," "properly," "with standard pronunciation")|word property:adverb

Etymology:
From Cockney + -ish + -ly.  "Cockney" originally referred to a city dweller, then narrowed to Londoners, and finally to those from the East End of London. "-ish" is a suffix forming adjectives meaning "of the nature of" or "somewhat like." "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives.
