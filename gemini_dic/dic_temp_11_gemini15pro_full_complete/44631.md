crown:/kraʊn/ | n. v. | 王冠，皇冠；顶点；加冕 | A circular ornamental headdress worn by a monarch as a symbol of authority.  To officially make someone a king or queen by placing a crown on their head. | The king wore his crown during the ceremony. 国王在仪式上戴着他的王冠。 | The team crowned their successful season with a championship victory.  这支队伍以一场锦标赛的胜利为他们成功的赛季加冕。 | synonyms: coronet, diadem, tiara, garland, wreath, cap, top, peak, summit, climax, pinnacle | antonyms: nadir, bottom, base | noun
crown:/kraʊn/ | v. | 加冕；为…加冕；表彰；使圆满完成 | To officially make someone a king or queen. To complete something successfully. |  They crowned him king. 他们为他加冕为王。 | Her efforts were crowned with success. 她的努力获得了成功。| synonyms: invest, enthrone, inaugurate, complete, cap, top off | antonyms: dethrone, depose | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *corone*, from Latin *corona* ("garland, crown"), from Proto-Italic *korōnā*, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱer- (“to turn, bend”). Cognates include Ancient Greek *κόρυμβος (kórumbos, “top of the head, highest point”)*.
