on-the-run:/ˌɑːn ðə ˈrʌn/ | adj. | 逃亡的，奔波的 |  Being a fugitive from the law; constantly moving from place to place, especially to avoid being caught.  | The police are searching for two on-the-run prisoners. 警察正在搜捕两名逃犯。 |  The salesman has an on-the-run lifestyle, always traveling to different cities.  这个销售员过着奔波的生活，总是到不同的城市出差。 | synonyms: fugitive, fleeing, absconding, escaping, nomadic | antonyms: captured, apprehended, settled, stationary | adjective

on-the-run:/ˌɑːn ðə ˈrʌn/ | adv. | 奔波地，匆忙地 |  In a state of constant movement or haste.  | He ate his lunch on the run because he was late for a meeting. 他匆忙地吃了午饭，因为他开会迟到了。 |  She's been living on the run ever since she left home. 自从离家出走后，她就一直过着奔波的生活。 | synonyms: hurriedly, hastily, quickly, on the go, in a rush | antonyms: leisurely, slowly, calmly, relaxedly | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
"On the run" originates from the literal act of running away from pursuers, particularly in the context of law enforcement. The phrase gradually expanded to encompass a broader sense of being in constant motion or haste, even without the implication of fleeing from authorities.
