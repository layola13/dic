boles:/boʊlz/| n. |树干，躯干|The boles of the ancient trees were massive. 古树的树干很粗壮。|Example: The logger measured the boles of the felled trees.  伐木工人测量了砍伐的树木的树干。

Example:  Sunlight dappled the forest floor through the boles of the trees. 阳光透过树干洒在森林地面上。|Synonyms: trunks, stems|Antonyms: branches, twigs|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bole*, from Old Norse *bolr*, meaning "trunk of a tree".  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *bulas*,  related to Old English *bolla*, meaning "bowl, cup".  The connection likely arises from the rounded shape of a tree trunk.
