cytococcus:/ˈsaɪtoʊkɒkəs/| n. |细胞球菌|A genus of bacteria that are typically nonmotile, gram-positive cocci, occurring singly, in pairs, or in irregular clusters. They are commonly found in soil and water and can be opportunistic pathogens.|Cytococcus species have been isolated from various clinical specimens, including blood and wounds.|近义词: Micrococcus (some species were formerly classified under Micrococcus), Staphylococcus (similar morphology)|反义词: N/A (Bacteria don't have direct antonyms)|词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From cyto- (meaning "cell") + coccus (meaning "berry-shaped bacterium").
