deprivative:/dɪˈprɪvətɪv/ | adj. n. | 剥夺的；剥夺性；剥夺物 |  Relating to or causing deprivation; something that deprives. |  The drug has a deprivative effect on the brain, reducing its access to essential nutrients.  这种药物对大脑有剥夺作用，减少了大脑对必需营养的吸收。 |  The constant noise was a deprivative factor impacting their sleep quality.  持续的噪音是一个剥夺性因素，影响他们的睡眠质量。 |  Synonyms: reductive, restrictive, subtractive | Antonyms: enriching, enhancing, augmentative | adjective
deprivative:/dɪˈprɪvətɪv/ | n. | 剥夺物；剥夺的事物 | Something that deprives someone of something. |  Lack of access to education can be a significant deprivative in a child's life.  缺乏教育机会可能是孩子生活中的一种重大剥夺。 | The new law acted as a deprivative, taking away certain rights from citizens. 这项新法律起到了剥夺作用，剥夺了公民的某些权利。 | Synonyms: loss, forfeiture, dispossession | Antonyms: gain, acquisition, benefit | noun
