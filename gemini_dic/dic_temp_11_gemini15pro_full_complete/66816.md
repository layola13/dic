expansionism:/ɪkˈspænʃənɪzəm/ | n. |扩张主义，扩张政策|The policy or practice of expanding a nation's territory or influence. |The 19th century was a period of aggressive expansionism for many European powers.| 近义词: imperialism, colonialism | 反义词: isolationism, contraction | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  From "expansion" + "-ism."  "Expansion" comes from Late Latin "expansio," from Latin "expandere"  (to spread out), from "ex-" (out) + "pandere" (to spread).  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, or doctrine.
