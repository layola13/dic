inalienablenesses:/ɪnˈeɪliənəblnəsəz/ |n. |不可剥夺性（复数）|The state of being inalienable; impossible to be taken away or given away.  It refers to rights or qualities that are inherent and cannot be surrendered or transferred.|
例句：The inalienablenesses of human rights are often debated. 人权的不可剥夺性经常被争论。

例句：He spoke of the inalienablenesses of life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness. 他谈到了生命、自由和追求幸福的不可剥夺性。|
近义词：inviolabilities, immutabilities|反义词:alienabilities, transferabilities|词性:名词

词源：From inalienable + -ness. Inalienable comes from French inaliénable, from in- + aliénable, from aliéner "to alienate," from Latin alienare "to estrange," from alienus "of another."
