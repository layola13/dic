flints:/flɪnts/| n.|燧石; 打火石; (旧式枪炮的) 击发装置|A piece of hard, gray stone that produces sparks when struck against steel, formerly used to start fires and ignite gunpowder in firearms.|Early humans used flints to make fire. 早期人类用燧石生火。|synonyms: firestone, chert, sparker|antonyms: tinder, kindling|noun

flints:/flɪnts/| v. (third-person singular simple present flints, present participle flinting, simple past and past participle flinted)| (使) 变硬; (使) 变得冷酷无情|To make or become hard or unfeeling.|His gaze flinted as he heard the news. 听到这个消息，他的目光变得冷酷无情。|synonyms: harden, steel, toughen|antonyms: soften, weaken|verb
