ensnare:/ɪnˈsneər/ | v. | 诱捕，使陷入圈套 | To catch or trap something, typically in a net or snare; to involve someone in a difficult situation from which it is hard to escape. | The spider ensnared a fly in its web. 蜘蛛用网困住了一只苍蝇。

The con man ensnared several elderly people in his elaborate scheme. 骗子用他精心策划的诡计诱骗了几位老人。 | 近义词: trap, catch, tangle, entrap | 反义词: release, free, liberate | 词性: 动词

词源:  From en- + snare.  "Snare" comes from the Old English "sneare" meaning "noose" or "trap." The prefix "en-" adds the sense of "in" or "into."
