infantilize: /ˈɪnfəntɪlaɪz/ (US), /ˈɪnfəntaɪlaɪz/ (UK)| v. | 幼稚化；把…当小孩看待 | To treat (someone) as if they were a child, especially by not allowing them to take responsibility for themselves or make their own decisions. |  He accused the media of infantilizing the public. 他指责媒体把公众幼稚化。| synonyms: patronize, condescend, coddle | antonyms: empower, mature | verb

infantilize: /ˈɪnfəntɪlaɪz/ (US), /ˈɪnfəntaɪlaɪz/ (UK)| n. | 幼稚化；把…当小孩看待(名词形式较少使用) | The act of treating someone as if they were a child. | The infantilization of young adults is a growing concern. 年轻人被幼稚化是一个日益严重的问题。| synonyms: patronization, condescension, coddling | antonyms: empowerment, maturation | noun
