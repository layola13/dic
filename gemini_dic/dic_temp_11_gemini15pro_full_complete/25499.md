bullheadedness:/ˌbʊlˈhɛdɪdnəs/| n.|固执，顽固，倔强|The quality of being unwilling to change one's mind or consider other people's opinions, even when it is clear that they are wrong.  Like a bull charging forward without regard for obstacles.|His bullheadedness cost him the election. 他的固执让他输掉了选举。|Synonyms: stubbornness, obstinacy, pigheadedness, tenacity, persistence|Antonyms: flexibility, open-mindedness, compliance, docility|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From bull-headed + -ness.  Bull-headed refers to the image of a bull stubbornly charging forward, regardless of obstacles.
