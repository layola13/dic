astride:/əˈstraɪd/| adv. prep.|跨着，骑着，两腿分开着，|With legs apart on either side of something, extending over or across it.  Being on both sides of something; spanning.|She sat astride the horse. 她骑着马。|Synonyms: across, over, straddling|Antonyms: beneath, under|Adverb, Preposition


astride:/əˈstraɪd/| v.|跨过，跨骑|To sit or stand with one leg on each side of something.|He strode across the stream, easily astriding the narrowest part. 他大步跨过小溪，轻松地跨过了最窄的部分。|Synonyms: bestride, straddle, mount|Antonyms: dismount|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From a- + stride.  The prefix "a-" signifies "on" or "in" a state or condition.  "Stride" refers to walking with long steps. Therefore, astride literally means "on a stride," suggesting a position with legs spread apart as if taking a long step.
