maunder:/ˈmɔːndər/ (British), /ˈmɑːndər/ (American)| v. n.| 闲逛；漫步；唠叨；抱怨|To talk in a rambling or incoherent way, often complaining.  To wander aimlessly.|He maundered on about his childhood for hours. 他絮絮叨叨地讲了他几个小时的童年.|近义词：ramble, mutter, grumble, meander, wander|反义词: articulate, be concise, be quiet|verb

词源：
From perhaps a dialectal variant of *mander 'to beg,' from Old French *mander 'to beg,' from Late Latin *mandicare 'to chew,' frequentative of Latin mandere 'to chew.'  The sense development is likely from 'mumbling' to 'talking incoherently.'
