capulin:/kəˈpuːlɪn/| n.|卡普林樱桃，黑樱桃 (一种墨西哥土生土长的樱桃)|A small, dark red, sweet cherry-like fruit native to Mexico, borne by a shrub or small tree (Prunus salicifolia). The fruit is often used to make jams, jellies, and liqueurs.|We picked fresh capulins from the tree and made a delicious jam.|Synonyms: Mexican cherry, black cherry|Antonyms: N/A|noun


capulin:/kæpəlɪn/| n.|火山锥|A small, conical volcanic hill, especially one composed of cinders and other loose volcanic material.|The hikers climbed to the top of the capulin and enjoyed the panoramic view.|Synonyms: cinder cone, volcanic cone|Antonyms: N/A|noun
