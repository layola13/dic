gestaltism:/ɡəˈʃtɑːltɪzəm/
n.
格式塔心理学；完形心理学
A theory of mind and brain that proposes that the operational principle of the brain is holistic, parallel, and analog, with self-organizing tendencies; or, that the whole is greater than the sum of its parts.
Gestaltism emphasizes that we perceive objects as unified wholes rather than as a collection of individual parts.  格式塔心理学强调我们感知物体是一个统一的整体，而不是单个部分的集合。

Gestaltism had a significant impact on the study of perception.  格式塔心理学对知觉研究有着重要的影响。

Synonyms: Gestalt psychology, configurationism
Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but contrasting theories include structuralism, behaviorism)
词性: noun
