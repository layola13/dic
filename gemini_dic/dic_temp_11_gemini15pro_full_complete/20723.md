blamed: /bleɪmd/ | v. | 指责，责怪 |  to say or think that someone or something did something wrong or is responsible for something bad happening |  He blamed his brother for the broken vase. 他把花瓶打碎的责任怪到他弟弟身上。| synonyms: accused, criticized, reprimanded | antonyms: praised, commended, acquitted | verb

blamed: /bleɪmd/ | adj. | 被责备的 |  Used in the passive voice to indicate that someone or something has been assigned responsibility for something negative.  | The blamed party denied any wrongdoing.  被指责的一方否认有任何不当行为。 | synonyms: accused, criticized, reprimanded | antonyms: praised, commended, acquitted | adjective

PHRASE->
be to blame (for something) = 对某事负有责任
Who is to blame for this mess? 谁该为这一团糟负责？

blame something on somebody/something = 把某事归咎于某人/某事
He blamed the accident on the icy roads. 他把事故归咎于结冰的路面。

take the blame (for something) = 承担责任
I'll take the blame for this. 我会承担这个责任。

put/lay the blame on somebody/something =  把责任推给某人/某事
Don't try to put the blame on me! 别想把责任推给我!

get the blame (for something) = 被责备
She always gets the blame for everything. 她总是被责备。
