in-grouper:/ˈɪnˌɡruːpər/ | n. | 小团体成员;圈内人 | A member of a particular in-group. |  He felt like an in-grouper after being accepted into the exclusive club. 他被接纳进入这个高级俱乐部后感觉像个圈内人。|  insider, clique member | outsider | noun

Etymology: "in-group" + "-er". "In-group" dates back to the early 20th century, derived from "in" + "group," referring to a social group to which a person psychologically identifies as being a member. The suffix "-er" is added to form a noun denoting a person belonging to such a group.
