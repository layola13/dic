gesticulation: /dʒɛˌstɪkjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 手势；姿态；做手势 |  A gesture, especially a dramatic one, used instead of speaking or to emphasize one's words. |  His gesticulations grew more frantic as he realized no one understood him. 当他意识到没有人理解他时，他的手势变得更加疯狂。


Etymology->
From Late Latin *gesticulātiō, from Latin gesticulor (“I make gestures”), from gesticulus, diminutive of gestus (“gesture, posture”), from gerō (“I bear, carry, manage”).

Synonyms->
gesture, motion, sign, signal, action, movement

Antonyms->
stillness, inaction, repose
