hucksterer:/ˈhʌkstərər/| n.|叫卖小贩；兜售者；骗子|A person who sells small items, either door-to-door or from a stall, often using aggressive or showy sales techniques.  A person who promotes or sells something in a dishonest or aggressive way.|The hucksterer at the fair promised his elixir would cure all ills.  那个在集市上的小贩承诺他的灵丹妙药可以治愈所有疾病。|Synonyms: peddler, hawker, vendor, salesman, charlatan, fraud, swindler|Antonyms: philanthropist, benefactor|noun

huckster:/ˈhʌkstər/| n. vi. vt.|叫卖小贩；兜售；以夸张的方式宣传|A person who sells small items, often using aggressive sales techniques. To promote or sell something aggressively or showily.|The market was full of hucksters selling their wares. 市场里到处都是兜售商品的小贩。 He was huckstering his new book on every TV show he could. 他在每个他能上的电视节目中都在兜售他的新书。|Synonyms: peddle, hawk, vend, promote, tout|Antonyms: donate, give|noun, verb, transitive verb, intransitive verb
