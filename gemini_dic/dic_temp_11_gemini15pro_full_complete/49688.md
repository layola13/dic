demiurges: /ˈdɛmiˌɜːrdʒz/ | n. | 造物主，创造者 |  A being regarded as the creator or originator of the universe.  A powerful creative force or personality.  Often used in Gnostic philosophy to refer to a lesser deity responsible for the creation of the material world. |  The ancient Greeks believed in demiurges who shaped the world from pre-existing matter.  Ancient Gnostics saw the demiurge as an imperfect being, distinct from the true, higher God. | 近义词: creator, maker, author, architect | 反义词: destroyer, demolisher | 词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *dēmiurgus*, from Greek *dēmiourgos* "craftsman, artisan," from *dēmios* "public, of the people" + *ergon* "work."
