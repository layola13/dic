bredenwoods: /ˈbrɛdnˌwʊdz/
proper noun|布雷顿森林|Bretton Woods, New Hampshire, USA;  site of the 1944 United Nations Monetary and Financial Conference, which established the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD), later part of the World Bank.  The agreements made at this conference established a new global monetary system.|The Bretton Woods Agreement established a fixed exchange rate system.|Synonym: Bretton Woods system, Bretton Woods Conference|Antonym: (No direct antonym, but contrasting concepts could be "floating exchange rate system," "gold standard")|
