overidealism:/ˌoʊvər aɪˈdiːəlɪzəm/| n.|过度理想主义|The state of having or pursuing ideals that are excessively optimistic or unrealistic, often leading to disappointment or disillusionment.|His overidealism led him to believe that the war could be won without any casualties. 他的过度理想主义使他相信这场战争可以在没有任何伤亡的情况下获胜。|近义词: utopianism, romanticism, naiveté|反义词: realism, pragmatism, cynicism|词性:名词

词源:
"over-" (prefix) + "idealism"
"over-" comes from Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond, across."
"idealism" comes from "ideal" + "-ism."  "Ideal" comes from Late Latin "idealis," meaning "existing in idea," from Greek "idea," meaning "form, pattern."  "-ism" is a suffix forming nouns of action, state, or quality.
