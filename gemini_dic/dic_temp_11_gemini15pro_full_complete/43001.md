cowardice:/ˈkaʊərdɪs/| n.|怯懦，胆小|Lack of bravery; fearfulness, especially in the face of danger or pain.  The soldier was accused of cowardice for deserting his post.|例句：His cowardice prevented him from speaking up. 他的胆怯使他不敢开口说话。

例句：She overcame her cowardice and confronted the bully. 她克服了胆怯，与恶霸对峙。|近义词：fearfulness, timidity, pusillanimity, faintheartedness|反义词：bravery, courage, valor, boldness|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *coardise*, from *coart* "cowardly," from *coe* "tail," from Latin *cauda*.  The image is of an animal tucking its tail between its legs in fear.
