overdainty:/ˈoʊvərˌdeɪnti/| adj.|过精致的，过讲究的，矫揉造作的|Excessively dainty or delicate; too fastidious or refined.|She was considered overdainty by her more robust peers, refusing to partake in any activity that might soil her clothes.|近义词：finicky, fussy, fastidious, squeamish, precious|反义词：coarse, robust, unrefined, down-to-earth|词性:形容词

词源:
From over + dainty.  "Dainty" comes from the Old French "daintie" meaning "delightful," which in turn comes from the Latin "dignitas" meaning "worth, dignity."  The prefix "over-" intensifies the meaning, suggesting an excessive or exaggerated daintiness.
