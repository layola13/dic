acclimatizing:/əˈklaɪməˌtaɪzɪŋ/ |v. |适应气候；适应环境|The process of adjusting to a new climate or environment. |例句：The climbers are acclimatizing to the altitude.  登山者正在适应海拔高度。|例句：She is still acclimatizing to the hot weather. 她还在适应炎热的天气。|近义词：adapting, adjusting, becoming accustomed|反义词: unaccustomed |词性: 动词(present participle)


acclimatize:/əˈklaɪməˌtaɪz/ |v. |适应气候；适应环境|To become accustomed to a new climate or environment. |例句：It took me a while to acclimatize to the cold weather.  我花了一段时间才适应寒冷的天气。|例句：The plants need time to acclimatize to the new soil. 这些植物需要时间来适应新的土壤。|近义词：adapt, adjust, become accustomed|反义词: unaccustomed |词性: 动词

acclimatization:/əˌklaɪməˌtaɪˈzeɪʃən/ |n. |适应；适应环境|The process of becoming accustomed to a new climate or environment. |例句：Acclimatization to high altitude takes time. 适应高海拔需要时间。|例句：Proper acclimatization is essential for preventing altitude sickness. 适当的适应环境对于预防高原反应至关重要。|近义词：adaptation, adjustment|反义词: N/A |词性: 名词


词源: From French *acclimater*, from *a-* (to) + *climater* (to acclimatize), from Latin *clima* (climate).
