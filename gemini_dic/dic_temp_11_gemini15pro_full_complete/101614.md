irreconcilably:/ˌɪrɛkənˈsaɪləbli/
adverb|不可调和地，无法和解地|in a way that cannot be reconciled; in a way that is impossible to resolve|The two sides were irreconcilably opposed. 双方处于不可调和的对立面。|
irreconcilably:/ˌɪrɛkənˈsaɪləbli/
adverb|无法妥协地|in a way that makes compromise impossible|Their views were irreconcilably different. 他们的观点截然不同，无法妥协。|
irreconcilably:/ˌɪrɛkənˈsaɪləbli/
adverb|水火不容地|in a way that is completely incompatible|The two families were irreconcilably hostile. 这两个家族水火不容。|
irreconcilably:/ˌɪrɛkənˈsaɪləbli/
adverb|绝对地，彻底地|in an absolute or complete manner|They were irreconcilably committed to their cause. 他们绝对忠于自己的事业。|
irreconcilably:/ˌɪrɛkənˈsaɪləbli/
adverb|不可调解地|in a way that cannot be mediated or settled|The dispute was irreconcilably bitter. 这场争论不可调解，十分激烈。|
近义词:incompatibly, implacably, intractably, unalterably
反义词:reconcilably, compatibly, harmoniously


词性:adverb


词源:  From irreconcilable + -ly.  Irreconcilable comes from in- (not) + reconcile (to make compatible), ultimately from Latin reconciliare (to bring together again).
