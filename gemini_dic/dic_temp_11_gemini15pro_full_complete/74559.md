fraudulence:/ˈfrɔːdʒələns/ | n. | 欺诈，欺骗，诈骗 | The act of deceiving or misrepresenting; trickery or deception. | The investigation revealed widespread fraudulence in the company's accounting practices. 调查揭露了公司会计实务中普遍存在的欺诈行为。|  The art dealer was accused of fraudulence after selling forged paintings.  这位艺术品经销商因出售伪造的画作而被指控欺诈。 | synonyms: deceit, dishonesty, trickery, deception, cheating, duplicity, chicanery | antonyms: honesty, integrity, truthfulness | noun

fraudulent:/ˈfrɔːdʒələnt/ | adj. | 欺诈的，欺骗的 | Obtained, done by, or involving deception, especially criminal deception. | The fraudulent scheme cost investors millions of dollars.  这个欺诈计划使投资者损失了数百万美元。| The company was fined for making fraudulent claims about its products.  该公司因对其产品做出虚假宣传而被罚款。 | synonyms: deceitful, dishonest, crooked, shady, spurious, bogus | antonyms: honest, genuine, authentic | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from Old French fraudeulence, from Latin fraudulent- ‘deceitful’, from the verb fraudare, from fraus, fraud- ‘deceit’.
