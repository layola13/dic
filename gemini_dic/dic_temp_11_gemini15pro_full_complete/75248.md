from-hand-to-mouth:/frəmˌhænd tə ˈmaʊθ/| adj.|勉强糊口的，仅能维持生计的|Living a precarious existence, barely having enough money for basic necessities.  Characterized by a constant struggle to meet basic needs, especially food.|例句：They lived a from-hand-to-mouth existence, never knowing where their next meal was coming from. 他们过着勉强糊口的日子，从来不知道下一顿饭在哪里。|近义词：precarious, impoverished, hand-to-mouth, struggling, indigent|反义词:wealthy, affluent, prosperous|词性:adjective

from hand to mouth:/frəm hænd tə ˈmaʊθ/| adv.|勉强糊口地，仅能维持生计地|In a manner that barely provides enough money for basic necessities.|例句：They lived from hand to mouth, relying on charity and odd jobs. 他们勉强糊口，依靠慈善和零工度日。|近义词：precariously, meagerly, barely, with difficulty|反义词:comfortably, luxuriously|词性:adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
The phrase "from hand to mouth" originates from the literal act of putting food directly from one's hand into one's mouth, implying a lack of resources to store or prepare food properly. This image evokes a sense of immediacy and scarcity, highlighting the constant struggle for survival.  The earliest recorded use of the phrase in its figurative sense dates back to the 16th century.
