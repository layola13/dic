nonbuoyantly: /nɒnˈbɔɪəntli/ | adv. | 非浮力地，不漂浮地 |  In a manner that does not float or rise in a fluid.  It describes something sinking or remaining submerged rather than floating. | The concrete block sank nonbuoyantly to the bottom of the lake.  水泥块沉到湖底，没有浮起来。| The submarine descended nonbuoyantly into the depths.  潜艇非浮力地下潜到深处。| 近义词: densely, heavily | 反义词: buoyantly, floatably | 词性: 副词

Etymology:
From "non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "buoyant" (adjective meaning "able to float") + "-ly" (adverb suffix). "Buoyant" comes from Old French "boiant," present participle of "boier" ("to float"), from Vulgar Latin "*boiare," of uncertain origin.
