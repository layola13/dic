outsized:/ˌaʊtˈsaɪzd/| adj.|特大的，超大的|Excessively large; exceeding the usual size.|例句：The company made an outsized donation to the charity.  公司向慈善机构捐了一大笔钱。

例句：His outsized ego made it difficult to work with him. 他过分的自负让人很难和他共事。|近义词：enormous, huge, gigantic, massive, colossal|反义词：tiny, small, miniature, minuscule|词性:形容词
ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + sized,  "sized beyond the ordinary or usual."  "Out-" acts as a prefix indicating "beyond" or "exceeding."  The word "sized" relates to the dimension or magnitude of something. The earliest recorded usage is from the late 19th century.
