chromatism: /ˈkroʊməˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 色差；彩色；半音阶 | The undesirable separation of light into different colors, typically in lenses or prisms; also, the use of the chromatic scale in music. | The photographer noticed chromatic aberration in the image, with a purple fringe appearing around the edges of objects.  摄影师注意到图像中有色差，物体边缘出现紫色条纹。 | synonyms: aberration, coloration, chromatic aberration | antonyms: achromatism | noun

chromatism: /ˈkroʊməˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 半音阶 | The use of semitones in a musical scale. | The composer's frequent use of chromaticism created a sense of dissonance and unease.  作曲家频繁使用半音阶，营造出一种不和谐和不安的感觉。| synonyms: chromatic scale, twelve-tone scale | antonyms: diatonicism | noun
