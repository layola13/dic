cross-sectioned:/ˌkrɔːsˈsekʃənd/ | adj. | 横截面的，剖面的 | Showing the internal structure of something as if it has been cut through. | The textbook included a cross-sectioned diagram of the human heart. 教科书中包含了一张人体心脏的剖面图。 |  The architect presented a cross-sectioned model of the building. 建筑师展示了该建筑物的剖面模型。 |  sectional, dissected | intact, whole | adjective

cross-section:/ˈkrɔːsˌsekʃən/ | n. | 横截面，剖面；（代表性的）一部分人 |  1. A surface or shape that is or would be exposed by making a straight cut through something, especially at right angles to its length. 2. A group of people or things that are typical of a larger group. | 1. The cross-section of the tree trunk revealed its age. 树干的横截面显示了它的年龄。 | 2. A cross-section of society was interviewed for the survey.  为了这项调查，采访了社会各阶层的人。| section, profile, sample | whole, entirety | noun
