factionist: /ˈfækʃənɪst/| n. |派系主义者；宗派主义者|A person who is strongly or excessively devoted to a faction or party, especially one who is willing to engage in unprincipled or disruptive behavior to promote its interests.|The factionist's disruptive behavior threatened the unity of the organization.|Synonyms: partisan, sectarian, zealot|Antonyms: moderate, conciliator|noun

factionist: /ˈfækʃənɪst/| adj. |派系的；宗派的|Of or relating to factionism; characterized by factionalism.|The factionist politics of the region have led to years of conflict.|Synonyms: partisan, sectarian|Antonyms: nonpartisan, nonsectarian|adjective
