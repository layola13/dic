ditcher:/ˈdɪtʃər/| n.|挖掘机；开沟机；抛弃者|A machine for digging ditches. A person who abandons or deserts someone or something.|The ditcher dug a trench for the pipeline. 挖掘机挖了一条管道沟。 He was a ditcher, leaving his family with nothing. 他是个抛弃者，离开家人时什么也没留下。|近义词：excavator, trencher, deserter|反义词：loyal, faithful|noun

ditches:/ˈdɪtʃɪz/| n.|沟渠；壕沟|Long narrow trenches dug in the ground, usually at the side of a road or field, to hold or carry away water.|The ditches alongside the road were filled with rainwater. 路边的沟渠里充满了雨水。|近义词：trenches, furrows, channels|反义词：ridges, mounds|noun

ditches:/ˈdɪtʃɪz/| v.|抛弃；丢弃|To get rid of something or someone that is no longer wanted.|She ditched her old car for a new one. 她把旧车换成了新车。 He ditched his friends at the party and went home alone. 他在派对上抛弃了朋友，独自回家了。|近义词：abandon, desert, discard|反义词：keep, retain|verb

ditching:/ˈdɪtʃɪŋ/| n.|抛弃；开沟|The act of ditching something or someone, or the act of digging ditches.|The ditching of the old car was long overdue.  这辆旧车早就该报废了。 The ditching took several days to complete. 开沟工作花了几天时间才完成。|近义词：abandonment, desertion, excavation|反义词：retention, keeping|noun
