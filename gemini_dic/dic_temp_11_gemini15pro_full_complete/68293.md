falcidian:/fɔːlˈsɪdiən/
adjective
法尔奇迪亚的；（指遗嘱）保留四分之一遗产给法定继承人的
Relating to the Falcidian law, a Roman law that guaranteed heirs at least one-quarter of an inheritance.  This law prevented testators from leaving away more than three-quarters of their estates in legacies.
The falcidian portion is one-quarter of the inheritance.  法尔奇迪亚部分是遗产的四分之一。
近义词:  testamentary,  hereditary
反义词:  N/A
词性: adjective

Etymology:
From Latin *Falcidia (lex)*,  "Falcidian (law)", named after the tribune Publius Falcidius, who introduced the law in 40 BC.
