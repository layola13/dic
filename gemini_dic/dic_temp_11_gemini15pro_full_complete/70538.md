filibuster: /ˈfɪlɪbʌstər/ (US) /ˈfɪlɪbʌstə/ (UK)| n. v.| 阻挠议事，长篇演说阻挠议事；掠夺者，海盗 |A prolonged speech or series of speeches made to delay action, especially in a legislative assembly. filibuster: /ˈfɪlɪbʌstər/ (US) /ˈfɪlɪbʌstə/ (UK)| n. | 阻挠议事者，长篇演说阻挠议事者；掠夺者，海盗 |A person who engages in a filibuster.|The filibuster finally yielded the floor after speaking for twelve hours.  这名阻挠议事者在讲了十二个小时后终于放弃了发言权。|近义词：obstructionist|反义词: cooperator|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *filibustero*, "freebooter," from Dutch *vrijbuiter*, from *vrij* "free" + *buiter* "plunderer". Originally referred to pirates in the Caribbean, later applied to adventurers who tried to incite revolutions in Central America.  The meaning shifted to its current political sense in the mid-19th century US.
