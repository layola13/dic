headfirst:/ˈhedˈfɜːrst/ (US), /ˈhedˈfɜːst/ (UK)| adv. adj.|头朝下地；轻率地；鲁莽地|Diving headfirst into a project means starting it energetically and without hesitation, often without thinking about the potential risks.|He jumped headfirst into the pool. 他一头扎进了游泳池。|近义词：recklessly, impulsively, hastily|反义词：cautiously, deliberately|词性: 副词, 形容词

Etymology:  A compound of "head" and "first."  "Head" comes from Old English "hēafod," and "first" comes from Old English "fyrst." The combination literally describes the physical act of going head first.  Figuratively, it implies plunging into something without careful consideration.
