insinuant:/ɪnˈsɪnjuːənt/| adj.|暗示的，谄媚的，奉承的|Suggesting or implying something bad or unpleasant in an indirect and unpleasant way; intended to gain someone's favor by flattery.|He gave her an insinuant smile. 他给了她一个谄媚的微笑。|近义词：ingratiating, flattering, unctuous, oily,  obsequious|反义词：frank, direct, blunt, forthright|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Late Latin insinuant- ‘inserting gently’, from the verb insinuare, from in- ‘into’ + sinuare ‘to curve’.
