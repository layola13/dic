moats:/məʊts/| n. |护城河，壕沟，防御手段|A deep, wide ditch surrounding a castle, fort, or town, typically filled with water and intended as a defense against attack.  Figuratively, it can refer to any protective barrier.|The castle was surrounded by a deep moat. 城堡周围环绕着一条深深的护城河。|近义词：ditch, trench, channel, barrier, defense|反义词:bridge, opening|词性:名词

词源: From Old French *mote (modern French motte), from a Vulgar Latin word *muttum, probably related to the verb movere ("to move"). The original meaning referred to a mound of earth, and the moat was the ditch dug around it.
