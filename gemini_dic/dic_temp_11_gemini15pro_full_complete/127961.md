neorealism:/ˌniːoʊˈriːəlɪzəm/ | n. | 新现实主义 | A movement in art, literature, and especially film, characterized by a close attention to social reality, an avoidance of artificiality and escapism, and often a focus on the lives of ordinary people. It originated in Italy after World War II. |  Example 1: Italian neorealism influenced filmmakers around the world.  意大利新现实主义影响了全世界的电影制作人。

Example 2: The film is a classic example of neorealism, depicting the struggles of working-class families. 这部电影是新现实主义的典型例子，描绘了工人阶级家庭的挣扎。 | 近义词: realism, naturalism | 反义词: romanticism, idealism | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From neo- + realism. Neo- is a combining form meaning “new,” “revived,” or “modified.” Realism refers to the representation of reality as it is, without idealization or romanticism.
