girdling:/ˈɡɜːrdlɪŋ/ | n. |环剥，环切；束缚 |The act of cutting a horizontal ring through the bark of a tree, often resulting in its death. | Girdling the tree eventually killed it. 环剥这棵树最终导致它死亡。 | synonyms: ringing, cincturing | antonyms: grafting, healing | noun

girdling:/ˈɡɜːrdlɪŋ/ | v. |环绕；包围；束缚 |To encircle or surround. | The mountains girdled the city. 群山环绕着这座城市。 | synonyms: encircle, surround, encompass | antonyms: free, release | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From *gird* (v.) + *-ling* (suffix forming nouns of action or process). *Gird* comes from Old English *gyrdan*, meaning "to gird, encircle," from Proto-Germanic *gurdjan*.
