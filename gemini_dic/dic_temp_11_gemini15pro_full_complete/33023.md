chortlers:/ˈtʃɔːrtlərz/
n.
欢笑者，愉快的人
People who chortle; those who express joy or amusement through a combination of chuckling and snorting.
The chortlers in the audience clearly enjoyed the comedian's performance.  观众席上的欢笑者显然很享受喜剧演员的表演。
近义词:laughers, gigglers, chucklers
反义词:mourners, wailers
词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
Coined by Lewis Carroll in his book "Through the Looking-Glass, and What Alice Found There" (1871), blending "chuckle" and "snort". 由路易斯·卡罗尔在其1871年出版的《爱丽丝镜中奇遇记》中创造，混合了“chuckle”（咯咯笑）和“snort”（哼笑）两个词。
