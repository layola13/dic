italicizing:/ɪˈtælɪsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 斜体化；用斜体印刷 |  Giving a slanted typeface to printed letters, often used for emphasis or to distinguish words or phrases. |  The editor italicized the foreign words in the article. 编辑将文章中的外来词用斜体印刷。 |  emphasize, highlight |  de-emphasize, downplay | verb

PHRASE->
There are not common phrases using the verb "italicizing."  It's primarily used in the context of describing the action of putting text in italics.


ETYMOLOGY->
From italic + -ize.  "Italic" ultimately derives from the Latin "Italicus," referring to the style of writing developed in Italy.
