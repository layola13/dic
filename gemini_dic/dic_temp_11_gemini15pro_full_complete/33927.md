cirrhoses:/sɪˈroʊsiːz/
noun
肝硬化
A chronic disease of the liver marked by degeneration of cells, inflammation, and fibrous thickening of tissue. It is typically a result of alcoholism or hepatitis.
The patient's cirrhoses was caused by years of heavy drinking.  这位病人的肝硬化是由多年的酗酒引起的。

cirrhoses:/sɪˈroʊsiːz/
noun
肝硬变
The end stage of chronic liver disease, characterized by fibrosis and nodule formation.
His cirrhoses required a liver transplant. 他的肝硬化需要肝移植。


Synonym:  liver cirrhosis, hepatic cirrhosis
Antonym:  healthy liver
