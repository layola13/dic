hyperfastidiously:/ˌhaɪpərˌfæˈstɪdiəsli/
| adv. | 过分讲究地，过分挑剔地 |  In an excessively fastidious manner; with extreme attention to detail and a tendency to be overly critical or demanding. | He hyperfastidiously arranged the books on his shelf, ensuring each spine was perfectly aligned. 他过分讲究地整理书架上的书，确保每本书的书脊都完美对齐。 | meticulously, scrupulously, excessively, painstakingly | carelessly, negligently, sloppily | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "hyper-" (Greek: ὑπέρ, hupér, "over, beyond") + "fastidious" (from Latin "fastidium" meaning "loathing, disgust").
