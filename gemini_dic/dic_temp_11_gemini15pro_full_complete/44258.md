croisade:/kruːˈseɪd/| n.| 十字军东征; 运动; 改革运动 |A medieval military expedition, one of a series made by Europeans to recover the Holy Land from the Muslims. / A vigorous concerted action for some cause or against some abuse. |例句：He led a crusade against government corruption. 他领导了一场反对政府腐败的运动。|例句：The First Crusade was launched in 1096. 第一次十字军东征始于1096年。|近义词：campaign, holy war, expedition|反义词：surrender, retreat|词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *croisade* "marked with a cross", from *croisier* "to mark with a cross", from Latin *crux* "cross".  The original crusaders wore a cross on their clothing as a symbol of their religious mission.
