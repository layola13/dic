goldilocks:/ˈɡəʊldɪlɒks/ (UK) /ˈɡoʊldilɑːks/ (US)| n. adj.|金发姑娘;恰到好处;温度适宜的|Referring to the fairy tale character Goldilocks, or something that is "just right," not too much or too little.|例句：The porridge was goldilocks – not too hot, not too cold, but just right. 粥的温度刚刚好——不冷不热，恰到好处。|近义词：perfect, ideal, just right|反义词:extreme, excessive, insufficient|词性:名词，形容词

goldilocks planet:/ˈɡəʊldɪlɒks ˈplænɪt/ (UK) /ˈɡoʊldilɑːks ˈplænət/ (US)| n.|宜居行星|A planet located within a star's habitable zone, where conditions are not too hot or too cold to support liquid water.|例句：Scientists are searching for goldilocks planets that could potentially harbor life. 科学家们正在寻找可能孕育生命的宜居行星。|近义词：habitable planet|反义词:uninhabitable planet|词性:名词

goldilocks economy:/ˈɡəʊldɪlɒks ɪˈkɒnəmi/ (UK) /ˈɡoʊldilɑːks ɪˈkɑːnəmi/ (US)| n.|金发姑娘经济|An economy that sustains moderate economic growth and low inflation.|例句：The country experienced a goldilocks economy for several years.  这个国家经历了多年的金发姑娘经济。|近义词：stable economy|反义词:unstable economy, recession|词性:名词

goldilocks principle:/ˈɡəʊldɪlɒks ˈprɪnsɪp(ə)l/ (UK) /ˈɡoʊldilɑːks ˈprɪnsəp(ə)l/ (US)| n.|金发姑娘原则|The principle that something must fall within certain margins, as opposed to reaching extremes.|例句：The goldilocks principle applies to many aspects of life, from choosing the right temperature to finding the perfect balance between work and leisure. 金发姑娘原则适用于生活的许多方面，从选择合适的温度到找到工作与休闲之间的完美平衡。|近义词：moderate approach|反义词:extreme approach|词性:名词
