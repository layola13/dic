layoff:/ˈleɪɒf/ (UK) /ˈleɪˌɔːf/ (US)| n.|裁员，解雇；停工，歇工|A layoff is a temporary or permanent discharge of employees for business reasons, such as when certain positions are no longer needed or when a company is experiencing financial difficulty.|The company announced layoffs of 100 employees. 公司宣布裁员100人。|近义词：redundancy (UK), dismissal, discharge, furlough|反义词：hiring, recruitment|词性:noun

layoff:/ˈleɪˈɔːf/ (US) /ˈleɪˈɒf/ (UK)| vt.|解雇，裁员|To lay off someone is to temporarily or permanently discharge them from their job.|The company had to lay off several workers due to the economic downturn. 由于经济衰退，公司不得不解雇几名工人。|近义词：dismiss, discharge, fire, let go|反义词：hire, employ, recruit|词性:verb


词源: lay + off.  "lay" 有放置、停止的意思，"off" 有离开、停止的意思。结合起来表示停止工作，也就是解雇或停工。
