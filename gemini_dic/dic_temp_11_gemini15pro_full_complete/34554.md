clear-out: /ˈklɪəraʊt/ (UK) /ˈklɪrˌaʊt/ (US)| n.| 清理；清仓大甩卖；清除|The act of removing unwanted items from a place, often in order to make space or improve organization; a sale of goods at reduced prices to get rid of them.|We had a big clear-out of the attic and found lots of old toys. 我们彻底清理了阁楼，找到了许多旧玩具。|近义词：cleaning, purge, sale|反义词：accumulation, hoarding|词性: noun

clear-out: /ˈklɪəraʊt/ (UK) /ˈklɪrˌaʊt/ (US)| n.| 离开；退出|The act of leaving a place or position, often suddenly or unexpectedly.|There's been a clear-out of senior managers at the company. 公司的高级经理都离开了。|近义词：departure, exit, exodus|反义词：arrival, entry|词性: noun
