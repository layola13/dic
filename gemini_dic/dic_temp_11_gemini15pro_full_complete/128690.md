nidering:/ˈnaɪdərɪŋ/ | adj. |卑鄙的，懦弱的，邪恶的 |Contemptible; cowardly; base.  Deserving of scorn and condemnation due to a lack of courage or moral integrity.  Often used in a literary or archaic context.|  "The nidering thief stole from the poor." 卑鄙的贼偷了穷人的东西。 |Synonyms: despicable, cowardly, base, vile, contemptible, dastardly |Antonyms: honorable, courageous, noble, valiant |adjective

Etymology->
From Middle English *nithering*, from Old English *nīþering*, present participle of *nīþerian* (“to humiliate, bring low”), from *nīþer* (“down, below, beneath”).  Related to nether.
