obduracy:/ˈɒbdjʊrəsi/ (US) /ˈɒbdʒərəsi/ (UK)| n. |顽固，执拗，冷酷无情|The obduracy of the management made negotiations difficult.  |Example:  His obduracy in the face of overwhelming evidence was astonishing. 他面对压倒性的证据仍然顽固不化，令人震惊。

Example:  The government's obduracy on the issue led to a prolonged strike. 政府在这个问题上的顽固态度导致了长期罢工。|近义词：stubbornness, intransigence, inflexibility, pertinacity|反义词:yielding, compliance, flexibility, amenability|词性:名词


obdurate:/ˈɒbdjʊrət/ (US) /ˈɒbdʒərət/ (UK)| adj. |顽固的，执拗的，冷酷无情的|He remained obdurate despite pleas from his family. |Example:  The obdurate child refused to eat his vegetables.  这个固执的孩子拒绝吃蔬菜。

Example:  Despite all our efforts, he remained obdurate in his refusal to cooperate. 尽管我们做了各种努力，他仍然顽固地拒绝合作。|近义词：stubborn, intransigent, inflexible, unyielding|反义词:yielding, compliant, flexible, amenable|词性:形容词


词源:  Late Middle English: from Old French obduré, past participle of obdurer ‘harden’, from Latin obdurare, from ob- ‘against’ + durare ‘harden’ (from durus ‘hard’).
