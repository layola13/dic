outsource: /ˈaʊtsɔːrs/ (British) /ˈaʊtsɔːrs/ (American)| v. n.|外包，外购；外部采购|To obtain (goods or a service) from an outside supplier, especially in place of an internal source.| Many companies outsource their IT operations to specialized firms. 许多公司将其IT运营外包给专业公司。|The company outsources its customer service. 这家公司将其客户服务外包。|Synonyms: subcontract, contract out, farm out|Antonyms: insource, internalize|verb, noun

outsources: /ˈaʊtsɔːrsɪz/ (British) /ˈaʊtsɔːrsɪz/ (American)| v. (third person singular present)|外包，外购；外部采购（outsource的第三人称单数形式）|Third person singular present tense form of the verb "outsource". |He outsources all of his administrative work. 他把所有的行政工作都外包出去了。|She outsources the manufacturing of her products. 她将产品的制造外包。|Synonyms: subcontracts, contracts out, farms out|Antonyms: insources, internalizes|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + source.  First recorded in the 1980s. "Out-" indicates external or outside, while "source" refers to the origin or provider.  Thus, "outsource" literally means to obtain something from an outside source.
