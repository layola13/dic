concupy: /kənˈkjuːpi/ | v. | 贪求，渴望 | To desire strongly, especially something considered wrong or immoral. |  He was concupying his neighbor's wife. 他贪恋着邻居的妻子。 | The king concupied power and wealth. 国王贪图权力和财富。 | 近义词: covet, crave, lust after | 反义词: abstain from, renounce | 词性: 动词

词源:  From Late Latin *concupire, from Latin concupiscere "to long for eagerly," from com- "together" + cupiscere "to desire."
