come to yourself:/kʌm tə jərˈsɛlf/
|phrasal verb|
|苏醒；恢复知觉；冷静下来|
|To regain consciousness or composure after fainting, being stunned, or experiencing a strong emotion.|
|After fainting, she slowly came to herself.  昏倒后，她慢慢地苏醒过来。|

|He finally came to himself and realized he'd overreacted. 他终于冷静下来，意识到自己反应过度了。|
|regain consciousness, recover, come round, come to, snap out of it|
|pass out, faint, lose consciousness|
|verb phrase|


come-to-your-senses:/kʌm tə jər ˈsɛnsɪz/
|phrasal verb|
|恢复理智；醒悟过来|
|To start behaving sensibly after a period of foolishness or irrationality.|
|He needs to come to his senses and stop spending so much money. 他需要恢复理智，别再乱花钱了。|

|She finally came to her senses and left her abusive husband. 她终于醒悟过来，离开了虐待她的丈夫。|
|see reason, think straight, regain composure, wise up|
|lose one's mind, act foolishly, be irrational|
|verb phrase|
