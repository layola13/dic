flyblown:/ˈflaɪˌbloʊn/| adj.| 苍蝇产卵的，腐败的，污秽的|Infested with fly larvae; tainted, putrid.  Figuratively, something that is flyblown is considered spoiled or corrupt.|The meat was left uncovered and became flyblown.  那块肉没盖上，招了苍蝇，生蛆了。|The politician's reputation was flyblown after the scandal.  丑闻之后，这位政治家的名声败坏了。|近义词:putrid, rotten, tainted, contaminated, corrupt|反义词:fresh, clean, pure|词性:形容词

词源: From "fly" (noun) + "blown" (past participle of "blow"), referring to the historical belief that flies spontaneously generated from rotting meat.  The "blown" part refers to the idea of the flies being "blown" onto the meat, or the eggs being "blown" onto it by the wind.
