evangelicalism:/ˌiːvænˈdʒɛlɪkəlɪzəm/ | n. |福音主义，福音派基督教|A worldwide transdenominational movement within Protestant Christianity maintaining that the essence of the gospel consists in the doctrine of salvation by grace through faith in Jesus Christ's atonement.  It emphasizes the experience of conversion or regeneration and the importance of evangelization. | The rise of evangelicalism in the 20th century has significantly impacted American politics. 20世纪福音主义的兴起对美国政治产生了重大影响。|  The preacher's sermon focused on the core tenets of evangelicalism. 牧师的布道集中在福音主义的核心原则上。|Synonyms: revivalism, fundamentalism | Antonyms: liberalism, secularism | noun
