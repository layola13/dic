circumstantialness:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈstænʃəlˌnəs/ /ˌsɝːkəmˈstænʃəlˌnəs/ | n. | 详尽；冗长；细节性 | The state of being detailed and thorough, often to the point of being excessive. |  His circumstantialness in describing the event bored everyone. 他对事件的冗长描述使每个人都感到厌烦。| 近义词：detail, particularity, thoroughness, prolixity | 反义词：brevity, conciseness | 词性:名词

circumstantial:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈstænʃəl/ /ˌsɝːkəmˈstænʃəl/ | adj. | 详细的；依情况而定的；偶然的；间接的 | Relating to, dependent on, or derived from circumstances.  | The evidence against him was purely circumstantial.  针对他的证据完全是间接的。| 近义词：indirect, incidental, contingent | 反义词：direct, conclusive | 词性:形容词

PHRASE->

what if = 如果...会怎么样
What if we try a different approach? 如果我们尝试不同的方法会怎么样？

what for = 为了什么
What are you saving money for? 你存钱是为了什么？

no matter what = 无论如何
No matter what happens, I'll always be there for you. 无论发生什么，我都会在你身边。

what's more = 而且；更重要的是
He's intelligent, and what's more, he's very kind. 他很聪明，而且，他还很善良。

what about = ...怎么样
What about going to the cinema tonight? 今晚去看电影怎么样？

词源：From Late Latin circumstantialis, from circumstantia "circumstance."
