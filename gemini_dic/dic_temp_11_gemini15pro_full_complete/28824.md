carousing: /kəˈraʊzɪŋ/ | n. | 欢闹，狂欢 | The act of drinking and enjoying oneself with others in a noisy, lively way. |  They spent the evening carousing in the pub. 他们在酒吧里度过了一个狂欢的夜晚。| 近义词：reveling, partying, merrymaking | 反义词: abstaining, sobriety | noun

carouse: /kəˈraʊz/ | v. | 欢闹，狂欢 | To drink and enjoy oneself with others in a noisy, lively way. | They caroused until the early hours of the morning.  他们狂欢到凌晨。| 近义词：revel, party, celebrate | 反义词: abstain, refrain | verb

Etymology: From Middle French *carouser*, from German *gar aus* "quite out," referring to emptying one's glass.
