baboos:/bəˈbuːz/| n.|老爷; (尤指殖民地时期模仿英国人举止的)印度人(常含贬义)|A term, now offensive, for a Hindu holding a clerical position in India, especially in the British colonial administration, or sometimes for any educated Hindu, often implying servility to the British.  It is often used in a derogatory sense to refer to someone who is perceived as overly officious or obsequious, especially in a colonial context.|The novel portrays the baboos as eager to please their British superiors.|近义词：officials, clerks, Indian gentlemen (historical), compradors|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps) rebels, revolutionaries|noun
