extrapolators:/ɪkˈstræpəleɪtərz/
|n.|外推者，推断者|Someone who extrapolates; a person who infers something by extending known information.|The extrapolators predicted a significant increase in sales based on the current trend.|近义词：forecasters, predictors, interpreters|反义词：N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From extrapolate + -or. Extrapolate comes from Latin extra- "outside" + polare "to polish, smooth" (from polus "end of an axis, pole").  The original sense was "to give a smoother finish to," later "to infer (an unknown) from something that is known."
