ipecac:/ˈɪpɪkæk/ (US), /ˈɪpɪkæk/ (UK)| n.|吐根，吐根糖浆|A medicine made from the dried root of a South American plant, used to cause vomiting, especially in cases of poisoning.|The doctor administered ipecac to induce vomiting after the child swallowed cleaning fluid.  医生给孩子服用了吐根糖浆，以便在孩子吞下清洁液后催吐。|Synonym: emetic|Antonym: antiemetic|noun
