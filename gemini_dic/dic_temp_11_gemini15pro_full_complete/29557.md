catching:/ˈkætʃɪŋ/ | adj. | 吸引人的；传染性的；| Appealing, attractive, or infectious. |  The melody is so catching, I can't stop humming it. 这旋律太抓耳了，我忍不住一直哼唱。| synonyms: infectious, contagious, captivating, alluring | antonyms: repulsive, unappealing, repellent | adjective

catching:/ˈkætʃɪŋ/ | n. | 接球；捕获物；| The act of catching something or something that has been caught. | The catching of the thief was a relief for the whole neighborhood.  抓住小偷让整个社区都松了一口气。| synonyms: capture, seizure, apprehension | antonyms: release, liberation, freeing | noun

catching:/ˈkætʃɪŋ/ | v. | 抓住；感染；理解；|  To grasp or seize, to contract a disease, or to understand something.  | I'm catching a cold. 我要感冒了。| synonyms: grasping, seizing, contracting, comprehending | antonyms: missing, releasing, avoiding, misunderstanding | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cacchen*, from Old North French *cachier*, from Vulgar Latin *captiāre*, frequentative of Latin *capere* "to take, seize."
