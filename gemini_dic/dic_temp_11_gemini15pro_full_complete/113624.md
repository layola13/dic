machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | adj. | 马基雅维利的；狡猾的；诡计多端的 |  Suggestive of or characterized by expediency, deceit, and cunning, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian political philosopher known for his treatise "The Prince," which advocated pragmatic and often ruthless tactics in political leadership. |  His machiavellian schemes to gain power ultimately failed. 他为了获得权力而耍的马基雅维利式的阴谋最终失败了。 | synonyms: cunning, devious, scheming, unscrupulous, artful, crafty | antonyms: honest, straightforward, principled, ethical, ingenuous | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Niccolò Machiavelli (1469-1527), Italian Renaissance political philosopher and author of The Prince.
