holocephali:/ˌhɒləˈsɛfəlaɪ/| n. |全头亚纲|A subclass of cartilaginous fish (Chondrichthyes) characterized by a single gill opening on each side, a large head, and tooth plates rather than individual teeth. They include chimaeras, also known as ratfish or rabbitfish.|The holocephali are an ancient group of fish.|近义词:Chimaeriformes|反义词: (No direct antonym, potentially other fish subclasses like Elasmobranchii (sharks, rays, skates))|词性:Noun
Etymology: From Greek *holos* (whole) + *kephalē* (head)
