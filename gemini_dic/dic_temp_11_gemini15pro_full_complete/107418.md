lammergeir:/ˈlæmərɡaɪər/| n.|胡兀鹫|A large Old World vulture (Gypaetus barbatus) with feathered head and neck, inhabiting mountainous regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa, and noted for its habit of carrying bones high into the air and dropping them onto rocks to break them open and expose the marrow. It is also called the bearded vulture.|The lammergeir soared high above the jagged peaks. 胡兀鹫在锯齿状的山峰上翱翔。|Synonyms: bearded vulture, bone-breaker|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a specific animal)|noun
