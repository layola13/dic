aquarobics:/ˈɑːkwəˈroʊbɪks/| n. |水中有氧运动|A form of exercise performed in water, typically in a swimming pool, designed to improve cardiovascular fitness and muscle strength.|She attends aquarobics classes three times a week. 她每周上三次水中有氧运动课。|Synonyms: water aerobics, aquatic fitness|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, could consider land-based exercise)|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From *aqua* (Latin, "water") + *aerobics* (from Greek *aēr*, "air" + *bios*, "life").  The term refers to exercises performed in the presence of oxygen (aerobic) and in water (aqua).
