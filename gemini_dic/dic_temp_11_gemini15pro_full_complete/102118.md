isotope:/ˈaɪsəˌtoʊp/ (US) /ˈaɪsətəʊp/ (UK)| n. |同位素|One of two or more species of atoms of a chemical element with the same atomic number and position in the periodic table and nearly identical chemical behaviour but with different atomic masses and physical properties.|Carbon-14 is a radioactive isotope of carbon. 碳-14是碳的放射性同位素。|Synonym: nuclide |Antonym: N/A (Isotopes don't have direct opposites.)|noun
