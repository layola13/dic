ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *corsnesse, *coursnesse, from Old French corse, from Latin cursus (“a running, course”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱers- (“to run”).  More at course.  The sense development is from “rough, coarse, common” to “rough in texture” and “rough in manners or speech.”
