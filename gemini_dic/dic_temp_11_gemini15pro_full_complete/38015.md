complin:/kəmˈplɪn/ | n. |  奉承;恭维话;客套话 | An expression of praise, admiration, or flattery. | He showered her with complins. 他对她大加赞美。 | 近义词: flattery, compliment, praise | 反义词: criticism, insult | 词性:名词

词源: From obsolete French *complin, from Latin complēre (“to fill up, complete”).  While similar in spelling and pronunciation to "compliment," "complin" is an older, now less common word with a distinct meaning closer to "flattery."  It carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting insincerity or excessive praise.
