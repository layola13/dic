effront:/ɪˈfrʌnt/ | n. | 厚颜无耻；放肆 | Shameless boldness; impudence. |  He had the effrontery to ask for a raise after only a week on the job.  他竟然敢在工作才一个星期就要求加薪。 | 近义词: impudence, audacity, insolence, temerity, cheek, nerve, gall | 反义词: deference, respect, modesty, humility | 词性: 名词

effront:/ɪˈfrʌnt/ | vt. | 冒犯；当面侮辱 | To insult openly; to treat with insolence or contempt. | The unruly child effronted the teacher by sticking out his tongue. 这个不守规矩的孩子向老师吐舌头，冒犯了老师。 | 近义词: insult, offend, affront, slight | 反义词: respect, honor, flatter | 词性: 动词

词源:
From Old French *effronter, from Late Latin exfrōntāre "to make shameless," from Latin ex- "out" + frōns "forehead, brow, face, front."  The sense evolution is from "to deprive of modesty" to "to insult openly."
