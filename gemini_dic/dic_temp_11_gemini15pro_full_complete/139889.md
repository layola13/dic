odd:/ɒd/ (UK) /ɑːd/ (US)| adj.|奇怪的，奇数的，临时的，剩余的|Strange or unusual; not regular or expected; (of numbers) not able to be divided exactly by two.|例句：He has some very odd habits. 他有一些非常奇怪的习惯。|例句：1, 3, 5, and 7 are odd numbers. 1，3，5和7是奇数。|近义词：strange, unusual, peculiar, bizarre, weird, singular, eccentric|反义词：even, normal, regular, usual, ordinary|词性:形容词

odd:/ɒd/ (UK) /ɑːd/ (US)| n.|可能性，奇特的事物|(usually plural) something that is not usual or expected|例句：The odds are against him winning. 他获胜的可能性很小。|例句：It's odds that he'll be late.  他很可能会迟到。|近义词：probability, chance, likelihood|反义词：certainty|词性:名词

词源: From Old Norse *oddi, meaning "third point", "triangle".  Related to Old English ord, meaning "point of a weapon".  The numerical sense developed from the idea of a third thing added to a pair, making it uneven.
