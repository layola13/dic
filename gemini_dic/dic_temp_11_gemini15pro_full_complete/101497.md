iriscope:/ˈaɪrɪskoʊp/| n. |虹膜镜，虹膜仪|An instrument used to examine the iris of the eye, often based on the belief that patterns, colors, and other characteristics of the iris can be examined to determine information about a patient's systemic health.|An iridologist uses an iriscope to assess the patient's health.|Synonyms: iridoscope|Antonyms: None|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From irido- + -scope.  Irido- comes from Greek iris, iridos, meaning "iris (of the eye)". -scope comes from Greek skopein, meaning "to look at".
