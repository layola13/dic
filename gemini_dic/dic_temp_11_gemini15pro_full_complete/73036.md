footholds:/ˈfʊthəʊldz/ (UK) /ˈfʊthoʊldz/ (US)| n. |立足点；稳固地位；支撑点|A secure place where you can put your foot when climbing or walking in a difficult place.  A relatively secure position from which further progress can be made.|The climbers searched for footholds in the rock face.|近义词：footing, grip, purchase, toehold|反义词: instability, insecurity|词性:名词

Etymology: From "foot" + "hold."  "Foot" traces back to Old English "fōt," and "hold" comes from Old English "healdan," meaning "to hold, keep, guard."
