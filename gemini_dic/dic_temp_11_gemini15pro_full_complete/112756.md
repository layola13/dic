ltes:/ɛl tiː iː ɛs/ | n. | Long Term Evolution (LTE) |  A standard for wireless broadband communication for mobile devices and data terminals, based on the GSM/EDGE and UMTS/HSPA network technologies. It is often marketed as 4G LTE, although it does not meet the technical criteria of a 4G wireless service. |  My phone uses LTE for fast internet access. 我的手机使用LTE进行快速互联网访问。|  Synonyms: 4G (often used interchangeably, though not technically correct), mobile broadband | Antonyms: 3G, 2G | noun

注意事项:  LTE is an initialism, and thus is usually written in all caps. While some pronounce it as a word (/ˈɛltiː/), the pronunciation as individual letters is more common.  LTE is sometimes mistakenly referred to as 4G, but it is technically a pre-4G technology.  5G is the successor to LTE.
