catcalling: /ˈkætˌkɔːlɪŋ/ (US), /ˈkætˌkɔːlɪŋ/ (UK)| n. | 街头搭讪，吹口哨调戏 |The act of shouting harassing and/or sexually suggestive comments at someone, typically a woman, publicly.|  She experienced frequent catcalling while walking home from work. 她下班回家时经常遇到街头搭讪。| 近义词：street harassment, sexual harassment | 反义词: respect, courtesy | 词性: 名词

Etymology:  A compound of "cat" (referring to a woman, often used dismissively or objectifyingly) and "calling" (shouting or making a call).  The term likely originated in the early to mid-20th century.
