outkick:/ˌaʊtˈkɪk/ | vt. |超越，胜过，超过(尤指在踢球方面) | to kick farther or more effectively than; surpass, exceed, especially in kicking. |  The rookie outkicked the veteran kicker by 10 yards.  这位新秀比经验丰富的踢球员多踢了10码。

ETYMOLOGY->
From out- + kick.  "Out-" prefix meaning "beyond" or "exceeding".  "Kick" referring to the action of striking with the foot.  Thus, "outkick" literally means to kick further than.
