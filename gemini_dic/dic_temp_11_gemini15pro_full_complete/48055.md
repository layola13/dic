declaiming:/dɪˈkleɪmɪŋ/
verb
慷慨陈词；朗诵；演讲；高谈阔论
delivering a speech, poem, etc. in an impassioned, theatrical manner.
The actor was declaiming his lines with great passion.  演员充满激情地朗诵着他的台词。
Synonyms: orating, reciting, speechifying, holding forth
Antonyms: whispering, muttering, mumbling
词性: 动词 (verb)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "declamare" (to cry out, speak loudly, declaim), from "de-" (intensive prefix) + "clamare" (to cry out, shout).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*kel-" (to shout).
