maligned:/məˈlaɪnd/ | adj. | 诽谤的，中伤的 | Having been spoken ill of; defamed. |例句：The maligned politician struggled to regain public trust. 这位被诽谤的政治家努力重新获得公众的信任。

例句：She felt maligned by the false accusations in the press. 她觉得被媒体的虚假指控诽谤了。

近义词：defamed, slandered, vilified, disparaged, calumniated | 反义词：praised, lauded, extolled | 词性：形容词

词源：From the Old French *malignier*, meaning "to speak ill of," derived from Latin *malignare*, from *malignus* "ill-disposed, wicked," from *malus* "bad" + *-gnus* (related to genus "birth, race, kind").
