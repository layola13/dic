disquiettude:/dɪsˈkwaɪəˌtjuːd/| n. |不安；焦虑；忧虑|A state of uneasiness or anxiety.|例句：A sense of disquiettude settled over the crowd as the news spread.  消息传开后，人群中弥漫着一种不安的感觉。

例句：The economic downturn caused widespread disquiettude among investors. 经济低迷引起了投资者普遍的不安。|近义词：unease, anxiety, worry, apprehension, perturbation|反义词：calm, tranquility, peace, composure|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *disquietude*, from Old French *desquietude*, from *desquieter* ("to disquiet"), from *des-* (expressing negation, reversal) + *quieter* ("to quiet"), from Latin *quies* ("rest, quiet").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kʷeyh₁-* ("to rest, lie down").
