loyalist:/ˈlɔɪəlɪst/| n. |忠诚者；保皇党|A person who remains loyal to a political cause, party, or government, especially during a time of revolt or change.|例句：The loyalists refused to abandon the king.  这些忠诚者拒绝放弃国王。|例句：During the American Revolution, loyalists supported the British crown. 在美国独立战争期间，保皇党支持英国王室。|近义词：patriot (when referring to one's own side), supporter, adherent, devotee|反义词：rebel, traitor, dissident, insurgent|词性: noun

Etymology:
From *loyal* + *-ist*. *Loyal* comes from Old French *loial*, from Latin *lēgālis* "legal, pertaining to the law," from *lēx* "law."
