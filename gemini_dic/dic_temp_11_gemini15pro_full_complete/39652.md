conservatism:/kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/| n.|保守主义，守旧性|A political or theological orientation advocating the preservation of the best in society and opposing radical changes.|Many people support conservatism because they believe it maintains stability.|近义词：traditionalism, orthodoxy|反义词：liberalism, radicalism|词性:noun
Etymology: From Late Latin *conservatismus*, from Latin conservatus, past participle of conservare “to preserve”. Ultimately from con- "together" + servare "to keep".
