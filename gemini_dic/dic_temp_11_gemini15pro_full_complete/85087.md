hardball:/ˈhɑːrdbɔːl/| n. adj.|强硬手段，强硬路线；硬式棒球|Uncompromising and ruthless methods or dealings, especially in politics or business.  Also refers to baseball played with a hard ball.|例句：They played hardball to get what they wanted. 他们采取强硬手段得到了他们想要的。|近义词：aggressive tactics, no-holds-barred, tough negotiations|反义词:softball, compromise, negotiation|词性:名词, 形容词


hardball:/ˈhɑːrdbɔːl/| adj.|强硬的，不妥协的|Uncompromising and ruthless.|例句：The senator was known for his hardball tactics.  这位参议员以其强硬的策略而闻名。|近义词：tough, uncompromising, ruthless|反义词:lenient, compromising, flexible|词性:形容词


词源:  Compound of *hard* and *ball*, referring originally to baseball played with a hard ball as opposed to softball.  The figurative meaning developed in the mid-20th century.
