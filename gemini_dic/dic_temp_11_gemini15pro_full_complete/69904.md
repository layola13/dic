fertilize:/ˈfɜːrtəlaɪz/ (US) /ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz/ (UK)| vt.|施肥，使肥沃，使受精|To make soil or land produce more by adding chemicals or natural substances; to cause an egg or seed to develop into a new animal or plant by joining it with a male cell.|例句：He fertilized the lawn with bone meal. 他用骨粉给草坪施肥。|近义词：enrich, nourish, inseminate|反义词:sterilize|词性:动词

fertilizer:/ˈfɜːrtəlaɪzər/ (US) /ˈfɜːtɪlaɪzər/ (UK)| n.|肥料|A natural or chemical substance added to soil or land to increase its fertility.|例句：The farmer spread fertilizer on the fields. 农民在地里撒肥料。|近义词：manure, compost|反义词:N/A|词性:名词

fertilization:/ˌfɜːrtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌfɜːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ (UK)| n.|施肥，受精|The act or process of fertilizing something, especially land or an egg.|例句：Fertilization of the egg occurs in the fallopian tube. 卵子的受精发生在输卵管中。|近义词：insemination, impregnation|反义词:sterilization|词性:名词

fertilized:/ˈfɜːrtəlaɪzd/ (US) /ˈfɜːtɪlaɪzd/ (UK)| adj.|已受精的，已施肥的|Having been made fertile or productive, especially by the addition of fertilizer.|例句：The fertilized egg will develop into an embryo. 受精卵将发育成胚胎。|近义词：enriched, nourished|反义词:unfertilized, sterile|词性:形容词
