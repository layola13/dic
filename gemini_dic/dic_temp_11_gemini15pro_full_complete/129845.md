non-idealistic:/ˌnɒnˌaɪdiːəˈlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 非理想主义的；务实的 | Not based on or believing in ideals or idealism; practical. |  She has a non-idealistic view of marriage, focusing on compatibility and shared goals rather than romantic love. 她对婚姻持非理想主义的观点，注重的是相容性和共同目标，而不是浪漫的爱情。

| Example:  He took a non-idealistic approach to the problem, focusing on achievable solutions. 他以务实的态度处理这个问题，专注于可实现的解决方案。


| Synonyms: pragmatic, practical, realistic, down-to-earth | Antonyms: idealistic, utopian, romantic, quixotic | Word Type: Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

From non- + idealistic.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of," and "idealistic" comes from "ideal" + "-istic."  "Ideal" ultimately derives from the Greek "idea" meaning "form" or "pattern."
