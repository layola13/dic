chinook: /tʃɪˈnʊk/ (US), /tʃɪˈnuːk/ (UK)| n.|奇努克风（一种温暖干燥的风）;奇努克人（北美印第安人的一支）|A warm, dry wind that blows down the eastern slopes of the Rocky Mountains. Also, a member of a Native American people originally inhabiting the region from the Columbia River to Puget Sound.|The chinooks melted the snow quickly. 奇努克风很快融化了雪。|近义词:foehn (föhn)|反义词:cold wind|词性:名词

Etymology: From Chinook Jargon tsinuk, meaning "people of Chinook," referring to a tribe at the mouth of the Columbia River.
