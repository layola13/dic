inauguration: /ɪˌnɔːɡjəˈreɪʃn/ | n. | 就职典礼；落成典礼；开幕式 |  A ceremony in which the president of a country or the like officially begins a term of office; a ceremony to mark the beginning or first use of something important, such as a new building. | The inauguration of the new president was a grand affair. 新总统的就职典礼是一场盛大的活动。

| The inauguration of the new museum was attended by many dignitaries. 新博物馆的开幕式有许多要人出席。 | 近义词：commencement, initiation, dedication, opening | 反义词：conclusion, termination | 词性: noun


Etymology:
From Late Latin inauguratio (“inauguration, consecration”), from Latin inaugurare (“to take omens from the flight of birds; to install in office after taking auguries, to inaugurate”), from in- (“in, on, upon”) + augurare (“to foretell, predict, divine, prophesy, conjecture”), from augur (“augur, diviner, soothsayer”).
