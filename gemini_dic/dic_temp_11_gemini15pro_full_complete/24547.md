broadsheet: /ˈbrɔːdʃiːt/ | n. | 大幅报纸；单张印刷品 | A newspaper printed on large sheets of paper, typically regarded as more serious and less sensationalist than tabloids. |  Example 1: He preferred reading broadsheets to tabloids, finding their analysis more in-depth. 他更喜欢读大幅报纸而不是小报，觉得它们分析得更深入。

Example 2: The broadsheet covered the political scandal in meticulous detail.  这份大幅报纸对政治丑闻进行了细致的报道。 | 近义词: newspaper, journal, quality paper | 反义词: tabloid | 词性: 名词

broadsheet: /ˈbrɔːdʃiːt/ | adj. (less common) | 大幅的；单张印刷的 | Relating to or characteristic of a broadsheet newspaper.  | Example 1:  The broadsheet format made the photographs particularly striking. 大幅的版式使照片格外引人注目。

Example 2: He took a broadsheet approach to design, prioritizing clarity and readability. 他采用大幅报纸的设计方法，优先考虑清晰度和可读性。 | 近义词: full-size, large-format | 反义词: tabloid | 词性: 形容词
