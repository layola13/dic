backlights:/ˈbækˌlaɪts/ | n. | 背光；背光灯 | A light source placed behind the subject, especially in photography or filmmaking, to create a silhouette or highlight the edges of the subject.  | The photographer used backlights to create a dramatic effect. 摄影师用背光营造出戏剧性的效果。 | Synonyms: backlight illumination, background light | Antonyms: front light, key light | noun

backlit:/ˌbækˈlɪt/ | adj. | 背光的 | Illuminated from behind. | The backlit keyboard made it easy to type in the dark.  背光键盘使在黑暗中打字变得很容易。 | Synonyms: illuminated from behind | Antonyms: front-lit | adjective

backlighting:/ˈbækˌlaɪtɪŋ/ | n. | 背光 | The act or technique of illuminating something from behind. | The backlighting in the photo made the subject appear ethereal. 照片中的背光使拍摄对象看起来飘渺。| Synonyms: backlighting effect | Antonyms: front lighting | noun
