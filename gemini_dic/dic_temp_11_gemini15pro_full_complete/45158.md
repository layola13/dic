cudweed:/ˈkʌdˌwiːd/| n. |鼠麴草，棉花草|A type of plant with small white or yellow flowers and leaves covered in soft, woolly hairs.  It belongs to the family Asteraceae. The name comes from the belief that cows chew it as a cud.|The field was covered in cudweed and other wildflowers.|近义词：everlasting, pussytoes|反义词:|n. (noun)
