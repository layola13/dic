Manichee: /ˈmænɪkiː/ | n. adj. | 摩尼教徒；二元论者；绝对主义者 | A follower of Manichaeism, a religion founded in the 3rd century AD by Mani, characterized by dualism, a belief in the opposing forces of good and evil. Also used to describe someone who sees things in black and white, with no shades of gray. |  He was accused of being a Manichee, seeing the world as a battle between good and evil.  他被指责是摩尼教徒，将世界视为善恶之战。 | Synonym: dualist, absolutist | Antonym: relativist | noun

Manichean: /ˌmænɪˈkiːən/ | adj. | 摩尼教的；二元论的；绝对主义的 | Relating to Manichaeism or its doctrines; dualistic. | His Manichean worldview prevented him from seeing the complexities of the situation. 他的二元世界观使他无法看到情况的复杂性。 | Synonym: dualistic, absolutist | Antonym: relativistic | adjective
