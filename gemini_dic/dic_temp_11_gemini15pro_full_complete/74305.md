fractious:/ˈfrækʃəs/ | adj. | 易怒的，暴躁的 | Easily irritated or annoyed; readily angered; unruly. | The fractious child refused to go to bed.  这个暴躁的孩子不肯上床睡觉。 |  The fractious crowd grew increasingly restless as the politician spoke. 躁动不安的人群随着政治家的讲话越来越焦躁不安。 | Synonyms: irritable, quarrelsome, peevish, unruly, refractory | Antonyms: docile, agreeable, placid, cooperative | adjective

fractious:/ˈfrækʃəs/ | adj. | (群体)不和的，难驾驭的 | (of a group or organization) difficult to control; unruly.  | The political party became increasingly fractious as the election approached. 随着选举临近，该政党内部越来越不和。 |  Managing a fractious team proved to be a real challenge. 管理一个难以驾驭的团队被证明是一个真正的挑战。 | Synonyms: divisive, dissenting, disagreeing, discordant | Antonyms: united, harmonious, cohesive | adjective
