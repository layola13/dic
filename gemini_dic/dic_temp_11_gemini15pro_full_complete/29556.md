catchinesses:/ˈkætʃɪnəsɪz/| n. |朗朗上口；吸引力；感染力|The quality of being easily remembered or catchy, often referring to melodies or slogans.|The song's catchiness ensured its popularity. 这首歌朗朗上口，保证了它的流行。|Synonyms: memorability, appeal, infectiousness|Antonyms: forgettability, dullness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From catchy + -ness.  Catchy itself comes from the verb "catch," meaning to seize or grasp, evolving to also encompass the idea of grasping attention or interest. The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective "catchy" into the noun "catchiness," indicating the state or quality of being catchy.
