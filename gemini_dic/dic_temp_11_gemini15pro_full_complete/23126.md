bourgeon:/ˈbɜːrdʒən/ /ˈbʊrdʒən/
| vi. n.|萌芽，发芽；迅速发展|To begin to grow or increase rapidly; flourish.  A bud or sprout.|
例句：
The trees bourgeon in the spring.
树木在春天萌芽。

New businesses are burgeoning in the city.
新的企业在城市里蓬勃发展。

|近义词：sprout, bud, blossom, flourish, thrive, proliferate|反义词：wither, die, decline, stagnate|词性:动词(vi.), 名词(n.)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *bourgeonner*, from Old French *burjon*, from a Vulgar Latin word *burrionem*, derived from Late Latin *burra* ("wool, flock of wool"), from Latin *burrus* ("reddish-brown"). The original sense was "to put forth buds or sprouts (like tufts of wool)."
