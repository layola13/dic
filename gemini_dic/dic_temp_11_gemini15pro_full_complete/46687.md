damp:/dæmp/ | n. |潮湿，湿气 |Moisture or humidity in the air or on a surface. | The damp in the basement made the air musty. 地下室的潮气使空气发霉。 | synonyms: moisture, humidity | antonyms: dryness, aridity | noun

damp:/dæmp/ | adj. |潮湿的 |Slightly wet. | The towel was still damp after hanging for an hour. 毛巾挂了一个小时后仍然潮湿。 | synonyms: moist, humid | antonyms: dry, arid | adjective

damp:/dæmp/ | v. |使潮湿；抑制；减弱 |To make slightly wet; to suppress or check. |  He damped the cloth before wiping the table. 他在擦桌子之前把布弄湿了。/ The news damped their spirits. 这个消息使他们的情绪低落。 | synonyms: dampen, moisten, stifle, suppress | antonyms: dry, encourage | verb

damps:/dæmps/ | n. | 瘴气，有害气体 |Noxious gases or vapors, especially in mines. | The miners were wary of damps in the poorly ventilated shaft. 矿工们对通风不良的矿井中的有害气体保持警惕。 | synonyms: fumes, vapors, miasma | antonyms: fresh air | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *damp*, from Old English *damp*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *dampr* ("vapor, steam").
