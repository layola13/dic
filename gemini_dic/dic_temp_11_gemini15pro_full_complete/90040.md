hospice:/ˈhɒspɪs/ (UK) /ˈhɑːspɪs/ (US)|n.|临终关怀医院；收容所|A hospice is a home providing care for the sick, especially the terminally ill.|She spent her last few weeks in a hospice.|Synonyms: sanatorium, rest home, nursing home|Antonyms: hospital (in the sense of active treatment)|noun


hospitable:/ˈhɒspɪtəbl/ (UK) /ˈhɑːspɪtəbl/ (US)|adj.|好客的；热情友好的；（环境）适宜的|Friendly and welcoming to guests or strangers.  Also, having a pleasant or agreeable environment.|They were very hospitable and made us feel welcome.|Synonyms: welcoming, friendly, generous, kind, warm, genial, convivial, agreeable|Antonyms: inhospitable, unfriendly, unwelcoming|adjective


hospitality:/ˌhɒspɪˈtælɪti/ (UK) /ˌhɑːspɪˈtælɪti/ (US)|n.|好客；热情款待|The friendly and generous reception and entertainment of guests, visitors, or strangers.|Thank you for your kind hospitality.|Synonyms: welcome, friendliness, generosity, warmth, kindness, cordiality|Antonyms: inhospitality, hostility|noun
