catatonics: /ˌkætəˈtɑːnɪks/ | n. |紧张症患者; 紧张症;|A group of people suffering from or exhibiting symptoms of catatonia.|The catatonics in the ward remained motionless for hours.  病房里的紧张症患者一动不动地待了几个小时。|Synonyms:  catatonic patients, individuals with catatonia |Antonyms:  active individuals, responsive individuals|Noun

catatonic: /ˌkætəˈtɑːnɪk/ | adj. |紧张症的; 紧张性精神分裂症的; 呆若木鸡的|Relating to or characterized by catatonia, a state of immobility and unresponsiveness.|He fell into a catatonic state after the trauma.  经历了创伤后，他陷入了紧张状态。|Synonyms:  stuporous, unresponsive, rigid, immobile|Antonyms:  responsive, active, mobile, alert|Adjective

Etymology:  From Greek "kata-" (down) + "tonos" (tension).  Originally referred to a type of mental disorder characterized by muscular rigidity.
