lazar:/ˈleɪzər/ (US), /ˈlæzər/ (UK)| n.|麻风病人；乞丐；复活的人|A person, especially one who is poor or diseased, regarded as having been brought back from a desperate situation.|He was a lazar, shunned by society. 他是一个麻风病人，被社会所唾弃。|Synonyms: beggar, pauper, outcast|Antonyms: rich man, king|noun

lazar:/ˈleɪzər/ (US), /ˈlæzər/ (UK)| adj. (literary, archaic)|麻风的；像乞丐的；贫困的|Of or relating to a lazar; resembling a lazar in appearance or condition.|He had a lazar-like appearance, with ragged clothes and sores on his skin. 他衣衫褴褛，皮肤溃烂，一副麻风病人的模样。|Synonyms: leprous, beggarly, destitute|Antonyms: healthy, wealthy|adjective
