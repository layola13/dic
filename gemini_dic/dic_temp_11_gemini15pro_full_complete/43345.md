crampons: /ˈkræmpɒnz/ (UK) /ˈkræmpɑːnz/ (US) | n. | 登山 crampons; 冰爪 | A pair of metal frames with spikes, attached to boots for walking on ice or snow. |  Example 1:  He strapped his crampons onto his boots before tackling the icy slope. 他在攀登冰坡之前把冰爪绑在了靴子上。

Example 2:  Without crampons, it would be impossible to climb the glacier. 没有冰爪，攀登冰川是不可能的。| 近义词: ice cleats, spikes | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology: From French *crampon*, from Old French *crampon* "hook, grapple," from *cramper* "to hook, to grasp."  Ultimately of Germanic origin, related to "cramp."
