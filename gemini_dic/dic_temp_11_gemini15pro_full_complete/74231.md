fowl:/faʊl/
noun|家禽；飞禽；禽肉|Any bird of the gallinaceous order, such as chickens, turkeys, ducks, or geese, especially when raised for food.|We raise chickens and other fowl. 我们饲养鸡和其他家禽。
fowl:/faʊl/
adjective|肮脏的；恶臭的；污秽的|Offensive to the senses, especially through having a disgusting smell or taste; revolting.|A fowl smell came from the drains. 排水沟里传来一股恶臭。
fowl:/faʊl/
verb|捕猎野禽|To hunt or kill wildfowl.|He spent the weekend fowling on the marshes. 他周末在沼泽地捕猎野禽。



ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fugol, from Proto-Germanic *fuglaz, from Proto-Indo-European *pleuk- (“to fly”). Cognate with Dutch vogel, German Vogel, Gothic fugls.
