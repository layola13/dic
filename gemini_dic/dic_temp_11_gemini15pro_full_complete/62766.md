engrailment:/ɪnˈɡreɪlmənt/| n. |锯齿状，锯齿形边缘；（纹章学）燕尾形|A series of concave curves along an edge, resembling a row of teeth; in heraldry, a line of partition formed of concave curves. |The engrailment on the shield indicated the knight's lineage.|近义词：indentation, serration, notching|反义词:smoothness|词性:noun

词源: From Old French *engreillement, from engreiller (“to engrail”), from greil (“gravel”), from Frankish *greot (“gravel, sand”).
