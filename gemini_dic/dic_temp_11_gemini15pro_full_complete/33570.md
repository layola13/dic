cili:/ˈsɪliə/ (singular), /ˈsɪliə/ or /ˈsɪliaɪ/ (plural)| n.| 纤毛|A short, microscopic, hairlike vibrating structure found in large numbers on the surface of certain cells, either causing currents in the surrounding fluid, or, in some protozoans and other small organisms, providing propulsion.|The paramecium uses its cilia for movement. 草履虫用纤毛来移动。|
cilia:/ˈsɪliə/ (singular), /ˈsɪliə/ or /ˈsɪliaɪ/ (plural)| n.| 睫毛|The eyelashes, especially of the upper eyelid.|Her long cilia made her eyes look even more beautiful. 她长长的睫毛使她的眼睛看起来更加美丽。|
近义词:flagella (for movement), eyelashes (for eyelid hairs)|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *cilium* "eyelid, eyelash," later generalized to "hair-like appendage."
