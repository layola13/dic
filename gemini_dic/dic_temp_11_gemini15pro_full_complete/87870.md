hidey-hole's:/ˈhaɪdi həʊlz/ | n. | 藏身之处；隐匿处 | A hidey-hole is a small, secret place where someone or something can be hidden.  It often implies a sense of secrecy or a need for protection. |  The squirrel stashed its nuts in its little hidey-hole. 松鼠把坚果藏在它的小藏身处。| synonyms: hiding place, secret place, nook, cranny | antonyms: open area, exposed location | noun
etymology-> From "hide" (Old English *hȳdan "to conceal") + "hole" (Old English hol "cavity, hollow"). The addition of "-y" creates a diminutive and slightly informal tone.
