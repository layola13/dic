interpellate:/ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪt/ | vt. |质询，質問，interpellate (a minister) on government policy|To formally question a government official.  The opposition interpellated the prime minister on the new economic policy.|例句：The journalist interpellated the mayor about the city's budget deficit. 记者就该市的预算赤字向市长提出质询。

例句：The member of parliament interpellated the minister of defense about the military spending. 国会议员就军事开支向国防部长提出质询。|近义词：question, challenge, interrogate, query|反义词: answer, respond|词性: verb
ETYMOLOGY->
From French *interpeller*, from Latin *interpellāre* ("to interrupt, disturb, impede"), from *inter-* ("between") + *pellāre* ("to push, drive, strike").
