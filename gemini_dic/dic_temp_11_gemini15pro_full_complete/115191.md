manichordon:/ˈmænɪkɔːrdən/ | n. | 古钢琴，小型拨弦键琴 | A small rectangular clavichord.  A musical instrument of the harpsichord family, differing from the virginal chiefly in being so constructed that the strings are set obliquely to the keyboards.  It also refers to a manic or obsessed state. | The musician played a delicate melody on the manichordon.  音乐家在小型拨弦键琴上演奏了一曲优美的旋律。|Synonym: clavichord, virginal | Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "modern piano" in a loose sense) | noun
