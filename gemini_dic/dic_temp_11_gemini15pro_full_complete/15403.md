backwoodsman:/ˈbækˌwʊdzmən/ | n. | 森林居民；住在偏远地区的人；乡下人 | A man who lives and works in a remote, wooded area, often skilled in woodcraft and self-sufficient living. |  The old backwoodsman lived a solitary life deep in the forest.  这位老森林居民在森林深处过着孤独的生活。

|  He was a skilled backwoodsman, able to hunt, fish, and build his own shelter. 他是一个熟练的森林居民，能够狩猎、捕鱼和建造自己的住所。 | 近义词: frontiersman, pioneer, woodsman, bushman | 反义词: city dweller, urbanite | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From backwoods + -man. Backwoods itself comes from back + wood, referring to a remote, undeveloped forested area.
