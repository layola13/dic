morbid:/ˈmɔːrbɪd/ | adj. | 病态的，不正常的；（思想）恐怖的，阴森的 | Characterized by or appealing to an abnormal and unhealthy interest in disturbing and unpleasant subjects, especially death and disease. |  He has a morbid fascination with serial killers. 他对连环杀手有一种病态的迷恋。| synonyms: macabre, gruesome, unhealthy, ghoulish, lurid | antonyms: healthy, wholesome, cheerful | adjective

morbid:/ˈmɔːrbɪd/ | adj. | （尤指疾病）致命的，危险的 | Of, relating to, or characteristic of disease. (Often used in combination) | The epidemic was morbid and widespread.  这种流行病是致命的，而且传播广泛。| synonyms: fatal, deadly, pernicious | antonyms: healthy, benign | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *morbidus* (“diseased, sickly”), from morbus (“disease, sickness”).
