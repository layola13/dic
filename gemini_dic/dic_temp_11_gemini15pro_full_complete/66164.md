excerptor:/ˈɛksɜːrptər/ | n. |摘录者，选录者 |A person who excerpts or selects passages from a larger work, such as a book, article, or film. | The excerptor chose the most poignant passages from the novel for the anthology.  选录者从小说中挑选了最感人的段落，汇编成选集。|  近义词：compiler, selector, anthologist | 反义词: None | 词性: Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "excerpt" + "-or" (agent suffix).  "Excerpt" comes from the Latin "excerptus," past participle of "excerpere," meaning "to pick out," from "ex" (out) + "carpere" (to pluck).
