beggaring:/ˈbɛɡərɪŋ/| adj.|使人贫穷的，极其严重的，难以置信的|Making extremely poor; reducing to poverty; so great or extreme as to be beyond description.|The beggaring effects of the war left many families homeless. 战争带来的贫困效应使许多家庭无家可归。|Synonyms: impoverishing, ruining, devastating|Antonyms: enriching, prospering|adjective


beggaring:/ˈbɛɡərɪŋ/| n.|乞讨|The act of begging.|Beggaring on the streets is illegal in many cities. 在许多城市，沿街乞讨是违法的。|Synonyms: begging, panhandling, mendicancy|Antonyms: donating, giving, philanthropy|noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *beggaren*, from Old French *begar*, from *be-*, intensive prefix + *gar*, to protect, warn (from Frankish *warnōn*). The meaning shifted from "to deceive, cheat" to "to impoverish" and finally to "to beg".
