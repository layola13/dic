meads:/miːdz/
|n.| 草地，牧场 |A mead is a piece of grassland, especially one used for hay. |The wild flowers bloomed in the meads. 野花在草地上盛开。|Synonyms: meadow, pasture, field|Antonyms: desert, barren land|noun


meads:/miːdz/
|n.|蜂蜜酒，蜜酒 |An alcoholic drink made from fermented honey and water. |He enjoyed a glass of mead at the medieval festival. 他在中世纪节上享用了一杯蜂蜜酒。|Synonyms: honey wine|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mēd, from Proto-Germanic *mēdwō, from Proto-Indo-European *médʰu (“honey; mead”). Cognate with Old Frisian mēd, Old Saxon medo, Old High German metu, Old Norse mjöð, Gothic ᒥ𐌸𐌿𐍃 (midus).
