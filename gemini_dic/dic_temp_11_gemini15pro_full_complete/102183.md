italianate:/ɪˌtæliəneɪt/ | adj. |意大利风格的，模仿意大利风格的|Resembling or imitating the Italian style, especially in architecture.|The house was built in an italianate style, with arched windows and a terracotta roof. 这座房子是以意大利风格建造的，带有拱形窗户和陶土屋顶。|Synonyms: Italianesque, Italian-style|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could be something like "Gothic" or "Modernist" depending on the context)|Adjective

italianate:/ɪˈtæliəneɪt/ | v. |使意大利化，赋予意大利风格|To make Italian in character; to give an Italian style to.|They italianated the garden with cypress trees and statues. 他们在花园里种上了柏树和雕像，使其具有意大利风格。|Synonyms: Italianize|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *italianato*, past participle of *italianare* "to make Italian," from *italiano* "Italian." Ultimately from Latin *Italia* "Italy."
