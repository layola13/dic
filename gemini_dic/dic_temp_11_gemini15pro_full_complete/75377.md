frowning:/ˈfraʊnɪŋ/ | adj. | 皱眉的，不悦的 |  An expression of displeasure or concentration characterized by a furrowing of one's brows. | She gave him a frowning look. 她给了他一个皱眉的表情。| synonyms: scowling, glowering, disapproving | antonyms: smiling, beaming, cheerful | word type: adjective

frown:/fraʊn/ | v. n. | 皱眉，蹙额 | To contract the brow, as in displeasure or deep thought. | He frowned at the complicated instructions. 他对着复杂的说明书皱起了眉头。| synonyms: scowl, glare, grimace | antonyms: smile, beam | word type: verb & noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *frounen*, from Old French *frogner* "to wrinkle," of uncertain origin, possibly related to Frankish *frus*, related to Old English *frosc* "frog."  The association might be with the wrinkled appearance of a frog's skin.
