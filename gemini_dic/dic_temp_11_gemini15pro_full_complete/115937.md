marquise:/mɑːrˈkiːz/ /mɑːrˈkwiːz/| n. |侯爵夫人；女侯爵；侯爵夫人的爵位；榄尖形；榄尖形宝石|A woman holding a rank equal to that of a marquess. A long and narrow diamond or other gemstone, pointed at both ends.|例句：The marquise attended the royal ball. 侯爵夫人参加了皇室舞会。

例句：She wore a marquise cut diamond ring. 她戴着一枚榄尖形切割的钻戒。|近义词：marchioness, peeress (for a female noble in general)|反义词: commoner|词性:noun


marquise:/mɑːrˈkiːz/ /mɑːrˈkwiːz/| adj. |榄尖形的|Having the shape of a marquise.|例句：The marquise cut of the diamond made it look larger. 钻石的榄尖形切割使它看起来更大。

例句：She preferred the marquise shape for her earrings. 她更喜欢榄尖形的耳环。|近义词：oval-shaped, pointed, elongated, navette, football-shaped|反义词: round, square|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *marquise*, feminine of *marquis* (“marquess”), from Old French *marchis*, from *marche* (“border, march”), from Frankish *marka* (“border”), from Proto-Germanic *markō* (“border, sign, horse”), from Proto-Indo-European *méh₁rḱos* (“boundary, border”).
