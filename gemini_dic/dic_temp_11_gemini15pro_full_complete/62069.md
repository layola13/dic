encaustic:/ɪnˈkɔːstɪk/| adj. n.| 蜡画的，蜡画； |A painting technique in which pigments are mixed with hot wax.  A medium for painting composed of beeswax, resin, and pigment. |例句：The artist specialized in encaustic painting, creating vibrant and luminous works.  这位艺术家擅长蜡画，创作出生动而明亮的作品。

例句：The museum displayed a collection of ancient Roman encaustics.  博物馆展出了一批古罗马时期的蜡画。|近义词：wax painting|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly fresco, tempera depending on context) |词性: adjective, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin encausticus, from Greek enkaustikos (“burnt in”), from enkaiein (“to burn in”), from en- (“in”) + kaiein (“to burn”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱeh₂p- (“to smoke, to burn”).
