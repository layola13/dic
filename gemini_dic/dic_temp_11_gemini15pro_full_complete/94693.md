improbably:/ɪmˈprɒbəbli/ | adv. | 不可能地，难以置信地 | Not likely to be true or to happen; unbelievably. | He improbably survived the plane crash. 他不可思议地在飞机失事中幸存下来。|  It was an improbably happy ending. 这是一个令人难以置信的快乐结局。| likely, probably, plausibly | unlikely, probably not, implausibly | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From im- (not) + probable (likely) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Probable" comes from Latin probabilis,  meaning "provable, credible," from probare "to prove, test."
