derogating:/dɪˈrɒɡeɪtɪŋ/
adj.
贬低的，减损的
belittling, disparaging; expressive of a low opinion
His derogating remarks about her appearance hurt her feelings. 他对她外貌的贬低言论伤害了她的感情。
近义词: disparaging, belittling, demeaning, denigrating
反义词: complimentary, flattering, praising, laudatory
词性: adjective


derogate:/ˈdɛrəˌɡeɪt/
v.
贬低，减损
to cause to seem inferior; disparage
It is wrong to derogate the importance of education.  贬低教育的重要性是错误的。
近义词: belittle, disparage, detract from, diminish
反义词: enhance, praise, extol, elevate
词性: verb


derogation:/ˌdɛrəˈɡeɪʃən/
n.
贬低，减损
a belittling or disparaging remark
The constant derogation of his abilities made him insecure.  不断地贬低他的能力使他感到不安。
近义词: disparagement, belittlement, detraction, denigration
反义词: praise, commendation, acclaim, exaltation
词性: noun


词源: From Late Latin *dērogātus, past participle of dērogāre (“to repeal [part of a law], diminish, detract from”), from dē- (“from, away from”) + rogāre (“to ask”).
