dreadnought:/ˈdrɛdnɔːt/| n.|无畏舰；巨舰；厚呢大衣|A type of battleship introduced in the early 20th century, larger and more heavily armed than any before. Also refers to a type of heavy overcoat.|The dreadnought quickly made older battleships obsolete. 这艘无畏舰迅速使旧式战列舰过时。|Synonyms: battleship, warship|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "dinghy" or "small craft" for contrast)|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "dread" (meaning fear nothing) + "nought" (meaning nothing), implying a ship that feared nothing.  Originally the name of a specific British battleship, HMS Dreadnought, launched in 1906.
