gauntlier:/ˈɡɔːntliər/| adj. | 更憔悴的，更瘦削的 | More thin and bony, especially from hunger, illness, or age.  Showing the effects of hardship or suffering. |  He looked gauntlier than ever after his illness. 他病后看起来比以往任何时候都更加憔悴。 |  thinner, leaner, bonier, haggard, emaciated |  plumper, fleshier, robust | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From gaunt + -er (comparative adjective suffix).  "Gaunt" comes from the Old North Norse *gauntr, meaning "lean, starved."
