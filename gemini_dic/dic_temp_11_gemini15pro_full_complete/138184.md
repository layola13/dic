nose-about:/ˈnoʊz əˈbaʊt/| phrasal verb | 探听;  四处打听 | To look around or search for something, often in a curious or inquisitive way. | She was nosing about in the attic, looking for old photographs.  她正在阁楼里四处翻找，寻找旧照片。|  例句：He spent the afternoon nosing about in the bookshop. 他下午在书店里逛来逛去。|近义词：pry, snoop, inquire |反义词: ignore, disregard |词性: 动词短语

Etymology:
"Nose" comes from Old English "nosu," meaning "nose." "About" comes from Old English "onbutan," meaning "around" or "on the outside." The combination "nose about" figuratively uses the image of an animal sniffing around to represent searching or inquiring.
