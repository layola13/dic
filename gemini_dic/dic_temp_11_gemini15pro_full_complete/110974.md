liquidated:/ˈlɪkwɪdeɪtɪd/
adjective
清算的；已清偿的；已偿付的
Converted into cash; paid off.
例句：The company's assets were liquidated to pay off its debts. 该公司的资产被清算以偿还债务。

例句：His liquidated assets amounted to over a million dollars.  他变现的资产超过一百万美元。


近义词：settled, paid off, cleared, dissolved
反义词：outstanding, unpaid, due, owing
词性: adjective

liquidate:/ˈlɪkwɪdeɪt/
verb
清算；清偿；偿付；清除；杀掉；消灭；摧毁；镇压
To eliminate, typically by violent means; to kill; to convert assets into cash.
例句：The company was liquidated after it went bankrupt.  该公司在破产后被清算。

例句：The hitman was hired to liquidate the CEO.  职业杀手被雇来暗杀首席执行官。


近义词：eliminate, abolish, dissolve, kill, destroy
反义词：establish, create, found, build
词性: verb

liquidation:/ˌlɪkwɪˈdeɪʃn/
noun
清算；清偿；偿付；清除；杀害
The process of converting assets into cash, settling debts, or eliminating something.
例句：The company went into liquidation after it failed to make a profit.  该公司在未能盈利后进入清算程序。

例句：The liquidation of the enemy troops was swift and brutal.  对敌军的清除迅速而残酷。


近义词：dissolution, elimination, clearance, killing
反义词：establishment, creation, founding
词性: noun
