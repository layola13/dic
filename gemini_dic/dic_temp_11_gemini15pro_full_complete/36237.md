cognitivism: /ˈkɒɡnɪtɪvɪzəm/ (UK) /ˈkɑːɡnɪtɪvɪzəm/ (US) | n. | 认知主义 | A theoretical framework in psychology that emphasizes the internal mental processes involved in learning and knowing, such as memory, attention, and problem-solving.  It contrasts with behaviorism, which focuses solely on observable behaviors. |  Cognitivism suggests that learners actively construct knowledge rather than passively receiving it. 认知主义认为学习者是主动构建知识，而不是被动地接受知识。|  Modern educational approaches are heavily influenced by cognitivism. 现代教育方法深受认知主义的影响。 | synonyms: mentalism, constructivism | antonyms: behaviorism | noun
