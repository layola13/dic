accommodation:/əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃn/ (UK) /əˌkɑːməˈdeɪʃn/ (US)| n.| 住宿，膳宿；适应；调解；和解；便利；贷款| a place to live, work, stay, etc. in: Hotels are expensive, the price of accommodation is high.|例句：The price of accommodation in this city is very high. 这个城市的住宿费用很高。

例句：They were unable to find suitable accommodation. 他们找不到合适的住处。|近义词：housing, lodging, shelter, quarters|反义词: eviction, displacement|词性: noun

accommodation:/əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃn/ (UK) /əˌkɑːməˈdeɪʃn/ (US)| n.| 适应；调节| the process of adapting or adjusting to someone or something:  accommodation to a new environment|例句：The accommodation of the eye to darkness takes about thirty minutes. 眼睛适应黑暗大约需要30分钟。

例句：She made an accommodation to her new work schedule. 她适应了新的工作时间表。|近义词：adaptation, adjustment|反义词: resistance|词性: noun

accommodation:/əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃn/ (UK) /əˌkɑːməˈdeɪʃn/ (US)| n.| 调解；和解| an agreement or arrangement between people or groups with differing opinions which is acceptable to all:  reach an accommodation with opposing factions|例句：They reached an accommodation with the management. 他们与管理层达成了和解。

例句：The two sides failed to reach an accommodation. 双方未能达成和解。|近义词：compromise, settlement|反义词: disagreement, conflict|词性: noun
