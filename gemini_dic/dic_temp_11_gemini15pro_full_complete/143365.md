outfangthief:/ˈaʊtˌfæŋˌθiːf/
n.|窃贼，尤指被捕获的窃贼|A thief, especially one caught in the act.|"The sheriff apprehended the outfangthief as he tried to escape with the stolen jewels." 警长抓住了那个正试图带着偷来的珠宝逃跑的窃贼。|Synonyms: robber, burglar, bandit|Antonyms: lawman, police officer|Noun

Etymology:
From Old English *ūt-fōn (to seize outside, capture outside one's jurisdiction) + þēof (thief).  "Outfang" referred to the right of a lord to pursue and capture a thief outside his own territory.
