hummock:/ˈhʌmək/ (US), /ˈhʌmək/ (UK)| n. |小丘，土墩，冰丘|A small hill or mound of earth or ice.  A hummock is a rounded knoll or small hill, typically found in a marshy or tundra environment. They are often formed by the movement of ice or by the accumulation of sediment.|例句：The tundra was dotted with hummocks of earth and ice. 冻原上点缀着泥土和冰形成的小丘。

例句：The explorers had to navigate around the hummocks of ice on the glacier. 探险者们不得不绕过冰川上的冰丘。|近义词：knoll, mound, hillock, dune|反义词:hollow, depression|词性:noun
