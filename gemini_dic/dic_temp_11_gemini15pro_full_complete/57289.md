downshifter:/ˈdaʊnˌʃɪftər/| n.|降档者；放慢生活节奏的人|A person who chooses to work less in order to have a less stressful life, even though this usually means earning less money.|Example:  He became a downshifter, trading his high-powered city job for a simpler life in the countryside.  他成为了一名降档者，放弃了高压的城市工作，选择在乡村过上更简单的生活。

Example: Many downshifters find greater fulfillment in their lives despite earning less. 许多降档者即使收入减少，也在生活中找到了更大的满足感。|近义词：simple-lifer, minimalist|反义词:workaholic, careerist|词性:noun

Etymology:  down- + shift + -er.  "Down-" indicates a reduction or lowering, "shift" refers to a change, and "-er" denotes a person who performs the action.
