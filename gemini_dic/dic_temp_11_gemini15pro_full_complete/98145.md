insatiabilities: /ɪnˌseɪʃiəˈbɪlɪtiz/
noun
贪得无厌，不知足
the state of being insatiable; inability to be satisfied
His insatiabilities for power led to his downfall. 他对权力的贪得无厌导致了他的垮台。
Synonyms: greed, avarice, rapacity, voracity
Antonyms: contentment, satisfaction, moderation
n.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin insatiabilitas, from Latin insatiabilis ("insatiable") + -ity.  "Insatiable" comes from in- ("not") + satiabilis ("capable of being satisfied"), from satiare ("to satisfy").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *sa- ("to satisfy").
