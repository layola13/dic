hacky:/ˈhæki/| adj.|蹩脚的；凑合的；缺乏优雅的；投机取巧的|A solution or piece of code that is not elegant or well-designed, but achieves the desired result. Often implemented quickly as a temporary fix.|The code is a bit hacky, but it works. 代码有点蹩脚，但能用。|近义词：clumsy, inelegant, quick and dirty, makeshift|反义词：elegant, refined, well-designed|词性:形容词

Etymology:  Derived from "hack," in the sense of "to cut or chop roughly," later evolving to mean "to program a computer in a quick and careless way."  Ultimately from the Old English *haccian*, "to cut, hew."
