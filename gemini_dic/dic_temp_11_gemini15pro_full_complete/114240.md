mailclad:/ˈmeɪlˌklæd/
adjective
身穿盔甲的;披甲的
covered with mail; wearing armor.
The mailclad knight rode into battle. 身穿盔甲的骑士策马奔腾，投入战斗。
近义词:armored, ironclad
反义词:unarmed, unprotected
词性:adjective

Etymology:
From "mail" (chainmail armor) + "clad" (clothed or covered). "Mail" ultimately comes from Old French "maile," from Latin "macula" (mesh of a net). "Clad" comes from Old English "clad" (clothed).
