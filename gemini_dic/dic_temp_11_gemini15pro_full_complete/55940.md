dizziest:/ˈdɪziɪst/| adj.|最晕眩的，最头晕目眩的|Superlative form of dizzy: most dizzy.  Feeling a sensation of whirling and a tendency to fall.|例句：After spinning around, I felt the dizziest I've ever been.  旋转之后，我感到前所未有的晕眩。

例句：The roller coaster ride left her feeling the dizziest. 过山车让她感到非常晕眩。|近义词：giddiest, lightheadedest, faintest|反义词:steady, stable|词性:adjective

dizzy:/ˈdɪzi/| adj.|晕眩的，头晕目眩的|Having or involving a sensation of spinning around and losing one's balance.|例句：I felt dizzy after standing up too quickly. 我站起来太快了，感到头晕。

例句：The heights made him dizzy. 高度让他感到头晕。|近义词：giddy, lightheaded, faint|反义词:steady, stable|词性:adjective


词源: From Old English *dysig, from *dīsian "to become stupid or foolish," related to Old High German tusic "frantic." The original sense was "foolish, stupid," later shifting to "confused, bewildered," and finally to the current sense of "vertiginous."
