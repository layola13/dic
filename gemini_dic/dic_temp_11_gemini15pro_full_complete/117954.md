meme's:/miːmz/ | n. | 模因；网络爆红事物（meme 的复数形式）| A unit of cultural information spread by imitation.  It can be an idea, a behavior, a style, or a usage that spreads from person to person within a culture. |  The internet is full of funny memes.  互联网上充满了有趣的梗。| synonyms: viral content, internet trend, online phenomenon | antonyms:  None (difficult to have a direct antonym for a cultural unit) | noun


meme's:/miːmz/ | n. | 模因；网络爆红事物（meme 的所有格形式，较少使用）| Belonging to a meme.  While grammatically correct, using the possessive form with "meme" is less common than using "of a meme."  | The meme's popularity surprised everyone. 这个梗的流行让每个人都感到惊讶。| synonyms:  N/A (possessive forms don't typically have synonyms) | antonyms: N/A | noun


Etymology:  The word "meme" was coined by Richard Dawkins in his 1976 book *The Selfish Gene*. He derived it from the Greek word *mimema*, meaning "something imitated."
