felicitously: /fəˈlɪsɪtəsli/ | adv. | 恰当地，贴切地，巧妙地 |  In a way that is suitable and appropriate, showing skill and sensitivity. | She felicitously expressed her gratitude in a heartfelt speech. 她在一场感人至深的演讲中恰如其分地表达了她的感激之情。 |  The author felicitously captured the atmosphere of the era. 作者巧妙地捕捉到了那个时代的氛围。 | aptly, suitably, appropriately, skillfully | inappropriately, awkwardly, clumsily | adverb

felicity: /fəˈlɪsɪti/ | n. | 幸福；恰当；巧妙 |  Intense happiness; the ability to express oneself in a clear and effective way. |  Their marriage was characterized by felicity. 他们的婚姻幸福美满。 | He spoke with great felicity. 他说话措辞非常得体。 | happiness, bliss, appropriateness, aptness | unhappiness, misery, inappropriateness, inaptness | noun

Etymology: From Latin *felicitas*, from felix (“lucky, happy, fruitful”).
