avulsion:/əˈvʌlʃən/ | n. | 脱落; 撕脱; 扯断; |The sudden tearing away of a piece of land by flowing water.  The avulsion of the cliff face caused by the storm created a dangerous overhang. | Example: The avulsion of the tendon from the bone required surgery. 肌腱从骨头上撕脱需要手术。| Synonym: rupture, tear, detachment, separation | Antonym: attachment, connection, union | noun

avulsion:/əˈvʌlʃən/ | n. | (感情的) 骤变，突变 | A sudden and complete change of feeling or affection. The avulsion of his affections from his family after the inheritance dispute was heartbreaking.  | Example: The political avulsion he experienced after the scandal led him to switch parties.  丑闻之后他经历的政治突变使他转了党。 | Synonym: alienation, estrangement, disaffection | Antonym: reconciliation, reunion, rapprochement | noun
