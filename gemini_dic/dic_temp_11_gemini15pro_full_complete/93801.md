immersionist:/ɪˈmɜːrʒənɪst/ | n. | 沉浸式学习者；沉浸主义者 | A person who advocates or practices immersion, especially in language learning, by being surrounded by the target language and culture. |  She's a true immersionist, spending a year in France to perfect her French. 她是一位真正的沉浸式学习者，在法国待了一年以完善她的法语。| synonym:  immersion learner, immersion student | antonym: traditional learner, non-immersion student | noun


immersionist:/ɪˈmɜːrʒənɪst/ | adj. | 沉浸式的 | Relating to or characterized by immersion, especially in language learning. | The school offers an immersionist program in Spanish. 这所学校提供西班牙语沉浸式课程。| synonym: immersive | antonym: traditional, non-immersive | adjective



ETYMOLOGY->
From immersion + -ist.  Immersion comes from Late Latin immersio, from Latin immergere "to plunge into," from in- "in" + mergere "to dip, plunge."
