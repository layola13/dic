accommodableness:/əˌkɒməˈdeɪtəblnəs/ | n. |随和；乐于助人；通融；适应性 |The quality of being accommodating; willingness to adapt or adjust to the needs of others. |Her accommodableness made her a popular colleague. 她的随和让她成为受欢迎的同事。|Synonyms: adaptability, flexibility, agreeableness, compliance |Antonyms: inflexibility, rigidity, stubbornness| noun

accommodating:/əˈkɒmədeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. |乐于助人的；随和的；肯通融的 |Willing to help; obliging; adaptable. |He was very accommodating and offered to drive us to the airport. 他非常乐于助人，主动提出开车送我们去机场。|Synonyms: helpful, obliging, cooperative, kind |Antonyms: uncooperative, unhelpful, inflexible| adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: from accommodate + -able + -ness.  Accommodate itself comes from late Latin accommodat- ‘adapted’, from the verb accommodare, from ad- ‘to’ + commodare ‘make fit’, from commodus ‘suitable’.
