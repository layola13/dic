flakers:/ˈfleɪkərz/| n.| 薄片, 小屑;  (尤指)不靠谱的人, 放鸽子的人 |People who flake out of commitments, especially social ones, are often called flakers.  那些不履行承诺的人，尤其是不参加社交活动的人，通常被称为放鸽子的人。| My friend is such a flaker; he always cancels plans at the last minute. 我的朋友真是个不靠谱的人，他总是最后一刻取消计划。| 近义词: no-shows, welshers | 反义词: reliable people, dependable people | 词性: 名词

flake:/fleɪk/| n. |薄片, 小屑|A small, thin piece of something, typically one that has broken away from a larger piece. 指一小块薄片，通常是从较大的碎片上脱落下来的。|The paint was coming off in flakes. 油漆成片地脱落。|近义词: chip, shaving, sliver | 反义词: lump, chunk | 词性: 名词

flake:/fleɪk/| v. |剥落, 脱落; ( informal ) (尤指在最后一刻) 突然退出, 放鸽子|To come away from a surface in small, thin pieces. 指从表面脱落成小而薄的碎片; To cancel plans, especially at the last minute. 指取消计划，尤其是在最后一刻。|The paint is flaking off the wall. 油漆正从墙上剥落。| He flaked on our dinner plans. 他放了我们晚餐的鸽子。|近义词: peel, chip; cancel, bail | 反义词: adhere, stick; attend, keep | 词性: 动词
