microzyme:/ˈmaɪkroʊzaɪm/| n.|微生物，酶|A microzyme is a microscopic organism, especially one that causes fermentation or disease, or a soluble protein produced by living organisms that acts as a catalyst to bring about a specific biochemical reaction.|Microzymes play a vital role in breaking down organic matter in the soil.  微生物在分解土壤中的有机物方面起着至关重要的作用。

Microzymes are used in some industrial processes.  微生物被用于一些工业过程。|近义词：microbe, enzyme, bacterium|反义词: None|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From micro- + -zyme, from Greek *zȳmē "leaven".
