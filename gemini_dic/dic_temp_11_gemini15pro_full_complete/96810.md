inertia:/ɪˈnɜːʃə/ (US) /ɪˈnɜːʃɪə/ (UK)| n. |惯性,惰性,迟钝|A property of matter by which it continues in its existing state of rest or uniform motion in a straight line, unless that state is changed by an external force.  A tendency to do nothing or to remain unchanged.|例句：The car's inertia kept it moving forward even after the brakes were applied. 汽车的惯性使它即使在刹车后仍继续向前移动。

例句：Because of inertia, the passengers were thrown forward when the bus stopped suddenly. 由于惯性，公共汽车突然停车时，乘客们向前倾倒。|近义词：inactivity, idleness, passiveness, sluggishness|反义词：activity, energy, dynamism|词性:noun
