lawgiver:/ˈlɔːɡɪvər/ | n. | 立法者 |  A person who creates or enacts laws. |  Moses is considered a great lawgiver in Jewish tradition. 摩西在犹太传统中被认为是一位伟大的立法者。

| Example:  The ancient lawgiver established a code of conduct that influenced generations. 古代立法者建立了一套影响了几代人的行为准则。

| Example:  The lawgiver's wisdom and foresight shaped the nation's legal system. 立法者的智慧和远见塑造了国家的法律体系。

| Synonyms: legislator, lawmaker | Antonyms: lawbreaker, anarchist | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *lagu-giefa, from lagu "law" + giefa "giver".  "Law" ultimately comes from Proto-Germanic *lagō "something laid down, that which is fixed or set", and "giver" comes from Proto-Germanic *gebōną "to give".
