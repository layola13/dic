antiempiricists:/ˌæntiɪmˈpɪrɪsɪsts/
|n.|反经验主义者|Those who oppose or are skeptical of empiricism, the theory that all knowledge is derived from sensory experience.|The antiempiricists argued that reason, not experience, is the primary source of knowledge.|近义词:rationalists|反义词:empiricists|词性:名词

Etymology:
anti- (prefix): against, opposed to (from Greek)
empiricist: one who believes in empiricism (from Late Latin empiricus, from Greek empeirikos "experienced")
-ist (suffix): forms agent nouns
