gipsies: /ˈdʒɪpsiz/ | n. | 吉普赛人；流浪者 | A traditionally nomadic ethnic group, believed to have originated in India, known for their rich culture, music, and fortune-telling traditions.  This term is now widely considered offensive due to its association with negative stereotypes and prejudice.  Use Roma or Romani instead. |  The gipsies (Roma) travelled across Europe for centuries.  吉普赛人（罗姆人）在欧洲各地旅行了几个世纪。|  Synonym: Roma, Romani (preferred), travellers (sometimes used) | Antonym: settled people | noun

gipsies: /ˈdʒɪpsiz/ | adj. (dated, offensive) | 吉普赛人的；流浪的 |  Relating to or characteristic of the Roma people, often used in a derogatory way. Again, this term is offensive and should be avoided. |  She admired what she perceived as their gipsies (Roma) lifestyle, free from the constraints of modern society.  她羡慕她所认为的他们吉普赛人（罗姆人）的生活方式，不受现代社会的约束。| Synonym: nomadic, wandering, itinerant (for lifestyle) | Antonym: settled, sedentary | adjective
