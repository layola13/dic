misty-eyed: /ˈmɪsti aɪd/ | adj. | 泪眼朦胧的，感动的 | Having eyes filled with tears or appearing as if about to cry, typically as a result of strong emotion. |  She became misty-eyed as she watched her daughter graduate. 她看着女儿毕业，眼里噙满了泪水。

| Example 1:  He was misty-eyed at the wedding.  他在婚礼上感动得热泪盈眶。

| Example 2:  The old man was misty-eyed as he recalled his childhood.  老人回忆起童年，不禁泪眼朦胧。

| Synonym:  teary, tearful,  | Antonym:  unemotional, unmoved | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY ->

From "misty" (covered with or resembling mist; dim or obscure) + "eyed" (having eyes of a specified kind). "Misty" itself comes from the Old English "mistig," from "mist" (fog).
