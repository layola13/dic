hokeday:/ˈhoʊkdeɪ/ | n. | 嬉戏日，玩笑日 | A day of revelry and practical jokes, traditionally the second Tuesday after Easter. |  In some English villages, Hocktide was celebrated with much revelry and the tradition of "hoking" or stopping passersby to demand money for the church. | 近义词: festival, celebration | 反义词: workday | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Middle English *hokeday, from Old English *hōcdæg ("Hock Day"), probably from *hōc ("hook," used in the festivities), akin to Old High German hāko ("hook").
