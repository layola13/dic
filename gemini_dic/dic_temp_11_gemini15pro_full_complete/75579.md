fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/| n.|老顽固，守旧的人|A person who is old-fashioned and fussy, especially about their appearance or behaviour.|例句：He's such a fuddy-duddy, always complaining about the music young people listen to. 他真是个老顽固，总是抱怨年轻人听的音乐。

例句：My grandmother can be a bit of a fuddy-duddy when it comes to new technology. 我的祖母在新科技方面有点守旧。|近义词：conservative, old-fashioned, traditionalist, stick-in-the-mud, fogey|反义词：modern, progressive, innovator, trendy|词性: noun
