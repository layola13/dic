ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fretan "gnaw, devour," from Proto-Germanic *fretan (source also of Old Frisian freta "gnaw," Old Saxon fretan "devour," Old High German frezzan "devour," German fressen "devour," Dutch vreten "devour," Old Norse freta "devour"), from Proto-Indo-European *gwere- "to swallow."  The meaning shifted from literal gnawing to figurative gnawing by worry or irritation.
