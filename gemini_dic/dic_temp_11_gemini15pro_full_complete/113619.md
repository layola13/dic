machiavellian:/ˌmækiəˈveliən/ | adj. | 马基雅维利的；权谋的；不择手段的 |  Cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics or in advancing one's career.  Derived from Niccolò Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian political philosopher whose book *The Prince* advocated pragmatism and amorality in political leadership. |  His machiavellian tactics ensured that he won the election, even though he lost the popular vote.  他玩弄权术，确保自己赢得选举，即使他失去了普选票数。 | synonyms: cunning, devious, manipulative, unscrupulous, artful, wily | antonyms:  honest, ethical, principled, straightforward, scrupulous | adjective
