feckless: /ˈfɛklɪs/
| adj. | 无能的; 软弱的; 不负责任的 | Lacking initiative or strength of character; irresponsible. |  A feckless youth with no direction in life. 一个没有人生方向的无能青年。 | incompetent, irresponsible, inefficient, shiftless, useless | responsible, competent, efficient, capable | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Scots *feckless, from feck (“effect, value, majority”) + -less.  Feck is from Middle English fek, from Old Norse feikn (“joy, delight, satisfaction”).  The current sense of feckless (“ineffective, worthless”) arose from the original sense (“lacking effect”).
