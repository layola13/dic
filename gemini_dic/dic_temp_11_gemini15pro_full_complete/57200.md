down-the-tube:/ˌdaʊn ðə ˈtuːb/ | adj. | 失败的；完蛋的；报废的 |  Ruined, failing, or no longer working properly. Often used to describe a business or project that has failed.  |  The company went down the tube after the CEO made a series of bad decisions.  这家公司在首席执行官做出了一系列糟糕的决定后就倒闭了。 |  failed, ruined, bankrupt, kaput | successful, thriving | adjective

down-the-tube:/ˌdaʊn ðə ˈtuːb/ | adv. |  朝着失败的方向 |  Towards failure or ruin. |  His career is heading down the tube. 他的职业生涯正走向失败。|  to ruin, to failure | to success | adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
The exact origin of "down the tube" is uncertain, but it's likely related to the image of something being flushed down a drainpipe or tube.  Some speculate it originated in the early 20th century, possibly in reference to pneumatic tubes used to send messages or items, with "down the tube" implying something being discarded or lost.  Another theory connects it to the disposal of torpedoes or other projectiles through tubes on ships or submarines.
