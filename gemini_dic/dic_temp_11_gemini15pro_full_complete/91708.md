hyperconservatively:/ˌhaɪpərˈkənˈsɜːrvətɪvli/ | adv. |极端保守地| In a way that is extremely averse to change or innovation, adhering rigidly to traditional values and practices. | The budget was drafted hyperconservatively, cutting funding for even the most essential programs.  预算起草得极其保守，甚至削减了最基本项目的资金。| The new CEO is managing the company hyperconservatively, refusing to take any risks.  新任首席执行官以极端保守的方式管理公司，拒绝承担任何风险。 |Synonyms: ultraconservatively, rigidly, inflexibly, reactionarily | Antonyms: progressively, liberally, radically, innovatively | adverb

hyperconservatism:/ˌhaɪpərˈkənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/ | n. |极端保守主义| The holding of extremely conservative political views, especially those characterized by a strong adherence to traditional values and practices and a resistance to change. |  Hyperconservatism has been on the rise in recent years, leading to a backlash against progressive policies. 近年来，极端保守主义抬头，导致对进步政策的强烈反对。 | The party's platform reflected a shift towards hyperconservatism, alienating many moderate voters. 该党的纲领反映出一种向极端保守主义的转变，疏远了许多温和派选民。 | Synonyms: ultraconservatism, reactionism, right-wing extremism | Antonyms: progressivism, liberalism, radicalism | noun
