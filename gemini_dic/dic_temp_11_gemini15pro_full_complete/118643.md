messing-up:/ˈmɛsɪŋ ʌp/ | phrasal verb | 搞砸，弄乱 | The act of making something untidy, disorganized, or spoiled.  It can also refer to making a mistake or causing a problem. |  I'm messing up my room. 我把我的房间弄乱了。

He messed up the project by missing the deadline.  他错过了截止日期，把项目搞砸了。 | synonyms: spoiling, ruining, botching, bungling, muddling | antonyms: organizing, tidying, fixing, improving | verb phrase


mess-up:/ˈmɛs ʌp/ | n. | 混乱，错误 | A situation that is disorganized, untidy, or spoiled, often due to a mistake or problem. | The party was a complete mess-up. 派对完全搞砸了。

There was a mess-up with the orders and no one got what they asked for. 订单出了问题，没有人拿到他们点的东西。| synonyms:  muddle, shambles, disaster, fiasco | antonyms: order, success, triumph | noun
