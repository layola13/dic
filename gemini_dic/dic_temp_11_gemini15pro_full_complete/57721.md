dreadfulnesses:/ˈdrɛdfʊlnəsɪz/
|n.|可怕，恐怖，令人不快的事情|The dreadfulnesses of war are etched in the memories of survivors. 战争的恐怖深深地印刻在幸存者的记忆中。|The sheer dreadfulnesses of the situation overwhelmed her.  形势的可怕程度让她不知所措。|近义词: horrors, terrors, atrocities|反义词: pleasantries, delights|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From dreadful + -ness + -es.  "Dreadful" comes from the Old English word "drædfull," meaning "full of dread."  The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality. The suffix "-es" creates the plural form.
