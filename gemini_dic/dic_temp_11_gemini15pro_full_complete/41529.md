coronated:/ˈkɔːrəneɪtɪd/ | adj. | 加冕的，戴冠的 | Having been formally invested with regal power and dignity by being crowned |  King Charles III was coronated in Westminster Abbey. 查尔斯三世国王在威斯敏斯特教堂加冕。| synonyms: crowned, enthroned, inaugurated | antonyms: dethroned, unseated | adjective

The king is coronated in a grand ceremony. 国王在一个盛大的仪式上加冕。
He is coronated as the new ruler. 他被加冕为新的统治者。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
He has been coronated as the new king. 他已被加冕为新国王。
She has been coronated twice in her life. 她一生中被加冕了两次。

现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
The ceremony has been going on for hours, and the king has been being coronated.  仪式已经进行了几个小时，国王一直在被加冕。 (This tense is awkward and rarely used in this context.  It's better to use the simple present perfect.)
