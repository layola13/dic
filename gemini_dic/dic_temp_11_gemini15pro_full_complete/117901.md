melos: /ˈmiːlɒs/ (UK), /ˈmeɪlɒs/ (US)| n. |旋律，曲调，音乐|A tune or melody, especially in ancient Greek music.  A general term for music, or a specific melodic form. It refers to the melodic aspect of music, encompassing pitch, rhythm, and other musical elements that contribute to the overall musical flow. |The melos of the ancient Greek hymn was hauntingly beautiful. 古希腊圣歌的旋律优美动听。|Synonyms: tune, melody, air, strain, theme |Antonyms: dissonance, cacophony |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek μέλος (mélos) meaning "limb, member, song, strain, melody".
