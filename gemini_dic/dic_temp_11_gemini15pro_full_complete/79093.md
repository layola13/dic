glacier:/ˈɡleɪʃər/ (US) /ˈɡlæsiə(r)/ (UK)| n. |冰川；冰河|A slowly moving mass or river of ice formed by the accumulation and compaction of snow on mountains or near the poles.|Scientists study glaciers to understand climate change. 科学家研究冰川以了解气候变化。|Synonyms: ice stream, ice flow|Antonyms: volcano, magma|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *glacier*, from Franco-Provençal *glaciēr*, from Vulgar Latin *glaciārium*, from Latin *glacies* ("ice").
