companionhood:/kəmˈpænjənˌhʊd/
|n.|陪伴；友谊；同伴关系|The state of being a companion; fellowship.|She valued the companionhood of her close friends. 她珍视与密友之间的友谊。|近义词：companionship, fellowship, camaraderie|反义词：isolation, loneliness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From companion + -hood.  "Companion" comes from Old French "compagnon" meaning "one who shares bread," from Late Latin "companionem" (nominative companio), from "com-" (together) + "panis" (bread).  The suffix "-hood" forms nouns denoting state, condition, quality, or character.
