kowtow:/ˌkaʊˈtaʊ/ | v. | 叩头，磕头；卑躬屈膝，唯命是从 | To act in an excessively subservient manner.  To show excessive deference or respect, often by bowing deeply. |  The officials kowtowed to the emperor. 官员们向皇帝叩头。 He refused to kowtow to the new management. 他拒绝向新管理层卑躬屈膝。 | synonyms: grovel, fawn,  prostrate oneself,  bow down,  defer | antonyms: defy, resist, challenge | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Chinese 磕頭 (kētóu), from 磕 (kē, “to knock”) + 頭 (tóu, “head”).
