hexapla:/ˈhɛksəplə/| n.|六联版圣经|A manuscript of the Bible with six versions of the Old Testament written in parallel columns (usually the Hebrew text, a Greek transliteration of the Hebrew, and four Greek versions – the Septuagint, Aquila, Symmachus, and Theodotion).|Origen’s Hexapla was a monumental work of biblical scholarship. 奥利金的六联版圣经是一部不朽的圣经学术著作。|Synonym: Polyglot Bible|Antonym: None|noun
