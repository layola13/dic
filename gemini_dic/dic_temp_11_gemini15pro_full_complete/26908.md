calamar:/kæləˈmɑːr/ | n. | 鱿鱼 | A cephalopod of the order Teuthida, having a long tapered body with ten arms, eight short and two long tentacles, typically able to squirt ink.|  I ordered calamari at the restaurant. 我在餐厅点了鱿鱼。

calamar:/kæləˈmɑːr/ | n. | 乌贼 |  A cephalopod of the order Sepiida, having a rounded body with ten arms, eight short and two long tentacles, and a hard internal shell called a cuttlebone, typically able to squirt ink.| The calamar quickly changed color to blend in with its surroundings. 乌贼迅速变色以融入周围环境。


Etymology: From Spanish *calamar*, from Catalan *calamar*, from Late Latin *calamarium* (“ink pot, inkwell”), from Latin *calamus* (“reed, pen”).  The association is with the ink sac of the squid.

Synonyms: squid, cuttlefish, cephalopod
