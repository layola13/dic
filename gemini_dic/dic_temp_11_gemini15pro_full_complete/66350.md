excursionist:/ɪkˈskɜːrʒənɪst/| n. | 旅行者；远足者；短途旅行者 | A person who goes on an excursion, especially a short journey made for pleasure. |  Example 1: The excursionists were delighted with the scenic views. 旅行者们对美丽的景色感到欣喜。

Example 2:  The boat was filled with excursionists heading to the island. 船上挤满了前往岛屿的短途旅行者。 | Synonyms:  tourist, traveler, sightseer, tripper, day-tripper | Antonyms: resident, local, native | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the late 18th century, from French *excursionniste*, from excursion + -ist. Excursion itself comes from Late Latin excursio, from Latin excurrere "to run out," from ex- "out" + currere "to run."
