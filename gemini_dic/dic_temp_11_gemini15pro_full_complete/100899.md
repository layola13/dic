intuitionism:/ɪnˌtuːˈɪʃənɪzəm/ | n. | 直觉主义 | The theory that primary truths and principles (especially those of ethics and metaphysics) are known directly by intuition. |  Intuitionism suggests that moral truths are self-evident and don't require proof. 直觉主义认为道德真理是不言自明的，不需要证明。

Intuitionism in mathematics rejects the law of excluded middle.  数学中的直觉主义拒绝排中律。 | 近义词：instinct, hunch | 反义词：rationalism, empiricism | 词性: noun
