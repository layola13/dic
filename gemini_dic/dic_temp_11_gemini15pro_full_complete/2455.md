adjurers:/əˈdʒʊərərz/
n.|恳求者；告诫者；施法者|People who earnestly urge or command someone to do something, often under oath or in a solemn manner; also refers to those who cast spells.|The adjurers begged the king to show mercy.  恳求者们乞求国王开恩。|Synonyms:  supplicants, implorers, exhorters, conjurers|Antonyms:  demanders, commanders, discouragers|noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *adjouren*, from Old French *adjurer*, from Latin *adjūrāre* (“to swear to”), from *ad-* (“to”) + *jūrāre* (“to swear”). The Latin *jūrāre* is related to *jūs* (“law, right”).
