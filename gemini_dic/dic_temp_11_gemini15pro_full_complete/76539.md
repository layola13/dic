gallanting: /ɡəˈlæntɪŋ/ | n. |献殷勤；护送；|The act of escorting someone, especially a woman, in a courteous and attentive manner.  |He spent the evening gallanting the young lady around town. 他整晚都在城里护送那位年轻女士。| 近义词: courting, escorting, wooing | 反义词: neglecting, ignoring | 词性: noun (gerund or present participle)

gallant: /ˈɡælənt/ | adj. | 英勇的；献殷勤的；堂皇的；|Brave, chivalrous, or showing courtesy and attention to women; stately and impressive. |He made a gallant attempt to rescue the child from the burning building. 他英勇地试图从燃烧的建筑物中救出孩子。  She was escorted by a gallant young man. 她由一位殷勤的年轻男士护送。 | 近义词: brave, courageous, chivalrous, courteous | 反义词: cowardly, craven, rude, discourteous | 词性: adjective

gallant: /ˈɡælənt/ | n. | 殷勤的男人；时髦男士；|A man who is courteous and attentive to women; a dashing man. |He was a true gallant, always opening doors for ladies. 他是一位真正的绅士，总是为女士们开门。 | 近义词: suitor, admirer, beau | 反义词: boor, churl | 词性: noun

gallant: /ɡəˈlænt/ | v. | 献殷勤；护送；|To escort or attend (someone) in a courteous and attentive manner. |He gallanted her to the opera. 他护送她去歌剧院。 | 近义词: escort, court, woo | 反义词: neglect, ignore | 词性: verb

词源: From Old French *galant, present participle of galer "to make merry, rejoice," probably of Germanic origin.
