displeasureably:/dɪsˈplɛʒərəbli/ | adv. | 不愉快地，令人不快地 |  In a way that causes or shows displeasure; unpleasantly. |  He coughed displeasureably when I mentioned the money. 当我提到钱的时候，他很不高兴地咳嗽了一声。|  unpleasantly, disagreeably, distastefully | pleasantly, agreeably, enjoyably | adverb

Etymology:
From displeasure + -ably. Displeasure comes from the Old French desplaisir, from des- (expressing negation) + plaisir (pleasure).  -ably is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives, meaning "in a ... manner" or "capable of being ...ed".
