geothermometer:/ˌdʒiːoʊθərˈmɒmɪtər/ | n. |地温计；地质温度计|An instrument or method used to determine the temperature at which geological formations were formed.  It uses the physical properties of minerals or the isotopic composition of elements in rocks or fluids to infer past temperatures.|Geologists used a geothermometer to estimate the temperature of the ancient magma chamber. 地质学家使用地温计来估计古代岩浆房的温度。|Synonyms: temperature indicator, thermal indicator|Antonyms: N/A|noun
