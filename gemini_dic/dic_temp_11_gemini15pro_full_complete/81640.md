greedsome:/ˈɡriːdsəm/| adj.|贪婪的，贪得无厌的|Excessively greedy; avaricious.  Having or showing a strong desire for more wealth, possessions, or power than is needed or deserved.|He was a greedsome landlord, always raising the rent.|Synonyms: avaricious, covetous, grasping, rapacious|Antonyms: generous, selfless, altruistic|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From greed + -some.  Greed comes from Old English grǣd "greed," from Proto-Germanic *grēdiz (source also of Old Saxon grēdag, Old Norse grāðr "greedy," Dutch gretig "eager, greedy," German gierig "greedy"), from PIE root *gher- (2) "to desire, like."  The suffix -some forms adjectives meaning "characterized by" or "given to."
