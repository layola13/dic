amphid:/ˈæmfɪd/| n. |感器，化学感受器|A sensory organ, especially one of a pair of anterior, chemoreceptive organs in certain nematodes.|The nematode uses its amphids to detect chemicals in its environment.|Near synonyms:  chemoreceptor, sensory organ, receptor|Antonym:  effector|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἀμφί (amphí, “on both sides, around”) +  ἰδέα (idéa, “form, appearance”).
