descriptiveness:/dɪˈskrɪptɪvnəs/ | n. | 描述性；描绘性；描写力 | The descriptiveness of the passage brought the scene to life. 这段文字的描述性使场景栩栩如生。 |  例句：The painting lacked descriptiveness, leaving the viewer unmoved. 这幅画缺乏描述性，无法打动观众。

例句：The author's descriptiveness is evident in her vivid portrayal of the characters. 作者的描写力在她对人物生动的刻画中显而易见。 | 近义词：vividness, detail, richness | 反义词：vagueness, ambiguity, obscurity | 词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From descriptive + -ness.  Descriptive comes from the Late Latin *descriptivus, from Latin describere "to write down, copy, portray," from de- "down" + scribere "to write."
