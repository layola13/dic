blowflies:/ˈbloʊflaɪz/| n. |绿头苍蝇，丽蝇|A type of fly, typically metallic green or blue, that is attracted to decaying flesh.  They are important in forensic science for estimating time of death.|例句：The blowflies were buzzing around the carcass. 绿头苍蝇在尸体周围嗡嗡作响。

例句：Forensic entomologists study blowflies to determine time of death. 法医昆虫学家研究绿头苍蝇来确定死亡时间。|近义词：carrion flies, bottle flies|反义词:N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From blow (verb) + fly (noun).  "Blow" in this context refers to the deposition of eggs, as in "the fly blows its eggs."  This sense of "blow" is related to the Old English blāwan, meaning "to blow, breathe, inflate."
