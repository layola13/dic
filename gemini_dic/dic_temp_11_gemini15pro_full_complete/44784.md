crunched:/krʌntʃt/| adj.|嘎吱作响的，被压碎的，紧张的|Crushed or squeezed, making a crackling sound; also used to describe a situation of intense pressure or stress.|The car's fender was crunched in the accident. 汽车的挡泥板在事故中被撞扁了。|近义词：crushed, compressed, strained, tense|反义词:smooth, relaxed, expanded|词性:形容词

crunched:/krʌntʃt/| v.|嘎吱作响地咀嚼，压碎，处理大量数据|Past tense and past participle of "crunch," meaning to crush or squeeze, making a crackling sound; also, to process large amounts of data.|He crunched the leaves under his feet. 他嘎吱嘎吱地踩着树叶。|近义词：crush, grind, process, compute|反义词: expand, smooth|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the imitative verb "crunch," likely originating in the late 16th century.  It is related to similar onomatopoeic words in other Germanic languages.
