contracting:/kənˈtræktɪŋ/ /kənˈtræktɪŋ/| adj. v.|收缩的，缩小的; 缔结; 感染;|Becoming smaller or shorter; entering into a formal agreement; becoming infected with a disease.|The contracting muscle caused the limb to move. 收缩的肌肉使肢体移动。|Synonyms: shrinking, reducing, compressing|Antonyms: expanding, enlarging, stretching|verb/adjective

contracting:/kənˈtræktɪŋ/ /kənˈtræktɪŋ/| n.|收缩，订约;|The process of becoming smaller or shorter; the act of entering into a formal agreement.|The contracting of the market led to job losses.  市场的萎缩导致了失业。|Synonyms: reduction, shrinkage, compression|Antonyms: expansion, enlargement, stretching|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *contracten*, from Latin *contractus*, past participle of *contrahere* (“to draw together, conclude a bargain”), from *com-* (“together”) + *trahere* (“to draw, pull”).
