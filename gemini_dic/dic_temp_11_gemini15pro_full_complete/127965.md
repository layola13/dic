neostriatum:/ˌniːoʊstraɪˈeɪtəm/| n. |新纹状体|A subcortical part of the forebrain, part of the basal ganglia system, involved in motor control, cognition, and reward.  It's composed of the caudate nucleus and the putamen.|The neostriatum plays a crucial role in motor learning.|Synonym: striatum (often used interchangeably but technically broader)|Antonym:  (No direct anatomical antonym)|noun
