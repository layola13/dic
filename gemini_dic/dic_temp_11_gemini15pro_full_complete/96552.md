indorse:/ɪnˈdɔːrs/ /ɪnˈdɔːrs/ | vt. | 背书；认可；签署 | To sign one's name on the back of a check or other document to make it payable to someone else; to give one's public approval or support to someone or something. |  He indorsed the check and handed it to the cashier. 他在支票背面签了名，然后把它交给了出纳员。

indorse:/ɪnˈdɔːrs/ /ɪnˈdɔːrs/ | vt. | 背书；认可；签署 | To sign one's name on the back of a check or other document to make it payable to someone else; to give one's public approval or support to someone or something. | The newspaper indorsed the mayor's re-election campaign. 这家报纸支持市长的连任竞选。


近义词: support, approve, sanction, endorse | 反义词: oppose, reject, disapprove | 词性: 动词

词源: From Anglo-French endosser, Old French endosser "to put on one's back," from en- "on" + dos "back," from Latin dorsum.  The spelling "indorse" is less common today, with "endorse" being preferred.
