conventionalizes:/kənˈvɛnʃənəˌlaɪzɪz/
|v.|使习俗化，使常规化|To make something conventional; to cause something to conform to established conventions or practices.|The company conventionalized its hiring process to ensure fairness.|近义词: standardize, normalize, formalize|反义词: revolutionize, innovate, transform|词性:动词

Etymology:
From conventional + -ize.  Conventional comes from the Late Latin *conventionālis, from conventiō (“agreement, covenant”), from convenīre (“to come together”), from com- (“together”) + venīre (“to come”).  The suffix -ize comes from the Greek suffix -ίζειν (-izein), used to form verbs.
