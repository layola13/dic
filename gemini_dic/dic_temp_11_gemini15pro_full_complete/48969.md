degenerate:/dɪˈdʒɛnəreɪt/ /dɪˈdʒenərət/ | v. | 退化，堕落 | To decline or deteriorate from a higher or better form or state, physically, mentally, or morally. |  例句：His eyesight began to degenerate with age. 他的视力随着年龄开始退化。

例句：The political debate degenerated into a shouting match.  政治辩论沦为了一场叫喊比赛。 | 近义词：deteriorate, decline, worsen, decay | 反义词：improve, progress, develop | 词性：动词


degenerate:/dɪˈdʒɛnərət/ /dɪˈdʒenərət/ | adj. | 退化的，堕落的 | Having declined or deteriorated from a higher or better form or state. | 例句：The building was in a degenerate state. 这栋建筑处于破败的状态。

例句：He considered the music to be degenerate and offensive. 他认为这种音乐是堕落的，令人反感的。 | 近义词：debased, corrupt, decadent, degraded | 反义词：noble, virtuous, upright, wholesome | 词性：形容词


degenerate:/dɪˈdʒɛnərət/ /dɪˈdʒenərət/| n. | 堕落者 | A person who has declined, especially morally. | 例句：He was seen as a degenerate by his peers. 他被同龄人视为堕落者。

例句：The novel portrays a group of degenerates living in a crumbling mansion. 这部小说描绘了一群堕落者住在一座破败的豪宅里。 | 近义词：reprobate, deviant, miscreant | 反义词：saint, paragon | 词性：名词
