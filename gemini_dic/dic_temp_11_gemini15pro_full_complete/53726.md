discodactyl: /ˈdɪskəʊˌdæktɪl/ (UK), /ˈdɪskoʊˌdæktəl/ (US) | adj. | 盘状指的，盘指的 | Having disc-shaped pads at the tips of the fingers and toes, typically for clinging or adhesion.  This term is often used in describing certain amphibian species, particularly tree frogs. | Example 1: The discodactyl frog easily climbed the smooth surface of the leaf. 盘状指的青蛙很容易爬上光滑的叶子表面。

Example 2: Discodactyl adaptations allow these frogs to thrive in arboreal habitats. 盘状指的适应性使这些青蛙能够在树栖环境中茁壮成长。 | Synonym: adhesive-toed, suction-toed | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but consider "non-adhesive" or "claw-toed") | Word Type: adjective
