languish:/ˈlæŋɡwɪʃ/| vi.|衰弱；萎靡；失去活力；渴望；苦思|To lose or lack vitality; to grow weak or feeble.  To exist in an unpleasant or unwanted situation, often for a long time.|The flowers languished in the hot sun.  这些花在烈日下枯萎了。|Synonyms: weaken, deteriorate, fade, pine, yearn|Antonyms: thrive, flourish, prosper|Verb

languishment:/ˈlæŋɡwɪʃmənt/| n.|衰弱；萎靡；憔悴|The state of languishing; loss of vitality.|Her languishment was evident in her pale face and listless manner.  她脸色苍白、无精打采，明显很虚弱。|Synonyms: decline, weakness, feebleness|Antonyms: vigor, strength, health|Noun
