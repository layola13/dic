alumnus:/əˈlʌmnəs/| n. |男校友|A male graduate or former student of a particular school, college, or university.|例句：He is an alumnus of Harvard University. 他是哈佛大学的校友。|例句：The university invited several distinguished alumni to speak at the graduation ceremony. 大学邀请了几位杰出的校友在毕业典礼上发言。|近义词：graduate|反义词:current student|词性:noun

alumna:/əˈlʌmnə/| n. |女校友|A female graduate or former student of a particular school, college, or university.|例句：She is an alumna of Yale University. 她是耶鲁大学的校友。|例句：The alumnae of the college established a scholarship fund. 这所学院的女校友设立了一个奖学金基金。|近义词：graduate|反义词:current student|词性:noun

alumni:/əˈlʌmnaɪ/| n. |校友（复数，男女皆可）|Graduates or former students of a particular school, college, or university.|例句：The alumni association organizes annual reunions. 校友会组织一年一度的聚会。|例句：The university relies on its alumni for donations. 这所大学依靠其校友的捐款。|近义词：graduates|反义词:current students|词性:noun

alumnist:/əˈlʌmnaɪst/| n. |校友（男女皆可，较少用）|A graduate or former student of a particular school, college, or university.  While technically referring to a male or mixed-gender group, "alumnus" and "alumni" are generally preferred for male and mixed groups, and "alumna" and "alumnae" for female groups. However, "alumnist" can sometimes be used as a gender-neutral singular, although "alum" is more common in this context.|例句：The alumnist returned to their alma mater to give a guest lecture.  这位校友回到母校做客座讲座。|例句：The school maintains a database of all its alumnists. 学校维护着一个所有校友的数据库。|近义词：graduate, alum|反义词:current student|词性:noun
