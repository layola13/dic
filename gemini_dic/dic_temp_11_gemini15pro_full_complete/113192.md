luridly:/ˈlʊrɪdli/ | adv. | 可怕地，耸人听闻地，| in a lurid manner; sensationally; shockingly.  Describing something presented or told in a way that emphasizes shocking or gruesome details, often to excite or horrify. | The tabloids luridly described the details of the crime.  小报耸人听闻地描述了犯罪细节。| synonyms: gruesomely, shockingly, sensationally, vividly, graphically | antonyms: tastefully, discreetly, subtly, plainly | adverb

Etymology: From the adjective "lurid," which comes from the Latin word "luridus," meaning "pale yellow, ghastly." The sense of "shocking" or "sensational" developed later.
