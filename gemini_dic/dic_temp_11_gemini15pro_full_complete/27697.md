candlewicks:/ˈkændəlˌwɪks/| n. |蜡烛芯|The braided strands of cotton or other fibrous material that draw molten wax up into the flame of a candle.|The candlewicks were too short and the candle wouldn't stay lit.|wick, wicks| |noun
Etymology: From Middle English *candelweke*, equivalent to candle + wick.
