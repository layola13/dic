inquiline:/ˈɪŋkwɪlaɪn/| n.|寄居生物，房客|An animal that lives commensally in the nest, burrow, or dwelling place of another animal.  An inquiline is an organism that lives in the dwelling of another organism.|The bird acted as an inquiline in the squirrel's nest.|Synonyms: lodger, tenant, resident, dweller|Antonyms: host, landlord|noun

inquiline:/ˈɪŋkwɪlaɪn/| adj.|寄居的|Living commensally in the nest, burrow, or dwelling place of another animal.|The inquiline insect caused no harm to its host.|Synonyms: commensal, symbiotic|Antonyms: parasitic, predatory|adjective
