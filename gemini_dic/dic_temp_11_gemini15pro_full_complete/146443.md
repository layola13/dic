overfranchised: /ˌoʊvərˈfrænˌtʃaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度特许经营的 | Having too many franchises, especially in a particular area, leading to increased competition and potentially reduced profitability for each franchisee.  This often saturates the market and can make it difficult for individual franchisees to succeed. | The market for frozen yogurt became overfranchised, leading to the closure of several stores.  冷冻酸奶市场过度特许经营，导致几家门店倒闭。| The burger chain is overfranchised in this city; there's one on every corner. 这座城市的汉堡连锁店过度特许经营；每个街角都有一家。 | oversaturated, oversupplied | undersupplied, lacking competition | adjective
