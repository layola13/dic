bifurcate: /ˈbaɪfərkeɪt/ (US) /ˈbaɪfəkeɪt/ (UK)
| v. | 分叉，分成两支 | To divide into two branches or forks. | The river bifurcates into two smaller streams a few miles downstream.  这条河在下游几英里处分岔成两条小溪。| synonyms: divide, fork, split, branch | antonyms: merge, join, unite | verb
bifurcate: /ˈbaɪfərkeɪt/ (US) /ˈbaɪfəkeɪt/ (UK)
| adj. | 分叉的 | Divided into two branches or forks. | The bifurcate stem of the plant was unusual.  这种植物分叉的茎很不寻常。 | synonyms: forked, branched, divided | antonyms: unified, single, merged | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin bifurcatus, past participle of bifurcare (“to divide into two forks”), from bi- (“two”) + furca (“fork”).
