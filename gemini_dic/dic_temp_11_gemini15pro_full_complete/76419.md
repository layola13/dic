gags:/ɡæɡz/ /ɡæɡ/| n. v. |笑话，堵嘴物，窒息|A joke or funny story, something put in or over someone's mouth to prevent them from speaking, or the act of choking or retching.|例句：The comedian's gags had the audience in stitches.  喜剧演员的笑话让观众捧腹大笑。

He stuffed a gag in her mouth to stop her screaming. 他用堵嘴物塞住她的嘴，阻止她尖叫。

|近义词：jokes, pranks, quips, muzzles, silences|反义词:seriousness, facts, truth|词性:名词, 动词

gags:/ɡæɡz/ /ɡæɡ/| v. |使窒息，塞住，抑制|To choke or retch, to silence someone by putting something in their mouth, or to suppress something.|例句：The smoke gagged me. 烟呛得我喘不过气来。

The government tried to gag the press. 政府试图压制新闻界。

|近义词：choke, stifle, suppress, muzzle, silence|反义词:express, encourage, free|词性:动词
