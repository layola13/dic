overbookishly:/ˌoʊvərˈbʊkɪʃli/ | adv. | 过于书呆子气地，过于学究式地 | In a manner that is excessively concerned with books and academic learning, often to the point of being impractical or pedantic. | He spoke overbookishly, using jargon that no one understood. 他说话过于学究，使用没人能懂的行话。|  He analyzed the situation overbookishly, missing the practical implications. 他过于书呆子气地分析了情况，忽略了实际意义。|近义词: pedantically, academically, scholastically |反义词: practically, realistically, pragmatically |词性:副词

Etymology:
"over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "bookish" (adjective meaning "given to reading; fond of books") + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  The word "bookish" itself comes from "book" + "-ish" (suffix forming adjectives).
