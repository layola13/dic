fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/| n.|老顽固，守旧的人|A person who is old-fashioned and fussy, unwilling to accept new ideas or ways of doing things.|例句：My grandfather is a bit of a fuddy-duddy when it comes to technology. 我祖父对科技有点老顽固。

例句：She dismissed them as a bunch of fuddy-duddies. 她把他们斥为一群老顽固。|近义词：conservative, traditionalist, diehard, stick-in-the-mud|反义词：modernist, progressive, innovator|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain. Possibly imitative of fussy or disapproving mutterings. First recorded in the early 20th century.
