intervolution:/ˌɪntərˈvəˈluːʃən/ | n. |内卷，内卷化|The inward curvature or rolling of an edge or surface.  A situation where individuals within a group are pressured to compete intensely with each other, often to the detriment of the group as a whole. |例句：The intervolution in the tech industry leads to burnout among employees. 科技行业的内卷导致员工倦怠。

例句：The intense intervolution in the education system puts immense pressure on students. 教育系统的激烈内卷给学生带来了巨大的压力。|近义词：inward spiral, rat race, cutthroat competition, escalation, internal competition |反义词:cooperation, collaboration, synergy |词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *intervolvere, from inter- (“between, among, mutually, reciprocally, together, during”) + volvere (“to roll”).  The biological sense referring to the rolling or turning inward of an edge or surface dates to the 17th century. The sociological/economic sense referring to excessive internal competition emerged in the late 20th/early 21st century, particularly in China.
