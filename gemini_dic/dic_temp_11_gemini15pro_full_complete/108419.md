lawns: /lɔːnz/ | n. | 草坪；草地 | An area of short, mown grass, especially around a house or in a park. |  The children were playing on the lawns. 孩子们在草坪上玩耍。|  grassy areas, yards, greens |  wastelands, barren areas | noun

Etymology: From Old French *launde (“heath, moor”), from Frankish *landa (“uncultivated land”), from Proto-Germanic *landą (“land”), from Proto-Indo-European *lendʰ- (“land, heath”). Cognate with Dutch land (“land”), German Land (“land”), English land. The plural form arose by analogy with words like "stones" and "bones."
