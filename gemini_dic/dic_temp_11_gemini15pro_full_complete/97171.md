infirmed:/ɪnˈfɜːrmd/
adjective
衰弱的，病弱的
Weakened by age or illness.
例句：His infirmed grandmother needed constant care. 他体弱多病的祖母需要持续的照顾。

例句：The infirmed patient was unable to walk without assistance.  这位病弱的病人无法在没有帮助的情况下行走。

近义词：weak, frail, feeble, debilitated
反义词：strong, healthy, robust
词性: adjective

infirm:/ɪnˈfɜːrm/
verb
使衰弱，使虚弱
To weaken or make feeble.  (Often used in the past participle form "infirmed")
例句：Long periods of inactivity can infirm the body. 长时间不活动会使身体衰弱。

例句：She was infirmed by a chronic illness. 她被一种慢性病折磨得身体虚弱。

近义词：weaken, debilitate
反义词：strengthen, invigorate
词性: verb

infirmity:/ɪnˈfɜːrməti/
noun
虚弱，病弱
Physical or mental weakness.
例句：Old age is often accompanied by infirmity. 老年往往伴随着体弱。

例句：He suffered from a variety of infirmities. 他患有多种疾病。

近义词：weakness, frailty, feebleness, debility
反义词：strength, health, robustness
词性: noun


词源:
From Old French *enfermer, from Latin *infermare "to make ill, weaken," from in- "in" + firmus "strong."  The meaning shifted from "to make ill" to "weakened by illness."
