at-risk:/æt ˈrɪsk/ | adj. | 处于危险中的，有风险的 |  Exposed to or likely to experience danger, harm, or loss.  It often refers to individuals or groups facing a greater than average probability of negative outcomes, such as academic failure, health problems, or social difficulties. |  Example: The program provides support services for at-risk youth.  这个项目为处于危险中的年轻人提供支持服务。 | Synonyms: endangered, vulnerable, imperiled, threatened, jeopardized | Antonyms: safe, secure, protected | adjective


at risk:/æt ˈrɪsk/ | adj. | 处于危险中的，有风险的 | Exposed to or likely to experience danger, harm, or loss. ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrase "at risk," meaning literally "at the state of being in danger." "Risk" originates from Old Italian "risco" and Old French "risque," possibly influenced by Arabic "rizq" (provision, lot).
