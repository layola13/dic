overoptimists:/ˌoʊvərˈɑːptɪmɪsts/ | n. | 过度乐观主义者 | People who are excessively optimistic, often to the point of unrealistic expectations.  They tend to overestimate the likelihood of positive outcomes and underestimate the potential for negative consequences. |  The overoptimists predicted a swift victory, ignoring the warnings of experienced analysts. 过度乐观主义者预测会迅速取得胜利，而忽略了经验丰富的分析师的警告。

The overoptimists continued investing heavily even as the market showed signs of decline.  即使市场出现衰退迹象，过度乐观主义者仍继续大量投资。 | 近义词: Pollyannas, utopians | 反义词: pessimists, realists | 词性: 名词

overoptimistic:/ˌoʊvərˌɑːptɪˈmɪstɪk/ | adj. | 过度乐观的 | Excessively optimistic; unrealistically positive. | He had an overoptimistic view of the company's future. 他对公司的未来过于乐观。

She was overoptimistic about her chances of winning the lottery. 她对赢得彩票的机会过于乐观。 | 近义词: excessively optimistic, unrealistically positive | 反义词: pessimistic, realistic | 词性: 形容词

词源: over- (prefix meaning "excessive") + optimistic.  "Over-" comes from Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond." "Optimistic" comes from Late Latin "optimus," meaning "best."
