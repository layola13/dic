hypervariabilities:/ˌhaɪpərˌvɛəriəˈbɪlɪtiz/ | n. | 高变异性，高度变异性 |  Regions of antibody molecules (specifically the complementarity-determining regions or CDRs) that are extremely variable in amino acid sequence. This variability allows for the recognition of a vast array of antigens. | The hypervariabilities of the antibody allowed it to bind specifically to the novel virus.  抗体的超变区使其能够特异性地结合新的病毒。 | synonyms: hypervariable regions, CDRs | antonyms: conserved regions | noun

hypervariability:/ˌhaɪpərˌvɛəriəˈbɪlɪti/ | n. | 高变异性，高度变异性 | The state or quality of being hypervariable; exhibiting a high degree of variability. |  The hypervariability of the virus made it difficult to develop a vaccine. 病毒的高度变异性使其难以开发疫苗。 | synonyms: high variability, extreme variability | antonyms: low variability, conservation | noun
