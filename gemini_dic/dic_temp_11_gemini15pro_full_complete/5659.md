altocumuli:/ˌæltoʊˈkjuːmjʊlaɪ/ | n. |高积云|A type of middle-altitude cloud characterized by a layer or patches of white or gray, often rounded masses or rolls.  They are typically found between 6,500 and 20,000 feet (2,000 and 6,000 meters).|The altocumuli clouds hinted at possible rain later in the day. 高积云暗示当天晚些时候可能会下雨。| Altocumuli lenticularis clouds look like flying saucers.  荚状高积云看起来像飞碟。|Near synonyms:  stratocumulus, altostratus|Opposite: clear sky|Noun
