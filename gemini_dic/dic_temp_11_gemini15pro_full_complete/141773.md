orator: /ˈɔːrətər/ (US) /ˈɒrətə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 演讲者，雄辩家|A skilled and eloquent public speaker, especially one who delivers formal speeches on important occasions.|  The orator captivated the audience with his powerful words.  这位演说家以其强有力的言辞吸引了观众。|Synonyms: speaker, lecturer, rhetorician, public speaker|Antonyms: listener, audience, silent person|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *ōrātor*, from *ōrāre* "to speak, plead, pray,"  from *ōs* "mouth." Related to "oral."
