enamellists:/ɪˈnæməlɪsts/
n.
珐琅师；搪瓷师；瓷釉工人
Artists or craftspeople who specialize in creating works of art or decorative objects using enamel.
The enamellists of Limoges were renowned for their intricate designs. 利摩日的珐琅师以其复杂的设计而闻名。
The exhibition showcased works by several contemporary enamellists. 展览展出了几位当代珐琅师的作品。
Synonyms: enamellers, enamel workers
Antonyms: N/A
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From enamel + -ist.  Enamel itself comes from Old French *esmail (modern French émail), perhaps from a Frankish *smalto, related to Old High German smalz "smelting, enamel," and ultimately from a Proto-Germanic *smeltaną.
