not-too-bad:/ˌnɒt tuː ˈbæd/ (UK) /ˌnɑːt tuː ˈbæd/ (US)| adj.|还不错，还好，过得去|An informal expression meaning "fairly good" or "acceptable," often used as an understated way of saying something is actually quite good.|"How was the movie?" "Not too bad, actually."  电影怎么样？“还不错，实际上。”|Synonyms: decent, alright, okay, reasonable, tolerable|Antonyms: terrible, awful, dreadful|Adjective

not-too-bad:/ˌnɒt tuː ˈbæd/ (UK) /ˌnɑːt tuː ˈbæd/ (US)| adv.|还不错地，还好地|Used to describe the manner in which something is done, indicating it was done to a fairly good or acceptable standard.|"He played not too badly, considering he'd never played before." 考虑到他以前从未演奏过，他演奏得还不错。 |Synonyms: decently, alright, okay, reasonably, tolerably|Antonyms: terribly, awfully, dreadfully|Adverb
