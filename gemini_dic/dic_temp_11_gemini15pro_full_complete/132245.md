nondecisively:/ˌnɒndɪˈsaɪsɪvli/| adv.|优柔寡断地，非决定性地|In a way that is not decisive; without reaching a clear decision or conclusion.  It describes actions or processes that lack firmness, clarity, or resolution.|The committee nondecisively discussed the proposal for hours without coming to a conclusion.|近义词：indecisively, hesitantly, tentatively, irresolutely|反义词：decisively, resolutely, firmly|词性:副词

Etymology:
non- (prefix):  From Latin *non-, meaning "not".
decisive (adjective): From Late Latin decisivus, from Latin decidere "to decide," from de- "down" + caedere "to cut".  The notion is of cutting off other possibilities.
-ly (suffix): Forms adverbs from adjectives.
