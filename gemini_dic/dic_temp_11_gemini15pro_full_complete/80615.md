goosander:/ˈɡuːsændər/ | n. | 秋沙鸭（指普通秋沙鸭） | A large fish-eating duck with a long serrated bill, found in rivers and lakes in northern parts of the northern hemisphere. |  例句：The goosander dived beneath the surface of the river, emerging moments later with a fish in its bill. 秋沙鸭潜入河面以下，过了一会儿嘴上叼着一条鱼浮出水面。

例句：We observed a pair of goosanders preening their feathers on a log. 我们观察到一对秋沙鸭在一根木头上梳理羽毛。 | 近义词：merganser, sawbill | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gosaundere, equivalent to goose + sawbill-like ending -ander (compare Old Dutch and Middle Dutch *saendere). The word "sawbill" refers to the serrated edges of the bill, resembling a saw.
