julep: /ˈdʒuːləp/ | n. | 朱利酒；薄荷酒 | A sweet alcoholic drink, traditionally made with bourbon, mint, sugar, and crushed ice. |  She sipped her mint julep slowly, savoring the cool sweetness. 她慢慢地啜饮着薄荷朱利酒，品味着清凉的甜味。

The Kentucky Derby is famous for its mint juleps. 肯塔基赛马会以其薄荷朱利酒而闻名。 | 近义词: cocktail, drink | 反义词: None | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Arabic جُلَاب (julāb, “rose water”), from Persian گلاب (golâb, “rose water”), from گل (gol, “rose, flower”) + آب (âb, “water”).  Originally a medicinal syrup, the term later came to refer to sweet, flavored drinks, and eventually to the mint julep as we know it today.
