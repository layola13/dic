obscurantist: /ɒbˈskjʊərəntɪst/ (British) /ˌäbˌsk(y)ʊˈrantəst/ (American)| n. adj.| 蒙昧主义者；故弄玄虚的人；晦涩难懂的|  A person who deliberately makes matters unclear, difficult to understand, or confusing, especially in order to conceal the truth.  A style or work that is deliberately unclear or abstruse.|  The professor's lectures were so obscurantist that even the brightest students struggled to understand them. 教授的讲座晦涩难懂，即使是最聪明的学生也难以理解。| Synonyms: obfuscator, mystifier, charlatan, sophist | Antonyms: elucidator, clarifier, explainer | Noun, Adjective
