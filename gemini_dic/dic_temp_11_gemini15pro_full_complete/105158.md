kettledrum:/ˈkɛtlˌdrʌm/ (US) /ˈkɛtlˌdrʌm/ (UK)| n.|定音鼓；铜鼓|A large drum with a round body of metal or pottery, with a parchment or plastic head that can be tuned by adjusting the tension on it.  It is played with soft-headed sticks.|The kettledrums provided a deep, resonant sound in the orchestra. 定音鼓在管弦乐队中发出深沉而洪亮的聲音。|synonyms:timpani, tympani|antonyms: (No direct antonyms for a musical instrument.  Perhaps "silent drum" for a humorous contrast)|noun
