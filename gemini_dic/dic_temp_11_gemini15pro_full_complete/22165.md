bombards:/bɒmˈbɑːdz/ (UK), /bɑːmˈbɑːrdz/ (US)| v. |轰炸，炮击；大量提问；涌向|To attack a place with continuous heavy fire from large guns or bombs; to direct a large number of questions or comments at someone, often aggressively; to be subjected to a great deal of something, often unwelcome.|The enemy bombarded the city for days. 敌人连续几天轰炸了这座城市。|近义词：shell, attack, pepper, inundate|反义词: protect, defend|词性:动词

bombardment:/bɒmˈbɑːdmənt/ (UK), /bɑːmˈbɑːrdmənt/ (US)| n. |轰炸，炮击；大量提问|A continuous attack with bombs, shells, or other missiles; a large number of questions or comments directed at someone, often aggressively.|The city suffered a heavy bombardment. 这座城市遭受了猛烈的轰炸。|近义词：barrage, shelling, onslaught|反义词:defense, protection|词性:名词

词源: From the Italian "bombarda," meaning a large cannon, ultimately from the Greek "bombos" meaning a deep hollow sound.
