orality:/ɔːˈrælɪti/ | n. | 口语性，口头性 | The quality of being spoken or verbally communicated; dependence on the spoken word.  The use of speech, especially in contrast to writing. |  The epic poem has a strong sense of orality, reflecting its origins in oral tradition. 这首史诗具有很强的口语性，反映了它起源于口头传统。

Oral histories provide valuable insights into the past. 口述历史为了解过去提供了宝贵的视角。 | 近义词：verbalism, spokenness | 反义词：literacy, writtenness | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *oralitas, from Latin oralis (“of the mouth”), from os (“mouth”).
