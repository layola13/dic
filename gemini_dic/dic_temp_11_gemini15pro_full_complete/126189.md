muzzle: /ˈmʌz.əl/ (US) /ˈmʌz.əl/ (UK)| n. vt. |口套，枪口；使…缄默|A covering for the nose and mouth of an animal, typically a dog, to prevent it from biting or barking.  Also, to prevent someone from speaking freely. |The dog's muzzle prevented it from biting. 狗的口套防止它咬人。|synonyms: gag, silence, restrain|antonyms: free, unleash, allow|noun, verb

muzzled: /ˈmʌz.əld/ (US) /ˈmʌz.əld/ (UK)| adj. |被戴上口套的；被压制言论的|Having a muzzle put on; prevented from speaking freely. |The muzzled dog whimpered softly. 被戴上口套的狗轻轻地呜咽着。|synonyms: gagged, silenced, suppressed|antonyms: unmuzzled, free, outspoken|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *musel (Modern French museau), diminutive of muse "snout, muzzle," from Vulgar Latin *mūsum, from Latin mūsus "snout, muzzle," of uncertain origin.
