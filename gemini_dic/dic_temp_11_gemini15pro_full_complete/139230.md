oblate:/ˈɒbleɪt/ /oʊˈbleɪt/| adj. n.|扁圆的；扁球形的；(献身于宗教的)还俗教士；|Having the shape of a sphere flattened at its poles, like the Earth.  Used to describe someone dedicated to religious life, particularly a lay person living under modified monastic vows. |例句：The Earth is an oblate spheroid. 地球是一个扁球体。

The oblate priest dedicated his life to helping the poor. 那位还俗教士毕生致力于帮助穷人。|近义词：flattened, compressed, spherical (for the shape); dedicated, devoted (for the person) |反义词:prolate, elongated (for the shape) |词性:adjective, noun

oblati:/oʊˈblɑːtiː/ /əˈbleɪtaɪ/ |n. (plural)|还俗教士；奉献者；| The plural form of "oblate," referring to multiple individuals dedicated to religious life, often lay persons living under modified monastic vows. Also can refer to things dedicated or offered. |例句：The oblati gathered for their annual retreat. 还俗教士们聚集在一起进行年度静修。

The oblati of her time and energy were offered willingly. 她自愿奉献出自己的时间和精力。|近义词：devotees, dedicants|反义词:|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *oblatus*, past participle of *offerre* ("to offer").  The sense of "flattened at the poles" arose from the idea of something being "offered up" or "spread out."
