holocaust: /ˈhɒləkɔːst/ (UK) /ˈhɑːləkɔːst/ (US)| n. |大屠杀，浩劫|destruction or slaughter on a mass scale, especially caused by fire or nuclear war.  Originally, a burnt offering.|The Holocaust was a horrific period in history.  纳粹大屠杀是历史上一个可怕的时期。|近义词：cataclysm, disaster, genocide, carnage|反义词: salvation, deliverance|词性:名词

词源: From Late Middle English *olocauste, from Old French holocauste, from Late Latin holocaustum, from Ancient Greek ὁλόκαυστον (holókauston, “burnt offering”), from ὅλος (hólos, “whole”) + καυστός (kaustós, “burnt”), verbal adjective of καίω (kaíō, “I burn”).
