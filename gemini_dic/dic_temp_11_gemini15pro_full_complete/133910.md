nonhabitabilities:/ˌnɒnhæbɪtəˈbɪlɪtiz/
n.
不适宜居住性；不适宜居住的状态
The nonhabitabilities of Mars pose a significant challenge for human colonization.
火星的不适宜居住性对人类殖民构成了重大挑战。
The report detailed the nonhabitabilities of the contaminated zone.
报告详细说明了污染区的不适宜居住状况。
uninhabitability, unsuitability
habitability, suitability
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + habitability.  Habitability comes from habitable + -ity. Habitable comes from the Old French habitable, from Late Latin habitabilis, from Latin habitare "to inhabit," from habitus "condition, appearance," past participle of habere "to have, to hold."
