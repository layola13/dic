diffluency:/dɪˈfluːənsi/ | n. |不流利，言语不畅|Lack of fluency or smoothness in speech, often characterized by hesitations, repetitions, or fillers.|Her diffluency made it difficult to understand her presentation. 她的言语不畅使得她的陈述难以理解。|近义词：hesitancy, stammering, faltering, dysfluency|反义词：fluency, eloquence|词性:名词

词源:  "Dis-" (Latin, meaning "not" or "apart") + "fluent" (from Latin "fluens," meaning "flowing") + "-cy" (suffix forming nouns of quality or state).  Essentially, it describes a state of "not flowing" in speech.
