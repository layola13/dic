johnny-come-lately: /ˈdʒɒni kʌm ˈleɪtli/ (British) /ˈdʒɑːni kʌm ˈleɪtli/ (American)| n.| 新来者；暴发户；后起之秀|Someone who has recently joined a group or activity and is therefore seen as less experienced or knowledgeable.  A newcomer, especially one perceived as an opportunist.|  Example: He was treated like a johnny-come-lately by the established members of the club.  他被俱乐部的元老们当作新来的人看待。| Example:  As a johnny-come-lately to the tech industry, she had a lot to learn. 作为科技行业的新人，她有很多东西要学。 |Synonym: newcomer, upstart, parvenu|Antonym: veteran, old-timer|Noun
