driveled:/ˈdrɪvəld/ (US), /ˈdrɪvld/ (UK)| v.|胡说，说傻话；流口水|To talk foolishly or incoherently; to let saliva drip from the mouth.|He driveled on and on about nothing important. 他一直在胡说八道些无关紧要的事情。|近义词：babbled, rambled, drooled|反义词：articulated, spoke clearly|词性: 动词

driveled:/ˈdrɪvəld/ (US), /ˈdrɪvld/ (UK)| adj.|胡言乱语的，愚蠢的|Characterized by foolish or incoherent talk.|His driveled pronouncements were met with derision. 他愚蠢的言论遭到了嘲笑。|近义词：incoherent, nonsensical, foolish|反义词：coherent, sensible, intelligent|词性: 形容词


词源:  From Middle English *drivelen, frequentative of *driven, from Old Norse *drífa (“to drip, to drive”).  The original sense was "to let saliva dribble," with the figurative meaning "to talk nonsense" developing later.
