deductible:/dɪˈdʌktɪbl/ | adj. n. |可扣除的；免赔额|Amount of money that must be paid by the insured before an insurance company will pay a claim.  The part of a loss or expense that is not covered by insurance. |例句：My car insurance has a $500 deductible. 我的汽车保险有 500 美元的免赔额。

例句：The deductible for this insurance policy is quite high. 这份保险单的免赔额相当高。|近义词：subtractable, allowance, exemption |反义词: nondeductible, inclusion |词性: adjective, noun

deductible:/dɪˈdʌktɪbl/ | adj. n. |可扣除的；免赔额|  Able to be deducted, especially from taxable income.  An amount that is deductible. |例句：Charitable donations are often deductible from your taxes.  慈善捐款通常可以从你的税款中扣除。

例句：The deductible on my health insurance plan is $1,000. 我的健康保险计划的免赔额是 1,000 美元。|近义词：allowable, claimable |反义词: non-deductible, unclaimable |词性: adjective, noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *deductible*, from Latin dēduct- (past participle stem of dēdūcere ‘lead down, derive’), from dē- ‘down, away from’ + dūcere ‘to lead’.
