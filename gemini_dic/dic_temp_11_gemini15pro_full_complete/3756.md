agistator:/ˈædʒɪteɪtər/| n.|煽动者，鼓动者|Someone who stirs up public opinion or causes unrest, often for political reasons.  A person who excites or agitates.|例句：The agitator was arrested for inciting violence.  煽动者因煽动暴力被捕。

例句：He was known as a political agitator, always stirring up trouble. 他被称为政治煽动者，总是挑起事端。|近义词：rabble-rouser, firebrand, provocateur, instigator|反义词:pacifier, mediator, peacemaker|词性:noun

agitate:/ˈædʒɪteɪt/| v.|煽动，鼓动；使…焦虑不安|To stir up public opinion or cause unrest. To make someone worried or nervous.|例句：The news agitated her. 这消息使她感到不安。

例句：His fiery speech agitated the crowd. 他激烈的演讲煽动了人群。|近义词：stir up, incite, provoke, disturb, upset|反义词:calm, soothe, pacify|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *agitator, from Latin agitatus, past participle of agitare "to put in motion, drive, stir up, discuss," frequentative of agere "to drive, lead, act, do."
