defoliates: /ˌdiːˈfoʊlieɪts/ | v. |  使…落叶；使… defoliate (单三形式) |Causes leaves to fall off, often prematurely, usually by chemical means or disease. |The chemical defoliates the trees, leaving them bare in the middle of summer. 这种化学物质使树木落叶，让它们在仲夏变得光秃秃的。|Synonyms: strip, denude, bare |Antonyms:  grow, flourish | verb

defoliate: /ˌdiːˈfoʊlieɪt/ | v. | 使…落叶；脱叶 | To remove the leaves from a tree or plant, often prematurely, usually by chemical means or disease. |  The strong winds defoliated the trees along the coast. 强风使沿海的树木落叶。 |Synonyms: strip, denude, bare |Antonyms: grow, flourish, leaf | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dēfoliāre, from dē- (away from, down from) + folium (leaf).
