condescendingly:/ˌkɒndɪˈsendɪŋli/ /ˌkɑːndɪˈsendɪŋli/
adverb
居高临下地，恩赐般地
in a way that shows that you think you are more intelligent or better than other people
He smiled condescendingly, as if she were a child. 他带着居高临下的微笑，就好像她是个孩子一样。

condescendingly:/ˌkɒndɪˈsendɪŋli/ /ˌkɑːndɪˈsendɪŋli/
adverb
傲慢地
in a haughty or patronizing manner.
"You're very clever, for an American," he remarked condescendingly. “作为一个美国人，你已经很聪明了，”他傲慢地说道。


condescendingly:/ˌkɒndɪˈsendɪŋli/ /ˌkɑːndɪˈsendɪŋli/
adverb
屈尊俯就地
in a way that shows that you are willing to do something that you consider to be below your dignity or level of importance
The celebrity condescendingly agreed to take a photo with the fan.  这位名人屈尊俯就地同意与粉丝合影。


近义词:patronizingly, superciliously, haughtily, disdainfully
反义词:respectfully, humbly, modestly

词源: From condescend + -ing + -ly.  Condescend comes from Latin condescendere, from com- "together" + descendere "to descend."
