mallophaga:/məˈlɒfəɡə/ | n. | 嚼虱目；羽虱；鸟虱 | An order of small, wingless insects that are parasitic on birds and mammals, commonly known as chewing lice or biting lice. They feed on feathers, hair, and skin debris. |  Mallophaga infest a wide variety of bird species. 嚼虱目寄生于各种鸟类。| Synonym: biting lice, chewing lice | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific taxonomic group) | Noun
