light-fingerednesses:/ˌlaɪtˈfɪŋɡərdnɪsnɪs/| n. | 手指灵巧；有偷窃癖；顺手牵羊|The act of being light-fingered, often implying a tendency towards theft.|His light-fingerednesses led to his expulsion from the market. 他的顺手牵羊导致他被赶出了市场。|Synonyms: thievishness, kleptomania|Antonyms: honesty, integrity|noun

Etymology:
From "light-fingered" + "-ness".  "Light-fingered" itself comes from the idea of having nimble fingers, light enough to steal without being noticed.  "Light" in this context refers to dexterity rather than weight.
