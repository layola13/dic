incultivated:/ˌɪnkʌltɪveɪtɪd/| adj.|未开垦的，荒芜的；未经耕作的；（人）未受教育的，粗野的|Not cultivated; undeveloped or unrefined.  Referring to land, it means not used for growing crops. Referring to a person, it implies a lack of education, refinement, or good manners.|The garden was overgrown and incultivated.  花园杂草丛生，无人耕种。|Synonyms: uncultivated, unrefined, crude, uncivilized, boorish|Antonyms: cultivated, refined, civilized, cultured, polished|adjective
