antecedent:/ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt/| n. adj.|先行词，先例，祖先|Something that existed before or logically precedes another.  A word or phrase that a pronoun refers to.|例句：The pronoun "he" refers to its antecedent, "John."  代词“他”指的是它的先行词“约翰”。

例句：His troubled childhood was the antecedent to his later problems. 他童年时的不幸是导致他后来出现问题的根源。|近义词：predecessor, precursor, forerunner|反义词:subsequent, descendant|词性:noun, adjective

antecedent:/ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt/| adj.|先前的，在前的|Existing, happening, or coming before something else.|例句：The antecedent events led to the war. 先前的事件导致了战争。

例句：He referred to the antecedent chapter in the book. 他提到了书中前面的章节。|近义词：previous, prior, earlier|反义词:subsequent, later|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English antecedent, from Old French antecedent, from Latin antecedentem (nominative antecedēns), present participle of antecedere (“to go before, precede”), from ante (“before”) + cedere (“to go”).
