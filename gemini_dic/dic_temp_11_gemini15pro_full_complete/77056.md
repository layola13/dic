garnet:/ˈɡɑːrnɪt/ (US), /ˈɡɑːnɪt/ (UK)| n. |石榴石；深红色|A hard, glassy mineral that is often used as a gemstone, typically red in color. |She wore a necklace of sparkling garnets. 她戴着一条闪耀的石榴石项链。|The wine was a deep garnet color.  这酒是深石榴红色的。|近义词:ruby, gemstone|反义词:N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gernet*, from Old French *grenat* (meaning "dark red"), and Anglo-Norman *garnet*, from Medieval Latin *granatum*, from Latin *grānum* ("grain, seed").  This is likely due to the resemblance of the small garnet crystals to the seeds of a pomegranate.
