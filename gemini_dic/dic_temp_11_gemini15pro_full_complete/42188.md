coulomb:/ˈkuːlɒm/ (UK) /ˈkuːlɑːm/ (US)| n. |库仑|The SI unit of electric charge, equal to the quantity of electricity conveyed in one second by a current of one ampere. |A battery can store a certain number of coulombs of charge.  电池可以储存一定数量的库仑电荷。|Synonyms:  unit of charge |Antonyms: N/A |noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *coulomb*, named after Charles-Augustin de Coulomb (1736-1806), French physicist.
