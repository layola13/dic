elephantiasis: /ˌɛlɪfənˈtaɪəsɪs/ | n. | 象皮病 | A parasitic disease characterized by the thickening of the skin and underlying tissues, especially in the legs and male genitals. It is caused by obstruction of the lymphatic vessels, usually by filarial worms. |  Elephantiasis is a debilitating condition that can cause significant disfigurement. 象皮病是一种使人衰弱的疾病，会导致严重的畸形。


Elephantiasis is most common in tropical regions. 象皮病在热带地区最为常见。


| 近义词: lymphatic filariasis | 反义词: None | 词性: Noun
