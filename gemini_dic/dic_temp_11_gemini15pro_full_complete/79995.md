go-in-one-ear-and-out-the-other:/ɡoʊ ɪn wʌn ɪr ænd aʊt ði ʌðər/ | idiom | 左耳进右耳出；当耳旁风 | Something that is said goes in one ear and out the other if it is heard but immediately forgotten. |  The teacher's instructions went in one ear and out the other. 老师的指示左耳进右耳出。| Synonyms:  fall on deaf ears, be ignored, be disregarded | Antonyms:  be taken to heart, be heeded, be remembered | idiom
