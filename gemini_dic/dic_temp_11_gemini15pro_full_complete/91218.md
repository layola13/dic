hydrobranchiata:/ˌhaɪdrəbræŋkiˈeɪtə/ | n. |水鳃类动物 |Aquatic animals that breathe through gills.  Specifically, an outdated taxonomic group that included crustaceans, annelids, and other invertebrates believed to breathe using gill-like structures. This term is no longer used in modern classification. | Example:  Early naturalists classified a diverse group of aquatic animals as Hydrobranchiata. 早期的博物学家将各种水生动物归类为水鳃类动物。 | Synonym: (No direct modern equivalent due to obsolescence; use more specific taxonomic names like Crustacea, Annelida etc.) | Antonym: (Not applicable as it's a taxonomic grouping) | Noun
