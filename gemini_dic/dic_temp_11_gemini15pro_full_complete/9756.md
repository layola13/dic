antlers: /ˈæntlərz/ (US), /ˈæntləz/ (UK) | n. | 鹿角，茸角 |  The bony, branching growths on the head of a deer, elk, moose, or other member of the Cervidae family, typically found only on males (except in reindeer/caribou).  They are shed and regrown annually. | The stag's antlers were magnificent.  雄鹿的鹿角非常壮观。| The elk shed its antlers in the spring.  麋鹿在春天脱落鹿角。| synonyms: horns (though not technically correct, often used interchangeably), rack | antonyms: N/A | noun
