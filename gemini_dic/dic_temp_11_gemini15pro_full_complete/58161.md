droughts: /draʊts/
|n.|干旱；旱灾|A prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall, leading to a shortage of water.|The severe droughts caused widespread crop failures. 严重的干旱造成了大面积的农作物歉收。|Synonyms: aridity, dryness|Antonyms: flood, deluge|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *droughte*, from Old English *drugoþ*, from Proto-Germanic *druguþiz* (“dryness, drought”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰrewgʰ- (“to be dry”). Cognate with Dutch *droogte* (“drought”), German *Dürre* (“drought”), Icelandic *þurrkur* (“drought”).
