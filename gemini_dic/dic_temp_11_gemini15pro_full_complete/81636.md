greediness:/ˈɡriːdinəs/| n.|贪婪，贪心|An excessive desire to acquire or possess more than one needs or deserves, especially with respect to material wealth.|His greediness led him to cheat his business partners. 他的贪婪导致他欺骗了他的生意伙伴。|近义词：avarice, cupidity, rapacity|反义词：generosity, altruism|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From greedy + -ness.  Greedy comes from Old English grædig "greedy, gluttonous, eager," from Proto-Germanic *gredigaz (source also of Old Saxon girdig, Old Frisian gredig, Middle Dutch gretich, Dutch gretig "greedy, desirous," Old High German grittag, German gierig "greedy," Old Norse græðigr "greedy for food"), from PIE root *ghredh- "to be hungry, greedy."  The suffix -ness forms nouns from adjectives.
