mispredicting:/ˌmɪsprɪˈdɪktɪŋ/
verb
错误预测，预测失误
Making an incorrect prediction about something that will happen in the future.
The economist was fired for mispredicting the market crash. 这位经济学家因错误预测市场崩盘而被解雇。
The weather forecast keeps mispredicting the rain. 天气预报总是错误地预测下雨。
近义词: miscalculating, misjudging, forecasting incorrectly
反义词: predicting accurately, foreseeing correctly
词性: verb (present participle)

Etymology:
From mis- (prefix meaning 'wrong' or 'badly') + predicting (present participle of 'predict'), from Latin praedict- ‘foretold’, from the verb praedicere, from prae- ‘beforehand’ + dicere ‘say’.
