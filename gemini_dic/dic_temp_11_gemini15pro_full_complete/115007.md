mandate:/ˈmændeɪt/ (US), /ˈmændeɪt/ (UK)| n. vt.|授权，命令，委托；强制执行|An official order or commission to do something.  The government has a mandate to reduce poverty. |例句：The election victory gave the party a clear mandate to implement its policies. 选举的胜利给了该党执行其政策的明确授权。

mandate:/ˈmændeɪt/ (US), /ˈmændeɪt/ (UK)| n. vt.|授权，命令，委托；强制执行|The authority given to an elected government by the electorate.  The president claimed a mandate for change. |例句：The United Nations issued a mandate for peacekeeping forces to enter the region. 联合国发布了维和部队进入该地区的授权。


mandate:/ˈmændeɪt/ (US), /ˈmændeɪt/ (UK)| n. vt.|授权，命令，委托；强制执行|To officially require something; to make mandatory. The law mandates that all drivers have insurance. |例句：The new law mandates the use of seat belts. 新法律强制要求使用安全带。

mandate:/ˈmændeɪt/ (US), /ˈmændeɪt/ (UK)| n. vt.|授权，命令，委托；强制执行|To give someone authority to act. The shareholders mandated the board to sell the company. |例句：The CEO mandated a review of all company policies. 首席执行官下令审查所有公司政策。


近义词: order, command, instruction, authorization, decree, directive

反义词: prohibition, forbiddance

词性: noun, verb

词源: From Latin *mandatum* "something entrusted," noun use of neuter past participle of *mandare* "to entrust, order," from *manus* "hand" + *dare* "to give."
