deliriously: /dɪˈlɪəriəsli/ /dəˈlɪriəsli/ | adv. | 狂喜地，发狂地 | In an extremely happy or excited way, often because of illness or drugs. | She laughed deliriously when she heard the good news. 她听到好消息后欣喜若狂地大笑起来。|  He was deliriously happy when his son was born. 当他的儿子出生时，他欣喜若狂。 | Synonyms: ecstatically, rapturously, joyfully, blissfully | Antonyms: miserably, sadly, despondently | 词性: 副词

delirious: /dɪˈlɪəriəs/ /dəˈlɪriəs/ | adj. |  神志不清的，精神错乱的，极度兴奋的 |  In a state of delirium; wild with excitement, enthusiasm, etc. | He was delirious with fever. 他因发烧而神志不清。| She was delirious with joy. 她欣喜若狂。 | Synonyms: frenzied, crazed, ecstatic, rapturous | Antonyms: lucid, sane, calm, composed | 词性: 形容词

delirium: /dɪˈlɪəriəm/ /dəˈlɪriəm/ | n. |  谵妄，精神错乱，极度兴奋 | A disturbed state of mind resulting from illness or intoxication and characterized by restlessness, illusions, and incoherence of thought and speech.  | He was in a state of delirium after the accident. 事故发生后，他处于谵妄状态。 | The patient experienced delirium due to high fever. 病人因高烧而出现谵妄。| Synonyms: frenzy, madness, craze, mania | Antonyms: lucidity, sanity, calmness, composure | 词性: 名词


词源: From Late Latin *dēliriōsus, from Latin dēlīrāre (“to be insane, rave”), from dē- (“from, out of”) + līra (“furrow, track”). The sense development is from "being out of one's furrow" to "being off track mentally".
