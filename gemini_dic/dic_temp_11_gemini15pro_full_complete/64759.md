escudo:/ɪˈskudoʊ/ (US), /ɪˈskuːdoʊ/ (UK)| n.|埃斯库多（葡萄牙、佛得角、东帝汶等国的货币单位）|A former monetary unit of Portugal, Cape Verde, and East Timor.  The word "escudo" itself means "shield" in Portuguese and Spanish.|Portugal used the escudo before adopting the euro. 葡萄牙在采用欧元之前使用埃斯库多。|Synonym: monetary unit, currency|Antonym: (No direct antonym for a currency unit)|noun

escudo:/ɪˈskudoʊ/ (US), /ɪˈskuːdoʊ/ (UK)| n.|盾牌（纹章学）|A shield or shield-shaped emblem bearing a coat of arms.|The family crest was displayed on an escudo.  家族徽章展示在一个盾牌上。|Synonym: shield, blazon, coat of arms|Antonym: (No direct antonym for a heraldic shield)|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Portuguese *escudo* and Spanish *escudo*, both from Latin *scūtum* ("shield").  The monetary usage derives from the fact that early Portuguese coins often depicted a shield.
