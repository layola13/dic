buffoonly: /bəˈfuːnli/ | adj. | 滑稽可笑的；丑角般的 | Resembling or characteristic of a buffoon; clownish. | His buffoonly antics amused the children. 他的滑稽动作逗乐了孩子们。| Synonyms: clownish, comical, ludicrous, farcical | Antonyms: serious, solemn, grave | adjective

buffoon: /bəˈfuːn/ | n. | 小丑；滑稽可笑的人 | A person who amuses others by tricks, jokes, odd gestures, and funny or silly behavior. | The king employed a buffoon to entertain the court. 国王雇了一个小丑来娱乐宫廷。| Synonyms: clown, jester, comedian, fool | Antonyms: sage, intellectual | noun

词源: From Italian *buffone*, from Late Latin *buffo* (“comic actor”).
