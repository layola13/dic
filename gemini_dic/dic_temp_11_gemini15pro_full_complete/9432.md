antirealism:/ˌæntiˈriːəlɪzəm/ | n. |反现实主义; 非实在论 |The philosophical doctrine that denies that objects of knowledge or thought necessarily exist independently of the mind.  It encompasses a variety of views across several philosophical domains, including ethics, aesthetics, science, mathematics, and semantics.|  Many philosophers see pragmatism as a form of antirealism. 许多哲学家将实用主义视为一种反现实主义。| synonyms: idealism, irrealism, nonrealism | antonyms: realism | noun
