metalworkers:/ˈmɛtlˌwɜːrkərz/ (US), /ˈmɛtlˌwɜːkəz/ (UK)| n. |金属工匠，金属工人|People who work with metal, especially by shaping it by hammering, forging, or other methods. |The metalworkers carefully shaped the steel into intricate designs. 金属工匠们小心翼翼地将钢材塑造成复杂的图案。|synonyms: blacksmiths, machinists, fabricators, tinsmiths, welders, ironworkers|antonyms: N/A (no direct antonym, perhaps "non-metalworkers")|noun
Etymology: metal + worker. "Metal" comes from Old French "metal" (from Latin "metallum"), and "worker" comes from Old English "wyrcan" (to work).
