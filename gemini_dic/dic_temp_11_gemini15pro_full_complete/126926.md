nark: /nɑːrk/ | n. | 告密者，密探；吸毒者 |  A police informer.  A drug addict. | The police used a nark to catch the drug dealers. 警察用了一个告密者来抓捕毒贩。|  informer, snitch, rat |  law-abiding citizen | noun

nark: /nɑːrk/ | v. |  告密；惹恼 | To inform on someone to the police. To annoy or irritate someone. | He narked on his classmates for cheating on the test. 他向老师告发他的同学考试作弊。 | inform, betray, irritate, annoy | protect, please | verb

Etymology:  Origin uncertain. Possibly from Romany *nak, "nose," related to the idea of sniffing out information.
