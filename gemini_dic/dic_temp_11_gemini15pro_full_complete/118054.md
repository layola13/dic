mendacious:/mɛnˈdeɪʃəs/
| adj. |
| 谎言的；虚假的；不真实的 |
| Given to lying; habitually dishonest.  Characterized by deception or falsehood. |
| The politician's mendacious statements eroded public trust.  这位政客的谎言损害了公众的信任。 |

|  He was known for his mendacious nature, twisting the truth to fit his narrative. 他以其虚假的本性而闻名，歪曲事实以符合他的叙述。 |
| lying, dishonest, deceitful, untruthful, false |
| truthful, honest, veracious |
| adjective |

Etymology->
From Latin *mendac-, stem of *mendax ("lying, deceitful"), present participle of *mendārī ("to lie, deceive"), of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to the Latin word mendum ("fault, defect").
