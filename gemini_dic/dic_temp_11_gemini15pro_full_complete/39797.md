consorter:/kənˈsɔːrtər/
n.|伙伴，合伙人，配偶|Someone who associates with a particular person or group, typically with the disapproval of others; a spouse or partner.|例句：She was known to be a consorter of criminals. 她因与罪犯勾结而闻名。|近义词：associate, companion, partner, spouse, confederate|反义词：enemy, opponent|词性: 名词

consorter:/kənˈsɔːrtər/
v.|陪伴，结交，勾结|To habitually associate with someone, typically with the disapproval of others.|例句：He consorted with known drug dealers. 他与已知的毒贩勾结。|近义词：associate, fraternize, mingle|反义词：avoid, shun|词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *consorten*, from Old French *consorter*, from Latin *consors*, *consortis* (“sharing, partaking, partner”), from *con-* (“with, together”) + *sors*, *sortis* (“lot, fate, share”).
