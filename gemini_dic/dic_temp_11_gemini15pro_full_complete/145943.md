overdiscreet:/ˈoʊvərˌdɪˈskriːt/| adj.|过于谨慎的，过分小心谨慎的|Excessively discreet or cautious; too reserved in speech or behavior.|She was so overdiscreet that she refused to even mention his name.|近义词：overcautious, excessively reserved, unduly careful|反义词：indiscreet, imprudent, careless|词性:形容词

Etymology:
"over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi "over, above") + "discreet" (from Old French discret, from Latin discretus "separated, distinct," past participle of discernere "to distinguish").  Essentially, "over-separated" in judgment or action, implying excessive caution.
