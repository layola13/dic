falter:/ˈfɔːltər/ (US) /ˈfɔːltə(r)/ (UK)| n.| 犹豫，迟疑；支吾；蹒跚|A wavering or hesitation in speech or action; a stumble.|There was a falter in her step as she approached the stage.  当她走近舞台时，脚步有些踉跄。| 近义词：hesitation, waver, stumble|反义词：  confidence, stability|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *falteren*, frequentative of *falten* (“to fold, stumble, fall”), from Old English *fealdan* (“to fold”), from Proto-Germanic *falþaną* (“to fold”), from Proto-Indo-European *pel- (“to fold, bend”). Related to fold.
