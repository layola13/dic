incontinence:/ɪnˈkɒntɪnəns/ (US: /ɪnˈkɑːntɪnəns/) | n. |失禁，不能自制|Inability to control the bladder or bowels.|She suffers from incontinence after the surgery.  她手术后患有失禁。|Near synonyms: inability to control urination, inability to control defecation, enuresis (for bedwetting)|Antonyms: continence, self-control|Noun

incontinences:/ɪnˈkɒntɪnənsiz/ (US: /ɪnˈkɑːntɪnənsiz/) | n. |(复数)失禁，不能自制(的情况)|Multiple instances of inability to control the bladder or bowels.|The patient reported several incontinences during the night. 病人报告说夜里发生了几次失禁。|Near synonyms: instances of incontinence, occurrences of inability to control urination/defecation|Antonyms: continence, self-control|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *incontinentia, from Latin in- (“not”) + continentia (“continence”).  The Latin continentia is from continēns, present participle of continēre (“to hold together, contain”), from con- (“together”) + tenēre (“to hold”).
