introductorinesses:/ˌɪntrəˈdʌktərɪnɪsɪz/
|n.|介绍性；入门性（通常用复数形式）|The state or quality of being introductory.  The numerous introductorinesses of the textbook made it suitable for beginners. 这本教科书的众多入门性内容使其适合初学者。|例句：The introductorinesses of the first few chapters eased the students into the complex subject. 前几章的介绍性内容使学生更容易理解这门复杂的学科。|近义词：preliminaries, introductions|反义词：conclusions, summaries|词性:名词

Etymology:
From introductory + -ness + -es.  "Introductory" comes from the Late Latin "introductorius," from "introducere" (to lead in). "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition. "-es" is a plural suffix.
