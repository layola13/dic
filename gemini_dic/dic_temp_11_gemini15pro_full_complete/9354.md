antiprohibitions:/ˌæntiˌprəʊɪˈbɪʃənz/ | n. | 反禁令主义，反对禁令的行动或观点 |  The act or practice of opposing prohibitions, especially those related to alcohol or other substances.  It encompasses the belief that individuals should have the freedom to make choices about their own lives, even if those choices involve activities that some consider harmful or immoral. |  The rise of antiprohibitions in the 1920s led to the repeal of Prohibition in the United States.  20世纪20年代反禁酒运动的兴起导致了美国禁酒令的废除。
