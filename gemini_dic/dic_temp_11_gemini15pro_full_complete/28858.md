carpetbag:/ˈkɑːrpɪtˌbæɡ/ | n. | 旅行袋；提包（尤指用旧地毯做的廉价而耐用的那种）；（贬义）投机政客 | A traveling bag, typically one made of carpet fabric; (derogatory) a political candidate who seeks election in an area where they have no local connections. |  He carried all his possessions in a battered carpetbag. 他所有的家当都装在一个破旧的旅行袋里。| synonyms: traveling bag, suitcase, valise | antonyms: None (concrete noun) | noun

carpetbagger:/ˈkɑːrpɪtˌbæɡər/ | n. |（尤指美国内战后）外来政客，投机政客 | (especially in the US after the Civil War) a politician who seeks election in an area where they have no local connections, often seen as exploiting the situation for personal gain. |  Many carpetbaggers were accused of corruption. 许多外来政客被指控腐败。| synonyms: opportunist, outsider, interloper | antonyms: native, local, resident | noun

词源:
Mid 19th century: from carpet + bag, denoting a type of cheap, sturdy luggage. The derogatory political sense arose during the Reconstruction era in the US (1865–77).
