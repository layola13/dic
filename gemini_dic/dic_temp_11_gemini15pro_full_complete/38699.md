condescend:/ˌkɒndɪˈsend/ (US) /ˌkɑːndɪˈsend/| v. |屈尊；俯就；摆架子|To behave in a way that shows you consider yourself socially superior to someone.|He condescended to speak to me after the meeting. 会议结束后，他屈尊与我交谈。|She condescended to help with the dishes. 她纡尊降贵地帮忙洗碗。|近义词：patronize, deign|反义词：respect, admire|词性:动词

condescension:/ˌkɒndɪˈsenʃn/ (US) /ˌkɑːndɪˈsenʃn/| n. |屈尊；俯就；摆架子|An attitude of patronizing superiority; disdain.|He spoke with such condescension that it offended everyone. 他的语气如此傲慢，冒犯了所有人。|Her condescension towards the staff was evident. 她对员工的傲慢态度显而易见。|近义词：patronage, disdain|反义词：respect, humility|词性:名词

词源: From Latin "condescendere" (to descend, stoop), from "com-" (together, with) + "descendere" (to climb down).
