haggardly:/ˈhæɡərdli/| adv.|憔悴地，疲惫不堪地|Looking exhausted and unwell, typically as a result of worry, tiredness, or illness.  Characterized by a worn and gaunt appearance.|She stared haggardly at her reflection. 她憔悴地盯着自己的倒影。|近义词：gauntly, wearily, exhausted|反义词:healthily, vibrantly|词性:副词
Etymology: From Middle English *hagger, from Old Norse *hagr, meaning "condition, state of mind," ultimately from Proto-Germanic *hagiz, meaning "condition, state."  The -ly suffix transforms it into an adverb.
