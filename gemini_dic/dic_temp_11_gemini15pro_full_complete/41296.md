cordon:/ˈkɔːrdən/ (US) /ˈkɔːdn/ (UK)| n. v.|警戒线，封锁线；绶带|A line or circle of police, soldiers, or guards preventing people from entering or leaving an area; a ribbon or cord worn as a badge of honor or membership.|The police set up a cordon around the crime scene. 警方在犯罪现场设置了警戒线。|近义词：barrier, barricade, line|反义词：access, entrance|词性: 名词, 动词

cordon:/ˈkɔːrdən/ (US) /ˈkɔːdn/ (UK)| v.|设置警戒线，封锁|To enclose or surround with a cordon.|The area was cordoned off after the explosion. 爆炸发生后，该地区被封锁了。|近义词：block off, seal off, close off|反义词：open, allow access|词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From French "cordon," meaning "ribbon," ultimately from Late Latin "chorda," meaning "string" or "rope," from Greek "chordē," also meaning "string" of a musical instrument or "gut."
