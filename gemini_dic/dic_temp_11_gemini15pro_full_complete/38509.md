conciliatingly:/kənˈsɪli.eɪ.tɪŋ.li/
adverb|抚慰地，调和地|in a way that is intended to make someone less angry or more friendly|He spoke conciliatingly, hoping to avoid further argument. 他语气和缓地说着，希望避免进一步的争论。|例句：She smiled conciliatingly at her angry boss. 她对着生气的老板安抚地笑了笑。|近义词：peacefully, appeasingly, soothingly|反义词：aggressively, antagonistically|词性:副词
词源: From conciliate + -ing + -ly.  Conciliate comes from Latin conciliare "to bring together, unite in feelings, make friendly," from concilium "council, meeting," literally "a calling together," from com- "together" (see com-) + calare "to call, summon" (from PIE root *kele- (2) "to shout").
