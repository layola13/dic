hellraiser:/ˈhɛlˌreɪzər/| n.|地狱制造者，捣乱者，引发骚动的人|Someone who causes trouble or disruption, especially in a lively or boisterous way.  A person who acts in a way that is considered shocking or scandalous.|例句：He's a real hellraiser, always getting into fights. 他真是个捣乱分子，总是惹是生非。

例句：She was known as a hellraiser in her youth, constantly challenging authority. 她年轻时以捣乱分子而闻名，不断挑战权威。|近义词：troublemaker, rabble-rouser, mischief-maker, firebrand|反义词:peacemaker, conformist|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hell" + "raiser."  "Hell" refers to a state of chaos or torment, while "raiser" implies someone who stirs up or incites such a state.  The term gained popularity after the release of the horror film "Hellraiser" in 1987, but its usage predates the film.
