mesogastrium:/ˌmɛzəˈɡæstriəm/| n.|肠系膜|The part of the peritoneum attached to the greater curvature of the stomach and continuing downwards as the greater omentum.  It suspends the stomach and other abdominal viscera from the dorsal body wall.|The mesogastrium plays a crucial role in the development of the digestive system.|Synonym: mesogaster|Antonym: None|noun

mesogastrium:/ˌmɛzəˈɡæstriəm/| n.|胃系膜|The fold of peritoneum that attaches the stomach to the posterior abdominal wall.|During embryonic development, the mesogastrium gives rise to the greater omentum.|Synonym: mesogaster|Antonym: None|noun
