gripple:/ˈɡrɪpəl/| n. v. |抓握器，夹子；抓紧，夹住|A device used for gripping and securing wire, rope, or cable.  It often consists of a wedge-shaped piece of metal with teeth or ridges that bite into the wire when tension is applied.  As a verb, it means to fasten or secure with a gripple.|

例句：
The builder used a gripple to secure the fence wire.  建筑工人用抓握器固定栅栏线。

The climber grippled the rope tightly.  攀登者紧紧抓住绳子。

近义词：clamp, fastener, grip, secure
反义词:release, loosen

词性:n. v.

词源:  A relatively recent word (late 20th century), it's likely a blend of "grip" and "nipple," referencing the shape and function of the device.
