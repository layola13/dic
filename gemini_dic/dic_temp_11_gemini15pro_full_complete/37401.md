commercialised: /kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 商业化的，商品化的 |  Made or adapted for sale, especially on a large scale.  Having been exploited for profit or for mass appeal. | The festival has become increasingly commercialised in recent years.  近年来，这个节日越来越商业化了。 | commercialized (US spelling), marketed, merchandised | non-commercial, uncommercial | adjective

commercialise: /kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪz/ | v. |  使商业化，使商品化 | To manage or exploit (something) in a way designed to make a profit. To develop (something, such as an invention) for sale or distribution. |  They commercialised the drug and made a fortune. 他们将这种药物商业化，赚了一大笔钱。 | commercialize (US spelling), market, merchandise |  decommercialize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From late 19th century: from commercial + -ize.
