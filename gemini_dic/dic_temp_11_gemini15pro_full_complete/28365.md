caravel:/ˈkærəˌvɛl/| n. |卡拉维尔帆船|A small, highly maneuverable sailing ship developed in the 15th century by the Portuguese to explore along the West African coast and into the Atlantic Ocean. It was later used by Spanish and Portuguese explorers.|例句：The caravel was instrumental in the Age of Discovery. 卡拉维尔帆船在大航海时代发挥了重要作用。

例句：Columbus sailed to the Americas in three caravels: the Niña, the Pinta, and the Santa María. 哥伦布乘坐三艘卡拉维尔帆船：尼娜号、平塔号和圣玛利亚号，航行到美洲。|近义词：ship, vessel|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Portuguese *caravela*, possibly from Late Latin *carabus* (“small boat”), of uncertain origin, possibly from Greek κάραβος (kárabos, “light ship”).
