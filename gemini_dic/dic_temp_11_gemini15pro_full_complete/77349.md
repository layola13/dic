gaudily:/ˈɡɔːdɪli/| adv.|花哨地，俗丽地|In a showy or excessively ornamented manner; flashily.|She was dressed gaudily in a bright pink dress and feather boa. 她穿着一件亮粉色的连衣裙和羽毛围巾，打扮得花枝招展。|近义词：garishly, flashily, showily, ostentatiously|反义词：simply, plainly, subtly, understatedly|词性:副词

gaudily:/ˈɡɔːdɪli/| adj.|花哨的，俗丽的|(This is a less common usage, primarily used predicatively) Showy or excessively ornamented; flashy.|Her outfit was gaudily bright. 她的服装俗艳无比。 |近义词：garish, flashy, showy, ostentatious|反义词：simple, plain, subtle, understated|词性:形容词 (predicative)

ETYMOLOGY->
From gaudy + -ly. Gaudy comes from the Middle French gaudi, meaning "rejoicing, merrymaking," ultimately from the Latin gaudium, "joy." The meaning shifted to "showy" and "tastelessly ornamented" in the 16th century.
