cavatina:/ˌkævəˈtiːnə/ | n. | 小夜曲，抒情短歌 | A short, simple melody, often of a sentimental character, typically found in operas or other dramatic works.  It is characterized by its lyrical and expressive qualities. | The soprano's cavatina in the second act was the highlight of the opera.  女高音在第二幕中的小夜曲是整部歌剧的亮点。

The cavatina was beautifully performed and deeply moving. 这首小夜曲演奏得优美动听，感人至深。 | 近义词:aria, song, melody | 反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "dissonance" in terms of musical quality) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *cavatina*, diminutive of *cavata*, literally "something dug out," from *cavare* "to dig, hollow out,"  from Latin *cavāre*
