domiciliar:/ˌdɒmɪˈsɪliəri/ (UK), /ˌdɑːmɪˈsɪliəri/ (US)| adj.| 居家的，住家的；送货上门的|Relating to the home or place of residence; providing services in the patient's home.|例句：Domiciliary care is provided for elderly people who can no longer live independently. 为无法独立生活的老人提供居家护理。

例句：They offer a domiciliary laundry service. 他们提供送货上门的洗衣服务。|近义词：residential, domestic, home-based|反义词：non-residential, institutional|词性: adjective

domicile:/ˈdɒmɪsaɪl/ (UK), /ˈdɑːməsaɪl/ (US)| n. vt.|住所，居所；(法律)户籍；使定居|A person's fixed, permanent, and principal home for legal purposes; to establish someone in a domicile.|例句：He changed his domicile from London to Paris. 他把户籍从伦敦迁到了巴黎。

例句：The company is domiciled in the UK. 这家公司的注册地是英国。|近义词：residence, abode, dwelling|反义词：N/A|词性: noun, verb


词源:  From Old French *domiciliarie, from Latin domicilium "dwelling, residence," from domus "house."
