catelectrotonus:/kætˌɪlɛkˈtrɒtənəs/| n.|阴极电紧张|A decrease in the excitability of a nerve or muscle in the region near the cathode during the passage of an electric current.|The experiment demonstrated catelectrotonus near the negatively charged electrode.|近义词: cathodal depression|反义词: anelectrotonus|词性:noun

Etymology: From Greek *kata-* (down, against) + *elektron* (amber, referring to electricity) + *-tonos* (tension).
