emasculating:/ɪˈmæskjʊleɪtɪŋ/
adjective
使人丧失男子气概的；使人柔弱的
Depriving a man of his male role or identity; making someone weaker or less effective
His constant criticism was emasculating. 他不断的批评使我感到很无力。

emasculate:/ɪˈmæskjʊleɪt/
verb
使丧失男子气概；使柔弱
To deprive a man of his male role or identity; to make someone weaker or less effective
The surgery emasculated him.  手术让他丧失了男子气概。
Critics charged that the new law would emasculate the power of the presidency. 批评人士指责这项新法律将削弱总统的权力。

近义词:weakening, debilitating, unmanning
反义词:empowering, strengthening
词性: adjective (emasculating), verb (emasculate)


词源: From Latin emasculatus, past participle of emasculare "to castrate, weaken," from ex- "out" + masculus "male."
