equerryship: /ˈɛkwərʃɪp/ | n. |侍从身份；马夫身份|  The position or rank of a squire, especially in medieval times, acting as an attendant to a knight.  Historically, it involved training in horsemanship, weaponry, and chivalrous conduct, often as a preparation for knighthood. | He served a long equerryship before finally being knighted. 他在最终被封为骑士之前，长期担任侍从。| Near synonyms: squireship, apprenticeship (in a historical context), service (in a historical context) | Antonyms: knighthood, mastery | Noun
