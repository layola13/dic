anomie: /ˈænəmi/ (US), /ˈænəmi/ (UK)| n. | 失范，混乱，社会反常状态|A state of normlessness; a social condition in which there is a disintegration or absence of social norms and values.|The rapid social changes led to a state of anomie. 快速的社会变革导致了失范状态。|Synonyms: lawlessness, normlessness, chaos, disorder|Antonyms: order, stability, conformity|Noun

anomie: /ˈænəmi/ (US), /ˈænəmi/ (UK)| n. | 个人迷茫，目标缺失|A personal state of alienation and purposelessness; a lack of direction or purpose in life.|He felt a deep sense of anomie after losing his job and his family.  失去工作和家庭后，他感到深深的迷茫。|Synonyms: alienation, aimlessness, purposelessness, rootlessness|Antonyms: purpose, direction, belonging|Noun

anomalies: /əˈnɒməliz/ (UK), /əˈnɑːməliz/ (US)| n. | 反常现象，异常，不规则|Deviation or departure from the normal or common order, form, or rule; irregularities.|The scientists were puzzled by the anomalies in the data. 科学家们对数据中的异常现象感到困惑。|Synonyms: irregularities, abnormalities, deviations, exceptions|Antonyms: norms, regularities, standards|Noun
