indiscretion:/ˌɪndɪˈskreʃn/ | n. | 轻率；不谨慎；言行失检 | An act or remark that shows a lack of good judgment, especially involving confidential information or personal matters. |  He committed several indiscretions in his youth that came back to haunt him later. 他年轻时犯了几次轻率的错误，后来给他带来了困扰。

indiscretion:/ˌɪndɪˈskreʃn/ | n. |  不检点的行为;  | An instance of behaving in a way that is not considered correct or moral. |  Her affair with a married man was a serious indiscretion. 她与一个已婚男人的婚外情是一种严重的不检点行为。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin indiscretio, from Latin indiscretus (“undiscerning, indiscreet”), from in- (“not”) + discretus (“separated, distinguished”).

SYNONYMS->
imprudence, folly, slip, blunder, lapse, error, mistake, faux pas, gaffe, impropriety

ANTONYMS->
prudence, discretion, caution, circumspection
