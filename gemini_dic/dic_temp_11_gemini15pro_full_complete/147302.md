overmodifying:/ˌoʊvərˈmɒdɪfaɪɪŋ/ |v. |过度修饰|Modifying something excessively, often to the point of making it unclear or awkward.  The act of using too many modifiers (adjectives, adverbs, etc.) in a sentence or phrase, resulting in cluttered and less effective communication.|She was overmodifying her essay with unnecessary adverbs and adjectives.|overusing, overdoing|understating, simplifying|verb
