inquisitorious:/ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːriəs/
adjective
爱打听的；好奇的；审问式的
Given to inquiry, especially of a searching or prying nature; excessively curious.
例句：The inquisitorious journalist relentlessly pursued the story.  爱打听的记者锲而不舍地追查着这条新闻。

例句：Her inquisitorious nature led her to ask many personal questions. 她爱打听的性格让她问了很多私人问题。

近义词：curious, inquisitive, prying, probing
反义词：apathetic, indifferent, uninterested
词性: adjective


inquisitorial:/ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːrɪəl/
adjective
审问官的；调查的；爱追根究底的
Relating to or resembling an inquisitor; tending to inquire closely, often in an overbearing or hostile manner.
例句：The inquisitorial tone of the interview made the applicant uncomfortable.  面试中审问般的语气让申请者感到不舒服。

例句：He adopted an inquisitorial approach to the problem, questioning everyone involved. 他对这个问题采取了追根究底的方法，询问了每个相关人员。

近义词：inquisitive, questioning, scrutinizing, probing
反义词：uninquiring, incurious
词性: adjective



词源：Late Middle English: from late Latin inquisitorius, from inquisitor ‘inquirer’, from inquirere (see inquire).
