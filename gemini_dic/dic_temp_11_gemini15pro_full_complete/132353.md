nondelegations: /ˌnɒndɛlɪˈɡeɪʃənz/ | n. | 不可委托，不授权限 | The principle of nondelegation doctrine prevents Congress from transferring its legislative power to another branch of government. |  Congress's power to declare war is a nondelegation example, meaning it cannot give this power to the President. | 近义词: non-transferability, reservation | 反义词: delegation, assignment | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
From non- + delegation.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "delegation" comes from the Latin word "delegare," meaning "to send, dispatch, or entrust."
