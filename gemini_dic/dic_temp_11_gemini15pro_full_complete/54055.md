disdaining:/dɪsˈdeɪnɪŋ/ | v. | 鄙视，蔑视 | Showing contempt or lack of respect; considering something beneath oneself. | She walked past him, disdaining to even acknowledge his presence. 她从他身边走过，甚至不屑于理会他的存在。| synonyms: scorning, despising, spurning | antonyms: admiring, respecting, revering | verb (present participle)

disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/ | n. v. | 鄙视，蔑视 |  A feeling of contempt or lack of respect; consider unworthy of one's notice. | He felt nothing but disdain for their behavior. 他对他们的行为只有蔑视。 | synonyms: contempt, scorn, derision | antonyms: respect, admiration, reverence | noun, verb

词源: From Old French *desdeignier*, from des- "away from" + deignier "to deign". Ultimately from Latin dignus "worthy".
