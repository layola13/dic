lezzy:/ˈlɛzi/| adj.|懒惰的，散漫的|Someone who is lezzy is lazy or unwilling to work.|She's so lezzy, she never helps with the chores. 她太懒了，从不帮忙做家务。|近义词：lazy, indolent, slothful|反义词:industrious, diligent, energetic|词性:形容词

词源:  "Lezzy" is a colloquial and informal variant of "lazy."  It likely arose through pronunciation shifts and shortening, similar to how other words develop informal variations.  The word "lazy" itself has a Germanic origin, tracing back to Middle English "lazi" and Old English "læs," meaning "weak" or "slow."
