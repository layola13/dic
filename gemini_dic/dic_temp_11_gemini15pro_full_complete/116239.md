mass:/mæs/| n. adj. v.|质量，大量，群众，弥撒|A large body of matter with no definite shape.  A large number of people or objects crowded together. The Christian ceremony commemorating the Last Supper. To come together in large numbers. |The mass of the Earth is about 5.97 × 10^24 kg. 地球的质量约为 5.97 × 10^24 千克。|近义词：bulk, weight, volume, crowd, multitude|反义词：minority, individual|词性:名词,形容词,动词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *masse*, from Latin massa "lump, kneaded dough, mass, dam," from Greek maza "barley cake, lump, mass," possibly of pre-Greek origin.
