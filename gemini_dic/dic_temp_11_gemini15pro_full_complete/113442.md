lynchers:/ˈlɪn.tʃər z/
noun
私刑者，处以私刑的人
People who participate in lynching, executing someone without due process of law.
The lynchers were never brought to justice. 那些处以私刑的人从未被绳之以法。
近义词:vigilantes, mob
反义词:law enforcers, protectors
词性:n.

lynchers:/ˈlɪn.tʃər z/
词源: From lynch (v.) + -er (agent suffix). Ultimately from Lynch's Law, referring to various extralegal punishments, possibly named after Charles Lynch (1736-1796), a Virginia planter and justice of the peace.
词性:n.
