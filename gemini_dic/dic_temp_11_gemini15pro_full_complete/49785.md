demonise: /ˈdiːmənaɪz/ | v. | 妖魔化，污魔化 |  to portray someone or something as wicked or threatening  |  They demonised the enemy in their propaganda. 他们在宣传中妖魔化了敌人。 | Near synonyms: vilify, denigrate, slander | Antonyms: praise, glorify, extol | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *demonisen*, from Old French demoniser, from Late Latin daemonizare "to be possessed by a demon," from Latin daemon "demon."  Ultimately from Ancient Greek δαίμων (daímōn, “deity, spirit”).
