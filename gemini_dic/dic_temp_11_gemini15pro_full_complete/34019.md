citizenries: /ˈsɪtɪzənriz/
n.
公民身份（集合）;全体公民;
The collective body of citizens of a nation or state.  A plural form of citizenry, referring to multiple groups of citizens.
The various citizenries of the world reacted differently to the global crisis. 世界各国的公民对全球危机作出了不同的反应。
The conference brought together representatives from the citizenries of several nations.  这次会议汇集了来自几个国家公民的代表。
近义词: citizens, populace, public
反义词:  (No direct antonym, could consider non-citizens, foreigners)
词性: Noun


Etymology:
From citizen + -ry.  "Citizen" comes from Old French "citeien," ultimately from Latin "civis" (city-dweller). The suffix "-ry" denotes a group or collective body.
