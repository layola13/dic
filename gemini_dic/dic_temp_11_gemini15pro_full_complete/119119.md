metropolitan:/ˌmɛtrəˈpɑlətən/ | adj. n. |大都市的，大都会的；大主教；|Relating to or characteristic of a metropolis, especially in respect of its greater size, wealth, sophistication, etc.  A bishop having authority over the bishops of a province.|例句：Tokyo is a metropolitan city. 东京是一座大都市。|例句：The metropolitan area includes the city and its surrounding suburbs.  大都市区域包括城市及其周围的郊区。|近义词：urban, cosmopolitan, municipal|反义词：rural, provincial|词性:形容词,名词

metropolitan:/ˌmɛtrəˈpɑlətən/ | n. |大城市居民，大主教 | An inhabitant of a metropolis. A bishop having authority over the bishops of a province. |例句：He is a metropolitan accustomed to the fast pace of life. 他是一个习惯了快节奏生活的大都市居民。|例句：The metropolitan presided over the important religious ceremony.  这位大主教主持了重要的宗教仪式。|近义词：city dweller, urbanite, archbishop (in some contexts)|反义词：country dweller, villager|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *metropolitanus*, from Greek μητροπολίτης (mētropolitikēs) "of or from the mother city," from μήτηρ (mētēr) "mother" + πόλις (polis) "city."  The sense of "archbishop" arose because the bishop of a metropolis was often the senior bishop of a province.
