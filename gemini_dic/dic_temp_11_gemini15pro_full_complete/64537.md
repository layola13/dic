errand:/ˈɛrənd/ (US), /ˈɛrənd/ (UK)| n.|差事，跑腿|A short trip that you make to do something, such as buying or delivering things.|例句：I have to run some errands this afternoon. 我今天下午要跑几个差事。|例句：She asked her son to do an errand for her. 她让她儿子帮她跑个腿。|近义词：task, chore, job|反义词: leisure, relaxation|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ærende (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *arundiō ("message, mission"). Cognate with Old Norse eyrindi ("errand, message").
