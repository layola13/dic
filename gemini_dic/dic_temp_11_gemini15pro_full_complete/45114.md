词源:
From Middle English *kukwold, from Old French cucuault, from cucu ("cuckoo"), referring to the cuckoo's habit of laying eggs in other birds' nests.  The term evolved to describe a man whose wife is unfaithful, implying he is unaware or powerless like the bird whose nest is invaded.
