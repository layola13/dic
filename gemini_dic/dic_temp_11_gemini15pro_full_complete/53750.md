discolour:/dɪsˈkʌlər/ /dɪsˈkʌlər/| v. |使变色，褪色|To change the colour of something, usually by making it paler or darker; to stain or tarnish.|The sunlight discoloured the curtains. 阳光使窗帘褪色了。|Synonyms: fade, stain, tarnish, bleach, discolor (US spelling)|Antonyms: brighten, enhance, colour, dye|verb
discolours:/dɪsˈkʌlərz/ /dɪsˈkʌlərz/| v. |(第三人称单数)使变色，褪色|Third person singular present tense form of "discolour".|The heat discolours the plastic.  高温使塑料变色。|Synonyms: fades, stains, tarnishes, bleaches, discolors (US spelling)|Antonyms: brightens, enhances, colours, dyes|verb
discoloured:/dɪsˈkʌlərd/ /dɪsˈkʌlərd/| adj. |变色的，褪色的|Having lost its original colour; stained or tarnished.|The old photographs were discoloured with age.  老照片因年代久远而褪色了。|Synonyms: faded, stained, tarnished, bleached, discolored (US spelling)|Antonyms: bright, vibrant, colourful, dyed|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (not, opposite of) + colour.  The prefix "dis-" indicates a reversal or negation of the meaning of "colour".
