mammy:/ˈmæmi/| n.|妈妈；(尤指黑人小孩称)保姆，奶妈|A term of endearment for a mother, or historically, a Black woman who worked as a nanny or housekeeper, often for a white family. This term is now considered offensive due to its association with slavery and racist stereotypes.|例句：The child ran to his mammy. 孩子跑向了他的妈妈。

例句：In the old South, many Black women worked as mammies for white families. 在旧时的南方，许多黑人妇女为白人家庭当奶妈。|近义词：mom, mother, nanny, housekeeper (historically)|反义词: None (The antonym would depend on the specific meaning used, e.g., child if referring to mother)|词性: noun
