ileus:/ˈɪliəs/| n. |肠梗阻|Obstruction of the bowel, characterized by the absence of peristalsis (the involuntary constriction and relaxation of the muscles of the intestine or another canal, creating wavelike movements that push the contents of the canal forward).  It can be caused by a variety of factors, including mechanical blockage (e.g., adhesions, tumors) or paralytic ileus (lack of intestinal muscle function).|The patient presented with symptoms of ileus, including abdominal pain, vomiting, and distension.|Synonyms: bowel obstruction, intestinal obstruction|Antonyms: peristalsis|noun

ileus:/ˈɪliəs/| n. |肠梗阻|A condition in which the normal movement of the bowels is blocked.  This blockage can be caused by physical obstructions like adhesions or tumors or functional problems like paralytic ileus, where the muscles of the intestines stop working properly. |After the surgery, the patient developed an ileus and required a nasogastric tube.|Synonyms: intestinal blockage, bowel obstruction|Antonyms: normal bowel function|noun
