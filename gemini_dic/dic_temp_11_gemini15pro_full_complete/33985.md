citadels:/ˈsɪtədəlz/| n. |城堡；要塞；堡垒|A fortress, typically on high ground, protecting or dominating a city.|The citadel overlooked the city, offering a strategic advantage in times of war. 城堡俯瞰着城市，在战争时期提供了战略优势。|Example: The ancient citadel still stands, a testament to the city's rich history. 这座古老的城堡依然屹立，证明了这座城市丰富的历史。|Synonyms: fortress, stronghold, bastion, fort|Antonyms: vulnerability, weakness|noun

citadel:/ˈsɪtədəl/| n. |中心；核心；大本营|A stronghold or center of power or influence.|The university is a citadel of learning. 这所大学是学习的中心。|Example:  Silicon Valley is considered a citadel of technological innovation. 硅谷被认为是技术创新的中心。|Synonyms: hub, center, core, headquarters|Antonyms: periphery, outskirts|noun
