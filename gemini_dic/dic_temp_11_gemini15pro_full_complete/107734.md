laow:/laʊ/ | n. | 老外（带有贬义）|  A derogatory slang term used primarily in Mandarin Chinese to refer to foreigners, especially white people. It carries connotations of othering, and sometimes even hostility or contempt. | Example:  Some expats feel uncomfortable when they hear the word "laow."  一些外籍人士听到“老外”这个词会感到不舒服。 | Synonym: foreigner (neutral), gweiren (Taiwanese, neutral) | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, as it's a slang term with complex connotations.  One could perhaps use "tongguo ren" (compatriot) for a contrasting concept.) | noun

那个老外很高。 (Nage laow hen gao.) That foreigner is tall.
这个老外会说中文。(Zhege laow hui shuo Zhongwen.) This foreigner can speak Chinese.
很多老外喜欢中国菜。(Hen duo laow xihuan Zhongguo cai.) Many foreigners like Chinese food.
不要叫他老外，这样不礼貌。(Bu yao jiao ta laow, zheyang bu limao.) Don't call him laow, it's impolite.
