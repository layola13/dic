disenchantment:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃæntmənt/ | n. | 失望，醒悟，不再着迷 | A feeling of disappointment about someone or something you previously respected or admired; disillusionment. |  Example 1:  Growing disenchantment with the government led to protests.  对政府日益增长的失望情绪导致了抗议活动。

Example 2: There's widespread disenchantment with the current education system.  人们对现行的教育制度普遍感到失望。 | Synonyms: disillusionment, disappointment, dissatisfaction | Antonyms:  enchantment, fascination, captivation | noun


disenchant:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃænt/ | vt. | 使清醒，使不再抱幻想 | To free from illusion or false belief; disillusion. | Example 1: The harsh realities of war disenchanted many young soldiers. 战争的残酷现实使许多年轻士兵不再抱有幻想。

Example 2:  She was quickly disenchanted with the glamorous world of fashion. 她很快就对光鲜亮丽的时尚界不再抱有幻想。 | Synonyms: disillusion, undeceive, disabuse | Antonyms: enchant, charm, captivate | verb
