duopolist: /djuˈɒpəlɪst/ (UK) /duˈɑːpəlɪst/ (US)| n.| 双头垄断者；寡头垄断者之一|One of two sellers in a duopoly, a market situation where two companies own all or nearly all of the market for a given product or service.|Example: The two duopolists controlled the entire telecommunications market. 这两家双头垄断者控制了整个电信市场。

Example:  As a duopolist, the company had significant influence on pricing. 作为双头垄断者，该公司对定价有重大影响。|近义词：oligopolist|反义词: competitor, monopolist|词性: noun

duopolists: /djuˈɒpəlɪsts/ (UK) /duˈɑːpəlɪsts/ (US)| n.| 双头垄断者；寡头垄断者们（duopolist的复数形式）|Two or more sellers in a duopoly. |Example: The duopolists engaged in fierce price wars.  这些双头垄断者进行了激烈的价格战。
