levirate: /ˈlɛvɪreɪt/ | n. | 父死后弟娶兄嫂制，兄死后弟娶嫂制 |  The custom of a man marrying his deceased brother's widow. This practice was common in ancient societies to ensure the continuation of the family line and the care of the widow and her children. |  The levirate marriage ensured that the deceased brother's property remained within the family.  父死后弟娶兄嫂制确保了已故兄弟的财产留在家族内部。

The levirate was a common practice in biblical times.  在圣经时代，父死后弟娶兄嫂制是一种常见的做法。 | 近义词: widow inheritance | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *lēvirātus, from Latin lēvir (“husband's brother”).
