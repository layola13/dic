feudalize:/ˈfjuːdəlaɪz/ | vt. | 封建化；使成为封地 | To make feudal; to reorganize (a system, society, etc.) on a feudal basis. | The Normans feudalized England after the conquest. 诺曼人征服英格兰后，将其封建化了。 |  The king feudalized the conquered territories, granting land to his loyal knights. 国王将征服的领土封建化，把土地赐予他忠诚的骑士。 | 近义词:  feudalise (British spelling), medievalize | 反义词: modernize, democratize | 词性: verb

feudalisation:/ˌfjuːdəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ | n. | 封建化 | The process of becoming feudal. | The feudalisation of England was a gradual process. 英格兰的封建化是一个渐进的过程。 | The rapid feudalisation of the region led to social unrest. 该地区的快速封建化导致了社会动荡。| 近义词: feudalism | 反义词: modernization | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *féodaliser*, from *féodal* (feudal) + *-iser* (suffix forming verbs of action or process).  Ultimately from Medieval Latin *feodalis*, from *feodum* (fief) + *-alis* (suffix forming adjectives).
