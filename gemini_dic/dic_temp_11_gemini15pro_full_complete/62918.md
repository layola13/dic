enlivenment:/ɪnˈlaɪvənmənt/| n.|活跃，生动，激励|The arrival of the clowns brought an enlivenment to the otherwise dull party. 小丑的到来给原本沉闷的派对增添了活力。|例句：The music provided a welcome enlivenment to the atmosphere.  音乐给气氛带来了受欢迎的活力。

The president's speech was an enlivenment to the nation.  总统的演讲鼓舞了全国人民。|近义词：animation, invigoration, stimulation|反义词：dullness, boredom, monotony|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From en- + liven + -ment.  "en-" is a prefix meaning "to cause to be," "to put into," or "to intensify." "liven" means "to make lively or spirited." "-ment" is a suffix forming nouns that denote an action, process, or result.
