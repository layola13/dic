flurrying: /ˈflɜːriɪŋ/ | adj. | 慌乱的，飞舞的 | Moving quickly and lightly in a confused way, like snowflakes in a flurry. | The flurrying leaves danced in the wind.  飞舞的树叶在风中飘荡。| 近义词: rushing, swirling, scattering | 反义词: calm, still | 词性: 形容词
flurry: /ˈflɜːri/ | n. | 一阵， flurry of | A sudden short period of activity or excitement. |  A flurry of activity filled the office as the deadline approached.  随着截止日期的临近，办公室里一片繁忙。| 近义词: burst, rush, spate | 反义词: lull, calm | 词性: 名词
flurry: /ˈflɜːri/ | v. |  使慌乱，使一阵忙乱 | To make (someone) flustered or confused. |  The sudden noise flurried the birds. 突然的噪音使鸟儿惊慌失措。| 近义词: fluster, confuse, agitate | 反义词: calm, soothe | 词性: 动词


词源: From the earlier *flurren, perhaps of imitative origin.  可能源于早期*flurren，或许是拟声词。
