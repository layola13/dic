disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/| n. vt.|厌恶，反感；使作呕|A feeling of strong disapproval and dislike, or a strong feeling of revulsion. |The smell of rotten eggs filled the air, causing disgust in everyone.  腐烂的鸡蛋味弥漫在空气中，让每个人都感到恶心。|近义词：revulsion, aversion, nausea, loathing|反义词：admiration, pleasure, delight|词性:名词,动词

disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/| vt.|使厌恶，使反感|To cause a feeling of strong disapproval and dislike, or a strong feeling of revulsion. |The violent scenes in the movie disgusted many viewers.  电影中的暴力场景让许多观众感到厌恶。|近义词：revolt, sicken, offend, nauseate|反义词：please, delight, attract|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desgouster*, from des- "dis-" + gouster "to taste," from Latin gustare "to taste."
