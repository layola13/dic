homotaxy:/ˌhɒməˈtæksi/ | n. | 同源异位 |  The correspondence or similarity of parts or organs acquired through independent evolutionary development and not by common descent.  It refers to structures that appear similar or have the same function but evolved separately in different lineages. |  Example 1: The wings of bats and birds are an example of homotaxy, as they evolved independently for flight.  蝙蝠和鸟类的翅膀是同源异位的一个例子，因为它们是独立进化而用于飞行的。

Example 2:  The streamlined body shape of sharks and dolphins is another example of homotaxy, resulting from similar environmental pressures.  鲨鱼和海豚的流线型体型是同源异位的另一个例子，这是由相似的环境压力造成的。 | 近义词: analogy, convergence | 反义词: homology | 词性: noun
