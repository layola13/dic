herd:/hɜːrd/ (US) /hɜːd/ (UK)| n. v. |兽群，人群；放牧|A large group of animals, especially hoofed mammals, that live together or are kept together as livestock; to gather or drive (animals or people) together.|
The shepherd herded the sheep into the pen. 牧羊人把羊群赶进羊圈。
The children were herded into the school bus. 孩子们被赶上了校车。

|反义词：individual, single|
|词性：noun, verb|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *heord, from Proto-Germanic *herdaz. Cognate with Old Saxon herda, Old Frisian herde, Middle Dutch herde, Dutch herder, Old High German hirti, German Hirte (“shepherd”), Gothic 𐌷𐌰𐌹𐍂𐌳𐌴𐌹𐍃 (hairdeis, “shepherd”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱerweh₂- (“to graze”).
