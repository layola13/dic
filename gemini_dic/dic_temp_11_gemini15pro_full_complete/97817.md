inkhorn:/ˈɪŋkhɔːrn/ | adj. n.|墨水瓶；学究气的；卖弄学问的|A container for ink, typically made of horn; pedantic or pretentiously learned language.|He used archaic, inkhorn terms that no one understood. 他使用了一些没人能懂的古旧的、学究气的词语。|Synonyms: pedantic, pompous, affected, pretentious|Antonyms: simple, natural, unaffected|adjective, noun

inkhorn:/ˈɪŋkhɔːrn/ | n.|墨水瓶；卖弄学问的人|A small bottle or container for holding ink. A person who uses or affects a pedantic style of writing or speaking.|The scholar kept his inkhorn close at hand. 学者把他的墨水瓶放在手边。|Synonyms: pedant, purist|Antonyms: commoner, layman|noun
