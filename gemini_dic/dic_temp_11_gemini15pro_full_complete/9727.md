antiutilitarianisms: /ˌæntijuːˌtɪlɪˈtɛəriənɪzəmz/ | n. | 反功利主义（的观点或学说） |  Philosophical doctrines opposed to utilitarianism, which reject the idea that the morality of an action is determined solely by its consequences and maximization of overall happiness. |  His antiutilitarianisms led him to believe that some actions are inherently wrong, regardless of their outcome. 他的反功利主义观点使他相信，有些行为本身就是错误的，无论其结果如何。| Synonyms: non-utilitarianism, deontology | Antonyms: utilitarianism | Noun
