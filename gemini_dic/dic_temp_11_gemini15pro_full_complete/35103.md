clots:/klɑːts/ (US), /klɒts/ (UK)| n. |血块，凝块|A thick mass of coagulated liquid, especially blood.|The doctor was concerned about the blood clots in his leg. 医生担心他腿上的血块。|近义词：coagulations, thrombi|反义词：(none, the opposite would be a lack of clotting or "thinning")|词性:noun


clot:/klɑːt/ (US), /klɒt/ (UK)| v. |凝结，使凝结|To form or cause to form clots.|His blood clotted quickly after the cut.  他的血液在割伤后迅速凝结。|近义词：coagulate, thicken|反义词：thin, dissolve|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clotten*, from Old English *clott*, akin to Middle Low German *klotte*, Dutch *klot*, German *Kloß* ("clod, lump").  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *klut- (“lump, ball”), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kleu- ("to stick, adhere").
