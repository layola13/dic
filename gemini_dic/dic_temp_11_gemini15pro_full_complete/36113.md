coercion:/koʊˈɜːrʒən/ | n. |强迫，胁迫 |The act of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats.|He claimed the confession was obtained under coercion. 他声称供词是在胁迫下获得的。|Synonyms: compulsion, force, duress, intimidation, pressure|Antonyms: persuasion, freedom, liberty|noun

coerce:/koʊˈɜːrs/ | vt. |强迫，胁迫 |To persuade someone forcefully to do something that they are unwilling to do.|The court heard that the businesswoman had been coerced into signing the contract. 法庭听取了这位女商人被胁迫签署合同的陈述。|Synonyms: compel, force, pressure, intimidate|Antonyms: persuade, encourage|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin coerciō, coerciōn-  "a restraining, repression," from Latin coercere "to restrain, confine, enclose; to repress, curb, control," from com- "together" (see com-) + arcēre "to enclose, confine, keep off" (source also of Greek arkein "to suffice, hold off," arktos "bear," literally "the one that confines the stars," i.e., the constellation Ursa Major).
