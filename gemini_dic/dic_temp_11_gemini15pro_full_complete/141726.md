oralism: /ˈɔːrəlɪzəm/ (US) /ˈɔːrəlɪzəm/ (UK)
n.
口语主义
A system of educating deaf people by using lip reading, speech, and mimicking mouth shapes, without using sign language.
Oralism emphasizes spoken language over sign language in the education of deaf children.  口语主义强调在聋童教育中使用口语而不是手语。
Many deaf educators advocate for a combination of oralism and sign language. 许多聋人教育家提倡口语和手语相结合的教育方式。
近义词: speech therapy, auditory-verbal therapy
反义词: manualism, sign language
词性: noun

词源: From Latin *ōrālis (“of the mouth”), from ōs (“mouth”) + -ism.
