mundaneness:/mʌnˈdeɪnnəs/
|n.| 平凡；世俗；单调
|The mundaneness of everyday life can be a source of both comfort and frustration.  日常生活的平凡既可以是舒适的来源，也可以是沮丧的来源。|
|例句：He found escape from the mundaneness of his job in writing poetry. 他在写诗中找到了逃避单调工作的途径。

|例句：The mundaneness of the task made it difficult to stay focused. 这项任务的单调性让人很难集中注意力。|
|近义词：ordinariness, commonplaceness, banality, routine, monotony|
|反义词： extraordinariness, excitement, novelty, uniqueness|
|词性: noun|


Etymology:
From mundane + -ness.  Mundane comes from Old French mondain, from Late Latin mundānus "belonging to the world," from Latin mundus "world, universe."
