cber:/ˈsiːbər/ | n. | 网络审查员; 网络评论员 |  A person employed to monitor and censor online content, often for a government or corporation.  This term is often used pejoratively, implying manipulation or suppression of information. | Example: The cbers quickly deleted any negative comments about the company.  网络审查员迅速删除了任何关于公司的负面评论。 | Example: Some believe cbers are stifling free speech online.  有些人认为网络评论员正在扼杀网上的言论自由。 | Synonym: censor, moderator, internet police | Antonym: free speech advocate, dissident | noun
