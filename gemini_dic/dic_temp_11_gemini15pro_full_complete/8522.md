anticausodic:/ˌæntikaʊˈzɑːdɪk/
adjective
反因果的，非因果的
Relating to or denoting a sequence of events in which the apparent cause occurs after the effect, typically in a narrative.  This often involves a manipulation of time or a subjective perspective that challenges traditional notions of causality.
The film employed an anticausodic narrative structure, revealing the murderer's identity before showing the crime itself.  这部电影采用了反因果叙事结构，在展示犯罪本身之前揭示了凶手的身份。
The anticausodic presentation of events in the novel created a sense of suspense and mystery. 小说中反因果的事件呈现方式营造了一种悬念和神秘感。
