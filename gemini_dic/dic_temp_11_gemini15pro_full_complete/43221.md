crackdowns:/ˈkrækdaʊnz/| n.|镇压，打击|A series of forceful actions taken to reduce or eliminate a problem, typically crime or dissent.|The government launched a series of crackdowns on illegal gambling. 政府发起了一系列打击非法赌博的行动。|近义词：clampdowns, suppression, repression|反义词:tolerance, leniency|名词

词源： "crackdown" comes from "crack down," which originated in American English in the early 20th century.  "Crack" in this sense means a sharp blow, like the cracking of a whip.  The image is of forceful action taken to suppress something.
