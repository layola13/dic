encroachment:/ɪnˈkroʊtʃmənt/ | n. |侵占，侵入，蚕食|An act of slowly beginning to cover more of an area, or of using something that belongs to someone else.  The gradual intrusion on a person's property, rights, etc.|例句：The encroachment of the sea on the land is a serious problem.  海水对陆地的侵蚀是一个严重的问题。|例句：The new shopping mall is an encroachment on valuable green space.  新的购物中心是对宝贵绿地的侵占。|近义词：intrusion, invasion, infringement|反义词：retreat, withdrawal|词性: noun

词源:
From Old French *encrochier "seize, usurp," from en- "in" + croc "hook, crook" (from Germanic).  The original sense was "to seize with a hook."
