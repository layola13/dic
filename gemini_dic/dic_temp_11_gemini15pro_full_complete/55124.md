disruptors:/dɪsˈrʌptərz/| n. |颠覆者，破坏者|Something or someone that interrupts an event, activity, or process by causing a disturbance or problem.  People or things that cause radical change in an industry or activity, especially by introducing new methods or technologies.|例句：The rise of online streaming services has been a major disruptor to the traditional television industry. 在线流媒体服务的兴起是对传统电视行业的重大颠覆。

例句：New technologies are disrupting traditional business models. 新技术正在颠覆传统的商业模式。|近义词：revolutionaries, innovators, game-changers, insurgents, radicals|反义词：conformists, traditionalists|词性:noun
