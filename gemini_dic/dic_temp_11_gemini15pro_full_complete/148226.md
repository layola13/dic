oversilentness: /ˈoʊvərˈsaɪləntnɪs/ | n. | 过分沉默，过度安静 |  The state of being excessively silent or quiet.  It implies an unnatural or unusual degree of silence, often suggesting withholding of information or an unwillingness to communicate. |  His oversilentness made everyone uncomfortable. 他的过分沉默让每个人都不舒服。|  The oversilentness of the witness aroused suspicion.  证人的过度沉默引起了怀疑。 | 近义词: reticence, taciturnity, reserve | 反义词: talkativeness, loquacity, volubility | 词性: noun

Etymology:
over- (prefix):  from Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi "over, above, beyond," from PIE *uper "over, above." Indicating excess or surpassing.
silent (adjective): from Old French silent, from Latin silent-, silens, present participle of silere "be still, be silent," of unknown origin.
-ness (suffix): from Old English -nes(s), forming nouns of state, condition, or quality.
