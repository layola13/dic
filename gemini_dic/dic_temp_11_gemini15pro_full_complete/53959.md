discreditable:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪtəbl/
adjective
丢脸的，不光彩的
Bringing disgrace or shame.  damaging to reputation.
His discreditable conduct led to his dismissal. 他的不光彩行为导致他被解雇。
Synonyms: shameful, disgraceful, dishonorable, ignominious, unworthy
Antonyms: creditable, honorable, respectable, admirable
词性: 形容词


discredit:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪt/
verb
使丢脸，败坏名声
To harm the reputation of.  To cause someone or something to seem dishonest or untrue.
The scandal discredited the politician.  这桩丑闻败坏了这位政治家的名声。
Synonyms: disgrace, shame, dishonor, tarnish, smear
Antonyms: credit, honor, praise, commend
词性: 动词


discredit:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪt/
noun
丢脸，耻辱
Loss or damage to reputation.  Lack of belief or trust.
The allegations brought discredit upon the entire organization. 这些指控给整个组织带来了耻辱。
Synonyms: disgrace, dishonor, shame, disrepute, opprobrium
Antonyms: credit, honor, prestige, esteem
词性: 名词


词源:
From Late Middle English *discredyten, from discrediten, from Old French des- + crediter (“to believe”), from Latin crēditus (“belief”), from crēdō.  The noun is from the verb.
