Acadians:/əˈkeɪdiənz/
noun
阿卡迪亚人
Descendants of French colonists who settled in Acadia (present-day Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, and Prince Edward Island) in the 17th and 18th centuries. Many were deported by the British in the Great Upheaval (Le Grand Dérangement).
The Acadians maintained their distinct culture and language despite facing hardship. 阿卡迪亚人尽管面临困境，仍然保持着独特的文化和语言。
Acadians celebrate their heritage with festivals and traditional music. 阿卡迪亚人通过节日和传统音乐来庆祝他们的遗产。

synonyms: Acadian French, French Canadians
antonyms: N/A
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From French Acadien, derived from Acadie (Acadia), possibly from the Mi'kmaq word *akatik meaning "place where things abound," or from the Italian navigator Giovanni da Verrazzano's use of the classical Greek name Arcadia, referring to a region in Greece known for its rustic beauty.
