outlandishly:/ˌaʊtˈlændɪʃli/
|adv.|古怪地；奇异地；异乎寻常地|in a bizarre and unconventional manner|He was dressed outlandishly in a bright pink suit. 他穿着一套亮粉色的西装，显得格外古怪。|
|例句：She behaved outlandishly at the party, singing loudly and dancing on the tables.  她在派对上的行为很古怪，大声唱歌还在桌子上跳舞。|
|例句：He decorated his apartment outlandishly, with zebra-striped walls and a chandelier made of antlers. 他把自己的公寓装饰得奇奇怪怪的，墙上是斑马条纹，还有一个用鹿角做的枝形吊灯。|
|近义词：bizarrely, eccentrically, strangely, unusually, oddly|
|反义词：normally, conventionally, ordinarily|
|词性：副词|


outlandish:/aʊtˈlændɪʃ/
|adj.|古怪的；奇异的；异乎寻常的|bizarre, strange, and unusual|Her outlandish outfit attracted a lot of attention. 她古怪的服装吸引了很多人的注意。|
|例句：His outlandish theories about the origin of the universe were met with skepticism. 他关于宇宙起源的古怪理论遭到了质疑。|
|例句：The outlandish costumes in the play added to the overall sense of absurdity.  戏剧中古怪的服装增加了整体的荒诞感。|
|近义词：bizarre, eccentric, strange, unusual, odd|
|反义词：normal, conventional, ordinary|
|词性：形容词|


词源:
From outland + -ish.  Outland originally referred to foreign lands, especially those considered remote or uncivilized.  The suffix "-ish" transforms it into an adjective meaning "characteristic of an outland."
