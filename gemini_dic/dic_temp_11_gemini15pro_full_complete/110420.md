like-to:/ˈlaɪk tuː/ | phrasal verb | 喜欢做... | To have a preference for doing something; to enjoy doing something. | I like to read books in my spare time. 我喜欢在空闲时间读书。| synonyms: enjoy, prefer, fancy | antonyms: dislike, hate | verb phrase

like-to:/ˈlaɪk tuː/ | phrasal verb | 想要做... | To want to do something; to have the intention of doing something. | I would like to visit Japan someday. 我想有一天去日本旅游。| synonyms: want to, wish to, intend to | antonyms: not want to, refuse to | verb phrase

Simple Present:
I like to swim every morning. 我喜欢每天早上游泳。
She likes to cook dinner for her family. 她喜欢为家人做晚餐。

Present Continuous:
I am liking to learn Spanish these days. 我最近喜欢学习西班牙语。(Note:  "Liking to" in present continuous is less common and might sound slightly unnatural.  "I'm enjoying learning Spanish" would be more natural.)
He is liking to play the guitar more and more. 他越来越喜欢弹吉他。(Same note as above.)

Present Perfect:
I have liked to paint since I was a child. 我从小就喜欢画画。
They have liked to travel to different countries. 他们一直喜欢去不同的国家旅行。

Present Perfect Continuous:
I have been liking to listen to classical music lately. 我最近一直喜欢听古典音乐。(Same note as Present Continuous)
She has been liking to exercise outdoors. 她一直喜欢在户外锻炼。(Same note as Present Continuous)

Simple Past:
I liked to play with dolls when I was young. 我小时候喜欢玩洋娃娃。
He liked to read comic books. 他喜欢看漫画书。

Past Continuous:
I was liking to go to the park every day last summer. (Less common - "I was enjoying going..." is preferred) 去年夏天我每天都喜欢去公园。
She was liking to watch movies with her friends. (Less common) 她喜欢和朋友们一起看电影。

Past Perfect:
I had liked to draw before I started playing the piano. 在我开始弹钢琴之前，我喜欢画画。
They had liked to go hiking until one of them injured their ankle. 在他们其中一人扭伤脚踝之前，他们喜欢去远足。

Past Perfect Continuous:
I had been liking to learn French before I switched to Spanish.  (Less common) 在我改学西班牙语之前，我一直喜欢学习法语。
He had been liking to play basketball until he hurt his knee. (Less common) 他一直喜欢打篮球，直到他伤了膝盖。


Simple Future:
I will like to try that new restaurant. 我会想去那家新餐馆试试。 (Expressing future intention is better conveyed with "I'd like to try..." or "I want to try...")
She will like to go to the beach this weekend. 她周末会想去海滩。 (Again, "She'll want to go..." is more natural)


Future Continuous:
I will be liking to spend time with my family next week. (Less common - "I'll be enjoying spending time..." is preferred) 下周我会喜欢和我的家人待在一起。
They will be liking to visit their grandparents. (Less common) 他们会喜欢去看望他们的祖父母。

Future Perfect:
By next year, I will have liked living in this city for five years. 到明年，我就喜欢在这个城市生活五年了。(This is awkward.  "By next year, I will have lived in this city for five years and liked it" is better.)
By the time he finishes school, he will have liked studying biology for many years. (Also awkward.  "By the time he finishes school, he will have studied biology for many years and liked it" is better.)

Future Perfect Continuous:
By next month, I will have been liking to practice yoga for a year. (Less common and awkward - rephrase as "By next month, I will have been practicing yoga for a year and enjoying it.") 到下个月，我就喜欢练习瑜伽一年了。
By the time she graduates, she will have been liking to play the violin for ten years. (Less common and awkward) 到她毕业的时候，她就会喜欢拉小提琴十年了。
