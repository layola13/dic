co-op:/ˈkəʊ ɒp/ (US), /ˈkəʊ ɒp/ (UK)| n. |合作社，合作公寓|A business or housing organization owned and run jointly by its members, who share the profits or benefits.|例句：My parents live in a co-op apartment building. 我父母住在一个合作公寓楼里。

例句：We buy our groceries from a local farmers' co-op. 我们从当地的农民合作社购买食品杂货。|近义词：cooperative, collective, commune|反义词:corporation, conglomerate|词性:名词


co-oped:/koʊˈɑːpt/ (US), /kəʊˈɒpt/ (UK)| vt. |吸收，接纳（新成员）；借用，挪用|To adopt (an idea or policy) for one's own use, especially in a way that distorts its original meaning; to appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members.|例句：The political party co-opted several popular policies from its rivals. 该政党借用了其竞争对手的一些受欢迎的政策。

例句：They co-opted her onto the committee. 他们邀请她加入了委员会。|近义词：appropriate, adopt, incorporate, enlist|反义词:reject, exclude|词性:动词
