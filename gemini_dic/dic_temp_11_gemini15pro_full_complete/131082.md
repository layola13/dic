nonbelligerency:/ˌnɒnˌbɛlɪˈdʒərənsi/ | n. |不交战状态，中立 |The state of not being at war, especially the state of a nation that takes no part in a war between other nations, but continues normal trade relations with them. |  Switzerland maintained a policy of nonbelligerency throughout World War II.  瑞士在整个二战期间都保持着不交战的状态。

The country declared its nonbelligerency in the conflict. 该国宣布在冲突中保持中立。 |近义词：neutrality, nonparticipation |反义词：belligerency, hostility | 词性: noun


nonbelligerent:/ˌnɒnˌbɛlɪˈdʒərənt/ | adj. |不交战的，中立的 |Not engaging in war or hostilities; neutral. |  The nonbelligerent nation continued to trade with both sides of the conflict.  这个不交战的国家继续与冲突双方进行贸易。

Despite the surrounding conflict, the small nation remained nonbelligerent. 尽管周围冲突不断，这个小国仍然保持中立。|近义词：neutral, uninvolved |反义词：belligerent, hostile | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + belligerent.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Belligerent" comes from the Latin "belligerant-" (present participle of belligerare "to wage war"), from bellum "war" + gerere "to wage, carry on."
