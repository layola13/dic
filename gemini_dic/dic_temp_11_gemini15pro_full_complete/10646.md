appropriating: /əˈprəʊprɪeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 挪用；占用；盗用；拨(专款) | Taking something for one's own use, typically without the owner's permission or improperly. |  He was accused of appropriating company funds. 他被指控挪用公司资金。 |  seizing, taking, acquiring, confiscating, commandeering, annexing | restoring, returning, relinquishing | verb

appropriate: /əˈprəʊprɪət/ | adj. | 适当的；合适的 | Suitable or proper in the circumstances. |  Is this film appropriate for children? 这部电影适合儿童观看吗？| suitable, proper, fitting, apt, relevant | inappropriate, unsuitable, improper | adjective

appropriate: /əˈprəʊprɪeɪt/ | v. | 拨(专款)；占用 | Devote (money or assets) to a special purpose. | Congress appropriated $5 million for the project. 国会为该项目拨款500万美元。 | allocate, assign, earmark, allot |  withhold, refuse | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin appropriatus, past participle of appropriare "to make one's own," from Latin ad- "to" + proprius "one's own."
