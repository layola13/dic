iron-clad:/ˈaɪərnˌklæd/ | adj. | 装甲的；坚不可摧的；严格的 | Covered or protected with iron plates; very strong and difficult to break or change. | Example 1: The ship had iron-clad sides. 这艘船有铁甲板。

Example 2:  They had an iron-clad agreement. 他们有一个牢不可破的协议。 | Near synonyms: armored, invincible, unbreakable, rigorous | Near antonyms: vulnerable, weak, flexible | Adjective


iron-clad:/ˈaɪərnˌklæd/ | n. | 铁甲舰 | A warship covered with iron plates. (This noun usage is less common now.) | Example 1: The iron-clad dominated the seas during the late 19th century. 铁甲舰在19世纪后期统治着海洋。


Example 2: The battle involved several iron-clads. 这场战斗涉及几艘铁甲舰。| Near synonyms:  battleship, warship | Near antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a type of ship) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "iron" + "clad" (past participle of "clothe," meaning to cover).  The term gained prominence during the mid-19th century with the development of ironclad warships.
