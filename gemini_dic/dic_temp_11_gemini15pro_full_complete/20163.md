bipolarizes:/baɪˈpoʊləˌraɪzɪz/
|v.|使...两极分化|To cause something to divide into two sharply contrasting groups or opinions.|The debate bipolarizes the community, with strong opinions on both sides. 这场辩论使社区两极分化，双方都有强烈的意见。|divides, polarizes|unifies, unites|verb

bipolarized:/baɪˈpoʊləˌraɪzd/
|adj.|两极分化的|Divided into two sharply contrasting groups or opinions.|The political landscape has become increasingly bipolarized in recent years. 近年来，政治格局日益两极分化。|divided, polarized|unified, united|adjective

bipolarizing:/baɪˈpoʊləˌraɪzɪŋ/
|adj.|导致两极分化的|Causing something to divide into two sharply contrasting groups or opinions.|The bipolarizing effect of social media is a concern for many. 社交媒体的两极分化效应是许多人关心的问题。|divisive, polarizing|unifying, uniting|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From bi- (two) + polar (having two poles) + -ize (to make or become).  The word "polarize" comes from the late 18th century, referring to the physical properties of light.  The metaphorical sense of creating opposing viewpoints emerged in the mid-19th century.
