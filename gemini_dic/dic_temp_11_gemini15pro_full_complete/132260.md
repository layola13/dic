nondecorousnesses:/nɒndɪˈkɔːrəsnəsɪz/
n.
lack of decorum; impropriety; unseemliness
Lack of good taste and propriety; improper behavior.  The plural form of nondecorousness.
Her nondecorousnesses at the formal dinner embarrassed her family.  她在正式晚宴上的不得体行为让她的家人感到尴尬。
Synonyms: improprieties, indecorums, indecencies, unseemliness
Antonyms: decorum, propriety, seemliness
noun


Etymology:
From non- + decorous + -ness + -es (plural suffix).  Decorous ultimately comes from the Latin decōrus meaning "becoming, fitting, proper."
