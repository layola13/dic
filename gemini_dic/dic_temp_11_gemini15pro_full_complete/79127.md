gladiatorship:/ˈɡlædiˌeɪtərʃɪp/| n. |角斗士身份；角斗士制度|The gladiatorship was a brutal form of entertainment in ancient Rome.  古罗马的角斗士制度是一种残酷的娱乐形式。|The rise and fall of gladiatorship mirrored the rise and fall of the Roman Empire. 角斗士制度的兴衰反映了罗马帝国的兴衰。|近义词：combat, fighting, arena|反义词:peace, pacifism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *gladiator* (swordsman, fighter in public games) + *-ship* (suffix forming abstract nouns).  The word *gladiator* is derived from *gladius*, meaning "sword".
