expiate:/ˈɛkspiˌeɪt/| v. |赎罪，弥补，补偿|To atone for (guilt or sin), as by suffering or doing good deeds.|He expiated his crime by serving a long prison sentence. 他通过服长期徒刑来赎罪。|近义词：atone for, make amends for, redeem, redress|反义词：commit, sin|词性:动词

expiated:/ˈɛkspiˌeɪted/| v. (past participle) |已赎罪的，已补偿的|Having atoned for (guilt or sin).|His sins were expiated through years of charitable work.  他多年的慈善工作赎清了他的罪孽。|近义词：atoned, redeemed|反义词：unatoned, unredeemed|词性:动词(过去分词)

expiation:/ˌɛkspiˈeɪʃən/| n. |赎罪，补偿|The act of making amends or atonement for guilt or sin.|He sought expiation for his past misdeeds. 他寻求对他过去不端行为的赎罪。|近义词：atonement, redemption, redress|反义词：sin, transgression|词性:名词
