antipathists:/ænˈtɪpəθɪsts/ | n. | 反感者；厌恶者 |  A person who feels antipathy; someone who strongly dislikes something or someone. |  The antipathists of the new policy staged a protest.  新政策的反感者举行了抗议。 |  opponents, adversaries, antagonists |  supporters, proponents, advocates | noun

antipathy:/ænˈtɪpəθi/ | n. | 反感；厌恶 | A deep aversion or dislike. | He felt a strong antipathy towards his boss. 他对他的老板感到强烈的反感。 | aversion, dislike, hostility |  affection, fondness, liking | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Ancient Greek ἀντιπάθεια (antipátheia, “mutual dislike, aversion”), from ἀντί (antí, “against, opposite”) + πάθος (páthos, “feeling, suffering, passion”).
