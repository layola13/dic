dither:/ˈdɪðər/| v. n.|犹豫，踌躇；抖动，颤动|To be indecisive; to hesitate between two or more choices. Also, to vibrate or tremble.|She dithered over which dress to wear. 她犹豫着不知道该穿哪件衣服。|Synonyms: hesitate, waver, vacillate|Antonyms: decide, resolve, determine|verb

dither:/ˈdɪðər/| n.|犹豫，踌躇；抖动，颤动|A state of indecision or trepidation.|She was in a dither about the upcoming exam. 她对即将到来的考试感到焦虑不安。|Synonyms: hesitation, indecision, vacillation|Antonyms: decision, resolution, determination|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *didderen*, frequentative of an unrecorded verb *diden*, probably of imitative origin.
