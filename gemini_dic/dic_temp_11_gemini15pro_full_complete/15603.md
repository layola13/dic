bailer:/ˈbeɪlər/| n.|保释人; 舀水的人或物|A person who provides bail for someone; a person or thing that bails (water).|The bailer paid the bond to release the defendant. 保释人支付了保释金释放了被告。|近义词:bondsman (for a person), scoop, dipper (for a thing)|反义词: N/A|noun
bailer:/ˈbeɪlər/| n.|舀水工具|A bucket or other container used for bailing water out of a boat. |The bailer was essential for keeping the small boat afloat.  舀水工具对于保持小船漂浮至关重要。|近义词:scoop, dipper|反义词: N/A|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From bail (v.) "to empty (water) from a boat," from Old French bailler "to give, deliver, control," from Latin bajulare "to bear a burden" (related to bajulus "porter").  The sense of "release on bail" comes from the notion of "delivering" someone from custody.
