colour-blind:/ˈkʌlə blaɪnd/ (US) /ˈkʌlə blaɪnd/ (UK)| adj.|色盲的|Unable to distinguish certain colours, especially red and green.|例句：My uncle is colour-blind and can't tell the difference between red and green. 我叔叔是色盲，分不清红色和绿色。

例句：Being colour-blind can make it difficult to choose matching clothes. 色盲会使选择搭配衣服变得困难。


|近义词：color-deficient, Daltonian|反义词:N/A|词性:adjective


colour-blindness:/ˈkʌlə ˈblaɪndnəs/ (US) /ˈkʌlə ˈblaɪndnəs/ (UK)| n.|色盲|The inability to distinguish certain colours, especially red and green.|例句：Colour-blindness is usually inherited. 色盲通常是遗传的。

例句：Tests for colour-blindness are often given to drivers.  司机经常要做色盲测试。


|近义词：color deficiency, Daltonism|反义词:N/A|词性:noun
