intimidatory:/ɪnˈtɪmɪdəˌtɔːri/| adj.|恐吓的，令人畏惧的|Frightening, especially in order to make someone do what you want.|His large size and booming voice were intimidatory.|近义词:threatening, menacing, daunting|反义词:reassuring, comforting|词性:形容词

intimidate:/ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪt/| vt.|恐吓，威胁|To frighten or threaten someone, usually in order to persuade them to do something that you want them to do.|They were intimidated into accepting a pay cut.|近义词:threaten, menace, browbeat|反义词:encourage, reassure|词性:动词

intimidation:/ɪnˌtɪmɪˈdeɪʃn/| n.|恐吓，威胁|The action of intimidating someone, or the state of being intimidated.|The government uses intimidation to suppress dissent.|近义词:threat, menace, coercion|反义词:encouragement, reassurance|词性:名词
