balefully:/ˈbeɪlfəli/| adv.|险恶地；不祥地；恶狠狠地|In a threatening or menacing way; expressing hatred or evil intent.|He stared balefully at his rival. 他恶狠狠地盯着他的对手。|Synonyms: menacingly, malevolently, malignantly, ominously, sinisterly|Antonyms: benignly, kindly, gently|词性:副词

balefulness:/ˈbeɪlfʊlnəs/| n.|险恶；凶兆|The quality or state of being baleful; a menacing or threatening nature.|The balefulness of the storm frightened the children. 暴风雨的凶险吓坏了孩子们。|Synonyms: malevolence, malignancy, ominousness, sinisterness|Antonyms: benignity, kindness, gentleness|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *baleful*, from bale (n.) "evil, woe, misery," + -ful.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *balwaz* "evil, destruction, torment," from Proto-Indo-European *bhol- "to strike."
