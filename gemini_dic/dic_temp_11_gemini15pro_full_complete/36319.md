cohort:/ˈkoʊhɔːrt/ (US) /ˈkəʊhɔːt/ (UK)| n.|同伙，一群人，同期组|A cohort is a group of people who share a common characteristic, typically their age or the period of time they experienced something.  It can also refer to a companion or supporter, especially in a derogatory sense.|例句：The 1990 birth cohort is entering the workforce. 1990年出生的一群人正在进入职场。

例句：The marketing campaign targeted the millennial cohort. 这场营销活动的目标人群是千禧一代。|近义词：group, band, company, generation, peer group|反义词:individual, loner|词性:noun

cohort:/ˈkoʊhɔːrt/ (US) /ˈkəʊhɔːt/ (UK)| n.|支持者，同伴（常含贬义）|A cohort can also mean a companion or supporter, often in a derogatory context, implying a close associate in questionable activities.|例句：The politician and his cohorts were accused of corruption.  这位政治家和他的同伙被指控腐败。

例句：She arrived with her usual cohort of hangers-on. 她带着她那一群通常的追随者来了。|近义词：accomplice, associate, crony|反义词:opponent, adversary|词性:noun

词源：From French cohorte, from Latin cohors (genitive cohortis) "enclosure, cohort," from com- "together" (see com-) + hortus "garden" (see yard (n.)). Originally a division of the Roman army, one-tenth of a legion. The sense of "companion, associate" is from 1670s.
