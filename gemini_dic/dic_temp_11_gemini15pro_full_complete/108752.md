learnedly:/ˈlɜːrnɪdli/ | adv. | 博学地；学识渊博地；卖弄学问地 |  In a manner that displays knowledge, often excessively or ostentatiously. | He spoke learnedly about the history of philosophy. 他博学地谈论了哲学史。| 近义词：eruditely, scholarly, knowingly | 反义词：ignorantly, unscholarly | 词性: 副词

词源: From "learned" (meaning "having or showing much knowledge") + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).  The word "learned" itself comes from Old English "leornod," past participle of "leornian" (to learn).
