alanine:/ˈæləˌniːn/ | n. | 丙氨酸 | A nonessential amino acid, one of the 20 building blocks of protein. It is synthesized in the body and does not need to be obtained from food.  It is involved in energy production and immune function. |  Alanine plays a role in glucose-alanine cycle. 丙氨酸在葡萄糖-丙氨酸循环中起作用。| Synonyms: Ala (abbreviation) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms for a specific amino acid) | noun
