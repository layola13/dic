burns:/bɜːrnz/ (BrE) /bɝːnz/ (AmE)| n. v. (burns, burning, burnt/burned)|烧伤；灼伤；燃烧；（burns）伯恩斯（姓氏）|

* **Noun (n.):**  injury to the skin or other organic tissue caused by fire, heat, chemicals, electricity, or radiation.  Also can refer to a Scottish surname.
* **Verb (v.):** be on fire; produce flames and heat. Can also mean to injure with fire or heat.


**English Explanation (as noun):**

* A burn can range from mild redness to severe charring of the skin and deeper tissues.  Burns are classified according to their depth and severity (first-degree, second-degree, third-degree).  "Burns" can also be a plural noun referring to multiple instances of this injury. As a surname, it's a common Scottish name.

**English Explanation (as verb):**

* To burn means to undergo combustion, producing heat and flames. It can also mean to damage or destroy something with fire or heat. The different forms of the verb are "burn" (present), "burning" (present participle), "burnt" or "burned" (past tense and past participle).  "Burnt" is more common in British English, while "burned" is more prevalent in American English.

**Example (noun):**
He suffered severe burns in the fire.  他在火灾中受了严重的烧伤。

She treated the minor burns with aloe vera. 她用芦荟治疗轻微烧伤。

**Example (verb):**
The fire burns brightly. 火烧得很旺。

She burnt the toast. 她把吐司烤焦了。

The wood burns easily. 木头容易燃烧。


**Synonyms:**
* **(n.)** scorch, scald, sear, char, blister
* **(v.)** blaze, ignite, incinerate, combust, singe

**Antonyms:**
* **(v.)** extinguish, douse, quench


**Etymology:**
From Old English *byrnan*, *bærnan* "to burn," from Proto-Germanic *brennanan*, from Proto-Indo-European *bhre-n- "to shine, glow."
