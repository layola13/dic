ill-mannered:/ˌɪlˈmænərd/ | adj. | 粗鲁的，无礼的 | Having bad manners; rude. |  He was criticized for his ill-mannered behavior at the dinner party.  他因在晚宴上的粗鲁行为而受到批评。| Synonyms: rude, impolite, disrespectful, discourteous, uncivil | Antonyms: polite, courteous, respectful, well-mannered | adjective

Etymology:
From ill (bad) + mannered (having manners).  The prefix "ill-" signifies "bad" or "poorly," and "mannered" refers to having or displaying manners.  Therefore, "ill-mannered" literally means having bad manners.
