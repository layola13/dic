centrist:/ˈsɛntrɪst/| n. adj.|中间派，中间派的|A person who holds moderate political views, especially one who avoids extreme left-wing or right-wing doctrines.  A centrist position or policy.|例句：The candidate positioned himself as a centrist to appeal to a wider range of voters.  这位候选人将自己定位为中间派，以吸引更广泛的选民。

例句：The party's centrist policies have alienated some of its more radical supporters. 该党的中间派政策疏远了一些更激进的支持者。|近义词：moderate, neutral|反义词：extremist, radical|词性:名词，形容词

Etymology: From center + -ist. Center from French centre, from Latin centrum, from Greek kentron "sharp point, stationary point of a pair of compasses, center".
