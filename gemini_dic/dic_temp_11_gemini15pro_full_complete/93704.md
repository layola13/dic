imitatorship:/ˈɪmɪteɪtərʃɪp/| n. |模仿；模仿的才能；效颦|The act or practice of imitating someone or something, especially their behavior or mannerisms.  The ability to imitate.|His imitatorship of famous actors is remarkable. 他对著名演员的模仿令人惊叹。|近义词：imitation, mimicry, impersonation|反义词：originality, authenticity|词性:noun

Etymology:
From imitate + -or + -ship.  Imitate comes from the Latin imitatus, the past participle of imitari "to copy, portray, imitate," from the noun imago "image, likeness."
