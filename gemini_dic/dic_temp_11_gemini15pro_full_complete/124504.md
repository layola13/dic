moulting:/ˈmoʊltɪŋ/ (US), /ˈməʊltɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |蜕皮; 换羽|The process of shedding old feathers, hair, or skin to make way for new growth.|The snake is moulting its skin. 这条蛇正在蜕皮。|Synonyms: shedding, sloughing|Antonyms: growing|noun

moulting:/ˈmoʊltɪŋ/ (US), /ˈməʊltɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |蜕皮的; 换羽的|Relating to or undergoing the process of moulting.|The moulting bird looked scruffy.  正在换羽的鸟看起来很邋遢。|Synonyms: shedding, sloughing|Antonyms: growing|adjective

moult:/moʊlt/ (US), /məʊlt/ (UK)| v. |蜕皮; 换羽|To shed old feathers, hair, or skin to make way for new growth.|The bird moults its feathers once a year. 这只鸟每年换一次羽毛。|Synonyms: shed, slough|Antonyms: grow|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mutian, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch muiten and German mausen ‘to moult’.
