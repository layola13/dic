distemper:/dɪˈstɛmpər/ | n. | 混乱，疾病，坏脾气 | A state of mental or physical disorder; illness, especially of dogs or cats.  A bad mood or sullen state of mind. | The dog was suffering from distemper. 这只狗得了犬瘟热。 | Synonyms: ailment, disease, malady, sickness, disorder, chaos, confusion, ill humor, bad mood | Antonyms: health, order, harmony, good humor, good mood | noun

distemper:/dɪˈstɛmpər/ | vt. | 使混乱，使激动，扰乱 | To disturb the composure or peace of; to agitate or ruffle.  To mix (pigments) with glue, size, or egg instead of oil. | The news distempered her calm. 这消息扰乱了她的平静。 | Synonyms: disturb, upset, agitate, ruffle, unsettle | Antonyms: calm, soothe, pacify, settle | verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old French *destemprer* "to upset the balance of humors," from *des-* "away" + *temprer* "to temper, moderate," from Latin *temperare* "to mix in due proportion, qualify, regulate." The sense of "illness" developed from the notion of a bodily imbalance.
