circumlocutionist: /ˌsɜːrkəmləˈkjuːʃənɪst/ /ˌsɝːkəmləˈkjuːʃənɪst/
n.
迂回说法者，冗长说话者
A person who uses circumlocution; someone who speaks or writes in a roundabout way, using many words where fewer would do.
The politician was a known circumlocutionist, rarely answering a question directly.  这位政客是个出了名的迂回说话者，很少直接回答问题。
He was such a circumlocutionist that even a simple request took several minutes to express. 他说话如此拐弯抹角，即使一个简单的请求也要花几分钟才能表达出来。
近义词:  prolix speaker, verbose speaker, rambler
反义词:  concise speaker, succinct speaker
词性: noun

circumlocution: /ˌsɜːrkəmləˈkjuːʃən/ /ˌsɝːkəmləˈkjuːʃən/
n.
迂回说法，冗长
The use of many words where fewer would do, especially in a deliberate attempt to be vague or evasive.
His circumlocution made it difficult to understand his true intentions. 他的迂回说法让人难以理解他的真实意图。
She avoided a direct answer through skillful circumlocution. 她通过巧妙的迂回说法避免了直接回答。
近义词: periphrasis, verbosity, wordiness
反义词:  conciseness, brevity, directness
词性: noun

词源: From Latin circumlocutio, from circum- ‘around’ + locutio, from loqui ‘to speak’.
