galvanic:/ɡælˈvænɪk/| adj.|電流的，電鍍的，驚人的|Of or relating to direct current electricity especially when produced by chemical action.  Producing or producing by galvanism.  Suddenly and excitingly impressive.|例句：The galvanic cell generated a steady current. 電流電池產生了穩定的電流。

例句：The news had a galvanic effect on the crowd.  這消息對人群產生了驚人的影響。|近義詞：electric, electrical, shocking, startling, electrifying|反義詞:calming, soothing|詞性:形容詞

Etymology->
From Italian *galvanico*, from the surname of Luigi Galvani + *-ico*.  Galvani's experiments with frogs' legs led to the discovery of bioelectricity.  The term "galvanic" came to be associated with direct current electricity produced by chemical action.
