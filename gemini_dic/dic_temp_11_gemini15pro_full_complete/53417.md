disannuller:/ˌdɪsəˈnʌlər/ | n. | 取消者，废除者 |  A person or thing that disannuls something, especially a law or marriage. | The king acted as the disannuller of the unpopular law. 国王废除了不受欢迎的法律。| 近义词：abrogator, nullifier, revoker | 反义词: ratifier, confirmer | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From dis- + annul + -er.  "Dis-" is a prefix meaning "apart," "asunder," "reversal," or "negation." "Annul" comes from the Old French "anuller," from Latin "annullare," meaning "to reduce to nothing, to obliterate."  "-er" is a suffix denoting an agent or one who performs an action.
