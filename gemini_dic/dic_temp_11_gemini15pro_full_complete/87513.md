heterodimer:/ˈhɛtərəʊˌdaɪmər/ (US) /ˈhɛtərəʊˌdaɪmər/ (UK)| n. |异二聚体|A protein composed of two different polypeptide chains.|The receptor is a heterodimer composed of two distinct subunits.|近义词:oligomer|反义词:monomer|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From hetero- (different) + dimer (a molecule or molecular complex consisting of two identical molecules linked together).  Hetero- comes from Greek héteros "other, another, different". Dimer comes from Greek di- "two" + meros "part".
