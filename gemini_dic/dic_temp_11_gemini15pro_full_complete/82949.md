guncher:/ˈɡʌntʃər/ | n. |关切人; 原住民（尤指加那利群岛的）|A native or inhabitant, especially of the Canary Islands.  Specifically refers to the aboriginal inhabitants of the Canary Islands. |The museum exhibit featured artifacts from the ancient Guanches, the gunchers of Tenerife.  博物馆展出了古代关切人的文物，他们是特内里费岛的原住民。| Near Synonyms: native, inhabitant, aborigine |Antonyms: foreigner, outsider, immigrant | noun

guncher:/ˈɡʌntʃər/ | n. | (rare, slang, sometimes offensive) A term of endearment, often used ironically or sarcastically. |"Alright, guncher, let's get this show on the road."  “好了，老兄，我们开始吧。” | Near Synonyms: mate, pal, buddy (when used as a term of endearment) | Antonyms: enemy, foe (when used ironically or sarcastically) | noun
