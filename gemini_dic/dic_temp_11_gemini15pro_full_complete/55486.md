distractibilities: /dɪˌstræktɪˈbɪlɪtiz/
|n.|分心，注意力分散|The state of being easily distracted; the tendency to be easily drawn away from one's focus of attention.  A person's susceptibility to distractions.|Her distractibilities make it difficult for her to study. 她容易分心，很难学习。|
|近义词：inattentiveness, distractability, short attention span|
|反义词：focus, concentration, attentiveness|
|词性: noun (plural)|


distractible: /dɪˈstræktəbl/
|adj.|易分心的，注意力不集中的|Easily distracted; having one's attention readily drawn away from the matter at hand.|He's very distractible and has trouble focusing on his work. 他很容易分心，无法集中精力工作。|
|近义词：inattentive, restless, fidgety|
|反义词：focused, attentive, concentrated|
|词性: adjective|


distract: /dɪˈstrækt/
|v.|使分心，转移(注意力)|To draw someone's attention away from something; to divert.|The noise from the street distracted me from my reading. 街上的噪音使我无法专心读书。|
|近义词：divert, sidetrack, perturb|
|反义词：focus, concentrate|
|词性: verb|


词源: From Late Latin *dīstractus*, past participle of dīstrahere (“to pull apart, divide, distract”), from dis- (“apart, away”) + trahere (“to draw, pull”).
