ill-disposedly:/ˌɪl dɪˈspəʊzɪdli/
adverb
恶意地，不怀好意地
in an unwilling or unfriendly manner
He looked at her ill-disposedly. 他不怀好意地看着她。
Synonyms: unkindly, malevolently, hostilely, grudgingly, resentfully
Antonyms: kindly, benevolently, amicably, favorably

Etymology:
From ill-disposed + -ly.  "Ill-disposed" comes from "ill" (bad, harmful) and "disposed" (inclined, having a tendency).  "Disposed" itself comes from Old French "disposer" (to arrange, order).
