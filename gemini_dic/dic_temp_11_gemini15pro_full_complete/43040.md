cowherd:/ˈkaʊhɜːrd/ (US), /ˈkaʊhɜːd/ (UK)| n. |牧牛人，牛仔|A person, often employed, who tends cows.|The cowherd led the cattle to the pasture. 牧牛人把牛群带到牧场。|Example:  The lonely cowherd spent his days whistling to the cows.  孤独的牧牛人整天对着牛群吹口哨。|Synonyms: herdsman, drover, stockman|Antonyms: None (occupation, no direct opposite)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cūhyrde (attested as dative plural cūhyrdum), from cū (cow) + hyrde (herder).  Cognate with German Kuhhirt, Dutch koeherder.
