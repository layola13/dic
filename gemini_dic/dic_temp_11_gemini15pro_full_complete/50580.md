derailed:/ˌdiːˈreɪld/ | adj. | 脱轨的，出轨的；偏离正轨的 |  Forced off the track; diverted from its intended course. Figuratively, it means thrown off course or disrupted. | The train was derailed by a landslide. 火车被山体滑坡导致脱轨。 | The project was derailed by unexpected budget cuts.  这个项目因意外的预算削减而搁浅。 | synonyms: sidetracked, disrupted, obstructed, thwarted | antonyms: on track, progressing, smooth, uninterrupted | adjective

derailed:/ˌdiːˈreɪld/ | v. |  (使)脱轨，(使)出轨；阻挠，破坏 |  To cause (a train or other vehicle) to leave its tracks accidentally. Figuratively, it means to obstruct or disrupt the progress of something. |  The explosion derailed several train cars. 爆炸使几节火车车厢脱轨。 | The scandal derailed his political career.  这桩丑闻毁了他的政治生涯。 | synonyms: obstruct, disrupt, thwart, sabotage | antonyms: advance, promote, further, aid | verb
