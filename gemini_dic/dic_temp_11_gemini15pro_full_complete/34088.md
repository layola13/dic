civilize:/ˈsɪvɪlaɪz/| vt.|使文明；使开化；教化|To bring out of a savage, uneducated, or unrefined state; to instruct in the arts of life; to enlighten; to refine.|例句：The missionaries worked to civilize the indigenous people. 传教士致力于教化土著居民。

例句：Education can civilize a nation. 教育可以使一个国家变得文明。|近义词：educate, refine, cultivate, enlighten|反义词：barbarize, brutalize|词性:动词

civilized:/ˈsɪvɪlaɪzd/| adj.|文明的；有教养的|Having an advanced or humane culture, society, etc.; polite, well-mannered.|例句：They live in a civilized society. 他们生活在一个文明的社会。

例句：He behaved in a civilized manner. 他的举止很文明。|近义词：cultured, refined, sophisticated, polite|反义词：uncivilized, barbaric, savage|词性:形容词
