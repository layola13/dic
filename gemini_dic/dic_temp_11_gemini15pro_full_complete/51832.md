dewlap:/ˈduːlæp/| n.|垂肉，嗉囊|A fold of loose skin hanging from the neck or throat of an animal, especially that of certain cattle, reptiles, or birds.|The iguana puffed out its dewlap in a display of dominance.  鬣蜥鼓起它的垂肉以示统治地位。|The old bull's dewlap swayed as he grazed.  老公牛吃草时，它的垂肉摇摆着。|Synonyms: wattle, double chin|Antonyms: None|Noun

dewlap:/ˈduːlæp/| v.|（垂肉）下垂，摇晃|To hang loosely and swing or wobble, typically referring to a dewlap.|The turkey's dewlap dewlapped as it gobbled. 火鸡咯咯叫的时候，它的垂肉摇晃着。|The dewlap dewlapped in the breeze. 垂肉在微风中摇摆。|Synonyms: dangle, swing, wobble|Antonyms:  tighten, firm|Verb
