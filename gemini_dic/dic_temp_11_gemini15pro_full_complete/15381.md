backward: /ˈbækwərd/ (US), /ˈbækwəd/ (UK)| adj. adv.|向后的，落后的，迟钝的|Moving or directed towards the back or rear; behind in development or progress; shy or reluctant.|The child took a backward step.  孩子向后退了一步。|Synonyms: reverse, rearward, behind, undeveloped, shy|Antonyms: forward, advanced, progressive|adjective

backward: /ˈbækwərd/ (US), /ˈbækwəd/ (UK)| adv.|向后地，相反地|In the direction of one's back; with the back foremost.|He glanced backward over his shoulder. 他回头看了一眼。|Synonyms: reversely, rearward|Antonyms: forward|adverb

backward: /ˈbækwərd/ (US), /ˈbækwəd/ (UK)| n.|后面，背后，过去|The part or position that lies at or to the rear.|She never looks backward. 她从不回头看过去。|Synonyms: rear, behind, past|Antonyms: front, future|noun
