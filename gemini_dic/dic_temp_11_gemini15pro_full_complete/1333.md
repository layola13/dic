accroach:/əˈkroʊtʃ/ | v. |侵占，蚕食；非法获取 | To gradually and unfairly take something, especially power, rights, or land, that belongs to someone else |  The government is accused of accroaching on the freedom of the press. 政府被指控侵犯新闻自由。 | The company was accused of accroaching on its rivals' territory.  该公司被指控蚕食其竞争对手的领地。 | synonyms: usurp, infringe, encroach, impinge | antonyms: relinquish, surrender, cede | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *acrochier "to seize, grasp, hook," from a- "to" (see ad-) + croc "hook," from Old Norse krókr.
