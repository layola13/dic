dilettanteism:/ˌdɪləˈtænˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 业余爱好；浅尝辄止；玩票 | The superficial pursuit of an art or branch of knowledge.  A frivolous or superficial interest in art or a branch of knowledge, especially as a pastime. | His dilettanteism in archaeology led him to purchase several fake artifacts. 他对考古学的浅尝辄止让他买了一些赝品。 | Synonyms: dabbling, superficiality, amateurism | Antonyms: professionalism, expertise, mastery | noun

dilettante:/ˌdɪləˈtænti/ | n. | 业余爱好者；半吊子 | A person who cultivates an area of interest, such as the arts, without real commitment or knowledge. | He was a dilettante in music, playing several instruments but mastering none. 他是个音乐爱好者，会演奏几种乐器，但都不精通。 | Synonyms: amateur, dabbler, trifler | Antonyms: professional, expert, master | noun

dilettantish:/ˌdɪləˈtænˌtɪʃ/ | adj. | 业余爱好的；浅尝辄止的 | Showing superficial interest in an art or pursuit. | His dilettantish approach to painting resulted in a series of unfinished canvases. 他对绘画的浅尝辄止导致了一系列未完成的画布。 | Synonyms: amateurish, superficial, trifling | Antonyms: professional, expert, masterful | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *dilettante*, present participle of *dilettare* "to delight," from Latin *delectare* "to delight, to please."
