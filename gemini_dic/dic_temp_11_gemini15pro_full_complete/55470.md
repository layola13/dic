distortingly:/dɪˈstɔːrtɪŋli/ | adv. | 扭曲地；歪曲地 |  In a way that twists or misrepresents the truth or reality.  It describes the manner in which something is done, specifically in a way that causes distortion. | The media distortingly portrayed the events, creating a false narrative. 媒体歪曲地描绘了这些事件，制造了虚假的叙述。 | synonym: misleadingly, falsely, inaccurately | antonym: truthfully, accurately, faithfully | 词性: 副词

distort:/dɪˈstɔːrt/ | v. | 扭曲；歪曲 | To pull or twist out of shape; to give a false or misleading account of; misrepresent. |  He distorted the facts to fit his theory. 他歪曲事实以符合他的理论。| synonym: twist, warp, misrepresent | antonym: clarify, represent accurately | 词性: 动词

distortion:/dɪˈstɔːrʃən/ | n. | 扭曲；歪曲 | The act of twisting or altering something out of its true shape or meaning. | The article contained several distortions of the truth. 这篇文章包含了几处对事实的歪曲。 | synonym: misrepresentation, falsification, perversion | antonym: accuracy, truth, fidelity | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *distorquere, from Latin dis- "apart" + torquere "to twist."
