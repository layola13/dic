augustan:/ɔːˈɡʌstən/ | adj. n. |奥古斯都式的，奥古斯都时代的人|Relating to the Roman emperor Augustus or his reign (27 BC – AD 14), a period marked by a flourishing of literature and art, or resembling the classical style characteristic of that period.  Characterized by grandeur, elegance, and a focus on order and reason. |例句：The Augustan age was a golden age of Roman literature. 奥古斯都时代是罗马文学的黄金时代。|近义词：classical, neoclassical, golden|反义词：barbaric, decadent|词性:形容词、名词

augustan:/ɔːˈɡʌstən/ | n. |奥古斯都时代的人|A person who lived during the reign of Augustus or a writer or artist of that period.  |例句：Many famous poets and writers were Augustans. 许多著名的诗人和作家是奥古斯都时代的人。|近义词：Roman, contemporary of Augustus|反义词：N/A|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *Augustānus*, relating to Augustus, derived from *Augustus*, the title of the Roman emperor Gaius Octavius (63 BC – AD 14).
