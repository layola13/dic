civilizable:/ˈsɪvɪlaɪzəbl/
adjective
可开化的，可教化的
Capable of being civilized; susceptible to refinement and education.
Many anthropologists believed that all cultures were civilizable. 许多人类学家认为所有文化都是可开化的。
The tribe, though primitive, was considered civilizable.  虽然这个部落很原始，但被认为是可教化的。
近义词: educable, teachable, refinable
反义词: uncivilizable, barbaric, savage
词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From civilize + -able.  "Civilize" comes from the French "civiliser," ultimately from the Latin "civilis" meaning "of or belonging to citizens, relating to public life, courteous, polite."
