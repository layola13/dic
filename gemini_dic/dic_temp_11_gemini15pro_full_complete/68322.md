fall-away:/ˈfɔːl əˌweɪ/ | adj. | 逐渐减少的，衰落的，倾斜的 | Gradually decreasing, declining, or sloping downward. | The company experienced a fall-away in sales during the recession.  该公司在经济衰退期间销售额逐渐下降。 |  The track had a fall-away bend that was difficult to navigate. 这条赛道有一个向下倾斜的弯道，很难驾驶。 | 近义词：declining, diminishing, sloping | 反义词：increasing, rising, ascending | 词性: 形容词

fall-away:/ˈfɔːl əˌweɪ/ | n. |  背叛者，脱党者| A person who abandons a cause, belief, or group. |  He was considered a fall-away from the party after publicly criticizing its leader.  在公开批评该党领导人后，他被认为是该党的背叛者。 | The church experienced a number of fall-aways during the scandal.  在丑闻期间，教会经历了大量信徒的流失。| 近义词：defector, deserter, renegade | 反义词：loyalist, adherent | 词性: 名词

词源:  "fall-away" is a compound word formed from the verb "fall" and the adverb "away."  The meaning is derived from the idea of something falling away or declining.
