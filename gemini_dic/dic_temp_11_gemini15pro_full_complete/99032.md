interalliance: /ˌɪntərəˈlaɪəns/
n.
联盟，同盟，联姻
A formal agreement or treaty between two or more groups, especially of countries, to work together for shared purposes; a close association or connection.
The two nations formed an interalliance to bolster their defenses.  这两个国家结成联盟以加强防御。
The interalliance between the two families strengthened their political influence. 这两个家族的联姻增强了他们的政治影响力。
synonyms: alliance, coalition, pact, league, union, partnership
antonyms:  division, separation, disunity, schism

Etymology:  From "inter-" (between) + "alliance" (union, association). "Inter-" comes from Latin, and "alliance" ultimately comes from Old French "alier" (to bind, unite).
