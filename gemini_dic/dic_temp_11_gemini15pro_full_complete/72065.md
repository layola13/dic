flits:/flɪts/ | v. | 轻快地飞; 掠过; 快速移动 | moves lightly and swiftly; darts; passes quickly | The butterfly flits from flower to flower. 蝴蝶在花丛间飞来飞去。|  The expression flitted across her face. 这个表情在她脸上掠过。 | synonyms: dart, flutter, skim, glance | antonyms: trudge, plod | verb

flits:/flɪts/ | n. |  轻快的飞行; 闪现 | a quick, light movement; a brief appearance |  She caught a flit of a bluebird in the garden. 她在花园里瞥见了一只蓝知更鸟。 |  The thought was just a flit of an idea. 这想法只是一闪而过。| synonyms: flutter, glimpse, flash | antonyms: stay, permanence | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *flyttan (unattested), a frequentative form of flēon "to flee," from Proto-Germanic *fleuhaną, from Proto-Indo-European *pleuk- "to flow, to swim, to flee."  The sense development is from "to flee quickly" to "to move quickly and lightly."
