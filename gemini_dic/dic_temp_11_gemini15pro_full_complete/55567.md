disturbances:/dɪˈstɜːrbənsɪz/
n.
扰乱，骚乱，不安
a state in which people behave violently in public, often as a protest
例句：The political disturbances led to many arrests. 政治骚乱导致许多人被捕。

例句：There were disturbances outside the stadium after the game.  比赛结束后，体育场外发生了骚乱。

近义词：unrest, turmoil, disorder, chaos, disruption, commotion
反义词：peace, calm, tranquility, order, stability
词性: 名词

disturbance:/dɪˈstɜːrbəns/
n.
干扰，打扰
something that interrupts someone or makes someone feel worried or upset
例句：Phone calls are a disturbance when I'm trying to work.  我工作时，电话会打扰我。

例句：I hate to be a disturbance, but could you help me with this? 我不想打扰你，但你能帮我一下吗？

近义词：interruption, interference, intrusion
反义词：peace, quiet, calm
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French desturbance, from desturber “to disturb,” from Latin disturbare, from dis- “apart, asunder” + turbare “to agitate, confuse, disturb.”
