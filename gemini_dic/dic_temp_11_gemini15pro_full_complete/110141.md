lie-ins:/ˈlaɪ ɪnz/ |n.|赖床；睡懒觉|The act of staying in bed later than usual, especially in the morning.|I love having lie-ins on weekends. 我喜欢周末睡懒觉。|Synonyms: sleeping in, lazing in bed|Antonyms: getting up early, early rising|Noun

lie-in:/ˈlaɪ ɪn/ |n.| 卧床休息；静卧抗议|1. The act of staying in bed, especially as a form of protest. 2. A period of resting in bed, especially during pregnancy or after an operation.|1.  They held a lie-in to protest against the war. 他们卧床抗议战争。 2. She had a lie-in after the birth of her baby. 她生完孩子后卧床休息了一段时间。|Synonyms: sit-in (for protest), bed rest|Antonyms: demonstration, protest march|Noun
