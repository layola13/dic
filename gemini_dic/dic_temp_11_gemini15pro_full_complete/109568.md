let oneself go: /lɛt wʌnˈsɛlf ɡoʊ/ | phrasal verb | 放纵自己；尽情享受；放松；忘乎所以 | To behave in a relaxed and uninhibited way, expressing one's feelings openly.  To lose control of one's emotions, especially through laughter or crying. To neglect one's appearance or responsibilities. | After the exams, we all let ourselves go and had a huge party. 考試結束後，我們都放鬆自己，舉辦了一個盛大的派對。 | Synonyms: unwind, loosen up, indulge, relax, let loose, cut loose | Antonyms: restrain oneself, control oneself, hold back, bottle up | verb phrase
