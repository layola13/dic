mistral:/ˈmɪstrəl, miˈstrɑːl/| n. |密史脱拉风;西北风|A strong, cold, dry northerly wind that blows through southern France into the Gulf of Lion. |例句：The mistral whipped through the valley, carrying dust and leaves. 密史脱拉风吹过山谷，卷起尘土和树叶。

例句：The vineyards are protected from the harsh mistral by rows of cypress trees. 柏树成排地保护着葡萄园免受凛冽的密史脱拉风侵袭。|近义词：north wind, northerly|反义词: southerly|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mistral*, from Provençal *mistral*, from *maestral*, from Latin *magistrālis* (“of a master, principal”), from *magister* (“master”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *megʰ₂- (“great”).
