bouillon:/ˈbuːjɒn/ (US) /ˈbuːjɔːŋ/ (UK)| n.| 肉汤，清汤 |A clear, seasoned broth, typically made from beef or chicken.|She sipped the hot bouillon to soothe her sore throat. 她喝了热肉汤来舒缓喉咙痛。|Example: The chef prepared a rich bouillon as the base for the soup.  厨师准备了浓郁的肉汤作为汤底。|Synonym: broth, stock, consommé|Antonym: (No direct antonym, but consider "thick soup" as a contrasting concept)|noun

Etymology: From French "bouillon,"  meaning "boiling," from the verb "bouillir" meaning "to boil." Ultimately from Latin "bullire."
