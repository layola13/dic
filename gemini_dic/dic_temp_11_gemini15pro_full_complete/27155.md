call-out:/ˈkɔːl aʊt/ (US) /ˈkɔːl aʊt/ (UK)| n. v.|呼叫，召唤；出动；大声说出；批评；|A call-out is a request for someone, such as a plumber or a doctor, to come to a particular place to deal with a problem or an emergency.  It can also mean to publicly criticize someone or something.|例句：The doctor received a call-out in the middle of the night.  医生半夜接到出诊电话。

|近义词：summon, alert, criticize, challenge|反义词:ignore, overlook|词性:noun, verb
