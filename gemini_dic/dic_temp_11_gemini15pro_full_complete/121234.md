misery:/ˈmɪzəri/| n.|痛苦，悲惨|A state of extreme unhappiness or suffering.|She lived a life of misery after the accident. 事故发生后，她过着悲惨的生活。|Synonyms: distress, suffering, anguish, torment|Antonyms: happiness, joy, bliss|noun

miserable:/ˈmɪzərəbl/| adj.|悲惨的，痛苦的|Extremely unhappy or uncomfortable.|The weather was miserable - cold and rainy. 天气很糟糕——又冷又下雨。|Synonyms: unhappy, wretched, sorrowful, dejected|Antonyms: happy, cheerful, joyful|adjective

miserableness:/ˈmɪzərəblnəs/ | n. | 悲惨; 凄惨 | The state or quality of being miserable. | The miserableness of their living conditions was shocking. 他们的生活条件之悲惨令人震惊。| Synonyms: misery, wretchedness, unhappiness | Antonyms: happiness, joy, bliss | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *miserie*, from Latin miseria "wretchedness, misery, misfortune, trouble," from miser "wretched, unfortunate," of uncertain origin.
