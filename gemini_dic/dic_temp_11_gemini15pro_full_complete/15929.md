balmer:/ˈbɑːlmər/ | n. |巴尔默|  Relating to or denoting a series of lines in the visible part of the hydrogen spectrum, discovered by Johann Jakob Balmer (1825–98), a Swiss mathematician and physicist.  The wavelengths of the lines can be represented by a formula giving their positions as a function of a single integer. This formula was later generalized by Rydberg. | The Balmer series is important in astronomy for identifying hydrogen in stars. | 近义词: spectral series (in the context of the hydrogen spectrum), hydrogen lines (informally)| 反义词: None | 词性: noun
