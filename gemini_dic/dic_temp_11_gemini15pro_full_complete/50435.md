deprecate:/ˈdɛprɪkeɪt/| v. | 贬低；反对；不赞成 | To express disapproval of; to belittle. |  The teacher deprecated the student's disruptive behavior in class. 老师谴责了学生在课堂上的破坏性行为。

The government deprecated the violence and called for calm. 政府谴责了暴力行为，呼吁保持冷静。 | 近义词：criticize, condemn, denounce, belittle, disparage | 反义词：praise, commend, endorse, approve |词性:动词

deprecate:/ˈdɛprəˌkeɪt/| v. | （计算机）弃用；不推荐使用| To mark (a software feature, etc.) as obsolete, usually while still supporting it. | The old version of the software is now deprecated.  旧版本的软件现在已被弃用。

Developers deprecated the function in the latest release. 开发人员在最新版本中弃用了该函数。 | 近义词：supersede, obsolete | 反义词：support, recommend, endorse |词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *deprecatus*, past participle of deprecari (“to pray against, avert by prayer; entreat, beg”), from de- (“down, away”) + precari (“to pray”).
