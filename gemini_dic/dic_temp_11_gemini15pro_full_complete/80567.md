goody-two-shoes: /ˌɡʊdi ˈtuː ʃuːz/
goody-two-shoes: n.|好好先生；伪善者|A person who is affectedly good or virtuous, often to an irritating degree.  Someone who always tries to appear morally superior to others.|例句：She's such a goody-two-shoes, always telling everyone what to do. 她真是个伪君子，总是告诉别人该怎么做。|近义词：prig, sanctimonious person|反义词：rebel, scoundrel|词性:名词

goody-two-shoes: /ˌɡʊdi ˈtuː ʃuːz/
goody-two-shoes: adj.|伪善的；装腔作势的|Excessively or affectedly virtuous.|例句：He gave me a goody-two-shoes lecture about the dangers of smoking. 他就吸烟的危害对我进行了一番假惺惺的教训。|近义词：sanctimonious, self-righteous|反义词：rebellious, wicked|词性:形容词

词源: The term originates from the title of a children's story, "The History of Little Goody Two-Shoes," published in 1765. The story features a poor orphan girl who is rewarded for her virtue.  Over time, the term evolved to have a more negative connotation.
