distract:/dɪˈstrækt/| v.|转移(注意力); 使分心|to take someone's attention away from something|The loud music distracted me from my work. 喧闹的音乐使我无法专心工作。|近义词：divert, sidetrack|反义词：focus, concentrate|词性:动词

distracted:/dɪˈstræktɪd/| adj.|心烦意乱的; 注意力分散的|unable to concentrate because you are worried or thinking about something else|He seemed distracted and didn't hear what I said. 他看起来心不在焉，没有听到我说的话。|近义词：preoccupied, inattentive|反义词：focused, attentive|词性:形容词

distracting:/dɪˈstræktɪŋ/| adj.|使人分心的|taking someone's attention away from what they are trying to do|The constant noise was very distracting.  持续不断的噪音令人十分分心。|近义词：disturbing, interrupting|反义词：engaging, captivating|词性:形容词

distraction:/dɪˈstrækʃn/| n.|分心的事; 使人分心的事物|something that takes your attention away from what you are doing or thinking about|The TV is a constant distraction. 电视总是让人分心。|近义词：diversion, interruption|反义词：focus, concentration|词性:名词
