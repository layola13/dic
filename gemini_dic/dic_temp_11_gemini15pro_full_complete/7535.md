annexe:/ˈæneks/ (US) /əˈneks/ (UK)| n. vt.|附件，附属建筑；增补，并吞|An annex is a building joined to or associated with a larger building, providing additional space or accommodations.  As a verb, it means to attach, append, or add something as a supplement.|The library has an annex that houses the rare book collection. 图书馆有一个附属建筑，收藏着珍本。|近义词：addition, extension, supplement, appendage, wing,  append, attach|反义词: detachment, separation|词性: noun, verb

annex:/ˈæneks/ (US) /əˈneks/ (UK)| vt.|增补，并吞|To annex something means to take possession or control of something, typically land or territory, and incorporate it into one's own domain.|The country annexed the neighboring territory after the war.  这个国家在战后吞并了邻近的领土。|近义词：seize, conquer, appropriate, acquire,  take over|反义词: relinquish, cede|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *annexus, past participle of annectere "to tie or bind to," from ad- "to" + nectere "to bind."
