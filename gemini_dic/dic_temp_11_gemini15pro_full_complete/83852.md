halberts:/ˈhɔːlbərts/ (US), /ˈhɔːlbəts/ (UK)| n.|戟; halberd的复数形式|A halberd is a two-handed pole weapon that came to prominent use during the 14th, 15th, and 16th centuries. It consists of an axe blade topped with a spike mounted on a long shaft. It always has a hook or thorn on the back side of the axe blade for grappling mounted combatants.|The Swiss Guard at the Vatican still carry halberts.  梵蒂冈的瑞士卫队仍然携带戟。

The museum displayed several halberts from the medieval period.  博物馆展出了几件中世纪的戟。|近义词：poleaxe, pike, spear|反义词: (No direct antonym for a specific weapon)|noun
