excruciatingly:/ɪkˈskruːʃieɪtɪŋli/
adverb
极其痛苦地，极度地
extremely painfully; intensely
例句：She described the experience as excruciatingly painful. 她形容这段经历极其痛苦。

例句：He spoke excruciatingly slowly. 他说话慢得令人难以忍受。

近义词：agonizingly, intensely, terribly, extremely
反义词：mildly, slightly, gently
词性：adverb


excruciate:/ɪkˈskruːʃieɪt/
verb
使极度痛苦，折磨
to inflict severe pain or agony on
例句：The memory of the accident still excruciates him. 事故的记忆仍然折磨着他。

例句：He was excruciated by guilt. 他被内疚折磨着。

近义词：torment, torture, agonize
反义词：soothe, comfort, relieve
词性：verb


excruciating:/ɪkˈskruːʃieɪtɪŋ/
adjective
极度痛苦的，折磨人的
extremely painful
例句：I have an excruciating headache. 我头疼得厉害。

例句：The process of recovery was excruciating. 康复的过程非常痛苦。

近义词：agonizing, torturous, unbearable
反义词：mild, slight, bearable
词性：adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin excruciatus, past participle of excruciare ("to torment, torture"), from ex- ("out of, from") + crux ("cross").  The original meaning referred to the torture of crucifixion.
