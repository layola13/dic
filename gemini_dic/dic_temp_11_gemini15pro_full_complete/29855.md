causativity:/ˌkɔːzəˈtɪvɪti/
n.
因果关系; 使役性
The property of a verb or construction that indicates that a subject causes someone or something else to do or be something.
The teacher encouraged the students' creativity. 老师鼓励学生的创造力。(This sentence implies a degree of causativity, as the teacher's encouragement caused the students to be creative).
近义词:causation, agency
反义词:inertness, passivity
词性:名词

causative:/ˈkɔːzətɪv/
adj.
使役的; 引起…的
Relating to or expressing causation.
Smoking is a causative factor in many lung diseases. 吸烟是许多肺部疾病的致病因素。
近义词:causal
反义词:resultant
词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *causativus, from Latin causatus (past participle of causari “to plead, allege as a cause”), from causa “cause”
