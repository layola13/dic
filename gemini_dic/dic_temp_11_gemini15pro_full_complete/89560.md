honors:/ˈɑnərz/ (US) /ˈɒnəz/ (UK)| n. pl.|荣誉，优等成绩，荣幸|A special award given to students who achieve high academic standards. It can also refer to a privilege or distinction.|She graduated with honors. 她以优异的成绩毕业了。|Synonyms: distinctions, accolades, awards, privileges|Antonyms: shame, disgrace|Noun

honors:/ˈɑnərz/ (US) /ˈɒnəz/ (UK)| v. (third-person singular simple present honors, present participle honoring, simple past honored or US also honoured, past participle honored or US also honoured)|尊敬，给予荣誉|To regard with great respect.|He was honored for his bravery. 他因其勇敢而受到表彰。|Synonyms: respect, revere, esteem, venerate|Antonyms: disrespect, dishonor, disdain|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *onor*, *onour*, from Latin *honor*, *honos* "esteem, respect, reputation, dignity, office, ornament."
