globalised:/ˈɡloʊbəlaɪzd/ (US), /ˈɡləʊbəlaɪzd/ (UK)| adj.|全球化的|Developed or operated on an international scale.|例句：The world has become increasingly globalised in recent decades. 近几十年来，世界变得越来越全球化。|例句：Many companies have globalised their operations to take advantage of lower labor costs in other countries. 许多公司已经将业务全球化，以利用其他国家较低的劳动力成本。|近义词：international, worldwide, global|反义词:local, regional|词性:形容词

globalize:/ˈɡloʊbəlaɪz/ (US), /ˈɡləʊbəlaɪzd/ (UK)| v.|全球化|Make or become worldwide in scope or application.|例句：The company decided to globalize its operations. 该公司决定将其业务全球化。|例句：The internet has helped to globalize communication. 互联网帮助实现了通信的全球化。|近义词：internationalize, broaden, expand|反义词:localize, restrict|词性:动词

globalisation:/ˌɡloʊbələˈzeɪʃn/ (US), /ˌɡləʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ (UK)| n.|全球化|The process by which businesses or other organizations develop international influence or start operating on an international scale.|例句：Globalization has led to increased trade and cultural exchange. 全球化导致了贸易和文化交流的增加。|例句：The globalization of the economy has had both positive and negative effects. 经济全球化既有积极的影响，也有消极的影响。|近义词：internationalization, integration|反义词:localization, regionalization|词性:名词
