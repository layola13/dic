non-orientability:/ˌnɒnˌɔːriənˈtəbɪləti/ | n. | 不可定向性 | The property of a surface or manifold that does not have a consistent orientation, meaning that a figure traveling along a closed loop on the surface can return to its starting point mirrored. | The Möbius strip is a classic example of non-orientability.  莫比乌斯环是不可定向性的经典例子。 | non-directedness | orientability | noun


non-orientable:/ˌnɒnˌɔːriənˈtəbəl/ | adj. | 不可定向的 | Not having a consistent orientation; said of a surface or manifold. | The Klein bottle is a non-orientable surface. 克莱因瓶是一个不可定向的表面。 | non-directed | orientable | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

From non- + orientable,  from orient + -able.  Orient ultimately derives from Latin *oriens, present participle of orīrī "to rise".
