overacuteness: /ˌoʊvərəˈkjutnəs/ | n. | 过于敏锐，过度敏感 | Excessive sharpness or acuteness, especially of the senses or the mind. |  Her overacuteness of hearing made loud noises unbearable. 她过分敏锐的听觉使得她无法忍受 loud noises。| hypersensitivity, oversensitivity | dullness, insensitivity | noun

Etymology:
From over- + acuteness.  "Over-" is an English prefix meaning "excessively," "too much," deriving from Old English "ofer." "Acuteness" comes from Late Middle English, from the Latin "acutus" (sharp, pointed), past participle of "acuere" (to sharpen), from "acus" (needle).
