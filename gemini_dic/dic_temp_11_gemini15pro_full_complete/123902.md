mordancy:/ˈmɔːrdənsi/| n.| 尖刻；辛辣；苛刻；腐蚀性|A quality of sharpness or biting wit.  The mordancy of her satire made her many enemies.|Her mordancy silenced the room. 她的尖刻让整个房间鸦雀无声。|Synonyms: acrimony, bitterness, causticity, sharpness, trenchancy|Antonyms: blandness, gentleness, mildness, sweetness|noun

mordant:/ˈmɔːrdənt/| adj.| 尖刻的；辛辣的；苛刻的；腐蚀性的|Sharp, biting, or sarcastic in thought, manner, or style. A mordant wit.|He delivered a mordant critique of the government's policies. 他对政府的政策进行了尖锐的批评。|Synonyms: acrimonious, bitter, caustic, cutting, incisive, sarcastic, scathing, trenchant|Antonyms: bland, gentle, kind, mild, sweet|adjective


词源:
From Old French *mordant (Modern French mordant), present participle of mordre "to bite," from Latin mordēre "to bite."  The literal meaning relating to chemical use is "biting" or "corrosive." The figurative sense of "biting wit" developed from this.
