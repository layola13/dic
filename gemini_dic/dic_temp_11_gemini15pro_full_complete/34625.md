clefts:/klefts/ /klɛfts/| n.|裂缝；裂隙；（尤指山间的）狭窄通道|A narrow fissure or split, especially in rock or the earth; a narrow passage between mountains or cliffs. |例句：The climbers found a handhold in the clefts of the rock face. 登山者在岩壁的裂缝中找到了一个抓手。

例句：Deep clefts in the glacier made it dangerous to cross.  冰川上深深的裂缝使其变得危险无法穿越。|近义词：fissures, cracks, crevices, gaps, chasms|反义词:closures, seals, joints|词性:noun

cleft:/kleft/ /klɛft/| adj.|劈开的；裂开的|Split or divided, especially of the chin or palate.|例句：He had a cleft chin, just like his father. 他有一个裂开的下巴，就像他父亲一样。

例句：Cleft palate is a birth defect that affects the roof of the mouth. 裂腭是一种影响口腔顶部先天性缺陷。|近义词：split, divided, forked, bifurcated|反义词:whole, intact, undivided|词性:adjective
