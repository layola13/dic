hypernormalness:/ˌhaɪpərˈnɔːrməlnəs/| n.| 超常态 |A condition in which something appears normal, but is actually deeply abnormal or dysfunctional, often due to prolonged exposure to abnormal or extreme conditions.|  People living under totalitarian regimes can develop a sense of hypernormalness, accepting absurd or oppressive conditions as everyday reality.| 近义词:surreality, absurdity, dysfunction, pretense, artificiality | 反义词:normality, normalcy, authenticity, genuineness | 词性:名词

Etymology:
From "hyper" (meaning "over, beyond") + "normalness".  The term was popularized by journalist Peter Pomerantsev in his 2014 book *Nothing Is True and Everything Is Possible*, describing life in post-Soviet Russia.
