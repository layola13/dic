felix:/ˈfiːlɪks/| adj.|幸福的；幸运的；吉祥的|From Latin felix "lucky, fortunate, successful, fruitful," from fe-"to suck, suckle" (related to fecundus "fruitful") + -lix, related to laetus "glad," possibly from PIE root *leg-"to collect, gather."  Meaning "fertile, fruitful" is oldest.  The sense evolution may have been "fruitful" > "prosperous" > "lucky, fortunate."|He led a long and felix life. 他度过了幸福长寿的一生。|Synonyms: fortunate, lucky, blessed, prosperous|Antonyms: unfortunate, unlucky, cursed, wretched|adjective
