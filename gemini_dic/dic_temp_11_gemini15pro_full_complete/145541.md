overcommercializes: /ˌoʊvər kəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪzɪz/ | v. | 过度商业化 | To exploit or promote excessively for profit, often at the expense of quality or artistic integrity. | The city has overcommercialized its historic district, turning it into a tourist trap.  这个城市过度商业化了它的历史街区，把它变成了一个旅游陷阱。 | overexploits, overmarkets | undersells, underpromotes | verb


overcommercialized: /ˌoʊvər kəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 过度商业化的 | Excessively exploited or promoted for profit. | The once-charming town now feels overcommercialized and artificial.  这个曾经迷人的小镇现在感觉过度商业化和人为化。| overexploited, overmarketed | understated, authentic | adjective


overcommercialization: /ˌoʊvər kəˌmɜːrʃələˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 过度商业化 | The act or process of overcommercializing something. | The overcommercialization of Christmas has led to a decline in its true meaning.  圣诞节的过度商业化导致其真正意义的下降。| overexploitation, overmarketing | understatement, authenticity | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "commercialize" (from "commerce" + "-ize," a suffix meaning "to make or become").  "Commerce" comes from Latin "commercium," from "com-" (together) + "merx" (merchandise).
