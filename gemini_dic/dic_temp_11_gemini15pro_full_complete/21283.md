bloodletters:/ˈblʌdˌlɛtərz/
n.|放血者，吸血鬼，嗜血成性的人|A person who draws blood, especially one who practices bloodletting (historically) or a person known for cruelty or violence.|The tyrant was a true bloodletter, responsible for the deaths of thousands.|Synonyms: leech, vampire, butcher, killer, slaughterer|Antonyms: healer, savior, protector|noun

bloodletters:/ˈblʌdˌlɛtərz/
n.|放血器，用来放血的工具 (古)|A tool or instrument used for bloodletting (historical).|Medieval bloodletters were often crude and unsanitary.|Synonyms: lancet, fleam|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps bandage,  tourniquet as tools that stop bleeding)|noun
