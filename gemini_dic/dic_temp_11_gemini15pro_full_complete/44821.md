crushed:/krʌʃt/| adj.|压碎的，被压坏的；挫败的，沮丧的|Broken or deformed by pressure; emotionally devastated.|例句：The car was crushed in the accident. 汽车在事故中被压坏了。|例句：He felt crushed by the news of his failure. 他被失败的消息击垮了。|近义词：smashed, flattened, devastated, heartbroken|反义词：intact, whole, elated, happy|词性:形容词

crushed:/krʌʃt/| v.|压碎，压坏；镇压；使…变形|Past participle of "crush". To press something so hard that it breaks or changes shape; to suppress or subdue completely; to cause someone to feel emotionally devastated.|例句：The grapes were crushed to make wine. 葡萄被压碎用来酿酒。|例句：The rebellion was quickly crushed by the government. 叛乱很快被政府镇压了。|近义词：smash, pulverize, suppress, subdue|反义词：repair, fix, encourage, uplift|词性:动词

词源: From Middle English *crushen*, from Old Norse *krussa* (“to squeeze, press together”).
