anomaly:/əˈnɒməli/ (US) /əˈnɑːməli/ (UK)| n. |异常，反常；不规则；异常的人或物|Something that deviates from what is standard, normal, or expected.|例句：The scientists were puzzled by the anomaly in the data. 科学家们对数据中的异常现象感到困惑。|例句：He is considered an anomaly in the political world. 他被认为是政界的一个异类。|近义词：aberration, irregularity, exception, oddity|反义词：norm, regularity, standard|词性:noun

anomalies:/əˈnɒməliz/ (US) /əˈnɑːməliz/ (UK)| n. |异常，反常；不规则；异常的人或物（复数）|The plural form of "anomaly," referring to multiple instances of something deviating from the norm.|例句：Several anomalies in the system caused the program to crash. 系统中的几个异常导致程序崩溃。|例句：The researchers documented all the anomalies they observed during the experiment. 研究人员记录了他们在实验过程中观察到的所有异常现象。|近义词：aberrations, irregularities, exceptions, oddities|反义词：norms, regularities, standards|词性:noun
