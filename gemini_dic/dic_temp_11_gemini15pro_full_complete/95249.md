incandescence:/ˌɪn.kænˈdes.əns/ | n. | 白炽；炽热；光辉 | the state of emitting light as a result of being heated; brilliant radiance. | The incandescence of the filament lit the room. 灯丝的白炽光照亮了房间。| The metal reached incandescence in the furnace.  金属在熔炉中达到了白炽状态。| synonyms: glow, luminosity, radiance, brilliance | antonyms: dullness, darkness, dimness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin incandescens, present participle of incandescere (“to glow, to become white-hot”), from in- (“in, on, at”) + candescere (“to become white-hot, to glow”), from candēre (“to shine, to glow white”).
