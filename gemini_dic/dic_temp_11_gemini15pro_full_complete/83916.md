half-blindnesses:/ˌhɑːf ˈblaɪndnɪsɪz/ | n. | 半盲; 部分失明 |  A state of having limited or impaired vision, not complete blindness, often affecting only one eye or part of the visual field. It implies having some remaining vision. |  His half-blindness made it difficult for him to navigate the city. 他的半盲使得他在城市里行走困难。

The half-blindnesses caused by the disease affected different parts of their visual fields. 这种疾病引起的半盲影响了他们视野的不同部分。 | 近义词: partial blindness, impaired vision | 反义词: perfect vision, full sight | 词性: 名词


词源:  "half" (Old English healf) + "blindness" (Old English blindnes)
