heathland:/ˈhiːθlənd/ | n. | 荒地，石南荒原 | An area of open uncultivated land typically covered with heather and other low-growing shrubs. |  The heathland was ablaze with the purple of flowering heather. 石南花盛开，荒野一片紫色。 | synonym: moor, heath | antonym:  woodland, forest | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hāþland (unattested), from hāþ (“heath”) + land (“land”). Cognate with Middle Low German hēdland, Dutch hei(d)land.
