dishonoured: /dɪsˈɒnəd/ (British) /dɪsˈɑːnərd/ (American)| adj. | 羞辱的，拒付的 | Treated with disrespect; rejected or refused (especially of a cheque or bill of exchange). |  The dishonoured cheque bounced. 这张拒付的支票被退回来了。| Synonyms: disgraced, shamed, humiliated, rejected, unpaid, bounced (of a cheque) | Antonyms: honoured, respected, paid | adjective

dishonour: /dɪsˈɒnə(r)/ (British) /dɪsˈɑːnər/ (American) | n. v. | 耻辱，丢脸；拒付 | Loss of honour or respect; to refuse to accept or pay (a cheque, bill of exchange, etc.). | He felt the dishonour keenly. 他深感耻辱。 The bank dishonoured the cheque. 银行拒付了这张支票。 | Synonyms: shame, disgrace, humiliation, discredit; reject, refuse | Antonyms: honour, respect, credit; accept, pay | noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY ->
From Old French *deshonorer*, from des- (expressing negation) + honorer (to honour), from Latin honor (honour).
