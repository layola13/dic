lithochromatics:/ˌlɪθəkrəˈmætɪks/
n.
石版印刷术，彩色石印术
The art or process of printing in color from stone.  Lithochromatics involves preparing a separate stone for each color used in the final image.
Lithochromatics was a popular method for creating detailed and vibrant illustrations in the 19th century.  彩色石印术是19世纪一种流行的创作细致而充满活力的插图的方法。
The museum's collection includes several examples of 19th-century lithochromatics.  博物馆的收藏包括几个19世纪彩色石印术的例子。
Synonyms: chromolithography, color lithography
Antonyms: None
