gannet:/ˈɡænɪt/| n. |塘鹅；鰹鳥|A large seabird with a mainly white body, a long neck, and a long pointed bill. It dives steeply into the sea to catch fish.|例句：The gannet plummeted from the sky, hitting the water with a splash. 塘鹅从天而降，溅起水花。

例句：Gannets are known for their spectacular diving skills. 塘鹅以其惊人的潜水技巧而闻名。|近义词：seabird, pelican (somewhat)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *ganot, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse gandr (“magic wand, staff”), referring to the bird's long bill.
