neo-classical:/ˌniːoʊˈklæsɪkəl/ | adj. | 新古典主义的 | Relating to a revival or adaptation of classical styles, especially in art, literature, and architecture. |  The building's neo-classical design featured columns and a pediment. 这座建筑的新古典主义设计以柱子和山形墙为特色。| synonyms: neoclassic, classicist | antonyms: modern, contemporary | adjective

neo-classical:/ˌniːoʊˈklæsɪkəl/ | n. | 新古典主义 | A revival or adaptation of classical styles, especially in art, literature, and architecture. | Neo-classical architecture is characterized by grandeur and symmetry. 新古典主义建筑的特点是宏伟和对称。| synonyms: neoclassicism, classicism | antonyms: modernism, contemporary art | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From neo- + classical.  "Neo-" is a combining form meaning "new," derived from the Greek "neos." "Classical" derives from the Latin "classicus," meaning "belonging to the highest class."
