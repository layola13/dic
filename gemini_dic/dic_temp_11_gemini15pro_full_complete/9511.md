antischolasticism:/ˌæntiˌskɒləˈstɪsɪzəm/ (US: /ˌæntiˌskɑːləˈstɪsɪzəm/)
n.
反对学究主义；反对经院哲学
Opposition to traditional academic methods or excessive emphasis on formal rules and theories.
The rise of humanism led to a wave of antischolasticism in the Renaissance. 文艺复兴时期人文主义的兴起导致了一波反对学究主义的浪潮。
近义词：anti-intellectualism, philistinism
反义词：scholasticism, academicism
词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- + scholasticism.  "Anti-" is a prefix meaning "against" or "opposed to". "Scholasticism" refers to the system of theology and philosophy taught in medieval European universities, based on Aristotelian logic and the writings of the Church Fathers.
