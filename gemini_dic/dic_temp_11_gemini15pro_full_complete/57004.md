double-jointedness:/ˌdʌbəlˈdʒɔɪntɪdnəs/ | n. |双关节；关节过度伸展 |The ability to bend joints beyond their normal range of motion, often due to increased laxity of ligaments and tendons. |She showed off her double-jointedness by bending her thumb backwards to touch her wrist. 她通过向后弯曲拇指使其触碰到手腕来炫耀她的双关节。| 近义词: hypermobility, flexibility | 反义词: stiffness, rigidity | 词性: noun

Etymology:
double + jointed + -ness
"double" from Old French "doble"
"jointed" from "joint" + "-ed"
"joint" from Old French "joint"
"-ness" Old English suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being"
