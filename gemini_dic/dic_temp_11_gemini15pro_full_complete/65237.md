Etruscans: /ɪˈtrʌskənz/ | n. | 伊特鲁里亚人 |  The Etruscans were a powerful and mysterious civilization that flourished in ancient Italy before the rise of Rome. They are known for their art, metalworking, and unique language. |  The Etruscans built elaborate tombs. 伊特鲁里亚人建造了精致的陵墓。

The influence of the Etruscans on Roman culture was significant. 伊特鲁里亚文化对罗马文化的影响是显著的。 | 近义词:  Tyrrhenians (古希腊人对伊特鲁里亚人的称呼) | 反义词:  Romans (在历史背景下可以视为对立或接替伊特鲁里亚人的势力) | 词性: 名词(plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin Etrusci, related to Greek Tyrrhēnoi (Τυρρηνοί). The origin of the Etruscan name for themselves (Rasenna) is unknown.
