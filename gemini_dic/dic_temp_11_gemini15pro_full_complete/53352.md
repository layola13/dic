disadvantaging: /ˌdɪsədˈvæntɪdʒɪŋ/
Verb
使处于不利地位，使吃亏
Putting someone in a worse position than they are now or compared to others.
The new regulations are disadvantaging small businesses. 新规定使小型企业处于不利地位。
Synonyms: hindering, hampering, obstructing, undermining
Antonyms:  advantaging, benefiting, aiding, assisting
词性: 动词 (present participle of disadvantage)

disadvantage: /ˌdɪsədˈvæntɪdʒ/
Noun
不利条件，劣势
A condition or situation that causes someone or something to be less successful than others.
One disadvantage of living in the city is the high cost of housing. 住在城市的一个缺点是住房成本高。
Synonyms: drawback, handicap, detriment, weakness
Antonyms: advantage, benefit, asset, strength
词性: 名词

disadvantaged: /ˌdɪsədˈvæntɪdʒd/
Adjective
处于不利地位的，弱势的
In a worse position than others, especially because of less money, fewer opportunities, etc.
Children from disadvantaged backgrounds often face greater challenges in education. 来自弱势背景的孩子在教育方面往往面临更大的挑战。
Synonyms: underprivileged, deprived, needy, impoverished
Antonyms: privileged, advantaged, affluent, wealthy
词性: 形容词
