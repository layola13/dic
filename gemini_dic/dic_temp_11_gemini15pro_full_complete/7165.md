anger:/ˈæŋɡə(r)/ (US) /ˈæŋɡə(r)/ (UK)| n. vt.|愤怒，生气；激怒|A strong feeling of displeasure and hostility.|She felt a surge of anger. 她感到一阵怒火涌上心头。|近义词：fury, rage, wrath, indignation, resentment|反义词：calm, peace, tranquility|词性:名词,动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *anger, angre, from Old Norse angr (“grief, sorrow, anxiety”), from Proto-Germanic *angustiz (“narrow, tight”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂énǵʰ- (“narrow, tight”).  The semantic shift from "grief" to "anger" likely occurred through an intermediate stage of "distress, suffering."  Related to angst.
