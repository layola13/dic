karmathian:/kɑːrˈmeɪθiən/
n.|卡尔马特派的；卡尔马特派的信徒|A member of a radical Ismaili Muslim sect active in the 9th and 10th centuries, known for their revolutionary doctrines and actions, including a revolt against the Abbasid Caliphate.|The Karmathians raided Mecca and stole the Black Stone.|Synonyms: Ismaili extremist, revolutionary, heretic (from the Abbasid perspective)|Antonyms: orthodox Muslim, Abbasid loyalist|noun

karmathian:/kɑːrˈmeɪθiən/
adj.|卡尔马特派的|Relating to the Karmathians, a radical Ismaili Muslim sect.|Karmathian beliefs challenged the established order.|Synonyms: Ismaili, heterodox, rebellious|Antonyms: orthodox, conformist|adjective
