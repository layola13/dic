anticoagulants:/ˌæntikoʊˈæɡjələnts/ | n. | 抗凝血剂 | Substances that prevent blood from clotting. |  The doctor prescribed anticoagulants to the patient after the surgery to reduce the risk of blood clots. 医生给病人开了抗凝血剂以降低术后血栓的风险。

Anticoagulants are crucial in preventing strokes in patients with atrial fibrillation. 抗凝血剂对于预防房颤患者的中风至关重要。 | Synonyms: blood thinners | Antonyms: coagulants, procoagulants | noun

anticoagulant:/ˌæntikoʊˈæɡjələnt/ | adj. | 抗凝的 | Having the effect of preventing coagulation of the blood. |  The patient was given anticoagulant therapy to prevent further clotting. 病人接受了抗凝治疗以防止进一步凝血。

Heparin is an anticoagulant medication. 肝素是一种抗凝药物。| Synonyms: antithrombotic | Antonyms: coagulant, procoagulant | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- (prefix meaning "against") + coagulant (from Latin coagulans, present participle of coagulare "to curdle, congeal," from co- "together" + agere "to drive, lead, do").
