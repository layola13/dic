iconoclast: /aɪˈkɑːnəˌklæst/ | n. | 破坏偶像者；攻击传统观念的人 |  A person who attacks cherished beliefs or institutions. | Martin Luther was considered an iconoclast for his challenge to the Catholic Church. 马丁·路德因挑战天主教会而被认为是一个反传统者。 |  The artist's iconoclastic work shocked the traditional art world. 这位艺术家的反传统作品震惊了传统的艺术界。 |  rebel, radical, nonconformist, dissident, heretic |  conformist, traditionalist | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *iconoclastes, from Byzantine Greek  εἰκονοκλάστης (eikonoklástēs), from  εἰκόνα (eikóna, “image”) + κλάστης (kláistēs, “breaker”), from κλάω (kláō, “I break”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱlew- (“to hook, bend”).
