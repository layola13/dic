airfoil:/ˈɛərfɔɪl/| n.|机翼，翼型|A surface, such as a wing, propeller blade, or rudder, whose shape and orientation control stability, direction, lift, thrust, or propulsion.|The airplane's airfoil generated enough lift to keep it airborne.|Synonyms: wing, blade, vane, aileron|Antonyms: N/A|noun
