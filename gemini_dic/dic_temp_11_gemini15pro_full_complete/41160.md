copy-book's: /ˈkɒpi bʊks/ | n. |字帖;习字簿; |A book containing models of handwriting for learners to imitate.  A copybook is traditionally used in teaching penmanship. It contains examples of letters, words, and sentences for students to copy. Now, metaphorically, it can refer to something considered a perfect example. |  My grandmother's copperplate handwriting looks like it came straight out of a copy-book. 我祖母的蝇头小楷看起来就像直接从字帖里出来的。The politician's speech was a copy-book example of how to avoid answering a question. 这位政客的演讲是如何避免回答问题的完美范例。 | 近义词: writing book, exercise book, model | 反义词: None | 词性: 名词

词源:  From "copy" (meaning a transcript or imitation) + "book".  The term originates from the practice of learning to write by copying examples from a book.
