low-churchism:/ˌloʊˈtʃɜːrtʃɪzəm/
noun
低教会主义
Emphasis on the Protestant aspects of the Church of England, characterized by a focus on the Bible and preaching, rather than elaborate rituals or sacraments.  It often involves a simpler form of worship and a less hierarchical church structure.
The vicar's sermons reflected his low-churchism, focusing on scriptural interpretation rather than liturgical tradition.
近义词: evangelicalism, puritanism
反义词: high-churchism, Anglo-Catholicism
词性: n.
