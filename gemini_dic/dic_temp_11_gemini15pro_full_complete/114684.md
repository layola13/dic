malignity:/məˈlɪɡnəti/ | n. | 恶意，恶毒，怨恨；(疾病的)严重性，恶性 | Intense ill will or hatred; the quality of being malevolent or harmful.  |  She spoke of him with surprising malignity. 她以惊人的恶意谈论他。 | Synonyms: malevolence, spite, venom, rancor, animosity, viciousness, hostility, malice | Antonyms: benevolence, kindness, goodwill, compassion | noun

malignant:/məˈlɪɡnənt/ | adj. | 恶意的，恶毒的；(疾病)恶性的，致命的 |  Having or showing a wish to do evil to others; (of a disease) very virulent or infectious. |  He gave her a malignant look. 他恶狠狠地瞪了她一眼。|  A malignant tumor. 恶性肿瘤。 | Synonyms: malevolent, spiteful, venomous, rancorous, hostile, vicious, malicious, cancerous | Antonyms: benevolent, kind, benign | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English malignite, from Old French malignité, from Latin malignitās (“badness, ill-will”), from malignus (“bad, wicked, ill-disposed”), from male (“badly”) + genus (“birth, race, kind”).
