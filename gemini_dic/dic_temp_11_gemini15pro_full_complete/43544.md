crayfishes:/ˈkreɪˌfɪʃɪz/
n.
小龙虾; 淡水螯虾
Small freshwater crustaceans resembling lobsters, with pincers.  They are also known as crawfish or crawdads.
Crayfishes are important to the ecosystem of many rivers. 小龙虾对许多河流的生态系统很重要。
Crayfishes were plentiful in the stream. 小溪里小龙虾很多。
synonyms: crawfish, crawdad, crawdaddies, craws, mudbugs
antonyms: None (Animals typically lack direct antonyms.)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *crevis, from Old French crevice (modern French écrevisse), from Frankish *krebiz, from Proto-Germanic *krabiz (“crab”), from Proto-Indo-European *gerb- (“to scratch, carve”).  The plural form "crayfishes" is less common than "crayfish" when referring to multiple individuals.
