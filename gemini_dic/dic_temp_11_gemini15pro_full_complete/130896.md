nonarticulateness:/ˌnɒnɑːrˈtɪkjʊlətnəs/
|n.|表达不清，不善言辞|The state of being unable to express oneself clearly or effectively, often due to shyness, lack of confidence, or difficulty in formulating thoughts into words.|His nonarticulateness made it difficult for him to share his feelings.  他表达不清，难以分享自己的感受。|inarticulacy, ineloquence|articulateness, eloquence|noun


nonarticulate:/ˌnɒnɑːrˈtɪkjʊlət/
|adj.|不善表达的，口齿不清的|Unable to express oneself clearly or effectively in words; not articulate.|He was nonarticulate about his feelings. 他无法清晰地表达自己的感受。|inarticulate, ineloquent|articulate, eloquent|adjective



ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + articulate + -ness.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," "articulate" comes from the Latin "articulatus," meaning "jointed" or "distinct," and "-ness" is a suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality.
