agitators:/ˈædʒɪteɪtərz/
n.|煽动者，鼓动者|A person who stirs up others to action, often of a rebellious or political nature. |The agitators were arrested for inciting violence. 煽动者因煽动暴力而被捕。|synonyms: instigators, rabble-rousers, provocateurs|antonyms: peacemakers, mediators, conciliators|noun

agitators:/ˈædʒɪteɪtərz/
n.|搅拌器|A device for stirring or shaking liquids. |The agitators in the washing machine help to clean the clothes thoroughly. 洗衣机里的搅拌器有助于彻底清洗衣物。|synonyms: stirrers, mixers|antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *agitator, from Latin agitatus, past participle of agitare “to put in motion, drive, excite, rouse, discuss,” frequentative of agere “to do, drive, lead, act”
