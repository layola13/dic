darbys:/ˈdɑːrbiz/| n.|达比鞋，一种系带皮鞋|A type of lace-up leather shoe, typically with a low heel and plain toe.  Originally worn for hunting and other outdoor pursuits, they are now considered a classic and versatile style. |He wore a pair of brown darbys to the office. 他穿了一双棕色达比鞋去上班。|Synonyms:  oxfords, bluchers|Antonyms:  boots, sneakers|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
Derived from the name of an English shoemaker, believed to be one of the first to popularize this style of shoe in the 19th century.
