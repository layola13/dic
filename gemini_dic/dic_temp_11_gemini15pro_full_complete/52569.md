diffuser:/dɪˈfjuːzər/| n.|扩散器；漫射器|A device that spreads something, such as light, heat, or sound, over a wider area.|The photographer used a diffuser to soften the harsh light.|Synonyms: distributor, spreader, disperser|Antonyms: concentrator, focuser|noun

diffused:/dɪˈfjuːzd/| adj.|弥漫的；分散的|Spread out over a wide area; not concentrated.|The light was diffused by the fog.|Synonyms: scattered, dispersed, spread out|Antonyms: concentrated, focused, localized|adjective

diffuse:/dɪˈfjuːz/| v.|扩散；传播；散布|To spread or cause to spread over a wide area or among a large number of people.|The scent of flowers diffused through the garden.|Synonyms: spread, disseminate, disperse|Antonyms: concentrate, gather, collect|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin diffusus, past participle of diffundere "to pour out, spread out," from dis- "out" + fundere "to pour."
