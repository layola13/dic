Crimea:/kraɪˈmiːə/| n.|克里米亚|a peninsula in eastern Europe, north of the Black Sea, which has been disputed territory between Russia and Ukraine. Russia annexed Crimea in 2014.|Russia invaded Crimea in 2014. |Synonyms:  Crimean Peninsula|Antonyms: N/A|noun


Etymology->
From Medieval Latin *Crimea, from Kipchak *Qırım, from Crimean Tatar Qırım. Ultimately from Cuman qır “hill, elevation”.
