imprudencies: /ɪmˈpruːdənsiz/ | n. | 轻率行为；鲁莽行为；不谨慎 | Lack of caution or good judgment; rashness. |  His youthful imprudencies cost him dearly later in life.  他年轻时的轻率行为在他以后的生活中让他付出了高昂的代价。 | Synonyms: follies, indiscretions, recklessness, carelessness | Antonyms: caution, prudence, wisdom, foresight | Noun

Etymology: Late Middle English: from Old French imprudence, from Latin imprudentia, from imprudent- ‘not foreseeing’, from in- ‘not’ + prudent- ‘foreseeing’.
