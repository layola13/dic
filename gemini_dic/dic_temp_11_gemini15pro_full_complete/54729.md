disparage: /dɪˈspærɪdʒ/ | vt. | 贬低，轻视，诋毁 | To speak about someone or something in a way that shows you do not respect or value them. | He disparaged his competitor's products. 他贬低了竞争对手的产品。|  The article disparaged the efforts of the volunteers. 这篇文章贬低了志愿者的努力。| synonyms: belittle, denigrate, deprecate, minimize, undervalue | antonyms: praise, commend, laud, extol | verb

disparaging: /dɪˈspærɪdʒɪŋ/ | adj. | 贬低的，轻蔑的 | Expressing a negative opinion of someone or something. |  He made disparaging remarks about her appearance. 他对她的外貌做出了贬损的评论。| She gave him a disparaging look. 她轻蔑地看了他一眼。| synonyms: derogatory, uncomplimentary, critical, negative | antonyms: complimentary, flattering, praising, positive | adjective

disparagement: /dɪˈspærɪdʒmənt/ | n. | 贬低，轻视 | The act of speaking about someone or something in a way that shows you do not respect or value them. |  The constant disparagement of her work made her quit.  对她工作的不断贬低使她辞职了。| He endured years of disparagement from his colleagues. 他忍受了同事们多年的贬低。| synonyms: denigration, belittlement, depreciation | antonyms: praise, commendation, acclaim | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desparagier "to marry someone of unequal rank," from des- "down" + parage "rank, lineage." The sense shifted to "degrade" and then to "belittle" in the 16th century.
