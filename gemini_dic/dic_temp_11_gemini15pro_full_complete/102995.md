jeerings:/ˈdʒɪərɪŋz/
n.
嘲笑，奚落，讥讽
The constant jeerings from the crowd made the performer nervous.
人群不断的嘲笑让表演者很紧张。
The jeerings of the opposing team's fans were relentless.
对方球队球迷的嘲笑毫不留情。
Synonyms:  mockery, taunts, ridicule, scoffs
Antonyms:  praise, applause, compliments, cheers
noun

Etymology:
From the verb "jeer," which originated in the late 16th century.  The exact origin is uncertain, but it may be related to the Middle Dutch "scheren" (to shear, cut), perhaps suggesting a cutting remark.
