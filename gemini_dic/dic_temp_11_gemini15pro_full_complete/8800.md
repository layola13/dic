antiempiricisms:/ˌæntiɪmˈpɪrɪsɪzəmz/
|n.|反经验主义|Theories that oppose or are skeptical of empiricism, the view that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience.|His antiempiricisms led him to distrust scientific findings.|近义词:rationalism, idealism|反义词:empiricism|词性:名词

Etymology:
"anti-" (Greek) meaning "against" + "empiricism" (from Greek "empeiria" meaning "experience").
