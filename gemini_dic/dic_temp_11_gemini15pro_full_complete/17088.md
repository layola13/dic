baton:/ˈbætɒn/ (UK), /bəˈtɑːn/ (US)| n.|指挥棒；警棍；接力棒|A stick used by a conductor, a police officer, or in a relay race. |The conductor raised his baton and the orchestra began to play. 指挥举起了指挥棒，乐队开始演奏。

The police officer used his baton to defend himself. 警官用警棍自卫。|近义词：stick, rod, club (for police baton), staff (for conductor's baton)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


baton:/ˈbætɒn/ (UK), /bəˈtɑːn/ (US)| v.|将…交给；传给（尤指责任、任务）|To pass something on to someone else, especially responsibility or a task.|She passed the baton of leadership to her successor. 她将领导的责任交给了她的继任者。

He was batoned by his boss to organize the next conference.  他的老板把组织下一次会议的任务交给了他。|近义词：pass on, hand over, transfer, delegate|反义词: keep, retain|词性:verb
