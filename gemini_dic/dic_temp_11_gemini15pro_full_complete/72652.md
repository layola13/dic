flywheels:/ˈflaɪˌwiːlz/ (US), /ˈflaɪˌwiːlz/ (UK)| n.|飞轮|A heavy revolving wheel in a machine that is used to increase the machine's momentum and thereby provide greater stability or a reserve of power.|The flywheel stores rotational energy and smooths out the power delivery of the engine.|Synonyms: inertia wheel, momentum wheel|Antonyms: brake|noun
