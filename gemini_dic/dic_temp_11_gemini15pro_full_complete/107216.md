laid-down:/leɪd daʊn/
adjective
规定的；确定的；制定的
established; fixed; formulated
The company has laid-down procedures for dealing with complaints.  公司制定了处理投诉的程序。
Synonyms: established, prescribed, stipulated, fixed
Antonyms: flexible, adaptable, changeable
词性: 形容词

laid-down:/leɪd daʊn/
adjective
（武器）放下的
(of weapons) put down
The peace treaty required all parties to lay down their arms. 和平条约要求所有各方放下武器。
Synonyms: surrendered, yielded, relinquished
Antonyms: taken up, wielded, brandished
词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the past participle of the phrasal verb "lay down," which combines the verb "lay" (meaning to put or place) with the adverb "down" (indicating a lower position or a state of rest). The verb "lay" has its roots in Old English "lecgan," while "down" comes from Old English "dūn."
