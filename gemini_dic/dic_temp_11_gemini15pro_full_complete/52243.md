dibasicity:/ˌdaɪbeɪˈsɪsɪti/
n.|二元性；二碱性|The property of having two replaceable hydrogen atoms in a molecule, particularly in an acid.  This means the acid can react with two equivalents of a base to form a salt.|The dibasicity of sulfuric acid allows it to form two series of salts.|近义词:bivalence|反义词:monobasicity|词性:noun
dibasicity:/ˌdaɪbeɪˈsɪsɪti/
n.|二元酸度；二碱基性|The property of a base having two sites that can accept a proton (H+).|The dibasicity of calcium hydroxide is reflected in its ability to neutralize two moles of a monoprotic acid.|近义词:bivalence|反义词:monobasicity|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From di- (two) + basic + -ity (state or quality).  "Basic" in this context refers to the ability to neutralize an acid, relating to the presence of replaceable hydrogen ions or the ability to accept protons.
