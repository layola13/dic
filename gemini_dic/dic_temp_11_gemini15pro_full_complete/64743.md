escorted: /ɪˈskɔːr.tɪd/ /ɛˈskɔr.tɪd/ | v. | 护送，陪同 |  Accompanied someone somewhere, especially for protection or guidance. |  The celebrity was escorted by bodyguards.  这位名人由保镖护送。|  guided, accompanied, chaperoned, shepherded | abandoned, deserted | verb

escorted: /ɪˈskɔːr.tɪd/ /ɛˈskɔr.tɪd/ | adj. | 被护送的 |  Having been accompanied by an escort.  | The escorted group arrived safely at the destination. 被护送的团队安全抵达目的地。 | guarded, protected, chaperoned | unescorted, unaccompanied | adjective

Etymology: From the French word "escorte," from Italian "scorta," ultimately from Latin "excorrigere" meaning "to set right."  The original meaning related to a military escort for protection.
