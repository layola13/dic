intransigent:/ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒənt/ | adj. | 不妥协的，不让步的 | Unwilling or refusing to change one's views or to agree about something. |  The government remained intransigent on the issue of tax cuts. 政府在减税问题上 remained 不妥协。|  Example: Her intransigent attitude made negotiations difficult.  她不妥协的态度使谈判变得困难。| Synonyms: uncompromising, inflexible, unyielding, obstinate, stubborn | Antonyms: compromising, flexible, yielding, amenable | 词性: 形容词

intransigence:/ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒəns/ | n. | 不妥协，不让步 | The state of being unwilling or refusing to change one's views or to agree about something. |  His intransigence on this one point threatened to derail the entire project. 他在这一点上的不让步有可能让整个项目脱轨。|  Example: The union's intransigence led to a prolonged strike. 工会的不妥协导致了旷日持久的罢工。| Synonyms: inflexibility, rigidity, stubbornness | Antonyms: flexibility, compromise | 词性: 名词
