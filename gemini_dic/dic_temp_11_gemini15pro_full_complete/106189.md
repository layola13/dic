knockoffs:/ˈnɑːkˌɔːfs/ (US) /ˈnɒkˌɒfs/ (UK)| n. | 仿制品，假货，山寨货 | A copy of something, usually of inferior quality and sold at a lower price. |  She bought a knockoff handbag that looked almost identical to the designer original. 她买了一个几乎和名牌包一模一样的仿制品。

knockoffs:| n. | 廉价复制品 | A cheaply made copy of something, especially clothing or accessories. | The market was full of knockoffs of famous brands.  市场上充斥着各种名牌的廉价仿制品。


knockoff:| n. | 轻松的胜利 | An easy victory or accomplishment. | The game was a knockoff for the home team. 这场比赛对主队来说是一场轻松的胜利。 (This meaning is less common than the "copy" meaning)


knockoff:| adj. | 仿造的，山寨的 | Made as a copy, typically of inferior quality. | He was wearing a knockoff Rolex watch. 他戴着一块仿造的劳力士手表。(This usage is less common than the noun form.)



近义词: imitations, copies, counterfeits, replicas, fakes
反义词: originals, authentics, genuine articles
词性: noun (primarily), adjective (less common)

词源:  "knockoff" originated in the early 20th century as slang, possibly related to "knock off" meaning "to stop working" or "to quickly produce something."  The idea is that a knockoff is quickly and easily produced, often illegally copying an original design.
