copses:/ˈkɒpsiːz/ (UK), /ˈkɑːpsiːz/ (US)| n. |矮树林，小树丛|A thicket of small trees or bushes, especially one regularly cut back for firewood or timber.| We walked through the copses of hazel and willow. 我们穿过榛树和柳树的小树林。|The birds nested in the dense copses.|鸟儿在茂密的矮树林里筑巢。|近义词：thickets, groves, woods|反义词：meadows, plains|词性:名词

copses:| n. |矮树林，小树丛|Plural form of copse.| Several copses dotted the landscape.  几片矮树林点缀着风景。|The copses provided shelter for the deer.|这些矮树林为鹿提供了庇护所。|近义词：thickets, groves, woods|反义词：meadows, plains|词性:名词


词源：From Middle English *copice, from Old French copeiz, from Late Latin colpare “to cut with a blow,” from Latin colaphus “a blow with the fist.”  The original meaning referred to the practice of periodically cutting back trees in a copse to stimulate growth.
