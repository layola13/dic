oratorio:/ˌɔːrəˈtɔːrioʊ/ (US), /ˌɒrəˈtɔːriəʊ/ (UK)
n.
清唱剧，神剧
A large-scale musical work for orchestra, choir, and soloists, usually on a religious theme, performed without costumes, scenery, or action.
Handel's "Messiah" is a famous oratorio.  亨德尔的《弥赛亚》是一部著名的清唱剧。
The oratorio was performed in a grand cathedral. 这部清唱剧是在一座宏伟的大教堂里演出的。
synonym: cantata, sacred music
antonym: opera
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *oratorio* "prayer hall, oratory," from Late Latin *oratorium* "place for prayer," neuter of *oratorius* "of prayer," from Latin *orare* "to pray, speak, plead."
