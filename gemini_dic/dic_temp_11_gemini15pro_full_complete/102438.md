jackeroo: /ˌdʒækəˈruː/ | n. | 牧场实习生，牛仔学徒 | A young man, often from Britain, working on a sheep or cattle station in Australia to gain experience. |  He started out as a jackeroo on a large cattle station in Queensland. 他最初在昆士兰州的一个大型养牛场当牧场实习生。

| 近义词: jillaroo (female equivalent), station hand, farmhand | 反义词: owner, manager | 词性: noun

jackeroo: /ˌdʒækəˈruː/ | n. | 牧场实习生，牛仔学徒 |  Derived from the Aboriginal word "jaragoo" meaning a horseman. Originally referred to a white man who lived with Aboriginal people. Later evolved to mean a young man learning the skills of stockmanship on a large Australian property. |  After graduating, he decided to become a jackeroo to experience the outback lifestyle. 毕业后，他决定成为一名牧场实习生，体验内陆生活方式。
