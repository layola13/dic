capitulators:/kəˈpɪtʃʊleɪtərz/
|n.|投降者，屈服者|People who surrender or give in under pressure are called capitulators.|例句：The capitulators were forced to sign a treaty. 投降者被迫签署条约。

例句：The city's capitulators were granted safe passage. 城市的投降者获得了安全通道。|近义词：surrenderers, submitters, yielders|反义词：resisters, fighters, rebels|词性: 名词

capitulate:/kəˈpɪtʃʊleɪt/
|v.|投降，屈服，让步|To cease resisting; to surrender often after negotiation of terms.|例句：After a long siege, the city finally capitulated.  经过长时间的围困，这座城市最终投降了。

例句：He capitulated to his daughter's demands for a new phone. 他屈服于女儿要一部新手机的要求。 |近义词：surrender, submit, yield, give in|反义词：resist, fight, oppose|词性: 动词
