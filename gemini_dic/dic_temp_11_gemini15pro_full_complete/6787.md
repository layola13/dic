anaphors:/ˈænəfɔːrz/| n.|回指, 前指, 指称语|A word or phrase that refers back to an earlier word or phrase in a text or conversation.  It helps to avoid repetition and maintain coherence. |The dog barked loudly. It then chased the cat.  (Here, "it" is an anaphor, referring back to "the dog") |近义词：reference, pronoun, antecedent|反义词:cataphor|noun
