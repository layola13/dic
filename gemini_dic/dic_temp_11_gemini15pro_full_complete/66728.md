exorbitant:/ɪɡˈzɔːr.bɪ.tənt/ /ɪɡˈzɔːr.bə.tənt/| adj.|过高的，过分的|Charging an exorbitant price; exceeding the customary or appropriate limits in intensity, quality, or size. |The price of gasoline these days is exorbitant.  这些天汽油价格过高。|The hotel charged an exorbitant fee for Wi-Fi.  这家酒店收取的Wi-Fi费用过高。|近义词：excessive, outrageous, unreasonable, preposterous|反义词：reasonable, moderate, fair, modest|词性:形容词

exorbitate:/ɪɡˈzɔːr.bɪ.teɪt/ /ɪɡˈzɔːr.bə.teɪt/| vt.|使加剧，使恶化|To make worse; to increase the severity, violence, or bitterness of; aggravate. |His insensitive comments only exorbitated the situation. 他的冷漠言论只会使情况恶化。|The ongoing drought exorbitated the already dire famine conditions.  持续的干旱加剧了本已严重的饥荒状况。|近义词：aggravate, worsen, exacerbate, intensify|反义词：alleviate, mitigate, improve, ameliorate|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin exorbitatus, past participle of exorbitare (“to deviate, go out of the track”), from ex- (“out of, from”) + orbita (“track, rut”).
