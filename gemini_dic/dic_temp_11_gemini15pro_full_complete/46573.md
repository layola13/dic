dam:/dæm/ | n. | 水坝，堤坝；障碍物 | A barrier constructed to hold back water and raise its level, forming a reservoir used to generate electricity or as a water supply.  Also, anything that obstructs or restrains. | The Hoover Dam is a marvel of engineering. 胡佛水坝是工程学的奇迹。|  barrier, embankment, obstacle |  opening, passage | noun
dam:/dæm/ | vt. | 筑坝，控制；抑制 | To build a dam; to hold back or restrain. | They dammed the river to create a reservoir.  他们在河上筑坝建造水库。 | restrict, control, contain | release, unleash | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *damme*, from Middle Dutch *dam*, *damm*, from Proto-Germanic *dammaz*. Cognate with Old English *fordemman* (to dam up, obstruct), German *Damm* (dam, dike), Old Norse *dammr* (dam, pond). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₂- (to put, place, do).
