moralizing: /ˈmɔːrəlaɪzɪŋ/ /ˈmɔːrələɪzɪŋ/
| adj. | 说教的，道德化的 | Engaging in or expressing moral pronouncements, especially in a self-righteous or tedious way. |  He gave a moralizing lecture about the dangers of drinking. 他做了一场关于饮酒危害的说教。 | 近义词: preachy, sanctimonious, didactic | 反义词: non-judgmental, accepting | 词性: 形容词

moralizing: /ˈmɔːrəlaɪzɪŋ/ /ˈmɔːrələɪzɪŋ/
| n. | 说教 | The act of expressing moral pronouncements, especially in a self-righteous or tedious way. |  His constant moralizing was tiresome. 他不断的 moralizing 令人厌烦。 | 近义词: preaching, sermonizing | 反义词: tolerance | 词性: 名词

词源: From the verb "moralize," which comes from the late Middle English word *moralisen*, derived from the Old French *moraliser* and ultimately from the Latin *moralis* (relating to character).
