ostentate: /ˌɒstənˈteɪt/ (US) /ˌɒstɛnˈteɪt/ (UK)| v. | 炫耀，卖弄 | to display or show something in a way that is meant to impress people | He ostentatiously displayed his expensive watch. 他炫耀地展示了他昂贵的手表。 | flaunt, show off, parade | downplay, hide, conceal | verb

ostentatious: /ˌɒstənˈteɪʃəs/ (US) /ˌɒstɛnˈteɪʃəs/ (UK)| adj. | 炫耀的，卖弄的 | characterized by or given to ostentation; pretentious | Her ostentatious jewelry attracted unwanted attention. 她炫耀的珠宝引起了不必要的注意。 | showy, flamboyant, pretentious | modest, understated, humble | adjective

ostentation: /ˌɒstənˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 炫耀，卖弄 | pretentious and vulgar display, especially of wealth and possessions | The ostentation of the wedding was criticized by many. 这场婚礼的铺张浪费受到了很多人的批评。 | showiness, flamboyance, extravagance | modesty, simplicity, humility | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Latin *ostentāre*, frequentative of *ostendere* "to exhibit, show," from *obs-* "before" (see ob-) + *tendere* "to stretch" (see tenet).  The frequentative suffix *-tāre* indicates repeated or intensive action.
