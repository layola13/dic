catapult:/ˈkætəˌpʌlt/ | /ˈkætəˌpʊlt/ | n. vt. |弹弓，投石器；(v.)猛投，发射；突然提升|A catapult is a device used to hurl or launch a projectile a considerable distance without the aid of explosive devices. As a verb, it means to hurl or launch something with or as if with a catapult, or to suddenly propel someone to a new level of fame, power, etc. | The soldiers used a catapult to launch rocks at the castle walls. 士兵们用投石器向城堡的城墙发射石块。| The scandal catapulted her into the media spotlight. 这桩丑闻使她一下子成了媒体关注的焦点。| synonyms: slingshot, launcher, propel, hurl, fling | antonyms: catch, receive, lower | noun, verb
