disgracing:/dɪsˈɡreɪsɪŋ/
verb
使丢脸，使蒙羞
bringing shame or dishonor to someone or something
例句：He was disgraced by his involvement in the scandal.  他因卷入丑闻而名誉扫地。

例句：She felt she was disgracing her family by her actions. 她觉得自己的行为让家人蒙羞。
近义词：shaming, humiliating, dishonoring
反义词：honoring, glorifying
词性：v.


disgrace:/dɪsˈɡreɪs/
noun
耻辱，丢脸
loss of reputation or respect, especially as the result of a dishonorable action
例句：His behavior brought disgrace upon his family. 他的行为给他的家庭带来了耻辱。

例句：It's a disgrace that so much food is wasted.  这么多食物被浪费真是太可惜了。
近义词：shame, dishonor, ignominy
反义词：honor, glory, credit
词性：n.


disgraceful:/dɪsˈɡreɪsfəl/
adjective
可耻的，丢脸的
shockingly unacceptable
例句：His behavior was absolutely disgraceful. 他的行为绝对是可耻的。

例句：The conditions in the prison were disgraceful.  监狱里的条件很糟糕。
近义词：shameful, dishonorable, shocking
反义词：honorable, respectable, admirable
词性：adj.
