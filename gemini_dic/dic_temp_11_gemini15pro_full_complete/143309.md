outduelled:/ˌaʊtˈdjuːəld/ | v. | 胜过，击败（尤指用剑） | To defeat someone in a duel or contest, especially one involving skill or cunning. | He outduelled his opponent in the fencing competition. 他在击剑比赛中击败了对手。 |  The politician outduelled his rival in the debate.  这位政治家在辩论中胜过了他的对手。 | 近义词：outwit, outmaneuver, defeat, vanquish, conquer | 反义词：lose to, be defeated by | 词性: 动词

词源:

"out-" (prefix):  meaning "surpassing," "exceeding," "external," related to Old English "ūt," "out."
"duel" (noun/verb): from Old French "duel" or Italian "duello," meaning "a fight between two persons," ultimately from Latin "duellum," originally "war," then "fight between two," literally "two," related to "duo."
"-ed" (suffix): past tense marker.
