haematoin:/ˌhiːməˈtəʊɪn/ /ˌheməˈtoʊɪn/ | n. | 血红素，高铁血红素 | A bluish-black or brown pigment formed by oxidation of heme, especially in bruised tissue.  It is the oxidized form of heme and contains iron in the ferric (Fe3+) state. |  Example 1: The bruise showed signs of haematoin deposition.  瘀伤显示出血红素沉积的迹象。

Example 2:  Haematoin is responsible for the characteristic colour of a bruise. 血红素是造成瘀伤特有颜色的原因。|Synonym: ferriheme | Antonym: heme | Noun
