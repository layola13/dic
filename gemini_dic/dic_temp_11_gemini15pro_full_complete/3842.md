agonothete:/ˈæɡənəˌθiːt/| n. |竞赛裁判，主持竞技的人|An official judge of contests, especially in ancient Greece.  A judge or superintendent of games.  Someone who presided over and directed public games, especially in ancient Greece.|The agonothete oversaw all aspects of the Panathenaic Games, from the selection of athletes to the awarding of prizes.|Synonym: judge, umpire, referee, arbiter, adjudicator|Antonym: contestant, competitor, participant|noun
