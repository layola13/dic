flightier:/ˈflaɪtɪər/ (US), /ˈflaɪtɪə/ (UK)| adj.|更轻浮的，更反复无常的，更易激动的|More frivolous, capricious, or easily excited; more flighty.|She's become flightier since she won the lottery.|Synonyms: fickle, frivolous, capricious, erratic, whimsical, volatile|Antonyms: steady, stable, constant, reliable, steadfast|Adjective


flighty:/ˈflaɪti/ (US), /ˈflaɪti/ (UK)| adj.|轻浮的，反复无常的，易激动的|Frivolous, capricious, or easily excited; given to sudden changes of mind or behavior.|He was a flighty young man, never settling down in one place for long.|Synonyms: fickle, frivolous, capricious, erratic, whimsical, volatile|Antonyms: steady, stable, constant, reliable, steadfast|Adjective



ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *flighti*, from Old English *flīhtig* (“apt to flee, timid, fleeting”), from *flēogan* (“to flee, fly”).
