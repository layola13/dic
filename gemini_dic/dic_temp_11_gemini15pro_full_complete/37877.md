complacent:/kəmˈpleɪsnt/ | adj. | 自满的，得意的 | Showing smug or uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements. |  He had become complacent after years of success.  他多年成功后变得自满了。 |  smug, self-satisfied, pleased with oneself  |  dissatisfied, concerned, anxious | adjective

complacent:/kəmˈpleɪsnt/ | adj. | 自满的，得意的 |  Unconcerned about the potential for improvement or change, often to one's detriment. | She was complacent about her exam results, not realizing she could have done much better. 她对自己的考试成绩感到自满，没有意识到自己本来可以做得更好。 |  apathetic, unconcerned |  driven, ambitious | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin complacens, present participle of complacere (“to please greatly”), from com- (“with, together”) + placere (“to please”).
