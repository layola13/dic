moraine:/məˈreɪn/| n. |冰碛|A mass of rocks and sediment carried down and deposited by a glacier, typically as ridges at its edges or extremity.|The hikers struggled across the rocky moraine.  徒步旅行者艰难地穿过岩石冰碛。|

The lateral moraine was clearly visible from the valley floor. 从谷底可以清楚地看到侧碛。|synonyms:  glacial drift, glacial till,  |antonyms: (No direct antonyms for a geological formation) |noun
