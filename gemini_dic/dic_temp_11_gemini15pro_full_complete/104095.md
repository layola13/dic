jump-on:/ˈdʒʌmp ɒn/ (UK) /ˈdʒʌmp ɑːn/ (US)| n.|猛烈的批评；突然的袭击；便宜货|A sudden, forceful criticism or attack; a bargain.|The politician received a jump-on from the media after the scandal broke.  这位政治家在丑闻曝光后受到了媒体的猛烈抨击。

The market stall had a jump-on sale of winter coats.  市场摊位上有冬季外套的廉价甩卖。|近义词：attack, criticism, bargain, deal|反义词:praise, commendation|noun

jump-on:/ˈdʒʌmp ɒn/ (UK) /ˈdʒʌmp ɑːn/ (US)| adj.|便宜的；特价的|Offered at a bargain price.|These jump-on prices won't last long. 这些特价不会持续很久。|近义词：cheap, bargain, discounted|反义词:expensive, costly|adjective
