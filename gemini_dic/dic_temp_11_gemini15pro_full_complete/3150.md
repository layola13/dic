aether:/ˈiːθər(UK), ˈiːθər(US)| n.| 以太，乙醚 |A subtle or mythical substance believed in ancient and medieval times to fill the upper regions of space and to be a medium for the transmission of light and heat. In modern physics, superseded by the theory of relativity. Also: the clear sky; the upper regions of air beyond the clouds. A volatile, flammable liquid used as an anaesthetic.|The spaceship drifted through the aether. 宇宙飞船漂流在以太中。|synonyms: sky, heavens, firmament, empyrean, ether|antonyms: earth, ground|noun


aether:/ˈiːθər(UK), ˈiːθər(US)| n.| 乙醚 |A colourless, volatile, flammable liquid with a characteristic sweetish odour, used as an anaesthetic and as a solvent.|The patient was given ether before the operation. 病人在手术前被给予了乙醚。|synonyms: diethyl ether, ethoxyethane|antonyms: N/A|noun
