mendaciousnesses:/ˌmɛnˈdeɪʃəsnəsɪz/
|n.| 谎言，虚伪|The mendaciousnesses of the politician were well documented.  这位政客的谎言都有详细记录。|
|Etymology: From mendacious + -ness.  Mendacious comes from Late Latin mendac-, stem of mendax "lying," present participle of mentiri "to lie," from PIE root *mend- "physical defect, fault."  The suffix -ness forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.|
|Synonyms: lies, falsehoods, untruths, fabrications, deceptions|
|Antonyms: truths, honesties, veracities|
|Word Type: Noun|
