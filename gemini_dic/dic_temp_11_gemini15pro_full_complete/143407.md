outflanked: /ˌaʊtˈflæŋkt/ | v. | 包抄，迂回，智胜 | to gain a strategic advantage over someone by maneuvering around their flank; to surpass or circumvent someone in a clever or skillful way | The army outflanked the enemy and forced them to retreat.  军队包抄了敌人，迫使他们撤退。 | The company outflanked its competitors by launching a new product line.  该公司通过推出新的产品线智胜了竞争对手。 | 近义词: bypass, circumvent, outmaneuver, surpass | 反义词: confront, face | 词性: 动词

词源:  "out-" (prefix meaning "beyond" or "outside") + "flank" (noun referring to the side of a military formation or, more generally, the side of anything).  The original military sense of the word meant to extend one's lines beyond the enemy's flank, thus gaining a strategic advantage.
