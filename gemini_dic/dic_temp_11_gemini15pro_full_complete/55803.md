diversion:/daɪˈvɜːrʒn/ /dɪˈvɜːrʒn/| n.|转移, 分散注意力, 消遣, 娱乐|An activity that diverts the mind from tedious or serious concerns; a recreation or pastime.|例句：Reading is my favorite diversion. 阅读是我最喜欢的消遣。

例句：The magician's sleight of hand was a clever diversion. 魔术师的手法是一种巧妙的转移注意力。|近义词：entertainment, amusement, recreation, pastime, distraction|反义词：work, labor, toil, business|词性:noun


divert:/daɪˈvɜːrt/ /dɪˈvɜːrt/| vt.|转移, 使转向, 分散注意力, 使娱乐|To turn aside from a course or direction; to distract.|例句：The loud noise diverted my attention. 喧闹的声音分散了我的注意力。

例句：The police diverted traffic around the accident. 警察将交通绕过事故现场。|近义词：redirect, reroute, distract, entertain, amuse|反义词：focus, concentrate|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *divertir*, from Latin dīvertere (“to turn aside, turn away”), from dī- (“aside, away”) + vertere (“to turn”).
