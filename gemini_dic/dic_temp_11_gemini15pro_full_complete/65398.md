euphemists:/ˈjuːfəmɪsts/| n.|委婉语使用者，委婉说法者|A euphemist is someone who uses euphemisms, which are mild or indirect words or expressions substituted for ones considered to be too harsh or blunt when referring to something unpleasant or embarrassing.|The euphemist preferred to say "passed away" instead of "died". 这位委婉语使用者更喜欢用“去世”而不是“死亡”。|synonyms:  understaters, softeners|antonyms:  blunt speakers, straight talkers|noun

euphemism:/ˈjuːfəmɪzəm/| n.|委婉语，委婉说法|A mild or indirect word or expression substituted for one considered to be too harsh or blunt when referring to something unpleasant or embarrassing.|“Passed away” is a euphemism for “died”. “去世”是“死亡”的委婉说法。|synonyms:  understatement, circumlocution|antonyms:  dysphemism, bluntness|noun
