dimeran: /daɪˈmɪrən/ |n.|二聚体；二聚物|A molecule or compound formed by the combination of two identical simpler molecules or monomers. |The dimeran formed a stable structure.|近义词：dimer|反义词：monomer|词性：名词


Etymology: From di- (two) + -mer (part) + -an (suffix forming nouns).  Ultimately from Greek *di- (two) and *meros (part).
