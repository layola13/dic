intrusionist:/ɪnˈtruːʒənɪst/
n.
入侵者；闯入者
Someone who intrudes, especially into a place or situation where they are not wanted.
The intrusionist was quickly apprehended by security.  保安迅速逮捕了入侵者。
He saw himself as an intrusionist in their family life. 他觉得自己是他们家庭生活中的入侵者。
近义词: trespasser, invader, encroacher
反义词: guest, host
词性: noun

intrusion:/ɪnˈtruːʒən/
n.
入侵；闯入
The act of intruding, especially into a place or situation where you are not wanted.
The intrusion of the paparazzi into her private life was relentless. 狗仔队对她私人生活的侵扰是无情的。
His unwelcome intrusion interrupted their conversation. 他不受欢迎的闯入打断了他们的谈话。
近义词: invasion, encroachment, interruption
反义词: retreat, withdrawal
词性: noun

intrude:/ɪnˈtruːd/
v.
侵入；闯入；打扰
To come or go into a place or situation where you are not wanted or expected.
I don't want to intrude on your privacy. 我不想侵犯你的隐私。
Don't intrude on their conversation. 不要打扰他们的谈话。
近义词: interrupt, interfere, encroach
反义词: retreat, withdraw
词性: verb

intruding:/ɪnˈtruːdɪŋ/
adj.
侵入的；打扰的
The act of intruding; intrusive.
The intruding noise from the street made it difficult to concentrate. 街上传来的噪音让人难以集中注意力。
Her intruding thoughts kept her awake at night. 她侵入的思绪让她夜不能寐。
近义词: invasive, interfering, disruptive
反义词: welcome, unobtrusive
词性: adjective

intrusive:/ɪnˈtruːsɪv/
adj.
侵入的；打扰的；唐突的
Causing disruption or annoyance through being unwelcome or uninvited.
The constant phone calls were intrusive and annoying.  不断的电话令人烦扰。
They found the security cameras intrusive. 他们觉得安全摄像头很唐突。
近义词: invasive, interfering, disruptive
反义词: welcome, unobtrusive
词性: adjective
