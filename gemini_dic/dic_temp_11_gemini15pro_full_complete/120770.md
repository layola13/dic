misappraise: /ˌmɪsəˈpreɪz/ | vt. | 误判，错误地估计 | To assess or evaluate something incorrectly, especially to underestimate its value or quality. | The antique dealer misappraised the vase and sold it for far less than it was worth. 古董商错误地估价了花瓶，以远低于其价值的价格出售了它。

The company misappraised the market demand for their new product and ended up with a surplus.  这家公司错误地估计了他们新产品的市场需求，最终导致了过剩。| 近义词: underestimate, undervalue, misjudge, miscalculate | 反义词: overestimate, overvalue, appraise correctly | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (meaning "wrongly" or "badly") + appraise (meaning "to assess the value or quality of"). The prefix "mis-" comes from Old English, while "appraise" comes from Old French "apreisier."
