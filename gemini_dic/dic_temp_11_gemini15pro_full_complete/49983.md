Denisovans:/dɪˈniːsəvənz/ /dəˈniːsəvənz/ | n. |丹尼索瓦人|An extinct species or subspecies of archaic human that ranged across Asia during the Lower and Middle Paleolithic. |例句：Denisovans interbred with both Neanderthals and modern humans. 丹尼索瓦人与尼安德特人和现代人都进行过杂交。

|例句：Scientists discovered the Denisovans through DNA analysis of a finger bone found in a Siberian cave. 科学家通过对在西伯利亚洞穴中发现的一块指骨进行DNA分析发现了丹尼索瓦人。|近义词：hominin, archaic human|反义词:modern human|词性:名词

Etymology:
The Denisovans are named after Denisova Cave in the Altai Mountains of Siberia, Russia, where the first fossil remains attributed to them were discovered.  The name "Denisova" itself comes from Russian, referring to a hermit named Denis who supposedly lived in the cave in the 18th century.
