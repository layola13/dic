inflected:/ɪnˈflektɪd/ | adj. | 屈折的，变形的 |  A word that has been changed in form to show grammatical features like tense, person, number, case, etc.  Meaning its ending has been altered.  | The verb "walked" is the inflected form of "walk" showing past tense.  他走过，walked是walk的屈折形式，表示过去式。 | 近义词：modified, altered, changed | 反义词：uninflected, base | 词性：形容词

inflect:/ɪnˈflekt/ | v. | 使屈折，使变形;  影响，左右(意见或观点) | To change the form of a word to show grammatical features like tense, person, number, case, etc., or to modulate the tone of one's voice. |  He inflected his voice to express sarcasm. 他变换声调以表达讽刺。 In Spanish, adjectives are inflected to agree with the nouns they modify. 在西班牙语中，形容词要变形以与它们修饰的名词相一致。 | 近义词：modify, alter, change, modulate | 反义词：maintain, keep | 词性：动词

inflection:/ɪnˈflekʃn/ | n. | 屈折变化，词尾变化; 语调变化 | A change in the form of a word to express a grammatical function or a change in pitch or tone of the voice.  | The inflection of his voice indicated his uncertainty.  他说话的语调表明了他的不确定。  The plural inflection of "cat" is "cats". cat的复数变形是cats. | 近义词：modulation, tone, change, modification | 反义词：monotony | 词性：名词
