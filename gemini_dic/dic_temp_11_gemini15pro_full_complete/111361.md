livered:/ˈlɪvərd/| adj.|面色苍白的，脸色发灰的；有…肝脏的(一般用于动物)；胆怯的，懦弱的|Having a liver; having a particular kind of liver; (figuratively) cowardly.  The figurative meaning derives from the ancient belief that the liver was the seat of courage. |例句：He looked livered and unwell. 他看起来面色苍白，不太舒服。

例句：The butcher prepared the livered calf for sale. 屠夫准备出售带肝脏的小牛。 (指动物的肝脏)

|近义词：pale, pallid, ashen, wan, cowardly, timid, faint-hearted|反义词：ruddy, flushed, brave, courageous, bold|词性:形容词

PHRASE->
white-livered coward  胆小鬼 (This combines "livered" with another term for paleness to emphasize cowardice.)
lily-livered  胆怯的  (Similar to "white-livered," using "lily" to denote paleness.)
chicken-livered  胆小的 (Another common idiom, comparing cowardice to a chicken's perceived timidity.)



词源:  From liver + -ed.  The figurative meaning of "cowardly" comes from the ancient belief that the liver was the seat of courage and passion.
