conquered: /ˈkɒŋkəd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːŋkərd/ (US)| v. adj.|征服，克服，战胜（动词）; 被征服的（形容词）|To defeat or overcome by force, especially in war; to gain control of (a place or people) by military force.  To successfully overcome (a feeling or problem).|
例句：
The Normans conquered England in 1066.  诺曼人于1066年征服了英格兰。

He finally conquered his fear of heights. 他终于克服了恐高症。

近义词：defeated, vanquished, overcome, subdued, mastered
反义词：surrendered, yielded, lost, failed
词性：verb (primarily), adjective (less common)

conquered: /ˈkɒŋkəd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːŋkərd/ (US)| adj.|被征服的|Having been defeated and brought under control by force, especially in war.|
例句：
The conquered territories were ruled by a foreign power. 被征服的领土由外国势力统治。

The conquered people were forced to pay tribute. 被征服的人民被迫纳贡。

近义词：defeated, vanquished, subdued
反义词：victorious, unconquered
词性：adjective
