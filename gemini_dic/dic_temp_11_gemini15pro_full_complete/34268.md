claptrap:/ˈklæptræp/| n. |空洞的言辞；哗众取宠的言论；花招；噱头|A claptrap is a remark or statement that sounds impressive but is actually insincere or meaningless.|The politician's speech was full of claptraps, but it lacked substance. 这位政客的演讲充满了哗众取宠的言论，但缺乏实质内容。|Synonyms: gimmick, trick, artifice, sham, platitude |Antonyms: sincerity, genuineness, authenticity|noun
claptraps:/ˈklæptræps/| n. |空洞的言辞（复数）|Plural form of claptrap.|His campaign promises were just a bunch of claptraps. 他的竞选承诺只是一堆空话。|Synonyms: gimmicks, tricks, artifices, shams, platitudes |Antonyms: sincerities, genuinenesses, authenticities|noun
