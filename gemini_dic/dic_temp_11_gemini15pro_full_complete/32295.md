chieftains:/ˈtʃiːftənz/| n.|酋长；头目；领导人|A person who is the leader of a group, especially a tribe or clan.  The chieftains met to discuss the terms of the treaty.|近义词：leaders, heads, bosses, rulers|反义词:followers, subordinates|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *chefteyn*, borrowed from Old French *chevetain*, from Late Latin *capitaneus* "chief, captain," from Latin *caput* "head."
