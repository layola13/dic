ctenoid:/ˈsiːtənɔɪd/ | adj. n. | 栉鳞的；栉鳞鱼 | (adjective) Having or relating to ctenoid scales, which are thin, overlapping scales with comb-like edges found on many bony fishes. (noun) A fish having ctenoid scales. | Example (adjective):  The perch has ctenoid scales. 河鲈有栉鳞。

Example (noun): The ctenoid showed vibrant colours under the light.  这条栉鳞鱼在灯光下展现出鲜艳的色彩。 | Near synonyms (adjective):  scaly, imbricate | Antonym (adjective): smooth, cycloid (referring to another type of fish scale) | Word type: Adjective, Noun


ctenoidean:/ˌsiːtənɔɪdiən/ | adj. n. | 栉鳞的；栉鳞鱼 | (adjective) Relating to or belonging to the Ctenoidei, a group of fishes characterized by ctenoid scales. (noun) A fish belonging to the Ctenoidei group. | Example (adjective): Ctenoidean fishes are common in tropical waters. 栉鳞鱼类在热带水域很常见。


Example (noun): The scientist studied the anatomy of the ctenoidean. 科学家研究了这种栉鳞鱼的解剖结构。| Near synonyms (adjective): acanthopterygian | Antonym (adjective):  cycloid (referring to another type of fish scale, not a true antonym but a contrasting group)  | Word type: Adjective, Noun
