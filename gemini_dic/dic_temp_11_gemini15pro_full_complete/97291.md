influxious:/ɪnˈflʌkʃəs/ | adj. | 流入的；涌入的；大量的 | Characterized by a great influx; flowing in.  Influx itself means the arrival or entry of large numbers of people or things.  While "influxious" describes something *marked by* a large influx. |  The influxious tide brought many new species to the shore.  涌入的潮水给海岸带来了许多新物种。|  The influxious crowds made it difficult to navigate the streets. 涌入的人群使得在街上行走变得困难。| 近义词: incoming, inundating, flooding | 反义词: outflowing, ebbing, receding | 词性: 形容词

词源:  From Latin "influxus" (a flowing in), past participle of "influere" (to flow in), from "in-" (in) + "fluere" (to flow).
