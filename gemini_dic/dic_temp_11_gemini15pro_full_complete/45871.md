cutbacks:/ˈkʌtbæks/
n.|削减，缩减，裁员|A reduction in something, typically expenses. |The company announced cutbacks in staffing due to declining profits. 由于利润下降，公司宣布裁员。|reductions, decreases, downsizing|increases, expansions|noun


cutback:/ˈkʌtbæk/
n.|削减，缩减|An act or instance of reducing something. |The government implemented a cutback on public spending. 政府实施了公共开支削减计划。|reduction, decrease, curtailment|increase, expansion|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "cut back," meaning to reduce or curtail something.  "Cut" comes from Old English *cutt- (a short blow, a piece cut off) and "back" from Old English bæc (back, backwards).
