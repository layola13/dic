overleaven: /ˌoʊvərˈlɛvən/ | vt. | 使过度发酵；使过度膨胀；加太多酵母 | To add too much leaven (yeast or other raising agent) to dough, causing it to rise excessively and often resulting in a coarse texture or unpleasant flavor.  It can also be used figuratively to mean to overdo or exaggerate something. | The baker overleavened the dough, and the bread came out with large, unsightly holes.  面包师在面团里放了太多的酵母，烤出来的面包有很多难看的大洞。|  The politician overleavened his speech with patriotic rhetoric. 这位政客在他的演讲中过度使用了爱国主义的华丽辞藻。 | overferment, overdo | underleaven | verb
