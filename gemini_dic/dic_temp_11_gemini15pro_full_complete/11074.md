archaeopteryx: /ˌɑːrkiːˈɒptərɪks/ | n. | 始祖鸟 | An extinct primitive toothed bird of the Jurassic period having a long feathered tail and regarded as an evolutionary link between reptiles and birds. |  The discovery of Archaeopteryx provided crucial evidence for the theory of evolution.  始祖鸟的发现为进化论提供了重要的证据。

Archaeopteryx fossils are extremely rare and valuable. 始祖鸟化石极其稀有和珍贵。 | 近义词: urvogel (German, literally "original bird") | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From Ancient Greek ἀρχαῖος (arkhaîos, “ancient”) + πτέρυξ (ptérux, “wing, feather”).
