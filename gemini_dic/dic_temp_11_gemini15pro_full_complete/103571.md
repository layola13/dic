johnnie-come-lately: /ˈdʒɒni kʌm ˈleɪtli/ (British) /ˈdʒɑːni kʌm ˈleɪtli/ (American)| n.| 新来的人；新手；后起之秀|A newcomer or novice, especially one who seeks to share in the benefits of an enterprise, movement, etc. after it has achieved success.|He was treated as a johnnie-come-lately and not taken seriously by the long-time members of the club.|近义词：newcomer, novice, upstart, latecomer|反义词：veteran, old-timer|词性:名词

词源: This term originated in the 19th century. While the exact origin is uncertain, it likely comes from the common personal name "Johnnie" combined with "come lately," literally meaning someone who has arrived recently.  The implication is that this person is arriving late to the party and trying to reap the benefits without having put in the early work.
