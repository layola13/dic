finestill:/ˈfaɪnstɪl/| n.|精馏器，蒸馏器|A finestill, also known as a pot still, is a type of still used in the distillation of spirits such as whisky, rum, and brandy. It typically produces a spirit with a lower proof and more congeners (flavor compounds) than a column still.|The distillery uses a traditional copper finestill to create its signature whisky. 这家酿酒厂使用传统的铜制精馏器来酿造其标志性威士忌。|近义词：pot still, alembic|反义词: column still|词性: 名词

Etymology->
From fine + still (n.).  "Fine" in this context refers to the refined product of the distillation process, not the quality of the still itself.  The term "still" comes from the Middle English "stille," from Old English "stille," meaning "standing without moving," referencing the stationary nature of the device.
