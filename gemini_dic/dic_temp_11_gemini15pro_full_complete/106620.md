Kulturkampf:/ˈkʊltʊrkɑːmpf/
noun
文化斗争
A German term referring to the conflict between the German government under Otto von Bismarck and the Roman Catholic Church, lasting from roughly 1871 to 1878.  It centered on the state's attempt to control aspects of Church life, such as education and appointment of clergy.
The Kulturkampf led to the imprisonment of many priests and bishops. 文化斗争导致许多神父和主教被监禁。
The government's actions during the Kulturkampf sparked widespread resistance from the Catholic population. 政府在文化斗争期间的行动引发了天主教民众的广泛抵抗。
synonyms: struggle, conflict, clash
antonyms: peace, harmony, agreement
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From German, literally "culture struggle."  From Kultur (culture) + Kampf (struggle, fight).
