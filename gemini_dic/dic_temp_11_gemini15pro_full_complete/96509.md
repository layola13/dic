indo-scythian:/ˈɪndoʊ ˈsɪðiən/| adj. n.|印度-斯基泰人的，印度-斯基泰人|Relating to or denoting the Indo-Scythians, a group of nomadic Iranian peoples who migrated into parts of Central Asia and the Indian subcontinent from the mid-2nd century BC to the 4th century AD.|The Indo-Scythian kingdom flourished for several centuries. 印度-斯基泰王国繁荣了几个世纪。|The museum houses a collection of Indo-Scythian artifacts.  博物馆收藏了印度-斯基泰人的文物。|Synonym: Saka (for the people),  Central Asian (broader geographic term)|Antonym:  Native Indian (in the context of Indian subcontinent)|adjective, noun

Etymology:
"Indo-" + "Scythian"
"Indo-"  from Greek "Indos," relating to India.
"Scythian" from Greek "Skythes,"  referring to a nomadic people originally from the Eurasian steppes.
