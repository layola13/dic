hypethral:/haɪˈθriːθrəl/
adjective
露天式的，无顶的
Open to the sky; roofless.
The ancient Greek temple was hypethral, allowing sunlight to illuminate the altar.  这座古希腊神庙是露天的，让阳光照射到祭坛上。
The Roman amphitheater was partly hypethral, offering some protection from the elements while still allowing for an open-air experience.  这座罗马圆形剧场部分是露天的，在提供一些保护免受风雨侵袭的同时，仍然保留了露天体验。
近义词: open-air, roofless, uncovered
反义词: roofed, covered, enclosed
词性: adjective

Etymology:
From Greek *ὑπαίθριος* (hypaithrios)  "under the sky," from *ὑπό* (hypo) "under" + *αἰθρία* (aithria) "clear sky, open air."
