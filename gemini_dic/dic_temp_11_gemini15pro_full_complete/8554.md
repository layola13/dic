antichronism:/ænˈtɪkrənɪzəm/
|n.|时代错误；年代误植；|An error in chronology in which a person, object, event, etc., is assigned a date or period other than the correct one.|The painting depicts a car in a scene set in ancient Rome, a clear anachronism.|近义词:error, misdating, misplacement|反义词:accuracy, correctness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From French *anachronisme*, from Late Latin *anachronismus*, from Ancient Greek *ἀναχρονισμός (anachronismos)*, from *ἀνά (ana, 'up, back, against')* + *χρόνος (khronos, 'time')* + *-ισμός (-ismos, '-ism')*.
