metalepsis:/ˌmɛtəˈlɛpsɪs/| n. | 转喻; 远喻 |A figure of speech in which a word or phrase from one domain of experience is transferred to another, often producing a surprising or incongruous effect.  It involves a "transumption of causality," where one thing is referred to by something else that is only remotely associated with it through a chain of cause and effect. |The poet used metalepsis, referring to the "shining spear" to represent the warrior himself. 诗人使用了转喻，用“闪亮的矛”来指代战士本人。

The film's metalepsis, showing the actor playing the character watching himself on screen, blurred the lines of reality. 电影中使用了转喻，展现了扮演角色的演员在屏幕上观看自己，模糊了现实的界限。 | 近义词：transumption, metonymy | 反义词: N/A | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *metalēpsis, from Ancient Greek μετάληψις (metálēpsis, “participation, receiving, exchange”), from μεταλαμβάνω (metalambánō, “I partake of, receive in exchange”), from μετά (metá, “with, after”) + λαμβάνω (lambánō, “I take”).
