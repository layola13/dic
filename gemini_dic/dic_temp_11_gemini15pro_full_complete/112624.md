low-impact:/ˌloʊ ˈɪmpækt/| adj.|低冲击力的；影响较小的|Having a small effect on something, especially the environment.|例句：Low-impact exercise is good for people with joint problems. 低冲击运动对关节有问题的人有好处。

例句：This type of farming has a low-impact on the environment. 这种耕作方式对环境的影响很小。|近义词：gentle, mild, minimal|反义词：high-impact, significant, major|词性：形容词

Etymology: "low" + "impact"

"low" comes from Old English "lah," meaning "not high, short."

"impact" comes from the Latin "impactus," past participle of "impingere," meaning "to drive or strike into." The modern sense of "influence" or "effect" developed in the 20th century.
