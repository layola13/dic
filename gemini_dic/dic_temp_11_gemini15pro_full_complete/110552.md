limescale:/ˈlaɪmskeɪl/
n.
水垢，石灰垢
A hard, chalky deposit, consisting mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3), that forms on the inside surfaces of kettles, hot-water boilers, and pipes.
The kettle was covered in limescale. 水壶里全是水垢。
近义词:scale, deposit
反义词:(No direct antonym)
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From lime (referring to calcium oxide or related compounds) + scale (a thin flake or layer).  The term refers to the scale-like deposits formed by lime salts.
