extorting: /ɪkˈstɔːrtɪŋ/ | v. | 勒索，敲诈 | The act of obtaining something, especially money, from someone by force, threats, or other unfair means. | He was arrested for extorting money from local businesses. 他因勒索当地企业钱财而被捕。| The kidnappers were extorting a ransom of $1 million. 绑匪勒索了100万美元的赎金。 |  近义词: blackmailing, coercing, forcing | 反义词: donating, giving | 词性: Verb (present participle)
词源: From Latin extortus, past participle of extorquēre "to wrest away, twist out, extort," from ex- "out" + torquēre "to twist."
