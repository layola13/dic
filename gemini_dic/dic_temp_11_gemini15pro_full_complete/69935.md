fescennine:/fɛˈsiːnaɪn/ | n. | 粗俗的歌曲或诗歌 | A coarse or indecent song or poem, especially one sung at weddings in ancient Rome. |  The museum exhibit included a display of ancient Roman fescennines.  博物馆的展览包括一个古罗马粗俗歌曲的展览。 | Synonyms: lampoon, satire (though often less pointed) | Antonyms: hymn, eulogy | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *fescenninus, relating to Fescennia (an ancient Etruscan town), or perhaps from fascinum (a charm, phallus), because of the supposed power of such verses to avert the evil eye.
