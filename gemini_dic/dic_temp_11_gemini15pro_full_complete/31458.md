charlatanistic:/ˌʃɑːrlətənˈɪstɪk/| adj.|江湖骗子的；装模作样的；庸医的|Relating to or characteristic of a charlatan; fraudulently claiming to have more knowledge or skill than one possesses.  Pretending to have skills or knowledge, especially in medicine.|例句：His charlatanistic claims about the miracle cure were quickly debunked by medical experts.  他关于神奇疗法的江湖骗子式说法很快就被医学专家揭穿了。

例句：The charlatanistic salesman promised that the elixir would cure all ills.  那个江湖郎中式的推销员承诺这种长生不老药能治百病。|近义词：fraudulent, deceptive, phony, pretentious, quack, sham|反义词：genuine, authentic, sincere, honest|词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *charlatan* (a quack), from Italian *ciarlatano*, from *ciarlare* (to chatter), from *ciarla* (chatter).  The suffix *-istic* transforms the noun into an adjective.
