exaggerated: /ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtɪd/ | adj. | 夸大的，夸张的 | Enlarged or altered beyond normal proportions, often to create a dramatic or humorous effect. |  He told an exaggerated story about his fishing trip. 他讲述了一个关于他钓鱼之旅的夸张故事。| The advertisement made exaggerated claims about the product's effectiveness.  这则广告夸大了产品功效。|  overstated, inflated, amplified | understated, minimized, downplayed | adjective

exaggerate: /ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪt/ | v. | 夸大，夸张 | To represent (something) as being larger, greater, better, or worse than it really is. | Don't exaggerate – it wasn't that bad!  别夸大了——没那么糟！| She tends to exaggerate when she's telling a story.  她讲故事时往往会夸大其词。| overstate, amplify, inflate | understate, downplay, minimize | verb
