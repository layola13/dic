chattel:/ˈtʃætəl/| n.|动产，奴隶|A chattel is an item of personal property which is movable, as distinct from real property (land or buildings).  It can also refer to a slave, highlighting the dehumanizing aspect of ownership.|例句：The farmer's chattels included livestock, tools, and furniture. 农民的动产包括牲畜、工具和家具。

例句：In some societies, people were treated as chattels, bought and sold like objects. 在某些社会中，人们被当作动产，像物品一样被买卖。|近义词：property, possession, goods, commodity (especially in the commercial sense), slave (historical)|反义词:real estate, land|词性:noun
