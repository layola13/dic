diviner:/dɪˈvaɪnər/| n.|占卜者，预言家|Someone who is believed to have the power to foretell future events or discover hidden knowledge, especially by supernatural means.|The diviner predicted a great victory for the king.|Synonyms: soothsayer, prophet, oracle, seer, fortune teller|Antonyms: skeptic, doubter|noun

divining:/dɪˈvaɪnɪŋ/| n.|占卜，预言|The practice of seeking knowledge of the future or the unknown by supernatural means.|The tribe relied on divining to make important decisions.|Synonyms: prophecy, fortune telling, soothsaying, augury|Antonyms:  scientific inquiry, rationalism|noun

divine:/dɪˈvaɪn/| adj.|神的，神圣的；极好的|1. Relating to or proceeding directly from God or a god. 2. Of, from, or like God or a god. 3.  Excellent; delightful.|1. The divine will is mysterious. 2. She had a divine beauty. 3. The meal was absolutely divine.|Synonyms: holy, sacred, heavenly, godly; wonderful, superb, excellent|Antonyms:  unholy, profane, secular; awful, terrible|adjective

divine:/dɪˈvaɪn/| v.|占卜，预测；猜到|1. Discover (something) by guesswork or intuition. 2. Discover (water, metal, etc.) using a divining rod.|1. He divined her intentions. 2.  He attempted to divine water using a forked stick.|Synonyms:  foretell, predict, foresee, prognosticate; guess, intuit, surmise|Antonyms: misinterpret, misunderstand|verb
