cosurfactants:/koʊˈsɜːrˌfæktənts/| n. |助表面活性剂|Substances that are added to surfactant solutions to enhance the performance of the surfactant, often by increasing its solubility or reducing its interfacial tension.  They can be short-chain alcohols, amines, or other amphiphilic molecules.|The cosurfactants helped to stabilize the emulsion. 助表面活性剂有助于稳定乳液。|Cosurfactants were added to the formulation to improve the wetting properties. 向配方中添加了助表面活性剂以改善润湿性能。|Synonyms: cosolvent, adjuvant|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but one could consider materials that destabilize surfactants.)|Noun
