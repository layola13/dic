melancholily: /ˌmɛlənˈkɒlɪli/ (US) /ˌmɛlənˈkɒlɪli/ (UK) | adv. | 忧郁地，悲哀地 | In a melancholic manner; sadly and pensively. | She walked melancholily through the deserted streets. 她忧郁地走过空旷的街道。|  He sighed melancholily and turned away. 他忧郁地叹了口气，转过身去。 | 近义词: sadly, gloomily, dejectedly, mournfully | 反义词: cheerfully, happily, joyfully | 词性: 副词

词源:
From melancholic + -ly.  Melancholic comes from Late Latin melancholicus, from Greek melankholikos, from melan- (black) + kholē (bile).  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy.
