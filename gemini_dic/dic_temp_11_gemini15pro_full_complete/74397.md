framings:/ˈfreɪmɪŋz/
n.
框架，制定，构成，措辞
the act of framing something; the way something is framed
例句：The framings of the debate influenced public opinion.  这场辩论的框架影响了公众舆论。

例句：Different framings of the issue led to different conclusions.  对这个问题的不同框架导致了不同的结论。

近义词：formulations, constructions, presentations, interpretations
反义词：N/A
词性:noun


framing:/ˈfreɪmɪŋ/
n.
框架，制定，构成，措辞
the act of framing something; the way something is framed
例句：The framing of the question biased the response. 问题的框架使回答产生了偏差。

例句：The framing of the artwork enhanced its beauty.  艺术品的框架增强了它的美感。

近义词：structure, framework, context, perspective
反义词：N/A
词性:noun



frame:/freɪm/
n.
框架，边框，眼镜框
a rigid structure that surrounds or encloses something
例句：The picture hung in a beautiful wooden frame.  这幅画挂在一个漂亮的木框里。

例句：He needed new frames for his glasses. 他需要一副新的眼镜框。

v.
构成，设计，陷害
to create or formulate (something)
例句：The lawyer framed the argument carefully. 律师仔细地构思了论点。

例句：He was framed for a crime he didn't commit. 他被诬陷犯了他没有犯的罪。

近义词：(n.) structure, framework, casing; (v.) formulate, devise, construct, incriminate
反义词：(v.) exonerate
词性:noun, verb


词源: From Middle English *framen, from Old French *framer (modern French framer), of Germanic origin, from Frankish *framjan "to be helpful, useful," related to Old English fremman "to perform, do, make," from Proto-Germanic *framjaną "to further, promote, be useful."
