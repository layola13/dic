organic:/ɔːrˈɡænɪk/ | adj. | 有机的；器官的；组织的；根本的 | Relating to or derived from living organisms; involving or relating to the use of fertilizers or pesticides that are strictly of animal or vegetable origin;  formed as a natural part of something |  例句：Organic farming is becoming increasingly popular. 有机农业正变得越来越受欢迎。

例句：The museum has a fascinating display of organic materials.  博物馆展出了很多迷人的有机材料。| 近义词：natural, biological, living | 反义词：inorganic, synthetic, artificial | 词性：形容词

organic:/ɔːrˈɡænɪk/ | n. | 有机物；有机体 |  A substance that contains carbon and is found in living things; an organism | 例句：Scientists are studying the organic matter found on Mars. 科学家们正在研究在火星上发现的有机物。

例句：The soil is rich in organics. 土壤富含有机物。| 近义词：organism, living thing | 反义词：inorganic matter | 词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *organike*, from Late Latin *organicus* (“serving as an organ, instrumental”), from Latin *organum* (“instrument, tool, organ of the body”), from Ancient Greek *ὄργανον* (*órganon*, “instrument, tool, organ”).
