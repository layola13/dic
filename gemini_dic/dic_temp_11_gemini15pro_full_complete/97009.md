infatuate: /ɪnˈfætʃueɪt/ /ɪnˈfætʃuˌeɪt/ | vt. | 使迷恋；使糊涂 | To inspire with an extravagant or foolish passion. | She was infatuated with the handsome actor. 她被这位英俊的演员迷住了。

He was so infatuated with her that he couldn't see her flaws. 他对她如此迷恋，以至于看不到她的缺点。

| 近义词:enamor, captivate, charm, bewitch | 反义词: disenchant, disillusion |词性:动词


infatuated: /ɪnˈfætʃueɪtɪd/ /ɪnˈfætʃuˌeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 迷恋的；着迷的 | Filled with or marked by foolish or extravagant passion. |  She was completely infatuated with him. 她完全被他迷住了。


He's infatuated with the idea of becoming a rock star. 他痴迷于成为摇滚明星的想法。


| 近义词: smitten, obsessed, enamored, captivated | 反义词: indifferent, apathetic |词性:形容词


infatuation: /ɪnˌfætʃuˈeɪʃn/ /ɪnˌfætʃuˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 迷恋；着迷 | An intense but short-lived passion or admiration for someone or something. | Her infatuation with him lasted only a few weeks. 她对他的迷恋只持续了几个星期。


He had a brief infatuation with sports cars. 他曾短暂地迷恋过跑车。


| 近义词: crush, obsession, passion | 反义词: indifference, apathy |词性:名词


词源: From Late Latin *infatuatus, past participle of *infatuare (“to make foolish”), from in- (“in, into”) + Latin fatuus (“foolish”).
