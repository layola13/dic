neoliberal:/ˌniːoʊˈlɪbərəl/ | adj. n. | 新自由主义的；新自由主义者 | Relating to a modified form of liberalism that emphasizes free-market capitalism. |  Example:  Neoliberal policies often favor deregulation. 新自由主义政策通常支持放松管制。

| Example:  He's a staunch neoliberal. 他是一个坚定的新自由主义者。 | Synonyms:  free-market, laissez-faire, capitalist | Antonyms: socialist, interventionist, protectionist | adjective, noun


neoliberalism:/ˌniːoʊˈlɪbərəlɪzəm/ | n. | 新自由主义 | A modified form of liberalism tending to favor free-market capitalism. | Example: The rise of neoliberalism in the 1980s. 20世纪80年代新自由主义的兴起。

| Example:  Critics argue that neoliberalism increases inequality. 批评家认为新自由主义加剧了不平等。 | Synonyms:  free-market capitalism, laissez-faire economics | Antonyms: socialism, interventionism | noun



ETYMOLOGY->
"neo-" (new) + "liberal" (favoring individual liberty and free markets).  The term emerged in the late 1930s, but gained prominence in the 1970s and 1980s.
