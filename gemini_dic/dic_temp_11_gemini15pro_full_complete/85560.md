hatchway:/ˈhætʃweɪ/ | n. | 舱口；通风孔；出入口 | An opening in a ship's deck, aircraft, or roof of a building, typically closed by a hatch. | The sailors opened the hatchway to access the cargo hold below. 水手们打开了舱口，以便进入下面的货舱。| synonyms: opening, hatch, doorway, portal | antonyms: closure, blockage | noun

hatchway:/ˈhætʃweɪ/ | n. | （尤指屋顶或天花板上的）活板门，天窗 | A hinged or sliding door covering a hatchway. | He climbed through the hatchway in the ceiling to the attic. 他爬过天花板上的活板门，来到阁楼。 | synonyms: trapdoor, skylight | antonyms: floor, base | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *haccheway, from Old English hæccweg, from hæcc (“hatch, gate”) + weg (“way”).
