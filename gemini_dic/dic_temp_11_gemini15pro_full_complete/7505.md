annals's:/ˈænəlzɪz/ | n. | 记录；年鉴；历史 |  A record of events year by year, typically in a historical context; historical records.  Derived from the Latin "annales" meaning "yearly records." The added 's represents a possessive form, though it is grammatically unconventional and generally avoided in formal writing. It suggests the annals belonging to something or someone. | Example:  The annals's of the town revealed a long history of shipbuilding. 该镇的记录揭示了其悠久的造船历史。 | Synonym: chronicles, records, archives | Antonym:  fiction, fabrication | Noun
