brutalize: /ˈbruːtəlaɪz/ | vt. | 残暴对待，野蛮对待，使麻木不仁 | to treat someone or something in an extremely cruel and violent way | The soldiers brutalized the villagers. 士兵们野蛮地对待村民们。|  The constant violence has brutalized the population.  持续的暴力已经使民众麻木不仁了。 | 近义词: batter, abuse, maltreat, mistreat | 反义词: cherish, protect, nurture | 词性: 动词
brutalization: /ˌbruːtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 残暴行为，野蛮行径 | the act of brutalizing someone or something | The brutalization of the prisoners of war was horrific. 战俘遭受的残暴行径令人发指。 | The report detailed the brutalization of civilians by the occupying army.  报告详细描述了占领军对平民的暴行。 | 近义词: cruelty, savagery, barbarity | 反义词: kindness, compassion, humanity | 词性: 名词

词源: From brutal + -ize.  Brutal comes from the Latin word "brutalis," meaning "of beasts," derived from "brutus," meaning "dull, senseless, irrational, brute, beast."
