coky:/ˈkoʊki/| adj.|傲慢的，自大的|Conceited or arrogant, especially in a bold or cheeky way.  Often implies a playful or swaggering kind of arrogance, rather than outright unpleasantness.|He strutted around with a cocky grin after winning the race. 他赢得比赛后趾高气扬地走来走去，脸上带着傲慢的笑容。|近义词：arrogant, conceited, presumptuous, swaggering, bold, cheeky|反义词：humble, modest, unassuming|词性:形容词

词源:
Likely a shortening of "cocky-olly," a dialectal term meaning "pert" or "saucy," which itself might be related to "cock" (male bird), referring to the strutting behavior of a rooster.
