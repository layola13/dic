bamboozlers:/bæmˈbuːzlərz/
| n. |骗子；欺骗者|People who deceive or trick others. Those who confuse or perplex others with elaborate stories or explanations.|The bamboozlers tricked the tourists into buying fake souvenirs. 这些骗子诱骗游客购买假纪念品。|Synonyms: swindlers, con artists, tricksters, deceivers, fraudsters|Antonyms: honest people, truth-tellers|noun


bamboozler:/bæmˈbuːzlər/
| n. |骗子；欺骗者|A person who deceives or tricks others. Someone who confuses or perplex others with elaborate stories or explanations.|The bamboozler made off with the elderly woman's life savings. 这个骗子卷走了老妇人的毕生积蓄。|Synonyms: swindler, con artist, trickster, deceiver, fraudster|Antonyms: honest person, truth-teller|noun


bamboozle:/bæmˈbuːzl/
| vt. |欺骗，迷惑|To deceive or trick someone. To confuse or perplex someone with elaborate stories or explanations.|He bamboozled them into believing he was a millionaire. 他骗得他们相信他是个百万富翁。|Synonyms: deceive, trick, hoodwink, swindle, mislead, confound, perplex|Antonyms: enlighten, clarify, be honest|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain, possibly from 18th-century underworld slang.  May be related to the verb "bamboozle," meaning to deceive or mystify, the etymology of which is also uncertain.
