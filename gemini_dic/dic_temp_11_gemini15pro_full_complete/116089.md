martyring: /ˈmɑːtərɪŋ/ (US), /ˈmɑːtərɪŋ/ (UK)
|v.|折磨；殉道；牺牲|The act of making oneself suffer or endure hardship, often for a cause or belief, or making someone else suffer.|She felt she was martyring herself for her children, constantly sacrificing her own needs.|近义词: sacrificing, immolating, tormenting|反义词: indulging, pampering|词性: 动词 (present participle of martyr)

martyring: /ˈmɑːtərɪŋ/ (US), /ˈmɑːtərɪŋ/ (UK)
|n.|折磨；殉道；牺牲|The act of being a martyr or making someone into a martyr.|Her constant martyring was starting to annoy her friends.|近义词: sacrifice, immolation|反义词: indulgence, pampering|词性: 名词 (gerund of martyr)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *martyren*, from Old French *martirer*, from Late Latin *martyrizare*, from Greek *μαρτυρίζειν (martyrizein)*, "to bear witness," from *μάρτυρ (martyr)*, "witness."
