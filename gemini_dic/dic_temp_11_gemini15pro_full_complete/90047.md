hospitalism:/ˈhɒspɪtəlɪzəm/ (US) /ˈhɑːspɪtəlɪzəm/| n. |住院病, 医院综合症|A state of emotional and physical deterioration, particularly in infants and young children, resulting from prolonged stays in hospitals or similar institutions, often characterized by listlessness, depression, and developmental delays, due to lack of stimulation and emotional support. |The child suffered from hospitalism after being confined to the hospital for several months.  这个孩子在医院住了几个月后患上了住院病。|Synonyms: institutionalization, deprivation syndrome |Antonyms:  thriving, flourishing| noun
