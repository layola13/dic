flea-pit:/ˈfliːˌpɪt/ | n. |跳蚤窝，廉价低级娱乐场所|A run-down, shabby, and often dirty place of entertainment, especially a cinema or theatre.| The old cinema was a real flea-pit, with sticky floors and ripped seats. 这家老电影院真是个跳蚤窝，地板黏糊糊的，座位也破烂不堪。|Synonyms: dive, dump, hole-in-the-wall |Antonyms: palace, luxury theatre| noun

Etymology:  A compound of "flea" and "pit."  "Flea" refers to the small, wingless insects known for infesting dirty places. "Pit" refers to a hole or hollow, often used to describe a low or unpleasant place. The combination suggests a place so dirty and neglected that it might be infested with fleas.
