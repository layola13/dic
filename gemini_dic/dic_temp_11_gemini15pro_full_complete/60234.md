effranchise:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ | vt. |解放，赋予权力（尤指选举权），给予公民权|To give someone the right to vote, or to give a group of people the same rights as other people in a society.|例句：
The Nineteenth Amendment effranchised women in the United States.  第十九修正案赋予了美国妇女选举权。

The company effranchised its employees by giving them stock options. 公司通过给予员工股票期权来赋予他们权力。

|近义词：empower, liberate, free|反义词: disenfranchise, enslave, restrict|词性:动词
