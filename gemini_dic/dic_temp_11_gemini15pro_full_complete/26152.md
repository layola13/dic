busybodied:/ˈbɪziˌbɒdid/ (US) /ˈbɪziˌbɒdid/ (UK)| adj.|爱管闲事的，好打听的|Given to interfering in other people's affairs.|She's such a busybodied woman, always poking her nose into everyone's business.|近义词: interfering, meddlesome, nosy, prying|反义词:  unconcerned, indifferent|词性:形容词

busybody:/ˈbɪziˌbɒdi/ (US) /ˈbɪziˌbɒdi/ (UK)| n.|爱管闲事的人，好打听的人|A person who habitually interferes in other people's affairs.|The neighborhood busybody was always watching everyone from her window.|近义词:  interferer, meddler, nosy parker, gossip|反义词:  mind-your-own-business type|词性:名词


词源:  From busy + body.  Originally (16th century) meaning 'actively or restlessly occupied', it later developed the negative connotation of meddling in others' affairs.
