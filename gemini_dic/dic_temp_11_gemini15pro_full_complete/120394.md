minister:/ˈmɪnɪstər/ (US), /ˈmɪnɪstə(r)/ (UK)| n. v.|部长；大臣；公使；牧师|A member of the government who is in charge of a particular department or has an important position in it; a diplomat who represents their government in a foreign country, ranking below an ambassador; a Christian priest or preacher.|The Prime Minister announced a cabinet reshuffle. 首相宣布改组内阁。|Synonyms: secretary, diplomat, envoy, clergyman, pastor, preacher|Antonyms: parishioner, layman|noun, verb

minister:/ˈmɪnɪstər/ (US), /ˈmɪnɪstə(r)/ (UK)| v.|服侍；照顾|To attend to the needs of someone, especially someone ill or in trouble.|She ministered to the sick and wounded. 她照顾病人和伤员。|Synonyms: tend, attend, care for, serve|Antonyms: neglect, ignore|verb
