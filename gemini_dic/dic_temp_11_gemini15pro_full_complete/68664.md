farand:/ˈfærənd/ | n. | 盛装；华丽的服饰；游行队伍；炫耀；排场 | An appearance or display, especially one that is ostentatious or showy.  A procession, especially one that is festive or celebratory.  |  The king's farand included a procession of richly adorned elephants. 国王的盛装出行包括装饰华丽的大象队伍。 | synonyms: pageant, parade, spectacle, pomp, display | antonyms: simplicity, modesty | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *farende, present participle of faren (“to go, travel, journey”), from Old English faran (“to go, travel, journey”), from Proto-Germanic *faraną (“to travel, fare”), from Proto-Indo-European *per- (“to lead, pass over”). Cognate with Scots fare (“to go, travel”), West Frisian fare (“to go, travel, fare”), Dutch varen (“to sail, navigate, fare”), German fahren (“to drive, travel, go”), Danish fare (“to go, travel, fare”), Swedish fara (“to travel, go, fare”), Icelandic fara (“to go, travel, fare”).
