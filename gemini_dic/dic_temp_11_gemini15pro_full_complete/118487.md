mesmerised:/ˈmezməraɪzd/ (UK), /ˈmezməraɪzd/ (US)| adj.| 着迷的，被迷住的 |Being completely fascinated or hypnotized by something or someone.|  The audience was mesmerised by the magician's performance.  观众被魔术师的表演迷住了。| synonyms: captivated, enthralled, fascinated, spellbound, hypnotized | antonyms: bored, disinterested, indifferent | adjective

mesmerizing:/ˈmezməraɪzɪŋ/ (UK), /ˈmezməraɪzɪŋ/ (US)| adj.|  令人着迷的，迷人的 | Having the power to mesmerize; captivating. | The dancer's mesmerizing performance left the audience breathless.  舞者令人着迷的表演让观众屏住了呼吸。| synonyms: captivating, enthralling, fascinating, spellbound, hypnotic | antonyms: boring, dull, uninteresting | adjective

mesmerize:/ˈmezməraɪz/ (UK), /ˈmezməraɪz/ (US)| v.|  使着迷，迷住 | To hypnotize or fascinate someone completely. | The snake mesmerized the rabbit before striking.  蛇在攻击前迷住了兔子。| synonyms: captivate, enthrall, fascinate, spellbind, hypnotize | antonyms: bore, repel, disgust | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Franz Mesmer (1734-1815), an Austrian physician who developed a theory of animal magnetism, later called mesmerism.  His name became associated with the act of hypnotizing or fascinating.
