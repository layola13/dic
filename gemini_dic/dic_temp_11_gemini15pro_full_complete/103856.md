jro:/ˈdʒroʊ/ | n. | Joint Resolution |联合决议；共同决议| A joint resolution is a legislative measure that requires the approval of both chambers of a bicameral legislature and, in most cases, the signature of the head of state to become law. It has the same legal force as a bill. | Example: The jro authorizing the use of military force was passed by both the House and the Senate.  联合授权使用武力的共同决议案已由众议院和参议院通过。| Synonym: resolution, enactment, decree | Antonym: veto, rejection | noun
