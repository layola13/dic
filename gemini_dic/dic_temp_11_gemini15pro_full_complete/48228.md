deconstruction:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkʃən/
|n.| 解构，拆解，分析|The act or process of deconstructing something, especially a text or an idea, by breaking it down into its constituent parts and exposing its underlying assumptions and contradictions. |The professor's deconstruction of the novel revealed its hidden meanings. 教授对这部小说的解构揭示了其隐藏的含义。| analysis, critique, interpretation | synthesis, construction | noun


Etymology:
From French *déconstruction*, from *dé-* (prefix denoting reversal or undoing) + *construction* (construction).  Ultimately from Latin *constructio* (building, construction).
