overbull:/ˈoʊvərˌbʊl/| v. n.|过分乐观；过分看涨；过度吹捧；牛市过度|To be excessively optimistic or enthusiastic, especially about the stock market; to exaggerate the positive aspects of something.  A state of excessive optimism or enthusiasm, especially about the stock market.|The analyst overbulled the tech stock, predicting unrealistic growth. 分析师过度吹捧了这只科技股，预测了不切实际的增长。|Investors who overbull the market are often disappointed. 过度看涨市场的投资者常常会失望。|synonyms: overestimate, exaggerate, hype, overhype, oversell, boost, tout|antonyms: underestimate, downplay, bearish, undersell|verb, noun
