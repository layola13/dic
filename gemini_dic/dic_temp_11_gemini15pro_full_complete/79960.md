go-alone:/ˌɡoʊ əˈloʊn/ | adj. |单独的，独自的|Going or acting without another person or others; solitary. |She decided to go-alone on the trip. 她决定独自旅行。| He prefers to work go-alone. 他更喜欢独自工作。 | 近义词：solo, single, unaccompanied, independent | 反义词：accompanied, together, joint | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
"go" (verb) + "alone" (adjective).  "Go" comes from Old English "gān," meaning "to go, walk, proceed." "Alone" comes from Old English "all āna," meaning "all one, quite by oneself."
