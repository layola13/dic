monarch:/ˈmɒnək/ (UK), /ˈmɑːnɑːrk/ (US)| n.|君主，帝王|A sovereign head of state, especially a king, queen, or emperor. |例句：The monarch addressed the nation. 国王向全国发表讲话。

例句：The young monarch was crowned in a lavish ceremony.  年轻的君主在一个奢华的仪式上加冕。

近义词：ruler, sovereign, king, queen, emperor|反义词：subject, citizen|词性：noun


monarchical:/məˈnɑːkɪkl/ (US), /məˈnɑːrkɪkəl/ (UK)| adj.|君主的，君主政体的|Relating to or characteristic of a monarch or monarchy. |例句：The country transitioned from a monarchical system to a republic.  这个国家从君主制过渡到共和国。

例句：  He holds a monarchical view of leadership. 他持有君主式的领导观。

近义词：royal, regal, sovereign|反义词：republican, democratic|词性：adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *monarche*, from Latin monarcha, from Greek monárkhēs (μονάρχης), from mónos (μόνος, “alone, single”) + árkhō (ἄρχω, “to rule”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *men- (“to think”).
