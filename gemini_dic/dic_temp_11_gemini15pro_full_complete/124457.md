motte:/mɒt/ (UK) /mɑːt/ (US)| n.|土堆, 小丘,城堡的土墩|A motte is a raised earthwork, a mound of earth forming a small hill, typically a foundation for a castle.|The Norman castle was built on a large motte overlooking the valley.  诺曼城堡建在一个可以俯瞰山谷的大土堆上。|The motte offered a good defensive position. 这个土堆提供了一个良好的防御阵地。|近义词:mound, hill, hillock|反义词:ditch, valley|词性:名词

Etymology: From Old French *motte, from Vulgar Latin *muttum, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Latin mutilus "mutilated, broken off."
