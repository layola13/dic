messing:/ˈmesɪŋ/| v. (present participle of mess)|弄乱，搞砸，瞎搞，干预|Messing with dangerous chemicals can be risky. 弄乱危险化学品可能有危险。|近义词：tampering, interfering, playing, fiddling|反义词：organizing, tidying, fixing|词性:动词(现在分词)

messing:/ˈmesɪŋ/| n. |胡闹，混乱|The kids' messing around in the kitchen made a huge mess. 孩子们在厨房里胡闹，弄得一团糟。|近义词：fooling around, playing around, mischief, chaos|反义词：order, tidiness|词性:名词

messing:/ˈmesɪŋ/| adj. |糟糕的，混乱的|The current political situation is a messing affair. 当前的政治局势一团糟。|近义词：chaotic, disordered, messy, troublesome|反义词：orderly, tidy, organized|词性:形容词


词源: From the verb "mess," which originated from Old French "mes," meaning a portion of food.  The meaning evolved to include "a state of disorder or confusion."
