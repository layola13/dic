pleuritis:/ˌplʊəˈraɪtɪs/| n. |胸膜炎|Pleuritis is an inflammation of the pleura, the thin membrane that surrounds the lungs and lines the inside of the chest cavity. It can be caused by various factors, including infections (viral, bacterial,or fungal), autoimmune diseases, and certain cancers. Symptoms may include chest pain, shortness of breath, and a dry cough.  Diagnosis typically involves a physical exam, chest X-ray, and sometimes CT scan or other imaging studies. Treatment depends on the underlying cause and may involve medication to manage pain and inflammation, or antibiotics if an infection is present.|例句：The doctor diagnosed her with pleuritis after reviewing her chest X-ray and listening to her lungs.
