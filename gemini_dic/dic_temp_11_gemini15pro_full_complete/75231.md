frolicful:/ˈfrɒlɪkfʊl/ (US) /ˈfrɒlɪkf(ə)l/ (UK)| adj.|嬉戏的；欢乐的；爱闹着玩的|Full of gaiety and playful activity; merry; sportive.|The children were frolicful in the park, running and laughing. 孩子们在公园里嬉戏玩耍，跑着笑着。|例句：The lambs were frolicful in the spring sunshine. 春天的阳光下，小羊羔们欢快地嬉戏着。|近义词：playful, sportive, frisky, merry, joyous|反义词：serious, solemn, somber, glum|词性:形容词

frolic:/ˈfrɒlɪk/ (US) /ˈfrɒlɪk/ (UK)| n. vi.|嬉戏；玩闹；娱乐|A playful action or movement; a merrymaking or festivity.|The dog's frolic in the snow delighted the children.  小狗在雪地里嬉戏玩耍，逗得孩子们很开心。|例句：Their summer evenings were spent in innocent frolic. 他们的夏夜都在天真无邪的嬉戏玩耍中度过。|近义词：play, romp, gambol, caper|反义词：work, toil, labor|词性:名词/动词


词源：From Middle English *frolik, from Middle Dutch vrolijc, from Old Dutch *frōlik "joyful," from Proto-Germanic *frōlijaz.
