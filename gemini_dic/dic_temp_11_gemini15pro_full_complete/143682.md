outlawing: /ˈaʊtˌlɔɪŋ/
词性: n.  (gerund, verbal noun)
中文翻译: 非法化，取缔，禁止
英文解析: The act of making something illegal or prohibiting it by law.  It's the gerund form of the verb "outlaw."
例句：The outlawing of smoking in public places has improved air quality.  在公共场所禁止吸烟改善了空气质量。

例句：The government is considering outlawing the sale of certain types of fireworks. 政府正在考虑禁止销售某些类型的烟花。

近义词: prohibiting, banning, forbidding, proscribing
反义词: legalizing, permitting, allowing
词性: n. (verbal noun)


outlaw: /ˈaʊtˌlɔː/
词性: vt.
中文翻译: 宣布…为不合法；禁止；剥夺…的法律保护
英文解析: To make something illegal; to prohibit something by law; to deprive someone of the protection of the law.
例句：The government outlawed the sale of certain drugs. 政府宣布某些药物的销售为非法。

例句：They outlawed smoking in restaurants. 他们禁止在餐馆吸烟。

近义词: ban, prohibit, forbid, proscribe
反义词: legalize, permit, allow
词性: vt.
