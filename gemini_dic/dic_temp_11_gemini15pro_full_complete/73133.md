foragers:/ˈfɔːrɪdʒərz/ (US) /ˈfɒrɪdʒəz/ (UK)| n.|觅食者；搜寻者|People who search for food or provisions.|例句：The foragers returned to the camp with baskets full of berries. 觅食者带着装满浆果的篮子回到了营地。

例句：Early humans were skilled foragers, relying on their knowledge of the natural world to survive. 早期人类是熟练的觅食者，依靠他们对自然世界的了解生存。|近义词：hunter-gatherers, scavengers, searchers|反义词:farmers, cultivators|词性:noun

forage:/ˈfɔːrɪdʒ/ (US) /ˈfɒrɪdʒ/ (UK)| v. n.|觅食；搜寻；饲料| (v.) To search for food or provisions. (n.) Food for animals, especially when taken from the ground.|例句：The pigs were foraging in the woods for acorns. 猪在树林里觅食橡子。

例句：The squirrels foraged for nuts in the park. 松鼠在公园里寻找坚果。|近义词：search, hunt, rummage, feed (v.) fodder, provender (n.)|反义词:plant, cultivate (v.)|词性:verb, noun
