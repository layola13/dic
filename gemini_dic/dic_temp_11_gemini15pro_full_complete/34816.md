clinched:/klɪntʃt/| adj. v.| 确定的；稳操胜券的；（拳头）紧握的；（牙齿）咬紧的 |Confirmed; secured; settled conclusively. Also describes a tightly closed fist or gritted teeth.  |He clinched the victory with a final goal. 他以最后一球锁定了胜局。|近义词：secured, confirmed, settled, guaranteed|反义词：lost, uncertain, precarious|词性:动词/形容词
clinched:/klɪntʃt/| adj. v.|紧握的,咬紧的|Held tightly; firmly grasped. Can refer to a fist, teeth, or a metaphorical grasp on something. |She clinched her fists in anger. 她愤怒地握紧了拳头。|近义词：gripped, grasped, clenched|反义词：released, loosened, open|词性:动词/形容词

词源: From the Middle English *clenchen*, from Old English *clencan* "to bend, twist," related to cling.
