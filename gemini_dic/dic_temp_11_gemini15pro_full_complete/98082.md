inquisitive:/ɪnˈkwɪzətɪv/| adj.|好奇的，爱打听的|Given to inquiry; eager for knowledge; intellectually curious.  Characterized by a desire to learn and understand by asking questions. |She has always been an inquisitive child, constantly asking "why?". 她一直是个好奇的孩子，总是问“为什么？”|Example: The inquisitive journalist dug deep into the story.  好奇的记者深入挖掘了这个故事。|近义词：curious, inquiring, questioning, prying, nosy|反义词：apathetic, indifferent, incurious|词性: adjective

inquisible:/ɪnˈkwɪzɪb(ə)l/| adj.|可询问的，可被调查的|Capable of being inquired into or investigated; subject to or liable to judicial inquiry. |The matter is inquisible by the court.  此事可由法院审理。|Example: The police considered the case inquisible and opened an investigation. 警方认为此案可以调查，并展开了调查。|近义词：investigable, researchable, scrutable|反义词：unquestionable, indisputable|词性: adjective
