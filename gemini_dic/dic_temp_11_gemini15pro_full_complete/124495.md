mouldwarp:/ˈmoʊldwɔːrp/ | n. | 鼹鼠 | A mole.  From its habit of casting up mounds of earth. |  He saw a mouldwarp disappear into the soft earth. 他看到一只鼹鼠消失在松软的泥土里。 | 近义词: mole | 反义词:  (没有直接的反义词) | 词性: 名词

Etymology:
From Middle English *moldwerp, from Old English *moldwyrp, from Proto-Germanic *muld-wurpiz ("earth-thrower"), equivalent to mold +‎ warp (“to throw”). Cognate with West Frisian mûlwerp, Dutch molworp, German Maulwurf.
