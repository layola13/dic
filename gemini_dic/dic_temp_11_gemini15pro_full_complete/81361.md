grapnel: /ˈɡræpnəl/ | n. | 抓钩，锚爪 | A small anchor with several flukes, used especially for dragging or grappling. | The ship's crew used a grapnel to retrieve the lost cargo from the seabed. 船员用抓钩从海底捞回丢失的货物。 |  The diver used a grapnel to secure himself to the wreck. 潜水员用抓钩把自己固定在沉船上。 | 近义词: anchor, hook, grapple | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "release") | noun


grapnel: /ˈɡræpnəl/ | v. | 用抓钩抓住 | To catch or seize with a grapnel. | They grapnelled the drifting boat and towed it to shore.  他们用抓钩钩住漂流的船，并把它拖到岸边。 |  The climbers grapnelled the rock face to ascend the cliff.  攀登者用抓钩抓住岩壁攀登悬崖。 | 近义词: grapple, hook, secure | 反义词: release, detach | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *grapinel, diminutive of grapin "grappling hook," from graper "to grasp," of Germanic origin.
