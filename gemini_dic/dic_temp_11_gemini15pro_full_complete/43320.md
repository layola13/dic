cram-down:/ˈkræmˌdaʊn/
n.|强制通过，强制执行|The act of forcing a plan or decision on someone, especially in a bankruptcy or restructuring context, even if they don't agree.|The creditors were unhappy with the cram-down of the reorganization plan.|imposition, enforcement|negotiation, compromise|noun
cram-down:|/ˈkræmˌdaʊn/|adj.|强制性的，强制执行的|Relating to something that is forced on someone, especially in a financial context.|The judge approved the cram-down provision in the bankruptcy agreement.|forced, imposed|voluntary, negotiated|adjective
