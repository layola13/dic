labor:/ˈleɪbər/ (US), /ˈleɪbə(r)/ (UK)| vi. vt.|劳动，工作；分娩；费力地前进|To work hard; to exert oneself.  To be in childbirth.|例句：She labored for hours in the garden.  她在花园里辛勤工作了几个小时。|近义词：work, toil, strive|反义词：rest, relax|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *labour* (Modern French *labeur*), from Latin *labor* "labor, toil, hardship, exertion, fatigue," related to *labōrāre* "to work, toil, exert oneself."
