disguised:/dɪsˈɡaɪzd/ | adj. |伪装的，掩饰的 |Having an appearance that hides the true form or nature of something or someone.  | The spy entered the enemy camp disguised as a soldier.  这个间谍伪装成士兵进入了敌军营地。| 近义词: camouflaged, masked, concealed, hidden | 反义词: revealed, exposed, obvious, apparent | 词性: adjective

disguise:/dɪsˈɡaɪz/ | n. |伪装，掩饰 | An outward form, appearance, or manner assumed to conceal one's true identity or intentions. | She wore a disguise to avoid being recognized. 她乔装打扮以避免被认出来。| 近义词: camouflage, mask, pretense, facade | 反义词: revelation, exposure | 词性: noun

disguise:/dɪsˈɡaɪz/ | v. |伪装，掩饰| To change the usual appearance, sound, taste, etc., of (someone or something) so as to conceal the true identity, form, or nature. | He disguised his voice on the phone. 他在电话里伪装了自己的声音。| 近义词: camouflage, mask, conceal, hide | 反义词: reveal, expose | 词性: verb
