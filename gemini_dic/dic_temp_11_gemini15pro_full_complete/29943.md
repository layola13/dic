caveatee:/ˈkæviəˈtiː/ |n.|受警告人；被禁止人|A person against whom a caveat has been filed, especially in probate or property law. A caveatee is typically the person whose right or interest is being challenged or restricted by the caveat. | The caveatee was notified of the caveat filed against his claim to the property.| 近义词: defendant, respondent, objector | 反义词: caveator, plaintiff, petitioner | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *caveatatus, past participle of *caveatare, from Latin *caveāre "to beware," from cavea "cage, enclosure, hollow place."
