existentialists:/ˌɛɡzɪˈstɛnʃəlɪsts/
|n.|存在主义者|People who adhere to the philosophy of existentialism, which emphasizes individual freedom, responsibility, and the search for meaning in a seemingly meaningless universe.|Many French writers of the 20th century were existentialists. 许多20世纪的法国作家都是存在主义者。|近义词:philosophers, thinkers|反义词:conformists, traditionalists|词性:noun


existentialist:/ˌɛɡzɪˈstɛnʃəlɪst/
|adj.|存在主义的|Relating to existentialism, a philosophical movement emphasizing individual freedom, responsibility, and the search for meaning in a seemingly meaningless universe.|The play explored existentialist themes of alienation and despair.  这出戏探讨了存在主义的异化和绝望主题。|近义词:philosophical|反义词:conventional|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *existentialiste*, derived from *existentialisme* (existentialism) + *-iste* (-ist).  Ultimately from Latin *existentia* (existence).
