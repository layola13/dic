overcunningly:/ˌoʊvərˈkʌnɪŋli/
adverb|过于狡猾地；过于精明地|In a way that is excessively cunning or clever, often to the point of being deceitful or manipulative.|He overcunningly plotted his revenge, ensuring no one suspected him.|Synonyms:  deceitfully, craftily, artfully, slyly, shrewdly|Antonyms: honestly, frankly, openly, naively, artlessly
词性: 副词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "over" (OE. ofer) + "cunning" (ME. connyng, from "cunnen," to know) + "-ly" (OE. -lice, adverbial suffix).  "Over" intensifies the meaning of "cunning," suggesting an excessive or extreme degree.
