atrium:/ˈeɪtriəm/ (US), /ˈeɪtriəm/ (UK)| n. |心房；中庭；天井|A large open space inside a building, often with a glass roof and plants, or in ancient Roman houses the main inner room.|例句：The hotel has a beautiful atrium filled with tropical plants.  这家酒店有一个充满热带植物的美丽中庭。

例句：The ancient Roman atrium was the center of family life.  古罗马的中庭是家庭生活的中心。|近义词：courtyard, lobby, foyer, hall|反义词:closet, cubicle|词性:noun

atrium:/ˈeɪtriəm/ (US), /ˈeɪtriəm/ (UK)| n. |（心脏的）心房|One of the two upper chambers of the heart that receive blood from the veins.|例句：The blood flows from the atrium to the ventricle. 血液从心房流到心室。

例句：The left atrium receives oxygenated blood from the lungs. 左心房接收来自肺部的含氧血液。|近义词：auricle (anatomy)|反义词:ventricle|词性:noun
