anesthetization:/ˌænɪsθətɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |麻醉；麻醉状态|The process of inducing a loss of sensation, especially for medical purposes. |The anesthetization was successful, and the patient felt no pain during the surgery. 麻醉成功了，病人在手术过程中没有感到疼痛。|Synonyms: numbing, narcosis |Antonyms: awakening, revival | noun



ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin anaesthetisationem, from Ancient Greek ἀναισθησία (anaisthesía, “lack of sensation”), from ἀν- (an-, “without”) + αἴσθησις (aísthesis, “sensation”).
