caliver:/ˈkælivər/ | n. | 轻型火绳枪 | A light matchlock musket. |  The soldier raised his caliver and fired. 士兵举起轻型火绳枪开火。 | Synonyms: musket, arquebus | Antonyms: None | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *calibre* "caliber, bore (of a gun)" (16c.), from Italian *calibro*, probably from Arabic *qalib* "mold, last." The sense shifted in English from the bore of a gun to a type of gun.
