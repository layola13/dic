mediatize:/ˈmiːdiətaɪz/| v. |使媒体化，通过媒体传播，使受媒体影响|to give media attention to something; to influence something by mass media|The presidential campaign was heavily mediatized. 总统竞选活动被大肆媒体化。|The constant mediatization of celebrities' lives can be overwhelming. 名人生活的持续媒体曝光可能会让人难以承受。|近义词：publicize, broadcast, disseminate|反义词：conceal, suppress|词性:动词

mediatize:/ˈmiːdiətaɪz/| adj. |媒体化的，受媒体影响的|influenced or characterized by mass media|Our society is becoming increasingly mediatized. 我们的社会正变得越来越媒体化。|The mediatized nature of modern politics presents both opportunities and challenges. 现代政治的媒体化特性既带来了机遇，也带来了挑战。|近义词：mediated, publicized|反义词：private, unpublicized|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From media + -ize.  "Media" itself comes from Latin "medium," meaning "middle, means."  The suffix "-ize" forms verbs meaning "to make or become like," "to treat with," or "to subject to the action of."
