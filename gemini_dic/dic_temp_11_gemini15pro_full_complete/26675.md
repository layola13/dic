cadaster:/kəˈdæstər/
n.
地籍；地籍册；土地登记簿
A public record, survey, or map of the value, extent, and ownership of land within a district for taxation purposes.
The city council voted to update the cadaster to reflect recent property sales. 市议会投票决定更新地籍以反映最近的房产销售。
cadastre:/ˈkædəstər/
n.
地籍；地籍册；土地登记簿
A public record, survey, or map of the value, extent, and ownership of land within a district for taxation purposes.
The government is creating a digital cadaster to improve land management. 政府正在创建一个数字地籍以改进土地管理。
synonym:register, record, survey
antonym:None
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French cadastre, from Italian catastro, from Venetian catastico "register for tax assessment," from Late Latin capitastum, from Latin caput "head," referring to a head tax or poll tax.  Originally, a cadaster was a register of individuals subject to a head tax, later evolving to refer to land registers for tax purposes.
