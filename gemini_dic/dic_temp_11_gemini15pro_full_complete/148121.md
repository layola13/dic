oversedation:/ˌoʊvərˌsɪˈdeɪʃən/ | n. | 过度镇静 | The state of being excessively sedated, typically by drugs.  This can lead to drowsiness, confusion, and difficulty breathing. | The patient experienced oversedation after the procedure and required monitoring. 手术后，病人出现了过度镇静，需要监护。|  The oversedation made him unresponsive. 过度镇静使他没有反应。 | 近义词: narcosis, stupor | 反义词: alertness, wakefulness | 词性: noun

词源: From 'over-' (excessive) + 'sedation' (the act of calming or inducing sleep). 'Sedation' comes from the Latin 'sedare' (to settle, calm).
