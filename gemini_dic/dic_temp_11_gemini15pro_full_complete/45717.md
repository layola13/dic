curvednesses:/ˈkɜːrvɪdnɪsɪz/| n. | 曲率，弯曲度 |The state or quality of being curved; the degree to which something is curved. |The curvednesses of the sculpture were striking and added to its dynamic form. 雕塑的曲率引人注目，增添了它的动感。|The road's many curvednesses made driving difficult. 这条路的许多弯道使驾驶变得困难。| synonyms: curvatures, bends, turns | antonyms: straightnesses, flatnesses | noun

Etymology:
From curved + -ness (suffix forming nouns of state or quality).  "Curved" itself comes from the verb "curve," which originates from the Old French "corbe" meaning "bent" or "crooked," ultimately from the Latin "curvus."
