camelid:/ˈkæməlɪd/| n.|骆驼科动物|A mammal of the family Camelidae, which includes camels, llamas, alpacas, vicuñas, and guanacos. These animals are known for their long necks, humped backs (in some species), and ability to adapt to harsh environments.|Llamas and alpacas are camelids native to South America.  美洲驼和羊驼是原产于南美洲的骆驼科动物。|Camels and dromedaries are camelids adapted to desert life.  骆驼和单峰骆驼是适应沙漠生活的骆驼科动物。|Synonyms: camel family|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From New Latin *Camelidae, from Latin camelus (“camel”) + -idae (family suffix).
