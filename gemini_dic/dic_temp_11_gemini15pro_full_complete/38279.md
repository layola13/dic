conatus:/kəˈneɪtəs/ | n. | 努力，倾向，本能 | An innate inclination or striving toward some end; an inherent tendency to exist or continue in being. |  Spinoza believed that all things possess a "conatus," a drive to persevere in their being. 斯宾诺莎认为万物都有一种“努力”，一种保持其存在的动力。

conatus:/kəˈneɪtəs/ | n. | 尝试，企图 |  An attempt or endeavor to do something. |  His conatus to reach the top of the mountain was thwarted by the blizzard. 他试图登上山顶的努力被暴风雪阻挠了。

conatus:/kəˈneɪtəs/ | n. | 内在动力 |  An inner drive or impulse. |  The artist's conatus to create beauty was evident in every brushstroke. 艺术家创造美的内在动力在每一笔触中都显而易见。


词源: From Latin *conātus*, past participle of *conārī* (“to attempt, endeavor”).

近义词:  impulse, drive, instinct, tendency, inclination, striving, endeavor, attempt

反义词:  inaction, apathy, indifference, passivity

词性: noun
