expecter:/ɪkˈspɛktər/| n.| 期望者，预料者|Someone who expects something; a person who anticipates or looks forward to something happening.|The expecters waited anxiously for the announcement.|近义词:anticipator, awaiter|反义词:doubter, skeptic|词性:noun
Etymology: From the Late Middle English *expectour*, derived from the Old French *espectour* and ultimately from the Latin *exspectātor*, meaning "observer" or "waiter."  The word shares a root with the verb "expect."
