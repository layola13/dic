mastigopoda:/ˌmæstɪˈɡɒpədə/ | n. | 鞭毛足类 | A taxonomic group (subclass) within the phylum Sarcomastigophora, characterized by locomotion using flagella. They are typically single-celled organisms found in aquatic environments. | Example: Mastigopoda, like the genus *Trypanosoma*, are important parasites of humans and animals.  鞭毛足类，例如锥虫属，是人类和动物的重要寄生虫。 | Synonym: Flagellata | Antonym:  N/A | Noun

Etymology:
From Greek *mastix* (whip) + *pous* (foot)
