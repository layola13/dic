exalted: /ɪɡˈzɔːltɪd/ | adj. | 高贵的；尊贵的；兴奋的；高尚的 | Raised to a high rank or position; of high moral or intellectual value; filled with or expressing great joy or triumph. |  He felt an exalted sense of pride as he accepted the award.  他接受奖项时感到一种崇高的自豪感。 |  The exalted status of the Queen. 女王的高贵地位。 | 近义词：noble, lofty, elevated, dignified, sublime, elated | 反义词：base, lowly, humble, degraded | 词性: 形容词

exalt: /ɪɡˈzɔːlt/ | vt. | 提升；提拔；赞扬；使兴奋 |  To raise in rank, power, or character; to praise highly; to make noble in character; to intensify. | The king exalted his loyal knight to a higher position. 国王将他的忠诚骑士提升到更高的职位。 |  She was exalted by the news of her son's success. 她儿子的成功消息让她欣喜若狂。 | 近义词：praise, elevate, promote, glorify | 反义词：debase, degrade, humble | 词性: 动词

词源:  From Late Latin exaltatus, past participle of exaltare "to raise, elevate," from Latin ex- "up, out" + altus "high."
