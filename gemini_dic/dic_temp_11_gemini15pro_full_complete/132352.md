nondelegation:/ˌnɒnˌdelɪˈɡeɪʃən/ | n. | 不可委托，不授权 | The principle that one to whom power or authority is delegated cannot in turn delegate that power or authority to another.  The inability of a party to an agreement to transfer its obligations under the agreement to another party. |  The nondelegation doctrine prevents Congress from shifting its legislative power to another branch of government.  国会不可授予原则阻止国会将其立法权转移到政府的其他部门。 |  The contract included a clause on the nondelegation of duties.  合同中包含一项关于职责不可委托的条款。 | Synonyms: nontransferability, undelegatability | Antonyms: delegation, assignment | noun
