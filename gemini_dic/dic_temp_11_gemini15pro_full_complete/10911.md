/ˌærəˈmeɪɪks/ | n. | 阿拉姆语 | Aramaic is a Northwest Semitic language that was the lingua franca of much of the Near East from the 8th century BC until the rise of Islam in the 7th century AD. |  Many ancient texts, including parts of the biblical books of Daniel and Ezra, are written in Aramaic.  许多古代文献，包括圣经但以理书和以斯拉记的部分内容，都是用阿拉姆语写成的。 | synonyms: Syriac (sometimes used interchangeably, especially for later forms) | antonyms: N/A | noun

/ˌærəˈmeɪɪk/ | adj. | 阿拉姆语的 | Relating to Aramaic, its speakers, or their culture. | The Aramaic alphabet is written from right to left. 阿拉姆字母是从右到左书写的。 | synonyms: Syriac (sometimes, especially for later forms) | antonyms: N/A | adjective
