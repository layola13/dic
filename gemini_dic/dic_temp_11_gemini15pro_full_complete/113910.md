madro:/ˈmɑːdroʊ/ (US), /ˈmædroʊ/ (UK)| adj.|精明的，狡猾的，熟练的|Skilled; adept; clever, especially in a tricky or cunning way.  Often used in a playful or admiring sense, particularly in American Spanish and Southwestern US English.|He's a real madro when it comes to negotiating deals. 他在谈判交易方面非常精明。|近义词: clever, shrewd, slick, sly, cunning, artful, adept, skilled|反义词: naive, gullible, clumsy, inept|词性:形容词

词源: From Spanish *madro*, meaning "sly," "cunning," or "roguish."  Possibly related to *madre*, meaning "mother," with the implication of worldly wisdom.
