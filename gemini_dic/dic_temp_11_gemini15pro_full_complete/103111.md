jerked:/dʒɜːrkt/ | v. | 猛地一拉；急抽；痉挛；| to move or make something move with a sudden short sharp movement; to make sudden, sharp, uncontrolled movements | He jerked the phone away from her. 他猛地把电话从她手里夺过来。|  The car jerked forward. 汽车猛地向前一冲。 | yank, tug, twitch, pull | push, smooth | verb

jerked:/dʒɜːrkt/ | adj. | (肉) 风干的 | preserved by being cut into strips and dried in the sun | jerked beef 风干牛肉 | dried, cured | fresh | adjective

**Etymology:**

From *jerk* + *-ed*. The verb *jerk* comes from Middle English *jerken*, of imitative origin.
