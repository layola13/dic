overplausiblenesses: /ˌoʊvərˌplɔːzəˈbɪlnəsɪz/ | n. | 过于似是而非 | The state or quality of being excessively or suspiciously plausible.  The overplausibleness of his alibi made the detective suspicious. |  His explanation had an unsettling overplausiblenesses, making it hard to trust. |  credibility, believability, convincingness | implausibility, unbelievability | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "over" (excessive) + "plausible" (appearing reasonable or probable) + "-ness" (forming nouns of quality or state).  "Plausible" ultimately comes from Latin "plausibilis" (worthy of applause), from "plaudere" (to clap).
