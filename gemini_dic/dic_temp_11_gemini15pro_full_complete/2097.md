adamantly:/ˈædəməntli/ | adv. | 坚定地，坚决地 | In a resolute, unwavering, and inflexible manner; refusing to be persuaded or to change one's mind. |  He adamantly refused to apologize. 他坚决拒绝道歉。|  resolutely, firmly, stubbornly, inflexibly |  yielding, waveringly, hesitantly | adverb

adamant:/ˈædəmənt/ | adj. | 坚定的，坚决的；固执的 | Refusing to be persuaded or to change one's mind. | He was adamant that he had not seen her. 他坚称他没有见过她。| resolute, firm, unyielding, inflexible | yielding, flexible, compliant | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *adamaunt, from Old French *adamaunt, from Latin adamantem, accusative singular of adamans (“adamant, diamond, hard steel”), from Ancient Greek ἀδάμας (adámas, “untamable, invincible, diamond, hard steel”), from ἀ- (a-, “not”) + δαμάω (damáō, “I tame, I subdue”).
