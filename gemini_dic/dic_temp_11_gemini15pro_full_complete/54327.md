disheartener:/dɪsˈhɑːrtnər/ | n. |令人沮丧的人或事|Something that causes someone to lose hope or enthusiasm.|The constant rain was a real disheartener for the team.| 近义词:discouragement, depressant, damper, downer|反义词:encourager, motivator|词性:名词

Etymology:
From dis- + hearten + -er.  "Dis-" is a prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of". "Hearten" comes from the Old English "hearty" meaning courageous or spirited.  "-er" is a suffix that forms nouns indicating a person or thing that performs the action of the verb.
