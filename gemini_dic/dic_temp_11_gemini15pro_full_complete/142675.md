ostrogoths:/ˈɒstrəˌɡɒθs/ /ˈɑːstrəˌɡɑːθs/| n. |东哥特人|An East Germanic people who formed a powerful kingdom in Italy in the 5th and 6th centuries AD.  They were originally part of the Gothic tribes who migrated from Scandinavia to the Black Sea region.  |The Ostrogoths, under Theodoric the Great, established a kingdom in Italy.  东哥特人在狄奥多里克大帝的领导下，在意大利建立了一个王国。

The Ostrogoths' rule in Italy marked a significant period in late antiquity. 东哥特人统治意大利标志着古代晚期的一个重要时期。|近义词：Goths, Visigoths (related but distinct groups) |反义词: Romans (often in conflict with)|词性:noun
