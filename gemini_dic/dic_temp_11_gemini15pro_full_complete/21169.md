bloat:/bloʊt/| v. n.|膨胀，肿胀；使…膨胀；|To swell or puff up, often uncomfortably or unhealthily; A swollen or puffed up condition.|My stomach bloats after I eat too much bread. 我吃了太多面包后肚子胀。|Synonyms: swell, distend, inflate|Antonyms: deflate, shrink, contract|verb, noun

bloats:/bloʊts/| v. (third-person singular simple present)|（使）膨胀，（使）肿胀|Third-person singular simple present form of the verb "bloat".|Eating too much salt bloats the body. 吃太多盐会使身体肿胀。|Synonyms: swells, distends, inflates|Antonyms: deflates, shrinks, contracts|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bloten, from Old Norse blautr (“soft, soaked”), from Proto-Germanic *blautaz (“soft, weak”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰleyt- (“soft, flabby”). Cognate with Icelandic blautr (“wet, soft”), Swedish blöt (“wet”), Danish blød (“soft”).
