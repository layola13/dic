infeudation:/ɪnˌfjuːˈdeɪʃn/ | n. | 封地授予；臣服；被束缚 | The act of granting land in exchange for service or allegiance; the state of being bound to a superior. |  The infeudation of the peasants to the local lord meant they were tied to the land.  农民被封给当地领主意味着他们被束缚在这片土地上。 | 近义词:enfeoffment, vassalage, subjection | 反义词: liberation, freedom, independence | 词性: noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *infeudatio, from infeudare "to enfeoff," from in- "in" + feudum "fief."
