nonintuitivenesses:/ˌnɒnɪnˈtjuːɪtɪvnəsɪz/ | n. | 非直观性；不直观之处 | The lack of clarity or predictability; the quality of not being easily understood or grasped without careful thought or reasoning.  | The nonintuitivenesses of quantum mechanics often baffle students. 量子力学的非直观性常常使学生感到困惑。|  The nonintuitivenesses of the new software interface frustrated users. 新软件界面的不直观性让用户感到沮丧。 | Synonyms: complexities, intricacies, subtleties, obscurities | Antonyms: simplicities, clarities, obviousnesses | noun

Etymology:
non- (prefix) + intuitive + -ness (suffix) + -es (plural suffix)

*   non-:  Latin origin, meaning "not"
*   intuitive: From Late Latin intuitivus, from past participle of intueri "to look at, contemplate,"
*   -ness: Old English suffix forming nouns from adjectives, meaning "state or quality of being"
*   -es: plural suffix
