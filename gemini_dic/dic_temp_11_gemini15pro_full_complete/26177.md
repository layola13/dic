butchers:/ˈbʊtʃərz/| n.|屠夫，肉店; 肉贩; 刽子手|A person who slaughters animals for meat or sells meat.  A butcher prepares and sells meat.|The butchers in the market are very busy on Saturdays.  市场里的屠夫在星期六很忙。|近义词：meat cutter, slaughterer|反义词:vegetarian, vegan|noun


butcher:/ˈbʊtʃər/| vt.|屠宰; 滥杀; 拙劣地演奏; 搞砸|To slaughter an animal for food. To ruin or spoil something.|He learned to butcher a pig when he was young. 他年轻时学会了宰猪。  The director completely butchered the play. 导演完全搞砸了这部戏。|近义词：slaughter, massacre, ruin, spoil|反义词:preserve, save|verb


butcher:/ˈbʊtʃər/| n.|屠杀; 惨败|A massacre or slaughter.|The battle was a complete butcher. 这场战斗是一场彻底的屠杀。|近义词：massacre, slaughter, carnage|反义词:victory, triumph|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *bochier* "butcher," literally "one who slaughters goats," from *boc* "goat" (from Vulgar Latin *bucca*), of unknown origin.
