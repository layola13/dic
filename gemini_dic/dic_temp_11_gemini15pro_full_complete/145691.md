overcorrupt:/ˌoʊvərˈkərʌpt/| adj.|过度腐败的，极度腐败的|Excessively corrupt; corrupt to an extreme degree.  It implies a state of corruption that is pervasive and deeply ingrained.|The overcorrupt government was finally overthrown by the people.  过度腐败的政府最终被人民推翻了。|The overcorrupt system allowed criminals to operate with impunity.  极度腐败的体制让罪犯得以逍遥法外。|近义词：extremely corrupt, deeply corrupt, hopelessly corrupt|反义词：honest, incorruptible|词性:形容词

词源:
"over-" (prefix) + "corrupt" (adjective)

"over-" comes from Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond, across."  It acts as an intensifier, indicating an excessive or extreme degree.

"corrupt" comes from Latin "corruptus," the past participle of "corrumpere," meaning "to break up, destroy, spoil, bribe."
