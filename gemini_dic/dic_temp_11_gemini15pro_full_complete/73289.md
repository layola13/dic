forebow:/ˈfɔːrboʊ/
n.
前甲板，艏楼
The part of a ship's upper deck forward of the foremast.
The sailors gathered on the forebow to watch the dolphins. 水手们聚集在艏楼上观看海豚。

Etymology: From Middle English *foreboue, from Old Norse forbogi ("fore-bow, curved part of a ship's prow"), from for- (“fore-”) + bogi (“bow, curve”). Equivalent to fore- +‎ bow.
fore- : prefix meaning "situated at the front"
bow: the front part of a ship


Synonyms: forecastle, prow, bow
Antonyms: stern, afterdeck
