flambe:/flɒmˈbeɪ/ (UK), /flɑːmˈbeɪ/ (US)| v. adj.|火焰烹调， flambéed(adj.)|To pour liquor over food and ignite it, usually just before serving.  If the food is described as flambéed, it means it has been prepared using this method.|The chef flambéed the cherries jubilee with brandy. 厨师用白兰地火焰烹调了樱桃禧年.|近义词：ignite, sear, flash-fry|反义词:extinguish, douse|词性：verb, adjective
ETYMOLOGY->
From French *flamber*, "to flame," from Old French *flamber*. Ultimately from Latin *flamma*, "flame."
