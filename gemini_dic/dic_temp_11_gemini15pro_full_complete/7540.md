annexing:/əˈnɛksɪŋ/ | v. |吞并，附加，合并|The process of adding something, typically territory, to one's own.  The act of joining or attaching something, especially territory, to a larger or more important thing. |例句：The annexing of the neighboring country sparked international outrage. 吞并邻国激起了国际社会的愤怒。

例句：The company is annexing a smaller firm to expand its market share. 这家公司正在吞并一家小公司以扩大其市场份额。|近义词：seizing, appropriating, conquering, acquiring, incorporating |反义词: ceding, relinquishing, surrendering |词性: 动词 (verb)

annex:/ˈæneks/ | n. |附件，附属建筑|Something added or attached, especially a supplement to a document or a building joined to a larger one.  A smaller building added to or situated near a larger one. |例句：The annex to the treaty addressed specific trade concerns. 该条约的附件涉及具体的贸易问题。

例句：The new annex provides additional office space. 新的附属建筑提供了额外的办公空间。|近义词：appendix, addition, attachment, extension, wing |反义词: main building, core |词性: 名词 (noun)


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *annexare, from Latin ad- (“to, towards”) + nexus (“bound, tied”), past participle of nectere (“to bind”).
