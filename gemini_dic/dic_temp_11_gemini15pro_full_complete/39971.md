consubstantialist:/ˌkɒnsəbˈstænʃɪəlɪst/ |n.| 同体论者；同质论者|A believer in consubstantiation, the doctrine that the substance of the body and blood of Christ are present together with the substance of the bread and wine in the Eucharist.|Martin Luther was a consubstantialist, believing that Christ's body and blood are present in the Eucharist alongside the bread and wine. 马丁·路德是一位同质论者，他相信基督的身体和血液与面包和酒一起出现在圣餐中。|Synonyms: coreligionist, fellow believer|Antonyms: nonbeliever, heretic|Noun
