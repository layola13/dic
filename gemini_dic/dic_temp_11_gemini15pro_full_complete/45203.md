cull:/kʌl/
verb
挑选，剔除
To select from a large quantity; obtain from a variety of sources.
例句：The farmer culled the weakest lambs from the flock.  农民从羊群中剔除了最弱的小羊。

例句：She culled information from various websites for her research paper. 她从各个网站收集信息用于她的研究论文。
近义词：select, choose, pick, glean, gather
反义词：reject, discard, include
词性: verb

cull:/kʌl/
noun
剔除，挑选
The act of selecting from a large quantity.
例句：The annual cull of deer helps to control the population.  每年对鹿的剔除有助于控制鹿群的数量。

例句：The data was obtained from a cull of thousands of research papers.  这些数据是从数千篇研究论文中挑选出来的。
近义词：selection, choice
反义词：rejection
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the Latin word *colligere*, meaning "to collect," which is composed of *com-* (together) and *legere* (to gather).  Over time, the meaning shifted from "collecting" to "collecting selectively," and then to the modern sense of "selecting and removing the less desirable."
