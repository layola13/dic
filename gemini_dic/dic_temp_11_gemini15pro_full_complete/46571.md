daltonism: /ˈdɔːltənɪzəm/ | n. | 色盲，道尔顿症 |  A hereditary form of color blindness, especially the inability to distinguish between red and green.  Named after John Dalton, a chemist who himself had the condition and published a scientific paper on it. |  Daltonism affects approximately 8% of men and 0.5% of women. 道尔顿症影响大约8%的男性和0.5%的女性。|  He couldn't become a pilot because of his daltonism. 因为他色盲，所以他不能成为飞行员。 | 近义词: color blindness | 反义词: normal color vision | 词性: noun
