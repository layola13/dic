catalyser:/ˈkætəlɪst/| n.|催化剂; 触媒; 促进因素|A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change.  A person or thing that precipitates an event.|The government's decision acted as a catalyst for change.|Synonyms: accelerator, stimulus, impetus|Antonyms: inhibitor, retardant|noun
