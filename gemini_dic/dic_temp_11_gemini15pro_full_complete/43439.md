crapshooting:/ˈkræpˌʃuːtɪŋ/| n.|掷骰子赌博；冒险的举动；碰运气|An activity that involves taking a risk or depends on chance, with uncertain results.  It can literally refer to gambling with dice, but more often it's used figuratively.|例句：Investing in that new company felt like crapshooting. 投资那家新公司感觉像是在赌博。

例句：He knew starting his own business was crapshooting, but he was willing to take the risk. 他知道自己创业是一场赌博，但他愿意冒险。|近义词：gambling, risk-taking, speculation|反义词：safe bet, sure thing|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "craps" (a dice game) + "shooting" (throwing dice).  The word "craps" likely originated from the French word "crabs," referring to the lowest dice roll.
