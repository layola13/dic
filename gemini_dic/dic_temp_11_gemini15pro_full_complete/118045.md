menagerie:/məˈnædʒəri/ |n.|动物园，动物展览馆；一批动物|A place where wild animals are kept, especially for exhibition; a collection of wild animals.|He inherited his father's fortune and his menagerie of exotic pets. 他继承了父亲的财产和他的一群奇异宠物。|Synonyms: zoo, collection, assemblage|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps wilderness)|Noun


menagerist:/məˈnædʒərɪst/ |n.|动物园老板；动物展览馆经营者|A person who owns or manages a menagerie.|The menagerist proudly showed off his newest acquisition, a rare white tiger.  动物园老板自豪地展示了他的最新藏品——一只珍稀的白虎。|Synonyms: zookeeper, animal keeper, curator (in some contexts)|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *ménagerie*, from *ménager* "to manage, keep house," from Late Latin *mansionaticum* "dwelling," from Latin *mansio* "dwelling, stopping place," from *manere* "to stay, remain."
