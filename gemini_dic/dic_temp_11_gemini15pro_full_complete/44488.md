crossed:/krɒst/ (UK) /krɑːst/ (US)| adj. v.|交叉的，生气的，反对的，杂交的|having lines, bars, etc. that go across each other; annoyed or angry; going across something; produced from parents of different breeds or groups|
例句：The wires were crossed, causing a short circuit. 电线交叉了，导致短路。
例句：She was crossed with him for being late. 她因为他迟到而生他的气。

近义词：intersect, intertwined, angry, irritated, hybrid, mixed|
反义词：parallel, straight, happy, pleased, purebred|
词性: adjective, past participle of verb 'cross'


crossed:/krɒst/ (UK) /krɑːst/ (US)| v. |穿过，交叉，杂交，反对，相遇|to go from one side of something to the other; to place one thing across another; to breed animals or plants of different kinds; to oppose someone or something; to meet and pass each other|
例句：He crossed the road carefully. 他小心翼翼地过马路。
例句：Our paths crossed many years ago. 我们的道路很多年前就相遇了。


近义词：traverse, intersect, hybridize, oppose, encounter|
反义词：stay, remain, separate, support, avoid|
词性: past tense and past participle of 'cross'


词源: From Old Norse *kross, from Old Irish cros, ultimately from Latin crux "cross".
