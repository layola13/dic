cavorter:/kəˈvɔːrtər/| vi.| 嬉戏；欢闹；寻欢作乐|To play boisterously; romp.  To make merry; revel.|The children cavortered in the park all afternoon. 孩子们整个下午都在公园里嬉戏玩耍。|Synonyms: frolic, gambol, romp, revel, carouse|Antonyms: mope, sulk|Verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Italian "cavortare," meaning "to prance, to curvet (as a horse)," ultimately from the Late Latin "caballus volitare," meaning "flying horse."
