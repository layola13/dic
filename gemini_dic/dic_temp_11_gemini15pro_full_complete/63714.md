ephemeron:/ɪˈfɛmərɒn/ (US) /ɪˈfɛmərən/ (UK)| n.|蜉蝣，朝生暮死之物，短暂存在的事物|Something that lasts for a very short time.|The beauty of the cherry blossoms is an ephemeron, lasting only a couple of weeks.  樱花的美丽是短暂的，只持续几周。|Synonyms: transient, fleeting, short-lived, temporary|Antonyms: permanent, enduring, lasting|noun


ephemera:/ɪˈfɛmərə/ (US) /ɪˈfɛmərə/ (UK)| n.|蜉蝣，朝生暮死之物，短暂存在的事物;  ephemera (plural) also means  printed matter (such as posters, tickets, or handbills) that is not meant to be retained or preserved.|Something that lasts for a very short time; (plural) collectible items, especially printed ones, that were originally expected to have only short-term use or interest.|He collected ephemera related to the early days of cinema. 他收集了与早期电影相关的印刷品。|Synonyms:  transients, memorabilia, collectibles (for the plural meaning)|Antonyms:  permanents, keepsakes (for the plural meaning)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἐφήμερος (ephḗmeros, “lasting for a day”), from ἐπί (epí, “on, upon”) + ἡμέρα (hēméra, “day”).
