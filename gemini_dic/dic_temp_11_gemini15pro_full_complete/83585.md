haematocrystallin: /ˌhiːmətəˈkrɪstəlɪn/ | n. | 血红蛋白 |  A crystalline protein containing iron, found in red blood cells and responsible for carrying oxygen from the lungs to the tissues. It is also called hemoglobin. |  Hemoglobin, or haematocrystallin, is essential for life in vertebrates. |  hemoglobin |  (No commonly used antonym) | noun

haemocrystalline: /ˌhiːmətəˈkrɪstəlɪn/ | adj. | 血红蛋白的；含血红蛋白的 |  Relating to or containing haematocrystallin (hemoglobin). |  The haematocrystalline structure is complex. |  hemoglobin-containing | (No commonly used antonym) | adjective
