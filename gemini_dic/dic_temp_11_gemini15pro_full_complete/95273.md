incapacitated:/ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪteɪtɪd/
adjective
丧失能力的；无能力的
Deprived of strength or power; disabled.  Unable to function normally, especially because of injury or illness.
He was incapacitated by a stroke. 他因中风而丧失了能力。
The accident left her incapacitated for months.  这场事故使她几个月都无法正常生活。
synonyms: disabled, debilitated, crippled, impaired, unfit
antonyms: able, capable, competent, fit, healthy
词性: adjective


incapacitate:/ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪteɪt/
verb
使无能力；使丧失能力；使不适合
To deprive of strength or power; disable. To make legally incapable or ineligible.
The injury incapacitated him from playing football.  这伤使他无法踢足球。
His illness incapacitated him for work. 他的病使他无法工作。
synonyms: disable, debilitate, cripple, impair
antonyms: enable, empower, rehabilitate
词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *incapacitatus, past participle of incapacitare (“to make incapable”), from in- (“not”) + capacitatus, past participle of  capacitare (“to make capable”), from Latin capax (“capable”).
