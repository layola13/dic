futurism:/ˈfjuːtʃərɪzəm/| n. |未来主义|An early-20th-century Italian art movement that emphasized the dynamism, speed, energy, and power of the machine and the vitality, change, and restlessness of modern life rejecting the past.|Italian Futurism celebrated speed, technology, youth, violence, and objects such as the car, the airplane, and the industrial city.|近义词:modernism, avant-garde|反义词:traditionalism, classicism|词性:noun

Etymology: From French *futurisme*, from Italian *futurismo*, from *futuro* (“future”)  + *-ismo* (suffix forming nouns of action, condition, doctrine, etc.). Ultimately from Latin *futūrus*.
