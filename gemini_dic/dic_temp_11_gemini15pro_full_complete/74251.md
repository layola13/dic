foxhounds:/ˈfɒksˌhaʊndz/
n.
猎狐犬（复数）
A breed of hound, typically having a short, dense coat and long ears, bred and trained to hunt foxes.
The foxhounds bayed excitedly as they picked up the scent. 猎狐犬嗅到气味后兴奋地吠叫。
近义词：hounds, hunting dogs
反义词:N/A
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From fox + hound. "Hound" comes from Old English *hund, from Proto-Germanic *hundaz, from Proto-Indo-European *kwntos.  "Fox" comes from Old English fox, from Proto-Germanic *fuhsaz, from Proto-Indo-European *puḱ- (tail).
