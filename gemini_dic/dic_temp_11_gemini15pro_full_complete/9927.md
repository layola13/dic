apertion:/əˈpɜːʃən/ /əˈpərʃən/ | n. | 孔, 开口 | An opening, hole, or gap, especially one that allows light to pass through.  |  The telescope's apertion allowed for a clear view of the distant galaxy.  这架望远镜的孔径可以清晰地看到遥远的星系。| 近义词: opening, orifice, hole, gap, aperture | 反义词: closure, blockage | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From Late Latin *apertionem, accusative of *apertio (“opening”), from Latin apertus (“opened”), past participle of aperire (“to open”), from ab (“away from”) + parire (“to bring forth, give birth to”).  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *per- (“to lead, pass over”).
