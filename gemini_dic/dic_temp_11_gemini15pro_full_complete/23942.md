breastbone: /ˈbrɛstbəʊn/ (UK) /ˈbrɛstˌboʊn/ (US) | n. | 胸骨 | The breastbone, also called the sternum, is a long flat bone located in the center of the chest. It connects to the ribs via cartilage and forms the front of the rib cage, thus helping to protect the heart, lungs, and major blood vessels from injury. | The doctor pressed on his breastbone to check for any tenderness. 医生按压他的胸骨检查是否有压痛。

The impact fractured his breastbone.  撞击使他的胸骨骨折。
