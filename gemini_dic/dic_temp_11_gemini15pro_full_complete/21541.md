bluecoat: /ˈbluːkoʊt/ (American English) /ˈbluːkəʊt/ (British English) | n. | 蓝衣,  蓝制服; (旧时)慈善学校的学生;  (常作 Bluecoat)英国利物浦的蓝衣学校 | A person who wears a blue coat, especially historically a soldier or a pupil at a charity school. Originally referred to the blue uniform coats worn by the children of Christ's Hospital school in London. |  例句：The bluecoats marched through the city streets. 蓝衣士兵们在城市街道上行进。

例句：He recognized the bluecoat as a student from the local charity school. 他认出那个穿蓝制服的是当地慈善学校的学生。 | 近义词：uniform, livery | 反义词: civilian clothes | 词性: noun


bluecoat: /ˈbluːkoʊt/ (American English) /ˈbluːkəʊt/ (British English) | adj. | 穿蓝制服的 | Wearing a blue coat. |  例句：The bluecoat soldiers stood guard at the palace. 穿蓝制服的士兵在宫殿站岗。

例句：The bluecoat children sang in the choir. 穿蓝制服的孩子们在唱诗班唱歌。| 近义词： uniformed | 反义词: plainclothes | 词性: adjective


词源：From the blue coats worn by certain groups, especially the pupils of Christ's Hospital school in London. The term later became generalized to refer to anyone wearing a blue coat.
