gloom:/ɡluːm/| n. |忧郁，阴暗，昏暗|A feeling of sadness and lack of hope; partial or total darkness.|例句：A heavy gloom settled over the town.  浓重的阴云笼罩着小镇。


例句：The news cast a gloom over the celebration. 这个消息给庆祝活动蒙上了一层阴影。

近义词：darkness, sadness, depression, melancholy, despair|反义词：brightness, happiness, cheerfulness, light|词性：名词

glooms:/ɡluːmz/| n. |忧郁，阴暗，昏暗（复数）|Plural form of "gloom," referring to multiple instances or aspects of sadness and darkness.|例句：He sank into deep glooms after losing his job.  失业后，他陷入了深深的忧郁之中。


例句：The glooms of winter descended upon the village. 冬天的阴霾降临在这个村庄。

近义词：darknesses, sadnesses, depressions|反义词：brightnesses, happinesses, cheerfulnesses|词性：名词

glooms:/ɡluːmz/| v. |变阴暗，变得忧郁（第三人称单数）|Third-person singular present tense form of the verb "gloom," meaning to become dark or depressing.|例句：The sky glooms as the storm approaches. 暴风雨来临，天空变得阴暗。


例句：He glooms whenever he thinks about the future. 每当他想到未来，他就变得忧郁。

近义词：darkens, saddens, depresses|反义词：brightens, cheers, lightens|词性：动词
