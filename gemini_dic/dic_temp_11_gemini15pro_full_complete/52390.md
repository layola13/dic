didacticity:/ˌdɪdækˈtɪsɪti/| n.|说教性，教训性|The quality of being didactic; intended to teach, particularly in having moral instruction as an ulterior motive.  The tendency to be overly instructive or moralizing.|The didacticity of the novel made it feel more like a sermon than a story. 这部小说的说教意味太浓，读起来更像布道而不是故事。|Synonyms: preachiness, moralizing, instructiveness|Antonyms: suggestiveness, subtlety, nuance|noun

didactic:/daɪˈdæktɪk/| adj.|教诲的，说教的|Intended to teach, particularly in having moral instruction as an ulterior motive. Inclined to teach or lecture others too much.|The professor's didactic lectures often left students feeling patronized.  这位教授的说教式讲课常常让学生觉得被居高临下地对待了。|Synonyms: instructive, pedagogic, preachy|Antonyms: suggestive, subtle, nuanced|adjective
