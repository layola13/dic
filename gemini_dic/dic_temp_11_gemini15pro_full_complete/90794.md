humour:/ˈhjuːmə(r)/ (UK) /ˈhjuːmər/ (US)| n.|幽默，诙谐；心情，情绪；体液（旧时医学理论）|Something that is designed to be amusing or comic.  In the past, it referred to one of the four fluids in the body that were believed to determine a person's temperament.|He has a good sense of humour. 他很有幽默感。|Synonyms: wit, comedy, fun, amusement|Antonyms: seriousness, gloom, depression|noun

humours:/ˈhjuːməz/ (UK)  /ˈhjuːmərz/ (US)| n.|（古）体液；（人的）性情、脾气；怪念头|Plural form of humour, especially in the historical context of the four humours.|The doctor believed his humours were out of balance. 医生认为他的体液失衡了。|Synonyms: moods, temperaments, whims|Antonyms: (For moods) stability, (For temperaments) calmness, (For whims) practicality|noun
