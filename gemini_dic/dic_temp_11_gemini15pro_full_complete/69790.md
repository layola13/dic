fermium:/ˈfɜːrmiəm/ | n. |鐨 |A synthetic radioactive metallic element, atomic number 100, the heaviest element that can be formed by neutron bombardment of lighter elements. It is one of the actinide series of elements.  | Fermium is only produced artificially in nuclear reactors. 鐨只在核反应堆中人工生产。 |  |  | noun
Etymology: Named after Enrico Fermi, an Italian-American physicist.
