falsifiability:/ˌfɔːlsɪfaɪəˈbɪləti/ /ˌfɑːlsɪfaɪəˈbɪləti/| n. | 可证伪性 | The inherent possibility that a statement, hypothesis, or theory can be proven false.  It is a key concept in the philosophy of science, associated with Karl Popper. |  The scientist stressed the importance of falsifiability in any valid scientific theory.  这位科学家强调了任何有效科学理论中可证伪性的重要性。

The falsifiability of his claim was questioned by the other researchers.  他的主张的可证伪性受到了其他研究人员的质疑。|近义词：testability, refutability |反义词：verifiability, unfalsifiability|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From falsify (from Late Latin falsificare, "to counterfeit," from Latin falsus, "false" + facere, "to make") + -able + -ity.
