heroic couplets:/hɪˈroʊɪk ˈkʌplɪts/| n.|英雄双韵体|A pair of rhyming lines in iambic pentameter.|例句：Alexander Pope was a master of heroic couplets. 亚历山大·蒲柏是英雄双韵体的大师。

例句：Many 18th-century poems were written in heroic couplets. 许多18世纪的诗歌都是用英雄双韵体写成的。|近义词：rhyming couplets|反义词:N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"heroic" (adj.) from late 14c., "having the characteristics of a hero," from Old French heroique (13c.) and directly from Latin heroicus "of a hero, heroic," from Greek heroikos, from heros "hero." "couplet" (n.) early 14c., cuplet "pair," from Old French couplet (13c.), diminutive of couple "couple, pair," from Latin copula "a bond, tie, link." The verse sense of "two rhyming lines" is from c. 1500. The combination "heroic couplet" is attested from 1690s.
