kibitz:/ˈkɪbɪts/| v. |闲聊，指点，对旁观者说闲话|To offer unsolicited advice or commentary, especially in a situation where one is not a participant; to chat or make small talk.|He couldn't resist kibitzing on my chess game, even though he knew I found it annoying. 他忍不住要对我下棋指手画脚，即使他知道我很烦。|Synonyms: meddle, interfere, butt in, offer unsolicited advice|Antonyms: abstain, refrain, mind one's own business|verb

kibitzer:/ˈkɪbɪtsər/| n. |爱指点的人，旁观者|A person who kibitzes; someone who offers unsolicited advice or commentary.|My uncle is a notorious kibitzer, always offering his two cents on everything. 我叔叔是个出了名的爱指点的人，对所有事情都爱发表意见。|Synonyms: meddler, interferer, busybody|Antonyms: bystander, onlooker (These are not perfect antonyms, but represent someone who observes without interfering)|noun
