lithofellic:/ˌlɪθəˈfɛlɪk/
adjective
亲岩的；亲石的
Having an affinity for rocks, or thriving in rocky environments.  Specifically refers to organisms, especially microorganisms, that live in or on rocks.
Lithofellic bacteria play a crucial role in weathering processes. 亲岩细菌在风化过程中起着至关重要的作用。

Lithofellic lichens can be found growing on bare rock surfaces.  亲石地衣可以在裸露的岩石表面生长。

近义词: rupestral, rock-dwelling, saxicolous
反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider terms like "aquatic," "terrestrial," or those relating to other specific habitats.)
词性: adjective


词源:
From Greek *lithos* (stone) + *-phile* (loving).
