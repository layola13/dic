lairs:/lerz/
| n. |巢穴，窝点；藏身处|A place where a wild animal lives, especially a predator, or a place where a person hides or spends time.|The fox retreated to its lair. 狐狸退回到它的巢穴。|Synonyms: den, nest, hideout, burrow|Antonyms: exposure, open, public|Noun

lairs:/lerz/
| n. |(尤指罪犯的) 藏身处，窝点|A place where a person, especially a criminal, hides.|The police raided the gang's lair. 警察突袭了该团伙的窝点。|Synonyms: hideaway, hideout, den|Antonyms: exposure, open, public|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *leger, from Proto-Germanic *legraną (“to lay, lie down”), from Proto-Indo-European *legh- (“to lie down, lay”). Cognate with German Lager (“camp, bed, store”), Dutch leger (“army, camp”), Swedish läger (“camp”).
