fictionalising:/ˈfɪkʃənəlaɪzɪŋ/
verb
虚构化；小说化
The act of turning something into fiction; inventing or embellishing details to create a fictional narrative.
He was accused of fictionalising his war experiences. 他被指控虚构了他的战争经历。
The movie fictionalises the life of a famous singer. 这部电影将一位著名歌手的生活虚构化了。
Synonyms: inventing, fabricating, romanticizing, dramatizing, novelizing
Antonyms: documenting, reporting, factualising, verifying
verb

fictionalise:/ˈfɪkʃənəlaɪz/
verb
虚构化；小说化
To make something fictional; to create a fictional account of something.
She fictionalized her childhood experiences in her latest novel.  她在她最新的小说中虚构了她的童年经历。
The events in the movie were fictionalized for dramatic effect.  电影中的事件被虚构化以增强戏剧效果。
Synonyms: invent, fabricate, romanticize, dramatize, novelize
Antonyms: document, report, factualise, verify
verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From fiction + -alize.  "Fiction" comes from the Latin "fictio," meaning "a fashioning or feigning."  The suffix "-alize"  means "to make or cause to be." Thus, "fictionalize" literally means to make something into a feigned or fashioned narrative.
