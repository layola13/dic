leaven:/ˈlɛvən/| n. v. |酵母，发酵剂；使发酵，使充满|Something, typically yeast, that is added to dough to make it rise. To permeate and transform.|The baker added leaven to the dough. 面包师在面团里加了酵母。|Synonyms: yeast, ferment,  raising agent|Antonyms: deflate, flatten|noun


leaven:/ˈlɛvən/| v. |使发酵，使充满|To permeate and transform; to cause to rise or grow lighter; to enliven.|Her laughter leavened the tense atmosphere. 她的笑声缓和了紧张的气氛。|Synonyms: permeate, imbue, elevate, inspire|Antonyms: dampen, deaden, depress|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *levain*, from Latin *levamen* "a lightening, alleviating," from *levare* "to lift, raise."
