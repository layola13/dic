crackpot:/ˈkrækˌpɒt/ (UK) /ˈkrækˌpɑːt/ (US)| n. adj.|怪人，疯子；不切实际的|A person who is considered eccentric or slightly mad; An impractical idea or scheme.|He dismissed her as a crackpot.|近义词：eccentric, lunatic, crank, nutty, wacky, harebrained|反义词：sane, sensible, rational|词性:名词,形容词

crackpot:/ˈkrækˌpɒt/ (UK) /ˈkrækˌpɑːt/ (US)| adj.|不切实际的|Impractical; foolish.|He had some crackpot idea about turning lead into gold.|近义词：impractical, foolish, absurd, ridiculous|反义词：practical, sensible, realistic|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "cracked pot," referring to a cracked cooking pot and the perceived mental instability that might result from eating food cooked in it.  The idea is that toxins could leach from a cracked pot into food, causing mental problems.
