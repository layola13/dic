kinslaying:/ˈkɪnsleɪɪŋ/| n.|弑亲；杀害亲属|The act of killing one's kin (relative).|The Greek tragedies are full of kinslaying and revenge.|近义词：familicide, parricide, fratricide (depending on the specific relationship)|反义词:  (No direct antonym, possibly "familial piety" or "kinship protection")|词性:noun


Etymology->
From kin + slaying (present participle of slay).  "Kin" comes from Old English "cynn" meaning "family, race, kind."  "Slay" comes from Old English "slean" meaning "to strike, kill."
