catalogs: /ˈkætəlɔːɡz/ (US) /ˈkætəlɒɡz/ (UK) | n. |目录；产品样本；一系列；（尤指按字母顺序排列的）清单| A list or record, systematically arranged, especially of items in a collection, often including descriptive information. |  I browsed through their holiday gift catalogs. 我浏览了他们的节日礼物目录。|synonyms: list, register, directory, index, inventory | antonyms: disorder, chaos | noun

catalogs: /ˈkætəlɔːɡz/ (US) /ˈkætəlɒɡz/ (UK) | v. |为……编目录；记载；登记| To make a systematic list or record of. | The librarian cataloged the new books. 图书管理员为新书编目。| synonyms: list, register, record, document | antonyms: disorganize, scramble | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *catalogue*, from Latin *catalogus*, from Greek *katálogos*  "list, register," from *kata-* "down" + *légein* "to gather, choose, speak, read."
