hidebound:/ˈhaɪdbaʊnd/| adj.|迂腐的，守旧的，墨守成规的|Unwilling or unable to change because of tradition or convention.  Restricted by petty rules or prejudices.|The hidebound school board refused to consider any changes to the curriculum.|近义词：conservative, traditional, inflexible, rigid, unyielding, dogmatic, narrow-minded, intolerant, bigoted|反义词：open-minded, progressive, liberal, flexible, adaptable|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "hide" (skin) + "bound" (constrained). Originally referred to animals whose skin was so tight it restricted their movement.  This physical sense was later extended metaphorically to describe people with rigid, inflexible views.
