huckstering:/ˈhʌkstərɪŋ/
n.|叫卖；兜售；强力推销|The act of aggressively promoting or selling something, often using questionable methods.|The huckstering of cheap souvenirs to tourists was relentless.  向游客强力推销廉价纪念品的行为没完没了。|近义词: peddling, hawking, touting|反义词: donating, giving|noun

huckster:/ˈhʌkstər/
n.|小贩；叫卖者|A person who sells goods, especially small items, in an aggressive or insistent way.|The huckster loudly advertised his wares.  那个小贩大声叫卖他的商品。|近义词: peddler, hawker, vendor|反义词: philanthropist|noun
v.|叫卖；兜售|To promote or sell something aggressively, often using questionable methods.|He was huckstering his latest invention at the trade show.  他在贸易展上兜售他的最新发明。|近义词: peddle, hawk, tout|反义词: donate, give|verb
