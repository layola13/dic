handkerchiefs:/ˈhæŋkərtʃɪfs/
n.|手帕（复数）|A piece of cloth, usually square, carried in the pocket or handbag and intended for blowing the nose or wiping the eyes.|She pulled out a lace handkerchief and dabbed her eyes. 她掏出一块蕾丝手帕，轻拭双眼。
|tissues, hankies| |noun|

handkerchief:/ˈhæŋkərtʃɪf/
n.|手帕（单数）|A piece of cloth, usually square, carried in the pocket or handbag and intended for blowing the nose or wiping the eyes.|He offered her his handkerchief. 他递给她他的手帕。
|tissue, hanky| |noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *handkerchief*, from *hand* + *kerchief* (a covering for the head), from Anglo-Norman *coverchef*, from *covrir* (to cover) + *chef* (head).  Ultimately from Latin *caput* (head).
