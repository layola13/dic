fullerenes:/ˈfʊləˌriːnz/ | n. |富勒烯|A form of carbon having a large spheroidal molecule consisting of a hollow cage of sixty or more atoms.  They are found in soot and interstellar space.|Scientists are exploring the potential uses of fullerenes in nanotechnology.|Synonyms: buckyballs, C60, carbon nanotubes|Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, perhaps other allotropes of carbon like graphite or diamond could be considered contrasting forms)|Noun
