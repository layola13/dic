fresh:/freʃ/
adjective|新鲜的；清新的；新颖的；精神饱满的；无经验的|Newly made or obtained; not stale or deteriorated; cool and refreshing; different and interesting; not tired; inexperienced.| The air was fresh and clean after the rain. 雨后的空气清新干净。 |
近义词:new, recent, clean, pure, invigorated|
反义词:stale, old, tired, polluted|
词性:adjective


fresh:/freʃ/
adjective|淡水的；无盐的|Containing no salt; relating to water that is not salty.|Fresh water is essential for life. 淡水对生命至关重要。|
近义词:unsalted, potable|
反义词:salty, saline, brackish|
词性:adjective


fresh:/freʃ/
adverb|(informal) |最近；刚刚|Recently; newly.|The paint is still fresh. 油漆还很新。|
近义词:recently, newly, lately|
反义词:long ago|
词性:adverb



ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fersċ*  "not stale, fresh," also "vigorous, lively," from Proto-Germanic *friskaz* (source also of Old Dutch *versch*, Old High German *frisc*, German *frisch*, Old Norse *ferskr* "fresh"), from Proto-Indo-European *preisk- "to jump, frisk, be lively."
