gaskins:/ˈɡæskɪnz/ | n. | 宽松马裤；灯笼裤 | Loose-fitting breeches or trousers.  From the 16th to the 18th centuries, gaskins were a popular form of men's attire, characterized by their wide, puffy shape. |  He wore a pair of extravagant gaskins to the Renaissance fair. 他穿着一双夸张的灯笼裤去参加文艺复兴节。

The nobleman strutted about in his fine velvet gaskins. 这位贵族穿着他精致的天鹅绒灯笼裤昂首阔步。 | synonyms: breeches, slops, trousers | antonyms: tight pants, skinny jeans | noun
