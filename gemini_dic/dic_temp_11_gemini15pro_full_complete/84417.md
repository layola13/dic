halloween: /ˌhæləˈwiːn/ (US) /ˌhæləʊˈiːn/ (UK)| n. |万圣节前夕，万圣节|The evening of October 31, a holiday observed in several countries, especially in the US and Canada, by children dressed in costumes who go from door to door requesting treats.|例句：Children dress up in costumes for Halloween. 孩子们在万圣节穿戴化装服饰。|例句：We went trick-or-treating on Halloween night.  我们在万圣节之夜去玩“不给糖就捣蛋”的游戏。|近义词：All Hallows' Eve, Samhain|反义词: None|词性:noun


词源: From All Hallows' Even, the eve of All Saints' Day (November 1st).  "Hallow" means "holy person" or "saint."  The "even" eventually became contracted to "een."
