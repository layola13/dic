cop-out:/ˈkɑːpˌaʊt/| n.|逃避；推卸；回避|An act of avoiding or backing out of something considered difficult or unpleasant.|He promised to help but coped out at the last minute. 他答应帮忙，但在最后一刻退缩了。|近义词：evasion, excuse, dodge, weasel out|反义词：commitment, follow-through|noun

cop-out:/ˈkɑːpˌaʊt/| vi.|逃避；推卸；回避|To avoid or back out of something considered difficult or unpleasant.|He always cops out of difficult situations. 他总是逃避困难的局面。|近义词：evade, dodge, weasel out|反义词：commit, follow through|verb

词源： "cop-out" originates from the 1950s American slang. "Cop" in this context means "to take" or "to seize", and "out" signifies an escape or avoidance.  Therefore, "cop-out" literally means "to take oneself out" of a situation.
