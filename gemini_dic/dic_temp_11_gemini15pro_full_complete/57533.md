drain:/dreɪn/ | n. v. | 排水沟，下水道；消耗，流失 |  A channel or pipe that carries away excess water or other liquid waste. To cause liquid to flow away from something; to empty or exhaust something. | The bathtub drain was clogged. (浴缸的排水管堵塞了。)  He drained the pasta. (他把意大利面滤干了。) | synonyms: channel, pipe, sewer, outlet, deplete, exhaust, sap | antonyms: fill, replenish, invigorate | noun, verb

drain:/dreɪn/ | n. | 排水沟，下水道 | A channel or pipe carrying off surplus liquid, especially rainwater or liquid waste. |  Heavy rain blocked the drains. (大雨阻塞了下水道。) The drain in the sink is clogged. (水槽里的排水管堵塞了。) | synonyms: sewer, gutter, channel, culvert | antonyms: inlet | noun

drain:/dreɪn/ | v. | 排空；流干；使精疲力竭 | To cause the liquid in something to run out, leaving it empty or dry; to become empty or dry in this way. To gradually make someone or something weaker or emptier, especially of energy, money, or resources. | Can you drain the pasta, please? (你能把意大利面滤干吗？)  The long war had drained the country’s resources. (漫长的战争耗尽了国家的资源。) | synonyms: empty, discharge, deplete, exhaust, sap | antonyms: fill, replenish, energize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *drægnian, from Proto-Germanic *dregnan, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰrewg- (“to draw, pull, drag”). Cognate with Dutch dreigen (“to drain, dry”), German tränen (“to water, shed tears”), Icelandic drena (“to drain”).
