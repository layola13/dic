overclever:/ˌoʊvərˈklɛvər/
|adj.|过于聪明的；自作聪明的；耍小聪明的|Too clever; trying too hard to be clever, often with negative results.|He thought he was being overclever, but his plan backfired.|近义词：cunning, sly, shrewd, scheming, artful|反义词：naive, ingenuous, artless, guileless|词性:形容词
Etymology: over- + clever. "Over-" as a prefix means excessively or too much. "Clever" comes from Old English "gleaw" meaning "wise" or "skillful."
