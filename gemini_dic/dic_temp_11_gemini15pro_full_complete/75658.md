fukuyama:/ˌfuːkuːˈjɑːmə/ | n. | 福山, 福山Francis | Refers to Yoshihiro Francis Fukuyama, an American political scientist, political economist, and author. He is known for his book "The End of History and the Last Man" (1992), which argued that the worldwide spread of liberal democracies may signal the endpoint of humanity's sociocultural evolution and the final form of human government. |  Fukuyama's thesis sparked widespread debate. 福山的论点引发了广泛的争论。 |  His later work focused on the role of trust in building social capital.  他后来的工作重点是信任在建立社会资本中的作用。| synonyms:  (no direct synonyms for a person, but related concepts):  liberal democracy advocate, political theorist, neoconservative (formerly) | antonyms: (no direct antonyms for a person, but opposing viewpoints): Marxist, socialist, communist | proper noun
