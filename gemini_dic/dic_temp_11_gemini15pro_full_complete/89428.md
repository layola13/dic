hon:/hʌn/ | n. | honey; dear; sweetheart | Term of endearment, often used for a woman. |  Example: "See you later, hon!" 再见，亲爱的！ | Synonyms: dear, sweetheart, darling, sweetie, babe | Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a term of endearment) | Noun


hon:/hɑːn/ | v. | to hone | To sharpen or refine something, often a skill. | Example: "She honed her writing skills over many years." 她多年来不断磨练她的写作技巧。 | Synonyms: sharpen, refine, perfect, improve, enhance | Antonyms: dull, blunt, worsen, deteriorate | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *hon*, a shortened form of *hony*, from Old English *hunig* (honey). The usage as a term of endearment likely arose from the sweetness associated with honey.
