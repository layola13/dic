dolefulnesses:/ˈdoʊlfʊlnəsɪz/
noun
悲伤，忧郁，凄凉
The state of being doleful; sadness or melancholy.
The dolefulnesses of the funeral procession were palpable. 葬礼队伍的悲伤气氛非常明显。
The protracted dolefulnesses of the rainy season began to wear on everyone's spirits.  漫长的雨季带来的持续的忧郁开始消磨每个人的精神。
Synonyms: sadness, melancholy, gloom, dejection, sorrow, grief, woe, misery
Antonyms: happiness, joy, cheerfulness, elation, glee, mirth
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From doleful + -ness + -es (plural suffix). Doleful comes from Middle English *doleful, from dole "grief, sorrow" (from Old French doel, ultimately from Latin dolus "pain, grief") + -ful.
