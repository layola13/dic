antitypous:/ænˈtɪtɪpəs/
adj. |对偶的，相对的，对应的|Of or relating to an antitype; representing or prefiguring an antitype.  Representing something in the future, especially in the Old Testament thought to prefigure something in the New Testament.|The Passover lamb is an antitypous figure of Christ.|近义词:typical, prefigurative, symbolic|反义词:atypical, unrepresentative|词性:adjective

Etymology:
From Late Latin *antitypus*, from Ancient Greek ἀντίτυπος (antítypos)  "a copy, image, counterpart",  from ἀντί (antí, “against, opposite”) + τύπος (túpos, “a blow, impression, mark, type”).
