client-server: /ˈklaɪənt ˈsɜːrvər/ (US) /ˈklaɪənt ˈsɜːvə/ (UK)| adj. n.|客户端-服务器; 客户机-服务器|A client-server architecture is a distributed application structure that partitions tasks or workloads between the providers of a resource or service, called servers, and service requesters, called clients.  In short, it's a way of organizing computer systems where some machines (servers) provide services, and other machines (clients) request those services.|The company uses a client-server architecture for its email system.| 近义词: distributed system, network computing| 反义词: peer-to-peer (P2P)| 词性: adjective, noun

client: /ˈklaɪənt/| n.|客户; 客户端; 委托人|A person or organization using the services of a lawyer or other professional person or company. Also, a computer that accesses information and services from a server.|The lawyer met with his client to discuss the case.  The client computer requested data from the server.|近义词: customer, user, consumer|反义词: server, provider|词性: noun

server: /ˈsɜːrvər/ (US) /ˈsɜːvə/ (UK)| n.|服务器; 服务员|A computer or program that manages access to a centralized resource or service in a network.|The server crashed, causing the website to go down. The server brought us our food.|近义词: host, provider|反义词: client, user|词性: noun
