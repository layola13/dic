moonwort:/ˈmuːnwɜːrt/| n.|月蕨，药蕨|A fern of the genus *Botrychium*, with fleshy, lobed fronds.  Believed to have magical properties in folklore, especially in relation to the moon and unlocking locks.|例句：The alchemist searched for moonwort, believing it could transmute silver into gold. 炼金术士寻找月蕨，相信它可以将银变成金。

例句：According to legend, moonwort could loosen horseshoes if placed beneath them.  根据传说，如果把月蕨放在马蹄下，它可以松开马蹄铁。|近义词：grape fern (for some species), adder's-tongue fern (for some species)|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *mōnanwyrt*, from *mōna* (moon) + *wyrt* (wort, plant).  The name derives from the shape of the fronds, thought to resemble a crescent moon.
