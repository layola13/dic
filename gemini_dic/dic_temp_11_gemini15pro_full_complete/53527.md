disattire:/ˌdɪsəˈtaɪər/| v. |使穿着随便；脱去…的盛装；使丢脸，使名誉扫地|To dress in casual or informal clothes, especially when formal attire is expected; to undress; to disgrace or dishonor.|He disattired himself quickly after the ball. 舞会结束后，他迅速脱掉了盛装。|近义词：undress, strip, divest|反义词：attire, dress, robe|词性：动词

Etymology:
From dis- (expressing reversal) + attire (to dress).  The "dis-" prefix indicates removal or reversal of the action of attiring.
