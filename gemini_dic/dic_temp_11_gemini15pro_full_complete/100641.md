intolerant: /ɪnˈtɒlərənt/ (British) /ɪnˈtɑːlərənt/ (American)| adj.|不宽容的；偏狭的|Not willing to accept ways of thinking and behaving that are different from your own.|She is intolerant of any opinion that differs from her own. 她不能容忍任何与她不同的观点.|Synonyms: bigoted, biased, prejudiced, narrow-minded|Antonyms: tolerant, accepting, open-minded|adjective

intolerance: /ɪnˈtɒlərəns/ (British) /ɪnˈtɑːlərəns/ (American)| n.|不宽容；偏狭|The fact of refusing to accept ideas, beliefs, or behaviour that are different from your own.|Religious intolerance can lead to conflict. 宗教不宽容会导致冲突.|Synonyms: bigotry, prejudice, bias, narrow-mindedness|Antonyms: tolerance, acceptance, open-mindedness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin intolerantem, accusative singular masculine of intolerans, present participle of intolerare (“to not endure, not tolerate”), from in- (“not”) + tolerare (“to bear, endure”).
