go-down:/ˈɡəʊdaʊn/ (UK) /ˈɡoʊdaʊn/ (US)| n.|仓库；贮藏室；（尤指贮藏酒的）地下室；（非正式）讨厌的事物或地方|A place where goods are stored; a cellar, especially one for storing wine; (informal) a disliked place or thing|The goods were stored in the go-down until they could be shipped. 货物存放在仓库里，直到可以装运为止。|synonyms: warehouse, storehouse, cellar, depot|antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "penthouse" in a figurative sense for a disliked place)|noun

go-down:/ˈɡəʊdaʊn/ (UK) /ˈɡoʊdaʊn/ (US)| vi.|（价格、价值或数量）下降；被接受；被记住；发生；（日、月）落下|To descend; to be swallowed; to be received or remembered in a specified way; to happen; (of the sun or moon) to set|The price of oil went down last week. 上周油价下跌了。|synonyms: decrease, decline, fall, sink, set|antonyms: rise, increase, ascend|verb
