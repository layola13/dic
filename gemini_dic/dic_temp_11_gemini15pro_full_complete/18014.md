beghard:/ˈbɛɡɑːrd/| n. |贝加德教徒；乞丐修士|A member of a medieval lay religious order, primarily in the Low Countries, who lived by begging and devoted themselves to a life of poverty and piety. They were similar to, but distinct from, the Beguines, a female religious order. |Example 1: The beghard wandered the countryside, relying on the charity of others for sustenance.  贝加德教徒在乡间游荡，依靠别人的施舍维持生计。

Example 2:  Historians debate the exact relationship between the beghards and the Franciscan order.  历史学家对贝加德教徒和方济各会的具体关系争论不休。|近义词: mendicant, friar|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly affluent person/noble)|词性:noun
