fact-checking:/ˈfæktˌtʃɛkɪŋ/ | n. |事实核查，事实查证 |The process of verifying the accuracy of factual information presented in a text, article, speech, etc. |  The news organization has a dedicated team for fact-checking. 这家新闻机构有一个专门的事实核查团队。 | verification, corroboration | fabrication, falsification | noun


Etymology:
fact (n.) + checking (n. from check (v.))

fact (n.): from Latin "factum" meaning "something done, deed, act, exploit."

check (v.): from Old French "eschequier," meaning "to check, attack, put in check (in chess)."
