impudence:/ˈɪmpjʊdəns/| n.|无礼，厚颜，冒昧|The act of being rude and disrespectful, lacking modesty.|He had the impudence to interrupt the professor's lecture.|近义词：insolence, audacity, cheek, nerve, boldness|反义词: respect, deference, modesty|词性:名词

impudent:/ˈɪmpjʊdənt/| adj.|无礼的，厚颜无耻的，冒昧的|Not showing due respect for another person; impertinent.|She asked a few impudent questions.|近义词：insolent, audacious, cheeky, brazen, bold|反义词: respectful, deferential, modest|词性:形容词

词源:
Late Middle English: from Old French impudence, from late Latin impudentia, from impudent- ‘shameless’, from in- ‘not’ + pudent- ‘modest, ashamed’ (from pudere ‘be ashamed’).
