feudatory:/ˈfjuːdəˌtɔːri/ (US), /ˈfjuːdətri/ (UK)
n.|封臣；附属国；诸侯|A person or state that holds land from a feudal lord in return for allegiance and service.  A vassal or dependent.| The small country was a feudatory of its larger and more powerful neighbor.|近义词:vassal, liegeman, tributary, dependent, subordinate|反义词:lord, sovereign, overlord|词性:名词

feudatory:/ˈfjuːdəˌtɔːri/ (US), /ˈfjuːdətri/ (UK)
adj.|封建的；诸侯的|Of or relating to a feudatory.  Held by feudal tenure.|The feudatory lord demanded complete loyalty from his subjects.|近义词:feudal, vassal, dependent, subordinate|反义词:independent, sovereign|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *feudataire*, from Medieval Latin *feudatarius*, from *feudum* "fief" (see fief).
