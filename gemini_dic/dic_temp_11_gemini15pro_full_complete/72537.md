fluster:/ˈflʌstər/ | v. |使慌乱，使不安，使狼狈| To make someone nervous and confused, especially by giving them a lot to do or by making them hurry. | The interviewer’s unexpected question completely flustered her.  面试官的意外提问让她完全慌了神。|

She was flustered by the sudden attention. 突如其来的关注让她感到慌乱。| 近义词:unnerve, agitate, confuse, disconcert | 反义词:calm, compose, soothe |词性:动词

flustered:/ˈflʌstərd/ | adj. |慌乱的，不安的，狼狈的 | Feeling nervous and confused, especially because you have a lot to do or are being hurried. | He seemed flustered and unsure of what to say. 他看起来很慌乱，不知道该说什么。|

She gave a flustered apology and hurried away. 她慌乱地道歉后匆匆离开了。 |近义词:nervous, agitated, confused, disconcerted |反义词:calm, composed, relaxed | 词性:形容词
