matter-of-factness:/ˌmætər əv ˈfæktnəs/ | n. | 实事求是；平淡；冷静 | An unemotional and practical way of considering or presenting something. | She recounted the incident with chilling matter-of-factness. 她以令人不寒而栗的平静语气讲述了这件事。|Synonyms: practicality, pragmatism, realism, objectivity | Antonyms: emotionalism, sentimentality, subjectivity | noun

matter-of-fact:/ˌmætər əv ˈfækt/ | adj. | 实事求是的；平淡的；冷静的 |  Unemotional and practical.  | "He spoke in a matter-of-fact way about the accident." 他以实事求是的语气谈论了那场事故。| Synonyms: unemotional, practical, pragmatic, realistic, objective | Antonyms: emotional, sentimental, subjective | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the phrase "matter of fact," meaning something that is actually true or existent.  The suffix "-ness" turns the adjective "matter-of-fact" into a noun denoting the quality or state of being matter-of-fact.
