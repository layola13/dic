hardliner:/ˈhɑːrdlaɪnər/| n.|强硬派，强硬路线者|A person who adheres rigidly to a set of ideas or policies, especially in politics, and is unwilling to compromise.|The hardliners in the party refused to negotiate with the opposition. 党内的强硬派拒绝与反对派谈判。|Synonyms: extremist, zealot, diehard, intransigent|Antonyms: moderate, compromiser, conciliator|noun

hard-line:/ˈhɑːrdˈlaɪn/| adj.|强硬的，强硬路线的|Uncompromising and inflexible.|The government took a hard-line stance on immigration. 政府对移民问题采取强硬立场。|Synonyms: uncompromising, inflexible, rigid, unyielding|Antonyms: moderate, flexible, compromising, yielding|adjective

Etymology:
"Hard line" emerged in the mid-20th century, likely influenced by the image of a firmly drawn line that cannot be crossed.  "Hardliner" followed as a natural derivative, referring to someone who adheres to such a line.
