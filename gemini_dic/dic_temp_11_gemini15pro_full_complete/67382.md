extenuatory:/ɪkˈstɛnjuːətɔːri/
adjective
减轻罪责的；情有可原的
lessening the seriousness of something by giving reasons or excuses
His youth and lack of experience were considered extenuating circumstances. 他的年轻和缺乏经验被认为是情有可原的情况。
近义词：mitigating,  palliating, excusing
反义词：aggravating, incriminating
词性：adjective

extenuate:/ɪkˈstɛnjuːeɪt/
verb
减轻；为…辩解
to make (guilt or an offense) seem less serious or more forgivable
The lawyer tried to extenuate his client's crime by claiming he had a difficult childhood. 律师试图通过声称他的当事人童年很艰难来减轻他的罪行。
近义词：excuse, mitigate, lessen
反义词：aggravate, worsen
词性：verb
