macadamed:/məˈkædəmd/ | adj. | 用碎石铺成的 | Covered with small, broken stones, especially as a road surface. | The macadamed road was dusty in the summer. 夏天，碎石路尘土飞扬。 | Example 2:  We drove along the newly macadamed driveway. 我们沿着新铺的碎石车道行驶。| 近义词: paved, surfaced, metalled | 反义词: unpaved, dirt | 词性: 形容词

Etymology: Named after John Loudon McAdam (1756–1836), a Scottish engineer who pioneered the method of road construction using broken stones.
