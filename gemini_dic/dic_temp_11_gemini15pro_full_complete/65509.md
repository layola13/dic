eurospeak:/ˈjʊərəʊspiːk/
noun
官样文章；欧盟式官话
Jargon or rhetoric, often convoluted and opaque, characteristic of European Union pronouncements and documents.
The document was full of eurospeak, making it difficult for the average citizen to understand. 这份文件充满了欧盟式官话，让普通公民难以理解。
近义词: jargon, officialese, doublespeak
反义词: plain language, straightforward language
词性: n.


Etymology:
A blend of "Euro-" (short for "European") and "speak."  "Euro-" began to be used as a combining form in the mid-20th century, often in reference to the growing European integration.  "Speak" derives from Old English "sprecan."
