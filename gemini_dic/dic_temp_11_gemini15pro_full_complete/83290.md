gynaecophore:/ˌɡaɪnɪˈkəfɔːr/ |n.|雌雄蕊柄；雌器柄|A stalk-like structure in some plants, notably mosses and liverworts, that bears the archegonia (female reproductive organs).|The gynaecophore of the liverwort is easily visible under a microscope.  肝叶苔的雌器柄在显微镜下很容易看到。| The fertilized egg develops into a sporophyte that remains attached to the gynaecophore. 受精卵发育成孢子体，仍然附着在雌器柄上。|stalk, receptacle|None|noun
