needleworkers:/ˈniːdlˌwɜːrkərz/| n. | 刺绣工人；针线工人 | People who do needlework, such as sewing, knitting, or embroidery. | My grandmother and her friends were avid needleworkers, creating beautiful quilts and tapestries. 我的祖母和她的朋友们都是狂热的针线活爱好者，创作了美丽的被子和挂毯。|  sewers, knitters, embroiderers | - | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From needle + worker.  "Needle" comes from Old English *nēdl, from Proto-Germanic *nēdlō, from Proto-Indo-European *snēh₂tlo- (“needle”). "Worker" comes from Old English wyrcan (“to work”), of Germanic origin.
