disenchanting:/ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃæntɪŋ/
adjective
令人失望的，令人清醒的
Causing someone to stop believing in something or someone that they previously believed in or admired; making something seem less attractive or pleasant.
The disenchanting experience made her lose faith in the political system.  这令人失望的经历使她对政治制度失去了信心。
The food at the restaurant was surprisingly disenchanting.  这家餐厅的食物令人意外地失望。
近义词:disappointing, disillusioning, disheartening
反义词:enchanting, charming, delightful
词性:adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + enchanting (filling with delight).  Ultimately from Latin incantare “to enchant”
