courtliness:/ˈkɔːrtlinəs/ | n. | 礼貌；殷勤；彬彬有礼 |  Politeness and graciousness, especially of a refined or elaborate kind.  | He treated everyone with the utmost courtliness. 他待人接物彬彬有礼。 |  politeness, civility, graciousness, courtesy, affability, gentility | rudeness, impoliteness, incivility, discourtesy | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *curteisnes*, from Old French *corteisie*, from *corteis* (“courteous”), from Vulgar Latin *cortis* (“court”), from Latin *cohors*, *cohort-* (“enclosure, retinue, cohort”).
