flauntier:/ˈflɔːntiər/ | adj. | 更轻佻的，更招摇的 | More showy and frivolous; displaying in a more ostentatious or disrespectful manner. | She walked with a flauntier step than usual, her new dress swirling around her. 她走起路来比平时更轻佻，她的新裙子在她周围旋转。|  She gave him a flauntier smile than he deserved. 她给了他一个比他应得的更轻佻的笑容。 | 近义词：showier, ostentatious, flamboyant, frivolous | 反义词：modest, reserved, demure, understated | 词性: 形容词

flaunt:/flɔːnt/ | vt. | 炫耀，夸示；招摇 | To display (something) ostentatiously, especially in order to provoke envy or admiration or to show defiance. |  He flaunted his wealth by driving a flashy sports car. 他开着炫目的跑车炫耀他的财富。 |  She flaunted her new designer handbag. 她炫耀她的新款名牌手提包。 | 近义词：show off, display, parade, boast | 反义词：hide, conceal, downplay | 词性: 动词

词源：From Old Norse *flaunta "to rush about, bustle." Related to Middle Low German flanten "to be vain, flaunt."
