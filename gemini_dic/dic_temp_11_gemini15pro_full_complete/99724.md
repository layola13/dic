interfluency:/ˌɪntərˈfluːənsi/ | n. |不流利，不流畅|The state or characteristic of not being fluent, especially in speech.  A lack of fluency or smoothness, often characterized by hesitations, repetitions, or false starts.|His interfluency made it difficult to understand his presentation. 他的不流利使得他的演讲很难理解。|近义词：disfluency, hesitancy, stammering|反义词: fluency, eloquence|词性:名词

Etymology:
From inter- (between, among) + fluency.  The term highlights the interruptions or breaks "between" moments of fluency.
