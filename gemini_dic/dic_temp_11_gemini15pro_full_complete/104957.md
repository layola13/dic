kelvin-scale:/ˈkɛlvɪn skeɪl/ | n. | 开尔文温标；绝对温标 |A temperature scale starting at absolute zero (-273.15°C or -459.67°F), where zero represents the complete absence of heat energy.  Temperatures on this scale are called kelvins (K). | The boiling point of water is 373.15 kelvins.  水的沸点是373.15开尔文。 | synonyms: absolute scale, thermodynamic scale | antonyms: Celsius scale, Fahrenheit scale | noun
