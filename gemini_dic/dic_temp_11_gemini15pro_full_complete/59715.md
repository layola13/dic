eclecticists:/ɪˈklɛktɪsɪsts/
|n.|兼收并蓄者；折衷主义者
|A person who derives ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.|
|She is known for her eclectic tastes in music, ranging from classical to hip-hop.|她以其兼收并蓄的音乐品味而闻名，从古典音乐到嘻哈音乐无所不包。
|His eclectic decorating style incorporated elements of Victorian, Art Deco, and modern design.|他兼收并蓄的装饰风格融合了维多利亚时代、装饰艺术和现代设计的元素。
|Synonym: generalists, syncretists|
|Antonym: purists, specialists|
|Noun|

eclectic:/ɪˈklɛktɪk/
|adj.|兼收并蓄的；折衷的
|Deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.|
|Her eclectic music collection includes everything from jazz to heavy metal.|她的音乐收藏包罗万象，从爵士乐到重金属乐应有尽有。
|The museum's eclectic exhibits range from ancient artifacts to contemporary art.|博物馆的展品种类繁多，从古代文物到当代艺术品应有尽有。
|Synonym: diverse, varied, broad-ranging|
|Antonym: narrow, limited, specialized|
|Adjective|

eclecticism:/ɪˈklɛktɪsɪzəm/
|n.|兼收并蓄；折衷主义
|The practice of deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.|
|Eclecticism in art allows artists to draw inspiration from various cultures and periods.|艺术中的折衷主义允许艺术家从不同的文化和时期汲取灵感。
|His philosophical eclecticism led him to combine elements of Stoicism, Epicureanism, and Skepticism.|他的哲学折衷主义使他将斯多葛主义、伊壁鸠鲁主义和怀疑主义的元素结合起来。
|Synonym: syncretism, pluralism|
|Antonym: purism, dogmatism|
|Noun|
