inebriation:/ɪnˌibriˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 醉酒；陶醉 |The state of being intoxicated; drunkenness.  |He was arrested for public inebriation. 他因公共场合醉酒被捕。| 近义词：drunkenness, intoxication, tipsiness | 反义词：sobriety | noun

inebriate:/ɪˈniːbrieɪt/ | v. | 使醉；使陶醉 |To make someone drunk; to intoxicate. | The strong alcohol quickly inebriated him.  烈酒很快让他醉了。| 近义词：intoxicate, drink | 反义词：sober | verb

inebriate:/ɪˈniːbrieɪt/ | n. | 醉汉；酒鬼 | A person who is habitually drunk. | The inebriate stumbled down the street. 醉汉在街上踉跄而行。| 近义词：drunkard, alcoholic | 反义词：teetotaler | noun

词源: Late Middle English: from Latin inebriat- ‘made drunk’, from the verb inebriare, from in- ‘into’ + ebrius ‘drunk’.
