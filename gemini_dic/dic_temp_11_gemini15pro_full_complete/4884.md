align:/əˈlaɪn/| v. |排列，对齐；使一致，使结盟| to arrange something in a straight line, or to be arranged in a straight line; to change something slightly so that it is in the correct relationship to something else; to publicly support a political group, country, or person|The desks were neatly aligned in rows. 桌子整齐地排列成行。|synonyms: line up, straighten, adjust, harmonize|antonyms: misalign, skew, disarrange|verb

align:/əˈlaɪn/| n. |(尤指车辆的)车轮定位| the correct position of the wheels of a vehicle in relation to each other and to the road|The mechanic checked the car's alignment. 机械师检查了汽车的车轮定位。|synonyms: positioning, orientation|antonyms: misalignment|noun
