neoplatonism: /ˌniːoʊˈpleɪtənɪzəm/ | n. | 新柏拉图主义 | A philosophical system that originated in the 3rd century AD, based on the teachings of Plato but incorporating elements of other philosophies, especially Aristotelianism and Stoicism. It emphasizes a hierarchical view of reality, emanating from a single, supreme source known as "The One." |  Neoplatonism influenced early Christian thought. 新柏拉图主义影响了早期的基督教思想。 |  近义词: idealism, Platonism | 反义词: materialism | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From neo- + Platonism.  "neo-"  means new or revived.  "Platonism" refers to the philosophy of Plato.
