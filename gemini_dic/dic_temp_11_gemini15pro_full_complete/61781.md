emperished:/ɪmˈpɛrɪʃt/
adjective
衰弱的，奄奄一息的；极度贫困的
very weak and about to die, especially because of lack of food; extremely poor
The refugees arrived at the camp, emperished and exhausted.  难民们到达营地时，疲惫不堪，奄奄一息。
The emperished family had to beg for food.  这个贫困的家庭不得不乞讨食物。
near synonyms: impoverished, destitute, indigent, impecunious
antonyms: thriving, prosperous, affluent, wealthy
词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *emperi(s)s-, past participle of empirir “to make worse, impair,” from em- “in” + pirir “to decay, rot,” from Latin putrescere “to rot, decay,” from putrere “to rot, stink.”  The original sense was "made worse, decayed," later "brought to ruin, destroyed," and finally, "reduced to extreme want."
