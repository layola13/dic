brassinesses:/ˈbræsɪnɪsɪz/
|n.|粗俗, 厚脸皮，炫耀；(声音)刺耳，(颜色)俗艳|The brassinesses of her behavior embarrassed everyone. 她的粗俗行为让每个人都感到尴尬。|
|English Explanation: Plural form of "brassiness," referring to the quality of being brash, bold, and showy, often in a vulgar or ostentatious way. Can also describe a harsh or strident sound, or a gaudy or tasteless color.|
|Example 1: The brassinesses of the music made it hard to enjoy.  音乐的刺耳声让人难以欣赏。|

|Example 2:  She toned down the brassinesses of her outfit for the funeral. 她为了葬礼而淡化了着装的俗艳。|
|Synonyms:  vulgarities, gaudiness, flashiness, ostentation, stridency|
|Antonyms:  subtlety, refinement, elegance, understatement, softness|
|Word Class: Noun|
