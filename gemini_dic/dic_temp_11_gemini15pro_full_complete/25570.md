bombast:/ˈbɒmbæst/ (US) /ˈbɒmbæst/ (UK)| n.|夸大的言辞；浮夸的语言|High-sounding language with little meaning, used to impress people.|His speech was full of bombast, but lacked substance. 他的演讲充满了夸夸其谈，但缺乏实质内容。|synonyms: rhetoric, bluster, grandiloquence|antonyms: understatement, simplicity, conciseness|noun

Etymology: From Middle French *bombase*, from Old Italian *bombas*, "cotton padding", from Latin *bombax*, "silk" ultimately from Greek *bómbux*, "silkworm". Originally referring to cotton padding used to stuff garments, the meaning shifted to inflated or pretentious language.
