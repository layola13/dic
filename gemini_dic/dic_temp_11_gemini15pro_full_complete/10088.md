apoda:/ˈeɪpədə/| n.|蚓螈目；無足目|An order of amphibians characterized by a snake-like or worm-like body without limbs, commonly known as caecilians.|Caecilians, members of the order Apoda, are burrowing amphibians found in tropical regions.|近义词：Gymnophiona (another name for the same order)|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps contrasting with other amphibian orders like Anura (frogs) or Urodela (salamanders))|词性:noun
