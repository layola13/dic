dislikelihood:/dɪsˈlaɪklihʊd/
n.
不可能性；嫌恶
The improbability of something happening; aversion or distaste.
There is a strong dislikelihood of snow in July. 七月份下雪的可能性很小。
He expressed his dislikelihood for the proposal. 他表达了对这项提议的厌恶。
近义词: improbability, unlikeliness, aversion, distaste
反义词: likelihood, probability, liking, fondness
词性: 名词

Etymology:  From dis- "not" + likelihood.  The word "likelihood" comes from Middle English "liklyhood," from "likli" (likely) + "-hood" (state, condition).  "Likely" ultimately derives from Old Norse "likligr" (likely, probable).
