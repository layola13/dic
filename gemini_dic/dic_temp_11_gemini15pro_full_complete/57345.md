dowress:/ˈdaʊərɪs/| n.|寡妇，继承亡夫遗产的女子|A woman who holds property by virtue of being the widow of the previous owner.  A widow who inherits property or a title from her deceased husband.|例句：The dowress lived a comfortable life thanks to her husband's estate.  这位寡妇托亡夫遗产的福过着舒适的生活。

例句：The young dowress was unprepared for the responsibilities of managing the vast estate.  年轻的寡妇对管理庞大遗产的责任毫无准备。|近义词：widow, relict|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly husband)|词性:noun

词源: From Anglo-French *doweresse, from dower (嫁妆) + -esse (feminine suffix). Ultimately from Latin dos (dowry).
