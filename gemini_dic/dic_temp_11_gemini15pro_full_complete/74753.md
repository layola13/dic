freewheelers:/ˈfriːwiːlərz/
n.
惯性滑行者；自由放任者；随心所欲的人
People who coast along without much effort or direction, often in a carefree or irresponsible manner.  Those who act independently and without constraint.
The freewheelers enjoyed the downhill ride. 惯性滑行者享受着下坡骑行。
The company's freewheelers often ignored deadlines. 公司里那些自由放任者经常忽视截止日期。
近义词:nonconformists, mavericks, bohemians
反义词:conformists, traditionalists
词性:名词

freewheeler:/ˈfriːwiːlə(r)/
n.
惯性滑行者；自由放任者；随心所欲的人
A person who coasts along without much effort or direction, often in a carefree or irresponsible manner. Someone who acts independently and without constraint.
He's a bit of a freewheeler, never sticking to a plan. 他有点随心所欲，从不坚持计划。
She was a freewheeler in college, taking whatever classes she fancied. 她大学时很自由，想上什么课就上什么课。
近义词:nonconformist, maverick, bohemian
反义词:conformist, traditionalist

词源:
From free + wheel + -er.  "Free wheel" refers to a mechanism on a bicycle or other vehicle that allows the driven wheels to rotate independently of the pedals.  The figurative sense of acting without constraint or responsibility developed from this mechanical meaning.
