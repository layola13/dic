courtly:/ˈkɔːrtli/ (US) /ˈkɔːtli/ (UK)| adj.| 彬彬有礼的；温文尔雅的；宫廷式的 |Refined and elegant in manner, suitable for a royal court; polite, gracious, and dignified.|He gave a courtly bow to the queen. 他向女王彬彬有礼地鞠了一躬。|Synonyms:  polite, gracious, refined, elegant, dignified, courteous, chivalrous|Antonyms: rude, impolite, coarse, vulgar, unrefined|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *corteis* (modern French *courtois*), from *cort* ("court") + *-eis* (adjectival suffix). Ultimately from Latin *cohors* (“enclosed yard, cohort”).
