overexplicit:/ˌoʊvərɪkˈsplɪsɪt/ | adj. | 过于直白的，过于露骨的 | Stated or shown too clearly and openly, leaving no room for doubt or interpretation; excessively detailed or graphic. | The movie was overexplicit in its depiction of violence.  这部电影对暴力的描写过于直白。 |  The sex education class was criticized for being overexplicit. 性教育课程因过于露骨而受到批评。 | synonyms: graphic, explicit, detailed, blunt, forthright,  unequivocal | antonyms: implicit, subtle, vague, ambiguous, suggestive | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + explicit.  "Over-" acts as an intensifier, meaning "excessively" or "too much". "Explicit" comes from the Latin "explicitus," past participle of "explicare" (to unfold, explain), from "ex-" (out) + "plicare" (to fold).
