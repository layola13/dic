hills:/hɪlz/ | n. | 小山，丘陵 |  A usually rounded natural elevation of land lower than a mountain. |  We climbed up the rolling hills. 我们爬上了连绵起伏的小山。 | synonyms: knolls, mounds, rises, dunes | antonyms: valleys, plains, depressions | noun

hills:/hɪlz/ | n. | 斜坡，坡道 | A sloping surface. | The car struggled up the steep hill. 汽车艰难地爬上陡峭的斜坡。 | synonyms: inclines, slopes, gradients | antonyms: flats, levels | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hyll (attested only in place names), from Proto-Germanic *hulniz (source also of Old Saxon hul, Old Norse hallr, Middle Dutch hulle, Dutch heuvel, Old High German hul, German Hügel), from Proto-Indo-European *kel- (meaning "to rise, to be prominent").
