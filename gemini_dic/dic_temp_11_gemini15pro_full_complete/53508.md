disassimilation:/dɪsˌəˌsɪmɪˈleɪʃən/
n.|异化，异化作用|The process by which one sound becomes less like a neighboring sound.  This often happens to make pronunciation easier.|The word "february" is often pronounced "febuary," an example of disassimilation.|近义词：differentiation, divergence|反义词: assimilation, homogenization|词性: Noun
Etymology: From Latin dis- "apart" + similis "like" + -ation (forming nouns of action).
