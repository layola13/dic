cardinalism:/ˈkɑːrdɪnəlɪzəm/| n.|基数主义；主教主义|A system of assigning numerical values to options, especially as a basis for decision making; the doctrine that the college of cardinals elects the pope. |Example 1:  Cardinalism plays a key role in welfare economics. 基数主义在福利经济学中扮演着关键角色。

Example 2: The principle of cardinalism is central to his philosophical arguments.  基数主义原则是他哲学论证的核心。|Near Synonyms: utilitarianism, quantitativism|Antonyms: ordinalism|noun
