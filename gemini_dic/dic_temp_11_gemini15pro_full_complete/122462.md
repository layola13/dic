mobilize:/ˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz/ (US) /ˈməʊbɪˌlaɪz/ (UK)| v. |动员; 调动; |To organize or prepare something, such as a group of people, for a particular purpose.|The government mobilized the army for war. 政府动员军队准备战争。|近义词:assemble, rally, marshal, deploy|反义词:demobilize, disband|verb

mobilize:/ˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz/ (US) /ˈməʊbɪˌlaɪz/ (UK)| v. |(使)能活动，(使)能行走; |To make something movable or capable of movement.|The doctor worked to mobilize the patient's stiff joints. 医生努力使病人的僵硬关节活动起来。|近义词:activate, energize|反义词:immobilize, restrict|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French mobiliser, from mobile (“movable”) + -iser (suffix forming verbs).  Mobile comes from Latin mobilis (“movable, flexible, nimble, quick”), from movēre (“to move”).
