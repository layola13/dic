obligated: /ˈɑːblɪɡeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 有义务的，必须的 | Feeling morally or legally bound to do something. | I felt obligated to help her because she had helped me so much in the past. 我觉得有义务帮助她，因为她过去帮了我很多。|  I am obligated to pay back the loan within a year. 我有义务在一年内偿还贷款。 | bound, compelled, required, duty-bound | optional, free, exempt | adjective

obligate: /ˈɑːblɪɡeɪt/ | vt. |  使…负有义务，强制 | To bind or compel (someone) legally or morally. | The contract obligates the company to provide health insurance to its employees. 合同规定公司有义务为其员工提供健康保险。 | The law obligates parents to send their children to school. 法律规定父母有义务送孩子上学。 | compel, bind, require, force | exempt, release, excuse | verb

词源: From Late Latin obligatus, past participle of obligare (“to bind, tie up, oblige”), from ob- (“towards”) + ligare (“to bind”).
