gallingly:/ˈɡɔːlɪŋli/ | adv. | 令人气恼地，令人恼火地 | In a way that is annoying or humiliating, especially because it seems unfair.  | It was gallingly obvious that he had no intention of helping.  他明显不想帮忙，真让人恼火。| 近义词: annoyingly, irritatingly, exasperatingly | 反义词: pleasantly, agreeably | 词性: 副词

词源:
From gall (v.) + -ing + -ly.  "Gall" originally referred to bile, a bitter fluid.  Metaphorically, it came to mean "to irritate or vex."  The "-ing" suffix creates the present participle form, often used adjectivally. The "-ly" suffix transforms the word into an adverb.
