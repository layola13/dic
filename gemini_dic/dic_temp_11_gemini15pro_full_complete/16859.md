basifugal:/ˌbeɪsɪˈfjuːɡəl/ | adj. | 底向逃的，离基的 | Moving or developing away from the base or point of attachment. | The basifugal maturation of xylem means that the oldest xylem cells are at the center of the stem, and the youngest are near the outside. 木质部的底向逃成熟意味着最老的木质部细胞位于茎的中心，而最年轻的木质部细胞位于靠近外部的位置。| Synonyms: acropetal | Antonyms: basipetal | adjective

Etymology: From Latin *basis* (base) + *fugere* (to flee).
