overaffirm:/ˌoʊvərəˈfɜːrm/ | v. | 过分肯定；过度确认 | To affirm something excessively or to an unwarranted degree.  It implies going beyond simple agreement or acknowledgement, often to an extent that seems insincere or exaggerated. | He overaffirmed his support for the candidate, raising suspicions about his true motives. 他过分强调了他对候选人的支持，让人怀疑他的真实动机。|  |  affirm, overstate, exaggerate | deny, downplay | verb
