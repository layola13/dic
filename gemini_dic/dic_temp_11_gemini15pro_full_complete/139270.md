oblique:/əˈbliːk/ /oʊˈbliːk/ | adj. | 倾斜的，间接的，不直截了当的 | slanting, indirect, not straightforward |  The architect incorporated oblique lines into the building's design. 建筑师将倾斜的线条融入建筑设计中。

The politician gave an oblique answer to the journalist's question.  这位政客对记者的提问给出了一个不直截了当的回答。 | synonyms: slanted, diagonal, indirect, evasive, implicit | antonyms: straight, direct, explicit | word type: adjective

obliqued:/əˈbliːkt/ /oʊˈbliːkt/ | v. |  倾斜，使倾斜 (past tense of oblique) | to slant or cause to slant | The path obliqued sharply to the left. 小路向左急转弯。

The artist obliqued the lines in the painting to create a sense of movement.  艺术家倾斜画作中的线条以营造动感。 | synonyms: slanted, angled, inclined | antonyms: straightened | word type: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *obliquus*, from *ob* (“towards”) + *liquis* (“slanting, oblique”).
