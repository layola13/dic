creance:/ˈkriːəns/| n.|信任; 信赖; 信用; (尤指猎鹰的)长绳|A long line attached to a hawk's leash, used in falconry.|The falconer held the creance tightly as the bird took its first flight.|近义词:trust, confidence, faith, belief|反义词:distrust, mistrust, suspicion|noun

creance:/ˈkriːəns/| n.|信用; 贷款; 债权|The belief in someone's ability to pay back borrowed money.|The bank extended creance to the small business owner.|近义词:credit, loan, debt|反义词:debt (from the borrower's perspective)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "creance," meaning "belief," "trust," "faith," "loan," derived from the verb "creire" (to believe), which ultimately comes from Latin "credere" (to believe).
