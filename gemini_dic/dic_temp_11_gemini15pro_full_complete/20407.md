bite-sized:/ˈbaɪtˌsaɪzd/| adj.|一口大小的，易理解的，小型的|Small enough to be eaten in one bite; also used figuratively to describe something small and manageable.|The company offered bite-sized pieces of information to help consumers understand their complex products.  这家公司提供了小块的信息，以帮助消费者理解他们复杂的产品。|The training program was broken down into bite-sized modules, making it easier for employees to digest the material.  培训计划被分解成小块的模块，使员工更容易消化这些材料。|近义词：small, manageable, digestible, easy-to-understand|反义词：large, complex, overwhelming|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From bite (n.) + sized (adj.).  "Bite" traces back to Old English "bītan," meaning "to bite." "Sized" comes from the verb "size," which means "to adjust to a particular size," ultimately derived from Old French "asise," meaning "assessment, tax."
