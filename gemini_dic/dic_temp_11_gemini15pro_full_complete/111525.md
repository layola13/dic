loathes:/loʊðz/| v. |憎恶，厌恶| To feel intense dislike or disgust for something or someone.  It expresses a stronger feeling than dislike. | He loathes hypocrisy. 他憎恶虚伪。| synonyms: detests, abhors, despises | antonyms: loves, adores, admires | verb (third-person singular simple present loathes, present participle loathing, simple past loathed, past participle loathed)


Etymology->
From Middle English *lothen*, from Old English *lāðian* (“to be hateful, to be displeasing”), from Proto-Germanic *laþōną* (“to invite, to summon, to call”), from Proto-Indo-European *lēdʰ- (“to make hateful, to abuse”).  The meaning shifted from "to be hateful" to "to hate" over time.
