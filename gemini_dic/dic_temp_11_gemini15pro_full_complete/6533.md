amygdalin:/əˈmɪɡdəlɪn/ | n. | 扁桃苷；苦杏仁苷 | A white, crystalline, cyanogenic glucoside, C₂₀H₂₇NO₁₁, found in the kernels of bitter almonds, peaches, apricots, and other fruits of the rose family. It is hydrolyzed to glucose, benzaldehyde, and hydrocyanic acid (hydrogen cyanide).  |  Amygdalin is found in the pits of many fruits. 扁桃苷存在于许多水果的果核中。 |  Amygdalin is sometimes promoted as a cancer treatment, but there's no scientific evidence to support this claim.  扁桃苷有时被宣传为一种癌症治疗方法，但没有科学证据支持这种说法。 | Synonyms: laetrile, vitamin B17 (although it's not a true vitamin) | Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms) | Noun
