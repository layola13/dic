hydrophoby:/ˌhaɪdrəˈfoʊbiə/ | n. | 恐水症；狂犬病；憎水性 | An abnormal or morbid dread of water.  Extreme or irrational fear of water, especially as a symptom of rabies in humans. Also, the property of repelling water. |  Example 1:  The dog exhibited signs of hydrophobia, including foaming at the mouth and an inability to drink. 狗表现出狂犬病的症状，包括口吐白沫和无法饮水。

Example 2:  The new coating gives the fabric a degree of hydrophoby.  这种新的涂层赋予织物一定的憎水性。 | Synonym: aquaphobia (fear of water), rabies (disease), water-repellency | Antonym: hydrophilia (attraction to water) | noun
