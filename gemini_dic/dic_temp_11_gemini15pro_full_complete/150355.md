The word "panino" itself comes from the Italian word "pane," meaning "bread."  Therefore, "panini" literally translates to "small breads" or,in the context of food, "small sandwiches." The diminutive suffix "-ino" gives it the connotation of being a smaller, more informal type of sandwich.  The popularity of panini outside Italy has led to its usage as a generic term referring to a type of grilled sandwich, even if not strictly adhering to traditional fillings andbread types.name for a specific type of food item.
