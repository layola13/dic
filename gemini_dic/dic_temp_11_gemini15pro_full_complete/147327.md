overmortgages:/ˌoʊvərˈmɔːrɡɪdʒɪz/ | n. | 过度抵押（通常用复数形式）| A situation where the total amount of mortgages secured on a property exceeds its market value.  This can occur when property values decline, leaving the borrower with negative equity. |  Example 1: The subprime mortgage crisis led to a significant number of overmortgages. 次贷危机导致大量的过度抵押。

Example 2:  Many homeowners found themselves with overmortgages after the housing bubble burst. 房地产泡沫破裂后，许多房主发现自己过度抵押了。 | 近义词: excessive mortgages | 反义词: under-mortgaged (this term isn't commonly used, but conveys the opposite meaning) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
"over" (Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond") + "mortgage" (Old French "mortgaige," meaning "dead pledge")
