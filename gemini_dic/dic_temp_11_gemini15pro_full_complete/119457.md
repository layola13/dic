micromanaging:/ˌmaɪkroʊˈmænɪdʒɪŋ/
|n.|微观管理；事无巨细地管理|The act of controlling every part of a situation, including small details, often in a way that is annoying or not necessary.|例句：Micromanaging your team can stifle their creativity and motivation.  微观管理你的团队会扼杀他们的创造力和积极性。

例句：He resented his boss's micromanaging and constant oversight. 他憎恨老板对他事无巨细的管理和不断的监督。|近义词：overmanaging, overcontrolling|反义词：delegating, empowering|词性: 动名词 (gerund) derived from the verb "micromanage"

micromanage: /ˌmaɪkroʊˈmænɪdʒ/
|v.|微观管理；事无巨细地管理|To control every part of a situation, including small details, often in a way that is annoying or not necessary.|例句：She felt micromanaged by her new supervisor, who constantly checked her work.  她觉得她的新主管对她进行微观管理，不断地检查她的工作。

例句：He tries not to micromanage his employees, trusting them to do their jobs effectively. 他尽量不去微观管理他的员工，相信他们能有效地完成工作。|近义词：overmanage, overcontrol|反义词：delegate, empower|词性: verb (动词)

micromanagement:/ˌmaɪkroʊˈmænɪdʒmənt/
|n.|微观管理；事无巨细地管理|The practice of controlling every part of a situation, including small details, often in a way that is annoying or not necessary.|例句：The company suffered from excessive micromanagement, which hindered employee morale.  公司受到过度微观管理的影响，这损害了员工的士气。

例句：Effective leadership involves finding a balance between guidance and micromanagement.  有效的领导力包括在指导和微观管理之间找到平衡。|近义词：overmanagement, overcontrol|反义词：delegation, empowerment|词性: noun (名词)
