opinicus:/ˌoʊpɪˈnaɪkəs/ | n. |狮鹫兽，奥皮尼库斯|A mythical creature, often described as a griffin-like beast with the body of a lion, the wings and head of an eagle, and sometimes the tail of a serpent or scorpion. It was believed to be a guardian of gold and treasure. |The opinicus was said to be fiercer than a lion and swifter than an eagle. 狮鹫兽据说比狮子更凶猛，比鹰更迅速。|近义词：griffin, hippogriff |反义词: (No direct antonym for a mythical creature) |词性:noun
