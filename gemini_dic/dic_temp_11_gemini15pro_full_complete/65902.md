evulsion: /ɪˈvʌlʃən/ | n. | 脱落; 撕脱; 扯断 | The sudden tearing away of a body part, such as a finger or toe. | The climber suffered an evulsion of his thumb when the rope snapped. 登山者绳子断裂时拇指被扯断了。| synonyms: avulsion, tearing, ripping, wrenching | antonyms: attachment, connection | noun

evulsion: /ɪˈvʌlʃən/ | n. | (土地的) 冲蚀, 剥蚀 | The process of rocks or soil being removed by the force of water or ice. | The heavy rains caused evulsion of the riverbank.  暴雨导致河岸被冲蚀。| synonyms: erosion, abrasion, denudation | antonyms: accretion, deposition | noun
