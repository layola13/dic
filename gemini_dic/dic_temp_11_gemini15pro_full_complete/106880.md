labyrinthibranch:/ˌlæbəˈrɪnθɪbræŋk/ | n. | 迷鳃鱼 |A fish possessing a labyrinthine organ, a structure in the gill cavity that allows the fish to breathe atmospheric air.| The labyrinthibranch, such as the Betta fish, can survive in oxygen-depleted water. 迷鳃鱼，例如斗鱼，可以在缺氧的水中生存。|  近义词: anabantoid | 反义词: - | 词性: noun

labyrinthibranch:/ˌlæbəˈrɪnθɪbræŋk/ | adj. | 迷鳃鱼的 |Relating to or characteristic of labyrinthibranch fishes. |  Labyrinthibranch fish are known for their ability to breathe air. 迷鳃鱼以其呼吸空气的能力而闻名。| 近义词: anabantoid | 反义词: - | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "labyrinth" + "branch."  "Labyrinth" comes from the Greek "labyrinthos," referring to a complex structure or maze. "Branch" comes from the Old French "branche," related to the Latin "bracchium" meaning "arm." The term refers to the complex, maze-like structure of the labyrinth organ in the fish's gills.
