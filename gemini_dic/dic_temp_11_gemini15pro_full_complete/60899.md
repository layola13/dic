elenchical:/ɪˈlɛŋkɪkəl/ | adj. | 驳斥的，反驳的，足以驳斥的 | Serving to refute; refutatory.  Relating to elenchus, a Socratic method of refutation by short questions and answers, often ending in a contradiction which exposes the fallacy of an opponent's position. | His elenchical arguments effectively dismantled the opposition's claims. 他的驳斥性论点有效地驳倒了反对派的声明。| Synonyms: refutatory, contradictory, disproving | Antonyms: supporting, confirmatory, corroborative | adjective

elenchus:/ɪˈlɛŋkəs/ | n. | 驳斥；反驳；辩驳 |  A logical refutation; an argument that refutes another argument by proving the contradictory of its conclusion. Especially: a method of refutation that proceeds by eliciting concessions from an opponent, demonstrating that his or her position leads to a contradiction.  |  The philosopher used elenchus to expose the flaws in his student's reasoning. 这位哲学家用辩驳法揭露了他学生推理中的缺陷。 | Synonyms: refutation, rebuttal, disproof | Antonyms: proof, confirmation, corroboration | noun
