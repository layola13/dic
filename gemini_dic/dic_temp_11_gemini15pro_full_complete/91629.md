hyperbatic: /ˌhaɪpərˈbætɪk/ | adj. | 倒装的，修辞倒装的 | Involving or characterized by hyperbaton, a figure of speech in which the normal word order of a sentence is inverted, especially for emphasis. |  His hyperbatic style made his poems difficult to understand. 他的倒装句式使得他的诗歌难以理解。 |  The poet employed a hyperbatic phrasing to create a sense of mystery.  这位诗人运用倒装的措辞来营造一种神秘感。| 近义词: inverted, transposed, contorted | 反义词: natural, normal, straightforward | 词性: adjective

Etymology: From Late Latin *hyperbaticus, from Greek hyperbatos (“transposed”), from hyperbainein (“to transgress, step over”), from hyper (“over”) + bainein (“to go, step, walk”).
