mrm:/ˈmɜːrm/| interj. | 嗯嗯，咕哝 | A low, continuous, indistinct sound, typically made when expressing doubt or confusion. | Example: "Mrm," he said, stroking his chin thoughtfully. 他摸了摸下巴，若有所思地咕哝了一声“嗯嗯”。 | Example: She just mrm-ed in response, clearly not convinced. 她只是咕哝了一声作为回应，显然并不信服。 | 近义词: murmur, mutter, hum | 反义词: shout, yell | 词性: interjection
词源:  Likely imitative of the sound itself.  Many languages have similar words for this low, indistinct sound.
