jaw-dropping:/ˈdʒɔːˌdrɑːpɪŋ/| adj.|令人惊叹的，令人瞠目结舌的|Something that is jaw-dropping is extremely impressive, surprising, or shocking.|The acrobat's performance was jaw-dropping.|Synonyms: astonishing, amazing, stunning, breathtaking|Antonyms: unimpressive, boring, mundane|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
"jaw-dropping" is a compound adjective formed from "jaw" (referring to the lower jawbone) and "dropping" (the present participle of the verb "drop").  The image evoked is that of someone's jaw falling open in amazement or shock.  The term became popular in the late 20th century.
