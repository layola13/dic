microbe:/ˈmaɪkroʊb/| n. |微生物，细菌|A microorganism, especially a bacterium causing disease or fermentation. |例句：The scientist studied the microbe under a microscope. 科学家在显微镜下研究微生物。

例句：Certain microbes are beneficial to the human gut.  某些微生物对人体肠道有益。|近义词：bacterium, germ, microorganism|反义词: (No direct antonym, possibly macroorganism) |词性:noun

Etymology->
From French *microbe*, from *micro-* (small) + *-be* (life). Ultimately from Greek *μικρός* (mikrós, “small”) + *βίος* (bíos, “life”).
