backdoor:/ˈbækˌdɔːr/ (US), /ˌbækˈdɔː(r)/ (UK)| n. adj.|后门，秘密通道；(计算机)后门程序|A backdoor is a method of bypassing normal security or authentication in a computer system, product, or embedded device, or other systems such as financial institutions, and organizations. |The hackers gained access to the system through a backdoor.|Synonyms: secret entrance, hidden access, loophole, trapdoor|Antonyms: front door, main entrance|noun

backdoor:/ˈbækˌdɔːr/ (US), /ˌbækˈdɔː(r)/ (UK)| adj.|秘密的，不正当的|Relating to or involving a backdoor.|The company accused its competitor of using backdoor tactics to gain market share.|Synonyms: clandestine, covert, surreptitious, underhand|Antonyms: overt, open, legitimate|adjective
