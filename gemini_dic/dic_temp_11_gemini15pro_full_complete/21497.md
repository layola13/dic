bludged:/blʌdʒd/ | adj.| 疲惫不堪的，精疲力竭的 | Exhausted, worn out, especially from hard work or exertion. |  I felt absolutely bludged after working a 12-hour shift. 我在工作了12个小时的轮班后感到筋疲力尽。|  He was bludged from the long journey.  漫长的旅程使他筋疲力尽。 | 近义词: exhausted, worn out, drained, spent, knackered | 反义词: energetic, refreshed, invigorated | 词性: 形容词

bludgeon:/ˈblʌdʒən/ | n. | 棍棒，短棒 | A short, heavy club. |  The attacker used a bludgeon to subdue his victim. 袭击者用一根棍棒制服了他的受害者。 | The knight carried a heavy bludgeon into battle. 骑士带着一根沉重的棍棒进入战斗。 | 近义词: club, cudgel, truncheon | 反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 weapon) | 词性: 名词

bludgeon:/ˈblʌdʒən/ | vt.| 用棍棒打，强迫，威逼 | To hit with a bludgeon; to force or coerce (someone) to do something. | The protesters were bludgeoned by the police. 抗议者遭到警察的殴打。|  He felt bludgeoned into accepting the deal. 他感觉自己被迫接受了这笔交易。 | 近义词: batter, club, coerce, force, compel | 反义词: persuade, coax | 词性: 动词

词源:  "Bludgeon" comes from the Old French word "bloignon," meaning a type of wood used for clubs.  The verb sense, “to coerce,” developed metaphorically from the literal act of hitting with a club.
