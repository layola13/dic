fitche:/fɪtʃ/ | n. | Fitch, Vair, Fitchy| A heraldic term for a stylized depiction of a weasel or polecat, especially when the animal's skin is used as fur. It's often depicted horizontally, with the head, tail, and feet visible.  In the vair pattern, it appears as a pointed, shield-shaped motif. | The duke's coat of arms featured three fitches on a field of azure. 公爵的盾形纹章在蔚蓝的底色上有三只鼬。 | Synonyms:  polecat, weasel (in a heraldic context), vair (when referring to the pattern) | Antonyms: N/A (No direct heraldic opposite) | Noun
