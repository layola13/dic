cherub:/ˈtʃɛrəb/ /ˈtʃɜːrəbz/| n. |小天使，胖娃娃|A cherub is a type of angel, often depicted as a beautiful, winged child.  It is also often used as an affectionate term for a cute, chubby baby or child.|He looked at his sleeping baby and thought, "What a little cherub." 他看着熟睡的婴儿，心想：“真是个小天使。”|Synonyms: angel, putto, toddler (when referring to children)|Antonyms: devil, demon|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cherub*, from Old French *cherubin*, from Late Latin *cherubin*, from Greek *χερουβείμ (kheroubeim)*, plural of *χερουβ (kheroub)*, from Hebrew *כְּרוּב‎ (kərūḇ)*.
