conjure:/ˈkʌndʒər/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒə(r)/ (UK)| v. |变戏法；使…呈现；使…想起|To perform magic tricks; to make something appear as if by magic; to evoke a feeling, memory, or image.|例句：The magician conjured a rabbit out of a hat. 魔术师从帽子里变出一只兔子。

例句：The smell of baking bread conjured up memories of my childhood. 烤面包的味道唤起了我对童年的回忆。|近义词：evoke, summon, invoke, materialize|反义词：banish, dispel, dismiss|词性：动词

conjures:/ˈkʌndʒərz/ (US), /ˈkʌndʒəz/ (UK)| v. |变戏法；使…呈现；使…想起 (第三人称单数)|Third person singular present tense form of "conjure".|例句：He conjures images of faraway lands in his paintings.  他在他的画作中描绘了遥远土地的景象。

例句：She conjures up a feast with whatever ingredients she can find. 她用任何能找到的食材变出一顿盛宴。|近义词：evokes, summons, invokes, materializes|反义词：banishes, dispels, dismisses|词性：动词

词源:
From Old French conjurer "to invoke (a devil or spirit), exorcise, cast a spell," from Late Latin conjurare "to swear together, conspire," from Latin com- "together" + jurare "to swear."  The meaning shifted in English to "perform magic tricks" in the late 16th century.
