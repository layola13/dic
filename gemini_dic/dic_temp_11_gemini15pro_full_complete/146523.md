overgesticulating: /ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛstɪkjʊˌleɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 过分做手势的，夸张地做手势的 |  Using too many gestures or making gestures that are too large or dramatic.  |  He was overgesticulating wildly as he told the story, making it hard to focus on what he was saying.  他讲故事的时候手势夸张，让人很难集中注意力听他讲的内容。 |  exaggerated, flamboyant, demonstrative | understated, restrained | Adjective


overgesticulate: /ˌoʊvərˈdʒɛstɪkjʊˌleɪt/ | v. | 过分做手势，夸张地做手势 |  To use too many gestures or make gestures that are too large or dramatic. | She tends to overgesticulate when she's nervous. 她紧张的时候容易做过多手势。 |  exaggerate, dramatize,  embellish | understate, downplay | Verb


Etymology:
From over- + gesticulate.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much." "Gesticulate" comes from the Latin word "gesticulatus," the past participle of "gesticulari," meaning "to gesture" or "make gestures."
