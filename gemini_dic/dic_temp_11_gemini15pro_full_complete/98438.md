inspectors:/ɪnˈspɛktərz/ | n. |检查员，巡视员| Officials who examine something carefully to ensure it meets regulations or standards. |The inspectors examined the factory for safety violations. 检查员检查了工厂的安全违规情况。|
inspectors:|/ɪnˈspɛktərz/ | n. |督察，督学| Officials who oversee a particular area or activity. |The school inspectors observed several classes. 学校督学观察了几个班级。|
inspectors:|/ɪnˈspɛktərz/ | n. |(票务)查票员| Officials who check tickets on public transport. |The inspectors came around to check our tickets on the train. 查票员在火车上过来查我们的票。|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *inspectour*, from Latin *inspector*, agent noun from *inspect-,* past participle stem of *inspicere* "to look into, examine," from *in-* "in, into" + *specere* "to look at."
