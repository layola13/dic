collative:/ˈkɒlətɪv/ /ˈkɑːlətɪv/| adj.|整理的，推论的，比较的|Relating to or involving the collection and comparison of data or information, often to infer or deduce something.  Used especially of legal proof based on inferences rather than direct evidence.|The lawyer presented a collative argument based on circumstantial evidence.|近义词：inferential, deductive, comparative|反义词：direct, empirical|词性:形容词
Etymology: From Late Latin *collativus*, from Latin collatus, past participle of conferre "to bring together, compare," from com- "together" + ferre "to carry, bring."
