frescoing:/ˈfrɛskoʊɪŋ/
noun, gerund or present participle
壁画绘制；湿壁画技法
The act of painting on fresh plaster.
例句：The frescoing of the Sistine Chapel took Michelangelo several years. 西斯廷教堂的壁画绘制花费了米开朗基罗数年时间。

例句：Frescoing is a demanding art form requiring skill and patience.  湿壁画是一种要求技巧和耐心的苛刻艺术形式。

近义词：mural painting, wall painting
反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "canvas painting" as a contrasting technique)
词性: Noun/Gerund/Present Participle


fresco:/ˈfrɛskoʊ/
noun
壁画
A painting done rapidly in watercolor on wet plaster on a wall or ceiling, so that the colors penetrate the plaster and become fixed as it dries.
例句：The frescoes in the church depict scenes from the Bible. 教堂里的壁画描绘了圣经中的场景。

例句：The artist is known for his vibrant and detailed frescoes.  这位艺术家以其充满活力和细节丰富的壁画而闻名。

近义词：mural
反义词: (No direct antonym)
词性: Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *fresco* "fresh," from Latin *frescus*.
