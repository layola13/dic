adages:/ˈædɪdʒɪz/ | n. | 谚语；格言；箴言 | A traditional saying expressing a common experience or observation. |  Example: "A stitch in time saves nine" is a useful adage.  “一针及时省九针”是一句有用的谚语。| Example: The adage "look before you leap" is good advice. 谚语“三思而后行”是一个好建议。 | Synonyms: proverb, saying, maxim, aphorism | Antonyms:  None (adages don't have direct opposites) | noun

PHRASE->
There are not many common phrases using "adages" specifically.  It's more common to see phrases using synonyms like "proverbs" or "sayings". However, here are a few examples using variations:

following the adage: 遵循谚语
as the adage goes: 正如谚语所说
contrary to the adage: 与谚语相反
an old adage says: 一句古老的谚语说
remember the adage: 记住这句谚语


Etymology:  From Late Middle English: from Old French, from adage ‘saying’, from Latin adagium, from adagere ‘to say in addition’.
