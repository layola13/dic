oversharpness:/ˈoʊvərˈʃɑːrpnəs/
|n.|过度锐化，过分鲜明|An excessive sharpness, often in an image or sound, that can appear unnatural or harsh.  It's often a result of over-processing or enhancing.|The oversharpness of the photo made the edges look jagged.|近义词：excessive sharpness, harshness, unnatural clarity|反义词:softness, blurriness|词性:名词

oversharp:/ˌoʊvərˈʃɑːrp/
|adj.|过度锐化的，过分鲜明的|Excessively sharp, often referring to an image or sound. It can create an unnatural or harsh appearance or sound.|The image was oversharp, making it look artificial.|近义词：excessively sharp, harsh, unnatural|反义词:soft, blurry, muted|词性:形容词

Etymology:
over- (prefix):  From Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *ubar, meaning "above," "across," "beyond," etc.  It indicates excess or surpassing a limit.
sharp (adjective/noun): From Old English scearp, from Proto-Germanic *skarpaz, meaning "having a cutting edge or point."  It refers to a distinct, well-defined, and often intense quality.
-ness (suffix):  Forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
