hyperight: /ˈhaɪpəˌraɪt/ | n. |过度权利；超级权利| A hypothetical right that transcends or supersedes other established rights.  This word is not commonly used and may be considered neologistic. |  The concept of "hyperight" is still developing, and there isn't much established usage.  A hypothetical example: "He claimed a hyperight to privacy that superseded freedom of information laws." | Near synonyms:  absolute right, paramount right | Antonyms:  limited right, qualified right | noun
