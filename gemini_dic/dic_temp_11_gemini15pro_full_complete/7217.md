anglicanism:/ˈæŋɡlɪkənɪzəm/| n.|英国国教, 圣公会主义|The doctrine and practice of the Church of England and of other churches in communion with it.  It is characterized by a combination of Catholic and Protestant traditions.|例句：Anglicanism is the dominant religion in England.  英国国教是英格兰的主要宗教。

例句：He converted to Anglicanism after years of being a Methodist.  在做了多年的卫理公会教徒之后，他皈依了英国国教。|近义词：Episcopalianism|反义词: Catholicism, Protestantism|词性:noun
Etymology:  From Anglo-French *Anglican, from Medieval Latin Anglicanus (“English”), from Angli (“the Angles”). + -ism.  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *Anglijaz.
