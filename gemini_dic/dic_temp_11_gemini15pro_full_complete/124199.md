moseyed:/ˈmoʊ.zid/ | v. | 漫步，闲逛 | To walk or move in a leisurely or aimless way. |  He moseyed down the street, looking in shop windows. 他在街上漫步，看着商店橱窗。| 近义词: amble, stroll, saunter, meander | 反义词: rush, hurry, sprint | 词性: 动词

词源: Originating in the American West in the mid-19th century, the etymology of "mosey" is uncertain. Some speculate it may be a variant of "mose," a now obsolete word meaning "to move," or perhaps related to the Spanish word "vamos," meaning "let's go."
