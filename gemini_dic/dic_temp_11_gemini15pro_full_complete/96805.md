inert gases: /ɪˈnɜːrt ˈɡæsɪz/ (ɪˈnɝːt ˈɡæsɪz) | n. |惰性气体|Gases that are very unreactive, also known as noble gases. They belong to Group 18 of the periodic table.|Helium is an inert gas used in balloons.|Synonyms: noble gases, rare gases|Antonyms: reactive gases|noun

inert: /ɪˈnɜːrt/ (ɪˈnɝːt) | adj. |惰性的，不活泼的|Lacking the ability or strength to move.|The inert gas didn't react with the other chemicals.|Synonyms: inactive, passive, motionless|Antonyms: active, reactive, mobile|adjective

gas: /ɡæs/ | n. |气体|A substance or matter in a state in which it will expand freely to fill the whole of a container, having no fixed shape or volume.|Oxygen is a gas essential for life.|Synonyms: vapor, fume|Antonyms: solid, liquid|noun
