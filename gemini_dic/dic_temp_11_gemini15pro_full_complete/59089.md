ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dēagian, from Proto-Germanic *daujaną (“to dye, color”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰewh₂- (“to dust, smoke, cloud”). Cognate with Old Frisian dāia (“to dye”), Dutch deien, German täuen (“to thaw”). Related to dew. The noun is from the verb.
