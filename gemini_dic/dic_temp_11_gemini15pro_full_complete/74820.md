frenchs:/ˈfrɛntʃɪz/ | n. |法国人（复数，非标准用法）|  "Frenchs" is not standard English. The correct plural of "French" (as a noun referring to people) is "French people" or less commonly, "the French".  It is sometimes seen as the possessive form of "French", but even then, it's usually written as "French's". | Example:  Incorrect: The Frenchs are known for their cuisine. | Correct: French people are known for their cuisine. | Synonym: (for French people) Frenchmen, Frenchwomen (outdated and less preferred) | Antonym: N/A | Noun
