bully:/ˈbʊli/ (US), /ˈbʊli/ (UK)| n. v. adj.| 欺负; 恃强凌弱者; 优秀的;| A person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those whom they perceive as vulnerable.  As a verb, it means to use superior strength or influence to intimidate (someone), typically to force him or her to do what one wants. As an adjective (dated, chiefly US), it means excellent or first-rate.|

He was bullied relentlessly by his classmates. 他不断地被同学们欺负。
