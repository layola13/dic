free-falling: /ˌfriːˈfɔːlɪŋ/ | adj. | 自由落体的；暴跌的 | Falling freely under the influence of gravity, typically without a parachute.  Also used figuratively to describe a rapid and uncontrolled decline. | The skydiver was free-falling for several seconds before deploying his parachute.  跳伞员在打开降落伞之前自由落体了几秒钟。|  The stock market is free-falling. 股票市场正在暴跌。 | 近义词： plummeting, plunging, dropping, descending | 反义词: rising, ascending, climbing | 词性: 形容词

free-fall: /ˈfriːfɔːl/ | n. v. | 自由落体；暴跌 |  n. The motion of a body when only the force of gravity is acting on the body. v. To fall freely under the influence of gravity. |  The free-fall seemed to last forever. 自由落体似乎持续了很久。| The company's profits are in free-fall. 公司的利润暴跌。 | 近义词： plunge, drop, descent (n.); plummet, plunge, drop (v.) | 反义词: rise, ascent, climb (n.); rise, ascend, climb (v.) | 词性: 名词，动词


词源:  "Free" (from Old English "freo," meaning "exempt, not in bondage") + "fall" (from Old English "feallan," meaning "to fall down").  The combined term describes a fall unimpeded by any restraint.
