mared:/mɛərd/ | adj. | 梦魇缠身的，噩梦般的 | Affected by nightmares; nightmarish. |  She awoke from a mared sleep, her heart pounding. 她从噩梦中惊醒，心跳加速。|  The child had a mared expression on his face.  孩子脸上带着噩梦般的表情。| Synonyms: nightmarish, haunted, disturbed, troubled | Antonyms: peaceful, restful, serene | adjective

词源: From Old English *marad, past participle of *marian "to dream, have a vision," from Proto-Germanic *marōn "to dream." Related to Old Norse mara "nightmare," Old High German mar "nightmare, incubus," Gothic marzjan "to offend, cause to stumble." The word "mare" in "nightmare" originally referred to a female demon or goblin believed to sit on people's chests while they slept, causing bad dreams.
