appurtenances: /əˈpɜːrtənənsɪz/
| n. |附属物；配件；附加物|Something added to another, more important thing; a secondary or less significant thing or part.  An accessory or item associated with a particular activity or style of living. Usually used in the plural.|The house comes with all appurtenances, including the swimming pool and the guest house. 这栋房子包含所有附属设施，包括游泳池和客房。|Synonyms: accessories, attachments, belongings, paraphernalia, trappings|Antonyms: necessities, essentials|Noun

appurtenance: /əˈpɜːrtənəns/
| n. |附属物；配件；附加物|Something added to another, more important thing; a secondary or less significant thing or part.  An accessory or item associated with a particular activity or style of living.|The shed is an appurtenance to the main house.  这个棚屋是主屋的附属建筑。|Synonyms: accessory, attachment, belonging, adjunct|Antonyms: necessity, essential|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *apartenance, from apartenir "to appertain," from a- "to" + partenir "to pertain," from Latin pertinēre "to belong," from per- "thoroughly" + tenēre "to hold."
