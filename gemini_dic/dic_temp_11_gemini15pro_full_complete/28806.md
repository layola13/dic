caroms:/ˈkærəmz/ | n. v. | 撞击得分，碰库球；使撞击得分 | The act of a billiard ball rebounding off one or more cushions or balls before striking another ball.  Also, the game played on a pocketless billiard table, in which points are scored by caroming. |  The cue ball caromed off two cushions and sank the eight ball. 母球撞击两个库边后击落八号球。| synonyms: rebounds, glances, cannons (UK) | antonyms: misses | noun

caroms:/ˈkærəmz/ | v. |  使撞击得分，碰库球 | To cause a ball to rebound off one or more cushions or balls before striking another ball. | He expertly caromed the cue ball off the side cushion to sink the nine ball. 他熟练地让母球撞击侧库边后击落九号球。| synonyms: rebound, glance, cannon (UK) | antonyms: miss | verb

词源:  From French *carambole*, of uncertain origin, perhaps from Spanish *carambola* "star fruit," from its shape.


注意事项:  Caroms is more commonly used as a noun referring to the game or the act of caroming.  As a verb, it's less frequent but still acceptable.  The UK term for carom is *cannon*.
