kun:/kʊn/ | n. | 鲲 | A giant fish in Chinese mythology that transforms into a giant bird called Peng.  It is a symbol of transformation and limitless potential. | Example: The kun, in Zhuangzi's philosophy, represents the possibility of boundless change. 庄子哲学中的鲲象征着无限变化的可能性。 | Example: Legends say the kun could swallow the ocean whole.  传说鲲可以吞下整个海洋。 | Synonym: giant fish, mythical creature | Antonym: N/A | noun

kun:/kʊn/ | adj. |  (internet slang, often derogatory) unskilled, noob-like (especially in video games) |  Derived from a mispronunciation of "koon," a racial slur. Although the intended meaning is different, due to its origin, it's considered highly offensive and should be avoided. | Example: "That player is so kun, they keep dying." 那个玩家太菜了，一直送人头。 | Example: "He's such a kun at this game, he doesn't even know the basic controls." 他玩这个游戏太菜了，连基本操作都不知道。 | Synonym: noob, unskilled, bad (at gaming) | Antonym: pro, skilled, expert | adjective
