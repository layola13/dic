bipolarized:/ˌbaɪˈpoʊləˌraɪzd/ | adj. | 双极化的，两极分化的 | Having two poles or extremes. Divided into two sharply contrasting groups or sets of opinions or beliefs. | The political landscape has become increasingly bipolarized in recent years. 近年来，政治格局日益两极分化。| The battery is bipolarized. 电池是双极化的。 | polarized, divided | unified, homogenous | adjective

Etymology:
From bi- (prefix meaning "two") + polarized (from polarize, from polar + -ize).  The word "polar" ultimately comes from the Greek word "polos" meaning "axis" or "pole."
