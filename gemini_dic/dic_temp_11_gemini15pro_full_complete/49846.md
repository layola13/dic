demoralized:/dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪzd/ /dɪˈmɔːrəˌlaɪzd/
adjective
士气低落的，沮丧的
Having lost confidence or hope; disheartened.
The team was demoralized after losing the championship game. 球队在输掉冠军赛后士气低落。
The constant criticism demoralized the employees.  不断的批评使员工们感到沮丧。
近义词:discouraged, disheartened, dejected, depressed
反义词:encouraged, heartened, optimistic, confident
词性:adjective

demoralize:/dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪz/ /dɪˈmɔːrəˌlaɪz/
verb
使士气低落，使沮丧
To cause (someone) to lose confidence or hope; dispirit.
The loss demoralized the entire army.  这场失败使整支军队士气低落。
His harsh words demoralized her. 他严厉的话让她很沮丧。
近义词:discourage, dishearten, dispirit, depress
反义词:encourage, hearten, inspire
词性:verb

词源:
From French *dé-* (expressing negation) + *moraliser*, from *moral* (“moral”)
