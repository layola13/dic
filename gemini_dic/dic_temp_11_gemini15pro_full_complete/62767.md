engrain:/ɪnˈɡreɪn/ | v. | 深深印入；使根深蒂固 | to fix deeply and firmly, as in the mind or memory | The lessons of childhood are often deeply engrained. 童年的教训常常根深蒂固。| 近义词：implant, embed, instill, fix | 反义词：erase, efface, obliterate | 词性：动词

engrained:/ɪnˈɡreɪnd/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；牢固的 | firmly fixed or established; deeply rooted |  He has an engrained prejudice against foreigners. 他对外国人有根深蒂固的偏见。| 近义词：inbred, ingrained, deep-rooted, fixed | 反义词：superficial, shallow | 词性：形容词

词源: From Old French *engrenier*, from *en-* (in) + *grain* (grain). Originally referring to dyeing with kermes, a red dye derived from insects, which penetrated the fabric like a grain.  后来引申为在头脑中牢固地建立或植入。
