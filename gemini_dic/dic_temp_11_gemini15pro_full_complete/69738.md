feram:/ˈfɛrɑːm/ | v. | I bear, carry; I endure |  This word is a first-person singular present active indicative form of the Latin verb "fero". It translates to "I bear," "I carry," or "I endure."  | I feram this burden. 我承受着这个负担。|  tolerate, withstand, support, carry | refuse, reject | verb

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
Onus fertur a me. = The burden is carried by me.
Responsabilitates feruntur ab illis. = The responsibilities are carried by them.


疑问句:
Ferisne onus? = Do you carry the burden?
Quid fers? = What do you carry?


否定句:
Non fero onus. = I do not carry the burden.
Non tulit dolorem. = He did not bear the pain.



PHRASE->
(Phrases as such don't exist in Latin in the same way as English. However, here are some common usages of "fero"):
ferre auxilium = to bring help
ferre legem = to propose a law
ferre sententiam = to express an opinion
ferre bellum = to wage war
ferre manum = to lend a hand

注意事项:

"feram" is only one form of the verb "fero."  This verb is highly irregular and has many different forms depending on tense, person, number, mood, and voice.

Latin grammar is complex, and these examples are simplified for illustrative purposes. A comprehensive understanding requires further study of Latin grammar.
