mercership:/ˈmɜːrsərʃɪp/
n.
商人的身份或职业；商业
The state or quality of being a merchant; the business of a merchant.
He inherited the family business and took on the mercership at a young age. 他继承了家族企业，并在年轻时就承担起了商人的身份。
The mercership thrived in the bustling port city. 商业在这个繁华的港口城市蓬勃发展。
近义词: trade, commerce, business
反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider unemployment, inactivity)


词性: noun


词源: From "mercer" (a dealer in textiles) + "-ship" (suffix denoting state, quality, or office).  "Mercer" comes from Old French "mercier," ultimately from Latin "merx" (merchandise).
