hydrostatician:/ˌhaɪdrəʊstəˈtɪʃən/ | n. | 流体静力学家 | A scientist or engineer specializing in hydrostatics, the branch of physics dealing with the properties of liquids at rest. |  Example 1: The hydrostaticians were consulted on the design of the dam.  流体静力学家被咨询了大坝的设计。

Example 2:  A skilled hydrostatistician can calculate the pressure exerted by a fluid at any given depth.  一名熟练的流体静力学家可以计算流体在任何给定深度施加的压力。 |  Synonyms: fluid mechanics expert, hydraulics engineer (somewhat broader) | Antonyms:  N/A (lack of a direct opposite concept) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From hydro- + static + -ian.  "Hydro-" comes from Greek "hydōr" meaning "water". "Static" comes from Greek "statikos" meaning "causing to stand," from "statos," meaning "standing."  "-ian" is a suffix denoting a person skilled in or specializing in something.
