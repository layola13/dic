compartmentalized:/kəmˌpɑːrtˈmɛntəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 分隔的，划分成各自独立部分的 | Divided into separate sections or categories, often with the implication that interaction or communication between them is limited. |  The company is highly compartmentalized, with each department operating independently.  这家公司被高度分隔，每个部门都独立运作。 |  The human brain is compartmentalized, with different areas responsible for different functions. 人脑是分隔的，不同的区域负责不同的功能。 |  segmented, partitioned, categorized, classified | integrated, unified, holistic | adjective

compartmentalize:/kəmˌpɑːrtˈmɛntəlaɪz/ | v. | 分隔，划分 | To divide into separate sections or categories. | She compartmentalizes her life, keeping her work and personal life completely separate. 她把自己的生活划分开来，把工作和私人生活完全分开。 | He tries to compartmentalize his emotions, but sometimes they overwhelm him. 他试图控制自己的情绪，但有时它们会让他不知所措。 |  categorize, classify, divide, separate, pigeonhole | integrate, unify, combine | verb

词源: From compartment + -alize.  Compartment comes from French compartiment, from compartir "to divide, share," from Latin compartiri, from com- "together" + partire "to divide."
