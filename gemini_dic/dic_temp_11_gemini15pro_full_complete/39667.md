conservator:/kənˈsɜːrvətər/ |kənˈsɝːvətər/| n. |管理员，保护者，文物修复师|A person who protects, preserves, or restores something, especially works of art, buildings, or other cultural artifacts. |The conservator carefully cleaned the ancient manuscript.  文物修复师小心翼翼地清洁了古代手稿。|Synonyms: curator, guardian, preserver, restorer |Antonyms: destroyer, vandal|noun

conservator:/kənˈsɜːrvətər/ |kənˈsɝːvətər/| n. |(法律) 保护人，监护人|A person appointed by a court to manage the affairs of someone legally incapable of doing so themselves. |The court appointed a conservator to oversee the elderly woman's finances. 法院指定了一名保护人来监督这位老妇人的财务。|Synonyms: guardian, trustee, custodian|Antonyms: ward|noun
