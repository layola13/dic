explode:/ɪkˈsploʊd/ (US) /ɪkˈspləʊd/ (UK)| v. |爆炸，爆发，激增|To suddenly increase greatly in size, number, or intensity; to burst or shatter violently as a result of internal pressure.|The population exploded in the last decade.  过去十年人口激增。

The bomb exploded, destroying the building. 炸弹爆炸，摧毁了建筑物。|近义词：burst, detonate, erupt, blow up|反义词:implode, collapse|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin explodere "to drive out by clapping, hiss off the stage," from ex- "out" + plaudere "to clap."  The sense evolution likely went from "hissing an actor off stage" to a more general "drive out forcefully" and then to "burst forth."
