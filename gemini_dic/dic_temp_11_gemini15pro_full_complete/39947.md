constructionist:/kənˈstrʌkʃənɪst/ | n. adj. | 建构主义者，建构主义的 | Someone who believes that knowledge and reality are created through social interaction and cultural experiences, rather than being objective or inherent.  A theory of knowledge that argues that humans generate knowledge and meaning from their experiences. |  Example (n.): The constructionist argued that gender roles are not biologically determined but are socially constructed.  建构主义者认为，性别角色不是由生物决定的，而是社会建构的。

Example (adj.):  A constructionist approach to learning emphasizes the active role of the learner in building understanding. 建构主义的学习方法强调学习者在构建理解中的积极作用。 | Near synonyms: constructivist, interpretivist | Antonyms: objectivist, realist, essentialist | Noun, Adjective
