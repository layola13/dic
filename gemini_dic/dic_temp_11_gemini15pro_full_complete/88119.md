hijack:/ˈhaɪdʒæk/ | n. vt. |劫持，挟持|  The act of illegally taking control of a vehicle, especially a plane, ship, or train while it is travelling; to take control of something and use it for your own purposes. | The terrorists hijacked the plane and forced it to fly to another country. 恐怖分子劫持了飞机，迫使它飞往另一个国家。| synonyms: seize, commandeer, appropriate | antonyms: release, surrender | noun, verb

hijacks:/ˈhaɪdʒæks/ | v. |劫持，挟持(第三人称单数)|  The act of illegally taking control of a vehicle, especially a plane, ship, or train while it is travelling; to take control of something and use it for your own purposes (third-person singular present tense). | He hijacks conversations to talk about himself. 他劫持谈话来谈论他自己。| synonyms: seizes, commandeers, appropriates | antonyms: releases, surrenders | verb
