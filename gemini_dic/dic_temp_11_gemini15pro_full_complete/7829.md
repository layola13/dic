antagonizing: /ænˈtæɡənaɪzɪŋ/
verb (present participle)
对抗；引起…的敌意；激怒
acting in a way that makes someone angry or hostile.
例句：His antagonizing behavior made him few friends. 他的对抗行为让他几乎没有朋友。

例句：She was constantly antagonizing her colleagues with her sarcastic remarks. 她不断用讽刺的言辞激怒她的同事。

近义词：provoking, irritating, alienating, exasperating
反义词：appeasing, conciliating, soothing, pacifying
词性: v.


antagonist: /ænˈtæɡənɪst/
noun
对手；敌手；反面人物
a person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone or something; an adversary.
例句：The antagonist in the story is a cruel and powerful wizard.  故事中的反面人物是一个残忍而强大的巫师。

例句：He plays the antagonist to the hero in the film. 他在电影里扮演与男主角对立的反面角色。

近义词：opponent, adversary, foe, rival
反义词：protagonist, ally, supporter, friend
词性: n.

antagonism: /ænˈtæɡənɪzəm/
noun
对抗；敌对；对立
active hostility or opposition.
例句：There is a long history of antagonism between the two countries. 这两个国家之间长期存在敌对关系。

例句：Her antagonism towards her boss was obvious. 她对她老板的敌意显而易见。

近义词：hostility, animosity, friction, opposition
反义词：friendship, amity, harmony, cooperation
词性: n.


antagonistic: /ænˌtæɡənˈɪstɪk/
adjective
对抗的；敌对的；对立的
showing or feeling active hostility or opposition.
例句：He has an antagonistic attitude towards authority. 他对权威持敌对态度。

例句：The two groups had an antagonistic relationship. 这两个群体之间关系敌对。

近义词：hostile, opposed, adversarial, unfriendly
反义词：friendly, amicable, cooperative, supportive
词性: adj.



词源:  From Late Latin *antagonista, from Ancient Greek ἀνταγωνιστής (antagonistēs, “rival, competitor, opponent”), from ἀντί (antí, “against”) + ἀγωνίζομαι (agōnizomai, “I contend for a prize”) + -τής (-tēs, agent noun suffix).
