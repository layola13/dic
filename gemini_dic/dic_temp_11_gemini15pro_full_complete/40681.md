conservationism: /ˌkɑːnsərˈveɪʃənɪzəm/ | n. | 保护主义，守旧主义 |  A philosophy that emphasizes the wise use and preservation of natural resources and existing institutions. | Conservationism is important for protecting endangered species. 保护主义对于保护濒危物种至关重要。 | The museum promotes conservationism through its exhibits.  博物馆通过展览宣传保护主义。| 近义词：environmentalism, preservationism | 反义词：radicalism, progressivism | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From conserve (v.) + -ation + -ism.  Conserve ultimately comes from Latin *conservāre, from com- (together) + servāre (to keep).  The suffix -ation forms nouns of action or process, while -ism denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.
