dapple:/ˈdæpəl/| v. n. adj.|斑点，斑纹，使…有斑点|A dapple is a patch of contrasting color or shade, often used to describe markings on animals or light filtering through trees.  To dapple something is to mark it with dapples.|The horse's coat was a beautiful dapple grey. 这匹马的皮毛是漂亮的斑点灰色。

The sunlight dappled the forest floor. 阳光透过树叶，在地面上形成斑驳的光点。|Synonyms: spot, speckle, mottle, fleck|Antonyms: solid, uniform|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *daplen, frequentative of *dappen (“to stain, spot”), of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Old Norse depla (“to splash”).
