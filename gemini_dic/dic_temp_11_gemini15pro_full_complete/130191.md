non-satiation:/ˌnɒnˌseɪʃiˈeɪʃn/ |n.| 不满足，不知足 |The state of not being satisfied or having enough; the desire for more. |The economist's model assumed non-satiation, meaning consumers always prefer more of a good. 经济学家的模型假设了不满足感，这意味着消费者总是想要更多的好处。

Economists assume non-satiation in consumer preferences. 经济学家假设消费者偏好中存在不满足感。|近义词：insatiability, greed, avarice|反义词：satisfaction, contentment| 词性: 名词

non-satiable: /ˌnɒnˈseɪʃəbl/ |adj.| 不知足的，无法满足的 |Not capable of being satisfied; insatiable. |His non-satiable appetite for knowledge led him to read hundreds of books. 他对知识永不满足的渴望使他阅读了数百本书。

She had a non-satiable desire for power. 她对权力有着永不满足的渴望。|近义词：insatiable, voracious, greedy|反义词：satiable, content| 词性: 形容词
