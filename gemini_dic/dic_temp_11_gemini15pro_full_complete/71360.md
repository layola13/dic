flag:/flæɡ/| n. v. |旗帜，标记；衰退，变弱|A piece of cloth, usually rectangular, of distinctive color and design, used as a symbol, standard, signal, or emblem.|The American flag waved in the breeze. 美国国旗在微风中飘扬。|近义词：banner, standard, ensign|反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑用antonym for the verb form: strengthen, revive)|词性: noun, verb

flag:/flæɡ/| v. |标记；衰退，变弱|To mark or identify with a flag; to become tired or weak.|I flagged the important pages in the book. 我在书中标记了重要的页面。  The runner's pace flagged near the end of the race.  赛跑选手在接近终点时速度慢了下来。|近义词：(for marking) mark, label, tag; (for weakening) weaken, decline, fade|反义词: (for marking) unmark; (for weakening) strengthen, revive|词性: verb
