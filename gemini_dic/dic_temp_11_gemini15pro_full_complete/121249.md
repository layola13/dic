misers:/ˈmaɪzərz/| n. |吝啬鬼，守财奴|A person who hoards wealth and spends as little money as possible.|Scrooge McDuck is a famous fictional miser who swims in his money. 史高治·麦克老鸭是一位著名的虚构吝啬鬼，他在自己的钱里游泳。|The old misers refused to give to charity. 这些老吝啬鬼拒绝捐款给慈善机构。|synonyms: skinflint, cheapskate, tightwad, penny-pincher|antonyms: spendthrift, philanthropist|noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *misere* "wretchedness, poverty, misfortune" from Latin *miseria* "wretchedness, misery, poverty," from *miser* "wretched, unfortunate." The sense shifted from "wretched person" to "greedy person" in the 16th century.
