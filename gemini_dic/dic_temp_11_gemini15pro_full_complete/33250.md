chronophotograph: /ˌkrəʊnəʊˈfəʊtəɡrɑːf/ /ˌkrɑːnəˌfoʊtəˈɡræf/ | n. |时间摄影照片，连续摄影照片 | A photograph that captures movement by recording a series of images in rapid succession, typically on a single plate or film strip.  It shows the subject in different positions over time, giving an impression of motion. | Eadweard Muybridge's chronophotographs of a galloping horse proved that all four hooves leave the ground at some point. 埃德沃德·迈布里奇拍摄的奔马的时间摄影照片证明了马的四个蹄子在某个时刻都会离开地面。

| Marey's chronophotographs revolutionized the study of human and animal locomotion. 马雷的时间摄影照片彻底改变了对人类和动物运动的研究。 | synonyms: sequence photography, motion study photograph, time-lapse photography | antonyms: still photograph, single exposure | noun
