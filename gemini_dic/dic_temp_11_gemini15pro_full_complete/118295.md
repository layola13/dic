mercuriality:/ˌmɜːrkjʊəriˈælɪti/ | n. | 善变；反复无常；活泼；水银性 | The state of being mercurial; characterized by rapid and unpredictable changeableness of mood. | His mercuriality makes him difficult to work with.  他的反复无常让他很难共事。 | Example: The actor's mercuriality allowed him to play a wide range of characters.  这位演员的性格多变使他能够扮演各种各样的角色。 | Synonyms: volatility, fickleness, capriciousness, inconstancy | Antonyms: stability, constancy, steadiness | noun

mercurial:/mɜːrˈkjʊəriəl/ | adj. |  善变的；反复无常的；活泼的；水银的 |  Characterized by rapid and unpredictable changeableness of mood.  | Her mercurial temperament made her unpredictable. 她反复无常的性情使她难以捉摸。 | Example: The stock market's mercurial nature can be both exciting and terrifying for investors. 股市的波动性对投资者来说既令人兴奋又令人恐惧。 | Synonyms: volatile, fickle, capricious, inconstant | Antonyms: stable, constant, steady | adjective
