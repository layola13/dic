cabinet:/ˈkæbɪnɪt/ | n. | 橱柜；内阁；展览柜 |A piece of furniture with shelves, drawers, and doors, used for storing or displaying things. A group of ministers who control a government. A small room used for a particular purpose. | The dishes are kept in the kitchen cabinet.  盘子放在厨房的橱柜里。| synonyms: cupboard, closet, chest, ministry, administration | antonyms:  N/A | noun

cabinet:/ˈkæbɪnɪt/ | adj. | 内阁的；秘密的 | Relating to a political cabinet or conducted in private. |  a cabinet meeting 内阁会议 | synonyms: ministerial, governmental, private, confidential | antonyms: public, open | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *cabinet*, diminutive of cabane "cabin, hut".  Ultimately from Late Latin capanna.  The sense of a private room for consultations led to the meaning of a group of advisors.
