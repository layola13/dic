feebleness:/ˈfiːblnəs/| n.|虚弱，衰弱|The feebleness of her voice indicated how ill she was. 她微弱的声音表明她病得很重。|例句：The feebleness of the government's response to the crisis was shocking. 政府对危机的反应软弱无力，令人震惊。

The feebleness of the old man made it difficult for him to walk. 老人的虚弱使他行走困难。

近义词：weakness, frailty, debility, infirmity|反义词:strength, robustness, vigor|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *febilnesse*, from Old French *feblesse*, from *feble* (“feeble”) + *-esse* (suffix forming abstract nouns of quality).  Ultimately from Latin *flebilis* (“lamentable, deplorable”), from *flēre* (“to weep”).
