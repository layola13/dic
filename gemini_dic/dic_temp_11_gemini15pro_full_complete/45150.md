cudgelers:/ˈkʌdʒələrz/| n. |棍棒打手；用棍棒打的人|People who beat with cudgels. |The cudgelers attacked the travelers on the lonely road. 棍棒打手袭击了在偏僻道路上的旅客。|Synonyms:  beaters, assailants, attackers|Antonyms: protectors, defenders|Noun

cudgel:/ˈkʌdʒəl/| n. vt.|棍棒；用棍棒打|A short, thick stick used as a weapon; to beat with a cudgel.|He defended himself with a cudgel. 他用棍棒自卫。  The farmer cudgelled the fox that was attacking his chickens. 农夫用棍棒打了袭击他鸡的狐狸。|Synonyms: club, bludgeon, stick, beat, bash|Antonyms: shield, protect|Noun, Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *kuggel*, from Old French *coignoier* "to wedge, fasten with wedges" (compare Modern French *coin* "wedge, corner"), from Latin *cuneus* "wedge." The sense shifted from "wedge-shaped club" to any short thick club.
