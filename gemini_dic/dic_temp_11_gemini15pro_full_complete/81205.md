grandmasterly:/ˌɡrændˈmɑːstərli/| adj.|大师级的，精湛的|Showing the skill or knowledge of a grand master; supremely skillful and accomplished. |His performance on the piano was grandmasterly. 他在钢琴上的演奏堪称大师级。|Near synonyms: masterful, expert, proficient, adept, virtuoso|Near antonyms: amateurish, clumsy, inept, unskilled|adjective

grandmasterly:/ˌɡrændˈmɑːstərli/| adv.|大师般地，精湛地|In a grandmasterly manner. (Less common usage)|He solved the complex puzzle grandmasterly. 他大师般地解开了这个复杂的谜题。|Near synonyms: masterfully, expertly, proficiently, adeptly|Near antonyms: amateurishly, clumsily, ineptly, unskillfully|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From grandmaster + -ly.  Grandmaster (n.) dates from the late 14th century, referring to the head of a military order (like the Knights Templar).  Later, it came to mean a great master of an art or craft.  The suffix -ly forms adverbs and adjectives, here indicating a quality or manner resembling that of a grandmaster.
