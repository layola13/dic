overorchestrating: /ˌoʊvərˈɔːrkɪstreɪtɪŋ/
verb
过度安排；过度精心策划
arranging or planning something in an excessively elaborate or complicated way, often to the point of being counterproductive.
The event was overorchestrated, leaving little room for spontaneity.  这个活动安排得过于精心，几乎没有留下任何即兴发挥的空间。
The marketing campaign was so overorchestrated that it felt inauthentic.  这次营销活动策划得过于复杂，以至于让人感觉不真实。
近义词: overmanaging, overplanning, micromanaging
反义词: simplifying, streamlining
词性: v.


overorchestrated: /ˌoʊvərˈɔːrkɪstreɪtɪd/
adjective
过度安排的；过度精心策划的
having been arranged or planned in an excessively elaborate or complicated way.
The overorchestrated wedding felt more like a performance than a celebration.  这场过度安排的婚礼感觉更像一场表演，而不是一次庆祝。
The presentation was overorchestrated and lacked a natural flow.  这个演讲过度精心策划，缺乏自然的流畅性。
近义词: overmanaged, overplanned
反义词: simple, streamlined
词性: adj.



词源: "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "orchestrate" (verb meaning "to arrange or score (music) for an orchestra")
