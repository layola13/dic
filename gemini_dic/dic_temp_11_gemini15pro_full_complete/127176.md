naturalize:/ˈnætʃrəlaɪz/ /ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪz/| v. |使归化；移植；使入籍；使习惯；同化|To grant full citizenship to (a foreigner).  To introduce (a plant or animal) to a new region and cause it to flourish as if native. To adopt (something foreign) as if it were one's own.|Many immigrants wish to naturalize as soon as possible. 许多移民希望尽快入籍。|synonyms:acclimatize, adapt, assimilate, integrate|antonyms:alienate, expatriate|verb

naturalize:/ˈnætʃrəlaɪz/ /ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪz/| adj. |归化的；驯化的； eingebürgert|Made to appear natural; informal. Existing in or caused by nature; not artificial.|He had a very naturalized accent. 他的口音很自然。|synonyms:indigenous, domestic, native|antonyms:foreign, exotic, alien|adjective
