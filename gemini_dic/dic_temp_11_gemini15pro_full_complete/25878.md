burlesque: /bərˈlɛsk/ (US), /bɜːˈlɛsk/ (UK) | n. adj.| 滑稽模仿；滑稽歌舞表演；滑稽作品 | A literary, dramatic or musical work intended to cause laughter by caricaturing the manner or spirit of serious works, or by ludicrous treatment of their subjects.  A theatrical entertainment of a broadly humorous, often earthy character, consisting of short turns, comic skits, and sometimes striptease. |  The play was a burlesque of Shakespearean tragedy.  这出戏是对莎士比亚悲剧的滑稽模仿。 | Synonyms: parody, satire, travesty, caricature, lampoon | Antonyms: serious, respectful, reverent | noun

burlesque: /bərˈlɛsk/ (US), /bɜːˈlɛsk/ (UK) | adj.| 滑稽模仿的；滑稽歌舞表演的 |  Characterized by or relating to burlesque. | It was a burlesque performance, full of slapstick and silly jokes.  这是一场滑稽的表演，充满了闹剧和愚蠢的笑话。 | Synonyms: parodic, satirical, comical, ludicrous | Antonyms: serious, dramatic, tragic | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *burlesco*, from *burla* "joke, mockery," perhaps from Old Spanish and Provençal *burla*, of uncertain origin.
