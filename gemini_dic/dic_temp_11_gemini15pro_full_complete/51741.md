devolutionary: /ˌdiːvəˈluːʃəneri/ /ˌdevəˈluːʃəneri/ | adj. |  权力下放的；退化的； 交还的 | Relating to devolution, the transfer or delegation of power to a lower level, especially by central government to local or regional administration; also, relating to degeneration or decline. | The Scottish Parliament is a product of the devolutionary settlement of 1998.  苏格兰议会是1998年权力下放协议的产物。

The devolutionary pressures within the empire eventually led to its collapse.  帝国内部的权力下放压力最终导致其崩溃。 | 近义词: decentralized, devolved | 反义词: centralized, unitary | 词性: adjective

Etymology:  From "devolution" + "-ary."  "Devolution" comes from Late Latin "devolutio,"  meaning "a rolling down," from the verb "devolvere," meaning "to roll down,"  formed from "de-" (down) + "volvere" (to roll).
