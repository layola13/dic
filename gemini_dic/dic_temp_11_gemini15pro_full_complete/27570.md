canalized:/ˈkænəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 沟渠化的，(发育)限制的，稳定的 |  restricted to a narrow range of variation or expression, typically as a result of genetic or developmental factors.  |  The development of the nervous system is highly canalized, meaning disruptions early on have limited impact. 神经系统的发育是高度限制的，这意味着早期的干扰影响有限。 |  channeled, fixed, restricted, entrenched | inflexible, variable, open, plastic | adjective

canalize:/ˈkænəlaɪz/ | v. | 使成沟渠，引导，限制 | To direct or constrain into a particular course or channel. To make into or provide with a canal or canals. In biology, to restrict the phenotypic expression of a genotype to a narrow range of outcomes. |  The river was canalized to prevent flooding. 这条河被开凿成运河以防止洪水泛滥。  The government tried to canalize public opinion in favor of the war. 政府试图引导公众舆论支持战争。  | channel, direct, guide, constrain | release, free, open | verb


Etymology:
From French canaliser, from canal (“canal”).  Ultimately from Latin canalis (“pipe, groove, channel”).
