defacing:/dɪˈfeɪsɪŋ/
|v.|污损，毁坏(外观)；丑化|marring the appearance of; vandalizing|The vandals were caught defacing the statue with graffiti. 那些故意破坏公物者在用涂鸦污损雕像时被抓获。|synonyms: vandalizing, disfiguring, spoiling, marring, blemishing|antonyms:  beautifying, adorning, enhancing, restoring|verb

defaced:/dɪˈfeɪst/
|adj.|被污损的，被毁坏的|having been marred or spoiled|The defaced monument was a sad sight. 被污损的纪念碑是一幅令人悲伤的景象。|synonyms: marred, spoiled, damaged, blemished, disfigured|antonyms: pristine, immaculate, unblemished, perfect|adjective

defaces:/dɪˈfeɪsɪz/
|v. (third person singular present)|污损，毁坏(外观)；丑化(第三人称单数)|mars the appearance of; vandalizes (in the third person singular)|He defaces public property whenever he gets the chance. 他一有机会就污损公共财产。|synonyms: vandalizes, disfigures, spoils, mars, blemishes|antonyms: beautifies, adorns, enhances, restores|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desfacer, from des- (expressing removal) + face (face). Ultimately from Latin dis- (apart, away) + facies (face, appearance).
