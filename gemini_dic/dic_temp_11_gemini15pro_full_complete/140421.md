okay:/oʊˈkeɪ/ (US) /əʊˈkeɪ/ (UK)| adj. adv. int.|好的，可以的，行了|An informal word indicating agreement or acceptance; all right.|例句：Okay, I'll see you tomorrow. 好的，明天见。|近义词：all right, fine, good, alright|反义词:no, bad, terrible|词性:adjective, adverb, interjection
Etymology:  Probably from "oll korrect," a humorous misspelling of "all correct," popularized in the 1830s US presidential campaign of Martin Van Buren.
