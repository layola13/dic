fayette:/feɪˈɛt/| n. |费耶特（地名，人名）|A surname and placename of French origin, derived from the Old French word "faiete" meaning "fallow land" or "beechwood". It is often used as a given name in the United States, particularly in honor of the Marquis de Lafayette, a French aristocrat and military officer who played a key role in the American Revolutionary War.|例句：Fayette County is located in Kentucky. 费耶特县位于肯塔基州。

例句：Marie-Joseph Paul Yves Roch Gilbert du Motier, Marquis de Lafayette, was a key figure in the American Revolution.  玛丽-约瑟夫·保罗·伊夫·罗奇·吉尔伯特·杜·莫蒂埃，拉法耶特侯爵，是美国独立战争中的关键人物。|近义词： (As a place name, synonyms depend on the specific place.  As a name, no direct synonyms.) |反义词: (None) |词性:noun
