discrepancy: /dɪˈskrepənsi/ | n. | 差异，不符，不一致 | a difference or inconsistency between two or more facts, figures, or claims | There's a discrepancy between what he told me and what she told me. 他告诉我的和她告诉我的之间存在差异。|  There is a large discrepancy between the ideal and the real. 理想与现实之间存在很大差距。 | synonyms: difference, inconsistency, variation, divergence, disparity, conflict, contradiction | antonyms: agreement, consistency, conformity, similarity, harmony | noun


discrepancies: /dɪˈskrepənsiz/ | n. | 差异，不符，不一致（复数）| differences or inconsistencies between two or more facts, figures, or claims |  There were several discrepancies in the witness's statements.  证人陈述中存在多处不符。| The audit revealed discrepancies in the company's accounts. 审计发现了公司账目中的差异。 | synonyms: differences, inconsistencies, variations, divergences, disparities, conflicts, contradictions | antonyms: agreements, consistencies, conformities, similarities, harmonies | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *discrepantia, from Latin discrepans, present participle of discrepare "to sound differently, disagree," from dis- "apart" + crepare "to rattle, creak, crack."
