adjoint:/ˈædʒɔɪnt/ /əˈdʒɔɪnt/ | adj. n.|伴随的，附属的；伴随矩阵；伴随算子|Something that is adjoint is added or joined to something else, but is not an essential part of it.  In mathematics, it refers to a specific type of matrix or operator.|The adjoint of a matrix is obtained by transposing the matrix and then taking the complex conjugate of each entry.|近义词：auxiliary, supplementary, accompanying|反义词:separate, detached|adjective

adjoint:/ˈædʒɔɪnt/ /əˈdʒɔɪnt/ | n.|伴随矩阵；伴随算子；助手；附属物|In mathematics, the adjoint is a specific type of matrix or operator. More generally, it can refer to an assistant or something added.|The adjoint of a differential operator is another differential operator.|近义词：assistant, adjunct, appendage|反义词:principal, primary|noun
