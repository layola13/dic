alliterate:/əˈlɪtəreɪt/| v.|押头韵；首音相同|to use words that begin with the same sound, especially in poetry|例句：Poets often alliterate to create a musical effect. 诗人经常使用押头韵来营造音乐效果。|例句：She alliterated cleverly in her speech, using many words starting with "s". 她在演讲中巧妙地使用了押头韵，用了很多以"s"开头的词。|近义词：assonate, rhyme|反义词:|词性:verb

alliteration:/əˌlɪtəˈreɪʃn/| n.|押头韵；头韵|the use of the same letter or sound at the beginning of words that are close together|例句：The alliteration in the poem enhanced its rhythm. 这首诗中的押头韵增强了它的节奏感。|例句："Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers" is a classic example of alliteration. “彼得·派珀摘了一小撮腌辣椒”是押头韵的经典例子。|近义词：assonance, consonance|反义词:|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin alliteratio, from allitera- ‘having letters in common’, from the verb alliterare, from ad- ‘to’ + littera ‘letter’.
