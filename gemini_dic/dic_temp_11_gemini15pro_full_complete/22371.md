booby-trapped:/ˈbuːbiˌtræpt/ |adj.|装有饵雷的，设下陷阱的| rigged with a trap to catch or harm the unwary| The soldiers cautiously entered the booby-trapped building. 士兵们小心翼翼地进入了装有饵雷的建筑物。| The enemy had booby-trapped the bridge. 敌人在这座桥上设下了陷阱。 |Synonyms: trapped, rigged, mined| Antonyms: safe, secure| Adjective

booby-trap:/ˈbuːbiˌtræp/ |n.|饵雷，陷阱| a trap designed to catch or harm someone, often disguised as something harmless| The soldiers discovered a booby trap hidden under some leaves. 士兵们发现树叶下藏着一个饵雷。|  They set a booby trap for the unsuspecting intruder. 他们为毫无戒心的入侵者设下了一个陷阱。 |Synonyms: trap, snare, ambush| Antonyms: safeguard, protection| Noun

booby-trap:/ˈbuːbiˌtræp/ |v.|在…装饵雷，设陷阱| to set a booby trap|  The guerrillas booby-trapped the road leading to the village. 游击队在通往村庄的道路上设置了饵雷。| They booby-trapped the door so it would explode when opened. 他们在门上装了饵雷，以便它在打开时爆炸。 |Synonyms: trap, rig, mine| Antonyms: secure, protect| Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "booby," a term for a foolish or naive person (from Spanish "bobo"), + "trap."  The image is of a trap designed to catch someone easily fooled.
