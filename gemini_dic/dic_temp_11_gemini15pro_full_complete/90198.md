hough: /hʌf/ | n. | 霍夫 | A variant spelling of huff, meaning a fit of anger or resentment.  A deep, audible breath. |  He left in a hough after the argument. 他在争吵后愤然离去。|She took a deep hough before diving into the cold water. 她深吸一口气，然后跳入冰冷的水中。 | synonyms: huff, pique, resentment, snit,  inhalation | antonyms:  calm, contentment, pleasure, exhalation | noun

hough: /hɒk/ | v. | 割断腿筋；飞奔；蹒跚 |  To hamstring (an animal). To move clumsily or with difficulty; hobble. To move quickly; run. |  The farmer houghed the cattle to prevent them from wandering too far.  农民割断了牛的腿筋，以防止它们走得太远。 | limp, hobble, stumble, run, dash | walk smoothly, glide | verb
