disparkle:/dɪˈspɑːrkl/| v. |失去光彩，黯淡|To lose sparkle or brilliance; to become dull or dim.|The fireworks disparkled in the night sky. 烟花在夜空中失去了光彩。|The once vibrant colors of the painting disparkled over time. 这幅画曾经鲜艳的色彩随着时间的推移而暗淡了。|近义词：fade, dim, dull|反义词：sparkle, brighten, shine|词性:动词

Etymology: dis- + sparkle

dis-:  a prefix meaning "apart," "asunder," "away," "utterly," or having a privative or negative force, occurring in words of Latin origin 

sparkle: From Middle English *sparklen, frequentative of sparken (“to emit sparks”).
