词源:
From French chagrin, from Old North French chagreignier (“to grieve”), probably from Vulgar Latin *carricāre (“to load”), from Latin carrus (“wagon”).  The original sense was of sadness or grief, later evolving to the current sense of annoyance and disappointment.
