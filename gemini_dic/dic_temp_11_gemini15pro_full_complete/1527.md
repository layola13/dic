achaeans:/əˈkiːənz/
| n. |阿凯亚人|A member of a Greek-speaking people who dominated mainland Greece from the 20th to the 16th century BC, traditionally believed to have been the destroyers of Troy.|The Achaeans were skilled warriors and sailors. 阿凯亚人是熟练的战士和水手。|Synonyms:  Mycenaeans, Argives, Greeks|Antonyms: Trojans (in the context of the Trojan War)|Noun
