henchman:/ˈhɛntʃmən/| n.|爪牙，帮凶，走狗|A faithful follower, especially one prepared to engage in crime or violence on behalf of their leader.|The dictator surrounded himself with ruthless henchmen. 独裁者身边都是一群残忍的爪牙。|Synonyms: minion, underling, lackey, accomplice, goon|Antonyms: leader, boss, chief|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *henxman*, from *hengest-man* (“groom”), from Old English *hengest* (“horse”) + *mann* (“man”).  Originally referred to a groom or squire, later evolving to mean a trusted follower, often involved in illicit activities.
