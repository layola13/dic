contextualised:/kənˈtekstʃuəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 上下文化的，根据上下文的 | Placed or studied in context; considered together with the surrounding words or situation. | The meaning of the word is contextualised by the surrounding sentences.  这个词的意思是由周围的句子 contextualised 的。 | contextual, situated | decontextualised | adjective

contextualised:/kənˈtekstʃuəlaɪzd/ | v. | 置于语境中，根据上下文理解 | To place (something, such as a word or activity) in a context. |  The teacher contextualised the historical event for the students. 老师为学生们讲解了这一历史事件的背景。 |  place in context, situate | abstract, isolate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From context + -ual + -ise/-ize.  Context comes from Latin contextus, meaning "a joining together," from con- "together" + texere "to weave."
