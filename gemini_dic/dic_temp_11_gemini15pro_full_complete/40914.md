cooled-off:/kuːld ˈɔːf/
adjective
冷却的；平静的；冷静的
Having become less hot, or having returned to a normal temperature after a period of anger or excitement.
The cooled-off lava formed new rock formations. 冷却的熔岩形成了新的岩层。
After a heated argument, they both needed some time to cooled-off.  经过激烈的争论后，他们都需要一些时间冷静下来。
近义词:calm, composed, serene, tranquil
反义词:heated, angry, agitated, excited
词性:adjective

cooled-off:/kuːld ˈɔːf/
verb
（使）冷却；（使）平静下来；（使）冷静下来
To become less hot, or to return to a normal temperature after a period of anger or excitement.
We let the soup cooled-off before eating it. 我们让汤冷却后再吃。
The police tried to cooled-off the angry protesters. 警察试图让愤怒的抗议者冷静下来。
近义词:calm down, settle down, sober up
反义词:heat up, fire up, agitate
词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "cool" + "off" (adverb/preposition indicating completion or separation). "Cool" comes from Old English "col," related to Old High German "kalt" (cold), and ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*kaldaz."  "Off" comes from Old English "of," meaning "away from," "off," or "from."
