calendrer: /kælɪnˈdrɜːr/ (If this is intended to be the French word) /ˈkælɪndərər/ (possible anglicized pronunciation)
| n. | 轧光机; 压延机 | A machine used to smooth or finish paper, cloth, or other materials by pressing them between rollers.  (If this is intended as a French word, it translates to "calender" in English and refers to a machine used to smooth or finish paper, cloth, or other materials by pressing them between rollers.) | The calendrer produced a smooth, glossy finish on the paper. 轧光机使纸张表面光滑有光泽。 | synonyms: rolling machine, calender, mangle | antonyms: N/A | noun

Etymology: From French *calendrer*, from Late Latin *calendāre* "to press cloth," from Latin *calendae*, the first day of the Roman month.  The original sense was related to pressing or smoothing cloth newly washed for special occasions.
