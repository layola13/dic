jitterbugging:/ˈdʒɪtərˌbʌɡɪŋ/
n.|吉特巴舞，跳吉特巴舞|A lively dance popular in the swing era, characterized by fast tempo and acrobatic movements.|例句：They were jitterbugging to the music of Glenn Miller. 他们在随着格伦·米勒的音乐跳吉特巴舞。|例句：The dance floor was packed with couples jitterbugging the night away. 舞池里挤满了跳吉特巴舞的夫妇，他们跳了一整夜。|近义词：swing dancing, lindy hop|反义词:waltz, slow dance|词性:noun

jitterbug:/ˈdʒɪtərˌbʌɡ/
n.|吉特巴舞爱好者|Someone who enjoys and frequently performs the jitterbug.|例句：He was a real jitterbug back in the day. 他以前可是个真正的吉特巴舞迷。|例句：My grandmother was a jitterbug in her youth. 我祖母年轻时是个吉特巴舞爱好者。|近义词：swing dancer, lindy hopper|反义词:None|词性:noun

jitterbug:/ˈdʒɪtərˌbʌɡ/
v.|跳吉特巴舞|To dance the jitterbug.|例句：They jitterbugged until the band stopped playing.  他们一直跳吉特巴舞，直到乐队停止演奏。|例句：We jitterbugged all night long. 我们跳了一整夜的吉特巴舞。|近义词：swing dance, lindy hop|反义词:waltz, slow dance|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From jitter + bug, likely influenced by the jerky movements of the dance resembling a jittery bug.  "Jitter" meaning to act nervously or excitedly, and "bug" referring to an insect.
