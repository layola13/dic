caretaking: /ˈkɛərˌteɪkɪŋ/ (US), /ˈkeəˌteɪkɪŋ/ (UK)
n.|照看；照顾；护理|The act of taking care of someone or something, especially a person who is ill, elderly, or needs help.|She took on the caretaking of her elderly parents.|近义词：caregiving, tending, nursing, looking after|反义词：neglecting, abandoning|词性:名词

caretaking: /ˈkɛərˌteɪkɪŋ/ (US), /ˈkeəˌteɪkɪŋ/ (UK)
adj.|照顾的；护理的|Relating to or involved in the act of taking care of someone or something.|She had caretaking responsibilities for her younger siblings.|近义词：caregiving, nurturing|反义词：neglectful, uncaring|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "care" + "taking". "Care" comes from Old English "caru, cearu" meaning "anxiety, sorrow, grief," later "serious attention, heed, caution." "Taking" comes from Old English "tacan," meaning "to grasp, seize, lay hold of."
