gauntlet:/ˈɡɔːntlɪt/ (US), /ˈɡɔːntlɪt/ (UK)| n.| 铁手套，挑战，严峻考验 |A strong, heavy glove, often made of leather and extending partway up the arm, worn for protection;  a challenge; an ordeal.| The knight threw down his gauntlet as a challenge. 骑士扔下他的铁手套以示挑战。 |  Synonyms: challenge, trial, ordeal | Antonyms: surrender, submission | noun

gauntlet:/ˈɡɔːntlɪt/ (US), /ˈɡɔːntlɪt/ (UK)| n.| 惩罚通道 | Two lines of men armed with clubs or other weapons through which a person had to pass while being struck as a punishment.|  The captured soldier was forced to run the gauntlet. 被俘的士兵被迫穿过惩罚通道。| Synonyms: ordeal, punishment | Antonyms: reward, praise | noun
