lymphocyte: /ˈlɪmfəsaɪt/ (US) /ˈlɪmfəsʌɪt/ (UK)| n. |淋巴细胞|A type of white blood cell that is part of the immune system and develops from stem cells in the bone marrow.  |  Example: Lymphocytes play a crucial role in fighting infections. 淋巴细胞在对抗感染中起着至关重要的作用。| Example:  A high lymphocyte count can indicate certain illnesses. 淋巴细胞计数高可能表明某些疾病。 |Synonym:  leukocyte, white blood cell| Antonym:  None (Lymphocytes are a specific type of cell; there isn't a direct opposite.) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From lymph + -cyte.  Lymph comes from Latin *lympha "water," related to Greek nymphe "nymph."  -cyte is a suffix denoting a cell, from Greek -kytos "hollow vessel, cell."
