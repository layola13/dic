dandyism's:/ˈdændiɪzəmz/
n.
纨绔主义, 花花公子作风
The philosophy or practice of a dandy; excessive concern with appearance, elegance, and fashion.
He inherited a fortune and embraced dandyism's pursuit of exquisite style. 他继承了一笔财产，并欣然接受了纨绔主义对精致风格的追求。
His dedication to fashion bordered on dandyism's extremes. 他对时尚的投入近乎纨绔主义的极端。
近义词: foppery, affectation, preciosity
反义词: unpretentiousness, simplicity, naturalness
词性: noun (possessive form)


ETYMOLOGY->
From dandy + -ism. Dandy originated in the late 18th century, possibly from a pet form of Andrew or a Scottish word meaning "a man who is excessively concerned with fashion." The suffix -ism denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.
