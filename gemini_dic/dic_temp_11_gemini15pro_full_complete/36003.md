codgers:/ˈkɒdʒəz/ (British), /ˈkɑːdʒərz/ (American)| n. |怪老头，老家伙（通常指一群）|An informal, often humorous term for an elderly man, typically used in the plural to refer to a group of older men.  It often carries a connotation of being eccentric or set in their ways.|The old codgers at the pub were grumbling about the price of beer. 酒吧里的老家伙们在抱怨啤酒的价格。|近义词：old men, geezers, seniors, retirees|反义词:youngsters, youths|noun

词源：
The origin of "codger" is uncertain. One theory suggests it comes from "cadger," meaning a beggar or peddler.  Another proposes a link to "cottager," referring to someone who lives in a cottage. The exact etymology remains debated.
