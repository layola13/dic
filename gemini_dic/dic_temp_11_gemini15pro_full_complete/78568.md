ghyll:/ɡɪl/ | n. |峡谷; 溪流; 狭窄的山谷 |A deep, narrow ravine, especially one with a stream running through it.  A ghyll is a deep, narrow valley or ravine, typically one formed by water erosion. |We hiked through the scenic ghyll, marveling at the waterfalls. 我们沿着风景优美的峡谷徒步旅行，惊叹于瀑布的美景。|The ghyll was filled with the sound of rushing water. 峡谷里充满了奔腾的水声。|近义词：ravine, gorge, canyon, valley|反义词:plain, plateau|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From the Old Norse *gil, meaning "ravine," related to "gully."  This word is primarily used in northern England, particularly the Lake District.
