Etymology: From Middle English *handkerchef*, from Anglo-Norman *handkerchief*, from Old French *couvrechief* (literally "cover-head"), from *covrir* ("to cover") + *chief* ("head").  The "hand" portion was added later due to its primary use in the hand.
