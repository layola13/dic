exercises:/ˈeksərˌsaɪzɪz/ (US) /ˈeksəsaɪzɪz/ (UK)
n.|练习，运动|A series of actions done regularly to improve or maintain one's skills or physical condition.|She does her exercises every morning. 她每天早上做运动。|practice, drills, workouts|inactivity, idleness|noun
exercises:/ˈeksərˌsaɪzɪz/ (US) /ˈeksəsaɪzɪz/ (UK)
v.|锻炼，练习，运用|To engage in physical activity to improve strength or fitness; to practice or use a skill, right, or quality.|He exercises his right to vote. 他行使了他的投票权。|train, practice, apply|neglect, ignore|verb

Etymology:  From Middle English *exercisen*, from Old French *exerciser*, from Latin *exercēre* ("to keep busy, train, exercise"), from *ex- ("out")* + *arcēre ("to keep off, confine, enclose")*.
