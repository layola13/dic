allomorph:/ˈæləmɔːrf/ (US) /ˈæləmɔːf/ (UK)| n. |同位异音，词素变体|One of the variant forms of a morpheme.  An allomorph is a variant form of a morpheme that occurs in different contexts but has the same meaning. For example, the plural morpheme in English has three allomorphs: /-s/, /-z/, and /-ɪz/.|The plural marker -s is an allomorph of the plural morpheme. 复数标记 -s 是复数词素的一个同位异音。

The allomorphs /-s/, /-z/, and /-ɪz/ all represent the plural morpheme. 同位异音 /-s/, /-z/, 和 /-ɪz/ 都代表复数词素。|近义词：variant, variation|反义词: (No direct antonym exists)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek  *ἀλλόμορφος (allómorphos, “having another form”), from ἄλλος (állos, “other”) + μορφή (morphḗ, “form”).
