accelerate: /əkˈseləreɪt/ (US) /əkˈsɛləreɪt/ (UK)| v. | 加速，促进 | To increase the speed of something; to cause something to happen sooner or more quickly. | The car accelerated quickly onto the highway. 汽车很快加速上了高速公路。|  The hot weather accelerated the growth of the plants. 炎热的天气促进了植物的生长。| synonyms: speed up, hasten, quicken, expedite | antonyms: decelerate, slow down, retard | verb

accelerating: /əkˈseləreɪtɪŋ/ (US) /əkˈsɛləreɪtɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. | 加速的，促进的 | Increasing in speed or rate. | The accelerating pace of life can be stressful.  快节奏的生活可能会让人感到压力很大。| The accelerating inflation is a serious concern for the government.  不断加剧的通货膨胀是政府严重关切的问题。| synonyms: increasing, rising, escalating | antonyms: decelerating, decreasing, slowing | adjective

acceleration: /əkˌseləˈreɪʃn/ (US) /əkˌsɛləˈreɪʃn/ (UK)| n. | 加速，加速度 | The rate at which the speed of something increases. | The car's acceleration was impressive. 这辆车的加速度令人印象深刻。|  The acceleration of technological advancement is remarkable. 技术进步的加速度是显著的。| synonyms: speeding up, quickening | antonyms: deceleration, slowing down | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *acceleratus, past participle of accelerare "to hasten," from Latin ad- "to" + celer "swift."
