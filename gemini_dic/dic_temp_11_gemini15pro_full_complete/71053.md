first-hand:/ˌfɜːrstˈhænd/ | adj. adv.| 第一手的，直接的 | Experienced or learned directly, not through another person or source. | I heard the news first-hand from the president. 我直接从总统那里听到了这个消息。|
first-hand:/ˌfɜːrstˈhænd/ | adj. adv.| 第一手的，直接的 | Obtained or acquired directly from the original source. |  She had first-hand experience of poverty. 她对贫困有 firsthand 的经验。 | 近义词: direct, immediate, personal | 反义词: indirect, second-hand, hearsay | 词性: adjective, adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "first" + "hand."  "First" ultimately comes from Proto-Germanic *furistaz, meaning "foremost." "Hand" comes from Old English hand, meaning "hand." The combined term emphasizes receiving something directly, as if from the original source's hand.
