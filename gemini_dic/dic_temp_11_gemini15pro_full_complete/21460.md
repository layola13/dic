ETYMOLOGY->
From the adjective "blowsy" + "-ly" (adverbial suffix). "Blowsy" originates from "blowze," meaning a ruddy-faced woman, particularly a peasant woman, often associated with a coarse or untidy appearance.  It likely relates to the idea of being windblown and disheveled.
