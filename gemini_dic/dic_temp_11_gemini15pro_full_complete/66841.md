expatriates:/ɛkˈspeɪtriəts/ /ɪkˈspeɪtriəts/
|n.|侨民，移居国外者|A person who lives outside their native country.|Many American expatriates live in Europe. 许多美国侨民居住在欧洲。|
expatriates:/ɛkˈspeɪtriəts/ /ɪkˈspeɪtriəts/
|n.|外派人员|A person who has been sent to live and work in a foreign country, usually for a specific period of time, by their employer.|The company offers excellent support for its expatriates. 这家公司为其外派人员提供优厚的支持。|
expatriate:/ɛkˈspeɪtriət/ /ɪkˈspeɪtriət/
|vt.|移居国外，逐出国外|To banish (someone) from their native country.|He was expatriated for his political activities. 他因政治活动而被驱逐出境。|
expatriate:/ɛkˈspeɪtriət/ /ɪkˈspeɪtriət/
|adj.|移居国外的，被逐出国外的|Living outside one's native country.|She enjoys her expatriate life in Paris. 她很享受在巴黎的侨居生活。|
近义词: emigrant, immigrant, exile
反义词: repatriate, native, resident
词性: n., vt., adj.
词源：From Late Latin expatriatus, past participle of expatriare (“to banish, exile”), from ex- (“out of, from”) + patria (“native land”).
