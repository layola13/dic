eglantine: /ˈɛɡlənˌtaɪn/ | n. | 野蔷薇，多花蔷薇 | A Eurasian rose (Rosa eglanteria) with prickly stems, fragrant leaves, pink flowers, and scarlet hips. It is also called sweetbrier. | The air was filled with the sweet scent of eglantine. 空气中弥漫着野蔷薇的甜香。| Near synonyms: sweetbrier, briar rose, wild rose | Antonyms: None (lacks a direct opposite) | noun

eglantine: /ˈɛɡlənˌtaɪn/ | n. | (诗歌用语) 野蔷薇，甜蔷薇，诗歌 |  A poetic term for any wild rose, especially the sweetbrier.  |  The poet described a bower of eglantine. 诗人描绘了一个野蔷薇凉亭。 | Near synonyms: wild rose, briar, rose | Antonyms: None (lacks a direct opposite, especially in a poetic context) | noun
