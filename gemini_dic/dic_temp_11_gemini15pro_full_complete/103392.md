jinked:/dʒɪŋkt/
verb
突然转向；闪避
made a quick, evasive turn
The driver jinked the car to avoid the pothole. 司机猛地转向避开了坑洞。

jinked:/dʒɪŋkt/
adjective
（因突然转向）改变方向的
having made a quick, evasive turn
The jinked movement of the rabbit made it hard to catch.  兔子突然转向的动作让它很难被抓住。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Scots *jink*, probably of imitative origin, suggesting a sudden movement.  Possibly related to Middle English *jinkin*, meaning "to frolic".

SYNONYMS->
dodge, swerve, veer, weave, zigzag

ANTONYMS->
straighten, continue, maintain course

VERB
The hare jinked to the left, leaving the hounds confused. 野兔突然向左一转，让猎犬们不知所措。
The speedboat jinked sharply to avoid a collision. 快艇急转弯以避免碰撞。


ADJECTIVE
The fox's jinked path through the snow made it difficult to track.  狐狸在雪地上蜿蜒曲折的路径使追踪变得困难。
The basketball player's jinked movements helped him evade his defender.  篮球运动员突然的转向动作帮助他躲避了防守队员。
