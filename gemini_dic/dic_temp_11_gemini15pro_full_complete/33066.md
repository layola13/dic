chowry:/ˈtʃaʊri/| n. |蝇拂；拂尘；牦牛尾拂|A ceremonial fan or whisk, often made of yak's tail, used to ward off flies or as a mark of royalty or religious significance.|The priest used a chowry to wave away the flies during the ceremony.  祭司在仪式中用拂尘驱赶苍蝇。|Synonyms: whisk, fan, fly-whisk|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps something like "fly swatter" in a very specific context)|noun

chowry:/ˈtʃaʊri/| n. |牦牛尾；牦牛尾毛|The hair of a yak's tail, often used to make chowries.|The chowry was made of fine yak's tail chowry. 这拂尘是用上好的牦牛尾毛制成的。|Synonyms: yak's tail hair|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|noun
