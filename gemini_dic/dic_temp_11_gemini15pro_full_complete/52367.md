diction:/ˈdɪkʃən/ | n. |措辞，用语；发音 |The choice and use of words and phrases in speech or writing. | Her diction is impeccable. 她的措辞无懈可击。| synonyms: wording, phrasing, vocabulary, language, terminology | antonyms: inarticulateness | noun

diction:/ˈdɪkʃən/ | n. |发音；吐字 | The style of enunciation in speaking or singing. | The actor's diction was so clear that everyone could understand every word.  这位演员的吐字清晰，每个人都能听懂每一个字。| synonyms: articulation, enunciation, pronunciation | antonyms: mumbling, slurring | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dictionem (nominative dictio) "a saying, expression, word," from dictus, past participle of dicere "to say, speak," related to dicare "proclaim, dedicate," from PIE root *deik- "to show," also "to pronounce solemnly."
