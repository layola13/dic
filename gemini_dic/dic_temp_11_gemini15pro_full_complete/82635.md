gueber:/ˈɡeɪbər/| n. |盖布尔，祆教徒|A Zoroastrian, especially one living under Muslim rule in Persia.  The term often carried a derogatory connotation.|Example 1:  The guebers maintained their faith despite persecution. 盖布尔们尽管受到迫害，仍然保持着他们的信仰。

Example 2:  Many guebers fled Persia to escape religious intolerance. 许多盖布尔逃离波斯以逃避宗教不容忍。|Synonym: Zoroastrian, fire-worshipper (often derogatory)|Antonym: Muslim (in the historical context of Persia), infidel (derogatory)|noun
