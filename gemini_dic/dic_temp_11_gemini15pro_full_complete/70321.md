field-marshal:/ˈfiːld ˌmɑːrʃəl/| n. |元帅；陆军元帅|A senior military rank, the highest rank in the army in many countries.|Field Marshal Montgomery was a prominent figure in World War II. 蒙哥马利元帅是二战中的杰出人物。|Synonyms: general, commander, chief|Antonyms: private, soldier|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *feldmareschal, from Old French mareschal, from Frankish *marhskalk "horse servant," from *marha- "horse" + *skalk- "servant."  The term originally denoted a high-ranking official in charge of the royal stables, but its meaning evolved over time to designate a senior military commander.
