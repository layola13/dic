antiparticles:/ˈæntiˌpɑːrtiklz/
|n.|反粒子
|A subatomic particle that has the same mass as another particle, but opposite values for some other property or properties, such as electric charge. For example, the antiparticle of the electron is the positron.|
|Electrons and their antiparticles, positrons, annihilate each other when they meet, releasing energy in the form of gamma rays.|电子和它们的互补粒子，正电子，相遇时会相互湮灭，以伽马射线的形式释放能量。

|Scientists are studying antiparticles to understand the fundamental nature of the universe.|科学家们正在研究反粒子以了解宇宙的基本性质。
|Synonym: counterpart|
|Antonym: particle|
|noun|
