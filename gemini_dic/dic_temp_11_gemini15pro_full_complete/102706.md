jangam:/ˈdʒʌŋɡəm/| n. | 圣人；可移动的圣地；（尤指印度教的）苦行僧；|A jangam is a wandering holy man, especially in Hinduism, often considered a living embodiment of the deity.|例句：The jangam blessed the villagers. 圣人为村民祈福。

例句：The procession was led by a jangam carrying a sacred flame.  队伍由一位手持圣火的苦行僧带领。|近义词：sadhu, ascetic, mendicant|反义词: householder (one who lives a settled life)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Kannada ಜಂಗಮ (jaṅgama), from Sanskrit जङ्गम (jaṅgama, “moving, living, animate”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *gʷem- (“to come, go”).
