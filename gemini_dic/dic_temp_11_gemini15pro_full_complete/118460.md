mesenteron:/ˈmɛzənˌtɛrɒn/, /ˈmɛzənˌtɛrɑːn/ | n. | 中肠 | The portion of the alimentary canal of an embryo that gives rise to the small intestine, cecum, colon, rectum, and cloaca.  The central part of the digestive tract, especially of invertebrates, between the stomodaeum and proctodaeum. | The mesenteron of the insect is responsible for most of the digestion and absorption of nutrients. 昆虫的中肠负责大部分营养物质的消化和吸收。 | synonyms: midgut, mid-intestine | antonyms: N/A | noun

mesentery:/ˈmɛzənˌtɛri/ | n. |  肠系膜 | A fold of the peritoneum that attaches the stomach, small intestine, pancreas, spleen, and other organs to the posterior wall of the abdomen. | The surgeon carefully examined the mesentery for any signs of inflammation. 外科医生仔细检查了肠系膜是否有炎症迹象。 | synonyms: peritoneum | antonyms: N/A | noun
