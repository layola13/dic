endorphins:/ɪnˈdɔːrfɪnz/ | n. | 内啡肽 | Any of a group of hormones secreted within the brain and nervous system and having a number of physiological functions. They are peptides which activate the body's opiate receptors, causing an analgesic effect. |  Exercise releases endorphins, which have mood-boosting effects. 运动会释放内啡肽，具有改善情绪的作用。| synonyms:  opioids, peptides | antonyms: None (Endorphins don't have direct antonyms, but their effects can be countered by things that increase pain or worsen mood.) | noun
