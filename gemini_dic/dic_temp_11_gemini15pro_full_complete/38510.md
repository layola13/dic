conciliation:/kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃn/
n.|和解，调解，安抚|The process of ending a disagreement and restoring a friendly relationship.  The union and management reached a conciliation after a long strike.|例句：Conciliation between the warring factions seemed impossible. 交战各派之间的和解似乎是不可能的。

例句：The government appointed a judge to act as a conciliation in the labor dispute. 政府任命了一名法官在劳资纠纷中担任调解人。
|近义词：reconciliation, mediation, appeasement, compromise|反义词：conflict, discord, antagonism|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin conciliatio, from conciliare "to bring together, win over," from concilium "council, assembly," from com- "together" + calare "to call, summon."
