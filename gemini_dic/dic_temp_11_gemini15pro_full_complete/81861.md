gridlock: /ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk/ (US) /ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk/ (UK) | n. | 交通瘫痪，僵局 | a situation where streets are so full of traffic that nothing can move |  The snowstorm caused gridlock throughout the city.  暴风雪导致整个城市交通瘫痪。 | synonyms: standstill, deadlock, impasse | antonyms: flow, movement | noun


gridlock: /ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk/ (US) /ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk/ (UK) | vt. | 使陷入瘫痪，使停顿 | to cause a traffic jam or standstill; to bring to a standstill | The accident gridlocked traffic for hours. 事故导致交通瘫痪数小时。 | synonyms: paralyze, immobilize | antonyms: expedite, facilitate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From "grid" (referring to the street grid) + "lock" (meaning a blockage or standstill).  First recorded use in the 1960s in the United States.
