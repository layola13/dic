hari-kari:/ˌhærɪˈkɑːri/ |n.|切腹自杀|Ritual suicide by disembowelment, formerly practiced by samurai in Japan.  It was considered an honorable way to die rather than be captured by the enemy or to avoid disgrace.|He committed hari-kari rather than face dishonor.|Synonyms: Seppuku (more accurate term), ritual suicide|Antonyms: Survival, life|Noun
