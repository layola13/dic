oakum: /ˈəʊkəm/ | n. | 麻絮；填絮；旧绳拆成的纤维 | Loose fibre obtained by untwisting and picking apart old ropes, used especially in caulking ships. |  The ship's carpenter used oakum to caulk the seams of the deck.  船上的木匠用麻絮填塞甲板的缝隙。 |  Example 2:  Oakum was once a common material for stuffing mattresses.  麻絮曾经是填充床垫的常用材料。 | 近义词: caulking, filler | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "sealant" in some contexts if referring to its function) | 词性: noun

词源: From Middle English *okome, from Old English ācumba (“tow”), equivalent to Old English ā (“away”) + Old English camb (“comb”). Cognate with Old High German āchumbi (“tow”).
