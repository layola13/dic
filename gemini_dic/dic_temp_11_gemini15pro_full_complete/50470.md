depressant:/dɪˈprɛsənt/| n. adj.|抑制剂，镇静剂；令人沮丧的|A depressant is a drug that slows down the central nervous system.  As an adjective, it describes something that lowers spirits or activity.|Example: Alcohol is a depressant. 酒精是一种抑制剂。|Synonyms: sedative, tranquilizer, downer|Antonyms: stimulant, upper|Noun, Adjective

depressant:/dɪˈprɛsənt/| n. adj.|抑郁的，沮丧的|Something that is depressant causes feelings of sadness or hopelessness.|Example: The news was very depressant. 这个消息令人非常沮丧。|Synonyms: disheartening, discouraging, depressing|Antonyms: uplifting, encouraging, cheering|Noun, Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *depressant-, present participle stem of depressare "to press down," from Latin de- "down" + premere "to press."
