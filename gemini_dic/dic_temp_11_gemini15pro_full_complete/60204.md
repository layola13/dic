effloraison:/ˌɛflɔːˈreɪsən/
n.|风化，开花，（酒的）醇化|The process by which a substance loses water of crystallization and crumbles into a powder; the formation of a powdery surface on a substance; the blossoming of flowers.|The effloraison on the surface of the rocks indicated they had been exposed to the elements for a long time. 岩石表面的风化表明它们已经暴露在自然环境中很长时间了。|近义词: blooming, flowering, weathering, disintegration|反义词: deterioration, decay|词性:名词

effloraison:/ˌɛflɔːˈreɪsən/
n.|（比喻）发展，繁荣|A state of flourishing or unfolding, like the blossoming of a flower.|The Renaissance marked a period of effloraison in art and literature. 文艺复兴标志着艺术和文学的繁荣时期。|近义词: blossoming, flourishing, development, growth|反义词: decline, stagnation|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *effloraison*, from *effleurir* "to blossom," from Latin *efflorescere* "to blossom, bloom," from *ex-* "out" + *florescere* "to begin to blossom," from *flos* "flower."
