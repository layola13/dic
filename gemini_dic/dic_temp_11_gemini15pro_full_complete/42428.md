counterculturalists:/ˈkaʊntərˈkʌltʃərəlɪsts/
n.
反文化者，反主流文化者
People who reject mainstream values and norms, often advocating alternative lifestyles and beliefs.
The counterculturalists of the 1960s challenged traditional societal structures.  20世纪60年代的反文化者挑战了传统的社会结构。
The festival attracted artists, musicians, and counterculturalists from around the world.  这个节日吸引了来自世界各地的艺术家、音乐家和反主流文化者。
近义词:nonconformists, rebels, dissidents, bohemians
反义词:conformists, traditionalists
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
counter- (prefix): against, opposite (from Old French *contre-, from Latin contra "against")
culture (noun): the customs, arts, social institutions, and achievements of a particular nation, people, or time (from Latin cultura "cultivation, agriculture," figuratively "care, culture, training")
-al (suffix): relating to, characterized by
-ist (suffix): a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system
