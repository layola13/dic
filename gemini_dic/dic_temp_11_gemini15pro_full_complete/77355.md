gaudy: /ˈɡɔːdi/ | adj. | 华而不实的，俗丽的 | Extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless. |  She wore a gaudy dress to the party. 她穿了一件俗气的连衣裙去参加聚会。|  The decorations were a bit too gaudy for my taste.  这些装饰对我来说有点太俗气了。 | 近义词:garish, flashy, ostentatious, flamboyant | 反义词: understated, subtle, elegant, refined | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From Middle English *gaude, from Old North French gaude (“showy ornament, trinket”), from Late Latin *galda, probably of Germanic origin.
