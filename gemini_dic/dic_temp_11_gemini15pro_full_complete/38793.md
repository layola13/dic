conductively:/kənˈdʌktɪvli/
|adv.|传导地，导电地|In a way that allows heat or electricity to pass through easily.|The material is conductively efficient. 这种材料导电效率高。|Synonym: efficiently, readily|Antonym: poorly, inefficiently|词性:副词

conductive:/kənˈdʌktɪv/
|adj.|传导的，导电的|Having the property of transmitting heat or electricity.|Copper is a highly conductive metal. 铜是高度导电的金属。|Synonym: transmitting, conducting|Antonym: nonconductive, insulating|词性:形容词

conduct:/kənˈdʌkt/
|n.|行为，举止；管理，实施|The manner in which a person behaves, especially on a particular occasion or in a particular context.  The process of organizing and carrying out a particular activity.|His conduct was unacceptable. 他的行为是不可接受的。  The conduct of the experiment was flawless. 实验的进行完美无瑕。|Synonym: behavior, demeanor, management|Antonym: mismanagement|词性:名词

conduct:/kənˈdʌkt/
|v.|引导，带领；指挥；传导|To lead or guide someone to a particular place. To direct the performance of musicians or singers. To transmit heat, electricity, or sound.|He conducted the tourists through the museum. 他带领游客参观博物馆。 She conducted the orchestra with great skill. 她以高超的技巧指挥乐队。  Copper conducts electricity well. 铜导电性能好。|Synonym: lead, guide, direct, transmit|Antonym: follow, obstruct|词性:动词


词源:  From Late Middle English *conductif, from Late Latin conductivus, from Latin conductus (“leading, conducting”), past participle of conducere (“to lead or bring together”).
