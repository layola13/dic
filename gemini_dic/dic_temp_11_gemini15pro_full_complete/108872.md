lectern: /ˈlɛktərn/ (US), /ˈlektən/ (UK)| n. |讲台，诵经台|A stand with a sloping top, used to support a book or script when reading aloud, especially in a church or lecture hall.|The speaker approached the lectern and began her address. 演讲者走到讲台前开始讲话。|
近义词: podium, rostrum, pulpit|
反义词: None (no direct opposite)|
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *lectrin, from Medieval Latin lectrinum "reading desk," from Latin lectura "reading," from legere "to read."
