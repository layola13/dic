faction:/ˈfækʃən/| n. |派系，小集团，内讧 |A dissenting group within a larger one, especially in politics.|  The party split into warring factions.  该党分裂成互相争斗的派系。 |synonyms: clique, group, cabal, bloc, wing, contingent, section, sector | antonyms: unity, whole, entirety | noun

faction:/ˈfækʃən/| n. |(小说、戏剧、电影中的)派别 | A group or clique within a larger organization.| The movie portrays two rival factions battling for control of the city. 这部电影描绘了两个敌对派系争夺城市控制权。|synonyms: side, camp, coalition, alliance | antonyms:  individual, loner | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *faccion*  (modern French *faction*), from Latin *factio* "a doing, making, performing, a deed, act, political party, faction," from *factus*, past participle of *facere* "to do, make."
