marguerite:/ˌmɑːrɡəˈriːt/ /ˌmɑːrɡəˈriːt/| n. |雏菊，玛格丽特（女子名）|A common name for several composite plants, especially the oxeye daisy, *Leucanthemum vulgare*, having white ray flowers surrounding a yellow disk.|She wore a crown of marguerites. 她戴着雏菊花冠。|近义词：daisy, oxeye daisy|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *marguerite*, from Late Latin *margarita*, from Latin *margarita* ("pearl"), from Ancient Greek *μαργαρῖτης* (*margarítēs*, “pearl”).  The flower was named for its resemblance to a pearl.
