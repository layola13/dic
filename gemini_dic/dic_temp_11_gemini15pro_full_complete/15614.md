bailiff: /ˈbeɪlɪf/| n.|法警；执行官；地主代理人|A bailiff is an officer of the court who is employed to execute writs and processes and make arrests, seizures, etc.|The bailiff evicted the tenants for non-payment of rent. 法警因租户未缴纳租金而将其驱逐。|The bailiff seized the debtor's property. 法警扣押了债务人的财产。|Synonyms: officer, sheriff, marshal|Antonyms: defendant, accused|noun

bailiwick: /ˈbeɪlɪwɪk/| n.|管辖范围；职责范围|A bailiwick is a person's area of skill, knowledge, authority, or work.|Financial planning isn't really my bailiwick. 财务规划并非我的专长。|The maintenance of public order is the bailiwick of the police. 维护公共秩序是警察的职责。|Synonyms: jurisdiction, domain, province, sphere, purview|Antonyms: N/A|noun

注意事项:

词源：bailiff 来自古法语 *baillif,  "administrator, custodian, guardian," 源自晚期拉丁语 *bajulivus "agent, officer,"  来自拉丁语 bajulus "porter, carrier."  bailiwick 来自 bailiff + -wick (古英语 -wic "dwelling, village, place").
