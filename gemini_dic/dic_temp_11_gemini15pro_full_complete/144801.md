over-garrisoning:/ˌoʊvərˈɡærɪsənɪŋ/
|n.|过度驻军，驻军过多|The act of stationing more troops in a location than is necessary or strategically sound.|The over-garrisoning of the small island proved to be a logistical nightmare, straining resources and morale.  过度驻扎在这个小岛上被证明是一场后勤噩梦，消耗了资源，也打击了士气。|Example:  The over-garrisoning of the area led to supply shortages. 该地区的过度驻军导致了物资短缺。|Synonym: overdeployment, excessive deployment|Antonym: under-garrisoning, insufficient garrison|noun

over-garrison:/ˌoʊvərˈɡærɪsən/
|v.|过度驻军|To station more troops in a location than necessary.|The general was criticized for over-garrisoning the border town. 将军因在边境城镇过度驻军而受到批评。|Example: They over-garrisoned the fort, stretching their supplies thin. 他们在这个要塞过度驻军，导致物资供应紧张。|Synonym: overdeploy|Antonym: under-garrison|verb
