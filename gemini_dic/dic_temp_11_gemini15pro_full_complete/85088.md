hardballed:/ˈhɑːrdbɔːld/ | v. | 采取强硬手段；(棒球)投快球 | to act aggressively and ruthlessly, especially in politics or business (often related to negotiations).  Originally derived from baseball, where a "hardball" is a fastball. |  They hardballed him into accepting the deal. 他们用强硬手段迫使他接受了这笔交易。| synonyms: pressured, coerced, intimidated, strong-armed | antonyms: compromised, negotiated, cooperated | verb

hardball:/ˈhɑːrdbɔːl/ | n. | 强硬态度；强硬手段；(棒球)快球 | aggressive and ruthless tactics, especially in politics or business. Also, a fastball in baseball. |  The union played hardball during the contract negotiations. 工会在合同谈判中采取了强硬态度。| synonyms:  tough tactics, aggressive negotiations, pressure tactics | antonyms: compromise, cooperation, negotiation | noun

词性: 以上两种词性 (动词和名词) 都存在，但动词 hardballed 更为常用。

词源:  From "hardball" (baseball), referring to a fastball, extended metaphorically to aggressive tactics.
