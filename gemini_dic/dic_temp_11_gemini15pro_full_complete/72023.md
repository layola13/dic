flip-flopping:/ˈflɪpˌflɑpɪŋ/ | n. |反复无常，出尔反尔|The act of changing one's opinion or position frequently, especially on an important issue. |The politician's flip-flopping on the issue cost him the election. 这位政治家在这个问题上的反复无常让他输掉了选举。|近义词：vacillation, wavering, indecision |反义词：consistency, steadfastness |词性:名词

flip-flop:/ˈflɪpˌflɑp/ | v. |改变主意，立场摇摆|To change one's opinion or position, especially frequently. |He flip-flopped on the issue several times. 他在这个问题上多次改变立场。|近义词：vacillate, waver |反义词：remain constant, stay firm |词性:动词

词源:  From the sound of a flip-flop sandal.  拟声词，源自人字拖的声音。
