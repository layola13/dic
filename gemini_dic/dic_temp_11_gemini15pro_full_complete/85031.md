harbingered:/ˈhɑːrbɪndʒərd/| v.|预示，预兆|To have been a sign that something was going to happen.|The dark clouds harbingered a storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨。|近义词：foreshadowed, presaged, portended|反义词:concealed, hid|词性:动词

词源: From Middle English *herberger, from Old French herbergier (“to provide lodging”), from Frankish *heriberga (“army shelter”), from *heri (“army”) + *berga (“protection”). The semantic shift to "herald" or "forerunner" occurred in the 16th century.
