metaphysically:/ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkli/| adv.|形而上学地，抽象地|In a way that relates to metaphysics; abstractly; in a way that transcends physical matter or the laws of nature.  Relating to the branch of philosophy that deals with the first principles of things, including abstract concepts such as being, knowing, substance, cause, identity, time, and space.|He spoke metaphysically about the nature of reality.|近义词：abstractly, philosophically, theoretically|反义词：physically, concretely|词性:副词
Etymology: From Late Latin metaphysica (neuter plural) "metaphysics" + -al + -ly.  The Late Latin term is a shortening of the Greek phrase (tà metà tà physiká biblía) "(the books) after the Physics," referring to Aristotle's treatise.
