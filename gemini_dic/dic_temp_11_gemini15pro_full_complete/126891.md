narcissist: /ˈnɑːrsɪsɪst/ (US) /ˈnɑːsɪsɪst/ (UK)| n.|自恋者，自负的人|A person who has an excessive admiration of themselves.  A narcissist is characterized by an inflated sense of self-importance, a need for attention and admiration, and a lack of empathy for others.|Example: He's such a narcissist; he spends hours staring at himself in the mirror. 他真是个自恋狂，他对着镜子照好几个小时。

Example:  She's a classic narcissist – always talking about herself and her accomplishments. 她是个典型的自恋者，总是谈论自己和自己的成就。|近义词：egotist, self-admirer, braggart|反义词:  humble person, altruist|词性: noun


narcissistic: /ˌnɑːrsɪˈsɪstɪk/ (US) /ˌnɑːsɪˈsɪstɪk/ (UK)| adj.|自恋的，自负的|Having or showing characteristics of excessive self-love or admiration.|Example: His narcissistic personality made it difficult to work with him. 他自恋的性格让他很难与人共事。

Example:  She posted a narcissistic selfie, pouting at the camera. 她上传了一张自恋的自拍照，对着镜头噘嘴。|近义词：egotistical, self-centered, vain|反义词: humble, modest, selfless|词性: adjective



ETYMOLOGY->
From Narcissus, a character in Greek mythology who fell in love with his own reflection.  The term entered English in the late 19th century.
