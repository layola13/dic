discount:/ˈdɪskaʊnt/ (US), /ˈdɪskaʊnt/ (UK)| n. vt.|折扣，打折|A reduction in the usual price of something.|The store offered a 10% discount on all purchases.  这家商店所有商品都打九折。|近义词：reduction, rebate, allowance|反义词:premium, surcharge|noun

discount:/ˈdɪskaʊnt/ (US), /ˈdɪskaʊnt/ (UK)| vt.|打折，不予考虑|Regard (a possibility, fact, or feeling) as being unworthy of consideration.|The police discounted the possibility of suicide. 警方排除了自杀的可能性。|近义词：disregard, ignore, minimize|反义词:value, emphasize|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desconter*, from des- (expressing reversal) + compter "to count," from Latin computare "to count, sum up."
