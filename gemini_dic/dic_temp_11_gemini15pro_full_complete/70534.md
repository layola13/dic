filibustering:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstərɪŋ/| n.|冗长演说；阻挠议事|The senator's filibustering prevented the bill from being voted on.  这位参议员的冗长演说阻止了该法案的投票。|例句：The opposition party used filibustering to delay the passage of the new law. 反对党利用冗长辩论来拖延新法律的通过。|近义词：stonewalling, delaying tactics|反义词:cooperation, expediting|noun


filibuster:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstər/| n.|阻挠议事的议员|The filibuster finally yielded the floor after speaking for twelve hours.  这名阻挠议事的议员在发言十二小时后终于放弃了发言权。|例句：The senator was known as a master of the filibuster. 这位参议员以精通阻挠议事而闻名。|近义词：obstructionist|反义词:supporter|noun

filibuster:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstər/| v.|以冗长演说阻挠议事|The minority party filibustered the bill to death. 少数党用冗长演说扼杀了这项法案。|例句：They plan to filibuster to prevent the bill from coming to a vote.  他们计划以冗长演说来阻止该法案进行投票。|近义词：stonewall, obstruct|反义词:concede, cooperate|verb
