insurrectionism:/ˌɪnsəˈrɛkʃənɪzəm/
n.
暴动主义；起义主义；叛乱主义
The advocacy of or engaging in insurrection against an established government or authority.
He was charged with insurrectionism after the failed coup. 他在政变失败后被指控煽动叛乱。
The government cracked down on insurrectionism with an iron fist. 政府以铁腕手段镇压叛乱。
近义词：rebellion, revolt, uprising, mutiny
反义词：obedience, loyalty, submission
词性：名词

insurrectionist:/ˌɪnsəˈrɛkʃənɪst/
n.
暴动者；起义者；叛乱者
A person who engages in or advocates insurrection.
The insurrectionists stormed the capitol building. 叛乱分子冲进了国会大厦。
The insurrectionist leader was arrested and imprisoned. 叛乱领导人被逮捕并监禁。
近义词：rebel, revolutionary, mutineer
反义词：loyalist, conformist
词性：名词

insurrectionary:/ˌɪnsəˈrɛkʃənɛri/
adj.
暴动的；起义的；叛乱的
Relating to or characteristic of insurrection.
The insurrectionary movement gained momentum quickly. 叛乱运动迅速发展壮大。
The government declared martial law to quell the insurrectionary activity. 政府宣布戒严令以平息叛乱活动。
近义词：rebellious, revolutionary, mutinous
反义词：loyal, obedient, submissive
词性：形容词


词源:  From Late Latin *insurrectio, from Latin insurgere "to rise up," from in- "against" + surgere "to rise."  The "-ism" suffix denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.
