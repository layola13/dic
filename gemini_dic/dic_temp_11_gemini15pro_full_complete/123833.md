moral-philosophies: /ˈmɔːrəl fɪˈlɒsəfiz/ | n. | 道德哲学 | Systems of thought that address fundamental questions about right and wrong, good and bad, virtue and vice.  |  Many different moral-philosophies have influenced Western thought, including utilitarianism and deontology.  许多不同的道德哲学影响了西方思想，包括功利主义和道义论。 | synonyms: ethics, moral systems, moral doctrines | antonyms: immorality, amorality | noun

Etymology:
"moral" comes from the Latin word "moralis," meaning "custom, manner, conduct."
"philosophy" comes from the Greek words "philo" (loving) and "sophia" (wisdom). Thus, "philosophy" literally means "love of wisdom."
