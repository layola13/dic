conventionalist:/kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪst/
n.|因循守旧者; 形式主义者|A person who conforms to accepted standards, especially in art or literature, or who believes that such standards should be strictly followed. |Example: The conventionalist refused to consider any ideas that challenged the established norms.  这个因循守旧者拒绝考虑任何挑战既定规范的想法。

Example:  The art critic dismissed the painter as a mere conventionalist, lacking in originality.  艺术评论家认为这位画家只是一个因循守旧的人，缺乏 originality。|近义词:conformist, traditionalist|反义词:iconoclast, rebel, nonconformist|词性:名词

conventionalist:/kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪst/
adj.|墨守成规的; 形式主义的|Conforming to accepted standards, especially in art or literature. |Example:  Her conventionalist approach to painting resulted in predictable and uninspired landscapes. 她墨守成规的绘画方法导致了毫无新意的风景画。

Example: He gave a rather conventionalist interpretation of the poem, focusing on its formal structure rather than its deeper meaning.  他对这首诗的解释相当墨守成规，专注于它的形式结构而不是更深的含义。|近义词:conformist, traditionalist, orthodox|反义词:unconventional, original, innovative|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From convention + -ist.  "Convention" comes from the Late Latin *conventionem (nominative conventio) "agreement, assembly," from past participle stem of convenire "to come together," from com- "together" + venire "to come."  "-ist" is a suffix forming agent nouns and nouns denoting adherents of a doctrine or system.
