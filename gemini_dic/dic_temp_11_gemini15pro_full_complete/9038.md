antimaterialists:/ˌænti məˈtɪəriəlɪsts/
| n. |反物质主义者|People who oppose materialism, the belief that physical matter is the only or fundamental reality. They often prioritize spiritual, ethical, or intellectual values over material possessions or economic gain.| Many antimaterialists find solace in minimalist lifestyles. 许多反物质主义者在极简的生活方式中找到了慰藉。|近义词：spiritualists, idealists, ascetics|反义词:materialists, consumerists|词性:Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
anti- (prefix): From Greek anti-, meaning "against," "opposite of."
materialist (noun): From Late Latin materialis, meaning "of matter."
-ist (suffix): Forms agent nouns, denoting a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or belief.
