narrowcasting:/ˈnæroʊˌkæstɪŋ/| n.|窄播，定向广播|The transmission of television programs to a comparatively small audience defined by special interest or geographical location.  The practice of disseminating a message to a select audience, often used in marketing and advertising.|Example 1: The company used narrowcasting to target its advertising campaign to specific demographics. 这家公司使用窄播技术将其广告宣传活动定位到特定的人口统计数据。

Example 2: Narrowcasting allows businesses to reach niche markets with tailored messages. 窄播允许企业通过定制信息触及利基市场。|近义词：niche marketing, targeted advertising, micro-marketing|反义词：broadcasting, mass marketing|词性: noun

词源:  "Narrowcasting" is a blend of "narrow" and "broadcasting."  It emerged in the late 20th century as a contrasting term to "broadcasting," which refers to transmitting information to a wide, undifferentiated audience.  "Narrow" emphasizes the focused and specific nature of the target audience.
