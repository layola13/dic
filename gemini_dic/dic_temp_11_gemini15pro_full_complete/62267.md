encyclopaedically: /ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdɪk(ə)li/ /ɪnˌsaɪkloʊˈpiːdɪk(ə)li/ | adv. | 百科全书式地；全面地 | In a manner that resembles an encyclopedia; comprehensively; exhaustively.  | He described the event encyclopaedically, covering every detail. 他百科全书式地描述了这一事件，涵盖了每一个细节。 |  She has an encyclopaedic knowledge of plants. 她对植物的知识非常全面。 | comprehensively, thoroughly, exhaustively | narrowly, superficially | adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From encyclopedia + -ically.  Encyclopedia comes from Modern Latin encyclopaedia, from Greek enkyklios paideia "general education," from enkyklios "circular, recurring" (from en "in" + kyklos "circle") + paideia "education."
