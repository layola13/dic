menial:/ˈmiːniəl/ | adj. n. | 卑微的，仆人的；佣人 |  (of work) not requiring much skill and lacking prestige.  Relating to or characteristic of a servant. |  She found herself stuck in a menial job. 她发现自己被困在一个卑微的工作中。|  He was tired of doing menial tasks around the house. 他厌倦了做家里的琐碎杂事。 | 近义词:lowly, humble, servile, subservient | 反义词:exalted, noble, prestigious | 词性:adjective, noun

menials:/ˈmiːniəlz/ | n. | 佣人，仆人 |  A person with a menial job. | The menials were expected to work long hours for little pay.  佣人们被期望长时间工作而报酬很少。 | The king treated his menials with contempt. 国王轻蔑地对待他的仆人。 | 近义词:servants, domestics, laborers | 反义词:masters, employers, aristocracy | 词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Anglo-Norman *menial, from Old French mesnie, mesnée (“household, retinue”), from Vulgar Latin *mansionata, from Latin mansio (“dwelling, staying”), from manēre (“to stay, remain”).
