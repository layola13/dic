adnominal:/ˌædˈnɒmɪnl/ /ˌædˈnɑːmɪnl/ | adj. |定语的，修饰名词的 |  Placed next to a noun; modifying a noun.  |  Example: The *adnominal* adjective "red" modifies the noun "car" in the phrase "red car".  这句中定语形容词“red”修饰名词“car”，组成了短语“red car”。

 Example:  An *adnominal* phrase, such as "of great importance," can also modify a noun.  像“of great importance”这样的定语短语也可以修饰名词。 | 近义词: attributive | 反义词: N/A | 词性: adjective

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
The adnominal adjective describes the noun's usual state.
定语形容词描述名词的通常状态。

The small dog barks loudly.
这条小狗叫声很大。

He wears a blue shirt every Friday.
他每个星期五都穿蓝色衬衫。


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
The adnominal adjective describes the noun's current state.
定语形容词描述名词的当前状态。

The shining sun is blinding.
闪耀的太阳很刺眼。

The blooming flowers are beautiful.
盛开的花朵很漂亮。


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
The adnominal adjective describes the noun's state resulting from a completed action.
定语形容词描述名词由于完成的动作而造成的状态。

The broken vase needs to be glued.
破碎的花瓶需要粘起来。

The finished painting is stunning.
完成的画作令人惊叹。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
The adnominal adjective describes the noun's state resulting from an ongoing action.
定语形容词描述名词由于正在进行的动作而造成的状态。

不断响起的电话很烦人。

The ever-changing landscape is captivating.
不断变化的景色令人着迷。

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):

定语形容词描述名词在另一个过去动作之前的状态。
