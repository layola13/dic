harangue:/həˈræŋ/ | n. vt.| 长篇大论；高谈阔论；大声训斥 | a long, loud, and angry speech; to speak to someone or a group of people, often criticizing them or telling them what to do in an angry way | The coach harangued the team after their poor performance. 教练在球队表现不佳后对他们进行了严厉的训斥。 |  | lecture, diatribe, tirade | discussion, conversation | noun, verb
harangue:/həˈræŋ/ | v. | 长篇大论；高谈阔论；大声训斥 | to deliver a harangue; to address (a person or crowd) in a vehement or abusive way; to lecture (someone) at length | He harangued us for hours about the evils of modern society. 他连续几个小时高谈阔论现代社会的弊端。 | lecture, rant, scold | discuss, converse | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French "harangue," from Old Italian "arangare" meaning "to speak in public," of Germanic origin. Ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*harihringaz" meaning "army ring" (place where proclamations were made to the army).
