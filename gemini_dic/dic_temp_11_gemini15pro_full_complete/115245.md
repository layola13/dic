maniple:/ˈmænɪpəl/| n. | 古罗马军团中的一个分队，一捆；一束；一把 | A subdivision of a Roman legion, originally one-third of a cohort; a bundle, handful, or bunch. | The centurion commanded a maniple of about 120 men. 百夫长指挥一个约120人的分队。|  The priest picked up a maniple of wheat. 神父拿起了一捆小麦。| synonyms: detachment, unit, squadron, bundle, handful, bunch | antonyms: whole, entirety | noun
