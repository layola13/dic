est:/ɪst/ | adj. | 最...的 (superlative) |  A suffix used to form the superlative degree of adjectives and adverbs, usually after a single syllable or after a word ending in -y, -le, -er, or -ow. |  He is the tallest boy in the class. 他是班里最高的男孩。|  biggest, smallest, fastest | worst, slowest  | adjective
est:/ɪst/ | n. | (古语) 第二人称单数 | Archaic form of the second person singular present tense of "to be". | Thou est the fairest of them all. 你是所有人中最美丽的。(古英语用法，现在不用了) | art |  (No antonym applicable) | noun/pronoun (archaic)

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
He is the tallest in his family. 他是家里最高的。
She is the smartest student in the class. 她是班里最聪明的学生。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
He is being the nicest he can be. 他正表现得尽可能好。 (Note: Superlative with present continuous is less common and usually implies temporary behavior)
She is acting the silliest I've ever seen her. 她表现得是我见过她最傻的样子。

现在完成时 (Present Perfect):

This has been the hardest year of my life. 这是我人生中最艰难的一年。

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):

She was acting the weirdest I'd ever seen. 她表现得是我见过的最奇怪的样子。

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
He had been the fastest runner in school. 他曾经是学校里跑得最快的人。
She had been the most popular girl in her class. 她曾经是她班上最受欢迎的女孩。

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
