observe:/əbˈzɜːrv/ (US) /əbˈzɜːv/ (UK)| v. |观察，注意到，遵守，评论|To watch someone or something carefully, to notice something, to obey a law, rule, or custom, to make a remark.|She observed the children playing in the park. 她观察孩子们在公园里玩耍。|近义词：watch, monitor, notice, comply with, comment|反义词：ignore, disregard, disobey|词性:动词

observer:/əbˈzɜːrvər/ (US) /əbˈzɜːvə(r)/ (UK)| n. |观察员，观察家|A person who watches something carefully, someone who is present at something but is not taking part in it.|The UN sent observers to monitor the elections. 联合国派观察员监督选举。|近义词：spectator, onlooker, witness, monitor|反义词：participant|词性:名词

observable:/əbˈzɜːrvəbl/ (US) /əbˈzɜːvəbl/ (UK)| adj. |可观察的，显著的|Noticeable or able to be seen.|There was an observable improvement in his performance. 他的表现有明显的进步。|近义词：noticeable, apparent, visible, perceptible|反义词：unnoticeable, imperceptible, invisible|词性:形容词
