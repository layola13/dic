frictions:/ˈfrɪkʃənz/| n.|摩擦；冲突；不和|The two countries have had a long history of frictions. 这两个国家长期以来一直存在摩擦。|例句：There are frictions between the two departments.  两个部门之间存在摩擦。

例句：Cultural frictions can arise when people from different backgrounds interact. 当来自不同背景的人们互动时，可能会出现文化摩擦。

近义词：conflict, disagreement, discord, strife, tension|反义词:harmony, agreement, concord, peace|词性:名词

friction:/ˈfrɪkʃən/| n.|摩擦；冲突；不和|The friction between the two surfaces creates heat. 两个表面之间的摩擦会产生热量。|例句：The constant friction between the gears caused the machine to break down. 齿轮之间不断的摩擦导致机器故障。

例句：Applying the brakes creates friction, which slows the car down.  刹车会产生摩擦力，从而使汽车减速。


近义词：abrasion, rubbing, resistance|反义词:smoothness, lubrication|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *frictionem, accusative singular of *frictio (“a rubbing”), from Latin fricare (“to rub”).
