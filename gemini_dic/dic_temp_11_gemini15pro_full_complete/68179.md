fainting:/ˈfeɪntɪŋ/| n.|昏厥，晕倒|A sudden, temporary loss of consciousness.|She suffered a fainting spell due to the heat. 她因为高温而晕倒了。|近义词：syncope, blackout, swoon|反义词:consciousness, alertness|noun

faint:/feɪnt/| adj.|微弱的，模糊的，虚弱的|Lacking brightness, clarity, or strength.|There was a faint smell of smoke in the air. 空气中有一股淡淡的烟味。|近义词：weak, dim, slight|反义词:strong, bright, clear|adjective

faint:/feɪnt/| v.|昏厥，晕倒|To lose consciousness briefly.|He fainted from the pain. 他疼得晕了过去。|近义词：swoon, collapse, pass out|反义词:awaken, revive|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *faintir, from Vulgar Latin *fīnīre, from Latin fīnis "end, limit," from Proto-Italic *dʰgʷʰnis, from Proto-Indo-European *dʰegʷʰ- "to reach, touch." The sense development is from "come to an end" to "lose strength, die," then to "lose consciousness."
