invasionist:/ɪnˈveɪʒənɪst/
n.|侵略主义者；主张侵略的人|A person who advocates or supports invasion, especially military invasion. |The invasionist rhetoric of the politician stirred up strong emotions.  这位政客的侵略主义言论激起了强烈的情绪。|近义词：warmonger, aggressor, hawk|反义词：pacifist, dove|词性:名词

Etymology:
From invasion + -ist.  "Invasion" comes from Late Latin invāsiōn- (stem of invāsiō ), from invādere "to go into, enter, attack," from in- "in, into" + vādere "to go, walk, step."  The suffix "-ist" forms agent nouns and nouns denoting adherents of doctrines, principles, etc.
