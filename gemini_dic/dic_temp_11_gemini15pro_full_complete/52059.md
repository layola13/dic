dialogism:/ˈdaɪəlɒdʒɪzəm/ | n. |对话主义，对话性|A philosophy that emphasizes the importance of dialogue and interaction in understanding meaning and knowledge, often associated with the work of Mikhail Bakhtin.  It highlights the idea that meaning is not inherent in words themselves, but is generated through the interaction between speakers and listeners, writers and readers, within specific social and historical contexts.| Bakhtin's concept of dialogism emphasizes the inherent multiplicity of voices within any utterance. 巴赫金的对话主义概念强调了任何话语中固有的多重声音。

The novel is a prime example of dialogism, with multiple characters and perspectives interacting and shaping the narrative. 这部小说是对话主义的典型例子，多个角色和视角相互作用并塑造叙事。 |近义词：intertextuality, polyphony|反义词：monologism|词性: noun
