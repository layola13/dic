greaser: /ˈɡriːzər/ (US), /ˈɡriːsə/ (UK)| n.|油脂工；涂油者；(尤指20世纪50年代的)飞机头；|A person, typically a young man, who uses a lot of hair grease.  Often used in the 1950s to refer to a specific subculture of working-class youth characterized by greased back hair, leather jackets, and a rebellious attitude. |例句：He was a greaser back in high school, always with his hair slicked back. 他高中时是个飞机头，头发总是梳得油光锃亮。

例句：The greasers hung out at the local diner, listening to rock and roll. 飞机头们聚集在当地的餐馆，听着摇滚乐。|近义词：hoodlum, delinquent, rocker|反义词:square, preppy|词性:noun
