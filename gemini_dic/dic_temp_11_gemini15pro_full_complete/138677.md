nugatoriness:/ˌnjuːɡəˈtɔːrɪnəs/
n.
无价值；琐碎；无用
The quality or state of being nugatory; triflingness; futility.
The nugatoriness of the evidence presented by the defense was apparent to everyone in the courtroom.  辩方提供的证据的无价值性，法庭上的每个人都显而易见。
近义词:futility, triviality, worthlessness, insignificance
反义词:value, worth, significance, importance
词性:noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *nūgātōrius* (“trifling, worthless”), from Latin *nūgae* (“trifles, jests, nonsense”).
