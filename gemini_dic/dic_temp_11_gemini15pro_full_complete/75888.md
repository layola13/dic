fundamentalistic: /ˌfʌndəmɛnˈtəlˈɪstɪk/ | adj. | 原教旨主义的 | Relating to or characteristic of a religious movement or point of view characterized by a return to fundamental principles, usually in a rigid and literal interpretation of sacred texts. |  The politician's views were described as fundamentalistic, adhering strictly to traditional interpretations. 这位政治家的观点被描述为原教旨主义的，严格遵守传统解释。| fundamentalist, orthodox, rigid | liberal, progressive | adjective


fundamentalistic: /ˌfʌndəmɛnˈtəlˈɪstɪk/ | adj. | 基要主义的 |  Adhering strictly to the basic principles of any subject or field. |  His fundamentalistic approach to economics ignored the complexities of the modern market. 他对经济学的基要主义方法忽略了现代市场的复杂性。|  basic, strict, unwavering | flexible, adaptable | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From fundamental + -istic. "Fundamental" traces back to Late Latin "fundamentalis," meaning "foundational," from "fundamentum," meaning "foundation."  The suffix "-istic" forms adjectives meaning "pertaining to" or "characteristic of."
