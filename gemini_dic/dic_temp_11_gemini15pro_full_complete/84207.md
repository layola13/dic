half-rights:/ˌhɑːf ˈraɪts/ |n.| 半吊子权利；不完全的权利；似是而非的权利 |A situation where someone has some, but not all, of the rights or privileges they should have. A claim or entitlement that is only partially valid or recognized.|  He argued that the new law granted only half-rights to the minority group. 他认为新法律只赋予了少数群体不完全的权利。| partial rights, imperfect rights, limited rights | complete rights, full rights | noun
