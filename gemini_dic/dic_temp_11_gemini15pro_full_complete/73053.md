footman:/ˈfʊtmən/| n. |男仆，侍者|A male servant in a grand house, especially one who serves at table and answers the door. |例句：The footman opened the carriage door for her. 男仆为她打开了马车门。

例句：The footman announced the arrival of the guests. 男仆通报了客人的到来。|近义词：valet, servant, attendant |反义词:master, employer|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *fotman*, from Old English *fōtmann* ("footman"), from *fōt* ("foot") + *mann* ("man").  Literally, "a man who goes on foot," referring to a servant who runs errands.
