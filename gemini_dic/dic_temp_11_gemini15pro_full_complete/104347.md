juxtapose:/ˌdʒʌkstəˈpoʊz/ (US) /ˌdʒʌkstəˈpəʊz/ (UK)| vt.|并置，并列，对比|To place two or more things together, side by side, especially for comparison or contrast.|The artist juxtaposed light and shadow in his paintings. 艺术家在他的画作中并置了光线和阴影。|synonyms: compare, contrast, place side by side, set side by side|antonyms: separate, divide, disconnect|verb

juxtaposition:/ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃn/ (US) /ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən/ (UK)| n.|并置，并列，对比|The act of placing two or more things together, side by side, especially for comparison or contrast.|The juxtaposition of these two images creates a striking effect.  这两幅图像的并置营造出一种惊人的效果。|synonyms: comparison, contrast, collocation, apposition|antonyms: separation, division, disconnection|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *juxtapositus*, past participle of *juxtaponere* (to place near to), from juxta (near to) + ponere (to place).
