horn:/hɔːrn/ (UK) /hɔːrn/ (US)| n. |角; 喇叭; 号角|A hard, pointed, often curved part that grows from the head of some animals; a device that makes a loud noise as a warning or signal. |The bull lowered its horns and charged. 公牛 lowered its horns and charged. |The driver honked his horn. 司机按响了他的喇叭。|Synonyms: antler, trumpet, siren, klaxon |Antonyms: silence, quiet|noun

horn:/hɔːrn/ (UK) /hɔːrn/ (US)| v. |按喇叭|To sound a horn. |He horned at the car in front. 他对前面的车按喇叭。|She horned impatiently at the cyclist. 她不耐烦地对骑自行车的人按喇叭。|Synonyms: beep, toot, honk |Antonyms: quiet, silence|verb
