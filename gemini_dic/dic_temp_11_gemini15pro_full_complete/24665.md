bromidiom:/ˈbroʊmɪdiəm/| n. |陈词滥调，老生常谈|A trite, hackneyed, or overused expression or idea, often one that has lost its original impact through overuse. |例句：The politician's speech was full of bromidioms about freedom and democracy.  这位政治家的演讲充满了关于自由和民主的陈词滥调。

例句：Using the bromidiom "time heals all wounds" felt dismissive to her recent grief.  用“时间会治愈一切创伤”这句陈词滥调来形容她最近的悲伤，感觉很不妥当。|近义词：cliché, platitude, commonplace, truism|反义词：originality, novelty, freshness|词性:noun
