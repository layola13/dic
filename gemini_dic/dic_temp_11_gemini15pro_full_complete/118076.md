mending:/ˈmɛndɪŋ/| n. |修补，缝补；改进，改善|the act of repairing something that is broken or damaged|例句：The mending of clothes is a useful skill.  缝补衣服是一项有用的技能。

|例句：The roof needs some mending. 屋顶需要一些修补。|近义词：repairing, fixing, patching|反义词:damaging, breaking|词性:noun

mending:/ˈmɛndɪŋ/| adj. |正在修理的|used to describe something that is being repaired|例句：The mending fence will keep the animals in. 正在修理的篱笆会把动物们围起来。

|例句：She has a pile of mending clothes. 她有一堆要缝补的衣服。|近义词：repairing, being fixed|反义词:broken, damaged|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *menden*, from Old English *mendan* (“to improve, correct, amend, repair”), from Proto-Germanic *mandjaną* (“to improve, adorn, provide with, furnish”), from Proto-Indo-European *mendʰ- (“to improve”). Cognate with Old Frisian *menda* (“to improve, amend”), Middle Dutch *menden* (“to improve, correct, repair”), Old High German *mentan* (“to remember”), German *münden* (“to flow into”).  Related to Latin *mandō* (“I entrust, I command”),  Sanskrit *मन्दते* (*mandate* “rejoices, adorns”).
