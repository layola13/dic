dilapidate:/dɪˈlæpɪdeɪt/
verb|使荒废，使破败|to cause (something, such as a building) to fall into partial ruin or decay especially through neglect or misuse|The old house was dilapidated from years of neglect.  这座老房子由于多年的疏于照顾而破败不堪。|Example: The once grand mansion has been left to dilapidate over the years.  这座曾经宏伟的宅邸多年来一直被任其荒废。|Synonyms:decay, deteriorate, crumble, ruin|Antonyms:restore, renovate, repair|v.

dilapidated:/dɪˈlæpɪdeɪtɪd/
adjective|破旧的，失修的|in a state of disrepair or ruin as a result of age or neglect|The dilapidated barn was barely standing.  破旧的谷仓几乎站不住了。|Example:  The dilapidated building was deemed unsafe for habitation. 这栋破旧的建筑被认为不适合居住。|Synonyms:ramshackle, run-down, derelict, tumbledown|Antonyms:pristine, well-maintained, renovated|adj.
