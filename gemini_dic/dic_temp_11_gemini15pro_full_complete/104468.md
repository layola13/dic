kaiserism:/ˈkaɪzərɪzəm/| n. |军国主义，专制统治|A policy of extending a country's power and influence through diplomacy or military force, specifically associated with the policies of Kaiser Wilhelm II of Germany before World War I.  It often involves aggressive nationalism, militarism, and authoritarianism.|例句：The historian argued that kaiserism was a key factor leading to the outbreak of World War I. 历史学家认为，军国主义是导致第一次世界大战爆发的一个关键因素。

例句：His policies were seen as a form of kaiserism, prioritizing military strength and expansion. 他的政策被视为一种军国主义，优先考虑军事实力和扩张。|近义词：militarism, imperialism, despotism, authoritarianism|反义词：pacifism, democracy, liberalism|词性:名词

词源: From German Kaiser (emperor) + -ism.  Ultimately from Latin Caesar.
