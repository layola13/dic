burnish:/ˈbɜːrnɪʃ/ /ˈbɝːnɪʃ/| v.  n.|抛光，擦亮; 使光亮; 改进，完善|to polish (something, especially metal) by rubbing; enhance or perfect (something, such as a reputation or a skill).|例句：He burnished the silver until it gleamed.  他把银器擦得闪闪发亮。

例句：She worked to burnish her image. 她努力提升自己的形象。|近义词：polish, shine, buff, perfect, refine, improve|反义词：dull, tarnish, dim, blemish|词性：动词; 名词

burnish:/ˈbɜːrnɪʃ/ /ˈbɝːnɪʃ/| n.|光泽，光亮|the shine on a highly polished surface.|例句：The burnish on the antique furniture was remarkable. 这件古董家具的光泽非凡。

例句：He admired the burnish of the newly waxed car. 他欣赏着新打蜡的汽车的光泽。|近义词：luster, sheen, gloss, polish|反义词：dullness|词性：名词
