downstairses: /ˌdaʊnˈstɛərzɪz/ | n. | 楼下，底层（复数，非常用） |  A plural form of "downstairs," referring to multiple lower floors or areas within a building.  It's generally considered non-standard and "downstairs" is typically used for both singular and plural. | Example:  (Generally avoid using this form.  Use "downstairs" instead.)  *He said all the downstairses were flooded. (Better: He said all the downstairs areas were flooded.)  *The downstairses of those old houses are dark and damp. (Better: The downstairs areas/floors of those old houses are dark and damp.) | Synonym: downstairs (preferred) | Antonym: upstairs | Noun
