drone:/droʊn (US), drəʊn (UK)| n. vi. vt. adj.|无人机；雄蜂；嗡嗡声；懒汉|A remote-controlled pilotless aircraft or missile.|例句：The military uses drones for surveillance. 軍方使用無人機進行監視。|例句：Amazon is experimenting with using drones for package delivery. 亞馬遜正在試驗用無人機運送包裹。|近义词：UAV, unmanned aerial vehicle, robot aircraft|反义词:manned aircraft|noun

drone:/droʊn (US), drəʊn (UK)| vi. vt.|发出嗡嗡声；懒散地生活|To make a continuous low humming sound.|例句：The bees droned lazily in the summer heat. 蜜蜂在夏日的炎熱中懶洋洋地嗡嗡叫。|例句：He droned on and on about his problems. 他不停地絮叨著他的問題。|近义词：hum, buzz, murmur|反义词:be active, be energetic|verb

drone:/droʊn (US), drəʊn (UK)| adj.|单调的；嗡嗡作响的|Monotonous, boring.|例句：The drone of the traffic lulled me to sleep. 單調的交通噪音使我昏昏欲睡。|例句：His drone of a voice made it hard to concentrate. 他單調的聲音讓人難以集中注意力。|近义词：monotonous, tedious, dull|反义词:exciting, interesting|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *drān, from Proto-Germanic *drōną (“drone bee”). Cognate with Middle Dutch drone (“drone bee”), Old High German treno (“bumblebee”), Old Norse drǫ́n (“male bee”).
