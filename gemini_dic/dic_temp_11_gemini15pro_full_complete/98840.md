intelligibility:/ɪnˌtɛlɪdʒəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可理解性，清晰度 | The quality of being easy to understand. |  The speaker's intelligibility was poor due to the noisy environment. 演讲者的声音因为嘈杂的环境而难以听清。 | clarity, comprehensibility, understandability | unintelligibility, incomprehensibility | noun


intelligible:/ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəbl/ | adj. | 可理解的，清晰的 |  Able to be understood; comprehensible. | Her explanation was clear and intelligible. 她的解释清晰易懂。 | clear, understandable, comprehensible | unintelligible, incomprehensible, obscure | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English *intelligible*, from Old French *intelligible*, from Latin *intelligibilis*, from *intelligere* "to understand," from *inter-* "between" + *legere* "to choose, gather, read."
