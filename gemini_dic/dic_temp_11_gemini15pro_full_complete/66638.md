existentialist:/ˌɛɡzɪˈstɛnʃəlɪst/ | n. adj. | 存在主义者；存在主义的 |  Relating to existentialism, a philosophical theory which emphasizes the existence of the individual as a free and responsible agent determining their own development through acts of will.  |  Example: Jean-Paul Sartre was a famous existentialist writer.  让-保罗·萨特是一位著名的存在主义作家。

| Example:  She took an existentialist approach to life, believing she was solely responsible for her own destiny. 她对生活采取了存在主义的态度，相信她要对自己的命运负全责。 |  Synonyms:  philosopher (as noun), existential (as adjective) | Antonyms:  conformist, determinist (as noun), conventional (as adjective) | Noun, Adjective


existentialism:/ˌɛɡzɪˈstɛnʃəlɪzəm/ | n. | 存在主义 | A philosophy that emphasizes individual existence, freedom, and responsibility.  |  Example: Existentialism was a popular philosophy in post-war France. 存在主义是战后法国的流行哲学。

| Example:  He studied existentialism in his philosophy class. 他在哲学课上学习了存在主义。 |  Synonyms:  absurdism, nihilism (partially) | Antonyms:  essentialism, determinism | Noun
