humanism:/ˈhjuːmənɪzəm/| n.|人文主义；人道主义；人本主义|A philosophical and ethical stance that emphasizes the value and agency of human beings, individually and collectively, and generally prefers critical thinking and evidence (rationalism and empiricism) over acceptance of dogma or superstition.|例句：Renaissance humanism emphasized the study of classical literature. 文艺复兴时期的人文主义强调对古典文学的研究。

例句：Her teaching reflected a deep humanism and concern for her students. 她的教学反映了深厚的人文主义情怀和对学生的关爱。|近义词：humanitarianism, anthropocentrism|反义词：divinity, supernaturalism|词性:noun

词源:
Late Middle English: from French humanisme, from late Latin humanitas ‘humanity’, from humanus (see human). The term was originally applied to the study of the humanities, and later to a system of thought attaching prime importance to human rather than divine or supernatural matters.
