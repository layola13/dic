counterbalance:/ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns/ /ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns/| n. v. |平衡，抵消|An act that holds something else in check by exerting an opposite force or effect.|The government's spending on infrastructure projects will hopefully counterbalance the effects of the recession. 政府在基础设施项目上的支出有望抵消经济衰退的影响。|synonyms: offset, compensate, neutralize|antonyms: enhance, amplify, aggravate|noun

counterbalance:/ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns/ /ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns/| v. |平衡，抵消|To oppose with an equal force.|He counterbalanced the weight on one side of the scale by adding more weight to the other side. 他通过在秤的另一侧增加更多重量来平衡一侧的重量。|synonyms: offset, compensate, neutralize|antonyms: enhance, amplify, aggravate|verb
