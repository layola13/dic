chortling: /ˈtʃɔːrtlɪŋ/ (US) /ˈtʃɔːtlɪŋ/ (UK)
n.|轻声欢笑，咯咯笑|A gleeful chuckle or snorting laughter.
The old man sat chortling to himself as he read the comics.|老人读着漫画，自顾自地轻声笑着。
近义词: chuckling, giggling, snickering
反义词: sobbing, crying, wailing
词性:  noun (present participle of chortle)

chortle: /ˈtʃɔːrtl/ (US) /ˈtʃɔːtl/ (UK)
v.|轻声欢笑，咯咯笑|To chuckle gleefully, often with a snorting sound.
He chortled with delight at his grandson's antics.|他孙子的滑稽动作让他高兴地咯咯笑了起来。
近义词: chuckle, giggle, snicker
反义词: sob, cry, wail
词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
Coined by Lewis Carroll in "Through the Looking-Glass" (1871), a blend of "chuckle" and "snort."
