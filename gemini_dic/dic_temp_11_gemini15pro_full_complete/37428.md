comminatory:/ˈkɒmɪnətəri/ (US) /kəˈmɪnətɔːri/ (UK)| adj.|恐吓的，威胁的|Expressing or conveying a threat.|The king issued a comminatory decree against the rebels. 国王发布了针对叛军的恐吓法令。|例句：Her comminatory tone made him uneasy. 她威胁的语气让他感到不安。|近义词：threatening, menacing, intimidatory|反义词：soothing, calming, reassuring|词性: adjective

commination:/ˌkɒmɪˈneɪʃn/ (US) /ˌkɒmɪˈneɪʃən/ (UK)| n.|恐吓，威胁，咒逐|The action of threatening divine vengeance.|The priest pronounced a commination against those who violated the sacred oaths.  神父对那些违背神圣誓言的人宣布了咒逐。|例句：His words were less a promise than a commination. 他的话与其说是承诺，不如说是威胁。|近义词：threat, menace, denunciation|反义词：blessing, benediction, praise|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *comminātōrius*, from Latin comminārī (“to threaten”), from com- (“together”) + minārī (“to threaten”).
