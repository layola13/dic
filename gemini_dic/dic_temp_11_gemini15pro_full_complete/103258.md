jewelweed:/ˈdʒuːəlˌwiːd/ | n. |凤仙花属植物；宝石草| A North American plant with watery stems and seed pods that burst open when touched, also known as touch-me-not. |  Jewelweed grows in damp places. 凤仙花生长在潮湿的地方。|  The juice from jewelweed can be used to soothe poison ivy rash. 宝石草的汁液可以用来舒缓毒葛引起的皮疹。 | synonyms: touch-me-not, spotted touch-me-not, orange balsam, wild balsam | antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "jewel" + "weed".  "Jewel" refers to the glistening appearance of water droplets on the leaves. "Weed" indicates its prolific growth habit.
