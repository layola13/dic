hagiarchy: /ˈhæɡiˌɑːrki/ | n. | 圣徒统治；圣人统治；圣贤统治 | A government of saints or holy persons; a system of government in which the ruler is believed to be divinely guided. |  The small island nation claimed to be a hagiarchy, ruled by a council of priests who interpreted the will of the gods.  这个小岛国家声称是一个圣徒统治的国家，由一个解释神意的祭司委员会统治。

The author envisioned a future hagiarchy where moral leaders guided society. 作者设想了一个未来的圣贤统治，由道德领袖引导社会。 |  hierocracy, theocracy | autocracy, secularism | noun
