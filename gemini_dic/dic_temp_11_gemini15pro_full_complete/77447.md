gawn:/ɡɔːn/| n. |凝视，注视| a foolish or rustic stare; an awkward, foolish person.  A prolonged open-mouthed stare, typically suggesting amazement,  bewilderment, or admiration.|He stood there, gawking at the accident scene.|近义词：stare, gaze, ogle|反义词: glance, glimpse|词性:noun

gawn:/ɡɔːn/| v. |呆呆地看，注视|To stare stupidly; to gape.|The tourists gawked at the ancient ruins.|近义词：stare, gape, gawk|反义词: glance, ignore|词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gawne, *gaune, of uncertain origin. Perhaps from Old Norse gauna (“to gape, yawn”), or from a frequentative form of Old English gēan (“to yawn”).
