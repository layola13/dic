antonomasy:/ænˌtɒnəˈmeɪzi/ /ænˌtɑːnəˈmeɪzi/ | n. |换称，代称|The substitution of a title, epithet, or descriptive phrase for a proper name, or the use of a proper name to designate a member of a class.  Replacing a name with a title or other descriptive phrase.|例句：Calling a king “His Majesty” is an example of antonomasy.  将国王称为“陛下”是换称的一个例子。

例句：Shakespeare is often called "The Bard."  莎士比亚常被称为“诗圣”。|近义词：metonymy, synecdoche |反义词: N/A |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *antonomasia*, from Ancient Greek ἀντονομασία (antonomasíā, “naming instead, change of name”), from ἀντί (antí, “instead of, against”) + ὀνομασία (onomasía, “naming”), from ὀνομάζω (onomázō, “I name, call by name”), from ὄνομα (ónoma, “name”).
