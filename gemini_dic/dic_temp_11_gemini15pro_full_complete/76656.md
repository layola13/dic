galumphing:/ɡəˈlʌmfɪŋ/ | adj. | 笨拙地行走；欢快地蹦跳 | Moving in a clumsy, heavy-footed way; often with a suggestion of boisterous good humor. | The children were galumphing around the garden. 孩子们在花园里蹦蹦跳跳。 |  The elephant galumphed across the field. 大象笨拙地走过田野。| synonyms: lumbering, stomping, cavorting, romping | antonyms: tiptoeing, creeping, gliding | verb/adjective (present participle)

ETYMOLOGY->
Coined by Lewis Carroll in "Through the Looking-Glass" (1871), likely a blend of "gallop" and "triumph," perhaps influenced by "umpire" (suggesting a heavy or decisive tread).
