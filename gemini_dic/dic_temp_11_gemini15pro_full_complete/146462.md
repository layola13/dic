overfrugal:/ˌoʊvərˈfruːɡəl/
adjective
过度节俭的
Excessively frugal; too economical.  Characterized by extreme economy or thrift, sometimes to the point of being stingy.
He was so overfrugal that he refused to buy new clothes, even when his old ones were falling apart. 他过度节俭，即使旧衣服都破烂了也拒绝买新衣服。
My overfrugal grandfather reused tea bags until they were practically flavorless. 我过度节俭的祖父反复使用茶包，直到它们几乎没有味道。
近义词: parsimonious, stingy, miserly, penny-pinching
反义词: extravagant, prodigal, spendthrift, lavish
词性: adjective

词源:
From "over-" (excessively) + "frugal" (economical).  "Frugal" comes from Latin "frugalis" (thrifty, economical), from "frux" (fruit, produce, profit).  The prefix "over-" emphasizes the excessive nature of the frugality.
