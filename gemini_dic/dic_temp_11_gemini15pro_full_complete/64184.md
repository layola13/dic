equerry: /ˈekwəri/ (US) /ˈɛkwəri/ (UK)| n.| 马夫；侍从武官| An officer of a royal or noble household, charged with the care of horses.  An attendant or officer. |The Queen's equerry rode beside her carriage.|Synonyms: groom, attendant, aide, squire|Antonyms: master, lord, superior|noun

equerry: /ˈekwəri/ (US) /ˈɛkwəri/ (UK)| n.| (古)马厩；养马场|A stable for horses; a place where horses are kept. (Archaic usage)|The King's equerry was vast and housed hundreds of horses. (Archaic Usage)|Synonyms: stable, mews|Antonyms: N/A|noun
