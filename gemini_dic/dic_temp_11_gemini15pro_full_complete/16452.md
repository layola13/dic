barel:/ˈbærəl/ | n. | 桶；枪管；炮管；一桶的量 |A cylindrical container bulging out in the middle, traditionally made of wooden staves bound by hoops and having a flat top and bottom.  A unit of liquid capacity equal in the US to 31.5 gallons (for most liquids) and in Britain to 36 imperial gallons (for beer) or 42 gallons (for oil). The metal tube of a gun through which the projectile is fired. |例句：The barrel of oil spilled onto the road.  一桶石油洒到了路上。

例句：He cleaned the barrel of his shotgun. 他擦拭了他的猎枪的枪管。 |近义词：cask, keg, drum, cylinder, tube |反义词:  (No direct antonym for a physical object like a barrel. Could consider "containerless" in certain contexts.) |词性:noun
