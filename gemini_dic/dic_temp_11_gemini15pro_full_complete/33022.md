chortler:/ˈtʃɔːrtlər/ | n. | 轻声欢笑者；咯咯笑的人 |  A person who chortles (laughs in a gleeful, chuckling way). | He was a jovial chortler, always ready with a hearty laugh. 他是一个快乐的轻声欢笑者，总是准备好发出爽朗的笑声。|  giggler, chuckler |  mourner, complainer | noun

Etymology: Coined by Lewis Carroll in his book "Through the Looking-Glass, and What Alice Found There" (1871), likely a blend of "chuckle" and "snort."
