gild: /ɡɪld/ | v. | 镀金，给...涂上金色，使...显得光彩夺目，美化，虚饰 | to cover something with a thin layer of gold or something that looks like gold; to give something a deceptively attractive or valuable appearance |  例句：They gilded the frame of the mirror. 他们给镜框镀了金。

例句：He tried to gild the unpleasant truth with a joke. 他试图用笑话来掩盖令人不快的真相。| 近义词：coat, adorn, embellish, varnish | 反义词：tarnish, dull, strip | 词性：动词

gild: /ɡɪld/ | n. | 行会，同业公会 | a medieval association of craftsmen or merchants, often having considerable power. | 例句：The goldsmiths' gild was very powerful in the city. 金匠行会在该市很有势力。

例句：He belonged to the carpenters' gild. 他属于木匠行会。 | 近义词：guild, association, union, society | 反义词： (无直接反义词) | 词性：名词
