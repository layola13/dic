breachers:/ˈbriːtʃərz/| n.|破坏者，违反者|People who break a law, rule, or agreement are breachers.  那些违反法律、规则或协议的人是违反者。|The breachers of the treaty were condemned internationally.  违反条约的人受到了国际社会的谴责。|近义词：violators, offenders, infringers|反义词：compliers, conformists|词性:Noun


breaches:/ˈbriːtʃɪz/| n.|违反，突破口|A breach is a violation of a law, rule, or agreement. 违反是指违反法律、规则或协议。|The data breach exposed sensitive customer information. 数据泄露暴露了敏感的客户信息。|近义词：violations, infractions, offenses|反义词：compliance, observance|词性:Noun


breach:/briːtʃ/| n. vt.|违反，突破；突破，违反|A breach is a violation of a law, rule, or agreement; To breach means to break or violate. 违反是指违反法律、规则或协议；突破是指打破或违反。|n. The breach in the dam caused widespread flooding. 大坝的决口造成了大范围的洪水。|vt.  They breached the contract by failing to deliver on time. 他们因未能按时交货而违反了合同。|近义词：n. gap, hole, crack; vt. violate, break, contravene|反义词：n. closure, seal; vt. comply, observe|词性:Noun, Verb

词源: From Old French *breche* "a break, opening," from a Germanic source related to Old English *bryce* "a break, fracture."
