fossiled:/ˈfɒsɪld/ (UK) /ˈfɑːsəld/ (US)| adj.|僵化的；成为化石的；老掉牙的|Preserved from a past geologic age; antiquated; outmoded.  Something that is fossiled is either literally turned into a fossil or metaphorically stuck in old ways.|The dinosaur bones were fossiled over millions of years.  恐龙骨骼经过数百万年变成了化石。

The company's fossiled management style is hindering innovation.  该公司僵化的管理方式阻碍了创新。|Synonyms: petrified, ancient, outdated, archaic|Antonyms: modern, contemporary, new, current|adjective

fossilize:/ˈfɒsɪlaɪz/ (UK) /ˈfɑːsəlaɪz/ (US)| v.|（使）变成化石；（使）僵化|To convert into a fossil; to become rigid and unchanging.|The remains of the ancient creature fossilized over time.  古代生物的遗骸随着时间的推移变成了化石。

Their thinking has fossilized over the years.  他们的思维多年来已经僵化。|Synonyms: petrify, ossify|Antonyms: modernize, adapt, evolve|verb
