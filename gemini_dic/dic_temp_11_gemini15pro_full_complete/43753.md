creepily:/ˈkriːpɪli/| adv.|令人毛骨悚然地，令人不寒而栗地|In a way that causes a feeling of fear and disgust.|He smiled creepily at her. 他令人毛骨悚然地对她笑了笑。|Synonyms: eerily, unsettlingly, disturbingly|Antonyms: comfortingly, reassuringly|Adverb
creepily:/ˈkriːpɪli/| adv.|偷偷地，悄无声息地|In a way that is slow, quiet, and careful, often in order to avoid being noticed.|The cat crept creepily towards the bird. 猫咪偷偷地向小鸟走去。|Synonyms: stealthily, furtively|Antonyms: openly, blatantly|Adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From creep (verb) + -ily (adverb suffix). The verb "creep" comes from Old English "crēopan," meaning "to crawl," related to Old Norse "krjūpa" and Old High German "kriuffan."  The suffix "-ily" forms adverbs from adjectives, in this case, from the adjective "creepy."
