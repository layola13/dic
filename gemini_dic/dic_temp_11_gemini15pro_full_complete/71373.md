flagellum:/fləˈdʒɛləm/ (plural flagella) | n. |鞭毛|A slender threadlike structure, especially a microscopic whiplike appendage that enables many protozoa, bacteria, spermatozoa, etc. to swim.|The bacterium uses its flagellum to propel itself through the liquid.  这种细菌用鞭毛推动自身在液体中游动。|Near synonyms: cilium, appendage|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps immotile structure)|Noun

flagella:/fləˈdʒɛl.ə/ (plural) | n. |鞭毛（复数）|Plural form of flagellum, referring to multiple whiplike appendages used for locomotion in microorganisms. |Many bacteria possess flagella that allow them to move towards nutrients. 许多细菌拥有鞭毛，使它们能够向营养物质移动。|Near synonyms: cilia, appendages|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps immotile structures)|Noun
