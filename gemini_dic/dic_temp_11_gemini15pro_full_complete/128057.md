nerve:/nɜːrv/ (US) /nɜːv/ (UK)| n. |神经；勇气；胆量|A fiber or bundle of fibers that transmits impulses of sensation to the brain or spinal cord, and impulses from these to the muscles and organs.|例句：The optic nerve connects the eye to the brain. 视神经连接眼睛和大脑。

例句：It takes a lot of nerve to stand up to a bully.  对抗恶霸需要很大的勇气。|近义词：courage, bravery, guts, fortitude, fiber|反义词：fear, cowardice, timidity|noun


nerve:/nɜːrv/ (US) /nɜːv/ (UK)| vt. |鼓起勇气；使紧张|To give strength or courage to; to brace.|例句：She nerved herself to ask for a raise. 她鼓起勇气要求加薪。

例句：He was nerved by the challenge.  挑战使他振奋。|近义词：embolden, strengthen, fortify|反义词：weaken, discourage, unnerve|verb
