carpetbagger: /ˈkɑːrpɪtˌbæɡər/ | n. | 投机分子；carpetbaggers(复数) | A person from the northern United States who went to the South after the Civil War to profit from the Reconstruction.  |  Example 1: Many carpetbaggers became wealthy by exploiting the South's postwar economic instability. 许多投机分子通过利用南方战后经济不稳定而致富。

Example 2:  He was accused of being a carpetbagger, only interested in personal gain. 他被指控为投机分子，只对个人利益感兴趣。 | Synonym: opportunist, speculator, profiteer | Antonym: native, local, resident | noun
