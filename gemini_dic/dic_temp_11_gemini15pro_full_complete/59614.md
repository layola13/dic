eccentric:/ɪkˈsɛntrɪk/ | adj. | 古怪的，反常的 | Deviating from the recognized or customary character, practice, etc.; peculiar; odd: unconventional;  not having the same center. |  Example 1: Her eccentric behaviour amused everyone. 她的古怪行为逗乐了所有人。

Example 2: He was known for his eccentric fashion sense.  他以其古怪的时尚感而闻名。 | Synonyms: peculiar, odd, strange, unusual, bizarre, quirky, unconventional | Antonyms: normal, conventional, ordinary, usual, typical | adjective

eccentricity:/ˌɛksɛnˈtrɪsɪti/ | n. | 古怪；反常；偏心率 | The quality of being eccentric; oddity;  the state or amount of being eccentric. In geometry, the ratio of the distance from a point on a conic section to a focus and the corresponding distance to the directrix. | Example 1: His eccentricity was part of his charm. 他的古怪是他魅力的一部分。

Example 2: The eccentricity of the ellipse is small. 这个椭圆的偏心率很小。 | Synonyms: peculiarity, oddity, strangeness, quirkiness, unconventionality | Antonyms: normality, conventionality, ordinariness, usualness | noun
