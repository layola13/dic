doctrinalist: /ˈdɒktrɪnəlɪst/ (UK) /ˈdɑːktrɪnəlɪst/ (US)| n.| 教条主义者; 纯理论家| A person who adheres rigidly to doctrines or dogma, especially in religion or politics. | He was known as a strict doctrinalist who refused to compromise on any of his beliefs. 他被称为一个严格的教条主义者，拒绝在他的任何信仰上妥协。| 近义词: dogmatist, ideologue | 反义词: pragmatist, freethinker | 词性: Noun
Etymology:  From "doctrine" (from Latin "doctrina" meaning "teaching, instruction") + "-alist" (suffix forming nouns denoting persons who adhere to a particular doctrine or system).
