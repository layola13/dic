obsesses:/əbˈsɛsɪz/| v.| 使痴迷，困扰 | to preoccupy intensely or abnormally; to dominate or haunt the thoughts of | He obsesses over every detail of his work. 他对工作的每个细节都非常痴迷。|Synonyms: preoccupies, consumes, haunts | Antonyms: ignores, neglects | verb (third-person singular simple present obsesses, present participle obsessing, simple past and past participle obsessed)

Etymology: From Late Latin obsessus, past participle of obsidere "to besiege, blockade, occupy," from ob- "against, toward" + sedere "to sit."  The original meaning related to a demon or evil spirit besieging a person.
