badly-off:/ˈbædli ˈɒf/ (UK) /ˈbædli ˈɔːf/ (US)| adj.|贫困的，境况不好的|Poor; not having enough money for basic needs.  Lacking in something desirable.|They were so badly-off they couldn't afford new shoes for their children. 他们很穷，没钱给孩子买新鞋。|Synonyms: poor, impoverished, needy, disadvantaged, hard up, strapped for cash|Antonyms: rich, wealthy, affluent, prosperous|adjective
