bottom-out:/ˈbɒtəm aʊt (UK), ˈbɑːtəm aʊt (US)| v. | 触底反弹；达到最低点 | To reach the lowest point before starting to rise again; to reach a point where a price or value stops declining. |例句：The recession bottomed out in the second quarter of the year. 经济衰退在今年第二季度触底反弹。

例句：House prices are expected to bottom out next year. 预计房价明年将触底反弹。|近义词：reach a low point, hit rock bottom, trough |反义词: peak, climax, top out |词性:动词

bottom-out:/ˈbɒtəm aʊt (UK), ˈbɑːtəm aʊt (US)| n. | 底部；最低点 | The lowest point or level. |例句：The bottom-out of the market provided a good buying opportunity. 市场的底部提供了一个很好的买入机会。

例句：Analysts predict the bottom-out for oil prices is still a few months away. 分析师预测油价的底部还有几个月的时间。|近义词：nadir, low point |反义词: peak, apex |词性:名词
