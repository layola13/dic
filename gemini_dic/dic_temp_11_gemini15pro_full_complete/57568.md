dramatizing:/ˈdrɑːməˌtaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 戏剧化；夸张；渲染 | The act of making something seem more dramatic or exciting than it really is. | She was dramatizing the story to make it more interesting for the children. 她为了让故事对孩子们更有趣而将其戏剧化。 | exaggerate, overstate, embellish | downplay, understate | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From drama + -tize + -ing.  "Drama" comes from the Greek "δρᾶμα" (drama), meaning "action, deed, play." "-tize" is a suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become," and "-ing" is a present participle suffix.
