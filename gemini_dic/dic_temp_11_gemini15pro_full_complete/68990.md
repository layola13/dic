fatimide:/ˈfætɪmaɪd/ | adj. n. | 法蒂玛王朝的；法蒂玛王朝成员 | Relating to the Fatimid dynasty, a Shi'a Islamic caliphate that ruled much of North Africa, Egypt, and the Levant from the 10th to the 12th centuries.  Also, a member of this dynasty. | The Fatimid caliphate was known for its patronage of the arts and sciences. 法蒂玛王朝以其对艺术和科学的赞助而闻名。|  The Fatimid rulers built magnificent mosques and palaces in Cairo. 法蒂玛王朝的统治者在开罗建造了宏伟的清真寺和宫殿。 | synonyms: Ismaili (sometimes), Shi'a (in a broad sense) | antonyms: Abbasid, Sunni (often in opposition) |  adjective, noun
