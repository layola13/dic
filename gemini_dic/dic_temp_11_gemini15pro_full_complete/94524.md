imposer:/ɪmˈpoʊzər (US) / ɪmˈpəʊzə(r) (UK)| n.|冒名顶替者，强加者|Someone who pretends to be someone else in order to deceive others; someone who forces their opinions or ideas on other people.|He was an imposter, claiming to be a doctor when he had no medical training. 他是个冒名顶替者，明明没有受过医学训练却声称自己是医生。|Synonyms: pretender, fraud, charlatan, fake|Antonyms: genuine person, real McCoy|noun

imposer:/ɪmˈpoʊzər (US) / ɪmˈpəʊzə(r) (UK)| n.|强加于人者|Someone who imposes something, such as a tax or a burden, on others.|The government was seen as an imposter, imposing heavy taxes on the people. 政府被视为强加于人者，向人民征收重税。|Synonyms: dictator, tyrant, oppressor|Antonyms: benefactor, helper, supporter|noun
