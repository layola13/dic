ETYMOLOGY->
de- (prefix): meaning "removal" or "reversal" from Latin
control (verb/noun): from Old French "controle" meaning "a check, register, roll," ultimately from Medieval Latin "contra rotulus" meaning "counter-roll" (used to verify accounts).
-ed (suffix): forms the past participle and past tense of verbs, but in this case, it creates an adjective indicating a state of having been decontrolled.
