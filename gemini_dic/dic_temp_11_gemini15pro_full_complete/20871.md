blaxploitation: /ˌblæksˌplɔɪˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 黑人剥削电影 | A genre of low-budget films made in the early 1970s, featuring African American actors in stereotypical roles, often as criminals or revolutionaries, and exploiting themes of violence, sex, and drug use. | Example:  "Shaft" is considered a classic blaxploitation film.  “《夏福特》被认为是一部经典的黑人剥削电影。” | Example: Blaxploitation films were both criticized and celebrated for their portrayal of Black life.  “黑人剥削电影因其对黑人生活的刻画而受到批评和赞扬。” | Synonym: ghetto action,  | Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "mainstream cinema") | Noun
