manias:/ˈmeɪniəz/| n. | 狂热，癖好 | A state of abnormally elevated arousal, affect, and energy level, or "a state of heightened overall activation with enhanced affective expression together with lability of affect."  A general term for excessive or unreasonable enthusiasm, interest, or desire; craze. | He has a mania for collecting old cars. 他有收集老爷车的癖好。|Synonyms: obsessions, crazes, fixations, passions, infatuations | Antonyms: apathy, indifference, calmness, composure | Noun

mania:/ˈmeɪniə/| n. | 狂热，癖好 | An excessive enthusiasm or desire; an obsession.  In psychiatry, a phase of bipolar disorder characterized by intense euphoria, hyperactivity, and uncontrolled excitement.  | She has a mania for cleanliness. 她有洁癖。| Synonyms: obsession, craze, fixation, passion, infatuation | Antonyms: apathy, indifference, calmness, composure | Noun
