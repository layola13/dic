bloviation: /ˌbloʊviˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 空话，夸夸其谈 | Talk that is empty, pompous, and long-winded. |  The politician's speech was filled with bloviation, but little substance.  这位政客的演讲充满了夸夸其谈，但没有什么实质内容。| synonyms: bombast, bluster, rhetoric, hot air | antonyms: conciseness, brevity, terseness | noun


bloviate: /ˈbloʊvieɪt/ | v. |  夸夸其谈，高谈阔论 | To talk at length in a pompous or boastful way. | He bloviated for hours about his accomplishments. 他滔滔不绝地吹嘘自己的成就。| synonyms: rant, rave, spout, declaim | antonyms: be quiet, listen, be concise | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From bloviate (v.), from blow + -iate, perhaps influenced by deviate.  First recorded in 1871. 源于动词bloviate，由blow+-iate构成，可能受deviate的影响。 首次记录于1871年。
