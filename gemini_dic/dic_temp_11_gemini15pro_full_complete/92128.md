hyperscrupulous:/ˌhaɪpərˈskruːpjələs/
adjective
过分谨慎的，过于拘泥的
Excessively scrupulous; extremely careful to do what is right or proper, often to an excessive degree.
He was so hyperscrupulous about following the rules that he often missed the bigger picture. 他对遵守规则过于谨慎，以至于经常忽略大局。
She is hyperscrupulous in her business dealings, ensuring every detail is perfect. 她在商业交易中非常谨慎，确保每个细节都完美无缺。
近义词: meticulous, conscientious, fastidious, punctilious
反义词: careless, negligent, remiss, unscrupulous
词性: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- (meaning "excessive, over") + scrupulous (from Latin scrupulus, meaning "small sharp stone," hence "anxiety, uneasiness," referring to the discomfort caused by a stone in one's shoe).
