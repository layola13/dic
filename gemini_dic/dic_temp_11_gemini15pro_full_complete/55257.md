dissimulators:/dɪˈsɪmjʊleɪtərz/
n.
掩饰者，伪装者
People who conceal their true feelings, intentions, or beliefs.  Those who practice dissimulation.
The dissimulators were eventually exposed, their elaborate charade crumbling under scrutiny.  这些伪装者最终被揭穿，他们精心策划的伪装在审查下土崩瓦解。
The dissimulators maintained their composure throughout the interrogation. 伪装者在整个审讯过程中都保持镇定。
近义词:hypocrites, pretenders, liars, deceivers
反义词:honest people, truth-tellers


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dissimulātor, from Latin dissimulō (“I disguise, conceal, dissemble”), from dis- (“not, un-”) + simulō (“I copy, imitate, feign, pretend”).
