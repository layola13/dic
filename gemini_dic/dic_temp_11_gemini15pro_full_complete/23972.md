breastwork: /ˈbrɛstˌwɜːrk/ (US) /ˈbrɛstˌwɜːk/ (UK)
noun
胸墙；防壁；胸部
A low, temporary wall built for defense, typically made of earth or sandbags.  A protective barrier, often hastily constructed.  (Figuratively) The chest or bosom.
The soldiers sheltered behind the breastwork as enemy fire rained down. 士兵们躲在胸墙后面，敌人的炮火倾泻而下。

The pioneers quickly erected a breastwork against the impending attack.  拓荒者们迅速竖起一道胸墙来抵御即将到来的攻击。

Her ample breastwork provided a comfortable resting place for the sleeping infant. 她丰满的胸部为熟睡的婴儿提供了一个舒适的安息之所。
Synonyms: parapet, rampart, barricade, bulwark, embankment
Antonyms:  opening, gap, breach
