out-of-step:/ˌaʊt əv ˈstep/ | adj. | 不合拍的，不协调的，格格不入的 | Not in agreement or harmony with someone or something; having a different opinion or behaving in a different way from others. | He felt out-of-step with his colleagues. 他感觉与同事们格格不入。| 近义词: out of sync, discordant, incompatible, alienated | 反义词: in step, harmonious, compatible, in sync | 词性: 形容词

out-of-step:/ˌaʊt əv ˈstep/ | n. | 失调，不协调 | The state of being out of harmony or agreement. | Her out-of-step with the latest fashion trends was obvious. 她与最新时尚潮流的不协调显而易见。 | 近义词: discordance, incompatibility, alienation | 反义词: harmony, compatibility, synchronization | 词性: 名词

词源:  "Out" (meaning outside or away from) + "of" + "step" (referring to a rhythmic movement of the feet).  The original meaning related to literal stepping, but it broadened to encompass figurative disharmony.
