overpolishes:/ˌoʊvərˈpɑːlɪʃɪz/ | v. | 过度抛光，过度润色 | To polish something excessively, often to the point of detracting from its appearance or quality. | He overpolished the car, and now it looks too shiny. 他把车擦得太亮了，现在看起来有点过了。 | The writer overpolishes his prose, making it sound artificial.  这位作家过度润色他的散文，使之听起来很做作。 | 近义词: over-refine, overdo | 反义词: neglect, understate | verb

overpolish:/ˌoʊvərˈpɑːlɪʃ/ | n. | 过度抛光，过度润色 | The act of overpolishing something. | The overpolish on the table made it look cheap. 桌子上过度的抛光使它看起来很廉价。 |  The overpolish of the essay made it lose its sincerity.  这篇论文的过度润色使其失去了真诚。 | 近义词: over-refinement | 反义词: neglect | noun


词源:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "polish" (from Old French "polir," ultimately from Latin "polire" meaning "to smooth").
