conchyliometry:/ˌkɒŋkɪliˈɒmɪtri/| n.|贝壳测量学; 贝类测定学|The study of mollusk shells, especially their measurements and variations, often used in taxonomy and paleontology.  It involves precise measurements of shell dimensions like length, width, aperture size, and spire height to analyze growth patterns, variations within populations, and evolutionary relationships.|Conchyliometry played a crucial role in understanding the evolution of these snails.|近义词:malacology|反义词:N/A|名词
