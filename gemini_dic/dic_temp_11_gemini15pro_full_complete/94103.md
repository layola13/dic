impeach:/ɪmˈpiːtʃ/| v. | 弹劾，控告，怀疑 | To charge (a public official) with misconduct in office.  To challenge the credibility of.| The president was impeached by the House of Representatives. 众议院弹劾了总统。|  accuse, indict, charge, question, challenge | acquit, exonerate, vindicate | verb

impeached:/ɪmˈpiːtʃt/ | adj. | 被弹劾的，被控告的 | Having been formally accused of misconduct in office.  Having had one's credibility challenged.| The impeached president faced a trial in the Senate. 被弹劾的总统在参议院面临审判。| accused, indicted, charged | acquitted, exonerated, vindicated | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *empeechier*, from Late Latin *impedicare* "to entangle, hinder," from Latin *in- + pedica* "fetter, shackle."  The original meaning was "to hinder, obstruct," later evolving to "accuse, charge."
