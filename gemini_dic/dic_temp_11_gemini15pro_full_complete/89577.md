hoo-hah:/ˈhuːhɑː/ /ˈhuːhɔː/
|n.|喧闹，骚动；抱怨，抗议；（尤指）麻烦，困难|A commotion or fuss, especially one arising from confusion or excitement. Also, can refer to trouble or difficulty.|例句：There was a big hoo-hah when the airline lost everyone's luggage. 航空公司把所有人的行李都弄丢了，引起了一片骚动。

例句：We had quite a hoo-hah trying to get the tent up in the wind. 我们在风中搭帐篷可是费了好一番周折。
|近义词：fuss, commotion, uproar, ruckus, kerfuffle|反义词：calm, peace, quiet|词性：名词

hoo-hah:/ˈhuːhɑː/ /ˈhuːhɔː/
|int.|（表示惊讶、轻蔑等）|An exclamation expressing surprise, scorn, etc.|例句：Hoo-hah! Look at that fancy car! 哇！看那辆豪华轿车！

例句：Hoo-hah! You think you can fool me?  哈！你以为你能骗我？
|近义词：wow, oh, huh|反义词：(No direct antonym for an interjection)|词性：感叹词


词源：
The origin of "hoo-hah" is uncertain, but it is thought to be imitative, possibly originating in the mid-19th century.  It may be related to other exclamations like "hoo-ray" or "ha-ha."
