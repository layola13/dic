gaucheness:/ˈɡɔːtʃnəs/ | n. | 笨拙，不圆滑，粗鲁 | Lack of social grace; awkwardness or clumsiness in social situations. |  Her gaucheness made her feel uncomfortable at formal events. 她的笨拙使她在正式场合感到不自在。| awkwardness, clumsiness, ineptitude, naiveté | grace, elegance, sophistication, poise | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From gauche (awkward, clumsy) + -ness (suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality).  Gauche comes from Old French gauche (left, left-handed), which was associated with awkwardness and clumsiness due to the traditional preference for right-handedness.
