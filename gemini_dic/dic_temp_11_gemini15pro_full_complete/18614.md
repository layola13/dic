bent:/bɛnt/ | adj. n. | 弯曲的；决意的；天赋 | curved; determined; a natural talent or inclination | The tree branch was bent under the weight of the snow.  树枝在雪的重压下弯曲了。| synonyms: curved, crooked, angled, determined, resolute | antonyms: straight, unbent, undecided, wavering | adjective

bent:/bɛnt/ | n. | 爱好；倾向 | a natural talent or inclination | He has a bent for music. 他有音乐天赋。| synonyms: inclination, talent, aptitude, predilection | antonyms: aversion, disinclination | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *benten*, from Old English *bendan* ("to bend, curve, make crooked"), from Proto-Germanic *bandaną* ("to bind, tie, curve"), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰendʰ- ("to bind").

PHRASE->
bent on something: determined to do something. He was bent on revenge. 他一心想复仇。
bent out of shape: become upset or angry. Don't get bent out of shape over this small issue.  别为这点小事生气。
have a bent for: have a natural talent or inclination for. She has a bent for languages. 她有语言天赋。
round the bend: crazy or insane.  He went round the bend after losing his job.  他失业后精神失常了。
bent double: bent over at the waist. He was bent double with laughter. 他笑得弯下了腰。
