ETYMOLOGY->

From callow + -ness.  Callow itself comes from Old English *calu "bald, bare," from Proto-Germanic *kalwaz "bald, bare," from PIE *galw- "bald, bare."  The original meaning referred to young birds without feathers, and the sense of "immature" developed from there.
