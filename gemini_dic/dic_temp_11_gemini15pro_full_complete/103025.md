jejunenesses:/ˌdʒɛdʒuˈnɛsnɪsɪz/
n.
空虚；贫乏；枯燥无味
the state of being jejune, lacking in substance or interest.
The jejunenesses of the politician's speeches left the audience bored and uninspired.  这位政客演讲的空洞乏味让听众感到无聊，毫无启发。
jejuneness, insipidity, vapidity
depth, richness, substance
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *jejunitas, from Latin ieiunus (“fasting, hungry; dry, barren, meagre”).
