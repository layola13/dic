mealy-mouthedly:/ˈmiːliˌmaʊðɪdli/
| adv. |吞吞吐吐地，拐弯抹角地|Speaking indirectly or evasively, especially for fear of giving offense.|He mealy-mouthedly suggested that perhaps the project wasn't going as well as planned. 他吞吞吐吐地暗示，也许这个项目进展得不如预期那么顺利。|近义词: evasively, indirectly, vaguely|反义词: directly, frankly, forthrightly|词性: 副词

mealy-mouthed:/ˈmiːliˌmaʊðd/
| adj. |说话吞吞吐吐的，拐弯抹角的|Not frank or direct in speech; insincere or evasive.|The politician gave a mealy-mouthed response to the scandal.  这位政治家对丑闻的回应吞吞吐吐。|近义词: evasive, indirect, vague|反义词: direct, frank, forthrightly|词性: 形容词

Etymology:
From "mealy" (soft, like meal) + "mouthed" (having a specified kind of mouth).  The implication is that the speaker's words are soft and bland, lacking substance or directness.
