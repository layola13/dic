ossify:/ˈɒsɪfaɪ/ (UK) /ˈɑːsɪfaɪ/ (US)| v. |骨化；僵化；使保守|To become bone or bony. To become rigid or inflexible, especially in attitudes or beliefs.|例句：The cartilage will ossify into bone as the child grows.  随着孩子的成长，软骨会骨化成骨。

例句：His thinking has ossified over the years. 多年来，他的思想僵化了。|近义词：harden, solidify, fossilize, stagnate|反义词:soften,liquify, adapt, progress|词性:动词

ossifying:/ˈɒsɪfaɪɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈɑːsɪfaɪɪŋ/ (US)| adj. |骨化的；僵化的；使保守的|Causing or tending to cause ossification. Becoming rigid or inflexible.|例句：The ossifying effects of tradition can stifle innovation.  传统僵化的影响会扼杀创新。

例句：He has an ossifying belief in the old ways. 他对旧的方式抱有僵化的信念。|近义词：hardening, solidifying, fossilizing, stagnating|反义词:softening,liquifying, adapting, progressing|词性:形容词
