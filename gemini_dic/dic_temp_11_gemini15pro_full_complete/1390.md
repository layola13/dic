accusative:/əˈkjuːzətɪv/ | n. adj. |宾格；控告的| The accusative case is a grammatical case used to mark the direct object of a transitive verb. As an adjective, it means relating to or involving an accusation. | The noun "dog" is in the accusative case in the sentence "The boy kicked the dog."  名词“狗”在句子“男孩踢了狗”中是宾格。| Synonyms: objective (as a noun) | Antonyms: nominative |词性: noun, adjective
