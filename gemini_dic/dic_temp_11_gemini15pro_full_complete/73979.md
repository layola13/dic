fortlet: /ˈfɔːrtlɪt/ | n. |小堡垒，炮台|A small fort, typically one intended to accommodate a small detachment of troops.| The soldiers held the fortlet against the enemy attack. 士兵们坚守小堡垒抵御敌人的进攻。|  Synonyms: redoubt, stronghold, fortress | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, possibly "vulnerable position") | noun
Etymology->
From French *fortelet*, diminutive of *fortel*, from Old French *fortiel*  ("small fort"), diminutive of *fort* ("strong"), from Latin *fortis*. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *fer- (“strong, firm”).
