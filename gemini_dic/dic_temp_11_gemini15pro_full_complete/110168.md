lien:/ˈliːən/ | n. |留置权；扣押权|A right to keep possession of property belonging to another person until a debt owed by that person is discharged.| The bank had a lien on the property until the loan was repaid. 银行对该房产拥有留置权，直到贷款还清。|Example: The mechanic placed a lien on the car until the owner paid for the repairs.  修理工对这辆车进行了留置，直到车主支付修理费。|Synonyms: claim, charge, encumbrance|Antonyms: release, discharge|noun

lien:/ˈliːən/ | v. | 对…有留置权| To have or assert a legal claim to something.| The creditor liened the debtor's assets. 债权人对债务人的资产拥有留置权。|Example: The bank liened his house after he defaulted on his mortgage. 他拖欠抵押贷款后，银行扣押了他的房子。|Synonyms: attach, seize, secure|Antonyms: release, relinquish|verb
