aretaics: /ˌærəˈteɪɪks/ | n. |  德性伦理学；美德伦理学 |  The branch of moral philosophy that emphasizes virtues of mind and character, such as wisdom, justice, fortitude, and temperance.  It focuses on what sort of person one should strive to be, rather than on specific actions. | Example:  Aristotle's Nicomachean Ethics is a foundational text in aretaics.  亚里士多德的《尼各马可伦理学》是德性伦理学的奠基文本。

Example: Modern aretaics has seen a resurgence of interest in virtue ethics. 现代德性伦理学见证了人们对美德伦理学的兴趣复苏。 | 近义词: virtue ethics, moral philosophy | 反义词: deontology, consequentialism | 词性: noun


Etymology: From Greek  ἀρετή (aretē, “excellence, virtue”) + -ics.
