apocalyptic:/əˌpɒkəˈlɪptɪk/ /əˌpɑːkəˈlɪptɪk/| adj.|末世的；启示录的；预示灾难的|Relating to the end of the world, or resembling the end of the world; momentous or catastrophic.|The apocalyptic scenes of the hurricane's aftermath were devastating.  飓风过后末日般的景象令人震惊。|
近义词:catastrophic, disastrous, doomsday, cataclysmic|
反义词:blessed, fortunate, auspicious|
词性:adjective


apocalypse:/əˈpɒkəlɪps/ /əˈpɑːkəlɪps/| n.|世界末日；大灾难；启示；天启|The complete destruction of the world as described in the biblical book of Revelation; any universal or widespread destruction or disaster.|Many people fear a nuclear apocalypse. 很多人害怕核末日。|
近义词:cataclysm, disaster, doomsday|
反义词:miracle, blessing|
词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *apocalypticus, from Ancient Greek ἀποκαλυπτικός (apokaluptikós, “revelatory”), from ἀποκαλύπτω (apokalúptō, “I uncover, reveal”), from ἀπό (apó, “from, away from”) + καλύπτω (kalúptō, “I cover, conceal”).
