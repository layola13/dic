grandiloquence: /ˌɡrændɪˈləkwəns/ | n. | 夸夸其谈；浮夸的言辞 |  Pompous or extravagant language, especially when intended to impress. |  The politician's speech was filled with grandiloquence, but lacked substance.  这位政客的演讲充满了夸夸其谈，但缺乏实质内容。| synonyms: bombast, rhetoric, pomposity | antonyms:  simplicity, conciseness, modesty | noun

grandiloquent: /ˌɡrændɪˈləkwənt/ | adj. | 夸夸其谈的；辞藻华丽的 |  Characterized by pompous or extravagant language. |  His grandiloquent style of writing often obscured the meaning. 他浮夸的写作风格常常掩盖了意义。| synonyms: pompous, bombastic, verbose | antonyms: simple, concise, understated | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin "grandiloquentia"  meaning "lofty speech,"  from "grandis" (great) + "loquentia" (speaking).
