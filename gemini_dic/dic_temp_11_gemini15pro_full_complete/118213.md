mephitism:/ˈmɛfɪtɪzəm/ | n. | 毒气；瘴气 | A noxious or poisonous exhalation from the earth.  The presence of foul-smelling and potentially harmful gases in the air. |  The mephitism of the swamp made it difficult to breathe. 沼泽里的瘴气使人难以呼吸。 | Synonyms: noxious fumes, toxic gases, miasma, stench | Antonyms: fresh air, clean air | noun

mephitic:/mɪˈfɪtɪk/ | adj. | 毒气的；瘴气的 | Relating to or resembling mephitis; foul-smelling; poisonous.  | The mephitic air of the cave was overpowering. 洞穴里毒气熏天的空气令人窒息。 | Synonyms: noxious, poisonous, toxic, foul, fetid | Antonyms: fresh, clean, pure | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *méphitisme*, from *méphitique* "noxious," from Late Latin *mephitis* "noxious exhalation from the earth," probably borrowed from Oscan, a language of ancient southern Italy.
