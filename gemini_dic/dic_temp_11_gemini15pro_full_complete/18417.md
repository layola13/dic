belt:/bɛlt/ | n. v. | 腰带，传送带；环绕；痛打 | A strip of flexible material, typically leather, used to fasten or secure clothing or other items around the waist.  To strike (someone) hard. | He tightened his belt.  他勒紧了腰带。| strap, sash, girdle | loosen | noun, verb

belt:/bɛlt/ | v. |  痛打，快速移动 | To hit someone hard. To move very fast. |  He belted the ball over the fence. 他用力把球击过了篱笆。 | hit, strike, thrash, whip | caress | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *belt "belt, girdle," from Proto-Germanic *baltjaz (source also of Old Norse belti, Old High German balte).  Ultimately perhaps related to bind.
