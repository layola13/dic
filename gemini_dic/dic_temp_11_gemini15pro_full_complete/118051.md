menace:/ˈmɛnɪs/| n. vt.|威胁，恐吓；危险的人（或物）|A menace is something that is likely to cause harm.|The rising crime rate is a menace to society. 犯罪率上升是对社会的威胁。|近义词：threat, danger, peril, hazard|反义词：safety, security|词性:名词, 动词

menaces:/ˈmɛnɪsɪz/| n. (menace的复数形式)|威胁，恐吓；危险的人（或物）|Menaces are things that are likely to cause harm.|The factory poses several environmental menaces. 这家工厂造成了几个环境威胁。|近义词：threats, dangers, perils, hazards|反义词：safeties, securities|词性:名词

menaced:/ˈmɛnɪst/| v. (menace的过去式和过去分词)|威胁，恐吓|Menaced means threatened or endangered.|The mugger menaced him with a knife. 抢劫犯用刀威胁他。|近义词：threatened, endangered, intimidated|反义词：protected, safeguarded|词性:动词

menacing:/ˈmɛnɪsɪŋ/| adj.|威胁的，恐吓的；险恶的|Menacing means suggesting the presence of danger; threatening.|He gave me a menacing look. 他给了我一个威胁的眼神。|近义词：threatening, intimidating, ominous, sinister|反义词：reassuring, comforting|词性:形容词

menacingly:/ˈmɛnɪsɪŋli/| adv.|威胁地，恐吓地；险恶地|Menacingly describes the way something is done in a threatening manner.|The dog growled menacingly. 狗发出威胁的低吼。|近义词：threateningly, intimidatingly, ominously, sinisterly|反义词：reassuringly, comfortingly|词性:副词


词源:  From Middle French *menace, from Late Latin minacia (“a threat”), from Latin minax (“projecting, overhanging, threatening”), from minārī (“to project, threaten”).
