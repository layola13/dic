digladiation:/dɪˌɡlædɪˈeɪʃən/
n.
角斗，争斗
A combat between two gladiators or warriors, often to the death.  A fierce struggle or contest.
The digladiation in the arena was brutal and bloody.  竞技场上的角斗残酷而血腥。
The political digladiation between the two parties continues.  两个政党之间的政治斗争仍在继续。
近义词:combat, battle, fight, struggle, contest
反义词:peace, truce, harmony, agreement
词性:noun

Etymology:
From Latin *digladiatio*, from digladiari (“to fight with swords, contend fiercely”), from di- (“thoroughly”) + gladiari (“to use a sword, fight with swords”), from gladius (“sword”).
