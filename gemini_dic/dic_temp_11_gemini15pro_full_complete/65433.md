euphuize:/ˈjuːfjuːaɪz/
verb
使…华丽；使…辞藻堆砌；使…用尤弗伊斯体
To express in a highly elaborate and affected style, characterized by excessive use of antithesis, alliteration, and far-fetched similes, as in the writings of John Lyly.
The writer euphuized his prose with elaborate metaphors and classical allusions.  这位作家用精巧的比喻和古典典故使他的散文华丽起来。
