good-heartedness:/ˌɡʊdˈhɑːrtɪdnəs/| n. |善良，好心肠|The quality of being kind and generous. |Her good-heartedness led her to volunteer at the homeless shelter. 她的善良使她自愿在流浪汉收容所服务。|Kindness, generosity, compassion, benevolence|Cruelty, malice, selfishness, meanness|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From good (adjective) + hearted (adjective) + -ness (suffix forming nouns expressing state or quality).  "Good" comes from Old English "gōd," ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*gōdaz." "Heart" comes from Old English "heorte," from Proto-Germanic "*herton," related to Dutch "hart" and German "Herz." "-ness" is a common Old English suffix.
