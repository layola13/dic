courts:/kɔːrts/ /kʊrts/| n.|法院；球场；庭院；宫廷|A place where legal cases are heard and judged; an area marked out for playing a game or sport; a courtyard, especially of a large house or palace; the official residence of a sovereign or other ruler.|The case will be heard in the higher courts.  这案子将由高等法院审理。|synonyms: tribunals, courtrooms (legal); fields, pitches, arenas (sport); yards, patios (courtyard); palaces, royal residences (palace)|antonyms: (for legal sense)  informal settings, mediation|noun


courts:/kɔːrts/ /kʊrts/| v.|追求；献殷勤；招致（危险）|To seek the affections of someone with the intent of marriage; to behave so as to invite or incur (something undesirable).|He's been courting her for months. 他追求她好几个月了。|synonyms: woo, pursue, romance; invite, risk, attract|antonyms: reject, spurn; avoid, prevent|verb
