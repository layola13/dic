enmity: /ˈenməti/ | n. | 敌意，仇恨 | The state of being actively opposed or hostile to someone or something. | There was a long-standing enmity between the two families.  这两个家族之间长期存在敌意。| synonyms: hostility, animosity, antagonism, antipathy, rancor, bitterness, acrimony, malice, malevolence, venom, spite, ill will, bad blood, hatred | antonyms: friendship, amity, goodwill, benevolence | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *enemité*, from Latin *inimicitia* (“enmity, hostility”),  from *inimicus* (“enemy, unfriendly”), from *in-* (“not”) + *amicus* (“friend, friendly, kind”).
