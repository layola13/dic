condescend:/ˌkɒndɪˈsɛnd/ /ˌkɑːndɪˈsɛnd/| v. |屈尊；俯就；|To behave as if one is conscious of descending from a superior position.  To deal with people in a patronizingly superior way.|He condescended to help her with her homework, even though he thought it was beneath him. 他屈尊帮她做作业，尽管他认为这对他来说是低人一等的事。|近义词：deign, stoop, patronize|反义词：respect, admire|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *condescendere, from Latin con- + descendere "to descend."  The original sense was "to yield, concede," later evolving to the current meaning of patronizingly lowering oneself.
