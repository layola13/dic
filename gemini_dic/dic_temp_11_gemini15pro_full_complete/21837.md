bodes:/boʊdz/| v.|预示，预兆|Indicates or portends something, usually something bad.|The dark clouds bode ill for the picnic.  乌云预示着野餐不妙。|Synonyms: presages, portends, augurs, foreshadows|Antonyms:  promises, assures|verb (third-person singular simple present bodes, present participle boding, simple past boded, past participle boded or boden)
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English boden, from Old English bodian (“to announce, proclaim, foretell”), from Proto-Germanic *baudijaną (“to offer, present, announce”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰewdʰ- (“to be aware, make aware”). Related to bid.
