disconfirmation:/ˌdɪskɑːnfərˈmeɪʃən/
n.
不确认，证伪，否定
The act of proving something to be false or invalid; the act of failing to confirm expectations.
The experiment resulted in the disconfirmation of his hypothesis.  这个实验导致他的假设被证伪。
近义词：refutation, invalidation, denial, contradiction
反义词：confirmation, validation, verification
词性: 名词

disconfirmation:/ˌdɪskɑːnfərˈmeɪʃən/
n.
未满足，期望落空
The state of having one's expectations unmet or contradicted.
His constant disconfirmation made her feel insecure. 他总是让她期望落空，这让她感到没有安全感。
近义词：disappointment, frustration
反义词：satisfaction, fulfillment
词性: 名词


词源: dis- (not) + confirm (to establish the truth or correctness of) + -ation (suffix forming nouns of action or process)
