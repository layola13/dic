hieratic:/ˌhaɪəˈrætɪk/
adjective
僧侣的；神职的；象形文字的
of or relating to a cursive form of hieroglyphic writing used by priests in ancient Egypt
The papyrus contained hieratic inscriptions that scholars are still deciphering. 这张莎草纸上刻有僧侣体文字，学者们仍在破译。
hieratic:/ˌhaɪəˈrætɪk/
adjective
威严的；神圣的；庄严的
characterized by a stylized formality appropriate to sacred uses or high rank
The queen maintained a hieratic composure throughout the ceremony. 女王在整个仪式中都保持着一种神圣的沉着。


Synonyms: priestly, sacred, formal, ceremonial, ritualistic
Antonyms: secular, informal, casual

Etymology:  From Late Latin *hieraticus, from Ancient Greek ἱερατικός (hieratikós, “priestly, hieratic”), from ἱερεύς (hiereús, “priest”) + -ικός (-ikós, adjectival suffix).
