diffraction:/dɪˈfrækʃən/ |n.|衍射，绕射|The bending of waves, such as light or sound, as they pass around an obstacle or through a narrow opening.|The diffraction of light through a prism creates a rainbow. 光通过棱镜的衍射形成了彩虹。|synonyms: scattering, dispersion|antonyms: reflection, refraction|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *diffractus, past participle of diffrangere ("to break apart"), from dis- ("apart") + frangere ("to break").  The term was coined by Italian physicist Francesco Maria Grimaldi in the 17th century.
