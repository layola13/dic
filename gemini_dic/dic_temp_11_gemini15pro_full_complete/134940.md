nonmystics:/ˌnɒnˈmɪstɪks/ | n. | 非神秘主义者；不相信神秘主义的人 | People who do not believe in or practice mysticism are called nonmystics. | Most scientists would likely consider themselves nonmystics, relying on empirical evidence rather than spiritual insight. | 近义词: pragmatists, rationalists, skeptics | 反义词: mystics, spiritualists | 词性: 名词

词源:  "non-" (prefix meaning "not") + "mystic" (from Late Latin mysticus, from Greek mystikos, relating to secret rites, from mystēs, initiate).
