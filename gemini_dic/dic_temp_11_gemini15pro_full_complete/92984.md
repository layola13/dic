idiolatry: /aɪˈdɒlətri/ (UK) /aɪˈdɑːlətri/ (US) | n. | 偶像崇拜；盲目崇拜 |  Extreme admiration, love, or reverence for something or someone. It often implies excessive devotion to the point of worship. |  His idiolatry of his favorite band bordered on obsession. 他对他最喜欢的乐队的狂热近乎痴迷。 |  worship, adoration, deification |  disdain, contempt, disregard | noun

Etymology: Late Middle English: from Old French idolatrie, via late Latin from Greek eidololatreia, from eidolon ‘image’ + latreia ‘worship.’
