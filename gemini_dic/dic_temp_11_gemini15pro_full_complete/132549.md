nondexterous: /ˌnɒnˈdɛkstrəs/ /ˌnɑːnˈdɛkstrəs/ | adj. | 不灵巧的，笨拙的 | Lacking dexterity; clumsy or awkward in the use of hands. |  He was nondexterous with tools and always seemed to drop things. 他不擅长使用工具，总是掉东西。|  The nondexterous surgeon struggled to complete the delicate procedure.  这位笨手笨脚的外科医生难以完成精细的手术。 | clumsy, awkward, inept, unskillful | dexterous, skillful, adept | adjective

Etymology:
From non- (not) + dexterous (skillful with hands).  Ultimately from Latin *dexter* (right, favorable).  The right hand was traditionally considered the more skillful and thus associated with positive qualities.
