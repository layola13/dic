disappoint:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt/ /ˌdɪsəˈpɔːɪnt/ | vt. |使失望，使扫兴 | To fail to fulfill the hopes or expectations of (someone).|
I didn't want to disappoint her, so I agreed to help. 我不想让她失望，所以我同意帮忙。

He was disappointed by the team's performance. 他对球队的表现感到失望。

|近义词：frustrate, let down, sadden |反义词: please, delight, satisfy|词性:动词


disappointed:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntɪd/ /ˌdɪsəˈpɔːɪntɪd/ | adj. |失望的，沮丧的 | Feeling sad or discouraged because one's hopes or expectations have not been fulfilled. |
I was disappointed that I didn't win the race. 我很失望我没有赢得比赛。

She felt disappointed with the outcome of the meeting. 她对会议的结果感到失望。

|近义词：disheartened, discouraged, let down |反义词: pleased, delighted, satisfied|词性:形容词


disappointing:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntɪŋ/ /ˌdɪsəˈpɔːɪntɪŋ/ | adj. |令人失望的 | Causing someone to feel disappointed. |
The movie was disappointing. 这部电影很令人失望。

It was a disappointing end to the season. 这是一个令人失望的赛季结局。

|近义词：unsatisfactory, underwhelming, frustrating |反义词: pleasing, delightful, satisfying|词性:形容词


disappointment:/ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt/ /ˌdɪsəˈpɔːɪntmənt/ | n. |失望，沮丧 | The feeling of being disappointed. |
His failure was a great disappointment to his family. 他的失败令他的家人非常失望。

To my disappointment, the concert was cancelled. 令我失望的是，音乐会被取消了。

|近义词：discouragement, letdown, dissatisfaction |反义词: pleasure, delight, satisfaction|词性:名词


词源: From Old French *desapointer "remove from an appointment," from des- "away, from" + apointer "to appoint." The modern meaning "fail to satisfy the hope or expectation of" arose in the late 16th century.
