ditransitive:/daɪˈtrænzɪtɪv/ | adj. | 双及物，双宾语 |  A ditransitive verb is a verb that takes both a direct object and an indirect object. The indirect object typically refers to the recipient or beneficiary of the action. |  She gave him the book. (book is the direct object, him is the indirect object) 她把书给了他。 |  近义词: N/A | 反义词: intransitive, monotransitive | 词性: adjective
