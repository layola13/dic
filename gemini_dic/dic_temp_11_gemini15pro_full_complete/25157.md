buckram:/ˈbʌkrəm/| n.|粗硬棉布，粗硬亚麻布|A stiff-finished cotton or linen fabric, typically heavily sized, used for interlinings, bookbindings, and hats.|The lawyer's briefcase was made of sturdy buckram.|Synonyms: stiff cloth, canvas, linen|Antonyms: soft fabric, silk, velvet|noun

buckrams:/ˈbʌkrəmz/| n.|粗硬棉布，粗硬亚麻布（复数）|The plural form of buckram, referring to multiple pieces or types of this stiff fabric.|He used different colored buckrams for the various book covers.|Synonyms: stiff cloths, canvases, linens|Antonyms: soft fabrics, silks, velvets|noun
