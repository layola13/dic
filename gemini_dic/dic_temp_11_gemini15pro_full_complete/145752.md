overcultivated: /ˌoʊvərˈkʌltɪveɪtɪd/ | adj. | 过度耕作的；过度开发的 | Excessively cultivated; exhausted by excessive tillage.  Used to describe land that has been farmed too intensively, leading to a decline in fertility. | The overcultivated fields were no longer productive.  过度耕作的田地不再肥沃了。 | The overcultivated soil was dry and cracked. 过度耕作的土壤干燥开裂。| 近义词: overfarmed, exhausted, depleted | 反义词: fertile, productive, uncultivated | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:  From "over-" (excessively) + "cultivated" (past participle of "cultivate," from Latin "cultivare," meaning "to till, cultivate," from "cultus," meaning "care, cultivation," past participle of "colere," meaning "to till, inhabit, cherish, protect").
