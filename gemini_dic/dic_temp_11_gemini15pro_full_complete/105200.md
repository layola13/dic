keyframes:/ˈkiːfreɪmz/| n.|关键帧|A keyframe in animation and filmmaking is a drawing that defines the starting and ending points of any smooth transition. The drawings are called "frames" because their position in time is measured in frames on a strip of film.  A sequence of keyframes defines which movement the viewer will see. |The animator set keyframes for the character's jump, defining the height and arc of the motion. 动画师为角色的跳跃设置了关键帧，定义了运动的高度和弧度。|synonyms: frame,  checkpoint| antonyms: N/A | noun
