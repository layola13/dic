burr:/bɜːr/ (US) /bɜː(r)/ (UK)| n. v.| 毛刺，刺果，嗡嗡声，粗喉咙的声音 |A rough or prickly edge or projection, especially one left on an object by a tool such as a saw or drill. A whirring sound. A guttural pronunciation of the letter 'r'.|
The metal casting had a burr that needed to be filed down.  金属铸件上有一个毛刺需要锉掉。

He spoke with a burr, rolling his 'r's. 他说话带点粗喉咙的声音，把r卷起来发音。

The drill left a burr on the edge of the hole.  钻头在孔的边缘留下了一个毛刺。

The dentist used a burr to remove the decayed part of the tooth. 牙医用了一个钻头去除牙齿的腐烂部分。


|近义词：barb, prickle, whir, bur,  (for sound) trill|反义词:smoothness (for texture)|noun, verb|

burr:/bɜːr/ (US) /bɜː(r)/ (UK)| v.| 去除毛刺，发出嗡嗡声，粗声地说 |To remove burrs from. To make a whirring sound. To pronounce with a guttural roll.|
He burred the edges of the newly cut metal. 他去除了新切割金属的毛刺。

The machine burred loudly as it worked. 机器运转时发出很大的嗡嗡声。


|近义词：trim, smooth, whir,  (for sound) trill|反义词:roughen (for texture)|verb|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *burre*, from Old Norse *burr* (“prickle, bristle”), from Proto-Germanic *burzō* (“thorn, bristle”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰers- (“to bristle, stand on end”). Cognate with Scots burr (“bur”), West Frisian burre (“burdock”), Dutch bur (“prickle”), German Bürste (“brush”).
