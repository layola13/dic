efflorescence:/ˌefləˈresəns/ | n. | 风化，开花，繁荣 | The state or a period of flowering. An example of efflorescence is when the cherry blossoms come out in Washington D.C. | The building's stonework showed signs of efflorescence. 这座建筑的石雕上出现了风化的迹象。|Synonyms: blooming, blossoming, flowering, flourishing | Antonyms: decline, decay, withering | noun

effloresce:/ˌefləˈres/ | v. |  风化，开花，繁荣 | (of a substance) Lose moisture and turn to a fine powder on exposure to air. To blossom or develop. | The salts effloresced on the surface of the brick. 盐分在砖块表面风化了。| Synonyms: bloom, blossom, flourish | Antonyms: wither, decay | verb
