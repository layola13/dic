fortuitous:/fɔːrˈtjuːɪtəs/| adj.|偶然的，意外的；幸运的|Happening by chance rather than intention, especially in a way that is fortunate or lucky.  Originating from the Latin word "fortuitus," meaning "accidental."  Initially, the word simply meant "happening by chance," but over time, it developed a positive connotation, often implying a lucky accident.|例句：The fortuitous meeting with the producer led to her big break in Hollywood.  与制片人的偶然相遇让她在好莱坞取得了重大突破。

例句：It was fortuitous that he had his umbrella with him that day.  那天他带着伞真是太幸运了。|近义词：lucky, fortunate, accidental, serendipitous, incidental|反义词:planned, intentional, deliberate, unlucky, unfortunate|词性:adjective
