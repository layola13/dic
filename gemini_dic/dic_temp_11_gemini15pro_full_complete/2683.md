adrenalin: /əˈdrɛnəlɪn/ (US) /əˈdrɛnəlɪn/ (UK)| n.| 肾上腺素 | A hormone secreted by the adrenal glands, especially in conditions of stress, increasing rates of blood circulation, breathing, and carbohydrate metabolism and preparing muscles for exertion. |  The sudden danger triggered an adrenalin rush. 突如其来的危险引发了肾上腺素激增。| synonym: epinephrine | antonym: None (Hormones don't typically have direct antonyms.) | noun
