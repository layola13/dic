descriptionist:/dɪˈskrɪpʃənɪst/
n.|描述主义者|Someone who believes that the meaning of a word is determined by how it is commonly used, rather than by any prescriptive rules.|Many linguists are descriptionists, focusing on observing language as it is used.|近义词: descriptivist|反义词: prescriptivist|词性: 名词

Etymology:  From "description" + "-ist". "Description" comes from the Latin "describere" (to write down, copy), from "de-" (down) + "scribere" (to write). "-ist" is a suffix indicating a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or theory.
