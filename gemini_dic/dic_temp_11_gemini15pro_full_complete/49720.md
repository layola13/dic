democratised:/dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzd/ | v. |使民主化，使大众化|To make something accessible to everyone, removing restrictions based on class, wealth, or privilege.  The internet has democratised information, making it available to anyone with a connection.|The invention of the printing press democratised knowledge.|Synonyms: popularized, made accessible, opened up, broadened|Antonyms: restricted, limited, monopolized|verb

democratizing:/dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |使民主化，使大众化（现在分词）|The act of making something accessible to everyone. |The democratizing influence of the internet is undeniable.|Synonyms: popularizing, making accessible, opening up, broadening|Antonyms: restricting, limiting, monopolizing|verb

democratization:/dɪˌmɒkrətɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |民主化，大众化|The process of making something accessible to everyone. |The democratization of education is a key goal in many developing countries.|Synonyms: popularization, accessibility, opening up, broadening|Antonyms: restriction, limitation, monopolization|noun
