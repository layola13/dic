interconfounds: /ˌɪntərˌkɑːnˈfaʊndz/ | v. |相互混淆，互相干扰| To mix up or confuse (variables or factors) in a way that makes it difficult to determine their individual effects. | The study's results were interconfounded by the presence of multiple uncontrolled variables, making it hard to isolate the impact of the intervention. 研究结果由于存在多个不受控制的变量而相互混淆，难以分离出干预措施的影响。|  The researchers failed to account for several key factors, which interconfounded the data and led to inconclusive findings. 研究人员未能考虑到几个关键因素，这些因素相互混淆了数据，导致结论不明确。 | 近义词: confuse, confound, intertwine, complicate | 反义词: clarify, distinguish, separate | 词性: 动词

interconfounds:/ˌɪntərˌkɑːnˈfaʊndz/ | n. |相互混淆的因素| Factors that are mixed up or confused in a way that makes it difficult to determine their individual effects. | The interconfounds in the experiment made it impossible to draw definitive conclusions. 实验中的混杂因素使得无法得出明确的结论。 | Addressing the interconfounds in the study design is crucial for obtaining reliable results. 解决研究设计中的混杂因素对于获得可靠的结果至关重要。 | 近义词: confounding factors, confounding variables | 反义词: controlled variables | 词性: 名词


词源:  "inter-" (between, among) + "confound" (to confuse, mix up). "Confound" comes from the Old French "confondre," ultimately from the Latin "confundere" (to pour together, mix up).
