antiempirically:/ˌæntiɪmˈpɪrɪkli/
| adv. | 反经验地 | In a way that rejects or is opposed to empiricism (the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience). | He argued antiempirically, prioritizing intuition over observation. 他反经验地争论，将直觉置于观察之上。|  He dismissed the findings antiempirically, claiming they contradicted established dogma.  他反经验地驳回了这些发现，声称它们与既定教条相矛盾。| 近义词:  nonempirically, a priori | 反义词: empirically | 词性: 副词
