inhabitednesses:/ɪnˈhæbɪtɪdnɪsɪz/
|n.|居住状态；有人居住的性质|The inhabitednesses of different regions vary greatly depending on climate, resources, and history. 不同地区的居住状态因气候、资源和历史而差异很大。|The study explored the inhabitednesses of several remote islands. 这项研究探讨了几个偏远岛屿的居住状态。|近义词: habitability, occupancy|反义词: uninhabitability, vacancy|词性:名词

Etymology:
From inhabit + -ed + -ness + -es.  "Inhabit" comes from the Latin "inhabitare," meaning "to dwell in."  "-ed" forms the past participle, "-ness" creates a noun denoting a state or quality, and "-es" marks the plural.
