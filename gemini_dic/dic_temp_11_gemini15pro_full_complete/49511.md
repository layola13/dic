demagoguing: /ˈdɛməˌɡɑːɡɪŋ/ | n. | 煽动，蛊惑人心 | The act of appealing to people's prejudices and emotions in order to gain power. | His campaign was marked by blatant demagoguing. 他的竞选活动充斥着公然的煽动行为。| The politician's demagoguing worried many voters.  这位政客的煽动性言论让许多选民感到担忧。 |近义词：rabble-rousing, inflammatory rhetoric, fearmongering |反义词: reasoned discourse, logical argument | 词性: noun (gerund or present participle)

demagogue: /ˈdɛməˌɡɑːɡ/ | n. | 煽动家，蛊惑民心的政客 | A political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument. |  He was accused of being a demagogue who exploited people's fears. 他被指责是一个利用人们恐惧的煽动家。 | History is full of demagogues who rose to power through manipulation. 历史上有很多通过操纵手段上台的煽动家。 | 近义词：agitator, firebrand, rabble-rouser | 反义词: statesman, conciliator | 词性: noun

demagogic: /ˌdɛməˈɡɑːɡɪk/ | adj. | 煽动性的，蛊惑人心的 |  Relating to or characteristic of a demagogue; appealing to people's emotions and prejudices rather than their reason. |  He delivered a demagogic speech filled with empty promises. 他发表了一场充满空洞承诺的煽动性演讲。 | Her demagogic tactics alienated many of her former supporters. 她的煽动策略疏远了许多她以前的追随者。 | 近义词：inflammatory, rabble-rousing, provocative | 反义词: reasoned, logical, conciliatory | 词性: adjective

词源:  From Greek dēmagōgos "leader of the people," from dēmos "people" + agōgos "leading, leader," from agein "to lead."
