omnivorism:/ˈɒmnɪvɔːrɪzəm (UK), ˈɑːmnɪvɔːrɪzəm (US)/| n.|杂食性|The practice of consuming both animal and plant foods as a regular part of one's diet.  It refers to the state or habit of being omnivorous.|Humans demonstrate omnivorism by eating both meat and vegetables.|近义词: polyphagy|反义词: herbivorism, carnivorism|词性: noun

omnivore:/ˈɒmnɪvɔː(r) (UK), ˈɑːmnɪvɔːr (US)/| n.|杂食动物|An animal that habitually eats both animal and plant material.|Bears are a classic example of an omnivore, consuming berries, fish, and insects.|近义词: generalist feeder|反义词: herbivore, carnivore|词性: noun

omnivorous:/ɒmˈnɪvərəs (UK), ɑːmˈnɪvərəs (US)/| adj.|杂食的|Eating both animal and plant foods as a regular part of one's diet. Describes an animal or person that is omnivorous.|Pigs are omnivorous animals and will eat almost anything.|近义词: all-eating|反义词: herbivorous, carnivorous|词性: adjective
