illocution:/ˌɪləˈkjuːʃən/ |n.| 言外之意；言语行为；施为句 |An illocutionary act is the performance of an act in saying something (as opposed to the general act of saying something).  It is what a speaker does in uttering a sentence, such as requesting, promising, apologizing, or threatening. |"By saying 'I do' at a wedding, the speaker performs the illocutionary act of marrying."  "我愿意"这句话在婚礼上不仅仅是说出这句话本身，更是一种结婚的言语行为。|synonyms: speech act, performative utterance, communicative act |antonyms: locution (单纯的说话行为) |noun
