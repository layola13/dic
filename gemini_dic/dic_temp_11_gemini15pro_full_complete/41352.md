corinthians:/kəˈrɪnθiənz/
n.
哥林多信徒；哥林多前后书
A person from the ancient Greek city of Corinth, or a member of the Christian church there.  Specifically refers to the recipients of the New Testament books of 1 and 2 Corinthians.
The Corinthians were rebuked by Paul for their divisions. 保罗责备哥林多信徒的分裂。
