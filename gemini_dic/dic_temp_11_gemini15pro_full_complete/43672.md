credence:/ˈkriːdəns/| n.|信任，相信；凭证|belief, acceptance that something is true|例句：The rumors gained credence when they were repeated in the press.  这些谣言在媒体上反复报道后，得到了更多人的相信。

例句：His theory is given little credence among his peers. 他的理论在他的同行中几乎不被相信。|近义词：belief, trust, faith, confidence, acceptance|反义词：disbelief, skepticism, doubt|词性:noun

词源: From Middle English *credence*, borrowed from Old French *creance* (“belief, trust, faith, confidence; loan, credit”), from *creant* (“believing”), present participle of *creire* (“to believe”), from Latin *crēdō* (“I believe, entrust”).
