circus: /ˈsɜːrkəs/ (US) /ˈsəːkəs/ (UK)| n.|马戏团，马戏表演；喧闹的场面，混乱的活动|A traveling company of acrobats, clowns, and other entertainers who perform stunts and tricks, typically in a large tent.  A situation or place that is characterized by confusion, disorder, and excitement.|例句：The children were thrilled to go to the circus. 孩子们对去看马戏很兴奋。

例句：The office was a complete circus during the holiday rush. 假期高峰期，办公室里一片混乱。|近义词：carnival, spectacle, show, commotion, pandemonium|反义词: order, calm, peace|词性:noun

词源: From Latin *circus* "circle, ring, racecourse," from Greek *kirkos* "ring, circle."
