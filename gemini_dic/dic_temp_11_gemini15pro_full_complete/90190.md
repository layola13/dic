hottentotism: /ˈhɒtəntɒtɪzəm/ (UK) /ˈhɑːtəntɑːtɪzəm/ (US) | n. |  霍屯督人文化；（贬义）原始文化；野蛮行为 |  A term, now considered offensive, historically used to refer to the customs and practices of the Khoekhoe people (formerly called Hottentots) of Southern Africa. It often carried connotations of primitiveness and savagery, reflecting the biased perspectives of European colonizers. |  The term "hottentotism" was used in the past to describe the Khoekhoe people's click consonants, but this usage is now considered derogatory.  过去，“hottentotism”一词被用来描述科伊科伊人的搭嘴辅音，但现在这种用法被认为是贬义的。

The anthropologist's use of "hottentotism" to describe certain cultural practices was met with criticism for its outdated and offensive connotations.  这位人类学家用“hottentotism”来描述某些文化习俗的做法遭到了批评，因为它具有过时和冒犯性的含义。
