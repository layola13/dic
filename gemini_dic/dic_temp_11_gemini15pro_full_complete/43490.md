cratering:/ˈkreɪtərɪŋ/| n. |陨石坑形成；形成坑；坑状疤痕|The process of forming craters, especially by the impact of meteorites or other celestial bodies.  Also refers to the resulting craters themselves or crater-like scars.|例句：The cratering on the moon's surface is evidence of intense bombardment by asteroids. 月球表面的陨石坑是小行星强烈撞击的证据。

例句：The explosion caused extensive cratering in the surrounding area. 爆炸在周围地区造成了大量的坑状疤痕。|近义词：pitting, pocking, scarring|反义词:smoothing, leveling|词性:noun


cratered:/ˈkreɪtərd/| adj. |布满陨石坑的；有坑的|Covered in craters; having a surface marked by craters or crater-like depressions.|例句：The cratered landscape of the moon is a stark reminder of its violent past. 月球上布满陨石坑的地貌 starkly 提醒着人们它暴力的过去。

例句：The old man's face was cratered with wrinkles. 老人的脸上布满了皱纹。|近义词：pitted, pocked, scarred|反义词:smooth, level|词性:adjective
