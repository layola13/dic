what:/wɒt/(British), /wʌt/(American)| pronoun, determiner, adverb|什么，多么|Used to ask for information about people or things.|例句：What is your name? 你叫什么名字？

例句：What a beautiful day! 多么美好的一天！|近义词：which, who |反义词: None (What doesn't have a direct antonym)|pron. det. adv.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hwæt (equivalent to hwā "who" + the neuter singular suffix -t), from Proto-Germanic *hwat, from Proto-Indo-European *kʷod. Cognate with Old Frisian hwet, Old Saxon hwat, Old High German hwaz (German was), Old Norse hvat, Gothic ƕa.  Relates to "who" and "which".
