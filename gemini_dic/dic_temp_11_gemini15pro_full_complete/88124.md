hijras:/ˈhɪdʒrəz/ | n. | 海吉拉人；阉人 |  A term used in South Asia, particularly in India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh, to refer to transgender people, intersex people, or eunuchs assigned male at birth who adopt feminine gender expression. Historically, hijras have held a unique social role, often performing at celebrations and ceremonies. In more recent times, there has been increasing advocacy for the recognition of hijras as a third gender, with legal recognition being granted in some regions. |  In India, hijras are a recognized third gender. 在印度，海吉拉被承认为第三性别。
