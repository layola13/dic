burntness:/ˈbɜːntnɪs/ | n. | 烧焦程度；烧焦 |The burntness of the toast made it inedible.  烤面包的烧焦程度使其无法食用。| English Explanation: The state or quality of being burnt.  It refers to the degree or extent to which something has been burned. | Example: The burntness of the wood was evident in its blackened color. 木头的烧焦程度在其变黑的顏色中显而易见。| Synonym: charring, scorching | Antonym: freshness, rawness | noun

Etymology: From burnt + -ness.  "Burnt" is the past participle of "burn," which comes from Old English "bærnan," of Germanic origin. The suffix "-ness" forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
