discomforts:/dɪsˈkʌmfərts/
|n.|不适，不便，令人不舒服的事物|A state of being uncomfortable physically or mentally; things that cause this state.  A lack of ease and well-being.|The flight was long and filled with discomforts like cramped seats and crying babies. 这趟航班很长，座位狭窄，婴儿啼哭，充满了各种不适。|Synonyms: inconveniences, troubles, annoyances, uneasiness, distress|Antonyms: comforts, ease, conveniences, pleasure|Noun


discomfort:/dɪsˈkʌmfət/
|n.|不适，不便|A state of being uncomfortable physically or mentally. A lack of ease and well-being.|The tight shoes caused him considerable discomfort.  紧的鞋子让他感到相当不舒服。|Synonyms: uneasiness, distress, pain|Antonyms: comfort, ease, relief|Noun


discomfort:/dɪsˈkʌmfət/
|vt.|使不舒服，使不安|To make someone feel uneasy or uncomfortable physically or mentally.|The news of the layoffs discomfited the entire staff.  裁员的消息让全体员工感到不安。|Synonyms: disturb, trouble, upset|Antonyms: comfort, soothe, reassure|Verb
