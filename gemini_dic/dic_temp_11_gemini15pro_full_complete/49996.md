denizenize:/ˈdɛnɪzəˌnaɪz/ | v. | 使成为居民；给予…公民权；使本土化 | To make or become a denizen; to grant citizenship to; to adapt to local conditions or customs. |  Many immigrants are denizenized after living in the country for several years. 许多移民在该国居住数年后获得了公民身份。

The company denizenized its operations by hiring local staff and sourcing materials locally.  这家公司通过雇用当地员工和采购当地材料来实现其业务的本土化。 | 近义词: naturalize, acclimatize, localize | 反义词: alienate, expatriate | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From denizen + -ize.  Denizen comes from Old French *denezein (modern French *d'en-zein*) "from within," from the phrase deinz ein "from within," from Latin de intus "from within." The suffix -ize is a productive verb-forming suffix meaning "to make or become like," "to treat with," or "to subject to the action of."
