beings:/ˈbiːɪŋz/| n. |生物，存在，人|Plural form of "being," referring to entities that exist, especially living creatures, or the state of existing. |例句：All living beings need water. 所有生物都需要水。

例句：Mythical beings populate many ancient stories.  许多古代故事中都有神话生物。|近义词：creatures, entities, individuals, organisms|反义词:non-beings, inanimate objects|词性:Noun (plural)

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *beinges*, plural of *beinge*, from Old English *bēinge*, verbal noun from *bēon* (“to be”).  Cognate with West Frisian *being*, Dutch *zijn* (“being”), German *Sein* (“being”), Swedish *varande* (“being”).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *beunjaną* (“to be, happen, become”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰuH-*.
