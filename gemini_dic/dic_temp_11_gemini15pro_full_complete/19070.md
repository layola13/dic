betook:/bɪˈtʊk/
verb
过去式
(literary or archaic) Go to; resort to.前往；求助于
He betook himself to the library. 他去了图书馆。
近义词:went, proceeded, repaired, resorted
反义词:remained, stayed
词性:verb

词源:
From Middle English *bitoken, from Old English betēon (“to bestow, apply, commit, assign, use, employ, take to oneself, partake of, enjoy, experience, occupy (oneself), give over, deliver, entrust”), from be- + tēon (“to draw, pull, tug, lead, accuse, blame”). Cognate with Old Frisian bitiā (“to offer, show”), Old Saxon bitihan, Old High German bizīhan (“to offer, show, accuse”), Gothic beitēn (“to assign”). More at be-, tow.

PHRASE-> (Betook itself is rarely used in phrases in modern English.  These examples illustrate the general idea of the verb.)

resort to:  The company resorted to layoffs to cut costs. 公司采取裁员来削减成本.
repair to:  After the concert, we repaired to a nearby restaurant. 音乐会结束后，我们去了附近的一家餐馆。
turn to:  When she needed help, she turned to her family. 当她需要帮助时，她向家人求助。
go to: When he felt stressed, he would go to the beach.  当他感到压力时，他会去海滩。
proceed to: After checking in, we proceeded to the boarding gate. 办理登机手续后，我们前往登机口。
