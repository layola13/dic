cytomegaloviruses:/ˌsaɪtoʊˈmɛɡəloʊˌvaɪrəsiz/ | n. | 巨细胞病毒 |  A group of herpesviruses that can cause infections, often without symptoms, but can be serious for people with weakened immune systems, pregnant women, and newborns. | Cytomegaloviruses can cause serious complications in newborns. 巨细胞病毒会导致新生儿出现严重的并发症。 | synonyms: CMV (abbreviation), human herpesvirus 5 (HHV-5) | antonyms: None (viruses don't have direct opposites) | noun
