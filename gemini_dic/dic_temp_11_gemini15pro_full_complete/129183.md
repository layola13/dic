nobble:/ˈnɒbəl/ (British), /ˈnɑːbəl/ (American)| vt.|暗中破坏，阻止，收买|To interfere with (something), especially by underhand or dishonest means; to sabotage or disable. Also, to get hold of (something) for oneself, often by unfair means. |The racehorse was nobbled before the big race.  这匹赛马在大赛前被人做了手脚。

The criminal gang nobbled the jury.  犯罪团伙收买了陪审团。|近义词：sabotage, thwart, fix, bribe, manipulate|反义词: aid, assist, help|词性: verb

nobbles (plural): /ˈnɒbəlz/ (British), /ˈnɑːbəlz/ (American)| vt.|暗中破坏，阻止，收买（复数）|Third-person singular present tense form of "nobble". |He often nobbles his competitors' efforts. 他经常暗中破坏竞争对手的努力。

She nobbles any attempts to change the company policy.  她阻止任何试图改变公司政策的尝试。|近义词：sabotages, thwarts, fixes, bribes, manipulates|反义词: aids, assists, helps|词性: verb


ETYMOLOGY->
Possibly from Romany *nak* "nose," influencing British slang *nob* "head, important person."  The original sense may have been "to hit on the head," later extended to "to disable" and "to cheat."
