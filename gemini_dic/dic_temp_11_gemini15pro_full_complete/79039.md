give-the-hook:/ɡɪv ðə hʊk/
| idiom (v.) |解雇；淘汰；抛弃；停止；终止|To dismiss or reject someone or something, often abruptly or unceremoniously.  It can also mean to end a relationship or stop doing something.|The director gave the actor the hook after he kept forgetting his lines. 导演因为那个演员总是忘词而把他解雇了。|Synonyms: dismiss, fire, sack, reject, ditch, dump, discontinue, terminate|Antonyms: hire, employ, accept, continue, start|idiom

give-the-hook:/ɡɪv ðə hʊk/
| idiom (v.) |赶下台；停止演出|To force a performer to leave the stage, usually because the audience is disapproving.|The crowd was booing so loudly, the manager had to give the comedian the hook.  观众嘘声震天，经理不得不让那个喜剧演员下台。|Synonyms:  pull, remove, eject|Antonyms: introduce, welcome|idiom

词源:
The phrase likely originated in the theatre, where a large hook was sometimes used to literally pull failing performers off the stage.  This literal usage evolved into the figurative meaning of dismissing or rejecting someone or something.
