gullibly:/ˈɡʌləbli/| adv.|轻信地，易受骗地|In a manner that shows a tendency to believe things easily; naively.|He gullibly believed everything the salesman told him. 他轻信了销售员所说的一切。|近义词：naively, credulously, unsuspectingly|反义词：skeptically, cynically, suspiciously|词性:副词
词源: From "gullible" + "-ly." "Gullible" comes from "gull" (verb, meaning to trick or deceive) + "-ible" (suffix meaning capable of).  The word "gull" ultimately comes from a now obsolete word meaning "young bird," perhaps referring to the ease with which young birds can be caught.
