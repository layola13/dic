flypast:/ˈflaɪpɑːst/| n.|低空飞越，空中分列式|A ceremonial flight of aircraft over a location, often in formation.|The Queen's birthday celebrations included a flypast by the Royal Air Force. 女王的生日庆典包括皇家空军的空中分列式。|近义词：flyover, aerial display|反义词: N/A|词性:名词
Etymology:  A compound of "fly" and "past".  "Fly" comes from Old English *flēogan, from Proto-Germanic *fleuganą. "Past" comes from Middle English, from past participle of passen “to pass,” from Old French passer.
