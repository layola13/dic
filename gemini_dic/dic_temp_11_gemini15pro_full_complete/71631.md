flatlining:/ˈflætˌlaɪnɪŋ/
verb|变得毫无生气；停滞不前；垂死挣扎|Becoming stagnant, unresponsive, or nearly dead; ceasing to make progress or show any sign of life.|The company's sales have been flatlining for months.  这家公司的销售额已经连续几个月停滞不前了。|The patient's heart rate started flatlining. 病人的心率开始变平，接近死亡。|stagnating, declining, deteriorating|improving, growing, thriving
verb|（尤指心率）变平，呈直线| (especially of a heart rate) to become level and straight, typically signifying death or near-death.|The doctor watched in horror as the patient's heart monitor began flatlining. 医生惊恐地看着病人心脏监护仪上的线条变平。|His heart flatlined twice during the operation. 手术期间他的心跳两次停止。| ceasing, stopping, failing|recovering, reviving, improving
