anapestic:/ˌænəˈpɛstɪk/ | adj. | 抑抑扬格的，抑抑扬格诗句的 | Of or relating to a metrical foot consisting of two short or unstressed syllables followed by one long or stressed syllable. |  Example:  The poem was written in anapestic tetrameter. 这首诗是用抑抑扬格四音步写的。

Example:  Shakespeare rarely used anapestic meter. 莎士比亚很少使用抑抑扬格韵律。 | Near Synonyms:  tribrachic | Antonyms:  iambic | Word type: adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἀνάπαιστος (anápaistos, “struck back, rebounding”), from ἀναπαίω (anapaíō, “I strike back”), from ἀνά (aná, “up, back”) + παίω (paíō, “I strike”).  The term refers to the "rebounding" rhythm of the meter.
