loaferdom:/ˈloʊfərˌdəm/| n. |懒散，游手好闲|The state of being a loafer; idleness. |He lived a life of luxurious loaferdom, spending his days lounging by the pool. 他过着奢华懒散的生活，整天在泳池边闲逛。|Synonyms: idleness, indolence, laziness, sloth |Antonyms: industry, diligence, activity|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From loafer + -dom. Loafer (n.) "idler," 1830, American English, origin uncertain. Perhaps from Dutch *loffer "vagabond," or German Laufer "runner," used in various special senses (including "messenger; vagrant; ship's rat"). -dom (suffix) Old English -dōm "state, condition, dignity, office, decree," from Proto-Germanic *-dōmaz.
