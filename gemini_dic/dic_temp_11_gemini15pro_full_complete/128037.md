nerd: /nɜːrd/ (British) /nɝːd/ (American) | n. |书呆子，电脑迷 | A person who is extremely enthusiastic and knowledgeable about a particular subject, especially one of specialist or niche interest. Often stereotyped as socially awkward or lacking in style. |  He was a real computer nerd in high school. 他高中时是个十足的电脑迷。 | synonyms: geek, dweeb, wonk | antonyms: jock, social butterfly | noun


nerd: /nɜːrd/ (British) /nɝːd/ (American) | adj. |书呆子气的 | Characteristic of a nerd. | That's such a nerd thing to say!  那话听起来真书呆子气！| synonyms: geeky, dweeby | antonyms: cool, hip | adjective

词源：
The origin of "nerd" is uncertain, but one popular theory traces it back to Dr. Seuss's 1950 book *If I Ran the Zoo*, which features a creature called a "nerd."  However, the word's current meaning didn't solidify until the 1960s and 1970s.
