low-keys: /ˌloʊˈkiːz/ |  adj. adv. (informal, often used in African American Vernacular English (AAVE)) | 低调的，不张扬的；冷静的，轻松的 |  Similar to "low-key," but often used as a plural adjective, especially in AAVE.  It emphasizes a relaxed, chill vibe or a desire to keep something understated. | 例句: "The party was low-keys; just a few friends hanging out."  派对很低调，就几个朋友一起玩。 | 例句: "Keep it low-keys, I don't want anyone to know." 保持低调，我不想让任何人知道。 | 近义词: low-key, chill, relaxed, understated | 反义词:  high-profile, ostentatious, dramatic | 词性: 形容词, 副词


词源: "Low-key" originated in the early 20th century, initially used in photography and film to describe subdued lighting.  It later broadened in meaning to describe something understated or restrained. The pluralized form "low-keys" is a more recent development, primarily associated with AAVE.
