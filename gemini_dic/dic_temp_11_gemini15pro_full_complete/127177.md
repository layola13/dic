naturalized:/ˈnætʃrəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 归化的；入籍的；引进的；移植的 |  Admitted to citizenship; made to feel or seem natural; adapted to a new environment. |  Example: He became a naturalized citizen of the United States. 他成为了美国公民。

Example:  The plant has become naturalized in the area. 这种植物已在该地区驯化。 | 近义词: adopted, acclimatized, integrated | 反义词: alien, foreign, indigenous | 词性: 形容词


naturalize:/ˈnætʃrəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使归化；移植；吸收 | To grant full citizenship to (a foreigner); to introduce (organisms) into a region and cause them to flourish as if native. | Example: The government naturalized many immigrants. 政府使许多移民归化。

Example:  They are trying to naturalize these exotic plants. 他们正试图移植这些外来植物。| 近义词: acclimatize, adapt, assimilate | 反义词: expatriate | 词性: 动词


词源: From Late Latin *naturalizatus, past participle of naturalizare “to naturalize,” from Latin naturalis “natural” (see natural).
