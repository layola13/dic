hallmark:/ˈhɔːlmɑːrk/| n.| 标志，特征，戳记|A distinctive feature, especially one of quality or excellence.  A mark stamped on articles of gold, silver, or platinum by the British assay offices, guaranteeing the standard of purity of the metal.| The intricate carvings are a hallmark of this artist's work. 这些精细的雕刻是这位艺术家的标志。| synonyms:  characteristic, trademark, indication, sign, emblem, badge, symbol,  | antonyms:  anonymity, obscurity | noun

hallmark:/ˈhɔːlmɑːrk/| vt.| 给…打上纯度标记，使…具有标志性特征 | To stamp with a hallmark; to be a typical feature or characteristic of.| The elegant design hallmarks all of her creations.  优雅的设计是她所有作品的标志。| synonyms:  characterize, distinguish, mark, identify | antonyms:  conceal, hide | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *heallmerc "hall-mark," a mark or stamp indicating quality, applied by a guildhall.
