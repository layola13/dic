grotesque:/ɡroʊˈtɛsk/ | adj. | 怪诞的，荒谬的，奇形怪状的 | Characterized by bizarre distortions, especially in appearance; ludicrously odd. | The gargoyle on the old church was truly grotesque.  教堂上的滴水嘴兽真是怪诞。 | synonyms: bizarre, distorted, misshapen, outlandish, preposterous, absurd | antonyms: beautiful, normal, ordinary, pleasing, symmetrical | adjective

grotesque:/ɡroʊˈtɛsk/ | n. | 怪诞的图案（或雕塑等），怪诞风格 | A style of ornamentation characterized by fanciful or fantastic human and animal forms often interwoven with foliage or similar figures that may distort the natural into absurdity, ugliness, or caricature. |  The artist specialized in grotesques, creating nightmarish visions in clay.  这位艺术家专攻怪诞风格，用粘土创作噩梦般的景象。 | synonyms: caricature, monstrosity | antonyms: beauty, normality | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *grotesque*, from Italian *grottesco* "of a grotto," from *grotta* "grotto, cave."  The style was so named because it was first discovered in the ruins of Nero's Golden House in Rome, where the rooms were thought to be caves (*grotte*) at the time of their rediscovery.
