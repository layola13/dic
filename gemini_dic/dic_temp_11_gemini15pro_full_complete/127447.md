nebular:/ˈnɛbjʊlə(r)/ /ˈnɛbjələr/| adj.|星云的；云状的；朦胧的|Relating to a nebula or nebulae; cloud-like, hazy, vague.|例句：The nebular hypothesis explains the formation of the solar system. 星云假说解释了太阳系的形成。|例句：His explanation was rather nebular and difficult to understand. 他的解释相当模糊，难以理解。|近义词：hazy, cloudy, misty, vague, indistinct|反义词：clear, distinct, sharp, precise|词性:形容词

nebula:/ˈnɛbjʊlə/| n.|星云|A cloud of gas and dust in outer space, visible in the night sky either as an indistinct bright patch or as a dark silhouette against other luminous matter.|例句：The Orion Nebula is a well-known example of a diffuse nebula. 猎户座星云是一个著名的弥漫星云的例子。|例句：Astronomers study nebulae to learn about the life cycle of stars. 天文学家研究星云以了解恒星的生命周期。|近义词：cloud (in space)|反义词：(not applicable)|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *nebula, from Latin nebula (“mist, vapor, fog, cloud, exhalation”), from PIE *nébʰōl, from root *nébʰ- (“cloud”). Cognate with Ancient Greek νέφος (néphos, “cloud”), Sanskrit nábhas (“mist, cloud, sky, atmosphere”), German Nebel (“fog”).
