methodistically:/ˌmɛθəˈdɪstɪkli/ | adv. | 有条不紊地，系统地 | In a methodical and systematic manner; according to a fixed plan or system. | She methodistically cleaned the entire house, room by room. 她有条不紊地打扫了整个房子，一间一间地打扫。|  She methodistically organized her notes before the exam. 她有条不紊地在考试前整理笔记。 | 近义词：systematically, orderly, diligently | 反义词：haphazardly, randomly, carelessly | 词性: 副词

methodical:/məˈθɒdɪkəl/ | adj. | 有条理的，井然的 | Done according to a systematic or established procedure. | He has a methodical approach to problem-solving. 他解决问题的方法很有条理。 |  She is a methodical worker and always finishes her tasks on time. 她工作很有条理，总是按时完成任务。 | 近义词：systematic, organized, orderly | 反义词：disorganized, chaotic, random | 词性: 形容词

method:/ˈmɛθəd/ | n. | 方法，方式 | A particular form of procedure for accomplishing or approaching something, especially a systematic or established one. |  The new teaching method has proved very effective.  新的教学方法证明非常有效。 | There are several methods of cooking rice.  煮饭有几种方法。 | 近义词：way, technique, procedure, approach | 反义词： (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑chaos, disorder表示缺乏方法的状态) | 词性: 名词


词源:  From Late Latin methodicus, from Greek methodos "way of teaching or going," from meta- "after, among, with" + hodos "way, road."
