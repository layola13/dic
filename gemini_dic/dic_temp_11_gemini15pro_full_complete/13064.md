astrolabes:/ˈæstrəˌleɪbz/
n.
星盘
An instrument formerly used to make astronomical measurements, typically of the altitudes of celestial bodies, and in navigation for calculating latitude, before the development of the sextant.
Early astronomers used astrolabes to observe and calculate the positions of stars. 早期的天文学家使用星盘来观测和计算恒星的位置。

astrolabes:/ˈæstrəˌleɪbz/
n.
星盘
A historical astronomical instrument used for observing and calculating the positions of celestial bodies.
Mariners relied on astrolabes for navigation before the invention of more modern instruments. 在更现代的仪器发明之前，航海家依靠星盘进行导航。

近义词:armillary sphere, quadrant
反义词:None
词性:Noun

词源: From Old French astrelabe, from Medieval Latin astrolabium, from Greek astrolabon, from astron "star" + lambanein "to take."
