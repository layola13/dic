deen:/diːn/
n.|喧嚣，喧闹; 山谷; (苏格兰)峡谷|A loud, confused noise; a din.  A valley, especially a wooded one (chiefly Scottish).|The deen of the city was overwhelming. 城市的喧嚣震耳欲聋。|The hikers descended into the deen.  徒步旅行者们下山进入了峡谷。|近义词：din, clamor, uproar, valley, glen|反义词：silence, quiet, hill|词性: 名词

词源:  From Middle English *dēne*, from Old English *dēn* ("valley"), from Proto-Germanic *danjaz* (“valley”). Cognate with Old Frisian *dene*, Middle Dutch *dēne*, *dēn*, Dutch *dein* (“dune”), Old High German *tenni* (“fir”), German *Tenne* (“threshing floor”).  Related also to Old English *dunn* (“hill, down”).  The sense of "loud noise" is a later development.
