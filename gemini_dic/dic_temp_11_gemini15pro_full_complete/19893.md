binomialism:/baɪˈnoʊmiəlɪzəm/| n. |二名法；双名命名法；二项式；二项式定理|A system of nomenclature in which each species of animal or plant receives a name of two terms of which the first identifies the genus to which it belongs and the second the species itself.  |Example 1: The binomialism system is crucial for classifying species in biology.  二名法系统对于生物学中物种的分类至关重要。

Example 2:  Linnaeus established the system of binomialism in the 18th century.  林奈在18世纪建立了二名法系统。|Synonym:  binomial nomenclature, binary nomenclature|Antonym:  monomial|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From bi- (two) + nomial (name) + -ism (system or doctrine).  Ultimately from Latin bīnōmius (having two names) and Greek ὄνομα (ónoma, name).
