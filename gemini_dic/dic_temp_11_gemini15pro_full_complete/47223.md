dazed:/deɪzd/| adj.| 茫然的，恍惚的，震惊的 |  stunned or confused and slow to react, typically as a result of a blow or shock. |  He looked dazed after the car accident. 他在车祸后看起来很茫然。| synonyms: stunned, bewildered, confused, disoriented | antonyms: alert, aware, focused, clear-headed | adjective

daze:/deɪz/| n. | 茫然，恍惚 | A state of stunned confusion or bewilderment. | She was in a daze after hearing the news. 听到这个消息后，她处于一种茫然的状态。| synonyms: stupor, trance, bewilderment | antonyms: alertness, awareness | noun

daze:/deɪz/| vt. | 使茫然，使恍惚 | To stun or confuse someone. | The blow to the head dazed him. 头部受到的打击使他茫然了。| synonyms: stun, bewilder, confuse, disorient | antonyms: enlighten, clarify | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *dasa (“to become exhausted”), from Proto-Germanic *dasaną (“to become tired”). Cognate with Swedish dasa (“to be lazy, sluggish”), Icelandic dasa (“to doze”).
