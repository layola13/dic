catabaptist:/ˌkætəˈbæptɪst/ | n. | 再洗礼派教徒 | A member of a radical Protestant sect that arose in Europe in the 16th century, rejecting infant baptism and advocating adult baptism, separation of church and state, and pacifism. |  The catabaptists were persecuted for their beliefs. 再洗礼派教徒因其信仰而受到迫害。| synonyms: Anabaptist | antonyms: N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *catabaptista, from Greek katabaptistēs, from katabaptízein "to rebaptize," from kata- "down" + baptízein "to baptize."
