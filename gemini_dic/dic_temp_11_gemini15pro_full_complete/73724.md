forked:/fɔːrkt/ /fɔːrkt/| v.|（使）分叉，叉开（fork 的过去式和过去分词）|divided into two or more branches; (of a road or path) divided into two or more branches|The road forked and we took the left path. 路分叉了，我们走了左边的路。|After the storm, the tree had forked into two separate trunks. 暴风雨过后，这棵树分叉成了两根独立的树干。|branched, divided|merged, joined|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *forcian (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *furkōjaną (compare West Frisian forkie "to fork, dig"), from *furkō "fork". Cognate with Dutch vorken ("to fork"), German forken ("to pitch with a fork").
