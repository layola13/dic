no-gooder:/ˈnoʊˌɡʊdər/| n.| 无用的人；窝囊废；坏蛋|A person who is useless, worthless, or bad. |He's a no-gooder who's never held down a job. 他是个从没保住过工作的窝囊废。|Synonyms: good-for-nothing, ne'er-do-well, scoundrel |Antonyms: achiever, go-getter, valuable person| noun


Etymology:  American English, formed by combining "no good" with the agentive suffix "-er."  "No good" as an adjective meaning "worthless" or "useless" dates back to the 19th century.
