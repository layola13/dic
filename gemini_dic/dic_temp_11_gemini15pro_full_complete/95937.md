incubating: /ˈɪŋkjʊˌbeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 孵化的；潜伏的；酝酿的 |  Developing slowly and not yet showing any significant effect or fully formed. |  The project is still incubating, but we expect big things from it.  这个项目仍在酝酿中，但我们期待它会有很大的发展。 | synonyms: developing, gestating, hatching, nurturing | antonyms: declining, stagnating, suppressing, mature, developed | adjective

incubate: /ˈɪŋkjʊˌbeɪt/ | v. | 孵化；培养；潜伏；酝酿 | (of a bird) sit on (eggs) in order to hatch them; (of an egg or eggs) hatch; develop slowly and not yet showing any significant effect or fully formed; maintain (something) at a suitable temperature for development, especially (of bacteria or a cell culture) in order to promote growth. | The female bird incubates the eggs for about three weeks.  雌鸟孵蛋大约三周。 | synonyms: cultivate, foster, nurture, develop, hatch | antonyms: neglect, suppress, stifle, kill | verb

incubation: /ˌɪŋkjʊˈbeɪʃən/ | n. | 孵化；潜伏期；培养 | The process of incubating eggs, cells, etc.; the development of something over a period of time. | The incubation period for this disease is two to three weeks. 这种疾病的潜伏期是两到三周。 | synonyms: development, gestation, hatching | antonyms: decline, stagnation, suppression | noun
