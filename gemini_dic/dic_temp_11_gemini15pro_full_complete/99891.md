internalizing:/ɪnˈtɜːrnəlaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 内化的；将（情感、想法等）藏于心中 | The process of making attitudes or behavior part of one's nature. | He was internalizing his anger and frustration. 他把愤怒和沮丧藏在心里。 | 近义词：incorporating, assimilating, integrating | 反义词：externalizing, expressing | 词性: adjective

internalize:/ɪnˈtɜːrnəlaɪz/ | v. | 内化；将（情感、想法等）藏于心中 | To make (attitudes or behavior) part of one's nature. |  She had internalized her parents' values. 她已经将父母的价值观内化于心。 | 近义词：incorporate, assimilate, integrate | 反义词：externalize, express | 词性: verb

词源: From internal + -ize.  "internal" comes from Late Latin "internalis," from "internus" meaning "inward, internal,"  and "-ize" is a suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become."
