conductorship:/kənˈdʌktərʃɪp/
n.
指挥(职位); 乐队指挥的职位
the position or period of being a conductor.
Example: He held the conductorship of the Berlin Philharmonic for many years.  他担任柏林爱乐乐团的指挥多年。

Example: Her conductorship was marked by innovative programming. 她的指挥以创新的节目编排而著称。

Synonym: leadership, direction, baton
Antonym:  (No direct antonym, but consider "subordination", "followership" in a broader sense)
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "conductor" + "-ship".  "Conductor" comes from the Late Latin "conductor," meaning "leader," from "conducere" (to lead together),  from "com-" (together) + "ducere" (to lead). "-ship" is an Old English suffix denoting state, condition, or office.
