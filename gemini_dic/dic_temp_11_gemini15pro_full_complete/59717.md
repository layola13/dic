eclecticism: /ɪˈklɛktɪsɪzəm/
n.
折衷主义；博采众长
the practice of deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.
Her home décor reflects her eclecticism, mixing antique furniture with modern art. 她的家居装饰反映了她的折衷主义，将古董家具与现代艺术相结合。
synonym:  pluralism, syncretism,  catholicity
antonym:  narrowness, dogmatism,  purism
noun

eclectic: /ɪˈklɛktɪk/
adj.
折衷的；兼收并蓄的
deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.
He has an eclectic taste in music, enjoying everything from classical to hip-hop. 他对音乐的品味不拘一格，从古典音乐到嘻哈音乐，什么都喜欢。
synonym: diverse, varied, assorted, broad, wide-ranging
antonym: narrow, limited, restricted, specific
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *eclecticus, from Ancient Greek ἐκλεκτικός (eklektikós, “selective”), from ἐκλεκτός (eklektós, “selected”), from ἐκλέγω (eklégō, “I pick out, choose”), from ἐκ (ek, “out”) + λέγω (légō, “I gather, collect, say”).
