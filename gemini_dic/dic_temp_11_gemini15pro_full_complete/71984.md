flincher:/ˈflɪntʃər/| n. |退缩者，畏缩的人|Someone who flinches (draws back or away) from something unpleasant or painful, physical or emotional.|He was no flincher when it came to difficult decisions.  在做困难的决定时，他毫不退缩。|Synonyms: coward, shirker, weakling|Antonyms: hero, brave, stalwart|noun

flinch:/flɪntʃ/ | v. |畏缩，退缩| To make a sudden, involuntary movement of the body, typically as a result of pain, fear, or surprise. |He flinched when the doctor gave him an injection. 当医生给他打针时，他畏缩了一下。|Synonyms: wince, recoil, shrink|Antonyms: stand firm, face, confront|verb
