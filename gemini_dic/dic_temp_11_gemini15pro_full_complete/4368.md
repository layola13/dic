词源：From Middle English *alack a day*, from *alack* + *a day*.  *Alack* is from *a* + *lak*, from Old Norse *lak*, meaning "loss, lack, fault".  Therefore, the original meaning was roughly "loss of a day," expressing regret or sorrow.
