breccia:/ˈbrɛtʃə/ (US), /ˈbrɛtʃɪə/ (UK)| n. |角砾岩|A rock composed of sharp-angled fragments embedded in a fine-grained matrix. It is formed by the consolidation of angular rock fragments, often at the base of a cliff.|The geologist examined the breccia, noting the various rock types within it. 地质学家检查了角砾岩，注意到其中各种各样的岩石类型。

The museum displayed a beautiful piece of breccia from the Dolomites. 博物馆展出了一块来自多洛米蒂山脉的美丽的角砾岩。|近义词:conglomerate, rubble |反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps a uniform, fine-grained rock like shale) |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian "breccia," meaning "broken fragments," from Vulgar Latin *breccia, of uncertain origin, possibly from a Germanic source.
