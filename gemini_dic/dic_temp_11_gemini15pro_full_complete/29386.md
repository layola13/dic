casuistries:/ˈkæʒuɪstriːz/
|n.|诡辩，决疑法，案例分析|The study of cases of conscience and the resolution of moral problems by the application of general ethical principles.  It can sometimes have a negative connotation, implying the use of clever but unsound reasoning, especially in relation to moral questions.|The lawyer's casuistries obscured the basic injustice of the case. 律师的诡辩掩盖了案件根本的不公正。|近义词：sophistry, quibbling, equivocation|反义词：directness, honesty, forthrightness|词性:noun

词源:
From Late Latin *casuista* "resolver of cases of conscience," from Latin casus "case" (from PIE root *kad- "to fall, befall").
