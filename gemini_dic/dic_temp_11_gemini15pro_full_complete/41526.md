coronary arteries: /ˈkɔːrəˌneri ˈɑːrtəriz/ (US) /ˈkɒrəˌnəri ˈɑːtəriz/ (UK)| n.|冠状动脉|The arteries that supply blood to the heart muscle itself.  They branch off from the aorta, just beyond the aortic valve.| Blockage in the coronary arteries can lead to a heart attack. 冠状动脉阻塞会导致心脏病发作。|Synonyms: cardiac arteries|Antonyms: N/A|noun

词源:  "coronary" comes from the Latin word "coronarius," meaning "of a crown," referring to the way the arteries encircle the heart like a crown.  "Artery" comes from the Greek word "artēria," originally meaning "windpipe" but later used to refer to arteries because they were thought to carry air.
