halloed:/ˈhæləʊd/ (UK) /ˈhæloʊd/ (US)| v. |被誉为，被尊为；(尤指宗教人物或圣地)视为神圣|Made holy; regarded as holy (especially of religious people or places).|The abbey was halloed ground for generations. 这座修道院几代以来都被视为圣地。| 近义词: revered, venerated, consecrated, sanctified | 反义词: desecrated, profaned | 词性: 动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *halowien, *halwen*, from Old English *hālgian* ("to hallow, consecrate, sanctify, make holy, ordain"), from Proto-Germanic *hailagōną* (“to hallow”), from Proto-Germanic *hailagaz* (“holy”). Cognate with West Frisian *helgje* (“to hallow”), Dutch *heiligen* (“to hallow, sanctify, canonize”), German *heiligen* (“to hallow, sanctify, canonize”), Swedish *helga* (“to hallow, sanctify, consecrate”). Related to holy.
