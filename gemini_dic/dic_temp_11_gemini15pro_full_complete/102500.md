jacobitic:/ˌdʒækəˈbɪtɪk/ | adj. n. | 雅各布派的，雅各布派支持者 | Relating to or supporting James II of England and his descendants' claim to the throne, or the movement to restore them.  Also, a supporter of this movement. |  例句：He had strong Jacobitic sympathies. 他对雅各布派抱有强烈的同情。 |  例句：The rising of 1745 was the last major Jacobitic rebellion. 1745年的起义是最后一次主要的雅各布派叛乱。 | 近义词：Jacobite, royalist,  | 反义词：Whig, Hanoverian | 词性:形容词,名词

词源:
From the Late Latin *Jacobitēs* (supporters of James),  derived from *Jacobus*, the Latin form of James.  James II of England (Latin name Jacobus II) was deposed in 1688, and his descendants, known as the Jacobites, attempted to regain the throne.
