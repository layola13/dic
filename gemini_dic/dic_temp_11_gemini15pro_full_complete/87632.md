hewed:/hjud/ | v. | 砍，劈，凿；坚持；遵守 | to chop or cut something with a sharp tool, especially an axe; to adhere to | The sculptor hewed the statue from a single block of marble. 雕塑家从一整块大理石上凿出雕像。  He hewed to his principles despite the pressure. 尽管有压力，他仍然坚持自己的原则。 | synonyms: chopped, shaped, carved, cleaved, adhered, conformed | antonyms: joined, combined, ignored, abandoned | verb

hewn:/hjun/ | adj. | 砍成的，凿成的 | made or shaped by cutting or chopping |  The table was made of roughly hewn planks. 这张桌子是用粗凿的木板制成的。 | synonyms: chopped, shaped, carved, cleaved | antonyms: smooth, polished, molded | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->

From Old English *hēowan (attested in the past participle gehēawen), from Proto-Germanic *hauwaną, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱewp- (“to hit, strike”). Cognate with Old Saxon hawan, Old Norse höggva, Old High German houwan, German hauen, Dutch houwen.
