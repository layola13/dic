bourdon:/ˈbʊrdən/ /bɔːrdɔ̃/ | n. | 低音管;嗡嗡声;熊蜂;权杖 | A deep, resonant sound, like that of a bumblebee or a large bell; a drone. Also, a bass pipe of an organ that produces such a sound.  In older usage, it can also refer to a pilgrim's staff. | The bourdon of the organ filled the cathedral.  低音管的声音充满了大教堂。| 近义词:drone, hum, buzz, staff | 反义词:silence, quiet | 词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *bordon*, from Late Latin *burdo*, meaning "mule," then coming to mean the humming sound a mule makes. The sense of "pilgrim's staff" likely derives from the staff's resemblance to a large insect.  The musical sense developed from the low humming sound.
