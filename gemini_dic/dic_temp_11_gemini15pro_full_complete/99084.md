interbrain:/ˈɪntərbreɪn/| n.|间脑|The interbrain, also known as the diencephalon, is a part of the forebrain located between the cerebral hemispheres and the midbrain. It contains the thalamus, hypothalamus, epithalamus, and subthalamus, which play crucial roles in sensory processing, hormone regulation, and autonomic functions.|The interbrain relays sensory information to the cerebral cortex. 间脑将感觉信息传递到大脑皮层。|Synonyms: diencephalon|Antonyms: None|Noun

Etymology: From inter- + brain.  "Inter-" is a prefix meaning "between" or "among."
