oches:/ˈɔːtʃɪz/ | n. | oches (French) | terraces, tiers, rows (especially in a theatre or stadium).  A raised area, as in a theater, for seating spectators. |  The oches were packed for the opening night of the opera.  歌剧的首演之夜，楼座座无虚席。| synonyms: tiers, balconies, galleries | antonyms: stage, pit, floor | noun


Etymology->
From French *oches*, plural of *oche*, from Old French *oche*, from Frankish *aukja* "increase, height". Related to Old High German *auh*, German *auch* "also".
