interdiction:/ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃn/ | /ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃən/
n.|禁止，禁令|An act of forbidding something; a prohibition.|The government placed an interdiction on the sale of alcohol. 政府禁止销售酒精。|prohibition, ban, veto|allowance, permission|noun


interdiction:/ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃn/ | /ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃən/
n.|阻断，封锁|The act of intercepting and preventing the movement of something.|The air force implemented an interdiction campaign to disrupt enemy supply lines. 空军实施了阻断行动以切断敌人的补给线。|blockade, obstruction, siege|access, opening|noun


Etymology: From Late Latin *interdictiō*, from Latin *interdictus*, past participle of *interdīcere* "to forbid," from *inter-* "between" + *dīcere* "to say, speak."
