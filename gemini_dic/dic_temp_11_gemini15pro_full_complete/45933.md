cuttlefish:/ˈkʌt(ə)lfɪʃ/| n.|墨鱼，乌贼|A marine mollusk with eight arms and two tentacles, typically able to change color.  It has an internal shell (the cuttlebone).|例句：The cuttlefish squirted ink to escape the predator. 墨鱼喷出墨汁以躲避捕食者。

例句：Cuttlefish are masters of camouflage.  乌贼是伪装大师。|近义词：squid, cephalopod|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


cuttlefishes:/ˈkʌt(ə)lfɪʃɪz/| n.|墨鱼，乌贼（复数）|The plural form of cuttlefish.|例句：The divers observed several cuttlefishes changing color. 潜水员观察到几只墨鱼改变颜色。

例句：Cuttlefishes are known for their intelligence. 乌贼以它们的智力而闻名。|近义词：squid, cephalopods|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cudelfishe, from Old English *cudele, ultimately from Late Latin *cuticula (“little skin, cuticle”) (perhaps referring to the cuttlebone) + fish.  The change from -dele to -tle likely arose from influence of Old Norse koddi (“cushion, pillow”).
