blackguardism:/ˈblækɡɑːrdɪzəm/| n.|无赖行为；流氓作风| Unscrupulous or dishonorable conduct; villainy.|  His blackguardism cost him his job and his reputation. 他的无赖行为让他丢了工作和名声。|近义词：villainy, scoundrelism, rascality|反义词：honor, integrity|词性:noun

blackguard:/ˈblækɡɑːrd/| n.|恶棍，无赖|A scoundrel; a villain.|He was known as a blackguard in the town. 在镇上，他被称为无赖。|近义词：villain, scoundrel, rogue|反义词：gentleman, hero|词性:noun


词源:
From black guard, originally referring to the lowest menials in a large household, such as kitchen help, who wore dirty clothes or black livery.  Over time, the term evolved to describe someone morally reprehensible.
