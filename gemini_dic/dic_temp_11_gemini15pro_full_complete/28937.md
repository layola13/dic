carronade:/ˈkærəneɪd/| n.|卡隆炮|A short, smoothbore cannon of large caliber, characterized by its light weight and relatively small charge, typically used in naval warfare during the late 18th and early 19th centuries.|The carronade was effective at close range but lacked the accuracy of longer cannons.|近义词：cannon, gun|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "musket" for a different type of weapon)|noun
