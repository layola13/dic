morph:/mɔːrf/ | v. n. | 变形，变体 | To change smoothly from one image to another by small gradual steps, or a shape or form resulting from such a change.  | Example: The caterpillar will morph into a butterfly. 毛毛虫会变成蝴蝶。 | Example:  The software can morph one face into another.  这个软件可以将一张脸变形为另一张脸。 | Synonyms: transform, change, mutate, metamorphose | Antonyms:  remain, stay, fix, stagnate | Verb, Noun

morph:/mɔːrf/ | n. |  (语言学)词素 | A meaningful unit of language that cannot be further divided. | Example: "Unbreakable" has three morphs: "un-", "break", and "-able".  “Unbreakable”有三个词素：“un-”，“break”和“-able”。 | Example:  The study of morphs is crucial in morphology. 词素的研究在形态学中至关重要。 | Synonyms: morpheme | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym) | Noun
