misincline:/ˌmɪsɪnˈklaɪn/ | v. | 使不情愿，使厌恶 | To make someone unwilling or averse to something. |  He was misinclined to accept the offer after hearing the terms.  他听到条件后不愿意接受这个提议。

The rainy weather misinclined her to go out. 下雨的天气使她不愿外出。 | 近义词: disincline, discourage, deter | 反义词: incline, encourage, motivate | 词性: 动词

词源:  From mis- (expressing wrongness or lack) + incline (to tend or lean towards).
