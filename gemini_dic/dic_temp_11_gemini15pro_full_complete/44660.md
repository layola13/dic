crucially:/ˈkruːʃəli/| adv.|至关重要地，关键地|In a crucial way; decisively.  It indicates something is of great importance or a deciding factor.|例句：Crucially, the evidence supports our hypothesis. 关键的是，证据支持我们的假设。|近义词：importantly, significantly, vitally, essentially|反义词:insignificantly, trivially|词性:副词
Etymology: From crucial + -ly.  Crucial comes from Late Latin crucialis "cross-shaped," from Latin crux "cross." The sense development likely arose from the image of a crossroads as a decisive point.
