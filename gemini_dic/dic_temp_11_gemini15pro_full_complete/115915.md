marooning:/məˈruːnɪŋ/
|n.| 遗弃; 使处于孤立无援的境地|The act of leaving someone trapped and isolated in an inaccessible place, especially an island.|例句：The sailors feared marooning on a deserted island.  水手们害怕被困在一个荒岛上。|近义词：abandonment, stranding, isolating|反义词:rescuing, saving|词性:名词

maroon:/məˈruːn/
|vt.|使孤立; 使处于孤立无援的境地|To leave someone trapped and isolated in an inaccessible place, especially an island.|例句：The pirates marooned their captain on a small island. 海盗把他们的船长遗弃在一个小岛上。|近义词：abandon, strand, isolate|反义词:rescue, save|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *marroner*, from Spanish *cimarron* "wild, untamed," referring to runaway slaves.  Originally referred to leaving someone in the wilderness, especially runaway slaves.  The connection to islands developed later.
