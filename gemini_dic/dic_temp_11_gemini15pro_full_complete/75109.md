fringy:/ˈfrɪndʒi/| adj.|边缘的，另类的，不重要的|Unconventional, marginal, or extreme, especially in social or political views.  Relating to or characteristic of a fringe group. |Her fringy political views often put her at odds with her family. 她的边缘政治观点经常使她与家人发生冲突。|The band has a fringy, experimental sound.  这支乐队的音乐风格另类，充满实验性。|近义词：unconventional, alternative, marginal, eccentric, offbeat, quirky, bohemian|反义词：mainstream, conventional, orthodox, traditional|词性:形容词

fringe:/frɪndʒ/| n.|边缘，穗，刘海|The outer or less important part of an area, group, or activity; a decorative border of hanging threads, cords, or strips, typically on a garment or curtain; a border of hair cut short and straight across the forehead.|She was always on the fringe of the popular group in school.  她在学校总是处于受欢迎群体的边缘。|The dress had a fringe of silk around the hem. 这件衣服的底边有一圈丝绸流苏。|近义词：border, edge, periphery, rim, margin, outskirts, fringe benefit|反义词：center, core, middle|词性:名词
