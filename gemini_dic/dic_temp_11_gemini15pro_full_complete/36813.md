colonialisms: /kəˈloʊniəlɪzəmz/
n.
殖民主义（复数）
The policies and practices of colonialism, especially as seen in the exploitation and domination of a weaker country by a stronger one.  It often refers to multiple instances of colonialism, or different types of colonial practices across different times and places.
The conference explored the lasting impacts of European colonialisms in Africa.  这次会议探讨了欧洲殖民主义在非洲的持久影响。
The speaker highlighted the various colonialisms that shaped the modern world. 演讲者强调了塑造现代世界的各种殖民主义。
近义词: imperialism, colonization
反义词: independence, decolonization
词性: noun


Etymology:
From colonialism + -s (plural suffix). Colonialism is derived from colony + -ism. Colony comes from the Latin colonia, meaning "farm, landed estate, settlement."  The suffix -ism denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice.
