coagulating:/koʊˈæɡjʊleɪtɪŋ/
verb
凝固，凝结
The process of changing from a liquid to a semisolid or solid state.
Blood began coagulating around the wound. 血液开始在伤口周围凝结。
synonyms: clotting, thickening, congealing
antonyms: liquefying, dissolving, thinning
v.

coagulate:/koʊˈæɡjʊleɪt/
verb
凝固，凝结
To change from a liquid to a semisolid or solid state.
The milk coagulated when we added lemon juice.  我们加了柠檬汁后，牛奶凝固了。
synonyms: clot, thicken, congeal
antonyms: liquefy, dissolve, thin
v.

coagulation:/koʊˌæɡjʊˈleɪʃən/
noun
凝固，凝结
The process of changing from a liquid to a semisolid or solid state.
The doctor was concerned about the slow coagulation of the patient's blood. 医生担心病人血液凝固缓慢。
synonyms: clotting, thickening, congealing
antonyms: liquefaction, dissolution, thinning
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin coagulātus, past participle of coagulāre (“to curdle, congeal”), from Latin co- (“together”) + agere (“to drive, do, act”).
