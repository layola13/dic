intrusive:/ɪnˈtruːsɪv/
|adj.|侵入的；打扰的；唐突的|Tending to intrude; coming without invitation or welcome. |The loud music from the party next door was very intrusive. 隔壁聚会传来的吵闹音乐很扰人。|Synonyms: interfering, meddlesome, obtrusive|Antonyms: discreet, respectful, unobtrusive|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English intrusen, from late Latin intrusus, past participle of intrudere (“to thrust in”), from in- (“in, into”) + trudere (“to thrust, push”).
