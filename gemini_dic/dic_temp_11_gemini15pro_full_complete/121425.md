misinformed:/ˌmɪsɪnˈfɔːrmd/| adj.|被误导的，信息不正确的|Having received or given incorrect information.|例句：The public was misinformed about the true nature of the event. 公众被误导了关于该事件的真实性质。|例句：She was misinformed about the deadline and submitted her application late. 她被告知了错误的截止日期，所以提交申请晚了。|近义词：deceived, misled, misguided|反义词：informed, educated, enlightened|词性:形容词
ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (expressing wrongness or error) + inform (to give knowledge of something to).  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old English "mis-" meaning "wrongly, badly, amiss." "Inform" comes from Old French "enformer" and ultimately from Latin "informare" meaning "to shape, train, instruct."
