brusquer:/ˈbrʌskər/ | adj. | 更粗鲁的，更唐突的 | More abrupt, blunt, and curt in manner than someone or something else. |  He was even brusquer than usual this morning. 他今天早上比平时更粗鲁。|近义词: curter, blunter, sharper, ruder, gruffer | 反义词: gentler, kinder, politer, more courteous | 词性: 形容词

brusque:/brʌsk/ | adj. | 唐突的，鲁莽的 | Abrupt or offhand in speech or manner. |  She asked for a day off and received a brusque refusal. 她请了一天假，却遭到断然拒绝。| 近义词: abrupt, curt, blunt, short, sharp, terse, gruff, rude, impolite, discourteous | 反义词: polite, courteous, gentle, kind | 词性: 形容词

词源: From French "brusque," from Italian "brusco" meaning "sour, tart," probably of Germanic origin.
