compartmentalize:/kəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlaɪz/
verb
划分；分隔；区分对待
To separate something into isolated compartments or categories, often as a coping mechanism.
She compartmentalizes her work life and her personal life, never letting the two overlap. 她将工作和生活划分开来，从不让两者重叠。
He compartmentalized his emotions after the trauma, rarely discussing his feelings.  他在遭受创伤后将自己的情绪区分开来，很少谈论自己的感受。
近义词: categorize, classify, separate, isolate, pigeonhole
反义词: integrate, combine, unify, merge
词性: verb

compartmentalization:/kəmˌpɑːrtˌmentələˈzeɪʃən/
noun
划分；分隔；区分对待
The act of compartmentalizing something.
The compartmentalization of her life helped her cope with stress.  将她生活划分开来有助于她应对压力。
Excessive compartmentalization can hinder personal growth. 过度区分对待会阻碍个人成长。
近义词: categorization, classification, separation, isolation
反义词: integration, combination, unification
词性: noun

compartmentalized:/kəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlaɪzd/
adjective
被划分开的；被区分对待的
Existing in or characterized by separate compartments or categories.
Her work life is highly compartmentalized. 她的工作生活被高度划分。
He has a compartmentalized mind, able to focus on one task at a time. 他有一个区分对待的头脑，能够一次专注于一项任务。
近义词: separated, categorized, classified, isolated
反义词: integrated, combined, unified
词性: adjective


词源: From compartment + -alize.  "compartment" 源自法语 "compartiment,"  意思是“分隔，隔间”。 后缀 "-alize"  表示“使...化”。
