carbonado:/ˌkɑːrbəˈnɑːdoʊ/ /ˌkɑːbəˈneɪdoʊ/ | n. | 卡巴度黑金刚石，黑金刚石 | A black, opaque, and granular aggregate of microscopic diamond crystals, found chiefly in Brazil and used as an abrasive. |  The drill bit was tipped with carbonado for extra strength.  钻头上装有黑金刚石以增强强度。| The jeweler examined the rare carbonado. 珠宝商检查了稀有的黑金刚石。 | Synonyms: black diamond, industrial diamond | Antonyms:  N/A | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Portuguese *carbonado* ("burnt," "carbonized"), past participle of *carbonar* ("to burn to charcoal"), from Latin *carbo* ("charcoal").  The name refers to its dark, burnt appearance.
