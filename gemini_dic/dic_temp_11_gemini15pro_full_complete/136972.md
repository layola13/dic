nonspeculativeness:/ˌnɒnspekjʊlətɪvnəs/
|n.|非投机性，不投机取巧|The nonspeculativeness of their investment strategy appealed to conservative clients. 他们的非投机性投资策略吸引了保守的客户。|nonspeculativeness describes the quality of not being based on speculation or conjecture; it implies a focus on facts and concrete evidence rather than risky assumptions or guesses.|Her nonspeculativeness in business dealings earned her a reputation for reliability. 她在商业交易中的不投机行为为她赢得了可靠的声誉。|近义词：pragmatism, practicality, realism|反义词：speculativeness, riskiness, conjecture|词性:noun

Etymology:
From non- + speculativeness.  Speculative comes from Late Latin speculativus, from Latin speculatus, past participle of speculari "to look at, observe," from specula "watchtower, lookout," from specere "to look at."
