nickel-and-diming:/ˈnɪkəl ən ˈdaɪmɪŋ/
| adj. |斤斤计较的；小气抠门的；通过收取许多小额费用来赚钱的|Charging many small amounts of money, often seen as unfair or exploitative.  The practice of charging small amounts of money for things that used to be free or included in a larger price.|The airline's new baggage fees felt like nickel-and-diming.|近义词:penny-pinching, cheap, stingy|反义词:generous, extravagant|词性:形容词

nickel-and-dime:/ˈnɪkəl ən ˈdaɪm/
| v. |斤斤计较；小气抠门；通过收取许多小额费用来赚钱|To charge many small amounts of money, often seen as unfair or exploitative.|The hotel nickel-and-dimed us with extra charges for everything from Wi-Fi to bottled water.|近义词:chisel, gouge|反义词:splurge|词性:动词

词源:  This term originated in the late 19th century in the United States, referring to the small denominations of the nickel (five cents) and dime (ten cents).  It initially described inexpensive items or establishments but evolved to describe the practice of accumulating small charges that add up to a significant amount.
