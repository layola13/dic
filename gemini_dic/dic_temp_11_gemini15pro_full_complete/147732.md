overprizes: /ˌoʊvərˈpraɪzɪz/
verb
定价过高；要价过高
charges too high a price for.
例句：Some retailers are accused of overpricing essential goods during natural disasters.  一些零售商被指控在自然灾害期间对必需品定价过高。

例句：The restaurant overprizes its wine, making it less appealing to customers.  这家餐馆的葡萄酒定价过高，对顾客的吸引力降低了。
近义词：overcharge, inflate, gouge
反义词：underprice, discount
词性：v.


overpriced: /ˌoʊvərˈpraɪst/
adjective
定价过高的；价格过高的
too expensive; costing more than it is worth.
例句：The handbags in that boutique are overpriced.  那家精品店的包包定价过高。

例句：Many consumers feel that new smartphones are often overpriced. 许多消费者认为新款智能手机通常定价过高。
近义词：expensive, exorbitant, costly
反义词：cheap, affordable, inexpensive
词性：adj.
