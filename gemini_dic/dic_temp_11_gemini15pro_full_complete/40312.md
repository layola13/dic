contortionist:/kənˈtɔːrʃənɪst/| n. |柔术演员，软骨功演员|A person, especially a performer, who is skilled in contorting their body into unusual positions.|The contortionist twisted her body into an unbelievable shape. 柔术演员将她的身体扭曲成一个令人难以置信的形状。|Synonyms: acrobat, gymnast, flexible person|Antonyms: stiff person, inflexible person|noun

contortion:/kənˈtɔːrʃən/| n. |扭曲，变形|The act of twisting or deforming something out of its natural shape.|The contortion of the metal was caused by the extreme heat. 金属的变形是由极端高温引起的。|Synonyms: twisting, bending, warping, deformation|Antonyms: straightening, flattening|noun

contort:/kənˈtɔːrt/| v. |扭曲，变形|To twist or deform something out of its natural shape.|His face was contorted in agony. 他的脸因痛苦而扭曲。|Synonyms: twist, bend, warp, deform|Antonyms: straighten, flatten|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contortionem, from Latin contortus (past participle of contorquēre ‘twist violently’), from con- ‘together’ + torquēre ‘twist’.
