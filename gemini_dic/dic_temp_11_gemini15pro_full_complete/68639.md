faquir:/fəˈkɪər/ | n. |  托钵僧；苦行僧 | A Muslim or Hindu religious ascetic who lives by begging. |  The faquir sat cross-legged on the mat, meditating. 托钵僧盘腿坐在垫子上冥想。 |  ascetic, mendicant | monk, priest | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Portuguese *faquir*, from Arabic *faqīr* (“poor, needy”), from *faqr* (“poverty”).
