cauliculus: /kɔːˈlɪkjələs/ | n. |  胚茎；子叶柄；上丘 | The part of a plant embryo or seedling below the cotyledons that develops into the stem. In neuroanatomy, a part of the midbrain involved in auditory processing (specifically the inferior colliculus) and in motor control (specifically the superior colliculus).|  The seedling emerged, its cauliculus pushing upwards. 幼苗破土而出，它的胚茎向上生长。| synonyms: hypocotyl (in botany), brain stem (in neuroanatomy, broadly) | antonyms: plumule (in botany, refers to the embryonic shoot) | noun
