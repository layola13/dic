antibodies:/ˈæntibɒdiz/ (UK) /ˈæntibɑːdiz/ (US)| n.|抗体|Proteins produced by the body's immune system to neutralize or destroy harmful substances (antigens) such as bacteria, viruses, or toxins.|The antibodies attacked the infection. 抗体攻击了感染。|Synonyms: immunoglobulin, antiserum|Antonyms: antigen|Noun

antibody:/ˈæntibɒdi/ (UK) /ˈæntibɑːdi/ (US)| n.|抗体|A blood protein produced in response to and counteracting a specific antigen. Antibodies combine chemically with substances which the body recognizes as alien, such as bacteria, viruses, and foreign substances in the blood.|Each antibody is highly specific for a single antigen.  每种抗体对单一抗原具有高度特异性。|Synonyms: immunoglobulin, antiserum|Antonyms: antigen|Noun
