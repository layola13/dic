lackey: /ˈlæki/ | n. | 仆从，走狗，跟班 | a servant, especially a liveried footman or manservant; a person who follows another's bidding servilely |  The dictator surrounded himself with lackeys who did his every bidding.  独裁者身边都是唯命是从的走狗。 | synonyms: servant, footman, flunky, toady, minion, sycophant | antonyms: master, boss, leader | noun

lackey: /ˈlæki/ | v. |  充当走狗，卑躬屈膝 | to act as a lackey; to behave servilely | He lackeyed for the wealthy businessman, hoping to gain favor. 他为那个富商卑躬屈膝，希望得到好处。| synonyms: fawn, grovel, kowtow | antonyms: lead, command, direct | verb
