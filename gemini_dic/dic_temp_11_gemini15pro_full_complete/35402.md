co-opt:/koʊˈɑːpt/ | vt. |吸收；拉拢；指派 | To add to a group or organization; to elect into a body by the votes of the existing members. | The committee co-opted three new members. 委员会吸收了三名新成员。 |  The rebels co-opted several prominent citizens into their government. 叛军拉拢了几位杰出的公民加入他们的政府。 | 近义词: elect, appoint, select, choose | 反义词: reject, exclude, dismiss | 词性: 动词

co-opt:/koʊˈɑːpt/ | n. | 指派; 拉拢; 吸收 | The act of co-opting. | The co-opt of the local businessman onto the council was seen as a strategic move. 将当地商人吸收进议会这一举动被视为一项战略举措。 | The co-opt of new members brought fresh perspectives to the team.  吸收新成员为团队带来了新的视角。 | 近义词: appointment, selection | 反义词: rejection, dismissal | 词性: 名词


词源:  From Latin *co-optāre*, from *co-* (together) + *optāre* (to choose, wish, desire).
