opioid:/ˈōpɪɔɪd/| n. adj.|类鸦片；阿片样物质|A synthetic or naturally occurring chemical substance that acts on opioid receptors in the body, producing morphine-like effects, often used to relieve pain.|The doctor prescribed an opioid for her severe back pain. 医生给她开了类鸦片药物来治疗严重的背痛。|Synonyms: opiate, narcotic|Antonyms: stimulant|noun, adjective
