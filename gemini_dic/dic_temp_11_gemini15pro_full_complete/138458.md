novel:/ˈnɒvəl/ (British) /ˈnɑːvəl/ (American)| n. adj.|小说；新奇的|A novel is a fictitious prose narrative of book length, typically representing character and action with some degree of realism.|例句：She is reading a novel by Jane Austen. 她正在读一本简·奥斯汀的小说。

例句：His first novel was published last year. 他的第一部小说去年出版了。|近义词：book, story, fiction, narrative|反义词：fact, truth|词性: 名词

novel:/ˈnɒvəl/ (British) /ˈnɑːvəl/ (American)| adj.|新奇的；新颖的|New and original, not like anything seen before.|例句：That's a novel idea! 那真是个新奇的想法！

例句：They used a novel approach to solve the problem. 他们用一种新颖的方法解决了这个问题。|近义词：original, innovative, fresh, new, unusual|反义词：common, ordinary, familiar, traditional|词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *novel, from Latin novellus “new, young, recent,” diminutive of novus “new.”  The literary sense developed from the Italian novella, referring to a short tale, part of a collection.
