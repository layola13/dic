acor:/ˈeɪkɔːr/ | n. |橡子，橡实 | An acorn is the nut of an oak tree. | Example: Squirrels bury acorns for the winter. 松鼠把橡子埋起来过冬。 | Example:  The acorn eventually grows into a mighty oak. 小小的橡子最终会长成参天橡树。 | Synonym: oaknut | Antonym: N/A | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *æcern (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *akraną ("acorn, fruit, berry"), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eǵ- ("sharp; bitter"). Cognate with Old Saxon akarn, Old High German akarn, Old Norse akarn, Gothic akran ("fruit, berry").
