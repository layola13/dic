impassivities:/ɪmˌpæsɪˈvɪtiz/| n. | 冷漠，无动于衷，缺乏感情 |  Lack of emotion or feeling; absence of passion or excitement.  It refers to a state of being unresponsive or unmoved by external stimuli. |  His impassivities during the crisis was unsettling. 他在危机期间的冷漠令人不安。 | The witness recounted the events with an unnerving impassivity. 目击者以令人不安的冷漠叙述了事件。|  apathy, indifference, stoicism, aloofness, detachment |  emotionality, passion, fervor, enthusiasm, excitability | noun


Etymology:
From Late Latin *impassivitas, from impassivus ("impassive"), from in- ("not") + passivus ("passive," from pati "to suffer, endure"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kwenth- (“to suffer”).
