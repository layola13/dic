modalist:/ˈmoʊdəlɪst/| n.|形态论者；形态主义者|A person who adheres to Modalism, a theological doctrine that emphasizes the oneness of God and denies the distinct persons of the Trinity, viewing Father, Son, and Holy Spirit as different modes or manifestations of the same divine being.|Modalists believe that God is one person who has revealed himself in different ways throughout history.|近义词：Sabellian, Monarchian|反义词：Trinitarian|词性:noun
