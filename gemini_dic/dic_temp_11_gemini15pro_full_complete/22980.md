botulism:/ˈbɒtʃʊlɪzəm/ (US) /ˈbɒtjʊlɪzəm/ (UK)| n. | 肉毒中毒 | A rare but serious paralytic illness caused by a nerve toxin that is produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum. | Example: The improperly canned food caused a severe case of botulism.  这罐头食品处理不当导致了严重的肉毒中毒。| Example:  Symptoms of botulism include blurred vision and difficulty swallowing. 肉毒中毒的症状包括视力模糊和吞咽困难。 | Synonym:  botulinus poisoning, food poisoning (in some cases) | Antonym:  None (Botulism is a specific illness, not a concept with an opposite.) | Noun
