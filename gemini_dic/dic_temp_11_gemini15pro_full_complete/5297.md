allotropic:/ˌæləˈtrɒpɪk/ | adj. |同素异形体的| Relating to or exhibiting allotropy; existing in different forms, in the same physical state, but with different physical properties.|Diamond and graphite are allotropic forms of carbon. 钻石和石墨是碳的同素异形体。|synonyms: polymorphic, isomeric | antonyms: monomorphic | adjective

allotropy:/əˈlɒtrəpi/ | n. |同素异形|The existence of an element in two or more different forms, in the same physical state, but with different physical properties.|Carbon exhibits allotropy, occurring as diamond, graphite, and fullerenes. 碳表现出同素异形，以金刚石、石墨和富勒烯的形式存在。|synonyms: polymorphism, isomerism | antonyms: monomorphism | noun
