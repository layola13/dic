gib:/dʒɪb/| n. |钩状物，挂钩；（动物的）嗉囊；（起重机等的）臂架；(gibs)猫的爪子|A gib is a triangular sail set on a small mast behind the foremast of a two-masted vessel.|The crane's gib swung out over the construction site. 起重机的臂架在建筑工地上方旋转。|Synonyms: hook, claw, arm, boom|Antonyms: N/A|noun

gib:/dʒɪb/| v. |阉割（雄猫）；去势|To gib a cat is to castrate it.|The farmer gibbed the tomcat to prevent unwanted kittens. 农夫阉割了公猫以防止不必要的幼猫出生。|Synonyms: castrate, neuter|Antonyms: N/A|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gibbe*, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old Norse *gegg*, meaning "pointed weapon". The nautical sense likely arose from the sail's pointed shape.
