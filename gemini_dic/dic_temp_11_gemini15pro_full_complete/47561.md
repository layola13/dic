debacchate:/ˌdɛbəˈkeɪt/ | v. | 狂欢，纵酒，放荡 | To indulge in excessive drinking and revelry; to act in a wild, unrestrained manner. | The partygoers debacchated until dawn.  狂欢者们纵情狂欢直到天亮。|Synonyms: revel, carouse, roister, indulge | Antonyms: abstain, refrain, behave, control | verb

debacchate:/ˌdɛbəˈkeɪt/ | n. | 狂欢，放荡 | An instance of excessive drinking and revelry. | The debacchate lasted for three days. 这场狂欢持续了三天。|Synonyms: revelry, carousal, orgy | Antonyms: abstinence, restraint, sobriety | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *dēbacchārī*, from *dē-* (down, away) + *Bacchus* (the Roman god of wine and revelry).  The word literally means "to behave like Bacchus is gone," implying a loss of restraint and control.
