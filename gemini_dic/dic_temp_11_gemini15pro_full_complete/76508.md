galeas:/ˈɡeɪliəs/ | n. | 加莱赛战船；大型桨帆船 | A large, low-built Mediterranean galley or war vessel, common from the 15th to 17th centuries, propelled by both oars and sails, often used for trade or as a flagship. | The Venetian galeas, armed with cannons, dominated the Adriatic Sea. 威尼斯加莱赛战船，装备着大炮，统治着亚得里亚海。 | synonyms: galley, warship | antonyms: dinghy, lifeboat | noun
