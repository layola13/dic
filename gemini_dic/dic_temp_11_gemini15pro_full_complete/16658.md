barracuda:/ˌbærəˈkuːdə/ | n. |梭鱼；奸诈的人|A large, predatory marine fish found in tropical and subtropical waters, known for its aggressive behavior and sharp teeth.  It can also metaphorically refer to a ruthless and opportunistic person.|例句：The barracuda flashed its sharp teeth. 梭鱼露出了锋利的牙齿。

例句：He's a real barracuda in the business world. 在商界，他是个十足的狠角色。|近义词：pike, mackerel, predator (figurative)|反义词: prey (figurative) |词性:noun


barracudas:/ˌbærəˈkuːdəz/ | n. |梭鱼（复数）；奸诈的人（复数）|Plural form of barracuda.  Refers to multiple barracuda fish or multiple individuals who are ruthless and opportunistic.|例句：Several barracudas were circling the reef. 几条梭鱼在环绕着珊瑚礁。

例句：The barracudas of Wall Street are always looking for the next big deal. 华尔街的狠角色们总是在寻找下一个大买卖。|近义词：pikes, mackerels, predators (figurative)|反义词: prey (figurative) |词性:noun
