apomorphine:/əˈpɑːmɔːrfɪːn/| n.|阿朴吗啡|A morphine derivative, C17H17NO2, that is a potent emetic and dopamine agonist used chiefly in the treatment of Parkinson's disease.|Apomorphine is used to treat Parkinson's disease.|近义词:dopamine agonist (as a class), levodopa|反义词:dopamine antagonist|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From apo- + morphine.  Apo-  means "derived from" or "related to." Morphine is named after Morpheus, the Greek god of dreams, due to its sleep-inducing properties.
