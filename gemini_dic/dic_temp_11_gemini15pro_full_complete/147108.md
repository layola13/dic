overlingers:/ˈoʊvərˌlɪŋɡərz/| n.|逗留者；流连忘返的人|People who stay longer than expected or necessary.  Those who linger.|The overlingers at the party finally left at 2 a.m.  派对上逗留的人最后在凌晨2点离开了。|近义词：loiterers, dawdlers, lingerers|反义词:leavers,goers|词性:noun


Etymology: From "over" + "linger" + "-er".  "Over" emphasizes the excessive nature of the lingering. "Linger" comes from the Middle English *lengen*, meaning "to reside, tarry," from the Old English *lengian*. "-er" is a suffix denoting a person who performs the action of the verb.
