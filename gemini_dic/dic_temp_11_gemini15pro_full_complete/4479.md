albian:/ˈælbiən/| adj. n.|阿尔比阶的，阿尔比期|Relating to or denoting the Albian stage of the Early Cretaceous epoch, or the system of rocks deposited during this time.  It is also used as a noun to refer to this period itself. |The Albian stage followed the Aptian and preceded the Cenomanian. 阿尔比阶位于阿普特阶之后，塞诺曼阶之前。

The ammonite fossils found in these rocks are characteristic of the Albian. 在这些岩石中发现的菊石化石是阿尔比阶的特征。|近义词：Early Cretaceous, Lower Cretaceous|反义词: N/A|词性：adjective, noun

Etymology: From Latin *Albia*, "white," referring to the white chalk cliffs of Dover, England, which are partly of Albian age.  The term was introduced by French geologist Alcide d'Orbigny in 1842. 源于拉丁语 *Albia*，意为“白色”，指的是英国多佛的白色垩土悬崖，部分属于阿尔比阶。该术语由法国地质学家阿尔西德·多比尼于1842年引入。
