holster:/ˈhəʊlstər/ (UK) /ˈhoʊlstər/ (US)| n. |皮套，枪套|A case, typically made of leather, for a pistol or other firearm, that is worn on a belt or strapped to the body.|The cowboy drew his gun from its holster. 牛仔从枪套里拔出了枪。|Synonyms: sheath, case, pouch|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, could consider "exposed" as an antonymic state for the weapon)|noun


holster:/ˈhəʊlstər/ (UK) /ˈhoʊlstər/ (US)| vt. |把…装入皮套|To place (something, especially a pistol) in a holster.|He holstered his weapon and walked away. 他把武器装回枪套，走开了。|Synonyms: sheathe, encase|Antonyms: unsheathe, draw, unholster|verb
