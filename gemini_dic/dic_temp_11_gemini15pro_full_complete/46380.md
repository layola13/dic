dado:/ˈdeɪdoʊ/| n.|护墙板， dado rail|A dado is the lower part of a wall, usually decorated differently from the upper part. |The dado in the dining room was paneled in dark oak. 餐厅的护墙板是用深色橡木镶板的。|synonyms: wainscot, baseboard|antonyms:  (No direct antonym, perhaps "upper wall")|noun

dado:/ˈdeɪdoʊ/| n.|（建筑）多面体，正方体；骰子；基座|A dado can also refer to a cube, especially in architecture or a die.  It can also refer to a pedestal or base.|The columns rested on a dado of carved stone.  这些柱子立在一个雕刻的石头基座上。|synonyms: cube, die, plinth, pedestal|antonyms: (No direct antonym)|noun

dado:/ˈdeɪdoʊ/| n.|槽，沟；榫头|A dado can be a groove or trench cut into a piece of wood to receive the edge of another piece, or a tenon. |He cut a dado in the shelf to fit the support bracket. 他在架子上切了一个槽来安装支撑架。|synonyms: groove, trench, slot, recess|antonyms: projection, protrusion|noun
