guardianships:/ˈɡɑːrdiənʃɪps/ | n. | 监护权；监护(权) | The legal right and responsibility to care for someone who cannot care for themselves, such as a child or someone with a disability. |  The court awarded her guardianship of her nephew. 法院授予了她对其侄子的监护权。

| 近义词: custodianship, wardship, care, protection | 反义词:  neglect, abandonment | 词性: 名词

guardianship:/ˈɡɑːrdiənʃɪp/ | n. | 监护；守护 | The state of being a guardian; the office or function of a guardian. |  He was given guardianship of the estate until the heir came of age.  他被授予管理遗产的监护权，直到继承人成年。

| 近义词: custodianship, wardship, safekeeping | 反义词:  neglect, abandonment | 词性: 名词
