farces:/ˈfɑːrsɪz/| n. |滑稽剧；闹剧|A light dramatic work in which highly improbable plot situations, exaggerated characters, and often slapstick elements are used for humorous effect.|The play was a complete farce, with ridiculous costumes and silly jokes.  这出戏完全是一场闹剧，服装滑稽可笑，笑话也愚蠢至极。|近义词：comedy, burlesque, parody|反义词：tragedy, drama|noun

farce:/ˈfɑːrs/| n. |滑稽剧；闹剧|A light dramatic work in which highly improbable plot situations, exaggerated characters, and often slapstick elements are used for humorous effect.|The whole election campaign has been a farce. 整个竞选活动简直就是一场闹剧。|近义词：comedy, burlesque, parody|反义词：tragedy, drama|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *farsir* "to stuff," from Latin *farcire* "to stuff."  Originally referred to comic interludes "stuffed" into religious plays.  The meaning later shifted to refer to any broadly humorous play.
