intercalate: /ɪnˈtɜːrkəleɪt/ (UK) /ɪnˈtɜːrkəˌleɪt/ (US)| n.|插入物|Something intercalated, especially an extra day or month.|例句：The intercalate in the lunar calendar keeps it aligned with the solar year. 阴历中的闰月使其与太阳年保持一致。|近义词：insertion, addition|反义词: removal, deletion|词性: noun


词源: From Latin intercalātus, past participle of intercalāre (“to proclaim (an extra day),” literally “to call between”), from inter- (“between”) + calāre (“to proclaim, call”).
