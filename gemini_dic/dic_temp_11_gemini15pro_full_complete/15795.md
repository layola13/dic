balkanises:/ˈbɔːlkənaɪzɪz/ /ˈbɑːlkənaɪzɪz/| v. |巴尔干化|To divide (a region or body) into smaller mutually hostile states or groups.|例句：The country was balkanised after the war.  战争结束后，这个国家分裂成几个敌对的小国。

|例句：Social media has been accused of balkanising public opinion. 社交媒体被指责分裂了公众舆论。|近义词：fragment, divide, split, partition|反义词：unite, integrate, consolidate|词性:动词

balkanizes (American English spelling):/ˈbɔːlkənaɪzɪz/ /ˈbɑːlkənaɪzɪz/| v. |巴尔干化|To divide (a region or body) into smaller mutually hostile states or groups.|例句：Analysts fear the region will be further balkanized. 分析人士担心该地区将进一步分裂。


|例句：The internet has the potential to both connect and balkanize societies. 互联网既有可能连接社会，也有可能使其分裂。|近义词：fragment, divide, split, partition|反义词：unite, integrate, consolidate|词性:动词


词源: From the Balkans, a region in southeastern Europe that has historically been divided into many small, often warring, states.  源自巴尔干半岛，欧洲东南部的一个地区，历史上一直分裂成许多小的、经常交战的国家。
