degeneracy:/dɪˈdʒɛnərəsi/ | n. | 退化；堕落；简并性 | A decline in quality, or the state of having declined in quality, especially in moral or cultural standards.  In physics, it refers to a situation where two or more different quantum states have the same energy. | The degeneracy of modern society is alarming. 现代社会的堕落令人担忧。|  The degeneracy of the genetic code refers to the fact that multiple codons can code for the same amino acid. 遗传密码的简并性指的是多个密码子可以编码相同的氨基酸。 | Synonyms: decline, deterioration, degradation, corruption, depravity | Antonyms:  improvement, elevation, progress | noun
