discriminative:/dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv/| adj.|区别的，歧视的，有识别力的|Making or showing a distinction; distinguishing.  Able to recognize or understand the difference between things.|例句：She has a discriminative taste in music. 她对音乐的品味很独特。|近义词：distinctive, discerning, selective, perceptive|反义词：indiscriminate, unselective|词性:形容词

discriminative:/dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv/| adj.|区别的，歧视的，有识别力的|Making or showing a distinction; distinguishing. Able to see subtle differences.  Can also, in some contexts, imply unfairly biased.|例句：The algorithm is discriminative and can identify spam emails accurately.  这个算法具有识别力，可以准确识别垃圾邮件。|近义词：astute, insightful, judicious|反义词：undiscerning, uncritical|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *discriminativus*, from Latin discriminat-, past participle stem of discriminare "to divide, separate, distinguish," from discrimen "interval, difference," from discernere "to distinguish between," from dis- "apart" + cernere "to distinguish, separate, sift."
