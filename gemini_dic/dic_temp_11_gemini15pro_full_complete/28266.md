captiousness:/ˈkæpʃəsnəs/
n.
吹毛求疵，挑剔
A disposition to find fault; a tendency to raise trivial objections.
His captiousness made it difficult to work with him. 他的吹毛求疵让人很难和他共事。
Synonyms: faultfinding, carping, quibbling, nitpicking, caviling
Antonyms: tolerance, acceptance, broad-mindedness, open-mindedness, magnanimity
noun


captious:/ˈkæpʃəs/
adj.
吹毛求疵的，挑剔的
Tending to find and call attention to faults.
She made a series of captious remarks about his presentation. 她对他的演讲提出了一系列吹毛求疵的评论。
Synonyms: faultfinding, carping, quibbling, nitpicking, caviling
Antonyms: tolerant, accepting, broad-minded, open-minded, magnanimous
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *captiosus "fallacious, deceitful," from Latin captio "a taking, catching," from capere "to take, seize."
