holistic-medicine:/həˈlɪstɪk ˈmɛdəsɪn/| n.|整体医学|A system of medicine that considers the whole person -- physical, mental, spiritual, and emotional -- in the treatment of illness.|Example 1: She turned to holistic medicine after conventional treatments failed to alleviate her chronic pain. 她转向了整体医学，因为传统的治疗方法未能减轻她的慢性疼痛。

Example 2: Holistic medicine emphasizes preventative care and lifestyle changes. 整体医学强调预防保健和生活方式的改变。|近义词:alternative medicine, complementary medicine, integrative medicine|反义词:conventional medicine, allopathic medicine|词性:noun


holistic:/həˈlɪstɪk/| adj.|整体的，全面的|Characterized by the belief that the parts of something are intimately interconnected and explicable only by reference to the whole.|Example 1: He took a holistic approach to his health, focusing on diet, exercise, and mindfulness. 他对自己的健康采取了整体的方法，注重饮食、锻炼和正念。

Example 2: The holistic design of the building considered its impact on the surrounding environment. 这座建筑的整体设计考虑了它对周围环境的影响。|近义词:comprehensive, integrated, whole|反义词:partial, fragmented, segmented|词性:adjective


medicine:/ˈmɛdəsɪn/| n.|医学，药|The science or practice of the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of disease.|Example 1:  She is studying medicine at Harvard University. 她在哈佛大学学习医学。

Example 2: He takes medicine for his high blood pressure. 他服用药物治疗高血压。|近义词:medication, drug, remedy|反义词:poison, toxin|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
holistic: from Greek *holos* meaning "whole"
medicine: from Old French *medecine*  ultimately from Latin *medicina* meaning "the healing art"
