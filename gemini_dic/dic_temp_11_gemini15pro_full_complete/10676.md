approximant:/əˈprɒksɪmənt/ (US) /əˈprɒksɪmənt/ (UK)| n. |近似音；近似值；近似物|A sound that is produced by bringing one articulator close to another, causing a narrowing of the vocal tract, but without creating turbulent airflow.  In phonetics, approximants include liquids (like /l/ and /r/) and glides (like /j/ and /w/).|The sounds /l/, /r/, /w/, and /j/ are approximants.|近义词:liquid, glide, semi-vowel|反义词:obstruent, fricative|noun

approximants:/əˈprɒksɪmənts/ (US) /əˈprɒksɪmənts/ (UK)| n. |近似音；近似值；近似物（复数）|Plural form of approximant.|The approximants in the word "yellow" are /j/ and /l/.|近义词:liquids, glides, semi-vowels|反义词:obstruents, fricatives|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *approximans, present participle of approximare (“to approach”), from Latin ad (“to”) + proximare (“to draw near”), from proximus (“nearest”).
