lobby:/ˈlɒbi/ (UK) /ˈlɑːbi/ (US)| n. v.|大厅，休息室；游说，疏通|A large room inside the entrance of a public building such as a hotel, theatre, or office building.  To try to persuade a politician or official group to do something.|The protesters gathered in the lobby of the parliament building. 抗议者聚集在议会大楼的大厅里.|synonyms: foyer, entrance hall, anteroom, waiting area|antonyms:  (v.) dissuade, discourage|noun, verb
