half-deprecating: /ˌhɑːf ˈdɛprɪkeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 半自嘲的，略带自谦的 | Expressing mild disapproval of oneself, often humorously; modestly understating one's own abilities or achievements.  |  He gave a half-deprecating smile as he accepted the award. 他在接受奖项时露出了略带自谦的微笑。 |  He spoke of his accomplishments in a half-deprecating way. 他以一种半自嘲的方式谈论他的成就。| Synonyms: self-effacing, modest, unassuming | Antonyms: boastful, arrogant, conceited | adjective

Etymology:
"half-" (Old English healf) + "deprecating" (from Latin deprecatus, past participle of deprecari "to avert by prayer," from de- "down" + precari "to pray")
