musketoon:/ˌmʌskɪˈtuːn/| n. |滑膛枪，短管火枪|A short-barreled firearm, typically smoothbore, used in the 17th and 18th centuries, often by cavalry or naval boarding parties.  It was lighter and easier to handle than a full-length musket.|The musketeer traded his cumbersome musket for a more manageable musketoon before boarding the enemy ship.  火枪手在登上敌舰之前，将笨重的火枪换成了更容易操作的滑膛枪。|Synonyms: blunderbuss, carbine|Antonyms: musket, rifle|noun
