legitimizing:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmaɪzɪŋ/
verb, present participle
使…合法化; 使…正当化
The process of making something legitimate or lawful.  This can involve formally recognizing something, justifying it, or making it conform to rules or standards.
The government is legitimizing the use of force. 政府正在将使用武力合法化。
The report sought to legitimize their actions. 该报告试图使他们的行为合法化。
近义词: validating, justifying, authorizing, sanctioning, ratifying
反义词: invalidating, condemning, denouncing, prohibiting, banning
词性: verb (present participle)

legitimate: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmət/
adjective
合法的; 正当的; 合理的
Conforming to the law or to rules.  Also, able to be defended with logic or justification; valid.
He had a legitimate claim to the throne. 他有继承王位的合法权利。
Her complaints were quite legitimate. 她的抱怨是完全合理的。
近义词: legal, lawful, valid, justifiable, reasonable
反义词: illegal, unlawful, invalid, unjustifiable, unreasonable
词性: adjective

legitimacy: /ləˈdʒɪtɪməsi/
noun
合法性; 正统性
The state or quality of being legitimate.  This can refer to conforming to the law, being in accordance with established rules or principles, or being generally accepted or recognized.
The legitimacy of the election results is being questioned.  选举结果的合法性受到质疑。
The study explored the legitimacy of government power. 该研究探讨了政府权力的合法性。
近义词: legality, lawfulness, validity, rightfulness, justifiability
反义词: illegality, unlawfulness, invalidity, wrongfulness, unjustifiability
词性: noun

Etymology: From Late Latin *legitimatus, past participle of legitimare “to make lawful,” from Latin legitimus “lawful,” from lex (genitive legis) “law.”
