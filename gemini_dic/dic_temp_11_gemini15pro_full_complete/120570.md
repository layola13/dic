mirier:/ˈmɪəriər/ | adj. | 更快乐的，更愉快的 | Happier; more cheerful.  More full of mirth. | The children were mirier after they opened their presents. 孩子们打开礼物后更开心了。|  The village seemed mirier during the festival. 节日期间，村庄显得更加欢乐。 | Synonyms: happier, cheerier, jollier, merrier, more joyful | Antonyms: sadder, gloomier, more depressing | adjective

Etymology:
From Middle English *mirier, comparative of mirie (“merry”), from Old English myrġe (“pleasant, merry, sweet, agreeable, delightful”), from Proto-Germanic *murgijaz (“merry”), from Proto-Indo-European *mṛ́gʰu- (“short, small”). Cognate with Scots mirrie (“merry”), Dutch moedig (“brave, courageous”), German munter (“cheerful, lively”), Danish modig (“brave, courageous”), Swedish modig (“brave, courageous”). More at merry.
