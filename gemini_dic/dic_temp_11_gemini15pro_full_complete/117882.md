melodramatists: /ˈmɛlədrɑːmətɪsts/
| n. |夸张的演员；渲染悲情的人|A melodramatist is someone who behaves or writes in a melodramatic way, often exaggerating emotions and creating overly dramatic situations.  This could be an actor specializing in melodrama, a writer of melodramatic stories, or even someone behaving dramatically in everyday life.|The melodramatists in the play were so over-the-top that the audience began to laugh.|近义词：dramatists, hams, overactors|反义词：understated performers, realists|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From melodrama + -ist. Melodrama comes from French mélodrame, from Greek melos "song" + French drame "drama".
