demagogged:/ˈdeməˌɡɑːɡd/
|v.|煽动蛊惑（过去分词）|To have appealed to people's prejudices and emotions in order to gain power or support (past participle).|The politician was accused of having demagogged the issue of immigration.|近义词:manipulated, exploited, inflamed|反义词:reasoned, educated, clarified|词性:动词(过去分词)

demagogue:/ˈdeməˌɡɑːɡ/
|n.|煽动者|A political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by using rational argument.|The demagogue rallied the crowd with his fiery rhetoric.|近义词:rabble-rouser, firebrand, agitator|反义词:statesman, diplomat, conciliator|词性:名词

demagoguery:/ˈdeməˌɡɑːɡəri/
|n.|煽动行为，蛊惑人心|The practice of appealing to people's prejudices and emotions in order to gain power or support.|The candidate's demagoguery alienated many moderate voters.|近义词:rabble-rousing,煽动,煽动性言论|反义词:reasoning, logic, diplomacy|词性:名词

demagogic:/ˌdeməˈɡɑːɡɪk/
|adj.|煽动性的|Relating to or characteristic of a demagogue or demagoguery.|His demagogic speeches incited violence.|近义词:inflammatory, provocative, rabble-rousing|反义词:reasonable, logical, diplomatic|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek dēmagōgos (meaning "a popular leader"), from dēmos ("people") + agōgos ("leading, leading the way").
