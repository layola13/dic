mzungu: /mˈzuːŋɡuː/ (East African pronunciation varies)
n.
white person; foreigner (especially of European descent)
A term used primarily in East Africa to refer to a person of European descent.  Originally Swahili, its usage and connotations can be complex and vary depending on context. While sometimes used neutrally, it can also carry negative connotations of colonialism and racial othering.
Example: The mzungu asked for directions to the market.  那个白人/外国人问去市场的路。

Example:  She was surprised to see a mzungu in such a remote village.  她很惊讶在这个偏远的村庄看到一个白人/外国人。
近义词:  foreigner, westerner (though these are not perfect equivalents and lack the specific historical and cultural baggage of "mzungu")
