acetosity:/ˌæsɪˈtɒsɪti/ /ˌæsɪˈtɑːsɪti/
noun
酮症酸中毒
a pathological condition characterized by the presence of ketone bodies in the blood, especially acetone, acetoacetic acid, and beta-hydroxybutyric acid, usually due to diabetes mellitus, starvation, or other metabolic disorders.
The patient exhibited signs of acetosity, including rapid breathing and fruity breath odor.  病人表现出酮症酸中毒的迹象，包括呼吸急促和呼吸中有水果味。
近义词：ketoacidosis, ketosis
反义词: (No direct antonym, consider health or alkalinity)
