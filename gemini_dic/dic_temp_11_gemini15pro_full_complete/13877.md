augurate: /ˈɔːɡjəreɪt/ | v. | 预示，预兆 | to make a prediction, especially a favorable one; to be a sign of something to come | The president's speech augured a new era of peace and prosperity. 总统的演讲预示着一个和平与繁荣的新时代。|  The flight of the eagle augured well for the journey.  老鹰的飞翔预示着旅途顺利。 | synonyms: presage, portend, foretell, foreshadow | antonyms: belie, contradict | verb

auguration: /ˌɔːɡjəˈreɪʃn/ | n. | 预兆，征兆 | an omen or sign; the act of predicting the future | The auguration of the new president took place in January. 新总统的就职典礼在一月份举行。| The discovery of the ancient tomb was seen as an auguration of further archaeological finds.  古墓的发现被视为进一步考古发现的预兆。 | synonyms: omen, portent, presage | antonyms:  -- | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *augurare*, from *augur* (soothsayer, diviner) + *-are* (infinitive suffix).  Ultimately from *avis* (bird) and perhaps a lost word related to *gustus* (taste, relish), suggesting divination by the flight or feeding of birds.
