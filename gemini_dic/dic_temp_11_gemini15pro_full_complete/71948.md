flies-out:/flaɪz aʊt/ | v. | 出局；飞出；迅速离开 |  A batter flies out when they hit a fly ball that is caught by a fielder before it touches the ground. Also used to mean leaving quickly. | The batter flies out to center field.  击球手飞球出局到中外野。He flies out to meetings all the time. 他经常飞去开会。| 近义词: departs, leaves quickly, strikes out (in baseball) | 反义词: arrives, stays | 词性: 动词


flies-out (This word functions primarily as a verb phrase)

词源:  "flies" comes from Old English *flēogan, from Proto-Germanic *fleuganą. "Out" comes from Old English ūt, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *ūt.
