cynicalnesses:/ˈsɪnɪkəlˌnəsɪz/
n.
愤世嫉俗，玩世不恭; 犬儒主义
the state or quality of being cynical; cynicism.  A general distrust of human sincerity or integrity.
He displayed a shocking cynicalness about the motives of others. 他对别人的动机表现出令人震惊的愤世嫉俗。
Synonyms: cynicism, distrust, skepticism, negativity, pessimism
Antonyms: optimism, trust, faith, idealism, positivism
词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From cynical + -ness.  Cynical comes from the Greek *kynikos* "dog-like," referring to the Cynic philosophers of ancient Greece, known for their rejection of conventional values.
