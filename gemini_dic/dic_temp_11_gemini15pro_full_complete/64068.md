epitrochlea:/ˌɛpɪˈtrɒkliə/| n. |肱骨内上髁|A small bony prominence on the medial aspect of the distal end of the humerus, serving as an attachment point for muscles and ligaments of the forearm.|The ulnar nerve passes behind the epitrochlea.|近义词:medial epicondyle|反义词:lateral epicondyle|词性:noun

Etymology:
From epi- + Greek trochlea "pulley" (from trekhein "to run").  So named because the groove formed by it resembles a pulley.
