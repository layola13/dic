bloviate: /ˈbloʊvieɪt/ (US) /ˈbləʊvieɪt/ (UK)| v. | 夸夸其谈；高谈阔论 | To speak or write verbosely and pompously. | He bloviated for hours about his supposed accomplishments. 他滔滔不绝地吹嘘着他所谓的成就。| The politician bloviated about the economy, offering no real solutions.  这位政客对经济夸夸其谈，没有提供任何真正的解决方案。 | 近义词：orate, pontificate, speechify, rant, ramble | 反义词: be concise, be succinct | 词性: 动词

bloviation: /ˌbloʊviˈeɪʃən/ (US) /ˌbləʊviˈeɪʃən/ (UK)| n. | 夸夸其谈；高谈阔论 |  The act of speaking or writing verbosely and pompously. | His bloviation bored everyone in the room. 他的夸夸其谈让房间里的每个人都感到无聊。| The lengthy bloviation in the article made it difficult to read. 文章中冗长的夸夸其谈使其难以阅读。 | 近义词：verbosity, pomposity, bombast | 反义词: conciseness, brevity | 词性: 名词
