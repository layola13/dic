funkiness:/ˈfʌŋkɪnɪs/
|n.|古怪；独特；时髦|The quality or state of being funky. It can refer to an unconventional style, a distinctive groove or rhythm in music, an earthy or soulful quality, or a general sense of coolness and unconventionality.|
|例句：She admired the funkiness of his apartment decor. 她很欣赏他公寓装饰的独特风格。|
|例句：The band's music is known for its infectious funkiness.  这支乐队的音乐以其富有感染力的独特韵律而闻名。|
|近义词：groove, soul, coolness, hipness, eccentricity|
|反义词：conventionality, blandness, ordinariness|
|词性：名词|


funky:/ˈfʌŋki/
|adj.|时髦的；古怪的；有独特节奏的|Having or expressing a distinctive, unconventional style, especially in music, fashion, or attitude.  It can also refer to a strong, earthy smell or flavor.|
|例句：The band played a funky tune that got everyone dancing. 乐队演奏了一首时髦的曲子，让每个人都跳起了舞。|
|例句：Her funky sense of style made her stand out from the crowd. 她时髦的风格让她在人群中脱颖而出。|
|近义词：groovy, hip, cool, stylish, unconventional, eccentric|
|反义词：conventional, bland, ordinary, traditional|
|词性：形容词|


词源：From "funk" (meaning a strong, unpleasant smell) + "-y" (adjective suffix).  The musical sense developed in the mid-20th century, likely influenced by the idea of earthy, soulful music.
