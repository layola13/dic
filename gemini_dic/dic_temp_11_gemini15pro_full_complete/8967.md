antihypnotics:/ˌænti.hɪpˈnɒt.ɪks/ | n. | 抗催眠药，促觉醒剂 |  Substances that counteract the effects of hypnotics, promoting wakefulness and alertness. | The doctor prescribed antihypnotics to combat the patient's excessive daytime sleepiness. 医生给病人开了抗催眠药来对抗他白天过度嗜睡。|  Stimulants, wakefulness-promoting agents |  Hypnotics, sedatives | noun

antihypnotic:/ˌænti.hɪpˈnɒt.ɪk/ | adj. | 抗催眠的，促觉醒的 | Having the effect of counteracting hypnosis or promoting wakefulness. |  The antihypnotic properties of the medication helped him stay awake during the long drive. 这种药物的抗催眠特性帮助他在长途驾驶中保持清醒。 | Stimulating, arousing | Hypnotic, soporific | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- (prefix meaning "against, opposite") + hypnotic (relating to hypnosis or sleep-inducing).  The prefix "anti-" comes from Greek, while "hypnotic" ultimately derives from the Greek word "hypnos" meaning "sleep."
