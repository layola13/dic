cinter: /ˈsɪntər/ | n. | 烧结物，熔块 | A coherent solid mass formed by heating a powder or granular material below its melting point. | The cinter was then crushed into smaller pieces. 烧结物随后被粉碎成小块。|Synonyms: agglomerate, clinker | Antonyms: powder, dust | noun


Etymology: From German Sinter, from sintern "to scorch," from Old High German sintar "slag, dross," related to Old English sinder "slag, cinders."
