hesitative:/ˈhezɪtətɪv/ (US), /ˈhezɪtətɪv/ (UK)| adj.|犹豫的，迟疑不决的|Inclined to hesitate; showing or indicating hesitation.|She gave a hesitant smile. 她犹豫地笑了笑。|Synonyms: uncertain, tentative, doubtful, reluctant|Antonyms: decisive, confident, assertive|adjective

hesitantly:/ˈhezɪtəntlɪ/ (US), /ˈhezɪtəntlɪ/ (UK)| adv.|犹豫地，迟疑不决地|In a hesitant manner.|He hesitantly agreed to the proposal. 他迟疑地同意了这个提议。|Synonyms: tentatively, uncertainly, doubtfully|Antonyms: decisively, confidently, assertively|adverb

hesitation:/ˌhezɪˈteɪʃn/ (US), /ˌhezɪˈteɪʃn/ (UK)| n.|犹豫，迟疑|The act of hesitating; indecision.|She answered without hesitation. 她毫不犹豫地回答了。|Synonyms: indecision, doubt, reluctance, wavering|Antonyms: decisiveness, confidence, certainty|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *haesitativus, from Latin haesitare "to stick, remain fixed," frequentative of haerere "to stick, cling, hesitate."
