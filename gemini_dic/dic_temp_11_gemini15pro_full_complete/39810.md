consortship:/kənˈsɔːrtʃɪp/
n.
陪伴；结交；配偶关系
the state of being a consort; association with someone, typically a spouse or lover.
Queen Elizabeth enjoyed a long and happy consortship with Prince Philip. 伊丽莎白女王和菲利普亲王的婚姻长久而幸福。
The artist's consortship with other creative individuals inspired his work.  这位艺术家与其他有创造力的人的交往激发了他的创作灵感。
近义词：companionship, partnership, marriage, union
反义词：separation, divorce, estrangement
词性: 名词

consortship:/kənˈsɔːrtʃɪp/
n.
(尤指统治者的)配偶
the spouse of a reigning monarch.
The Duke of Edinburgh held the title of Prince Consort, acknowledging his consortship to Queen Elizabeth II. 爱丁堡公爵拥有亲王配偶的头衔，承认他是伊丽莎白二世的配偶。
History books often overlook the important role played by the king's consortship. 历史书常常忽略国王配偶的重要作用。
近义词：spouse, partner, husband, wife
反义词：N/A
