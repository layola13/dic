affrontedness:/əˈfrʌntɪdnəs/
|n.|受辱，被冒犯|The state of being affronted; a feeling of indignation or offense.|Her affrontedness at his remark was palpable. 她对他的话感到明显的被冒犯。|
|近义词：indignation, offense, resentment, pique|
|反义词：pleasure, delight, satisfaction|
|词性：名词|

affront:/əˈfrʌnt/
|n. vt.|冒犯，侮辱|An action or remark that causes outrage or offense; to insult or offend someone.|His casual remark was an affront to her dignity.  他漫不经心的话语是对她尊严的冒犯。 /  He affronted her by ignoring her presence. 他故意忽视她的存在而冒犯了她。|
|近义词：insult, offend, slight|
|反义词：compliment, praise, honor|
|词性：名词，动词|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *afronter, from a- "to" + fronter "to confront," from front "forehead, front," from Latin frons "forehead, brow, front."  The original sense was "to confront boldly or defiantly."  The sense of "insult" developed from the idea of a bold confrontation.
