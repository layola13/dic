gleba:/ˈɡliːbə/| n. |孢体；菌髓|The fertile, spore-bearing inner mass of certain fungi, such as puffballs and stinkhorns. |The gleba of the stinkhorn emitted a foul odor.  臭角菌的孢体散发出一股恶臭。|Synonyms: spore mass, sporophore|Antonyms: peridium|noun
Etymology-> From Latin *glēba (“clod, lump, soil”), related to glomus (“ball”).
