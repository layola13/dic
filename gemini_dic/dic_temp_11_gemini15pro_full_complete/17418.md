beamlet:/ˈbiːmlɪt/| n.|细光束，极小光束|A small, narrow beam of light.  A beamlet is a smaller, often precisely controlled, component of a larger beam.  It is commonly used in scientific contexts, particularly in fields like optics and particle physics.|The laser emitted a series of precise beamlets.|Synonyms: ray, stream, pencil (of light)|Antonyms: flood (of light)|noun
