frontbench:/ˈfrʌntbɛntʃ/ | n. |前排议员席；前座议员；（政府）主要官员 | The frontbench is usually occupied by the most senior members of a political party. |  The opposition leader questioned the government's policy from the frontbench. | 近义词: government, cabinet (when referring to the government frontbench), opposition (when referring to the opposition frontbench) | 反义词: backbench | 词性: 名词

frontbencher: /ˈfrʌntˌbɛntʃər/ | n. |前座议员；主要官员 |  A frontbencher is a member of parliament who holds a significant position within their party. | The Prime Minister is a prominent frontbencher. | 近义词: minister, shadow minister, leader | 反义词: backbencher | 词性: 名词
