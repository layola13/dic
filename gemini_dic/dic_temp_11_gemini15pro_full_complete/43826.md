creolized:/ˈkriːəlaɪzd/| adj.|克里奥尔化的，混合的|Relating to or denoting a language that has developed from a pidgin by acquiring native speakers and becoming more complex grammatically.|The creolized language of the island is a fascinating blend of French and African dialects.|近义词：hybrid, mixed, blended|反义词：pure, unmixed|词性:形容词
Etymology: From French *créolisé*, past participle of *créoliser* "to creolized," from *créole* "creole." Ultimately from Portuguese *crioulo* "native to a colony," from *criar* "to create, raise," from Latin *creare* "to create."
