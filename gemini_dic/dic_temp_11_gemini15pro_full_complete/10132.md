apologetics:/əˌpɒləˈdʒetɪks/ | n. | 辩护学；护教学 |  The religious discipline of defending religious doctrines through systematic argumentation and discourse.  | He studied apologetics in seminary. 他在神学院学习辩护学。 |  The book offers a compelling case for Christian apologetics. 这本书为基督教辩护学提供了一个令人信服的案例。 |  theology, defense | criticism, refutation | noun

apologist:/əˈpɒlədʒɪst/ | n. | 辩护者；护教者 | A person who offers an argument in defense of something controversial. |  He's a well-known apologist for atheism. 他是一位著名的无神论辩护者。 | She became an apologist for animal rights. 她成为了一名动物权利的辩护者。 | advocate, defender, champion | critic, opponent | noun

apologete:/ˈæpəlɒˌdʒiːt/ (less common than apologist) | n. | 辩护者；护教者 | A person who speaks or writes in defense of a cause, especially a religious one. | C.S. Lewis was a prominent Christian apologetes.  C.S. Lewis 是一位杰出的基督教护教士。 |  He's a passionate apologetes for free speech. 他是一位言论自由的热情辩护者。| advocate, defender, champion | critic, opponent | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek ἀπολογία (apologia) meaning "speech in defense" from ἀπολογεῖσθαι (apologeisthai) "to speak in defense," from ἀπό (apo) "from, off, away" + λόγος (logos) "word, speech, reason."
