deludes:/dɪˈluːdz/| v. |欺骗，哄骗；使产生错觉|to make someone believe something that is not true|He deluded himself into thinking he could win.|Synonyms: deceives, misleads, tricks|Antonyms: enlightens, informs, undeceives|Verb

deluding:/dɪˈluːdɪŋ/| adj. |欺骗性的，使人产生错觉的|giving a false or misleading impression|The offer was delusive, promising riches but delivering nothing.|Synonyms: deceptive, misleading, illusory|Antonyms: genuine, real, truthful|Adjective

delusion:/dɪˈluːʒən/| n. |错觉，妄想|a false belief or opinion, especially one held despite evidence to the contrary|He suffered from delusions of grandeur.|Synonyms: misconception, fallacy, illusion|Antonyms: reality, truth, fact|Noun

词源:
