lash-out:/læʃ ˈaʊt/| phrasal verb|猛烈抨击；突然发作|to suddenly attack someone or something physically or criticize them in an angry way|例句：He lashed out at the photographer.|近义词：attack, criticize, berate, castigate|反义词：praise, commend|词性：动词短语

lash-out:/læʃ ˈaʊt/| noun|抨击；突然发作|a sudden, violent attack, either physical or verbal|例句：Her lash-out at the media was unexpected.|近义词：attack, outburst, tirade|反义词：compliment, praise|词性：名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From 'lash' (to strike with a whip or something similar) + 'out' (indicating a sudden, forceful movement).  The original sense was of a horse kicking out or a person striking out suddenly.  This evolved into the metaphorical sense of a verbal attack.
