lazuli: /ˈlæʒʊlaɪ/ /ˈlæʒjʊlaɪ/ | n. | 青金石，天蓝色 | A semiprecious stone of a rich azure blue color consisting primarily of lazurite, and occurring typically in massive form. | The artist used ground lapis lazuli to create the vibrant blue in the painting. 艺术家用磨碎的青金石来创作画作中鲜艳的蓝色。| Synonym: azure, ultramarine | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a specific color/mineral) | noun

Etymology: From Old French *l'azur* (Modern French *l'azur*)  "azure," from Arabic *al-lazward* "the lapis lazuli," from Persian *lajward* "lapis lazuli," ultimately from Sanskrit *raja varta* meaning "king's portion" or "king's enclosure."
