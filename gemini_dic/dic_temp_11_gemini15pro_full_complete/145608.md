overconsciousness:/ˌoʊvərˈkɒnʃəsnəs/| n.|过度意识; 过分在意; 自我意识过剩|An excessive awareness of oneself, one's appearance, or one's actions, often causing self-consciousness and awkwardness.  An acute sense of oneself as an individual, especially in relation to others or to the environment.|She blushed, an overconsciousness of her appearance washing over her. 她脸红了，对自己的外表过度在意让她浑身不自在。|Synonyms: self-consciousness, awkwardness, shyness, diffidence, insecurity|Antonyms: confidence, poise, self-assurance|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + consciousness.  "Over-" is a prefix meaning "excessive" or "too much." "Consciousness" refers to the state of being aware of and responsive to one's surroundings.
