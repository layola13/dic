hotfoot:/ˈhɒtfʊt/ (UK) /ˈhɑːtfʊt/ (US)| adv. n. v.| 匆忙地，急切地；急忙离去；匆忙的行程；使匆忙|To go or send someone somewhere quickly.|He hotfooted it back to the office when he heard the news. 他听到消息后急忙赶回办公室。|近义词：hurry, hasten, rush, dash|反义词：dawdle, linger|词性: adverb, noun, verb

Etymology:
From the earlier "hot foot" (n.), referring to a trick or punishment involving applying a hot substance to someone's foot to make them move quickly.  This sense dates back to the 16th century. The verbal and adverbial forms developed later.
