groyne:/ɡrɔɪn/ | n. |防波堤；堤坝；突堤 | A low wall or barrier built out into the sea from a beach to check erosion and drifting. |  A new groyne was constructed to protect the harbor entrance.  一个新的防波堤被建造用来保护港口入口。

| Example: The groynes helped to build up the beach. 这些防波堤帮助扩大了海滩面积。| Synonym: breakwater, jetty, seawall, mole | Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps "open sea" in a contextual sense) | noun

groin:/ɡrɔɪn/ | n. |腹股沟 | The area between the abdomen and the thigh, on either side of the body. | He felt a sharp pain in his groin. 他感到腹股沟一阵剧痛。

| Example:  The athlete pulled a muscle in his groin.  这名运动员腹股沟肌肉拉伤了。 | Synonym: inguen | Antonym: (No direct antonym) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *groine, from Anglo-Norman *groigne, *groing, from Old North French *groign, from Frankish *grūnja (“snout, muzzle”), from Proto-Germanic *grūnjō (“snout, muzzle”). Cognate with Middle Dutch groine (“snout, pig's snout”), Middle High German grüen (“snout”), Old English grūnd (“ground, bottom, foundation”).
