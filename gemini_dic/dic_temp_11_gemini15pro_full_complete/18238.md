belial: /ˈbiːliəl/ | n. | 邪恶; 恶魔; 堕落 | A Hebrew word signifying worthlessness and wickedness, often used as a name for Satan or a demon.  It represents a spirit of rebellion, lawlessness, and utter depravity. |  例句：His actions were so cruel they seemed inspired by Belial. 他的行为如此残忍，似乎受到了恶魔的驱使。

| 例句：The preacher warned against the influence of Belial in the hearts of men. 牧师警告人们要提防恶魔对人心的影响。 | 近义词：Satan, devil, Lucifer, evil | 反义词：God, good, holiness | 词性: 名词

词源: From Hebrew בְלִיַּעַל (beliyya'al), meaning "worthlessness," "wickedness," or "destruction."  Possibly derived from two words:  בְּלִי (beli) "without" and יַעַל (ya'al) "profit, advantage, or ascent."  Thus, "without worth" or "without ascent (to God)."
