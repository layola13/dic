antiphilosophy:/ˌænti fɪˈlɒsəfi/ | n. |反哲学|A stance that is critical of or opposed to traditional philosophy, often rejecting its methods, assumptions, or value.  It can also refer to a set of ideas or practices that challenge or subvert philosophical norms. | Many consider Dadaism a form of antiphilosophy because of its rejection of reason and logic.  达达主义因其对理性和逻辑的拒绝而被许多人视为一种反哲学形式。| Nietzsche is sometimes described as an anti-philosopher. 尼采有时被描述为反哲学家。|synonyms:  iconoclasm, heterodoxy, skepticism | antonyms:  philosophy, orthodoxy, dogmatism | noun
