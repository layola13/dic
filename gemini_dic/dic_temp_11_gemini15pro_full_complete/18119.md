behind-your-back:/bɪˈhaɪnd jɔːr bæk/
|adv.|背着你；在你背后；偷偷地|Done or said without someone's knowledge or consent.  It implies secrecy and often has a negative connotation, suggesting something dishonest or underhanded.|They were talking about me behind my back. 他们在背后议论我。|Synonyms: secretly, covertly, surreptitiously|Antonyms: openly, publicly, face-to-face|adverb

behind-your-back:/bɪˈhaɪnd jɔːr bæk/
|adj.|背地里的；秘密的|Describing actions or words that are done or said secretly and without someone's knowledge. |She made behind-your-back deals with the competition. 她与竞争对手进行了秘密交易。 |Synonyms: clandestine, furtive, underhand|Antonyms: open, overt, aboveboard|adjective
