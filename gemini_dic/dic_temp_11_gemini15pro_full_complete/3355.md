affricates:/ˈæfrɪkɪts/ | n. | 破擦音 |  A type of consonant sound that begins as a stop (with complete closure in the vocal tract) and releases into a fricative (with partial closure and turbulent airflow).  |  The sounds /tʃ/ (as in "church") and /dʒ/ (as in "judge") are affricates in English. | 近义词: None (as it's a technical phonetic term) | 反义词: None | 词性: noun

affricate:/ˈæfrɪkət/ | n. | 破擦音 | A single speech sound produced by stopping the airstream and then releasing it with friction, like the 'ch' in 'church'. |  The word "judge" begins with an affricate. | 近义词: None | 反义词: None | 词性: noun
