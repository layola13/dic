anticlericalists:/ˌæntiklɛrɪkəlɪsts/
| n. |反教权主义者；反对神职人员者|People who oppose the power and influence of the clergy in political and social affairs.  Those who believe that the clergy should not have political or social power. |The rise of anticlericalists in the 19th century led to significant changes in the relationship between church and state. 19世纪反教权主义者的兴起导致了教会与国家关系的重大变化。

Anticlericalists often advocate for the separation of church and state. 反教权主义者经常倡导政教分离。|近义词：secularists, freethinkers, nonconformists |反义词:clericalists, devout believers|Noun
