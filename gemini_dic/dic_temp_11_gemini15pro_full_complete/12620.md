assassinate: /əˈsæsɪneɪt/ | vt. | 刺杀，暗杀 | To murder (an important person) by sudden or secret attack, often for political reasons. |  John Wilkes Booth assassinated Abraham Lincoln. 约翰·威尔克斯·布斯刺杀了亚伯拉罕·林肯。

The journalist was assassinated for exposing corruption. 这名记者因揭露腐败而被暗杀。

| 近义词：murder, kill, execute, eliminate | 反义词：protect, save, defend | 词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *assassiner*, from Medieval Latin *assassinus*, from Arabic *ḥaššāšīn* "hashish-eaters," plural of *ḥaššāš* "hashish-eater," from *ḥašīš* "hashish."  The original Assassins were a sect of Nizari Ismaili Muslims who were reputed to use hashish and carry out targeted killings of political and religious figures during the medieval period.  The term later evolved to refer to any act of political assassination.
