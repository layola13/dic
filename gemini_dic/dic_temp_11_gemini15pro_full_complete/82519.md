gt:/dʒiːˈtiː/ |n.| Gran Turismo (伟大的旅行)|  A high-performance car, especially one designed for long-distance driving. | He just bought a new GT. 他刚买了一辆新的高性能跑车。 | 近义词: sports car, supercar | 反义词: economy car | 词性: 名词

gt:/dʒiːˈtiː/ |abbr.| Gigatransfer | A unit of data transfer rate equal to one billion transfers per second. | The network can achieve a GT of data transfer. 这个网络可以达到每秒十亿次的数据传输速率。 | 近义词: Gbps | 反义词: (No direct antonym for a unit of measurement) | 词性: 缩写


gt:/dʒiːˈtiː/ |abbr.| Genetically modified|  Containing genetic material that has been artificially altered so as to produce a desired characteristic. |  GT crops are becoming more common.  转基因作物正变得越来越普遍。 | 近义词: GMO, transgenic | 反义词: non-GMO, conventional | 词性: 缩写


词源: GT, when referring to cars, is an abbreviation of Gran Turismo, Italian for "Grand Touring."  This term originally referred to horse-drawn carriages designed for long journeys, and later came to describe high-performance cars suited for similar purposes.  When referring to data transfer, GT is short for Gigatransfer.  When referring to genetically modified organisms, GT is short for Genetically modified.
