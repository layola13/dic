crosswinds:/ˈkrɒswɪndz/ (US) /ˈkrɒswɪndz/ (UK)| n. |侧风；横风|Wind blowing across the direction of travel, especially of an aircraft. |The pilot had to contend with strong crosswinds during landing. 飞行员在着陆时不得不应对强劲的侧风。|

The ship listed badly in the crosswinds.  这艘船在侧风中严重倾斜。|近义词：side winds|反义词:headwinds, tailwinds|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From cross + wind.  "Cross" meaning "across" or "transverse" has been used in English since the Old English period (as "cros").  "Wind" also originates from Old English ("wind"). The combination "crosswind" is attested from the late 19th century.
