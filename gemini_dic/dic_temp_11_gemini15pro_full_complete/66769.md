exoteric:/ˌeksəʊˈtɛrɪk/| adj.| 外界的，公开的，通俗的| Intended for or likely to be understood by the general public; not esoteric or secret.|  The exoteric teachings of the philosopher were easily understood by everyone, unlike his more complex and private doctrines.| 近义词: public, popular, common, accessible, understandable, intelligible| 反义词: esoteric, arcane, obscure, private, secret| 词性: adjective
Etymology: From Ancient Greek ἐξωτερικός (exōterikós, “external, outer”), from ἐξω- (exō-, “outside”) + -τερικός (-terikós, adjective-forming suffix). Ultimately from the same root as "exo-" and "-terior".
