antiparalytic:/ˌæntiˌpærəˈlɪtɪk/| adj. n.|抗麻痹的；抗麻痹药|A substance or drug that counteracts or relieves paralysis.  It can refer to a medication or other treatment that helps restore muscle function or prevent paralysis.|The doctor prescribed an antiparalytic drug to help the patient regain movement. 医生给病人开了抗麻痹药，帮助病人恢复活动能力。|Synonyms: antispastic, neuromuscular blocking antagonist|Antonyms: paralytic, paralyzing|adjective

antiparalytic:/ˌæntiˌpærəˈlɪtɪk/| n.|抗麻痹药|A medication or other treatment that helps restore muscle function or prevent paralysis.|The antiparalytic was crucial in his recovery.  这种抗麻痹药对他的康复至关重要。|Synonyms: antispastic, neuromuscular blocking antagonist|Antonyms: paralytic, paralyzing|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From anti- "against" + paralytic "pertaining to paralysis."  Paralytic comes from Greek paralusis "a disabling," from para- "beside" + lyein "to loosen."
