Etymology:

* damning (adjective): from "damn" (verb), from Old English *dæmmian "to condemn, declare unfit," from Proto-Germanic *dammjan "to tame, subdue," from PIE root *demə- "to tame."  The meaning shifted from "to tame" to "to condemn" in the Christian context.
* -ness (suffix):  Old English -nes(s), -nis(s), -nyss, forming abstract nouns from adjectives, from Proto-Germanic *-in-assu-, from PIE *-n̥-ti- "action, state, condition."
* -'s (suffix):  possessive marker
