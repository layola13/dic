atonalism: /eɪˈtoʊnəlɪzəm/ | n. | 无调性 | A style of musical composition characterized by the avoidance of traditional tonality, or key.  It often features the use of all twelve notes of the chromatic scale in a relatively equal manner, without emphasizing any particular note as a tonic. | Schoenberg's music is often described as atonalism.  勋伯格的音乐常被描述为无调性。| The atonalism of the piece created a sense of unease. 这首曲子的无调性营造了一种不安感。 | synonyms: atonality, twelve-tone music, serialism | antonyms: tonality, diatonicism | noun
