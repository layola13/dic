free-trade:/ˌfriː ˈtreɪd/ | adj. n.| 自由贸易 | International trade left to its natural course without tariffs, quotas, or other restrictions. |  Many economists believe that free-trade policies benefit all countries involved. 许多经济学家认为自由贸易政策对所有参与国都有利。| near synonyms: unrestricted trade, open market, laissez-faire commerce | near antonyms: protectionism, trade restriction, closed market | adjective, noun


free-trade:/ˌfriː ˈtreɪd/ | adj. n.| 自由贸易 | The exchange of goods and services between countries without restrictions such as tariffs or quotas. | The government is committed to promoting free-trade agreements. 政府致力于促进自由贸易协定。 | near synonyms:  liberalized trade, deregulated trade | near antonyms: controlled trade, regulated trade| adjective, noun

Etymology:
"free" (Old English "freo" – free, exempt) + "trade" (Middle English "trade" - a path, course of life, occupation)
