determinant:/dɪˈtɜːrmɪnənt/| n. |行列式；决定因素|A determinant is a value derived from a square matrix in linear algebra. It's used to solve systems of linear equations and determine properties of matrices.  It can also refer to something that determines or influences the outcome of something. |The determinant of this matrix is zero. 这个矩阵的行列式为零。|Synonym: factor, influence|Antonym: inconsequential|noun

rank:/ræŋk/| n. v. |等级；排名；排列；(矩阵的)秩；|As a noun, it refers to a position in a hierarchy. As a verb, it means to assign a position in a hierarchy. In linear algebra, the rank of a matrix is the maximum number of linearly independent rows (or columns).|He holds the rank of captain. 他拥有上尉军衔。|Synonym: position, tier, rate, order|Antonym: disorder, disorganization|noun & verb

ranks:/ræŋks/| n. |队伍，行列；阶层|Plural form of "rank," often referring to a group of people considered together.|He rose through the ranks of the army. 他在军队中步步高升。|Synonym: rows, lines, tiers, echelons|Antonym: individual, singularity|noun
