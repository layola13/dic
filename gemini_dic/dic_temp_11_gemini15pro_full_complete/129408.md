nominalizes:/ˈnɒmɪnəlaɪzɪz/ | v. | 名词化；将…名词化 |  transforms a word or phrase (often a verb or adjective) into a noun. | The writer nominalizes verbs frequently, creating a dense and abstract style.  作者经常使用动词名词化，创造了一种密集而抽象的风格。 | Example: The verb "fail" can be nominalized into the noun "failure". 动词“失败”可以名词化为名词“失败”。 | synonyms: nounify, verbalize (as in turning into a verbal noun) | antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider: simplify, make concrete) | verb (third-person singular simple present)

nominalization:/ˌnɒmɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ | n. | 名词化；名词化作用 | the process of forming a noun from a verb or adjective. | The frequent nominalization in the text makes it difficult to understand. 文本中频繁的名词化使其难以理解。| Example:  The nominalization of "run" is "running". “跑”的名词化是“跑步”。 | synonyms: nounification, verbalization (as in turning into a verbal noun) | antonyms: (No direct antonym) | noun


Etymology: From nominal + -ize, ultimately from Latin *nomen, name.
