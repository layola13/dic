give-the-nod-to:/ɡɪv ðə nɒd tuː/ (British) /ɡɪv ðə nɑːd tuː/ (American)| v. phr.|批准，认可，同意|To give formal approval or permission for something to happen.|The committee gave the nod to the proposed changes. 委员会批准了提议的变更。|Synonyms: approve, authorize, sanction, endorse, ratify, greenlight|Antonyms: reject, refuse, decline, veto, disapprove|Verb Phrase

give-the-nod-to:/ɡɪv ðə nɒd tuː/ (British) /ɡɪv ðə nɑːd tuː/ (American)| v. phr.|选中，挑选|To choose or select someone or something.|The coach gave the nod to the rookie quarterback. 教练选中了那个新秀四分卫。|Synonyms: choose, select, pick, opt for|Antonyms: reject, pass over|Verb Phrase

ETYMOLOGY->
"Nod" comes from Old English *hnoddian, meaning "to shake, to wobble."  The phrase "give the nod to" likely evolved from the physical act of nodding one's head to signify agreement or approval.
