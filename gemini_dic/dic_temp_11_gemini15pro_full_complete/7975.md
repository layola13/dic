anthodium:/ænˈθoʊdiəm/| n. |头状花序|A flower head or capitulum, characteristic of plants in the family Asteraceae (Compositae), such as daisies and sunflowers. It is composed of many small flowers, or florets, crowded together on a receptacle, surrounded by an involucre of bracts.|The sunflower's anthodium is a striking example of this type of inflorescence.  向日葵的头状花序是这种花序类型的突出例子。|Synonym: capitulum, flower head|Antonym: N/A|noun
