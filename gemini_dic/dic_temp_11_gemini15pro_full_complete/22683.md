borders:/ˈbɔːrdərz/ (UK) /ˈbɔːrdərz/ (US)| n. v.|边界，国界；边缘；与…接壤；限制|A border is a line separating two countries, administrative divisions, or other areas.  The word can also refer to the edge or margin of something. As a verb, it means to form a common boundary with another area or to adjoin.|

例句：
The two countries share a long border. 这两个国家共享一条长长的边界。

The flower borders the path.  花朵沿着小路生长。


近义词：boundary, frontier, edge, limit, confine, restrict
反义词：center, interior, middle

词性: noun (plural), verb (third-person singular present)


词源: From Old French *bordure, from bord (“edge, side”), from Frankish *bord, from Proto-Germanic *burdiz (“plank, board”). Cognate with Old High German bort (“board, plank, edge, shore”).
