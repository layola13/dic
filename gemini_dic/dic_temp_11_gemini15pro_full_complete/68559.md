fanged:/fæŋd/ | adj. | 有尖牙的；獠牙的 | Having fangs or tusks; showing fangs. | The fanged creature lunged at its prey.  这长着獠牙的生物向它的猎物猛扑过去。|Synonyms: tusked, toothed, sharp-toothed | Antonyms: toothless, fangless | adjective

fanged:/fæŋd/ | adj. | (比喻)凶恶的；恶毒的 | (Figurative) fierce, venomous |  He gave her a fanged look. 他恶狠狠地瞪了她一眼。| Synonyms: fierce, venomous, vicious | Antonyms: gentle, kind | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *fangod, from fang (n.) + -ed (suffix denoting "having").  The word "fang" comes from Proto-Germanic *fangaz, meaning "something that seizes or catches," related to the verb "fōn" (to seize).
