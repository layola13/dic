morals: /ˈmɔːrəlz/ (US) /ˈmɒrəlz/ (UK)| n. |道德，伦理；寓意| standards of behaviour; principles of right and wrong |例句：His morals are questionable. 他的道德令人质疑。|例句：The morals of the story are important for children to learn.  这个故事的寓意对孩子们学习很重要。|近义词：ethics, principles, values|反义词：immorality, wickedness|词性: 名词

词源:  From Old French *moralitez (plural of moralité), from Late Latin mōrālitās (“manner, character, proper behavior”), from Latin mōrālis (“relating to character or morals”), from mōs (“custom, habit, manner”). Doublet of mores.
