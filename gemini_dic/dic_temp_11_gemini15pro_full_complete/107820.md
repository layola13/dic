lacquey:/ˈlæki/ | n. | 仆从，走狗，奴才 | A servile follower; a footman or liveried manservant. | The arrogant businessman treated his employees like lacqueys.  傲慢的商人把他的员工当作奴才。| Synonyms: servant, footman, valet, flunky, minion | Antonyms: master, boss, employer | Noun

lacquey:/ˈlæki/ | v. |  充当仆从，卑躬屈膝 | To act as a lacquey; to behave servilely. | He lacqueyed for the wealthy socialite, hoping to gain favor. 他为这位富有的社会名流卑躬屈膝，希望能得到好处。| Synonyms: fawn, grovel, kowtow | Antonyms: defy, resist, challenge | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French "laquais," ultimately from Spanish "lacayo."  The Spanish word is of uncertain origin, possibly from Arabic.
