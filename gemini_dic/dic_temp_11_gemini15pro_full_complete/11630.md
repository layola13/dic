armaturing:/ˈɑːrmətʃərɪŋ/| n.| 装配电枢, 电枢绕组,  armadura(西班牙语)|The process of winding coils of insulated wire around the iron core of an electrical machine such as a generator or motor, creating the armature. This armature rotates within a magnetic field, generating electricity in a generator or producing torque in a motor.|The armaturing of the generator was complex and required specialized tools.|近义词: winding, coil winding|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

armature:/ˈɑːrmətʃər/| n.| 电枢, 甲胄, 盔甲|1. The rotating part of an electric motor or generator, consisting of coils of wire wound around an iron core. 2. A framework or supporting structure, especially in sculpture or anatomy. 3. Armor, especially protective covering for the body.|1. The armature spins rapidly within the magnetic field. 2. The sculptor built an armature for his clay figure. 3.  The knight's armature protected him in battle. |近义词: rotor (for electric motors), framework, skeleton, armor|反义词: stator (for electric motors)|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin armatura "arms, armor," from Latin armare "to arm," from arma "arms, weapons."  The electrical sense developed from the idea of an "armed" or reinforced core.
