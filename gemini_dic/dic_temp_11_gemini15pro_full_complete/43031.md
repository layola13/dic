cowering:/ˈkaʊərɪŋ/| adj. | 畏缩的，蜷缩的 |  Cringing or shrinking in fear.  The act of crouching down in fear. | The dog was cowering in the corner after being scolded.  这只狗被责骂后蜷缩在角落里。|  The children were cowering under the table during the thunderstorm. 孩子们在雷雨天气时躲在桌子底下瑟瑟发抖。 | synonyms: shrinking, recoiling, flinching, crouching | antonyms:  braving, facing, confronting | verb (present participle)

cower:/ˈkaʊər/| v. | 畏缩，蜷缩 | To crouch down in fear. | The frightened rabbit cowered in the bushes. 受惊的兔子蜷缩在灌木丛里。 |  He cowered before the bully. 他在恶霸面前畏缩了。| synonyms: cringe, shrink, recoil, flinch | antonyms:  brave, face, confront | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *couren, from Old Norse *kura, from Proto-Germanic *hurōną, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)ker- (“to turn, bend”).  Cognate with Danish kure (“to huddle”), Swedish kura (“to cower, brood”), Icelandic kúra (“to doze”).
