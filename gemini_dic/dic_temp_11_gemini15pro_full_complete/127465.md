necessarianism:/nəˌsesəˈrɪərɪənɪzəm/| n. |必然论|The doctrine that all events are predetermined by antecedent causes and are therefore inevitable.|例句：Necessarianism implies that free will is an illusion. 必然论意味着自由意志是一种幻觉。

例句：Some philosophers argue that necessarianism is incompatible with moral responsibility. 一些哲学家认为必然论与道德责任不相容。|近义词：determinism, fatalism|反义词：indeterminism, libertarianism|词性:名词

Etymology: From "necessary" + "-arian" (denoting adherence to a doctrine) + "-ism" (denoting a doctrine or system).  "Necessary" comes from Old French "necessaire" and ultimately from Latin "necessarius,"  meaning "unavoidable, indispensable."
