knockabout:/ˈnɒkəbaʊt/ (UK) /ˈnɑːkəbaʊt/ (US)| adj. n.|耐用的，随便的；闹剧；|A knockabout comedy or performance involves a lot of rough, noisy action.  (referring to a comedy or performance involving a lot of rough, noisy action) A knockabout person is someone who likes to have fun and doesn't behave in a formal or polite way. (referring to a person who is boisterous and enjoys rough or noisy fun)|
例句：We saw a knockabout comedy at the theatre. 我们在剧院看了一场闹剧。

例句：He's a real knockabout guy, always up for a laugh. 他真是个爱闹腾的家伙，总是想找乐子。

近义词：slapstick, boisterous, rambunctious, rumbustious|
反义词：refined, sophisticated, subdued|
词性：adjective, noun


knockabout:/ˈnɒkəbaʊt/ (UK) /ˈnɑːkəbaʊt/ (US)| n.|旧式帆船|A small, sturdy sailboat, typically used for pleasure or racing.|
例句：They sailed their knockabout around the bay. 他们驾驶着他们的帆船绕着海湾航行。

例句：The knockabout capsized in the strong winds. 帆船在强风中倾覆了。

近义词：sailboat, yacht|
反义词：N/A|
词性：noun


词源:  knock + about. "Knock" suggesting rough handling and "about" implying movement and lack of restraint.  The earliest uses referred to boats designed to withstand rough handling, later extending to comedies and people with similar characteristics.
