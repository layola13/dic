dibses:/ˈdɪbzɪz/ | n. | 宣称所有权，非正式的预定 |  Dibses is a colloquial and informal way of claiming something, often something desirable or limited, before anyone else can.  It's often used playfully among children. | "I have dibses on the last slice of pizza!" = "我要最后一块披萨！" | 近义词：claim, call, reserve (more formal) | 反义词: relinquish, forfeit | 词性: noun (usually plural)

词源:
Originates from the children's game of "dibs," where calling "dibs" on something reserves it for the caller.  The exact etymology is uncertain, but it might be related to Yiddish "dibbuk," meaning a clinging spirit, or to a game called "dibstones" played with sheep knucklebones.
