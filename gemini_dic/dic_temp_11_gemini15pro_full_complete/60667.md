electoress: /ɪˈlɛktrəs/ | n. | 女选帝侯；有选举权的妇女 | A woman who has the right to vote, especially in electing a sovereign or supreme ruler.  Historically, this term specifically referred to a woman with the right to vote in the Holy Roman Empire, where the emperor was elected by a group of princes and prelates. |  In the 18th century, several powerful electoresses influenced the politics of the Holy Roman Empire. 18世纪，几位强大的女选帝侯影响了神圣罗马帝国的政治。  The electoress cast her vote for the candidate she believed would best serve the empire. 女选帝侯投票给她认为最能服务于帝国的候选人。 | 近义词: voter, constituent | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, consider perhaps "subject" in a historical context) | 词性: noun
