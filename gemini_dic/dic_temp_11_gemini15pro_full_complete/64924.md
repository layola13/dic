establishmentarian:/ɪˌstæblɪʃmənˈtɛəriən/
| adj. n. |建制派的；建制派人士|Relating to or supporting the established church or institutions; a person who supports the established church or institutions.|The professor held establishmentarian views, supporting the traditional power structures within the university.  这位教授持有建制派观点，支持大学内部传统的权力结构。|近义词：conservative, traditionalist, orthodox|反义词:radical, reformer, revolutionary|词性:adjective, noun

establishmentarian:/ɪˌstæblɪʃmənˈtɛəriən/
| n. |建制派人士|A person who supports the established church or institutions.|He was seen as an establishmentarian, always defending the status quo. 他被视为建制派，总是维护现状。|近义词：conservative, traditionalist|反义词:radical, reformer|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From establishment + -arian.  "Establishment" comes from the Old French "establissement" and ultimately from the Latin "stabilire" meaning "to make stable."  The suffix "-arian" denotes a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system.
