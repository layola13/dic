coachdog:/ˈkəʊtʃdɒɡ/ (UK) /ˈkoʊtʃdɔːɡ/ (US)| n. |马车犬，驿马犬|A large, short-coated breed of dog, historically used for running alongside carriages to guard them from highwaymen.|例句：The coachdog barked fiercely at the approaching stranger. 马车犬对着靠近的陌生人猛烈地吠叫。

例句：Coachdogs were prized for their speed and stamina. 马车犬因其速度和耐力而备受珍视。|近义词：Dalmatian (most common), carriage dog|反义词: N/A|词性: noun


Etymology:
From "coach" + "dog," literally a dog that runs with coaches.  The breed is believed to have originated in Dalmatia, a historical region on the eastern shore of the Adriatic Sea.
