assidean: /ˌæsɪˈdiːən/ | n. | 哈西典人；虔诚的犹太教徒 | A member of a Jewish sect or party of the second century BC and later, distinguished by strict observance of the Mosaic law and often in opposition to the Hellenizing tendencies of the time.  They are considered to be the forerunners of the Pharisees. |  The historian Josephus wrote extensively about the Essenes and the Asideans. 历史学家约瑟夫斯对艾赛尼派和哈西典人进行了广泛的著述。|  The Assideans were known for their piety and adherence to traditional Jewish practices. 哈西典人以其虔诚和坚持传统的犹太习俗而闻名。 | synonyms: Hasidim, pietist | antonyms: Hellenist, apostate | noun
