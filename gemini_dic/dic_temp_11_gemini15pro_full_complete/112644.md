lowballing: /ˈloʊˌbɔːlɪŋ/
| n. |低价报价；低估；压低价格|The act of making a deliberately low offer, typically in a negotiation or bidding process, in an attempt to secure a bargain or gain an advantage.|The seller accused the buyer of lowballing. 卖家指责买家压价。|underbidding, underquoting|overbidding, overestimating|noun

lowball: /ˈloʊˌbɔːl/
| v. |低价报价；低估；压低价格|To make a deliberately low offer, typically in a negotiation or bidding process, in an attempt to secure a bargain or gain an advantage.|They lowballed their offer, hoping the seller would be desperate. 他们故意压低报价，希望卖家会因为绝望而接受。|underbid, underquote|overbid, overestimate|verb
| adj. |低价的；低估的|Characterized by a deliberately low offer or estimate.|The initial offer was seen as lowball and insulting. 最初的报价被认为是低估的，甚至是侮辱性的。|underpriced, undervalued|overpriced, overvalued|adjective
