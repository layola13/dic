necessitarianism:/nəˌsesɪˈtɛəriənɪzəm/| n. |必然论；决定论|The philosophical doctrine that all events are determined by antecedent causes and are thus predictable.  The idea that free will is an illusion and that everything that happens is inevitable.|例句：He argued against necessitarianism, believing in the power of individual choice. 他反对必然论，相信个人选择的力量。

例句：Necessitarianism implies that every action, even seemingly random ones, is predetermined. 必然论意味着每一个行为，即使是看似随机的，也是预先决定的。|近义词：determinism, fatalism|反义词：indeterminism, libertarianism|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *necessitarius* (necessary) + -ism.  *Necessitarius* is derived from Latin *necessitas* (necessity).
