calculating:/ˈkælkjuleɪtɪŋ/| adj.|计算的，精明的，深谋远虑的|Present participle of calculate.  Meaning:  engaged in or requiring careful thought or planning, especially to achieve a desired result; shrewd and devious.|She had a calculating look in her eyes. 她眼神里流露出一丝精明.|近义词：scheming, cunning, shrewd, devious, manipulative|反义词：naive, ingenuous, artless|词性:形容词

Etymology: From Late Latin *calculatus*, past participle of *calculare* "to reckon, compute," from *calculus* "small stone," diminutive of *calx* (genitive *calcis*) "limestone, pebble."  Originally, calculations were performed using pebbles or stones.
