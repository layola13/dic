lay-line:/ˈleɪ laɪn/
noun
航线，方位线
An imaginary line extending from a mark or buoy to a boat, representing the optimal course to sail to reach the mark directly against the wind.  从标志物或浮标延伸到船只的假想线，代表直接逆风到达标志物的最佳航线。
The yachtsman was careful to sail along the lay-line to maximize his upwind speed.  帆船运动员小心翼翼地沿着方位线航行，以最大限度地提高逆风速度。
近义词: rhumb line, sailing line
反义词: None
词性: n.

lay-line:/ˈleɪ laɪn/
noun
(常作复数)能量线，灵脉
A supposed alignment of sites of spiritual or mystical power, such as ancient monuments or natural formations.  被认为是具有精神或神秘力量的地点（例如古代遗迹或自然构造）的连线。
Some people believe that Stonehenge is located on a powerful lay-line.  有些人相信巨石阵位于强大的能量线上。
近义词: ley line, energy line
反义词: None
词性: n.

词源:  The sailing term comes from "lay," meaning the direction a vessel lies to the wind. The mystical sense, popularized in the 20th century, is possibly a corruption of "ley line," referring to ancient straight tracks across the landscape.  航海术语来自“lay”，意思是船只相对于风的方向。神秘的含义在 20世纪流行起来，可能是“ley line”的讹误，指的是穿越地形的古代直线轨道。
