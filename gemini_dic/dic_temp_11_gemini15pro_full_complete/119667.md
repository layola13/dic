middlingly:/ˈmɪdlɪŋli/| adv.| 中等地，一般地，普通地|In a middling or moderate manner; to a moderate degree; neither very well nor very poorly.  表示程度适中，既不好也不坏。|He performed middlingly in the competition. 他在比赛中表现平平。|Synonyms: moderately, averagely, fairly, tolerably, so-so|Antonyms: extremely, exceptionally, remarkably, extraordinarily|词性:副词

middling:/ˈmɪdlɪŋ/| adj.| 中等的，普通的，一般的|Moderate in size, quality, or amount; neither very good nor very bad. 表示大小、质量或数量适中，既不好也不坏。|The restaurant served middling food. 这家餐厅的食物很一般。|Synonyms: average, moderate, ordinary, fair|Antonyms: excellent, exceptional, superior, inferior|词性:形容词

词源: From Middle English *middling, from middling (noun and adjective), present participle of midlen (“to divide in half”), from Old English *midlian, from middel (“middle”).  源自中古英语 *middling，来自 middling（名词和形容词），midlen（“分成两半”）的现在分词，来自古英语 *midlian，来自 middel（“中间”）。
