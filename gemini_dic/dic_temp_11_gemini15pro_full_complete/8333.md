anti-philosophy:/ˌænti fɪˈlɒsəfi/ | n. | 反哲学 | A stance that rejects or opposes traditional philosophical methods and assumptions, often by emphasizing the limitations or meaninglessness of philosophical inquiry. |  Example 1: His anti-philosophy stance led him to believe that searching for ultimate truth was futile. 他的反哲学立场使他相信追求终极真理是徒劳的。

Example 2:  Some see Dadaism as a form of artistic anti-philosophy. 有些人认为达达主义是一种艺术上的反哲学形式。 | Synonyms:  irrationalism, nihilism, skepticism | Antonyms:  philosophy, rationalism | noun
