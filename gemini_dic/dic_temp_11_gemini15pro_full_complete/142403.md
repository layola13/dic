orus: /ˈɔːrəs/ | n. | 金嘴，口才 |  A person's mouth, especially when considered as the source of eloquent or persuasive speech.  Derived from Latin "os," meaning "mouth."  Often used poetically or figuratively. |  "His silver orus moved the crowd to tears."  他的雄辩口才让听众感动得流下了眼泪。 | synonyms: voice, eloquence, tongue | antonyms: silence, muteness | noun
