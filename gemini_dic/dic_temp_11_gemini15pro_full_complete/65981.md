exaltate:/ɪɡˈzɔːlteɪt/ | adj. | 欣喜若狂的，欢欣鼓舞的 | Filled with or expressing great joy or triumph; jubilant. |  The team was exaltate after winning the championship.  球队赢得冠军后欣喜若狂。 | synonyms: elated, jubilant, triumphant, joyous | antonyms: dejected, depressed, despondent | adjective

exalt:/ɪɡˈzɔːlt/ | vt. | 提升；提拔；赞扬；使得意 | To raise in rank, power, or character; to elevate by praise or in estimation; to glorify. | The king exalted his loyal knight to a higher position. 国王将忠诚的骑士提升到更高的职位。| synonyms: elevate, promote, praise, glorify | antonyms:  debase, degrade, humble | verb


Etymology:
From Latin *exaltatus*, past participle of exaltare “to raise high, elevate,” from ex- “out, up” + altus “high.”
