clansmanship: /ˈklænzmənˌʃɪp/ | n. | 宗族观念，氏族精神，家族荣誉感 | The devotion and loyalty one feels towards their clan or family; a sense of belonging and shared identity within a clan. |  The highlanders' fierce clansmanship made them formidable warriors.  高地人的强烈宗族观念使他们成为令人生畏的战士。

The family reunion strengthened the clansmanship among the cousins.  家庭聚会加强了堂兄弟姐妹之间的宗族观念。 | 近义词: tribalism, kinship, fraternity | 反义词: individualism, disloyalty | 词性: noun

词源: From "clan" + "-ship." "Clan" comes from the Scottish Gaelic "clann" meaning "offspring," "family," "descendants."  "-ship" is a suffix forming abstract nouns denoting state, condition, or character.
