metaleptic:/ˌmɛtəˈlɛptɪk/ | adj. | 转喻的；跳跃的；超越时空的 |  A figure of speech in which a word or phrase from one level of narrative is used to represent another, creating a blending of levels and often a sense of surrealism or absurdity. It involves a transgression of the boundaries between levels of narrative or representation.  | The author's metaleptic narrative, where the narrator addresses the reader directly about plot decisions, blurred the lines between fiction and reality. 作者转喻式的叙事手法，叙述者直接向读者讲述情节的决定，模糊了虚构和现实之间的界限。|  The film's metaleptic ending, with characters stepping out of the screen, was both jarring and intriguing.  电影转喻式的结局，角色走出屏幕，既令人不和谐又引人入胜。 | 近义词:  transgressive, self-reflexive, metafictional | 反义词: conventional, traditional, straightforward | 词性: adjective

metalepsis: /ˌmɛtəˈlɛpsɪs/ | n. | 转喻; 跳跃 |  A figure of speech consisting in the use of one word or phrase for another by a kind of substitution or transfer of idea. |  The use of "the pen is mightier than the sword" is an example of metalepsis, substituting the instruments for the activities they represent (writing and warfare). “笔比剑更强大”的使用是转喻的一个例子，用工具代替它们所代表的活动（写作和战争）。 | The poet's frequent use of metalepsis gave his work a dreamlike, disorienting quality. 诗人频繁使用转喻，使他的作品具有梦幻般的、令人迷失方向的特质。| 近义词: metonymy, synecdoche | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but plain, literal language could be considered an opposite concept) | 词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *metalēpsis, from Ancient Greek μετάληψις (metálēpsis, “participation, exchange”), from μεταλαμβάνω (metalambánō, “I participate in, I receive in exchange”), from μετά (metá, “with, after”) + λαμβάνω (lambánō, “I take”).
