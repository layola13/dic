balachong:/ˈbɑːlətʃɒŋ/ (UK), /ˈbɑːlətʃɔːŋ/ (US)| n.|峇拉煎，虾酱|A pungent condiment made from fermented shrimp or prawns, commonly used in Southeast Asian cuisine, particularly in Malaysia, Singapore, and Indonesia. It is typically made by mixing ground shrimp or prawns with salt, chilies, and sometimes sugar, and allowing the mixture to ferment.  The resulting paste is then sun-dried and can be used as a flavoring agent in various dishes.|例句：


She added a spoonful of balachong to the stir-fry. 她往炒菜里加了一勺峇拉煎。


The aroma of balachong filled the kitchen. 厨房里弥漫着峇拉煎的香味。

|近义词：shrimp paste, prawn paste|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps mild spices)|词性:noun
