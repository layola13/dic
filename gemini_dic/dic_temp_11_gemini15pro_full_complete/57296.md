downsize:/ˈdaʊnsaɪz/| v. | 缩小规模，裁员 | To reduce the size of a company or organization by eliminating jobs. |  Many companies were forced to downsize during the recession. 许多公司在经济衰退期间被迫裁员。

The company downsized its workforce by 10%.  该公司裁员10%。 | 近义词：reduce, shrink, lay off | 反义词: expand, grow, hire | 词性: 动词


downsized:/ˈdaʊnsaɪzd/| adj. |  被缩减规模的，被裁员的 | Made smaller by reducing the number of employees. |  He was a downsized executive looking for a new job. 他是一位被裁员的高管，正在寻找新的工作。

The downsized department struggled to cope with the workload.  被缩减规模的部门难以应付工作量。 | 近义词：reduced, shrunk | 反义词: expanded, grown | 词性: 形容词
