overriding:/ˌoʊvərˈraɪdɪŋ/ | adj. | 最重要的；高于一切的；首要的 | Having superior power and influence; dominant. |  The overriding concern was the safety of the passengers. 最重要的考虑是乘客的安全。| Example: His overriding ambition is to become a successful writer. 他最大的抱负就是成为一名成功的作家。 | Synonyms: dominant, principal, paramount, chief, main, primary, foremost, leading, key, central | Antonyms: secondary, minor, subordinate | adjective

overrode:/ˌoʊvərˈroʊd/ | v. | 推翻；否决；不理会；（使）失效 |  To use one's authority to reject a decision or order made by someone at a lower level; To prevail over. | The chairman overrode the committee's decision. 主席否决了委员会的决定。 | Example: The CEO overrode the objections of the marketing team and launched the product anyway. 首席执行官不顾市场团队的反对，还是推出了这款产品。 | Synonyms:  overrule, overturn, cancel, reverse, revoke, veto, countermand, nullify, invalidate | Antonyms: uphold, sustain, approve, ratify, confirm | verb (past tense of override)
