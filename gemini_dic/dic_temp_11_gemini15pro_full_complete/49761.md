demolishment:/dɪˈmɒlɪʃmənt/ /dɪˈmɑːlɪʃmənt/| n. |拆除，摧毁|The act of demolishing or destroying something, especially a building.|The demolition of the old factory made way for a new shopping mall.  旧工厂的拆除为新的购物中心腾出了地方。|Synonyms: destruction, razing, wrecking|Antonyms: construction, building, erection|noun

demolish:/dɪˈmɒlɪʃ/ /dɪˈmɑːlɪʃ/| vt. |拆除，摧毁；驳倒|To destroy (a building, structure, etc.) by knocking it down or breaking it apart; to thoroughly refute an argument or theory.|They demolished the old building to make way for a new park. 他们拆除了旧建筑，以便为新公园腾出地方。 The lawyer demolished the witness's testimony. 律师驳倒了证人的证词。|Synonyms: destroy, raze, wreck, dismantle|Antonyms: construct, build, erect|verb
