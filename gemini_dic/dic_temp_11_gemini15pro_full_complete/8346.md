anti-platonists:/ˌæntiˈpleɪtənɪsts/ | n. |反柏拉图主义者| People who oppose or reject the philosophical theories of Plato, particularly his theory of Forms.|  Anti-platonists argue that abstract concepts like "justice" and "beauty" do not exist independently of human minds. 反柏拉图主义者认为，像“正义”和“美”这样的抽象概念并非独立于人类思维而存在。| synonyms: nominalists, empiricists | antonyms: Platonists, idealists | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "anti-" (against, opposite) + "Platonist" (a follower of Plato's philosophy). "Anti-" comes from Greek "anti" (against, opposite). "Platonist" derives from the name "Plato," the ancient Greek philosopher.
