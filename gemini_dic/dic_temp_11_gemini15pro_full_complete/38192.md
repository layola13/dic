compulsion:/kəmˈpʌlʃən/ | n. | 强迫，冲动 | An irresistible urge to behave in a certain way, especially against one's conscious wishes. | He felt a compulsion to tell her the truth. 他感到一种强烈的冲动，要告诉她真相。 | synonyms: urge, impulse, drive, need, obsession | antonyms: aversion, reluctance | noun

compulsive:/kəmˈpʌlsɪv/ | adj. | 强迫性的，难以控制的 | Resulting from or relating to an irresistible urge to behave in a certain way, especially against one's conscious wishes. | He's a compulsive gambler. 他是个嗜赌成瘾的人。 | synonyms: obsessive, inveterate, addictive, uncontrollable | antonyms: controlled, moderate | adjective

compulsively:/kəmˈpʌlsɪvli/ | adv. | 强迫性地，不由自主地 | In a compulsive manner. | He washes his hands compulsively. 他强迫性地洗手。 | synonyms: obsessively, involuntarily, uncontrollably | antonyms: moderately, restrainedly | adverb

compulsory:/kəmˈpʌlsəri/ | adj. | 必须做的，强制性的 | Required by law or a rule; obligatory. | Attendance at school is compulsory for children.  儿童必须上学。 | synonyms: mandatory, obligatory, required, necessary | antonyms: optional, voluntary | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin compulsiōn- (stem of compulsiō), from Latin compellere ‘to compel’.
