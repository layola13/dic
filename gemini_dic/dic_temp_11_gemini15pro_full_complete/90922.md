hurdler:/ˈhɜːrdlər/ (US) /ˈhɜːdələr/ (UK)| n. |跨栏运动员；克服障碍的人|A person who runs in races that involve jumping over hurdles or a person who overcomes obstacles.|例句：The hurdler cleared all the hurdles effortlessly.  这位跨栏运动员轻松越过了所有栏架。

例句：He's a real hurdler in life, always overcoming challenges. 他是生活中的真正跨栏运动员，总是克服挑战。|近义词：athlete, competitor, obstacle-overcomer|反义词: quitter, defeatist|词性: noun

hurdle:/ˈhɜːrdl/ (US) /ˈhɜːdl/ (UK)| n. v. |栏；障碍；跨栏；克服|A barrier used for jumping over in races, or an obstacle or difficulty.|例句：She tripped over the first hurdle and fell. 她被第一个栏架绊倒了。

例句：He hurdled the fence to escape the dog. 他跨过栅栏以躲避那条狗。|近义词：obstacle, barrier, difficulty, fence, jump, vault |反义词: advantage, assistance |词性: noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *hurdel, *hurdyl, from Old English *hyrdel, from Proto-Germanic *hurdilaz, from Proto-Indo-European *k̂r̥tis (“woven material, wickerwork”). Cognate with German Hürde (“hurdle”).
