enrapturer:/ɪnˈræptʃərər/ | n. |  使人狂喜的人或物 | Someone or something that causes someone to feel great pleasure or delight. | Her beauty was such that she was an enrapturer of all who met her. 她的美貌使所有见过她的人都为之倾倒。| synonyms: charmer, fascinator, captivator | antonyms: bore, nuisance | noun

enrapture:/ɪnˈræptʃər/ | vt. | 使狂喜，使入迷 | To fill with delight, to enchant. | The music enraptured the audience.  音乐使观众陶醉。| synonyms: enchant, captivate, enthrall | antonyms: bore, repel, disgust | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From en- (meaning "to cause to be in" or "to put into") + rapture (from Latin *raptura, from rapere "to seize, carry off").  So, to enrapture means to put someone into a state of rapture.
