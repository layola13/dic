anoplothere:/əˈnɒpləˌθɪər/ | n. | 踝节目兽 | An extinct, hoofed mammal of the Eocene and Oligocene epochs, related to the ruminants and swine, but lacking horns and having a long, heavy tail. |  The anoplothere was a common herbivore in prehistoric Europe. 踝节目兽是史前欧洲常见的食草动物。|  近义词:  Paleotherium (a related extinct mammal) | 反义词:  N/A (extinct animals don't have direct antonyms) | 词性: noun

Etymology: From French *anoplothérium*, from Ancient Greek *ἀν* (an, “without”) + *ὅπλον* (hóplon, “weapon, armor”) + *θηρίον* (thēríon, “wild beast”).  This refers to the animal's lack of horns or other obvious defenses.
