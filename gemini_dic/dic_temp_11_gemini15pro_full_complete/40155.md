contemptuousness:/kənˈtɛmptʃuəsnəs/
|n.|轻蔑，鄙视|The state of feeling or showing contempt; scornfulness.|Her contemptuousness towards her colleagues was evident in her tone of voice. 她对同事的轻蔑从她的语气中就能明显听出来。|Synonyms: disdain, scorn, arrogance, haughtiness|Antonyms: respect, admiration, reverence|noun

contempt:/kənˈtɛmpt/
|n.|轻蔑，鄙视|The feeling that a person or a thing is beneath consideration, worthless, or deserving scorn.|He looked at the politicians with contempt. 他轻蔑地看着那些政客们。|Synonyms: disdain, scorn, derision|Antonyms: respect, admiration, reverence|noun

contemptuous:/kənˈtɛmptʃuəs/
|adj.|轻蔑的，鄙视的|Showing contempt; scornful.|She gave him a contemptuous look. 她轻蔑地看了他一眼。|Synonyms: disdainful, scornful, derisive|Antonyms: respectful, admiring, reverent|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *contemptuosus*, from Latin contemptus (past participle of contemnere “to despise, scorn”), from com- intensive prefix + temnere “to despise.”
