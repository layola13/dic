chromaticisms:/ˌkroʊˈmætɪsɪzəmz/
|n.|半音阶, 色彩主义|The use of notes outside the prevailing diatonic scale, especially in passing notes or to create dissonance or a heightened emotional effect.  The frequent use of chromaticisms in Chopin's nocturnes adds to their expressive character. |
|词源: From Greek "chroma" (color) + "-atic" (relating to) + "-ism" (a doctrine, practice, or characteristic).|
|例句: The composer used chromaticisms to create a sense of unease.  作曲家运用半音阶来营造一种不安的感觉。|

|例句:  The chromaticisms in the melody gave it a haunting quality.  旋律中的半音阶赋予它一种萦绕的特性。|
|近义词:altered tones, accidentals, non-harmonic tones|
|反义词: diatonicism|
|词性: noun (plural)|
