maternalisms: /məˈtɜːrnəlɪzəmz/ | n. | 母性主义，母性化政策 |  Policies, practices, or ideologies that are based on the idea of maternal care and protection, often applied by governments or organizations to groups deemed in need of such care, but sometimes with paternalistic or controlling overtones.  This concept frequently intersects with social welfare programs, public health initiatives, and gender roles. | The government's new maternalisms, while aiming to support mothers, have been criticized for reinforcing traditional gender roles. | 近义词:  social welfare, welfarism, paternalism (sometimes) | 反义词: individualism, self-reliance | 词性: 名词
