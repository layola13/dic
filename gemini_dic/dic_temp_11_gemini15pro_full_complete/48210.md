deconglomerates:/ˌdiːkənˈɡlɒməreɪts/ |v. |分解，拆分（企业集团）| To separate a large business organization into several smaller independent companies. |Example 1: The company deconglomerated its media holdings to focus on its core business.  这家公司拆分了其媒体资产，以专注于其核心业务。

Example 2:  The government encouraged the deconglomeration of large monopolies to promote competition. 政府鼓励大型垄断企业分解，以促进竞争。|Synonyms: break up, dismantle, divest, separate, unbundle |Antonyms: merge, consolidate, amalgamate |Verb


deconglomerate:/ˌdiːkənˈɡlɒmərət/ |n. |分解，拆分（后的公司）|A company that has been separated from a larger conglomerate. |Example 1: The deconglomerate focused on innovative technologies after separating from its parent company.  这家拆分后的公司在脱离母公司后专注于创新技术。

Example 2: The deconglomerate experienced rapid growth after streamlining its operations. 这家拆分后的公司在精简运营后经历了快速增长。|Synonyms: spin-off, subsidiary, division |Antonyms: conglomerate, parent company, holding company |Noun
