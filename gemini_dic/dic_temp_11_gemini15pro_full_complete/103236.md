jettisons:/ˈdʒɛtɪsənz/
| v. |抛弃，丢弃|To throw or drop (something) from a moving vehicle, especially a plane or ship, or to discard (something) as unwanted or burdensome.|The captain jettisoned the cargo to lighten the ship in the storm. 船长在暴风雨中抛弃了货物以减轻船的重量。|近义词：discard, dump, eject, abandon|反义词：retain, keep, preserve|词性:动词
jettison:/ˈdʒɛtɪsən/
| n. |抛弃，丢弃（的行为）|The act of jettisoning something.|The jettison of the fuel tanks was necessary for the plane to land safely. 抛弃燃料箱对于飞机安全着陆是必要的。|近义词：discarding, dumping|反义词：retention, keeping|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "getaison" meaning "a throwing," from "geter" meaning "to throw," from Latin "jactare," frequentative of "jacere" meaning "to throw."
