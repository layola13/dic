incapsulation:/ɪnˌkæpsəˈleɪʃən/ | n. |封装，包封|The act of enclosing something in a capsule or other container; in computing, the bundling of data with the methods that operate on that data.|Example 1: The encapsulation of the medicine ensures its stability.  这种药的封装保证了它的稳定性。

Example 2: Data encapsulation is a key principle of object-oriented programming.  数据封装是面向对象编程的一个关键原则。|近义词：enclosure, packaging, wrapping, bundling (especially in computing)|反义词: exposure, revelation|词性:noun
