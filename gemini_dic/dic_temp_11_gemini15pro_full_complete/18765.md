berm:/bɜːrm/| n.|斜坡，堤岸，路肩|A narrow shelf, path, or ledge typically at the top or bottom of a slope; an artificial ridge or embankment, as along a road.|The cyclists pulled over onto the berm to avoid the traffic.|Synonyms: bank, embankment, shoulder, ledge|Antonyms: ditch, trench|noun
