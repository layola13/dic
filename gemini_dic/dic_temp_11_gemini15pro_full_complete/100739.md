intransigently:/ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒəntli/
adverb
不妥协地，固执地
in a way that is unwilling or refusing to change your opinions or behaviour
He intransigently refused to accept the court's decision. 他坚决拒绝接受法庭的判决。

The union intransigently refused to negotiate. 工会坚决拒绝谈判。


近义词:stubbornly, resolutely, unyieldingly, inflexibly
反义词:flexibly, compromisingly, yieldingly
词性:adverb


ETYMOLOGY->
From intransigent + -ly. Intransigent comes from Spanish intransigente, from in- (not) + transigente (compromising), from Latin transigēns (compromising), present participle of transigere (to come to an understanding).
