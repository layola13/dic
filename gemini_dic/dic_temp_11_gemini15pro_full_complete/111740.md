locust:/ˈloʊkəst/ (US), /ˈləʊkəst/ (UK)| n. |蝗虫；破坏者|A large, migratory grasshopper that often travels in vast swarms and strips areas bare of vegetation. |  Swarms of locusts devoured the crops.  蝗虫群吞噬了庄稼。| synonym: grasshopper, cicada | antonym: None | noun


locust:/ˈloʊkəst/ (US), /ˈləʊkəst/ (UK)| n. |刺槐，洋槐|A North American tree of the pea family with compound leaves and fragrant white flowers.| The locust trees were in full bloom. 刺槐树盛开了。| synonym: acacia, false acacia | antonym: None | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *locust*, from Old French *locuste*, from Latin *locusta* (“lobster, locust, grasshopper”), probably related to *locus* (“place”). The shift in meaning from “lobster” to “locust” likely occurred because of the swarming behavior of both creatures.
