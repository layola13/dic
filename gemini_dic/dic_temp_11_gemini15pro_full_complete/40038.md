consummate:/ˈkɑːnsəmət/ /ˈkɒnsəmət/| vt.|完成；实现|To make (a marriage or relationship) complete by having sexual intercourse|
They consummated their marriage on their honeymoon. 他们在蜜月旅行中圆房。|近义词：complete, finish, conclude, finalize|反义词：begin, start, initiate|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin consummatus, past participle of consummare "to sum up, complete, perfect," from Latin com-, intensive prefix (see com-), + summa "sum, total" (from PIE root *tep- "to extend").  The adjective sense of "perfect, complete" is from late 14c. The verb sense of "to complete (a marriage) by sexual intercourse" is from 1737.
