draughts:/drɑːfts/ /dræfts/
noun
西洋跳棋; (尤指房间、烟囱等供空气流通的) 气流
A game played by two people on a checkered board, each player having twelve pieces that can be moved diagonally to capture the opponent's pieces.  Also, a current of air (especially one that moves through a room, chimney, etc.)
例句：We played a game of draughts. 我们玩了一盘西洋跳棋。

例句：There's a terrible draught in this room. 这个房间里有一股很糟糕的穿堂风。

近义词：checkers (US), draft (US, air current)
反义词：(For air current) stillness, calm
词性:noun

draught:/drɑːft/ /dræft/
noun
(尤指从门窗缝隙吹进来的) 冷风; 通风; (尤指喝啤酒的) 一口，一大口;  草稿; 汇票
A current of air (especially one that moves through a room, chimney, etc.); an act of drinking or swallowing a liquid; a preliminary version of a piece of writing; a written order to pay a specified sum.
例句：She took a long draught of beer. 她喝了一大口啤酒。

例句：This is only the first draught of my novel. 这只是我小说的初稿。

近义词：draft (US), gulp, swig (for drinking), sketch, outline (for writing)
反义词：(For air current) stillness, calm
词性:noun
