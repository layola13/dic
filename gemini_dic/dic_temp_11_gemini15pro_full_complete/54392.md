disillusive:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːsɪv/
adjective
使人醒悟的；使人不再抱幻想的；令人失望的
Causing disillusionment; tending to destroy illusions or naive beliefs.
The disillusive experience of war changed his perspective on life.  战争令人失望的经历改变了他对人生的看法。
近义词:disappointing, disillusioning, sobering
反义词:encouraging, inspiring, heartening
词性:adjective


disillusion:/ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒən/
noun
vt.
失望；醒悟；不再抱幻想
A feeling of disappointment resulting from the discovery that something is not as good as one believed it to be.  To cause (someone) to stop believing that something is good, valuable, or true.
The disillusion of finding out Santa wasn't real was tough for the young boy. 发现圣诞老人不是真的，这让小男孩很失望。
The harsh realities of life disillusioned the idealistic young man.  残酷的现实让这位理想主义的年轻人不再抱有幻想。
近义词:disappointment, disenchantment
反义词:hope, faith, belief
词性:noun, verb

词源:
From the French word "desillusionner," from "des-" (meaning "away from") and "illusion" (meaning "illusion"). 最终源自拉丁语 "illusio"，意思是“嘲笑，戏弄”。
