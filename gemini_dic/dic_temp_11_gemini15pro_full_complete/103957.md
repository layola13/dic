judiciously:/dʒuˈdɪʃəsli/| adv.|明智地；审慎地；判断正确地|With good judgment or sense; wisely.  Showing or done with good judgment or sense.|She judiciously chose to remain silent during the heated argument. 她明智地选择在激烈的争论中保持沉默。|Synonyms: wisely, prudently, sensibly, discreetly, carefully, thoughtfully|Antonyms: foolishly, recklessly, unwisely, imprudently, rashly|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *judiciousli*, from Old French *judicieusement*, from Medieval Latin *iudiciosus* "judicious" + *-ment* (suffix forming adverbs). *iudiciosus* is from Latin *iudicium* "judgment, trial, judicial sentence" + *-osus* (suffix forming adjectives meaning 'full of, given to').  *iudicium* is from *iudex* "judge" (from *ius* "right, law" + *-dex* 'doer, agent').
