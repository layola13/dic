detouring:/ˌdiːˈtʊərɪŋ/
| n. |绕行，绕道|The act of taking a different route than the usual or intended one, typically to avoid an obstruction or problem.|Due to the road closure, we were detouring through the countryside. 由于道路封闭，我们绕道穿过乡间。|近义词：diverting, rerouting|反义词：directing|v.
detouring:/ˌdiːˈtʊərɪŋ/
| adj. |绕道的|Taking a different route than the usual or intended one.|The detouring traffic caused significant delays. 绕行的车辆造成了严重的延误。|近义词：diverting, circuitous|反义词：direct|adj.
detour:/ˈdiːtʊr/
| n. |绕道，绕行的路|A longer or roundabout route taken to avoid something or to visit somewhere along the way.|The accident forced us to take a detour.  事故迫使我们绕道而行。|近义词：bypass, diversion|反义词：direct route|n.
detour:/diːˈtʊr/
| v. |绕道，绕行|To take a different route than the usual or intended one.|We had to detour around the flooded area. 我们不得不绕过洪水泛滥的地区。|近义词：divert, bypass|反义词：go direct|v.


Etymology:
From the Old French *destorner "to turn aside, avert," from des- "away" + torner "to turn."  Entered English in the 18th century.
