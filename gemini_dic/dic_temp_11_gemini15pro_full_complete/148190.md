overshadowing:/ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊɪŋ/ | n. | 使失色；使相形见绌；蒙上阴影 | to make someone or something seem less important or impressive | Her success was overshadowed by her brother's. 她的成功被她哥哥的成就所掩盖。 | synonyms: eclipse, obscure, dwarf | antonyms: highlight, emphasize | noun


overshadow:/ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊ/ | vt. |  使失色；使相形见绌；蒙上阴影 | to make someone or something seem less important or impressive | The new skyscraper overshadowed the old buildings. 这座新的摩天大楼使旧建筑相形见绌。| synonyms: eclipse, obscure, dwarf | antonyms: highlight, emphasize | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From over + shadow + -ing.  "Over" implies exceeding or surpassing, while "shadow" refers to a darkened area. Thus, overshadowing literally means casting a large shadow over something, making it less noticeable or important.
