fawn:/fɔːn/| n. |幼鹿|A young deer, especially one less than a year old.|例句：The fawn hid in the tall grass. 幼鹿躲在高高的草丛里。

例句：The fawn, still spotted, stayed close to its mother.  身上仍然有斑点的小鹿紧紧地挨着它的母亲。

fawn:/fɔːn/| vi. |奉承，讨好|To seek favor or attention by flattery and obsequious behavior.|例句：The courtiers fawned over the king. 朝臣们向国王献媚。

例句：He fawned on his boss in hopes of a promotion.  他为了升职而讨好老板。


fawn:/fɔːn/| adj. |浅黄褐色的|A light yellowish-brown color.|例句：She wore a fawn-colored dress. 她穿着一件浅黄褐色的连衣裙。

例句：The walls were painted a soft fawn color.  墙壁被漆成了柔和的浅黄褐色。



近义词:young deer (n.), flatter, grovel (v.), beige, tan (adj.)
反义词:mature deer (n.), criticize, defy (v.)
词性:noun, verb, adjective
词源:From Old French *faon, from Vulgar Latin *fetōnem, accusative of *fetō, from Latin fetus (“offspring, childbirth”).
