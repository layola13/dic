haranguers:/həˈræŋərz/| n. | 演说者；高谈阔论者；训斥者 | People who deliver long, passionate, and often critical speeches; those who lecture or scold someone at length.  | The haranguers gathered in the square to protest the new law.  广场上聚集了抗议新法律的演说者。| The teacher acted as a haranguer, criticizing the students for their lack of effort. 老师像个训斥者一样，批评学生缺乏努力。 |  Synonyms: orators, lecturers, scolds, ranters  | Antonyms: listeners, audience, students | noun

harangue:/həˈræŋ/| n. vt. | 长篇大论；高谈阔论；训斥；| A long, passionate, and often critical speech; to lecture or scold someone at length. |  The politician's harangue lasted for over an hour. 这位政客的长篇大论持续了一个多小时。 |  He harangued the crowd about the importance of voting.  他向人群慷慨陈词，讲述投票的重要性。 | Synonyms: tirade, diatribe, lecture, rant | Antonyms: discussion, dialogue, conversation | noun, verb
