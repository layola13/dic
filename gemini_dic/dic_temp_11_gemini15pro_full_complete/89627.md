hook:/hʊk/| n. vt. vi.|钩，钩状物；陷阱，圈套；(拳击中的)勾拳；吸引人的东西|A curved piece of metal or other hard material used for catching, holding, or pulling something. Also, a method or trick for attracting attention or business; something that catches someone's attention or interest.|The fish was caught on a hook. 这条鱼上钩了。|近义词：snag, catch, clasp, fastener, lure, gimmick|反义词：release, detach|词性:名词,动词

hook:/hʊk/| vt. vi.|钩住，挂住；(拳击中)用勾拳击打；吸引，引诱|To catch or fasten something with a hook; to hit someone with a hook punch in boxing; to attract someone's attention or interest.|He hooked the fish and reeled it in. 他钓到了鱼并把它卷了进来。|近义词：snag, catch, secure, attract, entice|反义词：release, detach, repel|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *hōc*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *haak* and German *Haken*.

PHRASE->
Hook up (with someone):  与某人见面/联系/开始一段感情  I hooked up with some old friends last night. 昨晚我和一些老朋友聚了聚。
Hook something up: 连接  Can you hook up the stereo? 你能连接一下音响吗？
Off the hook: 脱离困境/摆脱责任 He's finally off the hook for that old debt. 他终于摆脱了那笔旧债。
By hook or by crook: 不择手段  I'll get that job by hook or by crook. 我要不择手段地得到那份工作。
Hook, line, and sinker: 完全地，彻底地  He fell for the story hook, line, and sinker. 他完全相信了那个故事。
