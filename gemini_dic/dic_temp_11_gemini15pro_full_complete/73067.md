footprint:/ˈfʊtprɪnt/| n.|足迹，脚印；影响，印记|The mark left by a foot, especially a bare foot. Also, the impact or effect that something has on the environment or a situation.|例句：The children's footprints were clearly visible in the wet sand. 孩子们的脚印在湿沙上清晰可见。

例句：The company is trying to reduce its environmental footprint.  这家公司正在努力减少其环境足迹。|近义词：trace, mark, impression, impact|反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider "preservation" in an environmental context)|noun

footprint:/ˈfʊtprɪnt/| n.|（建筑物、机器等的）占地面积|The area occupied by a building or machine.|例句：The new building has a smaller footprint than the old one. 新建筑的占地面积比旧的小。

例句：The machine's footprint is surprisingly small. 这台机器的占地面积出奇的小。|近义词：area, size, dimensions|反义词: (No direct antonym)|noun
