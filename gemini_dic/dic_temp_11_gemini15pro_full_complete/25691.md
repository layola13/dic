bunker:/ˈbʌŋkər/| n. v. |掩体，沙坑；燃料舱|A reinforced underground shelter, typically for use in wartime. A sandy hollow or area of rough ground, especially on a golf course. A large container or compartment for storing fuel, typically on a ship or aircraft.|The soldiers took shelter in the bunker during the bombardment. 士兵们在轰炸期间躲进了掩体。|近义词：shelter, dugout, trap, hazard (in golf)|反义词: open ground, fairway (in golf)|词性: noun, verb

bunker:/ˈbʌŋkər/| v. |加燃料；躲藏|To supply a ship or aircraft with fuel. To hide or take shelter.|The ship was bunkered in port before its long voyage. 这艘船在远航前在港口加了燃料。|近义词：refuel, hide, shelter|反义词: expose|词性: verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Scots *bunker* "bench, seat," perhaps from or related to Dutch *banker* "bench." The sense of "coal bin" arose in the 19th century, perhaps influenced by the shape. The military sense of "fortified shelter" developed during World War I.
