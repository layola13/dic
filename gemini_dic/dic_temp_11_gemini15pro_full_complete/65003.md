estray:/ɪˈstreɪ/ | n. | 走失的家畜；无主物 | A domestic animal found wandering at large or away from its owner's premises; an unclaimed article or piece of property. |  The farmer took the estray cow to the local pound. 农民把走失的牛送到了当地的牲畜栏。| synonyms: stray, waif, lost property | antonyms: owned property, claimed possession | noun

estray:/ɪˈstreɪ/ | adj. | 走失的；迷路的 |  (Of an animal) straying or having strayed, especially from its owner's property.  | The estray dog was eventually reunited with its owner.  这只走失的狗最终与它的主人团聚了。 | synonyms: straying, wandering, lost | antonyms: found, located, owned | adjective

estray:/ɪˈstreɪ/ | v. | 走失；迷路 | To stray, wander, or roam from one's proper place or limits. | The sheep estrayed from the flock during the storm. 暴风雨中，羊偏离了羊群。| synonyms: stray, wander, roam | antonyms: stay, remain, locate | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *estraier "to stray," from Latin extra "outside, beyond" + vagari "to wander."
