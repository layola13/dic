exaggeratingly: /ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtɪŋli/ | adv. | 夸张地 | in a way that makes something seem larger, more important, better, or worse than it really is |  He was exaggeratingly polite. 他过分客气了。|  She described the incident exaggeratingly, making it sound much worse than it actually was. 她夸大了事情的经过，使它听起来比实际情况糟糕得多。| 近义词: overstatedly, hyperbolically, dramatically | 反义词: understatedly, moderately, realistically | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From exaggerate + -ing + -ly.  Exaggerate comes from the Latin "exaggeratus," past participle of "exaggerare," meaning "to heap up, increase, aggravate," from "ex-" meaning "out" + "agger" meaning "heap of earth."
