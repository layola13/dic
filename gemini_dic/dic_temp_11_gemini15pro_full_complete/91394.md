hydrotropic:/ˌhaɪdrəˈtrɒpɪk/ | adj. | 水溶助长的，亲水的 |  Relating to or denoting a substance that increases the ability of a solvent, especially water, to dissolve another substance. |  Hydrotropic salts can increase the solubility of certain drugs in water. 水溶助长盐可以增加某些药物在水中的溶解度。 |  Synonyms: solubilizing,  | Antonyms: insolubilizing | adjective


hydrotrope:/ˈhaɪdrəˌtrəʊp/ | n. | 水溶助长剂 | A substance that increases the ability of a solvent, especially water, to dissolve another substance. | Sodium benzoate is a common hydrotrope used in the pharmaceutical industry.  苯甲酸钠是制药工业中常用的水溶助长剂。 | Synonyms: solubilizer | Antonyms:  precipitant | noun


Etymology:  From hydro- (water) + -tropic (turning, changing).
