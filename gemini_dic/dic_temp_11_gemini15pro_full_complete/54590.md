dismantle:/dɪsˈmæntl/ | v. | 拆除，拆卸 | To take apart a machine or structure so that it is in separate pieces. |  The workers dismantled the old bridge. 工人们拆除了旧桥。|  The mechanic dismantled the engine to find the problem.  机械师拆卸了发动机以查找问题。|  disassemble, take apart | assemble, construct | verb

dismantled:/dɪsˈmæntld/ | adj. | 已拆除的，已拆卸的 | Taken apart; disassembled. | The dismantled clock lay in pieces on the table.  拆卸的钟表零件散落在桌子上。| The dismantled stage set was stored in the warehouse. 拆卸的舞台布景被存放在仓库里。|  disassembled, taken apart | assembled, constructed | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desmanteler, from des- "apart" + manteler "to cloak, to cover with a mantle".  The original meaning was to strip a castle of its defenses or a person of their clothes.  The modern meaning of taking something apart developed later.
