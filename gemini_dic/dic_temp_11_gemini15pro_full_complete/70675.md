finagling:/fɪˈneɪɡlɪŋ/
n.|欺骗，哄骗，诈取|The act of obtaining something by trickery or devious means.|He got the tickets through some finagling with the box office.|近义词：scheming, manipulating, maneuvering, swindling|反义词: honesty, straightforwardness|词性:名词

finagle:/fɪˈneɪɡl/
v.|欺骗，哄骗，诈取|To obtain something by trickery or devious means.|She finagled a free upgrade to first class.|近义词：wangle, wheedle, coax, cajole|反义词: earn, deserve|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
Origin uncertain, possibly from German *fingern ("to handle, finger").  First recorded in American English in the mid-19th century.
