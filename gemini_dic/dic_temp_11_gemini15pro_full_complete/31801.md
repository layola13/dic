checkmated:/ˈtʃɛkmeɪtɪd/
|adj.|将死的；被彻底击败的；束手无策的|Being put in a position in chess where one's king is under immediate attack and cannot escape.  Figuratively, it means being defeated or thwarted completely.|
Example 1:  He was checkmated by his opponent's clever move. 他被对手的巧妙举动将死了。

Example 2:  The company felt checkmated by the new regulations.  这家公司感觉被新规定束手无策了。
|Synonym: defeated, thwarted, stymied, outmaneuvered|
|Antonym: victorious, triumphant|
|Word type: adjective (past participle used as adjective)|


checkmate:/ˈtʃɛkmeɪt/
|n.|将死；僵局|The situation in chess when the king is under attack and cannot escape. Also used figuratively to describe a hopeless situation.|
Example 1: The opponent resigned before checkmate. 对手在被将死前认输了。

Example 2:  The new evidence put the prosecution in checkmate. 新的证据使控方陷入僵局。
|Synonym: stalemate, deadlock, impasse|
|Antonym: advantage, breakthrough|
|Word type: noun|



checkmate:/ˈtʃɛkmeɪt/
|vt.|将死；使彻底失败；使束手无策|To put (an opponent's king) in checkmate. Figuratively, to defeat or thwart completely.|
Example 1:  She checkmated her opponent in four moves.  她四步将死了对手。

Example 2: The unexpected lawsuit checkmated their plans for expansion.  这起意外的诉讼彻底挫败了他们的扩张计划。
|Synonym: defeat, thwart, stymie, outmaneuver|
|Antonym: surrender, yield|
|Word type: verb (transitive)|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *eschec mat ("checkmate"), from Arabic *šāh māt, from Persian *šāh māt (“the king is helpless/defeated/stuck/unable to escape").  Literally, "the king is dead" is a common misconception.
