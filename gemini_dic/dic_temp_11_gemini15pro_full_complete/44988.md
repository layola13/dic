crystallized:/ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd/| adj. |结晶的；明确的；固定的|Having become fixed and definite in form or structure; (of sugar) having formed crystals.|The sugar syrup crystallized as it cooled. 糖浆冷却后结晶了。|Synonyms: solidified, hardened, congealed, set, jelled|Antonyms: fluid, liquid, molten, melted|adjective


crystallize:/ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd/| v. |结晶；使明确；使固定|Cause to form crystals or assume crystalline form; make (something) definite or clear-cut.|The events of that day crystallized his determination to leave. 那一天的事件坚定了他要离开的决心。|Synonyms: solidify, harden, congealed, set, jelled, coalesce, consolidate|Antonyms: dissolve, melt, liquefy|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cristalliser*, from cristal ("crystal") + -iser (suffix forming verbs from adjectives). Cristal is from Latin crystallum, from Ancient Greek κρύσταλλος (krústallos, “ice, crystal”), from κρύος (krúos, “icy cold, frost”).
