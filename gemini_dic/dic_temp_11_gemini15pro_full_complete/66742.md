exorcist:/ˈɛksɔːrsɪst/| n.|驱魔师，驱邪者|A person who expels or attempts to expel evil spirits from a person or place believed to be possessed.|The local priest acted as an exorcist when the young boy began exhibiting strange behavior.|synonyms: caster out, expeller of demons|antonyms: N/A|noun

exorcise:/ˈɛksɔːrsaɪz/| v.|驱魔，驱邪；除去（坏念头等）|To drive out or attempt to drive out (an evil spirit) from a person or place, or to purge something of a troublesome element.|The priest exorcised the demons from the possessed woman.  He tried to exorcise the painful memories from his mind.|synonyms: expel, cast out, purge|antonyms: invite, welcome|verb
