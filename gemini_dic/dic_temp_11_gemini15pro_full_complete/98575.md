institutionalization:/ˌɪnstɪˌtjuːʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |制度化；机构化|The process of establishing something as a convention or norm in an organization or culture, or the confinement of someone within an institution. |The institutionalization of racism is a deep-rooted problem. 种族主义的制度化是一个根深蒂固的问题。

The institutionalization of children with disabilities can have negative consequences. 将残疾儿童送进机构可能会有负面后果。|近义词：establishment, formalization, systematization |反义词: informality, disorganization |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From institution + -al + -ization.  "Institution" comes from the Latin "institutio" meaning "establishment" or "instruction."  "-al" forms adjectives from nouns. "-ization" is a suffix forming nouns meaning the act or process of making something or someone into a specified state.
