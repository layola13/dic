词源:

From Middle English *armes, *ermes, from Old English earmas (“arms, front legs, forelegs”), from Proto-Germanic *armaz (“arm”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ermo- (“shoulder, foreleg”).  The plural form "jarms" likely arose as a humorous or playful variation of "arms."
