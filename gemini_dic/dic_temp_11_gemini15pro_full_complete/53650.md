dischargees:/dɪsˌtʃɑːrˈdʒiːz/
n.
被解雇者；被释放者；退伍军人；出院病人
People who have been discharged from a place or situation, such as a hospital, the military, or a job.
The hospital is responsible for the care of its dischargees.  这家医院负责照顾其出院病人。
The government is working on programs to help military dischargees reintegrate into civilian life. 政府正在制定方案，帮助退伍军人重新融入平民生活。
近义词:ex-servicemen, veterans,  patients, releasees
反义词:employees, recruits, patients (depending on context)
词性:noun (plural)


discharge:/dɪsˈtʃɑːrdʒ/
v.
排出；释放；解雇；卸货
To allow someone or something to leave a place, such as a hospital, prison, or the military.
The doctor discharged the patient from the hospital. 医生让病人出院了。
The company discharged him for misconduct. 公司因行为不端解雇了他。
近义词:release, dismiss, expel
反义词:admit, hire, retain
词性:verb


discharge:/ˈdɪsˌtʃɑːrdʒ/
n.
排出；释放；解雇；卸货
The act of releasing someone or something from a place or situation.
The discharge from the factory polluted the river. 工厂的排放物污染了河流。
He received an honorable discharge from the army. 他光荣退伍。
近义词:release, dismissal, expulsion
反义词:admission, hiring, retention
词性:noun


词源: From Old French deschargier, from des- (expressing reversal) + charger "to load".
