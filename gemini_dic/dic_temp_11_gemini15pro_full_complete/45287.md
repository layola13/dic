cultured:/ˈkʌltʃərd/| adj.|有教养的，有文化的，文雅的；人工培养的|Having or showing good education, manners, and taste; grown or produced under artificial conditions.|She is a cultured woman with a refined taste in art. 她是一位有教养的女性，对艺术有高雅的品味。|The bacteria were cultured in a petri dish. 这些细菌是在培养皿中培养的。|Synonyms: refined, cultivated, educated, sophisticated (for people); cultivated, grown (for things) |Antonyms: uncultured, uncultivated, ignorant, boorish (for people); wild, natural (for things)|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the past participle of the verb "culture," which comes from the Latin word "cultura," meaning "cultivation" or "tilling."  The original meaning referred to the cultivation of land or crops.  Over time, the meaning expanded to encompass the cultivation of the mind and manners.
