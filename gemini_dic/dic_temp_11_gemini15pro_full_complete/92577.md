i'd:/aɪd/ | contraction | 我会; 我要; 我是 | Contraction of "I would," "I had," or "I should." | I'd like a cup of coffee. 我想要一杯咖啡。| I'd finished my work before he arrived. 在他到达之前我已经完成了我的工作。| synonyms: (depending on context) I would, I had, I should | antonyms: (depending on context) I wouldn't, I hadn't, I shouldn't | contraction


USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
N/A - i'd is not typically used in simple present tense.


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
N/A - i'd is not typically used in present continuous tense.


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
I'd already eaten when they arrived.  他们到达时我已经吃过了。
She'd seen the movie twice before I even saw it once. 在我 siquiera 看过一次之前，她已经看过这部电影两次了。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
I'd been working for hours when the power went out.  停电时我已经工作了好几个小时了。
They'd been waiting for the bus for over an hour. 他们等公交车已经等了一个多小时了。


一般过去时 (Simple Past):
N/A - Though 'I'd' can be a contraction of 'I had,' its use in simple past is replaced by clearer past perfect usage.


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):
N/A -  i'd is not typically used in past continuous tense.


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):
I'd forgotten my keys at home. 我把钥匙忘在家里了。
She'd already left by the time I arrived. 我到达时她已经离开了。


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):
I'd been studying English for five years before I moved to the US. 在我搬到美国之前，我已经学习英语五年了。
He'd been playing the piano since he was a child. 他从小就一直在弹钢琴。


一般将来时 (Simple Future):
I'd go to the party, but I have to work. 我想去参加派对，但我得工作。 (I'd = I would)
I'd rather stay home tonight. 我今晚宁愿待在家里。(I'd = I would)


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
N/A - i'd is not typically used in future continuous tense.


将来完成时 (Future Perfect):
By next year, I'd have finished my degree.  到明年，我将完成我的学位。(I'd = I would have)
By the time she arrives, I'd have cooked dinner. 到她到达时，我将已经做好晚饭了。(I'd = I would have)


将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):
N/A - i'd is not typically used in future perfect continuous tense.


虚拟语气:
I'd help you if I could. 如果可以的话，我会帮助你。（I'd = I would)
I'd rather be at the beach right now. 我宁愿现在在海滩。（I'd = I would）


被动语态:
N/A - i'd is not used in passive voice.


疑问句:
N/A - Though 'I'd' can be part of a question, it doesn't form the question itself.


否定句:

I told him I'd not be attending the meeting. 我告诉他我不会参加会议。(I'd = I would)
