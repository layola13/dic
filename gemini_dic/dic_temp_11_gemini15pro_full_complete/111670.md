lock-ins:/ˈlɒkɪnz/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkɪnz/ (US)| n.| 锁定；禁闭；（尤指酒吧）关门后顾客仍可继续喝酒；|An event where a group of people are intentionally kept inside a building or place, often for a specific purpose such as a party, sleepover, or promotional event.|The library held a lock-in for teenagers over the summer.  图书馆在夏天为青少年举办了一场通宵活动。|The store had a lock-in sale where shoppers could get huge discounts. 这家商店举办了一场关门促销活动，购物者可以获得巨大的折扣。| synonyms: confinement, shutdown| antonyms: release, opening| noun


lock-in:/ˈlɒkɪn/ (UK) /ˈlɑːkɪn/ (US)| n.| 锁定；禁闭；（尤指酒吧）关门后顾客仍可继续喝酒；|A situation where a customer is dependent on a particular vendor or product, often due to high switching costs or proprietary technology.|Once you choose a software platform, you're often stuck with a lock-in. 一旦你选择了一个软件平台，你往往就会被锁定。|Many businesses are trying to avoid vendor lock-in by using open-source software. 许多企业正试图通过使用开源软件来避免供应商锁定。| synonyms: dependence, tie-in| antonyms: independence, freedom| noun
