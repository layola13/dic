hercules:/ˈhɜːrkjʊˌliːz/ (US) /ˈhɜːkjʊˌliːz/ (UK)| n.|赫拉克勒斯;大力神;大力士|A hero of extraordinary strength and courage in Greek and Roman mythology, famous for his twelve labors. He is the Roman equivalent of the Greek Heracles.|Hercules is often depicted holding a club and wearing a lion skin. 赫拉克勒斯经常被描绘成手持棍棒，身披狮皮。|synonyms: Heracles, strongman, hero|antonyms: coward, weakling|noun
