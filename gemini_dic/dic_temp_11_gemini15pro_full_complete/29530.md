catbird seat:/ˈkætˌbɜːrd ˈsiːt/ (US), /ˈkætˌbəːd ˈsiːt/ (UK)| n.|有利地位，有利形势|An advantageous position; a position of power or prominence.  Originates from baseball announcer Red Barber's colorful description of a desirable commentary position.|He was in the catbird seat, having the support of both factions.|Synonyms:  vantage point, position of strength, driver's seat|Antonyms: disadvantage, precarious position|noun
