authoritarianisms:/əˌθɔːrɪˈtɛəriənɪzəmz/
n.
专制主义，独裁主义（复数）
Systems of government or rule characterized by absolute or blind obedience to authority, against individual freedom and related to the expectation of unquestioning obedience from the ruled.
Many countries in the 20th century suffered under brutal authoritarianisms.  许多20世纪的国家遭受了残酷的专制主义统治。
The rise of authoritarianisms around the world is a worrying trend. 全球专制主义的兴起是一个令人担忧的趋势。
近义词: dictatorships, tyrannies, despotisms
反义词: democracies, libertarianisms
词性: 名词

Etymology: From authoritarian + -ism (suffix forming nouns of action, state, condition, doctrine). Authoritarian is from authority + -arian (suffix forming adjectives and nouns).  Authority traces back to Latin auctoritas "official power, influence, command," from auctor "author, originator, founder."
