cheapskate: /ˈtʃiːpskeɪt/ | n. |吝啬鬼，小气鬼|A person who is unwilling to spend money, especially on other people. | He's such a cheapskate; he never offers to buy a round of drinks. 他真是个吝啬鬼，从来不主动请客喝酒。| Example: My uncle is a real cheapskate; he reuses tea bags! 我叔叔真是个小气鬼；他重复使用茶包！| Synonyms: miser, tightwad, penny-pincher | Antonyms: spendthrift, big spender | Noun

Etymology:  A combination of "cheap" and "skate." "Skate" was likely added as a rhyming slang element, and possibly carried a negative connotation of someone who is "cheap" and also untrustworthy or shifty.
