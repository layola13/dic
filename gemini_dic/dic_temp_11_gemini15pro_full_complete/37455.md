commissary: /ˈkɒmɪˌsɛri/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmɪˌsɛri/ (US)| n. | 军需官；代表；委员| An officer in charge of supplies, especially for an army; a deputy or delegate; a member of a committee. | He served as a commissary during the war, ensuring troops received adequate provisions.  他在战争期间担任军需官，确保部队得到充足的供应。| Synonyms: officer, representative, delegate, agent, deputy | Antonyms:  None (lack of a direct opposite concept)| noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *commissarius "one entrusted with a commission," from Latin commissus, past participle of committere "to commit, entrust."
