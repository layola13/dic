compurgator:/kəmˈpɜːrɡeɪtər/ | n. | 宣誓作证者；同誓者 | A person who swore an oath to support another person's innocence in a medieval trial.  In medieval law, a person who swore an oath to support the defendant's innocence or oath. | Example 1: The accused man called upon several compurgators to attest to his good character. 被告男子叫来几个宣誓作证者来证明他的良好品格。

Example 2:  In the absence of other evidence, the compurgators' oaths were crucial to the defendant's acquittal.  由于缺乏其他证据，宣誓者的誓言对被告的无罪释放至关重要。| Synonym: oath-helper, witness | Antonym: accuser, prosecutor | Noun
