metempiricism:/ˌmɛtəmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/| n.|形而上学经验主义; 超验经验主义|A philosophical position that maintains that some knowledge is derived from experience, but that there are also other sources of knowledge that transcend experience, often involving intuition, revelation, or a priori reasoning.| Kant's philosophy is sometimes classified as a form of metempiricism, as it incorporates both empirical and transcendental elements. 康德的哲学有时被归类为一种超验经验主义，因为它包含了经验主义和先验主义的元素。

Metempiricism allows for the possibility of knowledge about things that are not directly observable. 超验经验主义承认关于不可直接观察事物的知识的可能性。|近义词：transcendentalism, metaphysics|反义词：empiricism, positivism|词性: noun
