holing-up:/ˈhəʊlɪŋ ʌp/ (US) /ˈhəʊlɪŋ ʌp/ (UK)| v. |躲藏，藏匿|The robbers were holing up in a deserted warehouse.  抢劫犯藏匿在一个废弃的仓库里。|例句：They were holing up in a cabin in the mountains. 他们躲在山里的小屋里。|近义词：hiding, sheltering, concealing|反义词:emerging, appearing|词性:动词

Etymology: "hole" (n.) + "up" (adv.)

"Hole" traces back to Old English "hol," meaning "cavity, hollow place."  "Up" comes from Old English "up," meaning "upward, higher."  The combination "holing up" signifies seeking refuge in a secluded or concealed location, much like an animal in its burrow.
