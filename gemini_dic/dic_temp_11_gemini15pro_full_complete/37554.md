commonweal:/ˈkɒmənwiːl/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmənwiːl/ (US)| n.|公益，公共福利，国家|The state or condition of the people considered as a whole, especially in terms of their welfare.|例句：They felt it was their duty to work for the commonweal. 他们觉得为公众谋福利是他们的责任。

例句：The politician dedicated his life to the commonweal. 这位政治家毕生致力于公共福利。|近义词：public good, general welfare, common good|反义词：private interest, individual gain|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *comoun wele*, from Old English *gemǣne wela* (“common wealth, commonweal”), equivalent to *gemǣne* ("common") + *wela* ("wealth, well-being, prosperity, bliss"). Cognate with Scots *commonweill* (“commonwealth, republic”), West Frisian *gemeenweel* (“common good”).  Surface analysis is *common* + *weal*.
