battener:/ˈbætənər/ (US), /ˈbætənə(r)/ (UK)| n. |压条，板条；使…牢固的人（或物）|A long, thin strip of wood or metal used to strengthen or support something.|The battens on the roof held the tiles in place. 屋顶上的板条固定着瓦片。|近义词：strip, lath, support, brace|反义词: (No direct antonym, consider "weakener") |noun

batten:/ˈbætn/ (US), /ˈbætn/ (UK)| v. |用板条固定；使…牢固；准备…以应付（尤指坏天气）; 暴饮暴食; 贪婪地吃; (on/upon something)  |To fasten or secure with battens; to thrive; to feast.|They battened down the hatches before the storm.  他们在暴风雨来临前用板条固定了舱口。  |近义词：secure, fasten, reinforce, thrive, feast (depending on the meaning) |反义词: loosen, weaken (depending on the meaning) |verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *batoner "to beat, cudgel," from Old North French baton "stick, club."  The noun sense of "strip of wood" likely developed from the verb's association with reinforcing or securing something with sticks.
