acanthus:/əˈkænθəs/| n.|刺；棘；（建筑）叶形装饰；老鼠簕|A thorny or prickly outgrowth, especially of the plant acanthus; also, a stylized representation of such a plant, used as a motif in architectural decoration.|The acanthus leaves were carved intricately into the stone.|近义词：thorn, prickle, spine, acanth|反义词:smoothness|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *acanthus*, from Ancient Greek *ἄκανθος (ákanthos)* meaning "thorn" or a particular plant with thorny leaves.  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ḱengʰ- (“to prick”)*.
