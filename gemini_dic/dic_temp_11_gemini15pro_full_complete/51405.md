determinisms: /dɪˈtɜːmɪnɪzəmz/ | n. | 决定论 | The doctrine that all events, including human action, are ultimately determined by causes external to the will.  |  Some philosophers argue for hard determinism, believing free will is an illusion. 一些哲学家支持强决定论，认为自由意志是一种幻觉。

| Example:  The novel explores the conflict between free will and determinism. 这部小说探讨了自由意志与决定论之间的冲突。

| Example:  He rejected determinism and embraced the idea of personal responsibility. 他拒绝决定论，拥护个人责任的理念。

| Synonym:  fatalism, predestination, necessity | Antonym:  free will, indeterminism | Noun
