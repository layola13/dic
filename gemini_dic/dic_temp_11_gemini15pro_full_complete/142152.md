Oriskany: /ˌɔːrɪˈskeɪni/ | n. | 奥里斯卡尼 |  A place name, specifically referring to a town in New York and famously, the USS Oriskany, an Essex-class aircraft carrier. The name derives from an Iroquois word thought to mean "nettles growing in the water". | Example: The USS Oriskany was intentionally sunk to create an artificial reef. 奥里斯卡尼号航空母舰被故意沉没以建造人工鱼礁。| Example: My grandparents are from Oriskany, New York. 我的祖父母来自纽约州的奥里斯卡尼。 | Synonym: (For the ship) aircraft carrier, warship | Antonym: N/A | Noun
