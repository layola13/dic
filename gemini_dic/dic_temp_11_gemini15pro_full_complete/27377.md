came-down:/ˈkeɪm daʊn/ (British) /ˈkeɪm dɑʊn/ (American)| adj.| 衰败的；落魄的；没落的 |Reduced in status, dignity, or wealth; declined.| The once grand hotel now had a rather come-down feel. 这家曾经宏伟的酒店现在感觉相当衰败。|  近义词：declined, deteriorated, reduced | 反义词: improved, enhanced, elevated | 词性:形容词


词源：Phrasal verb derived from "come" and "down."  "Come down" as a verb has existed for centuries, meaning to descend literally. The noun and adjective forms evolved from this, referring to a metaphorical descent in status or fortune.
