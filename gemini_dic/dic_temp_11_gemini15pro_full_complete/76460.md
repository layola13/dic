gairishly:/ˈɡɛərɪʃli/ | adv. | 花哨地；艳丽地；俗气地 | In a garish or excessively showy manner; flashily. | She was garishly dressed in a bright pink dress and orange shoes. 她穿着艳粉色的连衣裙和橙色的鞋子，打扮得花里胡哨。|  例句：The casino was garishly decorated with flashing lights and bright colors. 赌场装饰得花里胡哨，到处是闪烁的灯光和鲜艳的色彩。| 近义词：gaudy, flashy, loud, ostentatious, showy | 反义词：understated, subtle, muted, elegant, refined | 词性: 副词

gairish:/ˈɡɛərɪʃ/ | adj. | 花哨的；艳丽的；俗气的 | Excessively bright and showy; offensively or tastelessly ornamented. | The decorations were a bit too garish for my taste.  这些装饰对我来说有点太花哨了。| 例句：He wore a garish Hawaiian shirt. 他穿着一件花哨的夏威夷衬衫。| 近义词：gaudy, flashy, loud, ostentatious, showy | 反义词：understated, subtle, muted, elegant, refined | 词性: 形容词

词源: From earlier *garnysshen, from Anglo-Norman *garnischen, *garnir (“to adorn, furnish”), from Frankish *warnōn, from Proto-Germanic *warnōną (“to provide, equip, warn”), from Proto-Indo-European *wer- (“to cover, protect”). Related to garnish. More at warn.
