friskily:/ˈfrɪskɪli/| adv.|活泼地，嬉戏地|In a playful and energetic way, often suggesting a happy, carefree attitude.|The lambs frisked friskily in the meadow. 羊羔在草地上欢快地嬉戏。|The puppies played friskily in the garden. 小狗在花园里嬉闹玩耍。|近义词：playfully, energetically, merrily, sprightly|反义词：sluggishly, listlessly, apathetically|词性:副词

frisk:/frɪsk/| v. n.|搜身，轻快地跳跃|
(v.) To quickly pass your hands over someone's clothes to search for hidden weapons or illegal objects.
(n.) A quick search of someone's clothes.
|(v.) The police officer frisked the suspect. 警官搜查了嫌疑犯的身。|(n.) He submitted to a frisk by the security guard. 他接受了保安的搜身。|近义词：(v.) search, pat down|(n.) search, check|反义词: None|词性:动词，名词

词源:  From the Old French *frisque (“fresh, lively”), from Frankish *frisc (“fresh”), from Proto-Germanic *friskaz (“fresh”), from Proto-Indo-European *preysk- (“to jump, spring”). Related to fresh.  The sense of "to search someone's clothes" is American English, first recorded in 1958.
