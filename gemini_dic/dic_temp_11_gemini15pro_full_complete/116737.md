maumet:/ˈmɔːmɪt/| n.|偶像，神像（带有贬义，指异教神像或假神）|An idol or image, especially one worshipped as a god but considered false.  It often carries a negative connotation, implying paganism or idolatry.|例句：The crusaders destroyed the maumets of the infidels.  十字军摧毁了异教徒的偶像。

例句：They accused her of worshipping a maumet. 他们指责她崇拜假神。|近义词：idol, fetish, image|反义词:god (in a monotheistic context)|词性:noun
词源:From Old French *mahomet, ultimately from Arabic مُحَمَّد (muḥammad, “Muhammad”).  The term originally referred to the prophet Muhammad, but due to Christian misunderstanding and prejudice, it came to be used pejoratively for any idol or false god.
