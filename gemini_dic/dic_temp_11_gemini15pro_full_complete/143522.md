outgrow: /ˌaʊtˈɡroʊ/
verb
长得太大穿不下（尤指衣服）; 长大成熟而不再有（某种习惯、爱好）
to grow too large for (something, typically clothing); to grow or mature so as to no longer have (a particular habit or interest).
She outgrew her clothes so quickly. 她长得太快，衣服很快就穿不下了。
He outgrew his childhood fears. 他长大后不再有童年时的恐惧了。
近义词: outstrip, surpass, exceed
反义词: shrink, diminish
词性: verb

词源: From out- + grow.  The sense of "growing too large for" is from the late 16th century. The sense of "growing beyond or leaving behind (a habit, etc.)" is from the late 19th century.
