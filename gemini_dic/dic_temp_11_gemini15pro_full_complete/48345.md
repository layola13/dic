decrustation:/diːkrʌˈsteɪʃən/
n.
去除外壳;清除污垢;脱壳
the act of removing a crust or coating from something.
The decrustation of the old vase revealed its intricate carvings.  去除旧花瓶上的污垢，展现了其复杂的雕刻。
removal, cleaning, scraping
incrustation, coating

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décrustation*, from *décruster* (“to remove the crust”), from *dé-* (prefix indicating removal) + *cruster* (“to form a crust”), from *croute* (“crust”), from Latin *crusta*.
