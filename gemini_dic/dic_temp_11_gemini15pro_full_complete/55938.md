dizzier:/ˈdɪziər/| adj.|更晕眩的，更眼花的|Comparative form of dizzy: more dizzy.  Feeling more unsteady, faint, or lightheaded. |例句：The higher I climbed, the dizzier I felt. 我爬得越高，就感觉越头晕。

The spinning ride made me dizzier than I expected. 旋转木马让我比预期更晕了。|近义词：giddier, lightheaded, faint, unsteady|反义词:steady, stable, grounded|词性:adjective

dizzy:/ˈdɪzi/| adj.|晕眩的，眼花的|Having a sensation of whirling and a tendency to fall.|例句：I felt dizzy after standing up too quickly.  我站起来太快了，感到头晕。

The heights made him dizzy. 高度让他感到头晕。|近义词：giddy, lightheaded, faint, unsteady|反义词:steady, stable, grounded|词性:adjective


词源: From Old English *dysig, from *dīsian "to become foolish," related to Old High German tusic "foolish."  The original sense referred to mental confusion, and the physical sense of "vertigo" developed later.
