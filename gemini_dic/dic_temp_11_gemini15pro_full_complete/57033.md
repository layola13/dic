doublethink:/ˈdʌbəlθɪŋk/ | n. |双想|The acceptance of or mental capacity to accept contrary opinions or beliefs at the same time, especially as a result of political indoctrination.|例句：The Party required its members to practice doublethink.  党要求其成员实行双重思想。

例句：Doublethink allows the government to control the narrative. 双重思想允许政府控制叙事。|近义词：hypocrisy, deception, self-deception, cognitive dissonance |反义词：integrity, honesty, consistency|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Coined by George Orwell in his novel *Nineteen Eighty-Four* (1949).  Formed from *double* + *think*.
