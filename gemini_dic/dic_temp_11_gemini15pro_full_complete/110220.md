lifebuoy:/ˈlaɪfˌbɔɪ/| n.|救生圈，救生衣|A ring-shaped or cylindrical device, typically made of cork or other buoyant material, used to keep a person afloat in water.|The lifebuoy was thrown to the man who had fallen overboard.  救生圈被扔给了落水的人。|Synonyms: life preserver, life belt, life ring, life jacket, float|Antonyms: anchor, weight|noun

lifebuoy:/ˈlaɪfˌbɔɪ/| n.|救生衣|A buoyant vest or jacket designed to keep a person afloat in water.|The child wore a bright orange lifebuoy when playing near the pool. 孩子在泳池附近玩耍时穿着一件亮橙色的救生衣。|Synonyms: life vest, life jacket, PFD (personal flotation device)|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider "weights" in the context of diving)|noun
