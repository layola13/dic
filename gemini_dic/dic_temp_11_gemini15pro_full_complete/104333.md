juvenile:/ˈdʒuːvənaɪl/ /ˈdʒuːvənəl/| adj. n.|青少年的，幼稚的；青少年|Relating to young people who are not yet adults, or suitable for them; a young person.|例句：Juvenile crime is a serious problem in many cities. 青少年犯罪是许多城市的一个严重问题。

例句：He was sent to juvenile detention. 他被送进了少年拘留所。|近义词：young, childish, immature, adolescent, youthful|反义词：adult, mature, grown-up|词性:形容词,名词


juvenal:/ˈdʒuːvənəl/| adj.|少年的，青少年的|Of or relating to youth or childhood.|例句：Juvenal literature is often aimed at teenagers.  青少年文学通常针对青少年。

例句：He retained a certain juvenal enthusiasm for life. 他对生活保持着某种少年般的热情。|近义词：young, childish, immature, adolescent, youthful|反义词：adult, mature, grown-up|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin juvenis (“young”), from Proto-Italic *iuwen-, from Proto-Indo-European *h₂yew- (“vital force, youthful energy”).
