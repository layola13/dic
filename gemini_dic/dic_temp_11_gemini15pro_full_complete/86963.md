词源:
From Middle English *hende, from Old English *ġehende (“near, at hand; pleasant, agreeable”), from Proto-Germanic *gandīną (“near; pleasant”), from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰen- (“to grasp, take, strike”). Cognate with Old Frisian hende (“convenient”), Old Saxon handi, hendi (“pleasant, agreeable”), Old High German ganti (“near, at hand; kind, pleasant”), Old Norse hendr (“dexterous, skillful”), Gothic 𐌷𐌰𐌽𐌳𐌿𐍃 (handus, “hand”).
