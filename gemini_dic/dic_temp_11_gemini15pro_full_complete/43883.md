cresting:/ˈkrɛstɪŋ/
verb
到达顶峰；形成浪峰；处于巅峰状态
reaching the highest point of something, like a wave or a hill; being at the peak of something.
The wave was cresting, about to crash on the shore. 海浪涌起浪峰，即将拍打在岸边。
The hikers finally crested the ridge and saw the valley below.  徒步旅行者终于到达山脊的顶峰，看到了下面的山谷。
synonyms: peaking, culminating, topping
antonyms: declining, falling, descending
词性: v.


crest:/krɛst/
noun
顶部，顶峰；浪尖；羽冠
the highest point of something, like a wave or a hill; a plume of feathers on a bird's head.
The bird had a bright red crest. 这只鸟有一个鲜红色的羽冠。
We reached the crest of the hill and could see for miles. 我们到达了山顶，可以看到数英里之外。
synonyms: peak, summit, top, apex
antonyms: base, bottom, trough
词性: n.


crest:/krɛst/
noun
纹章，徽章
a symbol or design used as a badge of honor or identity.
The family crest was engraved on the silver platter. 家族徽章被刻在银盘上。
The knight wore his crest on his shield. 骑士把他的徽章戴在盾牌上。
synonyms: emblem, insignia, badge, coat of arms
antonyms: N/A
词性: n.


词源: From Old French *creste, from Latin crista "tuft, plume, comb (of a bird)".
