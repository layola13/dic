mystique:/mɪˈstiːk/ | n. | 神秘感；神秘气氛；难以理解的魅力 | An aura of mystery or fascination surrounding something or someone. |  例句：The mystique of the ancient ruins drew tourists from all over the world. 古老遗迹的神秘感吸引了来自世界各地的游客。

例句：She cultivated an air of mystique by rarely giving interviews. 她很少接受采访，从而营造了一种神秘的气氛。 | 近义词：aura, charisma, charm, allure, magic, enigma | 反义词：clarity, openness, plainness | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *mystique*, from Late Latin *mysticus* ("mystical"), from Greek *mystikos* ("of mysteries, mystic"), from *mystes* ("one initiated into mysteries"). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *meu-* ("to close, shut").  The sense of "an aura of mystery" developed in the late 19th century.
