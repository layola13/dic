expediency:/ɪkˈspiːdiənsi/| n.|权宜之计；方便；利己|the quality of being convenient and practical despite possibly being improper or immoral; advantage, convenience|例句：Political expediency often outweighs long-term planning. 政治上的权宜之计往往胜过长远规划。|近义词：convenience, practicality, advantage, opportunism|反义词：principle, morality, ethics|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *expediencie, from Late Latin expedientia (“advantageousness, suitability”), from Latin expediēns (“useful, advantageous”), present participle of expediō (“extricate, set free, dispatch”).
