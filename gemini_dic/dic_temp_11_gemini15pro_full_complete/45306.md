cumber:/ˈkʌmbər/| v. |阻碍，拖累|To hinder or encumber; to burden or weigh down.| The heavy backpack cumbered his progress up the mountain.  沉重的背包阻碍了他上山的进程。| Synonyms: hinder, obstruct, impede, encumber | Antonyms: aid, assist, help | verb

cumbersome:/ˈkʌmbərsəm/| adj. |笨重的，累赘的|Large or heavy and therefore difficult to carry or use; unwieldy.|  The old furniture was cumbersome and difficult to move.  老式家具笨重，难以搬动。| Synonyms: unwieldy, awkward, bulky, clumsy | Antonyms: convenient, manageable, portable | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *combrer, from Late Latin *cumbrus (“dam, weir”), of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Vulgar Latin *concamerare (“to arch, to vault”).
