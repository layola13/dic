dissociation:/dɪˌsəʊʃɪˈeɪʃən/ /dɪˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən/ |n.| 分离，分裂，脱离，解离|The dissociation of water into hydrogen and oxygen. 水分解成氢和氧。|例句：He suffered a period of dissociation following the trauma. 他在遭受创伤后经历了一段时间的解离。

例句：The dissociation of church and state is a fundamental principle of democracy. 政教分离是民主的基本原则。


近义词：separation, detachment, disconnection, alienation|反义词：association, connection, union, integration|词性：名词

dissociate:/dɪˈsəʊʃɪeɪt/ /dɪˈsoʊʃieɪt/ |v.| 使分离，使脱离，将…分开|to dissociate oneself from a group  脱离一个团体|例句：She tried to dissociate herself from the scandal. 她试图把自己与丑闻撇清关系。

例句：He dissociated himself from the extremist views of his former colleagues. 他与前同事的极端观点划清界限。

近义词：separate, detach, disconnect, disunite|反义词：associate, connect, unite, integrate|词性：动词
