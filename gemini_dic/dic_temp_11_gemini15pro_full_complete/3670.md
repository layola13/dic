aggrace:/əˈɡreɪs/
verb
加重；使恶化
to make worse; to aggravate
His rudeness aggraced the situation. 他的粗鲁使情况恶化。
近义词: worsen, exacerbate, compound
反义词: alleviate, improve, ameliorate
词性: verb

aggrace:/əˈɡreɪs/
Etymology: From Latin *aggraviare, from ad- (“to”) + gravis (“heavy”).  Essentially the same word as aggravate, but less common.
英文解析: To make heavier, worse, or more burdensome; to aggravate.  While etymologically identical to "aggravate", "aggrace" is considered archaic or obsolete in modern English and is rarely used.
例句: His constant complaining only aggraced the already tense atmosphere. 他不断的抱怨只会加剧已经紧张的气氛。
The king's refusal to compromise aggraced the conflict, leading to outright war. 国王拒绝妥协加剧了冲突，导致了全面战争。
