apprehension:/ˌæprɪˈhɛnʃən/ | n. | 理解；忧虑；逮捕 | The act of understanding something; anxiety or fear that something bad or unpleasant will happen; the act of arresting someone. |  I had a growing apprehension that I would fail the exam. 我越来越担心我会考试不及格。

The police officer made the apprehension without any resistance. 警察毫不费力地逮捕了罪犯。 | 近义词：understanding, grasp, anxiety, fear, dread, arrest, capture | 反义词：misunderstanding, composure, calm, release | 词性: 名词

apprehend:/ˌæprɪˈhɛnd/ | v. | 理解；担心；逮捕 | To understand something; to fear that something bad or unpleasant will happen; to arrest someone. | I didn't fully apprehend the complexity of the situation. 我没有完全理解情况的复杂性。

The police apprehended the suspect near the border. 警方在边境附近逮捕了嫌疑人。| 近义词：understand, grasp, fear, dread, arrest, capture | 反义词：misunderstand, release | 词性: 动词
