maieutics:/meɪˈjuːtɪks/ /maɪˈjuːtɪks/| n. |产婆术；苏格拉底式问答法|The art of assisting someone to give birth to their own ideas, typically by asking leading questions; Socratic method.|例句：The teacher employed maieutics to help students discover the answers for themselves. 老师运用产婆术帮助学生自己找到答案。

例句：Through maieutics, she guided him to realize his own potential. 通过苏格拉底式问答法，她引导他认识到自己的潜力。|近义词：Socratic method, dialectic, guided discovery|反义词:didacticism, indoctrination|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek μαιευτικός (maieutikos, “of midwifery”), from μαῖα (maîa, “midwife”).  Relates to Socrates' mother being a midwife and his philosophical method being likened to assisting in the "birth" of ideas.
