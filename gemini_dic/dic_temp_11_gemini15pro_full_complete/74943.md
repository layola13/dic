freudianisms:/ˌfrɔɪdiənɪzəmz/
|n.|弗洛伊德主义，弗洛伊德学说|Theories or interpretations based on the works of Sigmund Freud, often referring to concepts like the Oedipus complex, the unconscious mind, or psychosexual development.  It can sometimes be used dismissively to suggest an overemphasis on psychological interpretations, especially those related to sex.|His analysis of the novel was full of freudianisms.|近义词：psychoanalytic interpretations, Freudian theories|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps) behaviorist interpretations|词性:noun
