colorize:/ˈkʌləraɪz/| vt.|使彩色化，给…着色|To add color to something, especially a black and white image or film.|They colorized the old black and white movie. 他们把那部黑白老电影彩色化了。|近义词：tint, dye, stain, paint|反义词:decolorize, bleach|verb

colorize:/ˈkʌləraɪz/| n.|彩色化|The process of adding color to something, especially a black and white image or film.|The colorization of the film was a painstaking process. 这部电影的彩色化是一个艰苦的过程。|近义词：tinting, dyeing, staining, painting|反义词:decolorization, bleaching|noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From color + -ize.  "Color" itself comes from Old French "colur," ultimately from Latin "color." The suffix "-ize" is of Greek origin, used to form verbs meaning "to make or cause to be."
