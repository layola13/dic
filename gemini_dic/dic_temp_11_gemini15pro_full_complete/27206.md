callus:/ˈkæləs/ (US) /ˈkæləs/ (UK)| n. |老茧；硬结；愈伤组织|A thickened and hardened part of the skin or soft tissue, especially in an area that has been subjected to friction.  In botany, a mass of undifferentiated plant cells formed in response to injury or in culture.|He developed a callus on his heel from wearing ill-fitting shoes. 他因为穿不合脚的鞋子，脚后跟长了老茧。|The plant callus began to differentiate into roots and shoots. 植物愈伤组织开始分化成根和芽。|近义词：callouses, thickening, induration|反义词: softness, tenderness|词性:名词
