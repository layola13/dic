entrenchment: /ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt/ | n. | 壕沟；防御工事；根深蒂固 | The act of digging trenches or establishing a strong defensive position; the state of being firmly established or entrenched. | The entrenchment of the enemy troops made it difficult to advance. 敌军的防御工事使前进变得困难。  The entrenchment of traditional values in society can hinder progress. 社会中传统价值观的根深蒂固会阻碍进步。 | 近义词：establishment, fortification, consolidation | 反义词:  removal, displacement, flexibility | 词性: 名词
