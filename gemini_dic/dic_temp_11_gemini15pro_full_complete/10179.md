apoplexy:/ˈæpəˌpleksi/| n. | 中风；(突然的)昏厥；暴怒 |Sudden loss of consciousness or bodily feeling, especially caused by the rupture or obstruction of a blood vessel in the brain. Also, a sudden fit of rage or passion. | He nearly had an apoplexy when he saw the bill. 他看到账单时差点儿气得中风。 | 近义词：stroke, seizure, fit, outburst | 反义词: calmness, tranquility | 词性: 名词

apoplectic:/ˌæpəˈplektɪk/| adj. | 中风的；易怒的；勃然大怒的 | Of, relating to, or resembling apoplexy; extremely angry; furious. | He was apoplectic with rage when his car was stolen. 他的车被偷后勃然大怒。| 近义词：furious, enraged, livid | 反义词: calm, peaceful | 词性: 形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *apoplexia, from Ancient Greek ἀποπληξία (apoplexía, “disablement from a stroke”), from ἀποπλήσσω (apoplēssō, “to disable by a stroke”), from ἀπό (apó, “off, away”) + πλήσσω (plēssō, “to strike”).
