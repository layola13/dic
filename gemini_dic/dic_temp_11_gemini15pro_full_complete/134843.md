nonmodal:/nɒnˈməʊdəl/ | adj. | 非情态的 | Not pertaining to or involving a modal verb or mood.  A nonmodal verb is any verb that is not a modal verb (like *can, could, may, might, must, shall, should, will, would*). |  She used a nonmodal verb in that sentence.  她在那句话中使用了一个非情态动词。| Synonyms:  non-auxiliary, main | Antonyms: modal | adjective

He writes clearly. 他写得很清楚。(nonmodal verb: writes)
They speak English fluently. 他们英语说得流利。(nonmodal verb: speak)

She is writing a letter. 她正在写一封信。(nonmodal verb: writing)
They are playing football now. 他们现在正在踢足球。(nonmodal verb: playing)

He has finished his work. 他已经完成了他的工作。(nonmodal verb: finished)

PHRASE->
(While "nonmodal" doesn't form phrases in the same way content words do, it's used to describe verbs in phrases.)

The verb "eat" in "I eat apples" is a nonmodal verb.  “我吃苹果”中的动词“吃”是非情态动词。
She wants to learn Spanish. (The verb "learn" is nonmodal) 她想学习西班牙语。（动词“学习”是非情态动词）
He enjoys playing football. (The verb "playing" which comes from "play" is nonmodal) 他喜欢踢足球。（动词“玩”，来自“玩”，是非情态动词）
They decided to build a house. (The verb "build" is nonmodal)  他们决定建房子。（动词“建”是非情态动词）
We need to sleep more. (The verb "sleep" is nonmodal) 我们需要多睡一会儿。（动词“睡觉”是非情态动词）
