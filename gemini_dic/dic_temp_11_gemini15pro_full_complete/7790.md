answered-back:/ˈɑːnsərd bæk/ | adj. |顶嘴的，反驳的 | Replying rudely or disrespectfully to someone in authority. |  The child answered back to his teacher and was sent to the principal's office.  这孩子和老师顶嘴，被送到了校长办公室。|  insolent, impudent, impertinent | respectful, obedient | adjective

answered-back:/ˈɑːnsərd bæk/ | v. |顶嘴，反驳 | To reply rudely or disrespectfully to someone in authority. |  She answered back when her boss criticized her work. 当老板批评她的工作时，她顶撞了回去。| retort, rebut, talk back | acquiesce, submit | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "answer back," combining "answer" (from Old English *andswarian*, from Proto-Germanic *andawordjan*, meaning "to give a counter-word") and "back" (from Old English *bæc*, meaning "backward, behind"). The combined meaning of "responding defiantly" developed later.
