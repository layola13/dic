connive (at):/kəˈnaɪv (æt)/| v.|默许；纵容；串通；密谋 (at something)|secretly allows something immoral, illegal, or harmful to happen|She connived at her husband's infidelities. 她纵容了她丈夫的不忠。|synonyms: collude, conspire, plot, scheme|antonyms: expose, obstruct, prevent|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *connivēre*, present active infinitive of *connivō* (“I close the eyes, wink, overlook”), from *com-* (“together, with”) + *nīvēre* (“to wink, blink”).
