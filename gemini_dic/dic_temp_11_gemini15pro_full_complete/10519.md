|verb|

apportion:/əˈpɔːrʃən/
|v.|分配，分摊|To divide and allocate something, typically resources or responsibilities, among different groups or individuals.|The judge apportioned the blame among the defendants. 法官将责任分摊给被告。|allocate, distribute, assign|withhold, keep, centralize
|verb|

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *aporcioner, from Latin apportiōnāre (“to divide proportionally, to distribute”), from ad (“to”) + portiō (“share, portion”).
