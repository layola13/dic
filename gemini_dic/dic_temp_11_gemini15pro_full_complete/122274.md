mitigator:/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪtər/| n.| 减轻者，缓和者，缓解因素 |Something that mitigates or makes less severe. |例句：The judge considered his youth as a mitigating factor in sentencing. 法官认为他的年轻是量刑中的一个减轻罪责的因素。

|例句：The air conditioner was a welcome mitigator against the summer heat.  空调是对抗夏季炎热的受欢迎的缓解因素。|近义词：alleviator, moderator, softener |反义词: aggravator, exacerbator |词性:noun

mitigate:/ˈmɪtɪɡeɪt/| v.| 减轻，缓和，缓解 |To make less severe, serious, or painful. |例句：The government is trying to mitigate the effects of the recession. 政府正在努力减轻经济衰退的影响。

|例句：Exercise can mitigate the risk of heart disease. 运动可以降低患心脏病的风险。|近义词：reduce, lessen, alleviate, diminish |反义词: aggravate, worsen, exacerbate |词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *mitigator, from Latin mitigatus (past participle of mitigare "to soften, make mild"), from mitis "mild, soft" + -igare (frequentative suffix).
