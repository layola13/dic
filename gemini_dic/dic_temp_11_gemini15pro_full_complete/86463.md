hegemonic:/ˌhɛdʒɪˈmɒnɪk/ (US) /ˌhiːɡɪˈmɒnɪk/ (UK)| adj.|霸权的，支配的|Ruling or dominant in a political or social context.  It describes a leading or paramount power or influence, often in a political or social context. It implies control or dominance, not necessarily through force, but often through ideological or cultural influence.|The US has enjoyed a period of hegemonic power since the end of the Cold War.  美国自冷战结束以来一直享有霸权地位。

The company's hegemonic position in the market allowed it to dictate prices. 这家公司在市场上的霸权地位使其能够控制价格。|近义词：dominant, leading, paramount, controlling, ruling|反义词：subordinate, subservient, powerless|词性: adjective

Etymology: From Greek *hēgemōn* (leader, commander), from *hēgeisthai* (to lead).
