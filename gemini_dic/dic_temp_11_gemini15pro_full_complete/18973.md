bestiary: /ˈbɛstiəri/ (US), /ˈbiːstiəri/ (UK)| n. |动物寓言集，怪兽图鉴|A collection of descriptions of real and mythical animals, often with moral or allegorical interpretations.| Medieval bestiaries often featured fantastical creatures like unicorns and griffins. 中世纪的动物寓言集通常以独角兽和狮鹫等奇幻生物为特色。|

Example 1:  The illuminated manuscript was a beautiful bestiary, full of vibrant depictions of both real and imagined animals.
这本泥金装饰手抄本是一部精美的动物寓言集，充满了对真实和虚构动物的生动描绘。

Example 2:  She was fascinated by the symbolism in the medieval bestiary.
她被中世纪动物寓言集中的象征意义所吸引。


|synonyms:  compendium,  encyclopedia,  collection,  menagerie (of descriptions)|antonyms:  None (lack of a direct antonym, perhaps "reality" in a figurative sense)|noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *bestiaire*, from Late Latin *bestiarium* "book about beasts," from Latin *bestia* "beast."
