glochidium:/ɡləˈkɪdiəm/| n.|钩介幼虫，瓣钩幼虫|A small, bivalved larval stage of certain freshwater mussels (Unionidae), characterized by sharp hooks or spines used to attach to the gills or fins of fish for dispersal and development.|The glochidium larva of the freshwater mussel attaches to the gills of a fish.|近义词:larva|反义词:adult|词性:noun

Etymology: From Modern Latin, diminutive of  Greek *glōkhís*  "point of an arrow," related to *glōkhīn* "point, barb."
