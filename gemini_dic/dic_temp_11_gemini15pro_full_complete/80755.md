gossamer:/ˈɡɑːsəmər/ | n. adj.| 薄纱，游丝；轻薄的，虚无缥缈的 | A fine, filmy substance consisting of cobwebs spun by small spiders, which is seen especially in autumn. Also, something light, delicate, or insubstantial. |  The dress was made of a gossamer fabric. 这件衣服是用薄纱织物制成的。 | synonyms:  sheer, diaphanous, filmy, gauzy, delicate, ethereal, flimsy | antonyms: thick, heavy, substantial, opaque | noun

gossamer:/ˈɡɑːsəmər/ | adj.| 轻薄的，虚无缥缈的 | Light, delicate, or insubstantial. |  He had gossamer wings. 他有着轻薄的翅膀。 | synonyms: sheer, diaphanous, filmy, gauzy, delicate, ethereal, flimsy | antonyms: thick, heavy, substantial, opaque | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *gosomer, from Old French gossampine ("cotton tree"),  from Medieval Latin gossipium, from Latin gossipyon, from Ancient Greek γοσσύπιν (gossúpin).  Ultimately probably of Semitic origin.
