connive: /kəˈnaɪv/ | v. | 纵容；默许；共谋；密谋 | secretly allow (something considered immoral, illegal, wrong, or harmful) to occur | The corrupt officials connived with the company to defraud the government. 腐败官员与该公司串通欺骗政府。|  She connived at her husband's infidelities. 她默许了她丈夫的不忠。 | synonyms: conspire, collude, plot, scheme | antonyms: expose, obstruct, prevent, thwart | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *connivāre*, present active infinitive of *connivō* (“I close the eyes, wink”), from *com-* (“together, with”) + *nīveō* (“I wink, blink, nod”).
