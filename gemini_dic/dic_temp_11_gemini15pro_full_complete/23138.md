bourrée:/bʊˈreɪ/ (US), /ˈbʊreɪ/ (UK)| n.|布列舞|A lively French folk dance and the music for it, in quick duple time and characterized by an upbeat. It is also a ballet step consisting of a quick movement of the feet close to the ground.|The band played a lively bourrée. 乐队演奏了一曲欢快的布列舞。|近义词：gavotte, jig|反义词: waltz|noun

bourrée:/bʊˈreɪ/ (US), /ˈbʊreɪ/ (UK)| n.|布列舞曲|A piece of music for or in the style of the bourrée.|The composer's latest work included a beautiful bourrée.  这位作曲家的最新作品包含一首优美的布列舞曲。|近义词：gavotte, jig|反义词: waltz|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French bourrée, of uncertain origin, possibly from a regional dance form in Auvergne or Berry, France.
