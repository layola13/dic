crag:/kræɡ/| n.|峭壁，悬崖|A steep, rugged rock face, especially one that forms part of a cliff.|The climbers scaled the crag with ropes and harnesses. 登山者用绳索和安全带攀登悬崖。|近义词：cliff, precipice, bluff|反义词：plain, flatland|词性:名词
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cragge*, from Old Norse *kragge* (projecting rock), from Proto-Germanic *kraggō* (hook, crooked thing), from Proto-Indo-European *grengʰ- (to bend, curve).
