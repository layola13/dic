hypochondriacism:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪæsɪzəm/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪəsɪzəm/ (UK)| n. |疑病症，臆想病|A psychological disorder characterized by excessive preoccupation with one's health and the conviction that one is or is about to become seriously ill, despite medical reassurance to the contrary. |例句：His hypochondriacism led him to visit the doctor multiple times a month. 他的疑病症导致他每个月去看医生好几次。

例句：Her hypochondriacism was so severe that she refused to leave her house. 她的疑病症非常严重，以至于她拒绝离开家。|近义词：health anxiety, illness anxiety disorder|反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps robust health or lack of health anxiety)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hypochondriacus, from Greek ὑποχόνδριος (hupochondrios, “hypochondriac”), from  ὑπό (hupo, “under”) + χόνδρος (chondros, “cartilage”).  The hypochondria (area below the ribs) was once thought to be the seat of melancholy and other emotions.
