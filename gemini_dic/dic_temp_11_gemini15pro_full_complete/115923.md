marquee:/mɑːrˈkiː/| n. |大帐篷；华盖；（剧院、电影院等入口上方）遮檐；（显示股票行情等的）电子显示屏|A large tent used for social or commercial functions. A rooflike projection over an entrance, as to a theater or hotel. An electronic display screen showing stock prices, news headlines, etc.|The wedding reception was held under a grand marquee. 婚礼招待会在一个巨大的帐篷下举行。|Synonyms: canopy, awning, tent, shelter|Antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, consider: inside, interior) |Noun


marquee:/mɑːrˈkiː/| adj. |显著的；重要的；一流的 (chiefly US) |Outstanding; leading; preeminent.|He was a marquee player in the NBA. 他是NBA的明星球员。|Synonyms: prominent, leading, star, top, chief|Antonyms: minor, insignificant, unimportant|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *marquise* ("marquee," literally "marchioness"), from Italian *marchesa*, ultimately from Frankish *marka* ("borderland, march"). The original sense referred to a large tent used by a marchioness. The association with theaters and cinemas likely stems from the grand tents used for traveling shows and fairs.
