diaphonics:/ˌdaɪəˈfɒnɪks/ | n. |双音；复音；多音|The simultaneous sounding of two or more notes on a wind instrument, especially a brass instrument, usually by humming while playing.  It produces a characteristically rough, multiphonic sound.|例句：The trumpeter used diaphonics to create a haunting, otherworldly effect.  小号手用双音创造了一种萦绕心头的、超凡脱俗的效果。

例句：Diaphonics are often used in contemporary classical music and jazz.  双音常用于当代古典音乐和爵士乐。|近义词：multiphonics|反义词:monophonics|词性:noun
