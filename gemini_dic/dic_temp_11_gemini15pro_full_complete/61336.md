embayment:/ɪmˈbeɪmənt/| n.|海湾，深入陆地的大水域|An embayment is a recess in a coastline forming a bay.|The Chesapeake Bay is a large embayment on the East Coast of the United States. 切萨皮克湾是美国东海岸的一个大海湾。|近义词：bay, gulf, inlet, cove|反义词:headland, promontory|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From en- + bay + -ment.  "En-" acts as a prefix meaning "in" or "into," "bay" refers to a body of water partially enclosed by land, and "-ment" is a suffix forming nouns of action or process, or the result of an action.
