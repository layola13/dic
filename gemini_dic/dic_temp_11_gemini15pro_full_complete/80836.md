gouging:/ˈɡaʊdʒɪŋ/| n. v.| 敲竹杠；诈骗； gouge的现在分词|The act of overcharging or charging excessively high prices, especially during a time of need or emergency.  Also refers to the act of scooping or digging out.|例句：The store was accused of price gouging during the hurricane.  这家商店被指控在飓风期间哄抬物价。

例句：The gouging of the wood created a beautiful pattern. 在木头上雕刻创造出一个美丽的图案。|近义词：overcharging, profiteering, swindling, exploitation, extortion|反义词:fair pricing, discounting|词性:noun, verb (present participle of gouge)

gouge:/ɡaʊdʒ/| n. v.| 凿子； 挖； 欺诈； 敲竹杠|A chisel with a concave blade, used for scooping or digging out.  As a verb, it means to make a groove or hole with such a tool, or figuratively, to extort money from.|例句：He used a gouge to carve the intricate details of the sculpture. 他用凿子雕刻雕塑的复杂细节。

例句：They were gouged by the taxi driver who charged them triple the normal fare.  出租车司机向他们收取了三倍于正常票价的费用，他们被敲了竹杠。|近义词：chisel, scoop, extort, overcharge|反义词:fill, donate|词性:noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *gouge (unattested), probably from Late Latin *gubia, of uncertain origin.
