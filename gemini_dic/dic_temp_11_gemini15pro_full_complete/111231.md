lithotriptor:/ˈlɪθəˌtrɪptər/| n. |碎石机|A medical device that uses shock waves or other means to break up kidney stones or other calculi (stones) in the body so they can be passed naturally.|The lithotriptor was used to pulverize the kidney stones.|Synonyms: lithotripter, stone crusher|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps something like "surgical scalpel" if contrasting methods)|noun

lithotripsy:/lɪˈθɒtrɪpsi/| n. |碎石术|The medical procedure of using a lithotriptor to break up kidney stones or other calculi.|The patient underwent lithotripsy to treat their kidney stones.|Synonyms: lithotripsy, extracorporeal shock wave lithotripsy (ESWL)|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "open surgery" for stone removal)|noun
