chatterer:/ˈtʃætərər/ | n. | 喋喋不休的人；饶舌的人 | A person who talks rapidly and continuously, especially about unimportant things. |  The child was a chatterer, always talking about toys and games.  这个孩子是个话痨，总是谈论玩具和游戏。|  He's such a chatterer, I could barely get a word in edgewise. 他太健谈了，我几乎插不上话。 |  talkative, loquacious, gossipy | reticent, taciturn | noun

chatterbox:/ˈtʃætərbɒks/ | n. | 喋喋不休的人 | A person who talks a lot. | The little girl is a real chatterbox.  这个小女孩真是个话匣子。 |  She's a chatterbox, but she's also very kind. 她很健谈，但也非常善良。| talkative, loquacious | quiet, reserved | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *chaterere, from chateren  (to chatter) + -er (suffix forming agent nouns).  Ultimately from imitative Proto-Germanic *kattrōną.
