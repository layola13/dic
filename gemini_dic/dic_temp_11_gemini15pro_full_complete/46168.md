cynoscephalae:/ˌsaɪnɒsˈsɛfəliː/| n. |狗头丘;犬头山|A place in ancient Greece, site of two important battles.  Specifically referring to two hills in Thessaly, Greece, resembling dog's heads, where significant battles took place in ancient times.  |The Battle of Cynoscephalae in 197 BC marked a decisive Roman victory over Macedon. |近义词: battlefield, hills |反义词: (No direct antonym, could consider "valley" in a geographical context) |词性:noun

Etymology: From Greek *kynos kephalai*, literally "dogs' heads."  *kyno* - dog's/of a dog  *kephalai* - heads
