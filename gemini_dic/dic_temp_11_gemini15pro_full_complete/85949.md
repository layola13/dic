headmistress:/ˌhedˈmɪstrəs/
|n.|女校长|A female principal of a school.|The headmistress addressed the students at the assembly. 女校长在集会上向学生们讲话。|近义词：principal (can be male or female) |反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "students" or "teachers" in a broad sense)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "head" (meaning leader, chief) + "mistress" (a woman in a position of authority or control, originally derived from "master").  The term reflects a time when leadership roles in education were often gender-specific.
