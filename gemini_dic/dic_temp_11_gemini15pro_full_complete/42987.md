covetousness:/ˈkʌvɪtəsnəs/| n. |贪婪，贪欲|An excessive or inordinate desire for wealth, possessions, or another's possessions.|He was consumed by covetousness and wanted everything his neighbor owned. 他被贪婪吞噬，想要邻居拥有的一切。|Synonyms: greed, avarice, rapacity, cupidity|Antonyms: generosity, contentment, altruism|noun

covetous: /ˈkʌvɪtəs/ | adj. |贪婪的，贪求的|Marked by or exhibiting an eager desire for something that belongs to another.|She cast covetous glances at her friend's new car. 她贪婪地盯着她朋友的新车。|Synonyms: greedy, avaricious, grasping, envious|Antonyms: generous, selfless, content|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From late Middle English: from Old French *coveitous, from coveiter ‘desire’, from Latin cupiditas, from cupidus ‘desirous’, from cupere ‘to desire’.
