ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *in ful*, equivalent to *in* (preposition) + *full* (adjective).  The phrase functioned as an adverb and adjective in Middle English.  Over time, it evolved into the single-word adverb and adjective "in-full," particularly common in modern usage.
