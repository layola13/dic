make hay while the sun shines:/meɪk heɪ waɪl ðə sʌn ʃaɪnz/| idiom | 利用好时机；趁热打铁 |  This proverb advises us to take advantage of favorable opportunities before they are gone.  It refers to the literal act of making hay, which can only be done while the sun is shining and drying the cut grass. | The farmers were out all day making hay while the sun shines. 农民们整天都在趁着阳光明媚的时候晒干草。|  Strike while the iron is hot,  carpe diem, seize the day | procrastinate, delay | idiom
ETYMOLOGY->
From the Middle English proverb "Whanne the sonne schyneth make hay," which itself may have originated from even earlier proverbs.  The image of haymaking is a very old and widespread metaphor for making the most of an opportunity.
