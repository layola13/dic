bay:/beɪ/
n.|海湾|A body of water partially enclosed by land, smaller than a gulf.|The boats sailed into the bay. 船驶进了海湾。|近义词：cove, gulf, inlet|反义词:ocean, sea|名词

bay:/beɪ/
n.|隔间|A section of a building or vehicle, often marked by pillars or partitions.|The horses were stabled in separate bays. 马被分别关在不同的隔间里。|近义词：compartment, section, alcove|反义词:whole, entirety|名词

bay:/beɪ/
vi.|（猎犬）吠叫|To bark or howl, especially in a deep, prolonged way.|The hounds bayed at the fox. 猎犬对着狐狸吠叫。|近义词：howl, bark, yell|反义词:whimper, whisper|动词

bay:/beɪ/
n.|月桂|A laurel tree or its leaves, used as a symbol of victory or honor.|The victors were crowned with bays. 胜利者被戴上了月桂花冠。|近义词：laurel|反义词:|名词

bay:/beɪ/
n.|（建筑）窗间壁|The part of a wall between two windows, doors, or other openings.|A large window filled the bay. 一个大窗户填满了窗间壁。|近义词:section, panel|反义词:|名词

词源:
From Old French *baee* "opening, gap," from Latin *badiare* "to gape, to be open."  Relates to words like "bad" (originally "wide open").
