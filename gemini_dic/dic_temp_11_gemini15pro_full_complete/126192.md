muzzlers: /ˈmʌzlərz/
noun
口罩；(动物)口套；枪口制退器
A device, typically made of straps or a cage, fastened over the nose and jaws of an animal, typically a dog, to prevent it from biting or barking. Also, anything that suppresses speech or expression.
The dogs were fitted with muzzlers before entering the crowded park. 狗进入拥挤的公园前都戴上了口罩。
The government's new law acted as a muzzler on the free press. 政府的新法律压制了新闻自由。
Synonyms: gag, silencer, suppressor
Antonyms: amplifier, megaphone
词性: 名词 (plural)

muzzle: /ˈmʌzl/
noun
口罩；(动物)口套；枪口制退器
The projecting part of the face, including the nose and mouth, of an animal such as a dog or horse. Also, a device, typically made of straps or a cage, fastened over the nose and jaws of an animal, typically a dog, to prevent it from biting or barking. Also, anything that suppresses speech or expression.
The dog licked its muzzle. 狗舔了舔它的鼻子。
The owner put a muzzle on the aggressive dog. 主人给这条好斗的狗戴上了口套。
Synonyms: snout, nose, gag, silencer
Antonyms: amplifier, megaphone
词性: 名词

muzzle: /ˈmʌzl/
verb
给…戴口套；钳制…的言论；封锁…的消息
To put a muzzle on (an animal). Also, to prevent (a person or group) from expressing their opinions freely.
The veterinarian had to muzzle the injured dog during the examination. 兽医不得不在检查时给受伤的狗戴上口套。
The dictator attempted to muzzle the opposition party. 独裁者试图压制反对党。
Synonyms: gag, silence, suppress, censor
Antonyms: free, liberate, empower
词性: 动词
