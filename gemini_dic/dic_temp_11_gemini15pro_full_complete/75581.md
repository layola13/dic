fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/| n.|老顽固，守旧的人|A person who is old-fashioned and fussy, unwilling to accept new ideas or ways of doing things.|例句：He's such a fuddy-duddy, he still uses a typewriter! 他真是个老顽固，还在用打字机！

例句：My grandfather is a bit of a fuddy-duddy when it comes to technology. 我祖父在科技方面有点守旧。|近义词：conservative, traditionalist, diehard, stick-in-the-mud|反义词:modernist, progressive, innovator|词性:noun
