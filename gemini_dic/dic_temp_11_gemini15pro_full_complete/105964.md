klutzier:/ˈklʌtsiər/ | adj. | 更笨拙的 | More clumsy or awkward.  A comparative form of "klutzy." | He's always been klutzier than his brother, tripping over his own feet. 他总是比他哥哥更笨拙，经常被自己的脚绊倒。|  clumsier, more awkward, less coordinated | more graceful, more dextrous | adjective


klutzy:/ˈklʌtsi/ | adj. | 笨拙的 | Clumsy or awkward. |  She's so klutzy; she spills something every time she eats. 她太笨拙了，每次吃饭都会洒东西。|  clumsy, awkward, ungainly, inept | graceful, dextrous, coordinated | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Yiddish קלאָץ (klots), meaning "wooden beam" or "block of wood," suggesting someone who is as ungainly as a piece of wood.
