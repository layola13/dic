drossel:/ˈdrɒsəl/ (UK), /ˈdrɑːsəl/ (US)| n. |节流阀，扼流圈；（电子） дроссель|A valve or other device for regulating the flow of a fluid, especially a fuel mixture to an engine.  Also, in electronics, a coil or choke that blocks high-frequency alternating current.|The drossel in the carburetor was faulty, causing the engine to sputter.|Synonyms: throttle, choke, regulator, valve|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, but consider "conduit", "channel" for the opposite function)|noun

drossel:/ˈdrɒsəl/ (UK), /ˈdrɑːsəl/ (US)| vt. |节流，扼流；抑制，压制|To restrict or reduce the flow of something, often a fluid or electricity, or to suppress something.|The government drosseled the flow of information to the public.|Synonyms: restrict, limit, curb, suppress, stifle|Antonyms:  release, unleash, promote, encourage|verb
