corner:/ˈkɔːrnər/ (UK) /ˈkɔːr.nɚ/ (US)| n. v.| 角落，拐角；困境；垄断；(金融)囤积 |A place or angle where two sides or edges meet.  Also, a difficult or awkward situation. In finance, it refers to controlling the supply of a commodity or stock.|

例句：
The cat sat in the corner of the room.
猫坐在房间的角落里。

He painted the corners of the walls white.
他把墙角漆成白色。


近义词：angle, nook, edge, predicament, dilemma|
反义词:center, middle, interior|
词性:noun, verb|

corner:/ˈkɔːrnər/ (UK) /ˈkɔːr.nɚ/ (US)| v.| 使陷入困境；垄断；(金融)囤积 |To force someone into a difficult situation, or to gain control of a particular market. |
例句：
The new regulations have cornered the market.
新规定垄断了市场。

They cornered him with difficult questions.
他们用难题把他逼入了困境。

近义词：trap, monopolize|
反义词:release, free|
词性:verb|
