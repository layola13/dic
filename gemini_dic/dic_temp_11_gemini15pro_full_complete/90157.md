hotfoot:/ˈhɒtfʊt/ (British) /ˈhɑːtfʊt/ (American)| v.|匆忙离开；急忙前往；催促；|To go or cause someone to go quickly.|He hotfooted it out of there as soon as he saw the police. 他一看到警察就赶紧溜了。|Synonyms: hurry, rush, hasten, scurry|Antonyms: dawdle, linger, tarry|verb

hotfoot:/ˈhɒtfʊt/ (British) /ˈhɑːtfʊt/ (American)| n.|催促；匆忙；|A hurried departure or journey.|He made a hotfoot for the exit. 他急忙向出口走去。|Synonyms: haste, rush, hurry|Antonyms: leisure, delay|noun

hotfoot:/ˈhɒtfʊt/ (British) /ˈhɑːtfʊt/ (American)| adj.|匆忙的；急忙的；|Done in a hurry.|He made a hotfoot retreat. 他匆忙撤退了。|Synonyms: hasty, hurried, rushed|Antonyms: leisurely, slow|adjective
