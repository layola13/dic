inexorabilities: /ɪnˌɛksərəˈbɪlətiz/ | n. | 不可抗拒性；必然性 | The quality of being inexorable; inevitability.  This refers to the plural form of "inexorability," highlighting multiple unavoidable forces or circumstances. | The inexorabilities of time and tide wait for no man. 时间和潮汐的不可抗拒性不会等待任何人。 | The inexorabilities of fate led him down a dark path.  命运的不可抗拒性使他走上了一条黑暗的道路。 |  近义词: inevitabilities, certainties | 反义词: uncertainties, possibilities | 词性: 名词

词源: From Late Middle English: from late Latin *inexorabilis, from in- ‘not’ + exorabilis ‘able to be moved by entreaty’ (from ex- ‘out, from’ + orare ‘pray’).
