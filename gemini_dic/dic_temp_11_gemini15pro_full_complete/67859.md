fabian:/ˈfeɪbiən/ | adj. n. | 费边式的，拖延时间的；费边主义者 | Relating to the Fabian Society, a British socialist organization advocating gradual social reform through political action rather than revolution.  A member of the Fabian Society. |例句：He took a Fabian approach to the problem, preferring a slow and steady solution. 他对这个问题采取了费边主义的方式，倾向于一个缓慢而稳定的解决方案。

例句：The Fabian Society played a significant role in the development of the British Labour Party. 费边社在英国工党的發展中扮演了重要的角色。| 近义词：gradualist, cautious, delaying | 反义词：radical, revolutionary, impetuous | 词性: adjective, noun

Etymology: From Quintus Fabius Maximus Verrucosus, a Roman general who avoided decisive battles against Hannibal during the Second Punic War, favouring a strategy of attrition.  This "Fabian strategy" was later adopted as the name of the Fabian Society.
