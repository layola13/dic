lie-in-state:/ˌlaɪ ɪn ˈsteɪt/| n.|瞻仰遗容；停灵|The former president will lie in state at the Capitol. 前总统的遗体将在国会大厦停灵供民众瞻仰。|例句：The queen's coffin lay in state for several days. 女王的灵柩停放了数日，供人瞻仰。

例句：Thousands of people queued to pay their respects as the beloved leader lay in state.  数千人排队向这位敬爱的领袖致敬，他的遗体停放于此，供人瞻仰。

近义词：viewing, public display|反义词: burial, cremation|词性:名词

Etymology:
From the phrase "lie in state," which literally describes the body lying in a state of rest before burial, particularly of a prominent person. The practice dates back to at least the medieval period.
