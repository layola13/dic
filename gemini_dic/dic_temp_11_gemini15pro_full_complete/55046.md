disquietude's:/dɪsˈkwaɪəˌtuːdz/ | n. |不安，忧虑，焦躁 |  The possessive form of "disquietude," referring to the uneasiness or anxiety belonging to something or someone. |  The disquietude's source was unknown, but its effect was palpable.  这种不安的来源不明，但其影响显而易见。 | Near synonyms: anxiety's, uneasiness's, worry's, perturbation's | Antonyms: calmness's, tranquility's, serenity's | noun (possessive)


Etymology:
From Middle French *disquietude*, from Late Latin disquietudo (“uneasiness, anxiety”), from Latin disquietus (“restless, unquiet”) (past participle of disquiesco (“I cease to rest”)), from dis- (“not”) + quiesco (“I rest, become quiet”), from quies (“rest, quiet”).
