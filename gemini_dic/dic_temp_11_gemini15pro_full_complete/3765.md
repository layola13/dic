agitatedly:/ˈædʒɪteɪtɪdli/ | adv. | 激动地，不安地 | In a manner showing agitation; nervously or excitedly. |  She paced the room agitatedly, waiting for the phone call. 她焦急地在房间里踱来踱去，等待着电话。| nervously, anxiously, excitedly, restlessly | calmly, peacefully | adverb

agitate:/ˈædʒɪteɪt/ | v. |  使激动；使不安；搅动 | To make someone troubled or nervous.  | The news agitated her greatly. 这消息使她非常不安。 | disturb, upset, worry, bother | calm, soothe, pacify | verb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *agitatus, past participle of agitāre (“to move, drive, stir, rouse, shake”), frequentative of Latin agere (“to drive, lead, act, do”).
