duvet:/ˈduːveɪ/ (US), /ˈduːveɪ/ (UK)| n. | 羽绒被 | A soft quilt filled with down, feathers, or a synthetic fiber, used instead of an upper sheet and blankets. |  I snuggled under the warm duvet. 我依偎在温暖的羽绒被下。 | Synonyms: comforter, quilt | Antonyms: sheet, blanket | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *duvet*, literally "down," from Old French *duvet* "down," of uncertain origin. Possibly related to *duf*, a variant of *dulf* "stupid," used figuratively of soft, loose material.
