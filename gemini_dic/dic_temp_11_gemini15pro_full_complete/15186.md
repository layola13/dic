backbone: /ˈbækˌboʊn/ (American) /ˈbækˌbəʊn/ (British)| n.| 支柱；骨干；脊梁；毅力|The essential supporting part of something.  A mountain range forming a spine or main support of a region; the spine of a vertebrate.|例句：The farming industry is the backbone of the rural economy.  农业是农村经济的支柱。|例句：The Andes form the backbone of South America. 安第斯山脉构成了南美洲的脊梁。|近义词：foundation, core, spine, support|反义词: weakness, fragility|词性: noun

backbone: /ˈbækˌboʊn/ (American) /ˈbækˌbəʊn/ (British)| n.| 毅力，骨气|Strength of character.|例句：She showed real backbone when she stood up to the bullies. 她勇敢地对抗恶霸，展现了真正的骨气。|例句：He doesn't have the backbone to stand up for what he believes in. 他没有勇气坚持自己的信仰。|近义词：courage, grit, determination|反义词: cowardice, timidity|词性: noun
