broke-back:/ˈbroʊkˌbæk/| adj.|驼背的，脊背受伤的|Having a broken or deformed back, often of an animal, especially a horse.  It can also refer to a person, implying physical hardship or weariness.|例句：The old horse had a broke-back from years of hard labor. 这匹老马由于多年的辛苦劳作而驼背了。

例句：He walked with a broke-back posture after the accident. 事故发生后，他走路的姿势驼背了。|近义词：humpbacked, swaybacked, crippled|反义词:straight-backed, upright|词性:adjective
词源:Compound word from "broke" (past participle of "break") and "back".  While "broken-back" is grammatically more correct, "broke-back" is a common variant, especially in informal language and certain dialects.  It evokes a sense of colloquialism and rusticity.
