nerve:/nɜːrv/ (British) /nɜːrv/ (American)| n. |神经; 勇气; 厚脸皮; (植物) 叶脉|A fiber or bundle of fibers in the body that transmits impulses of sensation to the brain or spinal cord, and impulses from these to the muscles and organs.|She touched a raw nerve when she mentioned his ex-wife. 她提起他的前妻，触及了他的痛处。|Synonyms: fiber, sinew, courage, bravery, audacity, cheek, chutzpah|Antonyms: timidity, cowardice|noun

nerve:/nɜːrv/ (British) /nɜːrv/ (American)| vt. |给予…勇气; 鼓励|To give someone the courage or confidence to do something.|He nerved himself to ask for a raise. 他鼓起勇气要求加薪。|Synonyms: embolden, encourage, strengthen, fortify|Antonyms: discourage, weaken, undermine|verb
