axiomatical:/ˌæksiəˈmætɪkl/ | adj. | 公理的；不言自明的 | Based on or resembling an axiom; self-evident. |  The axiomatical nature of his argument made it difficult to refute.  他的论证基于公理，难以反驳。| synonyms: self-evident, obvious, a priori, taken for granted | antonyms: questionable, debatable, uncertain | adjective

axiomatic:/ˌæksiəˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 公理的；不言自明的 | Based on or resembling an axiom; self-evident. | It is axiomatic that good health is essential to happiness.  良好的健康对幸福至关重要，这是不言而喻的。 | synonyms: self-evident, obvious, a priori, taken for granted | antonyms: questionable, debatable, uncertain | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *axiomaticus, from Ancient Greek ἀξίωμα (axíōma, “that which is thought worthy or fit; a self-evident principle; a dignity, privilege”), from ἀξιόω (axióō, “I deem worthy, I claim, I require”), from ἄξιος (áxios, “worthy, worth, of like value, weighing as much”).
