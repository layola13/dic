licentiate: /laɪˈsɛnʃɪət/ /laɪˈsɛnʃiət/
n.|学位获得者；领有执照者|A person who has been granted a license, especially in a profession or academic field. |She is a licentiate of the Royal College of Physicians. 她获得了皇家内科医学院的执照。|Synonyms: graduate, diploma holder, certified professional|Antonyms: amateur, unqualified individual|noun

licentiate: /laɪˈsɛnʃɪət/ /laɪˈsɛnʃiət/
n.|许可证，执照|A license or certificate granting permission to practice a profession. |He obtained his licentiate to practice law. 他获得了律师执照。|Synonyms: license, permit, certification|Antonyms: prohibition, ban|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *licentiatus*, past participle of licentiare "to permit," from Latin licentia "freedom, permission," from licens, present participle of licēre "to be permitted."
