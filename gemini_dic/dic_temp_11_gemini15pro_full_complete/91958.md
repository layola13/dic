hypermoderns:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɒdərnz/ | /ˌhaɪpərˈmɑːdɚnz/ | n. | 超现代主义者；极端现代主义者 | People or works characterized by hypermodernism, a cultural and intellectual movement emphasizing extreme modernity, often with a self-conscious or ironic awareness of its own artificiality. | The hypermoderns embraced technology and rejected traditional aesthetics. 超现代主义者拥抱科技并拒绝传统美学。 |  The museum's exhibition showcased works by various hypermoderns.  博物馆的展览展示了各位超现代主义者的作品。|Synonyms: avant-gardists, futurists, modernists | Antonyms: traditionalists, conservatives | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "hyper-" (Greek: ὑπέρ, hupér, "over, beyond") + "modern." "Hyper-" acts as a prefix indicating an excessive or extreme degree of something.
