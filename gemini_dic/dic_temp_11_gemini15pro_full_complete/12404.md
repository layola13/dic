aside:/əˈsaɪd/ | adv. n.| 旁边，撇开 |  To one side; out of the way.  |  He stepped aside to let her pass. 他走到一边让她过去。 | synonyms: apart, away, separately | antonyms: together, included | adverb

aside:/əˈsaɪd/ | n.|  A remark or passage by a character in a play that is intended to be heard by the audience but unheard by the other characters in the play. | Hamlet's asides to the audience reveal his inner thoughts. 哈姆雷特的旁白向观众揭示了他的内心想法。| synonyms: whisper, murmur, undertone | antonyms: statement, declaration, proclamation | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French "a costé" meaning "at/to the side".  Ultimately from Latin "ad" (to) + "latus" (side).
