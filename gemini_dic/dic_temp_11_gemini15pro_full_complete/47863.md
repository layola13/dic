|词性: noun (plural)|


词源:

deceivablenesses comes from deceivable + -ness.

*   **deceivable:** From deceive (meaning "to mislead") + -able (suffix meaning "capable of being").  "Deceive" ultimately derives from the Latin word *dēcīpere*, meaning "to ensnare, beguile, or lead astray."
*   **-ness:**  A suffix used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state, quality, or condition.
