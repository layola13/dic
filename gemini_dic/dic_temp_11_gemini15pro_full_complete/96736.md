inelegant:/ɪnˈɛlɪɡənt/| adj.|不雅的，粗俗的，笨拙的|Lacking grace, refinement, or polish; not elegant. |Her inelegant solution to the problem caused more issues than it solved. 她对这个问题笨拙的解决方法造成的麻烦比解决的更多。|The comedian's inelegant jokes offended many in the audience.  这位喜剧演员粗俗的笑话冒犯了许多观众。|近义词：awkward, clumsy, graceless, ungainly, unrefined|反义词：elegant, graceful, refined, stylish|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + elegant (from French élégant, from Latin elegantem, present participle of eligere “to choose, select”).  The sense is thus of something not chosen or selected for its grace or refinement.
