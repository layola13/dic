abolitionism:/ˌæbəˈlɪʃənɪzəm/
n.
废除主义；废奴主义
The principle or policy of abolition, especially of slavery.  The movement to end slavery.
The rise of abolitionism in the 19th century played a crucial role in ending slavery in many countries.  19世纪废奴主义的兴起在许多国家结束奴隶制方面发挥了至关重要的作用。
Abolitionism gained momentum in the United States leading up to the Civil War. 在美国内战前，废奴主义势头强劲。
synonyms:anti-slavery, emancipation movement
antonyms:pro-slavery, slavery
noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French abolitionnisme, from abolition + -isme.  Ultimately from Latin abolere (to abolish).
