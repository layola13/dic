crenel:/ˈkrɛnəl/ | n. | 城垛，垛口 | A rectangular gap in a parapet wall, typically one of a series, for observation or firing weapons. | The castle walls were lined with crenels. 城墙上排列着垛口。|  synonyms: battlement, loophole, embrasure | antonyms: solid wall | noun

crenellate:/ˈkrɛnəˌleɪt/ | vt. |  建垛口于,使有锯齿状 |  To furnish with crenels or battlements. | They crenellated the top of the wall for defensive purposes. 他们在墙顶上修建了垛口，用于防御。| synonyms: embattle, fortify | antonyms: smooth, level | verb

crenellation:/ˌkrɛnəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 城垛，垛口 | The action of crenellating or the state of being crenellated. | The crenellation of the tower provided a good vantage point. 塔上的垛口提供了一个很好的制高点。| synonyms: battlement, indentation | antonyms: smoothness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *crenel, diminutive of cren (notch), from Vulgar Latin *crena, from Latin crena (notch, indentation).
