overromanticizes: /ˌoʊvərroʊˈmæntɪsaɪzɪz/
|v.| 过度浪漫化；过分理想化| To romanticize something excessively, often to the point of idealization and ignoring negative aspects. |She overromanticizes the past, remembering only the good times and forgetting the struggles.|近义词: idealizes, glorifies, exalts|反义词:  debunks, criticizes, disparages|词性: verb (third-person singular simple present)


Etymology:
* Over- (prefix): From Old English *ofer, meaning "above, beyond, across."  Indicates excess or superfluity.
* Romanticize (verb): From romantic + -ize.  Meaning "to make romantic," or "to treat or view in a romantic way."  The word "romantic" ultimately derives from Old French "romaunce," referring to vernacular narratives.
