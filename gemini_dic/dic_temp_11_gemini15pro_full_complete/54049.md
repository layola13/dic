disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/| v. n.|鄙视，蔑视|To consider someone or something as unworthy of one's respect or attention.|She disdained to answer his rude question. 她不屑于回答他粗鲁的问题。|Synonyms: scorn, despise, contemn|Antonyms: admire, respect, appreciate|verb, noun

disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/| v. n.|鄙视，蔑视|To treat someone or something with contempt or scorn.|He disdained all forms of physical exercise. 他蔑视一切形式的体育锻炼。|Synonyms:  spurn, snub|Antonyms: value, cherish|verb, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desdeigner* "to disdain, scorn," from *des-* "dis-" + *deigner* "to deign, condescend," from Latin *dignari* "to deem worthy," from *dignus* "worthy."
