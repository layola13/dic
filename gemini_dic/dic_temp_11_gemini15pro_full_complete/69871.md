ferrule: /ˈfɜːrʊl/ /ˈfɛrʊl/ | n. | 金属箍；金属环；（老师用的）戒尺 | A ring or cap, typically a metal one, that strengthens the end of a handle, stick, or tube and prevents it from splitting or wearing.  A stick or ruler used to punish children, especially by striking them on the hand. | The ferrule on the end of the walking stick was loose. 手杖末端的金属箍松了。 |  In the old days, teachers would use a ferrule to discipline unruly students. 过去，老师会用戒尺来管教不守规矩的学生。 | 近义词: cap, ring, band, splint | 反义词:  (No direct antonym for the object itself; antonyms would apply to its purpose, such as "connector" if the ferrule joins things) | 词性: 名词


词源:  From Old French *virol, from Latin viriola, diminutive of viriae, plural of viria "armlet, bracelet."
