munificate:/mjuːˈnɪfɪkeɪt/| v. |慷慨捐赠，给予|To give or bestow (something, especially money) in a generous or lavish way. |The wealthy philanthropist munificated a large sum to the university.  这位富有的慈善家向这所大学慷慨捐赠了一大笔钱。| He munificated the museum with a collection of rare artifacts. 他向博物馆捐赠了一批珍贵的文物。| 近义词：donate, bestow, endow, contribute | 反义词：hoard, stint, begrudge |词性:动词

munificence:/mjuːˈnɪfɪsəns/| n. |慷慨，大方|The quality or action of being lavishly generous; great generosity. |The king was known for his munificence. 国王以慷慨大方而闻名。 |Her munificence allowed the library to purchase new books.  她的慷慨捐赠使图书馆得以购买新书。| 近义词：generosity, liberality, bounty, lavishness | 反义词：stinginess, parsimony, meanness |词性:名词

munificent:/mjuːˈnɪfɪsənt/| adj. |慷慨的，大方的|Characterized by or displaying great generosity. |A munificent patron of the arts. 一位慷慨的艺术赞助人。 | The university received a munificent donation.  这所大学收到了一笔慷慨的捐款。| 近义词：generous, liberal, bountiful, lavish | 反义词：stingy, parsimonious, mean |词性:形容词
