biasing:/ˈbaɪəsɪŋ/ | n. | 偏置, 偏差 |The act of introducing bias or systematically favoring one outcome or group over others.  Creating a predisposition toward a particular result. | The biasing of the sample led to inaccurate results. 样本的偏置导致结果不准确。| synonyms: prejudice, influencing, skewing, weighting, predisposition, prejudicing | antonyms: impartiality, fairness, objectivity | noun

biasing:/ˈbaɪəsɪŋ/ | adj. | 偏置的 | Exhibiting or causing bias; tending to favor one outcome or group over others. | The biasing effect of the judge's comments was clear. 法官评论的偏见效应很明显。 | synonyms: prejudiced, biased, unfair, partial, one-sided, slanted, skewed | antonyms: impartial, fair, objective, unbiased, neutral | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "bias," which originated from the Old French word "biais" meaning "slant" or "oblique." Ultimately, it traces back to the Provençal word "biais," possibly related to the Vulgar Latin "*bigassius," of unknown origin.
