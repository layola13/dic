extricating:/ˈɛkstrɪˌkeɪtɪŋ/
verb
解救；使摆脱困境
Freeing someone or something from a difficulty or constraint.
The firefighters worked tirelessly to extricate the survivors from the rubble. 消防队员不知疲倦地工作，从瓦砾中救出幸存者。
extricating themselves from the mud. 他们费力地从泥里爬出来。
synonyms: freeing, releasing, rescuing, delivering, disentangling, disengaging
antonyms: trapping, entangling, ensnaring
v.

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin extricare ("to disentangle, disengage"), from ex- ("out of, from") + tricae ("perplexities, hindrances, obstacles").
