charumati: /tʃɑːrʊˈmɑːtiː/ | n. |  妙音天女，乐天，辩才天女 | A Buddhist deity, often depicted playing a lute, associated with music, eloquence, and learning.  Charumati is a female bodhisattva in Mahayana Buddhism. She represents the embodiment of beautiful sound and eloquence. She is often depicted playing a lute and is associated with music, poetry, and learning.  Her name literally translates to "she who possesses beautiful speech" or "she who possesses lovely sound." | Example: Charumati is revered as the goddess of music and eloquence. 妙音天女被尊为音乐和雄辩的女神。

Example:  Offerings were made to Charumati to gain her blessings for artistic endeavors.  为了获得她在艺术方面的祝福，人们向妙音天女献祭。 | Synonym: Saraswati (Hindu counterpart), Benzaiten (Japanese counterpart) | Antonym: N/A | Noun
