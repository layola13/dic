nonspeciousness:/ˌnɒnspiːʃəsnəs/
n.
真实性；不华而不实；不似是而非
The quality of being genuine and not superficially plausible.
Her argument was marked by its nonspeciousness, relying on facts rather than rhetoric. 她的论证以其真实性为标志，依靠事实而不是华丽的辞藻。
近义词: genuineness, authenticity, sincerity, validity
反义词: speciousness, deceptiveness, superficiality, hollowness
词性: 名词

Etymology:
From non- (not) + specious (apparently good or right though lacking real merit) + -ness (forming nouns expressing state or quality).  "Specious" comes from Latin *speciōsus* "beautiful, plausible," from *speciēs* "appearance, form, kind."
