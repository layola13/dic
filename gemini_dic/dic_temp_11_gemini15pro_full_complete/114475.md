mala:/ˈmɑːlə/ | n. |念珠，花环; 坏的; 疾病| A string of beads, typically used for counting mantras in Hinduism and Buddhism. Also, a Sanskrit word meaning "garland" or "wreath". In some contexts, it can also refer to something bad or a disease.| She used her mala to meditate. 她用念珠冥想。|  Synonyms: rosary, beads, garland | Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for the object.  For the meaning "bad," antonyms would be good, virtuous) | noun


mala:/ˈmɑːlə/ | adj. |坏的; 不好的 (Sanskrit) | Bad, evil, or unlucky. This usage is less common in English and primarily found in Sanskrit or related contexts. | The guru warned of mala influences.  大师警告说会有不好的影响。 | Synonyms: bad, evil, inauspicious, unfortunate | Antonyms: good, auspicious, fortunate | adjective
