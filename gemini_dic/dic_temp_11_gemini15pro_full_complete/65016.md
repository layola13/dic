Etymology:
From Old French *estueance "boiling, heat," from estuer "to boil, heat," from Latin aestuare "to boil, surge, be agitated," from aestus "boiling, heat, tide."  The semantic shift occurred due to the turbulent mixing of fresh and saltwater in estuaries.
