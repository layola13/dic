aldol:/ˈældɒl/ (UK), /ˈɔːldɔːl/ (US)|n.|醛醇|An organic compound containing both an aldehyde group (-CHO) and an alcohol group (-OH), typically formed by the aldol reaction.|The aldol reaction is a powerful tool for forming carbon-carbon bonds.|Synonym: β-hydroxy aldehyde, β-hydroxy ketone|Antonym: N/A|noun
