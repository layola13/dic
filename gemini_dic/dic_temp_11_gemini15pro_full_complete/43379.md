craniota: /kreɪˈniːətə/ (Pronunciation may vary; no established standard pronunciation found in major dictionaries) | n. | 颅骨动物，有头盖骨的动物 | An animal that possesses a cranium (skull).  A member of the subphylum Craniata (sometimes used synonymously with Vertebrata). | Example: Craniota include fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals. 颅骨动物包括鱼类、两栖动物、爬行动物、鸟类和哺乳动物。 | Example:  The study of craniota encompasses a wide range of biological disciplines. 对颅骨动物的研究涵盖了广泛的生物学科。 | Synonym: vertebrate | Antonym: invertebrate | Noun
