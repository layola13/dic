benefitted:/ˈbɛnɪfɪtɪd/ | v. | 受益，得益 |past tense and past participle of benefit, meaning to receive an advantage; profit; to be helped by something |  The company benefitted from the new tax laws.  这家公司受益于新的税法。| Synonyms: profited, gained, improved | Antonyms: suffered, lost, declined | verb

benefitted:/ˈbɛnɪfɪtɪd/ | v. |受益，得益 | Another past tense and past participle of benefit, less common in American English but equally acceptable.  The community benefitted greatly from the new library.  这个社区从新图书馆中获益匪浅。| Synonyms: profited, gained, improved | Antonyms: suffered, lost, declined | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French bienfet, benefet (“good deed, kindness, benefit”), from bien (“well”) + fait (“deed”), past participle of faire (“to do”). Ultimately from Latin benefactum, from bene (“well”) + factum (“deed”).
