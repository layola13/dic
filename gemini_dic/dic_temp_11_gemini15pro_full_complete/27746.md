cankeredly:/ˈkæŋkərdli/
adverb
恶毒地，充满仇恨地
in a bitter, resentful, or spiteful manner; with ill will.
He spoke cankeredly of his former friend, revealing a deep-seated resentment. 他恶毒地谈论他以前的朋友，流露出根深蒂固的怨恨。
She glared cankeredly at him, her eyes full of unspoken anger. 她恶狠狠地瞪着他，眼里充满了无法言喻的愤怒。
Synonyms: bitterly, resentfully, spitefully, malignantly, venomously
Antonyms: kindly, gently, affectionately, charitably

Etymology:
From "cankered" + "-ly".  "Cankered" comes from "canker,"  from Old North French "cancre," meaning "cancer," ultimately from Latin "cancer." The metaphorical sense of "moral corruption" developed from the destructive nature of the disease.
