nonnaturalists:/ˌnɒnˈnætʃrəlɪsts/
|n.|非自然主义者|People who believe that not all phenomena are explainable by natural laws, especially in ethics, where they may believe moral properties exist independently of nature.|Nonnaturalists argue that moral properties are not reducible to natural properties like pleasure or happiness.|Synonyms: moral realists, intuitionists|Antonyms: naturalists, reductionists|Noun
