destroyed /dɪˈstrɔɪd/ | v. | 毁灭，摧毁，破坏 | to damage something so badly that it cannot be used or no longer exists | The fire destroyed the building.  大火摧毁了这栋建筑。 | damaged, ruined, demolished | repaired, restored | verb

destroyed /dɪˈstrɔɪd/ | adj. | 被摧毁的，毁灭的 |  extremely upset or broken down |  She was destroyed by the news of her husband's death. 她被丈夫去世的消息摧毁了。| devastated, heartbroken, shattered | comforted, healed | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *destroid, from Anglo-Norman destruit, past participle of destruire, from Latin dēstruere (“to pull down, demolish, destroy”), from dē (“down, from”) + struere (“to pile, build”).
