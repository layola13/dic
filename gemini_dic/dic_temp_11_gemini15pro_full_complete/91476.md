hylozoism:/ˈhaɪləˌzoʊɪzəm/ | n. |万物有生论；泛心论|The doctrine that all matter is in some sense alive.  The belief that all matter, including inanimate objects, possesses life or a life-like quality. |例句：Some ancient philosophers adhered to hylozoism, believing that even rocks possessed a rudimentary form of life. 一些古代哲学家坚持万物有生论，认为即使是岩石也拥有基本的生命形式。

例句：Hylozoism blurs the lines between animate and inanimate objects. 万物有生论模糊了生物和非生物之间的界限。|近义词：animism, panpsychism |反义词: materialism, mechanism |词性: noun
