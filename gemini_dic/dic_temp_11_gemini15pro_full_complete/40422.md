contrariant:/kənˈtrɛəriənt/ | adj. n.| 违逆的，好反驳的；反对者 | inclined to oppose or contradict; perverse.  A person who opposes or contradicts another. | He had a contrariant nature, always disagreeing for the sake of it. 他生性好反驳，总是为了反对而反对。 |  The contrariant refused to sign the agreement.  这个反对者拒绝签署协议。 | synonyms: perverse, contradictory, opposing, recalcitrant, obstinate, awkward, difficult | antonyms: agreeable, compliant, amenable, obedient, accommodating | adjective, noun

contrary:/ˈkɒntrəri/ | adj. n. adv.| 相反的，对立的；相反的事物；相反地 | opposite in nature, direction, or meaning. A fact or condition incompatible with another. In an opposite direction, way, or order. |  Contrary to popular belief, he's actually quite shy. 与普遍的看法相反，他其实很害羞。 | The contrary argument was presented with equal force. 相反的论点也同样有力地被提了出来。 | He acted contrary to my advice. 他违背我的建议行事。 | synonyms: opposite, conflicting, opposing, reverse, adverse, converse | antonyms: similar, alike, same, identical, harmonious | adjective, noun, adverb


词源:
Late Middle English: from Old French contrariant, present participle of contrarier, from Latin contrarius ‘opposite’, from contra ‘against’.
