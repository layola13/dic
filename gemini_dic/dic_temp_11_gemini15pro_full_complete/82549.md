guangong: /ɡwɑːŋ ɡʊŋ/ | n. | 关公 |  Guangdong, also romanized as Kwan Kung, Guan Yu, or Guan Di, is a general serving under the warlord Liu Bei during the late Eastern Han dynasty of China. He has become a figure of reverence, worshipped in Chinese folk religion, popular Confucianism, Taoism, and Chinese Buddhism, especially in southern China, Hong Kong, Macau, Taiwan and Southeast Asia.  | Example:  Guangdong is a symbol of loyalty and righteousness. 关公是忠义的象征。

Example: Many temples are dedicated to Guangdong. 很多寺庙供奉关公。 | Near synonyms: Guan Yu, Guan Di | Antonym:  N/A | Noun
