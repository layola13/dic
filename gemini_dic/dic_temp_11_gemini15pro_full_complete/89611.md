hoodwinkers:/ˈhʊdwɪŋkərz/
n.
欺骗者，骗子
People who deceive or trick others.
The hoodwinkers convinced the elderly woman to invest in a fake charity. 那些骗子说服了这位老妇人投资一个假的慈善机构。
Synonyms: tricksters, swindlers, charlatans, deceivers, fraudsters
Antonyms: honest people, truth-tellers


Etymology:
From the verb "hoodwink," which originated in the late 16th century.  "Hood" referred to a covering for the head, and "wink" meant to close the eyes briefly.  Therefore, "hoodwink" literally meant to cover someone's eyes to prevent them from seeing, thus making them easier to deceive.
