affecter:/əˈfɛktər/ | n. | 装腔作势的人；做作的人 | Someone who affects a particular manner or way of speaking, especially one that is not natural or sincere. | He's such an affecter, always trying to impress people with his phony sophistication. 他真是个装腔作势的人，总是试图用他虚假的成熟来打动别人。 | 近义词：poseur, pretender | 反义词：genuine person, sincere person | 词性: noun

Etymology: From French *affecter*, from Latin *affectāre*, frequentative of *afficere* "to influence, act on," from *ad-* "to" + *facere* "to make, do."
