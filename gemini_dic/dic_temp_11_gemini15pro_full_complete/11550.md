aristocracy: /ˌærɪˈstɒkrəsi/ (US) /ˌærɪˈstɑːkrəsi/ (UK)| n.|贵族；贵族统治；上层社会|A form of government in which power is held by the nobility.  A privileged class holding hereditary titles.|The French aristocracy lost much of its power during the Revolution. 法国贵族在革命期间失去了很多权力。|近义词：nobility, elite, upper class|反义词：commoners, proletariat|词性: noun

Etymology: From Middle French *aristocratie*, from Late Latin *aristocratia*, from Greek *aristokratía* "rule or government by the best," from *áristos* "best" + *-kratía* "power, rule."
