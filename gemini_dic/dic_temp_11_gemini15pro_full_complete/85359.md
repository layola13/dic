harrowed:/ˈhærəʊd/ (US) /ˈhærəʊd/ (UK)| adj.|痛苦的，折磨的|Deeply distressed or tormented.|She looked pale and harrowed after the ordeal.|近义词：distressed, tormented, anguished, traumatized|反义词：calm, peaceful, serene|词性:形容词
词源：From Old English *hergian "to harry, ravage," from Proto-Germanic *harjon, related to Old Norse herja "to harry, plunder." Originally meant "raked," as with a harrow, referring to the painful dragging of the implement across the ground. This sense transferred to emotional distress.
