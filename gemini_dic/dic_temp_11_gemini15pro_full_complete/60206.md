efflorescence:/ˌɛfləˈrɛsəns/| n.|风化，开花，繁荣，兴盛|The process of losing water of crystallization from a hydrated salt when exposed to air, or, the resulting powdery substance. Also, a period of flourishing or great development.|The efflorescence on the bricks was a sign of water damage.  砖块上的风化是水损坏的迹象。|Synonyms: blooming, blossoming, flowering, flourishing, ripening|Antonyms: decline, decay, deterioration, withering|noun

effloresce:/ˌɛfləˈrɛs/| v.|风化，开花，繁荣|To lose water of crystallization on exposure to air and become powdery on the surface, or, to reach a period of flourishing or great development.|The salts effloresced on the surface of the rock. 盐在岩石表面风化了。 The arts effloresced during the Renaissance. 文艺复兴时期艺术蓬勃发展。|Synonyms: bloom, blossom, flourish, thrive|Antonyms: decline, decay, wither|verb
