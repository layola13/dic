backlash:/ˈbækˌlæʃ/| n.|强烈反应；反冲；后冲；齿隙|a strong negative reaction by a large number of people, especially to a social or political change|例句：The government is facing a backlash from voters over its economic policies. 政府正面临选民对其经济政策的强烈反对。

例句：There was a public backlash against the new law. 新法律引起了公众的强烈反对。|近义词：reaction, resistance, opposition, recoil|反义词：support, approval|词性：名词

backlashes:/ˈbækˌlæʃɪz/| n.|强烈反应（复数）；反冲（复数）；后冲（复数）；齿隙（复数）|plural form of backlash|例句：The company faced several backlashes after announcing its new pricing policy.  公司在宣布新的定价政策后，面临着多次强烈反对。

例句：Political backlashes are common after controversial decisions.  有争议的决定之后，政治上的强烈反对很常见。|近义词：reactions, resistances, oppositions, recoils|反义词：supports, approvals|词性：名词
