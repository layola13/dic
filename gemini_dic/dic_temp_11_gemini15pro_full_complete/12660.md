assemble:/əˈsɛmb(ə)l/ | v. |集合，聚集；装配，组装 | To gather together in one place for a common purpose; to fit together the parts of something. | The students assembled in the auditorium for the morning assembly. 学生们聚集在礼堂参加晨会。| synonyms: gather, congregate, collect, convene, muster, amass, compile, construct, build | antonyms: disperse, scatter, dismantle, disassemble | verb

assemble:/əˈsɛmb(ə)l/ | n. |集会；装配 |  A gathering of people, often for a particular purpose; the act of assembling something. | The assemble of students protested the new school policy. 学生集会抗议新的学校政策。 (Note: This noun form is less common) | synonyms: gathering, meeting, assembly, collection | antonyms: dispersal, scattering | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *assembler, from Vulgar Latin *assimulare, from Latin ad- + simulare "to make similar, to copy, to feign."  The original sense was "to bring together things that are similar."
