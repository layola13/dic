corinthianism:/kəˌrɪnθiənɪzəm/| n. |放荡，奢侈，豪华；哥林多式|Luxurious or extravagant living, especially involving immoral behavior. It derives from the reputation of ancient Corinth as a city of wealth and vice.|The sheer corinthianism of the party shocked the more conservative guests.  这场派对的奢华程度让那些较为保守的客人感到震惊。|Synonyms: luxury, extravagance, debauchery, licentiousness|Antonyms: austerity, frugality, abstinence, modesty|noun

corinthian:/kəˈrɪnθiən/| adj. |哥林多的；放荡的，奢侈的|Relating to Corinth, an ancient Greek city known for its wealth and luxury; characterized by luxury and extravagance, sometimes with connotations of immorality.|The corinthian architecture of the building reflected the owner's opulent lifestyle. 这座建筑的科林斯式建筑风格反映了业主奢华的生活方式。|Synonyms: luxurious, opulent, lavish, extravagant|Antonyms: simple, austere, plain, modest|adjective
