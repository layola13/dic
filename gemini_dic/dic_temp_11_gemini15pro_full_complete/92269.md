hypervigilantnesses:/ˌhaɪpərˈvɪdʒɪləntnəsɪz/
|n.|过度警觉（状态）|The state of being excessively watchful, alert, or sensitive to potential threats or dangers, often to an unhealthy or dysfunctional degree. It's characterized by a heightened state of arousal and an exaggerated expectation of harm.|After the robbery, she experienced hypervigilantnesses, constantly scanning her surroundings for danger.|近义词: paranoia, anxiety, watchfulness, alertness|反义词: calmness, relaxation, obliviousness|词性:名词

Etymology:
From "hypervigilance" + "-nesses" (plural suffix). "Hypervigilance" is formed from "hyper-" (Greek prefix meaning "over, excessive") + "vigilance" (from Latin "vigilantia," meaning "watchfulness").
