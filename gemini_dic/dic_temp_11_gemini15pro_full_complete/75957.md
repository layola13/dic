funked:/fʌŋkt/| adj.| 恐惧的；害怕的；惊慌失措的； 处于恐慌状态的；恶臭的；发霉的； 惊恐万状的 |  Overwhelmed with fear, anxiety, or panic. Also, having a strong unpleasant smell; musty. Originates from "funk," meaning a state of fear or panic, particularly among soldiers in World War I, later evolving to include a sense of despair or depression, and also acquiring the olfactory connotation. |  He was completely funked by the prospect of public speaking. 他对公开演讲感到非常害怕。| Synonyms: terrified, petrified, scared stiff, stinky, musty | Antonyms: brave, courageous, fearless, fragrant, fresh | adjective

funked:/fʌŋkt/| v.| 吓坏了；使惊恐；惊慌失措；使发臭；使发霉| Past tense and past participle of "funk," meaning to avoid out of fear or cowardice, or to cause to have a strong unpleasant smell.  | I funked the exam because I hadn't studied. 因为没学习，我考试吓坏了。  | Synonyms:  shirked, dodged, evaded, stunk up, mildewed | Antonyms:  faced, confronted, embraced, freshened, aired out | verb
