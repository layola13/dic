apprehensivenesses:/ˌæprɪˈhɛnsɪvnəsɪz/| n. |忧虑，恐惧，担心|The state of being apprehensive; anxiety or fear that something bad or unpleasant will happen.|She felt a growing apprehensiveness about the future. 她对未来感到越来越焦虑。|Synonyms: anxiety, fear, worry, dread, nervousness, unease, disquiet, perturbation, trepidation, misgiving, foreboding, solicitude, angst|Antonyms: calmness, composure, confidence, ease, security|noun

Etymology:  From Late Middle English *apprehensifnes, from apprehensif + -nes.  Ultimately from Latin apprehendere ("to seize, grasp").
