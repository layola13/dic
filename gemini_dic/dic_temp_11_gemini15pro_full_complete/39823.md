conspiracies:/kənˈspɪrəsiz/
n.
阴谋，密谋，共谋
a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful
例句：They were accused of conspiracies against the government. 他们被指控阴谋反对政府。

例句：There are many conspiracies surrounding the assassination.  围绕这次暗杀有很多阴谋论。

近义词：plots, schemes, intrigues, machinations
反义词：honesty, openness, transparency
词性：名词（复数）


conspiracy:/kənˈspɪrəsi/
n.
阴谋，密谋，共谋
a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful
例句：He was involved in a conspiracy to overthrow the government. 他参与了推翻政府的阴谋。

例句：The conspiracy was uncovered by the police.  这个阴谋被警方揭露了。

近义词：plot, scheme, intrigue, machination
反义词：honesty, openness, transparency
词性：名词（单数）


词源：
From Late Middle English conspiracie, from Old French conspiracie, from Latin cōnspīrātiō (“agreement, concord, plot”), from cōnspīrō (“I conspire”), from con- (“together”) + spīrō (“I breathe”).
