epoch-making:/ˈɛpɒkˌmeɪkɪŋ/ (US) /ˈiːpɒkˌmeɪkɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|划时代的，开创性的|Marking a new and important period in history; very significant or influential.|The invention of the printing press was an epoch-making event. 印刷术的发明是一个划时代的事件。|近义词：groundbreaking, revolutionary, pioneering, momentous, seminal|反义词：insignificant, trivial|词性:形容词
词源:  From "epoch" (meaning a particular period of time in history or a person's life, especially one marked by notable events or particular characteristics) + "making" (from the verb "to make," meaning to bring into existence).  The word "epoch" ultimately derives from the Greek word "epokhē," meaning "pause."
