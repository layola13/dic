flirting: /ˈflɜːrtɪŋ/ (US) /ˈflɜːtɪŋ/ (UK)| n. | 调情，打情骂俏 | The act of behaving as though attracted to or trying to attract someone, but for amusement rather than with serious intentions. | He was flirting with the waitress. 他在和女服务员调情。 | 近义词：coquetting, teasing, dalliance | 反义词: seriousness, indifference | noun


flirting: /ˈflɜːrtɪŋ/ (US) /ˈflɜːtɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. | 调情的，轻浮的 | Behaving in a way that suggests playful sexual attraction. | She gave him a flirting glance. 她给了他一个轻浮的眼神。 | 近义词：coquettish, playful, teasing | 反义词: serious, reserved | adjective

词源: From the verb "flirt," which originated in the late 16th century.  Possibly imitative of a light, quick movement.
