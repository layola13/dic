filliping: /ˈfɪlɪpɪŋ/ | v. | 轻弹，快速地轻击 | Striking or flicking something with a finger | He was filliping a coin in the air. 他在空中轻弹一枚硬币。 |  She was filliping through the pages of a magazine. 她快速地翻阅杂志。 | flicking, tapping, flipping |  - | verb

filliping: /ˈfɪlɪpɪŋ/ | adj. |  (非正式)讨厌的，烦人的 | (informal) Used to express annoyance or disapproval |  This filliping rain is ruining my picnic! 这讨厌的雨毁了我的野餐! | annoying, irritating, bothersome | pleasant, agreeable | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "flip," likely a combination of "fly" and "leap."  The "-ing" ending creates the present participle form, which can function as a verb or, less commonly, an adjective.
