inarch:/ˈɪnɑːrtʃ/
n.|压条;压条法|A method of grafting, in which the scion is still attached to the parent plant until it has united with the stock.|The gardener inarched the branches of the apple tree to propagate a new variety.|近义词:grafting, layering|反义词:cutting|词性:名词
inarch:/ɪnɑːrtʃ/
vt.|压条;将…压条|To graft (a plant) by inarching.|He inarched the young sapling to a more established tree.|近义词:graft, layer|反义词:sever, detach|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *inarcatus, past participle of *inarcare "to arch in," from in- "in" + arcus "arch."
