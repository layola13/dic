nonexpansiveness:/ˌnɒnɪkˈspænsɪvnəs/| n.|非扩张性，非膨胀性|The property of a function or mapping where the distance between the images of any two points is less than or equal to the distance between the points themselves.  This concept is crucial in optimization and fixed point theory.|The nonexpansiveness of the operator guarantees the convergence of the algorithm.|近义词:contractiveness (in stricter sense),  boundedness (in a broader sense)|反义词:expansiveness|noun
