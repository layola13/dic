airbrushed: /ˈɛərbrʌʃt/ | adj. | 喷过漆的，修饰过的，美化过的 | Having been altered or enhanced with an airbrush, often to create an idealized or flawless appearance.  This can refer to photographs, illustrations, or even physical appearances (like makeup). | The model's face was heavily airbrushed in the magazine. 模特的臉在雜誌上被 heavily airbrushed。 | The cover photo was so airbrushed it looked unreal. 封面照片被修饰得看起来很不真实。 | Synonyms: retouched, altered, perfected, enhanced, glossed over | Antonyms: natural, unedited, raw, unaltered | Adjective

airbrush: /ˈɛərbrʌʃ/ | n. | 喷枪 | A tool that sprays paint by means of compressed air. | He used an airbrush to create a smooth gradient on the car. 他用喷枪在车上喷出平滑的渐变色。 |  The artist used an airbrush to add details to the mural. 艺术家用喷枪在壁画上添加细节。| Synonyms: spray gun, atomizer | Antonyms: brush, roller | Noun

airbrush: /ˈɛərbrʌʃ/ | v. | 用喷枪喷涂，修饰，美化 | To apply paint or other coloring matter using an airbrush.  Figuratively, to alter or enhance something, often to create an idealized or flawless impression. | They airbrushed the car to give it a custom finish. 他们用喷枪喷涂汽车，使其具有定制外观。| The advertising agency airbrushed the model's imperfections. 广告公司用喷枪修饰了模特的瑕疵。 | Synonyms: spray paint, retouch, doctor, manipulate | Antonyms: expose, reveal | Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From air + brush.  The tool itself is named for its function of using air to apply a brushed effect.
