overgloomily:/ˌoʊvərˈɡluːmɪli/
| adv. | 过于忧郁地，极度沮丧地 |  in an excessively gloomy manner; very despondently | He stared overgloomily out the window at the rain. 他忧郁地凝视着窗外的大雨。 |Synonyms: despondently, dejectedly, morosely, glumly | Antonyms: cheerfully, brightly, optimistically | 词性: 副词

overgloomily:/ˌoʊvərˈɡluːmɪli/
| adv. | 过于忧郁地，极度沮丧地 |  in a way that is extremely or excessively gloomy | She shook her head overgloomily and sighed. 她沮丧地摇了摇头，叹了口气。 |Synonyms: despairingly, miserably, sullenly | Antonyms: joyfully, happily, lightheartedly | 词性: 副词


词源:

From over- + gloomily.  "Gloomily" comes from "gloomy" + "-ly". "Gloomy" originates from the Old English word "glom," meaning "twilight" or "dusk."  The prefix "over-" intensifies the meaning, indicating an excessive degree of gloominess.
