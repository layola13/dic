fuddy-duddy:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi/ | n. | 老顽固，守旧的人 | A person who is old-fashioned and fussy, especially about their clothes, habits, or opinions. |  Example: My grandfather is a bit of a fuddy-duddy; he still wears a tie to dinner every night. 我的祖父有点老顽固；他仍然每晚都打领带吃饭。

Example:  She dismissed him as a fuddy-duddy who couldn't understand modern art. 她把他斥为不懂现代艺术的老顽固。 | 近义词: old fogey, stick-in-the-mud, conservative, traditionalist | 反义词: modernist, progressive, radical | 词性: 名词

fuddy-duddy's:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdiz/ | n. (possessive) | 老顽固的（东西）| Belonging to a fuddy-duddy. | Example: That fuddy-duddy's car is older than I am! 那老顽固的车比我还老！

Example:  He wouldn't be caught dead wearing one of those fuddy-duddy's hats. 他死也不会戴那种老顽固的帽子。 | 近义词: old-fashioned, outdated, antiquated | 反义词: modern, trendy, stylish | 词性: 名词 (所有格)
