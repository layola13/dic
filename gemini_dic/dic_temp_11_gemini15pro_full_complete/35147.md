clove: /kloʊv/ (US) /kləʊv/ (UK)| n.|丁香; 蒜瓣; |A small, aromatic flower bud of a tropical tree, used as a spice.  A segment of a bulb, such as garlic. |例句：I added a clove of garlic to the sauce. 我在酱汁里加了一瓣蒜。

例句：The scent of cloves filled the air.  空气中弥漫着丁香的香味。|近义词：segment (for garlic), spice|反义词: N/A|词性:noun


clove: /kloʊvd/ (US) /kləʊvd/ (UK)| adj.|有裂片的; 瓣状的|Divided into lobes or segments.|例句：A clove of garlic is a small, clove-shaped section. 一瓣大蒜是一个小的瓣状部分。

例句：The clove-shaped leaves of the plant are distinctive. 这种植物瓣状的叶子很独特。|近义词：lobed, segmented|反义词:whole, entire|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *clo, from Latin clavus "nail," referring to the nail-like shape of the spice.
