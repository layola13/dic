grovellers:/ˈɡrɒvələrz/ (British), /ˈɡrɑːvələrz/ (American)| n. |卑躬屈膝的人；奴颜婢膝的人|A person who grovels; someone who behaves in a subservient or demeaning manner to gain favor or avoid punishment. |The king was surrounded by grovellers, all vying for his attention. 国王身边围满了卑躬屈膝的人，都争着想得到他的注意。

The defeated general became a groveller before his conquerors.  战败的将军在他的征服者面前变得奴颜婢膝。| 近义词：sycophants, flatterers, yes-men, toadies| 反义词： rebels, dissenters, nonconformists |词性:noun (plural)
