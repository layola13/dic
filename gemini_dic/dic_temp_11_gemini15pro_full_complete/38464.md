concettism:/kənˈsɛtɪzəm/
n.
巧思；警句；奇想；(尤指17世纪西班牙文学的) 警句体
A style of writing characterized by elaborate and often strained metaphors and conceits.  A literary style in which ingenuity and wit are prioritized over straightforward expression.
His poems are full of the kind of concettism popular in the 17th century. 他的诗歌充满了17世纪流行的那种巧思。
近义词：witticism, conceit, quip, epigram
反义词：simplicity, plainness
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *concettismo*, from *concetto* ("concept, witty idea"), from Latin *conceptum* ("something conceived"), past participle of *concipere* ("to conceive"). Ultimately from *com-* ("together") + *capere* ("to take, seize").
