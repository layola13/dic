institutionalized:/ˌɪnstɪˈtuːʃənəlaɪzd/ | adj. |制度化的；机构化的；被收容在机构内的|Placed or treated in an institution, especially a hospital or prison; made into an accepted part of a formal system or culture, so that it is difficult to change.|
The orphanage housed many institutionalized children.  这家孤儿院收容了许多被收容在机构内的儿童。

He became so institutionalized after years in prison that he found it hard to adapt to life outside.  他在监狱里待了多年，变得非常制度化，以至于他发现很难适应外面的生活。


|近义词：established, ingrained, entrenched, inured |反义词:independent, free, liberated|词性:形容词|
