ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *dragan (unattested), from Proto-Germanic *draganą (“to draw, pull, carry, bear”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰregʰ- (“to draw, pull, move”). Cognate with Old Frisian draga (“to draw, carry”), Old Saxon dragan (“to draw, carry, bear, suffer, procreate”), Old High German tragan (“to carry, bear”), Old Norse draga (“to draw, pull, move”), Gothic 𐌳𐍂𐌰𐌲𐌾𐌰𐌽 (draggjan, “to draw, lead, pull, carry”).

PHRASE->
1. draw a blank:  一无所获  I drew a blank when I tried to remember his name. 我试图记起他的名字，但什么也想不起来。
2. draw attention to: 引起注意  The report draws attention to the problem of poverty. 该报告提请人们注意贫困问题。
3. draw a conclusion: 得出结论  We can draw a conclusion from the evidence. 我们可以从证据中得出结论。
4. draw near: 临近  Christmas is drawing near. 圣诞节快到了。
5. draw up: 起草；制定  They drew up a contract. 他们起草了一份合同。
