distaste:/dɪsˈteɪst/ | n. | 厌恶，反感 | A feeling of aversion or dislike; repugnance. |  He looked at the raw meat with distaste. 他厌恶地看着生肉。|Synonyms: aversion, dislike, repugnance, revulsion, loathing | Antonyms: liking, fondness, relish, appetite | noun

distasteful:/dɪsˈteɪstfʊl/ | adj. | 令人厌恶的，讨厌的 | Causing distaste; unpleasant or offensive. | The idea of eating insects is distasteful to many people. 吃昆虫的想法让很多人感到厌恶。| Synonyms: unpleasant, disagreeable, offensive, objectionable, repulsive | Antonyms: pleasant, agreeable, appealing, attractive | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *desgoust*, from des- (expressing negation) + goust "taste," from Latin gustus "taste."
