fossilism:/ˈfɒsɪlɪzəm/ | n. | 墨守成规；僵化；顽固 | The adherence to outdated ideas, methods, or technologies, often hindering progress or adaptation.  A reluctance to change or innovate, especially in relation to social or political issues. |  The party's fossilism prevented them from adapting to the changing political landscape.  该党的僵化使得他们无法适应不断变化的政治格局。 |  conservatism, traditionalism, rigidity, inflexibility |  progressivism, innovation, adaptability, flexibility | noun
