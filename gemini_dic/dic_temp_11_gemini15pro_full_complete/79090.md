glacier:/ˈɡleɪʃər/ (US), /ˈɡlæsiə(r)/ (UK)| n. |冰川；冰河|A large mass of ice formed from snow on mountains or near the poles, moving very slowly down a slope or valley.|The hikers were awestruck by the massive glacier.  徒步旅行者对巨大的冰川感到敬畏。

The glacier carved a deep valley over thousands of years.  几千年来，冰川雕刻出了一个深谷。|近义词：ice field, ice cap|反义词: (No direct antonym, perhaps "molten rock/lava" in a geological context)|noun
