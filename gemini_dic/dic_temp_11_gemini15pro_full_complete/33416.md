churchmanship:/ˈtʃɜːrtʃmənʃɪp/| n.|教会制度；教派主义；教派立场|The principles of adherence to a particular church, especially in regard to its forms of worship and organization.  It refers to a person's loyalty to and involvement in a particular church or denomination, including their beliefs about how the church should be run and what its role in society should be.|His churchmanship leaned towards the Anglo-Catholic tradition.|近义词：denominationalism, sectarianism|反义词: ecumenism, interdenominationalism|词性: noun
