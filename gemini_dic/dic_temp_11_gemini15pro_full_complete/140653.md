omasum:/ˈoʊˈmeɪsəm/ /ˈəʊməsəm/| n. |重瓣胃|The third compartment of the ruminant stomach, between the reticulum and the abomasum.  It receives the food from the reticulum and passes it to the abomasum. Its functions include further breaking down food particles and absorbing water. |The omasum is a highly folded structure, often called the "book stomach" because of its many leaves. 重瓣胃是一个高度折叠的结构，由于其多叶片状，通常被称为“牛百叶”或“书胃”。

The omasum plays a crucial role in water absorption in ruminants. 重瓣胃在反刍动物的水分吸收中起着至关重要的作用。|Synonym: psalterium, manyplies|Antonym: None|Noun
