excisions:/ɪkˈsɪʒənz/
|n.|切除；删除；切除物|The act of cutting something out; something that has been cut out.|The surgeon performed several excisions to remove the cancerous tissue. 外科医生进行了多次切除以去除癌变组织。|
|
excision:/ɪkˈsɪʒən/
|n.|切除；删除；切除物|The act of cutting something out; something that has been cut out.|The excision of the unnecessary scenes improved the pacing of the film. 删除不必要的场景改善了电影的节奏。|synonyms: removal, deletion, extraction|antonyms: insertion, addition, inclusion|noun
|
ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English excision, from Late Latin excisiō (“a cutting out”), from Latin excīsus, past participle of excīdere (“to cut out”), from ex- (“out”) + caedere (“to cut”).
