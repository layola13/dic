hyperspeculativeness:/haɪpərˌspɛkjʊlətɪvnəs/
|n.|过度投机; 过度推测; 过度臆测|The state or quality of being excessively or unduly speculative.  It refers to engaging in speculation to an extreme or unreasonable degree.|His hyperspeculativeness in the stock market led to significant losses. 他在股票市场上的过度投机导致了重大损失。|Near Synonyms: overspeculation, excessive speculation,  rash speculation|Antonyms: caution, prudence, circumspection|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
hyper- (prefix): from Greek "hyper" meaning "over, beyond, excessive"
speculative (adjective): from Latin "speculativus"  meaning "contemplative, theoretical,"  from "speculare" meaning "to look at, observe"
-ness (suffix):  forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality
