dramatizers: /ˈdræməˌtaɪzərz/
n.
戏剧化的人；渲染者；小题大做的人
People who exaggerate situations or emotions, often making them seem more important or serious than they really are.  Those who adapt something into a dramatic presentation.
The media are often accused of being dramatizers, sensationalizing stories to attract viewers. 媒体经常被指责为小题大做，为了吸引观众而夸大故事。
News dramatizers often embellish the facts.  新闻渲染者经常添油加醋。
近义词: exaggerators, sensationalists, melodramatists
反义词: minimizers, downplayers, understaters
词性: noun (plural)

dramatize: /ˈdræməˌtaɪz/
v.
戏剧化；渲染；改编成戏剧
To present or represent something in a dramatic or exaggerated way. To adapt something into a dramatic presentation, such as a play or film.
He tends to dramatize every minor setback. 他倾向于夸大每一个小挫折。
The novel was dramatized for television. 这部小说被改编成电视剧。
近义词: exaggerate, overstate, embellish, sensationalize
反义词: downplay, understate, minimize
词性: verb
