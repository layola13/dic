demoralization:/dɪˌmɔːrəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ /dɪˌmɔːrələˈzeɪʃn/| n. |士气低落，沮丧， demoralization|The team's demoralization was evident after their third consecutive loss. 球队在连续三次失利后士气明显低落。|The constant criticism led to a sense of demoralization among the staff.  不断的批评导致员工感到沮丧。|近义词：discouragement, despondency, dejection|反义词: encouragement, morale, inspiration|词性:noun

demoralize:/dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪz/ /dɪˈmɔːrələɪz/| vt. |使士气低落，使沮丧|The coach's harsh words demoralized the team. 教练的严厉批评使队员们士气低落。|Failing the exam demoralized him completely. 考试不及格让他彻底灰心丧气。|近义词：discourage, dishearten, dispirit|反义词: encourage, hearten, inspire|词性:verb

demoralized:/dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪzd/ /dɪˈmɔːrələɪzd/| adj. |士气低落的，沮丧的|The demoralized army retreated. 意志消沉的军队撤退了。|She felt demoralized after being rejected for the job.  求职被拒后，她感到很沮丧。|近义词：discouraged, disheartened, dejected|反义词: encouraged, heartened, inspired|词性:adjective
