morose:/məˈroʊs/| adj.|阴郁的；闷闷不乐的；沮丧的|Feeling glum and sullen; characterized by or expressing gloom.|He sat in the corner, morose and silent after hearing the bad news. 他听到坏消息后，阴郁地坐在角落里，一言不发。|
近义词：sullen, gloomy, glum, despondent, melancholy, dejected|反义词：cheerful, happy, jovial, optimistic|词性:形容词
词源：From Latin *morosus ‘habitual, capricious’, from mos ‘custom, habit’.

morosely:/məˈroʊsli/| adv.|阴郁地；闷闷不乐地；沮丧地|In a morose or sullen manner.|He morosely stared out the window, lost in thought. 他闷闷不乐地盯着窗外，陷入了沉思。|近义词：sullenly, gloomily, glumly|反义词：cheerfully, happily|词性:副词

moroseness:/məˈroʊsnəs/| n.|阴郁；闷闷不乐；沮丧|The state or quality of being morose.|Her moroseness cast a shadow over the entire gathering. 她的忧郁给整个聚会蒙上了一层阴影。|近义词：sullenness, gloominess, glumness, despondency|反义词：cheerfulness, happiness, joviality|词性:名词
