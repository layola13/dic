in-fact:/ɪn ˈfækt/| adv.|事实上，实际上|Used to emphasize the truth of a statement, often contrasting it with what might be expected or has been said.|I thought he was joking, but in fact, he was serious. 我以为他在开玩笑，但事实上他是认真的。|Synonyms: actually, really, truly, indeed|Antonyms: seemingly, apparently|adverb

in fact:/ɪn ˈfækt/| adv.|实际上，其实|Used to introduce a statement that strengthens or adds to a previous statement.|He was feeling ill. In fact, he had a fever. 他感觉不舒服。实际上，他发烧了。|Synonyms: as a matter of fact, in point of fact, actually|Antonyms: theoretically, hypothetically|adverb
