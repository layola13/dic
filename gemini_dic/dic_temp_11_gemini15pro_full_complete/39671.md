conservatrix:/kənˈsɜːvətrɪks/ /kənˈsɝːvətrɪks/| n. |女管理员，女保护人，女保管人|(especially of something such as an art collection or museum) a woman who is responsible for managing and protecting something.  A female conservator.|The conservatrix of the museum ensured the artifacts were well-preserved.  博物馆的女管理员确保了文物保存完好。|近义词：curator, guardian, keeper, custodian |反义词：destroyer, vandal|Noun

conservatrix:/kənˈsɜːvətrɪks/ /kənˈsɝːvətrɪks/| n. |(law) a female guardian or protector (especially of a minor or incompetent person).|The court appointed a conservatrix to manage the affairs of the elderly woman. 法院指定了一名女监护人来管理这位老妇人的事务。|近义词：guardian, protector, trustee|反义词：ward|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin conservatrix (feminine of conservator), from Latin conservare "to preserve," from com- "together" + servare "to keep, guard."
