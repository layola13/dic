fosterer:/ˈfɒstərə(r)/ (UK) /ˈfɑːstərər/ (US)
n.|寄养者，抚养者|A person who takes care of a child who is not their own, usually for a limited time, by arrangement with a local authority or other organization.|She's a fosterer for three young children. 她是一位三个小孩的寄养妈妈。|
近义词:guardian, caretaker|
反义词:biological parent, natural parent|
词性:名词

foster:/ˈfɒstə(r)/ (UK) /ˈfɑːstər/ (US)
v.|促进，培养，养育|Encourage or promote the development of (something, typically something regarded as good); bring up (a child that is not one's own).|The teacher fostered a love of reading in her students. 老师培养了学生对阅读的热爱。|They fostered the orphaned boy. 他们养育了这个孤儿。|
近义词:encourage, promote, nurture, raise|
反义词:neglect, hinder, discourage|
词性:动词


词源: From Middle English *fostren*, from Old English *fōstrian* (“to nourish, support, maintain”), from *fōstor* (“food, nourishment, sustenance”).
