ambush:/ˈæmbʊʃ/ (US) /ˈæmbʊʃ/ (UK)| n. v. |埋伏，伏击；埋伏突袭|A surprise attack by people lying in wait in a concealed position.|The soldiers were ambushed on their way back to base. 士兵们在返回基地的路上遭到伏击。|Synonyms: trap, waylay, surprise attack|Antonyms:  meet openly, confront, encounter|noun

ambush:/ˈæmbʊʃ/ (US) /ˈæmbʊʃ/ (UK)| v. |埋伏，伏击|To attack someone suddenly and unexpectedly from a hidden position.|The robbers ambushed the travelers in the forest. 强盗在森林里伏击了旅行者。|Synonyms:  waylay, entrap, surprise attack|Antonyms: confront, encounter, meet openly|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *embuschier*, from *en* "in" + *busch* "wood" (from Frankish *busk*, related to English "bush").  The original meaning was "to place in a wood," specifically for the purpose of a surprise attack.
