ethics: /ˈɛθɪks/ | n. | 道德；伦理；伦理学 | Moral principles that govern a person's behavior or the conducting of an activity. |  Example 1: Medical ethics prevent doctors from taking advantage of their patients. 医学伦理防止医生利用他们的病人。

Example 2:  He has questionable ethics. 他的道德操守值得怀疑。 | synonyms: morals, morality, principles, values, ideals, standards, code of conduct | antonyms: dishonesty, corruption, immorality, wickedness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: ethiques, from Old French: ethique, from Latin: ethica, from Greek: ēthikḗ (philosophía) ‘(study dealing with) morals’, feminine of ēthikós ‘ethical’, from êthos ‘character, custom’.
