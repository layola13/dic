confronting:/kənˈfrʌntɪŋ/ | adj. | 对抗的，面对的，令人不安的 |  Presenting a challenge; causing one to deal with something directly, often something difficult or unpleasant.  | The police were confronting the criminal. 警察正在与罪犯对峙。 |  challenging, daunting, intimidating | evasive, avoiding | adjective


confront:/kənˈfrʌnt/ | vt. | 面对，遭遇，对抗 | To face or deal with (something or someone) directly, especially something difficult or unpleasant; to bring face to face, as for a meeting or comparison. |  I confronted him with the evidence. 我用证据与他对质。 | face, encounter, tackle | avoid, evade | verb

词源: From Old French "confronter" (to border, compare, bring together), from Latin "con-" (together) + "frons" (forehead, front).  The original sense was to bring two people face to face, literally "forehead to forehead." The meaning later broadened to include facing a problem or challenge.
