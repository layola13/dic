namby-pambinesses:/ˈnæmbi ˈpæmbɪnɪsɪz/| n. |幼稚的行为；故作柔弱；多愁善感|Excessive sentimentality or affected delicacy.|His nambiness-pambinesses irritated everyone around him. 他的扭捏作态惹恼了周围的每个人。|近义词：sentimentality, effeminacy, preciousness, affectation|反义词：masculinity, toughness, strength, practicality|词性:noun

词源:
From namby-pamby (adj.) + -ness (suffix forming nouns of quality or state) + -es (plural suffix).  "Namby-pamby" itself is imitative, perhaps influenced by the name "Ambrose Philips," a poet whose work was considered overly sentimental by Henry Carey, who coined the term in mockery.
