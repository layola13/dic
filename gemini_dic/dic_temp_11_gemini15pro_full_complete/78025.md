gentrifier:/ˈdʒɛntrɪˌfaɪər/ | n. | 中产阶级化推动者；高档化推动者 | A person who moves into a working-class or poor neighborhood, often displacing the original residents due to rising property values and living costs. |  Example 1: The gentrifiers renovated the old buildings, but many long-time residents were forced to move out. 中产阶级化推动者翻新了旧建筑，但许多长期居民被迫搬走。

Example 2:  Local businesses catering to the original community often struggle to survive as gentrifiers bring in more upscale establishments.  随着高档化推动者引入更多高档场所，迎合原社区的当地企业往往难以生存。 | Synonyms:  hipster, yuppie, urban professional | Antonyms: resident, local, incumbent | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "gentrify," which is derived from "gentry" (upper or ruling class) + "-fy" (to make or become). The term emerged in the mid-20th century to describe the process of urban renewal and its social consequences.
