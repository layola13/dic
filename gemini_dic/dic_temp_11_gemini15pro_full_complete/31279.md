ETYMOLOGY->
From chapel + -ed.  "Chapel" comes from Old French "chapele," ultimately from Late Latin "cappella," meaning "short cloak" or "hood," referring to the relic of St. Martin's cloak preserved in a sanctuary that became known as a chapel. The suffix "-ed" is used to form adjectives indicating possession or having the characteristics of something.
