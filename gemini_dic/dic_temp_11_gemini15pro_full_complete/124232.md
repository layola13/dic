Mossadegh: /moʊsɑːˈdɛɡ/ | n. | 穆罕默德·摩萨台 |  Mohammad Mossadegh (1882-1967) was an Iranian politician who served as the democratically elected Prime Minister of Iran from 1951 until 1953, when his government was overthrown in a coup d'état orchestrated by the United States' Central Intelligence Agency and the United Kingdom's MI6. |  Mossadegh is remembered for his nationalization of the Iranian oil industry and his firm stance against foreign interference. | synonym: Premier, Prime Minister | antonym: N/A | noun
