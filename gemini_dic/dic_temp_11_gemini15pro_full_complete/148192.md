overshadows:/ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊz/| v. |使黯然失色；使蒙上阴影；使相形见绌|to make something seem less important or impressive by comparison|Her success was overshadowed by her brother's. 她的成功被她哥哥的成就黯然失色。|Synonyms: eclipse, obscure, dwarf|Antonyms: highlight, emphasize|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From over + shadow.  "Over" implies exceeding or surpassing, while "shadow" suggests darkness or concealment. Thus, "overshadow" literally means to cast a shadow over something, making it less noticeable.
