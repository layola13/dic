compartmentalism: /kəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlɪzəm/ | n. | 隔间化；划分；条块分割 | The division of something into separate compartments or categories, often with the implication that interaction between them is minimized or prevented.  |  He dealt with the trauma by compartmentalizing it, keeping it separate from his daily life. 他通过将创伤隔离开来，不让它影响日常生活来处理它。 |  Categorization, segmentation, partitioning, pigeonholing | Holism, integration, synthesis | noun

compartmentalize: /kəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlaɪz/ | v. | 分隔；划分；条块分割 | To separate something into isolated compartments or categories. | She compartmentalized her life, keeping her work separate from her personal life. 她把自己的生活划分开来，工作和私人生活互不干扰。 |  Categorize, separate, divide, isolate | Integrate, combine, unify | verb

compartmentalized: /kəmˌpɑːrtˈmentəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 分隔的；划分的；条块分割的 | Divided into separate compartments or categories. |  His life was highly compartmentalized, with little overlap between his different social circles. 他的生活被高度划分，不同的社交圈几乎没有重叠。 |  Segmented, partitioned, divided, categorized | Integrated, unified, holistic | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *compartiment* "compartment," from *compartir* "to divide, share," from Latin *compartirī* "to divide with, share," from *com-* "together" + *partirī* "to divide, share."  The suffix *-ism* forms nouns of action, practice, or state.
