blood:/blʌd/ | n. | 血液；血统；血气 |  The vital red fluid that circulates in the bodies of vertebrates. It carries oxygen and nutrients to the tissues and removes waste products. It also plays a crucial role in the immune system and in regulating body temperature. |  He lost a lot of blood in the accident. 他在事故中失了很多血。| 近义词: gore, lifeblood | 反义词: (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑 lymph 淋巴) | 词性: 名词

词源:
From Middle English *blod*, from Old English *blōd*, from Proto-Germanic *blōþą*, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰléh₁- (“to bloom, blossom, swell”). Cognate with Dutch *bloed*, German *Blut*, Icelandic *blóð*, Swedish *blod*.
