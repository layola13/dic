mouldering:/ˈmoʊldərɪŋ/ (US), /ˈməʊldərɪŋ/ (UK)| adjective|腐朽的，腐烂的，崩塌的|slowly decaying or disintegrating, especially because of dampness|The old books were mouldering in the damp basement.  这些旧书在地下室里受潮腐烂了。|decaying, rotting, disintegrating|fresh, new|adjective

mouldering:/ˈmoʊldərɪŋ/ (US), /ˈməʊldərɪŋ/ (UK)| verb|腐烂，崩塌|(of organic matter) to decay slowly; crumble|The wood was mouldering away. 木头正在慢慢腐烂。|decaying, rotting, disintegrating|growing, thriving|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *mouldren, from Old French moldrir, from Vulgar Latin *mollire, from Latin mollis (“soft”).
