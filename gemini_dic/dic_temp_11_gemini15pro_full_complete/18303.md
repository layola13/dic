bellarmine: /ˈbɛlɑːrˌmaɪn/ | n. |贝尔拉明罐, 一种用来装酒的陶罐 | A Bellarmine jar, also known as a Bartmann jug, is a type of stoneware jug with a distinctive rounded body and a bearded face on the neck.  It was popular in Europe, especially in England, from the 16th to the 18th centuries. | The archaeologists discovered several Bellarmine jars during the excavation. 考古学家在挖掘过程中发现了几个贝尔拉明罐。| Synonym: Bartmann jug, stoneware jug | Antonym: N/A | Noun
