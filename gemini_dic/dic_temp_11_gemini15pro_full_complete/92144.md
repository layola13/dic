hypersensitized:/ˌhaɪpərˈsɛnsɪtaɪzd/ | adj. | 过敏的，过度敏感的 |  Made excessively sensitive.  Having an abnormally high sensitivity to a substance (such as an allergen or antigen).  | The patient was hypersensitized to the medication after the first dose.  病人对药物在第一次服用后就产生了过敏反应。 | hypersensitive, allergic, oversensitive |  insensitive, tolerant | adjective

hypersensitize: /ˌhaɪpərˈsɛnsɪtaɪz/ | vt. | 使过敏，使过度敏感 | To make excessively sensitive. To cause to have an abnormally high sensitivity to a substance. |  Repeated exposure to the chemical hypersensitized his skin.  反复接触这种化学物质使他的皮肤变得过敏。| sensitize | desensitize | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From hyper- + sensitize.  "Hyper-" is a prefix meaning "over, excessive," from Greek hyper "over, beyond." "Sensitize" comes from  "sensitive" + "-ize." "Sensitive" comes from Late Latin sensitivus, from Latin sensus "feeling, sense," past participle of sentire "to feel."
