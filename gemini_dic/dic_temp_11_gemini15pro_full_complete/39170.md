congealableness:/kənˌdʒiːləbəlnəs/
n.
凝固性，易凝固性
The property of a substance that allows it to congeal; the tendency to become congeal.
The congealableness of the gelatin was evident as it cooled in the refrigerator.  这种明胶的凝固性在冰箱里冷却时很明显。
Near synonyms: coagulability, solidifiability
Antonyms: fluidity, liquidity
noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From congealable + -ness.  Congealable from congeal + -able. Congeal from Old French congeler, from Latin congelare "to freeze," from com- "together" + gelare "to freeze."
