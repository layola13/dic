first-ever:/ˈfɜːrstˈɛvər/ | adj. | 史上首次的；空前的 |  Meaning "the very first instance of something ever happening," it emphasizes the historical significance of the event. It is formed by combining "first," meaning earliest or initial, and "ever," meaning at any time. |  This was the first-ever time a woman had won the award. 这是女性首次获得该奖项。 |  inaugural, groundbreaking, pioneering, unprecedented |  subsequent, later | adjective

first-ever:/ˈfɜːrstˈɛvər/ | n. | 史上首次的事件或事物 |  Refers to the very first instance of something ever happening, treated as a noun. | The first-ever moon landing was a momentous occasion. 首次登月是一个意义重大的时刻。 | debut, premiere, inauguration |  repetition, sequel | noun
