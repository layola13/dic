Mohammedanism /məˈhɒmɪdənɪzəm/ | n. |  伊斯兰教 (旧称，现已弃用，带有贬义) | An outdated and offensive term for Islam, implying a worship of Muhammad rather than God.  The term centers on Muhammad, similar to how Christianity centers on Christ, but Muslims believe that their religion was revealed to Muhammad by God, not that Muhammad himself is divine. |  "The book used the term 'Mohammedanism' throughout, showing its age and bias." 这本书通篇使用“Mohammedanism”一词，显示了它的年代久远和带有偏见. | Synonym: Islam (preferred), Muslim faith | Antonym: None (as it's a religion) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Muhammad + -an + -ism.  Coined in the 16th century, it gained popularity in the 18th and 19th centuries but is now considered outdated and offensive.
