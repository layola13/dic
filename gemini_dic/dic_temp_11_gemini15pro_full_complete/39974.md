consubstantiate:/kənˌsəbˈstænʃiˌeɪt/ | v. |使同体，使同一实体，把…合为一体|To unite in one common substance or nature; to become of the same substance. The doctrine of consubstantiation holds that the body and blood of Christ coexist with the substance of the bread and wine in the Eucharist.|The priest consubstantiated the bread and wine during the communion.  牧师在圣餐仪式中将面包和酒合为一体。|Synonyms: combine, unite, merge, fuse |Antonyms: separate, divide, disintegrate|verb

consubstantiation:/kənˌsəbˈstænʃiˈeɪʃən/ | n. |同体，同一实体|The union of the substance of the body and blood of Christ with the consecrated bread and wine in the Eucharist.|The doctrine of consubstantiation is a key difference between Lutheranism and Catholicism. 同质说教义是路德教和天主教之间的一个关键区别。|Synonyms: union, fusion, merging |Antonyms: separation, division |noun
