duumvirate:/duːˈʌmvɪrət/| n. |双头政治；二人执政；二人统治|A government of two people.  A group of two people sharing power or authority.|The Roman Republic sometimes employed a duumvirate to complete specific tasks, like building a temple or organizing a festival.|近义词：diarchy, dyarchy|反义词: monarchy, autocracy|词性:noun
ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *duumvirātus*, from *duumvir* ("one of two men holding a joint office"), from *duo* ("two") + *vir* ("man").
