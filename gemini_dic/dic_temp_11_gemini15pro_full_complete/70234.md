ficklest:/ˈfɪk(ə)ləst/| adj.|最易变的，最反复无常的|Changing frequently, especially as regards one's loyalties, interests, or affection.|The fashion industry is notoriously fickle, with trends changing every season.  时尚界是出了名的善变，潮流每个季度都在变化。

The ficklest of voters will determine the outcome of this election.  最易变的选民将决定这次选举的结果。|近义词：capricious, erratic, mercurial, volatile, whimsical|反义词：constant, steadfast, loyal, steady|词性:形容词
词源: From Middle English *fikel, from Old English ficol (“deceitful, treacherous, cunning, inconstant”), from Proto-Germanic *fīkulaz (“changeable, variable, unreliable”), from Proto-Indo-European *peyk- (“to trick, deceive; variegated”). Cognate with Dutch fiek (“sly, cunning”), German feige (“cowardly”). More at fey.
