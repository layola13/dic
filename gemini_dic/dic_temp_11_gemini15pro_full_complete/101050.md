inveigler:/ɪnˈveɪɡlər/ | n. | 诱骗者；怂恿者 | Someone who persuades someone to do something by means of deception or flattery. | The inveigler promised riches and glory, leading many unsuspecting individuals into his trap. 那个诱骗者承诺财富和荣耀，将许多毫无戒心的人引入他的陷阱。| Synonyms: deceiver, enticer, temptor, seducer, coaxer | Antonyms: dissuader, preventer, protector | noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *invehiglāre, from Latin invehere (“to carry in, bring in, introduce; attack, assault”), from in- + vehere (“to carry”).  The sense development is from "attack with words" to "entice, persuade by trickery".
