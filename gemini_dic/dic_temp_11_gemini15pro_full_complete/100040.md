interpolating: /ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 插入的，内插的 | The act of inserting something between other things or parts, especially in a series, text, or piece of music.  It also refers to estimating a value within a known sequence of values. |  He interpolated a few remarks while I was speaking.  他在我讲话时插了几句话。|  interjecting, inserting, introducing | omitting, deleting | Verb (present participle)

interpolated: /ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 被插入的，内插的 | Having been inserted between other things or parts. | The interpolated data provided a smoother curve. 插入的数据提供了一条更平滑的曲线。| inserted, interjected | original, unaltered | Adjective

interpolate: /ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪt/ | v. | 插入，内插 | To insert (something) between other things or parts, especially in a series, text, or piece of music. It also refers to estimating a value within a known sequence of values. | He interpolated a song into the play. 他在剧中插入了一首歌。| interject, insert, introduce | omit, delete | Verb


Etymology: From late Latin interpolat-, past participle stem of interpolare "to refurbish, polish up, falsify," from inter- "between" + polare "to polish."
