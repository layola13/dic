cysticercosis:/ˌsɪstɪˌsɜːrˈkoʊsɪs/ | n. | 囊虫病 | A parasitic tissue infection caused by larval cysts of the tapeworm *Taenia solium*. Humans acquire this infection by ingesting eggs shed in the feces of a human tapeworm carrier.  These eggs hatch in the intestine, and the larvae penetrate the intestinal wall and migrate to various tissues, most commonly the brain, muscles, and eyes. |  Cysticercosis can cause seizures if the cysts are located in the brain. 如果囊肿位于大脑中，囊虫病会导致癫痫发作。| synonym:  N/A (No direct synonym, it's a specific disease) | antonym: N/A (No direct antonym for a disease) | noun
