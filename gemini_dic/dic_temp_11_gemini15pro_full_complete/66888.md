expediency:/ɪkˈspiːdiənsi/ /ɛkˈspiːdiənsi/ | n. |权宜之计；方便；合算|The quality of being convenient and practical despite possibly being improper or immoral; advantageousness.|  He acted out of expediency, not principle. 他出于权宜之计，而不是原则。|Synonyms: convenience, practicality, advantage, opportunism | Antonyms:  principle, morality, ethics | noun

expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/ /ɛkˈspiːdiənt/ | adj. |权宜的；方便的；有利的| (of an action) convenient and practical, although possibly improper or immoral.| It is expedient to use a cheaper material. 使用更便宜的材料是权宜之计。| Synonyms:  advantageous, convenient, practical, useful | Antonyms:  inconvenient, impractical, inexpedient | adjective

expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/ /ɛkˈspiːdiənsi/ | n. |权宜之计；应急手段| A means to an end; not necessarily a principled or just one. |  The government chose the political expedient of ignoring the protests. 政府选择了无视抗议的权宜之计。| Synonyms:  resource, measure, means, resort | Antonyms:  hindrance, obstacle | noun
