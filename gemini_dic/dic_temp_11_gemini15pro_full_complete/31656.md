chatter:/ˈtʃætər/ (US), /ˈtʃætə(r)/ (UK)| n.|喋喋不休，唠叨；（鸟）啁啾声；（牙齿）打颤声|The rapid continuous talk, especially about unimportant things; a series of short, quick sounds, like birds or monkeys.|The constant chatter of the children made it difficult to concentrate. 孩子们不停的叽叽喳喳让人很难集中注意力。|近义词：babble, prattle, jabber, twitter|反义词：silence, quiet|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *chateren, frequentative of *chatten (“to chat”), of imitative origin.
