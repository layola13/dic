mole:/moʊl/ (US) /məʊl/ (UK)| n.|鼹鼠，痣，防波堤，间谍，摩尔（物质的量单位）|A small, burrowing mammal with dark velvety fur, small eyes, and strong front feet adapted for digging.|例句：The mole burrowed a network of tunnels under the lawn. 鼹鼠在草坪下挖了一个隧道网络。|近义词：spy (for espionage sense), breakwater (for seawall sense), nevus (for skin blemish sense)|反义词: (No direct antonym, depends on the meaning used)

词性:noun

mole:/moʊl/ (US) /məʊl/ (UK)| n.|防波堤|A large solid structure built in the sea to protect a harbor or coast from the force of waves.|例句：The mole provided a safe haven for ships during the storm.  防波堤在暴风雨期间为船只提供了一个安全的避风港。|近义词：breakwater, jetty, pier|反义词: (No direct antonym)
词性:noun

mole:/moʊl/ (US) /məʊl/ (UK)| n.|间谍|A person who gradually achieves a position of trust within an organization, especially a government or intelligence agency, in order to betray it to another organization or government.|例句：The mole leaked classified information to the enemy. 间谍向敌人泄露了机密信息。|近义词：spy, infiltrator, double agent|反义词: (No direct antonym)
词性:noun

mole:/moʊl/ (US) /məʊl/ (UK)| n.|摩尔（物质的量单位）|The SI base unit of amount of substance, equal to the quantity containing as many elementary units as there are atoms in 0.012 kg of carbon-12.|例句：One mole of any substance contains Avogadro's number of particles. 一摩尔的任何物质都含有阿伏伽德罗常数个粒子。|近义词：(No direct synonym, it's a specific unit)|反义词: (No direct antonym)
