changeability:/ˌtʃeɪndʒəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可变性，易变性 | The changeability of the weather makes it difficult to plan outdoor activities. |  The changeability of his moods is hard to deal with.  | 近义词: mutability, variability, fluidity, instability | 反义词: stability, constancy, immutability, permanence | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From "changeable" + "-ity".  "Changeable" comes from the Middle English "changeable," from Old French "changeable," from Late Latin "cambiabilis," from Latin "cambiare" (to exchange, barter), related to "cambire" (to exchange). The suffix "-ity" forms abstract nouns expressing state or quality.
