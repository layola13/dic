misapprehend:/ˌmɪsæprɪˈhend/ | v. | 误解，误会 | To understand incorrectly; to mistake the meaning of. | She misapprehended his intentions and thought he was being rude. 她误解了他的意图，以为他很粗鲁。|  He completely misapprehended the instructions and did the opposite of what he was supposed to do. 他完全误解了指示，做了与他应该做的相反的事情。 |Synonyms: misunderstand, misconstrue, misinterpret | Antonyms: understand, comprehend, grasp | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From mis- (wrongly) + apprehend (to grasp mentally).  The prefix "mis-" comes from Old English, meaning "wrongly, badly, or amiss."  "Apprehend" comes from the Latin "apprehendere," meaning "to grasp, seize, or understand."
