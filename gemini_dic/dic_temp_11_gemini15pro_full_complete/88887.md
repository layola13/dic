hokeynesses:/ˈhɑːkinəsəz/
noun
滑稽模仿；滑稽行为（通常指模仿某人或某事）
A humorous imitation or parody of something, often a person's mannerisms or style.

Her hokeynesses of the teachers were hilarious and spot-on. 她对老师们的滑稽模仿既好笑又精准。

The play was full of hokeynesses of popular culture. 这部戏充满了对流行文化的滑稽模仿。

synonyms: parodies, imitations, impersonations, send-ups, spoofs, take-offs
antonyms: seriousness, sincerity, authenticity

ETYMOLOGY->
From "hokey" (meaning contrived, artificial, or overly sentimental) + "-ness" (suffix forming nouns indicating a state, quality, or condition). "Hokey" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly related to "hocus-pocus."
