middling-meat:/ˈmɪdlɪŋ miːt/ | n. | 中等质量的肉；次级肉 | Meat of medium quality, often from the less desirable parts of the carcass. It's not the best, but it's not the worst either. | The butcher recommended middling-meat for the stew.  屠夫推荐用中等质量的肉来炖菜。| The restaurant used middling-meat for its budget lunch special. 这家餐厅用中等质量的肉做它的特价午餐。 | Synonyms: average meat, ordinary meat | Antonyms: prime meat, choice meat | noun


Etymology:
middling (adjective) + meat (noun)

"middling" comes from Middle English *midlen*, from *middel* (middle) + *-ing* (suffix forming adjectives).
"meat" comes from Old English *mete* (food), from Proto-Germanic *mati* (food).
