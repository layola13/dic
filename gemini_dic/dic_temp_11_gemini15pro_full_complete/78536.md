ghostily:/ˈɡoʊstɪli/| adv.|鬼魅般地；幽灵似地|In a manner resembling or suggestive of a ghost; eerily.|The wind howled ghostily through the trees. 风鬼魅般地呼啸着穿过树林。|Synonyms: eerily, spookily, spectrally|Antonyms: cheerfully, brightly|Adverb

Etymology:
From ghost + -ly.  "Ghost" comes from Old English "gast" (spirit, good or bad angel, demon), of Germanic origin.  "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives, meaning "in a ... manner".
