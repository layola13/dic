lilt:/lɪlt/| n. vi.|轻快的节奏，抑扬顿挫的语调；轻快地说话或唱歌|A characteristic rising and falling of the voice; a rhythmical swing or cadence.  A pleasant, regularly alternating rise and fall in pitch.|Her voice had a lovely lilt. 她的声音悦耳动听，抑扬顿挫。|Synonyms: cadence, rhythm, swing, inflection|Antonyms: monotone, droning|noun, verb

lilt:/lɪlt/| vi.|轻快地说话或唱歌|To speak, sing, or sound with a lilt.|He lilted a merry tune. 他轻快地哼着一首欢乐的曲子。|Synonyms: sing, croon, hum, warble|Antonyms: drone, mumble|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From probably imitative origin, perhaps related to Norwegian *lilla, “to lull”.
