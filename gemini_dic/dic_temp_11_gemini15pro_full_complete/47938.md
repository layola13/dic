dechristianize: /diːˈkrɪʃtʃənaɪz/ | vt. | 使非基督教化，使世俗化 | To eliminate the Christian character or influence of; secularize. |  The French Revolution attempted to dechristianize France. 法国大革命试图使法国非基督教化。

The government's policies are seen by some as an attempt to dechristianize the country.  一些人认为政府的政策是试图使国家世俗化。 | 近义词: secularize, laicize | 反义词: Christianize, evangelize | 词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- + Christianize.  "De-" is a prefix meaning "remove" or "reverse". "Christianize" means to convert to Christianity.  Therefore, "dechristianize" literally means to remove the Christian influence or character.
