argumentativeness:/ˌɑːrɡjəˈmɛntətɪvnəs/
n.|好辩性，爱争论 |The lawyer's argumentativeness helped him win the case. 这位律师的好辩性帮助他赢得了这场官司。| English Explanation: The quality or state of being argumentative; a tendency to argue or disagree. | Example: Her argumentativeness made it difficult to have a civil conversation. 她好辩的性格使得很难进行一次文明的对话。|Synonyms: contentiousness, disputatiousness, quarrelsomeness|Antonyms: agreeableness, amiability, cooperativeness|noun

argumentative:/ˌɑːrɡjəˈmɛntətɪv/
adj.|好辩的，爱争论的|He's very argumentative and always wants to have the last word. 他很好辩，总是想说最后一句话。|English Explanation: Given to argument; tending to argue or disagree. |Example:  She became argumentative when he challenged her opinion. 当他质疑她的观点时，她变得好辩起来。|Synonyms: contentious, disputatious, quarrelsome|Antonyms: agreeable, amiable, cooperative|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *argumentativus, from Latin argumentum (“argument”) + -ivus (“-ive”).
