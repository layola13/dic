cheapening:/ˈtʃiːpənɪŋ/
v.|贬低；降低…的价值；使…显得廉价|Making something seem less valuable or important; reducing the value or price of something.|Mass production has cheapened the value of handmade goods. 大规模生产降低了手工制品的价值。|devaluing, degrading, lowering|enhancing, increasing, raising|verb


cheapening:/ˈtʃiːpənɪŋ/
n.|贬低；降低价值；使…显得廉价|The act of making something seem less valuable or important; the reduction in value or price of something.|The cheapening of art through commercialization is a concern for many artists.  艺术通过商业化而贬值是许多艺术家关心的问题。|devaluation, degradation|enhancement, increase|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From cheap + -en (suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become") + -ing (suffix forming present participles and gerunds).
