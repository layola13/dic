bleakly:/ˈblikli/ | adv. | 荒凉地；凄凉地；阴郁地 | In a bleak or desolate manner; drearily. | The wind whistled bleakly across the moors. 风凄厉地呼啸着掠过荒野。|  The future looked bleakly for the company. 公司的前景看起来很黯淡。 | 近义词：desolately, drearily, gloomily, starkly | 反义词：brightly, cheerfully, hopefully | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From bleak + -ly. Bleak itself comes from Old Norse *bleikr, meaning "pale, white," related to Old English blāc.  The sense of "exposed, desolate" developed later, perhaps influenced by the pale, bare appearance of landscapes in winter.
