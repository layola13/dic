externalising: /ɪkˈstɜːnəlaɪzɪŋ/ /ɛkˈstɝːnəˌlaɪzɪŋ/
|v.|外化；使外在化|The process of expressing or projecting one's inner thoughts, feelings, or conflicts onto the external world.|He was externalising his anger by shouting at everyone.|近义词:projecting, expressing, venting|反义词:internalising|词性:动名词

externalise: /ɪkˈstɜːnəlaɪz/ /ɛkˈstɝːnəˌlaɪz/
|vt.|外化；使外在化|To express or project (one's inner thoughts, feelings, or conflicts) onto the external world.|She externalized her anxiety by biting her nails.|近义词:project, express, vent|反义词:internalise|词性:动词

external: /ɪkˈstɜːnl/ /ɛkˈstɝːnl/
|adj.|外部的；外面的|Belonging to or forming the outer surface or structure of something.|The external walls of the house need painting.|近义词:outer, outside, exterior|反义词:internal, inner, interior|词性:形容词
