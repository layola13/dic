elegiacs: /ɪˈliːdʒiˌæks/ | n. | 挽歌; 哀歌; 哀诗;  (常作复数) 挽歌体诗 |A poem of mourning, typically for someone who has died, or the style of writing such poetry.  |  She wrote elegiacs for her deceased father. 她为她已故的父亲写了挽歌。 | Synonyms: lament, dirge, requiem | Antonyms: celebration, paean | noun


elegiac: /ɪˈliːdʒiˌæk/ | adj. | 哀伤的; 凄凉的;  | Relating to or characteristic of an elegy. | The music had an elegiac quality. 这音乐有一种哀伤的调子。 | Synonyms: mournful, plaintive, melancholic | Antonyms: joyful, cheerful | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *elegiacus, from Latin elegeia + -acus. Ultimately from Ancient Greek ἐλεγεία (elegeía).
