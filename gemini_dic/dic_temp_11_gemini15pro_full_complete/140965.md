one-way:/ˌwʌnˈweɪ/ | adj. |单程的，单向的 | Allowing travel in only one direction. |  Example: a one-way street 单行道 | Synonyms: unidirectional, single-direction | Antonyms: two-way, round-trip | adjective

one-way:/ˌwʌnˈweɪ/ | n. |单程票，单行道 | A ticket for a one-way journey; a street allowing travel in only one direction. | Example: I bought a one-way ticket to London. 我买了一张去伦敦的单程票。 | Synonyms: single, unidirectional ticket/street | Antonyms: return, round-trip ticket/ two-way street| noun

Etymology:  "one" + "way".  "One" from Old English *ān (from Proto-Germanic *ainaz), "way" from Old English weg (from Proto-Germanic *wegą).  The combined term "one-way" emerged in the later 19th century with the rise of railroads and organized street systems.
