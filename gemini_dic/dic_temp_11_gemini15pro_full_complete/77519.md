gazumping:/ɡəˈzʌmpɪŋ/
n.
抬价；临时加价；毁约加价
The act of raising a price at the last minute, especially the price of a house, after a buyer has agreed to buy it.
The seller gazumped the buyer by accepting a higher offer from someone else. 卖家通过接受别人更高的报价而抬高了买家的价格。
近义词：price hiking, profiteering
反义词：discounting, price reduction
词性：名词

Etymology: From Yiddish גאַזומפּען (gazumpen), meaning "to overcharge, swindle."  Ultimately of uncertain origin, possibly related to German sumpfen, "to drain, to swamp."
