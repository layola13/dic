flatfishes:/ˈflætˌfɪʃɪz/ | n. | 比目鱼; 扁鱼 |A general term for a variety of bottom-dwelling fish, such as flounder, halibut, and sole, characterized by their flattened bodies and both eyes on one side of the head.  They typically lie on the seabed, camouflaged against the bottom. |  Flatfishes are masters of camouflage on the ocean floor. 比目鱼是海底伪装大师。

Flatfishes are a popular food source in many cultures. 比目鱼在许多文化中是一种受欢迎的食物来源。 | 近义词: bottom-dwellers, benthic fish | 反义词: pelagic fish (远洋鱼类) | 词性: noun
