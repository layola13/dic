flinching:/ˈflɪntʃɪŋ/ | n. | 退缩，畏缩 | The act of making a sudden, small movement as a result of fear, pain, or surprise. |  He didn't even flinch when the needle went in. 他连针扎进去都没退缩一下。 | synonyms: wincing, recoiling, shrinking, cringing | antonyms:  braving, enduring, facing | noun

flinch:/flɪntʃ/ | v. | 退缩，畏缩 | To make a sudden, small movement as a result of fear, pain, or surprise. | She flinched at the loud noise. 她被巨大的噪音吓了一跳，畏缩了一下。| synonyms: wince, recoil, shrink, cringe | antonyms: brave, endure, face | verb
