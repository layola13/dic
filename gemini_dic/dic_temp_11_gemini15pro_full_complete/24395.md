brinkmanship: /ˈbrɪŋkmənʃɪp/ | n. |  边缘政策；冒险策略| The art or practice of pursuing a dangerous policy to the limits of safety before stopping, typically in politics. |  His brinksmanship during the crisis almost led to war. 他在危机期间的边缘政策几乎导致了战争。 | daredevilry, recklessness | caution, prudence | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From brink (edge) + -manship (skill, art).  The word was popularized during the Cold War to describe the tactic of escalating tensions to the point of near-war in order to achieve a political objective.
