deviates:/ˈdiːvieɪts/ (US), /ˈdiːviˌeɪts/ (UK)| v. |偏离，背离|To depart from an established course, standard, or norm.|例句：The plane deviated from its flight path due to strong winds. 飞机由于强风偏离了航线。

例句：His behavior deviates significantly from accepted social norms. 他的行为明显偏离了公认的社会规范。


|近义词：strays, digresses, diverges, departs|反义词: conforms, adheres|词性: verb


deviates:/ˈdiːvieɪts/ (US), /ˈdiːviˌeɪts/ (UK)| n. |偏离者，背离者|A person or thing that departs from an established course, standard, or norm.|例句：Statistical deviates can help identify outliers in data sets. 统计偏差可以帮助识别数据集中的异常值。


例句：He was considered a deviate by his peers due to his unconventional beliefs. 由于他非传统的信仰，他被同龄人视为异类。|近义词：nonconformist, maverick, outlier|反义词: conformist|词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin deviatus, past participle of deviare "to turn aside," from de- "away" + via "way, road."
