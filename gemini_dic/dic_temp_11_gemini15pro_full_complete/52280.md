dichotomist:/daɪˈkɒtəmɪst/ /daɪˈkɑːtəmɪst/| n.|二分法者，二元论者|A person who uses or believes in a system of classification into two opposing categories.|The dichotomist argued that all things could be classified as either good or evil.|Synonyms: dualist, splitter|Antonyms: unifier, synthesizer|noun

dichotomistic:/ˌdaɪkətəˈmɪstɪk/ /ˌdaɪkətəˈmɪstɪk/| adj.|二分法的，二元论的|Characterized by or based on dichotomy; dividing things into two opposing categories.|The dichotomistic approach to the problem failed to account for the nuances of the situation.|Synonyms: dualistic, binary|Antonyms: holistic, unified|adjective

dichotomize:/daɪˈkɒtəmaɪz/ /daɪˈkɑːtəmaɪz/| v.|使二分，分成二元|To divide or separate into two opposing groups or categories.|He tended to dichotomize complex issues into simple black and white options.|Synonyms: bifurcate, split|Antonyms: unite, integrate|verb

dichotomy:/daɪˈkɒtəmi/ /daɪˈkɑːtəmi/| n.|二分法，二元论|A division or contrast between two things that are or are represented as being opposed or entirely different.|There is a false dichotomy between freedom and security.|Synonyms: division, contrast, opposition|Antonyms: unity, harmony, integration|noun
