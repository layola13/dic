lieber:/ˈliːbər/| adj.|亲爱的，喜爱的|Dear, beloved, preferred. Used as a term of endearment or to express preference. From Old High German *liub*, meaning "dear, beloved". Cognate with English "love".|例句：Mein lieber Freund, ich vermisse dich.  My dear friend, I miss you.|近义词：beloved, dear, favorite|反义词：hated, despised|词性:adjective

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
Ich schreibe lieber Briefe. I prefer to write letters.
Er liest lieber Bücher als Zeitung. He prefers reading books to newspapers.


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
Ich bin gerade lieber am Lesen. I am currently preferring to read (This tense is less common with "lieber" as it expresses a general preference).
Sie ist lieber am Kochen, als auszugehen. She's preferring to cook rather than go out (Again, less common usage).


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
Ich habe lieber zu Hause gegessen. I preferred to eat at home (in a specific past situation).
Er hat lieber geschlafen, als zu lernen. He preferred to sleep rather than study (in a specific past situation).



现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
This tense is rarely used with "lieber".

过去进行时 (Past Continuous):

Sie war lieber am Kochen. She was preferring to cook (at that time - uncommon usage).


过去完成时 (Past Perfect):

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):

将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):

虚拟语气:
Ich würde lieber ins Kino gehen. I would prefer to go to the cinema.
Wenn ich könnte, würde ich lieber zu Hause bleiben. If I could, I would prefer to stay at home.


被动语态:"Lieber" is not used in the passive voice.


疑问句:
Gehst du lieber ins Kino oder ins Theater? Do you prefer going to the cinema or the theatre?
Liest du lieber Bücher oder Zeitschriften? Do you prefer reading books or magazines?

否定句:
Ich gehe nicht lieber ins Kino. I don't prefer going to the cinema. (Meaning: I don't particularly like going to the cinema more than other options).
Ich mag das nicht lieber. I don't prefer that.

PHRASE->
lieber nicht (rather not / I'd prefer not to)

注意事项:
"lieber" is primarily used in comparative contexts expressing preference. While it's grammatically possible to use it in some of the less common tenses, it feels unnatural and is rarely done by native speakers.  Focus on the common usage examples for best understanding.
