overaffirming:/ˌoʊvərəˈfɜːrmɪŋ/
| adj. | 过度肯定的，过分认可的 | Excessively validating or approving, often to an unrealistic or unhealthy degree.  Affirming something too much, beyond what is reasonable or warranted. | He was overaffirming in his praise of her mediocre performance, making her think she was much better than she actually was. 他过分肯定了她平庸的表现，让她误以为自己比实际优秀得多。| 近义词：overpraising, overvaluing, excessively supportive | 反义词：critical, disapproving, undervaluing | 词性: adjective
词源:  "Over-" (prefix meaning "excessively") + "affirming" (present participle of "affirm," from Latin *affirmare*, from *ad-* "to" + *firmare* "to strengthen," from *firmus* "strong").
