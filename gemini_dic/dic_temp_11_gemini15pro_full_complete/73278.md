forebitter:/fɔːrˈbɪtər/| n.|预先使…苦恼的人或事物；预先的苦恼之源|Something that causes anticipatory suffering or distress; a source of preemptive bitterness.|The looming deadline was a forebitter, casting a shadow over her holiday.|近义词：premonition, apprehension, dread, anxiety, fear|反义词：joy, happiness, excitement, anticipation|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From 'fore-' (meaning "beforehand," related to "before") + 'bitter' (meaning "having a sharp, pungent taste or smell; causing pain or unhappiness").  The word is a direct formation, combining the idea of bitterness with a sense of anticipation or preemption.
