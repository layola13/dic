forzando:/fɔrtˈsændoʊ/ (US), /fɔːtˈsandəʊ/ (UK)| adv. |强音地，突强地|A musical direction indicating a sudden, strong accent on a note or chord.|The pianist played the passage *forzando*, bringing a dramatic emphasis to the melody.|Synonyms: sforzando, accented, emphasized|Antonyms: softly, gently|adverb

forzando:/fɔrtˈsændoʊ/ (US), /fɔːtˈsandəʊ/ (UK)| n. |强音，突强|A sudden, strong accent on a note or chord.|The *forzando* in the final bar added a powerful conclusion to the piece.|Synonyms: sforzando, accent, emphasis|Antonyms: softness, gentleness|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Italian *forzando*, present participle of *forzare* "to force," from Latin *fortiāre* "to strengthen," from *fortis* "strong."
