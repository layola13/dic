dislocation:/ˌdɪsləˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |脱臼，错位；混乱，紊乱 |The act of dislocating something, especially a bone; a displacement of something from its proper place; disruption. |He suffered a dislocation of his shoulder in the accident. 他在事故中肩膀脱臼了。|synonyms: displacement, disruption, disturbance, disorder, chaos |antonyms: order, organization, arrangement| noun

dislocate:/ˌdɪsləˈkeɪt/ | vt. |使脱臼，使错位；扰乱，打乱 |To put out of joint; to disturb the order or arrangement of. |The fall dislocated his knee. 摔倒使他膝盖脱臼了。|synonyms: displace, disrupt, disturb, upset |antonyms: order, organize, arrange| verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dislocatio, from Latin dislocare "to displace," from dis- "apart" + locare "to place."
