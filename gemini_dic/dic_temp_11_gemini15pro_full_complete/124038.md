morpheme:/ˈmɔːrfiːm/ | n. |词素 |A morpheme is the smallest meaningful unit in a language.  It cannot be further divided into smaller meaningful parts. | Example: The word "unbreakable" has three morphemes: "un-", a prefix meaning "not"; "break", the root word; and "-able", a suffix meaning "capable of".  这个单词“unbreakable”包含三个词素：“un-”是一个前缀，意思是“不”；“break”是词根；“-able”是一个后缀，意思是“能够”。| Synonyms: unit, element, component | Antonyms: N/A | noun
