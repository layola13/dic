gregariously:/ɡrɪˈɡɛəriəsli/ | adv. | 合群地，群居地 | In a sociable or outgoing manner; enjoying the company of others. |  The students lived gregariously in dorms, sharing meals and studying together. 学生们群居在宿舍里，一起吃饭和学习。|  sociably, companionably, convivially | solitarily, aloofly, unsociably | adverb

gregarious:/ɡrɪˈɡɛəriəs/ | adj. | 合群的，群居的 | (of a person) Fond of company; sociable.  (of animals) living in flocks or loosely organized communities. | Penguins are gregarious birds, often found in large colonies.  企鹅是群居的鸟类，经常出现在大型群落中。 She's such a gregarious person, always surrounded by friends. 她是一个非常合群的人，总是被朋友包围。 | sociable, outgoing, convivial | solitary, antisocial, reclusive | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *gregarius* "belonging to a flock," from *grex* (genitive *gregis*) "flock, herd."
