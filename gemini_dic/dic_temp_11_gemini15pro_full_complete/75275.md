front-load: /ˌfrʌntˈloʊd/ (US) /ˌfrʌntˈləʊd/ (UK)
| v. n. adj.| 预先装载，预先加载；前置；集中在早期 | To concentrate the greatest effort, cost, or activity at the beginning of a process, project, or time period. |The company decided to front-load its marketing campaign to generate early buzz.  该公司决定预先加载其营销活动，以尽早引起轰动。|近义词：pre-load, prioritize, concentrate (at the beginning)|反义词: back-load, postpone, delay|词性: Primarily a verb, can also function as a noun (less common) or adjective.

front-load: /ˌfrʌntˈloʊd/ (US) /ˌfrʌntˈləʊd/ (UK)
| n.| 前置装载; 前期投入 | The allocation of a significant amount of resources or effort at the beginning of a project or process. |The front-load of investment required for this project is substantial. 这个项目所需的前期投入很大。|近义词：initial investment, upfront cost|反义词: later investment, deferred cost|词性: Noun

front-load: /ˌfrʌntˈloʊd/ (US) /ˌfrʌntˈləʊd/ (UK)

Etymology:  "Front-" (meaning the beginning or forward part) + "load" (meaning to burden or fill). The term gained prominence in the late 20th century, particularly in business and finance contexts.
