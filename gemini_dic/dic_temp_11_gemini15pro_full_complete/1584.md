achromatopsy:/əˌkrəʊməˈtɒpsi/ /əˌkroʊməˈtɑːpsi/| n. |全色盲，完全色盲|A medical condition characterized by the complete inability to perceive colors. It's typically a congenital condition, meaning present from birth, and results from the absence or malfunction of cone cells in the retina, which are responsible for color vision.  |Example 1:  Achromatopsy affected his ability to appreciate art.  全色盲影响了他欣赏艺术的能力。

Example 2:  She was diagnosed with achromatopsy at a young age. 她很小的时候就被诊断出患有全色盲。|Synonym: complete color blindness, monochromatism|Antonym:  trichromatism (normal color vision)|noun
