fasti:/ˈfæs.taɪ/ /ˈfæ.stiː/
|n. (pl.)|斋戒；禁食期；斋日|periods of fasting, especially for religious reasons; the days on which such fasting is observed|例句：Muslims observe fasti during Ramadan.  穆斯林在斋月期间守斋戒。

例句：The fasti of Lent are traditionally observed by many Christians.  许多基督徒传统上遵守四旬期的斋戒。
|近义词：abstinence, fast|反义词：feasting, indulgence|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *fastī* (plural), originally referring to lawful or permissible days for conducting business, later specifically days of religious abstinence. Related to *fāstus* "divine law," ultimately from *fārī* "to speak (divinely)."
