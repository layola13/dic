in-crowd:/ˈɪn kraʊd/| n.|小圈子，核心集团，受欢迎的群体|A small group of people who are considered fashionable, popular, or important, and who do not want other people to join them. |例句：She desperately wanted to be part of the in-crowd. 她渴望成为受欢迎的小圈子的一员。

例句：The in-crowd tends to stick together. 受欢迎的小圈子成员往往喜欢抱团。|近义词：clique, inner circle, elite|反义词：outcast, outsider|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->

From "in" (meaning "fashionable, popular") + "crowd" (a large number of people gathered together).  The term gained popularity in the mid-20th century.
