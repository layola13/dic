melancholist: /ˈmɛlənˌkɑːlɪst/ | n. | 忧郁症患者；忧郁的人 | A person who suffers from or is prone to melancholy; a gloomy person. |  He was a melancholist, prone to fits of despair. 他是一个忧郁症患者，容易陷入绝望。|  She was known as a melancholist, always dwelling on the negative. 她以忧郁著称，总是纠结于负面情绪。| 近义词: pessimist, depressive | 反义词: optimist, cheerful | 词性: noun

melancholy: /ˈmɛlənˌkɑːli/ | n. adj. | 忧郁；忧伤 | A feeling of pensive sadness, typically with no obvious cause. | A deep melancholy settled over him.  深深的忧郁笼罩着他。 | The melancholy strains of the music filled the room.  忧伤的音乐旋律充满了房间。 | 近义词: sadness, gloom, dejection | 反义词: happiness, joy, cheerfulness | 词性: noun, adjective


词源: From Middle French *melancolie*, from Late Latin *melancholia*, from Ancient Greek μελαγχολία (melankholía, “black bile, melancholy”), from μέλας (mélas, “black”) + χολή (kholḗ, “bile”).  The ancient Greeks believed that an excess of black bile caused melancholy.
