endosymbiosis:/ˌɛndoʊˌsɪmbaɪˈoʊsɪs/ | n. | 内共生 |Symbiosis in which one of the symbiotic organisms lives inside the other.  A theory stating that the eukaryotes evolved through a process whereby different types of free-living prokaryotes became incorporated inside larger prokaryotic cells and eventually developed into mitochondria, chloroplasts, and possibly other organelles.|  The theory of endosymbiosis explains the origin of mitochondria and chloroplasts. 内共生理论解释了线粒体和叶绿体的起源。| Example: Endosymbiosis is a key concept in evolutionary biology.  内共生是进化生物学中的一个关键概念。|  symbiosis, mutualism | parasitism | noun
