grounding:/ˈɡraʊndɪŋ/ (US), /ˈɡraʊndɪŋ/ (UK)| n. |接地, 基础, 根基; (青少年)禁足|The act of connecting something to the earth, either physically or metaphorically.  It can refer to an electrical connection, a basis for understanding, or a sense of stability.|The electrician checked the grounding of the appliance. 电工检查了电器的接地。|Synonyms: foundation, basis, anchoring, earthing (UK)|Antonyms: instability, disconnection|noun

grounding:/ˈɡraʊndɪŋ/ (US), /ˈɡraʊndɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. |扎实的，基础的|Providing a basis for understanding or stability.|His research provided a grounding in the subject. 他的研究为这门学科提供了基础。|Synonyms: fundamental, basic, foundational|Antonyms: superficial, unstable|adjective

词源: From ground (n.) + -ing (suffix forming nouns from verbs).  The original sense was of "running aground" (of a ship).  The electrical sense developed from the practice of connecting electrical circuits to the earth.
