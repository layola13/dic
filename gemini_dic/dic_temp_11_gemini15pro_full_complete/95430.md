incinerations:/ɪnˌsɪnəˈreɪʃənz/
|n.|焚烧；烧毁；火化|The act of incinerating something, reducing it to ashes.  The incinerations of medical waste are strictly regulated.|例句：The incinerations of the infected carcasses helped to prevent the spread of the disease. 烧毁受感染的尸体有助于防止疾病传播。

例句：The city's new waste management system includes high-temperature incinerations. 该市新的废物管理系统包括高温焚烧。|近义词：burnings, cremations, combustions|反义词:preservation, conservation|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *incinerationem, the accusative singular of incineratio (“a burning to ashes”), derived from incinero (“I reduce to ashes”), from in (“in, into”) + cinis (“ashes”).
