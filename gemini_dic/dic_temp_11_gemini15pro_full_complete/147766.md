overprolixness:/ˌoʊvərproʊˈlɪksnəs/| n. | 过于冗长，啰嗦 | The overprolixness of his writing made it difficult to follow his argument.  他写作的冗长使得他的论点难以理解。| Example: The lawyer's overprolixness bored the jury. 律师的冗长乏味让陪审团感到厌烦。| Synonyms: verbosity, wordiness, long-windedness | Antonyms: conciseness, brevity, succinctness | noun


Etymology: From "over" + "prolix" + "ness". "Prolix" comes from Latin "prolixus," meaning "extended," from "pro-" (forth) + "lixus" (fluid, flowing).  "Ness" is a suffix forming nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality.
