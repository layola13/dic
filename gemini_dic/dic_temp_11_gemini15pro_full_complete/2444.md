adjunct:/ˈædʒʌŋkt/| n. adj.|附属物，附件；助手；修饰语|Something added to something else as a supplementary rather than an essential part.  An assistant or helper. A word or phrase that adds information to a clause.|The website is designed as an adjunct to the TV show. (该网站是作为电视节目的附属产品而设计的。) He works as an adjunct professor at the university. (他在大学担任兼职教授。)  In the sentence "She sings beautifully," "beautifully" is an adjunct. (在句子“她唱得很好听”中，“beautifully”是一个修饰语。)|近义词：supplement, addition, accessory, assistant, helper|反义词:primary, main, essential|词性:名词,形容词

adjunct:/ˈædʒʌŋkt/| adj.|附属的，附加的|Added or connected in a subordinate capacity; attached to a faculty or staff in an auxiliary capacity.|He holds an adjunct position at the university. (他在大学担任兼职职位。)|近义词：auxiliary, supplementary, subordinate|反义词:primary, main, essential|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *adjunctus*, past participle of adjungere (“to join to”), from ad- (“to”) + jungere (“to join”).
