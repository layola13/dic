/ˈneɪmərz/ | n. | 命名者; 命名人的 | One that names, especially a person who chooses a name for a product, service, or organization.  |  The namers chose a catchy title for the new software. 命名者为新软件选择了一个朗朗上口的名字。 |  designators, christeners |  - | noun


/ˈneɪməz/ | possessive pronoun | 命名者的; 命名人的 | Belonging to or associated with a person or people who give names. | This is the namers' decision, and we should respect it. 这是命名者的决定，我们应该尊重它。 | - | - | pronoun (possessive)


ETYMOLOGY->
From 'namer' + '-s' (possessive suffix). 'Namer' comes from the verb 'name' (from Old English *namian, from Proto-Germanic *namōjaną, from *namō "name"), plus the agent suffix '-er'.
