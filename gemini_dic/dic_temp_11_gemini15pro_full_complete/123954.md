morigeration:/ˌmɒrɪdʒəˈreɪʃn/ /ˌmɔːrɪdʒəˈreɪʃn/ | n. | 顺从，驯服，服从 |  The act of complying with accepted standards of behavior; obedience to customs or rules.  It implies a disciplined and controlled behavior, often in response to authority or social norms. | His morigeration to the strict rules of the monastery was remarkable. 他对修道院严格规定的顺从令人瞩目。| 近义词: obedience, compliance, conformity, docility, subservience | 反义词: disobedience, defiance, rebellion, nonconformity | 词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *morigeratio*, from *morigerari* "to comply with, obey," from *mos* (genitive *moris*) "custom, habit" + *-ger* (from *agere* "to do, drive").
