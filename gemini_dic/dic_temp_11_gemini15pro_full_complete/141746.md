orangeman:/ˈɔːrɪndʒmən/ | n. |奥兰治人，橙衣人| A member of the Orange Order, a Protestant fraternal organization, primarily in Northern Ireland.  This term is often considered derogatory due to the Order's association with sectarianism. |  He was called an orangeman by his Catholic neighbours, which he found offensive. 他被他的天主教邻居称为橙衣人，他觉得这很冒犯。 |  The orangemen marched through the town, playing their drums and flutes. 橙衣人列队穿过城镇，吹奏着他们的鼓和笛子。|Synonyms: Orange Order member (more formal, less loaded), Protestant (broader) | Antonyms: Catholic (in the specific context of Northern Ireland), Nationalist (in the specific context of Northern Ireland)| noun
