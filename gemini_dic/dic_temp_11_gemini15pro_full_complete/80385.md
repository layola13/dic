goliath:/ɡəˈlaɪəθ/ | n. | 巨人；庞然大物；强大的对手 | A person or thing of enormous size, strength, or influence, especially one that is seemingly unbeatable.  Derived from the Philistine giant Goliath in the Bible, slain by David. |  The company is a goliath in the tech industry.  这家公司是科技行业的巨头。 | synonyms: giant, behemoth, colossus, titan | antonyms: dwarf, pygmy, midget | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Goliath, from Hebrew גָּלְיָת‎ (gālyāṯ, “Goliath”).
