inexpedience:/ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiəns/| n.|不适当；不明智；失策|The inexperience of the new CEO led to some costly mistakes.  新任首席执行官的缺乏经验导致了一些代价高昂的错误。|例句：The inexperience of the hiker led him to underestimate the dangers of the mountain. 这名徒步旅行者的缺乏经验导致他低估了这座山的危险性。

The committee decided against the proposal because of its inexperience. 委员会因为这项提议的不成熟而决定反对它。|近义词：inadvisability, imprudence, unwisdom, impolicy|反义词: wisdom, prudence, advisability, expediency|词性:noun

inexpedient:/ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiənt/| adj.|不适当的；不明智的；失策的|It is inexpedient to raise taxes during a recession. 在经济衰退期间加税是不明智的。|例句：It would be inexpedient to ignore the advice of our legal team. 忽略我们法律团队的建议是不明智的。

Making such a large purchase right now would be inexpedient given our current financial situation. 鉴于我们目前的财务状况，现在进行如此大规模的采购是不合适的。|近义词：inadvisable, imprudent, unwise, impolitic|反义词: wise, prudent, advisable, expedient|词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *inexpediēns*, present participle of *inexpedīre* (“to hinder, entangle”), from *in-* (“not”) + *ex-* (“out”) + *pedis* (“foot, fetter”).
