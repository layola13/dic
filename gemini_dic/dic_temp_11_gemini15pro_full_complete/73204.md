forcedly:/ˈfɔːrsɪdli/ (US), /ˈfɔːsɪdli/ (UK)| adv.|强迫地；勉强地；不自然地|Done in a manner that appears compelled or unnatural, rather than spontaneous or willing.|He laughed forcedly, trying to lighten the mood. 他勉强地笑了笑，试图缓和气氛。|

例句：
She smiled forcedly at the unwelcome guest.  她对这位不受欢迎的客人勉强一笑。

She spoke forcedly, her voice trembling slightly. 她说话很勉强，声音微微颤抖。

近义词：compulsorily, unwillingly, artificially, constrainedly|
反义词：willingly, naturally, voluntarily|
词性: adverb (副词)


词源:
From the adjective "forced" + the adverbial suffix "-ly." "Forced" comes from the Old French "forcer" (to force, break open, compel), itself from the Latin "fortis" (strong).
