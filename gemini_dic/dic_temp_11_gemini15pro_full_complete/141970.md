oreweed: /ˈɔːrwiːd/ | n. | 海藻，海草 | A type of seaweed, specifically kelp, that grows in the ocean and is often used as a fertilizer or food source.  It's particularly rich in iodine. | Kelp forests, composed primarily of oreweed, provide habitat for many marine species.  海带森林主要由海藻构成，为许多海洋物种提供了栖息地。 | synonyms: kelp, seaweed, wrack | antonyms:  (No direct antonyms, as it's a specific type of plant) | noun
