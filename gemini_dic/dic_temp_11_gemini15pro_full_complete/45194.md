culdee: /kʌlˈdiː/ /kʌlˈdeɪ/
n.
Culdee;圣徒;苦行僧;隐士
A member of a community of ascetic monks in Ireland, Scotland, and northern England in the early Middle Ages.
The Culdees lived a simple life of prayer and study on the remote island.  那些圣徒在偏远的岛屿上过着简朴的祈祷和学习生活。
近义词: hermit, anchorite, recluse, ascetic
反义词: socialite, extrovert
词性: noun

Etymology:
From Scottish Gaelic *Ceile Dé* (“spouse/servant of God”), from Old Irish *céile Dé* (“spouse/servant of God”).  Ultimately from Latin *famulus Dei* ("servant of God").
