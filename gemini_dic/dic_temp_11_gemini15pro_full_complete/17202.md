baumol: /ˈbaʊmɒl/ (US), /ˈbɔːməl/ (UK) | n. | 鲍莫尔 |  Refers to William J. Baumol (1922-2017), an American economist known for his work on entrepreneurship, innovation, and the performing arts.  The term "Baumol's cost disease" (or "Baumol effect") is frequently used in economics.  It describes the tendency for labor-intensive services, like the performing arts or healthcare, to increase in cost over time even if productivity remains static, because wages rise in line with more productive sectors. | The conference discussed Baumol's cost disease and its implications for the healthcare sector.  这次会议讨论了鲍莫尔成本病及其对医疗保健部门的影响。|  Baumol's groundbreaking work on entrepreneurship changed the field. 鲍莫尔在创业方面的开创性工作改变了该领域。 | synonym:  None (as it refers to a specific person and concept).
