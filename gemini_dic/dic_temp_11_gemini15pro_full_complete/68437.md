faltering:/ˈfɔːltərɪŋ/ (US) /ˈfɔːltərɪŋ/ (UK)| adj. | 犹豫的；蹒跚的；支支吾吾的 | losing strength or momentum; hesitant; unsteady |  His faltering steps revealed his age. 他蹒跚的步伐显露出了他的年龄。| synonyms: hesitant, wavering, unsteady, stumbling, stammering | antonyms: confident, decisive, firm, steady, fluent | adjective

falter:/ˈfɔːltər/ (US) /ˈfɔːltər/ (UK) | v. | 犹豫；蹒跚；支支吾吾 | to lose strength or momentum; to hesitate; to speak hesitantly |  She faltered for a moment, then continued speaking. 她犹豫了一下，然后继续说。| synonyms: hesitate, waver, stumble, stammer | antonyms: persevere, continue, persist | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *falteren*, frequentative of *falten* (“to fold, fall, stumble”), from Old English *feallan* (“to fall”), from Proto-Germanic *fallaną* (“to fall”), from Proto-Indo-European *phol-/*bʰol- (“to fall, stumble”).
