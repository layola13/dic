movent: /ˈmuːvmənt/ | n. | 运动，移动，活动; 乐章;  (政治、社会) 运动 |A movent is a distinct section of a larger musical work, such as a symphony or sonata. It can also refer to a group of people working together to advance shared political, social, or artistic ideas. Additionally, it describes the act of moving or changing position. | The second movent of the concerto was particularly moving. 协奏曲的第二乐章尤其动人。| synonym: motion, shift, action, campaign | antonym: stillness, inertia, inaction | noun
