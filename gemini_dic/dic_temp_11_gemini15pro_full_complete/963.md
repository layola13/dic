acadia:/əˈkeɪdiə/ | n. |阿卡迪亚|A region of eastern Canada comprising Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, and parts of Quebec. Historically a French colony, it is now primarily English-speaking.|例句：Acadia National Park is a beautiful place to visit. 阿卡迪亚国家公园是一个美丽的旅游胜地。

例句：The history of Acadia is filled with both triumph and tragedy. 阿卡迪亚的历史充满了胜利和悲剧。

近义词：Maritime Provinces, Maritimes|反义词: N/A |词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From French Acadie, from Mi'kmaq *akatik or *elakatik ("place abounds with"), referring to the Annapolis Valley area.  The name was first applied to the whole region by Samuel de Champlain.
