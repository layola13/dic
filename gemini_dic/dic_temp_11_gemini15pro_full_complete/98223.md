insecurity:/ˌɪnsɪˈkjʊrəti/| n. |不安全感，不牢靠，无把握|Lack of confidence or assurance; the state of being open to danger or threat.|She felt a deep sense of insecurity about her job. 她对自己的工作感到深深的不安。|近义词：anxiety, uncertainty, doubt, self-doubt, vulnerability|反义词：confidence, security, certainty, assurance|词性:名词

insecure:/ˌɪnsɪˈkjʊr/| adj. |不安全的，不牢靠的，无把握的|Not confident or assured; uncertain; not firmly fixed; likely to fail or give way.|He felt insecure about his ability to do the job. 他对自己的工作能力感到不自信。|近义词：anxious, uncertain, doubtful, shaky, unstable|反义词：confident, secure, certain, stable, firm|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From in- (not) + secure.  The word "secure" comes from Latin "securus," meaning "free from care, safe."
