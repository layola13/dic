complicate:/ˈkɒmplɪkeɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmplɪkeɪt/ (US)| v. |使复杂化；使恶化|To make something more difficult or complex.|The situation was further complicated by the arrival of the police. 警方到来使情况进一步复杂化。|近义词：aggravate, worsen, confuse, muddle|反义词：simplify, ease, clarify|词性:动词

complicated:/ˈkɒmplɪkeɪtɪd/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmplɪkeɪtɪd/ (US)| adj. |复杂的；难懂的|Difficult to understand or deal with because of having many parts or aspects.|The instructions were so complicated that I couldn't figure them out. 说明书太复杂了，我看不懂。|近义词：complex, intricate, involved|反义词：simple, straightforward, easy|词性:形容词

complication:/ˌkɒmplɪˈkeɪʃn/ (UK) /ˌkɑːmplɪˈkeɪʃn/ (US)| n. |并发症；复杂化；难题|A circumstance that makes something more difficult or complex.|There were complications during surgery. 手术期间出现了并发症。|近义词：difficulty, problem, snag, hitch|反义词：solution, simplification|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin complicātus, past participle of complicāre (“to fold together, embrace”), from com- (“together”) + plicāre (“to fold”).
