hypergrammaticalness:/ˌhaɪpərɡrəˈmætɪkəlnəs/ | n. |过度语法化；超语法化|The tendency to overapply grammatical rules, often resulting in unnatural or overly formal language.  It is sometimes observed in language learners or individuals with certain neurological conditions.|  His writing suffers from hypergrammaticalness, making it sound stiff and unnatural. 他的写作过度语法化，听起来僵硬不自然。|  She exhibited hypergrammaticalness by consistently using whom even in casual conversation. 即使在日常对话中，她也表现出过度语法化，总是使用“whom”。 |Near Synonyms: overformalism, pedantry, rigidity | Antonyms: ungrammaticality, colloquialism| noun
