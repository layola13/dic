break ranks: /breɪk ˈræŋks/
verb phrase
打破常规；公开反对；背叛
To go against the established norms or expectations of a group; to dissent publicly; to be disloyal.
The senator broke ranks with his party and voted against the bill.  这位参议员打破常规，投票反对该法案。
Soldiers are expected to obey orders and not break ranks.  士兵们应该服从命令，不要违抗命令。
Synonyms: dissent, disagree, rebel, defect, oppose
Antonyms: conform, agree, support, obey

ranks: /ræŋks/
noun
队伍；行列；等级；军衔
The lines of soldiers standing side by side; a position in a hierarchy.
词源：From Old French renc (“row, rank”), from Frankish *hring (“ring, circle”).
