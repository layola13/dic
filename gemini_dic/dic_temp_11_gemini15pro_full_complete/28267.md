captivate:/ˈkæptɪveɪt/| vt.| 迷住，吸引|to attract and hold the interest and attention of someone by being charming, interesting, etc.| Her singing captivated the audience. 她的歌声迷住了观众。|近义词：enchant, fascinate, mesmerize, charm|反义词：bore, repel, disgust|动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin captivatus, past participle of captivare "to take prisoner," from Latin captivus "captive," from captus, past participle of capere "to take, seize."  The meaning shifted from "taking prisoner" to "capturing the attention or affection" in the 16th century.
