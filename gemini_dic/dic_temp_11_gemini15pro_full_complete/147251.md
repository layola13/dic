overmercifulness:/ˌoʊvərˈmɜːrsɪfəlnəs/| n.|过度仁慈，过分怜悯|An excess of mercy or compassion, sometimes to the point of being detrimental.  A tendency to be too forgiving or lenient.|His overmercifulness towards his employees led to a decline in discipline.|近义词：excessive mercy, undue leniency, overindulgence, extreme compassion|反义词:ruthlessness, severity, harshness|词性:noun

Etymology:
From "over-" (excessive) + "merciful" (showing mercy) + "-ness" (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).  "Merciful" comes from "mercy" (from Old French "merci") + "-ful" (full of).
