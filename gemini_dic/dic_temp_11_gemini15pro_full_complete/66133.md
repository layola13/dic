exceptionable:/ɪkˈsɛpʃənəbl/| adj.|值得反对的；可非难的； objectionable|Open to objection; causing disapproval or offense.  Its meaning is often confused with "exceptional," which means "unusually good."|Her behavior at the dinner party was quite exceptionable, constantly interrupting and making rude remarks. 她在晚宴上的行为非常令人反感，不断打断别人并发表粗鲁的言论。|Synonyms: objectionable, offensive, reprehensible, deplorable, blameworthy|Antonyms: admirable, commendable, laudable, praiseworthy|adjective

exceptionable:/ɪkˈsɛpʃənəbl/| adj.|可取的；卓越的|In rare cases, *exceptionable* is used in its original sense, meaning "forming an exception; rare, unusual; excellent" – this usage is now archaic and potentially confusing.|His talent as a musician is exceptionable; he's truly a prodigy.  (Archaic usage) 他的音乐天赋非常卓越；他真是个天才。|Synonyms: exceptional, extraordinary, remarkable (in archaic usage)|Antonyms: ordinary, common, mediocre (in archaic usage)|adjective
