coordinates:/koʊˈɔːrdɪnɪts/ /kəʊˈɔːdɪnəts/ | n. |坐标；协调；配合|A set of numbers that show an exact position.|例句：The coordinates of the buried treasure are marked on the map.  埋藏宝藏的坐标标在地图上。

例句：The pilot radioed the coordinates of the plane to the control tower. 飞行员用无线电将飞机的坐标传送给控制塔。|近义词：position, location, placement|反义词: N/A |词性:noun


coordinates:/koʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ /kəʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ | v. |协调；配合|To make different things work effectively as a whole.|例句：She'll be coordinating the relief effort. 她将负责协调救援工作。

例句：We need to coordinate our efforts if we want to succeed. 如果我们想成功，就需要协调我们的努力。|近义词：harmonize, synchronize, integrate|反义词:clash, conflict |词性:verb


coordinates:/koʊˈɔːrdɪnɪt/ /kəʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ | adj. |同等的；并列的|Equal in importance or rank.|例句：Coordinate clauses are joined by coordinating conjunctions. 并列子句由并列连词连接。

例句：They hold coordinate jurisdiction in this matter. 他们在这个问题上拥有同等管辖权。|近义词：equivalent, corresponding, matching|反义词:subordinate, unequal |词性:adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *coördinātus*, past participle of coördināre (“to arrange together, coordinate”), from com- (“together”) + ordinātus (“arranged, ordered”), past participle of ordināre (“to set in order”), from ordo (“order, rank”).
