logwood:/ˈlɒɡwʊd/ (US) /ˈlɒɡwʊd/ (UK)| n.|原木；苏木；苏木染料|The heartwood of a tropical American tree (Haematoxylum campechianum), used to make a red dye.|The artist used logwood to create a rich, dark purple in his painting.|Synonyms: haematoxylin, bloodwood, campeachy wood|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for the substance itself, perhaps synthetic dyes could be considered)|Noun
