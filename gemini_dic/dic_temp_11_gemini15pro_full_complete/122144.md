mistresses:/ˈmɪstrɪsɪz/ | n. | 女主人，情妇 | A woman who is in a position of authority or control, especially in a household or other domestic environment; a woman other than his wife with whom a man has a continuing sexual relationship. |例句：The mistress of the house greeted us warmly. 女主人热情地迎接了我们。


例句：He kept his mistress a secret from his wife. 他对妻子隐瞒了他的情妇。

近义词：lover, paramour, concubine (for mistress in the second sense); owner, lady, madam (for mistress in the first sense) | 反义词：servant, maid (for mistress in the first sense) | 词性：名词

mistresses:/ˈmɪstrɪsɪz/ | n. | (尤指技艺或知识的)能手，女大师 | A woman who is highly skilled in something. | 例句：She is a mistress of disguise. 她是伪装高手。


例句：A mistress of the art of persuasion.  一位说服艺术的大师。


近义词：expert, virtuoso, adept | 反义词：novice, beginner | 词性：名词
