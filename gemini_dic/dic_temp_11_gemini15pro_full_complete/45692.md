curtsies:/ˈkɜːrtsiz/ (US) /ˈkɜːtsiːz/ (UK)| n. |屈膝礼，行屈膝礼|A curtsy is a gesture of respect or reverence made by women and girls, typically to royalty or other persons of high social standing. It involves bending the knees and slightly lowering the body.|The young girl curtsied to the queen. 小女孩向女王行屈膝礼。|Synonyms: bow, genuflection |Antonyms: stand, rise|noun

curtsy:/ˈkɜːrtsi/ (US) /ˈkɜːtsiː/ (UK)| n. vi.|屈膝礼，行屈膝礼| A curtsy is a gesture of respect or reverence made by women and girls.  As a verb, it means to perform this gesture. |She curtsied to the Duchess. 她向公爵夫人行屈膝礼。|Synonyms: bow, genuflect|Antonyms: stand, rise|noun, verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *curtesie*, from Old French *curtesie*, which itself derives from *cort*, meaning "court."  Originally referred to the elaborate etiquette and manners practiced in royal courts. The physical gesture of the curtsy evolved from this broader concept of courtly behavior.
