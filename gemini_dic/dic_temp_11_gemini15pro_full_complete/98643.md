insubstantially:/ˌɪnsəbˈstænʃəli/ | adv. | 无实体地；无实质地；脆弱地；虚弱地 |  Lacking substance or solidity; not firmly based; flimsy; weak. |  He built his argument insubstantially on hearsay and rumour. 他基于传闻和谣言，论证缺乏根据。 | Synonyms: flimsily, weakly, vaguely, slightly, tenuously | Antonyms: substantially, solidly, firmly, strongly | 词性: 副词

Etymology: From Late Latin *insubstantialis*, from in- "not" + substantialis "of substance," from substantia "substance."
