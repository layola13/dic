disfranchisement:/ˌdɪsˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/
n.
剥夺公民权; 剥夺选举权
The state of being deprived of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.
Disfranchisement of black voters was a common practice in the Jim Crow South.  在实行种族隔离的南方，剥夺黑人选民的选举权是一种普遍的做法。
The disfranchisement of felons is a controversial issue.  剥夺重罪犯的选举权是一个有争议的问题。
近义词:deprivation, disqualification
反义词:enfranchisement, empowerment


disfranchise:/ˌdɪsˈfræntʃaɪz/
vt.
剥夺公民权; 剥夺选举权
To deprive someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.
The new law disfranchised many poor and minority citizens.  这项新法律剥夺了许多贫困和少数族裔公民的权利。
They were disfranchised for not paying their poll tax. 他们因为没有缴纳人头税而被剥夺了选举权。
近义词:deprive, strip
反义词:enfranchise, empower

词性: 动词(verb), 名词(noun) -  disfranchise (v.), disfranchisement (n.)
