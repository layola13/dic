haemorrhage:/ˈhɛmərɪdʒ/| n. v.|出血；大量流失|A rapid and uncontrollable loss or outflow.|He suffered a massive brain haemorrhage. 他脑部大出血。|Synonyms: bleeding, leaking, losing|Antonyms: gaining, accumulating|noun, verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *hémorrhagie*, from Late Latin *haemorrhagia*, from Ancient Greek *αἱμορραγία* (haimorrhagía, “violent bleeding”), from *αἷμα* (haîma, “blood”) + *ῥήγνυμι* (rhḗgnumi, “to break, burst”).
