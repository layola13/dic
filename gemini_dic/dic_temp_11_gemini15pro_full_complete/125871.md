muscle:/ˈmʌsl/ (US), /ˈmʌsəl/ (UK)| n.  |肌肉；力量；实力|A band or bundle of fibrous tissue in a human or animal body that has the ability to contract, producing movement.  Also, can refer to physical strength or power, or influential power.|

例句：He strained a muscle in his leg. 他拉伤了腿部肌肉。

例句：The company is flexing its financial muscle. 这家公司正在展现其雄厚的财力。


近义词：sinew, brawn, strength, power|
反义词：weakness, impotence|
词性：noun|

muscle:/ˈmʌsl/ (US), /ˈmʌsəl/ (UK)| v. |硬挤；强行挤入|To move something or oneself forcefully or with difficulty.|

例句：He muscled his way through the crowd. 他挤过人群。

例句：She muscled the heavy box into the car.  她用力把沉重的箱子塞进车里。


近义词：force, shove, push|
反义词：yield, give way|
词性：verb|
