desertlike:/ˈdɛzətˌlaɪk/| adj.|像沙漠的，荒漠般的|Resembling a desert; barren, arid.|The landscape was desertlike, with little vegetation and scorching sun.  这片地貌像沙漠一样，植被稀少，阳光灼热。|Synonyms: barren, arid, desolate, infertile|Antonyms: fertile, lush, verdant|adjective

Etymology->
From desert + -like.  "Desert" comes from Old French "desert"  meaning "uninhabited place," ultimately from Latin "desertus," past participle of "deserere" "to abandon." "-like" is a suffix meaning "resembling" or "characteristic of."
