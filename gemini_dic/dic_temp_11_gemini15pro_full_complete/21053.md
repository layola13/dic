blinded:/ˈblaɪndɪd/ | adj. | 失明的，盲目的，被蒙蔽的 | Deprived of sight, unable to see; lacking perception or understanding; dazzled by a bright light. |  The accident left him blinded for life.  事故使他终身失明。| synonyms: sightless, unseeing, visually impaired, unaware, ignorant, oblivious | antonyms: sighted, seeing, aware, informed, perceptive | adjective


blinded:/ˈblaɪndɪd/ | v. | 使失明，使目眩，使失去判断力 |  Past participle of "blind". To deprive of sight, either permanently or temporarily; to dazzle with a bright light; to obscure or confuse (judgment or perception). | The bright headlights blinded me momentarily.  明亮的车头灯使我一时眼花缭乱。| synonyms: dazzle, daze, bedazzle | antonyms: enlighten, illuminate, clarify | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *blind, from Proto-Germanic *blindaz, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰlendʰ- (“to mix, blend, confuse”).  The original sense may have been "mixed up, confused," which later developed into the meaning of "unable to see."
