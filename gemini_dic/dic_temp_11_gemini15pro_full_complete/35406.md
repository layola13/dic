co-optative:/koʊˈɑːptətɪv/
adjective
选择性的；吸收的；可选择的
Relating to or expressing a wish or desire; opting in.
例句：The co-optative mood in some languages expresses a wish or hope.  一些语言中的选择语气表达愿望或希望。

例句：His co-optative approach to leadership allowed team members to choose their roles. 他选择性的领导方式允许团队成员选择自己的角色。
近义词：optional, elective, desirable, wishful
反义词：mandatory, compulsory, obligatory
词性: adjective


co-opt:/koʊˈɑːpt/
verb
吸收；增选；指派
To appoint to membership of a committee or other body by invitation of the existing members.  To adopt (an idea or policy).
例句：The committee co-opted several new members. 委员会增选了几名新成员。

例句：The rebels co-opted the original goals of the movement.  叛乱者采纳了运动最初的目标。
近义词：absorb, incorporate, appropriate, enlist
反义词：reject, exclude, expel
词性: verb
