fetus:/ˈfiːtəs/| n. |胎儿|An unborn offspring of a mammal, more specifically a human, in the later stages of its development, especially after the major structures have been differentiated.|The fetus is developing normally.  胎儿发育正常。|Synonym:  unborn child, embryo (in earlier stages)|Antonym:  baby, infant, newborn|noun

fetuous:/ˈfiːtəs/| adj. |愚蠢的，幼稚的|Silly and pointless.|Making a fetuous remark about the weather.  对天气发表愚蠢的评论。|Synonym:  silly, foolish, inane, puerile, vapid|Antonym:  sensible, intelligent, wise, mature|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *fetus* "offspring, bringing forth, fruitful," related to *fecundus* "fruitful, fertile."  The spelling with "oe" reflects an older pronunciation and is now considered archaic in American English, though still sometimes used in British English.
