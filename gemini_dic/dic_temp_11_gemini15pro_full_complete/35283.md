clutter:/ˈklʌtər/ (US), /ˈklʌtə(r)/ (UK)| n. v. |杂乱，混乱；使凌乱，使杂乱|A state of disorder, untidiness, or crowdedness.|The room was filled with clutter.|Synonyms: mess, disorder, chaos, confusion|Antonyms: order, tidiness, neatness|noun
clutter:/ˈklʌtər/ (US), /ˈklʌtə(r)/ (UK)| v. |使凌乱，使杂乱|To fill or cover with scattered or disordered things.|Too many pictures cluttered the wall.|Synonyms: mess up, litter, jumble, disorganize|Antonyms: tidy, organize, declutter, neaten|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clutteren, frequentative of clotteren (“to form clots”).
