overdecadences: /ˌoʊvərˈdɛkədənsɪz/ | n. | 过度颓废，过度放纵 |  Excessive decadence; a state of extreme decline and decay, often marked by excessive indulgence in pleasure or luxury.  This word is not commonly used and might be considered neologistic (newly coined). It's formed by adding the prefix "over-" to "decadences," emphasizing an extreme degree. |  The empire's overdecadences led to its inevitable downfall.  帝国的过度颓废导致了它不可避免的衰落。

The artist's work reflected the overdecadences of the era.  这位艺术家的作品反映了那个时代的过度颓废。 | 近义词：excess, debauchery, dissipation, profligacy | 反义词：austerity, moderation, restraint | 词性: 名词

词源:

"over-" (prefix): From Old English "ofer," meaning "above, beyond, across."

"decadence": From French "décadence," from Late Latin "decadentia," from "decadere" ("to fall down, decay"), from "de-" ("down") + "cadere" ("to fall").
