cutling: /ˈkʌtlɪŋ/ | n. | 插枝，插条；幼小动物 | A young animal or a plant cutting. |  The gardener carefully planted the rose cutlings. 园丁小心地种下了玫瑰插枝。| 近义词: slip, cutting, offspring | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps parent plant or mature animal) | 词性: noun


Etymology:  From the verb "to cut," meaning to sever or divide something.  The "-ling" suffix indicates a diminutive or young version.  Therefore, a cutling is a small piece that has been cut from a larger entity.
