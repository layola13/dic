cloistral:/ˈklɔɪstrəl/| adj.|修道院的；隐居的；与世隔绝的|Resembling or suggestive of a cloister; secluded from the world.|The scholar lived a cloistral life, dedicated to his studies.  这位学者过着隐居的生活，致力于他的研究。|例句：The library had a cloistral atmosphere, quiet and conducive to contemplation. 图书馆有一种修道院般的氛围，安静且有助于沉思。

例句：He preferred the cloistral calm of the countryside to the bustle of the city. 他喜欢乡村修道院般的宁静，胜过城市的喧嚣。|近义词：secluded, monastic, solitary, withdrawn, reclusive|反义词：worldly, gregarious, social, public|词性:adjective
ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *cloistral, *clostral, from Anglo-Norman clostral, from Old French cloistre (“cloister”), from Latin claustrum (“bolt, bar, lock, enclosed place”), from claudere (“to close, shut”).
