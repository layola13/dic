braggest: /ˈbræɡɪst/ | adj. (superlative) | 最爱吹牛的，最自夸的 |  Superlative form of "braggart," meaning most boastful or prone to bragging. It describes someone who boasts more than anyone else in a given group. | He's the braggest person I've ever met; he can't stop talking about himself. 他是我见过最爱吹牛的人；他总是滔滔不绝地谈论自己。|  He was the braggest kid in school, always boasting about his athletic abilities. 他是学校里最爱吹嘘的孩子，总是吹嘘自己的运动能力。| 近义词: most boastful, most arrogant | 反义词: most humble, most modest | 词性: 形容词

词源: From "brag" + "-est" (superlative suffix).  "Brag" comes from Middle English "braggen," of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old Norse "braka" (to crash, crack).
