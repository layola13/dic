ideal:/aɪˈdiːəl/ /aɪˈdiəl/| adj. n.|理想的，完美的；理想，典范|existing only in the imagination; desirable or perfect but not likely to become a reality.  A conception of something in its perfection.|She has the ideal qualities for the job. 她具备这项工作的理想素质。|近义词：perfect, optimal, exemplary|反义词：real, imperfect, flawed|adjective, noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin ideālis "existing in idea," from Latin idea "idea, mental image" (see idea).  The original sense was philosophical. Meaning "satisfying one's idea of perfection" is from 1610s; that of "existing only in idea, visionary" is from 1640s.  The noun meaning "a perfect example" is from 1758.
