libertarianism:/ˌlɪbərˈtɛəriənɪzəm/| n. |自由意志主义，自由主义|A political philosophy that emphasizes individual liberty, autonomy, and limited government intervention. It advocates for maximizing individual rights and minimizing the role of the state.|Example: Libertarianism promotes free markets and individual choice. 自由意志主义提倡自由市场和个人选择。

Example:  She found the principles of libertarianism appealing. 她觉得自由意志主义的原则很有吸引力。|Synonyms: individualism, liberalism (in some contexts), free-market capitalism|Antonyms: authoritarianism, statism, collectivism|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *libertaire* ("libertarian"), from Latin *libertarius* ("pertaining to liberty"), from *libertas* ("liberty"). The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, belief, or system.
