appropriates:/əˈproʊprieɪts/
v.|拨出; 占用; 盗用| to take something for one's own use, typically without the owner's permission or illegally. | The government appropriated land that was traditionally used by the indigenous people. 政府征用了原住民传统使用的土地。|
| |The author was accused of appropriating another writer's ideas.  该作者被指控盗用另一位作家的想法。|
|Synonyms:|allocate, assign, seize, usurp|
|Antonyms:|return, relinquish, restore|
|verb|


appropriate:/əˈproʊpriət/
adj.|适当的; 合适的;|suitable or proper in the circumstances.| It's appropriate to wear formal attire to a wedding.  穿正装参加婚礼是合适的。|
| |Her response was perfectly appropriate to the situation. 她的回应非常适合当时的情况。|
|Synonyms:|suitable, fitting, proper, apt, relevant|
|Antonyms:|inappropriate, unsuitable, improper, irrelevant|
|adjective|


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English: from late Latin appropriatus, past participle of appropriare ‘make one’s own’, from ad- ‘to’ + proprius ‘one’s own’.
