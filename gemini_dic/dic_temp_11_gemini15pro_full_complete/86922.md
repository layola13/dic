hemophilia:/ˌhiːməˈfɪliə/ (US), /ˌhiːməˈfɪliə/ (UK)| n. |血友病|A medical condition in which the ability of the blood to clot is severely reduced, causing the sufferer to bleed severely from even a slight injury.|My cousin has hemophilia and has to be very careful to avoid cuts and bruises.|Synonyms: bleeder's disease|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym for a disease, could consider "normal blood clotting")|noun
