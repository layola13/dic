lavour:/ˈleɪvə(r) (British English), ˈleɪvər (American English)| n. vt.| 味道，滋味；偏爱，喜爱；给…调味|A distinctive taste; to add a flavour to food or drink.|The dish had a distinct smoky flavour. 这道菜有一种明显的烟熏味. |She favoured him with a smile. 她对他笑了笑. |He favoured his youngest son. 他偏爱最小的儿子.|近义词：taste, savor, aroma, prefer, favor|反义词：blandness, dislike|词性: noun, verb

Etymology: From Old French *savor*, from Latin *sapor* "taste." The spelling with l- developed by influence of similar words like *flavor*.
