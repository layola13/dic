bowable:/ˈboʊəbl/| adj.|可鞠躬的，可弯曲的|Suitable for bowing; capable of being bowed.  This refers primarily to stringed instruments or the hair of a bow used to play them. It can also metaphorically describe something that can be bent or lowered, like one's head in submission. |The violin was old but still bowable.  这把小提琴虽然老了，但仍然可以演奏。|近义词：bendable, flexible, pliable|反义词:rigid, stiff|词性:形容词
ETYMOLOGY->
From bow (verb) + -able.  Bow (verb) meaning "to bend" comes from Old English *būgan, related to Old Norse bjúga.  The suffix "-able" comes from Old French -able and Latin -abilis, meaning "capable of being."
