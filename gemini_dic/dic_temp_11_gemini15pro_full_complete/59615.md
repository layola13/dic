eccentrically:/ɪkˈsɛntrɪkli/ | adv. | 古怪地；反常地 | In a strange or unusual way, deviating from the norm. | She dressed eccentrically, always wearing mismatched socks and a brightly coloured hat. 她穿着古怪，总是穿着不搭的袜子和一顶颜色鲜艳的帽子。 | The old man danced eccentrically in the park, twirling his cane and singing loudly.  这位老人在公园里古怪地跳舞，旋转着他的拐杖，大声唱歌。 | synonym: oddly, strangely, peculiarly, bizarrely | antonym: conventionally, normally, ordinarily | adverb

eccentric:/ɪkˈsɛntrɪk/ | adj. | 古怪的；反常的 |  Unconventional and slightly strange. |  My eccentric aunt collects porcelain dolls and believes she can talk to animals. 我古怪的阿姨收集瓷娃娃，并相信她可以和动物说话。 | He had an eccentric habit of wearing a different coloured sock on each foot. 他有一个古怪的习惯，每只脚都穿不同颜色的袜子。 | synonym: odd, strange, peculiar, unusual | antonym: conventional, normal, ordinary | adjective

eccentricity:/ˌɛksɛnˈtrɪsɪti/ | n. | 古怪；反常 | The quality of being eccentric. |  Her eccentricity was endearing to some and off-putting to others. 她的古怪行为让一些人觉得可爱，但也让另一些人感到厌烦。 |  The eccentricity of the artist's work made it both fascinating and unsettling.  这位艺术家的作品的古怪之处使它既迷人又令人不安。| synonym: strangeness, peculiarity, oddity, quirkiness | antonym: normality, conventionality, ordinariness | noun


ETYMOLOGY->

From Late Latin *eccentricus, from Greek ekkentros “out of center,” from ek “out of” + kentron “center.”
