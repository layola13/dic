bilboquet:/ˌbɪlbəˈkeɪ/ | n. | 空竹，杯球游戏 | A traditional toy consisting of a small wooden cup or spike attached to a handle by a string, the object being to toss the cup and catch it on the spike or in the cup. | He spent hours mastering the bilboquet, flicking the wooden cup onto the spike with surprising dexterity. 他花了几个小时掌握空竹，以惊人的灵巧将木杯抛到尖刺上。|  The child delighted in playing with the bilboquet, its bright colours flashing in the sunlight.  孩子高兴地玩着空竹，它鲜艳的颜色在阳光下闪烁。 |Synonyms: cup-and-ball, ball-in-cup | Antonyms: N/A | noun


Etymology: From French *bilboquet*, from *bille* (ball) + *bocquet* (small stick). Ultimately from Latin *bulla* (bubble, round object).
