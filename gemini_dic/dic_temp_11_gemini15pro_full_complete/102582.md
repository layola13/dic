jahveh:/ˈjɑːveɪ/ | n. |耶和华，上帝| A name for God, especially in the Old Testament.  Derived from the Hebrew YHWH, the Tetragrammaton, which was considered too sacred to be pronounced. |The name Jahveh appears frequently in some translations of the Bible.  耶和华这个名字在一些圣经译本中经常出现。|  Example 2: Some scholars believe Jahveh was originally a Canaanite god. 一些学者认为耶和华最初是迦南的神。| synonyms: God, Lord, Jehovah | antonyms: N/A (God has no direct opposite) | noun
