nonpersuasively:/ˌnɒnpəˈsweɪsɪvli/ /ˌnɑːnpərˈsweɪsɪvli/| adv.|不具说服力地，没有说服力地|In a manner that fails to convince or persuade.|He argued nonpersuasively, failing to win over the jury. 他辩护得没有说服力，没能赢得陪审团的支持。|Synonyms: unconvincingly, ineffectively, weakly|Antonyms: persuasively, convincingly, effectively|Adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- (not) + persuasive + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Persuasive" comes from Late Latin *persuasivus, from Latin persuasus, past participle of persuadere "to persuade," from per- "thoroughly" + suadere "to advise."
