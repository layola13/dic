deafening:/ˈdɛfənɪŋ/ (US), /ˈdɛfnɪŋ/ (UK)| adj.|震耳欲聋的；使人感到震耳欲聋的| extremely loud; so loud as to make it difficult to hear anything else|The music at the concert was deafening. 音乐会上的音乐震耳欲聋。|The roar of the jet engine was deafening. 喷气发动机的轰鸣声震耳欲聋。|近义词：loud, earsplitting, thunderous|反义词：quiet, silent|词性:形容词

deafenings:/ˈdɛfənɪŋz/ (US), /ˈdɛfnɪŋz/ (UK)| n.|震耳欲聋的声音（通常用复数形式）| loud, overwhelming noises (usually plural)|The sudden deafenings from the construction site startled everyone. 建筑工地突然传来的巨大噪音吓了所有人一跳。|The constant deafenings of city life can be stressful. 城市生活中持续不断的噪音会让人感到压力很大。|近义词：din, clamor, racket|反义词：silence, quiet|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From deafen + -ing. Deafen comes from the Old English *deafian, from deaf.  The suffix -ing forms present participles and gerunds, and can also be used to form nouns derived from verbs.
