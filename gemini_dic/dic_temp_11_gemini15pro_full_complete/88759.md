hocus-pocus:/ˌhoʊkəs ˈpoʊkəs/ | n. |戏法；胡言乱语；骗人的把戏|A seemingly magical spell or trickery used to deceive or mystify.  It refers to meaningless words used in magic or trickery.|The magician amazed the children with his hocus-pocus. 魔术师用他的戏法使孩子们惊叹不已。|Synonyms: trickery, deception, sleight of hand, conjuring, magic, mumbo jumbo|Antonyms: reality, truth, honesty|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From a mock Latin phrase used by jugglers and conjurers, perhaps formed by alteration of *Hoc est corpus meum* ("This is my body"), words used in the Eucharist.
