grenade: /ɡrəˈneɪd/ | n. | 手榴弹；榴弹 | A small bomb thrown by hand or launched mechanically. |  The soldier threw a grenade into the enemy bunker. 士兵向敌人的碉堡投掷了一枚手榴弹。

The grenade exploded with a deafening roar. 手榴弹爆炸发出震耳欲聋的巨响。 | synonyms: bomb, explosive | antonyms: peace,  disarmament | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *grenade* "pomegranate," from Old Provençal *granada*, from Latin *grānātum* (apple) "seeded," from *grānum* "grain, seed."  So called from its resemblance to a pomegranate in shape and the scattering of its fragments when it explodes.
