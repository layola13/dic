earthworks:/ˈɜːrθwɜːrks/
noun
土方工程；土方工事
Artificial changes to the ground, especially large ones, such as embankments or ditches, made usually for a road, railroad, fortification, etc.
The Roman earthworks are still visible today. 罗马时代的土方工程至今仍然可见。

近义词:excavations, embankments, fortifications, trenches
反义词:None (no direct opposite for a physical alteration to the land)
词性:n. (noun)

Etymology:
From 'earth' + 'work' (plural).  'Earth' from Old English *eorðe, from Proto-Germanic *erþō, from Proto-Indo-European *er-. 'Work' from Old English *worc, *weorc, from Proto-Germanic *werkaną, from Proto-Indo-European *werg-.
