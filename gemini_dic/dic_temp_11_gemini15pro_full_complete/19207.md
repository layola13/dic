beylic: /ˈbeɪlɪk/ | n. | 行政区；贝伊领；省 | A territory or province ruled by a bey. |  The Ottoman Empire was initially divided into several beylics. 奥斯曼帝国最初被划分为几个贝伊领。 | The beylic was known for its rich agricultural lands.  这个贝伊领以其肥沃的农田而闻名。|  province, territory, district | empire, kingdom | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Turkish *beylik*, from *bey* (lord, chief, prince) + *-lik* (suffix forming nouns of appurtenance or pertaining to).  Ultimately from Turkish *beg*, from Proto-Turkic *bäk* (lord, ruler).
