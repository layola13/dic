diagonal:/daɪˈæɡənəl/ /daɪˈæɡnəl/ | adj. n. | 对角线的，斜线的；对角线，斜线 | A diagonal line or plane is straight and sloping, not horizontal or vertical, and joins two opposite corners of a square, rectangle, or other flat shape. |

例句：
The architect drew a diagonal line across the plan.
建筑师在图纸上画了一条对角线。

例句：
The queen moves diagonally across the chessboard.
皇后在棋盘上斜着移动。


近义词：oblique, slanting, transversal | 反义词: horizontal, vertical | 词性: adjective, noun

diagonal:/daɪˈæɡənəl/ /daɪˈæɡnəl/ | n. | 对角线，斜线 | A straight line joining two opposite corners of a square, rectangle, or other straight-sided shape. |

例句：
The length of the diagonal of the square is 10 cm.
正方形对角线的长度是10厘米。


例句：
Fold the paper along the diagonal.
沿着对角线折叠纸张。


近义词：oblique line, slant line, transversal | 反义词: horizontal line, vertical line | 词性: noun


词源: From Late Latin *diagonalis, from Greek diagonios (διάγωνιος) "from angle to angle," from dia- (διά) "through, across" + gonia (γωνία) "angle, corner."
