cram:/kræm/ | v. | 塞满；填鸭式地学习； | to force a lot of things into a small space; to study hard in a short time just before a test | I crammed all my clothes into the suitcase. 我把所有衣服都塞进了手提箱。| synonyms: stuff, pack, jam | antonyms: empty, unpack | verb

crammed:/kræmd/ | adj. | 拥挤的；塞满了的 | full of people or things | The bus was crammed with passengers. 公交车上挤满了乘客。| synonyms: crowded, packed, full | antonyms: empty, spacious | adjective

crams:/kræmz/ | v. (third-person singular simple present) | 塞满；填鸭式地学习；(cram的第三人称单数形式) | He crams his schedule with appointments. 他把他的日程安排得满满的。| synonyms: stuffs, packs, jams | antonyms: empties, unpacks | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English crammen, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Old Norse kramma (“to grip, squeeze”).
