lavisher:/ˈlævɪʃər/ | n. | 挥霍者，浪费者 | Someone who spends or gives something valuable or desirable freely and in large amounts. | He was a lavisher of his talents, using them for trivial pursuits. 他挥霍自己的才能，将其用于琐碎的追求。|  The company is a lavisher of funds on advertising.  这家公司在广告上挥霍资金。 |近义词：spendthrift, prodigal, squanderer | 反义词：saver, miser | 词性: 名词

lavish: /ˈlævɪʃ/ | adj. | 奢华的，丰富的 | Sumptuously rich, elaborate, or luxurious. |  She threw a lavish party for her birthday. 她举办了一个奢华的生日派对。 | The hotel offered a lavish breakfast buffet.  酒店提供丰盛的自助早餐。 |近义词：luxurious, opulent, extravagant | 反义词：meager, sparse, frugal | 词性: 形容词

lavish: /ˈlævɪʃ/ | v. | 慷慨地给予，挥霍 | To bestow something in generous or extravagant quantities upon. |  He lavished attention on his new girlfriend. 他对他的新女友关怀备至。|  The company lavished money on the project. 公司在这个项目上投入了巨资。 |近义词：shower, bestow, heap | 反义词：stint, withhold | 词性: 动词
