lauded:/ˈlɔːdɪd/| v. adj.|赞扬，称赞|praised and admired publicly for something.  Derived from the Latin word "laudare," meaning "to praise." It began as the past participle of the verb "laud" but is increasingly used as an adjective.|Her performance was lauded by critics. 她的表演受到了评论家的赞扬。|Synonyms: praised, acclaimed, extolled, celebrated|Antonyms: criticized, condemned, denounced|Verb/Adjective
