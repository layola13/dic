bathos:/ˈbeɪθɒs/ (US) /ˈbæθɒs/ (UK)| n.| 平庸；陈腐；突降法|An abrupt transition from a lofty style to a commonplace or trivial one, often for humorous or satirical effect.  A sudden descent into the trivial or ridiculous after a sublime or dignified presentation.|The epic poem about a lost dog ended in bathos when the dog simply trotted home.|近义词：anticlimax, triteness, sentimentality|反义词：sublimity, loftiness|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek "bathos," meaning "depth." Originally referred to a stylistic descent in rhetoric, later broadened to include any anticlimactic or overly sentimental descent.
