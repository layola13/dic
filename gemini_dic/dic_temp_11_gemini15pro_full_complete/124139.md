mortification: /ˌmɔːrtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ /ˌmɔːrtəfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 羞辱；屈辱；苦行；坏疽 | A feeling of shame or humiliation. The subjection of the passions and appetites by abstinence or self-inflicted pain or discomfort.  The death of one part of the body, while the rest continues to live. |  She blushed with mortification when she realized her mistake. 她意识到自己的错误后羞愧得脸红了。| synonyms: humiliation, shame, embarrassment, chagrin, disgrace | antonyms: pride, honor, glory | noun


mortify: /ˈmɔːrtɪfaɪ/ /ˈmɔːrtəˌfaɪ/ | vt. | 使感羞辱；克制；抑制；使…坏死 | To cause (someone) to feel very embarrassed or ashamed. To subdue (the body or its needs) by abstinence or discipline. Cause (part of the body) to become gangrenous. |  He was mortified to discover his fly was undone. 他发现自己的拉链没拉上，感到非常尴尬。| synonyms: humiliate, shame, embarrass, chagrin, subdue | antonyms:  exalt, honor, glorify | verb
