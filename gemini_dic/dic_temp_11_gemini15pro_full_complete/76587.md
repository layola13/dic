gallinaceae:/ˌɡælɪˈneɪsiiː/ | n. | 鸡形目鸟类 |A family of heavy-bodied ground-feeding birds, including chickens, turkeys, pheasants, and partridges.  |  Example: The peacock is a member of the Gallinaceae family.  孔雀是鸡形目科的一员。 | Example:  Gallinaceae are known for their strong legs and ability to scratch the ground for food. 鸡形目鸟类以其强壮的腿和刨地觅食的能力而闻名。 | Synonym:  Phasianidae (雉科 - a more specific term, often used interchangeably) | Antonym:  (No direct antonym for a taxonomic family) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *gallinaceus* ("of or pertaining to hens"), from *gallina* ("hen") ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰel-/* ("to shine, yellow") from the yellowish color of chicks.
