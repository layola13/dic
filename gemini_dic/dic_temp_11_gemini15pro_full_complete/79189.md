glamorous: /ˈɡlæmərəs/ (US) /ˈɡlæmərəs/ (UK) | adj. | 迷人的，富有魅力的 | Attractive in an exciting and special way |  She led a glamorous life, traveling the world and meeting famous people. 她过着光鲜亮丽的生活，周游世界，结识名人。|  例句：The actress looked glamorous in her shimmering gown.  这位女演员穿着闪闪发光的礼服，看起来光彩照人。 | 近义词：alluring, charming, fascinating, attractive, stylish | 反义词：dull, plain, ordinary, unattractive | 词性: 形容词

glamour: /ˈɡlæmər/ (US) /ˈɡlæmər/ (UK) | n. | 魅力，吸引力 | The attractive or exciting quality that makes certain people or things seem appealing | The city has lost its glamour in recent years.  近年来，这座城市失去了它的魅力。| 例句：She was captivated by the glamour of Hollywood. 她被好莱坞的魅力所吸引。| 近义词：allure, charm, fascination, magic, appeal | 反义词：drabness, dullness, plainness | 词性: 名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From "grammar" influenced by "gramarye" (magic, enchantment) in the sense of casting a spell. Originally referred to a magic spell and later evolved to mean enchanting beauty or allure.
