miscorrelation:/ˌmɪskɒrəˈleɪʃən/ | n. |误相关，虚假关联|A statistical relationship between two variables that appears to be causal but is not, often due to a third, confounding variable.|Example:  There's a miscorrelation between ice cream sales and crime rates – both go up in the summer, but one doesn't cause the other.|Synonyms: spurious correlation, false correlation|Antonyms: true correlation, genuine correlation|Noun
