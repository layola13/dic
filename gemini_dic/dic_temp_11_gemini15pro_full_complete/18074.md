begumming:/bɪˈɡʌmɪŋ/ | n. | 对…册封为夫人；对…阿谀奉承 | The act of making someone a begum (a Muslim woman of high rank, especially in India during the Mogul empire) or behaving obsequiously towards someone. |  He resented the begumming of his boss, feeling it was insincere. 他憎恨对老板的阿谀奉承，觉得这很不真诚。| 近义词: flattering, fawning, obsequiousness | 反义词: disrespect, disregard | 词性: noun

Etymology: From "begum" (a title for a Muslim woman of high rank) + "-ing" (suffix forming nouns from verbs).  "Begum" itself comes from Urdu and Persian "begim", ultimately from Turkish "begüm," meaning "lady," "princess," or "female ruler."
