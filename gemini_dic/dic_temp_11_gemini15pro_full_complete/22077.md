bollard:/ˈbɒlərd/ (UK) /ˈbɑːlərd/ (US)| n. |系船柱；护柱；短柱|A short, thick post on a wharf or ship used to secure mooring lines. Also, a similar post used on land to control traffic or protect pedestrians. |例句：The ship was tied to the bollard. 这艘船被系在系船柱上。

例句：Bollards were installed along the sidewalk to prevent cars from parking there. 人行道上安装了护柱，防止汽车在那里停车。|近义词：post, pillar, stake|反义词: None|词性: Noun

bollard:/ˈbɒlərd/ (UK) /ˈbɑːlərd/ (US)| v. |用系船柱系(船)|To secure (a vessel) to a bollard. |例句：They bollarded the yacht securely before the storm. 他们在暴风雨来临前用系船柱把游艇牢牢地系住。

例句：The captain ordered the crew to bollard the ship. 船长命令船员将船系在系船柱上。|近义词：moor, tie up, secure|反义词: unmoor, cast off|词性: Verb
