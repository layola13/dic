heaper:/ˈhiːpər/| n. |堆积者，堆放者|Someone or something that heaps or piles things up.|The heaper carefully arranged the logs in a neat stack.|近义词：piler, stacker|反义词:scatterer|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "heap" + "-er" (agent suffix).  "Heap" comes from Old English *hēapian, from Proto-Germanic *haupijan, from Proto-Indo-European *keup- (to arch, curve, vault).
