indemnity:/ɪnˈdɛmnɪti/ | n. |赔偿；保障；豁免 |Protection against loss or other financial burden.  Compensation for loss or damage. |例句：The insurance company paid an indemnity to the insured for the damage to their house. 保险公司向被保险人支付了房屋损失的赔偿金。

例句：He demanded indemnity from the publisher for the libelous article. 他要求出版商对诽谤文章进行赔偿。 |近义词：compensation, restitution, redress, reparation |反义词:penalty, fine |词性:noun

indemnify:/ɪnˈdɛmnɪfaɪ/ | v. |赔偿；保障；豁免 |Compensate for loss or damage; secure against anticipated loss. |例句：The company will indemnify its employees against any legal action arising from their work. 公司将保障其员工免受因工作引起的任何法律诉讼的影响。

例句：The contract indemnified the seller against any defects in the product. 合同保障卖方免受产品任何缺陷的影响。|近义词：compensate, reimburse, protect |反义词:penalize |词性:verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Middle English *indempnite,* from Old French *indemnité,* from Latin *indemnitāt-*, from *indemnis* (“unharmed, uninjured”), from *in-* (“not”) + *damnum* (“loss, harm”).
