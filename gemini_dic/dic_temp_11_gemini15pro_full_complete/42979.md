coveter:/ˈkʌvɪtər/| n.| 贪婪者，垂涎者 |Someone who covets something desires it eagerly, often enviously. |例句：He was a coveter of his neighbor's wealth. 他垂涎邻居的财富。

例句：The king was a notorious coveter of land and power.  这位国王是一个臭名昭著的土地和权力贪婪者。|近义词：envier, desirer, yearner |反义词: ascetic,  renunciant |词性:noun
词源:From Late Middle English *covetour, *coveytour, from Anglo-Norman cuveitour, coveitour, from Old French coveiteor, agent noun from coveitier (“to covet”), from Latin cupīditās (“desire, longing, passion; greed, avarice”), from cupidus (“eager, passionate, desirous; greedy, avaricious”), from cupiō (“I desire, long for, wish; I am eager for”).
