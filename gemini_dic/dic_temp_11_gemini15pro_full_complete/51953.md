diachroneity:/ˌdaɪəkrəˈnɪsɪti/ | n. |历时性|The study of language development through time, considering how it changes and evolves.  This is opposed to synchronicity, which studies language at a specific point in time.|例句：The diachroneity of English vocabulary reveals the influence of various languages, such as French and Latin. 英语词汇的历时性揭示了各种语言（例如法语和拉丁语）的影响。

例句：His research focused on the diachroneity of grammatical structures. 他的研究集中在语法结构的历时性上。|近义词：historical linguistics, diachronic analysis|反义词：synchronicity, synchronic analysis|词性：名词

Etymology: From Greek *dia- "through" + *chronos "time" + *-ikos "relating to" + *-ia "state, quality."
