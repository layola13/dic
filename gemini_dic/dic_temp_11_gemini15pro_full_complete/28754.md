carney:/ˈkɑːrni/| n.|嘉年华会的工人；骗子；哄骗；花言巧语|A person employed at a carnival, especially one who entices customers to games or sideshows; a person who deceives or swindles someone.| The carney barked at passersby to try their luck at the ring toss.  嘉年华会的工人向路人叫喊，让他们试试运气玩套圈游戏。|synonyms:  hustler, con artist, swindler, sharper, fraudster|antonyms:  dupe, victim, sucker|noun

carney:/ˈkɑːrni/| v.|哄骗；用花言巧语骗|To entice or deceive someone, typically in order to extract money or other benefits.|He tried to carney me into buying a timeshare. 他试图哄骗我买分时度假房。|synonyms: wheedle, cajole, coax, inveigle, bamboozle|antonyms:  dissuade, deter|verb
