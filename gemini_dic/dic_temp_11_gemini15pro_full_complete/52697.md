digram:/ˈdaɪɡræm/| n. |双字母; 二合字母; 双元音|A digraph is a combination of two letters representing one sound, such as 'ph' in 'phone' or 'ea' in 'head'.|Example 1: The digraph 'ch' can make different sounds, as in 'church' and 'character'.  双字母组合 'ch' 可以发出不同的声音，例如在 'church' 和 'character' 中。

Example 2:  Learning digraphs is an important step in early literacy development. 学习双字母组合是早期读写能力发展的重要一步。|Synonym: ligature, diphthong (for vowels)|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, perhaps single letter/phoneme)|noun
