inadmissibly:/ˌɪnədˈmɪsəbli/| adv.|不可接受地，不能容许地|Not admissible; in a manner that cannot be admitted or allowed.  Derived from "in-" (not) + "admissible" (acceptable). The root "admit" comes from Latin "admittere," meaning "to send to," "to let in."|Her evidence was ruled inadmissibly obtained. 她的证据被裁定为非法获取。|The lawyer argued that the evidence was inadmissibly prejudicial. 律师辩称该证据带有不可接受的偏见。|近义词：unacceptably, improperly, wrongly|反义词:acceptably, properly, legitimately|词性:副词
