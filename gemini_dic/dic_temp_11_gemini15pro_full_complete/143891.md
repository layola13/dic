outposts:/ˈaʊtˌpoʊsts/ | n. | 前哨；偏远地区；边远村镇 | A small military camp or position established away from the main force, especially to protect a border or a line of communication.  A remote or isolated settlement or community. |  例句：The soldiers manned the lonely outpost high in the mountains. 士兵们驻守在高山上的孤零零的前哨。

例句：The small town served as an outpost of civilization in the vast wilderness.  这个小镇在广阔的荒野中充当了文明的前哨。 | 近义词：base, station, fort, garrison, settlement, colony | 反义词：center, headquarters, metropolis | 词性: 名词

outpost:/ˈaʊtˌpoʊst/ | n. | (比喻) 前哨；据点；中心 | A center of activity, influence, or introduction, especially one far removed from the main area of activity. | 例句：The university's research center serves as an outpost of innovation in the field of nanotechnology. 该大学的研究中心是纳米技术领域创新的前哨。

例句：The gallery is an outpost of contemporary art in a traditional city. 这个画廊是传统城市中当代艺术的前哨。| 近义词：beachhead, foothold, center, hub | 反义词：periphery, fringe | 词性: 名词
