metacausation:/ˌmetəˈkɔːˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 元因果; 超因果 |  The concept of metacausation refers to causation of causation, or the process by which causal relationships themselves are caused or influenced. It explores the origins and dynamics of cause-and-effect relationships, often delving into philosophical and metaphysical considerations. | The philosopher explored the complex notion of metacausation, questioning the very foundation of cause and effect. 哲学家探索了元因果关系的复杂概念，质疑因果关系的根基。

Metacausation plays a crucial role in understanding complex systems. 元因果关系在理解复杂系统中起着至关重要的作用。| 近义词: meta-causality, higher-order causation | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but concepts like randomness or acausality could be considered contrasting ideas) | 词性: noun
