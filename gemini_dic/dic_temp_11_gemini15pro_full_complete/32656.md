chitinization:/ˌkaɪtɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən/
|n.|几丁质化；甲壳化|The process by which chitin is deposited in or on a structure, forming a hard, protective covering.|The chitinization of the insect's exoskeleton provides protection against predators. 昆虫外骨骼的几丁质化提供了对捕食者的保护。|sclerotization, hardening|softening|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From chitin + -ization.  Chitin from French chitine, from Greek khitōn "tunic".
