fibrin:/ˈfaɪbrɪn/| n. |纤维蛋白|An insoluble protein formed from fibrinogen during the clotting of blood. It forms a fibrous mesh that impedes the flow of blood.|例句：Fibrin plays a crucial role in wound healing. 纤维蛋白在伤口愈合中起着至关重要的作用。

例句：The blood clot is primarily composed of fibrin. 血凝块主要由纤维蛋白组成。|近义词：coagulant, clot|反义词: None|词性: noun

fibrinogen:/faɪˈbrɪnədʒən/| n. |纤维蛋白原|A soluble protein present in blood plasma, from which fibrin is produced by the action of the enzyme thrombin.|例句：Fibrinogen levels can be measured with a blood test. 纤维蛋白原水平可以通过血液测试来测量。

例句：Fibrinogen is essential for blood coagulation. 纤维蛋白原对血液凝固至关重要。|近义词：clotting factor|反义词: None|词性: noun
