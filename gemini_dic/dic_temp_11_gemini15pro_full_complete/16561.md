barnacle:/ˈbɑːrnəkəl/ (US) /ˈbɑːnəkl/ (UK)| n. |藤壶；难以摆脱的人或事物|A small crustacean that attaches itself to rocks, ships' bottoms, etc.  A person or thing that clings tenaciously.|
The ship's hull was covered in barnacles.  船体上覆盖着藤壶。

The old man had become a barnacle at the club, never leaving.  那个老人成了俱乐部的钉子户，从来不离开。


近义词：limpet, freeloader (when referring to a person), parasite (when referring to a person)
反义词: None (for the crustacean).  Independent (for a person)
词性: noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bernakille*, from Old French *bernaque*, perhaps related to Late Latin *pernacula* ("small shellfish").  The false belief that barnacles hatched into barnacle geese contributed to the word's development.
