microinstruction:/ˌmaɪkroʊɪnˈstrʌkʃən/ | n. |微指令|A single, basic instruction that controls the operation of a computer's central processing unit (CPU) at a very low level, specifying operations like moving data between registers, performing arithmetic or logical operations, or fetching the next instruction.|The CPU executes a series of microinstructions to carry out a single machine-level instruction.|Synonym: micro-order, microcode|Antonym: macroinstruction|Noun
