naggingly:/ˈnæɡɪŋli/| adv.|唠叨地，不断地|In a persistently annoying or bothersome way; constantly reminding or urging someone to do something.|The thought nagged naggingly at the back of her mind.|近义词：persistently, constantly, incessantly, repeatedly|反义词：occasionally, rarely, intermittently|词性:副词
Etymology: From "nag" (verb) + "-ing" (present participle suffix) + "-ly" (adverb suffix).  "Nag" likely comes from Old Norse *nagga "to gnaw, bite," related to the idea of something persistently irritating.
