fretfulness:/ˈfrɛtfʊlnəs/
n.
烦躁不安；焦躁；易怒
the state of being easily annoyed or worried
The baby's fretfulness was due to teething. 婴儿的烦躁是由于长牙引起的。

His fretfulness made it difficult to have a conversation. 他烦躁不安，让人很难和他交谈。
近义词: irritability, peevishness, anxiety, nervousness
反义词: calmness, tranquility, serenity, placidity
词性: 名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From fretful + -ness.  Fretful comes from the Old English word *fretan (to devour, consume), which is related to the Old Norse word freta (to gnaw).  The sense of being agitated or worried likely developed from the idea of being "gnawed at" by anxiety or discomfort.
