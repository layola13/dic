hack-it:/ˈhæk ɪt/ | n. | 编程马拉松，黑客松 | A hackathon, often shortened to hack-it, is an event, usually hosted by a tech company or organization, where programmers get together for a short period to collaborate on software projects. |  We're going to a hack-it this weekend to build a new mobile app.  我们这周末要去参加一个编程马拉松，开发一个新的移动应用程序。 |  The hack-it lasted for 48 hours, and we barely slept.  这个编程马拉松持续了48小时，我们几乎没睡。 | 近义词: hackathon, codefest | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) | 词性: 名词

hack-it:/ˈhæk ɪt/ | v. |  (informal)  to manage to do something successfully, especially with difficulty; to cope with a challenging situation | To succeed in dealing with a difficult problem or situation, often using unconventional or resourceful methods.  | I don't know how I'm going to hack-it with all this work. 我不知道我该如何处理所有这些工作。 |  She managed to hack-it through college despite working full-time. 尽管做全职工作，她还是设法成功地完成了大学学业。 | 近义词: manage, cope, handle, survive | 反义词: fail, succumb | 词性: 动词
