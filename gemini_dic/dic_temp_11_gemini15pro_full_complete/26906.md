calamanco:/ˌkæləˈmæŋkoʊ/| n.|卡拉曼科呢，一种光滑的毛织品|A glossy woolen stuff, usually plain, but sometimes flowered or checkered. It was popular in the 17th and 18th centuries.|She wore a dress of rich calamanco.|Synonyms: worsted, serge, bombazine|Antonyms: None.|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *calamanque*, from Italian *calamanco*, perhaps from the name of the city of Calamanco (present day Süleymanlı) in Turkey.
