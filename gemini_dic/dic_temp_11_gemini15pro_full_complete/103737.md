jostlers:/ˈdʒɒslərz/ | n. | 竞争者；推挤者；争夺者 |  People who jostle, especially those who compete roughly for something.  | The jostlers at the market were elbowing each other to get the best deals. 市场上的竞争者互相推搡，以获得最优惠的价格。 |  competitors, rivals, contenders, scramblers | collaborators, allies | noun

jostle:/ˈdʒɒsəl/ | v. | 推挤；挤开；争夺 | To push or shove roughly in a crowd. |  He jostled his way through the crowd to get to the front. 他在人群中挤来挤去，挤到了前面。 | shove, push, elbow, crowd | yield, give way | verb
