haemocytometer:/ˌhiːməˈsaɪˈtɒmɪtər/ (US) /ˌhiːməˈsaɪˈtɒmɪtə(r)/ (UK)| n. |血细胞计数器|An instrument for visual counting of cells, such as blood cells or algae, suspended in a fluid. It typically consists of a thick glass microscope slide with a rectangular indentation that creates a chamber of precise volume. This chamber is etched with a grid of perpendicular lines. |The scientist used a haemocytometer to count the red blood cells in the sample.|Synonyms: cell counter, counting chamber, hemacytometer|Antonyms: N/A|noun
