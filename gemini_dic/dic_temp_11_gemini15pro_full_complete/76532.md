galiot:/ˈɡæliət/ | n. | 荷兰帆船；小型帆船 | A small, fast, two-masted sailing ship, especially of Dutch or Mediterranean origin, used for trade or warfare. | The galiot sailed swiftly across the calm waters. 这艘荷兰帆船在平静的水面上快速航行。


The pirates attacked the merchant galiot. 海盗袭击了这艘商船。 | Near synonyms: sloop, ketch, brigantine | Antonyms: (No direct antonyms, but consider) galleon, warship | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *galiote*, from Italian *galeotta*, diminutive of *galea* ("galley").  Ultimately from Late Latin *galea*.
