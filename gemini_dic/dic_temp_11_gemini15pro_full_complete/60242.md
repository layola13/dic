effront: /ɪˈfrʌnt/
verb
侮辱；冒犯
To insult or offend someone, especially by behaving boldly or without respect.
He effronted the judge by arguing with his decision. 他因质疑法官的判决而冒犯了法官。
She effronted her parents by staying out all night. 她整晚不回家，冒犯了她的父母。
Synonyms: insult, offend, outrage, affront
Antonyms: respect, honor, revere, esteem
词性: 动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *effronté*, past participle of *effronter* "to make brazen, lose respect for," from *ex-* "out" (see ex-) + *front* "forehead" (from Latin *frons*).  The original notion is of someone losing shame, symbolized by losing color in the forehead (blushing).
