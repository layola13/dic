divisivenesses:/dɪˈvaɪsɪvnɪsɪz/| n. |分裂，分歧|The state or quality of being divisive; a tendency to cause disagreement or hostility between people.|The divisivenesses within the party threatened to tear it apart.  党内的分裂有可能使其走向瓦解。|近义词：discord, disunity, contention, schism|反义词：unity, harmony, concord, agreement|词性:noun

Etymology: From divisive + -ness.  Divisive comes from divide (from Old French *dividier, from Latin dividere "to force apart, cleave, distribute") + -ive (suffix forming adjectives meaning "tending to; having the power or quality of").
