assentator:/əˈsentətər/ | n. | 谄媚者，奉承者 | someone who agrees with everything another person says, especially someone in a position of power, in order to gain their approval | The king was surrounded by assentators who never dared to contradict him. 国王周围都是些阿谀奉承的人，他们从来不敢反驳他。 | 近义词: flatterer, sycophant, yes-man | 反义词: critic, dissenter | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *assentator-, from Latin assentari "to flatter," frequentative of assentire "to agree to, approve of," from ad- "to" + sentire "to feel, think."
