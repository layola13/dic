nondualisms:/ˌnɒnˈdjuːəlɪzəmz/ | n. | 非二元论 |  The philosophical or spiritual concept that rejects the idea of a fundamental division between two opposing categories, such as mind and body, self and other, or subject and object.  It emphasizes the interconnectedness and unity of all things. |  Modern physics seems to support certain nondualisms, blurring the lines between observer and observed.  Nondualism is a core concept in Advaita Vedanta. | synonyms: monism, oneness, unity | antonyms: dualism, dichotomy | noun
