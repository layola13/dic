jabberers:/ˈdʒæbərərz/
n.
喋喋不休的人，饶舌的人
People who talk rapidly and excitedly, often about unimportant things.
The jabberers in the cafe made it difficult to concentrate. 咖啡馆里喋喋不休的人让人很难集中注意力。
The meeting was disrupted by a group of jabberers who wouldn't let anyone else speak.  会议被一群喋喋不休、不让别人说话的人打断了。
近义词：chatterboxes, gossips, prattlers
反义词：quiet people, reticent individuals
词性：名词(plural)


jabberer:/ˈdʒæbərər/
n.
喋喋不休的人，饶舌的人
A person who talks rapidly and excitedly, often about unimportant things.
He's such a jabberer, I can hardly get a word in. 他真是个喋喋不休的人，我几乎插不上话。
She ignored the jabberer and continued with her work. 她无视那个喋喋不休的人，继续工作。
近义词：chatterbox, gossip, prattler
反义词：quiet person, reticent individual
词性：名词(singular)


词源:
From Middle English *jaberen, of imitative origin.
