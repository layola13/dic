anaesthetising (British English) / anesthetizing (American English)
/əˈniːsθətaɪzɪŋ/ (British) /əˈnɛsθətaɪzɪŋ/ (American)| v. |麻醉; 使麻木|The process of administering an anesthetic to induce a loss of sensation or consciousness.  |The vet is anaesthetising the cat before the operation. 兽医正在给猫做手术前的麻醉。|Synonyms: numbing, desensitizing|Antonyms: stimulating, sensitizing|Verb (present participle)

anaesthetic (British English) / anesthetic (American English)
/ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk/ (British) /ˌænɪsˈθɛtɪk/ (American)| n. adj. |麻醉剂; 麻醉的|A substance that causes loss of sensation or consciousness.|The dentist used a local anaesthetic before filling the cavity. 牙医在补牙洞之前使用了局部麻醉剂。|Synonyms: narcotic, soporific|Antonyms: stimulant, invigorant|Noun, Adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Greek ἀναισθησία (anaisthesia)  'lack of sensation,' from ἀν- (an-) 'without' + αἴσθησις (aisthēsis) 'sensation.'
