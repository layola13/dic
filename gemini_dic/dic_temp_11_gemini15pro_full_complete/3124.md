aesculin:/ˈɛskjʊlɪn/| n.|七叶苷;七叶灵|A coumarin glycoside found in the bark of the horse chestnut tree (Aesculus hippocastanum), used as a fluorescent indicator and in the treatment of varicose veins and other circulatory disorders.| Aesculin is used in a microbiology test to aid in the identification of certain bacterial species, particularly Enterococci and Listeria monocytogenes. 七叶苷在微生物学测试中用于帮助鉴定某些细菌种类，特别是肠球菌和单核细胞增生李斯特菌。|Synonym: Esculin|Antonym: None|noun
