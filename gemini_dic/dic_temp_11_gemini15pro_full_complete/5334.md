alloyed:/əˈlɔɪd/ | adj. | 合金的；混合的；减少的；降低的 |  Combined with another metal; reduced in quality or purity. | The ring was made of alloyed gold. 这枚戒指是由合金黄金制成的。|  The joy of victory was alloyed with sadness. 胜利的喜悦中夹杂着悲伤。 |Synonyms: mixed, blended, combined, reduced, diminished, weakened | Antonyms: pure, unmixed, undiluted, enhanced, increased, strengthened | adjective

alloyed:/əˈlɔɪd/ | v. (past tense and past participle alloyed) |  混合；使减低；使降低 | to combine (a metal) with another metal; to reduce in quality or fineness by admixture; to debase. |  Copper is often alloyed with tin to make bronze. 铜通常与锡混合制成青铜。 |  Her happiness was alloyed by a sense of guilt.  她的幸福感被一种负罪感冲淡了。| Synonyms: mix, blend, combine, reduce, diminish, weaken | Antonyms: purify, separate, enhance, increase, strengthen | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *aloiier (Modern French allier), from Vulgar Latin *alligare, from Latin alligare "to bind to, tie up," from ad- "to" + ligare "to bind."  The original meaning referred to the binding or combining of metals.  The figurative sense of reducing quality or purity developed later.
