carbohydrate:/ˌkɑːrboʊˈhaɪdreɪt/ /ˌkɑːbəʊˈhaɪdreɪt/| n. |碳水化合物；糖类|A large group of organic compounds occurring in foods and living tissues and including sugars, starch, and cellulose. They contain hydrogen and oxygen in the same ratio as water (2:1) and typically can be broken down to release energy in the animal body.|例句：Rice is a good source of carbohydrate.  米饭是碳水化合物的一个很好的来源。

例句：Athletes often eat a high-carbohydrate diet. 运动员通常吃高碳水化合物的饮食。|近义词：saccharide|反义词: (No direct antonym, but consider "protein" or "fat" as alternative macronutrients)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *carbohydraté*, from carbo- (“carbon”) + hydrate (“hydrate”). Coined in 1860 by Marcellin Berthelot.
