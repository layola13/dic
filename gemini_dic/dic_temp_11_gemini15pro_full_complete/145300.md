overbreathing:/ˌoʊvərˈbriːðɪŋ/ | n. | 过度换气 | The act of breathing too deeply or too rapidly, resulting in an excessive loss of carbon dioxide from the blood. |  She was overbreathing due to the panic attack. 她因为恐慌症发作而过度换气。| hyperventilation | hypoventilation | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From over- + breathing, the present participle of breathe.  "Over-" signifies "excessive" or "too much". "Breathe" comes from Old English "brēthan," meaning "to smell, exhale."
