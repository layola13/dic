humorism:/ˈhjuːmərɪzəm/ /ˈhjuːmərɪˌzəm/| n. |体液学说；体液病理说|A system of medicine detailing the makeup and workings of the human body, adopted by the ancient Greek and Roman physicians and philosophers, positing that an excess or deficiency of any of four distinct bodily fluids in a person—known as humors or humours—directly influences their temperament and health. |例句：The ancient Greeks believed in humorism, a theory that health was based on a balance of four bodily fluids. 古希腊人相信体液学说，该理论认为健康基于四种体液的平衡。

例句：Humorism was a dominant medical theory for centuries, influencing medical practices across cultures. 体液学说几个世纪以来一直是主要的医学理论，影响着不同文化的医学实践。|近义词：humoralism, humoral pathology|反义词: (无明确的反义词，可以考虑现代医学理论 modern medical theories)|词性:noun
