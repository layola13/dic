outprices:/ˌaʊtˈpraɪsɪz/
verb
定价过高；价格高于…
charges more than a competitor; prices goods or services so high that others cannot compete
例句：Smaller independent bookstores have been outpriced by large chains. 小型独立书店的价格比大型连锁书店高。

例句：Luxury brands often outprice their products to maintain exclusivity. 奢侈品牌通常对其产品定价过高以保持排他性。

近义词：price out, undersell, overcharge
反义词：underprice

词性
v.
