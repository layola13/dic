compensating:/ˈkɒmpənˌseɪtɪŋ/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpənˌseɪtɪŋ/ (US)| adj. |补偿的，赔偿的；弥补的| offsetting or making up for something else, especially something unpleasant or unwelcome |  The company offered her a compensating payment for the extra hours she worked. 公司为她加班提供了补偿金。| synonyms: offsetting, counterbalancing, balancing, making up for,  recouping, remunerating | antonyms: exacerbating, aggravating, worsening | adjective

compensate: /ˈkɒmpənˌseɪt/ (UK) /ˈkɑːmpənˌseɪt/ (US) | v. |补偿，赔偿；弥补| give (someone) something, typically money, in recognition of loss, suffering, or injury; reduce or counteract (something unwelcome or unpleasant) by exerting an opposite force or effect | Nothing can compensate for the loss of a child.  没有什么可以弥补失去孩子的痛苦。| synonyms: recompense, repay, reimburse, indemnify | antonyms:  fail to reward, penalize, punish | verb


Etymology: From late Middle English: from late Latin *compensat- ‘weighed together’, from the verb compensare, from com- ‘together’ + pensare ‘weigh’.
