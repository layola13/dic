half-rotted:/ˌhɑːfˈrɒtɪd/ (US) /ˌhɑːfˈrɒtɪd/ (UK)| adj.|半腐烂的|Partially decayed or decomposed; starting to rot.|The half-rotted log was covered in moss.|Synonyms: decaying, decomposing, putrefying|Antonyms: fresh, new, intact|Adjective

half-rotted:/ˌhɑːfˈrɒtɪd/ (US) /ˌhɑːfˈrɒtɪd/ (UK)| adj.|半腐的|Partially decayed or decomposed; starting to rot.|The half-rotted fruit gave off a foul smell.|Synonyms: decaying, decomposing, putrid|Antonyms: fresh, ripe, unspoiled|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "half" + "rotted," the past participle of "rot."  "Rot" comes from Old Norse "rotna" (to rot).
