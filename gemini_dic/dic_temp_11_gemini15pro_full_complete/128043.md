nereites:/ˈnɪərɪˌaɪtiːz/
n.
nereites (plural nereites)
海底遗迹，蠕虫状痕迹化石，砂岩上的虫迹
A type of fossilized trail found in sandstone, believed to have been made by a worm-like creature.
Nereites are often found in Cambrian rocks.  这些蠕虫状痕迹化石经常出现在寒武纪岩石中。
Nereites are a common type of trace fossil.  蠕虫状痕迹化石是一种常见的遗迹化石。
Synonyms: trace fossil, ichnofossil
Antonyms: body fossil
Noun


Etymology:
From Nereus, a Greek sea god, + -ites.  源自希腊海神涅柔斯的名字Nereus，加上后缀-ites。
