barri:/ˈbæri/| n.|障碍; 屏障; 栅栏; 酒吧; 法官席; |A physical or abstract obstacle that prevents movement or access.  Something that serves as a barrier, such as a fence or railing. A counter or partition, such as in a bar or restaurant, where drinks or food are served. The legal profession or the collective body of barristers.|例句：The police put up a barri to control the crowd. 警察设置了路障来控制人群。
