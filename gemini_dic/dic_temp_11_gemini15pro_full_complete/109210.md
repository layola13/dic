leibniz: /ˈlaɪbnɪts/ | n. | 莱布尼茨 |  German polymath and philosopher Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz (1646-1716), known for his contributions to mathematics, philosophy, and logic.  He developed calculus independently of Newton and is also known for his philosophy of monads. |  Leibniz's work on calculus was groundbreaking. 莱布尼茨在微积分方面的工作是开创性的。 | synonym:  philosopher, mathematician, polymath | antonym: N/A | noun
