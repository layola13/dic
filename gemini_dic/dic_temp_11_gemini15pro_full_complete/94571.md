impound:/ɪmˈpaʊnd/| v. n.|扣押，没收；充公； impound (v.) /ˈɪmpaʊnd/  impound (n.) /ˈɪmpaʊnd/ |To seize and hold in the custody of the law; confine; restrain.  As a noun, a place where stray animals are kept. |The police impounded his car after he was caught driving under the influence.  警察在他被抓到酒后驾车后扣押了他的汽车。|Synonyms: confiscate, seize, sequester, appropriate|Antonyms: release, return, free|verb

impound:/ˈɪmpaʊnd/| n.|扣押，没收；|A place, typically enclosed, where stray animals are kept. |The dog was taken to the impound after it was found wandering the streets. 这只狗在街上游荡后被带到了动物收容所。|Synonyms: pound, holding pen|Antonyms:  N/A (for this noun meaning)|noun
