counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/
| adj. n.|反事实的，反事实的陈述|A counterfactual statement describes a hypothetical situation that did not actually happen. |例句：It's a counterfactual question, but what if the South had won the Civil War?  这是一个反事实的问题，但如果南方赢得了内战会怎么样？|近义词：hypothetical, speculative, conjectural|反义词：factual, real, actual|词性:形容词, 名词


counterfactual:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/
| n.|反事实的陈述|A counterfactual is a statement that describes a hypothetical situation that did not actually happen. |例句：Historians often engage in counterfactuals to explore possible outcomes of past events. 历史学家经常进行反事实的思考来探索过去事件的可能结果。|近义词：hypothetical scenario, alternative history|反义词：fact, reality|词性:名词

ETYMOLOGY->
From counter- + factual.  "Counter-" indicates opposition or contrast. "Factual" refers to something based on facts.  Therefore, "counterfactual" refers to something that is contrary to the facts.
