jerryism: /ˈdʒɛriˌɪzəm/ | n. | 杰瑞主义，指带有欺骗性或不道德的权宜之计；为了达到目的而不择手段的行为 | A jerryism is a makeshift repair or solution, usually of poor quality, done hastily and using whatever materials are at hand. It often implies a degree of deception or unethical expediency to achieve a desired end. | Example:  The politician's promise to lower taxes while increasing spending was a clear jerryism designed to win votes.  这位政客承诺在增加支出的同时降低税收，这显然是为了赢得选票而采取的权宜之计。

Example: Using chewing gum to fix a broken pipe is a classic jerryism. 用口香糖修理破裂的管道是一个典型的权宜之计。 | 近义词: makeshift, stopgap, improvisation, expedient, trickery | 反义词:  sound practice, ethical solution, proper procedure | 词性: noun
