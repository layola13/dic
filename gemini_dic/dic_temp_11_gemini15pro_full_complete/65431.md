euphuist:/ˈjuːfjuɪst/| n.|优浮伊斯特；文辞藻丽者|A euphuist is someone who uses a highly elaborate and artificial style of writing or speaking, characterized by antithesis, alliteration, and frequent use of similes drawn from natural history.|John Lyly, the author of Euphues, is considered the quintessential euphuist. 约翰·礼利，《优浮伊斯》的作者，被认为是典型的优浮伊斯特。|Synonyms: stylist, rhetorician, verbalist|Antonyms: plain speaker, simpleton|noun
