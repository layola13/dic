anthracite-coal:/ˈænθrəsaɪt koʊl/| n.|无烟煤|A hard, compact variety of coal that has a high luster and burns with little flame and smoke, composed almost entirely of carbon.|The blacksmith used anthracite-coal in his forge because it burns so hot.|Synonyms: hard coal, black diamond, stone coal|Antonyms: bituminous coal, lignite|noun
