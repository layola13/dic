一般现在时 (Simple Present):
I haven't seen him today. 我今天没见过他。
They haven't been to that restaurant before. 他们以前没去过那家餐厅。

现在进行时 (Present Continuous):
I haven't been feeling well lately. 我最近感觉不太好。
She hasn't been sleeping well recently. 她最近睡得不好。


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):
I haven't eaten breakfast yet. 我还没吃早餐。
We haven't finished the project yet. 我们还没有完成这个项目。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):
I haven't been working on that project very long. 我做那个项目的时间不长。
He hasn't been studying very hard this semester. 他这学期学习不太努力。

一般过去时 (Simple Past):  "Haven't" is not used in the simple past tense.  Use "didn't have".
I didn't have enough money. 我没有足够的钱。
She didn't have time to go to the party. 她没有时间去参加聚会。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):  "Haven't" is not used in the past continuous tense. Use "wasn't/weren't having".
I wasn't having any fun. 我玩得一点也不开心。
They weren't having any problems. 他们没有任何问题。


过去完成时 (Past Perfect): "Haven't" is not used in the past perfect tense. Use "hadn't had".
I hadn't had breakfast before the meeting. 会议前我还没吃早餐。
They hadn't had a chance to talk. 他们没有机会交谈。


过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous): "Haven't" is not used in the past perfect continuous tense.  Use "hadn't been having".
I hadn't been having any luck finding a job. 我一直没找到工作。
They hadn't been having any problems with their car. 他们的车一直没出什么问题。


一般将来时 (Simple Future): I won't have time tomorrow. 我明天没有时间。
They won't have any trouble finding the place. 他们找到这个地方不会有任何问题。


将来进行时 (Future Continuous): I won't be having dinner at that time. 我那个时候不会吃晚饭。
They won't be having a meeting then. 他们那时不会开会。



将来完成时 (Future Perfect): I won't have finished the project by then. 到那时我将还没有完成这个项目。
They won't have arrived by the time the party starts. 聚会开始的时候他们还没到。



将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous): I won't have been working here for very long by then. 到那时我在这里工作的时间不会很长。
They won't have been living there for long. 到那时他们住在那里不会很长时间。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't been so busy, I would have gone with you. 如果我没有那么忙，我就和你一起去了。
I wish I hadn't said that. 我希望我没有说过那句话。



被动语态: "Haven't" is not typically used in the passive voice in this way. You'd rephrase. For example, instead of  "The work hasn't been done," you'd say "The work has not been done."  


疑问句: Haven't you finished yet? 你还没完成吗？
Haven't they seen the movie? 他们还没看过这部电影吗？



否定句: I haven't seen him today. 我今天没见过他。
They haven't been to that restaurant before. 他们以前没去过那家餐厅。

词源： "Haven't" is a contraction of "have not". "Have" comes from Old English "habban", and "not" comes from Old English "nawiht", meaning "nothing".
