clerestory:/ˈklɪərstɔːri/ (US), /ˈklɪəstɔːri/ (UK)| n.|高侧窗，采光楼|A high section of wall that contains windows above eye level, admitting light to the center of a room.|The clerestory windows flooded the nave with light.|Synonyms: skylight, fanlight, transom|Antonyms: (No direct antonym, perhaps "solid wall")|noun


clerestory:/ˈklɪərstɔːri/ (US), /ˈklɪəstɔːri/ (UK)| adj.|高侧窗的，采光楼的|(Less common usage)  Having a clerestory.|The clerestory design maximized natural light.|Synonyms: (No direct synonym as an adjective)|Antonyms: (No direct antonym)|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *clerestorie*, from Old French *cler estoire* (“clear story”), from *cler* (“clear”) + *estoire* (“story, level of a building”). The "clear" likely refers to the windows themselves, or the light they admit.
