maceraters:/ˈmæsəˌreɪtərz/ | n. |浸渍器，软化器|A macerater is a device or substance used to soften or break down something, typically organic material, by soaking it in liquid.|The hospital uses a macerater to dispose of contaminated waste. 医院使用浸渍器处理污染废物。|The chef used a citrus macerater to tenderize the meat.  厨师使用柑橘浸渍器使肉变嫩。|Synonyms: softener, tenderizer, disintegrator |Antonyms: hardener, solidifier | noun


macerate:/ˈmæsəˌreɪt/ | v. |浸渍，软化|To macerate means to soften or break down something, typically organic material, by soaking it in liquid.|The fruit was macerated in wine overnight. 水果在葡萄酒中浸泡了一夜。|The body had been macerated in the bog for centuries. 这具尸体在沼泽中浸泡了几个世纪。|Synonyms: soak, steep, soften |Antonyms: harden, dry | verb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *maceratus, past participle of macerare (“to make soft, soak”), from Latin macer (“lean, thin”).  The original sense was "to make lean," referring to the practice of soaking food to reduce its bulk.
