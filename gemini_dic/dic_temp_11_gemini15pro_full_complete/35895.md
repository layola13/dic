cocooner:/kəˈkuːnər/| n.|茧居者；居家工作者；闭门不出者|A person who stays inside their home for extended periods, especially due to fear, anxiety, or a preference for a sheltered existence.|例句：He became a cocooner after losing his job, rarely venturing outside.  他失业后成了一个茧居族，很少出门。

例句：With the rise of remote work, more people are embracing the cocooner lifestyle. 随着远程工作的兴起，越来越多的人开始接受茧居的生活方式。|近义词：homebody, recluse, introvert|反义词:extrovert, social butterfly|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From cocoon + -er.  The term gained popularity in the 1980s, referencing the protective covering of a cocoon and reflecting a trend towards staying home and engaging in private leisure activities.
