inscape:/ˈɪnskeɪp/| n. |内在景色；内在景观；(诗歌的)意象|The unique inner nature of a person or object, especially as perceived or represented in art or literature.  It often refers to the distinctive features that make something what it is, especially in poetry.| Gerard Manley Hopkins, a Jesuit priest and poet, coined the term "inscape" to describe the unified complex of characteristics that give each thing its uniqueness and that differentiate it from other things.  他独特的内在景观在诗歌中得到了生动的体现。| synonyms: inner nature, essence, individuality, quiddity, character, peculiarity | antonyms: exteriority, superficiality, outward appearance | noun

inscape:/ˈɪnskeɪp/| n. |(艺术作品的)内在形式；(诗歌的)意象|The particular features of a work of art, especially a poem, that arise from the distinctive characteristics of its subject, as perceived by the artist. |The poem's inscape captured the essence of the natural world. 这首诗的意象捕捉到了自然世界的精髓。| synonyms: form, structure, style, design, composition | antonyms: formlessness, chaos, disorganization | noun
