decouples:/diːˈkʌpəlz/
|v.|解耦，分离，使…独立|To separate or disengage something from something else, often referring to systems or processes.|The new policy decouples the price of oil from the price of gasoline. 新政策使石油价格与汽油价格脱钩。|Synonyms: disconnect, separate, isolate, disengage|Antonyms: connect, couple, link, integrate|Verb

decouple:/diːˈkʌpəl/
|v.|解耦，分离，使…独立|To separate or disengage something from something else, often referring to systems or processes.|The government is trying to decouple economic growth from environmental damage. 政府正试图将经济增长与环境破坏分开。|Synonyms: disconnect, separate, isolate, disengage|Antonyms: connect, couple, link, integrate|Verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From de- + couple,  meaning to uncouple or separate.  The prefix "de-" signifies removal or separation.
