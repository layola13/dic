degenerated:/dɪˈdʒɛnəreɪtɪd/
adjective
退化的；堕落的
Having lost the physical, mental, or moral qualities considered normal and desirable; deteriorated.
例句：
The disease caused his muscles to degenerate.
这种疾病导致他的肌肉退化。

He had degenerated into a thief.
他已经堕落成一个小偷。

近义词: deteriorated, declined, worsened, decayed, atrophied, corrupt, depraved, degraded
反义词: improved, enhanced, developed, evolved, elevated


degenerating:/dɪˈdʒɛnəreɪtɪŋ/
adjective
退化的；堕落的
In the process of losing the physical, mental, or moral qualities considered normal and desirable; deteriorating.
例句：
He was worried about his degenerating eyesight.
他担心自己的视力正在衰退。

The social fabric of the community is degenerating.
这个社区的社会结构正在瓦解。


近义词: deteriorating, declining, worsening, decaying, atrophying
反义词: improving, enhancing, developing, evolving


degenerate:/dɪˈdʒɛnəreɪt/
verb
退化；堕落
To decline or deteriorate physically, mentally, or morally.
例句：
His condition began to degenerate rapidly.
他的病情开始迅速恶化。

The discussion degenerated into a shouting match.
讨论演变成了互相叫喊。


近义词: deteriorate, decline, worsen, decay, atrophy
反义词: improve, enhance, develop, evolve


degenerate:/ˈdɛdʒənərɪt/
noun
堕落的人
An immoral or corrupt person.
例句：
He was considered a degenerate by his peers.
他的同龄人认为他是一个堕落的人。

The book portrays a group of degenerates living in the city.
这本书描绘了一群生活在城市里的堕落的人。


近义词: reprobate, miscreant, scoundrel, villain
反义词: saint, paragon


词性: adjective, verb, noun
词源: From Latin degeneratus, past participle of degenerare (“to depart from one's kind, to deteriorate”), from de- (“from, away from”) + genus (“race, kind, origin”).
