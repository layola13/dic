copula:/ˈkɒpjʊlə/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpjələ/ (US)| n. |系动词；连接词|A copula is a word used to link the subject of a sentence with a predicate (a subject complement).  The most common copula in English is "be" (am, is, are, was, were). Other verbs that can function as copulas include "become," "seem," "appear," "feel," "look," "smell," "taste," "sound," and "remain."|The verb "to be" is a copula. 系动词“to be”是一个连接词。

The bread smells good.  这面包闻起来很香。（“smells”在此处作为系动词）|Synonyms: linking verb, connective|Antonyms: N/A|Noun


copulas:/ˈkɒpjʊləz/ (UK) /ˈkɑːpjələz/ (US)| n. |系动词；连接词（复数）|Plural form of "copula." Refers to multiple linking verbs or connectives.|The copulas in these sentences link the subjects to their predicates. 这些句子中的系动词将主语与其谓语连接起来。

Both "seem" and "appear" are copulas in this context. 在这种情况下，“seem”和“appear”都是系动词。|Synonyms: linking verbs, connectives|Antonyms: N/A|Noun
