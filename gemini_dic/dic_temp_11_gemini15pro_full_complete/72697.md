focalized:/ˈfoʊkəlaɪzd/ | adj. |聚焦的，集中的|Brought into focus; concentrated on a central point or area.  Specifically used in narrative theory to describe a narrative perspective limited to a particular character's perception or knowledge. |The novel is focalized through the eyes of the young protagonist. 这部小说是从年轻主人公的视角聚焦的。|近义词: focused, concentrated, centered, limited |反义词: unfocused, diffused, broad, omniscient |词性: adjective

focalized:/ˈfoʊkəlaɪzd/ | v. |聚焦，集中 (past participle of focalize) |To bring into focus; to concentrate on a central point or area. In narrative theory, to present a story through a particular point of view, limiting the reader's access to information. |The narrative is focalized through the protagonist's limited perspective, creating a sense of suspense.  叙事通过主人公有限的视角聚焦，营造了一种悬念感。|近义词: focus, concentrate, center, limit |反义词: diffuse, broaden, expand |词性: verb (past participle)

词源: From French *focaliser*, from *focal* (focal), from Latin *focus* (hearth, fireplace).  The term gained prominence in narratology through the work of Gérard Genette.
