babbitt:/ˈbæbɪt/| n.|巴比特合金，巴比特轴承，庸俗的市侩|A soft, white, nonferrous alloy of tin, antimony, copper, and usually lead, used as a bearing material.  Figuratively, a narrow-minded, self-satisfied businessman or bourgeois person, after a character in Sinclair Lewis's novel *Babbitt*.|The engine bearings were made of babbitt.|近义词:alloy, philistine, bourgeois|反义词:intellectual, artist|noun


babbitt:/ˈbæbɪt/| v.|使庸俗化|To make or become like Babbitt, the character in Sinclair Lewis's novel, especially to behave in a complacent, materialistic, and conformist way.|His years in corporate America had babbitted him.|近义词:conventionalize, materialize|反义词:individualize, enlighten|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
Named after Isaac Babbitt (1799–1862), American inventor, who patented the alloy in 1839.
