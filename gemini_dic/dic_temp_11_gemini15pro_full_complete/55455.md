distinguisher:/dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃər/| n.|区别者，识别器，鉴别器|A person or thing that distinguishes or differentiates.|The subtle use of color is the artist's distinguisher.|近义词: differentiator, identifier, discriminator|反义词: confuser|词性:名词

distinguish:/dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ/| vt. vi.|区分，辨别，识别|To recognize or note a difference; differentiate.|He can distinguish between different types of wine. 他能分辨不同类型的葡萄酒。|近义词: differentiate, discern, perceive|反义词: confuse, confound|词性:动词

distinguished:/dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt/| adj.|著名的，卓越的，高贵的|Successful, authoritative, and commanding great respect.|A distinguished scientist made a breakthrough in the field. 一位杰出的科学家在该领域取得了突破。|近义词: eminent, renowned, celebrated|反义词: undistinguished, ordinary|词性:形容词

distinguishable:/dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃəbl/| adj.|可区分的，可辨别的|Capable of being distinguished or differentiated.|The two species are barely distinguishable. 这两个物种几乎无法区分。|近义词: discernible, perceptible, noticeable|反义词: indistinguishable, imperceptible|词性:形容词
