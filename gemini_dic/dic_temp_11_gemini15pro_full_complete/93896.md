immortalization:/ɪˌmɔːrtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |不朽化，使永垂不朽|The act of making something or someone immortal, either literally (as in mythology) or figuratively (as in memory or art).|His bravery was immortalized in a song. 他的勇敢事迹在一首歌中被传颂。|近义词：perpetuation, commemoration, remembrance|反义词：oblivion, extinction|词性:名词

immortalize:/ɪˈmɔːrtəlaɪz/ | vt. |使不朽，使永垂不朽|To make something or someone immortal, either literally (as in mythology) or figuratively (as in memory or art).|Shakespeare immortalized the character of Hamlet.  莎士比亚使哈姆雷特这个角色永垂不朽。|近义词：commemorate, celebrate, eternalize|反义词：forget, extinguish|词性:动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *immortalizatus, past participle of immortalizare (“to make immortal”), from Latin immortalis (“immortal”) + -izare (“to make”).  Latin immortalis is from in- (“not”) + mortalis (“mortal”).
