high-maintenance:/ˌhaɪ ˈmeɪntənəns/ | adj. | 高维护的，难伺候的 |  Needing a lot of attention, effort, or money to maintain; demanding.  |  She's very high-maintenance; she expects to be treated like a princess. 她非常难伺候，总希望被像公主一样对待。 | My new car is high-maintenance; it constantly needs repairs. 我的新车维护费用很高，它经常需要修理。 | 近义词: demanding, fussy, needy | 反义词: low-maintenance, easygoing, undemanding | 词性: 形容词

Etymology:
* "high" (adj.): From Old English "hēah," meaning "tall," "lofty," "elevated."
* "maintenance" (n.): From Old French "maintenir" (to maintain), derived from Latin "manu tenere" (to hold in the hand).
