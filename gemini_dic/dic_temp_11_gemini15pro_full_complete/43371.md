craniologist:/ˌkreɪniˈɒlədʒɪst/| n. |颅骨学家，颅相学家|A person who studies craniology, the scientific study of the shape and size of skulls.|The craniologist examined the ancient skull, hoping to learn more about the individual's life and the population they belonged to.  颅骨学家检查了这具古代头骨，希望能更多地了解这个人的生活和他所属的族群。|Near synonyms:  osteologist, anthropologist|Antonym: N/A|noun


craniology:/ˌkreɪniˈɒlədʒi/ |n.|颅骨学，颅相学|The scientific study of the shape and size of skulls, especially in relation to character and intelligence (now largely discredited).|  Craniology was once considered a legitimate science, but its theories have been largely debunked. 颅骨学曾经被认为是一门合法的科学，但它的理论在很大程度上已被揭穿。|Near synonyms: phrenology|Antonym: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From cranio- + -logy.  Cranio- comes from the Greek "kranion" meaning "skull". -logy comes from the Greek "-logia"  meaning "study of".
