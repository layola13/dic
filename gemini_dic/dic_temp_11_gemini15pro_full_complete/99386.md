interdiction:/ˌɪntərˈdɪkʃən/| n.|禁止，阻止，禁令|The act of forbidding or prohibiting something, especially by law or official decree.  The state of being forbidden or prohibited.|The government imposed an interdiction on the sale of firearms. 政府禁止销售枪支。|Synonyms: prohibition, ban, embargo, proscription|Antonyms: permission, allowance, authorization|noun


Etymology->
From Middle English *interdiccioun, borrowed from Old French interdicion, from Latin interdictiō (“prohibition, interdict”), from interdictus, past participle of interdīcere (“to forbid, prohibit, interdict”), from inter (“between, among”) + dīcere (“to say, speak”).
