fichu:/ˈfiːʃuː/| n.|菲休, 三角形围巾, 胸巾|A lace or muslin scarf worn by women around the neck or shoulders, often triangular in shape.|She wore a delicate fichu over her shoulders.|Near synonyms: scarf, shawl, kerchief|Antonyms: (No direct antonym for a clothing item, perhaps 'bare' or 'uncovered' in context)|noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *fichu*, past participle of *ficher* "to fix, stick, drive in," from Vulgar Latin *ficcare*, from Latin *figere* "to fix, fasten." The original sense was of something "stuck" or pinned onto the clothing.
