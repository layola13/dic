blastocyst: /ˈblæstəsɪst/ | n. | 胚泡 |  A thin-walled hollow structure in early embryonic development that contains a cluster of cells called the inner cell mass from which the embryo arises. The outer layer of cells gives rise to the placenta and other supporting tissues needed for fetal development within the uterus. | The blastocyst implants in the uterine wall about a week after fertilization.  胚泡在受精后大约一周植入子宫壁。|  The blastocyst is a crucial stage in embryonic development. 胚泡是胚胎发育的关键阶段。 |Synonyms:  embryo (early stage) | Antonyms:  zygote, oocyte, sperm | noun
