bootlegged:/ˈbuːtˌleɡd/| adj.|走私的，非法制造的，盗版的|Made, copied, or sold illegally.|例句：The police seized a large number of bootlegged DVDs. 警察查获了大量盗版DVD。|例句：He was caught selling bootlegged software. 他因为贩卖盗版软件被抓。|近义词：illicit, illegal, contraband, pirated|反义词：legal, legitimate|词性:形容词

bootleg:/ˈbuːtˌleɡ/| v. n. adj.|走私，非法制造，盗版；走私的货物；私酒；走私的，非法制造的|To make, copy, or sell something illegally.|例句：They were bootlegging alcohol during Prohibition.  他们在禁酒令期间走私酒。|例句：The bootleg copies of the movie were of poor quality. 这部电影的盗版拷贝质量很差。|近义词：smuggle, traffic|反义词：legalize|词性:动词,名词,形容词


词源:  From the practice of concealing illicit goods, especially liquor, in the leg of a high boot.  源于将违禁品，特别是酒，藏在高筒靴靴筒里的做法。
