gilt-edged:/ˌɡɪltˈɛdʒd/| adj.|镀金边的；金边的；优良的；可靠的|Having gold edges, especially of high quality and financially sound/reliable.|例句：Gilt-edged securities are considered a safe investment. 镀金证券被认为是一种安全的投资。

例句：He inherited a gilt-edged portfolio of stocks and bonds. 他继承了一份优良的股票和债券投资组合。|近义词：high-grade, top-quality, blue-chip, secure, safe|反义词：risky, speculative, unreliable, low-grade|词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From the practice of printing bonds and certificates on paper with gilded edges, signifying their high quality and reliability.  "Gilt" refers to a thin layer of gold applied to a surface.  "Edged" refers to the border or edge of something.
