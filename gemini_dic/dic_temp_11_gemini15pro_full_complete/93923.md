immunise:/ˈɪmjʊnaɪz/ | v. | 使免疫，接种疫苗 | Makes someone immune to a disease, typically by vaccination. | I was immunised against tetanus. 我接种了破伤风疫苗。 |  They immunise all the cattle against anthrax. 他们给所有的牛接种了炭疽疫苗。| vaccinate, inoculate | infect | verb

Etymology:
From Late Latin *immunisāre, from Latin immunis (“exempt, free, not liable”)  + -ize.  The Latin immunis is from in- (“not”) + munis (“performing services, liable”).
