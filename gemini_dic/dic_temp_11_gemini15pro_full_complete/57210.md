downcastly: /daʊnˈkæstli/
downcastly| adv. |沮丧地，垂头丧气地|In a manner showing dejection or depression.  Expressing sadness or disappointment through one's appearance or behavior.|She walked downcastly to her room after hearing the bad news.  听到坏消息后，她垂头丧气地走到自己的房间。|Synonyms: dejectedly, sadly, glumly, despondently|Antonyms: cheerfully, happily, joyfully|adverb

downcast: /ˈdaʊnˌkæst/
downcast| adj. |沮丧的，垂头丧气的|Feeling or showing dejection or depression. Sad or disappointed.|He had a downcast expression on his face. 他脸上带着沮丧的表情。|Synonyms: dejected, sad, glum, despondent|Antonyms: cheerful, happy, joyful|adjective

Etymology:
From "down" + "cast".  "Cast" in this sense means to direct or turn (one's eyes or gaze) downwards.
