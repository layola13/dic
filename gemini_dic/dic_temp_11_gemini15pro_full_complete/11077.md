archaeozoic:/ˌɑːrkiːəˈzoʊɪk/ | adj. n. | 太古代的；太古代 |  Relating to or denoting the eon that lasted from about 4,000 million to 2,500 million years ago, when the earliest forms of life arose.  Also used as a noun to refer to this period. | The archaeozoic era saw the formation of the first continents. 太古代时期见证了第一批大陆的形成。|  The archaeozoic rock formations provide valuable insights into Earth's early history. 太古代岩层为了解地球早期历史提供了宝贵的见解。| Synonyms: precambrian, primordial | Antonyms: modern, recent | adjective, noun
