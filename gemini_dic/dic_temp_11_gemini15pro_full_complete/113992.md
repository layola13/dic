magisterially:/ˌmædʒɪˈstɪəriəli/ | adv. | 专横地；威严地；权威地 | In a manner befitting a master; authoritatively. |  He spoke magisterially on the subject, silencing all dissent. 他在这个问题上发表了权威性的讲话，让所有异议都沉默了。| authoritatively, imperiously, domineeringly, dictatorially | humbly, meekly, submissively | adverb

magisterial:/ˌmædʒɪˈstɪəriəl/ | adj. |  专横的；威严的；权威的 | Having or showing great authority; befitting a master. | The judge delivered his verdict in a magisterial tone. 法官以威严的语气宣布了他的判决。| authoritative, commanding, imperious, august | humble, meekly, unassuming | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *magisterialis*, from Latin magister (“master, teacher”).
