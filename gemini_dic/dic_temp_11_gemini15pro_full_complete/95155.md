inarching:/ˈɪnˌɑːrtʃɪŋ/
n.|压条法；靠接；接枝|The process of grafting by approach, where two plants are bent together and a portion of their bark removed where they touch, allowing the exposed surfaces to unite before one is severed from its original stem.  |Inarching was a common practice in Victorian gardens to propagate desirable fruit tree varieties.|近义词：grafting, layering, propagation|反义词: N/A
词性：名词
