oesophagus:/ɪˈsɒfəɡəs/ (UK), /ɪˈsɑːfəɡəs/ (US)| n. |食道|The tube that connects the throat to the stomach.|Food passes from the mouth, down the oesophagus, and into the stomach.|Synonym: gullet|Antonym: None|Noun

oesophaguses:/ɪˈsɒfəɡəsaɪz/ (UK), /ɪˈsɑːfəɡəsaɪz/ (US)| n. |食道（复数）|Plural form of oesophagus, referring to multiple esophagi.|In rare cases, some animals may have two oesophaguses.|Synonym: gullets|Antonym: None|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin oesophagus, from Greek oisophagos, from oisein "to carry" + phagein "to eat".  Literally, "that which carries food".
