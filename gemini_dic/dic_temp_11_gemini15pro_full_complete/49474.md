delusion:/dɪˈluːʒən/ |n.| 错觉，妄想 |A false belief, often of persecution or grandeur, that may accompany psychotic disorders.| He suffers from delusions of grandeur, believing he is a king. 他患有夸大妄想，认为自己是个国王。 |  Example: The patient was plagued by delusions of being followed.  病人被被跟踪的妄想所困扰。|Synonyms: illusion, hallucination, misconception, fallacy | Antonyms: reality, truth, fact | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *dēlusiōn-, from Latin dēlūs-, past participle stem of dēluere "to wash away, cleanse," from de- "down, away" + -luere "to wash."  The sense development is from "washing away" to "deceiving," then to "a false belief."
