oleograph: /ˈəʊliəɡrɑːf/ | n. | 彩色石印画 | A print produced by a process similar to lithography, giving the appearance of an oil painting. |  Example: The oleograph of sunflowers brightened up the kitchen.  厨房里的那幅向日葵彩色石印画使房间增色不少。

Example:  Oleographs were a popular and affordable way to own copies of famous paintings in the 19th century.  在19世纪，彩色石印画是一种流行且价格合理的拥有名画复制品的方式。| 近义词: chromolithograph, print, reproduction | 反义词: original painting | 词性: noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From oleo- (combining form of Latin oleum ‘oil’) + -graph (from Greek graphein ‘to write’).
