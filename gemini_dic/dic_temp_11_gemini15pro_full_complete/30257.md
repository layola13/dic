celsius-scale: /ˈsɛlsiəs skeɪl/ | n. |摄氏温标|A temperature scale in which 0 degrees represents the freezing point of water and 100 degrees represents the boiling point of water at standard atmospheric pressure.  It is also known as the centigrade scale.|Water boils at 100 degrees on the Celsius scale. 水在摄氏100度沸腾。|
The temperature today reached 30 degrees Celsius. 今天气温达到了摄氏30度。|
Synonyms: centigrade scale | Antonyms: Fahrenheit scale | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Named after Anders Celsius (1701-1744), a Swedish astronomer.  The word "scale" comes from the Old Norse "skál" meaning "bowl" or "drinking vessel," later referring to a weighing device and then to a graduated range of values.
