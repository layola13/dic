biased /ˈbaɪəst/ | adj. | 有偏见的；偏向的；偏心的 | Showing an unreasonable preference or dislike based on personal opinions; prejudiced. | The judge was accused of being biased towards the defendant.  法官被指控偏袒被告。|  The newspaper gave a biased account of the incident. 这家报纸对该事件的报道有失偏颇。 |  prejudiced, partial, unfair, one-sided | impartial, unbiased, neutral, objective | adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *biais* "oblique, slantwise," of uncertain origin. The sense of "prejudice" developed in the 16th century.
