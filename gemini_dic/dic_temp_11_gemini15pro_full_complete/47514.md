dearths:/dɜːrθs/
|n.|缺乏，不足，稀少|a scarcity or lack of something.|There is a dearth of affordable housing in the city. 城市里缺乏经济适用房。|近义词：scarcity, lack, shortage, paucity|反义词：abundance, plethora, surplus|词性:名词

词源:
From Middle English *derthe, from Old English *dierþu, from Proto-Germanic *diurþiz (“preciousness, glory, honor, high price, famine”), from Proto-Indo-European *(h₁)dewr- (“to hurt, harm”). Cognate with Old Frisian dierd (“dearth, famine”), Old Saxon diurtha (“dearth, famine”), Old High German tiurida (“dearth, famine”), Old Norse dýrð (“glory, honor”), Gothic 𐌳𐌹𐌿𐍂𐌸𐍃 (diurþs, “dearth, famine”). Related to dear.  The original meaning was "high price, expensiveness" which developed into "scarcity" and then "famine."
