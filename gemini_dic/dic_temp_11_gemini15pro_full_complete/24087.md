bretzel:/ˈbrɛtsəl/ (US), /ˈprɛtsəl/ (UK)| n. |椒盐脆饼干；一种脆饼干|A crisp biscuit, usually in the shape of a knot or loop, sprinkled with salt. It is of German origin.|I love to eat a soft pretzel with mustard. 我喜欢吃加芥末的软椒盐脆饼干。|Synonyms: pretzel, cracker, biscuit|Antonyms: N/A|noun

bretzel:/ˈbrɛtsəl/ (US), /ˈprɛtsəl/ (UK)| n. |椒盐卷饼|A type of glazed, salted bread, usually in the form of a loose knot.|She bought a warm bretzel from a street vendor. 她从一个街头小贩那里买了一个热乎乎的椒盐卷饼。|Synonyms: pretzel, soft pretzel|Antonyms: N/A|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From German *Brezel*, ultimately from Latin *brachium* (“arm”), referring to the shape of folded arms. Related to Old High German *brezitella* (“a kind of pastry”).
