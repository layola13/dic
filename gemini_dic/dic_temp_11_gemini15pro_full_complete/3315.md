affluent:/ˈæfluːənt/ | adj. n. | 富裕的；支流 |  (adj.) Having a great deal of money; wealthy. (n.) A stream that flows into a larger stream or river. | The affluent society of the West contrasts sharply with the poverty of many developing nations.  西方富裕的社会与许多发展中国家的贫困形成鲜明对比。| Synonyms: (adj.) wealthy, prosperous, rich, opulent; (n.) tributary, branch | Antonyms: (adj.) poor, impoverished, destitute; (n.) (No direct antonym for the noun form) | adjective, noun

affluents:/ˈæfluːənts/ | n. | 支流；富人 |  Rivers or streams flowing into a larger river or lake; wealthy or affluent people. | The Mississippi River has many affluents. 密西西比河有很多支流。| Synonyms: tributaries, branches, feeders (for rivers);  the rich, the wealthy (for people)| Antonyms: (For rivers - no direct antonym);  the poor, the impoverished (for people) | noun
