词源：de- (prefix meaning "away from, off, down") + phase (n.)

From French phase, from Latin phasis, from Greek phasis “appearance, phase of the moon,” from phainein “to show, to shine.”  The prefix "de-" adds the sense of removal or reversal, thus "dephase" means to take away or disrupt the phase.
