lyssa:/ˈlɪsə/ /ˈlɪsɑː/| n. |狂犬病；精神错乱；狂怒|A state of frenzy or rage; rabies.  It derives from the Greek word "λύσσα" (lyssa) meaning "rage," "fury," or "madness," which also referred to rabies in animals.  The term is often used in a literary or poetic context.|He was seized by a sudden lyssa and attacked the nearest person. 他突然一阵狂怒，攻击了最近的人。|Synonyms: frenzy, rage, fury, madness, hydrophobia|Antonyms: calmness, tranquility, sanity|noun
