joneses's:/ˈdʒoʊnzɪzɪz/
n.
the Joneses' (possessions, lifestyle, etc.)
琼斯一家人的（财产、生活方式等）
This refers to the possessions, lifestyle, or social status of one's neighbors or acquaintances, often seen as a standard to emulate or surpass.  It's typically used in the phrase "keeping up with the Joneses."
例句：They're always trying to keep up with the Joneses, buying the latest gadgets and cars. 他们总是试图跟上琼斯一家的步伐，购买最新的电子产品和汽车。

例句：Their constant focus on keeping up with the Joneses's lifestyle has left them deep in debt. 他们一直专注于跟上琼斯一家的生活方式，导致他们负债累累。

近义词:neighbors, peers, contemporaries
反义词: (No direct antonym; consider phrases like "being oneself" or "marching to the beat of one's own drum")
词性:noun (possessive plural)
