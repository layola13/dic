kasbahs:/kæzˈbɑːz/ /kɑːzˈbɑːz/
noun
城堡，聚居堡垒，(尤指北非的) 城堡
A citadel, especially one in northern Africa.  Typically, a kasbah was a fortress or fortified citadel within a city, often the largest and strongest building.  It served as the residence of the local ruler or governor and provided protection for the inhabitants during times of conflict.
We visited the ancient kasbahs of Morocco. 我们参观了摩洛哥的古老城堡。
synonyms: citadel, fortress, stronghold, fort
antonyms: village, town (in the sense of being unfortified)
