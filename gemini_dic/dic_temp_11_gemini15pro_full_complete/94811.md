impulsivist:/ɪmˈpʌlsɪvɪst/| n.|冲动主义者；凭冲动行事的人|A person who acts on impulse; someone who is impulsive.|He's an impulsivist, always buying things he doesn't need. 他是个冲动主义者，总是买些他不需要的东西。|She's such an impulsivist; she booked a flight to Hawaii on a whim. 她真是个冲动的人；她一时兴起就订了去夏威夷的机票。|近义词：impulsive person, hothead, daredevil|反义词：cautious person, planner, strategist|词性: noun

impulsive:/ɪmˈpʌlsɪv/| adj.|冲动的；易冲动的|Acting or done without forethought.|He made an impulsive decision to quit his job. 他一时冲动决定辞掉工作。|Her impulsive nature often gets her into trouble. 她冲动的性格经常让她陷入困境。|近义词：rash, impetuous, spontaneous|反义词：cautious, deliberate, planned|词性: adjective

impulsively:/ɪmˈpʌlsɪvli/| adv.|冲动地；易冲动地|In a way that is impulsive.|He impulsively bought a new car without consulting his wife. 他冲动地买了一辆新车，没有和妻子商量。|She impulsively grabbed his hand and ran. 她冲动地抓住他的手跑了。|近义词：rashly, impetuously, spontaneously|反义词：cautiously, deliberately, thoughtfully|词性: adverb

impulsiveness:/ɪmˈpʌlsɪvnəs/| n.|冲动；易冲动|The tendency to act on impulse.|His impulsiveness often leads to regret. 他的冲动常常导致后悔。|Her impulsiveness is both a strength and a weakness. 她的冲动既是优点也是缺点。|近义词：rashness, impetuosity, spontaneity|反义词：caution, deliberation, prudence|词性: noun

词源:  From *impulse* + *-ist*.  *Impulse* comes from Late Latin *impulsus*, past participle of *impellere* "to push, drive on," from *in-* "in" + *pellere* "to push".
