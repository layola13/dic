brigandish:/ˈbrɪɡəndɪʃ/| adj.|土匪似的，强盗般的，凶恶的|Resembling or characteristic of a brigand; like a robber or outlaw, often in appearance or behavior.  Having a wild, unruly, or menacing appearance.|He had a brigandish look about him with his scruffy beard and intense stare. 他蓬乱的胡须和犀利的眼神让他看起来像个土匪。|The brigandish gang terrorized the small village, stealing livestock and demanding tribute.  这群土匪似的团伙恐吓着小村庄，偷牲畜并索要贡品。|近义词：villainous, thuggish, piratical, outlaw, robber-like|反义词：gentle, kind, civilized, law-abiding|词性:adjective

词源:
From brigand + -ish.  Brigand comes from the Italian word "brigante," ultimately from a Vulgar Latin word meaning "to fight."
