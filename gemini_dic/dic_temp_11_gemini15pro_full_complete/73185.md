forbore:/fɔːrˈbɔːr/ | v. |  克制；忍住；避免 | refrained from doing something; politely or patiently restrain an impulse or desire. | She forbore from criticizing his work, even though she thought it was poor. 即使她认为他的工作很差，她也克制住不去批评。 | Synonyms: refrain, abstain, desist, withhold | Antonyms: indulge, yield, submit | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English forberen, from Old English forberan, from for- (prefix meaning "away, against, intensive") + beran (to bear, endure).  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *beranan, from Proto-Indo-European *bher- (to carry, bear).
