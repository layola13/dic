enfranchisement:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzmənt/ /ɪnˈfræntʃɪzmənt/
n.
解放，赋予权利
the act of giving a group of people the right to vote, or a right to vote
例句：The Nineteenth Amendment granted enfranchisement to women.  第十九修正案赋予了妇女选举权。

例句：The enfranchisement of Black Americans was a long and arduous struggle. 美国黑人的解放是一场漫长而艰巨的斗争。
近义词：liberation, empowerment, suffrage
反义词：disenfranchisement, oppression, subjugation
词性：名词

enfranchise:/ɪnˈfræntʃaɪz/ /ɪnˈfræntʃɪz/
vt.
赋予公民权，给予选举权
to give someone the right to vote
例句：Many people were enfranchised for the first time in the 19th century. 许多人在19世纪第一次获得了选举权。

例句：The company decided to enfranchise its employees by giving them stock options. 公司决定通过给予员工股票期权来赋予他们权力。
近义词：empower, liberate
反义词：disenfranchise, suppress
词性：动词
