garnished: /ˈɡɑːrnɪʃt/ | adj. | 装饰的，加装饰的 | Decorated or embellished, often with small additions of food used to enhance the appearance and sometimes the flavor of a dish. | The salad was garnished with edible flowers.  沙拉用可食用的花朵装饰。| synonyms: decorated, adorned, embellished, ornamented | antonyms: plain, undecorated, bare | adjective

garnish: /ˈɡɑːrnɪʃ/ | n. | 装饰物，配菜 | Something used to garnish food, such as a sprig of parsley or a slice of lemon.  | The chef used a lemon twist as a garnish. 厨师用柠檬皮作为装饰。| synonyms: decoration, adornment, embellishment, ornament | antonyms: plainness | noun

garnish: /ˈɡɑːrnɪʃ/ | v. | 装饰，加装饰 | To decorate or embellish (something, especially food).  | Garnish the fish with lemon wedges and fresh herbs.  用柠檬角和新鲜香草装饰鱼。| synonyms: decorate, adorn, embellish, ornament | antonyms: strip, denude | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *garniss-, stem of garnir "to furnish, equip, supply, provide," from Frankish *warnjan, from Proto-Germanic *warnijaną "to provide, equip, warn."  The culinary sense developed in the 15th century.
