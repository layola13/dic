anticonvulsants:/ˌæntiːkənˈvʌlsənts/| n.|抗惊厥药|Medications used to prevent or control seizures.|The doctor prescribed anticonvulsants to manage her epilepsy. 医生给她开了抗惊厥药来控制她的癫痫。|Synonyms: antiepileptics, antiseizure medications|Antonyms: convulsants|Noun

anticonvulsive:/ˌæntiːkənˈvʌlsɪv/| adj.|抗惊厥的|Relating to or having the effect of preventing or controlling seizures.|The anticonvulsive properties of the drug helped to reduce the frequency of her seizures. 这种药物的抗惊厥特性有助于减少她癫痫发作的频率。|Synonyms: antiepileptic, antiseizure|Antonyms: convulsive|Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From "anti-" (against) + "convulsant" (causing convulsions).  "Convulsant" derives from the Latin "convulsus," the past participle of "convellere," meaning "to pull violently," from "com-" (together, with) + "vellere" (to pluck, tear).
