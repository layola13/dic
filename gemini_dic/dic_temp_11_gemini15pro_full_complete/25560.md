bulwarking:/ˈbʊlwərkɪŋ/
n.|防御工事，保障|The act of building or strengthening something that protects or defends.  This includes both physical structures and abstract concepts like laws or policies.|The government invested heavily in bulwarking coastal defenses against rising sea levels. 政府大力投资修建海岸防御工事以抵御海平面上升。|近义词：fortifying, strengthening, protecting, shielding, safeguarding|反义词：weakening, undermining, exposing|词性:noun (present participle of bulwark)

bulwark:/ˈbʊlwərk/
n.|壁垒，保障|A defensive wall or rampart. Something that serves as a strong defense or protection.|Free speech is a bulwark against tyranny. 言论自由是抵御暴政的壁垒。|近义词：rampart, bastion, defense, safeguard, protection|反义词：weakness, vulnerability|词性:noun

bulwark:/ˈbʊlwərk/
v.|加固，防御|To fortify or protect with a bulwark.|The city was bulwarked against invaders by high walls. 这座城市用高墙防御入侵者。|近义词：fortify, strengthen, protect, shield, defend|反义词：weaken, undermine, expose|词性:verb


Etymology:
From Middle Dutch *bolwerc, from bole "tree trunk" + werc "work".  Originally referred to a defensive wall made of tree trunks.
