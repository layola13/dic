depressant:/dɪˈprɛsənt/ | n. | 镇静剂；抑制剂 | A substance that reduces nervous or functional activity. |  The doctor prescribed a mild depressant to help her sleep. 医生给她开了些温和的镇静剂来帮助她睡眠。


depressant:/dɪˈprɛsənt/ | adj. | 抑制的；使人沮丧的 | Causing or tending to cause depression. | The rainy weather had a depressant effect on everyone's mood.  阴雨天气让每个人的心情都很低落。



Etymology: From Late Latin *depressant-, present participle stem of depressare “to press down,” from Latin de- “down” + premere “to press.”


Synonyms: (n.) sedative, tranquilizer, hypnotic, narcotic; (adj.) depressing, disheartening, discouraging


Antonyms: (n.) stimulant, tonic; (adj.) uplifting, exhilarating, encouraging


Word type: Noun, Adjective
