euphuism:/ˈjuːfjuːɪzəm/ | n. | Euphuism; Euphemistic language; Affectedly elegant style | A high-flown, elaborate, and affected style of writing or speaking, characterized by excessive use of antithesis, alliteration, and similes drawn from mythology and nature. | Example 1:  John Lyly's prose is a prime example of euphuism, filled with elaborate comparisons and balanced sentences.  约翰·黎里的散文是尤弗伊斯姆的典型例子，充满了精巧的比喻和对仗句。

Example 2: Her speech was so full of euphuism that it was hard to understand what she was actually trying to say. 她的讲话充满了尤弗伊斯姆，以至于很难理解她到底想说什么。 | Synonym:  affectation, bombast, grandiloquence | Antonym:  plainness, simplicity, naturalness | noun
