covens:/ˈkʌvənz/| n.|女巫集会，巫师团|A group or gathering of witches, typically thirteen in number.|The covens met secretly in the woods at midnight. 女巫集会在午夜秘密地在树林里集会。|synonyms: circles, groups, assemblies|antonyms: individuals, loners|noun

Etymology:
From Middle English *covent, from Old French covent, from Latin conventus (“assembly, meeting”), past participle of convenīre (“to assemble”).  Ultimately from com- (“together”) + venīre (“to come”).
