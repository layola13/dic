anti-inductively:/ˌæntiɪnˈdʌktɪvli/
| adv. | 反归纳地 | In a manner that does not rely on inductive reasoning; proceeding from general principles to specific instances rather than the other way around. |  The philosopher argued anti-inductively, starting with abstract concepts and applying them to concrete examples. 哲学家反归纳地进行论证，从抽象概念开始，然后将其应用于具体例子。 |  deductively, a priori | inductively, empirically | adverb
