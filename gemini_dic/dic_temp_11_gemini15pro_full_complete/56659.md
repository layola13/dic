donut:/ˈdoʊnʌt/ (US), /ˈdəʊnʌt/ (UK)| n.|甜甜圈|A small fried cake of sweetened dough, typically in the shape of a ring or sphere and often topped with icing or frosting.|I bought a dozen donuts from the bakery. 我从面包店买了一打甜甜圈。|The donut was covered in sprinkles. 这个甜甜圈上面撒满了糖屑。|Synonym: doughnut, friedcake|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps healthy snack)|Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
Shortened form of *doughnut*, which is from *dough nut*, literally "nut of dough," referring to the original shape of these fried cakes.  The "nut" likely refers to a small ball or lump of dough, placed in the center to ensure even cooking. The spelling "donut" became popularized in the 20th century, particularly in the United States.
