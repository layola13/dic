handcuff:/ˈhændkʌf/ (US) /ˈhændkʌf/ (UK)| n. vt.|手铐；上手铐；束缚|A metal fastening, usually consisting of two circular or oval rings connected by a short chain, for locking around a person's wrists.  To restrain someone using handcuffs.|The police officer put handcuffs on the suspect. 警察给嫌疑犯戴上了手铐。|They handcuffed the prisoner and led him away. 他们给犯人戴上手铐，把他带走了。|近义词：manacle, shackle, restrain|反义词:release, free|词性:名词, 动词

handcuffs:/ˈhændkʌfs/ (US) /ˈhændkʌfs/ (UK)| n.|手铐|A pair of handcuffs.|The handcuffs were tight around his wrists. 手铐紧紧地铐在他的手腕上。|The officer removed the handcuffs after questioning.  警官讯问后取下手铐。|近义词：manacles, shackles|反义词:N/A|词性:名词


词源:  From hand + cuff (originally a glove or mitten).  The original sense was of a mitten or glove attached to a belt or girdle.  The current sense developed in the 17th century.
