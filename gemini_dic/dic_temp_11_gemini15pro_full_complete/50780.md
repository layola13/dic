descanter:/dɪˈkæntər/ | n. |  醒酒器；倾注器 | A decorative glass bottle used for serving wine or other alcoholic drinks, typically after they have been decanted. |  The waiter brought the wine in a beautiful crystal decanter. 服务员用一个漂亮的透明水晶醒酒器把酒端了上来。| synonym: carafe, flagon | antonym:  N/A | noun


descanter:/dɪˈkæntər/ | vt. | 轻轻倒出；倾注 | To pour (liquid, typically wine or port) from one container into another, usually to separate sediment or aerate the liquid. | He carefully decanted the wine into a crystal decanter. 他小心翼翼地把酒倒进一个透明水晶醒酒器里。| synonym: pour, transfer, siphon | antonym: N/A | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *décanter*, from *dé-* (prefix meaning "off," "away") + *canter* ("to sing," related to Latin *cantare*). The original sense was to pour wine gently so as not to disturb the sediment.
