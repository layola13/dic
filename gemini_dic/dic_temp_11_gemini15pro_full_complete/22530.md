boondogglers:/ˈbuːndɑːɡlər/| n. | 闲荡的人，游手好闲的人，浪费公款的人|A person who engages in wasteful or unnecessary work, often at public expense, typically as a way to appear busy or justify a position. |The project was overrun with boondogglers who spent more time gossiping than working.  这个项目充斥着游手好闲的人，他们花在闲聊上的时间比工作上的时间还多。|Synonyms: idler, loafer, shirker, time-waster|Antonyms: worker, hard worker, diligent person|Noun

Etymology:  From "boondoggle," referring to a braided leathercraft project, often made by Boy Scouts.  The term gained its pejorative meaning during the Great Depression, when it was used to describe make-work projects funded by the government.  词源来自“boondoggle”，指的是一种编织皮革工艺品，通常由童子军制作。  这个词的贬义含义是在大萧条时期获得的，当时它被用来描述政府资助的“制造工作”项目。
