nonmodernness:/ˌnɒnˈmɒdərnɪs/ |n.| 非现代性 |The state or quality of not being modern; lack of modern characteristics or attributes.  It encompasses a broad range of cultural, social, and philosophical perspectives that critique or reject aspects of modernity.|  His art explored themes of nonmodernness, drawing inspiration from pre-industrial societies. 他的艺术探索了非现代性的主题，从前工业社会汲取灵感。| The architect’s designs embraced nonmodernness, incorporating traditional building techniques. 这位建筑师的设计包含了非现代性，融合了传统的建筑技术。 | synonyms:  antimodernism, premodernity, traditionalism | antonyms: modernity, modernism, contemporaneity | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
non- (prefix):  from Latin *non, meaning "not"
modern (adjective): from Old French moderne, from Late Latin modernus,  meaning "recent, of the present time," from Latin modo, meaning "just now"
-ness (suffix):  forms nouns from adjectives, indicating a state, quality, or condition
