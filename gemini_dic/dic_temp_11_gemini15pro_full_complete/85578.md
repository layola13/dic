hating:/ˈheɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 憎恨，厌恶 |  The act of feeling intense dislike or aversion towards someone or something. |  She couldn't hide her hating expression when she saw him. 她看到他时无法掩饰厌恶的表情。| synonyms: detesting, loathing, abhorring, despising | antonyms: loving, adoring, cherishing | Verb (present participle)

hating:/ˈheɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 充满仇恨的，厌恶的 | Feeling or showing intense dislike or aversion. | He gave her a hating look. 他带着憎恨的眼神看着她。 | synonyms: hateful, spiteful, malicious | antonyms: loving, affectionate, kind | Adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hatynge*, from Old English *hatian* (“to hate”), from Proto-Germanic *hatōną* (“to hate”), from Proto-Indo-European *ḱad-/*ked- (“to grieve, be sad, angry”).
