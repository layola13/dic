fairy-tale:/ˈfeəri teɪl/ (US), /ˈfɛːri teɪl/ (UK)| n. adj.|童话故事；童话般的；虚构的|A children's story about magical and imaginary beings and lands.  Can also be used as an adjective to describe something unrealistic or magical.|例句：She used to read fairy-tales to her children every night. 她过去每晚都给孩子们读童话故事。|近义词：fantasy, fable, legend, myth|反义词：reality, truth|词性:名词，形容词

fairy-tale:/ˈfeəri teɪl/ (US), /ˈfɛːri teɪl/ (UK)| adj.|童话般的；虚构的|Resembling a fairy tale in being magical, unrealistic, or wonderful.|例句：Their wedding was a fairy-tale romance. 他们的婚礼是一场童话般的浪漫故事。|近义词：magical, enchanting, unreal, fictional|反义词：realistic, mundane, ordinary|词性:形容词

ETYMOLOGY->
From *fairy* + *tale*.  *Fairy* comes from Middle English *faierie*, from Old French *faerie* (enchantment, fairyland). *Tale* comes from Old English *talu* (speech, story).
