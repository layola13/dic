engrail:/ɪnˈɡreɪl/| v. n.|（纹章学）刻成齿状，齿形，锯齿形|To furnish with engrailed edges; to indent or make notches in, as in the edge of a border in heraldry.  In heraldry, an engrailed line is a line of small concave curves based on semicircles. It is the opposite of invected.|The shield was engrailed with a silver border.  盾牌刻有银色的齿状边缘。|近义词：indent, notch, crenate|反义词：invected|词性: 动词，名词

engrail:/ɪnˈɡreɪl/| n.|齿形，锯齿形|A series of notches in the edge of something, especially in heraldry. |The engrail on the shield was a mark of distinction. 盾牌上的锯齿形是区别的标志。|近义词：indentation, notch, crenation|反义词： invection|词性:名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *engreler, from en- + grele "gravel," from Latin grānum "grain."  The idea is of an edge notched like hailstones.
