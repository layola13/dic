catastrophe:/kəˈtæstrəfi/ /kəˈtɑːstrəfi/| n. |灾难，灾祸|A sudden event that causes very great trouble or destruction.|The earthquake was a catastrophe for the region.  这场地震对该地区来说是一场灾难。|Synonyms: disaster, calamity, cataclysm|Antonyms: blessing, miracle, godsend|noun

catastrophes:/kəˈtæstrəfiz/ /kəˈtɑːstrəfiz/| n. |灾难，灾祸（复数）|Plural form of catastrophe.|The region has suffered a series of catastrophes in recent years.  该地区近年来遭受了一系列灾难。|Synonyms: disasters, calamities, cataclysms|Antonyms: blessings, miracles, godsends|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin catastropha, from Ancient Greek καταστροφή (katastrophḗ, “overturning, sudden turn, a down-turning, overthrow, ruin”), from καταστρέφω (katastréphō, “I overturn”), from κατά (katá, “down”) + στρέφω (stréphō, “I turn”).
