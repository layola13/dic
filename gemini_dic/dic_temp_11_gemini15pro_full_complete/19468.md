bid-fair:/bɪd ˈfeər/ | v. |  似乎；看来好像；有希望 | To seem likely; to give a reasonable prospect. |  The team bid fair to win the championship.  这个队看来很有希望赢得冠军。 | synonyms: promise, appear, seem, look likely | antonyms:  have no chance, be unlikely, seem improbable | verb

bid-fair:/bɪd ˈfeər/ | adj. |  有希望的 | Showing promise of success. |  It was a bid-fair attempt, even if it ultimately failed. 这是一次有希望的尝试，即使最终失败了。| synonyms: promising, hopeful, auspicious | antonyms: unpromising, hopeless, inauspicious | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *bidden faire*, from Old English *bēodan* (to offer, command) + *fæger* (fair, beautiful). The original sense was "to offer something fair," later evolving to "to seem likely."
