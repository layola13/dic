knocked-out:/ˌnɒkt ˈaʊt/ (US) /ˌnɑːkt ˈaʊt/ (UK)| adj.| 击倒的，昏迷的，筋疲力尽的|Rendered unconscious by a blow, typically to the head; extremely tired or exhausted; (of a competition) having been eliminated.|The boxer was knocked out in the third round.  拳击手在第三回合被击倒了。|synonyms: unconscious, insensible, stunned, dazed,  exhausted, worn out, beat, drained|antonyms: conscious, awake, alert, energetic, invigorated|adjective

knocked-out:/ˌnɒkt ˈaʊt/ (US) /ˌnɑːkt ˈaʊt/ (UK)| n.| 淘汰赛，击倒|A knockout competition or a blow that renders someone unconscious.|The tournament was a straight knockout. 锦标赛是直接淘汰赛。|synonyms: knockout competition, elimination tournament|antonyms: round-robin tournament|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb phrase "knock out,"  "knock" (Old English *cnoccian "to strike, knock") + "out" (Old English ūt "outward, outside"). The adjectival and noun senses developed from the literal meaning of being knocked out of a fight or competition.
