credit:/ˈkrɛdɪt/ (US) /ˈkrɛdɪt/ (UK)| n. vt.|信用，信誉，贷款，学分，荣誉，功劳|A record of how well you have paid your bills in the past, used by lenders to decide whether to lend you money. Also, the money that is lent to you.  Recognition or praise given to someone for achieving something.|She has excellent credit. 她信用极佳。|synonyms: trust, belief, faith, confidence, loan, advance, acclaim, praise, commendation, recognition, acknowledgment, kudos, merit, honor, glory|antonyms: discredit, distrust, disbelief, doubt, debt|noun, verb

credit:/ˈkrɛdɪt/ (US) /ˈkrɛdɪt/ (UK)| vt.|把…记入贷方，相信，归功于|To add money to an account. To believe something to be true. To attribute something to someone.|He credited her account with $100. 他把100美元记入她的账户。|synonyms: believe, accept, trust, have faith in, ascribe, attribute, impute|antonyms: debit, disbelieve, distrust, doubt|verb
