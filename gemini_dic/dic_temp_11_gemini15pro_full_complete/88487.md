historicisms: /hɪˌstɔːrɪˈsɪzəmz/ | n. | 历史主义，历史相对主义; 过时的词语 |  The interpretation of historical events in terms of their context, emphasizing the unique characteristics of each period, and avoiding imposing present-day values. Also refers to words or expressions that are no longer current but evoke a particular historical period. |  The author's use of historicisms like "thee" and "thou" added authenticity to the novel. 作者在小说中使用“thee”和“thou”等历史词语，增加了小说的真实性。| 近义词: anachronisms, archaisms | 反义词: modernisms, contemporary language | 词性: noun

Etymology:  From German *Historismus*, from Historie "history" + -ismus "-ism."  Ultimately from Ancient Greek ἱστορία (historía, “inquiry; knowledge acquired by investigation; history”).
