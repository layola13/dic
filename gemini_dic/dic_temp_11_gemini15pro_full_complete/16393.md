barble:/ˈbɑːrbəl/ (US), /ˈbɑːbl/ (UK)| n.|倒刺，芒刺，触须|A slender, whisker-like sensory organ near the mouth of some fish, such as the catfish.|The catfish uses its barbels to locate food in murky water.  鲶鱼用它的触须在浑浊的水中寻找食物。|Synonyms: whisker, feeler, tentacle|Antonyms: N/A|noun

barble:/ˈbɑːrbəl/ (US), /ˈbɑːbl/ (UK)| v.|有倒钩；带刺地说|To speak in a critical or harsh manner.|He barbled something about the unfairness of the situation. 他喋喋不休地抱怨着情况的不公平。|Synonyms: criticize, carp, cavil|Antonyms: praise, commend|verb
