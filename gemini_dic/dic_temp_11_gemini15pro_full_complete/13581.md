attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD): /əˈtɛnʃən ˈdɛfɪsɪt/(hʌɪpərækˈtɪvɪti) dɪsˈɔːrdər/ | n. | 注意力缺陷/多动障碍 | A neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity.  It often begins in childhood and can persist into adulthood. | Example: Children with ADHD often have difficulty focusing in school.  患有ADHD的儿童通常难以集中注意力学习。

Example:  He was diagnosed with attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder. 他被诊断出患有注意力缺陷/多动障碍。 | Synonym: ADD, hyperkinetic disorder | Antonym: neurotypicality | Noun

attention-deficit disorder (ADD) (outdated term): /əˈtɛnʃən ˈdɛfɪsɪt dɪsˈɔːrdər/ | n. | 注意力缺陷障碍（过时术语）| An outdated term for attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder, specifically referring to presentations without significant hyperactivity. | Example:  In the past, ADD was used to describe children who were inattentive but not hyperactive. 过去，ADD被用来描述注意力不集中但不有多动的孩子.

Example: The term ADD is now largely replaced by ADHD.  现在ADD这个术语很大程度上被ADHD取代了。| Synonym: ADHD (now preferred) | Antonym: neurotypicality | Noun


**Etymology:**

* **attention:** From Late Latin *attentionem* (nominative *attentio*), meaning "a stretching out, application of the mind," from Latin *attendere* "to stretch toward, give heed to."
* **deficit:** From Latin *deficere* "to fail, desert, be wanting," from *de-* "down, away" + *facere* "to do, make."
* **hyperactivity:** From *hyper-* "over, excessive" + *activity*.
* **disorder:** From Old French *desordre* "confusion, disorder," from *des-* "dis-" + *ordre* "order."
