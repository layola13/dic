overscrupulously:/ˌoʊvərˈskruːpjələsli/| adv.| 过分谨慎地，过分小心地 |  In an excessively scrupulous manner; with extreme attention to detail and correctness, often to an unnecessary degree. | He overscrupulously checked every item on the list, taking far longer than necessary. 他过分谨慎地检查清单上的每一项，花费了比必要长得多的时间。|Synonyms: meticulously, painstakingly, excessively carefully, fastidiously|Antonyms: carelessly, negligently, recklessly| Word Type: Adverb

Etymology:
From "over-" (Old English *ofer, from Proto-Germanic *uberi, meaning "above, beyond") + "scrupulous" (from Latin scrūpulus, meaning "small sharp stone," hence "anxiety, uneasiness," via the notion of having a stone in one's shoe).
