logicalness:/ˈlɒdʒɪkəlnəs/ (UK) /ˈlɑːdʒɪkəlnɪs/ (US)| n. |逻辑性，合理性|The quality of being logical or reasonable.  The extent to which something makes sense.|The logicalness of her argument convinced the jury. 她的论证的逻辑性说服了陪审团。|近义词：reasonableness, rationality, validity, soundness|反义词：illogicality, irrationality, absurdity|词性:名词

Etymology:
From logical + -ness.  "Logical" comes from Late Latin *logicālis, from Latin logica "logic,"  from Greek logikē (tekhnē) "(art) of reason," from logos "word, reason."  "-ness" is a native English suffix forming nouns from adjectives, expressing state or quality.
