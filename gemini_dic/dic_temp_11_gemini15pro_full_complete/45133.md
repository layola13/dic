cudden:/ˈkʌdn/ | n. | 牛胃，反刍食物 | The cow regurgitated its cudden. 奶牛反刍了它的食物。|  Etymology: From Middle English *cudden*, from Old English *cudu*, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *kuđō* (“chewed thing”). Cognate with Dutch *kudde* (“flock, herd”), German *Kutte* (“cowl, frock”). The original sense was "chewed food," specifically that which is regurgitated by ruminants. | Synonyms: cud, chew, quid | Antonyms: N/A | noun
