annunciate:/əˈnʌnʃieɪt/ (US) /əˈnʌnsieɪt/ (UK)| v. |清晰地说；宣布；预示|To pronounce clearly; to proclaim or announce something officially; to foreshadow or indicate something|She carefully enunciated each word of the poem. 她仔细地念出了诗歌的每一个字。

The government announced its new economic policy. 政府宣布了新的经济政策。|近义词：articulate, pronounce, declare, proclaim|反义词: mumble, mutter|词性:动词

annunciation:/əˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃn/ (US) /əˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən/ (UK)| n. |宣布；通告；预示|The act of announcing something; a formal public announcement; the Annunciation (in Christian belief, the announcement by the angel Gabriel to Mary that she would be the mother of Jesus Christ)|The annunciation of the merger surprised everyone. 合并的宣布让每个人都感到惊讶。

The feast of the Annunciation is celebrated on March 25th. 圣母领报节在3月25日庆祝。|近义词：announcement, declaration, proclamation|反义词: suppression, concealment|词性:名词
