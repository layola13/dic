had-rather:/həd ˈrɑːðər/ /hæd ˈræðər/ |v. |宁愿，宁可| Expresses a preference for one thing over another. It indicates a slightly stronger preference than "would rather". |I had rather stay home tonight than go out. 我今晚宁愿待在家里也不愿出去。| 近义词: would rather, would prefer, would sooner | 反义词: would rather not, would prefer not to | 词性: verb phrase (acts as a modal verb)


USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):
I had rather you told the truth.  我宁愿你说实话。
She had rather walk than take a bus. 她宁愿走路也不愿坐公共汽车。

Not typically used in present continuous.

虚拟语气:
I had rather you didn't tell anyone. 我宁愿你不要告诉任何人。
He had rather she hadn't seen that movie.  他宁愿她没看过那部电影。


被动语态:
Not typically used in the passive voice.


疑问句:
Had you rather stay home? 你宁愿待在家里吗?
Had they rather we postponed the meeting? 他们宁愿我们推迟会议吗?


否定句:
I had rather not go. 我宁愿不去。
She had rather not say anything. 她宁愿什么也不说.


PHRASE->
had rather not = 宁愿不
had rather...than... = 宁愿...也不愿...
I had rather = 我宁愿
He had rather = 他宁愿
She had rather = 她宁愿

词源: "Had" in this context is a subjunctive form, and "rather" originally meant "earlier" or "sooner". The phrase evolved to express preference.
