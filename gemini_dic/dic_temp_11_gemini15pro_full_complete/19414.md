bicho:/ˈbiːtʃoʊ/ (Brazilian Portuguese), /ˈbitʃo/ (European Portuguese)| n. |虫子；动物；家伙；(俚语)麻烦；(俚语)奇怪的东西|A general term in Portuguese for "bug," "critter," "animal," or "creature." It can also be used colloquially to refer to a person, often in a playful or affectionate way, or to describe something strange or problematic.  The word derives from the Latin word *bestius*, meaning "beast."|Example 1:  Vi um bicho estranho no jardim. (I saw a strange bug in the garden.)
Example 2:  Ele é um bicho legal. (He's a cool guy.)|Synonym:  animal, criatura (creature), inseto (insect) (depending on context)|Antonym:  (No direct antonym, depends on specific meaning used) |Noun
