lackeyism:/ˈlækiɪzəm/| n. | 奴性，谄媚，卑躬屈膝 | Excessive obsequiousness shown to those in power; servile flattery. |  He displayed a nauseating lackeyism towards the CEO. 他对CEO表现出令人作呕的奴性。|  The politician was surrounded by aides who demonstrated the worst kind of lackeyism. 这位政客身边围绕着一群表现出最恶劣奴性的助手。| 近义词：servility, sycophancy, fawning, obsequiousness, toadyism | 反义词：independence, assertiveness, self-respect, defiance | 词性: 名词

Etymology: From lackey + -ism. Lackey (from French laquais) originally referred to a footman or servant, particularly a liveried male servant.  The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, belief, or practice. Thus, lackeyism refers to the practice of behaving like a lackey.
