Aegean: /ɪˈdʒiːən/ | adj. n. | 爱琴海的；爱琴海 | Relating to the Aegean Sea, an arm of the Mediterranean Sea located between Greece and Turkey.  It can also refer to the Aegean civilization that flourished in the Bronze Age. | The Aegean Sea is known for its beautiful islands.  爱琴海以其美丽的岛屿而闻名。| The Aegean civilization was a major maritime culture. 爱琴海文明是一个重要的海洋文化。 |Mediterranean, Greek | None | adjective, noun

Etymology->
From Ancient Greek *Αἰγαῖον (Aigaîon), of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to the Greek word αἴξ (aix, “goat”), referring to the numerous islands "hopping" like goats, or to Aegeus, a mythical king of Athens who drowned himself in the sea.
