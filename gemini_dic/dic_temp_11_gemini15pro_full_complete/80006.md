go-one-better:/ɡəʊ wʌn ˈbɛtə(r)/| v.| 更胜一筹; 技高一筹; 略胜一筹|To do something better than someone else has already done. |Example 1:  He always tries to go one better than his brother. 他总是试图比他哥哥做得更好。

Example 2: She went one better than her rival by scoring the winning goal. 她打进了制胜一球，比她的对手更胜一筹。|Synonyms: outdo, surpass, exceed, top, trump|Antonyms:  fall short, lose, fail|verb


ETYMOLOGY->

From the card game of bridge, where "going one better" means to bid one level higher than the previous bid. This meaning was then extended to general usage in the early 20th century.
