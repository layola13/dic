ecclesiasticus: /ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəs/ | n. | 传道经;  智慧之子书; | A deuterocanonical book of the Old Testament, also known as the Wisdom of Sirach or the Wisdom of Jesus son of Sirach.  It is considered canonical by Catholics and Orthodox Christians, but not by most Protestants. |  The book of Ecclesiasticus offers much wisdom on living a good life.  传道经提供了许多关于过好生活的智慧。| synonyms: Sirach, Wisdom of Sirach, Wisdom of Ben Sira, Book of Ben Sira | antonyms: N/A | noun


ecclesiasticus: /ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkəs/ | adj. | 教会的; 教士的; | Relating to or characteristic of the church or its clergy.  This meaning is less common than the noun form.  | He had an ecclesiasticus air about him. 他身上有一种教会人士的气质。 | synonyms: clerical, churchly, ecclesiastical | antonyms: secular, lay | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *Ecclesiasticus (liber) "(book) of the Church," from Latin ecclesiasticus "ecclesiastical," from Greek ekklēsiastikos, from ekklēsia "church assembly."
