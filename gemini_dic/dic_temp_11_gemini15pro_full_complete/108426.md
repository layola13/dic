lawsonia: /lɔːˈsoʊniə/ | n. | 指甲花；散沫花 | A tropical shrub or small tree cultivated for its leaves, which yield a reddish-orange dye used for coloring hair, skin, and fingernails.  |  Lawsonia is commonly used in traditional body art.  指甲花通常用于传统的彩绘艺术。| Synonym: henna | Antonym: N/A | Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Modern Latin, named after Isaac Lawson, an 18th-century Scottish physician.
