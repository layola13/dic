dichotomize:/daɪˈkɑːtəmaɪz/| v. |二分，分成两部分|To divide something into two parts or categories, often seen as mutually exclusive or opposite.|Researchers often dichotomize variables like income into high and low for easier analysis. 研究人员通常将收入等变量二分为高和低，以便于分析。|The survey dichotomized respondents into "satisfied" and "dissatisfied."  这项调查将受访者分为“满意”和“不满意”两类。|classify, categorize, divide, split|unite, combine, integrate|verb
dichotomy:/daɪˈkɑːtəmi/| n. |二分法，二元对立|A division or classification into two parts or categories, often seen as mutually exclusive or opposite.|There is often a false dichotomy between work and leisure. 工作和休闲之间通常存在错误的二分法。|The report highlighted the dichotomy between urban and rural development.  报告强调了城乡发展之间的二元对立。|division, separation, contrast, polarity|unity, harmony, integration|noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek  *δικhotomos* (dikhotomos, “cut in two”) < *δίχα* (dikhā, “in two, asunder”) + *τέμνω* (témnō, “I cut”).
