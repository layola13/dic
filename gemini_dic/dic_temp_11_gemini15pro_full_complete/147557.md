overpensively:/ˌoʊvərˈpɛnsɪvli/| adv.|沉思地，忧郁地|In a manner characterized by deep or serious thought, often tinged with melancholy or sadness.  It describes the way someone acts or behaves when they are lost in thought, usually about something serious or sad.|She stared overpensively out the window, lost in her thoughts about the future.|近义词：thoughtfully, meditatively, contemplatively, wistfully|反义词：frivolously, carelessly, lightheartedly|词性:副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From over + pensive + -ly.  "Over-" intensifies the meaning of "pensive," which comes from Old French "pensif" and ultimately from Latin "pensare" (to think, weigh, consider).
