glomerate:/ˈɡlɒmərət (UK), ˈɡlɑːmərət (US)/
n.|肾小球；聚集体；砾岩|A rounded mass formed of fragments of other rocks, cemented together. A clustered mass.|The doctor examined the glomerate under a microscope. 医生在显微镜下检查了肾小球。|Synonyms: cluster, aggregation, conglomerate|Antonyms: dispersion, separation|noun
glomerate:/ˈɡlɒmərət (UK), ˈɡlɑːmərət (US)/
v.|使成团，使聚集|To gather into a rounded mass or ball; to collect into a cluster.|The dust particles glomerated into larger clumps. 灰尘颗粒聚集成了更大的团块。|Synonyms: aggregate, coalesce, amass|Antonyms: disperse, scatter|verb
glomerate:/ˈɡlɒmərət (UK), ˈɡlɑːmərət (US)/
adj.|成团的，聚集的|Gathered into a rounded mass or ball; clustered.|The glomerate structure of the mineral was evident. 这种矿物的聚集结构很明显。|Synonyms: clustered, aggregated, compact|Antonyms: dispersed, scattered|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *glomerātus*, past participle of glomerāre (“to wind into a ball”), from glomus (“ball”), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *glembʰ- (“to roll up, to gather”).
