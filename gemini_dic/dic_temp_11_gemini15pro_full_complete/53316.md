disables:/dɪˈseɪblz/ | v. | 使无效，使残疾 |  to make something unable to work or function; to cause someone to have a disability | The virus disables the computer system. 病毒使计算机系统瘫痪。| synonyms: incapacitates, cripples, immobilizes | antonyms: enables, empowers, strengthens | verb (third-person singular simple present disables, present participle disabling, simple past and past participle disabled)


disables:/dɪˈseɪblz/ | n. | 残疾人士（通常用disabled people）| people who have a disability (This is less common and often considered less sensitive than "disabled people"). |  New ramps have been installed to help disables access the building. (Less preferred usage) 新的坡道已经安装，以帮助残疾人士进入大楼。| synonyms: N/A (Use "disabled people" instead) | antonyms: N/A | noun (plural)


ETYMOLOGY->
From dis- (expressing negation) + able.  Ultimately from Latin dis- (apart, away) and habilis (able).
