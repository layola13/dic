dwarfishly:/ˈdwɔːrfɪʃli/ | adv. | 矮小地；侏儒般地 | In a manner resembling a dwarf; stuntedly.  Like a dwarf, especially in size or stature. |  The bonsai tree was dwarfishly proportioned, yet perfectly formed. 盆景树的比例矮小，但却形态完美。

The child, stunted by malnutrition, grew dwarfishly. 由于营养不良，这个孩子长得矮小。 | 近义词: stuntedly, diminutively | 反义词: gigantically, enormously | 词性: 副词

ETYMOLOGY->
From dwarf + -ish + -ly.  "Dwarf" comes from Old English *dweorh, of Germanic origin, related to Old Norse dvergr and Dutch dwerg, of unknown ultimate origin. "-ish" is a suffix forming adjectives from nouns, meaning "like, resembling." "-ly" is a suffix forming adverbs from adjectives.
