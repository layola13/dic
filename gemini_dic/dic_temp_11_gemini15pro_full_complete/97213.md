inflate:/ɪnˈfleɪt/ /ɪnˈfleɪt/| v. |膨胀；充气；使通货膨胀|To fill with air or gas so that it becomes larger; to increase something in size or importance, often by artificial or unreasonable means.|例句：He inflated the balloons for the party. 他为派对吹了气球。

例句：The government's spending policies have inflated the currency. 政府的支出政策导致了货币膨胀。|近义词：expand, enlarge, swell, pump up|反义词: deflate, shrink, contract|词性:动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *inflāre*, present active infinitive of *inflō* (“blow into, inflate, puff up”), from *in* (“in, into, on, upon, against”) + *flō* (“blow, breathe, puff”).
