high-browish:/ˌhaɪˈbraʊɪʃ/ | adj. | 高雅的，自命不凡的 |  Suggesting intellectual or cultural superiority; pretentious.  Derived from "high-brow," referring to a supposedly intellectual forehead shape. The "-ish" suffix softens the meaning, implying a slight or somewhat high-brow attitude. | She had a rather high-browish taste in music, preferring opera to pop. 她对音乐的品味相当高雅，喜欢歌剧胜过流行音乐。 |  He gave a high-browish lecture on the philosophy of art. 他就艺术哲学发表了一场自命不凡的演讲。 | 近义词：pretentious, intellectual, sophisticated, snobbish | 反义词：lowbrow, unsophisticated, down-to-earth | 词性: adjective
