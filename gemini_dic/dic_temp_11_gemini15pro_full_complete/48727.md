defilement:/dɪˈfaɪlmənt/| n.|玷污，污损，亵渎|The defilement of the sacred temple shocked the community. 这座神圣寺庙的亵渎震惊了整个社区。|例句：The act of vandalism was a defilement of the artist's work. 这种故意破坏行为是对艺术家作品的玷污。

The oil spill resulted in the defilement of the pristine coastline.  石油泄漏导致了原始海岸线的污染。|近义词：pollution, contamination, desecration, profanation|反义词：purification, cleansing|词性:名词

Etymology:
From Old French *defouler*, from *de-* (expressing reversal) + *fouler* "to tread, trample," from Latin *fullāre* "to press grapes, to full cloth."  The sense shifted from "treading underfoot" to "making unclean by treading on."
