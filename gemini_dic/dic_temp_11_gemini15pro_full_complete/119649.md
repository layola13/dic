middlebrow: /ˈmɪdəlˌbraʊ/ | n. adj. | 中雅人士；中雅的 |  A person with conventional, middle-class tastes in literature, music, art, etc., thought of as being less sophisticated than someone with more intellectual or cultivated tastes.  Someone or something considered to be of moderate intellectual or cultural value or interest. |  Example: The magazine caters to a middlebrow audience. 这本杂志迎合中雅人士的口味。 | Synonyms: conventional, unpretentious, mainstream, Philistine (often used derogatorily) | Antonyms: highbrow, lowbrow, intellectual, sophisticated | Noun, Adjective

middlebrow: /ˈmɪdəlˌbraʊ/ | n. adj. | 中雅人士；中雅的 | He considered the novel a bit middlebrow. 他认为这部小说有点中雅。 | Synonyms:  middle-of-the-road | Antonyms: highbrow, lowbrow, intellectual, avant-garde | Noun, Adjective

词源：
From middle + brow.  "Brow" in this context refers to forehead, and by extension, intellect.  Highbrow referred to a high forehead, supposedly indicating greater intelligence. Middlebrow falls between highbrow and lowbrow.
