conspirant:/kənˈspɪrənt/| n.|同谋者，阴谋参与者|A person who takes part in a conspiracy; a plotter.|例句：The conspirants met in secret to plan their takeover. 同谋者秘密会面，计划他们的接管行动。

例句：She was identified as a conspirant in the assassination plot. 她被确认为暗杀阴谋的同谋者。|近义词：plotter, collaborator, schemer, intrigante|反义词：opponent, adversary|词性: noun

conspire:/kənˈspaɪər/| v.|密谋，共谋，协力|To secretly plan with others to do something illegal or harmful.|例句：They conspired to overthrow the government. 他们密谋推翻政府。

例句：The weather seemed to conspire against us, raining on the day of the picnic. 天气似乎在和我们作对，野餐那天下了雨。|近义词：plot, scheme, collude, intrigue|反义词：cooperate (in a positive sense)|词性: verb

词源:
From Late Latin conspirant-, conspirans, present participle of conspirare "to agree, unite, plot," from com- "together" (see com-) + spirare "to breathe" (see spirit (n.)).  The notion is of "breathing together," hence, "harmonizing, agreeing."
