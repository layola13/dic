hypnotic:/hɪpˈnɑːtɪk/ /hɪpˈnɒtɪk/
adjective
催眠的；有催眠作用的；使人着迷的
inducing or tending to induce sleep or a trancelike state; mesmerizing
例句：The rhythmic sound of the waves had a hypnotic effect. 海浪有节奏的声音有一种催眠的效果。

例句：Her performance was hypnotic, captivating the entire audience. 她的表演令人着迷，吸引了全场观众。

近义词：mesmerizing, captivating, entrancing, spellbinding
反义词：uninteresting, boring, tedious, repellent
词性: adjective


hypnotically:/hɪpˈnɑːtɪkli/ /hɪpˈnɒtɪkli/
adverb
催眠地；有催眠作用地；使人着迷地
in a hypnotic manner
例句：The snake swayed hypnotically before its prey. 蛇在猎物面前摇摆不定，像是在催眠。

例句：He stared hypnotically at the flickering candle flame. 他目不转睛地盯着闪烁的烛光。

近义词：mesmerizingly, captivatingly, entrancingly
反义词：uninterestingly, boringly, tediously
词性: adverb


hypnotism:/ˈhɪpnətɪzəm/
noun
催眠术；催眠状态
the practice of hypnotizing someone; the state of being hypnotized
例句：He used hypnotism to help people quit smoking. 他用催眠术帮助人们戒烟。

例句：She was fascinated by the power of hypnotism. 她对催眠的力量着迷。

近义词：hypnosis, mesmerism
反义词: N/A
词性: noun


hypnotist:/ˈhɪpnətɪst/
noun
催眠师
a person who practices hypnotism
例句：The hypnotist put the subject into a deep trance. 催眠师使受试者进入深度恍惚状态。

例句：He visited a hypnotist to help him overcome his fear of flying. 他去看催眠师，以帮助他克服飞行恐惧症。

近义词：mesmerist
反义词: N/A
词性: noun

词源: From French *hypnotisme*, from Greek *hypnos* "sleep".
