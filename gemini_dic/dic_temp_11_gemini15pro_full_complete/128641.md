nickerer:/ˈnɪkərər/| n.|偷马贼；马贩子|A person who steals horses or deals in horses, often with a negative connotation suggesting dishonesty or shadiness.|The old nickerer was known for selling lame horses to unsuspecting buyers. 老马贩子以把瘸马卖给不知情的买家而闻名。|近义词：horse thief, horse trader|反义词: law-abiding citizen|词性:noun


nicker:/ˈnɪkər/| n.|一声嘶叫；（英国俚语）一英镑|The sound a horse makes; (British slang) one pound sterling.|The horse let out a soft nicker. 马轻轻地嘶叫了一声。|He only paid a nicker for that old bicycle. 他只花了一英镑买了那辆旧自行车。|近义词：neigh, whinny (for horse sound); quid (for British slang)|反义词: (none for horse sound) |词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From the verb "to nicker," which imitates the sound a horse makes.  The slang meaning of "pound" likely arose from rhyming slang "nicker tam" (Vietnam), shortened to "nicker."
