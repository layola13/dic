cosmolabe: /ˈkɒzməˌleɪb/ (UK) /ˈkɑːzməˌleɪb/ (US) | n. |星盘，宇宙盘|An astronomical instrument of the Renaissance period, used for determining the positions of the sun, moon, and stars.|He used a cosmolabe to chart the stars. 他使用星盘绘制星图。|近义词: astrolabe, quadrant |反义词: None |词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old French *cosmolabe, from Medieval Latin cosmolabium, from Ancient Greek κόσμος (kósmos, “world, universe”) + λαμβάνειν (lambánein, “to take”).
