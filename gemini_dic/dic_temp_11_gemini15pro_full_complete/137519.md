nontraditionalists:/ˌnɑːn.trəˈdɪʃ.ən.əl.ɪsts/ | n. |非传统主义者；不遵循传统的人| People who do not adhere to traditional beliefs or practices. |  Many nontraditionalists are choosing to live together before marriage. 许多非传统主义者选择婚前同居。|synonyms: unconventionalists, innovators, reformers, radicals, mavericks, iconoclasts | antonyms: traditionalists, conservatives, conformists | noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From non- + traditional + -ist + -s.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not." "Traditional" refers to established customs or beliefs. "-ist" is a suffix forming nouns that denote a person who adheres to a particular doctrine or system.  "-s" is a plural suffix.
