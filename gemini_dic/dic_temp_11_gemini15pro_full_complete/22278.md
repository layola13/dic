boneshaker:/ˈboʊnˌʃeɪkər/| n. |骨头震动器；早期自行车|An early type of bicycle with a large front wheel and a small rear wheel, known for its bumpy ride.|例句：The boneshaker was a popular mode of transportation in the late 19th century. 19世纪后期，骨头震动器是一种流行的交通工具。

例句：Riding a boneshaker on cobblestone streets was quite an adventure. 在鹅卵石街道上骑骨头震动器真是一次冒险。|近义词：bicycle, velocipede, penny-farthing |反义词:modern bicycle, car|词性:名词

boneshaker:/ˈboʊnˌʃeɪkər/| adj. |颠簸的，震动骨头的|Something that causes a lot of shaking or jolting, typically a vehicle.|例句：The old truck was a real boneshaker, making the journey uncomfortable.  这辆旧卡车真是个骨头震动器，让旅程很不舒服。


例句：That rollercoaster was a boneshaker! I thought my teeth would fall out.  那个过山车真是个骨头震动器！我以为我的牙齿都要掉了。|近义词：bumpy, jarring, rough |反义词:smooth, comfortable|词性:形容词
