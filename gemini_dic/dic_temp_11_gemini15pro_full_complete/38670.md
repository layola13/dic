condemnation:/ˌkɑːndemˈneɪʃn/ /ˌkɒndemˈneɪʃn/| n. |谴责，定罪，非难|The act of condemning someone or something to a particular punishment or fate; strong disapproval; the state of being condemned.| The jury's condemnation of the defendant was swift and unanimous. 陪审团迅速一致地谴责了被告。 |The building was in a state of condemnation and scheduled for demolition. 这座建筑处于被谴责的状态，并计划拆除。|近义词：censure, denunciation, reprobation, conviction|反义词：praise, approval, acquittal|词性: 名词

Etymology:
From Late Latin *condemnationem (nominative condemnatio), noun of action from past participle stem of condemnare "to condemn, sentence, doom," from com- "together" (see com-) + damnare "to adjudge guilty; to condemn, blame, reject," from damnum "damage, loss, hurt" (see damn).
