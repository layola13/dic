gammoning:/ˈɡæmənɪŋ/ | n. | 欺骗；胡说；瞎扯 |Deception; misleading talk; nonsense. |He accused the salesman of gammoning him into buying a faulty car. 他指责销售员骗他买了辆有缺陷的车。|
gammoning:/ˈɡæmənɪŋ/ | v. | 欺骗；胡说；瞎扯 |To deceive; to talk misleadingly; to talk nonsense. |They gammoned their way into the VIP area. 他们骗过了保安，进入了贵宾区。|
gammoning:/ˈɡæmənɪŋ/ | adj.| 欺骗性的；胡扯的 |Deceptive; nonsensical.| His gammoning stories made everyone suspicious. 他那些胡编乱造的故事让每个人都起了疑心。|


Etymology: From gammon (n.), a cured ham, later slang for nonsense or deception.  The exact transition is unclear, but it may relate to the idea of "dressing up" something to make it seem better than it is, or to the notion of "hamming it up" theatrically.
