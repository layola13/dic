hussar:/həˈzɑːr/
noun
轻骑兵
A member of a class of light cavalry, originally of Hungarian origin, noted for their bravery and dashing uniforms.
The hussars charged across the field, their sabers flashing in the sunlight. 轻骑兵冲过战场，他们的马刀在阳光下闪闪发光。
The hussar's uniform was richly decorated with braid and gold buttons. 轻骑兵的制服上装饰着华丽的穗带和金纽扣。
synonyms: cavalryman, trooper, horseman
antonyms: infantryman, foot soldier
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Hungarian *huszár, from Serbian хусар (husar), from Medieval Latin cursarius (“pirate, raider”), from cursus (“course, running”). Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ḱers- (“to run”).
