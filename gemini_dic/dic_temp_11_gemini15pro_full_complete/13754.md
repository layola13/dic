aucupation: /ˌɔːkjuˈpeɪʃən/ | n. | 捕鸟; 诱捕; 阴谋 | The act of fowling, or catching birds; bird-catching.  Also, the practice of seeking or obtaining something by insidious art or stratagem. | His aucupation of rare butterflies led him to remote corners of the world.  他为了捕捉稀有蝴蝶而踏遍了世界偏远地区。 |  近义词: trapping, ensnaring, scheming | 反义词:  release, liberation | 词性: noun

词源: From Latin *aucupātio*, from *aucupārī* (“to catch birds”), from *avis* (“bird”) + *capere* (“to take”).
