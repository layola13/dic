flunk:/flʌŋk/
verb
不及格，失败
to fail, especially in a course or an examination
He flunked his math exam. 他数学考试不及格。
近义词:fail, bomb
反义词:pass, ace
词性:verb

flunk:/flʌŋk/
noun
不及格
a failing grade
He got a flunk in history. 他历史考试不及格。
近义词:failure
反义词:pass
词性:noun

PHRASE->
flunk out (of) = 因成绩不及格而退学
He flunked out of college after his first year. 他第一年后因成绩不及格而退学。
flunk a test = 考试不及格
She flunked the math test. 她数学考试不及格。
flunk a course = 课程不及格
He flunked his history course. 他历史课程不及格。
flunk someone =  (老师)让某人不合格
The professor flunked him because he plagiarized. 教授因为他剽窃而让他不及格。
be flunking something = 在某方面不及格
I'm flunking chemistry. 我化学不及格。


词源：Possibly from dialectal *flunk, of uncertain origin; perhaps a blend of *flank ‘to turn aside’ and funk ‘to shrink back from fear.’
