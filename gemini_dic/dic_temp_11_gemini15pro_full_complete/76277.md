fyrd:/fɪrd/| n.| 民兵，征召兵| An army of freemen, organized in Anglo-Saxon England, called to defend their territory when necessary. | The fyrd was called out to repel the Viking invaders.| 近义词: militia, levy| 反义词: standing army| 词性: 名词

Etymology:
Old English *fyrd, from Proto-Germanic *furdiz ("army, expedition," literally "a going forth"), from Proto-Indo-European *per- ("to try, strive, risk"). Cognate with Old Frisian fird ("army, military expedition"), Old Saxon firdi ("army, infantry"), Old High German firt ("military expedition, journey"), Old Norse ferð ("journey, expedition, military force").
