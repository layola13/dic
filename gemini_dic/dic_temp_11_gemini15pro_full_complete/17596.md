beautifuler: /bjuːtɪfʊlər/ (This form is generally considered non-standard) | adj. | 更美丽的 (Non-standard) |  "Beautifuler" is a non-standard and grammatically incorrect form of the comparative adjective of "beautiful." The correct comparative form is "more beautiful."  It arises from applying the regular comparative suffix "-er" to "beautiful," which is already a multi-syllable adjective and typically uses "more" for comparison. | *Incorrect:*  This flower is beautifuler than that one. 这朵花比那朵更漂亮。（错误用法）| Near synonyms: more beautiful, prettier | Antonyms: uglier, less beautiful | Word Class: Adjective (comparative - non-standard)
