muscarine:/ˈmʌskərɪn/ | n. |  毒蕈碱；蕈毒碱 | A toxic alkaloid found in certain mushrooms, especially the fly agaric (Amanita muscaria). It mimics the action of the neurotransmitter acetylcholine. |  Muscarine poisoning can cause symptoms such as sweating, salivation, and slowed heart rate. 毒蕈碱中毒可引起出汗、流涎和心率减慢等症状。 | Synonyms: cholinomimetic | Antonyms: atropine, scopolamine | noun
