filibusterers:/ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstərərz/
n.
阻挠议事的人，冗长发言的人
People who obstruct or delay legislative action, especially by making long speeches.
The filibusterers prevented the bill from coming to a vote. 冗长发言的人阻止了该法案进行投票。
Synonyms: obstructionists, delayers, procrastinators
Antonyms: expediters, facilitators


filibuster:/ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstər/
n.
阻挠议事，冗长发言
An action such as a prolonged speech that obstructs progress in a legislative assembly while not technically contravening the required procedures.
The senator launched a filibuster to prevent the bill from passing. 参议员发动了冗长发言以阻止该法案通过。
Synonyms: obstruction, delay, procrastination
Antonyms: expedition, facilitation


filibuster:/ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstər/
v.
阻挠议事，冗长发言
To obstruct or delay legislative action, especially by making long speeches.
The opposition party filibustered the bill for days. 反对党连续几天阻挠该法案。
Synonyms: obstruct, delay, procrastinate
Antonyms: expedite, facilitate
