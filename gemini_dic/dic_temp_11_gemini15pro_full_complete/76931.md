gape:/ɡeɪp/| v. n.|张口结舌；目瞪口呆；裂开；张开|To stare with an open mouth, typically in amazement or wonder; a wide opening.|例句：The children gaped at the magician's tricks. 孩子们张大嘴巴看着魔术师的戏法。

例句：The earthquake left a gaping hole in the road. 地震在路上留下了一个大洞。|近义词：stare, gawk, yawn, open wide|反义词：close, shut|词性：动词，名词

gape:/ɡeɪp/| n.|张口; 裂口|A wide opening.|例句：The gape in the fence was big enough for a dog to get through. 篱笆上的裂口大到足以让狗钻过去。

例句：He covered the gape in the wall with a piece of cardboard. 他用一块纸板盖住了墙上的裂缝。 |近义词：opening, chasm, crack, fissure|反义词：closure, seal|词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old Norse *gapa "to open wide, yawn," from Proto-Germanic *gapaną (source also of Old English geapian "yawn, gape," Old Saxon gapon, Old Frisian gapa, Middle High German gapen, German gaffen "to gape, stare").
