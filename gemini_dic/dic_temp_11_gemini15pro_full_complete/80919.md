gowns: /ɡaʊnz/ | n. | 长袍，礼服 |  A long, flowing garment, typically worn on formal occasions.  Usually loose-fitting and reaching to the ankles or floor. Originally referring to any long, loose garment, it now specifically indicates formal wear. | The queen wore a magnificent gown to the coronation. 女王在加冕典礼上穿了一件华丽的礼服。| synonyms: dress, robe, frock | antonyms: suit, casual wear | noun

gowns: /ɡaʊnz/ | v. |  (诗歌或文学) 穿着礼服，使穿着礼服| To clothe in or wear a gown.  Mostly used in poetic or literary contexts.  | The ladies were gowned in silk and velvet. 女士们穿着丝绸和天鹅绒的礼服。| synonyms: dress, attire, clothe | antonyms: undress, disrobe | verb
