ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *confectionarie (modern French confiserie), from Late Latin confectionarius (“a preparer of preserves, confectioner”), from Latin confectio (“a preparing, compounding, a confection”), from conficere (“to make up completely, to finish, to accomplish, to prepare”), from com- (“together”) + facere (“to do, to make”).
