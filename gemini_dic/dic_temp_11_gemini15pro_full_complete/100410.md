intertext:/ˌɪntərˈtekst/ | n. |互文性，文本互涉|The shaping of a text's meaning by another text. It can include an author’s borrowing and transforming of a prior text or to a reader’s referencing of one text in reading another.|The novel's intertextuality with classic Greek myths adds a layer of depth to the narrative. 这部小说与经典希腊神话的互文性为叙事增添了一层深度。|Synonyms: interrelationship, interconnectedness|Antonyms:  (No direct antonym, could consider isolation, independence)|noun
