hackneyman:/ˈhæknimən/ | n. |出租马车夫；雇佣文人|A driver of a hackney carriage (a carriage for hire); a person hired to do routine writing or other literary work. | In Victorian London, the hackneyman was a familiar sight. 在维多利亚时代的伦敦，出租马车夫是一个常见的景象。| The publisher hired a hackneyman to churn out cheap novels. 出版商雇佣了一个雇佣文人来粗制滥造廉价小说。 | 近义词: cabdriver, hack writer | 反义词:  (No direct antonym) | 词性: noun

hackneyed:/ˈhæknid/ | adj. |陈腐的；老套的|Made commonplace or trite; stale from overuse. | The plot of the movie was hackneyed and predictable.  这部电影的情节老套且可预测。|  She used a hackneyed phrase, and it made her writing seem unoriginal. 她用了一个陈腐的短语，这使得她的写作显得缺乏 originality。| 近义词:  trite, clichéd, banal, stale, overused | 反义词: original, fresh, novel, innovative | 词性: adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *hakeneie*, from Old French *haquenee*.  Originally referring to a horse suitable for ordinary riding, later to a horse or carriage let out for hire, and eventually to the driver of such a carriage. The sense of "a hired writer" developed figuratively.
