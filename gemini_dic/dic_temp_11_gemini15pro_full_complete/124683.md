moves:/muːvz/ | n. | 行动，步骤，移动 |  A series of actions or activities undertaken to achieve an objective. |  The company made several moves to improve its market share. 公司采取了几项行动来提高其市场份额。| synonyms: actions, steps, maneuvers | antonyms: inaction, stillness | noun

moves:/muːvz/ | v. | 移动，搬家，使感动 | To change the position of something, to change residence, or to cause someone to feel emotion. |  She moved the chair closer to the window. 她把椅子移近窗户。| synonyms: shift, relocate, touch | antonyms: stay, remain, unmoved | verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *moven*, from Old French *mover*, from Latin *movēre* ("to move, set in motion, stir, remove, disturb"), from Proto-Italic *movēō*, from Proto-Indo-European *meh₁- (“to push away”).
