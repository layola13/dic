altocumulus's:/ˌæltəʊˈkjuːmjələsɪz/ |n.| 高积云 |Altocumulus clouds are mid-level clouds characterized by white or gray patches or layered sheets, often arranged in groups or lines. They typically appear between 6,500 and 20,000 feet above the ground.  "Altocumulus" itself is derived from the Latin "altus" (high) and "cumulus" (heap).  The apostrophe-s indicates possession, meaning "belonging to the altocumulus." For example, the altocumulus's base appeared dark and ominous.  In this case, "base" belongs to the altocumulus cloud.|例句：The altocumulus's appearance indicated a change in weather. 高积云的出现预示着天气变化。

The altocumulus's shape shifted constantly in the wind.  高积云的形状在风中不断变化。|近义词：mid-level clouds, altocumuli |反义词: N/A |词性: Noun
