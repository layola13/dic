multiculturalism:/ˌmʌltiˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm/| n. |多元文化主义|The presence of, or support for the presence of, several distinct cultural or ethnic groups within a society.| Canada embraces multiculturalism and welcomes people from all over the world.  加拿大信奉多元文化主义，欢迎来自世界各地的人们。|
multiculturalism:/ˌmʌltiˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm/| n. |多元文化主义|The doctrine that several different cultures (rather than one national culture) can co-exist peacefully and equitably in a single country.|  Multiculturalism is a complex and often debated topic. 多元文化主义是一个复杂且经常引起争议的话题。|
multiculturalism:/ˌmʌltiˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm/| n. |多元文化主义|The policy or process whereby the distinctive identities of the cultural groups within such a society are maintained or supported.| The government's policy of multiculturalism has been praised by some and criticized by others. 政府的多元文化主义政策受到了一些人的赞扬，也受到了一些人的批评。|近义词:pluralism, diversity|反义词:monoculturalism, assimilation|


ETYMOLOGY->
From multi- (meaning "many") + cultural + -ism (a suffix forming nouns meaning a doctrine, belief, or practice).
