discreditably:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪtəbli/
adv. |不光彩地，丢脸地| In a manner that brings discredit; disgracefully. | He behaved discreditably at the party and embarrassed his family. 他在派对上的行为不光彩，让他的家人感到尴尬。| 近义词：shamefully, disgracefully, ignominiously | 反义词：honorably, creditably, respectably
词性: 副词

discredit:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪt/
v. |使丢脸，败坏名声|  To harm the reputation of; to damage the credibility of.  | The scandal discredited the politician. 这桩丑闻败坏了这位政治家的名声。 | 近义词：disgrace, shame, dishonor | 反义词：honor, praise, commend
词性: 动词

discredit:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪt/
n. |丢脸，耻辱| Loss or damage to reputation; disgrace. | The accusations brought discredit upon the entire organization. 这些指控给整个组织带来了耻辱。| 近义词：disgrace, shame, dishonor | 反义词：honor, credit, prestige
词性: 名词
