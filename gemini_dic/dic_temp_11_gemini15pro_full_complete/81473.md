grave:/ɡreɪv/| adj. n.|严肃的，严重的；坟墓|A matter of grave concern. 严重关切的事情。|例句：The situation is very grave.  情况非常严重。

例句：He visited his mother's grave. 他去拜祭了他母亲的坟墓。|近义词：serious, solemn, tomb, sepulcher|反义词：trivial, unimportant, flippant|词性：形容词, 名词

grave:/ɡreɪv/| v.|雕刻，铭记|To grave a design on a stone. 在石头上雕刻图案。|例句：His name was graved on the tombstone. 他的名字被刻在了墓碑上。

例句：The experience was graved in her memory. 这段经历深深地铭刻在她的记忆中。|近义词：carve, engrave, inscribe|反义词：erase|词性：动词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *græf, from Proto-Germanic *grabą (“grave, ditch”), from Proto-Indo-European *ghrebh- (“to dig, scratch, scrape”). Cognate with Dutch graf (“grave”), German Grab (“grave”), Swedish grav (“grave”).  The adjective meaning "serious" developed from the noun, referring to the seriousness of death.
