bad-mouthing:/ˌbæd ˈmaʊðɪŋ/
|n.| 诋毁，说坏话
|The act of criticizing someone or something unfairly, often behind their back.|
|He lost his job due to bad-mouthing his boss to colleagues.|他因为在同事面前说老板的坏话而丢了工作。

|
|
bad-mouthing:/ˌbæd ˈmaʊðɪŋ/
|v.|  说坏话，诋毁
|To criticize someone or something unfairly, often behind their back.|
|She was caught bad-mouthing her former employer.|她被发现诋毁她的前雇主。

|
|


近义词: criticizing, denigrating, slandering, backbiting, maligning
|
|
反义词: praising, commending, lauding, complimenting
|
|


词源:  "bad" (meaning negative or unfavorable) + "mouth" (referring to speech) + "-ing" (present participle suffix, indicating an ongoing action).  The term literally refers to using one's mouth to say bad things about someone or something.
