elites:/ɪˈliːts/ /eɪˈliːts/| n.|精英；精华；上层人士|A select group of people considered to be superior in terms of ability, talent, influence, or wealth.  A group or class of people seen as having the greatest power and influence in a society, especially because of their wealth or privilege.| The academic elites of the university were invited to the conference. 大学的学术精英被邀请参加了这次会议。|
synonyms: best, cream, pick, aristocracy, nobility|
antonyms: masses, commoners, proletariat|
noun


elites:/ɪˈliːts/ /eɪˈliːts/| adj. |精英的；精锐的|Relating to or characteristic of an elite.|  The elite troops were specially trained for the mission.  这支精锐部队是为这次任务专门训练的。|
synonyms: select, choice, top, prime, first-class|
antonyms: ordinary, common, average, mediocre|
adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *élite*, from past participle of *élire* "to elect, choose," from Latin *eligere* "to pick out, select," from *ex* "out" + *legere* "to choose, gather, read."
