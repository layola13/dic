ETYMOLOGY->
From far + off.  "Far" traces back to Old English "feorr," from Proto-Germanic "*ferraz," ultimately from Proto-Indo-European "*per-," meaning "forward, beyond." "Off" comes from Old English "of," meaning "away from, off."
