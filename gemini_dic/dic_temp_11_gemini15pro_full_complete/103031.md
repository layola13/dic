jekyll-and-hyde:/ˈdʒɛkəl ənd ˈhaɪd/ | n. | 杰基尔和海德; 双重人格者 |  Refers to a person with two distinct personalities, one good and one evil, derived from the novel "Strange Case of Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde" by Robert Louis Stevenson. | He's a real Jekyll and Hyde – charming one minute and aggressive the next. 他真是个双面人——一会儿很迷人，一会儿又很咄咄逼人。 | Synonyms: split personality, dual personality | Antonyms: consistent personality, stable personality | noun
