ichthyolatry: /ɪkθiˈɒlətri/ | n. | 鱼崇拜；拜鱼教 | The worship of fish or fish-shaped deities. |  In some ancient cultures, ichthyolatry was a prominent religious practice.  在一些古代文化中，鱼崇拜是一种重要的宗教习俗。 |  Example: Archaeological evidence suggests the prevalence of ichthyolatry in this region. 考古证据表明该地区鱼崇拜的盛行。| Near synonyms: fish worship, pisciculture (related but not synonymous - refers to fish farming) | Antonyms: None (difficult to have a direct antonym for a specific form of worship) | noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Ancient Greek ἰχθύς (ikhthús, “fish”) + -λατρεία (-latreía, “worship”), from λατρεύω (latreuō, “I serve, I worship”).
