boroughholder:/ˈbʌrəˌhəʊldər/
n.
自由地主; 市镇代表
A person who owned land in a borough and thus had voting rights, especially one who elected members of parliament.
In medieval England, boroughholders were influential figures in local politics. 在中世纪的英格兰，自由地主是地方政治中有影响力的人物。
freeman, burgess, citizen
landless peasant, serf
noun
