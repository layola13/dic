klondike:/ˈklɒnˌdaɪk/ (US) /ˈklɒnˌdaɪk/ (UK)| n.|克朗代克；繁荣时期；淘金热|A region in northwestern Canada, famous for the gold rush of 1897-1899.  It can also refer to any place of great activity or rapid development, especially one that creates sudden wealth.|例句：The Klondike Gold Rush drew thousands of prospectors. 克朗代克淘金热吸引了成千上万的探矿者。

例句：The internet boom was a modern-day Klondike. 互联网的繁荣是现代的克朗代克。|近义词：bonanza, gold rush, boom|反义词:bust, depression, slump|词性:noun
