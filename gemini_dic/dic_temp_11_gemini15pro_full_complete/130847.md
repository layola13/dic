nonapprehensive: /ˌnɒnˌæprɪˈhɛnsɪv/ | adj. | 不忧虑的，不担心的 |  Formed by adding the prefix "non-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") to the adjective "apprehensive." "Apprehensive" comes from the Latin word "apprehendere," meaning "to grasp, seize, or understand."  The addition of "non-" negates the meaning of apprehension, resulting in the meaning of "not apprehensive" or "free from worry." | The children were nonapprehensive about the trip, excited to explore a new place. 孩子们对这次旅行并不担心，对探索新地方感到兴奋。 | She remained nonapprehensive throughout the turbulent flight, trusting the pilots' skills. 在整个动荡的飞行过程中，她始终保持镇定，相信飞行员的技术。 | 近义词:  untroubled, serene | 反义词: uneasy, nervous | 词性: adjective
