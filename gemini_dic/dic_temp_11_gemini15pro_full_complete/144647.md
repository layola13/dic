outwoven:/ˌaʊtˈwoʊvən/
adjective
交织出来的，编织出来的
interwoven, usually used in a metaphorical sense, suggesting something intricately combined or blended.
The tapestry of life is outwoven with joy and sorrow.  生命的织锦交织着欢乐和悲伤。
The story is outwoven with threads of mystery and intrigue.  故事交织着神秘和阴谋的线索。
近义词: interwoven, interlaced, intertwined
反义词: separate, detached
词性: adjective

Etymology:
From "out-" (meaning "thoroughly, completely") + "weave" (to form (fabric or a fabric item) by interlacing long threads passing in one direction with others passing in a different direction).  The past participle "woven" is combined with "out-" to form "outwoven."
