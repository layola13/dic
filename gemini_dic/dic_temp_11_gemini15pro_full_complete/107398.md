laminarite:/ˈlæmɪnəˌraɪt/| n. |昆布|A brown alga of the family Laminariaceae, especially the large species Laminaria digitata, which grows in cold ocean waters. Laminarites are harvested for food and for producing algin.|Kelp forests, dominated by laminarites, provide habitat for many marine species.|Synonym: kelp, seaweed|Antonym: (No direct antonym, perhaps terrestrial plant)|noun
