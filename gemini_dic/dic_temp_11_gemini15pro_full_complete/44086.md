crippled:/ˈkrɪpəld/ | adj. | 跛的，残废的；严重受损的 |  (of a person) unable to walk or move properly because of an injury or disability; severely damaged or weakened. |  The accident left him crippled for life.  事故使他终身残疾。| synonyms: disabled, handicapped, impaired, lame | antonyms: able-bodied, healthy, sound | adjective

cripple:/ˈkrɪpəl/ | v. |  使跛，使残废；严重损坏，削弱 | cause (someone) to become unable to walk or move properly; severely damage or weaken. | The virus crippled the computer network. 病毒瘫痪了计算机网络。| synonyms: disable, incapacitate, paralyze, damage | antonyms:  strengthen, empower, enable | verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *crypel, *crypol, from a Proto-Germanic *krupilaz, meaning "to creep," related to Old Norse krypill "cripple." The meaning shifted from "creeping" to "lame" and then to "disabled."
