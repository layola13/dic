dynastidan:/daɪˈnæstɪdən/ | n. | 王朝成员；王朝动物 | A member of a dynasty; an animal belonging to the Dynastidae family (scarab beetles). |  The dynastidan displayed its impressive horns.  这只王朝动物展示了它令人印象深刻的角。| Near Synonyms:  dynast,  royal | Antonyms: commoner, peasant | Noun


Etymology:  From New Latin *Dynastidae, from Ancient Greek δυναστεία (dunasteía, “lordship, power, dominion”).  Ultimately from δύναμαι (dúnamai, “to be able; to have power”).
