musicologically:/ˌmjuːzɪkəˈlɒdʒɪkli/
adv.
音乐学地，从音乐学的角度
From the perspective of musicology; in a way relating to the study of music.
例句：The piece was analyzed musicologically, revealing its complex structure. 这首曲子从音乐学角度进行了分析，揭示了其复杂的结构。

例句：Musicologically speaking, this symphony represents a significant departure from the composer's earlier works. 从音乐学角度来说，这部交响曲代表了作曲家早期作品的重大突破。


近义词：analytically, critically, academically
反义词:intuitively, subjectively
词性:副词

词源:
music (n.) + -o- + -logical (adj.) + -ly (adv.)
music: from Old French musique (12c.), from Latin musica "music, the art of music," from Greek mousikē (tekhnē) "(art) of the Muses," from fem. of mousikos "pertaining to the Muses," from Mousa "Muse" 
-o-: connecting vowel
-logical: from French -logique, from Late Latin -logicus, from Greek -logikos, from logos "word, reason"
-ly: adverbial suffix
