overrationalize: /ˌoʊvərˈræʃənəlaɪz/ | v. | 过度合理化；过分理性解释 | To explain or justify (one's own or another's behavior) with logical reasons, even if these are not appropriate or accurate.  | He tried to overrationalize his decision to quit his job, but everyone knew he was just burned out. 他试图过度合理化他辞职的决定，但每个人都知道他只是精疲力竭了。

| She overrationalized her fear of flying by citing statistics about car accidents. 她引用车祸统计数据，过度合理化她对飞行的恐惧。 | 近义词: overthink, intellectualize, justify excessively | 反义词: accept, intuit, feel | 词性: verb

overrationalization: /ˌoʊvərˌræʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 过度合理化 | The act of overrationalizing. |  His overrationalization of the situation made it difficult to have an honest conversation. 他对情况的过度合理化使得很难进行坦诚的对话。

| The overrationalization of her eating habits masked a deeper emotional issue. 她对饮食习惯的过度合理化掩盖了更深层的情感问题。 | 近义词: overthinking, intellectualization, excessive justification | 反义词: acceptance, intuition, feeling | 词性: noun

词源:  The prefix "over-" signifies "too much" or "excessively," and "rationalize" comes from the Latin word "rationalis," meaning "reasonable."  Therefore, "overrationalize" literally means "to make something excessively reasonable."
