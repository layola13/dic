coarsen:/ˈkɔːrsən/ /ˈkoʊrsən/| v. |使粗糙，使粗俗，使低劣|to make something rougher or less refined; to become rougher or less refined|The strong winds coarsened her skin.  强风使她的皮肤变得粗糙。

The language of the debate coarsened as the evening wore on.  随着夜幕降临，辩论的语言变得粗俗起来。|近义词：roughen, toughen, degrade, deteriorate|反义词：refine, improve, soften|词性：动词

ETYMOLOGY->
From "coarse" + "-en".  "Coarse" comes from Middle English *cors*, from Old French *gros*, ultimately from Latin *grossus* meaning "thick, fat, coarse."  The suffix "-en" forms verbs meaning "to make or become."
