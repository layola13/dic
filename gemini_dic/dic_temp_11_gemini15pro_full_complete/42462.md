counterfactually:/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəli/ | adv. | 反事实地；与事实相反地 |  In a way that is not consistent with the facts; contrary to the truth. |  Counterfactually, if he had taken the other road, he would have avoided the accident. 与事实相反，如果他走了另一条路，他就不会发生事故了。| synonym: hypothetically, theoretically | antonym: factually, actually | adverb

Etymology:
From counter- (against, opposite) + factual + -ly (adverbial suffix).  The word "counterfactual" originated in the mid-20th century, combining the prefix "counter-" (meaning "against" or "opposite") with "factual" to denote something contrary to established facts.
