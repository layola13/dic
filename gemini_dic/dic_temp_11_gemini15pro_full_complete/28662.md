carin:/ˈkærɪn/ |n.| 龙骨，脊，突起 |A projecting structure or ridge, especially one on the bottom of a boat or ship, or along the breastbone of a bird. |The carin of the sailboat was damaged in the storm. 帆船的龙骨在暴风雨中受损了。|Synonym: keel, ridge, crest |Antonym: groove, hollow, depression |noun

carin:/ˈkærɪn/ |n.| (解剖学)隆起，隆凸 |A raised or prominent part of a bone or other anatomical structure. |The doctor pointed to the carin on the patient's sternum. 医生指着病人胸骨上的隆起。|Synonym: protuberance, prominence, bump |Antonym: indentation, hollow, depression |noun
