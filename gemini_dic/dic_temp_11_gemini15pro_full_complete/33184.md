chromolithograph: /ˈkrəʊməʊˈlɪθəɡrɑːf/ (UK) /ˈkroʊməʊˈlɪθəɡræf/ (US) | n. | 彩色石版画 | A picture printed in colours from a series of stone or zinc plates. |  Example: The antique shop had a beautiful chromolithograph of a Victorian garden scene. 古董店里有一幅维多利亚时代花园景象的彩色石版画。| Example: Chromolithographs were popular in the 19th century for reproducing famous paintings.  在19世纪，彩色石版画是复制名画的流行方式。| Synonym: color lithograph, print | Antonym:  original painting, photograph | noun
