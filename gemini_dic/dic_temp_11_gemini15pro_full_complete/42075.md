cothurnus: /kəˈθɜːrnəs/ | n. | 厚底靴；悲剧；高腔；高调 | A thick-soled boot or buskin worn by tragic actors in ancient Greek drama.  It also refers to the tragic genre itself, or a lofty or elevated style. | The actor donned the cothurnus and prepared to deliver his lines.  演员穿上了厚底靴，准备念台词。 |  The poet's work was marked by a distinct cothurnus. 这位诗人的作品以独特的高腔为特色。 | synonyms: buskin, tragedy, loftiness, elevation | antonyms: comedy, humor, lowliness, simplicity | noun
