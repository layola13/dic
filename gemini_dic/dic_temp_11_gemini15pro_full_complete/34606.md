cleavers:/ˈkliːvərz/| n. |猪殃殃；拉拉藤；鹅肠菜|A herbaceous plant with small hooked hairs that cling to clothing and fur. It is also known as goosegrass, stickyweed, catchweed, or sticky willy.|Example 1: The cleavers clung to my socks as I walked through the field. 我走过田野时，猪殃殃粘在我的袜子上。

Example 2:  We used to play with cleavers as children, sticking them to each other's clothes. 我们小时候常常玩猪殃殃，把它们粘在彼此的衣服上。|近义词：goosegrass, stickyweed, catchweed, sticky willy|反义词: N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *clīfre, from Proto-Germanic *klībijaną, related to cleave (meaning "to adhere").  The name refers to the plant's tendency to stick to things.
