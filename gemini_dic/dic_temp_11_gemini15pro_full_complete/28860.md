carpetbaggeries: /ˌkɑːrpɪtˈbæɡəriz/
| n. | 欺诈行为；掠夺行为；投机行为 |  Exploitation by outsiders of a region, especially for political or financial gain.  Often used to describe Northerners who moved to the South after the American Civil War to profit from the Reconstruction. |  The Reconstruction era was rife with carpetbaggeries, as unscrupulous individuals sought to exploit the South's instability.  重建时期充斥着欺诈行为，因为不道德的个人试图利用南方的动荡局势。

The historian documented the various carpetbaggeries that took place during Reconstruction, highlighting the corruption and exploitation that occurred. 这位历史学家记录了重建时期发生的各种欺诈行为，突出了当时的腐败和剥削。 | 近义词： exploitation, profiteering, opportunism | 反义词： altruism, philanthropy | 词性: 名词


词源:
From "carpetbagger," referring to Northerners who traveled South with their belongings in carpetbags (cheap suitcases made of carpet fabric).  Implied that they were opportunistic outsiders.
