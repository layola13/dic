hyperventilate: /ˌhaɪpərˈvɛntɪˌleɪt/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈvɛntɪleɪt/ (UK)| v. |过度换气，呼吸急促|To breathe at an abnormally rapid rate, so increasing the rate of loss of carbon dioxide from the blood.|She began to hyperventilate when she heard the bad news. 她听到坏消息后开始呼吸急促。|近义词：overbreathe|反义词: hypoventilate|词性：动词

hyperventilate: |v. |过度换气，呼吸急促|The act of breathing too rapidly and deeply, resulting in a decrease in the level of carbon dioxide in the blood.|He started to hyperventilate due to the stress of the exam. 由于考试的压力，他开始过度换气。|近义词：overbreathe|反义词: hypoventilate|词性：动词

Etymology: From hyper- "over, excessive" + ventilate "to air."  Ultimately from Latin *ventus "wind."
