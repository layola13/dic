intimidators:/ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪtərz/| n. |恐吓者，威胁者|A person who intimidates others, making them feel frightened or threatened.|The school bullies were notorious intimidators. 学校里的恶霸是臭名昭著的恐吓者。|Synonyms: bully, browbeater, menace, threatener|Antonyms: protector, defender, supporter|noun

intimidate:/ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪt/| v. |恐吓，威胁|To frighten or overawe (someone), especially in order to make them do what one wants.|They tried to intimidate him into signing the confession. 他们试图恐吓他签署供词。|Synonyms: frighten, threaten, menace, scare, terrorize|Antonyms: encourage, support, reassure|verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin intimidatus, past participle of intimidare "to make fearful," from Latin in- "in" + timidus "fearful."
