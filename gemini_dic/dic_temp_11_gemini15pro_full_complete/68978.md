fatigate:/ˈfætɪɡeɪt/| v. |使疲劳，使疲倦|To cause (someone) to feel tired or exhausted.|The long journey fatigued the travelers. 长途旅行使旅客们疲惫不堪。|Synonyms: tire, exhaust, weary, enervate|Antonyms: energize, invigorate, refresh|verb

fatigated:/ˈfætɪɡeɪtɪd/| adj. |疲劳的，疲倦的|Feeling tired or exhausted.|The fatigued workers longed for rest. 疲惫不堪的工人们渴望休息。|Synonyms: tired, exhausted, weary, worn out|Antonyms: energetic, invigorated, refreshed|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin fatigatus, past participle of fatigāre (“to tire, weary”), from Latin fatīgō (“I tire out”), from *fatī- (“I tire”), possibly related to Latin affatim (“sufficiently, abundantly”).
