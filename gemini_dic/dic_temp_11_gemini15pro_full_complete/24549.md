broadsided:/ˈbrɔːdsaɪdɪd/
verb
猛烈抨击，横向撞击
To attack or criticize someone harshly and unexpectedly; to hit the side of something, especially a ship.
The candidate was broadsided by accusations of corruption just days before the election.  候选人在选举前几天遭到腐败指控的猛烈抨击。
The ship was broadsided by a rogue wave. 船被一个巨浪横向撞击。
近义词:attacked, slammed, criticized, T-boned
反义词:praised, defended, supported
词性:v.


broadside:/ˈbrɔːdsaɪd/
noun
猛烈抨击，舷侧齐射，单页印刷品
A strong verbal attack; the simultaneous firing of all the guns on one side of a warship; a large sheet of paper printed on one side only, typically used for advertising or proclamations.
The newspaper launched a broadside against the government's new policy. 报纸对政府的新政策进行了猛烈抨击。
The ship delivered a devastating broadside.  这艘船进行了毁灭性的舷侧齐射。
They distributed broadsides advertising the upcoming concert.  他们分发了宣传即将举行的音乐会的单页印刷品。
近义词:attack, barrage, salvo, flyer, handbill, poster
反义词:defense, praise, commendation
词性:n.
