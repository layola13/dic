glassiest:/ˈɡlæsiɪst/ | adj. | 光滑如玻璃的；冷漠的；呆滞的 |  superlative adjective of glassy: most glassy. Having a smooth, shiny surface like glass; or having a fixed, unseeing stare |  Her eyes were glassiest after the shocking news. 她听到令人震惊的消息后，眼神变得呆滞。 The lake's surface was glassiest at dawn. 黎明时分的湖面光滑如镜。 | 近义词：smooth, shiny, glazed, vacant, expressionless | 反义词：rough, dull, expressive | 词性: 形容词

词源: From glass + -y + -est.  "glass" comes from Proto-Germanic *glasam, from Proto-Indo-European *ghel- (to shine).

PHRASE->

Not commonly used in phrases, as it's a superlative adjective.  However, it can be used in comparative constructions:

Even glassiest than before. 比以前更光滑。
The glassiest surface I've ever seen. 我见过的最光滑的表面。
Making it the glassiest possible.  使其尽可能光滑。
Not the glassiest of surfaces.  并非最光滑的表面。
Surprisingly glassiest.  出奇地光滑。
