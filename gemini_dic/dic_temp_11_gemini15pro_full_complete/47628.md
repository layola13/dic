debilitated:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪd/
adjective
衰弱的；虚弱的
Made weak and infirm.
The illness left her debilitated for months.  这病让她虚弱了好几个月。
debilitating:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪŋ/
adjective
使人衰弱的
Making someone weak and infirm.
The debilitating effects of the chemotherapy made it difficult for him to work. 化疗的副作用让他难以工作。
debilitate:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪt/
verb
使衰弱；使虚弱
To make someone weak and infirm.
The stroke debilitated his right side. 中风使他的右侧身体变得虚弱。
debilitation:/dɪˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən/
noun
衰弱；虚弱
The state of being weak and infirm.
The patient experienced significant debilitation following the surgery. 病人在手术后身体非常虚弱。


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin debilitatus, past participle of debilitare "to weaken," from de- "from, down" + bilitas "weakness," from habilis "able, apt."

近义词:weakened, enfeebled, incapacitated, frail, infirm
反义词:strengthened, invigorated, healthy, robust
