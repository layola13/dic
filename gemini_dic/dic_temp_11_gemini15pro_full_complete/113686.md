mackian:/ˈmækiən/ | adj. | 麦克式的，马基雅维利式的 | Of or relating to Niccolò Machiavelli or Machiavellianism, characterized by cunning, duplicity, and bad faith. |  His mackian tactics ensured he won the negotiation, but at the cost of his reputation. 他马基雅维利式的策略确保他在谈判中获胜，但却损害了他的声誉。 |  例句：Her mackian plan to climb the corporate ladder involved sabotaging her colleagues. 她为了往上爬而设计的马基雅维利式计划包括暗中破坏同事的工作。 | 近义词：cunning, devious, manipulative, unscrupulous | 反义词：honest, principled, straightforward | 词性: 形容词

词源: From Niccolò Machiavelli (1469-1527), Italian Renaissance diplomat, philosopher, historian, humanist, and writer, best known for his book *The Prince* (Il Principe).  The term "Machiavellian" (and "mackian" as a less common variant) came to describe a political philosophy that prioritizes pragmatism and expediency over morality.
