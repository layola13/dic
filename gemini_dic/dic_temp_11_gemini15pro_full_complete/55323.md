dissonant:/ˈdɪsənənt/ | adj. | 不和谐的，刺耳的 | Lacking harmony; clashing.  Characterized by a lack of agreement or compatibility. | The dissonant chords of the untuned piano grated on my nerves.  不和谐的未调音钢琴的和弦刺激了我的神经。| synonyms: discordant, jarring, cacophonous, inharmonious, clashing, conflicting | antonyms: harmonious, consonant, agreeable, concordant, tuneful | adjective

dissonance:/ˈdɪsənəns/ | n. | 不和谐，刺耳的声音 | A tension or clash resulting from the combination of two disharmonious or unsuitable elements. Lack of agreement or harmony between people or things.  |  The political dissonance in the country is alarming.  这个国家的政治不和谐令人担忧。 | synonyms: discord, conflict, disagreement, friction, clash | antonyms: harmony, agreement, concord, unison | noun

Etymology:
From Latin dissonant- ‘sounding differently’, from the verb dissonare, from dis- ‘apart’ + sonare ‘to sound’.
