overpreoccupying: /ˌoʊvərpriˈɒkjʊpaɪɪŋ/ | adj. | 过分关注的，过分投入的 | Excessively preoccupied; dominating one's thoughts to an excessive degree.  This adjective describes something that consumes someone's thoughts and attention to an unhealthy extent.  It implies a level of concern that is disproportionate to the actual importance of the issue. |  Her overpreoccupying thoughts about the upcoming exam made it difficult to focus on anything else. 她对即将到来的考试过分担忧，导致难以集中精力做其他事情。

The company's overpreoccupying focus on short-term profits ultimately led to its downfall.  公司过分关注短期利润，最终导致了它的垮台。 | 近义词: obsessive, consuming, all-consuming, fixated | 反义词: indifferent, apathetic, detached | 词性: adjective

词源:  "over-" (prefix meaning "excessive") + "preoccupy" (verb meaning "to absorb the attention of") + "-ing" (suffix forming present participle and adjective)  The word "preoccupy" comes from the Latin "praeoccupare," meaning "to seize beforehand."
