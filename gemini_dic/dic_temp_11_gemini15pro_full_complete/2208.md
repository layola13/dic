addles:/ˈædəlz/| vt.| 使糊涂，使混乱，腐坏（尤指鸡蛋）| to make someone unable to think clearly; to make a liquid or substance thick and bad  |The heat addled his brain. 高温使他神志不清。|Synonyms: confuse, muddle, bewilder, rot (especially of eggs) |Antonyms: clarify, enlighten, freshen|verb


addled:/ˈædəld/| adj.| 糊涂的，混乱的，腐坏的| confused and unable to think clearly; rotten (especially of eggs)|He had an addled mind from the fever. 他因为发烧而头脑混乱。|Synonyms: confused, muddled, bewildered, rotten (especially of eggs)|Antonyms: clear-headed, lucid, fresh|adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *adelen, from Old English adela (“liquid filth, mire”), from Proto-Germanic *adalą (“mire, mud”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ed- (“to flow, stream”). Cognate with Old Norse eðli (“liquid filth”).
