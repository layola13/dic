mononucleoses:/ˌmɒnəʊˌnjuːkliˈəʊsiːz/ /ˌmɑːnəˌnuːkliˈoʊsiːz/
noun
单核细胞增多症
An abnormally high number of mononuclear leukocytes (a type of white blood cell) in the blood.  Specifically, it often refers to infectious mononucleosis.
Infectious mononucleosis, often called mono or glandular fever, is usually caused by the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV). Symptoms include fatigue, fever, sore throat, and swollen lymph nodes.  The illness spreads through saliva.

近义词: glandular fever, mono (informal)
反义词:  None (This is a disease; it doesn't have a direct opposite.)
词性: noun


词源:  From mono- (one) + nucleus (kernel, central part) + -osis (condition, disease)
