ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle English *lēves, *lēfs, plural of lef, from Old English lēaf (“leaf, foliage, sheet of paper, petal”), from Proto-Germanic *laubą (“leaf”), from Proto-Indo-European *leubʰ- (“to peel, strip off, deprive of bark”). Cognate with Scots leaf, leif (“leaf”), West Frisian leaf (“leaf”), Dutch loof (“foliage”), German Laub (“foliage”), Danish løv (“foliage”), Swedish löv (“foliage”). Related also to Old English līf (“life, body”).  More at life.
