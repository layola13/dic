expurgatory:/ˈɛkspərɡətɔːri/ /ˈɛksˌpɝːɡəˌtɔri/
adjective
中文翻译：净化（的），删除不当内容（的）
英文解析：Tending to expurgate; removing objectionable passages or words from a written text.
例句：The editor made several expurgatory changes to the novel before publication.  编辑在小说出版前对小说做了几处删除不当内容的修改。

例句：The expurgatory version of the play omitted several scenes considered too violent for children.  该剧的净化版本删除了几个被认为对儿童来说过于暴力的场景。


近义词：bowdlerizing, censoring, cleansing, purifying
反义词：unabridged, unexpurgated, uncensored
词性：adjective


expurgate:/ˈɛkspərɡeɪt/ /ˈɛksˌpɝːɡeɪt/
verb
中文翻译：净化，删除（不当内容）
英文解析：To remove objectionable passages or words from a written text.
例句：The publisher decided to expurgate the offensive language from the book. 出版商决定从书中删除冒犯性的语言。

例句：Some parents want to expurgate certain books from school libraries. 一些家长希望从学校图书馆清除某些书籍。


近义词：censor, bowdlerize, purge, cleanse
反义词：include, retain, insert, add
词性：verb


expurgation:/ˌɛkspərˈɡeɪʃn/ /ˌɛksˌpɝːˈɡeɪʃn/
noun
中文翻译：净化，删除（不当内容）
英文解析：The act of removing objectionable passages or words from a written text.
例句：The expurgation of the text resulted in a much shorter version.  文本的净化导致版本精简了许多。

例句：The author disagreed with the expurgation of his work.  作者不同意删改他的作品。


近义词：censorship, bowdlerization, purging, cleansing
反义词：inclusion, retention, insertion, addition
词性：noun
