Fatimids: /ˈfætɪmɪdz/ | n. | 法蒂玛王朝 |  A member of a Muslim dynasty that traced its descent from Fatimah, daughter of Muhammad, and that built an empire in North Africa, Egypt, and Syria between the 10th and 12th centuries. | The Fatimids ruled Egypt for over two centuries. 法蒂玛王朝统治埃及超过两个世纪。 | The Fatimids controlled a vast territory stretching across North Africa and the Middle East. 法蒂玛王朝控制着广阔的领土，横跨北非和中东。 | synonyms: Fatimid Caliphate, Fatimid dynasty | antonyms: N/A | noun
