contradistinction:/ˌkɑːntrədɪˈstɪŋkʃn/ /ˌkɒntrədɪˈstɪŋkʃn/| n. |区别，对比，对照|The act of distinguishing by contrasting; the state of being contrasted. A distinction by contrast.  A striking difference that reveals clearly the distinct natures of the things compared.|He explained the difference between the two systems by contradistinction. 他通过对比解释了这两个系统的区别。|近义词：contrast, difference, differentiation, comparison|反义词：similarity, resemblance|词性: noun

Etymology:
From Late Latin *contradistinctio*, from contra- + distinctio "distinction."  The prefix *contra-* means "against" or "opposite."  The root *distinctio* derives from the verb distinguere, meaning "to separate, distinguish," ultimately from dis- "apart" + -stinguere "to prick, mark."
