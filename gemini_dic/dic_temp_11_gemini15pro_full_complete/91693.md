hyperconformist:/ˌhaɪpərˈkənˈfɔːrmɪst/| n.|超级从众者，极端遵从者|Someone who conforms to an excessive or extreme degree, often beyond what is considered normal or necessary.  A person who rigidly adheres to the prevailing norms and expectations of a group or society, often to an excessive or exaggerated extent.|The government was criticized for creating a society of hyperconformists, afraid to express individual opinions.|近义词：conformist, follower, traditionalist|反义词：nonconformist, rebel, maverick|词性:noun

Etymology:
From hyper- "excessive" + conformist. "Hyper-" comes from the Greek "hyper" meaning "over, above, beyond." "Conformist" comes from the verb "conform," which ultimately derives from the Latin "conformare," meaning "to shape in accordance with."
