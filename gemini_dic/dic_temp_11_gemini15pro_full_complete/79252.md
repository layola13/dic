glasshouse:/ˈɡlɑːshaʊs/ |ˈɡlæsˌhaʊs/ |n.|温室；玻璃厂|A building with walls and roof made chiefly of glass, used for growing plants that need protection from cold weather. |例句：The tomatoes are grown in a glasshouse. 西红柿是在温室里种植的。

例句：He works at a glasshouse making stained glass windows. 他在一家玻璃厂制作彩色玻璃窗。|近义词：greenhouse, conservatory |反义词: N/A |词性: noun


glasshouse:/ˈɡlɑːshaʊs/ |ˈɡlæsˌhaʊs/ |n.|（易受批评的）处境，地位|A situation in which someone is vulnerable to criticism. |例句：Politicians live in a glasshouse and should be careful about criticizing others. 政客们生活在一个易受批评的环境中，应该谨慎批评他人。

例句：Throwing stones while living in a glasshouse is risky.  身处玻璃屋还扔石头是很冒险的。|近义词：vulnerable position |反义词: N/A |词性: noun



ETYMOLOGY->

From Middle English *glashows*, from *glas* (“glass”) + *hows* (“house”).  Equivalent to glass + house.
