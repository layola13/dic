cardinalist:/ˈkɑːrdɪnəlɪst/| n. adj.|基数主义者；基数的|A supporter of or believer in cardinal utility theory, which assumes that utility can be measured and compared numerically.  Also, relating to or based on cardinal numbers.|Example: The economist was a staunch cardinalist, believing that utility could be quantified. 这位经济学家是坚定的基数主义者，他相信效用可以被量化。|Example: Cardinal numbers, unlike ordinal numbers, can be added and subtracted. 与序数不同，基数可以加减。|近义词:quantifier (as a noun), quantitative (as an adjective)|反义词:ordinalist (as a noun), ordinal (as an adjective)|词性:noun, adjective
