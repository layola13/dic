cantonment:/kænˈtʊnmənt/
|n.|
|兵营；驻扎地|
|A cantonment is a military camp or area where troops are permanently stationed.|
|The soldiers returned to their cantonment after the exercise.|士兵们演习后回到了他们的兵营。|

|The old cantonment was converted into a museum.|旧兵营被改建成了博物馆。|


|Synonyms: barracks, camp, base, garrison|
|Antonyms: civilian area, city|
|noun|


cantonment:/kænˈtʊnmənt/
|n.|
|（尤指印度的）市郊居民区，城区|
|A cantonment can also refer to a civilian residential area, particularly in India, adjacent to or surrounding a military station.|
|The cantonment area was known for its spacious bungalows.|这个城郊居民区以其宽敞的平房而闻名。|

|The market in the cantonment was always bustling with activity.|市郊居民区的市场总是熙熙攘攘。|



|Synonyms: suburb, residential area, township|
|Antonyms: city center, downtown|
|noun|


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cantonnement*, from *cantonner* "to allot quarters," from *canton* "corner, district."
