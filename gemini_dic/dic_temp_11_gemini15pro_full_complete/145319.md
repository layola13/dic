overbroadening:/ˌoʊvərˈbrɔːdnɪŋ/ | adj. | 过度泛化，过度扩展 |  The act of extending the application of a rule or concept to instances where it does not properly apply, often observed in language acquisition.  | Example: The child overbroadened the word "dog" to include all four-legged animals.  这个孩子过度泛化了“狗”这个词，把它用来指所有四足动物。 | Example:  Overbroadening of trademarks can lead to confusion in the marketplace. 商标的过度扩展会导致市场混乱。 | Synonyms: overgeneralization, overextension | Antonyms: narrowing, restriction | adjective
