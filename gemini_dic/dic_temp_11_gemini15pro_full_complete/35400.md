co-op:/ˈkoʊ ɑːp/ (US), /ˈkəʊ ɒp/ (UK)| n.|合作社，合作公寓|A business or organization owned and run jointly by its members, who share the profits or benefits.  A type of housing owned and managed by a cooperative association, whose members are the residents.|例句：We buy our groceries from the local co-op. 我们从当地的合作社购买食品杂货。

例句：She lives in a co-op in the city. 她住在城市里的一个合作公寓里。|近义词：cooperative, collective|反义词:corporation, conglomerate|词性:名词

co-oping:/koʊˈɑːpɪŋ/ (US), /kəʊˈɒpɪŋ/ (UK)| v.|合作，实习|Working together or participating in a cooperative program, often referring to a student work placement.|例句：They are co-oping on a new software project. 他们在合作开发一个新的软件项目。

例句：He's co-oping at a tech company this summer. 他今年夏天在一家科技公司实习。|近义词：collaborating, interning|反义词:competing, solo work|词性:动词
