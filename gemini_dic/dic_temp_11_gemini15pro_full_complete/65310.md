eudiometry:/ˌjuːdiˈɒmɪtri/ | n. | 气体分析(术)；气体测定(法) | The science of measuring the volume and composition of gases, especially as applied to the analysis of gases for medical or other purposes.  It involves techniques for determining the proportions of different gases in a mixture, often by absorbing specific components and measuring the resulting volume change. |  Eudiometry was crucial in the early days of chemistry for understanding the nature of gases. 气体分析在化学早期对于理解气体的性质至关重要。

Eudiometry is used to monitor air quality in industrial settings.  气体分析被用于监测工业环境中的空气质量。 | 近义词: gas analysis | 反义词:  N/A | 词性: noun

etymology:  From Greek *eu* (good) + *dia* (through) + *-meter* (measure) + *-y* (noun suffix).  Essentially, "measuring well" or "accurate measurement."
