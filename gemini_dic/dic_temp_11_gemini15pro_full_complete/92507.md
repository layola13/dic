hypothesized:/haɪˈpɒθəsaɪzd/ (US) /haɪˈpɒθɪsaɪzd/ (UK)| v. |假设的，假定的|put (something) forward as a hypothesis.|It was hypothesized that the drug would be effective in treating the disease.  据推测，这种药物可以有效治疗这种疾病。|Synonyms: postulated, theorized, conjectured, supposed, presumed|Antonyms: proven, demonstrated, confirmed, established|verb

hypothetical:/ˌhaɪpəˈθɛtɪkl/ (US) /ˌhaɪpəˈθetɪkəl/ (UK)| adj. |假设的，假定的|based on or serving as a hypothesis.|Let's consider a hypothetical situation.  让我们考虑一个假设的情况。|Synonyms: theoretical, conjectural, speculative, assumed, supposed|Antonyms: real, actual, factual, proven|adjective

ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin *hypothesizatus, past participle of hypothesizare "to hypothesize," from Ancient Greek ὑποτίθεσθαι (hupotíthesthai, “to suppose”), from ὑπό (hupó, “under”) + τίθεσθαι (títhesthai, “to put, place”).
