cartulary:/ˈkɑːrtjʊˌlɛri/
n.
案卷簿，图表集，文献集
A collection of charters or other documents relating to a monastery, church, or other institution, often bound into a volume.
The historian consulted the cartulary to verify the abbey's land holdings.  这位历史学家查阅了案卷簿，以核实修道院的土地所有权。
The cartulary provided valuable insights into the medieval economy.  这本案卷簿为了解中世纪经济提供了宝贵的资料。
Synonyms: archive, register, record
Antonyms: (No direct antonym, but perhaps) fabrication, fiction
