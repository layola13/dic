dervish:/ˈdɜːrvɪʃ/| n. |托钵僧，苦行僧|A member of a Muslim (especially Sufi) religious order who has taken vows of poverty and austerity.  They are known for their ascetic practices, which may include whirling dances as a form of devotion.|The dervish whirled ecstatically, eyes closed in prayer.|近义词：ascetic, mystic, monk|反义词:hedonist, materialist|词性:noun
Etymology: From Persian درویش (darvīsh), from Proto-Iranian *dru̯iš-, meaning "poor, needy".
