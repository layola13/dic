The word "emplead" appears to be a misspelling of the Spanish word "empleado" or the similarly spelled words in other Romance languages like Portuguese.  There is no English word "emplead".  I will therefore analyze the Spanish word "empleado".


empleado: /em.pleˈa.ðo/ (Spanish pronunciation) | n. adj.| 受雇的; 雇员，员工 | employed; employee |  例句：El empleado trabaja en la oficina. 这位员工在办公室工作。| 近义词：trabajador (worker), obrero (worker - more manual labor) | 反义词：empleador (employer), jefe (boss), desempleado (unemployed) | 词性: Noun (primarily), Adjective (can be used as an adjective)

empleado: /em.pleˈa.ða/ (Spanish pronunciation) | n. adj.| 受雇的; 女雇员，女员工 | employed; female employee |  例句：La empleada es muy eficiente.  这位女员工非常高效。| 近义词：trabajadora (female worker), obrera (female worker - more manual labor) | 反义词：empleadora (female employer), jefa (female boss), desempleada (unemployed female) | 词性: Noun (primarily), Adjective (can be used as an adjective)



**Etymology:**

From Spanish *emplear* ("to employ"), from Latin *implicāre* ("to involve, entangle, imply"), from *in-* + *plicāre* ("to fold").  The past participle *empleado* thus originally meant "involved" or "engaged," and came to mean "employed."


**USAGE (Examples using "qué" - "what" in Spanish - to illustrate question formation):**

**一般现在时 (Simple Present):**

¿Qué hace el empleado? = What does the employee do?

¿Qué idioma habla la empleada? = What language does the employee speak?


**现在进行时 (Present Continuous):**

¿Qué está haciendo el empleado ahora? = What is the employee doing now?

¿Qué está leyendo la empleada en este momento? = What is the employee reading at the moment?


**现在完成时 (Present Perfect):**
Spanish uses the preterite more often where English uses the present perfect, so a direct translation isn't always natural.  However, here are examples using the present perfect in Spanish:

¿Qué ha hecho el empleado hoy? = What has the employee done today?

¿Qué ha logrado la empleada hasta ahora? = What has the employee achieved so far?


**现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):**
This tense is less common in Spanish and often expressed with other constructions.

¿Qué ha estado haciendo el empleado todo el día? = What has the employee been doing all day?

¿En qué ha estado trabajando la empleada últimamente? = What has the employee been working on lately?


**一般过去时 (Simple Past - Preterite in Spanish):**

¿Qué hizo el empleado ayer? = What did the employee do yesterday?

¿Qué dijo la empleada en la reunión? = What did the employee say at the meeting?


**过去进行时 (Past Continuous - Imperfect in Spanish):**

¿Qué estaba haciendo el empleado cuando llamaste? = What was the employee doing when you called?

¿En qué estaba pensando la empleada? = What was the employee thinking about?


**过去完成时 (Past Perfect - Pluperfect in Spanish):**

¿Qué había hecho el empleado antes de que llegaras? = What had the employee done before you arrived?

¿Qué habían discutido las empleadas antes de la reunión? = What had the employees discussed before the meeting?


**过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):**
This is rarely used in Spanish.  Other constructions are generally preferred.

¿Qué había estado haciendo el empleado antes del accidente? = What had the employee been doing before the accident?

¿En qué habían estado trabajando las empleadas durante tanto tiempo? = What had the employees been working on for so long?



**一般将来时 (Simple Future):**

¿Qué hará el empleado mañana? = What will the employee do tomorrow?

¿Qué dirá la empleada en la presentación? = What will the employee say in the presentation?


**将来进行时 (Future Continuous):**

¿Qué estará haciendo el empleado a esta hora mañana? = What will the employee be doing this time tomorrow?

¿En qué estará trabajando la empleada la semana que viene?  = What will the employee be working on next week?


**将来完成时 (Future Perfect):**

¿Qué habrá logrado el empleado para el próximo año? = What will the employee have achieved by next year?

¿Qué habrá terminado la empleada para entonces? = What will the employee have finished by then?


**将来完成进行时 (Future Perfect Continuous):**
This is rarely used in Spanish.

¿Qué habrá estado haciendo el empleado durante diez años? = What will the employee have been doing for ten years?

¿En qué habrá estado trabajando la empleada para su graduación? = What will the employee have been working on for her graduation?
