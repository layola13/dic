discern:/dɪˈsɜːrn/ (US); /dɪˈsɜːn/ (UK)| v. |辨别，识别；察觉，发觉|To perceive or recognize something, often with difficulty. |例句：It was hard to discern his motives. 很难辨别他的动机。

例句：She could discern a faint light in the distance. 她能辨认出远处微弱的光。

近义词：perceive, detect, distinguish, recognize, differentiate|反义词：miss, overlook, ignore|词性：动词

discernible:/dɪˈsɜːnəbl/ (US); /dɪˈsɜːnɪbl/ (UK)| adj. |可辨别的，可识别的|Capable of being discerned; perceptible. |例句：There is a discernible difference between the two wines. 这两种葡萄酒之间有明显的区别。

例句：The path was barely discernible in the fading light.  小路在昏暗的光线下几乎难以辨认。

近义词：perceptible, noticeable, detectable, apparent, visible|反义词：imperceptible, invisible, unnoticeable|词性：形容词

discernment:/dɪˈsɜːnmənt/ (US); /dɪˈsɜːnmənt/ (UK)| n. |辨别力，识别力；洞察力|The ability to judge well. |例句：He showed great discernment in choosing his friends. 他在选择朋友方面表现出极强的洞察力。

例句：Her literary discernment is highly respected. 她的文学鉴赏力备受推崇。

近义词：judgment, insight, perception, acumen, wisdom|反义词：naiveté, foolishness, ignorance|词性：名词
