inching:/ˈɪntʃɪŋ/| v.|缓慢移动，一点一点地移动|To move slowly and carefully in a particular direction, especially when space is limited.|The snail was inching along the wall.  蜗牛正沿着墙缓慢爬行。|例句：He inched the car forward into the parking space. 他一点一点地把车挪进停车位。|近义词：creeping, edging, nudging|反义词：rushing, speeding|词性:动词

inching:/ˈɪntʃɪŋ/| n.|缓慢移动|The act of moving slowly and carefully.|The inching of the glacier carved out the valley. 冰川的缓慢移动雕琢出了山谷。|例句：The inching of the queue was frustrating. 队伍缓慢移动令人沮丧。|近义词：creep, nudge|反义词：rush, sprint|词性:名词


词源: From the verb "inch," which comes from Old English *ynce, from Latin uncia "twelfth part," related to ounce.
