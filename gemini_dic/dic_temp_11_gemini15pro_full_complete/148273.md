oversmoothly:/ˌoʊvərˈsmuːðli/ | adv. | 过于平滑地，过度平滑地 |  In a way that is too smooth, often suggesting a lack of authenticity or naturalness.  It implies an excessive smoothing, often to the point of being artificial or insincere. |  He oversmoothly denied any involvement in the scandal, which made him seem even more suspicious. 他过于平滑地否认了与丑闻有任何牵连，这让他看起来更加可疑。 | Synonyms: excessively smoothly, too smoothly | Antonyms: roughly, unevenly, jaggedly | word property: adverb

oversmooth:/ˌoʊvərˈsmuːð/ | v. | 过度平滑，过度修饰 | To make something too smooth, often resulting in a loss of character or detail.  It can refer to physical smoothing, like sanding wood too much, or to smoothing out something abstract, like a performance or a narrative.  | The editor oversmoothed the author's prose, removing all the quirks and personality. 编辑过度润色了作者的散文，去掉了所有的怪癖和个性。 | Synonyms: overpolish, overrefine | Antonyms: roughen, coarsen | word property: verb

词源:  "Oversmoothly" is derived from the verb "oversmooth," which is formed by adding the prefix "over-" (meaning "excessively" or "too much") to the adjective "smooth."  "Smooth" itself has Old English roots.
