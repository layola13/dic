carryon:/ˈkæriɒn/ (UK), /ˈkæriˌɑːn/ (US)| n.|随身行李；喧闹，骚动；吵闹，抱怨；（尤指不正当的）男女关系；（食物的）剩余部分|A small suitcase or bag that you can take onto a plane with you.  Also can refer to disruptive behaviour, a fuss, or an illicit affair.|例句：I put my carryon in the overhead compartment. 我把随身行李放在头顶的行李箱里。

例句：There was a terrible carryon when the airline lost their luggage. 航空公司弄丢了他们的行李，引起了一场可怕的骚动。|近义词：luggage, baggage (for the noun meaning), commotion, fuss, uproar (for the other meanings)|反义词: peace, quiet (for the commotion meaning)|词性:noun


carry on:/ˈkæri ɒn/ (UK), /ˈkæri ˌɑːn/ (US)| v.|继续；进行；从事；（与某人）有不正当的男女关系；表现得异常或愚蠢；吵闹，抱怨|To continue an activity or task. Also, to behave in an uncontrolled, excited, or foolish way.|例句：Please carry on with your work. 请继续你的工作。

例句：He was carrying on about the unfairness of it all. 他一直在抱怨这一切的不公平。|近义词：continue, proceed, persist, behave badly|反义词：stop, cease, halt|词性:verb
