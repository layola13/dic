jump-the-shark:/ˈdʒʌmp ðə ʃɑːrk/| idiom (noun phrase)| 巅峰不再；走下坡路；过气；衰落 |  Refers to the moment when something that was once popular or successful begins to decline in quality or popularity. It is often used to describe television shows, but can be applied to other forms of media, products, or even people.  The phrase originated from a scene in the TV show "Happy Days" where Fonzie literally jumped over a shark on water skis.  The scene was seen as a sign that the show had become outlandish and was losing its way. | The Simpsons have jumped the shark several times, but they always seem to bounce back.  《辛普森一家》已经好几次走下坡路了，但他们似乎总是能恢复过来。

Many critics believe the show jumped the shark when they introduced the new character. 许多评论家认为，这部剧在引入了新角色后就开始走下坡路了。 |  decline, go downhill, lose its touch, run out of steam,  become stale |  improve,  get better,  gain popularity,  revive | idiom
