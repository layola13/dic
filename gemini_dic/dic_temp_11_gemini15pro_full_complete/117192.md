measuredly:/ˈmeʒərdli/| adv.|慎重地；谨慎地；适度地|In a measured and deliberate manner; carefully and slowly, without excess. |She spoke measuredly, choosing her words with care. 她说话谨慎，字斟句酌。|He walked measuredly across the room, as if trying not to disturb anyone. 他小心翼翼地走过房间，仿佛不想打扰任何人。|carefully, deliberately, cautiously, slowly, deliberately, steadily|rashly, hastily, impulsively, carelessly|adverb

ETYMOLOGY->
From "measured" + "-ly".  "Measured" comes from the verb "measure" (from Old French "mesurer," ultimately from Latin "mensus," past participle of "metiri" "to measure") + the suffix "-ed" forming the past participle and adjective. The suffix "-ly" transforms adjectives into adverbs.
