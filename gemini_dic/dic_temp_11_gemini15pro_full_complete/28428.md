carbonizing:/ˈkɑːrbənaɪzɪŋ/
verb
碳化，炭化处理
The process of heating a substance in the absence of air to drive off volatile components and leave a residue of carbon.
The wood was carbonized in a kiln. 木头在窑中被碳化。
Synonyms: charring, coking
Antonyms:  None (The opposite process would be something like "purifying" or "decarbonizing," but these are not direct antonyms.)
verb

carbonize:/ˈkɑːrbənaɪz/
verb
碳化，炭化处理
To heat a substance in the absence of air to drive off volatile components and leave a residue of carbon.
The intense heat carbonized the wood.  高温使木头碳化了。
Synonyms: char, coke
Antonyms: None (Similar to the -ing form, there isn't a direct antonym.)
verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From the French word "carboniser," derived from "carbone" (carbon) + "-iser" (to make). Ultimately from Latin "carbo" (charcoal).
