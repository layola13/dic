cavezon:/ˌkævəˈzɒn/ (US), /ˌkævəˈzɒn/ (UK)| n.|  嚼子，笼头|A type of noseband used on horses, often with a lever action, designed to exert pressure on the nose and jaw. It is used as a training aid or for horses that are difficult to control.|The rider used a cavezon to control the young horse.|Synonyms: noseband, hackamore (loosely)|Antonyms: bridle (though not a direct opposite, it's another type of headgear)|noun
