dismal:/ˈdɪzməl/| adj.| 阴沉的，凄凉的，忧郁的，令人沮丧的| causing gloom or depression; dreary; cheerless; causing a lack of hope; gloomy| The weather was dismal, with gray skies and constant rain. 天气阴沉沉的，天空灰蒙蒙的，还下着不停的雨。|  The economic forecast is dismal. 经济预测令人沮丧。 | synonyms: bleak, dreary, gloomy, somber, depressing, cheerless | antonyms: cheerful, bright, sunny, optimistic, hopeful | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old French *dismal (modern French *dixmal*), "tenth, tithe," from Late Latin *decimus* ("tenth"), from Latin *decem* ("ten").  The original sense was related to paying tithes, which was apparently perceived as depressing. The meaning shifted to "gloomy" or "depressing" over time.
