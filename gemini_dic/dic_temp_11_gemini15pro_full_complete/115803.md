marjoram: /ˈmɑːrdʒərəm/ (US), /ˈmɑːdʒərəm/ (UK)| n. |墨角兰; 马郁兰|An aromatic herb of the mint family, used as a culinary herb, especially in Mediterranean cuisine. It has a warm, slightly bitter flavor.|The lamb was seasoned with marjoram and thyme.|近义词: oregano, thyme|反义词: N/A|词性: noun

Etymology:
From Old French *majorane, from Medieval Latin majorana, perhaps from Latin amaracus (“marjoram”).
