indolence: /ˈɪndələns/ | n. | 懒惰，怠惰 | Habitual laziness; inactivity resulting from a dislike of work. | He was fired for indolence. 他因为懒惰被解雇了。|  Synonyms: laziness, sloth, idleness, inactivity, shiftlessness | Antonyms: diligence, industry, activity, energy | noun

indolent: /ˈɪndələnt/ | adj. | 懒惰的，好逸恶劳的 | Wanting to avoid activity or exertion; lazy. |  She is an indolent person who spends most of the time sleeping. 她是一个懒惰的人，大部分时间都在睡觉。| Synonyms: lazy, idle, slothful, inactive, shiftless | Antonyms: diligent, industrious, active, energetic | adjective


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *indolence*, from Latin *indolentia* "freedom from pain; insensibility," from *in-* "not" (see in- (1)) + *dolentia* "pain, grief," from *dolēre* "to feel pain, grieve." The sense shift in Latin from "freedom from pain" to "ease, comfort" led to the modern meaning.
