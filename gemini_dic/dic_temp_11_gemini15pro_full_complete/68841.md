fashion:/ˈfæʃən/ (US), /ˈfæʃn/ (UK)| n. v. adj.|时尚，流行；方式，方法|A popular trend, especially in styles of dress and ornament or manners of behavior.  A manner or way of doing something.|

例句：
The latest fashion is to wear bright colors. = 最新的时尚是穿鲜艳的颜色。

She always dresses in the latest fashions. = 她总是穿着最新的时装。


近义词：trend, style, vogue, mode, manner, way|
反义词：old-fashioned, outdated, unfashionable|
词性：名词 (n.)


fashion:/ˈfæʃən/ (US), /ˈfæʃn/ (UK)| v.|制作，塑造|To make or shape something, usually skillfully.|

例句：
He fashioned a small boat out of wood. = 他用木头做了一艘小船。

The sculptor fashioned the clay into a beautiful figure. = 雕塑家用粘土塑造了一个美丽的形象。


近义词：create, make, form, shape, construct|
反义词：destroy, dismantle, ruin|
词性：动词 (v.)


fashion:/ˈfæʃən/ (US), /ˈfæʃn/ (UK)| adj.|流行的|Characteristic of or conforming to the current fashion.|

例句：
This is a fashion item. = 这是一个时尚单品。

Fashion shoes are often uncomfortable. = 时尚的鞋子通常不舒服。

近义词：stylish, trendy, fashionable|
反义词：unfashionable, outdated|
词性：形容词 (adj.)



词源: From Old French *façon (“fashion, manner, shape”), from Latin factiō (“a making, a doing, a faction”), from faciō (“I do, I make”).
