kite:/kaɪt/| n.|风筝；鸢|A toy consisting of a light frame with thin material stretched over it, flown in the wind at the end of a long string.|The children were flying a kite in the park. 孩子们在公园里放风筝。|Synonyms:  glider, bird (informal)|Antonyms: N/A|noun

kite:/kaɪt/| v. |使用空头支票骗钱；(股票价格) 暴涨|To defraud by using bad checks or to increase rapidly (as in price). |The stock price kited after the positive news.  利好消息一出，股价暴涨。|Synonyms: soar, rocket, escalate |Antonyms: plummet, crash, tumble|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *cyta (unattested), akin to Old Norse kīta (“female cat”). The bird-of-prey sense emerged in the 15th century, perhaps influenced by the bird's hovering, kite-like flight. The sense of “toy” developed from this, referring to the bird-like appearance of the toy.
