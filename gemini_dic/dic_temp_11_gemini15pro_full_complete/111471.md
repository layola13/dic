load-shedding:/ˈləʊd ʃɛdɪŋ/
noun
停电，拉闸限电
The deliberate shutdown of electric power in a part of a power-distribution system, typically to prevent the failure of the entire system when the demand strains the capacity of the system.
South Africa has been experiencing frequent load-shedding due to insufficient power generation. 由于发电不足，南非一直频繁停电。
Power outage, rolling blackout, brownout
Continuous power supply
