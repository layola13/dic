anthracite:/ˈænθrəˌsaɪt/| n.|无烟煤|A hard, compact variety of coal that has a high luster and burns with little flame or smoke. It is the highest rank of coal based on carbon content.|The blacksmith used anthracite in his forge because it burns with intense heat.|Near synonyms: hard coal, black coal, stone coal|Near antonyms: lignite, peat|noun
