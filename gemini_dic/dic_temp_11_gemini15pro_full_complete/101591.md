irradiatingly:/ɪˈreɪdieɪtɪŋli/
adverb
光芒四射地，容光焕发地
in a way that emits light or heat; radiantly; brightly.  Giving off or reflecting much light.
Her face was irradiatingly happy as she announced her engagement. 她宣布订婚时，脸上洋溢着幸福的光芒。
Synonyms: radiantly, brightly, luminously, brilliantly, dazzlingly
Antonyms: dimly, darkly, dully
词性: 副词

irradiate:/ɪˈreɪdieɪt/
verb
照射，照耀；放射；使焕发
1. (with reference to light or heat) shine brightly; emit light or heat. 2. expose to radiation. 3. (of a person's face or expression) show (a particular emotion) clearly.
The sun irradiated the fields. 太阳照耀着田野。
Synonyms: illuminate, shine, beam, radiate
Antonyms: darken, obscure, dim
词性: 动词

radiation:/ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃn/
noun
辐射；放射线；放射物
the emission of energy as electromagnetic waves or as moving subatomic particles, especially high-energy particles that cause ionization.
Exposure to radiation can be harmful. 暴露在辐射下可能是有害的。
Synonyms: emission, emanation, radioactivity
Antonyms: absorption
词性: 名词
