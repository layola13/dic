guilefulness:/ˈɡaɪlfəlnəs/
|n.|狡猾，诡诈|The guilefulness of the fox allowed it to steal the chickens. 狐狸的狡猾使它偷走了鸡。|Etymology: From "guileful" + "-ness". "Guile" comes from Old French "guile" and ultimately from Germanic roots meaning "trickery" or "deceit".|例句：His guilefulness made him a successful spy. 他的狡猾使他成为一名成功的间谍。

例句：The politician's guilefulness was evident in his evasive answers.  这位政客的狡猾体现在他闪烁其词的回答中。


近义词：cunning, craftiness, slyness, deceitfulness, trickery|反义词：honesty, sincerity, forthrightness, candor|词性：名词

guileful:/ˈɡaɪlfʊl/
|adj.|狡猾的，诡诈的|A guileful plan was hatched to overthrow the king.  一个狡猾的计划被策划出来，意图推翻国王。|Etymology: From "guile" + "-ful". "Guile" comes from Old French "guile" and ultimately from Germanic roots meaning "trickery" or "deceit".|例句：The guileful salesman convinced her to buy the overpriced car.  狡猾的推销员说服她买了那辆标价过高的汽车。


例句：Be wary of his guileful charm; he's not as innocent as he seems.  要小心他狡猾的魅力；他不像看起来那么单纯。

近义词：cunning, crafty, sly, deceitful, tricky|反义词：honest, sincere, forthright, candid|词性：形容词
