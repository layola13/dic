cinematograph: /ˌsɪnɪˈmætəɡræf/ (UK), /ˌsɪnɪˈmætəɡræf/ (US)
n.|电影摄影机; 电影放映机; 电影术|A motion-picture camera, projector, or the process of making films.|The Lumière brothers patented their cinematograph in 1895.  卢米埃尔兄弟在1895年获得了他们的电影摄影机的专利。|Synonyms: movie camera, film projector, motion-picture camera|Antonyms: None|noun

cinematograph: /ˌsɪnɪˈmætəɡræf/ (UK), /ˌsɪnɪˈmætəɡræf/ (US)
v.|拍摄电影|To film or photograph a motion picture.|They cinematographed the entire performance. 他们拍摄了整场演出。|Synonyms: film, shoot, record|Antonyms: None|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From French *cinématographe*, from Ancient Greek *κίνημα* (*kínēma*, “movement”) + *γράφειν* (*gráphein*, “to write, record”).
