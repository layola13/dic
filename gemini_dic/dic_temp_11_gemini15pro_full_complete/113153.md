lupercal:/ˈluːpərkl/ | n. | 罗马古城的卢珀卡尔洞穴; 牧神节 | A cave on the Palatine Hill in Rome, traditionally believed to be the place where Romulus and Remus, the legendary founders of Rome, were suckled by a she-wolf.  It was also associated with the festival of Lupercalia. |  The annual festival of Lupercalia was celebrated at the lupercal. 每年一度的卢珀卡利亚节在卢珀卡尔洞穴举行。| 近义词: cave, grotto | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, perhaps consider "summit" if contrasting location) | 词性: noun
