glacier:/ˈɡleɪʃər/ (US), /ˈɡlæsiə/ (UK)| n. |冰川；冰河|A large mass of ice formed from snow on high ground, which moves very slowly down a mountain or valley.|例句：The glacier carved a deep valley into the mountainside. 冰川在山坡上刻出了一条深谷。

例句：Glaciers are melting at an alarming rate due to climate change. 由于气候变化，冰川正在以惊人的速度融化。|近义词：ice field, ice cap|反义词:N/A|词性:noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From French *glacier*, from Franco-Provençal *glaciér*, from Vulgar Latin *glaciārium*, from Latin *glacies* ("ice").  Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *ǵleyH-/*ǵliH- ("to shine, be greasy, sticky").
