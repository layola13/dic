backshift:/ˈbækʃɪft/| n.|回移，后移; (时态)倒退|A backshift is a change from a present tense to a past tense, typically when reporting speech.  It reflects the change in perspective from the original speaker to the person reporting the speech.|He said he was going to the store. (Backshift from "I am going to the store.") 他说他要去商店（从“我要去商店”倒退）。|近义词:sequence of tenses|反义词:无|词性:名词

USAGE->

一般现在时 (Simple Present):

He said that he works at a bank. 他说他在银行工作。

She told me she loves chocolate. 她告诉我她喜欢巧克力。


现在进行时 (Present Continuous):

He said he was watching TV. 他说他正在看电视。

She told me she was going to the store. 她告诉我她正要去商店。


现在完成时 (Present Perfect):

He said he has finished his homework. 他说他已经完成了作业。

She told me she has been to Paris. 她告诉我她去过巴黎。


现在完成进行时 (Present Perfect Continuous):

He said he had been working on the project all day. 他说他整天都在做这个项目。

She told me she had been waiting for an hour. 她告诉我她已经等了一个小时了。

She told me she saw a bird. 她告诉我她看到了一只鸟。


过去进行时 (Past Continuous):

过去完成时 (Past Perfect):

过去完成进行时 (Past Perfect Continuous):

She told me she had been waiting for him all day. 她告诉我她等了他一整天。

She told me she would call me later. 她告诉我她会晚点给我打电话。


将来进行时 (Future Continuous):
