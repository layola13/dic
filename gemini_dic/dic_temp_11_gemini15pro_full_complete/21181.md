block-in: /blɑːkˈɪn/ | n. | 草图；概略；(尤指戏剧的)走台排练 | A rough sketch or outline, especially a rehearsal of a play in which the actors move through their parts without memorizing lines. | The director did a quick block-in of the first scene to establish the actors' positions. 导演快速地排练了第一场戏，以确定演员的位置。|  The artist did a quick block-in of the landscape before starting the detailed painting. 艺术家在开始详细绘画之前快速地画了一幅风景草图。| synonym: outline, sketch, draft, rehearsal | antonym: finished product, final version | noun

block-in: /blɑːkˈɪn/ | v. |  画草图；概略地描述；(尤指戏剧的)走台排练  | To create a rough sketch or outline, especially to rehearse a play by moving through the parts without memorizing lines. | They blocked in the scene to get a feel for the pacing. 他们排练了这场戏，以感受一下节奏。 |  She blocked in the basic composition of the painting with charcoal. 她用木炭画出了这幅画的基本构图。 | synonym: sketch, outline, draft, rehearse | antonym: finalize, complete, perfect | verb
