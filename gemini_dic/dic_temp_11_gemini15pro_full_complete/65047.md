etching:/ˈetʃɪŋ/
n.|蚀刻;蚀刻画;蚀刻术|The process of using strong acid to cut into the unprotected parts of a metal surface to create a design or picture, or a picture produced in this way. |The museum has a fine collection of Rembrandt etchings. 该博物馆收藏了伦勃朗的许多精美蚀刻画。|近义词：engraving, carving|反义词:painting|词性:noun

etching:/ˈetʃɪŋ/
v.|蚀刻|to cut lines into a piece of glass, metal, etc. in order to make a picture or words|He etched his name on the glass. 他把他的名字蚀刻在玻璃上。|近义词：engrave, carve|反义词:paint|词性:verb

ETYMOLOGY->
From Dutch *etsen*, frequentative of *eten* "to eat," from Proto-Germanic *etaną* "to eat."  The notion is of the acid "eating" into the metal.
