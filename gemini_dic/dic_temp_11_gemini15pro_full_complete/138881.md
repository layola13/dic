nurturers:/ˈnɜːrtʃərərz/ | n. | 养育者，培育者 |  People who encourage and support the growth and development of others, especially children or young people.  |  The nurturers in the community work hard to provide a safe and supportive environment for children. 社区的养育者们努力为孩子们提供安全和支持的环境。 | synonyms: caregivers, guardians, mentors, teachers | antonyms: neglecters, abusers | noun

nurture:/ˈnɜːrtʃər/ | v. | 养育，培育 | To care for and encourage the growth or development of. |  She nurtured her plants with care and attention. 她细心照料她的植物。 | synonyms: cultivate, foster, raise, rear | antonyms: neglect, stifle, hinder | verb

nurture:/ˈnɜːrtʃər/ | n. | 养育，培育 | The process of caring for and encouraging the growth or development of someone or something. |  The nurture she received as a child shaped her into the person she is today. 她儿时受到的养育塑造了她今天的性格。| synonyms: upbringing, training, development, cultivation | antonyms: neglect, deprivation | noun
