corralling:/kəˈrælɪŋ/
verb
围拢，聚集
The cowboys were corralling the cattle for branding. 牛仔们正把牛群赶到一起准备烙印。
The teacher corralled the students into the auditorium. 老师把学生们聚集到礼堂里。
Synonyms: gathering, herding, rounding up, assembling
Antonyms: scattering, dispersing
v.

corralling:/kəˈrælɪŋ/
noun
围拢，聚集（的行为）
The corralling of the wild horses was a difficult task.  围捕野马是一项艰巨的任务。
Synonyms: gathering, herding, round-up
Antonyms: dispersal
n.


ETYMOLOGY->
From Spanish *corral* (noun, meaning "yard, enclosure") and ultimately from Latin *currere* (verb, meaning "to run"). The original sense referred to a pen or enclosure for livestock, and the verb sense developed from the act of driving animals into such an enclosure.
