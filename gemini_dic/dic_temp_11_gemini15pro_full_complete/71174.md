fissirostres:/fɪˈsɪrəˌstriːz/ | n. | 裂口目鸟类 |  An outdated taxonomic group of birds characterized by a wide gape, typically insect-eating birds like swallows, nightjars, and swifts.  This term is no longer widely used in modern ornithology. |  Fissirostres were once thought to be a distinct order of birds.  裂口目鸟类曾被认为是一个独特的鸟类目。|  Obsolete classifications listed nightjars as fissirostres.  过时的分类将夜鹰列为裂口目鸟类。|  Near synonyms (based on older classifications):  Caprimulgiformes, Apodiformes |  Antonyms:  N/A (No direct antonym for a taxonomic group) | Noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *fissus* (cleft, split) + *rostrum* (beak, bill)
