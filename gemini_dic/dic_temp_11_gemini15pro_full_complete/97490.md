ingenuousnesses:/ɪnˈdʒɛnjuːəsnɪsɪz/
|n.| 天真烂漫，纯真无邪 |The state or quality of being ingenuous; artlessness; frankness; sincerity.  |Her ingenuousnesses was charming, but also made her vulnerable. 她的天真烂漫很迷人，但也让她容易受伤害。|Synonyms: innocence, naiveté, artlessness, candor, simplicity |Antonyms: guile, cunning, sophistication, worldliness|Noun

ETYMOLOGY->
From Middle French *ingénuité*, from Latin *ingenuus* ("native, freeborn, noble, frank, honest").  The suffix *-ness* is used to form nouns from adjectives, indicating a state, quality, or condition.
