marginalise:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəlaɪz/ /ˈmɑːrɡənəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使边缘化；使处于社会边缘；忽视；排斥 | To treat (a person, group, or concept) as insignificant or peripheral. |例句：The new policy marginalized the elderly. 这项新政策使老年人边缘化。

例句：Refugees are often marginalized by society. 难民经常被社会边缘化。

近义词：exclude, isolate, sideline, ostracize | 反义词：include, integrate, centralize | 词性：动词

marginalized:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəlaɪzd/ /ˈmɑːrɡənəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 边缘化的；处于社会边缘的 | Treated as insignificant or peripheral. | 例句：Marginalized groups often face discrimination. 边缘化群体经常面临歧视。

例句：She felt marginalized in the male-dominated industry. 在男性主导的行业中，她感到自己被边缘化了。

近义词：excluded, isolated, sidelined | 反义词：included, integrated, central | 词性：形容词

marginalization:/ˌmɑːrdʒɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ /ˌmɑːrɡənəlaɪˈzeɪʃn/ | n. | 边缘化 | The process of making somebody feel as if they are not important and cannot influence decisions or events; the act of marginalizing somebody or something. | 例句：The report discusses the marginalization of women in the workplace.  这份报告讨论了职场女性的边缘化。

例句：Economic marginalization can lead to social unrest. 经济边缘化会导致社会动荡。

近义词：exclusion, isolation | 反义词：inclusion, integration | 词性：名词


词源: From margin (边缘) + -alize (使...化)
