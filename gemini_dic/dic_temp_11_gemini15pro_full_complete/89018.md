hollows:/ˈhɒləʊz/ (UK), /ˈhɑːloʊz/ (US)| n.|低洼地，山谷；洞，窟窿|Low-lying areas, often surrounded by higher ground; depressions or cavities in a surface.|The deer grazed in the hollows of the hills. 鹿在山间的低洼处吃草。|synonyms: valleys, depressions, basins, cavities, holes|antonyms: peaks, summits, ridges|noun

hollow:/ˈhɒləʊ/ (UK), /ˈhɑːloʊ/ (US)| adj.|空的，中空的；空洞的，虚伪的|Having an empty space inside; lacking sincerity or meaning.|The tree trunk was hollow. 树干是空的。  His promises were hollow. 他的承诺是空洞的。|synonyms: empty, vacant, void, insincere, meaningless|antonyms: full, solid, sincere, meaningful|adjective

hollow:/ˈhɒləʊ/ (UK), /ˈhɑːloʊ/ (US)| v.|挖空，使凹陷|To make a hole or depression in something.|The birds hollowed out a nest in the tree. 鸟儿在树上挖了一个窝。|synonyms: excavate, gouge, scoop|antonyms: fill, pack|verb


ETYMOLOGY->
From Old English *holh, from Proto-Germanic *hulwaz, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kelH- (“to bend, curve”).  Cognates include German hohl, Dutch hol, and Gothic 𐌷𐌿𐌻𐍃 (huls).  The sense of "insincere" developed later, perhaps influenced by the idea of emptiness.
