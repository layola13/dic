ill-advisedly: /ˌɪl ədˈvaɪzɪdli/
adverb
不明智地；欠考虑地
in a way that is not sensible or wise; unwisely.
She ill-advisedly told her boss what she really thought of him. 她不明智地告诉了老板她对他的真实看法。
He invested his money ill-advisedly in a failing company. 他不明智地把钱投资于一家快要倒闭的公司。
近义词: unwisely, foolishly, imprudently, rashly
反义词: wisely, prudently, judiciously
词性: adverb

词源:
From ill-advised + -ly.  "Ill-advised" itself comes from "ill" (meaning bad or harmful) + "advised" (past participle of "advise"). The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective "ill-advised" into the adverb "ill-advisedly".
