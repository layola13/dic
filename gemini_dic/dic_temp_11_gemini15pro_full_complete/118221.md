mercantilism: /ˈmɜːrkəntɪlɪzəm/ | n. | 重商主义 | An economic theory and practice common in Europe from the 16th to the 18th centuries that promoted governmental regulation of a nation's economy for the purpose of augmenting state power at the expense of rival national powers. It was the economic counterpart of political absolutism.  |  Mercantilism was a dominant economic system in Europe for centuries.  重商主义在欧洲几个世纪以来一直是占主导地位的经济体系。 |  Near Synonyms:  protectionism, commercialism | Antonyms: free trade, laissez-faire | noun
