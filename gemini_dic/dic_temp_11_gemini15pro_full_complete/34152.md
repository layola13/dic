clam:/klæm/| n. v.|蛤蜊；沉默；守口如瓶；(非正式)美元|A marine bivalve mollusk with shells of equal size. (noun)  To become silent suddenly. (verb) To refuse to speak, especially when you know something that other people want to know. (verb)|例句：We dug clams at the beach. 我们在海滩挖蛤蜊。|近义词：mollusk, shellfish (n.); hush, quiet (v.)|反义词:speak, talk (v.)|词性:noun, verb
clam:/klæm/| v.| 挖蛤; 闭嘴; 坚持要; 紧紧抓住|To dig for or collect clams. To suddenly become silent; to refuse to speak. To stubbornly insist on something. To hold onto something tightly.|例句：He clammed up when asked about the incident. 当被问及此事时，他闭口不言。|近义词：hush, quiet|反义词:speak, talk|词性:verb
