brickfielder:/ˈbrɪkˌfildər/| n.|砖匠风，砖场风；干燥炎热的北风或西北风（澳大利亚俚语）|A hot, dry, dusty north wind or northwest wind that blows in southeastern Australia, especially in the summer.  It is so called because it seems to blow from the brickfields around Sydney.|例句：The brickfielder blew all day, making it unbearably hot.  砖场风刮了一整天，热得让人难以忍受。

例句：The sudden brickfielder coated everything in a fine layer of red dust. 突如其来的砖匠风把一切都覆盖上了一层细细的红尘。|近义词：desert wind, dust storm, northerly (in Australia)|反义词:southerly (in Australia)|词性:noun


ETYMOLOGY->
From "brickfield" (a field where bricks are made) + "-er" (suffix forming nouns denoting things associated with a particular place or activity).  The wind was thought to originate in the brickfields west of Sydney.
