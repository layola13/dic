repellent: /rɪˈpɛlənt/ | adj., n. | 驱虫剂；令人厌恶的；防水的 |

英文解析:
As an adjective, "repellent" means causing strong dislike or disgust; highly objectionable. As a noun, it typically refers to a substance that repels or keeps away insects or other pests. It can also describe something that repels water, making it waterproof.

例句:
The smell was utterly repellent.  = 那种气味极其令人作呕。

The new fabric is water repellent. = 这种新型面料具有防水功能。

近义词:
(adj.) repulsive, disgusting, loathsome, objectionable, waterproof
(n.) insect repellent, pesticide, deterrent

反义词:
(adj.) attractive, appealing, pleasant, alluring
(n.) attractant


词性: adjective, noun

词源:
From the verb "repel" (to drive or force back),  plus the suffix "-ent" (forming adjectives).  "Repel" itself comes from the Old French "repeler," from the Latin "re-" (back) + "pellere" (to drive, push).
