insulars:/ˈɪnsjʊlərz/ | n. | 岛民；岛屿居民 | People who live on islands; those who are isolated or narrow-minded.  Insulars often develop unique cultures and traditions due to their geographical isolation.  However, the term can also carry a negative connotation, suggesting narrow-mindedness or a lack of exposure to different perspectives. | Example: The insulars of the remote Pacific island maintained their ancient customs for centuries.  岛屿偏远的太平洋岛屿的居民几个世纪以来一直保持着他们的古老习俗。  The debate highlighted the insular attitudes of some members of the committee. 这场辩论突显了委员会一些成员的偏狭态度。|近义词：islanders, provincials |反义词：cosmopolitans, global citizens
