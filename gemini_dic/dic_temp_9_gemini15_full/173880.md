quasi-regulated: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈreɡjʊleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 半受管制的，准受管制的 |  Nearly or seemingly regulated; subject to some but not all the controls or regulations of a fully regulated system.  It implies a level of control that is less strict or comprehensive than true regulation. |


例句：

The quasi-regulated market experienced significant price fluctuations. = 半受管制的市场经历了剧烈的价格波动。

The industry operates under a quasi-regulated framework, leading to inconsistencies. = 该行业在半受管制的框架下运作，导致了不一致性。


近义词：partially regulated, loosely regulated, semi-regulated


反义词：fully regulated, unregulated, uncontrolled


词源：

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."

* **regulated**: From the verb "regulate," ultimately deriving from the Latin *regula* ("rule," "straight line").


USAGE->

This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations or tenses like "what" does.  The grammatical usage depends on the sentence's structure.  Examples illustrating usage in different sentence types are below:


肯定句:

The financial sector is quasi-regulated in some countries. = 在一些国家，金融部门是半受管制的。

This quasi-regulated industry needs more oversight. = 这个半受管制的行业需要更多的监督。

否定句:

The market is not fully regulated; it’s quasi-regulated at best. = 这个市场并非完全受管制；充其量只是半受管制的。

The system isn't completely unregulated, but it is certainly quasi-regulated and lacks proper enforcement. = 这个系统并非完全不受管制，但它肯定是半受管制的，并且缺乏适当的执法。


疑问句:

Is this industry truly quasi-regulated, or is it simply poorly regulated? = 这个行业真的是半受管制的，还是仅仅监管不力？

Are the benefits of a quasi-regulated system outweighing its risks? = 半受管制系统的益处是否超过了它的风险？
