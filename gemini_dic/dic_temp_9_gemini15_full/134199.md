nonincrimination:/ˌnɒnɪnˌkrɪmɪˈneɪʃən/| n. |免于指控；不予起诉；自证无罪权|The protection of someone from being compelled to incriminate themselves.  This is a fundamental legal right in many jurisdictions, often enshrined in constitutions or statutes.  It prevents the government from forcing individuals to testify against themselves in criminal proceedings. |Example: The Fifth Amendment of the US Constitution guarantees the right of nonincrimination. 美国宪法第五修正案保障自证无罪权。

The right against self-incrimination ensures that individuals cannot be forced to provide evidence that could lead to their conviction.  This prevents coercion and protects against the possibility of false confessions or accusations.

|近义词：immunity from prosecution, protection from self-incrimination|反义词：compulsion to testify, forced confession|名词
