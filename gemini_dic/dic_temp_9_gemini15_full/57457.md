draft:/dræft/| n. v.| 草稿；汇票；征兵；起草；选拔；|A preliminary version of a piece of writing, a plan, or a design.|例句：I've finished the first draft of my essay. 我已经完成了我的论文初稿。|例句：The team drafted a new strategy.  团队起草了一个新战略。|近义词：sketch, outline, plan,  |反义词：final version, finished product


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drafts a letter every week. = 他每周都起草一封信。
She drafts new marketing plans. = 她起草新的营销计划。

一般过去时:
They drafted a new constitution. = 他们起草了一部新宪法。
He drafted a response to the email. = 他起草了一封回复邮件。

现在完成时:
She has drafted several proposals this month. = 这个月她已经起草了几份提案。
We have drafted a preliminary budget. = 我们已经起草了一份初步预算。

过去完成时:
They had drafted the agreement before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前已经起草好了协议。
She had drafted the report by the deadline. = 她在截止日期前已经起草好了报告。

将来时:
He will draft a speech for the conference. = 他将为会议起草一篇演讲稿。
They will draft a new policy next week. = 他们下周将起草一项新政策。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would draft a longer report. = 如果我有更多时间，我会起草一份更长的报告。
I wish I had drafted a better proposal. = 我希望我起草了一个更好的提案。

被动语态:
The contract was drafted by the lawyer. = 合同是由律师起草的。
The proposal is being drafted by the committee. = 提案正在由委员会起草。

疑问句:
Have you drafted the letter yet? = 你已经起草好信了吗？
Who drafted this report? = 谁起草了这份报告？

否定句:
I haven't drafted the email yet. = 我还没起草好邮件。
They didn't draft the plan properly. = 他们没有恰当地起草计划。


PHRASE->
draft a document: 起草文件
draft a bill: 起草法案
draft a contract: 起草合同
draft a response: 起草回复
draft a budget: 起草预算


词源:
From Old French *drafter, from draf, variant of traire "to draw".  The original meaning referred to the act of drawing liquid, then extended to the act of drawing up a written document.
