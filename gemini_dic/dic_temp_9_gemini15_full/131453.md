noncogently:/ˌnɒnˈkɒdʒəntli/ | adv. | 不令人信服地；论证无力地 |  Failing to compel belief; unconvincingly.  It refers to an argument or reasoning that lacks force or persuasiveness.  | 例句：He argued noncogently for the new policy, failing to address the key objections. 他为这项新政策辩论得无力，未能解决关键异议。


The argument was presented noncogently, leaving the audience unconvinced.  论证不令人信服，使听众无法信服。| 近义词：inconclusively, weakly, unconvincingly | 反义词：cogently, persuasively, convincingly
