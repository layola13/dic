legend:/ˈlɛdʒənd/ | n. | 传说，传奇人物；图例 | A legend is a traditional story, especially one explaining some natural or historical event, and typically involving supernatural beings or events.  It can also refer to a person who is famous and admired for their achievements. | 例句：The legend of King Arthur is well-known.  亚瑟王的故事广为人知。  | 例句：She became a legend in the music industry. 她成为了音乐界的传奇人物。 | 近义词：myth, story, tale, fable, icon | 反义词：reality, fact

词源:
From Old French *legende, from Latin legenda "things to be read," plural of legendum "something to be read," from legere "to read."  The shift in meaning from "things to be read" to "stories" is a natural progression, as legends were often read aloud and passed down through oral tradition.
