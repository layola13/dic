The word "draughts" is not correctly written as "draughts's".  The apostrophe-s ('s) is used to show possession.  "Draughts" itself already represents a noun, so adding "'s" is grammatically incorrect.  We need to analyze "draughts" instead.


/drɑːfts/ | n. |  国际象棋(英式)/跳棋 |  A board game played by two people on a checkered board, using pieces that are moved diagonally.  In British English, it typically refers to checkers; in American English, it's "checkers".  It can also refer to a draft of air or a draught of liquid. | Example:  He enjoys playing draughts in the evenings. 他喜欢晚上玩跳棋。  They had a refreshing draught of cold lemonade. 他们喝了一杯清爽的冰柠檬水。 | 近义词: checkers (AmE), draughts (BrE),  draft (air), gulp (liquid) | 反义词: (no direct antonyms, depends on context)
