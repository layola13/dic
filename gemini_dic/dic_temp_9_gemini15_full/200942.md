slipway:/ˈslɪpweɪ/ | n. |滑道；造船台 | A slipway is a paved or concrete slope leading down to water, used for launching or hauling boats.  It's often found in boatyards or harbors. | Example: The small fishing boat was launched from the slipway. 这艘小渔船是从滑道上发射的。|近义词：launching ramp, boat ramp | 反义词：dry dock

词源:
The word "slipway" combines "slip," meaning to slide or move easily, and "way," meaning a path or road.  Therefore, a slipway is literally a path for sliding.  The etymology of "slip" traces back to Old English *slipan,  and "way"  to Old English weg.
