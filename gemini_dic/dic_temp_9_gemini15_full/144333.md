词源：
The word "outstaggered" is formed from the prefix "out-" (meaning "out of" or "beyond") and the verb "staggered," the past tense of "stagger."  "Stagger" itself has origins in Old Norse and Old English, ultimately related to words suggesting unsteadiness or stumbling.
