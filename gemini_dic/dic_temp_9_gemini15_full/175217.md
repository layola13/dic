raiding:/ˈreɪdɪŋ/| v.|袭击；抢劫；搜查|The act of making a surprise attack on a place or people, typically to steal something or to cause damage.  It can also refer to searching a place thoroughly, often for evidence.|例句：The Vikings were known for their raiding of coastal towns.  维京人以袭击沿海城镇而闻名。

例句：Police raided the suspect's house looking for drugs.  警方搜查了嫌疑人的住所以寻找毒品。

近义词：attacking, pillaging, plundering, searching

反义词：defending, protecting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are raiding the bank. = 他们在抢劫银行。
The police are raiding the house. = 警察正在搜查这栋房子。

一般过去时:
The pirates raided the ship. = 海盗袭击了那艘船。
She raided the fridge for a snack. = 她翻冰箱找零食吃。

现在完成时:
They have raided several shops this week. = 他们本周已经抢劫了几家商店。
The police have raided the building twice. = 警察已经搜查过那栋建筑两次了。

过去完成时:
The thieves had raided the museum before the alarm went off. = 在警报响起之前，小偷已经洗劫了博物馆。
The detectives had raided the apartment previously. = 侦探们之前已经搜查过那间公寓。

将来时:
They will raid the enemy's camp tonight. = 他们今晚将袭击敌人的营地。
The inspectors will raid the factory tomorrow. = 检查员明天将突击检查工厂。

虚拟语气:
If they raided the building, they would find evidence. = 如果他们搜查那栋建筑，他们会找到证据。
I wish they hadn't raided my house. = 我真希望他们没有搜查我的房子。

被动语态:

The house is being raided by the police. = 这栋房子正在被警察搜查。

疑问句:

否定句:
