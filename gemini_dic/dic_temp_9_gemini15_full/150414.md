panselectionism:/ˌpænˌsɛlˈɛkʃənɪzəm/| n. |泛选择论|A biological theory proposing that natural selection acts on all levels of biological organization, from genes to ecosystems, not just on individual organisms.  It emphasizes the importance of multilevel selection in evolution. |Example: Panselectionism challenges the traditional neo-Darwinian view of evolution focused solely on individual selection. 泛选择论挑战了传统的新达尔文进化论，后者只关注个体选择。|近义词：multilevel selection, hierarchical selection|反义词：individual selection, gene selection (in the context of its exclusive dominance)
