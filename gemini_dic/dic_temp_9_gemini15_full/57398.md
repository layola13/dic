dozing:/ˈdoʊzɪŋ/| v.|打瞌睡，昏昏欲睡|To sleep lightly; to be half asleep.|例句：The old man was dozing in his armchair. 老人在扶手椅上打瞌睡。|例句：I was dozing off during the lecture. 讲座期间我一直在打瞌睡。|近义词：sleeping lightly, napping, slumbering|反义词：wide awake, alert


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dozes off every afternoon. 他每天下午都会打瞌睡。
She often dozes during long meetings. 她经常在冗长的会议上打瞌睡。

一般过去时:
The baby dozed off in its crib. 婴儿在婴儿床里睡着了。
I dozed off while watching TV. 看电视的时候我睡着了。

现在完成时:
I have dozed off several times today. 我今天已经打瞌睡好几次了。
She has been dozing on and off all afternoon. 她整个下午断断续续地打瞌睡。

过去完成时:
He had dozed off before the movie ended. 电影结束前他已经睡着了。
They had been dozing for hours before they were woken by the storm. 暴风雨来临前他们已经睡了好几个小时了。

将来时:
I think I will doze off soon. 我想我很快就会睡着了。
The children will probably doze off during the long car ride. 孩子们在漫长的车程中可能会睡着。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't dozed off, I would have heard the announcement. 如果我没有打瞌睡，我就会听到通知了。
I wish I hadn't dozed off during the exam. 我真希望考试期间我没有打瞌睡。


被动语态: (Doze is rarely used in the passive voice)  This verb is not typically used passively.


疑问句:
Were you dozing during the presentation? 你在演示期间打瞌睡了吗？
Did he doze off during the meeting? 他在会议期间睡着了吗？


否定句:
He didn't doze off despite the boring lecture. 尽管演讲很无聊，他也没有打瞌睡。
I didn't doze off, I was just resting my eyes. 我没有睡着，我只是闭目养神。


PHRASE->
fall into a doze:  陷入昏睡
drift off to doze: 慢慢睡着
nod off and doze: 打瞌睡
doze off to sleep: 昏昏沉沉睡去
doze in the sun: 晒太阳睡着
