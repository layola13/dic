pre-eclampsia:/ˌpriːɪˈklæmpsiə/| n. |先兆子痫| A serious condition in pregnancy characterized by high blood pressure and the presence of protein in the urine. It can lead to serious complications for both the mother and the baby if left untreated. | Example: The doctor monitored the pregnant woman closely for signs of pre-eclampsia. 医生密切监测孕妇是否有先兆子痫的迹象。|近义词：toxemia (less common now)|反义词：None.
