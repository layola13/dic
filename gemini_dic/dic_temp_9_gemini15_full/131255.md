noncarriers:/ˌnɒnˈkæriərz/ | n. |非携带者，非载体 | Individuals or things that do not carry or transmit something, often referring to diseases or genes.  For example, in epidemiology, noncarriers are individuals who are not infected with a particular pathogen and therefore cannot transmit it. In genetics, noncarriers do not possess a specific gene or allele. | Example:  The study focused on identifying noncarriers of the mutated gene to understand the disease's inheritance patterns.  这项研究的重点是识别未携带突变基因的个体，以了解该疾病的遗传模式。| 近义词:uninfected, unaffected, free from | 反义词:carriers, infected, affected
