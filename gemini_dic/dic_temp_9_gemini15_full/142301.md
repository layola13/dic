orthoconic:/ˌɔːrθəˈkɒnɪk/| adj.|正锥形的；直锥形的|Having a straight, conical shell, as certain extinct cephalopods.  The term refers to the shape of the shell, which is straight and tapers to a point. |Example:  The *orthoconic* nautiloids were among the earliest cephalopods.  正锥形的鹦鹉螺是早期头足类动物之一。|近义词：straight-shelled (in the context of cephalopods)|反义词：cyrtoconic (curved shell), gyroconic (coiled shell)
