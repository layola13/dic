capability:/ˌkeɪpəˈbɪləti/| n. |能力，才能；性能；容量|The ability or potential to do something.  It refers to a person's skill, competence, or power to perform a task or function, or to the potential or capacity of a thing to achieve a certain result.|例句：He has the capability to lead a team. 他有领导团队的能力。|例句：The new software has enhanced capabilities. 新软件增强了功能。|近义词：ability, capacity, potential, competence|反义词：inability, incapacity


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has the capability to learn quickly. = 他有快速学习的能力。
The system demonstrates excellent data processing capability. = 该系统展现出卓越的数据处理能力。

一般过去时:
The old machine lacked the capability to handle large files. = 旧机器缺乏处理大型文件的能力。
She demonstrated remarkable problem-solving capability during the crisis. = 在危机期间，她展现了非凡的解决问题的能力。

现在完成时:

They have significantly improved their manufacturing capabilities. = 他们已经大大提高了他们的制造能力。

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he had the capability, he would have finished the project on time. = 如果他有能力，他就能按时完成项目。
It is essential that the system possess the capability to handle large amounts of data. = 系统必须具备处理大量数据的能力。

被动语态:

否定句:
