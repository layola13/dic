polit: /pəˈliːt/ | adj. |有礼貌的；客气的；有教养的 | Polite means having or showing behavior that is respectful and considerate of other people.  It implies good manners and a courteous attitude. | Example: He was always polite to his elders. 他总是对长辈很客气。  She made a polite request for help. 她礼貌地请求帮助。 |近义词：courteous, civil, respectful, well-mannered |反义词：rude, impolite, discourteous, ill-mannered |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is polite to everyone. = 他对每个人都很礼貌。
She is always polite in her dealings. = 她待人接物总是很客气。

一般过去时:
He was polite to the waiter. = 他对服务员很客气。
They were polite and helpful. = 他们有礼貌且乐于助人。

现在完成时:
He has always been polite to me. = 他一直对我很有礼貌。
She has been remarkably polite throughout the negotiation. = 在整个谈判过程中，她都表现得非常有礼貌。

过去完成时:
He had been polite to her before, but not today. = 他以前对她很客气，但今天不是。
They had been polite, but their patience was wearing thin. = 他们一直很客气，但他们的耐心快磨光了。

将来时:
He will be polite to the guest. = 他会对客人很客气。
I will try to be more polite in the future. = 我将努力在未来变得更有礼貌。

虚拟语气:
If he were polite, he would be more likable. = 如果他更有礼貌，他会更讨人喜欢。
I wish he would be polite to his colleagues. = 我希望他会对他同事有礼貌。

被动语态:
Polite behavior is expected. = 预期要有礼貌的行为。

疑问句:
Are you being polite? = 你有礼貌吗？

否定句:
