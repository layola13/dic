emancipate:/ɪˈmæn.sɪ.peɪt/| vt.| 解放；使自由；释放 | To set someone free from legal, social, or political restrictions.  |例句：The slaves were emancipated after the Civil War.  内战后，奴隶获得了解放。

例句：The movement fought to emancipate women from traditional roles.  这场运动致力于解放妇女摆脱传统角色的束缚。


近义词：liberate, free, release, liberate

反义词：enslave, imprison, subjugate, bind


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government emancipates prisoners after they serve their sentences. = 政府在囚犯服刑完毕后释放他们。
She emancipates herself from the constraints of society. = 她从社会的束缚中解放了自己。


一般过去时:
The abolition of slavery emancipated millions. = 废除奴隶制解放了数百万奴隶。
He emancipated himself from his controlling family. = 他从控制他的家庭中解放了自己。


现在完成时:
The women's movement has emancipated many women from traditional gender roles. = 女权运动已经使许多妇女摆脱了传统的性别角色。
They have emancipated themselves from the shackles of poverty. = 他们已经从贫困的枷锁中解放了自己。


过去完成时:
By the time she turned 30, she had emancipated herself financially. = 到她30岁的时候，她已经在经济上实现了独立。
The former colonies had emancipated themselves from British rule. = 前殖民地已经摆脱了英国的统治。


将来时:
The new law will emancipate many people from discrimination. = 新法律将使许多人摆脱歧视。
He hopes to emancipate himself from debt soon. = 他希望很快能摆脱债务。


虚拟语气:
If the law were passed, it would emancipate many prisoners. = 如果这项法律通过，它将使许多囚犯获得自由。
I wish I could emancipate myself from this difficult situation. = 我希望我能摆脱这种困境。


被动语态:
Many slaves were emancipated during the 19th century. = 19世纪许多奴隶获得了解放。
He felt emancipated by the court's decision. = 他感到法院的判决使他解脱了。


疑问句:
Will this policy emancipate the poor? = 这项政策会使穷人获得解放吗？
Has the government done enough to emancipate marginalized groups? = 政府是否做了足够的努力来解放边缘群体？


否定句:
The law did not emancipate all the people it was intended to. = 这项法律并没有解放所有它原本打算解放的人。
He has not yet emancipated himself from his past mistakes. = 他还没有从过去的错误中解脱出来。

词源:
From Late Latin *emancipare, from Latin manus "hand" + capere "to take".  The original meaning referred to the legal process of freeing a son from the power of his father.  The meaning has since broadened to encompass freeing from any kind of oppression or restriction.
