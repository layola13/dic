caldera:/kælˈdɛrə/ | n. |火山口，破火山口 | A caldera is a large, basin-shaped depression that forms following the emptying of a magma chamber in a volcanic eruption.  The collapse of the chamber creates a large crater, often much larger than the original vent of the volcano. |Example: The island of Santorini is famous for its stunning caldera, formed by a massive volcanic eruption thousands of years ago.  圣托里尼岛以其壮丽的火山口而闻名，该火山口是由数千年前一次巨大的火山爆发形成的。|近义词：crater (though usually smaller), basin |反义词：volcanic cone
