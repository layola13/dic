pilastered:/ˈpɪləstərd/| adj.|有壁柱的；饰以壁柱的|Adorned with pilasters.  A pilastered building is one that has pilasters as a decorative or structural feature.  The term implies a more formal or classical style of architecture.|例句：The pilastered facade of the building gave it a grand appearance. = 这座建筑物饰以壁柱的正面使其显得宏伟壮丽。

The hallway was pilastered, creating a sense of elegance. = 走廊装饰着壁柱，营造出优雅的感觉。|近义词：columned, pilastered,  |反义词：plain, undecorated

词源:
From "pilaster," a shallow, decorative column attached to a wall, ultimately from Italian *pilastro*, from Latin *pila*, "pillar."  The suffix "-ed" indicates that something is adorned or characterized by pilasters.
