electively:/ɪˈlɛktɪvli/ | adv. | 选择地；随意地 |  done by choice or at one's own discretion; optionally.  | 例句：The course is electively chosen by students. 这门课由学生自选。 | 例句：She electively chose to work from home. 她选择在家工作。 | 近义词：optionally, voluntarily, freely | 反义词：compulsorily, obligatorily, mandatorily


词源:

The word "electively" derives from the adjective "elective," which comes from the Latin word "eligere," meaning "to choose" or "to select."  The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective into an adverb, indicating the manner or way in which something is done.


用法说明:

"Electively" emphasizes the aspect of choice or selection in the action being described.  It suggests that the action was not obligatory or mandatory, but rather a matter of personal preference or decision.  It implies freedom of choice.

The word is commonly used in contexts related to education, employment, and personal decisions, where individuals have the autonomy to make their own choices.
