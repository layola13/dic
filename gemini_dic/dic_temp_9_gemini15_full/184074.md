resignationism: /ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃənɪzəm/ | n. | 听天由命；逆来顺受；宿命论 | The acceptance of things as they are, often implying a passive or fatalistic attitude.  It suggests a belief that resistance is futile and that one should simply accept one's fate or the existing social or political order.  | Example: His resignationism in the face of overwhelming odds surprised everyone.  他的逆来顺受面对压倒性的几率让每个人都感到惊讶。 | 近义词：fatalism, passivism, acquiescence | 反义词：activism, resistance, defiance |  词性: 名词
