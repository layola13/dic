isolationists: /ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪsts/ | n. |孤立主义者 |Those who advocate a policy of national isolation by avoiding international political and economic entanglements.  They believe a country should focus on its own internal affairs and avoid foreign alliances or interventions. |Example:  Many isolationists opposed the United States' entry into World War I.  许多孤立主义者反对美国加入第一次世界大战。  |近义词：protectionists, nationalists |反义词：internationalists, globalists |名词
