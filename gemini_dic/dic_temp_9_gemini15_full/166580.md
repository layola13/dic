prescriptive:/prɪˈskrɪptɪv/| adj.|规定性的；命令式的；指示性的| Relating to or denoting the prescribing of a course of action, remedy, or treatment;  giving a rule or instruction.  It often implies an authoritative or obligatory nature.  The opposite of descriptive.|The prescriptive grammar rules dictate that we should use "I" instead of "me" as the subject of a sentence.  规定性语法规则规定我们应该使用“I”而不是“me”作为句子的主语。|近义词：mandatory, obligatory, authoritative, dictatorial|反义词：descriptive, permissive, flexible


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The school prescribes a strict dress code. = 学校规定了严格的着装规范。
This medication prescribes rest and fluids. = 此药规定要休息和多喝水。


一般过去时:
The doctor prescribed antibiotics for the infection. = 医生开了抗生素来治疗感染。
The teacher prescribed extra homework for the students. = 老师给学生布置了额外的家庭作业。


现在完成时:
The government has prescribed new regulations for data privacy. = 政府已经颁布了关于数据隐私的新规章。
The committee has prescribed a plan for improving infrastructure. = 委员会已经制定了一个改善基础设施的计划。


过去完成时:
The judge had prescribed a lengthy prison sentence for the criminal. = 法官已经判决罪犯长期监禁。
They had prescribed a strict diet for the patient's recovery.  = 他们已经为病人的康复制定了严格的饮食计划。


将来时:
The council will prescribe new building codes next year. = 理事会明年将制定新的建筑规范。
The doctor will prescribe a course of physical therapy. = 医生将开出一套物理治疗方案。


虚拟语气:
If the doctor prescribed this medicine, I would take it. = 如果医生开了这种药，我会服用。
I wish the government would prescribe stricter environmental regulations. = 我希望政府能制定更严格的环境法规。


被动语态:
A strict diet was prescribed by the nutritionist. = 营养师制定了严格的饮食计划。
New rules were prescribed by the board of directors. = 新规章是由董事会制定的。


疑问句:
What treatment did the doctor prescribe? = 医生开了什么治疗方案？
What measures has the government prescribed to address the crisis? = 政府采取了什么措施来应对这场危机？


否定句:
The doctor did not prescribe any medication. = 医生没有开任何药。
The school does not prescribe uniforms. = 学校没有规定校服。
