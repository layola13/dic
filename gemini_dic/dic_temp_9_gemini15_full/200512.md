slanting:/ˈslæntɪŋ/ | adj. |倾斜的；偏斜的；有偏见的 | Inclined from a vertical or horizontal position; not straight or upright.  Also implies a bias or prejudice in presentation or viewpoint. |例句：The slanting roof leaked badly.  = 倾斜的屋顶漏得很厉害。

The reporter's slanting article favored the politician. = 记者有偏见的文章偏袒了这位政治家。


近义词：oblique, inclined, tilted, biased, prejudiced | 反义词：vertical, horizontal, upright, unbiased, impartial


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The picture is slanting to the right. = 图片向右倾斜。
His remarks are always slanting towards his own interests. = 他的话总是偏向他自己的利益。


一般过去时:
The tower slanted dangerously after the earthquake. = 地震后，塔楼危险地倾斜了。
His account of the event slanted heavily in favor of his friend. = 他对事件的描述严重偏袒他的朋友。


现在完成时:
The road has slanted gradually over the years. = 这条路多年来逐渐倾斜了。
The media has slanted the news to create a negative impression. = 媒体歪曲了新闻以制造负面印象。


过去完成时:
The tree had slanted significantly before it finally fell. = 这棵树在最终倒下之前已经严重倾斜了。
The evidence had slanted the jury's perception of the defendant. = 证据已经歪曲了陪审团对被告的看法。


将来时:
The building will slant further if the foundation isn't repaired. = 如果地基不修复，建筑物将进一步倾斜。
The upcoming election coverage will slant in favor of the incumbent, I fear. = 我担心即将到来的选举报道会偏袒现任者。


虚拟语气:
If the post were slanting less, the mail wouldn't get stuck. = 如果柱子倾斜得少一些，邮件就不会卡住了。
If the news reports hadn't slanted the facts, public opinion would be different. = 如果新闻报道没有歪曲事实，公众舆论就会不同。


被动语态:
The photograph was slanted to create a dramatic effect. = 照片被倾斜以营造戏剧性的效果。
The narrative was slanted to portray the hero in a positive light. = 叙事被歪曲以正面描绘英雄。


疑问句:
Is the fence slanting? = 栅栏倾斜了吗？
Is the journalist slanting the story? = 记者在歪曲故事吗？


否定句:
The lines on the drawing are not slanting. = 图纸上的线条没有倾斜。
The newspaper did not slant its reporting in favor of any candidate. = 这家报纸没有偏袒任何候选人的报道。
