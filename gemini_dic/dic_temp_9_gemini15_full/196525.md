servitudes:/ˈsɜːrvɪtjuːdz/| n. |役权；地役权；劳役；奴役状态| A servitude is a right held by one person to use the land of another for a specific purpose.  It is a burden on the land, rather than on the owner. This can include things like rights of way, easements, or restrictions on the use of property.  For example, a right of way across a neighbor's property to access a road is a servitude.|The farmer had a servitude on his neighbor's land for water access.| easements, rights of way | ownership, freedom | 名词
