immunizations:/ˌɪmjuːnəˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. |免疫接种，免疫程序 |The process of making a person or animal immune to an infectious disease, typically by administering a vaccine.| 例句：Children need immunizations to protect them from various diseases.  孩子们需要接种疫苗来预防各种疾病。

The government launched a campaign to increase childhood immunizations rates. 政府发起了一场运动来提高儿童免疫接种率。


近义词：vaccinations, inoculations | 反义词：None (反义词难以直接对应，可以考虑与疾病相关的词汇，例如 infection, disease) | 词性：名词

词源:
The word "immunization" comes from the Latin word "immunis," meaning "exempt," "free," or "safe from."  The suffix "-ization" indicates the process of making something immune.  Therefore, immunization refers to the process of making someone immune to disease.
