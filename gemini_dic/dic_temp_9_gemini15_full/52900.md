dimmed:/dɪmd/| adj.|暗淡的，模糊的，减弱的|Reduced in brightness or intensity; made less bright or distinct.|The lights dimmed as the movie began. 电影开始时灯光暗了下来。The sound of the music dimmed gradually. 音乐的声音逐渐减弱。|bright, brilliant, vivid|bright, vivid, intense|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The light dims slowly. 灯光慢慢变暗。
The colors in the painting seem dimmed. 画中的颜色看起来暗淡了。


一般过去时:
The sun dimmed behind the clouds. 太阳被云彩遮挡变暗了。
He dimmed the headlights to avoid blinding the oncoming traffic. 他调暗了车灯，以免晃到迎面而来的车辆。


现在完成时:
The once-bright colors have dimmed over time.  曾经鲜艳的色彩随着时间的推移而暗淡了。
My enthusiasm has dimmed considerably since the project failed.  自从项目失败后，我的热情已经大大减弱了。


过去完成时:
The lights had dimmed before the speaker began. 演讲者开始之前，灯光已经变暗了。
The colors of the photograph had dimmed considerably after years of exposure to sunlight. 照片的颜色在经过多年的阳光照射后已经暗淡了很多。


将来时:
The lights will dim automatically at sunset. 日落时分，灯光会自动变暗。
The excitement will dim as the event draws to a close.  随着活动的结束，兴奋感将会减弱。


虚拟语气:
If the lights dimmed, we would have had a better view of the stars. 如果灯光暗一些，我们就能更好地观赏星星了。
I wish the pain would dim a little. 我希望疼痛能减轻一点。


被动语态:
The lights were dimmed to create a more romantic atmosphere. 灯光被调暗以营造更浪漫的气氛。
The image was dimmed by the fog. 图像被雾气模糊了。


疑问句:

否定句:
The lights did not dim. 灯光没有变暗。
The memory did not dim with time. 这段记忆并没有随着时间而淡忘。
