madrigals:/ˈmædrɪɡəlz/ | n. | 牧歌；多声部歌曲 |  Secular vocal music, typically for several voices without instrumental accompaniment, that was popular in Italy during the Renaissance and later spread throughout Europe.  Madrigals are characterized by their expressive text-painting and word-painting techniques, often depicting the emotions and imagery of the poem through musical means. | 例句：The choir sang a beautiful madrigal, its harmonies perfectly reflecting the poem's melancholic mood.  合唱团演唱了一首优美的牧歌，其和声完美地反映了诗歌忧郁的氛围。

The Renaissance madrigals were often performed in the courts of European nobility. 文艺复兴时期的牧歌经常在欧洲贵族的宫廷中演出。


近义词：part-song, vocal piece | 反义词：instrumental music
