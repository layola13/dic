landsman:/ˈlænzˌmæn/| n. |同乡；同胞；老乡；（尤指）海员；陆地人| A fellow countryman; especially, a sailor; a person who lives on land.  |例句：He's a landsman, not a sailor. 他是陆地人，不是水手。

例句：After years at sea, he felt like a true landsman again upon returning to his hometown. 在海上漂泊多年后，回到家乡，他又感觉自己像个真正的陆地人了。


近义词：countryman, compatriot, fellow citizen

反义词：sailor, seaman

词性:名词

词源:
The word "landsman" is a compound word combining "land" and "man."  It emerged in Middle English, reflecting a clear distinction between those who lived and worked on land versus those who worked at sea.  The "man" suffix denotes a person or male inhabitant of a specified place.
