/ˌpriːkəˈluʒən/ | n. |预谋勾结；预先串通|The term "precollusion" refers to an agreement or conspiracy made in advance of an event or action, typically to deceive or defraud others.  It implies a secret understanding or plan to act together for a shared, usually illicit, purpose before the overt actions take place.  The emphasis is on the *planning* and *premeditation* before any visible collusion occurs.|The evidence suggests precollusion between the two companies to fix prices.  证据表明两家公司事先串通好了价格。|collusion, conspiracy, connivance|independent action, fair competition|名词
