deems:/diːmz/| vt.|认为；相信；视为；判决|To consider or judge something in a particular way.|The judge deems the evidence insufficient.  法官认为证据不足。|He deems it necessary to take action. 他认为采取行动是必要的。|considers, believes, judges, regards|disregards, ignores, overlooks|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deems the project successful. = 他认为这个项目成功了。
She deems it important to study hard. = 她认为努力学习很重要。

一般过去时:
The court deemed him guilty. = 法庭判决他有罪。
They deemed the risk too high. = 他们认为风险太高了。

现在完成时:
The committee has deemed the proposal acceptable. = 委员会已认为该提案可以接受。
He has deemed it necessary to resign. = 他认为有必要辞职。

过去完成时:
She had deemed the situation hopeless before the new evidence emerged. = 在新的证据出现之前，她认为情况毫无希望。
They had deemed the investment unwise before the market crash. = 在市场崩盘之前，他们认为这项投资不明智。

将来时:
The jury will deem the defendant innocent or guilty. = 陪审团将判定被告无罪或有罪。
He will deem it appropriate to respond later. = 他认为稍后再回应比较合适。

虚拟语气:
If the judge deemed it necessary, he would issue a warrant. = 如果法官认为有必要，他会发出逮捕令。
I wish he deemed my suggestion worthwhile. = 我希望他认为我的建议有价值。

被动语态:
The proposal was deemed unacceptable by the board. = 该提案被董事会认为不可接受。
His actions were deemed inappropriate by many. = 许多人认为他的行为不当。

疑问句:
Does she deem this a serious problem? = 她认为这是一个严重的问题吗？
Do they deem the plan feasible? = 他们认为这个计划可行吗？

否定句:
He doesn't deem it necessary to apologize. = 他认为没有必要道歉。
They didn't deem the information credible. = 他们认为该信息不可信。
