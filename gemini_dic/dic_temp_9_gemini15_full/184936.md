retardment: /rɪˈtɑːrdmənt/ | n. |发育迟缓；阻滞；延迟 | The slowing or hindering of a process or development.  It often refers to developmental delays in children, but can also apply to other areas like technological progress.  The term is now considered outdated and potentially offensive in many contexts due to its association with derogatory language targeting people with intellectual disabilities. |例句：The doctor noted a significant retardment in the child's language development. 医生注意到孩子语言发育明显迟缓。

The project experienced a retardment due to unforeseen circumstances.  该项目因不可预见的情况而延误。 |近义词：delay, hindrance, impediment, setback |反义词：acceleration, advancement, progress |词性:名词

词源:  The word "retardment" comes from the verb "retard," which originates from the Old French word "retarder," meaning "to delay" or "to hinder." The word's usage has shifted significantly over time, with its current application being largely restricted due to its offensive connotations.
