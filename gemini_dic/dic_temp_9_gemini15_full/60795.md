electronegativity: /ˌɪˌlɛktrəʊˌnɛɡəˈtɪvɪti/ | n. | 电负性 |  The tendency of an atom to attract a shared pair of electrons towards itself in a chemical bond.  It's a measure of the ability of an atom in a molecule to attract electrons to itself.  Higher electronegativity means a stronger pull on electrons. |  The electronegativity of oxygen is higher than that of hydrogen. 氧的电负性高于氢。

The difference in electronegativity between two atoms determines the type of bond they will form: a large difference leads to an ionic bond, while a small difference leads to a covalent bond.  两个原子之间电负性的差异决定了它们将形成的键的类型：较大的差异导致离子键，而较小的差异导致共价键。


近义词：electron affinity (though not a perfect synonym, as electron affinity measures the energy change when an electron is added to a gaseous atom, while electronegativity considers the atom within a molecule)  电子亲和力（虽然不是完美的同义词，因为电子亲和力测量的是气态原子添加电子时的能量变化，而电负性则考虑分子中的原子）


反义词：electropositivity (tendency to lose electrons) 电正性（失去电子的趋势）


词性：名词


词源：
* **electro-**:  from Greek "elektron," meaning amber (a substance that exhibits static electricity).  来自希腊语“elektron”，意思是琥珀（一种表现出静电的物质）。
* **-negativ-**: from Latin "negativus," meaning denying or negative.  来自拉丁语“negativus”，意思是否定或负面的。
* **-ity**: a suffix forming nouns denoting a quality or state.  一个后缀，形成表示质量或状态的名词。

Therefore, electronegativity literally means "the quality of being negative towards electrons"  因此，电负性字面意思是“对电子呈负面的特性”
