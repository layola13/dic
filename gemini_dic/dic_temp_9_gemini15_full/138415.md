noumenal:/ˈnuːmənəl/| adj.|本体的；超验的；非经验的|Relating to or denoting the thing-in-itself, as opposed to its appearance to the senses; transcendental.  The term is used in Kantian philosophy to describe things as they are in themselves, independent of our experience and perception.  It contrasts with phenomenal, which refers to things as they appear to us.|The noumenal realm is beyond human comprehension.|近义词：transcendental, metaphysical|反义词：phenomenal
