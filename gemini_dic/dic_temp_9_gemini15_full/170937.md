pseudoneurotic:/ˌsuːdoʊnʊərˈɑːtɪk/|/ˌsjuːdəʊnjʊəˈrɒtɪk/| adj.|似神经衰弱的，假性神经症的 | seemingly neurotic but not actually suffering from a diagnosable neurotic disorder; exhibiting symptoms suggestive of neurosis but lacking the underlying psychological basis.  The term often implies a personality disorder manifested as anxiety, depression, or other neurotic-like behaviors without meeting the full criteria for a specific neurotic diagnosis. |Example: His pseudoneurotic behavior, while disruptive, didn't indicate a clinically significant anxiety disorder.  他的似神经衰弱的行为虽然具有破坏性，但并不表明存在临床上显著的焦虑症。

Example:  Her pseudoneurotic complaints masked a deeper-seated issue of unresolved grief. 她的似神经衰弱的抱怨掩盖了更深层次的未解决的悲伤问题。|近义词：neurotic-like,  hysterical (in some contexts),  maladjusted |反义词：psychologically healthy,  well-adjusted,  stable


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have tenses like verbs.  It describes a state or condition.  Therefore, verb conjugations are not applicable.


PHRASE->
pseudoneurotic symptoms: 似神经衰弱的症状
pseudoneurotic personality: 似神经衰弱的人格
pseudoneurotic reactions: 似神经衰弱的反应
pseudoneurotic depression: 假性神经衰弱性抑郁
presenting with pseudoneurotic symptoms: 表现出似神经衰弱的症状


词源:
pseudo- (Greek, meaning "false" or "sham") + neurotic (referring to neurosis, a now largely obsolete term for a group of mental disorders characterized by anxiety, depression, or obsessive behavior).  The term reflects the appearance of neurotic symptoms without the underlying pathology of a true neurosis.
