sibilancy:/ˈsɪbɪlənsi/| n. |咝音，齿擦音|The quality of sound produced by the emission of breath through a narrow channel in the vocal tract, as in the pronunciation of /s/, /z/, /ʃ/, /ʒ/, /tʃ/, and /dʒ/.  It refers to the hissing or buzzing sounds created by these consonants.  The term is often used in phonetics and linguistics.|例句：The poem's sibilancy created a sense of mystery and suspense. 这首诗的咝音营造出一种神秘和悬念的感觉。

The excessive sibilancy in her speech made it difficult to understand. 她说话中过多的咝音使人难以理解。|近义词：hissing, buzzing|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with sounds lacking this quality, such as vowel sounds. |名词

The word "sibilancy" derives from the Latin word "sibilare," meaning "to hiss."  This, in turn, is related to the Latin word "sibilare," with the same meaning, and ultimately to a Proto-Indo-European root associated with hissing sounds.  The suffix "-ancy" indicates a state or quality.
