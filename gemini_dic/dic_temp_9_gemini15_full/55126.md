disrupture:/dɪsˈrʌptʃər/| n. |中断；扰乱；破坏|The disruption of the power grid caused widespread chaos.  电力网中断造成了大范围的混乱。|近义词：interruption, disturbance, breakdown |反义词：continuity, order, stability


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The construction causes disruption to traffic. = 建设工程扰乱了交通。
This software update disrupts the existing system. = 这个软件更新扰乱了现有系统。

一般过去时:
The storm caused significant disruption to travel. = 暴风雨严重扰乱了交通。
The sudden announcement disrupted the meeting. = 突如其来的公告打乱了会议。

现在完成时:
The pandemic has caused widespread disruption to global economies. = 疫情给全球经济带来了广泛的冲击。
Recent events have disrupted the company's plans. = 最近发生的事件打乱了公司的计划。

过去完成时:
The power outage had already disrupted the production line before the backup generator kicked in. = 断电已经在备用发电机启动之前扰乱了生产线。
The unexpected rain had disrupted their outdoor picnic plans. = 意想不到的雨水打乱了他们的户外野餐计划。

将来时:
The strike will disrupt train services. = 罢工将扰乱火车服务。
The new regulations will disrupt the market. = 新法规将扰乱市场。

虚拟语气:
If the internet goes down, it will disrupt our workflow. = 如果互联网中断，它将扰乱我们的工作流程。
I wish the noise hadn't disrupted my sleep. = 我真希望噪音没有扰乱我的睡眠。

被动语态:
The meeting was disrupted by a sudden power outage. = 会议因突然停电而中断。
The schedule was disrupted by unforeseen circumstances. = 时间表因不可预见的情况而被打乱。

疑问句:
What caused the disruption to the service? = 什么原因导致了服务中断？
Will the protests disrupt the city's transportation? = 抗议活动会扰乱城市的交通吗？

否定句:
The change didn't disrupt the workflow significantly. = 这一改变并没有显著地扰乱工作流程。
The new system didn't disrupt the established routines. = 新系统没有扰乱既定的流程。
