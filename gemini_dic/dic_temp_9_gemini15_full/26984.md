calcitration:/ˌkælsɪˈtreɪʃn/| n. |钙化；钙质沉着|The process of depositing calcium salts in tissues, resulting in hardening.  This is a pathological process that can occur in various parts of the body, leading to stiffness and dysfunction.  For example, calcification can occur in arteries, leading to atherosclerosis, or in joints, causing arthritis.|例句：The X-ray showed significant calcification in his arteries. X光显示他的动脉有明显的钙化。|例句：The patient's joint pain was attributed to calcification in the cartilage. 患者的关节疼痛归因于软骨钙化。|近义词：petrification, ossification|反义词：decalcification|名词
