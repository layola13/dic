nonnarcotic: /ˌnɑːnɑːrˈkɒtɪk/ | adj. |非麻醉的，非成瘾性的 | Not producing a narcotic effect; not habit-forming.  This term refers to medications and substances that do not have the properties of narcotics, meaning they don't cause dependence or addiction and don't have the ability to dull the senses or induce sleep in the way that opioids do. | 例句：The doctor prescribed a nonnarcotic pain reliever for the patient.  医生为病人开了非麻醉性止痛药。


例句：Many over-the-counter pain relievers are nonnarcotic.  许多非处方止痛药是非麻醉性的。


近义词：nonaddictive, nonopioid


反义词：narcotic, addictive


词性：形容词


词源：The word "nonnarcotic" is a compound word.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," and "narcotic" comes from the Greek word "narcotikos," meaning "to make numb" or "to benumb."  The term ultimately relates to the effects of narcotic substances.
