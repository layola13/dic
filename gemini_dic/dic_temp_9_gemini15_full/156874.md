pigweed: /ˈpɪɡwiːd/ | n. |猪草，藜|A common name for several weedy plants, especially those of the genus *Amaranthus*, characterized by their broad leaves and often reddish stems.  These plants are considered undesirable weeds in many agricultural settings due to their rapid growth and competition with crops for resources. |例句：The farmer diligently weeded the pigweed from his cornfield.  农民勤奋地从玉米地里除掉了猪草。

The pigweed grew profusely near the fence. 猪草在栅栏附近疯长。


近义词：weed, amaranth (depending on the specific species) |反义词：crop (in the context of agricultural competition) |词性：名词


词源:  The name "pigweed" derives from the fact that pigs are known to eat these plants. The "pig" part refers to the animal, and "weed" indicates its undesirable nature as an unwanted plant in cultivated areas.  The specific species included under the common name "pigweed" vary regionally.
