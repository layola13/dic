conjugates:/ˈkɒndʒʊɡeɪts/ /ˈkɑːndʒʊɡeɪts/ | v. | 结合；共轭；(动词)变位 | To combine or unite; to form a conjugate pair;  in grammar, to list the various forms of a verb according to person, number, tense, mood, and voice. |

Example 1: The chemist conjugates two compounds to create a new molecule.  化学家结合两种化合物以创造一个新的分子。

Example 2:  In Spanish, the verb "to be" conjugates differently depending on the subject. 在西班牙语中，“是”动词的变位根据主语的不同而不同。


Near synonyms: combine, unite, link, inflect (in grammatical context)


Antonyms:  separate, disconnect, disjoin (in the sense of combining)


Word Origin:  From Latin *coniugāre "to join together, yoke," from con- "together" + iugāre "to yoke." The grammatical sense developed later.
