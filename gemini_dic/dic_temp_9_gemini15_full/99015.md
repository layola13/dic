interadaptation:/ˌɪntərˌædəpˈteɪʃən/| n. |相互适应，共同适应|The process by which two or more species or organisms adapt to each other's presence and influence.  This often involves reciprocal evolutionary changes.  For example, the coevolution of a predator and its prey, or a parasite and its host. | Example: The interadaptation of predator and prey led to increased speed in the prey and improved hunting strategies in the predator.  相互适应的天敌和猎物导致猎物速度加快，捕食者狩猎策略改进。|近义词：coadaptation, reciprocal adaptation |反义词：maladaptation, incompatibility |名词
