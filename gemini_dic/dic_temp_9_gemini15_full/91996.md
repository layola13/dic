hypernym: /ˈhaɪpɚnɪm/ | n. | 上义词；上位词 | A word that is more general in meaning than a given word, and that can be substituted for it in a sentence.  For example, "animal" is a hypernym of "dog," "cat," and "bird." |  The word "animal" is a hypernym of "dog".  Animals such as dogs, cats, and birds are found in many parts of the world. |近义词:superordinate, generic term |反义词:hyponym

词源:
The word "hypernym" is a modern coinage, combining the Greek prefix "hyper-" (meaning "over," "above," or "beyond") with the element "-nym," which is derived from the Greek word "onoma" (meaning "name").  Therefore, it literally means "over-name" or "higher-level name."
