buttressing: /ˈbʌtrəsɪŋ/ | n. |支撑，加固；支持 | The act of supporting or strengthening something, especially a building or structure.  It can also refer to something that provides support or reinforcement.  |例句：The buttressing of the ancient walls prevented their collapse.  古老的城墙得到了支撑，才没有倒塌。

例句：His arguments provided strong buttressing for his theory. 他的论点有力地支持了他的理论。


近义词：support, reinforcement, strengthening, bolstering | 反义词：weakening, undermining, destabilization | 词性：名词


USAGE->

The columns buttress the roof. = 柱子支撑着屋顶。
This policy buttresses the government's image. =这项政策巩固了政府的形象。

一般过去时:

现在完成时:
They have buttressed the crumbling walls with new supports. = 他们用新的支撑物加固了摇摇欲坠的墙壁。
The government has buttressed its economy through various reforms. = 政府通过各种改革来支撑其经济。

过去完成时:
The building had been buttressed many times over the centuries. = 这座建筑物几个世纪以来已经多次得到加固。
The team had buttressed their strategy with extensive research. = 该团队已经通过广泛的研究来加强他们的战略。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

If they buttressed their arguments with more evidence, they would be more convincing. = 如果他们用更多证据来支持他们的论点，他们会更有说服力。


被动语态:

否定句:

词源:
Ultimately derived from Old French *boter, meaning "to thrust," "to push."  This evolved into the Middle English word "boteras," referring to a projecting support. The modern English word "buttress" reflects this historical development.
