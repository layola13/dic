monopolising: /məˈnɒpəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 垄断；独占；独吞 |  To control or take over the whole or most of something, especially in a way that prevents others from using or enjoying it. | 例句：The company is monopolising the market for mobile phones. 这家公司垄断了手机市场。  | 例句：He monopolises the conversation, never letting anyone else speak. 他独占了谈话，从不让别人说话。 | 近义词：dominate, control, corner, hog | 反义词：share, distribute, relinquish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He monopolises the conversation. = 他独占谈话。
They are monopolising the resources. = 他们垄断了资源。

一般过去时:
She monopolised the attention of the audience. = 她吸引了观众的所有注意力。
The company monopolised the market. = 这家公司垄断了市场。

现在完成时:
He has monopolised the discussion for too long. = 他已经独占讨论太久了。
They have monopolised the best seats. = 他们已经占据了最好的座位。

过去完成时:
She had monopolised the conversation before I could speak. = 我还没来得及说话她就独占了谈话。
The company had monopolised the industry for decades. = 这家公司几十年来一直垄断着这个行业。

将来时:
He will monopolise the spotlight at the awards ceremony. = 他将在颁奖典礼上成为焦点。
They will monopolise the market share. = 他们将会垄断市场份额。

虚拟语气:
If he monopolised the market, he would become very rich. = 如果他垄断了市场，他会变得非常富有。
I wish she wouldn’t monopolise the conversation. = 我希望她不要独占谈话。

被动语态:
The market is being monopolised by a single company. = 市场正被一家公司垄断。
The resources were monopolised by the government. = 资源被政府垄断了。

疑问句:
Is he monopolising the project? = 他是否在独占这个项目？
Are they monopolising the resources? = 他们是否在垄断资源？

否定句:
He doesn't monopolise the conversation. = 他并没有独占谈话。
They didn't monopolise the market. = 他们并没有垄断市场。
