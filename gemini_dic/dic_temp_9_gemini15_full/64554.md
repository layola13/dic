erred:/ˈɛrd/| v.|犯错，误入歧途|To make a mistake; to do wrong.  |例句：I erred in my judgment. = 我判断失误了。
He erred in his calculations. = 他的计算错了。|近义词：mistook, blundered, faltered|反义词：succeeded, excelled


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He errs in his judgment frequently. = 他经常在判断上犯错误。
She errs on the side of caution. = 她总是过于谨慎。


一般过去时:
The judge erred in his decision. = 法官的判决有误。
I erred by trusting him. = 我错信了他。


现在完成时:
He has erred in his approach to the problem. = 他在解决这个问题的方法上犯了错。
She has often erred in the past, but she's learning. = 她过去经常犯错，但她正在学习。


过去完成时:
I realized I had erred in my calculations. = 我意识到我的计算错了。
They discovered they had erred in their assessment of the situation. = 他们发现他们对局势的评估错了。


将来时:
He will likely err again if he doesn't learn from his mistakes. = 如果他不吸取教训，他很可能会再次犯错。
She will probably err on the side of caution. = 她可能会过于谨慎。


虚拟语气:
If I had not erred, the outcome would have been different. = 如果我没有犯错，结果就会不同。
I wish I hadn't erred in my judgment. = 我真希望我没有判断失误。


被动语态:
The plan was found to have erred significantly. = 发现该计划有重大错误。
Mistakes were made; the strategy erred in its execution. = 犯了错误；战略执行有误。


疑问句:
Did I err in my understanding of the instructions? = 我对指示的理解有误吗？
Where did the company err in its marketing strategy? = 公司的营销策略错在哪里？


否定句:
I did not err in my assessment. = 我的评估没有错误。
The doctor did not err in his diagnosis. = 医生诊断没有错。


PHRASE->
err on the side of caution:  谨慎行事
to err is human: 人非圣贤，孰能无过
err in judgment: 判断失误
err in one's ways: 行为错误
err from the truth: 偏离事实
