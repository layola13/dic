self-administering: /ˌsɛlf ədˈmɪnɪstəriŋ/ | adj. | 自我管理的，自主管理的 | Able to manage or govern oneself without external control or interference.  This term often refers to systems or organizations that are capable of managing their own operations and resources without significant external oversight. |  例句：The self-administering university had considerable autonomy in setting its curriculum.  = 这所自我管理的大学在制定课程方面拥有相当大的自主权。

The project requires a self-administering database system for efficient data management. = 该项目需要一个自我管理的数据库系统来有效地管理数据。


近义词：autonomous, independent, self-governing, self-regulating | 反义词：dependent, controlled, managed


词源:  The word is a compound of "self-" (meaning "of or by oneself") and "administering" (from "administer," ultimately deriving from the Latin "administrare," meaning "to serve," "to manage," or "to govern"). The "-ing" suffix indicates the present participle, functioning as an adjective here.
