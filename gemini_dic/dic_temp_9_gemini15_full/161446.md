pot-liquor:/ˈpɑːtˌlɪkər/|/ˈpɒtˌlɪkwər/| n. |菜汤；烹调肉类时流出的汁水|The liquid remaining in a pot after meat has been cooked, often used as a base for soup or gravy.  It's typically flavorful and rich with the essence of the cooked meat and vegetables.|例句：The pot-liquor from the roast chicken was used to make a delicious gravy.  烤鸡剩下的汤汁被用来做美味的肉汁。

The pot-liquor, though simple, added depth to the stew.  这锅菜汤，虽然简单，却为炖菜增添了丰富的味道。


近义词：broth, stock, gravy | 反义词：无


词性：名词


Etymology:

The word "pot-liquor" is a compound word.  "Pot" refers to the cooking pot, and "liquor" historically meant any liquid, but in this context, specifically the liquid resulting from cooking.  Its origins trace back to Middle English, reflecting a time when resourceful cooks maximized the flavor and nutrients from their ingredients.
