paraffins:/ˈpærəfɪnz/ | n. |石蜡；烷烃 |Paraffins are a group of saturated hydrocarbons with the general formula CnH2n+2, where n is the number of carbon atoms. They are alkanes, meaning they are composed solely of single carbon-carbon bonds.  They are typically colorless, odorless, and tasteless, and exist in various states depending on the chain length – from gases (e.g., methane) to waxy solids (e.g., paraffin wax).  Paraffins are used in a wide range of applications, from candles and lubricants to cosmetics and plastics. |Example:  Paraffin wax is used to make candles. 石蜡用于制作蜡烛。 Paraffin oil is a common lubricant. 石蜡油是一种常见的润滑剂。|近义词：alkanes, saturated hydrocarbons |反义词：olefins (alkenes), unsaturated hydrocarbons |n.
