gaggle:/ˈɡæɡəl/| n. |鹅群；一群人（尤指吵闹的）|A gaggle is a group of geese, or, more generally, a group of people, especially a noisy or disorderly group.  |Example: A gaggle of geese honked loudly as they flew overhead.  一群鹅在头顶飞过时大声鸣叫。


Example: A gaggle of tourists crowded around the famous landmark. 一群游客挤在著名的地标建筑周围。|近义词：flock, group, crowd |反义词：individual, single

词源:
The word "gaggle" ultimately derives from the Old English word "gagel," which referred to a flock of geese.  Its evolution reflects the onomatopoeic quality associated with the sounds geese make.
