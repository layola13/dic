foregrounds:/ˈfɔːrˌɡraʊndz/ | n. |前景；突出 |To emphasize or bring to the forefront.  The artist foregrounded the central figure in the painting.  The report foregrounds the need for immediate action.|近义词：emphasize, highlight, promote |反义词：background, downplay


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The artist foregrounds the main character in his paintings. =艺术家在他的画中突出主要人物。
They foreground the importance of education. =他们强调教育的重要性。


一般过去时:
The speaker foregrounded the key issues in his speech. =演讲者在他的演讲中突出了关键问题。
The museum foregrounded the artist's early works in the exhibition. =博物馆在展览中突出了这位艺术家的早期作品。


现在完成时:
The campaign has foregrounded the issue of climate change. =这场运动已经突出了气候变化的问题。
She has consistently foregrounded the rights of women. =她一直强调妇女的权利。


过去完成时:
He had foregrounded his concerns about the project before the meeting. =在会议之前，他已经强调了他对项目的担忧。
They had foregrounded the risks involved in the investment. =他们已经强调了这项投资中涉及的风险。


将来时:
The upcoming conference will foreground the latest research findings. =即将举行的会议将突出最新的研究成果。
We will foreground the benefits of the new policy in our presentation. =我们将在演示文稿中突出新政策的益处。


虚拟语气:
If the artist foregrounded the landscape, the painting would be different. =如果艺术家突出风景，这幅画就会不一样。
I wish they had foregrounded the safety concerns earlier. =我希望他们早些强调安全问题。


被动语态:
The problem was foregrounded in the discussion. =这个问题在讨论中得到了突出。
The importance of teamwork is being foregrounded by the company. =公司正在强调团队合作的重要性。


疑问句:
What aspects of the report are foregrounded? =报告中突出了哪些方面？
What issues will be foregrounded in the debate? =在辩论中将突出哪些问题？


否定句:
The report did not foreground the economic implications. =报告没有突出经济影响。
They did not foreground the potential risks. =他们没有突出潜在的风险。


PHRASE->
foreground something: 突出某事
bring something to the foreground: 使某事突出
in the foreground: 在前景中
push something to the foreground: 把某事推到前台
foreground a discussion: 突出讨论
