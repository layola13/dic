jump:/dʒʌmp/ | /dʒʌmp/ | v. n. | 跳跃；跳动；骤增 | To move quickly and suddenly from one place to another by pushing oneself off the ground with one's legs.  It can also refer to a sudden increase or rise. | 例句：The frog jumped into the pond. 青蛙跳进了池塘。

例句：The price of oil jumped dramatically last week. 上周油价暴涨。

近义词：leap, hop, spring, bound | 反义词：stay, remain


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jumps rope every morning. = 他每天早上跳绳。
She jumps to conclusions too quickly. = 她下结论太快了。


一般过去时:
I jumped over the fence. = 我跳过了栅栏。
The rabbit jumped into the bushes. = 兔子跳进了灌木丛。


现在完成时:
He has jumped the gun several times. = 他已经几次操之过急了。
She has jumped at the opportunity. = 她欣然接受了这个机会。


过去完成时:
I had jumped to the wrong conclusion. = 我得出了错误的结论。
They had jumped the queue. = 他们插队了。


将来时:
I will jump for joy when I hear the good news. = 当我听到好消息时，我会欣喜若狂。
He will jump into action as soon as he receives the order. = 他一接到命令就会立即行动。


虚拟语气:
If I could jump that high, I would. = 如果我能跳那么高，我会跳。
I wish I could jump higher. = 我希望我能跳得更高。


被动语态:
The conclusion was jumped to prematurely. = 结论下得太草率了。
The opportunity was jumped at by many. = 许多人抓住这个机会。


疑问句:
Can you jump over that wall? = 你能跳过那堵墙吗？
Does he jump rope regularly? = 他经常跳绳吗？


否定句:
I didn't jump to any conclusions. = 我没有妄下结论。
The horse didn't jump the fence. = 马没有跳过栅栏。
