immortal:/ɪˈmɔːrtl/ | adj. | 不朽的；永生的 |  adj. Living forever; never dying or decaying.  | Example:  The ancient myths tell stories of immortal gods.  古代神话讲述了不朽的神的故事。

Example: Some people believe that the soul is immortal. 一些人认为灵魂是不朽的。


近义词：eternal, everlasting, deathless | 反义词：mortal, perishable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is immortal. 他是不朽的。
The gods are immortal. 神是不朽的。


一般过去时:
The hero was believed to be immortal.  人们相信这位英雄是不朽的。
They thought the king was immortal. 他们认为国王是不朽的。


现在完成时:
She has become immortalized in song. 她已在歌中永垂不朽。
His achievements have made him immortal. 他的成就使他永垂不朽。


过去完成时:
They had believed the emperor to be immortal. 他们曾认为皇帝是不朽的。
It had been thought that the queen was immortal.  人们曾认为王后是不朽的。


将来时:
He will be immortalized in history. 他将在历史上永垂不朽。
The legend will make her immortal. 这个传说会使她永垂不朽。


虚拟语气:
If he were immortal, he would not fear death. 如果他是永生的，他就不会害怕死亡。
I wish I were immortal. 我希望我是不朽的。


被动语态:
His name was immortalized in the book. 他的名字在书中被永载史册。
The artist was immortalized by his work.这位艺术家因其作品而名垂千古。


疑问句:
Is he immortal? 他是不朽的吗？
Are these gods immortal? 这些神是不朽的吗？


否定句:
He is not immortal; he will die someday. 他并非不朽；他总有一天会死。
The hero was not immortal, he died in battle.  这位英雄并非不朽，他死于战斗中。
