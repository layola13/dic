magistracy:/ˈmædʒɪstrəsi/| n. |治安官职位；司法权；法官的统治；行政管理 |The exercise of magisterial authority; the office or dignity of a magistrate.  The system or body of magistrates in a particular place or time. |例句：The magistracy was corrupt and ineffective. = 司法机构腐败且效率低下。

例句：The local magistracy worked hard to maintain order. = 当地治安官努力维持秩序。


近义词：judiciary, judicature, administration, governance |反义词：anarchy, lawlessness


词源:  From Middle French *magistrature, from Latin magistratus "magistrate," from magister "master, teacher."  The suffix "-cy" denotes "state, office, or rank."
