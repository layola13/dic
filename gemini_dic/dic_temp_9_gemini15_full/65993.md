exalts:/ɪɡˈzɔːlts/| vt.| 提升；赞扬；使得意；使兴高采烈 |To raise to a higher rank or position; to praise highly; to elate; to make proud or joyful. |例句：The people exalted their new king.  人民拥戴他们的新国王。

The victory exalted the team's spirits.  这场胜利使球队士气高涨。


近义词：elevate, uplift, praise, glorify | 反义词：debase, demean, humiliate, degrade |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exalts his achievements. 他夸耀他的成就。
The church exalts God. 教会尊崇上帝。


一般过去时:
The crowd exalted the hero. 人群欢呼英雄。
She exalted in her success. 她为自己的成功而欣喜若狂。


现在完成时:
They have exalted him to a position of power. 他们已将他提升到权力地位。
She has exalted the virtues of hard work. 她一直赞扬勤劳的美德。


过去完成时:
The people had exalted him before his downfall.  在他垮台之前，人们曾将他捧得很高。
He had exalted himself above his peers. 他曾把自己抬到同辈之上。


将来时:
They will exalt their leader. 他们将拥戴他们的领导人。
The media will exalt the athlete's win. 媒体将赞扬这位运动员的胜利。


虚拟语气:
If they exalted him, he would be very happy. 如果他们赞扬他，他会非常高兴。
I wish they would exalt my efforts. 我希望他们能赞扬我的努力。


被动语态:
He was exalted to the position of CEO. 他被提升为首席执行官。
Her work was exalted by the critics. 她的作品受到评论家的称赞。


疑问句:
Will they exalt him to a higher position? 他们会将他提升到更高的职位吗？
Does the company exalt its employees' contributions? 公司是否赞扬员工的贡献？


否定句:
They did not exalt him for his efforts. 他们并没有因为他的努力而赞扬他。
The company does not exalt mediocrity. 公司不赞扬平庸。
