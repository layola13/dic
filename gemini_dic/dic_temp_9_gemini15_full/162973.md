precipitancy: /prɪˈsɪpɪtənsi/ | n. |轻率；仓促；鲁莽 |  The quality or state of being precipitate; rashness; hastiness.  It suggests acting too quickly without sufficient thought or consideration, often leading to negative consequences.  |


例句：

His precipitancy in signing the contract led to unforeseen problems.  他的轻率签署合同导致了无法预料的问题。

The precipitancy of their decision surprised everyone.  他们仓促的决定让每个人都感到惊讶。


近义词：rashness, hastiness, impulsiveness, recklessness, hurry, urgency


反义词： deliberation, carefulness, cautiousness, slowness


词性：名词


词源：源于拉丁语 *praecipitāre (to throw headlong, to hasten)，  pre- (before) + caput (head)


USAGE->

This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  Therefore, tense examples are not applicable.  However, we can show its use in different sentence structures.


简单句:  Her precipitancy was the cause of the accident. 她的轻率导致了事故。

复合句: Because of his precipitancy, he missed a crucial detail. 因为他轻率，他错过了一个关键细节。

并列句:  He acted with precipitancy; consequently, he made a mistake. 他行事轻率；结果，他犯了一个错误。
