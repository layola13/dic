denisovans:/dɛˌnɪsəˈvɑːnz/| n. |丹尼索瓦人 |An extinct species or subspecies of archaic humans in the genus Homo.  Denisovans are known primarily from DNA evidence extracted from fossils found in Denisova Cave in Siberia.  Their relationship to Neanderthals and modern humans is still being studied. They coexisted with and interbred with Neanderthals and early Homo sapiens. |The discovery of Denisovan DNA in some modern human populations suggests interbreeding occurred. |近义词：archaic humans, hominins |反义词：modern humans
