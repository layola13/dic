counterreform: /ˈkaʊntərˌriːfɔːrm/ | n. | 反改革；对改革的抵制 | A counterreform is an attempt to reverse or undo reforms that have already been implemented. It often involves restoring previous policies, practices, or institutions.  Counterreforms are usually driven by opposition to the changes brought about by the original reforms, and can be motivated by political, social, economic, or ideological reasons. | Example: The rise of conservative forces led to a counterreform that dismantled many of the progressive social programs enacted in the previous decade.  |近义词：reaction, reversal, rollback | 反义词：reform, innovation, progress | 名词
