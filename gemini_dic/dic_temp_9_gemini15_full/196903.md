shades:/ʃeɪdz/ | n. |阴影；色度；遮光物；眼镜；细微差别 |The word "shades" refers to several things, primarily:  1. Areas of darkness caused by something blocking light. 2. Variations or degrees of a color. 3. Eyeglasses, especially sunglasses. 4. Subtle differences or nuances. |例句：The tall trees cast long shades across the lawn.  高大的树木在草坪上投下长长的树荫。

The painting uses subtle shades of blue and green. 这幅画使用了微妙的蓝绿色调。

She put on her shades to protect her eyes from the sun. 她戴上墨镜以保护眼睛免受阳光照射。

There are shades of meaning in his words that I don't fully understand. 他的话语中有一些我并不完全理解的含义。|近义词：tones, tints, nuances, hues, sunglasses |反义词：brightness, light, brilliance

词源:
The word "shade" comes from Old English *scēadu, meaning "shadow."  Its evolution reflects the various meanings it now encompasses, branching out from the basic concept of darkness or shadow to include the more figurative uses related to color and nuance.
