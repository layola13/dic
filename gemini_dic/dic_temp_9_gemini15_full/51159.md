despotic: /dɪˈspɒtɪk/ | adj. | 专制的；暴虐的；独裁的 | Relating to or characteristic of a despot; tyrannical.  Characterized by or exercising absolute power, especially in a cruel or oppressive way. |例句：The despotic ruler ignored the pleas of his people.  这个暴君无视他人民的恳求。

The country suffered under years of despotic rule. 这个国家在多年的专制统治下饱受折磨。


近义词：tyrannical, autocratic, authoritarian, dictatorial, oppressive | 反义词：democratic, liberal, benevolent, just, merciful | 词性:形容词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Late Latin despoticus, from Greek despōtikós, from despótēs master, lord, from des- + -pótēs master, from PIE *poti- master.


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The king acts despotically towards his subjects. 国王对他的臣民采取专制统治。
His management style is despotic and unfair. 他的管理风格专制且不公平。


一般过去时:
The emperor ruled despotically over his vast empire. 皇帝在其广阔的帝国中专制统治。
The dictator's regime was despotic and brutal. 独裁者的政权专制而残酷。


现在完成时:
The country has suffered under despotic rule for centuries. 这个国家几个世纪以来一直遭受专制统治的苦难。
His actions have proven him to be a despotic leader. 他的行为证明他是一个专制的领导者。


过去完成时:
The people had endured years of despotic leadership before the revolution. 在革命爆发之前，人民已经忍受了多年的专制统治。
They had already experienced the despotic nature of his rule. 他们已经体验到他统治的专制本质。


将来时:
If he gains power, he will likely rule despotically. 如果他获得权力，他很可能会实行专制统治。
The people fear that the new leader will rule despotically. 人们担心新领导人会实行专制统治。


虚拟语气:
If he were not despotic, the people would be happier. 如果他不专制，人民会更幸福。
I wish the government were less despotic. 我希望政府不那么专制。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
The government is not despotic; it is democratic. 政府不是专制的；它是民主的。
