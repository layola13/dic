Ersatz: /ˈɛərzɑːts/ | adj. n. | 代替品；假的；劣质的 |  A substitute, typically an inferior one.  Ersatz products are often used when the real thing is unavailable or too expensive.  The word carries a connotation of being less desirable or of lower quality than the original. | 例句：The coffee shop served ersatz coffee made from chicory. = 这家咖啡店供应用菊苣制成的劣质咖啡。


The cake was made with ersatz butter, and it tasted awful. = 蛋糕是用劣质黄油做的，味道很糟糕。| 近义词：substitute, replacement, imitation, makeshift | 反义词：genuine, authentic, real, original |  adj. n.


词源:  The word "Ersatz" originates from the German word meaning "substitute" or "replacement." It gained widespread use during World War I and World War II in Germany, when shortages of many goods led to the widespread use of substitute materials.


用法说明:

Ersatz can function as both an adjective and a noun.

As an adjective, it modifies a noun to describe something that is a substitute, often inferior:

例句:  They used ersatz sugar in the recipe. = 他们在这个食谱中使用了代糖。

As a noun, it refers to the substitute itself:

例句: This coffee is just ersatz; I prefer real coffee beans. = 这咖啡只是代用品；我更喜欢真正的咖啡豆。


由于"Ersatz"带有负面含义，暗示替代品不如原品，因此在使用时应注意语境。
