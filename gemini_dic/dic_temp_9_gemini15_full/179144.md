redictated:/ˌriːˈdɪkteɪtɪd/| adj.|重新口述的；重新听写的|  This is a word formed by combining "re-" (meaning again) and "dictated" (past participle of "dictate," meaning to speak or read something aloud to be written down). It describes something that has been dictated again, perhaps due to errors or for clarification.  There is no standard usage of this word in common dictionaries.  It's more likely to appear in specialized contexts or as a neologism. |Example: The lawyer asked the witness to redictate her testimony after a misunderstanding. = 律师要求证人在发生误解后重新口述证词。|近义词: re-recorded, repeated, rewritten|反义词:original, first version|adj.
