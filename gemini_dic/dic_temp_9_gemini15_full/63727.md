ephraim:/ˈiːfreɪm/| n. |以法莲(人名，以色列支派)|Ephraim is a male given name of Hebrew origin, meaning "fruitful" or "double." It refers to one of the twelve tribes of Israel, descended from Ephraim, the younger son of Joseph.  The name is associated with fertility, abundance, and prosperity.  It's a relatively common name in some cultures and less so in others. |Example:  The tribe of Ephraim was a powerful and influential one in ancient Israel. 以法莲支派在古代以色列是一个强大而有影响力的支派。


The name Ephraim comes from the Hebrew word אֶפְרָיִם (Ephrayim), which combines the preposition "eph" (אֶפ) meaning "upon" or "over" and the root "raim" (רָיִם) meaning "double" or "fruitful."  Therefore, the name's meaning is interpreted as "double" (implying a doubling of blessings, fruitfulness, or prosperity) or "fruitful" (referring to abundance and fertility).
