disdains:/dɪsˈdeɪnz/| vt.|轻蔑；鄙视；不屑；认为不值得 | to consider (someone or something) unworthy of one's consideration |She disdains cheap imitations. 她鄙视廉价的仿制品。|近义词：scorn, despise, contemn |反义词：admire, respect, esteem


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disdains their offers of help. 他不屑于接受他们提供的帮助。
She disdains to associate with such people. 她不屑与这样的人交往。


一般过去时:
They disdained the easy way out. 他们不屑于走捷径。
He disdained to answer their questions. 他不屑回答他们的问题。


现在完成时:
She has always disdained gossip. 她一直以来都鄙视流言蜚语。
He has disdained the opportunity to advance his career. 他不屑于抓住提升事业的机会。


过去完成时:
They had disdained the warnings, and now they were in trouble. 他们之前不屑于理会警告，现在他们陷入困境了。
She had disdained his advances for months. 她已经不屑于他的追求好几个月了。


将来时:
He will disdain to compromise his principles. 他将不屑于妥协他的原则。
She will disdain any attempt to manipulate her. 她将鄙视任何试图操纵她的企图。


虚拟语气:
I would disdain to lie to you. 我不屑于对你撒谎。
If he were in my position, he would disdain such behavior. 如果他处于我的位置，他会鄙视这种行为。


被动语态:
His efforts were disdained by his colleagues. 他的努力遭到同事们的轻蔑。
Her talent was disdained by the judges. 她的才能被评委们所轻视。


疑问句:
Does he disdain the work of contemporary artists? 他是否轻视当代艺术家的作品？
Do they disdain to participate in the competition? 他们是否不屑于参加比赛？


否定句:
He does not disdain hard work. 他并不轻视努力工作。
She does not disdain to ask for help when she needs it. 她需要帮助时并不不屑于寻求帮助。
