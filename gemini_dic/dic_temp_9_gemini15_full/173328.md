quasi-deliberately: /ˈkwɑːzi dɪˈlɪbərətli/ | adj. | 似是而非地故意地；貌似故意地 | Seemingly or apparently deliberate, but perhaps not entirely intentional;  suggesting a lack of full commitment or conscious effort.  It implies an action that has some element of intentionality but falls short of being fully or completely deliberate. |

例句：

He quasi-deliberately ignored her calls, hoping she would get the message. = 他似是而非地故意不接她的电话，希望她能明白他的意思。

She quasi-deliberately avoided eye contact, a subtle way of showing her displeasure. = 她貌似故意地避免眼神接触，这是一种微妙的方式来表达她的不满。


近义词：seemingly intentionally, apparently deliberately, half-heartedly, unintentionally (with a hint of intention), with a pretense of deliberation


反义词：deliberately, intentionally, accidentally, unconsciously


词性：形容词


词源：

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "as it were," "almost," or "nearly."  It indicates a resemblance or partial similarity.

* **deliberately**: From Middle English *deliberatli*, from Old French *deliberer* (to consider, deliberate), from Latin *deliberare* (to weigh, consider), from *libero* (to free, liberate).  This signifies conscious and intentional action.
