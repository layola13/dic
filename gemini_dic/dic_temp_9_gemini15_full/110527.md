limbs:/lɪmz/| n.|肢体，四肢|The parts of a body that stick out from the main trunk, such as arms and legs.  They are used for movement and support.|例句：The injured man couldn't move his limbs. 受伤的男子无法动弹他的四肢。

例句：Animals have different numbers of limbs. 动物有不同数量的肢体。


近义词：extremities, members|反义词：trunk, torso|词性:名词

词源:
The word "limb" comes from Old English *lim, meaning "limb, member, branch," related to Old High German *lim "limb," Old Norse *limi "member."  It ultimately traces back to Proto-Germanic *limbaz, suggesting a common ancestor among Germanic languages for this term related to body parts.
