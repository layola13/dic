capsizing:/ˈkæpsʌɪzɪŋ/| v.|倾覆；翻船|The act or process of a boat or ship turning over; to overturn or turn upside down.|例句：The small boat capsized in the rough sea. 小船在汹涌的海浪中翻了。|例句：The strong winds almost capsized the sailboat. 强风差点儿把帆船掀翻。|近义词：overturning, upsetting, sinking|反义词：righting, stabilizing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The boat capsizes easily in strong winds. = 这艘船在大风中很容易倾覆。
He capsizes his kayak frequently during training. = 他在训练期间经常翻他的皮划艇。


一般过去时:
The ship capsized during the storm. = 船在暴风雨中倾覆了。
The canoe capsized, and we had to swim to shore. = 独木舟翻了，我们不得不游回岸边。


现在完成时:
The small fishing boat has capsized several times this year. = 这艘小渔船今年已经倾覆了好几次了。
Three people have capsized their kayaks in the race. = 三个人在比赛中翻了他们的皮划艇。


过去完成时:
The life raft had capsized before the rescue team arrived. = 救生艇在救援队到达之前就已经倾覆了。
They had capsized their boat hours before we found them. = 在我们找到他们之前，他们的船几个小时前就已经翻了。


将来时:
The overloaded ferry will likely capsize if the storm hits. = 如果暴风雨来临，超载的渡轮很可能会倾覆。
If the wind picks up, the sailboat might capsize. = 如果风力加大，帆船可能会倾覆。


虚拟语气:
If the boat capsized, we would have been lost at sea. = 如果船翻了，我们就会在海上迷失。
I wish the kayak hadn't capsized; I lost my paddle. = 我真希望皮划艇没有翻，我的桨掉了。


被动语态:
The sailboat was capsized by a sudden gust of wind. = 帆船被一阵突如其来的大风掀翻了。
The small boat was capsized in the rapids. = 小船在激流中翻了。


疑问句:
Did the ship capsize? = 船翻了吗？
Has the boat capsized yet? = 船翻了吗？


否定句:
The boat did not capsize despite the rough weather. = 尽管天气恶劣，船却没有翻。
The ship didn't capsize, even though it was taking on water. = 尽管进水，船却没有翻。
