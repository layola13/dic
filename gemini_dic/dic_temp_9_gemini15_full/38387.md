conceptualising: /kənˈsɛptʃuəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 概念化；构想；设想 | The act of forming a concept or idea; to form a concept or idea of something.  |  She is conceptualising a new marketing campaign. 她正在构思一个新的营销活动。  He was conceptualising the plot of his new novel. 他正在构思他新小说的情节。 |  imagining, envisioning, conceiving |  unrealising, materialising | 动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They are conceptualising a new product. = 他们正在构思一款新产品。
We conceptualise our work differently. = 我们对工作的概念化方式不同。


一般过去时:
The artist conceptualised the painting in vivid detail. = 艺术家对这幅画进行了生动的构思。
They conceptualised a new approach to the problem. = 他们构思了一种解决这个问题的新方法。


现在完成时:
She has conceptualised several successful advertising campaigns. = 她已经构思出几个成功的广告宣传活动。
We have conceptualised a plan to improve efficiency. = 我们已经构思出一个提高效率的计划。


过去完成时:
He had conceptualised the design before starting the project. = 他在开始项目之前就已经构思好了设计。
They had conceptualised the solution before presenting it. = 他们在提出解决方案之前就已经构思好了。


将来时:
We will conceptualise a new strategy for the next quarter. = 我们将在下个季度构思一个新的战略。
They will conceptualise the event in a unique way. = 他们将以独特的方式构思这次活动。


虚拟语气:
If I could conceptualise a better solution, I would. = 如果我能构思出一个更好的解决方案，我会的。
I wish I could conceptualise a way to solve this problem. = 我希望我能想出一个解决这个问题的方法。


被动语态:
The project was conceptualised by a team of experts. = 这个项目是由一个专家团队构思的。
The new marketing plan is being conceptualised at the moment. = 新的营销计划正在制定中。


疑问句:
How did they conceptualise the artwork? = 他们是如何构思这件艺术品的？
Have you conceptualised a solution yet? = 你已经构思出解决方案了吗？


否定句:
I couldn't conceptualise a way to do it. = 我无法构思出一种方法来做这件事。
They didn't conceptualise the project thoroughly. = 他们没有彻底构思这个项目。


PHRASE->
Conceptualising a new idea = 构思一个新想法
Conceptualising a strategy = 构思一个策略
Conceptualising a design = 构思一个设计
Conceptualising a solution = 构思一个解决方案
Conceptualising a problem = 构思一个问题


词源:

The word "conceptualising" comes from the noun "concept" (from Latin "conceptus," past participle of "concipere," meaning "to conceive") plus the suffix "-alising," indicating the process of forming or developing.
