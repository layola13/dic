/mɪˈsteɪks/ | n. | 错误，过失 | A mistake is an action, statement, or judgment that is incorrect or wrong.  Mistakes can be unintentional or due to a lack of knowledge or understanding. | Example:  I made a mistake in my calculations. 我计算错了。|近义词：error, blunder, fault, slip |反义词：correctness, accuracy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He makes many mistakes. = 他犯了很多错误。
She makes a mistake in her spelling. = 她在拼写上犯了一个错误。


一般过去时:
I made a mistake yesterday. = 我昨天犯了一个错误。
They made several mistakes in the report. = 他们在报告中犯了几个错误。


现在完成时:
I have made several mistakes this week. = 我这周犯了好几个错误。
She has never made such a big mistake before. = 她以前从未犯过这么大的错误。


过去完成时:
I had already made several mistakes before I realized it. = 我意识到之前就已经犯了好几个错误了。
They had made a serious mistake in their planning. = 他们在计划中犯了一个严重的错误。


将来时:
I will make mistakes in the future, but I will learn from them. = 我将来会犯错，但我将从中学习。
He will likely make a mistake if he rushes. = 如果他赶时间，很可能会犯错误。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't made that mistake, I would have won the game. = 如果我没有犯那个错误，我就能赢得比赛了。
I wish I hadn't made so many mistakes. = 我真希望我没有犯那么多错误。


被动语态:
Many mistakes were made in the construction. = 建筑中犯了许多错误。
A mistake was made in the accounting. = 会计工作中犯了一个错误。


疑问句:
Did you make any mistakes? = 你犯了什么错误吗？
What mistakes did you make? = 你犯了什么错误？


否定句:
I didn't make any mistakes. = 我没有犯任何错误。
He didn't make a single mistake. = 他没有犯一个错误。


PHRASE->
make a mistake = 犯错
avoid mistakes = 避免错误
correct mistakes = 纠正错误
learn from mistakes = 从错误中学习
a series of mistakes = 一系列错误
