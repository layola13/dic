rhapsodised:/ˈræpsədaɪzd/| v.|狂想；热情洋溢地表达；赞美；|to speak or write about something with great enthusiasm and feeling, often in a way that is somewhat disorganized or rambling.|例句：She rhapsodised about her trip to Italy. 她热情洋溢地描述了她去意大利的旅行。|She rhapsodised over the beauty of the sunset. 她陶醉于日落的美景。|近义词：rave, enthuse, extol|反义词：criticize, condemn, disparage


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He rhapsodizes about his childhood memories. = 他兴高采烈地谈论着他的童年回忆。
She rhapsodizes beautifully about nature. = 她优美地赞颂着大自然。


一般过去时:
He rhapsodized about the concert last night. = 他昨晚兴高采烈地谈论着这场音乐会。
She rhapsodized eloquently about the painting. = 她雄辩地赞美了这幅画。


现在完成时:
He has rhapsodized about that book for weeks. = 他几周来一直兴高采烈地谈论那本书。
She has rhapsodized about her travels extensively. = 她对她的旅行做了大量的热情洋溢的描述。


过去完成时:
He had rhapsodized about the play before we even saw it. = 在我们看戏之前，他就已经兴高采烈地谈论过这部戏剧了。
She had rhapsodized about her new job throughout the interview. = 在整个面试过程中，她一直热情洋溢地谈论着她的新工作。


将来时:
He will rhapsodize about his success at the meeting. = 他将在会议上热情洋溢地谈论他的成功。
She will rhapsodize about the wedding for years to come. = 今后几年她都会热情洋溢地谈论这场婚礼。


虚拟语气:
If he saw the artwork, he would rhapsodize about its beauty. = 如果他看到这件艺术品，他会热情洋溢地谈论它的美丽。
I wish I could rhapsodize as eloquently as she does. = 我希望我能像她一样雄辩地表达我的热情。


被动语态:
His achievements were rhapsodized about in the newspaper. = 他的成就被报纸热情洋溢地报道。
The beauty of the landscape was rhapsodized in his poem. = 他在他的诗歌中热情洋溢地赞美了这片美丽的风景。


疑问句:
Does he often rhapsodize about his travels? = 他经常热情洋溢地谈论他的旅行吗？
Will she rhapsodize about the concert tonight? = 她今晚会热情洋溢地谈论这场音乐会吗？


否定句:
He did not rhapsodize about the film; he found it quite boring. = 他并没有对这部电影赞不绝口；他觉得它相当无聊。
She didn't rhapsodize about the food, instead, she criticized it. = 她并没有对食物赞不绝口，相反，她批评了它。



PHRASE->
rhapsodize about something = 热情洋溢地谈论某事
rhapsodize on a subject = 就某一主题热情洋溢地发表意见
rhapsodize over something = 陶醉于某事物
rhapsodize with enthusiasm = 热情洋溢地赞美
rhapsodize in detail = 详细地热情洋溢地描述


词源:
From the Greek word "rhapsodia," meaning "stitched together song," referring to the way ancient Greek poets would stitch together verses to create a longer narrative.  The word evolved through French (rhapsodie) to its modern English form.
