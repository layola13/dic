pardonably:/ˈpɑːdnəbli/| adj.| 可原谅的，情有可原的 |  Adjective.  Forgivable; excusable.  It implies that an action or behavior, while wrong or inappropriate, can be understood and accepted due to mitigating circumstances. |例句：Her lateness was pardonably due to the unexpected traffic jam.  她的迟到是因为意外的交通堵塞，是可以原谅的。

Her outburst was pardonably emotional, considering the circumstances. 考虑到当时的状况，她的爆发是可以理解的。|近义词：forgivable, excusable, understandable, justifiable |反义词：unforgivable, inexcusable, unjustifiable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His behavior is pardonably childish. = 他的行为虽然幼稚，但可以理解。
The delay is pardonably understandable given the circumstances. = 考虑到情况，延误是可以理解的。


一般过去时:

His absence was pardonably explained. = 他的缺席得到了情有可原的解释。


现在完成时:
His actions have been pardonably explained by his difficult childhood. = 他的行为因其艰难的童年而得到了情有可原的解释。
Everything has been pardonably overlooked. = 一切都被宽容地忽略了。


过去完成时:
The error had been pardonably overlooked. = 这个错误已经被原谅了。
It had been pardonably forgotten. = 这已经被情有可原地忘记了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:

词源:
Pardon (v.) + able (adj.)  “pardon” 源自古法语"pardonner"，最终来自拉丁语“perdonare”，意为“给予；原谅”。 “able”是常见的形容词后缀，表示“能够…的”。
