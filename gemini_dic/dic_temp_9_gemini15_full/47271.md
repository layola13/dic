de-anglicizing: /ˌdiːˈæŋɡlɪsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 去英语化；去除英语影响 | The process of removing or reducing the influence of English language and culture.  This can involve replacing English words with native words, adapting English loanwords to fit the phonology and grammar of the target language, or promoting the use of the native language in various domains. | 例句：The government initiated a policy of de-anglicizing the national curriculum. = 政府启动了一项去英语化国家课程的政策。


例句：The movement aimed at de-anglicizing the country's media. = 这场运动旨在使国家的媒体去英语化。


近义词：vernacularization, indigenization, nationalization (in the context of language) | 反义词：anglicization, americanization


词性：动词
