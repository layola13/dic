guilds:/ɡɪldz/| n.|行会；协会；公会|Groups of artisans or merchants who formed associations to protect their interests, regulate their craft, and maintain standards.  They often held significant social and economic power in the medieval period and beyond.|Example: The medieval guilds were powerful organizations that controlled the production and sale of goods. 中世纪的行会是控制商品生产和销售的强大组织。|近义词：association, society, union|反义词：None directly applicable.
