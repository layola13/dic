counterincentives: /ˌkaʊntərɪnˈsɛntɪvz/ | n. | 反激励措施 | Things that discourage or prevent a particular action.  Counterincentives are designed to deter undesirable behavior or choices by making the alternative less appealing or more costly.  They are often used in policy making, economics, and behavioral psychology. | Example:  High taxes on cigarettes act as a counterincentive to smoking.  高额的香烟税是对吸烟的反激励措施。  The threat of punishment serves as a counterincentive to criminal activity. 惩罚的威胁是对犯罪活动的反激励措施。 | 近义词: deterrents, disincentives, penalties | 反义词: incentives, encouragements |  名词
