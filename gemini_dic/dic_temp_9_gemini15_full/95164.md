inartificial: /ˌɪnˌɑːrtɪˈfɪʃəl/ | adj. | 不自然的；非人工的；自然的 | Not artificial; natural.  The inartificial beauty of the landscape captivated us.  | 近义词：natural, genuine, uncontrived | 反义词：artificial, synthetic, manufactured |


英文解析:The word "inartificial" is formed by the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "artificial." Therefore, it directly means "not artificial," emphasizing a lack of artificiality or pretense. It suggests something genuine, natural, or uncontrived.

例句:

The inartificial charm of the old village contrasted sharply with the polished artificiality of the new town.  = 古老村庄质朴的魅力与新城人工雕琢的精致形成鲜明对比。

His inartificial manner put people at ease; there was no pretense or affectation in his speech or behavior. = 他自然不做作的态度使人感到轻松自在；他的言谈举止中没有任何虚伪或矫饰。


词源:

* **in-**:  A prefix meaning "not," "without," or "opposite of."  This is a common negative prefix in English, derived from Old English and Latin.

* **artificial**:  Derived from the Latin "artificialis," meaning "skillful," "artifex" (artist, craftsman).  It later came to mean "made by human skill, not occurring naturally."

用法说明:

"Inartificial" is primarily used as an adjective to describe something that is not artificial, that lacks pretense, or is naturally occurring.  It is often used to highlight a positive quality of genuineness or authenticity.  The word carries a slightly formal tone.
