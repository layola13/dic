gridlocking:/ˈɡrɪdˌlɑːkɪŋ/ | n. |交通瘫痪；严重堵塞；僵局 | The act of bringing a system, especially a transportation network, to a standstill due to congestion or obstruction.  It often implies a complex and difficult-to-resolve situation. |Example: The city's gridlocking traffic made it impossible to reach the airport on time.  城市的交通严重堵塞，导致无法按时到达机场。

The heavy snow caused a gridlock on the major highways.  大雪导致主要高速公路交通瘫痪。|近义词：traffic jam, standstill, deadlock, impasse |反义词：smooth flow, free movement
