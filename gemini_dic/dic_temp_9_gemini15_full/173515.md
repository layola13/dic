quasi-freely:/ˈkwɑːzaɪˈfriːli/ | adv. | 准自由地，近乎自由地 | Almost freely; with a degree of freedom but not complete freedom.  The term often implies some constraints or limitations on the freedom. | Example: The electrons in a metal behave quasi-freely, meaning their movement is restricted by the crystal lattice but not completely confined. 电子在金属中表现出准自由性，这意味着它们的运动受到晶格的限制，但并非完全受限。| 近义词:relatively freely, nearly freely | 反义词:completely bound, entirely restricted | 词性:副词
