sins:/sɪnz/| n.|罪过，罪恶|wrongdoings, especially those that are considered morally or religiously wrong.  The word refers to transgressions against religious or moral laws, often resulting in feelings of guilt or shame. |例句：He confessed his sins to the priest. 他向牧师忏悔了他的罪过。

例句：Her sins were many, but she hoped for forgiveness. 她的罪孽很多，但她希望能得到宽恕。


近义词：wrongdoings, transgressions, offenses, crimes, misdeeds

反义词：virtues, righteousness, goodness

词源:
The word "sins" is the plural form of the Old English word "sinn," meaning "sin, offense, transgression."  It ultimately derives from Proto-Germanic *sunjon, and is related to words in other Germanic languages with similar meanings. The religious and moral connotations are ancient and deeply ingrained in the word's usage.
