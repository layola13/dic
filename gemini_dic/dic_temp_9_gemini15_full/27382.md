camel:/ˈkæməl/| n.|骆驼|A large, humped mammal adapted to living in deserts. Camels are known for their ability to endure long periods without water and food.|例句：The camel crossed the vast desert. 这只骆驼穿越了广阔的沙漠。|例句：Camels are well-suited to desert life. 骆驼非常适合沙漠生活。|近义词：dromedary|反义词:无


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Camels carry heavy loads. = 骆驼驮运重物。
The camel drinks a lot of water after a long journey. = 骆驼长途跋涉后会喝很多水。

一般过去时:
The camel caravan crossed the Sahara Desert. = 骆驼商队穿越了撒哈拉沙漠。
We saw a camel in the zoo yesterday. = 我们昨天在动物园里看到了一只骆驼。

现在完成时:
The camel has travelled many miles. = 这只骆驼已经旅行了许多英里。
She has ridden a camel before. = 她以前骑过骆驼。

过去完成时:
The camel had already drunk before we arrived. =在我们到达之前，骆驼已经喝过水了。
They had seen many camels on their trip. = 他们在旅行中看到过许多骆驼。


将来时:
We will ride a camel tomorrow. = 我们明天将骑骆驼。
The camel will carry our supplies across the desert. = 骆驼将把我们的物资运过沙漠。


虚拟语气:
If I had a camel, I would travel across the desert. = 如果我有一只骆驼，我就会穿越沙漠。
I wish I could ride a camel. = 我真希望我能骑骆驼。


被动语态:

Many camels are used for transportation in desert regions. = 在沙漠地区，许多骆驼被用于运输。


疑问句:
Is that a camel? = 那是一只骆驼吗？
How many camels are there in the desert? = 沙漠里有多少只骆驼？


否定句:

词源:
The word "camel" entered English from Old French *camel*, ultimately from Latin *camelus*, borrowed from Greek κάμηλος (kámēlos), which in turn may be of Semitic origin.  Different theories exist regarding the precise Semitic etymology.
