gradualistic:/ˌɡrædʒuˈælɪstɪk/| adj.| 渐变的；渐进的；逐步的 |  Relating to or characterized by gradualism; proceeding by gradual stages or degrees.  It emphasizes slow, incremental change over time, often in contrast to punctuated equilibrium. |例句：The gradualistic approach to reform proved more effective in the long run. 渐进的改革方法从长远来看证明更有效。

The company adopted a gradualistic strategy for market expansion, entering new territories one by one.  这家公司采用渐进的市场扩张战略，一个接一个地进入新的领域。| 近义词：incremental, progressive, phased, step-by-step | 反义词：sudden, abrupt, rapid, instantaneous


词源:  Derived from "gradual" + "-istic,"  "gradual" originating from Old French *graduel, from Latin *gradualis, from gradus ("step").  The suffix "-istic" denotes "relating to" or "characterized by."
