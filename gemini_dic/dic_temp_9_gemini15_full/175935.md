rationalists:/ˈræʃənəlɪsts/| n. |理性主义者|A person who believes in the importance of reason and logic in gaining knowledge.  Rationalists emphasize reason and logic over experience or faith in acquiring knowledge. They believe that the world is governed by rational principles that can be discovered through reason and logical analysis. |Example: Many Enlightenment thinkers were rationalists who believed in the power of human reason to solve societal problems.  许多启蒙思想家都是理性主义者，他们相信人的理性能够解决社会问题。|近义词：intellectuals, philosophers, empiricists (in contrast, but often overlap)|反义词：empiricists (in some contexts), fideists, mystics|名词
