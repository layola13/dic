momentous:/moʊˈmɛntəs/ | adj. | 重要的；具有重大意义的；值得纪念的 | extremely important, significant, or memorable.  The momentous occasion marked a turning point in history.  This decision will have momentous consequences. |近义词：significant, important, momentous, crucial, vital, consequential | 反义词：insignificant, unimportant, trivial


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The event is momentous. = 这个事件很重要。
This decision is momentous for the company. = 这个决定对公司至关重要。

一般过去时:
The signing of the treaty was a momentous event. = 签订条约是一个重要的事件。
The discovery was momentous for scientific research. = 这一发现对科学研究具有重大意义。

现在完成时:
The changes have been momentous. = 这些变化意义重大。
The impact of the invention has proven momentous. = 这项发明的冲击已被证明意义重大。

过去完成时:
They hadn't realized how momentous their decision had been. = 他们没有意识到他们的决定有多么重要。
The consequences of the war had become increasingly momentous. = 战争的后果变得越来越严重。

将来时:
The upcoming election will be a momentous occasion. = 即将到来的选举将是一个重要的时刻。
The effects of this policy will be momentous in the years to come. =这项政策的影响将在未来几年产生重大影响。

虚拟语气:
If he had known the decision would be so momentous, he would have acted differently. = 如果他知道这个决定如此重要，他会采取不同的行动。
It would be a momentous occasion if peace were achieved.  = 如果和平实现，这将是一个重要的时刻。

被动语态:
The event was considered momentous by many historians. = 许多历史学家认为这一事件意义重大。
The implications of the discovery are being hailed as momentous. = 这一发现的意义被誉为意义重大。

疑问句:
Is this a momentous occasion? = 这是一个重要的时刻吗？
Will this decision prove to be momentous? = 这个决定会证明很重要吗？

否定句:
The meeting wasn't momentous at all. = 会议一点也不重要。
The changes weren't as momentous as some people claimed. = 变化并没有像有些人声称的那样重要。


PHRASE->
a momentous decision = 一个重大的决定
a momentous occasion = 一个重要的时刻
a momentous event = 一个重大的事件
of momentous importance = 至关重要
momentous change = 重大的变化

词源:
From moment + -ous.  The suffix "-ous" means "full of" or "having the quality of."  Therefore, "momentous" literally means "full of moment" or "having great importance."  The word "moment" here refers to importance or significance, not a point in time.
