disavow:/dɪsæˈvaʊ/| vt.|否认；与…断绝关系；拒绝承认|To deny or repudiate; to refuse to acknowledge or accept.  The politician disavowed any knowledge of the scandal. |近义词：deny, repudiate, renounce, reject|反义词：affirm, acknowledge, admit, endorse|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disavows any responsibility for the accident. = 他否认对事故有任何责任。
The company disavows the actions of its former CEO. = 公司否认其前任首席执行官的行为。


一般过去时:
She disavowed her previous statement. = 她否认了她之前的声明。
The government disavowed the actions of its agents. = 政府否认其特工的行为。


现在完成时:
He has disavowed all connection with the organization. = 他已经否认与该组织有任何联系。
The witness has disavowed her earlier testimony. = 证人已经否认了她早前的证词。


过去完成时:
They had disavowed their allegiance to the king. = 他们已经否认了对国王的效忠。
She had disavowed her belief in the theory. = 她已经否认了她对这个理论的信仰。


将来时:
He will disavow any involvement in the plot. = 他将否认参与该阴谋。
The company will disavow the fraudulent activities. = 公司将否认欺诈活动。


虚拟语气:
If he disavowed his actions, he might avoid prosecution. = 如果他否认他的行为，他可能会避免起诉。
I wish he had disavowed the rumors earlier. = 我希望他早点否认这些谣言。


被动语态:
His statement was disavowed by the organization. = 他的声明被该组织否认了。
The deal was disavowed by both parties. = 这笔交易被双方否认了。


疑问句:
Will the government disavow the report? = 政府会否认这份报告吗？
Did the suspect disavow his involvement? = 嫌疑人是否否认了他的参与？


否定句:
He did not disavow the accusations against him. = 他没有否认针对他的指控。
The company will not disavow its responsibility. = 公司不会否认其责任。


PHRASE->
disavow responsibility for = 否认责任
disavow allegiance to = 否认效忠于
disavow knowledge of = 否认知道
disavow one's past = 否认自己的过去
disavow a claim = 否认一项说法


词源:
The word "disavow" comes from the Old French word "desavouer," which is composed of the prefix "des-" (meaning "to do the opposite of") and "avouer" (meaning "to confess" or "to admit").  Therefore, "disavow" literally means "to un-confess" or "to deny."
