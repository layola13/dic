overtures:/ˈoʊvərtʃərz/ | n. |序曲；开场白；表示友好或合作的姿态 | An overture is an introductory act or statement.  It can refer to a musical composition, a preliminary negotiation, or an attempt to initiate a friendly relationship. | 例句：The orchestra played a beautiful overture before the opera began. 乐队在歌剧开始前演奏了一首优美的序曲。


The peace overtures were rejected by the opposing side.  对方拒绝了和平的表示。| 近义词：prelude, introduction, approach, advance | 反义词：conclusion, finale, rejection


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company makes overtures to potential investors. = 公司向潜在投资者示好。
His overtures of friendship are sincere. = 他友好的表示是真诚的。


一般过去时:
They made overtures to negotiate a peace treaty. = 他们主动提出谈判签订和平条约。
She rejected his romantic overtures. = 她拒绝了他的求爱。


现在完成时:
The government has made several overtures to resolve the conflict. = 政府已经做出几次努力来解决冲突。
He has made numerous overtures to his estranged brother. = 他多次向疏远的兄弟示好。


过去完成时:
Before the meeting, they had made overtures to the other party. = 会议前，他们已经向对方示好。
The company had made overtures to acquire the smaller firm before the offer was publicly announced. = 在收购要约公开宣布之前，该公司已试图收购这家较小的公司。


将来时:
The diplomat will make overtures to improve relations between the two countries. = 这位外交官将努力改善两国关系。
They will make overtures to secure a new business partnership. = 他们将努力争取新的商业伙伴关系。


虚拟语气:
If they had made more conciliatory overtures, the conflict might have been avoided. = 如果他们做出更缓和的表示，冲突本可以避免。
I wish he had responded positively to my overtures of friendship. = 我真希望他对我的友好表示有积极回应。


被动语态:
Peace overtures were ignored by the warring factions. = 交战各方无视和平的表示。
His overtures were met with suspicion. = 他的表示受到了怀疑。


疑问句:
What overtures have been made to resolve the dispute? = 为解决争端已经采取了什么措施？
Were any overtures made before the war broke out? = 在战争爆发前有没有任何和解的表示？


否定句:
They made no overtures towards reconciliation. = 他们没有做出任何和解的表示。
The company didn't make any overtures to the striking workers. = 公司没有向罢工工人做出任何表示。



PHRASE->
make overtures to = 向…表示友好
reject overtures = 拒绝表示
peaceful overtures = 和平的表示
romantic overtures = 求爱
hostile overtures = 敌意的表示


词源:
源自法语 *ouverture*,  最终源自拉丁语 *aperire* ("to open").  最初指歌剧或其他音乐作品的序曲，后来引申出其他含义。
