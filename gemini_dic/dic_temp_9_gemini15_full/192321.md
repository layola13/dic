scribbles:/ˈskrɪbəlz/ | n. |潦草的字迹；涂鸦 |  Rapidly and carelessly written or drawn marks;  a messy piece of writing. |例句：The child's scribbles covered the entire page.  孩子的涂鸦覆盖了整页纸。|例句：His notes were just a collection of illegible scribbles. 他的笔记只是一些难以辨认的潦草字迹。|近义词： doodles, scrawls, jottings | 反义词： elegant writing, calligraphy | 名词

词源:
The word "scribble" originates from the Middle English word "scriblen," which likely came from a combination of the Old French word "escribler" (to write hastily) and  the Old English "scrífan" (to write).  The evolution reflects the sense of quick, careless writing.
