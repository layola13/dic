preinduce:/ˌpriːɪnˈdjuːs/| vt.| 预先诱导；预先促成 | To cause (something) to happen or exist before its usual or expected time.  It suggests a proactive and anticipatory action to bring about a certain outcome. |例句：The company tried to preinduce a positive response to their new product by releasing teaser advertisements. 公司试图通过发布预告广告来预先引发对其新产品的积极回应。

例句：The early frost preinduced a premature leaf fall.  早霜导致提前落叶。


近义词：anticipate, preempt, forestall, initiate, prompt


反义词：delay, postpone, hinder, obstruct


词性:动词
