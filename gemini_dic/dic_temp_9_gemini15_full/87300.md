hermitish: /hɜːˈmɪtɪʃ/ | adj. | 隐居的；苦行的；似隐士的 |  Characterized by or suggestive of a hermit; solitary; reclusive.  The word implies a preference for seclusion and a simple life, often associated with religious or philosophical contemplation.  |

例句：

He led a hermitish existence in a remote cabin in the mountains. 他在山上偏僻的小屋里过着隐居的生活。

The hermitish lifestyle appealed to her desire for peace and quiet. 隐居的生活方式吸引了她对平静的渴望。


近义词：reclusive, solitary, secluded, ascetic

反义词：sociable, gregarious, outgoing


词源：

Derived from "hermit," ultimately from the Late Latin word *heremita*, which comes from the Greek word *erēmitēs*, meaning "desert dweller."  This, in turn, is related to *ēremos*, meaning "desert."  The suffix "-ish" adds a slightly less intense or more approximate quality to the adjective.
