interpellate:/ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪt/ | vt. |质问；打断；向…提出质疑 | To interrupt (someone) with a question or remark; to formally accuse or challenge.  |例句：The senator interpellated the minister about the new policy. 参议员就新政策质问了部长。

例句：The lawyer interpellated the witness to clarify their statement. 律师打断证人以澄清其证词。


近义词：question, challenge, interrogate, accost, heckle

反义词：support, endorse, confirm


词源：From Latin *interpellare, from inter- "between" + pellare "to drive, urge".  The original sense was to interrupt or call upon someone.
