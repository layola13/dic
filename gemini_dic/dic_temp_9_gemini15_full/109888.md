liaised:/laɪˈeɪzd/ | v. | 联系；协商；合作 | To communicate with someone and exchange information, especially in order to work together effectively. |例句：The two departments liaised closely on the project.  = 两个部门在项目上密切合作。

例句：We liaised with the local authorities to obtain the necessary permits. = 我们与当地政府联系以获得必要的许可证。


近义词：collaborated, cooperated, coordinated, consulted, communicated | 反义词：isolated, separated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The marketing team liaises with the sales team regularly. = 市场团队定期与销售团队联系。
She liaises with several international organizations. = 她与几个国际组织联系。

一般过去时:
The project manager liaised with the client to discuss the progress. = 项目经理与客户联系讨论进度。
They liaised with each other to solve the problem. = 他们互相联系以解决问题。

现在完成时:
We have liaised with the supplier about the delivery schedule. = 我们已经与供应商联系过交货时间表。
The company has liaised effectively with its stakeholders. = 公司有效地与其利益相关者进行了沟通。

过去完成时:
They had liaised with the engineers before making the final decision. = 他们在做出最终决定之前已经与工程师联系过。
The team had liaised extensively before presenting their findings. = 该团队在提交研究结果之前进行了广泛的沟通。

将来时:
The team will liaise with the experts to get their feedback. = 该团队将与专家联系以获得他们的反馈。
We will liaise with the relevant authorities as soon as possible. = 我们将尽快与相关部门联系。

虚拟语气:
If we had liaised more effectively, the outcome would have been better. = 如果我们沟通更有效，结果会更好。
It would be helpful if the two teams liaised more frequently. = 如果两个团队更频繁地联系，将会有所帮助。

被动语态:
The information was liaised to the relevant departments. = 信息已与相关部门沟通。
The proposal will be liaised with the board of directors. = 该提案将与董事会进行沟通。

疑问句:
Did you liaise with the legal team before signing the contract? = 在签署合同之前，你是否与法律团队联系过？
Has the project manager liaised with all stakeholders? = 项目经理是否与所有利益相关者进行了联系？

否定句:
The two teams did not liaise effectively. = 这两个团队没有有效地进行联系。
We haven't liaised with the client yet. = 我们还没有与客户联系。


PHRASE->
liaise with = 与...联系
liaise closely = 密切联系
liaise effectively = 有效联系
liaise regularly = 定期联系
maintain liaison = 保持联系


词源:
From French *liaison*, meaning "link" or "connection".  The word entered English in the early 20th century, reflecting the increasing importance of communication and coordination in modern organizations.
