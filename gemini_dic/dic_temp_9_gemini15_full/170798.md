pseudoedema:/ˌsuːdoʊˈiːdɪmə/|/ˈsjuːdoʊiˈdiːmə/| n. |假性水肿|Apparent swelling without the presence of fluid accumulation in the tissues;  a condition that mimics the appearance of edema but lacks its underlying pathophysiological mechanism. It can be caused by various factors, including inflammation, venous congestion, or lymphatic obstruction, resulting in tissue thickening or changes in skin texture.  |Example:  The patient presented with pseudoedema in the lower extremities, which was later determined to be caused by skin thickening due to chronic inflammation.|近义词：apparent edema, simulated edema|反义词：true edema, genuine edema
