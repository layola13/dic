destabilised:/ˌdiːˈsteɪbəlaɪzd/| v. |使不稳定；动摇；扰乱 |To make something unstable or insecure.  The sudden economic downturn destabilised the government. |The political crisis destabilised the region.  |近义词：unsettle, disrupt, undermine |反义词：stabilize, strengthen, secure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant changes destabilise the workforce. = 持续的变化使员工队伍不稳定。
Economic uncertainty destabilises markets. = 经济不确定性使市场动荡不安。


一般过去时:
The earthquake destabilised the building's foundations. = 地震使建筑的地基不稳定。
The coup destabilised the country. = 政变使国家动荡不安。


现在完成时:
Years of conflict have destabilised the region. = 多年的冲突使该地区动荡不安。
The reforms have destabilised the existing power structure. = 改革动摇了现有的权力结构。


过去完成时:
The company had already been destabilised before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，公司就已经不稳定了。
The government had destabilised the economy through its policies. = 政府的政策已经使经济动荡不安。


将来时:
The new regulations will destabilise the industry. = 新的法规将使该行业不稳定。
The proposed changes will destabilise the peace process. = 提出的改变将破坏和平进程。


虚拟语气:
If the government hadn't intervened, the situation would have destabilised further. = 如果政府不干预，情况将会进一步恶化。
I wish they hadn't destabilised the peace talks. = 我真希望他们没有破坏和平谈判。


被动语态:
The region was destabilised by the ongoing war. = 该地区因持续的战争而动荡不安。
The market was destabilised by the unexpected news. = 市场因意外消息而动荡不安。


疑问句:
What factors destabilised the government? = 哪些因素导致了政府不稳定？
Has the recent crisis destabilised the financial system? = 最近的危机是否动摇了金融体系？


否定句:
The reforms did not destabilise the economy. = 改革并没有使经济动荡不安。
The president's speech did not destabilise the political situation. = 总统的讲话并没有使政治局势动荡不安。
