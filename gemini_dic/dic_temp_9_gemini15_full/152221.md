patristical: /pætˈrɪstɪkəl/ | adj. | 教父的；教会早期教父的 | Relating to the early Christian Fathers or their writings.  The term refers to the period roughly from the 2nd to the 8th centuries CE, encompassing the writings and theological perspectives of influential figures like Augustine and Origen.  These writings significantly shaped early Christian doctrine and practice. |  The patristical writings offer valuable insights into the development of Christian theology.  | 近义词：early Christian, fatherly (in a figurative sense) | 反义词：modern, contemporary
