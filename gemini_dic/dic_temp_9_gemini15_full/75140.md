frissons:/ˈfrɪsɒnz/| n. |一阵阵战栗；一阵阵兴奋；激动 | A sudden feeling of excitement or fear, often accompanied by a slight shiver.  |例句：The announcement sent a wave of frissons through the audience.  公告让观众一阵阵激动。

例句：A frisson of fear ran down her spine as she heard the noise.  听到那个声音，她感到一阵恐惧。


近义词：shivers, thrills, chills, tremors | 反义词：calmness, tranquility


词源: 法语 frisson, from Old French frissoner "to shiver," of imitative origin.


用法说明:

frissons 通常指一种短暂而强烈的生理和情感反应，通常伴随着轻微的颤抖或战栗。它可以是积极的（如兴奋、激动），也可以是消极的（如恐惧、不安）。  它通常以复数形式出现。


例句：

* The suspenseful music created frissons of anticipation.  悬念迭起的音乐营造出期待的颤栗感。
*  She felt a frisson of excitement at the prospect of travel. 她对旅行的前景感到一阵兴奋。
* He experienced a frisson of unease when he saw the shadowy figure. 当他看到那个黑影时，他感到一阵不安。


注意：frissons 一般不作动词使用。
