satires:/ˈsætaɪrz/| n.|讽刺作品；讽刺文学|Works of satire use humor, irony, exaggeration, or ridicule to expose and criticize folly or vice.  Satires can take many forms, including novels, poems, plays, and essays. |例句：Jonathan Swift's "Gulliver's Travels" is a famous satire.  乔纳森·斯威夫特的《格列佛游记》是一部著名的讽刺作品。|例句：The cartoon was a biting satire on the government's policies. 这幅漫画是对政府政策的尖锐讽刺。|近义词：parody, lampoon, caricature|反义词：praise, eulogy|名词
