gossiper:/ˈɡɑːsɪpər/ | n. |说闲话的人，爱嚼舌根的人 | A gossiper is a person who enjoys talking about other people's private lives, often spreading rumors or sharing information that may not be true.  | Example: She's a notorious gossiper; everyone avoids her. 她是个臭名昭著的说闲话的人；大家都躲着她。  |近义词：rumormonger, talebearer, chatterbox |反义词：discreet person, private person |名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He gossips about his neighbors every day. = 他每天都在说他邻居的闲话。
She gossips with her friends at lunch. = 她午餐时和朋友们说闲话。


一般过去时:
They gossiped about the celebrity couple last night. = 他们昨晚说起了那对名人的闲话。
He gossiped to his colleagues about the company's problems. = 他向同事们嚼舌根，谈论公司的困境。


现在完成时:
She has gossiped about that matter to everyone in the office. = 她已经把这件事告诉了办公室里的每个人。
They have gossiped relentlessly since the scandal broke. = 自从丑闻爆发以来，他们就一直在没完没了地说闲话。


过去完成时:
Before the party, she had gossiped with her friends about the host. = 在聚会之前，她已经和朋友们谈论了主人的闲话。
They had gossiped about the new employee before he even started working. = 在新员工开始工作之前，他们就已经说他的闲话了。


将来时:

They will gossip about this incident for weeks to come. = 他们会在接下来的几周里谈论这件事。


虚拟语气:
If she were not such a gossiper, she would have more friends. = 如果她不是那么爱说闲话，她就会有更多朋友。

被动语态:

疑问句:
