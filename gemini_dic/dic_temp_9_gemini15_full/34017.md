citizenish: /ˈsɪtɪzənɪʃ/ | adj. |略带公民意味的；像公民一样的；公民似的 |  Suggesting or characteristic of a citizen; having the qualities of a citizen.  The word implies a slightly informal or less-than-perfect embodiment of citizenship. It often suggests a naive or slightly presumptuous approach to civic matters. |例句：His citizenish attempts at community organizing were well-intentioned but ultimately ineffective.  他的公民式社区组织尝试出发点良好，但最终无效。

His citizenish pronouncements on the matter were met with polite skepticism. 他关于此事公民式的声明受到了礼貌的怀疑。


近义词：civic, citizenly (more formal),  common, ordinary


反义词：uncivic, anti-social, unpatriotic


词源：源自citizen (公民) + -ish (后缀，表示略带…的性质，程度不强)。  The suffix "-ish" adds a sense of approximation or imperfection to the base word.
