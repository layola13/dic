injunctively:/ɪnˈdʒʌŋktɪvli/ | adv. |  禁令地；强制性地；命令式地 |  In an injunctive manner; by means of an injunction. PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "injunctively".  The word itself is used to describe the manner of legal action.


词源:
Derived from the noun "injunction," meaning a judicial order restraining a person from beginning or continuing an action threatening or invading the legal right of another, or compelling a person to carry out a certain act.  The suffix "-ly" transforms the noun into an adverb.
