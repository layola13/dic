foisted:/ˈfɔɪstɪd/| vt.|强加；硬塞；欺骗|To force (something unwanted or unwelcome) upon someone.  To impose something on someone by trickery or coercion. |例句：He foisted a worthless painting on me. 他强加给我一幅毫无价值的画。

例句：They foisted the extra work onto the new employee. 他们把额外的工作强加给了新员工。


近义词：impose, thrust, palm off, unload


反义词：offer willingly, give freely


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He foists his opinions on everyone. = 他把他的意见强加给每一个人。
She foists her old clothes on her sister. = 她把旧衣服硬塞给她的妹妹。


一般过去时:
They foisted a fake antique on the collector. = 他们把一件假古董强加给了收藏家。
He foisted the blame onto his colleague. = 他把责任推卸给了他的同事。


现在完成时:
They have foisted their ideas upon the reluctant committee. = 他们把他们的想法强加给了不情愿的委员会。
She has foisted a substandard product on her customers. = 她把劣质产品强加给了她的顾客。


过去完成时:
The salesman had foisted a faulty washing machine on the unsuspecting customer. = 销售员已经把一台有故障的洗衣机卖给了毫无戒心的顾客。
Before the meeting, they had foisted a flawed proposal on the board. = 会议前，他们已经向董事会提交了一个有缺陷的提案。


将来时:
They will foist their agenda on the unsuspecting public. = 他们将把他们的议程强加给毫无戒心的公众。
He will foist his beliefs onto his children. = 他会把他的信仰强加给他的孩子。


虚拟语气:
If they foisted this on us, we would protest. = 如果他们把这个强加给我们，我们会抗议。
I wish they hadn't foisted that responsibility on me. = 我真希望他们没有把那项责任强加给我。


被动语态:
A defective product was foisted on the unsuspecting buyer. = 一件有缺陷的产品被强加给了毫无戒心的买家。
The new rules were foisted upon the employees without any consultation. = 新规定在没有任何协商的情况下被强加给了员工。


疑问句:
Was this problem foisted upon us? = 这个问题是强加给我们的吗？
Who foisted this responsibility on him? = 谁把这个责任强加给了他？


否定句:
They did not foist their beliefs on anyone. = 他们没有把他们的信仰强加给任何人。
We will not foist this inferior product on our customers. = 我们不会把这种劣质产品强加给我们的顾客。


PHRASE->
foist something on/upon someone = 把某物强加给某人
foist oneself on someone = 纠缠某人
to foist a lie on someone = 向某人撒谎
to foist a responsibility on someone = 把责任推卸给某人
foist off (something) on someone = 把（某物）推卸给某人


词源:
The word "foist" ultimately derives from the Old French word *foister*, meaning "to thrust," "to push," or "to force."  The meaning shifted over time to encompass the sense of fraudulently imposing something on someone.
