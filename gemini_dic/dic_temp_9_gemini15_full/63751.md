epicenism:/ˈɛpɪsənɪzəm/| n. |通性；雌雄同体；阴阳人|The use of one word (typically a noun or pronoun) to refer to both male and female individuals; a word or name that can be used for both males and females.  Epicenism is a linguistic phenomenon where a single term is used to denote both sexes.  This contrasts with the use of separate terms for each sex (e.g., "actor" vs. "actress").|Example:  The word "student" is epicene, referring to both male and female students.  The use of "chairperson" instead of "chairman" or "chairwoman" demonstrates epicenism. |近义词：gender neutrality, common gender|反义词：sex-specific language, gendered language
