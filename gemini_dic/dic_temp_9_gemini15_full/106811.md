labipalp:/ˈlæbɪpæl/| n. |唇须；口须|A labipalp is a sensory appendage found near the mouth of some invertebrates, such as crustaceans and insects.  They are used for sensing food and manipulating it before ingestion.  The term is formed from "labium" (lip) and "palp" (feelers).|例句：The shrimp used its labipalps to carefully examine the algae before eating it.  这只虾用它的唇须仔细检查藻类后再吃。|近义词：maxilliped (in some contexts)|反义词:|无|
