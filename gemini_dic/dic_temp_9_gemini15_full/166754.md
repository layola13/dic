presignaling:/ˌpriːˈsɪɡnəlɪŋ/| n. |预先信号；预警信号|The act of giving a signal or warning before something else happens.  It often involves providing information or clues in advance to prepare someone for a future event or situation.  This can be in various contexts, from biological processes to technological systems.|例句：The presignaling of the earthquake was minimal, leaving many unprepared. 地震预警信号微弱，导致许多人措手不及。


The early presignaling of a stock market crash might allow investors to protect their assets. 股票市场崩盘的早期预警信号可以让投资者保护自己的资产。|近义词：forewarning, advance notice, premonition, warning|反义词：surprise, unexpectedness|名词
