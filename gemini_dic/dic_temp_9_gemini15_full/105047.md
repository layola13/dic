keratidoma:/ˌkɛrəˈtaɪdoʊmə/| n. |角化瘤|A keratidoma is a benign (non-cancerous) tumor composed of keratinocytes, the cells that produce keratin, a protein that makes up the skin, hair, and nails.  These tumors are often slow-growing and can appear on various parts of the body. They can be caused by various factors, including genetic predisposition, sun exposure, and chronic irritation.  Diagnosis is typically made through physical examination and sometimes biopsy.|The dermatologist diagnosed a keratidoma on my patient's scalp.|近义词：keratinizing tumor, horn (in some cases)|反义词：keratinolytic lesion (a lesion that breaks down keratin)
