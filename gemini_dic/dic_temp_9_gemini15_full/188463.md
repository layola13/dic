rudimentary:/ˌruːdɪˈmɛntəri/(美式)/ˌruːdɪˈmɛntəri/(英式)| adj. | 基本的；初步的；未发展的 |  Rudimentary means being in the earliest stages of development; basic or undeveloped. It implies a lack of completeness or sophistication.  | Example: He has only a rudimentary understanding of the subject. 他对这个主题只有初步的了解。  |近义词: basic, elementary, undeveloped, primitive |反义词: advanced, sophisticated, developed, complex |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His knowledge of music is rudimentary. = 他对音乐的了解很基本。
The tools they used were rudimentary. = 他们使用的工具很简陋。


一般过去时:
Her understanding of the process was rudimentary. = 她对这个过程的理解很肤浅。
Their initial attempts were rudimentary. = 他们的初步尝试很粗糙。


现在完成时:
They have only achieved a rudimentary level of understanding. = 他们只达到了一个初步的理解水平。
He has developed only rudimentary skills in programming. = 他只开发出了基本的编程技能。


过去完成时:

Her skills were rudimentary, having had only limited training. = 她的技能很基础，因为只接受过有限的训练。


将来时:
His skills will likely remain rudimentary without further training. = 如果不进行进一步的培训，他的技能可能会停留在初级阶段。
We expect a rudimentary version of the software to be released soon. = 我们预计很快就会发布该软件的简易版本。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:
