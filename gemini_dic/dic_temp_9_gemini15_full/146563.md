overgrade:/ˈoʊvərɡreɪd/ | vt. |超等级评定；给予过高的等级|To assign a higher grade or rank to (someone or something) than is justified.  This often implies an unfair or inaccurate assessment.|例句：The teacher overgraded the student's essay, giving it a higher mark than it deserved.  老师对学生的论文评级过高，给出的分数高于应得的分数。

The professor overgraded his exams, resulting in inflated scores for the entire class. 教授对考试评级过高，导致全班分数虚高。

近义词：overrate, overvalue, exaggerate

反义词：underrate, undervalue

词源:
The word "overgrade" is a compound word formed from the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") and the noun "grade" (meaning "a level of quality or achievement").  It's a relatively straightforward derivation reflecting its meaning.
