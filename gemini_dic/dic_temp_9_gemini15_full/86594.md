heliographies:/ˌhiːliˈɒɡrəfiz/ | n. |日光照相术，日光印画法 |The process of creating images using sunlight to expose photosensitive materials.  Heliography is a precursor to photography. | Example:  Nicéphore Niépce's famous heliograph, "View from the Window at Le Gras," is considered one of the earliest surviving photographs.  尼塞福尔·涅普斯的著名日光照相作品《勒格拉斯的窗户景色》被认为是最早幸存的照片之一。|近义词：sun printing, photogravure (in some contexts) |反义词：digital imaging
