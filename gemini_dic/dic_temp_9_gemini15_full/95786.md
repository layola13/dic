incontrovertibility:/ˌɪnkɒntrəvɜːtəˈbɪləti/ | n. |无可辩驳性，不容置疑性 | The incontrovertibility of the evidence led to the defendant's conviction.  证据无可辩驳，导致被告被判有罪。|近义词：certainty, irrefutability, undeniable nature |反义词：doubtfulness, disputability


英文解析：The quality or state of being incontrovertible; the inability to be denied or disputed.  指某种事物无法被否认或争辩的状态或性质。

例句：

The incontrovertibility of the scientific findings silenced the doubters. 科学发现的无可辩驳性使怀疑者闭嘴了。

The incontrovertibility of the eyewitness testimony secured the conviction. 目击证词的无可辩驳性确保了定罪。


词源:

* **in-**:  prefix indicating negation or reversal (否定或反转的前缀)
* **controvert**: verb meaning "to dispute or oppose" (动词，意为“争论或反对”)  This comes from the Latin *controvertere*, meaning "to turn against" (拉丁语，意为“反对”)
* **-ible**: suffix making an adjective (形容词后缀)
* **-ity**: suffix making a noun (名词后缀)


因此，incontrovertibility 的字面意思是“不能被反驳的状态”。
