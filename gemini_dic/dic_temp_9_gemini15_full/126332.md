myos:/ˈmaɪoʊs/| n. |肌，肌肉|The combining form of "muscle" in scientific terminology. It is derived from the Greek word "mys," meaning "muscle".  This combining form is used to create compound words relating to muscles.|Example: Myocardium (heart muscle), Myositis (muscle inflammation)|近义词:muscle, musculature|反义词:None


USAGE->
This word is not used independently as a noun in common English usage. It's primarily a combining form used in scientific and medical terminology.  Therefore, providing grammatical examples in various tenses is not applicable.  The word requires a suffix to form a complete word and have a grammatical function.
