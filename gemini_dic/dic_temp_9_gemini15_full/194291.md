self-oppressors:/ˌsɛlf əˈprɛsərz/| n.|自我压迫者，自我压抑者|People who actively or passively suppress their own thoughts, feelings, desires, or potential.  They may do this through various mechanisms, such as self-doubt, negative self-talk, perfectionism, or avoidance of challenges.  The term often implies a conscious or unconscious internal conflict that hinders personal growth and fulfillment.|例句：Many marginalized groups become self-oppressors due to internalized oppression.|近义词：self-saboteurs, self-limiters|反义词：self-actualizers, self-empowerers
