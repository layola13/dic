preempt:/priˈempt/| vt. |抢先；先发制人；取代；阻止；(广播电视节目) 抢占播放时间| to take action before someone else in order to prevent them from doing something; to replace something with something else, especially a television program with another|例句：The government preempted the crisis by raising interest rates. 政府通过提高利率来先发制人地化解危机。|例句：The news report was preempted by a special announcement. 新闻报道被一条特别公告抢占了播放时间。|近义词：anticipate, forestall, supersede|反义词：postpone, delay
