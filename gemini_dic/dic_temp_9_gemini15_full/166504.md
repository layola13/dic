presaging: /prɪˈseɪdʒɪŋ/ | v. | 预示，预兆 |  To indicate or suggest beforehand; to foreshadow.  | 例句：The dark clouds presaged a storm.  =  乌云预示着暴风雨即将来临。

The ominous silence presaged a terrible event. = 不祥的寂静预示着一场可怕的事件。


近义词：foretelling, foreshadowing, portending, auguring, indicating


反义词：following, succeeding


词性: 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The economic indicators presage a recession. = 经济指标预示着经济衰退。
These early symptoms presage a serious illness. = 这些早期症状预示着严重的疾病。


一般过去时:
The rumbling in the distance presaged an earthquake. = 远处隆隆的响声预示着地震。
Her uneasy feeling presaged the bad news. = 她不安的感觉预示着坏消息。


现在完成时:
Recent events have presaged a change in government policy. = 最近发生的事件预示着政府政策的改变。
The scientists' findings have presaged a breakthrough in cancer research. = 科学家的发现预示着癌症研究的突破。


过去完成时:
The strange dreams had presaged the accident. = 奇怪的梦预示着事故的发生。
The dark clouds had presaged the heavy rain that followed. = 乌云预示着随后而来的大雨。


将来时:
Experts predict that the new technology will presage a revolution in manufacturing. = 专家预测这项新技术将预示着制造业的革命。
Analysts believe that the current trends will presage a period of economic growth. = 分析师认为目前的趋势将预示着一个经济增长的时期。


虚拟语气:
If only we had heeded the signs that presaged the crisis! = 如果我们能注意到预示危机的迹象就好了！
If the data had presaged such a dramatic outcome, we would have acted differently. = 如果数据预示着如此戏剧性的结果，我们将采取不同的行动。


被动语态:
The coming storm was presaged by a sudden drop in temperature. = 即将到来的风暴是由气温骤降预示的。
The success of the project was presaged by careful planning. = 项目的成功是由周密的计划预示的。


疑问句:
Does this unusual behavior presage something more serious? = 这种异常行为是否预示着更严重的事情？
What do the latest polls presage for the upcoming election? = 最新的民调预示着即将到来的选举将会如何？


否定句:
The initial reports did not presage the severity of the damage. = 初始报告并没有预示损害的严重性。
Nothing presaged the sudden turn of events. = 没有什么预示着事态的突然转变。
