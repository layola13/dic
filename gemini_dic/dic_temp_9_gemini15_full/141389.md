operettist:/ˌɒpəˈretɪst/ | n. |轻歌剧作曲家 | A composer of operettas.  Operettists are known for writing music for light-hearted, often comedic, theatrical works that incorporate singing, dialogue, and dancing.  | Example: Gilbert and Sullivan were renowned operettists of the Victorian era.  吉尔伯特和萨利文是维多利亚时代著名的轻歌剧作曲家。  | 近义词: composer (of light opera) | 反义词:  None directly applicable, but one could contrast with a composer of serious opera.
