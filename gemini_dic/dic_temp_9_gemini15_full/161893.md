praetores:/ˈpreɪtɔːrz/| n. |执政官（复数）| Roman magistrates who held considerable power, responsible for administering justice and commanding armies.  They were elected annually and ranked below the consuls.  In later periods, their functions became more specialized.|例句：The praetors held court and dispensed justice throughout the Roman Republic.  执政官在罗马共和国期间主持法庭并执行正义。

The praetors' power extended beyond the city walls, allowing them to command legions in times of war. 执政官的权力延伸到城墙之外，允许他们在战争时期指挥军团。|近义词：magistrates, judges, officials |反义词：subjects, citizens (in the context of their authority)
