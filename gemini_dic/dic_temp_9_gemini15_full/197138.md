shanghaied:/ˈʃæŋheɪd/| vt.|强行绑架上船（尤指强迫加入船员）；欺骗；诱骗|To shanghai someone is to force them aboard a ship, especially to compel them to join the crew.  The term often implies trickery or coercion. |例句：He was shanghaied into joining the navy. 他被强行绑架上了海军军舰。

例句：They shanghaied him in a bar and forced him to work on a ship bound for Australia.  他们在酒吧里把他骗上船，强迫他到澳大利亚的一艘船上工作。


近义词：kidnap, abduct, press-gang, coerce, trick, deceive

反义词：release, free, liberate


词源:The term originates from the practice of kidnapping men in Shanghai, China, and forcing them into service on ships heading to various destinations.  The practice was particularly prevalent during the 19th century.  The word itself entered common English usage in the late 19th century.
