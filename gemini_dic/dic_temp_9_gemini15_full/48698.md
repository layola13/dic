defiatory: /dɪˈfaɪətəri/ | adj. | 蔑视的，反抗的 | Defiatory describes an action or attitude that shows defiance or contempt.  It implies a bold and open challenge to authority or established norms. |例句：His defiatory stance towards the law landed him in serious trouble.  他的蔑视法律的态度使他陷入严重的麻烦。


The company's defiatory response to the criticism only worsened the situation. 公司对批评的蔑视回应只会使情况恶化。|近义词：defiant, contemptuous, rebellious, insolent |反义词：obedient, submissive, compliant, respectful |词性：形容词

词源:
The word "defiatory" is derived from the verb "defy," meaning to openly resist or challenge.  The suffix "-atory" forms adjectives indicating a tendency or relation to the action of the verb.  Therefore, "defiatory" signifies relating to or characterized by defiance.
