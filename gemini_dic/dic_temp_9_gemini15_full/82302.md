groups:/ɡruːps/| n.|群体；组；类；集团|A number of people or things that are located close together or are considered or classed together.|例句：The children were divided into groups of four. 孩子们被分成四个人一组。|例句：There are several groups of birds in the garden. 花园里有几群鸟。|近义词：assemblies, collections, sets|反义词：individuals, singles


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher divides the students into groups. = 老师把学生分成几组。
These flowers are grouped by color. = 这些花按颜色分组。

一般过去时:
They grouped themselves according to their interests. = 他们根据自己的兴趣组合在一起。
She grouped the documents by date. = 她按日期对文件进行了分类。

现在完成时:
We have grouped all the data for analysis. = 我们已经将所有数据分组进行分析。
They have grouped together to support the cause. = 他们团结起来支持这项事业。

过去完成时:
They had already grouped the items before the presentation. = 他们在演示之前就已经把物品分组了。
She had grouped the evidence carefully before presenting her case. = 在陈述她的案情之前，她已经仔细地整理了证据。

将来时:
We will group the participants into teams of five. = 我们将把参与者分成五人一组。
The company will group its products into different categories. = 公司将把产品分成不同的类别。

虚拟语气:
If we grouped the data differently, we might see different results. = 如果我们对数据进行不同的分组，可能会看到不同的结果。
I wish we had grouped the tasks more efficiently. = 我希望我们把任务安排得更有效率一些。

被动语态:
The students are grouped by ability level. = 学生们按能力水平分组。
The items were grouped according to size and weight. = 物品按大小和重量分组。

疑问句:
How many groups are there? = 有多少组？
How are the data grouped? = 数据是如何分组的？

否定句:
The items are not grouped correctly. = 物品分组不正确。
They did not group the participants effectively. = 他们没有有效地将参与者分组。
