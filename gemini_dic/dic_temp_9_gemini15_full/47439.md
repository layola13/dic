deafened:/ˈdɛfənd/| adj.|使聋的；震耳欲聋的|Made deaf or unable to hear properly, typically temporarily; extremely loud.|The loud explosion deafened me for a moment.  一声巨响使我暂时失聪。|The music was so loud it deafened the audience. 音乐声太大，震聋了观众的耳朵。|deprived of hearing, stunned|hearing, quiet|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud music deafens me. = 吵闹的音乐使我耳聋。
This noise deafens my ears. = 这个噪音使我耳朵发聋。

一般过去时:
The explosion deafened everyone nearby. = 爆炸使附近的人都暂时失聪了。
The concert deafened her for several hours. = 音乐会让她几个小时都听不见东西。

现在完成时:
The constant exposure to loud noises has deafened him. = 长期暴露在巨大的噪音下使他失聪了。
That rock concert has deafened me; I'm still experiencing ringing in my ears. = 那场摇滚音乐会让我耳聋了，我的耳朵现在还嗡嗡作响。

过去完成时:
He had been deafened by a previous explosion before the accident. 事故发生前，他曾因之前的爆炸而失聪。
She had already been deafened by the prolonged exposure to loud machinery before she retired.  在她退休之前，长时间接触巨大的机器噪音已经让她失聪了。

将来时:
The fireworks display will deafen the nearby residents. = 烟花表演会让附近的居民暂时失聪。
Prolonged exposure to such loud music will deafen you.  长时间暴露在这种大声的音乐下会让你失聪。

虚拟语气:
If I had worn earplugs, I wouldn't have been deafened. = 如果我戴了耳塞，我就不会暂时失聪。
If the band played less loudly, they wouldn't deafen the audience.  如果乐队演奏得小声一点，他们就不会震聋观众的耳朵。


被动语态:
Many people were deafened by the powerful blast. = 许多人被强大的爆炸声震聋了。
She was deafened by the overwhelmingly loud sound system. 她被压倒性的巨大音响系统震聋了。


疑问句:
Were you deafened by the fireworks? = 你被烟花声震聋了吗？
Was the crowd deafened by the loudspeakers? = 观众被扬声器震聋了吗？


否定句:
The music wasn't loud enough to deafen me. = 音乐声不够大，不足以让我失聪。
The sound wasn't so loud that it deafened us.  声音没大到让我们失聪的程度。

PHRASE->
be deafened by = 被...震聋

temporarily deafened = 暂时性失聪

deafening silence = 令人毛骨悚然的寂静 (反义用法，指寂静得令人不安)
