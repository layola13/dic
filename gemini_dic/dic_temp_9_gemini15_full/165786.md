preobligation:/ˌpriːˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃən/| n. |预先义务；预先承诺 |A preobligation is a commitment or responsibility that exists before a particular event or action.  It's an obligation that is incurred or established in advance.  This often involves a prior agreement or understanding that creates a duty or requirement before a specific event triggers the obligation's fulfillment. |Example: The company had a preobligation to provide safety training to all employees before starting the new project.  这家公司在启动新项目之前有义务为所有员工提供安全培训。


The bank had a preobligation to secure sufficient funds before lending to the client.  银行在向客户贷款之前有义务确保有足够的资金。


近义词：prior commitment, advance undertaking, prior arrangement, antecedent obligation|反义词：postobligation (a commitment arising after an event)

词性：名词

词源:
The word "preobligation" is a compound word formed from the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the noun "obligation" (meaning "a moral or legal duty").  Therefore, its meaning directly reflects its constituent parts: an obligation that exists or is incurred before something else.
