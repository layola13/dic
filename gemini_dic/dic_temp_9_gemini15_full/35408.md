co-opting: /ˌkoʊˈɑːptɪŋ/ | v. | 挪用；吸收；篡夺；利用 | To adopt or incorporate (ideas, methods, etc.) from another source.  To take (something, such as a process or idea) for one's own use without authorization or acknowledgment. To recruit someone for a particular purpose, typically a political one, by offering them a position or advantage. |

例句：

The company co-opted many ideas from its competitors. = 这家公司从竞争对手那里借鉴了许多想法。

The opposition party accused the government of co-opting their policy proposals. = 反对党指责政府盗用他们的政策提案。


近义词：appropriate, adopt, usurp, exploit, commandeer


反义词：reject, relinquish, abandon, forsake


词性：动词
