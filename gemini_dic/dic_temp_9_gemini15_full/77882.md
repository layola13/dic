/ˌdʒɛnɪˈveɪnɪzəm/| n. |日内瓦教派主义；日内瓦式清教徒主义|Genevanism refers to the strict Calvinistic religious principles and practices associated with Geneva, Switzerland, during the era of John Calvin.  It emphasizes a rigorous moral code, often associated with puritanical views and a strong emphasis on predestination.  The term often carries a negative connotation, implying excessive severity or intolerance.|The strict Genevanism of the community led to a rigid social structure and limited personal freedoms. |Puritanism, Calvinism|Liberalism, laxity|
