pliocene:/ˈplaɪəˌsiːn/| n. |上新世 |The Pliocene Epoch is a geologic epoch that lasted from about 5.333 million to 2.58 million years ago. It is the second-to-last epoch of the Neogene Period in the Cenozoic Era.  The Pliocene is characterized by significant climate change, with a general cooling trend leading to the onset of glacial conditions in the Pleistocene.  | Example: The fossils found in this layer of rock are from the Pliocene epoch.  这个岩层的化石来自上新世。|近义词: Pleistocene (following epoch) |反义词: Miocene (preceding epoch)
