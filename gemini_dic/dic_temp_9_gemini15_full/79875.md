gnash:/næʃ/| vt.|咬牙切齿，磨牙|To strike the teeth together, especially repeatedly and forcefully, usually expressing anger or frustration.|例句：He clenched his fists and gnashed his teeth in rage. 他握紧拳头，愤怒地磨牙。
例句：The frustrated child gnashed his teeth in defiance.  沮丧的孩子不服气地磨牙。|近义词：grind, grit|反义词：smile, grin|动词

词源:
The word "gnash" comes from the Old English word "gnægnan," meaning "to gnaw" or "to bite."  The meaning evolved from a general gnawing action to the specific action of grinding one's teeth.
