implying:/ɪmˈplaɪɪŋ/ | v. | 暗示；意味着 |  To suggest (something) without stating it directly; to express indirectly.  | His silence implied consent. 他的沉默暗示着同意。  He looked at his watch, implying it was time to go. 他看了看手表，暗示该走了。 | suggesting, hinting, signifying | stating explicitly, declaring directly | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is implying that I am wrong. = 他暗示我错了。
She is implying a lack of confidence in my abilities. = 她暗示我对我的能力缺乏信心。


一般过去时:
The politician implied a scandal was brewing. = 这位政客暗示一场丑闻正在酝酿。
The article implied a connection between the two events. = 这篇文章暗示了这两件事之间的联系。


现在完成时:
His behavior has implied a change of heart. = 他的行为暗示了他的想法改变了。
The evidence has implied the suspect's guilt. = 证据暗示了嫌疑人的罪行。


过去完成时:
His tone implied he had already known the truth. = 他的语气暗示他已经知道真相了。
The letter implied they had previously discussed the matter. = 这封信暗示他们之前讨论过这件事。


将来时:
His actions will imply a decisive stance on the issue. = 他的行为将暗示他对这个问题的坚决立场。
The results will imply the need for further research. = 结果将暗示需要进一步的研究。


虚拟语气:
If he implied such a thing, I would be furious. = 如果他暗示那样的事情，我会很生气。
I wish he hadn't implied I was incompetent. = 我真希望他没有暗示我无能。


被动语态:
It was implied that the project was failing. = 暗示该项目正在失败。
His guilt was implied by the evidence. = 证据暗示了他的罪行。


疑问句:
What are you implying? = 你在暗示什么？
Is he implying something negative? = 他是在暗示一些负面的东西吗？


否定句:
He didn't imply any wrongdoing. = 他没有暗示任何不当行为。
The report didn't imply that the company was bankrupt. = 报告并没有暗示该公司破产。


PHRASE->
imply something to someone  = 向某人暗示某事
imply that = 暗示...
imply by = 通过...暗示
strongly imply = 强烈暗示
clearly imply = 清晰地暗示
