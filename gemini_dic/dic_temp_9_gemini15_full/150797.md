paradoxes:/ˈpærədɒksɪz/| n. |悖论；似是而非的论点 |Statements or situations that seem impossible or contradictory but are actually true.  Paradoxes challenge our assumptions and force us to reconsider our understanding of a situation or concept. They often involve a conflict between intuition and logic. |Example:  The paradox of thrift suggests that individual saving can be harmful to the overall economy.  节俭的悖论表明，个人的储蓄可能对整体经济有害。  Another example is the statement "This statement is false," which is a self-contradictory paradox.  另一个例子是“这个陈述是假的”这句话，这是一个自相矛盾的悖论。 |近义词：contradictions, enigmas, anomalies |反义词：certainties, truths, consistencies |名词
