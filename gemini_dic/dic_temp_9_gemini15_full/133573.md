nonflagrantly: /ˌnɒnˈfleɪɡrəntli/ | adv. | 不公然地；不显著地；不嚣张地 |  Not in a flagrant or conspicuous manner; subtly or inconspicuously.  It implies a lack of overtness or boldness in an action or behavior, often suggesting an attempt to avoid detection or attention. |

例句：

He nonflagrantly violated the rules, hoping to avoid punishment. = 他偷偷摸摸地违反了规定，希望能避免惩罚。

The company nonflagrantly manipulated the market, making it difficult to detect their actions. = 这家公司暗中操纵市场，使得很难发现他们的行为。


近义词：subtly, secretly, covertly, discreetly, underhandedly


反义词：flagrantly, openly, conspicuously, blatantly, obviously


词源:

* **non-**:  A prefix meaning "not."
* **flagrant**:  Derived from the Latin "flagrans," meaning "burning," implying something obvious and scandalous.


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It describes how an action is performed.  There's no need for tense conjugation examples as with verbs.


PHRASE->

There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "nonflagrantly."  Its usage is primarily standalone to modify verbs.
