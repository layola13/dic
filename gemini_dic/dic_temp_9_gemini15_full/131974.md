词源:
The word "nonconversant" is formed by adding the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") to the adjective "conversant," which means "familiar" or "well-acquainted."  "Conversant" ultimately derives from the verb "converse," meaning "to talk," and shares a root with words like "conversation."
