hummed:/hʌmd/ | v. |哼唱，发出嗡嗡声 | To produce a low, continuous sound, typically with the lips closed; to sing a tune in a low voice without words. |例句：She hummed a happy tune as she worked. 她一边工作一边哼着快乐的曲子。|例句：The engine hummed quietly. 发动机发出轻微的嗡嗡声。|近义词：murmur, drone, buzz |反义词：shout, scream


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hums a tune every morning. = 他每天早上都哼着歌。
She hums while she cooks. = 她做饭时哼着歌。

一般过去时:
He hummed a familiar song. = 他哼唱了一首熟悉的歌曲。
She hummed softly to herself. = 她轻声自语地哼着歌。

现在完成时:
I have hummed this song all day. = 我整天都在哼这首歌。
They have hummed along to the music. = 他们随着音乐哼唱。

过去完成时:
She had hummed the melody before the concert began. = 音乐会开始前，她已经哼唱过这支曲调了。
He had hummed the tune countless times. = 他已经哼唱过无数次这支曲调了。

将来时:
I will hum you a lullaby. = 我会给你哼一首摇篮曲。
She will hum along to the radio. = 她会随着收音机哼唱。

虚拟语气:
If I knew the words, I would hum the song. = 如果我知道歌词，我就会哼唱这首歌。
I wish I could hum as beautifully as she does. = 我希望我能像她一样哼唱得那么美妙。

被动语态:  (Hummed is rarely used passively)
The tune was hummed by many people. = 这支曲调被许多人哼唱。  (不太自然)
A low hum was heard from the machine. = 从机器里听到低沉的嗡嗡声。(更自然的被动表达)


疑问句:
Are you humming a song? = 你在哼歌吗？
Was she humming when you entered the room? = 你进房间时她在哼歌吗？

否定句:
He didn't hum during the meeting. = 他在会议期间没有哼歌。
She doesn't hum often. = 她不经常哼歌。
