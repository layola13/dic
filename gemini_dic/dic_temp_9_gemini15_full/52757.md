dilatations: /ˌdaɪləˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 扩张；扩大；膨胀 | The widening or enlargement of a vessel, opening, or structure.  Dilatations are often used in medical contexts to describe the expansion of blood vessels or other body parts. | 例句：The doctor observed several dilatations in the patient's arteries. 医生观察到病人动脉有几处扩张。

近义词：expansions, widenings, enlargements | 反义词：constrictions, narrowings, contractions | 词性：名词


Etymology:

The word "dilatations" is derived from the Latin word "dilatare," meaning "to widen" or "to expand."  The suffix "-ation" denotes the process or result of the action.  Therefore, "dilatations" literally refers to the instances or results of widening or expanding.
