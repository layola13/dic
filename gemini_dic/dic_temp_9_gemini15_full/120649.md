mis-sampling:/ˈmɪsˌsæmplɪŋ/| n.|错误抽样，抽样偏差|The act of obtaining a sample that does not accurately represent the population from which it was drawn.  This can lead to inaccurate conclusions and biased results.|例句：Mis-sampling can lead to skewed results in a survey. 错误抽样会导致调查结果出现偏差。|例句：The study suffered from mis-sampling, as the participants were not representative of the wider population. 这项研究受到了错误抽样的影响，因为参与者并不代表更广泛的人群。|近义词：biased sampling, flawed sampling, inaccurate sampling|反义词：representative sampling, accurate sampling|名词
