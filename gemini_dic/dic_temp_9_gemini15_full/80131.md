gobsmacking: /ˈɡɒbsmækɪŋ/ | adj. |令人震惊的；令人吃惊的；极其令人惊讶的 | Extremely surprising or shocking; astonishing.  |例句：The magician's final trick was absolutely gobsmacking. 魔术师的最后一个戏法真是令人震惊。

例句：I was gobsmacked by the news of his sudden death.  我对他突然去世的消息感到震惊。


近义词：astonishing, astounding, breathtaking, shocking, staggering, flabbergasting


反义词： unsurprising, expected, predictable, commonplace


词源：The word is a relatively recent coinage, combining "gob" (mouth) and "smacking" (hitting hard).  It suggests being so shocked that one's mouth literally drops open.  The exact origin is difficult to pinpoint but it gained popularity in British English in the latter half of the 20th century.
