convicting:/kənˈvɪktɪŋ/| v. |证明有罪；使确信；令人信服的 | To prove (someone) guilty of a crime in a court of law.  It also means to persuade someone of something. |例句：The evidence against him was convicting.  证据足以证明他有罪。

The jury found the defendant convicting. 陪审团认定被告有罪。


近义词：incriminating, condemning, proving guilty, persuading

反义词：acquitting, exonerating


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The evidence convicts him of the crime. = 证据证明他犯了罪。
This argument convicts me of my ignorance. = 这个论点让我相信我的无知。


一般过去时:
The court convicted him of murder. = 法院判定他犯有谋杀罪。
Her explanation convicted him of his mistake. = 她的解释使他相信自己犯了错。


现在完成时:
The police have convicted several suspects. = 警方已经逮捕了几名嫌疑人。
His actions have convicted him in the eyes of the public. = 他的行为在公众眼中使他显得有罪。


过去完成时:
They had convicted him before the new evidence emerged. = 新证据出现之前，他们已经判定他犯有罪。
She had convicted herself of making a poor decision. = 她已经意识到自己做了一个糟糕的决定。


将来时:
The judge will convict the criminal. = 法官将宣判罪犯有罪。
I believe this evidence will convict him. = 我相信这个证据会让他定罪。


虚拟语气:
If the jury convicted him, he would go to prison. = 如果陪审团判定他有罪，他就会入狱。
If the evidence were more convincing, they would have convicted him. = 如果证据更有说服力，他们就会判他罪。


被动语态:
He was convicted of fraud. = 他被判犯有欺诈罪。
The criminal was convicted on multiple charges. = 该罪犯被判犯有多项罪名。


疑问句:
Will they convict the suspect? = 他们会判决嫌疑人有罪吗？
Has he ever been convicted before? = 他以前有没有被判过罪？


否定句:
The jury did not convict the defendant. = 陪审团没有判定被告有罪。
They couldn't convict him due to lack of evidence. = 由于证据不足，他们无法判他罪。


PHRASE->
convicting evidence = 令人信服的证据
convicting atmosphere = 令人信服的气氛
convicting argument = 令人信服的论点
highly convicting = 极具说服力
self-convicting = 不言自明；自证其罪


词源:
From Old French *convicte, past participle of convaincre "to convince," from Latin convincere "to overcome, convict," from com- "together" + vincere "to conquer."  The sense evolved from "overcome" to "overcome in argument" to "prove guilty."
