nonpopulously:/ˌnɒnˈpɒpjʊləsli/| adv. |非密集地，人口稀少地 |The word "nonpopulously" describes a place or area with a low population density. It indicates a lack of crowding or congestion.  It's the adverbial form of "nonpopulous".|例句：The countryside was nonpopulously settled, with farms miles apart.  乡村人口稀疏，农场相隔数英里。


The town developed nonpopulously compared to the bustling metropolis. 与繁华的大都市相比，这个城镇发展得人口稀少。|近义词：sparsely, thinly, scantily|反义词：densely, thickly, crowdedly


USAGE->
This word is an adverb and doesn't have verb conjugations like a verb.  Its usage is straightforward: describing how something is distributed spatially in terms of population.


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "nonpopulously".  The word itself is relatively rare and used in specific contexts describing population distribution.
