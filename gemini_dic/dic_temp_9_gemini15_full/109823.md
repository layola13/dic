lexicality:/ˌlɛksɪˈkæləti/| n. |词汇性，词典性|The property of being a word or part of the lexicon; the status of being a word in a language.  It refers to whether a string of sounds or letters is recognized and understood as a word by speakers of a language.  This can vary across different individuals and dialects.|The lexicality of "chortle" is high, as it's a widely understood word. However, the lexicality of newly coined internet slang might be low for some speakers. |近义词：wordhood, vocabulary status|反义词：non-wordhood, unintelligibility
