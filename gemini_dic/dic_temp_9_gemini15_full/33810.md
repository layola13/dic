circumflexes:/ˈsɜːrkəmˌflɛksɪz/| n. |抑扬符；尖音符|Circumflexes are diacritical marks placed above a letter to indicate a modification of pronunciation.  They are used in various languages, such as French and Portuguese. For example, in French, the circumflex indicates a historically long vowel sound.|The word "hôtel" uses a circumflex above the 'o'.|近义词：diacritic, accent mark|反义词：无
