deceitfulness: /dɪˈsiːtfʊlnəs/ | n. |欺骗性，虚伪 | The deceitfulness of his words made it hard to trust him.  他的话语的欺骗性使得很难相信他。|近义词：dishonesty, duplicity, fraudulence, guile |反义词：honesty, truthfulness, sincerity | 名词


英文解析:  Deceitfulness refers to the quality or state of being deceitful; the tendency to deceive or mislead others through dishonesty. It implies a lack of integrity and trustworthiness.


例句:

The deceitfulness of the salesman's promises led many customers to lose their money.  推销员承诺的欺骗性导致许多顾客损失了钱财。


Her deceitfulness was eventually revealed, damaging her reputation irreparably.  她的欺骗行为最终被揭露，不可挽回地损害了她的声誉。


词源:

The word "deceitfulness" is derived from the adjective "deceitful," which in turn comes from the noun "deceit."  "Deceit" originates from Old French "deçit," ultimately from Latin "deceptus," the past participle of "decipere," meaning "to deceive," "to trick," or "to mislead."  The suffix "-fulness" adds the meaning of "full of" or "characterized by."
