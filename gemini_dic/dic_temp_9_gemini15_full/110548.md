limerick:/ˈlɪmərɪk/| n. |五行打油诗|A limerick is a five-line poem with a specific rhyming scheme and rhythm.  It's typically humorous and often nonsensical. The rhyme scheme is AABBA, and the rhythm is typically anapestic.  |Example: There once was a fellow named Kyle, / Whose jokes made the audience smile. / He'd say with a grin, / "Let the laughter begin!" / And leave them all happy a while.|近义词：humorous verse, short poem|反义词：epic poem, sonnet
