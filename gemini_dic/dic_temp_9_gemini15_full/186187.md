rhetorication:/ˌrɛtərɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |修辞化；运用修辞|The process of using rhetorical devices or techniques in speech or writing.  It involves shaping language to achieve a particular effect or persuade an audience.  Rhetorication can involve the use of figures of speech, stylistic devices, and persuasive strategies.|例句：The  rhetorication of the political speech aimed to sway the voters.  政治演讲的修辞手法旨在影响选民。 The excessive rhetorication of the essay obscured its central argument.  文章中过度的修辞手法模糊了其中心论点。|近义词： ornamentation, embellishment,  flourishing|反义词： plainness, simplicity, directness|名词
