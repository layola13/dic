pyrosulphate:/ˌpaɪroʊˈsʌlfeɪt/| n. |焦硫酸盐|Pyrosulphate refers to the salt of pyrosulfuric acid (also known as disulfuric acid), which is formed by the dehydration of sulfuric acid.  It contains the S2O7²⁻ anion.  These salts are often formed by heating metal bisulfates. | Example:  Sodium pyrosulphate is used as a flux in glassmaking. 焦硫酸钠在玻璃制造中用作助熔剂。|近义词：disulfate (less common)|反义词：无|名词
