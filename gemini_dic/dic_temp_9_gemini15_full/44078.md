crinoline:/ˈkrɪnəliːn/| n. |衬裙；硬质衬裙；裙撑|A stiff, usually starched, petticoat worn to give fullness to a skirt, typically in the 19th century.  It can also refer to a fabric used to make such a petticoat.|例句：The crinoline gave her dress a beautiful, bell-shaped silhouette.  这件衬裙使她的裙子呈现出美丽的钟形轮廓。

例句：Victorian women often wore crinolines under their dresses to achieve the desired shape. 维多利亚时代的妇女经常在裙子下面穿衬裙以达到理想的形状。


近义词：petticoat, hoop skirt, bustle (for a different style of fullness)|反义词：None directly applicable, but  "plain skirt" could be considered an antonym in terms of style.|词性:名词
