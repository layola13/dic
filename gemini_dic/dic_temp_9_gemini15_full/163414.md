preconstitutes:/ˌpriːkənˈstɪtjuːts/| vt.|预先构成；预先制定；预先组成|To form or constitute beforehand or in advance.|The committee preconstitutes its agenda before each meeting.  委员会在每次会议前预先拟定议程。|The contract preconstitutes the terms of the agreement. 合同预先规定了协议条款。|近义词：prearrange, predetermine, prefigure|反义词：postpone, improvise


USAGE->

The company preconstitutes its marketing strategy for the year. = 公司预先制定了今年的营销策略。
They preconstitute a team for the upcoming project. = 他们为即将到来的项目预先组建了一个团队。
