fill-the-bill: /fɪl ðə bɪl/ | phrasal verb | 符合要求；胜任；足够好 | To satisfy a need or requirement; to be adequate or suitable. |例句：His experience and skills fill the bill perfectly for this job.  他的经验和技能完全胜任这份工作。


例句：This new software fills the bill for our needs. 这个新软件满足了我们的需求。


近义词：suffice, satisfy, meet the requirements, be adequate, be suitable


反义词：be inadequate, be insufficient, fall short, be unsuitable

词性: phrasal verb


USAGE->
(由于fill the bill 是一个短语动词，没有对应的各种时态变化，以下列出不同语境下的例句)


肯定句:
This car fills the bill for a family vehicle. 这辆车很适合家庭使用。
That restaurant fills the bill for a romantic dinner. 那家餐厅很适合浪漫晚餐。


否定句:
Unfortunately, his qualifications don't quite fill the bill for this senior position. 不幸的是，他的资历并不完全符合这个高级职位的要求。
The proposed solution didn't fill the bill; we need a more comprehensive approach.  提出的解决方案并不适用；我们需要更全面的方法。


疑问句:
Does this candidate fill the bill for the marketing manager position?  这位候选人符合市场经理职位的要求吗？
Will this budget fill the bill for the project? 这个预算够项目用吗？


条件句:
If this plan fills the bill, we'll proceed with it. 如果这个计划可行，我们将继续进行。
Provided this software fills the bill, we can save a lot of time.  如果这个软件好用，我们可以节省很多时间。


PHRASE->
fill the bill perfectly: 完全符合要求
fill the bill nicely: 非常合适
fill the bill for sth: 满足...的需求
more than fill the bill:  绰绰有余
barely fill the bill:勉强够用


词源:
The phrase "fill the bill" originates from the 19th century.  "Bill" in this context refers to a list of requirements or a specification.  The phrase implies that something completely satisfies the items on that list, hence "filling" it.  The exact origin is difficult to pinpoint, but its usage became widespread in common parlance over time.
