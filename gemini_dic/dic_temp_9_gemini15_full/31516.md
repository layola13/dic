charred:/tʃɑːrd/| adj.|烧焦的，炭化的|  Burned or scorched to a black color.  The adjective describes something that has been subjected to intense heat, resulting in a blackened, often brittle state. |例句：The charred remains of the house were all that remained after the fire.  大火过后，只剩下房屋烧焦的残骸。

The charred wood crackled in the fireplace. 壁炉里烧焦的木头噼啪作响。|近义词：burnt, scorched, singed|反义词：unburnt, fresh


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The wood is charred. = 木头烧焦了。
The steak is charred on the outside. = 牛排外面烧焦了。


一般过去时:
The fire charred the fence. = 大火烧焦了栅栏。
The bread charred in the oven. = 面包在烤箱里烤焦了。


现在完成时:
The sun has charred the paint. = 太阳把油漆晒焦了。
The fire has charred the forest floor. = 大火烧焦了森林地面。


过去完成时:
The wood had charred before the firefighters arrived. = 在消防员到达之前，木头已经烧焦了。
The meat had charred, making it inedible. = 肉已经烤焦了，变得不能吃了。


将来时:
The sun will char the leaves. = 太阳会把叶子晒焦。
The intense heat will char the metal. = 剧烈的热量会使金属炭化。


虚拟语气:
If the food had been cooked properly, it wouldn't have been charred. = 如果食物煮得当，就不会烧焦了。
If I had stayed longer, my skin would have been charred. = 如果我待的时间更长，我的皮肤就会被晒伤。


被动语态:
The logs were charred by the flames. = 木头被火焰烧焦了。
The marshmallows were charred by the campfire. = 棉花糖被营火烧焦了。


疑问句:

否定句:
The bread wasn't charred; it was just golden brown. = 面包没有烤焦；它只是金黄色的。
The vegetables aren't charred; they are perfectly cooked. = 蔬菜没有烤焦；它们煮得恰到好处。
