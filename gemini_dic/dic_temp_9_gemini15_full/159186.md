podophthalmous: /ˌpɒdɒpˈθælməs/ | adj. | 突出眼的；具柄眼的 | Having prominent eyes on stalks.  The term refers to creatures with eyes that are located on stalks or stems, extending from the body. This is a common feature in many crustaceans and some other invertebrates.  | Example:  Many crustaceans, such as shrimps and crabs, are podophthalmous.  许多甲壳类动物，如虾和蟹，都是突出眼的。| 近义词: stalked-eyed, protruding-eyed | 反义词: sessile-eyed (eyes not on stalks) |  词性：形容词
