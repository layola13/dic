joshed:/dʒoʊzd/| vt.|开玩笑，戏弄|To josh someone means to tease or playfully make fun of them.  It implies a good-natured ribbing, not malicious intent. |例句：He joshed her about her new haircut. 他就她的新发型和她开玩笑。|例句：Don't josh him; he's very sensitive. 不要戏弄他；他很敏感。|近义词：tease, kid, rib, joke with|反义词：insult, offend, seriously criticize|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He joshes his friends frequently. = 他经常和朋友开玩笑。
She joshes her brother about his height. = 她经常就她弟弟的身高和他开玩笑。

一般过去时:
They joshed him about his clumsy dance moves. = 他们就他笨拙的舞姿取笑他。
I joshed her about her new boyfriend. = 我就她的新男朋友和她开了个玩笑。

现在完成时:
He has joshed me about my driving skills all day. = 他一整天都在取笑我的驾驶技术。
She has joshed him relentlessly about his singing. = 她一直无情地取笑他的歌声。

过去完成时:
They had joshed him mercilessly before he even started his speech. = 他还没开始演讲，他们就无情地取笑他了。
I had joshed her about her cooking before, so she was prepared for my comments. = 我以前就她的厨艺开过玩笑，所以她已经准备好应对我的评论了。

将来时:
I will josh him about his new car tomorrow. = 我明天会就他的新车和他开玩笑。
She will josh her friend about her upcoming wedding. = 她会就她朋友即将到来的婚礼和她开玩笑。

虚拟语气:
If I were brave enough, I would josh the boss. = 如果我足够勇敢，我会和老板开玩笑。
I wish I hadn't joshed him so much; he got really upset. = 我真希望我不要那么取笑他；他真的很难过。


被动语态:
He was joshed relentlessly by his colleagues about his mistake. = 他因为自己的错误而被同事无情地取笑。
She was often joshed about her accent. = 她经常因为口音而被取笑。

疑问句:
Were you joshed about your outfit? = 你因为你的穿着打扮而被取笑了吗？
Is he being joshed about his new job? = 他因为他的新工作而被取笑吗？

否定句:
I didn't josh her; I was being serious. = 我没有和她开玩笑；我是认真的。
They didn't josh him at all; they were genuinely impressed. = 他们根本没有取笑他；他们是真的印象深刻。
