communalization: /ˌkəˌmjuːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 共享化；公社化；集体化 | The process of making something communal or shared; the act of transforming something into a communal resource or entity.  It often refers to the process of transforming private property into collective ownership, or the segregation of communities based on religious or ethnic lines. | 例句：The communalization of land led to increased agricultural production.  土地的公社化导致农业产量增加。

The communalization of resources in the disaster relief effort ensured fair distribution. 灾难救济工作中资源的共享确保了公平分配。


近义词：collectivization, socialization, collectivization


反义词：privatization, individualization


词源:  Derived from "communal" + "-ization". "Communal" originates from the Latin "communis" meaning "common," "shared," or "public."  The suffix "-ization" indicates the process of making something or becoming something.
