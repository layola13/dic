fanned:/fænd/ | v. |煽动；扇；激起；加剧 | To move air with a fan or something similar; to stir up or excite (something).  | 例句：She fanned herself with a palm leaf. 她用棕榈叶扇风。
He fanned the flames of the fire. 他煽动火焰。 |近义词：stir, agitate, excite | 反义词：calm, soothe, quell |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fans himself with a newspaper. = 他用报纸扇风。
The wind fans the flames. = 风煽动着火焰。

一般过去时:
She fanned the embers to rekindle the fire. = 她煽动余烬以重新燃起火。
The crowd fanned the rumors. = 人群散布了谣言。

现在完成时:
The media has fanned the flames of controversy. = 媒体加剧了争议。
He has fanned the flames of rebellion. = 他煽动了叛乱。

过去完成时:
They had fanned the flames of discontent before the riots. = 暴乱前他们已煽动不满情绪。
The newspaper had fanned public anxieties before the election. = 报纸在选举前煽动了公众的焦虑。


将来时:
The politician will fan the flames of hatred during his speech. = 这位政治家将在他的演讲中煽动仇恨。
The wind will fan the fire and spread the blaze. = 风会煽动火焰并蔓延大火。

虚拟语气:
If he fanned the flames, the fire would have spread faster. = 如果他煽动火焰，火势就会蔓延得更快。
I wish the news hadn't fanned the flames of fear. = 我真希望新闻没有煽动恐惧情绪。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

He didn't fan himself because it wasn't hot. = 他没有扇风，因为不热。
