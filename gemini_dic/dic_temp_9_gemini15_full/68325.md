fall behind:/fɔːl bɪˈhaɪnd/| v.|落后，拖欠，疏于 | To fail to keep up with something or someone; to become overdue or neglected. |例句：He fell behind in his studies. 他学习落后了。 |例句：She fell behind on her rent payments. 她拖欠房租了。|近义词：lag, delay, default |反义词：lead, surpass, excel


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They fall behind in their work every week. = 他们每周工作都落后。
We fall behind schedule regularly. = 我们经常落后于进度。


一般过去时:
The project fell behind schedule due to unexpected delays. =由于意外延误，项目进度落后了。
He fell behind in his payments last month. = 他上个月拖欠了付款。


现在完成时:
The team has fallen behind in the competition. = 这个团队在比赛中落后了。
I have fallen behind on my reading. = 我的阅读落后了。


过去完成时:
By the time the reinforcements arrived, the army had fallen behind enemy lines. = 当增援部队到达时，军队已经落后于敌军防线。
We realized that we had fallen behind in our preparations for the presentation. = 我们意识到我们在演示准备方面落后了。


将来时:
If we don't work harder, we will fall behind. = 如果我们不更加努力，我们将落后。
The company fears it will fall behind its competitors. = 公司担心它将落后于竞争对手。


虚拟语气:
If I had studied harder, I wouldn't have fallen behind. = 如果我学习更努力，我就不会落后了。
I wish I hadn't fallen behind on my assignments. = 我真希望我没有拖欠作业。


被动语态:
The project was allowed to fall behind schedule. = 项目被允许落后于进度。
The students were often found to have fallen behind in their studies. = 经常发现学生们在学习上落后。


疑问句:
Has the construction fallen behind schedule? = 建筑工程进度落后了吗？
Are we falling behind our competitors? = 我们正在落后于竞争对手吗？


否定句:
We didn't fall behind on the project. = 我们没有在项目上落后。
He didn't fall behind in his studies this semester. = 这个学期他的学习没有落后。
