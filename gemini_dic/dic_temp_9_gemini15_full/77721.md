gemmipara:/dʒɛmˈɪpərə/| n. |胎生动物|A gemmipara is an organism that reproduces by gemmation, a process of asexual reproduction where new individuals bud off from the parent organism.  Gemmiparity is common in many invertebrate animals, such as hydroids and bryozoans. | Example:  Hydra reproduce asexually through gemmiparity.  水螅通过出芽生殖进行无性繁殖。|近义词:budding organism,  |反义词: oviparous, viviparous.
