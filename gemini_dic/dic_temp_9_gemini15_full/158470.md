pledges:/ˈplɛdʒɪz/| n. v.|保证；誓言；抵押；许诺| a solemn promise or undertaking.  A pledge can refer to a formal promise to do something, a promise of support, or something given as security for a loan.|例句：He pledged his support for the candidate. 他保证支持这位候选人。
The company pledged to improve its environmental record.  这家公司保证改善其环保记录。|近义词：promise, vow, oath, guarantee|反义词：break, renege


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pledges his allegiance to the country. = 他宣誓效忠国家。
She pledges to donate to charity. = 她承诺捐款给慈善机构。


一般过去时:
They pledged their support for the project. = 他们保证支持这个项目。
He pledged his loyalty to the king. = 他向国王宣誓效忠。


现在完成时:
The government has pledged to reduce pollution. = 政府已经承诺减少污染。
She has pledged her life savings to the cause. = 她已经为这项事业捐出了毕生的积蓄。


过去完成时:
They had pledged their support before the campaign began. = 竞选开始前他们就已承诺支持。
He had pledged his word, and he couldn't go back on it. = 他已经许下诺言，不能反悔。


将来时:
We will pledge our resources to the relief effort. = 我们将把我们的资源投入到救援工作中。
She will pledge to work harder in the future. = 她将承诺将来更加努力工作。


虚拟语气:
If he pledged his support, we would win. = 如果他承诺支持，我们将获胜。
I wish he had pledged his support earlier. = 我希望他早些承诺支持。


被动语态:
The money was pledged to the hospital. = 这笔钱捐给了医院。
Support was pledged by many organizations. = 许多组织都承诺提供支持。


疑问句:
Did he pledge to help us? = 他承诺帮助我们吗？
Has she pledged her allegiance yet? = 她已经宣誓效忠了吗？


否定句:
He did not pledge his allegiance. = 他没有宣誓效忠。
She didn't pledge to donate any money. = 她没有承诺捐款。


PHRASE->
