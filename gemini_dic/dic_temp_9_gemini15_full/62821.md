exhort:/ɪɡˈzɔːrt/| vt.| 劝告；力劝；激励 | To urge or strongly encourage (someone) to do something.  |例句：The coach exhorted his team to play harder. 教练力劝他的队员更努力地比赛。

例句：She exhorted them to fight for their rights. 她力劝他们为自己的权利而斗争。


近义词：urge, encourage, incite, spur | 反义词：discourage, dissuade

词源:  Middle English: from Old French *exhorter, from Latin exhortārī ‘to encourage’, from ex- ‘out’ + hortārī ‘to encourage’.  The root *hort- is also found in words like 'hortatory' (giving encouragement) and 'horticulture' (the cultivation of gardens).

He exhorts his followers to remain faithful. 他劝告他的追随者保持忠诚。

一般过去时:
The general exhorted his troops before the battle. 将军在战斗前激励他的军队。

现在完成时:

The preacher has exhorted his congregation to repentance. 牧师一直在劝诫他的会众悔改。


过去完成时:
The captain had exhorted his crew to abandon ship. 船长曾劝告他的船员弃船。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
