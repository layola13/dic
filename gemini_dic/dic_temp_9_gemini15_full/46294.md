/dʒʌdʒ/ | vt. n. | 判断；裁判；法官 | To form an opinion or judgment about something or someone.  | The judge sentenced him to five years in prison. 法官判处他五年监禁。| assess, evaluate, determine | accept, believe | v.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The judge judges the case fairly. = 法官公平地审理案件。
He judges people by their actions. = 他根据人们的行为来判断他们。

一般过去时:
The jury judged him guilty. = 陪审团判定他有罪。
She judged the cake to be delicious. = 她认为蛋糕很好吃。

现在完成时:
They have judged the competition fairly. = 他们已经公平地评判了比赛。
I have judged him wrongly. = 我误判了他。

过去完成时:
The judge had already judged the case before the new evidence emerged. = 在新的证据出现之前，法官已经审理了此案。
They had judged the painting to be a masterpiece. = 他们曾认为这幅画是杰作。

将来时:
The committee will judge the entries next week. = 委员会下周将评判参赛作品。
I will judge the situation carefully before I act. = 我会在采取行动之前仔细判断形势。

虚拟语气:
If I were to judge, I would say he's innocent. = 如果要我判断，我会说他是无辜的。
I wish I had judged him more kindly. = 我希望我当时对他更友善一些。

被动语态:
The case was judged fairly. = 此案审理得很公平。
The winner will be judged on merit. = 获胜者将根据成绩来评判。

疑问句:
How do you judge the quality of the product? = 你如何判断产品的质量？
How will the judges judge the contestants? = 评委将如何评判参赛者？

否定句:
I don't judge people based on their appearance. = 我不会以貌取人。
He didn't judge the situation correctly. = 他没有正确判断形势。


PHRASE->

judge of = 对……有鉴赏力
