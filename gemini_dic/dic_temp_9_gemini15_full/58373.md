dualistic:/duˈælɪstɪk/| adj.|二元论的；二元性的| Relating to or characterized by dualism; consisting of two parts or elements.  The term often refers to a worldview that divides reality into two opposing and often irreconcilable entities, such as good and evil, mind and body, or spirit and matter. |例句：His philosophical approach is distinctly dualistic, seeing the world as a constant struggle between opposing forces. 他的哲学方法明显是二元论的，认为世界是相互对立的力量之间的持续斗争。

例句：Many religions hold a dualistic view of the afterlife, separating heaven and hell. 许多宗教对来世持有二元论的观点，将天堂和地狱分开。


近义词：dichotomous, binary, twofold, bipolar|反义词：monistic, unitary, holistic


USAGE->
(由于dualistic是形容词，没有动词或名词的时态变化，以下只提供例句展示其在不同句式中的用法)

否定句:
The theory isn't entirely dualistic; it incorporates elements of holism. = 这个理论并非完全是二元论的；它融合了整体论的元素。
His worldview is not strictly dualistic; he sees shades of gray. = 他的世界观并非严格的二元论；他看到了灰色地带。


疑问句:
Is his perspective truly dualistic, or is there a more nuanced understanding? = 他的观点真的是二元论的吗？还是有更细致的理解？
Could this dualistic approach be applied to other fields of study? = 这种二元论的方法可以应用于其他研究领域吗？


比较级:
The more dualistic the approach, the less effective it proved in solving the problem. = 方法越二元论，在解决问题上就越无效。
This philosophy is less dualistic than previous interpretations. = 这套哲学比之前的解释更少二元论。
