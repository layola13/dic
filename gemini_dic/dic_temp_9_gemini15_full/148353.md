overspeculation:/ˌoʊvərˌspɛkjəˈleɪʃən/| n. |过度投机；过度推测 |The act of speculating excessively, often involving high risk and potential for significant losses.  It can refer to financial markets, real estate, or any area where predictions about future value are made.  |例句：The housing market crash was partly attributed to overspeculation in the years leading up to it.  房地产市场崩溃部分归因于之前几年的过度投机。

The recent surge in cryptocurrency prices is driven by overspeculation.  加密货币价格的近期飙升是由过度投机驱动的。


近义词：excess speculation, excessive speculation, undue speculation |反义词：cautious investment, prudent investing

词源:
The word "overspeculation" is a combination of "over-" (meaning "excessively" or "too much") and "speculation" (from Latin "speculare," meaning "to look," "observe," or "consider").  Therefore, it literally means "excessive speculation" or "too much speculation".
