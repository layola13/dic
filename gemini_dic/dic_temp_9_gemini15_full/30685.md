certainness: /ˈsɜːtnənəs/ | n. |确定性，确信；确实|The state of being certain; firm conviction.  It implies a strong belief or knowledge that something is true or will happen.  |例句：Her certainness about the outcome of the election was striking. 她对选举结果的确定性令人吃惊。

例句：The experiment yielded results of remarkable certainness. 实验产生了令人瞩目的确切结果。

近义词：certainty, assurance, confidence, conviction |反义词：doubt, uncertainty, insecurity, skepticism |词性:名词


Etymology:

The word "certainness" comes from the Old French word "certeint," which itself derives from the Latin word "certus," meaning "sure," "certain," or "fixed."  The suffix "-ness" is a common English suffix indicating a state or quality.
