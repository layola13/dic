preculture:/ˈpriːkʌltʃər/ | n. | 预培养；预培养物 | A preculture is a small-scale culture of microorganisms grown before inoculation into a larger-scale culture.  This is done to ensure a sufficient number of cells are available for the main experiment and to ensure the cells are in a healthy, actively growing state.  | Example: We prepared a preculture of *E. coli* overnight to ensure enough cells for the transformation experiment tomorrow. 我们准备了一个大肠杆菌的预培养物过夜，以确保明天转化实验有足够的细胞。 | 近义词：inoculum, starter culture | 反义词：main culture, final culture | 名词
