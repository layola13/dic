old-mannish:/ˌoʊldˈmænɪʃ/ | adj. | 老态龙钟的；像老人的；| Having the characteristics of an old man;  suggesting age, feebleness, or senility.  It often implies a negative connotation, suggesting weakness or being overly cautious and conservative. |


He had an old-mannish gait, shuffling slowly along the street. 他步履蹒跚，像个老人一样慢慢地走在街上。

She adopted an old-mannish approach to the problem, focusing on caution rather than innovation. 她对这个问题采取了一种老套的方法，专注于谨慎而不是创新。


近义词：senile, doddering, feeble, decrepit,  elderly (less negative)

反义词：youthful, vigorous, energetic, spry
