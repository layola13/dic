organize:/ˈɔːrɡənaɪz/| vt. |组织；安排；筹备|To arrange or put in order; to form into a structured whole.|例句：We need to organize the files before the meeting. 我们需要在会议前整理好文件。|例句：She organized a surprise party for her friend. 她为朋友举办了一个惊喜派对。|近义词：arrange, structure, systematize|反义词：disorganize, disorder, chaos|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She organizes her work efficiently. = 她高效地组织工作。
They organize events for the community. = 他们为社区组织活动。

一般过去时:
He organized a fundraising dinner last year. = 他去年组织了一场筹款晚宴。
We organized the books by author. = 我们按作者整理了书籍。


现在完成时:
They have organized several successful campaigns. = 他们已经组织了几次成功的活动。
She has organized her thoughts for the presentation. = 她已经整理好演讲思路。


过去完成时:
He had organized everything before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前他已经把一切都安排好了。
They had organized a protest march the previous week. = 他们前一周组织了一场抗议游行。


将来时:
We will organize a trip to Europe next summer. = 我们明年夏天将组织一次欧洲之旅。
They will organize the conference in the spring. = 他们将在春天组织这次会议。


虚拟语气:
If I organized my time better, I would be less stressed. = 如果我更好地安排时间，我会压力小一些。
I wish I had organized the party more efficiently. = 我希望我更有效率地组织了这次派对。


被动语态:
The event was organized by a local charity. = 这场活动是由当地一个慈善机构组织的。
The documents are being organized into folders. = 文件正在被整理到文件夹中。


疑问句:
How do you organize your schedule? = 你如何安排你的日程？
Who organized the meeting? = 谁组织的会议？


否定句:
He didn't organize the meeting properly. = 他没有好好组织会议。
They haven't organized the details of the trip yet. = 他们还没有安排好旅行的细节。


PHRASE->
organize a meeting = 组织会议
organize a team = 组织团队

organize a campaign = 组织一场活动
organize data = 整理数据
