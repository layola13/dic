increased:/ɪnˈkriːst/ | adj. v. | 增加的；增长的；提高的 |  To become or make greater in size, amount, number, or degree.  | 例句：The company's profits increased significantly last year.  去年公司利润显著增长。

例句：The demand for electric vehicles has increased dramatically.  对电动汽车的需求急剧增加。

近义词：augmented, amplified, escalated, heightened | 反义词：decreased, diminished, reduced, lessened | 词性：verb (及物和不及物), adjective


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The price increases every year. = 价格每年都在上涨。
Demand for the product increases steadily. = 对该产品的需求稳步增长。

一般过去时:
The temperature increased sharply yesterday. = 昨天的气温急剧上升。
The number of participants increased by 20%. = 参加人数增加了20%。

现在完成时:
The population has increased significantly in recent years. = 近年来人口数量显著增加。
The company's market share has increased since the launch of the new product. = 自推出新产品以来，公司的市场份额有所增长。

过去完成时:
The water level had increased before the flood. = 洪水来临之前水位已经上涨了。
The debt had increased considerably before the crisis. = 在危机爆发前，债务已经大幅增加。

将来时:
The cost of living will increase next year. = 明年的生活成本将会上涨。
The company's sales are projected to increase next quarter. = 预计公司下季度的销售额将会增长。

虚拟语气:
If the temperature increased further, the ice would melt. = 如果气温进一步升高，冰就会融化。
If the government had increased funding, the project would have succeeded. = 如果政府增加资金，项目本来就会成功。

被动语态:
The tax was increased by 10%. = 税收增加了10%。
The speed limit has been increased to 70 mph. = 限速已提高到每小时70英里。

疑问句:
Has the number of students increased this year? = 今年学生人数增加了多少？
Did the unemployment rate increase last month? = 上个月失业率上升了吗？

否定句:
The price has not increased this month. = 本月价格没有上涨。
The company's profits did not increase last quarter. = 上季度公司利润没有增长。


PHRASE->
increased demand = 增加的需求
increased production = 产量增加
increased efficiency = 效率提高
increased competition = 竞争加剧
increased risk = 风险增加


词源:
The word "increased" comes from the verb "increase," which has Old French origins.  It's ultimately derived from the Latin "incrementare," meaning "to increase," combining "in-" (in, into) and "crescere" (to grow).
