razor-sharp: /ˈreɪzərˌʃɑːrp/ | adj. | 锋利的，锐利的 | Extremely sharp, like a razor blade.  The adjective combines "razor," referring to the extremely sharp blade of a razor, with "sharp" to emphasize the degree of sharpness.  It implies a precision and potential for cutting or piercing that goes beyond simply being sharp. | 例句：The knife had a razor-sharp edge. 这把刀刃锋利无比。  The critic's razor-sharp wit dissected the film's flaws. 批评家犀利的评论剖析了电影的缺陷。 | 近义词：acute, keen, incisive, cutting | 反义词：blunt, dull, obtuse
