preendorsement:/ˌpriːɪnˈdɔːsmənt/ | n. | 预先背书；预先认可 | The act of endorsing a negotiable instrument before it is transferred to another party.  This often involves signing the instrument before the actual transfer of ownership or rights.  It can provide additional security or guarantee to the subsequent holder. | Example: The preendorsement of the check by the company's treasurer ensured its validity. 预先由公司财务主管背书的支票确保了其有效性。 | 近义词：prior endorsement, advance endorsement | 反义词：postendorsement
