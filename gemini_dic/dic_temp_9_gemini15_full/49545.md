dematerialised: /ˌdiːməˈtɪəriəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 非物质化的；无形的 |  Converted from a physical to a digital or electronic form; existing only as data.  | 例句：The company's records have been dematerialised and stored on a secure server. 公司的记录已经非物质化并存储在安全的服务器上。


The increasing use of electronic trading has led to the dematerialisation of many financial assets. 电子交易的日益普及导致许多金融资产非物质化。| 近义词：virtual, intangible, digitized | 反义词：material, tangible, physical |  形容词

词源:

* **de-:**  prefix indicating removal or reversal. (表示去除或反转的前缀)
* **material:**  relating to matter or physical substance. (与物质或物理物质相关的)
* **-ised:**  past participle suffix indicating a process or state of being. (过去分词后缀，表示一个过程或状态)

Therefore, "dematerialised" means to have removed the material aspect of something, transforming it into a non-physical form. (因此，“非物质化”意味着去除了事物的物质方面，将其转化为非物理形式。)
