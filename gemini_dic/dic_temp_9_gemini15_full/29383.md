casuist:/ˈkæzjʊɪst/| n. |诡辩家；细致分析道德难题者|A casuist is someone who is skilled in or given to casuistry; someone who makes subtle distinctions in moral questions, sometimes to justify questionable actions.  They often engage in sophisticated reasoning to resolve moral dilemmas, sometimes stretching ethical principles to accommodate their desired outcomes. |The lawyer, a notorious casuist, found loopholes in the law to defend his client.  这位律师，一个臭名昭著的诡辩家，找到了法律漏洞来为他的客户辩护。|sophist, logician, ethicist |moralist, absolutist |名词
