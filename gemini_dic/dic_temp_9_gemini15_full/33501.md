ciborium:/ˈsɪbəriəm/| n. |圣餐宝盒；(盛圣餐饼的)器皿|A ciborium is a covered, usually gold or silver, cup or vessel used in some Christian churches to hold the consecrated bread (the Eucharist) during the celebration of the Eucharist (Holy Communion).  It is often elaborately decorated. |The ciborium sat on the altar, gleaming under the candlelight.  圣餐宝盒在祭坛上闪耀着烛光。  The priest carefully placed the consecrated host into the ciborium.  牧师小心翼翼地将圣体放入圣餐宝盒。|pyxis, monstrance|—|
