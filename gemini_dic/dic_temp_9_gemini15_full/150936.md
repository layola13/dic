paralogy:/ˈpærəlɒdʒi/ | n. | 并系同源，旁系同源 | The evolutionary relationship between genes or proteins that arose from gene duplication within a species.  Paralogs are homologous genes that diverged after a gene duplication event.  They often have similar but not identical functions. | Example:  The different globin genes in humans are paralogs, having arisen from gene duplication events. 人类不同的珠蛋白基因是旁系同源基因，它们起源于基因复制事件。 |近义词：ortholog (with qualification), homolog |反义词：orthology
