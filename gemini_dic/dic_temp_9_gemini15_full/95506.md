inclined:/ɪnˈklaɪnd/ | adj. | 倾向于；倾斜的；有…的意向 | Having a tendency or disposition to do something or feel a certain way;  sloping or leaning. |例句：I'm inclined to agree with you. 我倾向于同意你的观点。

例句：The tower is inclined slightly to the south. 塔身略微向南倾斜。

近义词：disposed, prone, tending, slanted, sloping

反义词：upright, vertical, straight, opposed, unwilling


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is inclined to be lazy. 他倾向于懒惰。
She is inclined to believe him. 她倾向于相信他。


一般过去时:
I was inclined to think so. 我当时倾向于这样认为。
They were inclined to accept the offer. 他们倾向于接受这个提议。


现在完成时:
I have been inclined to help her. 我一直倾向于帮助她。
She has been inclined to keep her opinions to herself. 她一直倾向于保守自己的意见。


过去完成时:
He had been inclined to give up before he tried. 他在尝试之前就倾向于放弃。
They had been inclined to disagree on this matter.  他们一直倾向于在这个问题上意见相左。


将来时:
I will be inclined to make a decision tomorrow. 我明天将倾向于做出决定。
She will be inclined to reconsider her position. 她将会倾向于重新考虑她的立场。


虚拟语气:
I would be inclined to help if I had the time. 如果我有时间，我会倾向于帮忙。
If he were inclined to listen, he would learn a lot. 如果他愿意倾听，他会学到很多。


被动语态:
His opinion is inclined to be ignored. 他的意见往往被忽视。
The building is inclined at a dangerous angle. 建筑物以危险的角度倾斜。


疑问句:
Are you inclined to accept the invitation? 你倾向于接受邀请吗？
Is he inclined to work overtime? 他倾向于加班吗？


否定句:
I am not inclined to believe his story. 我并不倾向于相信他的故事。
She was not inclined to compromise. 她不愿意妥协。


PHRASE->
be inclined to do sth.  倾向于做某事
inclined plane 斜面
inclined orbit 倾斜轨道
be strongly inclined to  非常倾向于
have an inclination to 有…的倾向


词源:
The word "inclined" comes from the Old French word "encliner," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "inclinare," meaning "to bend, to lean, to incline."  The prefix "in-" means "in" or "into," and the root "-clinare" is related to the word "cline," meaning "to lean" or "to bend."
