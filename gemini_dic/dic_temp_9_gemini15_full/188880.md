rusticate:/ˈrʌstɪkeɪt/ | v. | 使回到乡村；送回乡下；过乡村生活 | To compel (a student) to leave a university or college for a period, typically as a punishment.  Also to live in or spend time in the countryside; to give something a rustic appearance. | Example: He was rusticated from Oxford for a year. 他因受处罚而被牛津大学勒令休学一年。  Example:  They decided to rusticate for the summer, escaping the city's hustle. 他们决定夏天去乡下避暑，逃离城市的喧嚣。| 近义词：exile, banish, deport (in the context of punishment); retire, withdraw (in the context of living in the countryside) | 反义词：urbanize, civilize (in the context of lifestyle)
