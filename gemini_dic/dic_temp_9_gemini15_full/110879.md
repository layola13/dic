lionizers: /ˈlaɪənaɪzərz/ | n. | 崇拜者；吹捧者 | Those who lionize someone excessively praise or admire them, often to an excessive or uncritical degree.  They elevate the person to a heroic or legendary status. | The media lionized the athlete after his incredible victory.  媒体在他不可思议的胜利后将他捧为英雄。 |  admirers, flatterers, idolizers | critics, detractors


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They lionize the famous actor. = 他们崇拜这位著名的演员。
The public lionizes successful entrepreneurs. = 公众崇拜成功的企业家。


一般过去时:
The press lionized the young novelist. = 出版界捧红了这位年轻的小说家。
People lionized the war hero. = 人们将这位战争英雄捧上了天。


现在完成时:
The city has lionized its founding fathers. = 这个城市一直以来都崇敬它的开国元勋。
The media has lionized this singer for years. = 媒体多年来一直追捧这位歌手。


过去完成时:
They had lionized him before his scandal. = 在他丑闻曝光之前，人们一直很崇拜他。
The town had lionized the local artist for decades. = 这个小镇几十年来一直崇拜这位当地艺术家。


将来时:
They will lionize her after the release of her new album. = 她的新专辑发行后，人们将会崇拜她。
Future generations will lionize this scientist for his groundbreaking work. = 后代将因为这位科学家的开创性工作而崇拜他。


虚拟语气:
If he won the Nobel Prize, people would lionize him. = 如果他获得诺贝尔奖，人们就会崇拜他。
I wish they wouldn't lionize such a controversial figure. = 我希望他们不要过度崇拜这样一个有争议的人物。


被动语态:
He was lionized by the critics. = 他受到了评论家的推崇。
The athlete was lionized for his achievements. = 这位运动员因其成就而备受推崇。


疑问句:
Will they lionize the new champion? = 他们会崇拜新的冠军吗？
Are people still lionizing that outdated musician? = 人们还在崇拜那位过时的音乐家吗？


否定句:
They did not lionize the quiet researcher. = 他们并没有崇拜这位安静的研究人员。
The public did not lionize him despite his success. = 尽管他成功了，公众并没有崇拜他。
