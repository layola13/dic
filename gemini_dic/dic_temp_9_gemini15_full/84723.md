handiness's:/ˈhændinəsɪz/| n.|灵巧；便利；熟练；方便|The word "handiness's" is a somewhat unusual and arguably incorrect form.  It appears to be an attempt to pluralize "handiness," which is already a noun.  The correct plural form is simply "handinesses."  Handiness refers to the quality of being handy or convenient; skill and dexterity in using one's hands; or the state of being readily available or easily used. |例句：The handiness of this tool made the job much easier.  这个工具的便捷使工作容易多了。The carpenter's handiness was evident in the finely crafted furniture. 木匠的灵巧在精美的家具上显而易见。|近义词：dexterity, convenience, usefulness, aptness|反义词：clumsiness, inconvenience, awkwardness|名词
