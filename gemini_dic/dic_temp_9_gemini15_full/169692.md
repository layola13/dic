propositions:/ˌprɒpəˈzɪʃənz/ /ˌprɑːpəˈzɪʃənz/| n.|命题，提议，建议 | A proposition is a statement that expresses a judgment or opinion.  It can be true or false. In logic, propositions are the basic units of reasoning.  In everyday language, a proposition is a suggestion or proposal. |例句：He made a proposition to buy the house. 他提议买下这栋房子。  The professor explained the logical propositions in detail. 教授详细解释了逻辑命题。|近义词：proposal, suggestion, assertion, statement|反义词：conclusion, fact (in the sense that a proposition might be *false*)|名词

词源:
From Old French *proposicioun*, from Latin *propositio*, from *proponere* "to put before, offer," from *pro-* "forth, forward" + *ponere* "to put, place."  The word's evolution reflects its core meaning of something put forward for consideration.
