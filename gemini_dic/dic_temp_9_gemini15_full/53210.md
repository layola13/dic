direct:/dəˈrekt/|adj., v., n.|直接的；指导；导演|

英文解析：As an adjective, "direct" means straightforward, without intermediaries. As a verb, it means to guide or manage. As a noun, it refers to a director or a straight path.

例句：
The direct route is faster. = 直达路线更快。

He directed the film. = 他执导了这部电影。


近义词：straightforward, immediate, guide, manage, oversee

反义词：indirect, circuitous, roundabout, meandering


词性: adjective, verb, noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He directs the orchestra. = 他指挥乐队。
She directs the marketing team. = 她领导营销团队。

一般过去时:
I directed him to the station. = 我指引他去车站。
They directed the traffic. = 他们指挥交通。

现在完成时:
She has directed several successful films. = 她执导过几部成功的电影。
He has directed the company for ten years. = 他领导这家公司十年了。

过去完成时:
They had directed the workers to the new site before the accident. = 事故发生前他们已将工人带到新的工地。
She had directed the project effectively before her promotion. = 她升职前有效地领导了这个项目。

将来时:
I will direct the meeting tomorrow. = 我明天将主持会议。
He will direct the play next year. = 他明年将执导这部戏。

虚拟语气:
If he directed the project, it would be finished on time. = 如果他来领导这个项目，它就能按时完成。
I wish I had directed my efforts differently. = 我真希望我当初能把精力放在别的地方。

被动语态:
The film was directed by a famous director. = 这部电影是由一位著名导演执导的。
The traffic was directed by the police officers. = 交通由警官指挥。

疑问句:
Do you direct your own projects? = 你自己领导项目吗？
Who directed the movie? = 谁执导的这部电影？

否定句:
He doesn't direct the company anymore. = 他不再领导这家公司了。
I didn't direct my efforts toward that goal. = 我没有把精力放在那个目标上。
