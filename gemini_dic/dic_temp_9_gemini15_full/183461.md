republics:/rɪˈpʌblɪks/| n. |共和国（复数）|A republic is a state in which supreme power is held by the people and their elected representatives, and which has an elected or nominated president rather than a monarch.  It emphasizes the sovereignty of the people and representative government.|Example: Many countries around the world are republics, including France and the United States.|近义词：commonwealths, states|反义词：monarchies, kingdoms|名词

一般现在时:
The United States and France are republics. = 美国和法国都是共和国。
Many countries operate as republics. = 许多国家以共和国的形式运作。


一般过去时:
Ancient Rome was a republic for centuries. = 古罗马曾经是共和国几个世纪。
Several colonies declared themselves republics. = 几个殖民地宣布自己为共和国。


现在完成时:
Many former colonies have become republics. = 许多前殖民地已经成为共和国。
Several nations have transitioned to republics in recent years. = 近年来，一些国家已经过渡到共和国。


过去完成时:
