/ˈdaɪəɡræf/| n. |双字母组合，双音字母|A digraph is a combination of two letters representing one sound.  For example, "sh" in "ship" or "ch" in "chair".  Digraphs can be made up of vowels or consonants or a combination of both. They are frequently used in spelling and phonics. |The digraph "th" is pronounced differently in "thin" and "this".|近义词：double letter, diphthong (though diphthong refers to vowel sounds)|反义词：monograph (a single letter representing a sound)|名词
