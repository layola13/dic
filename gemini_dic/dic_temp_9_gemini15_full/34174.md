clamminess:/ˈklæmɪnəs/ | n. |潮湿；黏糊；发粘 |The unpleasant feeling of being damp and sticky; a state of being clammy.  It often refers to skin that is cold and moist to the touch, usually due to illness, fear, or exertion.|例句：The clamminess of his skin alarmed the doctor.  他的皮肤潮湿黏糊，这使医生感到不安。

The clamminess of the air made it difficult to breathe. 空气的潮湿黏糊让人难以呼吸。

近义词：dampness, stickiness, moistness, sogginess |反义词：dryness


词源:  Derived from the adjective "clammy," which in turn comes from the Middle English word "clam," meaning "sticky" or "gluey."  This is likely related to the Old Norse word "klíma," meaning "clay."
