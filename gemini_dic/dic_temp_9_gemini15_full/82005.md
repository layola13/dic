griped:/ɡraɪpt/| v.|抱怨，牢骚；抓住，紧握|to complain or grumble; to hold or grasp firmly. |例句：He griped about the long hours. 他抱怨工作时间太长。  He griped the steering wheel tightly. 他紧紧地握住方向盘。|近义词：complain, grumble, moan, clutch|反义词：praise, compliment

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gripes about his job every day. = 他每天都抱怨他的工作。
They gripe constantly about the noise. = 他们不断地抱怨噪音。

一般过去时:
She griped to her friend about her boyfriend. = 她向朋友抱怨她的男朋友。
He griped the rope and pulled himself up. = 他抓住绳子，把自己拉了上去。

现在完成时:
I have griped about this problem for weeks. = 我已经抱怨这个问题几周了。
She has griped the handle for a long time. = 她已经握住把手很久了。

过去完成时:
He had griped about the injustice before he left. = 他在离开之前就抱怨过这种不公正。
They had griped the railing to steady themselves. = 他们抓住栏杆来稳住自己。

将来时:
He will gripe to his boss about the unfair treatment. = 他将会向老板抱怨不公平的待遇。
She will gripe the bag tightly during the journey. = 她会在旅途中紧紧地抓住包。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't gripe so much. = 如果我是你，我就不会抱怨这么多。
I wish he wouldn't gripe all the time. = 我希望他不要总是抱怨。

被动语态:
The situation was griped about by many people. = 许多人抱怨这种情况。
The handle was griped firmly by her. = 她的手紧紧地握着把手。

疑问句:
What are you griping about? = 你在抱怨什么？
Does he often gripe about his work? = 他经常抱怨他的工作吗？

否定句:
He didn't gripe about the food. = 他没有抱怨食物。
She didn't gripe the opportunity to speak up. = 她没有抓住发言的机会。
