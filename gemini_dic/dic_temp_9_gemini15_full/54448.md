disinheritance: /ˌdɪsɪnˈhɛrɪtəns/ | n. | 剥夺继承权；被剥夺继承权 | The act of depriving someone of their inheritance or right to inherit.  It often involves a legal process where a testator (the person making a will) specifically excludes someone from receiving any property or assets upon their death. | Example:  The disinheritance of his eldest son caused a major family feud. = 他长子的被剥夺继承权导致了一场严重的家庭纠纷。  His disinheritance was a result of his repeated acts of rebellion. = 他被剥夺继承权是因为他屡次叛逆的行为。 | 近义词: dispossession, deprivation | 反义词: inheritance, bequest
