front:/frʌnt/| n. adj. adv.|前面；前部；前线；前排；最重要的；主要的；最显著的；向前；在前地|The front of the house is painted white.  房子的前面漆成白色。|The front page of the newspaper featured the story.  报纸的头版刊登了这个故事。|近义词：forefront, leading, foremost|反义词：rear, back


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The car is parked in front of the house. = 车停在房子前面。
She sits in the front row of the classroom. = 她坐在教室的前排。


一般过去时:
He stood at the front of the line. = 他站在队伍的最前面。
They marched at the front during the parade. = 他们在游行队伍的最前面行进。


现在完成时:
They have moved the furniture to the front of the room. = 他们已将家具搬到房间的前面。
She has always been at the front of her class. = 她一直是班上的佼佼者。


过去完成时:
The soldiers had reached the front lines before nightfall. = 士兵们在天黑前到达了前线。
He had already positioned himself at the front of the stage. = 他已经在舞台前就位了。


将来时:
We will meet you at the front of the building. = 我们将在建筑物前面与你会面。
The company will launch its new product at the front and center of the marketing campaign. = 公司将在营销活动的核心位置推出其新产品。


虚拟语气:
If I were at the front, I would see the situation clearly. = 如果我在前线，我会清楚地看到情况。
I wish I had been at the front of the innovation. = 我希望我当时处于创新的前沿。


被动语态:
The new building is being constructed at the front of the property. = 新建筑正在房产的前面建造。
The issue was brought to the front during the meeting. = 这个问题在会议上被提了出来。


疑问句:
Is there a store in front of the library? = 图书馆前面有商店吗？
Who sat in the front seat of the car? = 谁坐在汽车的前座？


否定句:
There is nothing in front of the building. = 建筑物前面什么也没有。
He did not sit at the front of the class. = 他没有坐在教室的前排。


PHRASE->

bring to the front:  使突出；重视
at the front of: 在…前面
in front of: 在…前面
from the front: 从前面
the front line: 前线


WORD ORIGIN->

Old English *frunt, from Latin *frons, frontis ("forehead, front").  The meaning extended from the literal "forehead" to the metaphorical "front" over time.
