kick-starting:/ˈkɪkˌstɑːrtɪŋ/| v. |启动，发起，激发| To give something a sudden start or boost; to initiate something with energy and enthusiasm.  |例句：We need to kick-start the economy. 我们需要刺激经济。|例句：The new marketing campaign kick-started sales. 新的营销活动刺激了销售。|近义词：initiate, launch, jump-start, begin, commence |反义词：halt, stop, cease, end


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They kick-start their day with a run. = 他们每天跑步开始新的一天。
The company kick-starts new projects every quarter. = 公司每个季度启动新的项目。

一般过去时:
The government kick-started the infrastructure project. = 政府启动了基础设施项目。
The new manager kick-started a team-building exercise. = 新经理发起了一次团队建设活动。

现在完成时:
The investment has kick-started economic growth in the region. = 这项投资已经带动了该地区的经济增长。
They have kick-started a fundraising campaign. = 他们已经发起了一场筹款活动。

过去完成时:
They had kick-started the project before the funding was secured. = 在资金到位之前，他们已经启动了这个项目。
The team had kick-started the renovations before the deadline. = 在截止日期之前，团队已经启动了翻新工程。

将来时:
We will kick-start the engine in the morning. = 我们明天早上将启动引擎。
The company will kick-start a new product line next year. = 公司明年将启动一条新的产品线。

虚拟语气:
If we had more resources, we could kick-start the project sooner. = 如果我们有更多资源，我们就可以更快地启动这个项目。
I wish I could kick-start my career more effectively. = 我希望我能更有效地启动我的职业生涯。

被动语态:
The project was kick-started by the government. = 该项目是由政府启动的。
The engine is being kick-started by the mechanic. = 机械师正在启动引擎。

疑问句:
How can we kick-start this stalled negotiation? = 我们如何才能启动这陷入僵局的谈判？
When will they kick-start the construction? = 他们什么时候开始建设？

否定句:
We couldn't kick-start the car. = 我们无法启动汽车。
They failed to kick-start the business. = 他们未能启动业务。
