knockout: /ˈnɑːkaʊt/ | /ˈnɒkaʊt/ | n., v. |击倒；淘汰；极出色的人或事物 |

英文解析:As a noun, a knockout refers to a blow that knocks someone unconscious.  It can also describe something exceptionally attractive or impressive. As a verb, it means to knock someone unconscious or to eliminate someone from a competition.

例句:

n. (击倒):
The boxer delivered a knockout punch, ending the fight instantly. = 拳击手打出一记重拳，瞬间结束了比赛。

The news was a real knockout; I couldn't believe it. = 这个消息真是令人震惊；我简直不敢相信。


v. (击倒):
The heavyweight boxer knocked out his opponent in the first round. = 重量级拳击手在第一轮就将对手击倒。

The strong wind knocked out the power. = 强风导致停电。


n. (淘汰):
He was a knockout in the first round of the tournament. = 他在比赛的第一轮就被淘汰了。

She was a knockout from the competition. = 她被淘汰出局了。


n. (极出色的人或事物):
That dress is a real knockout! = 那件衣服真是太漂亮了！

The new car is a knockout. = 这辆新车真棒！


近义词:
n.:  (击倒) blow, punch, hit; (淘汰) elimination, defeat; (极出色的人或事物) stunner, beauty, marvel

v.:  (击倒)  knock unconscious, floor; (淘汰) eliminate, defeat


反义词:
n.: (击倒)  victory; (淘汰) victory, win; (极出色的人或事物)  dull, boring


词性:
noun, verb
