secretive: /ˈsiːkrətɪv/ | adj. | 偷偷摸摸的；保密的；秘而不宣的 |  Given to keeping secrets; unwilling to share information.  |例句：He was secretive about his past. 他对自己的过去守口如瓶。

例句：She gave a secretive smile. 她神秘地一笑。


近义词：closemouthed, clandestine, private, reticent, reserved | 反义词：open, frank, candid, communicative


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is secretive about his personal life. = 他对自己的私生活很保密。
They are secretive about their plans. = 他们对他们的计划秘而不宣。


一般过去时:
She was secretive during the interrogation. = 她在审讯期间非常隐秘。
He was secretive about his whereabouts. = 他对自己的行踪秘而不宣。


现在完成时:
He has been secretive ever since the incident. = 自从那件事发生后，他就一直很神秘。
They have been secretive about their relationship. = 他们对他们的关系一直保密。


过去完成时:
She had been secretive about her illness for years. = 她多年来一直对自己的病情保密。
He had been secretive about his financial dealings. = 他一直对他的财务往来秘而不宣。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he were less secretive, we would know more. = 如果他不那么保密，我们会知道更多。
I wish he weren't so secretive about his feelings. = 我希望他不必对他的感情如此保密。


被动语态:

疑问句:
