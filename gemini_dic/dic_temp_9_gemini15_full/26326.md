buzz:/bʌz/| n., v.|嗡嗡声；兴奋；闲聊；传播；忙碌 |The continuous low humming sound of bees or insects; a state of excitement or great activity; informal conversation; to spread information quickly; to be busy and active. |例句：The bees were buzzing around the hive. 蜜蜂在蜂箱周围嗡嗡作响。 The city buzzed with excitement before the game.  比赛前城市里一片兴奋。|近义词：hum, drone, excitement, rumour, hustle|反义词：silence, quiet, calm, stillness


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The bees buzz loudly. = 蜜蜂嗡嗡地叫着。
He buzzes around all day. = 他整天都在忙忙碌碌。


一般过去时:
The phone buzzed and I answered it. = 电话响了，我接了。
The news buzzed through the town. = 这个消息在镇上迅速传播开来。


现在完成时:
The news has buzzed all over social media. = 这个消息已经在社交媒体上疯传了。
She has buzzed with energy all day. = 她一整天都精力充沛。


过去完成时:
The room had buzzed with anticipation before the announcement. = 在宣布之前，房间里充满了期待。
The rumour had buzzed through the company before the meeting. = 会议之前，谣言已经在公司里传开了。


将来时:
The stadium will buzz with the crowd. = 体育场将挤满人群，热闹非凡。
The city will buzz with activity during the festival. = 节日期间，城市将充满活力。


虚拟语气:
If the phone buzzed, I'd answer it. = 如果电话响了，我会接听。
If the city buzzed with excitement, I would want to be there. = 如果城市充满兴奋，我想去那里。


被动语态:
The news was buzzed around the office. = 这个消息在办公室里传开了。

疑问句:
Is the phone buzzing? = 电话在响吗？

否定句:

The news didn't buzz around quickly enough. = 这个消息传播得不够快。
