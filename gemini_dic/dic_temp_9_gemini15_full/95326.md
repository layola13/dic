incendiary: /ɪnˈsɛndiˌɛri/ | adj., n. | 纵火的；煽动性的；燃烧弹 |

英文解析:
adj.  Causing or tending to cause fire; relating to arson.  Also, tending to excite or inflame; provocative.
n. A person who deliberately sets fire to something; a firebomb.


例句:
adj. (causing fire):
The arsonist planted an incendiary device in the building. = 纵火犯在建筑物内放置了一个燃烧装置。

The speaker's incendiary remarks ignited a heated debate. = 演讲者的煽动性言论引发了一场激烈的辩论。


adj. (provocative):
His incendiary rhetoric fueled the conflict. = 他煽动性的言辞加剧了冲突。

The article contained incendiary accusations against the government. = 这篇文章包含了针对政府的煽动性指控。


n. (firebomb):
The police discovered several incendiary devices hidden in the car. = 警方在车内发现了几枚燃烧弹。

They launched incendiary bombs at the enemy position. = 他们向敌军阵地投掷燃烧弹。


近义词:
adj. (causing fire): inflammatory, combustible, flammable
adj. (provocative): inflammatory, provocative, agitating, inflammatory
n. (firebomb): firebomb, Molotov cocktail


反义词:
adj. (causing fire): fire-retardant, fireproof
adj. (provocative): calming, soothing, pacific


词性:
adj., n.
