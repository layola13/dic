pseudo-Mexicans: /ˌsuːdoʊˈmɛksɪkənz/ | n. | 假墨西哥人；冒充墨西哥人的人 |  This term refers to people who are not of Mexican descent but who adopt Mexican cultural elements, often superficially or for perceived advantage. It carries a negative connotation, implying inauthenticity, appropriation, or even mockery.  The term is often used in discussions about cultural appropriation and identity politics.  | Example: Some critics argue that certain Hollywood portrayals of Mexicans are simply pseudo-Mexicans, lacking genuine understanding or respect for the culture. | 近义词: cultural appropriators, imitators, posers | 反义词: authentic Mexicans, genuine Mexicans |  名词
