preaffirming: /ˌpriːəˈfɜːrmɪŋ/ | v. | 预先肯定；预先确认 |  To assert or confirm something before it has happened or been proven.  It implies a strong belief or conviction in the future occurrence of something.  The act often involves a commitment or pledge, and may be seen as presumptuous or even arrogant if not based on solid evidence. |

例句：

He was preaffirming his success even before the competition began. = 他甚至在比赛开始之前就预言自己会成功。

She preaffirmed her support for the project, promising significant funding. = 她预先肯定自己支持该项目，并承诺提供大量资金。


近义词：predetermine, prejudge, anticipate (in a positive and assertive way), precommit


反义词：deny, refute, contradict, doubt


词性：动词
