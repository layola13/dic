predisplays:/ˌpriːdɪˈspleɪz/ | v. | 预先展示，预先显示 | To display or show something beforehand.  It implies a showing before the main or intended display. | 例句：The museum predisplayed some of the artifacts before the grand opening. = 博物馆在盛大开幕前预先展出了一些文物。


例句：The company predisplayed its new product line at a trade show. = 公司在贸易展上预先展示了其新的产品线。


近义词：preview, pre-show, showcase (in advance) | 反义词：hide, conceal, keep secret | 词性: verb
