enteradenography:/ˌɛntərədɪˈnɑːɡrəfi/| n. |肠系膜淋巴管造影术|Enteradenography is a medical imaging technique used to visualize the lymphatic vessels of the mesentery (the membrane connecting the intestines to the abdominal wall).  It involves injecting a contrast medium into the lymphatic vessels, allowing visualization of their structure and function. This procedure is less commonly used now due to the advent of other imaging techniques.|The enteradenography revealed a blockage in the lymphatic vessels. 肠系膜淋巴管造影术显示淋巴管阻塞。|lymphangiography, lymphography| --- |noun
