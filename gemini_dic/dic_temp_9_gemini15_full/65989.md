exalter:/ɪɡˈzɔːltər/| vt. | 赞扬；提升；使得意；| To praise highly; to raise in rank, power, or importance; to elevate in spirit or feeling. | The preacher exalted the virtues of faith and charity.  传教士赞扬了信仰和慈善的美德。

The king exalted his loyal general to the rank of duke. 国王将他的忠诚将军提升为公爵。


近义词：praise, glorify, elevate, uplift | 反义词：debase, degrade, humiliate, belittle


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exalts the power of love. 他赞扬爱的力量。
She exalts her children's achievements. 她赞扬她孩子的成就。


一般过去时:
They exalted him to a position of leadership. 他们将他提升到领导地位。
The crowd exalted the victorious team. 人群欢呼胜利的球队。


现在完成时:
He has exalted himself above his peers. 他已经把自己抬高到同侪之上。
The movement has exalted the importance of individual liberty. 这场运动抬高了个体自由的重要性。


过去完成时:
They had exalted him before his downfall. 他们在其垮台之前就抬高了他。
The artist had exalted the beauty of nature in his paintings. 艺术家在其绘画中已经歌颂了大自然的美丽。


将来时:
The company will exalt its new product at the trade show. 公司将在贸易展上推崇其新产品。
We will exalt the winners at the ceremony. 我们将在仪式上赞扬获胜者。


虚拟语气:
If they exalted their virtues, they would be more respected. 如果他们高扬他们的美德，他们会得到更多尊重。
I wish they would exalt the importance of education. 我希望他们会重视教育的重要性。


被动语态:
His achievements were exalted by the media. 他的成就受到媒体的赞扬。
The principles of democracy are exalted in the constitution. 民主原则在宪法中受到推崇。


疑问句:
Does the poem exalt the beauty of nature? 这首诗歌颂大自然的美丽吗？
Will they exalt the new leader? 他们会推崇新领导吗？


否定句:
The article did not exalt the achievements of the government. 这篇文章没有赞扬政府的成就。
He did not exalt himself above others. 他没有把自己摆在别人之上。


PHRASE->
