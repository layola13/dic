interceding: /ˌɪntərˈsiːdɪŋ/ | v. | 恳求；调解；说情 |

英文解析：To intercede is to plead on behalf of another person, to act as a mediator or go-between in a dispute, or to intervene in order to prevent or alter a course of action.  It implies a degree of influence or authority, although not necessarily formal authority.

例句：

The priest interceded with the king on behalf of the condemned prisoner.  = 牧师为被判处死刑的囚犯向国王求情。

She interceded in the argument between her two children. = 她在她两个孩子争吵时进行调解。


近义词：mediate, intervene, plead, advocate, arbitrate


反义词：ignore, neglect, abandon


词性：动词

词源:  The word "intercede" comes from the Latin prefix "inter-" (between, among) and "cedere" (to yield, to go).  It literally means "to go between."
