inducting:/ɪnˈdʌktɪŋ/ | v. | 引导；引入；使就职；归纳；安装 |  The act of introducing someone into a group or position, or of introducing something into a system.  It can also refer to the process of deducing a general principle from specific observations. |

例句：

They are inducting new members into the club this week. = 他们本周正在吸收新会员加入俱乐部。

The ceremony inducted him into the Hall of Fame. = 典礼上他被引入了名人堂。


近义词：introduce, initiate, install, enroll, admit

反义词：expel, remove, dismiss, eject


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are inducting new members. = 他们正在吸收新会员。
The company is inducting new employees into the training program. = 公司正在将新员工纳入培训项目。

一般过去时:
They inducted him into the secret society. = 他们把他引入了秘密社团。
The scientists inducted their findings into a comprehensive report. = 科学家们将他们的发现归纳到一份综合报告中。

现在完成时:
They have inducted several new scholars this year. = 他们今年已经吸收了几位新学者。
The museum has inducted many important artifacts into its collection. = 博物馆已经将许多重要的文物纳入其收藏中。

过去完成时:
Before the ceremony, they had inducted all the new officers. = 在仪式之前，他们已经将所有新军官引入了。
The committee had inducted the evidence into their final report. = 委员会已将证据纳入其最终报告。


将来时:
They will induct the new president next month. = 他们下个月将新总统就职。
The university will induct the honorary doctorate recipients. = 大学将为荣誉博士获得者举行就职仪式。


虚拟语气:
If they inducted him, he would be very honored. = 如果他们让他就职，他会非常荣幸。
I wish they had inducted her into the team earlier. = 我希望他们早点让她加入团队。


被动语态:
He was inducted into the army last year. = 他去年入伍了。
The new software was inducted into the system without any problems. = 新软件顺利安装到系统中。


疑问句:
When will they be inducting the new employees? = 他们什么时候吸收新员工？
Will they be inducting her into the organization? = 他们会让她加入这个组织吗？


否定句:
They did not induct him into the club. = 他们没有让他加入俱乐部。
The company did not induct the new system immediately. = 公司没有立即安装新系统。



PHRASE->
induct into: 引入...；使...就职
induct by: 通过...的方式引入
induct with: 用...引入
induct under: 在...下引入
induct upon: 赋予（权力、职位等）


词源:
From Latin *inducere "to lead in," from in- "in" + ducere "to lead."  The meaning evolved over time to encompass various forms of leading in, including initiation into a group or position.
