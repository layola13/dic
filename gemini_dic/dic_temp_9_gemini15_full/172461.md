pylorus:/ˈpaɪlərəs/| n. |幽门|The pylorus is the opening between the stomach and the duodenum (the first part of the small intestine).  It's a muscular valve that controls the passage of food from the stomach into the small intestine. The pylorus plays a crucial role in digestion by regulating the rate at which chyme (partially digested food) enters the duodenum.  Problems with the pylorus can lead to various digestive issues.|The pylorus was constricted, delaying gastric emptying. 幽门狭窄，延缓了胃排空。

The surgeon carefully examined the pylorus during the operation.  外科医生在手术中仔细检查了幽门。|近义词：gastric outlet, stomach outlet|反义词：None specifically, but terms describing its opposite function (e.g., free passage) could be considered antonyms.|名词
