dalit:/ˈdɑːlɪt/| n.|达利特人（贱民）|A member of the lowest caste in the traditional Hindu social system of India.  The term is now often used as a self-designation, emphasizing agency and resistance to the historical oppression experienced by this group.  The term Dalit translates to "broken" or "scattered" and reflects the social fragmentation and marginalization of this community. |The Dalit community has fought for social justice and equality for many years. 达利特社群多年来一直在争取社会正义和平等。|近义词：untouchable (outdated and offensive), oppressed people|反义词：Brahmin (highest caste)
