resection:/rɪˈsɛkʃən/| n. |切除；切除术；部分切除|The surgical removal of a part of an organ or structure.  Resection is a common surgical procedure used to treat a variety of conditions, including cancer, trauma, and infections.|例句：The surgeon performed a resection of the damaged lung tissue.  外科医生切除了受损的肺组织。|The patient underwent a bowel resection to remove a cancerous tumor. 病人接受了肠切除手术以切除癌性肿瘤。|近义词：excision, removal, ablation|反义词：implantation, addition, augmentation|

词源:
The word "resection" comes from the Latin prefix "re-" (again, back) and the verb "secāre" (to cut).  It literally means "to cut back" or "to cut again".  The surgical meaning developed over time, referring to the cutting away of a part of a body structure.
