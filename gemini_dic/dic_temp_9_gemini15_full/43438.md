crapshoot:/ˈkræpʃuːt/| n. |碰运气的事；孤注一掷；不确定的事|A crapshoot is a situation or undertaking with an unpredictable outcome, heavily reliant on chance or luck.  It often implies a high-risk, high-reward scenario where success is largely a matter of fortune. |例句：Investing in the stock market can be a crapshoot. 投资股票市场可能是一场赌博。

例句：The whole project felt like a crapshoot from the start.  整个项目从一开始就感觉像是一场赌博。


近义词：gamble, risk, long shot, lottery, uncertain venture

反义词：certainty, sure thing, predictable outcome


词源:The term originates from the dice game craps.  The outcome of a craps game is largely determined by chance, mirroring the unpredictable nature of a "crapshoot" in other contexts.  The term's figurative use emerged in the early to mid-20th century.
