inflammableness:/ɪnˌflæməˈbɪlnəsɪz/ | n. | 易燃性；可燃性 | The inflammableness of the material makes it unsuitable for use near open flames.  这种材料的易燃性使其不适合在明火附近使用。 | 近义词：flammability, combustibility | 反义词：non-flammability, incombustibility | 名词


英文解析：Inflammableness refers to the ability of a substance to catch fire easily and burn rapidly.  It is a noun form derived from the adjective "flammable" or "inflammable," both meaning easily ignited and burning.


例句：

The high inflammableness of gasoline requires careful handling and storage.  汽油的高度易燃性需要小心处理和储存。

The building codes strictly regulate the inflammableness of materials used in construction. 建筑规范严格规定了建筑材料的易燃性。


词源：

The word "inflammableness" is derived from the Latin word "inflammare," meaning "to set on fire" or "to inflame."  The prefix "in-" intensifies the meaning, while "-able" signifies capability.  The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective into a noun, indicating a state or quality.
