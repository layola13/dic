marinating:/ˈmærɪneɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 腌制；浸渍 | To soak food in a marinade, typically to tenderize or add flavor.  | 例句：I'm marinating the chicken for tonight's dinner.  我正在腌制今晚晚餐的鸡肉。

例句：We marinated the steak in a red wine sauce for several hours. 我们把牛排浸泡在红酒酱汁里几个小时。


近义词：soaking, pickling, brining | 反义词：drying, roasting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The chef is marinating the fish. = 厨师正在腌制鱼。
They always marinate their tofu before cooking it. = 他们总是在烹饪豆腐之前腌制它。


一般过去时:
She marinated the pork chops in a teriyaki sauce. = 她用照烧酱腌制猪排。

过去完成时:

词源:
From French *mariner* "to pickle, preserve in brine," from Old French *mariner*  (same meaning), from Vulgar Latin *marināre* "to salt," from Latin *mare* "sea."  The original sense relates to preserving food using sea salt.  The modern culinary sense developed later.
