liberalise: /ˈlɪbərəlaɪz/ | vt. | 自由化；放宽管制 | To make something less strict or more liberal.  | The government is trying to liberalise the economy. 政府正在努力使经济自由化。 | 近义词：deregulate, reform, loosen | 反义词：restrict, tighten, control | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government liberalises the market. = 政府正在自由化市场。
They liberalise their immigration policies. = 他们正在放宽移民政策。


一般过去时:
The country liberalised its trade laws. = 这个国家放宽了它的贸易法。
The company liberalised its dress code. = 公司放宽了着装规定。


现在完成时:
The reforms have liberalised the financial sector. = 改革已经使金融部门自由化。
They have liberalised their visa requirements. = 他们已经放宽了签证要求。


过去完成时:
The company had liberalised its hiring practices before the lawsuit. = 在诉讼之前，公司已经放宽了招聘政策。
They had liberalised the media before the revolution. = 他们在革命前已经放宽了媒体管制。


将来时:
The government will liberalise the telecommunications market next year. = 政府明年将开放电信市场。
They will liberalise the education system. = 他们将使教育体制自由化。


虚拟语气:
If they liberalised the laws, more people would invest. = 如果他们放宽法律，更多的人会投资。
It would be better if the government liberalised the economy. = 如果政府能够使经济自由化就好了。


被动语态:
The market was liberalised by the government. = 市场被政府自由化了。
The rules were liberalised last year. = 规则去年被放宽了。


疑问句:
Will the government liberalise the banking sector? = 政府会开放银行业吗？
Has the company liberalised its hiring process? = 公司是否已经放宽了招聘流程？


否定句:
The government did not liberalise the trade restrictions. = 政府没有放宽贸易限制。
The company hasn't liberalised its policies yet. = 公司还没有放宽其政策。


PHRASE->
liberalise the economy = 自由化经济
liberalise trade = 自由化贸易
liberalise immigration laws = 放宽移民法
liberalise the media = 放宽媒体管制
liberalise access to information = 放宽获取信息渠道


词源:
源自liberal (自由主义的) + -ise (动词后缀)  最终源于拉丁语 liberalis (自由的)
