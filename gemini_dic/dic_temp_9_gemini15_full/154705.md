perspectography:/pərˈspektɒɡrəfi/ | n. | 透视画法；透视摄影术 | The technique of creating realistic three-dimensional images on a two-dimensional surface by using the principles of perspective.  It involves the accurate representation of objects and their relative sizes and positions as they appear to the eye from a particular viewpoint. Perspectography can be achieved through drawing, painting, or photography.  | Example: The artist's masterful perspectography made the painting incredibly lifelike.  艺术家精湛的透视画法使这幅画栩栩如生。 | 近义词：perspective drawing, perspective rendering | 反义词：None directly applicable.  A lack of perspectography would result in a distorted or unrealistic image.
