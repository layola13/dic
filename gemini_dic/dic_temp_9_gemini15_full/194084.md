self-idolatry: /ˌsɛlfˈaɪdələtri/ | n. | 自我崇拜；自我陶醉 | The excessive admiration of oneself;  a preoccupation with one's own importance or abilities, often to the exclusion of others and reality.  It can manifest as narcissism, vanity, or egotism. | Example: His self-idolatry prevented him from seeing his flaws and accepting constructive criticism. 他对自己的崇拜让他看不到自己的缺点，也无法接受建设性的批评。 | Near synonyms: narcissism, egotism, vanity, self-obsession, self-absorption | Antonyms: humility, modesty, self-deprecation |  词性:名词

词源:
The word "self-idolatry" is a compound word.  "Self" refers to oneself, and "idolatry" comes from the Greek words "eidōlon" (image, idol) and "-latreia" (worship, service). Therefore, self-idolatry literally means the worship of oneself as an idol.
