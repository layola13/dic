fluttering: /ˈflʌtərɪŋ/ | adj. | 飘动的；颤抖的；激动不安的 |  Describes a light, irregular movement, often of something delicate, like wings or fabric, or a feeling of nervousness or excitement.  | 例句：The butterfly's wings were fluttering gently in the breeze.  蝴蝶的翅膀在微风中轻轻地飘动。

The fluttering of her heart betrayed her nervousness. 她怦怦乱跳的心脏暴露了她的紧张。| 近义词：trembling, quivering, flapping, fluttering | 反义词：still, motionless, calm, steady |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The leaves are fluttering in the wind. = 叶子在风中飘动。
His heart is fluttering with excitement. = 他激动得心跳加速。


一般过去时:
The flag fluttered in the strong wind. = 旗帜在强风中飘扬。
Her eyelids fluttered before she fell asleep. = 她眼皮扑闪了几下然后睡着了。


现在完成时:
The bird has been fluttering around the feeder all morning. = 这只鸟整个上午一直在喂食器周围飞来飞去。
My hands have been fluttering since I heard the news. = 自从听到这个消息以来，我的手一直在颤抖。


过去完成时:
The butterflies had been fluttering around the flowers before the rain started. = 下雨前蝴蝶一直在花朵周围飞舞。
She had been fluttering nervously before the interview. = 面试前她一直紧张地颤抖。


将来时:
The leaves will be fluttering in the autumn breeze. = 秋风中树叶会飘动。
I'm sure my heart will be fluttering when I see her. = 我确信见到她时我的心跳会加速。


虚拟语气:
If the flag were fluttering, it would mean there was a breeze. = 如果旗子飘动，那就意味着有微风。
If my heart weren't fluttering, I wouldn't be so nervous. = 如果我的心跳不加速，我就不会这么紧张。


被动语态:
The flag was fluttered by the wind. = 旗帜被风吹得飘动。
Her nerves were fluttered by the unexpected news. = 她被突如其来的消息弄得心慌意乱。


疑问句:
Is the bird still fluttering? = 鸟还在飞舞吗？
Are your hands still fluttering? = 你的手还在颤抖吗？


否定句:
The leaves weren't fluttering in the calm air. = 在平静的空气中树叶没有飘动。
His heart wasn't fluttering, he was surprisingly calm. = 他一点也不紧张，出奇地冷静。
