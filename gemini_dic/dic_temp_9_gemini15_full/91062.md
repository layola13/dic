hustling: /ˈhʌs(ə)lɪŋ/ | v. | 努力工作；奔忙；欺骗 |

英文解析：Hustling refers to working hard and energetically, often in a busy or competitive environment. It can also imply a less scrupulous approach, involving aggressive or manipulative tactics to achieve success.


例句：

He's been hustling all day to meet the deadline.  = 他为了赶期限，一整天都在努力工作。

She hustled through the airport to catch her flight. = 她匆匆忙忙地穿过机场去赶飞机。


近义词： bustling, striving, scrambling, rushing, pushing


反义词： loafing, idling, slacking, relaxing


词性: 动词


USAGE->

He hustles every day to make ends meet. = 他每天都在努力工作以维持生计。

They hustle clients to secure new business. = 他们积极争取客户以获得新业务。


一般过去时:

She hustled to finish the project before the presentation. = 她在演示文稿之前努力完成项目。

He hustled the deal through despite objections. = 尽管有人反对，他还是强行达成了交易。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish I had hustled more when I was younger. = 我希望我年轻的时候更加努力。


被动语态:

The project was hustled to completion under pressure. = 项目在压力下仓促完成。


疑问句:

否定句:
