despondence: /dɪˈspɒndəns/ | /dɪˈspɑːndəns/ | n. |沮丧，失望 | A state of low spirits caused by loss of hope or courage.  It implies a feeling of dejection and hopelessness, often lasting for a considerable period. |例句：Her despondence was palpable; she slumped in her chair, eyes fixed on the floor.  她的沮丧显而易见；她无力地瘫坐在椅子上，眼睛盯着地板。

例句：After failing the exam, he fell into a state of deep despondence. 考试不及格后，他陷入了深深的沮丧。


近义词：dejection, despair, depression, discouragement, gloom |反义词：elation, euphoria, optimism, hope, cheerfulness |词性:名词
