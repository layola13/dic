contemplators:/ˈkɒntɛmˌpleɪtərz/| n. |沉思者，冥想者 |Those who contemplate; people given to deep thought and reflection.  They spend time considering things deeply and thoughtfully. | The silent contemplators sat cross-legged, lost in their own worlds.  沉思者盘腿而坐，静静地沉浸在自己的世界里。|thinkers, meditators, reflectors|actors, doers


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The contemplators meditate daily. = 沉思者每天冥想。
Many people are contemplators of life's mysteries. = 许多人都在沉思人生的奥秘。

一般过去时:
The ancient philosophers were great contemplators. = 古代哲学家是伟大的沉思者。
The artist spent hours as a contemplator before starting the painting. = 在开始绘画之前，艺术家花了数小时沉思。

现在完成时:
She has been a contemplator of nature for many years. = 多年来她一直是自然界的沉思者。
We have become contemplators of our own mortality. = 我们已经开始思考自身的死亡。

过去完成时:
They had been contemplators of the situation for weeks before making a decision. = 在做出决定之前，他们已经思考这个情况数周了。
The monk had been a contemplator of Buddhist philosophy for decades. = 那位僧侣几十年来一直是佛教哲学的沉思者。

将来时:
The students will be contemplators of the philosophical text. = 学生们将要思考这本哲学文本。
Future generations will be contemplators of our current actions. = 后代将思考我们现在的行为。

虚拟语气:
If I were a contemplator, I would understand better. = 如果我是一个沉思者，我会更好地理解。
I wish I were a better contemplator of my own feelings. = 我希望我能更好地思考自己的感受。

被动语态:
The issue was contemplated by many serious contemplators. = 这个问题被许多严肃的沉思者思考过。
The problem is being contemplated by the experts. = 这个问题正在由专家们思考。


疑问句:

否定句:
He is not a contemplator; he is a man of action. = 他不是沉思者；他是一个行动派。
They were not contemplators of the consequences. = 他们没有思考后果。
