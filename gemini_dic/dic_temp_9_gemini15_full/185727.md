revesture:/rɪˈvɛstʃər/| n. |重新覆盖；再投资；恢复；重新赋予|The act of revesting or being revested; the state of being revested.  It often refers to the regaining of possession or rights, or the act of clothing or covering something again.|例句：The king's revesture of his power after the rebellion solidified his rule. 国王在叛乱后重新获得权力，巩固了他的统治地位。|例句：The revesture of the old building with modern materials gave it a new life. 用现代材料重新覆盖旧建筑物赋予了它新的生命。|近义词：reinstatement, restitution, recouping|反义词：divestment, deprivation

一般现在时:
The company re-invests its profits each year. = 公司每年都将利润进行再投资。
He re-invests his time and energy in his new project. = 他将时间和精力重新投入到他的新项目中。


一般过去时:
The government revested the land to its original owners. = 政府将土地归还给了原来的所有者。
She revested herself in her old role with ease. = 她轻松地重新扮演了她的老角色。

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
