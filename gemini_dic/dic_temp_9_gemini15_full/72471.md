flunkeyisms:/ˈflʌŋkiɪzəm/| n. |谄媚言行；逢迎拍马；奴才习气 | Servile or obsequious behavior or language; the use of excessively flattering or fawning language.  It often implies insincerity and a desire to please those in power, regardless of principle. | Example:  His speech was full of flunkeyisms, praising the leader's every decision without critical thought.  他的演讲充满了谄媚之词，不加批判地赞扬领导的每一个决定。|近义词：servility, obsequiousness, sycophancy, toadying, fawning |反义词：frankness, sincerity, honesty
