outbribing:/ˌaʊtˈbraɪbɪŋ/| v.|贿赂胜过；以贿赂超过|To surpass or exceed someone in bribery.  This word is relatively rare and often used in a humorous or ironic context, implying an excessive level of bribery. |例句：The rival company tried to outbribe us, but we stood firm.  竞争对手试图贿赂我们超过他们，但我们坚持住了。


The mayor was accused of outbribing his predecessor. 市长被指控贿赂超过了他的前任。|近义词：outdo, surpass, exceed (in bribery)|反义词：Underbribe (This is not a real word, but illustrates the opposite concept).|动词
