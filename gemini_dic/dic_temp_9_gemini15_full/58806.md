duograph:/ˈduːəɡræf/ | n. |双图, 双影照片 | A duograph is a picture or image produced by a process that combines two separate photographic images, often to create a three-dimensional or stereoscopic effect.  It can also refer to a type of printing or illustration technique involving two impressions. | Example: The museum displayed a remarkable duograph showcasing a detailed view of the ancient ruins.  博物馆展示了一张非凡的双影照片，详细展现了古代遗址。|近义词：stereograph,  diagraph (less common)|反义词：monograph (single image)
