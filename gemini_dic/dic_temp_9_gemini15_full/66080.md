excarnation:/ˌɛksˈkɑːrˈneɪʃən/| n. |脱肉，遗体处理|The removal of flesh from a body, typically as part of a ritual or burial practice.  This term often appears in discussions of ancient burial rites or fictional scenarios involving extreme body modification.  It contrasts with the more common term "cremation," which involves burning the body. |Example: The ancient tribe practiced excarnation, leaving bodies to decompose naturally before collecting the bones.  古代部落实行脱肉仪式，让尸体自然腐烂后再收集骨头。|近义词:dismemberment (if focusing on the act of removing flesh), mummification (if the flesh is preserved),  decomposition|反义词:embalsming, preservation
