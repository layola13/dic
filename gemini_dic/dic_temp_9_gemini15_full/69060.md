faulter:/ˈfɔːltər/| v.| 犯错；犹豫；蹒跚；衰弱 | To make a mistake or stumble; to hesitate or waver; to move unsteadily; to weaken or decline.  |例句：He faulted in his judgment. 他判断失误了。

The project faltered due to lack of funding.  由于缺乏资金，项目进展缓慢。|近义词：stumble, hesitate, waver, falter, stumble, weaken |反义词：succeed, excel, thrive, strengthen


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often faulters in his speech. = 他说话经常结巴。
The engine faulters under heavy load. = 发动机在重负荷下运转不稳定。

一般过去时:
She faultered in her attempt to climb the mountain. = 她试图攀登那座山时失败了。
The negotiations faltered at the last minute. = 谈判在最后一刻破裂了。

现在完成时:
The economy has faltered in recent months. = 近几个月来经济发展迟缓。
My resolve has faltered several times. = 我的决心动摇了好几次。

过去完成时:
The plan had faltered before they even started. = 他们甚至还没开始计划就失败了。
The runner had faltered several times during the race. = 跑步者在比赛中几次都差点摔倒。

将来时:
I fear the project will falter without more support. = 我担心如果没有更多支持，这个项目将会失败。
The talks are likely to falter if no compromise is reached. = 如果达不成妥协，会谈可能会破裂。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The soldier did not falter in the face of danger. = 面对危险，士兵没有退缩。

词源:
The word "faulter" comes from the Old French word "falterer," meaning "to stammer" or "to stumble."  This ultimately derives from the Proto-Germanic root "*falt-", meaning "to fold" or "to bend," which is related to the concept of something being unsteady or wavering.
