gone-on:/ɡɑːn ˈɒn/ | adj. | 继续的；进行的；沉迷的 |  Describes something that has continued for a period of time, often implying a significant duration or intensity, sometimes with a negative connotation suggesting obsession or excessive involvement.  It can also describe a romantic relationship that has developed. | 例句：The party went on late into the night. = 晚会持续到深夜。 The argument went on for hours. = 争论持续了好几个小时。近义词：continued, ongoing, protracted, engrossed, involved | 反义词：stopped, ceased, ended


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project is going on. = 项目正在进行中。
The meeting is going on now. = 会议现在正在进行。


一般过去时:
The concert went on for two hours. = 音乐会持续了两个小时。
The rain went on all day. = 雨下了一整天。


现在完成时:
The relationship has gone on for five years. = 这段关系已经持续了五年。
This project has gone on longer than expected. = 这个项目持续的时间比预期的要长。


过去完成时:
The party had gone on for hours before he arrived. = 他到达之前，聚会已经持续了好几个小时。
The argument had gone on for days before they finally resolved it. = 他们最终解决这个问题之前，争论已经持续了好几天。


将来时:
The play will go on as scheduled. = 戏剧将按计划进行。
The construction will go on for another month. = 建设工程还将持续一个月。


虚拟语气:
If the project went on much longer, we would be in trouble. = 如果项目持续更长时间，我们会陷入困境。
I wish this headache would go on all day. = 我真希望这个头痛能持续一整天。（反语）


被动语态:
The game was going on when the power went out. = 停电时比赛正在进行。
The discussion was gone on without a conclusion. = 讨论没有结果就结束了。


疑问句:
Is the meeting still going on? = 会议还在进行吗？
How long has the play been going on? = 戏剧已经进行多久了？


否定句:
The music didn't go on too late. = 音乐没有持续到太晚。
The celebration didn't go on as planned. = 慶祝活動没有按计划进行。


PHRASE->
go on and on = 喋喋不休
