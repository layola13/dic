expecting:/ɪkˈspektɪŋ/ | v. | 期待；预期；等待；怀孕 |  Expecting something means believing or knowing that it will happen.  Expecting a baby means a woman is pregnant. | 例句：I'm expecting a phone call from my mother. 我在等妈妈的电话。

例句：She is expecting her first child. 她正怀着她的第一个孩子。


近义词：anticipating, awaiting, looking forward to, pregnant (for expecting a baby) | 反义词：unprepared, surprised, unaware


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I am expecting a parcel today.  = 我今天期待一个包裹。
They are expecting guests for dinner. = 他们期待客人来吃晚饭。


一般过去时:
We were expecting a storm. = 我们预料到一场暴风雨。
She was expecting a promotion. = 她期待升职。


现在完成时:
I have been expecting this for a long time. = 我期待这件事很久了。
They have been expecting a reply. = 他们一直在等待回复。


过去完成时:
I had been expecting him to call, but he didn't. = 我一直在等他打电话，但他没打。
We had expected better results. = 我们原本期待更好的结果。


将来时:
I will be expecting your call tomorrow. = 我明天会等着你的电话。
They will be expecting us at the party. = 他们在派对上等着我们。


虚拟语气:
I would be expecting a miracle if that happened. = 如果那样发生了，我会期待一个奇迹。
If I were expecting guests, I would clean the house. = 如果我期待客人来，我会打扫房子。


被动语态:
The package is expected to arrive tomorrow. = 包裹预计明天到达。
A lot of rain is expected next week. = 下周预计会有很多雨。


疑问句:
Are you expecting a baby? = 你怀孕了吗？
Is she expecting any visitors today? = 她今天期待任何访客吗？


否定句:
I am not expecting any trouble. = 我不期待任何麻烦。
They were not expecting such a large crowd. = 他们没想到会有这么大的阵容。


PHRASE->
be expecting = 期待；怀孕
expectantly = 期待地
expect to = 预期会
expect something of someone = 对某人抱有期望
expect the unexpected = 预料到意外的事情
