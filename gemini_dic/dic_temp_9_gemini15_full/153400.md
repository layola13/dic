peninsulas: /pəˈnɪnsjʊləz/ | n. | 半岛（复数） | A peninsula is a piece of land almost entirely surrounded by water, but connected to a larger landmass.  Peninsulas are formed by geological processes such as tectonic plate movement and erosion.  They can vary greatly in size and shape, from small promontories to large landmasses. | 例句：The Iberian Peninsula is home to Spain and Portugal. 伊比利亚半岛是西班牙和葡萄牙的所在地。

The Korean Peninsula is a strategically important region. 朝鲜半岛是一个具有战略意义的地区。


近义词：promontory, cape | 反义词：island, continent | 词性: noun
