exclaimed:/ɪkˈskleɪmd/| vt.|惊呼；大声呼喊|to cry out or speak suddenly and vehemently.|He exclaimed in surprise when he saw the gift. 他看到礼物时惊讶地惊呼起来。|近义词：cried, shouted, called out|反义词：whispered, murmured


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exclaims with joy every time he sees his son. 他每次看到儿子都会高兴地惊呼。
She exclaims her opinions loudly and often. 她经常大声说出自己的意见。


一般过去时:
He exclaimed with delight at the news. 他听到这个消息高兴地惊呼。
She exclaimed that she was very happy. 她大声说她非常高兴。


现在完成时:
He has exclaimed his love for her many times. 他已经多次表达了他对她的爱。
She has exclaimed against the injustice many times. 她多次抗议这种不公正。


过去完成时:
He had exclaimed in anger before he realized his mistake. 他在意识到自己的错误之前就已经愤怒地喊叫了。
She had exclaimed her disapproval before anyone else spoke. 在其他人说话之前，她就大声表示了反对。


将来时:
He will exclaim in astonishment when he sees it. 当他看到它时，他会惊讶地惊呼。
She will exclaim with frustration if she fails the exam. 如果考试不及格，她会沮丧地大喊。


虚拟语气:
If he had seen the accident, he would have exclaimed in horror. 如果他看到事故，他会惊恐地大喊。
If she knew the truth, she would exclaim in disbelief. 如果她知道真相，她会难以置信地惊呼。


被动语态:

否定句:
He did not exclaim when he saw the snake. 他看到蛇时并没有惊呼。
She didn't exclaim at the unexpected news. 她对这个意外的消息并没有惊呼。
