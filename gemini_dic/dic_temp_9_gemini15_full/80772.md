gossiper:/ˈɡɑːsɪpər/ | n. |说闲话的人，爱搬弄是非的人 | A gossiper is a person who enjoys spreading rumors and talking about other people's private lives.  They often share information that may not be true or is intended to cause harm. | Example: She's a terrible gossiper; I wouldn't trust her with a secret. 她是个爱嚼舌根的人；我不会把秘密告诉她。  |近义词：rumormonger, scandalmonger, talebearer | 反义词：discreet person, private person | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gossips about his neighbours every day. = 他每天都跟人八卦他的邻居。
She gossips with her friends during lunch break. = 她在午休时间和朋友们说闲话。


一般过去时:
They gossiped about the celebrity couple last night. = 他们昨晚八卦那对名人夫妇。
He gossiped to his colleagues about the company's financial problems. = 他向同事们八卦公司的财务问题。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
