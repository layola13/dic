reins:/reɪnz/ | n. |缰绳；控制；统治 | Straps or ropes used to control a horse or other animal; control or guidance; power or authority. |例句：The rider pulled on the reins to stop the horse. 骑手拉紧缰绳以阻止马前进。

例句：She has a firm grip on the reins of power. 她牢牢掌握着权力。


近义词：controls, bridles, governance | 反义词：lack of control, freedom


词源：源自古英语 *ragene，意为“带子，绳索”。


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The rider holds the reins. = 骑手握着缰绳。
He controls the reins of government. = 他掌控着政府的权力。


一般过去时:
The cowboy pulled on the reins. = 牛仔拉紧了缰绳。
She relinquished the reins of the company. = 她放弃了公司的领导权。


现在完成时:
She has held the reins for many years. = 她掌权多年了。
They have tightened the reins on spending. = 他们收紧了开支。


过去完成时:
He had loosened the reins before the race. = 比赛前他已经放松了缰绳。
The manager had already relinquished the reins to his successor. = 经理已经把权力交给了他的继任者。


将来时:
She will take the reins of the project. = 她将接管这个项目。
He will tighten the reins on his employees. = 他将加强对员工的管控。


虚拟语气:
If he had held the reins firmly, the situation would have been different. = 如果他牢牢掌握缰绳，情况就会不同。
I wish I had the reins of power. = 我真希望我掌握权力。


被动语态:
The reins were pulled sharply. = 缰绳被猛地拉了一下。
The reins of power were handed over to the new leader. = 权力交给了新的领导人。


疑问句:

否定句:
