racialization:/ˌreɪʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |种族化；赋予种族特征|The process by which social groups are categorized and ranked by race.  This process is often used to create and maintain social hierarchies and power imbalances.  It's important to note that race is a social construct, not a biological reality, and racialization is a powerful tool used to create and perpetuate inequalities.|Example: The racialization of immigrants often leads to discrimination and marginalization.  种族化移民通常会导致歧视和边缘化。|近义词：ethnicization, racial categorization|反义词：de-racialization
