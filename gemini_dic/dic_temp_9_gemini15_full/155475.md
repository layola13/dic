phenomenologists:/ˌfɪˌnɒmɪˈnɒlədʒɪsts/ | n. |现象学家 | Scholars who study phenomenology, a philosophical movement emphasizing the study of consciousness and experience as it appears to the individual.  Phenomenologists analyze subjective experiences to understand the structures of consciousness and the nature of reality as it is perceived. |  The phenomenologists argued that consciousness is not a passive reflection of reality but an active process of shaping it.  现象学家认为意识不是对现实的被动反映，而是一个主动塑造现实的过程。 |  philosophers, ontologists,  existentialists |  (no direct antonyms; opposing schools of thought include behaviorism, positivism) | 名词
