salinities:/səˈlɪnətiz/ | n. | 盐度 (复数) | The saltiness of something, often referring to the concentration of salts in a solution, especially water.  It's a measure of how salty something is. | Example: The varying salinities of the ocean affect marine life.  海洋不同盐度影响着海洋生物。 The researchers measured the salinities of the different water samples. 研究人员测量了不同水样的盐度。|近义词：salt concentrations, salt levels | 反义词：freshness (lack of salinity)


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The salinity of the Dead Sea is very high. = 死海的盐度很高。
These waters have different salinities. = 这些水域的盐度不同。


一般过去时:
The salinity of the lake was measured yesterday. = 昨天测量了湖水的盐度。
The salinities were significantly higher last year. = 去年盐度明显更高。


现在完成时:
Scientists have studied the salinities of various oceans. = 科学家们已经研究了各个海洋的盐度。
We have observed changes in salinities over time. = 我们已经观察到盐度随时间的变化。


过去完成时:
The researchers had already analyzed the salinities before publishing their findings. = 研究人员在发表研究结果之前就已经分析了盐度。
