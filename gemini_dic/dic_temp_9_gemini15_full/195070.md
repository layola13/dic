semicaptivity:/ˌsɛmɪkæpˈtɪvɪti/| n. |半囚禁状态；部分禁锢| A state of being partly confined or imprisoned;  a condition of limited freedom.  It suggests a situation where someone is not completely free, but not entirely imprisoned either.  The restrictions on their freedom might be less severe than full captivity, perhaps involving some degree of autonomy or limited movement. |The zoo animals, while cared for, lived in a state of semicaptivity. 动物园的动物虽然得到照顾，但生活在半囚禁状态下。| partial confinement, restricted freedom | complete freedom, liberty
