criticist:/ˈkrɪtɪsɪst/| n.|批评家，评论家|A person who judges the merits of literary, artistic, or musical works, or a person who offers judgments of matters of public interest.|The critic praised the novel's innovative plot.|近义词：reviewer, commentator, analyst|反义词：supporter, admirer


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The critic writes insightful reviews. = 批评家撰写富有见地的评论。
He often criticizes the government's policies. = 他经常批评政府的政策。


一般过去时:
The critic reviewed the play last night. = 批评家昨晚评论了这部戏剧。
She harshly criticized his performance. = 她严厉批评了他的表演。


现在完成时:
The critic has reviewed many books this year. = 这位批评家今年已经评论了许多书籍。
He has consistently criticized the lack of funding. = 他一直批评资金不足。


过去完成时:
The critic had already written his review before the play ended. = 在戏剧结束之前，这位批评家已经写好了他的评论。
She had often criticized his work in the past. = 她过去经常批评他的作品。


将来时:
The critic will review the new movie next week. = 批评家下周将评论这部新电影。
He will likely criticize the proposed changes. = 他可能会批评拟议中的变化。


虚拟语气:
If he were a better critic, he would be more objective. = 如果他是一位更好的批评家，他会更客观。
I wish the critic had been kinder in his review. = 我希望这位批评家在评论中能更友善一些。


被动语态:
The movie was critically acclaimed by many critics. = 这部电影受到许多影评家的好评。
His work has been frequently criticized for its lack of originality. = 他的作品因缺乏原创性而经常受到批评。


疑问句:
What did the critic think of the performance? = 批评家对这场演出怎么看？
Who is your favorite critic? = 你最喜欢的评论家是谁？


否定句:
The critic did not enjoy the concert. = 批评家不喜欢这场音乐会。
She did not criticize his work publicly. = 她没有公开批评他的作品。
