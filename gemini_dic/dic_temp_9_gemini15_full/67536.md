extraforaneous:/ˌɛkstrəˈfeɪrɪniəs/| adj. | 外来的；无关的；非本质的 | Extraneous means not forming a vital part of something; irrelevant or unrelated.  It implies something is external, added, or unnecessary. | Example:  The extraneous details in the report obscured the main findings.  报告中无关紧要的细节掩盖了主要发现。


The extraneous noise made it difficult to concentrate.  外界的噪音使人难以集中精力。|近义词：irrelevant, impertinent, immaterial, superfluous, beside the point |反义词：essential, relevant, pertinent, intrinsic, germane

Etymology:
The word "extraforaneous" is derived from the Latin words "extra" (meaning "outside" or "beyond") and "foraneus" (meaning "foreign" or "external").  The combining of these roots creates a word that emphasizes something being outside or beyond the essential nature of something else.
