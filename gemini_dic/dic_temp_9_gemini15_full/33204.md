chromule:/ˈkroʊmjuːl/| n. |色素体；色素粒|A chromule is a small, colored body in a cell, especially a pigment-containing granule in certain algae and plants.  It's often used interchangeably with the term "plastid," but it specifically refers to the colored components. Chromules are responsible for the color of many organisms. |Example: The chromules in the algae gave the water a vibrant green hue.  藻类中的色素体使水呈现出鲜艳的绿色。|近义词:plastid, pigment granule |反义词:none
