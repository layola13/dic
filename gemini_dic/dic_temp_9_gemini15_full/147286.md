overmobilizing: /ˌoʊvərˈmoʊbəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 过度动员；过度调动 |  The act of mobilizing resources or people to an excessive degree, often leading to inefficiency or negative consequences.  It implies an over-allocation of resources or personnel to a task or situation, resulting in potential waste or disruption. | Example: The company's overmobilizing of staff for the small project led to decreased productivity in other areas.  公司为这个小项目过度调动员工，导致其他领域的生产力下降。 | 近义词：overdeploying, overextending, overtasking | 反义词：undermobilizing, understaffing |  动词
