outsprung: /ˌaʊtˈsprʌŋ/ | v. |突然出现；突然跳出；起源于|The word "outsprung" is the past tense and past participle of the verb "outspring," which means to spring or come forth suddenly and unexpectedly. It suggests a forceful and rapid emergence.  The word is less common than its simpler counterparts but adds a sense of dynamism and suddenness. |例句：The tiger outsprung from the bushes, startling the hikers.  老虎突然从灌木丛中跳出来，吓坏了徒步旅行者。


The rabbit outsprung from its burrow when it heard the dog barking. 兔子听到狗叫，就从地洞里窜了出来。|近义词：leaped, sprang, burst forth, emerged |反义词：slowly emerged, crept out
