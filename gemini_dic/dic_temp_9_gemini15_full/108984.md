left-handed: /ˈlɛftˌhændɪd/ | adj. | 左撇子的；用左手的 |  Describing a person who uses their left hand more skillfully than their right hand.  It can also describe things designed for left-handed use. |

例句：

He's left-handed, so he uses a left-handed scissors. 他是左撇子，所以他用左撇子剪刀。

She writes with her left hand; she is left-handed. 她用左手写字；她是左撇子。


近义词：southpaw (informal)

反义词：right-handed


词源:

The word "left-handed" is a compound word formed from "left" and "handed".  "Left" originates from Old English "lyft", meaning "left" (as opposed to right). "Handed" is derived from the Old English "hand", meaning "hand", with the "-ed" suffix indicating an adjective form related to hands or their use.  The combination indicates someone who uses their left hand preferentially.
