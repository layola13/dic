hypochlorite:/ˈhaɪpəklɔːraɪt/| n. |次氯酸盐|A salt of hypochlorous acid (HClO), typically used as a bleaching agent and disinfectant.  Hypochlorites are strong oxidizing agents and are effective in killing bacteria and other microorganisms.  They are commonly found in household bleach.|例句：Sodium hypochlorite is the active ingredient in many household bleaches.  次氯酸钠是许多家用漂白剂中的活性成分。|近义词：bleach, disinfectant|反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "hypochlorite" is derived from the Greek word "hypo-" (meaning "under" or "less than") and "chlorite," which refers to chlorine.  The "-ite" suffix indicates a salt.  Therefore, hypochlorite literally means a salt of an acid containing less oxygen than chloric acid.
