hagiocracy:/ˌhæɡiˈɒkrəsi/| n. |圣人统治；圣徒统治；被圣徒统治的社会|A hagiocracy is a system of government or society ruled or dominated by saints or holy people.  It often implies a society where religious figures hold significant political power, and decisions are made based on religious principles and interpretations.  This can lead to a rigid and inflexible social structure. |The medieval church exerted a strong influence, sometimes bordering on hagiocracy.  中世纪教会施加了强大的影响力，有时近乎于圣人统治。|theocracy, hierocracy|secular government, democracy|名词
