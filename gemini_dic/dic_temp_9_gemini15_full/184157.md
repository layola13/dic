resituated:/ˌriːˈsɪtʃuˌeɪtɪd/| v. |重新安置；重新定位；重新调整|To place or establish something again in a different position or situation.  It implies a more deliberate and planned repositioning than simply moving something.|例句：The company resituated its headquarters to a more central location. 公司将其总部迁至更中心的位置。

例句：The artist resituated the sculpture in the garden to better showcase its form. 艺术家将雕塑重新摆放在花园里，以更好地展示其形态。


近义词：relocate, reposition, rearrange, readjust |反义词：displace (in some contexts) |词性：动词
