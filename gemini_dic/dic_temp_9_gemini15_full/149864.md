WORD ORIGIN->
The word "palfrey" comes from Old French "palefroi," ultimately derived from the Latin "paraveredus," meaning "a sumpter horse" or "a post horse."  The original meaning referred to a horse used for carrying supplies or messages, often for official purposes. Over time, the meaning shifted to emphasize its suitability for riding, particularly for women and those seeking a gentler mount.
