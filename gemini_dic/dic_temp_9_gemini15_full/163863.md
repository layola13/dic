predesignate:/ˌpriːˈdɛzɪɡneɪt/| vt.|预先指定，预先选定|To designate or appoint beforehand.  It implies a selection or assignment made in advance of an event or need.|The committee predesignated the location for the next meeting. 委员会预先指定了下次会议的地点。|The company predesignated its new CEO before the annual meeting. 公司在年会之前预先选定了新的首席执行官。|nominate, appoint, select|  (无明显的反义词，反义词取决于语境，例如：取消指定，可以表达为cancel, revoke)|动词
