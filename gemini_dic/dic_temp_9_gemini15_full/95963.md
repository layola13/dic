inculpable: /ɪnˈkʌlpəbl/ | adj. |无可指责的，无辜的 | Not deserving blame or punishment.  | The evidence clearly shows him to be inculpable.  证据清楚地表明他是无辜的。| blameless, guiltless, innocent | culpable, guilty, blameworthy | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is inculpable in this matter. = 他在这件事上是无辜的。
She is always inculpable in her actions. = 她的行为总是无可指责的。


一般过去时:
The court found him inculpable. = 法院认定他无罪。
They were deemed inculpable after a thorough investigation. = 经过彻底调查后，他们被认为是无辜的。


现在完成时:
The investigation has proven him inculpable. = 调查已经证明他是无辜的。
She has always remained inculpable despite the accusations. = 尽管受到指控，她始终保持着清白。


过去完成时:
It was later discovered that he had been inculpable all along. = 后来发现他一直是无辜的。
They had been inculpable, but the evidence against them was circumstantial. = 他们原本是无辜的，但对他们的证据是间接的。


将来时:
The jury will likely find her inculpable. = 陪审团可能会判定她无罪。
He will remain inculpable, even with new evidence surfacing. = 即使出现新的证据，他仍然是无辜的。


虚拟语气:
If he were inculpable, he would not be in jail. = 如果他是无辜的，他就不会在监狱里。
I wish the judge had realized that he was inculpable. = 我希望法官当时意识到他是无辜的。


被动语态:
He was declared inculpable by the judge. = 他被法官宣布无罪。
The defendant was found to be inculpable. = 被告被发现是无辜的。


疑问句:
Is the suspect truly inculpable? = 嫌疑人真的是无辜的吗？

否定句:
