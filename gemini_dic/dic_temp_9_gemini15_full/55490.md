distracting: /dɪˈstræktɪŋ/ | adj. | 分心的；使人分心的 |  Something that is distracting takes your attention away from what you are trying to do. It can be a person, thing, or situation. |

例句：

The loud music was very distracting. = 吵闹的音乐非常令人分心。

The bright colors on the website were distracting. = 网站上鲜艳的颜色令人分心。


近义词： diverting, diverting, confusing, sidetracking


反义词： focusing, concentrating, absorbing


词源:

The word "distracting" comes from the verb "distract," which has its roots in the Latin word "distrahere," meaning "to draw apart" or "to pull in different directions."  The prefix "dis-" indicates separation or negation, while "trahere" means "to draw" or "to pull." Thus, the original meaning involved drawing something away from its proper course or focus.


用法说明：

distracting通常作形容词使用，修饰名词，表示“使人分心的”。它可以用来描述人、事物或情况。
