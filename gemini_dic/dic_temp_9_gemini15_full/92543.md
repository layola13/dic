hyrcan:/ˈhɜːrkæn/| adj.| 海尔卡尼亚的；（指波斯北部地区）| Hyrcanian refers to Hyrcania, a historical region located on the southern coast of the Caspian Sea, roughly corresponding to parts of modern-day northern Iran and Turkmenistan.  It is often used in literature and poetry to evoke a sense of exoticism, mystery, or wildness associated with the region's historical reputation.  |Example: The poem described a hyrcan tiger, prowling through dense forests. = 这首诗描写了一只海尔卡尼亚虎在茂密的森林中潜行。|近义词：Caspian (in some contexts)|反义词：无|adj.
