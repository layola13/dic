poultice:/ˈpʊltɪs/| n.|药膏，热敷剂|A soft, moist mass of material, typically of herbs and other substances, applied to the body to soothe or heal an injury or inflammation.  A poultice is often used as a topical remedy for various ailments.|Example: The doctor applied a warm poultice to the injured area to reduce swelling. 医生在受伤部位敷上温热的药膏以消肿。|近义词：compress, cataplasm|反义词：None specifically, but terms like "surgery" or "medication" could be considered contrasting approaches.
