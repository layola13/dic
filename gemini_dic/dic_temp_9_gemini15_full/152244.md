patronate:/ˈpeɪtrənət/| n. v.|赞助；保护；庇护；|The act of patronizing; support or protection given by a patron.  It can refer to the patronage itself or the group or individual being patronized.|例句：The arts foundation provides patronage to young artists. 艺术基金会为年轻艺术家提供赞助。|例句：The king took the young scholar under his patronage. 国王将这位年轻学者收为门下。|近义词：sponsorship, protection, support|反义词：opposition, neglect, abandonment|名词，动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The university patronates many student organizations. = 这所大学赞助许多学生组织。
He patronates the local theatre. = 他赞助当地剧院。

一般过去时:
The wealthy merchant patronated the young painter. = 那位富有的商人资助了这位年轻画家。
The government patronated the arts during that period. = 那段时期政府资助艺术事业。


现在完成时:
The family has patronated this charity for many years. = 这个家族多年来一直资助这家慈善机构。
She has patronated several small businesses in the community. = 她已经资助了社区中的几家小企业。


过去完成时:
The artist had been patronated by the royal family for decades. = 这位艺术家几十年来一直受到皇室的资助。
Before the funding cuts, the museum had been patronated by several corporations. = 在经费削减之前，博物馆曾得到几家公司的资助。


将来时:
The company will patronate the upcoming film festival. = 这家公司将赞助即将到来的电影节。
They will patronate a new research project in renewable energy. = 他们将资助一个关于可再生能源的新研究项目。


虚拟语气:
If the company patronated our project, we could succeed. = 如果公司资助我们的项目，我们就能成功。
I wish the government patronated more scientific research. = 我希望政府能资助更多的科学研究。


被动语态:
The young artist was patronated by a wealthy collector. = 这位年轻艺术家受到一位富有的收藏家的资助。
Many local initiatives are patronated by the city council. = 许多地方倡议都得到了市议会的资助。


疑问句:
Does the government patronate any environmental projects? = 政府是否资助任何环保项目？
Who patronated the construction of this building? = 谁资助了这座建筑物的建造？


否定句:
The company did not patronate the controversial artist. = 这家公司没有资助这位有争议的艺术家。
He refused to patronate any organization that discriminated against minorities. = 他拒绝资助任何歧视少数民族的组织。

词源:

Patronate ultimately derives from the Latin word *patronus*, meaning "protector," "patron," or "advocate."  This word entered Middle English via Old French.  The evolution reflects the historical role of patrons in providing support and protection, often to artists and scholars.
