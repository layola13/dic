letter:/ˈlɛtər/ | n. | 信件；字母；文字 | A letter is a written message sent by mail.  |  I received a letter from my grandmother. 我收到奶奶的一封信。 |  missive, epistle, note |  telegram, email | noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He writes a letter every week. 他每周写一封信。
She sends letters to her pen pal. 她给她的笔友写信。

一般过去时:
They wrote a letter to the editor. 他们给编辑写了一封信。
He mailed the letter yesterday. 他昨天寄了这封信。

现在完成时:
I have written several letters this month. 这个月我已经写了几封信。
She has received many letters of support. 她收到了许多支持信。

过去完成时:
He had written the letter before he went to the post office. 他去邮局之前已经写好了信。
She had received the letter before the meeting. 她会议前就收到了信。

将来时:
I will write a letter to my parents. 我将写信给我父母。
They will send the letter tomorrow. 他们明天将寄出这封信。

虚拟语气:
If I had known her address, I would have written her a letter. 如果我知道她的地址，我就会给她写信。
I wish I had written a thank-you letter. 我真希望我写了一封感谢信。

被动语态:
The letter was written by hand. 这封信是手写的。
The letter is being sent by express mail. 这封信正在通过快递邮寄。

疑问句:
Did you receive my letter? 你收到我的信了吗？
What does the letter say? 这封信说了什么？

否定句:
I didn't write a letter. 我没有写信。
She hasn't sent the letter yet. 她还没寄信。
