maculated:/ˈmækjuleɪtɪd/| adj.| 有斑点的；污点的 | Spotted or stained; marked with spots or blotches.  The maculated butterfly was a beautiful sight.  这只斑驳的蝴蝶真漂亮。|近义词：spotted, blotched, speckled, stained |反义词：unspotted, immaculate, clean


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The leopard is maculated. = 豹子身上有斑点。
The shirt is maculated with mud. = 衬衫沾上了泥巴。


一般过去时:
The marble was maculated by age. = 大理石因年代久远而斑驳。
His reputation was maculated by scandal. = 他的名誉因丑闻而受损。


现在完成时:
The wall has been maculated by graffiti. = 墙壁被涂鸦弄脏了。
His character has been maculated by years of dishonesty. = 他的人格因多年的不诚实而受到损害。

否定句:

词源:

From Latin *maculātus, past participle of maculāre "to spot, stain," from macula "spot, stain."  The word ultimately derives from the Proto-Indo-European root *mek- "to wipe, smear."
