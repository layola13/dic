establishes:/ɪˈstæblɪʃɪz/ | vt. | 建立；创立；确立；证实 | To set up (an organization, system, or set of rules) on a firm or permanent basis.  To prove or show something to be true. |

例句：
The company established a new branch in London. = 这家公司在伦敦建立了一个新的分公司。

The research established a link between smoking and lung cancer. = 这项研究证实了吸烟与肺癌之间的联系。


近义词：found, create, institute, confirm, verify

反义词：destroy, abolish, dismantle, refute, disprove

词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government establishes new policies every year. = 政府每年都制定新的政策。
She establishes her credibility through hard work. = 她通过努力工作确立了自己的信誉。

一般过去时:
They established a strong relationship with their clients. = 他们与客户建立了牢固的关系。
He established his innocence in court. = 他在法庭上证明了自己的清白。

现在完成时:
The company has established itself as a leader in the industry. = 这家公司已经确立了自己在行业中的领导地位。
She has established a reputation for excellence. = 她已经建立了卓越的声誉。

过去完成时:
Before the war, they had established a thriving trade network. = 在战争爆发前，他们已经建立了一个繁荣的贸易网络。
By the time he retired, he had established a successful business. = 到他退休的时候，他已经建立了一家成功的企业。

将来时:
They will establish a new research center next year. = 他们明年将建立一个新的研究中心。
The treaty will establish peace between the two countries. = 这项条约将建立两国之间的和平。

虚拟语气:
If they established a better system, they would have fewer problems. = 如果他们建立一个更好的系统，他们就会少遇到一些问题。
I wish they had established clear guidelines earlier. = 我希望他们早些制定明确的指导方针。

被动语态:
The rules were established many years ago. = 这些规则是许多年前制定的。
The company's new policy will be established next month. = 公司的新政策将在下个月实施。

疑问句:
Did they establish the company in 1990? = 他们是在1990年成立这家公司的吗？
Has the research established a causal link? = 这项研究是否已经确定了因果关系？

否定句:
The plan failed because they didn't establish a solid foundation. = 计划失败了，因为他们没有打下坚实的基础。
He couldn't establish his claim due to lack of evidence. = 由于缺乏证据，他无法证明自己的说法。
