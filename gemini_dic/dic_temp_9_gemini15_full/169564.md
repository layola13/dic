prophecy:/ˈprɒfəsi/ /ˈprɑːfəsi/| n. |预言；预示|A prophecy is a prediction of the future, often made by someone who is believed to have special powers or knowledge.  It can refer to a statement or event that foretells future events.|例句：The oracle's prophecy foretold the king's downfall.  先知的预言预示了国王的垮台。|例句：The ancient prophecy spoke of a great hero who would save the world.  古老的预言讲述了一个将拯救世界的伟大英雄。|近义词：prediction, forecast, augury|反义词：reality, fact|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The prophet prophecies the coming storm. = 先知预言即将到来的风暴。
She prophecies great things for the future. = 她预言未来会有伟大的事情发生。


一般过去时:
The soothsayer prophesied the emperor's death. = 占卜者预言了皇帝的死。
He prophesied doom and gloom for the nation. = 他预言了国家的不幸和悲观。


现在完成时:
The seers have prophesied the end of the world many times. = 预言家多次预言过世界末日。
No one has prophesied such a dramatic change. = 没有人预言过如此剧烈的变化。


过去完成时:
They had prophesied rain, but the sun shone brightly. = 他们曾预言过下雨，但阳光灿烂。
The elders had prophesied a time of great prosperity. = 长老们曾预言过一个繁荣昌盛的时代。


将来时:
The fortune teller will prophesy your future. = 算命先生会预言你的未来。
They will prophesy the outcome of the election. = 他们将预言选举的结果。


虚拟语气:
If the prophecy were true, we would be in grave danger. = 如果预言是真的，我们将处于极大的危险之中。
I wish the prophecy had been wrong. = 我希望预言是错的。


被动语态:
The coming of the comet was prophesied centuries ago. = 彗星的到来几个世纪前就被预言了。
The king's death was prophesied by the witch. = 国王的死被女巫预言了。


疑问句:
What did the prophecy foretell? = 预言预示了什么？
Does the prophecy hold true? = 预言是真的吗？


否定句:
The fortune teller could not prophesy the future accurately. = 算命先生无法准确地预言未来。
They did not prophesy the earthquake. = 他们没有预言地震。
