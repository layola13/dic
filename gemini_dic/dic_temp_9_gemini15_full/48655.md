defensibilities: /dɪˌfɛnsəˈbɪlətiz/ | n. | 可防御性；可辩护性 | The qualities or features that make something defensible.  The state or condition of being defensible.  This word refers to the ability to be defended or justified.  It often refers to military strategies, legal arguments, or ethical positions. |


例句：

The defensibilities of the castle were greatly enhanced by the new walls.  = 新建的城墙大大增强了城堡的防御能力。

The lawyer questioned the defensibilities of the prosecution's case. = 律师质疑了检方案件的可辩护性。
