ringworms:/ˈrɪŋwɜːrmz/ | n. | 癣，圆癣 | A contagious fungal infection of the skin, typically appearing as a reddish ring-shaped rash.  It's caused by dermatophytes, a type of fungus that thrives in warm, moist areas.  The rash is often itchy and can spread through direct contact or indirect contact with contaminated objects. Treatment usually involves antifungal creams, lotions, or oral medications. |  My child has ringworm on their arm. = 我的孩子手臂上长了癣。 The athlete developed ringworm after sharing towels in the locker room. = 这位运动员在更衣室共用毛巾后患上了癣。 |  tinea, dermatophytosis |  healthy skin |
