photophily:/foʊˈtɒfɪli/|/ˈfoʊtoʊfɪli/| n. |嗜光性；趋光性|Love of light; the tendency of an organism to move towards light.  This term is primarily used in biology and botany to describe the behavior of organisms, particularly plants, that orient themselves towards a light source.|The sunflower's photophily is evident in its constant turning towards the sun.|近义词：heliotropism|反义词：photophobia
