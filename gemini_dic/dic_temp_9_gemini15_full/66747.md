exorcizing: /ˈɛksɔːrsɑɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 驱魔；去除（坏运气等）| The act of driving out evil spirits or influences.  The process involves rituals and prayers aimed at freeing a person or place from demonic possession or negative forces. |例句：The priest was exorcizing the demon from the possessed man. = 牧师正在为附身的人驱魔。

The shaman spent hours exorcizing the evil spirits from the house. = 萨满花了数小时为房子驱除邪灵。


近义词：banishing, expelling, casting out | 反义词：invoking, summoning

词源:

From the Late Latin *exorcismus, from Greek  exorcizesthai, from exorxizein "to adjure," from ex- "out" + horkos "oath," hence "to adjure solemnly."  The word's evolution reflects its ritualistic and oath-bound nature.
