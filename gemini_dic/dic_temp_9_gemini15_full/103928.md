judger:/ˈdʒʌdʒər/| n.|评判者，裁判员，法官|A person who judges.  Someone who forms opinions or makes judgments, often critically.  This can refer to a formal role, like a judge in a court, or an informal role, like someone who frequently offers opinions and critiques.|例句：The judge delivered the verdict.  法官宣判了。|例句：He's a harsh judger of people's character. 他是一个对人们性格严厉评判的人。|近义词：critic, evaluator, assessor, referee|反义词：supporter, admirer


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He judges people too harshly. = 他对人评价过于苛刻。
She judges the quality of the products. = 她评判产品的质量。

一般过去时:
The judges judged the contestants fairly. = 评委们公平地评判了参赛者。
He judged the distance incorrectly. = 他错误地判断了距离。

现在完成时:
They have judged many competitions. = 他们已经评判过许多比赛。
She has judged him unfairly. = 她对他评价不公。

过去完成时:
The panel had judged the case before the appeal. = 专家组在上诉之前已经审理了此案。
He had judged the situation wrongly. = 他之前已经错误地判断了形势。

将来时:
They will judge the entries next week. = 他们下周将评判参赛作品。
She will judge the winner based on their performance. = 她将根据他们的表现来评判获胜者。

虚拟语气:
If I were a judge, I would be fair. = 如果我是法官，我会公平公正。
I wish I hadn't judged him so quickly. = 我真希望我没有那么快就对他做出评价。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
