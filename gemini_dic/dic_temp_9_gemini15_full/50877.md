desensitizes: /ˌdiːˈsɛnsaˌtaɪzɪz/ | vt. | 使麻木；使不敏感；使脱敏 | To make someone less sensitive to something.  |  例句：Constant exposure to violence desensitizes people to its brutality.  持续的暴力接触会让人们对其残酷性麻木。  | 近义词：numb, deaden, dull | 反义词：sensitize, heighten

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant noise desensitizes her to sound. = 持续的噪音让她对声音变得不敏感。
This medication desensitizes patients to allergies. = 这种药物可以使病人对过敏原脱敏。

一般过去时:
The war desensitized him to suffering. = 那场战争让他对苦难麻木了。
Years of exposure to violence desensitized the community. = 多年的暴力接触使这个社区麻木了。

现在完成时:
The media has desensitized us to the horrors of war. = 媒体让我们对战争的恐怖麻木了。
Repeated exposure to graphic images has desensitized many viewers. = 反复接触血腥图像已经让许多观众麻木了。

过去完成时:
He had desensitized himself to the criticism. = 他已经让自己对批评麻木了。
By then, they had desensitized themselves to the harsh realities of poverty. = 到那时，他们已经对贫困的残酷现实麻木了。

将来时:
The constant bombardment of information will desensitize us to important issues. =  持续的信息轰炸将使我们对重要问题麻木。
This new technology may desensitize future generations to the value of human connection. = 这项新技术可能会使后代对人际交往的价值麻木。

虚拟语气:
If we weren't so desensitized, we would react differently. = 如果我们没有那么麻木，我们会做出不同的反应。
I wish I hadn't become so desensitized to the suffering of others. = 我希望我没有对别人的痛苦变得如此麻木。

被动语态:
The audience was desensitized by the graphic nature of the film. = 观众被电影血腥的画面弄得麻木了。
He felt desensitized by the constant negativity surrounding him. = 他觉得周围持续的消极情绪让他麻木了。

疑问句:
Does constant exposure to violence desensitize people? = 持续的暴力接触会让人们麻木吗？
Has the media desensitized us to the suffering of others? = 媒体让我们对别人的痛苦麻木了吗？

否定句:
The experience didn't desensitize him; it made him more compassionate. = 那次经历并没有让他麻木；反而让他更有同情心了。
She refused to let herself be desensitized by the harsh realities around her. = 她拒绝让自己对周围的残酷现实麻木。


PHRASE->

become desensitized: 变得麻木
desensitized response:  麻木的反应

词源:
The word "desensitize" is formed from the prefix "de-" (meaning "to remove or reverse"), the adjective "sensitive" (referring to the ability to perceive or feel), and the suffix "-ize" (meaning "to make or cause to become").  Therefore, it literally means "to make not sensitive."
