premorally: /ˌpriːˈmɔːrəli/ | adj. | 道德之前的；先于道德的 |  Describes something that exists or occurs before the establishment or influence of a moral code or system. It implies a state of existence where moral considerations are absent or underdeveloped. | Example: The premoral stage of human development is characterized by a lack of understanding of right and wrong. = 人类发展的道德前阶段的特点是不理解对错。 | Near synonyms: primal, primitive, amoral (in some contexts) | Antonyms: moral, ethical, virtuous |  词性:形容词
