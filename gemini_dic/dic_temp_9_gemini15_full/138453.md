novatian:/noʊˈveɪʃən/| n. |诺瓦提安派；诺瓦提安主义者|A follower of Novatian, a 3rd-century Roman presbyter who broke with the Church over the question of readmission of lapsed Christians.  Novatians believed in the unforgivability of certain sins, especially apostasy (abandonment of faith under persecution). They formed a schismatic group, separate from the mainstream Christian Church.|The Novatianist movement challenged the authority of the Church’s hierarchy in matters of forgiveness and penance. |近义词：heretic, schismatic |反义词：orthodox
