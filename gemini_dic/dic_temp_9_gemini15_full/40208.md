contester's:/kənˈtɛstərz/| n. |抗议者，竞争者（的）|The word "contester's" is a possessive form of the noun "contester," indicating belonging or relationship.  A contester is someone who challenges or disputes something, such as an election result or a claim.  The possessive form adds 's to show ownership or connection.  For example, "the contester's argument" refers to the argument made by the contester.|例句：The contester's arguments were compelling.  (抗议者的论点很有说服力。) The election contester's lawyer filed an appeal. (选举抗议者的律师提出了上诉。) |近义词：challenger's, opponent's, disputant's |反义词：supporter's, endorser's
