galenism:/ˈɡeɪlənɪzəm/| n. |盖伦医学；盖伦主义|The medical theories and practices of Galen, a prominent physician in the Roman Empire, emphasizing the balance of humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile) in the body.  Galenism dominated medical thought for centuries and influenced the development of various medical systems.|The physician's treatment was firmly rooted in Galenism, focusing on balancing the four humors.|近义词：humoral medicine|反义词：modern medicine
