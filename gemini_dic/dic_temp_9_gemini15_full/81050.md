grafts:/ɡræfts/ | n. v. | 嫁接；移植；贪污受贿 |

英文解析：
As a noun, "grafts" refers to the act of grafting (joining a cutting or shoot of one plant to another so that they unite and grow together), or the transplantation of living tissue from one part of a body to another, or the transplantation of an organ or tissue from one person to another. It can also refer to pieces of material that are grafted.  As a verb, "grafts" means to perform such a grafting or transplantation.  In a more informal context, "grafts" can also refer to illegal acquisition of money through bribery or corruption.

例句：
The gardener skillfully performed the grafts on the apple trees.  园丁熟练地对苹果树进行了嫁接。

The surgeons successfully completed the kidney graft.  外科医生成功完成了肾移植手术。

He was accused of taking grafts from the construction project. 他被指控从建筑项目中收受贿赂。
