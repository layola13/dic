reforms:/rɪˈfɔːrmz/| n.|改革；改良；改进|The process of improving or amending something, especially a social, political, or economic system.|例句：The government is implementing reforms to the healthcare system. 政府正在对医疗体系进行改革。|例句：The reforms brought about significant improvements in living standards.  这些改革带来了生活水平的显著提高。|近义词：improvements, amendments, changes|反义词：stagnation, deterioration


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government implements reforms regularly. = 政府定期实施改革。
These reforms address critical social issues. = 这些改革解决了重要的社会问题。


一般过去时:
The country underwent significant reforms in the 1990s. = 这个国家在20世纪90年代经历了重大的改革。
They enacted reforms to improve education. = 他们制定了改革以改善教育。


现在完成时:
The company has implemented several reforms this year. = 公司今年已经实施了几项改革。
Significant reforms have been made to the tax system. = 税收制度已经进行了重大改革。


过去完成时:
By the time the new leader arrived, many reforms had already been initiated. = 当新领导人到达时，许多改革已经启动。
They had completed most of the reforms before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期之前完成了大部分改革。


将来时:
The government will introduce further reforms next year. = 政府明年将推出进一步的改革。
The reforms will hopefully lead to greater efficiency. = 这些改革有望提高效率。


虚拟语气:
If the reforms were implemented correctly, the results would be positive. = 如果这些改革实施得当，结果将会是积极的。
I wish the reforms had been introduced sooner. = 我希望这些改革早些推出。


被动语态:
Reforms are being implemented across the country. = 改革正在全国范围内实施。
Many reforms were proposed but never enacted. = 许多改革被提议，但从未实施。


疑问句:
What reforms are needed to address this problem? = 需要什么改革来解决这个问题？
What were the main reforms of the 20th century? = 20 世纪的主要改革是什么？


否定句:
The proposed reforms did not gain enough support. = 提出的改革没有获得足够的支持。
No significant reforms were made to the legal system. = 法律体系没有进行任何重大改革。
