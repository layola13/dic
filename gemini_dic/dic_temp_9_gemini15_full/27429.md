camisard:/ˈkæmɪzɑːrd/| n. |加米萨尔德教徒；卡米萨尔派成员 |A Camisard was a member of a Protestant group in the Cévennes mountains of southern France during the early 18th century, who engaged in guerrilla warfare against the French authorities.  They were known for their religious zeal and often violent resistance to religious persecution. |The Camisards fought bravely against the overwhelming forces of the French army.  加米萨尔德教徒勇敢地对抗法国军队强大的力量。|近义词：Huguenot (though not precisely the same)|反义词：无
