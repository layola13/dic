obscurer:/əbˈskjʊərər/ | v. | 使模糊；使难以理解；遮掩；使黯淡 | To make something difficult to understand or see; to make something less noticeable or important. | The fog obscured the landscape.  浓雾遮蔽了风景。 The complex language obscured the meaning of the poem. 复杂的语言使这首诗的意思难以理解。| clarify, darken | illuminate, clarify, enlighten


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He obscures the truth. = 他隐瞒真相。
The clouds obscure the sun. = 云彩遮蔽了太阳。


一般过去时:
The artist obscured the flaws in his painting. = 艺术家掩盖了他画作中的瑕疵。
The dense fog obscured the road ahead. = 浓雾遮蔽了前方的道路。


现在完成时:
She has obscured the evidence. = 她已经隐瞒了证据。
Years of neglect have obscured the building's original beauty. = 多年的忽视使这座建筑的原始美景蒙尘。


过去完成时:
They had obscured the facts before the investigation began. = 他们在调查开始之前就隐瞒了事实。
The snow had obscured the mountain trails. = 雪已经遮盖了山间小路。


将来时:
The new policy will obscure the company's true financial position. = 新政策将掩盖公司真实的财务状况。
He will obscure the details of the agreement. = 他将隐瞒协议的细节。


虚拟语气:
If he obscured the evidence, he would be in serious trouble. = 如果他隐瞒证据，他将会遇到大麻烦。
I wish he hadn't obscured the truth from me. = 我希望他不要对我隐瞒真相。


被动语态:
The truth was obscured by propaganda. = 真相被宣传所掩盖。
The details of the incident have been obscured. = 事件的细节已被掩盖。


疑问句:
Does the fog obscure your vision? = 雾是否遮挡了你的视线？
Does this complex jargon obscure the message? = 这种复杂的术语是否模糊了信息？


否定句:
The government did not obscure the facts of the case. = 政府没有隐瞒此案的事实。
The writer did not obscure his intentions in the novel. = 作家在小说中并没有隐瞒他的意图。
