self-satirizing: /ˌsɛlfˈsætəˌraɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 自我讽刺的 |  Describes something that uses humor to make fun of itself or its own characteristics, often in a lighthearted or playful way.  It implies a self-aware and often ironic approach to the subject matter. | 例句：The film's self-satirizing humor kept the audience engaged despite the serious underlying themes. 这部电影的自我讽刺式幽默让观众在严肃的潜在主题下依然投入其中。

The novel employs a self-satirizing tone, mocking the clichés of the genre. 这部小说运用自我讽刺的语气，嘲笑该类型小说的陈词滥调。


近义词：self-deprecating, ironic, humorous, witty | 反义词：self-serious, earnest, pompous
