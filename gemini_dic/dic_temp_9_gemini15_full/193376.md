seigneurial:/ˌseɪnˈjʊəriəl/ | adj. | 庄园主的；封建领主的；具有封建领主特权的 | Relating to a seigneur or seigniory; characteristic of feudal lords or their estates.  The word evokes a sense of feudal authority, privilege, and often, a somewhat antiquated or aristocratic system of land ownership. |例句：The seigneurial system in France was gradually dismantled during the Revolution. 法国封建领主制度在革命期间逐渐被废除。

The seigneurial manor house was a magnificent example of 17th-century architecture.  那座庄园主宅邸是17世纪建筑的杰出范例。


近义词：feudal, manorial, lordly | 反义词：democratic, egalitarian


词源: From Old French *seigneurial, from seigneur "lord," from Vulgar Latin *senior "elder," from Latin senior "older."  The suffix "-ial" is of Latin origin, forming adjectives.
