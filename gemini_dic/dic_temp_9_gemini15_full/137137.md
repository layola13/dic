nonsubstantially:/ˌnɒnsəbˈstænʃəli/ | adv. |非实质性地，不重要地 |  Significantly less than substantially; to an insignificant degree.  It describes something that is not significant or important. | The changes to the contract were nonsubstantially different from the original.  合同的修改与原稿相比，差异微乎其微。


The company's losses were nonsubstantially affected by the market downturn. 公司的损失受市场低迷的影响微不足道。| 近义词：immaterially, insignificantly, trivially | 反义词：substantially, significantly, materially | 无


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  It modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.


例句1: He nonsubstantially altered the design, making only minor tweaks. 他对设计进行了微小的改动，只做了一些细微的调整。
例句2: The new policy nonsubstantially impacted employee morale. 新政策对员工士气的影响微乎其微。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "nonsubstantially".  The word is used independently to modify other parts of a sentence.
