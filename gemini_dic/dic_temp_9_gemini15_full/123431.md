monopolist:/məˈnɒpəlɪst/ | n. | 垄断者；独占者 | A monopolist is a person or company that has complete control of the supply of a particular good or service.  They can set prices and control the market without significant competition. | Example: The company was accused of being a monopolist in the software market. 这家公司被指控在软件市场上拥有垄断地位。|近义词：monopoly holder, trust, cartel | 反义词：competitor


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The monopolist controls the market. = 垄断者控制着市场。
He monopolizes the conversation. = 他垄断了谈话。


一般过去时:
The monopolist set high prices last year. = 垄断者去年设定了高价。
She monopolized the attention of the audience. = 她吸引了观众的所有注意力。


现在完成时:
The monopolist has stifled competition. = 垄断者扼杀了竞争。
He has monopolized the best seats. = 他独占了最好的座位。


过去完成时:
The monopolist had already gained control before the investigation began. = 在调查开始之前，垄断者已经获得了控制权。
They had monopolized the market for years. = 他们垄断了市场多年。


将来时:
The monopolist will likely face legal challenges. = 垄断者可能会面临法律挑战。
She will monopolize the stage during her performance. = 她在表演期间将占据舞台中心。


虚拟语气:
If he were a monopolist, he would set exorbitant prices. = 如果他是垄断者，他会设定过高的价格。
I wish the monopolist wouldn't control the market so tightly. = 我希望垄断者不要如此严密地控制市场。


被动语态:
The market was monopolized by a single company. = 市场被一家公司垄断。
The resources were monopolized by the powerful. = 资源被权势者垄断。


疑问句:
Is he a monopolist? = 他是垄断者吗？
Does the company have a monopolistic position? = 这家公司拥有垄断地位吗？


否定句:
He is not a monopolist. = 他不是垄断者。
The company does not have a monopoly. = 这家公司没有垄断地位。
