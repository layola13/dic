pedantically:/pɛˈdæntɪkli/| adv.|卖弄学问地，迂腐地，学究气地|In a manner characterized by a display of learning or scholarship, often in a tedious or ostentatious way.  It implies excessive attention to detail and rules, often to the point of being irritating. |例句：He pedantically corrected every grammatical error in my essay. 他卖弄学问地纠正了我论文中的每一个语法错误。

例句：She pedantically explained the intricacies of the tax code. 她迂腐地解释了税法的复杂之处。


近义词：ostentatiously, meticulously, precisely, formally,  fussy


反义词：casually, informally, loosely, roughly


词源：源自pedant (n.) “炫耀学问的人”，最终源自意大利语pedante,可能源自希腊语pais “男孩”，指在学校学习的男孩。  后缀-ically 表示“以…的方式”。
