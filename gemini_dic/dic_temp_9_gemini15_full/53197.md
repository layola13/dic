dipterous:/ˈdɪptərəs/| adj.|双翅目的；双翅类的|Having two wings.  The term is used to describe insects of the order Diptera, which includes flies, mosquitoes, and gnats. These insects are characterized by having only one pair of functional wings, with the hindwings reduced to halteres (small, club-shaped balancing organs).|Example:  Many dipterous insects are important pollinators. 许多双翅目昆虫是重要的传粉者。  The dipterous larvae were found in the decaying matter. 在腐烂的物质中发现了双翅目幼虫。|近义词：two-winged|反义词：four-winged (referring to insects with four functional wings)|


Etymology:  From Latin *dipterus, from di- "two" + pteron "wing".
