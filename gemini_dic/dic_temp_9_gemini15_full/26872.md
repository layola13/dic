cajun:/ˈkeɪdʒən/| adj. n.|卡津的；卡津人|Of or relating to the Cajuns or their culture.  Cajun refers to the descendants of Acadians who were expelled from Acadia (now Nova Scotia, Canada) in the 18th century and settled in Louisiana.  The term can describe their language, cuisine, music, and culture. |例句：The restaurant serves authentic Cajun cuisine. 这家餐馆供应正宗的卡津菜。

例句：She's proud of her Cajun heritage. 她为自己的卡津血统而自豪。


近义词：Acadian (though not always interchangeable, as "Acadian" refers to the broader group and "Cajun" specifically to those in Louisiana)


反义词：None directly applicable.  The antonym would depend on the context (e.g., "non-Cajun" cuisine).


词性: Adjective, Noun
