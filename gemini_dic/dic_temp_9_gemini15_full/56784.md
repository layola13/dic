dopper:/ˈdɒpə(r)/| n. |多普勒,多普勒效应|A doppler is a device that uses the Doppler effect to measure the speed of moving objects, such as blood flow or weather patterns.  It's also used as a shortened form of Doppler radar or Doppler ultrasound.|Example: The police used a doppler to measure the speed of the car.|近义词: Doppler radar, Doppler ultrasound|反义词: N/A
