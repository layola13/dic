radioligand: /ˌreɪdiəʊˈlɪɡænd/ | n. |放射性配体| A ligand that has been labeled with a radioactive isotope.  It is used in various biological assays to study the binding of molecules to receptors or other target sites.  Radioligands allow researchers to quantify and visualize the interactions between ligands and their targets. | Example: Researchers used a radioligand binding assay to determine the affinity of a drug candidate for its target receptor.  研究人员使用放射性配体结合试验来确定候选药物与其靶受体的亲和力。|近义词:labeled ligand, radioactive tracer|反义词:  None specifically, but could be contrasted with a non-radioactive ligand.
