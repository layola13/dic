chiding:/ˈtʃaɪdɪŋ/| n. |责备；斥责；训斥 | The act of scolding or rebuking someone.  It often implies a gentle or mild reprimand, rather than a harsh punishment. |例句：Her chiding was gentle but firm.  她的责备温和而坚定。

例句：The teacher's chiding had a positive effect on the student's behavior. 老师的训斥对学生的表现产生了积极的影响。


近义词：reprimand, rebuke, scolding, admonishment, reproof |反义词：praise, commendation, compliment |词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher is chiding the student for being late. = 老师因为学生迟到而责备他。
His constant chiding is wearing me down. = 他不断的责备让我受不了。


一般过去时:
My mother chided me for breaking the vase. = 我妈妈因为我打破花瓶而责备我。
She chided him gently for his carelessness. = 她温和地责备了他的粗心大意。


现在完成时:
She has chided him many times for his bad habits. = 她已经多次因为他的坏习惯而责备他了。
Have you ever been chided by your boss? = 你曾经被老板责备过吗？


过去完成时:
He had chided her before, but she ignored him. = 他之前已经责备过她了，但她不理他。
I had been chided for my mistakes before I learned my lesson. = 我在吸取教训之前就已经因为我的错误被责备过了。


将来时:
I will chide him if he does it again. = 如果他再这样做，我会责备他。
She will likely chide him for his lateness. = 她很可能会因为他的迟到而责备他。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't chide him so harshly. = 如果我是你，我不会这么严厉地责备他。
I wish I hadn't chided her so severely. = 我真希望我没有那么严厉地责备她。


被动语态:
He was chided for his poor performance. = 他因为表现不佳而受到责备。
The child was often chided for his mischievous acts. = 这个孩子经常因为恶作剧而受到责备。


疑问句:
Was he chided for his mistake? = 他因为他的错误而受到责备了吗？
Will she be chided for her behaviour? = 她会因为她的行为而受到责备吗？


否定句:
He wasn't chided for what he did. = 他没有因为他的所作所为而受到责备。
She wasn't chided, she was praised. = 她没有受到责备，而是受到了表扬。

词源:
From Middle English *chiden, from Old English *cīdan ("to chide, scold, rebuke").  The word's history reflects a long-standing use in the Germanic languages to describe vocal expressions of disapproval.
