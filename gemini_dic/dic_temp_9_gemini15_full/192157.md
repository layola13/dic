scrambling: /ˈskræmblɪŋ/ | v. |  scrambling |  scrambling |  争先恐后；爬行；搅乱；（无线电信号）互相干扰 |

英文解析:To scramble means to move quickly and clumsily, often in a hurried or disorganized way. It can also refer to the act of climbing or scrambling over something, or to the mixing or scrambling of things together. In the context of radio signals, it means to interfere with each other.

例句:
The children were scrambling over the rocks. = 孩子们在岩石上攀爬。

The climbers were scrambling up the steep mountainside. = 登山者正在陡峭的山坡上攀爬。


近义词: scramble, clamber, climb, scramble, jumble, mix, confuse

反义词: organize, arrange, sort, clarify
