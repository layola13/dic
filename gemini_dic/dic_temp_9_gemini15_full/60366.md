egret:/ˈiːɡrɛt/| n. |白鹭|A white heron, especially one of several species of the genus *Egretta*, characterized by long legs and neck, and often by plumes during the breeding season.  Egret refers to various species of wading birds in the family Ardeidae.  They are known for their elegant appearance and graceful movements.|例句：An egret stood motionless in the shallows, patiently waiting for a fish.  一只白鹭一动不动地站在浅滩上，耐心地等待着鱼儿。

例句：Several snowy egrets were fishing in the rice paddy. 几只雪鹭在稻田里捕鱼。


近义词：heron, crane |反义词：无


词源:  The word "egret" comes from the Old French word *aigrette*, meaning "egret" or "plume". This, in turn, is probably of imitative origin, referring to the bird's cry.  The word entered English in the 16th century.
