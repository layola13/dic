re-binding:/ˌriːˈbaɪndɪŋ/ | n. |重新装订；重新绑定 | The act of binding something again, typically a book or document.  This often involves replacing a worn-out cover or binding, or repairing damage to the existing binding. | Example: The library offered re-binding services for damaged books. = 图书馆提供受损书籍的重新装订服务。 The re-binding of the ancient manuscript was a painstaking process. = 这本古代手稿的重新装订是一个费力的过程。|近义词：rebinding, repair, restoration |反义词：unbinding, discarding |名词
