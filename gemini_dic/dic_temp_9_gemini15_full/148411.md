overstaying: /ˌoʊvərˈsteɪɪŋ/ | v. | 超期逗留；逾期居留 |  To remain in a place beyond the permitted or expected time.  |例句：He is facing deportation for overstaying his visa. 他因为签证逾期而面临驱逐出境。

例句：  The tourists were fined for overstaying their welcome at the hotel.  游客因在酒店逗留时间过长而被罚款。


近义词：outstaying, remaining beyond the allotted time


反义词：leaving on time, departing as scheduled


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He overstays his welcome at parties. = 他在派对上总是待得太久。
They often overstay their visas. = 他们经常逾期居留。

一般过去时:
We overstayed at the restaurant last night. = 我们昨晚在餐馆待的时间太长了。
She overstayed her visa and was fined. = 她签证逾期并被罚款。

现在完成时:
He has overstayed his welcome again. = 他又一次待得太久了。
They have overstayed their visa by several months. = 他们的签证已经逾期好几个月了。

过去完成时:
I realized I had overstayed my welcome when everyone started yawning. = 当每个人都开始打哈欠的时候，我意识到我待的时间太长了。
The guests had overstayed, leaving a mess in the living room. = 客人们待的时间太长了，把客厅弄得乱七八糟。

将来时:
I think we will overstay if we don't leave now. = 如果我们现在不走，我想我们会待得太久。
They will likely overstay their visa unless they renew it. = 除非他们续签，否则他们很可能会逾期居留。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't overstayed, I wouldn't have missed the train. = 如果我没有待得太久，我就不会错过火车了。
I wish I hadn't overstayed at the party. = 我真希望我没有在派对上待得太久。

被动语态:
The visa was overstayed by many tourists. = 许多游客都逾期居留了。 (This is less common usage)
His welcome was definitely overstayed. = 他的确待得太久了。


疑问句:

否定句:

He didn't overstay his visa this time. = 他这次没有逾期居留。


PHRASE->
