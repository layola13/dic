octene:/ˈɒkˌtiːn/| n. |辛烯|Octene refers to any alkene with eight carbon atoms.  It is an unsaturated hydrocarbon with a double bond.  Different isomers exist depending on the position of the double bond.  Octenes are colorless liquids with a characteristic hydrocarbon odor. They are used in various industrial applications as intermediates in the synthesis of other chemicals and as components in fuels and lubricants. |Example:  Several isomers of octene are used in the production of plastics.  几种辛烯异构体用于塑料生产。|近义词:octylene (less common)|反义词:None specifically, but saturated hydrocarbons like octane are chemically different.
