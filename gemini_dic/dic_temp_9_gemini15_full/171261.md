pterodactyl:/ˈtɛrədæktɪl/| n. |翼手龙|A flying reptile of the extinct order Pterosauria, characterized by a long neck, a relatively small body, and a long pointed head with sharp teeth.  Pterodactyls lived during the Mesozoic Era, particularly the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods.  They varied greatly in size, with some species having wingspans of only a few feet, while others had wingspans of up to 30 feet.|例句：The museum displayed a remarkably preserved fossil of a pterodactyl. 博物馆展出了一块保存完好的翼手龙化石。

The discovery of the pterodactyl fossil shed light on the evolution of flight. 翼手龙化石的发现揭示了飞行进化的奥秘。|近义词：pterosaur|反义词：None
