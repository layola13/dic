pterodactylids:/ˌtɛrəˈdæktɪlɪdz/| n. |翼手龙科动物|The pterodactylids were a diverse group of pterosaurs that lived during the Late Jurassic and Early Cretaceous periods. They were characterized by their long necks, large heads, and relatively short tails.  Many species possessed toothless beaks. |例句：The fossil evidence suggests a wide geographic distribution for various pterodactylid species.  化石证据表明各种翼手龙科动物具有广泛的地理分布。

The discovery of a new pterodactylid species in China expanded our understanding of pterosaur evolution.在中国发现的一个新的翼手龙科物种扩展了我们对翼龙进化史的理解。|近义词：pterosaurs (更广泛的类别)|反义词：无
