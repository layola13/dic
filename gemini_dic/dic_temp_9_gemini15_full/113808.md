mad:/mæd/ | adj. | 疯狂的；生气的；狂热的 | Extremely angry; mentally ill; enthusiastic or passionate.  |例句：He went mad after his wife died. 他妻子死后就疯了。 |例句：She was mad at him for being late. 她因为他迟到而生气。 |近义词：insane, angry, furious, crazy, enraged |反义词：calm, sane, rational, peaceful


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is mad at me. = 他生我的气。
She is mad about dogs. = 她非常喜欢狗。


一般过去时:
He was mad when he heard the news. = 他听到这个消息后很生气。
They were mad with joy. = 他们欣喜若狂。


现在完成时:
I have been mad at him for weeks. = 我已经生他几周的气了。
She has gone mad. = 她疯了。


过去完成时:
I had been mad at him before he apologized. = 他道歉之前我一直在生他的气。
He had gone completely mad. = 他已经完全疯了。


将来时:
I will be mad if he does that again. = 如果他再那样做，我会生气的。
She will probably go mad if she finds out. = 如果她发现的话，她可能会疯掉。


虚拟语气:
If I were mad, I would scream. = 如果我疯了，我会尖叫。
I wish I weren't so mad at him. = 我真希望我不那么生他的气。


被动语态:
(Mad is rarely used in the passive voice.  A better choice of word would be needed depending on the context.)


疑问句:
Are you mad at me? = 你生我的气吗？
Is he mad? = 他疯了吗？


否定句:
I am not mad at you. = 我没有生你的气。
He isn't mad, he's just stressed. = 他没疯，只是压力过大。


PHRASE->
go mad = 发疯
mad as a hatter = 疯疯癫癫的
drive someone mad = 逼疯某人
like mad = 疯狂地
mad with = 因...而发狂


词源:
The word "mad" ultimately derives from the Proto-Germanic word *gamōþiz, meaning "angry, furious."  This evolved through Old English *mǣd, and later Middle English *mad. The meaning broadened over time to include mental illness.
