complemental: /ˌkɒmplɪˈmɛntəl/ | /ˌkɑːmplɪˈmɛntəl/ | adj. | 补充的；互补的；完成的 |

英文解析: Serving to complete or enhance something; supplementary.  Often used to describe something that completes or enhances another thing, making them work together effectively.  It implies a relationship where each part is necessary for the whole.

例句:
The two departments have a complemental relationship, each supporting the other's work.  = 这两个部门的关系是互补的，每个部门都支持另一个部门的工作。

The new software provides complemental features to the existing system. = 新软件为现有系统提供了补充功能。


近义词: supplementary, complementary, additional, ancillary

反义词: contradictory, conflicting, opposing


词性: adjective


USAGE->  (This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms in common usage; therefore, tense examples are not applicable)


PHRASE->
complementary angles: 互余角
complementary colors: 互补色
complementary medicine: 补充医学
complementary skills: 互补技能
complementary goods: 互补品


词源:  The word "complemental" derives from the Latin word "complementum," meaning "something that completes."  It entered English through the French word "complément."  The suffix "-al" transforms the noun into an adjective.
