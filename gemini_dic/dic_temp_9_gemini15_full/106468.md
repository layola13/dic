kowtow:/ˈkaʊtaʊ/| vt. & n.|叩头；卑躬屈膝； |To kneel and touch one's forehead to the ground as a sign of respect or submission, especially in former China.  This act often implies excessive subservience.| 例句：He kowtowed to the emperor. 他向皇帝叩头。 They kowtowed to the demands of the market. 他们屈服于市场的需求。|近义词：prostrate, prostrate oneself, bow down, cringe, fawn |反义词：defy, resist, stand up to


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He kowtows to his boss. = 他对他的老板卑躬屈膝。
They kowtow to the powerful. = 他们对权势者卑躬屈膝。

一般过去时:
She kowtowed to the pressure. = 她屈服于压力。
The company kowtowed to its shareholders. = 公司对股东们卑躬屈膝。

现在完成时:
He has kowtowed to every whim of his client. = 他一直对客户的每一个怪癖都唯命是从。
They have kowtowed to the demands of the market for years. = 他们多年来一直屈服于市场的需求。

过去完成时:
He had kowtowed to his father's wishes his whole life. = 他一生都顺从他父亲的意愿。
She had kowtowed to the authority figures throughout her career. = 她在整个职业生涯中都对权威人士卑躬屈膝。

将来时:
They will kowtow to the new regulations. = 他们将屈服于新的规章制度。
He will kowtow to anyone with money. = 他会对任何有钱人都卑躬屈膝。

虚拟语气:
If he wanted the promotion, he would kowtow to his superiors. = 如果他想要晋升，他会对上司卑躬屈膝。
I wish I hadn't kowtowed to their demands. = 我真希望我没有屈服于他们的要求。

被动语态:
His demands were kowtowed to. = 他的要求得到了满足（被屈服）。
The rules were kowtowed to without question. = 规则被毫无疑问地遵守（被屈服）。

疑问句:
Will they kowtow to the pressure? = 他们会屈服于压力吗？
Does he often kowtow to his superiors? = 他经常对上司卑躬屈膝吗？

否定句:
He did not kowtow to anyone. = 他不向任何人卑躬屈膝。
She refused to kowtow to the pressure. = 她拒绝屈服于压力。


PHRASE->
kowtow to someone = 对某人卑躬屈膝
kowtow to pressure = 屈服于压力
kowtow to authority = 屈服于权威
kowtow to the demands = 屈服于要求
kowtow to the market = 屈服于市场


词源:
The word "kowtow" comes from the Cantonese term  "gāutàu," which itself is an onomatopoeic word mimicking the sound of the head hitting the ground during the act of prostration.  The word entered English in the 19th century through contact with China.
