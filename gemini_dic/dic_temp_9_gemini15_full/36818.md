colonializes:/kəˈloʊniˌaɪzɪz/ | vt. |殖民化；使殖民地化 | To establish control over (a place or people) and impose one's culture, language, and institutions on them.  This often involves exploitation and suppression of the indigenous population. |例句：European powers colonialized much of Africa and Asia during the 19th century. = 19世纪，欧洲列强殖民了非洲和亚洲的大部分地区。

例句：The company's marketing strategy colonializes local traditions for profit. = 该公司的营销策略为了利润而殖民化了当地传统。


近义词：conquer, subjugate, dominate, exploit, annex

反义词：decolonize, liberate, emancipate


USAGE->

The government colonializes new territories. = 政府正在殖民新的领土。

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

The indigenous people had already been colonialized before the arrival of the Europeans. = 在欧洲人到来之前，土著人民已经被殖民化了。

虚拟语气:
