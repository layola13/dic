long-drawn-out: /ˌlɒŋˈdrɔːnˌaʊt/ /ˌlɔːŋˈdrɔːnˌaʊt/ | adj. |冗长的，漫长的，拖沓的 |  Describes something that takes a long time and is often tedious or boring. It implies unnecessary length and delay. |例句：The meeting was long-drawn-out and unproductive.  会议冗长且毫无成效。

The negotiation process was long-drawn-out, lasting several months.  谈判过程冗长，持续了几个月。


近义词：lengthy, protracted, extended, tedious, wearisome | 反义词：brief, short, concise, succinct


词源:  The word is a compound adjective formed from "long," "drawn," and "out."  "Drawn out" originally meant "extended" or "prolonged,"  and adding "long" emphasizes the excessive length.  The etymology of "drawn" here relates to its sense of being stretched or extended in time.
