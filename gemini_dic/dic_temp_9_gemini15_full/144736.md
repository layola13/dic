over-annotated:/ˌoʊvərˈænəˌteɪtɪd/ | adj. | 过度注释的 |  Containing excessive or unnecessary annotations; having more notes or explanations than are needed or helpful.  The essay was over-annotated, making it difficult to focus on the main points. | 近义词：over-explained, excessively commented, overloaded with notes | 反义词：under-annotated, sparsely annotated


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "what".  Therefore, tense forms are not applicable.  Examples will focus on how the adjective is used in different sentence structures.


Declarative Sentences:
The document was over-annotated; it was hard to read. = 这份文件注释过多，难以阅读。
The professor's lecture notes were over-annotated, distracting students. = 教授的讲义注释过多，分散了学生的注意力。


Interrogative Sentences:
Is the textbook over-annotated for beginners? = 这本教科书对初学者来说注释是否过多？
Were the student's papers over-annotated by the teacher? =  学生的论文是否被老师过度注释了？


Negative Sentences:
The manuscript was not over-annotated; it was perfectly clear. = 手稿注释并不多，非常清晰。
The research paper was not over-annotated, making it easy to understand. = 研究论文注释不多，很容易理解。


PHRASE->

"dangerously over-annotated" = 注释过多到令人费解的程度
"pointlessly over-annotated" = 无意义地注释过多


Word Origin:
The word is a combination of "over-" (meaning excessively or to a greater extent than is necessary) and "annotated" (meaning to add notes to a text).  "Annotate" comes from the Latin "annotare," meaning "to note."
