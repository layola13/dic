/proʊˌroʊmænˈtɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 前浪漫主义 |  Proromanticism refers to the intellectual and artistic movements and styles that preceded and influenced Romanticism. It encompasses a variety of trends in the late 18th century, characterized by a shift away from Enlightenment rationalism towards a greater emphasis on emotion, intuition, and the individual.  It's a transitional period, laying the groundwork for the full flowering of Romanticism in the early 19th century.  | The Sturm und Drang movement in German literature is a key example of proromanticism.  |  Pre-romanticism, Pre-romantic  |  Neoclassicism, Enlightenment rationalism | 名词
