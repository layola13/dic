capable:/ˈkeɪpəbl/ | adj. | 有能力的；能够的；胜任的 | Having the ability or qualities necessary to do something.  | 例句：She is capable of great things. 她能够成就大事。 | 例句：He is a capable manager. 他是一位能干的经理。 | 近义词：competent, able, proficient | 反义词：incapable, incompetent, unable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is capable of finishing the project on time. = 他有能力按时完成项目。
She is capable of speaking three languages. = 她能说三种语言。

一般过去时:
They were capable of handling the crisis effectively. = 他们有能力有效地处理危机。
He was capable of lifting heavy weights. = 他能够举起重物。

现在完成时:
She has been capable of managing large teams for years. = 多年来她一直能够管理大型团队。
We have proven capable of adapting to change. = 我们已经证明自己能够适应变化。

过去完成时:
They had been capable of producing high-quality products before the merger. = 合并之前，他们一直能够生产高质量的产品。
He had always been capable of independent thinking. = 他一直都能够独立思考。

将来时:
She will be capable of driving after she passes her test. = 她通过考试后就能开车了。
They will be capable of handling the increased workload. = 他们将能够处理增加的工作量。

虚拟语气:
If she were capable of it, she would have helped. = 如果她能够做到，她就会帮忙了。
If he had been capable, he would have finished the task earlier. = 如果他有能力，他就会早点完成任务了。

被动语态:
The task is capable of being completed quickly. = 这项任务能够快速完成。
The problem is capable of being solved easily. = 这个问题很容易解决。

疑问句:
Is she capable of doing the job? = 她能胜任这份工作吗？
Are they capable of understanding complex instructions? = 他们能够理解复杂的指令吗？

否定句:
He is not capable of understanding abstract concepts. = 他无法理解抽象概念。
She was not capable of completing the marathon. = 她无法完成马拉松比赛。


PHRASE->
be capable of: 有能力做…
highly capable: 高度胜任的
more than capable:绰绰有余
quite capable:相当有能力的
incapable of: 不能胜任…
