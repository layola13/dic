penny-pincher: /ˈpɛniˌpɪntʃər/ | n. | 吝啬鬼，小气鬼 | A person who is unwilling to spend money; someone who is excessively frugal.  They are often seen as being overly cautious with their finances, sometimes to the point of being miserly. |  He's such a penny-pincher; he wouldn't even buy a new pair of shoes when his old ones were falling apart. 他真是个吝啬鬼，旧鞋都破了还不舍得买双新的。 |近义词：miser, scrooge, tightwad, cheapskate |反义词：spendthrift, prodigal, generous

词源:
The word "penny-pincher" is a compound word.  "Penny" refers to a small unit of currency, and "pincher" refers to someone who tightly holds onto something, in this case, their money.  The term likely emerged in the late 19th or early 20th century as a descriptive metaphor for someone excessively frugal.
