prezygapophysis:/ˌpriːziɡæˈpɒfɪsɪs/| n. |前关节突|The prezygapophysis is a bony process projecting from the anterior (front) part of a vertebra that articulates with the postzygapophysis of the vertebra above.  It forms a joint that helps to stabilize the vertebral column and limit its range of motion. |例句：The prezygapophysis of the lumbar vertebra is significantly larger than that of the cervical vertebra.  腰椎的前关节突比颈椎的前关节突大得多。|近义词：anterior articular process |反义词：postzygapophysis
