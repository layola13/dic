euhemerist:/ˈjuːhiːmərɪst/| n. |尤希默主义者|A person who interprets myths and legends as accounts of historical events, often attributing supernatural elements to the exaggeration or misrepresentation of real occurrences.  They believe that gods and heroes were originally real people whose actions were later embellished and mythologized. |Example:  Many euhemerists believe the Greek gods were based on powerful rulers or figures from early Greek history.  许多尤希默主义者认为希腊诸神是基于早期希腊历史上的强大统治者或人物。|近义词：rationalist (in this context), historical interpreter |反义词：mythologist (in the sense of believing myths are purely fictional), supernaturalist
