phthisipneumonia: /ˌfθɪsɪpnuˈmoʊniə/ | n. |肺结核性肺炎|  A severe lung infection combining tuberculosis and pneumonia.  It's a rare and potentially fatal condition characterized by the simultaneous presence of both Mycobacterium tuberculosis (causing tuberculosis) and pneumonia-causing bacteria or viruses.  The symptoms are a combination of those seen in tuberculosis and pneumonia, including cough, fever, shortness of breath, chest pain, and weight loss. |例句：The patient presented with symptoms suggestive of phthisipneumonia, necessitating immediate hospitalization and aggressive treatment.  患者出现疑似肺结核性肺炎的症状，需要立即住院和积极治疗。


The term is a combination of "phthisis" (consumption, an older term for tuberculosis) and "pneumonia".|近义词：tuberculous pneumonia, severe lung infection |反义词： (There isn't a direct antonym, but words suggesting lung health could be considered opposites)  healthy lungs, lung recovery
