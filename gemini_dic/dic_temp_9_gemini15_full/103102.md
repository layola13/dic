jeremiah:/ˌdʒɛrɪˈmaɪə/| n. |耶利米；耶利米书|The name Jeremiah comes from the Hebrew name יִרְמְיָהוּ (Yirmiyahu), meaning "Yahweh will exalt" or "Yahweh will raise up".  It refers to the biblical prophet Jeremiah, whose book in the Old Testament details his prophecies and lamentations during a period of significant turmoil in Judah. The name is also used in some contexts to refer to the Book of Jeremiah itself.|The prophet Jeremiah warned of impending doom for Judah due to its wickedness. |近义词：prophet (in a biblical context)|反义词：None directly applicable.|名词
