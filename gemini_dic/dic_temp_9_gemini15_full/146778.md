overidealizing: /ˌoʊvərˌaɪdiəlˈaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 过度理想化 |  The act of placing someone or something on a pedestal, attributing to them qualities or characteristics that are far superior to reality;  exaggerating positive attributes and ignoring or downplaying negative ones.  It often leads to disappointment and disillusionment when the reality falls short of the idealized image. |

例句：

She was overidealizing her relationship, ignoring the flaws and focusing only on the positive aspects. = 她对这段感情过度理想化，忽略了缺点，只关注积极方面。

He overidealized his childhood, remembering only the good times and forgetting the hardships. = 他过度理想化了他的童年，只记得快乐时光，忘记了苦难。


近义词：romanticizing, idealizing excessively,  infatuating with,  putting on a pedestal


反义词：being realistic about, seeing clearly,  facing the truth, acknowledging flaws


词性: 动词 (现在分词形式)


词源:

* **over-**: prefix indicating "too much," "excessively," or "beyond."
* **idealize**: verb meaning "to represent or think of as perfect or ideal."  It ultimately derives from the Greek word "ideals" (ἰδέα), meaning "form," "idea," or "appearance."
