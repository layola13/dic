scandaled:/ˈskændəld/| adj.| 丑闻缠身的；感到震惊的；被激怒的 | Feeling or showing shock and disapproval; scandalized.  The public was scandalized by the politician's behavior.  | The mayor's scandalous affair was front-page news.  The revelations scandalized the entire community. | shocked, appalled, outraged | unmoved, unimpressed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is scandalized by the news. = 他对这个消息感到震惊。
She is scandalized by his behavior. = 她对他的行为感到震惊。


一般过去时:
I was scandalized by the film. = 我被这部电影震惊了。
They were scandalized by her actions. = 他们被她的行为激怒了。


现在完成时:
The public has been scandalized by the recent revelations. = 近期的曝光事件激怒了公众。
We have been scandalized by the corruption in government. = 我们对政府的腐败感到震惊。


过去完成时:
