inept:/ɪnˈept/| adj.|无能的；笨拙的；不合适的 | Inept means lacking skill or ability; clumsy or awkward.  It can refer to a person's lack of skill in a particular task or a general lack of competence.  It can also describe something that is unsuitable or inappropriate for a particular purpose. |例句：He was inept at handling the situation. 他处理这种情况很笨拙。

He made an inept remark that offended everyone. 他说了一番不得体的话，冒犯了所有人。


近义词：unskilled, clumsy, incompetent, awkward, bungling, gauche |反义词：skilled, competent, adept, skillful, dexterous

词源:  The word "inept" comes from the Latin "inaptus," meaning "unfit" or "unsuitable."  The prefix "in-" means "not," and "aptus" means "fit" or "suitable."  Therefore, "inept" literally means "not fit" or "not suitable."
