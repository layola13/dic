self-variations:/selfˌveəriˈeɪʃənz/| n. |自身变异；自我变体|Self-variations refer to the different forms or expressions a thing or concept can take within itself, without external influence.  It emphasizes internal diversity or change.  This term is often used in fields like biology (referring to variations within a single organism or clone), linguistics (variations in pronunciation or grammar within a single dialect), or even philosophy (variations in self-perception).|The artist explored self-variations in her latest series, creating multiple interpretations of a single theme.  |近义词：internal variations, self-modifications, intrinsic changes |反义词：external variations,  mutations (in a biological context implying external influence), uniformity
