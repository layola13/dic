leaseback:/ˈliːsˌbæk/| n.|售后回租|A financial transaction in which a company sells an asset (typically real estate or equipment) and simultaneously leases it back from the buyer.|The company receives immediate cash from the sale and continues to use the asset.  This is often used to raise capital quickly. |Example: The company used a leaseback agreement to free up capital for expansion. 公司使用售后回租协议来为扩张筹集资金。|近义词：sale and leaseback, sale-leaseback|反义词：N/A|名词

The company leases back its building every year. = 公司每年都将自己的建筑物售后回租。
They lease back equipment to improve their cash flow. = 他们售后回租设备以改善现金流。
