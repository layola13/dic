chamfers:/ˈtʃæmfərz/| n. | 倒角；斜角 |  A chamfer is a bevelled edge or corner. It's a sloped edge created by cutting away a small portion of a sharp edge or corner. Chamfers are often used in carpentry, woodworking, and metalworking to soften sharp edges, improve aesthetics, and reduce stress concentrations.  | Example: The carpenter chamfered the edges of the wooden beam to prevent splintering.  木匠将木梁的边缘倒角以防止劈裂。 |近义词：bevel, slant, edge |反义词：square edge, sharp corner | 名词
