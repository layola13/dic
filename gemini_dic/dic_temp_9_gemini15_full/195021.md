semianarchism:/ˌsemiˈænərkiːzəm/| n. |半无政府主义|A political philosophy that advocates for a partial or limited implementation of anarchist principles within an otherwise existing governmental structure.  It often involves a degree of decentralization, self-governance, and individual liberty, but stops short of complete abolition of the state.|Example:  Some proponents of semianarchism suggest a system where local communities manage their own affairs with minimal intervention from a national government.  一些半无政府主义的支持者建议建立一个系统，让地方社区管理自己的事务，而国家政府的干预最少。|近义词：limited anarchism, decentralized governance|反义词：authoritarianism, totalitarianism|名词
