non-imitative: /ˌnɒnɪˈmɪtətɪv/ | adj. | 非摹仿的；非拟声的 | Not imitating or mimicking; not representing sounds or actions by words that resemble them.  The term often applies to words whose meaning isn't derived from the sounds they make.  | Example: The word "table" is non-imitative, unlike "buzz" or "hiss".  | 近义词: non-onomatopoeic, descriptive, literal | 反义词: imitative, onomatopoeic

PHRASE->
There are not many common phrases directly using "non-imitative".  The concept is usually expressed indirectly.  Instead, phrases describing opposite concepts are more frequent.  For example:


Onomatopoeic words:  words that imitate sounds.
Descriptive language: language that paints a picture, may include imitative words or not.
Literal language:  non-figurative language, often non-imitative.
Figurative language: can include onomatopoeia or other forms of imitation.
Representational language: language that aims to represent reality, may or may not use imitation.
