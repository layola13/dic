scrooging:/ˈskruːdʒɪŋ/| v.|吝啬；节俭；过度节省|The act of being excessively frugal or miserly, often to an unreasonable extent.  It implies a reluctance to spend money even when necessary or appropriate.|例句：He was scrooging on the Christmas presents this year, buying only the cheapest things.  今年他圣诞节礼物很吝啬，只买了最便宜的东西。|例句：Scrooging on essential home repairs could lead to more expensive problems later.  对必要的房屋维修过于节省可能会导致以后出现更昂贵的问题。|近义词：stinginess, miserliness, parsimony, frugality (but with a stronger negative connotation)|反义词：generosity, extravagance, profligacy|动词
