constructivist: /kənˈstrʌktɪvɪst/ | adj. | 建构主义的；建构论者的 |

英文解析:Relating to or denoting a philosophy of learning in which knowledge is constructed by the learner through experience rather than passively received from the external world.  It emphasizes the active role of the learner in creating meaning and understanding.  In education, constructivist approaches focus on active learning, collaboration, and problem-solving.  In art and architecture, it refers to a style that emphasizes the use of geometric forms and industrial materials.


例句:

His teaching methods are rooted in constructivist principles.  他的教学方法植根于建构主义原则。

The museum showcased a collection of early constructivist art.  博物馆展出了一批早期的构成主义艺术作品。


近义词:  constructionalist (in some contexts), experiential

反义词:  transmissionist (in education), objectivist (in philosophy)


词性: adjective
