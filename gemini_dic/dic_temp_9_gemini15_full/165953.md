preparation:/ˌprepəˈreɪʃən/ | n. |准备，预备 | The act or process of making ready or preparing something.  It involves arranging or getting things ready beforehand.  |例句：The preparation for the party took all day.  派对的准备工作花了一整天。

例句：Careful preparation is essential for success.  细致的准备对于成功至关重要。


近义词：readiness, planning, arrangement | 反义词：neglect, carelessness


词源:  From Old French *preparacion, from Latin pr?par?ti? (stem of pr?par?tus), past participle of pr?par?re "to prepare," from prae- "before" + par?re "to make ready," from PIE *pere- "to furnish, provide."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He makes thorough preparation for his exams. 他为考试做充分的准备。
My preparation for the presentation is coming along nicely. 我对演示的准备进展顺利。


一般过去时:
She made careful preparation before the interview. 她在面试前做了细致的准备。
They completed all the preparations for the trip. 他们完成了旅行的所有准备工作。


现在完成时:
We have completed our preparations for the project. 我们已经完成了项目的准备工作。
I have made adequate preparation for the unexpected. 我已经为意外情况做好了充分的准备。


过去完成时:
They had made extensive preparations before launching the product. 他们在推出产品之前做了大量的准备工作。
She had finished her preparations before the guests arrived. 客人到达之前，她已经完成了准备工作。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
