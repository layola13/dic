silences:/ˈsaɪlənsɪz/ | n. |沉默，寂静；静默 |The absence of sound; a period of quiet.  It can also refer to the act of keeping quiet or suppressing speech. |例句：The room was filled with a heavy silence.  房间里充满了沉重的寂静。

The teacher asked for silence before beginning the lesson.  老师在开始上课前要求安静。


近义词：stillness, quiet, hush, calm |反义词：noise, clamor, uproar, racket |词性: 名词

词源:
The word "silence" comes from the Old French word "silence," which in turn derives from the Latin word "silentium," meaning "stillness, quiet."  The Latin word is related to the verb "silēre," meaning "to be silent."
