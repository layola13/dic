peroratorically:/ˌpɛrəˈreɪtərɪkli/| adv.|雄辩地；滔滔不绝地；夸夸其谈地|In a manner characteristic of a peroration; in a highly rhetorical or declamatory style.  The lawyer peroratorically summed up his case, leaving no stone unturned in his passionate appeal to the jury.|近义词：rhetorically, eloquently, bombastically|反义词：tersely, succinctly, briefly


例句：

He spoke peroratorically,  his voice booming across the hall. = 他滔滔不绝地讲话，声音在整个大厅回荡。

The politician delivered a peroratorically charged speech, full of fiery rhetoric. = 这位政治家发表了一篇充满激昂辞令和夸夸其谈的演讲。


词源：

Derived from "peroration," the concluding part of a speech,  ultimately from Latin "perorare" (to speak to the end), from "per-" (through, completely) + "orare" (to speak).  The "-ically" suffix indicates an adverbial form.
