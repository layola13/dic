orthoptera:/ɔːrˈθɒptərə/| n. |直翅目昆虫|Orthoptera is an order of insects characterized by their straight, leathery forewings (tegmina) and usually membranous hindwings, used for flight. They also have chewing mouthparts and undergo incomplete metamorphosis.  This order includes grasshoppers, crickets, katydids, and locusts. |Example: Many insects belonging to the order Orthoptera are known for their chirping sounds. 许多属于直翅目的昆虫以其鸣叫声而闻名。|近义词：insect order (泛指)|反义词：无
