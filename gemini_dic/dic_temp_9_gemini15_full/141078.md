onomatopoeia: /ˌɒnəˌmætəˈpiːə/ | /ˌɑːnəˌmætəˈpiːə/ | n. |拟声词；拟声法 |The formation of a word from a sound associated with what is named (e.g., cuckoo, sizzle ).  Onomatopoeia is a literary technique where words are used to mimic the sounds they describe.  This can involve sounds of animals, natural phenomena, or even actions. |例句：The buzzing of bees filled the air. 蜜蜂的嗡嗡声充满了空气。

The sputtering engine finally died.  发动机发出噼里啪啦的声音后终于熄火了。


近义词：sound imitation, mimetic word | 反义词：None (no direct opposite) | 词性：Noun
