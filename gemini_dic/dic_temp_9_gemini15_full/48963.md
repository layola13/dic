degeneracy:/dɪˈdʒɛnərəsi/ | n. | 退化；堕落；简并；(数学)奇异性 | The decline or deterioration of something.  It can refer to a decline in quality, morality, or function. In mathematics and physics, it refers to a situation where a system has multiple states with the same energy. | Example: The degeneracy of the moral standards in the city worried the mayor.  城市的道德标准堕落让市长担忧。


The increasing degeneracy of the software led to frequent crashes. 软件的日渐退化导致频繁崩溃。


近义词: degradation, deterioration, decline, corruption | 反义词: improvement, enhancement, elevation, perfection


词源:  From Late Latin *degenerāre "to degenerate," from Latin dē- "away, down" + genus "kind, race, birth."  The original meaning implied a deviation from an ancestral type or standard.
