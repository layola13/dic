eponymic:/ɪˈpɒnɪmɪk/| adj.|以人名命名的；来自人名的 | Relating to or denoting a person after whom something is named.  The term is derived from the Greek words "epi" (upon, on) and "onyma" (name).  It describes something named after a person, often a person who is associated with its discovery, invention, or creation. | Example:  The term "sandwich" is eponymic, named after the Earl of Sandwich. | 近义词:namesake, after  | 反义词:anonymous,unnamed | 词性:adjective
