pre-pay:/ˌpriːˈpeɪ/| v.|预付，预先付款|To pay for something before you receive it or use it.|例句：I pre-paid for my flight tickets. 我预付了我的机票费用。|例句：The company requires all employees to pre-pay their travel expenses. 公司要求所有员工预付差旅费用。|近义词：pay in advance, advance payment|反义词：post-pay, pay later


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They pre-pay their bills every month. = 他们每个月都预付账单。
We pre-pay for our internet service. = 我们预付互联网服务费用。


一般过去时:
She pre-paid for the concert tickets last week. = 她上周预付了演唱会门票的费用。
He pre-paid his hotel reservation. = 他预付了他的酒店预订费用。


现在完成时:
I have pre-paid my membership fee for the year. = 我已经预付了今年的会员费。
They have pre-paid for their holiday accommodation. = 他们已经预付了他们的假期住宿费用。


过去完成时:
She had pre-paid the deposit before the contract was signed. = 合同签署之前，她已经预付了押金。
He had pre-paid the tuition fees for his son's education. = 他已经预付了他儿子教育的学费。


将来时:
We will pre-pay the rent for the next quarter. = 我们将预付下一季度的租金。
They will pre-pay the down payment on the new house. = 他们将预付新房的首付款。


虚拟语气:
If I had pre-paid the tickets, we wouldn't have missed the show. = 如果我预付了票，我们就不会错过演出。
I wish I had pre-paid my parking fee. = 我真希望我预付了停车费。


被动语态:
The tickets can be pre-paid online. = 门票可以在线预付。
The service fee must be pre-paid. = 服务费必须预付。


疑问句:
Can I pre-pay for this item? = 我可以预付这件商品的费用吗？
Do you accept pre-paid orders? = 你接受预付订单吗？


否定句:
I didn't pre-pay for the taxi. = 我没有预付出租车的费用。
He did not pre-pay his membership dues. = 他没有预付他的会员费。



PHRASE->
pre-pay for：预付...费用
pre-pay a bill：预付账单
pre-pay the deposit：预付押金
pre-pay the rent：预付房租
pre-pay an order: 预付订单费用
