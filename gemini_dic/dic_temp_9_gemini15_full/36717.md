collocated:/ˈkɒləkeɪtɪd/ | adj. |搭配的；共生的|  The word "collocated" describes words that frequently appear together in a language. It suggests a natural and expected combination, often reflecting common usage and idiomatic expressions.  The collocation is a key element in achieving fluency and naturalness in a language. For example, "strong coffee" is a common collocation, while "powerful coffee" is less common, though grammatically correct. The meaning or impact of a word can also be significantly altered by its collocates.  Collocation analysis is a valuable tool in lexicography, language teaching, and machine translation. |The words "heavy rain" are collocated; they often appear together.|近义词：associated, combined, linked |反义词：unrelated, disparate
