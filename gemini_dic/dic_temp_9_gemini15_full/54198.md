disfaith:/dɪsˈfeɪθ/| n. |不信任，怀疑；缺乏信仰 |Lack of faith; distrust.  It implies a loss of trust or belief, often in something previously held dear or sacred.  The word carries a stronger sense of disillusionment than mere doubt. |例句：His disfaith in the government led him to protest. 他的政府的不信任感使他走上抗议的道路。

例句：The scandal caused widespread disfaith in the company's leadership. 这场丑闻导致公司领导层普遍失去信任。


近义词：distrust, disbelief, suspicion, doubt |反义词：faith, trust, belief, confidence |词性：名词
