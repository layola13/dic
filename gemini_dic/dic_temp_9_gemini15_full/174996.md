radiciform:/ˈrædɪsɪfɔːrm/ | adj. | 根形的；根状的 | Resembling a root in form; root-like.  The term describes structures that have the shape or appearance of a root, often referring to plant parts or other organic structures.  | Example: The radiciform appendages of the plant helped anchor it to the rocky soil.  植物的根状附属物帮助它固定在岩石土壤中。| 近义词：root-like, root-shaped | 反义词：shoot-like, stem-like | 形容词


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun conjugations in the way "what" does.  Therefore, tense, voice, etc. are not applicable.  The usage focuses on its descriptive nature.


例句1: The parasitic plant had long, thin, radiciform stems that penetrated the host plant.  这种寄生植物具有细长、根状的茎，穿透寄主植物。

例句2:  The geologist identified the radiciform structures as fossilized roots from an ancient tree.  地质学家将这些根状结构鉴定为古代树木的化石根。
