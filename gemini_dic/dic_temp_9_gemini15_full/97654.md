inhalation:/ˌɪnhəˈleɪʃən/| n. |吸入；吸气|The act of inhaling or breathing in.  Inhalation can refer to the process of breathing in air, or to the process of taking in a substance, such as medication, through breathing.|例句：Deep inhalation can help to calm the nerves. 深呼吸有助于镇定神经。
例句：Accidental inhalation of toxic fumes led to his hospitalization.意外吸入有毒烟雾导致他住院。|近义词：inspiration, aspiration|反义词：exhalation|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The patient inhales deeply during the treatment. = 病人在治疗过程中深呼吸。
He inhales the sweet scent of flowers. = 他吸入花朵的芬芳。


一般过去时:
She inhaled sharply at the sight of the accident. = 她看到事故时倒吸一口冷气。
He inhaled the smoke from the fire. = 他吸入了火灾的烟雾。


现在完成时:
She has inhaled too much dust and is coughing. = 她吸入了太多灰尘，正在咳嗽。
He has inhaled the medication as prescribed. = 他按照处方吸入了药物。


过去完成时:
He had inhaled a large amount of smoke before he collapsed. = 他在倒下之前吸入了大量的烟雾。
They had inhaled the toxic fumes for hours before realizing the danger. = 他们在意识到危险之前已经吸入了数小时的毒气。


将来时:
The doctor will instruct the patient to inhale deeply. = 医生将指示病人深呼吸。
He will inhale the anesthetic before the surgery. = 他将在手术前吸入麻醉剂。


虚拟语气:
If he had inhaled the antidote sooner, he might have survived. = 如果他早点吸入解毒剂，他也许就能活下来。
I wish I hadn't inhaled so much smoke. = 我真希望我没有吸入那么多烟。


被动语态:

The fumes were inhaled by the workers. = 工人们吸入了烟雾。


疑问句:

否定句:

She does not inhale the pollen. = 她不会吸入花粉。
