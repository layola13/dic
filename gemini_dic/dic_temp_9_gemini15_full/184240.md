resolve:/rɪˈzɒlv/ /rɪˈzɑːlv/| vt. n.|解决；决定；决心；分辨率|To find an answer or solution to a problem, difficulty, or question. To firmly decide to do something.  A firm decision to do something.|例句：We must resolve this issue before it escalates. 我们必须在问题恶化之前解决这个问题。

例句：She resolved to quit smoking. 她决心戒烟。

近义词：settle, solve, determine, decide|反义词：create, cause, worsen, complicate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He resolves conflicts peacefully. = 他和平地解决冲突。
She resolves to exercise daily. = 她决心每天锻炼。


一般过去时:
They resolved the dispute quickly. = 他们迅速解决了争端。
He resolved to change his life. = 他决心改变生活。


现在完成时:
We have resolved most of the problems. = 我们已经解决了大部分问题。
She has resolved to be more patient. = 她已经下定决心要更有耐心。


过去完成时:
They had resolved the issue before the meeting. = 他们会议前已经解决了这个问题。
She had resolved to go to college before she graduated high school. = 她高中毕业前就决心上大学。


将来时:
We will resolve this matter soon. = 我们很快就会解决这个问题。
He will resolve to study harder next semester. = 下学期他将下定决心更加努力学习。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would resolve the matter immediately. = 如果我是你，我会立即解决这个问题。
I wish I had resolved to study harder earlier. = 我真希望我早点下定决心努力学习。


被动语态:
The problem was resolved quickly. = 问题很快得到解决。
The issue will be resolved by the committee. = 这个问题将由委员会解决。


疑问句:
Have you resolved the issue? = 你解决这个问题了吗？
Will they resolve the conflict peacefully? = 他们会和平地解决冲突吗？


否定句:
He didn't resolve to participate. = 他没有决心参与。
They haven't resolved the problem yet. = 他们还没有解决这个问题。



PHRASE->
resolve a conflict = 解决冲突
resolve an issue = 解决问题
resolve a dispute = 解决争端
resolve a mystery = 解开谜团
resolve to do something = 决心做某事

词源:
The word "resolve" comes from the Old French word "resoudre," meaning "to loosen, untie, solve."  This ultimately derives from the Latin "resolvere," formed from the prefix "re-" (again) and "solvere" (to loosen).  The sense of "to make a firm decision" developed later.
