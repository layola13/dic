pil:/pɪl/ | n. |药丸，药片 | A small, rounded mass of medicine to be swallowed. |例句：He swallowed the pill with a glass of water. 他用一杯水吞下了药丸。|近义词：tablet, capsule |反义词:N/A


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The doctor prescribes a pill for my headache. = 医生给我开了一颗治头痛的药。
She takes a vitamin pill every morning. = 她每天早上都吃一片维生素片。


一般过去时:
He took a pain reliever pill last night. = 他昨晚吃了一颗止痛药。
I swallowed the pill whole. = 我整颗药丸都吞下去了。


现在完成时:
She has taken all her prescribed pills. = 她已经服用了所有医生开的药。
I have not taken my medication pill yet. = 我还没吃药。


过去完成时:
He had already taken his pills before the doctor arrived. = 在医生到达之前，他已经吃过药了。
She had forgotten to take her allergy pill that morning. = 她那天早上忘了吃过敏药。


将来时:
I will take the pill before bedtime. = 我会在睡前吃药。
She will need to take this pill twice a day. = 她需要每天服用两次这种药。


虚拟语气:
If I had taken the pill earlier, I wouldn't feel so bad now. = 如果我早点吃药，现在就不会这么难受了。
I wish I had taken the pill before the headache started. = 我真希望头痛开始之前我就吃了药。


被动语态:
The pill was swallowed easily. = 这药丸很容易吞咽。
The pills are manufactured by a pharmaceutical company. = 这些药丸是由一家制药公司生产的。


疑问句:
Have you taken your pill today? = 你今天吃药了吗？
What kind of pill is this? = 这是什么药？


否定句:
He didn't take the pill as prescribed. = 他没有按照处方服用药物。
I don't need to take any pills right now. = 我现在不需要吃任何药。
