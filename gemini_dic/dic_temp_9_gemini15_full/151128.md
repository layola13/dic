parasymbiotic: /ˌpærəˌsɪmbaɪˈɑːtɪk/ | adj. | 共生的；寄生共生的 |  Relating to or denoting a symbiotic relationship in which one organism benefits and the other is neither benefited nor harmed.  This is a less common term than mutualistic (where both benefit) or parasitic (where one benefits and the other is harmed).  It describes a relationship where one organism gains an advantage, while the other is unaffected. | Example: "The relationship between the remora and the shark is often described as parasymbiotic, with the remora gaining food scraps and protection without significantly impacting the shark." | 近义词: commensal | 反义词: parasitic, mutualistic
