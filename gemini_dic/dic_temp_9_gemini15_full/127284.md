navigability:/ˌnævɪɡəˈbɪləti/| n. |可航行性，通航性 |The navigability of a river or waterway refers to its suitability for navigation by ships or boats.  This depends on factors such as depth, width, presence of obstacles, and currents.  A river with shallow waters or numerous rocks might have poor navigability. |The navigability of the Mississippi River is crucial for the transport of goods. 密西西比河的可航行性对于货物运输至关重要。 The Suez Canal's navigability was temporarily disrupted by the grounding of a large container ship. 苏伊士运河的可航行性曾因一艘大型集装箱船搁浅而暂时中断。|accessibility, passability |impassability, unnavigable
