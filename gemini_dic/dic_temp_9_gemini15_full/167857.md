primp:/prɪmp/| v.|打扮，梳妆打扮|To groom or dress oneself meticulously and often excessively.  It often implies a vain or self-conscious effort to look attractive.|例句：She spent hours primping before the party. 她花了几个小时在派对前打扮自己。|例句：He primped his hair in the mirror. 他对着镜子梳理他的头发。|近义词：adorn, preen, doll up|反义词：scowl, neglect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She primps before every date. = 她每次约会前都会打扮一番。
He primps his hair every morning. = 他每天早上都梳理头发。

一般过去时:
She primped for hours before the wedding. = 婚礼前她打扮了好几个小时。

现在完成时:
She has primped herself for the interview. = 她已经为面试打扮好了自己。
He has primped and preened all morning. = 他整个上午都在打扮和梳妆。
