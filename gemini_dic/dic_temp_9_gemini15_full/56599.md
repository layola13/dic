donatism:/ˈdoʊnəˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 多纳图斯主义 | A 4th-century Christian heresy asserting that the validity of the sacraments depends on the moral worthiness of the minister.  It emphasized the holiness of the clergy and questioned the efficacy of sacraments administered by unworthy priests.  This led to significant schisms within the early Church. | The Donatist controversy deeply divided the North African Church. |  近义词：schism, heresy | 反义词：orthodoxy
