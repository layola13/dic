quadruplane:/ˈkwɒdrəpleɪn/ | n. | 四翼飞机 | A quadruplane is an airplane with four main wings, stacked one above the other.  This design, though historically used, is now largely obsolete due to its aerodynamic inefficiencies compared to monoplanes and biplanes. | Example: The early 20th century saw several experimental quadruplanes, but they quickly fell out of favor.  = 20 世纪初出现了几种实验性的四翼飞机，但它们很快就被淘汰了。|近义词：multiplane (泛指多翼飞机)|反义词：monoplane (单翼飞机), biplane (双翼飞机)
