inculcating: /ɪnˈkʌlkeɪtɪŋ/ | v. |灌输；谆谆教诲 |  To inculcate something means to instill an idea, belief, or habit into someone's mind, especially by persistent instruction.  It implies a gradual and often repetitive process of teaching. |

例句：
The school aims at inculcating good moral values in its students. = 学校的目标是向学生灌输良好的道德价值观。

The teacher diligently inculcated the importance of hard work in her students. = 老师勤奋地向学生灌输努力工作的重要性。


近义词：instill, implant, imbue, impress, teach


反义词：eradicate, remove, suppress, dispel


词性:动词

词源:
From Latin *inculcāre, from in- "in" + *culcāre "to tread, trample," from calx "heel."  The original sense involved repeatedly treading something into the ground to make it firm, which evolved to the metaphorical sense of firmly establishing an idea or belief.
