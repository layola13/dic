dispelled:/dɪˈspeld/ | vt. |驱散；消除；打消| To drive away or cause to vanish; to remove or alleviate a feeling, doubt, or misconception. |例句：The rising sun dispelled the morning mist.  旭日驱散了晨雾。|例句：Her explanation dispelled my doubts. 她的解释消除了我的疑虑。|近义词：disperse, dissipate, banish, eliminate |反义词：gather, accumulate, foster, cultivate |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun dispels the fog every morning. = 太阳每天早上驱散雾气。
He dispels rumors with facts. = 他用事实来消除谣言。


一般过去时:
The wind dispelled the clouds. = 风吹散了云彩。
The evidence dispelled any suspicion. = 证据消除了任何怀疑。


现在完成时:
The good news has dispelled our fears. = 好消息打消了我们的恐惧。
The meeting has dispelled much of the confusion. = 会议消除了许多困惑。


过去完成时:
They had dispelled all doubts before the presentation. = 他们在演示之前已经打消了一切疑虑。
The explanation had dispelled her anxieties. = 解释消除了她的焦虑。


将来时:
The rain will dispel the heat. = 雨会驱散酷暑。
This report will dispel any misconceptions. = 这份报告将消除任何误解。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, it would dispel all doubt. = 如果证据更充分，它就能消除一切疑虑。
I wish this medicine could dispel my headache. = 我希望这药能消除我的头痛。


被动语态:
The mist was dispelled by the sun. = 雾被太阳驱散了。
All doubts were dispelled by his clear explanation. = 他清晰的解释消除了所有疑虑。


疑问句:
Will the coming storm dispel the haze? = 即将来临的风暴会驱散雾霾吗？
Can this evidence dispel their suspicions? = 这个证据能消除他们的怀疑吗？


否定句:
The explanation did not dispel her worries. = 解释并没有打消她的担忧。
His words failed to dispel the rumors. = 他的话没能消除谣言。


PHRASE->
dispel doubts = 消除疑虑
dispel fears = 打消恐惧
dispel a myth = 破除神话
dispel the darkness = 驱散黑暗
dispel misconceptions = 消除误解

词源:
dispelled 源自于古法语单词 despellier，其自身源于拉丁语单词 dispergere，意思是“散开，分散”。  前缀 dis- 表示“分开”，pel  与推或驱动有关。
