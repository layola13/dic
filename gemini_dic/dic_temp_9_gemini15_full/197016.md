shako:/ˈʃækoʊ/| n. |沙科帽，高筒帽 |A shako is a military headdress, typically tall and cylindrical, with a front plate or plume.  It was widely worn in the 19th century. |例句：The soldier proudly wore his shako, adorned with a plume of feathers.  士兵骄傲地戴着他的沙科帽，上面装饰着羽毛饰物。

The officer's shako bore the insignia of his regiment. 军官的沙科帽上戴着他的团徽。|近义词：helmet, cap (in some contexts) |反义词：N/A


词源:  The word "shako" comes from the Hungarian word *csákó*, which itself is of uncertain origin, possibly from a Slavic or Turkic root.  Its adoption into English reflects the widespread use of the shako in European armies, particularly during the Napoleonic Wars.
