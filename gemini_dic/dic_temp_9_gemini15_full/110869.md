lionised:/ˈlaɪənaɪzd/| vt.| 崇拜；赞扬；追捧 | To treat someone as a celebrity or important person.  To give someone a lot of praise and attention. |例句：The author was lionised by critics and the public alike.  这位作家受到评论家和公众的一致推崇。

例句：After his successful film debut, the young actor was lionised by the media.  这位年轻的演员在电影处女作大获成功后，受到了媒体的追捧。


近义词：celebritised, idolized, glorified, celebrated, acclaimed

反义词：ignored, neglected, scorned, criticized, denigrated

词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They lionise him for his achievements. = 他们因为他的成就而崇拜他。
The media lionises every new pop star. = 媒体追捧每一个新的流行歌星。


一般过去时:
The town lionised the returning hero. = 城镇的人们崇拜着归来的英雄。
She was lionised after winning the Nobel Prize. = 她在获得诺贝尔奖后受到了人们的追捧。


现在完成时:
He has been lionised throughout his career. = 他在整个职业生涯中都受到人们的推崇。
The band has been lionised by fans worldwide. = 这个乐队受到了全世界歌迷的追捧。


过去完成时:
They had already lionised him before the award ceremony. = 颁奖典礼之前，人们就已经开始崇拜他了。
The artist had been lionised for decades before his death. =  这位艺术家在去世前几十年就已受到人们的推崇。


将来时:
They will lionise him for his groundbreaking work. = 他们将会因为他的开创性工作而崇拜他。
The young athlete will be lionised if he wins the Olympics. = 如果这位年轻的运动员赢得奥运会，他将受到人们的追捧。


虚拟语气:
If he won the award, he would be lionised. = 如果他获奖，他将受到人们的推捧。
I wish I were lionised for my writing. = 我希望我的写作能够受到人们的推崇。


被动语态:
He was lionised by the press. = 他受到了媒体的追捧。
She was lionised for her contribution to science. = 她因为对科学的贡献而受到人们的推崇。


疑问句:
Will he be lionised after this performance? =  这场演出之后，他会受到人们的推崇吗？
Is she lionised for her beauty or her talent? = 她是因为美貌还是才华而受到人们的推捧？


否定句:
They did not lionise him, despite his efforts. = 尽管他付出了努力，他们并没有崇拜他。
She was not lionised by the critics. = 她并没有受到评论家的推崇。

词源:
From the noun "lion," referring to the majestic and powerful nature of a lion, and the suffix "-ised," indicating the process of being treated like a lion (i.e., treated with great respect and admiration).  The word ultimately reflects the historical association of lions with royalty, power, and strength.
