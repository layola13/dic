hoes:/hoʊz/ | n. |锄头；女性的轻蔑称呼|A hoe is a tool used for gardening, typically with a flat blade attached to a long handle.  It's also a derogatory slang term for a promiscuous woman. |例句：He used a hoe to cultivate the soil. 他用锄头耕地。|近义词：(as a tool) cultivator, shovel; (as a slang term) slut, whore |反义词：(as a tool) rake, plough; (as a slang term)  None (due to derogatory nature)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hoes the garden every morning. = 他每天早上都锄地。
She hoes the weeds from the flowerbed. = 她把花坛里的杂草锄掉了。


一般过去时:
They hoed the field all afternoon. = 他们整个下午都在锄地。
He hoed the rows of corn. = 他锄了玉米的行。


现在完成时:
I have hoed the entire vegetable patch. = 我已经锄完了整个菜园。
She has hoed the garden three times this week. = 她这周已经锄了三次园子了。


过去完成时:
They had hoed the potatoes before the rain started. = 在下雨之前他们已经锄过土豆了。
He had hoed the cornfield before the sun set. = 太阳下山之前他已经锄完了玉米地。


将来时:
We will hoe the garden tomorrow. = 我们明天将要锄地。
They will hoe the potatoes next week. = 他们下周将要锄土豆。


虚拟语气:
If I had a hoe, I would hoe the garden myself. = 如果我有一把锄头，我会自己锄地。
I wish I had hoed the garden sooner. = 我真希望我早点锄了地。


被动语态:
The garden was hoed yesterday. = 花园昨天被锄过了。
The field is being hoed by the farmers. = 田地正在被农民们锄。


疑问句:
Do you hoe the garden regularly? = 你经常锄地吗？
Has she hoed the vegetables yet? = 她锄过菜了吗？


否定句:
He doesn't hoe the garden very often. = 他不经常锄地。
She didn't hoe the field today. = 她今天没有锄地。
