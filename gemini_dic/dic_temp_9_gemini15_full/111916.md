loiteringly:/ˈlɔɪtərɪŋli/ | adv. | 徘徊地；磨蹭地；懒洋洋地 | In a slow, idle, or lingering manner; with delay.  It describes an action performed slowly and without urgency, often implying a lack of purpose or aimless wandering. |例句：He walked loiteringly down the street, seemingly without a destination. 他懒洋洋地走在街上，似乎没有目的地。


The children loiteringly gathered their belongings, delaying their departure. 孩子们磨磨蹭蹭地收拾东西，耽误了离开。| 近义词：slowly, languidly, idly, dawdlingly | 反义词：quickly, hastily, rapidly, expeditiously

否定句:

词源:  Derived from the verb "loiter," which ultimately comes from Old French "loiterer," meaning "to delay" or "to idle."  The "-ingly" suffix forms the adverb, indicating the manner of the action.
