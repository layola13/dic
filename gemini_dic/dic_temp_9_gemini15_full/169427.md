pronounces:/prəˈnaʊnsɪz/ | v. | 发音，宣告，断言 | To say or pronounce words; to declare or state something formally or officially. | 例句：He pronounces the word perfectly. 他把这个词发音很完美。| 例句：The judge pronounced the verdict. 法官宣判了判决。| 近义词：articulates, enunciates, declares, proclaims | 反义词：mumbles, whispers


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pronounces each word carefully. = 他仔细地发每个音。
She pronounces her opinions clearly. = 她清楚地表达自己的观点。

一般过去时:
The judge pronounced the defendant guilty. = 法官宣判被告有罪。
He pronounced the speech with great passion. = 他充满激情地发表了演讲。

现在完成时:
She has pronounced the results of the election. = 她已经宣布了选举结果。
The doctor has pronounced him fit for duty. = 医生宣布他适合工作。

过去完成时:
The jury had pronounced a verdict before the judge entered. = 在法官进入之前，陪审团已经做出了裁决。
They had pronounced him dead at the scene. = 他们当时就宣布他死亡了。

将来时:
The president will pronounce a statement later today. = 总统将在今天晚些时候发表声明。
She will pronounce the winning number. = 她将宣布中奖号码。

虚拟语气:
If he pronounced the words correctly, he would have passed the test. = 如果他正确地发音，他就会通过考试。
I wish she had pronounced my name correctly. = 我希望她能正确地念我的名字。

被动语态:
The verdict was pronounced by the judge. = 判决是由法官宣判的。
The winner's name will be pronounced at the end of the ceremony. = 获胜者的名字将在仪式结束时宣布。

疑问句:
How do you pronounce this word? = 你怎么发这个词的音？
How did he pronounce the sentence? = 他是怎么宣读判决的？

否定句:
He didn't pronounce the words clearly. = 他没有清晰地发音。
The teacher didn't pronounce the student's expulsion. = 老师没有宣布开除这个学生。
