demagogging: /ˈdɛməɡɑːɡɪŋ/ | v. |煽动；蛊惑；利用公众情绪 | The act of practicing demagoguery; appealing to the emotions and prejudices of the populace rather than using rational argument.  | Example: The politician was accused of demagogging the voters with promises he couldn't keep.  政治家被指控利用无法兑现的承诺来煽动选民。 | 近义词：incitement, agitation, rabble-rousing | 反义词：reasoning, persuasion, statesmanship | 动词

一般现在时:
He is demagogging the crowd with his inflammatory rhetoric. 他正在用煽动性的言辞煽动人群。
They are constantly demagogging the public on social media. 他们在社交媒体上不断煽动公众情绪。


一般过去时:
The speaker demagogged the audience into a frenzy. 演讲者煽动听众陷入狂热之中。
The dictator demagogged his people into war. 独裁者煽动人民发动战争。


现在完成时:
He has been demagogging the public for years. 他多年来一直在煽动公众情绪。
They have demagogged their supporters into believing false narratives. 他们煽动支持者相信虚假叙事。


过去完成时:
The candidate had demagogged the electorate before the election. 候选人在选举前就煽动了选民。
The leader had demagogged his followers into a state of blind obedience. 领导者已煽动追随者进入盲目服从的状态。


将来时:
He will demagog the masses with his populist slogans. 他将用民粹主义口号来煽动群众。
She will continue demagogging her followers until the election. 她将继续煽动她的追随者直到选举。


虚拟语气:
If he demagogged the people, they would rise up against him. 如果他煽动人民，他们就会起来反抗他。
If she had not demagogged the voters, she might have won the election. 如果她没有煽动选民，她可能就赢得了选举。


被动语态:
The voters were demagogged by the charismatic leader. 选民被有魅力的领导人煽动。
The public is being demagogged by misleading information. 公众正在被误导信息所煽动。


疑问句:
Is he demagogging the public with his lies? 他是否在用谎言煽动公众？
Are they demagogging the people to achieve their goals? 他们是否在煽动人民以实现他们的目标？


否定句:
He did not demagog the crowd; he simply stated the facts. 他没有煽动人群；他只是陈述了事实。
They refused to demagog the population with false promises. 他们拒绝用虚假承诺煽动人民。
