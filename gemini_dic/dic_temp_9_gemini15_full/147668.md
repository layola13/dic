overpositiveness:/ˌoʊvərˈpɑːzɪtɪvnəs/ | n. | 过分自信，盲目乐观 | Excessive confidence or optimism; a tendency to be overly positive, often to the point of being unrealistic or ignoring potential risks.  |  His overpositiveness led him to underestimate the challenges of the project. 他的过分自信让他低估了项目的挑战。  |近义词：overconfidence, optimism,  exuberance | 反义词：pessimism, negativity, realism | 名词


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It doesn't have tenses or voice changes.  However, it can be used in various sentence structures.

Example Sentences:

Simple Sentence: Her overpositiveness was her downfall.  她的过分自信是她失败的原因。

Complex Sentence: Because of his overpositiveness, he didn't prepare adequately for the exam, resulting in a poor grade. 因为他过于自信，所以没有充分准备考试，导致成绩很差。

Compound Sentence:  The team's overpositiveness was evident, but their skills were lacking; consequently, they lost the game. 球队的过分自信很明显，但他们的技能却不足；因此，他们输掉了比赛。


PHRASE->
There are no common set phrases directly using "overpositiveness."  However, phrases expressing similar ideas can be used:


A rose-tinted view:  This indicates an overly optimistic perspective.  (粉红色的眼镜：这表明一种过于乐观的视角。)
Blind optimism: This describes a state of optimism ignoring potential problems. (盲目乐观：这描述了一种忽视潜在问题的乐观状态。)
Unrealistic expectations: This highlights the disconnect between positive belief and reality. (不切实际的期望：这突出了积极信念与现实之间的脱节。)
Pollyannaish attitude: Referring to a relentlessly cheerful and optimistic person, often to the point of naivete. (波丽安娜式态度：指的是一个始终乐观开朗的人，经常天真到有点傻的地步。)
Head in the clouds: This idiom describes someone who is not grounded in reality due to excessive optimism. (脑袋在云里：这个习语形容一个人因为过于乐观而脱离现实。)
