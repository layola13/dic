quasi-bankrupt: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈbæŋkrʌpt/ | adj. | 准破产的，濒临破产的 |  Near bankruptcy; financially unstable and on the verge of insolvency.  The term suggests a state closer to bankruptcy than merely experiencing financial difficulties, but not yet officially declared bankrupt. |

例句：

The company is quasi-bankrupt, struggling to meet its debt obligations and facing potential liquidation.  = 这家公司濒临破产，难以偿还债务，面临潜在的清算。

Several small businesses became quasi-bankrupt after the pandemic hit. =  疫情爆发后，几家小企业都陷入了准破产状态。


近义词：insolvent, bankrupt (but less severe), financially distressed, near-bankrupt

反义词：solvent, financially sound, prosperous, thriving

词源:

* **quasi-**:  from Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."
* **bankrupt**: from Italian *bancarotta*, ultimately from *banco rotto*, literally "broken bench," referring to the medieval practice of breaking a money-lender's bench as a sign of bankruptcy.
