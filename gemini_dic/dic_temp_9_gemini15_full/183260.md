reprieving:/rɪˈpriːvɪŋ/| v.|暂缓执行；缓刑；使缓刑；使免于；使恢复生机 | to delay the punishment of (someone, especially someone condemned to death); to postpone or delay something unpleasant |例句：The governor reprieved the condemned man. = 州长赦免了死刑犯。
The doctor’s treatment reprieved him from the brink of death. = 医生的治疗使他从死亡边缘逃脱。|近义词：postpone, delay, suspend, pardon |反义词：execute, punish, condemn


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The judge reprieves the prisoner. = 法官赦免了囚犯。
He reprieves his decision until tomorrow. = 他将决定推迟到明天。


一般过去时:
The king reprieved the traitor. = 国王赦免了叛徒。
She reprieved her plans to move. = 她放弃了她搬家的计划。


现在完成时:
The court has reprieved the death sentence. = 法院已暂缓执行死刑。
They have reprieved their decision to close the factory. = 他们已经放弃了关闭工厂的决定。


过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
