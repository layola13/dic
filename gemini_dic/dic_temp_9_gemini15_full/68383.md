fallouts:/ˈfɔːlˌaʊts/ | n. | 后果，辐射尘，附带损害 | The long-term fallouts of the war are still being felt.  战争的长期后果仍在持续影响着人们。|  The nuclear fallout after the bomb was devastating.  炸弹爆炸后的核辐射非常可怕。 | consequences, repercussions, aftermath |  benefit, advantage | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The fallout from the scandal is affecting many people. = 这场丑闻的后果正在影响很多人。
Nuclear fallout poses a serious threat. = 核辐射构成严重的威胁。


一般过去时:
The fallout from the economic crisis was severe. = 经济危机的后果非常严重。
There was significant fallout after the announcement. = 公告发布后出现了重大影响。


现在完成时:
The company has experienced considerable fallout from the lawsuit. = 这家公司因诉讼而遭受了相当大的损失。
We have seen the fallout of his decisions. = 我们已经看到了他决定带来的后果。


过去完成时:
By the time the investigation concluded, the fallout had already damaged the company's reputation. = 当调查结束时，其后果已经损害了公司的声誉。
The fallout from the earlier scandal had not yet subsided. = 此前丑闻的影响尚未消退。


将来时:
Experts predict significant fallout from the new regulations. = 专家预测新规将产生重大影响。
We will see the fallout of these actions in the coming months. = 我们将在接下来的几个月里看到这些行动的后果。


虚拟语气:
If the project had failed, the fallout would have been catastrophic. = 如果项目失败了，后果将是灾难性的。
I wish there were less fallout from this disagreement. = 我希望这场争论带来的负面影响少一些。


被动语态:
The city was devastated by the fallout from the volcano. = 这座城市被火山灰摧毁了。
The company's image is being damaged by the negative fallout. = 公司的形象正在因负面影响而受损。


疑问句:
What is the fallout from this decision? = 这个决定的后果是什么？
Are there any potential fallouts we should consider? = 我们应该考虑哪些潜在的影响？


否定句:
There was no significant fallout from the incident. = 这起事件没有造成重大影响。
