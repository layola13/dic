conventionalization:/kənˌvɛnʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |常规化；约定俗成；传统化 |The process by which something becomes conventional or accepted as a standard.  It refers to the adoption of a particular practice, belief, or behavior as the norm within a society or group. This can happen through various means, including repetition, social influence, and institutionalization. | Example: The conventionalization of online communication has led to the widespread use of emojis and abbreviations.  在线交流的常规化导致表情符号和缩写的广泛使用。  The conventionalization of certain musical forms over time resulted in the development of distinct genres.  某些音乐形式随着时间的推移而常规化，导致了不同类型的音乐发展。|近义词：standardization, normalization, routinization |反义词：innovation, unconventionality
