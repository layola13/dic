downturn:/ˈdaʊntɜːrn/| n. |经济衰退；下降；低迷| A downturn is a period of decline in economic activity.  It's characterized by reduced production, employment, and consumer spending.  This can be applied to various aspects, not just the economy. |例句：The company experienced a sharp downturn in sales after the scandal. 公司在丑闻后销售额急剧下降。|例句：There's been a downturn in the housing market recently. 最近房地产市场低迷。|近义词：decline, recession, slump, depression |反义词：upswing, boom, recovery, growth |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The economy shows a downturn. = 经济显示出低迷。
A downturn affects many businesses. = 低迷影响许多企业。


一般过去时:
The market experienced a significant downturn last year. = 市场去年经历了严重的低迷。
Sales showed a downturn after the holiday season. = 假期过后销售额下降。


现在完成时:
The region has seen a downturn in tourism. = 该地区旅游业已经低迷。
We have experienced several downturns in the past decade. = 我们在过去十年中经历了几次经济低迷。


过去完成时:
The company had already experienced a downturn before the new CEO arrived. = 新任CEO上任前，公司已经经历了经济低迷。
The housing market had suffered a downturn before the government intervention. = 在政府干预之前，房地产市场已经遭受了低迷。


将来时:
Experts predict a downturn in the global economy next year. = 专家预测明年全球经济将出现低迷。
The analysts believe a downturn will affect the stock market. = 分析师认为低迷将影响股市。


虚拟语气:
If there were a downturn, we would need to cut costs. = 如果出现经济低迷，我们将需要削减成本。
It is vital that we prepare for a possible downturn. = 我们必须为可能的经济低迷做好准备。


被动语态:
The downturn was caused by rising interest rates. = 经济低迷是由利率上升引起的。
The company's poor performance was attributed to the recent downturn. = 公司业绩不佳归因于最近的经济低迷。


疑问句:

否定句:
There was no downturn in the technology sector. = 科技行业没有出现低迷。
The company did not experience a significant downturn. = 公司没有经历严重的经济低迷。
