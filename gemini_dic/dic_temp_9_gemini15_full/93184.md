ignominiously:/ɪɡˈnɒmɪniəsli/ | adj. | 可耻地；不光彩地 |  In a shameful or dishonorable way; disgracefully.  The politician was ignominiously defeated in the election.  这位政客在选举中可耻地落败了。 | 近义词：shamefully, disgracefully, dishonorably | 反义词：gloriously, honorably, triumphantly |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ignominiously fails his exams every year. = 他每年考试都可耻地失败。
They ignominiously lose the game every time they play. = 他们每次比赛都可耻地输了。


一般过去时:
The general ignominiously surrendered his army. = 将军可耻地投降了他的军队。
The thief ignominiously fled the scene of the crime. = 小偷可耻地逃离了犯罪现场。

The company will ignominiously collapse if it doesn't change its strategies. = 如果公司不改变策略，它将可耻地倒闭。


虚拟语气:

If she had told the truth, she would not have been ignominiously dismissed from her position. = 如果她说实话，她就不会被可耻地解雇。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
From Latin *ignominia ("disgrace, dishonor"), from in- ("not") + nomen ("name").  The sense is of being without a good name, or having a bad reputation.
