candour: /ˈkændər/ | n. |坦率；坦诚；直率 |  Honesty of expression; frankness; openness.  It implies a willingness to speak the truth, even if it is unpleasant or embarrassing. |


例句：

Her candour surprised everyone.  = 她的坦率让每个人都感到惊讶。

He spoke with refreshing candour about his past mistakes. = 他坦率地谈论了他过去的错误，令人耳目一新。


近义词：frankness, honesty, openness, forthrightness, sincerity


反义词：dishonesty, deception, hypocrisy, secretiveness


词源:

The word "candour" comes from the Old French word "candor," which in turn originates from the Latin word "candor," meaning "whiteness," "brightness," or "purity."  The connection lies in the association of purity and whiteness with honesty and openness.  The metaphorical extension from physical brightness to moral clarity is a common one in language.
