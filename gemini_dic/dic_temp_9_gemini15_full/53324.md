disaccept:/dɪsækˈsɛpt/ | vt. |拒绝接受；不认可|  To refuse to accept; to reject. The word is formed by adding the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") to "accept". It implies a stronger rejection than simply not accepting something; it suggests an active refusal or disapproval. |例句：The committee disaccepted his proposal due to its impracticality.  委员会由于他的提议不切实际而拒绝接受。


The university disaccepted his application because of his low grades. 大学因为他的低分而拒绝了他的申请。|近义词：reject, refuse, decline, repudiate |反义词：accept, approve, endorse |动词
