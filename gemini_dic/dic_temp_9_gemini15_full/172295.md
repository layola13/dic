put-ons:/ˈpʊtˌɒnz/ | n. | 欺骗；作伪；炫耀；戏弄；(衣服等的)装饰物 | A put-on is something that is deliberately intended to deceive or impress others, or a playful trick. It can also refer to something added for decoration or effect. | 例句：His whole story was a put-on.  他的整个故事都是骗人的。

例句：The elaborate costume was just a put-on for the party.  那件精心制作的服装只是派对上的装饰。


近义词：hoax, sham, deception, pretense, affectation

反义词：truth, genuineness, authenticity


词源:  The term "put-on" originates from the verb "put on," meaning to place something on or to pretend.  The noun form evolved to describe something that is artificially added or a false pretense.  The evolution reflects the figurative use of "put on" to describe the act of feigning or fabricating something.
