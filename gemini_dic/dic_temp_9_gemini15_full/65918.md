ex-pressing: /ɪkˈsprɛsɪŋ/ | v. | 表达；挤压；压制 | Expressing oneself is important for mental health. 表达自我对心理健康很重要。| 近义词： conveying, articulating, squeezing | 反义词： suppressing, concealing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He expresses his opinions freely. = 他自由地表达自己的意见。
She expresses her gratitude regularly. = 她定期表达她的感激之情。

一般过去时:
He expressed his concerns to the manager. = 他向经理表达了他的担忧。
She expressed a desire to travel. = 她表达了旅行的愿望。

现在完成时:
They have expressed their support for the project. = 他们已经表达了对该项目的支持。
He has expressed interest in the position. = 他已经表达了对这个职位的兴趣。

过去完成时:
She had already expressed her reservations before the meeting. = 会议之前她已经表达了她的保留意见。
They had expressed their disappointment earlier. = 他们之前已经表达了他们的失望。

将来时:
I will express my appreciation in writing. = 我将以书面形式表达我的感谢。
She will express her concerns at the next meeting. = 她将在下次会议上表达她的担忧。

虚拟语气:
If I could express myself better, I would. = 如果我能更好地表达自己，我会的。
I wish I could express my feelings more clearly. = 我希望我能更清晰地表达我的感受。

被动语态:
His views were expressed clearly. = 他的观点表达得很清楚。
The concerns were expressed to the appropriate authorities. = 这些担忧已向有关部门反映。

疑问句:
How does he express his love? = 他如何表达他的爱？
How are you expressing your feelings today? = 你今天如何表达你的感受？

否定句:
He didn't express any interest in the job. = 他对这份工作没有表示任何兴趣。
She couldn't express her sadness adequately. = 她无法充分表达她的悲伤。
