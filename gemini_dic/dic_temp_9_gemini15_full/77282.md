gat:/ɡæt/| n. vt.|闸门；水门；障碍；（非正式）枪；获得|A gate is a structure that can be opened and closed to control the passage of people or vehicles.  A gat is also slang for a gun.|例句：The farmer opened the gate to let the sheep out.  农民打开闸门让羊出去。|近义词：barrier, door, sluice|反义词：opening, access


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The gate opens at 8 am. = 闸门在早上8点打开。
The farmer closes the gate every night. = 农民每晚都关上闸门。


一般过去时:
The gate opened unexpectedly. = 闸门意外打开了。
He closed the gate after the sheep entered the field. = 羊进入田地后，他关上了闸门。


现在完成时:
They have opened the gate for the parade. = 他们为游行打开了闸门。
The gate has been closed since yesterday. = 闸门从昨天起就关上了。

The gate had already been opened when we arrived. = 我们到达时，闸门已经打开了。
He had closed the gate before the storm hit. =暴风雨来临之前，他已经关上了闸门。


将来时:
The gate will open at noon. = 闸门将在中午打开。

虚拟语气:
If the gate were open, we could go through. = 如果闸门开了，我们就能过去了。
I wish the gate were unlocked. = 我希望闸门没锁。


被动语态:
