recalibration:/ˌriːˌkæliˈbreɪʃən/ | n. |重新校准；再校正 |The act of recalibrating something; adjusting something to a standard or norm again.  Recalibration involves making adjustments to ensure accuracy or proper functioning.  It often implies a previous calibration that has drifted or become inaccurate. |例句：The scientist performed a recalibration of the instrument to ensure accurate measurements. 科学家对仪器进行了重新校准，以确保测量准确。

The GPS system required recalibration after the power surge.  GPS系统在电源浪涌后需要重新校准。|近义词： readjustment, recalculation, readjustment, re-evaluation |反义词：miscalibration, inaccuracy
