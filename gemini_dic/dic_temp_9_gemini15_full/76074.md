furo:/ˈfʊroʊ/| n. | furore | 狂怒；轰动；激烈的争论 |  A furore erupted over the government's new policy.  政府的新政策引发了轩然大波。 | 近义词：commotion, uproar, turmoil,  | 反义词：calm, peace, tranquility | 名词


USAGE->
This word is a noun, and therefore does not conjugate through tenses like verbs.  It does not have a passive voice or a negative form in the same way as a verb.  The usage demonstrates changes in context and sentence structure.


Example 1 (Simple Sentence): The furore surrounding the scandal was intense.  围绕丑闻的喧嚣非常激烈。

Example 2 (Complex Sentence):  Despite attempts to quell the furore, public anger continued to simmer. 尽管试图平息骚动，公众的愤怒仍在继续。


Example 3 (Sentence with prepositional phrase): The furore over the election results lasted for weeks. 选举结果引发的喧嚣持续了几周。

Example 4 (Sentence showing cause and effect): His controversial remarks caused a furore in the media. 他有争议的言论在媒体上引起轩然大波。


Example 5 (Sentence with an intensifier): An absolute furore broke out when the news was announced.  当新闻发布时，爆发了绝对的喧嚣。

Example 6 (Sentence using a synonym): The uproar over the decision was deafening.  关于这个决定的喧嚣震耳欲聋。
