intimater:/ˈɪntɪmeɪtər/| n. | 知己，密友；促成者 | A person who is very close and familiar to someone else; someone who brings about or facilitates something. | Example:  He was an intimater of the President and privy to many secrets. 他是总统的密友，知道许多秘密。|近义词：confidant, close friend, associate|反义词：stranger, outsider|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He intimates his feelings to his close friend. = 他向他的密友倾诉他的感情。
She intimates with her family members regularly. = 她经常与家人亲近。


一般过去时:
He intimated his displeasure to his boss. = 他向老板暗示了他的不满。
They intimated their intentions to the committee. = 他们向委员会暗示了他们的意图。


现在完成时:
She has intimated her support for the project. = 她已经暗示她支持这个项目。
He has intimated to me that he is leaving the company. = 他已经暗示我他要离开公司。


过去完成时:
He had intimated his retirement plans months before. = 他几个月前就暗示了他的退休计划。
They had intimated their concerns to the manager previously. = 他们之前已经向经理暗示了他们的担忧。


将来时:
She will intimate her decision at the meeting tomorrow. = 她将在明天的会议上宣布她的决定。
He will intimate his plans to his colleagues next week. = 他下周将向他的同事们透露他的计划。


虚拟语气:
If he intimated his concerns earlier, things might have been different. = 如果他早点暗示他的担忧，情况可能会有所不同。
I wish he had intimated his intentions more clearly. = 我希望他更清楚地表明他的意图。


被动语态:
His feelings were intimated to his closest friend. = 他的感情被告知了他最亲密的朋友。
The company's plans were intimated to the public through a press release. = 公司的计划通过新闻发布会向公众暗示。


疑问句:
Did he intimate anything about his future plans? = 他有没有暗示他未来的计划？
What did she intimate to you during the meeting? = 在会议上她向你暗示了什么？


否定句:
He didn't intimate his disapproval of the plan. = 他没有暗示他不赞成这个计划。
She didn't intimate her true feelings to anyone. = 她没有向任何人透露她的真实感受。
