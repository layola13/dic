macadams:/məˈkædəmz/ | n. | 麦卡丹路面；碎石路面 | A type of road surface made of small, closely packed stones, bound together with tar or other binding material.  Macadam roads are known for their durability and ability to withstand heavy traffic.  The term is derived from the name of John Loudon McAdam, a Scottish engineer who pioneered this type of road construction in the early 19th century. | Example: The old country roads were made of macadam.  这条古老的乡村道路是用碎石路面铺成的。 |近义词：pavement, roadway | 反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with modern asphalt or concrete roads. |名词
