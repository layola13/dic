rabbinist:/ˈræbɪnɪst/| n. |犹太教法学家；犹太教律法专家|A rabbinist is a scholar or expert in rabbinic Judaism, specializing in the study and interpretation of Jewish law and tradition.  They are deeply knowledgeable in the Talmud, halakha (Jewish law), and other rabbinic texts.  Their expertise often informs religious practice and decision-making within Jewish communities.|例句：The renowned rabbinist offered a compelling interpretation of the ancient text.  这位著名的犹太教法学家对古代文本作出了令人信服的解释。

The rabbinist's lecture illuminated the complexities of Jewish dietary laws.  这位犹太教律法专家的讲座阐明了犹太饮食法的复杂性。|近义词：Talmudist, rabbi (though not all rabbis are rabbinists)|反义词：None specifically antonymous, but perhaps someone who actively rejects rabbinic authority.|词性：名词
