/ˈkætɪɡɔːriz/ | n. | 类别，范畴 |  The process of classifying things into categories is essential for organizing information.  对事物进行分类对于组织信息至关重要。 | 近义词：class, group, type | 反义词：chaos, disorder | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This store categorizes its products by brand. =这家商店按品牌对产品进行分类。
We categorize our expenses into housing, food, and entertainment. =我们将我们的开支分为住房、食品和娱乐几类。

一般过去时:
The librarian categorized the new books by subject. = 图书管理员按主题对新书进行了分类。
Scientists categorized the fossils according to their age. = 科学家们根据化石的年代对它们进行了分类。

现在完成时:
Researchers have categorized hundreds of plant species. = 研究人员已经对数百种植物进行了分类。
She has categorized all the emails into folders. = 她已将所有电子邮件分类到不同的文件夹中。

过去完成时:
They had categorized the data before running the analysis. = 他们在运行分析之前已经对数据进行了分类。
The museum had categorized its artifacts by region before the exhibit. = 博物馆在展览之前已按地区对文物进行了分类。

将来时:
We will categorize the responses according to their sentiment. = 我们将根据情绪对回复进行分类。
The team will categorize the data by demographic information. = 该团队将按人口统计信息对数据进行分类。

虚拟语气:
If we categorized the data differently, the results might be different. = 如果我们对数据进行不同的分类，结果可能会不同。
I wish I had categorized my notes more effectively. = 我真希望我更有效地整理了我的笔记。

被动语态:
The items were categorized by size and weight. = 这些物品按大小和重量进行了分类。
The data is being categorized using a new algorithm. = 数据正在使用一种新的算法进行分类。

疑问句:
How are these items categorized? = 这些物品是如何分类的？
By what criteria are the students categorized? = 学生们是根据什么标准进行分类的？

否定句:
The system did not categorize the document correctly. = 系统没有正确地对文档进行分类。
He hasn't categorized his thoughts yet. = 他还没整理好他的思绪。
