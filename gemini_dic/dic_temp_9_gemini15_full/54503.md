disir:/ˈdɪzɪər/| n.|欲望，渴望 |The word "disir" is an Old Norse word meaning "desire," "wish," or "fate." It refers to female figures in Norse mythology associated with prophecy, fate, and the power to influence events.  These figures are often depicted as possessing both benevolent and malevolent aspects, capable of granting or denying wishes and shaping destiny.|例句：The hero sought the favor of the disir to guide his quest. 英雄寻求女命运女神的恩惠来指引他的旅程。|近义词：desire, longing, yearning, wish|反义词：aversion, dislike, indifference|名词
