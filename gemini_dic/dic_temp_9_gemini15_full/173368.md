quasi-disgustedly: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ dɪsˈɡʌstɪdli/ | adv. |几乎厌恶地，似是而非地厌恶地 |  The word combines "quasi-" (meaning seemingly, apparently, or to a certain extent) with the adverb "disgustedly" (expressing disgust). It describes an action or expression that shows a degree of disgust but not full, complete disgust.  It suggests a subtle or hesitant display of aversion. | 例句：He looked quasi-disgustedly at the overflowing trash can. 他几乎厌恶地看了一眼溢出的垃圾桶。

She ate the questionable food quasi-disgustedly, picking at it with her fork. 她几乎厌恶地吃着那可疑的食物，用叉子拨弄着它。


近义词：slightly disgustedly, faintly nauseatedly, with mild aversion, with a hint of disgust | 反义词：enthusiastically, delightedly, eagerly


词性：副词
