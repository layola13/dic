nonromanticism: /ˌnɒnroʊˈmæntɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 非浪漫主义；反浪漫主义 |  The rejection or opposition to the principles and characteristics of Romanticism, often emphasizing reason, objectivity, and realism. It encompasses various artistic, literary, and philosophical movements that reacted against the perceived excesses or limitations of Romanticism.  | Example: The rise of realism in the late 19th century can be seen as a form of nonromanticism.  非19世纪后期的现实主义的兴起可以被看作是一种非浪漫主义。| 近义词：realism, classicism, anti-romanticism | 反义词：romanticism |  名词
