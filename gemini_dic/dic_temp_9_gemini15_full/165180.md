preinflections:/ˌpriːɪnˈflekʃənz/| n. |词缀前缀| Prefixes and other elements added to the beginning of a word before inflectional affixes are added.  Preinflections are a part of morphology, the study of word formation. They are often used to modify the meaning or function of the base word.  For example, in the word "unbreakable," "un-" is a preinflection that changes the meaning of "breakable" to its opposite. |The analysis of preinflections is crucial for understanding the complex processes involved in word formation.|近义词：prefixes (in some contexts), derivational affixes|反义词：suffixes (in the sense of being added to the end of a word)
