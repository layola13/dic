headline:/ˈhɛdlaɪn/ | pron.|名词|标题，新闻标题|A headline is a title at the top of a newspaper article or news item.  It is designed to be concise and attention-grabbing, summarizing the main point of the story. |Example: The headline read, "Local Team Wins Championship!"  这个标题写着：“当地球队赢得冠军！”|近义词：title, heading, caption|反义词：body (of text)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The headline announces a new policy. = 标题宣布了一项新政策。
This headline summarizes the main points. = 这个标题总结了要点。


一般过去时:
The headline declared victory for the opposition. = 标题宣布反对派获胜。
Yesterday's headline focused on the economic crisis. = 昨天的标题关注的是经济危机。


现在完成时:
The headline has already caught the attention of many readers. = 标题已经吸引了许多读者的注意。
The newspaper has published several headlines on this issue. = 这家报纸已经发表了几篇关于这个问题的标题。


过去完成时:
The headline had already been written before the event took place. = 事件发生之前标题就已经写好了。
By the time I arrived, the headline had been changed. = 我到达时，标题已经被改了。


将来时:
The headline will announce the winner of the election. = 标题将宣布选举的获胜者。
The newspaper will publish a headline about the upcoming storm. = 报纸将刊登一篇关于即将到来的暴风雨的标题。


虚拟语气:
If the headline were more interesting, more people would read the article. = 如果标题更有趣，更多的人会阅读这篇文章。
I wish the headline were less sensational. = 我希望标题不那么耸人听闻。


被动语态:
The headline was written by a seasoned journalist. = 标题是一位经验丰富的记者写的。
The headline is being changed due to new information. = 由于新的信息，标题正在被修改。


疑问句:

否定句:
