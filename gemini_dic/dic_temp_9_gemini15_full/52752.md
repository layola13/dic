dilatabilities:/ˌdaɪlətəˈbɪlətiz/| n.| 可扩张性，扩张能力 | The ability of something to be dilated or expanded.  This term is rarely used in everyday language and is more common in scientific or technical contexts, particularly in relation to materials science or medicine.  | Example: The dilatabilities of the blood vessels were measured to assess the patient's vascular health.  血管的扩张能力被测量以评估患者的血管健康。 |近义词：expandability, extensibility |反义词：incompressibility, inelasticity
