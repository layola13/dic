presumptively:/prɪˈzʌmptɪvli/| adv.|推定地；据推测；大概|  It is an adverb that means something is assumed to be true based on the available evidence, but not definitively proven. It suggests a degree of uncertainty or probability.  |例句：Presumptively, the suspect is guilty, but we need more evidence before we can convict him.  推定地说，嫌疑犯有罪，但我们需要更多证据才能定罪他。|例句：The meeting was presumptively canceled due to the storm.  由于暴风雨，会议据推测被取消了。|近义词：presumably, supposedly, apparently, likely|反义词：certainly, definitively, conclusively|副词
