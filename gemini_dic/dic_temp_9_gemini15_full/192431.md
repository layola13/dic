scrounge:/ˈskraʊndʒ/| vt. & n.| 搜刮；蹭吃蹭喝；勉强取得 | To search for and collect usable items from discarded materials; to obtain something by begging or borrowing. |例句：He scrounged around for some spare change. 他到处搜寻一些零钱。

例句：She scrounged a meal from her neighbour. 她向邻居蹭了一顿饭。|近义词：beg, cadge, sponge, mooch |反义词：give, donate, contribute |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scrounges food from his friends. = 他向朋友们蹭吃的。
They scrounge for information online. = 他们在网上搜集信息。

一般过去时:
I scrounged a cigarette from him. = 我向他讨了一根烟。
She scrounged up enough money for the bus. = 她勉强凑够坐公交车的钱。

现在完成时:
He has scrounged a living from odd jobs. = 他靠打零工勉强维持生计。
They have scrounged together enough materials for the project. = 他们勉强凑齐了项目所需的材料。

过去完成时:
She had scrounged a lift to the party. = 她搭便车去了派对。
They had scrounged all the food before we arrived. = 我们到达之前，他们已经把所有的食物都搜刮光了。

将来时:
I will scrounge some money from my parents. = 我将向父母要一些钱。
We will scrounge around for a solution. = 我们将四处寻找解决办法。

虚拟语气:
If I needed money, I would scrounge it from my friends. = 如果我需要钱，我会向朋友们借。
I wish I hadn't scrounged that old chair; it's falling apart. = 我真希望我没有捡那个旧椅子，它快要散架了。

被动语态:
A meal was scrounged from the neighbours. = 从邻居那里蹭到了一顿饭。
The money was scrounged up from various sources. = 钱是从各个渠道凑集来的。

疑问句:

否定句:
I didn't scrounge anything from anyone. = 我没有向任何人讨要任何东西。
They didn't scrounge a single penny. = 他们一分钱都没弄到。

词源:
The word "scrounge" likely originated in the early 20th century, possibly from a dialectal or slang term.  Its exact etymology is uncertain, but some theories suggest it might be related to words implying scraping or foraging for food.
