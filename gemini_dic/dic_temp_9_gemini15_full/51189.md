destabilizing: /ˌdiːˈsteɪbəlaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 不稳定的；破坏稳定的；动摇的 | Making something unstable or unsteady; causing a system, situation, or government to become unstable.  | The destabilizing effect of the political crisis led to widespread unrest.  政治危机的破坏性影响导致了广泛的动乱。 The constant changes in the company's leadership had a destabilizing effect on morale. 公司领导层的不断变化对士气产生了破坏性影响。 | destabilizing, disruptive, unsettling | stabilizing, strengthening, reinforcing |  形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant criticism is destabilizing the team. =  持续的批评正在破坏团队的稳定。
This policy is destabilizing the economy. =这项政策正在扰乱经济。

一般过去时:
The sudden news destabilized the market. =突如其来的消息扰乱了市场。
His actions destabilized the entire project. =他的行为使整个项目动荡不安。

现在完成时:
The government's reforms have destabilized the region. =政府的改革已经使该地区不稳定。
Years of conflict have destabilized the country. =多年的冲突使这个国家动荡不安。

过去完成时:
The company had already been destabilized before the merger. =在合并之前，公司就已经不稳定了。
The rumors had destabilized the public's confidence. =谣言已经动摇了公众的信心。

将来时:
The upcoming elections will destabilize the country further. =即将到来的选举将进一步使国家动荡不安。
The new law may destabilize the current system. =新法可能会扰乱现行制度。

虚拟语气:
If the government continues this policy, it will destabilize the entire region. =如果政府继续这项政策，它将使整个地区动荡不安。
I wish the situation weren't so destabilizing. =我希望情况不要这么不稳定。

被动语态:
The region was destabilized by the civil war. =该地区被内战扰乱了。
The market was destabilized by the economic downturn. =市场因经济衰退而动荡不安。

疑问句:
Is this new technology destabilizing the industry? =这项新技术是否正在扰乱行业？
What factors are destabilizing the political situation? =哪些因素正在扰乱政治局势？

否定句:
The reforms were not destabilizing, but rather strengthening the economy. =这些改革并没有破坏经济，而是增强了经济。
The new system is not destabilizing; it is quite stable. =新系统并非不稳定；它相当稳定。


PHRASE->

highly destabilizing = 极度不稳定
potentially destabilizing = 潜在的不稳定
destabilizing effect = 破坏性影响
