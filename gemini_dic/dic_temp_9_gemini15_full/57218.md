downer:/ˈdaʊnər/ | n. adj. |令人沮丧的人或事，令人扫兴的事物；令人沮丧的 | A person or thing that causes unhappiness or disappointment; something depressing; depressing. |例句：The bad news was a real downer.  坏消息真是令人沮丧。
The constant rain was a downer.  持续的雨天真扫兴。|近义词：depressant, disappointment, letdown |反义词：uplifter, booster, joy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
That movie is a real downer. = 那部电影真令人沮丧。
This weather is such a downer. = 这样的天气真让人扫兴。

一般过去时:
The party was a bit of a downer. = 聚会有点扫兴。
His rejection was a downer for him. = 他的被拒绝让他很沮丧。

现在完成时:
The whole day has been a downer. = 整天都让人很沮丧。
This news has been a downer for everyone. = 这个消息让每个人都很沮丧。

过去完成时:
The news had been a downer all week. = 这个消息一整周都让人很沮丧。
I had been feeling like a downer since I lost my job. = 自从失业后，我一直感觉很沮丧。

将来时:
The meeting will probably be a downer. = 会议可能会很令人沮丧。
I think tomorrow will be a downer. = 我想明天会很令人沮丧。

虚拟语气:
If only the news wasn't such a downer. = 但愿这个消息不会这么令人沮丧。
I wish that meeting hadn't been such a downer. = 我真希望那次会议不会那么令人沮丧。

被动语态:
The project was considered a downer by many. = 许多人认为这个项目很令人沮丧。
The situation is being viewed as a major downer. = 这种情况被认为是令人非常沮丧的。

疑问句:
Is this party a downer? = 这个派对很扫兴吗？
Was the movie a downer? = 这部电影令人沮丧吗？

否定句:
The concert wasn't a downer at all. = 音乐会一点也不令人沮丧。
The news wasn't a downer, actually, it was quite uplifting. = 事实上，这个消息并没有令人沮丧，反而令人振奋。
