oversay:/ˈoʊvərseɪ/| vt.|说得过多；过分强调；说得太长|To say something more than is necessary or appropriate; to exaggerate or emphasize excessively.|例句：He oversaid his welcome and stayed too long. 他待的时间太长，不受欢迎了。|例句：Don't oversay the importance of this meeting; it's just a routine check-in.|不要过分强调这次会议的重要性；这只是一次例行的签到。|近义词：overstate, exaggerate, overemphasize|反义词：understate, downplay|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He oversays his accomplishments in interviews. = 他在面试中夸大了自己的成就。
She oversays the risks involved in the project. = 她夸大了项目中涉及的风险。


一般过去时:
He oversaid himself during the debate. = 他在辩论中说得太多了。
She oversaid the deadline for the report. = 她把报告的截止日期说晚了。


现在完成时:
He has oversaid his welcome at several parties. = 他在几次聚会上都待得太久了。
She has oversaid the potential benefits of the new technology. = 她夸大了这项新技术的潜在好处。


过去完成时:

She had oversaid the amount of work required for the project. = 她夸大了该项目所需的工作量。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
