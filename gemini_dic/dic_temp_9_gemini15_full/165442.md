/priːˈledʒəndz/| n.|先祖传说，前传奇|A prelegends refers to stories, myths, or legends that predate a known or established mythology or historical narrative.  These are often fragmented, obscure, or only partially understood, representing the earliest forms of storytelling within a culture.|Example:  The discovery of the ancient cave paintings offered a glimpse into the prelegends of the region.  =  古老洞穴壁画的发现让我们得以一窥该地区先祖传说的景象。|近义词：ancient myths, early legends, primal stories|反义词：modern legends, contemporary myths|名词
