reestablish: /ˌriːɪˈstæblɪʃ/ | vt. |重建；重新确立；恢复；使…复兴 | To establish something again after it has been lost or destroyed.  |例句：The government is trying to reestablish order after the riots. 政府正在试图在暴乱后恢复秩序。

例句：They are working hard to reestablish the company's reputation. 他们正在努力重建公司的声誉。


近义词：restore, reinstate, renew, revive |反义词：destroy, abolish, dismantle |词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reestablish diplomatic relations every year. = 他们每年都重建外交关系。
The company reestablishes its market share gradually. = 公司逐渐恢复市场份额。

一般过去时:
The government reestablished control over the region. = 政府重新控制了该地区。
She reestablished contact with her old friends. = 她与老朋友们重新取得了联系。

现在完成时:
They have reestablished their position in the market. = 他们已经重新确立了他们在市场上的地位。
We have reestablished the old traditions. = 我们已经恢复了古老的传统。

过去完成时:
They had reestablished their business before the crisis hit. = 危机来临之前，他们已经重建了他们的生意。
She had reestablished her confidence by then. = 到那时，她已经恢复了自信。

将来时:
The team will reestablish its dominance in the league. = 该队将在联赛中重新确立其统治地位。
We will reestablish communication as soon as possible. = 我们将尽快恢复联系。

虚拟语气:
If they had acted sooner, they could have reestablished the bridge. = 如果他们早点行动，他们就能重建那座桥。
I wish we could reestablish the old friendship. = 我希望我们能够恢复旧日的友谊。

被动语态:
Order was reestablished by the police. = 秩序由警方恢复。
The old building was reestablished as a museum. = 这座旧建筑被重建成博物馆。

疑问句:
Can we reestablish the lost data? = 我们能恢复丢失的数据吗？
Will the company be able to reestablish itself after the scandal? = 丑闻过后，这家公司还能东山再起吗？

否定句:
They failed to reestablish contact with the missing person. = 他们未能与失踪人员取得联系。
The attempt to reestablish the old system failed. = 试图恢复旧制度的尝试失败了。
