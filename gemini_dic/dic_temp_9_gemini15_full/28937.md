carronade:/ˌkærəˈneɪd/| n. |卡罗纳德炮 |A short-barreled cannon, typically of large bore, used in the late 18th and early 19th centuries, especially on ships.  It fired a large, heavy shot at a relatively low velocity.|The carronade's short barrel meant it had a lower range but could inflict devastating damage at close quarters.|近义词：cannon, short gun |反义词：long gun
