intransigentism:/ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒənˌtɪzəm/| n. |顽固; 不妥协;  抵抗 |Intransigentism refers to the refusal to compromise, negotiate, or concede; an unwillingness to change one's views or beliefs, often in the face of opposing arguments or evidence. It often implies a rigid and inflexible adherence to one's own position. |The intransigentism of the negotiating parties led to a deadlock.  顽固的谈判双方导致了僵局。  The government's intransigentism on the issue of tax reform angered many citizens. 政府在税制改革问题上的顽固态度激怒了许多公民。|近义词：obstinacy, stubbornness, inflexibility, rigidity |反义词：compromise, flexibility, adaptability, conciliation
