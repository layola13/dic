remonstrates:/rɪˈmɒnstreɪts/| v. |抗议；提出异议；反驳；规劝；告诫|To reason with (someone) against something; to protest or complain strongly.  It often implies a forceful and reasoned argument, rather than just a simple complaint.|例句：The workers remonstrated against the unfair treatment. 工人们抗议不公平待遇。

例句：She remonstrated with him about his reckless driving. 她规劝他不要鲁莽驾驶。


近义词：protest, object, complain, argue, expostulate, plead


反义词：acquiesce, comply, agree


词性:动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He remonstrates against the new policy. 他反对这项新政策。
They frequently remonstrate with their children about their behavior. 他们经常就孩子们的行为规劝他们。


一般过去时:
She remonstrated with the manager about the poor service. 她就糟糕的服务向经理提出抗议。
The students remonstrated against the increase in tuition fees. 学生们抗议学费上涨。


现在完成时:
He has remonstrated several times, but nothing has changed. 他已经抗议过好几次了，但什么都没变。
They have remonstrated against the decision, but to no avail. 他们已经对这个决定提出异议，但没有用。


过去完成时:
She had remonstrated with him before, but he had ignored her. 她以前就劝过他，但他没理她。
The workers had remonstrated repeatedly, but their concerns were dismissed. 工人们一再抗议，但他们的担忧被忽视了。


将来时:
He will remonstrate with his boss about the unfair workload. 他将向老板抗议不公平的工作量。
They will remonstrate against the proposed changes to the law. 他们将反对拟议中的法律修改。


虚拟语气:
If he had remonstrated earlier, the situation might have been different. 如果他早点抗议，情况可能就不同了。
I wish I had remonstrated more forcefully. 我希望我当时能更强有力地抗议。


被动语态:
His arguments were remonstrated against by many people. 许多人反对他的论点。
The unfair decision was remonstrated against by the public. 公众对不公平的决定表示抗议。


疑问句:
Did he remonstrate with his supervisor about the project? 他是否就这个项目向他的主管提出抗议？
Will they remonstrate against the new regulations? 他们会反对新的规章制度吗？


否定句:
He did not remonstrate against the decision. 他没有反对这个决定。
They did not remonstrate with her about her mistake. 他们没有就她的错误规劝她。


PHRASE->
remonstrate against: 反对
remonstrate with: 向...提出抗议
remonstrate vehemently: 强烈抗议
remonstrate politely: 礼貌地抗议
remonstrate in vain: 徒劳地抗议


词源:
From Old French *remonstrer, from Latin remonstrāre "to show again, demonstrate," from re- "again" + monstrāre "to show."
