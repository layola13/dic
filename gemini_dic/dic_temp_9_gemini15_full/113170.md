lurched:/lɜːtʃt/| v.| 蹒跚；突然倾斜；猛冲；|To make a sudden, unsteady movement or lurch.  It often implies a lack of control or balance.|例句：The old car lurched violently as it started.  这辆旧车启动时剧烈地颠簸了一下。
The ship lurched dangerously in the storm. 船在暴风雨中危险地摇晃。|近义词：stagger, sway, jolt, pitch|反义词：glide, flow, sail


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The old bus lurches violently around corners. = 这辆旧公共汽车在拐弯时剧烈摇晃。
He often lurches when he walks. = 他走路时经常摇晃。


一般过去时:
The boat lurched to the side, throwing everyone off balance. = 船向一边倾斜，使每个人都失去平衡。
The train lurched suddenly, causing several passengers to fall. = 火车突然猛冲了一下，导致几名乘客跌倒。


现在完成时:
The car has lurched several times since we started the journey. = 自从我们开始旅程以来，汽车已经好几次猛冲了。
The economy has lurched from one crisis to another. = 经济一直在一次又一次的危机中摇摆不定。


过去完成时:
The building had lurched alarmingly before finally collapsing. = 这座建筑物在最终倒塌之前已经摇晃得厉害了。
The horse had lurched violently before the rider fell off. = 马剧烈地猛冲了一下，然后骑手掉了下来。


将来时:
The ship will lurch violently in the upcoming storm. = 这艘船将在即将到来的暴风雨中剧烈摇晃。
I think the old house will lurch and collapse soon. = 我认为这座旧房子很快就会摇晃然后倒塌。


虚拟语气:
If the car had lurched any more violently, we would have crashed. = 如果汽车再剧烈地摇晃一下，我们就会撞车了。
If the ship had lurched to the left, it would have struck the rocks. = 如果船向左倾斜，它就会撞到岩石上。


被动语态:
The passengers were lurched forward when the brakes were suddenly applied. = 当刹车突然踩下时，乘客们被向前猛冲。
The small boat was lurched violently by a sudden gust of wind. = 一阵突如其来的狂风使小船剧烈摇晃。


疑问句:
Did the building lurch before the earthquake? = 地震前建筑物是否摇晃过？
Will the rollercoaster lurch violently during the ride? = 过山车在行驶过程中会剧烈摇晃吗？


否定句:
The car didn't lurch at all during the drive. = 汽车在行驶过程中根本没有摇晃。
The plane didn't lurch, despite the turbulence. = 尽管有气流，飞机也没有摇晃。
