fawningness:/ˈfɔːnɪŋnəs/| n. |谄媚；奉承；巴结 |The excessive display of flattery and subservience; obsequious behavior.  It suggests insincere praise given to win favor. |例句：Her fawningness towards the boss was sickening.  她的对老板的谄媚令人作呕。

例句：The excessive fawningness of the courtiers was evident to all. 朝臣的过度谄媚显而易见。


近义词：obsequiousness, sycophancy, flattery, servility

反义词：frankness, honesty, sincerity, straightforwardness

词源: 源自动词fawn，意为“讨好，奉承”。  Ultimately derived from Old English *fehan, meaning "to seize, catch."  The sense shifted to "to fawn" through a sense of eager, almost grasping, attempts to please.
