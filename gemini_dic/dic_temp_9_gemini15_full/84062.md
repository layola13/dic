half-great:/hɑːf ɡreɪt/| adj.| 半伟大的，相当不错的 |  Describes something that is good but not excellent;  partially great.  It implies a degree of greatness that is less than complete or perfect.  The term is informal and often used in a humorous or self-deprecating way. | Example: "My presentation was half-great; some parts were excellent, others needed more work." | 近义词：fairly good, pretty good, decent, respectable | 反义词：terrible, awful, dreadful, poor
