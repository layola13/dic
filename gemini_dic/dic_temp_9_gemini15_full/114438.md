The word "maked" does not exist in standard English.  It's likely a misspelling of "made," which I will analyze below.


made:/meɪd/ | v. | 做，制造，使得 | The past tense and past participle of "make."  It indicates the completion of an action of creating, producing, or causing something to happen. |  Examples: She made a cake. (她做了一个蛋糕。) He made a mistake. (他犯了一个错误。) |近义词：created, produced, constructed, formed | 反义词：destroyed, ruined, undone


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I make coffee every morning. = 我每天早上都做咖啡。
They make toys in that factory. = 那个工厂生产玩具。


一般过去时:
I made a delicious meal last night. = 我昨晚做了一顿美味的饭菜。
She made a beautiful dress for the party. = 她为派对做了一件漂亮的裙子。


现在完成时:
I have made several attempts to contact him. = 我已经尝试几次联系他了。
They have made significant progress on the project. = 他们在这个项目上取得了显著的进展。


过去完成时:
I had made the decision before he called. = 他打电话之前我已经做出了决定。
She had made all the arrangements for the trip. = 她已经为这次旅行做好了所有的安排。


将来时:
I will make a reservation for dinner. = 我将会预订晚餐。
They will make a final decision next week. = 他们下周将做出最终决定。


虚拟语气:
If I had made a different choice, things would be different now. = 如果我做了不同的选择，现在情况就会不一样了。
I wish I had made more effort. = 我希望我付出了更多努力。


被动语态:
The cake was made with fresh ingredients. = 这个蛋糕是用新鲜的食材做的。
The decision was made by the committee. = 这个决定是由委员会做出的。


疑问句:
Did you make the bed? = 你铺床了吗？
Who made this mess? = 谁弄的这摊烂事？


否定句:
I didn't make a sound. = 我一声不吭。
They didn't make it to the meeting on time. = 他们没有按时参加会议。



PHRASE->
make up: 弥补，编造
make out: 理解，辨认
make do: 将就
make sense: 合情合理
make a difference: 有影响


词源:
The word "make" has Old English roots, deriving from the Proto-Germanic *makōn.  Its history traces back to even older Indo-European roots related to shaping or forming.
