creeky:/ˈkriːki/| adj.| 嘎吱作响的；摇摇欲坠的；破旧的 |Creaky describes something that makes a creaking sound, often because it is old, worn, or poorly maintained. It can also imply a sense of instability or fragility.  |例句：The old wooden floorboards were creaky underfoot.  老木地板踩上去嘎吱作响。

The creaky chair threatened to collapse.  那把摇摇欲坠的椅子随时可能塌掉。|近义词：squeaky, rickety, shaky, dilapidated |反义词：sturdy, solid, stable, firm

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The old door creaks every time someone opens it. = 每当有人开门时，旧门就会发出吱吱响。
This chair creaks when I sit on it. = 我坐在椅子上时，它会发出吱吱响。


一般过去时:
The floorboards creaked as I walked across the room. = 我穿过房间时，地板嘎吱作响。
The old house creaked ominously in the wind. = 老房子在风中发出不祥的嘎吱声。


现在完成时:
That old rocking chair has creaked for years. = 那把旧摇椅已经吱吱响了好多年了。
The hinges have creaked since the last rain. = 自从上次下雨以来，铰链就一直在吱吱作响。


过去完成时:
The stairs had creaked before I even stepped on them. = 我还没踩上去，楼梯就先嘎吱作响了。
The floorboards had creaked all night, disturbing my sleep. = 地板整夜都在嘎吱作响，扰乱了我的睡眠。


将来时:
The roof will probably creak in the storm. = 屋顶可能在暴风雨中发出吱吱响。
I think those stairs will creak when you go upstairs. = 我认为当你上楼时，那些楼梯会发出吱吱响。


虚拟语气:
If the door creaked less, it would be less annoying. = 如果门吱吱响的声音小一些，就不会那么烦人了。
I wish this chair wouldn't creak so loudly. = 我希望这把椅子不要发出这么大的吱吱响。


被动语态:
The old house was creaked by the wind. = 老房子被风吹得嘎吱作响。
The floorboards are creaked by the weight of the furniture. = 地板被家具的重量压得嘎吱作响。(This passive voice usage is less common.)


疑问句:
Does the floor creak when you walk on it? = 你走在地板上时，它会嘎吱作响吗？
Why does the chair creak so much? = 为什么这把椅子吱吱响得这么厉害？


否定句:
The new floor doesn't creak at all. = 新地板一点也不吱吱响。
The bed didn't creak when she got in. = 她躺上床时，床并没有发出吱吱响。
