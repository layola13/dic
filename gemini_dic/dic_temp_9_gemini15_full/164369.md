preemotion:/ˌpriːɪˈmoʊʃən/| n. | 预感，先兆情绪 | A feeling or emotion experienced before the event or situation that causes it.  It's a subtle anticipation of an emotion that is yet to fully manifest.  | Example: She felt a preemotion of anxiety before the job interview. 她面试前感到一阵焦虑的预感。 | 近义词：foreboding, presentiment, apprehension | 反义词：calmness, equanimity


USAGE->

He feels a preemotion of joy. = 他感到一阵喜悦的预感。
She experiences preemotions of sadness before family gatherings. = 她每次家族聚会前都会感到悲伤的预感。
