non-cooperator:/ˌnɑːnkoʊˈɒpəreɪtər/| n. |不合作的人；不合作方|A person or entity that refuses to cooperate or participate in a collaborative effort.  This term often implies a deliberate and conscious refusal to work together, potentially for selfish reasons or to obstruct progress.|例句：The non-cooperator held up the entire project by refusing to share vital information.  不合作的人拒绝分享重要信息，从而阻碍了整个项目。

The team struggled to reach a consensus because of the influence of a single non-cooperator. 由于一个不合作者的影响，团队难以达成共识。|近义词：obstructionist, resister, defector, renegade|反义词：collaborator, cooperator, team player|词性 n.


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like a verb.  Therefore, tense changes are not applicable.  However, it can be used in various sentence structures.


Example sentences demonstrating different sentence structures:

Simple Sentence: The non-cooperator was isolated from the group. 不合作的人被孤立于群体之外。

Complex Sentence: Because of the non-cooperator's actions, the meeting ended prematurely. 由于不合作者的行为，会议提前结束了。

Compound Sentence: The team tried to persuade the non-cooperator, but he remained unmoved; therefore, the project stalled. 团队试图说服不合作的人，但他依然不动摇；因此，项目停滞不前。


PHRASE->
There aren't common idiomatic phrases directly using "non-cooperator."  However, phrases describing the actions or characteristics of a non-cooperator are common:

refusal to cooperate: 拒绝合作
lack of cooperation: 缺乏合作
obstructing progress: 阻碍进步
working against the team: 与团队对抗
undermining efforts: 破坏努力
