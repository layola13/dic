scorner:/ˈskɔːrnər/| n. |嘲笑者；蔑视者 | A scorner is a person who shows contempt or derision towards someone or something.  They often express their disdain openly and with a lack of respect. |He was a scorner of all things traditional. 他是个蔑视所有传统事物的人。|  mocker, jeerer, ridiculer |admirer, supporter, respecter |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The scorner laughs at the misfortunes of others. = 嘲笑者嘲笑别人的不幸。
He scorners those who disagree with him. = 他蔑视那些不同意他的人。


一般过去时:
The crowd scorned the politician's empty promises. = 人群嘲笑这位政治家的空洞承诺。
She scorned his advances. = 她蔑视他的求爱。


现在完成时:
He has scorned all attempts at reconciliation. = 他一直蔑视所有和解的尝试。
They have scorned the advice of experts. = 他们一直轻视专家的建议。


过去完成时:
She had scorned his offer before he even finished speaking. = 他话还没说完她就轻蔑地拒绝了他的提议。
They had scorned the warnings, and now they faced the consequences. = 他们之前无视警告，现在他们面临着后果。


将来时:
They will scorn his efforts to help. = 他们将轻视他帮助的努力。
She will scorn any suggestion of compromise. = 她将鄙视任何妥协的建议。


虚拟语气:
If he were a better person, he wouldn't scorn others so easily. = 如果他是一个更好的人，他不会那么轻易地蔑视别人。
I wish he wouldn't scorn my ideas. = 我希望他不鄙视我的想法。


被动语态:
His ideas were scorned by the committee. = 他的想法被委员会所蔑视。
Their efforts were scorned by the public. = 他们的努力被公众所轻视。


疑问句:

否定句:

She didn't scorn his apology. = 她没有蔑视他的道歉。
