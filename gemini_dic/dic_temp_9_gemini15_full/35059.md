closeout:/ˈkloʊsaʊt/| n., v.| 清仓甩卖；结清；结束 |

英文解析:As a noun, "closeout" refers to a sale of goods at reduced prices, typically to clear out remaining stock. As a verb, it means to conclude or settle something, such as an account or a business deal.  It can also mean to eliminate or remove something.


例句:

(Noun):
The department store is having a huge closeout sale on summer clothing. = 这家百货公司正在举行夏季服装的大规模清仓甩卖。

The closeout of the project was delayed due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，项目的结算是被推迟了。


(Verb):
We need to closeout this account before the end of the fiscal year. = 我们需要在财政年度结束前结清这个账户。

The company decided to closeout the unprofitable branch. = 公司决定关闭那个无利可图的分支机构。


近义词: (Noun) clearance sale, liquidation sale, going-out-of-business sale; (Verb) conclude, finalize, settle, terminate, eliminate


反义词: (Noun)  new stock, full price; (Verb) begin, start, initiate, continue


词性: noun, verb
