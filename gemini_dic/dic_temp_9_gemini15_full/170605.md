pseudo-European:/ˌsuːdoʊˈjʊərəpiːən/| adj. |伪欧洲的；假欧洲的；模仿欧洲的 |  Appearing or claiming to be European, but lacking genuine European characteristics or authenticity.  It often implies an artificial or superficial imitation of European culture, style, or values.  The term can be used to describe things like architecture, design, or even cultural practices. |例句：The building's pseudo-European facade was a poor imitation of true European architecture. 这座建筑物伪欧洲风格的正面是真正欧洲建筑的拙劣模仿。

The restaurant's menu, with its pseudo-European names for dishes, was ultimately just American food. 这家餐厅的菜单，用伪欧洲的名字来命名菜肴，最终只是美国食物。 |近义词：affected, artificial, imitation, sham |反义词：authentic, genuine, real, true

词源:
The word "pseudo-European" is a compound word.  "Pseudo-" is a prefix derived from the Greek word "pseudēs" (ψευδής), meaning "false" or "sham." "European" refers to Europe and its culture.  Therefore, "pseudo-European" literally means "falsely European" or "imitating European."
