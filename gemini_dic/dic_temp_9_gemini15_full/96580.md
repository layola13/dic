induces:/ɪnˈdʒuːs/ | vt. | 引导；导致；引起；诱导；劝诱 | To persuade someone to do something or to cause something to happen. |

例句：
The doctor induced labor to avoid complications.  医生为了避免并发症而诱发了分娩。

The low air pressure induced heavy rainfall. 低气压导致了强降雨。


近义词：cause, bring about, lead to, persuade, influence

反义词：prevent, hinder, deter


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The medicine induces sleep.  这种药能催眠。
Stress often induces headaches. 压力常常会导致头痛。

一般过去时:
The advertisement induced many people to buy the product. 这则广告诱使许多人购买了该产品。
His speech induced a feeling of hope in the audience. 他的演讲在听众中引起了一种希望的感觉。

现在完成时:
His actions have induced a great deal of controversy. 他的行为引起了很大的争议。
The heat has induced a drought in the region. 炎热导致了该地区干旱。

过去完成时:
The evidence had induced the jury to reach a guilty verdict. 证据已促使陪审团作出有罪判决。
The initial shock had induced a period of silence. 最初的震惊导致了一段时间的沉默。

将来时:
The new policy will induce economic growth. 新政策将促进经济增长。
The upcoming election will induce significant political changes.  即将到来的选举将引起重大的政治变化。

虚拟语气:
If the weather were better, it would induce more tourists to visit. 如果天气更好，它将吸引更多游客前来参观。
If I had known better, I wouldn't have induced him to do that. 如果我早知道，我就不会劝他那样做了。

被动语态:
Sleep was induced by the medication. 睡眠是由药物诱导的。
Economic growth is induced by government policies. 经济增长是由政府政策引起的。

疑问句:
What induced you to make such a decision? 什么促使你做出这样的决定？
What factors induced the change in climate? 哪些因素导致了气候变化？

否定句:
Nothing induced him to change his mind. 没有什么能让他改变主意。
The doctor's efforts failed to induce a response. 医生的努力未能引起反应。


PHRASE->

词源:
From Old French *induire, from Latin inducere "to lead in," from in- "in, into" + ducere "to lead."  The meaning has evolved over time from the original sense of "leading in" to the more figurative sense of "persuading" or "causing."
