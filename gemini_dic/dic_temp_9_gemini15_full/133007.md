nonentailed:/ˌnɒnɪnˈteɪld/ | adj. |非蕴含的，非必然的|Not logically implied; not a necessary consequence.  This term is primarily used in logic and linguistics, referring to a conclusion that does not follow necessarily from the premises.  For example, if the premise is "All men are mortal," and the conclusion is "Socrates is mortal," the conclusion is entailed. However, if the conclusion were "Socrates likes cheese," that would be nonentailed.|The conclusion that Socrates likes cheese is nonentailed from the premise that all men are mortal.|近义词：unimplied, unrelated, independent|反义词：entailed, implied, necessary
