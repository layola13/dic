forfeit:/ˈfɔːrfɪt/| vt. n.|丧失；罚没；放弃|To lose or give up something as a penalty for wrongdoing or failure to fulfill an obligation.  It can also refer to something that is lost or given up in this way.|例句：He forfeited his right to vote. 他丧失了他的投票权。|例句：The team forfeited the game after several players were injured.  由于几名球员受伤，球队放弃了比赛。|近义词：lose, surrender, relinquish|反义词：gain, win, acquire|动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He forfeits his possessions. 他丧失了他的财产。
She forfeits her right to appeal. 她失去了上诉的权利。


一般过去时:
They forfeited the match due to bad behavior. 他们因为行为不端而输掉了比赛。
He forfeited his bond. 他违反了保释条件。


现在完成时:
They have forfeited their chance to win. 他们已经失去了获胜的机会。
She has forfeited her place in the competition. 她已经失去了在比赛中的位置。


过去完成时:
He had forfeited his savings before the market crash. 在市场崩盘之前，他已经损失了他的积蓄。
They had forfeited their advantage by making a poor decision. 他们因为做出了一个糟糕的决定而失去了优势。


将来时:
If he continues this behavior, he will forfeit his job. 如果他继续这种行为，他将失去工作。
The team will forfeit the game if they don't show up. 如果他们没有出现，球队将输掉比赛。


虚拟语气:
If he had followed the rules, he wouldn't have forfeited the prize. 如果他遵守规则，他就不会失去奖品。
I wish I hadn't forfeited my opportunity. 我真希望我没有放弃我的机会。


被动语态:
The game was forfeited by the opposing team. 比赛被对方球队放弃了。
His rights were forfeited due to his actions. 他的权利由于他的行为而被剥夺了。


疑问句:
Will they forfeit the deposit? 他们会失去押金吗？
Did he forfeit his license? 他是否失去了执照？


否定句:
He did not forfeit his claim. 他没有放弃他的要求。
She did not forfeit the match. 她没有输掉比赛。

词源:
The word "forfeit" comes from the Old French word "forfaire," which means "to do wrong" or "to commit a fault."  This ultimately derives from the Latin "foris" (outside) + "facere" (to make or do), suggesting something done outside the bounds of acceptable behavior, leading to a penalty.
