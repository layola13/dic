intrenching: /ɪnˈtʃɛntʃɪŋ/ | v. | 构筑工事；确立；使…牢固 |  The act of digging trenches or creating fortifications; to establish or strengthen a position, especially in a defensive way. | 例句：The soldiers were intrenching themselves against the expected attack. = 士兵们正在构筑工事以抵御预期的攻击。


The enemy was intrenching its position in the mountains. = 敌人正在山区巩固其阵地。


近义词：fortifying, entrenching, strengthening | 反义词：weakening, undermining, abandoning

词性：动词

否定句:

词源:
源自古法语 *en-trencher，来自古法语 trenche “沟渠”，最终源自拉丁语 *trans-hendere “跨越，横越”。  The meaning evolved from the literal sense of digging trenches to the figurative sense of establishing a strong, defensible position.
