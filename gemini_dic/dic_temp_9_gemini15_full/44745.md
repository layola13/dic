crumble:/ˈkrʌmbəl/ | n., v. | 崩塌；碎裂；崩溃；弄碎；少量 | to break into small pieces; to fall apart; to deteriorate | 近义词：break, disintegrate, collapse | 反义词：solidify, unite, strengthen


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The cake crumbles easily. = 蛋糕很容易碎。
The old building is crumbling. = 这座旧建筑正在崩塌。


一般过去时:
The empire crumbled after the war. = 战争之后，帝国崩溃了。
He crumbled the biscuit into his tea. = 他把饼干弄碎放进茶里。


现在完成时:
The bridge has crumbled after years of neglect. = 这座桥由于多年的疏于维护而坍塌了。
The evidence has crumbled under scrutiny. = 证据经不起仔细审查。


过去完成时:
The walls had crumbled before the rescue team arrived. = 在救援队到达之前，墙壁已经坍塌了。
The agreement had crumbled before the negotiations even started. = 协议在谈判开始之前就已经破裂了。


将来时:
The old house will crumble eventually. = 这座旧房子最终会倒塌。
The support for the government will crumble if they don't change their policies. = 如果他们不改变政策，对政府的支持将会瓦解。


虚拟语气:
If the government hadn't ignored the warnings, the system wouldn't have crumbled. = 如果政府没有忽视警告，系统就不会崩溃。
I wish my dreams wouldn't crumble before my eyes. = 我希望我的梦想不会在我眼前破灭。


被动语态:
The building was crumbled by the earthquake. = 建筑物被地震摧毁了。
The old castle was crumbled by the passage of time. = 古堡被时间的流逝摧毁了。


疑问句:
Will the deal crumble under pressure? = 这笔交易会在压力下破裂吗？
Is the evidence crumbling? = 证据正在瓦解吗？


否定句:
The structure didn't crumble despite the strong winds. = 尽管风很大，结构并没有倒塌。
The cake didn't crumble when I cut it. = 我切蛋糕的时候，它没有碎。


PHRASE->
crumble to dust = 化为灰烬
crumble away = 逐渐消失
crumble under pressure = 在压力下崩溃
crumble into pieces = 变成碎片
a crumble topping = 粗碎面包屑


词源:
From Middle English *cromblen, from Old North French *crombler, of uncertain origin. Possibly related to Old French *croumler (“to crush, to crumple”), or possibly from a Germanic source.
