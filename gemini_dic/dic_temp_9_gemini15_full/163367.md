preconjecturing: /ˌpriːkənˈdʒɛktʃərɪŋ/ | v. | 预想；预断；预料 |  The act of forming a judgment or opinion before having sufficient evidence.  It implies making assumptions or guesses based on incomplete information.  | Example:  He was preconjecturing about the outcome of the election before the polls even closed. 他在投票站关闭之前就对选举结果进行了预判。 | 近义词: anticipating, presuming, prejudging | 反义词: verifying, confirming
