hyposkeletal: /ˌhaɪpoʊˈskɛlətəl/ | adj. | 次生的骨骼的；发育不全的骨骼的 | relating to or characterized by an underdeveloped or secondary skeleton.  The term often refers to the reduced or simplified skeletal structures found in some animals, especially those adapted to particular lifestyles or environments. For example, some deep-sea creatures exhibit hyposkeletal features as an adaptation to their environment. | The deep-sea anglerfish possesses a hyposkeletal structure, reflecting its adaptation to the pressures of the deep ocean. | 近义词：underdeveloped, rudimentary | 反义词：well-developed, robust
