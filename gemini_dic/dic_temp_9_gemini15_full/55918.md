divulging: /dɪˈvʌldʒɪŋ/ | v. |泄露；透露；公开 |  To divulge something means to reveal or make known something that was previously secret or confidential.  It often implies an intentional act of revealing information that should have been kept private. |


例句：

He was arrested for divulging state secrets. 他因泄露国家机密而被捕。

She refused to divulge the source of her information. 她拒绝透露消息来源。


近义词：reveal, disclose, unveil, uncover, betray


反义词：conceal, hide, secrete, suppress, keep secret


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He divulges company secrets to his competitors. 他向竞争对手泄露公司秘密。
She divulges her deepest fears to her therapist. 她向她的治疗师吐露了她最深层的恐惧。

一般过去时:
He divulged the truth to the police. 他向警方透露了真相。
She divulged the location of the hidden treasure. 她泄露了隐藏宝藏的地点。

现在完成时:
He has divulged too much information already. 他已经泄露了太多信息。
She has divulged her plans to her family. 她已经向家人透露了她的计划。

过去完成时:
He had divulged the secret before the meeting. 他在会议之前已经泄露了秘密。
She had divulged the information to her friend. 她已经向她的朋友透露了信息。

将来时:
He will divulge the details in his upcoming book. 他将在即将出版的书中透露细节。
She will divulge the truth when the time is right. 她会在时机成熟时说出真相。


虚拟语气:
If he divulged the information, he would be in great trouble. 如果他泄露了信息，他就会有大麻烦。
I wish she hadn't divulged my secret. 我真希望她没有泄露我的秘密。


被动语态:
The information was divulged to the press. 信息被泄露给了媒体。
The secret was divulged by a trusted employee. 秘密被一位可信赖的员工泄露了。


疑问句:
Will he divulge the truth? 他会说出真相吗？
Did she divulge the source of her information? 她透露了她的消息来源吗？


否定句:
He didn't divulge anything important. 他没有泄露任何重要的事情。
She refused to divulge the details of the scandal. 她拒绝透露丑闻的细节。


PHRASE->
divulge a secret: 泄露秘密
divulge information: 透露信息
divulge the truth: 揭露真相
divulge a plan: 公开计划
