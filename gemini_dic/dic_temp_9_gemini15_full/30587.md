cercus:/ˈsɜːrkəs/| n. |尾须，尾铗|A cercus is a pair of sensory appendages at the end of the abdomen of many insects and other arthropods.  They are typically used for sensing, balance, and sometimes defense.  |例句：The grasshopper's cerci were constantly twitching, detecting the slightest air movement.  蚱蜢的尾须不断抽动，探测着最轻微的气流。|近义词：appendage, anal cerci|反义词：无
