retinas: /ˈrɛtɪnəz/ | n. |视网膜|The retinas are the light-sensitive tissues lining the back of the eye that convert light into electrical signals, which are then sent to the brain for processing.  They are essential for vision.|Example: Damage to the retinas can cause vision impairment or blindness.  视网膜损伤会导致视力障碍或失明。|近义词：fundus (眼球后部)|反义词：无
