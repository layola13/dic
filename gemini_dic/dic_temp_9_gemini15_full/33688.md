cinnamyl:/ˈsɪnəməl/| adj. n.|肉桂基的；肉桂酰基的|Derived from cinnamic acid, cinnamyl refers to a group of organic compounds containing the cinnamyl group (C6H5CH=CHCH2−),  a derivative of cinnamic acid.  It's often found in essential oils and resins from plants like cinnamon. As a noun, it refers to the cinnamyl radical itself. |The scent of cinnamyl alcohol is reminiscent of cinnamon.  Cinnamyl acetate contributes to the flavor of cinnamon.  |近义词：cinnamic (related but not identical)|反义词：无
