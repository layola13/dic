preobjection:/ˌpriːəbˈdʒɛkʃən/| n. |预先反对；先发制人的反对|An objection raised before something is even proposed or presented.  It's often a preventative measure to stop something from even being considered. |例句：The committee raised preobjections to the proposed budget, citing concerns about its feasibility.  委员会对拟议的预算提出了预先反对意见，理由是担心其可行性。  |近义词：anticipation, preemptive strike, head-off|反义词：acceptance, approval, endorsement|名词
