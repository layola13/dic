riding:/ˈraɪdɪŋ/| v.|骑乘；乘坐；倚靠；漂浮|The act of sitting on and controlling a horse, bicycle, or other vehicle; to sit on and control (an animal, especially a horse) while it moves; to travel on (a vehicle)|例句：I enjoy riding my bike to work. 我喜欢骑自行车上班。

例句：She is riding a horse in the field. 她在田野里骑马。


词源:源自古英语rīdan，意为“骑”。


近义词：cycling, driving, mounting


反义词：walking, running


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rides his horse every day. 他每天骑马。
She rides the bus to school. 她乘公共汽车去上学。


一般过去时:
I rode my bike to the park yesterday. 我昨天骑自行车去了公园。
They rode horses across the plains. 他们骑马穿过平原。


现在完成时:
I have ridden this horse many times. 我骑过这匹马很多次了。
She has ridden a camel before. 她以前骑过骆驼。


过去完成时:
He had ridden his bike to the store before it started to rain. 下雨之前他已经骑车去商店了。
They had ridden for hours before they reached the village. 他们骑行了数小时才到达村庄。


将来时:
I will ride my bike to the beach tomorrow. 我明天将骑自行车去海滩。
We will ride the train to the city. 我们将乘火车去城市。


虚拟语气:
If I had a horse, I would ride it every day. 如果我有一匹马，我会每天骑它。
I wish I could ride a motorcycle. 我真希望我能骑摩托车。


被动语态:
The horse was ridden by a skilled rider. 这匹马由一位熟练的骑手骑乘。
The bicycle is being ridden by a young boy. 这辆自行车正被一个年轻男孩骑着。


疑问句:
Do you ride a bike? 你骑自行车吗？
Did she ride her horse today? 她今天骑马了吗？


否定句:
I don't ride a horse. 我不骑马。
He didn't ride his bike to school today. 他今天没有骑自行车去上学。


PHRASE->
go for a ride: 去兜风
take a ride: 搭便车
give someone a ride: 送某人一程
piggyback ride: 骑在背上
joyride: 兜风，狂飙
