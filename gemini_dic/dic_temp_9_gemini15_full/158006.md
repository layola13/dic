planoblast:/ˈplænəʊblæst/| n. |成胚细胞，成芽细胞|A planoblast is a cell that develops into a planula larva in certain cnidarians (like jellyfish and corals).  It is an undifferentiated cell capable of giving rise to various tissues and structures within the developing larva.  The term is specifically used in the context of cnidarian reproduction and development.|Example: The planoblasts in the polyp stage of the jellyfish undergo asexual reproduction to produce medusae.|近义词: blastomere (more general term)|反义词:  None readily available.
