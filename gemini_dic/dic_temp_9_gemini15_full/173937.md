quasi-scholastically:/ˈkwɑːziˌskɒlæstɪkli/ | adv. | 近似学术地，似是而非地学术地 | In a manner resembling that of a scholar, but not entirely or genuinely so; in a way that is superficially academic or scholarly.  It often implies a pretense or affectation of scholarship. | 例句：He discussed the topic quasi-scholastically, peppering his conversation with impressive-sounding but ultimately irrelevant terminology. 他以近似学术的方式讨论了这个话题，在谈话中穿插了一些听起来令人印象深刻但最终无关紧要的术语。


He argued his case quasi-scholastically, citing obscure texts and making convoluted arguments. 他以似是而非的学术方式论证了他的观点，引用了晦涩的文本并提出了复杂的论点。| 近义词：academically (but less implying pretense), pedantically, pseudo-intellectually | 反义词：informally, casually, simply


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It doesn't have tenses.  Its usage is primarily in describing the *manner* in which something is done.

PHRASE->
There are no common set phrases directly using "quasi-scholastically".  Its use is typically within a sentence describing an action or speech.  For example, "He responded quasi-scholastically to the simple question."
