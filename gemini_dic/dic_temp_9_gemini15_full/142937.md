out-valued:/ˌaʊtˈvæljuːd/| v.| 被超越价值, 价值被超过 | To be surpassed in value or importance.  This word is not a standard dictionary entry; it's a relatively uncommon word formed by combining "out-" (meaning to surpass or exceed) and "valued" (past participle of "value").  It implies that something previously considered valuable has been superseded by something else of greater worth. | Example: The older model of the phone was quickly out-valued by the release of the newer, improved version. 旧款手机很快就被新款改进版超越了价值。|近义词: surpassed, superseded, exceeded | 反义词: undervalued, appreciated
