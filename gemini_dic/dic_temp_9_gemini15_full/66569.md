exhibits:/ɪɡˈzɪbɪts/ | v. n. | 展览；展示；表现 | To show or display something publicly;  a thing displayed publicly. | The museum exhibits a vast collection of ancient artifacts.  博物馆展出大量古代文物。  The patient exhibits symptoms of the flu.  病人表现出流感的症状。 | displays, shows, presents | conceals, hides | v. n.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exhibits his paintings at the gallery. = 他在画廊展出他的画作。
The museum exhibits a wide range of artifacts. = 博物馆展出各种各样的文物。


一般过去时:
The artist exhibited his work last year. = 这位艺术家去年展出了他的作品。
They exhibited great courage in the face of danger. = 面对危险，他们表现出极大的勇气。


现在完成时:
She has exhibited her sculptures in several countries. = 她已经在多个国家展出过她的雕塑作品。
He has exhibited remarkable resilience throughout his illness. = 在整个疾病过程中，他表现出非凡的韧性。


过去完成时:
They had exhibited their paintings before the competition. = 他们在比赛前已经展出过他们的画作。
She had exhibited signs of stress before the exam. = 考试前她已经表现出压力的迹象。


将来时:
He will exhibit his new collection next month. = 他下个月将展出他的新系列作品。
The company will exhibit its latest products at the trade show. = 公司将在贸易展上展出其最新产品。


虚拟语气:
If he exhibited more confidence, he would succeed. = 如果他表现出更多的自信，他就会成功。
I wish he exhibited more interest in his studies. = 我希望他对学习表现出更多兴趣。


被动语态:
The paintings were exhibited in the main hall. = 画作在主厅展出。
His talent was exhibited early in his career. = 他的天赋在他职业生涯早期就展现出来了。


疑问句:
Does the museum exhibit any contemporary art? = 博物馆展出任何当代艺术吗？
Will they exhibit their work at the festival? = 他们会在节日上展出他们的作品吗？


否定句:
The gallery does not exhibit abstract art. = 画廊不展出抽象艺术。
He did not exhibit any signs of nervousness. = 他没有表现出任何紧张的迹象。


PHRASE->
exhibit A = 物证A
exhibit patience = 表现耐心
exhibit signs of = 表现出…的迹象
to exhibit one's work = 展出作品
exhibit a talent for = 表现出…的天赋


词源:
From Latin *exhibere "to hold forth, offer, present," from ex- "out" + habere "to have, hold."  The meaning evolved to encompass the act of displaying or showing something.
