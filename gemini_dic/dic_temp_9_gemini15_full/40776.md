convincing: /kənˈvɪnsɪŋ/ | adj. |令人信服的；有说服力的 |  able to persuade someone that something is true; believable.  | 例句：His argument was so convincing that everyone agreed with him. 他的论点如此令人信服，以至于每个人都同意他的观点。

His evidence was convincing enough to win the case.  他的证据足以赢得官司。


近义词：persuasive, compelling, credible, believable | 反义词： unconvincing, unpersuasive, dubious


词源:  Comes from the verb "convince," which is from Old French *convincre, from Latin *convincere "to overcome, conquer," from *com- "completely" + *vincere "to conquer."  The -ing suffix transforms the verb into an adjective.
