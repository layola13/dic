mallophaga:/mælˈɒfəɡə/| n. | 咬羽虱目 |  Mallophaga is an order of insects, commonly known as chewing lice, that are obligate ectoparasites of birds and mammals. They are characterized by their chewing mouthparts, which they use to feed on feathers, hair, and skin debris.  Unlike sucking lice (Phthiraptera), they do not suck blood. |  The bird was infested with mallophaga.  这只鸟感染了咬羽虱。  |  chewing lice, biting lice |  sucking lice (Anoplura) | 名词
