landmarks:/ˈlændmɑːrks/| n.|地标，里程碑|A landmark is a readily identifiable geographic feature or other distinguishing object in a landscape.  It can be a naturally occurring feature, such as a mountain or a river, or a man-made structure, such as a building or a monument. Landmarks are often used as points of reference for navigation and orientation. They also often hold cultural or historical significance.|例句：The Eiffel Tower is a famous landmark in Paris.  埃菲尔铁塔是巴黎著名的地标。

The ancient pyramids are important landmarks of Egyptian civilization. 古代金字塔是埃及文明的重要地标。|近义词：milestone, monument, point of interest|反义词:unremarkable place, insignificance
