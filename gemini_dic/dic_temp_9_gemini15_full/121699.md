mispractising: /ˌmɪsˈpræktɪsɪŋ/ | v. | 渎职；误诊；行为不端 |  The act of performing a professional duty incorrectly or improperly, often with negative consequences. This usually implies a deviation from accepted standards of practice, potentially causing harm or damage.  The term is often used in professional contexts like medicine or law. | 例句：The doctor was accused of mispractising and causing the patient's death. = 这名医生被指控渎职并导致病人死亡。  The lawyer was found guilty of mispractising after falsifying evidence. = 这名律师在伪造证据后被判犯有渎职罪。 | 近义词：malpractice, misconduct, wrongdoing, negligence | 反义词：proper practice, ethical conduct
