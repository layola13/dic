maligner:/məˈlaɪnər/ | vt. | 诽谤；中伤；污蔑 | To speak false and malicious statements about someone or something; to defame. | 例句：He was maligned by the press for his controversial views. 他因其有争议的观点而受到媒体的诽谤。 | 例句：Don't malign your friend just because you're angry. 不要因为生气就诽谤你的朋友。 | 近义词：slander, defame, vilify, traduce | 反义词：praise, commend, extol


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They malign their opponents in the election. = 他们在选举中诽谤他们的对手。
He constantly maligns his former business partner. = 他不断诽谤他以前的商业伙伴。

一般过去时:
The newspaper maligned the politician's reputation. = 这家报纸诽谤了这位政治家的名誉。
She maligned her neighbor to the police. = 她向警察诽谤她的邻居。

现在完成时:
The company has been maligned for its unethical practices. = 这家公司因其不道德的经营行为而受到了诽谤。
He has maligned me for years. = 他诽谤我多年了。

过去完成时:
The candidate had been maligned before the debate even started. = 辩论甚至开始之前，候选人就已经受到了诽谤。
They had maligned her character for so long that no one believed her. = 他们诽谤她的品格已经很久了，以至于没有人相信她。

将来时:

They will malign her career if she doesn't comply. = 如果她不遵守，他们将会诽谤她的职业生涯。

虚拟语气:

I wish they hadn't maligned my family. = 我真希望他们没有诽谤我的家人。

被动语态:
He was maligned by the press for his controversial views. = 他因其有争议的观点而受到媒体的诽谤。

疑问句:

否定句:

She would not malign her colleagues. = 她不会诽谤她的同事。
