publicities:/pʌbˈlɪsətiz/ | n. |宣传，广告活动 |Publicity refers to the attention given to someone or something by the media or the public.  It can be positive or negative, and is often used in marketing and public relations. | The band's new album received a lot of publicity. 乐队的最新专辑获得了大量的宣传。  The company is trying to generate positive publicity for its new product. 公司正试图为其新产品创造积极的宣传。| advertising, promotion, marketing | negative press, bad press | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company receives a lot of publicity. = 这家公司收到很多宣传。
Their new product enjoys considerable publicity. = 他们的新产品享有相当大的宣传。


一般过去时:
The event received significant publicity last year. = 该活动去年获得了大量的宣传。
The scandal generated negative publicity for the politician. = 这场丑闻给这位政治家带来了负面宣传。


现在完成时:
The film has received worldwide publicity. = 这部电影已获得全球宣传。
The campaign has generated a great deal of publicity. = 这场运动已经产生了大量的宣传。


过去完成时:
The company had already received considerable publicity before the launch. = 公司在发布之前就已经获得了相当大的宣传。
They had anticipated negative publicity but it was worse than expected. = 他们预料到负面宣传，但实际情况比预期更糟。


将来时:
The concert will receive extensive publicity. = 这场音乐会将获得广泛的宣传。
The product launch will have significant publicity. = 产品发布会将有大量的宣传。


虚拟语气:
If the product had received more publicity, it would have sold better. = 如果该产品获得更多宣传，它就会卖得更好。
I wish the company had handled the negative publicity better. = 我希望公司能更好地处理负面宣传。


被动语态:
The news was given widespread publicity. = 这条新闻得到了广泛的宣传。
The event is being publicized extensively. = 此事件正在被广泛宣传。


疑问句:

否定句:
The product didn't receive much publicity. = 该产品没有得到多少宣传。
The company avoided negative publicity through careful planning. = 公司通过仔细的规划避免了负面宣传。
