prerepresented:/ˌpriːˈreprɪˈzɛntɪd/| adj.| 预先呈现的；预先代表的 |  Describes something that has been presented or represented beforehand or in advance.  It implies a prior action of presentation or representation. |The prerepresented data was used in the final analysis. 预先呈现的数据用于最终分析。 The prerepresented interests of the shareholders were overlooked. 股东的预先代表的利益被忽视了。 |近义词：pre-presented, previously represented |反义词：newly represented, subsequently presented


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company prerepresents its products at trade shows. = 公司在贸易展会上预先展示其产品。
He prerepresents the interests of his constituents in the legislature. = 他在立法机关中预先代表其选民的利益。


一般过去时:
The artist prerepresented his work to the gallery owner. = 艺术家向画廊老板预先展示了他的作品。
They prerepresented their concerns to the committee before the meeting. = 他们会在会议前向委员会预先陈述他们的担忧。


现在完成时:
The organization has prerepresented its case to the court. = 该组织已向法院预先陈述了其案情。
She has prerepresented her ideas to the board numerous times. = 她多次向董事会预先陈述了自己的想法。


过去完成时:
The lawyer had prerepresented the evidence to the judge. = 律师已向法官预先提交了证据。
They had prerepresented their position to the negotiating team. = 他们已向谈判小组预先阐述了自己的立场。


将来时:
The spokesperson will prerepresent the company's position at the press conference. = 发言人将在新闻发布会上预先陈述公司的立场。
He will prerepresent the findings of his research at the conference. = 他将在会议上预先陈述其研究结果。


虚拟语气:
If they had prerepresented their concerns, the outcome might have been different. = 如果他们预先陈述了他们的担忧，结果可能会有所不同。
I wish he had prerepresented the risks involved. = 我希望他预先说明了所涉及的风险。


被动语态:
The proposal was prerepresented to the committee last week. = 该提案在上周已预先提交给委员会。
The data was prerepresented in the preliminary report. = 数据已在初步报告中预先呈现。


疑问句:

否定句:
The facts were not prerepresented in the initial report. = 事实并未在初步报告中预先呈现。
The company did not prerepresent its financial difficulties. = 公司没有预先说明其财务困难。
