cryptogamian:/krɪptəˈɡeɪmiən/| n. |隐花植物；孢子植物|A cryptogam is a plant that reproduces by spores, rather than by seeds or flowers.  They are often characterized by a lack of visible reproductive structures like flowers or seeds.  The term encompasses a wide range of plants, including algae, fungi, lichens, and ferns.|Example:  Mosses and ferns are examples of cryptogams. 苔藓和蕨类植物是隐花植物的例子。|近义词: spore plant, thalophyte |反义词: phanerogam
