non-evidential:/ˌnɒnˌevɪˈdɛnʃəl/ | adj. | 非证据性的，不需证据的 |  Not requiring or based on evidence; not supported by evidence.  This term is often used in linguistics to describe a grammatical category or feature that does not necessitate the speaker's direct knowledge or experience of the event being described. For example, in some languages, non-evidential statements simply report information without indicating the source of the information. |  The claim was non-evidential and lacked any supporting data.  =  这个说法缺乏证据，没有任何支持性数据。
