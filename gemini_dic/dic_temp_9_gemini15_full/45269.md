cultivating: /ˈkʌltɪveɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 耕作；培养；陶冶；发展 |

英文解析：Cultivating refers to the process of preparing land for growing crops,  preparing and improving something for future use, or developing a skill, quality, or idea.  It implies a deliberate and ongoing effort to nurture growth and development.

例句：

He spent years cultivating his garden. 他花了数年时间耕作他的花园。

She is cultivating a strong network of contacts.  她在培养一个强大的联系网。


近义词： developing, fostering, nurturing, improving, promoting, refining

反义词： neglecting, ignoring, destroying, hindering


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They are cultivating a new strain of rice.  他们在培育一种新的水稻品种。
Farmers are cultivating the land for the next harvest. 农民们正在耕种土地，为下一季收成做准备。

一般过去时:
She cultivated a friendship with her colleague. 她和同事培养了友谊。
The ancient civilization cultivated a unique culture. 古代文明培养了一种独特的文化。

现在完成时:
They have cultivated a successful business.  他们已经发展了一家成功的企业。
We have been cultivating this relationship for years. 我们多年来一直培养这种关系。

过去完成时:
He had cultivated his skills for many years before he finally achieved success. 在他最终取得成功之前，他已经磨练了自己的技能多年。
The company had cultivated a positive image before the scandal.  丑闻发生前，该公司已经树立了积极的形象。

将来时:
They will cultivate more land to increase their production. 他们将耕种更多的土地以增加产量。
She will cultivate her talent to become a better artist. 她将培养自己的才能，成为一名更好的艺术家。

虚拟语气:
If they cultivated their garden more carefully, they would have a better harvest. 如果他们更仔细地耕种他们的花园，他们就会有更好的收成。
If I had cultivated my skills earlier, I would be in a better position now. 如果我早点培养我的技能，我现在就会处于更好的位置。

被动语态:
The land was cultivated by the farmers.  土地由农民耕种。
The friendship was cultivated through mutual respect. 友谊是通过相互尊重培养起来的。

疑问句:

否定句:

She didn't cultivate her talents. 她没有培养她的天赋。
