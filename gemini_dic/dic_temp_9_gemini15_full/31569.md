chasuble:/ˈtʃeɪzəbəl/| n. |祭衣，法衣|A chasuble is a liturgical vestment worn by a priest or other officiant during the celebration of Mass in many Christian denominations. It is a sleeveless, usually ornate garment that covers the shoulders and back. |Example: The priest wore a beautiful embroidered chasuble during the Christmas Eve Mass.  祭司在平安夜弥撒中穿着一件美丽的刺绣祭衣。|近义词：vestment, cope|反义词：N/A
