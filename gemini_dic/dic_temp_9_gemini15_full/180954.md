reimagined:/ˌriːɪˈmædʒɪnd/| v. |重新构想，重新想象 |To imagine or conceive of something again, often in a new or different way.  It implies a significant alteration or improvement on a previous conception. |例句：The old building has been reimagined as a modern art gallery. 老建筑已被重新构想成一个现代艺术画廊。

The novel was reimagined as a successful movie. 这部小说被重新构想成一部成功的电影。


近义词：re-envisioned, reconceived, redesigned, reinvented |反义词：unchanged, unaltered

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reimagine the product design every year. = 他们每年都会重新构思产品设计。
We reimagine our marketing strategies regularly. = 我们定期重新构思我们的营销策略。

一般过去时:
The artist reimagined the myth in a contemporary context. = 艺术家在一个当代语境中重新构想了这个神话。
