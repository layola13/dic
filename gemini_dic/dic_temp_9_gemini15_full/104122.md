jumps:/dʒʌmps/| v.|跳跃；猛增|To move quickly and suddenly from one place to another by propelling oneself into the air with one's legs.  It can also refer to a sudden increase.|例句：The cat jumps onto the table. 猫跳到桌子上。

The price of oil jumped sharply last week. 油价上周急剧上涨。|近义词：leap, hop, spring, soar|反义词：fall, descend


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jumps over the fence every day. 他每天都跳过栅栏。
The rabbit jumps quickly. 兔子跳得很快。


一般过去时:
She jumped with joy. 她高兴地跳了起来。
The frog jumped into the pond. 青蛙跳进了池塘。


现在完成时:
He has jumped the hurdle. 他已经越过了障碍。
The stock market has jumped significantly. 股市已经大幅上涨。


过去完成时:
The dog had jumped the wall before I arrived. 我到达之前，狗已经跳过了墙。
They had jumped to conclusions. 他们妄下结论了。


将来时:
I will jump for joy when I hear the news. 当我听到这个消息时，我会高兴地跳起来。
The number of applicants will jump next year. 明年的申请人数将会猛增。


虚拟语气:
If I could jump that high, I would be in the Olympics. 如果我能跳那么高，我就能参加奥运会了。
I wish I could jump as high as he can. 我真希望我能像他那样跳得高。


被动语态:
The rope was jumped over by the gymnast. 体操运动员跳过了绳子。( less common usage)
The conclusion was jumped to prematurely. 结论过早地下定了。


疑问句:
Can you jump that high? 你能跳那么高吗？
Does the kangaroo jump far? 袋鼠跳得远吗？


否定句:
He didn't jump the gun. 他没有操之过急。
I couldn't jump over the fence. 我无法跳过栅栏。



PHRASE->
jump to conclusions: 妄下结论
jump for joy: 高兴地跳起来
jump the gun: 操之过急
jump in: 插手；加入
jump on (someone): 责备；斥责
