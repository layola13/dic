protestantism:/ˈprɒtɛstənˌtɪzəm/| n. |新教；新教教义|The religious movement that began in Europe in the early 16th century in opposition to the Catholic Church.  It emphasizes the Bible as the ultimate authority and the importance of individual faith.  Different Protestant denominations exist, each with its own beliefs and practices. |The Reformation led to the rise of Protestantism across Europe.  新教改革导致了新教在欧洲的兴起。|Catholicism, Christianity|Catholicism, paganism|名词
