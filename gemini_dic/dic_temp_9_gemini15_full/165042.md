preidentifications: /ˌpriːˌaɪdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 预先识别，初步鉴定 |  The act of identifying something before a more thorough examination or analysis. It often involves preliminary assessment or screening to determine the nature or characteristics of something.  | 例句：The preidentifications made by the field team allowed for a more focused investigation.  = 田间小组的预先识别使后续调查更有针对性。


The preidentifications of potential hazards helped prevent accidents. = 对潜在危险的预先识别有助于预防事故。


近义词： preliminary identifications, initial classifications, preliminary screenings | 反义词：final identifications, definitive classifications


词源：

* **pre-**: prefix meaning "before" or "in advance of"
* **identification**:  from "identify" (to establish the identity of), ultimately from Latin *identi-*, combining form of *idem* "the same".

用法说明：

由于"preidentifications"本身是一个较少使用的词，其用法通常限制在需要预先识别或初步鉴定的专业领域，例如：医学诊断、犯罪调查、工程安全评估等。  它强调的是在正式鉴定或分析之前的初步判断。  并没有像"identification"那样广泛的用法。  因此，不提供详细的各种时态例句。  它的用法主要体现在名词短语中。
