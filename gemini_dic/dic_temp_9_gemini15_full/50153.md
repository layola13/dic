denunciatory: /dɪˈnʌnsieɪtəri/ | adj. | 谴责的；告发的；非难的 | Accusatory; condemnatory; containing or expressing denunciation.  It implies a strong and public condemnation, often of wrongdoing or immoral behavior. |

The denunciatory tone of the editorial was evident in its harsh criticism of the government's policies.  = 这篇社论的谴责语气在其对政府政策的严厉批评中显而易见。

The witness's denunciatory statement led to the arrest of the suspect. = 证人的谴责性陈述导致了嫌疑人的逮捕。


近义词：condemnatory, accusatory, censorious, critical, reproachful

反义词：approving, laudatory, complimentary


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The article is denunciatory of the company's practices. = 这篇文章谴责了该公司的行为。
His speech is highly denunciatory of corruption. = 他的演讲强烈谴责腐败。


一般过去时:
The letter was denunciatory in its accusations. = 这封信在指控中充满了谴责。
Her testimony was very denunciatory of the defendant. = 她的证词对被告进行了非常强烈的谴责。

The report has been denunciatory of the company's safety record. = 该报告谴责了该公司的安全记录。


过去完成时:

The newspaper had been consistently denunciatory of the government's handling of the crisis. = 报纸一直持续谴责政府处理危机的措施。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If the evidence were more denunciatory, the judge would convict the defendant. = 如果证据更有谴责力，法官就会判决被告有罪。
I wish the report were less denunciatory and more constructive. = 我希望报告少一些谴责，多一些建设性。


被动语态:
The company's actions were denounced by many. = 该公司的行为受到许多人的谴责。
The politician's words were denounced as inflammatory and divisive. = 这位政治家的言论被谴责为煽动性和分裂性。


疑问句:
Is the article denunciatory of the mayor's policies? = 这篇文章是否谴责市长的政策？

否定句:
The review was not denunciatory; it was quite balanced. = 这篇评论并非谴责性的；它相当平衡。
The judge's decision was not denunciatory of the defendant. = 法官的判决并未谴责被告。

词源:  From "denounce" + "-atory,"  meaning "tending to" or "characterized by."  "Denounce" itself comes from Old French *denoncier, from Latin *denuntiare, from *de- ("down, away") + *nuntiare ("to announce, report").
