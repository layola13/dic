individualism: /ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlɪzəm/ | n. | 个人主义；个性；个人独立性 | The belief that the individual should be free to act and think as they choose, without undue interference from the state or any other authority.  It emphasizes individual rights, autonomy, and self-reliance. |  The rise of individualism in the 19th century led to significant social and political changes.  个人主义在19世纪的兴起导致了重大的社会和政治变革。| Collectivism, cooperation | Conformity, collectivism | 名词

词源:

The word "individualism" originates from the French word "individualisme," coined in the 19th century.  It's derived from the Latin word "individuum," meaning "indivisible," referring to a single, unique person.
