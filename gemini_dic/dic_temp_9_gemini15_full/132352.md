nondelegation: /ˌnɒnˌdɛləˈɡeɪʃən/ | n. | 不可转让；授权不可转让 | The act of not delegating or assigning a responsibility or power to another.  It often refers to the principle in law that certain powers cannot be transferred to another entity.  This is particularly relevant in constitutional law, where certain powers are vested solely in specific branches of government. | 例句：The nondelegation doctrine prevents Congress from delegating its legislative powers to other branches of government.  =  非授权原则阻止国会将其立法权授予政府其他部门。


The Supreme Court has upheld several instances of nondelegation in recent years. = 最高法院近年来维持了几起非授权的案例。


近义词：nontransfer, inalienability | 反义词：delegation, assignment


词源:  The word "nondelegation" is a compound word.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not,"  "delegation" comes from the Latin word "delegare," meaning "to send," "to entrust," or "to appoint."  Therefore, "nondelegation" literally means "not entrusting" or "not assigning."
