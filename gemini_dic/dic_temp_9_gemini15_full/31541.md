charters:/ˈtʃɑːrtərz/ | n. |宪章；特许状；许可证；(飞机的)包机 |A charter is a document that gives rights and privileges.  It can be a formal document establishing the rights and privileges of a group, organization, or institution, or a document granting permission for a specific activity.  It can also refer to a rented aircraft. |例句：The company obtained a charter to operate in the country. 这家公司获得了在该国运营的特许状。

The university operates under its own charter. 这所大学根据其自身的章程运作。


近义词：document, license, permit, grant |反义词：revocation, cancellation |词性：n.

The school follows its charter. = 学校遵守其章程。
This company charters planes frequently. = 这家公司经常包机。


一般过去时:
The king granted a royal charter. = 国王颁布了一项皇家特许状。

现在完成时:

词源:
The word "charter" comes from the Old French "chartre," meaning "written document," ultimately deriving from the Latin "charta," meaning "paper, papyrus."  The sense of a formal document granting rights and privileges evolved over time.
