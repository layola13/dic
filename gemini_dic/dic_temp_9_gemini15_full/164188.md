predistrusting: /ˌpriːdɪsˈtrʌstɪŋ/ | adj. | 预先不信任的，抱有偏见的 |  Showing or characterized by a lack of trust beforehand;  suspicious in advance. This word is not commonly used and is considered somewhat archaic or overly formal. It emphasizes a prior state of distrust, rather than simply distrust. | Example:  His predistrusting nature made it difficult for him to form close relationships. 他先入为主的不信任使得他很难建立亲密关系。  | Near Synonyms: suspicious, skeptical, distrustful, wary | Antonyms: trusting, believing, confident |  adj.
