precipitant: /prɪˈsɪpɪtənt/ | n. adj. | 沉淀剂；促成因素；鲁莽的，仓促的 |

英文解析:
As a noun, a precipitant is a substance that causes a precipitate (a solid) to separate from a solution.  It can also refer to something that causes a sudden event or action, acting as a catalyst or trigger. As an adjective, it describes something done hastily or rashly, without due consideration.

例句:
The addition of silver nitrate acted as a precipitant, causing the chloride ions to form a solid precipitate.  = 硝酸银的加入起到了沉淀剂的作用，导致氯离子形成固体沉淀。

The economic downturn was a precipitant for social unrest. = 经济衰退是社会动荡的诱因。


近义词:
(noun) catalyst, trigger, cause, agent
(adjective) hasty, rash, impulsive, reckless

反义词:
(noun) inhibitor, deterrent, preventative
(adjective) deliberate, cautious, considered


词源:
From the Latin word "praecipitare," meaning "to throw headlong," "to hasten," or "to precipitate."  This reflects the rapid and sudden nature of both the chemical process and the figurative sense of the word.
