cytomegalovirus:/ˌsaɪtoʊˌmɛɡəloʊˈvaɪrəs/| n. |巨细胞病毒|Cytomegalovirus (CMV) is a common herpes virus that can infect people of all ages.  It usually doesn't cause symptoms in healthy adults, but it can be serious for people with weakened immune systems, newborns, and organ transplant recipients.  The virus is spread through bodily fluids, such as saliva, urine, blood, and semen.|The pregnant woman was tested for cytomegalovirus to rule out any potential risk to the fetus.|近义词：herpesvirus|反义词：无
