microcontrollers:/ˌmaɪkroʊkənˈtroʊlər(z)/| n. |微控制器|A microcontroller is a small, single-chip computer that contains a central processing unit (CPU), memory, and input/output (I/O) peripherals on a single integrated circuit (IC).  It's designed to control embedded systems, meaning systems where the computer is integrated into a larger device or machine. Unlike general-purpose computers, microcontrollers are typically dedicated to a specific task.|Example:  Many modern appliances, from washing machines to cars, use microcontrollers to manage their functions.  许多现代家用电器，从洗衣机到汽车，都使用微控制器来管理其功能。|近义词：microprocessor (though less precisely), embedded system |反义词：mainframe computer, supercomputer
