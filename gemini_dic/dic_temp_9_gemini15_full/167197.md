pretences: /prɪˈtɛnsɪz/ | n. | 假装；虚饰；借口 | The plural form of "pretence," referring to acts of feigning or pretending, or the things pretended.  It often implies a deceptive or insincere attempt to appear different than one actually is. | Examples:  His cheerful pretences couldn't hide his deep unhappiness. 他快乐的假象掩盖不了他内心的深深不快乐。 Her claims of innocence were mere pretences. 她声称清白纯属虚伪。 | 近义词：sham, affectation, simulation, deception | 反义词：genuineness, authenticity, sincerity |  名词

词源:
From the Old French *pretense*, from Latin *praetendere*, meaning "to hold out, to allege, to pretend."  The meaning evolved from a simple act of presenting or putting forward to the more modern connotation of deceptive presentation.
