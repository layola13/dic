malleabilities:/ˌmælɪəˈbɪlətiz/| n.|延展性；可塑性；适应性|The capacity of a substance to be deformed under stress without fracturing.  This refers to the ability of a material to be hammered or rolled into thin sheets without breaking. It also has a broader metaphorical sense, referring to the ability of something (a person, an idea, a system) to be adapted or changed.  |例句：The malleability of clay allows sculptors to create intricate forms.  黏土的可塑性使雕塑家能够创造出复杂的形态。

The malleabilities of the political system allowed for swift adaptation to the changing economic climate.  政治体系的可塑性使其能够迅速适应不断变化的经济环境。|近义词：ductility, flexibility, adaptability, plasticity|反义词：rigidity, inflexibility, brittleness

词源:
The word "malleabilities" is the plural form of "malleability."  "Malleability" derives from the Latin word "malleare," meaning "to hammer."  The suffix "-ability" indicates the capacity or potential for something.  Therefore, "malleability" literally means "the capacity to be hammered."
