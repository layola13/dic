/kənˈfaɪnərz/ | n. | 限制者；约束物 | Things that restrict or confine something or someone.  Confiners can be physical objects, like walls or cages, or abstract concepts, like rules or laws. |  The high walls acted as effective confiners, keeping the prisoners inside. 高墙充当了有效的限制物，将囚犯困在里面。 The strict rules of the monastery served as confiners to the monks' lives. 修道院的严格规定限制了僧侣的生活。 | restricters, restrainers, limiters | liberators, emancipators | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
These walls confine the prisoners. = 这些墙限制了囚犯。
The rules confine our choices. = 这些规则限制了我们的选择。

一般过去时:
The cage confined the bird. = 鸟笼限制了那只鸟。

现在完成时:
The laws have confined their movements. = 法律已经限制了他们的行动。
The drought has confined the crops' growth. = 干旱已经限制了农作物的生长。

过去完成时:
The old laws had confined their rights. = 旧的法律已经限制了他们的权利。
The previous agreement had confined their options. = 先前的协议已经限制了他们的选择。

将来时:
The new regulations will confine their access. = 新规定将限制他们的进入。
The coming storm will confine us to our homes. = 即将来临的风暴将把我们限制在家中。

虚拟语气:

否定句:
