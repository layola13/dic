monad:/ˈmɒnæd/ /ˈmɑːnæd/| n. |单元，独体；整体；（哲学）独一者；（程序设计）单子|A monad is a single, independent unit or entity. In philosophy, it refers to a single, indivisible substance or being. In computer programming, a monad is a design pattern that allows you to chain together functions in a specific way, often used for handling side effects or asynchronous operations.  |Example:  In functional programming, the Maybe monad handles potential null values gracefully.  在函数式编程中，Maybe 单子优雅地处理潜在的空值。 |近义词：unit, entity, single, whole |反义词：plurality, multiplicity, many
