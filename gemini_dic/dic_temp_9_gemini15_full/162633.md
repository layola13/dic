preapprobation:/ˌpriːæprəˈbeɪʃən/| n. |预先赞同；预先批准|Prior approval or consent, often given before a project or action is fully undertaken.  It suggests a positive anticipation or favorable predisposition towards something before it's fully developed or implemented.|The project received preapprobation from the board, which greatly expedited the funding process.|近义词：preapproval, prior consent, advance sanction|反义词：prejudice, disapproval, objection

USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "What" example.  It's used as a single word.  Therefore, grammatical tenses and voice are not applicable.

例句1：The artist submitted his proposal, hoping for preapprobation before starting the large-scale mural. = 艺术家提交了他的提案，希望在开始大型壁画之前获得预先批准。

例句2：  Securing preapprobation for the loan significantly reduced the stress of the home-buying process. = 获得贷款预先批准大大减轻了购房过程的压力。
