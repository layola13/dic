drowns:/draʊnz/ | vt. |淹没；使淹死；使沉浸；|to be submerged in and die in water or another liquid; to be overwhelmed by a feeling or situation|The heavy rain drowned the streets.  大雨淹没了街道。|The child almost drowned in the river.  孩子差点淹死在河里。|近义词：submerge, immerse, overwhelm|反义词：save, rescue|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The river often drowns the low-lying areas. = 这条河经常淹没低洼地区。
He drowns his sorrows in alcohol. = 他借酒消愁。

一般过去时:
The boat capsized and several people drowned. = 船翻了，好几个人都淹死了。
She drowned her anxieties in work.  = 她埋头工作以消除焦虑。

现在完成时:
The heavy rain has drowned the crops. = 大雨淹没了庄稼。
He has drowned himself in his studies. = 他埋头苦读。

过去完成时:
The villagers had drowned their fears in celebration. = 村民们在庆祝中忘记了恐惧。
They had drowned their sorrows in drink before the news came. = 消息传来之前，他们借酒消愁。

将来时:
The rising tide will drown the coastal villages. = 上涨的潮水将淹没沿海村庄。
If we don't act quickly, the project will drown in debt. = 如果我们不迅速采取行动，这个项目将会陷入债务危机。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't swum out so far, he wouldn't have drowned. = 如果他不游那么远，他就不会淹死了。
I wish I hadn't drowned my feelings in work. = 我真希望我没有把我的感情埋没在工作中。

被动语态:
Many people were drowned in the flood. = 洪水中许多人被淹死了。
His worries were drowned out by the noise of the city. = 城市的喧嚣盖过了他的担忧。

疑问句:
Did anyone drown in the accident? = 事故中有人淹死吗？
Were his hopes drowned by the reality? = 他的希望被现实击碎了吗？

否定句:
The cat fell into the water, but it didn't drown. = 猫掉进了水里，但它没有淹死。
He didn't drown his feelings; he confronted them. = 他没有掩盖自己的感情；他正视了它们。
