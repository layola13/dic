carrions:/ˈkæriən/| n.|腐肉；死尸|The noun "carrion" refers to the decaying flesh of a dead animal. It is often used to describe food for scavengers like vultures and hyenas.  The word evokes a sense of unpleasantness and decay.|例句：Vultures circled the carrion on the hillside.  秃鹫盘旋在山坡上的腐肉上。

The dogs were scavenging for carrion in the woods. 狗在树林里寻找腐肉。|近义词：腐肉，死肉， carcass,  remains|反义词：fresh meat,  living flesh|名词

词源:
The word "carrion" comes from the Old French "carogne," meaning "carcass" or "rotten flesh."  This, in turn, likely derives from a Vulgar Latin word, possibly related to the Latin "caro," meaning "flesh."
