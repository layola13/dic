The word "sanguinary" ultimately derives from the Latin word "sanguis," meaning "blood."  The suffix "-ary" forms adjectives meaning "of the nature of" or "pertaining to."  Therefore, "sanguinary" literally means "relating to or characterized by blood," often in a violent context.
