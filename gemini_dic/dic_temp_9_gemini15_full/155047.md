petitionist: /pəˈtɪʃənɪst/ | n. |请愿人，请愿者 | A person who signs or presents a petition.  Someone who makes a formal written request to an authority. |例句：The petitionist presented the document to the mayor.  请愿人将文件递交给市长。

例句：Many petitionists gathered outside the parliament building to voice their concerns. 许多请愿者聚集在议会大厦外表达他们的担忧。


近义词：supplicant, petitioner, applicant


反义词：opponent, adversary (这些词语与请愿者的目的相反)


词源：源自petition (请愿书) + -ist (表示从事某种职业或活动的人)  The word "petition" comes from the Old French "petition," ultimately from the Latin "petitio," meaning "a seeking," "request," or "demand."
