echeloning:/ˈɛʃəlɒnɪŋ/ | n. |梯队编队；梯次部署；逐级安排 | The arrangement of troops, ships, aircraft, or other units in a series of parallel lines or echelons.  This term often implies a staggered or overlapping arrangement to provide support and coverage across a broad area or to achieve a specific tactical objective. | 例句：The aircraft were echeloned to provide continuous air cover.  飞机采用梯队编队以提供持续的空中掩护。

The army deployed its forces in an echeloning formation to advance across the difficult terrain. 军队采用梯次部署的方式在崎岖的地形上推进。


近义词：staging, deployment, arrangement, tiered formation | 反义词：dispersion (in the sense of scattering units widely) |  名词

词源:
