enmeshment: /ɪnˈmɛʃmənt/ | n. | 纠缠；陷入；牵连 | The state of being entangled or involved in something complex or difficult from which it is difficult to extricate oneself.  It often implies a sense of being trapped or overwhelmed. | 例句：The family was caught in a web of enmeshment, with each member's problems intertwined with the others'. 家庭陷入纠缠的泥沼，每个成员的问题都与其他人交织在一起。


The enmeshment of their lives made it hard to distinguish one person's struggles from another's. 他们生活的纠缠使人们难以区分一个人的挣扎与另一个人的挣扎。

近义词：entanglement, involvement, intertwinement, complication | 反义词：separation, detachment, independence |  词性：名词

词源:
The word "enmeshment" is derived from the verb "enmesh," which itself is a combination of the prefix "en-" (meaning "in" or "into") and the noun "mesh" (referring to a network or a tangled mass).  Therefore, "enmeshment" literally means the state of being caught or entangled in a mesh or network.
