janus:/ˈdʒeɪnəs/| n. |雅努斯；两面神；两面派；矛盾 |The Roman god Janus, depicted with two faces, one looking forward and one looking backward, represents beginnings, endings, transitions, and duality.  Janus is also used to describe a person or situation with two opposing aspects or a double nature. |例句：The company's Janus-faced approach to environmental issues—promoting green initiatives while simultaneously engaging in polluting practices—drew criticism.  这家公司在环境问题上采取两面派的作法——一方面宣传绿色环保倡议，另一方面却从事污染行为——招致批评。|近义词： duality, paradox, dichotomy |反义词：singularity, simplicity
