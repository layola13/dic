phonetization: /ˌfoʊnɪˈzeɪʃən/ | /ˌfəʊnɪˈteɪʃən/ | n. |语音化；语音学描写|The process of representing the sounds of speech in writing, often using a phonetic alphabet.  It involves analyzing and transcribing the sounds of a language as accurately as possible. This is distinct from spelling, which represents words according to conventional orthography.  Phonetization is crucial in linguistics, speech therapy, and language learning.|例句：The phonetization of the dialect revealed several unique sounds not found in standard English.  语音学对该方言的描述揭示了标准英语中找不到的几种独特的声音。|近义词：transcription, transliteration |反义词：spelling (in the sense of conventional, non-phonetic spelling)
