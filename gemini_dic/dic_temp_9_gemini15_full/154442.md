permutationist: /ˌpɜːmjuˈteɪʃənɪst/ | n. | 排列主义者，置换论者 | A permutationist is someone who believes in or advocates for permutationism, a philosophical position related to the nature of identity, change, and persistence.  This often involves the idea that objects or entities are constituted by the relations they bear to other things, rather than by intrinsic properties.  Different permutations of these relations might still constitute the same object. |  The philosopher David Lewis is often considered a prominent permutationist. | 近义词:  relationist (in some contexts) | 反义词:  substantialist (in some contexts)
