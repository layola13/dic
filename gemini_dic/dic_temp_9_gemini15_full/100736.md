intransigent:/ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒənt/| adj.|不妥协的；固执的；不让步的| unwilling or refusing to change one's views or to agree about something.  The intransigent leader refused to negotiate.  |The intransigent union leader refused all compromise. = 那个不妥协的工会领导拒绝一切妥协。  |近义词：unyielding, inflexible, obstinate, stubborn |反义词：compliant, yielding, flexible, amenable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is intransigent in his views. = 他在观点上很固执。
The government remains intransigent on this issue. = 政府在这个问题上仍然不妥协。


一般过去时:
She was intransigent in her refusal to cooperate. = 她拒绝合作的态度很坚决。
The negotiators were intransigent throughout the talks. = 谈判代表在整个谈判过程中都很固执。


现在完成时:
They have been intransigent throughout the negotiations. = 他们在谈判中一直都很不妥协。
The company has remained intransigent despite the workers' protests. = 尽管工人们抗议，该公司仍然不妥协。


过去完成时:
He had been intransigent for years, refusing to budge on his position. = 他多年来一直很固执，拒绝改变立场。
The committee had been intransigent in its decision, ignoring all appeals. = 委员会的决定很固执，无视所有上诉。


将来时:
She will likely remain intransigent even with new evidence. = 即使有了新的证据，她也很可能会继续固执己见。
The board will be intransigent in its pursuit of profits. = 董事会将坚持追求利润。


虚拟语气:
If he were less intransigent, we could reach an agreement. = 如果他不那么固执，我们就能达成协议。
I wish he weren't so intransigent in his beliefs. = 我希望他的信仰不要那么固执。


被动语态:
His intransigence was noted by all parties involved. = 所有相关方都注意到了他的固执。
No compromise was reached because of the intransigence of both sides. = 由于双方都不妥协，所以没有达成任何协议。


疑问句:
Is he always so intransigent? = 他总是这么固执吗？
Are they being intransigent on purpose? = 他们故意不妥协吗？


否定句:
He is not intransigent; he is simply cautious. = 他并不固执，他只是谨慎。
The team was not intransigent; they were willing to listen to suggestions. = 这个团队并不固执；他们愿意听取建议。


PHRASE->
remain intransigent = 依然固执
become intransigent = 变得固执
an intransigent attitude = 不妥协的态度
an intransigent response = 不让步的回应
display intransigence = 表现出固执


词源:
From Latin *intransīgens, present participle of *intransīgēre, from in- “not” + transīgēre “to come to an agreement,” from trans- “across” + agere “to do, drive.”
