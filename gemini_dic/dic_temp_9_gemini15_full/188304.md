rubber-neck:/ˈrʌbərˌnek/ | v. | 扭头看; 为了看而伸长脖子 | To turn one's head or neck to look at something, especially in a way that is considered rude or inconsiderate.  | 例句：Everyone rubber-necked as the celebrity walked by. 大家都扭头去看名人走过。  | 例句：Don't rubber-neck while driving; it's dangerous. 开车时不要东张西望，很危险。 | 近义词： gawk, stare, ogle | 反义词：ignore, disregard |  动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rubber-necks at every accident. = 他对每一起事故都探头去看。
They rubber-neck at the parade. = 他们伸长脖子看游行。

一般过去时:
She rubber-necked at the car crash. = 她扭头去看车祸。
We rubber-necked as the police chased the suspect. = 我们扭头看着警察追捕嫌疑犯。

现在完成时:
I have rubber-necked too many times today. = 我今天已经多次东张西望了。
They have rubber-necked at every interesting sight on the trip. = 在这次旅行中，他们对每一个有趣的景象都探头去看。

过去完成时:
He had rubber-necked so much that his neck was sore. = 他看得太多了，脖子都酸了。
We had rubber-necked at the accident before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，我们就已经探头去看事故了。

将来时:
I will rubber-neck if I see something interesting. = 如果我看到什么有趣的事情，我会扭头去看。
They will rubber-neck at the fireworks display. = 他们会伸长脖子看烟花表演。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't rubber-neck so much. = 如果我是你，我就不会那么东张西望。
I wish I hadn't rubber-necked at that fight. = 我真希望我没有去看那场打斗。

被动语态:  (This verb is rarely used passively)
The driver was distracted by the people rubber-necking.  = 司机被那些东张西望的人分散了注意力。(较为少见)
Nothing was gained by rubber-necking at the scene. = 在现场东张西望并没有什么好处。(较为少见)

疑问句:
Did you rubber-neck at the accident? = 你看车祸了吗？
Will you rubber-neck at the procession? = 你会去看游行吗？

否定句:
He didn't rubber-neck at the commotion. = 他没有去看那场骚动。
They didn't rubber-neck, even though the scene was exciting. = 即使现场很激动人心，他们也没有东张西望。
