insensibleness: /ɪnˈsɛnsɪblnəs/ | n. | 无感觉；麻木；不敏感 | The lack of feeling or responsiveness; inability to feel or respond.  It can refer to physical insensitivity (lack of feeling in a body part), emotional insensitivity (lack of empathy or understanding), or intellectual insensitivity (lack of awareness or perception). | 例句：The numbness in his hand was a sign of insensibleness. 他手上的麻木是无感觉的迹象。

The insensibleness of his remarks caused offense. 他话语的麻木不仁引起了反感。


近义词：apathy, insensitivity, numbness, unawareness | 反义词：sensitivity, responsiveness, awareness, feeling


词源:  From "insensible" + "-ness". "Insensible" comes from the Old French *insensible*, from Latin *insensibilis*, from *in-* (not) + *sensibilis* (capable of feeling).  The suffix "-ness" is a common English suffix forming nouns of state or quality.
