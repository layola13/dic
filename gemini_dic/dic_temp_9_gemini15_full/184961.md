retching:/ˈretʃɪŋ/| n. |干呕；呕吐 |The act of trying to vomit, often without successfully bringing anything up.  It is characterized by involuntary spasms of the muscles of the stomach and chest.|例句：The smell made me feel nauseous and start retching.  闻到这个味道让我恶心想吐。|例句：She spent the night retching and groaning. 她整夜都在干呕和呻吟。|近义词：vomiting, heaving, gagging|反义词：swallowing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often retches after eating spicy food. = 他吃辛辣食物后经常干呕。
She retches when she sees blood. = 她看到血就会干呕。

一般过去时:
The baby retched after drinking too much milk. = 宝宝喝太多奶后干呕了。
I retched violently after riding the roller coaster. = 坐在过山车上后我剧烈地干呕。

现在完成时:
He has retched several times this morning. = 他今天早上已经干呕好几次了。
She has been retching all night long. = 她整夜都在干呕。

过去完成时:
She had retched before she finally vomited. = 她最终呕吐之前已经干呕了。
He had retched all day and felt exhausted. = 他整天都在干呕，感到筋疲力尽。

将来时:
I think I will retch if I eat that. = 我想如果我吃那个我会干呕。
She believes she will retch on the boat. = 她相信她会在船上干呕。

虚拟语气:
If I ate that, I think I would retch. = 如果我吃了那个，我想我会干呕。
I wish I hadn't eaten so much; I feel like I'm going to retch. = 我真希望我没有吃那么多；我觉得我要吐了。

被动语态:
(This word is rarely used in the passive voice.  The focus is usually on the person retching.)


疑问句:
Are you retching? = 你在干呕吗？
Has he been retching all day? = 他整天都在干呕吗？


否定句:
I didn't retch, even though I felt nauseous. = 即使我觉得恶心，我也没有干呕。
She didn't retch after the surgery. = 她手术后没有干呕。


PHRASE->
a fit of retching = 一阵干呕
violent retching = 剧烈干呕
spasms of retching = 干呕抽搐
repeated retching = 反复干呕
a bout of retching = 一次干呕


词源:
The word "retching" comes from the Middle English word "wrechchen," meaning "to writhe" or "to twist."  This reflects the physical contortions associated with the act of trying to vomit.  It's related to the Old English word "wræccan," with similar meanings.
