luridly:/ˈlʊrɪdli/| adv.|令人毛骨悚然地，恐怖地，过分艳丽地 |In a shockingly vivid way, especially so as to be sensational or horrifying.  Describes something excessively vivid or sensational, often in a disturbing or unpleasant way.  |例句：The murder was described luridly in the newspapers.  凶杀案在报纸上被描述得极其恐怖。

The accident was luridly recounted by witnesses.目击者绘声绘色地讲述了这场事故。


近义词：gruesomely, ghoulishly, vividly, sensationally |反义词：subtly, faintly, delicately


WORD ORIGIN->
From Middle English *lurid, from Old French *luride, from Latin *lūridus, of uncertain origin, possibly connected with *luere "to wash, cleanse." The original meaning referred to a pale, sickly yellow color; the modern sense developed through association with the ghastly pallor of death.
