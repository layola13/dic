oblation:/ɔːˈbleɪʃən/| n. |供奉；祭品；奉献 |The act of offering something to a deity or a sacred cause; a gift or sacrifice made to a god or religious figure.  |Example: The priest received the oblation of bread and wine.  祭司接受了面包和葡萄酒的供奉。|近义词：offering, sacrifice, donation |反义词：retention, withholding

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The priest accepts many oblations daily. = 祭司每天接受许多供奉。
She makes an oblation to the goddess of love. = 她向爱神献祭。

一般过去时:
They offered an oblation to the spirits of their ancestors. = 他们向祖先的亡灵进行了祭祀。
The ancient civilization made oblations to appease the gods. = 古代文明通过祭祀来安抚神灵。

现在完成时:
He has made many oblations throughout his life. = 他一生做了许多奉献。
The community has completed the oblation ceremony. = 社区完成了祭祀仪式。

过去完成时:
They had already made their oblations before the ceremony began. = 在仪式开始之前，他们已经完成了祭祀。
The worshippers had prepared the oblations for many weeks. = 敬拜者们已经准备了几个星期的祭品。

将来时:
We will offer an oblation at the upcoming festival. = 我们将在即将到来的节日献祭。
She will make an oblation of her first harvest. = 她将献上她第一次收成的祭品。

虚拟语气:
If I had more, I would make a larger oblation. = 如果我有更多，我会做更大的奉献。
I wish I could offer a more significant oblation. = 我希望我能奉献更重要的祭品。

被动语态:

The oblations were carefully prepared. = 祭品被仔细地准备好了。

疑问句:

否定句:
They did not offer an oblation to the angry god. = 他们没有向愤怒的神灵献祭。

词源:
From Late Latin *oblatio, from oblatus, past participle of offerre "to offer."  Ultimately from Latin *ob- "toward, against, before" + *ferre "to carry, bear."
