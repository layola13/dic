rehearse:/rɪˈhɜːrs/| vt. n.|排练；预演；练习|To practice or rehearse something, especially a play or a speech, in preparation for a public performance.  It can also refer to going over something repeatedly to prepare for it.|例句：The actors rehearsed their lines for hours.  演员们排练台词好几个小时了。

The band rehearsed their new song before the concert.乐队在音乐会之前排练了他们的新歌。


近义词：practice, prepare, review|反义词：improvise, wing it|动词
USAGE->
一般现在时:
They rehearse every day. = 他们每天都排练。
She rehearses her speech before every presentation. = 她每次演讲前都会练习演讲稿。

一般过去时:
We rehearsed the play all afternoon. = 我们整个下午都在排练戏剧。
He rehearsed his lines until he felt confident. = 他反复练习台词直到感觉自信为止。

现在完成时:
They have rehearsed the play many times. = 他们已经排练了这出戏很多次了。
She has rehearsed her speech for weeks. = 她已经练习演讲稿好几个星期了。

过去完成时:
They had rehearsed the play before the first performance. = 他们在第一次演出之前已经排练过这出戏了。
She had rehearsed her speech several times before the interview. = 面试前她已经练习过几次演讲稿了。

将来时:
We will rehearse the scene again tomorrow. = 我们明天将再次排练这场戏。
They will rehearse the entire play next week. = 他们下周将排练整出戏。

虚拟语气:
If we had more time, we would rehearse more. = 如果我们有更多的时间，我们会排练更多。
I wish I had rehearsed my presentation better. = 我希望我更好地练习我的演示文稿。

被动语态:
The play was rehearsed extensively. = 这出戏经过了大量的排练。
The speech was rehearsed before the important meeting. = 重要的会议之前，演讲稿进行了排练。

疑问句:
Have they rehearsed yet? = 他们排练过了吗？
Will you rehearse tomorrow? = 你明天会排练吗？

否定句:
They didn't rehearse enough. = 他们排练得不够。
She hasn't rehearsed her lines yet. = 她还没排练她的台词。

PHRASE->
rehearse a speech = 排练演讲
rehearse a play = 排练戏剧
rehearse a scene = 排练一场戏
rehearse a role = 排练一个角色
rehearse for a performance = 为演出排练
