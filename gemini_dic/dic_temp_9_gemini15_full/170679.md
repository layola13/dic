pseudo-Victorians:/ˈsuːdoʊvɪkˈtɔːriənz/|/ˈsjuːdəʊvɪkˈtɔːriənz/| adj.|伪维多利亚式风格的；模仿维多利亚时代风格的|Relating to or characteristic of a style or movement that imitates or evokes the Victorian era (roughly 1837-1901) but lacks its authenticity or depth.  This often manifests in superficial aspects like aesthetics, without capturing the complexities or social context of the original period.  It can refer to art, literature, architecture, fashion, or other cultural productions.|The novel displayed pseudo-Victorian sensibilities, with its melodramatic plot and idealized characters, but lacked the social commentary of its true Victorian counterparts.|近义词：faux-Victorian, neo-Victorian (but neo-Victorian often implies a more conscious and deliberate engagement with Victorian themes)|反义词：authentically Victorian, genuinely Victorian
