deride:/dɪˈraɪd/| vt.|嘲笑；嘲弄；奚落|To speak of or treat with contempt; ridicule.  To laugh at scornfully. |例句：The critics derided his new play as a failure. 评论家们嘲笑他的新剧是一部失败之作。

例句：They derided his efforts as childish. 他们嘲笑他的努力是幼稚的。


近义词：mock, scorn, ridicule, jeer, sneer|反义词：praise, commend, applaud|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He derides his opponents' arguments. = 他嘲笑对手的论点。
They deride the new fashion trend. = 他们嘲笑新的时尚潮流。

一般过去时:
She derided his attempts to cook. = 她嘲笑他做饭的尝试。
The audience derided the comedian's jokes. = 观众嘲笑这位喜剧演员的笑话。

现在完成时:
The media has derided the politician's policies. = 媒体一直嘲笑这位政治家的政策。
They have derided his ideas for years. = 他们多年来一直嘲笑他的想法。

过去完成时:
The critics had already derided the movie before its release. = 电影上映前，评论家们就已经嘲笑这部电影了。
They had derided his work for so long that he gave up.  = 他们嘲笑他的作品那么久，以至于他放弃了。

将来时:
They will deride his efforts no matter what. = 不管怎样，他们都会嘲笑他的努力。
The public will likely deride his new proposal. = 公众很可能会嘲笑他的新提议。

虚拟语气:
If I were to suggest that, they would deride me. = 如果我提出那个建议，他们会嘲笑我。
I wish they wouldn't deride my efforts so much. = 我希望他们不要再那么嘲笑我的努力了。

被动语态:
His ideas were derided by the experts. = 他的想法被专家们嘲笑了。
The proposal was derided as unrealistic. = 这个提案被嘲笑为不切实际的。

疑问句:
Will they deride my suggestion? = 他们会嘲笑我的建议吗？
Why do people deride his work? = 为什么人们嘲笑他的作品？

否定句:
They did not deride his talent. = 他们没有嘲笑他的才能。
I would not deride his attempts, even if they failed. = 即使他失败了，我也不会嘲笑他的尝试。


PHRASE->
to deride someone's efforts = 嘲笑某人的努力
to deride someone's ambition = 嘲笑某人的抱负

词源:
From Old French *derider, from Latin deridere "to deride, mock," from de- "away, down" + ridere "to laugh."
