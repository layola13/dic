dactyls:/ˈdæktɪlz/| n.|音步(诗歌韵律单位)|A dactyl is a metrical foot consisting of one stressed syllable followed by two unstressed syllables.  It's a common metrical foot in poetry, particularly in classical Greek and Latin verse.  The word is also used in botany to refer to a finger-like projection. |Example: The word "poetry" is a dactyl (PO-e-try). The rhythmic pattern of  "murmuring" is also dactylic (MUR-mur-ing).|近义词：trochee (反义), spondee (反义)|反义词：trochee, spondee|名词
