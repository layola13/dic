incriminated:/ɪnˈkrɪmɪneɪtɪd/| adj.|被控告的；被牵连的；|被指控犯有罪行或与罪行有关联。|The evidence incriminated him in the robbery.  证据表明他参与了抢劫案。|accused, implicated, charged|exonerated, acquitted, cleared|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The evidence incriminates him. = 证据表明他犯了罪。
She is incriminated by the witness's testimony. = 证人的证词使她受到了牵连。


一般过去时:
The suspect was incriminated by the fingerprints. = 嫌疑人被指纹所牵连。
The police incriminated him in the murder. = 警方将他牵涉到谋杀案中。


现在完成时:
The investigation has incriminated several high-ranking officials. = 调查已经牵涉到几名高级官员。
He has been incriminated by new evidence. = 新证据使他受到了牵连。


过去完成时:
The witness's testimony had already incriminated the defendant before the lawyer objected. = 在律师提出异议之前，证人的证词已经使被告受到了牵连。
By the time the trial ended, the evidence had incriminated him beyond a reasonable doubt. = 到审判结束时，证据已经将他定罪。


将来时:
The upcoming report will likely incriminate more individuals. =  即将发布的报告可能会牵连更多的人。
New evidence will incriminate the suspect. = 新证据将使嫌疑人受到牵连。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, it would more convincingly incriminate the suspect. = 如果证据更充分，它将更有力地使嫌疑人受到牵连。
If the witness had not testified, he would not have been incriminated. = 如果证人没有作证，他不会被牵连。


被动语态:
He was incriminated by the forged documents. = 他被伪造的文件所牵连。
The politician was incriminated in a corruption scandal. =  这位政治家卷入了一起腐败丑闻。


疑问句:
Was the defendant incriminated by the prosecution's evidence? = 原告的证据是否使被告受到牵连？
Has anyone been incriminated in this case yet? =  在这个案件中，是否有人已经被牵连？


否定句:
The investigation did not incriminate him. = 调查没有牵连到他。
The witness's statement did not incriminate the suspect. = 证人的陈述没有牵连到嫌疑人。


PHRASE->
be incriminated in = 牵涉到...中
incriminated by = 被...所牵连
incriminated evidence = 导致定罪的证据
incriminated testimony = 导致定罪的证词
indirectly incriminated = 间接牵连


词源:
From incriminate + -ed.  Incriminate comes from Latin *incrimināre, from in- "in" + crīmen "accusation".
