reidentified:/ˌriːaɪˈdɛntɪfaɪd/| v. |重新识别，再次识别 | To identify (someone or something) again or anew, often after a period of time or after some change has occurred.  This often implies a previous identification that may have been lost or uncertain.|例句：The stolen painting was reidentified after being found in a private collection.  被盗的画作在私人收藏中被发现后，被重新识别。

The suspect was reidentified through DNA evidence. 嫌疑人通过DNA证据被重新确认身份。

近义词：reinvent, rediscover, reassess, re-examine|反义词：misidentify, overlook
