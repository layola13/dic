discern:/dɪˈsɜːrn/| vt.|辨别；看出；察觉|To perceive or recognize (something) clearly, especially with the intellect.|例句：I could discern a flicker of amusement in his eyes. 我能看出他眼中闪过一丝笑意。|例句：She was able to discern the subtle difference between the two wines. 她能够辨别出两种葡萄酒之间的细微差别。|近义词：distinguish, perceive, recognize, detect|反义词：misunderstand, misinterpret|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He discerns the truth behind the lies. 他辨别出谎言背后的真相。
They discern subtle differences in the artwork. 他们辨别出艺术品中的细微差别。


一般过去时:
She discerned a pattern in the data. 她辨认出数据中的模式。
The detective discerned a clue that led to the solution.  侦探发现了一个线索，从而找到了答案。


现在完成时:
Scientists have discerned a new type of cell. 科学家们已经发现了一种新型细胞。
We have discerned a significant improvement in the product. 我们已经发现产品有了显著的改进。


过去完成时:
After careful examination, the expert had discerned a forgery. 经过仔细检查，专家已经辨别出这是一件赝品。
They had discerned a problem with the system before it failed. 他们在系统故障之前就发现了问题。


将来时:
Future research will discern more details about the phenomenon. 未来的研究将揭示关于这种现象的更多细节。
We will discern the results of the experiment next week. 我们下周将了解实验的结果。


虚拟语气:
If I had better eyesight, I could discern the distant stars. 如果我的视力更好，我就能分辨出远处的星星。
I wish I could discern the meaning of his cryptic message. 我真希望我能理解他那含糊不清的信息。


被动语态:
The truth was discerned eventually. 真相最终被揭露了。
The differences between the two proposals were easily discerned.  这两个提案之间的区别很容易被辨别出来。


疑问句:

否定句:
