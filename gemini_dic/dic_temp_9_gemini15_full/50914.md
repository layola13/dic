deserving:/dɪˈzɜːrvɪŋ/| adj. |应得的；值得的 |Worthy of being given or received in return for effort, merit, or behavior.  It implies a right or entitlement based on actions or qualities. |例句：She's deserving of all the praise she's receiving. 她理应得到她所获得的所有赞扬。

例句：His hard work is deserving of a promotion. 他的努力工作值得升职。


近义词：worthy, merited, entitled, justified |反义词：undeserving, unworthy, unjustified |词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is deserving of a reward. = 他应该得到奖励。
The team is deserving of victory. = 这个团队值得胜利。

一般过去时:
She was deserving of our sympathy. = 她值得我们同情。
They were deserving of punishment. = 他们应该受到惩罚。

现在完成时:
He has been deserving of recognition for years. = 多年来，他一直应该得到认可。
She has deserved this success. = 她应得这份成功。

过去完成时:
They had been deserving of help, but no one offered it. = 他们本来应该得到帮助，但没有人提供。
He had deserved a better fate. = 他本应该得到更好的命运。

将来时:
She will be deserving of a raise soon. = 她很快就会值得加薪。
They will deserve the accolades they receive. = 他们将当之无愧地获得赞誉。

虚拟语气:
If he had worked harder, he would have been deserving of the promotion. = 如果他工作更努力，他将值得升职。
She deserves a break, were she to work this hard.  = 如果她这么努力工作，她就应该休息一下。


被动语态:
The award was deserved by the winner. = 奖项是赢家应得的。(被动语态更常用在"deserved"上而不是"deserving")
His efforts were deserving of praise. = 他的努力值得赞扬。

疑问句:
Is he deserving of a second chance? = 他值得得到第二次机会吗？
Are their actions deserving of condemnation? = 他们的行为值得谴责吗？

否定句:
He is not deserving of such harsh criticism. = 他不值得受到如此严厉的批评。
Their behavior was not deserving of reward. = 他们的行为不值得奖励。


PHRASE->
be deserving of = 值得…
deserving praise = 值得称赞
highly deserving = 非常值得
well-deserving = 非常值得
more than deserving = 非常值得


词源:
The word "deserving" comes from the Old English word "deserian," meaning "to deserve."  This in turn has roots in the Latin word "deservire," meaning "to serve," "to merit," or "to deserve."  The "-ing" suffix transforms the verb into a present participle, functioning as an adjective.
