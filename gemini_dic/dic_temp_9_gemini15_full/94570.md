impounding: /ɪmˈpaʊndɪŋ/ | v. | 扣押；充公；积聚；(水)积聚 |  To seize and keep (something, especially property) legally, usually as security or payment for a debt.  |  例句：The police impounded the car because it was being driven without insurance.  警方扣押了那辆车，因为它没有保险。

例句：The river impounded a large amount of water after the heavy rain.  大雨过后，河里积聚了大量的水。


近义词：confiscate, seize, impound, retain, accumulate | 反义词：release, liberate, free


USAGE->

The police impound vehicles regularly. = 警方经常扣押车辆。
The dam impounds water for irrigation. = 水坝蓄水用于灌溉。
