exulcerative: /ɪɡˈzʌlsəreɪtɪv/ | adj. | 溃疡性的；腐蚀性的 | Causing or characterized by the formation of ulcers.  This adjective describes something that produces or is associated with ulcers, which are open sores or lesions on the skin or mucous membranes. | Example:  The patient presented with exulcerative lesions on their lower extremities.  这位病人下肢出现溃疡性病变。


近义词: ulcerating, ulcerogenic, erosive

反义词: healing, soothing, restorative

词源:  The word "exulcerative" is derived from the Latin word "ulcus," meaning "sore" or "ulcer,"  with the prefix "ex-" indicating "out of" or "from," and the suffix "-ative" indicating "tending to" or "causing."  Therefore, exulcerative literally means "causing sores" or "producing ulcers."
