metatarsal:/ˌmɛtəˈtɑːrsəl/| n.|跖骨|Any of the five long bones in the foot extending from the ankle to the toes.  They form the midfoot and provide support and mobility.  Metatarsals are numbered from one to five, starting from the big toe.|例句：The fracture of his metatarsal kept him off the court for several weeks.他的跖骨骨折使他几周都不能上场。

例句：She injured one of her metatarsals during a marathon.她在马拉松比赛中伤到了一根跖骨。


近义词：midfoot bone


反义词：无

词源:
From Modern Latin *metatarsus, from Greek μετατάρσιος (metatársios), from μετά (metá) "after" + ταρσός (tarsós) "tarsus".  The "tarsus" refers to the ankle bones.  Therefore, "metatarsal" literally means "after the tarsus" or "beyond the tarsus".
