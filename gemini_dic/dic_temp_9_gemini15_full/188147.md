/raʊst/| vt.| 唤醒；驱逐；猛推；使…振作起来|To rouse someone from sleep or inactivity; to forcibly remove someone from a place; to push or shove roughly.| The farmer roused the sleeping workers before dawn.  = 农民在天亮前唤醒了正在睡觉的工人。
The police roused the protesters from the building. = 警察将抗议者从大楼里驱逐出去。|wake, stir, expel|sleep, stay|


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He rouses his children every morning at 7 a.m. = 他每天早上七点叫醒他的孩子们。
The guard rouses the sleeping soldiers regularly. = 警卫定期叫醒熟睡的士兵。


一般过去时:
She roused herself from her chair and walked to the window. = 她从椅子上站起来走到窗边。
The loud noise roused me from my sleep. = 巨响把我吵醒了。


现在完成时:
The news has roused public anger. = 这则新闻激起了公众的愤怒。
The speech has roused the audience to action. = 这篇演讲激起了听众的行动。


过去完成时:
He had roused himself to face the challenge. = 他已经振作起来去面对挑战。
The storm had roused the sea to a frenzy. = 暴风雨已将大海激怒成狂暴状态。


将来时:
The announcement will rouse much debate. = 这则公告将会引起激烈的争论。
The coming election will rouse the voters' enthusiasm. = 即将到来的选举将会激起选民的热情。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would rouse myself and get to work. = 如果我是你，我会振作起来开始工作。
If the alarm had roused him, he wouldn't have missed the train. = 如果闹钟把他吵醒的话，他就不会错过火车了。


被动语态:
The sleeping passengers were roused by the sudden stop of the train. = 乘客们被火车突然刹车吵醒了。
The crowd was roused by the passionate speech. = 人群被热情洋溢的演讲所鼓舞。


疑问句:
What roused you from your slumber? = 是什么把你从睡梦中惊醒？
What will rouse the public from their apathy? = 什么才能唤醒公众的冷漠？


否定句:
Nothing could rouse him from his depression. = 什么也无法让他从沮丧中振作起来。
The gentle breeze did not rouse the sleeping cat. = 轻柔的微风没有唤醒熟睡的猫。


PHRASE->
rouse oneself = 振作起来
rouse to action = 激励行动
rouse suspicion = 引起怀疑
rouse the public = 激起民愤
rouse interest = 激发兴趣

词源:
源自古英语 *hrūsian，意思是“摇动，搅动”。
