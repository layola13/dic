incapacitated: /ɪnˈkæpəseɪtɪd/ | adj. | 无能力的；丧失能力的 | rendered incapable; disabled.  | The accident incapacitated him for several months.  事故使他丧失能力数月。| disabled, disabled, crippled, unfit | able, capable, competent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is incapacitated by illness. = 他因病丧失了能力。
The storm incapacitated the power grid. = 暴风雨使电网瘫痪。


一般过去时:
The stroke incapacitated him. = 中风使他丧失了能力。
The earthquake incapacitated the city's infrastructure. = 地震使该市的基础设施瘫痪。


现在完成时:
The injury has incapacitated him for life. = 这伤让他终身残疾。
Years of overwork have incapacitated her. = 多年的过度工作使她丧失了能力。


过去完成时:
He had been incapacitated by illness before the accident. = 他在事故发生前就已经因病丧失了能力。
The company had been incapacitated by the scandal. = 这家公司因丑闻而瘫痪。


将来时:

The virus may incapacitate many people. = 病毒可能会使许多人丧失能力。


虚拟语气:

疑问句:
