panpsychism:/ˈpænˈsaɪkɪzəm/| n. |泛心论|The philosophical view that consciousness or mind is a fundamental and ubiquitous feature of reality, not an emergent property of complex systems.  It suggests that even basic physical entities, like atoms or elementary particles, possess some rudimentary form of consciousness or experience.| Panpsychism is a controversial theory, but it offers a potential solution to the hard problem of consciousness. |近义词：universal consciousness, mind-stuff theory|反义词：materialism, physicalism
