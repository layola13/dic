calumniate:/kəˈlʌmnieɪt/| vt. |诽谤；中伤；污蔑 |To make false and defamatory statements about someone.  |例句：He was calumniated by his rivals for years.  他多年来一直受到对手的诽谤。|例句：Don't calumniate your friend behind his back. 不要在背后诽谤你的朋友。|近义词：slander, defame, vilify, traduce |反义词：praise, commend, extol


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He calumniates his opponents in every speech. = 他在每一次演讲中都诽谤他的对手。
She calumniates her colleagues regularly. = 她经常诽谤她的同事。


一般过去时:
They calumniated the mayor in the newspaper. = 他们在报纸上诽谤了市长。
The politician calumniated his rival during the debate. = 在辩论中，这位政治家诽谤了他的对手。


现在完成时:
He has calumniated many people throughout his career. = 他在职业生涯中诽谤过很多人。
She has calumniated her former boss to numerous people. = 她向许多人诽谤过她以前的上司。


过去完成时:
They had calumniated him before he even had a chance to defend himself. = 在他甚至没有机会为自己辩护之前，他们就诽谤了他。
The company had calumniated its competitor for months before the lawsuit. = 在诉讼之前，该公司几个月来一直在诽谤其竞争对手。


将来时:
They will calumniate him again in the next election. = 他们在下一次选举中还会再次诽谤他。
She will calumniate her neighbor to the building manager. = 她会向大楼经理诽谤她的邻居。


虚拟语气:
If he calumniated her, she would sue him. = 如果他诽谤她，她就会起诉他。
I wish they hadn't calumniated my reputation. = 我希望他们没有诽谤我的名誉。


被动语态:
He was calumniated by the press. = 他被媒体诽谤了。
His reputation was calumniated by false accusations. = 他的名誉因虚假指控而受损。


疑问句:
Were they calumniated unfairly? = 他们是否受到了不公平的诽谤？
Has she ever been calumniated in the past? = 她过去是否曾被诽谤过？


否定句:
He did not calumniate her; he simply stated the facts. = 他没有诽谤她；他只是陈述了事实。
They would never calumniate someone without proof. = 他们绝不会在没有证据的情况下诽谤某人。



PHRASE->
falsely calumniate = 虚假诽谤
publicly calumniate = 公开诽谤
viciously calumniate = 恶毒诽谤
repeatedly calumniate = 反复诽谤
maliciously calumniate = 恶意诽谤


词源:
From Latin *calumniari, from calumnia "false accusation," from calvus "bald, bare," perhaps referring to a bald head as a sign of shame or disgrace.
