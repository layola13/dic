sideshow:/ˈsaɪdʃoʊ/| n. |副表演；次要事件；无关紧要的事物 |A sideshow is a secondary event or attraction, often less important than the main event.  It can also refer to something that distracts attention from the main issue. |例句：The political scandal became a sideshow to the main economic debate.  政治丑闻成为主要经济辩论的次要事件。

The circus sideshow featured a bearded lady and a strongman. 马戏团的副表演节目包括一位长胡子的女士和一位大力士。|近义词：diversion, distraction, minor event |反义词：main event, central issue
