dampen:/ˈdæmpən/ | vt. adj.|使潮湿；抑制；减弱 | to make something slightly wet; to make something less strong or successful |例句：The rain dampened the ground.  雨水使地面潮湿。

The bad news dampened our spirits.  坏消息使我们泄气。


近义词：moisten,  depress, subdue, weaken |反义词：dry,  intensify, enhance, strengthen |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rain dampens the mood. = 雨水使气氛低沉。
He dampens his enthusiasm with a sigh. = 他叹了口气，抑制了自己的热情。


一般过去时:
The news dampened her hopes. = 这个消息使她失去了希望。
The sudden downpour dampened the bonfire. = 突如其来的暴雨浇灭了篝火。


现在完成时:
The constant criticism has dampened his spirit. = 持续的批评已经打击了他的精神。
The long drought has dampened the crops. = 长期的干旱已经损害了庄稼。


过去完成时:
They had dampened the fire before leaving. = 他们离开前已经把火弄灭了。
The prior setbacks had dampened their optimism. = 先前的挫折已经削弱了他们的乐观情绪。


将来时:
The upcoming storm will dampen the outdoor party. = 即将来临的风暴将会影响户外派对。
I fear the bad weather will dampen the festival. = 我担心恶劣天气会影响节日。


虚拟语气:
If it rained, it would dampen the picnic. = 如果下雨，野餐就会受影响。
I wish the criticism hadn't dampened his confidence. = 我真希望批评没有打击他的信心。


被动语态:
Their enthusiasm was dampened by the failure. = 他们的热情被失败所打击。
The fire was dampened by the rain. = 火被雨水浇灭了。


疑问句:
Will the rain dampen the parade? = 雨会影响游行吗？
Does the cold weather dampen your spirits? = 冷天气会使你情绪低落吗？


否定句:
The good news didn't dampen their spirits. = 好消息并没有使他们情绪低落。
Nothing could dampen his enthusiasm for the project. = 没有任何事情能够抑制他对这个项目的热情。
