captivater:/ˈkæptɪveɪtər/| n. |迷人的人或事物，吸引人的事物 |Something or someone that captivates; something that holds the attention.|The captivating performance left the audience speechless.  令人着迷的表演让观众说不出话来。|近义词：fascinator, charmer, enchantress |反义词：repellent, bore


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The magician captivates his audience with amazing tricks.  魔术师用令人惊叹的魔术吸引了他的观众。
Her beauty captivates everyone who sees her. 她的美丽吸引了每一个看到她的人。


一般过去时:
The story captivated me from beginning to end.  这个故事从头到尾都吸引着我。
The music captivated the listeners. 音乐吸引了听众。


现在完成时:
The artist's work has captivated art lovers for decades. 艺术家的作品几十年来一直吸引着艺术爱好者。
This novel has captivated me completely. 这部小说完全吸引了我。


过去完成时:
He had been captivated by her charm since their first meeting. 从他们第一次见面起，他就已经被她的魅力所吸引。
I had been captivated by the beauty of the scenery before I realized how late it was. 在我意识到时间已经很晚之前，我就已经被风景的美丽所吸引住了。


将来时:
The new exhibit will captivate visitors with its stunning visuals. 新展览将以其令人惊叹的视觉效果吸引游客。
I think that this movie will captivate you. 我认为这部电影会吸引你。


虚拟语气:
If only I could find something that would captivate me like that again.  如果我能再次找到那样吸引我的东西就好了。
If the play were more captivating, more people would come to see it. 如果这出戏更吸引人，就会有更多人来看。


被动语态:

Many viewers were captivated by the story of the film. 许多观众被电影的故事所吸引。


疑问句:
What captivated you most about the trip?  你对这次旅行最感兴趣的是什么？

否定句:

The lecture failed to captivate the students. 讲座未能吸引学生。
