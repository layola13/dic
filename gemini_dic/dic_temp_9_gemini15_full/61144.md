eloquential: /ɪˈlɒkwənt/ /ɛˈloʊkwənt/ | adj. |雄辩的；口才好的；有说服力的 | eloquent means fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing.  It suggests a skillful and effective use of language to express ideas or feelings.  The word often implies a certain grace and power in the delivery or presentation. |例句：His eloquent speech moved the audience to tears.  他的雄辩的演讲使听众感动得流泪。

His eloquent defense of the accused won him the case. 他为被告所作的雄辩的辩护为他赢得了官司。


近义词：articulate, fluent, persuasive, expressive, silver-tongued |反义词：inarticulate, speechless, tongue-tied, ineloquent


词源:  The word "eloquent" comes from the Latin word "eloquens," meaning "able to speak well" or "fluent of speech."  This, in turn, is derived from the verb "eloqui," meaning "to speak out" or "to express oneself."
