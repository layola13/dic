courses:/ˈkɔːrsɪz/| n.|课程；过程；一道菜|Courses are series of lessons or classes on a particular subject.  They can also refer to the direction or path something takes, or a series of events. In culinary terms, a course is a single dish served as part of a meal.|例句：He's taking a course in advanced calculus. 他正在上高等微积分课程。|例句：The river takes a southerly course. 这条河向南流去。|近义词：classes, lessons, subjects, sequence, progression|反义词：single item, one-off


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The course covers many topics. = 这门课程涵盖许多主题。
Many courses are offered this semester. = 这个学期开设了很多课程。

一般过去时:
I took a cooking course last year. = 我去年上了一门烹饪课。
The race followed a challenging course. = 比赛沿着一条充满挑战的路线进行。

现在完成时:
She has completed all her courses. = 她已经完成了所有的课程。
The river has changed its course several times. = 这条河的河道已经改变过几次了。

过去完成时:
He had finished the course before the deadline. = 他在截止日期之前完成了课程。
The ship had altered its course to avoid the storm. = 船舶已经改变航向以避开风暴。

将来时:
I will enroll in a photography course next month. = 我下个月将报名参加摄影课程。
The rocket will follow a predetermined course. = 火箭将沿着预定的轨道飞行。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would take a painting course. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会去上绘画课。
I wish I had taken a more challenging course. = 我希望我以前选修一门更有挑战性的课程。

被动语态:
The course is taught by Professor Smith. = 这门课由史密斯教授讲授。
The course was canceled due to low enrollment. = 由于报名人数太少，课程被取消了。

疑问句:
What courses are you taking this semester? = 这个学期你上什么课？
What is the best course of action? = 最好的行动方案是什么？

否定句:
He didn't take any courses last summer. = 他去年夏天没有上任何课程。
The project did not follow the planned course. = 项目没有按照计划进行。
