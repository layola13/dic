rehearsed:/rɪˈhɜːrst/ | vt. adj.|排练；预演；练习；熟练的 | To practice or rehearse something, especially a play or speech, in preparation for a performance.  |例句：The actors rehearsed the play for weeks before the opening night.  演员们在开幕之夜之前排练了几个星期的戏剧。

The band rehearsed their new song until it was perfect. 乐队反复排练他们的新歌直到完美无缺。


近义词：practiced, prepared, reviewed | 反义词：improvised, impromptu


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They rehearse every day. = 他们每天排练。
He rehearses his speech before important meetings. = 他在重要会议前排练他的演讲。


一般过去时:
She rehearsed the song until she knew all the words. = 她反复排练这首歌直到记住所有歌词。
We rehearsed the scene several times. = 我们排练了这个场景好几次。


现在完成时:
The orchestra has rehearsed the symphony for months. = 乐队已经排练这首交响乐好几个月了。
They have rehearsed this play many times. = 他们排练过这出戏很多次。


过去完成时:
They had rehearsed the play several times before the final performance. = 在最终演出之前，他们已经排练了这出戏好几次了。
She had rehearsed her lines perfectly. = 她已经把台词排练得非常完美。


将来时:
We will rehearse the dance routine tomorrow. = 我们明天将排练舞蹈套路。
The choir will rehearse their piece before the concert. = 合唱团将在音乐会前排练他们的曲目。


虚拟语气:
If they had rehearsed more, they would have performed better. = 如果他们排练更多，他们的表演会更好。
I wish I had rehearsed my presentation more thoroughly. = 我希望我更彻底地排练我的演示文稿。


被动语态:
The play was rehearsed for six weeks. = 这出戏排练了六个星期。
The speech was carefully rehearsed before delivery. = 演讲在发表前经过仔细的排练。


疑问句:
Have you rehearsed your part? = 你排练过你的部分了吗？
Did they rehearse the play last night? = 他们昨晚排练这出戏了吗？


否定句:
They didn't rehearse enough. = 他们排练得不够。
She hadn't rehearsed her lines before the audition. = 她试镜前没有排练过她的台词。
