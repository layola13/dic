leak-out: /ˈliːk aʊt/ | v. | 泄露；走漏；渗出 |  To escape or become known gradually or unintentionally.  |例句：News of the merger leaked out before the official announcement.  合并的消息在正式宣布前就泄露了出去。|近义词：escape, divulge, seep out |反义词：conceal, keep secret, suppress |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The information leaks out slowly. = 信息慢慢泄露出来。
Secrets leak out from time to time. = 秘密不时地泄露出来。


一般过去时:
The news leaked out last night. = 消息昨晚泄露了。
Details of the plan leaked out to the press. = 该计划的细节泄露给了媒体。


现在完成时:
The story has leaked out to the public. = 故事已经泄露给了公众。
Rumors have leaked out about a possible takeover. = 关于可能收购的谣言已经泄露出来。


过去完成时:
The scandal had leaked out before the meeting. = 丑闻在会议之前就已经泄露了。
Word of the layoffs had leaked out among the employees. = 裁员的消息已经在员工中泄露了。


将来时:
The truth will leak out eventually. = 真相最终会泄露出来。
I'm afraid the secret will leak out soon. = 我担心秘密很快就会泄露。


虚拟语气:
If the truth leaked out, there would be chaos. = 如果真相泄露，就会造成混乱。
I wish the information hadn't leaked out. = 我希望信息没有泄露。


被动语态:
The information was leaked out by a disgruntled employee. = 信息被一位不满的员工泄露了。
The details of the project were leaked out to the competitor. = 项目的细节泄露给了竞争对手。


疑问句:
How did the secret leak out? = 秘密是怎么泄露的？
Did the news leak out on purpose? = 消息是故意泄露的吗？


否定句:
The company tried to prevent the information from leaking out. = 公司试图阻止信息泄露。
The secret didn't leak out until months later. = 秘密直到几个月后才泄露。
