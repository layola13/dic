noble:/ˈnoʊbl/ | /ˈnəʊbl/ | adj., n. | 高贵的；贵族的；崇高的；高尚的 |

英文解析:
Of an elevated rank or status; belonging to the nobility.  Also used to describe something of high moral quality; excellent.  As a noun, it refers to a person of noble birth or high rank.


例句:
He comes from a noble family. = 他出身于名门望族。

She showed noble character in the face of adversity. = 面对逆境，她表现出了高尚的品格。


近义词:
high-born, aristocratic, refined, elevated, exalted, superior, honourable, virtuous


反义词:
ignoble, base, low, mean, vulgar, common


词性:
adjective, noun

词源:
From Old French *noble, from Latin nobilis "well-known, distinguished, famous," from  gnōscere "to know."  The sense shifted from "well-known" to "of high social standing" and then to "morally excellent."
