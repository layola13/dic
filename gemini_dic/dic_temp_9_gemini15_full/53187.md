dipsomaniac:/ˌdɪpsəˈmeɪniæk/| n. |酒瘾患者，嗜酒者 |A dipsomaniac is a person who suffers from dipsomania, an uncontrollable craving for alcoholic drinks.  They have a chronic and compulsive need to drink alcohol.  This is often associated with severe withdrawal symptoms when alcohol is not consumed. |例句：He was a known dipsomaniac, and his drinking eventually led to his demise. 他是一个众所周知的酒瘾患者，他的酗酒最终导致了他的死亡。


The unfortunate consequence of his dipsomaniac tendencies was the loss of his job and family. 他嗜酒的恶果是失去了工作和家庭。|近义词：alcoholic, drunkard, sot |反义词：teetotaler, abstainer

词源:
The word "dipsomaniac" is derived from the Greek words "dipsa" (δίψα), meaning "thirst," and "mania" (μανία), meaning "madness" or "insanity."  It literally translates to "mad thirst," reflecting the uncontrollable craving for alcohol characteristic of the condition.
