caravel:/ˈkærəvəl/| n. |轻型帆船|A small, fast sailing ship, especially one used by the Portuguese and Spanish in the 15th and 16th centuries for exploration and trade.  It was characterized by its lateen sails, which allowed it to sail close to the wind.|例句：Portuguese caravels were instrumental in exploring the coasts of Africa and the Americas.  葡萄牙轻型帆船在探索非洲和美洲海岸方面发挥了重要作用。|近义词：carrack, galleon (though larger)|反义词：None specifically, but terms suggesting larger, slower ships could be considered antonyms.
