half-afloat:/ˌhæf əˈfloʊt/ | adj. | 半浮的；勉强维持的 | Partially afloat; barely managing to stay afloat, either literally or figuratively.  The term suggests a precarious state, close to sinking or failure. |例句：The small boat was half-afloat, tossed about by the waves.  小船半浮在水上，被海浪冲来冲去。

The struggling company was half-afloat, barely making enough money to pay its bills.  这家苦苦挣扎的公司勉强维持，勉强赚够钱支付账单。|近义词：partially submerged, barely afloat, precarious, unstable |反义词：fully afloat, submerged, stable, secure


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "to be".  Therefore, tense variations are not applicable.  Its usage focuses on describing a state of being.

例句1: The ship, damaged in the storm, was left half-afloat.  (暴风雨中受损的船只，留在了半浮状态。)
例句2:  His business, after the economic downturn, was half-afloat. (经济衰退后，他的生意勉强维持。)


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly incorporating "half-afloat".  The term itself functions as a standalone descriptive adjective.
