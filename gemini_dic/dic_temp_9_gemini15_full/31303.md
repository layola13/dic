chaplain:/ˈtʃæplɪn/| n.|牧师，随军牧师|A chaplain is a member of the clergy, especially a Christian priest or minister, attached to a particular institution or group, such as the army or a hospital, to conduct religious services.|例句：The chaplain offered prayers for the soldiers before the battle.  随军牧师在战斗前为士兵们祈祷。|例句：The hospital chaplain visited the sick patients daily. 医院牧师每天探望生病的病人。|近义词：priest, minister, padre|反义词：None specifically, but the antonym would depend on the context (e.g., atheist, non-believer).
