preregulate:/ˌpriːˈreɡjuleɪt/| v.|预先管制，预先规定|To regulate something before it happens or is put into effect.|The government decided to preregulate the new technology to ensure its safe use.  政府决定预先管制这项新技术以确保其安全使用。|近义词：predetermine, predefine, control in advance|反义词：deregulate, leave unregulated


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They preregulate the import of certain goods. = 他们预先管制某些商品的进口。
The committee preregulates the competition rules. = 委员会预先规定比赛规则。


一般过去时:
The agency preregulated the drug's release. = 该机构预先管制了该药物的发布。
They preregulated the construction project's timeline. = 他们预先规定了建筑项目的进度安排。


现在完成时:
The city has preregulated the use of fireworks. = 该城市已经预先管制了烟花的燃放。
The company has preregulated its emission levels.  = 该公司已经预先规定了其排放水平。


过去完成时:
They had preregulated the event's security measures. = 他们预先规定了该活动的安保措施。
The council had preregulated the zoning laws. = 理事会已预先制定了分区法律。


将来时:
The government will preregulate the new financial products. = 政府将预先管制新的金融产品。
They will preregulate the internet access. = 他们将预先管制互联网接入。


虚拟语气:
If they had preregulated the process, it would have been smoother. = 如果他们预先管制了这个流程，它就会更顺利。
It would be better if they preregulated the risks involved. = 如果他们预先管制相关的风险，将会更好。


被动语态:
The new regulations were preregulated by the authorities. = 新的法规由当局预先制定。
The safety protocols were preregulated before the experiment. = 安全规程在实验前已预先规定。


疑问句:
Did they preregulate the product launch? = 他们是否预先管制了产品发布？
Will the company preregulate its marketing campaign? = 公司是否会预先管制其营销活动？


否定句:
They did not preregulate the software update. = 他们没有预先管制软件更新。
The committee did not preregulate the meeting's agenda. = 委员会没有预先规定会议议程。
