quadrireme:/ˈkwɒdrɪriːm/| n. |四桨战船|A quadrireme was an ancient Greek and Roman warship propelled by four banks of oars.  It was a significant development in naval warfare, representing a substantial increase in size and power compared to earlier triremes.  The arrangement of oars and rowers required sophisticated engineering and organization.  Quadriremes were heavily armed and played a crucial role in naval battles of the classical era.|The Roman navy used quadriremes extensively in their campaigns throughout the Mediterranean. |近义词：warship, galley|反义词：无
