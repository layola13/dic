constituter:/ˈkɒnstɪtjuːtə(r)/| n. v.|构成者；组成部分；制定者|To make up or form something; to establish or create something.|The various parts constitute the whole machine.  各种零件构成整台机器。|The committee constituted itself into a working group. 委员会组成一个工作小组。|component, element, ingredient|destructor, dissolver


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The atoms constitute the molecule. = 原子构成分子。
These elements constitute a new compound. = 这些元素构成了一种新的化合物。


一般过去时:
The treaty constituted a significant step towards peace. = 这项条约是迈向和平的重要一步。
They constituted a new company last year. = 他们去年成立了一家新公司。


现在完成时:
The evidence has constituted a strong case against the defendant. = 证据已经构成了对被告的有力指控。
Many factors have constituted the current economic crisis. = 许多因素构成了当前的经济危机。


过去完成时:
The agreement had constituted a major breakthrough in negotiations. = 该协议在谈判中取得了重大突破。
Before the war, they had constituted a powerful alliance. = 在战争之前，他们组成了一个强大的联盟。


将来时:
The new law will constitute a major change in policy. = 新法律将构成一项重大政策变化。
The results of the election will constitute a mandate for change. = 选举结果将构成变革的授权。


虚拟语气:
If this evidence were to constitute sufficient proof, the case would be closed. = 如果这项证据足以构成充分的证据，案件就会结案。
If the board were to constitute a committee, it could address the issues more efficiently. = 如果董事会成立一个委员会，它就能更有效地解决这些问题。


被动语态:
The committee was constituted by the president. = 委员会是由总统组成的。
The new government was constituted after the election. = 新政府是在选举后组成的。


疑问句:
What constitutes a fair trial? = 什么才算公平审判？
What constitutes plagiarism in academic writing? = 学术写作中的剽窃行为是什么？


否定句:
The evidence did not constitute sufficient grounds for prosecution. = 证据不足以构成起诉理由。
The agreement did not constitute a binding contract. = 该协议并非具有约束力的合同。
