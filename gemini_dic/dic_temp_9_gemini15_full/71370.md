flagellator:/ˈflædʒəleɪtər/| n. |鞭毛虫；带鞭毛的生物|A flagellator is a single-celled organism that possesses one or more flagella, which are whip-like appendages used for locomotion.|Example:  Many protists, such as Euglena, are flagellators.|近义词：flagellate, ciliate|反义词：None specifically, but organisms without flagella could be considered antonyms in a contextual sense.
