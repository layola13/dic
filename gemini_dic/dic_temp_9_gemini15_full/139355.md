obscure:/əbˈskjʊr/| adj.|模糊的；晦涩的；鲜为人知的|difficult to understand or interpret; not clearly expressed or easily understood; unknown to most people. |例句：The meaning of the poem is obscure. 这首诗的意思很晦涩。

例句：His early life remains obscure.  他早年的生活鲜为人知。


近义词：unclear, vague, enigmatic, mysterious, unknown

反义词：clear, plain, obvious, well-known, famous


词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The instructions are obscure. = 说明很模糊。
The artist's work is obscure. =这位艺术家的作品鲜为人知。


一般过去时:
The message was obscure. = 这条信息很模糊。
His origins were obscure. =他的出身不明。


现在完成时:
The details have become obscure over time. = 细节随着时间的推移变得模糊了。
His contributions have remained obscure until recently. = 直到最近，他的贡献才为人所知。


过去完成时:

The path had been obscure, hidden by overgrown vegetation. =这条路很隐蔽，被茂密的植被遮挡住了。


将来时:

His legacy will remain obscure without further research. = 没有进一步的研究，他的遗产将依然鲜为人知。
