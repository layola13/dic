misgrade:/mɪsˈɡreɪd/| vt.|误评，错等，打错分数|To assign the wrong grade or mark to (something, especially a student's work).  This typically refers to an error in grading, not a deliberate act of unfairness. |例句：The teacher misgraded several papers, giving some students lower scores than they deserved.  老师误判了几份试卷，给一些学生打了低于他们应得的分数。

The professor misgraded my exam; I actually scored higher than what was recorded. 教授误判了我的考试；我的实际分数比记录的分数高。|近义词：misjudge, misrate, err in grading |反义词：grade correctly, assess accurately |动词
