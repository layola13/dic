hazarded:/ˈhæzərd/| vt.|冒险；尝试；冒昧地说；|to put (something) at risk; to express (something) in a tentative or cautious way; to attempt something risky or dangerous.|例句：He hazarded a guess at the answer. 他冒险猜测答案。

He hazarded a remark about the weather. 他冒昧谈论了一下天气。


近义词：risk, venture, gamble, speculate|反义词：guarantee, ensure, secure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hazards a guess every day. = 他每天都冒险猜测。
She hazards a new business idea. = 她冒险尝试一个新的商业创意。


一般过去时:
They hazarded a trip across the mountains. = 他们冒险穿越了山脉。
He hazarded a comment on the controversial issue. = 他对有争议的问题发表了评论。


现在完成时:
She has hazarded several investments this year. = 她今年已经冒险进行了几项投资。
He has hazarded his reputation by speaking out. = 他因公开发表意见而冒了名誉风险。


过去完成时:
They had hazarded their lives to save the child. = 为了救孩子，他们已经冒着生命危险。
She had hazarded a guess, but it turned out to be wrong. = 她冒险猜测了一下，但结果是错的。


将来时:
He will hazard a climb up the dangerous cliff. = 他将冒险攀登危险的悬崖。
They will hazard a new approach to the problem. = 他们将尝试一种解决问题的新方法。


虚拟语气:
If he hazarded a guess, he might be right. = 如果他冒险猜测一下，他可能对了。
I wish I hadn't hazarded my savings on that investment. = 我真希望我没有把积蓄冒险投资到那上面。


被动语态:
A guess was hazarded at the meeting. = 会议上有人冒险猜测了一下。
His reputation was hazarded by his actions. = 他的名誉因他的行为而受到损害。


疑问句:
Would you hazard a guess at the solution? = 你敢冒险猜测一下解决方案吗？
Did he hazard an opinion on the matter? = 他对这件事发表了意见吗？


否定句:
He did not hazard a guess. = 他没有冒险猜测。
They did not hazard a journey in the storm. = 他们在暴风雨中没有冒险旅行。


PHRASE->
hazard a guess: 冒险猜测
hazard an opinion: 冒昧发表意见
hazard a suggestion: 冒昧提出建议
hazard a comment: 冒昧评论
hazard a journey: 冒险旅行


词源:
源自古法语 *hasard，最终源自阿拉伯语 az-zahr（骰子）。  最初指游戏中的风险或偶然性，后来引申出更广泛的“冒险”含义。
