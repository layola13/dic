haranguer:/ˈhærənɡər/| n. |长篇大论者；慷慨激昂的演说者 | A haranguer is a person who delivers a long, passionate, and often aggressive speech, typically one that is intended to persuade or incite the audience.  They often use strong language and emotional appeals. | Example: The politician was a skilled haranguer, capable of captivating large crowds with his fiery speeches.  这位政治家是一位熟练的长篇大论者，能够以他热情的演讲吸引大批人群。|近义词：lecturer, orator, demagogue |反义词：listener, silent observer |名词

词源:
The word "haranguer" comes from the French word "harangue," which in turn originates from the Spanish word "arenga."  Ultimately, its roots trace back to the Old French "harangue," meaning "a noisy speech."
