predicting: /prɪˈdɪktɪŋ/ | v. | 预言；预测 |  The act of saying or estimating that something will happen in the future, based on known facts or information.  It involves making a statement about a future event based on evidence or reasoning. |


例句：
The scientists are predicting a severe storm. = 科学家们预测将有一场严重的暴风雨。

The model accurately predicting customer behavior. = 该模型准确地预测了客户行为。


近义词：forecasting, foretelling, anticipating, projecting, estimating


反义词：underestimating


词性：动词 (verb)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He predicts the future. = 他预测未来。
She predicts the weather every day. = 她每天都预测天气。


一般过去时:
He predicted the winner of the race. = 他预测了比赛的获胜者。
They predicted a successful outcome. = 他们预测结果会成功。


现在完成时:
Scientists have predicted a rise in sea levels. = 科学家们已经预测到海平面上升。
She has predicted the market crash correctly. = 她准确地预测了市场崩盘。


过去完成时:
The analysts had predicted a recession. = 分析师们已经预测到经济衰退。
They had predicted the earthquake's location. = 他们已经预测了地震的位置。


将来时:
The meteorologist will predict the path of the hurricane. = 气象学家将预测飓风的路径。
Experts will predict the election results. = 专家们将预测选举结果。


虚拟语气:
If I had predicted the stock market crash, I would have made a fortune. = 如果我预测到股市崩盘，我就会发财了。
I wish I had predicted the rain. = 我真希望我预测到了下雨。


被动语态:
The earthquake was predicted by many scientists. = 地震被许多科学家预测到。
The economic downturn was accurately predicted. = 经济低迷被准确地预测到。


疑问句:
Can you predict the winner? = 你能预测赢家吗？
What do you predict will happen next? = 你预测接下来会发生什么？


否定句:
I cannot predict the future. = 我无法预测未来。
They did not predict the severity of the storm. = 他们没有预测到风暴的严重程度。
