defloration:/ˌdiːflɔːˈreɪʃən/| n. |毁坏(花朵)；破坏(贞操)；采摘花朵 |The act of deflowering something, especially a flower or a woman's virginity.  It often carries negative connotations, implying violence or the destruction of something beautiful or innocent. |例句：The defloration of the rose was a shame. 玫瑰花的凋谢是一种耻辱。  The defloration of the young woman was a terrible crime.  那个年轻女子贞操的丧失是一起可怕的罪行。|近义词：violation, ravishment, despoiling, ruin, destruction |反义词：preservation, protection, safeguarding
