shareholdings: /ˈʃeəˌhoʊldɪŋz/ | n. | 股份；持股 | The ownership of shares in a company.  Shareholdings can refer to the total number of shares owned by an individual or entity, or it can refer to the act of owning shares. |

例句：

His shareholdings in the company are substantial. = 他在该公司中的股份相当可观。

The company's shareholdings are widely distributed among small investors. = 该公司的股份在众多小投资者中广泛分配。


近义词：stock ownership, equity holdings

反义词：无直接反义词，但可以考虑与“no ownership”或“lack of shares”相对比


词源:

* **share:**  From Old English *scīr, *scīre, meaning "portion, piece, division, district."  Its meaning evolved to include a part of something owned or possessed,  leading to its use in the context of company ownership.

* **holding:** From Old English *haldan, meaning "to hold, keep, possess." This reflects the act of possessing shares.

* **shareholdings:** A combination of "share" and "holdings," indicating the shares that are held or owned.  The word emerged as a natural combination reflecting the concept of owning shares in a company.
