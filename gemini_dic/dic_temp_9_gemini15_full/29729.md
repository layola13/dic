catkins:/ˈkæt.kɪnz/| n.|葇荑花序|Soft, cylindrical flower clusters, typically drooping, found on certain trees such as willows and poplars.  They are composed of numerous small flowers without petals.  The term is often used to describe the male flowers of these trees, but sometimes encompasses both male and female structures.|例句：The willow tree was covered in delicate, silvery catkins.|近义词：aments|反义词:无
