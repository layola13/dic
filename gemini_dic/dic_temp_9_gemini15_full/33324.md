chuff:/tʃʌf/| v. n. adj.| 闷闷不乐，生气；（使）满意；冒烟；火车头；|

英文解析:As a verb, "chuff" means to become or make someone feel very pleased or satisfied.  As a noun, it can refer to a puff of smoke or steam, or more colloquially, a person who is grumpy or easily annoyed. As an adjective, it describes something that is pleasing or satisfying. The origin is uncertain but possibly imitative of the sound of a puff of smoke.


例句:
He was chuffed to bits with his new car. = 他对他的新车非常满意。

The train chuffed along the track. = 火车沿着铁轨发出噗噗声前进。


近义词:
(Verb) please, delight, gratify, satisfy
(Noun) puff, plume
(Adj) pleased, delighted, happy


反义词:
(Verb) disappoint, displease, frustrate
(Noun) grump, grouch
(Adj) displeased, unhappy, disappointed


词性:
Verb, Noun, Adjective
