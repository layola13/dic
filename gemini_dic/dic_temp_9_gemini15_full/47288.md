de-employed:/ˌdiːɪmˈplɔɪd/| adj.|失业的，被解雇的|  A person who has been de-employed is no longer employed by a company or organization.  This term is less common than "unemployed" and often implies a more formal or official termination of employment. |例句：He was de-employed due to company restructuring. 他因为公司重组而被解雇。|例句：The de-employed workers received severance pay. 被解雇的工人收到了遣散费。|近义词：unemployed, jobless, laid off, dismissed, terminated|反义词：employed, hired, working

PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "de-employed".  The adjective is used primarily on its own to describe a person's employment status.


词源:
The word "de-employed" is a relatively recent coinage. It's formed by adding the prefix "de-" (meaning "removal from" or "opposite of") to the word "employed".  The prefix "de-" is of Latin origin.
