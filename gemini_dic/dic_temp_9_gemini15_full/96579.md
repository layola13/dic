inducer: /ɪnˈduːsər/ | n. |诱导物，诱导剂 | A substance or agent that initiates or increases the activity of something, especially an enzyme or gene.  It can also refer to someone or something that causes something else to happen. | 例句：The enzyme inducer increased the rate of reaction. 酶诱导剂提高了反应速率。

例句：The new policy acted as an inducer of economic growth. 新政策起到了刺激经济增长的作用。


近义词：activator, stimulant, catalyst, instigator | 反义词：inhibitor, suppressor, deterrent


词源:  From the verb "induce," meaning "to lead or bring on," from Latin *inducere, from *in- ("in, into, on") + *ducere ("to lead").  The suffix "-er" denotes an agent or doer.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The drug acts as an inducer of sleep. = 这种药物具有催眠作用。
Heat acts as an inducer of chemical reactions. = 热量能诱导化学反应。


一般过去时:
The hormone induced a significant increase in cell growth. = 激素诱导细胞生长显著增加。
The presence of lactose induced the production of lactase. = 乳糖的存在诱导了乳糖酶的产生。


现在完成时:
Scientists have identified several gene inducers. = 科学家们已经鉴别出几种基因诱导剂。
Exposure to certain chemicals has induced mutations in the DNA. = 接触某些化学物质已导致DNA突变。


过去完成时:
The researchers had already identified the inducer before the experiment began. = 研究人员在实验开始之前就已经鉴别出了诱导剂。
The high temperature had induced a phase transition in the material. = 高温已导致材料发生相变。


将来时:
The treatment will induce remission in many patients. = 该疗法将使许多患者缓解。
The new policy is expected to induce significant changes in the market. = 预计这项新政策将对市场带来重大变化。


虚拟语气:
If we added more inducer, the reaction would proceed faster. = 如果我们添加更多诱导剂，反应速度会更快。

被动语态:
The reaction was induced by the addition of a catalyst. = 反应是由添加催化剂引起的。
Gene expression is often induced by specific signals. = 基因表达通常是由特定信号诱导的。


疑问句:
