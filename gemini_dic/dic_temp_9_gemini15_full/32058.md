cherry-picks:/ˈtʃɛriˌpɪks/ | v. | 挑选（尤指有利的证据）；选择性地引用 | To select only the most favorable or desirable items from a larger group, often ignoring or excluding less favorable items.  This often implies a bias or manipulation of information. | 例句：The politician cherry-picked statistics to support his argument.  这位政治家挑选了一些统计数据来支持他的论点。


The media cherry-picked quotes to create a negative narrative. 媒体挑选了一些语录来制造负面叙事。


近义词：select, choose, cull, handpick | 反义词：include all, consider all, fairly represent | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cherry-picks data to prove his point. = 他挑选数据来证明他的观点。
They cherry-pick the best fruits from the orchard. = 他们从果园里挑选最好的水果。

一般过去时:
She cherry-picked the most flattering photos for her profile. = 她为自己的个人资料挑选了最漂亮的照片。
The journalist cherry-picked quotes to distort the interviewee's message. = 记者挑选了一些语录来歪曲被采访者的意思。


现在完成时:
He has cherry-picked evidence to support his theory. = 他已经挑选证据来支持他的理论。
They have cherry-picked the best students for the program. = 他们已经为这个项目挑选了最好的学生。


过去完成时:
She had cherry-picked the best apples before we arrived. = 在我们到达之前，她已经挑选了最好的苹果。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
