prealludes: /ˌpriːˈæljuːdz/ | v. | 预示；预告；是…的先兆 | To serve as an indication or warning of something that is going to happen; to hint at something beforehand.  | The dark clouds preluded a storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨的来临。| The unusual silence preluded a major announcement.  不寻常的寂静预示着重大公告的到来。 | foreshadow, foretell, herald |  (无明确反义词，可考虑：follow, succeed) | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The music preludes the main act. = 音乐预示着主要节目的开始。
His nervous laughter preludes a confession. = 他紧张的笑声预示着一次坦白。


一般过去时:
The rumbling in the distance preluded an earthquake. = 远处隆隆的响声预示着地震的发生。
The strange behavior of the animals preluded the flood. = 动物们奇怪的行为预示着洪水的到来。


现在完成时:
The recent economic downturn has preluded a period of uncertainty. = 最近的经济衰退预示着一时期的不确定性。
Several warning signs have preluded the company's bankruptcy. = 几个警告信号预示着这家公司的破产。


过去完成时:
The tense atmosphere had preluded the violent argument. = 紧张的气氛预示着激烈的争吵。
Days of heavy rain had preluded the devastating landslide. = 多日的大雨预示着毁灭性的山体滑坡。


将来时:
These events will prelude a time of great social change. = 这些事件将预示着一个伟大的社会变革时代。
The upcoming election will prelude a period of political uncertainty. = 即将到来的选举将预示着一时期政治上的不确定性。


虚拟语气:
If the symptoms preluded a serious illness, we should seek immediate medical attention. = 如果症状预示着严重的疾病，我们应该立即就医。
If the strange noises had preluded a fire, we would have evacuated sooner. = 如果奇怪的噪音预示着火灾，我们会更早疏散。


被动语态:
The concert was preluded by a short speech. = 音乐会由简短的讲话开场。
The storm was preluded by a sudden drop in temperature. = 暴风雨由气温骤降预示。


疑问句:
Does this unusual silence prelude something important? = 这种不寻常的沉默预示着什么重要的事情吗？
Do these warning signs prelude a potential crisis? = 这些警告信号预示着潜在的危机吗？


否定句:
The initial optimism did not prelude the eventual failure. = 最初的乐观并没有预示最终的失败。
The calm before the storm did not prelude any significant changes. = 风暴前的平静并没有预示任何重大变化。


PHRASE->
prelude to something = ...的序幕
a prelude to disaster = 灾难的序幕
prelude to war = 战争的序幕
prelude to a new era = 新纪元的序幕
prelude to success = 成功的前奏
