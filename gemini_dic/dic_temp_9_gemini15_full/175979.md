rattled:/ˈrætl/| adj. v.|  不安的；慌乱的；喋喋不休的；使不安；使慌乱 |  To be rattled means to be disturbed or unsettled, often causing nervousness or anxiety. It can also refer to a repetitive or nervous speech. |例句：The unexpected news rattled him.  意外的消息使他慌乱。|近义词：unsettled, disturbed, agitated, flustered |反义词：calm, composed, tranquil


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud noise rattles the windows. = 大声的噪音震动着窗户。
The constant interruptions rattle her concentration. =  不断的打断扰乱了她的注意力。

一般过去时:
The earthquake rattled the city. = 地震震动了这座城市。
The bad news rattled her to the core. =  坏消息让她内心极度不安。


现在完成时:
The news has rattled the stock market. =  这个消息已经震动了股市。
The experience has rattled him considerably. =  这次经历让他相当不安。


过去完成时:
He had been rattled by the previous interview. = 他已经被之前的面试弄得心烦意乱。
The unexpected question had rattled her confidence. =  那个意料之外的问题动摇了她的信心。


将来时:
The announcement will rattle the opposition party. =  这个声明将会让反对党感到不安。
The upcoming exam will rattle many students. =  即将到来的考试会让很多学生紧张不安。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't been rattled, I would have performed better. = 如果我没有那么紧张，我会表现得更好。
I wish I hadn't been so rattled during the presentation. =  我希望在演示期间我没有那么紧张。


被动语态:
The building was rattled by the explosion. =  建筑物被爆炸震动了。
He was rattled by the aggressive questioning. =  咄咄逼人的盘问让他心烦意乱。


疑问句:
Was he rattled by the criticism? = 他被批评弄得心烦意乱了吗？
Is she easily rattled by pressure? = 她容易被压力弄得慌乱吗？


否定句:
The news didn't rattle him at all. =  这个消息一点也没有让他不安。
She wasn't rattled by the difficult questions. =  她并没有被那些难题难倒。
