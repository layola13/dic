liberalism: /ˈlɪbərəlɪzəm/ | n. |自由主义；自由化；自由观点 |

英文解析：Liberalism is a political philosophy or worldview founded on ideas of liberty and equality.  It advocates for individual rights, limited government intervention, and free markets, although different types of liberalism exist with varying approaches to these principles.  Classical liberalism emphasizes individual liberty and minimal state intervention, while social liberalism incorporates a greater role for the government in addressing social and economic inequalities.

例句：

The rise of liberalism in the 19th century significantly changed political landscapes across Europe.  19世纪自由主义的兴起极大地改变了欧洲的政治格局。

His political views are considered to be center-left, leaning towards social liberalism. 他的政治观点被认为是中左翼，倾向于社会自由主义。


近义词： progressivism, reformism, radicalism (depending on the specific type of liberalism)

反义词： conservatism, authoritarianism, totalitarianism

词源:

The word "liberalism" derives from the Latin word "liber," meaning "free."  It emerged in the 19th century as a political ideology emphasizing individual liberty and limited government.  The term's meaning and application have evolved significantly over time, leading to diverse interpretations and applications of the concept.
