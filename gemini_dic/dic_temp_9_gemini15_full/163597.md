precursors:/ˈpriːkɜːrsərz/| n. |先驱，前兆，前体|Something that comes before something else and may influence or cause it.  A precursor often suggests a causal relationship, implying that the precursor leads to or contributes to the later event or thing. | Examples: The heavy rain was a precursor to the flood. 大雨是洪水的前兆。The discovery of penicillin was a precursor to the development of many antibiotics. 青霉素的发现是许多抗生素开发的先驱。|近义词：predecessors, forerunners, antecedents, harbingers |反义词：successors, consequences, results |名词
