pseudopolitic: /ˌsuːdəʊˈpɒlətɪk/ | adj. | 似是而非的政治；伪政治的 |  Relating to or characteristic of politics that are superficial, deceptive, or lacking in genuine substance or principle; often used to describe political actions or rhetoric that are primarily aimed at appearances or short-term gains rather than genuine policy or ideological commitment.  | 例句：His speech was full of pseudopolitical rhetoric, promising much but delivering little. 他的演讲充满了似是而非的政治辞令，许诺很多却几乎没有兑现。


The campaign was criticized for its pseudopolitical posturing. 这场竞选活动因其虚假的政治姿态而受到批评。 | 近义词：sham, specious,  phony,  artificial,  pretentious | 反义词：genuine, authentic, sincere,  substantial


词源:  The word is a combination of "pseudo" (from Greek *pseudes*, meaning "false" or "lying") and "politics."  It reflects the nature of something that only superficially resembles genuine political action or thought.
