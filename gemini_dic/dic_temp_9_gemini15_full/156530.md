pick-at: /pɪkˈæt/ | vt. | 挑剔；一点点地吃；拨弄； | to repeatedly touch or pull at something gently, often in a nervous or restless way; to eat something in small amounts or without much enthusiasm. | 例句：She picked at her food, showing she wasn't hungry. 她挑剔地吃着食物，表明她不饿。  He nervously picked at the loose thread on his shirt. 他紧张地拨弄着衬衫上松开的线头。 | 近义词：prod, fidget with, nibble at,  | 反义词：devour, gobble,  |  动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He picks at his food every meal. = 他每顿饭都挑食。
She picks at the loose string on her sweater. = 她拨弄着毛衣上松开的线头。


一般过去时:
The child picked at the scab on his knee. = 这个孩子拨弄着膝盖上的伤口结痂。
He picked at his food because it was cold. = 他挑剔地吃着食物，因为它凉了。


现在完成时:
She has picked at her nails all afternoon. = 她整个下午都在拨弄指甲。
He has picked at his salad, leaving most of it uneaten. = 他只挑着沙拉吃，大部分都没吃。


过去完成时:
She had picked at her food all evening, so she was still hungry. = 她整个晚上都在挑食，所以她仍然很饿。
He had picked at the problem for weeks before finding a solution. = 他在找到解决方案之前，已经纠结这个问题好几个星期了。


将来时:

He will pick at the loose button on his coat. = 他会拨弄外套上松开的纽扣。


虚拟语气:
