nonfalsifiable: /ˌnɒnˈfælsifaɪəbl/ | adj. | 不可证伪的 |  Unable to be proven false; incapable of being disproved.  A nonfalsifiable statement or theory cannot be tested in a way that could potentially show it to be incorrect, even if it is incorrect.  This is a key concept in the philosophy of science, particularly in discussions of demarcation between science and pseudoscience.  | The theory that the universe was created by a deity is nonfalsifiable because there's no conceivable test that could definitively disprove it. | falsifiable, verifiable | refutable, testable
