crisis:/ˈkraɪsɪs/| n.|危机，危急关头|A crisis is a time of great difficulty, danger, or uncertainty.|例句：The country is facing a serious economic crisis.  这个国家正面临严重的经济危机。|例句：The doctors managed to pull him through the crisis. 医生们成功地帮助他度过了危机。|近义词：emergency, predicament, trouble|反义词：calm, peace, stability|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The current crisis demands immediate action. = 目前的危机需要立即采取行动。
This crisis affects many people. =这场危机影响很多人。

一般过去时:
The 2008 financial crisis had a devastating impact. = 2008年的金融危机产生了毁灭性的影响。
A major crisis occurred last year. = 去年发生了一场重大危机。

现在完成时:
The company has overcome several crises in its history. = 这家公司在其历史上克服了多次危机。
We have faced many challenges and crises. = 我们面临过许多挑战和危机。

过去完成时:
Before the intervention, the situation had already reached a crisis point. = 在干预之前，情况已经达到了危急关头。
The company had already weathered several crises before this one. = 在这次危机之前，该公司已经度过了几次危机。

将来时:
Experts predict a future water crisis. = 专家预测未来将出现水危机。
The looming crisis will test our resilience. = 即将来临的危机将考验我们的韧性。

虚拟语气:
If we don't act now, we will face a major crisis. = 如果我们现在不采取行动，我们将面临一场重大危机。
It would be a crisis if the dam were to break. = 如果大坝决堤，那将是一场危机。

被动语态:
The crisis was handled effectively by the government. = 政府有效地处理了这场危机。
The situation is being closely monitored to prevent a crisis. = 正在密切监测局势，以防止危机发生。

疑问句:
What caused this crisis? = 导致这场危机的因素是什么？
Is there a solution to this crisis? = 有解决这场危机的办法吗？

否定句:
The situation is not yet a crisis. = 情况还没有达到危机的地步。
There was no crisis after all. = 毕竟没有发生危机。
