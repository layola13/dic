penicillin: /ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪn/ | n. |青霉素|A group of antibiotics derived from the Penicillium mold, used to treat bacterial infections.  Penicillin was the first antibiotic to be discovered and widely used, revolutionizing medicine.|  Example: The doctor prescribed penicillin to treat his bacterial infection.  医生开了青霉素来治疗他的细菌感染。|近义词：antibiotic|反义词：无

词源:
The word "penicillin" is derived from the genus of fungi *Penicillium*, from which the antibiotic is derived.  The suffix "-cillin" is added to indicate its antibiotic nature.  The name reflects its origin and function.
