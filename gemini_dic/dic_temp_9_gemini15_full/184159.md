resituating: /ˌriːˈsɪtʃueɪtɪŋ/ | v. |重新定位；重新安置；重新考虑 |  To place or establish something again in a different position, context, or understanding.  This involves reconsidering the significance or meaning of something. | 例句：The museum is resituating its collection to better reflect diverse cultures. = 博物馆正在重新调整其收藏，以更好地反映不同的文化。

The government is resituating its priorities to address climate change. = 政府正在重新调整其优先事项以应对气候变化。


近义词：reposition, relocate, reinterpret, reassess | 反义词：fix, stabilize, maintain


词源：re- (again) + situate (to place or locate) + -ing (present participle)  The word combines the prefix "re-" indicating repetition or renewal, with the verb "situate," which means to place or locate. The "-ing" suffix transforms it into a present participle, often used as a gerund (a verb acting as a noun) or to describe an ongoing action.
