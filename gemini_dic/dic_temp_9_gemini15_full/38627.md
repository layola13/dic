concubinage:/ˈkɒŋkjʊbɪnɪdʒ/| n. |妾居；同居；姘居|The state or practice of cohabiting with a person to whom one is not married, especially a woman kept as a mistress.  Concubinage often implies a long-term relationship, sometimes with some degree of social acceptance, though it lacks the legal recognition of marriage.|例句：The king's concubinage was a well-known secret. 国王的妾室关系是一个众所周知的秘密。|例句：Living together without marriage is a form of concubinage. 未婚同居是一种姘居形式。|近义词：cohabitation, co-living, liaison|反义词：marriage
