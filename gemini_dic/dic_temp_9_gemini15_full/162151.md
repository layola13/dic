pre-Greeks: /ˌpriːˈɡriːks/ | adj. | 前希腊人的；希腊化之前的 |  Referring to the period or cultures that existed in Greece before the classical Greek civilization (roughly before the 8th century BC).  It encompasses various Aegean civilizations like the Minoan and Mycenaean cultures.  These cultures left behind significant archaeological evidence, including palaces, settlements, and art, offering glimpses into their societies and belief systems. However, their writing systems are largely undeciphered, making full understanding challenging. |  The pre-Greek settlements were destroyed by a catastrophic earthquake.  | 近义词：prehistoric, antecedent | 反义词：classical Greek, Hellenic
