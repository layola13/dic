growth:/ɡroʊθ/| n. |生长；增长；发展|The process of increasing in size, amount, or importance.|例句：The company experienced significant growth last year.  这家公司去年经历了显著的增长。|例句：The growth of the plant was stunted by the lack of sunlight. 植物的生长由于缺乏阳光而受到阻碍。|近义词：development, increase, expansion|反义词：decline, decrease, shrinkage|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The plant growth is slow this year. = 今年植物生长缓慢。
Economic growth is crucial for a nation's prosperity. = 经济增长对一个国家的繁荣至关重要。


一般过去时:
The city experienced rapid growth in the 1990s. = 这个城市在20世纪90年代经历了快速发展。
His business growth slowed down after the recession. = 经济衰退后，他的业务增长放缓了。


现在完成时:
The company has shown impressive growth since its inception. = 自成立以来，该公司已显示出令人印象深刻的增长。
The population has experienced significant growth in recent decades. = 近几十年来，人口经历了显著增长。


过去完成时:
By the end of the year, the company had achieved remarkable growth. = 到年底，该公司已取得了显著的增长。
Before the investment, the project had shown little growth. = 在投资之前，该项目几乎没有增长。


将来时:
The company projects significant growth in the next quarter. = 公司预计下个季度将实现显著增长。
Experts predict rapid population growth in the coming years. = 专家预测未来几年人口将快速增长。


虚拟语气:
If we had invested more, the business growth would have been faster. = 如果我们投资更多，业务增长就会更快。
I wish the company's growth had been more sustainable. = 我希望公司的增长更可持续。


被动语态:
The project's success was attributed to its rapid growth. = 项目的成功归功于其快速增长。
Significant growth is expected to be achieved in the coming years. = 预计未来几年将取得显著增长。


疑问句:
What factors contribute to economic growth? = 哪些因素促进了经济增长？
Is there a limit to population growth? = 人口增长是否有上限？


否定句:
The plant showed no growth after the drought. = 干旱后，植物没有生长。
There was no significant growth in sales this month. = 本月销售额没有显著增长。
