live-high-off-the-hog: /ˌlɪv ˈhaɪ ɔːf ðə hɒɡ/ | idiom |过着奢侈的生活；过着挥霍无度的日子|To live luxuriously and wastefully, often implying excessive consumption of food and drink.  The phrase originates from the idea of pigs living in abundance, feeding on the best parts of the hog. |例句：They've been living high off the hog ever since they won the lottery.  他们自从赢得彩票后就过着奢侈的生活。

They live high off the hog, constantly traveling and dining at expensive restaurants.他们过着挥霍无度的日子，经常旅行并在昂贵的餐厅用餐。

近义词：live in the lap of luxury, live it up, roll in money,  splurge, revel |反义词：scrimp and save, live frugally, be impoverished, be destitute


USAGE->
This idiom is primarily used in the present tense to describe a current lifestyle.  Past tense can be used to describe a past period of lavish living.  Future tense is less common but can be used to express an expectation of future luxury.  The idiom doesn't lend itself to passive or subjunctive forms.


一般现在时:
They live high off the hog. = 他们过着奢侈的生活。
We are living high off the hog this week. = 我们这周过着奢侈的生活。


一般过去时:
They lived high off the hog for years. = 他们过着奢侈的生活好几年了。
We lived high off the hog during our vacation. = 我们在假期里过着奢侈的生活。


现在完成时:
They have lived high off the hog since inheriting the fortune. = 自从继承了财产以来，他们就过着奢侈的生活。
We have been living high off the hog lately. = 最近我们一直在过着奢侈的生活。


将来时:
They will live high off the hog once they sell the business. = 一旦他们卖掉公司，他们就会过着奢侈的生活。
We expect to live high off the hog on our honeymoon. = 我们期待在蜜月期间过着奢侈的生活。


PHRASE->
live high on the hog: (same meaning as live high off the hog)
high on the hog: (implies a state of luxury and contentment)
off the hog: (can be used to imply a period of indulgence)
living the high life: (similar meaning, more general)
to live large: (similar meaning, emphasizes lavish spending)
