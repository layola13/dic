nonsystematic:/ˌnɑːnˌsɪstəˈmætɪk/ | adj. |非系统的，无规律的 |Lacking a system or plan; haphazard.  It implies a lack of order, organization, or method. |例句：His approach to the problem was nonsystematic and inefficient.  他的解决问题的方法是非系统的，效率低下。

例句：The data collection was nonsystematic, making it difficult to draw reliable conclusions. 数据收集是非系统的，这使得难以得出可靠的结论。


近义词：unsystematic, haphazard, random, disorganized, irregular, chaotic

反义词：systematic, organized, methodical, orderly, planned


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "systematic," which derives from "system" (from Latin *systema, from Greek σύστημα (sýstēma), meaning "a whole composed of parts").  Therefore, "nonsystematic" literally means "not having a system."
