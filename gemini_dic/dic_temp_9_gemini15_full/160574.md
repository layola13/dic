porphyre:/ˈpɔːrfəri/| n. |斑岩|A volcanic rock consisting of a fine-grained matrix with larger crystals embedded in it.  Porphyry is formed when magma cools slowly at depth, allowing large crystals to grow, and then is erupted quickly, causing the remaining magma to cool rapidly, forming the fine-grained matrix.  The larger crystals are called phenocrysts, and the fine-grained matrix is called the groundmass.|The countertops are made of a beautiful red porphyry.|granite, basalt|sandstone|名词
