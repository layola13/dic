scapegoats: /ˈskeɪpɡoʊts/ | n. |替罪羊；背黑锅的人 | A scapegoat is a person or thing carrying the blame for others.  It originates from a biblical ritual where a goat was symbolically burdened with the sins of the community and sent away. |例句：He became the scapegoat for the company's failures. 他成了公司失败的替罪羊。

The team's poor performance was blamed on the coach, making him the scapegoat. 球队的糟糕表现被归咎于教练，使他成了替罪羊。|近义词：whipping boy, fall guy, patsy |反义词：hero, champion
