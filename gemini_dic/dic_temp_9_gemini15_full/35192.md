clubbism:/ˈklʌbɪzəm/ | n. |俱乐部主义；派系主义 | The prioritizing of the interests of one's own club or group over broader interests.  It often involves favoritism, cronyism, and a lack of transparency.  Clubbism can be found in various settings, from social clubs to political parties and even professional organizations. | Example: The promotion was clearly a case of clubbism; the candidate with the best qualifications was overlooked in favor of someone with connections.  =晋升显然是派系主义；最合格的候选人被忽视，而有利于有关系的人。|近义词：factionalism, cronyism, favoritism |反义词：impartiality, meritocracy
