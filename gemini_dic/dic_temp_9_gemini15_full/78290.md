germinate:/ˈdʒɜːrmɪneɪt/ | v. |发芽；萌芽；开始发展 | To begin to grow, or to cause something to begin to grow.  | The seeds will germinate in the spring.  种子将在春天发芽。|The idea germinated slowly in his mind. 这个想法在他的脑海中慢慢萌发。| sprout, bud, develop | decay, wither

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The seeds germinate in spring. = 种子在春天发芽。
Plants germinate under the right conditions. = 植物在合适的条件下发芽。

一般过去时:
The seeds germinated quickly after the rain. = 雨后种子迅速发芽。
The idea germinated in his mind last year. = 这个想法去年在他脑海中萌发。

现在完成时:
The seeds have germinated. = 种子已经发芽了。
The plan has finally germinated after years of work. = 经过多年的努力，计划终于启动了。

过去完成时:
The seeds had already germinated before the frost hit. = 霜冻来临之前，种子已经发芽了。
He had been working on the project for years before the idea finally germinated. = 在这个想法最终萌发之前，他已经在这个项目上工作了多年。

将来时:
The seeds will germinate next spring. = 种子将在明年春天发芽。
I believe this idea will germinate into a successful business. = 我相信这个想法将发展成为成功的企业。

虚拟语气:
If the seeds were planted properly, they would germinate. = 如果种子种植得当，它们就会发芽。
If only this idea had germinated sooner! = 但愿这个想法早点萌发！

被动语态:
The seeds were germinated in a controlled environment. = 种子在受控环境中发芽。
The idea was germinated by a conversation with a friend. = 这个想法是在与朋友的谈话中产生的。

疑问句:
Have the seeds germinated yet? = 种子发芽了吗？
Will this idea germinate and become reality? = 这个想法会萌芽并成为现实吗？

否定句:
The seeds did not germinate because of the drought. = 由于干旱，种子没有发芽。
The plan did not germinate due to lack of funding. = 由于缺乏资金，计划未能启动。


PHRASE->
germinate an idea = 产生一个想法
germinate a seed = 使种子发芽
germinate in the soil = 在土壤中发芽
germinate quickly = 快速发芽
germinate slowly = 缓慢发芽
