propitiators:/prəˈpɪʃieɪtərz/| n.| 息事宁人者；奉承者；讨好者 |Those who seek to appease or conciliate, often through offerings or gestures of goodwill. They attempt to win favor or avert anger, typically of a deity or superior being.  |例句：The ancient priests acted as propitiators, offering sacrifices to appease the gods. 古代祭司充当息事宁人者，献祭以平息众神。

The villagers offered gifts to the mountain spirits as propitiators, hoping for a good harvest. 村民向山神献礼以求息事宁人，希望能获得好收成。|近义词：appeasers, conciliators, pacifiers, mediators |反义词：antagonists, provocateurs, agitators |名词
