self-selections:/ˌsɛlf sɪˈlɛkʃənz/ | n. |自主选择，自行选择 |The process by which individuals or groups choose options or make decisions independently, without external coercion or influence.  This often involves a range of choices presented to the individual or group. |例句：The students were given self-selections in their course options. 学生们可以选择自己想上的课程。  The survey allowed for self-selections of preferred methods of communication. 调查允许自行选择首选的沟通方式。|近义词：free choices, personal choices, options, preferences |反义词：assignments, mandates, dictates |名词
