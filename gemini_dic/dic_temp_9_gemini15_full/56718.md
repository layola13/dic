door:/dɔːr/| /dɔːr/| n.|门|A door is a hinged or sliding barrier at the entrance to a building, room, or vehicle.|例句：He opened the door and stepped inside. 他打开门走了进去。|例句：The door was locked, so I couldn't get in. 门锁上了，所以我进不去。|近义词：entrance, gateway, opening|反义词：wall


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The door opens inward. = 门向内开。
The door is always closed. = 门总是关着的。


一般过去时:
He closed the door quietly. = 他轻轻地关上门。
The door slammed shut. = 门砰地一声关上了。


现在完成时:
I have painted the door red. = 我把门漆成了红色。
The door has been broken. = 门坏了。


过去完成时:
The door had already been opened before he arrived. = 他到达之前门已经被打开了。
We had locked the door before we left. = 我们离开之前已经锁上门了。


将来时:
I will open the door for you. = 我会为你开门。
The door will be repaired tomorrow. = 门明天将被修理。


虚拟语气:
If the door were open, we could go in. = 如果门开着，我们就能进去了。
I wish the door were stronger. = 我希望门更结实一些。


被动语态:
The door was opened by the wind. = 门被风吹开了。
The door is being repaired now. = 门现在正在修理。


疑问句:
Is the door locked? = 门锁了吗？
Which door should I use? = 我应该用哪扇门？


否定句:
The door wasn't closed properly. = 门没有关好。
He didn't open the door. = 他没有开门。
