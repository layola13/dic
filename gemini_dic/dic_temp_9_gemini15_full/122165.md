mistrusts:/mɪsˈtrʌsts/| v.|不信任，怀疑|To have a lack of trust or confidence in (someone or something).|例句：He mistrusts the government's promises. 他不相信政府的承诺。|例句：Many people mistrust social media algorithms. 许多人不信任社交媒体算法。|近义词：distrust, doubt, suspect |反义词：trust, believe, confidence |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He mistrusts her motives. = 他怀疑她的动机。
They mistrust the new management. = 他们不信任新的管理层。

一般过去时:
She mistrusted his intentions. = 她不相信他的意图。
The villagers mistrusted the stranger. = 村民们不信任那个陌生人。

现在完成时:
I have mistrusted him ever since that incident. = 从那件事起，我就一直不信任他。
We have mistrusted the company's claims for years. = 多年来，我们一直不相信这家公司的说法。

过去完成时:
She had mistrusted him for a long time before she finally confronted him. = 在她最终与他摊牌之前，她已经不信任他很久了。
They had mistrusted his leadership before the project failed. = 在项目失败之前，他们就不信任他的领导能力了。

将来时:
I will mistrust his words in the future. = 将来我不会相信他的话。
They will mistrust any promises he makes. = 他们不会相信他做出的任何承诺。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't mistrusted him, I wouldn't have lost so much money. = 如果我不那么不信任他，我就不会损失那么多钱了。
I wish I hadn't mistrusted her advice. = 我真希望我没有不相信她的建议。

被动语态:
His judgment was mistrusted by the jury. = 他的判断不被陪审团所信任。
The government's policies are mistrusted by many. = 政府的政策不被许多人信任。

疑问句:
Do you mistrust his abilities? = 你不相信他的能力吗？
Does she mistrust his judgment? = 她不相信他的判断力吗？

否定句:
I don't mistrust his honesty. = 我不怀疑他的诚实。
They don't mistrust their employees. = 他们不怀疑他们的员工。
