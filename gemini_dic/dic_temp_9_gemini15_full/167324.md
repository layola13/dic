pretranslating: /ˌpriːtrænzˈleɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 预翻译 | The act of translating a text before it is finalized or before it is translated into the target language for a specific purpose.  It often involves initial translation to facilitate further editing, review, or adaptation.  This is often done to identify potential issues early, ensuring a higher quality final translation. | Example:  Pretranslating the document allowed the team to identify and correct several ambiguities before the final translation was produced. = 预翻译该文件使团队能够在最终翻译制作之前识别并纠正一些歧义。 |近义词: preliminary translation, advance translation |反义词: post-editing, final translation |动词
