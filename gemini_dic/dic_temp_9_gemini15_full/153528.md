pentacoccous:/ˌpentəˈkɒkəs/| adj.|五室的；具五个心皮的|Having five carpels.  The term refers to a type of fruit or ovary structure in plants where the ovary is divided into five compartments or locules. Each compartment typically contains ovules, which develop into seeds after fertilization.|Example: The pentacoccous fruit of the plant readily separated into five distinct parts.|近义词：five-carpellate|反义词：monocarpellate, bicarpellate
