ignorantism: /ˈɪɡnərəntɪzəm/ | n. |无知主义；愚昧；无知论 | The deliberate refusal to acquire knowledge or the advocacy of such refusal.  Ignorantism is a philosophical position that values ignorance or rejects knowledge as unnecessary or harmful. It's different from simple ignorance; it's an active choice to remain uninformed.  | Example:  His ignorantism was evident in his dismissal of scientific evidence.  他的无知主义在他对科学证据的漠视中显而易见。 | 近义词：ignorance, nescience, obtuseness | 反义词：knowledge, enlightenment, erudition |  n.


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations.  The usage focuses on expressing the concept of the deliberate rejection of knowledge.


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "ignorantism."  However, related phrases could express similar ideas:

* willful ignorance: 故意的无知
* blissful ignorance:  幸福的无知
* closed-mindedness:  思想封闭
* intellectual laziness:  智力懒惰
* anti-intellectualism: 反智主义
