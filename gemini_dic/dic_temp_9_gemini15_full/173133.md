quasi-affectionately: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ əˈfɛkʃənətli/ | adj. |近乎深情地，假装亲昵地 |  The word combines "quasi" (meaning seemingly, apparently, or to a certain extent) with "affectionately" (meaning in a loving or tender manner). It describes an action or expression that shows a degree of affection, but is not entirely genuine or heartfelt. It might be a polite gesture, a social convention, or an attempt to mask other feelings. |例句：He patted her shoulder quasi-affectionately, but his eyes betrayed his true indifference. 他近乎深情地拍了拍她的肩膀，但他的眼神出卖了他真正的冷漠。  She smiled quasi-affectionately at the new employee, trying to make him feel welcome. 她对新员工报以近乎亲切的笑容，试图让他感到受欢迎。 |近义词：tenderly (but insincerely), politely, formally, superficially  |反义词：coldly, cruelly, hatefully, disdainfully |
