profound: /prəˈfaʊnd/ | adj. | 深奥的；深刻的；深厚的；极度的 |

英文解析:  Extremely deep; intellectually deep; showing great knowledge or understanding;  intense or extreme in a particular way.  It can describe something intellectually complex, a feeling of great intensity, or a significant impact.

例句:

His profound knowledge of history was evident in his lectures.  他的历史知识渊博，在讲座中显而易见。

She experienced a profound sense of loss after the death of her grandmother.  祖母去世后，她经历了深深的失落感。


近义词: deep, intense, significant, momentous, weighty, insightful, penetrating

反义词: superficial, shallow, trivial, insignificant


词源:  From Old French *profund, from Latin profundus, from pro- ("forth, forward") + fundus ("bottom").  Literally meaning "from the bottom," it evolved to denote depth of various kinds.
