randomizing: /ˈrændəmaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使随机化；随机分配 | The act of making something random or the process of being made random.  It involves introducing randomness into a process, system, or selection. | 例句：The researchers are randomizing the participants to ensure a fair comparison between the treatment and control groups.  研究人员正在对参与者进行随机分组，以确保治疗组和对照组之间进行公平的比较。


例句：The software is designed to randomize the order of questions in the quiz. 该软件旨在随机排列测验中的问题顺序。


近义词：shuffling, scrambling, jumbling


反义词：ordering, arranging, systematizing


词源:  Comes from the verb "randomize," formed by adding the suffix "-ize" (meaning "to make" or "to cause to become") to the adjective "random," ultimately deriving from the Old French word "randon" meaning "a violent rush or movement," implying a lack of order or pattern.
