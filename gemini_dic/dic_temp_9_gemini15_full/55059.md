disarray:/dɪsərˈeɪ/| n.|混乱，杂乱；狼狈不堪|a state of disorder or confusion|The room was in complete disarray after the party.  派对后房间一片狼藉。|近义词：chaos, disorder, confusion|反义词：order, organization


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The messy room is in disarray. =凌乱的房间一片狼藉。
Her thoughts are in disarray. =她的思绪一片混乱。


一般过去时:
The army was thrown into disarray by the sudden attack. =军队遭到突然袭击而陷入混乱。
The news put his plans in disarray. =这个消息使他的计划陷入混乱。


现在完成时:
The recent scandal has thrown the company into disarray. =最近的丑闻使公司陷入混乱。
Years of neglect have left the garden in disarray. =多年的忽视使花园一片杂乱。


过去完成时:
The team had been in disarray before the crucial game. =在关键比赛之前，球队就已经处于混乱状态。
The evidence had been in disarray, making the investigation difficult. =证据混乱不堪，使得调查变得困难。


将来时:
The upcoming changes will likely throw the market into disarray. =即将到来的变化可能会使市场陷入混乱。
The protest could put the city in disarray. =抗议活动可能会使城市陷入混乱。


虚拟语气:
If the government hadn't intervened, the country would have been in complete disarray. =如果政府不干预，国家就会完全陷入混乱。
I wish my life weren't in such disarray. =我希望我的生活不要如此混乱。


被动语态:
The schedule was thrown into disarray by the unexpected delay. =由于意外延误，日程安排被打乱了。
His plans were put into disarray by the sudden change of events. =由于事态突然变化，他的计划被打乱了。


疑问句:
Is everything in disarray? =一切都乱套了吗？
What caused the disarray in the ranks? =是什么导致了队伍的混乱？


否定句:
The garden wasn't in disarray; it was beautifully organized. =花园并没有杂乱无章；它被组织得井井有条。
His thoughts were not in disarray; he was calm and collected. =他的思绪并没有混乱；他冷静而沉着。
