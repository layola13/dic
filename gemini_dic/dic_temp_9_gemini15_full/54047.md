disdain:/dɪsˈdeɪn/| n., v.|轻蔑，鄙视；不屑；|The feeling that someone or something is unworthy of one's consideration or respect; to refuse or reject something or someone as unworthy of consideration.|He looked at their offer with disdain. 他轻蔑地看着他们的提议。|近义词：contempt, scorn, contempt, scorn, derision|反义词：admiration, respect, approval|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disdains their petty squabbles. = 他鄙视他们的琐碎争吵。
She disdains cheap imitations. = 她不屑于廉价的仿制品。


一般过去时:
He disdained to answer their questions. = 他不屑回答他们的问题。
She disdained the offer of help. = 她拒绝了帮助的提议。


现在完成时:
They have disdained all attempts at reconciliation. = 他们一直轻蔑所有和解的尝试。
He has disdained to explain himself. = 他不屑于解释自己。


过去完成时:
She had disdained his advances for years. = 她多年来一直轻蔑他的追求。
They had disdained the warnings of the experts. = 他们一直轻视专家的警告。


将来时:
He will disdain any attempt to control him. = 他将鄙视任何试图控制他的企图。
She will disdain to participate in such a trivial matter. = 她将不屑于参与如此琐碎的事情。


虚拟语气:
If he were a better man, he would not disdain the poor. = 如果他是个更好的人，他就不会鄙视穷人。
I wish she would not disdain my help. = 我希望她不要轻视我的帮助。


被动语态:
His ideas were disdained by the establishment. = 他的想法被当权者所轻蔑。
Her efforts were disdained by her colleagues. = 她的努力被她的同事们所轻视。


疑问句:
Does he disdain our efforts? = 他是否轻视我们的努力？
Why do they disdain to answer? = 他们为什么不屑回答？


否定句:
He does not disdain hard work. = 他并不轻视辛勤工作。
She did not disdain to ask for help. = 她并不羞于请求帮助。


PHRASE->
show disdain for= 对...表示轻蔑
hold someone/something in disdain= 轻视某人/某事
cast disdainful glances= 投以轻蔑的目光
with disdain= 轻蔑地
express disdain= 表达轻蔑
