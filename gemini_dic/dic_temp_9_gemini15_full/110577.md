limited:/ˈlɪmɪtɪd/| adj.|有限的，受限制的 |Having a limit or end; restricted in size, amount, extent, or duration.|The company's resources are limited. 这家公司的资源有限。|近义词：restricted, confined, circumscribed |反义词：unlimited, boundless, unrestricted


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The supply of water is limited. = 水的供应有限。
His knowledge of history is limited. = 他对历史的了解有限。

一般过去时:
The meeting was limited to two hours. = 会议限制在两个小时内。
Their options were limited. = 他们的选择有限。


现在完成时:
The damage has been limited. = 损失已被控制在一定范围内。
Progress has been limited due to funding cuts. = 由于资金削减，进展有限。


过去完成时:
The damage had been limited before the firefighters arrived. = 在消防员到达之前，损失已被控制在一定范围内。
His opportunities had been limited by his background. = 他的机会因他的背景而受到限制。


将来时:
The project will be limited to a small scale. = 该项目将限制在小规模进行。
The damage will be limited if we act quickly. = 如果我们迅速行动，损失将会有限。


虚拟语气:
If the resources were unlimited, we could do more. = 如果资源无限，我们可以做更多的事情。
I wish my time were not so limited. = 我希望我的时间不要这么有限。


被动语态:
The participants were limited to ten. = 参加者限制在十人以内。
Access to the building is limited to authorized personnel. = 进入大楼的权限仅限于授权人员。


疑问句:
Is the time limited for this task? = 完成这项任务的时间有限制吗？
Are the number of participants limited? = 参加者人数有限制吗？


否定句:
The possibilities are not limited. = 可能性并非有限。
His ambition is not limited. = 他的雄心壮志没有限制。
