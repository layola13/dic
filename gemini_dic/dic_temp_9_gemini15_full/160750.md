poser:/ˈpoʊzər/| n. |故作姿态者；装腔作势的人；难题；令人困惑的问题 | A poser is someone who tries to appear more important, intelligent, or skilled than they really are.  It can also refer to a difficult problem or question. |例句：He's such a poser, always talking about things he doesn't understand. 他真是个装腔作势的人，总是谈论他不懂的事情。

例句：The exam proved to be a real poser for many students.  考试对许多学生来说都是一个真正的难题。

近义词：pretender, show-off, charlatan, enigma, puzzle

反义词：genuine, authentic, straightforward, simple


词源：源自法语“poser”，意为“to pose”（摆姿势），引申出“装腔作势”的意思。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He poses as an expert. = 他假装是专家。
That question poses a challenge. = 那个问题提出了一个挑战。

一般过去时:
She posed as a princess. = 她假扮公主。
The problem posed a significant difficulty. = 这个问题带来了相当大的困难。


现在完成时:
He has posed as a doctor for years. = 他假扮医生多年了。
The scientist has posed many challenging questions. =这位科学家提出了许多具有挑战性的问题。


过去完成时:
They had posed as tourists before entering the building. = 他们在进入大楼前假扮成游客。
The issue had posed a dilemma for the committee. = 这个问题曾让委员会陷入困境。


将来时:
She will pose as a reporter at the event. = 她将在活动中假扮记者。
The new technology will pose new challenges. = 新技术将带来新的挑战。


虚拟语气:
If he posed as a professional, he might get the job. = 如果他假扮专业人士，他可能会得到这份工作。
If the question posed a different problem, the answer would be different. = 如果问题提出的是不同的问题，答案就会不同。


被动语态:
He was posed a difficult question. = 他被问了一个难题。
The problem was posed by the professor. = 这个问题是由教授提出的。


疑问句:
Does he pose a threat? = 他构成威胁吗？
What problems does this pose? = 这会带来什么问题？


否定句:
He doesn't pose any real threat. = 他没有构成任何真正的威胁。
The situation doesn't pose any significant problems. = 这种情况没有造成任何重大问题。


PHRASE->
