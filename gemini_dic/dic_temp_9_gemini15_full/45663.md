curtain-call:/ˈkɜːrtən kɔːl/| n. |谢幕；演员谢幕|The final scene of a play, during which the actors come to the front of the stage to acknowledge the applause of the audience.  It's a brief appearance by the performers after the end of a performance to acknowledge the audience's appreciation.|Example: The actors received a thunderous curtain call after their amazing performance.  演员在精彩的演出结束后获得了雷鸣般的谢幕。 The play ended with a standing ovation and a lengthy curtain call.  这出戏以全体起立鼓掌和长时间的谢幕而结束。|近义词：encore, bow|反义词：None
