pathfinder:/ˈpæθˌfaɪndər/ | n. |探路者，先驱，开拓者 | A pathfinder is a person who explores new routes or areas, often in difficult or unknown territory.  They are pioneers who pave the way for others.  The term can also refer to a tool or system that helps navigate or find a way. | 例句：The explorers acted as pathfinders, charting a course through the unmapped jungle.  探险家们充当了探路者的角色，绘制出一条穿过未绘制地图的丛林的路线。


例句：The new software acts as a pathfinder, guiding users through complex procedures. 这款新软件充当了探路者的角色，引导用户完成复杂的程序。


近义词：pioneer, explorer, trailblazer, innovator


反义词：follower, imitator


词性: 名词

词源:
The word "pathfinder" is a compound word formed from "path" and "finder."  "Path" comes from Old English "pǣþ," meaning "way," "road," or "track."  "Finder" comes from the Old English "findan," meaning "to find."  Therefore, "pathfinder" literally means "one who finds paths."
