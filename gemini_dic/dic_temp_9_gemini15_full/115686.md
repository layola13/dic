margin:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪn/| n.|页边空白；利润；优势；余地|The empty space around the text on a page; the amount of profit made on a sale; an advantage or superiority; a range or amount of freedom |例句：The essay needs wider margins.  这篇论文需要更大的页边距。|例句：The company achieved a healthy profit margin last year.  该公司去年获得了可观的利润率。|近义词：border, edge, profit, advantage, leeway|反义词：center, core, loss, disadvantage, constraint|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The margin is too narrow. = 页边距太窄了。
The profit margin is excellent. = 利润率极好。

一般过去时:
The margin was sufficient. = 页边距足够了。
The profit margin was low last quarter. = 上个季度的利润率很低。

现在完成时:
The company has increased its profit margin. = 公司提高了利润率。
They have significantly reduced the margin of error. = 他们显著地减少了误差范围。

过去完成时:
We had already reached the margin of the forest. = 我们已经到达了森林的边缘。
The company had consistently maintained a high profit margin. = 公司一直保持着较高的利润率。

将来时:
The printer will automatically adjust the margins. = 打印机将自动调整页边距。
We will need to improve our profit margin next year. = 我们明年需要提高利润率。

虚拟语气:
If the margin were wider, the text would be easier to read. = 如果页边距更宽，文本就更容易阅读。
If we had a larger profit margin, we could invest more. = 如果我们有更大的利润率，我们就可以进行更多投资。

被动语态:
The margins were set by the editor. = 页边距由编辑设定。
The profit margin is determined by several factors. = 利润率由多种因素决定。

疑问句:
What is the margin of error? = 误差范围是多少？
What is the profit margin on this product? = 此产品的利润率是多少？

否定句:
There is no margin for error. = 没有出错的余地。
The company did not achieve its targeted profit margin. = 公司没有达到预期的利润率。
