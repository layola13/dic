precompilers:/ˌpriːˈkɑːmpaɪlərz/| n. |预编译器|A precompiler is a program that processes source code before it is compiled by a compiler.  It typically handles preprocessor directives, macros, and other language-specific features.  Precompilers often improve code readability, maintainability, and efficiency. |Example:  The C precompiler handles directives like #include and #define.  C预编译器处理#include和#define之类的指令。|近义词：preprocessor|反义词：compiler (in the sense of being the opposite stage of the compilation process)|名词
