/nɒnˈriːəlɪzəm/ | n. |非现实主义；反现实主义|Non-realism refers to any artistic, philosophical, or literary movement that rejects or departs from the principles of realism.  It emphasizes subjective experience, imagination, and the expression of inner states over objective representation of reality. This can manifest in various ways, including surrealism, expressionism, and magical realism, each with its own unique characteristics.  Examples include the use of fantastical elements, distorted perspectives, and symbolic imagery.|The painting's vibrant colors and distorted figures are clear examples of non-realism.|Surrealism, Expressionism, Idealism|Realism|
