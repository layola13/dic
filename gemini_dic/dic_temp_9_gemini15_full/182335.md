renationalized: /ˌriːˈnæʃənəlaɪzd/ | v. | 再国有化，重新收归国有 | To return something (especially an industry or company) to state ownership or control after a period of private ownership.  | The government renationalized the railways after years of private mismanagement.  政府在私营管理不善多年后，对铁路进行了再国有化。 | nationalize, reappropriate | privatize, deregulate | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government renationalizes failing industries. = 政府对陷入困境的行业进行再国有化。
They renationalize the bank every year. = 他们每年都会对银行进行再国有化。

一般过去时:
The country renationalized its airline last year. = 该国去年将其航空公司收归国有。
They renationalized the oil industry in the 1970s. = 他们在20世纪70年代将石油工业收归国有。

现在完成时:
The government has renationalized several key industries. = 政府已经将几个关键产业收归国有。
Several countries have renationalized their water supplies. = 若干国家已经将其供水系统收归国有。

过去完成时:
By 1950, the government had renationalized most of the major industries. = 到1950年，政府已经将大部分主要产业收归国有。
Before the crisis, they had not renationalized any banks. = 在危机之前，他们还没有对任何银行进行再国有化。

将来时:
The party promises to renationalize the energy sector if elected. = 如果当选，该党承诺将对能源部门进行再国有化。
They will likely renationalize the railways in the next few years. = 他们很可能会在未来几年内对铁路进行再国有化。

虚拟语气:
If the economy worsened, they might renationalize the banks. = 如果经济恶化，他们可能会对银行进行再国有化。
It would have been better if they had renationalized the company earlier. = 如果他们早点将公司收归国有就好了。

被动语态:
The railways were renationalized in 2000. = 铁路于2000年被收归国有。

否定句:

They have not yet decided whether to renationalize the banks. = 他们尚未决定是否对银行进行再国有化。
