preembarrassing: /ˌpriːɪmˈbærəsɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人尴尬的（尤指在尴尬发生之前） |  Describes a situation or event that is likely to cause embarrassment in the near future;  suggests anticipation of embarrassment.  It emphasizes the *imminent* nature of the embarrassment. | Example:  The preembarrassing silence hung in the air before he announced his resignation.  = 他宣布辞职前，空气中弥漫着令人尴尬的沉默。  | Near synonyms: awkward, uncomfortable, potentially embarrassing | Antonyms: comfortable, reassuring, relaxing |  词性:形容词
