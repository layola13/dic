prudence:/ˈpruːdəns/ | n. |谨慎；慎重；节俭 | Prudence is the quality of being wise and careful in making judgments and decisions.  It involves thinking carefully about the possible consequences of actions before taking them.  |例句：She showed great prudence in handling the delicate situation. 她以极大的谨慎处理了棘手的情况。

例句：The company's prudence in investing prevented it from suffering large losses during the recession.  这家公司投资谨慎，避免了在经济衰退期间遭受重大损失。


近义词：caution, carefulness, discretion, wisdom, foresight |反义词：recklessness, rashness, impulsiveness, folly


词源:  From Old French *pruidence, from Latin prūdentia, from prūdens, present participle of prūdēre "to be wise, to foresee," from prō- "before" + vidēre "to see."  The word ultimately derives from the ability to "see before," to anticipate and plan for the future.
