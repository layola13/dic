militate:/ˈmɪlɪteɪt/| v.|影响；起作用；反对；妨碍|To have a significant effect on something, especially to influence it negatively.|例句：His lack of experience militated against his chances of getting the job.  他的缺乏经验妨碍了他获得这份工作的机会。
The evidence militates against the defendant's claims.  证据对被告的主张不利。|近义词：influence, affect, counteract, impede|反义词：aid, assist, promote, facilitate|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The high cost militates against buying a new car. = 高昂的成本阻碍了购买新车的计划。
His strong personality militates against cooperation. = 他强烈的个性不利于合作。


一般过去时:
His poor health militated against his success in the competition. = 他糟糕的身体状况影响了他比赛的成功。
The bad weather militated against our plans for a picnic. = 恶劣的天气阻碍了我们野餐的计划。


现在完成时:
His lack of confidence has militated against his progress. = 他缺乏自信一直阻碍着他的进步。
The economic downturn has militated against business growth. = 经济衰退一直阻碍着商业发展。


过去完成时:
The previous administration's policies had militated against economic recovery. = 前一届政府的政策曾阻碍了经济复苏。
His previous bad record had militated against his promotion. = 他之前的劣迹曾阻碍了他的晋升。


将来时:
The new regulations will militate against small businesses. = 新规将不利于小型企业。
Increased competition will militate against our market share. = 激烈的竞争将影响我们的市场份额。


虚拟语气:
If the weather had been better, it would not have militated against our trip. = 如果天气更好，它就不会影响我们的旅行了。
If he had more experience, his lack of qualifications wouldn't have militated against his application. = 如果他有更多经验，他的资格不足就不会影响他的申请了。


被动语态:
His arguments were militated against by the facts. = 他的论点被事实驳斥了。
The project was militated against by a lack of funding. = 该项目因缺乏资金而受阻。


疑问句:
Does his age militate against his chances of employment? = 他的年龄是否影响了他的就业机会？
Will the rising cost of living militate against family budgets? = 日益上涨的生活成本是否会影响家庭预算？


否定句:
His skills did not militate against his success. = 他的技能并没有妨碍他的成功。
The favorable market conditions did not militate against their investment. = 有利的市场条件并没有影响他们的投资。


PHRASE->
militate against:  不利于；妨碍
militate in favor of: 有利于
militate strongly against: 强烈反对
militate against the interests of:  损害...的利益
militate powerfully against:  有力地反对

词源:
From Latin *militāre "to serve as a soldier," from mīles "soldier."  The original sense was "to serve in the army,"  then it evolved to "to fight against," and then to the modern meaning of "to have an effect, usually a negative one."
