raymarch:/ˈreɪmɑːrtʃ/| v. |光线行进，光线追踪|To raymarch is a technique in computer graphics used to render 3D scenes without explicitly defining meshes or polygons.  It simulates the path of light rays through a scene by iteratively stepping along a ray, calculating the distance to the nearest surface at each step, and accumulating color information.  It’s particularly useful for rendering complex, implicit surfaces and procedural generation. | Example: The artist used raymarching to create the stunningly realistic clouds in the game.  The raymarching algorithm efficiently renders the fractal landscape. |近义词：ray tracing (but raymarching is generally less computationally expensive and uses a different approach)|反义词：N/A
