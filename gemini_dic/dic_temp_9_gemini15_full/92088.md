hyperrealists:/ˈhaɪpərˈriːəlɪsts/| n. |超现实主义者（复数）|Hyperrealists are artists who create extremely realistic paintings, sculptures, or other artworks that often go beyond the level of detail found in photographs, aiming for a level of realism that surpasses reality itself.  They meticulously depict minute details and textures to achieve an almost photographic precision. |The hyperrealists of the late 20th century pushed the boundaries of representational art. |Photorealists, Superrealists|Abstract expressionists|
