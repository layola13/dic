ceviche:/səˈviːtʃeɪ/| n. |秘鲁腌鱼|A Latin American dish of raw fish marinated in citrus juices, typically served chilled.  It's a popular appetizer or starter in many coastal regions of Latin America.  The acidity of the citrus "cooks" the fish, making it safe to eat raw.| Example:  We enjoyed a delicious ceviche with fresh lime juice and cilantro. 我们享用了一道美味的酸橘汁腌鱼，里面有新鲜的酸橙汁和香菜。|近义词： escabeche (though escabeche often includes cooked ingredients)|反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "ceviche" comes from the Quechua word *siwichi*, referring to a type of fresh fish preparation in pre-Columbian Peru.  The Spanish conquistadors adopted the word, eventually evolving into the modern "ceviche."  Its usage spread throughout Latin America.
