conscience: /ˈkɒnʃəns/ /ˈkɑːnʃəns/ | n. | 良心；道德心 | The inner sense of right and wrong that guides a person's behavior.  It is a moral sense of right and wrong, viewed as acting as a guide to one's behavior. | 例句：He had a guilty conscience after lying to his parents. 他对父母撒谎后感到良心不安。

例句：Following his conscience, he refused to participate in the unethical project.  凭着良心，他拒绝参与那个不道德的项目。


近义词：moral sense, inner voice, scruples | 反义词：remorse, guilt (These are feelings associated with wrongdoing, not direct opposites of conscience itself.)


词性：名词
