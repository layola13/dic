pseudo-Indians:/ˈsuːdoʊˈɪndiənz/ | n. |假印第安人，冒充印第安人的人 |指那些并非真正印第安人血统，却以印第安人自居或模仿印第安人文化的人。  他们可能出于各种原因这样做，例如为了获得利益、融入特定群体或出于文化认同的误解。这个词带有贬义，因为它暗示了对真正印第安人文化的歪曲和不尊重。|例句：The festival featured a group of pseudo-Indians performing a poorly researched version of a traditional dance.  这个节日中有一群假印第安人表演了一个经过粗略研究的传统舞蹈版本。


The term "pseudo-Indians" is often used to describe individuals or groups who are not of Native American descent but who adopt Native American attire, customs, or traditions, often for personal gain or entertainment. This term is widely considered offensive and inaccurate because it perpetuates stereotypes and disrespects the authentic cultures and histories of Native American peoples.  It reduces complex Indigenous identities to a caricature, obscuring the diversity and richness of actual Native American cultures.


近义词：imposters, pretenders, fraudsters (in this context)


反义词：Native Americans, Indigenous peoples


词性：名词
