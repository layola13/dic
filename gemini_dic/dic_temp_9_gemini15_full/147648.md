overpollinate: /ˌoʊvərˈpɑːləneɪt/ | vt. |过度授粉|To pollinate excessively or to a degree that is harmful. This term implies that the amount of pollen applied is more than beneficial, potentially leading to negative consequences for the plant or crop.  |例句：Overpollinating the apple blossoms led to smaller, less flavorful fruit.  过度授粉苹果花导致果实更小，味道更差。
The farmer accidentally overpollinated his entire field of sunflowers, resulting in stunted growth. 农民不小心过度授粉了他所有的向日葵田，导致生长迟缓。|近义词：overfertilize, overdust  |反义词：underpollinate

词源:
The word "overpollinate" is a combination of the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") and the verb "pollinate" (to transfer pollen from the anther to the stigma of a flower).  Therefore, it literally means to pollinate to an excessive degree.
