convocation's:/ˌkɒnvəˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 召集；会议；集会 | The convocation of the college was held in the main hall. | 近义词：assembly, meeting, gathering | 反义词：dispersion, scattering


英文解析：Convocation refers to a formal assembly of people, typically a large gathering for a specific purpose, often academic or religious.  It can be a meeting of the members of a college, university, or other learned body, or a large gathering of people, often for a religious ceremony.  The possessive 's' indicates that it is referring to something belonging to or associated with the convocation. For example, "the convocation's agenda" refers to the plan or schedule of the convocation.

例句：

The convocation's keynote speaker was a renowned professor.  =  大会的主旨演讲者是一位著名的教授。

The convocation's schedule was packed with events. =  大会的日程安排很紧凑。
