contrivance:/kənˈtraɪvəns/ | n. |人为的安排；巧妙的装置；发明；策略 |A contrivance is something that has been invented or cleverly created, often to achieve a particular purpose. It can refer to a device or a plan, and often implies artificiality or lack of naturalness.  |例句：The whole plot of the play felt like a contrived contrivance.  整部戏剧的情节感觉像是一个人为的安排。

The elaborate clockwork mechanism was a remarkable contrivance of its time.  这个复杂的钟表装置是当时一项了不起的发明。|近义词：invention, device, gadget, scheme, stratagem |反义词：naturalness, simplicity, genuineness |名词
