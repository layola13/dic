diatonics: /ˌdaɪəˈtɒnɪks/ | /ˌdaɪəˈtɑːnɪks/ | n. | 半音阶的，自然音阶的 | Diatonics refers to a musical scale consisting of seven notes, including five whole steps and two half steps, arranged in a specific order.  This is the most common type of scale used in Western music.  It forms the basis of many musical modes and keys. | Example: The melody is based on a diatonic scale. 这段旋律基于一个自然音阶。 | 近义词: natural scale, major scale, minor scale | 反义词: chromatic scale
