distinguish-from:/dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ frɒm/ | v. |辨别；区分；区别|To recognize or treat (someone or something) as different from others.  It often implies a careful or detailed examination to identify the differences.|

例句：

I can distinguish the different types of birds by their songs. = 我能通过它们的歌声来区分不同种类的鸟。

It’s difficult to distinguish fact from fiction in his stories. = 在他的故事里很难区分事实和虚构。


近义词：differentiate, discriminate, separate, discern

反义词：confuse, mix up, blend


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I distinguish red from green. = 我能区分红色和绿色。
She distinguishes good from bad. = 她能区分好坏。


一般过去时:
I distinguished the genuine painting from the forgery. = 我区分了真画和赝品。
They distinguished the symptoms of the two diseases. = 他们区分了两种疾病的症状。


现在完成时:
I have distinguished myself in my field. = 我在我的领域里已经脱颖而出。
Scientists have distinguished several new species. = 科学家们已经区分出几种新的物种。


过去完成时:
I had distinguished the subtle differences before the presentation. = 在演示之前我已经区分出细微的差别。
She had distinguished herself as a talented writer before she graduated. = 在毕业之前，她已经以才华横溢的作家而闻名。


将来时:
I will distinguish the truth from the lies. = 我将区分真相和谎言。
They will distinguish the good apples from the bad ones. = 他们将把好苹果和坏苹果区分开来。


虚拟语气:
If I could distinguish the notes, I would play the song. = 如果我能区分音符，我就会演奏这首歌。
I wish I could distinguish his voice from others. = 我希望我能区分出他的声音和其他人的声音。


被动语态:
The two species were distinguished by their different habitats. = 这两个物种因其不同的栖息地而有所区别。
The winner was distinguished from the other contestants by her speed. = 获胜者因其速度而与其他参赛者区分开来。


疑问句:
Can you distinguish the flavors? = 你能区分这些味道吗？
How do you distinguish truth from falsehood? = 你如何区分真假？


否定句:
I cannot distinguish the twins. = 我无法区分这对双胞胎。
He couldn't distinguish the subtle nuances in the painting. = 他无法区分绘画中的细微差别。


PHRASE->
