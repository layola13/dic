hylozoic:/haɪloʊˈzɔɪɪk/ | adj. | 活的；具有生命力的；(指物质)具有生命特征的 |  Relating to or characterized by the belief that matter is inherently alive or has a vital principle.  The term is often used in discussions of the origin of life and the nature of matter.  It suggests a continuous spectrum between inanimate matter and living organisms. | Example: Some early theories of life proposed hylozoic principles, suggesting that life emerged from the inherent vitality of matter itself.  一些早期的生命理论提出了活力论的原则，认为生命本身就起源于物质固有的活力。 | 近义词:vitalistic, animistic | 反义词: mechanistic, inanimate
