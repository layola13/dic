excess:/ˈɛkses/ | n. |过量，过度；过剩 |Excessive consumption of alcohol can lead to health problems.  过量饮酒会导致健康问题。|近义词：surplus, superfluity, overabundance |反义词：deficiency, shortage, scarcity |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
There is an excess of sugar in this cake. = 这个蛋糕糖分过量。
The company has an excess of inventory. = 公司库存过剩。

一般过去时:
There was an excess of rain last month. = 上个月雨水过多。
An excess of spending led to the company's bankruptcy. = 过度支出导致公司破产。

现在完成时:
We have had an excess of complaints recently. = 最近我们收到了过多的投诉。
The government has accumulated an excess of debt. = 政府积累了过多的债务。

过去完成时:
There had been an excess of applications for the job. = 之前已经有太多人申请这份工作了。
They had already spent an excess of their budget. = 他们已经超支了预算。

将来时:
There will be an excess of demand for electricity this summer. = 今年夏天电力需求将过剩。
We anticipate an excess of production next quarter. = 我们预计下个季度产量过剩。

虚拟语气:
If there were no excess of food, people would suffer from hunger. = 如果没有剩余食物，人们就会挨饿。
I wish there wasn't an excess of paperwork. = 我希望没有那么多文书工作。

被动语态:
Excess baggage is subject to extra charges. = 超重行李需另收费。
The excess water was drained away. = 多余的水被排掉了。

疑问句:
Is there an excess of inventory in the warehouse? = 仓库里库存过剩吗？
What causes an excess of greenhouse gases? = 什么导致温室气体过量？

否定句:
There is no excess of salt in this dish. = 这道菜盐分不超标。
The company doesn't have an excess of funds. = 公司没有多余的资金。
