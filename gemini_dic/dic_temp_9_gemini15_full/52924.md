dimwitted:/dɪmˈwɪtɪd/| adj. |愚笨的，迟钝的 |Lacking intelligence; slow-witted.  This word implies a lack of intelligence that is noticeable and perhaps even somewhat endearing, rather than suggesting a serious intellectual deficiency. |He's a bit dimwitted, but he's got a good heart. 他有点迟钝，但心地很好。|近义词：stupid, foolish, slow-witted, obtuse |反义词：intelligent, bright, clever, sharp

词源:
The word "dimwitted" is a compound word.  "Dim" means dull or lacking in brightness or clarity, while "witted" is an archaic form related to "wit," meaning intelligence or cleverness.  Therefore, "dimwitted" literally means "dull-witted" or "having dull intelligence."
