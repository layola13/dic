dinoceras: /ˌdaɪnəˈsɪərəs/ | n. | 恐角兽 | Dinoceras is an extinct genus of large, herbivorous mammals that lived during the Eocene epoch (around 56 to 34 million years ago) in North America.  They were characterized by three pairs of horns on their skulls and a relatively large body size.  | The fossils of Dinoceras are found primarily in the Bridger Basin of Wyoming. | 近义词: Uintatherium (closely related genus) | 反义词:  (no direct antonyms, as it refers to a specific extinct animal)
