monopolize:/məˈnɒpəlaɪz/| vt.|垄断；独占；专卖|To control or take up the largest share of (something).  To dominate or occupy exclusively.|例句：He tried to monopolize the conversation. 他试图独占谈话。

例句：The company monopolizes the market for that product. 这家公司垄断了该产品的市场。


近义词：dominate, control, corner, seize, appropriate|反义词：share, distribute, divide


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He monopolizes the conversation. = 他独占谈话。
They monopolize the resources. = 他们垄断资源。

一般过去时:
She monopolized the attention of the audience. = 她吸引了观众的所有注意力。
The company monopolized the market for years. = 这家公司垄断了市场多年。

现在完成时:
He has monopolized the discussion. = 他已经独占了讨论。
They have monopolized the best seats. = 他们已经独占了最好的座位。

过去完成时:
She had monopolized the stage for hours. = 她已经独占舞台数小时了。
They had monopolized the supply chain. = 他们已经垄断了供应链。

将来时:
He will monopolize the spotlight. = 他将成为众人瞩目的焦点。
They will monopolize the market share. = 他们将垄断市场份额。

虚拟语气:
If he monopolized the market, he would be rich. = 如果他垄断了市场，他就会富有。
I wish she hadn't monopolized the conversation. = 我真希望她没有独占谈话。

被动语态:
The market was monopolized by a single company. = 市场被一家公司垄断。
The resources are being monopolized by the government. = 资源正被政府垄断。

疑问句:
Does he monopolize the conversation? = 他是否独占谈话？
Are they monopolizing the resources? = 他们是否正在垄断资源？

否定句:
He doesn't monopolize the conversation. = 他并不独占谈话。
They didn't monopolize the market. = 他们没有垄断市场。

词源:
mono- (源自希腊语 monos，意思是“一个，单个”) + -polize (源自希腊语 polein，意思是“卖”)  字面意思为“独家销售”。
