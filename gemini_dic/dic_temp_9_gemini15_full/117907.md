melt:/melt/| vt. vi. n.|融化；熔化；消解|To change from a solid to a liquid state, usually because of heat.  The process of becoming liquid. |例句：The ice cream melted in the sun. 冰淇淋在阳光下融化了。

The butter melted slowly in the pan. 黄油在锅里慢慢融化了。


近义词：fuse, dissolve, liquefy|反义词：freeze, solidify|动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ice melts quickly on a hot day. = 炎热的天气里冰很快融化。
The chocolate melts in your mouth. = 巧克力在嘴里融化。


一般过去时:
The snow melted overnight. = 雪一夜之间融化了。
The wax melted and dripped down the candle. = 蜡融化了，滴在蜡烛上。


现在完成时:
The snow has melted completely. = 雪已经完全融化了。
The ice cream has melted all over my shirt. = 冰淇淋融化在我的衬衫上了。


过去完成时:
The ice had melted before we arrived. = 我们到达之前冰已经融化了。
The chocolate had melted by the time we got home. = 我们到家的时候巧克力已经融化了。


将来时:
The snow will melt when the sun comes out. = 太阳出来后雪就会融化。
The ice will melt in a few hours. = 冰过几个小时就会融化。


虚拟语气:
If the ice melted, the boat would sink. = 如果冰融化了，船就会沉。
If the butter melted, the cake would be ruined. = 如果黄油融化了，蛋糕就会毁了。


被动语态:
The chocolate was melted slowly over a low heat. = 巧克力在小火上慢慢融化。
The ice was melted by the warm air. = 冰被暖空气融化了。


疑问句:
Does the ice melt quickly? = 冰融化得快吗？
Will the butter melt in the oven? = 黄油会在烤箱里融化吗？


否定句:
The ice didn't melt. = 冰没有融化。
The snow hasn't melted yet. = 雪还没融化。


PHRASE->
melt away: 消失；逐渐减少
melt down: 熔化；垮台
melt into: 融入；溶入
melt off: 融化消失
melt with: 充满...感情


词源:
Old English *meltan, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch smelten and German schmelzen.  The meaning is connected to the idea of softening or becoming less hard.
