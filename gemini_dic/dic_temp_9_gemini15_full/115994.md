marshaling: /ˈmɑːʃəlɪŋ/ | v. | 整理；调动；排列；指挥 | The act of assembling and organizing something, especially troops or resources.  It implies careful arrangement and preparation for a specific purpose. |例句：The general is marshaling his troops for the upcoming battle. 将军正在调动他的军队准备即将到来的战斗。


The company is marshaling its resources to launch a new product. 公司正在调集资源以推出新产品。|近义词：organizing, arranging, mobilizing, assembling |反义词：disorganizing, scattering, disbanding


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is marshaling his arguments for the debate. 他正在为辩论整理论据。
She marshals her resources efficiently. 她有效地调配资源。

一般过去时:
They marshaled their forces before the attack. 他们在攻击前集结了兵力。
The company marshaled its resources to overcome the crisis. 公司调集资源以克服危机。

现在完成时:
She has marshaled considerable evidence to support her claim. 她已收集了大量证据来支持她的说法。
The team has marshaled its strength for the final game.  团队已经积蓄力量准备决赛。

过去完成时:

将来时:
