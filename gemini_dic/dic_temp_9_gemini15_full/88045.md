higher-up:/ˈhaɪər ʌp/ | n. | 上级，上司 | A person of higher rank or authority within an organization.  | Example: I need to discuss this with my higher-ups before making a decision. 我需要和我的上级讨论一下才能做决定。 |近义词：superior, boss, manager, official | 反义词：subordinate, underling


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The higher-ups are reviewing the proposal. = 上级正在审核提案。
My higher-ups make all the important decisions. = 我的上级做所有重要决定。

一般过去时:
The higher-ups approved the budget last week. = 上周上级批准了预算。
Higher-ups didn't respond to our emails. = 上级没有回复我们的邮件。


现在完成时:
The higher-ups have already made their decision. = 上级已经做出了决定。
Have the higher-ups contacted you yet? = 上级联系你了吗？


过去完成时:
The higher-ups had already left when I arrived. = 我到达时，上级已经离开了。
We learned that the higher-ups had rejected the plan before we presented it. = 我们在提案前得知上级已经否决了计划。


将来时:
The higher-ups will announce the changes next month. = 上级将在下个月宣布这些变化。
Will the higher-ups be available for a meeting next week? = 下周上级有时间开会吗？


虚拟语气:
If the higher-ups were here, they would approve this immediately. = 如果上级在这里，他们会立即批准。
I wish the higher-ups understood our difficulties. = 我希望上级能理解我们的困难。


被动语态:
The decision was made by the higher-ups. = 决定是由上级做出的。
The matter is being handled by the higher-ups. = 这件事正在由上级处理。


疑问句:
Are the higher-ups aware of the problem? = 上级知道这个问题吗？
What do the higher-ups expect from us? = 上级对我们的期望是什么？


否定句:
The higher-ups did not approve our request. = 上级没有批准我们的请求。
The higher-ups are not satisfied with the results. = 上级对结果不满意。
