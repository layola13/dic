self-exculpation: /ˌsɛlf ɪkˈskʌlpeɪʃən/ | n. | 自我开脱；为自己辩护 | The act of clearing oneself from blame or guilt; self-justification.  It often implies a less than honest attempt to avoid responsibility. | 例句：His self-exculpation rang hollow given the overwhelming evidence against him.  他为自己辩护的声音听起来很空洞，因为有压倒性的证据反对他的说法。


His self-exculpation was unconvincing and only served to further damage his reputation.他的自我开脱毫无说服力，只会进一步损害他的声誉。


近义词：self-justification, vindication, exoneration, apology (in some contexts) | 反义词：self-incrimination, confession, admission of guilt | 词性：名词
