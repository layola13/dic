lexes:/ˈlɛksɪz/| n.|词素，词位|A lexeme is the basic unit of meaning in a language,  abstracting away from the various forms a word might take (inflections).  For example, "run," "runs," "running," and "ran" are all forms of the same lexeme.|The lexes of English include words like "cat," "dog," and "run."|近义词：word, morpheme|反义词：无

PHRASE->
lexical field (词汇场): A set of words related in meaning.
lexical ambiguity (词汇歧义):  Multiple meanings for a single word.
lexical semantics (词汇语义学): The study of word meaning.
lexical entry (词典词条):  A dictionary definition of a word.
lexical density (词汇密度): The ratio of content words to function words in a text.
