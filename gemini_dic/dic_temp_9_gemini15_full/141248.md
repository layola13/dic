opacity:/oʊˈpæsɪti/| n. |不透明度，不透明性 |The opacity of the glass prevented us from seeing inside.  玻璃的不透明性阻止了我们看到里面。|近义词：cloudiness, denseness |反义词：transparency, clarity |名词


英文解析:The degree to which something is not transparent; the extent to which light is blocked by something.  The state of being opaque.


例句:

The opacity of the fog made driving dangerous.  雾的浓厚使驾驶变得危险。

The high opacity of the paint required two coats.  油漆的不透明度很高，需要涂两层。


词源:

From Latin *opacitas, from opacus "shaded, dark," from ob "against" + pacere "to fasten, fix."  The modern sense developed in the 17th century.
