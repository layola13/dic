dehydration:/ˌdiːhaɪˈdreɪʃən/ | n. | 脱水；干燥 | The loss of water from the body or from a substance.  It can range from mild to severe and is characterized by symptoms such as thirst, dry mouth, fatigue, and dizziness. Severe dehydration can be life-threatening. | Example:  Prolonged exposure to the sun can lead to dehydration.  长时间暴露在阳光下会导致脱水。

The athlete suffered from severe dehydration after the marathon.  这位运动员马拉松后严重脱水。


近义词：desiccation, dryness | 反义词：hydration

词源:
The word "dehydration" is derived from the prefix "de-" (meaning "removal of" or "lack of"), the word "hydrate" (meaning "to combine with water"), and the suffix "-tion" (indicating a process or state).  Therefore, dehydration literally means the "removal of water" or "lack of water".
