nonapostolically:/ˌnɒnəˌpɒstɒlɪkli/ | adj. |非使徒地，非宗徒地 | Not in accordance with the teachings or practices of the apostles.  It implies a lack of apostolic authority, tradition, or succession. |例句：The church's practices were considered nonapostolically by some scholars, leading to significant theological debates.  一些学者认为教会的实践是非使徒的，导致了重大的神学辩论。


The theological interpretation of certain scriptures was viewed nonapostolically by the early Church Fathers. 早期教会教父们认为某些经文的解释是非使徒的。


近义词：uncanonical, heterodox, unorthodox | 反义词：apostolically, canonically, orthodox | 词性：形容词


词源:

* **non-**:  A prefix meaning "not."
* **apostolic**:  Derived from "apostle," ultimately from the Greek word "apostolos" (ἀπόστολος), meaning "one who is sent," referring to the twelve original disciples of Jesus Christ and later those sent to spread the Christian faith.


用法说明:

This word is primarily used in theological and ecclesiastical contexts.  It describes actions, beliefs, or practices that deviate from what is considered authentic apostolic teaching or tradition.  The term often carries a negative connotation, implying a departure from established orthodoxy.  It's not a word used in everyday conversation.
