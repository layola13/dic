chowk:/tʃɔːk/ | n. | 岔路口，十字路口，拥挤的地方 | A chowk is a place where two or more roads meet, often a busy and crowded intersection. 词源:
The word "chowk" is derived from the Hindi word चौक (chauk), meaning "square" or "open space," often referring to a public square or market area where roads meet.  Its usage has spread through various South Asian languages and is now commonly understood in English within the context of South Asian urban geography.
