crowning:/ˈkraʊnɪŋ/| n., adj., v.|加冕；顶峰；最佳；加冕的；顶尖的|

英文解析:
As a noun, "crowning" refers to the act of placing a crown on someone's head, typically a monarch, or the ceremony surrounding this act.  It can also figuratively refer to the highest point or peak of something, the best or most important part. As an adjective, it describes something that is at the highest point or most important stage. As a verb, it means to place a crown on someone's head or to top or complete something.

例句:
The crowning of the new king was a spectacular event. = 新国王的加冕典礼是一场壮观的盛事。

The crowning achievement of her career was winning the Nobel Prize. = 她职业生涯中最伟大的成就就是获得了诺贝尔奖。


近义词:
(n.) coronation, culmination, apex, pinnacle, climax
(adj.) supreme, ultimate, top, best

反义词:
(n.) beginning, start, base, bottom
(adj.) lowest, worst, inferior


词性:
noun, adjective, verb
