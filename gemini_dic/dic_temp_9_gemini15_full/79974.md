go-betweens:/ˈɡoʊˌbɪtwiːnz/ | n. |中间人，调解人 |A go-between is a person who acts as a mediator or intermediary between two parties who are unable or unwilling to communicate directly.  They facilitate communication and negotiation, often in situations involving conflict or differing interests. |例句：The lawyer acted as a go-between in the divorce negotiations.  律师在离婚谈判中充当了中间人的角色。

例句：She served as a go-between for the warring factions. 她充当了交战各派系之间的中间人。


近义词：intermediary, mediator, negotiator, liaison, messenger

反义词：N/A (lack of a direct antonym, as the word describes a function rather than a state)

词性:名词


Etymology:

The word "go-between" is a compound word formed from the verb "go" and the preposition "between." Its meaning developed naturally from the literal sense of someone or something that "goes between" two other things or people.  The hyphen indicates its compound nature. The earliest recorded usage dates back to the 16th century.
