reshuffling:/ˌriːˈʃʌflɪŋ/ | n. |重新洗牌；改组；调整 |The act of rearranging or reorganizing something, especially the members of a group or the elements of a structure.  This often implies a change in order or position, with the goal of improving efficiency or effectiveness. |例句：The company is undergoing a major reshuffling of its management team. 公司正在对其管理团队进行大规模改组。
例句：The reshuffling of the deck of cards changed the game's outcome. 洗牌改变了游戏的结果。|近义词：rearrangement, reorganization, reshaping, shuffling|反义词：stabilization, maintenance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The team is reshuffling its strategy. = 团队正在调整其策略。
He reshuffles the cards before each game. = 他在每场比赛前都洗牌。


一般过去时:
They reshuffled the cabinet after the election. = 他们在选举后改组了内阁。
She reshuffled the furniture to create more space. = 她重新摆放家具以创造更多空间。
