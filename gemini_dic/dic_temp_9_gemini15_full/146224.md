overexplicit: /ˌoʊvərɪkˈsplɪsɪt/ | adj. | 过分明确的，过于详细的 | Excessively explicit; leaving no room for doubt or interpretation.  It implies an unnecessary level of detail or clarity that may be tiresome or even off-putting. | The instructions were overexplicit, making the task seem far more complicated than it actually was.  |近义词： verbose, detailed, explicit,  wordy | 反义词： implicit, vague, ambiguous, understated


例句：

The teacher's overexplicit explanation of the simple concept confused the students. 教师对简单概念的过度详细解释反而让学生们糊涂了。

His overexplicit description of the accident left nothing to the imagination. 他对事故的过度详细描述，让人没有任何想象的空间。


词源：

* **over-**:  prefix indicating "excessive" or "too much"
* **explicit**:  from Latin "explicitus," past participle of "explicare," meaning "to unfold, explain."


用法说明:

overexplicit主要用作形容词，修饰名词，例如解释、说明、指示等。它强调的是信息的表达方式过于详细，甚至到了令人厌烦或反感的程度。  与simply explicit的区别在于，explicit仅仅表示明确，而overexplicit则表达了明确到过分的程度。
