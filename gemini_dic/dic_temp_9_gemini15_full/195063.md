semibull:/ˈsemiˌbʊl/| n.|半牛市|A semibull market is a period in the stock market where prices are generally rising, but at a slower pace than a full bull market.  It's characterized by periods of both gains and losses, and a lack of strong upward momentum.  Investors may be hesitant or cautious during a semibull market.|Example: After a period of rapid growth, the stock market entered a semibull phase, with modest gains interspersed with periods of correction.|近义词：weak bull market, consolidating market|反义词：bear market, semibear market|名词
