durables:/ˈdʒʊərəbəlz/| n.|耐用品，耐用消费品|Goods that are expected to last for three years or more.  They are usually more expensive than non-durable goods and are often purchased less frequently. Examples include furniture, appliances, and cars.|例句：The sale of durables increased significantly last quarter.  上季度耐用品的销量大幅增加。|例句：Consumers are delaying purchases of major durables due to economic uncertainty. 由于经济不确定性，消费者正在推迟购买主要耐用品。|近义词：consumer durables, long-lasting goods|反义词：non-durables, consumables|名词
