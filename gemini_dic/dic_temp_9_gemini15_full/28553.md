cardiosclerosis: /ˌkɑːrdiˌoʊskləˈroʊsɪs/ | n. | 心肌硬化 |  Hardening of the heart muscle. This condition is characterized by the thickening and stiffening of the heart muscle, reducing its ability to pump blood effectively.  It can be caused by various factors including hypertension, diabetes, and coronary artery disease. |  The doctor diagnosed her with cardiosclerosis after reviewing her echocardiogram.  医生在查看她的超声心动图后诊断她患有心肌硬化。 |  Cardiomyopathy, myocardial fibrosis |  Cardiomyopathy (in the sense of a healthy heart),  normal heart function |  n.
