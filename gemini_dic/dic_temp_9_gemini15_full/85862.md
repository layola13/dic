head-on:/ˌhedˈɒn/|/ˌhedˈɑːn/| adj. |迎面地；正面地；直接地|Directly from the front; without evasion or compromise.|The car crashed head-on into a tree.  汽车迎头撞上了树。|They decided to tackle the problem head-on. 他们决定直接解决这个问题。|indirect, sideways, obliquely|indirectly


USAGE->

The train approaches head-on. = 火车迎面驶来。
We meet head-on every morning. = 我们每天早上迎面相遇。

一般过去时:
The two cars collided head-on. = 两辆车迎面相撞。
They confronted the issue head-on. = 他们正面应对这个问题。

现在完成时:
