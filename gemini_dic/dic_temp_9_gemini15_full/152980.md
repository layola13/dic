pedireme:/pɛdɪˈriːmiː/| n. |轻型战船，快速小船| A pedireme was a type of ancient Greek and Roman warship, smaller and faster than a trireme, typically propelled by a smaller number of oarsmen (typically one or two banks of oars).  It was used for scouting, raiding, and other fast maneuvers.|Example: The Athenian navy used pediremes for reconnaissance missions along the enemy coastline.  雅典海军用轻型战船在敌人的海岸线上执行侦察任务。|近义词：galley, bireme(双桨战船)|反义词：trireme(三层桨战船), quinquereme(五层桨战船)
