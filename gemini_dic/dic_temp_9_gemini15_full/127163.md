naturalise: /ˈnætʃrəlaɪz/ | v. | 使归化；使入籍；使自然化 | To grant someone the rights and privileges of a citizen of a country they were not born in.  It can also refer to the process of something becoming natural or adapting to a new environment. |

例句：
He naturalised in Canada after living there for five years. = 他在加拿大住了五年后入籍了。

She naturalised the wild plants in her garden. = 她把野生植物驯化到她的花园里。


近义词：acclimatize, assimilate, integrate, adopt


反义词：denaturalize, expatriate


词性:动词

词源:
The word "naturalise" comes from the Old French word "naturaliser," which in turn comes from the Latin word "naturalis," meaning "of or pertaining to nature." The suffix "-ise" is a common English verb ending indicating to make or become something.
