dioptry:/ˈdaɪɒptri/| n.|屈光度；度数|A unit of measurement of the refractive power of a lens, equal to the reciprocal of the focal length in meters.|例句：The optician prescribed glasses with a dioptry of -2.  验光师开了-2度数的眼镜。|近义词：power (in the context of lenses)|反义词：无


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb forms or tenses like "what" does.  Therefore, the traditional tense conjugations are not applicable.  We can, however, show its usage in different sentence structures.


In a simple sentence:
His prescription is +2 diopters. = 他的处方是2度。

In a comparative sentence:
Her diopter needs are higher than his. = 她的屈光度需求比他高。

In a complex sentence:
Because the patient's dioptry was high, the doctor suggested surgery. = 因为病人的屈光度很高，医生建议手术。

In a question:
What is the dioptry of your lenses? = 你的镜片度数是多少？

In a sentence with a prepositional phrase:
The glasses are categorized by dioptry. = 眼镜按度数分类。

In a passive voice sentence:
The diopter was accurately measured by the optometrist. = 验光师准确地测量了屈光度。
