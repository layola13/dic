photopsy:/ˈfoʊtoʊpsaɪ/ | n. | 光幻视，光视错觉 |  A visual illusion caused by exposure to bright light or intense visual stimuli;  a condition where you see things that aren't actually there due to light exposure.  It often involves seeing flashes, streaks, or other visual disturbances after looking at a bright light source. | Example:  After staring at the sun, she experienced photopsy, seeing bright spots and trails in her vision.  = 盯着太阳看后，她出现了光幻视，眼前出现明亮的光点和轨迹。| 近义词：phosphenes, afterimage | 反义词：N/A
