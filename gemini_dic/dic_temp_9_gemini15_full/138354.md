noticing: /ˈnoʊtɪsɪŋ/ | v. | 注意到；觉察到 | The act of becoming aware of something or someone.  It often implies a degree of observation and understanding. |


例句：
I noticed a mistake in the report. = 我注意到报告中有一个错误。

He noticed her sadness in her eyes. = 他注意到她眼中流露出的悲伤。


近义词：observing, perceiving, detecting, remarking, spotting


反义词：ignoring, overlooking, missing


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I notice the birds singing every morning. = 我每天早上都注意到鸟儿在唱歌。
She notices every detail. = 她注意到每一个细节。


一般过去时:
I noticed a strange man lurking near the house. = 我注意到一个奇怪的男人潜伏在房子附近。
They noticed the change in her behavior. = 他们注意到她行为的变化。


现在完成时:
I have noticed a pattern in their actions. = 我已经注意到他们行为中的一种模式。
She has noticed an improvement in her health. = 她已经注意到她的健康状况有所改善。


过去完成时:
I had noticed the problem before he mentioned it. = 在他提到之前，我已经注意到这个问题了。
They had noticed the discrepancy in the accounts. = 他们已经注意到账户中的差异。


将来时:
I will notice if anything changes. = 如果有任何变化，我会注意到的。
She will notice the difference in quality. = 她会注意到质量的差异。


虚拟语气:
If I had noticed the warning sign, I would have avoided the accident. = 如果我注意到警告标志，我就不会发生事故了。
I wish I had noticed the error sooner. = 我希望我早点注意到这个错误。


被动语态:
The mistake was noticed by the editor. = 编辑注意到了这个错误。
The change in policy was noticed by many people. = 许多人都注意到了政策的变化。


疑问句:
Did you notice anything unusual? = 你注意到什么异常了吗？
Have you noticed the new furniture? = 你注意到新的家具了吗？


否定句:
I didn't notice the time. = 我没有注意到时间。
She didn't notice him leave. = 她没注意到他离开。


PHRASE->
notice something amiss = 注意到一些不对劲的地方
notice the difference = 注意到不同
worth noticing = 值得注意
bring something to sb's notice = 把某事告知某人
escape sb's notice = 没有被某人注意到
