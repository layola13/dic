skew:/skjuː/| n., v.|歪斜；偏差；歪曲 |To cause something to be not straight or level; to distort or misrepresent something.|The strong wind skewed the results of the experiment. 强风使实验结果出现偏差。 The media skewed the facts to create a negative impression. 媒体歪曲事实以制造负面印象。|distort, slant, warp|straighten, align, rectify|名词，动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The wind skews the data. = 风使数据出现偏差。
He skews the facts to support his argument. = 他歪曲事实以支持他的论点。


一般过去时:
The accident skewed the traffic flow. = 事故导致交通流量发生偏差。
The artist intentionally skewed the perspective in the painting. = 艺术家故意在画中歪曲了透视角度。


现在完成时:
Years of bias have skewed public opinion. = 多年的偏见已经歪曲了公众舆论。
The recent scandal has skewed the company's reputation. = 最近的丑闻损害了公司的声誉。


过去完成时:
The faulty equipment had skewed the earlier results. = 有问题的设备已经歪曲了早期的结果。
His preconceived notions had skewed his interpretation of the events. = 他先入为主的观念歪曲了他对事件的解读。


将来时:
The upcoming election will likely skew the political landscape. = 即将到来的选举可能会歪曲政治格局。
I fear the new policy will skew the market. = 我担心新政策会扭曲市场。


虚拟语气:
If the data were not skewed, the conclusions would be different. = 如果数据没有偏差，结论就会不同。
I wish the media hadn't skewed the story. = 我希望媒体没有歪曲这个故事。


被动语态:
The results were skewed by human error. = 结果因人为错误而出现偏差。
The survey was skewed by a biased sample. = 调查由于样本偏差而出现偏差。


疑问句:
Is the data skewed? = 数据有偏差吗？
Does this perspective skew the argument? = 这种观点是否歪曲了论点？


否定句:
The experiment did not skew the results. = 实验没有使结果出现偏差。
The reporter tried to remain objective and not skew the facts. = 记者试图保持客观，不歪曲事实。
