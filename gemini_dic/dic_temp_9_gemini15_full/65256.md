etymon:/ˈɛtɪmɒn/| n. |词源，语源 |A etymon is the earliest form or source from which a word is derived. It refers to the original word or root from which a later word evolved.  It's often cited in discussions of linguistic history and word formation. |Example: The etymon of "television" is derived from Greek words meaning "far" and "seeing."|近义词：root, origin, source |反义词：derivative, offshoot
