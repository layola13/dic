forfeiter:/ˈfɔːrfɪtər/ | n. | 丧失者；放弃者；没收者 | One who forfeits something.  A person who loses or gives up something as a penalty or consequence. | He was declared a forfeiter of his rights. 他被宣布丧失了他的权利。 | loser, relinquisher | winner, keeper | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The gambler forfeits his winnings. = 赌徒输掉了他的奖金。
She forfeits her right to appeal. = 她放弃了上诉的权利。


一般过去时:
He forfeited his bond. = 他丧失了他的债券。
They forfeited the game due to poor sportsmanship. = 由于缺乏体育精神，他们输掉了比赛。


现在完成时:
He has forfeited his chance of promotion. = 他已经失去了晋升的机会。
She has forfeited all her savings. = 她已经损失了她所有的积蓄。


过去完成时:
They had forfeited their advantage by making that mistake. = 他们因犯了那个错误而失去了优势。
He had forfeited his right to speak by interrupting constantly. = 他因不断打断而失去了发言权。


将来时:
If he continues to violate the rules, he will forfeit his membership. = 如果他继续违反规章制度，他将失去会员资格。
She will forfeit her deposit if she cancels the contract. = 如果她取消合同，她将失去她的押金。


虚拟语气:
If I had played better, I wouldn't have forfeited the match. = 如果我打得更好，我就不会输掉比赛了。
If he had followed the instructions, he wouldn't have forfeited his prize. = 如果他按照指示去做，他就不会失去奖品了。


被动语态:
His property was forfeited to the state. = 他的财产被没收归国家所有。
The contract was forfeited because of a breach. = 合同因违约而作废。


疑问句:
Will he forfeit his position? = 他会失去他的职位吗？
What will be forfeited if we fail to meet the deadline? = 如果我们没有按时完成，将会失去什么？


否定句:
He did not forfeit his rights. = 他没有丧失他的权利。
They did not forfeit their opportunity. = 他们没有错过他们的机会。


PHRASE->

forfeiture of rights: 权利的丧失
forfeiture of property: 财产的没收
forfeiture of contract: 合同的失效
forfeiture of bail: 保释金的没收
forfeiture clause: 没收条款
