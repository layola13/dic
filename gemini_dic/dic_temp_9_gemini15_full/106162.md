knock-about:/ˈnɒkəbaʊt/ | /ˈnɑːkəbaʊt/ | n. adj. v. | 嬉闹；打闹；破旧的；不重要的；随意地对待 | A knock-about is a rough and tumble game or activity, often involving physical contact.  It can also describe something that is old, worn, or in disrepair. As a verb, it means to treat something or someone roughly or carelessly. | 例句：The children had a knock-about in the garden.孩子们在花园里嬉戏打闹。


The old car was a real knock-about. 这辆旧车破旧不堪。


近义词：roughhouse (n.), ramshackle (adj.),  scuffle (v.),  rough (adj.),  casual (adj.) | 反义词：delicate (adj.),  refined (adj.),  gentle (adj.),  meticulous (adj.)
