occlusion:/əˈkluːʒən/ | n. | 闭塞；阻塞；遮挡 | The blocking or closing of something, such as a blood vessel, passage, or opening.  This can refer to physical blockage or a metaphorical blocking of something like vision or communication.  | 例句：The occlusion of the artery caused a stroke.  动脉闭塞导致中风。


The occlusion of the sun by the moon caused a solar eclipse. 太阳被月亮遮挡造成了日食。


近义词：blockage, obstruction, closure, blockage, shutting | 反义词：opening, clearance, unobstructed


词源:  From Latin *occludere, meaning "to shut up, close."  The prefix "oc-" is from "ob-" (towards, against) and "-cludere" is from "claudere" (to close, shut).
