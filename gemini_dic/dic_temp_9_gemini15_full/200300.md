/ˈstraɪk/| n. v.| 罢工；打击；袭击；发现；打动 |  A sudden, forceful blow or impact; a work stoppage caused by a labor dispute; to hit forcefully; to discover something unexpectedly; to impress or affect someone deeply. |例句：The miners went on strike for better wages.  矿工们为了争取更好的工资而罢工。 |例句：Lightning struck the tree.  闪电击中了树木。|近义词：hit, attack, impress, discover |反义词：miss, fail


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The workers strike for higher pay. = 工人们为争取更高的薪水而罢工。
He strikes the ball hard. = 他用力击球。


一般过去时:
The team went on strike last week. = 团队上周进行了罢工。
She struck a match to light the candle. = 她划了一根火柴来点燃蜡烛。


现在完成时:
The writers have struck a deal with the publisher. = 作家们已经与出版商达成协议。
A great idea has struck me! = 我突然想到一个好主意！


过去完成时:
The workers had struck several times before the agreement was reached. = 在达成协议之前，工人们已经罢工好几次了。
He had struck the wrong key. = 他按错了键。


将来时:
The union will strike if their demands are not met. = 如果他们的要求得不到满足，工会将举行罢工。
I think it will strike you as odd. = 我认为你会觉得它很奇怪。


虚拟语气:
If the workers struck, the company would lose a lot of money. = 如果工人罢工，公司将损失大量资金。
If he had struck the ball harder, he might have scored. = 如果他击球更用力，他可能就得分了。


被动语态:
The tree was struck by lightning. = 这棵树被闪电击中了。
The deal was struck after hours of negotiation. =经过数小时的谈判后，交易达成。


疑问句:
Did the match strike you as interesting? = 你觉得这场比赛有趣吗？
When did the strike begin? = 罢工是什么时候开始的？


否定句:
The plan didn't strike me as feasible. = 我觉得这个计划不可行。
He didn't strike me as being very honest. = 我觉得他不怎么诚实。
