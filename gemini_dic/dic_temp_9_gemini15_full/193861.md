self-disclosures: /ˌsɛlf dɪˈskloʊʒərz/ | n. |自我揭露，自我披露 | The act of revealing personal information about oneself to others.  This can range from relatively superficial information to deeply personal and intimate details.  The extent and nature of self-disclosure vary greatly depending on the context, the relationship between individuals, and cultural norms. |

例句：

Her self-disclosures in therapy helped her process her trauma.  | 她对治疗师的自我揭露帮助她处理了创伤。

His willingness to make self-disclosures fostered a deeper connection with his colleagues. | 他愿意进行自我揭露，这促进了他和同事之间更深厚的联系。


近义词：confessions, revelations, admissions, sharing


反义词：secrecy, concealment, reservation


词性：名词
