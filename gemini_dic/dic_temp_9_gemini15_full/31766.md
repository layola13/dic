check-off:/ˈtʃɛkɒf/ | n. v. | 核对，勾选；(对清单上的项目)打勾 |  A check-off is the act of marking something on a list as complete or verified.  It can also refer to the list itself or a system of verifying items. As a verb, it means to mark something on a list as complete. | 例句：The teacher did a check-off of the students' names.  老师对学生的名字进行了核对。

The mechanic performed a check-off of all the car's components before returning it. 技师在归还汽车之前对汽车的所有部件进行了核对。


近义词：verification, confirmation, inspection, tally | 反义词：omission, oversight


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He checks off each item as he completes it. 他完成每一项后都会打勾。
She checks off the tasks on her to-do list every day. 她每天都会核对她的待办事项清单上的任务。


一般过去时:
I checked off all the boxes on the form. 我在表格上勾选了所有方框。

He has checked off all the requirements for graduation. 他已经核对了他毕业的所有要求。


过去完成时:
They had checked off all the safety procedures before starting the experiment. 在开始实验之前，他们已经核对过所有安全程序。
She had checked off every item on the shopping list before returning home. 在回家之前，她已经核对过购物清单上的每一项物品。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

否定句:
