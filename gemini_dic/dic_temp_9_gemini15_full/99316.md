intercrust:/ˈɪntərkɹʌst/| n. |层层叠加的硬壳；(尤指地质学中的)互层 |A layer of rock or other material that is formed between two other layers.  It often refers to geological formations where layers interlock or are tightly bound together.  |例句：The intercrust of the sedimentary rock showed evidence of significant pressure.  沉积岩的层层叠加的硬壳显示出巨大的压力证据。|The intercrust of the ancient volcano was incredibly dense. 古代火山的层层叠加的硬壳密度惊人。|近义词：stratum, layer, bed |反义词：unlayered, homogeneous
