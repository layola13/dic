schadenfreude: /ˈʃɑːdənfrɔɪdə/ | n. |幸灾乐祸； Schadenfreude |  A feeling of pleasure derived from another person's misfortune.  | 例句：He felt a pang of schadenfreude when he heard his rival had been fired. 他听到他的竞争对手被解雇了，感到一阵幸灾乐祸。


例句：I couldn't help but feel a little schadenfreude when I saw my annoying neighbor's car get towed.  当我看到我那个讨厌的邻居的车被拖走时，我不禁感到一丝幸灾乐祸。


近义词：malicious joy, gloating, relish in misfortune, glee at another's downfall


反义词：sympathy, empathy, compassion

词源:
The word "schadenfreude" is of German origin. It is a compound word formed from:

* Schaden: harm, damage, loss
* Freude: joy, pleasure

Therefore, the literal translation is "harm-joy" or "damage-joy," perfectly capturing the meaning of the word.  It entered English in the late 19th century.
