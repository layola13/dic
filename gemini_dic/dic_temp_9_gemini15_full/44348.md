cross-beak:/ˈkrɒsbiːk/| n.| 交叉喙鸟 | A cross-beak is a type of bird with mandibles that cross each other.  This unusual beak structure is an adaptation for specialized feeding habits, often involving extracting seeds from cones. | Example: The cross-beak expertly extracted seeds from the pine cone. 交叉喙鸟熟练地从松果中提取种子。|近义词：None (it's a specific type of bird)|反义词：None
