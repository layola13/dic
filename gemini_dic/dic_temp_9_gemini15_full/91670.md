hyperchlorination: /ˌhaɪpərˌklɔːrɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. | 过度加氯 | The process of adding an excessive amount of chlorine to something, typically water.  This can lead to undesirable tastes, odors, and potential health risks. | Example:  The hyperchlorination of the swimming pool left a strong chlorine smell in the air.  过度加氯的游泳池导致空气中弥漫着浓烈的氯气味。 | 近义词: overchlorination | 反义词: underchlorination
