engel:/ˈɛŋɡəl/| n.|天使；恩格尔（人名）|The word "engel" is derived from the Old High German word "engil," meaning "messenger" or "angel."  It's related to the Old English "engel" and the Old Norse "engill," all sharing a common Proto-Germanic root.  In modern usage, it primarily refers to an angel in a religious context, or it can be a surname.|例句：In Christian theology, angels are often depicted as messengers of God.  在基督教神学中，天使通常被描绘为上帝的使者。


The surname Engel is quite common in German-speaking countries.  恩格尔这个姓氏在德语系国家相当常见。|近义词：seraph, cherub (for specific types of angels) |反义词：demon, devil
