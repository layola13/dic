overemphasis:/ˌoʊvərˈɛmfəsɪs/ | n. |过分强调；过分重视 | The overemphasis on grades can lead to unhealthy competition among students. 过分强调成绩会导致学生之间不健康的竞争。 |近义词：exaggeration, undue stress |反义词：underemphasis, neglect

英文解析：Overemphasis refers to placing too much importance or significance on something, often to the detriment of other aspects.  It implies an imbalance, where one element is highlighted disproportionately.

例句：

The overemphasis on testing has narrowed the curriculum.  对考试的过分重视导致课程内容缩窄了。

His overemphasis on detail made the presentation tedious. 他对细节的过分强调使演示文稿变得冗长乏味。


词源:

* **over-**:  prefix meaning "too much," "excessive," or "beyond."
* **emphasis**: from Greek "emphasis" (ἔμφασις), meaning "stress," "force," or "expression."  This, in turn, comes from the verb "emphasize" (ἐμφαίνειν), meaning "to show," "to appear," or "to make clear."
