nightjars: /ˈnaɪtˌdʒɑːrz/ | n. |夜鹰 |A nightjar is a nocturnal bird belonging to the family Caprimulgidae, characterized by their cryptic plumage, large eyes, and insectivorous diet. They are often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and scrublands.  They are also known for their distinctive calls. | Example:  We heard the eerie calls of nightjars throughout the night.  我们在夜间听到夜鹰怪异的叫声。

词源:
The word "nightjar" is a compound word.  "Night" refers to its nocturnal nature, and "jar" likely refers to its harsh, jarring call.  The "jar" is not related to the glass container.  Some suggest a connection to the Old English word *gear, meaning "cry".
