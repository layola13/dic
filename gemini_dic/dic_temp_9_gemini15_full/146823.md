overimpress:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈprɛs/| vt.|使印象过深，过度留下深刻印象|To impress someone too much; to make too strong an impression on someone.  This often implies that the attempt to impress was excessive or unsuccessful.|例句：He tried to overimpress his new boss with his knowledge of obscure financial instruments, but it backfired. 他试图用他掌握的冷门金融工具知识给新老板留下深刻印象，但这适得其反。

例句：Her elaborate attempt to overimpress her date with her knowledge of wine only made him uncomfortable. 她试图用她对葡萄酒的知识给约会对象留下深刻印象，结果却让他感到不舒服。


近义词：overwhelm, astound, dazzle, impress excessively|反义词：underwhelm, disappoint, bore|动词
