cover-ups: /ˈkʌvərˌʌps/ | n. |掩盖；掩饰；遮盖物 | An attempt to prevent the truth about something unpleasant or embarrassing from becoming known.  A cover-up is an action taken to hide something that is considered shameful, illegal, or embarrassing. This could range from a small lie to a large-scale conspiracy.  |例句：The government is accused of a massive cover-up concerning the environmental damage. 政府被指控掩盖了有关环境破坏的大量事实。

The police investigation uncovered a cover-up of the accident. 警方调查揭露了对事故的掩盖。


近义词：concealment, suppression, masking, whitewash | 反义词：disclosure, revelation, transparency
