oxyhaemocyanin: /ˌɒksiˈhiːməʊsaɪənɪn/ | n. |血蓝蛋白|

英文解析:Oxyhaemocyanin is a type of hemocyanin, a copper-containing protein that transports oxygen in the hemolymph (blood) of some arthropods and mollusks. Unlike hemoglobin, which contains iron, hemocyanin is blue when oxygenated and colorless when deoxygenated.  It's a large, complex protein with multiple subunits.


例句:

The researchers analyzed the oxyhaemocyanin levels in the blood of the giant squid. = 研究人员分析了大王乌贼血液中的血蓝蛋白水平。

The presence of oxyhaemocyanin indicates the animal's ability to efficiently transport oxygen. = 血蓝蛋白的存在表明动物具有高效运输氧气的能力。


近义词:hemocyanin (a broader term encompassing both oxygenated and deoxygenated forms)


反义词:None (there's no direct opposite, but deoxyhaemocyanin refers to the deoxygenated form)


词性:名词
