precontemporaneity:/ˌpriːkənˌtɛmpəˈreɪniəti/| n. |先于当代；先于同时代|The state or quality of existing or happening before the contemporary period;  anticipation of contemporary trends or ideas.  It often implies a surprising or uncanny foreshadowing of later developments.|例句：The surprising precontemporaneity of the novel's themes is striking.|近义词：precursor, anticipation, foreshadowing|反义词：contemporaneity, simultaneity
