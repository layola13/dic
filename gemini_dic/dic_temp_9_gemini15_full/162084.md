pre-combinations:/ˌpriːkɒmbɪˈneɪʃənz/ | n. | 预先组合；预先搭配 | Pre-combinations refer to combinations or arrangements that are prepared or determined in advance.  They are often used in contexts where elements are combined before a specific process or event. For example, in chemistry, pre-combinations might refer to the preparation of reactant mixtures before a reaction. In software development, it could refer to pre-built components or modules. | The chef prepared the pre-combinations of ingredients for the evening's dishes. |近义词：preparations, premixes |反义词：improvisations, ad-hoc combinations |名词
