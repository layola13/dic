They are produced by various immune cells, such as lymphocytes, macrophages, and fibroblasts, and play a vital role in regulating immune responses, inflammation, and hematopoiesis (blood cell formation).  Different interleukins have distinct functions and effects on different types of cells.  For example, some interleukins promote inflammation, while others suppress it.
