/kliː/ | adj. | 清晰的；干净的；明白的 | Clear means free from obscurity or ambiguity; easily understood or seen.  It can also refer to something that is free from dirt or obstruction. | The instructions were clear and easy to follow.  = 说明清晰易懂。

The lake water is clear and sparkling. = 湖水清澈透明。|  obvious, distinct, transparent | unclear, muddy, ambiguous, obscure | adjective


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sky is clear tonight. = 今晚天空晴朗。
The explanation is clear to everyone. = 解释对每个人都很清楚。


一般过去时:
The path was clear yesterday. = 昨天路很清晰。
The instructions were clear before the exam. = 考试前说明很清楚。


现在完成时:
The weather has been clear all week. = 这周天气一直晴朗。
The situation has become much clearer now. = 现在情况已经明朗多了。


过去完成时:
The road had been clear until the storm hit. = 直到暴风雨来临前道路一直都很清晰。
The evidence had been clear from the beginning. = 从一开始证据就很清晰。


将来时:
The sky will be clear tomorrow. = 明天天空将会晴朗。
The results will be clear after the test. = 测试后结果将很清楚。


虚拟语气:
If the instructions were clearer, I would have understood. = 如果说明更清楚，我就明白了。
I wish the water were clearer in the lake. = 我希望湖水更清澈。


被动语态:
The misunderstanding was cleared up quickly. = 误解很快被澄清了。
The path is being cleared of debris. = 道路正在清理杂物。


疑问句:
Is the message clear? = 信息清楚吗？
Is the path clear ahead? = 前方的路清晰吗？


否定句:
The instructions are not clear enough. = 说明不够清楚。
The water is not clear; it's muddy. = 水不干净，很浑浊。
