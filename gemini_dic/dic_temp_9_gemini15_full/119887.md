militarised:/ˈmɪlɪtəraɪzd/| adj.|军事化的；武装的|Made suitable for or adapted to military use; having a strong military presence or influence.|例句：The country has become increasingly militarised in recent years. 近年来，这个国家日益军事化。The border region is heavily militarised. 边境地区军事化严重。|近义词：armed, fortified, militaristic|反义词：demilitarized, pacifistic|

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government militarises the border. = 政府将边境军事化。
This region is heavily militarised. = 这个地区高度军事化。

一般过去时:
They militarised the island during the war. = 战争期间他们使该岛屿军事化。
The country was quickly militarised under the new regime. = 在新政权下，这个国家迅速军事化。

现在完成时:
The region has been militarised since the conflict began. = 自冲突开始以来，该地区已被军事化。
They have militarised their police force. = 他们已经将其警察部队军事化。

过去完成时:
The army had militarised the area before the invasion. = 军队在入侵之前已将该地区军事化。
The government had already militarised the port before the crisis. = 政府在危机发生之前就已经将港口军事化。

将来时:
They will militarize the airspace. = 他们将使领空军事化。
The government will further militarize the country's economy. = 政府将进一步使国家的经济军事化。

虚拟语气:
If they militarized the zone, there would be protests. = 如果他们将该区域军事化，将会发生抗议。
I wish they hadn't militarised the schools. = 我希望他们没有将学校军事化。

被动语态:
The city was militarised by the occupying forces. = 该城市被占领军军事化。
The border region is being militarised by the government. = 边境地区正在被政府军事化。

疑问句:
Has the country been militarised? = 这个国家已经被军事化了吗？
Why was the area militarised? = 为什么该地区被军事化？

否定句:
The government did not militarize the school. = 政府没有将学校军事化。
The island has not been fully militarised. = 该岛屿尚未完全军事化。
