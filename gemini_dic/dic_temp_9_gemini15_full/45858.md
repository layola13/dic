cut-off:/ˈkʌtɔːf/ | n., adj., v. |截止日期；中断；切断；脱离 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "cut-off" refers to a point in time after which something is no longer possible or accepted.  It can also refer to a physical separation or interruption, like a cut in a road or a power cut-off. As an adjective, it describes something that is separated or discontinued. As a verb, it means to interrupt or stop something abruptly.

例句:
The cut-off date for applications is December 31st.  截止申请日期是12月31日。

The sudden power cut-off caused widespread disruption.  突然的停电造成了大范围的混乱。

词源:
The word "cut-off" is a compound word formed from the verb "cut" and the preposition "off."  "Cut" originates from Old English *cuttan, and "off" from Old English of.  The meaning of "cut-off" developed naturally from the literal sense of physically cutting something off to the metaphorical sense of stopping or terminating something.
