isolationism:/ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪzəm/| n. |孤立主义；闭关自守|A policy of remaining apart from the affairs or interests of other groups, especially the political affairs of other countries.|例句：The country's isolationism prevented it from benefiting from international trade. 这个国家的孤立主义阻止了它从国际贸易中获益。

例句：Many believe that strict isolationism is no longer a viable option in today's interconnected world. 许多人认为，在当今互联互通的世界中，严格的孤立主义已不再是一种可行的选择。


近义词：seclusion, withdrawal, autarky|反义词：internationalism, globalism


词源:源自isolate (孤立) + -ism (主义)  The suffix "-ism" indicates a doctrine, belief, or practice.  The word evolved in the late 19th and early 20th centuries as a description of the foreign policies of certain nations.
