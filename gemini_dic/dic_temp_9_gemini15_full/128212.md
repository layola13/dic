neurobiotaxis:/ˌnʊroʊbaɪ.oʊˈtæksɪs/| n. |神经生物趋向性|The movement of a neuron or its axon towards or away from a specific chemical or electrical signal.  This process is crucial for the development and organization of the nervous system.  It describes the directional growth of nerve fibers guided by chemical or electrical cues in the surrounding environment. |Example:  Neurobiotaxis plays a critical role in the formation of neural circuits during embryonic development. 神经生物趋向性在胚胎发育过程中神经回路的形成中起着至关重要的作用。|近义词: chemotaxis, galvanotaxis |反义词:  (no direct antonym, but the opposite would be random growth or lack of directional guidance)|名词
