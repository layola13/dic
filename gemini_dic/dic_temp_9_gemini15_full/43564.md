crazy:/ˈkreɪzi/| adj.|疯狂的；狂热的；古怪的；不合理的 |  Crazy describes someone or something that is insane, wildly enthusiastic, or illogical. It can also refer to something extremely exciting or intense.  | 例句：He's crazy about cars. 他痴迷于汽车。  | 例句：That's a crazy idea!  那是个疯狂的想法！|近义词：insane, mad, wild, frantic,  eccentric,  unreasonable |反义词：sane, rational, reasonable, calm, sensible


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is crazy about music. = 他痴迷于音乐。
She is crazy for chocolate. = 她对巧克力很狂热。


一般过去时:
He was crazy when he heard the news. = 他听到这个消息时很疯狂。
They were crazy enough to try it. = 他们疯狂到足以尝试。


现在完成时:
I have been crazy busy lately. = 我最近忙得发疯。
She has gone crazy with worry. = 她担心得快要疯了。


过去完成时:
He had been crazy about her for years. = 他多年来一直对她痴迷。
They had gone crazy before the accident. = 事故发生前他们就已经疯了。


将来时:
I will go crazy if I don't get some sleep. = 如果我不睡一会儿，我会疯掉的。
He will be crazy excited to see her. = 他看到她会兴奋得发疯。


虚拟语气:
If I were crazy, I would do it. = 如果我疯了，我会这么做。
I wish I weren't so crazy busy. = 我希望我不那么忙。


被动语态:
(Not typically used in passive voice.  The adjective "crazy" doesn't usually take a passive form.)


疑问句:
Are you crazy? = 你疯了吗？
Is that crazy or what? = 那是不是很疯狂？


否定句:
He isn't crazy; he's just eccentric. = 他不疯，只是有点古怪。
That plan isn't crazy, it's actually quite clever. = 那个计划并不疯狂，实际上相当聪明。


PHRASE->
go crazy = 发疯
drive someone crazy = 把某人逼疯
crazy in love = 陷入热恋
like crazy = 疯狂地
crazy quilt = 百衲被
