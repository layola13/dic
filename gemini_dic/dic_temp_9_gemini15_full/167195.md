pretenceful: /prɪˈtɛnsfəl/ | adj. | 做作的；虚伪的；矫揉造作的 |  Characterized by pretense; affected; artificial.  It suggests an excessive or insincere display of something, often to impress others. |例句：Her pretenceful politeness grated on my nerves.  她的矫揉造作的礼貌让我很反感。

例句：His pretenceful display of wealth was easily seen through. 他虚张声势的炫富很容易被识破。


近义词：affected, artificial, insincere, ostentatious, showy, theatrical


反义词：genuine, sincere, authentic, natural, unpretentious


词源：源自pretence (n.)  "an act of pretending; a false show; a claim not supported by facts,"  from Old French *pretence, from Latin praetendere "to stretch forth, put forward," from prae- "before" + tendere "to stretch."  The suffix "-ful" adds the meaning "full of" or "characterized by."
