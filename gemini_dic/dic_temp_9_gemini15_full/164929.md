pregrading:/ˌpriːˈɡreɪdɪŋ/| n.|预评分，预先评级|The act of assigning grades or scores to something before a formal evaluation.  It is often used in education to provide preliminary feedback or in other contexts to give an initial assessment.|例句：The teacher did some pregrading of the essays to get a sense of the overall class performance. 教师对论文进行了预评分，以了解整个班级的整体表现。|例句：The judges conducted a pregrading of the competition entries to streamline the final judging process. 评委对参赛作品进行了预评分，以简化最终评审流程。|近义词：preliminary assessment, advance rating, initial grading |反义词：final grading, post-evaluation
