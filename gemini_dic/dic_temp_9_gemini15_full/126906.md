narcotics:/nɑːrˈkɒtɪks/ | n. | 麻醉剂；毒品 |  Substances that affect the central nervous system and can cause temporary changes in mood, perception, or behavior, often leading to addiction.  Many narcotics are controlled substances and their use is illegal in many countries.  | 例句：The sale and possession of narcotics are strictly prohibited.  贩卖和持有麻醉品是被严格禁止的。

The doctor prescribed narcotics to manage the patient's severe pain. 医生开了麻醉剂来控制病人的剧烈疼痛。


近义词：drugs, opiates, analgesics | 反义词：None (Antonyms are not readily applicable as it refers to a class of substances) | 词性:noun
