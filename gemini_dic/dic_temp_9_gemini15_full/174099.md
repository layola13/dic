quatrains:/ˈkwɒtrɛɪnz/| n. |四行诗；四行诗节|A quatrain is a stanza of four lines, usually with a specific rhyme scheme.  It's a common poetic form found in many languages and literary traditions.|Example:  The following is a Shakespearean quatrain:  
The curfew tolls the knell of parting day,
The lowing herd wind slowly o'er the lea,
The ploughman homeward plods his weary way,
And leaves the world to darkness and to me.|近义词：stanza, verse|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with longer poetic forms like sonnets or cantos.
