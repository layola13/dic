passerine:/ˈpæsərɪn/| n.|雀形目鸟类；雀形鸟|A passerine is a bird belonging to the order Passeriformes, which includes the majority of songbirds.  They are characterized by their perching feet (three toes pointing forward and one pointing backward), and often exhibit complex vocalizations. |Example:  Robins, sparrows, and blue jays are all examples of passerine birds.|近义词：songbird (often used informally)|反义词：None (it's a taxonomic category)|
