explosion:/ɪkˈsploʊʒən/| n. |爆炸；爆发；激增 |A sudden, violent burst of energy, often accompanied by a loud noise and the scattering of material.  It can refer to a physical event like a bomb detonating or a metaphorical event like a sudden increase in popularity. |例句：The explosion shattered the windows. 爆炸震碎了窗户。

The explosion of interest in AI is remarkable. 对人工智能的兴趣激增令人瞩目。

|近义词：burst, detonation, eruption, outburst |反义词：implosion, collapse


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bomb explodes. = 炸弹爆炸了。
An explosion occurs frequently in this area. = 这个地区经常发生爆炸。

一般过去时:
The volcano exploded last night. = 火山昨晚爆发了。
A huge explosion rocked the city. = 一场巨大的爆炸震动了这座城市。

现在完成时:
Several explosions have occurred in the past week. = 在过去的一周里发生了数起爆炸事件。
The popularity of the band has exploded. = 这个乐队的名气已经爆涨。

过去完成时:
The building had exploded before the firefighters arrived. = 消防员到达之前，大楼已经爆炸了。
The company's stock had exploded before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，公司的股票已经暴涨。

将来时:
The rocket will explode on impact. = 火箭撞击时将会爆炸。
The market is predicted to explode next year.  = 预计市场明年将爆发式增长。

虚拟语气:
If the pressure increased, the container might explode. = 如果压力增大，容器可能会爆炸。
I wish the news hadn't exploded like that. = 我真希望这个消息没有这样突然爆发。

被动语态:
The building was exploded by a bomb. = 这座建筑物被炸弹炸毁了。
The issue was exploded into the media. = 这个问题被媒体大肆报道。

疑问句:
What caused the explosion? = 爆炸是由什么引起的？
When did the explosion happen? = 爆炸是什么时候发生的？

否定句:
The bomb didn't explode. = 炸弹没有爆炸。
The party didn't explode as expected. = 派对并没有像预期那样热闹非凡。
