laryngismus:/læˌrɪŋˈɡɪzməs/| n.|喉痉挛，喉头痉挛|A spasm of the larynx (voice box), causing temporary obstruction of breathing, often seen in children.  It is characterized by stridor (a harsh, high-pitched sound during breathing) and can be a life-threatening emergency if severe.|例句：The child experienced a sudden attack of laryngismus, causing difficulty breathing.  孩子突然发生了喉痉挛，导致呼吸困难。|近义词：laryngospasm, laryngeal spasm|反义词：None specifically, but the absence of laryngismus could be described as normal breathing.
