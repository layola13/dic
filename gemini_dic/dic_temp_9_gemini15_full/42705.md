countertradition: /ˌkaʊntətrəˈdɪʃən/ | n. | 反传统；对抗传统 | A practice, belief, or artistic style that deliberately opposes or challenges established traditions.  It often involves a conscious rejection of conventional norms and values, seeking to create something new and different.  | Example:  The artist's work is a striking example of countertradition, rejecting the established norms of realism in favor of abstract expressionism.  The band's music is a countertradition to the mainstream pop music of the time. |近义词：opposition, rebellion, defiance |反义词：tradition, convention, orthodoxy | 词性：名词
