repriced:/ˌriːˈpraɪst/| vt.|重新定价|To set a new price for something, usually after a review of market conditions or costs.  This often involves adjusting the price upward or downward.|例句：The company repriced its products after a surge in raw material costs. 公司在原材料成本飙升后重新定价了其产品。
例句：After a period of low sales, the retailer repriced the item to boost demand. 销售低迷一段时间后，零售商对商品重新定价以刺激需求。|近义词：revalue, readjust, reset|反义词：maintain the price, keep the price unchanged|动词
