genghiskhan:/ˈdʒɛŋɡɪs ˈkɑːn/| n.|成吉思汗|Genghis Khan (also Chinggis Khan, Genghis Khan, etc.) was the founder and first Great Khan of the Mongol Empire, which became the largest contiguous land empire in history.  He came to power by uniting many of the nomadic tribes of Northeast Asia. His conquests spanned much of Eurasia, resulting in significant cultural and political changes. |The legacy of Genghis Khan is complex and debated; he is revered as a brilliant military strategist and unifier by some, while condemned as a brutal conqueror by others.|近义词：Chinggis Khan, Temüjin|反义词：N/A
