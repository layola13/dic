loculous:/ˈlɒkjʊləs/ | n. |小室；小腔；小格|A loculus is a small cavity or compartment, often within a larger structure.  For example, in botany, it refers to a small chamber within a fruit or other plant structure. In anatomy, it might describe a small cavity or compartment within an organ.  |The seeds were neatly arranged in separate loculi within the fruit.  种籽整齐地排列在果实内的各个小室中。|compartment, cell, chamber|  (Near antonyms depend heavily on context and require specifying the larger structure the loculus is part of.)
