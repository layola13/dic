recognizances:/ˈrekəɡnɪzənsɪz/ | n. | 保释；保证；认领 | A recognizance is a formal undertaking, usually entered into before a court, that a person will do or refrain from doing a particular act. It typically involves a promise to appear in court or to fulfill some other obligation, and may be secured by a bond or other form of security.  | Example: He was released on his own recognizance. 他被无保释放。|近义词: bail, bond, surety | 反义词: imprisonment, detention

词源:
The word "recognizance" comes from the Anglo-French word "reconissance," meaning "acknowledgment" or "recognition."  It reflects the act of acknowledging a legal obligation.  The suffix "-ance" indicates a state or process.
