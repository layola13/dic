convinced:/kənˈvɪnst/ | adj. v. |确信的；使确信 |

英文解析:  The adjective "convinced" means firmly persuaded or made certain; having no doubt.  The verb "convince" means to persuade someone to believe something or to do something.


例句(adj.):

The jury was convinced of his guilt. = 陪审团确信他有罪。

I'm convinced that she's telling the truth. = 我确信她在说实话。


例句(v.):

He convinced me to change my mind. = 他说服我改变主意。

Nothing could convince her to leave. = 什么也无法说服她离开。


近义词(adj.): certain, sure, persuaded, satisfied, assured

近义词(v.): persuade, satisfy, assure, sway

反义词(adj.): unconvinced, doubtful, uncertain, dubious

反义词(v.): dissuade


词性: adjective, verb


词源:  From Old French *convincre, from Latin convincere "to overcome, convict," from com- "together" + vincere "to conquer."  The sense shifted from "overcome" to "persuade" in the Middle Ages.
