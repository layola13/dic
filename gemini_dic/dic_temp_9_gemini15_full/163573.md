precriticizes: /ˌpriːkrɪˈtaɪsɪz/ | vt. | 预先批评；事先批评 | To criticize beforehand or in anticipation.  This word is not commonly used and is considered somewhat archaic or informal. It implies a criticism made before something has happened, often based on prediction or expectation. | 例句：The committee precriticized the proposal before it was even presented. = 委员会在提案提交之前就对其进行了预先批评。


The critics precriticized the play, claiming it would be a flop before opening night. = 批评家们在首映夜之前就预先批评了这部戏，声称它会失败。 | 近义词：prejudge, anticipate criticism of, forewarn about flaws | 反义词：praise beforehand, endorse in advance | 动词
