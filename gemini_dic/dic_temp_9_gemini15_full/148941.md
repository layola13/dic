overwrestle: /ˌoʊvərˈrɛsəl/ | v. | 纠缠不清；过度较劲；过度斗争 | To wrestle excessively or with undue persistence.  It implies a struggle that goes beyond what is necessary or productive.  | 例句：They overwrestled the details of the contract, delaying the signing. = 他们对合同细节纠缠不清，延误了签字。  They overwrestled the point, making it less clear. = 他们对这一点过度较劲，反而使其变得模糊不清。 | 近义词：struggle excessively, contend excessively, fight needlessly | 反义词：compromise, concede, cooperate

词源:
The word is a combination of "over-" (meaning excessively or too much) and "wrestle" (meaning to struggle or contend).  It's a relatively recent formation, not found in older dictionaries, illustrating the dynamic nature of language creating new words to describe nuanced situations.
