doggednesses:/ˈdɒɡɪdnəsɪz/ | n. | 顽强；固执；坚定不移 | The doggednesses of the mountaineers allowed them to conquer the treacherous peak.  登山者的顽强让他们征服了险峻的山峰。|近义词：perseverance, tenacity, persistence |反义词：give-up, surrender, quit |名词


英文解析:Doggedness refers to the quality of being stubbornly determined and persistent, often in the face of difficulties. It implies a refusal to give up, even when faced with setbacks or obstacles.  The plural form, "doggednesses," emphasizes the multiple instances or aspects of this stubborn perseverance.


例句：

His doggednesses in the face of adversity were truly remarkable. 他面对逆境时的顽强精神令人惊叹。

The project's success was due in large part to the team's combined doggednesses.  项目的成功很大程度上归功于团队共同的坚持不懈。


词源：

Derived from the adjective "dogged," meaning stubbornly determined.  "Dogged" itself has roots in the Old English word "dogga," meaning "dog."  The connection lies in the image of a dog's tenacious grip or unwavering pursuit of its target.  The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective into a noun, signifying the quality or state of being dogged.
