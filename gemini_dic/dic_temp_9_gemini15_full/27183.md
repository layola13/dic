calling:/ˈkɔːlɪŋ/ | /ˈkɑːlɪŋ/ | n. v. | 呼叫；职业；召唤 |  The act of calling; a vocation; a strong urge or inclination. |例句：I received a calling from my mother. = 我接到了我母亲的电话。|例句：Teaching is a noble calling. = 教书育人是高尚的职业。|近义词：vocation, profession, occupation, summons |反义词：silence, ignoring


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He calls me every day. = 他每天都打电话给我。
She calls her mother frequently. = 她经常打电话给她母亲。

一般过去时:
They called the police last night. = 他们昨晚报了警。
He called her name from across the street. = 他隔着街道喊她的名字。

现在完成时:
I have called him three times already. = 我已经给他打过三次电话了。
She has called the doctor about her headache. = 她因为头痛已经给医生打过电话了。

过去完成时:
I had called before he arrived. = 他到达之前我已经打过电话了。
They had called the restaurant to make a reservation. = 他们已经打电话给餐厅预订了。

将来时:
I will call you later. = 我待会儿给你打电话。
She will call him when she gets home. = 她回家后会给他打电话。

虚拟语气:
If I called, would they answer? = 如果我打电话，他们会接吗？
I wish I had called sooner. = 我真希望我早点打电话。

被动语态:
The meeting was called to order. = 会议开始。
The ambulance was called immediately. = 救护车立即被叫来了。

疑问句:
Are you calling me? = 你在叫我吗？
Who called just now? = 刚才是谁打电话？

否定句:
I didn't call anyone. = 我没有给任何人打电话。
She doesn't call often. = 她不经常打电话。
