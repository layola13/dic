gastropods:/ˈɡæstrəpɒdz/ | n. |腹足类动物| Gastropods are a large group of mollusks that typically have a single, spiraled shell, a muscular foot for locomotion, and a radula for scraping food. They include snails, slugs, and limpets.  |Example:  Snails and slugs are common examples of gastropods.  蜗牛和蛞蝓是腹足类动物的常见例子。

The giant African land snail is a large gastropod. 巨大的非洲陆地蜗牛是一种大型的腹足类动物。|近义词:mollusk (more general term)|反义词:None (no direct antonym)|

Etymology:

The word "gastropod" comes from the Greek words  "gaster" (γάστρ), meaning "belly" or "stomach," and "pous" (πούς), meaning "foot." This refers to the characteristic of gastropods using their large, muscular foot for locomotion, which is essentially their belly.
