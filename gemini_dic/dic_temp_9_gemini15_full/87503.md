heterochrony:/hɛtərˈkrɒniː/| n. |异时性，异时发育|The evolutionary change in the timing or rate of developmental events.  Heterochrony can lead to significant changes in the morphology of an organism. For example, paedomorphosis is a type of heterochrony where juvenile features are retained in the adult form.|Example:  The elongated necks of giraffes are an example of heterochrony, resulting from an extended period of cervical vertebral growth.|近义词：developmental shift, evolutionary timing change|反义词：isochrony (same timing)|名词
