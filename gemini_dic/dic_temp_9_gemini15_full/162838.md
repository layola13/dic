precapturing:/ˌpriːˈkæptʃərɪŋ/ | v. | 预先捕获；预先捕捉 |  The act of capturing something before it is fully formed or realized; anticipating and addressing potential problems or opportunities before they arise. This term is primarily used in technical contexts, particularly in computer science and software engineering, referring to the process of handling data or events before they are officially processed by the main system.  | Example: The system's precapturing mechanism prevented data loss during the network outage.  系统预先捕获机制防止了网络中断期间的数据丢失。 |近义词：anticipating, preempting |反义词：post-processing, reacting
