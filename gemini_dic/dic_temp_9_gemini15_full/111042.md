lisper:/ˈlɪspər/ | n. | 说话有口齿不清的人（尤其指说话时将s和z发成th）| A person who speaks with a lisp, especially one who habitually substitutes th-sounds for s- and z-sounds.  | Example:  He's a lisper; he pronounces "sun" as "thun." 他说话有点大舌头；他把“sun”发成“thun”。 |近义词:  stammerer, stutterer (though these refer to different speech impediments) |反义词:  (no direct antonym, but one could say "person with clear speech") |名词

He lisps when he speaks. = 他说话时有点大舌头。
She is a lisper, but it doesn't affect her communication. = 她说话有点大舌头，但这并不影响她的沟通。

现在完成时:

否定句:

PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "lisper".  The word is primarily used descriptively.  Phrases would instead describe the action of lisping or the person's condition, for example:

to have a lisp  = 有点大舌头
a slight lisp = 轻微的大舌头
a noticeable lisp = 明显的大舌头
to correct a lisp = 纠正大舌头
suffering from a lisp = 患有说话大舌头的毛病
