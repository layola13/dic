counter-myth: /ˈkaʊntərˌmɪθ/ | n. | 反神话；对神话的驳斥 | A counter-myth is a narrative or belief that directly contradicts or challenges a widely held myth or misconception.  It often aims to correct inaccuracies or biases present in the original myth. | Example:  The idea that all scientists are men is a myth; the counter-myth highlights the significant contributions of women in science.  | 近义词：rebuttal, refutation, counter-narrative | 反义词：myth, legend, fallacy


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb or adjective forms in common usage.  Therefore, grammatical tenses and voices are not applicable in the same way as for verbs.  However, we can show how it functions in different sentence structures.


In a declarative sentence:
The counter-myth to the idea of inherent female inferiority is the vast record of female accomplishment throughout history. =  关于女性天生劣势的观点，其反驳是贯穿历史的女性成就的巨大记录。


In an interrogative sentence:
Is there a strong counter-myth to the pervasive belief in the effectiveness of this treatment? =  对于这种疗法有效性的普遍信念，是否存在强有力的反驳？


In an exclamatory sentence:
What a powerful counter-myth to the prevailing narrative of victimhood! =  多么有力的反驳了盛行的受害者叙事！


In a complex sentence:
While the original myth persisted for centuries, a compelling counter-myth gradually emerged, challenging its core tenets. =  虽然最初的神话延续了几个世纪，但一个令人信服的反神话逐渐出现，挑战了其核心原则。
