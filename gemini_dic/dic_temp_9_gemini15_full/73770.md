formalises: /ˈfɔːrməlaɪzɪz/ | v. | 使正式化；使制度化 | To give a definite and official form to (something); to make something official or systematic. | 例句：The company formalized its procedures.  公司使其程序正规化了。 | 例句：They formalized their agreement with a contract. 他们用合同正式确定了他们的协议。 | 近义词：systematize, codify, regulate, standardize | 反义词：informalize, casualize


USAGE->

The government formalises new laws every year. = 政府每年都会使新的法律正式化。
She formalises her thoughts before writing. = 她写作前会使自己的想法正式化。

一般过去时:
He formalized his proposal yesterday. = 他昨天正式提出了他的建议。

过去完成时:

词源:
formalise源于法语"formaliser",  最终源于拉丁语"forma" (形式，形状)。  "-ise"是英语动词后缀，表示“使…化”。
