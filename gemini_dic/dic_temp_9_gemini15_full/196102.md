sepsis:/ˈsɛpsɪs/| n. |败血症；脓毒症 |A serious condition caused by the body's overwhelming response to an infection. It can lead to organ failure and death if not treated promptly. Sepsis is characterized by a widespread inflammatory response, often involving a drop in blood pressure, rapid heart rate, and fever.  | Example: The patient was rushed to the hospital suffering from severe sepsis.  病人因严重的败血症被紧急送往医院。|近义词：blood poisoning, septicemia |反义词：health, wellness
