casting-out: /ˈkæstiŋ aʊt/ | v. |逐出；驱逐；抛弃 | To expel or drive out from a place, especially forcibly; to discard or reject something.  |例句：The evil spirit was casting out from the possessed man.  邪灵被从着魔的人身上逐出。

The villagers were casting out the stranger from their community. 村民们把陌生人逐出了他们的社区。


近义词：expelling, ejecting, banishing, discarding, rejecting |反义词：welcoming, accepting, including, retaining


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They are casting out the old furniture. = 他们在扔掉旧家具。
The company is casting out underperforming employees. = 公司正在解雇表现不佳的员工。


一般过去时:
She cast out her old clothes. = 她扔掉了旧衣服。
The king cast out his enemies from the kingdom. = 国王把他的敌人逐出了王国。


现在完成时:
They have cast out all the demons. = 他们已经驱逐了所有恶魔。
The church has cast out many members for misconduct. = 教堂因行为不端而开除了许多成员。


过去完成时:
He had cast out his doubts before making the decision. = 他在做决定之前已经打消了疑虑。
The witch had cast out the curse before the sun rose. = 女巫在太阳升起之前解除了诅咒。


将来时:
We will cast out the rubbish tomorrow. = 我们明天会扔垃圾。
The government will cast out the corrupt officials. = 政府将清除腐败官员。


虚拟语气:

I wish they had cast out the rumors earlier. = 我希望他们早点消除谣言。


被动语态:

The evil spirits were cast out by the priest. = 邪灵被牧师驱逐了。


疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->

词源:

The verb "cast out" originates from the Old English verb *cēastān "to throw," related to the Old High German *geizzen, and the Old Norse *kasta.  The "out" is a preposition indicating direction.  The meaning evolved from a literal throwing to a figurative expulsion or rejection.
