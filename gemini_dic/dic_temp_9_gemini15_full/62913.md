enlivened:/ɪnˈlaɪvənd/ | adj. | 使活跃的；使生动的；使振奋的 | Made more lively or interesting; invigorated.  | The party was enlivened by the arrival of the band. 晚会因为乐队的到来而变得热闹起来。 | 近义词：animated, invigorated, brightened, spirited | 反义词：dull, lifeless, boring, subdued | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The music always enlivens the atmosphere. = 音乐总是活跃气氛。
Her presence enlivens every meeting. = 她的出现使每次会议都充满活力。


一般过去时:
The unexpected news enlivened the otherwise dull meeting. = 这个意外的消息使原本沉闷的会议变得活跃起来。
The comedian's jokes enlivened the audience. = 喜剧演员的笑话逗乐了观众。


现在完成时:
The new decorations have enlivened the room. = 新的装饰使房间生机勃勃。
The addition of fresh herbs has enlivened the dish. = 添加新鲜香草使这道菜更加美味可口。


过去完成时:
He had enlivened the conversation with his witty remarks before the argument started. = 在争吵开始之前，他用他机智的言辞活跃了谈话气氛。
The story had enlivened the children's imaginations before they fell asleep. = 在孩子们睡着之前，这个故事激发了他们的想象力。


将来时:
The fireworks display will enliven the night sky. = 烟火表演将点亮夜空。
The upcoming festival will enliven the town. = 即将来临的节日将使小镇充满活力。


虚拟语气:
If the music were louder, it would enliven the party. = 如果音乐再响亮一些，它会使派对更热闹。
I wish the speaker had enlivened his presentation. = 我希望演讲者能使他的演讲更生动一些。


被动语态:
The party was enlivened by the unexpected guests. = 晚会因为意外到来的客人而变得热闹起来。

疑问句:

否定句:
The boring lecture did not enliven the students. = 枯燥的讲座并没有使学生们兴奋起来。
