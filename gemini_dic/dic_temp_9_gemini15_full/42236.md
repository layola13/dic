countabilities:/ˌkaʊntəˈbɪlətiz/ | n. | 可数性；可数名词的属性 | The grammatical property of nouns that allows them to be counted as singular or plural.  This property determines whether a noun can be used with singular or plural determiners (a, an, the, this, that, these, those, etc.) and verb forms. | Example:  The countabilities of "cat" and "water" differ significantly.  "Cat" is countable, while "water" is uncountable. | 近义词：countableness | 反义词：uncountability
