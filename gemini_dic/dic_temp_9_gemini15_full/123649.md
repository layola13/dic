monumentalisms:/ˌmɑːnjuˈmɛntəlɪzəm/| n.|纪念碑主义；纪念性建筑风格|The term "monumentalism" refers to a style of architecture and art characterized by large-scale, imposing structures and a sense of grandeur and permanence.  It often conveys a message of power, authority, or national identity.  The specific meanings and connotations can vary depending on context and historical period.|The ancient Roman Colosseum is a prime example of monumentalism, showcasing impressive scale and engineering. The massive pyramids of Egypt also represent monumentalism, reflecting the power of the pharaohs and their belief in the afterlife. |grandiosity, grandeur, magnificence|minimalism, simplicity|名词
