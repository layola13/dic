chopping:/ˈtʃɒpɪŋ/|/ˈtʃɑːpɪŋ/| v. |剁，砍，切碎|The act of cutting something into pieces, typically with repeated blows of a sharp instrument.  It can also refer to the sound of this action.|例句：He was chopping wood in the backyard. 他在后院砍柴。

例句：The chef is chopping vegetables for the soup. 厨师正在为汤切菜。


近义词：mincing, dicing, slicing, hacking |反义词：joining, uniting, combining


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He chops vegetables every day. = 他每天都切菜。
She chops the onions finely. = 她把洋葱切得很细。

一般过去时:
He chopped down the old tree. = 他砍倒了那棵老树。
They chopped the meat into small pieces. = 他们把肉切成小块。

现在完成时:
I have chopped all the vegetables for the stew. = 我已经把炖肉的所有蔬菜都切好了。
She has chopped enough wood for the fire. = 她已经砍够了生火的柴火。

过去完成时:
He had chopped the wood before the fire went out. = 在火熄灭之前他已经砍好了柴火。
They had chopped all the ingredients before starting to cook. = 他们在开始做饭之前已经把所有的配料都切好了。

将来时:
I will chop the carrots tomorrow. = 我明天会切胡萝卜。
She will chop the herbs before adding them to the dish. = 她会在把香草加到菜里之前把它们切碎。

虚拟语气:
If I had a sharp knife, I would chop the meat more easily. = 如果我有一把锋利的刀，我就能更容易地切肉了。
I wish I had chopped the vegetables earlier. = 我真希望我早点把蔬菜切好。

被动语态:
The vegetables were chopped finely. = 蔬菜被切得很细。
The wood was chopped by the lumberjack. = 木材被伐木工砍倒了。

疑问句:
Are you chopping onions? = 你在切洋葱吗？
Did he chop the tree down? = 他把树砍倒了吗？

否定句:

She doesn't chop vegetables very well. = 她不太会切菜。
