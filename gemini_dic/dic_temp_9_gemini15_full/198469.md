shrift:/ʃrɪft/| n.|宽恕；忏悔；责备；训斥|The act of giving absolution or forgiveness;  also, a severe reprimand or rebuke.|例句：He begged for shrift from the angry mob. 他向愤怒的暴民乞求宽恕。|例句：The teacher gave the student a severe shrift for cheating.老师严厉斥责学生作弊。|近义词：forgiveness, pardon, reprimand, rebuke |反义词：condemnation, punishment


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He seeks shrift for his sins. = 他寻求宽恕他的罪过。
The priest gives shrift to the penitent. = 牧师赦免忏悔者。


一般过去时:
The king granted shrift to the prisoner. = 国王赦免了囚犯。
She received a harsh shrift for her insolence. = 她因为她的无礼而受到了严厉的训斥。


现在完成时:
He has received shrift for his past mistakes. = 他已获得过去错误的宽恕。
The court has yet to give shrift to the defendant's pleas. = 法院尚未对被告的请求做出裁决。


过去完成时:
The judge had already given shrift to the criminal before the evidence was presented. = 在证据提交之前，法官已经对罪犯做出了判决。
She had sought shrift from God for her sins. = 她曾向上帝祈求宽恕她的罪过。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
Shrift was given to the repentant sinner. = 悔过自新的罪人得到了宽恕。
The criminal's pleas were given little shrift. = 罪犯的请求几乎没有得到理会。


疑问句:
Will he be given shrift? = 他会得到宽恕吗？
What shrift did she receive? = 她受到了什么责备？


否定句:
He was given no shrift by the judge. = 法官没有宽恕他。
The arrogant man received no shrift for his apologies. = 那个傲慢的人的道歉没有得到任何原谅。


PHRASE->
ask for shrift = 请求宽恕
give shrift to = 赦免；责备
receive shrift = 得到宽恕/责备
short shrift = 粗暴的拒绝；严厉的斥责
make short shrift of = 快速处理；迅速解决


词源:
Old English *scrīft, meaning "confession," "penance," or "absolution," from the verb *scrīfan, "to write," "to confess."  The modern meaning evolved from the religious context of confession and absolution to include the broader sense of judgment, reprimand, and the result thereof.
