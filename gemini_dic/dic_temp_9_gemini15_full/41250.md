corbeled:/kɔːrˈbɛld/| adj.| 迭层砌筑的；挑出的 |  Describes a structure, typically a wall or arch, built with stones or bricks projecting progressively inward from each layer, creating a corbel.  This technique allows for the construction of overhanging structures without the use of centering or formwork.  | Example: The ancient castle walls featured impressive corbeled construction.  古代城堡的城墙采用了令人印象深刻的迭层砌筑结构。 |近义词： projecting, cantilevered | 反义词： plumb, vertical
