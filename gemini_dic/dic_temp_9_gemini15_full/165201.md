/ˌpriːɪnˈhɛrɪtənsɪz/ | n. | 预先继承；先祖遗产 |  A preinheritance refers to property or assets that are expected to be inherited in the future, but haven't been received yet.  It encompasses the anticipated inheritance, including the potential benefits and liabilities associated with it.  The term highlights the planning and consideration individuals might give to future inheritances, even before they are legally entitled to them. | The family lawyer advised them to plan for their preinheritances, considering potential taxes and estate management. = 家庭律师建议他们规划好未来的继承财产，考虑到潜在的税收和遗产管理问题。  He began investing with an eye towards managing his preinheritances effectively. = 他开始投资，着眼于有效管理他未来的遗产。|  anticipated inheritance, prospective inheritance |  no direct antonym, but the opposite concept could be "no anticipated inheritance" | 名词
