semiclinical:/ˌsɛmɪˈklɪnɪkəl/| adj.| 半临床的；介于临床和非临床之间的 |  Relating to or characteristic of a situation or study that is partly clinical (involving patients) and partly non-clinical (e.g., laboratory-based research).  It often refers to studies or trials that bridge the gap between laboratory research and full-scale clinical trials.  The term implies a level of realism beyond purely laboratory settings, but not yet the full complexity and ethical considerations of a true clinical trial involving human subjects. |  The researchers conducted a semiclinical study to test the efficacy of the new drug before moving to human trials. = 研究人员在进行人体试验之前进行了一项半临床研究，以测试这种新药的疗效。  A semiclinical trial showed promising results, paving the way for a full clinical trial. = 半临床试验显示出令人鼓舞的结果，为全面临床试验铺平了道路。| 近义词：preclinical, quasi-clinical | 反义词：clinical, fully clinical
