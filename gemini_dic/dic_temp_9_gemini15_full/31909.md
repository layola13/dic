cheesy:/ˈtʃiːzi/| adj.|俗气的，矫揉造作的，劣质的，奶酪味的|  Something cheesy is of poor quality, or is exaggerated and sentimental in a way that is not sincere.  It can also refer to the taste or smell of cheese.|例句：That movie was so cheesy!  那部电影太俗气了！

例句：The romance novel had a cheesy plot. 这本言情小说情节很俗套。


近义词：tacky, corny, sentimental, artificial, insincere|反义词：sophisticated, refined, genuine, authentic

词源:  The word "cheesy" originates from the word "cheese," and its figurative meaning evolved from the idea that something overly rich, overly sweet, or overly sentimental, like some types of cheese, can be perceived as cloying or excessive, hence "cheesy."
