flashs:/flæʃz/ | n. |闪光；闪现；短暂的景象 | A sudden, brief burst of bright light.  It can also refer to a sudden, brief experience or event. |例句：The camera flashes blinded me. 闪光灯闪瞎了我的眼睛。|例句：He had a flash of inspiration. 他灵光一闪。|近义词：glimmer, gleam, flicker |反义词：darkness, dullness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The camera flashes brightly. = 相机闪光很亮。
Lightning flashes across the sky. = 雷电划破天空。

一般过去时:
The lightning flashed and thunder roared. = 闪电划过，雷声轰鸣。
The screen flashed a warning message. = 屏幕闪现了一个警告信息。

现在完成时:
The firefly has flashed several times. = 萤火虫已经闪了几次了。
The news has flashed across the world. = 这个消息已经传遍了全世界。

过去完成时:
The light had flashed before the explosion. = 爆炸前灯闪了一下。
He had flashed a warning sign before the accident. = 事故发生前他已经发出警告信号了。

将来时:
The camera will flash when you press the button. = 按下按钮相机就会闪光。
The sign will flash intermittently. = 信号灯会间歇性闪烁。

虚拟语气:
If the light flashed, I would know it was a signal. = 如果灯闪了，我就知道那是信号。
If only the idea had flashed to me earlier! = 要是我早点想到这个主意就好了！

被动语态:
The photograph was flashed using a powerful strobe. = 照片是用强力闪光灯拍摄的。
The warning was flashed on the screen. = 警告信息在屏幕上闪烁。

疑问句:
Did the light flash? = 灯闪了吗？
Has the camera flashed yet? = 相机闪光了吗？

否定句:
The light didn't flash. = 灯没有闪。
The camera didn't flash because the batteries were dead. = 相机没有闪光，因为电池没电了。
