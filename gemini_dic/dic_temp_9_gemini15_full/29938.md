cave-in:/ˈkeɪvɪn/| n. v.|坍塌；塌陷；让步；屈服|The sudden collapse of a structure, typically underground.  A giving way under pressure. |例句：The mine suffered a cave-in, trapping several miners.  矿井发生了坍塌，几名矿工被困。

The roof caved in during the earthquake. 地震时屋顶塌了。


近义词：collapse, subsidence, downfall, surrender, yield|反义词：erection, construction, resistance, defiance|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The roof caves in during heavy snow.  大雪期间屋顶会坍塌。
The government rarely caves in to pressure from protesters. 政府很少屈服于抗议者的压力。


一般过去时:
The tunnel caved in after the explosion. 爆炸后隧道坍塌了。
He finally caved in and admitted he was wrong. 他最终屈服并承认自己错了。


现在完成时:
Several mines have caved in this year due to heavy rainfall.  今年由于强降雨，几座矿井已经坍塌。
She has never caved in to peer pressure. 她从未屈服于同伴压力。


过去完成时:
The bridge had caved in before the rescue team arrived.  救援队到达之前桥梁已经坍塌了。
He had caved in to his boss's demands before the meeting. 会议前他已经屈服于老板的要求。


将来时:
If the ground continues to shift, the building will cave in. 如果地基继续移动，建筑物将会坍塌。
I think he will cave in eventually. 我认为他最终会屈服。


虚拟语气:
If the supports had been stronger, the ceiling wouldn't have caved in. 如果支撑更坚固，天花板就不会塌陷。
I wish I hadn't caved in to his demands. 我真希望我没有屈服于他的要求。


被动语态:
The road was caved in by the flood. 道路被洪水冲垮了。
His arguments were easily caved in by the opponent. 他的论点很容易被对手驳倒。


疑问句:
Did the building cave in during the storm?  暴风雨期间建筑物是否坍塌了？
Will they cave in to the public's demands? 他们会屈服于公众的要求吗？


否定句:
The structure didn't cave in despite the heavy load.  尽管负荷很重，结构却没有坍塌。
He didn't cave in to the pressure, despite the threats.  尽管受到威胁，他也没有屈服于压力。


PHRASE->

词源:
From "cave" (a hollow place in the earth) + "-in" (a suffix indicating a process or action).  字面意思为“洞穴进入”，引申为“坍塌”。
