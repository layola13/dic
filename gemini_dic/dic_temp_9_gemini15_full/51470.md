detour:/ˈdiːtʊr/ | n. |绕路，弯路 | A roundabout or indirect way.  A detour is a deviation from a direct course or plan. |例句：We had to take a detour because of the road closure.  我们因为道路封闭不得不绕路。

例句：The unexpected traffic jam forced us to take a detour.  意想不到的交通堵塞迫使我们绕路行驶。


近义词：deviation, diversion, roundabout, circuitous route | 反义词：direct route, straight path | 词性：名词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The road is closed, so we take a detour. = 道路封闭，所以我们绕路走。
A detour often adds time to your journey. = 绕路通常会增加你的行程时间。


一般过去时:
We took a detour to avoid the accident. = 我们绕路以避开事故。
They made a detour to visit their friends. = 他们绕路去拜访朋友。


现在完成时:
We have taken many detours on this trip. = 在这次旅行中，我们已经绕了很多路。
She has avoided the main road by taking a detour. = 她通过绕路避开了主干道。


过去完成时:
We had already taken a detour before the storm hit. = 在暴风雨来临之前，我们已经绕路了。
They had planned a detour to see the sights. = 他们已经计划好绕路去观光。


将来时:
We will take a detour if the bridge is closed. = 如果桥梁封闭，我们将绕路。
They will likely need to take a detour due to construction. = 由于施工，他们很可能需要绕路。


虚拟语气:
If we had known about the road closure, we would have taken a detour. = 如果我们知道道路封闭，我们就绕路了。
I wish we had taken a detour; the traffic was terrible. = 我真希望我们绕路了；交通太糟糕了。


被动语态:
A detour was suggested by the GPS. = GPS 建议绕路。
The detour was necessary due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，绕路是必要的。


疑问句:
Is there a detour around the construction? = 施工路段有绕行路线吗？
Should we take a detour to avoid the traffic? = 我们应该绕路避开交通拥堵吗？


否定句:
We didn't take a detour; we went straight through. = 我们没有绕路；我们直接过去了。
They chose not to take the suggested detour. = 他们选择不走建议的绕路。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "detour" comes from the Old French word "detourner," meaning "to turn aside" or "to divert."  It entered English in the 19th century.
