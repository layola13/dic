radicalise: /ˈrædɪkəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使激进化；使极端化 | To cause (someone) to adopt radical or extreme political or social views.  | 例句：The internet has been accused of radicalising young people.  互联网被指责使年轻人激进化。 | 例句：His experiences radicalised him, turning him into a revolutionary. 他的经历使他激进化，变成了一名革命者。| 近义词：extremize, incite, inflame | 反义词：moderate, calm, pacify | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government's policies radicalise the public. = 政府的政策使公众激进化。
Social media platforms often radicalise users. = 社交媒体平台经常使用户激进化。


一般过去时:
The event radicalised the movement. = 这件事使该运动激进化了。
His imprisonment radicalised his views. = 他被监禁使他的观点激进化了。


现在完成时:
Online extremism has radicalised many young people. = 网络极端主义已经使许多年轻人激进化了。
The ongoing conflict has radicalised the population. = 持续的冲突已经使民众激进化了。


过去完成时:
The leader had already radicalised his followers before the rebellion. = 在叛乱之前，领导人已经使他的追随者激进化了。
Years of oppression had radicalised the community. = 多年的压迫已经使这个社区激进化了。


将来时:

The rising inequality will radicalise the masses. = 不断加剧的不平等将会使大众激进化。


虚拟语气:
If the government hadn't acted, it would have radicalised the entire nation. = 如果政府不采取行动，它将会使整个国家激进化。
I wish the media wouldn't radicalise the debate. = 我希望媒体不要使这场辩论激进化。


被动语态:

The population was radicalised by years of hardship. = 人们由于多年的苦难而激进化了。


疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
From radical + -ise.  The suffix "-ise" (or "-ize") is a verb-forming suffix of Greek origin, meaning "to make" or "to become."  "Radical" itself derives from the Latin word "radicalis," meaning "of or pertaining to the root," eventually evolving to mean fundamental or extreme.
