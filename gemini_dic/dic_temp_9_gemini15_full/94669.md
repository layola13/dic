imprimatur:/ɪmˈprɪmætər/| n. |许可；批准；认可|The official license to publish a book or other work, especially one that has been censored or requires authorization.|The book finally received the imprimatur of the Church. 这本书最终获得了教会的许可。|近义词：authorization, sanction, approval|反义词：prohibition, ban, rejection|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The board gives its imprimatur to the project. = 董事会批准了该项目。
His work receives the imprimatur of experts. = 他的作品得到了专家的认可。


一般过去时:
The church gave its imprimatur to the translation. = 教会批准了该译本。
The censors gave their imprimatur to the film. = 审查员批准了这部电影。


现在完成时:
The novel has received the imprimatur of several critics. = 这部小说已经获得了几位评论家的认可。
The plan has received the imprimatur of the CEO. = 该计划已经得到了首席执行官的批准。


过去完成时:
The manuscript had already received the imprimatur before it was published. = 手稿在出版之前就已经获得了许可。
The project had received the imprimatur of the committee before it began. = 该项目在启动之前就已经得到了委员会的批准。


将来时:
The committee will give its imprimatur to the proposal next week. = 委员会下周将批准该提案。
The university will give its imprimatur to the research. = 大学将批准这项研究。


虚拟语气:
If the book received the imprimatur, it would be widely published. = 如果这本书获得许可，它将被广泛出版。
I wish my work had received the imprimatur of a famous author. = 我希望我的作品能得到一位著名作家的认可。


被动语态:
The document was given the imprimatur by the authorities. = 该文件得到了当局的批准。
The proposal is being given the imprimatur by the board. = 该提案正在得到董事会的批准。


疑问句:

Will the project receive the imprimatur in time? = 该项目能够及时获得批准吗？


否定句:
The book did not receive the imprimatur of the government. = 这本书没有得到政府的批准。
The plan did not receive the imprimatur of the stakeholders. = 该计划没有得到利益相关者的批准。
