olefiant:/ˈoʊlifiənt/| adj.|油状的，油性的；易燃的|From the Latin *oleum* "oil" + *facere* "to make".  It refers to a substance that produces oil or is oily in nature. Historically, it was specifically used to describe ethylene, due to its flammable nature and the oily residue produced upon combustion.  The term is now largely obsolete in modern chemical nomenclature, replaced by more precise terms. |The olefiant gas burned with a bright flame. =易燃气体燃烧着明亮的火焰。| flammable, inflammable | non-flammable, incombustible |形容词
