protests:/ˈproʊtests/| n. v.|抗议，反对|The act of expressing strong disapproval and opposition, typically in a public and organized way.|例句：The students staged a protest against the tuition fee increase. 学生们组织了一场反对学费上涨的抗议活动。|近义词：demonstrations, objections, complaints|反义词：acceptance, approval


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They protest the government's policies. = 他们抗议政府的政策。
People often protest against injustice. = 人们经常抗议不公正现象。


一般过去时:
The workers protested against low wages. = 工人们抗议低工资。
She protested her innocence. = 她为自己辩白，表示无辜。


现在完成时:
They have protested many times this year. = 今年他们已经抗议过很多次了。
Activists have protested against the war for years. = 积极分子多年来一直在抗议战争。


过去完成时:
They had protested before the new law was passed. = 新法律通过之前，他们就已经抗议过了。
He had protested the decision, but to no avail. = 他曾抗议过这个决定，但没有用。


将来时:
We will protest if the bill is passed. = 如果该法案通过，我们将抗议。
They will protest again next week. = 他们下周将再次抗议。


虚拟语气:
If they protested more forcefully, they might be heard. = 如果他们更强有力地抗议，他们可能会被听到。
I wish they had protested earlier. = 我希望他们早点抗议。


被动语态:
The protest was violently suppressed by the police. = 抗议活动遭到警方暴力镇压。
The new law is being protested by many people. = 许多人正在抗议这项新法律。


疑问句:
Are they protesting the new regulations? = 他们在抗议新的规章制度吗？
What are they protesting about? = 他们在抗议什么？


否定句:
They did not protest the decision. = 他们没有抗议这个决定。
She didn't protest when they took her car. = 当他们拿走她的车时，她没有抗议。


PHRASE->
file a protest = 提出抗议
lodge a protest = 提出抗议
stage a protest = 举行抗议活动
public protest = 公开抗议
violent protest = 暴力抗议
