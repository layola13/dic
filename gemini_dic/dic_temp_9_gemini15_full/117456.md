meditator: /ˈmɛdɪteɪtər/ | n. | 冥想者，沉思者 | A person who practices meditation.  Someone who spends time in quiet thought. | Example:  The meditator sat silently, focusing on her breath.  冥想者静静地坐着，专注于呼吸。  The experienced meditator guided the group through a progressive relaxation exercise.  经验丰富的冥想者引导小组进行渐进式放松练习。 |近义词：contemplator, thinker, reflector | 反义词：agitator, disturber

词源:
The word "meditator" is derived from the verb "meditate," which comes from the Latin word "meditari," meaning "to think, consider, or plan."  The suffix "-ator" is a common agent suffix in English, indicating the person who performs the action.  Thus, a "meditator" is a person who performs the action of meditating.
