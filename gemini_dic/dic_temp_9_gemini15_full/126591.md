nagging:/ˈnæɡɪŋ/| adj., v.|唠叨的；不断抱怨的；持续存在的；折磨人的| persistently complaining or criticizing;  a persistent ache or pain|His nagging doubts about the project prevented him from fully committing. 他对该项目的持续怀疑使他无法全力以赴。  The nagging pain in his shoulder kept him awake. 他肩膀上持续的疼痛使他无法入睡。| persistent, irritating, bothersome | pleasant, soothing|形容词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The children are nagging their mother for candy. = 孩子们一直在缠着妈妈要糖果。
He is nagging me about my messy room. = 他一直在唠叨我的房间太乱。

一般过去时:
She nagged her husband all evening about his work. = 她整个晚上都在唠叨她丈夫的工作。
They nagged at him until he agreed. = 他们一直缠着他直到他同意。

现在完成时:
I have been nagging him to fix the leaky faucet for weeks. = 我已经催了他几周要他修理漏水的龙头了。
She has nagged me about my diet constantly. = 她一直不断地唠叨我的饮食。

过去完成时:
He had nagged her for days before she finally gave in. = 他在她最终让步之前已经唠叨了她好几天了。
They had nagged at the problem for months without a solution. = 他们几个月来一直在纠结这个问题，却始终没有找到解决办法。

将来时:
I will be nagging him until he cleans his room. = 我会一直唠叨他直到他打扫房间。
She will probably nag him about it again tomorrow. = 她明天可能会再次为此唠叨他。

虚拟语气:
If I didn't nag him, he would never do his homework. = 如果我不唠叨他，他永远不会做作业。
I wish I hadn't nagged her so much. = 我真希望我没有那么唠叨她。

被动语态:
He was nagged into submission. = 他被唠叨得不得不屈服。
She was constantly nagged by her conscience. = 她不断被良心谴责。

疑问句:
Is he still nagging you about the report? = 他还在为报告的事唠叨你吗？
Are they nagging you to go to the party? = 他们在催你去参加派对吗？

否定句:
I don't nag my husband about his work. = 我不会唠叨我丈夫的工作。
They didn't nag her to change her mind. = 他们没有唠叨她改变主意。

nagging pain: 持续的疼痛
nagging worry: 挥之不去的担忧
nagging problem: 棘手的问题
