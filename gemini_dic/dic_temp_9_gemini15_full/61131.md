elongation:/ˌiːlɒŋˈɡeɪʃən/|/ˌɛlɒŋˈɡeɪʃən/| n. |伸长；延长；拉长 |The process of becoming longer or extending in length.  It can refer to physical stretching or metaphorical extension. |例句：The elongation of the rubber band was significant.  橡胶带的伸长很明显。

例句：The economic elongation in the last quarter was unexpected.  上个季度的经济增长出乎意料。


近义词：extension, lengthening, prolongation, stretching |反义词：shortening, contraction, compression


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The metal elongates under stress. = 金属在压力下伸长。
This project elongates the deadline. = 这个项目延长了截止日期。


一般过去时:
The bridge elongated significantly after the earthquake. = 地震后桥梁明显伸长。
He elongated his stay in the city. = 他延长了在城市的逗留时间。


现在完成时:
The rubber band has elongated considerably. = 橡胶带已经相当拉长了。
The negotiations have elongated beyond expectations. = 谈判时间比预期延长了。


过去完成时:

The project had elongated due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，项目已经延长了。


将来时:
The ice will elongate as the temperature rises. = 温度升高时，冰会伸长。
They will elongate the working hours next week. = 他们下周会延长工作时间。


虚拟语气:
If the material elongated too much, it would break. = 如果材料伸长过多，它就会断裂。
I wish the day would elongate so I could finish my work. = 我希望这一天能延长，这样我就能完成我的工作了。


被动语态:

疑问句:
