词源:
The word "pom-pom" is of French origin.  It's an onomatopoeic word, meaning it imitates the sound, likely referencing the sound made when shaking or striking the fluffy object.  The French word "pompon"  (meaning "tuft," "tassel," or "button") itself has an uncertain ultimate etymology, possibly related to the word "pomme" (apple), but the connection is not certain. The word entered English sometime in the late 19th or early 20th century, coinciding with the rise in popularity of cheerleading.
