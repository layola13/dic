entombs:/ɪnˈtuːmz/| vt.| 埋葬；使...陷入困境 |to place (a body) in a tomb; to entomb someone or something means to bury it, often in a grand or ceremonial way, or to trap something within something else.  |例句：They entombed the pharaoh in a magnificent pyramid.  他们把法老埋葬在一座宏伟的金字塔里。

The ancient city was entombed by volcanic ash.  这座古城被火山灰掩埋了。|近义词：bury, inter, immure |反义词：exhume, unearth

词源:
The word "entomb" comes from the Old French word "entomber," which itself is derived from the Old French prefix "en-" (meaning "in") and the Old French word "tombe" (meaning "tomb").  Therefore, the word literally means "to put into a tomb."
