phased-in: /feɪzd ˈɪn/ | adj. | 分阶段实施的；逐步推行的 | Gradually introduced or implemented in stages.  | The new system will be phased in over the next six months.  新的系统将在未来六个月内分阶段实施。 | implemented gradually, rolled out gradually | abruptly implemented, immediately implemented


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company is phasing in a new software. = 公司正在逐步实施新软件。
They phase in new policies annually. = 他们每年分阶段实施新政策。

一般过去时:
The government phased in the new tax laws last year. = 政府去年分阶段实施了新的税法。
We phased in the changes over a period of three months. = 我们在三个月的时间里逐步进行了更改。

现在完成时:
They have phased in the new security measures. = 他们已经逐步实施了新的安全措施。
The school has phased in a new curriculum. = 学校已经分阶段引进了新的课程。

过去完成时:
They had phased in the updated system before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期前已分阶段实施了更新后的系统。
The company had phased in the new software before the merger. = 公司在合并之前已分阶段实施了新软件。

将来时:
The company will phase in the new benefits package next year. = 公司明年将分阶段推出新的福利计划。
They will phase in the updated regulations in the coming months. = 他们将在接下来的几个月内分阶段实施更新后的规章。

虚拟语气:
If they phased in the changes more slowly, there would be fewer problems. = 如果他们更缓慢地分阶段实施这些变化，问题就会少一些。
I wish they had phased in the new system more gradually. = 我希望他们能更逐步地实施新系统。

被动语态:
The new regulations were phased in over a two-year period. = 新规在两年时间内分阶段实施。

疑问句:

否定句:
