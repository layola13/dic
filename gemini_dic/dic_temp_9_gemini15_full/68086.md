faded:/ˈfeɪdɪd/ | adj. |褪色的；暗淡的；微弱的 | Describing something that has lost its original color, brightness, or intensity.  It can refer to colors, sounds, memories, or even feelings. |例句：The faded photograph showed a young couple in love.  褪色的照片上显示一对年轻的情侣相爱。

The faded blue paint peeled from the old house. 旧房子的蓝色油漆已经褪色并剥落了。


近义词：pale, washed-out, dull, weak, muted |反义词：bright, vivid, vibrant, strong


词源:  From Old English *fādian, meaning "to grow weak or feeble."  The modern sense of color loss developed later.
