ecclesiology:/ˌɛkliːziˈɒlədʒi/ | n. |教会学，教会论 |The study of the nature and structure of the Church, including its doctrines, practices, and organization.  It explores the Church's relationship with God, the world, and its own members.  |Example:  His doctoral dissertation focused on the ecclesiology of the early church.  他的博士论文集中在早期教会的教会论上。


The study of ecclesiology often involves examining different perspectives on the nature of the Church, such as its visible and invisible aspects, its unity and diversity, and its role in the world. |近义词：ecclesiastical studies, church history (partially), theology (partially)|反义词：N/A |名词


词源:
The word "ecclesiology" is derived from the Greek words "ekklesia" (ἐκκλησία), meaning "church" or "assembly," and "-logia" (-λογία), meaning "study of" or "science of."  Therefore, ecclesiology literally means "the study of the church."
