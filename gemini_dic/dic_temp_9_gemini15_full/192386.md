scripturism:/ˈskrɪptʃərɪzəm/| n. |圣经主义；唯圣经论|The belief that the Bible is the sole and infallible source of religious truth and authority, often leading to literal interpretations and rejection of other sources of knowledge or faith.|例句：His scripturism led him to reject scientific findings that contradicted his literal reading of Genesis.  他的圣经主义使他拒绝了与他逐字解读创世纪相矛盾的科学发现。|近义词：bibliolatry, biblical literalism|反义词：liberalism (in religious interpretation), modernism (in religious thought)
