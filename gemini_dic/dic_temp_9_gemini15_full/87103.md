heralding:/ˈhɛrəldiŋ/ | v. | 预告；预示；宣布；开端 |  Announcing or signaling the arrival of something; being a sign that something is about to happen. | The church bells heralded the start of the wedding ceremony. 教堂的钟声预示着婚礼的开始。  The discovery heralded a new era in medical research.  这一发现预示着医学研究新时代的到来。|  foretelling, foreshadowing, announcing, proclaiming |  ignoring, disregarding


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The newspaper headlines herald a major political shift. 报纸头条预示着重大的政治转变。
Birdsong heralds the arrival of spring. 鸟鸣预示着春天的到来。


一般过去时:
The crack of thunder heralded the approaching storm. 雷声预示着暴风雨即将来临。
The rising sun heralded a new day.  冉冉升起的太阳预示着新的一天。


现在完成时:
Recent discoveries have heralded a new age of space exploration. 近期的发现预示着太空探索的新时代。
The success of the project has heralded a new phase in the company's development.  项目的成功预示着公司发展的新阶段。


过去完成时:
The dark clouds had heralded the coming rain.  乌云预示着即将下雨。
The falling leaves had heralded the arrival of autumn. 落叶预示着秋天的到来。


将来时:
The election results will herald a change in government. 选举结果将预示着政府的更迭。
This new technology will herald a revolution in communication. 这项新技术将预示着通讯领域的革命。


虚拟语气:
If the signs were correct, this would herald a period of peace. 如果迹象正确，这将预示着和平时期。
Were this discovery true, it would herald a scientific breakthrough. 如果这一发现属实，这将预示着科学的突破。


被动语态:
The arrival of spring was heralded by the blooming of flowers. 春天的到来以花朵盛开为预兆。
The new policy was heralded as a step forward. 新政策被誉为向前迈进的一步。


疑问句:
Does this event herald a new beginning?  这个事件预示着一个新的开始吗？
What does this discovery herald for the future?  这一发现预示着未来的什么？


否定句:
The silence did not herald a peaceful resolution.  寂静并没有预示着和平的解决。
The initial reports did not herald any significant changes.  初步报告并没有预示着任何重大变化。
