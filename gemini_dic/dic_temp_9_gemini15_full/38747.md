condolence:/kənˈdəʊləns/| n. |慰问；吊唁 |Expression of sympathy with someone's grief.|I offered my condolences on the death of her father. 我对她的父亲去世表示慰问。|sympathy, commiseration|indifference, celebration|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My condolences go to the bereaved family. = 我的慰问送给丧亲的家人。
He expresses his condolences to the victim's family. = 他向受害者家属表示慰问。


一般过去时:
She offered her condolences to the grieving widow. = 她向悲痛的寡妇表示慰问。
They sent a letter of condolence to the family. = 他们给家人寄去了一封慰问信。


现在完成时:
I have already expressed my condolences. = 我已经表达了我的慰问。
They have sent many condolences to the family. = 他们已经向家人表达了许多慰问。


过去完成时:
Before the funeral, we had already sent our condolences. = 在葬礼之前，我们已经表达了我们的慰问。
By the time the news reached them, they had already received many condolences. = 当消息传到他们那里时，他们已经收到了许多慰问。


将来时:
I will offer my condolences at the funeral. = 我将在葬礼上表示慰问。
We will send a card of condolence next week. = 我们下周会寄一张慰问卡。


虚拟语气:
If I had known, I would have expressed my condolences sooner. = 如果我知道的话，我会早点表示慰问。
I wish I could have offered my condolences in person. = 我希望我能亲自表示慰问。


被动语态:
Condolences were offered by many people. = 许多人都表示了慰问。
A message of condolence was sent to the family. = 一封慰问信被寄给了家人。


疑问句:
Have you offered your condolences yet? = 你已经表示慰问了吗？
What form of condolence is most appropriate? = 什么形式的慰问最合适？


否定句:
I did not offer my condolences. = 我没有表示慰问。
They didn't express their condolences publicly. = 他们没有公开表达他们的慰问。
