open-ended:/ˈoʊpənˈɛndɪd/ | adj. |开放式的，无限制的，不确定的 | Having no predetermined limits or boundaries; allowing for flexibility and multiple possibilities.  It implies a lack of restriction or constraint, offering numerous avenues for response or development. |例句：The interview was open-ended, allowing candidates to fully express their ideas.  这次面试是开放式的，允许候选人充分表达他们的想法。

The teacher gave the students an open-ended assignment, encouraging creativity. 老师给学生们布置了一项开放式的作业，鼓励他们发挥创造力。


近义词：unrestricted, flexible, free-form, unrestricted, indeterminate |反义词：closed-ended, restricted, limited, predetermined, structured


词源:  The term is a compound of "open" and "ended."  "Open" signifies a lack of closure or restriction, while "ended" refers to the conclusion or termination of something.  The combination suggests a conclusion that is not fixed or predetermined.
