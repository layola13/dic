pycnodont:/ˈpɪknədɒnt/| n. |密齿鱼类|A pycnodont is an extinct group of mostly marine bony fish characterized by their numerous, closely packed teeth.  They thrived during the Mesozoic Era.  They are known from fossils found worldwide.|Example:  The discovery of a new pycnodont fossil in the Cretaceous layers provided valuable insights into the evolution of this ancient fish group. 密齿鱼类在白垩纪地层中的新发现为了解这种古老鱼类的进化提供了宝贵的见解。|近义词:  (no direct synonyms, as it's a specific extinct group) |反义词: (no direct antonyms)|名词
