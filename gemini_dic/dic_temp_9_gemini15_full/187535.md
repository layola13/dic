romancing:/rəˈmænsɪŋ/| v.|浪漫化；虚构；追求（异性）|To represent or treat (something) in a romantic or idealized way; to engage in courtship.|例句：He was romancing the idea of a life in the countryside. 他在浪漫化乡村生活的想法。|例句：She was romancing a handsome stranger.  她在追求一位英俊的陌生人。|近义词：idealizing, fictionalizing, courting, wooing |反义词：realistic depiction, factual account


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He romances the past. = 他在美化过去。
She romances every new boyfriend. = 她对每个新男友都充满浪漫幻想。

一般过去时:
They romanced in the moonlight. = 他们在月光下谈恋爱。
He romanced the idea of early retirement.  = 他对提前退休的想法很向往。

现在完成时:
She has romanced several men this year. = 她今年已经追求过好几个男人了。
He has been romancing the idea for months. = 他已经幻想好几个月了。

过去完成时:
They had romanced for years before they married. = 他们结婚前已经恋爱多年了。
He had romanced the notion of escape for a long time. = 他对逃跑的想法已经幻想很久了。

将来时:
I will romance the details of my trip. = 我将美化我的旅行细节。
She will romance him with flowers and chocolates. = 她将用鲜花和巧克力来追求他。

虚拟语气:
If I were to romance the story, it would be a bestseller. = 如果我要美化这个故事，它将会成为畅销书。
I wish I could romance my way out of this problem. = 我真希望我能用浪漫的方式解决这个问题。

被动语态:
The story was romanced to appeal to a wider audience. = 故事被美化以吸引更广泛的观众。
The historical event was unfairly romanced in the film. = 这部电影不公平地美化了这一历史事件。

疑问句:
Is he romancing her? = 他在追求她吗？
Are you romancing the truth? = 你在粉饰真相吗？

否定句:
He didn't romance the details of the accident. = 他没有美化事故的细节。
She doesn't romance reality; she faces it head-on. = 她不会粉饰现实；她会正面面对现实。
