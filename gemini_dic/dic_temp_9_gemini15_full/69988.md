fetch: /fetʃ/ | vt. n. | 取来；拿来；搜索；(尤指电脑)提取；取回 | To go after and bring back; to retrieve.  Also, to bring or get something. As a noun, it means the act of fetching something. | 近义词：retrieve, get, obtain, collect | 反义词：leave, abandon


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dog fetches the stick. = 狗去取回棍子。
She fetches the mail every day. = 她每天去取邮件。

一般过去时:
He fetched a bucket of water. = 他提了一桶水来。
The doctor fetched the necessary medicine. = 医生取来了必要的药。

现在完成时:
I have fetched the children from school. = 我已经从学校把孩子们接来了。
She has fetched the newspaper from the mailbox. = 她已经从邮箱里取了报纸。

过去完成时:
He had fetched the tools before starting the repair. = 他在开始修理之前已经取来了工具。
They had fetched the guests from the airport. = 他们已经从机场接来了客人。

将来时:
I will fetch you some coffee. = 我会给你拿些咖啡。
He will fetch the car from the garage. = 他会去车库取车。

虚拟语气:
If you needed help, I would fetch you. = 如果你需要帮助，我会去帮你。
I wish I could fetch a doctor immediately. = 我希望我能够立即叫来医生。

被动语态:
The ball was fetched by the dog. = 球被狗取来了。
The documents were fetched from the archive. = 文件是从档案馆取来的。

疑问句:
Could you fetch me a glass of water? = 你能帮我拿杯水吗？
Will someone fetch the doctor? = 会有人去叫医生吗？

否定句:
He didn't fetch the newspaper. = 他没有取报纸。
I couldn't fetch the book from the shelf. = 我够不着书架上的书。


PHRASE->
fetch a price  =  卖到…价钱
fetch and carry =  跑腿；服侍
fetch up =  呕吐；最终到达
go fetch = 去拿来
fetch someone something = 为某人取来某物


词源:
Old English *feccan, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *vetch-en and German *fegen.  The original meaning was more related to "to bring" or "to carry".  The modern sense of "retrieve" developed later.
