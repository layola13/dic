grunted:/ˈɡrʌntɪd/| v.|咕哝；哼；(猪等) 哼哼叫 | To make a low, guttural sound, especially expressing displeasure, pain, or effort.  |例句：He grunted in pain after falling. 他摔倒后痛苦地哼了一声。|例句：The pig grunted contentedly as it ate. 猪吃东西时心满意足地哼哼叫着。|近义词：mumbled, groaned, grumbled |反义词：shouted, whispered


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grunts when he's annoyed. = 他生气时会哼哼。
She grunts with effort during her workout. = 她锻炼时费力地哼哼。

一般过去时:
He grunted a response. = 他哼哼地回答。
She grunted with exertion. = 她用力地哼哼。

现在完成时:
He has grunted his displeasure all morning. = 他整个上午都在哼哼表示不满。
She has grunted throughout the entire exercise session. = 她在整个锻炼过程中一直在哼哼。

过去完成时:
He had grunted before he finally spoke. = 他在最终开口说话之前哼哼过。
She had grunted several times before giving up. = 她放弃之前哼哼了好几次。

将来时:
He will grunt when he sees the bill. = 他看到账单时会哼哼。
She will grunt with disapproval. = 她会哼哼表示反对。

虚拟语气:
If he were in pain, he would grunt. = 如果他很痛苦，他会哼哼。
I wish she wouldn't grunt so loudly. = 我希望她不要哼哼那么大声。

被动语态:
(This verb is rarely used passively.)  The displeasure was grunted out.  = 不满被哼哼出来。(罕见用法)
(This verb is rarely used passively.) The effort was expressed in grunts. = 努力通过哼哼来表达。(罕见用法)


疑问句:

否定句:

She didn't grunt; she spoke clearly. = 她没有哼哼；她说话很清楚。
