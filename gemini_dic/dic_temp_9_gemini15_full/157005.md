pillory:/ˈpɪləri/| n. vt.| 颈手枷；示众；公开谴责 | A pillory is a wooden framework with holes for the head and hands, in which offenders were formerly locked and exposed to public ridicule.  It can also refer to the act of publicly criticizing or ridiculing someone. |例句：He was put in the pillory for his treasonous acts. 他因叛国罪被投入颈手枷示众。


The author was pilloried by critics for his controversial views.  这位作者因其有争议的观点而受到评论家的公开谴责。|近义词：public shaming, ridicule, rebuke, castigate |反义词：praise, commendation, applaud

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
From Old French *pilori, from Late Latin pilōrius, from Latin pilum ("stake," "pile").  The original meaning referred to the instrument of punishment itself.  The figurative meaning of public shaming developed later.
