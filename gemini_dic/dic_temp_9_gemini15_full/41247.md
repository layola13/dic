corbel: /ˈkɔːrbəl/ | n. | 拱座，托架 | A projecting bracket, typically of stone or wood, used to support a weight above, such as a cornice or a statue.  | Example: The corbels supported the weight of the heavy stone cornice.  拱座支撑着沉重的石制檐口重量。|近义词：bracket, console, cantilever | 反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "corbel" comes from the Old French word "corbel," which in turn derives from the Old Provençal word "corbella." Ultimately, the word's origins are likely Germanic, possibly related to words meaning "basket" or "crow's nest," referring to the shape of some corbels.
