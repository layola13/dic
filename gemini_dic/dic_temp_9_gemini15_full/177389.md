reaver:/ˈriːvər/| n. |掠夺者，海盗|A reaver is someone who seizes and carries off property by force; a raider, especially a pirate.  Reavers are often depicted as violent and ruthless individuals who operate outside the law. |例句：The Viking reaver plundered the coastal villages.  维京掠夺者洗劫了沿海村庄。
The spacefaring reaver attacked the defenseless colony. 太空掠夺者袭击了毫无防备的殖民地。|近义词：pirate, raider, plunderer, marauder |反义词：protector, defender, benefactor

词源:
The word "reaver" comes from the Old Norse word "reiðari," meaning "rider," particularly a raider on horseback.  Over time, its meaning evolved to encompass any type of raider or plunderer, especially pirates at sea.
