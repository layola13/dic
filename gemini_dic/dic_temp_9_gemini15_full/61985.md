enactment:/ɪˈnæktmənt/ | n. | 法令；制定；演出 | The enactment of the new law caused widespread debate.  新法律的颁布引起了广泛的争论。 The play's enactment was breathtaking. 这出戏的演出令人叹为观止。 |近义词：establishment, legislation, performance | 反义词：repeal, abolition


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government enacts new laws every year. = 政府每年都会颁布新法律。
This theater group enacts Shakespeare's plays. = 这个剧团上演莎士比亚的戏剧。

一般过去时:
Congress enacted the Civil Rights Act in 1964. = 国会在1964年颁布了民权法案。
The actors enacted the scene with great emotion. = 演员们充满感情地演绎了这个场景。

虚拟语气:
If they enacted stricter penalties, crime might decrease. = 如果他们实施更严格的惩罚，犯罪率可能会下降。
If they had enacted the law sooner, the damage could have been less. = 如果他们早点颁布这项法律，损失可能会更小。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
