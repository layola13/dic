evinces:/ˈɛvɪnsəz/ | vt. | 显示；表明；证明 | To reveal the presence of (a quality or feeling); to show clearly.  The evidence evinces a clear pattern of negligence. 证据表明存在明显的疏忽行为。|近义词：demonstrates, reveals, shows, indicates | 反义词：conceals, hides, masks | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His smile evinces his happiness. = 他的笑容表明他很快乐。
Her actions evince a deep commitment to her work. = 她的行为表明她对工作非常投入。

一般过去时:
The study evinced a strong correlation between the two variables. = 这项研究表明这两个变量之间存在很强的相关性。
His letter evinced his displeasure with the situation. = 他的信显示他对这种情况不满。

现在完成时:
The results have evinced the effectiveness of the new treatment. = 结果已经证明了新疗法的有效性。
Her behavior has evinced a growing maturity. = 她的行为表现出日益成熟的迹象。

过去完成时:
The archeological findings had evinced a previously unknown civilization. = 考古发现已经表明存在一个以前未知的文明。
The investigation had evinced a conspiracy to defraud the company. = 调查已经表明存在一个欺诈公司的阴谋。


将来时:
The upcoming election will evince the public's opinion on the matter. = 即将来临的选举将表明公众对这件事的看法。
Further research will evince the long-term effects of the drug. = 进一步的研究将表明这种药物的长期影响。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence evinced a different conclusion, we would have to reconsider our hypothesis. = 如果证据表明不同的结论，我们就必须重新考虑我们的假设。
I wish the data evinced a clearer trend. = 我希望数据能显示出更清晰的趋势。


被动语态:
A clear pattern of negligence was evinced by the evidence. = 证据表明存在明显的疏忽行为。（被动语态）
The effectiveness of the new treatment was evinced by the results. = 新疗法的有效性已由结果证明。（被动语态）


疑问句:
Does this behavior evince a lack of respect? = 这种行为是否表现出缺乏尊重？
What does the data evince about the market trend? = 数据显示了哪些关于市场趋势的信息？


否定句:
The study did not evince any significant differences between the groups. = 这项研究没有显示出各组之间有任何显著差异。
His demeanor did not evince any anxiety. = 他的举止没有表现出任何焦虑。


PHRASE->
evince a desire to = 表达…的愿望
evince a willingness to = 表达…的意愿
evince great interest in = 对…表现出极大的兴趣
evince a need for = 表明需要…
evince a lack of = 表明缺乏…


词源:
From Old French *eveigner (to come forth, happen), from Latin *evenire (to come out, happen), from *ex- (out) + venire (to come).  The original meaning was closer to "happen" or "occur," but it evolved to its current meaning through the idea of something "coming out" or being revealed.
