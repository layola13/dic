poachers:/ˈpoʊtʃərz/| n.|偷猎者，非法捕猎者|A poacher is a person who hunts or fishes illegally.  They often target protected animals or exceed legal limits.|例句：The poachers were caught with a large number of endangered birds. 偷猎者被抓获，身上带着许多濒危鸟类。

例句：The park rangers are working hard to stop poachers from killing elephants for their ivory.  公园管理员正在努力阻止偷猎者杀害大象以获取象牙。|近义词：illegal hunters, game thieves|反义词：conservationists, game wardens|词性:名词

词源:
The word "poacher" ultimately derives from the Old French word "pochier," meaning "to poke" or "to thrust."  This refers to the way poachers would secretly and quickly take game.  Over time, the meaning evolved to specifically refer to the illegal hunting of game.
