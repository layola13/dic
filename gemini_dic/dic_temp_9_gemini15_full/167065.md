presumptiveness:/prɪˈzʌmptɪvnəs/| n.|冒失；专横；自以为是；武断|The quality or state of being presumptuous;  an instance of presumptuous behavior. It suggests acting on assumptions without sufficient evidence, often showing arrogance or a lack of respect for others' feelings or rights. |例句：His presumptiveness in assuming the role of leader without being elected caused resentment among his colleagues.  他的专横，在未经选举就承担领导角色，引起了同事们的怨恨。
例句：The presumptiveness of his demands was shocking. 他要求的专横令人震惊。|近义词：arrogance, audacity, impertinence, forwardness|反义词：humility, modesty, deference, respect
