explodes:/ɪkˈspləʊdz/ | v. | 爆炸；爆发；激增 | To burst or break apart violently, often with a loud noise; to suddenly increase rapidly. | 例句：The bomb exploded with a deafening roar.  炸弹发出震耳欲聋的巨响爆炸了。| 例句：The popularity of the new game exploded overnight.这款新游戏的受欢迎程度一夜之间激增。|近义词：burst, detonate, erupt |反义词：implode, collapse


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The volcano explodes every hundred years. = 火山每百年爆发一次。
The business explodes with new opportunities. = 这项业务充满了新的机遇。

一般过去时:
The balloon exploded when he poked it. = 他戳破气球时，气球爆炸了。
The crowd exploded in cheers. = 人群爆发出欢呼声。

现在完成时:
The population has exploded in the last decade. = 在过去的十年里，人口激增。
My anger has exploded after hearing that news. = 听到那个消息后，我的怒火爆发了。

过去完成时:
The pressure had exploded before the safety valve opened. = 安全阀打开之前，压力已经爆发了。
The argument had exploded into a full-blown fight. = 争论已经演变成一场全面的打斗。

将来时:
The rocket will explode on impact. = 火箭撞击时将会爆炸。
The demand for the product is predicted to explode next year. = 预计明年对该产品的需求将会激增。

虚拟语气:
If the pressure increased further, the container would explode. = 如果压力进一步增加，容器就会爆炸。
I wish the situation hadn't exploded so quickly. = 我希望情况没有这么快恶化。


被动语态:
The building was exploded by a bomb. = 这栋建筑物被炸弹炸毁了。
The secret was exploded by the media. = 这个秘密被媒体曝光了。


疑问句:
When did the bomb explode? = 炸弹是什么时候爆炸的？
Will the market explode next year? = 明年市场会爆发吗？


否定句:
The bomb didn't explode. = 炸弹没有爆炸。
The plan didn't explode as expected. = 计划没有像预期的那样成功。
