catalogize:/ˈkætəlɒɡaɪz/ | vt. | 编目；分类；登记 | To arrange or list systematically in a catalog.  | The librarian carefully catalogized the new books. 图书管理员仔细地为新书编目。 | classify, index, register |  disorganize, misclassify | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She catalogizes the library's collection every year. = 她每年都为图书馆的藏书编目。
We catalogize our products by category. = 我们按类别对产品进行分类。

一般过去时:
The museum catalogized the artifacts from the excavation. = 博物馆为挖掘出来的文物编了目。
They catalogized the data for easy retrieval. = 他们对数据进行了编目，以便于检索。

现在完成时:
The archivist has catalogized thousands of documents. = 档案管理员已经为数千份文件编了目。
We have catalogized all the new arrivals. = 我们已经为所有新到的物品编了目。

过去完成时:
The historian had catalogized the letters before the exhibition. = 历史学家在展览前已经为信件编了目。
They had catalogized the specimens before the study began. = 他们在研究开始之前已经对样本进行了编目。

将来时:
The team will catalogize the entire archive next year. = 该团队明年将为整个档案编目。
She will catalogize her extensive stamp collection. = 她将为她大量的邮票收藏编目。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would catalogize my photographs. = 如果我有更多时间，我会为我的照片编目。
It would be helpful if we catalogized all the evidence. = 如果我们能为所有证据编目，那将会有所帮助。

被动语态:
The books were catalogized by the librarian. = 这些书是由图书管理员编目的。
The data is being catalogized using a new software. = 数据正在使用新的软件进行编目。

疑问句:
Have you catalogized the documents yet? = 你已经为文件编目了吗？
How will the artifacts be catalogized? = 文物将如何编目？

否定句:
They didn't catalogize the old maps. = 他们没有为旧地图编目。
We haven't finished catalogizing the collection. = 我们还没有完成对藏品的编目。
