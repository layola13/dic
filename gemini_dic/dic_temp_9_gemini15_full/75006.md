friending:/ˈfrɛndɪŋ/| v.|结交朋友；成为朋友|The act of becoming friends with someone, often through online platforms or social media.|例句：Friending someone on Facebook doesn't automatically make you best friends. 在Facebook上加某人为好友并不自动意味着你们是最好的朋友。|例句：She enjoys friending new people on her travels. 她喜欢在旅行中结交新朋友。|近义词：befriending, making friends|反义词：unfriending, estrangement


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are friending each other online. = 他们在网上互相加为好友。
She is friending many new people at the conference. = 她在会议上结交了许多新朋友。


一般过去时:
We friended him on Instagram last week. = 我们上周在Instagram上加了他为好友。
He friended all his classmates on Facebook. = 他在Facebook上加了他所有的同学为好友。


现在完成时:
I have friended several people from my online course. = 我已经加了几位来自我的在线课程的朋友。
They have friended each other for over a year. = 他们互相认识一年多了。


过去完成时:
She had friended him before they met in person. = 她在他们见面之前就已经加了他为好友。
They had friended many people through their shared hobby. = 他们通过共同的爱好结交了许多朋友。


将来时:
I will friend him after I get his contact information. = 我拿到他的联系方式后就会加他为好友。
She will friend everyone she meets at the party. = 她将在派对上结交所有她遇到的人。


虚拟语气:
If I knew her better, I would friend her on social media. = 如果我更了解她，我会在社交媒体上加她为好友。
I wish I had friended her sooner. = 我真希望我早点加她为好友。


被动语态:
He was friended by many people after his speech. = 演讲结束后，许多人都加了他为好友。
She was friended by a famous actor on Twitter. = 她被一位著名演员在推特上加为好友。


疑问句:
Have you friended her yet? = 你加她为好友了吗？
Are you going to friend him on Instagram? = 你打算在Instagram上加他为好友吗？


否定句:
I didn't friend him because I didn't know him. = 我没有加他为好友，因为我不认识他。
She hasn't friended anyone new recently. = 她最近没有结交任何新朋友。
