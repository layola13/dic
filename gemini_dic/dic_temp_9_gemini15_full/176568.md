re-spacing:/ˌriːˈspeɪsɪŋ/| n. |重新间距，重新排列间距 |The act of adjusting the spacing between elements, such as lines of text, images, or objects.  It often involves increasing or decreasing the distance between items to improve visual appeal, readability, or functionality. |Example: The designer suggested re-spacing the paragraphs to improve the readability of the document.  重新排列段落间距以提高文档的可读性。|近义词：rearrangement, readjustment |反义词：crowding, compression |名词
