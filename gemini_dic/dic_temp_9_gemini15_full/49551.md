deme:/ˈdiːm/| n. |种群，宗族，集团 | A deme is a local, geographically restricted population of closely related organisms that share a common gene pool.  It's often used in population genetics and evolutionary biology to refer to a group of individuals that breed more frequently with each other than with members of other demes.  | Example: The researchers studied the genetic diversity within and between different demes of the mountain lion population.  研究人员研究了山狮种群内和不同种群间的遗传多样性。|近义词：population, group, community |反义词：individual
