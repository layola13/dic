haight:/heɪt/ | n. | 海特街（旧金山地名） | Haight Street is a street in San Francisco, California, known for its association with the counterculture movement of the 1960s. | 近义词：无 | 反义词：无 | 名词


英文解析:Haight Street is a major thoroughfare in San Francisco, California, particularly famous for its association with the hippie counterculture movement of the 1960s.  During that era, it became a center for psychedelic music, free love, and experimentation, attracting young people from across the country and beyond.  Today, while retaining some remnants of its past, Haight-Ashbury is a vibrant and diverse neighborhood with a mix of shops, restaurants, and residences.


例句:

I spent a week exploring the vibrant shops and cafes on Haight Street. = 我花了一周时间探索海特街充满活力的商店和咖啡馆。

词源:The name "Haight" comes from Henry Haight, a prominent 19th-century California politician who served as the 11th Governor of California.  The street was named in his honor.
