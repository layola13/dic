prebestowed:/ˌpriːbɪˈstoʊd/| adj.| 预先给予的；预先授予的 |  A prebestowed gift or right is one that has been given or granted in advance.  The word combines "pre-" (before) and "bestow" (to give). It is a relatively rare word, primarily found in formal or literary contexts. |例句：The prebestowed honour was a source of great pride to him.  = 预先授予的荣誉让他非常自豪。

The prebestowed privileges were revoked due to misconduct. = 由于行为不端，预先授予的特权被撤销。|近义词：pregranted, advanced, prior, beforehand |反义词：postponed, delayed, withheld
