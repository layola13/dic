dungeons:/ˈdʌnʒənz/| n.|地牢，地下室|A dungeon is a dark, underground prison, especially one in a castle.  The word often evokes images of damp, cold, and unpleasant conditions.  It's used figuratively to describe any unpleasant or confining situation.|例句：The prisoner was thrown into a dark, damp dungeon.  囚犯被扔进了一个黑暗潮湿的地牢。

例句：The game features a series of challenging dungeons to explore. 游戏包含一系列具有挑战性的地牢供玩家探索。


近义词：cell, prison, jail, underground chamber|反义词：palace, paradise, heaven


词源:The word "dungeon" comes from the Old French word "donjon," which originally referred to the main keep or fortified tower of a castle. Over time, the meaning shifted to refer specifically to the prison cells within the castle.  The etymology reflects the evolution of castles from primarily defensive structures to ones incorporating prisons.
