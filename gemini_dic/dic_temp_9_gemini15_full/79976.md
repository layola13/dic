go-bush:/ɡoʊ bʊʃ/| phrasal verb|进入丛林；逃亡；隐居；冒险；|To go into the bush is to go into a wild, undeveloped area, typically for a period of time, often for adventure, survival, or to escape something.|例句：He went bush for six months after his business failed. 他生意失败后，去了丛林里待了六个月。|例句：The rebels went bush to avoid capture.  叛军逃入丛林以躲避抓捕。|近义词：escape, flee, disappear, abscond |反义词：return, settle down


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They go bush every summer. = 他们每年夏天都去丛林探险。
We go bush to escape the city noise. = 我们去丛林是为了逃离城市的喧嚣。

一般过去时:
He went bush after the war ended. = 战争结束后，他去了丛林。
They went bush to find themselves. = 他们去丛林是为了寻找自我。

现在完成时:
She has gone bush several times. = 她已经去过丛林好几次了。
They have gone bush and haven't been seen since. = 他们去了丛林，此后再也没有被看到过。

过去完成时:
He had gone bush before the news reached him. = 消息传到他那里之前，他已经去了丛林。
They had gone bush for weeks before anyone noticed their absence. = 他们已经去了丛林几周了，才有人注意到他们的失踪。

将来时:
We will go bush next year. = 我们明年要去丛林。
They will go bush as soon as they finish their work. = 他们一完成工作就去丛林。

虚拟语气:
If I had the time, I would go bush. = 如果我有时间，我会去丛林。
I wish I could go bush right now. = 我真希望现在就能去丛林。

被动语态:
(This phrasal verb is rarely used passively.)


疑问句:
Did he go bush alone? = 他一个人去丛林吗？
Will they go bush this weekend? = 他们这个周末要去丛林吗？

否定句:
They didn't go bush last year. = 他们去年没有去丛林。
We won't go bush because of the bad weather. = 因为天气不好，我们不会去丛林。


PHRASE->
