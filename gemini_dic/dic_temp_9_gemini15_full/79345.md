gleeman:/ˈɡliːmən/| n. |吟游诗人，乐师|A gleeman was a professional entertainer in the Middle Ages, typically singing, playing music, and telling stories.  They often travelled from place to place, performing for audiences in exchange for food, money, or lodging.  The term encompasses a broader range of entertainers than just singers, including those who performed acrobatic feats or other forms of entertainment.|例句：The gleeman entertained the court with songs and tales of chivalry.  吟游诗人用骑士歌曲和故事招待朝廷。 The gleeman's lively performance delighted the villagers. 吟游诗人的精彩表演让村民们喜悦。|近义词：minstrel, bard, troubadour, jongleur |反义词：N/A
