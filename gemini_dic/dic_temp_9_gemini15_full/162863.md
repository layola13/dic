precedent:/ˈpresɪdənt/ | n. |先例，前例；判例 |A precedent is an earlier event or action that is regarded as an example or guide to be considered in subsequent similar circumstances.  It sets a standard or pattern for future situations. |The court's decision set a precedent for future cases involving similar issues.  法庭的判决为将来涉及类似问题的案件树立了先例。| example, instance, case | anomaly, exception | 名词


USAGE->

This precedent guides our current decision. = 这个先例指导我们目前的决定。
The precedent influences many legal interpretations. = 这个先例影响许多法律解释。


一般过去时:

过去完成时:

The court had examined several precedents before issuing its ruling. = 法院在发布裁决之前已经审查了几个先例。


将来时:
