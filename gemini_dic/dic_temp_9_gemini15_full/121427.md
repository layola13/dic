misinformers:/ˌmɪsɪnˈfɔːrmərz/ | n. |造谣者，传播虚假信息者 |Those who deliberately spread false or inaccurate information.  They often do this to manipulate public opinion or achieve personal gain. |Example: The misinformers on social media are responsible for the spread of dangerous conspiracy theories. 社交媒体上的造谣者应该为危险阴谋论的传播负责。|近义词：liars, deceivers, propagandists |反义词：truth-tellers, fact-checkers |名词

词源:
The word "misinformers" is derived from the verb "misinform," which combines the prefix "mis-" (meaning "wrongly" or "badly") with the verb "inform" (meaning "to give information to").  Therefore, "misinformers" refers to those who wrongly or badly inform, thus spreading false information.
