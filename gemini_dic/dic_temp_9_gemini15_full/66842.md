expatriating: /ɪkˈspætrieɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 移居海外；使移居海外 | The act of leaving one's native country to live in another.  It often implies a more permanent move than simply traveling abroad.  The term can also refer to the process of assisting someone in relocating to a foreign country. | 例句：Many professionals are expatriating to find better job opportunities. 许多专业人士正在移居海外以寻找更好的工作机会。

He is expatriating himself to avoid political persecution. 他正在移居海外以避免政治迫害。


近义词：emigrating, relocating (to a foreign country), going abroad (permanently) | 反义词：repatriating, returning home, immigrating (to one's native country) | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are expatriating to Canada. = 他们正在移居加拿大。
She expatriates herself every summer. = 她每年夏天都移居海外。

一般过去时:
My uncle expatriated to Australia in 1990. = 我叔叔在1990年移居澳大利亚。
The family expatriated to escape the war. = 这家人移居海外以逃离战争。

现在完成时:
He has expatriated to several countries. = 他已经移居过好几个国家了。
She has expatriated herself for over a decade. = 她移居海外已经超过十年了。

过去完成时:
They had expatriated before the revolution broke out. = 革命爆发之前他们就已经移居海外了。
She had expatriated to South America before I met her. = 在我认识她之前，她已经移居南美洲了。

将来时:
We will expatriate to France next year. = 我们明年将移居法国。
They will expatriate themselves once they retire. = 他们退休后将移居海外。

虚拟语气:
If I were wealthy, I would expatriate to a tropical island. = 如果我有钱，我会移居到一个热带岛屿。
I wish I could expatriate to escape this harsh climate. = 我希望我能移居海外以逃离这种恶劣的气候。

被动语态:
Many people are being expatriated due to economic hardship. = 许多人由于经济困难而被迫移居海外。
He was expatriated by his company to a new branch office. = 他被公司派到新的分公司工作（相当于被公司安排移居海外）。

疑问句:

否定句:

She won't expatriate until she finds a suitable job. = 在她找到合适的工作之前，她不会移居海外。
