modulating: /ˈmɒdjʊleɪtɪŋ/ /ˈmɑːdjʊleɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 调制；调节；缓和；改变音调 |  To modulate something means to adjust or regulate it, often gradually or subtly. This can refer to sound, light, signals, or even emotions.  |


例句：

The singer modulated her voice to a softer tone. = 歌手把她的声音调低了。

He carefully modulated the speed of the car as he approached the corner. = 他在接近拐角时小心地调节了车速。


近义词：adjust, regulate, vary, temper, control


反义词：intensify (in some contexts), leave unchanged


词性：动词


词源：

From Middle French *moduler, from Latin modulārī "to regulate, measure in rhythm," from modulus "measure, rhythm," related to modus "manner, measure," from PIE *med- "to measure."
