preconclusion:/ˌpriːkənˈkluːʒən/| n. | 预先结论；草率结论 | A preconclusion is a conclusion reached before all the evidence has been considered or before sufficient investigation has taken place.  It often implies a hasty or premature judgment. | Example:  His preconclusion that the project would fail proved to be inaccurate after further analysis.  他的项目会失败的预先结论在进一步分析后被证明是不准确的。 |近义词：prejudgment, hasty conclusion, snap judgment | 反义词：well-considered conclusion, informed decision |  名词
