elapsing: /ɪˈlæpsɪŋ/ | v. |消逝；过去；流逝 | The process of time passing; the act of something passing or going by.  |例句：Time is elapsing quickly. 时间过得很快。

例句：The years elapsed quickly.  这些年过得很快。


近义词：passing, going by, lapsing, slipping away | 反义词：beginning, commencing, starting | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Time is elapsing. = 时间正在流逝。
The deadline is elapsing soon. = 最后期限即将到来。


一般过去时:
Several years elapsed before we met again. = 我们再次见面之前过了好几年。
A long time elapsed before he responded. = 他很久才回应。


现在完成时:
Several years have elapsed since the accident. = 事故发生后已经过去好几年了。
Much time has elapsed since we last talked. = 自从我们上次谈话以来已经过去了很久。


过去完成时:
Many years had elapsed before the truth was discovered. = 真相被发现之前已经过去了许多年。
A considerable amount of time had elapsed before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前已经过去了相当长的时间。


将来时:
Another year will elapse before the project is completed. = 项目完成还需要一年时间。
Several months will elapse before we see results. = 我们要过几个月才能看到结果。


虚拟语气:
If a few more weeks elapsed, we would have enough data. = 如果再过几周，我们就会有足够的数据。
If only more time had elapsed before the decision was made! = 要是做决定之前能再多一点时间就好了！


被动语态:
(The verb "elapse" is intransitive and does not typically use the passive voice.)


疑问句:
How much time has elapsed? = 过去了多长时间？
How long has it been elapsing since the start? = 自开始以来已经过去了多久？


否定句:
Not much time elapsed before he called back. = 他很快就回电话了。（否定的是“没有太多时间”）
Little time had elapsed before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前几乎没过多久。（否定的是“时间很少”）


PHRASE->
let time elapse = 让时间过去
allow time to elapse = 允许时间流逝
the elapsing of time = 时间的流逝
the years that elapsed = 逝去的岁月
as time elapsed = 随着时间的推移
