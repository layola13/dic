couplet:/ˈkʌplɪt/ | n. |对句；双行诗；对联 | A couplet is a pair of successive lines of verse, especially a pair rhyming together, forming a complete thought or syntactic unit.  It is often used in poetry and song lyrics. |Example:  The following lines from Shakespeare's Sonnet 18 are a couplet: "So long as men can breathe or eyes can see, / So long lives this, and this gives life to thee." 这两句莎士比亚第18首十四行诗中的诗句是一对对句："只要人们还能呼吸，眼睛还能看见，/这首诗就将永存，并赋予你生命。" |近义词：distich, pair, rhyming pair |反义词：None specifically.
