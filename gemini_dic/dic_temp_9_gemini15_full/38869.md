confederations:/ˌkənˌfedəˈreɪʃənz/| n.|邦联；联盟；同盟|A confederation is an organization that consists of a number of states, groups, or individuals with a common purpose.  It often implies a looser association than a federation, with member states retaining greater autonomy. |Example: The Articles of Confederation governed the newly independent United States for several years.  美国独立后的几年里，邦联条例是管理新生的美国的。|近义词：league, alliance, federation, union|反义词：empire, single state
