discover:/dɪˈskʌvər/| vt.|发现，发觉|To find something or someone unexpectedly or while searching, or to learn something previously unknown.|例句：Columbus discovered America in 1492. 哥伦布在1492年发现了美洲。|例句：Scientists have discovered a new species of plant. 科学家们发现了一种新的植物物种。|近义词：find, uncover, detect|反义词：hide, conceal, obscure|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Scientists discover new planets regularly. = 科学家定期发现新的行星。
I discover new things every day. = 我每天都会发现新的事物。

一般过去时:
They discovered a hidden treasure. = 他们发现了一笔隐藏的宝藏。
She discovered her talent for painting. = 她发现了自己绘画的天赋。

现在完成时:
We have discovered the cause of the problem. = 我们已经发现了问题的根源。
They have discovered a new cure for the disease. = 他们已经发现了一种新的治疗这种疾病的方法。

过去完成时:
He had discovered the truth before anyone else. = 他在其他人之前就发现了真相。
Archaeologists had discovered ancient ruins beneath the city. = 考古学家们在城市下面发现了古代遗址。

将来时:
We will discover more about the universe in the future. = 我们将来会发现更多关于宇宙的知识。
Scientists believe they will discover a new element soon. = 科学家们相信他们很快就会发现一种新的元素。

虚拟语气:
If I discovered a lost city, I would be famous. = 如果我发现了一个失落的城市，我会成名。
I wish I could discover the secret to happiness. = 我希望我能发现幸福的秘诀。

被动语态:
America was discovered by Columbus. = 美洲是被哥伦布发现的。
The new planet was discovered by a team of astronomers. = 这个新的行星是由一个天文学家团队发现的。

疑问句:
Did they discover anything interesting? = 他们发现了什么有趣的东西吗？
Have you discovered the solution yet? = 你找到解决方法了吗？

否定句:
They didn't discover any evidence. = 他们没有发现任何证据。
I haven't discovered the answer to the riddle yet. = 我还没有找到谜语的答案。


PHRASE->
discover the truth = 发现真相
discover a new talent = 发现新的才能
discover a new world = 发现新世界
discover a secret = 发现秘密
discover a pattern = 发现规律


词源:
The word "discover" comes from the Old French word "descouvrir," which itself comes from the Latin "dis-" (meaning "apart" or "away") and "cooperire" (meaning "to cover").  Therefore, the literal meaning is "to uncover" or "to reveal."
