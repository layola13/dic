dejecture:/dɪˈdʒɛktʃər/| n. |推测，猜测；臆断|The act of making a conjecture; a guess or supposition.  It often implies a lack of sufficient evidence or reasoning.  |例句：His dejecture about the cause of the accident proved to be wrong. 他的关于事故原因的推测被证明是错误的。

例句：The detective's dejecture, based on circumstantial evidence, led to a dead end.  侦探基于间接证据的推测导致了死胡同。


近义词：conjecture, speculation, surmise, guess, hypothesis |反义词：certainty, fact, proof


词源:  From Latin *de- + *iacere "to throw," related to iacere (throw).  The original sense suggests a throwing forth of an idea or opinion, often without firm evidence.
