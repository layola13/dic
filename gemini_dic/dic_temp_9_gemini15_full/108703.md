lean-on: /ˌliːnˈɒn/ | v. | 依靠，依赖，凭借 | To rely on for support, either physical or emotional.  |例句：I lean on my friends for emotional support. 我依靠我的朋友获得情感支持。|近义词：rely on, depend on, count on |反义词：stand alone, be independent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He leans on his cane for support. = 他依靠拐杖支撑。
She leans on her family for advice. = 她依靠家人寻求建议。


一般过去时:
I leaned on my brother when I lost my job. = 我失业时依靠我的哥哥。
They leaned on each other during difficult times. = 他们共度难关互相依靠。


现在完成时:
She has leaned on her faith throughout her illness. = 在她生病的整个过程中，她一直依靠她的信仰。
He has leaned on his colleagues for help with the project. = 他一直在依靠他的同事帮助完成这个项目。


过去完成时:
I had leaned on my parents for financial help before I got my job. = 在我找到工作之前，我一直依靠我的父母提供经济上的帮助。
They had leaned on each other for years before the final breakup. = 在最终分手之前，他们互相依靠了很多年。


将来时:
I will lean on my experience to guide my decisions. = 我将依靠我的经验来指导我的决定。
She will lean on her team to complete the project on time. = 她将依靠她的团队按时完成项目。


虚拟语气:
If I needed help, I would lean on my close friends. = 如果我需要帮助，我会依靠我的密友。
I wish I could lean on someone during this difficult time. = 我希望在这个困难时期我能依靠某个人。


被动语态:
(The verb "lean on" is rarely used in the passive voice. A more natural phrasing would be needed to convey the same meaning.)


疑问句:
Who do you lean on when you're feeling down? = 当你情绪低落时，你依靠谁？
Can I lean on you for help with this task? = 我可以依靠你帮忙完成这项任务吗？


否定句:
He doesn't lean on others for help; he's very independent. = 他不依靠别人帮忙；他非常独立。
She doesn't want to lean on her family for financial support. = 她不想依赖她的家人提供经济上的支持。
