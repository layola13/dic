degarnishment: /ˌdiːɡɑːrnɪʃmənt/ | n. | 撤去装饰；去除装饰物 | The act of removing decorations or embellishments from something.  It often implies a removal that is deliberate and possibly intended to simplify or diminish something's appearance. | Example: The degarnishment of the hall for the austere meeting left it stark and bare.  =  为简朴的会议而撤去了大厅的装饰，使其显得冷清而空旷。 | 近义词：removal of decorations, stripping, simplification | 反义词：decoration, embellishment, ornamentation |
