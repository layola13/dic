pavement:/ˈpeɪvmənt/| n. |人行道；路面|A pavement is a paved surface for pedestrians, usually alongside a road.|例句：I walked on the pavement to avoid the traffic. 我走在人行道上以避开交通。|例句：The pavement was cracked and uneven. 人行道破裂且不平整。|近义词：sidewalk, walkway|反义词：roadway, carriageway


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The pavement is wet after the rain. = 雨后人行道湿漉漉的。
The pavement needs repairing. = 人行道需要修理。


一般过去时:
The children played on the pavement. = 孩子们在人行道上玩耍。
I slipped on the icy pavement. = 我在结冰的人行道上滑倒了。


现在完成时:
They have repaved the pavement in front of the school. = 他们已经重新铺设了学校前面的人行道。
We have walked many miles on this pavement. = 我们在这条人行道上走了许多英里。


过去完成时:
The car had crashed into the pavement before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，汽车已经撞上了人行道。
They had already walked along the pavement before it started to rain. = 在开始下雨之前，他们已经沿着人行道走过了。


将来时:
They will pave the pavement next week. = 他们下周将铺设人行道。
The new pavement will be completed by the end of the month. = 新的人行道将在月底完工。


虚拟语气:
If the pavement were wider, it would be safer. = 如果人行道更宽，就会更安全。
I wish the pavement wasn't so cracked. = 我希望人行道不要这么裂。


被动语态:
The pavement is being repaired. = 人行道正在修理中。
The pavement was damaged by the storm. = 人行道被风暴破坏了。


疑问句:
Is the pavement safe to walk on? = 人行道安全可行走吗？

否定句:

There is no pavement on this side of the road. = 道路这边没有铺设人行道。
