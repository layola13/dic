ferments:/fərˈmɛnts/| v. |发酵；煽动；使激动|To undergo fermentation; to cause to ferment; to excite or agitate.|例句：The grapes are fermenting in the barrels. 葡萄在桶里发酵。The politician's speech fermented unrest in the crowd. 这位政治家的演讲煽动了人群的骚动。|近义词：brew, leaven, excite, agitate|反义词：calm, soothe


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The yeast ferments the sugar. = 酵母使糖发酵。
He ferments his own beer. = 他自己酿造啤酒。


一般过去时:
The bread fermented overnight. = 面包一夜之间发酵了。
The crowd fermented with anger. = 人群怒气冲冲。


现在完成时:
The mixture has fermented for three days. = 混合物已经发酵了三天。
The news has fermented public opinion. = 这条新闻激起了民愤。


过去完成时:
The dough had fermented before baking. = 面团在烘烤之前已经发酵了。
The situation had fermented into a full-blown crisis. = 局势已经演变成一场全面的危机。


将来时:
The wine will ferment for several months. = 这葡萄酒将发酵数月。
This issue will ferment public debate for weeks. = 这个问题将会激起公众数周的辩论。


虚拟语气:
If the temperature were higher, the mixture would ferment faster. = 如果温度更高，混合物会发酵得更快。
If we hadn't intervened, the situation would have fermented into a riot. = 如果我们没有干预，情况就会演变成暴乱。


被动语态:
The wort is fermented in large tanks. = 麦芽汁在大型罐中发酵。
Public opinion is easily fermented by media reports. = 公众舆论很容易被媒体报道所煽动。


疑问句:
Does this mixture ferment easily? = 这种混合物容易发酵吗？
Is the situation fermenting into a crisis? = 局势正在演变成危机吗？


否定句:
The batter didn't ferment properly. = 面糊没有完全发酵。
The government's actions did not ferment any significant public opposition. = 政府的行动并没有激起任何重大的公众反对。


PHRASE->
ferment of excitement = 兴奋的骚动
ferment of ideas = 思想的激荡
ferment a revolution = 煽动革命
ferment a rebellion = 煽动叛乱
undergo fermentation = 经历发酵
