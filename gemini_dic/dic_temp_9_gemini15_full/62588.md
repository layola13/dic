enfeeble:/ɪnˈfiːbl/ | vt. |使衰弱；使虚弱 |To weaken or sap the strength of (someone or something).  |例句：The illness enfeebled him.  疾病使他虚弱。|例句：Years of overwork had enfeebled her.  多年的过度工作使她虚弱。|近义词：weaken, debilitate, impair, undermine |反义词：strengthen, invigorate, fortify |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The illness enfeeble him. = 疾病使他虚弱。
Overwork enfeeble many people. = 过度工作使许多人虚弱。

一般过去时:
The accident enfeebled him. = 那次事故使他虚弱。
The long journey enfeebled the travelers. = 漫长的旅程使旅客们虚弱。

现在完成时:
Years of hard work have enfeebled him. = 多年的辛勤工作使他虚弱了。
The drought has enfeebled the crops. = 干旱使庄稼枯萎了。

过去完成时:
He had been enfeebled by illness before the race. = 他在比赛前就已经被疾病折磨得虚弱了。
The prolonged war had enfeebled the nation's economy. = 长期战争削弱了国家的经济。

虚拟语气:

否定句:

词源:
The word "enfeeble" comes from the Old French word "enfeeblir," which is a combination of the prefix "en-" (meaning "in" or "on") and "feble" (meaning "weak").  Therefore, it literally means "to make weak."
