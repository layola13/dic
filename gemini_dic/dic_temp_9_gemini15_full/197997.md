shock:/ʃɑːk/ /ʃɒk/| n., v., adj.|震惊；休克；电击；令人震惊的事物；使震惊；使休克；电击|

英文解析:
As a noun, "shock" refers to a sudden, upsetting, or surprising experience.  It can also refer to a sudden, violent physical reaction (like electric shock) or a medical condition (shock). As a verb, "shock" means to cause someone to feel surprise or distress, or to administer an electric shock. As an adjective, "shock" describes something that is surprising or upsetting.

例句：
The news of his death was a terrible shock.  他的死讯令人震惊。

The accident left him in a state of shock.  这场事故使他处于休克状态。

The doctor used a defibrillator to shock the patient's heart.  医生使用除颤器电击病人的心脏。

The color of the dress was a bit of a shock.  这件衣服的颜色有点令人吃惊。

近义词：
surprise, astonishment, stun, daze, trauma, jolt


反义词：
calm, peace, tranquility, composure


词性：
noun, verb, adjective
