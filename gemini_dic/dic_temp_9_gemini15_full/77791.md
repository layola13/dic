generalities:/ˌdʒɛnəˈrælɪtiz/ | n. |概论；一般性陈述；笼统说法 |General statements or observations that lack detail or specific information.  They often oversimplify complex issues. |例句：He spoke in generalities, avoiding specifics about the budget. 他泛泛而谈，回避了关于预算的细节。  |例句：Her analysis was full of generalities and lacked concrete evidence. 她的分析充满了概括性的说法，缺乏具体的证据。|近义词：vaguenesses, generalities, abstractions, broad statements |反义词： specifics, details, particulars

词源:
The word "generalities" comes from the French word "généralité," meaning "generality."  It ultimately derives from the Latin word "generalis," meaning "general,"  from "genus," meaning "kind" or "race."  The suffix "-ities" forms a plural noun.
