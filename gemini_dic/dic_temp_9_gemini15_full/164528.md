preesteem:/ˌpriːɪˈstiːm/| n.|预先尊重；先有敬意|Preesteem refers to respect or admiration felt beforehand, before any direct interaction or knowledge of a person or thing.  It's a relatively rare word, often found in academic contexts discussing social psychology or the formation of opinions.|例句：The preesteem he felt for the renowned professor influenced his decision to attend the lecture. 他对这位著名教授的预先尊重影响了他参加讲座的决定。

例句：Her preesteem for the historical figure led her to meticulously research their life and work. 她对这位历史人物的预先敬意促使她一丝不苟地研究了他们的一生和作品。|近义词：anticipation, prior respect, preconceived admiration|反义词：prejudice, preconceived dislike, disdain|词性:名词
