piloting:/ˈpaɪlətɪŋ/| v.|驾驶；试运行；引导|The act of steering or controlling a vehicle, especially an aircraft or ship; the process of testing or operating something new or experimental before full-scale launch.|例句：He is piloting a new software program. 他正在试运行一个新的软件程序。|例句：The captain expertly piloted the ship through the storm. 船长熟练地驾驶着船只穿过暴风雨。|近义词：flying, navigating, guiding, testing, experimenting|反义词：None specifically, but antonyms could be related to inaction or failure.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pilots the plane every day. = 他每天驾驶飞机。
She is piloting a new marketing strategy. = 她正在试运行一种新的市场营销策略。

一般过去时:
He piloted the ship safely into port. = 他安全地驾驶船只驶入港口。
They piloted the new system for a month before launch. = 他们在新系统正式发布前试运行了一个月。

现在完成时:
She has piloted many different aircraft. = 她已经驾驶过许多不同类型的飞机。
He has piloted the project from its inception. = 他从项目开始就一直负责领导。

过去完成时:
The pilot had piloted many flights before the incident. = 事故发生前，这位飞行员已经驾驶过许多航班。
They had piloted the program successfully in several smaller cities. = 他们在几个较小的城市成功试运行了该项目。

将来时:
He will pilot the spacecraft to Mars. = 他将驾驶宇宙飞船前往火星。
They will pilot the new software next quarter. = 他们将在下个季度试运行新软件。

虚拟语气:
If he piloted the plane correctly, the landing would have been smoother. = 如果他正确驾驶飞机，着陆就会更平稳。
I wish I could pilot a helicopter. = 我真希望我能驾驶直升机。

被动语态:
The plane was piloted by a veteran pilot. = 这架飞机由一名经验丰富的飞行员驾驶。
The new system is being piloted in three different locations. = 新系统正在三个不同的地点进行试运行。

疑问句:
Who piloted the boat? = 谁驾驶的船？
Is the new software being piloted successfully? = 新软件的试运行是否成功？

否定句:
He did not pilot the plane successfully. = 他没有成功驾驶飞机。
The new system was not piloted extensively before release. = 新系统在发布前没有进行广泛的试运行。
