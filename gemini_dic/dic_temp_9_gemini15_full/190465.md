sassenach:/ˈsæsənæk/| n.|苏格兰盖尔语中的外乡人，外国人|A term used in Scotland, originally meaning "Saxon" or "Englishman", now often used to refer to an outsider or foreigner, especially an English person in Scotland.  The word carries a slightly negative connotation, implying unfamiliarity with Scottish culture and customs.|例句：The Highlander glared at the sassenach trespassing on his land.  = 高地人怒视着侵犯他土地的外国人。 He was considered a sassenach by the locals because he spoke no Gaelic. = 因为他不会说盖尔语，当地人认为他是外乡人。|近义词：foreigner, outsider, stranger|反义词：native, local
