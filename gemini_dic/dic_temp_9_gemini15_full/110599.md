limit:/ˈlɪmɪt/ | n. v. | 限制；限度；界限 |  To restrict or confine within limits; the point beyond which something cannot proceed. |例句：The speed limit is 60 mph. = 限速60英里/小时。 There is a limit to what one person can do. = 人的能力是有限的。 |近义词：restriction, boundary, constraint |反义词：infinity, boundlessness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The speed limit limits drivers to 60mph. = 限速限制司机时速为60英里。
This rule limits the number of participants. = 这条规则限制了参与者的数量。


一般过去时:
The government limited the import of certain goods. = 政府限制了某些商品的进口。
They limited their spending to save money. = 他们限制开支以攒钱。


现在完成时:
We have limited our sugar intake. = 我们已经限制了糖的摄入量。
The drought has limited the crop yield this year. = 旱灾限制了今年的作物产量。


过去完成时:
They had limited their contact with outsiders. = 他们之前已经限制了与外界的接触。
The company had limited its losses by quick action. = 公司通过迅速行动将损失降到了最低。


将来时:
The new law will limit the power of the corporation. = 新法律将限制公司的权力。
We will limit our discussion to this topic. = 我们将把讨论限制在这个主题上。


虚拟语气:
If we limited our expenses, we would save more money. = 如果我们限制开支，就能省更多钱。
I wish I had limited my alcohol consumption. = 我真希望我之前能限制一下酒精摄入量。


被动语态:
The number of participants was limited to 100. = 参与人数被限制在100人以内。
Access to the building is limited by security measures. = 安全措施限制了进入大楼的权限。


疑问句:
What limits the growth of the economy? = 什么限制了经济的增长？
What are the limits of human endurance? = 人类的耐力极限是什么？


否定句:
There is no limit to his ambition. = 他的野心是无限的。
The possibilities are unlimited. = 可能性是无限的。


PHRASE->
limit of tolerance = 耐受极限
limit of liability = 责任限额
beyond the limit = 超过限制
within the limits of = 在...的范围内
place a limit on = 对...加以限制
