citadel: /ˈsɪtədɛl/ | n. |城堡；要塞；坚固的防御工事 | A citadel is a fortress, typically one that is situated on high ground and commands a city or other area.  It is a strong, fortified place, often used as a refuge during times of war or siege.  The term often implies a historical significance and a sense of strength and impregnability. | 例句：The ancient citadel overlooked the entire valley. 这座古老的城堡俯瞰着整个山谷。

The citadel withstood the siege for months.  这座城堡抵御了几个月的围攻。 | 近义词：fortress, stronghold, fortress, rampart | 反义词：open area, vulnerable spot |
