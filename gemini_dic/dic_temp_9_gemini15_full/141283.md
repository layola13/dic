open:/ˈoʊpən/ /ˈəʊpən/ | adj., v., n. | 开着的；公开的；开放的；打开；开放；开始 |


英文解析:
As an adjective, "open" means not closed or shut; accessible; available; frank and honest; willing to consider new ideas. As a verb, it means to make something accessible or available; to begin something; to start something. As a noun, it refers to an open space or area, or an opening.


例句:
The door is open.  门是开着的。

The meeting is open to the public.  会议向公众开放。


近义词:
adj.: unclosed, unlocked, accessible, available, frank, receptive
v.: begin, commence, start, initiate, unlock


反义词:
adj.: closed, shut, secret, private, closed-minded
v.: close, shut, end, finish


词性:
adj., v., n.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He opens the door every morning. 他每天早上开门。
The shop opens at 9 am. 商店早上九点开门。


一般过去时:
She opened the present carefully. 她小心翼翼地打开了礼物。
They opened a new restaurant last week. 他们上周开了一家新餐馆。


现在完成时:
I have opened the window. 我已经打开了窗户。
They have opened three new branches this year. 他们今年已经开了三个新分店。


过去完成时:
He had opened the letter before he went to bed. 他睡觉前已经打开了信。
We had opened the champagne before the guests arrived. 客人到达之前，我们已经开了香槟。


将来时:
I will open the gift later. 我稍后会打开礼物。
The museum will open next month. 博物馆下个月开业。


虚拟语气:
If I had the key, I would open the door. 如果我有钥匙，我会开门。
I wish I could open this jar. 我真希望我能打开这个罐子。


被动语态:

The new store will be opened tomorrow. 新店明天开业。


疑问句:

否定句:

The door won't open. 门打不开。
