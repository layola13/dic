depart:/dɪˈpɑːrt/|/dɪˈpɑːrt/| vt. vi. n.|离开，出发；去世；背离|To leave; to go away from a place.  It can also refer to death or a deviation from a standard or principle. |例句：The train departs at 8:00 a.m. = 火车早上8点出发。

The old man departed peacefully in his sleep. = 老人安详地睡梦中去世了。

近义词：leave, go, set off, depart from(背离)|反义词：arrive, return, come back|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The train departs at noon. = 火车中午出发。
He departs for London tomorrow. = 他明天动身去伦敦。


一般过去时:
They departed from the airport yesterday. = 他们昨天从机场出发了。
She departed this life last week. = 她上周去世了。


现在完成时:
The plane has already departed. = 飞机已经起飞了。
He has departed from his original plan. = 他已经偏离了他最初的计划。


过去完成时:
The train had already departed before we arrived at the station. = 我们到达车站之前，火车已经开走了。
They had departed from the agreed-upon terms. = 他们已经违反了商定的条款。


将来时:
We will depart for Paris next month. = 我们下个月将动身去巴黎。
The flight will depart on time. = 飞机将会准时起飞。


虚拟语气:
If he had departed earlier, he would have avoided the traffic. = 如果他早点出发，他就能避开交通堵塞了。
I wish I had departed sooner. = 我真希望我早点离开。


被动语态:
The meeting was departed from due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，会议被取消了。 (Note: Passive voice is less common with "depart".)
The decision was departed from by the committee. = 委员会没有采纳这个决定。(Note: This implies a deviation, not a physical departure.)


疑问句:
When does the bus depart? = 公共汽车几点出发？
Has the train departed yet? = 火车已经出发了吗？


否定句:
The plane did not depart on time. = 飞机没有准时起飞。
We did not depart from our original plan. = 我们没有偏离我们的最初计划。


PHRASE->
depart from: 偏离，背离
depart this life: 去世，死亡
depart for: 动身前往
on departure: 起飞时，出发时
safe departure: 平安出发


词源:
The word "depart" comes from the Old French word "departir," which in turn comes from the Latin word "departire," meaning "to divide," "to share," or "to separate."  The modern English meaning evolved from the sense of separating oneself from a place or situation.
