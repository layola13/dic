disenfranchise: /ˌdɪsɪnˈfrænˌtʃaɪz/ | vt. |剥夺…的权利；使失去公民权 | To deprive someone of the right to vote or participate in political processes.  |The new law disenfranchised many poor people.  新法律剥夺了许多穷人的投票权。  |近义词：disqualify, deprive, disenable |反义词：enfranchise, empower


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government disenfrancises voters through restrictive laws. = 政府通过限制性法律剥夺选民的投票权。
This policy disenfrancises a significant portion of the population. =这项政策剥夺了很大一部分人口的权利。


一般过去时:
The poll tax disenfranchised many working-class citizens. =人头税剥夺了许多工薪阶层公民的权利。
They were disenfranchised because of their criminal records. = 他们因为犯罪记录而被剥夺了权利。


现在完成时:

The new voting restrictions have disenfranchised thousands of people. =新的投票限制已经剥夺了成千上万人的权利。


过去完成时:
Before the Civil Rights Act, many African Americans had been disenfranchised. =在民权法案之前，许多非洲裔美国人已经被剥夺了权利。
They had been disenfranchised for years before the law was changed. = 在法律改变之前，他们已经被剥夺权利多年了。
