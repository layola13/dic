paleothere:/ˈpeɪlioʊθɪər/| n. |古兽；始新马|An extinct perissodactyl mammal of the Eocene and Oligocene epochs, related to horses and rhinoceroses.  Paleotheres were herbivores with long necks and relatively long legs. They were diverse in size and form, some resembling tapirs. |The fossil remains of paleothere have been found in Europe and Asia.  古兽的化石遗骸在欧洲和亚洲都有发现。|近义词：extinct mammal, perissodactyl|反义词：modern mammal
