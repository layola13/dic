rebuilded:/ˌriːˈbɪldɪd/ | v. |重建的，改建的 | The past participle of "rebuild," indicating something has been rebuilt.  It describes a state or result after a process of reconstruction. |例句：The bridge, rebuilded after the storm, is stronger than before.  暴风雨后重建的桥比以前更坚固了。 The city, rebuilded after the war, now thrives. 战后重建的城市现在欣欣向荣。 |近义词：reconstructed, renovated, restored |反义词：destroyed, demolished, ruined |动词


USAGE->

They rebuild houses after natural disasters. = 他们在自然灾害后重建房屋。
We rebuild our strength after a long journey. = 我们在长途旅行后恢复体力。
