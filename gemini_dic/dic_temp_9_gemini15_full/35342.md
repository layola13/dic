co-determine:/ˌkoʊdɪˈtɜːrmɪn/| vt.|共同决定，协同决定|To jointly decide or influence the outcome of something.  It implies a shared responsibility and power in making a decision.|例句：The workers and management co-determined the company's future. 工人和管理层共同决定了公司的未来。例句：The government and the opposition party co-determined the new environmental policy. 政府和反对党共同决定了新的环境政策。|近义词：jointly decide, collaborate on, concur on|反义词：dictate, unilaterally decide|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The union and the employer co-determine the working conditions. = 工会和雇主共同决定工作条件。
They co-determine the project's budget every year. = 他们每年共同决定项目的预算。


一般过去时:
The committee co-determined the winner of the competition. = 委员会共同决定了比赛的获胜者。
Last year, they co-determined the school's curriculum. = 去年，他们共同决定了学校的课程。


现在完成时:
They have co-determined the strategies for the next five years. = 他们已经共同决定了未来五年的策略。
The board has co-determined the company's new direction. = 董事会已经共同决定了公司的新方向。


过去完成时:
Before the meeting, they had co-determined the main points of discussion. = 会议之前，他们已经共同决定了讨论的主要内容。
They had co-determined the allocation of resources before the project began. = 项目开始之前，他们已经共同决定了资源的分配。


将来时:
We will co-determine the next steps in the investigation. = 我们将共同决定调查的下一步措施。
They will co-determine the design of the new building. = 他们将共同决定新建筑的设计。


虚拟语气:
If they co-determined the outcome, it would be fairer. = 如果他们共同决定结果，那将会更公平。
I wish we had co-determined the budget earlier. = 我希望我们早点共同决定预算。


被动语态:
The final decision was co-determined by both parties. = 最终决定是由双方共同决定的。
The company's policy is co-determined by the employees and the management. = 公司的政策是由员工和管理层共同决定的。


疑问句:
How will the project's success be co-determined? = 项目的成功将如何共同决定？
Who co-determined the terms of the agreement? = 谁共同决定了协议的条款？


否定句:
They did not co-determine the final result. = 他们没有共同决定最终结果。
The two parties could not co-determine the best solution. = 双方无法共同决定最佳方案。
