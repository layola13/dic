paleocrinoidea:/ˌpeɪliːoʊkraɪˈnɔɪdiə/| n. |古海百合类|Paleocrinoidea is a subclass of extinct crinoids, a group of marine animals that resemble lilies.  They were abundant in Paleozoic seas and are important index fossils. |The paleocrinoidea flourished during the Paleozoic Era.  古海百合类在古生代繁盛。|近义词: (None precisely equivalent; related terms include crinoids, echinoderms)|反义词: (None directly opposite)|
