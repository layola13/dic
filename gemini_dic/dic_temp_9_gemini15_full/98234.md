insensibilities:/ˌɪnsɛnsəˈbɪlətiz/| n.| 感觉迟钝；麻木；无情；无知觉|A lack of feeling, emotion, or sensitivity; the state of being insensitive.  It can refer to a lack of physical sensation or a lack of emotional responsiveness.  The plural form indicates multiple instances of insensitivity or lack of feeling.|例句：His insensibilities to the suffering of others shocked everyone.  他的冷漠无情震惊了所有人。

例句：The medication caused a temporary loss of feeling in his fingertips, a kind of insensibilities.  药物导致了他的指尖暂时失去知觉，一种麻木。


近义词：apathy, callousness, insensitivity, numbness, unawareness

反义词：sensitivity, empathy, compassion, responsiveness


词性：名词


词源:  The word "insensibilities" derives from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") and the word "sensibilities," which itself comes from the Latin word "sensibilis" (meaning "capable of feeling").  Therefore, "insensibilities" literally means "not capable of feeling."
