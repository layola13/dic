cripple:/ˈkrɪpəl/ | n., v. | 跛子；残废的人；使残废；严重削弱 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "cripple" refers to a person who is unable to walk normally because of a physical disability.  As a verb, it means to severely damage or impair something, often leaving it ineffective or useless.  The word carries a strong negative connotation and is considered by many to be offensive.  More sensitive alternatives are generally preferred.

例句:
Noun: He was a cripple from birth. = 他天生就是个跛子。

Verb: The storm crippled the city's power grid. = 暴风雨严重破坏了城市的电力网。


近义词:
Noun: invalid, disabled person, handicapped person
Verb: disable, impair, incapacitate, weaken, paralyze


反义词:
Noun: able-bodied person
Verb: strengthen, enable, rehabilitate


词性:
Noun, Verb
