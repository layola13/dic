lamprey: /ˈlæmpri/ | n. |七鳃鳗，八目鳗 | A jawless fish with a sucking mouth, parasitic on other fish.  Lampreys are ancient creatures, existing for over 300 million years. They are characterized by their eel-like body, circular sucking mouth, and numerous gill openings.  | Example: The river is known for its population of sea lampreys. 这条河以其大量的海七鳃鳗而闻名。 | 近义词:hagfish (though not strictly a lamprey, it shares some similar characteristics) | 反义词: None truly applicable.
