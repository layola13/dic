jagging:/ˈdʒæɡɪŋ/| n. adj. v.| 锯齿状；不规则；使成锯齿状 |  A sharp, uneven edge or surface; the act of making something jagged.  |例句：The mountain range had a jagged skyline.  那座山脉的天际线是锯齿状的。


The rough, uneven surface of the broken glass was quite jarring.破碎玻璃的粗糙不平的表面非常令人震惊。


近义词：serrated, notched, ragged, irregular | 反义词：smooth, even, regular | 词性: noun, adjective, verb

词源:

The word "jagging" comes from the Middle English word "jaggen," meaning "to prick or pierce."  This, in turn, likely derives from a Proto-Germanic root. The association with uneven, sharp points developed naturally from the image of piercing or pricking.
