/pætrənəˈmɑːjəlɒdʒi/ | n. |  патронимия学 | The study of patronymics (surnames derived from father's name).  The field explores the origins, evolution, and cultural significance of surnames based on paternal lineage. It involves analyzing linguistic patterns, historical records, and genealogical data to understand how patronymics reflect societal structures and kinship systems.  | Example: Patronymology can help trace family histories and migrations across generations.  | 近义词: onomastics, genealogy | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but opposing fields might include matrilineal studies focusing on mother's lineage) |
