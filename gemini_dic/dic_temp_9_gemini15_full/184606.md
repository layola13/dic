restrain:/rɪˈstreɪn/| vt. |抑制，克制，约束|To prevent (someone or something) from doing something; to hold back or control (a feeling or impulse).|例句：He struggled to restrain his anger. 他努力克制住怒火。|例句：The police restrained the suspect. 警察控制住了嫌疑犯。|近义词：control, suppress, curb, check |反义词：unleash, release, liberate

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government restrains inflation through monetary policy. = 政府通过货币政策抑制通货膨胀。
She restrains herself from eating too much junk food. = 她克制自己不吃太多垃圾食品。

一般过去时:
He restrained his laughter during the serious meeting. = 在严肃的会议上，他忍住了笑。
The parents restrained their child from running into the street. = 父母阻止孩子跑到街上。

现在完成时:
She has restrained herself from speaking out against her boss. = 她一直克制自己不公开反对她的老板。
They have restrained the spread of the virus through quarantine measures. = 他们通过隔离措施阻止了病毒的传播。

过去完成时:
He had restrained his emotions until the end of the ceremony. = 直到仪式结束，他才控制住自己的情绪。
The police had restrained the crowd before the violence escalated. = 警察在暴力升级之前控制住了人群。

将来时:
They will restrain the prisoner with handcuffs. = 他们将用锁链铐住囚犯。
The doctor will restrain the patient to prevent self-harm. = 医生将约束病人以防止自残。

虚拟语气:
If only I could restrain my impulsive nature. = 要是我能克制我的冲动就好了。
I wish I had restrained myself from saying that. = 我真希望自己当时没有说那些话。

被动语态:
His anger was restrained by his sense of duty. = 他的愤怒被他的责任感所抑制。
The protesters were restrained by the police. = 抗议者被警察控制住了。

疑问句:
Can you restrain your dog from barking? = 你能阻止你的狗叫吗？
How can we restrain the spread of misinformation? = 我们如何才能阻止虚假信息的传播？

否定句:
He couldn't restrain his tears. = 他忍不住哭了。
She didn't restrain herself from expressing her opinions. = 她没有克制自己表达意见。
