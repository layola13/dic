interweaving:/ˌɪntərˈwiːvɪŋ/ | v. n. adj. | 交织，编织；互相交织的事物 | The act of weaving together; a combination of things that are closely connected or intertwined.  |例句：The story is a complex interweaving of fact and fiction. 故事是事实与虚构的复杂交织。


The threads were interweaving beautifully. 线织得非常漂亮。


近义词：intertwining, blending, combining | 反义词：separating, disentangling


词性: Verb (v.), Noun (n.), Adjective (adj.)


词源:  The word "interweaving" comes from the prefix "inter-" (meaning "between" or "among") and the verb "weave," which has Old English roots ("wefan").  The meaning thus developed as the act of weaving things together, and then extended metaphorically to describe the close connection or intertwining of different elements.
