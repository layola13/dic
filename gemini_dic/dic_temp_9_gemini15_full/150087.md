paludamentum: /ˌpæljuˈdæmənˌtəm/ | n. | 军袍，将军披风 | A large, military cloak worn by Roman generals and other high-ranking officers.  It was typically purple or scarlet and often richly decorated. | The general marched proudly, his paludamentum billowing behind him.  将军骄傲地行进着，他的军袍在身后飘动。|  cloak, mantle |  (no direct antonym, but could be contrasted with simpler garments like a toga) | 名词
