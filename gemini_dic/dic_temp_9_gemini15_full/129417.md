nominating:/ˈnɑːmɪneɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 提名，推荐 |  The act of proposing or suggesting someone for a position, award, or honor.  It implies a formal process of selection. | Example:  The committee is currently nominating candidates for the vacant position.  委员会目前正在提名空缺职位的候选人。


Example: The party is nominating a strong candidate for the upcoming election. 该党正在为即将到来的选举提名一位强大的候选人。


近义词: proposing, suggesting, recommending


反义词: rejecting, dismissing, disqualifying


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are nominating a new CEO. = 他们正在提名一位新的首席执行官。
We nominate candidates every year. = 我们每年都提名候选人。

一般过去时:
She nominated him for the award. = 她提名他获得该奖项。
The board nominated three candidates. = 董事会提名了三位候选人。

现在完成时:
They have nominated several people for the job. = 他们已经提名了几个人担任这个职位。
The committee has already nominated its choice. = 委员会已经提名了他们的选择。


过去完成时:
They had nominated him before the deadline. = 他们已在截止日期前提名了他。
The school had nominated her for the scholarship. = 学校已经提名她获得奖学金。


将来时:
They will nominate a new president next week. = 他们下周将提名一位新总统。
We will be nominating candidates soon. = 我们很快就会提名候选人。


虚拟语气:
If I were to nominate someone, I would choose her. = 如果我要提名某人，我会选择她。
I wish I could nominate him, but I can't. = 我希望我能提名他，但我不能。


被动语态:
He was nominated for an Oscar. = 他被提名为奥斯卡奖候选人。
Several candidates were nominated for the position.  = 几位候选人被提名为该职位。


疑问句:
Who did you nominate? = 你提名谁了？
Will you be nominating anyone for the prize? = 你会提名任何人获得该奖项吗？


否定句:
They did not nominate her for the job. = 他们没有提名她担任这份工作。
I won't be nominating anyone this year. = 我今年不会提名任何人。


PHRASE->
nominate someone for something: 提名某人获得某物
self-nominate: 自我提名
nominate a candidate: 提名候选人
put up for nomination: 提出候选人
nominating committee: 提名委员会
