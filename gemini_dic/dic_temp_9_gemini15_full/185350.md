retroactive:/ˌrɛtroˈæktɪv/| adj.|追溯的，溯及既往的|Having effect from a date before the one on which it is officially put into force.|The new law is retroactive to January 1st. 这项新法律追溯到1月1日生效。|近义词：backdated, retrospective|反义词：prospective


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rule is retroactive. = 这条规则是追溯生效的。
This policy is retroactive to last year. =这项政策追溯到去年。


一般过去时:
The law was retroactive to the previous year. = 这项法律追溯到前一年。
The decision was retroactive, affecting past actions. = 这个决定是追溯生效的，影响过去的行动。


现在完成时:
The change has been retroactive. = 这一变化是追溯生效的。
The company has made a retroactive pay adjustment. = 公司已经进行了追溯性的工资调整。


过去完成时:
The rule had been retroactive for several months. = 这条规则已经追溯生效好几个月了。
They had not realized the policy was retroactive until it was too late. = 直到为时已晚，他们才意识到这项政策是追溯生效的。


将来时:
The new regulation will be retroactive to the beginning of the fiscal year. = 新规定将追溯到财政年度的开始。
The changes will be retroactive, affecting prior transactions. = 这些更改将具有追溯力，影响之前的交易。


虚拟语气:
If the ruling were retroactive, many would be affected. = 如果裁决具有追溯力，许多人将受到影响。
It would be unfair if the tax increase were retroactive. = 如果增税具有追溯力，那将是不公平的。


被动语态:
The payment was made retroactively. = 付款是追溯进行的。
The changes were applied retroactively to all previous accounts. = 这些更改已追溯应用于所有以前的帐户。


疑问句:
Is the new policy retroactive? = 新政策是追溯生效的吗？
Are these changes retroactive to the last quarter? = 这些更改是否追溯到上一季度？


否定句:
The new law is not retroactive. = 新法律不具有追溯力。
The regulation is not retroactive; it only applies to future events. = 该规章没有追溯力；它仅适用于未来的事件。
