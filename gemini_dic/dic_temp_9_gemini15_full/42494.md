counterimages:/ˈkaʊntərɪmɪdʒɪz/| n. |反图像；对立形象；对照物|Images that oppose or contrast with other images, often used to challenge or subvert dominant narratives or ideologies.  They can be literal visual representations or metaphorical concepts.  Counterimages are used to create a dialectical tension, forcing viewers to reconsider their assumptions and perspectives.|Example: The artist used counterimages of idyllic landscapes juxtaposed with scenes of urban decay to critique the romanticized vision of progress.  艺术家用田园风光的反图像与城市衰败的景象并置，来批判对进步的浪漫化愿景。|近义词：antithesis, opposite, contrast, foil|反义词：reinforcement, confirmation, support|名词
