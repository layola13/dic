coccyx:/ˈkɒksɪks/|n.|尾骨|The coccyx is the small, triangular bone at the base of the spine, formed from the fusion of several rudimentary vertebrae. It is also known as the tailbone.  |例句：The fall caused a coccyx injury.  摔倒导致尾骨受伤。

例句：She experienced persistent pain in her coccyx after the accident. 事故后，她尾骨持续疼痛。


近义词：tailbone


反义词：无


词性：名词

Etymology:

The word "coccyx" comes from the Greek word κόκκυξ (kokkyx), meaning "cuckoo". This is because the shape of the bone resembles the beak of a cuckoo bird.  The anatomical term was adopted into Latin and then into English.
