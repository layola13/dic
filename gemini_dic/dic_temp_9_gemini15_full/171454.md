puff-out:/pʌf aʊt/| vt.| 鼓起；使膨胀；炫耀；|to swell or expand suddenly, often with a feeling of pride or arrogance.  It can also refer to expelling air forcefully.|例句：He puffed out his chest with pride. 他骄傲地挺起了胸膛。
The balloon puffed out when it was inflated. 气球充气后鼓了起来。|近义词：inflate, swell, expand, boast|反义词：deflate, shrink, contract


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He puffs out his cheeks when he's angry. = 他生气时会鼓起腮帮子。
The baker puffs out the dough before baking. = 面包师在烘焙前会把面团鼓起来。


一般过去时:
She puffed out her dress to show off the embroidery. = 她把裙子撑起来炫耀刺绣。
The tire puffed out after the puncture. = 轮胎扎破后鼓了起来。


现在完成时:
He has puffed out his chest all day, boasting about his promotion. = 他一整天都挺着胸脯，吹嘘他的升职。
The frog has puffed out its throat to attract a mate. = 青蛙鼓起喉咙来吸引配偶。


过去完成时:
The balloon had puffed out before I could tie it. = 我还没来得及系上气球，它就已经鼓起来了。
She had puffed out her resume, exaggerating her accomplishments. = 她夸大了自己的成就，把简历吹嘘得很厉害。


将来时:
The hot air balloon will puff out as the burner heats the air. = 热气球的燃烧器加热空气后，气球就会鼓起来。
He will puff out his chest when he receives the award. = 他领奖的时候会挺起胸膛。


虚拟语气:
If he puffed out his chest any more, he'd burst. = 如果他再把胸挺得更直，他会爆掉的。
I wish I could puff out my skills as easily as he does. = 我希望我能像他一样轻松地炫耀我的技能。


被动语态:
The sails were puffed out by the strong wind. = 强风把帆鼓了起来。
His claims were puffed out, making them sound more impressive. = 他的说法被夸大，听起来更令人印象深刻。


疑问句:

否定句:
The tire didn't puff out as expected. = 轮胎没有像预期那样鼓起来。
