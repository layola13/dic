disendow: /ˌdɪsɪnˈdaʊ/ | vt. |剥夺；取消…的权利或特权 | To deprive someone or something of an endowment or privilege, especially a right, property, or position. |例句：The university was disendowed of its substantial land holdings.  大学被剥夺了大量的土地所有权。

The government disendowed the hospital, leading to staff cuts.  政府取消了医院的拨款，导致人员精简。

近义词：deprive, divest, strip

反义词：endow, grant, bestow

词源:
The word "disendow" is formed by adding the prefix "dis-" (meaning "removal of" or "opposite of") to the verb "endow," which means "to provide with a gift or talent."  Therefore, "disendow" literally means "to remove an endowment" or "to deprive of an endowment."
