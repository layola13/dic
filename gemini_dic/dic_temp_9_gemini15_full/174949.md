radiated:/ˈreɪdieɪtɪd/ | v. | 发射；辐射；散发；流露 | to send out energy, heat, or light in rays; to spread out from a central point | The sun radiated heat.  太阳散发热量。

The fire radiated warmth. 火散发温暖。

近义词：emit, emanate, diffuse, exude | 反义词：absorb

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun radiates heat and light.  太阳辐射热和光。
The heater radiates warmth into the room.  暖气机向房间散发温暖。


一般过去时:
The star radiated energy across the galaxy.  恒星向整个星系辐射能量。
She radiated confidence during her presentation.  她在演讲中散发自信。


现在完成时:
The radioactive material has radiated harmful rays for years.  放射性物质多年来一直辐射有害射线。
His happiness has radiated outwards to everyone around him.  他的快乐感染了周围所有人。


过去完成时:
The reactor had radiated dangerous levels of radiation before the shutdown.  反应堆在关闭之前已辐射出危险等级的辐射。
Before the accident, the plant had radiated only low levels of radiation. 事故发生前，工厂只辐射低水平的辐射。


将来时:

They expect the source will radiate for many years to come.  他们预计该光源将在未来许多年内持续辐射。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:
