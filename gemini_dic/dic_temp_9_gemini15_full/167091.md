presuppositions:/ˌpriːˌsʌpəˈzɪʃənz/| n. |预设，预想，预先假定 |Things that are assumed to be true before something else can be considered; underlying assumptions.  These are often implicit and unstated. For example, the question "When did you stop beating your wife?" presupposes that the person did beat their wife. |The success of the project presupposes the cooperation of all team members.  这个项目的成功预设了所有团队成员的合作。

The article's argument presupposes a familiarity with economic theory. 这篇文章的论点预设了读者熟悉经济理论。|近义词：assumptions, preconditions, implications |反义词：facts, realities
