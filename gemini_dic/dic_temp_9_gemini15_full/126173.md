mutually:/ˈmjuːtʃuəli/| adv. |相互地，彼此地 |Reciprocally; in a way that is felt or done by each of two or more parties toward the other or others.|The two countries mutually agreed to reduce their military forces.|近义词：reciprocally, jointly, together |反义词：unilaterally, independently


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They mutually respect each other. = 他们互相尊重。
We mutually support each other's projects. = 我们互相支持彼此的项目。


一般过去时:
The two companies mutually benefited from the merger. = 这两家公司从合并中互惠互利。
The siblings mutually agreed to divide the inheritance equally. = 兄弟姐妹们互相同意平均分配遗产。


现在完成时:
They have mutually helped each other throughout their careers. = 他们职业生涯中一直互相帮助。
The nations have mutually pledged to cooperate on climate change. = 各国已互相承诺在气候变化问题上进行合作。


过去完成时:
They had mutually decided to end their partnership before the disagreement escalated. = 在分歧升级之前，他们已经互相决定结束合作关系。
The friends had mutually promised to keep each other's secrets. = 朋友们互相承诺保守彼此的秘密。


将来时:
They will mutually assist each other during the difficult times ahead. = 他们将在未来的艰难时期互相帮助。
The countries will mutually benefit from the new trade agreement. = 各国将从新的贸易协定中互惠互利。


虚拟语气:
If they mutually agreed, the project could be completed on time. = 如果他们互相同意，项目就能按时完成。
I wish they had mutually understood each other's perspectives. = 我希望他们能互相理解彼此的观点。


被动语态:
The benefits were mutually shared by all participants. = 所有参与者都互相分享了利益。
The responsibilities were mutually assigned to the team members. = 责任被互相分配给团队成员。


疑问句:
Are they mutually exclusive options? = 他们是互相排斥的选择吗？
Do they mutually understand the terms of the contract? = 他们互相理解合同条款吗？


否定句:
Their goals are not mutually exclusive. = 他们的目标并不互相排斥。
The two statements are not mutually consistent. = 这两个陈述并不互相一致。


PHRASE->
mutually beneficial = 互利的
mutually exclusive = 互相排斥的
mutually agreed = 互相同意
mutually supportive = 互相支持的
mutually assured destruction (MAD) = 互相保证毁灭


词源:
From Middle English *mutuelly, from Old French *mutuellement, from Latin *mutuālimēnte, from mutuālis ("mutual").  The Latin root *mutuālis ultimately derives from *mutuus ("exchanged," "borrowed"), from the Proto-Indo-European root *mew- ("to exchange").
