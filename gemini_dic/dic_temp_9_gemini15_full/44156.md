criticise: /ˈkrɪtɪsaɪz/ | vt. | 批评，批判 | To express disapproval of someone or something.  |例句：The critics criticised his latest novel for its lack of originality. 批评家批评了他的最新小说缺乏原创性。
例句：She criticised him for being late. 她批评他迟到。|近义词：condemn, rebuke, censure, fault | 反义词：praise, commend, applaud | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He criticises my work regularly. = 他经常批评我的工作。
They criticise the government's policies. = 他们批评政府的政策。

一般过去时:
She criticised his performance. = 她批评了他的表演。
The teacher criticised the student's essay. = 老师批评了学生的论文。

现在完成时:
He has criticised the plan several times. = 他已经多次批评了这个计划。
They have criticised her behaviour. = 他们已经批评了她的行为。

过去完成时:
I had criticised his ideas before he presented them. = 在他提出他的想法之前，我已经批评过了。
She had criticised the proposal, but no one listened. = 她已经批评了这个提案，但没有人听。

将来时:
He will criticise the new design. = 他将批评新的设计。
They will criticise the decision tomorrow. = 他们明天将批评这个决定。

虚拟语气:
If he criticised her, she would be upset. = 如果他批评她，她会不高兴的。
I wish he hadn't criticised my work so harshly. = 我真希望他没有那么严厉地批评我的工作。

被动语态:
His work was criticised by many people. = 他的作品受到了许多人的批评。
The plan was criticised for its lack of detail. = 该计划因缺乏细节而受到批评。

疑问句:
Did she criticise his approach? = 她批评了他的方法吗？
Why did they criticise the movie? = 他们为什么批评这部电影？

否定句:
He didn't criticise her work. = 他没有批评她的作品。
They didn't criticise the new policy. = 他们没有批评新政策。


PHRASE->

criticise constructively = 建设性地批评
criticise severely = 严重批评
criticise unfairly = 不公平地批评
self-criticise = 自我批评
