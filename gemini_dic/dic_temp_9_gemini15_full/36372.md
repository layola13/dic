coiner:/ˈkɔɪnər/ | n. |造币者；发明者；创造者 | A person who invents or introduces a new word or phrase.  | Example:  Shakespeare is considered a coiner of many words still in use today.  莎士比亚被认为是许多仍在使用的单词的发明者。 |近义词: inventor, creator, innovator |反义词:  None specifically, but antonyms could relate to those who destroy or erase words.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He coins new words for his blog. = 他为他的博客创造新词。
The mint coins money for the government. = 造币厂为政府铸造货币。


一般过去时:
She coined a new term for the scientific discovery. = 她为这项科学发现创造了一个新术语。
They coined a catchy slogan for the advertising campaign. = 他们为广告宣传活动创造了一个朗朗上口的标语。


现在完成时:
He has coined several memorable phrases throughout his career. = 他在他的职业生涯中创造了几个令人难忘的短语。
The company has coined a new brand name to attract younger customers. = 公司创造了一个新的品牌名称来吸引年轻客户。


过去完成时:
Before the presentation, she had coined a clever title for her project. = 在演示之前，她已经为她的项目想出了一个巧妙的标题。
They had coined the term "blog" long before it became popular. = 他们在“博客”这个词流行之前很久就已经创造了这个词。


将来时:
She will coin a new phrase to describe the situation. = 她将创造一个新的短语来描述这种情况。
The team will coin a name for their new product. = 团队将为他们的新产品命名。


虚拟语气:
If he coined a better name, the product might sell better. = 如果他取个更好的名字，产品可能会卖得更好。
I wish I had coined that clever phrase myself. = 我真希望我自己能想出那个巧妙的短语。


被动语态:
A new term was coined to describe the phenomenon. = 一个新的术语被创造出来描述这种现象。
The slogan was coined by a marketing team. = 这个标语是由一个营销团队创造的。


疑问句:
Who coined the term "selfie"? = 谁创造了“自拍”这个词？
What new word has she coined recently? = 她最近创造了什么新词？


否定句:
He didn't coin that phrase; it's been around for years. = 他并没有创造那个短语；它已经存在很多年了。
They didn't coin the term successfully; it didn't catch on. = 他们没有成功地创造出这个词；它没有流行起来。
