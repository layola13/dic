cross-checks:/ˈkrɒsˌtʃɛks/ | vt. n. |交叉检查；相互核对 | To verify information or data by comparing it with other sources or methods.  It involves checking one piece of information against another to ensure accuracy and consistency. As a noun, it refers to the act of performing this verification. |例句：The accountant cross-checked the figures to ensure accuracy. 会计交叉核对数字以确保准确性。

The team conducted thorough cross-checks before submitting the report. 团队在提交报告之前进行了彻底的交叉检查。


近义词：verify, check, confirm, corroborate | 反义词：neglect, disregard, overlook | 词性: verb & noun

词源:
The word "cross-check" is a compound word.  "Cross" means to go across or intersect, and "check" means to examine or verify.  Therefore, "cross-check" literally means to check something by comparing it to something else.  Its usage emerged naturally from the combination of these two common verbs.
