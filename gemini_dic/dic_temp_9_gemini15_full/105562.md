kin:/kɪn/| n.|亲属，亲戚|Relatives; family; people related by blood or marriage.  The word often implies close family ties.|My kin are scattered across the country.|近义词：relatives, family, folk, relations|反义词：strangers, outsiders|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My kin live nearby. = 我的亲戚住在附近。
His kin are coming for the holiday. = 他的亲戚要来过节了。


一般过去时:
We visited our kin last summer. = 我们去年夏天拜访了我们的亲戚。
Her kin helped her move house. = 她的亲戚帮她搬家。


现在完成时:
I haven't seen my kin in years. = 我多年没见过我的亲戚了。
They have supported their kin through difficult times. = 他们在困难时期一直支持着他们的亲戚。


过去完成时:
I had already contacted my kin before the event. = 我在活动之前就已经联系了我的亲戚。
They had helped their kin before they needed help themselves. = 他们在需要帮助之前就帮助过他们的亲戚。


将来时:
I will visit my kin next month. = 我下个月将拜访我的亲戚。
We will invite our kin to the wedding. = 我们将邀请我们的亲戚参加婚礼。


虚拟语气:
If I had more kin, I would feel less lonely. = 如果我有更多的亲戚，我就不会感到那么孤单了。
I wish my kin were closer. = 我希望我的亲戚住得近一些。


被动语态:
The inheritance was divided among the kin. = 遗产被分给了亲属们。
The family history was carefully preserved by the kin. = 家族历史被亲属们小心地保存下来了。


疑问句:

Were there many kin at the reunion? = 团聚时有很多亲戚吗？


否定句:
