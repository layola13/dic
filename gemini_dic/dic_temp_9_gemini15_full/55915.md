divulger:/dɪˈvʌldʒər/| n. |泄密者；揭露者 |A divulger is someone who reveals secrets or confidential information.  |例句：The journalist was accused of being a divulger of state secrets.  记者被指控泄露国家机密。|近义词：revealer, informer, informant, betrayer |反义词：concealer, keeper of secrets |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He divulges company secrets. = 他泄露公司机密。
She divulges little about her personal life. = 她很少透露她的私生活。


一般过去时:
He divulged the truth to the police. = 他向警方透露了真相。
She divulged her plans to her friends. = 她向朋友们透露了她的计划。


现在完成时:
He has divulged too much information already. = 他已经泄露了太多的信息。
She has divulged nothing of importance. = 她什么重要的事情都没有透露。


过去完成时:
He had divulged the secret before the meeting. = 他在会议之前就泄露了秘密。
She had divulged her location to the kidnappers. = 她已经向绑匪透露了自己的位置。


将来时:
He will divulge the information soon. = 他很快就会泄露这些信息。
She will divulge the details in her next book. = 她将在她的下一本书中透露细节。


虚拟语气:
If he divulged the truth, he would be in trouble. = 如果他泄露真相，他就会有麻烦。
I wish she had not divulged my secret. = 我真希望她没有泄露我的秘密。


被动语态:
The information was divulged to the press. = 信息被泄露给了媒体。
The secret was divulged by a disgruntled employee. = 这个秘密被一位不满的员工泄露了。


疑问句:
Who divulged the news to the public? = 谁把这个消息泄露给了公众？
Will he divulge his sources? = 他会透露他的消息来源吗？


否定句:
He did not divulge any sensitive information. = 他没有泄露任何敏感信息。
She refused to divulge the details of the case. = 她拒绝透露案件的细节。


PHRASE->
divulge a secret = 泄露秘密
divulge information = 透露信息
