reinflations:/ˌriːɪnˈfleɪʃənz/| n. |再通货膨胀，再次通货膨胀|The process of restarting or renewing inflation after a period of deflation or low inflation.  Reinflations are often implemented by governments or central banks to stimulate economic growth. However, they can also lead to undesirable consequences such as higher prices and reduced purchasing power.|例句：The government's policies led to a period of reinflations, causing prices to rise sharply.|近义词：renewed inflation, inflationary resurgence|反义词：deflation, disinflation
