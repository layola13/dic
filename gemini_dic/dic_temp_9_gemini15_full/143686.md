outlay:/ˈaʊtleɪ/ | n. |支出，费用，开销 |The amount of money spent on something.  It refers to the initial investment or expenditure made for a particular purpose. |例句：The outlay for the new equipment was substantial. 新设备的支出相当可观。

例句：The initial outlay for the project was unexpectedly high.  这个项目的初始支出意外地高。


近义词：expenditure, expense, cost, investment |反义词：income, revenue, profit |词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The outlay for this project is significant. = 这个项目的支出很大。
Their monthly outlay on food is quite high. = 他们每月的伙食费很高。

一般过去时:
The outlay for the renovation was more than we anticipated. = 装修的费用比我们预期的要高。
Last year's marketing outlay proved to be effective. = 去年的市场推广费用证明是有效的。

过去完成时:

词源:
The word "outlay" comes from the Old English "utlēgan," which is a combination of "ūt" (out) and "lēgan" (to lay).  It literally meant "to lay out" in the sense of spending or expending.  The modern meaning evolved from this original sense of physically laying out money or resources.
