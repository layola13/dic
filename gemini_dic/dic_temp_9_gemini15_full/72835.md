folioing: /ˈfoʊliɪŋ/ | v. | 编号；装订成册；整理成卷宗 |  The act of numbering and arranging pages or leaves of a book or document, often in preparation for binding.  It also refers to the process of organizing documents into a portfolio or file. | 例句：The intern spent the afternoon folioing the research papers. 实习生一下午都在给研究论文编号并整理。

The editor carefully folioed the manuscript before sending it to the printer. 编辑在送印前仔细地给稿件编号。| 近义词：numbering, paging, indexing, filing | 反义词：disorganizing, scattering

词源:
From Middle English *folien, from Old French *folier, from Latin *folium ("leaf").  The original sense was related to the leaf-like pages of a book.  The modern sense developed from the practice of numbering pages in preparation for binding.
