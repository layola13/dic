drowse:/draʊz/| /ˈdraʊz/| v.|昏昏欲睡；打瞌睡|To sleep lightly; doze.|例句：I started to drowse during the boring lecture. 我在枯燥的讲座中开始打瞌睡。|例句：The warm sun induced a pleasant drowse.  温暖的阳光让人昏昏欲睡。|近义词：doze, doze off, nap|反义词：be awake, be alert|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drowses in the afternoon. = 他下午打瞌睡。
She often drowses while reading. = 她看书时经常打瞌睡。

一般过去时:
I drowsed off during the movie. = 我在电影中睡着了。
He drowsed by the fireplace. = 他在壁炉旁打瞌睡。

现在完成时:
I have drowsed a lot recently due to stress. = 最近我因为压力睡了很多。
She has drowsed off several times today. = 她今天已经打瞌睡好几次了。

过去完成时:
I had drowsed for a while before the alarm woke me. = 闹钟把我吵醒之前，我已经昏昏欲睡了一会儿了。
They had drowsed through most of the meeting. = 他们大部分会议时间都在打瞌睡。

将来时:
I think I will drowse during the long flight. = 我想我会在漫长的飞行中打瞌睡。
She will probably drowse on the train. = 她可能会在火车上打瞌睡。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't had coffee, I would have drowsed off during the meeting. = 如果我没有喝咖啡，我就会在会议上睡着了。
I wish I hadn't drowsed off during the important part of the presentation. = 我真希望我没有在演示的重要部分睡着。

被动语态:  (很少用被动语态)
The audience was hardly drowsed by the exciting presentation. = 观众们并没有被激动人心的演讲弄得昏昏欲睡。


疑问句:
Does he often drowse in class? = 他上课经常打瞌睡吗？
Will you drowse on the journey? = 你在旅途中会打瞌睡吗？


否定句:
I didn't drowse at all last night. = 我昨晚一点也没打瞌睡。
She doesn't usually drowse during the day. = 她通常白天不打瞌睡。
