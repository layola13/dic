fronting:/ˈfrʌntɪŋ/| v.|前移，使突出，优先处理 |To place or bring something to the front or forefront; to give prominence or priority to something.|例句：The company fronted its new product at the trade show. 公司在贸易展会上重点推出了他们的新产品。|例句：They fronted the issue of climate change in their campaign. 他们在竞选中优先处理了气候变化问题。|近义词：prioritize, emphasize, highlight|反义词：neglect, ignore, downplay


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He fronts the band. = 他是乐队的台前人物。
The company fronts its new product. = 公司主推其新产品。

一般过去时:
She fronted the meeting. = 她主持了会议。
The newspaper fronted the story on the front page. = 报纸将这则新闻放在头版显著位置。

现在完成时:
They have fronted the campaign with a new strategy. = 他们用新策略主导了这场竞选活动。
The band has fronted many successful tours. = 乐队已经成功举办过许多巡回演出。

过去完成时:
He had fronted the project before the deadline. = 他在截止日期前完成了项目的前期工作。
The team had fronted the most challenging tasks. = 团队已经完成了最具挑战性的任务。

将来时:
She will front the negotiations next week. = 她下周将领导谈判。
The company will front its new marketing campaign in the spring. = 公司将在春季推出新的营销活动。

虚拟语气:
If he fronted the project, it would be more successful. = 如果他负责这个项目，它会更成功。
I wish she had fronted the issue earlier. = 我希望她早点提出这个问题。

被动语态:
The issue was fronted by the CEO. = 这个问题由首席执行官提出。
The new product is being fronted by a massive marketing campaign. = 新产品正在通过大规模的营销活动进行推广。

疑问句:
Who fronted the meeting? = 谁主持了会议？
Which product will the company front next? = 公司接下来会主推哪个产品？

否定句:
He didn't front the issue. = 他没有正视这个问题。
The company didn't front its new product effectively. = 公司没有有效地推广其新产品。


PHRASE->
front and center: 最重要的；最显著的
bring to the front: 突出；强调
front runner: 领跑者；领先者
front line: 前线；最前沿
front page news: 头条新闻
