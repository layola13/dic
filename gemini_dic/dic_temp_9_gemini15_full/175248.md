railroads:/ˈreɪlˌroʊdz/| n. |铁路系统，铁路公司 |A system of railway lines and the companies that operate them. Railroads are vital for transporting goods and people over long distances.  |Example: The railroads played a crucial role in the industrial revolution.  铁路在工业革命中发挥了至关重要的作用。 The company owns several railroads across the country. 这家公司拥有遍布全国的几条铁路。|近义词：railways, railway systems |反义词：None (antonyms are not applicable to this noun).|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The railroads transport millions of tons of cargo annually.  铁路每年运输数百万吨货物。
This railroad connects several major cities. 这条铁路连接着几个主要城市。


一般过去时:
The railroads were significantly less efficient before the invention of the steam engine. 蒸汽机发明之前，铁路的效率要低得多。
Many railroads were built during the 19th century. 19世纪建造了许多铁路。


现在完成时:
Railroads have revolutionized transportation and trade. 铁路彻底改变了交通和贸易。
The government has invested heavily in upgrading the railroads. 政府已大力投资升级铁路。


过去完成时:
Before the interstate highway system was built, railroads had been the primary mode of long-distance transport. 在州际公路系统建成之前，铁路是主要的远程运输方式。
By the time the war ended, the railroads had suffered significant damage. 到战争结束时，铁路已经遭受了严重的破坏。


将来时:

High-speed railroads will connect more cities in the future. 高速铁路将来会连接更多的城市。
