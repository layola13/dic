ridicule:/ˈrɪdɪkjuːl/| n. vt.|嘲笑；奚落；愚弄 | to make fun of someone or something; to mock; to treat with contempt.  |例句：He was ridiculed for his unusual clothes. 他因为穿着奇特而遭到嘲笑。

例句：The comedian's act relied heavily on ridicule.  这位喜剧演员的表演很大程度上依赖于嘲笑。


近义词：mock, deride, scorn, jeer, sneer |反义词：praise, commend, applaud


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They ridicule his ideas. = 他们嘲笑他的想法。
People ridicule her for her beliefs. = 人们因为她的信仰而嘲笑她。


一般过去时:
They ridiculed his proposal. = 他们嘲笑他的提议。
She was ridiculed for her accent. = 她因为口音而遭到嘲笑。


现在完成时:
He has been ridiculed for years. = 他多年来一直被嘲笑。
The team has been ridiculed for their poor performance. = 该团队因为糟糕的表现而遭到嘲笑。


过去完成时:
He had been ridiculed before, so he didn't mind. = 他以前就被嘲笑过，所以他不在乎。
They had ridiculed her ideas many times. = 他们多次嘲笑过她的想法。


将来时:
They will ridicule his new invention. = 他们会嘲笑他的新发明。
People will ridicule him for his mistake. = 人们会因为他的错误而嘲笑他。


虚拟语气:
If I were to wear that, I would be ridiculed. = 如果我穿成那样，我会被嘲笑的。
I wish they wouldn't ridicule her so much. = 我希望他们不要再那样嘲笑她了。


被动语态:
His ideas were ridiculed by many. = 他的想法被许多人嘲笑。
Her performance was ridiculed by the critics. = 她的表演受到评论家的嘲笑。


疑问句:
Why are you ridiculed? = 为什么你被嘲笑？
Were they ridiculed for their beliefs? = 他们因为信仰而被嘲笑吗？


否定句:
They didn't ridicule him. = 他们没有嘲笑他。
She wasn't ridiculed for her opinion. = 她没有因为她的意见而被嘲笑。


PHRASE->
hold up to ridicule = 暴露于嘲笑之下
subject to ridicule = 遭受嘲笑
object of ridicule = 嘲笑的对象
become an object of ridicule = 成为嘲笑的对象
a target of ridicule = 受嘲笑的靶子

词源:
来自中古英语 *ridicule，源于古法语 *ridicule，最终源于拉丁语 *ridiculus，“可笑的”，是 ridere 的形容词形式，“笑”。
