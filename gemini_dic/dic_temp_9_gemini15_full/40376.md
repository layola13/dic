contradiction:/ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkʃən/| n. |矛盾；反驳；否认；抵触 |A contradiction is a statement, belief, or action that is the opposite of another.  It can also refer to the act of contradicting someone or something.  |例句：There's a contradiction between what he says and what he does.  他言行不一。|例句：She offered a strong contradiction to his argument. 她强烈反驳了他的论点。|近义词：inconsistency, discrepancy, conflict, opposition |反义词：consistency, agreement, harmony


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His statement is a contradiction of the facts. = 他的陈述与事实相矛盾。
The twins' personalities are a stark contradiction. = 双胞胎的性格截然相反。

一般过去时:
His actions contradicted his words. = 他的行动与他的话语相矛盾。
The witness's testimony contradicted the defendant's alibi. = 证人的证词与被告的不在场证明相矛盾。

现在完成时:
The new evidence has contradicted the initial findings. = 新证据与初步调查结果相矛盾。
Recent studies have contradicted earlier research. = 最近的研究与早期的研究相矛盾。

过去完成时:
The lawyer had contradicted himself several times during the trial. = 律师在审判中自相矛盾好几次。
His previous statements had contradicted his current testimony. = 他之前的陈述与他现在的证词相矛盾。

将来时:
His upcoming book will contradict many established theories. = 他即将出版的书将与许多既定的理论相矛盾。
The new data will likely contradict previous assumptions. = 新数据很可能会与之前的假设相矛盾。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't contradicted himself, he might have won the argument. = 如果他没有自相矛盾，他可能就赢得了这场辩论。
If the evidence contradicted the theory, we would have to reconsider it. = 如果证据与理论相矛盾，我们就必须重新考虑它。

被动语态:
His ideas were contradicted by recent scientific discoveries. = 他的想法被最近的科学发现所驳斥。
The witness's statement was contradicted by security footage. = 证人的陈述被安全录像所驳斥。

疑问句:
Is there any contradiction in his story? = 他的故事中有什么矛盾吗？
Does this evidence present a contradiction to the defendant's claim? = 这证据是否与被告的主张相矛盾？

否定句:
There is no contradiction in his explanation. = 他的解释中没有任何矛盾。
The two accounts do not contradict each other. = 这两个说法并不相互矛盾。


PHRASE->
in contradiction to = 与...相矛盾
a contradiction in terms = 自相矛盾
internal contradiction = 内在矛盾
self-contradiction = 自相矛盾
beyond contradiction = 无可辩驳


词源:
The word "contradiction" comes from the Latin word "contradictio," which is formed from "contra" (against) and "dictio" (speaking).  It literally means "speaking against."
