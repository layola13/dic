The name "citronellol" is derived from "citronella" (the plant source of the oil) and the suffix "-ol," which indicates an alcohol in organic chemistry.  Citronella itself comes from the French word "citronelle," a diminutive of "citron," meaning lemon. Therefore, the name reflects the lemon-like scent and its alcoholic nature.
