labadist:/ˈlæbədɪst/| n. |拉巴迪派成员；拉巴迪教派信徒 | A member of a religious community founded in 1666 by Jean de Labadie in Altona, near Hamburg, Germany.  The Labadists were known for their communal living, strict religious practices, and emphasis on personal piety and social reform.  They were eventually dispersed due to internal conflicts and external pressures. |The Labadists were known for their austere lifestyle and commitment to community. |近义词：Pietist, communalist |反义词：individualist
