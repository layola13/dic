overseer:/ˈoʊvərsɪər/ | n. |监督者；监工；管理者 | A person who supervises others, especially workers.  |例句：The overseer ensured that the workers followed safety regulations. 监工确保工人们遵守安全规章。|例句：The project overseer reported on the progress to the board. 项目负责人向董事会汇报了进展情况。|近义词：supervisor, superintendent, foreman, manager |反义词：subordinate, employee

词源:
The word "overseer" comes from the Old English "ofersēon," a combination of "ofer" (over) and "sēon" (to see).  It literally meant "to oversee," implying supervision from above or a position of authority.  The meaning has evolved to encompass the person who performs the act of overseeing.
