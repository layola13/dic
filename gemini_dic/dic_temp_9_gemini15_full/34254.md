clapped:/klæpt/| v.|鼓掌，拍手|To strike the palms of the hands together repeatedly, typically to express approval or applause.|例句：The audience clapped enthusiastically after the performance.  观众在演出结束后热烈鼓掌。|例句：She clapped her hands together in delight. 她高兴地拍起了手。|近义词：applauded, applauded, hand-clapped|反义词：booed, hissed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He claps his hands. = 他拍手。
They clap rhythmically. = 他们有节奏地鼓掌。

一般过去时:
She clapped loudly. = 她大声鼓掌。
The crowd clapped for the winner. = 人群为获胜者鼓掌。

现在完成时:
We have clapped our hands until they hurt. = 我们一直鼓掌到手疼。
They have clapped for over an hour. = 他们鼓掌了一个多小时。

过去完成时:
The audience had already clapped before the singer finished. = 歌手还没唱完，观众就已经鼓掌了。
He had clapped his hands in excitement. = 他兴奋地拍手。

将来时:
I will clap if I like the show. = 如果我喜欢这场演出，我会鼓掌。
They will clap enthusiastically. = 他们会热烈鼓掌。

虚拟语气:
I wish I had clapped louder. = 我真希望我当时鼓掌的声音更大一些。
If I were there, I would have clapped. = 如果我在那里，我会鼓掌。

被动语态:
The performance was clapped for. = 这场演出受到了鼓掌。
His speech was well clapped. = 他的演讲受到了热烈的鼓掌。

疑问句:
Did they clap? = 他们鼓掌了吗？
Will you clap for me? = 你会为我鼓掌吗？

否定句:
They didn't clap. = 他们没有鼓掌。
She didn't clap at the end. = 她最后没有鼓掌。
