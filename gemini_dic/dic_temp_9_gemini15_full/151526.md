parsimonies:/ˈpɑːrsɪməniːz/ | n. |节俭；吝啬；俭省；节省；（尤指）过度的节俭 |  The plural of "parsimony," referring to instances or examples of being frugal or stingy. It often implies excessive frugality to the point of being undesirable. |例句：His parsimonies extended to refusing to buy his children new shoes. 他的节俭到了不肯给孩子们买新鞋的地步。

例句：The company's parsimonies in research and development led to its eventual decline. 公司在研发上的吝啬导致了它最终的衰落。


近义词：frugality, thrift, economy, stinginess, miserliness | 反义词：extravagance, profligacy, lavishness, generosity | 词性: noun


词源:
源自古法语 *parsimonie,  最终源自拉丁语 *parsus (过去分词形式 of *parcere "to spare").  反映了节俭的本质，即“节省”或“不浪费”。
