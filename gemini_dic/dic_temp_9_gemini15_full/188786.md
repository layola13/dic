rupture:/ˈrʌptʃər/ | n., v. | 破裂；断裂；决裂 | The sudden breaking or bursting of something.  A disruption of friendly relations. |例句：The pipe ruptured, causing a flood.  = 管子破裂了，导致洪水。

The argument caused a rupture in their friendship. =  这场争吵导致了他们友谊的破裂。

近义词：break, burst, crack, split, breach | 反义词：repair, mend, heal, unite


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The pipe ruptures frequently. = 管子经常破裂。
He ruptures the agreement. = 他撕毁了协议。


一般过去时:
The dam ruptured last night. = 水坝昨晚决堤了。
Their relationship ruptured after a huge fight. = 他们因为一场大吵而关系破裂。


现在完成时:
The negotiations have ruptured. = 谈判破裂了。
The water pipe has ruptured again. = 水管又破裂了。


过去完成时:
The relationship had ruptured before they even met. = 他们的关系在他们见面之前就已经破裂了。
The dam had ruptured hours before the rescue team arrived. = 水坝在救援队到达之前几个小时就已经决堤了。


将来时:
The volcano might rupture soon. = 火山可能很快就会爆发。
The negotiations will likely rupture if they don't compromise. = 如果他们不妥协，谈判很可能会破裂。


虚拟语气:

If the dam were to rupture, the town would be flooded. = 如果水坝决堤，小镇就会被淹没。


被动语态:
The agreement was ruptured by the other party. = 协议被对方撕毁了。
The pipe was ruptured during the earthquake. = 水管在地震中破裂了。


疑问句:
Did the water main rupture? = 自来水管破裂了吗？

否定句:
The water pipe didn't rupture. = 水管没有破裂。
The negotiations did not rupture. = 谈判没有破裂。
