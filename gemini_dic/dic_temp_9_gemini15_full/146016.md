overdrafts:/ˈoʊvədræfts/ | n. |透支；透支额 | An overdraft is an amount of money that you withdraw from a bank account that exceeds the available balance.  This results in a negative balance. |例句：He incurred a significant overdraft after spending more than he earned. 他入不敷出导致透支很多。


例句：The bank charged a hefty fee for the overdraft.  银行对透支收取了高额费用。


近义词：deficit, debt | 反义词：credit, surplus


词源:  The word "overdraft" is a compound word formed from "over-" (meaning "beyond" or "exceeding") and "draft" (meaning a drawing of money from an account).  The "draft" element ultimately derives from the Old French word "drafter," meaning "to draw."
