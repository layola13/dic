co-sleep:/ˈkoʊsliːp/ | v. | 与孩子同睡 | The practice of a parent or caregiver sleeping in the same bed or room as a child. This is often practiced for breastfeeding convenience, bonding, or comfort.  | Example: Many cultures co-sleep, believing it promotes closeness and security. 许多文化都实行与孩子同睡，认为这能促进亲密和安全感。 | 近义词: bed-sharing, shared sleeping | 反义词: separate sleeping


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They co-sleep with their baby every night. = 他们每晚都和他们的婴儿同睡。
Parents often co-sleep to facilitate breastfeeding. = 父母经常同睡以方便母乳喂养。

一般过去时:
We co-slept with our daughter until she was two. = 我们和女儿同睡直到她两岁。
My parents co-slept with me when I was a baby. = 我小时候，我的父母和我同睡。

现在完成时:
They have co-slept with their child since birth. = 他们从孩子出生起就和孩子同睡。
Many parents have co-slept at some point in their children's lives. = 许多父母在孩子的某个阶段都和孩子同睡过。

过去完成时:
Before the baby arrived, they had never considered co-sleeping. = 在婴儿出生之前，他们从未考虑过同睡。
We had co-slept for years before we moved to a bigger house. = 在我们搬到更大的房子之前，我们同睡了很多年。

将来时:
We will co-sleep with our newborn for the first few months. = 我们将在新生儿出生后的头几个月与他们同睡。
They plan to co-sleep until their child is potty-trained. = 他们计划一直和孩子同睡到孩子能够自己上厕所。

虚拟语气:
If we had a bigger bed, we would co-sleep more comfortably. = 如果我们有一张更大的床，我们会睡得更舒服。
I wish we had co-slept more when the baby was younger. = 我希望婴儿小的时候我们能多同睡一会儿。

被动语态:
Co-sleeping is practiced in many cultures around the world. = 在世界许多文化中都实行同睡。
