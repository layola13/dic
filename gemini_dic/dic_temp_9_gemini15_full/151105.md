parasite:/ˈpærəsaɪt/ | n. |寄生虫，寄生植物，寄生者 | An organism that lives on or in a host and gets its food from or at the expense of its host.  A person who takes advantage of others. |例句：Fleas are parasites that feed on the blood of mammals.跳蚤是靠吸食哺乳动物血液为生的寄生虫。


例句：He's become a parasite on his parents, relying on them for everything. 他已经成为他父母的寄生虫，依赖他们的一切。


近义词：hanger-on, leech, sponger |反义词：host, benefactor


词源:  From Middle French *parasite, from Late Latin *parasitus, from Greek παράσιτος (parasitos), literally "one who eats at another's table," from παρά (para-) "beside, near" + σῖτος (sitos) "food, grain."
