crenellated:/krəˈnɛlətɪd/ | adj. | 垛口状的；城垛状的 | Having battlements; adorned with crenels.  This adjective describes structures, especially walls or buildings, that have a series of regularly spaced indentations or notches along the top. These notches, called crenels, were originally defensive features in medieval fortifications. | The castle walls were crenellated, offering protection to archers.  = 城堡的城墙是垛口状的，为弓箭手提供了保护。  The crenellated roofline added to the building's medieval charm. = 垛口状的屋顶轮廓增添了建筑的中世纪魅力。 | embattled, fortified | smooth, plain
