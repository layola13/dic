capital-intensive: /ˈkæpɪtəl ɪnˈtɛnsɪv/ | adj. | 资本密集型的 | Requiring large amounts of capital in relation to labor or other inputs.  This is typically characterized by high fixed costs and relatively low variable costs. Industries like manufacturing, utilities, and mining are often capital-intensive. | Example: The automobile industry is a highly capital-intensive sector, requiring significant investments in machinery and infrastructure. 汽车行业是一个高度资本密集型行业，需要在机械和基础设施方面进行大量投资。 | Near synonyms: capital-rich, asset-heavy | Antonyms: labor-intensive, low-capital  | 词性：形容词
