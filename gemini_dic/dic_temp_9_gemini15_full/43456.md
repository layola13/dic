crash-landings: /ˈkræʃˌlændɪŋz/ | n. | 迫降；坠毁着陆 |  The act of an aircraft making an emergency landing, often resulting in damage to the aircraft.  This term usually implies a forced landing due to an emergency, not a planned landing in an unsuitable location.  |  例句：The pilot skillfully executed a crash-landing in a field, saving all passengers. 飞行员熟练地在田野里进行了迫降，挽救了所有乘客的生命。

例句：Several crash-landings were reported after the storm. 暴风雨过后，据报道发生了多次迫降事件。


近义词：emergency landing, forced landing, hard landing | 反义词：smooth landing, controlled landing | 词性：名词
