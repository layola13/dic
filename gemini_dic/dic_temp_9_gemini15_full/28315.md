caracara:/ˈkærəkærə/| n.|卡拉卡拉鹰|A bird of prey in the family Falconidae, characterized by its long legs, relatively short wings, and often a crested head.  Caracaras are known for their scavenging habits and opportunistic feeding behavior. They are found in the Americas.|例句：The caracara perched on a fence post, eyeing the nearby roadkill. 卡拉卡拉鹰栖息在栅栏柱上，注视着路边被撞死的动物。|例句：Several caracaras were seen scavenging leftovers near the picnic area.  几只卡拉卡拉鹰在野餐区附近觅食残羹剩饭。|近义词：falcon, hawk, kite|反义词：None specifically.
