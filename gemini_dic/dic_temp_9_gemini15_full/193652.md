self-censorship: /ˌsɛlfˈsɛnsərʃɪp/ | n. |自我审查；自我约束 | The act of suppressing or altering one's own thoughts, feelings, or expressions to conform to perceived social norms or avoid potential negative consequences.  This can involve withholding information, modifying opinions, or avoiding certain topics altogether. | 例句：The journalist practiced self-censorship, avoiding controversial topics to keep her job.  这位记者进行了自我审查，避免有争议的话题以保住工作。


例句：Many artists engage in self-censorship to avoid offending potential audiences or sponsors. 许多艺术家为了避免冒犯潜在的观众或赞助商而进行自我审查。


近义词：self-restraint, self-regulation, suppression, restraint


反义词：free expression, outspokenness


词性: 名词
