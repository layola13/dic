die-down:/daɪˈdaʊn/| v.|逐渐减弱；逐渐平息；逐渐消失 | to gradually become less strong or intense; to diminish gradually |例句：The excitement gradually died down after the game ended.  比赛结束后，兴奋之情逐渐平息。

The storm slowly died down after several hours. 暴风雨持续数小时后逐渐平息。


近义词：subside, lessen, abate, decrease |反义词：intensify, increase, escalate

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The noise dies down slowly. = 噪音慢慢减弱。
The fever dies down after taking medicine. = 服药后，发烧逐渐退去。


一般过去时:
The fire died down after midnight. =  午夜后火势逐渐减弱。
The argument died down once everyone calmed down. = 大家平静下来后，争论逐渐平息。


现在完成时:
The pain has died down since I took the painkillers. = 自从服了止痛药后，疼痛已经减轻了。
The protests have died down considerably in recent days. = 近日抗议活动已明显减少。


过去完成时:
The wind had died down before we set sail. = 我们起航前风已经停了。
The commotion had died down by the time the police arrived. = 警察到达时骚动已经平息了。


将来时:
The storm will die down by tomorrow morning. = 到明天早上暴风雨就会平息。
Hopefully, the traffic will die down after rush hour. = 希望高峰期过后交通会好转。


虚拟语气:
If the wind died down, we could go sailing. = 如果风停了，我们就能去航行了。
I wish the noise would die down so I could concentrate. = 我希望噪音能小一点，这样我就能集中注意力了。


被动语态:
The fire was allowed to die down naturally. = 火势被允许自然减弱。
The rumors were gradually allowed to die down. = 谣言逐渐被平息。


疑问句:
Has the excitement died down yet? = 兴奋劲儿过去了吗？
Will the protests die down soon? = 抗议活动很快就会平息吗？


否定句:
The wind didn't die down all night. = 风整夜都没停。
The anger didn't die down easily. = 怒气难以平息。


PHRASE->
