skelter:/ˈskɛltər/| adv.|仓促地；慌乱地；|in a hurried and disorderly way|The children skeltered down the stairs.孩子们慌慌张张地跑下楼。|hurriedly, rapidly, precipitately|calmly, slowly, deliberately|adv.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They skelter through the streets. = 他们慌忙地跑过街道。
We skelter about the house. = 我们在房子里乱跑。


一般过去时:
The crowd skeltered away from the fire. = 人群慌忙地逃离了大火。
He skeltered down the hill. = 他慌忙地跑下山。


现在完成时:
They have skeltered through the forest. = 他们已经慌慌张张地穿过森林。
She has skeltered into the house. = 她已经慌忙地跑进屋里。


过去完成时:
They had skeltered to safety before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们已经慌忙逃到了安全地带。
He had skeltered away before I could ask him any questions. = 我还没来得及问他问题，他就已经慌忙逃跑了。


将来时:
They will skelter from the building. = 他们将慌忙逃离大楼。
We will skelter to find shelter from the rain. = 我们将慌忙寻找避雨处。


虚拟语气:
If they had seen the danger, they would have skeltered to safety. = 如果他们看到了危险，他们就会慌忙逃到安全地带。
If I knew where to go, I would skelter to that place. = 如果我知道该去哪里，我就会慌忙地跑到那里。


被动语态:
(The adverb "skelter" doesn't typically take a passive voice.)


疑问句:
Did they skelter away in fear? = 他们害怕地慌忙逃走了吗？
Why did they skelter through the park? = 他们为什么慌忙地跑过公园？


否定句:
They didn't skelter; they walked calmly. = 他们没有慌忙，而是平静地走着。
He didn't skelter away; he faced the danger. = 他没有慌忙逃走；他面对了危险。


PHRASE->
(The word "skelter" is rarely used in phrases. It primarily functions as an adverb.)  
skelter and scatter: 仓皇逃窜
skelter away: 慌忙逃走
skelter down: 慌忙跑下
skelter through: 慌忙穿过

词源:
The word "skelter" likely originates from the Middle English word "skelten," which meant to "scatter" or "run quickly."  Its exact etymology is uncertain, but the sense of hurried, disorderly movement is consistent throughout its usage.
