mete:/miːt/| vt.|分配，给予；衡量，测量|to distribute or apportion (something, especially something unpleasant) to someone.|The teacher meted out punishment to the disruptive students.老师惩罚了那些扰乱课堂秩序的学生。|allocate, allot, dispense|retain, keep|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He metes out justice fairly. 他公平地执行正义。
She metes out the tasks to her team. 她把任务分配给她的团队。


一般过去时:
The judge meted out a harsh sentence. 法官判处了重刑。
They meted out punishment to the offenders. 他们惩罚了罪犯。


现在完成时:
The government has meted out significant funds to the project. 政府已经为该项目拨付了大量资金。
She has meted out her share of the workload. 她已经完成了自己份额的工作量。


过去完成时:
The king had meted out justice for centuries. 国王已经执政几百年了，期间一直秉公执法。
They had meted out punishment before the trial. 他们在审判前就进行了惩罚。


将来时:
The court will mete out punishment according to the law. 法院将根据法律进行处罚。
He will mete out the resources carefully. 他会谨慎地分配资源。


虚拟语气:
If the judge meted out a lighter sentence, there would be public outcry. 如果法官判处较轻的刑罚，将会引发公众抗议。
I wish the teacher had meted out the punishment more fairly. 我希望老师能更公平地进行惩罚。


被动语态:
Justice was meted out swiftly and decisively. 正义得到了迅速而果断的执行。
The resources were meted out unfairly. 资源分配不公平。


疑问句:
How will the resources be meted out? 资源将如何分配？
How was the punishment meted out? 惩罚是如何执行的？


否定句:
The judge did not mete out a fair sentence. 法官没有判处公平的判决。
They did not mete out the punishment as promised. 他们没有按照承诺进行惩罚。
