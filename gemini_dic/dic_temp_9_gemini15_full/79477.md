globalisation:/ˌɡloʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |全球化 |The process of interaction and integration among people, companies, and governments worldwide.  This process is driven by international trade and investment and aided by information technology.  |Example: Globalisation has led to increased economic interdependence between nations. 全球化导致各国经济相互依赖性增强。|近义词：globalization, worldwide integration, internationalization |反义词：localization, isolation, protectionism

Globalisation affects every aspect of our lives. = 全球化影响着我们生活的方方面面。
The company expands its business through globalisation. = 这家公司通过全球化扩张业务。


一般过去时:
The rapid globalisation of the late 20th century transformed the world economy. = 20 世纪后期的快速全球化改变了世界经济。
Globalisation significantly reduced the cost of many goods. = 全球化大大降低了许多商品的成本。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:
