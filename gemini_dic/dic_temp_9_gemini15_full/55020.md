disputison:/ˌdɪspjuˈteɪʃən/| n. |争论；辩论；论战 |A formal discussion or debate, especially one on a particular topic.  It often implies a prolonged and serious argument.|例句：The disputison on the merits of free trade lasted for hours.这场关于自由贸易优缺点的辩论持续了数小时。

例句：The academic disputison about the origins of the universe continues to this day. 关于宇宙起源的学术争论至今仍在继续。


近义词：debate, argument, contention, controversy, discussion


反义词：agreement, accord, harmony, consensus


词性：名词

词源：源自拉丁语 "disputare" (争论，辩论)，加上 "-tion" (名词后缀)。


USAGE->  (由于disputison本身是名词，所以没有动词的时态变化。以下例句展示名词在不同句法中的用法)

主语：The disputison between the two candidates was heated. (两位候选人之间的争论非常激烈。)
宾语：He avoided the disputison by changing the subject. (他通过转移话题来避免争论。)
同位语：Their disagreement, a lengthy disputison, delayed the project. (他们之间的分歧，一场冗长的辩论，延误了项目。)
定语：The disputison panel consisted of three experts. (辩论小组由三位专家组成。)
介词宾语：The judge presided over the disputison. (法官主持了辩论。)


PHRASE-> (disputison本身较为正式，常用词组较少，以下列举与争论相关的词组)

engage in a disputison: 参与争论
subject to disputison: 引起争论的
point of disputison: 争论点
heated disputison: 激烈的争论
fruitless disputison: 无效的争论
