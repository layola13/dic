/mɑːrkɪdnəsiz/ | n. |显著性；显著特征；标记性|Markednesses refer to the features of a linguistic element that make it stand out or easily identifiable compared to other elements.  They are often analyzed in phonology and morphology to understand how sounds or morphemes contrast and create meaning. For example, the markedness of a sound might be its rarity or complexity compared to other sounds in a language.  In morphology, markedness can refer to a less frequent or more complex form of a morpheme compared to its unmarked counterpart.|The markedness of the voiceless aspirated stops in English distinguishes them from their voiced counterparts.  |近义词：distinctiveness, salience, prominence |反义词：unmarkedness, typicality |n.
