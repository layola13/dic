patronymics:/pætˈrɒnɪmɪks/ | n. |  父系名，父名继承制 | Names derived from the name of a father or ancestor.  Patronymics are a naming system where a person's name is derived from the name of their father or another male ancestor. This practice is common in many cultures throughout history and continues in some regions today.  | Example:  Johnson (son of John),  MacDonald (son of Donald),  O'Brien (grandson of Brian). |近义词:  inherited names, ancestral names | 反义词: matronymics (母系名) | 词性: noun
