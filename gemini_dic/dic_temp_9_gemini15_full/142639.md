ostmen:/ˈɒstmən/| n.|奥斯曼人，东斯拉夫人|The term "ostmen" refers to Scandinavian mercenaries and traders who settled in Eastern Europe, particularly in areas around the Baltic Sea and the rivers of modern-day Russia and Ukraine, during the Viking Age and the early medieval period.  They played a significant role in the political and economic life of these regions.|Example: The ostmen established trading posts along the rivers, facilitating commerce between East and West.|近义词：Varangians|反义词：无
