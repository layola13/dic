infantilisms: /ˈɪnfəntaɪlɪzəm/ | n. |幼稚行为；幼稚的言行|  The persistence of childish behaviors or attitudes into adulthood.  These behaviors can manifest in various ways, from temper tantrums and emotional outbursts to a lack of responsibility and dependence on others.  The term often carries a negative connotation, suggesting immaturity and a failure to develop appropriate adult coping mechanisms. |

例句：

His constant whining and demands for attention are clear signs of infantilisms.  他的不断抱怨和对关注的要求是幼稚行为的明显迹象。

Her refusal to take responsibility for her actions is a form of infantilism. 她拒绝为自己的行为负责是一种幼稚行为。


近义词：childishness, immaturity, puerility

反义词：maturity, adulthood, responsibility


词性：名词


词源：源自拉丁语 *infantilis  (infans 的形容词形式) + -ism (表示行为或状态的后缀)  infans 本意是“不能说话的人”，即婴儿。
