organoids:/ˈɔːrɡənɔɪdz/| n.|类器官；器官样结构|Three-dimensional tissue structures that mimic the structure and function of organs.  They are created in vitro from stem cells or other progenitor cells.  |例句：Scientists are using organoids to model diseases and test new drugs.  科学家们正在利用类器官来模拟疾病和测试新药。

例句：The development of brain organoids has revolutionized neuroscience research.  脑类器官的发展彻底改变了神经科学研究。


近义词：tissue culture, organotypic culture|反义词：无|词性:名词

词源:
The word "organoid" is a combination of "organ" (meaning a part of the body with a specific function) and the suffix "-oid" (meaning resembling or having the form of).  It's a relatively recent coinage reflecting the development of this specific area of biological research.
