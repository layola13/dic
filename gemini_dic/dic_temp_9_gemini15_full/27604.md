canceling: /ˈkænsəlɪŋ/ | v. | 取消；抵消 | The act of canceling something; making something null and void; counteracting or neutralizing something.  |例句：He is canceling his trip to Europe. 他正在取消他去欧洲的旅行。 |例句：The positive and negative charges canceled each other out. 正负电荷互相抵消了。|近义词：counteracting, nullifying, voiding, annulling |反义词：confirming, approving, ratifying | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are canceling the meeting. = 他们正在取消会议。
She cancels her subscription every year. = 她每年都取消订阅。


一般过去时:
He canceled the order last week. = 他上周取消了订单。
We canceled our plans due to the rain. = 由于下雨，我们取消了计划。


现在完成时:
I have canceled my appointment with the doctor. = 我已经取消了和医生的预约。
They have canceled all flights due to the storm. = 由于暴风雨，他们取消了所有航班。


过去完成时:
She had canceled the reservation before we called. = 我们打电话之前，她已经取消了预订。
They had canceled the project before the funding ran out. = 在资金用尽之前，他们就已经取消了项目。


将来时:
I will cancel the contract tomorrow. = 我明天将取消合同。
They will cancel the show if the tickets don't sell. = 如果票卖不出去，他们将取消演出。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would cancel the trip. = 如果我是你，我会取消这次旅行。
I wish I had canceled my flight earlier. = 我真希望我早点取消我的航班。


被动语态:
The meeting was canceled due to unforeseen circumstances. = 会议由于不可预见的情况而被取消。
The order has been canceled by the customer. = 订单已被客户取消。


疑问句:
Are you canceling your membership? = 你要取消你的会员资格吗？
Has he canceled his flight yet? = 他已经取消航班了吗？


否定句:
I am not canceling my plans. = 我不会取消我的计划。
They did not cancel the game despite the bad weather. = 尽管天气不好，他们也没有取消比赛。


PHRASE->
cancel out: 抵消

cancel a meeting: 取消会议

cancel a subscription: 取消订阅
