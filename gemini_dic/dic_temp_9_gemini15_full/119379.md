microcrystalline: /ˌmaɪkroʊˈkrɪstəlɪn/ | adj. | 微晶的，微晶体的 | Consisting of, or characterized by, very small crystals.  The term refers to a material's structure, where the individual crystals are too small to be seen with the naked eye, typically requiring magnification for observation.  This fine-grained structure often leads to specific physical properties like increased strength or different optical characteristics.


例句：

The sample exhibited a microcrystalline texture under the microscope. = 在显微镜下，样品显示出微晶结构。

Microcrystalline wax is often used in cosmetics and pharmaceuticals. = 微晶蜡常用于化妆品和药品。


近义词：fine-grained, small-grained


反义词：macrocrystalline, coarse-grained


词源:

* **micro-**:  From Greek *mikros* meaning "small".
* **crystalline**: From Greek *krystallos* meaning "ice", later referring to quartz and other crystalline substances.  The suffix *-ine* denotes "of the nature of" or "relating to".


用法说明：

microcrystalline 主要用作形容词，修饰名词，描述材料的晶体结构。  它不单独作为名词使用。  通常用来描述岩石、蜡、金属等材料。


PHRASE->

microcrystalline cellulose = 微晶纤维素
microcrystalline wax = 微晶蜡
microcrystalline silicon = 微晶硅
microcrystalline structure = 微晶结构
microcrystalline material = 微晶材料
