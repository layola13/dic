reinstating: /ˌriːɪnˈsteɪtɪŋ/ | v. |恢复；使复职；重新启用|To restore to a previous position, rank, or status; to put back into effect.|

The company is reinstating its employee after a period of suspension.  = 公司在停职一段时间后恢复了该员工的职务。

The government reinstated the old tax laws. = 政府恢复了旧的税法。


近义词：restore, reinstate, resume, renew

反义词：abolish, cancel, remove, terminate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reinstate employees after investigations. = 他们会在调查之后恢复员工的职务。
The school reinstates the program every year. = 学校每年都会重新启用该项目。

一般过去时:
The judge reinstated the verdict. = 法官恢复了判决。
The committee reinstated the rule. = 委员会恢复了该规定。

现在完成时:
The court has reinstated his driving license. = 法院已经恢复了他的驾照。
They have reinstated the old system. = 他们已经恢复了旧系统。

过去完成时:
The manager had reinstated the employee before the meeting. = 经理在会议前已经恢复了该员工的职务。
They had reinstated the policy before the incident occurred. = 他们在事件发生前已经恢复了该政策。

将来时:
The company will reinstate the employee next week. = 公司下周将恢复该员工的职务。
They will reinstate the service after the repairs. = 他们会在维修之后恢复服务。

虚拟语气:
If they reinstated the program, more students would participate. = 如果他们恢复了这个项目，会有更多学生参与。
I wish the government would reinstate the funding. = 我希望政府能够恢复拨款。

被动语态:
The employee was reinstated after the appeal. = 该员工在申诉后被恢复了职务。
The old system was reinstated last month. = 旧系统在上个月被恢复了。

疑问句:
Will they reinstate the employee? = 他们会恢复该员工的职务吗？
Has the program been reinstated? = 该项目已经被恢复了吗？

否定句:
They did not reinstate the employee. = 他们没有恢复该员工的职务。
The company will not reinstate the old policy. = 公司不会恢复旧政策。
