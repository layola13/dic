petaurist:/ˈpɛtjuːrɪst/| n. |飞鼯，鼯鼠|A petaurist is a gliding possum, a nocturnal marsupial mammal characterized by its ability to glide through the air using a patagium (a membrane of skin stretching between its limbs). They are found in Australia, New Guinea, and nearby islands.|Example: The sugar glider is a well-known species of petaurist.|近义词：gliding possum, flying squirrel (though not taxonomically related)|反义词：无
