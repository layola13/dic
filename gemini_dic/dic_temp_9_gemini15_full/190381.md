sarin:/ˈsærɪn/| n. |萨林（神经毒剂）|A highly toxic nerve agent that is a colorless, odorless liquid.  It was developed in Germany during World War II.  Exposure can cause symptoms such as blurred vision, nausea, and respiratory failure.  Sarin is a weapon of mass destruction and is prohibited under the Chemical Weapons Convention.|The attack involved the use of sarin gas, resulting in numerous casualties.  萨林毒气袭击导致多人伤亡。|近义词：VX,  tabun (other nerve agents)|反义词：无|名词
