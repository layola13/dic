flagging: /ˈflæɡɪŋ/ | adj., v. | 疲惫的；衰弱的；减弱的；标志；标示 |

英文解析:
As an adjective, "flagging" describes something that is weakening or becoming less strong. It can refer to physical energy, interest, or even a signal. As a verb, it means to mark or signal something, often with a flag.

例句:
(adjective)
His flagging energy made it difficult to finish the marathon.  = 他日渐衰竭的体力使得完成马拉松变得很困难。

His interest in the project was clearly flagging. = 他对这个项目的兴趣明显下降了。


(verb)
They flagged the dangerous area with caution tape. = 他们用警戒线标记了危险区域。

The soldiers flagged down a passing car for help. = 士兵们拦下一辆过往的汽车求助。


近义词: (adjective) weakening, waning, declining, drooping, sluggish; (verb) marking, signaling, indicating


反义词: (adjective) strong, energetic, vigorous, thriving; (verb)  (no direct antonyms, depends on context)


词性: adjective, verb
