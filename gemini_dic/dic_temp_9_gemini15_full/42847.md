court-martialing: /ˌkɔːrtˈmɑːrʃəlɪŋ/ | v. |军事法庭审判|The act of trying a member of the armed forces in a court-martial.  It refers to the process itself, not just the outcome.| The soldier was court-martialed for desertion.  这名士兵因擅离职守而被军事法庭审判。| to try (someone) in a court-martial |  No direct antonyms;  the opposite would be a civilian trial or no trial at all.

词源:
The word is a compound of "court" and "martial." "Court" refers to a judicial body, and "martial" relates to military or warfare, deriving from Latin "Martius" (of Mars, the Roman god of war).  The term thus signifies a court specifically dealing with military matters.
