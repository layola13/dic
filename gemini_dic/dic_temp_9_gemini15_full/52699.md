/ˈdaɪɡræf/| n. |双字母组合，双字母音位|A digraph is a combination of two letters representing one sound, such as 'sh' in 'ship' or 'ch' in 'chair'.  Digraphs are common in many languages, and they can sometimes represent sounds that are not represented by single letters.  |Example: The digraph "th" in "think" represents a single consonant sound.  双字母组合“th”在“think”（思考）中代表一个单一的辅音音。|近义词：compound letter, double letter |反义词：monograph (single letter)
