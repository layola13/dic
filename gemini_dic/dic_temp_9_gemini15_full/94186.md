imperativist:/ɪmˈpærətɪvɪst/| n. |命令论者，命令主义者|A person who believes in or advocates for imperativism, a philosophical approach that emphasizes the importance of commands, duties, or obligations in ethics or morality.  Imperativists often focus on how we *ought* to act rather than on descriptive accounts of how we *do* act.  They might argue that ethical principles are fundamentally commands or directives issued by a higher power, reason, or moral intuition.|Example:  The philosopher Kant was a prominent imperativist, believing that moral actions stem from adherence to categorical imperatives.  Many deontological ethicists are considered imperativists.|近义词：deontologist (部分情况), moralist|反义词：consequentialist, utilitarian
