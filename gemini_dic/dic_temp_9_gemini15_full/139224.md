objurgatory: /əbˈdʒɜːɡəˌtɔːri/ | adj. | 斥责的；谴责的；责骂的 |  Characterized by or expressing harsh rebuke or criticism.  It implies a strong and often angry disapproval. |


例句：

His objurgatory tone left no doubt about his displeasure.  他的斥责语气毫不掩饰他的不满。

The objurgatory letter criticized the company's unethical practices. 这封谴责信批评了公司的违反道德行为。


近义词：reproachful, scolding, censorious, vituperative, excoriating

反义词：approving, complimentary, praising, laudatory


词源：

From  Latin *objurgatorius, from *objurgare "to rebuke, chide," from ob- "against" + jurāre "to swear."  The word ultimately relates to the idea of swearing against someone, expressing strong disapproval.


用法说明:

objurgatory 主要用作形容词，修饰名词，例如 tone (语气),  letter (信),  speech (讲话),  remark (评论) 等。它强调的是说话或写作的语气和内容，而不是行为本身。
