USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and thus does not conjugate like verbs.  It doesn't have tenses or voice.  It modifies verbs, describing *how* an action is performed.

Example 1 (Simple Present): He walks contemplatingly through the park. 他沉思地走在公园里。
Example 2 (Simple Past): She smiled contemplatingly at her child's drawing. 她若有所思地笑着看着孩子的画。
Example 3 (Present Perfect):  He has been sitting contemplatingly by the fire all evening. 他整个晚上都沉思地坐在火炉旁。
Example 4 (Future): She will look contemplatingly out the window tomorrow. 她明天会若有所思地望着窗外。

词源:
The word is derived from the verb "contemplate," which comes from the Late Latin *contemplari*, meaning "to observe, consider, contemplate."  The suffix "-ingly" transforms the verb into an adverb, indicating the manner in which the action is performed.
