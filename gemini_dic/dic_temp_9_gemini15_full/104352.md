juxtaposition:/ˌdʒʌkstəˈpəʊʒən/| n. |并置，并列；对比 |The arrangement of two or more things side by side or close together, typically to compare or contrast them.  It is often used to create a particular effect or to highlight a contrast.|例句：The juxtaposition of the old and the new architecture in the city is striking. 城市中新旧建筑的并置令人注目。

例句：The artist used juxtaposition to create a sense of irony in the painting.  艺术家运用并置的手法在绘画中创造出一种讽刺感。


近义词：contrast, comparison, apposition |反义词：separation, dissociation |词性: noun
