footnotes:/ˈfʊtnəʊts/ | n. |脚注，注脚 | Notes placed at the bottom of a page, usually providing additional information or clarification for something in the main text.  They are commonly used in academic writing, books, and articles to expand on points made in the main text without interrupting the flow of reading. | Example: The footnotes provided further details on the historical context of the event. 脚注提供了关于事件历史背景的更多细节。 |近义词:endnotes, annotations, notes |反义词:main text, body text
