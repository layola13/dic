conservancies:/kənˈsɜːvənsiz/| n. |自然保护区；保护协会|Areas dedicated to the conservation of natural resources, often involving wildlife management and habitat preservation.  They can be governmental, private, or community-based organizations.|Example:  Many African countries have established conservancies to protect endangered species. 许多非洲国家已经建立了自然保护区来保护濒危物种。|近义词：reserves, sanctuaries, preserves|反义词：destruction, exploitation

词源:
The word "conservancy" derives from the verb "conserve," meaning "to protect, preserve, or keep from loss or harm."  The suffix "-ancy" indicates a state, quality, or action.  Thus, "conservancy" refers to the act or practice of conserving something, often natural resources, and the place where this occurs.
