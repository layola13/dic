predocumentary:/ˌpriːˈdɒkjʊmɛntəri/| n.|前期纪录片，纪录片雏形|A predocumentary is a preliminary stage in the creation of a documentary film, often involving research, interviews, and gathering footage before the final editing and production process begins.  It might be a rough cut or a collection of raw materials.  It's essentially a work-in-progress.|Example: The filmmaker spent months creating a predocumentary, gathering interviews and footage before starting the actual editing process.|近义词：preliminary film, rough cut, early footage|反义词：finished documentary, final cut|名词
