scooping:/ˈskuːpɪŋ/| v.| 舀取；铲取；抢先报道 | The act of using a scoop or similar implement to lift and move something, typically a loose material such as ice cream, sand, or snow. It can also refer to the act of obtaining exclusive news before others. |例句：He was scooping ice cream for the children. 他正在为孩子们舀冰淇淋。

She scooped up the fallen leaves. 她把落叶扫了起来。


近义词：digging, ladling, shoveling |反义词：pouring, dropping


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scoops ice cream every day. 他每天都舀冰淇淋。
She scoops the sand into the bucket. 她把沙子舀进桶里。

一般过去时:
They scooped the snow off the driveway. 他们铲掉了车道上的雪。
He scooped up the puppy and hugged it. 他抱起小狗并拥抱它。

现在完成时:
I have scooped all the flour into the bowl. 我已经把所有的面粉都舀到碗里了。
She has scooped the winning lottery ticket. 她已经中了彩票的头奖。

过去完成时:
They had scooped out the pumpkin before carving it. 他们在雕刻南瓜之前已经把南瓜掏空了。
We had scooped up all the toys before bedtime. 我们在睡前已经把所有的玩具都收起来了。

将来时:
I will scoop the dog's food into its bowl. 我会把狗食舀到它的碗里。
She will scoop the ice cream into the cones. 她会把冰淇淋舀进蛋筒里。

虚拟语气:
If I had a bigger shovel, I would scoop the snow faster. 如果我有一个更大的铲子，我会铲雪更快。
I wish I could scoop up all the money. 我真希望我能把所有的钱都弄到手。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
