nonmenial: /ˌnɒnˈmiːniəl/ | adj. | 非卑贱的；非劳务的；体面的 | Not menial; not relating to or involving manual labor or unskilled work; respectable.  The word implies a job or task that is not degrading or lowly. |  例句：She secured a nonmenial position in a prestigious firm. 她在一间声望卓著的公司获得了一份体面的工作。


例句：He aspired to a nonmenial job that would allow him to use his skills and education. 他渴望一份能让他运用技能和教育的体面工作。


近义词：respectable, dignified, upscale, high-class


反义词：menial, degrading, lowly, humble


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "menial" (meaning "of or relating to servants or manual labor; lowly; degrading").  "Menial" itself originates from the Old French word "menial," meaning "belonging to a household," ultimately derived from the Latin word "mansionem" (household).
