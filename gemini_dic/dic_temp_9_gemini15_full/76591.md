galling:/ˈɡɔːlɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人恼怒的；令人苦恼的；摩擦的 |  causing irritation or annoyance; chafing.  | The galling injustice of it all made him furious.  这令人恼火的非正义行为使他大怒。 | infuriating, irritating, vexing, annoying | pleasing, comforting | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This constant criticism is galling. = 这种不断的批评令人恼火。
His arrogance is truly galling. = 他的傲慢真是令人恼火。


一般过去时:
The delay was incredibly galling. = 延误令人难以置信地恼火。
Finding out the truth was galling to him. = 发现真相让他很生气。


现在完成时:
It's been galling to watch their success. = 看他们的成功真是令人恼火。
Having to wait so long has been galling. = 等了这么久真是令人恼火。


过去完成时:
It had been galling to realize he'd been tricked. = 意识到自己被骗了，这让他很生气。
She had been galling at the unfair treatment she received. = 她一直对受到的不公平待遇感到恼火。


将来时:
It will be galling to lose the game after all that training. = 在经过所有训练之后输掉比赛将会令人恼火。
Knowing they'll get away with it will be galling. = 知道他们会逍遥法外将会令人恼火。


虚拟语气:
It would be galling if they won without any effort. = 如果他们不费吹灰之力就赢了，那将是令人恼火的。
It would have been galling to lose that much money. = 损失那么多钱本来会令人恼火的。


被动语态:
He was galled by their constant interruptions. = 他被他们不断的打断弄得很恼火。
She felt galled by the lack of recognition for her work. = 她对自己的工作没有得到认可感到恼火。


疑问句:
Isn't it galling to see such blatant disregard for the rules? = 看见如此公然无视规则，难道不令人恼火吗？
Was it galling to discover the deception? = 发现欺骗行为是不是令人恼火？


否定句:
The situation wasn't galling at all; it was quite funny. = 情况一点也不令人恼火；它相当有趣。
I didn't find their behavior galling; I found it amusing. = 我觉得他们的行为并不令人恼火；我觉得它很有趣。

词源:
From Middle English *gallen, from Old English *geallan, from Proto-Germanic *galjaną ("to be bitter, irritate"), from Proto-Indo-European *gʰel- ("to shine, be bright").  The original sense referred to a feeling of bitterness or irritation, which then evolved to describe something that causes this feeling.
