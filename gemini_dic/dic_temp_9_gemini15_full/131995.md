noncoordinating:/ˌnɒnkoʊˈɔːrdɪneɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 非协调的，并列的 |  Describes words, phrases, or clauses that are not grammatically connected by a coordinating conjunction (like and, but, or, nor, for, so, yet).  They are of equal grammatical rank and are often joined by punctuation alone. | Example: The cat sat on the mat; the dog slept on the rug. (Two independent clauses joined by a semicolon.) | 近义词: parallel, independent | 反义词: coordinating
