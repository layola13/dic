glistered:/ˈɡlɪstərd/| v.|闪闪发光；闪耀|To shine with a soft, reflected light.  The word often implies a gentle, shimmering light, rather than a bright, intense shine.|例句：The dew glistened on the spiderwebs. 露珠在蜘蛛网上闪闪发光。

The newly polished floor glistened under the lamplight.  新擦亮的木地板在灯光下闪闪发光。|近义词：sparkled, shimmered, shone|反义词：dull, matte


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The surface glisters in the sunlight. = 表面在阳光下闪闪发光。
The jewels glister beautifully. = 宝石闪闪发光。


一般过去时:
The wet leaves glistened in the morning sun. = 湿漉漉的树叶在清晨的阳光下闪闪发光。
His eyes glistened with tears. = 他的眼睛闪着泪光。


现在完成时:

The car has glistened since it was washed. = 自从洗车后，汽车一直闪闪发光。


过去完成时:
The snow had glistened before the storm hit. = 在暴风雨来临之前，雪一直闪闪发光。
The ice had glistened under the moonlight. = 冰在月光下闪闪发光。

虚拟语气:
If the sun shone, the dew would glister. = 如果阳光照射，露珠就会闪闪发光。

疑问句:

否定句:
The metal did not glister; it was dull. = 金属没有闪光；它是暗淡的。
The old painting didn't glister; the colors were faded. = 那幅旧画没有闪光；颜色已经褪色了。



PHRASE->
There are fewer common phrases directly using "glister" than some other verbs.  The verb is more often used alone. However, we can construct phrases:

glistening eyes: 闪闪发光的眼睛
glistening surface: 闪闪发光的表面
glistening dew: 闪闪发光的露珠
glistening with excitement: 兴奋地闪闪发光
glistening with sweat:  汗水淋漓地闪闪发光
