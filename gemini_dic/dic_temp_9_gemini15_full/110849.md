lintels:/ˈlɪntlz/| n.|门楣，过梁|A lintel is a horizontal structural member placed over an opening (such as a door or window) to carry the weight of the material above it.  Lintels can be made of stone, wood, steel, or concrete.|例句：The builders carefully installed the stone lintel above the doorway.  建筑工人仔细地将石制门楣安装在门上方。


The ancient Roman aqueduct used massive stone lintels to support its arches.  古罗马渡槽使用巨大的石制门楣来支撑其拱门。|近义词：header, transom (in some contexts)|反义词：None specifically, but a failing lintel could be contrasted with a strong support beam.|词性:名词
