dancer:/ˈdænsər/| n. |舞蹈演员，舞蹈家|A person who dances, especially professionally.  A dancer needs grace, strength, and stamina.|例句：She is a talented ballet dancer.  她是位才华横溢的芭蕾舞演员。

例句：The dancer moved with effortless grace across the stage. 舞蹈演员优雅自如地穿梭于舞台之上。


近义词：performer, choreographer (if also creating dances) |反义词：None directly applicable.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dances every Saturday. = 他每个星期六都跳舞。
She dances beautifully. = 她跳得很漂亮。


一般过去时:
He danced at the party last night. = 他昨晚在派对上跳舞了。
She danced with her friends. = 她和朋友们一起跳舞。


现在完成时:
I have danced since I was five years old. = 我从五岁就开始跳舞了。
She has danced in many different countries. = 她曾在许多不同的国家跳舞。


过去完成时:
He had danced before he became a professional. = 他在成为职业舞者之前就跳过舞了。
She had danced all night before she collapsed from exhaustion. = 她跳了一整夜，之后才因筋疲力尽而倒下。


将来时:
I will dance at the wedding. = 我将在婚礼上跳舞。
She will dance the lead role in the upcoming performance. = 她将在即将上演的演出中担任主角。


虚拟语气:
If I were a dancer, I would travel the world. = 如果我是一位舞蹈演员，我会周游世界。
I wish I could dance as well as she does. = 我希望我能像她一样跳得好。


被动语态:
The dance was danced with great passion. = 这支舞跳得激情澎湃。
The choreography was danced perfectly by the troupe. = 这支舞团完美地演绎了这套编舞。


疑问句:
Do you dance? = 你跳舞吗？
Can she dance the tango? = 她会跳探戈吗？


否定句:
He doesn't dance very often. = 他不经常跳舞。
She didn't dance at the wedding. = 她没有在婚礼上跳舞。
