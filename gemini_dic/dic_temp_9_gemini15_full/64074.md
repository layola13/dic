epizoic:/ˌɛpɪˈzoʊɪk/ | adj. |附生的；寄生的（指动物）| Relating to animals living on the surface of another animal.  Epizoic organisms are not parasitic; they do not harm their host.  They simply use the host as a substrate. | Example: Barnacles are a common example of epizoic organisms, often found attached to whales.  鲸鱼身上常见的藤壶就是附生生物的一个例子。 | 近义词：commensal (in some contexts), ectosymbiotic | 反义词：endoparasite, parasite
