representativeness:/ˌreprɛzɛnˈteɪtɪvnəs/| n. |代表性，典型性|The quality of being representative; the extent to which something is typical of a larger group or category.  It refers to the degree to which a sample accurately reflects the characteristics of the population from which it was drawn.  This concept is crucial in statistics and research, ensuring the validity and generalizability of findings.|The survey lacked representativeness because it only included people from one city.|近义词：typicality, representationality|反义词：unrepresentativeness, atypicality
