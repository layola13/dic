postmodern:/ˌpoʊstˈmɒdərn/| adj.|后现代的；后现代主义的|Postmodern refers to a period of thought, art, architecture, and criticism that emerged after World War II, characterized by skepticism towards grand narratives, objective truth, and traditional authority. It embraces irony, pastiche, and the deconstruction of established norms.  |The postmodern novel often plays with metafiction and unreliable narrators. 后现代小说经常玩弄元小说和不可靠的叙述者。|contemporary, modern|classical, traditional|形容词
