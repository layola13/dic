crystallin: /ˈkrɪstəlɪn/| n. |晶状体蛋白|A transparent protein found in the lens of the eye.  Crystallins are responsible for the transparency and refractive index of the lens, enabling clear vision. They are highly stable proteins and remarkably resistant to aggregation and denaturation. Different types of crystallins exist, including α, β, and γ crystallins, each with its own unique structure and function.  | Example:  The researchers studied the structure of α-crystallin to understand how it prevents cataract formation. 研究人员研究了α-晶状体蛋白的结构，以了解它如何防止白内障的形成。|近义词：lens protein (晶状体蛋白)|反义词：N/A
