onomatopoeia: /ˌɒnəˌmætəˈpiːə/ | /ˌɑːnəˌmætəˈpiːə/ | n. |拟声词；拟声法|The formation of a word from a sound associated with what is named (e.g., cuckoo, sizzle ).  Onomatopoeia is a word (or a phrase) that imitates the sound it represents.  Examples include animal sounds like "meow," "woof," and "oink," as well as sounds like "buzz," "hiss," and "splash."|The buzzing of bees annoyed the picnic-goers. 蜜蜂的嗡嗡声扰乱了野餐的人们。The water splashed against the rocks. 水溅在岩石上。|synonym: mimetic word, echoism|antonym:  N/A|名词
