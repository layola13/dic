predictability:/ˌprɛdɪktəˈbɪləti/| n. |可预测性，预见性 |The predictability of the outcome made the game less exciting.  结果的可预测性使比赛变得不那么刺激。|近义词：foreseeability,  predictiveness |反义词：unpredictability, randomness

英文解析：The quality or state of being predictable; the extent to which something can be predicted.  It refers to the likelihood that a future event or outcome can be accurately anticipated based on past experience or current data.

例句：

The predictability of the weather in this region is quite low.  这个地区的天气可预测性很低。


The high predictability of the stock market led to many investors making large profits. 股市的高可预测性导致许多投资者获得了巨额利润。


词源：

From "predictable,"  itself derived from the verb "predict," ultimately from Latin *praedīcāre ("to proclaim, foretell"), from prae- ("before") + dīcāre ("to say, speak").  The suffix "-ability" indicates the quality or state of being something.
