chronicals: /ˈkrɒnɪkəlz/ | n. |编年史，记录|A historical account of events in the order in which they occurred.  A chronicle is a detailed and factual record of events, often arranged chronologically.|The chronicles of the Anglo-Saxon period are fascinating.  |近义词： annals, history, record |反义词：fiction, fabrication

一般现在时:
The king's chronicler chronicles the events of the year. = 国王的编年史作者记录了这一年的事件。
These chronicles detail the daily life of the medieval town. = 这些编年史详细地描述了中世纪城镇的日常生活。


一般过去时:
The monk meticulously chronicled the abbey's history. = 修士一丝不苟地记录了修道院的历史。
Historians chronicled the rise and fall of the Roman Empire. = 历史学家记录了罗马帝国的兴衰。


现在完成时:
Scholars have chronicled the development of language over centuries. = 学者们已经记录了几个世纪以来语言的发展。
She has chronicled her family's journey across the country. = 她已经记录了她家人穿越全国的旅程。


过去完成时:
Before the printing press, scribes had chronicled events by hand. = 在印刷术出现之前，抄写员们手工记录事件。
They had chronicled the king's reign for many years before his death. = 在国王去世之前，他们已经记录了他的统治多年。


将来时:
Future generations will chronicle our current technological advancements. = 后代将记录我们当前的技术进步。
The journalist will chronicle the events of the upcoming election. = 记者将记录即将到来的选举的事件。


虚拟语气:
