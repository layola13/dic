distraught: /dɪˈstrɔːt/ | adj. | 极其痛苦的；心烦意乱的 | Extremely upset and agitated; mentally disturbed.  | The distraught mother frantically searched for her lost child.  (心烦意乱的母亲疯狂地寻找她丢失的孩子。) He was distraught over the news of his father's death. (他因父亲去世的消息而悲痛欲绝。) | distressed, agitated, frantic, upset | calm, composed, peaceful, serene | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is distraught over the exam results. = 他对考试结果感到非常沮丧。
She is always distraught when her cat is missing. =  她的猫丢失时，她总是很焦虑不安。

一般过去时:
He was distraught after the accident. = 事故发生后，他非常痛苦。
She was distraught when she lost her job. = 她失业时非常沮丧。


现在完成时:
I have been distraught since I heard the news. = 自从听到这个消息以来，我一直很焦虑。
They have been distraught ever since their house burned down. = 自从他们的房子烧毁以来，他们一直很痛苦。


过去完成时:
She had been distraught for weeks before she finally sought help. = 在她最终寻求帮助之前，她已经焦虑了几个星期。
He had been distraught all day before he finally broke down and cried. = 他整天都很沮丧，最后终于崩溃大哭。


将来时:
I will be distraught if I fail the exam. = 如果我考试不及格，我会非常沮丧。
She will be distraught if her son doesn't come home. = 如果她的儿子不回家，她会非常焦虑。


虚拟语气:
If I were in his shoes, I would be distraught. = 如果我处于他的境地，我也会非常痛苦。
I wish I weren't so distraught about this situation. = 我希望我不必为这种情况如此焦虑。


被动语态:
(Distraught is rarely used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
Is she distraught about something? = 她是不是因为什么事而心烦意乱？
Are you distraught over the loss of your pet? = 你因为失去宠物而心烦意乱吗？


否定句:
He wasn't distraught at all; he seemed quite calm. = 他一点也不沮丧；他看起来很平静。
She wasn't distraught; she just looked tired. = 她并没有心烦意乱；她只是看起来很疲倦。



PHRASE->
become distraught = 变得心烦意乱
feel distraught = 感到心烦意乱
look distraught = 看起来心烦意乱
utterly distraught = 极其痛苦的
seem distraught = 看起来很焦虑
