moundbuilder:/ˈmaʊndˌbɪldər/| n. |土堆建造者；(尤指北美) 史前土堆建筑者 |A moundbuilder is a person who constructs mounds, especially referring to the prehistoric peoples of North America who built large earthen mounds for various purposes.  The term often implies a connection to a specific culture or civilization known for its mound-building practices. |Example: The ancient moundbuilders of the Mississippi Valley left behind impressive earthworks.  古代密西西比河谷的土堆建造者留下了令人印象深刻的土方工程。|近义词：earthwork constructor, ancient builder |反义词: mound destroyer
