catchpenny: /ˈkætʃˌpɛni/ | adj. |廉价的；吸引人的；骗人的 | Designed to attract and appeal to people, especially by being cheap or superficially attractive, often implying a lack of substance or value.  It suggests something is designed to catch the eye and entice a purchase, but ultimately offers little real value. | 例句：The market was full of catchpenny souvenirs.  = 市场里充斥着廉价的纪念品。

The carnival game was a blatant catchpenny scheme. =  那个嘉年华游戏是一个明显的骗局。


近义词：cheap, tawdry, gimmicky, flashy, showy, trifling, worthless, insubstantial

反义词：valuable, worthwhile, substantial, genuine, high-quality


词源:  The word is a compound of "catch" (to attract or capture) and "penny" (a small coin, implying low cost).  Its origins trace back to the 19th century, reflecting a time when inexpensive, often low-quality goods were marketed to a broad audience.
