nolo:/ˈnoʊloʊ/| adj.| 不愿的；不情愿的 | unwilling; reluctant; not wishing |  The defendant entered a nolo contendere plea.  被告进行了无罪抗辩。|近义词：reluctant, unwilling, averse |反义词：willing, eager

PHRASE->
nolo contendere:  无罪抗辩 (a plea by which a defendant in a criminal prosecution accepts conviction as though a guilty plea had been entered, but does not admit guilt.)
nolo episcopis:  不服从主教的权力 (a historical term referring to a refusal to submit to ecclesiastical authority)
nolo prosequi:  撤销起诉 (a formal entry on the record of a case stating that the prosecutor will not pursue the case)
nolo molestare:  不骚扰 (a legal term meaning "not to molest" or "not to harass")
nolo pacis:  不愿和解 (a refusal to make peace)


词源:
The word "nolo" comes from the Latin "nolle," meaning "to be unwilling" or "to not want."  It's a shortened form primarily used in legal contexts.
