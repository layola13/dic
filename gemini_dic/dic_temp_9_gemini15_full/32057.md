cherry-picking:/ˈtʃeriˌpɪkɪŋ/| n. | 挑选（有利的证据）；选择性引用 | The act of choosing only the most favorable evidence or examples while ignoring others that contradict or weaken one's argument or position. It often involves a deliberate bias in selecting data to support a preconceived conclusion. |例句：The politician was accused of cherry-picking data to support his claims about economic growth.  这个政治家被指控挑选数据来支持他对经济增长的说法。

例句：He cherry-picked the best apples from the basket. 他从篮子里挑选了最好的苹果。 |近义词：selective quotation, biased selection, hand-picking |反义词：objective analysis, unbiased reporting |词性:名词
