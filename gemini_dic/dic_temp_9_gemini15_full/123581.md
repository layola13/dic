montaged:/mɒnˈtɑːʒd/ | /mɑːnˈtɑːʒd/ | v. (过去式和过去分词) | 蒙太奇剪辑；组合；拼凑 | To assemble (film sequences, etc.) into a continuous whole; to combine various elements into a unified whole.  | The film was cleverly montaged, seamlessly blending documentary footage with dramatic recreations. 这部电影巧妙地进行了蒙太奇剪辑，无缝地将纪录片素材与戏剧性重现融合在一起。  | assembled, compiled, combined | disassembled, separated | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He montaged the film footage into a compelling narrative. = 他将电影片段剪辑成一个引人入胜的故事。
They montage images and sounds to create a powerful effect. = 他们将图像和声音组合在一起以产生强大的效果。


一般过去时:
The director montaged the scenes to create a sense of urgency. = 导演剪辑这些场景是为了营造一种紧迫感。
She carefully montaged the photographs into a beautiful collage. = 她仔细地将照片组合成一幅美丽的拼贴画。


现在完成时:
They have montaged the documentary using various techniques. = 他们运用各种技术完成了这部纪录片的剪辑。
He has montaged a stunning visual presentation for the conference. = 他为会议制作了一个令人惊叹的视觉演示。


过去完成时:
The editor had montaged the rough cut before the director's review. = 编辑在导演审查之前已经完成了粗剪的剪辑。
They had montaged the music video by the time the deadline arrived. = 截止日期到来时，他们已经完成了音乐视频的剪辑。


将来时:
The team will montage the footage into a short film for the festival. = 该团队将把素材剪辑成一部短片，参加电影节。
She will montage the images and text for her presentation tomorrow. = 她将在明天为她的演示文稿组合图像和文本。


虚拟语气:
If they had montaged the scenes differently, the film would have felt less disjointed. = 如果他们以不同的方式剪辑这些场景，这部电影就不会感觉那么支离破碎。
I wish I had montaged the photos more carefully. = 我希望我当时更仔细地剪辑照片。


被动语态:
The film was montaged by a skilled editor. = 这部电影是由一位经验丰富的编辑剪辑的。
The images were montaged together to create a powerful message. = 这些图像被组合在一起以创造一个有力的信息。


疑问句:
How was the documentary montaged? = 这部纪录片是如何剪辑的？
Was the video montaged using special effects? = 视频是否使用了特效进行剪辑？


否定句:
The editor did not montage the scenes in chronological order. = 编辑没有按时间顺序剪辑这些场景。
They haven't yet montaged all the footage. = 他们还没有剪辑完所有的素材。


PHRASE->
film montage  电影蒙太奇
photo montage 照片蒙太奇
sound montage 音响蒙太奇

artistic montage 艺术蒙太奇


词源:
From French *montage*, from *monter* "to mount, to assemble".  The word entered English in the early 20th century, largely through the influence of French filmmaking techniques.
