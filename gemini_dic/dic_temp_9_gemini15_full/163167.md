precomparisons:/ˌpriːkəmˈpærɪzənz/ | n. | 预先比较；事前比较 |  Comparisons made before a main comparison or decision.  The act of comparing things before a final decision is made. This often involves examining various options or data points to inform the ultimate choice. | 例句：The precomparisons of different software packages helped the company choose the best fit for their needs. 预先比较不同的软件包帮助公司选择了最适合他们需求的软件。

The team conducted thorough precomparisons before selecting the final design.  团队在选择最终设计之前进行了彻底的预先比较。| 近义词： preliminary comparisons, prior comparisons, advance comparisons | 反义词： postcomparisons, final comparisons | 名词
