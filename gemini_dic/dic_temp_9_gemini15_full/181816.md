relish:/ˈrɛlɪʃ/| n. v.|享受； relish (something)  |享受；品味；佐料|英文解析：To enjoy greatly; to savor. As a noun, it refers to great enjoyment or zest, or a savory condiment. |例句：She relished the challenge. 她喜欢这个挑战。

I relished every bite of the delicious meal. 我细细品味了这顿美味佳肴的每一口。


近义词：enjoy, savor, appreciate, zest, gusto |反义词：dislike, abhor, detest


词性：名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He relishes spicy food. 他喜欢吃辛辣的食物。
She relishes her work. 她热爱她的工作。

一般过去时:
I relished the quiet evening. 我享受了宁静的夜晚。
They relished the opportunity to travel. 他们珍惜这次旅行的机会。

现在完成时:
We have relished many adventures together. 我们一起经历了许多冒险，并从中获得乐趣。
She has relished the freedom of retirement. 她享受退休后的自由。

过去完成时:
He had relished the anticipation of the trip. 他曾很享受旅行前的期待。
They had relished the delicious meal before the unexpected guest arrived. 在意外的客人到来之前，他们已经享用过美味佳肴。

将来时:
I will relish the opportunity to learn new skills. 我将珍惜学习新技能的机会。
We will relish the quiet moments at the beach. 我们将在海滩上享受宁静的时光。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would relish the chance to read more books. 如果我有更多的时间，我会珍惜阅读更多书籍的机会。
I wish I could relish a relaxing vacation. 我希望我能享受一次轻松的假期。

被动语态:
The delicious cake was relished by everyone. 美味的蛋糕受到了所有人的喜爱。
The challenging project was relished by the ambitious team.  雄心勃勃的团队很享受这个具有挑战性的项目。

疑问句:
Do you relish the thought of retirement? 你喜欢退休的想法吗？
Does she relish the opportunity to work on this project? 她喜欢参与这个项目的机会吗？

否定句:
I don't relish the idea of public speaking. 我不喜欢公开演讲的想法。
He didn't relish the prospect of working overtime. 他不喜欢加班的前景。


PHRASE->
relish the moment:  享受当下

relish the challenge:  享受挑战
have a relish for:  对...有兴趣/爱好
lose your relish: 失去兴趣
