peeve:/piːv/| n. v.|烦扰，恼怒；使恼怒 | a feeling of annoyance; to annoy someone |例句：That constant dripping noise is a real peeve of mine.  那持续不断的滴水声真让我烦。  His constant complaining is a major peeve. 他不停的抱怨真让人讨厌。|近义词：annoyance, irritation, vexation |反义词：pleasure, satisfaction |名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
That noise really peeves me. = 那个噪音真让我烦。
This constant rain peeves my neighbor. = 这不停的雨让我的邻居很烦。


一般过去时:
The traffic jam peeved me yesterday. = 昨天的交通堵塞让我很烦。
His rude behavior peeved her greatly. = 他粗鲁的行为让她非常生气。


现在完成时:
The delay has peeved me all day. = 延迟让我一整天都很烦。
His forgetfulness has peeved her many times. = 他健忘的毛病已经让她生气很多次了。


过去完成时:
I had been peeved by his attitude before the meeting. = 会议之前我就被他这种态度惹恼了。
She had already been peeved by his lack of consideration. = 她早就因为他缺乏考虑而生气了。


将来时:
The long flight will probably peeve me. = 漫长的飞行可能会让我感到烦躁。
That constant noise will peeve everyone in the office. = 那持续不断的噪音会惹恼办公室里的每个人。


虚拟语气:
If he knew it would peeve me, he wouldn't have done it. = 如果他知道这会让我生气，他不会那样做的。
I wish that loud music wouldn't peeve my neighbors so much. = 我希望那大声的音乐不会让我的邻居那么生气。


被动语态:
He was peeved by her sharp words. = 他被她尖刻的话语激怒了。
They were peeved by the unexpected delay. = 他们因为意外的延误而感到恼火。


疑问句:
Does this situation peeve you? = 这种情况让你生气吗？
What peeves you the most about this job? = 你最讨厌这份工作的什么？


否定句:
The news didn't peeve me as much as I thought it would. = 这个消息并没有像我想象的那样让我生气。
This small problem doesn't usually peeve him. = 这个小问题通常不会让他生气。


PHRASE->
a pet peeve =  最讨厌的事
to have a peeve with someone = 对某人生气
to get one's peeve up = 生气
to be a peeve to someone = 成为某人的眼中钉
to work one's peeve out = 发泄怒气


词源:
The word "peeve" likely originates from the Middle English word "pēven," meaning "to be angry" or "to fret."  Its ultimate origins are uncertain, but possible connections have been suggested to words related to the idea of puffing up or swelling with anger.
