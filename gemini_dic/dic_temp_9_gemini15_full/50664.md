derivatives:/dɪˈrɪvətɪvz/| n.|衍生品，派生物|Something derived or obtained from something else; a product obtained from another product.  Derivatives are financial instruments whose value is derived from an underlying asset.  They can be complex and risky, but also offer opportunities for hedging and speculation.|例句：Stock options are derivatives whose value depends on the price of the underlying stock. 股票期权是衍生品，其价值取决于标的股票的价格。
The company developed several derivatives from its original product. 这家公司从其原产品中开发出几种衍生产品。|近义词：offshoots, byproducts, spin-offs|反义词：originals, primaries|名词
