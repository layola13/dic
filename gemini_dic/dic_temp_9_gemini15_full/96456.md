individualised: /ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 个人化的；个性化的 |  Adapted or designed to meet the needs of a particular person or group.  It emphasizes tailoring something to fit the unique characteristics of an individual. |


例句：

The teacher individualised the lesson plans to cater to the diverse learning styles of her students. = 老师个性化地制定了教学计划，以适应学生不同的学习风格。

The software allows for individualised settings to optimise user experience. = 该软件允许个性化设置以优化用户体验。


近义词：personalized, customized, tailored, bespoke


反义词：standardized, generic, uniform


词源:  Derived from "individual" + "-ised" (a suffix indicating the act of making something).  "Individual" itself comes from the Late Latin word "individuus," meaning "indivisible," ultimately from "in-" (not) + "dividuus" (divisible).
