paper-pusher: /ˈpeɪpərˌpʊʃər/ | n. |  办事员；文书；只会做些例行公事的人 |

英文解析: A person whose job involves a lot of paperwork and routine tasks, often implying a lack of importance or creativity.  The term often carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting the job is monotonous and unchallenging.

例句:

He's just a paper-pusher in a government office.  他只是政府机关里一名办事员。

She felt like a mere paper-pusher, her talents wasted on such mundane tasks. 她感觉自己只是一个文书，她的才能浪费在这些平凡的任务上。


近义词: clerk, bureaucrat, file clerk, office worker (with negative connotation)

反义词:  executive, decision-maker, innovator, leader


词源:  The word is a compound of "paper" and "pusher."  "Paper" refers to the documents and paperwork involved in office jobs, and "pusher" suggests the act of moving or processing these documents, often in a repetitive and seemingly unimportant manner. The term emerged in the mid-20th century as office work became more prevalent.  It reflects a common perception of certain office jobs as being tedious and lacking in intellectual stimulation.
