dalits:/ˈdælɪts/| n. |达利特人（印度贱民）|Dalits, also known as "untouchables," are members of the lowest caste in the traditional Hindu social hierarchy in India. They have historically faced severe discrimination and social exclusion.  The term "Dalit" means "oppressed" or "broken" in Sanskrit and is preferred by many over the older, pejorative term "untouchable".|The Dalits have fought for their rights and equality for many years.  Many Dalits still face discrimination and poverty.|近义词：untouchables (贬义), Scheduled Castes|反义词：Brahmins (婆罗门)
