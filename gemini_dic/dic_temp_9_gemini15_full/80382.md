goliard:/ˈɡoʊliɑːrd/ | n. |游吟诗人，流浪学者|A goliard was a medieval student or cleric known for their wandering lifestyle, satirical writings, and often bawdy songs.  They were often associated with universities and were known for their irreverent humor and criticism of the church and society. |Example: The goliard's songs were full of witty and often scandalous verses.  这位游吟诗人的歌充满了诙谐且常常是丑闻式的诗句。 |近义词：wandering scholar, medieval student, minstrel |反义词：monk (in the sense of a strictly religious and disciplined life) |名词
