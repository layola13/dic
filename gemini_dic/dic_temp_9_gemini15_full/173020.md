quarrel:/ˈkwɒrəl/ /ˈkwɔːrəl/| n., v.|争吵，争论| a noisy or angry argument|例句：They had a quarrel about the money. 他们因为钱而争吵。

They had a bitter quarrel and haven't spoken since. 他们发生了一场激烈的争吵，从那以后就没说过话。


近义词：dispute, argument, fight, squabble|反义词：agreement, harmony, accord|词性: 名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They quarrel frequently. = 他们经常吵架。
He quarrels with his neighbors. = 他和邻居们吵架。


一般过去时:
They quarreled loudly last night. = 他们昨晚大声争吵。
We quarreled over a trifle. = 我们为一件小事争吵。


现在完成时:
They have quarreled several times this week. = 他们本周已经吵了好几次架了。
We haven't quarreled in years. = 我们多年没吵过架了。


过去完成时:
They had quarreled before I arrived. = 我到达之前他们就已经吵过架了。
She had quarreled with her sister before the party. = 派对前她和姐姐已经吵过架了。


将来时:
I think they will quarrel again soon. = 我想他们很快又会吵架。
They will quarrel if you don't intervene. = 如果你不干预，他们会吵架的。


虚拟语气:
If they quarreled, I would try to mediate. = 如果他们吵架，我会尝试调解。
I wish they hadn't quarreled. = 我希望他们没有吵架。


被动语态:
The matter was often quarreled over. = 这件事经常被争论。
The issue is being quarreled about. = 这个问题正在被争论。


疑问句:
Are they quarreling again? = 他们又在吵架吗？
Why are they quarreling? = 他们为什么吵架？


否定句:
They didn't quarrel yesterday. = 他们昨天没有吵架。
We don't quarrel often. = 我们不常吵架。


PHRASE->
have a quarrel = 发生争吵
pick a quarrel = 挑起争端
quarrel with someone = 与某人争吵
quarrel over something = 为某事争吵
a petty quarrel = 一场小争吵

词源:
The word "quarrel" comes from the Old French word "querelle," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "querela," meaning "complaint" or "grievance."  The evolution reflects a shift in meaning from a formal complaint to a heated argument.
