earshot:/ˈɪəʃɒt/| n. |听力范围，听得见的距离|The range within which one can hear.  It refers to the distance within which sounds are audible.|They spoke in hushed tones, just outside earshot.|近义词：hearing distance, range of hearing|反义词：out of earshot


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is always within earshot of his mother. = 他总是待在他母亲的听力范围内。
The children are within earshot. = 孩子们在听力范围内。


一般过去时:
She was just outside earshot when they discussed the plan. = 当他们讨论计划时，她刚好不在听力范围内。
The teacher stayed within earshot of the students. = 老师待在学生的听力范围内。


现在完成时:
He has remained within earshot the entire time. = 他一直都在听力范围内。
We have been within earshot of the music all evening. = 我们整个晚上都在音乐的听力范围内。


过去完成时:
She had been within earshot before the argument started. = 在争吵开始之前，她一直在听力范围内。
They had moved out of earshot before the explosion. = 在爆炸发生之前，他们已经移出了听力范围。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he were within earshot, he would hear them. = 如果他在听力范围内，他会听到他们。
I wish I were within earshot to hear what they are saying. = 我真希望我在听力范围内，能听到他们在说什么。


被动语态:
The conversation was easily overheard because they were within earshot. = 因为他们在听力范围内，所以很容易听到他们的谈话。
The announcement was made within earshot of everyone present. = 宣布是在所有在场人员的听力范围内进行的。


疑问句:

否定句:
They were out of earshot. = 他们不在听力范围内。
The sound was barely audible, even within earshot. = 即使在听力范围内，声音也很难听到。
