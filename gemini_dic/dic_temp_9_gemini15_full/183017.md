replicabilities: /ˌrɛplɪkəˈbɪlətiz/ | n. | 可重复性，可复制性 | The ability of a study or experiment to be reproduced with consistent results.  It refers to the extent to which a study can be replicated by independent researchers and obtain similar findings.  This is crucial for establishing the validity and reliability of scientific findings. | Example: The high replicability of the experiment strengthened its conclusions.  这个实验的高可重复性加强了它的结论。 The low replicability of the study raised concerns about its methodology. 这项研究的可重复性低，让人对其方法论产生担忧。 | 近义词：reproducibility, repeatability | 反义词：unreliability, inconsistency
