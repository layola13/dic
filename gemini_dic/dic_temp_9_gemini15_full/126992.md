nasalising: /ˈneɪzəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 鼻化 | The act of producing a sound with air passing through the nose, as in nasal consonants or vowels.  It often involves lowering the velum (soft palate) to allow air to flow through the nasal cavity during speech.  | Example:  Nasalising the vowels gives a distinct quality to the pronunciation. 鼻化元音使发音具有独特的音质。 | 近义词: nasalization | 反义词: oralization
