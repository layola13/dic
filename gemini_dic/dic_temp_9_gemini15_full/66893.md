expedients:/ɪkˈspiːdiənts/ | n. |权宜之计；临时措施；方便之物 |A means to an end, often a temporary or less-than-ideal solution chosen for its practicality.  Expedient actions are often taken to solve an immediate problem, even if they may have undesirable long-term consequences. |例句：They resorted to expedients to overcome the shortage of supplies. 他们采取权宜之计来克服物资短缺。

例句：As a temporary expedient, we used plastic sheeting to cover the roof. 作为权宜之计，我们用塑料薄膜盖住了屋顶。


近义词：measures, devices, solutions, makeshifts, stopgaps | 反义词：permanent solutions, long-term strategies, ideal solutions | 词性：名词


词源:  From Middle English *expedient, from Anglo-French *expedient, from Latin *expedire (to extricate, free, expedite).  The sense shifted from "expeditious" to "convenient" and then to "a convenient means."
