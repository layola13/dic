die:/daɪ/| v.|死，死亡；枯萎；消亡 |to cease to live; to become extinct or defunct; to fade or wither|例句：He died peacefully in his sleep. 他在睡梦中安详地去世了。
The old tree died last winter.  这棵老树去年冬天死了。|近义词：expire, perish, decease |反义词：live, survive


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dies every time he watches a horror movie. 他每次看恐怖片都吓死了。
The flower dies without water. 花儿缺水就会枯萎。

一般过去时:
My grandfather died last year. 我的祖父去年去世了。
The plant died because it lacked sunlight.  植物因为缺乏阳光而枯死了。

现在完成时:
She has died from a sudden illness. 她死于突发疾病。
The company has died a slow death. 这家公司慢慢地走向了衰亡。

过去完成时:
He had died before the doctor arrived.  医生到达之前他已经去世了。
The project had died before it even began.  这个项目还没开始就夭折了。

将来时:
He will die soon if he doesn't get treatment. 如果他不接受治疗，他很快就会死。
The flowers will die without regular watering.  这些花如果不经常浇水就会枯死。

虚拟语气:
If I didn't have my family, I would die of loneliness. 如果我没有我的家人，我会孤独而死。
I wish I could die peacefully in my sleep. 我希望我能够安详地睡梦中死去。

被动语态:
The enemy was reported to have died in the battle. 据报道，敌人在战斗中阵亡。
Many soldiers died in the war. 许多士兵死于战争。 (Note:  "die" is rarely used passively in this simple sense.)


疑问句:
Did he die a natural death? 他是自然死亡的吗？
When will this plant die? 这株植物什么时候会死？

否定句:
He did not die immediately. 他没有立即死亡。
The rumor that he died was false.  关于他死亡的谣言是假的。


PHRASE->
die down:逐渐平息；减弱
die away:逐渐消失；减弱
die hard:顽固存在；难以改变
die of:死于（疾病等）
die for:渴望；非常想要


词源:
The word "die" comes from the Old English word *dēag, which meant "to die".  This word is related to other Germanic words with similar meanings.  Its ultimate origins are Proto-Germanic *dē- and Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁-, which had a broader meaning related to "to perish" or "to pass away".
