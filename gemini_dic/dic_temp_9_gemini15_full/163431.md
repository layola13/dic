preconsumers: /ˌpriːkənˈsjuːmɚz/ | n. | 预消费者，潜在消费者 | Individuals or groups who are likely to become consumers in the future.  They may not currently have the purchasing power or need for a particular product or service, but are expected to in the future.  The term is often used in market research and marketing strategies to identify and target potential customers. | Example:  Marketers are increasingly focusing their efforts on preconsumers, particularly younger generations with high future earning potential.  市场营销人员越来越关注预消费者，特别是那些未来收入潜力高的年轻一代。| 近义词: potential customers, future consumers, prospective buyers | 反义词: existing customers, current consumers | 名词
