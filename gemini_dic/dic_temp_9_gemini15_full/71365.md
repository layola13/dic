flagellata:/ˌflædʒəˈlætə/| n.|鞭毛虫；鞭毛生物|A single-celled organism with one or more flagella (whip-like appendages) used for locomotion.  They are a diverse group found in various environments, including freshwater, saltwater, and soil.|Example:  Many *flagellata* are parasitic, causing diseases in humans and animals. 许多鞭毛虫是寄生虫，会导致人和动物患病。|近义词：mastigophora (less common)|反义词：无
