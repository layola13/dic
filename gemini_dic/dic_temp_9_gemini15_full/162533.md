prealarmed:/ˌpriːəˈlɑːrmd/| adj.| 预先恐慌的；过早地感到惊恐的 |  Prealarmed describes someone who is feeling alarm or fear before something has happened or before there is a real reason to be afraid. It implies an excessive or premature anxiety. | Example: The prealarmed mother checked her child's temperature every hour.  这位过早感到恐慌的母亲每小时都检查孩子的体温。| 近义词：anxious, apprehensive, worried, nervous | 反义词：calm, composed, relaxed, unconcerned
