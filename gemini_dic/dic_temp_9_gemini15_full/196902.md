shaders:/ˈʃeɪdərz/ | n. |着色器 |A shader is a small program that runs on a graphics processing unit (GPU) to determine the color of each pixel in an image.  Shaders are used in computer graphics to create realistic and visually appealing images and effects. They are written in specialized shading languages like GLSL (OpenGL Shading Language) or HLSL (High-Level Shading Language).| Example:  Modern video games rely heavily on shaders to render complex scenes and effects.  现代电子游戏很大程度上依赖着色器来渲染复杂的场景和特效。|近义词:program, filter (in some contexts) |反义词: N/A
