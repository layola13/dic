carpooler:/ˈkɑːrpuːlər/| n. |拼车者，合乘者 | A person who participates in carpooling; someone who regularly shares a car ride with others, typically to commute to work or school.  | Example:  She's a carpooler, sharing rides with her neighbors to save on gas. 她是一个拼车者，和邻居合乘以节省汽油。 |近义词：ride-sharer, commuter (in the context of carpooling) |反义词：solo driver, individual commuter


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He carpools to work every day. = 他每天拼车上班。
They carpool with their neighbors. = 他们和邻居拼车。


一般过去时:
She carpooled with her colleagues last year. = 去年她和同事拼车。
We carpooled to the concert. = 我们拼车去参加音乐会。


现在完成时:
He has carpooled for five years. = 他拼车五年了。
They have carpooled to school since September. = 从九月起他们就拼车去学校。


过去完成时:
Before she bought a car, she had carpooled for years. = 在她买车之前，她拼车好几年了。
They had carpooled to the airport before the flight was delayed. = 在航班延误之前，他们已经拼车去了机场。

I wish I could carpool, but it's too inconvenient. = 我真希望我能拼车，但是太不方便了。


被动语态:

否定句:

词源:
The word "carpooler" is a relatively modern term, a combination of "car" and "pool."  "Pool" in this context refers to the shared resource or collective effort.  The term emerged as carpooling became a more common practice, particularly in the mid-20th century.
