peacocked:/ˈpiːkɒkt/| v.|炫耀，卖弄，自鸣得意|To behave in a vain or ostentatious manner, typically to attract attention.|例句：He peacocked around the party, showing off his new car. 他在派对上炫耀他的新车，自鸣得意。

例句：She peacocked her expensive jewelry at the gala. 她在盛会上炫耀她昂贵的珠宝。


近义词：show off, boast, swagger, strut

反义词：humble oneself, be modest, be self-effacing

词源:源自孔雀（peacock）的行为，孔雀会展开华丽的尾羽以吸引异性。  The verb "peacock" is derived from the behavior of peacocks, who display their impressive plumage to attract mates.  This behavior is associated with vanity and ostentation.
