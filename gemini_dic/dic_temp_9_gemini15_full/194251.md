self-multiplied:/ˌsɛlfˈmʌltɪplaɪd/| adj.| 自乘的，自身相乘的 |  Describes a number or quantity that is multiplied by itself.  It implies a process of repeated multiplication where the result of each multiplication is used as a factor in the subsequent multiplication.  This term is commonly used in mathematics, particularly in discussions of exponential growth or geometric sequences.| 例句：The self-multiplied value quickly became astronomical. 自乘的值迅速变得天文数字般巨大。
