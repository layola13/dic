lunatic:/ˈluːnətɪk/ | adj., n. | 疯子；精神错乱的；荒唐的 | A lunatic is a person who is mentally ill, especially one exhibiting violent or erratic behavior.  The word can also be used informally to describe someone behaving irrationally or foolishly. |例句：He acted like a complete lunatic, driving his car at dangerous speeds. 他像个疯子一样开车，车速很快很危险。

The doctor diagnosed him as a lunatic. 医生诊断他为精神错乱。

近义词：insane, mad, crazy, demented, deranged | 反义词：sane, rational, sensible, composed

词源:
The word "lunatic" ultimately derives from the Latin word "luna," meaning "moon."  The ancient belief linked mental illness to the phases of the moon, hence the association.  The word evolved through Old French "lunatique" before entering English.
