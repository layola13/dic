glamorises:/ˈɡlæməraɪzɪz/| v.|美化；使具有魅力；渲染；|To represent something in an attractive and exciting way, often ignoring its less pleasant aspects.|The film glamorises the life of a drug dealer, ignoring the harmful consequences of his actions. 这部电影美化了毒贩的生活，忽略了他行为的有害后果。|glorifies, romanticizes, idealizes|exposes, criticizes, denounces|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The media glamorises celebrity culture. = 媒体美化了名流文化。
This advertisement glamorises unhealthy eating habits. = 这则广告美化了不健康的饮食习惯。


一般过去时:
Old Hollywood films glamorized the lives of the rich and famous. = 好莱坞老电影美化了富人和名人的生活。
The magazine glamorized a dangerous lifestyle. = 这本杂志美化了一种危险的生活方式。


现在完成时:
Society has glamorized wealth and power for centuries. = 社会几个世纪以来一直美化财富和权力。
The fashion industry has glamorized unrealistic body images. = 时尚产业一直在美化不切实际的体型形象。


过去完成时:
They had glamorized the war before it even started. = 战争甚至开始之前，他们就已经美化了它。
The media had glamorized the athlete's achievements before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，媒体就已经美化了这位运动员的成就。


将来时:
Future films will glamorize space exploration. = 未来的电影将美化太空探索。
The campaign will glamorize sustainable living. = 这场运动将美化可持续的生活方式。


虚拟语气:
If the media didn't glamorize violence, perhaps there would be less of it. = 如果媒体不美化暴力，也许暴力就会减少。
I wish they wouldn't glamorize such irresponsible behavior. = 我希望他们不要美化这种不负责任的行为。


被动语态:
The dangers of smoking are often glamorized in movies. = 吸烟的危险常常在电影中被美化。
Unrealistic beauty standards are constantly glamorized by the advertising industry. = 不切实际的美丽标准不断被广告业美化。


疑问句:

否定句:

This article does not glamorize the celebrity's life. = 这篇文章并没有美化这位名人的生活。
