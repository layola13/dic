nonerasable: /ˌnɒnɪˈreɪsəbl/ | adj. | 不可擦除的，永久性的 |  The word "nonerasable" means incapable of being erased or removed.  It typically refers to data or markings that are permanently fixed and cannot be deleted or altered.  This characteristic might be due to the nature of the storage medium or a deliberate design choice. |


例句：

The data on the chip is nonerasable.  芯片上的数据是不可擦除的。

The inscription on the monument is nonerasable, a testament to their enduring love.  纪念碑上的铭文是不可磨灭的，证明了他们永恒的爱。


近义词：permanent, indelible, immutable, fixed


反义词：erasable, removable, alterable, changeable


词源：

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not"
* **erasable**:  adjective derived from the verb "erase," meaning "to remove" or "to obliterate."  "Erase" ultimately comes from the Old French *eraser* and Latin *radere* ("to scrape").
