nazarite:/ˈnæzəraɪt/| n.|纳西尔人，纳细耳人|A member of a group of Israelites who took a special vow of consecration to God, involving abstaining from wine, strong drink, and certain foods, and letting their hair grow long.  They were considered holy and set apart for God's service.|The Nazirites played a significant role in ancient Israelite religion, often associated with prophecy and divine power.  Samson is a famous example of a Nazirites.|近义词：consecrated person, devotee|反义词：profane person
