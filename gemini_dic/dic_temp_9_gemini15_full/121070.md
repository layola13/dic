misconstrued:/ˌmɪskənˈstruːd/| v. |误解，曲解 |To understand wrongly; interpret incorrectly.  |例句：His remarks were completely misconstrued. 他的话完全被误解了。
例句：I think my intentions have been misconstrued. 我认为我的意图被误解了。|近义词：misinterpret, misunderstand, misrepresent |反义词：understand, comprehend


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He misconstrues my intentions. = 他误解了我的意图。
They misconstrue the evidence. = 他们曲解了证据。

一般过去时:
She misconstrued the instructions. = 她误解了指示。
He misconstrued my silence as agreement. = 他误解了我的沉默为同意。

现在完成时:
I have misconstrued the meaning of the poem. = 我误解了这首诗的意思。
They have misconstrued the situation. = 他们误解了形势。

过去完成时:
She had misconstrued his motives from the beginning. = 她从一开始就误解了他的动机。
He realized he had misconstrued the map. = 他意识到他误解了地图。

将来时:
They will misconstrued his actions. = 他们将会误解他的行为。
She will probably misconstrued the message. = 她可能会误解这条信息。

虚拟语气:
If I had not misconstrued his words, I wouldn't have reacted so angrily. = 如果我没有误解他的话，我就不会反应那么生气了。
I wish I hadn't misconstrued her intentions. = 我真希望我没有误解她的意图。

被动语态:
His words were misconstrued by the audience. = 他的话被听众误解了。
The data was misconstrued by the researchers. = 数据被研究人员误解了。

疑问句:
Did they misconstrued the agreement? = 他们是否误解了协议？
Could my words have been misconstrued? = 我的话会被误解吗？

否定句:
I didn't misconstrued his message. = 我没有误解他的信息。
They did not misconstrued the instructions. = 他们没有误解指示。


PHRASE->
be misconstrued = 被误解
easily misconstrued = 容易被误解
commonly misconstrued = 常被误解
intentionally misconstrued = 故意曲解
seriously misconstrued = 严重误解


词源:
mis- (错误地) + construe (解释，理解)  The verb "construe" itself comes from the Latin "construere" (to build, construct), reflecting the idea of building an interpretation from something.
