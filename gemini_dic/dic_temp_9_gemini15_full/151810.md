passe:/ˈpɑːseɪ/| adj.|过时的；过期的；不再流行的|something that is passe is no longer fashionable or popular.  It is outdated or old-fashioned.|That hairstyle is so passe.|近义词：outdated, old-fashioned, outmoded, démodé|反义词：fashionable, trendy, modern, current


词源:
From French *passé*, past participle of *passer*, to pass.  The word reflects the sense of something having "passed" its time of popularity.


用法说明:

例句:

His ideas on education are rather passe. 他的教育理念相当过时。

That style of dress is completely passe now. 那种穿着风格现在已经完全过时了。


PHRASE->

passe partout (法语):  a universal key or passport; something that allows access to many things.  (This phrase is less common in English, but included for completeness given the word's French origin)


注意：passe主要用作形容词，没有名词或动词的用法。  因此，没有一般现在时、一般过去时等语法范例，也无被动语态或虚拟语气例句。  由于它是一个形容词，否定句和疑问句的构成方式与其他形容词相同。

例句 (否定句):

That design is not passe; it's making a comeback.  那个设计并没有过时；它正在卷土重来。

The music isn't passe; it's classic. 这音乐并没有过时；它是经典的。


例句 (疑问句):

Is that fashion passe? 那种时尚过时了吗？

Do you think this technology is passe? 你认为这项技术过时了吗？
