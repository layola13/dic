choleric: /ˈkɒlərɪk/ | /ˈkɑːlərɪk/ | adj. | 易怒的；暴躁的；脾气坏的 | Easily angered; bad-tempered.  |


英文解析:The word "choleric" describes someone who is easily angered, irritable, and prone to outbursts of temper.  It derives from the historical belief in the four humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile), with yellow bile ("choler") being associated with anger and irritability.


例句:

He has a choleric temperament and often loses his temper.  = 他脾气暴躁，经常发脾气。

The choleric general barked orders at his troops. =  那个易怒的将军向他的部队咆哮着下达命令。


近义词: irascible, irritable, testy, quick-tempered, short-tempered, hot-headed, fiery


反义词: placid, calm, even-tempered, good-natured, serene


词源: From Medieval Latin *cholericus, from Latin cholera "bile," from Greek kholé "bile."  The association of bile with anger is ancient.
