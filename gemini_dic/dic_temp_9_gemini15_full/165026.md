preholding: /ˌpriːˈhoʊldɪŋ/ | n. | 预先持有；预先掌控；预先控制 |  The act of holding something beforehand, often in preparation for a later action or event.  It suggests a temporary possession or control before a more permanent or official arrangement.  | 例句：The preholding of evidence is crucial for a successful prosecution.  预先持有证据对成功的起诉至关重要。

The preholding of funds ensured the project's timely completion. 预先控制资金确保了项目的按时完成。| 近义词：advance possession, prior control, preliminary holding | 反义词：release, relinquishment, surrender |  词性:名词
