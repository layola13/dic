evictions:/ɪˈvɪkʃənz/| n.|驱逐，逐出|The act of evicting someone, especially a tenant, from a property.  Evictions often result from non-payment of rent or violation of lease agreements.|例句：The evictions left many families homeless. 驱逐行动使许多家庭无家可归。|例句：The recent wave of evictions has sparked protests. 最近的驱逐浪潮引发了抗议活动。|近义词：expulsions, removals, ousters|反义词：installations, admissions|名词

Evictions are a serious problem in many cities. = 驱逐在许多城市是一个严重的问题。

Several families experienced evictions last year. = 去年有几个家庭经历了驱逐。
The court ordered the evictions of several squatters. = 法院下令驱逐几名非法居住者。

被动语态:

词源:
The word "eviction" comes from the Old French word "eviction," meaning "a driving out," which in turn derives from the Latin word "evictio," meaning "a dispossession."  The root is the verb "evicere," meaning "to drive out," "to expel," or "to dispossess."
