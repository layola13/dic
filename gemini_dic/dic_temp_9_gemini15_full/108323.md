laurus:/ˈlɔːrəs/| n.|月桂树；桂冠；荣誉|The word "laurus" refers to the bay laurel tree (Laurus nobilis), a type of evergreen tree with aromatic leaves.  It is also used figuratively to represent victory, glory, or honor, often in the form of a laurel wreath.|例句：The athlete was crowned with a laurus after winning the race. 运动员在赢得比赛后戴上了桂冠。|近义词：bay tree, laurel wreath, glory, honor |反义词：shame, dishonor


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and does not conjugate through tenses like verbs.  Therefore, tense examples are not applicable.


PHRASE->
laurus nobilis: 月桂树 (the scientific name)
laurel wreath: 桂冠
bay leaf: 月桂叶
laureate: 桂冠诗人/获奖者
on one's laurels:  躺在功劳簿上 (resting on past achievements)
