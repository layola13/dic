mass-observationist:/ˈmæsˌɒbzəˈveɪʃənɪst/ | n. | 大众观察者 | A participant in mass-observation, a research technique involving the collection of data from a large number of people to study social trends and behaviors.  They contribute observations of daily life, opinions, and experiences. | The mass-observationist meticulously recorded their observations of everyday life in their diary.  大众观察者一丝不苟地将他们对日常生活的观察记录在日记中。| participant, observer, recorder |  (无明显的反义词，可考虑 non-participant) | 名词

词源:
The word is a combination of "mass-observation" (a social research technique) and "-ist" (a suffix indicating a person who does something).  Mass-observation itself originated in the 1930s in Britain as a method for studying the behavior and opinions of ordinary people on a large scale.
