seizing: /ˈsiːzɪŋ/ | v. | 抓住；夺取；没收；利用 | To take hold of something suddenly and forcefully; to take control of something; to confiscate. | 近义词：grabbing, snatching, capturing, appropriating | 反义词：releasing, relinquishing, surrendering


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The police are seizing illegal drugs. = 警方正在没收非法毒品。
She is seizing the opportunity to travel. = 她正在抓住旅行的机会。

一般过去时:
He seized the thief's weapon. = 他夺下了小偷的武器。
The government seized the company's assets. = 政府没收了该公司的资产。

现在完成时:
The authorities have seized a large quantity of counterfeit goods. = 当局已经查获了大量的假冒商品。
They have seized control of the situation. = 他们已经控制住了局面。

过去完成时:
The police had seized the evidence before the suspect arrived. = 在嫌疑犯到达之前，警方已经查获了证据。
Before the revolution, the king had seized all the power. = 在革命之前，国王已经掌握了所有的权力。


将来时:
The police will seize the contraband. = 警方将会查抄违禁品。
We will seize this chance to improve our skills. = 我们将抓住这个机会提高我们的技能。

虚拟语气:
If I seized the opportunity, I could have achieved more. = 如果我抓住了机会，我本可以取得更大的成就。
I wish I had seized the moment and told her how I felt. = 我真希望我当时抓住了机会，告诉她我的感受。

被动语态:
The stolen goods were seized by the police. = 被盗的货物被警方查获了。
The building was seized by the authorities. = 这座建筑物被当局查封了。

疑问句:
Were the documents seized by the authorities? = 文件是被当局没收的吗？
Will the property be seized? = 房产会被没收吗？

否定句:
The police did not seize any weapons. = 警方没有缴获任何武器。
She didn't seize the chance to speak. = 她没有抓住机会说话。


PHRASE->
seize control = 控制
seize an opportunity = 抓住机会
seize power = 夺取政权
seize the moment = 抓住时机
seize upon = 抓住；利用


词源:
From Old French *seisir, from Vulgar Latin *exquirere, from Latin ex- "out" + quaerere "to seek".  The original meaning was to take possession of, which evolved to its current meanings.
