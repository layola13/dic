scrambler:/ˈskræmblər/| n. v.|扰频器；搅乱者；（食物）搅拌器；(使)混乱；(使)难以理解| A scrambler is a device that makes a signal difficult to understand, often used to protect communications.  It can also refer to something or someone that causes confusion or disorder. As a verb, it means to make something into a confused or disordered state.|例句：The police used a signal scrambler to intercept the criminal's communication. 警方使用信号扰频器拦截罪犯的通讯。

The chef scrambled the eggs for breakfast. 厨师为早餐炒鸡蛋。|近义词：jammer, mixer, confuser |反义词：clarifier, organizer
