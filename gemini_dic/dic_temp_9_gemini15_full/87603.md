heterotopy:/ˈhɛtərətoʊpi/ | n. |异位；异位现象；异位器官|  A heterotopy is the presence of tissue or an organ in an abnormal location.  This can occur during embryonic development, where cells migrate to an incorrect location, or it can be a result of other developmental processes.  Heterotopies can be benign or malignant, depending on the type of tissue involved and its behavior. | Example:  The patient presented with a gastric heterotopy in the pancreas.  这位病人胰腺出现了胃组织异位。|近义词：ectopia, displacement |反义词：normal location, in situ
