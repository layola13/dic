doodled:/ˈduːdəld/ | v. |涂鸦；乱画 |To draw or scribble something carelessly or without much thought.  It often implies a lack of serious intention or artistic skill. |例句：I doodled in my notebook during the boring lecture.  我在无聊的讲座上在笔记本上涂鸦。

例句：The children doodled all over the walls. 孩子们在墙上到处乱涂乱画。


近义词：scribbled, sketched (lightly), scrawled |反义词：painted (carefully), drew (precisely)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He doodles in his spare time. = 他在空闲时间涂鸦。
She doodles on napkins while waiting. = 她等候时在餐巾纸上乱涂乱画。


一般过去时:
I doodled a funny face on my homework. = 我在我的作业上涂鸦了一个滑稽的脸。
They doodled during the meeting. = 他们会议期间在乱涂乱画。


现在完成时:
He has doodled all over his textbook. = 他在他的教科书上到处乱涂乱画。
She has doodled a lot since she was a child. = 她从小就画了很多涂鸦。


过去完成时:
I had doodled several designs before settling on one. = 我在决定一个设计之前已经画了好几个涂鸦。
She had doodled all over the paper before I saw it. = 我看到之前，她已经在纸上到处乱涂乱画了。


将来时:

She will doodle some pictures for the children. = 她将为孩子们画一些涂鸦。


虚拟语气:

I wish I could doodle as well as she does. = 我希望我的涂鸦技术能和她一样好。


被动语态:
