polymers:/ˈpɑːlɪmɜːrz/ (US) /ˈpɒlɪmərz/ (UK)| n. |聚合物，高分子|A polymer is a large molecule, or macromolecule, composed of many repeated subunits.  These subunits are called monomers. Polymers are found everywhere, from natural materials like DNA and proteins to synthetic materials like plastics and rubber.  They have a wide range of properties depending on the type and arrangement of their monomers.|例句：Many plastics are polymers. 许多塑料都是聚合物。|例句：The study of polymers is a vast and important field of chemistry.  聚合物研究是化学领域中一个庞大而重要的分支。|近义词：macromolecule,  polymeric substance|反义词：monomer (single unit)
