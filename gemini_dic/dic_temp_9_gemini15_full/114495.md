malacostracan:/ˌmæləˈkɒstrəkən/| n. |软甲纲动物|A malacostracan is a crustacean belonging to the class Malacostraca, the largest class of crustaceans, which includes familiar animals such as crabs, lobsters, shrimps, and prawns.  They are characterized by a segmented body with a distinct head, thorax, and abdomen, and typically have ten legs. |Example: Lobsters and crabs are examples of malacostracans.  龙虾和螃蟹是软甲纲动物的例子。|近义词: crustacean (泛指甲壳类)|反义词:  (无明确的反义词，可考虑非甲壳类动物如昆虫等，但意义上并非反义)|名词

词源:
The word "malacostracan" comes from the scientific classification of crustaceans.  "Malacostraca" is the name of the class, derived from Greek:  μαλακός (malakos) meaning "soft" and ὄστρακον (ostrakon) meaning "shell".  The "-an" suffix indicates a member of a group.  Therefore, the name refers to crustaceans with a relatively soft shell compared to some other groups.
