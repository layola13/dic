chasm:/ˈkæzəm/| n.|深渊；裂缝；分歧|A chasm is a deep crack or opening in the ground. It can also refer to a significant difference or division between people or things.  |例句：A deep chasm separated the two mountain peaks.  一条深深的裂缝将两座山峰隔开。

The chasm between the rich and the poor is widening. 富人和穷人之间的差距越来越大。|近义词：gulf, abyss, rift, divide|反义词：unity, agreement, closure


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The chasm yawns before us. = 深渊在我们面前张开。
A significant chasm exists between their opinions. = 他们的意见之间存在着巨大的分歧。


一般过去时:
A deep chasm opened up in the earth. = 地面上出现了一个深深的裂缝。
A chasm developed between the two political parties. = 两大政党之间产生了分歧。


现在完成时:
A vast chasm has formed in the ice sheet. = 在冰盖上形成了一道巨大的裂缝。
A chasm has opened up between the government and the people. = 政府和人民之间产生了隔阂。


过去完成时:
A chasm had already formed before the earthquake. = 地震发生前就已经形成了裂缝。
A chasm had developed in their relationship before the argument. = 争吵之前，他们的关系就已经破裂了。


将来时:

A chasm will inevitably form between the two groups if they continue to disagree. = 如果他们继续意见不合，两组人之间不可避免地会产生分歧。


虚拟语气:
If there were no chasm, we could cross easily. = 如果没有裂缝，我们可以轻松地过去。

被动语态:
