pivot:/ˈpɪvət/ | n., v. |枢轴，中心点；旋转；转向 |

英文解析:As a noun, a pivot refers to a central point, person, or thing on which something else turns or depends.  As a verb, it means to turn or change direction.  It can also refer to the act of changing one's strategy or focus.

例句：

The company's success pivots on the launch of its new product.  = 公司的成功取决于其新产品的发布。

The dancer pivoted gracefully on one foot. = 舞者优雅地单脚旋转。

近义词：hinge, turning point, fulcrum, rotate, shift, change

反义词：remain static, stay fixed, continue unchanged


词性:Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pivots the table to face the window. = 他旋转桌子使它面向窗户。
The economy pivots on the success of small businesses. = 经济的运转取决于小型企业的成功。


一般过去时:
She pivoted sharply to avoid the oncoming car. = 她迅速转向以避开迎面而来的汽车。
The government pivoted its policy after the election. = 政府在大选后改变了其政策。


现在完成时:
The company has pivoted its marketing strategy several times. = 公司已经多次调整了其营销策略。
The situation has pivoted dramatically in the last few weeks. = 在过去的几周里，情况发生了巨大的变化。


过去完成时:

The team had pivoted its focus to the new project by the deadline. = 团队在截止日期前已经将重点转向了新的项目。


将来时:

The company will pivot to a new market next year. = 公司明年将进军新的市场。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
