marshalled:/ˈmɑːʃəld/ | vt. |  整理；部署；引导；排列 | To arrange or organize (a group of people or things) in a orderly way; to lead or guide (troops, etc.) |例句：The troops were marshalled for the parade.  军队列队准备阅兵。

The general marshalled his forces before the battle. 将军在战斗前部署了他的部队。


近义词：arrange, organize, deploy, muster | 反义词：disorganize, scatter, disperse | 词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The conductor marshals the orchestra before the concert. 指挥在音乐会前指挥乐队。
She marshals her arguments effectively in her essay.  她在论文中有效地组织了她的论据。


一般过去时:
The police marshalled the crowd away from the scene of the accident. 警察将人群疏散到事故现场以外。
He marshalled his thoughts before answering the question. 他在回答问题之前整理了一下思路。


现在完成时:
She has marshalled considerable evidence to support her claim. 她已收集了大量证据来支持她的说法。
The company has marshalled its resources to meet the challenge. 公司已集中资源以应对挑战。


过去完成时:
The general had marshalled his troops before the enemy attack. 将军在敌人进攻前已部署好他的军队。
They had marshalled their arguments carefully before the debate. 他们在辩论前已仔细地组织好了他们的论据。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

词源:
源自古法语 *maréchal，意思是“马夫”，最终源自日耳曼语系，与英语“master”同源。  最初指管理马匹的人，后来引申为军队指挥官，再后来引申为“整理”、“部署”等含义。
