deflations:/dɪˈfleɪʃənz/| n. |通货紧缩；紧缩；放气|The reduction in the general level of prices for goods and services in an economy.  It is the opposite of inflation. Deflation can be caused by a decrease in consumer spending, a decrease in the money supply, or an increase in the productivity of goods and services.|例句：The government is worried about the possibility of deflation. 政府担心可能出现通货紧缩。|例句：The deflation of the tires made the car difficult to drive. 轮胎放气使得汽车难以驾驶。|近义词：depression, contraction, shrinkage|反义词：inflation
