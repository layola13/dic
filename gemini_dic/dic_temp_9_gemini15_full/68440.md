falters:/ˈfɔːltərz/|/ˈfɔːltɚz/| v.| 蹒跚；犹豫；衰弱；衰退 | To move or act with uncertainty or hesitancy; to stumble; to weaken or decline. |例句：His voice faltered as he tried to speak. 他说话时声音颤抖。

例句：The negotiations faltered due to a lack of trust.  由于缺乏信任，谈判陷入僵局。


近义词：hesitate, stumble, waver, weaken, decline | 反义词：strengthen, improve, succeed, persevere


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He falters in his speech. = 他说话结巴。
The economy falters under the pressure. = 经济在压力下衰退。


一般过去时:
My courage faltered at the last moment. = 我在最后时刻胆怯了。
The project faltered due to funding issues. =  由于资金问题，项目进展受阻。


现在完成时:
His confidence has faltered since the accident. = 自从事故发生后，他的信心动摇了。
The market has faltered in recent weeks. = 近几周市场表现不佳。


过去完成时:
His resolve had faltered before the challenge. = 他在挑战面前动摇了决心。
The plan had faltered long before its deadline. = 计划在截止日期很久之前就已经失败了。


将来时:
I fear my resolve will falter if things get worse. = 我担心如果情况恶化，我的决心会动摇。
The company's growth is expected to falter next year. = 预计该公司明年的增长将会放缓。


虚拟语气:
If his courage had not faltered, he would have won the race. = 如果他没有胆怯，他就会赢得比赛。
I wish my strength wouldn't falter during the marathon. = 我希望我在马拉松比赛中不会体力不支。


被动语态:
His steps were faltered by the uneven ground. = 他在崎岖不平的地面上步履蹒跚。
The negotiations were faltered by disagreements. = 谈判因分歧而受阻。


疑问句:
Does your support falter in the face of adversity? = 面对逆境，你的支持会动摇吗？
Will the government's reforms falter under criticism? = 政府的改革会在批评声中失败吗？


否定句:
His determination did not falter despite the setbacks. = 尽管遇到挫折，他的决心并没有动摇。
The soldier's resolve never faltered during the battle. = 在战斗中，士兵的决心从未动摇。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "falter" comes from the Old English word "fealtian," meaning "to stumble" or "to fail."  It's related to other Germanic words with similar meanings.
