cobishop:/ˈkoʊˌbɪʃəp/| n.|助理主教|A cobishop is a bishop who assists another bishop, typically in a large diocese or archdiocese.  The specific responsibilities and authority of a cobishop vary depending on the church and its internal structure.  A cobishop might share in the administration of the diocese, assist with pastoral duties, or have specialized responsibilities.  The term is less common than "assistant bishop" but implies a closer working relationship and a sharing of responsibility.|The Archbishop appointed a cobishop to help manage the sprawling diocese.|近义词：assistant bishop, suffragan bishop|反义词：None|名词
