labiodental:/ˌleɪbiəʊˈdentəl/| adj.|唇齿的|Relating to or involving both the lips and the teeth.  The term is commonly used in phonetics to describe sounds produced by bringing the lower lip into contact with the upper teeth.  For example, the English sounds /f/ and /v/ are labiodental fricatives.|The /f/ sound in "fish" is a labiodental fricative.|近义词：labiodental sound|反义词：None specifically, but antonyms could be described as sounds produced by other articulatory mechanisms (e.g., alveolar, velar).
