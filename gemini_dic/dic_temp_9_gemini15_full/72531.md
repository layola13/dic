fluster:/ˈflʌstər/| vt. & n. |使慌乱；使激动；慌乱；激动|To make (someone) agitated or confused.  To be agitated or confused.|The unexpected news flustered her.  Unexpected news flustered her. |近义词：agitate, confuse, perturb, upset |反义词：calm, soothe, tranquilize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud noise flusters the birds. = 响亮的噪音使鸟儿惊慌。
He flusters easily in social situations. = 他在社交场合很容易紧张。


一般过去时:
The unexpected question flustered him. = 意外的问题使他慌乱。
The sudden announcement flustered the audience. = 突然的宣布使观众慌乱。


现在完成时:
The children have flustered me with their constant demands. = 孩子们不断的要求让我心烦意乱。
She has flustered herself by trying to do too much. = 她因为试图做太多事情而弄得自己手忙脚乱。


过去完成时:
He had flustered himself by overthinking the presentation. = 他因为过度思考演示而弄得自己紧张不安。
She had flustered the waiter with her numerous requests. = 她因为许多要求而使服务员手忙脚乱。


将来时:
The upcoming exam will fluster many students. = 即将到来的考试会让许多学生紧张。
I think the speech will fluster him. = 我认为演讲会让他紧张。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't flustered myself, I would have given a better speech. = 如果我没有让自己紧张，我会发表更好的演讲。
I wish I hadn't flustered her with my questions. = 我真希望我没有用我的问题让她紧张。


被动语态:
He was flustered by the unexpected visitor. = 他被不速之客弄得慌乱。
The students were flustered by the difficult exam. = 学生们被困难的考试弄得慌乱。


疑问句:
Does this situation fluster you? = 这种情况让你慌乱吗？
What flustered you so much? = 什么事让你如此慌乱？


否定句:
The news didn't fluster her at all. = 这个消息一点也没让她慌乱。
He wasn't flustered by the criticism. = 他并没有因为批评而慌乱。
