druid:/ˈdruːɪd/| n. |德鲁伊，古代凯尔特祭司|A member of the ancient Celtic priesthood in Gaul, Britain, and Ireland, who was believed to possess supernatural powers and knowledge. Druids served as religious leaders, teachers, judges, and healers, holding a significant position in Celtic society. Their practices involved rituals, divination, and herbal medicine.  While much of their beliefs and practices remain shrouded in mystery, historical accounts and archaeological discoveries offer glimpses into their world.|The ancient druids performed rituals in sacred groves. 古代德鲁伊在神圣的树林中举行仪式。|priest, shaman, sage | layman, non-believer | 名词
