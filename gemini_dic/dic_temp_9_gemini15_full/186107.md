rhabdology:/ræbˈdɒlədʒi/| n. |算筹学；算筹术|The study and use of calculating rods or sticks, a form of early calculation.  Rhabdology was a method of performing arithmetic operations using numbered rods.  It was popular in Europe from the 17th century. |Example:  John Napier's invention of Napier's bones simplified rhabdology, making calculations faster and easier.  约翰·纳皮尔的纳皮尔骨头简化了算筹学，使计算更快更容易。|近义词：calculation, computation, arithmetic|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with modern computational methods like digital computing.|名词
