/ˈkætə/ | n. |灾难，大祸 | A catastrophy is a sudden and widespread disaster.  It often involves significant loss of life, property damage, and social disruption. The word is often used to describe events like earthquakes, floods, and major accidents. | Example: The earthquake was a terrible cata; thousands of people lost their homes.  这场地震是一场可怕的灾难；数千人失去了家园。  The volcanic eruption was a cata of epic proportions. 火山爆发是一场史诗般的灾难。|近义词：disaster, calamity, catastrophe |反义词：blessing, fortune, boon |名词
