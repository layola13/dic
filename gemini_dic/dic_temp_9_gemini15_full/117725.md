melanesia:/ˌmɛləˈniːʒə/| n. |美拉尼西亚|A large group of islands in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, including Fiji, Vanuatu, New Caledonia, and the Solomon Islands.  Melanesia is characterized by its diverse cultures, languages, and geographies. The term "Melanesia" derives from Greek words meaning "black islands," referring to the dark skin of the indigenous peoples.|Example:  Melanesia is known for its stunning coral reefs and rich biodiversity. 美拉尼西亚以其令人惊叹的珊瑚礁和丰富的生物多样性而闻名。|近义词：Oceania (a broader term)|反义词：N/A
