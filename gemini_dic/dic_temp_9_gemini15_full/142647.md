ostracising: /ˈɒstrəsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 排斥；放逐；冷落 |  The act of excluding someone from a society or group.  This often involves deliberate avoidance and shunning.  The term originates from ancient Greece, where ostracism was a formal process of banishing a citizen. |

例句：

He was ostracized by his colleagues after he made a controversial statement. = 他发表了一番有争议的言论后，被同事们排斥了。

The new student felt ostracized because no one talked to her. = 新生感到被排斥，因为没有人和她说话。


近义词：shunning, excluding, isolating, blackballing, boycotting

反义词：including, welcoming, accepting, embracing
