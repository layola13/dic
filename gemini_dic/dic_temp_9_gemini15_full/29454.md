catalysed:/ˈkætəlaɪzd/| v.|催化；促成|To speed up (a chemical reaction) by catalysis.  To act as a catalyst in (a process or reaction).  It can also mean to bring about or accelerate the occurrence of something.|例句：The reaction was catalysed by the addition of a small amount of platinum.  添加少量铂催化了该反应。

The discovery catalysed further research in the field.  这一发现促进了该领域进一步的研究。|近义词：accelerated, facilitated, spurred, triggered|反义词：inhibited, retarded, hindered


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The enzyme catalyses the reaction. = 这种酶催化该反应。
Heat catalyses the decomposition of the substance. = 热量催化该物质的分解。


一般过去时:
The addition of acid catalysed the reaction. = 添加酸催化了该反应。
The meeting catalysed a new wave of optimism. = 会议催生了新一轮的乐观情绪。


现在完成时:
The new policy has catalysed economic growth. = 新政策促进了经济增长。
This discovery has catalysed significant advances in medicine. = 这一发现促进了医学的重大进步。


过去完成时:

将来时:

The upcoming summit will catalyse discussions on climate change. = 即将召开的峰会将促进关于气候变化的讨论。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:
