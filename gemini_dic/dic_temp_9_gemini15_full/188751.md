runaround: /ˈrʌnəraʊnd/ | n. v. | 奔波；瞎忙；欺骗；逃避；（非正式）闲逛 |


英文解析:
As a noun, "runaround" refers to a situation involving much pointless activity or a frustrating experience with many delays and obstacles.  It can also refer to a deceptive maneuver or evasion. As a verb, it means to run about aimlessly, to evade responsibility, or to deceive someone.

例句:
(Noun):  The new regulations caused a lot of runaround for small businesses. = 新规章给小型企业带来了很多麻烦。

(Noun): He gave me the runaround about when the report would be ready. = 他对我什么时候能准备好报告含糊其辞。


(Verb):  I spent the whole day running around trying to find the missing documents. = 我花了一整天的时间到处寻找丢失的文件。

(Verb):  Don't try to runaround your responsibilities. = 不要试图逃避你的责任。


近义词:
(Noun): hassle, trouble, deception, evasion, chase
(Verb): evade, dodge, deceive, fool, wander


反义词:
(Noun): efficiency, straightforwardness, honesty, directness
(Verb): cooperate, confront, be honest


词性:
Noun, Verb
