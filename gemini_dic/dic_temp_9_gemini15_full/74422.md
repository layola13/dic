franchises:/ˈfrænˌtʃaɪzɪz/ | n. |特许经营权；特许经营企业；专营权 |  The right granted by a company to an individual or group to sell its goods or services in a particular area.  A franchise agreement allows the franchisee to use the franchisor's trademarks, brand names, and business systems in exchange for fees and royalties.  This often involves a detailed operating manual and ongoing support from the franchisor. |

例句：
Many fast-food restaurants operate under franchises. = 许多快餐店都在特许经营下运作。

The company is expanding its business by granting more franchises. = 公司通过授予更多特许经营权来扩展业务。


近义词：concessions, licenses, privileges

反义词：None directly opposite, but  "independent businesses" could be considered a contrasting concept.

词性：名词
