reregulate:/ˌriːˈreɡjuleɪt/ | vt. |重新规范，再次管制 | To regulate something again or differently.  This often implies a previous regulatory framework was inadequate or needs adjustment.  |例句：The government decided to reregulate the financial industry after the crisis. 政府决定在危机后重新规范金融业。|例句：They reregulated the water usage to conserve resources. 他们重新规范用水以节约资源。|近义词：reorganize, readjust, reformulate |反义词：deregulate, liberalize |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government reregulates the industry annually. = 政府每年都会重新规范该行业。
They reregulate the traffic flow to reduce congestion. = 他们重新规范交通流量以减少拥堵。


一般过去时:
The council reregulated the building codes after the earthquake. = 地震后，市议会重新规范了建筑规范。
They reregulated the fishing quotas to protect the fish stocks. = 他们重新规范了捕捞配额以保护鱼类资源。


现在完成时:
The agency has reregulated the emission standards several times. = 该机构已经几次重新规范了排放标准。
They have reregulated the market to prevent monopolies. = 他们已经重新规范了市场以防止垄断。


过去完成时:
The company had reregulated its internal procedures before the audit. = 公司在审计之前已经重新规范了其内部程序。
They had reregulated the pricing strategy after losing market share. = 在失去市场份额后，他们已经重新规范了定价策略。


将来时:

They will reregulate the transportation system to improve efficiency. = 他们将重新规范交通系统以提高效率。


虚拟语气:
If the government reregulated the market, the price would stabilize. = 如果政府重新规范市场，价格就会稳定。
