presiding:/prɪˈzaɪdɪŋ/| adj. v.|主持的；担任主席的；主导的| presiding over,  in charge of,  leading, dominating, governing |例句：The judge presided over the trial.  法官主持了审判。|近义词：chairing, conducting, managing, overseeing |反义词：subordinate, following, obeying


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The judge presides over the courtroom. = 法官主持法庭。
The committee presides over the organization's finances. = 委员会负责该组织的财务。

一般过去时:
The mayor presided over the city council meeting. = 市长主持了市议会会议。
She presided over the ceremony with grace. = 她优雅地主持了仪式。

现在完成时:
He has presided over the company for twenty years. = 他掌管这家公司已有二十年了。
The chairman has presided over many important decisions. = 主席主持过许多重要的决定。

过去完成时:
Before the new CEO arrived, she had presided over the department for five years. = 在新任首席执行官到来之前，她已经管理该部门五年了。
They had presided over the club for decades before retiring. = 他们在退休前几十年一直担任俱乐部的负责人。

将来时:
The veteran politician will preside over the upcoming election. = 资深政治家将主持即将到来的选举。
She will preside over the meeting tomorrow. = 她明天将主持会议。

虚拟语气:
If she were to preside, the meeting would be more efficient. = 如果她来主持，会议将会更高效。
I wish a more experienced person would preside over the project. = 我希望一个更有经验的人来主持这个项目。

被动语态:
The meeting was presided over by the headmaster. = 会议由校长主持。
The conference will be presided over by a renowned expert. = 会议将由一位知名专家主持。

疑问句:
Who is presiding over the debate? = 谁在主持辩论？
Who will be presiding at the wedding ceremony? = 婚礼仪式将由谁主持？

否定句:
He did not preside over the meeting. = 他没有主持会议。
No one presided over the chaotic situation. = 没有人控制住混乱的局面。
