chronogram:/ˈkrɒnəɡræm/ | n. |纪年诗；纪年铭文 |A chronogram is a text containing letters or numbers which can be selected to represent a date.  These selected letters, usually Roman numerals, are arranged to spell out a significant date within a longer phrase or sentence.  For example, a sentence might contain letters which when added together equal 1666, representing the date. | The inscription on the tombstone was a chronogram, cleverly concealing the year of death within the epitaph. |近义词：dated inscription, commemorative inscription |反义词：无
