interrepulsions:/ˌɪntərɪˈpʌlʒənz/| n. |相互排斥作用|The mutual repulsion between particles.  This refers to the forces causing particles or objects to repel each other.  Examples include the electrostatic repulsion between similarly charged particles or the steric repulsion between atoms in a molecule that are too close together.  The strength of interrepulsions depends on the nature of the interacting entities and the distance separating them.|The interrepulsions between the electrons in the atom were significant.|近义词：repulsions, mutual repulsions|反义词：attractions, interattractions
