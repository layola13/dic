invokes:/ɪnˈvoʊks/ | vt. | 祈求；恳求；引起；唤起；实施；援引 | To invoke something is to call upon it, especially in a formal or solemn way.  It can refer to calling upon a deity, a law, a feeling, or a memory. | 例句：The shaman invoked ancient spirits to heal the sick.  萨满祭司祈求古代神灵治愈病人。

The government invoked emergency powers to deal with the crisis. 政府援引紧急权力来应对危机。


近义词：evoke, summon, invoke, call upon,  enact | 反义词：ignore, disregard, neglect | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He invokes the name of God in his prayers. 他在祈祷中祈求上帝的名。
The law invokes a strict penalty for this crime. 这项法律对这项罪行施以严厉的惩罚。


一般过去时:
She invoked her rights under the constitution. 她援引宪法赋予她的权利。
The judge invoked a precedent in his ruling. 法官在他的裁决中援引了一个先例。


现在完成时:
They have invoked the help of international organizations. 他们已经请求国际组织的帮助。
The company has invoked its right to appeal the decision. 这家公司已经行使了上诉的权利。


过去完成时:
He had invoked the protection of the gods before the battle. 他在战斗前已经祈求众神的保护。
The lawyer had invoked several legal precedents in her argument. 律师在她的论点中已经援引了几个法律先例。

虚拟语气:

疑问句:
Will the court invoke the relevant legislation? 法院会援引相关的立法吗？

否定句:
The defendant did not invoke his right to remain silent. 被告没有行使他保持沉默的权利。
