morasses:/məˈræsɪz/| n.|沼泽；困境；混乱状态|A morass is an area of muddy or boggy ground.  Figuratively, it can refer to a difficult or confusing situation. |例句：The project quickly became a morass of conflicting ideas and missed deadlines.  这个项目很快变成了充满冲突想法和错过最后期限的困境。

例句：He found himself stuck in a morass of paperwork. 他发现自己陷入了文书工作的泥潭。


近义词：bog, swamp, mire, quagmire, predicament, mess


反义词：clarity, order, simplicity


词性:名词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The marsh is a morass. = 这片沼泽是一片泥潭。
This project is a morass of problems. = 这个项目是一堆问题的泥潭。

一般过去时:
The soldiers got stuck in a morass. = 士兵们陷入了沼泽。

现在完成时:

We have finally escaped the morass of debt. = 我们终于摆脱了债务的泥潭。

将来时:

词源:
The word "morass" comes from the Old French word "mareis," meaning "marsh" or "swamp."  This, in turn, derives from the Latin word "mare," meaning "sea" or "lake."  The figurative meaning of "morass" – a difficult or confusing situation – developed later.
