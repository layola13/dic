purchasing: /ˈpɜːrtʃəsɪŋ/ | n. | 采购；购买 | The act or process of acquiring goods or services.  It involves identifying needs, selecting suppliers, negotiating prices, and managing the delivery and payment of goods or services. | 例句：The company's purchasing department is responsible for sourcing all raw materials. 公司的采购部门负责采购所有原材料。


例句：Our purchasing strategy focuses on long-term relationships with reliable suppliers. 我们的采购策略侧重于与可靠的供应商建立长期关系。


近义词：procurement, buying, acquisition | 反义词：selling, disposal


词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company is purchasing new equipment. = 公司正在采购新设备。
Purchasing decisions are made by the manager. = 采购决定由经理做出。


一般过去时:
They purchased a large quantity of supplies last month. = 他们上个月采购了大量物资。
The department purchased new software. = 部门购买了新的软件。


现在完成时:
We have purchased all the necessary materials for the project. = 我们已经为项目采购了所有必要的材料。

过去完成时:

将来时:

词源:
The word "purchasing" comes from the verb "purchase," which originated from Old French *purchaser (to acquire by purchase),  from Late Latin *purchare (to acquire),  ultimately from a Germanic root related to the Old High German word *for-kaufen (to buy).
