desperados:/ˌdɛspəˈrɑːdoʊz/| n.|亡命之徒，歹徒|Desperate individuals, especially outlaws.  The term often implies a sense of recklessness and defiance.|例句：The desperados robbed the bank and fled into the mountains.  亡命之徒抢劫了银行，然后逃进了山里。

例句：The town was terrified by the presence of the desperados.  这个镇子被亡命之徒的存在吓坏了。


近义词：outlaws, bandits, thugs, criminals, villains

反义词：law-abiding citizens, peacemakers


词源:  From Spanish *desperado*, past participle of *desesperar* "to despair," from *des-* "without" + *esperar* "to hope."  The word entered English in the late 19th century, largely through its use in stories of the American Wild West.
