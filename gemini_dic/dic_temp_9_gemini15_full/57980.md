drive-through:/ˈdraɪvθruː/| n. adj.|得来速；免下车；| A drive-through is a business establishment (such as a restaurant or bank) where customers can be served without leaving their vehicles.  It's characterized by a lane or lanes where vehicles can proceed and receive service. |例句：I ordered my coffee at the drive-through. = 我在得来速点了一杯咖啡。

例句：That bank has a convenient drive-through ATM. = 那家银行有一个方便的得来速ATM机。


近义词：drive-in (less common for establishments other than theaters)


反义词：walk-in (requiring customers to enter the establishment)
