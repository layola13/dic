dargue:/dɑːrɡjuː/|/dɑːrɡjuː/| vt.|争论，辩论|To argue or debate a point of view.|The lawyers argued the case before the judge. 律师们在法官面前辩论此案。|argue, debate, discuss|agree, consent|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They argue about politics every day. = 他们每天都争论政治。
He argues that the earth is flat. = 他认为地球是平的。

一般过去时:
We argued for hours about the best way to proceed. = 我们就最佳行动方案争论了数小时。
She argued with her brother about borrowing his car. = 她和她哥哥因为借车的事争吵了。

现在完成时:
They have argued about this issue many times. = 他们已经多次就这个问题争论过。
He has argued convincingly for stricter gun control. = 他已令人信服地论证了更严格的枪支管制。

过去完成时:
They had argued before, so the meeting was tense. = 他们以前就吵过架，所以会议气氛很紧张。
She had argued with her boss about her salary increase. = 她曾和老板就加薪问题发生过争执。

将来时:
I will argue my case before the committee. = 我将在委员会面前陈述我的理由。
They will argue the merits of both sides. = 他们将辩论双方的优点。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't argue with him. = 如果我是你，我就不会和他争吵。
I wish I hadn't argued with my friend yesterday. = 我真希望昨天没有和我的朋友吵架。

被动语态:
The point was argued extensively in the meeting. = 会议上对这一点进行了广泛的讨论。
The case is being argued in the Supreme Court. = 此案正在最高法院审理。

疑问句:
Are they arguing again? = 他们又在争吵了吗？
What are you arguing about? = 你在争论什么？

否定句:
I didn't argue with him; I simply explained my point of view. = 我没有和他争论，我只是简单地解释了我的观点。
They didn't argue about the decision. = 他们没有就这个决定争论。


PHRASE->
argue with someone = 与某人争论
argue a point = 论证一个观点
argue against something = 反驳某事
argue for something = 为某事辩护
argue the toss = 争论不休
