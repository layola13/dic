devisee:/ˌdɛvɪˈziː/| n. | 受遗赠人 | A person who receives real or personal property under a will.  | Example:  The devisee inherited a large estate from her grandmother.  受遗赠人从她祖母那里继承了一大笔遗产。|近义词：legatee, beneficiary |反义词：testator, devisor


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense and other verb conjugations are not applicable.  It's usage focuses on its role in a sentence.

Example Sentence 1:
The devisee was surprised by the size of the inheritance. 受遗赠人对继承的规模感到惊讶。

Example Sentence 2:
The will clearly names the devisee and the specific property bequeathed.  遗嘱清楚地指明了受遗赠人和所遗赠的具体财产。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly using "devisee."  The word is used in legal contexts.  Phrases involving wills and inheritance might include:

named devisee: 指定的受遗赠人
sole devisee: 唯一受遗赠人
devisee under the will:  遗嘱中的受遗赠人
remainder devisee:  剩余受遗赠人 (receiving property after a prior beneficiary)
residuary devisee:  残余受遗赠人 (receiving remaining assets after specific bequests)
