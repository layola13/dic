foreword:/ˈfɔːrwɜːrd/|/ˈfɔːrˌwɜːrd/| n. |前言；序言 |A foreword is an introductory statement at the beginning of a book, usually written by someone other than the author.  It often provides context, background information, or a personal perspective on the work. |Example: The book began with a foreword by the author's mentor. 这本书以作者导师所写的序言开头。


The foreword to this edition offers insights into the author's life.  这版书的前言提供了对作者生平的见解。|近义词：introduction, preface, prologue |反义词：afterword, epilogue


词源:
The word "foreword" comes from the Old English "for" meaning "before" and "word,"  literally meaning "before word".  It reflects its position at the beginning of a book.
