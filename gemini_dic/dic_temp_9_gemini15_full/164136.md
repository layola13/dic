predisposal: /ˌpriːdɪsˈpoʊzəl/ | n. | 倾向；素质；预先处置 | A predisposition is an inclination or tendency towards something, often a negative trait or condition.  It suggests an increased likelihood or susceptibility, often due to genetics or prior experiences. | 例句：He has a predisposition to heart disease due to family history. = 他由于家族病史而有患心脏病的倾向。  Her childhood trauma gave her a predisposition to anxiety. = 她童年的创伤使她容易焦虑。 | 近义词：tendency, inclination, susceptibility, proneness | 反义词：resistance, immunity

一般过去时:
