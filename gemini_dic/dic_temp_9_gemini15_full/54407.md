disincentive's: /ˌdɪsɪnˈsɛntɪvz/ | n. | 阻碍因素；制约；反激励 | Something that discourages someone from doing something.  | The high taxes acted as a disincentive to investment. 高额税收对投资起到了阻碍作用。 | deterrent, obstacle, handicap | incentive, encouragement | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The high price acts as a disincentive to many buyers. = 高价对许多买家起到了阻碍作用。
Lack of career progression serves as a disincentive for many employees. = 缺乏职业发展机会对许多员工来说是一种阻碍。


一般过去时:
The threat of punishment served as a disincentive. = 惩罚的威胁起到了威慑作用。
The previous experience acted as a strong disincentive. = 之前的经历起到了强烈的阻碍作用。


现在完成时:
The new regulations have created a disincentive to innovation. = 新规章制度对创新造成了阻碍。
Several factors have combined to create a disincentive to invest. = 几个因素共同造成了投资的阻碍。


过去完成时:
The poor results had already created a disincentive for further research. = 不好的结果已经对进一步的研究造成了阻碍。
His past failures had acted as a disincentive to trying again. = 他过去的失败已经对他再次尝试起到了阻碍作用。


将来时:
The proposed changes will likely create a disincentive for small businesses. = 提出的变化可能会对小型企业造成阻碍。
Increased regulation will be a disincentive to economic growth. = 加强监管将阻碍经济增长。


虚拟语气:
If there were fewer disincentives, more people would invest. = 如果阻碍因素较少，更多的人会投资。
I wish there weren't so many disincentives to starting a business. = 我希望创业的阻碍因素少一些。


被动语态:

The project was delayed by disincentives that were not properly addressed. = 该项目因未妥善解决的阻碍因素而被推迟。


疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "disincentive" is formed from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") and the noun "incentive" (something that motivates or encourages).  "Incentive" ultimately derives from Latin *incentare "to spur on, incite," from *incendere "to set on fire".
