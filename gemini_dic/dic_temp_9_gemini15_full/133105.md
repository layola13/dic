nonerratics: /ˌnɒnɪˈrætiks/ | n. |非流浪的，非漂砾的 |  Nonerratics refers to geological materials, specifically rocks and boulders, that have not been transported by glacial action.  They are found in situ, meaning they have remained in the place where they were originally formed.  This is in contrast to erratics, which are rocks transported and deposited by glaciers. |  The study revealed a high concentration of nonerratics in the bedrock, indicating a lack of significant glacial activity in the area.  |近义词: in-situ materials, indigenous rocks | 反义词: erratics
