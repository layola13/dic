regild:/riːˈɡɪld/| vt.|镀金，重新镀金|To cover (something, especially metal) with a layer of gold again.|The artist decided to regild the frame of the antique mirror.|近义词：replate, gild anew|反义词：de-gild


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He regilds old picture frames. = 他重新镀旧画框的金色。
They regild the statue every ten years. = 他们每十年重新给雕像镀金。


一般过去时:
She regilded the locket after it was scratched. = 她的锁匙被刮花了之后，她重新镀了金。
The craftsman regilded the altar for the church's anniversary. = 工匠为教堂的周年纪念日重新镀了祭坛的金色。


现在完成时:
He has regilded all the furniture. = 他已经重新给所有家具镀金了。
They have regilded the dome of the building. = 他们已经重新为建筑物的圆顶镀金了。


过去完成时:
The artist had regilded the frame before the exhibition. = 艺术家在展览前已经重新给画框镀金了。
Before the renovation, they had regilded the crown. = 在翻新之前，他们已经重新为皇冠镀金了。


将来时:
They will regild the gates next month. = 他们下个月将重新为大门镀金。
She will regild the religious icon for the festival. = 她将在节日为宗教圣像重新镀金。


虚拟语气:
If I had more money, I would regild the entire staircase. = 如果我有更多钱，我会重新给整个楼梯镀金。
I wish I could regild the antique clock, but I lack the skills. = 我真希望我能重新为古董钟镀金，但我缺乏这方面的技能。


被动语态:
The frame was regilded by a professional. = 画框是由专业人士重新镀金的。

疑问句:
Will they regild the dome? = 他们会重新为圆顶镀金吗？

否定句:

PHRASE->
regild the lily: 画蛇添足 (to improve something that is already perfect or good enough)
regild a thing: 重新镀金某物
regild the gingerbread:  (idiomatic, humorous) to make something already attractive even more attractive
regild the past: 试图美化过去
regilding the age:  (figurative) restoring something to its former glory
