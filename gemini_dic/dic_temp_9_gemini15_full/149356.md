pachyglossal: /ˌpækiˈɡlɒsəl/ | adj. | 厚舌的；舌头厚的 |  Relating to or characterized by a thick tongue.  The term is primarily used in zoology and comparative anatomy to describe the condition of having a thick, fleshy tongue. | Example:  The pachyglossate frog species are known for their ability to capture prey with their powerful tongues. 厚舌蛙类以其强大的舌头捕食猎物而闻名。 |近义词: thick-tongued (less formal)| 反义词:  thin-tongued  |


词源:

* **pachy-**:  From Greek *παχύς* (pakhús), meaning "thick," "dense," or "strong."  This prefix is found in many scientific terms related to thickness or density.

* **glosso-**: From Greek *γλῶσσα* (glôssa), meaning "tongue." This root appears in numerous words related to the tongue and language.

* **-al**:  A suffix forming adjectives, meaning "relating to" or "characterized by."


用法说明:

由于该词主要用于专业领域，所以没有常见的语法变形例句。  以下是一些例句展示其在不同语境中的使用：


例句：

* The researchers observed several pachyglossal characteristics in the newly discovered amphibian species. 研究人员在新发现的两栖动物物种中观察到几种厚舌特征。


*  Unlike many other lizard species, this one possesses a remarkably pachyglossal tongue. 与许多其他蜥蜴物种不同，这种蜥蜴拥有非常厚的舌头。


* The pachyglossal morphology of the species allows for efficient prey capture. 该物种的厚舌形态允许有效捕获猎物。


PHRASE->

由于pachyglossal本身就是一个形容词，它不构成词组。  它通常与名词搭配使用，例如：pachyglossal frog (厚舌蛙), pachyglossal tongue (厚舌), pachyglossal morphology (厚舌形态).
