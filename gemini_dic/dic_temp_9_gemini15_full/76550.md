galled:/ɡɔːld/| adj.|使人恼怒的；擦伤的；疼痛的|irritated; chafed; sore|The constant criticism galled him.  持续的批评使他恼火。|His neck was galled by the rough collar. 粗糙的衣领擦伤了他的脖子。|annoyed, irritated, chafed|pleased, soothed, calmed|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant noise galls me. = 持续的噪音让我恼火。
This rough fabric galls my skin. = 这粗糙的布料磨损我的皮肤。


一般过去时:
The unfair treatment galled her deeply. = 不公平的待遇深深地激怒了她。
The saddle galled the horse's back. = 马鞍擦伤了马背。


现在完成时:
His arrogance has galled me for years. = 他的傲慢多年来一直让我恼火。
The tight shoes have galled my heels. = 紧的鞋子磨破了我的脚后跟。


过去完成时:
She had been galled by his lies for months. = 她几个月来一直被他谎言激怒。
The long journey had galled his feet. = 漫长的旅程磨破了他的脚。


将来时:
The new regulations will gall many businesses. = 新的规章制度将激怒许多企业。
I'm sure that new harness will gall the horse. = 我确信新的马具会擦伤马匹。


虚拟语气:
If he knew how his words galled her, he might be more careful. = 如果他知道他的话让她多么恼火，他可能会更小心。
I wish that tight collar didn't gall my neck so much. = 我希望那件紧领子不会这么磨我的脖子。


被动语态:
Many people were galled by the politician's remarks. = 许多人对这位政治家的言论感到恼火。
His skin was galled by the rough rope. = 他的皮肤被粗糙的绳子擦伤了。


疑问句:
Does the new policy gall you? = 新政策让你恼火吗？
Is your skin galled by that jewelry? = 你的皮肤被那珠宝磨伤了吗？


否定句:
The criticism didn't gall her; she was used to it. = 批评并没有让她恼火；她已经习惯了。
The soft leather didn't gall his skin. = 柔软的皮革没有擦伤他的皮肤。
