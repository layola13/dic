flare-up:/ˈfleər ʌp/| n./v.|突然发作；加剧；闪耀；突然爆发 |A sudden worsening of a problem or disease; a sudden brief burst of something.|例句：The old wound flared up again.  旧伤又复发了。|例句：There was a sudden flare-up of fighting. 突然爆发了战斗。|近义词：eruption, outburst, intensification|反义词：subside, abate, calm down|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The problem flares up periodically. = 这个问题定期加剧。
His anger flares up easily. = 他很容易发火。

一般过去时:
The fire flared up briefly. = 火短暂地燃烧起来。
Her rash flared up after she ate shellfish. = 她吃了海鲜后皮疹加重了。

现在完成时:
The conflict has flared up several times this year. = 今年冲突已经爆发了几次。
My allergies have flared up due to the pollen. = 我的过敏症因为花粉而加重了。

过去完成时:
The symptoms had flared up before he got to the hospital. = 他到医院之前症状已经加重了。
The argument had flared up before I even arrived. = 我还没到的时候争吵就已经爆发了。

将来时:
The volcano might flare up again soon. = 火山可能很快会再次爆发。
I think his anger will flare up if you mention it. = 我认为如果你提起这件事，他会发火的。


虚拟语气:
If the pain flared up again, I'd go to the doctor. = 如果疼痛再次加重，我会去看医生。
If the disease flared up, he would need immediate treatment. = 如果病情加重，他需要立即治疗。


被动语态:
The situation was flared up by the media. = 局势被媒体激化了。
The conflict was flared up by the political leaders. = 冲突被政治领导人激化了。


疑问句:
Did the argument flare up again? = 争论再次爆发了吗？
Will his condition flare up again? = 他的病情还会再次加重吗？


否定句:
The fire didn't flare up again. = 火没有再次燃烧起来。
His knee didn't flare up during the game. = 他在比赛中膝盖没有加重。


PHRASE->
flare-up of violence = 暴力事件的突然爆发
flare-up of asthma = 哮喘的突然发作
flare-up of eczema = 湿疹的突然加重
flare-up of anger = 怒火的突然爆发
flare-up of the old conflict = 旧冲突的再次爆发
