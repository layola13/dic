outgain:/ˌaʊtˈɡeɪn/| vt. |超过，胜过|To surpass or exceed in gain or profit; to achieve more success or progress than.|The company outgained its competitors in the market.|近义词：surpass, exceed, outstrip, outperform |反义词：underperform, lag behind


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Our team outgains the opponent in every game. = 我们队在每一场比赛中都胜过对手。
The new marketing strategy outgains the old one significantly. = 新的营销策略明显优于旧策略。

一般过去时:
Last year, our sales outgained those of our rivals. = 去年，我们的销售额超过了竞争对手。
The athlete outgained his opponent in the final sprint. = 运动员在最后的冲刺中超过了他的对手。

现在完成时:
The company has outgained its competitors in market share this year. = 今年，该公司在市场份额上已超过了竞争对手。
She has outgained her expectations in her career. = 她在事业上已经超过了自己的预期。

过去完成时:
By the end of the project, our team had outgained the initial target by 15%. = 在项目结束时，我们的团队已超过了最初的目标 15%。
They had outgained all their previous records before the final race. = 在最后的比赛之前，他们已经超过了他们之前所有的记录。

将来时:
We expect to outgain our competitors in the next quarter. = 我们预计在下个季度将超过我们的竞争对手。
With this new technology, we will outgain the market leader in performance. = 凭借这项新技术，我们在性能方面将超过市场领导者。

虚拟语气:
If we worked harder, we might outgain our rivals. = 如果我们更努力工作，我们可能会超过竞争对手。
I wish I had outgained my fears and taken the risk. = 我希望我克服了恐惧并冒险一试。

被动语态:
Our team was outgained by the opposing team in the final match. = 我们队在决赛中被对方队击败。
The old strategy was outgained by the new one in terms of efficiency. = 就效率而言，旧策略被新策略超越了。

疑问句:
Did our team outgain the opponent? = 我们队战胜了对手吗？
Will the new product outgain the existing ones in sales? = 新产品在销量上会超过现有产品吗？

否定句:
Our efforts did not outgain the challenges we faced. = 我们的努力未能克服我们面临的挑战。
They failed to outgain their competitors in the long run. = 从长远来看，他们未能超越竞争对手。
