cognizor:/ˈkɒɡnɪzɔːr/| n. |知情者，知悉者，认可者 |A cognizor is a person who has knowledge or awareness of something.  They are aware of a fact, situation, or event.  The term often appears in legal contexts, referring to someone who is aware of a transaction or agreement. |例句：The bank manager, as a cognizor of the fraudulent transaction, was obligated to report it to the authorities. 银行经理作为这笔欺诈交易的知情者，有义务向当局报告。|近义词：knower, aware person, participant |反义词：ignoramus, unaware person
