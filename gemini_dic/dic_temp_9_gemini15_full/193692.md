self-complacence:/ˌsɛlfkəmˈpleɪsns/| n. |自满；自鸣得意 |A feeling of smug or uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements.  It suggests an excessive pride in one's own abilities or accomplishments, often to the point of ignoring flaws or shortcomings. | Example:  His self-complacence prevented him from seeing the flaws in his work.  他自满的情绪让他看不到作品中的缺陷。|近义词：self-satisfaction, smugness, complacency, conceit |反义词：humility, self-criticism, modesty
