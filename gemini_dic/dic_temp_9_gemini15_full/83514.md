had:/hæd/ | v. | 有；曾经；过去常常 |  The past tense of "have". It indicates possession, experience, or a completed action in the past.  It is also used to form the past perfect tense. |

例句：
I had a great time at the party. = 我在派对上玩得很开心。

He had already left when I arrived. = 我到的时候他已经离开了。


近义词：possessed, owned (for possession); experienced (for experience)

反义词：lacked, missed (for possession);  


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I have a car. = 我有一辆车。
They have three children. = 他们有三个孩子。


一般过去时:
I had a car. = 我曾经有一辆车。(现在可能没有了)
They had three children. = 他们曾经有三个孩子。(现在可能数量不同了)


现在完成时:
I have had a car for five years. = 我已经有五年车了。
They have had three children. = 他们已经有三个孩子了。


过去完成时:
I had had a car before I moved to the city. = 我搬到城里之前就有一辆车了。
They had had three children before their divorce. = 他们离婚前已经有三个孩子了。


将来时:
(Had is not typically used in future tense constructions)


虚拟语气:
If I had known, I would have told you. = 如果我知道的话，我就告诉你了。(与过去事实相反)
I wish I had gone to the concert. = 我真希望我去了音乐会。(对过去的遗憾)


被动语态:
The work had been completed before the deadline. = 工作在截止日期前就完成了。
The problem had been discussed many times. = 这个问题已经讨论过很多次了。


疑问句:
Had you seen the movie before? = 你以前看过这部电影吗？
Had they finished their work? = 他们完成工作了吗？


否定句:
I did not have enough money. = 我没有足够的钱。
They had not seen each other for years. = 他们多年没见过面了。


PHRASE->
had better: 最好
had to: 不得不
would have: 本来会
could have: 本来可以
should have: 本来应该


词源:
The word "had" comes from the Old English verb "hæbbe," the past tense of "habban," meaning "to have."  This ultimately traces back to Proto-Germanic and further to Proto-Indo-European roots related to possession and holding.
