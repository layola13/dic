postphlebitic: /ˌpoʊstˈflɛbɪtɪk/ | adj. | 术后静脉炎的；静脉炎后的 |  Relating to or denoting the condition of a limb after phlebitis (inflammation of a vein).  It often involves chronic venous insufficiency, leading to swelling, pain, and skin changes. | Example: The patient presented with postphlebitic syndrome, characterized by edema and discoloration of the leg.  患者出现术后静脉炎综合征，其特征是腿部水肿和变色。 | 近义词: postthrombotic | 反义词:  N/A (Describes a condition, not something with a direct opposite) | 词性: adjective

词源:
The word "postphlebitic" is a combination of "post-" (meaning after) and "phlebitic" (relating to phlebitis).  Phlebitis itself comes from the Greek words "phleps" (vein) and "-itis" (inflammation).  Therefore, postphlebitic literally means "after vein inflammation".
