nonredeemable:/ˌnɒnrɪˈdiːməbl/| adj. |不可赎回的，不能兑换的| incapable of being redeemed or exchanged for something else, typically cash.  This usually refers to securities, bonds, or similar financial instruments. |Example:  The nonredeemable bonds offered a higher interest rate.  不可赎回的债券提供了更高的利率。
The company issued nonredeemable preferred stock. 公司发行了不可赎回的优先股。|近义词：irredeemable, unredeemable|反义词：redeemable


USAGE->
(由于nonredeemable是形容词，其用法随其修饰的名词而变化，以下例句展示不同句型)

陈述句:
The certificate is nonredeemable.  这张证书是不可赎回的。
These bonds are nonredeemable after five years. 这些债券五年后将不可赎回。


疑问句:
Are these shares nonredeemable? 这些股票是不可赎回的吗？
Is the gift card nonredeemable? 这张礼品卡是不可兑换的吗？


否定句:
The shares are not nonredeemable; they can be redeemed before the maturity date. 这些股份并非不可赎回；它们可以在到期日前赎回。
That's not a nonredeemable coupon; you can use it for a discount. 那不是不可兑换的优惠券；你可以用它来打折。

词源:
The word "nonredeemable" is formed by combining the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") with the adjective "redeemable".  "Redeemable" itself derives from the verb "redeem," which comes from the Old English "rēman," meaning "to buy back, ransom," ultimately from Proto-Germanic "*rauzōnan" with similar meaning.  The "re-" prefix indicates a back-action or reversal, emphasizing the act of buying back or reclaiming something.
