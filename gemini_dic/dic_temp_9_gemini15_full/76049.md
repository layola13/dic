furloughs:/ˈfɜːrloʊz/|/ˈfɜːrloʊz/| n. |休假；暂时解雇 |A furlough is a period of time when someone is temporarily allowed to leave their job, especially in the military, or a period when an employee is temporarily laid off.  |The company announced temporary furloughs for its employees due to the economic downturn.  公司宣布由于经济低迷，员工将暂时休假。|layoff, leave of absence |reinstatement, employment |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company often grants furloughs during slow seasons. = 公司在淡季经常会批准休假。
He takes furloughs to visit his family. = 他休假去看望家人。


一般过去时:
The soldiers were granted furloughs to see their families. = 士兵们获准休假去看望家人。
She took a furlough last month. = 她上个月休假了。


现在完成时:
Many employees have experienced furloughs due to the pandemic. = 许多员工由于疫情经历了休假。
The government has announced new furlough schemes. = 政府宣布了新的休假计划。


过去完成时:

将来时:

She will take a furlough next month. = 她下个月将休假。
