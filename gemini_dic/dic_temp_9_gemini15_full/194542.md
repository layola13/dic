self-shelter:/ˈsɛlf ʃɛltər/| n.| 自建避难所；自行寻求庇护 | A self-shelter is a temporary structure or location used for personal protection during an emergency, such as a natural disaster or attack, that is created or found by the individual seeking shelter.  It contrasts with a pre-designated public shelter. | 例句：They built a self-shelter in their basement to protect themselves from tornadoes. 他们在地下室建造了一个避难所来保护自己免受龙卷风的袭击。

They sought self-shelter under a sturdy bridge during the bombing raid.  他们在空袭期间在结实的桥下寻求庇护。| 近义词： improvised shelter, makeshift shelter, temporary refuge | 反义词：public shelter, designated safe zone
