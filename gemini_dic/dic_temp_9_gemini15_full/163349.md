preconfusion: /ˌpriːkənˈfjuːʒən/ | n. | 事先的困惑；预先的混乱 |  A state of confusion or uncertainty that exists before something happens. It refers to the anxiety or bewilderment felt *before* an event, decision, or encounter, rather than the confusion resulting from it.  | 例句：The preconfusion about the exam made him unable to focus on his studies.  考试前的焦虑让他无法集中精力学习。


The preconfusion surrounding the merger negotiations caused delays. 合并谈判之前的混乱导致了延误。

近义词：anticipation (with negative connotations), apprehension, anxiety, uncertainty | 反义词：clarity, certainty, confidence | 词性: 名词


USAGE->
This word is a relatively new coinage and doesn't have established usage in various grammatical tenses as common words do.  Its usage is primarily in the present tense, and less often in past tense to describe a past state of pre-event confusion.  Examples using different tenses are artificial unless within a specific narrative context.
