irreconcilabilities: /ˌɪrɛkənˈsaɪləbɪlɪtiz/ | n. | 不可调和的分歧；无法弥合的矛盾 |  Things that are impossible to reconcile or bring into agreement.  The fundamental irreconcilabilities between their views made compromise impossible. | 近义词：inconsistencies, incompatibilities, differences, conflicts | 反义词：reconciliations, agreements, harmonies


例句：

The deep irreconcilabilities between the two factions prevented any hope of a peaceful settlement.
两个派系之间深刻的不可调和的分歧，使得和平解决的希望破灭。


The court found insurmountable irreconcilabilities in the evidence presented by both sides.
法院发现双方提出的证据存在无法克服的矛盾。


词源：

* **ir-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "without"
* **reconcile**: verb meaning "to restore friendly relations between," "to make compatible"  (from Latin *reconciliare*, from *re-* "again" + *conciliare* "to bring together")
* **-able**: suffix meaning "capable of"
* **-ilities**: suffix forming plural nouns


用法说明：

irreconcilabilities 通常用复数形式，表示多种无法调和的矛盾或分歧。 它强调的是这些分歧的根本性和无法克服性，暗示着无法达成一致或妥协。


由于irreconcilabilities本身就是一个名词，所以它没有动词、形容词等其他词性变化。  它主要用于陈述句中，描述客观存在的不和谐状态。
