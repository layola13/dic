phylactolaematous:/ˌfaɪlækˈtɒləmətəs/| adj.|具育珠体的；有保护膜的|Having or relating to a phylum (Phylactolaemata) of freshwater bryozoans characterized by a protective gelatinous covering or statoblast.  The term describes the characteristic feature of these bryozoans which involves a protective covering for their statoblasts (resting buds).|The phylactolaematous bryozoans are found in freshwater environments.|近义词：protected, encased|反义词：unprotected, exposed
