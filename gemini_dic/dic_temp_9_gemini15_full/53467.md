disarmed: /dɪsˈɑːrmd/ | v. |解除武装；使…无力；消除…的武装 | to deprive (someone or something) of weapons; to make (someone or something) harmless or ineffective | 近义词：demilitarized, neutralized, pacified | 反义词：armed, empowered


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The police disarm the suspect. = 警察解除嫌疑人的武装。
He disarms his critics with humor. = 他用幽默化解了批评者的批评。


一般过去时:
The soldiers were disarmed after the battle. = 战后士兵们被缴械了。
She disarmed the bomb before it exploded. = 她拆除了炸弹，阻止了爆炸。


现在完成时:
The army has disarmed the rebels. = 军队已经缴械了叛军。
The new policy has disarmed the opposition. = 新政策已经瓦解了反对派的力量。


过去完成时:
They had disarmed the enemy before the truce. = 他们休战前已经缴械了敌人。
The negotiators had disarmed the tensions before the summit. = 谈判代表在峰会前已经缓和了紧张局势。


将来时:
The police will disarm the criminals. = 警察将会解除罪犯的武装。
The treaty will disarm the nation's nuclear arsenal. = 条约将使该国的核武库失去效力。


虚拟语气:
If the police had disarmed him earlier, the tragedy could have been avoided. = 如果警察早点解除他的武装，悲剧本来是可以避免的。
I wish I had disarmed my fears before the presentation. = 我希望在演示之前我已经克服了我的恐惧。


被动语态:
The prisoners were disarmed by the guards. = 囚犯们被警卫缴了械。
His arguments were disarmed by the facts. = 他的论点被事实驳倒了。


疑问句:
Were the soldiers disarmed? = 士兵们被缴械了吗？
Has the threat been disarmed? = 威胁已经被解除了吗？


否定句:
The rebels were not disarmed. = 叛军没有被缴械。
The government did not disarm the opposition. = 政府没有解除反对派的武装。


PHRASE->
disarm a bomb = 拆除炸弹
disarm an opponent = 使对手无力
disarm suspicion = 消除怀疑
disarm criticism = 化解批评
disarm a threat = 消除威胁

词源:
The word "disarmed" comes from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "without" or "opposite of") and the verb "arm" (meaning to equip with weapons).  Therefore, "disarmed" literally means "without arms" or "deprived of weapons."
