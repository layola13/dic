deposit:/dɪˈpɒzɪt/ /dəˈpɑːzɪt/ | n. vt. |存款；押金；沉积物；沉淀物 |

英文解析:
Noun:  A sum of money placed or kept in a bank account, usually to gain interest.  Also, a sum of money given as a guarantee or part payment. As a geological term, it refers to matter that has been deposited by a natural process.

Verb: To put or place (money) in a bank account. To put or lay down (something) in a particular place.


例句：
Noun:
The bank requires a 10% deposit on all new accounts. = 银行要求所有新账户存入10%的押金。

She made a deposit of $1000 into her savings account. = 她向储蓄账户存入了1000美元。


Verb:
I deposited the check into my account this morning. = 我今天早上把支票存入了我的账户。

The river deposited a layer of silt on the floodplain. = 河流在洪泛区沉积了一层淤泥。


近义词:
Noun:  payment, pledge, contribution, sedimentation, sediment

Verb:  place, put, lodge, settle


反义词:
Noun: withdrawal (opposite of deposit as a financial transaction), erosion (opposite of deposit as a geological process)

Verb: withdraw (opposite of deposit as a financial transaction)


词性:
Noun, Verb
