grandstanding:/ˈɡrændˌstændɪŋ/| n. v.|哗众取宠；卖弄；炫耀；故作姿态|The act of behaving in a way that is intended to attract attention and admiration, often in a showy or exaggerated way.  It often implies a lack of genuine substance or sincerity.|例句：He was accused of grandstanding during the debate, more interested in impressing the audience than addressing the issue. 他被指责在辩论中哗众取宠，更关心的是给观众留下印象而不是解决问题。

例句：The politician's grandstanding speeches were criticized as empty rhetoric.  这位政治家的夸夸其谈的演讲被批评为空洞的辞令。


近义词：showboating, posturing, showing off, playing to the gallery|反义词：humility, modesty, sincerity
