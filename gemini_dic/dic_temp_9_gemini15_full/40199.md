contest:/ˈkɒntest/ /ˈkɑːntɛst/| n. vt.|比赛；竞争；争夺；质疑|A contest is a competition in which people try to win a prize.  It can also refer to a struggle or dispute.|例句：The singing contest was fierce. 歌唱比赛竞争激烈。|例句：He contested the election results. 他对选举结果提出质疑。|近义词：competition, rivalry, challenge, dispute|反义词：agreement, cooperation, accord|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They contest the election results every year. = 他们每年都对选举结果提出质疑。
Many athletes contest in the Olympic Games. = 许多运动员参加奥运会比赛。

一般过去时:
She contested the will. = 她对遗嘱提出质疑。
He contested the championship last year. = 他去年参加了锦标赛。

现在完成时:
They have contested the decision. = 他们已经对这个决定提出质疑。
She has contested many beauty pageants. = 她参加过许多选美比赛。

过去完成时:
He had contested the point before, but no one listened. = 他之前就对这一点提出过异议，但没有人听。
The team had contested many matches before winning the championship. = 这支球队在赢得冠军之前已经参加过许多场比赛。

将来时:
They will contest the upcoming election. = 他们将参加即将到来的选举。
She will contest the decision in court. = 她将在法庭上对该决定提出质疑。

虚拟语气:
If I were stronger, I would contest the championship. = 如果我更强壮，我会参加锦标赛。
If he had more evidence, he would contest the verdict. = 如果他有更多证据，他会对判决提出质疑。

被动语态:
The election results were contested. = 选举结果受到了质疑。
The championship was contested fiercely. = 锦标赛竞争激烈。

疑问句:
Will you contest the decision? = 你会对这个决定提出质疑吗？
Who will contest the election? = 谁将参加这次选举？

否定句:
He did not contest the findings of the report. = 他没有对报告的调查结果提出异议。
She didn't contest the will. = 她没有对遗嘱提出异议。
