reprimand:/ˈrɛprɪmænd/| vt. n.|训斥；谴责；责备|To reprimand someone means to express strong disapproval of their actions or behavior, often formally or officially.  It implies a more serious rebuke than a simple scolding.|例句：The teacher reprimanded the student for talking in class. 老师因学生在课堂上讲话而训斥了他。


例句：The manager reprimanded the employee for their repeated lateness. 经理因员工反复迟到而责备了他。


近义词：rebuke, scold, reproach, admonish, chastise|反义词：praise, commend, applaud|动词
