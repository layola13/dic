eardrums:/ˈɪərdrʌmz/| n. |耳膜|The eardrums are thin membranes that vibrate when sound waves hit them, transmitting the vibrations to the inner ear, allowing us to hear.  Eardrums can be damaged by loud noises or infections.|The loud music damaged his eardrums.|tympanic membranes|  (无明确反义词，可考虑damaged eardrums)|名词
