plunking:/ˈplʌŋkɪŋ/| v.|拨弄；砰地放下；笨拙地演奏|The act of playing a stringed instrument in a clumsy or unskilled manner, or of dropping something with a dull thud.  It can also describe the sound produced.|例句：He was plunking away at the piano, making a terrible racket. 他笨拙地弹着钢琴，发出可怕的噪音。


例句：She plunked the heavy bag down on the floor. 她砰地把沉重的包扔在地上。


近义词：thumping, dropping, strumming (in the musical sense)


反义词：playing skillfully (in the musical sense), placing gently


词性:动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He plunks the guitar strings. = 他拨弄吉他弦。
She plunks the coins into the jar. = 她把硬币砰地扔进罐子里。

一般过去时:
He plunked the guitar strings last night. = 他昨晚拨弄吉他弦。
She plunked the heavy box on the table. = 她把沉重的箱子砰地放在桌子上。

现在完成时:
He has plunked out a few tunes on the piano. = 他在钢琴上笨拙地弹奏了几支曲子。
She has plunked all her savings into the bank. = 她把所有的积蓄都存进了银行。

过去完成时:
He had plunked the money down before I could stop him. = 我还没来得及阻止他，他就已经把钱付了。
She had plunked the keys onto the counter, and then left. = 她把钥匙砰地扔在柜台上，然后离开了。

将来时:
He will plunk the notes on the keyboard. = 他将拨弄键盘上的琴键。

虚拟语气:

I wish she hadn’t plunked the vase on the floor. = 我真希望她没有把花瓶扔在地上。
