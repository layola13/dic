heidegger:/ˈhaɪdɛɡər/| n. |海德格尔|Martin Heidegger (1889-1976), a highly influential German philosopher known for his work on existentialism, phenomenology, and hermeneutics.  He is particularly famous for his concepts of Being, Dasein, and "thrownness." His works are often challenging and require careful study.  |Example: Heidegger's concept of Dasein profoundly impacted 20th-century philosophy.  海德格尔的“存在”概念深刻地影响了20世纪的哲学。|近义词:None (as a proper noun)|反义词:None (as a proper noun)|名词
