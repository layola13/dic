predicates:/ˈprɛdɪkətts/| n.|谓语，述语|A predicate is the part of a sentence or clause that contains the verb and states something about the subject.  It typically includes the verb and any objects, complements, or adverbials that modify the verb.|例句：In the sentence "The cat sat on the mat," "sat on the mat" is the predicate.|近义词：verb phrase, clause|反义词：subject


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dog barks loudly. = 狗大声吠叫。
She sings beautifully. = 她唱得很好听。

一般过去时:
He played the piano yesterday. = 他昨天弹钢琴。
They went to the park. = 他们去了公园。

现在完成时:
I have finished my work. = 我已经完成了我的工作。
She has lived here for ten years. = 她在这里住了十年了。

过去完成时:
He had eaten before I arrived. = 我到达之前他已经吃过了。
They had finished the project before the deadline. = 他们在大限之前完成了项目。

将来时:
We will go to the beach tomorrow. = 我们明天要去海滩。
She will call you later. = 她稍后会给你打电话。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would study harder. = 如果我是你，我会更努力学习。
I wish I had more time. = 我希望我有更多的时间。

被动语态:
The cake was baked by my mother. = 蛋糕是我妈妈烤的。
The problem is being discussed now. = 现在正在讨论这个问题。

疑问句:
Does he like coffee? = 他喜欢咖啡吗？
Did she go to the party? = 她去参加聚会了吗？

否定句:
He does not like spicy food. = 他不喜欢辛辣的食物。
She did not go to school yesterday. = 她昨天没去上学。
