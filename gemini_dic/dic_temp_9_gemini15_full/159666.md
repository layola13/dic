polyacid: /ˌpɒliˈæsɪd/| /ˌpɑːliˈæsɪd/ | n. |多元酸；多羧酸 | A polyacid is a type of acid that contains more than one carboxyl group (-COOH) per molecule.  These multiple carboxyl groups contribute to the polyacid's acidic properties and its ability to form multiple bonds with other molecules.  Polyacids are found in various natural and synthetic compounds and play important roles in various chemical and biological processes. | Example: Citric acid, a common polyacid found in citrus fruits, has three carboxyl groups.  柠檬酸，一种常见的多元酸，存在于柑橘类水果中，具有三个羧基。 |近义词: polycarboxylic acid | 反义词:monobasic acid
