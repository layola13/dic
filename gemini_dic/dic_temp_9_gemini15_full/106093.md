knife-edges: /ˈnaɪfˌɛdʒɪz/ | n. |刀刃；极度危险的境地；关键点 |  A very critical or precarious situation; a very finely balanced state. The term refers to the extremely sharp edge of a knife, implying a situation where a small shift can have significant consequences.  | Example: The company is on a knife-edge, facing potential bankruptcy if sales don't improve. 公司正处于生死攸关的时刻，如果销售额不提高，就可能面临破产。  The negotiations were on a knife-edge, with both sides refusing to compromise.  谈判正处于非常关键的时刻，双方都拒绝妥协。|近义词：precarious situation, critical juncture, tipping point |反义词：safe situation, stable condition, secure position |
