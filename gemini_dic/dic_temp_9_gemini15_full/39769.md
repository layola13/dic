consolidators:/kənˈsɒlɪdeɪtərz/| n. |巩固者；合并者；整合者| A consolidator is a person or company that combines or merges things, often smaller entities into larger ones, for greater efficiency or effectiveness. This can apply to various fields, such as businesses, transportation, or data.  | Example: The airline industry has seen a rise in consolidators who purchase smaller airlines to expand their market share.  航空业出现了越来越多的并购商，他们收购小型航空公司以扩大市场份额。|近义词：merger, combiner, integrator |反义词：divider, separator, disintegrator
