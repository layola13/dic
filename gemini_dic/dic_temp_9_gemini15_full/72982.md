foolhardiness:/ˈfulˌhɑːrdinɪs/ | n. |鲁莽；蛮勇；轻率| Reckless boldness or daring; a willingness to take unnecessary risks.  It suggests a lack of caution and a disregard for potential consequences.|  His foolhardiness in climbing the treacherous cliff nearly cost him his life.  他的鲁莽行为险些让他在攀登险峻的悬崖时丧命。|近义词：recklessness, rashness, audacity, temerity |反义词：caution, prudence, circumspection

词源:
The word "foolhardiness" is a combination of "fool" (meaning a foolish person) and "hardiness" (meaning boldness or courage).  It evolved from the older word "foolhardy," which meant recklessly bold.  The "-ness" suffix transforms the adjective into a noun.
