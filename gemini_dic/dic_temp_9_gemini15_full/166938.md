prestock:/priːˈstɒk/ | v. | 预先储存；预先备货 | To store or acquire something in advance of need.  | The company prestocks its warehouses with holiday merchandise in the summer. 公司在夏季预先在仓库储存节日商品。 | 近义词：stockpile, hoard, accumulate | 反义词：deplete, exhaust


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They prestock the shelves every morning. = 他们每天早上都预先储备货架上的商品。
The store prestocks seasonal items. = 这家商店预先储备季节性商品。


一般过去时:
We prestocked the pantry before the hurricane. = 我们在飓风来临之前预先储备了食品室。
The farmer prestocked his silo with grain for the winter. = 农民预先在谷仓储存了过冬的粮食。


现在完成时:
They have prestocked enough supplies for the project. = 他们已经为项目预先储备了足够的物资。
The company has prestocked its inventory for the upcoming sale. = 公司已经为即将到来的促销活动预先储备了库存。


过去完成时:
She had prestocked her emergency kit before the earthquake. = 地震发生前，她已经预先储备了她的急救包。
The team had prestocked the equipment before starting the experiment. = 团队在开始实验之前已经预先储备了设备。


将来时:
We will prestock the materials next week. = 我们下周将预先储备材料。
The restaurant will prestock ingredients for the busy weekend. = 餐厅将为繁忙的周末预先储备食材。


虚拟语气:
If they prestocked more, they wouldn't have run out of supplies. = 如果他们预先储备更多，就不会缺货了。
I wish we had prestocked more food for the trip. = 我真希望我们为这次旅行预先储备更多食物。


被动语态:
