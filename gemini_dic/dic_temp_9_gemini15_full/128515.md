newt:/nuːt/| n.|蝾螈|A newt is a small amphibian with smooth skin, typically having four legs and a long tail.  Newts are closely related to salamanders but are generally smaller and have more aquatic habits.|例句：I saw a newt swimming in the pond. 我看到一只蝾螈在池塘里游泳。

例句：The children were fascinated by the brightly colored newts in the aquarium. 孩子们被水族馆里色彩鲜艳的蝾螈迷住了。


近义词：salamander, eft (eft is a terrestrial juvenile newt)
反义词：None.

词源:
The word "newt" comes from Old English "efet,"  which is related to the Old High German "eweza" meaning "lizard".  Over time, the "e" in "efet" changed to "n" and the meaning narrowed to the specific amphibian.
