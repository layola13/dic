latched: /lætʃt/ | v. | 扣住；闩上；锁住 | To fasten or secure (something) with a latch.  |例句：He latched the door behind him. 他在他身后闩上门了。
                                                                                                    She latched onto the opportunity. 她抓住了这个机会。|近义词：fasten, secure, lock |反义词：unlock, unfasten

词源:
The word "latch" ultimately derives from Old English *læccan, meaning "to catch, seize, grip."  This is related to words like "catch" and "clutch." The modern sense of fastening a door evolved over time from the basic meaning of grasping or holding.
