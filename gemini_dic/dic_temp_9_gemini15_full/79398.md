glimmer:/ˈɡlɪmə(r)/| n. vi.|微光，闪烁；闪现；(希望等的)微露|A faint or unsteady light.  A glimmer of hope.|例句：A glimmer of light appeared in the distance. 远处出现了一丝亮光。

例句：There was a glimmer of hope in her eyes.  她的眼里闪现出一丝希望。

近义词：flicker, gleam, shimmer, twinkle|反义词：brightness, blaze


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The stars glimmer in the night sky. = 星星在夜空中闪烁。
A faint light glimmers in the distance. = 远处闪烁着微弱的光芒。

一般过去时:
The candle glimmered weakly before going out. = 蜡烛微弱地闪烁了一下然后熄灭了。
Hope glimmered in her heart as she received the good news. = 收到好消息后，希望在她心中闪现。

现在完成时:
The distant lights have glimmered throughout the night. = 远处的灯光整夜都在闪烁。
A new understanding has glimmered in my mind. = 我心中闪现出一个新的理解。

过去完成时:
The setting sun had glimmered on the horizon before disappearing completely. = 夕阳在完全消失之前，曾在地平线上闪烁。
Before the breakthrough, success had only glimmered as a possibility. = 在取得突破之前，成功只像是一个可能性微弱地闪现过。

将来时:
The fireflies will glimmer in the meadow tonight. = 今晚萤火虫将在草地上闪烁。
I hope a solution will glimmer on the horizon soon. = 我希望很快就能看到解决问题的希望。

虚拟语气:
If the sun glimmered, we might have a chance to see the path. = 如果太阳能发出微光，我们或许有机会看到路。
If only a glimmer of hope glimmered, we wouldn't have given up so easily. = 如果有一丝希望闪现，我们就不会这么轻易放弃。

被动语态:
The distant city was glimmered by the moonlight. = 遥远的城市被月光映照得闪烁不定。(This is a less common usage)
The path was only faintly glimmered in the darkness. = 小路在黑暗中只能隐约看到微弱的光芒。(This is a less common usage)

疑问句:
Does the firefly glimmer? = 萤火虫在闪光吗？
Did the distant lights glimmer last night? = 远处的灯光昨晚有闪烁吗？

否定句:
The stars did not glimmer tonight. = 今晚星星没有闪烁。
No light glimmered in the dark cave. = 黑暗的洞穴里没有一丝光亮。


PHRASE->
a glimmer of hope = 一丝希望
a glimmer of understanding = 一丝理解
a glimmer in the eye = 眼中闪现的光芒
to glimmer faintly = 微弱地闪烁
to glimmer with excitement = 因兴奋而闪闪发光


词源:
From Old English *glimerian, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch glimmen and German glimmen ‘to gleam’.
