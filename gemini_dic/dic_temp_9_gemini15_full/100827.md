introduction:/ˌɪntrəˈdʌkʃən/ | n. | 介绍；引言；入门 | An introduction is a preliminary explanation or description of something. It can be a short speech at the beginning of a meeting, a chapter in a book, or a first encounter with someone or something new.  | Example:  The professor gave a brief introduction to the course before starting the lecture. 教授在开始讲课前对课程做了简短的介绍。

The book begins with a fascinating introduction to the author's life. 这本书以对作者生平的精彩介绍开头。|近义词：preface, prelude, commencement, beginning |反义词：conclusion, ending, termination | 名词

词源:
The word "introduction" comes from the Old French word "introduction," which in turn comes from the Latin word "introductionem," a noun of action from the verb "introducere," meaning "to lead in."  This verb is composed of "in-" (in, into) and "ducere" (to lead).
