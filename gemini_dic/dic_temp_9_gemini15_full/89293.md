homoeomery:/ˌhoʊmiˈɒməri/ | n. |同质异形性，同形异构性 |The theory that all parts of an organism are composed of the same basic substance, differing only in form and arrangement.  This concept, prevalent in ancient Greek philosophy,  suggests that the body is made up of identical basic units that are arranged differently to form diverse structures.  The term is sometimes used in a broader context to describe the similarity of different parts of a system. | Example:  The ancient philosophers' concept of homoeomery attempted to explain the growth and regeneration of organisms.  | 近义词:  similarity, uniformity (in a limited context) | 反义词: heterogeneity, diversity
