schelling:/ˈʃɛlɪŋ/| n.|谢林，（尤指）谢林点|The name Schelling refers primarily to the German philosopher Friedrich Schelling (1775-1854).  In game theory, a Schelling point is a solution that people tend to choose even if they have never explicitly communicated.  |Example: The Schelling point for meeting in New York City is probably Times Square. 谢林点在纽约市的会面地点可能是时代广场。|近义词：focal point, equilibrium point|反义词：无
