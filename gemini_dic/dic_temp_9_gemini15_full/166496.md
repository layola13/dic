presage:/ˈprɛseɪdʒ/| v. n.| 预兆，预示；预感 | To be a sign or warning that something, especially something bad, will happen in the future; a feeling that something is going to happen, especially something bad. |例句：The dark clouds presaged a storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨的来临。
The unsettling silence presaged disaster.  令人不安的寂静预示着灾难。


近义词：foretell, foreshadow, augur, portend, forebode | 反义词：ignore, disregard


词性:动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The economist presages a recession. = 经济学家预言经济衰退。
Dark clouds presage rain. = 乌云预示着下雨。


一般过去时:
Ancient oracles presaged the fall of empires. = 古代的预言预示着帝国的衰落。
The doctor's grim expression presaged bad news. = 医生严峻的表情预示着坏消息。


现在完成时:
Recent events have presaged a change in government policy. = 最近发生的事件预示着政府政策的改变。
The signs have presaged a difficult winter. = 迹象表明将是一个艰难的冬天。


过去完成时:
The ominous silence had presaged the explosion.  不祥的寂静预示着爆炸的发生。
The initial successes had presaged a long and difficult war. = 最初的成功预示着一场漫长而艰难的战争。


将来时:
Experts presage a significant increase in energy prices. = 专家们预言能源价格将大幅上涨。
Meteorologists presage heavy snowfall for the weekend. = 气象学家预言周末将有大量降雪。


虚拟语气:
If the signs presaged disaster, we should have taken precautions. = 如果这些迹象预示着灾难，我们应该采取预防措施。
Had the omens presaged such a catastrophe, we would have acted differently. = 如果预兆预示着这样的灾难，我们会采取不同的行动。


被动语态:
A recession was presaged by many economists. = 许多经济学家都预言了经济衰退。
The storm was presaged by a sudden drop in temperature. = 暴风雨是由气温骤降预示的。


疑问句:
Does this event presage a more serious conflict? = 这件事是否预示着更严重的冲突？
What does the increase in crime presage? = 犯罪率的上升预示着什么？


否定句:
The initial reports did not presage a major crisis. = 初步报告没有预示着重大的危机。
Nothing presaged the sudden outbreak of violence. = 没有什么预示着突然爆发的暴力事件。



PHRASE->
a presage of doom  厄运的预兆
a sure presage of success  成功的可靠预兆
a clear presage of the future  对未来的清晰预兆
an ill presage  不祥的预兆
a false presage  错误的预兆

词源:
源自古法语 *presager， 来自拉丁语 praesagire “to perceive beforehand, to foreknow”，  由 prae- “before” + sagire “to perceive, feel”构成。
