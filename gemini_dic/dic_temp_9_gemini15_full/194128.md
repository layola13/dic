self-induced:/ˌsɛlfɪnˈdʒuːst/| adj.| 自我诱发的，自己造成的 |  Caused by oneself; brought about by one's own actions or behavior.  | 例句：His depression was self-induced.  他的抑郁症是自己造成的。

His self-induced coma lasted for three days. 他自己诱导的昏迷持续了三天。

| 近义词：self-inflicted, voluntary, intentional | 反义词：involuntary, unintentional, accidental | 词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The stress is self-induced. 压力是自己造成的。
He self-induces a state of calm. 他让自己处于平静的状态。


一般过去时:
She self-induced a fever. 她让自己发烧。
The problem was self-induced.  问题是自己造成的。


现在完成时:
He has self-induced a lot of pressure lately. 最近他给自己带来了很多压力。
She has self-induced a state of anxiety. 她让自己陷入焦虑状态。


过去完成时:
He had self-induced a migraine before the meeting. 会议前他已经让自己患上了偏头痛。
She had self-induced a sense of guilt. 她之前已经让自己产生了一种负罪感。


将来时:
I will self-induce a hypnotic state. 我将让自己进入催眠状态。
He will self-induce a panic attack. 他将让自己引发恐慌症。


虚拟语气:
If I had not self-induced this pressure, I would be healthier. 如果我没有给自己带来这种压力，我会更健康。
If he hadn’t self-induced this situation, things would be better. 如果他没有造成这种情况，事情会更好。


被动语态:  (Self-induced is rarely used passively because it implies active self-action.)  “Self-induced”本身很少用被动语态，因为它暗示了主动的自我行为。


疑问句:
Is the problem self-induced? 这个问题是自己造成的吗？
Was the injury self-induced? 伤势是自己造成的吗？


否定句:
The illness was not self-induced; it was caused by a virus.  这种疾病不是自己造成的；它是由病毒引起的。
The damage was not self-induced; it was accidental. 损害不是自己造成的；是意外造成的。
