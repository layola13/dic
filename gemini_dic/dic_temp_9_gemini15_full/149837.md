paleotherian:/ˌpeɪlioʊˈθɪəriən/| n. |古兽；古兽类动物|A paleotherian is an extinct mammal belonging to the order Paleotheriida.  They were herbivores that lived during the Eocene and Oligocene epochs.  They resembled horses but were more closely related to rhinoceroses. | Example:  Fossil evidence suggests that paleotherians were widespread across Europe during the Eocene.  古生物证据表明，始新世时期古兽广泛分布在欧洲各地。|近义词:extinct mammal,  ancient ungulate|反义词: modern mammal
