eclipse:/ɪˈklɪps/| n. vt.|日食；月食；黯然失色；遮蔽|An eclipse is an astronomical event that occurs when one celestial body moves into the shadow of another.  It can refer to a solar eclipse (when the moon blocks the sun) or a lunar eclipse (when the earth blocks the sun's light from reaching the moon).  As a verb, it means to overshadow or surpass.|例句：A total solar eclipse will occur next year.  明年将发生一次日全食。

The new building eclipsed all the others in the area. 这座新建筑使该地区所有其他建筑黯然失色。|近义词：overshadow, obscure, darken|反义词：illuminate, brighten
