fuels:/ˈfjuːəlz/ | n. |燃料；刺激物 | Fuels are substances that provide energy when burned.  燃料是燃烧时提供能量的物质。| Coal, oil, and natural gas are fossil fuels. 煤炭、石油和天然气是化石燃料。 | energy sources, power sources |  non-renewable resources


USAGE->

一般现在时:
Fossil fuels fuel most of the world's transportation. = 化石燃料为世界大部分交通运输提供动力。
This new technology fuels innovation in the industry. = 这项新技术推动了该行业的创新。


一般过去时:
Wood fueled the fires in the old days. = 古时候人们用木材生火。
The discovery fueled public speculation about alien life. = 这一发现引发了公众对地外生命的猜测。


现在完成时:
The debate has fueled much public discussion. = 这场辩论引发了大量的公众讨论。
This incident has fueled the flames of controversy. = 这起事件加剧了争议。


过去完成时:
The rumors had fueled his anxieties for weeks. = 这些谣言几周来一直加剧了他的焦虑。
Years of drought had fueled the wildfires. = 多年的干旱加剧了野火。


将来时:
Renewable energy sources will fuel the future. = 可再生能源将成为未来的动力。
This new policy will fuel economic growth. = 这项新政策将促进经济增长。


虚拟语气:
If we had more renewable energy, it would reduce our reliance on fossil fuels. = 如果我们有更多可再生能源，就能减少对化石燃料的依赖。
I wish this project would fuel my career advancement. = 我希望这个项目能够促进我的职业发展。


被动语态:
The car is fueled by gasoline. = 这辆车用汽油驱动。
The fire was fueled by strong winds. = 大火被强风助长。


疑问句:
What fuels your passion for art? = 什么激发了您对艺术的热情？
What fuels this economic growth? = 什么促进了这种经济增长？


否定句:
This engine doesn't fuel efficiently. = 这台发动机效率不高。
That argument didn't fuel any further discussion. = 那场争论没有引发进一步的讨论。


PHRASE->

fuel conflict: 激化冲突
