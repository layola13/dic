expatriates:/ˈɛkspætrieɪts/ | n. |居住在国外的侨民；移居海外者 | People who live in a country other than their native country.  Expatriates often maintain strong ties to their homeland, but they have chosen to reside and often work in a foreign land. |Example: Many  expatriates in Thailand work in the tourism industry.  许多在泰国的侨民都在旅游业工作。  |近义词:emigrants, residents abroad |反义词:immigrants, repatriates


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Many expatriates live in major cities. = 许多侨民居住在大城市。
She works as a teacher for expatriates. = 她为侨民做老师。


一般过去时:
He was an expatriate in France for ten years. = 他在法国做了十年侨民。
They lived as expatriates in London before returning home. = 他们以前在伦敦作为侨民居住，之后才回国。


现在完成时:

否定句:

词源:
The word "expatriate" comes from the Latin words "ex" (out) and "patria" (fatherland, native country).  Therefore, it literally means "one who is out of their fatherland."  The verb form, "expatriate," meaning to banish or exile, predates the noun form.
