词源:
The word "ham" ultimately derives from the Old English word *hamm, meaning "crooked piece of land" or "bend of a river," likely referencing the shape of a pig's leg.  This evolved into Middle English hamme, referring to the thigh of an animal, and eventually to the cured meat we know today.
