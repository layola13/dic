embellishment: /ɪmˈbɛlɪʃmənt/ | n. | 装饰；润色；修饰 | The addition of decorative details or features to something to make it more attractive.  It can refer to both physical objects and abstract concepts like writing or stories. | 例句：The cake was a masterpiece of embellishment, covered in intricate icing flowers.  蛋糕是装饰的杰作，上面覆盖着复杂的糖霜花。

The writer used clever embellishments to make the story more engaging.  作者用巧妙的润色使故事更引人入胜。


近义词：adornment, ornamentation, decoration, enhancement | 反义词：simplification, plainness, austerity

The artist adds embellishments to her paintings. = 艺术家在她的画作上添加装饰。
He embellishes his stories with fictional details. = 他用虚构的细节来润色他的故事。
