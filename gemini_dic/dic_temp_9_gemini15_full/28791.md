carolingians:/ˌkærəlɪnˈdʒiən/| n. |卡洛林王朝；卡洛林人 |The Carolingians were a Frankish dynasty that ruled much of Western Europe from the 8th to the 10th centuries.  Their reign is considered a significant period in the early Middle Ages, marked by advancements in art, culture, and governance. |The Carolingian Renaissance saw a revival of learning and art.  |近义词：Franks (as a related people),  |反义词：无
