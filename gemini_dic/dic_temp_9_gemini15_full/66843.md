expatriation: /ˌɛkspætəriˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 移居国外；流亡；脱离国籍 | The act of leaving one's native country to live permanently in another; also, the state of being an expatriate.  It can also refer to the formal revocation of citizenship. |


例句：

His expatriation to France marked a new chapter in his life. = 他移居法国标志着他人生的新篇章。

The forced expatriation of political dissidents is a violation of human rights. = 强迫政治异议人士流亡是侵犯人权的行为。


近义词：emigration, exile, banishment


反义词：repatriation, immigration, return


词源:

From Late Latin *expatriatio, from Latin ex- ("out of," "from") + patria ("fatherland," "native country").  The word reflects the literal sense of being "out of one's fatherland."
