epizoon:/ˈɛpɪzoʊn/| n.|附生动物|An animal that lives on the surface of another animal, typically without being parasitic.  An epizoon is an organism that lives on the surface of another organism, usually a larger animal, for support or protection, but without being parasitic.  Unlike parasites, epizoans generally do not harm their hosts. |Example: Barnacles are common epizoans found on whales.  鲸鱼身上常见的附生动物是藤壶。|近义词：commensal, symbiont|反义词：parasite
