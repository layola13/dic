/kaʊntərlæðɪŋ/| n. | 对旋车床| Counterlathing refers to a technique in woodworking or metalworking where two layers of laths are arranged at right angles to each other. This creates a stronger and more stable structure, often used in ceilings, walls, or other framework.  It's essentially a criss-cross pattern of thin strips.  The term is less common in modern usage, often replaced by more descriptive terms like "cross-lathing" or descriptions of the specific application.| The ceiling was reinforced with counterlathing to prevent sagging.  天花板用对旋车床加固以防止下垂。| cross-lathing, latticework |  (No direct antonym, as it describes a structure rather than a quality.)| n.
