neutrinos:/njuːˈtriːnoʊz/| n. |中微子|Subatomic particles with no electric charge and very little mass, that travel close to the speed of light.|  Neutrinos are produced in nuclear reactions, such as those in the sun and in nuclear reactors. They are extremely difficult to detect because they interact very weakly with matter. |近义词: lepton (a broader category)|反义词: N/A
