characterise: /ˈkærəktəraɪz/ | vt. |  刻画；描绘；使具有…的特性 | To describe the distinctive features or qualities of someone or something.  |  The novel characterises the struggles of young people in the city. 这部小说刻画了城市青年人的奋斗历程。 The disease is characterised by fever and rash.  这种疾病的特征是发烧和皮疹。 | depict, portray, describe, define |  differentiate, distinguish |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The artist characterises the model's beauty in the painting. =艺术家在画中刻画了模特的美丽。
This period is characterised by rapid technological advancements. =这个时期以快速的科技进步为特征。


一般过去时:
Historians characterised the era as one of great social change. =历史学家将那个时代描述为一个伟大的社会变革时期。
The author characterised the main character as a complex and flawed individual. =作者将主人公刻画成一个复杂而有缺陷的个体。


现在完成时:
Scientists have characterised the virus's genetic makeup. =科学家们已经确定了这种病毒的基因构成。
Researchers have characterised several new species of plants. =研究人员已经描述了几种新的植物物种。


过去完成时:
Scholars had characterised the author's work as groundbreaking before the new findings. =在新的发现之前，学者们已经将作者的作品描述为具有开创性。
The critics had characterised the play as a masterpiece before its official premiere. =在正式首映之前，评论家们已经将这部剧评为杰作。


将来时:
Future studies will characterise the long-term effects of climate change. =未来的研究将描述气候变化的长期影响。
They will characterise the newly discovered mineral's properties. =他们将描述这种新发现矿物的特性。


虚拟语气:
If we characterised the problem differently, we might find a better solution. =如果我们对这个问题有不同的描述，我们可能会找到更好的解决方案。
I wish I could characterise my feelings more accurately. =我希望我能更准确地描述我的感受。


被动语态:
The painting is characterised by its vibrant colours. =这幅画以其鲜艳的色彩为特征。
The experiment was characterised by a high degree of accuracy. =该实验具有高度的准确性。


疑问句:
How would you characterise the current political climate? =你怎么形容当前的政治气候？
How can we best characterise the impact of social media? =我们如何最好地描述社交媒体的影响？


否定句:
The report did not accurately characterise the situation on the ground. =该报告并没有准确地描述当地的实际情况。
I cannot characterise the experience adequately with words. =我无法用语言充分地描述这种经历。


PHRASE->
Characterised by: 以...为特征
Characterised as: 被描述为
Well-characterised: 特征明确的
Poorly characterised: 特征不明确的
Strongly characterised: 特征明显的
