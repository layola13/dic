sandbars:/ˈsændbɑːrz/| n. |沙洲，沙坝 |A sandbar is a ridge of sand, gravel, or other sediment that is deposited by water currents, often found in rivers, lakes, or seas.  They are typically submerged at high tide and exposed at low tide. Sandbars can be temporary or permanent features, shifting with changes in water flow and sediment supply.|Example: The boat ran aground on a sandbar near the shore.  船在靠近海岸的沙洲上搁浅了。


The children played on the exposed sandbar at low tide. 孩子们在退潮时暴露出来的沙洲上玩耍。|近义词：sandbank, shoal |反义词：deep water, channel
