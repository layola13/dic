capsule: /ˈkæpsjuːl/| n. |胶囊；太空舱；囊；荚 |A small container or covering, typically cylindrical and made of gelatin, containing medicine to be swallowed whole.  Also refers to a spacecraft designed to carry astronauts. |例句：The doctor prescribed a course of antibiotics in capsule form. 医生开了处方，用胶囊形式服用抗生素。

例句：The astronauts landed safely in the capsule. 宇航员安全地降落在太空舱里。|近义词：container, compartment, pod |反义词： bulk, loose

词源:
From Late Latin capsula, diminutive of capsa "box," from capere "to take, hold."  The modern English use reflects both the container aspect (like a small box) and the enclosing aspect (like a protective shell).
