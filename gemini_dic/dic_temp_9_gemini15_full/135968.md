/ˌnɒnkwɒntɪˈteɪtɪvnesɪz/ | n. |非数量性；非定量性 |The word "nonquantitativenesses" refers to the state or quality of being non-quantitative;  the absence of quantifiable characteristics or data. It highlights the lack of numerical or measurable aspects in something.  This is a very rarely used word, formed by adding the suffix "-nesses" (pluralizing a quality) to the adjective "nonquantitative".  The word is cumbersome and usually avoided in favor of simpler alternatives. |The study focused on the nonquantitativenesses of the artistic expression, emphasizing its subjective and emotional aspects rather than measurable elements.|近义词：qualitative aspects, subjective characteristics, immeasurable qualities |反义词：quantifiable aspects, measurable elements, numerical data | noun
