indo-Europeans:/ˌɪndoʊ jʊərəˈpiːənz/| n. | 印欧人 |  A group of peoples whose languages are related and are believed to have originated in a single prehistoric language.  The Indo-European languages are spread across a vast geographical area, encompassing most of Europe and parts of Asia. They include English, Spanish, Hindi, Persian, and many others.  | The Indo-Europeans migrated across Europe and Asia, spreading their languages and cultures. | 近义词：Indo-European speakers | 反义词：无
