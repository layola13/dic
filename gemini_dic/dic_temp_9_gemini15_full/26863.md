cajolement:/kəˈdʒoʊlmənt/| n. | 哄骗；劝诱；甜言蜜语 | The act of persuading someone to do something by flattery or coaxing.  It often implies a degree of deception or manipulation. |例句：Through sheer cajolement, she convinced him to invest in her risky venture. 通过纯粹的哄骗，她让他投资了她那冒险的企业。

例句：His cajolement was ultimately unsuccessful, as she remained firm in her decision. 他的劝诱最终失败了，因为她对自己的决定依然坚定。


近义词：coaxing, persuasion, flattery, wheedling, blandishment

反义词：coercion, intimidation, force

词性：名词


词源：源自法语单词 *cajouler*，意思是“to coax” or “to flatter”，最终源自古法语 *cajolaire*，其起源尚不明确，可能与拉丁语中的某种词根有关，但确切的词源联系尚未建立。
