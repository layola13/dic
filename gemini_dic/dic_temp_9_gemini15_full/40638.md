conventionalists:/kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪsts/| n. |传统主义者|Those who adhere to conventional beliefs, practices, or attitudes; those who favor or uphold established customs and traditions.  They often resist change and innovation.|例句：The conventionalists in the art world scoffed at the avant-garde movement.  传统艺术界的保守派人士嘲笑先锋派运动。


The conventionalists argued for maintaining the traditional curriculum.  传统主义者主张维持传统的课程。|近义词：traditionalists, conservatives, reactionaries |反义词：radicals, innovators, progressives|名词
