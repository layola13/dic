linchpins:/ˈlɪntʃpɪnz/| n. |关键人物；核心要素；枢轴销 |A linchpin is a person or thing vital to an organization or system.  It is the crucial element on which everything else depends. The word derives from its literal meaning: a pin that passes through the end of an axle to keep a wheel in place.  | Example:  He was the linchpin of the company's success. 他是公司成功的关键人物。

The team's success depends on the linchpin of its strategy. 团队的成功取决于其战略的核心要素。|近义词：keystone, cornerstone, pivotal element |反义词：nonessential, peripheral
