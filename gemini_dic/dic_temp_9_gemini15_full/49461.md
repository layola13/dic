deluded:/dɪˈluːdəd/| adj.| 欺骗自己的；被欺骗的；妄想的 |  Having a false belief or impression; suffering from delusions. Characterized by or resulting from delusion. |例句：He was deluded into thinking he could win. 他被蒙蔽了，以为自己能赢。

例句：She's completely deluded about her chances of success. 她完全被自己成功的几率给蒙蔽了。


近义词：deceived, misled, mistaken, fantastical, hallucinatory

反义词：realistic, clear-sighted, lucid, rational, sane


词源：源自动词delude，由de- (表示“去掉，取消”) +lude (源自拉丁语ludere，意思是“玩耍，欺骗”)构成，字面意思是“被玩弄，被欺骗”。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is deluded about his abilities. = 他对自己能力有错误的认识。
She is deluded into believing his lies. = 她被骗相信了他的谎言。


一般过去时:
He was deluded by her charm. = 他被她的魅力迷惑了。
They were deluded into thinking they were safe. = 他们被骗以为自己是安全的。


现在完成时:
He has been deluded for years. = 他被欺骗多年了。
She has deluded herself into believing she's talented. = 她一直欺骗自己相信自己很有天赋。


过去完成时:
He had been deluded by false promises. = 他一直被虚假的承诺欺骗着。
They had deluded themselves into thinking they could win. = 他们一直欺骗自己认为他们能赢。


将来时:
He will be deluded by his own ambition. = 他将被自己的野心蒙蔽。
She will delude herself into thinking she is right. = 她将会欺骗自己认为自己是正确的。


虚拟语气:
If he weren't deluded, he would see the truth. = 如果他不被欺骗，他会看到真相。
I wish I weren't deluded about my prospects. = 我希望我没有对自己的前景抱有不切实际的幻想。


被动语态:
He was deluded by a clever con artist. = 他被一个狡猾的骗子欺骗了。
The public was deluded by the politician's promises. = 公众被这位政治家的承诺所欺骗。


疑问句:
Is he deluded about his chances of winning? = 他对赢得比赛的机会是否抱有不切实际的幻想？
Are they deluded into believing anything he says? = 他们是否被骗相信了他说的任何话？


否定句:
He is not deluded; he knows the truth. = 他没有被欺骗；他知道真相。
She is not deluded about her limitations. = 她没有对自己能力的局限性抱有不切实际的幻想。

utterly deluded = 完全被欺骗
