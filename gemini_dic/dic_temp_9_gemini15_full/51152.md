despondent: /dɪˈspɒndənt/ | adj. | 沮丧的，失望的 | Feeling or showing discouragement or depression.  | Example:  He became despondent after failing the exam. 他考试不及格后变得沮丧。  She felt despondent about the future of her business. 她对公司的前景感到失望。|近义词: depressed, disheartened, dejected | 反义词: cheerful, optimistic, hopeful | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is despondent about the future. = 他对未来感到沮丧。
She is always despondent when it rains. = 下雨天她总是情绪低落。

一般过去时:
He was despondent after losing his job. = 他失业后很沮丧。
They were despondent because of the bad news. = 因为坏消息他们很沮丧。

现在完成时:
She has been despondent since her grandmother died. = 自从祖母去世后，她一直很沮丧。
He has become despondent over the years. = 他这些年来变得越来越沮丧。

过去完成时:
He had been despondent for weeks before he finally sought help. = 在他最终寻求帮助之前，他已经沮丧了好几个星期了。
She had been despondent all day before she received the good news. = 在她收到好消息之前，她整天都情绪低落。

将来时:
I fear he will be despondent if he doesn't get the promotion. = 我担心如果他得不到晋升，他会很沮丧。
She will likely be despondent if her plan fails. = 如果她的计划失败，她很可能会沮丧。

虚拟语气:
If I failed, I would be despondent. = 如果我失败了，我会很沮丧。
I wish I wasn't so despondent. = 我希望我不那么沮丧。

被动语态:
(Despondent is rarely used in the passive voice.  It describes a state of being, not an action performed on someone.)  N/A

疑问句:
Is he despondent about his prospects? = 他对自己的前景感到沮丧吗？
Are you feeling despondent today? = 你今天感到沮丧吗？

否定句:
He is not despondent; he's just tired. = 他并不沮丧，他只是累了。
She was not despondent despite the setbacks. = 尽管遭遇挫折，她并没有沮丧。
