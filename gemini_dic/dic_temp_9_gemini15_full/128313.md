neuroticism:/ˌnʊərəˈtɪsɪzəm/| n. |神经质；神经症倾向 |A tendency to experience negative emotions, such as anxiety, anger, and depression.  It is one of the five broad personality traits described in the Five-Factor Model (FFM) of personality. People high in neuroticism tend to be more emotionally reactive, vulnerable to stress, and prone to negative thoughts. |She suffers from high levels of neuroticism, which makes it difficult for her to cope with stress. 她患有高度的神经质，这使得她难以应对压力。|anxiety, nervousness, instability |emotional stability, calmness, composure |名词
