randomising: /ˈrændəmaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 随机化 | The process of making something random or introducing randomness into a system or process.  It often involves using algorithms or methods to ensure that selection or assignment is unbiased and unpredictable. | 例句：The researchers are randomising the participants into two groups for the study. 研究人员正在将参与者随机分成两组进行研究。

例句：The software uses a randomising algorithm to generate unique passwords. 该软件使用随机化算法生成唯一的密码。


近义词：shuffling, scrambling, mixing, jumbling


反义词：ordering, arranging, systematizing


词源：源自动词 "randomise" (英式拼写) 或 "randomize" (美式拼写)，由 "random" (随机的) + "-ise"/-ize" (动词后缀) 构成。 "Random"  itself originates from the Old French word "randon," meaning "violent, impetuous," which ultimately derived from a Germanic root.  The sense of "random" as "without a pattern" evolved later.
