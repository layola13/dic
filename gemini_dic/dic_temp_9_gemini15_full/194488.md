self-ridicules:/ˌsɛlfˈrɪdɪkjuːlz/| v.|自嘲，自我解嘲 | To make fun of oneself; to mock or ridicule oneself.  |例句：He self-ridicules his clumsy dancing. 他自嘲自己笨拙的舞姿。|例句：She often self-ridicules her forgetfulness. 她经常自嘲自己的健忘。|近义词：self-deprecates, mocks oneself, makes fun of oneself |反义词：self-aggrandizes, brags, boasts |动词


USAGE->

He self-ridicules his mistakes. = 他自嘲自己的错误。
She self-ridicules her singing voice. = 她自嘲自己的歌声。

一般过去时:
He self-ridiculed his poor attempt at cooking. = 他自嘲自己糟糕的烹饪尝试。

现在完成时:
He has self-ridiculed himself countless times. = 他无数次地自嘲自己。
She has self-ridiculed her attempts at learning a new language. = 她自嘲自己学习一门外语的尝试。

过去完成时:
He had self-ridiculed his earlier claims. = 他之前已经自嘲过自己之前的言论。
She had self-ridiculed her naivety before realizing the truth. = 在意识到真相之前，她已经自嘲过自己的天真。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he were more confident, he wouldn't self-ridicule himself so often. = 如果他更有自信，他就不会那么经常自嘲自己了。
If she hadn't been so self-critical, she wouldn't have self-ridiculed her work. = 如果她没有那么苛刻地批评自己，她就不会自嘲自己的作品了。

被动语态:  (This verb is rarely used passively.)  His clumsiness was often self-ridiculed by him. = 他的笨拙经常被他自己所自嘲。(较为不自然)
His attempts were often subject to his own self-ridicule. = 他的尝试经常成为他自己自嘲的对象。(更自然的表达方式)
