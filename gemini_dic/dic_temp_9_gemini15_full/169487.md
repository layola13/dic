propagandizer:/ˌprɒpəˈɡændɪzər/| n. |宣传者，鼓吹者 |A propagandizer is someone who spreads propaganda, often with the intention of influencing public opinion. They may use various methods, such as speeches, writings, or media appearances, to promote a particular cause or ideology.  |Example: The government employed many propagandizers to boost support for the war.  政府雇佣了许多宣传者来提升对战争的支持。  The opposition party accused the ruling party of using propagandizers to spread misinformation. 反对党指责执政党利用宣传者散布虚假信息。|近义词：propagandist, agitator, advocate |反义词：truth-teller, fact-checker


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He propagandizes for the new political party. = 他为新政党做宣传。
She propagandizes against the proposed tax increase. = 她反对提议的增税。

一般过去时:
They propagandized heavily during the election campaign. = 他们在竞选期间大力宣传。
He propagandized his ideas through various media outlets. = 他通过各种媒体宣传他的想法。

现在完成时:
The government has propagandized effectively to change public opinion. = 政府有效地进行宣传以改变公众舆论。
They have propagandized for years to achieve their goals. = 他们多年来一直在进行宣传以实现他们的目标。

过去完成时:
Before the war, they had propagandized extensively about the enemy. = 在战争爆发前，他们对敌人进行了广泛的宣传。
She had propagandized against the regime for many years before her arrest. = 在被捕之前，她多年来一直对该政权进行宣传。

虚拟语气:
