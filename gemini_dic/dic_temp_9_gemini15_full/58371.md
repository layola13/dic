dualisms:/ˈduːəlɪzəm/| n. |二元论；对立统一；二元性 |The philosophical concept that reality consists of two irreducible opposites.  It often refers to mind and body, good and evil, or other contrasting pairs.  Dualism is a framework for understanding the world through these opposing forces. | Example:  Many religions embrace a dualism of good and evil. 许多宗教都包含善恶二元论。


The study of dualisms in literature explores contrasting themes. 文学中对二元论的研究探讨了对比鲜明的主题。|近义词：dichotomy, antithesis, duality |反义词：monism, unity, synthesis |名词
