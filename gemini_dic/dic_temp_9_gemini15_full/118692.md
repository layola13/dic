metacarpal:/ˌmɛtəˈkɑːrpəl/| n. |掌骨|The metacarpals are the five long bones in the hand, between the wrist and the fingers.  They form the palm of the hand and provide support for the fingers.|例句：The fracture involved one of the metacarpals in his right hand.  他右手的一根掌骨骨折了。|例句：The surgeon carefully realigned the broken metacarpal. 外科医生仔细地将断裂的掌骨复位。|近义词：hand bone|反义词：phalanges (指骨)

词源:
The word "metacarpal" comes from the Greek words "meta" (meaning "beyond" or "after") and "carpus" (meaning "wrist").  Therefore, metacarpal literally means "beyond the wrist," accurately reflecting its anatomical location.
