chondropterygii:/ˌkɒndrəˈptɛrɪdʒiː/|/ˌkɑːndrəˈptɛrɪdʒiː/| n. |软骨鱼纲|Chondrichthyes, also known as cartilaginous fishes, is a class of fish characterized by a cartilaginous skeleton, rather than the bony skeletons of most other fish.  They include sharks, rays, and chimaeras.  The name comes from the Greek words "chondros" (cartilage) and "pteryx" (fin/wing).|Example:  Sharks, rays, and skates belong to the class Chondrichthyes (Chondropterygii).  鲨鱼、鳐鱼和滑冰鱼都属于软骨鱼纲。|近义词：Chondrichthyes|反义词：Osteichthyes (bony fish)
