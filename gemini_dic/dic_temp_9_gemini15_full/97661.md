inhibiting: /ɪnˈhɪbɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 抑制的；阻止的 |  Having the power to restrain, hinder, or prevent.  It describes something that slows down, prevents, or suppresses something else. |

例句：

The drug has inhibiting effects on the growth of cancer cells.  该药物对癌细胞的生长具有抑制作用。

His shyness was an inhibiting factor in his career advancement. 他的害羞是阻碍他事业发展的因素。


近义词：restrictive, suppressing, restraining, hindering, deterring

反义词：stimulating, encouraging, promoting, facilitating


词源:

From the verb "inhibit," ultimately from Latin "inhibere," meaning "to hold back, restrain."  The prefix "in-" means "in" or "on," and "hibere" means "to hold."

一般过去时:
The cold weather inhibited plant growth. = 寒冷的天气抑制了植物生长。
The heavy rain inhibited our plans for a picnic. = 大雨打乱了我们的野餐计划。


现在完成时:

His anxiety has inhibited his performance in recent competitions. = 他的焦虑已经抑制了他最近比赛中的表现。


过去完成时:
The prior injury had inhibited his participation in the marathon. = 先前的伤势已经妨碍了他参加马拉松比赛。
Previous failures had inhibited her from trying again. = 之前的失败让她裹足不前。


将来时:

Increased security measures will inhibit terrorist activities. = 加强的安全措施将抑制恐怖活动。


虚拟语气:
If the government hadn't intervened, the crisis would have inhibited economic growth. = 如果政府不干预，这场危机就会抑制经济增长。
If I wasn't so nervous, I wouldn't be inhibited in my presentation. = 如果我不那么紧张，我的演讲就不会那么拘谨。


被动语态:
The project was inhibited by unexpected delays. = 该项目因意外延误而受阻。
His progress was inhibited by a lack of resources. = 他的进步由于缺乏资源而受阻。


疑问句:

否定句:
