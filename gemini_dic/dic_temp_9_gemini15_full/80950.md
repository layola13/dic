grace:/ɡreɪs/ | n. v. adj. |优雅，恩典，慈悲；使优美，使优雅；优雅的，优美的 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "grace" refers to elegance and refinement of movement or appearance. It can also mean a courteous, kind, or forgiving manner.  In a religious context, it signifies divine favor or blessing. As a verb, "grace" means to adorn or enhance something with elegance or beauty. As an adjective, it describes something that is elegant, graceful, or pleasing.


例句:
She moved with effortless grace. = 她优雅地移动着。

The dancer's grace captivated the audience. = 舞者的优雅深深吸引了观众。

He showed great grace in accepting defeat. = 他以极大的风度接受了失败。

The artist's work was full of grace and beauty. = 艺术家的作品充满了优雅和美丽。

The building's graceful lines impressed everyone. = 建筑物优雅的线条给每个人留下了深刻的印象。


近义词:
elegance, charm, beauty, favor, mercy, politeness, refinement


反义词:
awkwardness, clumsiness, rudeness, discourtesy, ugliness, cruelty


词性:
noun, verb, adjective
