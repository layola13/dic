fallacy:/ˈfæləsi/ | n. |谬误，谬论，错误的推理 | A fallacy is a mistaken belief, especially one based on unsound argument.  It is a flaw in reasoning that renders an argument invalid.  Fallacies can be accidental or intentional, used to deceive or simply due to a lack of critical thinking. | Example:  It's a fallacy to assume that because someone is wealthy, they are happy.  = 认为有钱人就幸福是一种谬误。

The argument that all swans are white because only white swans have ever been seen is a fallacy. = 所有天鹅都是白色的，因为人们只见过白色的天鹅，这是一个谬论。|近义词：error, mistake, misconception, delusion |反义词：truth, validity, soundness, correctness |

词源:  Middle English: from Old French *fallace, from Latin fallacia ‘deception, trickery’, from fallere ‘to deceive’.
