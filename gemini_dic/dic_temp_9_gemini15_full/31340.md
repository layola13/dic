characterises: /ˈkærəktəraɪzɪz/ | v. | 表征；具有…的特征；描绘…的特性 | To describe the distinctive features or characteristics of someone or something. |  例句：His arrogance characterises his leadership style.  他的傲慢是其领导风格的特征。

例句：The novel characterises the struggles of working-class families in the 1930s. 这部小说描绘了20世纪30年代工人阶级家庭的挣扎。


近义词：distinguishes, defines, marks, typifies, depicts


反义词：obscures, misrepresents, disguises


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His confidence characterises him. = 他的自信是他的特征。
Her kindness characterises her interactions with others. = 她的善良是她与他人互动时的特征。


一般过去时:
The artist's bold brushstrokes characterised his paintings. = 艺术家大胆的笔触是其绘画的特色。
The war characterised that era. = 那场战争是那个时代的特征。


现在完成时:
His ambition has characterised his entire career. = 他的雄心壮志贯穿了他的整个职业生涯。
Her resilience has characterised her response to adversity. = 她的韧性是她应对逆境的特征。


过去完成时:
The social unrest had characterised the political landscape before the revolution. = 在革命爆发前，社会动荡是政治局面的特征。
The economic depression had characterised the previous decade. = 经济萧条是前十年的特征。


将来时:
Technological advancements will characterise the future of work. = 技术进步将成为未来工作的特征。
Increased globalisation will characterise the coming years. = 日益增长的全球化将是未来几年的特征。


虚拟语气:
If his honesty characterised his actions, he wouldn't be in this mess. = 如果他的诚实是其行为的特征，他不会陷入这种困境。
If her compassion characterised her leadership, the company would thrive. = 如果她的同情心是其领导力的特征，公司将会兴旺发达。


被动语态:
The painting is characterised by its vibrant colours. = 这幅画的特点是色彩鲜艳。
The event was characterised by a sense of urgency. = 这件事的特点是紧急感。


疑问句:
What characterises this particular species of bird? = 这种鸟类的特征是什么？
How can we characterise the impact of climate change? = 我们如何描述气候变化的影响？


否定句:
His actions did not characterise his true nature. = 他的行为并不代表他真正的本性。
The report did not accurately characterise the situation on the ground. = 这份报告没有准确地描述实况。


PHRASE->
Characterise by:  用...来表征
Characterise as: 将...描述为
Characterise the: 表征…的
Characterise oneself as:  把自己描绘成
Characterised with:  以...为特征


词源:
characterises comes from the word "character," ultimately derived from the Greek word "charaktēr" (χαρακτήρ), meaning "mark, stamp, engraving," referring to the distinctive mark impressed on something.  The suffix "-ise" is a verb-forming suffix indicating to make or become something.
