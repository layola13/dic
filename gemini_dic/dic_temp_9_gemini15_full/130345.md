non-tribal: /ˌnɑːnˈtraɪbəl/ | adj. | 非部落的；非宗族的 | Not belonging to or characteristic of a tribe or tribal society.  It often implies a lack of tribal affiliations, customs, or structures.  The term is often used in contexts concerning social organization, governance, or cultural identity. |

例句：

The non-tribal population of the region is growing rapidly. = 该地区非部落人口正在迅速增长。

He chose a non-tribal style of leadership. = 他选择了一种非部落的领导风格。


近义词：non-tribalistic, non-native (in some contexts)


反义词：tribal, indigenous (in some contexts)


词源:

The word is a combination of "non-" (a prefix meaning "not") and "tribal" (referring to a tribe or tribal society).  "Tribal" itself derives from the Old French "tribal," ultimately from the Latin "tribus," meaning "tribe."
