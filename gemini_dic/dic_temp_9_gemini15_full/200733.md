sleevehand:/sliːvˌhænd/| n.|袖手旁观|A person who does nothing to help in a situation; someone who remains passive or inactive.  This is not a standard dictionary word but rather a compound word formed from "sleeve" and "hand". It describes someone whose hands are figuratively in their sleeves, implying inaction.|例句：He was a sleevehand during the crisis, offering no assistance to those in need. 他在危机期间袖手旁观，没有向需要帮助的人提供任何援助。|近义词：bystander, onlooker, passive observer|反义词：helper, intervener, participant|名词
