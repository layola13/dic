homograph:/ˈhɒməɡræf/ /ˈhɑːməɡræf/| n. |同形异义词 |A word that has the same spelling as another word but a different meaning and may or may not have a different pronunciation.  Homographs are words that are spelled the same but have different meanings.  They can be pronounced the same (perfect homonyms) or differently. |Examples:  The bank where I keep my money is near the river bank. (Different pronunciations, different meanings)  Lead (metal) and lead (to guide). (Different pronunciations, different meanings) |近义词：同形词 (synonym only in the context of linguistic analysis)|反义词：无|名词
