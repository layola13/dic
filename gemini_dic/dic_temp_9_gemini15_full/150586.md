paper-shuffler: /ˈpeɪpərˌʃʌflər/ | n. |文件整理员；办事员；（尤指）效率低下的办事员 | A paper-shuffler is a person whose job involves a lot of routine paperwork, often implying a lack of significant responsibility or impact.  The term often carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting inefficiency or a lack of genuine contribution. | Example: He felt like a mere paper-shuffler in his dead-end job. 他觉得他在这份没有前途的工作中只是一个简单的办事员。  | 近义词: clerk, file clerk, office worker, bureaucrat | 反义词:  executive, leader, decision-maker | 名词
