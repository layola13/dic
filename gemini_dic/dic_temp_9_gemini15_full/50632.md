deride:/dɪˈraɪd/| vt.|嘲笑；嘲讽；奚落 |To express contempt for; ridicule.  |例句：The critics derided his new play as a failure. 批评家们嘲笑他的新剧是一部失败之作。

例句：They derided his efforts as childish and naive. 他们嘲笑他的努力幼稚和天真。


近义词：mock, scorn, ridicule, jeer, sneer

反义词：praise, commend, applaud

词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He derides his opponent's policies. 他嘲笑对手的政策。
They deride the new fashion trend. 他们嘲笑新的时尚潮流。


一般过去时:

过去完成时:
The critics had already derided the book before it was even published.  评论家们在书出版之前就已经嘲笑了。
He had derided her dreams for years, until he saw her succeed. 他多年来一直嘲笑她的梦想，直到他看到她成功。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
They did not deride his performance, instead they praised it. 他们没有嘲笑他的表现，而是赞扬了他。

词源:
From Old French *derider, from Latin deridere "to mock, laugh at," from de- "away, down" + ridere "to laugh."
