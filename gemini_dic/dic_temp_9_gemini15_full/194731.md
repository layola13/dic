selfish:/ˈsɛlfɪʃ/| adj. |自私的，利己的 |Characterized by or manifesting concern primarily for oneself and one's own needs or desires; egoistic.  It implies a disregard for the feelings and needs of others. |例句：It was selfish of him to eat all the cake without offering any to others. 他把蛋糕全吃了，一点也不分给别人，真是自私。

例句：Her selfish actions caused a lot of problems for her friends. 她的自私行为给她的朋友们带来了很多麻烦。


近义词：egoistic, self-centered, egoistical, narcissistic, inconsiderate |反义词：selfless, altruistic, unselfish, generous, kind


USAGE->

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

将来时:

被动语态:

词源:

The word "selfish" comes from the Old English "self" meaning "self" and the suffix "-ish," which forms adjectives meaning "having the nature or characteristics of."  Thus, "selfish" literally means "having the nature of self," prioritizing the self above all else.
