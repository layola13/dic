phthisipneumony:/ˌθɪsɪpnuˈmoʊni/| n. |肺结核性肺炎|Phthisipneumony is a now-obsolete term referring to a lung infection characterized by both tuberculosis and pneumonia.  It describes a condition where the lungs are affected by both diseases simultaneously, resulting in a more severe and potentially life-threatening illness. The term reflects a time when the understanding of these diseases was less precise.  Today,  tuberculosis and pneumonia are diagnosed and treated separately, although co-infection is still possible.|The patient presented with symptoms consistent with phthisipneumony, exhibiting both tubercular and pneumonic features.|近义词：tuberculous pneumonia, pulmonary tuberculosis with pneumonia|反义词：healthy lungs, lung health
