self-suppression:/ˌsɛlf səˈprɛʃən/| n. |自我抑制，自我压制|The act of inhibiting or suppressing one's own thoughts, feelings, or behaviors.  This can be a conscious or unconscious process, and it can be motivated by various factors, including social norms, fear of judgment, or a desire to maintain control.  It often involves a conflict between internal drives and external constraints. |例句：His self-suppression of his anger was admirable, but ultimately unhealthy.  他的怒火自我压制令人钦佩，但最终并不健康。

例句：The constant self-suppression of her emotions led to a mental breakdown. 她持续压抑自己的情绪最终导致精神崩溃。


近义词：self-control, repression, inhibition, restraint |反义词：self-expression, outburst, indulgence
