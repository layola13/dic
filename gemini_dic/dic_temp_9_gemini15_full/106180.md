knockdown:/ˈnɒkdaʊn/ /ˈnɑːkdaʊn/| n., adj., v.|击倒；拆卸；廉价出售的；|

英文解析:
As a noun, "knockdown" refers to the act of knocking someone or something down, or the state of being knocked down. It can also refer to a cheap price, often for a quick sale.  As an adjective, it describes something that is easily knocked down or assembled, or something sold at a reduced price. As a verb, it means to knock someone or something down.

例句:

n. (击倒):
The boxer delivered a powerful knockdown punch. 拳击手打出了一记强有力的击倒拳。

n. (拆卸):
The furniture arrived in a knockdown state, requiring assembly. 家具以散件的形式到达，需要组装。


adj. (廉价出售的):
They offered a knockdown price to clear their inventory. 他们以低价出售商品以清理库存。

adj. (易于拆卸的):
The knockdown shelves were easy to put together. 易于拆卸的书架很容易组装。


v.:
The strong wind knocked down the trees. 大风把树吹倒了。


近义词:
n.: defeat, demolition, reduction
adj.: cheap, inexpensive, readily assembled
v.: fell, topple, demolish


反义词:
n.: victory, construction, high price
adj.: expensive, costly, firmly fixed
v.: erect, build, raise


词性:
n., adj., v.
