jaywalkers:/ˈdʒeɪˌwɔːkərz/| n. |乱穿马路的人|A person who crosses a road without paying attention to traffic.|例句：The jaywalkers were nearly hit by a car. 乱穿马路的人差点被车撞到。|近义词：pedestrian (if breaking traffic laws), road crosser|反义词：careful pedestrian


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Many people jaywalk every day. = 每天很多人乱穿马路。
He often jaywalks across the street. = 他经常横穿马路。


一般过去时:
I jaywalked across the street yesterday. = 我昨天乱穿马路。
She jaywalked and almost got hit by a bus. = 她乱穿马路，差点被公共汽车撞到。


现在完成时:
They have jaywalked many times this week. = 他们本周已经多次乱穿马路了。
He has never jaywalked before. = 他以前从未乱穿马路过。


过去完成时:
I had jaywalked before I realized it was dangerous. = 在我意识到很危险之前，我已经乱穿马路了。

将来时:

疑问句:
