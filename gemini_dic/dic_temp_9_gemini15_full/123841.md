moralisingly:/ˌmɒrəˈlaɪzɪŋli/ | adv. | 道貌岸然地；说教地 | In a way that shows a strong moral disapproval of something or someone.  It often implies a self-righteous or sanctimonious tone. |例句：He spoke moralisingly about the evils of gambling. 他道貌岸然地谈论赌博的罪恶。

He lectured moralisingly on the importance of honesty. 他说教般地讲解诚实的重要性。


近义词：preachily, sanctimoniously, self-righteously, judgmentally, didactically

反义词：tolerantly, understandingly, compassionately


词源:  Derived from the verb "moralise" (to preach or moralize), which itself comes from "moral" (relating to principles of right and wrong). The suffix "-ingly" forms an adverb, indicating "in a moralising manner".
