interjected:/ˌɪntərˈdʒektɪd/| v.|打断；插嘴；突然插入|To insert (a word, remark, etc.) into a conversation|He interjected a witty remark into the serious discussion. 他在严肃的讨论中插了一句俏皮话。|近义词：interrupt, insert, butt in|反义词：omit, exclude


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He interjects comments during the lecture. 他在讲座期间插话。
She interjects frequently in meetings. 她在会议中经常插嘴。


一般过去时:
He interjected a question during the presentation. 他在演示过程中插了一个问题。
She interjected a joke to lighten the mood. 她插了一个笑话来活跃气氛。


现在完成时:
He has interjected several times already. 他已经插了好几次话了。
She has interjected her opinion into the debate. 她已经将她的意见插进了辩论中。


过去完成时:
Before I could finish my sentence, he had interjected with a correction.  在我能说完话之前，他已经插话纠正了。
She had interjected several times before anyone realized. 在任何人意识到之前，她已经插话好几次了。


将来时:
He will interject when he thinks it is appropriate. 他认为合适的时候就会插话。
She will interject a comment later in the meeting. 她将在会议稍后插话评论。


虚拟语气:
If he had not interjected, the argument would have escalated. 如果他没有插话，争论就会升级。
I wish she hadn't interjected so rudely. 我真希望她不要那么粗鲁地插话。


被动语态:
A comment was interjected by a member of the audience.  一位观众插话评论。
His opinion was interjected into the conversation unexpectedly. 他的意见出人意料地被插入谈话中。


疑问句:
Was a comment interjected during the performance? 演出期间有插话评论吗？
Has anything been interjected into the discussion that we should be aware of?  讨论中是否有我们应该注意的任何插话？


否定句:
He did not interject during the important speech. 他在重要的演讲中没有插话。
She didn't interject, letting the others speak first. 她没有插话，让其他人先发言。
