rebels:/ˈrɛbəlz/| n.|叛乱者，反抗者|People who fight against their government or ruler.  They are often involved in armed conflict or civil disobedience.|例句：The rebels seized control of the capital city. 叛军占领了首都。|例句：The government is struggling to suppress the rebels. 政府正在努力镇压叛乱分子。|近义词：insurgents, revolutionaries, mutineers|反义词：loyalists, government supporters, conformists|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rebels fight against the government. = 叛军反抗政府。
The group rebels against the new rules. = 该团体反抗新规。

一般过去时:
The rebels attacked the city last night. = 叛军昨晚袭击了这座城市。
The people rebelled against the king's tyranny. = 人民反抗国王的暴政。

现在完成时:
The rebels have captured several towns. = 叛军已经占领了几座城镇。
The citizens have rebelled against the unfair taxes for years. = 公民多年来一直反抗不公平的税收。

过去完成时:
The rebels had already taken the bridge before the army arrived. = 在军队到达之前，叛军已经占领了桥梁。
The people had rebelled many times before this final uprising. = 在这次最终起义之前，人民已经多次反抗。

将来时:
The rebels will likely attack again soon. = 叛军很可能会很快再次发动袭击。
The oppressed people will rebel against the dictator. = 被压迫的人民将反抗独裁者。

虚拟语气:
If the rebels were stronger, they would win. = 如果叛军更强大，他们就会获胜。
I wish the rebels would lay down their arms. = 我希望叛军放下武器。

被动语态:
The city was captured by the rebels. = 这座城市被叛军占领了。
The rebellion was brutally suppressed by the government. = 叛乱被政府残酷镇压。

疑问句:
Are the rebels gaining ground? = 叛军是否正在取得进展？
How many rebels are there? = 有多少叛军？

否定句:
The rebels did not succeed in their attempt. = 叛军的企图没有成功。
The people did not rebel against the new law. = 人民没有反抗新法律。
