shatter-proof: /ˈʃætərpruːf/ | adj. | 防碎的，抗碎裂的 |  Resistant to shattering or breaking.  The term implies a significant degree of protection against impact or stress that would normally cause an object to break into pieces. |  例句：The phone case is shatter-proof, protecting the phone from accidental drops. 手机壳是防碎的，可以保护手机免受意外跌落。

The lenses are made of shatter-proof glass for enhanced safety. 镜片由防碎玻璃制成，以增强安全性。| 近义词：unbreakable, impact-resistant, crack-resistant | 反义词：fragile, breakable, easily-shattered |  词性：形容词

词源:
The word "shatter-proof" is a compound word.  "Shatter" comes from the Old English word *scaterian, meaning "to scatter,"  and  "-proof" is a suffix meaning "resistant to," "protected from," or "immune to."  Therefore, "shatter-proof" literally means "resistant to shattering."
