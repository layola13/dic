embankments:/ɪmˈbæŋkmənts/| n. |堤岸；路堤；堆土护岸 |Artificial structures built to control or prevent the flow of water, often along rivers or coastlines. They can also be used to support roads or railways.|例句：The river embankments were reinforced after the recent floods.  最近的洪水过后，河堤得到了加固。|例句：The railway runs along high embankments. 铁路沿着高高的路堤延伸。|近义词：dams, levees, dikes, seawalls |反义词：depressions, valleys

The river embankments protect the town from flooding. = 河堤保护着城镇免受洪水侵袭。
