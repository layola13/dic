rating:/ˈreɪtɪŋ/| n. |等级，评级，收视率 |A numerical or letter grade assigned to something to indicate its quality, value, or performance.  It can refer to a movie, a restaurant, a product, or even a person's creditworthiness.  |例句：The movie received a high rating from critics. 这部电影受到了影评人的高度评价。|例句：The restaurant's rating has dropped since the new chef arrived.自从新厨师来了之后，这家餐馆的评分下降了。|近义词：ranking, score, assessment, evaluation |反义词： none specifically, but low rating is the opposite concept.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The hotel has a high rating. = 这家酒店的评分很高。
This product receives a low rating. = 这个产品的评分很低。


一般过去时:
The restaurant's rating was excellent last year. = 去年这家餐馆的评分非常好。
The film received a poor rating at its premiere. = 这部电影在首映式上获得了糟糕的评价。


现在完成时:
The show has received consistently high ratings. = 这部剧一直收视率很高。
This restaurant has maintained a five-star rating for years. = 这家餐厅多年来一直保持着五星级评价。


过去完成时:
The book had already received positive ratings before its official release. = 这本书在正式发行前就已经获得了积极的评价。
The product had a lower rating before the improved version was launched. = 在改进版推出之前，该产品的评分较低。


将来时:
We expect the movie to receive a high rating. = 我们预计这部电影会获得很高的评价。
I think the new album will have a good rating. = 我认为这张新专辑的评价会很好。


虚拟语气:
If the restaurant had a higher rating, it would attract more customers. = 如果这家餐馆的评分更高，它会吸引更多顾客。
If the movie had received a better rating, it would have won more awards. = 如果这部电影获得更好的评价，它就能赢得更多奖项。


被动语态:
The product is rated highly by consumers. = 该产品受到消费者的高度评价。
The restaurant's rating is determined by customer reviews. = 餐馆的评分是由顾客评论决定的。


疑问句:
What is the rating of this hotel? = 这家酒店的评分是多少？
What's the rating for this new game? = 这个新游戏的评分是多少？


否定句:
The restaurant didn't receive a good rating. = 这家餐馆没有收到好的评价。
The product's rating wasn't as high as expected. = 该产品的评价不如预期的高。
