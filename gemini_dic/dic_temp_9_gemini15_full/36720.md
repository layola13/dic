collocation:/ˌkɒləˈkeɪʃən/| /ˌkɑːləˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |搭配；词语搭配 |The habitual juxtaposition of a particular word with other words; a sequence of words or terms that co-occur more often than would be expected by chance.  It refers to words that frequently appear together. Understanding collocations is crucial for natural and fluent English. | Example:  "Strong coffee" is a common collocation, while "powerful coffee" sounds less natural.  The words "make" and "decision" frequently collocate.  |近义词：combination, phrase, expression |反义词：None
