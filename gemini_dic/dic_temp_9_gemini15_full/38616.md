concretionary:/kənˈkriːʃənəri/ | adj. | 成岩的；结核状的 |  Relating to or characterized by concretions; formed into a hard mass.  Describes a process or material where substances are accumulated and cemented together to form a hard mass, often within a softer matrix. |The concretionary limestone was difficult to break.  这块成岩的石灰石很难打破。 |The geologist identified several concretionary structures in the rock. 地质学家在岩石中发现了几个结核状结构。|nodular, aggregated |  dispersed, scattered
