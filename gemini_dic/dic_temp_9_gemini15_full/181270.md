reinstitutionalizations:/ˌriːɪnstɪtjuːʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. | 再制度化；重新收容；重新机构化 | The act or process of returning individuals or groups to institutional settings, often after a period of deinstitutionalization. This can refer to various contexts, including mental health care, juvenile justice, and social welfare.  | Example: The rising rates of homelessness have led to concerns about the re-institutionalization of mentally ill individuals.  重新出现无家可归现象导致人们担心精神病患者会再次被收容。|近义词:reinstatement, re-confinement|反义词:deinstitutionalization
