incubated:/ˈɪŋkjuˌbeɪtɪd/| v. |孵化；培养；酝酿|To keep eggs warm so that they hatch, or to keep something at a suitable temperature for development.|The hen incubated her eggs for three weeks.|近义词：hatch, brood, foster|反义词：destroy, kill


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The hen incubates her eggs diligently. = 母鸡勤奋地孵蛋。
Scientists incubate cell cultures in controlled environments. = 科学家在受控环境中培养细胞培养物。


一般过去时:
The farmer incubated the eggs until they hatched. = 农民孵蛋直到它们孵化出来。
The company incubated a new business idea for several months. = 公司酝酿了一个新的商业创意几个月。


现在完成时:
She has incubated several innovative projects this year. = 她今年已经孵化了几个创新项目。
The lab has incubated the bacteria for 24 hours. = 实验室已经培养细菌24小时了。


过去完成时:
They had incubated the experiment for a week before observing results. = 他们在观察结果之前已经培养了实验一个星期。
The entrepreneur had incubated the startup for two years before seeking funding. = 企业家在寻求资金之前已经孵化了这家初创公司两年。


将来时:
We will incubate the samples at 37 degrees Celsius. = 我们将在37摄氏度下培养样品。
The incubator will incubate the eggs automatically. = 孵化器将自动孵化鸡蛋。


虚拟语气:
If the temperature were higher, the eggs would not incubate properly. = 如果温度更高，鸡蛋就不能正常孵化。
If they had incubated the bacteria longer, the results might have been different. = 如果他们培养细菌的时间更长，结果可能会有所不同。


被动语态:
The eggs were incubated in a warm, dark place. = 鸡蛋在温暖黑暗的地方孵化。
The project was incubated by a large corporation. = 该项目由一家大公司孵化。


疑问句:
How long will the eggs be incubated? = 鸡蛋要孵化多久？
Were the samples properly incubated? = 样品是否被正确地培养？


否定句:
The eggs did not incubate successfully. = 鸡蛋没有成功孵化。
The company did not incubate that idea further. = 公司没有进一步培养那个想法。
