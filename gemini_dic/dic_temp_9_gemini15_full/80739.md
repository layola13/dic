goshan:/ˈɡoʊʃæn/| n. |歌珊 |Goshen is a place name primarily associated with the biblical story of the Israelites' sojourn in Egypt.  It refers to a fertile land in Egypt where the Israelites lived during their enslavement and later, where Joseph and his family settled. The exact location of ancient Goshen remains a subject of scholarly debate. |例句：The Israelites found refuge and prosperity in Goshen. 以色列人在歌珊找到了避难所和繁荣。|近义词：haven, refuge |反义词:  desolation, wasteland |名词
