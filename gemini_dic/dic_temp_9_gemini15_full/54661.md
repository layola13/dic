disobeying: /ˌdɪsoʊˈbeɪɪŋ/ | v. |不服从，违抗 | The act of not obeying or refusing to obey a rule, order, or person.  | 例句：The children were punished for disobeying their parents. 孩子们因为不服从父母而受到惩罚。 |近义词：defying, flouting, disregarding | 反义词：obeying, complying


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is disobeying the rules. = 他正在违反规定。
They are disobeying orders. = 他们在违抗命令。


一般过去时:
She disobeyed her teacher. = 她违抗了老师。
The soldiers disobeyed the general's command. = 士兵们违抗了将军的命令。


现在完成时:
He has disobeyed the law several times. = 他多次违反法律。
They have disobeyed their parents' wishes. = 他们违背了父母的意愿。


过去完成时:
The dog had disobeyed the command before the accident. = 那条狗在事故发生前就违抗了命令。
She had disobeyed her boss many times before she was fired. = 在被解雇之前，她已经多次违抗老板的命令。


将来时:
He will disobey the rules if he is not careful. = 如果他不小心，他会违反规定的。
They will disobey the orders if they are not given a good reason. = 如果没有充分的理由，他们将违抗命令。


虚拟语气:
If he disobeyed, he would be punished. = 如果他违抗，他就会受到惩罚。
I wish he hadn't disobeyed me. = 我真希望他没违抗我。


被动语态:
The rules were disobeyed. = 规则被违反了。
Orders were disobeyed by the soldiers. = 命令被士兵们违抗了。


疑问句:
Were the instructions disobeyed? = 指示被违反了吗？
Is he disobeying the law? = 他在违反法律吗？


否定句:
He did not disobey the orders. = 他没有违抗命令。
They were not disobeying the rules. = 他们没有违反规定。


PHRASE->
disobeying orders = 违抗命令
