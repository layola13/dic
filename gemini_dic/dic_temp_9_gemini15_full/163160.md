precommunication: /ˌpriːkəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |预沟通；前沟通；沟通前的准备 |The process of preparing for communication, including establishing context, anticipating responses, and choosing appropriate communication channels.  It encompasses all activities that occur before the actual exchange of information begins.  This might include researching your audience, outlining your message, or choosing a suitable communication medium. | Example:  Careful precommunication, including thorough research of the client's needs, ensured the presentation was a success.  = 仔细的预沟通，包括对客户需求的彻底研究，确保了演示的成功。


近义词：preparation, groundwork, groundwork, planning, strategizing | 反义词：postcommunication (not a standard word)
