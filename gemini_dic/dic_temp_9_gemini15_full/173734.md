/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈnæʃənəli/| adj.|准国家；类似国家的；|The term "quasi-nationally" describes entities or organizations that exhibit many characteristics of a nation-state but lack full sovereignty or international recognition.  It implies a degree of self-governance, cultural unity, and perhaps even a defined territory, but without the complete legal and political independence of a recognized nation.  This can apply to various groups, from indigenous populations with significant autonomy to regions with strong separatist movements. |The Basque region, with its own language and culture, operates in a quasi-nationally manner within Spain.|近义词：semi-national, near-national, self-governing|反义词：international, fully independent
