look-on:/ˈlʊkɒn/| v.|旁观；观看；看待|To watch something without taking part.  It often implies passive observation.|例句：He just stood there looking on as the fight progressed. 他只是站在那里看着打斗进行。
例句：She looked on with a mixture of amusement and horror. 她既觉得好笑又觉得可怕地看着。|近义词：observe, watch, witness|反义词：participate, intervene


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They look on with interest. = 他们饶有兴致地旁观。
We look on while they argue. = 我们看着他们争吵。


一般过去时:
She looked on silently. = 她默默地旁观。
The crowd looked on in amazement. = 人群惊奇地旁观。


现在完成时:
I've looked on many times. = 我已经旁观过很多次了。
They have looked on passively. = 他们一直被动地旁观。


过去完成时:
He had looked on before speaking. = 他在发言前先旁观了一会。
She had looked on with disapproval. = 她一直不赞成地旁观。


将来时:
They will look on from afar. = 他们将从远处旁观。
We will look on and see what happens. = 我们将旁观并看看会发生什么。


虚拟语气:
If I were there, I would look on. = 如果我在那里，我会旁观。
I wish I hadn't looked on so passively. = 我真希望我不要那么被动地旁观。


被动语态:
The event was looked on with skepticism. = 这件事被人们怀疑地看待。
The situation is looked on as hopeless. = 这种情况被认为是绝望的。


疑问句:
Did they look on during the incident? = 他们在事件发生期间有旁观吗？
How did she look on the matter? = 她对这件事怎么看？


否定句:
He didn't look on; he intervened. = 他没有旁观；他进行了干预。
They didn't look on approvingly. = 他们没有赞许地旁观。
