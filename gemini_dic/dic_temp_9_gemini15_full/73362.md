foregleam:/ˈfɔːrɡliːm/| n.|预兆，先兆|A faint indication or sign of something that is going to happen, especially something momentous or significant.  It often implies a glimpse or foreshadowing of something future and potentially important.|例句：The distant rumble was a foregleam of the approaching storm.  遥远的隆隆声是即将到来的暴风雨的预兆。

The subtle shift in public opinion was a foregleam of the coming political upheaval.  公众舆论的微妙转变预示着即将到来的政治动荡。|近义词：premonition, foreshadowing, portent, omen|反义词：aftermath, consequence, result
