kibosh:/ˈkaɪbɒʃ/| n.|彻底的结束；阻止；破坏|The sudden storm put the kibosh on our picnic.  突如其来的暴风雨彻底破坏了我们的野餐。|近义词：end, halt, stop, finish|反义词：beginning, start, commencement|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The kibosh puts a stop to the project. = 彻底的结束阻止了这个项目。
This new rule puts the kibosh on their plans. = 这条新规彻底破坏了他们的计划。


一般过去时:
The rain put the kibosh on the outdoor concert. =  雨水彻底打乱了户外音乐会。
The sudden announcement put the kibosh on all speculation. = 突然的宣布结束了所有的猜测。


现在完成时:
The unexpected delay has put the kibosh on our schedule. =  意外的延误打乱了我们的日程安排。
The government's decision has put the kibosh on the deal. = 政府的决定彻底结束了这笔交易。


过去完成时:

将来时:
Bad weather will put the kibosh on the sporting event. = 恶劣的天气将彻底取消体育赛事。
Lack of resources will put the kibosh on the research project. = 资源匮乏将彻底终止研究项目。


虚拟语气:

If the new regulations were implemented, they would put the kibosh on our business. = 如果实施新的规章制度，那将彻底摧毁我们的业务。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
