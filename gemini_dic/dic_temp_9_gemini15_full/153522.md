pentachloride:/ˌpɛntəˈklɔːraɪd/| n. |五氯化物|A chemical compound containing five chlorine atoms.  Pentachlorides are typically formed by elements with a high oxidation state. They often exhibit strong oxidizing properties and are frequently reactive. |Example: Phosphorus pentachloride (PCl5) is a common example of a pentachloride used in organic chemistry.  五氯化磷（PCl5）是常用的一种五氯化物，用于有机化学。|近义词:  (depends on the specific pentachloride)|反义词: (No direct antonym exists; depends on the context)|名词
