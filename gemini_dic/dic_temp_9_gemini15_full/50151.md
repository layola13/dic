denunciative: /dɪˈnʌnsɪeɪtɪv/ | adj. | 谴责的；告发的；揭露的 |  Characterized by denunciation; expressing condemnation or accusation.  It implies a strong and often public expression of disapproval or criticism. | The denunciative tone of the article left no doubt about the author's opinion. 这篇文章的谴责语气毫无疑问地表达了作者的观点。|近义词：condemnatory, accusatory, censorious | 反义词：approving, laudatory, complimentary


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "What" in the example.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  Instead, here are examples illustrating how to use "denunciative" in different sentence structures:


肯定句:
The speech was highly denunciative of the government's policies.  这篇演讲强烈谴责政府的政策。

否定句:
Her response was not denunciative, but rather conciliatory.  她的回应并非谴责，而是调解。


疑问句:
Was the letter denunciative in its tone? 这封信的语气是否具有谴责性？


比较级和最高级:
The more denunciative the comments became, the more heated the debate grew.  评论越具谴责性，辩论就越激烈。  (This shows comparative use.  Superlative is less common with this word).


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "denunciative."  However, here are some phrases that could be used in conjunction with it:


a strongly denunciative statement 一个强烈的谴责声明
a highly denunciative tone  一种高度谴责的语气
a publicly denunciative act 一种公开谴责的行为
utterly denunciative remarks 完全谴责的言论
a carefully worded but denunciative article 一篇措辞谨慎但谴责意味的文章


词源:
From "denounce" + "-ative,"  "denounce" ultimately derives from Old French "denoncier" and Latin "denuntiare," meaning "to announce publicly," "to proclaim,"  "to inform against."  The "-ative" suffix forms an adjective.
