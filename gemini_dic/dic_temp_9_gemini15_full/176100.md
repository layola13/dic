rayah:/ˈraɪjɑː/| n.|拉亚人；受统治的臣民|The word "rayah" (رعية) refers to the non-Muslim subjects of the Ottoman Empire.  It is derived from the Arabic word for "flock" or "subjects."  The term often carried connotations of second-class citizenship and was used to distinguish the non-Muslim population from the ruling Muslim elite.|例句：The Ottoman Empire governed millions of rayah across its vast territories. 奥斯曼帝国统治着其广大领土上的数百万拉亚人。|近义词：subject, vassal, commoner|反义词：ruler, sovereign, elite|名词
