desperate:/ˈdɛspərɪt/| adj.|绝望的；孤注一掷的；非常需要的|extremely wanting or needing something; showing this feeling;  characterized by despair and a lack of hope.  The word implies a sense of urgency and a willingness to take drastic measures.|例句：He was desperate for a job. 他非常需要一份工作。

例句：She made a desperate attempt to save her child. 她不顾一切地试图救她的孩子。


近义词：hopeless, frantic, urgent, dire

反义词：hopeful, calm, relaxed, easy


词源:  From Old French *desesperer  (to lose hope), from Latin *de- (away from) + *sperare (to hope).


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is desperate for money. = 他急需钱。
They are desperate to find a solution. = 他们急于找到解决方案。

一般过去时:
She was desperate after losing her job. = 她失业后感到绝望。
He was desperate to escape. = 他拼命想逃脱。

现在完成时:
I have been desperate for a break. = 我一直渴望休息一下。
They have become desperate in their search. = 他们在搜寻中变得绝望了。

过去完成时:
He had been desperate for weeks before he finally gave up. = 他在最终放弃之前已经绝望了几周。
She had been desperate to contact him, but she couldn't. = 她一直渴望联系他，但她联系不上。

将来时:
I will be desperate if I fail the exam. = 如果考试不及格，我会绝望的。
They will be desperate if their plan fails. = 如果他们的计划失败，他们会绝望的。

虚拟语气:
If I were desperate, I would do anything. = 如果我绝望了，我会不顾一切。
I wish I weren't so desperate. = 我希望我不那么绝望。

被动语态:
The situation was felt to be desperate. = 人们觉得情况危急。
His need for help is described as desperate. = 他对帮助的需求被描述为绝望的。

疑问句:
Is he desperate for a solution? = 他急于寻找解决方案吗？
Are they desperate enough to take risks? = 他们绝望到愿意冒险吗？

否定句:
He isn't desperate yet. = 他还没绝望。
She wasn't desperate to get married. = 她并不急于结婚。
