laughing:/ˈlæfɪŋ/| v. adj.|笑，发笑；可笑的|The act of making sounds and movements with your mouth and face to express happiness or amusement.  It can also be used as an adjective to describe something that is funny or amusing.|例句：The children were laughing loudly. 孩子们大声笑着。|例句：That's a laughing matter. 那是件可笑的事。|近义词：chuckle, giggling, smiling|反义词：crying, weeping


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is laughing at the joke. = 他正在嘲笑这个笑话。
They laugh every day. = 他们每天都笑。

一般过去时:
She laughed heartily. = 她开怀大笑。
We laughed until our sides hurt. = 我们笑得肚子疼。


现在完成时:
I have laughed so hard today. = 我今天笑得很开心。
They have been laughing for hours. = 他们已经笑了好几个小时了。

过去完成时:
He had laughed before he saw the punchline. = 他在看到笑话的结尾之前就已经笑了。
She had been laughing for a while before I noticed her. = 我注意到她之前，她已经笑了一会儿了。

将来时:
We will laugh together again soon. = 我们很快会再次一起开怀大笑。
They will be laughing at this for years to come. = 他们以后几年都会嘲笑这件事。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't laugh at him. = 如果我是你，我就不会嘲笑他。
I wish I had laughed more when I was younger. = 我希望我年轻的时候能多笑一笑。

被动语态:
His jokes were laughed at by everyone. = 他的笑话被每个人都嘲笑了。(被动语态较少用于此词)
The comedian's performance was laughed at by the audience. = 喜剧演员的表演被观众嘲笑了。(被动语态较少用于此词)

疑问句:
Are you laughing at me? = 你在嘲笑我吗？
Why are they laughing? = 他们为什么在笑？

否定句:
He wasn't laughing at all. = 他根本没有笑。
I don't laugh at cruel jokes. = 我不会嘲笑残酷的笑话。


PHRASE->
burst into laughter = 突然大笑起来
