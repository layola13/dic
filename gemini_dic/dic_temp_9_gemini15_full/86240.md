heatstroke:/ˈhiːtstroʊk/| n. |中暑|A serious condition caused by overheating of the body, typically resulting from prolonged exposure to high temperatures.  Symptoms include high body temperature, headache, dizziness, nausea, and confusion.  In severe cases, heatstroke can lead to organ damage and death.|The athlete collapsed on the field, suffering from heatstroke.|sunstroke, hyperthermia|hypothermia|名词
