johnny-come-lately:/ˈdʒɑːni kʌm ˈleɪtli/ | n. |新来者，后起之秀，暴发户 | A newcomer who has suddenly become successful or prominent, often implying a lack of experience or genuine merit.  It often carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting arrogance or a lack of respect for those who have been around longer. |Example:  He's a johnny-come-lately in the business world, making bold claims despite his limited experience. 他是商界的新来者，尽管经验有限，却夸夸其谈。


Example:  Don't listen to that johnny-come-lately; he doesn't know what he's talking about. 不要听那个新来者的话；他不知道自己在说什么。|近义词：newcomer, upstart, parvenu |反义词：veteran, old-timer, established figure |名词
