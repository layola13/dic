levier:/ləˈviːeɪ/| n.|杠杆；手段；影响力|A lever is a simple machine consisting of a rigid bar that pivots around a fixed point (fulcrum) and is used to lift or move a load.  It can also refer to something used to achieve a particular result.|例句：He used his influence as a lever to get the project approved. 他利用自己的影响力作为杠杆促使项目获得批准。|近义词：influence, tool, means|反义词：obstacle, impediment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The lever lifts the heavy weight. = 杠杆举起了重物。
This lever provides significant mechanical advantage. = 这个杠杆提供了显著的机械优势。


一般过去时:
She used a lever to pry open the door. = 她用撬棍撬开了门。
The manager leveraged his contacts to secure the deal. = 经理利用他的人脉关系确保了这笔交易。


现在完成时:
They have leveraged their resources effectively. = 他们有效地利用了他们的资源。
We have used this lever to overcome many challenges. = 我们已经利用这个杠杆克服了许多挑战。


过去完成时:
He had already used the lever before I arrived. = 我到达之前他已经用过杠杆了。
The company had leveraged its brand to great success. = 公司已经成功地利用了其品牌。

词源:
From Old French *levier, from lever "to raise," from Vulgar Latin *levare, from Latin levare "to lift, raise."  The modern sense of "means of influence" developed later.
