malapropism:/ˈmæləˌprɒpɪzəm/ | n. | 字语错用；词语误用 | The humorous misuse of a word in place of a similar-sounding one, often with unintentionally amusing effect.  It's named after Mrs. Malaprop, a character in Sheridan's play "The Rivals" known for this habit. |  He committed a malapropism when he said "dance attendance" instead of "pay attention." 他说“dance attendance”（侍奉）而不是“pay attention”（注意），犯了一个词语误用的错误。  She made a malapropism when she said "for all intensive purposes" instead of "for all intents and purposes". 她把“for all intensive purposes”（为了所有密集的目的）说成“for all intents and purposes”（就所有意图和目的而言），犯了一个词语误用的错误。| 近义词：misuse, misapplication, blunder, solecisms | 反义词：accuracy, precision
