heteropodous:/ˌhɛtərˈɒpədəs/| adj.| 异足的；有不同形状足的 | Having feet of different shapes or sizes.  This term is primarily used in zoology, describing animals with dissimilar legs or feet. For example, many insects exhibit heteropodous legs, where the forelegs might be modified for grasping, the midlegs for walking, and the hindlegs for jumping. | Example: The insect displayed heteropodous legs, adapted for various functions.  The grasshopper's heteropodous legs allow for powerful jumping. | 近义词: dissimilar-footed | 反义词: homopodous
