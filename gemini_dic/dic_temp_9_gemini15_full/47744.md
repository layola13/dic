decalcomania:/ˌdɛkælˈkoʊmeɪniə/| n. | décalcomanie；转印画；拓印术 | A process of transferring a design from a specially prepared surface to another, often using pressure and a transfer medium;  also refers to the resulting artwork.  It often involves the use of paints, inks, or other materials applied to a surface which is then pressed onto another, creating a unique and often abstract image.  The term can also refer to the resulting image itself. | The artist created a beautiful decalcomania using vibrant inks and a textured surface.  艺术家用鲜艳的墨水和有纹理的表面创作了一幅美丽的转印画。 |  rubbing, impression, transfer |  (no direct antonym, but opposite concept could be original painting) | 名词
