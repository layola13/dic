lynching:/ˈlɪntʃɪŋ/ | n. |私刑，私法处决 | The act of killing someone, especially by hanging, by an informal group of people, without a trial. This is usually motivated by racism or other forms of prejudice.  | Example: The lynching of Black Americans in the Southern United States during the Jim Crow era is a dark chapter in American history.  私刑处决美国南部在种族隔离时期对非洲裔美国人的私刑是美国历史上黑暗的一章。| 近义词：murder, execution, extrajudicial killing | 反义词：due process, trial, justice |
