hit the nail on the head: /hɪt ðə neɪl ɒn ðə hed/ | phrasal verb | 正中要害；说到点子上；一针见血 |  This idiom means to describe something perfectly or to do something exactly right. It originates from the action of hammering a nail: if you hit the nail squarely on the head, you drive it straight in efficiently.  | 例句：His analysis of the situation hit the nail on the head.  他的情况分析一针见血。

His description of the problem hit the nail on the head; I couldn't have said it better myself. 他对问题的描述正中要害；我再怎么说也不如他说的好。


近义词：be spot on, be exactly right, be perfectly accurate,  get it right,  be right to the point

反义词：miss the mark, be wide of the mark, be inaccurate,  be completely wrong, be off the mark
