lectern:/ˈlɛktərn/| n. |讲台；读经台| A lectern is a stand, typically with a slanted top, used to hold a book or notes, especially when giving a speech or reading aloud.  It is often used in churches, schools, and other public speaking venues. |Example: The priest stood at the lectern to deliver his sermon.  牧师站在讲台上讲道。

The professor placed her notes on the lectern before beginning her lecture. 教授在开始讲座之前把笔记放在讲台上。|近义词：podium, pulpit |反义词：None.
