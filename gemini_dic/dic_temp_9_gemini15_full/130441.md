nonabsoluteness:/ˌnɒnˈæbsəluːtnəs/ | n. |非绝对性，相对性 | The state or quality of not being absolute; relativity.  It refers to the lack of absolute truth or certainty, often in philosophical or ethical contexts.  The concept suggests that truth or values are dependent on context, perspective, or interpretation. | Example: The nonabsoluteness of moral principles was a key theme in his philosophical work.  他的哲学著作中，道德原则的非绝对性是一个关键主题。| 近义词：relativity, contingency, conditionality | 反义词：absoluteness, certainty, universality
