jacobinism:/ˈdʒækəbɪnɪzəm/| n. |雅各宾主义|Extreme revolutionary political doctrines and actions, especially those associated with the French Revolution.  The term derives from the Jacobin Club, a powerful political club during the French Revolution.  Jacobinism is characterized by its radical, often violent, pursuit of egalitarianism and social reform.  It often involves the suppression of opposition and the establishment of a highly centralized government.|The Reign of Terror was a period of intense Jacobinism in France. | 近义词：radicalism, extremism, revolutionism|反义词：conservatism, reactionism, moderation|名词
