correlate:/ˈkɔːrəleɪt/|/ˈkɔːrəleɪt/| v. |关联；相互关联；与…相关 |To have a mutual relationship or connection, often implying a statistical relationship.  |The study correlated smoking with lung cancer. 这项研究将吸烟与肺癌联系起来。| relate, connect, associate |disassociate, disconnect|


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The researchers correlate the data to find a pattern. = 研究人员关联数据以寻找模式。
Stress levels correlate with heart disease. = 压力水平与心脏病相关。


一般过去时:
The scientists correlated the two sets of results. = 科学家关联了两组结果。
The study correlated higher education levels with increased income. = 这项研究将较高的教育水平与更高的收入联系起来。


现在完成时:
Studies have correlated lack of sleep with impaired cognitive function. = 研究表明睡眠不足与认知功能障碍相关。
They have correlated the increase in temperature with the melting of glaciers. = 他们已经将温度升高与冰川融化联系起来。


过去完成时:
The analysis had correlated the economic downturn with rising unemployment. = 分析已经将经济衰退与失业率上升联系起来。
Researchers had correlated the data before drawing any conclusions. = 研究人员在得出任何结论之前已经关联了数据。


将来时:
Future studies will correlate genetic factors with susceptibility to disease. = 未来的研究将把遗传因素与疾病易感性联系起来。
Scientists will correlate the data from different sources. = 科学家将关联来自不同来源的数据。


虚拟语气:
If the two variables correlated, it would support the hypothesis. = 如果这两个变量相关，那将支持该假设。
I wish the data correlated more strongly. = 我希望数据关联性更强。


被动语态:
The data were correlated using advanced statistical methods. = 使用先进的统计方法关联了数据。
The results are being correlated with previous findings. = 结果正在与之前的发现进行关联。


疑问句:
Do these two factors correlate? = 这两个因素相关吗？
How do these variables correlate? = 这些变量如何关联？


否定句:
The study did not correlate the two events. = 这项研究没有将这两个事件联系起来。
There is no correlation between the two variables. = 这两个变量之间没有相关性。



PHRASE->
