preaccepted:/ˌpriːəkˈsɛptɪd/| adj. |预先接受的，预先同意的|Already accepted beforehand; accepted in advance.  This adjective describes something that has been accepted before a formal or official process. It implies a prior agreement or understanding.|例句：The preaccepted terms of the contract were favorable to both parties.  合同中预先接受的条款对双方都有利。

The proposal was preaccepted by the committee before the formal presentation.  提案在正式提交之前就被委员会预先接受了。|近义词：preapproved, beforehand accepted|反义词：unaccepted, rejected

词源：
The word "preaccepted" is formed by combining the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before" or "in advance") with the past participle "accepted" (from the verb "accept").  It's a relatively modern formation, reflecting the increasing use of pre-approval and pre-agreement processes in various contexts.
