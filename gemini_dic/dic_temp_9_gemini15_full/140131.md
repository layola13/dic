off-the-ground:/ˌɔːf ðə ɡraʊnd/| adj.|非地面的；崭新的；起步的|  Literally meaning "not on the ground," this adjective is used figuratively to describe something that is new, innovative, or just beginning.  It suggests a departure from the established or traditional. |例句：His new business is finally off the ground. 他的新生意终于起步了。
The airplane took off, off the ground.飞机起飞了，离开了地面。|近义词：new, innovative, nascent, fledgling|反义词：established, traditional, mature


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project is off the ground. = 项目已经启动。
This new idea is off the ground. = 这个新想法已经开始实施。


一般过去时:
The company went off the ground last year. = 这家公司去年开始运营。
The rocket lifted off the ground. = 火箭离开了地面。


现在完成时:
The initiative has been off the ground for six months. = 这个倡议已经启动六个月了。
The play has finally gone off the ground. = 这部戏终于上演了。


过去完成时:
The research had been off the ground for years before they saw results. = 在他们看到结果之前，这项研究已经进行了多年。
The project had gone off the ground before the funding was secured. = 在资金到位之前，项目就已经启动了。


将来时:
The startup will be off the ground by next year. = 这家初创公司将在明年启动。
The building will be off the ground soon. = 这座建筑很快就会破土动工。


虚拟语气:
If the funding were secured, the project would be off the ground. = 如果资金到位，项目就能启动。
I wish my novel was off the ground already. = 我希望我的小说已经开始创作了。


被动语态:
The plan was finally gotten off the ground. = 计划终于启动了。
The project is being gotten off the ground. = 项目正在启动中。


疑问句:
Is the project off the ground yet? = 项目启动了吗？
Is the new product line off the ground? = 新产品线启动了吗？


否定句:
The plan is not yet off the ground. = 计划尚未启动。
The business hasn't gotten off the ground yet. = 这项业务还没有起步。
