cadacity: /kəˈdjuːsəti/ | n. |早衰；易落性；短暂性| The quality or state of being caducous; the tendency to fall off or shed prematurely.  It often refers to plant parts, such as leaves or petals, that fall off earlier than usual.  The term can also be used more broadly to describe anything that is short-lived or ephemeral.|例句：The caducity of the autumn leaves was striking. 秋叶的早落令人注目。


The caducity of the agreement meant it needed renegotiation.  协议的短暂性意味着它需要重新谈判。|近义词：prematurity, ephemerality, transience, brevity|反义词：permanence, durability, longevity
