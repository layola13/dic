remortgage: /ˌriːˈmɔːrɡɪdʒ/ | vt. | 再次抵押；转按揭 | To obtain a new mortgage on a property that is already mortgaged, typically to obtain a lower interest rate or release equity.  | 例句：They remortgaged their house to consolidate their debts. 他们再次抵押他们的房子来整合他们的债务。

They remortgaged their home to pay for their daughter's education. 他们再次抵押他们的房子来支付女儿的教育费用。


近义词： refinance (to obtain new financing for something) | 反义词：  None specifically, but the opposite action would be paying off the mortgage. | 词性: Verb (动词)

词源:  The word is a combination of "re-" (again) and "mortgage" (a legal agreement by which a bank lends money at interest in exchange for taking title of the debtor's property, with the condition that the conveyance of title becomes void upon the payment of the debt).
