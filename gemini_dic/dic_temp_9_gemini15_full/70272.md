fiddlers:/ˈfɪdəlz/ | n. | 演奏小提琴的人；小提琴手 | A fiddler is a person who plays the fiddle (violin).  The term often implies a less formal or skilled player than a violinist. | 例句：The fiddlers played lively jigs at the ceilidh.  小提琴手在凯利舞会上演奏了欢快的快步舞曲。

The street fiddler earned a few coins from passersby. 街头小提琴手从路人那里赚到了一些零钱。


近义词：violinist, musician | 反义词： (无明确反义词)


词源:  The word "fiddler" comes from the Middle English word "fidlere," which in turn derives from the Old French "fidèle," meaning "fiddle player."  The word "fiddle" itself has a complex etymology, ultimately related to words meaning "to play" or "to rub."
