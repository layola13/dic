menace: /ˈmænɪs/ | n. v. | 威胁；危险；令人讨厌的人或事物 | a person or thing that is likely to cause harm; a threat or danger. |  例句：The storm was a menace to shipping.  暴风雨对航运构成威胁。  |近义词：threat, danger, peril, hazard | 反义词：safety, security, protection


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dog menaces the cat. = 这只狗威胁着那只猫。
That loud noise menaces my concentration. = 那巨大的噪音威胁着我的专注力。

一般过去时:
The bully menaced the smaller children. = 那个恶霸威胁着那些更小的孩子。
The dark clouds menaced rain. = 乌云预示着要下雨。

现在完成时:
The criminal has menaced the community for years. = 这个罪犯多年来一直威胁着这个社区。
His actions have menaced the company's reputation. = 他的行为威胁到了公司的声誉。

过去完成时:
He had menaced her with a knife before. = 他以前曾用刀威胁过她。
The storm had menaced the coastal towns for days. = 暴风雨几天来一直威胁着沿海城镇。


将来时:
The approaching hurricane will menace the coastline. = 逼近的飓风将威胁海岸线。
They believe the new policy will menace small businesses. = 他们认为这项新政策将会威胁到小型企业。


虚拟语气:
If he menaced me again, I would call the police. = 如果他再次威胁我，我会报警。
I wish he hadn't menaced the witness. = 我真希望他没有威胁证人。


被动语态:
The town was menaced by the rising floodwaters. = 城镇受到上涨的洪水的威胁。
The residents felt menaced by the recent increase in crime. = 居民们感到最近犯罪的增加对他们构成了威胁。


疑问句:
Does the situation menace our safety? = 这种情况会威胁到我们的安全吗？
What menaces the environment the most? = 什么对环境的威胁最大？


否定句:
The situation doesn't menace us. = 这种情况不会威胁到我们。
The police assured us that we weren't menaced. = 警察向我们保证我们没有受到威胁。


PHRASE->

a potential menace  潜在的威胁

feel menaced 感到受到威胁
