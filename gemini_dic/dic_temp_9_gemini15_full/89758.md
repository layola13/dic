hopscotching:/ˈhɑːpskɑːtʃɪŋ/ | v. |跳房子游戏；跳跃前进 |To move quickly and lightly from one place or thing to another.  It often implies a somewhat haphazard or irregular progression.|例句：He hopscotched across the room to answer the phone. 他飞快地穿过房间去接电话。

例句：The company is hopscotching its way across the market, acquiring smaller firms one after another. 这家公司正在跳跃式地进军市场，一家接一家地收购小型公司。


近义词：skipping, leaping, jumping,  bounding


反义词：crawling, dragging, creeping, inching


词性：动词

词源:
The word "hopscotch" combines "hop," meaning to jump on one foot, and "scotch," an older word related to "score" or "mark," referring to the markings on the ground for the game.  The word's origin reflects the game's action of hopping over marked spaces.
