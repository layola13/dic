job-hunted: /ˈdʒɑːb ˌhʌntɪd/ | adj. | 求职的；积极寻找工作的 |  actively seeking employment; describing someone engaged in a job search.  | Example:  He spent months job-hunted before landing his dream role. 他花了几个月的时间求职，才找到理想的工作。  | 近义词: job-seeking, employed | 反义词: unemployed, retired


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is job-hunting. = 他正在求职。
She job-hunts every day. = 她每天都在求职。


一般过去时:
He job-hunted for six months. = 他求职了六个月。
She job-hunted aggressively last year. = 她去年积极地求职。


现在完成时:
He has job-hunted since graduating. = 他从毕业以来一直在求职。
She has job-hunted in several cities. = 她已经在几个城市求职了。


过去完成时:
He had job-hunted for a year before he found a position. = 他求职了一年后才找到工作。
She had job-hunted extensively before giving up. = 在放弃之前，她已经广泛地求职了。


将来时:
He will job-hunt in the new year. = 他将在新年求职。
She will job-hunt more strategically this time. = 她这次将更策略性地求职。


虚拟语气:
If I were job-hunting, I would update my resume. = 如果我在求职，我会更新我的简历。
I wish I hadn't waited so long to start job-hunting. = 我真希望我没有等那么久才开始求职。


被动语态:  (The adjective "job-hunted" is not typically used in passive voice.  The verb "job-hunt" would be used instead.)
The positions were aggressively job-hunted.  = 这些职位受到了积极的求职竞争。
Many candidates were contacted for job interviews. = 很多候选人被联系进行工作面试。


疑问句:
Is he job-hunting actively? = 他正在积极求职吗？
Has she been job-hunted successfully? = 她求职成功了吗？


否定句:
He isn't job-hunting at the moment. = 他现在没有在求职。
She wasn't job-hunting when I met her. = 我遇到她的时候，她没有在求职。
