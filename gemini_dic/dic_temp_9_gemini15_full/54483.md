disinterests:/ˌdɪsˈɪntrɪst/| v.|使不感兴趣；使漠不关心|To make someone lose interest in something.|  He tried to disinterest her in the project, but she remained enthusiastic. 他试图让她对这个项目失去兴趣，但她仍然很热情。|bore, alienate|interest, engage|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He disinterests his students in the topic. = 他让学生对这个话题失去兴趣。
The boring lecture disinterests the audience. = 枯燥的演讲使听众失去兴趣。


一般过去时:
The news disinterested him in his work. = 这个消息让他对工作失去了兴趣。
The failure of the project disinterested the investors. = 项目的失败使投资者失去了兴趣。


现在完成时:

Repeated failures have disinterested him in his career. = 反复的失败使他对自己的事业失去了兴趣。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:

The exciting plot didn't disinterest the readers. = 激动人心的情节并没有让读者失去兴趣。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases using "disinterest" as a verb.  The word is primarily used in its literal sense.  However,  "lose interest in" is a close synonym and commonly used in phrases.
For example:
lose interest in the game = 对游戏失去兴趣
lose interest in studying = 对学习失去兴趣
lose interest in her = 对她失去兴趣
lose interest in life = 对生活失去兴趣
lose interest in work = 对工作失去兴趣
