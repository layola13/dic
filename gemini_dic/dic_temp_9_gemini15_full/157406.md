piquancy:/ˈpɪkənsi/| n.|辛辣；刺激；趣味性 | A quality that is pleasantly stimulating or exciting to the mind;  a pleasantly sharp or pungent taste.  It suggests a delightful sharpness or zest, often relating to both taste and intellectual stimulation. |例句：The piquancy of the curry was delightful.  咖喱的辛辣美味可口。

The piquancy of her wit kept the conversation lively. 她机智的谈吐使谈话生动活泼。|近义词：zest, spice, tang, pungency,  liveliness,  vivacity |反义词：blandness, dullness, insipidity

WORD ORIGIN->

From the French word "piquant," meaning "prickly," "pungent," or "sharp," ultimately from the past participle of the verb "piquer," meaning "to prick" or "to sting."  This reflects the sharp, stimulating quality associated with the word.
