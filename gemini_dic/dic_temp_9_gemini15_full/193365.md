segregations:/ˌseɡrɪˈɡeɪʃənz/ | n. | 种族隔离；隔离；分离 | The act or practice of separating people into different groups based on race, ethnicity, religion, or other characteristics.  This often involves denying opportunities or services to certain groups. | Example: The Civil Rights Movement fought against racial segregations in the United States.  美国民权运动反对美国的种族隔离。

The history of racial segregations in the South is well-documented. 南方种族隔离的历史有据可查。


近义词：separation, isolation, discrimination, apartheid
反义词：integration, inclusion, unification
