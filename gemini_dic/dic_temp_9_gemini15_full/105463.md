kill-the-fatted-calf: /kɪl ðə ˈfætɪd kælf/ | n. phrase |宰肥牛犊 |  A celebratory feast, typically one prepared for the return of a prodigal son or a long-absent loved one.  It originates from the biblical parable of the prodigal son, where the father orders the fattened calf to be killed to celebrate his son's return. The phrase now symbolizes a lavish celebration or welcome for someone's return or a significant event. | Example:  They killed the fatted calf when their daughter graduated from college.  他们女儿大学毕业时，他们大摆筵席庆祝。


近义词：lavish celebration, grand feast, joyous reception

反义词：modest celebration, somber occasion


词性: Noun Phrase (名词短语)
