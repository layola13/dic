litigates:/ˈlɪtɪɡeɪts/| v.|提起诉讼，打官司|To engage in a lawsuit or legal proceedings.  It implies a formal legal process.|The company litigates frequently to protect its intellectual property. 这家公司经常提起诉讼来保护其知识产权。|sue, prosecute, take to court|settle, resolve, conciliate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He litigates against his former employer. = 他正在起诉他的前雇主。
The law firm litigates many cases each year. = 这家律师事务所每年处理许多案件。


一般过去时:
She litigated the matter for over a year. = 她为此问题打了官司一年多。
They litigated the dispute in civil court. = 他们在民事法庭上解决了纠纷。


现在完成时:
They have litigated this issue multiple times. = 他们已经多次就这个问题提起诉讼。
The company has litigated successfully against several competitors. = 这家公司已经成功地对几家竞争对手提起诉讼。


过去完成时:
Before the trial, they had litigated the case in mediation. = 在审判之前，他们已通过调解解决了此案。
She had litigated several cases before becoming a judge. = 在成为法官之前，她已经处理过几起案件。


将来时:
We will litigate this case if necessary. = 如果需要，我们将对该案提起诉讼。
They will likely litigate this matter for years to come. = 他们很可能会在未来几年内就此事提起诉讼。


虚拟语气:
If they litigated responsibly, they would have a better chance of winning. = 如果他们负责任地提起诉讼，他们将有更大的胜算。
I wish they hadn't litigated this so aggressively. = 我真希望他们没有这么咄咄逼人地提起诉讼。


被动语态:
The case was litigated in the Supreme Court. = 此案在最高法院审理。
The matter is being litigated currently. = 此事目前正在审理中。


疑问句:
Are they litigating this case? = 他们正在为这个案子打官司吗？
Has the company ever litigated before? = 这家公司以前打过官司吗？


否定句:
They did not litigate the matter further. = 他们没有进一步提起诉讼。
He chose not to litigate the dispute. = 他选择不打这场官司。



PHRASE->
litigate a case = 诉讼一案
litigate a dispute = 解决纠纷
litigate in court = 在法庭上打官司
litigate aggressively = 激烈地打官司
litigate successfully = 成功地打赢官司
