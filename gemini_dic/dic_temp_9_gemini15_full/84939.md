hanukka:/ˈhɑːnəkə/| n. |光明节|The Jewish Festival of Lights, commemorating the rededication of the Second Temple in Jerusalem.  It is an eight-day festival that begins on the 25th day of Kislev according to the Hebrew calendar, usually falling in late November or December in the Gregorian calendar.  The festival involves the lighting of a special nine-branched candelabrum called a menorah, each night adding another candle.  It is a time of celebration, family gatherings, and the eating of traditional foods such as latkes (potato pancakes) and sufganiyot (jelly doughnuts).|Example: We celebrated Hanukkah with family and friends, lighting the menorah each night.  我们和家人朋友一起庆祝光明节，每晚点亮烛台。|近义词:Festival of Lights|反义词:None.
