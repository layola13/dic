countersign:/ˈkaʊntərsaɪn/|/ˈkaʊntɚsaɪn/| vt. n. | 副署；签署；密码 |To countersign something means to sign it in addition to another person's signature, usually to confirm or authorize it.  As a noun, it refers to a countersignature or a secret password. |例句：The document needs to be countersigned by the manager. 这份文件需要经理副署。  The soldiers exchanged countersigns to verify their identities. 士兵们交换密码以验证身份。 |近义词：cosign, endorse, authorize, password |反义词：unauthorize
