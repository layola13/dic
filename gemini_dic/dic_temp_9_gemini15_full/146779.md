overidentification: /ˌoʊvərˌaɪdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |过度认同；过度识别| The act of identifying too strongly with something or someone, often to the point of losing one's own identity or perspective.  It can refer to a psychological phenomenon, a statistical concept, or a social issue.  | 例句：His overidentification with the villain in the movie led him to sympathize with his actions. 他对电影中反派的过度认同导致他同情其行为。

His overidentification with his father's expectations prevented him from pursuing his own dreams. 他过度认同父亲的期望，以至于无法追求自己的梦想。


近义词：excessive identification, strong identification, empathy (in certain contexts), fusion | 反义词：objectivity, detachment, distance, disidentification


词源:  The word is a combination of "over-" (meaning excessively or too much) and "identification" (the act of identifying something or someone).  "Identification" itself comes from the Latin "identifficatio,"  from "identi-," meaning "same," and "-ficatio," a suffix indicating an action or process.
