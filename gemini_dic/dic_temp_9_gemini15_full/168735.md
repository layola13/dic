procure:/prəˈkjʊr/| vt.|获得，取得，设法弄到|to obtain or get something, especially with care or effort |例句：She managed to procure a ticket for the concert. 她设法弄到了一张音乐会的票。|例句：The company is trying to procure new equipment. 公司正试图购置新设备。|近义词：obtain, acquire, secure|反义词：lose, forfeit


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They procure supplies regularly. = 他们定期采购物资。
He procures rare books for his collection. = 他为自己的收藏寻找珍稀书籍。

一般过去时:
She procured a visa for her trip. = 她为她的旅行办理了签证。
We procured the necessary materials for the project. = 我们为项目采购了必要的材料。

现在完成时:
They have procured all the necessary funding. = 他们已经筹集到了所有必要的资金。
She has procured a rare painting for her gallery. = 她为她的画廊购得了一幅珍贵的画作。

过去完成时:
He had procured the tickets before the concert sold out. = 音乐会门票售罄前，他已经买好了票。
They had procured the necessary permits before starting construction. = 他们在开工前已经获得了必要的许可证。

将来时:
We will procure the equipment next week. = 我们下周将采购设备。
They will procure the necessary documents for the application. = 他们将为申请准备必要的材料。

虚拟语气:
If we could procure the funding, we could start the project. = 如果我们能筹集到资金，我们就能启动项目。
I wish I could procure a first edition of that book. = 我真希望我能买到那本书的初版。

被动语态:
The materials were procured by the company. = 材料由公司采购。
The necessary permits were procured before construction began. = 在开工前获得了必要的许可证。

疑问句:
How did you procure that antique? = 你是怎么弄到那件古董的？
Where can we procure the necessary components? = 我们在哪里可以采购必要的部件？

否定句:
He couldn't procure a ticket for the sold-out show. = 他没能买到那场售罄演出的票。
They failed to procure the necessary resources for the project. = 他们未能为项目采购必要的资源。


PHRASE->

procure a job = 找到工作
