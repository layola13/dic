mickeys:/ˈmɪkiːz/| n.|米老鼠，(非正式)少量现金|  The word "mickeys" refers primarily to Mickey Mouse, the famous cartoon character.  Informally, it can also mean a small amount of money, typically in the context of petty cash or a bribe. This informal usage likely derives from the association of Mickey Mouse with something small and insignificant.|例句：The kids were thrilled to see all the Mickey Mouse merchandise. 孩子们看到所有米老鼠商品都很兴奋。

The bartender slipped him a few mickeys under the table. 酒保在他桌子底下偷偷塞给他一些小钱。|近义词： (for the money sense)  a little bit, a few bucks, some change |反义词： (for the money sense)  a fortune, a large sum
