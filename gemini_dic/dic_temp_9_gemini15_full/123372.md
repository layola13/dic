monomanias:/ˌmɒnəˈmeɪniəz/| n. |偏执狂；狂热；单一观念 |A monomania is an excessive focus on a single idea or subject, often to the exclusion of all others.  It can manifest as an obsession, a delusion, or a strong, unwavering belief.  The term often implies a pathological or unhealthy preoccupation. |例句：His monomania about collecting stamps consumed all his time and money.  他的集邮狂热耗尽了他的时间和金钱。
His monomania for success led him to neglect his family and friends. 他对成功的偏执狂使他忽视了家人和朋友。|近义词：obsession, fixation, mania, delusion |反义词：balanced, well-rounded, rational

词源:
The word "monomania" is derived from the Greek words "monos" (μόνος), meaning "single" or "alone," and "mania" (μανία), meaning "madness" or "insanity."  Therefore, it literally translates to "single madness" or "obsession with one thing."
