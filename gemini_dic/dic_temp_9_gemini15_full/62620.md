enforce:/ɪnˈfɔːrs/ | vt. | 强制执行；实施；强迫 | To make people obey a rule or law.  |例句：The government will enforce new environmental regulations. 政府将强制执行新的环保法规。|例句：The teacher enforced the school rules strictly. 老师严格执行校规。|近义词：implement, execute, impose |反义词：cancel, abolish, repeal |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The police enforce the law. = 警方执法。
They enforce a strict dress code. = 他们执行严格的着装规范。

一般过去时:
The court enforced the judgment. = 法院强制执行判决。
The school enforced a new policy. = 学校实施了一项新政策。

现在完成时:
The government has enforced stricter penalties. = 政府已经实施了更严厉的处罚。
They have enforced the rules consistently. = 他们一直严格执行规章制度。

过去完成时:
The company had enforced the deadline before the project started. = 项目开始前，公司已经强制执行了最后期限。
Before the accident, the factory had not enforced safety regulations. = 事故发生前，工厂没有执行安全规章。

将来时:
The authorities will enforce the new law next month. = 当局将在下个月实施新法律。
The company will enforce its new policy starting next year. = 公司将从明年开始执行其新政策。

虚拟语气:
If they enforced the rules more strictly, there would be fewer accidents. = 如果他们更严格地执行规章制度，事故就会减少。
I wish they would enforce the parking regulations more effectively. = 我希望他们能更有效地执行停车规定。

被动语态:
The rules were enforced strictly. = 规则被严格执行。
The new regulations will be enforced by the government. = 新规章将由政府强制执行。

疑问句:
How will the new regulations be enforced? = 新规章将如何执行？
Do they effectively enforce the laws in this country? = 他们在这个国家有效地执法吗？

否定句:
The school did not enforce the dress code. = 学校没有执行着装规范。
The government failed to enforce the environmental protection laws. = 政府未能执行环境保护法。


PHRASE->
enforce the law = 执法
enforce a rule = 执行规则
enforce a contract = 强制执行合同
enforce a judgment = 强制执行判决
enforce one's rights = 主张自己的权利

词源:
enforce 源自古法语enforcer，由en- (表示“in”, “on”, “upon”)和forcer (表示“to force”)组成，字面意思是“to force into”。  最终追溯到拉丁语的 *fortis (“strong”)。
