satirizer:/ˈsætəraɪzər/| n. |讽刺者，挖苦者 |A satirizer is a person who uses satire to expose or criticize folly, vice, or stupidity.  They often employ humor, irony, exaggeration, or ridicule to achieve their purpose. |Example:  The satirizer Jonathan Swift used his wit to critique the political and social issues of his time.  讽刺作家乔纳森·斯威夫特用他的才智批判了他那个时代的政治和社会问题。|近义词：critic, caricaturist, lampooner |反义词：praiser, flatterer |名词
