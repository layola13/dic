obligate:/ˈɑːblɪɡeɪt/| vt.|使负有义务；强制；使承担责任|To make someone legally or morally bound to do something.|The contract obligates the company to provide health insurance. 合同规定公司必须提供医疗保险。|近义词：bind, compel, require|反义词：free, release


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The contract obligates the company to pay. = 合同要求公司付款。
This law obligates citizens to vote. = 这项法律要求公民投票。


一般过去时:
The judge obligated the defendant to pay damages. = 法官判决被告赔偿损失。
The agreement obligated both parties to keep the information confidential. = 协议规定双方都必须保守秘密。


现在完成时:
The government has obligated itself to reduce emissions. = 政府已承诺减少排放。
She has obligated herself to help with the project. = 她已承诺协助该项目。


过去完成时:
He had obligated himself to a loan before the bankruptcy. = 破产前他已经欠了一笔贷款。
They had obligated themselves to finish the work by Friday. = 他们已经承诺在周五之前完成工作。


将来时:
The new law will obligate businesses to report their emissions. = 新法律将要求企业报告其排放量。
The court will obligate him to appear in court. = 法院将强制他出庭。


虚拟语气:
If the contract obligated them to pay, they would have done so. = 如果合同要求他们付款，他们就会付款。
I wish the agreement obligated them to provide more details. = 我希望协议要求他们提供更多细节。


被动语态:
The company is obligated to provide annual reports. = 公司有义务提供年度报告。
The citizens were obligated to pay taxes. = 公民有义务纳税。


疑问句:
Is the company obligated to pay overtime? = 公司有义务支付加班费吗？
Are we obligated to attend the meeting? = 我们有义务参加会议吗？


否定句:
The company is not obligated to disclose that information. = 公司没有义务披露该信息。
I am not obligated to help him. = 我没有义务帮助他。


PHRASE->
be obligated to = 有义务…
feel obligated = 感到有义务
legally obligated = 法律上强制
morally obligated = 道义上强制
mutually obligated = 互相有义务
