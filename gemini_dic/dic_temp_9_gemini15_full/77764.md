genealogists:/ˌdʒiːniˈælədʒɪsts/| n.| 系谱学家；家谱研究者 |  A genealogist is a person who studies family history and ancestry. They trace lineages back through generations, often using documents such as birth certificates, marriage licenses, and census records. | The genealogist meticulously researched the family tree, tracing it back several centuries.  系谱学家一丝不苟地研究家谱，追溯到几个世纪以前。 | historian, ancestry researcher |  (无明确反义词, 可以用non-historian等表示对立概念) | 名词
