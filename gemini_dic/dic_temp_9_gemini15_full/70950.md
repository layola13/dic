fireflaire:/ˈfaɪərˌfleər/| n. |火炬, 火光, 闪光|A fireflaire is a bright, brief burst of light, often associated with a fire or similar intense heat source.  It can refer to a literal flare, or a metaphorical burst of energy or excitement.|例句：The fireflaire from the burning building illuminated the night sky.  燃烧的建筑物发出的火光照亮了夜空。The announcement of the award caused a fireflaire of excitement in the room.  获奖公告在房间里引起一阵兴奋的火花。|近义词：flare, blaze, flash|反义词：darkness, dullness|名词
