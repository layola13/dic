mosses:/ˈmɒsɪz/| n. |苔藓植物；苔藓|Small, flowerless plants that typically grow in dense green clumps or mats, especially in damp or shady locations. They lack true roots, stems, and leaves, but have rhizoids for anchorage and simple structures for photosynthesis. Mosses play a vital role in ecosystems, contributing to soil formation and water retention. |Example: The old stone walls were covered in a thick carpet of mosses.  古老的石墙上覆盖着一层厚厚的苔藓。

The mosses grew luxuriantly in the damp forest.  苔藓在潮湿的森林里茂盛地生长。|近义词：liverworts, lichens|反义词：flowering plants
