qed:/kwɛd/|abbr.|  完毕，证明完毕|  Abbreviation for "quod erat demonstrandum," a Latin phrase meaning "that which was to be demonstrated" or "which was to be proved." It is used at the end of a mathematical proof or logical argument to signify that the proposition has been proven. |例句：The theorem is proved. QED.  定理已被证明。完毕。
|近义词：proven, demonstrated|反义词：unproven,undemonstrated|abbr.
