pre-Hebrews:/ˌpriːˈhiːbruːz/| n. |前希伯来人，希伯来人之前的居民|The term "Pre-Hebrews" refers to the populations inhabiting the Levant before the arrival and establishment of the Hebrews.  The identity and cultural characteristics of these groups are debated and remain largely unknown. Archaeological findings suggest a complex and diverse cultural landscape.  |Example:  The archeological dig unearthed artifacts suggesting a complex society among the Pre-Hebrews of the region. 前希伯来人地区的考古发掘出土的文物表明，该地区存在一个复杂的社会。|近义词:Canaanites, Amorites (depending on the specific context and time period)|反义词:Hebrews|名词
