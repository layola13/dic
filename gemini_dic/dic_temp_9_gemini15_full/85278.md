harmonised:/ˈhɑːmənaɪzd/ | v. | 使协调；使和谐；统一 | To bring into harmony or agreement; to standardize.  | The committee worked to harmonise the different viewpoints. 委员会努力协调不同的观点。 |  coordinate, unify, reconcile | discord, conflict | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The EU harmonises regulations across member states. = 欧盟统一成员国的法规。
They harmonise their schedules to spend more time together. = 他们协调他们的日程安排以便有更多时间在一起。


一般过去时:
The company harmonised its branding strategy last year. = 公司去年统一了其品牌战略。
The two countries harmonised their trade policies. = 这两个国家统一了它们的贸易政策。


现在完成时:
The government has harmonised tax laws nationwide. = 政府已经统一了全国的税法。
They have harmonised their processes to improve efficiency. = 他们已经协调了他们的流程以提高效率。


过去完成时:
Before the merger, the companies had harmonised their accounting systems. = 在合并之前，这些公司已经统一了它们的会计系统。
The team had harmonised their approach before presenting the proposal. = 在提交提案之前，团队已经协调了他们的方法。


将来时:
The organization will harmonise its operations next year. = 该组织明年将统一其运营。
The two departments will harmonise their procedures to avoid duplication. = 这两个部门将协调他们的程序以避免重复。


虚拟语气:
If they harmonised their efforts, they would achieve more. = 如果他们协调他们的努力，他们将取得更多成就。
It would be helpful if all departments harmonised their reporting methods. = 如果所有部门都统一他们的报告方法，那将很有帮助。


被动语态:
The regulations were harmonised by the international body. = 法规由国际机构统一。
The procedures have been harmonised to improve workflow. = 程序已被协调以改进工作流程。


疑问句:
Have the standards been harmonised yet? = 标准已经统一了吗？
How can we harmonise these conflicting interests? = 我们如何协调这些冲突的利益？


否定句:
The two systems were not fully harmonised. = 这两个系统没有完全统一。
They failed to harmonise their schedules. = 他们未能协调他们的日程安排。
