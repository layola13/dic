exaggerating:/ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtɪŋ/| v. |夸张，夸大|To represent (something) as being larger, better, or worse than it really is.|He was exaggerating the dangers of the situation. 他夸大了局势的危险性。|overstate, amplify, inflate|downplay, understate, minimize|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is exaggerating his accomplishments. = 他在夸大他的成就。
She exaggerates her problems to get attention. = 她夸大她的问题以引起注意。

一般过去时:
He exaggerated the size of the fish he caught. = 他夸大了他钓到的鱼的大小。
She exaggerated her illness to avoid work. = 她夸大她的病情以逃避工作。

现在完成时:
He has been exaggerating his abilities for years. = 他多年来一直在夸大自己的能力。
She has exaggerated the impact of the news. = 她夸大了这个新闻的影响。

过去完成时:
He had exaggerated his qualifications on his resume. = 他在简历上夸大了自己的资格。
She had exaggerated the difficulty of the task. = 她夸大了这项任务的难度。

将来时:
He will exaggerate his role in the project. = 他会夸大他在项目中的作用。
She will exaggerate the cost of the repairs. = 她会夸大修理的费用。

虚拟语气:
If he exaggerated the truth, he would be in trouble. = 如果他夸大了事实，他就会有麻烦。
If she exaggerated the situation, she would have created unnecessary stress. = 如果她夸大了情况，她就会制造不必要的压力。

被动语态:
The dangers of the situation were exaggerated by the media. = 媒体夸大了局势的危险性。
The importance of the event was exaggerated by the organizers. = 组织者夸大了这次事件的重要性。

疑问句:
Is he exaggerating the facts? = 他是在夸大事实吗？
Are you exaggerating your capabilities? = 你是在夸大你的能力吗？

否定句:
He didn't exaggerate the situation; he told the truth. = 他没有夸大情况；他说了实话。
She didn't exaggerate her feelings; she was genuinely upset. = 她没有夸大她的感受；她确实很不安。


PHRASE->
