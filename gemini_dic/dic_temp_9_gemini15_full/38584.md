concordances:/kənˈkɔːrdənsɪz/ | n. | 索引；索引表；和谐；一致 | A concordance is an alphabetical index of the principal words in a book or body of text, showing every instance of their occurrence with the context in which they appear.  It's a valuable tool for linguistic analysis, literary study, and Bible study.  | Example: The researcher used a concordance to track the frequency of specific words in Shakespeare's plays.  | 近义词：index, list, register | 反义词：discord, disagreement
