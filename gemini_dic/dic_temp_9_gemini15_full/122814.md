mole:/moʊl/| n.|痣；鼹鼠；间谍；克分子|A mole is a small, dark spot on the skin.  It can also be a small burrowing mammal, or a spy.  In chemistry, a mole is a unit of measurement.|例句：She has a small mole on her cheek. 她脸颊上有一颗小痣。

例句：The mole burrowed underground.  鼹鼠在地下挖洞。


近义词：birthmark (痣), spy (间谍),  (化学) gram-molecule


反义词： N/A (取决于语境)


词源：From Old English *mōl "spot, stain, blemish," also "mole (animal)," from Proto-Germanic *mōlā-.  The chemical meaning dates to the 19th century.
