scolopendrine:/skɒləˈpɛndrɪn/ | adj. |蜈蚣的；似蜈蚣的| Relating to or characteristic of scolopendras (centipedes).  The term often describes creatures with a scolopendra-like appearance or behavior, particularly their many legs and predatory nature. |Example: The scolopendrine creature scuttled across the forest floor.  这只蜈蚣状的生物在森林地面上快速爬行。


The scolopendrine predator ambushed its prey with lightning speed.  这只蜈蚣类的捕食者以闪电般的速度伏击猎物。

词源:  Derived from the genus name *Scolopendra*, which is itself from the Ancient Greek word  σκολόπενδρα (skolopéndra), meaning "a kind of centipede."  The suffix "-ine" is a common adjectival suffix indicating "belonging to" or "of the nature of".
