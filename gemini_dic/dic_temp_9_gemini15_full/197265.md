sharp:/ʃɑːrp/ /ʃɑːrp/ | adj., n., v. |  锋利的；尖锐的；敏锐的；刺耳的；突然的；精确的 |

英文解析:
As an adjective, "sharp" describes something pointed or having a fine edge, capable of cutting or piercing. It can also describe sounds that are high-pitched and unpleasant, or a quick, sudden movement or change.  Figuratively, it describes someone intelligent, quick-witted, and perceptive.  As a noun, it refers to a sharp sound or a sharp musical note. As a verb, it means to make something sharp or to suddenly turn.

例句:
He used a sharp knife to cut the bread. = 他用一把锋利的刀切面包。

The sound of the whistle was sharp and piercing. =  哨声尖锐刺耳。

She made a sharp turn to avoid the car. = 她急转弯避开汽车。

He's a sharp businessman. = 他是个精明的商人。

近义词:
pointed, keen, acute, abrupt, sudden, precise, crisp, clear

反义词:
dull, blunt, obtuse, slow, gradual, vague, fuzzy
