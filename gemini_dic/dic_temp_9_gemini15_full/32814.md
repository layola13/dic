choke-off:/tʃoʊk ˈɔːf/| vt.|使窒息；掐断；压制；阻止|To stop something completely, often by preventing the flow of something essential, such as air, water, or supplies.|The heavy snow choked off the power supply to the village. 大雪切断了村庄的电力供应。|近义词：suffocate, stifle, suppress, cut off|反义词：unleash, encourage, stimulate


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The government is trying to choke off illegal immigration. = 政府正试图阻止非法移民。
He chokes off his anger. = 他压制住自己的怒火。

一般过去时:
The fire choked off the oxygen supply. = 大火切断了氧气供应。
They choked off the enemy's advance. = 他们阻止了敌人的前进。


现在完成时:
The drought has choked off the river. = 干旱已经使河流干涸。
The new regulations have choked off small businesses. = 新的法规扼杀了小型企业。


过去完成时:
The flood had choked off all communication. = 洪水已经切断了所有通讯。
They had choked off the escape route. = 他们已经切断了逃生路线。


将来时:
The rising sea level will choke off coastal communities. = 海平面上升将使沿海社区窒息。
They will choke off funding for the project. = 他们将切断该项目的资金。


虚拟语气:
If they choked off the funding, the project would fail. = 如果他们切断资金，项目就会失败。
I wish they hadn't choked off our access to information. = 我真希望他们没有切断我们获取信息的机会。


被动语态:
The pipeline was choked off by a landslide. = 管道被山体滑坡堵塞了。
The protests were choked off by the police. = 抗议活动被警方压制了。


疑问句:
Will the sanctions choke off the country's economy? = 制裁会扼杀该国的经济吗？
Did the storm choke off the transportation routes? = 暴风雨是否切断了交通路线？


否定句:
The company didn't choke off its marketing efforts. = 公司没有停止其营销工作。
He didn't choke off his tears. = 他没有忍住眼泪。
