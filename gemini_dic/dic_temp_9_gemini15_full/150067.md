palstave:/ˈpælˌsteɪv/| n. |平头斧|A palstave is a type of bronze axe head, characteristic of the late Bronze Age in Europe.  It has a relatively flat blade and a long, relatively thin shaft hole, often with a prominent swelling or "stop-ridge" near the blade to prevent the haft from slipping.  Palstaves were used for felling trees, woodworking, and possibly warfare. | Example:  The archaeologist unearthed a well-preserved palstave dating back to the 10th century BC.  考古学家发现了一把保存完好的青铜平头斧，可以追溯到公元前10世纪。|近义词：bronze axe, socketed axe|反义词：无
