monoousious:/ˌmɒnəˈuːsiəs/| adj. | 单一实体的，同质的 |  Referring to the Christological doctrine that Christ possesses only one nature (divine), not two (divine and human). This term was used in the Christological controversies of the 4th and 5th centuries.  The opposite view, that Christ possessed two natures, is known as "diousious". |  The Council of Nicaea rejected the monoousious view.  |近义词：unitary, single-natured |反义词：diousious, binatured
