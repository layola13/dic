metaphysician:/ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪʃən/| n. |形而上学家|A metaphysician is a person who studies metaphysics, the branch of philosophy that deals with the fundamental nature of reality and existence.  Metaphysicians explore questions about being, time, space, causality, and the relationship between mind and matter. They often grapple with abstract concepts and attempt to provide overarching frameworks for understanding the universe. |Example:  Many famous philosophers, such as Plato and Aristotle, were also influential metaphysicians. 许多著名的哲学家，例如柏拉图和亚里士多德，也是有影响力的形而上学家。|近义词：philosopher (with a focus on metaphysics), ontologist |反义词：empiricist (focuses on observable evidence rather than abstract concepts)
