sestine:/ˈsɛstaɪn/| n.|六行诗|A sestine is a complex French verse form consisting of six stanzas of six lines each, followed by a three-line envoy.  Each stanza uses the same six end words, but in a patterned sequence that is repeated and varied throughout the poem. The envoy incorporates all six end words.  The form is known for its intricate structure and intellectual challenge. |The poet carefully arranged the end words in the sestine to create a pleasing effect. |近义词：complex verse form|反义词：simple verse form|名词
