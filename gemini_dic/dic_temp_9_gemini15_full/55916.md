divulgers:/dɪˈvʌldʒərz/| n.|泄密者，揭露者|Those who reveal secrets or confidential information.|The divulgers of state secrets faced severe punishment.|近义词：revealers, informers, whistleblowers|反义词：concealers, protectors


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The journalist divulges secrets to the public. = 记者向公众泄露秘密。
Divulgers often face legal consequences. = 泄密者常常面临法律后果。


一般过去时:
He divulged the company's plans to a rival. = 他向竞争对手泄露了公司的计划。
The whistleblower divulged the corruption scandal. = 举报人揭露了腐败丑闻。


现在完成时:
She has divulged too much information already. = 她已经泄露了太多的信息。
They have divulged the location of the hidden treasure. = 他们已经泄露了隐藏宝藏的位置。


过去完成时:
He had divulged the secret before the meeting. = 他在会议之前就已经泄露了秘密。
The spy had divulged crucial information to the enemy. = 间谍已经向敌人泄露了关键信息。


将来时:
He will divulge the truth soon. = 他很快就会说出真相。
They will divulge their findings in the report. = 他们将在报告中公布他们的发现。


虚拟语气:
If he divulged the truth, he would be in trouble. = 如果他泄露真相，他就会有麻烦。
I wish I hadn't divulged that secret. = 我真希望我没有泄露那个秘密。


被动语态:
The information was divulged to the press. = 信息被泄露给了媒体。
The secret plans were divulged by a traitor. = 秘密计划被叛徒泄露了。


疑问句:
Who divulged the confidential information? = 谁泄露了机密信息？
Will they divulge the details of the investigation? = 他们会公布调查细节吗？


否定句:
He did not divulge any sensitive information. = 他没有泄露任何敏感信息。
She refused to divulge the source of her information. = 她拒绝透露她的消息来源。


PHRASE->
