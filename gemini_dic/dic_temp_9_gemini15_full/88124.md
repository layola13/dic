hijras:/ˈhɪdʒrɑːz/| n.|希杰拉人（印度次大陆的跨性别者群体）|A hijra is a member of a third gender community in South Asia, primarily in India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Nepal.  They are often assigned male at birth but identify and live as women, sometimes undergoing surgeries or hormone treatments.  Their roles in society have historically varied, including religious figures, entertainers, and beggars.  The community's history is complex and often involves social stigma and marginalization.  |例句：The documentary explores the lives and experiences of hijras in Mumbai.  这部纪录片探讨了孟买希杰拉人的生活和经历。
