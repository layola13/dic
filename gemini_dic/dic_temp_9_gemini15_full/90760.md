humoralism:/ˈhjuːmərəlɪzəm/ | n. |体液说；体液学说 |The theory that a person's health is determined by the balance of four fluids in the body: blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile.  This ancient medical theory posited that imbalances in these humors caused illness.  |Example:  Humoralism was the dominant medical theory in Europe for centuries.  体液说在欧洲盛行了几个世纪。|近义词： ancient medicine, four humors theory |反义词： modern medicine, germ theory
