predeclining: /ˌpriːdɪˈklaɪnɪŋ/ | adj. | 预先下降的；提前减少的 |  Describing something that is decreasing or declining before its expected or usual time.  This word combines "pre-" (before) and "declining" (decreasing). It implies a decline that occurs earlier than anticipated. | The predeclining sales figures worried the CEO. = 预先下降的销售数字让首席执行官感到担忧。 The predeclining population of the town led to the closure of the school. = 城镇人口的提前减少导致学校关闭。 | 近义词：early declining, advance falling, prior decrease | 反义词：postponed increase, late rise
