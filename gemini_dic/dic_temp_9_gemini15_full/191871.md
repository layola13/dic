scoffing: /ˈskɒfɪŋ/ |/ˈskɑːfɪŋ/ | n. v. | 嘲笑；挖苦；轻蔑地拒绝 |

英文解析:The act of scoffing involves expressing contempt or derision, often through mocking laughter or scornful remarks.  It can be directed at a person, idea, or situation. As a verb, it means to speak to someone or something derisively; to show contempt for.

例句:

He scoffed at my suggestion. = 他嘲笑我的建议。

Her scoffing laughter echoed through the room. = 她嘲笑的 laughter 回荡在房间里。


近义词: derision, mockery, scorn, ridicule, jeering, sneering

反义词: praise, approval, admiration, respect
