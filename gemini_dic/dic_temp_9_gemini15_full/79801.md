glycide:/ˈɡlaɪsaɪd/| n. |甘油醚；缩水甘油；环氧丙烷|Glycide refers to the simplest epoxide, also known as oxirane or ethylene oxide.  It is a three-membered cyclic ether with a high degree of ring strain, making it highly reactive and prone to ring-opening reactions.  Glycides are also commonly used as building blocks in the synthesis of various organic compounds.|The glycide group is a key component in many epoxy resins.|近义词：oxirane, epoxypropane|反义词：无
