pooly:/ˈpuːli/| adj.|糟糕的，不好的，可怜的 | poorly means in a bad or unsatisfactory way;  in a way that shows a lack of skill or care. It can also describe someone's health as being weak or unwell. |例句：He played poorly in the match. 他在比赛中表现很糟糕。

The work was done poorly. 工作做得不好。


近义词：badly, badly done, deficiently, inadequately, imperfectly, insufficiently

反义词：well, excellently, superbly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He plays poorly in every game. 他每一场比赛都打得很糟糕。
She sings poorly, but she enjoys it. 她唱歌不好，但她很享受。


一般过去时:
The team performed poorly last night.  球队昨晚表现很糟糕。
He slept poorly because of the noise. 因为噪音他睡得很不好。


现在完成时:
She has played poorly throughout the season.  她整个赛季表现都很糟糕。
He has felt poorly for several days. 他已经感到不舒服好几天了。


过去完成时:
They had played poorly in the previous match. 他们在上一场比赛中表现很糟糕。
She had felt poorly all week before finally seeing a doctor.  她在看医生之前整整一周都感觉不舒服。


将来时:
He will probably play poorly again tomorrow. 他明天可能还会表现得很糟糕。
I think I will feel poorly tomorrow if I don’t rest.如果我不休息，我觉得我明天会不舒服。


虚拟语气:
If he had played better, the team would have won. 如果他打得好一点，球队就会赢了。
If she had felt better, she would have gone to the party. 如果她感觉好一点，她就会去参加派对了。


被动语态:
The task was done poorly. 任务完成得很糟糕。
The song was sung poorly. 歌唱得很糟糕。


疑问句:
Did he play poorly? 他打得糟糕吗？

否定句:
He didn't play poorly; he played exceptionally well. 他打得并不糟糕，他打得非常好。
She doesn't usually feel poorly. 她通常不会感到不舒服。
