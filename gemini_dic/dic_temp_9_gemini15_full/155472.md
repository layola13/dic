phenomenist:/fɪˈnɒmənɪst/| n. |现象论者，唯现象论者|A phenomenist is someone who adheres to phenomenology, a philosophical movement emphasizing the study of consciousness and experience as it appears to the individual.  They believe that our understanding of the world is fundamentally shaped by our subjective experiences. | Example:  Many phenomenists argue that objective reality is inaccessible, only our perception of it is knowable.  许多现象论者认为客观现实是不可知的，只有我们对它的感知才是可知的。|近义词：idealist (in some contexts), subjectivist |反义词：realist, objectivist |名词
