capitulated:/kəˈpɪtʃuleɪtɪd/ | v. | 投降；屈服；(被迫)同意 | to cease resisting an opponent or an unwelcome demand; surrender | The army capitulated after a long siege.  军队在长期围困后投降了。| The government capitulated to the protesters' demands. 政府屈服于抗议者的要求。|  surrender, yield, succumb | resist, defy, fight


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He capitulates to pressure easily. 他很容易屈服于压力。
They capitulate to the demands of their customers. 他们屈服于顾客的要求。


一般过去时:
The rebels capitulated to the government forces.  叛军向政府军投降了。
France capitulated to the German army in 1940. 法国在1940年向德军投降。


现在完成时:
The company has capitulated to market forces.  这家公司已经屈服于市场力量。
She has capitulated to his charms. 她已经屈服于他的魅力。


过去完成时:
The general had capitulated before the reinforcements arrived. 增援部队到达之前，将军已经投降了。
They had capitulated after weeks of intense fighting.  经过几周激烈的战斗后，他们投降了。


将来时:
They will capitulate if they run out of supplies. 如果他们耗尽了补给，他们将投降。
The city will likely capitulate within days.  这座城市很可能在几天内投降。


虚拟语气:
If he had capitulated earlier, there would have been less bloodshed. 如果他早点投降，就不会有那么多流血事件了。
I wish they hadn't capitulated so easily. 我真希望他们不要这么轻易地投降。


被动语态:
The fortress was capitulated after a week-long bombardment.  要塞在一周的炮击后被攻陷了。
The terms of surrender were capitulated upon by both sides. 双方都接受了投降条款。


疑问句:
Will the enemy capitulate without a fight? 敌人会不战而降吗？
Did they capitulate to the demands of the terrorists? 他们屈服于恐怖分子的要求了吗？


否定句:
They refused to capitulate, even in the face of overwhelming odds. 即使面对压倒性的优势，他们也拒绝投降。
The rebels would not capitulate under any circumstances. 叛军在任何情况下都不会投降。


PHRASE->

capitulate to: 向…屈服
capitulate unconditionally: 无条件投降
capitulate under pressure: 在压力下屈服
forced to capitulate: 被迫投降
unconditional capitulation: 无条件投降
