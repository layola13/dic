damocles:/ˈdæməkliːz/| n. |达摩克利斯之剑；潜在的危险|A metaphor referring to imminent danger or peril, often of a catastrophic nature, hanging precariously over someone.  It originates from a Greek legend about Damocles, a courtier who envied the seemingly happy life of his king, Dionysius I of Syracuse.  Dionysius, to illustrate the precariousness of power and wealth, seated Damocles at a lavish feast with a sword suspended above his head by a single hair. This symbolized the constant threat of impending doom that accompanies power and privilege.|The looming deadline felt like a Damocles' sword hanging over our heads.|近义词：threat, peril, danger, menace|反义词：safety, security, peace of mind|名词
