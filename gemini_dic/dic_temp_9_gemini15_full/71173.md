fissirostral: /ˌfɪsɪˈrɒstrəl/ | adj. | 裂隙吻部的；裂吻的 | Relating to or having a cleft or split rostrum (beak or snout).  The term is primarily used in zoology and paleontology to describe the morphology of certain animals, particularly extinct ones.  | Example: The fossilized skull exhibited a distinctly fissirostral morphology.  化石头骨呈现出明显的裂隙吻部形态。  | Near synonyms: cleft-snouted,  bifid-rostral (less common) | Antonyms: entire-rostral, unsplit-rostral (these are not common terms) |


词源:
The word "fissirostral" is a compound word formed from:

* **fiss-**: From Latin *fissus*, meaning "split" or "cleft".
* **-irostral**:  From Latin *rostrum*, meaning "beak" or "snout," and the combining form *-irostral*.


用法说明:

This word is highly specialized and mainly used within scientific contexts, particularly in descriptions of animal anatomy, especially those of extinct species whose beak or snout displays a cleft or split.  It’s not a word you'd use in everyday conversation.  There are no common grammatical variations or phrases specifically using "fissirostral" beyond its descriptive function within a sentence.  The word itself functions as an adjective modifying a noun (e.g., "fissirostral skull," "fissirostral dinosaur").
