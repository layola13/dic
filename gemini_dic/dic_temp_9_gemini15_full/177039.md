reannexation: /ˌriːænekˈseɪʃən/ | n. |重新兼并；重新吞并 | The act of annexing something again, especially territory previously annexed and then lost.  It implies a previous annexation followed by a period where the territory was not under the control of the annexing power, and then a subsequent re-acquisition. | Example: The reannexation of Crimea by Russia in 2014 was widely condemned internationally.  俄罗斯2014年重新吞并克里米亚受到了国际社会的广泛谴责。|近义词：reoccupation, reconquest, recapture |反义词：cession, relinquishment
