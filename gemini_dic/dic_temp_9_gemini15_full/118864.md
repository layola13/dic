metapodiale: /ˌmɛtəˈpɔːdiəl/ | n. |掌骨；跖骨| A metapodial is a bone in the foot or hand of a vertebrate.  Specifically, it refers to one of the long bones in the metacarpus (hand) or metatarsus (foot) between the carpals/tarsals and the phalanges (fingers/toes).| 例句：The horse fractured a metapodial bone in its front leg. 这匹马的前腿一根掌骨骨折了。|  The x-ray showed a clear break in the metapodial. X光片显示掌骨明显骨折。 | 近义词：metacarpal (hand), metatarsal (foot)| 反义词：carpal, tarsal, phalanx | 名词
