cholesterin:/kəˈlɛstrɒl/| n. |胆固醇|A waxy, fat-like substance that's essential for building and maintaining healthy cells.  However, high levels of cholesterol in the blood can increase the risk of heart disease. |例句：High cholesterol can lead to heart disease. 高胆固醇会导致心脏病。|例句：My doctor advised me to lower my cholesterol levels. 我的医生建议我降低胆固醇水平。|近义词：lipid |反义词：无|名词

词源:
The word "cholesterol" is derived from the Greek words "chole" (bile) and "stereos" (solid) and the suffix "-ol" (alcohol).  This reflects its original discovery in gallstones.
