deputizes:/ˈdepjʊtaɪzɪz/| v. |授权；代理；委任；|To act or serve as a deputy or substitute for someone.  To temporarily assign (someone) the power or authority of a higher-ranking person. |The manager deputizes his assistant while he's on vacation.  经理在他休假期间授权他的助理代理他的工作。|The sheriff deputized several volunteers to help with crowd control. 警长委任了几名志愿者协助维持秩序。|assigns, delegates, commissions |—|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deputizes his colleague when he is away. 他不在的时候，他委托他的同事代理他的工作。
She deputizes for the CEO during her absence.  她不在的时候，由她代理CEO的工作。


一般过去时:
The mayor deputized a city council member to speak at the event. 市长委任了一位市议员在活动上发言。
He deputized his son to run the family business. 他授权他的儿子经营家族企业。


现在完成时:
The governor has deputized several officials to investigate the matter. 州长已委任几名官员调查此事。
She has deputized her assistant many times this month. 这个月她已经多次委托她的助理代理她的工作。


过去完成时:
They had deputized a representative before the meeting began. 会议开始前，他们已经委任了一位代表。
The headmaster had deputized a teacher to take his place at the ceremony. 校长已委托一位老师代替他出席典礼。


将来时:
The president will deputize a cabinet member to attend the summit. 总统将委派一位内阁成员出席峰会。
He will deputize his wife to handle the finances while he is traveling. 他旅行期间将授权他的妻子处理财务。


虚拟语气:
If the manager were unavailable, I would deputize for him. 如果经理不在，我会代理他的工作。
I wish he would deputize someone to help with the project. 我希望他会委托某人来帮忙完成这个项目。


被动语态:
A junior staff member was deputized to handle the urgent case. 一名初级职员被授权处理紧急案件。
Several volunteers were deputized to assist the police officers. 几名志愿者被委派协助警务人员。


疑问句:
Will the director deputize anyone to cover his absence? 主任会委托任何人来弥补他的缺席吗？
Who will be deputized to manage the project in his stead? 谁将被委派来接替他管理这个项目？


否定句:
The boss did not deputize anyone to handle the complaint. 老板没有委托任何人处理投诉。
He refused to deputize his assistant for the important meeting. 他拒绝委托他的助理参加重要的会议。
