chronicler:/ˈkrɒnɪklər/| n. |编年史作者，记录者|A chronicler is a person who writes or compiles chronicles, which are historical accounts arranged in chronological order.  They often focus on specific events, periods, or individuals, providing detailed narratives. |Example:  The chronicler meticulously documented the king's reign, including every battle and political maneuver.  编年史作者一丝不苟地记录了国王的统治，包括每一次战斗和政治策略。|近义词：historian, recorder, annalist |反义词：None directly opposite, but could be considered the opposite of someone who deliberately obscures or distorts historical events.
