cincinnatus: /ˌsɪnsɪˈneɪtəs/ | n. | 辛辛那提（人名）；退隐的政治家 |  The name Cincinnatus refers to Lucius Quinctius Cincinnatus, a Roman patrician and statesman known for his selflessness and dedication to the Roman Republic. He is famously depicted as a farmer who twice left his plow to serve as dictator of Rome, only to return to his simple life after resolving the crisis. His name has become synonymous with civic virtue, selfless service, and the rejection of power.  | Example:  Cincinnatus is often cited as an example of a virtuous leader. = 辛辛那提经常被引用为一位德高望重的领导人的例子。  The story of Cincinnatus embodies the ideal of republican simplicity. = 辛辛那提的故事体现了共和主义的简单理想。 | 近义词： selfless leader, virtuous statesman | 反义词：tyrant, power-hungry leader |  n.
