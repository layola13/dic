fulminations:/ˌfʊlmɪˈneɪʃənz/| n.| 痛斥；怒斥；猛烈抨击 | Loud, vehement, and often angry pronouncements; a series of denunciations.  The speaker's fulminations against the government were met with both cheers and jeers.  |近义词：denunciations, tirades, condemnations, outbursts |反义词：praise, commendation, approval


英文解析:Fulminations are strong, angry expressions of disapproval or criticism. They are typically delivered forcefully and vehemently, often in a series of pronouncements.  The word suggests a powerful and intense outburst of anger or condemnation.

His fulminations against the unfairness of the system were ignored. = 他对系统不公平的强烈谴责被忽视了。
