reregulated: /ˌriːˈreɡjuleɪtɪd/ | adj. |重新管制过的；再次规范的 |  Having been regulated again; subject to new or renewed regulations.  The word describes a state or condition after a second or subsequent act of regulation. | 例句：The reregulated industry saw a surge in competition.  重新管制的行业竞争激增。

The reregulated fishing quotas allowed for a more sustainable harvest. 重新规范的捕捞配额允许更可持续的捕捞。| 近义词：re-regulated,  re-controlled, newly regulated | 反义词：deregulated, uncontrolled

词源:
The word "reregulated" is formed by adding the prefix "re-" (meaning "again" or "anew") to the word "regulated," which itself comes from the verb "regulate."  "Regulate" ultimately derives from the Latin word "regulare," meaning "to make straight, to adjust, to govern."
