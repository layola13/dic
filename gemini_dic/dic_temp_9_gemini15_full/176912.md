realigned:/ˌriːəˈlaɪnd/| vt. |重新调整；重新排列；使重新结盟|To align again; to adjust or arrange in a new or different alignment; to cause (something, such as a group or organization) to form new alliances.|例句：The company realigned its marketing strategy to target a younger demographic.  公司重新调整了其营销策略以瞄准更年轻的人群。

例句：After the merger, the two companies realigned their operations to improve efficiency. 合并后，两家公司重新调整了运营以提高效率。

近义词：rearrange, readjust, reorganize, realign

反义词：disorganize, misalign


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The team realigns its priorities every quarter. = 团队每个季度都会重新调整其优先级。
He realigns his furniture every spring. = 他每年春天都会重新摆放家具。

一般过去时:
They realigned their political alliances after the election. = 他们在大选后重新调整了他们的政治联盟。
The government realigned its economic policies to stimulate growth. = 政府重新调整了其经济政策以刺激增长。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

The team had realigned its strategy after the initial setback. = 在最初的挫折之后，团队已经重新调整了战略。

将来时:

The company will realign its departments to improve collaboration. = 公司将重新调整其部门以改善协作。

虚拟语气:
