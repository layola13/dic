jump-started:/ˈdʒʌmpˌstɑːtɪd/ | v. |  启动，使…重新开始，使…恢复活力 | To jump-start something is to give it a sudden boost of energy or activity to get it going again, often after a period of inactivity or failure.  This is often used in the context of a car engine but also metaphorically. | 例句：He jump-started his career with a new job at a tech company. 他通过在一家科技公司找到新工作来重振事业。

例句：We jump-started the stalled project by hiring a new project manager. 我们通过聘请一位新的项目经理来启动停滞不前的项目。


近义词：revitalize, restart, kick-start, reinvigorate | 反义词：halt, stop, cease, shut down | 词性：动词


USAGE->
