existentialist:/ˌɛɡzɪˈstɛnʃəlɪst/ | n. |存在主义者，存在主义哲学家 |A person who follows existentialism.  Existentialists believe that existence precedes essence, meaning that individuals are born into the world without a predetermined purpose or nature, and it is through their choices and actions that they create their own meaning and essence. |Example: Sartre was a prominent existentialist writer and philosopher.  萨特是一位著名的存在主义作家和哲学家。|近义词：existential philosopher |反义词：essentialist
