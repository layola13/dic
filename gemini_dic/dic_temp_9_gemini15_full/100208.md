interrhyming: /ˌɪntərˈraɪmɪŋ/ | adj. | 内韵的，内部押韵的 |  The use of rhyming words within a single line or verse of poetry.  It creates a musicality and emphasis within the text.  | Example:  "The silken, sad, uncertain rustling of each purple curtain" (Poe) demonstrates internal rhyme with "silken" and "uncertain." | 近义词：internal rhyme | 反义词：无
