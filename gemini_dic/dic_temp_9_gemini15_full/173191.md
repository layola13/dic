quasi-bronze: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ brɒnz/ | adj. |准青铜时代;类似青铜的|  Describing a period or artifact that exhibits characteristics of the Bronze Age but doesn't fully meet its criteria.  It might refer to a time before widespread bronze use, a culture using bronze sparsely, or objects made with bronze-like materials.  The term highlights the transitional or incomplete nature of the bronze aspect. | The archaeological findings suggest a quasi-bronze culture existed in this region before the full adoption of bronze metallurgy.  |近义词: proto-bronze, pre-bronze | 反义词:  full bronze age


USAGE->
This word is primarily used as an adjective and does not have verb or noun forms in standard usage. Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  The usage is limited to descriptive contexts within archaeology and related fields.


例句1:
The artifacts discovered show evidence of a quasi-bronze technology, suggesting an early experimentation with metalworking. = 发现的文物显示出一种准青铜技术，表明了早期对金属加工的实验。

例句2:
Some scholars argue that the region experienced a prolonged quasi-bronze period before the flourishing of a fully developed Bronze Age. = 一些学者认为，在完全发达的青铜时代蓬勃发展之前，该地区经历了漫长的准青铜时期。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "quasi-bronze."  The term is used independently within its specialized context.
