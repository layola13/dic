clap:/klæp/ | vt., n. | 拍手；鼓掌 | To strike the palms of one's hands together, typically to express approval or applause.  | 例句：The audience clapped enthusiastically after the performance.  观众在演出结束后热烈鼓掌。 | 例句：She clapped her hands in delight. 她高兴地拍起手来。 | 近义词：applaud, applaud, clap hands | 反义词：boo, hiss


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He claps his hands to get attention. = 他拍手以引起注意。
They clap rhythmically to the music. = 他们有节奏地随着音乐拍手。


一般过去时:
The crowd clapped loudly after the speech. = 演讲结束后，人群大声鼓掌。
She clapped her hands together in excitement. = 她兴奋地拍着手。


现在完成时:
The baby has clapped his hands for the first time. = 宝宝第一次拍手了。
They have clapped and cheered throughout the game. = 他们在比赛中一直拍手欢呼。


过去完成时:
The audience had already clapped before the singer finished the song. = 在歌手唱完歌之前，观众已经鼓掌了。
They had clapped enthusiastically at the previous performance. = 他们在之前的演出中热烈鼓掌。


将来时:
The audience will clap when the curtain falls. = 幕布落下时，观众会鼓掌。
She will clap her hands in surprise. = 她会惊讶地拍手。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would clap for his talent. = 如果我是你，我会为他精彩的表现鼓掌。
I wish I could clap louder to show my appreciation. = 我希望我能鼓掌更响亮来表达我的赞赏。


被动语态:
His performance was clapped by the audience. = 他的表演受到了观众的鼓掌。
The hands were clapped together in prayer. = 手掌合十祈祷。


疑问句:
Did the audience clap at the end of the show? = 演出结束后观众鼓掌了吗？
Will they clap for the winning team? = 他们会为获胜的队伍鼓掌吗？


否定句:
The audience didn't clap at all. = 观众根本没有鼓掌。
He didn't clap because he didn't like the performance. = 他没有鼓掌，因为他不喜欢这场演出。
