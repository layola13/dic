cantoris: /kænˈtɔːrɪs/ | n. | 唱诗班歌手；领唱；(教堂中的)唱诗班席位 |  A cantoris is a singer in a choir, especially the leading singer or the section of the choir seated on one side of the chancel.  It also refers to the location of the choir stalls in a church, usually on the north side. | 例句：The cantoris section of the choir sang the responses beautifully.  唱诗班的领唱部分优美地唱出了回应。

The cantoris was known for his powerful voice.  那位领唱以其强大的嗓音而闻名。


近义词：chorister, soloist, singer | 反义词：audience, congregation


词源：源自拉丁语 *cantoris，是 *canto（唱歌）的名词性所有格，表示“歌手的”。  The word reflects the traditional placement of the choir in churches, with one section designated as *cantoris* and the other as *decani*.
