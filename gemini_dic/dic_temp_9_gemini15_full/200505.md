slant:/slænt/ | /slɑːnt/| n., v.|倾斜；斜面；偏见；歪曲；使倾斜|

英文解析:
As a noun, "slant" refers to a sloping surface, an oblique direction, or a particular bias or viewpoint. As a verb, it means to slope or incline, or to present information in a biased way.


例句:
The roof had a noticeable slant. = 屋顶有明显的倾斜。

The journalist slanted the story to favor the government. = 记者歪曲报道以偏袒政府。


近义词:
(n.) slope, incline, bias, prejudice, angle
(v.) incline, tilt, skew, distort, bias


反义词:
(n.) vertical, perpendicular, upright, unbiased, objective
(v.) straighten, align


词性:
Noun, Verb


词源:
From Old English *slan "to slant, slope," from Proto-Germanic *slankan (source also of Old High German slanc "oblique," Old Norse ská "slant, oblique").  Related to Old English *slanc "slender."
