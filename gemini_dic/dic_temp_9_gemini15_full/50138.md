denudement: /dɪˈnuːdmənt/ | n. |  剥夺；剥光；裸露；荒芜 | The removal of a covering or layer; the state of being stripped bare.  It often refers to the removal of vegetation, soil, or other coverings from a surface.  | 例句：The denudement of the forest left the land vulnerable to erosion. 森林的剥蚀使土地容易受到侵蚀。

The prolonged denudement of the topsoil led to desertification.  长期土壤剥蚀导致荒漠化。


近义词：depletion, stripping, exposure, bareness, devastation | 反义词：covering, enrichment, growth, afforestation


词源:  Comes from the verb "denude," meaning "to strip bare," which itself originates from the Latin word "denudare," a compound of "de-" (away, off) and "nudare" (to make naked).
