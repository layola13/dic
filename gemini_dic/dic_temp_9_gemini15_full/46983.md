darwinists:/ˈdɑːrwɪnɪsts/ | n. | 达尔文主义者 | Adherents of Darwinism, the theory of evolution by natural selection.  They believe that species originate and develop through the natural selection of small, inherited variations that increase the individual's ability to compete, survive, and reproduce. | Example:  Many darwinists see the fossil record as strong evidence for evolution. 许多达尔文主义者认为化石记录是进化论的有力证据。  |近义词: evolutionists, naturalists | 反义词: creationists
