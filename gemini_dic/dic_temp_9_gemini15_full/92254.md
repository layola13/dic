hypervariability: /ˌhaɪpərˌveəriəˈbɪləti/ | n. |高度变异性，超变异性 | The property of exhibiting extremely high levels of variation, often exceeding the expected range of natural variation.  This is frequently observed in certain genetic regions, particularly in rapidly evolving viruses and some immune system genes. | Example:  The hypervariability of the influenza virus's surface proteins makes it difficult to develop a long-lasting vaccine.  高度变异的流感病毒表面蛋白使得研制持久的疫苗变得困难。 | 近义词：high variability, extreme variation, great diversity | 反义词：monomorphism, uniformity, constancy |  词性：名词
