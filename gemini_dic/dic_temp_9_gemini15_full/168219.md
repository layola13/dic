pro-form:/ˈproʊfɔːrm/| n. |代词|A pro-form is a word or phrase that stands in for another linguistic unit, often a larger phrase or clause, avoiding repetition or unnecessary detail.  It's a grammatical placeholder that derives its meaning from its antecedent (the element it replaces).  Common examples include pronouns (he, she, it, they), pro-verbs (do so, do it), and pro-adjectives (such).|

例句：

He lost his keys, and he couldn't find them. ("them" is a pro-form replacing "his keys")

The dog chased the ball, and it ran away quickly. ("it" is a pro-form replacing "the dog")


近义词：placeholder, substitute, representative

反义词：full form, explicit form


词性：名词
