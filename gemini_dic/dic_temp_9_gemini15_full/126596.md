naggy:/ˈnæɡi/| adj. v.|唠叨的；喋喋不休的；令人厌烦的 |不停抱怨的；爱挑剔的 |The nagging feeling that something was wrong proved to be correct. 令人不安的感觉证明是正确的。|近义词：irritating, annoying, bothersome, pestering |反义词：calm, quiet, peaceful


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He nags his wife about her spending habits. = 他唠叨他妻子的花钱习惯。
She nags her children to clean their rooms. = 她唠叨她的孩子们打扫他们的房间。

一般过去时:
He nagged me all day about the report. = 他整天唠叨我关于报告的事。
She nagged him until he finally agreed. = 她一直唠叨他直到他最终同意。

现在完成时:
She has nagged him about that for weeks. = 她已经为此唠叨他几周了。
He has been nagging me to fix the leaky faucet. = 他一直在唠叨我修理漏水的龙头。

过去完成时:
I had nagged him repeatedly before he finally listened. = 在他最终听取我的意见之前，我已经反复地唠叨他了。
She had nagged her husband for years about getting a new car. = 她多年来一直唠叨她的丈夫买一辆新车。


将来时:
I know she will nag me about it again tomorrow. = 我知道她明天还会为此唠叨我。
He will probably nag her until she gives in. = 他可能会一直唠叨她直到她屈服。

虚拟语气:
If I didn't nag him, he wouldn't get things done. = 如果我不唠叨他，他就不会把事情做完。
I wish he wouldn't nag so much. = 我希望他不那么唠叨。

被动语态:
He was nagged relentlessly by his mother. = 他被母亲无情地唠叨。
She felt nagged by her conscience. = 她感到良心不安。

疑问句:
Does he often nag you? = 他经常唠叨你吗？
Is she always nagging about something? = 她总是为某事唠叨吗？

否定句:
He doesn't nag as much as he used to. = 他不像以前那样唠叨了。
She didn't nag him about it this time. = 这次她没有为此唠叨他。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "naggy" is derived from the Old Norse word "nagga," meaning "to gnaw" or "to bite."  This reflects the persistent and irritating nature of nagging.  The sense evolved from the physical act of gnawing to the persistent, irritating verbal behavior.
