meddler:/ˈmɛdlər/| n.|爱管闲事的人，干涉者|a person who interferes in other people's affairs, often in a way that is annoying or unwelcome.  |例句：He's a meddler; he's always sticking his nose into other people's business. 他是个爱管闲事的人；他总是把鼻子伸到别人的事里。|例句：Don't be a meddler!  别管闲事！|近义词：busybody, interferer, interloper |反义词：non-interferer, bystander


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He meddles in everyone's affairs. = 他干涉每个人的事情。
She meddles with my things. = 她乱动我的东西。


一般过去时:
He meddled in the company's affairs. = 他干涉了公司的业务。
She meddled with the evidence. = 她篡改了证据。


现在完成时:
He has meddled in politics for years. = 他多年来一直干预政治。
She has meddled with my computer files. = 她篡改了我的电脑文件。


过去完成时:
He had meddled in the negotiations before I arrived. = 我到达之前，他已经干预了谈判。
She had meddled with the documents before the trial. = 审判前，她已经篡改了文件。


将来时:
He will meddle in the election. = 他会干预选举。
She will meddle in her sister's life again. = 她还会再次干涉她妹妹的生活。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't meddled, things would have been better. = 如果他不干预，情况会更好。
I wish she hadn't meddled in our business. = 我真希望她不要干预我们的事情。


被动语态:
His affairs were meddled with. = 他的事情被干预了。
The evidence was meddled with. = 证据被篡改了。


疑问句:

否定句:
He didn't meddle in the argument. = 他没有干预这场争论。
She didn't meddle with my belongings. = 她没有乱动我的东西。
