reordain:/ˌriːɔːˈdeɪn/| vt.|重新任命(圣职人员);重新奉献(圣物)|To ordain again; to restore to sacred office.  The bishop decided to reordain the priest after the scandal. 主教决定在丑闻之后重新任命这位牧师。|近义词：reinstall, reinstate|反义词：defrock, unordain


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reordains priests every year. = 他每年都重新任命牧师。
The church reordains ministers who have been suspended. = 教堂重新任命被停职的牧师。


一般过去时:
They reordained him after a period of reflection. = 经过一段时间的反思后，他们重新任命了他。
The archbishop reordained the disgraced priest. = 大主教重新任命了那位名誉扫地的牧师。


现在完成时:
The church has reordained several priests this year. = 教堂今年已经重新任命了几位牧师。
He has reordained many times throughout his career. = 他在他的职业生涯中重新任命过很多次。


过去完成时:
They had reordained him before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，他们已经重新任命了他。
The diocese had reordained several priests by the end of the year. = 到年底，教区已经重新任命了几位牧师。


将来时:
The bishop will reordain him next month. = 主教下个月将重新任命他。
The church will reordain the suspended minister next year. = 教堂明年将重新任命被停职的牧师。


虚拟语气:
If he repented, the bishop would reordain him. = 如果他悔过，主教就会重新任命他。
I wish they had reordained him sooner. = 我希望他们早点重新任命他。


被动语态:
He was reordained after a thorough investigation. = 经过彻底调查后，他被重新任命了。
Many priests have been reordained after completing further studies. = 许多牧师在完成进一步学习后被重新任命。


疑问句:
Will he be reordained? = 他会被重新任命吗？
Has she ever been reordained? = 她曾经被重新任命过吗？


否定句:
They did not reordain him. = 他们没有重新任命他。
The church will not reordain him under any circumstances. = 教堂无论如何都不会重新任命他。


PHRASE->
