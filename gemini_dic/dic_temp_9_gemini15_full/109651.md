leukaemia: /liˈuːkiːmiə/ | n. | 白血病 |  A cancer of the blood or bone marrow, characterized by an abnormal increase of immature white blood cells in the blood.  It is a serious and potentially fatal disease affecting the body's ability to fight infection.  There are various types of leukaemia, classified by the type of white blood cell affected and how quickly the disease progresses. |  My grandmother sadly passed away after a long battle with leukaemia.  = 我的祖母在与白血病长期斗争后不幸去世。

The diagnosis of acute myeloid leukaemia came as a devastating shock to the family. = 急性髓细胞白血病的诊断对这个家庭来说是一个毁灭性的打击。


近义词: blood cancer, leukemia (US spelling) | 反义词:  None (it's a disease, not something with a direct opposite) | 词性: Noun

词源:  The word "leukaemia" (or "leukemia") ultimately derives from the Greek words  λευκός (leukos), meaning "white," and  αἷμα (haima), meaning "blood."  This refers to the increased number of white blood cells characteristic of the disease.  The "-emia" suffix indicates a blood condition.
