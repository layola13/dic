precollapse: /ˌpriːkəˈlæps/ | adj. | 崩塌前的，坍塌之前的 |  Describing the state or period immediately before a collapse or downfall.  It often implies a period of instability or weakening that precedes the ultimate failure. | Example: The precollapse signs of the bridge were largely ignored, leading to a tragic accident.  桥梁坍塌前的迹象在很大程度上被忽视了，导致了一场悲惨的事故。|近义词: preceding, prior to, before the collapse, antecedent | 反义词: postcollapse, after the collapse |  形容词
