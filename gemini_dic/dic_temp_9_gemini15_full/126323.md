myopia:/maɪˈoʊpiə/| n. |近视，近视眼 | A condition of the eye where the person can see near objects clearly, but distant objects appear blurry.  This is because the eyeball is too long, or the cornea (the clear front part of the eye) is too curved, causing the light rays to focus in front of the retina instead of on it. |  He suffers from myopia and needs glasses to drive. 他患有近视，需要戴眼镜开车。  Myopia is a common vision problem, especially among young people. 近视是一种常见的视力问题，尤其是在年轻人中。 | hyperopia (远视), hypermetropia (远视) |  emmetropia (正视)
