rotoscoping:/ˈroʊtəˌskoʊpɪŋ/| n. |描图动画，描线动画|The process of tracing over live-action footage frame by frame to create an animation.  This technique is often used to create realistic-looking animated characters or scenes.  It can also be used to add animation to still images.|The animator used rotoscoping to bring the dancer's movements to life in the animated film.  动画师利用描图动画技术将舞蹈演员的动作融入动画电影中。|animation, cel animation|  (no direct antonym, but opposite in style could be: pure animation)|n.
