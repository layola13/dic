cringed:/krɪndʒd/| v.|畏缩；尴尬；退缩|To make a grimace or shrink back, typically in disgust, embarrassment, or pain.  It implies a feeling of shame or discomfort. |例句：I cringed when I heard my embarrassing childhood story being retold.  我听到我的尴尬童年故事被重提时感到很窘迫。

例句：He cringed at the thought of singing in front of so many people. 他一想到要在这么多人面前唱歌就感到害怕。


近义词：winced, recoiled, shrank, flinched|反义词：enjoyed, relished, embraced

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cringes every time he hears that song. = 他每次听到那首歌都会感到尴尬。
She cringes at the thought of public speaking. = 她一想到公开演讲就感到害怕。

一般过去时:
I cringed when I saw my old photos. = 我看到我的旧照片时感到很尴尬。
He cringed at her inappropriate joke. = 他对她不恰当的笑话感到厌恶。

现在完成时:
I have cringed so many times today. = 我今天已经无数次感到尴尬了。
She has cringed every time she's seen that movie. = 她每次看到那部电影都会感到尴尬。

过去完成时:
I had cringed before he even began to speak. = 在他开始说话之前我就已经感到尴尬了。
She had cringed at his behavior the entire evening. = 她整个晚上都对他行为感到厌恶。

将来时:
I will cringe when I see him again. = 我再次见到他时会感到尴尬。
She will cringe if she sees that video. = 如果她看到那个视频，她会感到尴尬。

虚拟语气:
I would cringe if I had to sing that song. = 如果我不得不唱那首歌，我会感到很尴尬。
She would have cringed if she had known what he was going to say. = 如果她知道他要说什么，她会感到很尴尬。

被动语态:
(This verb is rarely used in the passive voice, as it focuses on the subject's reaction.)

疑问句:
Do you cringe when you hear that song? = 你听到那首歌时会感到尴尬吗？
Would you cringe if someone told that joke? = 如果有人讲那个笑话，你会感到尴尬吗？

否定句:
I didn't cringe at his performance. = 我对他表演并不感到尴尬。
She didn't cringe when he told her the truth. = 当他告诉她真相时，她并没有感到尴尬。
