prewitnessed:/ˌpriːˈwɪtnɪst/ | v. | 预先目击；事先见证 | To have witnessed something before a particular event or time.  This word is a relatively rare and newly coined word, not found in standard dictionaries. It combines "pre-" (before) and "witnessed". |例句：The detective prewitnessed the suspect's alibi, finding inconsistencies.  侦探预先目击了嫌疑人的不在场证明，发现了不一致之处。

The jury prewitnessed the crucial evidence before the trial began. 陪审员在审判开始前预先目击了关键证据。|近义词：foreseen, anticipated, observed beforehand |反义词：postwitnessed (a newly coined antonym, not in standard dictionaries)
