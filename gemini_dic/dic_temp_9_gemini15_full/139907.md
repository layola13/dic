ode:/oʊd/ | n. |颂歌，诗歌 |A lyric poem, typically one of elaborate or irregular form and expressing exalted or enthusiastic emotion.  It often praises a person, event, or thing. |Example: Keats' "Ode to a Nightingale" is a famous example of the ode form.  济慈的《夜莺颂》是颂歌体裁的著名例子。|近义词：poem, hymn, lyric |反义词：prose


USAGE->
This word is a noun, and thus does not conjugate through tenses like verbs.  The usage focuses on its function in a sentence.

Subject of a sentence:
The ode was beautifully written. 这首颂歌写得很美。

Object of a sentence:
He recited an ode to the fallen soldiers. 他朗诵了一首献给阵亡士兵的颂歌。

Appositive:
Keats' famous work, an ode to a Grecian urn, is studied widely. 济慈的著名作品，一首献给希腊花瓶的颂歌，被广泛研究。

Part of a prepositional phrase:
The book contained several odes to nature. 这本书包含几首关于自然的颂歌。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly using "ode" as a core component.  The word is typically used independently to refer to a type of poem.
