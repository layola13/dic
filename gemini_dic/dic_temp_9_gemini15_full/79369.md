gley:/ɡleɪ/| n. |灰化土，灰壤|A type of soil characterized by a greyish or bluish-grey colour due to the reduction of iron compounds. This colouration is typically caused by waterlogging and poor drainage, leading to anaerobic conditions. Gley soils are often found in poorly drained areas such as wetlands, floodplains, and depressions. They are typically low in organic matter and can be difficult to cultivate.|The field was characterized by gley soil, making it unsuitable for growing most crops. 这块田地以灰化土为特征，使其不适合种植大多数作物。|黏土，沼泽土|无|名词
