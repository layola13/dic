liveth:/ˈlɪvɪθ/| v.|活著；生存|The archaic third-person singular present indicative form of the verb "to live."  It emphasizes the state of being alive, often in a more poetic or formal context.  It is rarely used in modern English.|例句：And the Lord God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man liveth.  = 上帝用地上的尘土造人，又将生气吹在他鼻孔里，他就活了。|近义词：lives, exists, survives|反义词：dies, perishes|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He lives a simple life. = 他过着简朴的生活。
The tree lives for many years. = 这棵树活了很多年。

一般过去时:
The old man lived a long and happy life. = 那位老人过着漫长而幸福的生活。
She lived in London for ten years. = 她曾在伦敦住了十年。

现在完成时:
He has lived in this house for twenty years. = 他在这栋房子里住了二十年。
She has lived a remarkable life. = 她过着非凡的生活。

过去完成时:
Before he died, he had lived a full life. = 他死前过着充实的生活。
They had lived in Paris before moving to New York. = 他们在搬到纽约之前住在巴黎。

将来时:
I will live in the countryside one day. = 我有朝一日会住在乡下。
He will live to see his grandchild. = 他将活到看到他的孙子。

虚拟语气:
If I lived in a castle, I would be very happy. = 如果我住在城堡里，我会非常开心。
I wish I lived closer to my family. = 我希望我能离我的家人更近一些。

被动语态:
(This verb form is rarely used in the passive voice due to its archaic nature.)

疑问句:
Does he live here? = 他住在这里吗？
Where do they live? = 他们住在哪里？


否定句:
He does not live here. = 他不住在这里。
She didn't live in the city. = 她不住在城市里。
