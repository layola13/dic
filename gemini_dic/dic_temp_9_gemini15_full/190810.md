savvy:/ˈsævi/| adj., n.|精明的，有见识的；知识，专门知识|Having or showing practical knowledge and good judgment.  |例句：She's a savvy businesswoman.  她是一位精明的女商人。
She has a savvy for investing. 她有投资方面的专门知识。|近义词：shrewd, astute, clever, knowledgeable|反义词：naive, inexperienced, unintelligent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is savvy about technology. = 他对科技很精明。
She is savvy in the art market. = 她精通艺术市场。

一般过去时:
He was savvy enough to avoid the scam. = 他足够精明，避免了骗局。
They were savvy investors. = 他们是精明的投资者。

现在完成时:
She has become savvy in her field. = 她在她所在的领域变得精明了。
He has always been savvy about money. = 他一直很精明地处理钱财。

过去完成时:
They had been savvy enough to anticipate the problem. = 他们已经足够精明地预料到了这个问题。
She had been savvy in her career choices. = 她在她职业生涯的选择上一直很精明。

将来时:
He will be savvy enough to handle the situation. = 他将足够精明地处理这种情况。
She will become very savvy in business. = 她将在商业方面变得非常精明。

虚拟语气:
If I were savvy, I would have invested in that company. = 如果我精明的话，我就会投资那家公司。
I wish I were more savvy about finance. = 我希望我对财务更精明。

被动语态:
The strategy was deemed savvy by the experts. = 专家们认为这个策略很精明。
His decisions are often considered savvy. = 他的决定常常被认为是精明的。

疑问句:
Is he savvy enough to lead the team? = 他够精明来领导团队吗？
Are you savvy about computers? = 你对电脑很精明吗？

否定句:
He isn't savvy enough to understand the complexities. = 他不够精明，无法理解这些复杂性。
She wasn't savvy about the local customs. = 她对当地习俗不了解。
