pressurising: /ˈprɛʃəraɪzɪŋ/ | v. |施加压力，使…承受压力 | To put pressure on someone or something to make them do something.  |例句：The government is pressurising the company to clean up the pollution. 政府正向该公司施压，要求其清理污染。

例句：They are pressurising me to make a quick decision. 他们正逼迫我迅速做出决定。


近义词：pressuring, coercing, compelling, influencing


反义词：supporting, encouraging, assisting


词性:动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The manager is pressurising the team to meet the deadline. = 经理正在向团队施压，要求他们按时完成任务。
They are constantly pressurising him to work overtime. = 他们不断地给他施压，让他加班。

一般过去时:
The landlord pressurised the tenant to pay the rent. = 房东向房客施压，要求他支付房租。
She pressurised her son to go to medical school. = 她强迫儿子去读医学院。


现在完成时:
They have been pressurising the government for months. = 他们几个月来一直在向政府施压。
The company has pressurised its suppliers to lower prices. = 公司一直在向供应商施压，要求降低价格。


过去完成时:
He had pressurised his colleagues into accepting his plan. = 他此前已向同事施压，迫使他们接受他的计划。
The board had pressurised the CEO to resign. = 董事会此前已向首席执行官施压，迫使其辞职。


将来时:
The union will pressurise the company for better wages. = 工会将向公司施压，争取更高的工资。
They will pressurise him to admit his mistake. = 他们将向他施压，让他承认错误。


虚拟语气:
If they pressurised him any harder, he would quit. = 如果他们再对他施加更大的压力，他会辞职的。
I wish they wouldn't pressurise me so much. = 我希望他们不要给我这么大的压力。


被动语态:
The employees felt pressurised into working overtime. = 员工感到被迫加班。
He was pressurised into accepting the job. = 他被迫接受了这份工作。


疑问句:
Are they pressurising you to leave your job? = 他们是否在逼你辞职？
Is the government pressurising the banks to lend more money? = 政府是否在向银行施压，要求它们放出更多贷款？


否定句:
They did not pressurise him to sign the contract. = 他们没有强迫他签合同。
The manager didn't pressurise his team to work late. = 经理没有强迫他的团队加班。
