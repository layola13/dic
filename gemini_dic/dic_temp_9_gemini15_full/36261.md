coguardian:/ˌkoʊˈɡɑːrdʒən/| n. |共同监护人 |A person who shares the responsibility of guardianship with another person.  A co-guardian shares legal and physical custody of a child or other dependent. |Example: After the parents' death, the aunt and uncle became co-guardians of the child.  在父母去世后，阿姨和叔叔成为了孩子的共同监护人。|近义词：joint guardian, shared guardian |反义词：sole guardian


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are co-guardians of their niece. = 他们是他们侄女的共同监护人。
The couple act as co-guardians for their grandchildren. = 这对夫妇担任他们孙子的共同监护人。

一般过去时:
My aunt and uncle were co-guardians of me when my parents passed away. = 我父母去世后，我的阿姨和叔叔是我的共同监护人。
They served as co-guardians for several years. = 他们担任了几年的共同监护人。

现在完成时:
They have been co-guardians since 2020. = 自2020年以来，他们一直是共同监护人。
We have acted as co-guardians for many children over the years. = 多年来，我们一直担任许多孩子的共同监护人。

过去完成时:
Before the court hearing, they had already agreed to be co-guardians. = 在庭审之前，他们已经同意成为共同监护人。
The grandparents had been co-guardians for a short time before the parents regained custody. = 在父母重新获得监护权之前，祖父母曾短暂地担任共同监护人。

将来时:
They will be co-guardians of the child after the adoption is finalized. = 收养手续完成后，他们将成为孩子的共同监护人。
The court will appoint them as co-guardians of the estate. = 法院将任命他们为遗产的共同监护人。

虚拟语气:
If they were co-guardians, things would be different. = 如果他们是共同监护人，情况就会有所不同。
I wish my parents had appointed co-guardians. = 我希望我父母当时指定了共同监护人。

被动语态:
The siblings were appointed as co-guardians by the court. = 兄弟姐妹被法院任命为共同监护人。
Co-guardians are often chosen when parents are unable to care for their children. = 当父母无法照顾孩子时，通常会选择共同监护人。

疑问句:
Are they co-guardians of the twins? = 他们是双胞胎的共同监护人吗？
Who will be the co-guardians after the divorce? = 离婚后谁将成为共同监护人？

否定句:
They are not co-guardians; one is the sole guardian. = 他们不是共同监护人；一人是唯一监护人。
She was not appointed as a co-guardian in the case. = 在此案中，她未被任命为共同监护人。
