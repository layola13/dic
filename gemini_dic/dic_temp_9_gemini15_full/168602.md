prochronism:/ˈproʊkrɒnɪzəm/ | n. | 年代错误；时代错误；时间上的倒错 | The placement of an event, object, custom, etc., in a period to which it does not belong.  Anachronism in time.  For example, depicting a character using a cell phone in a historical drama set in the 18th century is a prochronism. | Example: The appearance of a modern plastic bottle in a film set in ancient Rome is a clear prochronism.  The use of electric lights in a painting depicting a medieval scene is a prochronism.| 近义词: anachronism | 反义词:  None specifically, but could be considered the opposite of accurate historical representation. | noun
