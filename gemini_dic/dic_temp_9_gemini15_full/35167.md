clownish:/ˈklaʊnɪʃ/| adj.|小丑似的；滑稽的；笨拙的|  Suggestive of a clown; clumsy or awkward.  The adjective "clownish" describes someone or something that is silly, clumsy, or awkward in a way that is amusing rather than offensive. It often implies a lack of grace or coordination. |例句：His clownish attempts at dancing made everyone laugh. 他笨拙的舞蹈逗笑了所有人。

例句：The clownish behavior of the politician during the debate damaged his credibility.  这位政治家在辩论中滑稽的行为损害了他的信誉。


近义词：buffoonish, comical, ludicrous, silly, clumsy, awkward|反义词：graceful, elegant, refined, polished, skillful

词源:  Derived from "clown," ultimately from the Latin word "colonus," meaning "peasant" or "rustic."  Over time, "clown" came to refer to a performer who engaged in buffoonery and clowning.  The suffix "-ish" adds a quality or characteristic.
