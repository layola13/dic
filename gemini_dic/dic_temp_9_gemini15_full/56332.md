dogmatize:/ˈdɒɡmətaɪz/|/ˈdɑːɡmətaɪz/| vt.| 武断地断言；教条式地宣称 | To express opinions or beliefs as if they were certainly true, without considering other opinions.  |例句：He dogmatized about the superiority of his own ideas. 他武断地宣称自己的想法优越。
例句：Don't dogmatize; there are many different perspectives on this issue. 不要武断；这个问题有很多不同的观点。|近义词：assert, affirm, preach, insist |反义词：doubt, question, consider, ponder |动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dogmatizes about politics. = 他对政治问题妄加断论。
They dogmatize on matters of faith. = 他们在信仰问题上武断地宣称。


一般过去时:
She dogmatized about the benefits of her diet. = 她武断地宣称她的饮食习惯有很多好处。
The speaker dogmatized throughout his presentation. = 演讲者在整个演讲中都表现得很武断。


现在完成时:
He has dogmatized on this topic for years. = 他多年来一直对这个话题妄加断论。
Scientists have dogmatized less since the advent of the scientific method. = 自科学方法出现以来，科学家们已经减少了武断的言论。


过去完成时:
He had dogmatized for so long that no one dared to contradict him. = 他武断地宣称了这么久，以至于没有人敢反驳他。
They had dogmatized about the effectiveness of the treatment before any clinical trials were conducted. = 在进行任何临床试验之前，他们就对治疗的有效性妄加断论。


将来时:
She will dogmatize again at the next meeting. = 她将在下一次会议上再次武断地宣称。
He will likely dogmatize about his investment strategies. = 他很可能会对他投资策略妄加断论。


虚拟语气:
If he dogmatized less, people would listen to him more. = 如果他不那么武断，人们就会更愿意听他的话。
I wish he wouldn't dogmatize so much. = 我希望他不要那么武断。


被动语态:
His ideas were dogmatized by his followers. = 他的想法被他的追随者们奉为圭臬。
The theory was dogmatized and accepted without question. = 这一理论被武断地宣称并毫无疑问地被接受。


疑问句:
Does he always dogmatize in this way? = 他总是这样武断地宣称吗？
Why do they dogmatize about such unimportant matters? = 为什么他们要对这些无关紧要的事情妄加断论？


否定句:
He did not dogmatize; he presented a balanced perspective. = 他没有武断地断言；他提出了一个平衡的视角。
She refused to dogmatize on such a complex issue. = 她拒绝对这样一个复杂的问题妄加断论。


PHRASE->
dogmatize about = 对…妄加断论
dogmatize on = 对…武断地宣称
dogmatize concerning = 关于…武断地断言
dogmatize over = 对…过度坚持己见
dogmatize without evidence = 没有证据地武断地宣称


词源:
From Late Latin *dogmatizāre, from dogma ("opinion, doctrine").  Ultimately from Greek δόγμα (dógma, "opinion, doctrine").
