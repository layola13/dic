jacobitical:/dʒəˈkɒbɪtɪkəl/| adj.| 雅各布派的支持者；拥护詹姆斯二世的| Relating to or supporting the Jacobites, the supporters of James II and his heirs after the Glorious Revolution of 1688.  The term often implies a belief in the divine right of kings and a resistance to the Protestant succession. |The Jacobites staged several uprisings in an attempt to restore the Stuart monarchy.|近义词：Royalist (in this specific historical context), pro-Stuart|反义词：Whig, Hanoverian
