nominalist:/ˈnɒmɪnəlɪst/ | n. |名义论者；唯名论者 | A nominalist is someone who subscribes to nominalism, the philosophical doctrine that only individual things exist and that general or abstract concepts are merely names or labels applied to collections of similar individuals.  They deny the existence of universals (abstract concepts like "redness" or "justice"). | Example:  Many medieval philosophers were nominalists, arguing against the realist view of universals.  许多中世纪哲学家都是唯名论者，反对实在论者的普遍性观点。 |近义词: conceptualist (有时，取决于语境)|反义词: realist
