poulter:/ˈpɔːltər/ | n. | 卖禽肉者；家禽屠宰商 | A poulter is a person who sells poultry and sometimes other meats.  The term is now largely archaic, though it might be encountered in historical contexts.  | Example: The poulter offered a wide selection of geese and ducks for the upcoming feast.  卖禽肉者提供各种鹅和鸭子以备即将到来的盛宴。 | 近义词：butcher (in a broader sense), poultry seller | 反义词：无  | 名词

词源:
The word "poulter" comes from the Middle English word "poulter," which in turn derives from the Old French word "poultier," meaning "poulterer" or "dealer in poultry."  The ultimate origin is likely from the Latin word "pullus," meaning "chick" or "young bird."
