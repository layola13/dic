echopathy:/ˈɛkəʊpæθi/| n. |模仿言语行为|The pathological repetition of words or phrases spoken by another person.  This is often observed in individuals with certain neurological or psychiatric conditions, such as echolalia in autism spectrum disorder.  It involves involuntary mirroring of another's speech or actions, not necessarily indicative of understanding or agreement. |Example:  The child exhibited echopathy, repeating every sentence the therapist uttered.  孩子表现出模仿言语行为，重复治疗师说的每一句话。|近义词：echolalia, mimicry|反义词：originality, independence
