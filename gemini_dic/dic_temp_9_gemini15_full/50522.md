depth-of-focus: /ˌdɛpθ əv ˈfoʊkəs/ | n. | 景深 | The distance range within which objects in a photograph appear acceptably sharp.  It's determined by the lens aperture, focal length, and the distance to the subject. A large depth of field means a large range of distances appear sharp, while a shallow depth of field means only a narrow range appears sharp. | The photographer carefully adjusted the aperture to achieve a shallow depth of field, blurring the background and highlighting the subject.  摄影师仔细调整光圈以获得浅景深，使背景模糊并突出主题。|  depth of field, range of focus | shallow depth of field, narrow depth of field | noun
