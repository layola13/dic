cotransporting:/ˌkoʊtrænsˈpɔːrtɪŋ/ | v. | 协同运输 | The process where two or more substances are transported across a biological membrane simultaneously, often with one substance's movement driving the other's.  This often involves membrane proteins that couple the transport of one molecule to the transport of another. | Example:  Sodium ions are cotransported with glucose across the intestinal epithelium. 钠离子与葡萄糖一起通过肠上皮细胞进行协同运输。|近义词：symport, coupled transport |反义词：antiport (countertransport)
