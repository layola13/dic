insulted:/ɪnˈsʌltɪd/| v. |侮辱；冒犯|To offend someone by saying or doing something that is rude or disrespectful.  The act of insulting someone; an offense to someone's dignity. |例句：He felt insulted by her remarks. 他觉得她的评论很侮辱人。

例句：I was deeply insulted by his accusations. 我被他指控的话深深地侮辱了。


近义词：offended, affronted, humiliated, disgraced, maligned

反义词：complimented, praised, flattered


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He insults his colleagues regularly. 他经常侮辱他的同事。
She insults people who disagree with her. 她侮辱与她意见相左的人。


一般过去时:
He insulted me in front of everyone. 他当着所有人的面侮辱我。
She insulted the waiter for a small mistake. 她因为一个小的错误而侮辱了服务员。


现在完成时:
They have insulted me repeatedly. 他们一再侮辱我。
He has insulted his boss, and now he is in trouble. 他侮辱了他的老板，现在他麻烦大了。


过去完成时:
I had been insulted several times before I finally reacted. 在我最终做出反应之前，我已经多次受到侮辱。
She had insulted him so many times that he decided to quit his job. 她已经侮辱了他很多次，以至于他决定辞职。


将来时:
He will insult her again if given the chance. 如果有机会，他会再次侮辱她。
She will probably insult him if he criticizes her work. 如果他批评她的工作，她可能会侮辱他。


虚拟语气:
If he insulted me again, I would report him. 如果他再次侮辱我，我会向有关部门举报他。
I wish he hadn't insulted my family. 我希望他没有侮辱我的家人。


被动语态:
He was insulted by the rude customer. 他被粗鲁的顾客侮辱了。
The politician was insulted during the debate. 这位政治家在辩论中受到了侮辱。


疑问句:
Were you insulted by his comments? 你被他评论侮辱了吗？
Has she ever been insulted publicly? 她曾经在公众场合受过侮辱吗？


否定句:
I did not insult him; I simply stated my opinion. 我没有侮辱他；我只是陈述了我的观点。
She would never insult someone intentionally. 她绝不会故意侮辱别人。


PHRASE->

a gross insult 极大的侮辱
a personal insult 人身攻击
feel insulted 感到受辱
