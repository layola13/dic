outscolding:/ˌaʊtˈskoʊldɪŋ/| n.|大声斥责；严厉训斥|The act of scolding someone loudly and harshly.  It implies a more intense and public scolding than a simple rebuke.|例句：The teacher gave the student an outscolding for his disruptive behavior.  老师因学生的扰乱行为而严厉训斥了他。

例句：The manager's outscolding of the employee was overheard by everyone in the office.  经理对员工的严厉训斥被办公室里每个人都听到了。|近义词：rebuke, reprimand, berate, lambaste|反义词：praise, commend, compliment|词性:名词
