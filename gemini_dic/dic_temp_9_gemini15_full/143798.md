outoffice:/ˌaʊt əv ˈɒfɪs/ | adj. |不在办公室；外出；暂停办公|  Describes a person who is not currently at their workplace, or a business that is temporarily closed.  |例句：He's out of office until next week. 他下周才回办公室。  |近义词：away, absent, unavailable |反义词：in office, present, available |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is out of office today. = 他今天不在办公室。
She is out of office for a meeting. = 她出去开会了。


一般过去时:
He was out of office yesterday. = 他昨天不在办公室。
The office was out of office during the storm. = 暴风雨期间办公室暂停办公。


现在完成时:
She has been out of office since Monday. = 她从星期一开始就不在办公室了。
The company has been out of office for the holiday. = 公司因假期暂停办公。


过去完成时:
He had been out of office for a week before I called. = 我打电话之前他已经离开办公室一个星期了。
The store had been out of office all day. = 商店整天都暂停营业。


将来时:
He will be out of office next week. = 他下周不在办公室。
The bank will be out of office on Sundays. = 银行星期天休息。


虚拟语气:
If he were out of office, I wouldn't bother calling. = 如果他不办公室，我就不会费心打电话了。
I wish he weren't out of office right now. = 我希望他现在不在办公室。


被动语态:  (The phrase "out of office" is rarely used passively.)


疑问句:
Is he out of office? = 他不在办公室吗？
Was the shop out of office yesterday? = 商店昨天没开门吗？


否定句:
He is not out of office; he's just in a meeting. = 他没不在办公室，他只是在开会。
The store was not out of office yesterday. = 商店昨天没有关门。


PHRASE->

be out of office = 不在办公室
