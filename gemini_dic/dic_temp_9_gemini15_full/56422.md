dollarize:/ˈdɒləraɪz/| vt.|美元化；以美元计价|To adopt the United States dollar as the official currency of a country or region, or to conduct transactions predominantly in US dollars.|Example:  Ecuador dollarized its economy in 2000.  厄瓜多尔在2000年将经济美元化。|近义词：de-nationalize (currency), adopt the dollar|反义词：denationalize (in a different context)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government dollarizes the economy. = 政府使经济美元化。
They dollarize their transactions. = 他们用美元进行交易。


一般过去时:
Ecuador dollarized its currency. = 厄瓜多尔将货币美元化。
The company dollarized its accounts. = 公司将其账户美元化。


现在完成时:
Many countries have dollarized their economies. = 许多国家已经将经济美元化。
The region has largely dollarized its trade. = 该地区贸易已基本美元化。


过去完成时:
Before the crisis, they had already dollarized their savings. = 危机爆发前，他们已将储蓄美元化。
The country had dollarized its banking system years ago. = 该国几年前已将银行系统美元化。


将来时:
The nation will dollarize its currency next year. = 该国明年将货币美元化。
They will dollarize their international payments. = 他们将用美元支付国际款项。


虚拟语气:
If they dollarized their economy, it might stabilize. = 如果他们使经济美元化，它可能会稳定下来。
I wish we had dollarized our assets earlier. = 我希望我们早点将资产美元化。


被动语态:
The economy was dollarized under the new government. = 在新政府领导下，经济实现了美元化。
The transactions are being dollarized to facilitate trade. = 交易正在美元化以促进贸易。


疑问句:
Will the country dollarize its currency? = 该国会将货币美元化吗？
Have they dollarized their debts? = 他们已经将债务美元化了吗？


否定句:
The country did not dollarize its currency. = 该国没有将货币美元化。
They haven't yet fully dollarized their operations. = 他们的业务尚未完全美元化。


PHRASE->
