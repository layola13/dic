fantasising: /ˈfæntəsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 幻想；空想；编造 |  The act of indulging in fantasy; imagining or daydreaming.  | 例句：She spent hours fantasising about her dream wedding. 她花了几个小时幻想她梦想中的婚礼。


例句：He was fantasising about winning the lottery. 他在幻想赢得彩票。


近义词：daydreaming, dreaming, imagining, fantasizing


反义词：facing reality, being realistic


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fantasises about travelling the world. 他幻想环游世界。
She fantasises about becoming a famous writer. 她幻想成为一名著名作家。

一般过去时:
I fantasised about living in a castle. 我曾经幻想住在城堡里。
They fantasised about escaping their mundane lives. 他们曾经幻想逃离他们平淡的生活。

现在完成时:
She has fantasised about this moment for years. 她多年来一直幻想这一刻。
He has fantasised about owning a sports car. 他一直幻想拥有一辆跑车。

过去完成时:
I had fantasised about meeting her before I actually did. 在我真正遇到她之前，我一直幻想过和她见面。
They had fantasised about winning the competition before the results were announced. 在结果公布之前，他们一直幻想过赢得比赛。

将来时:
I will fantasise about my future success. 我将幻想我未来的成功。
She will fantasise about her upcoming vacation. 她将幻想她即将到来的假期。

虚拟语气:
If I had the money, I would fantasise less about travelling and more about doing it. 如果我有钱，我就会少幻想旅行，多去做。
I wish I could fantasise less about work and more about relaxation. 我希望我能少幻想工作，多想想放松。


被动语态:
His dreams were often fantasised about by his colleagues. 他的梦想经常被他的同事们幻想。 (This usage is less common)
The future is often fantasised about by young people. 未来经常被年轻人幻想。 (This usage is less common)


疑问句:
What are you fantasising about? 你在幻想什么？
What did he fantasise about during the meeting? 他在会议期间幻想了什么？


否定句:
I don't fantasise about unrealistic things. 我不会幻想不切实际的事情。
She didn't fantasise about her ex-boyfriend anymore. 她不再幻想她的前男友了。



PHRASE->

fantasise creatively: 富有创造性地幻想
