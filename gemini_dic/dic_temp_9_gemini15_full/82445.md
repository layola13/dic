grumblesome: /ˈɡrʌmbəlsəm/ | adj. |抱怨的；爱抱怨的；令人厌烦的 |  Characterized by grumbling or complaining; causing or tending to cause grumbling.  It describes something or someone that is prone to complaining or that evokes complaints. |


例句：

The grumblesome old man complained about the weather every day. =  那个爱抱怨的老头每天都抱怨天气。

Her grumblesome disposition made her difficult to be around. = 她爱抱怨的性格让她难以相处。


近义词：complaining, querulous, grumpy, irritable, crotchety

反义词： cheerful, pleasant, agreeable, amiable, good-natured


词源:

The word "grumblesome" is derived from the verb "grumble,"  which ultimately traces back to the Old Norse word "gromla," meaning "to growl" or "to murmur." The suffix "-some" is an Old English suffix that indicates "full of" or "tending to."  Therefore, "grumblesome" literally means "full of grumbling" or "having a tendency to grumble."
