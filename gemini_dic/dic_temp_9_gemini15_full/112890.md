ludendorf:/ˈluːdənˌdɔːrf/| n. |卢登道夫| Erich Ludendorff was a German general during World War I, known for his extreme militarism and influence on the German High Command.  He played a key role in the Schlieffen Plan and advocated for unrestricted submarine warfare.  After the war, he became involved in far-right politics and played a role in the Beer Hall Putsch.| Erich Ludendorff, a prominent German general during World War I, is remembered for his aggressive military strategies and influence on the German government.  His actions significantly impacted the course of the war. |近义词：general, military leader |反义词：pacifist
