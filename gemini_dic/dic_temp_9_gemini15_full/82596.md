guardianship: /ˈɡɑːrdɪənʃɪp/ | n. |监护权；保护；守护 | The legal right and responsibility to care for a person (usually a minor or someone incapacitated) and manage their affairs.  It involves making decisions on their behalf regarding their welfare, education, and property.  |例句：The court awarded guardianship of the child to her aunt. 法院判决孩子的监护权归她阿姨。

例句：He felt a strong sense of guardianship towards his younger siblings. 他对他的弟弟妹妹有一种强烈的保护感。


近义词：custody, protection, care, wardship | 反义词：neglect, abandonment
