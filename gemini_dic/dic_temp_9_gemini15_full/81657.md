green-around-the-gills: /ˌɡriːn əˈraʊnd ðə ɡɪlz/ | adj. |脸色苍白的，病态的，吓得脸色发青的 |Pale, sickly looking, often from fear or illness.  The phrase originates from the appearance of fish gills when they are removed from water and begin to die; they turn a greenish color due to lack of oxygen.  This visual analogy is transferred to the pallor of a person's face when they are unwell or frightened. | Example:  He looked green around the gills after hearing the bad news. 他听到坏消息后脸色苍白。 She went green around the gills when she saw the spider. 她看到蜘蛛后吓得脸色发青。| 近义词：pale, sickly, ashen, wan, deathly pale | 反义词：rosy, flushed, healthy-looking | 词性：形容词
