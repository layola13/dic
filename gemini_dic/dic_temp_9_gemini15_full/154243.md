periscope:/ˈpɛrɪskoʊp/ | n. |潜望镜 | An optical instrument for observation over, around, or through an obstacle, especially one consisting of a tube with mirrors or prisms at each end enabling a viewer to see an image of an object not directly in his line of sight.  It's commonly used in submarines to see above the surface of the water. | The submarine commander used the periscope to observe the enemy ship.  潜艇指挥官用潜望镜观察敌舰。| 近义词：viewer, observation device | 反义词：无

词源:

The word "periscope" is derived from the Greek words "peri" (around, about) and "skopein" (to look, to see).  It literally means "to look around."
