ramped:/ræmpt/ | adj. | 斜坡的；倾斜的；加剧的 |  Steeply inclined; having a ramp or ramps.  Also used to describe something that has been significantly increased or intensified. |例句：The road was ramped, making it difficult to drive. 这条路是斜坡的，很难驾驶。 The pressure on the hospital ramped up during the flu season. 流感季节医院的压力加剧了。|近义词：sloped, inclined, escalated |反义词：level, flat


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The road ramps upward sharply. = 这条路急剧向上倾斜。
The company ramps up production. = 公司正在加大生产。


一般过去时:
They ramped up the marketing campaign. = 他们加大了市场营销力度。
The car ramped over the curb. = 汽车冲上了路沿。


现在完成时:
The government has ramped up its efforts to combat crime. = 政府加大了打击犯罪的力度。
The company has ramped up its social media presence. = 公司已经加强了其社交媒体的影响力。


过去完成时:
They had ramped up production before the holiday season. = 他们在大假之前就加大了生产力度。
The tension had ramped up before the announcement. = 在宣布之前，紧张气氛已经加剧了。


将来时:
We will ramp up the advertising budget next quarter. = 我们将在下个季度增加广告预算。
They will ramp up security measures for the event. = 他们将为活动加强安保措施。


虚拟语气:
If they ramped up production, they would meet the demand. = 如果他们加大生产，就能满足需求。
I wish they had ramped up the security earlier. = 我希望他们早点加强安保。


被动语态:
Production was ramped up to meet the increased demand. = 加大生产以满足日益增长的需求。
Security measures were ramped up after the incident. = 事故发生后，加强了安全措施。


疑问句:
Has the company ramped up its hiring process? = 公司是否加快了招聘流程？
Will they ramp up the volume of their music? = 他们会加大音乐音量吗？


否定句:
They did not ramp up production as quickly as expected. = 他们的生产并没有像预期的那样迅速增加。
The government didn't ramp up its response to the crisis. = 政府没有加强对危机的应对。
