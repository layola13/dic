slave-trading: /ˈsleɪvˌtreɪdɪŋ/ | n. |奴隶贸易 | The practice of buying and selling humans as property.  It involved the forced transportation and enslavement of people, often across vast distances, for labor purposes. This horrific practice has left a lasting scar on human history, causing immense suffering and shaping social and political structures in many parts of the world.  | Example: The transatlantic slave trade was a brutal and inhumane system that lasted for centuries. = 横跨大西洋的奴隶贸易是一个残酷且不人道的制度，持续了几个世纪。

The triangular trade, a prominent example of slave-trading, involved transporting enslaved Africans to the Americas in exchange for goods like sugar and rum. = 三角贸易是奴隶贸易的一个突出例子，它涉及到将非洲奴隶运送到美洲，以换取糖和朗姆酒等商品。

近义词：human trafficking, chattel slavery (depending on context) | 反义词： abolition of slavery, human rights
