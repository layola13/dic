calabashes:/kəˈlæbæʃɪz/ | n. |葫芦，葫芦科植物 |The word "calabashes" refers to the dried, hard shells of certain types of gourds or pumpkins, often used as containers or utensils.  It can also refer to the plants themselves.  The plural form is used because it refers to multiple gourds or their shells.|例句：The villagers used calabashes to store water. 村民们用葫芦盛水。

例句：Many traditional African instruments are made from calabashes. 许多非洲传统乐器是用葫芦做的。


近义词：gourds, pumpkins (in some contexts) |反义词：None specifically, as it describes a type of object. |词性：名词


词源:

The word "calabash" originates from the Spanish word "calabaza," which ultimately comes from the Arabic word "qal'abas."  These words all refer to various types of gourds and pumpkins.  The English word entered the language through Spanish and Portuguese colonial influences.
