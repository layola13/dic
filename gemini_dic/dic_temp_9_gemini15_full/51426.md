detest:/dɪˈtɛst/| vt.|厌恶，憎恨|To dislike intensely; feel strong aversion for.|例句：I detest violence. 我厌恶暴力。  I detest liars. 我讨厌说谎的人。|近义词：abhor, loathe, hate|反义词：adore, love|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I detest his behavior. = 我讨厌他的行为。
She detests loud music. = 她讨厌吵闹的音乐。

一般过去时:
He detested the food. = 他讨厌那食物。
They detested the long journey. = 他们讨厌漫长的旅程。

现在完成时:
I have detested spinach since I was a child. = 从小我就讨厌菠菜。
She has detested him ever since that incident. = 从那件事起她就一直讨厌他。

过去完成时:
I had detested him for years before I finally told him. = 在我最终告诉他之前，我已经讨厌他多年了。
She had detested the job long before she quit. = 在她辞职很久以前，她就讨厌这份工作了。

将来时:
I will detest this task until it's finished. = 在这项任务完成之前，我都会讨厌它。
They will detest the new policy if it's implemented. = 如果这项新政策实施，他们将会讨厌它。

虚拟语气:
I would detest having to do that again. = 我不愿再次做那件事。
If I knew he was like that, I would have detested him from the start. = 如果我知道他那样，我一开始就会讨厌他。

被动语态:
His arrogance is detested by everyone. = 他的傲慢受到所有人的厌恶。
The new rules are detested by the students. = 学生们讨厌新的规章制度。

疑问句:
Do you detest anything in particular? = 你特别讨厌什么吗？
Does she detest cats? = 她讨厌猫吗？

否定句:
I don't detest all vegetables. = 我并非讨厌所有蔬菜。
He doesn't detest his job, actually. = 事实上，他不讨厌他的工作。


PHRASE->
detest doing something: 讨厌做某事
detest the thought of: 讨厌想到…
detest someone intensely: 强烈厌恶某人
cannot detest: 不能讨厌
detest with a passion: 非常讨厌
