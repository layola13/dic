cottar:/ˈkɒtər/| n. |佃农，农舍居住者 | A cotter is a tenant farmer who occupies a small cottage or croft, typically holding a small plot of land.  They often work the land for a landowner in exchange for rent or a share of the produce.  The term is largely associated with Scotland and Ireland. |Example:  Many cotters in the Highlands relied on subsistence farming. 许多高地地区的佃农依靠自给自足的农业。 The cotter's family lived a simple life in their small cottage.  佃农一家住在他们的小农舍里过着简朴的生活。 |近义词：tenant farmer, crofter, smallholder |反义词：landowner, landlord
