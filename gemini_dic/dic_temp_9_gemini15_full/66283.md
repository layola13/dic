excommunication:/ˌɛksˌkɒmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |逐出教籍；开除教籍 | The formal act of excluding a person from a religious community, especially the Christian Church.  This often involves the denial of certain sacraments and privileges. | The priest performed the excommunication, barring the heretic from the church.  牧师进行了逐出教籍仪式，将异教徒逐出教会。


The excommunication of Galileo was a significant event in the history of science. 加利略被逐出教籍是科学史上一个重要的事件。|近义词：exclusion, banishment, ostracism |反义词：inclusion, acceptance, reinstatement |名词

PHRASE->

threat of excommunication: 逐出教籍的威胁

词源:

From Late Latin *excommunicatio, from Latin ex- ("out of, from") + communicatio ("sharing, fellowship").  The word reflects the act of removing someone from the communion (fellowship) of the church.
