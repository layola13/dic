demonstrator:/ˈdɛmənˌstreɪtər/| n. |示威者；演示者；实验者| A person who participates in a public demonstration, typically to express political or social views.  A person who shows how something works or is used. |例句：The demonstrators marched through the city streets. 示威者游行穿过城市街道。 The sales assistant was a good demonstrator, showing us how to use the new machine. 销售助理是一位很好的演示者，向我们展示了如何使用新机器。|近义词：protester,exhibitor,presenter|反义词：observer


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The demonstrators protest every Saturday. = 示威者每周六都抗议。
He demonstrates the new software to clients. = 他向客户演示新软件。


一般过去时:
The demonstrators protested against the new law. = 示威者抗议新法律。
She demonstrated the technique flawlessly. = 她完美地演示了这项技术。


现在完成时:
The group has demonstrated its commitment to change. = 该团体已经证明了其致力于变革的承诺。
He has demonstrated his skills many times. = 他已经多次展示了他的技能。


过去完成时:
The demonstrators had already assembled before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，示威者已经集结完毕。
She had demonstrated the procedure several times before the presentation. = 在演示之前，她已经多次演示过这个程序。


将来时:
The demonstrators will march on Washington next week. = 示威者下周将游行到华盛顿。
He will demonstrate the product at the trade show. = 他将在贸易展上展示该产品。


虚拟语气:
If they demonstrated more effectively, they might achieve their goals. = 如果他们更有效地进行示威，他们可能会实现他们的目标。
I wish he had demonstrated a better understanding of the issue. = 我希望他对这个问题有更好的理解。


被动语态:
The new technology was demonstrated to the board of directors. = 新技术向董事会进行了演示。
The effectiveness of the method was demonstrated by the results. = 结果证明了该方法的有效性。


疑问句:

否定句:

He did not demonstrate the correct method. = 他没有演示正确的方法。
