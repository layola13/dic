high-flying:/ˈhaɪˌflaɪɪŋ/| adj.| 成功的；飞黄腾达的；高调的 |  Ambitious and successful, often implying a fast rise to a prominent position.  It can also suggest a flamboyant or showy lifestyle. |例句：He's a high-flying executive at a major corporation. 他是大型公司里一位飞黄腾达的高管。

例句：Her high-flying career took her all over the world. 她辉煌的职业生涯带她走遍了世界各地。


近义词：successful, ambitious, prominent, upwardly mobile, fast-rising

反义词：unsuccessful, struggling, low-level,  unambitious


词源:The term combines "high," meaning elevated or superior, with "flying," suggesting rapid upward movement and success.  The imagery evokes a soaring bird, quickly gaining altitude.  Its usage likely developed in the mid-20th century, reflecting the post-war boom and the increasing emphasis on career advancement.
