rankling: /ˈræŋklɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人恼火的；令人苦恼的 |  causing irritation or resentment;  a feeling of persistent annoyance or irritation. |例句：The injustice of it all was rankling.  这其中的不公正令人非常恼火。

The constant criticism was rankling him.  持续不断的批评让他感到苦恼。

近义词：irritating, annoying, galling, chafing, vexing | 反义词：soothing, comforting, pleasing


词源:  From Middle English *ranklen, from Old North French rancler "to fester, to rankle," from ranc "rancid, foul."  The sense development is from a physical festering to a mental irritation.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The injustice rankles. = 不公正令人恼火。
His constant complaints are rankling me. = 他不断的抱怨让我很烦。


一般过去时:
The insult rankled for days. = 侮辱让他恼火了好几天。
Her rejection rankled deeply. = 她的拒绝让他深深地苦恼。


现在完成时:
That slight has been rankling ever since. = 从那以后，那个轻蔑一直让我耿耿于怀。
His words have rankled in my mind. = 他的话一直在我脑子里挥之不去。


过去完成时:
I had been rankling over the unfair treatment for weeks. = 我已经因为不公平的待遇而苦恼了好几个星期了。
She had rankled over the lost opportunity for months. = 她已经因为错失的机会而苦恼了好几个月了。


将来时:
This unfair judgment will rankle for years to come. = 这种不公平的判决将在未来几年内持续让人恼火。
His betrayal will rankle long after he is gone. = 即使他走了很久，他的背叛仍然会让人耿耿于怀。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't been so stubborn, it wouldn't have rankled so much. = 如果我不那么固执，就不会那么恼火了。
I wish that his words hadn't rankled me so deeply. = 我希望他的话没有让我如此苦恼。


被动语态:
He was rankled by the constant interruptions. = 他被不断的打断弄得很烦。
She was rankled by the perceived injustice. = 她为被认为的不公正而苦恼。


疑问句:
Does this injustice rankle you? = 这不公正的事情让你恼火吗？
Is his behavior rankling you? = 他的行为让你苦恼吗？


否定句:
The decision didn't rankle him as much as he thought it would. = 这个决定并没有像他想象的那样让他恼火。
The criticism didn't rankle her; she simply ignored it. = 批评并没有让她苦恼；她只是忽略了它。
