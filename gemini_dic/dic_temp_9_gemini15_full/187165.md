roadrunners: /ˈroʊdˌrʌnərz/ | n. |走鹃（鸟类）|A type of fast-running bird of the cuckoo family, known for its long legs and ability to run at high speeds.  They are often depicted in cartoons as being pursued by coyotes.| 例句：Roadrunners are known for their incredible speed. = 走鹃以其惊人的速度而闻名。

Roadrunners are native to the southwestern United States and Mexico. = 走鹃原产于美国西南部和墨西哥。


近义词：暂无完全相同的近义词，可根据语境用其他快速奔跑的动物代替。|反义词：暂无


词源:  The name "roadrunner" is a descriptive term combining "road," referring to their habitat often near roads, and "runner," highlighting their running ability. The scientific name likely reflects their taxonomic classification within the cuckoo family.
