paynim:/ˈpeɪnɪm/| n. |异教徒；非基督徒 |A person who is not a Christian; a pagan.  The term is archaic and often carries a negative connotation.  It was frequently used during the Crusades and in medieval literature to refer to Muslims and other non-Christians.| Example: The crusaders saw the paynim as infidels and enemies of God.  十字军将异教徒视为异教徒和上帝的敌人。|近义词：infidel, pagan, heathen |反义词：Christian, believer
