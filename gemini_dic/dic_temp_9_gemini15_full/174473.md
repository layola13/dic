quinism:/ˈkwɪnɪzəm/| n.|奎宁中毒；奎宁主义|Quinism refers to a condition caused by excessive intake of quinine, a drug derived from the bark of the cinchona tree. Symptoms can include tinnitus (ringing in the ears), hearing loss, visual disturbances, and gastrointestinal issues. In historical context, "quinism" sometimes referred to a set of beliefs or practices centered around quinine's supposed medicinal properties, often to an excessive or unfounded degree.  |例句：Prolonged use of quinine for malaria treatment resulted in quinism, manifesting as severe tinnitus.  长期使用奎宁治疗疟疾导致奎宁中毒，表现为严重的耳鸣。|近义词：quinine toxicity, quinine poisoning|反义词：N/A
