kiva:/ˈkiːvə/| n.|基瓦（美国西南部落的地下室）|A kiva is a room, typically underground or partially underground, used by Puebloan peoples for religious ceremonies.  Kivas are often circular or square and have a sipapu, a small opening in the floor symbolizing the emergence of the people from the underworld. |Example: The archaeologist carefully documented the features of the ancient kiva, noting the position of the sipapu and the presence of painted murals.  考古学家仔细记录了古代基瓦的特点，注意到了西帕普的位置和壁画的存在。|近义词：ceremonial chamber, ritual room|反义词：None|名词
