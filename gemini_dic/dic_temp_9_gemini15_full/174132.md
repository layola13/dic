queasier:/ˈkwiːziər/| adj.|更令人作呕的；更令人不安的；更不舒服的 |More nauseous; more uneasy; more uncomfortable.  The rough boat ride made me feel queasier than I had been before.  颠簸的船程让我感觉比以前更恶心。|近义词：nauseated, uneasy, sick, unwell |反义词：comfortable, well, at ease

词源:
Queasier is the comparative form of the adjective queasy, meaning nauseated or uneasy.  It comes from the Middle English *queisy*, possibly related to the Old Norse *kvesa*, meaning to compress or squeeze, suggesting a feeling of tightness or constriction in the stomach associated with nausea.
