setiparous:/ˌsɛtɪˈpærəs/| adj.|胎生的|Producing young alive, rather than laying eggs.  This term is primarily used in zoology and biology to describe animals that give birth to live young.  The offspring develop inside the mother's body, receiving nourishment through a placenta or other similar structure.|例句：Most mammals are setiparous, giving birth to live young.  大多数哺乳动物是胎生的，产下活的幼崽。|例句：Unlike oviparous reptiles, many snakes are actually setiparous in certain species. 与卵生的爬行动物不同，某些种类的蛇实际上是胎生的。|近义词：viviparous|反义词：oviparous
