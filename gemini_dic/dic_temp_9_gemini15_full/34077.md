civilises: /ˈsɪvəlaɪzɪz/ | v. | 使文明；使开化 | to bring (a person or place) to a more advanced state of social development and organization.  | 例句：Education helps civilise societies. 教育有助于使社会文明。  | 例句：The city was civilised after centuries of conflict.  经过几个世纪的冲突后，这座城市变得文明了。 | 近义词：cultivate, refine, enlighten | 反义词：barbarize, brutalize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He civilises the barbarians. 他使野蛮人文明。
She civilises the remote village. 她使偏远村庄文明。

一般过去时:
The Roman Empire civilised much of Europe. 罗马帝国使欧洲大部分地区文明。
They civilised the local tribes. 他们使当地部落文明。

现在完成时:
Education has civilised many societies. 教育使许多社会文明。
The government has civilised the country through infrastructure development. 政府通过基础设施建设使国家文明。

过去完成时:
The missionaries had civilised the island before the colonisers arrived. 传教士在殖民者到来之前使岛屿文明。
They had civilised the region through trade and cultural exchange. 他们通过贸易和文化交流使该地区文明。

将来时:
We will civilise the planet through collaboration. 我们将通过合作使地球文明。
They will civilise the area with new schools and hospitals. 他们将通过新的学校和医院使该地区文明。

虚拟语气:
If we worked together, we could civilise the world. 如果我们一起努力，我们可以使世界文明。
I wish they had civilised the country sooner. 我希望他们早点使国家文明。

被动语态:
The tribes were civilised by the missionaries. 部落被传教士使文明。
The region was civilised by the influx of new ideas. 该地区因涌入新的思想而变得文明。

疑问句:
How can we civilise the underdeveloped areas? 我们如何使欠发达地区文明？
Will technology civilise humanity? 技术会使人类文明吗？

否定句:
The rulers did not civilise their people. 统治者没有使他们的人民文明。
They failed to civilise the savage tribes. 他们未能使野蛮部落文明。
