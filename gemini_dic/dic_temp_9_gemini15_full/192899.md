seatmate: /ˈsiːtmeɪt/ | n. | 同座，邻座 | A person sitting next to someone else, especially on a plane, train, or bus.  | Example: I struck up a conversation with my seatmate on the flight to London.  我与飞往伦敦航班上的邻座进行了交谈。| 近义词: companion (in a specific context), neighbor (in a less specific context) | 反义词:  None specifically, but antonyms could be related to concepts like "loneliness" or "isolation".

词源:

The word "seatmate" is a compound word formed from "seat" and "mate."  "Seat" refers to a place to sit, and "mate" in this context means companion or fellow passenger.  The word's origin is relatively modern, arising from the common experience of sharing a seat with someone on public transportation.
