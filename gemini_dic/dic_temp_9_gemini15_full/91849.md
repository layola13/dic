hyperhypocrisy:/ˌhaɪpərhaɪˈpɒkrəsi/ | n. |超级虚伪，高度伪善 |The term "hyperhypocrisy" refers to a level of hypocrisy that goes beyond typical insincerity or duplicity. It suggests a deliberate and often ostentatious display of moral superiority while simultaneously engaging in actions that directly contradict those professed values.  This often involves a public performance of virtue masking a private life of vice.  It’s a heightened, almost satirical form of hypocrisy. |The politician's speech about honesty was a prime example of hyperhypocrisy, given his history of corruption.  |近义词：extreme hypocrisy,  rank hypocrisy,  blatant hypocrisy |反义词：integrity, honesty, sincerity |名词
