confronting:/kənˈfrʌntɪŋ/| v. adj. |面对；对抗；使面临；令人不安的 |  to meet (someone) face to face, especially as a challenge; to deal with a difficult or unpleasant situation |例句：He confronted the thief and demanded his money back. 他面对小偷，要求他把钱还回来。  |例句：She was confronting a difficult decision. 她正面临一个艰难的决定。|近义词：facing, tackling, addressing, daunting |反义词：avoiding, ignoring, shunning


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He confronts his fears every day. = 他每天都直面自己的恐惧。
They are confronting the challenges of climate change. = 他们正在应对气候变化的挑战。


一般过去时:
She confronted her boss about the unfair treatment. = 她向老板投诉不公平待遇。
The police confronted the suspect with the evidence. = 警方用证据质问嫌疑人。


现在完成时:
We have confronted many obstacles in this project. = 我们在这个项目中遇到了许多障碍。
She has confronted her past traumas and is healing. = 她已经直面了过去的创伤，并且正在康复。


过去完成时:
He had confronted the problem before, so he knew how to handle it. = 他以前就遇到过这个问题，所以他知道该如何处理。
They had confronted the difficult truth, and it changed everything. = 他们直面了残酷的真相，这改变了一切。


将来时:
I will confront him about his lies tomorrow. = 我明天将要揭穿他的谎言。
They will confront the consequences of their actions. = 他们将要承担他们行为的后果。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would confront the issue head-on. = 如果我是你，我会直接面对这个问题。
I wish I had confronted him sooner. = 我真希望我早点和他对质。


被动语态:
The problem was confronted by the team with great determination. = 团队以极大的决心解决了这个问题。
The issue is being confronted by the government. = 政府正在处理这个问题。


疑问句:
How will you confront this difficult situation? = 你将如何应对这个困境？
Are they prepared to confront the challenges ahead? = 他们准备好迎接未来的挑战了吗？


否定句:
He didn't confront his feelings. = 他没有直面自己的感情。
She refused to confront the evidence against her. = 她拒绝面对对她不利的证据。
