drives:/draɪvz/| v. n.|驾驶；驱动；驱使；促使|To operate a vehicle, especially a car.  Also, to force or propel something forward; to be the motivating force behind something. As a noun, it refers to a journey or trip, and the way something is operating.|例句：He drives to work every day. 他每天开车上班。
She drives a fast car. 她开一辆快车。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drives a truck for a living. 他以开卡车为生。
She drives me crazy with her constant chatter. 她喋喋不休地让我抓狂。


一般过去时:
I drove to the beach yesterday. 我昨天开车去了海滩。
The storm drove the boat ashore. 暴风雨把船吹上了岸。


现在完成时:
He has driven across the country three times. 他已经三次横跨了全国。
Her ambition has driven her to great success. 她的雄心壮志让她取得了巨大的成功。


过去完成时:
I had driven for hours before I finally found a motel. 我开了几个小时的车才最终找到一家汽车旅馆。
They had driven themselves to exhaustion before they stopped working. 他们在停止工作之前已经累坏了自己。


将来时:
I will drive you to the airport tomorrow. 我明天会送你去机场。
The wind will drive the ships away from the coast. 风会把船吹离海岸。


虚拟语气:
If I had a car, I would drive to the mountains. 如果我有一辆车，我会开车去山上。
I wish I could drive a race car. 我真希望我能开赛车。


被动语态:
The car is driven by a skilled driver. 这辆车由一位熟练的司机驾驶。
The negotiations were driven by a desire for peace. 谈判是由对和平的渴望所驱动的。


疑问句:
Do you drive a car? 你会开车吗？
What drives your passion for art? 什么驱使你对艺术的热情？


否定句:
He doesn't drive anymore. 他不再开车了。
The engine isn't driven by electricity.  发动机不是由电力驱动的。


PHRASE->
drive someone crazy =  使某人发疯
drive someone to do something = 驱使某人做某事
drive-in movie = 汽车影院
drive somebody away = 把某人赶走
test drive = 试驾


近义词: operate, propel, motivate, impel
反义词:  (针对动词含义)  stop, halt, restrain; (针对名词含义)  walk, cycle


词性: verb (主要), noun (次要)
