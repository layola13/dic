counterscientific: /ˌkaʊntərˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk/ | adj. | 反科学的，违背科学的 | Opposing or rejecting the methods or findings of science.  This term describes ideas, theories, or practices that contradict established scientific principles or are not supported by scientific evidence. It often implies a deliberate disregard for scientific methodology or a preference for non-scientific explanations. |


例句：

His claims are counterscientific and lack any credible evidence.  他的说法是反科学的，缺乏任何可信的证据。

The theory is considered counterscientific by the majority of the scientific community.  大多数科学界人士认为该理论是反科学的。


近义词：unscientific, anti-scientific, pseudoscientific, irrational

反义词：scientific, evidence-based, rational


词源:

The word is a compound of "counter-" (meaning against or opposite) and "scientific" (relating to science).  The prefix "counter-" indicates opposition or contradiction.  "Scientific" derives from "science," ultimately from the Latin word "scientia," meaning "knowledge."
