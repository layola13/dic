firths:/fɜːθz/| n. |河口；海峡；峡湾 | A firth is a long, narrow inlet of the sea, typically with steep sides.  It's a type of estuary, often found in areas with glacial activity.  |例句：The cruise ship sailed through the majestic firth. 这艘游轮驶过雄伟的峡湾。

例句：The town is situated on the shores of a wide firth.  这个小镇坐落在一个宽阔的河口岸边。


近义词：estuary, inlet, bay, sound |反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with terms like "mountain range" or "open ocean". |词性:名词


词源:  The word "firth" comes from Old English *firþ, meaning "river," "stream," or "ford."  This is related to other Germanic words with similar meanings.  Over time, its meaning shifted to refer specifically to the type of narrow inlet of the sea we now associate with the term.
