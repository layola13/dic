reamputation:/ˌriːæmpjuˈteɪʃən/| n. | 再截肢 | The surgical removal of a limb or part of a limb that has already been amputated once.  This is often necessary due to infection, complications from the initial amputation, or persistent pain.  | Example:  The patient required a reamputation due to a severe infection in the residual limb.  = 由于残肢严重感染，病人需要再次截肢。  |近义词：second amputation, repeated amputation |反义词：limb reattachment, limb preservation
