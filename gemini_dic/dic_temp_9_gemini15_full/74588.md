freak:/friːk/| n., v., adj.|怪人；怪事；狂热爱好者；（使）狂热；极度兴奋；反常；畸形|

英文解析：
As a noun, "freak" refers to a person or thing that is strikingly unusual or abnormal. It can also mean a person with an unusual enthusiasm or interest in a particular subject.  As a verb, it means to become extremely excited or enthusiastic. As an adjective, it describes something unusual, abnormal, or bizarre.  The word carries a range of connotations, from mildly amusing to deeply offensive, depending on context.


例句：

名词用法(n.):
He's a real freak for vintage cars. = 他是个真正的古董车迷。

The storm caused some freak weather conditions. = 暴风雨造成了一些异常的天气状况。


动词用法(v.):
She freaked out when she saw the spider. = 她看到蜘蛛时吓坏了。

The crowd freaked out during the concert. = 音乐会期间人群陷入狂热。


形容词用法(adj.):
That's a freak accident. = 那是一场意外事故。

He had a freakishly tall height. = 他有着异常的高个子。

noun: oddball, weirdo, eccentric, anomaly, marvel, enthusiast

adjective: strange, unusual, bizarre, abnormal, extraordinary


反义词：

verb: calm down
adjective: normal, ordinary, typical
