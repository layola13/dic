dandling: /ˈdændlɪŋ/ | v. | 逗弄；轻摇；娇惯 |  The act of gently moving or tossing a baby or small child.  It can also imply a pampering or over-indulgent way of treating someone. |例句：The mother was dandling her baby to sleep. 妈妈轻轻地摇晃着婴儿哄他睡觉。

The grandmother spent hours dandling her grandchild. 奶奶花了几个小时逗弄她的孙子。


近义词：cooing, rocking, pampering, spoiling | 反义词：neglecting, ignoring


词源:  Comes from the Middle English word "dandlen," which likely originated from a combination of Old English words suggesting a gentle rocking or swaying motion.
