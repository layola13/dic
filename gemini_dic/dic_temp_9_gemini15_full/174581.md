quirk:/kwɜːrk/|n.|怪癖；奇特之处；意外；难题|An unusual habit or feature; a strange or unexpected event.|例句：He has a quirk of always wearing mismatched socks. 他有个怪癖，总是穿不配套的袜子。|例句：The quirk in the system caused a major delay. 系统中的故障导致了重大延误。|近义词：oddity, peculiarity, eccentricity|反义词：normality, regularity


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His quirk is his love for collecting stamps. = 他的怪癖是喜欢集邮。
That quirk always makes me laugh. = 那个怪癖总是让我发笑。

一般过去时:
A quirk in the software caused the crash. = 软件的一个小故障导致了崩溃。
The unexpected quirk delayed the project. = 意想不到的故障延迟了项目。

现在完成时:
That quirk has always been a part of his personality. = 那个怪癖一直都是他性格的一部分。
Several quirks have emerged during testing. = 在测试过程中出现了一些故障。

过去完成时:
I had never noticed that quirk before. = 我以前从未注意到那个怪癖。
The team had overlooked a crucial quirk in the design. = 团队忽略了设计中一个至关重要的缺陷。

将来时:
That quirk will likely cause problems later. = 那个怪癖可能会在以后造成问题。
We will address any quirks found during the beta testing. = 我们将在测试期间解决发现的任何故障。

虚拟语气:
If only that quirk didn't exist. = 要是没有那个怪癖就好了。
I wish that quirk would disappear. = 我希望那个怪癖能消失。

被动语态:
The problem was caused by a quirk in the code. = 这个问题是由代码中的一个缺陷引起的。
The unexpected result was attributed to a quirk of the experiment. = 出乎意料的结果被归因于实验的一个意外情况。

疑问句:
What's the quirk with the machine? = 这台机器有什么问题？
Is that a quirk of your personality? = 那是你个性中的一个怪癖吗？

否定句:
There is no quirk in the system. = 系统没有任何问题。
That's not a quirk; it's a feature. = 那不是一个缺陷；它是一个功能。
