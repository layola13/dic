regrafting:/ˌriːˈɡræftɪŋ/ | n. | 再嫁接；重新嫁接 | The process of grafting again, typically after a previous graft has failed or been removed.  This involves attaching a new scion (cutting) to a rootstock.  Regrafting is common in horticulture and can be used to improve the yield or quality of a plant, or to replace a diseased or damaged part. | Example:  The orchard owner had to perform regrafting on several apple trees after a harsh winter damaged the original grafts. = 果园主在严冬损坏了原来的接穗后，不得不对几棵苹果树进行再嫁接。


The roses needed regrafting after the disease wiped out the original graft. = 疾病摧毁了最初的嫁接后，玫瑰需要重新嫁接。|近义词：replant, regrow, replace (in context)| 反义词：graft removal, removal
