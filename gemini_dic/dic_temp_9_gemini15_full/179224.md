redispatching:/ˌriːˈdɪspætʃɪŋ/ | v. |重新调度，重新分配 | The act of sending something again, typically after it has been delayed or rerouted;  re-routing or reallocating resources.  This often refers to power grids, transportation, or communication systems. | Example:  The power company is redispatching electricity to meet peak demand.  = 供电公司正在重新分配电力以满足高峰需求。 The airline is redispatching the flight due to mechanical issues. = 由于机械故障，航空公司正在重新调度航班。 |近义词：rerouting, reallocating, rescheduling | 反义词：centralizing, consolidating |动词
