lucked:/lʌkt/| v.|运气好，侥幸成功|to have good luck; to succeed by chance|例句：I lucked out and got the last ticket. 我运气好，拿到了最后一张票。

例句：She lucked into a great job. 她幸运地得到了一份好工作。


近义词：succeeded, prospered| 反义词：failed, unluckily


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I luck into good things sometimes. = 我有时会幸运地得到好东西。
He often lucks out in his business deals. = 他在生意场上经常很幸运。


一般过去时:
We lucked out and found a parking space right away. = 我们很幸运，立刻找到了一个停车位。
They lucked into winning the lottery. = 他们幸运地中了彩票。


现在完成时:
I've lucked out on several occasions this year. = 今年我已经几次走运了。
She's lucked into a promotion unexpectedly. = 她意外地获得了晋升。


过去完成时:
We had lucked out by finding a hotel room before the festival. = 我们很幸运，在节日开始前找到了酒店房间。
They had lucked into a great deal on a used car. = 他们幸运地以低价买到了一辆二手车。


将来时:
I think we'll luck out and have good weather for the picnic. = 我想我们会很幸运，野餐时天气会很好。
Hopefully, they'll luck into a solution soon. = 希望他们很快能幸运地找到解决方案。


虚拟语气:
If I lucked out, I might buy that house. = 如果我运气好，我可能会买那栋房子。
I wish I had lucked out in the job interview. = 我真希望我在面试中运气好一点。


被动语态:
(The verb "luck" is not typically used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:

否定句:
