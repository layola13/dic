porcelainite:/ˌpɔːrsəlɪˈnaɪt/| n. |瓷质岩|A hard, fine-grained, metamorphic rock that resembles porcelain in appearance and texture.  It is typically composed of quartz, feldspar, and mica, and is often formed by the metamorphism of clay-rich sediments.  |Example: The geologist identified the sample as porcelainite due to its characteristic glassy luster and conchoidal fracture.|近义词：porcellanite,  meta-porcelain|反义词：无
