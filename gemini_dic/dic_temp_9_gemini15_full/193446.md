selaginella: /ˌsɛlədʒɪˈnɛlə/ | n. | 卷柏属植物 | A genus of about 700 species of mostly tropical and subtropical plants in the spikemoss family Selaginellaceae.  They are characterized by their small, scale-like leaves and their ability to survive drought conditions by curling into a ball.  Many species are epiphytes, growing on other plants. | Example: Many species of *selaginella* are popular as ornamental houseplants due to their interesting textures and resilience.  许多卷柏属植物因其有趣的纹理和抗性而作为观赏性室内植物很受欢迎。
