cool:/kuːl/ | adj., v., n. | 凉爽的；酷的；冷静的 |  Describes something that is pleasantly low in temperature;  stylish, fashionable, or impressive;  calm and composed. | 例句：The evening breeze felt cool on my skin. (晚风吹在我的皮肤上感觉很凉爽。)  His new car is really cool. (他的新车真的很酷。)近义词：chilly, fresh, stylish, trendy, calm, relaxed | 反义词：hot, warm, unfashionable, agitated, nervous


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The water is cool. = 水很凉。
This new design is cool. = 这款新设计很酷。

一般过去时:
The air was cool last night. = 昨晚空气很凉爽。
She remained cool under pressure. = 她在压力下保持冷静。

现在完成时:
It has been cool all week. = 整周天气都很凉爽。
He has remained cool throughout the crisis. = 在整个危机中他都保持冷静。

过去完成时:
The room had been cool before the sun rose. = 太阳升起之前房间很凉爽。
She had remained cool despite the chaos. = 尽管一片混乱，她仍然保持冷静。

将来时:
It will be cool tomorrow. = 明天会很凉爽。
He will stay cool even if things get tough. = 即使情况变得艰难，他也会保持冷静。

虚拟语气:
If the room were cool, I would sleep better. = 如果房间凉爽一些，我会睡得更好。
I wish it were cool enough to go swimming. = 我希望天气凉爽到足以去游泳。

被动语态:
The drinks were kept cool in the refrigerator. = 饮料放在冰箱里保持凉爽。
His behavior was described as cool and collected. = 他的行为被描述为冷静沉着。

疑问句:
Is the water cool enough? = 水够凉吗？
Is he cool with the plan? = 他同意这个计划吗？（口语，是否接受）

否定句:
The room isn't cool enough. = 房间不够凉爽。
He wasn't cool about the situation. = 他对这种情况并不冷静。
