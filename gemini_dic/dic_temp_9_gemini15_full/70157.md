flaunt: /flɔːnt/ | /flɑːnt/ | vt. |炫耀，夸示 | To show off something in a proud or boastful way. |例句：He flaunted his wealth by driving a fancy car. 他开着一辆豪华轿车炫耀他的财富。 |例句：She flaunted her new diamond necklace at the party. 她在派对上炫耀她新买的钻石项链。|近义词：show off, boast, parade, display |反义词：hide, conceal, downplay | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flaunts his success to everyone he meets. = 他向遇到的每一个人炫耀他的成功。
They flaunt their wealth with expensive clothes and cars. = 他们穿着昂贵的衣服和开着豪车炫耀他们的财富。

一般过去时:
She flaunted her knowledge of art history at the museum. = 她在博物馆炫耀她对艺术史的知识。
He flaunted his new girlfriend in front of his ex. = 他在他前女友面前炫耀他的新女友。

现在完成时:
He has flaunted his achievements throughout his career. = 他在整个职业生涯中一直炫耀他的成就。
She has flaunted her beauty and charm for years. = 她多年来一直炫耀她的美貌和魅力。

过去完成时:
He had flaunted his connections to get the job. = 他曾炫耀他的关系以获得这份工作。
She had flaunted her independence before she met him. = 在遇到他之前，她曾炫耀过她的独立性。

将来时:
He will flaunt his new skills at the upcoming competition. = 他将在即将到来的比赛中炫耀他的新技能。
She will flaunt her designs at the fashion show. = 她将在时装秀上炫耀她的设计。

虚拟语气:
If he flaunted his wealth less, people would like him more. = 如果他不那么炫耀他的财富，人们会更喜欢他。
I wish she wouldn't flaunt her intelligence so much. = 我希望她不要那么炫耀她的才智。

被动语态:
His success was flaunted by him to everyone. = 他的成功被他向每个人炫耀。
Her beauty was flaunted in the magazine advertisement. = 她的美貌在杂志广告中被炫耀。

疑问句:
Does he flaunt his wealth to impress others? = 他炫耀财富是为了给他人留下深刻印象吗？
Why does she flaunt her achievements so openly? = 她为什么如此公开地炫耀她的成就？

否定句:
He doesn't flaunt his abilities; he lets his work speak for itself. = 他不炫耀自己的能力；他让他的作品为自己说话。
She didn't flaunt her connections to get the job. = 她没有炫耀她的人脉关系来获得这份工作。


PHRASE->
flaunt one's wealth = 炫耀财富
flaunt one's power = 炫耀权力
flaunt one's knowledge = 炫耀知识
flaunt one's achievements = 炫耀成就
flaunt one's beauty = 炫耀美貌


词源:
The word "flaunt" ultimately derives from the Old French word "flaunter," meaning "to wave or flutter."  This likely refers to the way a flag or banner is proudly displayed, and the sense of showing off something prominently evolved from this original meaning.
