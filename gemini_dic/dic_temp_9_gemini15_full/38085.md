compostela:/ˌkɒmpəˈstɛlə/| n. |圣地亚哥·德孔波斯特拉；孔波斯特拉之路|The city of Santiago de Compostela in Galicia, Spain, is the destination of the famous pilgrimage route, the Way of St. James.  Compostela also refers to the route itself.|Example: Many pilgrims walk the Camino de Compostela to reach Santiago de Compostela.|近义词:Santiago de Compostela (the city), Camino de Santiago (the way)|反义词: N/A|名词

词源:
The name "Compostela" is a corruption of the Latin "Campus Stellae," meaning "Field of Stars."  This refers to a legend connecting the discovery of the apostle St. James's remains to the shining of stars.
