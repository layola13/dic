preindebted:/ˌpriːɪnˈdɛtɪd/| adj. | 预先负债的；预先感激的 | Already indebted; owing a debt beforehand; feeling gratitude in advance.  The term suggests a feeling of obligation before a favor is received.  | 例句：I feel preindebted to my mentor for the opportunities he's provided. = 我对我的导师给予我的机会感到预先感激。  He felt preindebted to his parents for their sacrifices. = 他对父母的牺牲感到预先负债。|近义词：obligated, grateful, thankful |反义词：ungrateful, unthankful | 形容词

词源:  The word is a relatively modern coinage, combining "pre-" (before) and "indebted" (owing a debt, usually a debt of gratitude).  It's not found in older dictionaries.
