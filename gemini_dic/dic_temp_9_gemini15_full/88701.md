hobbledehoy: /ˈhɒbldeˌhɔɪ/ | /ˈhɑːbldeˌhɔɪ/ | n. | 笨拙的少年；瘦长而笨手笨脚的少年 | An awkward, gangly adolescent boy.  The term often implies clumsiness and lack of grace. |  He's a clumsy hobbledehoy, always tripping over his own feet.  他是个笨手笨脚的毛头小子，总是绊倒自己的脚。


The term "hobbledehoy" is a somewhat archaic and humorous word.  It's not commonly used in modern English but appears occasionally in literature or to evoke a sense of old-fashioned charm or irony.


近义词: lanky youth, gawky teenager, adolescent, stripling


反义词:  (There isn't a direct antonym, but words suggesting grace and maturity could be considered opposites)  graceful young man, poised adolescent, mature young man


词性: 名词
