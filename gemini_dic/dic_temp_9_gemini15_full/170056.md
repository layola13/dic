proteroglypha:/ˌproʊtərəˈɡlaɪfə/| n. |沟牙毒蛇；前沟牙蛇 |Proteroglypha is a subfamily of venomous snakes characterized by fixed, hollow fangs located at the front of the upper jaw.  These fangs are used to inject venom into their prey.  They are typically small to medium-sized snakes, and many are brightly colored. |Example: Many species within the subfamily Proteroglypha are found in tropical and subtropical regions.  许多沟牙毒蛇物种都分布在热带和亚热带地区。|近义词: elapid (broad term)|反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it refers to a taxonomic group)|名词
