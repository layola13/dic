sentimentalizing: /ˌsɛntɪmənˈtælaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 感伤化；使多愁善感；矫揉造作地表达感情 | To make something sentimental or to treat something in a sentimental way; to excessively indulge in sentimentality.  | 例句：He was sentimentalizing the past, ignoring its harsh realities. 他在怀旧，忽略了它残酷的现实。

She sentimentalized her childhood memories, glossing over the difficult parts. 她将童年回忆浪漫化，忽略了艰难的部分。


近义词：romanticizing, idealizing, romanticizing, mawkishly expressing

反义词：objectifying, rationalizing, de-romanticizing


词源:  The word derives from the noun "sentiment," which comes from the  Latin word "sentire," meaning "to feel." The suffix "-alizing" indicates the process of making something sentimental.
