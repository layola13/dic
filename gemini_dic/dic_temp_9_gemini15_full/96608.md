inducts:/ɪnˈdʌkts/| vt.|介绍；使就职；接纳；引入|To formally admit someone into a group or organization, or to formally introduce someone to a new job or position.|例句：The ceremony inducted the new members into the society.  仪式上接纳了新会员加入协会。

例句：The company inducted its new employees last week. 公司上周为新员工举行了入职仪式。

近义词：initiate, install, admit, introduce

反义词：expel, dismiss, remove


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company inducts new employees every month. = 公司每月都接纳新员工。
He inducts new members into the club. = 他把新成员介绍到俱乐部。


一般过去时:
They inducted her into the Hall of Fame. = 他们把她引入了名人堂。
The university inducted him as an honorary member. = 大学接纳他为名誉会员。


现在完成时:
The organization has inducted many prominent figures. = 该组织已经接纳了许多杰出人物。
She has been inducted into the national academy. = 她已被接纳进入国家学院。


过去完成时:
They had inducted him before the ceremony began. = 在仪式开始之前，他们已经接纳了他。
Before the announcement, they had inducted the new CEO. = 在宣布之前，他们已经引进了新的首席执行官。


将来时:
They will induct the new president next week. = 他们下周将接纳新总统。
The school will induct its graduating class. = 学校将为毕业班举行毕业典礼。


虚拟语气:
If they inducted him, he would be very happy. = 如果他们接纳他，他会非常高兴。
I wish they had inducted me into the club. = 我希望他们把我介绍到俱乐部。


被动语态:
He was inducted into the army last year. = 他去年入伍了。
She will be inducted into the organization next month. = 她下个月将被接纳到该组织。


疑问句:
Will they induct him into the team? = 他们会把他招进队里吗？
Was she inducted into the society? = 她被接纳到协会了吗？


否定句:
They did not induct him into the club. = 他们没有把他介绍到俱乐部。
The committee did not induct her into the society. = 委员会没有接纳她进入协会。


PHRASE->
inducted into = 被接纳到...
inducted as = 被任命为...
formally inducted = 正式引进
newly inducted = 新近入选的
inducted member = 入选成员
