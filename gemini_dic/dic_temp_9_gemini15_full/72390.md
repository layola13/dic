fluctuates:/ˈflʌktʃueɪts/| v.|波动；起伏；变化|To change or vary irregularly; to rise and fall; to shift back and forth.|The stock market fluctuates daily. 股票市场每天都在波动。|The temperature fluctuates throughout the day.气温全天都在波动。|rises, varies, changes|remains stable, stays constant|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The price of oil fluctuates constantly. = 石油价格不断波动。
The number of students fluctuates from year to year. = 学生人数每年都在波动。

一般过去时:
The value of the currency fluctuated wildly last year. = 去年货币价值剧烈波动。
The temperature fluctuated between 20 and 30 degrees. = 气温在20到30度之间波动。

现在完成时:
The stock market has fluctuated significantly this month. = 本月股市波动显著。
The company's profits have fluctuated since the merger. = 自从合并以来，公司的利润一直在波动。

过去完成时:
The economy had fluctuated before the government intervention. = 在政府干预之前，经济已经波动了一段时间。
The patient's blood pressure had fluctuated throughout the night. = 病人的血压整夜都在波动。

将来时:
The weather forecast predicts that the temperature will fluctuate tomorrow. = 天气预报预测明天的气温将会波动。
The exchange rate is expected to fluctuate in the coming weeks. = 预计汇率在未来几周内将会波动。

虚拟语气:
If the price fluctuated too much, we would lose money. = 如果价格波动太大，我们会赔钱。
I wish the stock market didn't fluctuate so much. = 我希望股市不要波动这么大。

被动语态:
The data was found to have fluctuated significantly. = 发现数据波动很大。
The results were significantly fluctuated by the experimental error. = 结果受到实验误差的显著影响而波动。

疑问句:
Does the water level fluctuate often? = 水位经常波动吗？
How much does the price fluctuate each day? = 价格每天波动多少？

否定句:
The level of the river doesn't fluctuate much. = 河水水位变化不大。
The supply of this product hasn't fluctuated recently. = 最近这种产品的供应量没有波动。
