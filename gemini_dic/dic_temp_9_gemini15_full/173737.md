quasi-naturally: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈnætʃərəli/ | adj. | 准自然地，似自然地 |  Approximating or resembling something natural; almost natural, but not entirely so. It suggests a process or phenomenon that mimics natural processes but may involve some artificial or controlled aspects.  |例句：The experiment simulated quasi-naturally occurring conditions. 实验模拟了准自然发生的条件。

The growth of the crystals was quasi-naturally induced, using a carefully controlled environment. 晶体的生长是在精心控制的环境下准自然诱导的。|近义词：semi-natural, artificially natural, near-natural |反义词：unnatural, artificial, synthetic |

词源:
The word "quasi-naturally" is a combination of "quasi-" (from Latin, meaning "as if," "seemingly," or "almost") and "naturally" (from "nature").  It indicates a resemblance to natural processes, but with a crucial difference implying some degree of artificiality or control.
