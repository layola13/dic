italicising: /ɪˈtælɪsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 斜体书写；用斜体字印刷 |  The act of setting type or writing in italics.  It is used to emphasize words or phrases, indicate titles of works, or show foreign words. | 例句：Please italicise the book titles in your bibliography. 请将你参考文献中的书名用斜体字表示。

例句：The editor asked me to italicise the foreign words. 编辑让我把外来词用斜体字表示。


近义词：italicised, slant


词源:  From the word *italic*, referring to a type style derived from the italic script of Renaissance Italy. The suffix "-ising" indicates the act of doing something.  The ultimate origin is from the Italian word *italiano* (Italian), referencing the style's origin.
