lock-up:/ˈlɒkʌp/ /ˈlɑːkʌp/| n. v.| 禁闭；拘留；封存；(资金)冻结 | A secure place for keeping prisoners or valuables;  the act of securing something, such as a building or funds. |例句：The police took him to the lock-up.  警察把他带到拘留所。|The company has put a lock-up on the shares. 公司已冻结股票交易。|近义词：confinement, detention, imprisonment, seizure |反义词：release, liberation, freedom


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They lock up the office every night. = 他们每晚都锁上办公室。
The bank locks up its assets to prevent fraud. = 银行冻结其资产以防止欺诈。

一般过去时:

过去完成时:
