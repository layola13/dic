roasted:/ˈroʊstɪd/ | adj. | 烤制的；烘烤过的 |  Describes food that has been cooked by dry heat, typically in an oven.  It implies a browning and often a crisping of the exterior. | Example: The roasted chicken was delicious. 烤鸡真好吃。 The potatoes were perfectly roasted. 土豆烤得恰到好处。|近义词：baked, grilled (sometimes), broiled (sometimes) | 反义词：raw, uncooked


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The chef roasts the vegetables every day. = 厨师每天都烤蔬菜。
They roast coffee beans in small batches. = 他们小批量烘焙咖啡豆。

一般过去时:
She roasted a turkey for Thanksgiving. = 她烤了一只火鸡过感恩节。
We roasted marshmallows over the campfire. = 我们在营火旁烤棉花糖。

现在完成时:
He has roasted countless chickens in his career. = 他在他职业生涯中烤过无数只鸡。
They have roasted the nuts to perfection. = 他们把坚果烤得完美无缺。

过去完成时:
She had roasted the vegetables before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，她已经烤好了蔬菜。
They had roasted the lamb for three hours. = 他们烤了三小时的羊肉。

将来时:
We will roast a pig for the party. = 我们将为派对烤一头猪。
She will roast the peppers with garlic and herbs. = 她将用大蒜和香草烤辣椒。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would roast a whole chicken. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会烤一只整鸡。
I wish I had roasted the potatoes longer. = 我真希望我把土豆烤久一点。

被动语态:
The chicken was roasted until golden brown. = 鸡肉被烤至金棕色。
The vegetables are being roasted in the oven. = 蔬菜正在烤箱里烤着。

疑问句:
Is the meat roasted enough? = 肉烤够了吗？

否定句:
