reescalating: /ˌriːˈskæleɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 重新升级；再次升级；(冲突、紧张局势等)再次加剧 |  The act of escalating something again, often referring to conflict, tension, or a problem.  It implies a renewed increase in intensity or severity after a period of relative calm or de-escalation. |


例句：

The conflict is reescalating after a brief period of peace talks.  = 在短暂的和平谈判之后，冲突再次升级。

Tensions are reescalating between the two countries, threatening further military action. = 两国之间的紧张局势正在再次升级，威胁到进一步的军事行动。


近义词：reintensifying, exacerbating, reigniting, escalating anew


反义词：deescalating, deintensifying, mitigating, resolving


词源:

* **re-:**  prefix meaning "again" or "back"
* **escalate:** from  Italian *scalare* "to climb," ultimately from Latin *scala* "ladder."  The verb *escalate* originally referred to a gradual increase or rise, often in a hierarchical structure.  The modern sense of escalating conflict developed later.
