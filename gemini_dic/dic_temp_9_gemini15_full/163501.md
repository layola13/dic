preconvicting:/ˌpriːkənˈvɪktɪŋ/| adj.| 预先定罪的；预先判罪的 |  Describing the act of convicting someone before a trial has taken place, implying a prejudgment of guilt.  It suggests a violation of due process and fairness. | Example: The preconvicting atmosphere in the courtroom made it difficult for the defendant to receive a fair trial.  预先定罪的法庭气氛使得被告很难得到公正的审判。|近义词：prejudging, prejudicing, predisposing |反义词：fair, impartial, unbiased
