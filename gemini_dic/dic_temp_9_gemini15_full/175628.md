rankle:/ˈræŋkəl/ | v. | 使生气；使烦恼；积怨 | To cause continued annoyance or resentment.  |The insult rankled in his mind for days.  这侮辱让他好几天都耿耿于怀。 | irritate, annoy, vex | soothe, appease, pacify | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His words rankle her. = 他的话让她生气。
The injustice rankles in her heart. = 这不公正让她心中愤愤不平。

一般过去时:
The criticism rankled him deeply. = 这次批评让他深感苦恼。
Her rejection rankled for months. = 她的拒绝让他苦恼了好几个月。

现在完成时:
That slight has rankled with me for years. = 那次轻蔑已经让我生气多年了。
His betrayal has rankled in my memory. = 他的背叛一直让我耿耿于怀。

过去完成时:
The incident had rankled in his mind since childhood. = 这件事从他童年时代就一直让他耿耿于怀。
She had rankled over the unfair treatment for days. = 她对这种不公平的待遇已经生气好几天了。

将来时:
This injustice will rankle with him for a long time. = 这不公正会让他很久都生气。
His comments will likely rankle many people. = 他的评论可能会让很多人生气。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't said that, it wouldn't have rankled her so much. = 如果他不这么说，就不会让她那么生气了。
I wish that insult hadn't rankled me so long. = 我真希望那次侮辱没有让我生气这么久。

被动语态:
He was rankled by the unfair accusation. = 他对不公平的指控感到愤愤不平。
She felt rankled by his behavior. = 她对他的行为感到生气。


疑问句:
Does this injustice rankle with you? = 这不公正让你生气吗？
Does his behavior rankle you? = 他的行为让你生气吗？


否定句:
The incident didn't rankle him as much as he thought it would. = 这件事并没有让他像他想象的那样生气。
Her words didn't rankle him at all. = 她的言语丝毫没有让他生气。


PHRASE->
rankle in one's mind/heart/memory = 在某人的头脑/心中/记忆中挥之不去
rankle with resentment = 充满怨恨
rankle under/with = 因...而生气
a rankling injustice = 一件令人愤愤不平的不公正之事
rankling suspicion = 挥之不去的怀疑


词源:
The word "rankle" ultimately derives from the Old French word *rancler*, meaning "to fester," "to become inflamed," or "to grow sore."  This reflects the original sense of the word, implying a deep-seated irritation that persists and festers like a wound.
