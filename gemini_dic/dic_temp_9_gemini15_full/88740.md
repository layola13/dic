hobson:/ˈhɒbsən/| n. |霍布森的选择；唯一的选择|Hobson's choice refers to a situation where only one option is presented, and it is not a real choice because there are no alternatives. It comes from the name of a 17th-century Cambridge livery stable keeper, Thomas Hobson, who famously insisted that his customers take the horse nearest the door, regardless of its condition. |He had no choice but to accept Hobson's choice. 他别无选择，只能接受霍布森的选择。|  |  |名词
