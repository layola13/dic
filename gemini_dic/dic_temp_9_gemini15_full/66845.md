expatriatism: /ˌɛkspætəriəˈtɪzəm/ | n. | 移居国外；侨居；海外定居 | The practice of residing in a foreign country; the state of being an expatriate.  It often implies a degree of permanence and integration into the host country's society, though not necessarily renouncing one's original citizenship. | 例句：The increasing popularity of expatriatism reflects a growing globalized world.  越来越多的侨居现象反映了一个日益全球化的世界。

例句：His long years of expatriatism in France gave him a deep understanding of French culture. 他在法国多年的侨居生活使他深刻地了解了法国文化。


近义词：emigration, exile (though exile often carries a connotation of forced departure), foreign residency


反义词：repatriation (returning to one's native country), immigration (moving to a new country)


词源：From "expatriate" + "-ism".  "Expatriate" itself comes from Latin "ex-" (out of) + "patria" (fatherland, native country).  The suffix "-ism" denotes a state, practice, or doctrine.
