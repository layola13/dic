miniver:/ˈmɪnɪvər/| n.|小鼠皮，貂皮|The fur of a small mammal, especially the European mink;  a garment or trimming made from this fur.|例句：Her coat was trimmed with luxurious miniver.  她的外套饰有奢华的小鼠皮。

例句：The medieval tapestry depicted figures clad in miniver. 中世纪挂毯描绘了穿着貂皮的人像。


近义词：fur, ermine (especially if referring to white miniver)

反义词：None directly;  antonyms would relate to the opposite of the material (e.g., cotton, wool) or the opposite of the luxurious connotation (e.g., coarse fabric).


词源:
Middle English *minivere, from Old French menu vair, literally "small vair," vair being a type of fur, probably from Frankish *war, related to German *weher, "weasel".  The term originally referred to a type of squirrel fur, later extending to the fur of other small mammals, particularly minks.
