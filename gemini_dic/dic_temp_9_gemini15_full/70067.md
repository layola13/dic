feudalisms:/ˈfjuːdəlɪzəmz/| n. |封建制度，封建主义|A system of social organization in which land is held by a lord or noble in exchange for service or labor, typically involving a hierarchy of land ownership and allegiance. Feudalisms often characterized by decentralized political power and a rigid social structure.  |The decline of feudalisms in Europe led to the rise of nation-states.|近义词：feudalism (singular), manorialism|反义词：democracy, republicanism
