petechiae:/pɪˈtiːkiːaɪ/ | n. |皮下出血点，紫癜 | Small, round, purplish-red spots appearing on the skin as a result of bleeding under the skin.  They are usually less than 2 millimeters in diameter and do not blanch (turn white) when pressed. Petechiae are caused by a variety of factors, including trauma, infections, blood disorders, and certain medications.  | Example: The doctor noticed several petechiae on the patient's legs, indicating a possible blood clotting problem.  医生注意到病人腿上有一些皮下出血点，这可能表明存在血液凝固问题。| purpura, ecchymosis |  normal skin | 名词
