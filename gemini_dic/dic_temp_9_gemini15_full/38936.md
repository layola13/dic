confidence: /ˈkɒnfɪdəns/ /ˈkɑːnfɪdəns/ | n. |自信，信心；信任；秘密 |  A feeling of self-assurance and belief in one's own abilities or qualities; trust or faith in a person or thing.  | 例句：She has the confidence to succeed. 她有成功的信心。 | 例句：I have complete confidence in his abilities. 我完全相信他的能力。 | 近义词：self-assurance, assurance, belief, trust, faith | 反义词：doubt, uncertainty, insecurity, mistrust


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has confidence in his team. = 他对他的团队充满信心。
She shows great confidence in her work. = 她对自己的工作表现出极大的信心。

一般过去时:
He showed great confidence during the presentation. = 他在演示中表现出极大的信心。
They had confidence that they would win. = 他们相信自己会赢。

现在完成时:
She has gained confidence since joining the club. = 自从加入俱乐部以来，她变得自信多了。
I have lost confidence in his judgment. = 我对他判断力失去了信心。

过去完成时:
He had already lost confidence in his abilities before the competition. = 在比赛开始之前，他已经对自己的能力失去了信心。
They had built their confidence through years of hard work. = 他们通过多年的努力建立了信心。

将来时:
She will gain more confidence with experience. = 她会随着经验的积累而更加自信。
They will have the confidence to overcome any challenge. = 他们将有信心克服任何挑战。

虚拟语气:
If I had more confidence, I would try for the promotion. = 如果我更有信心，我会争取晋升。
I wish I had the confidence to speak in public. = 我真希望我有勇气在公众场合发言。

被动语态:
His confidence was shaken by the criticism. = 他的信心受到了批评的打击。
Her confidence was boosted by her recent success. = 她最近的成功增强了她的信心。

疑问句:
Do you have confidence in your abilities? = 你相信自己的能力吗？
Does she lack confidence in her skills? = 她缺乏技能方面的自信吗？

否定句:
He lacks confidence in himself. = 他缺乏自信。
She doesn't have much confidence in her writing. = 她对自己的写作不太自信。
