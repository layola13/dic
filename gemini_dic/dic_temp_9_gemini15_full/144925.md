over-tinting: /ˌoʊvərˈtɪntɪŋ/ | n. | 过度着色，色彩过浓 | The act of applying too much color or tint to something, resulting in an unnatural or exaggerated appearance. Word Origin:

The word is a compound word formed from:

* **over-**:  A prefix indicating excess or to a greater degree than normal.
* **tinting**:  The act of applying a tint or color.  "Tint" itself originates from the Old French word "teinte," meaning "dyed, coloured."
