laid-up:/leɪd ˈʌp/| adj.| 搁置的；生病卧床的；(机器等)损坏不能使用的 |  laid-up describes something that is unusable, often due to damage or illness.  It can refer to a person who is confined to bed due to illness or injury, or to a machine or vehicle that is out of service due to a malfunction. | 例句：He's been laid up with the flu for a week. 他因流感卧床一周了。

The old car has been laid up in the garage for years.  这辆旧车多年来一直搁置在车库里。| 近义词：disabled, incapacitated, out of commission, broken down | 反义词：operational, functioning, healthy, well


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The machine is laid up. = 机器坏了。
He is laid up with a bad cold. = 他因重感冒卧床不起。


一般过去时:
The ship was laid up after the storm. = 船在暴风雨后被搁置了。
She was laid up for months after the accident. = 事故后她卧床几个月。


现在完成时:
The factory has been laid up since the fire. = 自从火灾以来，工厂一直处于停工状态。
He has been laid up with back pain for weeks. = 他因背痛卧床几周了。


过去完成时:
The engine had been laid up for several years before they repaired it. = 在他们修理之前，发动机已经搁置了好几年了。
She had been laid up with a broken leg before she finally recovered. = 她腿骨折后卧床不起，直到最终康复。


将来时:
The plane will be laid up for maintenance next week. = 飞机下周将停飞进行维护。
I think I'll be laid up with a fever tomorrow. = 我想我明天会发烧卧床不起。


虚拟语气:
If the engine were laid up, we couldn't deliver the goods on time. = 如果发动机坏了，我们就无法按时交付货物。
If I were laid up for a week, I'd miss the deadline.  = 如果我卧床一周，我就会错过截止日期。


被动语态:
The car was laid up because of a faulty engine. = 汽车因为发动机故障而被搁置。
The project was laid up due to lack of funding. = 该项目因缺乏资金而搁置。


疑问句:
Is the equipment laid up? = 设备坏了/搁置了吗？
Is your grandfather laid up again? = 你爷爷又卧床了吗？


否定句:
The machinery isn't laid up; it's working perfectly. = 机器没有坏/搁置；它运行良好。
He wasn't laid up; he just took a day off. = 他没有卧床；他只是休假一天。
