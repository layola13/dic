radiocarbon:/ˌreɪdiˈoʊkɑːrbən/| n. |放射性碳;碳14 |A radioactive isotope of carbon, used in radiocarbon dating.  Radiocarbon is formed in the upper atmosphere by cosmic rays and incorporated into living organisms. The decay of radiocarbon after the death of an organism allows scientists to estimate the age of organic materials.|例句：Radiocarbon dating revealed the artifact to be approximately 3,000 years old. 放射性碳测年法显示该文物大约有3000年的历史。|近义词：carbon-14, 14C |反义词:  None.
