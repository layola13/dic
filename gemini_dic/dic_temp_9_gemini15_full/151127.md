parasymbiosis:/ˌpærəˈsɪmbaɪoʊsɪs/ | n. |共栖，偏利共生 | A biological interaction in which one organism benefits and the other is neither harmed nor significantly benefited.  It differs from mutualism (where both organisms benefit) and commensalism (where one benefits and the other is neither harmed nor benefited, but the relationship is less intimate). | Example:  Some bacteria live on the skin of humans; they benefit from access to nutrients, while humans are generally unaffected.  This could be described as a parasymbiosis. | 近义词: commensalism (in some contexts)| 反义词: parasitism, mutualism
