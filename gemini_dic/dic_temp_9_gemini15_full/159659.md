poltroonish: /pɒlˈtruːnɪʃ/ | adj. | 懦弱的；胆小的；胆怯的 | Cowardly; lacking courage; craven.  | The poltroonish general refused to lead his troops into battle.  这个懦弱的将军拒绝率领他的军队作战。 |近义词： cowardly, pusillanimous, faint-hearted, timorous | 反义词：brave, courageous, valiant, bold


词源:

The word "poltroonish" derives from the Middle French word "poltron," meaning "coward," which in turn originates from the Old French "poltron."  The suffix "-ish" adds an adjective quality, indicating a characteristic or resemblance to the noun.


用法说明:

Poltroonish is primarily used as an adjective to describe someone who lacks courage and is easily intimidated. It carries a strong connotation of disapproval and often implies a degree of contemptibility.  It's not typically used in formal writing unless the context specifically requires a strong, negative description.


例句:

His poltroonish behavior during the crisis cost the company millions. 他在危机期间懦弱的行为使公司损失了数百万美元。

The poltroonish soldier deserted his post under fire.  那个胆小的士兵在炮火下逃离了岗位。


近义词:

* cowardly: lacking courage; timid
* pusillanimous: lacking courage and resolution; timid
* faint-hearted: lacking courage
* timorous: showing or suffering from nervousness or a lack of confidence; timid

反义词:

* brave: ready to face and endure danger or pain; showing courage
* courageous: not deterred by danger or pain; brave
* valiant: possessing or showing courage or determination
* bold: showing an ability to take risks; confident and daring
