preallege:/ˌpriːˈælɪdʒ/| v.|预先声称，提前主张|To assert or claim something beforehand or in advance.  It often implies a claim that may later be proven or disproven.| The lawyer prealleged that the defendant was guilty before presenting any evidence. 律师在出示任何证据之前就预先声称被告有罪。|  allege, claim, assert|deny, refute|动词

She prealleges her superior knowledge in every meeting. = 她每次会议都预先声称自己知识渊博。
