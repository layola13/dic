half-famished: /ˌhɑːfˈfæmɪʃt/ | adj. | 半饿的，非常饥饿的 | Extremely hungry, but not completely starved.  The word combines "half" indicating a partial state and "famished" meaning extremely hungry.  |  例句：After hiking for hours, they were half-famished and eagerly awaited their lunch.  几个小时的徒步旅行后，他们又饿又渴，急切地等待着午餐。

| 近义词：hungry, starving, peckish, ravenous | 反义词：full, satiated, replete | 词性：形容词

词源:

* **half:**  Old English *healf, *hælfe, from Proto-Germanic *halbaz (compare Dutch *half, German *halb).

* **famished:** From Old French *famis, from Latin *famēs "hunger".  The suffix "-ed" is a past participle marker, giving the word an adjectival quality describing a state of being.
