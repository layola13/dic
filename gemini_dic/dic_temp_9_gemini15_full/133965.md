nonheritability: /ˌnɒnˌhɛrɪtəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 非遗传性，不遗传性 | The extent to which a trait is not determined by genes.  It represents the influence of environmental factors and random chance on an individual's characteristics.  | Example:  The high nonheritability of height in this population suggests a significant role of environmental factors in determining adult stature.  非遗传性高的身高群体表明环境因素在决定成年身高方面起着重要作用。|近义词：environmental influence, non-genetic influence | 反义词：heritability


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "hello".  It describes a characteristic, not an action.  Therefore, verb tenses are not applicable.  We can, however, use it in various sentence structures.
