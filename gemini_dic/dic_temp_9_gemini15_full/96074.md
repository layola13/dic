indeed:/ɪnˈdiːd/ | adv. |的确，确实，实在|Indeed is an adverb used to emphasize a statement or to express agreement or confirmation.  It can also be used to express surprise or irony.|例句：He is indeed a talented musician. 他确实是一位才华横溢的音乐家。

例句：
"Are you going to the party?" "Indeed, I am."  “你要去参加聚会吗？”“是的，我要去。”


近义词：certainly, truly, really, actually, in fact

反义词：falsely, wrongly


词源:
Old English *indēde, from in + dēde deed.  The original meaning was "in deed," emphasizing the action or reality of something.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is indeed happy. = 他确实很开心。
They are indeed working hard. = 他们确实很努力工作。

一般过去时:
She was indeed surprised. = 她确实很惊讶。
It was indeed a long journey. = 这确实是一段漫长的旅程。

现在完成时:
They have indeed finished the project. = 他们确实完成了项目。
I have indeed seen that movie. = 我确实看过那部电影。

过去完成时:
He had indeed told me the truth. = 他确实告诉我真相了。
We had indeed expected a better result. = 我们确实期望一个更好的结果。

将来时:
It will indeed be a great day. = 这确实将会是美好的一天。
They will indeed arrive on time. = 他们确实会准时到达。

虚拟语气:
If it were indeed true, I would be shocked. = 如果这确实是真的，我会很震惊。
If he had indeed known, he would have acted differently. = 如果他确实知道，他会采取不同的行动。

被动语态:
The plan was indeed approved. = 计划确实得到了批准。
The problem is indeed being addressed. = 问题确实正在得到解决。

疑问句:
Is it indeed finished? = 它确实完成了吗？
Is he indeed the author? = 他确实是作者吗？

否定句:
It is not indeed a simple task. = 这确实不是一件简单的事。
She is not indeed to blame. = 她确实没有责任。
