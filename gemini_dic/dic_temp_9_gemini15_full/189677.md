salmonoid:/ˈsælmənɔɪd/| n.|鲑形目鱼类；鲑鱼状的|A salmonoid is a fish belonging to the order Salmoniformes, which includes salmon, trout, char, and related species.  They are typically characterized by their streamlined bodies, adipose fins, and anadromous habits (in many species).|Example: Many salmonoid species are important for both commercial and recreational fishing.|近义词：salmon, trout (depending on the specific species)|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with fish from other orders.
