marched:/mɑːtʃt/|/ˈmɑːrʃt/| |v.| 行军；前进；迫使前进；|To walk with a steady, firm step, often in a group.  It can also refer to forcefully moving something forward.|例句：The soldiers marched in formation. 士兵们正步前进。

The protesters marched to the government building. 抗议者们向政府大楼进军。


近义词：walked, proceeded, advanced, paraded|反义词：retreated, withdrew, stood still


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They march every day. = 他们每天都行军。
The band marches in the parade. = 乐队在游行中行进。


一般过去时:
We marched for miles. = 我们走了几英里。
The army marched towards the enemy. = 大军向敌人进发。


现在完成时:
They have marched all day. = 他们已经行军了一整天。
The troops have marched across the desert. = 部队已经穿越了沙漠。


过去完成时:
They had marched for hours before they rested. = 他们在休息之前已经行军了好几个小时。
The soldiers had marched twenty miles before reaching the village. = 士兵们在到达村庄之前已经行军了二十英里。


将来时:
We will march tomorrow. = 我们明天将要行军。
The demonstrators will march on the capital. = 示威者们将要向首都进军。


虚拟语气:
If they marched together, they would be stronger. = 如果他们一起行军，他们会更强大。
I wish I had marched with them. = 我真希望我当时和他们一起行军。


被动语态:
The troops were marched to the front lines. = 部队被带到了前线。
The prisoners were marched to their cells. = 囚犯们被押送到他们的牢房。


疑问句:
Did they march yesterday? = 他们昨天行军了吗？
Will they march tomorrow? = 他们明天会行军吗？


否定句:
They didn't march today. = 他们今天没有行军。
We won't march without proper equipment. = 没有合适的装备，我们不会行军。


PHRASE->

march to: 向...行军
