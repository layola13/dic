collapse:/kəˈlæps/| v. n.|倒塌；崩溃；瓦解|To fall down suddenly and completely; to break down or fail suddenly.|The building collapsed after the earthquake. 地震后，建筑物倒塌了。The negotiations collapsed without agreement. 谈判破裂，没有达成协议。|crumble, cave in, fall apart|stand, endure, survive|动词，名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The bridge collapses under heavy load.  桥梁在重压下倒塌。
The government's policy is collapsing. 政府的政策正在崩溃。

一般过去时:
The tent collapsed in the storm.  帐篷在暴风雨中倒塌了。
The stock market collapsed last year. 股市去年崩盘了。

现在完成时:
The roof has collapsed due to the heavy snow. 屋顶因大雪而倒塌了。
The company has collapsed into bankruptcy. 公司破产倒闭了。

过去完成时:
The bridge had collapsed before the rescue team arrived.  救援队到达之前，桥梁已经倒塌了。
The talks had collapsed before any agreement was reached. 会谈在达成任何协议之前就破裂了。

将来时:
The old house will collapse soon. 这座旧房子很快就会倒塌。
The economy might collapse if the government doesn't intervene. 如果政府不干预，经济可能会崩溃。

虚拟语气:
If the dam collapsed, the whole town would be flooded. 如果大坝倒塌，整个城镇都会被淹没。
I wish the negotiations hadn't collapsed. 我希望谈判没有破裂。

被动语态:
The building was collapsed by the explosion. 建筑物被爆炸摧毁了。
The project was collapsed due to lack of funding.  由于资金不足，项目被取消了。

疑问句:
Did the structure collapse? 结构倒塌了吗？
Has the system collapsed? 系统崩溃了吗？

否定句:
The building did not collapse. 建筑物没有倒塌。
The negotiations did not collapse. 谈判没有破裂。
