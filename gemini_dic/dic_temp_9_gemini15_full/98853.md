intemperament: /ɪnˈtɛmpərəmənt/ | n. | 性情暴躁；坏脾气；性情古怪 | Lack of self-control; a volatile or unpredictable disposition.  It often refers to a person's tendency to behave in an irritable, angry, or unreasonable manner. |例句：His intemperament led to many arguments with his colleagues. 他的坏脾气导致他和同事们发生了很多争吵。

His intemperament made it difficult to work with him.  他的古怪脾气使和他一起工作变得很困难。


近义词：irritability, irascibility, hot temper, volatility,  testiness |反义词：calmness, equanimity, patience, composure


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not" or "lacking") and the word "temperament."  Temperament itself has roots in the Latin word "temperamentum," meaning "a mixing, proportion, or blending," referring to the balance of humors in the body believed to affect personality.
