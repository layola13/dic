sidelined:/ˈsaɪdlaɪnd/ | vt. adj. | 使…处于闲置状态；使…退出；被冷落；替补的 | To remove (someone) from a position or activity; to prevent (someone) from participating;  to put aside or ignore. |


例句：

The injured player was sidelined for the rest of the season.  受伤的球员在本赛季剩余时间里都处于替补状态。

His controversial comments sidelined him from the political debate. 他有争议的言论使他被排除在政治辩论之外。


近义词：bench, exclude, marginalize, shelve


反义词：include, promote, feature, central


词性：动词，形容词

一般现在时:

He sidelines his concerns about the project. 他把对这个项目的担忧放在一边。

They sideline dissenting voices in their meetings. 他们在会议上压制异议的声音。


一般过去时:

The coach sidelined the star player due to injury. 教练因伤将明星球员放在替补席上。

The scandal sidelined the politician's career. 丑闻毁掉了这位政治家的职业生涯。


现在完成时:

The company has sidelined its plans for expansion. 公司已经搁置了扩张计划。

Injuries have sidelined him from several important matches. 伤病使他缺席了几场重要的比赛。


过去完成时:

He had been sidelined for weeks before the final game. 在决赛之前，他已经缺席了几周。

They had already sidelined the issue before the meeting. 会议之前，他们就已经把这个问题搁置了。


将来时:

The new policy will sideline many small businesses. 新政策将使许多小型企业被边缘化。

His health problems will likely sideline him for the next few months. 他的健康问题很可能会让他在接下来的几个月里处于闲置状态。


虚拟语气:

If he hadn't been sidelined, the team might have won. 如果他没有被排除在外，球队可能会赢。

I wish I hadn't been sidelined by that illness. 我真希望我没有因为那场病而被搁置。


被动语态:

He was sidelined by a sudden illness. 他因突发疾病而被排除在外。

The proposal was sidelined due to lack of funding. 由于缺乏资金，该提案被搁置了。


疑问句:

Was he sidelined due to his poor performance? 他是因为表现不佳而被冷落的吗？

否定句:

The company did not sideline its research and development efforts. 公司并没有搁置其研发工作。

They didn't sideline the important issues in the discussion. 他们没有在讨论中回避重要的问题。
