/ʃwɑː/|/ˈʃwɑː/| n. |舒哇音(元音)|The schwa sound is a neutral, unstressed vowel sound in many languages, including English.  It's often represented by the symbol ⟨ə⟩ in phonetic transcription. It's the most common vowel sound in English.|The schwa sound is the most common vowel sound in English, as in the "a" in "about" or the "e" in "taken".|近义词: unstressed vowel|反义词:  stressed vowel|名词


USAGE->
This word itself doesn't have grammatical usage in the way a verb or noun does.  The *sound* it represents, however, is used in every grammatical tense and structure in English.  Examples showing the schwa sound (ə) are below.  Note that the schwa sound is represented by different letters in spelling and its pronunciation is subtle and varies slightly depending on the context.

一般现在时:
I am a student. = 我是一个学生。(the "a" in "a" and the "e" in "student" often contain schwa sounds)
He eats apples. = 他吃苹果。(the "e" in "He" and the "e" in "eats" often contain schwa sounds)


一般过去时:
She lived in London. = 她住在伦敦。(the "e" in "She" and "e" in "lived" often contain schwa sounds)
They played football. = 他们踢足球。(the "e" in "They" and the "a" in "played" often contain schwa sounds)


现在完成时:
I have finished my work. = 我已经完成了我的工作。(the "a" in "have" and "e" in "finished" often contain schwa sounds)
She has been to Paris. = 她去过巴黎。(the "a" in "has" and the "e" in "been" often contain schwa sounds)


过去完成时:
He had already left. = 他已经离开了。(the "a" in "had" and the "e" in "already" often contain schwa sounds)
We had seen that movie before. = 我们以前看过那部电影。(the "a" in "had" and the "e" in "seen" often contain schwa sounds)


将来时:
I will go to the park. = 我要去公园。(the "i" in "will" often contains a schwa sound)
They will be here soon. = 他们很快就会来。(the "i" in "will" and the "e" in "here" often contain schwa sounds)


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would do it. = 如果我是你，我会那样做。(the "e" in "were" often contains a schwa sound)
I wish I were taller. = 我希望我更高。(the "e" in "were" often contains a schwa sound)


被动语态:
The book was written by him. = 这本书是他写的。(the "e" in "was" and "e" in "written" often contain schwa sounds)
The cake is being eaten. = 蛋糕正在被吃。(the "i" in "is" and "e" in "being" often contain schwa sounds)


疑问句:
Are you happy? = 你快乐吗？(the "e" in "Are" often contains a schwa sound)
Where is the key? = 钥匙在哪里？(the "e" in "Where" and "e" in "the" often contain schwa sounds)


否定句:
I don't like it. = 我不喜欢它。(the "o" in "don't" often contains a schwa sound)
He isn't coming. = 他不来了。(the "i" in "isn't" often contains a schwa sound)



PHRASE->
The schwa sound is present in many phrases; it is not a word that combines with others in phrases.  Examples showing schwa sounds are below.

What's up? = 怎么了？/ 你好吗？ (multiple schwa sounds)
How are you? = 你好吗？ (schwa sounds in "are" and "you")
Let's go. = 我们走吧。(schwa sound in "Let's")
Take it easy. = 放轻松。(schwa sound in "it")
It's okay. = 没关系。(schwa sound in "it's" and "okay")
