premonition: /ˌpriːməˈnɪʃən/ | n. | 预感，预兆 | A strong feeling that something is going to happen, especially something unpleasant.  | 例句：She had a premonition of disaster. 她有一种灾难将要来临的预感。

例句：He had a premonition that something bad was about to happen. 他预感到不好的事情即将发生。


近义词：foreboding, presentiment, hunch, feeling, intuition | 反义词：certainty, assurance


词性：名词
