词源:
The word "nonapprehensive" is formed by adding the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") to the adjective "apprehensive," which derives from the Latin word "apprehendere" meaning "to seize, grasp, understand."  Therefore, "nonapprehensive" literally means "not grasping with anxiety" or "not fearing."
