overadjusting: /ˌoʊvərəˈdʒʌstɪŋ/ | v. | 过度调整；矫枉过正 |  The act of making excessive or unnecessary adjustments, often leading to a worse outcome than the initial problem.  This can apply to various contexts, from technical settings (e.g., adjusting audio levels) to interpersonal relationships (e.g., overcompensating for a perceived fault). |


例句：

He was overadjusting the brakes, making the car harder to control.  = 他过度调整了刹车，使汽车更难以控制。

She kept overadjusting her posture, making herself appear stiff and unnatural. = 她不断地过度调整姿势，让自己显得僵硬不自然。


近义词：overcorrecting, overcompensating, fine-tuning excessively


反义词：underadjusting, neglecting adjustment


词性：动词
