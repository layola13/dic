postordination:/ˌpoʊstɔːrdɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. |  祝圣后；任命后 | The act of conferring holy orders or a similar position of authority after a period of service or training.  It typically refers to the process of formally installing someone into a religious office or position after they have already completed their theological education or training. | Example: His postordination sermon was a powerful reflection on his years of ministry.  他的祝圣后讲道深刻地反映了他多年的牧师生涯。 | 近义词：installation, investiture, induction | 反义词：preordination (if used in a religious context)
