luke:/luːk/| n. |路加（男子名）；福音书（指路加福音）| Luke is a common given name for boys, derived from the Greek name Loukas.  It is also the name of one of the four Gospels in the New Testament.  The Gospel of Luke details the life, ministry, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ. |例句：My friend's name is Luke. 我的朋友名叫路加。

The Gospel of Luke is a cherished part of Christian scripture. 路加福音是基督教圣经中珍贵的一部分。|近义词：Lucas (variant spelling)|反义词：无
