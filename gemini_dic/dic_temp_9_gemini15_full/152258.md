patronize:/ˈpeɪtrənaɪz/ | vt. n. | 惠顾；以高人一等的态度对待；俯视；资助 |  To treat (someone) as if you were superior to them; to be a customer of (a shop, restaurant, etc.) ; to support (an artist, writer, etc.) financially. | 例句：He patronized the local bakery every Saturday. 他每周六都惠顾这家当地的面包店。  He patronized the young artist by buying several of his paintings. 他以高人一等的态度对待这位年轻的艺术家，买了他的几幅画。| 近义词：condescend, support, sponsor | 反义词：respect, treat equally


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He patronizes the local coffee shop. = 他惠顾当地的咖啡店。
She patronizes the museum regularly. = 她定期惠顾博物馆。


一般过去时:
They patronized that restaurant for years. = 他们多年来一直惠顾那家餐馆。
He patronized the struggling artist with a generous donation. = 他慷慨捐款，资助这位挣扎的艺术家。


现在完成时:
We have patronized this store since it opened. = 自从这家商店开业以来，我们就一直惠顾它。
She has patronized many local businesses over the years. = 多年来，她一直惠顾许多当地企业。


过去完成时:
They had patronized the same tailor for decades before he retired. = 在裁缝退休之前，他们几十年来一直惠顾同一家裁缝店。
The company had patronized several artists before settling on a designer. = 在确定设计师之前，该公司曾资助过几位艺术家。


将来时:
I will patronize your new restaurant next week. = 我下周会惠顾你的新餐馆。
The art gallery hopes to attract new patrons who will patronize the artists exhibiting their work. = 画廊希望吸引新的赞助人来资助参展的艺术家。


虚拟语气:
If I were richer, I would patronize more local businesses. = 如果我有钱些，我会惠顾更多的当地企业。
I wish I could patronize all the talented artists in the city. = 我希望我能资助城市里所有有才华的艺术家。


被动语态:
The small business was patronized by many loyal customers. = 这家小企业受到许多忠实顾客的惠顾。
The artists were patronized by wealthy collectors. = 这些艺术家受到富有的收藏家的资助。


疑问句:
Do you patronize any local charities? = 你资助任何当地慈善机构吗？
Where do you usually patronize for your groceries? = 你通常在哪里买杂货？


否定句:
They do not patronize businesses that don't treat their employees well. = 他们不会惠顾那些不善待员工的企业。
He refused to patronize the arrogant shopkeeper. = 他拒绝惠顾那位傲慢的店主。
