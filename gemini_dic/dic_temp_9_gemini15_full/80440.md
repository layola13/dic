gondwanas:/ɡɒndˈwɑːnəz/| n.|冈瓦纳古陆（复数）|The Gondwanas were a supercontinent that existed from the Neoproterozoic (about 550 million years ago) until the Jurassic (about 180 million years ago).  They eventually broke apart to form the continents we know today, including parts of South America, Africa, Antarctica, India, and Australia. |The breakup of the Gondwanas significantly impacted the Earth's climate and the distribution of flora and fauna. |近义词：supercontinent (单数)|反义词：无
