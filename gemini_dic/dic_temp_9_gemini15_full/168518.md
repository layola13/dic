proboscis:/prəˈbɒsɪs/| n. |长鼻；象鼻；喙 |A proboscis is a long, slender, often flexible, tubular mouthpart of an animal, used for sucking up liquids or grasping objects.  It's typically found in insects, but can also refer to the trunk of an elephant. |例句：The butterfly used its proboscis to drink nectar from the flower. 蝴蝶用它的喙吸食花蜜。The elephant used its proboscis to pick up some leaves.  大象用它的象鼻卷起一些树叶。|近义词：trunk (of an elephant), snout, beak, rostrum |反义词：None specifically, but the opposite concept would be a short, blunt mouth.
