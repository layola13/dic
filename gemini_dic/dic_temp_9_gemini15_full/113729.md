macrodiagonal:/ˌmækroʊdaɪˈæɡənəl/| n.|主对角线|A macrodiagonal is a diagonal of a matrix that runs from the upper left to the lower right. In a square matrix, this is the main diagonal. In non-square matrices, it's the longest diagonal.  The term emphasizes the scale or "macro" nature of the diagonal, especially when compared to smaller internal diagonals within a larger structure. |Example: The macrodiagonal of this matrix contains the elements 1, 5, and 9.  矩阵的主对角线包含元素1、5和9。|近义词：main diagonal (for square matrices)|反义词：antidiagonal, secondary diagonal|名词
