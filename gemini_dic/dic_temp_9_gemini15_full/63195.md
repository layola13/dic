entasia:/ɛnˈteɪʒə/| n. |陶醉；狂喜；沉迷|A state of intense emotional absorption or delight; rapture.  The word suggests a deep and overwhelming feeling of joy or pleasure, often associated with artistic or spiritual experiences.|例句：The musician experienced a profound entasia during his performance, completely losing himself in the music.  这位音乐家在演出中体验到了深刻的陶醉，完全沉浸在音乐之中。|近义词：ecstasy, rapture, bliss, enchantment |反义词：misery, despair, apathy


USAGE->

This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, grammatical tenses (present, past, future, etc.) don't apply.  The word itself describes a state of being.

PHRASE->

There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "entasia."  Its usage is generally as a stand-alone noun.
