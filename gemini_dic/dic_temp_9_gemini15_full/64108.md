epoxies:/ɪˈpɒksɪz/ | n. |环氧树脂（复数） | A type of thermosetting polymer that is known for its strength, durability, and resistance to chemicals and heat.  Epoxies are formed through a chemical reaction between an epoxy resin and a hardener.  They are used in a wide variety of applications, including adhesives, coatings, and structural components. | Example:  The engineer used epoxies to repair the cracked pipe. 工程师用环氧树脂修补了破裂的管道。


The bridge was reinforced with epoxy resins. 这座桥用环氧树脂加固了。|近义词：resins, adhesives |反义词： (no direct antonyms, but materials with opposite properties could be considered, e.g.,  water-soluble polymers) |
