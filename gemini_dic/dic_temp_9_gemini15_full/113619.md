machiavellian:/ˌmækɪəˈvɛliən/ | adj. | 权谋的；狡诈的；马基雅维利的 |  Characterized by cunning, deceit, and a lack of moral scruples;  relating to or characteristic of Niccolò Machiavelli or his writings.  It describes someone who is skilled in political intrigue and manipulation, often at the expense of morality. |


例句：

His Machiavellian tactics ensured his victory, but at a considerable cost to his reputation.  他的权谋策略确保了他的胜利，但却以他名誉的巨大损失为代价。

The CEO's Machiavellian scheme to acquire the competing company involved a series of secret meetings and underhanded deals.  首席执行官收购竞争公司的权谋计划涉及一系列秘密会议和卑鄙交易。


近义词：cunning, shrewd, devious, manipulative, unscrupulous, scheming


反义词：honest, straightforward, upright, virtuous, moral, ethical


词性：形容词
