/dʒuːniər/|/ˈdʒuːnjər/ | adj. |  年少的；初级的；下级的 | Junior means younger, less experienced, or of lower rank. It is often used to distinguish between people with the same name, particularly in family situations or professional settings.  | He's the junior partner in the law firm. 他是这家律师事务所的初级合伙人。| senior, younger | senior, senior |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a junior member of the team. = 他是团队中的年轻成员。
She is a junior high school student. = 她是一名初中生。


一般过去时:
He was a junior associate last year. = 他去年是初级助理。
She was a junior programmer at that company. = 她在那家公司担任初级程序员。


现在完成时:
He has been a junior researcher for three years. = 他已经做了三年的初级研究员。
She has only recently become a junior partner. = 她最近才成为初级合伙人。


过去完成时:
He had been a junior analyst before he got promoted. = 他在升职之前是初级分析师。
She had already worked as a junior doctor for two years. = 她此前已经做了两年的初级医生。


将来时:
He will be a junior manager next year. = 他明年将成为初级经理。
She will likely become a junior executive soon. = 她很可能很快就会成为初级主管。


虚拟语气:
If he were a junior, he wouldn't have that much responsibility. = 如果他是初级员工，他不会有那么多责任。
I wish I were a junior again, without so many worries. = 我希望我还能回到年轻的时候，没有那么多担忧。


被动语态:
He was chosen as a junior representative. = 他被选为初级代表。
She was promoted from junior assistant to senior. = 她从初级助理晋升为高级助理。


疑问句:
Is he a junior or a senior? = 他是初级还是高级？
Are you a junior member of the club? = 你是俱乐部的初级会员吗？


否定句:
He is not a junior anymore. = 他不再是初级员工了。
She is not eligible because she is a junior. = 她因为是初级员工而没有资格。
