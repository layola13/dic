nonstructural: /ˌnɒnˈstrʌktʃərəl/ | adj. |非结构的，非构造的 | Not forming part of the main structure or framework; not relating to the structure.  |例句：The nonstructural components of the building, such as the furniture and decorations, were damaged in the fire. 建筑物的非结构性部件，如家具和装饰品，在火灾中受损。|近义词：unstructured, ancillary, supplementary |反义词：structural, integral, essential


The word "nonstructural" is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "structural,"  derived from "structure."  The origin of "structure" traces back to the Latin word "structūra," a combination of "struere" (to build, arrange) and the suffix "-tura" (act of, result of).
