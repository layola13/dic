criticized:/ˈkrɪtɪsaɪzd/| v. |批评，批判|To express disapproval of someone or something.|The film was criticized for its violence.  这部电影因其暴力而受到批评。|The politician's policies were heavily criticized.  这位政治家的政策受到了严厉批评。|praised, commended|approved, supported|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He criticizes everything. = 他批评一切。
They criticize her work constantly. = 他们不断地批评她的工作。

一般过去时:
The teacher criticized his essay. = 老师批评了他的作文。
She criticized my performance. = 她批评了我的表现。

现在完成时:
They have criticized the government's handling of the crisis. = 他们已经批评了政府处理危机的方案。
The public has criticized the company's response. = 公众批评了公司的回应。

过去完成时:
He had criticized the plan before the meeting. = 他在会议前就批评了这个计划。
They had criticized her ideas repeatedly. = 他们已经反复批评了她的想法。

将来时:
She will criticize his new book. = 她将批评他的新书。
They will criticize the proposal. = 他们将批评这项提案。

虚拟语气:
If he criticized me, I wouldn't care. = 如果他批评我，我也不会在乎。
I wish he hadn't criticized her so harshly. = 我希望他没有如此严厉地批评她。

被动语态:
His work was criticized by many people. = 他的作品受到了许多人的批评。
The new policy was severely criticized. = 新政策受到了严厉批评。

疑问句:
Why did they criticize him? = 他们为什么批评他？
Has anyone criticized her new design? = 有人批评过她的新设计吗？

否定句:
He didn't criticize her work. = 他没有批评她的工作。
They haven't criticized the plan yet. = 他们还没有批评这个计划。


PHRASE->
be criticized for = 因...而受批评
come under criticism = 受到批评
level criticism at = 对...提出批评
face criticism = 面临批评
open to criticism =  易受批评
