/ˌpærəˈnɒməzi/ | /ˌpærəˈnɑːməzi/ | n. | 双关语；谐音词 | Paronomasia, also known as punning, is a figure of speech in which similar-sounding words but with different meanings are used in close proximity to create a humorous or witty effect.  It often relies on wordplay and the exploitation of multiple meanings of a term or of terms that sound alike. |  He's got a real passion for his work – it's his passion, and his wage!
