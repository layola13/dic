caveated:/ˈkævɪeɪtɪd/| adj.|附有警告的；附有保留条件的|having conditions or limitations attached; qualified.  |例句：The agreement was caveated with a clause protecting the company from liability.  协议中有一条条款保护公司免受责任，从而使其附带了限制条件。|近义词：qualified, conditional, restricted, limited|反义词：unqualified, unconditional, unrestricted


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The contract is caveated with several conditions. = 合同附带了一些条件。
His statement is caveated by his lack of experience. = 他的声明由于缺乏经验而有所保留。


一般过去时:
The proposal was caveated by the board of directors. = 该提案受到了董事会的限制。
Her acceptance was caveated with a few specific requirements. = 她的接受附带了一些具体的条件。


现在完成时:
The deal has been caveated to ensure fairness. = 这笔交易已被附带条件以确保公平。
The agreement has been caveated by recent events. =  协议因最近发生的事件而有所修改。


过去完成时:
The decision had been caveated before the final vote. = 在最终投票之前，该决定就已经附带了条件。
The offer had been caveated with a time limit. = 报价已附带时间限制。


将来时:
The contract will be caveated to protect both parties. = 合同将附带条款以保护双方。
The announcement will be caveated with a strong warning. = 公告将附带严厉警告。


虚拟语气:
If the agreement were caveated, we might reconsider. = 如果协议附带条件，我们可能会重新考虑。
I wish the instructions had been caveated more clearly. = 我希望说明能更清晰地附带条件。


被动语态:
The statement was caveated to avoid misunderstandings. = 该声明附带条件以避免误解。
The research findings were caveated due to methodological limitations. = 由于方法论的局限性，研究结果附带了限制条件。


疑问句:
Is the offer caveated in any way? = 报价是否附带任何条件？
Are there any caveats to the proposed solution? = 提议的解决方案是否有任何限制条件？


否定句:
The guarantee was not caveated; it was absolute. = 保证并非附带条件；它是绝对的。
His promise was not caveated by any limitations. = 他的承诺没有任何限制条件。
