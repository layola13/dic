honorariums:/ˌɑːnəˈreəriəm/| n.|酬金，报酬|A payment made for services rendered, especially to someone who is not an employee.  It often implies a token payment rather than a proper salary or fee.|The guest speaker received an honorarium for his presentation.  演讲嘉宾收到了一笔演讲酬金。|The university offered her an honorarium for her work on the project. 大学为她在项目上的工作提供了一笔酬金。|fee, payment, remuneration|salary, wages|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company gives honorariums to its consultants. = 公司给顾问们支付酬金。
He receives an honorarium for each lecture. = 他每讲一次课就收到一笔酬金。

一般过去时:
She received a small honorarium for her translation work. = 她因为翻译工作收到了一笔小酬金。
They paid an honorarium to the musician for his performance. = 他们为音乐家的演出支付了一笔酬金。


现在完成时:
The organization has paid out many honorariums this year. = 今年该组织已经支付了许多酬金。

过去完成时:

将来时:
