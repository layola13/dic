disqualifying: /dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪɪŋ/ | adj. | 取消资格的，使失去资格的 |  Making someone ineligible; causing someone to be disqualified.  | 例句：His aggressive behavior was disqualifying for the competition.  他的侵略性行为使他失去了比赛资格。

His use of prohibited substances led to his disqualifying from the race. 他使用违禁药物导致他被取消比赛资格。


近义词：ineligible, invalidating, barring, debarring | 反义词：qualifying, enabling


词源:  The word "disqualifying" is formed from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of"), the verb "qualify" (meaning "to be or make suitable or eligible"), and the suffix "-ing" (forming a present participle or adjective).  The root of "qualify" comes from the Latin "qualificare," meaning "to make fit," from "qualitas," meaning "quality."
