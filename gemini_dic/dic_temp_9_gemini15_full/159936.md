polyphonism:/pɒliˈfəʊnɪzəm/| n. |复调；多声部；多音性|The use of several independent melodic lines simultaneously, characteristic of much Western classical music.  It refers to the texture created by combining multiple independent musical voices or parts.  The term is often used in music theory and analysis. |Example: The composer's mastery of polyphonism is evident in the intricate interplay of the vocal lines.  作曲家对复调的掌握在其人声线条的复杂交织中显而易见。  |近义词：polyphony, counterpoint |反义词：monophony, homophony
