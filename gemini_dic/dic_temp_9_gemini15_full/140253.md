offset:/ˈɒfset/ /ˈɔːfset/| n. v.|补偿；抵消；偏移；|To counteract or compensate for something.|The raise offset the increased cost of living. 加薪抵消了生活成本的增加。|近义词：counteract, compensate, balance|反义词：reinforce, aggravate


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The extra money offsets the higher taxes. = 这笔额外的钱抵消了更高的税收。
This advantage offsets the disadvantages. = 这种优势抵消了劣势。


一般过去时:
The good news offset the bad news. = 好消息抵消了坏消息。
The discount offset the high price. = 折扣抵消了高价。


现在完成时:
The increase in sales has offset the rising costs. = 销售额的增长已经抵消了成本的上升。
Their efforts have offset the negative impact. = 他们的努力抵消了负面影响。


过去完成时:
The earlier gains had been offset by recent losses. = 早期收益已被近期损失抵消。
The positive effects had been largely offset by unforeseen problems. = 正面影响在很大程度上被不可预见的问题抵消了。


将来时:
The government plans to offset the tax increase with spending cuts. = 政府计划通过削减开支来抵消税收的增加。
Increased efficiency will offset the rising energy costs. = 效率的提高将抵消不断上涨的能源成本。


虚拟语气:
If we could offset the risks, we would proceed. = 如果我们可以抵消风险，我们将继续进行。
If only we could offset the damage caused. = 要是我们能够抵消造成的损害就好了。


被动语态:
The negative effects were offset by government intervention. = 负面影响被政府干预抵消了。
The losses will be offset by future profits. = 损失将由未来的利润来弥补。


疑问句:
How can we offset the impact of climate change? = 我们如何才能抵消气候变化的影响？
Does this bonus offset the extra hours worked? = 这个奖金是否抵消了加班时间？


否定句:
Nothing could offset the devastation caused by the storm. = 没有任何东西能够抵消风暴造成的破坏。
The benefits did not offset the costs. = 收益并没有抵消成本。
