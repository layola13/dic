non-satiation:/ˌnɒnˌsæʃiˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 不满足感，贪欲 | The state or quality of not being satiated; insatiable desire or appetite.  It often refers to an unending desire for more of something, whether material possessions, experiences, or power. |  例句：The non-satiation of the wealthy led to the exploitation of the poor.  富人的贪得无厌导致了对穷人的剥削。

例句：Her non-satiation for knowledge drove her to pursue advanced studies. 她对知识永无止境的渴望驱使她继续深造。


近义词：insatiability, greed, avarice, rapaciousness

反义词：satiation, contentment, satisfaction


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the noun "satiation" (the act of satisfying a desire or need to the full). "Satiation" itself comes from the Latin word "satiare," meaning "to satisfy."  Therefore, "non-satiation" literally means "not being satisfied."
