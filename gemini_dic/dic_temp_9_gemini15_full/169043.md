prognosis:/prɒɡˈnəʊsɪs/| /prɑːɡˈnoʊsɪs/| n. |预后，预测，预兆 |The prediction of the likely course of a disease or ailment.  A prognosis is a forecast of the probable outcome of a disease or situation. It often involves considering various factors, including the patient's health status, the nature of the illness, and the effectiveness of treatment. |例句：The doctor gave a positive prognosis for her recovery.  医生对她康复的预后很乐观。

The prognosis for the economy is uncertain. 经济前景不明朗。|近义词：forecast, prediction, prediction, forecast, outlook |反义词：retrospect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The doctor gives a good prognosis. = 医生给出了一个良好的预后。
Her prognosis is positive. = 她的预后是积极的。


一般过去时:
The doctor gave a poor prognosis. = 医生给出了一个糟糕的预后。
They received a discouraging prognosis. = 他们收到了一个令人沮丧的预后。


现在完成时:
Doctors have given a cautiously optimistic prognosis. = 医生给出了一个谨慎乐观的预后。
The prognosis has improved significantly. = 预后已显著改善。


过去完成时:
The initial prognosis had been very bleak. = 最初的预后非常黯淡。
They had received a grim prognosis before the surgery. = 他们在手术前收到了一个严峻的预后。


将来时:
The prognosis will likely be better with early treatment. = 早期治疗可能会使预后更好。
We will know the prognosis after the tests. = 我们将在测试后知道预后。


虚拟语气:
If the prognosis were better, we would be less worried. = 如果预后更好，我们会少担心一些。
I wish the prognosis was more optimistic. = 我希望预后更乐观一些。


被动语态:
The prognosis was determined to be favorable. = 预后被确定为良好。
A positive prognosis is being given to the patient. = 患者正在得到积极的预后。


疑问句:
What is the prognosis? = 预后如何？

否定句:
The prognosis is not good. = 预后不好。
The doctor did not give a favorable prognosis. = 医生没有给出良好的预后。
