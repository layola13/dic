cystid:/ˈsɪstɪd/| n. |囊体，胞囊|A cystid is a type of extinct echinoderm, characterized by its sac-like body and the absence of arms.  They are known from fossils found in Paleozoic rocks. |Example:  The researchers discovered a well-preserved cystid fossil in the Ordovician strata.  研究人员在奥陶纪地层中发现了一个保存完好的囊体化石。|近义词：echinoderm (broadly)|反义词：None specifically|名词
