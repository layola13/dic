quasi-zealous: /ˈkwɑːzaɪˈzeləs/ | adj. | 似热心的；貌似热情的 |

英文解析:  Appearing zealous or enthusiastic, but not genuinely so; superficially fervent.  It suggests a show of zeal that lacks true conviction or depth of feeling.

例句:

His quasi-zealous support for the project seemed to wane as soon as funding was threatened. = 他对这个项目的似热心支持，一旦资金受到威胁就似乎减弱了。

The quasi-zealous campaigner spoke passionately, but his actions contradicted his words. = 这位貌似热情的竞选者慷慨激昂地演讲，但他的行动与他的话语相矛盾。


近义词:  pretentious, affected, insincere, half-hearted, perfunctory

反义词:  genuine, sincere, heartfelt, passionate, ardent


词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "as it were," "almost," or "seemingly."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation.

* **zealous**: From Middle English *zelous*, from Old French *zelos*, from Latin *zelus*, from Greek *zêlos* meaning "zeal," "emulation," "rivalry," "jealousy."
