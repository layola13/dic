papershuffler's: /ˈpeɪpərˌʃʌflər/ | n. | 文件管理员；办事员；（贬义）官僚；只会做些无关紧要工作的人 |


英文解析：A papershuffler is someone whose job involves a lot of routine paperwork and administrative tasks, often implying a lack of significant responsibility or intellectual stimulation.  The term often carries a negative connotation, suggesting inefficiency or a lack of genuine contribution.


例句：

He's just a papershuffler in the government office, pushing papers around all day. 他只是政府办公室里的一名办事员，整天处理文件。

They accused the manager of being a papershuffler, more concerned with appearances than results. 他们指责经理是只会做表面文章的官僚，更关心的是外表而不是结果。


近义词：clerk, bureaucrat, administrator, file clerk, office worker (with negative connotations)


反义词：executive, leader, innovator, decision-maker
