ephor:/ˈɛfɔːr/| n.|监察官；执政官|An ephor was a magistrate in ancient Sparta, one of five annually elected officials who held considerable power in the state.  They supervised the education of youth, controlled the kings, and acted as judges in certain cases.  Their power was significant in limiting the authority of the Spartan kings.|The Ephors held considerable power in ancient Sparta.|近义词：magistrate, overseer, supervisor|反义词：subject, subordinate
