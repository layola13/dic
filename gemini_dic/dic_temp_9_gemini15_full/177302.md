reassortments:/ˌriːəˈsɔːrtmənts/ | n. |重新分类；重新分配；基因重排 |The process of rearranging or regrouping items or elements, often in a systematic way.  In biology, it refers to the rearrangement of genetic material, particularly RNA segments in viruses. |例句：The reassortments of influenza viruses can lead to new strains. 流感病毒的基因重排会导致新的病毒株。

The company's reassortments of inventory improved efficiency. 公司对库存的重新分类提高了效率。|近义词：rearrangements, regroupings, reshufflings |反义词：uniformity, consistency
