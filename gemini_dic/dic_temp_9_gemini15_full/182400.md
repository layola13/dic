reneging:/rɪˈniːɡɪŋ/| v.|违约；食言；背弃；|The act of going back on a promise, commitment, or contract.  It implies a deliberate and often unfair breaking of an agreement.|例句：He's reneging on his promise to help us. 他违背了帮助我们的承诺。|例句：The company is reneging on its contract with the supplier.  这家公司正在违反与供应商的合同。|近义词：breaking a promise, defaulting, backing out, welshing|反义词：keeping a promise, honoring a commitment, upholding a contract|

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reneges on his deals frequently. = 他经常违背他的交易。
They renege on their responsibilities. = 他们逃避责任。


一般过去时:
She reneged on her agreement to sell the house. = 她违背了出售房子的协议。
He reneged on his promise to pay back the loan. = 他违背了偿还贷款的承诺。


现在完成时:
They have reneged on their commitment to the project. = 他们已经违背了对项目的承诺。
The government has reneged on its promises to reduce taxes. = 政府已经违背了减税的承诺。


过去完成时:
I discovered he had reneged on our deal before I had even signed the contract. = 我发现在他签合同之前他就已经违背了我们的协议。
She realized that he had reneged on his promise to be there. = 她意识到他违背了要在那里等她的承诺。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
