词源:
The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "hackneyed," which itself derives from "hackney," an old word for a horse for hire, implying something overused and commonplace.  Therefore, "nonhackneyed" signifies something that is *not* overused or commonplace.
