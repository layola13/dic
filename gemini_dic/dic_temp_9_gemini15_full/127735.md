negotiable:/nɪˈɡoʊʃiəbl/| adj. | 可协商的，可谈判的 | Capable of being negotiated or bargained over; able to be changed or altered by discussion or agreement.  | Example: The price is negotiable. 价格可议。  The deadline is not negotiable. 截止日期不可协商。|近义词：discussable, debatable, flexible |反义词：fixed, inflexible, immutable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The price is negotiable. = 价格可协商。
The terms of the contract are negotiable. = 合同条款可协商。


一般过去时:
The salary was negotiable. = 工资是可以协商的。
The details of the agreement were negotiable. = 协议的细节是可以协商的。


现在完成时:
The terms have been negotiable since the beginning. = 条款从一开始就可协商。
We have made the schedule negotiable for your convenience. = 为了您的方便，我们已经使时间表可以协商。


过去完成时:
The conditions had been negotiable before the final decision. = 在最终决定之前，条件是可以协商的。
They had already made it clear that the price was negotiable. = 他们已经明确表示价格是可以协商的。


将来时:
The contract will be negotiable next week. = 合同下周可以协商。
The payment terms will be negotiable once we receive the proposal. = 一旦我们收到提案，付款条款就可以协商。


虚拟语气:
If the price were negotiable, I would buy it. = 如果价格可以协商，我会买下它。
I wish the deadline were negotiable. = 我希望截止日期可以协商。


被动语态:
The issue is being negotiated between the two parties. = 这个问题正在双方之间进行协商。
The contract was negotiated by both sides. = 合同由双方协商达成。


疑问句:
Is the price negotiable? = 价格可议吗？
Are the terms of service negotiable? = 服务条款可协商吗？


否定句:
The deadline is not negotiable. = 截止日期不可协商。
The rules are not negotiable. = 规则不可协商。


PHRASE->
highly negotiable:  非常可以协商的
reasonably negotiable:  比较可以协商的
open to negotiation:  可以协商的
subject to negotiation:  有待协商的
mutually negotiable:  双方可以协商的
