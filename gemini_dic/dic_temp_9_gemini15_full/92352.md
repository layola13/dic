hypocapnic:/ˌhaɪpəʊˈkæpnɪk/| adj. |低碳酸血症的|Characterized by or causing abnormally low levels of carbon dioxide in the blood.  This condition can result from hyperventilation (breathing too fast), which expels too much carbon dioxide. Symptoms can include dizziness, lightheadedness, tingling in the extremities, and in severe cases, loss of consciousness.|The patient presented with hypocapnic symptoms after a period of rapid breathing.|近义词：hypocarbia|反义词：hypercapnic
