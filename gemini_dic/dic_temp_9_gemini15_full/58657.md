dumdum: /ˈdʌmˌdʌm/ | n. adj. | 笨蛋；傻瓜；钝头弹 |

英文解析：Dumdum can be used as both a noun and an adjective. As a noun, it refers to a stupid or foolish person. As an adjective, it describes something blunt or dull, particularly referring to a type of bullet.  The origin of the word as a noun for a fool is uncertain, but possibly onomatopoeic. The use referring to bullets comes from the Dum Dum Arsenal in India where this type of ammunition was developed.

例句：

He's such a dumdum; he can't even tie his shoelaces.  = 他真是个笨蛋，连鞋带都不会系。

That knife is a bit dumdum; it needs sharpening. = 那把刀有点钝，需要磨一磨。


近义词 (noun):  idiot, fool, imbecile, moron

近义词 (adjective): blunt, dull, obtuse


反义词 (noun): genius, intellectual, bright

反义词 (adjective): sharp, pointed


词源:

The noun usage's origin is uncertain, potentially onomatopoeic mimicking a sound associated with stupidity. The adjective usage originates from the Dum Dum Arsenal in Kolkata, India, where a type of expanding bullet (dumdum bullet) was developed.  This bullet is designed to inflict more severe wounds than a standard bullet.
