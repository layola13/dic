fulminatory:/ˈfʊlmɪnətɔːri/|/ˈfʌlmɪnətɔːri/| adj.| 怒斥的；谴责的；爆炸的 | Characterized by or given to vehement denunciation; explosive.  The speaker's fulminatory speech against the government ignited the crowd.  |近义词：thunderous, explosive, denunciatory, condemnatory |反义词：approving, praising, laudatory


例句：

His fulminatory rhetoric against the opposition party was met with boos and jeers. = 他对反对党的强烈谴责受到了嘘声和嘲笑。

The professor's fulminatory lecture left the students feeling intimidated. = 教授的怒斥性讲座让学生们感到害怕。

词源:

From Latin *fulminatōrius, from fulmināre "to strike with lightning," from fulmen "lightning."  The sense evolved from the literal "lightning-like" to the figurative "sudden and forceful."
