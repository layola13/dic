repossessions:/ˌriːpəˈzeʃənz/ | n. | 资产收回；收回；查封 | The act of taking back property that has been pledged as security for a loan when the borrower defaults.  It often refers to the process of reclaiming vehicles, homes, or other assets. |例句：The bank initiated repossessions on several properties due to unpaid mortgages. = 由于未偿还抵押贷款，银行对几处房产启动了收回程序。

The increasing number of repossessions is a sign of economic hardship. =  越来越多的资产收回是经济困难的一个迹象。|近义词：recovery, seizure, foreclosure |反义词：acquisition, purchase
