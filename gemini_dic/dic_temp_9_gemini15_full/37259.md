coming:/ˈkʌmɪŋ/| adj. |即将到来的，未来的；有前途的 |Approaching in time; about to happen or arrive.  Showing promise or potential for future success. |例句：The coming week will be busy. = 接下来的一周将会很忙碌。  He's a coming man in the business world. = 他是商界冉冉升起的新星。|近义词：forthcoming, approaching, imminent, promising |反义词：past, gone, departed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The train is coming. = 火车来了。
Something good is coming. = 好事将近。

一般过去时:
The letter came yesterday. = 信昨天到了。
He was coming home when it rained. = 他回家时下起了雨。

现在完成时:
The package has come. = 包裹已经到了。
Spring has come early this year. = 今年春天来得早。

过去完成时:
She had come before I arrived. = 我到之前她就来了。
They had come to a decision before the meeting. = 会议之前他们已经做出了决定。

将来时:
The bus will be coming soon. = 公车很快就要来了。
I will be coming to your party next week. = 我下周会来参加你的派对。


虚拟语气:
If spring came earlier, we could start planting sooner. = 如果春天来得早一些，我们就能早点开始播种了。
I wish summer was coming sooner. = 我希望夏天快点到来。


被动语态:
The news was coming over the radio. = 新闻正在广播中播放。
The decision was coming soon. = 决定即将做出。


疑问句:
Is the bus coming? = 公车来了吗？
When is the next train coming? = 下一趟火车什么时候来？


否定句:
The train isn't coming yet. = 火车还没来。
Nothing good is coming from this situation. = 这种情况不会带来任何好处。


PHRASE->
coming of age = 成年；成熟
coming soon = 即将到来
coming events = 未来事件
the coming year = 来年
coming to grips with = 开始认真处理


词源:
The word "coming" originates from the Old English verb *cuman*, meaning "to come".  This verb has Germanic roots and is related to similar words in other Germanic languages.  The present participle "coming" developed naturally from this base verb.
