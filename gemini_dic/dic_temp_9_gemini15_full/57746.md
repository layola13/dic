dreamful: /ˈdriːmfʊl/ | adj. | 梦幻般的，充满梦想的，沉思的 | Full of dreams; dreamy; lost in thought.  The word suggests a state of being lost in thought, often associated with pleasant or romantic imaginings. It implies a somewhat wistful or pensive quality. |例句：The dreamful gaze in her eyes hinted at a world of unspoken desires. 她眼神中梦幻般的凝视暗示着无数未言明的渴望。

The soft, dreamful music filled the room. 柔和梦幻的音乐充满了房间。


近义词：dreamy, fantastical, visionary, imaginative, pensive | 反义词：realistic, pragmatic, down-to-earth


词源:  The word "dreamful" is a relatively straightforward combination of "dream" and the suffix "-ful," which indicates "full of."  The root "dream" has Old English origins, tracing back to *dream, with Germanic roots suggesting a connection to concepts of joy or gladness. The "-ful" suffix is a common English derivational suffix creating adjectives.
