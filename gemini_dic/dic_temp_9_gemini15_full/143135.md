outcasts:/ˈaʊtˌkæsts/| n.|被抛弃者，被逐出者，不受欢迎的人|People who have been rejected or excluded from society or a group.  They are often marginalized and ostracized due to their differences or perceived flaws.|例句：The outcasts formed their own community. 被抛弃的人们形成了他们自己的社区。

例句：He felt like an outcast in his new school. 在他的新学校里，他感觉像个局外人。


近义词：pariahs, rejects, exiles, misfits, undesirables


反义词：favorites, insiders, elites, darlings


词性:名词

词源:
The word "outcast" comes from the Old English words "ūt" (out) and "caest" (cast), meaning "thrown out."  It reflects the literal act of expelling someone from a group or society.
