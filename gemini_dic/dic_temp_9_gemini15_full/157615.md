pitiable:/ˈpɪtɪəbl/| adj. |可怜的；值得同情的；令人厌恶的 | deserving or causing pity; wretched; contemptible.  |例句：The homeless man's condition was pitiable.  那个无家可归的人的境况令人怜悯。

例句：His attempts at humor were pitiable. 他的幽默尝试很糟糕。

近义词：pathetic, lamentable, deplorable, wretched, miserable

反义词：admirable, commendable, enviable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The child's situation is pitiable. = 这孩子的处境很可怜。
His performance is pitiable. = 他的表演很糟糕。


一般过去时:
Her plight was pitiable. = 她的困境令人同情。
His excuse was pitiable. = 他的借口很可笑。


现在完成时:
His behavior has been pitiable. = 他的行为一直很糟糕。
The state of the building has become pitiable. = 这座建筑物的状况变得很糟糕。


过去完成时:
The dog's condition had been pitiable before we rescued it. = 在我们救它之前，这只狗的状况很可怜。
His efforts had been pitiable from the start. = 从一开始他的努力就令人失望。


将来时:
The situation will become pitiable if we don't act soon. = 如果我们不尽快采取行动，情况将会变得很糟糕。
His health will be pitiable without treatment. = 不治疗他的健康状况将会很糟糕。


虚拟语气:

I wish his attempts weren't so pitiable. = 我希望他的尝试不会这么糟糕。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
From Middle English *pitiable, from pitien "to pity" + -able.  Ultimately from Old French pitier, from Latin pati "to suffer".
