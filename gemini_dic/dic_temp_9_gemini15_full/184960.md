retches: /ˈretʃɪz/ | v. |干呕；恶心；呕吐 | To make the sound and movements of vomiting, without actually vomiting.  |例句：He retched violently after drinking the spoiled milk. 他喝了变质的牛奶后剧烈地干呕。

The feeling of nausea made her retch. 恶心让她想吐。|近义词：gag, heave, vomit |反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "retch" comes from the Middle English word *rechen*, meaning "to stretch, reach," which is related to the Old English word *rǣcan*.  The sense of "to vomit" developed later, likely from the physical act of straining and reaching during vomiting.
