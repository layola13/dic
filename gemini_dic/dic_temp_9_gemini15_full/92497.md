hypothermia:/ˌhaɪpəˈθɜːrmiə/|/ˌhaɪpoʊˈθɜːrmiə/| n. |体温过低，低体温症 |A condition in which the body's temperature falls below the normal range, typically below 35°C (95°F).  This can be caused by prolonged exposure to cold temperatures, or by certain medical conditions.  Symptoms can include shivering, confusion, drowsiness, and slow heart rate.  Severe hypothermia can be life-threatening.|The hiker suffered from severe hypothermia after being stranded in the blizzard for hours.  登山者在暴风雪中被困数小时后患上了严重的低体温症。| hyperthermia, fever |normothermia, hyperthermia
