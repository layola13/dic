/ˌpriːˈpʌnɪʃ/ | v. | 预先惩罚 | To punish beforehand or in anticipation of an offense.  It implies a punishment given before the actual wrongdoing, often as a preventative measure or a form of intimidation. | The dictator prepunished any potential dissenters by arresting them without trial.  独裁者通过未经审判就逮捕潜在的异议者来预先惩罚他们。|  近义词：preemptively punish, anticipate and punish | 反义词：reward, pardon

He prepunishes potential threats. = 他预先惩罚潜在的威胁。
The company prepunishes employees for minor infractions. = 公司对员工的轻微违规行为进行预先惩罚。
