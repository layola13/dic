pedipalp:/ˈpɛdɪpælp/| n. |触肢|The pedipalps are a pair of appendages near the mouth of arachnids (like spiders, scorpions, and mites) that are used for a variety of functions, depending on the species.  They can be used for sensing, capturing prey, manipulating food, mating, or defense.|Example: The scorpion uses its pedipalps to grasp and subdue its prey before injecting venom.  蝎子用它的触肢抓住并制服猎物，然后注射毒液。|近义词: palp (less specific)|反义词: N/A
