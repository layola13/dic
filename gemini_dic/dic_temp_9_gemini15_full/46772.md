dangerous:/ˈdeɪndʒərəs/| adj.|危险的，不安全的|The word "dangerous" describes something that poses a risk or threat of harm, injury, or damage.  It implies a significant potential for negative consequences. |例句：That area is dangerous at night.  那个区域晚上很危险。
例句：Handling this chemical is dangerous without proper safety equipment.  如果没有合适的安全设备，处理这种化学物质很危险。|近义词：hazardous, perilous, risky, unsafe|反义词：safe, secure, harmless


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The situation is dangerous. = 情况很危险。
That dog looks dangerous. = 那条狗看起来很危险。

一般过去时:
The bridge was dangerous. = 那座桥很危险。
The journey was more dangerous than we expected. = 这段旅程比我们预期的更危险。

现在完成时:
The situation has become dangerous. = 情况变得危险了。
Things have become increasingly dangerous recently. = 最近情况越来越危险了。

过去完成时:
We didn't realize how dangerous the situation had become. = 我们没有意识到情况已经变得多么危险。
The warning signs indicated that the area had been dangerous for years. = 警示标志表明该地区多年来一直很危险。

将来时:
The mission will be dangerous. = 这项任务将会很危险。
It will become dangerous to stay here. = 待在这里将会变得危险。

虚拟语气:
If I knew it was dangerous, I wouldn't have gone. = 如果我知道很危险，我就不会去了。
It would be dangerous to ignore the warning signs. = 忽视警告标志是很危险的。

被动语态:
The area is considered dangerous. = 该地区被认为很危险。
The building was declared dangerous and condemned. = 该建筑被宣布为危险建筑并被拆除。

疑问句:
Is this path dangerous? = 这条路危险吗？
Are the chemicals dangerous to handle? = 这些化学品危险吗？

否定句:
The road isn't dangerous during the day. = 白天这条路不危险。
It's not dangerous if you follow the safety precautions. = 如果你遵循安全预防措施，那就不会危险。
