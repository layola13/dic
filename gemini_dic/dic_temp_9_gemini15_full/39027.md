conflate:/kənˈfleɪt/ | vt. |合并；混淆；融合 |To combine or merge into a single entity.  To combine (things, especially ideas) into a whole that is more complex or confused than its parts. |例句：The author conflates several different historical events in his narrative. 作者在他的叙事中将几个不同的历史事件混杂在一起。

例句：Don't conflate correlation with causation. 不要将相关性与因果关系混淆。

近义词：combine, merge, blend, fuse, amalgamate

反义词：separate, differentiate, distinguish, sever

词源:
From Latin *conflāre "to blow together," from com- "together" + flāre "to blow."  The original sense referred to the melting of metals, which evolved to the metaphorical sense of blending or merging things together.
