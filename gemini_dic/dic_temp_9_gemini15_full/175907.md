ratify:/ˈrædəfaɪ/ | vt. |批准；认可；正式确认 | To approve and officially confirm something, especially a treaty or agreement.  | The Senate ratified the treaty after a lengthy debate. 参议院在长时间的辩论后批准了该条约。 |approve, confirm, sanction |reject, annul, invalidate |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government ratifies many international agreements each year. = 政府每年批准许多国际协议。
They ratify the contract after careful consideration. = 他们在仔细考虑后批准了合同。


一般过去时:
The parliament ratified the new law last week. = 议会在上周批准了新法律。
The committee ratified his proposal unanimously. = 委员会一致批准了他的提案。


现在完成时:
They have ratified the amendment to the constitution. = 他们已经批准了宪法修正案。
The treaty has been ratified by all signatory states. = 所有签署国都已批准该条约。


过去完成时:
The agreement had been ratified before the deadline. = 该协议在截止日期前已获得批准。
By the time the meeting ended, the proposal had been ratified. = 会议结束时，提案已经获得批准。


将来时:
The council will ratify the budget next month. = 理事会将在下个月批准预算。
We expect the court will ratify the decision. = 我们预计法院将批准这项决定。


虚拟语气:
If the president ratified the bill, it would become law. = 如果总统批准该法案，它将成为法律。
I wish they would ratify the agreement quickly. = 我希望他们能迅速批准这项协议。


被动语态:
The treaty was ratified by the Senate. = 该条约由参议院批准。
The amendments will be ratified by the general assembly. = 修正案将由大会批准。


疑问句:
Has the treaty been ratified yet? = 该条约已经批准了吗？
Will the agreement be ratified by the end of the year? = 该协议将在年底前获得批准吗？


否定句:
The legislature did not ratify the proposed changes. = 立法机构没有批准拟议的变更。
The bill failed to be ratified by the Senate. = 该法案未获得参议院批准。
