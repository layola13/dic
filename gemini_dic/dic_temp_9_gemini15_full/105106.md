kerosene:/ˈkɛrəsiːn/ | n. |煤油，火油 | A clear, colorless or yellowish flammable liquid hydrocarbon oil obtained by distilling petroleum, used as a fuel and solvent.  |The lamp was fueled by kerosene. 这盏灯用煤油作燃料。|  petrol, paraffin |  (no direct antonym) |名词

词源:
The word "kerosene" originates from the Greek word "keras" meaning "wax" and was coined in the mid-19th century.  The suffix "-ene" is a common chemical suffix for hydrocarbons.  Different spellings and names were used before it standardized.
