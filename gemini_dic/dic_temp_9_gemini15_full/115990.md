marshaled: /ˈmɑːʃəld/ | v. |  整理；调动；排列；引导 | To arrange or assemble (troops, equipment, etc.) in order; to arrange systematically; to lead or guide. |

例句：
The general marshaled his troops for battle. = 将军把他的军队整顿好准备战斗。

The evidence was carefully marshaled to support the prosecution's case. = 证据被仔细地整理以支持起诉方的案情。


近义词：arranged, organized, assembled, mobilized, mustered

反义词：disorganized, scattered, disordered

词性：动词

词源:
源自古法语 *maréchal，意为“马夫”，后演变为军事指挥官的称号，最终引申出“整理”、“调动”等含义。  The word ultimately derives from the Old French *maréchal, meaning "horseman," which later became a title for a military commander.  The meaning evolved to encompass the sense of organizing and assembling.
