over-hard: /ˌoʊvərˈhɑːrd/ | adj. | 煎得很老的（指鸡蛋）|  Describes an egg that has been fried until the whites are completely set and the yolk is firm and possibly slightly browned or even crispy.  It's a term specifically used in the context of cooking eggs. | Example: I like my eggs over-hard.  我喜欢我的鸡蛋煎得很老。| 近义词：well-done (in the context of eggs), crispy (referring to the yolk)| 反义词：sunny-side up, over-easy, over-medium


USAGE->

This word is an adjective and thus does not have verb conjugations.  It describes the state of a fried egg.  Grammatical examples would be used in sentences describing eggs cooked in this way.  The examples below show how "over-hard" is used in different sentence structures.


Declarative Sentence:
The eggs were over-hard and a little dry. = 鸡蛋煎得很老，有点干。

Interrogative Sentence:
Do you prefer your eggs over-hard or over-medium? = 你喜欢你的鸡蛋煎得很老还是半熟？


Exclamatory Sentence:
These eggs are over-hard! = 这些鸡蛋煎得太老了！

Negative Sentence:
I don't like my eggs over-hard; I prefer them over-easy. = 我不喜欢我的鸡蛋煎得很老；我更喜欢半熟的。
