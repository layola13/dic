heptateuch:/ˈheptətuːk/| n. |七经；七卷书|A heptateuch is a collection of seven books, particularly referring to the first seven books of the Bible (Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy, Joshua, and Judges) considered as a unit.  This term is less common than "Pentateuch" (the first five books).|The heptateuch is sometimes studied as a single narrative unit, emphasizing the continuity of the story from creation to the settlement of the Israelites in Canaan.|近义词：Pentateuch (but includes fewer books)|反义词：无
