loopholes:/ˈluːphɔːlz/ | n. |漏洞，空子；缺陷 | A loophole is a flaw or ambiguity in a system, plan, or law that can be exploited to gain an unfair advantage or avoid compliance.  It's a way to get around something, often in a legal or regulatory context. | Example: The company found a loophole in the tax laws that allowed them to avoid paying millions of dollars.  这家公司找到了税法中的漏洞，从而避免支付数百万美元的税款。


Example:  The contract contained several loopholes that could be exploited. 这份合同包含几个可以利用的漏洞。|近义词：flaw, defect, gap, weakness |反义词：integrity, soundness, perfection
