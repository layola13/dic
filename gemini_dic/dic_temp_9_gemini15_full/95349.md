incentivized:/ɪnˈsɛntaˌvaɪzd/ | adj. | 激励的；有动机的 |  Provided with an incentive; motivated.  The new policy incentivized employees to work harder.  新的政策激励员工更加努力工作。 | motivated, stimulated, encouraged | discouraged, demotivated | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company incentivizes its employees with bonuses. = 公司用奖金激励员工。
This program incentivizes students to study harder. = 这个项目激励学生更加努力学习。

一般过去时:
The government incentivized renewable energy development with tax breaks. = 政府通过减税来激励可再生能源发展。
They incentivized participation by offering free gifts. = 他们通过提供免费礼物来激励参与。

现在完成时:
The reforms have incentivized foreign investment. = 改革已经激励了外国投资。
Increased pay has incentivized better performance. = 加薪激励了更好的表现。

过去完成时:
The company had incentivized its sales team before the launch. = 公司在产品发布前已经激励了销售团队。
They had incentivized employees with promotions in the past. = 他们过去曾通过晋升来激励员工。

将来时:
The new policy will incentivize greater efficiency. = 新政策将激励更高的效率。
We will incentivize our customers with loyalty points. = 我们将用积分奖励我们的客户。

虚拟语气:
If the company incentivized its employees more, productivity would increase. = 如果公司更多地激励员工，生产力就会提高。
I wish they had incentivized participation earlier. = 我希望他们早点激励参与。

被动语态:
Employees were incentivized with early retirement packages. = 员工被激励接受提前退休方案。
The project was incentivized by government funding. = 该项目得到了政府资金的激励。

疑问句:
How are employees incentivized in your company? = 贵公司如何激励员工？
What methods are used to incentivize innovation? = 使用什么方法来激励创新？

否定句:
The lack of incentives did not incentivize employees. = 缺乏激励没有激励员工。
The policy failed to incentivize change. = 该政策未能激励变革。
