etesian:/ɪˈtiːziən/| adj.|季风的；信风的|  A term referring to winds that blow regularly during the summer in the Mediterranean region.  These winds are typically northerly, originating from the Eurasian landmass, and are associated with high pressure systems. They bring dry and hot weather.| Example: The etesian winds brought a welcome respite from the intense summer heat in Greece.|近义词：summer monsoon, northerly wind |反义词：winter monsoon, southerly wind
