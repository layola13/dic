self-mutilating: /ˌsɛlf ˈmjuːtɪleɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 自残的；自我伤害的 |  Describing the act of intentionally injuring oneself, often as a coping mechanism for emotional distress or mental illness.  It implies a repetitive and harmful behavior. |


例句：

The therapist noticed signs of self-mutilating behavior in her patient.  = 治疗师注意到她的病人有自残行为的迹象。

His self-mutilating tendencies were a symptom of his deep-seated depression. = 他的自残倾向是他根深蒂固的抑郁症的症状。


近义词：self-harming, self-injurious, masochistic (in some contexts)


反义词：self-caring, self-preserving


词性：形容词


词源:

* **self-**:  Prefix meaning "of or relating to oneself."  From Old English *self.

* **mutilate**:  Verb meaning "to inflict serious damage on; to injure or impair." From Latin *mutilāre ("to maim," "to cripple"), from *mutilus ("maimed," "incomplete").
