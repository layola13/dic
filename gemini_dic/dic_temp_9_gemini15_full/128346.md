neutralization:/ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 中和作用；抵消；使无效 | The process of rendering something neutral or ineffective, especially by counteracting its effects.  This can refer to chemical reactions where acids and bases react to form neutral salts and water, or to other contexts where opposing forces cancel each other out. | Example: The addition of an alkali caused the neutralization of the acid.  加入碱导致酸的中和。

The neutralization of the enemy's attack was crucial to our victory.  敌方攻击的中和对我们的胜利至关重要。|近义词：counteraction, cancellation, inactivation |反义词：activation, intensification
