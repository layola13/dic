busy:/ˈbɪzi/ | adj. | 忙碌的，繁忙的 |  Busy means occupied or actively engaged in work or activity. It can refer to a person, place, or thing.  |例句：I'm too busy to go out tonight.  今晚我太忙了，不能出去。

I have a busy schedule this week.  我这周日程安排很紧。


近义词：occupied, engaged, hectic, active | 反义词：free, idle, unoccupied, leisurely


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is busy with his work. = 他忙于他的工作。
She is always busy. = 她总是很忙。


一般过去时:
I was busy yesterday. = 我昨天很忙。
They were busy preparing for the exam. = 他们忙着准备考试。


现在完成时:
I have been busy all day. = 我一整天都很忙。
She has been busy with her project. = 她一直忙于她的项目。


过去完成时:
I had been busy all week before the holiday. = 假期前我忙了一周。
They had been busy working on the report. = 他们一直忙着做报告。


将来时:
I will be busy tomorrow. = 我明天会很忙。
He will be busy with the meeting. = 他将忙于会议。


虚拟语气:
If I weren't so busy, I would travel more. = 如果我不那么忙，我会多旅行。
I wish I weren't so busy. = 我希望我不那么忙。


被动语态: (Busy itself is rarely used in the passive voice.  It's usually the person or thing that is described as busy.)
The office is always busy. = 办公室总是很忙。(Here, "busy" describes the state of the office.)


疑问句:
Are you busy right now? = 你现在忙吗？
Is he busy this afternoon? = 他今天下午忙吗？


否定句:
I'm not busy today. = 我今天不忙。
She wasn't busy yesterday. = 她昨天不忙。



PHRASE->
busy signal: 占线信号
busy body: 爱管闲事的人
too busy: 太忙
keep busy: 保持忙碌
get busy: 开始忙碌
