defamous:/ˈdɛfəməs/| adj.|诽谤的；中伤的；声名狼藉的|Damaging the good reputation of someone; causing disgrace or dishonor.|The defamatory article ruined his career. 这篇诽谤的文章毁了他的事业。|近义词：slanderous, libelous, calumnious|反义词：laudatory, complimentary


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is defamatory to his colleagues. 他诽谤他的同事。
That statement is defamatory. 那句话是诽谤性的。


一般过去时:
She defamed him in public. 她在公众场合诽谤他。
The newspaper defamed the politician. 报纸诽谤了这位政治家。


现在完成时:
They have defamed him repeatedly. 他们反复诽谤他。
He has been defamed by the media. 他受到了媒体的诽谤。


过去完成时:
They had defamed her before the trial. 他们在审判前就诽谤了她。
The rumors had defamed his reputation long before the scandal broke.  谣言早在丑闻爆发之前就毁了他的名誉。


将来时:
They will defame him again if given the chance.  如果给他们机会，他们还会再次诽谤他。
He will be defamed if he continues his actions. 如果他继续他的行为，他将会被诽谤。


虚拟语气:
If he had not defamed her, she would not have sued him. 如果他没有诽谤她，她就不会起诉他。
I wish they hadn't defamed my good name. 我希望他们没有诽谤我的好名声。


被动语态:
He was defamed by his rival. 他被他的竞争对手诽谤了。
His reputation was defamed by false accusations. 他的名誉被虚假指控所玷污。


疑问句:
Was he defamed by the press? 他被媒体诽谤了吗？
Were they defamed in the article?  他们在文章中被诽谤了吗？


否定句:
He did not defame her intentionally. 他并非故意诽谤她。
The article did not defame anyone. 这篇文章没有诽谤任何人。
