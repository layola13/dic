emergency:/ɪˈmɜːrdʒənsi/| n.|紧急情况，突发事件|An unexpected and serious situation requiring immediate action.  It often involves danger or risk to life, health, or property.|例句：The hospital declared an emergency after the earthquake.  医院在地震后宣布进入紧急状态。

例句：We need to call for emergency services; the situation is critical. 我们需要呼叫紧急服务；情况危急。


近义词：crisis, urgent situation, predicament|反义词：routine, normalcy, calm

The hospital handles many emergencies every day. 医院每天处理许多紧急情况。

词源:

The word "emergency" ultimately derives from the Old French word "emerger," meaning "to rise," "emerge," or "come out."  This, in turn, comes from the Latin word "emergere," with the same meaning. The sense of "unexpected event requiring immediate action" developed over time.
