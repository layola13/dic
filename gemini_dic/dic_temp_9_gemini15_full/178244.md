recompensing:/ˌriːˈkɒmpɛnsɪŋ/ | v. | 补偿；报答；酬谢 |  To give something to someone in return for something else, especially for work or loss.  It implies a fair or just exchange, often involving payment or reward. |


例句：

The company recompensed its employees for their hard work with a bonus. = 公司以奖金补偿员工的辛勤工作。

The government recompensed the victims of the flood with financial aid. = 政府以财政援助补偿洪水灾民。


近义词：compensating, rewarding, remunerating, making amends for


反义词：penalizing, punishing


词性：动词
