cockneys:/ˈkɒkniːz/ | n. | 伦敦东区人；考克尼人 | Cockneys are people who were traditionally born within earshot of the Bow Bells, the bells of St. Mary-le-Bow church in Cheapside, London.  They are known for their distinctive accent, Cockney rhyming slang, and working-class background.  The term has evolved, and while geographical limitations are less strict now, the association with a specific London accent remains. | Example:  Many famous actors are Cockneys.  许多著名的演员是考克尼人。  |近义词: East Ender (less formal) | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but could use terms implying upper class or non-Londoner) | 名词
