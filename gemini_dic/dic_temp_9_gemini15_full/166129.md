prepractises:/ˌpriːˈpræktɪsɪz/| v. |预先练习，提前练习| To practice something beforehand; to rehearse.  This is not a standard English word; it's a combination of "pre-" and "practises."  It's understandable but uncommon. |例句：She prepractises her presentation every night before the big day. = 她每天晚上都在大日子之前预先练习她的演讲。

The athlete prepractised his long jump technique. = 运动员预先练习了他的跳远技术。


近义词：rehearse, prepare, review

反义词：improvise, wing it


词性:动词
