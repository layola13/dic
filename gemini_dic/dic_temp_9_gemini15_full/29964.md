caves:/keɪvz/| n.|洞穴，山洞|Natural underground spaces, large enough to enter, formed by the erosion of rock.  They often feature stalactites and stalagmites.|例句：The explorers discovered a system of caves deep within the mountain. 探险家们在深山中发现了一个洞穴系统。|例句：Bats often live in caves. 蝙蝠通常住在洞穴里。|近义词：caverns, grottos|反义词：open space, surface


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The water caves through the cracks. = 水从裂缝中渗透。
He caves under pressure. = 他在压力下屈服。


一般过去时:
The miners caved in the tunnel. = 矿工们在隧道里挖出了一个洞穴。
The roof caved in during the storm. = 暴风雨期间屋顶塌陷了。


现在完成时:
The pressure has caved the pipes. = 压力已经压坏了管道。
The team has caved to demands for a pay rise. = 团队已经屈服于加薪的要求。


过去完成时:
The ground had caved in before they could escape. = 地面塌陷之前他们无法逃脱。
The negotiations had caved before the deadline. = 谈判在截止日期之前破裂了。


将来时:
The old building will cave in soon. = 这座旧建筑很快就会倒塌。
They will cave to public pressure. = 他们将屈服于公众压力。


虚拟语气:
If the roof caved in, we would be trapped. = 如果屋顶塌陷，我们就会被困住。
If they had caved earlier, the outcome would have been different. = 如果他们早点妥协，结果就会不一样。


被动语态:

The deal was caved by the competitor’s offer. = 这笔交易被竞争对手的报价破坏了。


疑问句:

否定句:
