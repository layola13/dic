echinoderm:/ɪˈkaɪnədɜːrm/| n. |棘皮动物|An echinoderm is any marine invertebrate of the phylum Echinodermata, characterized by a radial symmetry (usually five-point), a water vascular system, and typically a spiny or bumpy skin.  Examples include starfish, sea urchins, and sea cucumbers. |Example: Sea stars are a type of echinoderm.  海星是一种棘皮动物。|近义词: none (due to being a specific phylum)|反义词: none (phylum classification)|名词

词源:

The word "echinoderm" is derived from the Greek words "echinos" (εχῖνος), meaning "hedgehog" or "sea urchin," and "derma" (δέρμα), meaning "skin."  This reflects the spiny or bumpy skin characteristic of many echinoderms.
