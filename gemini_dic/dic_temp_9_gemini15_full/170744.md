pseudobenevolent:/ˌsuːdəʊˈbenɪvələnt/ | adj. | 假慈悲的，伪善的 | Seemingly benevolent but actually selfish or malicious.  It describes actions or behavior that appear kind and charitable on the surface but are driven by ulterior motives. | 例句：His pseudobenevolent offer to help was really a ploy to gain access to her files.  他的假慈悲帮忙其实是为了获取她的档案。


The seemingly pseudobenevolent charity was later found to be laundering money. 这家表面上假慈悲的慈善机构后来被发现是在洗钱。| 近义词：hypocritical, sanctimonious, self-serving, disingenuous | 反义词：benevolent, altruistic, genuinely kind


词源:

* **pseudo-**:  From Greek *pseudes* meaning "false" or "lying".
* **benevolent**: From Latin *benevolens*, combining *bene* ("well") and *volens* ("wishing").  Thus, originally meaning "well-wishing".


用法说明:

This word is primarily used as an adjective to describe people, actions, or institutions that present a facade of kindness or generosity while concealing selfish or harmful intentions.  It emphasizes the deceptive nature of the apparent benevolence.  It carries a strong negative connotation.
