roadhouse: /ˈroʊdhaʊs/ | n. |路边小酒店；乡村旅馆 | A roadside establishment, typically a small hotel or motel, often with a bar and restaurant, catering to travelers, especially in remote areas.  It often implies a somewhat rough or rustic atmosphere. |Example:  We stopped at a dusty roadhouse for a quick bite to eat. 我们在一个尘土飞扬的路边小酒店停下来快速吃点东西。  The roadhouse was known for its lively atmosphere and cheap drinks. 这家路边小酒店以其热闹的气氛和廉价的酒水而闻名。|近义词：inn, tavern, motel, bar|反义词：luxury hotel, five-star hotel

词源:
The word "roadhouse" is a compound word combining "road" and "house."  It emerged in the early 20th century to describe establishments built along roads to cater to travelers, particularly during the rise of automobiles.  The term reflects the often-rustic and sometimes rough nature of these early roadside accommodations.
