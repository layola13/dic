scrutinies:/ˈskruːtəniz/ | n. | 仔细审查；认真检查；严格审查 | The act of examining something closely and critically.  It often implies a thorough and detailed investigation, sometimes with suspicion or skepticism.  |例句：The company's financial records underwent intense scrutinies after the allegations of fraud.  公司在被指控欺诈后，财务记录受到了严格审查。

The proposal faced numerous scrutinies before it was finally approved.  该提案在最终获得批准之前经历了多次审查。


近义词：examination, inspection, investigation, review, scrutiny, analysis

反义词：superficial glance, cursory examination


词性:名词

词源:
The word "scrutiny" comes from the Late Middle English word *scrutini*, which in turn is derived from the Old French word *scrutine*, meaning "a searching examination," ultimately deriving from the Latin word *scrutari*, meaning "to search," "to examine," or "to investigate."
