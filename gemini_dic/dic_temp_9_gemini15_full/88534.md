hit-out: /hɪtˈaʊt/ | v. |痛打；猛烈抨击；尽情享乐 | To attack someone or something violently or forcefully; to criticize someone or something severely; to indulge in unrestrained enjoyment. |例句：The boxer hit out at his opponent with a powerful right hook. 拳击手用有力的一记右勾拳猛击对手。


例句：The politician hit out at the opposition's policies.  这位政治家猛烈抨击了反对党的政策。


近义词：attack, assault, lash out, criticize, condemn, denounce,  indulge, revel


反义词：praise, commend, defend, support

PHRASE->
hit out at someone/something: 抨击某人/某事
hit out in anger: 愤怒地抨击
hit out with a vengeance:  猛烈抨击
hit out against injustice:  抨击不公正
hit out for a good time:  尽情享乐


词源:  The phrasal verb "hit out" combines the verb "hit" (to strike) with the adverb "out" (expressing direction or completeness), suggesting a forceful and complete action.  The meaning has evolved to include both physical and verbal attacks.
