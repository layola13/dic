cryptocurrencies:/ˌkrɪptoʊˈkʌrənsiz/| n. |加密货币|Digital or virtual currencies designed to work as a medium of exchange through a computer network that is not reliant on any central authority, such as a government or bank, to uphold or manage it.  They typically use cryptography to secure and verify transactions as well as to control the creation of new units of the currency. |例句：Bitcoin and Ethereum are examples of cryptocurrencies. 比特币和以太坊是加密货币的例子。

例句：The volatility of cryptocurrencies makes them a risky investment. 加密货币的波动性使其成为一项高风险投资。


近义词：digital currencies, virtual currencies


反义词：fiat currencies (法定货币)


词性: 名词
