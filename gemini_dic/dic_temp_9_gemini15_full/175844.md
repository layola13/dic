Rastafarians:/ˌrɑːstəˈfɛəriənz/| n. |拉斯特法里教信徒|Followers of Rastafarianism, a religious movement that originated in Jamaica in the 1930s.  Rastafarians believe in the divinity of Haile Selassie I, the former Emperor of Ethiopia, and emphasize principles of  black liberation, self-reliance, and spiritual awakening. Their culture is rich and includes distinctive music (reggae),  fashion, and lifestyle.| Many Rastafarians follow a vegetarian diet and use cannabis as a sacrament. The community is diverse, with varying levels of adherence to its tenets.|The Rastafarian movement gained international recognition through reggae music. |近义词: Rastafarians (no direct synonyms, but terms like "Rastaman" or "Rasta" are used informally)|反义词: (No direct antonyms, the term refers to a specific group.)
