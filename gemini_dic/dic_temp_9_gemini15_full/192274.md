screenplay:/ˈskriːnˌpleɪ/| n. |剧本，电影剧本|A written script for a film, television program, or play.|The screenplay for the movie was nominated for an award.  这部电影的剧本获得了奖项提名。|script, scenario|novel, story|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The writer is working on a new screenplay. = 编剧正在创作一个新的剧本。
This screenplay focuses on the lives of young people. = 这个剧本关注年轻人的生活。

一般过去时:
He wrote a screenplay for a historical drama. = 他为一部历史剧写了一个剧本。
She adapted her novel into a screenplay. = 她把她的小说改编成了剧本。

现在完成时:
They have finished writing the screenplay. = 他们已经完成了剧本的写作。
The director has approved the screenplay. = 导演已经批准了剧本。

过去完成时:
The actors had already read the screenplay before the rehearsal. = 演员们在排练前已经读过剧本了。
The producer had rejected several screenplays before choosing this one. = 制片人之前拒绝了好几个剧本才选择了这个。

将来时:
She will write a screenplay based on a true story. = 她将根据一个真实故事写一个剧本。
They will start filming after the screenplay is finished. = 剧本完成后他们将开始拍摄。

虚拟语气:
If I had a better screenplay, I would produce the film. = 如果我有一个更好的剧本，我会制作这部电影。
I wish I had written a more compelling screenplay. = 我希望我写了一个更引人入胜的剧本。

被动语态:
The screenplay was written by a famous novelist. = 剧本是由一位著名小说家写的。
The screenplay is being revised by the director. = 剧本正在由导演修改。

疑问句:
Who wrote this screenplay? = 谁写的这个剧本？
Is the screenplay ready for filming? = 剧本准备好拍摄了吗？

否定句:
He didn't write the screenplay himself. = 他不是自己写的剧本。
The screenplay wasn't well-received by the critics. = 剧本没有受到评论家的好评。
