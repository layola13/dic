imperceptibility:/ˌɪmpɜːsɛptəˈbɪləti/ | n. |难以察觉；不易觉察；微不可觉 | The imperceptibility of the change made it difficult to detect.  变化的难以察觉使得难以探测到它。| 近义词：invisibility, subtlety, undetectability | 反义词：obviousness, visibility, perceptibility


词源:

* **im-**:  prefix indicating negation or reversal (否定或反转)
* **percept-**: from Latin *percipere* meaning "to perceive" (感知)
* **-ibility**: suffix indicating capacity or capability (能力或可能性)


英文解析:

Imperceptibility refers to the state or quality of being imperceptible;  meaning  difficult or impossible to perceive or detect with the senses or the mind.  It suggests something is so subtle, faint, or gradual that it's almost impossible to notice.


例句:

The magician's movements were characterized by imperceptibility, making his tricks seem magical. 魔术师的动作以难以察觉为特征，使他的戏法看起来很神奇。

Due to the imperceptibility of the symptoms, the disease went undiagnosed for a long time. 由于症状不易察觉，这种疾病长期未被诊断出来。
