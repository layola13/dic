quasi-fashionable:/ˈkwɑːziˌfæʃənəbl/| adj. |似是而非的时尚的；半流行的|  Something that is quasi-fashionable is somewhat fashionable but not truly or completely so; it might be on the verge of becoming fashionable, or it might be a style that appeals to a small, specific group.  It suggests a lack of complete acceptance or widespread popularity.| Example:  Her quasi-fashionable outfit was a mix of vintage and modern elements that only partially worked together.  她的看似时尚的服装混合了复古和现代元素，但只部分融合在一起。|近义词：semi-fashionable, trendy (but not fully), mildly fashionable|反义词：unfashionable, outdated, passé
