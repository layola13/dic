overmodification:/ˌoʊvərˌmɑːdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |过度修饰，矫枉过正|The act of modifying something excessively, often to the point of diminishing its original quality or effectiveness.  This can apply to various contexts, including language, design, or even genetic engineering.  For example, in writing, overmodification might involve using too many adjectives or adverbs, making the writing sound unnatural or cluttered. In genetic engineering, it refers to the unintended and excessive alteration of a gene or genome.|例句：The essay suffered from overmodification; the excessive use of adjectives obscured its central argument. = 这篇文章由于过度修饰而显得糟糕；过多的形容词掩盖了其核心论点。

The excessive editing of the photograph resulted in overmodification, making it look artificial. = 照片过度编辑导致过度修饰，使其看起来很做作。|近义词：overelaboration, embellishment (in the sense of excessive), overdoing|反义词：understatement, simplicity, minimalism|词性：名词
