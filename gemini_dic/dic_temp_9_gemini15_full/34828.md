clingers:/ˈklɪŋərz/| n.|粘着的人；依附者；坚持者|People who cling to something or someone; those who are reluctant to let go of something or someone, often out of dependence, fear, or insecurity.  |例句：The clingers in the company hindered progress by resisting change. 公司里那些墨守成规的人阻碍了进步。
例句：He was a political clinger, always seeking approval from his superiors. 他是一个依附型政客，总是寻求上级的认可。|近义词：adherents, dependants, devotees |反义词： independents, free spirits


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The child clings to his mother. = 孩子紧紧抱着他的母亲。
Those plants cling to the rocks. = 那些植物依附在岩石上。

一般过去时:
She clung to the railing as the boat rocked. = 船摇晃时，她紧紧抓住栏杆。
He clung to the hope of success. = 他紧紧抓住成功的希望。

现在完成时:
They have clung to their traditions for generations. = 世世代代他们都坚持着自己的传统。
She has clung to that belief despite evidence to the contrary. = 尽管有相反的证据，她仍然坚持那个信念。

过去完成时:
He had clung to the idea for years before finally giving up. = 在最终放弃之前，他坚持这个想法多年了。
They had clung to the wreckage for hours before being rescued. = 在获救之前，他们紧紧抓住残骸几个小时。

将来时:
I will cling to my principles, no matter what happens. = 无论发生什么，我都会坚持我的原则。
She will cling to the memories of her childhood. = 她将会紧紧抓住童年记忆。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would cling to your dreams. = 如果我是你，我会坚持你的梦想。
I wish I hadn't clung to that old belief for so long. = 我真希望我没有坚持那个旧的信念那么久。

被动语态:
The child was clung to by his mother. = 孩子被他的母亲紧紧抱着。（不常用）
The vine was clinging to the wall. = 葡萄藤攀附在墙上。（被动语态更常用于植物）

疑问句:
Why do you cling to these outdated ideas? = 你为什么坚持这些过时的想法？
Does he still cling to the hope of reconciliation? = 他仍然抱有和解的希望吗？

否定句:
He didn't cling to his anger for long. = 他没有长时间地生气。
She didn't cling to the life raft.  = 她没有抓住救生筏。
