ricinine: /ˈrɪsɪnɪn/ | n. | 羽扇豆碱 | A toxic alkaloid found in castor beans (Ricinus communis) and other plants of the Euphorbiaceae family. 词源:

The name "ricinine" is derived from *Ricinus communis*, the scientific name for the castor bean plant, from which the alkaloid was first isolated.  "ricin" is also from this source, referring to another toxin found in castor beans.  The suffix "-ine" is a common suffix used to denote alkaloids.
