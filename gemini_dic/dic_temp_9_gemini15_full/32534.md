chip-off-the-old-block: /ˌtʃɪp ˈɒf ði ˌoʊld ˈblɒk/ | n. |  像极父母的孩子 | A person who is very similar in character or ability to their parent, especially their father. This idiom emphasizes a strong resemblance in personality, skills, or temperament.  | Example:  He's a chip off the old block; he's got his father's quick wit and charm. 他真像他父亲，有着他父亲的机智和魅力。 | 近义词: spitting image, look-alike, carbon copy | 反义词:  the black sheep (of the family)
