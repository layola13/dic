cankerous: /ˈkæŋkərəs/ | adj. | 恶性的；腐蚀性的；坏的；有害的 |  Characterized by or tending to cause canker; malignant; destructive.  It suggests something that is destructive, spreading, and causing harm, often in a subtle or insidious way.  The term often carries a strong negative connotation.


英文解析:  The word "cankerous" derives from "canker," which originally referred to a disease affecting plants, particularly a type of sore or ulcer.  Over time, its meaning broadened to encompass anything that is malignant, corrupting, or destructive, especially in a gradual or insidious manner.  It suggests a slow, spreading decay or corruption.


例句:

His cankerous resentment festered for years. = 他多年来一直怀恨在心，这怨恨像溃疡一样腐蚀着他。

The cankerous gossip spread through the office like wildfire. = 恶毒的流言蜚语像野火一样在办公室里蔓延。


近义词: malignant, virulent, corrosive, destructive, harmful, pernicious, infectious, festering


反义词: benign, wholesome, healthy, beneficial, innocuous


词性: adjective
