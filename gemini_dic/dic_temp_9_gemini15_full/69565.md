felon:/ˈfɛlən/| n. |重罪犯|A felon is a person who has been convicted of a serious crime, typically one punishable by imprisonment for more than one year.  Felons often lose certain rights, such as the right to vote or own firearms.|例句：He was arrested and charged as a felon for armed robbery. 他因持械抢劫被捕并被指控为重罪犯。|例句：Because of his felony conviction, he couldn't get a job. 因为他有重罪前科，所以找不到工作。|近义词：criminal, offender, convict|反义词：law-abiding citizen
