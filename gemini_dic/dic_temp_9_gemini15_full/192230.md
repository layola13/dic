scrawls:/skrɔːlz/| n. v.|潦草的字迹；乱涂乱画；乱写|To write or draw in a careless or hurried way, resulting in illegible or messy marks.  |例句：The child scrawled all over the wall with crayon.  孩子用蜡笔在墙上乱涂乱画。

The note was a mere scrawl, impossible to decipher.  便条潦草难辨。|近义词：scribble, doodle, daub|反义词：write neatly, print


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scrawls his signature on the document. = 他在文件上潦草地签了名。
She scrawls a quick note to her friend. = 她匆匆给朋友写了一张便条。

一般过去时:
He scrawled his name on the sand. = 他在沙滩上潦草地写下自己的名字。
She scrawled a message on a napkin. = 她在一张餐巾纸上潦草地写了一条信息。

现在完成时:
He has scrawled his name on countless forms. = 他在无数表格上潦草地签过名。
She has scrawled several angry letters, but hasn't sent them. = 她潦草地写了几封气愤的信，但还没寄出去。

过去完成时:
He had scrawled a cryptic message before he collapsed. = 他昏倒前潦草地写下了一条神秘的信息。
She had scrawled the address on the envelope, making it difficult to read. = 她把地址潦草地写在信封上，很难辨认。

将来时:

She will scrawl a quick reply to the email. = 她将草草回复邮件。

虚拟语气:

I wish I hadn't scrawled my name so badly. = 我真希望我没有把我的名字写得那么潦草。

否定句:

词源:
The word "scrawl" comes from the Middle English word "scrawlen," meaning "to scratch or scrape."  This in turn likely derives from a Proto-Germanic root related to scratching or scraping sounds.
