forelands:/ˈfɔːrˌlændz/ | n. |前陆；沿海低地；海角 |A foreland is a piece of land that projects out into the sea, often forming a cape or promontory.  It can also refer to a coastal lowland area.  The term is often used in geology to describe the landward edge of a forearc basin. |例句：The ship rounded the foreland and entered the sheltered bay. 这艘船绕过海角驶入平静的海湾。

The picturesque fishing village nestled against the rugged foreland.  风景如画的渔村依偎在崎岖的海角边。


近义词：promontory, headland, cape |反义词：inland


词性：名词
