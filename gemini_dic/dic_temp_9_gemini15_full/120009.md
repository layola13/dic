millimicron:/ˈmɪlɪˌmaɪkrɒn/| n. |毫微米（旧称，现已不用）|A millimicron (mµ) is an obsolete unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter (10⁻⁹ m) or one nanometer (nm).  It was formerly used in various scientific fields, particularly spectroscopy and microscopy, to express wavelengths of light and dimensions of particles. However, it has been largely replaced by the nanometer (nm).|The wavelength of visible light ranges from 400 to 700 millimicrons.|近义词：nanometer (nm)|反义词：无|名词
