preordinances:/ˌpriːˈɔːrdɪnənsɪz/ | n. | 预先的法令；预先的命令 |  Preordinances refer to ordinances or decrees established beforehand, often implying a divine or predetermined nature.  They are actions or laws set in place before a specific event or time.  The term suggests a pre-existing plan or design. | The preordinances of the ancient gods were said to govern the fate of mortals.  古代众神的预先法令据说支配着凡人的命运。


| 近义词: predecrees, foreordainments, prior enactments | 反义词: ad hoc decisions, improvisations | 词性: noun
