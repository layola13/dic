hassidism:/ˈhæsɪdɪzəm/| n. |哈西迪犹太教|A mystical Jewish movement that arose in Eastern Europe in the 18th century, emphasizing fervent piety and the importance of experiencing God's presence in everyday life.  It is characterized by its distinctive customs, dress, and communal life.|The Hasidim believe in the importance of prayer, study, and acts of loving-kindness.  |近义词：Jewish mysticism, pietism|反义词：secularism, rationalism
