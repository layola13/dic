reckon with: /ˈrɛkən wɪð/ | vt. | 认真对待；处理；预料到；与…算账 | to take into account; to deal with; to expect something to happen and be prepared for it; to punish someone for something they have done | 近义词：consider, deal with, contend with, anticipate | 反义词：ignore, neglect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We reckon with the possibility of rain. = 我们考虑到下雨的可能性。
They reckon with the challenges ahead. = 他们认真对待即将面临的挑战。


一般过去时:
She reckoned with her past mistakes. = 她认真思考了她过去的错误。
He reckoned with the consequences of his actions. = 他承担了他行为的后果。


现在完成时:
We have reckoned with many obstacles in this project. = 在这个项目中，我们已经克服了许多障碍。
They have reckoned with the criticism levelled against them. = 他们已经认真处理了针对他们的批评。


过去完成时:
They had reckoned with the possibility of failure before starting the project. = 他们在启动项目之前就已经考虑到了失败的可能性。
She had reckoned with the emotional toll of the experience. = 她已经预料到这次经历会带来的情感代价。


将来时:
We will reckon with the fallout from this decision. = 我们将应对这一决定带来的后果。
They will reckon with the opposition in the upcoming election. = 他们将在即将到来的选举中与反对派对抗。


虚拟语气:
If we reckoned with the risks involved, we might have acted differently. = 如果我们当时考虑到所涉及的风险，我们可能会采取不同的行动。
I wish I had reckoned with the potential consequences. = 我真希望我当时考虑到了潜在的后果。


被动语态:
The problem must be reckoned with immediately. = 这个问题必须立即得到解决。
The consequences of his actions were reckoned with swiftly. = 他行为的后果很快得到了处理。


疑问句:
How do you reckon with such a complex issue? = 你如何处理如此复杂的问题？
Are you reckoning with the potential delays? = 你是否考虑到了潜在的延误？


否定句:
We didn't reckon with the unexpected surge in demand. = 我们没有预料到需求的意外激增。
They failed to reckon with the opposition's strength. = 他们没有估计到反对派的强大实力。
