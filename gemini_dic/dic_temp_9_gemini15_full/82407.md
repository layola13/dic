grudger:/ˈɡrʌdʒər/| n. | 吝啬鬼；怨恨者 | A person who is unwilling to give or share things, or who feels or shows resentment.  | He's a grudger and never tips well. 他是个吝啬鬼，从不给小费。She's a grudger, always complaining about things.她是个怨恨者，总是抱怨事情。| miser, begrudger | giver, benefactor | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grudges every penny. = 他吝啬每一分钱。
She grudges her time spent helping others. = 她吝啬她花在帮助别人身上的时间。

一般过去时:
He grudged paying the bill. = 他很不情愿付账。
They grudged the effort required for the project. = 他们对完成项目所需付出的努力感到不满。

现在完成时:
He has grudged me my success ever since. = 从那以后，他就一直嫉妒我的成功。
She has grudged paying taxes for years. = 她多年来一直不愿纳税。

过去完成时:
He had grudged giving her the promotion for a long time. = 他很久以前就不情愿给她升职。
They had grudged the extra work assigned to them. = 他们对分配给他们的额外工作感到不满。

将来时:
He will grudge paying the fine. = 他将不情愿支付罚款。
She will grudge the effort needed to clean the house. = 她将不情愿付出打扫房子的力气。

虚拟语气:
If he were less of a grudger, he would be more popular. = 如果他不那么吝啬，他会更受欢迎。
I wish I hadn't grudged him that opportunity. = 我真希望我没有嫉妒他那个机会。

被动语态:
The extra work was grudged by many of the employees. = 许多员工对额外的工作感到不满。
His success was grudged by his colleagues. = 他的成功遭到同事们的嫉妒。

疑问句:

Do they grudge the time dedicated to charity? = 他们是否吝啬奉献给慈善的时间？

否定句:
He doesn't grudge sharing his knowledge. = 他不吝啬分享他的知识。
She doesn't grudge spending money on her family. = 她不吝啬为家人花钱。
