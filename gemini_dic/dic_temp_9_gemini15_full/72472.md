flunking:/ˈflʌŋkɪŋ/| v.|不及格，失败|The act of failing a test or course; failing to meet expectations.|例句：He was flunking all his classes. 他所有课程都考试不及格。|例句：The project was flunking because of poor management.  这个项目因为管理不善而失败。|近义词：failing, bombing, failing to pass|反义词：passing, succeeding, acing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is flunking math. = 他数学不及格。
She is flunking her driving test repeatedly. = 她驾照考试反复不及格。

一般过去时:
I flunked my history exam. = 我历史考试不及格。
They flunked out of college. = 他们大学退学了。

现在完成时:
He has flunked three exams this semester. = 这个学期他已经不及格三次考试了。
She has been flunking all her classes. = 她所有课程都考试不及格。

过去完成时:
I had flunked all my exams before I decided to drop out. = 在我决定退学之前，我已经所有考试都考砸了。
They had flunked the same test twice before they finally passed. = 他们在最终通过之前，已经两次不及格同一次考试。

将来时:
I think I will flunk this test. = 我觉得我会这次考试不及格。
If he continues like this, he will flunk out. = 如果他继续这样下去，他会退学的。

虚拟语气:
If I had studied harder, I wouldn't have flunked the exam. = 如果我学习更努力，我就不会考试不及格。
I wish I hadn't flunked that important test. = 我真希望我没有考砸那场重要的考试。

被动语态:
The student was flunked for cheating. = 这个学生因为作弊而不及格。
The project was flunked due to lack of funding. = 这个项目由于缺乏资金而失败。

疑问句:
Are you flunking any subjects? = 你有任何科目不及格吗？
Did he flunk the exam? = 他考试不及格了吗？

否定句:
He didn't flunk the test. = 他考试没有不及格。
She wasn't flunking any classes. = 她没有哪门课不及格。


PHRASE->
flunk out = 退学
flunk a test = 考试不及格
flunk a course = 课程不及格
flunk an exam = 考试不及格
to flunk (someone) out = 开除某人
