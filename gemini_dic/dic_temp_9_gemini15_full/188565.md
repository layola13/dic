ruing:/ˈruːɪŋ/| v.|后悔，悔恨；破坏；毁灭 | expressing great regret or sorrow; spoiling or destroying something. |例句：He spent the evening ruing his decision. 他整个晚上都在后悔自己的决定。  He was ruing the day he’d ever met her. 他后悔自己曾经遇到过她。|近义词：regretting, repenting, lamenting, spoiling, destroying |反义词：rejoicing, celebrating, enjoying

USAGE->
一般现在时:
I am ruing my hasty words. = 我正在后悔我轻率的话语。
They are ruing the damage they caused. = 他们正在后悔他们造成的损害。


一般过去时:
She rued her impulsive purchase. = 她后悔自己冲动购物。
We rued the lost opportunity. = 我们后悔错过了机会。


现在完成时:
I have been ruing my decision ever since. = 从那以后我一直在后悔我的决定。
They have rued their actions for years. = 他们多年来一直后悔自己的行为。


过去完成时:
He had rued his mistake before it was too late. = 在为时已晚之前，他已经后悔自己的错误了。
She had rued her silence for many years. = 她多年来一直后悔自己的沉默。


将来时:
I will rue this day for the rest of my life. = 我将在余生中后悔今天。
They will rue the day they crossed me. = 他们将后悔与我作对的那一天。


虚拟语气:
If I had listened to him, I wouldn't be ruing my choices now. = 如果我听了他的话，我现在就不会后悔我的选择了。
I wish I hadn't been so careless; I'd be ruing it less. = 我真希望自己不要那么粗心；那样我就会少后悔一些。


被动语态:
His actions were rued by many. = 他的行为受到了许多人的谴责和后悔。
The lost opportunity was greatly rued by the team. = 这个失去的机会让团队非常后悔。


疑问句:
Are you ruing your decision? = 你后悔你的决定吗？
Do you ever rue your past mistakes? = 你是否后悔你过去的错误？


否定句:
I don't rue my decision at all. = 我一点也不后悔我的决定。
He didn't rue his actions, even after the consequences. = 即使在后果出现后，他也不后悔自己的行为。


PHRASE->
rue the day: 后悔某一天
rue the hour: 后悔某个时刻
rue one's folly: 后悔自己的愚蠢
rue one's haste: 后悔自己的轻率
rue bitterly: 深深地后悔
