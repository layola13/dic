recoiling: /rɪˈkɔɪl/ | v. | 畏缩；后退；反冲 | To suddenly move back from something dangerous or unpleasant; to spring back.  | The horse recoiled in terror from the loud noise.  马因巨大的声响而惊恐地后退。| 近义词：shrink, flinch, recoil from | 反义词：advance, approach


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He recoils from the touch of spiders. 他对蜘蛛的触摸感到畏缩。
The spring recoils after being compressed. 弹簧被压缩后反弹。


一般过去时:
She recoiled in horror at the sight of the blood. 她看到血后惊恐地退缩了。
The gun recoiled violently after firing. 枪射击后猛烈地后坐。


现在完成时:
He has recoiled from public life since the scandal. 自丑闻发生以来，他已退出了公众生活。

过去完成时:

将来时:
