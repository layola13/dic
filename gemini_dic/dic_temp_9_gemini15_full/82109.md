groggier:/ˈɡrɒɡiər/|/ˈɡrɑːɡiər/| adj.| 更加疲惫的，更加昏昏沉沉的 | More tired and weak; more unwell or dizzy.  This word describes a state of being more tired and unwell than simply "groggy". It implies a worsening of the condition. | Example: After a night of little sleep and too much alcohol, he felt even groggier than usual.  (一夜没怎么睡，又喝了太多酒，他感觉比平时还要疲惫。)  He woke up groggier than before, the effects of the medication lingering. (他醒来后比之前更昏昏沉沉，药物的影响还在持续。) |近义词：drowsy, sluggish, lethargic, woozy|反义词：alert, energetic, refreshed|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He feels groggier than usual. = 他感觉比平时更疲惫。
She is groggier after a long journey. = 长途旅行后她感到更昏昏沉沉。

一般过去时:
I felt groggier after the surgery. = 手术后我感觉更虚弱了。
They were groggier than expected after the marathon. = 马拉松之后他们比预期的更疲惫。

现在完成时:
He has been feeling groggier since he caught the flu. = 自从得了流感后，他一直感觉更疲惫。
She has become groggier over the past few days. = 过去几天里她变得越来越昏沉。

过去完成时:
I had been feeling groggier all day, so I went to bed early. = 我一整天都感觉很疲惫，所以我早早睡了。
They had become groggier after a long day of work. = 经过一天漫长的工作后，他们变得更加疲惫。


将来时:
I will be groggier tomorrow if I don't get enough sleep tonight. = 如果今晚睡不好，我明天会更疲惫。
She will feel groggier if she continues to drink so much. = 如果她继续喝这么多酒，她会感觉更昏沉。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't drunk so much, I wouldn't be feeling so groggier now. = 如果我没喝那么多酒，我现在就不会这么疲惫了。
If she had slept more, she wouldn't have been so groggier this morning. = 如果她睡得多一点，今天早上她就不会这么昏沉了。
