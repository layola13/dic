curtal:/kɜːrtl/| v.|缩短；删节；削减|To shorten something by cutting off a part of it; to reduce or diminish something.|The editor curtailed the article to fit the space available. 编辑缩短了文章以适应可用空间。|They curtailed their holiday because of the bad weather. 他们因为坏天气而缩短了假期。|shorten, abridge, truncate|lengthen, extend|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He curtails his spending. = 他缩减开支。
She curtails her work hours. = 她缩短了工作时间。

一般过去时:
The government curtailed funding for the project. = 政府削减了该项目的资金。
They curtailed the debate due to time constraints. = 由于时间限制，他们缩短了辩论。

现在完成时:
They have curtailed their travel plans. = 他们已经缩短了他们的旅行计划。
She has curtailed her social activities. = 她已经减少了社交活动。

过去完成时:
He had curtailed his speech before the audience grew restless. = 他在观众变得焦躁不安之前缩短了他的演讲。
They had curtailed their vacation to return home early. = 他们缩短了假期以便早点回家。

将来时:
We will curtail our expenses next month. = 我们下个月将削减开支。
The company will curtail production due to low demand. = 由于需求低迷，该公司将削减产量。

虚拟语气:
If they curtailed their spending, they would save more money. = 如果他们减少开支，他们就能省更多钱。
I wish they had curtailed the meeting. = 我希望他们当时缩短了会议。

被动语态:
The program was curtailed due to lack of funds. = 该项目由于资金不足而被缩短。
His speech was curtailed by the chairman. = 他的发言被主席打断了。

疑问句:
Will the government curtail the budget? = 政府会削减预算吗？
Did they curtail the performance? = 他们缩短了演出吗？

否定句:
They did not curtail their holiday. = 他们没有缩短假期。
The company will not curtail its operations. = 公司不会缩短其运营。
