pseudoaristocratically: /ˌsuːdoʊˌærɪstəˈkrætɪkli/ | adv. |伪贵族地，假贵族般地 |  In a manner characteristic of or affectedly resembling an aristocrat;  pretentiously aristocratic.  | 例句：He spoke pseudoaristocratically, using overly formal language and affected pronouncements. 他说话装腔作势，像个伪贵族，用过于正式的语言和做作的声明。


例句：She dressed pseudoaristocratically, hoping to impress the guests at the gala. 她穿着打扮得像个假贵族，希望能给晚会的宾客留下深刻印象。


近义词：affectedly, pretentiously, snobbishly


反义词：democratically, humbly, modestly


词源：

* **pseudo-**:  From Greek *pseudo-* meaning "false," "sham," or "pretended."

* **aristocrat**: From French *aristocrate*, ultimately from Greek *aristokratēs*, from *aristos* ("best") + *kratos* ("power," "rule").  Thus, originally meaning "one of the best rulers."

* **-ically**: Adverbial suffix derived from Greek *-ikos*.
