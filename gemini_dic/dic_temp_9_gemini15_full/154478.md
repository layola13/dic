perorations:/ˌpɛrəˈreɪʃənz/| n.|结束语；冗长的演说|A formal speech or concluding statement, often one that is long and elaborate.  It often summarizes the main points of a speech or argument and leaves a lasting impression on the audience.  The term often carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting the speech is excessively long or drawn out. | Example: The lawyer's peroration lasted for over an hour, leaving the jury exhausted.  律师的总结陈词持续了一个多小时，让陪审团筋疲力尽。|近义词：concluding remarks, summation, valedictory, epilogue|反义词：prelude, introduction, beginning
