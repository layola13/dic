expulsion:/ɪkˈspʌlʒən/| n. |驱逐，除名，排出|The act of expelling someone or something; the state of being expelled.  It often refers to forcing someone to leave a place or organization. |例句：His expulsion from school was a result of his repeated misbehavior.  他的退学是因为他屡教不改的恶劣行为。

例句：The expulsion of the harmful gases from the volcano caused widespread damage. 火山喷出的有害气体造成了广泛的破坏。


近义词：banishment, ejection, dismissal, ouster|反义词：admission, acceptance, inclusion|词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The school expels students for serious misconduct. = 学校因严重违规行为而开除学生。
His constant lateness leads to his expulsion from the team. = 他经常迟到导致他被踢出队伍。


一般过去时:
The dictator was expelled from power. = 独裁者被赶下台了。
They expelled him from the club for breaking the rules. = 他们因为他违反规定而将他逐出俱乐部。


现在完成时:
He has been expelled from several universities. = 他已经被几所大学开除了。
The country has expelled several diplomats in retaliation. = 该国已经驱逐了几名外交官作为报复。


过去完成时:
They had expelled him before the scandal broke. = 丑闻爆发前他们就已经将他开除了。
The organization had expelled her membership before the evidence surfaced. = 在证据出现之前，组织已经取消了她的会员资格。


将来时:
The government will expel any foreign agents found. = 政府将驱逐任何被发现的外国特工。
They will expel him if he continues to break the rules. = 如果他继续违反规定，他们将会将他开除。


虚拟语气:
If he had been a better student, he wouldn't have been expelled. = 如果他是一个好学生，他就不会被开除了。
I wish they hadn't expelled her from the team. = 我希望他们没有把她从队伍里开除。


被动语态:
He was expelled for cheating on the exam. = 他因为考试作弊而被开除了。
The members were expelled for violating the club's constitution. =  会员们因为违反俱乐部章程而被开除了。


疑问句:
Will he be expelled from school? = 他会被学校开除吗？
Was she expelled for her political views? = 她因为政治观点而被开除吗？


否定句:
They did not expel him despite his bad behavior. = 尽管他的行为很糟糕，他们也没有开除他。
The university didn't expel her, despite the accusations. =  尽管有指控，大学也没有开除她。
