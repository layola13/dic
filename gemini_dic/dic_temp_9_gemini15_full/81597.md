great-deal: /ɡreɪt diːl/ | n. | 许多；大量；大笔交易 | A large amount or quantity; a significant transaction.  |例句：I bought a great deal of furniture. 我买了许多家具。

例句：They struck a great deal on the property. 他们在房产交易中达成了一笔大买卖。


近义词：a lot, much, many, plenty, substantial amount, bargain

反义词：a little, few, small amount, insignificant transaction


词性: 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
A great deal of work needs to be done. = 需要做很多工作。
He spends a great deal of time on his hobby. = 他在爱好上花费大量时间。


一般过去时:
A great deal of money was spent on the project. = 这个项目花费了大量的钱。
They made a great deal on the used car. = 他们以低价买到了一辆二手车。


现在完成时:
A great deal of progress has been made. = 已取得很大进展。
We have made a great deal this year. = 我们今年赚了很多钱。


过去完成时:

将来时:

被动语态:

词源:

"Great" derives from Old English *grēat, meaning "great, big, large."  "Deal" comes from Old English *dǣl, meaning "part, portion, share."  Therefore, "great deal" literally means a "large portion" or "large amount."
