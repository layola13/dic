regressing:/rɪˈɡresɪŋ/| v.|倒退；退化；回归|To return to a former or less developed state.  This can refer to a physical, mental, or social state.  It can also describe a statistical process. |例句：The patient is regressing after the surgery. 手术后病人的病情恶化了。|例句：The economy is regressing back to pre-war levels. 经济正在倒退到战前水平。|近义词：deteriorating, declining, reverting|反义词：progressing, advancing, improving


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The child is regressing in his speech development. = 孩子在语言发展方面倒退了。
The company's market share is regressing steadily. = 公司的市场份额稳步下降。


一般过去时:
His mental state regressed after the trauma. = 他在经历创伤后精神状态恶化了。
The team regressed to their old bad habits. = 团队又回到了他们以前不好的习惯。


现在完成时:
The country has regressed significantly since the coup. = 自政变以来，这个国家已经严重倒退了。
Her health has regressed despite the treatment. = 尽管接受了治疗，她的健康状况还是恶化了。


过去完成时:
They had regressed to a primitive way of life before the discovery. = 在发现之前，他们已经退回到了原始的生活方式。
The economy had regressed considerably before the reforms were implemented. = 在改革实施之前，经济已经大幅倒退。


将来时:
The situation will regress further without intervention. = 如果不干预，情况将会进一步恶化。
The patient's condition is likely to regress if the medication is stopped. = 如果停止服药，病人的病情可能会恶化。


虚拟语气:
If we don't act now, the environment will regress to a dangerous state. = 如果我们现在不采取行动，环境将会恶化到危险的状态。
I wish my symptoms weren't regressing. = 我希望我的症状不要恶化。


被动语态:
The project was regressed to an earlier version. = 项目被回退到了之前的版本。
The patient's condition is being regressed by the illness. = 病人的病情正在被疾病恶化。


疑问句:
Is the patient regressing? = 病人病情恶化了吗？
Are we regressing as a society? = 我们作为一个社会正在倒退吗？


否定句:
The symptoms are not regressing. = 症状没有恶化。
The technology is not regressing; it's advancing. = 技术没有倒退，而是在进步。


PHRASE->

regress to the mean: 回归平均值
regress towards the mean: 向平均值回归
linear regression: 线性回归
multiple regression: 多元回归
logistic regression: 逻辑回归

词源:

The word "regressing" comes from the Latin word "regredi," meaning "to go back" or "to return."  The "re-" prefix indicates a backward movement, and "-gress" is related to the English word "progress," but with the opposite implication.
