piqued:/piːkt/| adj. v.|激怒的；感到好奇的；刺激的 |To arouse interest or curiosity; to provoke or irritate.  |例句：Her unusual attire piqued my curiosity.  她的不寻常的服装激起了我的好奇心。
His constant criticisms piqued her. 他不断的批评激怒了她。|近义词：provoked, irritated, intrigued, stimulated|反义词：soothed, calmed, uninterested


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His remarks pique my interest. = 他的话引起了我的兴趣。
The painting piques the viewer's curiosity. = 这幅画激起了观众的好奇心。

一般过去时:
Her rudeness piqued him. = 她的粗鲁激怒了他。
The mystery piqued the detective's interest. = 这个谜团激起了侦探的兴趣。

现在完成时:
His behavior has piqued my curiosity. = 他的行为激起了我的好奇心。
The news has piqued the public's interest. = 这个新闻激起了公众的兴趣。

过去完成时:
The strange noises had piqued her curiosity all night. = 一整夜奇怪的声音都激起了她的好奇心。
The initial success had piqued their ambition. = 最初的成功激起了他们的雄心。

将来时:
The new policy will pique public debate. = 新政策将会激起公众的争论。
I think the announcement will pique her interest. = 我想这个公告会引起她的兴趣。

虚拟语气:
If the article piqued your interest, you should read it. = 如果这篇文章引起了你的兴趣，你应该读一读。
I wish the mystery hadn't piqued my curiosity so much. = 我真希望这个谜团没有这么激起我的好奇心。

被动语态:
His pride was piqued by her rejection. = 他的自尊心因她的拒绝而受损。
Their curiosity was piqued by the strange sounds. = 他们对奇怪的声音感到好奇。

疑问句:
Does this new discovery pique your interest? = 这个新发现引起你的兴趣了吗？
Is his arrogance piquing your patience? = 他的傲慢正在考验你的耐心吗？

否定句:
The presentation didn't pique my interest. = 这次演示没有引起我的兴趣。
The offer didn't pique her interest. = 这个提议没有引起她的兴趣。


PHRASE->
pique someone's interest = 激起某人的兴趣
pique one's curiosity = 激起某人的好奇心
pique a person's pride = 伤害某人的自尊心
pique oneself on something = 以某事自豪
a pique of jealousy = 一阵嫉妒


词源:
From Old French piquer, meaning "to prick, sting, pierce".  The sense of "to excite or irritate" developed later.
