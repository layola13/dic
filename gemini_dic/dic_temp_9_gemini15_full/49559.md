demeans:/dɪˈmiːnz/ | vt. |贬低；降低；使丢脸 | To lower the status or reputation of someone or something; to cause to seem less worthy or important.  |例句：His behavior demeans his position as a leader.  他的行为贬低了他作为领导的地位。

例句：That kind of language demeans the entire profession.  那种语言贬低了整个行业。


近义词：belittle, degrade, humiliate, disparage, lower

反义词：exalt, elevate, honor, praise, uplift

词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He demeans his colleagues with his constant criticism. = 他不断地批评同事，贬低了他们。
The company's actions demean their commitment to sustainability. = 公司的行为贬低了他们对可持续发展的承诺。


一般过去时:
His harsh words demeaned her in front of everyone. = 他尖刻的话语在大家面前贬低了她。
The scandal demeaned the reputation of the organization. = 丑闻损害了该组织的声誉。


现在完成时:
His actions have demeaned the entire family's reputation. = 他的行为已经损害了整个家庭的声誉。
She feels that her contributions have been demeaned by her boss. = 她觉得她的贡献被她的老板贬低了。


过去完成时:
They had demeaned the importance of the research before the presentation. = 他们在演示之前就已经贬低了这项研究的重要性。
He realized he had demeaned his friend with his thoughtless comment. = 他意识到他用不经大脑的评论贬低了他的朋友。


将来时:
His behavior will demean him in the eyes of the public. = 他的行为将在公众面前使他丢脸。
I fear that this decision will demean the value of our work. = 我担心这个决定会贬低我们工作的价值。


虚拟语气:
If he continued to demean others, he would lose his friends. = 如果他继续贬低别人，他会失去朋友。
I wish he wouldn't demean himself with such petty arguments. = 我希望他不应该用这种琐碎的争论贬低自己。


被动语态:
His achievements were demeaned by the biased media coverage. = 他的成就被有偏见的媒体报道贬低了。
The proposal was demeaned due to lack of sufficient evidence. = 由于缺乏足够的证据，该提案被贬低了。


疑问句:
Does his attitude demean his profession? = 他的态度是否贬低了他的职业？
Does this policy demean the rights of minorities? = 这项政策是否贬低了少数群体的权利？


否定句:
He did not demean his opponent, even after the loss. = 即使失败后，他也没有贬低他的对手。
The company strives not to demean its employees' efforts. = 公司努力不贬低员工的努力。
