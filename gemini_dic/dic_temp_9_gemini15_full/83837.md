hakkas:/ˈhækəz/| n. |客家人；客家话|Hakkas refers to the Hakka people, an ethnic group primarily residing in southern China, Taiwan, and other parts of Southeast Asia.  They are known for their unique culture, language (Hakka Chinese), and history.  The term itself is derived from the Chinese word 客家 (Hák-kâ), meaning "guest families" or "guest people," reflecting their historical migration and settlement in various regions. |例句：Many Hakkas migrated to Southeast Asia during the Qing dynasty. 许多客家人在清朝时期迁移到了东南亚。|近义词：Hakka Chinese (referring to the language) |反义词:无
