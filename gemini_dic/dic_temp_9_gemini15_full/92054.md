hyperplasia:/ˌhaɪpərˈpleɪʒə/ | n. |增生，过度增殖 |The abnormal multiplication or increase in the number of normal cells in normal tissue.  This increase in cell number is due to increased cell division.  It is different from neoplasia (cancer), which is an abnormal increase in the number of cells that are also abnormal. |Example:  Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) is a common condition in older men. 良性前列腺增生(BPH)是老年男性常见的一种疾病。


The hormonal changes during pregnancy can cause hyperplasia of the mammary glands. 怀孕期间荷尔蒙的变化会导致乳腺增生。|近义词：proliferation, hypertrophy (though hypertrophy refers to increase in cell size, not number) |反义词：hypoplasia (underdevelopment of an organ or tissue)

词源:

* **hyper-**:  from Greek ὑπέρ (hupér), meaning "over," "above," "beyond"
* **plasia**: from Greek πλάσις (plásis), meaning "formation," "molding"


Therefore, hyperplasia literally means "excessive formation" or "overgrowth."
