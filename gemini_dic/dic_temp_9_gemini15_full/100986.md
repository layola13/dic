invalidating: /ɪnˈvælɪdeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 使无效；使作废；推翻 | To make something invalid or null; to weaken or destroy the force or effect of something. |

例句：

His actions invalidated the contract.  = 他的行为使合同无效。

The new evidence invalidated the previous theory. = 新证据推翻了之前的理论。


近义词：nullifying, annulling, canceling, voiding

反义词：validating, confirming, supporting


USAGE->

一般现在时:

The new law invalidates many previous practices. = 新法律使许多以前的做法无效。

This evidence invalidates his alibi. = 这证据推翻了他的不在场证明。


一般过去时:

The judge invalidated the will. = 法官宣布遗嘱无效。

The storm invalidated the flight schedule. = 暴风雨使航班时刻表作废。


现在完成时:

Recent studies have invalidated the earlier conclusions. = 最近的研究已经推翻了早前的结论。

His confession has invalidated the case against him. = 他的供词使针对他的案件无效。


过去完成时:

They discovered that the data had invalidated their hypothesis. = 他们发现数据已经推翻了他们的假设。

The evidence had invalidated his claim before the trial even began. = 在审判开始之前，证据就已经推翻了他的说法。


将来时:

This decision will invalidate their previous agreement. = 这个决定将使他们之前的协议无效。


虚拟语气:

If we had known this information sooner, it would have invalidated our entire strategy. = 如果我们早点知道这些信息，它就会使我们的整个策略无效。

If the results were different, they would invalidate our findings. = 如果结果不同，它们就会使我们的发现无效。


被动语态:

The contract was invalidated by the court. = 合同被法院宣布无效。

The argument was invalidated by the presented evidence. = 论点被提出的证据推翻了。


疑问句:

Does this information invalidate our earlier assumptions? = 这个信息是否推翻了我们早先的假设？

否定句:

The judge did not invalidate the contract. = 法官没有宣布合同无效。

Nothing could invalidate the truth of his statement. = 没有任何事情能够推翻他陈述的真实性。
