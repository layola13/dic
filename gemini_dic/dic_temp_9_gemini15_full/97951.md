innuendo:/ˌɪnjuˈɛndoʊ/| n. |影射；暗讽；弦外之音 | A hint or suggestion of something bad or unpleasant:  an indirect suggestion or intimation.  It often involves a double meaning or veiled reference, leaving the listener to infer the underlying meaning.  The implication is usually negative or critical, but not stated directly. |例句：His speech was full of innuendo, hinting at corruption within the government. =他的讲话充满了影射，暗示政府内部存在腐败。

His silence was a clear innuendo of disapproval. =他的沉默是对其明显的不赞成。

近义词：insinuation, implication, suggestion, hint |反义词：direct statement, explicit statement |词性：名词

词源:
From Italian *innuendo*, from Latin *innuere* "to nod," "to beckon," "to hint."  The Italian word originally meant "nodding" or "winking," implying a gesture suggesting something without openly stating it.  The English sense developed from this suggestive, indirect meaning.
