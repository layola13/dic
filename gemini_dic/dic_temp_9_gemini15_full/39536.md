conquistadors:/ˌkɒŋkɪsˈtɑːdɔːrz/| n. |征服者（尤指16世纪西班牙征服美洲的探险者）|Spanish explorers, especially those who conquered the Americas in the 16th century. They were driven by a combination of religious zeal, the desire for wealth, and a thirst for adventure. Their actions led to the colonization of vast territories and had a profound and often devastating impact on the indigenous populations.|The conquistadors brought disease and devastation to the New World. |explorers, conquerors|collaborators, natives (in the sense of those who resisted conquest)|名词
