scrounging:/ˈskraʊndʒɪŋ/| n. | 搜刮；蹭吃蹭喝；勉强维持生计 | The act of searching for and obtaining things, especially food or money, in a casual or opportunistic way. It often implies a lack of resources or a reluctance to pay. | 例句：He was scrounging around for a cigarette. 他四处搜寻香烟。

例句：They were scrounging for food after the hurricane. 飓风过后，他们四处寻找食物。| 近义词：scavenging, foraging, begging, mooching | 反义词：providing, donating, giving


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is scrounging for change. = 他正在四处寻找零钱。
They are always scrounging off their friends. = 他们总是蹭朋友的东西。

一般过去时:
She scrounged a meal from her neighbour. = 她向邻居讨了一顿饭。
We scrounged some wood for the fire. = 我们搜集了一些柴火生火。

现在完成时:
He has been scrounging around for weeks. = 他已经四处寻找了好几个星期了。
They have scrounged enough money for the trip. = 他们已经为旅行攒够了钱。


过去完成时:
She had scrounged a lift to the station. = 她之前已经搭便车到车站了。
They had scrounged all the food they could find. = 他们已经搜刮了所有能找到的食物。

将来时:
I will scrounge up some money for you. = 我会为你弄到一些钱。
They will be scrounging for accommodation. = 他们将四处寻找住宿的地方。

虚拟语气:

I wish I hadn't scrounged that last cigarette. = 我真希望我没有抽那支最后的一根香烟。

被动语态:

疑问句:
