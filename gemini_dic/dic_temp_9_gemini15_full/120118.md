mimicries:/ˈmɪmɪkriːz/| n.|模仿；拟态；模仿行为|The act of imitating someone or something, especially in a way that is not serious or skillful;  In biology, mimicry refers to the close resemblance of one organism (the mimic) to another organism (the model) .|例句：Her mimicries of the teachers were hilarious.  她的老师模仿滑稽可笑。

例句：The viceroy butterfly exhibits mimicry of the monarch butterfly to avoid predation.  副王蝴蝶模仿帝王蝶来躲避捕食。|近义词：imitations, simulations, impersonations|反义词：originality, authenticity
