matilda:/məˈtɪldə/ | n. | 马蒂尔达 | Matilda is a popular given name for girls, often associated with strength and intelligence.  It is of German origin, ultimately derived from the Germanic element "maht," meaning "might" or "power," combined with "hild," meaning "battle" or "war."  Thus, the name suggests a "powerful battle maiden." The name gained popularity in English-speaking countries after the publication of Roald Dahl's popular children's book "Matilda." | Example: My daughter's name is Matilda. 我的女儿名叫马蒂尔达。|近义词:  Mathilda (variant spelling)|反义词:  None.
