defin:/ˈdɛfɪn/| vt.| 定义，解释 | To state or explain the meaning of (a word, phrase, or concept).  | Example:  The dictionary defines "definitive" as "conclusive."  字典将“最终的”定义为“决定性的”。 |近义词：explain, clarify, explicate | 反义词：obfuscate, confuse


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He defines success as achieving one's goals. = 他把成功定义为实现自己的目标。
The teacher defines the key terms before the test. = 老师在考试前解释了关键词汇。


一般过去时:
She defined her role in the project clearly. = 她清楚地界定了她在项目中的角色。
The scientist defined the parameters of the experiment. = 科学家定义了实验的参数。


现在完成时:
They have defined the scope of the research. = 他们已经界定了研究的范围。
Scientists have defined the function of this gene. = 科学家已经定义了这个基因的功能。


过去完成时:
The author had defined the characters before writing the novel. = 作者在写作小说之前就已定义了人物。
They had defined the problem before attempting a solution. = 他们在尝试解决方案之前已经定义了问题。


将来时:
She will define her future career path carefully. = 她将仔细规划未来的职业道路。
The team will define the project objectives in the meeting. = 团队将在会议上定义项目目标。


虚拟语气:
If I defined my goals clearly, I would be more successful. = 如果我明确地定义我的目标，我会更成功。
I wish I had defined my priorities earlier. = 我真希望我早点确定我的优先级。


被动语态:
Success is defined in many different ways. = 成功可以用许多不同的方式来定义。
The term was defined by the Oxford English Dictionary. = 这个术语由牛津英语词典定义。


疑问句:
How do you define happiness? = 你如何定义幸福？
How is the term "sustainability" defined in this context? = 在这种情况下，“可持续性”是如何定义的？


否定句:
He didn't define his terms clearly enough. = 他没有足够清晰地定义他的术语。
The contract doesn't define the responsibilities of each party. = 合同没有定义各方的责任。
