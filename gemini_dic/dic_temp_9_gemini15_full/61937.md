emulous:/ˈemjʊləs/ | adj. | 羡慕的；嫉妒的；努力模仿的 |  Characterized by a desire to equal or excel others; imitative.  It implies a strong desire to match or surpass someone else's achievements, often accompanied by a degree of rivalry or competition.  | 例句：He was emulous of his brother's success. 他羡慕他哥哥的成功。

He felt emulous of the artist's skill and tried to copy his style. 他羡慕这位艺术家的技巧，并试图模仿他的风格。


近义词：envious, jealous, competitive, imitative | 反义词：indifferent, unconcerned, apathetic


词源:  From Latin *aemulus ("rival"), from aemuli ("rivals").  The word's history reflects a sense of rivalry and competition, aiming to equal or surpass.
