ear:/ɪər/|n.|耳朵；听力；倾听；(植物的)穗|The organ of hearing and balance in vertebrates.|例句：I have an ear infection. 我耳朵感染了。|例句：He has a good ear for music. 他对音乐很有鉴赏力。|近义词：hearing, aural sense|反义词：无


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He listens with his ears. = 他用耳朵倾听。
She has sensitive ears. = 她耳朵很灵敏。


一般过去时:
The dog pricked its ears. = 狗竖起了耳朵。
I strained my ears to hear. = 我竖起耳朵仔细听。


现在完成时:
I have not hurt my ears. = 我没有伤到耳朵。
She has had excellent hearing all her life. = 她一生都拥有极好的听力。


过去完成时:
They had covered their ears before the explosion. = 爆炸前他们已经捂住了耳朵。
I had never heard such a beautiful sound. = 我从未听过如此美妙的声音。


将来时:
I will cover my ears during the concert. = 音乐会期间我会捂住耳朵。
He will need a hearing aid in the future. = 他将来需要助听器。


虚拟语气:
If I had better ears, I could hear you. = 如果我的耳朵更好，我就能听到你。
I wish I hadn't strained my ears yesterday. = 我真希望昨天我没有过度用耳。


被动语态:
His ears were examined by the doctor. = 他的耳朵被医生检查了。
Her ears were filled with water after swimming. = 她游泳后耳朵灌满了水。


疑问句:
Do you have any ear problems? = 你有耳朵问题吗？
Can you hear me with your left ear? = 你左耳能听到我说话吗？


否定句:
I cannot hear you with my right ear. = 我右耳听不见你说话。
He didn't like having his ears touched. = 他不喜欢别人摸他的耳朵。


PHRASE->
bend one's ear = 向某人倾诉
