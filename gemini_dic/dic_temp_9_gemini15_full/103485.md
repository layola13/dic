jobsworths:/ˈdʒɒbzwɜːðz/ | n. |墨守成规者，死板的官僚 |  A jobsworth is a person who insists on following rules and regulations rigidly, even when it is unreasonable or inconvenient to do so. They often prioritize following procedure over achieving results or helping people. This term carries a strong negative connotation, implying inflexibility and a lack of common sense. | Example: "The jobsworth at the counter refused to help me unless I had the exact form filled out, even though I explained my urgent situation." | 近义词: bureaucrat, pedant, stickler | 反义词: flexible, helpful, understanding
