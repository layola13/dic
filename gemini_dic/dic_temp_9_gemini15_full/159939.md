polyphony:/pɒˈlɪfəni/ /pɑːˈlɪfəni/| n. |复调；多声部音乐|The simultaneous combination of different melodic lines in a musical composition.  It is characterized by the independent movement of multiple voices or parts, creating a rich and complex texture. Polyphony contrasts with monophony (a single melodic line) and homophony (a melody with accompaniment).|Example:  Bach's fugues are masterful examples of polyphony.|近义词：counterpoint, multi-voicedness|反义词：monophony, homophony
