innovator:/ˈɪnəveɪtər/ | n. |创新者，革新者 | A person who introduces new methods, ideas, or products.  |  Steve Jobs was a renowned innovator in the technology industry. 史蒂夫·乔布斯是科技行业一位著名的创新者。|  pioneer, trailblazer, groundbreaker | imitator, traditionalist | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The innovator designs new products. = 创新者设计新产品。
She innovates constantly in her field. = 她不断在她所在领域进行创新。


一般过去时:
The innovator created a groundbreaking invention. = 这位创新者创造了一项突破性的发明。
He innovated in the field of sustainable energy. = 他在可持续能源领域进行了创新。


现在完成时:
The innovator has developed several successful products. = 这位创新者已经开发了几种成功的产品。
She has innovated in various fields throughout her career. = 她在职业生涯中各个领域都进行了创新。


过去完成时:
The innovator had already patented his invention before the conference. = 在会议之前，这位创新者已经为他的发明申请了专利。
They had innovated significantly in their approach before achieving success. = 在取得成功之前，他们已经大大改变了他们的方法。


将来时:
The innovator will launch a new product next year. = 创新者明年将推出新产品。
She will continue to innovate and push boundaries. = 她将继续创新并突破界限。


虚拟语气:
If he were an innovator, he would have a different approach. = 如果他是一位创新者，他会采取不同的方法。
It is essential that the innovator be open to new ideas. = 创新者必须对新思想持开放态度。
