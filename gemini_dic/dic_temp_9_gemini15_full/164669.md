preexplanatory:/ˌpriːˌɛksplænəˈtɔːri/| adj.| 预先解释的；解释性的 |  Giving an explanation beforehand; serving as a preliminary explanation.  It implies providing an explanation before the main event or topic is fully presented.  The word combines "pre-" (before) and "explanatory" (giving an explanation). |例句：The preexplanatory remarks clarified the complex data presented in the following graphs. 预先的解释性说明澄清了随后图表中呈现的复杂数据。

The professor offered a preexplanatory lecture on the subject before the students began their research. 教授在学生开始研究之前就该主题做了一个预先解释的讲座。|近义词：introductory, preliminary, preparatory |反义词：post-explanatory, subsequent
