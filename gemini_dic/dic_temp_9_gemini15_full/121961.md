missionist:/ˈmɪʃənɪst/| n. |传教士|A person who is sent on a mission, especially a religious mission to spread their faith to a foreign country or region.  A missionist typically engages in missionary work, including preaching, teaching, and providing social services. |例句：The missionist dedicated his life to converting people to Christianity in remote villages.这位传教士将毕生精力奉献给了在偏远村庄传教，使人们皈依基督教。

例句：Many missionists worked tirelessly to improve the lives of the indigenous people.许多传教士为改善当地土著人民的生活而不懈努力。


近义词：missionary, evangelist, proselytizer|反义词：None specifically, but words implying opposition to missionary work could be considered antonyms, such as secularist, atheist. |词性：名词
