nonempirically:/ˌnɒnɪmˈpɪrɪkli/ | adj. | 非经验地；非实证地 | Not based on or verifiable by observation or experience; theoretical.  It refers to conclusions or arguments that are not derived from empirical evidence or scientific experimentation.  | Example:  The philosopher argued nonempirically for the existence of God.  哲学家以非经验的方式论证了上帝的存在。  | 近义词: theoretically, speculatively, hypothetically | 反义词: empirically, experimentally
