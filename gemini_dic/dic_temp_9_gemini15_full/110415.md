like mad: /laɪk mæd/ | adj. | 非常，极其，疯狂地 | Extremely; intensely; furiously.  This phrase is used to emphasize the degree to which something is happening. It often implies a lack of control or a frenzied activity. | 例句：He was working like mad to finish the project before the deadline. 他为了在截止日期前完成项目而疯狂地工作。

He was running like mad to catch the bus. 他为了赶公共汽车而飞快地跑。


近义词：furiously, frantically, wildly, intensely, desperately | 反义词：slowly, calmly, leisurely


词性：形容词 (adverbial phrase)


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They work like mad every day. = 他们每天都疯狂地工作。
She talks like mad when she's excited. = 她兴奋的时候会滔滔不绝地说个不停。


一般过去时:
He ate like mad at the buffet. = 他在自助餐上狼吞虎咽。
They danced like mad at the party. = 他们在派对上疯狂地跳舞。


现在完成时:
She has worked like mad all year. = 她一年来一直在拼命工作。
He has driven like mad to get here on time. = 他为了准时到达这里而疯狂地开车。


过去完成时:
They had worked like mad before the presentation. = 他们在演示之前拼命工作过。
She had run like mad to escape the danger. = 她为了逃离危险而拼命地跑过。


将来时:
We will work like mad to meet the target. = 我们将努力工作以达到目标。
He will drive like mad to get to the airport. = 他将疯狂地开车去机场。


虚拟语气:
If I had known, I would have worked like mad. = 如果我知道的话，我会拼命工作的。
If she had more time, she would study like mad. = 如果她有更多的时间，她会疯狂地学习。


被动语态:
The work was done like mad to meet the deadline. = 工作为了赶上截止日期而被疯狂地完成。
The preparations were made like mad for the event. = 活动的准备工作被疯狂地进行。


疑问句:

Were they driving like mad? = 他们是不是疯狂地开车？


否定句:
He didn't work like mad; he took his time. = 他没有拼命工作；他慢慢来。
She didn't study like mad for the exam. = 她没有为考试而疯狂地学习。
