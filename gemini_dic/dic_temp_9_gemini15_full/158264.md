play-politics:/pleɪ ˈpɑːlətɪks/| v. |玩弄政治；搞政治阴谋；从事政治活动 |To engage in political maneuvering, often in a manipulative or underhanded way. It suggests actions are primarily motivated by self-interest or partisan advantage, rather than genuine concern for the public good.  |例句：The mayor is accused of playing politics with the city's budget. 市长被指控在市预算上玩弄政治。

He's been playing politics for years, always putting his own interests first. 他多年来一直在玩弄政治，总是把自己的利益放在首位。|近义词：politicking, maneuvering, scheming, intrigue |反义词：being honest, acting impartially, serving the public interest |动词
