inlumine:/ɪnˈluːmɪneɪt/| v.|照亮，阐明|To illuminate means to light up or brighten something. It can also mean to make something clearer or easier to understand.|例句：The moon illuminated the night sky. 月亮照亮了夜空。 The professor's lecture illuminated a complex topic. 教授的讲座阐明了一个复杂的话题。|近义词：light up, brighten, clarify, elucidate|反义词：darken, obscure, confuse


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun illuminates the earth. = 太阳照亮地球。
The lamp illuminates the room. = 灯照亮了房间。

一般过去时:
The fireworks illuminated the night sky. = 烟花照亮了夜空。
The flash illuminated the scene. = 闪光灯照亮了现场。

现在完成时:
The discovery has illuminated the mystery. = 这一发现阐明了这个谜团。
The new evidence has illuminated the case. = 新证据阐明了这个案件。

过去完成时:
The research had illuminated the problem before the presentation. = 研究在演示之前已经阐明了这个问题。
The previous studies had illuminated the pathway. = 先前的研究已经阐明了这条途径。


将来时:
The new streetlights will illuminate the park. = 新的路灯将照亮公园。

虚拟语气:
If the sun illuminated the earth constantly, life would be different. = 如果太阳持续照亮地球，生命将会不同。
If the lecture had illuminated the topic better, I would have understood it. = 如果讲座更好地阐明了主题，我就会理解它了。

被动语态:
The room was illuminated by the chandelier. = 房间被枝形吊灯照亮了。
The mystery was illuminated by the detective's work. = 这个谜团被侦探的工作阐明了。

疑问句:

否定句:
The darkness was not illuminated by any light. = 黑暗没有被任何光照亮。
The lecture failed to illuminate the difficult concepts. = 讲座未能阐明那些难懂的概念。
