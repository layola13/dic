convicted:/kənˈvɪktɪd/| adj.|被判有罪的，确信的|Having been proved guilty of a criminal offence in a court of law.  The adjective form of the verb "convict."|例句：He was convicted of murder. 他被判犯有谋杀罪。

例句：The jury convicted him despite the lack of solid evidence. 尽管缺乏确凿证据，陪审团还是判定他有罪。


近义词：guilty, condemned, sentenced

反义词：acquitted, exonerated


词源:  Comes from the verb "convict," which originates from the Old French word "convicte," meaning "convinced" or "overcome."  This in turn comes from the Latin word "convincere," meaning "to overcome," "conquer," or "prove guilty." The addition of "-ed" creates the past participle and adjectival form.
