reestablished: /ˌriːɪˈstæblɪʃt/ | v. |重建；恢复；重新建立 | To establish something again after it has been lost or destroyed.  This implies a previous existence that has been interrupted. |例句：The government reestablished diplomatic relations with the neighboring country after years of conflict.  政府在多年的冲突后重新与邻国建立了外交关系。

例句：After the fire, the family reestablished their home and lives.  火灾过后，这家人重建了家园和生活。


近义词：restore, reinstate, renew | 反义词：destroy, abolish, dismantle | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reestablish their business every year. = 他们每年都重建他们的业务。
The company reestablishes its market position. = 公司正在重建其市场地位。


一般过去时:
The king reestablished his authority after the rebellion. = 反叛之后，国王重新确立了他的权威。
She reestablished contact with her old friends. = 她重新联系了她的老朋友。


现在完成时:
The government has reestablished order in the city. = 政府已经恢复了城市的秩序。
They have reestablished their reputation. = 他们已经重建了他们的声誉。


过去完成时:
He had reestablished his business before the economic crisis hit. = 在经济危机爆发之前，他已经重建了他的事业。
They had reestablished their friendship before the wedding. = 婚礼之前，他们已经恢复了友谊。


将来时:
We will reestablish the old traditions in our family. = 我们将在我们家庭中恢复旧传统。
The company will reestablish its branch in that city. = 公司将在那个城市重建其分公司。


虚拟语气:
If they had more money, they would reestablish their business. = 如果他们有更多钱，他们就会重建他们的业务。
I wish I could reestablish contact with my childhood friend. = 我希望我能重新联系我的童年朋友。


被动语态:
The bridge was reestablished after the storm. = 暴风雨过后，桥梁被重建了。
Order was reestablished in the streets. = 街道上的秩序得到了恢复。


疑问句:
Will the peace talks reestablish the relationship between the two countries? = 和谈能否恢复两国之间的关系？
Has the company reestablished its presence in the market? = 公司是否已经重新确立了在市场上的地位？


否定句:
They did not reestablish their business after the bankruptcy. = 破产后，他们没有重建他们的业务。
The government failed to reestablish control over the region. = 政府未能重新控制该地区。
