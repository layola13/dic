gloryingly:/ˈɡlɔːriɪŋli/| adv.|光荣地；辉煌地；值得赞扬地 |  Adverb formed from "gloriously," intensifying the sense of glory or splendor. It describes something done in a way that brings great honor, praise, or renown.  The word is relatively uncommon and often found in literary or dramatic contexts. | 例句：He fought gloryingly for his country. 他为国而战，战绩辉煌。
She spoke gloryingly of her achievements. 她光荣地谈论着她的成就。| 近义词：gloriously, triumphantly, splendidly | 反义词：shamefully, disgracefully, ignominiously


USAGE->
This word is an adverb and doesn't have different tenses like verbs.  The tense is determined by the verb it modifies.

Example Sentences illustrating different tenses using "gloriously" (since "gloryingly" is rare):

一般现在时:
He sings gloriously. 他唱得非常出色。
They live gloriously. 他们过着辉煌的生活。

一般过去时:
He sang gloriously. 他唱得非常出色。(过去)
They lived gloriously. 他们过着辉煌的生活。(过去)

现在完成时:
He has sung gloriously many times. 他多次唱得非常出色。
They have lived gloriously throughout their lives. 他们一生都过着辉煌的生活。

过去完成时:
He had sung gloriously before the audience arrived.  观众到来之前，他已唱得非常出色。
They had lived gloriously before facing adversity.  在面临逆境之前，他们过着辉煌的生活。

将来时:
He will sing gloriously at the concert. 他将在音乐会上唱得非常出色。
They will live gloriously in their new home. 他们将在新家过着辉煌的生活。


虚拟语气:
If he sang gloriously, the audience would be thrilled. 如果他唱得出色，观众将会欣喜若狂。
If they had lived gloriously, they wouldn't regret their choices. 如果他们过去生活辉煌，他们就不会后悔自己的选择。

被动语态:
His performance was gloriously received. 他的表演受到了热烈的欢迎。
Their lives have been gloriously celebrated. 他们的人生得到了辉煌的赞扬。

疑问句:
Did he sing gloriously? 他唱得好吗？
Have they lived gloriously? 他们过着辉煌的生活吗？

否定句:
He didn't sing gloriously. 他唱得不好。
They haven't lived gloriously. 他们没有过着辉煌的生活。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases using "gloryingly" or even "gloriously" that function as single units of meaning.  The word is typically used as a straightforward adverb modifying a verb.
