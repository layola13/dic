scleroid:/ˈsklɪərɔɪd/| n.|硬化物；石细胞；硬化组织|A scleroid is a hard, sclerenchymatous cell or group of cells, often found in plant tissues, providing structural support.  They are typically lignified (containing lignin) and have thickened cell walls.  Scleroids can be found in various shapes and sizes, contributing to the hardness and texture of fruits, seeds, and other plant parts.|例句：The gritty texture of the pear is due to the presence of numerous scleroids in its flesh. 梨子的沙粒状质地是由于果肉中存在许多石细胞。|近义词：sclerenchyma, stone cell|反义词：parenchyma
