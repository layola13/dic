self-nourished: /ˌsɛlfˈnɜːrɪʃt/ | adj. | 自养的；自给自足的 | Self-nourished refers to organisms, particularly plants, that can produce their own food from inorganic sources, typically through photosynthesis.  Unlike heterotrophic organisms, which obtain nutrients by consuming other organisms, self-nourished organisms are autotrophic.  | Example:  Algae are self-nourished organisms that convert sunlight into energy.  藻类是能够将阳光转化为能量的自养生物。 |近义词：autotrophic, self-sufficient | 反义词：heterotrophic, dependent
