英文解析：The plural form of "inviolability," referring to the state or quality of being inviolable; something that cannot be violated or infringed upon.  It often refers to rights, principles, or places that are considered sacred or protected.

例句：

The inviolabilities of personal privacy are increasingly threatened by technology. 个人隐私的不可侵犯性正日益受到技术的威胁。

The constitution guarantees certain inviolabilities to its citizens. 宪法保障公民享有某些不可侵犯的权利。


词源:

* **in-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "without"
* **viol-**: from Latin "violare," meaning "to violate," "to injure," or "to profane."
* **-able**: suffix meaning "capable of" or "worthy of"
* **-ity**: suffix forming nouns of quality or state
