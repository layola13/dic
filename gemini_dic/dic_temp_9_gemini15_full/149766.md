palatinates:/ˈpæləteɪts/| n. |腭化音|Palatinates are sounds produced by raising the tongue towards the hard palate (the roof of the mouth).  They are a type of consonant sound.  The exact articulation can vary depending on the language and specific sound.|The English /j/ sound in "yes" is a palatal approximant, while the /ʃ/ sound in "ship" is a palatal fricative.  Many languages feature a variety of palatal consonants and vowels. |近义词: palatalized sounds |反义词:  (No direct antonym; the opposite would be sounds produced elsewhere in the mouth, like velar or labial sounds.) |
