scantle: /ˈskæntəl/ | v. | 少量供应；吝啬地给予；减少 | To give or supply sparingly; to stint; to reduce.  | The farmer scantled the grain, giving only a small portion to each worker.  农民吝啬地分配粮食，只给每个工人一小部分。 | stint,  shorten, reduce |  waste, squander | 动词

词源:
The word "scantle" comes from the Middle English word "scantlen," which itself derives from the Old Norse word "skamtla," meaning "to cut short" or "to curtail."  It reflects the sense of reducing or limiting something.
