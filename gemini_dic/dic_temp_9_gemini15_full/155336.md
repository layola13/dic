pharmacist:/ˈfɑːrməsɪst/| n. |药剂师|A pharmacist is a licensed professional who prepares and dispenses medications, counsels patients on drug use, and provides other healthcare services.  Pharmacists work in various settings, including community pharmacies, hospitals, and research institutions. |The pharmacist filled my prescription for antibiotics.  药剂师为我配好了抗生素处方。|chemist, druggist|doctor, physician|noun

词源:

The word "pharmacist" comes from the Greek word "pharmakeus," meaning "one who prepares drugs" or "medicine seller."  This, in turn, is related to the Greek word "pharmakon," meaning "drug" or "medicine." The suffix "-ist" indicates a person who practices or is skilled in a particular field.
