metempiric:/ˌmɛtɛmˈpɪrɪk/| adj.|超经验的；经验之外的 |Beyond empirical knowledge or verification;  transcending experience.  It refers to knowledge or concepts that cannot be obtained or verified through sensory experience or empirical observation. This often involves concepts that are considered a priori, inherent to the mind, or based on reason rather than observation. |The philosopher argued for a metempirical understanding of reality, one that goes beyond the limitations of sensory perception.  哲学家主张对现实进行超经验的理解，这种理解超越了感官知觉的局限性。|近义词：transcendental, a priori, metaphysical|反义词：empirical, experiential
