nebulous:/ˈnɛbjələs/ | adj. |模糊的，朦胧的；不明确的 | unclear, vague, or ill-defined; indistinct |The future seemed nebulous and uncertain. 未来看起来模糊而不可预测。  His explanation was nebulous and unconvincing. 他的解释模糊不清，令人难以信服。| clear, definite, precise |

词源:
From Latin *nebula ("mist, cloud, vapor"), from PIE *nebh- ("cloud").  The word's connection to nebulae (astronomical clouds of gas and dust) is direct.

用法说明:

The adjective "nebulous" describes something that is unclear, vague, or indistinct. It can refer to physical things, like a hazy sky, or to abstract concepts, like a plan or an idea.  The vagueness often stems from a lack of detail or precision.


例句:

He had a nebulous idea of what he wanted to do with his life. 他对自己想做什么还只有一个模糊的想法。

The details of the agreement were nebulous, leading to much confusion.  协议的细节模糊不清，导致了很大的混乱。


近义词:

vague, unclear, indistinct, hazy, fuzzy, ambiguous, obscure, indeterminate, amorphous


反义词:

clear, definite, precise, distinct, lucid, explicit, transparent
