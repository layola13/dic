conciliation: /ˌkɒnsɪliˈeɪʃən/ | /ˌkɑːnsɪliˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 调解；调和；安抚 | The act of conciliating; the appeasement or overcoming of distrust or hostility.  It involves bringing opposing parties together to find a mutually acceptable solution. |例句：The conciliation between the two warring factions was a delicate process.  两个交战派系的调解是一个微妙的过程。

例句：Through conciliation, they reached an agreement that satisfied both sides. 通过调解，他们达成了双方都满意的协议。


近义词：mediation, reconciliation, appeasement, pacification | 反义词：estrangement, antagonism, discord, conflict | 词性：名词

词源:
Conciliation comes from the Latin word *conciliare*, meaning "to reconcile, win over."  The prefix "con-" means "together," and "-ciliare" is related to the word "call," suggesting the act of bringing together and calling for agreement.
