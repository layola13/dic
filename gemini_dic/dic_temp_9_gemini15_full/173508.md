quasi-formidable:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈfɔːrmɪdəbl/| adj.|似是而非的可怕；表面可怕| seemingly formidable; giving a deceptive impression of being formidable.  The task, while quasi-formidable in appearance, proved surprisingly manageable.|近义词：apparently formidable, seemingly daunting, superficially intimidating |反义词：trivial, insignificant, easily manageable


例句：

The project seemed quasi-formidable at first, with its extensive requirements and tight deadlines, but we managed to complete it ahead of schedule. =  这个项目一开始看起来很可怕，因为它要求很严格，而且截止日期很紧，但我们还是提前完成了。


The opponent's reputation was quasi-formidable, but in reality, they were easily defeated. = 对手的名声很吓人，但实际上，他们很容易被打败。


词源：

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."

* **formidable**: From Old French *formidable*, from Latin *formidābilis*, meaning "causing fear,"  from *formīdō*, meaning "fear," "terror."
