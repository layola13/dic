jeopardising:/ˈdʒepədaɪzɪŋ/| v.|危及；使处于危险境地|To put something or someone at risk of being harmed, damaged, or destroyed.|例句：His reckless driving was jeopardising the lives of others.  他的鲁莽驾驶危及他人的生命。
例句：The company's poor financial decisions are jeopardising its future. 公司糟糕的财务决策正在危及它的未来。|近义词：endangering, risking, threatening|反义词：safeguarding, protecting, securing|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is jeopardising his health by smoking. = 他吸烟正在危及他的健康。
They are jeopardising the project by missing deadlines. = 他们因为错过最后期限而危及了项目。

一般过去时:
She jeopardised her career by making that mistake. = 她因为犯了那个错误而危及了自己的职业生涯。
The storm jeopardised the harvest. = 暴风雨危及了收成。

现在完成时:
His actions have jeopardised the company's reputation. = 他的行为已经危及了公司的声誉。
They have jeopardised the peace talks by walking out. = 他们退场已经危及了和平谈判。

过去完成时:
He had jeopardised his chances of success by being late. = 他因为迟到而已经危及了他成功的几率。
They had jeopardised the mission by revealing the plan. = 他们因为泄露计划而已经危及了任务。

将来时:
The new policy will jeopardise the rights of minorities. = 新政策将危及少数群体的权利。
Continued pollution will jeopardise the environment. = 持续的污染将危及环境。

虚拟语气:
If he continued to drink, he would jeopardise his liver. = 如果他继续喝酒，他会危及他的肝脏。
I wish he hadn't jeopardised the deal. = 我真希望他没有危及那笔交易。

被动语态:
The negotiations were jeopardised by the unexpected announcement. = 谈判因意外的声明而受到了威胁。
The project was jeopardised by a lack of funding. = 该项目由于缺乏资金而受到了威胁。

疑问句:
Is he jeopardising his job by being so outspoken? = 他如此直言不讳是否在危及他的工作？
Are they jeopardising their safety by climbing that mountain? = 他们攀登那座山是否在危及他们的安全？

否定句:
He did not jeopardise his reputation. = 他没有危及他的名誉。
They did not jeopardise the mission. = 他们没有危及任务。


PHRASE->
jeopardise one's health = 危及某人的健康
jeopardise a relationship = 危及一段关系
jeopardise national security = 危及国家安全
jeopardise the environment = 危及环境
jeopardise the peace process = 危及和平进程

词源:
From Old French *jeoparder, from jeu "game, stake" + parier "to wager".  Essentially, to put something at stake in a game or gamble.
