shepherd: /ˈʃɛpərd/ | n. | 牧羊人；牧师；指导者 | A shepherd is a person who takes care of sheep.  They guide and protect the flock.  The word can also be used metaphorically to describe someone who guides or protects a group of people. | 例句：The shepherd led his flock to greener pastures.  牧羊人带领他的羊群到更绿的牧场。


The shepherd guided his lost sheep back to the fold. 牧羊人引导他迷失的羊回到羊圈。


近义词：herdsman, pastor, guide | 反义词：flock (in the sense that a shepherd leads a flock, not the other way around)


词性：名词

词源:
The word "shepherd" comes from Old English *scēaphyrde, a compound of scēap ("sheep") and hyrde ("keeper").  The word's meaning and usage have evolved over time to include metaphorical applications.
