sentencing:/ˈsɛntən(t)sɪŋ/| n.|判刑，宣判；句子构成|The process of determining and imposing a penalty for a crime.  It involves considering the severity of the offense and the defendant's background.  The judge's sentencing was considered lenient by many.|近义词：judgement, condemnation, adjudication|反义词：acquittal


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The judge sentences the criminal to ten years in prison. = 法官判处罪犯十年监禁。
The lawyer carefully crafts the sentencing arguments. = 律师仔细地构思量刑论点。


一般过去时:
The court sentenced him to life imprisonment. = 法庭判处他无期徒刑。
She was sentenced to community service. = 她被判处社区服务。


现在完成时:
The jury has sentenced the defendant to death. = 陪审团已判处被告死刑。(较为少见，因为判决通常是法官进行)
They have just completed the sentencing phase of the trial. = 他们刚刚完成了审判的量刑阶段。


过去完成时:
The judge had already sentenced the criminal before the appeal was filed. = 在上诉提出之前，法官已经判处了罪犯。
After the evidence had been presented, the sentencing began. = 证据提交后，宣判开始了。


将来时:
The judge will sentence the defendant tomorrow. = 法官将于明天对被告进行判决。
He will likely receive a harsh sentence. = 他很可能会受到严厉的惩罚。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, the sentence would be harsher. = 如果证据更充分，判决会更严厉。
I wish the sentencing guidelines were more flexible. = 我希望量刑指导原则更灵活一些。


被动语态:
The criminal was sentenced to twenty years. = 罪犯被判处二十年徒刑。
The sentence was deemed too harsh by the appeals court. = 上诉法院认为判决过于严厉。


疑问句:
What sentence did the judge give? = 法官判了什么刑？
What is the appropriate sentencing for this crime? = 对此罪行应判处什么刑罚？


否定句:
The judge did not sentence him to the death penalty. = 法官没有判处他死刑。
He was not sentenced to prison time. = 他没有被判处监禁。
