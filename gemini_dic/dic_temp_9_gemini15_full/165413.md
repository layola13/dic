prelateship: /ˈprɛlətʃɪp/ | n. |主教职位；高级教士的地位 | The office or dignity of a prelate; the state of being a prelate.  It refers to the position and authority held by a high-ranking member of the clergy, such as a bishop, archbishop, or abbot.  | Example:  His prelateship was marked by both piety and political acumen.  他的主教职位以虔诚和政治手腕并重为标志。  | 近义词：prelacy, episcopate | 反义词：layperson, parishioner
