fluctuate:/ˈflʌktʃueɪt/ | v. |波动，起伏，变化 |To rise and fall irregularly in number, amount, or level.  |例句：The price of oil fluctuates depending on global events.  石油价格根据全球事件而波动。

例句：The temperature fluctuates throughout the day.  一天中的气温波动很大。


近义词：vary, waver, oscillate, undulate | 反义词：stabilize, remain constant


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The stock market fluctuates daily. = 股市每天都在波动。
The water level fluctuates with the tides. = 水位随着潮汐而波动。

一般过去时:
The temperature fluctuated wildly yesterday. = 昨天的气温剧烈波动。
The company's profits fluctuated throughout the year. = 公司的利润在全年波动。

现在完成时:
The price of gold has fluctuated considerably in recent months. = 近几个月来金价波动相当大。
The economy has fluctuated since the pandemic. = 自疫情以来，经济一直在波动。

过去完成时:
The market had fluctuated significantly before the crash. = 在市场崩盘之前，市场已经大幅波动。
The patient's blood pressure had fluctuated throughout the night. = 病人的血压在整个晚上都波动。


将来时:
The exchange rate is expected to fluctuate next week. = 预计下周汇率将波动。
The weather is predicted to fluctuate throughout the week. = 预报本周天气将波动。


虚拟语气:
If the price fluctuated less, we could plan better. = 如果价格波动较小，我们可以更好地计划。
I wish the value of my investment didn't fluctuate so much. = 我希望我的投资价值不要波动这么大。


被动语态:
The data was found to have fluctuated significantly. = 发现数据波动很大。
The results are expected to be fluctuated by external factors. = 预计结果将受到外部因素的影响而波动。


疑问句:
Does the voltage fluctuate often? = 电压经常波动吗？
Does the level of the river fluctuate seasonally? = 河流水位会季节性波动吗？


否定句:
The number of applicants did not fluctuate much. = 申请人数波动不大。
The quality of the product has not fluctuated since the improvements. = 自改进以来，产品的质量没有波动。



PHRASE->
fluctuate wildly = 剧烈波动
fluctuate between...and... = 在...和...之间波动
fluctuate slightly = 轻微波动
fluctuate considerably = 相当波动
fluctuate significantly = 大幅波动


词源:
From Late Latin *fluctuāre, from Latin fluctu-, stem of fluctus ("wave").
