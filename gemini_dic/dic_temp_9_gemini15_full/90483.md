hubristically:/ˈhjuːbrɪstɪkli/| adv. |傲慢地，自负地 | exhibiting excessive pride or self-confidence.  Acting with arrogant disregard for the consequences. |例句：He hubristically dismissed his critics' concerns. 他傲慢地驳斥了批评者的担忧。

例句：She hubristically declared her project a guaranteed success. 她自负地宣布她的项目一定能成功。


近义词：arrogantly, presumptuously, haughtily, superciliously

反义词：humbly, modestly, meekly


词源:  Derived from the Greek word "hubris," meaning excessive pride or arrogance, often leading to a downfall.  The suffix "-istically" transforms the noun into an adverb, indicating the manner in which something is done.
