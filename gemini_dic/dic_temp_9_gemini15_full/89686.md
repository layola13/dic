hoot:/huːt/| n., v.| 呼喊；嘲笑；猫头鹰叫声 | To make a loud, harsh cry, like an owl; to shout or laugh derisively. | 例句：The owl hooted all night. 猫头鹰整夜都在叫。The children hooted with laughter. 孩子们哄堂大笑。|近义词：shout, yell, scream, jeer, mock |反义词：whisper, murmur


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The owl hoots every night. = 猫头鹰每晚都在叫。
They hoot at the comedian's jokes. = 他们嘲笑喜剧演员的笑话。

一般过去时:
The owl hooted loudly last night. = 昨晚猫头鹰大声叫唤。
The audience hooted their disapproval. = 观众大声表示反对。

现在完成时:
The owl has hooted several times tonight. = 猫头鹰今晚已经叫了好几次了。
They have hooted at him all afternoon. = 他们整个下午都在嘲笑他。

过去完成时:
The owl had hooted before I went to bed. = 我睡觉前猫头鹰已经叫过了。
They had hooted at his performance before he finished. = 他表演还没结束，他们就已经开始嘲笑他了。

将来时:
The owl will hoot again soon. = 猫头鹰很快还会再叫。
They will hoot if he tells another joke like that. = 如果他再讲那样一个笑话，他们就会嘲笑他。

虚拟语气:

I wish they hadn't hooted so loudly. = 我希望他们不要叫那么大声。

被动语态:
His jokes were hooted down by the audience. = 他的笑话被观众嘲笑了。
The performance was hooted off the stage. = 表演被嘘下台了。

疑问句:

Why are they hooting? = 他们为什么在叫喊？

否定句:

词源:
The word "hoot" originates from the Old English word "hūtan," meaning "to shout" or "to cry out,"  which further relates to the Proto-Germanic *hūtan-. The onomatopoeic quality of the word reflects the sound of an owl's call.
