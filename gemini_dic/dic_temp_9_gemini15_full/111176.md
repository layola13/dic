lithoglyph:/ˈlɪθəɡlɪf/| n.|石刻; 岩刻|A lithoglyph is a carving or inscription made on a stone surface, often found in ancient or prehistoric sites.  It differs from petroglyphs, which are carvings made *into* the stone.|Example: The archaeologists discovered several intriguing lithoglyphs depicting ancient hunting scenes.  考古学家发现了几幅描绘古代狩猎场面的引人入胜的石刻。|近义词：petroglyph (though distinct), inscription, carving|反义词：None specifically, but the opposite would be something painted or drawn on stone.|名词
