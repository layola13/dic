phagocytically:/ˌfæɡəʊsaɪˈtɪkli/| adv. |吞噬地；吞噬性地|In a manner characteristic of phagocytosis, the process by which a cell engulfs and digests foreign particles or microorganisms.  |例句：The immune cells acted phagocytically, engulfing and destroying the invading bacteria.|近义词：devouringly, voraciously|反义词：passively


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and therefore doesn't conjugate like a verb.  It describes the *manner* of an action, not the action itself.  Therefore, tense, voice, etc., are irrelevant to the word itself.  The tense, voice, etc., would apply to the verb it modifies.


Example Sentences illustrating different verb tenses modified by "phagocytically":

Present Simple:  The macrophages phagocytically consumed the debris.  (巨噬细胞吞噬性地吞噬了碎片。)

Past Simple: The neutrophils phagocytically ingested the pathogens. (中性粒细胞吞噬性地吞噬了病原体。)

Present Perfect: The immune system has phagocytically eliminated many threats. (免疫系统已经吞噬性地消除了许多威胁。)

Future Simple: The body will phagocytically clear the infection. (身体将吞噬性地清除感染。)

Passive Voice: The bacteria were phagocytically destroyed by the white blood cells. (细菌被白细胞吞噬性地摧毁了。)


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases using "phagocytically".  Its usage is primarily limited to scientific or medical contexts.
