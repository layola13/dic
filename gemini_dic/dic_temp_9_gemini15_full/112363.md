los:/lɔːs/ | n. |损失，丢失 | losses refer to the things that are lost, such as money, possessions, or opportunities.  It can also refer to the act of losing something. | Example: The company reported significant losses last quarter.  这家公司上季度报告了巨额亏损。 | The storm caused heavy losses to the farmers. 暴风雨给农民造成了巨大的损失。 | gains, profits | gains, profits | noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company loses money every year. = 这家公司每年都在亏损。
He loses his keys frequently. = 他经常丢钥匙。


一般过去时:
They lost their home in the fire. = 他们在火灾中失去了家园。
I lost my wallet yesterday. = 我昨天丢了钱包。


现在完成时:
She has lost her patience. = 她已经失去了耐心。
We have lost contact with him. = 我们已经和他失去了联系。


过去完成时:
They had lost all their savings before the crisis. = 危机爆发前他们已经损失了所有的积蓄。
He had lost his way before he found the hotel. = 他在找到酒店之前迷路了。


将来时:
We will lose the game if we don't try harder. = 如果我们不更加努力，我们会输掉比赛。
They will lose their jobs if the factory closes. = 如果工厂倒闭，他们将会失去工作。


虚拟语气:
If I lost my job, I would be devastated. = 如果我失业了，我会非常沮丧。
I wish I hadn't lost my phone. = 我真希望我没有丢手机。


被动语态:
The battle was lost. = 战斗输了。
Many lives were lost in the accident. = 许多人在事故中丧生。


疑问句:
Did you lose your keys? = 你丢了钥匙吗？
Have you lost weight recently? = 你最近减重了吗？


否定句:
We didn't lose the match. = 我们没有输掉比赛。
He hasn't lost hope yet. = 他还没有失去希望。
