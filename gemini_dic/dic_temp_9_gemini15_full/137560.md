nontransitiveness:/ˌnɒntrænˈzɪtɪvnəs/ | n. |非及物性，不及物性|The property of a verb or other element not requiring a direct object.  It refers to the grammatical feature of verbs that do not take a direct object.  This contrasts with transitivity, where a verb acts upon a direct object. |The nontransitiveness of the verb "sleep" means it doesn't need a direct object; you simply "sleep."  The nontransitiveness of the preposition "near" means it doesn't take a direct object in the same way as "to".|近义词：intransitivity|反义词：transitivity|名词
