prerejoice:/ˌpriːrɪˈdʒɔɪs/| v.|提前庆祝；预先欢喜|To feel joy or happiness before something good happens.  It often implies that the anticipated event may not actually occur.|例句：They prerejoiced at the prospect of a promotion, only to be disappointed.  他们对晋升的前景提前庆祝，结果却失望了。


例句：The team prerejoiced over their likely victory, but then lost in the final game. 球队对可能的胜利提前庆祝，但在决赛中却输了。|近义词：anticipate, expect with pleasure|反义词：lament, mourn

They prerejoice every year before the harvest festival. = 每年丰收节前他们都会提前庆祝。
