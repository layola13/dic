denormalization:/ˌdiːˈnɔːrməlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |反规范化，反规范化处理 | The process of intentionally introducing redundancy into a database design to improve performance.  This is often done by adding extra columns or tables to store data that could be derived from other tables, thus reducing the need for complex joins during query processing. | Example: Denormalization can improve query speed by eliminating joins, but it increases storage space and can lead to data inconsistencies if not managed carefully.  反规范化可以通过消除连接来提高查询速度，但它会增加存储空间，如果管理不当，还会导致数据不一致。|近义词：redundancy, data redundancy |反义词：normalization
