knock-out:/ˈnɒk.aʊt/ /ˈnɑːk.aʊt/| n. adj. vt.|击倒；淘汰赛；极有吸引力的人或事物；令人惊叹的；使…失去知觉；击倒；淘汰|

英文解析:As a noun, "knock-out" refers to a blow that renders someone unconscious, a stage in a competition where contestants are eliminated, or something exceptionally attractive or impressive. As an adjective, it describes something stunning or exceptionally good. As a verb, it means to render someone unconscious by striking them, or to eliminate someone from a competition.

例句:
The boxer delivered a powerful knock-out punch. 拳击手打出了一记强有力的击倒拳。

The knock-out stage of the tournament is highly anticipated. 锦标赛的淘汰赛阶段备受期待。

She's a real knock-out; everyone admires her beauty. 她真是个尤物；每个人都欣赏她的美貌。


近义词:
noun: knockout blow,  TKO (technical knockout), elimination, stunner, beauty

反义词:
noun: victory (in competition),  insignificant, unattractive
