overstated:/ˌoʊvərˈsteɪtɪd/ | adj. |夸大的；言过其实的 | Exaggerated; representing something as larger, better, or worse than it really is.  |The report overstated the company's profits.  这份报告夸大了公司的利润。|The dangers of smoking are often overstated. 吸烟的危害常常被夸大。| understated, downplayed | accurate, factual


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The news often overstates the impact of minor events. 新闻报道经常夸大一些小事件的影响。
He overstates his qualifications in his resume. 他在简历中夸大了自己的资历。


一般过去时:
She overstated her case in the meeting. 她在会议上夸大了自己的说法。
The politician overstated his accomplishments during the campaign.  这位政治家在竞选期间夸大了自己的成就。


现在完成时:
The media has overstated the severity of the crisis. 媒体夸大了危机的严重性。

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
