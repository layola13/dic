debuted:/dɪˈbjuːtɪd/| v.|首次亮相，初次登台|To make a first appearance or performance, especially in a public context.  |例句：The band debuted their new album last night. 乐队昨晚首发了他们的新专辑。
例句：The young actress debuted on Broadway last year.  这位年轻女演员去年在百老汇首演。|近义词：launched, premiered, introduced, unveiled |反义词：retired, ended, concluded


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He debuts his new painting at the gallery. = 他在画廊展出他的新画。
The singer debuts a new song every year. = 歌手每年都会首发一首新歌。

一般过去时:
The film debuted to rave reviews. = 这部电影首映获得好评如潮。
She debuted her new collection at Paris Fashion Week. = 她在巴黎时装周发布了她的新系列。

现在完成时:
The play has debuted in several major cities. = 这部戏剧已在几个主要城市上演。
They have debuted their new product to great success. = 他们的新产品发布非常成功。

过去完成时:
The artist had debuted several works before he gained recognition. = 在获得认可之前，这位艺术家已经展出过几件作品。
Before the concert, the band had debuted several songs online. = 音乐会之前，乐队已经在网上首发了多首歌曲。

将来时:
The band will debut their new album next month. = 乐队下个月将发行他们的新专辑。
The company will debut its new technology at the trade show. = 公司将在贸易展上推出其新技术。

虚拟语气:
If the play had debuted earlier, it might have been more successful. = 如果这部戏早些时候首演，可能会更成功。
I wish I had debuted my novel sooner. = 我希望我早点发表我的小说。

被动语态:
The new car was debuted at the auto show. = 新车在车展上首次亮相。
The album was debuted to mixed reviews. = 专辑首发反响不一。

疑问句:
When did the artist debut his first painting? = 艺术家什么时候展出了他的第一幅画？
Has the band debuted their new single yet? = 乐队是否已经发布了他们的新单曲？

否定句:
The band did not debut their album on time. = 乐队没有按时发行他们的专辑。
The product has not yet been debuted to the public. = 该产品尚未向公众推出。
