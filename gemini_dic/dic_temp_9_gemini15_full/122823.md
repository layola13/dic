molehill:/ˈmoʊlˌhɪl/ | n. |土堆；小障碍；微不足道的事 | A small mound of earth thrown up by a mole.  It is often used figuratively to represent a small problem or difficulty that is made to seem much larger than it actually is. |例句：He made a molehill out of a mountain. 他小题大做。|例句：The delay was just a molehill compared to the other problems we faced.  与我们面临的其他问题相比，这次延误只是小事一桩。|近义词：bump, hillock, trifling matter |反义词：mountain, major problem
