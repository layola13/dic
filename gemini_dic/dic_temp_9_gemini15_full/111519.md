loanwords:/ˈloʊnˌwɜːrdz/ | n. | 借词 | Words adopted into one language from another.  Loanwords are incorporated into a language's vocabulary without undergoing significant phonological or morphological changes.  They reflect the influence of other cultures and languages on a particular language.  | Example: The word "tsunami" is a Japanese loanword in English.  | 近义词：borrowed words, foreign words | 反义词：native words, indigenous words
