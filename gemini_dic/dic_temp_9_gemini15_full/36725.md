collocation:/ˌkɒləˈkeɪʃən/| /ˌkɑːləˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |搭配；词语搭配 |A collocation is a combination of words that frequently occur together.  These words don't necessarily have a grammatical connection, but they are often used together because of their meaning and usage. Understanding collocations is crucial for sounding natural and fluent in a language. For example, "heavy rain" is a collocation, while "strong rain" is less common, even though both are grammatically correct. | Example:  The collocation "make a mistake" is more natural than "do a mistake".  The phrase "strong coffee" is a common collocation, while "powerful coffee" sounds less natural. | 近义词：combination, phrase, expression | 反义词：None  | 名词
