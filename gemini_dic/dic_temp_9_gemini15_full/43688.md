credit:/ˈkrɛdɪt/ | n., v. | 信用，学分，贷方；相信，归功于 |  Trust or confidence placed in someone or something; a unit of academic work completed; an entry on the credit side of an account. | To believe or accept that something is true or correct; to attribute something to someone.  |近义词：belief, trust, loan, score; ascribe, attribute | 反义词：debt, debit, distrust, discredit


USAGE->

一般现在时:
I credit him with the discovery. = 我相信这个发现归功于他。
She credits her success to hard work. = 她把她的成功归功于努力工作。


一般过去时:
The bank credited my account with $100. = 银行将我的账户贷记了100美元。
He credited the author with a great imagination. = 他认为这位作者拥有丰富的想象力。


现在完成时:
They have credited the film with revitalizing the genre. = 他们认为这部电影使该类型电影焕发了活力。
I have credited all my sources in the bibliography. = 我已在参考文献中列出了所有来源。


过去完成时:
She had credited her father with teaching her everything she knew. = 她过去一直认为她所知道的一切都是她父亲教的。
The company had credited the wrong account. = 公司把款记入了错误的账户。


将来时:
The bank will credit your account tomorrow. = 银行明天将把款项记入你的账户。
We will credit your account once we receive your payment. = 我们收到您的付款后，将把款项记入您的账户。


虚拟语气:
If I were to credit his claims, I would be a fool. = 如果我相信他的说法，我就是一个傻瓜。
I wish I could credit him with more sincerity. = 我希望我能更真诚地相信他。


被动语态:
The account was credited with the deposit. = 账户已记入存款。
His work was credited to the wrong person. = 他的作品被错误地归功于他人。


疑问句:
To whom do you credit this invention? = 你认为这项发明是谁的功劳？
Do you credit his explanation? = 你相信他的解释吗？


否定句:
I don't credit his story. = 我不相信他的故事。
The bank did not credit my account. = 银行没有将钱记入我的账户。
