huaca: /ˈwɑːkə/ | n. |瓦卡（印加文化中的神圣场所）| A huaca is a sacred place or object in Inca culture, often associated with ancestor veneration and ritual practices.  These could be natural features like mountains or springs, or artificial structures like temples or pyramids.  The term encompasses a wide range of sites and objects imbued with spiritual significance. |The ancient Incas revered the huacas, offering sacrifices and performing ceremonies at these sacred sites.  |近义词: shrine, sanctuary, sacred place |反义词: profane, unholy |名词
