preorganize: /ˌpriːˈɔːrɡənaɪz/ | vt. |预先组织；预先安排 | To organize something in advance.  |例句：We need to preorganize the logistics for the conference. 我们需要预先安排会议的物流。

例句：The team preorganized their tasks to ensure efficiency.  团队预先安排了他们的任务以确保效率。


近义词：plan, arrange, prepare | 反义词：disorganize, improvise | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They preorganize their schedules every Sunday. = 他们每周日都会预先安排他们的日程。
We preorganize our marketing campaigns months in advance. = 我们提前几个月预先安排我们的营销活动。


一般过去时:
She preorganized the birthday party meticulously. = 她一丝不苟地预先安排了生日派对。
They preorganized the evacuation plan before the hurricane. = 他们在飓风来临之前预先安排了疏散计划。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:
