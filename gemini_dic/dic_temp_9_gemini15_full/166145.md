preprohibitions:/ˌpriːprəʊhɪˈbɪʃənz/| n.| 预先禁止；先期禁止 |  The term "preprohibitions" refers to actions or regulations put in place to prevent something before it even becomes a problem or possibility.  It suggests a proactive approach to avoiding future prohibitions.  This word is quite rare and not commonly used in everyday language. It's more likely to be found in academic or highly specialized contexts. | 例句：The committee discussed preprohibitions on certain types of hazardous waste disposal to prevent future environmental contamination. = 委员会讨论了对某些类型的危险废物处置进行预先禁止，以防止未来的环境污染。


The company implemented preprohibitions on the use of certain chemicals to comply with upcoming safety regulations. = 公司实施了对某些化学品使用的预先禁止，以遵守即将出台的安全法规。


近义词： preventative measures, anticipatory restrictions, preemptive bans | 反义词：post-facto prohibitions, belated restrictions |  词性：名词
