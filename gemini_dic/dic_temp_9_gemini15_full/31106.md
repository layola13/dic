champlevé:/ʃæmˈleɪv/| n.|掐丝珐琅|Champlevé is an enameling technique in which cells or compartments are carved into the metal surface to hold the enamel.  The metal itself forms the outlines of the design. This is in contrast to cloisonné, where thin metal strips are soldered onto the surface to create the cells.|The 12th-century champlevé enamel reliquary of the Three Kings in Cologne Cathedral is a masterpiece of the technique.|近义词：cloisonné (though a different technique)|反义词：N/A
