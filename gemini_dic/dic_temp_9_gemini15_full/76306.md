gabby:/ˈɡæbi/| adj.|喋喋不休的，话多的 | talkative; given to excessive talk |例句：She's a very gabby person; I can barely get a word in edgewise. 她是个非常话多的人；我几乎插不上话。|例句：He was gabby about his trip to Europe. 他喋喋不休地谈论着他去欧洲的旅行。|近义词：talkative, loquacious, voluble |反义词：taciturn, reticent, silent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is gabby today. = 他今天话很多。
She is always gabby on the phone. = 她打电话时总是喋喋不休。

一般过去时:
He was gabby at the party. = 他在派对上话很多。
She was gabby about her new job. = 她对她的新工作喋喋不休。

现在完成时:
He has been gabby all afternoon. = 他整个下午都在喋喋不休。
She has been gabby since she got the good news. = 自从她得到好消息以来，她就一直喋喋不休。

过去完成时:
He had been gabby before the meeting started. = 会议开始前他就一直在喋喋不休。
She had been gabby all morning, so no one wanted to listen anymore. = 她整个上午都在喋喋不休，所以没有人想再听了。

将来时:
He will be gabby at the dinner party. = 他在晚宴上将会话很多。
She will be gabby about her achievements. = 她将会喋喋不休地谈论她的成就。


虚拟语气:
If he were less gabby, people would listen more. = 如果他不那么话多，人们会更多地倾听。
I wish she weren't so gabby. = 我希望她不要那么话多。

被动语态:
(Gabby is rarely used in the passive voice.  A sentence like "He was gabbed at" would be unusual and require a different meaning of "gab".)


疑问句:

否定句:

She wasn't usually so gabby. = 她通常不会那么话多。
