prick:/prɪk/| n., v.| 刺，扎；戳；刺激；令人讨厌的人 |

英文解析：
As a noun, "prick" refers to a small, sharp point, such as a pinprick or thorn.  It can also be used figuratively to describe a person who is unpleasant or annoying.  As a verb, "prick" means to pierce or puncture with a sharp point, or to cause a stinging or irritating sensation.


例句：
The rose prick pierced my finger.  玫瑰刺扎破了我的手指。

He felt a sharp prick of conscience. 他感到一阵强烈的良心谴责。

Verb: pierce, puncture, jab, sting, irritate


反义词：
