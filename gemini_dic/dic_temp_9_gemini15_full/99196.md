interclashing: /ˌɪntərˈklæʃɪŋ/ | adj. | 交错冲突的，互相冲突的 |  The word describes a situation where multiple things or ideas are conflicting or clashing with each other simultaneously. It emphasizes the interwoven and simultaneous nature of the conflict.  | Example: The interclashing ideologies of the two political parties made compromise impossible.  两个政党的意识形态相互冲突，使得妥协成为不可能。 | 近义词：conflicting, clashing, colliding, overlapping | 反义词：harmonious, concordant, compatible

词源:

The word "interclashing" is formed by combining the prefix "inter-" (meaning "between" or "among") with the verb "clash" (meaning to collide or conflict).  The "-ing" suffix transforms the verb into a present participle, functioning as an adjective in this case.
