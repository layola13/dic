honoured:/ˈɒnəd/ /ˈɑːnərd/| adj.| 尊敬的；荣幸的；获得荣誉的 |Feeling great respect for someone or something; given an honour or award.| I am honoured to receive this award. 我很荣幸获得这个奖项。| respected, esteemed, privileged | dishonoured, disgraced, shamed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is honoured by their invitation. 他很荣幸收到他们的邀请。
She feels honoured to be part of the team. 她很荣幸能成为团队的一员。

一般过去时:
They were honoured guests at the ceremony. 他们是典礼上的贵宾。
I was honoured to meet the president. 我很荣幸能见到总统。

现在完成时:
She has been honoured with many awards. 她获得了许多奖项。
He has been honoured for his contributions to science. 他因对科学的贡献而受到表彰。

过去完成时:
The team had been honoured for their performance.  团队因其表现而获得表彰。
They had been honoured to have hosted such an event. 他们很荣幸能主办这样的活动。

将来时:
He will be honoured at the upcoming gala. 他将在即将到来的盛典上受到表彰。
She will be honoured for her lifetime achievement. 她将因其毕生成就而受表彰。

虚拟语气:
I would be honoured if you accepted my invitation. 如果你接受我的邀请，我将不胜荣幸。
She would have been honoured to attend, but she was ill. 她本来很荣幸能参加，但她病了。

被动语态:
He was honoured for his bravery. 他因勇敢而受到表彰。
The award was honoured by many people. 许多人对这个奖项表示敬意。

疑问句:
Are you honoured to be here? 你很荣幸来到这里吗？
Was he honoured by the promotion? 他对晋升感到荣幸吗？

否定句:
He was not honoured by their comments. 他对他们的评论并不感到荣幸。
She did not feel honoured by the gesture. 她对这个姿态并不感到荣幸。
