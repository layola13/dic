punctuator: /ˈpʌŋktʃueɪtər/ | n. |标点符号 |A punctuator is a symbol or character used in writing to separate sentences, clauses, or phrases, and to indicate pauses or intonation.  It helps clarify meaning and structure in written text. Examples include periods (.), commas (,), question marks (?), exclamation points (!), and colons (:).|  The writer used too many commas, making the sentence difficult to understand.  |近义词：punctuation mark |反义词：无
