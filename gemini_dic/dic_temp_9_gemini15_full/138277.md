notarially:/noʊˈteəriəli/ | adv. | 公证地；公证员地 | Done or executed with the formalities required by law for a notary public to attest to the authenticity of a signature or document.  It implies legal validity and official attestation by a notary. | The document was notarially sealed to ensure its legal validity. 这份文件加盖了公证章以确保其法律效力。 | officially, legally | informally, illegally | 副词


USAGE->
This word is an adverb and doesn't have tenses like verbs.  It describes how an action is performed.  Therefore, typical verb tense examples are not applicable.  Instead, we'll show how it modifies different types of sentences.


Declarative Sentence:
The contract was notarially witnessed. = 合同经公证见证。

The deed was notarially executed. = 该契约已依法公证办理。


Interrogative Sentence:
Was the will notarially attested? = 这份遗嘱是否经过公证？

Is this document notarially valid in this jurisdiction? = 这份文件在本司法管辖区是否具有公证效力？


Negative Sentence:
The agreement wasn't notarially sealed. = 这份协议没有加盖公证章。

The signature was not notarially verified. = 签名未经公证验证。



PHRASE->
There are no common set phrases using "notarially".  Its usage is primarily standalone to describe the manner in which a legal action or document is handled.
