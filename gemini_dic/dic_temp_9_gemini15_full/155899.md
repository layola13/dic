photochemotherapy:/ˌfoʊtoʊˌkemoʊˈθerəpi/ | n. | 光化学疗法 | Treatment that combines the use of light (usually ultraviolet A or UVA) with a photosensitizing drug (psoralen) to treat skin conditions.  This therapy is often used to treat psoriasis, vitiligo, and cutaneous T-cell lymphoma. | Example:  The dermatologist recommended photochemotherapy as a treatment option for her psoriasis. 皮肤科医生建议她采用光化学疗法治疗牛皮癣。

|近义词: PUVA (psoralen and ultraviolet A therapy) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific treatment) | 词性: n.
