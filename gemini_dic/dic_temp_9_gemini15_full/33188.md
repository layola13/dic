chromophane:/ˈkroʊmoʊfeɪn/| n. |色素体，显色体|A chromophane is a substance that produces color, often used in the context of biological or chemical staining techniques.  It can refer to a naturally occurring pigment or a synthetic dye that imparts color to a material or tissue for better visualization or analysis. |Example:  The chromophane stained the cells a vibrant blue, allowing for easier identification under the microscope.  = 显色体将细胞染成鲜艳的蓝色，以便在显微镜下更容易识别。|近义词：pigment, dye, stain |反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with colorless substances or bleaching agents.
