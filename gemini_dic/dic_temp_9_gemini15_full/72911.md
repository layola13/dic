foments:/fəˈment/| vt.|煽动；激起；酿成|To incite or stir up (an undesirable or violent feeling, especially among a group of people).  It can also refer to the process of something gradually developing or worsening.|例句：The politician's speech fomented unrest in the city.  这位政治家的演讲煽动了该市的骚乱。

The rumors fomented fear and distrust among the villagers.  谣言在村民中煽起了恐惧和不信任。|近义词：incite, instigate, stir up, provoke|反义词：calm, pacify, quell, soothe|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He foments discord among his colleagues. = 他在同事中煽动不和。
The media often foments public anxiety. = 媒体经常煽动公众的焦虑。


一般过去时:
The general fomented a rebellion against the king. = 将军煽动了一场反对国王的叛乱。
His actions fomented a crisis in the company. = 他的行为在公司酿成了一场危机。


现在完成时:
They have fomented hatred and division for years. = 他们多年来一直在煽动仇恨和分裂。
The opposition party has fomented discontent among the people. = 反对党一直在煽动人民的不满。


过去完成时:
He had fomented a coup before fleeing the country. = 他在逃离国家之前策划了一场政变。
The rebels had fomented unrest for months before the uprising. = 反叛分子在起义前几个月就一直在煽动骚乱。


将来时:
They will foment trouble if we don't address their concerns. = 如果我们不解决他们的担忧，他们将会制造麻烦。
The extremists will foment violence in the streets. = 极端分子将在街头煽动暴力。


虚拟语气:
If he fomented a revolution, he would be hailed as a hero. = 如果他发动革命，他将被誉为英雄。
I wish he hadn't fomented such discord in the family. = 我真希望他没有在家庭中煽动如此多的不和。


被动语态:
Discontent was fomented by the government's policies. = 不满是由政府的政策煽动的。
Trouble was fomented by the irresponsible media reporting. = 麻烦是由不负责任的媒体报道煽动的。


疑问句:
What factors are fomenting this unrest? = 哪些因素在煽动这种骚乱？
Who is fomenting this hatred? = 谁在煽动这种仇恨？


否定句:
The government did not foment the protests. = 政府没有煽动抗议活动。
He didn't want to foment any trouble. = 他不想制造任何麻烦。


PHRASE->
foment trouble = 挑起事端

词源:
From Old French *fomenter, from Latin *fomentare, frequentative of *fovēre "to keep warm," "cherish," "foster," related to Latin focus "hearth." The sense evolution is from "warm" to "cherish" to "foster" to "incite"
