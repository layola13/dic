molded:/ˈmoʊldɪd/| adj. v. | 模制的；塑造的；形成的 | Shaped or formed in a particular way, often by being poured into a mold.  |例句：The clay was molded into a beautiful vase.  黏土被塑造成一个美丽的的花瓶。
The politician molded public opinion with his speeches.  这位政治家通过演讲塑造了公众舆论。|近义词：shaped, formed, cast, fashioned |反义词：unshaped, amorphous


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He molds clay into various shapes. = 他把黏土塑造成各种形状。
She molds her students into responsible citizens. = 她把她的学生塑造成负责任的公民。

一般过去时:
The sculptor molded the statue from bronze. = 雕塑家用青铜铸造了这尊雕像。
They molded the plastic into a car part. = 他们将塑料塑造成汽车零件。

现在完成时:
The artist has molded countless sculptures throughout her career. =这位艺术家在她的职业生涯中塑造了无数的雕塑。
He has molded his company into a market leader. = 他已将他的公司塑造成市场领导者。

过去完成时:
The chef had molded the dough before adding the filling. =厨师在添加馅料之前已经揉好了面团。
She had molded her life around her children's needs. = 她一直以孩子的需求为中心安排自己的生活。

将来时:
The designer will mold the metal into a futuristic design. = 设计师将把金属塑造成未来主义的设计。
They will mold the public's perception through advertising. = 他们将通过广告来塑造公众的认知。

虚拟语气:
If I had the right tools, I would mold the wax into a candle. = 如果我有合适的工具，我会把蜡塑造成蜡烛。
I wish I could mold my future as easily as clay. = 我希望我能像捏黏土一样轻松地塑造我的未来。

被动语态:
The chocolate was molded into festive shapes. = 巧克力被塑造成节日形状。
The opinions of the public were molded by the media. = 公众的意见受到了媒体的塑造。

疑问句:

否定句:
