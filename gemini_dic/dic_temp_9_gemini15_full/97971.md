inobservable: /ˌɪnəbˈzɜːrvəbl/ | adj. |不可观察的，不可见的 |  Something that is inobservable cannot be seen, detected, or perceived.  It is beyond the range of observation or detection using current methods or technology.  This often applies to things that are too small, too distant, too fast, or too subtle to be observed. |


例句：

The effects of dark matter are inobservable directly, but they can be inferred from their gravitational effects. 暗物质的影响是直接不可观察的，但可以从其引力效应推断出来。

Quantum phenomena at the subatomic level are often inobservable to the naked eye. 亚原子水平的量子现象通常用肉眼无法观察到。


近义词：undetectable, imperceptible, invisible, unobservable

反义词：observable, detectable, visible, perceptible

词性：形容词
