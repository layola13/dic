mandated:/ˈmænˌdeɪtɪd/ | v. | 命令；授权；委托 |  To officially order or demand something, or to give someone the official authority to do something. |The government mandated the use of seat belts. 政府强制要求使用安全带。|The court mandated a solution to the environmental problem. 法院责令解决环境问题。|近义词：ordered, required, authorized, commissioned |反义词：permitted, allowed, optional


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government mandates the use of helmets. = 政府强制要求使用头盔。
The company mandates regular safety training. = 公司强制要求定期进行安全培训。


一般过去时:
The judge mandated a new trial. = 法官下令进行新的审判。
The school mandated a new dress code. = 学校颁布了新的着装规定。


现在完成时:
The committee has mandated several changes. = 委员会已经强制实施了几项更改。
The regulations have mandated stricter environmental controls. = 法规已经强制执行了更严格的环境控制。


过去完成时:
They had mandated a curfew before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们已经强制实行宵禁。
The board had mandated the changes before the meeting. = 会议前董事会已经批准了这些变更。


将来时:
The council will mandate the construction of a new bridge. = 理事会将强制要求建造一座新桥。
The company will mandate the use of new software. = 公司将强制要求使用新软件。


虚拟语气:
If the law mandated it, we would comply. = 如果法律强制要求，我们将遵守。
I wish the school mandated more physical education. = 我希望学校强制要求更多的体育课。


被动语态:
The use of pesticides was mandated by the government. = 杀虫剂的使用是由政府强制规定的。
New safety regulations were mandated last year. = 去年强制实施了新的安全条例。


疑问句:
Was the change mandated by the board? = 董事会是否强制要求了这一变化？
Has the company mandated any new policies? = 公司是否强制实施了任何新的政策？


否定句:
The law did not mandate the use of that product. = 法律没有强制要求使用该产品。
The school did not mandate uniforms. = 学校没有强制要求穿制服。
