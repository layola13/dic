cavilous:/ˈkævɪləs/| adj.|吹毛求疵的，苛求的 |  Inclined to raise trivial objections; tending to find fault. Characterized by or given to caviling. |例句：His cavilous criticism was more annoying than helpful.  他的吹毛求疵的批评弊大于利。

例句：She's known for her cavilous nature, always finding something wrong with everything. 她以吹毛求疵的性格而闻名，总是对每件事都挑毛病。


近义词：critical, faultfinding, hypercritical, nitpicking, quibbling


反义词：uncritical, accepting, tolerant, forgiving


词源：源自拉丁语 *cavillare “to jest, quibble”,  a frequentative of *cavere “to beware, take heed”.  The original sense implied a clever but petty argument, often used to avoid a more serious issue.


用法说明:

cavilous 主要用作形容词，修饰人或其行为。它强调的是对细节的过度关注以及对微不足道的缺陷的强调，而不是对主要问题的实质性批评。  它通常带有负面含义，暗示批评者过于苛刻或不公正。
