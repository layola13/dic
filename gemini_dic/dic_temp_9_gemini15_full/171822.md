puncturing: /ˈpʌŋktʃərɪŋ/ | adj., v. | 刺穿的；穿孔的；（使）泄气；批评； |

英文解析:  As an adjective, "puncturing" describes something that pierces or makes a hole. As a verb, it means to make a hole in something with a sharp point, or to deflate something, or to criticize someone or something sharply.

例句:

The sharp point of the needle made a puncturing wound.  = 针尖造成的伤口是刺穿的。

His puncturing criticism of the proposal left the team demoralized. = 他对该提案的尖锐批评使团队士气低落。


近义词:  piercing, perforating, deflating, criticizing, debunking

反义词:  repairing, patching, inflating, praising, supporting


词性: Adjective, Verb
