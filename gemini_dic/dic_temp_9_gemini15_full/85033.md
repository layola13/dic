harbingers:/ˈhɑːrbɪndʒərz/| n.|预兆，先兆，先驱|A harbinger is a person or thing that foreshadows a future event, especially an unwelcome one.  It suggests something is coming, often something significant or momentous, but not necessarily negative.|例句：The dark clouds are harbingers of a storm.  乌云是暴风雨的预兆。

例句：The early frost is a harbinger of winter. 早霜是冬季的先兆。


近义词：omen, precursor, forerunner, portent, sign

反义词：aftermath, consequence, result (these are opposites in the sense that they are what *follows*, not what *precedes*)

词性：名词


词源：源自古法语 *harbinger*,  最终源自古高地德语 *heriberi*  "messenger".  The original meaning was a messenger or herald, which evolved to its current meaning of something that precedes and indicates a future event.
