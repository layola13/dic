parashah: /pəˈrɑːʃə/ | n. | 帕夏（犹太教经文的一段） | A parashah (plural parashat or parashot) is a section of the Torah (the five books of Moses) that is read in a synagogue on a particular Sabbath or holiday.  The Torah is divided into 54 parashot, each typically read over a period of several weeks.  | Example: This week's parashah discusses the laws of tithing.  本周的帕夏讨论的是什一税的律法。 | 近义词: reading, section, passage | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it refers to a specific unit of text)
