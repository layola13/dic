ineradicable: /ˌɪnɪˈrædɪkəbl/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；无法根除的 | incapable of being eradicated or removed; persistent.  | The prejudice against women in the workplace is ineradicable. 工作场所对女性的偏见是根深蒂固的。 |  incurable, indelible, ingrained, permanent | erasable, removable, curable


USAGE->

词源:

* **in-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "without"
* **eradicate**:  verb meaning "to remove or destroy completely" (from Latin *ēradicāre*, "to root up," from *ē-*, "out" + *radix*, "root")
* **-able**: suffix meaning "capable of being"


Therefore, "ineradicable" literally means "not capable of being eradicated."
