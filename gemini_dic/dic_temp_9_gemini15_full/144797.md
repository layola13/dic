over-focused: /ˌoʊvərˈfoʊkəst/ | adj. | 过度专注的，注意力过于集中的 |  Describes someone who concentrates too intensely on one thing, often to the detriment of other important aspects.  This can lead to tunnel vision and neglecting broader perspectives or other tasks. |

例句：He became over-focused on his work and neglected his family and health.  = 他过度专注于工作，而忽略了家庭和健康。

例句：Her over-focused approach to the project prevented her from seeing alternative solutions. = 她对项目的过度专注让她无法看到其他的解决方案。


近义词：obsessed, preoccupied, single-minded, tunnel-visioned

反义词：distracted, unfocused, broad-minded, versatile


词源：The word is a combination of "over-" (meaning excessively or too much) and "focused" (from "focus," meaning the center of interest or attention).  The root of "focus" traces back to Latin "focus" (hearth).
