nurturing: /ˈnɜːrtʃərɪŋ/ | adj. | 养育的；培育的；关爱的 | Providing care and support for someone or something to help it grow and develop.  |例句：Her nurturing approach to teaching helped her students thrive.  她的关爱式教学方法帮助她的学生茁壮成长。
例句：The nurturing environment of the preschool allowed the children to explore their creativity.  幼儿园的关爱环境让孩子们能够探索他们的创造力。|近义词：cherishing, fostering, cultivating, supporting |反义词：neglecting, ignoring, abandoning


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher is nurturing her students' talents. = 老师正在培养学生们的才能。
He is nurturing a small plant in his garden. = 他正在花园里培育一株小植物。

一般过去时:
She nurtured her child with love and care. = 她用爱和关怀抚养她的孩子。
The gardener nurtured the seedlings carefully. = 园丁小心翼翼地培育幼苗。

现在完成时:
They have nurtured a strong bond between them. = 他们已经培养了彼此之间牢固的联系。
She has nurtured her artistic skills over many years. = 她多年来一直培养自己的艺术技巧。

过去完成时:
He had nurtured his ambition for years before achieving success. = 在取得成功之前，他已经多年来培养自己的雄心壮志。
The company had nurtured a culture of innovation. = 公司已经培养了一种创新文化。

将来时:
We will nurture the seeds and watch them grow. = 我们将培育种子并观察它们的生长。
She will nurture her baby with tender care. = 她将用温柔的关怀抚养她的婴儿。

虚拟语气:
If I had nurtured my talent, I might have been successful. = 如果我培养了我的才能，我可能会成功。
I wish I had nurtured my relationships better. = 我希望我更好地维护了我的关系。

被动语态:

The children were nurtured in a loving home. = 孩子们在一个充满爱的家中长大。

疑问句:

否定句:
He did not nurture his friendships. = 他没有培养他的友谊。
They did not nurture their potential. = 他们没有培养他们的潜能。
