make waves:/meɪk weɪvz/| v.|兴风作浪；引起轰动；造成影响|To cause a commotion or disturbance; to create a significant impact or stir; to become noticeable or prominent.|例句：Her new book is making waves in the literary world. 她的新书在文学界引起了轰动。|例句：He's trying to make waves by challenging the company's policies. 他试图通过挑战公司的政策来兴风作浪。|近义词：cause a stir, create a sensation, ruffle feathers|反义词：make no difference, remain unnoticed, be inconspicuous|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They make waves in the community every year with their charity work. = 他们每年都通过慈善工作在社区引起轰动。
The new policy is making waves among the employees. = 新政策在员工中引起了反响。

一般过去时:
His speech made waves at the conference. = 他的演讲在会议上引起了轰动。
The scandal made waves across the nation. = 这场丑闻在全国引起了轩然大波。

现在完成时:
The band has made waves in the music industry. = 这个乐队在音乐界已经引起轰动。
Her groundbreaking research has made waves in the scientific community. = 她开创性的研究在科学界引起了轰动。

过去完成时:
Before the announcement, the company had made waves with its innovative products. = 在公告发布之前，该公司凭借其创新产品已经引起轰动。
They had made waves in the political arena before the election. = 他们在选举前就在政坛上引起过轰动。

将来时:
His upcoming movie is expected to make waves at the box office. = 他的即将上映的电影有望在票房上取得成功。
The new technology will make waves in the market. = 这项新技术将在市场上掀起波澜。

虚拟语气:
If he had followed the rules, he wouldn't have made waves. = 如果他遵守规则，就不会引起麻烦了。
I wish I hadn't made waves at the party. = 我真希望我没有在派对上引起麻烦。

被动语态:
Waves were made by the unexpected announcement. = 意外的公告引起了轰动。
Significant waves were made in the industry by the new technology. = 新技术在行业中引起了巨大的反响。

疑问句:

否定句:
The changes didn't make waves. = 这些变化没有引起什么轰动。
The new product launch didn't make waves as expected. = 新产品的发布并没有像预期的那样引起轰动。
