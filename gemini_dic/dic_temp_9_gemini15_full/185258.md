retransformation:/ˌriːtrænsfərˈmeɪʃən/ | n. | 再转化；重塑；再转变 | The process of transforming something again, often after an initial transformation.  It can refer to a biological process, a social process, or a technological process. For example, in genetics, it refers to the process by which a transformed cell loses its transformed phenotype and reverts to its original state.  Another example is a company undergoing retransformation after a merger to redefine its identity and operations. | Example: The retransformation of the derelict building into a modern art gallery was a remarkable feat.  =  废弃建筑物改造成现代艺术馆是一项了不起的成就。 The bacteria underwent retransformation, losing their antibiotic resistance. = 细菌发生了再转化，失去了抗生素抗性。| 近义词：restructuring, metamorphosis, regeneration | 反义词：degeneration, decay
