discrimination: /dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. |歧视，辨别 | The act of making a distinction; treating someone differently based on prejudice.  It can refer to unfair or prejudicial treatment of people based on their race, religion, sex, age, etc. |例句：Racial discrimination is illegal in many countries. 种族歧视在许多国家都是非法的。

例句：The company's hiring practices showed a clear pattern of gender discrimination. 这家公司的招聘制度显示出明显的性别歧视。


近义词：prejudice, bias, bigotry, unfairness |反义词：fairness, equality, justice


词源:  From Middle French *discrimination (16th century), from Latin discriminatio, from discriminare "to separate, distinguish," from dis- "apart" + crimen "something judged, a charge" (related to cernere "to separate, distinguish").  The original meaning was simply "the act of distinguishing," but the modern meaning, emphasizing unfair treatment, developed later.
