show-off: /ˈʃoʊ ɔːf/ | n. v. |炫耀者；炫耀；卖弄 | A person who boasts about their achievements or possessions; the act of boasting or displaying something in an ostentatious way. |例句：He's such a show-off, always talking about his expensive car. 他真是个爱炫耀的人，总是吹嘘他的豪车。


He gave a show-off of his new skills on the guitar. 他炫耀了他新的吉他技巧。|近义词：boaster, braggart, exhibitionist |反义词：humble, modest, self-effacing |动词

词源:
The word "show-off" is a compound word, combining "show" and "off."  "Show" meaning to display or exhibit, and "off" as an adverb indicating a complete or thorough action, in this case, a complete display.  The combination suggests a deliberate and ostentatious display.  Its usage as a noun and verb developed naturally from its descriptive function.
