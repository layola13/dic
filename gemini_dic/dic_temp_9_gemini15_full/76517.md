galenist:/ˈɡeɪlənɪst/| n. |盖伦主义者，古希腊医学家盖伦的追随者|A Galenist is a follower of Galen, a prominent Greek physician whose medical theories dominated Western medicine for centuries.  Galenists adhered to Galen's humoral theory of medicine, which emphasized the balance of four humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile) in the body.  Their medical practices often involved bloodletting, purging, and other methods aimed at restoring this balance.|The physician, a staunch Galenist, believed bloodletting was the cure for most ailments.|近义词：humoralist (体液说支持者)|反义词：iatrochemist (化学医家) (a term used in contrast to Galenic medicine)
