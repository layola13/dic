photopolarimetry: /ˌfoʊtoʊpoʊləˈrɪmətri/ | n. | 光偏振测量法 | The measurement of the polarization properties of light.  This technique is used to analyze the polarization state of light reflected or emitted by a surface or object, providing information about its physical and chemical properties.  | Example:  Photopolarimetry was used to study the surface composition of the asteroid.  光偏振测量法被用来研究小行星的表面成分。 | 近义词: polarimetric analysis, polarization measurement | 反义词:  (无直接反义词，可考虑与非偏振光测量方法对比)
