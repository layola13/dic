designates:/ˈdɛzɪɡneɪts/| vt.|指定；指派；标示；称作|To indicate, appoint, or name specifically.|The map designates the location of the treasure.|近义词：appoint, assign, specify, identify|反义词：neglect, ignore


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The law designates certain areas as protected wildlife habitats. = 法律将某些地区指定为受保护的野生动物栖息地。
The company designates a specific employee to handle customer complaints. = 公司指定一名特定员工处理客户投诉。


一般过去时:
The committee designated John as the project leader. = 委员会指定约翰为项目负责人。
The teacher designated specific chapters for the upcoming exam. = 老师指定了即将到来的考试的特定章节。


现在完成时:
The government has designated several regions for disaster relief. = 政府已指定几个地区用于救灾。
The organization has designated a significant portion of its budget for research. = 该组织已将其预算的很大一部分指定用于研究。


过去完成时:
They had designated a meeting place before the protest began. = 在抗议开始之前，他们已经指定了一个集会地点。
The manager had designated the tasks before the team arrived. = 经理在团队到达之前已经分配了任务。


将来时:
The council will designate a new park in the city center. = 理事会将在市中心指定一个新的公园。
The company will designate a team to work on the new product. = 公司将指定一个团队来开发新产品。


虚拟语气:
If the board designated a new CEO, the stock price would likely increase. = 如果董事会指定一位新的首席执行官，股价可能会上涨。
I wish the committee had designated a more experienced person for the job. = 我希望委员会为这项工作指定一位更有经验的人。


被动语态:
This area was designated a national park in 1970. = 这个地区在1970年被指定为国家公园。
He was designated as the spokesperson for the group. = 他被指定为该组织的发言人。


疑问句:
What areas have been designated for redevelopment? = 哪些地区被指定用于重建？
Who designated this person to lead the project? = 谁指定这个人领导这个项目？


否定句:
The document does not designate any specific responsibilities. = 该文件没有指定任何具体的责任。
The plan did not designate a clear timeline for completion. = 该计划没有指定明确的完成时间表。
