lippy:/ˈlɪpi/| adj. n.|嘴唇的；口红的；喋喋不休的；鲁莽的|Characterized by or given to speaking boldly or impudently; impertinent; saucy.  Also, relating to or resembling lips or lipstick.|例句：She applied a bright red lippy. 她涂上了鲜艳的红色口红。

例句：Don't be so lippy with your elders!  别对长辈这么无礼！


近义词：bold, sassy, impertinent, cheeky, saucy|反义词：respectful, polite, timid, shy|形容词，名词

词源:
The word "lippy" ultimately derives from "lip," referring to the lips. The adjective sense of being bold or impudent likely arose from the association of lip movements and gestures with assertive or defiant speech.  The noun usage referencing lipstick is a more recent development.
