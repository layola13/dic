referee:/ˌrɛfəˈriː/ | n. |裁判，仲裁人 | A person who supervises a sports game and makes sure that the rules are followed.  Also, a person who settles a dispute. | The referee blew the whistle to signal the end of the game.  裁判吹哨示意比赛结束。|  umpire, arbitrator |  - |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The referee watches the game carefully. = 裁判仔细地观看比赛。
He referees many important matches. = 他执法过许多重要的比赛。


一般过去时:
The referee stopped the fight. = 裁判阻止了这场打斗。
She refereed the debate between the two candidates. = 她担任了两位候选人辩论的仲裁人。


现在完成时:
He has refereed over 100 games this year. = 他今年已经执法了100多场比赛。
They have refereed several disputes successfully. = 他们已经成功地仲裁了几起纠纷。


过去完成时:
The referee had already blown the whistle before the player fell. = 在球员倒下之前，裁判已经吹响了哨子。
The arbitrator had reviewed all the evidence before making a decision. = 仲裁员在做出决定之前已经审查了所有证据。


将来时:
He will referee the final match tomorrow. = 他明天将执法决赛。
She will referee the dispute between the two companies. = 她将仲裁两家公司之间的纠纷。


虚拟语气:
If he were a referee, he would be fair. = 如果他是裁判，他会公平公正。
If she had refereed the case differently, the outcome might have been different. = 如果她对案件的仲裁方式不同，结果可能会有所不同。


被动语态:
The game was refereed by a very experienced official. = 比赛由一位经验丰富的官员执法。
The dispute will be refereed by an independent arbitrator. = 纠纷将由一位独立的仲裁员仲裁。


疑问句:
Who will referee the match? = 谁将执法这场比赛？
Will she be able to referee the heated argument? = 她能够仲裁这场激烈的争论吗？


否定句:
He did not referee the game fairly. = 他没有公平地执法这场比赛。
They didn't want to referee the conflict between the neighbours. = 他们不想仲裁邻居之间的冲突。
