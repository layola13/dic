order:/ˈɔːrdər/ /ˈɔːrdɚ/ | n., vt. | 命令；顺序；订单；订购 |  To give a command or instruction; a sequence or arrangement;  a request for goods or services; to request goods or services. |例句：The general ordered his troops to attack. (将军命令他的部队进攻。)  She placed an order for a new computer. (她订购了一台新电脑。) |近义词：command, instruction, sequence, request |反义词：chaos, disorder

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He orders a coffee every morning. = 他每天早上都点一杯咖啡。
They order their supplies from a local supplier. = 他们从当地供应商那里订购物资。

一般过去时:
She ordered a pizza for dinner. = 她订购了披萨当晚餐。
The king ordered the execution of the traitor. = 国王下令处决叛徒。

现在完成时:
I have ordered the cake for the party. = 我已经订好了派对的蛋糕。
They have ordered new equipment for the lab. = 他们已经为实验室订购了新设备。

过去完成时:
He had ordered the supplies before the project started. = 项目开始前他已经订购了物资。
She had ordered the dress but it was out of stock. = 她已经订购了那件衣服，但是它缺货了。

将来时:
I will order a taxi to the airport. = 我会叫一辆出租车去机场。
We will order the materials next week. = 我们下周会订购材料。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would order the steak. = 如果我是你，我会点牛排。
I wish I had ordered the vegetarian option. = 我真希望我点了素食。

被动语态:
The books were ordered online. = 书籍是在网上订购的。
The new furniture is being ordered. = 新家具正在订购中。

疑问句:
Did you order the food? = 你点餐了吗？
What did they order? = 他们点了什么？

否定句:
I didn't order anything. = 我什么也没点。
He didn't order the right size. = 他没有订购正确的尺寸。


PHRASE->
in order to = 为了
out of order = 坏了；失灵的
in order = 井然有序地
on order = 已订购
to order = 定做的
