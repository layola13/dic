relinquished:/rɪˈlɪŋkwɪʃt/| v. |放弃；交出；让与 | To give up something such as a right, possession, or claim.  |例句：He relinquished his claim to the throne. 他放弃了他对王位的继承权。
She relinquished control of the company to her daughter. 她把公司的控制权交给了女儿。|近义词：abandon, surrender, cede, forfeit |反义词：retain, keep, hold, acquire |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He relinquishes his rights every year. = 他每年都放弃他的权利。
She relinquishes her position to a younger colleague. = 她把自己的职位让给了一位年轻的同事。

一般过去时:
They relinquished their land to the government. = 他们把土地交给了政府。
The soldiers relinquished their weapons. = 士兵们交出了他们的武器。

现在完成时:
He has relinquished his claim to the inheritance. = 他已经放弃了对遗产的索取权。
She has relinquished all hope of finding her lost dog. = 她已经放弃了找到她丢失的狗的全部希望。

过去完成时:
They had relinquished their dreams before they were even twenty. = 他们甚至在二十岁之前就已经放弃了他们的梦想。
The company had relinquished its market share to its competitors. = 这家公司已经将其市场份额拱手让给了竞争对手。

将来时:
He will relinquish his post next month. = 他下个月将辞去职务。
She will relinquish her power peacefully. = 她将和平地放弃权力。

虚拟语气:

I wish he had relinquished his stubborn attitude. = 我真希望他早点放弃他那固执的态度。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
