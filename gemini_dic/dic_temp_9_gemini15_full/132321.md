nondefinitive: /ˌnɒnˈdɛfɪnətɪv/ | adj. | 不确定的；非决定性的；非最终的 | Not conclusive or definitive; not settling a matter finally.  It suggests a lack of certainty or finality. |

例句：

The initial test results were nondefinitive, requiring further investigation. = 初步检测结果并不确定，需要进一步调查。

The doctor's diagnosis was nondefinitive, leaving the patient anxious about the future. = 医生诊断结果不确定，让病人对未来感到焦虑。


近义词：inconclusive, tentative, provisional, preliminary, uncertain, ambiguous

反义词：definitive, conclusive, certain, final


词源:

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of".
* **definitive**: adjective meaning "conclusive," "final," or "authoritative."  It ultimately derives from the Latin word "definire," meaning "to define" or "to limit."


用法说明:


"Nondefinitive" is primarily used as an adjective to describe something that is not conclusive or definitive. It implies that there is a lack of certainty or that more information is needed before a final decision can be made.  It's often used in contexts where a result or conclusion is uncertain or subject to change.  The word is commonly found in scientific, medical, and legal contexts where precision and certainty are paramount.
