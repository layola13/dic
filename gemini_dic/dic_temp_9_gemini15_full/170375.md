provo:/ˈproʊvoʊ/| v.| 挑衅，激怒，煽动| To stimulate or incite to action, especially to anger or violence; to try to make someone angry. |例句：He tried to provo me into a fight. 他试图激怒我，让我跟他打架。|例句：Her speech provoked a storm of protest. 她的讲话激起了抗议的风暴。|近义词：incite, goad, instigate,  |反义词：soothe, appease, pacify


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He provokes his brother constantly. = 他不断地激怒他的弟弟。
The speaker provokes the audience with controversial statements. = 演讲者用有争议的言论激怒听众。


一般过去时:
The news provoked a riot in the streets. = 这则新闻在街上引发了骚乱。
His actions provoked a strong reaction from his colleagues. = 他的行为激起了同事们的强烈反应。


现在完成时:
The government's policies have provoked widespread discontent. = 政府的政策已经引发了广泛的不满。
His behavior has provoked several complaints. = 他的行为已经引起了几起投诉。


过去完成时:
The argument had provoked a bitter feud between the two families. = 那场争论已经引发了两家人之间的激烈争吵。
The teacher had provoked the students into a discussion about the topic. = 老师已经激起学生们对这个话题的讨论。


将来时:
I predict that his actions will provoke a scandal. = 我预言他的行为将会引发丑闻。
The announcement will likely provoke a lot of debate. = 这一宣布很可能会引发大量的争论。


虚拟语气:
If he had provoked a fight, he would have been punished. = 如果他挑起了一场打架，他就会受到惩罚。
If she provoked him, he might have reacted violently.  = 如果她激怒了他，他可能会做出暴力反应。


被动语态:
The crowd was provoked into violence by the police. = 暴徒被警察激怒而变得暴力。
He was provoked by their insults. = 他被他们的侮辱激怒了。


疑问句:
What provoked his outburst? = 什么激怒了他？
What will provoke the next conflict? = 什么会引发下一场冲突？


否定句:
His speech did not provoke any response. = 他的讲话没有引起任何回应。
They tried to provoke him, but he remained calm. = 他们试图激怒他，但他保持冷静。


PHRASE->
provoke a reaction = 激起反应
provoke a debate = 引发辩论
provoke an argument = 挑起争论
provoke a response = 激起回应
provoke laughter = 引起笑声
