cowbird:/ˈkaʊbɜːrd/ | n. |布谷鸟； кукушка | A bird of the family Icteridae, many species of which lay their eggs in the nests of other birds. Cowbirds are brood parasites, meaning they rely on other birds to raise their young.  They are known for their distinctive songs and often exhibit polygynous mating systems. | Example: The cowbird laid its egg in the robin's nest.  布谷鸟把蛋下在知更鸟的巢里。 The female cowbird is smaller than the male. 母布谷鸟比公布谷鸟小。| 近义词：brood parasite,  brown-headed cowbird | 反义词： (no direct antonym, but birds that raise their own young could be considered an indirect opposite)
