monists:/ˈmɒnɪsts/ | n. |一元论者，一元论者|A monist is someone who believes that reality is fundamentally one thing.  This can manifest in various ways, such as believing that everything is ultimately material (physicalism), or that everything is ultimately mental (idealism), or that everything is ultimately a single spiritual substance.  The core idea is a rejection of dualism or pluralism. | Example:  Many philosophers are monists, believing that mind and body are not separate entities. 许多哲学家是一元论者，他们认为身心并非独立的实体。 |近义词：unitarist, reductionist |反义词：dualist, pluralist |名词
