lieges:/ˈliːdʒɪz/| n.|臣民，子民|People who are subjects of a sovereign; those who owe allegiance to a ruler.|例句：The king addressed his lieges. 国王向他的子民发表讲话。|近义词：subjects, people, vassals|反义词：rulers, sovereigns


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The king protects his lieges. = 国王保护他的子民。
His lieges obey the law. = 他的子民遵守法律。

一般过去时:
The queen ruled her lieges wisely. = 女王明智地统治她的子民。
The lieges supported their leader. = 子民们支持他们的领导人。

现在完成时:
The king has always cherished his lieges. = 国王一直珍视他的子民。
The lieges have shown their loyalty. = 子民们表现出了他们的忠诚。

过去完成时:
The lieges had already sworn allegiance before the coronation. = 在加冕典礼之前，子民们已经宣誓效忠。
The king had ruled his lieges for many years. = 国王统治他的子民多年了。

将来时:
The prince will one day rule his lieges. = 王子有一天将统治他的子民。
The lieges will remain loyal to the crown. = 子民们将继续忠于王室。

虚拟语气:
If the king were wise, he would better care for his lieges. = 如果国王英明，他会更好地照顾他的子民。
I wish the lieges were more prosperous. = 我希望子民们更加富裕。

被动语态:
The lieges were protected by the army. = 子民们受到军队的保护。
The lieges are ruled justly by the monarch. = 子民们受到君主的公正统治。

疑问句:
Who are the lieges of this kingdom? = 这个王国的子民是谁？
Do the lieges support the new laws? = 子民们支持新的法律吗？

否定句:
The king did not neglect his lieges. = 国王没有忽视他的子民。
The lieges were not disloyal. = 子民们没有不忠。

词源:
From Old French liege, from Frankish *leud, from Proto-Germanic *leudą, meaning "people".  The word evolved to denote those bound by loyalty to a lord.
