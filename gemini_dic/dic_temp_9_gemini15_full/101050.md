inveiglers:/ɪnˈveɪɡlərz/| n. | 诱骗者，欺诈者 |Those who use deception or flattery to persuade someone to do something.  People who subtly coax or entice others into doing something, often for their own benefit. |The inveiglers promised him riches and glory to get him to sign the contract.  诱骗者许诺给他财富和荣耀以让他签署合同。


The police caught the inveiglers red-handed. 警方当场抓获了这些诱骗者。|近义词：deceivers, tricksters, swindlers, con artists |反义词：helpers, benefactors
