conceptionalist: /kənˌsɛpʃənəlɪst/ | n. | 概念主义者 | A person who emphasizes concepts or ideas over concrete realities.  Someone who prioritizes abstract thought and theoretical frameworks in their approach to art, philosophy, or other fields.  They might focus on the underlying meaning or structure of a work rather than its surface features. | Example:  The artist is considered a conceptionalist, preferring conceptual art that challenges viewers to engage with ideas rather than aesthetics.  这位艺术家被认为是一位概念主义者，更喜欢挑战观众参与思想而不是审美的概念艺术。|近义词：conceptualist, idealist | 反义词：empiricist, realist |
