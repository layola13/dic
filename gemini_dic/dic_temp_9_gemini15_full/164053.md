predisclosure: /ˌpriːdɪˈskloʊʒər/ | n. | 预先披露；提前公开 | The act of disclosing information before it is officially released or expected.  It often refers to the release of confidential or sensitive information before its authorized release. |例句：The predisclosure of the merger plans caused significant market fluctuations. 预先披露并购计划导致市场大幅波动。

例句：The company faced legal repercussions due to the predisclosure of its financial results.  公司因提前公开财务业绩而面临法律后果。


近义词：pre-release, advance disclosure, leak, tip-off

反义词：concealment, secrecy, suppression


词源:  The word is a combination of "pre-" (meaning before) and "disclosure" (the act of revealing something).  "Disclosure" itself originates from the Old French "desclore," meaning "to uncover" or "to reveal," ultimately derived from the Latin "discludere," composed of "dis-" (apart) and "claudere" (to close).
