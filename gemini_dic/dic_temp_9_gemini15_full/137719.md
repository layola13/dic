nonvacuous: /nɒnˈvækjʊəs/ | adj. |非空洞的；有意义的；重要的 | Not empty or meaningless; significant.  A nonvacuous statement is one that is not trivially true or false, and carries substantive meaning.  It implies a content or substance beyond mere form. |

例句：

His arguments, while lengthy, were ultimately nonvacuous and provided valuable insights.  他的论点虽然冗长，但最终并非空洞，并提供了宝贵的见解。

The professor demanded nonvacuous answers to his questions, rejecting simple "yes" or "no" responses. 教授要求对他的问题给出有意义的答案，拒绝简单的“是”或“否”回答。


近义词：meaningful, significant, substantial, pertinent, relevant

反义词：empty, meaningless, trivial, insubstantial, irrelevant


词源:

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not"
* **vacuous**:  from Latin *vacuus* meaning "empty," related to *vacare* "to be empty."
