formalism:/ˈfɔːrməlɪzəm/ | n. |形式主义；形式论；程式化 |The strict adherence to a set of forms, conventions, or rules, often at the expense of spontaneity or originality.  It can refer to various fields like art, literature, music, and even philosophy.  | Example:  The artist's work was criticized for its excessive formalism, lacking emotional depth.  艺术家作品因过度形式化而受到批评，缺乏情感深度。  |近义词：conventionalism, traditionalism, scholasticism |反义词：naturalism, expressionism, realism |
