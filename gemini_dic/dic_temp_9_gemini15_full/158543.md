plesiohedron:/ˌpliːziˈoʊhiːdrən/ | n. |拟等轴晶体 |A plesiohedron is a crystal form that is nearly isometric (having equal dimensions in three directions), but not perfectly so.  It deviates slightly from perfect cubic symmetry.  The term is often used in mineralogy and crystallography to describe crystals that appear cubic at first glance but exhibit subtle variations in their axial lengths or angles upon closer examination. |例句：The mineral sample showed a plesiohedral crystal habit, appearing cubic but with slight distortions upon precise measurement.  该矿物样本呈现拟等轴晶体习性，乍一看是立方体，但在精确测量后显示出轻微的变形。
