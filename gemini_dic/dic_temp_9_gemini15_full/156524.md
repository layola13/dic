piciformes:/ˈpɪsɪfɔːrmiz/| n. |鴷形目|The order Piciformes is a group of birds that includes woodpeckers, toucans, barbets, and honeyguides.  These birds are characterized by their zygodactyl feet (two toes pointing forward and two pointing backward), which are well-suited for climbing trees. Many species have strong beaks adapted for drilling wood or probing for insects.  The order is widely distributed across the globe, with the greatest diversity found in tropical regions. |例句：The family Ramphastidae, which includes toucans, belongs to the order Piciformes.  鴷形目包括巨嘴鸟科，其中包括巨嘴鸟。|近义词：None directly equivalent, but other bird orders could be mentioned (e.g., Passeriformes,  Columbiformes) depending on the context.|反义词：None|名词
