lecithins:/ˈlɛsɪθɪnz/| n.|卵磷脂|A group of naturally occurring phosphatidylcholine compounds found in many plant and animal tissues. Lecithins are a type of phospholipid and are essential components of cell membranes. They are also used as emulsifiers in food and other products.  |Example: Soy lecithin is commonly used as an emulsifier in food processing.  大豆卵磷脂常用作食品加工中的乳化剂。|phosphatidylcholines, phospholipids|  |名词
