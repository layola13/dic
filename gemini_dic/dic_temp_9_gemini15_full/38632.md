concubine:/ˈkɒŋkjuːbaɪn/|/ˈkɑːŋkjuːbaɪn/| n. |妾，情妇|A concubine is a woman who lives with a man but is not his wife, often in a socially recognized or legally sanctioned relationship, particularly in historical contexts.  The term often implies a lower status than a wife and may involve a degree of subservience.  The precise meaning and implications vary greatly depending on cultural and historical context.|例句：In some ancient cultures, wealthy men often had multiple wives and concubines.  在一些古代文化中，富有的男人通常有多个妻子和妾室。

In many historical societies, concubines held a different social and legal standing than wives. 在许多历史社会中，妾的地位与妻子的社会地位和法律地位不同。|近义词：mistress, paramour|反义词：wife
