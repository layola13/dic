rehearsal:/rɪˈhɜːrsəl/ | n. | 排练；预演；练习 | A rehearsal is a practice session for a performance, such as a play, concert, or speech.  It allows performers to refine their skills and coordination. |  The actors had a rehearsal for the play tonight.  演员们今晚排练了这部戏。
The orchestra held a rehearsal before the concert.  乐队在音乐会前进行了排练。| practice, run-through, dry run | performance, premiere | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The band rehearses every Tuesday. = 乐队每周二进行排练。
She rehearses her speech before every presentation. = 她在每次演讲前都会排练自己的发言。

一般过去时:
They rehearsed the scene several times. = 他们排练了那个场景好几次。
We rehearsed the song until we got it right. = 我们反复排练这首歌，直到把它弄对为止。

现在完成时:
The actors have rehearsed for weeks. = 演员们已经排练了好几个星期了。
She has rehearsed her lines many times. = 她已经排练过很多次台词了。

过去完成时:
They had rehearsed the dance routine before the performance. = 他们在演出之前已经排练过舞蹈了。
The band had rehearsed the entire album before the tour. = 乐队在巡演之前已经排练过整张专辑了。

将来时:
We will rehearse the play tomorrow. = 我们明天将排练这出戏。
They will rehearse the music for the ceremony next week. = 他们下周将为仪式排练音乐。


虚拟语气:
If we had more time, we would rehearse more. = 如果我们有更多时间，我们会排练更多。
I wish I had rehearsed more for the audition. = 我希望我为试镜排练得更多。

被动语态:
The play was rehearsed extensively. = 这出戏进行了大量的排练。
The music was rehearsed by the orchestra for many hours. = 这段音乐由乐队排练了数小时。


疑问句:
Have they rehearsed yet? = 他们排练了吗？
When will we rehearse next? = 我们下次什么时候排练？


否定句:
They didn't rehearse the song properly. = 他们没有好好排练那首歌。
We haven't rehearsed enough for the concert. = 我们为音乐会排练得还不够。
