词源:

The word "centrepiece" is a compound word formed from "centre" (or "center" in American English) and "piece."  "Centre" comes from the Old French word "centre," ultimately deriving from the Latin word "centrum," meaning "center." "Piece" comes from the Old French word "piece," also ultimately deriving from the Latin word "pěcĭus," meaning a "patch" or "portion."  Therefore, "centrepiece" literally means "a central piece."
