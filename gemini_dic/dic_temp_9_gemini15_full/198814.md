side-track: /ˈsaɪdtræːk/ | vt. |使偏离正轨；使分心；岔开话题 | To divert or distract (someone or something) from a main course or purpose.  |例句：The unexpected phone call side-tracked him from his work.  意外的电话使他无法专心工作。

例句：Don't let gossip side-track you from your goals. 不要让闲话让你偏离目标。


近义词：divert, distract, deflect, derail | 反义词：focus, concentrate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He side-tracks the conversation whenever it gets too serious. 他只要谈话变得严肃就岔开话题。
The project is side-tracked by unexpected technical issues.  这个项目因为意料之外的技术问题而偏离了正轨。


一般过去时:
The meeting was side-tracked by a lengthy discussion about budgets. 会议由于冗长的预算讨论而偏离了主题。
She side-tracked him with a story about her cat. 她用关于她猫的故事把他注意力转移开了。


现在完成时:
They have side-tracked the investigation with irrelevant details. 他们用无关紧要的细节把调查搞偏了方向。
The government has been side-tracked by the ongoing political crisis.  政府一直被持续的政治危机牵制住了。


过去完成时:
The team had side-tracked their efforts by focusing on minor details.  团队由于专注于次要细节而偏离了目标。
He had side-tracked himself from his studies by playing too many video games. 他因为玩太多电子游戏而耽误了学业。


将来时:
I fear that the new regulations will side-track the company's growth. 我担心新的规章制度会阻碍公司的发展。
The unexpected snowstorm will likely side-track our travel plans.  突如其来的暴风雪可能会打乱我们的旅行计划。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't been side-tracked, he would have finished the report on time. 如果他没有分心，他就能按时完成报告。
If we hadn't been side-tracked by the detour, we would have arrived earlier. 如果我们没有因为绕路而耽误，我们就能早点到达。


被动语态:
The project was side-tracked by a lack of funding.  该项目由于资金不足而搁浅了。
His attention was side-tracked by the loud music.  他的注意力被嘈杂的音乐分散了。


疑问句:
Was the conversation side-tracked by the interruption?  谈话被打断后是否偏离了主题？
Will the new policy side-track the ongoing negotiations? 新政策会阻碍正在进行的谈判吗？


否定句:
He didn't let the distractions side-track him from his goal. 他没有让干扰分散他的注意力，偏离他的目标。
The team didn't allow themselves to be side-tracked by minor setbacks.  团队没有让自己被小的挫折所阻碍。

词源:
The word "side-track" is a compound word, combining "side" and "track".  The original meaning referred to a railway siding, a short length of track branching off from the main line.  Figuratively, it came to mean diverting something from its main course.
