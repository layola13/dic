lady's:/ˈleɪdiz/ | n. | 女士的；夫人的 |  The word "lady's" is a possessive form of the noun "lady," indicating belonging or association with a woman of refined manners. It's often used as an adjective modifying another noun.  | Example:  That's a lady's handbag.  那是女士的手提包。   |近义词:woman's, female's | 反义词:  None specifically, but contextually could be contrasted with "gentleman's," "man's" | 词性:n.
