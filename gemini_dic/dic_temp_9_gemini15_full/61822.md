empirically:/ɪmˈpɪrɪkli/| adv. |根据经验地；实证地 |Based on observation or experiment; verifiable or provable by means of observation or experiment.  |The hypothesis was empirically tested and proven correct.  该假设经过实验证实是正确的。|experimentally, observationally |theoretically, hypothetically |副词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
We empirically test new products. = 我们根据经验测试新产品。
Scientists empirically study the effects of climate change. = 科学家们根据经验研究气候变化的影响。


一般过去时:
Researchers empirically investigated the phenomenon. = 研究人员根据经验调查了这一现象。
They empirically determined the best course of action. = 他们根据经验确定了最佳行动方案。


现在完成时:
Scientists have empirically demonstrated the link between smoking and cancer. = 科学家们已经通过实验证明了吸烟与癌症之间的联系。
Researchers have empirically found a correlation between diet and health. = 研究人员已经通过实验证明了饮食与健康之间的相关性。


过去完成时:
They had empirically established the effectiveness of the treatment before publishing the results. = 他们在发表结果之前已经通过实验证明了治疗的有效性。
The scientists had empirically observed the behavior of the particles before formulating the theory. = 科学家们在制定理论之前已经通过实验证明了粒子的行为。


将来时:
We will empirically evaluate the results of the study. = 我们将根据经验评估研究结果。
They will empirically verify the findings of the experiment. = 他们将通过实验证实实验结果。


虚拟语气:
If we had more data, we could empirically prove the theory. = 如果我们有更多的数据，我们就可以用实验证明这个理论。
If they were to conduct further research, they might empirically support their hypothesis. = 如果他们进行进一步的研究，他们可能会通过实验证明他们的假设。


被动语态:
The effects of the drug were empirically investigated. = 药物的影响已通过实验进行了调查。
The results of the study were empirically confirmed. = 研究结果已通过实验证实。


疑问句:
Can we empirically prove this statement? = 我们能用实验证明这个说法吗？
How can we empirically measure these effects? = 我们如何用实验证量这些影响？


否定句:
This theory cannot be empirically verified. = 这个理论无法通过实验证实。
The hypothesis was not empirically supported by the data. = 数据不支持该假设。


PHRASE->
empirically supported = 经实证支持
empirically derived = 经经验得来
empirically validated = 经实验证实
empirically tested = 经实验测试
empirically determined = 经经验确定


词源:
From Middle English empirike, borrowed from Late Latin empiricus, from Greek empeirikós, from empeiros "experienced," from en "in" + peira "trial, experience."  最终源自希腊语，与“经验”有关。
