mass-markets:/ˈmæs ˈmɑːrkɪts/| n.|大众市场|The term "mass markets" refers to the large-scale consumer markets targeting a broad audience with standardized products or services.  These markets are characterized by high volume sales and relatively low profit margins per unit.  The focus is on reaching a large number of people rather than catering to specific niche interests.| Example:  The company's new product is designed for mass markets, aiming for high sales volume.|近义词：consumer markets, popular markets, retail markets|反义词：niche markets, specialized markets
