mastersinger: /ˈmɑːstərsɪŋər/ | n. |大师歌手，行会歌手 | A mastersinger is a member of a medieval German singing guild that followed strict rules of composition and performance.  They were skilled in composing and singing songs according to specific rules and traditions.  These guilds were important centers of musical and social life in many German cities during the late Middle Ages and Renaissance. | Example:  The mastersingers of Nuremberg were renowned for their musical skill and adherence to tradition.  纽伦堡的大师歌手以其精湛的技艺和对传统的坚持而闻名。 |近义词:  guild singer, song composer (in this specific historical context) | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but one could say) amateur singer, untrained singer
