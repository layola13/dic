monoglyceride:/ˈmɒnəʊˈglɪsəraɪd/|/ˈmɑːnəʊˈɡlɪsəraɪd/| n. |单甘油酯|A monoglyceride, also known as a monoacylglycerol, is a type of lipid formed from the esterification of one molecule of glycerol with one molecule of fatty acid.  They are important components of many fats and oils and are used in food processing as emulsifiers.|例句：Monoglycerides are commonly used as emulsifiers in food products to prevent separation of oil and water. 单甘油酯常被用作食品中的乳化剂，以防止油水分离。|例句：The study investigated the effect of different monoglycerides on the texture of baked goods.  这项研究调查了不同单甘油酯对烘焙食品质地的影响。|近义词：monoacylglycerol|反义词：triglyceride
