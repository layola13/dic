deescalate: /ˌdiːˈɛskəleɪt/ | vt. |使缓和；使降级；使平静下来 | To reduce the intensity or seriousness of (something, typically a conflict or difficult situation).  |例句：The police successfully de-escalated the tense situation.  警察成功地缓和了紧张的局势。

例句：Negotiators worked hard to de-escalate the conflict between the two countries.  谈判代表努力缓和两国之间的冲突。


近义词：reduce, lessen, alleviate, mitigate, calm, soothe

反义词：escalate, intensify, aggravate, exacerbate

词源:  The word "deescalate" is a back-formation from "escalation."  "Escalation" entered English in the mid-20th century, with roots in the Italian word "scala" (ladder, stairs).  The prefix "de-" indicates the reversal or removal of the action.
