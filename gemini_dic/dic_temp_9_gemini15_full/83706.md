hagiographies:/ˌhæɡiˈɒɡrəfiz/| n. |圣徒传记；颂扬性传记|Biographies that idealize or exaggerate the virtues of their subjects, often saints or other figures of religious importance.  They tend to focus on positive aspects and downplay or omit negative ones. |Example: Many hagiographies of historical figures present a sanitized and unrealistic view of their lives.|近义词： panegyrics, eulogies, biographies (positive slant)|反义词： exposés, critiques, debunking biographies
