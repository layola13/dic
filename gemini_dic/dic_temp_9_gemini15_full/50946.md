designated:/ˈdezɪɡneɪtɪd/ | adj. | 指定的；选定的 |  Appointed or assigned to a specific purpose, task, or role.  It implies a formal or official selection. | 例句：This area is designated for parking. = 这个区域被指定为停车场。  This is the designated meeting place. = 这是指定的会面地点。 | 近义词：specified, appointed, allocated, assigned | 反义词：undesignated, unspecified


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government designates this area a national park. = 政府将这个地区指定为国家公园。
They designate him as the team leader. = 他们指定他为团队领导。

一般过去时:
The committee designated a spokesperson. = 委员会指定了一位发言人。
The school designated a new principal. = 学校指定了一位新的校长。


现在完成时:
The company has designated a new marketing strategy. = 公司已经指定了一个新的营销策略。
They have designated a specific budget for the project. = 他们已经为该项目指定了一笔具体的预算。


过去完成时:
The court had designated the property as evidence. = 法院已将该房产指定为证据。
Before the game, they had designated a captain. = 在比赛之前，他们已经指定了一位队长。


将来时:
The council will designate a new building site. = 理事会将指定一个新的建筑工地。
They will designate a specific time for the meeting. = 他们将为会议指定一个具体的时间。


虚拟语气:
If the council designated a new park, we would celebrate. = 如果理事会指定一个新的公园，我们将庆祝。
I wish they had designated a clearer plan. = 我希望他们制定一个更清晰的计划。


被动语态:
This area was designated a wildlife sanctuary. = 这个地区被指定为野生动物保护区。
He was designated as the project manager. = 他被指定为项目经理。


疑问句:
Has the date for the event been designated? = 活动的日期是否已确定？
Who was designated to lead the team? = 谁被指定领导这个团队？


否定句:
No area has been designated for camping. = 没有区域被指定为露营区。
He was not designated to make that decision. = 他没有被授权做出那个决定。
