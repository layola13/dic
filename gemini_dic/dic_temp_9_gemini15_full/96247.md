indicative:/ɪnˈdɪkətɪv/ | adj. | 陈述的；指示的；象征的 | Relating to or conveying a statement of fact or opinion.  It is the grammatical mood used for making statements of fact or expressing opinions.  | Example:  The indicative mood is used in most everyday sentences.  陈述语气用于大多数日常句子中。 |近义词：declarative, assertive | 反义词：subjunctive, imperative


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He walks to school every day. = 他每天步行上学。
She speaks fluent French. = 她说法语流利。


一般过去时:
They went to the cinema last night. = 他们昨晚去看电影了。
The dog barked loudly. = 狗大声叫。


现在完成时:
I have finished my work. = 我已经完成了我的工作。
She has lived in London for five years. = 她已经在伦敦住了五年了。


过去完成时:
They had eaten dinner before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前他们已经吃过晚饭了。
He had already left when I called. = 我打电话时他已经离开了。


将来时:
We will go to the beach tomorrow. = 我们明天要去海滩。
She will be happy to see you. = 她会很高兴见到你。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would study harder. = 如果我是你，我会更加努力学习。（注意虚拟语气中的were）
I wish I had more time. = 我希望我有更多的时间。


被动语态:
The car was stolen last night. = 汽车昨晚被偷了。
The letter is being written now. = 信现在正在被写。


疑问句:
Is it raining? = 天在下雨吗？
What is your name? = 你叫什么名字？


否定句:
I don't like coffee. = 我不喜欢咖啡。
He wasn't at home yesterday. = 他昨天不在家。


PHRASE->
indicative mood = 陈述语气
indicative of = 表明…；象征…
indicative sign = 预兆；征兆
highly indicative = 非常具有指示意义的
indicative sentence = 陈述句
