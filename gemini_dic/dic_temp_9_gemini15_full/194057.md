self-harming: /ˌsɛlfˈhɑːrmɪŋ/ | v. | 自残 | The act of intentionally injuring oneself, typically as a way of coping with emotional distress.  It can involve a range of behaviors, from cutting or burning to scratching or hitting.  Self-harming is often a symptom of underlying mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety, or post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).|  He admitted to self-harming after a particularly stressful period at work. 他承认在工作压力特别大的时期有过自残行为。

She engages in self-harming behaviors as a way to manage overwhelming emotions. 她通过自残行为来应对难以承受的情绪。


近义词: self-injury, self-mutilation, self-abuse | 反义词: self-care, self-preservation
