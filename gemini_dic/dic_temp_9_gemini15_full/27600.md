canceled: /ˈkænsld/ | v. |取消，作废 | To decide that a planned event will not take place.  |例句：The concert was canceled due to the storm.  这场音乐会因暴风雨而取消。|例句：They canceled their trip because of the pandemic.  他们因为疫情取消了旅行。|近义词：called off, annulled, scrapped |反义词：confirmed, scheduled


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They cancel the meeting every Friday. = 他们每个星期五都取消会议。
He cancels his appointments frequently. = 他经常取消约会。


一般过去时:
She canceled her flight yesterday. = 她昨天取消了她的航班。
They canceled the project last week. = 他们上周取消了那个项目。


现在完成时:
We have canceled our subscription. = 我们已经取消了我们的订阅。
They have canceled all future events. = 他们已经取消了所有未来的活动。


过去完成时:
He had canceled the reservation before we arrived. =在我们到达之前他已经取消了预订。
She had canceled the order before realizing the mistake. = 她意识到错误之前已经取消了订单。


将来时:
They will cancel the game if it rains. = 如果下雨，他们将取消比赛。
We will cancel the trip if the weather is bad. = 如果天气不好，我们将取消旅行。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would cancel the contract. = 如果我是你，我会取消合同。
I wish I had canceled the appointment earlier. = 我真希望我早点取消了预约。


被动语态:
The meeting was canceled due to unforeseen circumstances. = 会议由于不可预见的情况而被取消。
The flight was canceled because of bad weather. = 由于天气恶劣，航班被取消。


疑问句:
Was the meeting canceled? = 会议取消了吗？
Have they canceled the order yet? = 他们已经取消订单了吗？


否定句:
They did not cancel the trip. = 他们没有取消旅行。
The show was not canceled. = 演出没有取消。
