/aɪmɪŋ/| v. |正在做…；打算…|The word "im'ing" is a contraction of "I am" followed by the present participle of a verb, forming a present continuous tense.  It's used in informal writing and speech to express an ongoing action or a planned future action.  The specific meaning depends entirely on the verb that follows.|I'm going to the store. = 我要去商店。|I'm eating lunch. = 我正在吃午饭。|going, doing, planning |stopping, finishing |动词

I'm reading a book. = 我正在看书。
I'm working on a project. = 我正在做一个项目。

虚拟语气:
If I were going to the party, I'd be excited. (Note: This uses "were going," not "im'ing," as "im'ing" is informal.) = 如果我去参加聚会，我会很兴奋。
I wish I were swimming right now. (Note: This uses "were swimming," not "im'ing," as "im'ing" is informal.) = 我真希望我现在正在游泳。


被动语态:
"Im'ing" cannot be used in the passive voice.  The passive voice requires a form of "to be" + past participle, which is incompatible with the contraction "im'ing."
