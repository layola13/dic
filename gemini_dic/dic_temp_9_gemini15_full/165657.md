premodifications:/ˌpriːˌmɒdɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/| n. |前置修饰语|A premodification is a word or phrase that comes before the head noun in a noun phrase to modify or describe it.  It provides additional information about the noun, often specifying its attributes, quantity, or type.  Premodifications can include adjectives, nouns, participles, and prepositional phrases.|Example: The *tall, dark, and handsome* stranger entered the room. (Here, "tall, dark, and handsome" are premodifications describing the stranger.)|近义词：attributive modifiers, descriptive phrases|反义词：postmodifications
