leeching:/ˈliːtʃɪŋ/| v.| 渗出；吸取；榨取 | The process of extracting or removing something gradually or insidiously.  It can refer to the extraction of a substance from a material, the draining of something away, or the parasitic draining of resources or energy. |例句：The heavy rain caused leeching of nutrients from the soil. 大雨导致土壤养分流失。

例句：The company was accused of leeching profits from its workers. 这家公司被指控榨取工人的利润。


近义词： draining, extracting, depleting, siphoning

反义词： replenishing, restoring, enriching

词源:
The word "leeching" comes from the noun "leech," which refers to a bloodsucking worm.  The verb form developed from the leech's action of sucking blood, leading to the broader meaning of extracting or draining something gradually.  The ultimate origin is Proto-Germanic *lūxō.
