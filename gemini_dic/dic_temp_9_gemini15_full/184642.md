restricting: /rɪˈstrɪktɪŋ/ | v. adj. | 限制，约束；受限制的 | Limiting or controlling access to something.  |  The restricting regulations prevented the company from expanding.  这些限制性规定阻止了公司扩张。  |近义词：limiting, confining, restraining | 反义词：unrestricting, liberating, expanding | 动词（现在分词）


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government is restricting access to the site. = 政府正在限制进入该网站。
They are restricting the number of participants. = 他们正在限制参与者的数量。


一般过去时:
The school restricted the use of cell phones. = 学校限制了手机的使用。
They restricted access to the building after hours. = 他们限制了在非工作时间进入大楼。


现在完成时:
They have restricted the flow of information. = 他们已经限制了信息的流动。
The new rules have restricted our options. = 新规定限制了我们的选择。


过去完成时:
They had restricted travel before the pandemic. = 大流行之前，他们已经限制了旅行。
The company had restricted spending in previous years. = 公司在之前的几年里已经限制了开支。


将来时:
The authorities will restrict movement in the affected area. = 当局将限制受灾地区的通行。
They will restrict access to the data until further notice. = 他们将限制访问数据，直到另行通知。


虚拟语气:
If they restricted the budget, the project would fail. = 如果他们限制预算，项目就会失败。
I wish they restricted the noise pollution. = 我希望他们能限制噪音污染。


被动语态:
Access to the server was restricted. = 服务器的访问权限被限制了。
Spending will be restricted by the board of directors. = 开支将受到董事会的限制。


疑问句:
Are they restricting the number of attendees? = 他们是否限制了与会者的数量？
Is the government restricting freedom of speech? = 政府是否限制了言论自由？


否定句:
They did not restrict the use of the equipment. = 他们没有限制设备的使用。
The company does not restrict its employees' internet access. = 公司没有限制员工的互联网访问。
