fumbling:/ˈfʌmblɪŋ/| adj. v.|笨拙的；摸索；|笨拙地做事；摸索；|He made a fumbling attempt to open the door. 他笨拙地试图打开门。|clumsy, awkward, bungling|skillful, deft, adroit|形容词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fumbles with the key. = 他笨拙地摆弄着钥匙。
She fumbles her words. = 她说话结结巴巴的。


一般过去时:
He fumbled in his pocket for his keys. = 他在口袋里摸索钥匙。
She fumbled the ball and dropped it. = 她接球时手忙脚乱，把球掉了。


现在完成时:
He has fumbled his way through the presentation. = 他磕磕绊绊地完成了演示。
She has fumbled several times during the game. = 比赛中她好几次都手忙脚乱。


过去完成时:
He had fumbled the crucial pass, costing his team the game. = 他错失了关键的传球，导致球队输掉了比赛。
She had fumbled with the buttons for ages before she managed to fasten her coat. = 她费了好大劲才把外套扣上。


将来时:
He will fumble the speech if he doesn't practice. = 如果他不练习，他将会演讲结巴。
She will probably fumble the next catch. = 她下次接球很可能会手忙脚乱。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't fumbled the ball, they would have won the game. = 如果他没有失误，他们就赢了比赛。
I wish I hadn't fumbled my lines during the performance. = 我真希望我在表演中没有说错台词。


被动语态:
The answer was fumbled by the contestant. = 选手支支吾吾地回答了问题。(被动语态中fumble用得较少)
The ball was fumbled and dropped. = 球被弄掉了。


疑问句:
Did he fumble the catch? = 他接球失误了吗？
Is she always fumbling with her bag? = 她总是笨手笨脚地拿着她的包吗？


否定句:
He didn't fumble the important decision. = 他没有在重要的决定上犯错。
She didn't fumble her lines during the play. = 她在剧中没有说错台词。


PHRASE->
fumble around = 瞎摸索
fumble for = 摸索寻找
fumble through = 磕磕绊绊地完成
fumble with = 笨拙地摆弄
fumble the ball = 接球失误
