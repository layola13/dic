skylarker:/ˈskaɪˌlɑːrkər/| n. |云雀；（尤指）兴高采烈的人 | A skylarker is a person who is full of cheer and high spirits, often associated with the carefree joy of a skylark's song.  It can also refer to a bird, specifically the skylark. |例句：The children, full of youthful energy, were skylarkers all day long. 孩子们精力充沛，一整天都兴高采烈。  The skylarker soared high above the fields, its song filling the air. 云雀在田野上空高飞，歌声回荡在空中。|近义词： merrymaker, reveller, enthusiast |反义词：pessimist, cynic
