self-employed:/ˌsɛlf ɪmˈplɔɪd/| adj.| 自营的，自由职业的 | Someone who is self-employed works for themselves, rather than for an employer. They are their own boss and responsible for their own income.  |  He's self-employed as a freelance writer. 他是一名自由撰稿人，自己当老板。She is self-employed and runs her own bakery.  她是自由职业者，经营着自己的面包店。| employed, freelance, independent | employed, salaried


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is self-employed and works from home. = 他是自由职业者，在家工作。
She is self-employed and sets her own hours. = 她是自由职业者，自己安排工作时间。


一般过去时:
He was self-employed for many years before retiring. = 他退休前做了多年的自由职业者。
She was self-employed and ran a successful consulting business. = 她曾是自由职业者，经营着一家成功的咨询公司。


现在完成时:
He has been self-employed since graduating from college. = 他从大学毕业后就一直是自由职业者。
She has been self-employed for over a decade. = 她做自由职业者已经超过十年了。


过去完成时:
He had been self-employed for five years when he decided to take a salaried position. = 他做了五年自由职业者后决定找一份有固定薪水的职位。
She had been self-employed before starting her current job. = 在开始现在这份工作之前，她做过自由职业者。


将来时:
He will be self-employed even after his current project is completed. = 即使他目前的项目完成了，他将来也将是自由职业者。
She will be self-employed and focus on her own business. = 她将成为自由职业者，专注于自己的事业。


虚拟语气:
If I were self-employed, I would have more flexibility. = 如果我是自由职业者，我会更有时间安排上的灵活性。
I wish I were self-employed so I could set my own hours. = 我希望我是自由职业者，这样我就可以自己安排工作时间了。


被动语态:
(This adjective doesn't typically use passive voice.  The passive voice would focus on the action of *being made* self-employed, which is less common.)


疑问句:
Are you self-employed? = 你是自由职业者吗？
Is she self-employed or does she work for a company? = 她是自由职业者还是在公司上班？


否定句:
He is not self-employed; he works for a large corporation. = 他不是自由职业者；他在一家大公司工作。
She is not self-employed; she has a full-time job. = 她不是自由职业者；她有一份全职工作。


PHRASE->
be self-employed = 自己当老板
become self-employed = 开始自己创业
remain self-employed = 保持自由职业者身份
highly self-employed =  高度自由职业的 (emphasizes a high degree of independence)
newly self-employed = 新近开始自己创业的
