hypoxaemia:/ˌhaɪpɒkˈsiːmiə/| n. |低氧血症 |An abnormally low level of oxygen in the blood.  This condition can be caused by various factors, including respiratory problems, heart problems, and high altitude.  It often leads to reduced oxygen supply to the body's tissues and organs. |Example:  The patient presented with symptoms of hypoxaemia, including shortness of breath and cyanosis.  患者出现低氧血症症状，包括呼吸急促和发绀。|近义词：hypoxemia, low blood oxygen |反义词：hyperoxemia, high blood oxygen|名词
