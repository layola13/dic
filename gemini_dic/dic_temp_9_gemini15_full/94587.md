impracticably:/ɪmˈpræktɪkəbli/ | adv. | 不切实际地；难以实行地 |  In a way that is impossible or extremely difficult to do or put into practice.  | The plan was impractically ambitious and ultimately failed.  这个计划野心勃勃，不切实际，最终失败了。| practically, feasibly | practically, feasibly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
It is impractically difficult to finish the project within a week. = 在一周内完成这个项目是不切实际的困难。
The task is impractically complex for a single person. = 这项任务对一个人来说过于复杂，难以完成。


一般过去时:
They found the solution impractically complicated to implement. = 他们发现这个解决方案过于复杂，难以实施。
The schedule was impractically tight, leading to many problems. = 时间安排过于紧凑，导致许多问题。


现在完成时:
We have found the suggested approach impractically expensive. = 我们发现建议的方法过于昂贵。
The company has proven that the original plan was impractically risky. = 公司证明了最初的计划是不切实际的冒险。


过去完成时:
They had already realized the proposal was impractically detailed. = 他们已经意识到这个提案过于详细。
The team had discovered that the method was impractically time-consuming. = 团队发现这种方法过于耗时。


将来时:
The project will be impractically challenging to complete on time. = 这个项目要在规定时间内完成将极具挑战性。

虚拟语气:
If the budget were larger, it wouldn't be impractically difficult to proceed. = 如果预算更大，进行下去就不会那么困难。
I wish it weren't impractically hard to find qualified personnel. = 我希望找到合格的人员不会如此困难。


被动语态:

疑问句:

Is the current solution impractically inefficient? = 目前的解决方案是不是效率低下？


否定句:
The plan is not impractically difficult; it just requires more resources. = 这个计划并不难以实施，只是需要更多资源。
It's not impractically impossible, but it will take significant effort. = 它并非不可能，但需要付出巨大的努力。


PHRASE->
impracticably difficult = 极难的
impracticably expensive = 极其昂贵的
impracticably complex = 极其复杂的
impracticably ambitious = 野心勃勃的，不切实际的
impracticably long = 极其漫长的

词源:
im- (否定前缀) + practicable (可行的) + -ly (副词后缀)  "practicable"  源于拉丁语 "practicabilis,"  由 "practicare" (实践，练习) 派生而来。
