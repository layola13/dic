scowls:/skaʊlz/| v.| 皱眉，怒视 | To make a facial expression showing displeasure or anger.  |例句：She scowled at the rude customer. 她对那个粗鲁的顾客皱起了眉头。|例句：He scowled when he heard the bad news. 他听到坏消息时皱起了眉头。|近义词：frown, glare, glower |反义词：smile, grin


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scowls at the television. = 他对着电视皱眉。
She scowls every time she sees him. = 她每次看到他都皱眉。

一般过去时:
He scowled at the referee's decision. = 他对裁判的判罚皱起了眉头。
She scowled when I told her the truth. = 我告诉她真相时，她皱起了眉头。

现在完成时:
He has scowled all afternoon. = 他整个下午都在皱眉。
She has scowled since she received the letter. = 从收到这封信以来，她一直愁眉苦脸。

过去完成时:
He had scowled before I even spoke. = 我还没开口他就已经皱起了眉头。
She had scowled throughout the entire meeting. = 整个会议期间她都一直愁眉苦脸。

将来时:
He will scowl if you tell him that. = 如果你告诉他那样的话，他会皱眉头的。
She will scowl when she finds out the truth. = 当她发现真相时，她会皱眉的。

虚拟语气:
He would scowl if he knew the truth. = 如果他知道真相，他会皱眉的。
She would have scowled if she had seen what happened. = 如果她看到发生的事情，她会皱眉的。

被动语态: (Scowl is rarely used passively)
N/A

疑问句:
Does he often scowl? = 他经常皱眉吗？
Why is she scowling? = 她为什么皱眉？

否定句:
He didn't scowl at all. = 他根本没有皱眉。
She didn't scowl; she smiled. = 她没有皱眉；她笑了。


PHRASE->
scowl at someone = 对某人皱眉
scowl with anger = 愤怒地皱眉
scowl in displeasure = 不悦地皱眉
scowl at the injustice = 对不公正皱眉
scowl in frustration = 沮丧地皱眉
