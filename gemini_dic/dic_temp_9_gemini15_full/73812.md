formed:/fɔːrmd/| /fɔːrːmd/| v. adj.|形成的；塑造的；组成的|To create something by shaping or assembling materials, or to come into existence in a particular shape or form.|例句：The clay was formed into a beautiful vase.  黏土被塑造成一个美丽的瓶子。|例句：The committee was formed to investigate the matter.  该委员会成立以调查此事。|近义词：shaped, molded, created, constituted|反义词：destroyed, demolished, undone, unstructured


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The potter forms the clay. = 陶工塑造黏土。
She forms her opinions carefully. = 她谨慎地形成自己的观点。


一般过去时:
He formed a new company last year. = 他去年成立了一家新公司。
The rock formations were formed millions of years ago. = 这些岩石构造形成于数百万年前。


现在完成时:
They have formed a strong bond. = 他们已经建立了牢固的联系。
The government has formed a new policy. = 政府已经制定了一项新政策。


过去完成时:
The foundation had been formed before the building began. = 在建筑开始之前，地基就已经打好了。
The decision had been formed after careful consideration. = 经过仔细考虑后，做出了决定。


将来时:
They will form a new alliance. = 他们将组建一个新的联盟。
The clouds will form into a storm. = 云将形成风暴。


虚拟语气:
If they formed a coalition, they would have more power. = 如果他们组成联盟，他们就会更有力量。
I wish I had formed better habits earlier. = 我希望我早些养成更好的习惯。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
