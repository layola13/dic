grabber:/ˈɡræbər/| n. v.|吸引人的事物；夺取者；抓取器|Something that attracts attention or interest; a person or thing that grabs or seizes something.|例句：The movie's opening scene was a real grabber. 这部电影的开场场景非常吸引人。|近义词：attention-getter, hook,  eye-catcher|反义词：repellent, bore


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The advertisement grabs the attention of the audience. = 广告吸引了观众的注意。
This fishing grabber helps me catch fish easily. = 这个钓鱼抓取器帮助我轻松捕鱼。


一般过去时:
The headline grabbed my attention immediately. = 标题立刻抓住了我的注意力。
He grabbed the opportunity to speak. = 他抓住了说话的机会。


现在完成时:
The new marketing strategy has grabbed a significant market share. = 新的营销策略已经获得了相当大的市场份额。
She has grabbed several awards for her acting. = 她凭借其演技获得了多个奖项。


过去完成时:
They had grabbed the best seats before the concert started. = 音乐会开始前，他们已经抢到了最好的座位。
He had grabbed the microphone before anyone else could. = 他抢在其他人之前抓起了麦克风。


将来时:
The company will grab a larger share of the market with its innovative product. = 公司将凭借其创新产品占据更大的市场份额。
I will grab the chance to travel abroad. = 我将抓住出国旅行的机会。


虚拟语气:
If I had grabbed that opportunity, I would be in a better position now. = 如果我抓住了那个机会，我现在的情况就会更好。
I wish I had grabbed the chance to study abroad. = 我真希望我抓住了出国留学的机会。


被动语态:
The ball was grabbed by the player. = 球被球员抓住了。
The attention of the audience was grabbed by the speaker. = 观众的注意力被演讲者吸引住了。


疑问句:
What grabbed your attention in the meeting? = 会议上什么东西吸引了你的注意力？
Did you grab the newspaper this morning? = 你今天早上拿报纸了吗？


否定句:
The advertisement didn't grab my attention. = 广告没有吸引我的注意力。
He didn't grab the chance to explain. = 他没有抓住机会解释。
