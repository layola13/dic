held-down:/ˌheldˈdaʊn/| adj.|  被压制的；被控制的；稳定的；不景气的|  Describes something that is suppressed, controlled, or kept at a low level; also can describe a stable job or economy.  |例句：The rebellion was quickly held down by the army.  军队迅速镇压了叛乱。

例句：The economy is currently held down by high inflation. 高通货膨胀目前抑制了经济增长。


近义词：suppressed, controlled, restrained, stable, stagnant

反义词：uncontrolled, unrestrained, booming, flourishing


词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government holds down inflation. = 政府抑制通货膨胀。
This job holds me down. = 这份工作束缚着我。

一般过去时:
The police held down the protesters. = 警察压制了抗议者。
He held down a steady job for twenty years. = 他从事一份稳定的工作达二十年之久。


现在完成时:
They have held down costs effectively. = 他们有效地控制了成本。
She has held down three jobs to support her family. = 她打三份工来养家糊口。


过去完成时:
The dictator had held down the population for decades. = 独裁者压制了人民几十年。
They had held down the fort while the boss was away. = 老板不在的时候，他们坚守岗位。


将来时:
We will hold down the fort while you are away. = 你不在的时候，我们会坚守岗位。
The new policy will hold down unemployment. = 新政策将抑制失业。


虚拟语气:
If we acted decisively, we could have held down the spread of the disease. = 如果我们果断行动，就能控制住疾病的传播。
I wish we could hold down the rising costs. = 我希望我们能够抑制不断上涨的成本。


被动语态:
Inflation was held down by government intervention. = 通货膨胀因政府干预而得到控制。
The riot was held down by the police. = 暴乱被警察镇压了。


疑问句:
Can we really hold down costs this year? = 我们今年真的能够控制成本吗？
How can we hold down the rising crime rate? = 我们如何才能抑制不断上升的犯罪率？


否定句:
They couldn't hold down the rebellion. = 他们无法镇压叛乱。
He couldn't hold down a steady job. = 他找不到稳定的工作。
