pushes:/ˈpʊʃɪz/| vt. n.| 推，推动；促进；强迫；|To exert force on (someone or something) so as to move it away from oneself.|例句：He pushes the door open. 他推开门。|例句：The new policy pushes the company forward.  新政策推动公司向前发展。|近义词：shove, propel, urge|反义词：pull, draw


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pushes the cart. = 他推着购物车。
She pushes her limits. = 她挑战自己的极限。

一般过去时:
They pushed the car to the side of the road. = 他们把车推到路边。
He pushed his way through the crowd. = 他挤过人群。

现在完成时:
She has pushed herself too hard. = 她把自己逼得太紧了。
They have pushed for change. = 他们一直争取改变。

过去完成时:
He had pushed the button before the alarm sounded. = 警报响起前他已经按下了按钮。
She had pushed her agenda throughout the meeting. = 她在整个会议期间都在推行她的计划。

将来时:
They will push the boundaries of innovation. = 他们将突破创新的界限。
I will push myself harder next time. = 下次我会更加努力。

虚拟语气:
If he pushed harder, he might succeed. = 如果他更努力地推，他可能会成功。
I wish I had pushed myself more in school. = 我真希望我在学校里更努力些。

被动语态:
The button was pushed accidentally. = 按钮是不小心按到的。
The reforms were pushed through despite opposition. = 尽管遭到反对，改革还是得以推进。

疑问句:
Who pushed the table over? = 谁把桌子推倒了？
What are you pushing for? = 你在争取什么？

否定句:
He didn't push hard enough. = 他用力不够大。
She didn't push the issue further. = 她没有进一步追究这个问题。


PHRASE->
push back = 推迟
push through = 推动，完成
push for = 争取，推动
push ahead = 继续前进
push the envelope = 突破界限
