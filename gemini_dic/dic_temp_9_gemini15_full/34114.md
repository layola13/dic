clachan:/ˈklɑːkən/| n. |村庄；小村落；石屋群|A clachan is a small village, especially one in the Scottish Highlands, consisting of a cluster of houses, often built of stone.  The term often evokes a sense of a close-knit, isolated community. |Example: The remote clachan nestled in the valley was untouched by the modern world.  这个位于山谷中偏远的村庄未受现代世界的影响。  The old clachan, with its stone walls and thatched roofs, was a picturesque sight.  古老的村庄，有着石墙和茅草屋顶，景色如画。|近义词：hamlet, village, settlement|反义词：metropolis, city
