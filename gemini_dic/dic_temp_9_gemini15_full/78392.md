get-ahead: /ˌɡɛt əˈhɛd/ | adj. | 成功的，有前途的 |  Having or showing ambition and determination to succeed.  This phrase describes someone who is proactive and ambitious, striving to improve their position or status.  It implies a forward-thinking approach and a willingness to work hard to achieve goals. | 例句：He's a real get-ahead guy, always looking for new opportunities. 他是一个非常有进取心的人，总是在寻找新的机会。

He's a get-ahead type, always striving for promotion. 他是一个有上进心的人，总是努力争取晋升。


近义词：ambitious, driven, successful, progressive, aspiring | 反义词：lazy, indolent, unambitious, complacent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gets ahead in his career by working hard. = 他通过努力工作在事业上取得成功。
She gets ahead by networking effectively. = 她通过有效的人脉关系取得成功。


一般过去时:
He got ahead in business by taking risks. = 他通过冒险在生意上取得成功。
She got ahead by consistently exceeding expectations. = 她通过不断超越预期取得成功。


现在完成时:
He has gotten ahead by investing wisely. = 他通过明智的投资取得了成功。
She has gotten ahead through hard work and dedication. = 她通过努力工作和奉献取得了成功。


过去完成时:

She had gotten ahead by the time she turned 30. = 到她30岁的时候，她已经取得了成功。


将来时:
He will get ahead with his new business venture. = 他将凭借他的新商业冒险取得成功。
She will get ahead if she continues to work diligently. = 如果她继续努力工作，她将取得成功。


虚拟语气:

If she had taken more risks, she might have gotten ahead faster. = 如果她承担更多风险，她可能早就取得成功了。


被动语态:  (The phrase "get ahead" is not typically used in the passive voice.)
