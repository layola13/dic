languidly:/ˈlæŋɡwɪdli/| adv. | 懒洋洋地；无精打采地；缓慢地 | In a slow, relaxed, and often lazy manner.  Showing a lack of energy or enthusiasm. | 例句：She languidly fanned herself with a palm leaf. 她懒洋洋地用棕榈叶扇着自己。

She moved languidly across the room. 她懒洋洋地穿过房间。


近义词：listlessly, sluggishly, lethargically, indolently, dreamily

反义词：energetically, vigorously, briskly, actively


词源:  From languid (adjective), ultimately from Latin *languescere "to grow weak," from *langue- "weak," related to Latin lingua "tongue," suggesting a connection to the idea of weakness or debility associated with a drooping tongue.  The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective into an adverb.
