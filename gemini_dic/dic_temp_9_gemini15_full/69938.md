fess:/fɛs/ | vt. |承认；供认；招认| To confess; to admit.  |例句：He finally fessed up to stealing the money. 他最终承认偷了钱。|近义词：confess, admit, acknowledge |反义词：deny, conceal |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fesses up to his mistakes. = 他承认自己的错误。
She fesses to knowing the truth. = 她承认知道真相。

一般过去时:
He fessed up to the police. = 他向警方自首了。
She fessed that she was wrong. = 她承认自己错了。

现在完成时:
He has fessed up to everything. = 他已经承认了一切。
She has fessed to her involvement. = 她已经承认了她的参与。

过去完成时:
He had fessed before the trial began. = 在审判开始之前，他已经承认了。
She had fessed to her parents earlier that day. = 那天早些时候，她已经向父母承认了。

将来时:
He will fess up eventually. = 他最终会承认的。
She will fess to the authorities soon. = 她很快就会向当局承认。

虚拟语气:
If he fessed up, he might get a lighter sentence. = 如果他认罪，他可能会得到较轻的判决。
I wish she had fessed earlier. = 我真希望她早点承认。

被动语态:
The truth was fessed by him. = 真相被他承认了。(此用法较少见，主动语态更常用)
His guilt was eventually fessed to the court. = 他的罪行最终在法庭上被承认了。(此用法较少见，主动语态更常用)


疑问句:
Will he ever fess? = 他会承认吗？
Did she fess to the crime? = 她承认了罪行吗？


否定句:
He didn't fess up to his wrongdoing. = 他没有承认自己的过错。
She wouldn't fess, no matter what. = 无论如何，她都不肯承认。


PHRASE->
fess up: 承认；坦白
fess to something: 承认某事
fess all: 全盘托出
fess it up: 赶紧承认吧
fess freely: 坦白地承认


词源:
The word "fess" is a shortened and informal version of "confess."  Its origins trace back to the Old French word "confesser," meaning "to confess."  The shortening and informal use likely arose in colloquial English, gaining popularity as slang.
