self-sanctification: /ˌsɛlfˌsæŋktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |自我圣化；自以为是；自命清高 | The act of sanctifying oneself;  the process by which one believes oneself to be morally superior or spiritually pure, often without genuine justification. It often involves a sense of self-righteousness and a tendency to judge others harshly. | 例句：His self-sanctification was evident in his constant criticism of others.  他的自我圣化在他不断批评他人中显而易见。

His self-sanctification blinded him to his own flaws. 他的自我圣化使他看不到自身的缺点。


近义词：self-righteousness, sanctimoniousness, moralizing, self-justification | 反义词：humility, self-awareness, self-criticism | 词性: noun
