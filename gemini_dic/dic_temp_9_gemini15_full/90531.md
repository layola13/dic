huffer:/ˈhʌfər/| n. |怒气冲冲的人；大口吸烟者；(火车)呼哧呼哧响的机车| A person who huffs and puffs, especially one who smokes a pipe or cigarette rapidly and aggressively.  Also refers to a steam locomotive emitting puffs of steam. |例句：He was a grumpy old huffer, always complaining about something. 他是个脾气暴躁的家伙，总是抱怨一些事情。


例句：The old steam train was a huffer, puffing out clouds of steam as it chugged along. 那辆老式蒸汽火车呼哧呼哧地冒着蒸汽前进。


近义词：grumbler, puffer, grump, smoker (in the context of smoking) |反义词：calm person, gentle person, non-smoker


词源:  The word "huffer" derives from the verb "huff," meaning to breathe heavily, puff, or snort in anger or exertion.  This verb itself has roots in Old Norse and Old English, reflecting the sound of forceful breathing.
