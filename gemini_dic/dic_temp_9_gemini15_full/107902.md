larrikin:/ˈlærikɪn/| n. |流氓；恶作剧者；淘气鬼 |A larrikin is a young person, typically male, who is considered unruly and rebellious, often engaging in pranks and minor acts of delinquency.  They are characterized by a cheeky, mischievous, and often defiant attitude.  The term often carries a connotation of playful mischief rather than serious criminality. |例句：He was a bit of a larrikin in his youth, always getting into trouble.  他年轻时有点像个小流氓，总是惹麻烦。

The group of larrikins caused a ruckus in the town square.  这群小流氓在城镇广场闹哄哄的。

|近义词：hooligan, rascal, scamp, rogue, delinquent |反义词：goody-goody, angel, saint, well-behaved


词源:

The word's origin is uncertain but is believed to be Australian slang, possibly derived from the name of a 19th-century Australian bushranger, "Larry", or from a combination of elements suggesting a rough, disorderly character.  Its exact etymology remains debated among linguists.
