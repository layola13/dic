farcical:/ˈfɑːrsɪkəl/ | adj. | 滑稽的；荒谬的；闹剧的 |  Something that is farcical is ridiculously unreasonable, illogical, or absurd.  It often involves a chaotic or ludicrous situation, suggesting a comedy of errors.  | The whole situation was utterly farcical.  整个情况完全是滑稽可笑的。

The meeting descended into a farcical argument. 会议演变成一场荒谬的争吵。| ludicrous, absurd, ridiculous, comical | serious, sensible, logical |  形容词

词源:
From Middle French *farce, from Old French *farce ("stuffing; farce"), from Vulgar Latin *farcia ("stuffing"), from Latin farcire ("to stuff").  The sense development is from "stuffing" (as in a play with padding) to "a ludicrous or nonsensical play" to the modern adjectival sense.
