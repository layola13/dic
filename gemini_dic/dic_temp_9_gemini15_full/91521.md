hyomandibular:/ˌhaɪoʊmænˈdɪbjələr/| n. |舌颌骨|The hyomandibular is a bone in the head of fishes and some amphibians, which supports the jaw and operculum (gill cover). It is part of the hyoid arch, a series of bones that support the tongue and other structures in the head.  |Example: The hyomandibular's articulation with the quadrate bone helps facilitate jaw movement. 舌颌骨与方骨的连接有助于颌骨的运动。|近义词:None directly equivalent|反义词:None directly opposite|名词
