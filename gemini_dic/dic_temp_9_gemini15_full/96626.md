indulging:/ɪnˈdʌldʒɪŋ/ | v. |沉溺；纵容；满足；迁就 | Allowing oneself to enjoy a particular pleasure, often to excess.  |例句：He indulged in a long, hot bath after a tiring day. 他在辛苦的一天后洗了个长时间的热水澡来放松自己。|例句：She indulged her sweet tooth with a large piece of chocolate cake. 她吃了一大块巧克力蛋糕来满足她对甜食的渴望。|近义词：pampering, gratifying, satisfying |反义词：restraining, denying, resisting |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He indulges in chocolate every day. = 他每天都沉溺于巧克力。
They indulge their children too much. = 他们太纵容他们的孩子了。

一般过去时:
She indulged in a glass of wine after work. = 她下班后喝了一杯葡萄酒来放松。
I indulged myself with a luxurious spa treatment. = 我给自己做了一个奢华的水疗护理来放松。

现在完成时:
He has indulged in too many sweets lately. = 他最近吃了太多甜食。
She has indulged her passion for painting. = 她一直沉溺于绘画的热情中。

过去完成时:
They had indulged in a hearty meal before the long journey. = 他们长途旅行前吃了一顿丰盛的饭菜。
She had indulged her fantasies for years before finally writing the novel. = 她多年来一直沉溺于幻想，最终才写出了这部小说。

将来时:
I will indulge in a good book this weekend. = 我这个周末要沉迷于一本好书。
They will indulge themselves with a vacation in the Caribbean. = 他们将在加勒比海度假来好好放松一下。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would indulge in a hobby. = 如果我有更多时间，我会沉迷于一个爱好。
I wish I could indulge in a week-long holiday. = 我希望我能享受一个星期的假期。

被动语态:
His desires were indulged by his parents. = 他的愿望被父母满足了。
Her cravings were indulged with a delicious dessert. = 她对美食的渴望得到了满足。

疑问句:
Does he often indulge in fast food? = 他经常吃快餐吗？
Do they indulge their pets excessively? = 他们过度溺爱他们的宠物吗？

否定句:
She didn't indulge in any unhealthy habits. = 她没有养成任何不健康的习惯。
He didn't indulge his anger. = 他没有放纵自己的怒火。


PHRASE->

indulge one's appetite = 满足食欲
indulge one's passions = 满足热情
