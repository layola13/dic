expiated:/ˈɛkspieɪtɪd/| v.|赎罪，补偿，抵偿|to make amends or reparation for (guilt or wrongdoing); atone for.  |例句：He expiated his sins by donating to charity. 他通过捐款来赎罪。|例句：She tried to expiate her guilt by volunteering at a homeless shelter. 她试图通过在流浪者收容所做义工来弥补自己的罪过。|近义词：atone, redeem, compensate|反义词：aggravate, worsen


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He expiates his guilt through acts of kindness. = 他通过善良的行为来赎罪。
She expiates her mistakes by working harder. = 她通过更努力的工作来弥补错误。

一般过去时:
He expiated his crime by confessing his wrongdoing. = 他通过坦白自己的罪行来赎罪。
She expiated her past mistakes with years of service. = 她用多年的服务来弥补过去的错误。

现在完成时:
He has expiated his sins through years of devotion. = 他通过多年的奉献赎清了他的罪孽。
She has expiated her guilt by making amends to those she wronged. = 她通过向那些她伤害过的人赔礼道歉来赎罪。

过去完成时:
He had expiated his earlier transgressions before finding peace. = 他在找到平静之前已经赎清了他早期的罪过。
She had expiated her youthful indiscretions before starting a family. = 在组建家庭之前，她已经弥补了她年轻时的轻率行为。

将来时:
He will expiate his debts by working overtime. = 他将通过加班来偿还债务。
She will expiate her wrongs by making a public apology. = 她将通过公开道歉来弥补自己的过错。

虚拟语气:
If he had expiated his sins earlier, he would have felt less guilt. = 如果他早点赎罪，他就不会感到这么内疚了。
If she had expiated her actions, the situation might have been different. = 如果她当时弥补了自己的行为，情况可能会有所不同。

被动语态:
His sins were expiated through prayer and repentance. = 他的罪孽通过祈祷和悔过得到了赎罪。
Her mistakes were expiated by her selfless dedication. = 她的错误通过她无私的奉献得到了弥补。

疑问句:

How will she expiate the harm she has caused? = 她将如何弥补她造成的伤害？

否定句:
He did not expiate his wrongdoing; he simply ignored it. = 他没有为自己的错误赎罪；他只是忽略了它。
She has not yet expiated the damage she caused to their reputation. = 她还没有弥补她对他们名誉造成的损害。
