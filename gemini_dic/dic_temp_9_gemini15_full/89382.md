homophonous: /hoʊˈmɒfənəs/ | /hoʊˈmɑːfənəs/ | adj. | 同音的 | Having the same sound but a different meaning and spelling.  Words that are homophonous are called homophones.  For example, "to," "too," and "two" are homophonous.

英文解析:  The term describes words that share the same pronunciation but have different meanings and spellings.  These words are often a source of confusion for language learners. The distinction between homophonous words relies entirely on context to determine the intended meaning.

例句:

"There" and "their" are homophonous words. = "there"和"their"是同音词。

The words "hear" and "here" are homophonous, causing occasional spelling errors. =  "hear"和"here"是同音词，有时会导致拼写错误。


近义词:  homophonic (more technical term)

反义词:  There isn't a direct antonym, but words with distinct pronunciations and meanings would be the opposite concept.

词性: adjective
