saponin:/ˈsæpəʊnɪn/ | n. |皂苷，皂素|A group of naturally occurring glycosides characterized by their soap-like properties.  They are found in many plants and have various biological activities, including antimicrobial, hemolytic, and immunostimulatory effects.  Saponins are amphipathic molecules, meaning they have both hydrophilic (water-loving) and hydrophobic (water-fearing) parts.|例句：Many plants contain saponins, which contribute to their bitter taste and potential medicinal properties. 许多植物含有皂苷，这使得它们具有苦味和潜在的药用价值。

例句：The hemolytic activity of saponins can be exploited for certain medical applications, but must be carefully controlled. 皂苷的溶血活性可以用于某些医疗应用，但必须仔细控制。
