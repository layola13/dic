panderage: /ˈpændərɪdʒ/ | n. | 迎合；逢迎；谄媚；讨好 | The act of pandering; excessive flattery or subservience to gain favor.  It often implies a morally questionable motivation, catering to base desires or weaknesses. |例句：The politician's panderage to the electorate was evident in his promises of tax cuts and increased spending.  这位政治家为了讨好选民，承诺减税和增加支出，其迎合之举显而易见。


例句：The author's panderage to popular taste resulted in a shallow and unoriginal novel.  作者迎合大众口味，创作出一部浅薄、缺乏原创性的作品。


近义词：fawning, sycophancy, toadyism, adulation, apple-polishing | 反义词：integrity, honesty, straightforwardness


词源:  Derived from the verb "to pander," which ultimately comes from the Greek word "pandaros," referring to Pandarus, a character in Greek mythology who acted as a procurer or panderer.  Over time, the meaning broadened to encompass any form of excessive flattery or subservience for personal gain.
