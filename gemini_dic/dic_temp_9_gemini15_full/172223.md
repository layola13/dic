pushed:/pʊʃt/| v.| 推，推动；敦促，力劝 | To exert force on (someone or something) so as to move it away from oneself or cause it to move in a particular direction.  |例句：He pushed the door open. 他推开了门。|例句：She pushed her way through the crowd. 她挤过人群。|近义词：shove, propel, urge, press |反义词：pull, draw


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pushes the cart. = 他推着车。
She pushes her limits. = 她挑战自己的极限。

一般过去时:
I pushed the button. = 我按了按钮。
They pushed the project forward. = 他们推动了项目进展。

现在完成时:
He has pushed himself too hard. = 他把自己逼得太紧了。
She has pushed for change. = 她一直在争取改变。

过去完成时:
I had pushed the door before it opened. = 门打开之前我已经推过了。
They had pushed the agenda for hours. = 他们已经讨论议程几个小时了。

将来时:
I will push the boundaries. = 我将突破界限。
They will push for a better deal. = 他们将争取更好的交易。

虚拟语气:
If I pushed harder, I might have succeeded. = 如果我更努力地推，我可能会成功。
I wish I had pushed him to apply. = 我真希望我当时敦促他申请。

被动语态:
The button was pushed accidentally. = 按钮是不小心按下的。
The reforms were pushed through by the government. = 改革是由政府推行的。

疑问句:
Who pushed the table? = 谁推倒了桌子？
What are you pushing for? = 你在争取什么？

否定句:
He didn't push hard enough. = 他没有用力推。
She didn't push the issue further. = 她没有进一步追究这个问题。


PHRASE->
push back = 推迟
push through = 推动，完成
push for = 争取，力求
push ahead = 继续前进
push over = 推倒


词源:
The word "pushed" comes from the Old English word *pūsian*, meaning "to push" or "to thrust".  This word has Germanic roots, related to other words in various Germanic languages with similar meanings.
