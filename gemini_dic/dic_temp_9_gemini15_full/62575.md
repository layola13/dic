enervates:/ɪˈnɜːrvets/| vt.|使衰弱；使无力；使疲惫|To weaken or destroy the strength or vitality of (someone or something).|The long journey enervated the travelers.|近义词：weaken, debilitate, sap|反义词：invigorate, strengthen, energize|动词

一般现在时:
The hot weather enervates me. = 炎热的天气使我感到疲惫。
This monotonous work enervates the spirit. = 这单调的工作使人精神萎靡。


一般过去时:
The illness enervated him for weeks. = 这场病让他虚弱了好几周。
The long speech enervated the audience. = 漫长的演讲让听众感到疲惫。

过去完成时:

词源:
The word "enervate" comes from the Latin word "enervāre," which means "to weaken" or "to deprive of strength."  The Latin word itself is formed from "e-" (out, away) and "nervus" (sinew, nerve).  Therefore, the original meaning relates to removing the sinews or strength.
