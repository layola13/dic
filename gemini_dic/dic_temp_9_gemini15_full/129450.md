non-activity:/nɒnˈæktɪvɪti/ | n. |非活动；不活动；懒散 |The lack of activity; a state or period of inactivity.  It often implies a lack of engagement or participation, or a deliberate avoidance of action.  |例句：The non-activity in the stock market worried investors.  股市的不景气让投资者担忧。

例句：His prolonged non-activity led to muscle atrophy. 他长时间的不活动导致肌肉萎缩。


近义词：inactivity, idleness, dormancy, stagnation

反义词：activity, dynamism, action, liveliness


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the noun "activity" (meaning "the state of being active").  The root of "activity" traces back to the Latin word "activus," meaning "active," which is derived from the verb "agere," meaning "to do" or "to act."


USAGE->
There is no verb form of "non-activity".  It is a noun and thus only used in noun phrases and sentences.  The grammatical usages are limited to the following:

Subject of a sentence:
Non-activity can be detrimental to one's health.  不活动对健康有害。
Prolonged non-activity is a concern for astronauts. 长期不活动是宇航员关注的问题。

Object of a sentence:
The doctor warned him about the dangers of non-activity. 医生警告他注意不活动带来的危险。
We observed a period of non-activity before the eruption.  我们在爆发前观察到了一段不活动的时期。

Part of a noun phrase:
A period of non-activity followed the initial burst of energy.  最初的能量爆发之后是一段不活动的时期。
The report highlighted the link between obesity and non-activity.  报告强调了肥胖与不活动之间的联系。

In prepositional phrases:
Because of the non-activity, the project was delayed.  由于不活动，项目被推迟了。
Despite the non-activity, the team remained optimistic.  尽管没有活动，团队仍然保持乐观。



PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "non-activity".  However, phrases expressing similar meanings can be used:

a lack of activity: 活动的缺乏
a period of inactivity: 一段不活动的时期
a state of dormancy: 休眠状态
a time of stagnation: 停滞时期
absence of movement: 缺乏运动
