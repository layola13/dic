directrix:/daɪˈrɛtrɪks/| n. |准线，直母线|A fixed line used in defining a conic section (such as a parabola, ellipse, or hyperbola).  The conic section is the set of all points whose distance from a fixed point (the focus) is a constant multiple of its distance from the directrix. |Example: The parabola is defined by its focus and directrix.  抛物线由其焦点和准线定义。 The distance from a point on the ellipse to the focus is proportional to its distance from the directrix. 椭圆上一点到焦点的距离与其到准线的距离成比例。|近义词：directing line (less common)|反义词：focus (in the context of conic sections)
