centralise:/ˈsɛntrəlaɪz/| vt.| 集中；中央集权化 | To bring or pull (things) together to a central point or place; to concentrate the control of (an organization, industry, etc) in a single place or authority. | 例句：The company decided to centralise its operations in London.  公司决定将其业务集中在伦敦。

例句：The government is centralising power.  政府正在集中权力。


近义词：concentrate, consolidate, unify | 反义词：decentralise, disperse, diffuse | 词性:动词


USAGE->

The government centralises power. = 政府权力集中。
They centralise their resources. = 他们集中资源。

词源:
From Middle French *centraliser, from Latin centrum (“centre”) + -iser.  The word ultimately derives from the Greek word κέντρον (kéntron), meaning "center" or "point".
