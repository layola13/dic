enmeshes:/ɪnˈmeʃɪz/| vt.|使纠缠；使陷入；使卷入|To involve someone or something in a difficult or complicated situation from which it is hard to escape.|The scandal enmeshed the mayor in controversy.  丑闻使市长陷入争议。|The climber was enmeshed in the thick vines. 登山者被缠在茂密的藤蔓中。|entangle, involve, implicate|disentangle, free, liberate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The complicated regulations enmesh many small businesses. = 复杂的规章制度使许多小型企业陷入困境。
This problem enmeshes the entire department. = 这个问题使整个部门陷入困境。

一般过去时:
The investigation enmeshed him in a web of lies. = 调查将他卷入谎言的漩涡。
The scandal enmeshed the politician's reputation. = 丑闻损害了这位政治家的名誉。

现在完成时:

Years of bad decisions have enmeshed the family in financial ruin. = 多年的错误决定使这个家庭陷入经济困境。

过去完成时:

By the time the police arrived, the suspect had cleverly enmeshed himself in the crowd. = 当警察到达时，嫌疑人已经巧妙地混入了人群中。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
