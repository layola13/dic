incompetentness: /ɪnˈkɑːmpɪtənts/ | n. | 无能；不称职 | Lack of ability or skill; incompetence.  The project failed due to the manager's incompetentness.  |近义词：inability, ineptitude, inadequacy |反义词：competence, ability, proficiency


例句：

His incompetentness led to the company's downfall.  他的无能导致了公司的倒闭。

The incompetentness of the government was evident in its handling of the crisis. 政府在处理危机中的无能是显而易见的。


词源:

The word "incompetentness" is derived from the adjective "incompetent," which is formed by adding the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") to the word "competent."  "Competent" ultimately comes from the Latin word "competens," meaning "suitable, fit, sufficient."


用法说明:

Incompetentness is a noun referring to a lack of ability or skill. It is usually used to describe a person's lack of competence in a specific area or task.  It can also refer to a general lack of ability.  The word carries a negative connotation.
