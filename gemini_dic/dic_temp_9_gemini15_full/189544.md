sale-leasebacks:/ˈseɪlˈliːsˌbæks/| n.|售后回租|A financial transaction where a company sells an asset (usually real estate or equipment) and immediately leases it back from the buyer.  This allows the company to raise capital while retaining the use of the asset.|例句：The company used a sale-leaseback arrangement to free up capital for expansion.  这家公司利用售后回租安排释放资金用于扩张。

例句：Many businesses utilize sale-leasebacks to improve their cash flow. 许多企业利用售后回租来改善现金流。


近义词：finance lease, leveraged lease|反义词：direct purchase, outright sale|词性：Noun
