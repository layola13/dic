demiurgic: /ˌdɛmiˈɜːrdʒɪk/ | adj. | 创造世界的；造物主的；具有创造力的 | Relating to or characteristic of a demiurge; creative; world-creating.  The term often implies a powerful, often impersonal, creative force, not necessarily benevolent or even conscious. |例句：The ancient myth describes a demiurgic being who shaped the world from chaos.  古代神话描述了一个从混沌中塑造世界的造物主。


The demiurgic power of nature is evident in the intricate design of a snowflake.  大自然的创造力在雪花复杂的结构中显而易见。


近义词：creative, generative, formative, productive |反义词：destructive, nihilistic, chaotic

词源:  From Late Latin *demiurgus, from Greek dēmiourgos (δημιουργός), from dēmos (δῆμος) "people" + ergon (ἔργον) "work".  Literally, "craftsman of the people," but in philosophical contexts, it evolved to refer to a creator god, especially in Neoplatonism.
