obtusion:/ɒbˈtjuːʒən/ | n. |迟钝；愚钝；麻木；(感觉的)减弱|The lack of sharpness or sensitivity; dullness or bluntness of mind or feeling.  It can refer to a lack of intelligence, understanding, or sensitivity.  |例句：His obtuse responses showed a lack of understanding.  他的迟钝回应显示出缺乏理解。

例句：The medication caused a gradual obtusion of his senses.  这种药物导致他的感官逐渐麻木。


近义词：dullness, bluntness, insensitivity, numbness, stupidity


反义词：sharpness, acuteness, sensitivity, intelligence


词源:  From Latin *obtusus, past participle of obtundere "to blunt, dull," from ob- "against" + tundere "to beat, strike."  The noun "obtusion" developed from the adjective "obtuse," meaning "blunt" or "dull," which itself derived from the same Latin root.
