reelectrifications:/ˌriːˌɪˌlɛktrɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 再电气化；重复电气化 | The process of reintroducing or restoring electrical power to something that has been de-electrified, or the multiple instances of such a process.  This term often refers to large-scale infrastructure projects, such as upgrading aging power grids or electrifying previously non-electrified areas. | Example: The country's reelectrifications program aims to modernize its outdated power grid and expand access to electricity in rural areas.  = 该国的再电气化项目旨在对其过时的电网进行现代化改造，并扩大农村地区的电力供应。 The numerous reelectrifications undertaken across the nation demonstrate a commitment to sustainable energy. = 全国范围内进行的多次再电气化工程表明了对可持续能源的承诺。 | 近义词：repowering, revitalization (in the context of power grids) | 反义词：de-electrification, decommissioning |  名词
