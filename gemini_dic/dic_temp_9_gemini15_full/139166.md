obiter:/ˈoʊbaɪtər/| adj.|顺便说的；附带的；非主要的|Said incidentally or by the way; not forming part of the formal judgment or decision of a court of law.  It often refers to a judge's remarks or observations that are not essential to the decision in a case.|例句：The judge's obiter dicta on the issue of privacy were widely discussed, even though they weren't legally binding.  法官关于隐私问题的附带意见被广泛讨论，即使它们在法律上不具有约束力。|近义词：incidental, casual, digressive|反义词：essential, crucial, central
