ex·e·cut·ed: /ˈɛksɪkjutɪd/ | v. | 执行；处决；完成；签署 |

英文解析：To carry out or put into effect; to kill (someone) as a legal punishment.  The word has multiple meanings depending on context. It can refer to the completion of a task, the carrying out of a plan, or the legal act of putting someone to death.

例句：

The government executed its new economic policy. = 政府执行了新的经济政策。

The prisoner was executed for murder. = 犯人因谋杀被处决。


近义词：carried out, implemented, performed, finalized, put to death

反义词：aborted, cancelled, postponed, halted, spared


词源：

From Middle English *executen, from Old French executer, from Latin exsecūtōrem (nominative exsecūtor), from ex- (“out, completely”) + secūtor (“follower, pursuer”).  The original sense was "to follow out," "to complete," which evolved to include the legal sense of carrying out a sentence.
