/ˈiːbɒnɪksɪz/ | n. | 黑人英语；非标准英语 | Ebonics is a term for the varieties of English spoken by many African Americans.  It's characterized by unique grammatical structures, pronunciation, and vocabulary.  The term is often controversial due to its varied interpretations and potential for stigmatization. | The study of Ebonics has revealed fascinating insights into the evolution of language and the cultural identity of African Americans. | African American Vernacular English (AAVE), Black English | Standard English | 名词
