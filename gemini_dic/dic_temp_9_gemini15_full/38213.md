compurgation:/ˌkɒmpɜːˈɡeɪʃən/| n. |清白证明；誓证；共同宣誓 |The act of proving one's innocence by the sworn testimony of others;  a legal procedure in medieval Europe where an accused person's innocence was established by a number of oath-helpers swearing to their belief in the person's innocence.  |例句：In medieval times, compurgation was a common method of resolving legal disputes. = 在中世纪，清白证明是解决法律纠纷的常用方法。

The accused relied on compurgation to clear their name. = 被告依靠清白证明来洗清自己的名声。


近义词：oath-helping,  exculpation (partial synonym, as exculpation focuses on proving innocence, not necessarily through oaths) |反义词：conviction, condemnation, incrimination |词性：名词


词源：来自中古英语 *compurgacioun，源自古法语 *compurgacion，源自拉丁语 *compurgatio，由 *com-  (together) + *purgo (to cleanse, purify) 构成。
