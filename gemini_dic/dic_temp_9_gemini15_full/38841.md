/kənˈfeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 混合的；混淆的；混乱的 |  Confused or mixed together in a disordered way.  The conflated accounts of the incident made it difficult to determine the truth. | 近义词：jumbled, muddled, confused, mingled | 反义词：separated, distinct, clear


例句：

The two stories were so conflated in his mind that he couldn't separate them.  = 他脑中两个故事混杂在一起，无法区分。

His recollection of events was conflated with fantasy. = 他对事件的回忆与幻想混杂在一起。

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
From Latin *conflāre "to blow together," from com- "together" + flāre "to blow."  The original sense involved melting metals together; the figurative sense of blending or confusing developed later.
