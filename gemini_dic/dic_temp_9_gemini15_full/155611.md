philosophistical: /ˌfɪləˈsɒfɪkəl/ | adj. | 哲学的；好思辩的；好空谈的 | Relating to or characteristic of philosophy; involving or based on philosophical inquiry.  Often implies a fondness for abstract reasoning, sometimes to the point of being impractical or overly theoretical. |

例句：His philosophistical arguments, while interesting, lacked practical application.  他的哲学论证虽然很有趣，但却缺乏实际应用。

例句：She engaged in philosophistical discussions about the meaning of life. 她参与了关于生命意义的哲学讨论。


近义词：philosophical, speculative, theoretical, abstract

反义词：practical, concrete, pragmatic


词性：形容词


词源:  Derived from "philosophy" + "-ical" (adjectival suffix).  "Philosophy" itself originates from the Greek words  "philos" (φιλος) meaning "love" and "sophia" (σοφια) meaning "wisdom."  Therefore, "philosophistical" ultimately traces back to a love of wisdom.
