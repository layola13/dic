reimposed:/ˌriːɪmˈpoʊzd/| v.|重新实施，再次实行|To impose something again; to reinstate a law, rule, or tax.|The government reimposed travel restrictions due to the rising number of COVID-19 cases. 政府由于新冠肺炎病例的增加而重新实施了旅行限制。|近义词：reinstate, reintroduce|反义词：abolish, repeal|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reimpose the curfew every night. = 他们每晚都重新实施宵禁。
The school reimposes the uniform policy this year. = 学校今年重新实施了校服政策。

一般过去时:
The city reimposed parking restrictions after the festival. = 节日之后，城市重新实施了停车限制。
The judge reimposed the sentence after the appeal was rejected. = 上诉被驳回后，法官重新判决。

现在完成时:
The government has reimposed sanctions on the country. = 政府已经对该国重新实施了制裁。
They have reimposed the tax after a period of exemption. = 在一段时间的免税期后，他们重新征收了税款。

过去完成时:
They had reimposed the rules before the chaos started. = 混乱开始之前，他们已经重新实施了规则。
The company had reimposed the dress code after several complaints. = 公司在收到几起投诉后重新实施了着装规范。

将来时:
The authorities will reimpose the lockdown if the virus spreads again. = 如果病毒再次传播，当局将重新实施封锁。
The management will reimpose fines for late submissions. = 管理层将对迟交作业重新处以罚款。

虚拟语气:
If the situation worsened, they would reimpose the travel ban. = 如果情况恶化，他们将重新实施旅行禁令。
I wish they hadn't reimposed the restrictions so quickly. = 我希望他们不要这么快就重新实施限制。

被动语态:
The restrictions were reimposed due to public safety concerns. = 出于公共安全考虑，限制措施被重新实施。
The new rules will be reimposed next month. = 新规将于下个月重新实施。

疑问句:
Will the government reimpose the tax next year? = 政府明年会重新征税吗？
Have they reimposed the ban on plastic bags? = 他们是否重新禁止使用塑料袋？

否定句:
The company did not reimpose the dress code. = 公司没有重新实施着装规范。
They have not reimposed the fines yet. = 他们还没有重新处以罚款。
