catallacta: /ˌkætəˈlæktə/ | n. | 卡塔拉克塔（经济学中的自发秩序） |  The spontaneous order arising from the interaction of many individuals in a free market.  It refers to the unintended and emergent consequences of individual actions within a market system.  The term emphasizes the decentralized and unplanned nature of market processes, contrasting with centralized planning.  | Example: The efficient allocation of resources in a free market is often cited as an example of catallaxy.  卡塔拉克塔经常被引用为自由市场中资源有效配置的一个例子。 | 近义词: spontaneous order, free market, market mechanism | 反义词: central planning, command economy | 名词
