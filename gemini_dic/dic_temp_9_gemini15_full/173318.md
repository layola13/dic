quasi-deafening: /ˈkwɑːziˌdefənɪŋ/ | adj. | 几乎震耳欲聋的；近乎令人耳聋的 | Almost deafening; very loud and likely to cause deafness.  The word combines "quasi" meaning "seemingly," "apparently," or "to a certain extent" with "deafening," which describes a sound so loud it makes one unable to hear.  It implies a sound that is extremely loud but perhaps not quite entirely deafening. |

例句：

The concert was quasi-deafening; I had to cover my ears.  这场音乐会几乎震耳欲聋；我不得不捂住耳朵。

The roar of the crowd was quasi-deafening, making conversation impossible.  人群的喧嚣声几乎震耳欲聋，使交谈成为不可能。


近义词：very loud, extremely loud, deafeningly loud, thunderous, tumultuous

反义词：quiet, silent, hushed, soft

词性：形容词
