fond:/fɒnd/ /fɑːnd/| adj.|喜欢的，喜爱的；宠爱的；怀念的 |Having a strong liking or affection for something or someone.  It implies a deep and often long-lasting feeling of affection or attachment.|例句：She's very fond of her grandchildren. 她非常疼爱她的孙子孙女们。

例句：I have fond memories of my childhood. 我对童年有着美好的回忆。


近义词：affectionate, loving, attached, devoted | 反义词：disliking, hating, indifferent, apathetic | 词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is fond of his cat. = 他很喜欢他的猫。
She is fond of reading novels. = 她喜欢阅读小说。

一般过去时:
They were fond of each other. = 他们彼此相爱。
He was fond of playing the piano. = 他喜欢弹钢琴。

现在完成时:
I have been fond of this song since childhood. = 从小我就喜欢这首歌。
She has always been fond of animals. = 她一直都很喜欢动物。

过去完成时:
He had been fond of her for many years. = 他喜欢她很多年了。
They had been fond of travelling before they got married. = 结婚前他们喜欢旅游。

将来时:
I will always be fond of you. = 我将永远喜欢你。
She will probably be fond of her new job. = 她可能会喜欢她的新工作。

虚拟语气:
If I were fond of cooking, I would make dinner more often. = 如果我喜欢烹饪，我会更经常做饭。
I wish I were fond of vegetables. = 我希望我喜欢蔬菜。

被动语态:  (Fond is rarely used passively)  The passive voice is not natural or common with "fond."

疑问句:
Are you fond of spicy food? = 你喜欢辛辣食物吗？
Is he fond of classical music? = 他喜欢古典音乐吗？

否定句:
I am not fond of crowds. = 我不喜欢人群。
She wasn't fond of his jokes. = 她不喜欢他的笑话。


PHRASE->
