departments: /dɪˈpɑːrtmənts/ | n. | 部门，系，科 |  A department is a distinct section or division of a larger organization, institution, or company, typically responsible for a specific area of activity or function.  | Example: The marketing department launched a new advertising campaign. 市场部发起了一场新的广告宣传活动。 |近义词：division, section, branch, unit |反义词：whole, entirety


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The marketing department handles all advertising. = 市场部负责所有广告业务。
The sales department meets its targets every quarter. = 销售部每个季度都达到目标。


一般过去时:
The finance department audited the accounts last month. = 财务部上个月审计了账目。
The research department completed the study in 2022. = 研究部门在2022年完成了这项研究。


现在完成时:
The HR department has hired ten new employees this year. = 人力资源部今年已经招聘了十名新员工。
The IT department has resolved the network issue. = IT部门已经解决了网络问题。


过去完成时:
The engineering department had finished the design before the meeting. = 工程部在会议之前已经完成了设计。
The production department had already shipped the goods when the order was cancelled. = 订单取消时，生产部已经发货了。


将来时:
The legal department will review the contract tomorrow. = 法务部明天将审核合同。
The accounting department will prepare the financial report next week. = 会计部下周将准备财务报告。


虚拟语气:
If the sales department had performed better, the company would have made more profit. = 如果销售部业绩更好，公司本可以赚更多利润。
I wish the HR department had more resources. = 我希望人力资源部有更多资源。


被动语态:
The budget was approved by the finance department. = 预算已获得财务部批准。
The new software is being implemented by the IT department. = 新软件正在由IT部门实施。


疑问句:
Which department is responsible for this project? = 哪个部门负责这个项目？
What does the customer service department do? = 客户服务部门做什么？


否定句:
The R&D department did not develop a new product this year. = 研发部门今年没有开发新产品。
The accounting department hasn't finished the audit yet. = 会计部还没有完成审计。
