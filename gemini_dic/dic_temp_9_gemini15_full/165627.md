preminister: /ˌpriːˈmɪnɪstər/ | n. | 预任总理；候任总理 | A person appointed or elected to be a prime minister but not yet officially in office.  This word is rarely used and is considered informal or even incorrect.  The term "prime minister-designate" is more commonly and formally used. | Example:  The preminister addressed the press before the official swearing-in ceremony.  预任总理在正式宣誓就职典礼前会见了记者。 | 近义词：prime minister-designate, incoming prime minister | 反义词：outgoing prime minister | 名词
