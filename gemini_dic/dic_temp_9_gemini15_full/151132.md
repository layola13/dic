paratactic:/ˌpærəˈtæktɪk/| adj. | 并列的；（句子结构）并列的 | Characterized by the use of parataxis; characterized by the juxtaposition of clauses or sentences without the use of coordinating or subordinating conjunctions.  Paratactic style is often characterized by short, simple sentences and a lack of complex grammatical structures. | Example:  "I came, I saw, I conquered." This sentence is paratactic because the clauses are simply placed one after another without conjunctions.  | 近义词：coordinate, juxtaposed | 反义词：hypotactic
