espaliered:/ɛsˈpælɪərd/| adj.| espalier 修剪的； espalier 式种植的 | Trained or grown as an espalier.  An espalier is a plant, typically a fruit tree or vine, that is trained to grow flat against a wall or other support, often in a decorative pattern.  The word "espaliered" describes something that has been trained in this way.|The espaliered pear trees lined the brick wall, their branches carefully pruned and tied to create a beautiful display.|近义词：trained, trellised|反义词：untrained, unkempt

词源:
From the French word *espalière*, ultimately from the Old French *espaliere*, from the Italian *spalliera*, from *spalla* ("shoulder").  The original meaning referred to a shoulder rest or back rest, later evolving to describe the training of plants against a wall, resembling the shape of a shoulder rest.
