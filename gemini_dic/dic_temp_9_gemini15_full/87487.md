hetero-agglutinin: /ˌhɛtərəʊəˈɡluːtɪnɪn/ | n. | 异凝集素 | A heteroagglutinin is an antibody that agglutinates (clumps together) red blood cells from a different species than the one it was produced in.  It's a type of antibody with broad reactivity, meaning it can bind to antigens on the surface of red blood cells from various species, not just the species it originated from. This property makes heteroagglutinins useful in various research and diagnostic applications. |  The researchers used a plant-derived heteroagglutinin to study the surface antigens of different animal red blood cells.  研究人员使用植物来源的异凝集素来研究不同动物红细胞的表面抗原。| isoagglutinin, alloagglutinin | autoagglutinin | noun
