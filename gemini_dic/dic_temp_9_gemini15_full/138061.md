norma:/ˈnɔːrmə/| n.|规范；标准；准则|The norma for this job is to work 8 hours a day.  这项工作的规范是每天工作8小时。|近义词：standard, rule, criterion|反义词：abnormality, exception


英文解析：A norma is a standard or pattern, especially of behavior, that is considered typical or expected.  It can also refer to a usual level, quantity, or rate.


例句：

The social normas of this country are quite different from those of my own. 这个国家的社会规范与我自己的国家大相径庭。

He violated the company's safety normas. 他违反了公司的安全规范。


词源:

From Latin *norma ("carpenter's square, rule, pattern"), from Proto-Italic *normā, from Proto-Indo-European *(h₁)n̥rmā-.  The word's evolution reflects its original meaning as a tool for measuring and establishing regularity, which then extended to encompass social and behavioral standards.


用法说明：

norma 通常作不可数名词使用，但有时也可以用作可数名词，表示具体的规范或标准。


注意：没有动词或形容词形式的“norma”。  如果需要表达与规范相关的动词或形容词，需要使用其他的词语，例如：conform to (符合规范),  standardize (标准化),  normal (正常的),  standard (标准的)。
