patrolling:/pəˈtroʊlɪŋ/ | v. |巡逻；巡查 | The act of regularly going around an area to watch for problems or dangers.  |例句：The police are patrolling the streets tonight. 今晚警察正在街上巡逻。

例句：Soldiers are patrolling the border. 士兵们正在边境巡逻。


近义词：guarding, monitoring, surveying, inspecting | 反义词：neglecting, ignoring


USAGE->

They patrol the streets every night. = 他们每晚都在街上巡逻。

词源:
From the verb "patrol," which comes from the French word "patrouille," meaning "a small party of soldiers sent out to explore or guard."  The ultimate origin is uncertain, but possible connections exist to words meaning "to beat a path" or "to tread."
