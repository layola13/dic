known:/noʊn/| adj.|已知的，著名的，熟知的| familiar or recognizable; widely known and acclaimed. |例句：The thief is known to the police.  这个贼警方认识。|例句：She's known for her beautiful voice. 她以其美妙的嗓音而闻名。|近义词：familiar, famous, renowned, celebrated|反义词：unknown, unfamiliar, obscure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is known for his kindness. = 他以他的善良而闻名。
The answer is known to everyone. = 答案众所周知。


一般过去时:
The truth was known only to a few people. = 真相只有少数人知道。
The location was known to the enemy. = 敌方知道地点。


现在完成时:
The results have been known for a week. = 结果已经为人所知一周了。
This fact has long been known to scientists. = 科学家们早就知道这个事实了。


过去完成时:
The secret had been known to him for years. = 这个秘密他早已知道多年了。
It had been known that the project would fail. =  人们早就知道这个项目会失败。


将来时:
The winner will be known tomorrow. = 明天的获胜者将被宣布。
The truth will soon be known to all. = 真相很快就会为所有人所知。


虚拟语气:
If I knew the answer, I would tell you. = 如果我知道答案，我会告诉你。
I wish I knew more about this subject. = 我希望我对这个主题了解更多。


被动语态:
The problem is known to be complex. =  众所周知这个问题很复杂。
The solution was known to have been found. =  已知解决方案已被找到。


疑问句:
Is the answer known yet? = 答案已经知道了吗？
Is he known to you? = 你认识他吗？


否定句:
The cause of the accident is not yet known. = 事故原因尚不清楚。
She was not known to many people. = 她不为很多人所熟知。


PHRASE->
well-known = 著名的
little-known = 鲜为人知的
as known as = 和...一样出名
be known to = 为...所知
known fact = 众所周知的事实
