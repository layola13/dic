knifepoint:/ˈnaɪfˌpɔɪnt/| n. |刀尖；刀口；紧急关头|At knifepoint means at the point of a knife, implying a threatening or dangerous situation where someone is forced to do something against their will because a knife is pointed at them.  It can also refer to a critical or crucial moment.|例句：He was forced to hand over the money at knifepoint. 他被迫在刀尖下交出钱财。

例句：The company was at knifepoint, facing bankruptcy. 这家公司到了生死存亡的关头。|近义词：threat, danger, crisis, emergency|反义词：safety, security, peace
