rally:/ˈræli/ | n., v. | 集会；恢复；反弹；团结；|

英文解析：As a noun, a rally refers to a public meeting or demonstration, often for a political or social cause.  As a verb, it means to come together for a cause, to recover strength or spirit, or to increase in price or value after a decline.

例句：
The protestors held a rally to demonstrate against the new law.  抗议者举行集会以示反对这项新法律。

The team rallied in the final quarter and scored the winning goal.  球队在最后一节奋起直追，攻入制胜球。


近义词：(n.) gathering, demonstration, meeting; (v.) recover, revive, rebound, unite, consolidate

反义词：(v.) collapse, deteriorate, decline, disintegrate


词性：Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The market rallies every Monday. = 市场每个星期一都会反弹。
She rallies her troops before the battle. = 她战前激励她的军队。


一般过去时:
The price of oil rallied sharply last week. = 上周油价大幅上涨。
The team rallied together after their loss. = 球队在失利后团结一心。


现在完成时:
The stock market has rallied significantly this year. = 股市今年已大幅上涨。
The community has rallied around the family since the accident. = 自事故发生以来，社区一直团结在那个家庭周围。


过去完成时:
The soldiers had rallied their courage before the attack. = 士兵们在进攻前鼓起了勇气。
The economy had rallied slightly before the recession hit. = 经济在衰退来临之前略有复苏。


将来时:
The party expects to rally its supporters before the election. = 该党预期会在选举前团结支持者。
We believe the economy will rally next year. = 我们相信经济明年将会复苏。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
