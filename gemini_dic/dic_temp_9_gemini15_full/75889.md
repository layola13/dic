fundamentalists:/ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪsts/| n. |原教旨主义者，原义论者|People who adhere strictly to fundamental principles, especially in religion or politics.  They often interpret religious texts literally and resist modern interpretations. |例句：Some fundamentalists believe the Bible should be interpreted literally.  一些原教旨主义者认为圣经应该逐字解释。|例句：Political fundamentalists often cling to traditional values and oppose change. 政治原教旨主义者往往坚持传统价值观并反对变革。|近义词：traditionalists, purists, literalists|反义词：liberals, progressives, modernists|名词
