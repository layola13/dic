eucopolysaccharides: /ˌjuːkoʊˈpɒlɪsækəraɪdz/ | n. |真核多糖|  Eucopolysaccharides are complex carbohydrates found in eukaryotic organisms, typically composed of many monosaccharide units linked together. They play vital roles in various biological processes, including structural support, energy storage, and cell recognition.  The term "eucopolysaccharide" emphasizes its origin within eukaryotic cells, differentiating it from other polysaccharides found in prokaryotes or produced by other means. |  Example:  The study investigated the eucopolysaccharides present in the cell walls of various plant species.  这项研究调查了各种植物细胞壁中存在的真核多糖。 |近义词: polysaccharides, glycans | 反义词:  (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑与其成分相对的单糖monosaccharides)| 词性: n.
