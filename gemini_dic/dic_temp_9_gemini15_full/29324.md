castigation:/ˌkæstɪˈɡeɪʃən/ | n. | 严厉批评；惩戒；责骂 | The act of punishing someone severely, especially by criticizing them.  It implies a severe and often public rebuke, aimed at correcting wrongdoing. |例句：The teacher's castigation of the student was harsh but necessary. 老师对学生的严厉批评虽然严厉，却是必要的。

The author received considerable castigation for his controversial new novel. 作者因其有争议的新小说而受到了严厉批评。


近义词：rebuke, reprimand, reproach, chastisement, censure | 反义词：praise, commendation, approval | 词性：名词


词源:

The word "castigation" comes from the Latin word "castigatio," which means "punishment," "correction," or "chastisement."  It is derived from the verb "castigare," meaning "to chastise," "to punish," or "to correct."  The Latin root is "castig-", related to "castus," meaning "pure," implying that punishment aims to purify or correct.


用法说明:

Castigation is typically used to describe a severe and formal criticism or punishment.  It often implies a sense of moral judgment and is not simply a light scolding.  The act of castigation is usually public or at least known to others, increasing its impact.  The subject of castigation is often someone who has committed a serious offense or made a significant mistake.


例句：

The general's castigation of his troops was a demoralizing event. 将军对部队的严厉批评是一件令人沮丧的事件。

Her public castigation by the media was brutal. 媒体对她的公开谴责是残酷的。
