clasping: /ˈklæspɪŋ/ | v. | 紧握；抱紧；扣紧 |

英文解析:To hold something or someone firmly with your hands or arms.  It can also refer to the act of fastening something, such as a clasp.

例句:
She was clasping her baby tightly to her chest. = 她紧紧地抱着她的婴儿贴在胸前。

He clasped the handle of the suitcase firmly. = 他紧紧抓住手提箱的把手。


近义词: gripping, grasping, holding, embracing, clutching

反义词: releasing, letting go, loosening

词源:
The word "clasping" comes from the Old English word "claspian," meaning "to seize, grasp, or embrace."  This in turn is related to other Germanic words with similar meanings.  The modern English word retains this core sense of firm holding or fastening.
