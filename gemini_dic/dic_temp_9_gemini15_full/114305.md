mainstreams:/ˈmeɪnˌstriːmz/ | n. |主流，主流趋势 | The mainstream of public opinion supports the government's policy. 公众舆论的主流支持政府的政策。|近义词：prevailing trend, majority opinion |反义词：fringe, minority, avant-garde


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The mainstream media reports on the election. = 主流媒体报道了选举。
What are the mainstream views on climate change? = 关于气候变化的主流观点是什么？


一般过去时:
The mainstream culture embraced rock and roll in the 1950s. = 20 世纪 50 年代，主流文化接受了摇滚乐。
His ideas were outside the mainstream in those days. = 在那个年代，他的想法不属于主流。


现在完成时:
Mainstream thinking has shifted considerably in recent years. = 近年来，主流思想发生了很大的转变。
The company has successfully integrated itself into the mainstream market. = 这家公司已成功地融入主流市场。


过去完成时:
Before the invention of the internet, the mainstream media controlled the flow of information. = 在互联网发明之前，主流媒体控制着信息流。
By the time the scandal broke, it had already become mainstream news. = 当丑闻爆发时，它已经成为主流新闻了。


将来时:
The new technology will likely become mainstream in the next few years. =这项新技术很可能在未来几年成为主流。
Experts predict that this trend will become mainstream by 2030. = 专家预测，到 2030 年，这一趋势将成为主流。


虚拟语气:
If his ideas were mainstream, he would have received more support. = 如果他的想法是主流，他会得到更多支持。
I wish this innovative product could quickly become mainstream. = 我希望这款创新产品能迅速成为主流。


被动语态:
The new policy was quickly adopted by the mainstream. = 新政策很快被主流所采纳。
The issue is being discussed by the mainstream media. = 主流媒体正在讨论这个问题。


疑问句:
Is this approach part of the mainstream? = 这种方法是主流的一部分吗？
What are the mainstream solutions to this problem? = 解决这个问题的主流方案是什么？


否定句:
His work is not part of the mainstream art scene. = 他的作品并非主流艺术界的一部分。
That theory is not accepted by the mainstream scientific community. = 那个理论不被主流科学界接受。
