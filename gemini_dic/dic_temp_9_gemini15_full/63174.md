entailer:/ɪnˈteɪlər/| n. |蕴含者；推论者； entail 的施动者|A person or thing that entails something;  someone or something that implies or suggests something else.  An entailer is the thing that logically necessitates or implies something else.|例句：The high cost of the project is an entailer of significant financial risk.|近义词：implier, indicator, suggester|反义词：disprover, refuter|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The high cost entails significant risk. = 高昂的成本意味着巨大的财务风险。
This job entails long hours. = 这份工作需要长时间工作。


一般过去时:
The decision entailed unforeseen consequences. = 这个决定带来了意想不到的后果。
The treaty entailed significant territorial concessions. = 该条约带来了重大的领土让步。


现在完成时:
The project has entailed more work than anticipated. = 该项目比预期的工作量更大。
The renovations have entailed considerable expense. = 装修花费巨大。


过去完成时:
The investigation had entailed interviewing numerous witnesses. = 调查包括采访众多证人。
The war had entailed immense human suffering. = 战争造成了巨大的人类苦难。


将来时:
The new regulations will entail significant changes to our operations. = 新规将对我们的运营带来重大变化。
The expansion will entail hiring additional staff. = 扩张将需要招聘更多员工。


虚拟语气:
If the plan entailed such risks, we wouldn't pursue it. = 如果计划包含如此大的风险，我们不会继续进行。
I wish the job didn't entail so much travel. = 我希望这份工作不需要那么多出差。


被动语态:
Significant changes were entailed by the new policy. = 新政策带来了重大的变化。
Additional costs were entailed by the unexpected delays. = 意外延误带来了额外的成本。


疑问句:
What does this position entail? = 这个职位需要做什么？
What exactly does the contract entail? = 合同到底包含什么？


否定句:
The agreement didn't entail any additional obligations. = 该协议没有任何额外的义务。
The task didn't entail any special skills. = 这项任务不需要任何特殊技能。


PHRASE->
entail upon: 使承担；强加于
entail in: 包含在...中
entail with: 伴随；与...相关
entail by: 由...引起
entailment of: ...的必然结果
