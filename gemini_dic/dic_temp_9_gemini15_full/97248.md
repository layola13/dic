inflexibleness: /ˌɪnˌflɛksəˈbɪlnəs/ | n. | 不灵活；僵硬；顽固 | The inflexibleness of the rules made it difficult to adapt to changing circumstances.  规则的僵硬使得适应变化的环境变得困难。|近义词：rigidity, stiffness, inflexibility |反义词：flexibility, adaptability, pliability


词源:

* **in-**:  prefix indicating negation or reversal. (否定或反转的前缀)
* **flex-**: from Latin *flectere* meaning "to bend." (来自拉丁语 *flectere*，意思是“弯曲”)
* **-ible**: suffix indicating capable of being. (表示能够…的后缀)
* **-ness**: suffix forming nouns of quality or state. (构成表示品质或状态的名词的后缀)

Therefore, "inflexibleness" literally means "the state of not being able to bend." (因此，“inflexibleness”字面意思是“不能弯曲的状态”)


用法说明:

Inflexibleness is primarily used as a noun to describe the quality or state of being inflexible, rigid, or unyielding. It can refer to:


* **Physical inflexibility:**  Lack of physical flexibility or suppleness. (缺乏身体的灵活性和柔软性)
* **Mental inflexibility:**  An unwillingness or inability to change one's opinions, methods, or plans. (不愿或不能改变自己的意见、方法或计划)
* **Institutional inflexibility:**  The rigidity of rules, systems, or organizations that prevents adaptation to change. (规则、制度或组织的僵化阻碍了对变化的适应)


例句:

The inflexibleness of his personality made it hard to work with him. 他性格的僵硬使得和他一起工作很困难。

The inflexibleness of the company's policies led to its eventual downfall. 公司政策的僵硬导致了它最终的垮台。


近义词：

rigidity, stiffness, inflexibility, inflexibility, unyieldingness, stubbornness


反义词：
