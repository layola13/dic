holdover's:/ˈhoʊldˌoʊvər/ | n. | 剩余部分；遗留者；延续的事物 | A person or thing that continues from a previous time or situation.  It often implies something remaining from a previous period,  perhaps unexpectedly or temporarily. |例句：The old system is a holdover from a previous administration.  旧制度是前一届政府遗留下来的。

The remaining tickets are holdovers from the last show. 剩下的票是上次演出的剩余票。
