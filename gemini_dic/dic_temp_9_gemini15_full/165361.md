prejudgments:/ˈpriːdʒʌdʒmənts/ | n. |偏见；预断；先入之见 | Opinions or judgments formed before sufficient knowledge is available.  Prejudgments often stem from biases and can lead to unfair or inaccurate conclusions. |例句：His prejudices and prejudgments made it difficult for him to consider the case fairly. 他的偏见和预断使他难以公平地审理此案。

例句：It's important to avoid prejudgments when interviewing job candidates. 面试求职者时，避免预先判断很重要。


近义词：preconceptions, biases, assumptions, predispositions |反义词：objective assessment, unbiased opinion, fair judgment |词性:名词
