carbide:/ˈkɑːrbaɪd/ | n. |碳化物 |A chemical compound of carbon with another element, especially a metal.  Carbide is typically hard and resistant to heat. |Example: Tungsten carbide is used in cutting tools because of its extreme hardness. 钨碳化物因其极高的硬度而被用于切割工具。 Calcium carbide reacts with water to produce acetylene gas. 碳化钙与水反应生成乙炔气体。|近义词：metal compound|反义词:  (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑与“柔软的化合物”相对)|名词

词源:
The word "carbide" comes from the combining form "carb-" (from carbon) + "-ide" (a suffix indicating a binary compound).  The suffix "-ide" suggests a compound formed from two elements.
