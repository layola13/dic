hoity-toity: /ˈhɔɪti ˈtoɪti/ | n. adj. | 傲慢的；自以为是的；势利的 |

英文解析:  Used to describe someone who is arrogant, snobbish, and excessively proud of their social status or accomplishments.  The term implies a pretentious and affected manner.

例句:

She acted all hoity-toity when she learned I didn't go to an Ivy League college. = 她得知我没有上常青藤大学后，就摆出一副盛气凌人的样子。

The hoity-toity restaurant refused to seat us without a reservation. = 那家高傲的餐馆拒绝在我们没有预订的情况下为我们安排座位。


近义词: snobbish, pretentious, arrogant, supercilious, haughty, stuck-up

反义词: humble, modest, unassuming, down-to-earth


词源: The origin is uncertain, but it's likely a reduplication of an unknown word or phrase, possibly a combination of words suggesting affected elegance or high-class behavior.
