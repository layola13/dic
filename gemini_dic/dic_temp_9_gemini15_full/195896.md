sense:/sɛns/| n. v.|感觉，意义，理解力；感知|The word "sense" can refer to one of the five physical senses (sight, hearing, smell, taste, and touch), or to a feeling or understanding. It can also be used as a verb meaning to perceive or understand something.|例句：I have a sense that something is wrong. 我感觉有什么不对劲。|近义词：feeling, meaning, perception, understanding|反义词：nonsense, meaninglessness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He senses danger. = 他感觉到了危险。
I sense a change in the atmosphere. = 我感觉气氛变了。

一般过去时:
She sensed his hesitation. = 她感觉到了他的犹豫。
They sensed a shift in public opinion. = 他们感觉到了民意的转变。

现在完成时:
Have you sensed any problems with the project? = 你有没有感觉到项目有什么问题？
We have sensed a growing unease among the population. = 我们已经感觉到民众日益增长的不安。

过去完成时:
I had sensed his disapproval beforehand. = 我事先就感觉到了他的反对。
She had sensed a lack of enthusiasm in his voice. = 她之前就感觉到了他声音里缺乏热情。

将来时:
I think I will sense the difference soon. = 我想我很快就会感觉到区别。
They will sense the opportunity and grasp it. = 他们将会感觉到机会并抓住它。

虚拟语气:
If I sensed any danger, I would leave immediately. = 如果我感觉到任何危险，我会立即离开。
I wish I had sensed the problem earlier. = 我真希望我早点感觉到这个问题。

被动语态:
The change was sensed by everyone. = 变化被每个人都感觉到了。
The danger was sensed only by a few. = 危险只有少数人感觉到了。

疑问句:
Do you sense any hostility in his words? = 你从他的话语中感觉到敌意吗？
Can you sense the tension in the room? = 你能感觉到房间里的紧张气氛吗？

否定句:
I didn't sense any ill will in her actions. = 我没有从她的行为中感觉到恶意。
He didn't sense the gravity of the situation. = 他没有意识到情况的严重性。

PHRASE->
