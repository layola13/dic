commons:/ˈkɒmənz/ /ˈkɑːmənz/| n. |公地；公共用地；普通民众；下议院；共有物|The word "commons" refers to land or resources that are shared by a community and not owned privately.  It can also refer to the House of Commons in the UK Parliament, or the common people.  The meaning often depends on the context.|例句：The villagers shared the use of the commons for grazing their animals. 村民们共同使用公地放牧他们的牲畜。

The House of Commons debated the new bill. 下议院就新法案进行了辩论。|近义词：common land, public land, shared resources, common people |反义词：private property, exclusive rights

词源:
The word "commons" originates from the Old French word "comuns," meaning "common," which in turn derives from the Latin word "communis," also meaning "common," "shared," or "belonging to all."  The evolution reflects the historical significance of shared land and resources in communities.
