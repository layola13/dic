hellenistic:/ˌhɛləˈnɪstɪk/| adj.| 希腊化的；亚历山大时期的 | Relating to the Hellenistic period (roughly 323-31 BC) in the history of the ancient Greek world, following the death of Alexander the Great.  This period is characterized by the spread of Greek culture and language across a vast empire, leading to a fusion of Greek and other cultures. | The Hellenistic period saw the rise of new artistic styles and philosophical schools.  |近义词：Alexandrine, Greco-Roman |反义词：pre-Hellenistic
