preemptor: /prɪˈɛmptər/ | n. |先发制人者；抢先者；预先占有者 | One who preempts; one who takes precedence over others.  A preemptor acts before others, often to secure an advantage. | Example: The company was a preemptor in the market, launching its product before any competitors. 这家公司是市场上的先发制人者，在任何竞争对手之前推出了其产品。| 近义词: anticipator, usurper, forestaller | 反义词: follower, laggard


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The preemptor secures the best resources. = 先发制人者获得了最好的资源。
He preempts the discussion with his own ideas. = 他用自己的想法抢先开始了讨论。


一般过去时:
The general preempted the enemy's attack. = 将军抢先发动了攻击，阻止了敌人的进攻。
She preempted the inheritance by claiming it early. = 她提前提出继承权，从而抢先继承了遗产。


现在完成时:
They have preempted the crisis by taking preventive measures. = 他们通过采取预防措施，已先发制人地化解了危机。
He has preempted the objections by addressing them beforehand. = 他事先解决了异议，从而避免了反对。


过去完成时:
The government had preempted the opposition's strategy. = 政府已先发制人地挫败了反对党的策略。
She had preempted the sale by buying the item herself. = 她自己买了那件商品，从而抢先阻止了商品的出售。


将来时:
We will preempt any attempt to sabotage the project. = 我们将先发制人地阻止任何破坏项目的企图。
The company plans to preempt the market with a new product line. = 公司计划用新的产品线来抢占市场。


虚拟语气:

I wish I had preempted his actions. = 我真希望我当时能先发制人地阻止他的行动。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
