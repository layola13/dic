jesters:/ˈdʒɛstərz/| n. |戏弄者；小丑；滑稽演员 | A jester is a professional fool who entertained people in medieval courts. They were known for their wit, humor, and often provocative antics.  Their role extended beyond mere entertainment, sometimes serving as advisors or critics due to their freedom of speech. |例句：The king's jester amused the court with his witty remarks. 国王的弄臣以其诙谐的言辞逗乐了朝臣。

The jester's antics were often a thinly veiled commentary on courtly life. 小丑的滑稽表演常常是对宫廷生活的含蓄评论。|近义词：clown, buffoon, fool |反义词：serious person, solemn figure
