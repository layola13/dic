prejournalistic: /ˌpriːˌdʒɜːrnəlˈɪstɪk/ | adj. | 预新闻的，新闻业之前的 |  Relating to the period or activities before the development of modern journalism.  This term is rarely used and often found in academic contexts discussing the history of communication or information dissemination. It describes the methods of information sharing and news distribution that existed before the establishment of formalized journalistic practices and institutions.


例句：

The study examined prejournalistic methods of disseminating information in 17th-century Europe. = 这项研究考察了17世纪欧洲传播信息的预新闻方法。


The dissemination of news through town criers represents a prejournalistic form of communication. = 通过报童传播新闻代表了一种预新闻的沟通形式。


近义词：antecedent, prior, preceding, pre-press


反义词：journalistic, postjournalistic


词性：形容词
