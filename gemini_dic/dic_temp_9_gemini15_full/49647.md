demilitarizing: /ˌdiːˌmɪlɪtəraɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 非军事化；解除武装 | The act of removing military forces, weapons, or fortifications from a place or region.  It aims to reduce the potential for conflict and promote peace. |例句：The agreement called for the complete demilitarizing of the border region.  协议要求完全非军事化边境地区。

例句：The UN is working to demilitarize the conflict zone.  联合国正在努力使冲突地区非军事化。


近义词：disarming, neutralizing, pacifying | 反义词：militarizing, fortifying


词源:  The word "demilitarizing" is derived from the prefix "de-" (meaning "to remove" or "to deprive of"), "military" (relating to the armed forces), and the suffix "-izing" (indicating the process of making something).  Therefore, it literally means "the process of removing military characteristics or functions".
