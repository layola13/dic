parsimony:/ˈpɑːrsɪməni/| n. |吝啬；节俭；节约；俭省 |Extreme unwillingness to spend money or use resources; stinginess.  The noun "parsimony" refers to the quality of being excessively frugal or miserly. It implies a reluctance to spend money or resources even when it would be reasonable or beneficial to do so. |His parsimony prevented him from enjoying the comforts of life.  他的吝啬使他无法享受生活的舒适。| frugality, thrift, economy | extravagance, profligacy, generosity | 名词
