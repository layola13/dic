disdainable: /dɪsˈdeɪnəbəl/ | adj. | 令人鄙视的，可鄙的 | Deserving contempt or scorn; contemptible.  |例句：His behavior was utterly disdainable. 他的行为完全令人鄙视。

例句：I find his arrogance disdainable. 我觉得他的傲慢令人鄙视。


近义词：contemptible, despicable, despicable, shameful, reprehensible | 反义词：admirable, laudable, commendable

词源:  From disdain + -able.  "Disdain" itself comes from Old French *desdaigner, from des- ("from," "away from") + daigner ("to deign," "to think worthy").  The suffix "-able" indicates something that is capable of being or worthy of.
