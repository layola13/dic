neoconservatives:/ˌniːoʊkənˈsɜːrvətɪvz/| n. |新保守主义者|New conservatives are a group of politically conservative individuals who advocate for policies that are often characterized by a strong belief in military strength, a skeptical view of international organizations, and a preference for free-market capitalism.  They generally favor assertive foreign policy and a more limited role for government in domestic affairs.  Their ideology stems from a reaction against the perceived failures of liberal policies, particularly in the 1960s and 1970s. |Example:  The neoconservatives' influence on foreign policy was evident in the lead-up to the Iraq War. 新保守主义者对伊拉克战争之前的对外政策影响显而易见。|近义词：conservatives (but with a stronger emphasis on military and assertive foreign policy), hawks|反义词：liberals, progressives, doves
