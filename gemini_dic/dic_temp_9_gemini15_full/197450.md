sheepherders:/ˈʃiːpˌhɜːrdərz/ | n. |牧羊人 |People who herd sheep.  They are responsible for the care and management of a flock of sheep, often in remote or rural areas.  Their duties typically involve guiding the sheep to pasture, protecting them from predators, and ensuring their overall well-being. |例句：The sheepherders led their flocks to the lush green pastures. 牧羊人带领他们的羊群前往青翠的牧场。

The sheepherders worked tirelessly throughout the day, moving the sheep from one field to another. 牧羊人整天辛勤工作，将羊群从一个牧场转移到另一个牧场。|近义词：shepherds, stockmen |反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with city dwellers or office workers.
