liquidated:/ˈlɪkwɪdeɪtɪd/| adj. v. | 清算的；被清算的；偿付的；变现的 | To convert assets into cash; to settle a debt; to dissolve a business; to kill. |The company was liquidated after it filed for bankruptcy.  这家公司申请破产后被清算。|近义词：settled, dissolved, extinguished, terminated |反义词：established, founded, created

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company liquidates its assets every year.  这家公司每年都会清算其资产。
They liquidate their debts promptly.  他们迅速偿还债务。


一般过去时:
The court liquidated the company's holdings. 法院清算了公司的资产。
He liquidated his investments to pay off his debts. 他变现了他的投资来偿还债务。


现在完成时:
The bank has liquidated all the bad loans.  银行已经清算所有不良贷款。
They have liquidated their partnership. 他们已经解散了他们的合伙企业。


过去完成时:
The business had liquidated its assets before the lawsuit.  在诉讼之前，这家企业已经清算其资产。
They had liquidated their savings before the emergency. 他们在紧急情况发生前已用光了积蓄。


将来时:
The firm will liquidate its remaining assets.  该公司将清算其剩余资产。
We will liquidate our debts within the year. 我们将在一年内偿清债务。


虚拟语气:
If the company were to be liquidated, many people would lose their jobs. 如果公司被清算，许多人将失去工作。
I wish I had liquidated my stocks before the market crash. 我真希望我在市场崩盘前已经清算我的股票。


被动语态:
The assets were liquidated by the court.  这些资产由法院清算。
The business was liquidated due to financial difficulties.  由于财务困难，这家企业被清算。


疑问句:
Has the company been liquidated yet?  这家公司已经被清算了吗？
Will the assets be liquidated soon?  这些资产会很快被清算吗？


否定句:
The company has not yet been liquidated. 这家公司尚未被清算。
The debts were not fully liquidated.  债务没有完全偿清。
