self-quotation:/ˌself kwouˈteɪʃən/| n. |自我引用；自我引述 |The act of quoting oneself, either directly or indirectly.  This can involve repeating one's own words or ideas, often for emphasis or to create a particular effect. Self-quotation can be found in various forms of writing and speech, from academic papers to everyday conversations. |Example:  The author's self-quotation throughout the book felt repetitive and self-indulgent.  作者在书中反复自我引用，感觉重复且自负。|近义词：repetition, reiteration |反义词：originality, novelty
