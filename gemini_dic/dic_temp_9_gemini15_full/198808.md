side-on:/ˈsaɪdɒn/| adj.|侧面；侧向的|Seen or viewed from the side.  Describes something presented or viewed from a sideways perspective, rather than directly from the front or back.|例句：The car was hit side-on. 这辆车侧面被撞了。  The building looked impressive side-on. 这座建筑物从侧面看很壮观。|近义词：lateral, sideways, oblique|反义词：frontal, head-on


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The car is parked side-on. = 车子侧着停着。
The building presents a striking appearance side-on. = 这栋建筑物从侧面看非常引人注目。


一般过去时:
The cyclist was hit side-on by a car. = 骑自行车的人被一辆车侧面撞了。
We viewed the sculpture side-on to appreciate its unique design. = 我们从侧面观看雕塑以欣赏其独特的设计。

The artist has painted the subject side-on many times. = 艺术家多次从侧面绘制了这个主题。

疑问句:

否定句:
The photograph was not taken side-on; it was a frontal shot. = 这张照片不是从侧面拍摄的；它是正面照。
The artist did not paint the model side-on; she was facing the viewer. = 艺术家没有从侧面画模特；她是面对观众的。
