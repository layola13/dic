preanticipating: /ˌpriːænˈtɪsɪpeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 预期；预料；先发制人地 |  This word is a present participle form of the verb "preanticipate," meaning to anticipate something before it is even anticipated by others; to act proactively to address a potential event. It implies a higher level of foresight and proactive planning than simple anticipation. | 例句：The company was preanticipating potential supply chain disruptions by securing alternative suppliers.  这家公司通过确保替代供应商来预先应对潜在的供应链中断。


The general manager, preanticipating a market downturn, implemented cost-cutting measures. 总经理预料到市场低迷，实施了削减成本的措施。| 近义词：foreseeing, preempting, proactively addressing | 反义词：neglecting, ignoring, reacting  | 动词
