prefrozen:/ˌpriːˈfroʊzən/| adj.|预先冷冻的；预冷冻的 |Pre-frozen refers to something that has been frozen before being sold or used. It implies that the freezing process happened before the item reached its current state or point of sale.|例句：The pre-frozen vegetables are convenient for quick meals. 预先冷冻的蔬菜方便快捷地做饭。  The restaurant uses only pre-frozen seafood to ensure freshness. 餐厅只使用预先冷冻的海鲜以确保新鲜。|近义词：frozen, previously frozen |反义词：fresh, unfrozen
