panslavism: /ˈpænˌslɑːvɪzəm/ | n. |泛斯拉夫主义|The political ideology advocating the unity of all Slavic peoples.  It encompasses various forms, from cultural and linguistic cooperation to the creation of a single Slavic state.  The movement gained momentum in the 19th century, fueled by shared cultural heritage and a desire for independence from foreign powers. However, its pursuit often led to conflict and internal divisions among Slavic nations themselves.|

例句：

The rise of panslavism in the 19th century significantly impacted the political landscape of Eastern Europe.  = 19世纪泛斯拉夫主义的兴起极大地影响了东欧的政治格局。

Panslavism played a crucial role in the events leading up to World War I. = 泛斯拉夫主义在导致第一次世界大战的事件中扮演了至关重要的角色。


近义词：Slavic unity, Slavic solidarity

反义词：Slavic division, national separatism
