microculture:/ˈmaɪkroʊkʌltʃər/| n. |微文化；亚文化；小组文化 |A microculture is a smaller group within a larger culture that shares distinct values, beliefs, behaviors, and practices.  It often exists within a dominant culture but maintains its own unique identity.  Examples might include a specific ethnic group within a nation, a professional organization, or a particular social club. |Example:  The company fostered a microculture of innovation, encouraging employees to experiment with new ideas. 公司营造了一种创新的小组文化，鼓励员工尝试新想法。|近义词：subculture, subgroup, community |反义词：macroculture, mainstream culture
