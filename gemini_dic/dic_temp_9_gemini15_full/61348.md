embellisher:/ɪmˈbɛlɪʃər/ | n. | 装饰者；美化者 | A person or thing that embellishes something.  An embellisher adds decorative or superficial details to something, often to make it more attractive or impressive. | The artist was a skilled embellisher, adding intricate details to her paintings.  这位艺术家是一位技艺精湛的装饰者，在她的绘画中添加了复杂的细节。|  decorator, ornamenter, beautifier | simplifier, plain speaker | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He embellishes his stories to make them more interesting. = 他美化他的故事以使它们更有趣。
She embellishes her apartment with colorful decorations. = 她用色彩鲜艳的装饰品装饰她的公寓。


一般过去时:
The writer embellished the facts to create a more dramatic narrative. = 作家美化了事实以创造更戏剧性的叙述。
He embellished his resume to impress potential employers. = 他美化了他的简历以打动潜在的雇主。


现在完成时:
She has embellished her garden with beautiful flowers. = 她已经用美丽的鲜花装饰了她的花园。
They have embellished the report with unnecessary details. = 他们已经用不必要的细节美化了报告。


过去完成时:
He had embellished his account of the incident to protect himself. = 他已经美化了他对事件的描述以保护自己。
The artist had embellished the portrait with gold leaf. = 艺术家已经用金箔装饰了肖像。
