injection:/ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən/ | n. |注射；注入；注射剂 |The act of injecting a substance into something, or the substance injected.  It can refer to the act of injecting a liquid into a body, or the injection of funds into a business. |例句：The doctor gave the patient an injection.  医生给病人注射了一针。

例句：The government's injection of capital saved the failing company.  政府注资拯救了这家即将倒闭的公司。|近义词：inoculation, infusion, shot |反义词：extraction, removal


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The nurse gives injections every day. = 护士每天都打针。
He regularly injects insulin. = 他定期注射胰岛素。


一般过去时:
She received an injection yesterday. = 她昨天打了一针。
They injected the dye into the vein. = 他们将染料注入静脉。


现在完成时:
I have had many injections in my life. = 我一生中打过很多针。
The company has injected millions into the project. = 公司已向该项目注资数百万美元。


过去完成时:
The patient had already received two injections before the doctor arrived. = 在医生到达之前，病人已经打了兩针。
The business had injected significant funds before the crisis. = 在危机爆发前，企业已经注入了大量的资金。


将来时:

They will inject more money into the research. = 他们将向研究投入更多资金。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:
