regained:/riˈɡeɪnd/| vt.|重新获得，恢复|To get something back that was lost or taken away.  It implies a previous possession or state that has been restored.|例句：He regained consciousness after the accident. 他在事故后恢复了意识。

例句：She regained her composure after the initial shock. 她最初的震惊过后恢复了镇静。


近义词：recovered, repossessed, retrieved, recouped|反义词：lost, forfeited


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He regains his strength slowly. = 他慢慢地恢复了体力。
She regains her composure quickly after a stressful event. = 她在压力事件后迅速恢复镇静。


一般过去时:
The team regained the lead in the final minutes. = 球队在最后几分钟重新取得领先。
He regained his sight after the surgery. = 他手术后恢复了视力。


现在完成时:
She has regained her confidence after overcoming her fear. = 她克服恐惧后重拾信心。
They have regained control of the situation. = 他们已经重新控制了局面。


过去完成时:
By the time he reached the summit, he had regained his breath. = 等他到达山顶时，他已经恢复了呼吸。
Before the final round, she had regained her focus. = 在最后一轮比赛前，她已经重新集中注意力。


将来时:
He will regain his full mobility after the rehabilitation. = 康复后他会恢复全部行动能力。
The company expects to regain its market share next year. = 公司预计明年将重新获得市场份额。


虚拟语气:
If he had trained harder, he might have regained his title. = 如果他训练更刻苦，他可能已经夺回了冠军。
I wish I could regain my youth. = 我真希望我能恢复青春。


被动语态:
The stolen painting was eventually regained by the police. = 失窃的画最终被警方追回。
His lost trust cannot be easily regained. = 他失去的信任难以轻易挽回。


疑问句:
Has she regained her appetite? = 她胃口恢复了吗？
Will they ever regain their former glory? = 他们还能恢复昔日的辉煌吗？


否定句:
He has not yet regained full use of his arm. = 他还没有完全恢复手臂的使用功能。
Despite the effort, they failed to regain their lost territory. = 尽管努力，他们还是没能夺回失去的领土。
