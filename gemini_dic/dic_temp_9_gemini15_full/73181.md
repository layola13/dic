forbids:/fɚˈbɪdz/|/fəˈbɪdz/| vt.|禁止，不准|To officially prevent something from happening.|The law forbids smoking in public places. 法律禁止在公共场所吸烟。The doctor forbids him from drinking alcohol. 医生禁止他喝酒。|近义词：prohibit, ban, prevent|反义词：allow, permit|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The school forbids the use of mobile phones. 学校禁止使用手机。
The rules forbid eating in the classroom.  规定禁止在教室里吃饭。


一般过去时:
The king forbade the publication of the book. 国王禁止出版这本书。
My parents forbade me from going to the party. 我父母禁止我去参加派对。


现在完成时:
The government has forbidden the import of certain goods. 政府已经禁止进口某些商品。
She has forbidden her children from watching violent movies. 她一直禁止她的孩子们观看暴力电影。


过去完成时:
He had forbidden her from contacting him. 他以前禁止她联系他。
They had forbidden access to the building. 他们之前禁止进入该建筑物。


将来时:
The council will forbid the construction of new buildings. 理事会将禁止建造新的建筑物。
They will forbid entry without proper identification. 他们将禁止未经适当身份证明进入。


虚拟语气:
If they forbade smoking here, the air would be cleaner. 如果他们禁止在这里吸烟，空气会更清洁。
I wish they forbade the use of plastic bags. 我希望他们禁止使用塑料袋。


被动语态:
Smoking is forbidden in this area.  这个区域禁止吸烟。
The use of cameras is forbidden. 禁止使用照相机。


疑问句:
Does the law forbid the sale of alcohol to minors? 法律是否禁止向未成年人出售酒精？
Does this sign forbid parking here? 这个标志是否禁止在此停车？


否定句:
The rules do not forbid bringing your own lunch. 规则并没有禁止自带午餐。
The regulations don't forbid the use of bicycles on the path. 规定没有禁止在小路上骑自行车。
