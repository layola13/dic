fall-out: /ˈfɔːlˌaʊt/ | n. | 后果，辐射尘，争吵 | The consequences or results of an action or event, especially undesirable ones; radioactive particles dispersed into the atmosphere by a nuclear explosion; a quarrel or disagreement. |例句：The fallout from the scandal damaged his reputation.  这场丑闻的后果损害了他的名誉。 |近义词：consequence, aftermath, repercussions, fallout (radioactive), quarrel, dispute |反义词：resolution, agreement, harmony


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The fallout from the decision is unpredictable. = 决定的后果是不可预测的。
Nuclear fallout poses a serious threat. = 核辐射尘构成了严重的威胁。

一般过去时:
The fallout from the election was immediate. = 选举的后果是立即显现的。
There was considerable fallout after the argument. = 争吵之后产生了相当大的后果。

现在完成时:

We have seen the devastating fallout of the war. = 我们已经看到了战争的毁灭性后果。

过去完成时:

将来时:
The fallout will likely be severe. = 后果很可能会很严重。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

The environment is being damaged by nuclear fallout. = 环境正在受到核辐射尘的破坏。

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "fallout" originated in the 1950s, referring to the radioactive particles that fell to earth after nuclear explosions.  The word later expanded its meaning to encompass the broader consequences of any action or event.  It is a compound word formed from "fall" and "out".
