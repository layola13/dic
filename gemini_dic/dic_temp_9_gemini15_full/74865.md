fresco:/ˈfrɛskoʊ/| n.|壁画；湿壁画|A fresco is a painting done rapidly in watercolor on wet plaster on a wall or ceiling, so that the colors penetrate the plaster and become very durable.|The Sistine Chapel is famous for its frescoes.|近义词：mural, painting|反义词：


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The artist paints a fresco on the church wall. = 艺术家在教堂墙上画壁画。
This fresco depicts a biblical scene. = 这幅壁画描绘了一个圣经场景。


一般过去时:
Michelangelo painted many frescoes in the Sistine Chapel. = 米开朗基罗在西斯廷教堂绘制了许多壁画。
They restored the damaged fresco last year. = 他们去年修复了受损的壁画。


现在完成时:
The museum has acquired several new frescoes. = 博物馆已经收购了几幅新的壁画。
They have carefully preserved the ancient frescoes. = 他们小心翼翼地保存着古代的壁画。


过去完成时:
The fresco had already been damaged before they started restoration. = 在他们开始修复之前，壁画就已经受损了。
The artist had completed the majority of the fresco before the deadline. = 在截止日期之前，艺术家已经完成了大部分壁画。


将来时:
The team will paint a large fresco in the town square. = 该团队将在城镇广场绘制一幅大型壁画。

虚拟语气:
If I were an artist, I would paint a fresco. = 如果我是艺术家，我会画一幅壁画。
I wish I could see the frescoes in Pompeii. = 我希望我能看到庞贝的壁画。


被动语态:

疑问句:
