fundamental: /ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl/ | adj., n. | 基础的；根本的；要素 |  Something that is fundamental is basic and necessary; it forms the foundation or base of something else.  It is a primary or essential element. |

例句：

The fundamental principles of democracy are freedom and equality. = 民主的基本原则是自由和平等。

Education is fundamental to a better future. = 教育是更好未来的基础。


近义词：basic, essential, primary, underlying, foundational

反义词：superficial, trivial, inconsequential, peripheral


词性:  adjective, noun

词源:

The word "fundamental" comes from the Late Latin word "fundamentalis," meaning "belonging to a foundation," derived from the Latin word "fundamentum," meaning "foundation,"  itself from "fundare," meaning "to found" or "to establish."  The evolution reflects the core meaning of the word: something that forms the base or foundation of something else.
