graunt:/ɡrɑːnt/| v.|授予，给予，准许|To bestow or confer something on someone.|The king granted him a title.|近义词：bestow, award, concede|反义词：deny, refuse, withhold|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grants her permission. = 他准许她。
The university grants scholarships. = 大学提供奖学金。

一般过去时:
She granted my request. = 她答应了我的请求。
The government granted them asylum. = 政府给予他们政治庇护。

现在完成时:
They have granted him a visa. = 他们已经给他签证了。
The committee has granted funding for the project. = 委员会已经批准了该项目的资金。

过去完成时:
He had already granted the wish before she asked. = 她还没开口要之前，他已经答应了她的愿望。
The court had granted her a divorce before he appealed. = 法院在他上诉之前就已经判决她离婚了。

将来时:
They will grant him a hearing. = 他们将给他一次听证会。
The council will grant planning permission next week. = 理事会下周将批准规划许可。


虚拟语气:
If only she would grant me one wish. =  但愿她能满足我一个愿望。
I wish he would grant me an interview. = 我希望他能给我一次面试机会。


被动语态:
Permission was granted to the protestors. = 示威者获得了许可。
Funding was granted for the research project. = 研究项目获得了资金支持。


疑问句:
Will the judge grant the motion? = 法官会批准这项动议吗？
Did the bank grant him a loan? = 银行给他贷款了吗？


否定句:
He didn't grant her request. = 他没有答应她的请求。
The application was not granted. = 申请没有得到批准。


PHRASE->
grant a favor  = 帮个忙
grant a wish = 满足愿望
grant immunity = 授予豁免权
take for granted = 想当然
grant money = 拨款
