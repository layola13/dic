formulator:/ˈfɔːrmjuleɪtər/ | n. | 制定者；配方师；公式化者 | One who formulates; a person or thing that creates or develops a plan, system, or product.  A formulator in the context of cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, or food science is a specialist in creating formulas and recipes. | The team leader acted as the formulator of their strategy. 制定策略的是团队领导。The chemist is a skilled formulator of perfumes.  这位化学家是位熟练的香水配方师。 | developer, creator, architect |  deconstructor, dismantler


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He formulates a new marketing plan every year. 他每年都会制定一个新的营销计划。
She formulates her thoughts carefully before speaking.  她说话前会仔细地组织自己的思路。


一般过去时:
The company formulated a new product last year. 公司去年研发了一种新产品。
The scientist formulated a hypothesis to explain the phenomenon. 科学家制定了一个假设来解释这种现象。


现在完成时:
They have formulated a comprehensive strategy for the project. 他们已经为这个项目制定了一个全面的策略。
She has formulated her response to the criticism. 她已经拟好了对批评的回应。


过去完成时:
They had formulated their plans before the meeting began. 会议开始前他们已经制定好了计划。
The chef had formulated the recipe after years of experimentation.  这位厨师经过多年的实验才制定出这个食谱。


将来时:
We will formulate a detailed budget next week. 我们下周将制定一份详细的预算。
She will formulate her arguments before the debate.  她将在辩论前准备好她的论据。


虚拟语气:
If I were the formulator, I would choose a different approach. 如果我是制定者，我会选择不同的方法。
I wish I had formulated a better plan. 我希望我制定了一个更好的计划。


被动语态:
The policy was formulated by a committee of experts.  这项政策是由专家委员会制定的。
The new software is being formulated by a team of programmers. 新软件正在由一个程序员团队开发。


疑问句:
Who formulated this plan?  谁制定的这个计划？

否定句:
