queue up: /kjuː ˈʌp/ | phrasal verb | 排队；轮候 | To form or join a line of people or things waiting for something. | 例句：We had to queue up for hours to get tickets. 我们不得不排队几个小时才能买到票。

例句：Many people queued up outside the store before it opened. 许多人在商店开门前排起了长队。


近义词：line up, stand in line, wait in line | 反义词：N/A


词性: phrasal verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They queue up for the bus every morning. = 他们每天早上都排队等公交车。
People queue up to buy the latest iPhone. = 人们排队购买最新的iPhone。


一般过去时:
We queued up for hours to see the concert. = 我们为了看音乐会排了好几个小时的队。
They queued up patiently for their turn. = 他们耐心地排队等候轮到自己。


现在完成时:
They have queued up for over an hour already. = 他们已经排了一个多小时的队了。
She has queued up for her favourite coffee. = 她已经排队等候她最喜欢的咖啡了。


过去完成时:
We had queued up for a long time before the doors opened. = 门打开之前，我们已经排了很长时间的队了。
They had queued up all morning before the sale began. = 促销开始前，他们已经排队等候了一上午。


将来时:
We will queue up early to get good seats. = 我们会早点排队，以便拿到好座位。
They will queue up for the new movie release. = 他们将会排队观看新电影的首映。


虚拟语气:
If we queued up earlier, we would have gotten better seats. = 如果我们早点排队，就能拿到更好的座位了。
If they had queued up earlier, they wouldn't have missed the concert. = 如果他们早点排队，就不会错过音乐会了。


被动语态:
The customers were queued up efficiently. = 顾客们被有效地安排排队。
The tickets were distributed to those who had queued up. = 门票分发给了排队的人。


疑问句:
Are you queuing up for the same thing? = 你也排队买这个吗？
How long have you been queuing up? = 你排队多久了？


否定句:
They didn't queue up for the movie because they bought tickets online. = 他们没有排队看电影，因为他们在网上买了票。
We didn't queue up; we went to a different location. = 我们没有排队，我们去了另一个地方。
