segregation:/ˌseɡrɪˈɡeɪʃən/| n. |隔离；种族隔离 |The enforced separation of different racial groups in a country, community, or establishment.  It refers to the systemic separation of people based on their race or ethnicity, often involving unequal access to resources and opportunities. |例句：The Civil Rights Movement fought against racial segregation in the United States.  美国民权运动反对美国的种族隔离。|例句：School segregation was declared unconstitutional in the 1954 Brown v. Board of Education Supreme Court case.  1954年布朗诉教育委员会案最高法院判决学校隔离违宪。|近义词：separation, isolation, apartheid |反义词：integration, inclusion
