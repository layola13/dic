plateauing: /plæˈtoʊɪŋ/ | v. | 停滞不前；达到稳定状态 |  To reach a stage where further progress or development is very slow or stops completely.  This often refers to a period of stagnation in a career, skill development, or other area of progress. |

例句：

His career seems to be plateauing. = 他的事业似乎停滞不前了。

After years of rapid growth, the company's profits are plateauing. = 在多年的快速增长之后，公司的利润正在趋于平稳。


近义词：stagnating, leveling off, stalling, remaining static

反义词：progressing, developing, improving, advancing

词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is plateauing in his studies. = 他的学习停滞不前。
The economy is plateauing after a period of rapid growth. = 经济在经历了一段快速增长后趋于平稳。

一般过去时:
The project plateaued after the initial success. = 项目在最初的成功之后停滞不前了。
Her performance plateaued during the second half of the season. = 她在赛季下半段的表现停滞不前。

现在完成时:
The sales have plateaued since the new product launch. = 自从新产品发布以来，销量一直停滞不前。
He has plateaued in his weight loss journey. = 他减肥的旅程停滞不前了。

过去完成时:
The negotiations had plateaued before the final agreement was reached. = 在达成最终协议之前，谈判已经陷入僵局。
The stock price had plateaued for several months before the sudden drop. = 股价在暴跌之前已经停滞了几个月。

将来时:
The project is likely to plateau next year. = 该项目明年可能会停滞不前。
The researchers predict that the drug's effectiveness will plateau in the long term. = 研究人员预测该药物的疗效长期来看会停滞不前。

虚拟语气:
If he continues like this, he will plateau in his career. = 如果他继续这样下去，他的事业将会停滞不前。
I wish my progress hadn't plateaued so soon. = 我希望我的进步不会这么快就停滞不前。

被动语态:
Progress on the project was plateaued by unexpected delays. = 项目的进展因意外延误而停滞不前。
The development of the technology was plateaued by funding issues. = 该技术的开发因资金问题而停滞不前。

疑问句:
Is your learning plateauing? = 你的学习停滞了吗？
Has the market plateaued? = 市场停滞了吗？

否定句:
His skills are not plateauing; they are improving. = 他的技能没有停滞不前，而是在进步。
The company's growth is not plateauing; it's accelerating. = 公司的增长并没有停滞，而是在加速。
