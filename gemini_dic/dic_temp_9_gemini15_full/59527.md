eat-high-off-the-hog: /ˌiːt haɪ ˈɔːf ðə hɒɡ/ | v. phr. |痛痛快快地吃；尽情享乐 | To eat a large, lavish, and satisfying meal; to live luxuriously and enjoy oneself immensely.  This phrase originates from the practice of Southern US barbecue, where the most prized cuts of meat were those from the hog's upper body ("high on the hog"). Eating these parts indicated high social status and abundance. |例句：They ate high off the hog at the Thanksgiving feast.  他们感恩节盛宴上吃得非常尽兴。

They celebrated their success by eating high off the hog at a fancy restaurant. 他们在一个高档餐厅大吃一顿来庆祝他们的成功。|近义词：feast, gorge, revel, indulge |反义词：starve, scrimp, deprive
