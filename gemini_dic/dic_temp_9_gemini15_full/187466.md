roll:/roʊl/ /rəʊl/| n., v.| 卷，滚动；角色；名单 | to move by turning over and over; a list or register; a part played by an actor | The ball rolled down the hill.  球滚下了山。| The teacher called the roll. 老师点名。|近义词：turn, revolve, scroll, list | 反义词：unroll, unfold


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ball rolls down the hill. = 球滚下山。
He rolls the dough before baking. = 他在烘焙前擀面团。


一般过去时:
She rolled the dice. = 她掷骰子。
The car rolled over several times. = 车子翻了好几圈。


现在完成时:
The snow has rolled into large drifts. = 雪已经堆成了大雪堆。
I have rolled the film into the camera. = 我已经把胶卷卷进相机了。


过去完成时:
The stone had rolled down the mountain before we arrived. = 我们到达之前石头已经滚下山了。
They had rolled out the red carpet for the VIP guests. = 他们为贵宾铺上了红地毯。


将来时:
He will roll the ball to his dog. = 他会把球滚给他的狗。
The company will roll out a new product next month. = 公司下个月将推出新产品。


虚拟语气:
If I rolled the dice, I might win. = 如果我掷骰子，我可能会赢。
I wish I had rolled a higher number. = 我希望我掷出一个更大的数字。


被动语态:
The dough was rolled thinly. = 面团被擀得很薄。
The logs were rolled into the river. = 原木被滚进了河里。


疑问句:
Will you roll the window down? = 你要摇下车窗吗？
How many times did the ball roll? = 球滚了几次？


否定句:
The car didn't roll far. = 车没滚多远。
He didn't roll his eyes at me. = 他没有翻白眼看我。



PHRASE->
roll over = 翻身
roll out = 推出（新产品等）
roll with the punches = 灵活应变
roll in = 大量涌入
roll up = 卷起
