nonadaptations:/ˌnɒnˌædəpˈteɪʃənz/| n.|非适应性，非适应特征|Features or traits of an organism that are not adaptations, meaning they do not enhance survival or reproduction.  They may be neutral or even detrimental.  They can arise through various mechanisms, including random genetic drift, pleiotropy (where a gene affects multiple traits), or historical contingency.|The presence of vestigial organs, like the human appendix, is often cited as an example of nonadaptation.  The color of an organism might also be a nonadaptation in certain environments.|近义词：non-adaptive traits, neutral traits|反义词：adaptations
