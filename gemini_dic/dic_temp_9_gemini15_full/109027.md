leg:/lɛɡ/| n.|腿，腿部|A leg is one of the two limbs that support a person or animal and enable them to stand and walk.  It also refers to a similar part of a piece of furniture.|例句：The child scraped his leg on the pavement.  孩子在人行道上擦伤了腿。|例句：The table has four legs. 这张桌子有四条腿。|近义词：limb, shank|反义词：arm, hand


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has strong legs. = 他腿很强壮。
The table has three legs. = 这张桌子有三条腿。

一般过去时:
She broke her leg last year. = 她去年摔断了腿。
The chair lost a leg. = 椅子少了一条腿。

现在完成时:
I have hurt my leg. = 我伤了我的腿。
The dog has broken its leg. = 这只狗摔断了腿。

过去完成时:
He had already injured his leg before the race. = 他在比赛前就已经伤了腿。
The old chair had lost two legs. = 那把旧椅子丢了俩腿。

将来时:
I will need crutches if I break my leg. = 如果我摔断了腿，我就需要拐杖。
They will repair the broken leg of the table. = 他们会修理桌子断掉的腿。

虚拟语气:
If I had stronger legs, I could run a marathon. = 如果我的腿更强壮，我就能跑马拉松了。
If the table had four legs, it would be more stable. = 如果桌子有四条腿，它会更稳定。

被动语态:
Her leg was broken in the accident. = 她的腿在事故中断了。
The table's leg was replaced. = 桌子的腿被替换了。

疑问句:
Do you have a sore leg? = 你的腿疼吗？
How many legs does a spider have? = 蜘蛛有几条腿？

否定句:
He doesn't have a limp in his leg. = 他的腿没有跛。
The stool doesn't have enough legs to stand properly. = 凳子腿不够，站不稳。
