donatist: /ˈdoʊnəɪst/ | n. | 多纳图斯派信徒；多纳图斯主义者 | A follower of Donatism, a 4th-century North African Christian sect that emphasized the importance of the moral purity of the clergy and rejected the validity of sacraments administered by morally flawed priests.  | The Donatists believed that the church should be composed only of saints, and that sacraments performed by unworthy ministers were invalid. | 近义词：Donatist, schismatic | 反义词：Catholic (in the context of 4th-century Christianity)
