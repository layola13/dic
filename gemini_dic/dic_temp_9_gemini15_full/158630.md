plicature:/ˈplɪkətʃər/| n. |蕴含意义，含蓄表达|A plicature is an implicature derived not from the conventional meaning of words, but from the way in which they are used in a particular context.  It's a subtle, often unintended, meaning conveyed through the speaker's choice of words and the overall conversational context.  It's a nuanced aspect of pragmatics, going beyond the literal meaning of an utterance.|The speaker's sarcastic tone created a plicature of disapproval, even though the words themselves were neutral.  |近义词：implicature, implication, suggestion|反义词：explicit statement, direct meaning|名词
