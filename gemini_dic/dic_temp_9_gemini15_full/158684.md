plosive:/ˈplɒsɪv/| /ˈploʊsɪv/| n.| 爆破音 | A plosive is a consonant sound produced by completely stopping the airflow in the vocal tract, then releasing it suddenly.  This creates a brief burst of sound.  Examples include /p/, /b/, /t/, /d/, /k/, and /ɡ/ in English. | The word "pop" contains two plosive sounds, /p/ and /p/.|近义词：stop consonant|反义词：fricative, continuant
