meritocratic: /ˌmɛrɪtəˈkrætɪk/ | adj. | 精英统治的；能力至上的 |  Based on or characterized by meritocracy; a system in which success is based on ability and talent rather than on wealth or social class.  |  例句：The company boasts a meritocratic culture where promotions are based on performance. 这家公司拥有精英管理文化，晋升基于业绩。 | 近义词：elitist, deserved, based on merit | 反义词：plutocratic, aristocratic, nepotistic

词源:
The word "meritocratic" is a relatively modern coinage, blending "merit" (deserving reward or recognition) with "-cracy" (rule or government).  It reflects the growing emphasis in the 20th century on assessing individuals based on their abilities and accomplishments rather than inherited status.
