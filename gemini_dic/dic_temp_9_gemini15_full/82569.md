guaranty:/ɡærənˈtiː/| n. |保证；担保；保证书 | A guaranty is a promise or assurance, especially one given in writing, that something will be done or will happen.  It often involves a commitment to compensate for a loss or failure. | The bank required a personal guaranty before approving the loan.  银行在批准贷款之前要求提供个人担保。 | warranty, assurance, pledge | breach of guaranty, failure | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company guarantees its products for one year. = 公司对其产品保证一年。
This contract guarantees your rights. = 这份合同保障您的权利。


一般过去时:
They guaranteed the loan to the small business. = 他们向这家小企业担保了贷款。
He guaranteed the success of the project. = 他保证项目会成功。


现在完成时:
The manufacturer has guaranteed the quality of its goods. = 制造商已保证其产品的质量。
We have guaranteed the timely delivery of the goods. = 我们已保证货物会准时送达。


过去完成时:
The seller had guaranteed the buyer a full refund. = 卖家已向买家保证全额退款。
They had guaranteed the performance of the machine. = 他们已保证机器的性能。


将来时:
The company will guarantee the performance of the software. = 公司将保证软件的性能。
We will guarantee that your money is safe.  = 我们将保证您的资金安全。


虚拟语气:
If the company guaranteed a refund, I would buy the product. = 如果公司保证退款，我会购买该产品。
I wish they had guaranteed the quality of the service. = 我希望他们当时保证了服务的质量。


被动语态:
The loan was guaranteed by a third party. = 这笔贷款由第三方担保。
The product's quality is guaranteed by the manufacturer. = 产品的质量由制造商保证。


疑问句:
Does the warranty guarantee parts and labor? = 保修是否包括零件和人工？
Does this policy guarantee my privacy? = 这项政策是否保障我的隐私？


否定句:
The company did not guarantee the product's longevity. = 公司没有保证产品的寿命。
They didn't guarantee the accuracy of the information. = 他们没有保证信息的准确性。
