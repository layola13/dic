intervening: /ˌɪntərˈviːnɪŋ/ | adj. | 干预的；介入的；中间的 | Coming between; occurring in the time between; mediating.  |例句：The intervening years saw significant changes in the company.  这些年间公司发生了巨大的变化。

例句：An intervening event prevented them from meeting.  一件突发事件阻止了他们的会面。


近义词：interceding, mediating, mediating, intervening, intermediate | 反义词：final, ultimate, concluding


词源:  From Middle English *intervenyng, from Old French intervenir, from Latin intervenire "to come between," from inter "between" + venire "to come."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government intervenes in the economy. = 政府干预经济。
He intervenes in every argument. = 他插手每一场争论。

一般过去时:
She intervened to stop the fight. = 她介入阻止了这场争斗。
The police intervened in the dispute. = 警察介入调解了这场纠纷。

现在完成时:
They have intervened several times in the conflict. = 他们已经几次介入这场冲突。
The UN has intervened in numerous humanitarian crises. = 联合国已经介入过许多人道主义危机。

过去完成时:
He had intervened before the situation got worse. = 在情况恶化之前，他已经介入了。
The judge had intervened to protect the witness. = 法官已经介入保护证人。

将来时:
The army will intervene if necessary. = 必要时军队将进行干预。
We will intervene if the situation escalates. = 如果情况恶化，我们将进行干预。

虚拟语气:

I wish someone had intervened to help us. = 我希望有人介入帮助我们。

否定句:
