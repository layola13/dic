depress:/dɪˈprɛs/ | vt. vi. adj. | 使沮丧；使萧条；降低；压抑；使低落 | to make someone feel very unhappy and without hope | 近义词：sadden, discourage, deject | 反义词：elate, uplift, encourage |


英文解析:
As a verb, "depress" means to lower someone's spirits, causing feelings of sadness, dejection, or hopelessness.  It can also refer to lowering something physically, such as lowering a lever or depressing a button.  As an adjective, it means feeling low in spirits or experiencing a period of economic downturn.

例句:
The bad news deeply depressed her.  坏消息使她非常沮丧。

The economic recession depressed the housing market.  经济衰退压低了房地产市场。

词源:
The word "depress" comes from the Latin word "deprimere," which means "to press down."  The prefix "de-" means "down," and "primere" means "to press."  The meaning evolved from the literal sense of pressing down to the figurative sense of lowering spirits or suppressing something.
