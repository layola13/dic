parasymbiont: /ˌpærəˈsɪmbaɪɒnt/ | n. |共生生物，偏利共生生物 | A parasymbiont is an organism that lives in close association with another organism, benefiting from the relationship while having little or no effect on its host.  Unlike mutualistic symbionts, parasymbionts do not provide reciprocal benefits to their hosts.  They can be either commensal (neither harming nor benefiting the host) or slightly beneficial to the host. | Example: Certain bacteria residing in the human gut are considered parasymbionts; they benefit from the nutrients available but may not significantly impact the host's health. |近义词：commensal, symbiont |反义词：parasite, pathogen
