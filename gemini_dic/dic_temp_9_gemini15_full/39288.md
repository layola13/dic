conhydrine:/kɒnˈhaɪdriːn/ | n. | 东莨菪碱 | Conhydrine is a tropane alkaloid found in poison hemlock (Conium maculatum). It is a chiral molecule, existing as two enantiomers.  It has toxic effects similar to coniine, another alkaloid found in the same plant. |  The toxic effects of conhydrine are similar to those of coniine, causing paralysis and potentially death. | 近义词: coniine (毒芹碱) | 反义词:  (无直接反义词，可考虑解毒剂) | 名词
