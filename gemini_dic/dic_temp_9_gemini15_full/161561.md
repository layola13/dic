potshotting: /ˈpɑːtʃɒtɪŋ/ | v. |  随意射击；随意批评；信口开河 |  The act of making casual or ill-considered criticisms or attacks; taking potshots.  It often implies a lack of serious intent or preparation. |

例句：

He's constantly potshotting the government in his blog. = 他在他的博客里不断地随意批评政府。

The comedian spent much of his act potshotting celebrities. = 喜剧演员在表演中大部分时间都在随意调侃名流。


近义词：criticizing, sniping, attacking, belittling, lambasting


反义词：praising, complimenting, supporting, endorsing


词源:

The word originates from the phrase "pot shot," which refers to an informal or unplanned shot at a target, often with a simple firearm like a shotgun.  The "-ing" suffix transforms it into a gerund, signifying the act of doing so.  The "pot" likely refers to a simple, easily available container, perhaps implying the casual nature of the action.
