preconcerted:/ˌpriːkənˈsɜːtɪd/| adj.| 预先商定的；预先安排的 | Preconcerted means planned or arranged in advance. It implies a degree of secrecy or collusion, suggesting that the plan is not openly known or declared.  |例句：Their meeting wasn't accidental; it was a preconcerted rendezvous.  他们的会面并非偶然；那是预先约好的会合。

Their actions seemed spontaneous, but in reality, they were preconcerted. 他们的行为看起来很自然，但实际上是预先策划好的。|近义词：prearranged, planned, arranged, concerted |反义词：spontaneous, unplanned, impromptu
