precriticize:/ˌpriːkrɪˈtaɪz/| v.|预先批评；事先批判|To criticize something before it has been completed or released.|The film was precriticized for its controversial plot before its release.  这部电影在上映前就因为其有争议的情节而受到了预先批评。|近义词：prejudge, prejudicate|反义词：post-criticize (not a common word)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They precriticize every new policy before implementation. 他们在实施任何新政策之前都会事先进行批评。
He precriticizes his own work to ensure high quality. 他预先批评自己的作品以确保高质量。


一般过去时:
The committee precriticized the proposal, delaying the project. 委员会预先批评了该提案，从而延误了项目。
She precriticized his speech, offering constructive feedback. 她预先批评了他的演讲，并提供了建设性的反馈。


现在完成时:
They have precriticized the design several times, leading to many revisions. 他们已经多次预先批评了这个设计，导致了很多修改。
She has precriticized the manuscript and provided helpful suggestions. 她已经预先批评了手稿并提供了有益的建议。


过去完成时:
The author had precriticized his own work, anticipating potential criticism. 作者预先批评了自己的作品，预料到了潜在的批评。
They had precriticized the plan, so they were prepared for objections. 他们预先批评了这个计划，所以他们已经准备好应对反对意见。


将来时:
The critics will precriticize the album before it is released. 批评家们将在专辑发行前对其进行预先批评。
He will precriticize his essay to improve its clarity. 他将预先批评他的论文以提高其清晰度。


虚拟语气:
If they had precriticized the software, they might have found the bugs earlier. 如果他们预先批评了软件，他们可能就会更早地发现错误。
I wish I had precriticized my presentation before giving it. 我希望我在做演讲之前预先批评过我的演示文稿。


被动语态:
The proposal was precriticized by the board before approval. 该提案在获得批准之前受到了董事会的预先批评。

疑问句:

否定句:

The author did not precriticize her novel before publication. 作者在出版前没有预先批评她的小说。
