dialectician:/ˌdaɪəˈlɛktɪʃən/| n. |方言学家，辩证学家 |A dialectician is a person skilled in or given to dialectic; someone who studies or is expert in dialects.  They might analyze the variations in language use across different regions or social groups, or they may be skilled in the art of logical argument and debate. |Example:  The renowned dialectician presented a compelling argument, skillfully navigating the complexities of the debate.  这位著名的辩证学家提出了一个令人信服的论点，巧妙地处理了辩论的复杂性。|近义词：linguist, logician, philosopher |反义词：None readily available.  The antonym would depend on the context, perhaps someone unskilled in logic or argument.
