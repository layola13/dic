quern:/kwɜːrn/| n.|磨石，手磨｜A quern is a hand-operated grain mill.  It consists of two stones, a lower stationary one and an upper rotating one, used for grinding grain into flour.  The upper stone is usually rotated by hand.  Querns were widely used before the invention of watermills and windmills. |The ancient Egyptians used querns to grind grain. 古代埃及人使用磨石来磨谷物。


The villagers used a quern to make flour for their bread. 村民们用磨石磨面粉做面包。|近义词：millstone (in the context of a hand-operated mill)|反义词：None specifically, but modern electric mills could be considered an antonym in terms of functionality.|名词

词源:
The word "quern" comes from Old English *cweorn,  ultimately from Proto-Germanic *kwernō.  Cognates exist in other Germanic languages (e.g., Old High German *chwerna, Old Norse *kvern). The word's origins are possibly linked to roots related to grinding or turning.
