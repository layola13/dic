galactose:/ɡəˈlæktoʊs/| n. |半乳糖|A type of sugar that is a component of lactose (milk sugar) and is found in many plants and animals. It is a monosaccharide, meaning it cannot be broken down into simpler sugars.  Galactose is an important component of glycolipids and glycoproteins, which are found on the surfaces of cells and play a role in cell recognition and communication.  It is also a precursor to other important molecules in the body.| Example:  Galactose is a crucial component of lactose, the sugar found in milk.|近义词：None (it's a specific type of sugar)|反义词：None (no direct opposite)|名词
