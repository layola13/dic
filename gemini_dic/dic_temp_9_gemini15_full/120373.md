minion:/ˈmɪnjən/| n. |（尤指权力人物的）追随者，爪牙；仆人；小人物；|A minion is a follower or underling, especially of a powerful person.  The term often carries a connotation of subservience and lack of independent thought.  |例句：He was merely a minion of the dictator. 他只是独裁者的爪牙。|例句：The queen surrounded herself with minions who obeyed her every whim. 女王身边围绕着一群对她言听计从的奴仆。|近义词：follower, henchman, lackey, subordinate, servant |反义词：leader, boss, superior


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a minion of the wealthy CEO. = 他是那位富有CEO的爪牙。
They are minions of the king. = 他们是国王的仆从。


一般过去时:
He was a loyal minion to the emperor. = 他是皇帝忠实的追随者。
She acted as a minion to her ambitious boss. = 她充当了她野心勃勃的老板的爪牙。


现在完成时:

She has served as a minion for many years. = 她做了多年的仆人。


过去完成时:

She had already become a minion of the new regime. = 她已经成为了新政权的追随者。

虚拟语气:
If he were not a minion, he would speak his mind. = 如果他不是个爪牙，他会说出自己的想法。
I wish I weren't a minion of this company. = 我真希望我不是这家公司的小人物。
