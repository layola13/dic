canon:/ˈkænən/| n. |规章；教规；正典；标准；佳作；大炮|A canon is a general law, rule, principle, or criterion by which something is judged.  It can refer to a body of accepted works (like the canon of Shakespeare's plays) or a church's official list of sacred books. It can also refer to a large gun. |例句：The school has a strict canon of conduct. 这所学校有严格的行为准则。

The novel quickly became part of the literary canon. 这部小说迅速成为文学经典。


近义词：rule, regulation, standard, principle, classic

反义词：irregularity, exception, deviation

词源:
From Old French *canon, from Late Latin canōn, from Greek kanōn ("straight rod, rule, standard").  The meaning evolved from a physical "rule" or "standard" to a metaphorical one representing principles and laws.
