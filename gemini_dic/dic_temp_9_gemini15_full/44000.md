criminalizations:/ˌkrɪmɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. | 犯罪化；定为犯罪 | The process of making something a crime.  It refers to the act of declaring an action or behavior illegal and punishable by law.  This often involves enacting new laws or reinterpreting existing ones to encompass the specific behavior. | Example: The increasing criminalization of drug use has led to mass incarceration.  越来越多的吸毒行为被定为犯罪，导致了大规模监禁。


The criminalization of minor offenses can overburden the justice system. 轻微罪行的犯罪化会使司法系统不堪重负。


近义词：incrimination, penalization | 反义词：decriminalization, legalization


词源:  Derived from the verb "criminalize," which itself comes from the noun "criminal" (from Old French *criminel, from Latin *criminalis, from crimen "accusation, charge") + the suffix "-ize" (meaning "to make or become").  The "-ation" suffix denotes the process or result of an action.
