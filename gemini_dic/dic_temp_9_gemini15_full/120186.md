minds:/maɪndz/ | n. |思想，智力；头脑；想法；意见 |The minds of the two scientists were focused on the same problem. 两位科学家的思想都集中在同一个问题上。 |近义词：intellect, brain, thoughts, opinions |反义词：unconsciousness, ignorance

USAGE->
一般现在时:
His mind wanders during long meetings. = 他在冗长的会议期间思绪会开小差。
What's on your mind? = 你在想什么？


一般过去时:
Her mind raced as she considered the options. = 她考虑各种选择时，思绪飞快地运转。
I changed my mind about going to the party. = 我改变了去参加派对的主意。


现在完成时:
I've made up my mind to quit my job. = 我已经下定决心辞职。
Have you changed your mind yet? = 你改变主意了吗？


过去完成时:
I had changed my mind before he called. = 他打电话之前我已经改变主意了。
She had already made up her mind to leave. = 她已经下定决心要离开了。


将来时:
I will change my mind if I receive better offer. = 如果我收到更好的提议，我会改变主意。
What do you think you will change your mind in the future? = 你认为将来你会改变什么主意？


虚拟语气:
If I had a brilliant mind, I would solve this problem. = 如果我有一个聪明的头脑，我就能解决这个问题。
I wish I had a better mind for business. = 我希望我更有经商的头脑。


被动语态:
The minds of the public were manipulated by the media. = 公众的思想被媒体操纵了。
His mind was already made up. = 他的主意已经打定了。


疑问句:
What's on your mind, dear? = 亲爱的，你在想什么？
What's the mind behind this plan? = 这个计划背后的想法是什么？


否定句:
He doesn't have the mind to do this. = 他没有做这件事的头脑。
I don't have a mind to argue with you. = 我不想和你争论。
