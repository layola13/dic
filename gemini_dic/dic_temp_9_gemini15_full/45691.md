curtsied:/ˈkɜːtsi/| v.| 行屈膝礼 | To perform a curtsy; to make a courtesy.  A curtsy is a respectful bow or gesture of greeting made by women. | Example: The young girl curtsied to the queen.  小女孩向女王行屈膝礼。 She curtsied gracefully before taking her seat.  她优雅地行了个屈膝礼才坐下。|近义词：bow, bob, dip|反义词：stand stiffly

PHRASE->

词源:
The word "curtsied" is the past tense of "curtsy," which ultimately derives from the Old French word "courtoisie," meaning "courtesy."  The evolution involved a shift from a noun denoting courtesy to a verb describing the act of showing courtesy through a specific gesture.
