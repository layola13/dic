hard-boiled: /ˈhɑːdˈbɔɪld/ | adj. | 煮得很老的；冷酷的；坚强的；现实的 |

英文解析:  The adjective "hard-boiled" literally refers to eggs cooked until the yolk is firm and solid.  Figuratively, it describes a person who is cynical, tough, and unemotional, often associated with a realistic and pragmatic worldview. It can also describe a style of fiction characterized by such characters and themes.

例句:

The eggs were hard-boiled and a little overcooked.  = 鸡蛋煮得很老，有点过头了。

He was a hard-boiled detective, unmoved by the suffering he witnessed. = 他是一个冷酷的侦探，他对目睹的痛苦无动于衷。


近义词: tough, cynical, pragmatic, unflappable, stoic, realistic

反义词: sentimental, naive, idealistic, emotional, soft-hearted


词源:  The term originates from the culinary sense of "hard-boiled eggs," and its figurative meaning emerged later, reflecting the perceived qualities of a person who is tough and unemotional like a hard-boiled egg.  The association with detective fiction solidified its usage in this context.
