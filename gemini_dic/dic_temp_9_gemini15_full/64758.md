escudo:/eˈskuːdoʊ/| n. |盾牌；埃斯库多（货币单位）|A shield; a former monetary unit of several countries.  The word derives from the Old Spanish word "escudo," meaning "shield."  It's used in a historical context referring to the currency, and figuratively to describe something protective.|例句：The knight carried his escudo into battle.  骑士带着他的盾牌上战场。|例句：Portugal used the escudo as its currency until adopting the euro.  葡萄牙在采用欧元之前使用埃斯库多作为其货币。|近义词：shield, buckler, aegis |反义词：None specifically, but  "vulnerability" could be considered an antonym in the figurative sense.
