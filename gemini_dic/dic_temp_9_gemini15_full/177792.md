receipts:/rɪˈsiːts/| n.|收据；收入；收到的东西|A receipt is a written acknowledgment that something has been received, especially money or goods.  It often serves as proof of purchase or payment.|例句：I need a receipt for the purchase. 我需要购买的收据。
例句：The company's receipts increased this year.  公司的收入今年增加了。|近义词：invoice, bill, acknowledgment|反义词：expenditure, disbursement

词源:
The word "receipt" comes from the Old French word "receit," meaning "receiving" or "reception."  This, in turn, derives from the Latin verb "recipere," meaning "to receive" or "to take back."  The evolution reflects the original function of a receipt as a record of something received.
