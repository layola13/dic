reticle:/ˈrɛtɪkl/| n. |瞄准器；分划板；刻度盘|A reticle is a pattern of thin lines, dots, or other markings etched onto a transparent material, placed in the focal plane of an optical instrument (such as a telescope or microscope) to provide a reference grid for aiming or measurement.|Example: The astronomer carefully adjusted the reticle in the telescope to center the distant star. 天文学家仔细调整望远镜中的分划板，以将遥远的恒星居中。|近义词：crosshairs, graticule|反义词：None|名词
