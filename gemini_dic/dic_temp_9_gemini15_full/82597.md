guardianships:/ˈɡɑːrdɪənʃɪps/| n. |监护权；监护制度|The legal right and responsibility to care for a person or their property.  It often refers to the care of a minor or someone deemed incapable of self-care.|例句：The court awarded the guardianships of the children to their aunt. 法院将孩子的监护权判给了他们的阿姨。


例句：The grandparents fought for guardianships of their grandchildren after the parents' death.  祖父母在父母去世后为争取孙子女的监护权而斗争。


近义词：custody, wardship, protection


反义词：abandonment, neglect


词性：名词
