palatalised:/ˈpælətaɪzd/ | adj. |软腭化的；颚化的 |Having or relating to the articulation of a consonant sound using the palate.  This usually involves raising the tongue towards the hard palate during pronunciation, resulting in a sound different from the unpalatalized counterpart.  The palatalization of a sound is often represented by a small 'j' or 'ʲ' in phonetic transcription.  | Example: The English "y" sound is a palatalised version of "d". The Russian "лʲ" (l') is a palatalised "l". | 近义词：palatal, softened  | 反义词：unpalatalised
