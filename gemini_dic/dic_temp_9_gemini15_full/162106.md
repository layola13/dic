pre-Crusade: /ˌpriːˈkruːseɪd/ | adj. | 十字军东征前的 |  Relating to the period of time before the Crusades.  This adjective describes events, people, or conditions that existed before the major military campaigns launched by European Christians between the 11th and 13th centuries to reclaim the Holy Land. | Example: Pre-Crusade relations between Christians and Muslims in the Levant were complex and varied greatly depending on location and time period. 十字军东征前的黎凡特地区基督教徒和穆斯林之间的关系复杂多样，并且根据具体的地点和时间而差异很大。 | 近义词: ante-Crusade, pre-1096 (referencing the start of the First Crusade) | 反义词: post-Crusade
