impropitious: /ˌɪmprəˈpɪʃəs/ | adj. | 不吉利的；不利的；不适宜的 | unfavorable; unlucky; not auspicious; suggesting bad luck; likely to result in failure |

英文解析:  The adjective "impropitious" describes circumstances or events that are unfavorable, unlucky, or likely to result in failure. It suggests a lack of promise or a high likelihood of negative outcomes.  It implies an atmosphere or situation that is not conducive to success or well-being.

例句:

The stormy weather proved to be an impropitious time to launch the boat.  = 暴风雨天气被证明是下水的不利时机。

The impropitious market conditions led to the company's bankruptcy. = 不利的市场环境导致了公司的破产。


近义词: unfavorable, unlucky, inauspicious, adverse, unfortunate, ill-omened, unpropitious

反义词: auspicious, favorable, fortunate, propitious, lucky


词源:

The word "impropitious" comes from the Latin prefix "im-" (not) + "pro-" (forward, in favor of) + "pitious" (causing pity, merciful, favorable).  The root "pitious" is related to "pity," highlighting the sense of something being unfavorable or unfortunate.  The word evolved through Old French.
