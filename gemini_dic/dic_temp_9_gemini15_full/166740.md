presides:/prɪˈzaɪdz/| v.|主持，担任主席，主导|To be in charge of a meeting or other formal gathering; to dominate or control something.|The judge presides over the courtroom.  法官主持法庭。|The chairman presides over the meeting. 主席主持会议。| governs, chairs, leads | yields, submits


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The judge presides over the court. = 法官主持法庭。
She presides over the meeting with grace. = 她优雅地主持会议。

一般过去时:
The mayor presided over the ceremony. = 市长主持了仪式。
He presided at the board meeting last week. = 他上周主持了董事会会议。

现在完成时:
She has presided over the company for many years. = 她多年来一直担任公司的主席。
The committee has presided over several important decisions. = 委员会主持了几个重要的决定。

过去完成时:
Before the new CEO arrived, the old manager had presided over the company for 10 years. = 在新首席执行官到来之前，老经理已经担任公司总裁10年了。
They had presided over the negotiations for months before reaching an agreement. = 在达成协议之前，他们已经主持了几个月的谈判。

将来时:
The new chairman will preside over the next meeting. = 新任主席将主持下次会议。
She will preside over the graduation ceremony. = 她将主持毕业典礼。


虚拟语气:
If she were to preside over the meeting, it would be more efficient. = 如果由她主持会议，效率会更高。
I wish he had presided over the debate; it would have been fairer. = 我希望他主持了这场辩论；那样会更公平。


被动语态:
The meeting was presided over by the CEO. = 会议由首席执行官主持。
The ceremony will be presided over by the mayor. = 典礼将由市长主持。

疑问句:
Who presided over the last conference? = 谁主持了上次会议？
Will the principal preside over the graduation? = 校长会主持毕业典礼吗？


否定句:
He did not preside over the meeting; he was only an attendee. = 他没有主持会议；他只是一个与会者。
The judge could not preside over the case due to a conflict of interest. = 由于利益冲突，法官无法审理此案。
