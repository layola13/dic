self-proclaiming: /ˌsɛlf prəˈkleɪmɪŋ/ | adj. | 自封的，自称的 |  Describing someone or something that declares itself to be something without external verification or confirmation.  It implies a lack of official recognition or legitimacy. |


例句：

He is a self-proclaiming expert on ancient civilizations, but his claims lack evidence. = 他自称是古代文明专家，但他的说法缺乏证据。

The self-proclaiming king ruled only a small territory. =  这位自封的国王只统治了一个小地区。


近义词：self-styled, self-appointed, self-declared


反义词：officially recognized, officially appointed, legitimate


词性：形容词
