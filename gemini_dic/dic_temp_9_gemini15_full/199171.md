silicle:/ˈsɪlɪkl/| n. |短角果|A silicle is a type of dry fruit, a short siliqua, that is typically less than twice as long as it is wide.  It is a characteristic feature of certain plants in the Brassicaceae (mustard) family. The silicle is a two-celled fruit that develops from a superior ovary and has a thin, papery wall. Upon maturity, the silicle splits open along two sutures to release the seeds.|Many plants in the genus *Alyssum* have silicles.  The seeds are easily dispersed once the silicle splits.|近义词：siliqua (but longer)|反义词：None specifically, but it contrasts with longer fruits like siliquas.
