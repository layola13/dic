characterised:/ˈkærəktəraɪzd/| v.|描绘…的特征；具有…的特征 | to describe the qualities or features of someone or something | The novel is characterised by its dark humour. 这部小说以其黑色幽默为特色。 | 近义词：described, defined, distinguished | 反义词：misrepresented, obfuscated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The city is characterised by its tall buildings. = 这个城市以其高楼大厦为特色。
His work is characterised by its meticulous detail. = 他的作品以其细致入微的细节为特色。

一般过去时:
The meeting was characterised by heated debate. = 会议的特点是激烈的辩论。
Her childhood was characterised by poverty and hardship. = 她的童年以贫困和艰辛为特征。

现在完成时:
His career has been characterised by a series of successes. = 他的职业生涯以一系列的成功为特征。
The decade has been characterised by rapid technological advancements. = 这十年以快速的科技进步为特征。

过去完成时:
The situation had been characterised by uncertainty and fear. = 情况的特点是不确定性和恐惧。
The previous year had been characterised by low rainfall. = 前一年降雨量很少。

将来时:
The upcoming election will be characterised by intense campaigning. = 即将到来的选举将以激烈的竞选活动为特征。
The next chapter will be characterised by a shift in tone. = 下一章的特点是基调的变化。

虚拟语气:
If the project were characterised by efficiency, it would be completed on time. = 如果这个项目以效率为特征，它就能按时完成。
I wish my writing were characterised by greater clarity. = 我希望我的写作更清晰。

被动语态:
The painting is characterised as a masterpiece. = 这幅画被认为是杰作。
His actions were characterised as reckless and irresponsible. = 他的行为被认为是鲁莽和不负责任的。

疑问句:
How is this era characterised? = 这个时代有什么特点？
What characteristics characterised the Renaissance period? = 文艺复兴时期有什么特点？

否定句:
The film is not characterised by its originality. = 这部电影的特点不是它的原创性。
Her personality cannot be characterised as shy or timid. = 她的人格不能被描述为害羞或胆怯。


PHRASE->
be characterised by = 以...为特征
strongly characterised = 强烈地体现
well characterised = 特征鲜明
poorly characterised = 特征模糊
clearly characterised = 特征清晰
