psychoticism:/ˌsaɪkəʊˈtɪsɪzəm/| n. |精神病性，精神病态|The tendency towards psychosis, characterized by unusual thoughts, feelings, and behaviors.  It is a dimension of personality, not a diagnosis.  High scores on psychoticism scales suggest a predisposition towards impulsivity, aggression, and a lack of empathy. |Example:  The study examined the relationship between psychoticism and criminal behavior. 这项研究调查了精神病性和犯罪行为之间的关系。|近义词：psychopathy,  paranoia,  abnormality|反义词：normality, sanity,  well-adjustedness
