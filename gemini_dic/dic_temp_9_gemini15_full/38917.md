confessionalists:/kənˈfɛʃənəlɪsts/| n. |忏悔者；自白者；信奉告解教义者|Those who believe in or practice confession, particularly within a religious context.  They emphasize the importance of confessing sins and receiving absolution.  The term can also refer to writers or artists who focus on personal experiences and emotions in their work, often revealing intimate details of their lives.|The confessionalists in the church were known for their deep faith and willingness to share their struggles.  |近义词：penitents, confessors |反义词：None specifically, but antonyms could relate to concepts opposing confession, such as secrecy or self-righteousness.
