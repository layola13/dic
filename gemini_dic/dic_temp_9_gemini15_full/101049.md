inveigle:/ɪnˈveɪɡəl/| vt.|诱骗；巧妙地劝诱|To persuade (someone) to do something, typically by using deception or flattery.  |例句：He inveigled his way into her confidence. 他巧妙地获得了她的信任。|例句：They inveigled him into signing the contract. 他们诱骗他签了合同。|近义词：cajole, coax, entice, wheedle|反义词：deter, dissuade, repel


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He inveigles his way into the party. = 他设法混进了派对。
She inveigles information from her colleagues. = 她从同事那里巧妙地套取信息。


一般过去时:
The salesman inveigled the customer into buying the expensive car. = 销售员诱骗顾客购买了昂贵的汽车。
They inveigled the secret from the old man. = 他们从老人那里套取了秘密。


现在完成时:
She has inveigled her way into a high-paying job. = 她已经设法获得了一份高薪工作。
He has inveigled the information from his rivals. = 他已经从竞争对手那里套取了信息。


过去完成时:
They had inveigled the password before the meeting. = 他们早在会议之前就套取了密码。
She had inveigled him into marriage before he realized her true nature. = 她在他意识到她的真实本性之前就诱骗他结婚了。


将来时:
He will inveigle his way into the secret society. = 他将设法混入这个秘密社团。
They will inveigle the truth out of the suspect. = 他们将从嫌疑人那里套取真相。


虚拟语气:
If I could inveigle him to help, the project would succeed. = 如果我能诱使他帮忙，项目就能成功。
I wish I could inveigle her into changing her mind. = 我希望我能诱使她改变主意。


被动语态:

The information was inveigled from the confidential files. = 信息是从机密文件中套取的。


疑问句:
How did he inveigle his way into the building? = 他是怎么混进大楼的？
Can you inveigle the truth from him? = 你能从他那里套取真相吗？


否定句:
He couldn't inveigle the information from the witness. = 他无法从证人那里套取信息。
She didn't try to inveigle him into doing anything illegal. = 她没有试图诱骗他做任何违法的事情。


PHRASE->
inveigle someone into something = 诱使某人做某事
inveigle oneself into something = 设法进入某处/获得某物
inveigle a secret from someone = 从某人那里套取秘密
inveigle a confession from someone = 从某人那里套取供词
inveigle someone's trust = 骗取某人的信任


词源:
The word "inveigle" comes from the Old French word "engigler," meaning "to entangle."  The word's evolution reflects a shift from the literal meaning of entrapment to the figurative meaning of skillful persuasion, often involving some degree of deception.
