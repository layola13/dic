prohibited:/prəˈhɪbɪtɪd/| adj.|禁止的，严禁的|Something that is prohibited is forbidden or not allowed.  It is something that is against the rules or laws. |Example: Smoking is prohibited in this building.  这个建筑物内禁止吸烟。

The sale of alcohol to minors is strictly prohibited. 向未成年人出售酒精饮料是被严格禁止的。|近义词：forbidden, banned, outlawed, illegal |反义词：allowed, permitted, legal


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rule prohibits smoking. 这条规则禁止吸烟。
This area prohibits parking. 这个区域禁止停车。

一般过去时:
The government prohibited the import of certain goods. 政府禁止进口某些商品。
They prohibited the use of mobile phones during the exam. 他们禁止考试期间使用手机。

现在完成时:
The authorities have prohibited public gatherings. 当局已经禁止公众集会。
They have prohibited access to the building. 他们已经禁止进入该建筑物。

过去完成时:
They had prohibited the use of the equipment before the accident. 事故发生前，他们就已经禁止使用该设备了。
The school had prohibited outside food before the lunch break. 学校在午休前就已经禁止携带外来食物了。

将来时:
The council will prohibit the construction of new high-rises. 理事会将禁止建造新的高层建筑。
They will prohibit the sale of those products. 他们将禁止销售那些产品。

虚拟语气:
If they prohibited the use of cars, pollution would decrease. 如果他们禁止使用汽车，污染就会减少。
I wish they prohibited smoking in restaurants. 我希望他们禁止在餐馆吸烟。

被动语态:
The sale of drugs is prohibited. 毒品买卖是被禁止的。
The event was prohibited due to bad weather. 由于天气恶劣，活动被禁止了。

疑问句:
Is smoking prohibited here?  这里禁止吸烟吗？
Are dogs prohibited in this park? 这个公园禁止带狗吗？

否定句:
Smoking is not prohibited outdoors. 户外吸烟不被禁止。
The use of cameras is not prohibited. 使用相机不被禁止。


PHRASE->
be prohibited from: 被禁止做…

prohibited area: 禁区
prohibited item: 禁忌物品
