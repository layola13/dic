catastrophism:/kəˈtæstrəfɪzəm/ | n. |灾变论；突变论 |The theory that changes in the earth's crust during geological history have resulted chiefly from sudden violent and unusual events.  Catastrophism is a geological theory that explains the Earth's features as the result of sudden, short-lived, violent events, in contrast to uniformitarianism, which explains the Earth's features as the result of slow, gradual processes. |The Grand Canyon was once explained by catastrophism, as a massive flood carving out the canyon in a short time.  大峡谷曾经被解释为灾变论，认为大洪水在短时间内雕刻出了峡谷。

|近义词：sudden change, upheaval |反义词：uniformitarianism |名词
