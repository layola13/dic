farewells:/ˌfeərˈwɛlz/| n.|告别，辞行|The act of saying farewell; a leave-taking.|例句：We exchanged farewells before parting ways.  我们道别后才分开。|例句：She offered her heartfelt farewells to her colleagues. 她向同事们表达了衷心的告别。|近义词：goodbyes, leave-takings, parting|反义词：greetings, welcomes|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The train departs; we exchange farewells. = 火车出发了；我们互相道别。
Farewells are often bittersweet. = 告别常常是苦乐参半的。


一般过去时:
They said their farewells and left. = 他们道别后离开了。
We exchanged tearful farewells at the airport. = 我们在机场依依不舍地告别。


现在完成时:
I have said my farewells to my family. = 我已经向家人道别了。
She has already said her farewells and departed. = 她已经道别离开了。


过去完成时:
They had said their farewells before the ship sailed. = 船开航前他们已经道别了。
We had exchanged brief farewells earlier that day. = 我们当天早些时候已经简短地道别了。


将来时:
We will say our farewells tomorrow. = 我们明天将道别。
They will be saying their farewells soon. = 他们很快就要道别了。


虚拟语气:
If I had known it was our last meeting, I would have said a proper farewell. = 如果我知道那是我们最后一次见面，我会好好道别的。
I wish I had said a more heartfelt farewell. = 我希望我当时道别时更真挚一些。


被动语态:
Farewells were exchanged between the two friends. = 这两个朋友互相道别了。
A formal farewell was given to the retiring CEO. =  即将退休的首席执行官受到了正式的告别。


疑问句:
Have you said your farewells? = 你道别了吗？
Did they say their farewells before leaving? = 他们离开前道别了吗？


否定句:
They didn't say their farewells properly. = 他们没有好好道别。
I didn't get a chance to say my farewells. = 我没有机会道别。
