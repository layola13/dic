disbelief:/ˌdɪsbiˈliːf/ | n. |不相信，怀疑 |The state of not believing something; incredulity.  It often involves a strong emotional response to something unexpected or unbelievable. |例句：His story was met with disbelief. 他的故事遭到了质疑。

例句：Their disbelief was palpable when they heard the news. 当他们听到这个消息时，他们的不相信是显而易见的。


近义词：incredulity, skepticism, doubt, mistrust |反义词：belief, faith, credence

词源:  The word "disbelief" is formed from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") and the noun "belief."  "Belief" ultimately comes from Old English "gɪlēafa," meaning "faith, trust, belief," which is related to Old High German "giloubo" and Gothic "galaubein."
