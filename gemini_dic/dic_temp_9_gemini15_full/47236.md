dazzling: /ˈdæzəlɪŋ/ | adj. | 耀眼的；令人眼花缭乱的；极好的 | Extremely impressive, attractive, or exciting.  Something dazzling is so bright or impressive that it is difficult to look at or take in. |

例句：

The dazzling sunlight hurt my eyes. = 耀眼的阳光刺痛了我的眼睛。

The dazzling performance left the audience speechless. = 令人眼花缭乱的表演让观众说不出话来。


近义词：brilliant, dazzling, glittering, radiant, resplendent, shimmering, sparkling, stunning

反义词：dull, drab, lackluster, ordinary


词源:

From *dazzle* (verb), ultimately from Old Norse *daska* ("to strike, stun").  The sense of "to blind with light" developed in Middle English. The adjective form followed later.
