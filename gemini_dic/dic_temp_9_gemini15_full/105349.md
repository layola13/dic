kickback:/ˈkɪkbæk/| n., v.|回扣；反冲；反作用力；报复|

英文解析:
As a noun, "kickback" refers to a secret payment made in return for a favor or service, often illegal.  It can also refer to a sudden, forceful recoil or reaction.  As a verb, it means to recoil or to retaliate.

例句:
The politician received a large kickback for awarding the contract.  这位政客因为授予合同而收受了巨额回扣。

He was injured when the gun kicked back.  枪支后坐力使他受伤。


近义词:
Noun: bribe, rebate, backlash, recoil
Verb: recoil, retaliate, react

反义词:
Noun: reward (in the sense of legitimate payment),  forward momentum
Verb:  cooperate, comply


词性:
Noun, Verb
