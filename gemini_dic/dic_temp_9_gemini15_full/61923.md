emulate:/ˈɛmjuleɪt/ | vt. |模仿，效仿，与…竞争 | To imitate with the intent of equaling or surpassing the model.  It often implies striving to achieve a similar level of excellence. | 例句：She emulated her favorite author's writing style. 她模仿了她最喜欢的作者的写作风格。

例句：Many young musicians emulate their idols. 许多年轻音乐家都模仿他们的偶像。


近义词：imitate, copy, mimic, follow, rival


反义词：ignore, neglect, disregard


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He emulates his father's work ethic. 他效仿他父亲的职业道德。
She emulates the style of famous painters. 她模仿著名画家的风格。


一般过去时:
The student emulated the professor's teaching methods. 学生模仿了教授的教学方法。
They emulated the successful strategies of their competitors. 他们效仿了竞争对手成功的策略。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

They had emulated the design of the winning product before launching their own. 在推出自己的产品之前，他们已经模仿了获奖产品的設計。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:

词源:
The word "emulate" comes from the Latin word "aemulari," meaning "to rival," "to strive to equal," or "to imitate."  The prefix "aem-" is related to the Latin word "aemulus," meaning "rival."  The word's meaning has evolved over time to encompass both the competitive and imitative aspects.
