commandeered:/ˌkɒmənˈdɪəd/|/kɑːmənˈdɪrd/| vt. |征用；强占；霸占 |To take (something, especially property or a vehicle) for military or other official use, without the owner's consent.  |例句：The army commandeered all available vehicles.  军队征用了所有可用的车辆。


例句：The rebels commandeered the television station to broadcast their message.  叛军占领了电视台以广播他们的信息。


近义词：seize, requisition, appropriate, expropriate |反义词：release, relinquish, surrender |词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government commandeers private land for public projects. = 政府征用私人土地用于公共项目。
They commandeer resources whenever they need them. = 他们需要资源时就会征用。


一般过去时:
The soldiers commandeered a truck from a farmer. = 士兵从一个农民那里征用了一辆卡车。
The police commandeered the building during the hostage situation. = 在人质事件期间，警方控制了该建筑物。


现在完成时:
The military has commandeered several buildings in the city. = 军队已经征用了该市几座建筑物。
They have commandeered all available vehicles for the evacuation. = 他们已经征用了所有可用的车辆进行疏散。


过去完成时:
The authorities had commandeered the bridge before the floodwaters arrived. = 洪水到来之前，当局已经控制了桥梁。
The rebels had commandeered the train before we could stop them. = 在我们阻止他们之前，叛军已经控制了火车。


将来时:
The army will commandeer any resources necessary for the operation. = 军队将征用行动所需的任何资源。
They will commandeer the airport if the situation worsens. = 如果情况恶化，他们将控制机场。


虚拟语气:
If the army needed it, they would commandeer the factory. = 如果军队需要，他们就会征用工厂。
I wish they hadn't commandeered my car. = 我真希望他们没有征用我的车。


被动语态:
The building was commandeered by the government. = 这座建筑物被政府征用了。
Several vehicles were commandeered for the emergency response. = 几辆车被征用用于紧急救援。


疑问句:
Were any private properties commandeered during the war? = 战争期间征用了任何私人财产吗？
Will the airport be commandeered for military use? = 机场会被征用作军事用途吗？


否定句:
The government did not commandeer any private land this year. = 政府今年没有征用任何私人土地。
The police did not commandeer the suspect's car. = 警方没有征用嫌疑人的汽车。


PHRASE->
commandeer a vehicle = 征用车辆
commandeer resources = 征用资源
commandeer property = 征用财产
commandeer a building = 征用建筑物
commandeer supplies = 征用物资

词源:
From French *commander* "to command," from Latin *commandare* "to entrust, order," from *com-* "together" + *mandare* "to order," from manus "hand."  The sense development is from "to entrust" to "to order" and finally "to seize for official use."
