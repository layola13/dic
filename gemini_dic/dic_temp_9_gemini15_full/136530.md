nonruminants: /ˌnɒnˈruːmɪnənts/ | n. | 非反刍动物 | Animals that do not have a rumen, the first stomach compartment of ruminants.  They lack the ability to digest cellulose efficiently through microbial fermentation in a specialized stomach compartment.  Examples include humans, pigs, horses, and carnivores. | Example:  Horses are nonruminants and rely on a different digestive system than cows.  马是非反刍动物，其消化系统与牛不同。|近义词: monogastrics |反义词: ruminants
