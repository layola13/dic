infantility: /ɪnˈfæntaɪləti/ | n. |幼稚，孩子气；婴儿期 | The state or quality of being infantile; childishness.  It often refers to the persistence of childish or immature behavior into adulthood.  |例句：His constant need for reassurance revealed a surprising degree of infantility.  他的不断需要保证的行为暴露出令人惊讶的幼稚程度。

例句：The artist's work displays a captivating blend of infantility and sophistication.  这位艺术家的作品展现了幼稚和成熟的迷人融合。


近义词：childishness, immaturity, puerility, babyishness

反义词：maturity, adulthood, sophistication


词性: 名词


词源:  From Middle English *infantilite, from Old French *infantilité, from Latin *infantilitas, from infans "infant," from in- "not" + fari "to speak."  The sense development is from "inability to speak" (characteristic of infants) to "childish behavior."
