scandalising: /ˈskændəlaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人震惊的；丑闻的；令人反感的 | Causing or likely to cause scandal; shocking.  | The scandalising details of the affair were splashed across the front pages.  令人震惊的婚外情细节占据了头版头条。 | shocking, outrageous, offensive, scandalous | acceptable, inoffensive, respectable


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The news scandalises the public. = 这则新闻令公众震惊。
His behavior scandalises his family. = 他的行为令家人感到震惊。


一般过去时:
The revelation scandalized the entire nation. = 这项揭露震惊了全国。
The politician's actions scandalized his constituents. = 这位政治家的行为令他的选民感到震惊。


现在完成时:
The scandal has scandalized many people. = 这场丑闻令许多人感到震惊。
His betrayal has scandalized his friends. = 他的背叛令他的朋友们感到震惊。


过去完成时:
They had been scandalized by the previous administration's actions. = 他们对上一届政府的行为感到震惊。
The public had already been scandalized by earlier reports. = 公众已经被早先的报道震惊了。


将来时:
The upcoming report will scandalize the public. = 即将发布的报告将令公众震惊。
His actions will scandalize everyone who knows him. = 他的行为将令所有认识他的人感到震惊。


虚拟语气:
If the truth were known, it would scandalize everyone. = 如果真相大白，它将会震惊所有人。
I wish this wouldn't scandalize my parents. = 我希望这不会令我的父母震惊。


被动语态:
The public was scandalized by the politician's lies. = 公众被这位政治家的谎言震惊了。
The community is scandalized by the recent events. = 社区对最近发生的事件感到震惊。


疑问句:
Would this scandalize the community? = 这会令社区震惊吗？
Does this behavior scandalize you? = 这种行为令你震惊吗？


否定句:
The event didn't scandalize me as much as I expected. = 这件事并没有像我预期的那样令我震惊。
The news didn't scandalize the younger generation. = 这则新闻并没有令年轻一代震惊。
