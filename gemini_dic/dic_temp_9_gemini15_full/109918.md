libeled:/ˈlaɪbəld/ | vt. |诽谤；中伤 | To make a false and damaging statement about someone, typically in writing.  |例句：He was libeled in a newspaper article. 他在一篇报刊文章中受到了诽谤。

例句：The politician sued the magazine for libeling him.  这位政客以诽谤罪起诉了这家杂志。


近义词：slander, defame, calumniate | 反义词：praise, commend


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He libels his opponents regularly. 他经常诽谤他的对手。
The magazine libels many public figures. 这本杂志诽谤许多公众人物。


一般过去时:
They libeled her in the local newspaper. 他们在当地报纸上诽谤了她。
The journalist libeled the mayor in his report.  记者在他的报道中诽谤了市长。


现在完成时:
They have libeled him repeatedly over the years. 多年来他们一再诽谤他。
She has been libeled in several publications.  她在几家出版物中受到了诽谤。


过去完成时:
He had libeled her before, so she wasn't surprised. 他以前就诽谤过她，所以她并不感到惊讶。
The court found that they had libeled the witness. 法院认定他们诽谤了证人。


将来时:
They will libel him again if given the chance.  如果给他们机会，他们还会再次诽谤他。
I fear she will libel me in her upcoming book. 我担心她会在即将出版的书中诽谤我。


虚拟语气:
If he had not libeled her, she wouldn't have sued him. 如果他没有诽谤她，她就不会起诉他。
I wish they hadn't libeled my family.  我希望他们没有诽谤我的家人。


被动语态:
He was libeled in a national newspaper. 他在一份全国性报纸上受到了诽谤。
The company was libeled in a recent article.  该公司在最近的一篇文章中受到了诽谤。


疑问句:
Were they libeled in that article?  在那篇文章中他们被诽谤了吗？
Has she been libeled by the press? 她被媒体诽谤了吗？


否定句:
He did not libel his business partner. 他没有诽谤他的商业伙伴。
They have not libeled anyone in this campaign.  他们在这次竞选中没有诽谤任何人。


PHRASE->
to be libeled: 被诽谤
to libel someone: 诽谤某人
a libel suit: 诽谤诉讼
to file a libel suit:提起诽谤诉讼
to retract a libel: 撤回诽谤言论

词源:
源自古法语 libel,  最终源自拉丁语 libellus, 指的是“小书”，暗示诽谤最初通常是书面形式的。
