pseudomilitary: /ˌsuːdoʊˈmɪləteri/ | adj. |准军事的，类似军事的 |  Relating to or resembling military organizations but not officially part of the armed forces.  It often refers to organizations that adopt military-style structures, tactics, or discipline, but lack the official status and authority of a true military. |例句：The paramilitary group engaged in pseudomilitary activities, including drills and training exercises. 这个准军事组织从事准军事活动，包括演习和训练。

The private security company adopted a pseudomilitary structure, with ranks and uniforms. 这家私人保安公司采用了准军事结构，带有军衔和制服。


近义词：paramilitary, quasi-military,  militant (in some contexts) | 反义词：civilian, non-military


词源:  The word is a combination of "pseudo-" (meaning false or resembling) and "military."  "Pseudo-" comes from the Greek word  ψευδής (pseudēs), meaning "false" or "lying," while "military" ultimately derives from the Latin word "miles," meaning "soldier."
