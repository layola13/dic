chorepiscopus: /ˌkɔːriˈɛpɪskəpəs/ | n. |主教助理，区域主教 | A chorepiscopus was a type of bishop in the early Christian Church, typically responsible for a rural area or a specific community within a larger diocese.  They were subordinate to the diocesan bishop but had significant authority within their assigned region.  The exact nature of their authority varied across different regions and time periods.  The office gradually declined in influence and eventually disappeared in most parts of the Western Church. | The chorepiscopus oversaw the spiritual needs of his assigned community. |  近义词：rural bishop, assistant bishop (in a limited sense) | 反义词：diocesan bishop (in terms of authority level)
