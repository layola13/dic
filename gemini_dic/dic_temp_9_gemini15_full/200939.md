slipstreams: /ˈslɪpˌstriːmz/ | v. n. | 尾流，顺风行驶；搭便车；|

英文解析:To slipstream is to follow closely behind another vehicle, especially a faster one, to benefit from its reduced air resistance.  As a noun, it refers to the area of reduced air resistance behind a moving vehicle.  Figuratively, it can also mean to take advantage of someone else's success or work.


例句:

The cyclist slipstreamed the leader in the final sprint. =  在最后的冲刺中，这位自行车运动员紧跟在领先者的后面。

The small company successfully slipstreamed the market leader's innovations. = 这家小公司成功地利用了市场领导者的创新成果。


近义词:draft, follow, piggyback (figuratively)

反义词:lead, pioneer (figuratively)


词性:Verb, Noun
