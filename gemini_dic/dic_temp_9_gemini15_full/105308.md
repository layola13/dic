kibibit:/ˈkaɪbɪbɪt/| n. |千比特（1024比特）|A kibibit (KiB) is a unit of information or computer storage equal to 1024 bits.  It's a binary prefix, using the "kibi" prefix to avoid confusion with the kilobit (kb), which is 1000 bits.  The difference stems from the use of base-2 (binary) versus base-10 (decimal) systems in computing. |The file size is 10 KiB.|近义词：1024 bits|反义词：无
