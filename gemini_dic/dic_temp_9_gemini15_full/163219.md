precompressing: /ˌpriːkəmˈprɛsɪŋ/ | v. | 预压缩 | The act of compressing data or images before further processing, typically to reduce storage space or transmission time.  This is often done to improve efficiency or performance in subsequent stages. For example, images might be precompressed before uploading to a website or video might be precompressed before streaming. | The software precompresses the images before uploading them to the cloud.  软件在将图像上传到云端之前对其进行预压缩。 The video was precompressed to reduce its file size.  视频经过预压缩以减小其文件大小。 | compressing,  packing | decompressing |
