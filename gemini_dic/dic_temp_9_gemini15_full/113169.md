lurch:/lɜːrtʃ/| n., v.|突然倾斜；蹒跚；急剧摇摆；|A sudden, unsteady movement; a sudden, violent, or awkward movement.|The old house lurched violently in the storm.  古老的房子在暴风雨中剧烈摇晃。|The drunkard lurched down the street.  醉汉在街上蹒跚而行。|近义词：stumble, sway, stagger, reel|反义词：steady, stable, balanced|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ship lurches violently in the waves. = 船在波浪中剧烈摇晃。
He lurches from one extreme to another. = 他从一个极端走向另一个极端。


一般过去时:
The car lurched forward unexpectedly. = 汽车意外地向前猛冲了一下。
She lurched to her feet, startled by the noise. = 她被噪音吓了一跳，猛地站了起来。


现在完成时:
The boat has lurched several times during the voyage. = 这艘船在航行过程中已经摇晃了好几次。
The economy has lurched into recession. = 经济已经骤然陷入衰退。


过去完成时:
The bus had lurched violently before coming to a stop. = 公共汽车剧烈摇晃了一下才停下来。
He had lurched forward, almost falling. = 他猛地向前倾，差点摔倒。


将来时:
The building will likely lurch in the next earthquake. = 在下一次地震中，这座建筑很可能会摇晃。
I think the stock market will lurch downwards. = 我认为股市将会暴跌。


虚拟语气:
If the bridge hadn't lurched, the car wouldn't have fallen. = 如果桥没有摇晃，汽车就不会掉下去。
I wish the plane hadn't lurched so violently during takeoff. = 我希望飞机起飞时不要摇晃得那么厉害。


被动语态:
The car was lurched violently by the sudden impact. = 汽车由于突然的撞击而剧烈摇晃。
The small boat was lurched about by the strong wind. = 小船被强风吹得摇晃不定。


疑问句:
Did the train lurch suddenly? = 火车突然摇晃了吗？
Will the building lurch during the storm? = 这座建筑物会在暴风雨中摇晃吗？


否定句:
The ship did not lurch during the calm sea. = 在平静的海面上，船没有摇晃。
The old man did not lurch as he walked. = 老人走路时没有蹒跚。


PHRASE->
a lurch to the left/right = 向左/右猛冲
a lurch in the market = 市场波动
take a lurch = 突然失去平衡
a lurch of hope = 一线希望
a lurch forward = 突然向前冲


词源:
The word "lurch" ultimately derives from the Middle Dutch word "lurken," meaning "to lie in wait," "to lurk," or "to lie hidden."  Over time, the meaning shifted to describe a sudden, awkward movement, likely due to the image of something suddenly emerging from hiding in an unsteady way.
