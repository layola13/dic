non-compulsory:/ˌnɒn kəmˈpʌlsəri/ | adj. | 非强制的，非义务的 | Not required; optional.  | Example:  Attendance at the meeting is non-compulsory.  会议出席是非强制性的。  |近义词：optional, voluntary, elective | 反义词：compulsory, mandatory, obligatory


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The course is non-compulsory. = 这门课是非强制性的。
This activity is non-compulsory for students. =这项活动对学生来说是非强制性的。

一般过去时:
The exam was non-compulsory last year. = 去年考试是非强制性的。
That rule was non-compulsory before the amendment. = 在修正案之前，那条规则是非强制性的。

现在完成时:
The government has made the vaccination non-compulsory. = 政府已将疫苗接种改为非强制性。
The school has made many activities non-compulsory this year. = 学校今年已将许多活动改为非强制性。


过去完成时:
The rule had been non-compulsory for many years before the change. = 在改变之前，这条规则多年来一直是非强制性的。
The participation had been non-compulsory in previous years. = 在前几年，参与是非强制性的。

虚拟语气:
If the rule were non-compulsory, more people would participate. = 如果规则是非强制性的，更多人会参与。
I wish the course were non-compulsory; I don't have time for it. = 我希望这门课是非强制性的；我没有时间学它。

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "non-compulsory" is formed by combining the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") with the adjective "compulsory" (meaning "required").  "Compulsory" originates from the Latin word "compellere," meaning "to compel" or "to force."
