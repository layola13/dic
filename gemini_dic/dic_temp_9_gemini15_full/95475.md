incito:/ɪnˈsaɪtoʊ/| v.|煽动；激励；刺激|To incite means to encourage or stir up strong feelings, especially anger or violence, in someone.  It can also mean to urge someone to do something. |例句：The politician's speech incited the crowd to riot.  政治家的演讲煽动了人群暴动。

例句：His words incited me to action. 他的话激励我采取行动。|近义词：provoke, instigate, rouse, spur, goad|反义词：calm, soothe, pacify, discourage


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He incites violence through his speeches. = 他通过演讲煽动暴力。
The advertisement incites consumers to buy the product. = 广告诱使消费者购买产品。

一般过去时:
The rebel leader incited his followers to revolt. = 叛军领导人煽动他的追随者叛乱。
The teacher incited the students to learn more. = 老师激励学生们学习更多。

现在完成时:
His actions have incited much public anger. = 他的行为激起了公众的强烈愤怒。
The company has incited controversy with its new policy. = 公司的新政策引发了争议。


过去完成时:
The rumors had incited fear among the villagers. = 谣言已在村民中引起恐慌。
They had incited a rebellion before the king's arrival. = 在国王到来之前，他们煽动了一场叛乱。

将来时:
The upcoming election will incite passionate debate. = 即将到来的选举将引发激烈的辩论。
I fear his words will incite further violence. = 我担心他的话会引发进一步的暴力。

虚拟语气:
If he incited the crowd, there would have been a riot. = 如果他煽动人群，就会发生暴乱。
I wish he hadn't incited such hatred. = 我真希望他没有煽动如此大的仇恨。

被动语态:
The crowd was incited to violence by the speaker. = 演讲者煽动人群暴力。
The workers were incited to strike by unfair treatment. = 工人们因不公平待遇而被煽动罢工。

疑问句:
Did his speech incite any violence? = 他的演讲煽动任何暴力了吗？
What incited the riot? = 什么煽动了暴乱？

否定句:
The police tried to prevent the protesters from inciting violence. = 警方试图阻止抗议者煽动暴力。
His calm demeanor did not incite any fear. = 他冷静的态度没有引起任何恐惧。


PHRASE->

词源:
incito comes from the Latin verb incitare, meaning "to urge on," "to incite," or "to stimulate."  The prefix "in-" intensifies the meaning, and "-citare" is related to the Latin word "citus," meaning "moved" or "excited."
