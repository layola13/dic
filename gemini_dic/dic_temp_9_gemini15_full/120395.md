ministered:/ˈmɪnɪstərd/| vt.| 服务；照料；给予；施行；执行 | To give assistance or provide care to someone or something.  |例句：The nurses ministered to the wounded soldiers. 护士们护理受伤的士兵。 |例句：The priest ministered to the spiritual needs of his congregation. 牧师满足了他教区的精神需求。|近义词：served, attended, cared for |反义词： neglected, ignored


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She ministers to the sick. = 她照顾病人。
He ministers to the needs of the poor. = 他满足穷人的需求。

一般过去时:
They ministered to the victims of the earthquake. = 他们帮助地震的受害者。
The doctor ministered to the injured man. = 医生医治了受伤的男子。

现在完成时:
She has ministered to the homeless for many years. = 她多年来一直服务无家可归者。
He has ministered to the needs of his community. = 他一直满足他社区的需求。

过去完成时:
They had ministered to the injured before the ambulance arrived. = 救护车到达之前，他们已经护理了伤者。
She had ministered to her family for decades. = 她几十年来一直在照顾她的家人。

将来时:
They will minister to the needs of the refugees. = 他们将满足难民的需求。
She will minister to the sick in the hospital. = 她将在医院照顾病人。

虚拟语气:
If I were a doctor, I would minister to the sick. = 如果我是一名医生，我会照顾病人。
I wish I could minister to everyone in need. = 我希望我能帮助所有需要帮助的人。

被动语态:
The injured were ministered to by the paramedics. = 伤员得到了医护人员的救治。
The needs of the community are ministered to by various organizations. = 社区需求由各个组织来满足。

疑问句:
Who ministered to the patient? = 谁护理了病人？
Does he minister to the needs of his family? = 他是否满足了他家人的需求？

否定句:
They did not minister to the needs of the poor. = 他们没有满足穷人的需求。
She could not minister to him because she was ill. = 她因为生病而无法照顾他。
