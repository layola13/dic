department:/dɪˈpɑːrtmənt/ | n. | 部门，系，科 | A section or division of a larger organization, company, or institution, typically responsible for a specific function or area of activity.  |  The marketing department is responsible for advertising campaigns. 市场部负责广告宣传。 |division, section, branch, unit |  (no direct antonym, but context-dependent opposites might include individual, person) | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sales department handles customer inquiries.  销售部门处理客户咨询。
The accounting department prepares the financial reports. 会计部门准备财务报告。


一般过去时:
The engineering department designed the new product. 工程部设计了新产品。
The HR department hired ten new employees last month. 人力资源部上个月招聘了十名新员工。


现在完成时:
The marketing department has launched several successful campaigns this year. 市场部今年已经启动了几次成功的活动。
The research department has made significant progress on the project. 研究部门在项目上取得了重大进展。


过去完成时:
The finance department had already approved the budget before the meeting. 财务部门在会议之前就已经批准了预算。
The IT department had fixed the server issue before the deadline. IT部门在截止日期之前已经解决了服务器问题。


将来时:
The production department will increase output next quarter. 生产部门下个季度将提高产量。
The legal department will review the contract before signing. 法律部门将在签署前审查合同。


虚拟语气:
If the design department had more time, they would create a better product. 如果设计部门有更多时间，他们会创造更好的产品。
I wish the customer service department were more responsive. 我希望客户服务部门更积极响应。


被动语态:
The report was prepared by the finance department. 报告由财务部门准备。
The new policy was implemented by the human resources department. 新政策由人力资源部实施。


疑问句:
Which department handles customer complaints?  哪个部门处理客户投诉？
To which department should I submit this form? 我应该向哪个部门提交这份表格？


否定句:
The marketing department did not reach its sales target this quarter. 市场部本季度没有达到销售目标。
The IT department was not able to resolve the problem immediately. IT部门无法立即解决这个问题。
