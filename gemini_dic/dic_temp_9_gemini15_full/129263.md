nodding-off:/ˈnɒdɪŋ ɒf/ | v. | 打瞌睡；快要睡着 | To begin to fall asleep, especially unintentionally.  | Example: I was nodding off during the lecture. 我在讲座上打起了瞌睡。  |近义词：dozing off, dropping off, falling asleep | 反义词：being wide awake


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He nods off every afternoon after lunch. 他每天午饭后都会打瞌睡。
She often nods off while watching TV. 她看电视时经常打瞌睡。

一般过去时:
I nodded off during the boring movie. 我在看那部无聊的电影时睡着了。
He nodded off at the meeting and missed the important announcement. 他在会议上睡着了，错过了重要的通知。

现在完成时:
I have nodded off several times today. 我今天已经打瞌睡好几次了。
She has nodded off during every class this week.  她这周每节课都打瞌睡。

过去完成时:
I had nodded off before the speaker finished his presentation. 演讲者结束演讲之前我就睡着了。
They had nodded off before the movie ended. 电影结束前他们就睡着了。

将来时:
I think I will nod off if I don't have some coffee. 如果我不喝点咖啡，我想我会睡着的。
He’ll probably nod off during the long flight. 他在漫长的飞行途中可能会睡着。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't had that extra cup of coffee, I would have nodded off. 如果我没有喝那杯额外的咖啡，我就睡着了。
I wish I hadn't nodded off during the exam. 我真希望考试的时候我没有睡着。

被动语态: (This verb is rarely used in the passive voice.)  被动语态很少用在此动词上。

疑问句:
Did you nod off during the film? 你在看电影的时候睡着了吗？
Does he often nod off in class? 他经常在课堂上睡着吗？

否定句:
I didn’t nod off, despite the late night. 尽管熬夜了，但我并没有睡着。
She didn't nod off even though the meeting was very long. 尽管会议很长，但她也没有睡着。

nod off unintentionally: 无意中睡着
