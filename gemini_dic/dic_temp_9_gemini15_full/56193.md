dodged:/dɒdʒd/| /dɑːdʒd/| vt.|躲避；避开；巧妙地避开|To move quickly and suddenly to one side in order to avoid being hit by something.  It can also refer to avoiding something unpleasant or difficult.|例句：He dodged the flying ball. 他躲开了飞来的球。

例句：She cleverly dodged the question. 她巧妙地避开了这个问题。

近义词：evade, avoid, circumvent|反义词：confront, face


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dodges the traffic skillfully. = 他熟练地躲避交通。
She dodges her responsibilities. = 她逃避责任。


一般过去时:
The driver dodged a pothole. = 司机躲避了一个坑洞。
He dodged the reporters' questions. = 他躲避了记者的提问。


现在完成时:
I have dodged a bullet. = 我躲过了一劫。
She has dodged the issue for too long. = 她已经回避这个问题太久了。


过去完成时:
He had dodged the police for weeks. = 他已经躲避警察好几个星期了。
They had dodged the problem for years. = 他们多年来一直回避这个问题。


将来时:
I will dodge the incoming blow. = 我将躲避即将到来的打击。
He will dodge the responsibility again. = 他将再次逃避责任。


虚拟语气:
If I had dodged the car, I would not have been injured. = 如果我躲开了那辆车，我就不会受伤了。
I wish I had dodged that question. = 我真希望我当时躲开了那个问题。


被动语态:
The question was dodged by the politician. = 这个问题被政客回避了。
The bullet was dodged by a hair's breadth. = 子弹是擦身而过的。


疑问句:
Did he dodge the responsibility? = 他逃避责任了吗？
Can you dodge the incoming object? = 你能躲避迎面而来的物体吗？


否定句:
He didn't dodge the issue. = 他没有回避这个问题。
She couldn't dodge the responsibility any longer. = 她再也无法逃避责任了。


PHRASE->
