fraternities:/frəˈtɜːrnətiz/ | n. | 兄弟会；联谊会 |  A fraternity is a society or club of male students at a university or college.  Fraternities often involve social events, philanthropy, and networking opportunities. Members typically live together in a fraternity house. | Many college students join fraternities to make friends and build lasting bonds. | 近义词： brotherhood, sorority (female equivalent) | 反义词：None directly applicable.

WORD ORIGIN->
From Medieval Latin *fraternitas, from Latin frater ("brother").  The word reflects the sense of brotherhood and camaraderie within these organizations.
