prestandardizing: /ˌpriːˈstændərdʌɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 预先标准化 |  The process of standardizing something before it is used or implemented.  This often involves setting specific parameters or criteria in advance to ensure consistency and quality.  |  例句：Prestandardizing the data ensured the accuracy of the analysis. 预先标准化数据确保了分析的准确性。 The company prestandardized its manufacturing process to improve efficiency.  公司预先标准化其制造流程以提高效率。 | 近义词：pre-setting, pre-calibrating, normalizing (in some contexts) | 反义词：post-standardizing, improvising

词源:

The word "prestandardizing" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the verb "standardizing" (from "standard").  Therefore, it literally means "to standardize beforehand" or "to standardize in advance".  The root "standard" comes from Old French and ultimately from Latin.
