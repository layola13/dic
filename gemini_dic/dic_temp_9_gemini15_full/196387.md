sermonizing:/ˈsɜːrmənˌaɪzɪŋ/| v. |说教；教训；长篇大论地劝诫 |The act of delivering a sermon or preaching;  speaking or writing in a preachy or moralizing manner, often excessively or tediously.  |例句：His constant sermonizing about the dangers of technology became tiresome. 他不断地唠叨科技的危险，令人厌烦。

例句：She sermonized on the importance of hard work and discipline. 她长篇大论地谈论勤奋和自律的重要性。


近义词：preaching, moralizing, lecturing, hectoring, haranguing

反义词：praising, encouraging, supporting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sermonizes every Sunday. = 他每周日都讲道。
She sermonizes about the importance of saving money. = 她总是劝诫要存钱的重要性。


一般过去时:
The priest sermonized on the theme of forgiveness. = 牧师以宽恕为主题讲道。
He sermonized for an hour about the evils of gambling. = 他花了整整一个小时教训赌博的危害。


现在完成时:
He has sermonized on this topic many times before. = 他以前就多次就这个话题讲过道。
She has sermonized to her children about the dangers of drugs. = 她已经多次告诫她的孩子们毒品的危害。


过去完成时:
He had sermonized for years before he finally retired. = 他退休前已经讲道多年了。
She had sermonized to her class about the importance of education before the exam. = 考试前，她已经多次向她的班级灌输教育的重要性。


将来时:
He will sermonize on the importance of charity next week. = 他下周将就慈善的重要性进行布道。
She will sermonize at the church tomorrow morning. = 她明天早上将在教堂讲道。


虚拟语气:
If he sermonized less, people might listen more. = 如果他少说教一些，人们可能会多听一些。
I wish he wouldn't sermonize so much. = 我希望他不要再说教那么多了。


被动语态:
The topic of forgiveness was sermonized upon by the priest. = 牧师以宽恕为主题进行了布道。
The importance of healthy eating habits was sermonized on during the health fair. = 健康饮食习惯的重要性在健康博览会上被反复强调。


疑问句:
Does he often sermonize to his employees? = 他经常对他的员工说教吗？
Is she going to sermonize again tonight? = 她今晚还会再说教吗？


否定句:
He didn't sermonize at all during the meeting. = 他在会议上根本没有说教。
She didn't sermonize; she simply offered advice. = 她没有说教；她只是提出了建议。


PHRASE->
sermonizing on a topic  = 就某个话题说教
sermonizing against something = 反对某事说教
constant sermonizing = 不停的说教
tiresome sermonizing = 令人厌烦的说教
endless sermonizing = 没完没了的说教


词源:
源自于名词sermon（布道，讲道），加上动词后缀-ize，表示“使…化”，“进行…”。  Sermon本身源自于古法语"sermon,"  最终源于拉丁语 "sermo" (speech, conversation, discourse).
