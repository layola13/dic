deglorifies:/dɪˈɡlɔːrɪfaɪz/ | vt. |使不再光荣；贬低；使蒙羞 | To diminish the glory or honor of someone or something; to bring into disrepute.  | The scandal deglorified the once-respected politician.  丑闻使这位曾经受人尊敬的政治家名誉扫地。 The overuse of the term has deglorified its original meaning. 这个词语的过度使用使其原意黯然失色。 | 近义词：dishonor, disgrace, discredit, debase, denigrate | 反义词：glorify, honor, exalt, praise


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deglorifies his achievements. 他贬低自己的成就。
The media deglorifies the athlete's performance. 媒体贬低了这位运动员的表现。


一般过去时:
The incident deglorified the company's image.  这起事件损害了公司的形象。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:
They had deglorified the hero's story before the truth came out. 在真相大白之前，他们已经玷污了这位英雄的故事。
The constant criticism had deglorified her work in the eyes of the public.  持续不断的批评已经在她公众面前贬低了她的作品。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
