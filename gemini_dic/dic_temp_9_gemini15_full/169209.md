prolly:/ˈprɑːbli/ | adv. |大概，可能 |Probably, likely, most likely.  It's an informal contraction of "probably." Used to express a high degree of likelihood. | 例句：I'll prolly be late. = 我可能要迟到了。
                                                                                                  Prolly it will rain tomorrow. = 明天大概会下雨。|近义词：probably, likely, possibly |反义词：certainly (in the context of certainty), impossibly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I prolly go to the gym every day. = 我大概每天都去健身房。
She prolly works late most nights. = 她大概大多数晚上都工作到很晚。


一般过去时:
I prolly went to the store yesterday. = 我可能昨天去商店了。
He prolly missed the bus this morning. = 他可能今天早上错过了公交车。


现在完成时:
I prolly have already eaten. = 我可能已经吃过了。
She prolly has finished her homework. = 她可能已经完成了作业。


过去完成时:
I prolly had already left when you called. = 你打电话的时候我可能已经离开了。
They prolly had finished the project before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期之前可能已经完成了项目。


将来时:
I prolly will go to the beach tomorrow. = 我明天可能要去海滩。
It prolly will snow later. = 晚些时候可能要下雪。


虚拟语气:
I prolly would have gone if I had known. = 如果我知道的话，我可能就去了。
She prolly would have helped if she had the time. = 如果她有时间的话，她可能就会帮忙了。


被动语态:
The work prolly will have been completed by Friday. = 工作可能在周五之前完成。
The email prolly has been sent. = 邮件可能已经发送了。


疑问句:
Prolly he is coming? = 他可能要来吗？
Prolly she will attend? = 她可能要参加吗？


否定句:
I prolly won't be able to make it. = 我可能去不了。
She prolly didn't see me. = 她可能没看见我。


PHRASE->
prolly not = 可能不会
prolly gonna = 可能要
prolly should = 可能应该
prolly could = 可能可以
prolly won't = 可能不会
