half-plucked:/hɑːfˈplʌkt/ | adj. | 半拔的，半摘的 | Partially plucked;  having only some feathers or hairs removed.  This word is typically used to describe birds or animals that have been partially plucked for feathers or fur, often implying an incomplete or unfinished process. |例句：The chicken was half-plucked, with feathers still clinging to its legs. 这只鸡只拔了一半的毛，腿上还粘着羽毛。
