ectoparasite:/ˈɛktoʊˌpærəsaɪt/| n. |外寄生虫|An ectoparasite is a parasite that lives on the outside of its host's body.  Unlike endoparasites, which live inside the host, ectoparasites typically attach themselves to the skin or fur. They feed on the host's blood, skin, or other bodily fluids.  Examples include fleas, ticks, lice, and mites.|Example: Fleas are a common ectoparasite of dogs and cats.跳蚤是狗和猫常见的体外寄生虫。|近义词：external parasite|反义词：endoparasite|名词
