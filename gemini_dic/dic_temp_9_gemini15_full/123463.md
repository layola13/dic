monosaccharide:/ˈmɒnəʊˌsækəraɪd/ /ˌmɑːnoʊˈsækəraɪd/| n. |单糖|A simple sugar that cannot be hydrolyzed to give a simpler sugar. Monosaccharides are the basic building blocks of carbohydrates. They are typically colorless, crystalline solids that are soluble in water.  Examples include glucose, fructose, and galactose.|Glucose is a monosaccharide that provides energy to the body. |近义词：simple sugar |反义词：polysaccharide
