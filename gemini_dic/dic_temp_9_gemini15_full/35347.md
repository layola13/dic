co-estate:/ˌkoʊˈesteɪt/| n. |共有遗产，共同财产|A co-estate refers to property or assets jointly owned by two or more individuals, often as a result of inheritance or a shared business venture.  It implies shared ownership and control, with the specifics determined by the agreement or legal documents establishing the co-ownership.| Example:  The brothers inherited a co-estate consisting of a large farm and several commercial buildings.  兄弟俩继承了一处共有遗产，包括一个大型农场和几座商业建筑。|近义词：joint estate, common property|反义词：sole estate, separate property|名词
