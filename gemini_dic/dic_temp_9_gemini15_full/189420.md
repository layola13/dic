sail:/seɪl/| n., v.|航行；帆；乘船航行|

英文解析:
Noun: A sail is a piece of fabric used to catch the wind and propel a boat or ship. It can also refer to the act of sailing.
Verb: To sail means to travel on water in a boat or ship, using sails or an engine.


例句:
The ship set sail at dawn. = 这艘船在黎明时启航。

We sailed across the ocean. = 我们横渡了海洋。


近义词:
voyage, cruise, navigate


反义词:
None directly antonymous, but  "remain ashore" could be considered a contextual opposite.


词性:
Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The boat sails smoothly. = 这艘船航行平稳。
They sail every weekend. = 他们每个周末都去航海。

一般过去时:
We sailed to the island yesterday. = 我们昨天航行到那个岛屿。
The ship sailed under full sail. = 那艘船在满帆情况下航行。

现在完成时:
They have sailed around the world. = 他们已经环游世界了。
She has sailed this route many times. = 她已经多次航行过这条路线了。

过去完成时:
They had sailed for days before they reached land. = 他们在到达陆地之前已经航行了数天。
The captain had sailed those waters many years ago. = 船长多年前就航行过那些水域。

将来时:
We will sail to Greece next summer. = 我们明年夏天将航行到希腊。
The yacht will sail at noon. = 游艇将在中午启航。

虚拟语气:
If I had a boat, I would sail the seven seas. = 如果我有一艘船，我将航行七大洋。
I wish I could sail to that distant island. = 我希望我能航行到那个遥远的岛屿。

被动语态:
The boat was sailed by an experienced captain. = 这艘船由一位经验丰富的船长驾驶。
The ship is being sailed towards the harbor. = 船正在驶向港口。

疑问句:
Does the boat sail today? = 这艘船今天航行吗？
When will the ship sail? = 船什么时候启航？

否定句:
The ship did not sail because of the storm. = 由于暴风雨，船没有航行。
They won't sail in this rough weather. = 他们不会在这种恶劣天气下航行。


PHRASE->
set sail: 起航
under sail: 在航行中
full sail: 满帆
sail close-hauled: 迎风紧帆航行
sail close to the wind: 冒险行事


词源:
From Old English *sægel, from Proto-Germanic *segilą, from Proto-Indo-European *segh-.  Cognates exist in other Germanic languages (e.g., German Segel).  The word's evolution reflects the importance of sailing in early Germanic cultures.
