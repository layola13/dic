induce:/ɪnˈdjuːs/ | vt. | 引诱；导致；引起；促使 | To persuade or influence (someone) to do something. To bring about or cause (something) to happen. | 例句：The advertisement is designed to induce people to buy the product.  这个广告旨在诱导人们购买该产品。

The doctor induced labor to avoid complications.  医生为了避免并发症而催产。


近义词：persuade, influence, cause, bring about, trigger | 反义词：deter, prevent, discourage


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He induces sleep with meditation. = 他通过冥想催眠。
The low pressure induces rain. = 低气压导致降雨。


一般过去时:
The evidence induced the jury to reach a guilty verdict. = 证据促使陪审团做出有罪判决。
The doctor induced vomiting to remove the poison. = 医生诱导呕吐以排出毒物。


现在完成时:
His speech has induced many people to donate. = 他的演讲促使许多人捐款。
The heatwave has induced a drought. = 热浪导致了干旱。


过去完成时:
The misleading information had induced them to make a wrong decision. = 误导性信息已导致他们做出错误的决定。
His previous experience had induced him to act cautiously. = 他之前的经验促使他谨慎行事。


将来时:
The new policy will induce economic growth. = 新政策将促进经济增长。
The rising temperature will induce melting of glaciers. = 气温上升将导致冰川融化。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, it might induce a different verdict. = 如果证据更充分，可能会导致不同的判决。
I wish the medicine would induce sleep quickly. = 我希望这种药能迅速催眠。


被动语态:
Sleep was induced by the medication. = 睡眠是由药物诱导的。
The change was induced by external factors. = 这种变化是由外部因素引起的。


疑问句:
What factors induced this change? = 什么因素导致了这种变化？
What can induce a person to act so recklessly? = 什么能诱使一个人如此鲁莽行事？


否定句:
The teacher's methods failed to induce any improvement in the student's grades. = 老师的方法未能提高学生的成绩。
Nothing could induce him to change his mind. = 什么都不能让他改变主意。


PHRASE->
induce vomiting = 催吐
induce labor = 催产
induce current = 感应电流
induce a reaction = 引起反应
induce hypothermia = 诱导低温


词源:
From Old French *induire, from Latin inducere "to lead in," from in- "in" + ducere "to lead."
