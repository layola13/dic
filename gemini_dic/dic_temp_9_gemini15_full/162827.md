precanvass:/ˌpriːˈkæn.væs/ | vt. |预先调查；提前宣传 | To make a preliminary survey or canvass (of votes, opinions, etc.), especially before an election.  |例句：The campaign team precanvassed the district to identify potential supporters.  竞选团队预先调查了该地区以确定潜在的支持者。
The candidate precanvassed the neighborhood, distributing flyers and speaking with residents.  候选人在附近地区提前宣传，分发传单并与居民交谈。|近义词：preliminary survey, advance polling, sound out |反义词：post-election analysis
