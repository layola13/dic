graded:/ˈɡreɪdɪd/| adj.|分级的，分等级的，评分的|Having been arranged or classified into grades or categories, often according to quality, level, or achievement.|The school uses a graded system to assess students' performance. 这所学校使用分级制度来评估学生的成绩。|近义词：ranked, classified, stratified, tiered|反义词：ungraded, unranked


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher graded the exams carefully. = 老师仔细批改了考试卷。
The company graded its employees based on performance. = 公司根据业绩对员工进行了分级。

一般过去时:
The professor graded the papers last night. = 教授昨晚批改了论文。
She graded the apples according to size. = 她按大小给苹果分级。

现在完成时:
He has graded all the assignments. = 他已经批改了所有的作业。
The committee has graded the proposals. = 委员会已经对提案进行了评级。

过去完成时:
They had graded the essays before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期前已经批改了论文。
The judges had graded the competitors before announcing the results. = 评委在宣布结果之前已经对参赛者进行了评分。

将来时:
She will grade the tests tomorrow. = 她明天将批改试卷。
The school will grade the students based on their final exams. = 学校将根据学生的期末考试成绩对学生进行分级。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would have graded the papers more thoroughly. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会更仔细地批改论文。
I wish I had graded the exams more fairly. = 我希望我更公平地批改了考试卷。


被动语态:
The exams were graded by the teacher. = 考试卷由老师批改。
The students' work will be graded anonymously. = 学生的作业将匿名评分。

疑问句:
Have the papers been graded yet? = 论文批改好了吗？
How were the projects graded? = 项目是如何评分的？


否定句:
The teacher hasn't graded the tests yet. = 老师还没批改试卷。
The essays were not graded fairly. = 论文评判不公平。
