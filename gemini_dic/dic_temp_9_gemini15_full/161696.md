poverty-stricken: /ˈpɒvətiˌstrɪkən/ /ˈpɑːvərtiˌstrɪkən/ | adj. | 贫困交加的，穷困潦倒的 |  Extremely poor; suffering from poverty.  This adjective describes a state of being severely affected by poverty, often implying a lack of basic necessities and hardship. |

例句：

Many poverty-stricken families rely on government assistance.  许多贫困家庭依靠政府救济。

The poverty-stricken areas of the city lack adequate sanitation.  城市贫困地区的卫生设施不足。


近义词：impoverished, destitute, indigent, needy, deprived

反义词：affluent, wealthy, rich, prosperous

词性：形容词

词源:

* **poverty:** From Old French *poverte*, from Latin *paupertas*, from *pauper* "poor."
* **stricken:** Past participle of the verb *strike*, meaning "to affect suddenly and severely."  In this context, it implies being severely affected by poverty.
