go-for-broke:/ˌɡoʊ fɔːr ˈbroʊk/ | phrasal verb |孤注一掷；竭尽全力；铤而走险 | To risk everything in an attempt to achieve something.  This idiom implies a willingness to accept significant losses in pursuit of a potentially large gain.  It often suggests desperation or a lack of other options. |例句：He went for broke in the final round of the poker tournament, betting all his chips on one hand. 他在扑克锦标赛的最后一轮孤注一掷，把所有的筹码都押在一张牌上。


例句：The startup went for broke with their new marketing campaign, spending their entire remaining budget. 这家初创公司在其新的营销活动中孤注一掷，花掉了他们剩余的所有预算。


近义词：take a gamble, risk it all, play it all, bet the farm


反义词：play it safe, hedge one's bets, be cautious


词性：phrasal verb


USAGE->
This phrasal verb doesn't have tenses in the same way that regular verbs do. Its meaning remains consistent regardless of the grammatical tense used in the surrounding sentence.  The tense of the sentence simply shows *when* the action of "going for broke" took place.


一般现在时:
They go for broke every time they launch a new product. = 他们每次推出新产品都孤注一掷。
She goes for broke in her career. = 她为事业孤注一掷。


一般过去时:
They went for broke and lost everything. = 他们孤注一掷，输掉了所有东西。
He went for broke on that deal and made a fortune. = 他在那笔交易中孤注一掷，赚了一大笔钱。


现在完成时:
They have gone for broke several times, and it's paid off. = 他们已经好几次孤注一掷了，而且得到了回报。
She has gone for broke in her pursuit of a scholarship and got it. = 她为了获得奖学金而孤注一掷，并获得了它。


过去完成时:
They had gone for broke before, so they were cautious this time. = 他们以前曾孤注一掷过，所以这次他们很谨慎。
She had gone for broke with her investments and lost a significant amount of money. = 她曾对投资孤注一掷，损失了一大笔钱。


将来时:
They will go for broke in the next election. = 他们在下次选举中将孤注一掷。
He will go for broke to win the championship. = 他将孤注一掷赢得冠军。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't go for broke. = 如果我是你，我不会孤注一掷。
I wish I hadn't gone for broke. = 我真希望我没有孤注一掷。


被动语态:  The phrase "go for broke" is inherently active and rarely used passively.  A passive construction would sound unnatural.


疑问句:
Will they go for broke on this project? = 他们在这个项目上会孤注一掷吗？
Did she go for broke in the negotiations? = 她在谈判中孤注一掷了吗？


否定句:
They didn't go for broke; they played it safe. = 他们没有孤注一掷；他们很谨慎。
He decided not to go for broke this time. = 他这次决定不孤注一掷。



PHRASE->
go for broke on something:  孤注一掷做某事
go for broke in something:  在某事上孤注一掷
go for broke with something: 用某物孤注一掷
go for broke to do something: 为了做某事而孤注一掷
go for broke and ...: 孤注一掷并...
