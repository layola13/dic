met-with:/mɛt wɪð/ | v. | 会见；遭遇；受到；对…作出反应 | To encounter or experience something, often unexpectedly or with a particular reaction.  |例句：The proposal was met with strong opposition.  这个提案遭到了强烈反对。
The team met with success in their latest project.  团队在最新的项目中获得了成功。|近义词：encountered, faced, experienced |反义词：avoided, ignored


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The team meets with clients every week. = 团队每周都会和客户会面。
He meets with resistance to his ideas. = 他的想法遭到了抵制。


一般过去时:
We met with the professor yesterday. = 我们昨天会见了教授。
The project met with unforeseen difficulties. = 项目遇到了意想不到的困难。


现在完成时:
They have met with several potential investors. = 他们已经会见了几个潜在的投资者。
The company has met with considerable success recently. = 公司最近取得了相当大的成功。


过去完成时:
She had met with the board before making the decision. = 她做出决定之前已经与董事会会面了。
The negotiations had met with a deadlock before we intervened. = 在我们介入之前，谈判陷入了僵局。


将来时:
We will meet with the committee next week. = 我们下周将与委员会会面。
The new policy will likely be met with criticism. = 新政策可能会遭到批评。


虚拟语气:
If we had met with him earlier, the situation would have been different. = 如果我们早点见到他，情况就会不一样了。
I wish we had met with more success in our fundraising efforts. = 我希望我们在筹款工作中能取得更大的成功。


被动语态:
The proposal was met with immediate rejection. = 该提案立即遭到拒绝。
His concerns were met with understanding. = 他的担忧得到了理解。


疑问句:
Did you meet with any problems during the trip? = 你在旅途中遇到什么问题了吗？
Will the new law be met with approval? = 新法律会得到批准吗？


否定句:
We didn't meet with any resistance. = 我们没有遇到任何阻力。
The plan didn't meet with the expected success. = 计划没有取得预期的成功。


PHRASE->
be met with = 受到；遭遇
meet with an accident = 遭遇事故
meet with approval = 得到批准
meet with success = 获得成功
meet with disaster = 遭遇灾难
