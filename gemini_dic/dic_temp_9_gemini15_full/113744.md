macrolinguistics:/ˌmækroʊlɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks/| n. |宏观语言学|The study of language in its social and cultural context, considering factors such as language variation, language change, language contact, and the relationship between language and society.  It examines large-scale patterns and structures in language use and development. | Example: Macrolinguistics explores how societal structures influence language evolution. 宏观语言学探讨社会结构如何影响语言演变。|近义词：sociolinguistics,  ethnolinguistics |反义词：microlinguistics
