nonmarketability:/ˌnɒnˌmɑːkɪtəˈbɪləti/ | n. |不可销售性，缺乏市场价值 | The inability of a product or asset to be readily sold or traded in the market. This often arises from factors such as lack of demand, legal restrictions, or inherent characteristics of the product.  | Example: The nonmarketability of the specialized equipment made it difficult to sell the factory.  这家工厂难以出售，原因在于其专用设备的不可销售性。 |近义词：unsalability, illiquidity |反义词：marketability
