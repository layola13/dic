doozy:/ˈduːzi/| n. |了不起的人或事物，极好的东西，难题|a remarkably good, bad, or unusual person or thing|That cake was a real doozy!  那个蛋糕真是太棒了！|近义词：marvel, wonder, stunner, cracker|反义词：dud, nothing special


USAGE->

一般现在时:
That car is a doozy. = 那辆车真是了不起。
This problem is a real doozy. = 这个问题真是个难题。

一般过去时:
The party was a doozy. = 那次派对非常棒。
The test was a doozy. = 那次考试很难。

现在完成时:
We've had a doozy of a week. = 我们度过了艰难的一周。
She’s baked a doozy of a cake. = 她烤了一个很棒的蛋糕。


过去完成时:
He'd already faced a doozy of a challenge. = 他已经面临过一个巨大的挑战。
That was the dooziest thing I'd ever seen. = 那是我见过的最了不起的事情。

将来时:
The next project will be a doozy. = 下一个项目将会非常棘手。
This coming week promises to be a doozy. = 未来一周将会非常忙碌。

虚拟语气:
If I had known it was going to be such a doozy, I wouldn’t have gone. = 如果我知道会这么难，我就不去了。
It would be a doozy of a task to finish this in a week. = 要在一周内完成这项任务将是一项艰巨的任务。

被动语态:
The problem was considered a doozy by all. = 大家都认为这个问题很难。
The exam was described as a doozy by the students. = 学生们形容这次考试非常难。

疑问句:
Is this job a doozy? = 这份工作很难吗？
Was that cake a doozy? = 那块蛋糕很棒吗？

否定句:
That wasn't a doozy of a performance. = 那次表演并不精彩。
The movie wasn't a doozy. = 这部电影并不精彩。
