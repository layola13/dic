reunifying: /ˌriːˈjuːnɪfaɪɪŋ/ | v. | 再统一，重新统一 |  The act of uniting again; bringing together something that was previously divided.  The present participle of the verb "reunify". |

例句：
The country is undergoing a process of reunifying after decades of civil war. = 这个国家在经历了数十年的内战后正在进行重新统一的过程。

The reunifying of the family after years of separation was an emotional moment. = 分离多年后，家人重聚是一个令人动情的时刻。


近义词：reuniting, consolidating, unifying, integrating

反义词：dividing, separating, fragmenting

词性：动词（现在分词）


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government is reunifying the divided regions. = 政府正在统一分裂的地区。
They are reunifying their family business. = 他们正在重整他们的家族企业。


一般过去时:
The treaty reunified the two countries. = 这项条约使两国重新统一。
The company reunified its operations last year. = 该公司去年将其业务重新整合。


现在完成时:
They have reunified their family after many years apart. = 许多年分离后，他们已经使家庭重归于好。
The efforts to reunify the country have been largely successful. = 统一国家的努力已取得很大成功。


过去完成时:
Before the reunification, they had reunified their forces. = 在统一之前，他们已经统一了他们的部队。
The warring factions had reunified under a single leader. = 交战各派已在一个领导下统一。


将来时:
The leaders hope to reunify the nation by next year. = 领导人希望到明年统一国家。
They will reunify their assets after the merger. = 合并后，他们将重新整合资产。


虚拟语气:
If we could reunify the country, it would bring peace. = 如果我们能够统一国家，那将带来和平。
I wish they could reunify their families. = 我希望他们能够使家庭重归于好。


被动语态:
The divided territories were reunified by force. = 分裂的领土被武力统一。
The company's operations are being reunified under a new management team. = 公司的业务正在一个新的管理团队下重新整合。


疑问句:
Will the two countries ever reunify? = 两国最终会统一吗？
How can we reunify the fragmented community? = 我们如何才能使支离破碎的社区重新团结起来？


否定句:
They have not yet reunified the territories. = 他们尚未统一这些领土。
The attempts to reunify the country failed. = 统一国家的尝试失败了。


PHRASE->
reunifying forces = 重新整合力量
reunifying families = 使家庭重归于好
reunifying a nation = 统一一个国家
reunifying the economy = 恢复经济统一
reunifying the church = 使教会重归于好

词源:
re- (prefix indicating again or anew) + unify (from Latin "unus" meaning one, and "facere" meaning to make)  Therefore, reunifying means "making one again".
