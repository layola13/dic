ecossaise:/ˌɛkəˈseɪz/| n. |苏格兰舞曲；一首苏格兰舞曲风格的乐曲 |A lively Scottish dance in 2/4 or 6/8 time, typically with a characteristic rhythmic pattern.  It's often characterized by a light and graceful style. |例句：The orchestra played a spirited ecossaise. 管弦乐队演奏了一首充满活力的苏格兰舞曲。

The dancers performed an elegant ecossaise. 舞蹈演员表演了一段优雅的苏格兰舞曲。


近义词： reel, jig, strathspey (all refer to types of lively dances) |反义词：None (no direct antonym, as it refers to a specific type of dance) |词性:名词

词源: From the French word "écossaise," meaning "Scottish," ultimately derived from the earlier form "Écosse" (Scotland).  The dance itself originated in Scotland.
