sankhya:/ˈsæŋkjə/| n. |数论；萨满教；数论学派|Sankhya is a philosophical school of thought in ancient India that emphasizes the dualism of purusha (consciousness) and prakriti (matter). It's a system of understanding the universe and human existence through the lens of numbers and categories.|Sankhya philosophy posits that the universe is composed of 25 tattvas, or principles. |近义词：philosophy, metaphysics|反义词：无
