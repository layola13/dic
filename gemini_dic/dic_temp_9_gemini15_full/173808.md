quasi-plentifully:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈplɛntifli/| adj.| 准充足的，近乎丰富的 |  The word combines "quasi" (meaning seemingly, apparently, or to a certain extent) and "plentifully" (meaning in abundance). It describes a situation where something is almost, but not quite, plentiful.  It suggests a degree of scarcity despite appearing abundant. |例句：The resources, while quasi-plentifully available at first glance, proved insufficient for the project's demands.  乍一看资源似乎很充足，但事实证明它们不足以满足项目的需要。  |近义词：abundant, copious, ample, sufficient (but less so)|反义词：scarce, meager, insufficient, scanty|
