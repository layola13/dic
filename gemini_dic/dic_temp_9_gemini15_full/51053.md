desolating: /ˈdɛsəleɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 荒凉的；令人沮丧的；使人悲痛的 |  causing great sadness or distress; bleak and deserted.  | 例句：The desolating landscape stretched for miles.  = 荒凉的景色绵延数英里。

The desolating news of his death left us heartbroken. = 他去世的噩耗让我们心碎。


近义词：devastating, disheartening, depressing, desolate, bleak, forlorn

反义词：uplifting, encouraging, cheering, joyful, vibrant, lively


词源:  From Middle English *desolat, from Old French *desoler, from Latin *desolare "to lay waste," from *de- "completely" + *solare "to lay bare," related to *solus "alone."
