non-acute: /ˌnɒn əˈkjuːt/ | adj. | 非急性的；不严重的 | Not sharp or pointed; not severe or critical.  Describes conditions or illnesses that are not sudden or severe in onset and progression. |


例句：

The patient presented with non-acute abdominal pain. = 病人表现为非急性的腹痛。

The doctor determined the condition to be non-acute and treatable with medication. = 医生认定病情不严重，可以用药物治疗。


近义词：mild, chronic, gradual, slow-onset


反义词：acute, severe, critical, sudden-onset


词源:

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not" or "without".  Derived from Old French "non" and ultimately from Latin "non".

* **acute**:  Derived from Latin "acutus," meaning "sharp," "pointed," or "severe."  In medical contexts, it signifies a sudden onset and rapid progression of a condition.
