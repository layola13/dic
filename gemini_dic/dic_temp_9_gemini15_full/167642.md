priceable: /ˈpraɪsəbl/ | adj. | 可定价的，可估价的 | Capable of being priced or valued.  Something that is priceable can have a monetary value assigned to it.  | The antique vase was priceable, though determining its exact worth proved difficult.  | valuable, assessable, estimable | priceless, invaluable


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and therefore doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  The usage is straightforward, describing something that can be assigned a price.

例句1: The artwork is priceable, but its value is subjective.  = 这件艺术品是可以定价的，但其价值是主观的。

例句2:  All the items in the auction were priceable and catalogued. = 拍卖会上的所有物品都是可以定价的，并且已经编入目录。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "priceable".  The word is generally used independently to describe the characteristic of something.
