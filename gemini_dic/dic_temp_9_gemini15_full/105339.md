kick-start:/ˈkɪkˌstɑːrt/ | vt. n. |强行启动；刺激；推动；改进；|To start (an engine) by kicking a starter, or to give something a sudden boost to get it going.|例句：He kick-started the engine. 他强行启动了引擎。|例句：The government's new policies are intended to kick-start the economy. 政府的新政策旨在刺激经济。|近义词：boost, jump-start, stimulate |反义词：halt, stop, hinder


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They kick-start their projects every Monday. = 他们每周一启动他们的项目。
We kick-start the engine using the starter. = 我们用启动器强行启动引擎。


一般过去时:
She kick-started her career with a successful book. = 她凭借一本成功的书开启了自己的职业生涯。
