flogging:/ˈflɒɡɪŋ/ | n. v. |鞭打；痛斥；严厉批评 |The act of whipping or beating someone with a whip or similar instrument; a severe criticism or condemnation. |例句：The prisoner received a flogging for his crime.  囚犯因其罪行而受到鞭打。


The teacher gave the student a flogging for his bad behavior. 老师因学生的恶劣行为而严厉批评了他。|近义词：whipping, beating, lashing, castigation, rebuke |反义词：praise, commendation, reward


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flogs the horse mercilessly. 他无情地鞭打马匹。
She flogs the idea relentlessly in her speeches.  她在演讲中毫不留情地宣扬这个主意。


一般过去时:
They flogged the slaves every day. 他们每天鞭打奴隶。
The critic flogged the new film in his review.  评论家在他的评论中严厉批评了这部新电影。


现在完成时:
The government has flogged the same old policies for years. 政府多年来一直在推行同样的旧政策。(暗指批评)
He has flogged himself with guilt since the accident. 自事故发生以来，他一直为自己的罪恶感所折磨。


过去完成时:
The captain had flogged the sailors before the mutiny. 船长在水兵哗变之前鞭打了他们。
She had flogged herself with self-doubt for months. 她几个月来一直被自我怀疑所折磨。


将来时:
The farmer will flog his horses if they don't work harder. 如果马匹不更加努力工作，农夫将会鞭打它们。
The media will likely flog this story for weeks. 媒体可能会持续几周报道这个故事（指过度报道和批评）。


虚拟语气:
If he flogged the horse again, I would report him. 如果他再次鞭打那匹马，我会举报他。
If they had flogged the issue more publicly, maybe it would have been resolved sooner. 如果他们更公开地讨论这个问题，也许它会更快得到解决。


被动语态:
The slaves were flogged regularly. 奴隶定期被鞭打。
The new policy was flogged by the opposition party.  新政策遭到反对党的严厉批评。


疑问句:
Was the prisoner flogged? 囚犯被鞭打了吗？
Will this issue be flogged endlessly in the media?  这个问题会在媒体上被没完没了地批评吗？


否定句:
They didn't flog the suspect. 他们没有鞭打嫌疑犯。
The speaker didn't flog the same old points in his speech. 演讲者在演讲中没有反复强调同样的老观点。


PHRASE->
flog a dead horse:  徒劳无功
flog oneself: 自我鞭挞
flog the market:  操纵市场
flog the idea:  大力宣传某个想法
flog to death:  过度强调


词源:
Old English *flogan, from Proto-Germanic *flugōn.  The original meaning was "to flay" (remove skin), but the meaning shifted to whipping.  The connection is likely through the image of skin being flayed.
