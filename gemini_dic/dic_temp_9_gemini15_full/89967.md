horseshoeing: /ˈhɔːrsʃuːɪŋ/ | n. | 马蹄铁钉制；钉马蹄 | The act of fitting horseshoes onto a horse's hooves.  This involves shaping and fitting the horseshoe to the horse's hoof and then nailing it securely in place. | Example: The farrier was skilled in horseshoeing and quickly finished the job.  铁匠擅长钉马蹄，很快就完成了工作。| 近义词: shoeing (horses) | 反义词:  removing horseshoes

一般现在时:
The farrier horseshoes the horse every six months. = 蹄铁匠每六个月给马钉一次蹄铁。
He carefully examines the horse's hooves before horseshoeing. = 在钉马蹄之前，他仔细检查马蹄。
