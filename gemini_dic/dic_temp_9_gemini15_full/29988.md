cavin: /ˈkævɪn/ | n. | 坍塌；陷落；凹陷 | A sudden collapse or falling in of the earth's surface.  It can refer to a hollow or depression in the ground, often caused by subsidence or erosion.  |例句：The heavy rain caused a cavin in the road.  暴雨导致道路塌陷。


The miners discovered a large cavin in the mine shaft. 矿工们在矿井里发现了一个大坑。|近义词：sinkhole, collapse, subsidence, crater |反义词：elevation, uplift, rise |名词

词源:  The word "cavin" is a variant spelling of "cave," ultimately deriving from the Old French word "cave" meaning "hollow," "cave," or "vault."  The sense of collapse is a later development, reflecting the way in which caves can sometimes collapse.
