gallant:/ˈɡælənt/| adj.|英勇的；勇敢的；潇洒的；殷勤的|Brave, courteous, and chivalrous.  Often used to describe a man's behavior towards a woman, implying politeness and respect.|例句：He was a gallant knight, always ready to defend the weak. 他是一位英勇的骑士，总是乐于保护弱者。

例句：He made a gallant attempt to save the drowning child. 他勇敢地尝试救落水儿童。


近义词：brave, courageous, valiant, chivalrous, courteous


反义词：cowardly, craven, discourteous, rude


词源:  The word "gallant" comes from the Old French word "galant," meaning "lover,"  "lover,"  "boyfriend," or "gallant." This in turn derived from the Vulgar Latin word *gallant-, which had a similar meaning. The original sense emphasized fashionable elegance and courtliness, before evolving to encompass bravery and chivalry.
