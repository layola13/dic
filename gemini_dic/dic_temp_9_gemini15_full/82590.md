guardian:/ˈɡɑːrdiən/ /ˈɡɑːrdʒən/| n. |监护人，保护者，守护者 |A guardian is a person who protects someone or something.  It can refer to a legal protector of a minor or a person who safeguards something valuable. |例句：My aunt is my legal guardian.  我的姑姑是我的法定监护人。

The police act as guardians of the law. 警察是法律的守护者。|近义词：protector, custodian, keeper, ward |反义词：attacker, destroyer, adversary


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is the guardian of his younger siblings. 他是他弟弟妹妹的监护人。
She acts as a guardian of the ancient forest. 她充当着古老森林的守护者。

一般过去时:
My uncle was my guardian after my parents died. 我父母去世后，我的叔叔是我的监护人。
The knight was a guardian of the kingdom.  这位骑士是王国的守护者。

现在完成时:

He has acted as guardian for many orphaned children. 他为许多孤儿做过监护人。

过去完成时:
They had been guardians of the family's secret for generations.  他们世世代代守护着家族的秘密。
Before the war, he had been guardian of the royal treasury. 战争爆发前，他是王室金库的守护者。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If I had a guardian, I would feel safer. 如果我有一个监护人，我会感到更安全。

被动语态:
