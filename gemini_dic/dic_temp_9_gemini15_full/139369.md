obscurity:/əbˈskjʊərɪti/ | n. |模糊；晦涩；默默无闻 |The state or quality of being obscure; not easily understood; unknown or inconspicuous.  |例句：The meaning of the poem was lost in obscurity. 这首诗的意思被晦涩难懂掩盖了。
例句：He lived a life of obscurity, unknown to the wider world. 他过着默默无闻的生活，世人皆不知晓。|近义词：vagueness, ambiguity, darkness, anonymity |反义词：clarity, fame, prominence, renown |

词源:
From Old French obscurité, from Latin obscuritas, from obscurus ("dark, hidden").  The word's journey reflects its meaning: from "darkness" to "lack of clarity" and finally to "unnoticed, unknown".
