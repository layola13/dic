mistrust:/ˌmɪsˈtrʌst/| n.|不信任，怀疑|The feeling of not trusting someone or something; suspicion.|例句：Her mistrust of strangers made it difficult for her to make new friends.  她的不信任感使她难以结交新朋友。


例句：The incident fueled mistrust between the two countries.  这起事件加剧了两国之间的不信任。


近义词：suspicion, doubt, disbelief, skepticism|反义词：trust, confidence, faith|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He mistrusts the government's promises. 他不相信政府的承诺。
I mistrust his motives. 我不相信他的动机。

一般过去时:
She mistrusted him from the start. 她从一开始就不信任他。
They mistrusted her intentions. 他们不相信她的意图。

现在完成时:
I have mistrusted him ever since the incident. 从那件事之后我就一直不信任他。
We have mistrusted their leadership for years. 多年来我们一直不信任他们的领导。

过去完成时:
He had mistrusted her for a long time. 他已经不信任她很久了。
They had mistrusted the information received earlier. 他们早就不相信收到的信息了。

将来时:
I will mistrust his words in the future. 我将来不会相信他的话。
She will mistrust any promises he makes. 她不会相信他做出的任何承诺。

虚拟语气:
If I had not mistrusted him, I would have been better off. 如果我不曾不信任他，我的处境会更好。
I wish I hadn't mistrusted her judgment. 我真希望我没有不信任她的判断。

被动语态:
His honesty was mistrusted by many. 许多人不相信他的诚实。
The company's claims were mistrusted by investors. 投资人不相信该公司的说法。

疑问句:
Do you mistrust his abilities? 你不相信他的能力吗？
Why do they mistrust the new system? 他们为什么不信任新系统？

否定句:
I don't mistrust his judgment. 我不怀疑他的判断。
