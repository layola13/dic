obstreperousness: /ˌɒbˈstrɛpərəsnəs/ | /ˌɑːbˈstrɛpərəsnəs/ | n. |喧嚣；吵闹；难控制 | The quality or state of being obstreperous; noisily and stubbornly defiant; unruly.  It refers to loud and defiant behavior, often characterized by noise and a lack of control. |例句：The obstreperousness of the children made it impossible to have a peaceful conversation.  孩子们吵闹喧哗，根本无法平静地交谈。

The obstreperousness of the crowd at the concert became unbearable.  演唱会上的观众喧闹不堪，令人难以忍受。


近义词：noisiness, clamor, turbulence, rowdiness, boisterousness | 反义词：quietness, calmness, peacefulness, orderliness


词源:  From Middle English *obstreperous, from Anglo-French *obstreperus, from Latin obstreperō “make a noise against, shout down,” from ob- “against” + strepō “make a noise.”
