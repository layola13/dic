nickel: /ˈnɪkəl/ | n. |镍；五分镍币 |

英文解析:Nickel is a hard, silvery-white metal that is resistant to corrosion.  It is also the name of a five-cent coin in the United States and Canada.  The chemical symbol for nickel is Ni.

例句:

The necklace is made of nickel.  这条项链是用镍做的。

I need some nickels for the vending machine. 我需要一些五分镍币来用自动售货机。


近义词:  (as a metal)  metal,  (as a coin)  five-cent piece, dime (in terms of value)


反义词:  (There aren't direct antonyms for "nickel" in either its metal or coin sense.)


词源:The word "nickel" comes from the German word "Kupfernickel," literally meaning "copper devil." This name was given to a mineral (nickeline) that resembled copper ore but yielded no copper, hence the "devilish" connotation.  The name eventually shortened to "nickel," specifically referring to the metal itself.
