monopolization:/ˌmənɒpəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |垄断；独占|The act or process of monopolizing; the state of being monopolized.  It refers to the exclusive possession or control of the supply or trade in a commodity or service.  This often leads to higher prices and reduced competition. |例句：The monopolization of the oil market led to soaring fuel prices.  石油市场的垄断导致燃油价格飙升。|例句：The government is investigating allegations of monopolization in the telecommunications industry. 政府正在调查电信行业的垄断指控。|近义词：control, domination, domination, cornering the market|反义词：competition, free market

一般现在时:
The company monopolizes the market. = 这家公司垄断了市场。
This technology monopolizes the industry’s attention. =这项技术独占了业界的注意力。


一般过去时:
Standard Oil monopolized the oil industry in the late 19th century. = 19世纪后期，标准石油公司垄断了石油工业。
The railroad company monopolized transportation in that region. = 那家铁路公司垄断了该地区的交通运输。


现在完成时:
They have monopolized the conversation for the past hour. = 他们过去一小时一直在独占谈话。
This company has monopolized the software market for years. = 这家公司多年来一直垄断着软件市场。


过去完成时:
Before the antitrust laws, several companies had monopolized various sectors. = 在反托拉斯法出台之前，几家公司已经垄断了各个行业。
The firm had monopolized the local market for decades before facing competition. = 在面临竞争之前，这家公司几十年来一直垄断了当地市场。


将来时:
Will this new technology monopolize the market? =这项新技术会垄断市场吗？
They predict that AI will monopolize many jobs in the future. = 他们预测人工智能将来会垄断许多工作。


虚拟语气:
If this company were allowed to continue, it would monopolize the entire market. = 如果允许这家公司继续下去，它将垄断整个市场。
I wish the government would prevent this company from monopolizing the market. = 我希望政府能够阻止这家公司垄断市场。


被动语态:
The market was monopolized by a single company. = 市场被一家公司垄断。
The conversation was monopolized by one person. = 谈话被一个人独占了。


疑问句:
Does this company monopolize the market? = 这家公司垄断了市场吗？
Is this technology likely to monopolize the industry? =这项技术可能会垄断行业吗？


否定句:
The company does not monopolize the market. = 这家公司没有垄断市场。
This technology did not monopolize the industry's attention. = 这项技术并没有独占业界的注意力。
