pettily:/ˈpɛtɪli/| adv.| 小气地；琐碎地；卑鄙地 | In a petty or mean-spirited way;  trivially.  It describes actions or behaviors characterized by small-mindedness, resentment, or spitefulness, often over insignificant matters. |例句：He pettily refused to share his toys with his younger brother. 他小气地拒绝和弟弟分享玩具。

例句：She complained pettily about the small inconvenience. 她为这件小事琐碎地抱怨。


近义词：meanly, spitefully, grudgingly, ungenerously

反义词：generously, magnanimously, kindly, charitably


词源:  Derived from the adjective "petty," meaning small, insignificant, or of little importance. The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective into an adverb, modifying verbs and describing how an action is performed.  The ultimate origin traces back to Old French "petit," meaning "small."
