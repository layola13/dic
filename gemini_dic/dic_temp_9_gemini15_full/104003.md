jugulars:/ˈdʒʌɡjʊlərz/ | n. |颈静脉(复数) |The jugulars are the large veins in the neck that carry blood from the head and brain back to the heart.  They are crucial for blood circulation and are sometimes a site for medical procedures like venipuncture. |Example: The doctor carefully examined the patient's jugulars to check for any signs of infection. 医生仔细检查了病人的颈静脉，查看是否有任何感染迹象。

The surgeon needed to access the patient's jugular veins during the operation. 外科医生在手术中需要进入病人的颈静脉。|近义词:neck veins |反义词:None
