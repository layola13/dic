marionettes:/ˌmærɪəˈnet/| n. |木偶，傀儡|A marionette is a puppet operated by strings attached to its limbs.  Marionettes are often used in puppet shows and theatrical performances. They are typically made of wood or other lightweight materials.|例句：The children were mesmerized by the intricate movements of the marionettes.孩子们被木偶复杂的动作迷住了。

The puppeteer skillfully manipulated the marionettes, bringing them to life.  木偶戏演员熟练地操纵着木偶，使它们栩栩如生。|近义词：puppets, dolls|反义词：None specifically, but could consider "real people" as a contrasting concept.

词源:
From Italian *marionetta*, diminutive of *Maria*, name of a popular puppet figure.  The name spread to other languages and eventually became "marionette" in English.  This reflects the historical association of puppets with religious or folk traditions.
