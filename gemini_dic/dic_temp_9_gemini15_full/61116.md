elohist:/ˈɛləhaɪst/| n. |以罗作者；以罗文作者|A writer or school of writers whose work is characterized by a particular style and theological perspective found in certain passages of the Hebrew Bible, particularly those focusing on the covenant relationship between God and Israel.  The Elohist's writing is distinguished from that of the Yahwist by its use of "Elohim" (God) as the primary name for God, its more formal and less anthropomorphic portrayal of God, and its focus on northern Israel.|The Elohist source is considered one of the four main sources of the Pentateuch (JEDP).|近义词：Yahwist, Priestly writer, Deuteronomist |反义词：无
