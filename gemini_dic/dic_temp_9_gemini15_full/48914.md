defrocking:/dɪˈfrɒkɪŋ/| v.|剥夺圣职；解除神职；|The act of stripping someone of their clerical or religious office or status.  This typically involves a formal process and often carries significant consequences for the individual's career and reputation.|例句：The priest was defrocked for misconduct.  这位牧师因行为不端被剥夺了圣职。

The bishop decided to defrock the vicar after the scandal broke. 主教决定在丑闻爆发后将牧师逐出教籍。|近义词：unfrocking, dismissing, removing, ousting |反义词：ordaining, consecrating

词源:
The word "defrocking" comes from the verb "defrock," which is formed by adding the prefix "de-" (meaning "to remove" or "to deprive of") to the noun "frock," referring to the long gown worn by clergy.  Therefore, "defrocking" literally means "removing the frock" of a clergyman.
