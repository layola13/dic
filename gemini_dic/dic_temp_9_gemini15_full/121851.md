misrepresent:/ˌmɪsˌreprɪˈzɛnt/| vt.|歪曲；误传；不实地描述|To give a false or misleading account of; to present wrongly.|例句：He misrepresented the facts to the police. 他向警方歪曲了事实。|例句：The article misrepresented the findings of the study. 这篇文章歪曲了研究结果。|近义词：distort, falsify, misstate|反义词：represent accurately, truthfully portray


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He misrepresents his qualifications on his resume. = 他在简历上歪曲了他的资历。
The company misrepresents its products in its advertising. = 公司在其广告中歪曲了其产品。


一般过去时:
The witness misrepresented the events of that night. = 证人歪曲了那天晚上发生的事情。
She misrepresented her age to get the job. = 她谎报年龄以获得这份工作。


现在完成时:
The politician has repeatedly misrepresented his position on the issue. = 这位政治家多次歪曲了他在这个问题上的立场。
They have misrepresented the risks involved in the investment. = 他们歪曲了这项投资所涉及的风险。


过去完成时:
He had misrepresented his intentions from the very beginning. = 从一开始他就歪曲了他的意图。
The media had misrepresented the situation before the full facts emerged. = 在全部事实出现之前，媒体已经歪曲了情况。


将来时:
They will misrepresent the data to support their argument. = 他们会歪曲数据来支持他们的论点。
He will likely misrepresent the situation to his boss. = 他很可能会向老板歪曲情况。


虚拟语气:
If he misrepresented the facts, he would be in trouble. = 如果他歪曲事实，他就会有麻烦。
I wish he hadn't misrepresented my ideas in his report. = 我真希望他在报告中没有歪曲我的想法。


被动语态:
The facts were misrepresented in the news report. = 新闻报道歪曲了事实。

疑问句:

否定句:
He did not misrepresent the facts, he simply omitted some details. = 他没有歪曲事实，只是省略了一些细节。
The article did not misrepresent the scientific findings. = 这篇文章没有歪曲科学发现。
