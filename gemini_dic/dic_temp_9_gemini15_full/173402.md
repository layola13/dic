quasi-eligible: /ˈkwɑːziˈɛlɪdʒəbəl/ | adj. | 准合格的，几乎合格的 |  Almost eligible; meeting most but not all the requirements for eligibility.  It implies a state of near-eligibility, often suggesting a conditional or provisional status. |


例句：

He was quasi-eligible for the scholarship, lacking only a single required course. = 他几乎符合奖学金的资格，只差一门必修课。

The company considered her quasi-eligible for promotion, pending completion of a training program. = 公司认为她几乎符合晋升的条件，但需完成培训计划后才能确定。


近义词：nearly eligible, almost eligible, provisionally eligible


反义词：ineligible, unqualified


词性：形容词


词源:

* **quasi-**:  from Latin *quasi* meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation.

* **eligible**: from Old French *eligible*, from Latin *eligere* "to choose," "to select."
