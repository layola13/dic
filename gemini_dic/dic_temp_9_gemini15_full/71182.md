fissuring: /ˈfɪʃəriŋ/ | v. | 裂缝；产生裂缝；分裂；出现分歧 | The process of forming fissures or cracks; splitting or breaking apart.  It can also refer to the gradual development of divisions or disagreements within a group or organization. |例句：The earthquake caused extensive fissuring in the ground. 地震在地面上造成了严重的裂缝。

The prolonged drought led to fissuring in the riverbed. 长时间的干旱导致河床出现裂缝。


近义词：cracking, splitting, fracturing, breaking apart, dividing

反义词：uniting, merging, consolidating, joining, healing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ground is fissuring due to the drought. = 由于干旱，地面正在裂开。
The political party is fissuring over internal disagreements. = 该政党因内部意见分歧而出现分裂。


一般过去时:
The ancient vase fissured during the move. = 古董花瓶在搬运过程中裂开了。
The team fissured after a series of defeats. = 该团队在连续的失败后分崩离析。


现在完成时:
The concrete has fissured in several places. = 混凝土在几个地方已经裂开了。
The relationship has fissured beyond repair. = 这段关系已经裂痕难补。


过去完成时:
The dam had fissured before the flood. = 大坝在洪水到来之前就已经出现裂缝了。
The alliance had fissured irrevocably before the negotiations began. = 在谈判开始之前，联盟就已经不可挽回地分裂了。


将来时:
The old building will fissure if not properly maintained. = 如果不进行适当的维护，这座旧建筑将会开裂。
The company will fissure under the pressure of competition. = 在竞争压力下，这家公司将会分裂。


虚拟语气:
If the foundation hadn't been weak, the wall wouldn't have fissured. = 如果地基不那么薄弱，墙就不会裂了。
If the negotiations had been more successful, the group would not have fissured. = 如果谈判更成功，这个团体就不会分裂了。


被动语态:
The road was fissured by the heavy traffic. = 道路因交通繁忙而开裂。
The agreement was fissured by opposing factions. = 协议被对立派系破坏了。


疑问句:
Has the wall fissured anywhere? = 墙壁有任何地方裂开了吗？
Is the company fissuring due to financial problems? = 公司是因为财务问题而出现分裂吗？


否定句:
The foundation did not fissure during the earthquake. = 地基在地震中没有裂开。
The team did not fissure despite the setbacks. = 尽管遭受挫折，团队并没有分裂。

词源:
From Middle English *fissuren, from Old French fissurer, from Latin fissura (“a cleft, fissure”), from findere (“to cleave, split”).
