co-morbidity:/ˌkoʊmɔːrˈbɪdəti/ | n. |合并症，共病| The simultaneous presence of two or more diseases or medical conditions in a patient.  It refers to the occurrence of one or more additional diseases or disorders in a person who already has one disease.  The additional diseases may or may not be related to the primary disease. | Example:  The study found a high rate of co-morbidity between obesity and type 2 diabetes. 这项研究发现肥胖症和2型糖尿病之间存在很高的合并症发生率。


Example:  Patients with chronic heart failure often experience co-morbidities such as hypertension and diabetes. 患有慢性心力衰竭的患者常常伴有高血压和糖尿病等合并症。

近义词：concurrent disorders, multiple diseases, associated conditions

反义词：single disease, isolated condition

词源:
The word "co-morbidity" is a modern medical term.  It's a combination of "co-" (meaning "together" or "with") and "morbidity" (meaning "the state of being diseased").  Therefore, "co-morbidity" literally means "the state of having multiple diseases together".
