pilo:/ˈpaɪloʊ/| n. |飞行员，领航员|A pilot is a person who flies an aircraft.  Pilots are highly trained professionals responsible for the safe operation of aircraft.|例句：The pilot landed the plane safely despite the storm.  飞行员在暴风雨中安全地降落了飞机。|近义词：aviator, airman, flight crew|反义词：passenger


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The pilot flies the plane every day. = 飞行员每天都驾驶飞机。
The pilots check the aircraft before takeoff. = 飞行员在起飞前检查飞机。


一般过去时:
The pilot flew the plane to London. = 飞行员驾驶飞机飞往伦敦。
The pilots checked the weather before the flight. = 飞行员在飞行前检查了天气。


现在完成时:
The pilot has flown over 10,000 hours. = 这位飞行员已经飞行了超过一万小时。
The pilots have completed their pre-flight checks. = 飞行员已经完成了他们的飞行前检查。


过去完成时:
The pilot had already landed before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，飞行员已经降落了。
The pilots had reviewed the flight plan before departure. = 飞行员在出发前已经审查了飞行计划。


将来时:
The pilot will fly to Paris tomorrow. = 飞行员明天将飞往巴黎。
The pilots will be conducting training exercises next week. = 飞行员下周将进行训练演习。


虚拟语气:
If the pilot were more experienced, he would have handled the situation better. = 如果飞行员更有经验，他会更好地处理这种情况。
I wish the pilot had warned us about the turbulence. = 我希望飞行员能警告我们气流颠簸。


被动语态:
The plane was piloted by a highly skilled aviator. = 这架飞机由一位技术精湛的飞行员驾驶。
The flight plan is being reviewed by the pilots. = 飞行计划正在由飞行员审查。


疑问句:
Who is piloting the plane? = 谁在驾驶飞机？
How many pilots are on board? = 机上有多少飞行员？


否定句:
The pilot did not see the other aircraft. = 飞行员没有看到另一架飞机。
The pilots were not able to land due to bad weather. = 由于天气恶劣，飞行员无法降落。
