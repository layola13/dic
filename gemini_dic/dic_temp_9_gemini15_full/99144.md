intercessor:/ˌɪntərˈsɛsər/| n. |代祷者；调解人；说情者 |A person who intercedes on behalf of another.  Someone who acts as a mediator or go-between, especially in prayer or pleading. |例句：She acted as an intercessor between the warring factions. 她充当了交战各派之间的调解人。

例句：The priest served as an intercessor, praying for the healing of the sick.  这位牧师充当了代祷者，为病人的康复祈祷。


近义词：mediator, advocate, pleader, go-between

反义词：opponent, adversary


词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The priest intercedes for the forgiveness of sins. = 牧师为罪的赦免代祷。
She intercedes on behalf of her friend. = 她为朋友说情。


一般过去时:
He interceded with the judge for a lighter sentence. = 他向法官求情以争取从轻判决。
The ambassador interceded in the conflict. = 大使调解了冲突。


现在完成时:
She has interceded many times for her family. = 她多次为家人说情。
They have interceded on behalf of the refugees. = 他们为难民说情。


过去完成时:
He had interceded with his father before asking for the car. = 他在向父亲要车之前已经向他求过情了。
They had already interceded with the authorities before the meeting. = 会议前他们已向当局求过情了。


将来时:
