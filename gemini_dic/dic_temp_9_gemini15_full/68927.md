fatalistically: /ˌfeɪtəˈlɪstɪkli/ | adv. | 宿命论地，认命地 |  Accepting or enduring unavoidable evils or difficulties with passive resignation.  It implies a belief that events are predetermined and inevitable. |


例句：

He accepted his fate fatalistically. = 他宿命论地接受了他的命运。

She fatalistically shrugged and went back to work. = 她认命地耸耸肩，又回去工作了。


近义词：resignedly, passively, helplessly, uncomplainingly

反义词：actively, defiantly, hopefully, optimistically


词源:

From *fatalistic* + *-ly*.  *Fatalistic* derives from *fatalist* (a believer in fatalism), which in turn comes from *fatalism* (the doctrine that all events are predetermined and inevitable).  *Fatalism* ultimately stems from *fatal* (causing death or ruin; inevitable), which has roots in the Latin word *fatalis* ("fateful," "destined").
