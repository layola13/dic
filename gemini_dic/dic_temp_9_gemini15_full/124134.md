mortices:/ˈmɔːrtɪsiz/| n.|榫眼；榫孔|A mortise is a hole cut into a piece of wood to receive a tenon, forming a joint.  Mortices are typically rectangular or square.|例句：The carpenter carefully chiseled out the mortices for the wooden joints. 木匠仔细地凿出木质接头的榫眼。  The strength of the chair depends on the precision of the mortices and tenons. 椅子结实与否取决于榫眼和榫头的精确度。|近义词：hole, socket, recess |反义词：tenon (the complementary piece)
