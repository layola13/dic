malacology:/ˌmæləˈkɒlədʒi/| n. |软体动物学|The branch of zoology that studies mollusks.  It encompasses their anatomy, physiology, behavior, ecology, and evolution.  Malacology involves the study of a wide variety of mollusks, including snails, clams, squid, and octopuses.|例句：She dedicated her life to malacology, publishing several groundbreaking papers on cephalopod intelligence.|近义词：conchology (if focusing on shells)|反义词：无|名词
