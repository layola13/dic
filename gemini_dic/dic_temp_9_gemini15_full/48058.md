declamations:/ˌdekləˈmeɪʃənz/ | n. |雄辩的演说；朗诵；慷慨激昂的演说 |The act of declaiming; a formal speech delivered in a rhetorical manner.  It often involves passionate and expressive delivery. |例句：His declamations against the government were met with both applause and outrage.  他的反对政府的慷慨激昂的演说既赢得了喝彩也招致了愤怒。

例句：The student's declamations were impressive, showcasing their mastery of rhetoric. 学生的朗诵令人印象深刻，展现了他们对修辞的掌握。


近义词：orations, speeches, addresses, pronouncements | 反义词：whispers, murmurs


词源:  From Latin *declamatio, from *declamare "to cry out," "to speak loudly," from de- "down, thoroughly" + clamare "to shout."  The word's evolution reflects its historical association with public speaking and dramatic performances.
