monospore:/ˈmɒnəʊspɔːr/|/ˈmɑːnoʊˌspoʊr/| n. |单孢子|A monospore is a spore produced singly, as opposed to a spore produced in a cluster or group.  It is a single reproductive cell capable of developing into a new organism without fusion with another cell.  The term is often used in the context of fungi and algae. |Example:  The fungus produced a single monospore, which then germinated to form a new mycelium.  单细胞真菌产生了一个单孢子，然后发芽形成新的菌丝体。|近义词：single spore|反义词：multiple spore, spore cluster
