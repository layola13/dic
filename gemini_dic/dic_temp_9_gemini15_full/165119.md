preincentives:/ˌpriːɪnˈsɛntɪvz/ | n. | 预奖励；事前激励；提前激励 |  Preincentives refer to rewards or motivators offered *before* a task or performance is completed, aiming to encourage desired behavior or outcomes.  They are distinct from post-incentives which are given after the completion of a task.  They are often used in behavioral economics and organizational behavior to influence actions. | Example: The company offered preincentives in the form of bonuses to employees who completed their training early. 公司以提前完成培训的员工奖金的形式提供预奖励。


近义词：advance motivators, upfront rewards, prior inducements

反义词：post-incentives, retrospective rewards


词源:  The word is a combination of "pre-" (meaning before) and "incentives" (motivators or rewards).  "Incentive" itself originates from the Latin word "incentivus," meaning "inciting" or "urging."
