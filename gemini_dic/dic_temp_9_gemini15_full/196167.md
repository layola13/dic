septuagint:/ˈsɛptjuədʒɪnt/| n. |七十人译本|The Septuagint is a Greek translation of the Hebrew Bible, traditionally believed to have been produced by 70 or 72 scholars in Alexandria, Egypt, in the 3rd century BC.  It is a crucial text in the history of Christianity and Judaism, as it became the basis for many later translations and interpretations of the Bible.  It's considered a significant historical and religious artifact.|Example: The Septuagint played a vital role in the development of early Christianity.|近义词：Greek Old Testament translation|反义词：无
