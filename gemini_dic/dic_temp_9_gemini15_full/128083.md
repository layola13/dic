nervous:/ˈnɜːrvəs/| adj.|紧张的，焦虑的|Feeling or showing nervousness.  Characterized by or causing nervousness.|例句：She felt nervous before the interview. 她面试前感到紧张。

例句：He was nervous about the upcoming exam. 他对即将到来的考试感到紧张。


近义词：anxious, apprehensive, uneasy, tense, agitated|反义词：calm, relaxed, composed, tranquil


词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is nervous about the test. 他对考试感到紧张。
She is always nervous before public speaking. 她每次在公众面前讲话前都感到紧张。


一般过去时:
I was nervous during the flight. 我在飞行过程中感到紧张。
They were nervous when they heard the bad news. 他们听到坏消息时感到紧张。


现在完成时:
I have been nervous all day. 我一整天都感到紧张。
She has never been so nervous in her life. 她一生从未如此紧张过。


过去完成时:
I had been nervous all week leading up to the event. 在活动前的一周里，我一直都很紧张。
They had been nervous about the results for months. 几个月来，他们一直对结果感到紧张。


将来时:
I will be nervous when I meet her parents. 当我见到她父母时我会很紧张。
He will probably be nervous during the presentation. 他在演示过程中可能会很紧张。


虚拟语气:
If I weren't nervous, I would perform better. 如果我不紧张，我会表现得更好。
I wish I weren't so nervous right now. 我希望我现在不要这么紧张。


被动语态: (很少用被动语态)  The audience was made nervous by his strange behavior. 他的奇怪行为让观众感到紧张。
The children were made nervous by the loud noises. 嘈杂的声音让孩子们感到紧张。


疑问句:
Are you nervous about the exam? 你对考试紧张吗？
Is she nervous about the upcoming trip? 她对即将到来的旅行紧张吗？


否定句:
I am not nervous about the speech. 我对演讲并不紧张。
He wasn't nervous at all during the performance. 他在表演过程中一点也不紧张。


PHRASE->
nervous breakdown: 精神崩溃
nervous system: 神经系统
get nervous: 变得紧张
make someone nervous: 使某人紧张
nervous energy: 紧张的精力
