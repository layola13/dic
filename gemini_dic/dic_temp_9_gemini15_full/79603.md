glorifies:/ˈɡlɔːrɪfaɪz/ | vt. | 赞美；颂扬；使…显得光荣 | To praise or celebrate something or someone; to make something seem more important or impressive than it really is. | 例句：The poem glorifies the beauty of nature. 这首诗歌赞美了大自然的美丽。

The media glorifies violence in some movies.  媒体在一些电影中美化了暴力。


近义词：exalt, praise, celebrate, magnify, extol | 反义词：denigrate, belittle, disparage, condemn, criticize | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He glorifies his achievements. = 他赞美他的成就。
The church glorifies God. = 教堂颂扬上帝。

一般过去时:
They glorified their ancestors. = 他们歌颂他们的祖先。
The artist glorified the human form. = 艺术家美化了人体。

现在完成时:
She has glorified her country through her work. = 她通过她的工作使她的国家更加光荣。
The media has glorified the celebrity's lifestyle. = 媒体美化了这位名人的生活方式。

过去完成时:
The propaganda had glorified the leader's image. = 之前的宣传已经美化了领导人的形象。
They had glorified their victory for years. = 他们多年来一直歌颂他们的胜利。

将来时:
He will glorify his family name. = 他将光宗耀祖。
The new building will glorify the city skyline. = 新建筑将使城市天际线更加辉煌。

虚拟语气:
If he won, he would glorify his victory. = 如果他赢了，他会赞美他的胜利。
I wish they would glorify the unsung heroes. = 我希望他们能赞美那些无名英雄。

被动语态:
His actions were glorified by the media. = 他的行为被媒体赞扬。
The event was glorified in the history books. = 这个事件在史书中被美化了。

疑问句:
Does the novel glorify war? = 这部小说是否美化了战争？
What aspects of life does this painting glorify? = 这幅画美化了生活的哪些方面？

否定句:
The article did not glorify the violence. = 这篇文章没有美化暴力。
He refused to glorify his own achievements. = 他拒绝赞美自己的成就。
