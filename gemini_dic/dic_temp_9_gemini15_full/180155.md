reform:/rɪˈfɔːrm/|/rɪˈfɔːrm/| n./vt.|改革；改良；改造；改进|The act of improving or amending something, typically a social, political, or economic institution or practice.|例句：The government is planning extensive reforms to the healthcare system.  政府正计划对医疗体系进行大规模改革。|例句：She advocated for reforms to improve working conditions. 她提倡改革以改善工作条件。|近义词：amendment, improvement, revision|反义词：deterioration, decline, corruption|名词/动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government reforms the education system. = 政府正在改革教育体系。
She reforms her lifestyle. = 她正在改变她的生活方式。

一般过去时:
They reformed the legal system. = 他们改革了法律体系。
He reformed his bad habits. = 他改掉了他的坏习惯。

现在完成时:
They have reformed the tax laws. = 他们已经改革了税法。
She has reformed her approach to work. = 她已经改变了她的工作方式。

过去完成时:
They had reformed the company before the merger. = 在合并之前，他们已经改革了公司。
He had reformed his life before he met her. = 他在遇到她之前已经改变了自己的生活。

将来时:
They will reform the prison system. = 他们将改革监狱制度。
She will reform her spending habits. = 她将改变她的消费习惯。

虚拟语气:
If they reformed the voting system, more people would participate. = 如果他们改革了投票制度，更多的人会参与。
I wish they would reform the healthcare system. = 我希望他们能改革医疗体系。

被动语态:
The education system is being reformed. = 教育体系正在被改革。
His bad habits were reformed. = 他的坏习惯被改掉了。

疑问句:
Will they reform the social welfare system? = 他们会改革社会福利制度吗？
Has she reformed her ways? = 她改过自新了吗？

否定句:
They did not reform the economic policies. = 他们没有改革经济政策。
She has not reformed her spending habits yet. = 她还没有改变她的消费习惯。
