coinvestors: /ˌkoʊɪnˈvɛstərz/ | n. |共同投资者 |  Individuals or entities who invest in the same venture capital deal or project.  Coinvestors often collaborate to share risks and resources, potentially leading to a more robust investment strategy.  They may bring complementary expertise or networks to the table. | Example: Several angel investors acted as coinvestors in the startup's seed round, pooling their capital and knowledge.
