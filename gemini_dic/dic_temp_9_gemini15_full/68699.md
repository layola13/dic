fare:/feər/| n. v.| 票价；费用；食物；进展；过活|The fare on the bus was quite reasonable. 公交车票价相当合理。  The restaurant serves a variety of fares. 这家餐馆供应各种各样的食物。 How did you fare on your exam? 你的考试情况如何？|近义词：price, cost, food, progress|反义词：N/A


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bus fare costs $2. = 公交车票价是2美元。
The flight fare varies depending on the season. = 飞机票价根据季节而变化。

一般过去时:
The train fare was higher than I expected. = 火车票价比我预期的要高。
We fared well on our journey. = 我们旅途进展顺利。

现在完成时:
The fares have increased significantly this year. = 今年票价大幅上涨。
How have you fared since you started your new job? = 自从你开始新工作以来，你的情况如何？


过去完成时:
They had already paid their fares before boarding the train. = 他们在上火车之前已经付了车费。
We had fared poorly in the previous round of negotiations. = 我们在上一轮谈判中进展不顺利。


将来时:
The airfare will probably be expensive during peak season. = 在旺季，机票可能会很贵。
I hope we fare better next time. = 我希望我们下次能做得更好。


虚拟语气:
If the fare were lower, I would travel more often. = 如果票价更低，我会更频繁地旅行。
I wish the fare had been cheaper. = 我希望票价能便宜些。


被动语态:
The fares are determined by the transportation company. = 票价由运输公司决定。
The food was fared well by the critics. = 这食物受到评论家的好评。


疑问句:
What is the fare to London? = 到伦敦的票价是多少？
How did your presentation fare? = 你的演示文稿进展如何？


否定句:
The fare did not include baggage fees. = 票价不包含行李费。
The team did not fare well in the competition. = 该队在比赛中表现不佳。
