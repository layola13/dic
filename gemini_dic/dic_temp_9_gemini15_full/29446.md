catalogued: /ˈkætəlɒɡd/ | v. | 编目，登录 | To systematically record and list items in a catalog.  The librarian meticulously catalogued the newly acquired books.  图书馆员仔细地为新到的书籍编目。 | indexed, listed, registered | uncatalogued | 动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The museum curator catalogues the artifacts. = 博物馆馆长正在为文物编目。
She catalogues her extensive stamp collection. = 她正在为她大量的邮票收藏编目。


一般过去时:
He catalogued all the books in the library. = 他为图书馆的所有书籍编了目。
They catalogued the museum's exhibits last year. = 他们去年为博物馆的展品编了目。


现在完成时:
The archivist has catalogued thousands of documents. = 档案管理员已经为数千份文件编了目。
We have catalogued all the items for the auction. = 我们已经为拍卖的所有物品编了目。


过去完成时:
She had catalogued the entire collection before the exhibition opened. = 在展览开幕之前，她已经为整个收藏编了目。
The researchers had catalogued the data before analyzing it. = 研究人员在分析数据之前已经为其编了目。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would catalogue my photographs. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会为我的照片编目。
It would be helpful if the items were catalogued more efficiently. = 如果物品能够更有效率地编目，那就太好了。
