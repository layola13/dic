destinate:/ˈdɛstɪneɪt/| vt.|指定，注定，预定|To appoint or assign to a particular purpose or task; to intend or destine for a particular purpose or fate.|例句：The funds were destined for the relief of the victims. 这些资金被指定用于救济受害者。|例句：He was destined to become a great leader. 他注定要成为一位伟大的领导者。|近义词：allocate, assign, appoint, intend |反义词：undestine


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They designate a specific area for the project. = 他们为该项目指定了一个特定区域。
The company designates a budget for marketing. = 公司为市场营销指定了预算。


一般过去时:
She designated her son as her heir. = 她指定她的儿子为继承人。
The committee designated a spokesperson. = 委员会指定了一位发言人。


现在完成时:
The government has designated several areas as national parks. = 政府已将几个地区指定为国家公园。
We have designated a team to handle the crisis. = 我们已指定了一个团队来处理这场危机。


过去完成时:
They had designated a meeting place before the event. = 他们在那次活动之前已指定了一个会议地点。
The board had designated a new CEO before the announcement. = 董事会在宣布之前已指定了一位新的首席执行官。


将来时:
The council will designate a new building for the library. = 理事会将指定一栋新楼作为图书馆。
They will designate a special team for the investigation. = 他们将为调查指定一个特别小组。


虚拟语气:
If they designated more funds, the project would succeed. = 如果他们拨款更多，项目就会成功。
I wish they had designated a clearer plan. = 我希望他们制定一个更清晰的计划。


被动语态:
The land was designated for agricultural use. = 这块土地被指定用于农业用途。
The funds were designated for education. = 这些资金被指定用于教育。


疑问句:
What area has been designated for the new park? = 哪个地区被指定为新的公园？
Has a date been designated for the meeting? = 会议日期是否已确定？


否定句:
They did not designate a leader for the team. = 他们没有为团队指定领导者。
The committee did not designate a specific budget. = 委员会没有指定具体的预算。
