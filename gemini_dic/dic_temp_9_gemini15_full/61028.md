elite's:/eɪˈliːts/| n.|精英的（elite 的所有格）|The elite's power is undeniable. 精英的力量是不可否认的。|近义词：the ruling class's, the upper class's|反义词：the masses', the common people's|名词


WORD ORIGIN->
The word "elite" originates from the French word "élite,"  meaning "the choice," "the select," or "the best." It entered English in the 19th century.  The suffix '-s' indicates possessive case, showing that something belongs to or is associated with the elite.
