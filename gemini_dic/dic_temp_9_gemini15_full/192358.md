scringe:/skrɪndʒ/| v.|畏缩；退缩；局促不安|To make a grimace or shrink back, typically in a feeling of embarrassment or disgust.  |例句：He scringed at the sight of the bloody accident. 他看到血淋淋的事故后畏缩了。|例句：She scringed when she heard her own voice on the recording. 听到录音里自己的声音，她感到很尴尬。|近义词：cringe, recoil, flinch|反义词：stand tall, boldly face


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scringes at loud noises. = 他害怕大声响。
She scringes whenever she sees her old photos. = 她每当看到自己的旧照片时都会感到尴尬。

一般过去时:
He scringed when he saw the spider. = 他看到蜘蛛时畏缩了。
She scringed at the thought of her embarrassing mistake. = 她一想到自己犯的尴尬错误就感到局促不安。

现在完成时:
I've scringed so many times today. = 我今天已经好几次感到尴尬了。
He has scringed every time he’s seen that video. = 他每次看到那个视频都会感到很尴尬。

过去完成时:
I had scringed before he even started speaking. = 他还没开始说话我就已经感到尴尬了。
She had scringed many times before finally admitting her mistake. = 在最终承认错误之前，她已经无数次感到尴尬了。

将来时:
I will scringe if he sings again. = 如果他再唱歌，我会感到尴尬。
She will scringe when she sees the video of herself dancing. = 她看到自己跳舞的视频时会感到很尴尬。

虚拟语气:
I would scringe if I were in his shoes. = 如果我是他，我会感到很尴尬。
If I had known, I would have scringed less. = 如果我知道的话，我本来就不会那么尴尬。

疑问句:
Does he scringe easily? = 他容易感到尴尬吗？
Would you scringe if you saw that? = 如果你看到那个你会感到尴尬吗？

否定句:
He didn't scringe at the horror movie. = 他看恐怖电影时并没有感到害怕。
She didn't scringe; she laughed. = 她没有感到尴尬；她笑了。


PHRASE->
scringe away = 畏缩躲开
scringe in embarrassment = 尴尬地畏缩
scringe with disgust = 厌恶地畏缩
scringe at the thought of = 一想到…就畏缩
to scringe up = 蜷缩起来 (This usage is less common and more figurative)
