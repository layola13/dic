chronically:/ˈkrɒnɪkli/ /ˈkrɑːnɪkli/| adj. |慢性的，长期的，习惯性的| existing or happening over a long period; persistent.|He suffers from chronically low blood pressure. 他患有长期低血压。She's chronically late for work. 她总是上班迟到。|近义词：persistently, constantly, habitually |反义词：acutely, briefly, temporarily |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He chronically avoids difficult conversations. 他总是避免进行艰难的对话。
She chronically misinterprets my intentions. 她总是误解我的意图。

一般过去时:
He chronically underestimated the risks involved. 他过去总是低估所涉及的风险。
She chronically neglected her health. 她过去总是忽视自己的健康。


现在完成时:
He has chronically struggled with anxiety. 他长期以来一直与焦虑作斗争。
She has chronically relied on caffeine to stay awake. 她长期以来一直依靠咖啡因来保持清醒。


过去完成时:
He had chronically ignored the warning signs. 他一直无视警告信号。
She had chronically failed to meet deadlines. 她一直未能按时完成工作。


将来时:
He will chronically procrastinate on this project. 他将会一直拖延这个项目。
She will chronically overspend her budget. 她将会一直超支预算。


虚拟语气:
If he chronically neglected his duties, he would be fired. 如果他长期忽视他的职责，他会被解雇。
If she chronically lied, no one would trust her. 如果她总是撒谎，没有人会相信她。


被动语态:
The problem was chronically overlooked by management. 管理层长期以来一直忽视这个问题。
The situation has been chronically misrepresented in the media. 媒体长期以来一直歪曲了这种情况。


疑问句:
Is he chronically ill? 他是不是患有慢性病？
Is she chronically unhappy? 她是不是长期不快乐？


否定句:
He is not chronically ill, just temporarily unwell. 他并没有患慢性病，只是暂时身体不适。
She is not chronically disorganized, just occasionally forgetful. 她并非总是缺乏条理，只是偶尔健忘。
