half-conservatively: /ˌhɑːf kənˈsɜːvətɪvli/ | adv. | 半保留地 |  Describes a process, especially in molecular biology, where one strand of a DNA molecule is conserved (retained) during replication, while a new complementary strand is synthesized. This results in two DNA molecules, each containing one original strand and one newly synthesized strand.  | Example: DNA replicates half-conservatively.  DNA以半保留方式复制。 | 近义词: semi-conservatively | 反义词: conservatively (in the context of DNA replication;  no direct antonym for the general adverbial sense)
