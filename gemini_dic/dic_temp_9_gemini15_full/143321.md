outearning: /ˌaʊtˈɜːrnɪŋ/ | v. | 赚得比…多；比…挣钱多 |  To earn more money than someone or something else. |例句：She's outearning her husband. 她挣的钱比她丈夫多。  |近义词：outstripping, surpassing, exceeding | 反义词：underearning


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He outearns his brother. = 他比他弟弟赚得多。
They outearn their parents. = 他们的收入超过了父母。

一般过去时:
She outearned him last year. = 去年她赚的比他多。
The company outearned its projections. = 公司的盈利超过了预期。

现在完成时:
He has outearned his colleagues this year. = 他今年的收入超过了同事们。
The firm has outearned its competitors for five years running. = 这家公司连续五年盈利超过竞争对手。

过去完成时:
By the age of 30, she had outearned all her friends. = 到30岁时，她的收入已经超过了所有朋友。
The project had outearned its initial budget. = 项目的收入已经超过了最初的预算。

将来时:
They will outearn their rivals next quarter. = 下个季度他们将超过竞争对手的收入。
We expect to outearn our targets this year. = 我们预计今年将超过我们的目标收入。

虚拟语气:
If he worked harder, he might outearn his boss. = 如果他更努力工作，他可能会比老板赚得多。
I wish I could outearn my debt. = 我真希望我能赚到比我的债务还多的钱。

被动语态:
His salary is outearned by many in the field. = 他的薪水被该领域许多人超过。
The company's performance was outearned by its competitors. = 公司的表现被竞争对手超越了。

疑问句:
Does she outearn her spouse? = 她的收入超过她的配偶吗？
Do the top executives outearn the average workers? = 高管的收入是否超过普通员工？

否定句:
He doesn't outearn his wife. = 他的收入没有超过他妻子的。
The smaller company didn't outearn the larger one. = 小公司没有超过大公司的盈利。
