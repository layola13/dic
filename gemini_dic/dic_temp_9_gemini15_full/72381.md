flubbing:/ˈflʌbɪŋ/ | v.ing | 搞砸；弄糟；说错话 | The act of making a mistake, blundering, or botching something.  It often refers to a performance or speech that goes poorly. | 例句：He was flubbing his lines during the play. 他在剧中把台词都念错了。

例句：She completely flubbed the interview, forgetting her key points. 她彻底搞砸了面试，忘记了关键点。


近义词：botching, messing up, screwing up, blundering, muffing


反义词：excelling, mastering, succeeding


词性:动名词 (Verb-ing form)

词源:
The word "flub" likely originated as an onomatopoeia, mimicking the sound of a clumsy or bungled action.  Its origins are uncertain, but its use in the sense of making a mistake dates back to the early 20th century.
