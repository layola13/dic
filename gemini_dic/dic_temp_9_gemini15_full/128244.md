neurolinguistics: /ˌnʊəroʊlɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks/ | n. | 神经语言学 | The study of the neural mechanisms in the brain that control the comprehension, production, and acquisition of language.  It explores the relationship between language and the brain, encompassing areas like language processing, language acquisition, and language disorders.  | Example: Neurolinguistics research often utilizes brain imaging techniques like fMRI to investigate language processing in the brain. 神经语言学研究通常利用fMRI等脑成像技术来研究大脑中的语言处理。 | 近义词: psycholinguistics (心理语言学), cognitive linguistics (认知语言学) | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it's a field of study) |


词源:

* **neuro-**:  From Greek *νεῦρον* (neuron), meaning "nerve" or "sinew."
* **lingu-**: From Latin *lingua*, meaning "tongue" or "language."
* **-istics**:  A suffix forming nouns denoting a branch of study or science.
