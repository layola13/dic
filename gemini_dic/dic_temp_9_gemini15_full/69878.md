ferryboat:/ˈfɛriboʊt/| n. |渡船，摆渡船 | A ferryboat is a boat used to transport passengers and/or vehicles across a body of water, typically a river, lake, or strait, often on a regular schedule.  |例句：We took the ferryboat across the river. 我们乘渡船过了河。|例句：The ferryboat was crowded with tourists. 渡船上挤满了游客。|近义词：ferry, barge (if carrying goods)|反义词：无

词源:
ferry + boat.  "Ferry" comes from Old French *ferrie, from Latin *ferire "to carry."  "Boat" has a long and complex etymology tracing back to Proto-Germanic roots.  The combination creates a compound noun specifying a type of boat.
