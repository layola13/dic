laurel:/ˈlɔːrəl/ | n. | 月桂树；桂冠；荣誉 |  A laurel is an evergreen tree or shrub with fragrant leaves, and its leaves were used in ancient Greece and Rome to make wreaths for victors.  The word also refers to the wreath itself, symbolizing victory, achievement, or honor. | Example:  The Olympic champion received a laurel wreath. 奥运冠军获得了一个桂冠。

She was crowned with laurels for her literary achievements. 她因文学成就而荣获桂冠。| 近义词：honor, award, accolade | 反义词：failure, defeat, disgrace |
