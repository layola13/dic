outsmarting: /ˌaʊtˈsmɑːrtɪŋ/ | v. |智胜，以智取胜 | The act of being more clever or intelligent than someone else, often to gain an advantage.  |例句：His outsmarting of the opponent was a remarkable feat. 他智胜对手是一项了不起的壮举。

例句：The clever fox's outsmarting of the hunter was a testament to its cunning.  狡猾的狐狸智胜猎人证明了它的狡猾。


近义词：outwitting, outmaneuvering, deceiving, tricking

反义词：being outsmarted, being tricked, being defeated


词性：动名词 (gerund)

词源:
The word "outsmarting" is derived from the verb "outsmart," which combines the prefix "out-" (meaning "to a greater extent" or "surpass") with the noun "smart" (meaning "clever" or "intelligent").  Therefore, "outsmarting" literally means "surpassing in cleverness or intelligence."
