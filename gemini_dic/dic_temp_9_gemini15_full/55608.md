disvaluation: /ˌdɪsˌvæljuˈeɪʃən/ | n. |贬值；低估；取消估价 | The act of reducing something's value or importance; the process of assigning a lower value to something.  It can refer to financial devaluation, a decrease in perceived worth, or the removal of a valuation. |例句：The recent economic downturn led to a disvaluation of the company's assets. 近期经济衰退导致公司资产贬值。

The rapid inflation caused a significant disvaluation of the national currency. 迅速的通货膨胀导致国家货币大幅贬值。|近义词：depreciation, devaluation, downplaying, undervaluation |反义词：appreciation, revaluation, upvaluation

词源:

The word "disvaluation" is formed from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not," "opposite of," or "removal of") and the noun "valuation" (meaning the act of assigning a value to something).  Therefore, "disvaluation" signifies the act of removing or negating a valuation, or assigning a lower value.
