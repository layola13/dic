jilt:/dʒɪlt/| vt.|抛弃；甩掉；辜负|To suddenly end a romantic relationship with (someone).  To abandon or reject (someone or something) suddenly and unfeelingly.|例句：He jilted his fiancée at the altar. 他在婚礼祭坛上抛弃了他的未婚妻。

例句：She jilted him for a richer man. 她为了一个更富有的男人抛弃了他。


近义词：dump, desert, abandon, forsake|反义词：cherish, support, commit to|动词

词源:
The word "jilt" originates from the Old English word "gilden," meaning "to trick, deceive, or betray."  Over time, its meaning narrowed to specifically refer to the act of abandoning a romantic partner.  The modern spelling evolved through various phonetic shifts.
