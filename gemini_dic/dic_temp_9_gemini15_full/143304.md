outdrove:/ˌaʊtˈdroʊv/| v.|超过，胜过，驱逐|To surpass or exceed in driving or speed; to drive out or expel.|例句：He outdrove all other competitors in the race. 他在比赛中超过了所有其他竞争对手。

例句：The farmer outdrove the sheep from the field. 农夫把羊赶出了田地。

近义词：outdistance, surpass, exceed, expel

反义词：be overtaken, be surpassed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He outdrives everyone on the track. = 他在赛道上超过所有人。
She outdrives her colleagues in terms of sales. = 她在销售方面超过了她的同事。


一般过去时:
He outdrove his opponent in the final lap. = 他在最后一圈超过了他的对手。
The police outdrove the criminals' car. = 警察超过了罪犯的车。


现在完成时:
He has outdriven all his previous records. = 他已经打破了他所有的之前的记录。
She has outdriven the competition this year. = 今年她已经超过了竞争对手。


过去完成时:
He had outdriven the others before the final turn. = 在最后转弯之前他已经超过了其他人。
They had outdriven the storm and reached safety. = 他们已经躲过了暴风雨并到达了安全地带。


将来时:
He will outdrive his rivals in the upcoming race. = 他将在即将到来的比赛中超过他的对手。
She will outdrive the current speed record. = 她将打破现有的速度记录。


虚拟语气:
If he had trained harder, he would have outdriven his competitor. = 如果他训练更努力，他就会超过他的竞争对手。
I wish I could outdrive my anxieties. = 我希望我能克服我的焦虑。


被动语态:
He was outdriven by a younger competitor. = 他被一个更年轻的竞争对手超过了。
The slower car was outdriven and left behind. = 较慢的车被超过并落在了后面。


疑问句:
Can he outdrive the current champion? = 他能超过目前的冠军吗？
Did she outdrive her expectations? = 她是否超出了预期？


否定句:
He didn't outdrive his opponent in the race. = 他在比赛中没有超过他的对手。
She couldn't outdrive the effects of the bad weather. = 她无法克服恶劣天气的影响。
