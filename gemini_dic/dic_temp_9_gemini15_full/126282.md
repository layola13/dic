mylohyoid:/ˌmaɪloʊˈhaɪɔɪd/| n. |下颌舌骨肌|The mylohyoid muscle is a thin, sheet-like muscle located in the floor of the mouth. It forms the floor of the mouth and plays a role in swallowing and speech.  It's innervated by the mylohyoid nerve, a branch of the inferior alveolar nerve.|The mylohyoid muscle helps elevate the floor of the mouth during swallowing. |近义词：None specifically, but muscles with similar functions in the floor of the mouth could be considered.|反义词：None specifically.|名词
