coming-out: /ˈkʌmɪŋ aʊt/ | n. v. |公开承认；出柜；首次公演；问世|

英文解析:
As a noun, "coming-out" refers to the act of a person publicly acknowledging their sexual orientation, especially as a homosexual or LGBTQ+ individual.  It can also refer to the first public appearance or performance of a new play, book, or product. As a verb, "coming out" describes the action of making a public declaration, especially regarding one's sexuality.

例句:
Her coming-out was a momentous occasion for her and her family.  = 她的出柜对她和她家人来说是一个意义重大的时刻。

His new novel is a coming-out party for his unique writing style. = 他的新小说是他独特写作风格的首次亮相。
