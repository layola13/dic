pontifice:/ˈpɒntɪfɪs/| n. |教宗；大主教；桥梁建造者 |A pontiff is the Pope or a bishop, especially the Pope.  The word also historically referred to a builder of bridges. |例句：The pontiff delivered a powerful sermon on peace. 教宗发表了一篇关于和平的有力布道。

例句：The ancient Romans were skilled pontiffs, building impressive structures across rivers and valleys. 古罗马人是熟练的桥梁建造者，在河流和山谷之间建造了令人印象深刻的建筑物。


近义词：pope, bishop, high priest |反义词：layperson


词源：From Late Latin *pontifex, from Latin pons (“bridge”) + facere (“to make”).  The original meaning referred to a Roman priest responsible for maintaining bridges and conducting sacred rites.
