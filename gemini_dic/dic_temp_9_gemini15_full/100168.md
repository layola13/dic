interreflects: /ˌɪntərɪˈflɛkts/ | v. |相互反射；互相影响 |

英文解析:To interreflect means that two or more surfaces reflect light or other radiation back and forth between them.  It can also be used figuratively to describe ideas or influences that mutually affect each other.

例句:

The polished surfaces of the mirrored cubes interreflected light, creating a dazzling effect.  =  抛光镜面立方体的表面相互反射光线，营造出炫目的效果。

The economic and social policies of the two countries interreflect, making it difficult to analyze one without considering the other. = 两国的经济和社会政策相互影响，使得难以在不考虑另一国的情况下分析其中一国。


近义词:  reflect mutually, interact, influence reciprocally

反义词:  have no effect on each other, be independent
