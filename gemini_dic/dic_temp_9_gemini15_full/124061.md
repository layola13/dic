morphologically:/ˌmɔːrfəˈlɒdʒɪkli/ | adv. |形态学上地，从形态学的角度|In linguistics, morphologically refers to the study of the forms of words and how they are formed. It analyzes word structure, including prefixes, suffixes, roots, and inflections, to understand how meaning is created and conveyed.  |Example: Morphologically, the word "unbreakable" is composed of the prefix "un-", the root "break," and the suffix "-able."  形态学上，“unbreakable”这个词由前缀“un-”，词根“break”和后缀“-able”组成。|近义词：structurally, formally |反义词：semantically (in the context of linguistic analysis)
