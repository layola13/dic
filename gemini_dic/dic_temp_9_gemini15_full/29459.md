catalysts:/ˈkætəlɪsts/| n.|催化剂，刺激因素|A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change.  It can also refer to a person or thing that precipitates or speeds up a process or event.|例句：Enzymes are biological catalysts. 酶是生物催化剂。|例句：The economic reforms acted as a catalyst for growth. 经济改革成为增长的催化剂。|近义词：accelerant, stimulant, impetus|反义词：inhibitor, retardant|名词

词源:
The word "catalyst" comes from the Greek word "kata" (down) and "lyein" (to loosen).  Originally referring to a substance that loosens or breaks down other substances in a chemical reaction, the modern meaning emphasizes the speeding up of the reaction.
