idyls:/ˈɪdɪlz/| n. |田园诗；牧歌；（理想化的）乡村生活；宁静美好的景象|A short poem or passage that describes a peaceful and idyllic scene, often involving shepherds or rural life.  It can also refer to a period or experience of such peaceful and blissful nature.|例句：Tennyson's poems are often described as idyls, filled with images of nature and simple rural life.  田园诗人丁尼生的诗歌常被描述为田园诗，充满了自然和简单的乡村生活的意象。

The novel depicts a series of idyllic scenes of family life in the countryside. 这部小说描绘了一系列田园诗般的乡村家庭生活场景。|近义词：pastorals, poems, romances|反义词：tragedies, nightmares, dystopias|名词

词源:
The word "idyll" comes from the Greek word "eidyllion," a diminutive of "eidos," meaning "form" or "shape."  Originally, it referred to a short, descriptive poem, often of a pastoral or rural nature.  The English word "idyll" entered the language through French.
