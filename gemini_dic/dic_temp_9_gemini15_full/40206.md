contested:/kənˈtɛstɪd/ | adj. | 有争议的；被争论的；对…提出异议的 | Disputed; challenged; argued about.  |例句：The election results were heavily contested.  选举结果受到了强烈质疑。

例句：The contested will led to a lengthy court battle.  这份有争议的遗嘱导致了一场旷日持久的官司。


近义词：disputed, challenged, debated, controversial, litigated

反义词：uncontested, undisputed, agreed-upon, settled


词源:  From the verb "contest," ultimately from the Old French "contester," meaning "to contest, dispute," from "con-" (together) + "tester" (to witness).


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The election is contested by several candidates.  几个候选人对选举结果提出异议。
This point is contested by many scholars.  许多学者对这一点有争议。

一般过去时:
The decision was heavily contested last year.  这个决定去年受到了强烈的质疑。
The claim was contested in court.  这项说法在法庭上受到了质疑。

现在完成时:
The authorship of the painting has been contested for centuries.  这幅画的作者身份几个世纪以来一直存在争议。
The land rights have been contested since the colonial era.  土地所有权自殖民时代以来一直存在争议。

过去完成时:
The treaty had been contested before it was ratified.  该条约在批准之前就受到了质疑。
The verdict had been contested by the defendant's lawyers.  被告的律师对判决提出了异议。

将来时:
The upcoming election will likely be contested.  即将到来的选举很可能会出现争议。
The new law will undoubtedly be contested in the courts.  新法律无疑会在法庭上受到质疑。

虚拟语气:
If the results were contested, a recount would be necessary. 如果结果有争议，则需要重新计票。
If the evidence had been contested earlier, the trial might have had a different outcome. 如果证据早些时候就被质疑，审判结果可能会有所不同。

被动语态:
The game was contested fiercely.  比赛进行得很激烈。（这里contested指“激烈地进行”）
The results of the study are being contested by other researchers.  这项研究的结果正受到其他研究人员的质疑。

疑问句:
Is the proposed merger being contested?  拟议中的合并是否正在受到质疑？
Were the findings of the report contested at the meeting?  报告的调查结果在会议上是否受到质疑？

否定句:
The witness's testimony was not contested.  证人的证词没有受到质疑。
The agreement was not contested by either party.  协议未受到任何一方的质疑。
