dystopia:/daɪˈstoʊpiə/| n. |反乌托邦；反理想国 |A dystopia is a fictional society that is undesirable or frightening. It is often characterized by oppressive societal control and the suppression of individuality.  Dystopian societies often feature themes of environmental destruction, technological control, and social inequality. | Example:  The novel depicts a dystopian future where citizens are constantly monitored and their freedoms are severely restricted.  这部小说描绘了一个反乌托邦的未来，公民受到持续监控，自由受到严重限制。|近义词：anti-utopia, nightmare world |反义词：utopia, paradise


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It's used to describe a setting or situation.  Therefore,  standard verb tenses, passive voice, etc., are not applicable to the word "dystopia" itself, but rather to the verbs used to describe or discuss dystopias.

For example:

The novel *describes* a chilling dystopia. 这部小说描述了一个令人不寒而栗的反乌托邦社会。
Many films *portray* dystopian futures. 许多电影都描绘了反乌托邦的未来。
We *must avoid* creating a dystopia. 我们必须避免创造一个反乌托邦社会。
Scientists *warned* against the potential for a dystopian future. 科学家警告说，未来可能出现反乌托邦的情况。
The author *explored* themes of control and oppression in her dystopian novel. 作者在她的反乌托邦小说中探讨了控制和压迫的主题。
