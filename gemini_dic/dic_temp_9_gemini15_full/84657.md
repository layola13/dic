handbell: /ˈhændbɛl/ | n. | 手铃 | A small bell rung by hand, often used in pairs or sets for musical performances or signaling.  Handbells are typically made of metal and have a clapper inside that strikes the bell when it is rung. They are often used in handbell choirs, which create complex musical arrangements. | The children enjoyed ringing the handbells during the Christmas carol service. 孩子们在圣诞颂歌仪式上很享受敲响手铃。  The handbell choir played a beautiful rendition of "Silent Night." 手铃乐队演奏了一首优美的《平安夜》。 |  small bell, chime |  (no direct antonym,  perhaps large bell) | noun
