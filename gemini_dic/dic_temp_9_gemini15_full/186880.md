rinky-dink:/ˈrɪŋkiˌdɪŋk/ | adj. | 劣质的；低劣的；不重要的 |  Describes something of poor quality, insignificant, or cheap. It often implies a somewhat playful or dismissive tone.  | Example: "That rinky-dink car broke down after only a week."  那辆破旧不堪的车只开了一周就坏了。 | 近义词：cheap, shoddy, inferior, paltry, insignificant | 反义词：high-quality, superior, excellent, important

词源:

It's believed to have originated in American English in the early 20th century.  Its exact origin remains obscure and is a subject of ongoing linguistic speculation.
