nonflakier:/ˌnɒnˈfleɪkiə(r)/| adj.|不脱落的；不片状的|Not tending to flake or peel; smooth and consistent in texture.  This word describes a substance that does not easily break into small, thin pieces or layers.  |例句：The nonflakier paint ensured a smooth, even finish on the walls. = 不易脱落的油漆确保墙壁表面光滑平整。

The nonflakier surface of the new countertop made cleaning easier. = 新台面的不脱落表面使清洁更容易。|近义词：smooth, solid, intact |反义词：flaky, peeling, brittle

词源:
The word "nonflakier" is formed by adding the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") to the comparative adjective "flakier" (comparative form of "flaky").  "Flaky" ultimately derives from the Old English word "flæc" meaning "flat" or "thin," reflecting the nature of flaky substances.
