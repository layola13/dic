sisters-in-law: /ˈsɪstərz ɪn lɔː/ | n. | 妯娌；弟媳；嫂子 |

英文解析:The wives of one's brothers or the sisters of one's spouse.  This term can refer to multiple women.

例句:
My sisters-in-law are all very kind. = 我的妯娌都很善良。

I'm going to visit my sisters-in-law next week. = 我下周要去看望我的妯娌。


近义词: spouse's sisters, brother's wives (more formal and specific)


反义词:  There isn't a direct antonym, as the term describes a specific familial relationship.  You could perhaps use "unrelated women" in a context where the contrast is relevant.


词性: noun (plural)

词源:  The word is a compound, combining "sister" with "in-law," which itself is a compound of "in" and "law."  "In-law" indicates a familial relationship through marriage, not blood.  The term's evolution reflects the gradual incorporation of marital relatives into family structures.
