leapfrogged:/ˈliːpfrɒɡd/| v.|超越，跳跃式前进|To surpass or overtake (someone or something) by a large margin, often in a rapid or unexpected way.  It implies a significant advancement beyond the previous position or competitor.|例句：China has leapfrogged many Western countries in mobile payments. 中国在移动支付方面已经超越了许多西方国家。

例句：The smaller company leapfrogged its competitors by adopting innovative technology. 这家规模较小的公司通过采用创新技术超越了竞争对手。


近义词：surpass, overtake, outstrip, outpace


反义词：lag behind, fall behind


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He leapfrogs his competitors in the market. 他在市场上超越了他的竞争对手。
The company leapfrogs its rivals with its new product. 这家公司凭借其新产品超越了竞争对手。


一般过去时:
She leapfrogged her colleagues to become CEO. 她跳过同事成为首席执行官。
Our team leapfrogged the other team in the competition. 我们的团队在比赛中超越了另一支队伍。


现在完成时:

The country has leapfrogged many developed nations in technology. 这个国家在科技方面已经超越了许多发达国家。


过去完成时:

The company had leapfrogged its competitors before the market crashed. 在市场崩盘之前，该公司已经超越了竞争对手。

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "leapfrog" is a compound word combining "leap" (to jump) and "frog" (the amphibian). The image is of children playing the game of leapfrog, where one child jumps over another.  The figurative meaning developed from this literal image, signifying a rapid and significant advancement over something or someone else.
