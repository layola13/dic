preobviate:/ˌpriːˈɒbvieɪt/| vt.| 预先排除；使…成为不必要 | To remove (a need or difficulty): to make unnecessary.  Preobviate implies removing something beforehand, preventing it from arising in the first place. |The new system preobviates the need for manual data entry.  这个新系统消除了手动数据录入的需要。|  render unnecessary, eliminate, avoid | exacerbate, necessitate |动词

词源:

From Latin *praeobviāre, from prae- ("before") + obviāre ("to meet, encounter").  The original meaning implied "to meet beforehand" or "anticipate and prevent," evolving to its current sense of making something unnecessary.
