protosalt:/ˈproʊtoʊsɔːlt/ | n. | 原盐，酸式盐 | A protosalt is a salt formed by the incomplete neutralization of a polyprotic acid.  It contains both acidic hydrogen atoms and a cation. For example, sodium hydrogen carbonate (NaHCO₃) is a protosalt of carbonic acid (H₂CO₃).  | Example: Sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO₃) is a common example of a protosalt. 碳酸氢钠(NaHCO₃)是原盐的一个常见例子。|近义词:acid salt, half-neutralized salt | 反义词:normal salt
