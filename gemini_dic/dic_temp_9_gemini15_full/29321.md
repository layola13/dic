castigated:/ˈkæstɪɡeɪtɪd/| v. |严厉批评；惩戒|To criticize someone or something severely.|The professor castigated the student for plagiarism. 教授严厉批评了学生抄袭的行为。|近义词：rebuke, reprimand, chastise, scold|反义词：praise, commend


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He castigates his employees for their mistakes. 他严厉批评他的员工犯的错误。
The teacher castigates students who are disruptive in class. 老师严厉批评课堂上捣乱的学生。


一般过去时:
The coach castigated the team after their loss. 教练在球队输球后严厉批评了他们。
She was castigated for her insensitive remarks. 她因为不敏感的言论受到了严厉批评。


现在完成时:
The media has castigated the politician for his lies. 媒体已经因他的谎言而严厉批评了这位政治家。
He has been castigated repeatedly for his poor judgment. 他因其糟糕的判断而屡次受到严厉批评。


过去完成时:
The judge had castigated the defendant for his actions. 法官已经因被告的行为而严厉批评了他。
They had been castigated before for similar misconduct. 他们之前就因为类似的不当行为而受到过严厉批评。


将来时:
The manager will castigate the employees who miss the deadline. 经理将会严厉批评错过截止日期的员工。
The newspaper will likely castigate the company for its unethical practices. 报纸可能会因其不道德的经营行为而严厉批评这家公司。


虚拟语气:
If I were to make such a mistake, I would be castigated by my boss. 如果我犯了这样的错误，我会被老板严厉批评。
I wish I hadn't made that comment; I'd rather not be castigated. 我真希望我没有说过那句话；我宁愿不被严厉批评。


被动语态:
The student was castigated for cheating on the exam. 学生因考试作弊而受到严厉批评。
The politician was castigated by the public for his corruption. 这位政治家因腐败而受到公众的严厉批评。


疑问句:
Will he be castigated for his actions? 他会因为他的行为而受到严厉批评吗？
Was she castigated for her part in the scandal? 她因为参与丑闻而受到严厉批评了吗？


否定句:
The boss did not castigate the employee for the minor mistake. 老板没有因为这个小错误而严厉批评员工。
The teacher did not castigate the students for their honest attempts. 老师没有因为学生诚实的尝试而严厉批评他们。


PHRASE->
be castigated for  因...而受严厉批评
publicly castigated 公开谴责
severely castigated 严厉批评
unjustly castigated 不公正地批评
rightly castigated 理所当然地批评

词源:
From Latin castigatus, past participle of castigare "to chastise, correct," from castus "pure, chaste."  The original meaning related to purification or correction, implying a purging of error or wrongdoing.
