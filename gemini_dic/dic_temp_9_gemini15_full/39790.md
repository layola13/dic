consonants: /ˈkɒnsənənts/ /ˈkɑːnsənənts/ | n. |辅音|Consonants are speech sounds that are produced with some obstruction of airflow in the vocal tract.  They contrast with vowels, which are produced with open airflow.|The English alphabet contains many consonants, such as b, c, d, f, g, h, j, k, l, m, n, p, q, r, s, t, v, w, x, y, and z.|近义词：consonantal sounds|反义词：vowels
