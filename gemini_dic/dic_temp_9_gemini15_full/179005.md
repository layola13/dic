redefine:/ˌriːˈdiːfaɪn/| vt.|重新定义，改写定义|To define something again or in a new way.|The company has redefined its target market. 公司重新定义了其目标市场。|近义词：revise, reinterpret, reformulate|反义词：define, establish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They redefine their brand image every year. = 他们每年都会重新定义他们的品牌形象。
We redefine success based on our values. = 我们根据自己的价值观重新定义成功。


一般过去时:
The scientist redefined the theory of relativity. = 科学家重新定义了相对论。
The court redefined the boundaries of free speech. = 法院重新定义了言论自由的界限。


现在完成时:
They have redefined the rules of the game. = 他们已经重新定义了游戏规则。
The artist has redefined the concept of beauty. = 艺术家已经重新定义了美的概念。


过去完成时:
They had redefined the terms of the agreement before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前已经重新定义了协议的条款。
She had redefined her priorities after the incident. = 事故发生后，她重新定义了自己的优先级。


将来时:
We will redefine our strategy for the next quarter. = 我们将重新定义下一季度的战略。
The company will redefine its approach to customer service. = 公司将重新定义其客户服务方式。


虚拟语气:
If we redefined our goals, we would achieve more. = 如果我们重新定义我们的目标，我们会取得更大的成就。
I wish they had redefined the project's scope earlier. = 我希望他们早点重新定义项目的范围。


被动语态:
The concept of family has been redefined in modern society. = 家庭的概念在现代社会已经被重新定义了。
The rules of the competition were redefined after several complaints. = 在几次投诉之后，比赛规则被重新定义了。


疑问句:
How will we redefine our mission statement? = 我们将如何重新定义我们的使命宣言？
Will they redefine their business model? = 他们会重新定义他们的商业模式吗？


否定句:
They did not redefine their marketing strategy. = 他们没有重新定义他们的营销策略。
We have not yet redefined our long-term goals. = 我们尚未重新定义我们的长期目标。
