filleting:/fɪˈleɪtɪŋ/| v. |去骨；切成鱼片|The process of removing the bones from fish or meat, often to prepare it for cooking.  It can also refer to the act of cutting something into fillets.|例句：He spent the morning filleting the salmon. 他上午花了时间给鲑鱼去骨。

例句：The chef expertly filleted the fish before grilling it.  厨师熟练地将鱼去骨后才进行烧烤。


近义词：boning, slicing

反义词：boning (in the context of leaving bones in)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fillets the fish every day. 他每天都给鱼去骨。
She fillets the chicken breast for the salad. 她把鸡胸肉切成片做沙拉。


一般过去时:
He filleted the salmon yesterday. 他昨天给鲑鱼去骨了。
She filleted the fish quickly and efficiently. 她快速有效地将鱼去骨了。


现在完成时:
I have filleted all the fish. 我已经把所有的鱼都去骨了。
She has filleted many fish in her career.  她职业生涯中已经给很多鱼去骨了。


过去完成时:
He had filleted the fish before the guests arrived. 客人到达之前，他已经把鱼去骨了。
She had filleted the chicken before she started cooking.  她在开始做饭之前已经把鸡肉切成片了。


将来时:
I will fillet the fish tomorrow. 我明天会给鱼去骨。
She will fillet the salmon for the dinner party. 她会为晚宴给鲑鱼去骨。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would fillet the fish myself. 如果我有更多时间，我会自己给鱼去骨。
I wish I had filleted the fish earlier. 我希望我早点把鱼去骨了。


被动语态:
The fish was filleted by the chef. 鱼是由厨师去骨的。
The chicken breasts are being filleted for the sandwiches. 鸡胸肉正在被切成片做三明治。


疑问句:

否定句:

She doesn't fillet fish often. 她不经常给鱼去骨。

词源:  From Old French *fileter, from Old French file "thread, wire," referring to the thin, thread-like shape of the resulting pieces of meat.
