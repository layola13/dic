/ɪˈpænəstrəfiː/| n.|迭句法，反复法|The repetition of a word or phrase at the end of successive clauses.|例句：He is a man of action, a man of courage, a man of determination. 他是一个行动的人，一个勇敢的人，一个有决心的人。|近义词：epizeuxis, anaphora|反义词：无


USAGE->
This word is a literary device and doesn't have typical grammatical usages like verbs or nouns.  It describes a rhetorical technique.  Therefore, there are no tenses or voice considerations.

EXAMPLE SENTENCES->
Here are examples demonstrating epanastrophe in different contexts:

*  "She is a woman of grace, a woman of strength, a woman of wisdom."  她是一位优雅的女性，一位坚强的女性，一位智慧的女性。
*  "Government of the people, by the people, for the people, shall not perish from the earth."  民有、民治、民享的政府，将不会从地球上消失。
*  "Easy come, easy go." 来得容易，去得容易。 (This is a shorter, simpler example)


PHRASE->
Epanastrophe doesn't have common phrases associated with it.  It's a rhetorical device, not a word or phrase itself.  Its usage is solely within sentence construction.
