necrolatry:/nɛˈkrɒlətri/| n.| 崇拜死者；对死者的迷恋 | The worship of the dead; an excessive preoccupation with death and the dead.  Necrolatry can manifest in various ways, from elaborate funeral rites to the preservation of bodies and the creation of memorials.  It can also involve a morbid fascination with death and decaying matter. |  The ancient Egyptians practiced necrolatry, evident in their elaborate mummification processes and tomb construction.  | 近义词：death cult, ancestor worship | 反义词：None directly opposite, but could be considered the opposite of a vibrant celebration of life.
