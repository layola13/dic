culminant: /ˈkʌlmɪnənt/ | adj. | 顶点的；达到顶峰的；最终的 | Reaching the highest or most decisive point; final.  | The culminating event of the festival was the fireworks display.  节日的高潮是烟火表演。 |  highest, peak, apex, ultimate | lowest, nadir


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The culminating point is often the most exciting part. = 顶点往往是最令人兴奋的部分。
This is the culminating argument in the debate. = 这是这场辩论中最终的论点。


一般过去时:
The culminating moment of the play was truly dramatic. = 这出戏的高潮确实非常戏剧化。
His career culminated in a prestigious award. = 他的职业生涯最终以一个令人瞩目的奖项而告终。


现在完成时:
The project has culminated in a successful launch. = 该项目最终成功启动。
Their efforts have culminated in a significant breakthrough. = 他们的努力最终取得了重大突破。


过去完成时:
By the time he retired, his career had culminated in a position of great influence. = 到他退休时，他的职业生涯已达到了极具影响力的地位。
The investigation had culminated in the arrest of the suspect. = 调查最终导致了嫌疑人的逮捕。


将来时:
The negotiations will culminate in a final agreement next week. = 谈判将于下周达成最终协议。
The festival will culminate in a grand parade. = 节日将以盛大的游行作为高潮。


虚拟语气:
If the project culminated successfully, we would celebrate. = 如果项目成功完成，我们将庆祝。
I wish my efforts had culminated in a better outcome. = 我希望我的努力能有一个更好的结果。


被动语态:
The event was culminated by a spectacular performance. = 活动以一场壮观的演出作为高潮。
The investigation is being culminated with a press conference. = 调查正在以新闻发布会作为结束。


疑问句:
When will the competition culminate? = 比赛什么时候结束？
At what point does the story culminate? = 故事在哪个点达到高潮？


否定句:
The negotiations did not culminate in an agreement. = 谈判没有达成协议。
His efforts did not culminate in the desired results. = 他的努力没有达到预期的结果。
