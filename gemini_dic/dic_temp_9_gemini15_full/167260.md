/prēˈtɛstɪfaɪɪŋ/ | v. | 预先作证；提前作证 |  The act of giving testimony or evidence before an official proceeding, often in anticipation of a later, more formal hearing.  It's typically done to preserve testimony or evidence that might be lost or unavailable later.  | The lawyer arranged for the witness to be pretestifying before the trial. = 律师安排证人在审判前预先作证。 The police officer's pretestifying secured vital information for the upcoming investigation. = 警官的预先作证为即将进行的调查获得了重要信息。|  testify, depose |  (no direct antonym, but perhaps) withhold testimony | 动词
