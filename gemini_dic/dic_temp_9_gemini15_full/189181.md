sacramentalism: /ˌsækrəˈmɛntəlɪzəm/ | n. |圣礼论；圣事主义 | The theological doctrine that the sacraments are efficacious signs of God's grace, conveying grace ex opere operato (by the work worked), not merely signifying it.  It emphasizes the outward and visible signs of the sacraments as essential to receiving God's grace.  | Example:  The debate between proponents of sacramentalism and those who emphasized faith alone raged for centuries.  圣礼论和强调唯独信心的论点之间的争论持续了几个世纪。 | 近义词：sacramental theology,  transubstantiation (in some contexts) | 反义词：fideism (in some contexts),  symbolicism (in some contexts)
