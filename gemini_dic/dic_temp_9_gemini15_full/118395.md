meroistic: /ˌmɛroʊˈɪstɪk/ | adj. |  全细胞性的；部分细胞性的 | Relating to or characterized by merogony, a type of asexual reproduction in which a part of an organism develops into a new individual.  It refers to a type of oogenesis where the cytoplasm is divided unequally amongst the developing oocytes, resulting in one large functional ovum and several smaller, non-functional polar bodies. |  Example:  "The meroistic oogenesis in Drosophila results in a single functional egg and several polar bodies." | 近义词: merogonic (in the context of reproduction) | 反义词: panoistic (referring to a type of oogenesis where the cytoplasm is divided equally)
