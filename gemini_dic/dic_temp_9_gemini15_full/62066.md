encashments:/ɪnˈkæʃmənts/| n. |兑现；变现；提款|The act of converting something into cash.  This typically refers to the process of exchanging something of value, such as a check, bond, or other negotiable instrument, for its equivalent in cash.  It can also refer to the total amount of cash received through such conversions. |例句：The bank processed a large number of encashments that day.  银行那天处理了大量的兑现业务。


例句：The company's encashments increased significantly after the successful product launch. 公司产品成功发布后，其现金收入显著增加。


近义词：cashing, redemption, realization|反义词：depositing, investing
