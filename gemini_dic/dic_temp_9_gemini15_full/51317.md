detainer:/dɪˈteɪnər/| n. |扣留者；扣押令；阻止者| A person or thing that detains;  a legal writ ordering someone to be held in custody; something that holds or keeps something else.  It can also refer to a person or thing that delays or prevents something. |例句：The police issued a detainer for the suspect.  警方对嫌疑人发出了拘留令。

The landlord filed a detainer action against the tenant. 房东对租户提起了驱逐诉讼。|近义词：detention, restraint, custody, writ |反义词：release, liberation, discharge

词源:
The word "detainer" comes from the Old French word "detenir," meaning "to detain,"  which itself is derived from the Latin "detinere," composed of "de-" (from) and "tenere" (to hold).  The evolution reflects the core meaning of holding or keeping something or someone.
