relinquishment: /rɪˈlɪŋkwɪʃmənt/ | n. | 放弃；放弃的行为；让与 | The act of relinquishing something; giving something up.  Relinquishment can refer to the act of giving up possession, control, or a claim to something.  It often implies a voluntary act, though it can also be forced. |

例句：

The relinquishment of his claim to the throne surprised everyone. = 他放弃王位继承权令所有人都感到惊讶。

Her relinquishment of her parental rights was a difficult decision. = 她放弃抚养权是一个艰难的决定。


近义词：abandonment, surrender, cession, release, forfeiture

反义词：retention, acquisition, claim, retention

词源:

Comes from the verb "relinquish" (to give up, abandon).  "Re-" is a prefix meaning "again" or "back," while "linquish" comes from the Latin "linquere," meaning "to leave" or "to abandon."  Therefore, "relinquishment" literally means "the act of leaving again" or "the act of abandoning."
