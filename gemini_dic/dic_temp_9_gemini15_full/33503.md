cicada: /sɪˈkɑːdə/ | n. | 蝉 | A large insect with transparent wings, that makes a loud, high-pitched buzzing sound, especially in hot weather.  Cicadas are known for their distinctive calls, which are produced by vibrating membranes on their bodies. They undergo a long life cycle, often spending years underground as nymphs before emerging as adults. | Example:  The cicadas' incessant buzzing filled the summer air.  蝉的持续嗡嗡声充满了夏日的空气。  The children were fascinated by the large cicada clinging to the tree. 孩子们被依附在树上的大蝉迷住了。| 近义词：locust (in some contexts), insect | 反义词：None specifically.
