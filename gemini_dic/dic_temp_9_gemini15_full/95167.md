ination:/ˌɪmɪˈneɪʃən/| n.|想象力，创造力|The ability to form new ideas, or images in your mind.|例句：His imagination led him to write a fantastical novel. 他的想象力使他写了一部奇幻小说。|近义词：fantasy, creativity, invention|反义词：reality, fact


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Her imagination runs wild. = 她的想象力很丰富。
His imagination creates wonderful worlds. = 他的想象力创造了美好的世界。

一般过去时:
His imagination failed him at that moment. = 那一刻他的想象力让他失望了。
She used her imagination to solve the problem. = 她用想象力解决了这个问题。


现在完成时:
My imagination has created many stories. = 我的想象力创造了许多故事。
She has developed a vivid imagination. = 她已经培养出了丰富的想象力。

过去完成时:
I had never used my imagination so much before. = 我以前从未如此充分地运用过我的想象力。
They had exhausted their imaginations by the end of the project. = 到项目结束时，他们已经耗尽了想象力。


将来时:
He will use his imagination to design a new product. = 他将运用他的想象力设计一款新产品。
We will need to use our imaginations to find a solution. = 我们需要运用我们的想象力来找到解决方案。


虚拟语气:
If I had a better imagination, I could write a better story. = 如果我的想象力更好，我就能写出更好的故事。
I wish my imagination were more vivid. = 我希望我的想象力更丰富。


被动语态:
His work was inspired by his vivid imagination. = 他的作品受到了他丰富想象力的启发。
The story was created by a young girl's wild imagination. = 这个故事是由一个年轻女孩丰富的想象力创造的。


疑问句:

否定句:
He lacks imagination. = 他缺乏想象力。
The design shows no imagination at all. = 这个设计完全没有想象力。
