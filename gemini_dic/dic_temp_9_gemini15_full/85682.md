have-a-way-with:/ˌhæv əˈweɪ wɪð/ | adj. phr. | 善于处理…；对…很有一套 |  Possessing a natural skill or aptitude for dealing with or influencing something or someone. This phrase often implies a certain charm or finesse. | 例句：She has a way with children.  她很会哄孩子。

She has a way with words. 她很会说话。


近义词：skillful with, adept at, masterful in,  gifted at


反义词：clumsy with, inept at, awkward with


词性：形容词短语 (adjectival phrase)


USAGE->


一般现在时:
He has a way with computers. = 他很擅长电脑。
She has a way with difficult clients. = 她很会处理难缠的客户。


一般过去时:
He had a way with words that captivated the audience. = 他说话很有技巧，迷住了观众。
She had a way with animals that made them calm. = 她有一套能让动物平静下来的方法。


现在完成时:
He has always had a way with people. = 他一直都很会与人相处。
She has developed a way with public speaking. = 她已经练就了一套优秀的公开演讲技巧。


过去完成时:
He had had a way with negotiations, which is why the deal went smoothly. = 他过去很擅长谈判，所以交易进行得很顺利。
She had had a way with horses since she was a child. = 她从小就擅长骑马。


将来时:
He will have a way with the new software once he learns it. = 一旦他学会了新软件，他就能熟练地使用它。
She will have a way with the children after some practice. = 多练习之后，她就能更好地与孩子们相处了。


虚拟语气:
If he had a way with money, he wouldn't be in debt. = 如果他擅长理财，他就不会负债了。
I wish I had a way with public speaking. = 我真希望我擅长公开演讲。


被动语态: (This phrase doesn't typically use the passive voice)


疑问句:
Does he have a way with computers? = 他擅长电脑吗？
Does she have a way with difficult situations? = 她擅长处理棘手的情况吗？


否定句:
He doesn't have a way with numbers. = 他不擅长数字。
She doesn't have a way with angry customers. = 她不擅长处理生气的顾客。
