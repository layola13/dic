hoisted:/ˈhɔɪstɪd/| vt.| 提升；吊起；升起；强行举起 | To raise or lift something heavy, using a machine or by hand.  |例句：They hoisted the flag to the top of the pole. 他们把旗帜升到旗杆顶端。

例句：The workers hoisted the heavy beam into place. 工人们把沉重的横梁吊放到位。


近义词：raise, lift, elevate, hoist up


反义词：lower, drop, descend


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hoists the sail every morning. 他每天早上都升起帆。
They hoist the cargo onto the ship. 他们把货物吊上船。


一般过去时:
The sailors hoisted the anchor. 水手们起锚了。
She hoisted the suitcase onto the rack. 她把行李箱抬到行李架上。


现在完成时:
They have hoisted the new sign. 他们已经挂上了新标志。
We have hoisted the sails and are ready to set off. 我们已经升起了帆，准备出发了。


过去完成时:
They had hoisted the tent before the rain started.  下雨前他们已经支起了帐篷。
He had hoisted the flag before the ceremony began. 典礼开始前他已经升起了旗帜。


将来时:
They will hoist the new banner tomorrow. 他们明天将升起新的旗帜。
We will hoist the sails as soon as the wind picks up. 风一强起来我们就升起帆。


虚拟语气:
If they had hoisted the sails earlier, they would have reached the island sooner. 如果他们早点升起帆，他们就会早点到达岛屿。
I wish I had hoisted the warning flag before the storm. 我真希望在暴风雨来临之前我已经升起了警告旗。


被动语态:
The flag was hoisted at sunrise. 旗帜在日出时升起。
The heavy equipment was hoisted onto the truck. 重型设备被吊上了卡车。


疑问句:
Have they hoisted the sails yet? 他们已经升起帆了吗？
Will the crane be able to hoist such a heavy load? 起重机能够吊起这么重的货物吗？


否定句:
They didn't hoist the flag because of the storm. 因为暴风雨，他们没有升旗。
He couldn't hoist the box onto the shelf by himself. 他一个人无法把箱子抬到架子上。


PHRASE->
hoist up:  吊起
hoist with one's own petard: 自食其果

hoist the colours: 升起旗帜
hoist a flag: 升旗

词源:
The word "hoist" ultimately derives from the Middle Dutch word *heffen*, meaning "to lift" or "to raise," which in turn has cognates in other Germanic languages.  The word entered English through various routes, with its current form solidifying over time.
