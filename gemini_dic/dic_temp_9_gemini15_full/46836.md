dapifer:/ˈdæpɪfər/| n.|分酒官，侍酒官|A dapifer was a high-ranking official in medieval courts, primarily responsible for managing the royal household's food and drink, especially wine.  Their duties often extended beyond simple service to include overseeing banquets and feasts, and even playing a role in courtly ceremonies. The position held significant prestige and influence.| Example: The dapifer oversaw the preparation of the king's celebratory feast, ensuring the finest wines were served.  分酒官负责国王庆典宴会的准备工作，确保提供最好的葡萄酒。|近义词：cupbearer, steward, butler|反义词：None specifically.  The opposite would be someone with no such responsibility for food or drink service.
