raven-like:/ˈreɪvənlaɪk/| adj. |像乌鸦的；乌鸦似的；阴郁的；不祥的 |Resembling a raven in color, appearance, or character; often implying darkness, gloom, or ill omen.  |例句：The raven-like bird perched on the gnarled branch.  一只像乌鸦一样的鸟栖息在粗糙的树枝上。

The raven-like shadows stretched across the valley. 乌鸦似的阴影笼罩着山谷。|近义词：crow-like, dark, gloomy, ominous, sinister |反义词：bright, cheerful, sunny, auspicious

词源:
The word "raven-like" is a compound adjective formed from "raven" (a large black bird) and "-like" (a suffix indicating resemblance).  The word "raven" itself has Old English origins, stemming from the Proto-Germanic *hrabaną.
