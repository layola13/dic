marginalize:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəlaɪz/ | vt. |边缘化；使…处于社会边缘 | To treat (a person, group, or concept) as insignificant or peripheral.  To place in a position of marginal importance, influence, or power. |例句：The government's policies marginalized ethnic minorities. 政府的政策使少数民族处于边缘地位。

例句：The media tends to marginalize the voices of the working class. 媒体倾向于边缘化工人的声音。


近义词：exclude, sideline, ostracize, isolate, relegate

反义词：centralize, empower, include, integrate


词源：源自margin（边缘）+ -alize（动词后缀）。  The word evolved from the concept of being placed at the margins of society or a group.
