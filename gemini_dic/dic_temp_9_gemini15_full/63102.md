enshrines:/ɪnˈʃraɪnz/ | vt. |奉为神圣；珍藏；铭记|To cherish or preserve something as sacred or important; to enshrine a principle or value means to formally incorporate it into a system or set of beliefs.|The museum enshrines the history of the city.  博物馆珍藏着这座城市的历史。|The constitution enshrines the rights of citizens. 宪法保障公民的权利。|preserve, cherish, consecrate |desecrate, profane|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He enshrines his family values. = 他珍视他的家庭价值观。
The museum enshrines local artifacts. = 博物馆珍藏当地文物。

一般过去时:

The king enshrined the new law. = 国王将新法典奉为神圣。

现在完成时:
The organization has enshrined its mission statement. = 该组织已经将它的使命宣言奉为圭臬。
