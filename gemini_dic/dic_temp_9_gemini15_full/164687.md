preexpressions:/ˌpriːɪkˈspreʃənz/ | n. | 预先表达；预先表达的词语 |  Preexpressions refer to words or phrases that are uttered or written before the main expression of an idea or thought, often serving to introduce, preface, or prepare the audience for what is to follow. They can be used to signal a change of topic, to create suspense, or to simply make the communication smoother and more effective.  They are frequently used in both spoken and written communication. | Example: "To begin with, I'd like to thank everyone for attending." (预先表达：首先，我想感谢各位的光临。) |近义词：introduction, preface, prelude | 反义词：conclusion, postscript
