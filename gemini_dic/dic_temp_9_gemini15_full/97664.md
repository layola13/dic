inhibitions:/ˌɪnɪˈbɪʃənz/ | n. |抑制；约束；顾虑 | Feelings or beliefs that prevent one from doing what one wants to do.  Inhibitions are restraints on behavior or thoughts, often stemming from social norms, personal anxieties, or learned responses. They can manifest in various ways, from shyness and reluctance to speak up to a complete avoidance of certain activities or relationships. |She overcame her inhibitions and spoke to the handsome stranger. 她克服了羞怯，和那个英俊的陌生人说话了。 |Reservations, restraints, constraints, scruples |Freedom, liberation, boldness |名词
