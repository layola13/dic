scrumptious:/ˈskrʌmpʃəs/| adj.|美味的，可口的，令人愉快的|Delicious and pleasing, especially to the taste.  It often implies a luxurious or decadent quality.|例句：The cake was absolutely scrumptious. = 蛋糕非常美味。

The meal was scrumptious; I ate far too much. = 这顿饭太美味了；我吃得太多了。|近义词：delicious, delightful, delectable, yummy, appetizing|反义词：disgusting, unappetizing, repulsive

词源:
The word "scrumptious" is of uncertain origin.  It likely emerged in the late 19th or early 20th century as informal slang, possibly by blending or altering existing words to create an expressive term for something delicious.  There's no single definitive etymological source.  The sound and feel of the word suggest a sense of delight and abundance.
