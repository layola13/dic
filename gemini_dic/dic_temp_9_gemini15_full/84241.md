half-sighted:/ˈhɑːfˌsaɪtɪd/| adj.|视力不好的，目光短浅的|Having poor eyesight or lacking foresight.  The term suggests a limited perspective, both literally and figuratively.|例句：He's half-sighted and needs glasses. 他视力不好，需要戴眼镜。

例句：Her half-sighted approach to the problem led to unforeseen consequences. 她对这个问题目光短浅的做法导致了无法预料的后果。


近义词：partially sighted, visually impaired, short-sighted (figurative sense), myopic (figurative sense)

反义词：clear-sighted, far-sighted, perspicacious


PHRASE->
There is no common phrase directly using "half-sighted".  The concept can be expressed with phrases like:

poor eyesight = 视力差
lacking foresight = 缺乏远见
short-sighted = 短视的
limited perspective = 视野有限


USAGE->

This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms commonly used.  Therefore, tense conjugation is not applicable in the same way as with verbs.  The usage is descriptive.


例句:  The half-sighted man stumbled on the uneven pavement.  视力不好的男人在不平整的人行道上绊倒了。

例句:  The half-sighted artist still produced beautiful, if somewhat imprecise, paintings.  视力不好的艺术家仍然创作出美丽的，尽管有些不够精确的绘画作品。
