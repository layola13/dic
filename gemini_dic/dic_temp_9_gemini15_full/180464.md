regarrisons: /ˌriːˈɡærɪsən/ | v. |重新驻军，重新配置兵力 | To garrison (a place) again; to reinforce the garrison of (a place).  The word is a combination of "re-" (again) and "garrison". | Example: The general ordered the regarrisoning of the fort after the enemy attack.  将军下令在敌人袭击后重新驻守要塞。|近义词：refortify, reinforce | 反义词：evacuate, abandon |


USAGE->

The army regarrisons the border towns every year. = 军队每年都会重新驻守边境城镇。
They regarrison the fort to strengthen its defenses. = 他们重新驻守要塞以加强防御。


一般过去时:
