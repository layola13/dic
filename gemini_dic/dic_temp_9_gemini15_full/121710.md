misprincipled: /ˌmɪsˈprɪnsəpəld/ | adj. | 缺乏原则的；不道德的；不正当的 |  Lacking principles; unscrupulous; unethical.  It describes someone or something that is guided by selfish motives rather than ethical considerations.  |  例句：The misprincipled CEO prioritized profit over employee safety.  = 那个缺乏原则的首席执行官为了利润而牺牲了员工的安全。

The company's misprincipled actions led to a significant loss of public trust. = 这家公司的不道德行为导致公众信任的大量丧失。| 近义词：unprincipled, unscrupulous, immoral, unethical, dishonest | 反义词：principled, ethical, moral, honest, righteous |


USAGE->
This word is an adjective, so it doesn't have verb conjugations.  It modifies nouns.  Examples below demonstrate usage in various sentence structures.

简单句:
His misprincipled behavior shocked everyone. = 他不道德的行为震惊了所有人。
The misprincipled deal was quickly exposed. = 这笔不正当的交易很快就被揭露了。

复合句:

并列句:
The politician was misprincipled, and his constituents soon realized it. = 这位政治家缺乏原则，他的选民很快就意识到了这一点。
His actions were both misprincipled and illegal. = 他的行为既不道德又违法。

疑问句:
Was the decision truly misprincipled, or simply misguided? = 这个决定真的是不道德的吗，还是仅仅是误导？
Are we sure their motivations were entirely misprincipled? = 我们确定他们的动机完全是不道德的吗？


否定句:
The judge was not misprincipled; he simply made a mistake. = 法官并非缺乏原则；他只是犯了一个错误。
Their actions, while questionable, were not necessarily misprincipled. = 他们的行为虽然值得怀疑，但不一定是不道德的。
