co-owning:/koʊˈoʊnɪŋ/| v. |共有，共同拥有 |The act of two or more people owning something together.  It implies shared responsibility and benefits.|例句：They are co-owning a small business. 他们共同拥有一家小企业。  They decided to co-own the property.他们决定共同拥有这处房产。|近义词：joint ownership, shared ownership|反义词：sole ownership, individual ownership


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They co-own a holiday home. = 他们共同拥有一栋度假屋。
We co-own the copyright to this book. = 我们共同拥有这本书的版权。


一般过去时:
We co-owned a restaurant in the past. = 我们过去共同经营一家餐厅。
They co-owned the land for many years. = 他们共同拥有那块土地很多年了。


现在完成时:
They have co-owned the company for ten years. = 他们共同拥有这家公司十年了。
We have co-owned this property since 2010. = 自2010年以来，我们一直共同拥有这处房产。


过去完成时:
Before the merger, they had co-owned separate businesses. = 在合并之前，他们各自拥有独立的企业。
She had co-owned several properties before she retired. = 在退休之前，她共同拥有过几处房产。


将来时:
We will co-own a vineyard in the future. = 我们将来会共同拥有一家葡萄园。
They will co-own the intellectual property rights. = 他们将共同拥有知识产权。


虚拟语气:
If they co-owned the business, they would share the profits. = 如果他们共同拥有这家企业，他们就会分享利润。
I wish we had co-owned that property. = 我真希望我们当时共同拥有那处房产。


被动语态:
The property is co-owned by three siblings. = 这处房产由三兄弟姐妹共同拥有。
The land was co-owned by the families for generations. = 这块土地世世代代由这些家庭共同拥有。


疑问句:
Do they co-own the apartment building? = 他们共同拥有那栋公寓楼吗？
Who co-owns the patent? = 谁共同拥有这项专利？


否定句:
They do not co-own the business anymore. = 他们不再共同拥有这家企业了。
We did not co-own the car.  = 我们没有共同拥有那辆车。
