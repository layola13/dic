excoriated: /ɪkˈskɔːrieɪtɪd/ | v. | 严厉批评；痛斥；剥皮；损伤 | To criticize severely; to denounce; to flay; to abrade. |

例句：

The critic excoriated the author's new novel, calling it "uninspired and derivative."  批评家严厉批评了作者的新小说，称其“缺乏灵感且模仿抄袭”。

The media excoriated the politician for his handling of the crisis.  媒体严厉批评了这位政治家处理危机的做法。


近义词：denounce, condemn, criticize severely, lambaste, castigate, rebuke, upbraid

反义词：praise, commend, applaud, compliment

词源:

From Latin *excoriāre "to flay," from ex- "out" + corium "skin, hide."  The sense extended from the literal meaning of "to flay the skin" to the figurative meaning of "to severely criticize."
