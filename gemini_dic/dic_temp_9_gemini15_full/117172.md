meas:/mɛz/|v.|测量，衡量|to determine the extent, amount, or capacity of something, typically with an instrument.|例句：The tailor measured me for a new suit.  裁缝为我量体裁衣。|例句：We measured the room to see if the new sofa would fit. 我们测量了房间以查看新沙发是否合适。|近义词：gauge, assess, estimate|反义词：estimate (in the sense of guessing without precision)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He measures the wood before cutting it. 他在切割木材之前测量它。
She measures the ingredients carefully. 她仔细测量配料。

一般过去时:
The scientist measured the temperature. 科学家测量了温度。
They measured the distance between the two cities. 他们测量了两个城市之间的距离。

现在完成时:
I have measured the fabric. 我已经测量了布料。
She has measured the room three times. 她已经测量了房间三次。

过去完成时:
He had measured the land before he started building. 他在开始建造之前已经测量了土地。
They had measured the ingredients before they began cooking.  他们在开始烹饪之前已经测量了配料。


将来时:
We will measure the height of the building. 我们将测量建筑物的高度。
They will measure the flow rate of the river. 他们将测量河流的流速。


虚拟语气:
If I had a ruler, I would measure the length. 如果我有一把尺子，我会测量长度。
If she had measured more accurately, the results would have been better. 如果她测量得更精确，结果就会更好。


被动语态:
The length of the table was measured. 桌子的长度被测量了。
The ingredients are carefully measured by the chef. 配料由厨师仔细测量。


疑问句:
How do you measure success? 你如何衡量成功？
What instrument did they use to measure the pressure? 他们用什么仪器测量压力？


否定句:
I didn't measure the sugar correctly. 我没有正确测量糖。
They didn't measure the depth of the well. 他们没有测量井的深度。


PHRASE->
