laurels:/ˈlɔːrəlz/| n.|桂冠；荣誉；桂树|The word "laurels" refers to the branches of the laurel tree, which were used in ancient Greece and Rome to crown victors and heroes.  It now symbolizes achievement, honor, and glory.  The plural form is typically used to represent the collective accolades received.|例句：She rested on her laurels after winning the award. 她获奖后便安于现状了。|例句：His achievements earned him many laurels. 他的成就为他赢得了许多荣誉。|近义词：accolades, honors, awards, glory |反义词：failure, disgrace, shame
