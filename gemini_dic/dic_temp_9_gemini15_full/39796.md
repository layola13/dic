consorted:/kənˈsɔːtɪd/ | v. | 结交；陪伴；(尤指秘密地)共谋；| to keep company; associate, especially secretly or in a dubious way | The king consorted with his advisors. 国王与他的顾问们商议。|She consorted with known criminals. 她与已知的罪犯交往。|近义词：associate, mingle, collaborate | 反义词：isolate, estrange


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He consorts with wealthy businessmen. 他与富有的商人交往。
They consort regularly at the club. 他们经常在俱乐部聚会。

一般过去时:
She consorted with the enemy. 她与敌人勾结。
The two men consorted secretly to plan the heist. 这两个人秘密勾结策划抢劫。

现在完成时:
He has consorted with dubious characters throughout his career. 他职业生涯中一直与一些可疑人物来往。
They have consorted together for many years. 他们多年来一直在一起。


过去完成时:
Before the scandal broke, she had consorted with several high-profile politicians. 在丑闻爆发之前，她与几位高调的政治家交往。
They had consorted in secret for months before the plot was revealed.  他们在阴谋败露前秘密勾结了好几个月。


将来时:
He will consort with the opposition party to form a new government. 他将与反对党合作组建新政府。
She will continue to consort with those who share her ideals. 她将继续与那些与她志同道合的人交往。


虚拟语气:
If he had not consorted with those individuals, his reputation would not have been damaged. 如果他不与那些人交往，他的名誉就不会受损。
I wish I had not consorted with such a dishonest person. 我真希望我没有与这样一个不诚实的人交往。


被动语态:
He was consorted with by several influential figures. 他得到了几位有影响力的人物陪伴。
The plan was consorted upon in secret.  这个计划是秘密商定的。


疑问句:
With whom did he consort? 他和谁交往？
Did she consort with the rebels? 她与叛军勾结了吗？


否定句:
He did not consort with them. 他没有与他们交往。
She refused to consort with those who opposed her. 她拒绝与反对她的人交往。


PHRASE->

secretly consort: 秘密勾结
openly consort: 公开交往
