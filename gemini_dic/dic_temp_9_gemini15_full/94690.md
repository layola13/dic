improbable:/ɪmˈprɒbəbl/| adj. |不太可能的；未必的|unlikely to be true or to happen; doubtful|The theory is improbable but not impossible.  这个理论不太可能，但并非不可能。|It's improbable that he'll win the race. 他赢得比赛的可能性不大。|likely, possible, plausible |certain, likely, probable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The idea seems improbable. = 这个想法似乎不太可能。
His explanation is improbable. = 他的解释不太可信。


一般过去时:
The story seemed improbable. = 这个故事似乎不太可信。
The result was improbable. = 结果不太可能。


现在完成时:
The event has proved improbable. = 这件事已经被证明不太可能发生。
It has become increasingly improbable that he will win. = 他获胜的可能性越来越小了。


过去完成时:
It had seemed improbable that he would succeed. = 他会成功似乎不太可能。
The outcome had been improbable from the start. = 从一开始结果就不太可能。


将来时:
It will be improbable for him to finish the work on time. = 他按时完成工作的可能性很小。
The project is improbable to succeed without more funding. = 没有更多资金，这个项目不太可能成功。


虚拟语气:
It would be improbable for them to agree. = 他们同意是不太可能的。
If he were to win, it would be improbable. = 如果他赢了，那将是不太可能的。


被动语态:
The theory is considered improbable. = 这个理论被认为不太可能。
His alibi was deemed improbable by the jury. = 陪审团认为他的不在场证明不可信。


疑问句:
Is it improbable that she'll come? = 她会来不太可能吗？
Isn't it improbable he would do that? = 他会那样做是不是不太可能？


否定句:
It's not improbable that it will rain. = 下雨并非不可能。
It is not improbable that he will change his mind. = 他改变主意并非不可能。
