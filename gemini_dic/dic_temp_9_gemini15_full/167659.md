pricing:/ˈpraɪsɪŋ/| n. |定价，价格策略 |The process of determining the value or cost of goods or services.  It involves considering factors such as production costs, market demand, and competition. |Example:  The company's pricing strategy is to offer high-quality products at a premium price. 这家公司定价策略是提供高品质产品，并以高价出售。|近义词：costing, valuation, pricing policy |反义词：N/A


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The store's pricing is competitive. = 这家商店的价格很有竞争力。
Our pricing reflects the quality of our products. = 我们的定价反映了我们产品的质量。

一般过去时:
Their pricing was too high last year. = 他们去年的价格太高了。
The pricing changed after the new regulations. = 新规之后，价格改变了。

现在完成时:
They have revised their pricing several times this year. = 今年他们已经修改了几次价格。
The company has maintained its pricing despite increased costs. = 尽管成本增加，该公司仍保持其价格。

过去完成时:
Before the sale, their pricing had been much higher. = 促销之前，他们的价格要高得多。
The company had already finalized its pricing before the meeting. = 会议之前，公司已经确定了价格。

将来时:
They will adjust their pricing based on market conditions. = 他们将根据市场情况调整价格。
We will announce our new pricing next week. = 我们下周将公布我们的新价格。

虚拟语气:
If their pricing were lower, we would buy more. = 如果他们的价格更低，我们会买更多。
I wish their pricing was more transparent. = 我希望他们的价格更透明。

被动语态:
The pricing was determined by market research. = 价格是由市场调查决定的。
The new pricing is being finalized this week. = 新的价格本周正在最终确定。

疑问句:
What is your pricing on bulk orders? = 大宗订单的价格是多少？
How is your pricing structured? = 你们的定价结构是怎样的？

否定句:
Their pricing is not competitive enough. = 他们的价格竞争力不足。
We are not happy with their pricing. = 我们对他们的价格不满意。
