epagoge:/ɪˈpæɡədʒi/| n. |归纳法，例证法 |A type of argumentation or rhetorical device that uses examples to support a claim or generalization. It differs from deduction in that it moves from specific instances to a general conclusion, rather than the other way around.  Epagoge is often used to make an argument more persuasive by illustrating its points with concrete examples. |The lawyer used epagoge, citing several similar cases to support his client's innocence. |induction, illustration, exemplification |deduction |名词
