culdee:/ˈkʌldiː/| n. |早期苏格兰修道士|A member of a monastic order in early medieval Scotland, particularly associated with Iona and other western Scottish islands.  They were known for their piety, learning, and preservation of ancient traditions.|The culdees were renowned for their devotion and scholarship.|近义词：monk, hermit|反义词：layperson
