photoheliograph:/ˌfoʊtoʊˈhiːliəɡræf/| n. |摄影日像仪|A photoheliograph is a specialized telescope designed to photograph the sun, particularly its surface features.  It typically uses a long-focus lens to achieve high resolution images of sunspots and other solar phenomena.  Early versions were crucial in the development of solar astronomy.|Example:  The early astronomers used a photoheliograph to meticulously record sunspot activity.|近义词：solar telescope, solar camera |反义词：无
