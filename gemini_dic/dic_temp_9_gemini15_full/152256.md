patronization: /ˌpætɹənaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 施舍；恩赐；以恩人自居；屈尊俯就 | The act of treating someone as if they are inferior or less intelligent.  It often involves talking down to someone, offering unsolicited advice or help in a condescending manner, or displaying a superior attitude. |  例句：Her constant patronization made him feel belittled.  她的不断施舍让他感觉自己很渺小。

例句：The patronizing tone of his comments offended many people. 他说话的居高临下的语气冒犯了许多人。


近义词：condescension, condescencion, superiority, superciliousness | 反义词：respect, equality, consideration


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He patronizes his employees. = 他以恩人自居对待他的员工。
She patronizes local artists. = 她资助当地艺术家（含轻蔑意味）。


一般过去时:
They patronized the small cafe regularly. = 他们经常惠顾那家小咖啡馆。(中性用法)
He patronized her with unwanted advice. = 他以居高临下的姿态对她提出不想要的建议。


现在完成时:
She has patronized that charity for years. = 她多年来一直资助那家慈善机构。(中性用法)
He has patronized me throughout our conversation. = 他在整个谈话过程中一直对我很轻蔑。


过去完成时:
They had patronized that restaurant before, so they knew what to expect. = 他们以前去过那家餐馆，所以知道会发生什么。(中性用法)
She had patronized him so much that he finally snapped. = 她对他如此轻蔑，以至于他终于爆发了。


将来时:
I will patronize that new bookstore. = 我将惠顾那家新书店。(中性用法)
He will continue to patronize her, despite her protests. = 尽管她抗议，他仍将继续以恩人自居对待她。


虚拟语气:
If I were to patronize him, he might feel insulted. = 如果我对他施舍，他可能会感到受辱。
I wish I hadn't patronized her. = 我真希望我没有对她那么轻蔑。


被动语态:
The small business was patronized by the community. = 这家小企业受到了社区的支持。(中性用法)
She felt patronized by his comments.  = 她觉得他的话让她感觉受到了轻蔑。


疑问句:
Do you feel patronized by his behavior? = 你觉得他的行为让你感觉受到了轻蔑吗？
Does the company patronize any local arts organizations? = 这家公司资助任何地方艺术组织吗？（中性用法）


否定句:
I don't want to be patronized. = 我不想被轻视。
The teacher did not patronize her students. = 老师并没有轻视她的学生们。


PHRASE->
patronize a business = 惠顾一家企业
patronize the arts = 资助艺术
patron of the arts = 艺术赞助人
under the patronage of = 在…的赞助下
royal patronage = 皇室赞助


词源:
Patronization derives from the word "patron," which comes from the Latin word "patronus," meaning "protector," "advocate," or "supporter."  Over time, the meaning shifted to encompass the often condescending aspect of a superior acting as a protector or benefactor.
