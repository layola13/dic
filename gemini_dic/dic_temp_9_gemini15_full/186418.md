/ˈrɪbɛntrɒp/| n. |里宾特洛甫| Joachim von Ribbentrop (1893-1946) was a German Nazi politician who served as the Minister of Foreign Affairs under Adolf Hitler from 1938 to 1945. He played a significant role in negotiating agreements that led to World War II, including the Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact.  His name is largely synonymous with appeasement and the diplomatic maneuvering that preceded the war. He was convicted of war crimes at the Nuremberg trials and executed.|例句：Ribbentrop's signing of the Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact is considered a pivotal event leading to World War II.  里宾特洛甫签署《苏德互不侵犯条约》被认为是导致二战的关键事件。|近义词：Nazi official, diplomat, foreign minister|反义词：peacemaker, pacifist
