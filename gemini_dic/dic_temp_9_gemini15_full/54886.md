/dɪˈploʊsɪv/| n. |爆破音|A plosive is a consonant sound produced by completely stopping the airflow in the vocal tract, then suddenly releasing it.  This creates a brief burst of sound.  Plosives are also known as stops. |Examples include the sounds /p/, /b/, /t/, /d/, /k/, and /ɡ/ in English. The /p/ sound in "pen" and the /b/ sound in "bed" are both plosives. |近义词：stop, occlusive |反义词：fricative, approximant
