nod-off:/nɒd ɒf/| v.|打瞌睡，睡着|To fall asleep, especially unintentionally and briefly.  |例句：I nearly nod off during the boring lecture. 我在枯燥的演讲中差点睡着。
例句：He nodded off in front of the television. 他在电视机前睡着了。|近义词：doze off, drift off, drop off|反义词：stay awake, remain alert


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He nods off every afternoon. = 他每天下午都打瞌睡。
She often nods off during long meetings. = 她经常在冗长的会议上打瞌睡。


一般过去时:
I nodded off during the movie. = 我在看电影的时候睡着了。
He nodded off while driving, luckily without an accident. = 他开车时睡着了，幸运的是没有发生事故。


现在完成时:
I've nodded off several times this week. = 我这周已经好几次打瞌睡了。
She hasn't nodded off at work yet today. = 她今天上班还没打瞌睡。


过去完成时:
I had nodded off before the speaker finished. = 演讲者还没讲完我就睡着了。
They had nodded off before the concert ended. = 音乐会还没结束他们就睡着了。


将来时:
I think I'll nod off if I don't get some coffee. = 我想如果我不喝点咖啡，我会睡着的。
He will probably nod off during the long flight. = 他在漫长的飞行中可能会睡着。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't had so much coffee, I would have nodded off. = 如果我没有喝那么多咖啡，我就睡着了。
I wish I hadn't nodded off during the important presentation. = 我真希望我没有在重要的演示中睡着。


被动语态:
(This verb is rarely used in the passive voice because it's intransitive.)


疑问句:

否定句:
