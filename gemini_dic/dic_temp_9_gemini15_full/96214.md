indexical:/ɪnˈdɛksɪkəl/ | adj. | 指示性的；语境相关的 |  Relating to or denoting an expression whose meaning depends on the context of its use.  Indexicals are words or phrases whose reference depends on the context in which they are uttered.  For example, "I," "here," "now," and "this" are all indexicals because their meaning changes depending on who is speaking, where they are speaking, and when they are speaking. | The pronoun "I" is indexical, referring to the speaker.  The word "here" is indexical, denoting the location of the speaker. |近义词：context-dependent, situational | 反义词：invariant, constant |
