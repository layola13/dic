nurturers:/ˈnɜːrtʃərərz/| n.|养育者，培养者|Those who care for and encourage the growth or development of someone or something.|例句：Teachers are nurturers of young minds.  老师是年轻心灵的培养者。|例句：Good parents are nurturers, providing love and support to their children. 好父母是养育者，为孩子提供爱和支持。|近义词： caregivers, educators, developers, fosterers|反义词：neglectors, abandoners


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher nurtures her students' creativity. = 老师培养学生的创造力。
Parents nurture their children's talents. = 父母培养孩子的天赋。

一般过去时:
She nurtured her plants with care. = 她细心地培育她的植物。
He nurtured his dream of becoming a doctor. = 他悉心培育着他成为医生的梦想。

现在完成时:
They have nurtured a strong sense of community. = 他们已经培养了强烈的社区意识。
She has nurtured her skills over many years. = 她多年来一直培养自己的技能。

过去完成时:
He had nurtured his business for ten years before selling it. = 他在卖掉公司之前已经经营了十年。
The gardener had nurtured the seedlings for months before transplanting them. = 园丁在移植幼苗之前已经培育了几个月。

将来时:
She will nurture her child's potential. = 她将培养孩子的潜能。
They will nurture their friendship for years to come. = 他们将继续培养他们的友谊多年。

虚拟语气:
If she nurtured her talent, she would be a great artist. = 如果她培养她的天赋，她会成为一位伟大的艺术家。
I wish I had nurtured my musical abilities when I was younger. = 我希望我年轻的时候能培养我的音乐才能。

被动语态:

The child's talent was nurtured by his parents. = 孩子的天赋是由他的父母培养的。

疑问句:

否定句:
