domesticators:/dəˈmɛstɪkeɪtərz/| n. |驯化者，驯养者|People or groups who domesticated animals or plants.  They played a crucial role in the development of agriculture and human civilization.  |Example: The early domesticators of wheat in the Fertile Crescent significantly altered the course of human history. 早期在肥沃新月地带驯化小麦的人们极大地改变了人类历史的进程。|近义词：breeders, cultivators, tamers |反义词：wilders (not a direct antonym, but implies the opposite action)


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The farmer domesticates chickens. = 农民驯养鸡。
Domesticators carefully select animals for breeding. = 驯化者仔细挑选动物进行繁殖。


一般过去时:
Ancient peoples domesticated many animals. = 古代人驯化了许多动物。
Early farmers domesticated various plants. = 早期农民驯化了各种植物。


现在完成时:
Humans have domesticated many species. = 人类已经驯化了许多物种。
Scientists have studied the process of domestication. = 科学家们已经研究了驯化的过程。


过去完成时:
Before the Neolithic Revolution, humans had not domesticated many animals. = 在新石器革命之前，人类还没有驯化许多动物。
By the Bronze Age, many civilizations had already domesticated horses. = 到青铜时代，许多文明已经驯化了马。


将来时:
Researchers will continue to study animal domestication. = 研究人员将继续研究动物驯化。
Future generations may domesticate new species. = 后代可能会驯化新的物种。


虚拟语气:
If they had domesticated more plants, their lives would have been easier. = 如果他们驯化了更多的植物，他们的生活就会更容易。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
