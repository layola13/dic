/ˌɪntərˈmɛʒərz/ | n. | 互测；交互测量 | Intermeasures refer to the process of measuring something multiple times, often using different methods or instruments, to ensure accuracy and reliability.  The results of these multiple measurements are then compared to assess the consistency and validity of the findings.  | Example:  The researchers conducted intermeasures to validate the accuracy of the new blood pressure monitor.  研究人员进行了互测以验证新型血压计的准确性。

|近义词: cross-validation, repeated measures, comparative measurements |反义词: single measurement, isolated measurement |名词
