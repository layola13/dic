endoskeletons:/ˌɛndoʊˈskɛlətənz/| n. |内骨骼|An endoskeleton is an internal skeleton, found within the soft tissues of an organism.  It provides structural support and protection for the body.  Unlike exoskeletons, which are external, endoskeletons grow with the organism.  Examples include the bony skeletons of vertebrates and the cartilaginous skeletons of some invertebrates.|例句：Humans have an endoskeleton made of bone. = 人类拥有由骨骼构成的内骨骼。

例句：The shark's endoskeleton is made of cartilage, not bone. = 鲨鱼的内骨骼由软骨构成，而不是骨头。
