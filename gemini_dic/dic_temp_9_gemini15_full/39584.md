consecrate:/ˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/ /ˈkɑːnsɪkreɪt/| vt.|奉献；祝圣；使神圣|To make or declare sacred; dedicate formally to a religious or divine purpose; to set apart for a special purpose.|例句：The church was consecrated in 1880. 这座教堂于1880年祝圣。

例句：He consecrated his life to the service of others. 他将一生奉献于为他人服务。|近义词：dedicate, sanctify, hallow|反义词：desecrate, profane, defile|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He consecrates his time to his family. = 他将时间奉献给他的家人。
She consecrates her life to charity work. = 她将生命奉献给慈善事业。


一般过去时:
They consecrated the new temple last week. = 他们上周为新庙宇举行了祝圣仪式。
The king consecrated the land to the gods. = 国王将这片土地献给神明。


现在完成时:
She has consecrated her life to helping the poor. = 她已将一生奉献给帮助穷人。
He has consecrated many years to his research. = 他已将许多年投入到他的研究中。


过去完成时:
They had consecrated the altar before the ceremony began. = 仪式开始前，他们已将祭坛祝圣。
The priest had consecrated the bread and wine. = 牧师已将面包和葡萄酒祝圣。


将来时:
The bishop will consecrate the new cathedral next month. = 主教下个月将为新大教堂举行祝圣仪式。
They will consecrate the building to the memory of their ancestors. = 他们将把这座建筑物奉献给他们的祖先的纪念。


虚拟语气:
If they consecrated the land, the harvest would be bountiful. = 如果他们将土地奉献给神明，收成将会丰收。
I wish I could consecrate more time to my hobbies. = 我希望我能将更多的时间奉献给我的爱好。


被动语态:
The land was consecrated centuries ago. = 这片土地几个世纪前就被奉献了。
The new church will be consecrated next Sunday. = 新教堂将在下周日举行祝圣仪式。


疑问句:
Was the building consecrated? = 这座建筑物是否被祝圣了？
Will the priest consecrate the chalice? = 牧师会祝圣圣杯吗？


否定句:
They did not consecrate the temple. = 他们没有为庙宇举行祝圣仪式。
He did not consecrate his life to material pursuits. = 他没有将生命奉献给物质追求。


PHRASE->
consecrate oneself to = 将自己奉献给…
consecrate one's life to = 将一生奉献给…
consecrate to God = 献给上帝
consecrate a building = 为建筑物举行祝圣仪式
consecrated ground = 圣地


词源:
From Latin *consecrāre, from con- "together" + sacrāre "to make sacred".  The word's journey reflects its meaning:  to make something wholly and completely sacred, uniting it with a divine or deeply significant purpose.
