mispublicized: /ˌmɪspʌˈblɪsaɪzd/ | adj. | 宣传失实的，宣传错误的 |  Something that is mispublicized has been publicized incorrectly or misleadingly, often resulting in a distorted or negative perception.  |  The new product was mispublicized, leading to lower-than-expected sales. 新产品宣传失实，导致销量低于预期。  |近义词: misrepresented, wrongly advertised,  badly publicized | 反义词: accurately publicized, well-publicized


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company mispublicizes its products. = 公司对它的产品进行虚假宣传。
This event is mispublicized by the media. = 这个事件被媒体宣传错误了。


一般过去时:
The politician mispublicized his achievements. = 这位政治家夸大了他的成就。
The movie was mispublicized, leading to low audience numbers. = 这部电影宣传失实，导致观众数量较少。


现在完成时:
They have mispublicized the risks involved. = 他们已经歪曲了所涉及的风险。
The government has mispublicized its response to the crisis. = 政府对危机的应对进行了错误的宣传。


过去完成时:
The scandal had been mispublicized before the truth came out. = 在真相大白之前，这起丑闻就已经被歪曲宣传了。
The company had mispublicized its financial performance for years. = 这家公司多年来一直夸大其财务业绩。

虚拟语气:
If they had not mispublicized the product, sales would have been better. = 如果他们没有对产品进行虚假宣传，销售额就会更好。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The company did not mispublicize its intentions. = 公司并没有歪曲其意图。
