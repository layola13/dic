dickinsonia:/dɪkɪnˈsoʊniə/| n. |狄更逊虫|Dickinsonia is a genus of extinct organisms from the Ediacaran biota, known for their segmented, frond-like bodies.  They are considered to be among the earliest known complex multicellular organisms.  Their exact phylogenetic placement is still debated, with suggestions ranging from early animals to lichens. | Example:  The discovery of Dickinsonia fossils provided valuable insights into the early evolution of life. 狄更逊虫化石的发现为了解早期生命进化提供了宝贵的见解。|近义词:Ediacaran organism,  (no perfect synonyms due to unique nature)|反义词: (none applicable)|名词
