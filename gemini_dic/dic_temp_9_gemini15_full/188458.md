rudiment:/ˈruːdɪmənt/| n. |基本原理；初步；基本知识；残余|The basic principles or elements of a subject.  Rudiments often refer to the most elementary stages of learning or development.  They can also describe something that is rudimentary or undeveloped, existing only in a basic or undeveloped form.|The course covered the rudiments of calculus. 这门课程讲解了微积分的基本原理。

He only possesses the rudiments of French. 他只会一点法语基础。


近义词：basics, fundamentals, elements, beginnings, essentials

反义词：advanced, sophisticated, complex, developed


词性: 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The course teaches the rudiments of grammar. = 这门课程教授语法基础。
These are the rudiments of good writing. = 这些是良好写作的基本要素。

She learned the rudiments of music as a child. = 她小时候学习了音乐基础。
He mastered the rudiments of carpentry quickly. = 他很快掌握了木工的基本技巧。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

He had learned the rudiments of chess before joining the club. = 加入俱乐部之前，他已经学习了国际象棋的基本规则。
