fraying: /ˈfreɪɪŋ/ | adj. | 磨损的；蓬乱的；紧张的 |  The process of becoming worn or unraveled; a state of disorder or disarray;  a situation characterized by increasing tension or conflict. | 例句：The edges of the carpet are fraying.地毯的边缘磨损了。


The relationship between them is fraying at the edges. 他们之间的关系正变得紧张。


近义词：wearing, unraveling, deteriorating, weakening,  tense, strained | 反义词：intact, strong, stable, firm, calm


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rope is fraying. = 绳子正在磨损。
His nerves are fraying. = 他的神经快要崩溃了。


一般过去时:
The fabric frayed after several washes. = 这块布料洗了几次后就磨损了。
Their friendship frayed under the pressure. = 他们的友谊在压力下破裂了。


现在完成时:
The edges of the blanket have frayed. = 毛毯的边缘已经磨损了。
My patience has frayed after waiting for hours. = 等了几个小时后，我的耐心已经耗尽了。


过去完成时:
The rope had frayed before it snapped. = 绳子断裂前就已经磨损了。
His confidence had frayed considerably before the presentation. = 演讲前他的信心已经大受打击。


将来时:
The fabric will fray if you don't take care of it. = 如果你不注意保养，这块布料会磨损。
The negotiations will fray if they don't reach a compromise. = 如果他们达不成妥协，谈判将会破裂。


虚拟语气:
If the rope hadn't frayed, it wouldn't have broken. = 如果绳子没有磨损，它就不会断了。
If their relationship hadn't frayed, they might still be together. = 如果他们的关系没有破裂，他们可能仍然在一起。


被动语态:

The peace talks were frayed by disagreements. = 和谈因分歧而破裂了。


疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
From Old French *freier, from Frankish *frajan, from Proto-Germanic *frajanan ("to rub, to fray").  Related to the English word "fret" (to worry).
