objecting:/əbˈdʒɛktɪŋ/ | v. |反对；提出异议 | Expressing disapproval of or opposition to something. |例句：He's objecting to the new rules. 他反对新规。|例句：Many people are objecting to the proposed development. 许多人反对拟议中的开发项目。|近义词：protesting, opposing, dissenting, remonstrating |反义词：approving, consenting, agreeing |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are objecting to the changes. = 他们在反对这些改变。
We object to the noise. = 我们反对噪音。

一般过去时:
She objected to his proposal. = 她反对他的提议。
He objected strongly to the decision. = 他强烈反对这个决定。

现在完成时:
They have objected to the plan several times. = 他们已经几次反对这个计划了。
I have never objected to his ideas before. = 我以前从未反对过他的想法。

过去完成时:
He had objected to the idea before the meeting. = 他在会议之前就反对过这个想法。
She had already objected when he proposed the plan. = 当他提出计划时，她已经反对了。

将来时:
They will object to the new policy. = 他们将会反对这项新政策。
I think he will object to our suggestion. = 我认为他会反对我们的建议。

虚拟语气:
I would object if they tried to change the rules. = 如果他们试图改变规则，我会反对。
If he objected, we would have to reconsider. = 如果他反对，我们就必须重新考虑。

被动语态:
The proposal was objected to by many people. = 许多人反对这项提议。
The changes were strongly objected to. = 这些改变受到了强烈的反对。

疑问句:
Are you objecting to anything? = 你反对什么吗？
Is anyone objecting to the proposal? = 有人反对这项提议吗？

否定句:
I'm not objecting to the idea. = 我不反对这个主意。
She didn't object to the plan. = 她没有反对这个计划。


PHRASE->
object to something = 反对某事
object strongly = 强烈反对
have no objection = 没意见
raise an objection = 提出异议
lodge an objection = 提出异议
