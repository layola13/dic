determinative: /dɪˈtɜːrmɪnətɪv/ | adj. |限定的，决定性的 | A determinative word or element is one that limits or specifies the meaning of another word or phrase.  It is often used in grammar to describe words like articles (a, an, the), demonstratives (this, that, these, those), possessives (my, your, his, her, its, our, their), and quantifiers (some, any, many, few).  These words specify which noun or noun phrase is being referred to. |  The determinative clause in the sentence modifies the subject.  限定性从句修饰了句子的主语。|  limiting, restrictive, defining | non-determinative, indefinite
