indiction:/ɪnˈdɪkʃən/| n.|指示；征兆；宣布；(尤指罗马皇帝的)即位；|The act of indicating or pointing out; a sign or indication; the formal announcement, especially of the accession of a Roman emperor.|例句：The dark clouds were an indication of an approaching storm.  乌云是暴风雨即将来临的征兆。|例句：The king's death led to the immediate indiction of his heir. 国王的去世导致他的继承人立即即位。|近义词：sign, symptom, announcement, proclamation|反义词：concealment, suppression


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The doctor's examination gives an indication of the patient's condition. = 医生检查显示了病人的病情。
This map provides an indication of the area's population density. = 这张地图显示了该地区的人口密度。


一般过去时:
The rising tide gave an indication that the storm was coming. = 上涨的潮水预示着暴风雨即将来临。
His behavior gave a clear indication of his guilt. = 他的行为清楚地表明了他有罪。


现在完成时:
Recent studies have given an indication of the effectiveness of the new drug. = 最近的研究表明这种新药的疗效。
The results have given an indication of the challenges ahead. = 结果显示了未来的挑战。


过去完成时:
The broken window had given an indication that a robbery had taken place. = 打碎的窗户表明发生了抢劫。
The earlier symptoms had given an indication of the serious illness. = 早期的症状表明了严重的疾病。


将来时:
Future economic indicators will give an indication of the country's growth. = 未来的经济指标将显示该国的增长情况。
The weather forecast will give an indication of tomorrow's conditions. = 天气预报将显示明天的天气状况。


虚拟语气:
If there were a clear indication of danger, we would evacuate. = 如果有明显的危险迹象，我们将撤离。
I wish there had been an earlier indication of the problem. = 我希望早些时候就有这个问题的迹象。


被动语态:
The patient's condition was indicated by several tests. = 病人的病情由几项检查显示。
The problem was indicated by the low test scores. = 低分显示了这个问题。


疑问句:
What indication do you have that he is guilty? = 你有什么迹象表明他有罪？
Does this data give any indication of the trend? = 这些数据能显示出任何趋势吗？


否定句:
There was no indication of foul play. = 没有犯规的迹象。
The report gave no indication of the cause of the accident. = 报告没有说明事故的原因。
