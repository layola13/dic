half-inclined: /ˌhɑːfɪnˈklaɪnd/ | adj. | 半心半意的；有点倾向于 |  Feeling or showing some but not complete willingness or inclination to do something.  It implies a hesitant or uncertain attitude. |

例句：

I'm half-inclined to go to the party, but I'm not sure yet. = 我有点想去参加派对，但我还没确定。

She was half-inclined to believe him, but something felt off. = 她有点相信他，但感觉有点不对劲。


近义词：wavering, hesitant, irresolute, tentative, unsure

反义词：determined, resolute, decided, certain


词性：形容词

词源:

The word is a combination of "half," meaning "partially" or "to a limited extent," and "inclined," meaning "leaning toward a particular action or opinion."  The root of "inclined" comes from the Latin word "inclinare," meaning "to bend or lean."
