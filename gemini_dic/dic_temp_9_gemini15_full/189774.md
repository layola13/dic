salut:/ˈsɑːluːt/| n. int.|敬礼；问候|A formal greeting or farewell.|例句：The soldiers gave a salut to the flag.士兵向国旗敬礼。|近义词：greeting, hello, regards |反义词:disrespect, insult.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gives a salut to his superior. = 他向他的上司敬礼。
They exchange salutations before the meeting. = 他们在会议前互相问候。

一般过去时:
The officer saluted the general. = 军官向将军敬礼。
She gave a polite salut to her neighbor. = 她礼貌地向她的邻居问候。


现在完成时:
The troops have saluted the commander. = 部队已经向指挥官敬礼。
He has given many salutations throughout his career. = 他在他的职业生涯中给予了许多问候。


过去完成时:
The soldiers had saluted before the ceremony began. = 典礼开始前，士兵们已经敬礼了。
She had already given her salut before he arrived. = 他到达之前，她已经问候过了。


将来时:
They will salut the flag tomorrow. = 他们明天将向国旗敬礼。
He will give a formal salut to the guests. = 他将向宾客正式致意。


虚拟语气:
If I were to meet the president, I would give him a respectful salut. = 如果我见到总统，我会向他致以尊敬的敬礼。
I wish I had given a more heartfelt salut to my grandmother. = 我希望我曾向我的祖母表达更衷心的问候。


被动语态:
The flag was saluted by the soldiers. = 国旗受到了士兵们的敬礼。
The guests were greeted with warm salutations. = 客人们受到了热烈的问候。


疑问句:
Did he give a salut? = 他敬礼了吗？
Did they exchange salutations? = 他们互相问候了吗？


否定句:
He did not give a salut. = 他没有敬礼。
They did not exchange salutations. = 他们没有互相问候。


PHRASE->
salut to = 向…敬礼
