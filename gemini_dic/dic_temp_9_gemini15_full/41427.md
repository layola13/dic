corners:/ˈkɔːrnərz/ | n. |角落，拐角；困境 |The four corners of the room are painted blue.  房间的四个角都漆成蓝色。 He painted himself into a corner with his lies. 他因说谎而陷入困境。 |近义词：angle, edge |反义词：center, middle |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The cat sits in the corners of the room. = 猫坐在房间的角落里。
He always cuts corners when doing his work. = 他做工作总是偷工减料。


一般过去时:
She turned the corner and saw her friend. = 她转过弯，看到了她的朋友。
They cut corners to finish the project on time. = 他们为了按时完成项目而偷工减料。


现在完成时:
We have turned many corners on this long journey. = 在这段漫长的旅程中，我们已经走过了许多弯路。
They have cut too many corners, and now the product is faulty. = 他们偷工减料太多，现在产品有缺陷了。


过去完成时:
He had already rounded the corner before I saw him. = 我看到他之前，他已经转过弯了。
The company had cut so many corners that it eventually went bankrupt.  = 那家公司偷工减料太多，最终破产了。


将来时:
We will turn the corner soon and reach the destination. = 我们很快就会转弯到达目的地。
They will likely cut corners again to meet the deadline. = 他们很可能会再次偷工减料以赶上截止日期。


虚拟语气:
If I had turned the corner sooner, I would have seen the accident. = 如果我早点转弯，我就会看到事故了。
If they hadn't cut corners, the project would have been successful. = 如果他们没有偷工减料，项目就会成功。


被动语态:
The corners of the painting were damaged. = 画的角落被损坏了。
Corners are often cut in the manufacturing process to save costs. = 为了节省成本，制造过程中经常会偷工减料。


疑问句:
Are there any sharp corners in this room? = 这个房间里有没有尖锐的角落？

否定句:
The table doesn't have any sharp corners. = 这张桌子没有任何尖锐的角落。
They didn't cut corners on this project; they did a thorough job. = 他们在这个项目上没有偷工减料；他们做得非常彻底。

词源:
The word "corner" comes from the Old English word "cornor," which itself derives from the Latin word "cornu," meaning "horn."  This reflects the shape of a corner, resembling a horn.
