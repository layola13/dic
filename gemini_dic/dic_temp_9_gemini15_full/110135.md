lie: /laɪ/ | n., v. | 谎言；说谎 |  A statement that is not true, or the action of making such a statement.  | 例句：He told a lie about where he had been. 他撒谎说他去过哪里了。  | 近义词： falsehood, fib, deception | 反义词： truth, honesty |  动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He lies to his mother every day. = 他每天都对他母亲撒谎。
She lies about her age. = 她谎报年龄。


一般过去时:
He lied to the police. = 他对警察撒谎了。
She lied about seeing the accident. = 她撒谎说没看见事故。


现在完成时:
He has lied to me repeatedly. = 他一再对我撒谎。
She has lied about her qualifications. = 她对自己的资历撒谎了。


过去完成时:
He had lied to her before, so she didn't trust him. = 他以前就对她撒谎过，所以她不信任他。
She had lied to her parents for years. = 她多年来一直对她父母撒谎。


将来时:
He will lie again if he gets the chance. = 如果有机会，他会再次撒谎。
She will lie to protect her friend. = 她会撒谎来保护她的朋友。


虚拟语气:
If he lied, he would be punished. = 如果他撒谎，他将受到惩罚。
I wish she hadn't lied to me. = 我真希望她没有对我撒谎。


被动语态:
Lies were told to the jury. = 对陪审团说了谎。
The truth was hidden, and lies were spread. = 真相被隐藏，谎言被传播。


疑问句:
Did he lie to you? = 他对你撒谎了吗？
Is she lying to us? = 她对我们撒谎吗？


否定句:
He didn't lie to me. = 他没有对我撒谎。
She doesn't lie often. = 她不常撒谎。


PHRASE->
tell a lie = 撒谎
a white lie = 善意的谎言
lie down = 躺下
lie in = 赖床
lie with = 与…同眠


词源:
The word "lie" comes from the Old English word "lēogan," meaning "to lie, tell falsehoods."  This word has Germanic roots, related to words in other Germanic languages with similar meanings.  The noun sense ("a falsehood") developed later from the verb sense.
