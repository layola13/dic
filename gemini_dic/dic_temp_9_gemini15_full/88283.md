hinting:/ˈhɪntɪŋ/| v. |暗示；示意；提示|To suggest or indirectly indicate something.  The act of giving hints. |例句：She was hinting at trouble with her boyfriend. 她暗示她和男朋友之间出了问题。

例句：He hinted that he might be leaving his job. 他暗示他可能要辞职了。


近义词：suggesting, implying, suggesting, insinuating|反义词：stating directly, explicitly declaring


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is hinting at a promotion. = 他暗示升职。
She hints at her dissatisfaction daily. = 她每天都暗示她的不满。

一般过去时:
He hinted at a problem yesterday. = 他昨天暗示了一个问题。
She hinted that she needed help. = 她暗示她需要帮助。

现在完成时:
He has been hinting at leaving for months. = 他已经暗示要离开好几个月了。
She has hinted at her plans several times. = 她已经几次暗示了她的计划。

过去完成时:
He had hinted at his resignation before the meeting. = 他在会议之前就暗示过要辞职。
She had hinted at her unhappiness for a long time. = 她很久以前就暗示过她的不快乐。

将来时:
He will hint at his intentions soon. = 他很快就会暗示他的意图。
She will hint at the surprise next week. = 她下周会暗示这个惊喜。

虚拟语气:
If he hinted at the truth, I would believe him. = 如果他暗示真相，我会相信他。
I wish she had hinted at the difficulties earlier. = 我希望她早点暗示这些困难。

被动语态:
The problem was hinted at in the report. = 报告中暗示了这个问题。
The solution was hinted at during the discussion. = 在讨论中暗示了解决方案。

疑问句:
Was he hinting at something? = 他是在暗示什么吗？
Is she hinting at a secret romance? = 她是在暗示一段秘密恋情吗？

否定句:
He didn't hint at any problems. = 他没有暗示任何问题。
She didn't hint at her true feelings. = 她没有暗示她真实的想法。


PHRASE->
hint at something: 暗示某事

take a hint: 领会暗示
a broad hint: 明显的暗示
subtle hint:  微妙的暗示
