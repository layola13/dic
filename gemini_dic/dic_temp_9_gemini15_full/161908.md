pragmatisms:/ˈpræɡməˌtɪzəmz/| n. |实用主义；实用论|The philosophical approach that emphasizes practical consequences and real-world applications over abstract theories.  It focuses on what works and is effective, often disregarding idealistic or purely theoretical considerations. |例句：His pragmatism led him to compromise on some ideals to achieve his goals.  他的实用主义使他为了达到目标而妥协了一些理想。|例句：The company's pragmatism in dealing with the crisis saved it from bankruptcy. 公司在处理危机时的实用主义使它避免了破产。|近义词：practicality, realism, expediency|反义词：idealism, theory, impracticality|名词
