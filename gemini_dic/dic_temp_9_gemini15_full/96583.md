induct:/ɪnˈdʌkt/| vt.| 吸收；使就职；引入；归纳；接纳 | To admit (someone) formally into a group or organization, especially with a ceremony.  To introduce or install someone into an office or position. To lead or guide someone into a particular state or situation.  To deduce (a general principle) from particular facts or instances. | 例句：The company inducted its new employees last week. 公司上周吸收了新员工。  They inducted him into the Hall of Fame. 他们将他引进名人堂。  The evidence inducts us to believe that he is guilty. 证据使我们相信他是有罪的。 | 近义词：initiate, install, introduce, enroll, admit | 反义词：expel, dismiss, remove, eject


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company inducts new employees every month. = 公司每月都吸收新员工。
They induct him into the club. = 他们吸收他加入俱乐部。

一般过去时:
The professor inducted his students into the mysteries of quantum physics. = 教授引导他的学生进入量子物理学的奥秘。
They inducted her into the secret society. = 他们吸收她加入秘密社团。

现在完成时:
He has been inducted into the army. = 他已被征入伍。
The organization has inducted many talented people. = 该组织吸收了许多人才。

过去完成时:
Before the ceremony, they had already inducted the new members. = 典礼之前，他们已经吸收了新成员。
She had been inducted into the society several years ago. = 几年前她就加入了那个社团。

将来时:
The university will induct the graduating class. = 大学将举行毕业典礼。
They will induct the new officers next week. = 他们下周将任命新官员。

虚拟语气:
If they inducted him, the team would be much stronger. = 如果他们吸收了他，球队就会强大得多。
I wish they had inducted her into the team earlier. = 我希望他们早点让她加入球队。

被动语态:
The new members were inducted into the club. = 新成员被吸收进俱乐部。
He was inducted into the army last year. = 他去年被征入伍。

疑问句:
Will they induct her into the organization? = 他们会吸收她加入组织吗？
Was he inducted into the hall of fame? = 他被引进名人堂了吗？

否定句:
They did not induct him into the club. = 他们没有吸收他加入俱乐部。
She was not inducted into the secret society.  = 她没有被吸收进入秘密社团。


PHRASE->

induct into:  引进…；使…加入
induct someone into office: 使某人就职
induct someone into a secret: 向某人泄露秘密
induct a charge: 提出控告
be inducted into: 被吸收；被介绍
