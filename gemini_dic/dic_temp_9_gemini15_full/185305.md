retrenchers:/rɪˈtrentʃərz/| n. |节约者；裁员者|People who implement retrenchment, either by reducing expenses or laying off employees.  They are often associated with times of economic hardship or organizational restructuring.  |例句：The company's retrenchers announced a 10% reduction in workforce.  这家公司的裁员者宣布裁员10%。 The retrenchers implemented strict cost-cutting measures. 节约者实施了严格的成本削减措施。|近义词：cost-cutters, downsizers, budget-reducers |反义词：expanders, investors


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company retrenches to improve profitability. = 公司正在节约开支以提高盈利能力。
The retrenchers are cutting costs across the board. = 裁员者正在全面削减成本。

一般过去时:
The government retrenched spending last year. = 政府去年削减了开支。
They retrenched heavily after the financial crisis. = 他们在金融危机后进行了大幅裁员。

现在完成时:
The company has retrenched significantly in recent years. = 近年来，公司已经大幅度裁员。
They have retrenched their marketing budget. = 他们已经削减了营销预算。

过去完成时:
The company had already retrenched before the merger. = 公司在合并之前就已经进行了裁员。
They had retrenched staff several times in the past decade. = 他们在过去十年中多次裁员。

将来时:
The company will retrench if profits continue to fall. = 如果利润继续下降，公司将进行裁员。
They will retrench their operations in the struggling market. = 他们将在低迷的市场中精简运营。

虚拟语气:
If the company hadn't retrenched, it would have gone bankrupt. = 如果公司没有裁员，它早就破产了。
I wish they hadn't retrenched so many talented employees. = 我真希望他们没有裁掉那么多有才华的员工。

被动语态:
Spending was retrenched by the government. = 开支被政府削减了。
The workforce was retrenched due to economic downturn. = 由于经济低迷，员工被裁掉了。

疑问句:

Will they retrench more staff next year? = 他们明年还会裁员吗？

否定句:
