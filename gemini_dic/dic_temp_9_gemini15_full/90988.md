hurting:/ˈhɜːrtɪŋ/ | v. adj. | 疼痛；受伤；令人痛苦的 |  To cause physical pain or emotional distress.  It can be used as a verb describing the act of causing pain or as an adjective describing something that causes pain. |例句：My head is hurting. = 我的头很痛。

The news was really hurting him. = 这个消息真的让他很难过。


近义词：aching, painful, agonizing, distressing | 反义词：soothing, comforting, healing | 词性：动词，形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My back is hurting. = 我的背很疼。
This situation is hurting the business. = 这种情况正在损害生意。


一般过去时:
My knee hurt badly after the fall. = 我摔倒后膝盖很疼。
The criticism hurt her feelings. = 批评伤害了她的感情。


现在完成时:
My foot has been hurting all day. = 我的脚一整天都疼。
The scandal has hurt the company's reputation. = 丑闻损害了公司的声誉。


过去完成时:
My head had been hurting for days before I went to the doctor. = 在我去看医生之前，我的头已经疼了好几天了。
The drought had hurt the farmers' crops severely. = 干旱严重损害了农民的庄稼。


将来时:
I think my tooth will be hurting later. = 我想我的牙齿以后会疼。
The new regulations will hurt small businesses. = 新规将损害小型企业。


虚拟语气:
If I knew it would hurt so much, I wouldn't have done it. = 如果我知道会这么痛，我就不会做了。
I wish this headache would stop hurting. = 我希望这个头痛能停止。


被动语态:
His feelings were hurt by her words. = 他的感情被她的话伤害了。
The company's profits were hurt by the economic downturn. = 公司的利润受到了经济衰退的损害。


疑问句:
Is your arm still hurting? = 你的胳膊还疼吗？
Is this decision hurting the project? = 这个决定会损害项目吗？


否定句:
My leg isn't hurting anymore. = 我的腿不疼了。
The changes won't hurt the environment. = 这些变化不会损害环境。


PHRASE->
be hurting for something = 缺乏…；急需…
hurt one's feelings = 伤害某人的感情
hurt oneself = 受伤
hurting like hell = 剧烈疼痛
a hurting economy = 疲软的经济
