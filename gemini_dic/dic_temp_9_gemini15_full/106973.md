lackeyship: /ˈlækiʃɪp/ | n. | 谄媚；奴性；奴才行为 | The state or condition of being a lackey; servile behavior; obsequiousness.  It implies a fawning and submissive attitude towards someone in a position of power, often involving the abandonment of one's own principles or interests. | Example: His lackeyship was evident in his constant attempts to please his boss, even when it meant compromising his own integrity.  他的谄媚行为在他不断讨好老板的尝试中显而易见，即使这意味着要牺牲自己的正直。|近义词：servility, obsequiousness, fawning, toadying |反义词：independence, autonomy, self-respect
