non-sustainable:/ˌnɒn səˈsteɪnəbəl/| adj. |不可持续的，不能维持的| Not able to be maintained at the same level; not able to continue at the same rate.  This refers to processes, practices, or systems that deplete resources or cause environmental damage at a rate that cannot be sustained indefinitely. |例句：The current rate of resource consumption is non-sustainable.  当前的资源消耗速度是不可持续的。

例句：Their business model proved to be non-sustainable in the long run. 他们的商业模式最终证明是不可持续的。


近义词：unsustainable, untenable, unviable, short-sighted

反义词：sustainable, maintainable, viable


词源：

* **non-**:  A prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of".
* **sustainable**:  Derived from "sustain" (to support or maintain), which comes from the Old French word *soutenir* (to hold up, support).  The "-able" suffix indicates capability or possibility.
