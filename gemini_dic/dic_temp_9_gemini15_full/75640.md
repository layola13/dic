fugues:/fjuːɡz/| n.|赋格曲，赋格(音乐形式)|A fugue is a contrapuntal compositional technique in music where a melodic idea (the subject) is introduced in one voice, then imitated in other voices, often at different intervals and pitches.  It's characterized by its intricate interplay of voices and its complex structure.|Example: Bach's "Well-Tempered Clavier" contains many famous fugues. 巴赫的《平均律钢琴曲集》包含许多著名的赋格曲。|近义词：canon (though fugues are more complex)|反义词：homophonic texture (music with a single melodic line and accompaniment)
