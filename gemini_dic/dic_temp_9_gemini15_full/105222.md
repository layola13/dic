keystones: /ˈkiːstoʊnz/ | n. | 拱心石；关键；基石 |

英文解析:A keystone is the central stone at the top of an arch, which holds the other stones in place.  Figuratively, it refers to something that is essential to the success or effectiveness of a larger project or system.

例句:
The keystone of the arch is perfectly positioned.  拱心石的位置非常完美。

The new policy is considered the keystone of the government's economic plan.  这项新政策被认为是政府经济计划的基石。


近义词: cornerstone, foundation, linchpin, essential element

反义词:  unimportant part, peripheral element


词性: noun (名词)
