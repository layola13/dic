opine:/oʊˈpaɪn/| vt.|发表意见；认为；主张|To express an opinion; to believe or hold as an opinion.|The critics opined that the play was a masterpiece.  评论家们认为这部剧是杰作。|He opined that the new policy would be beneficial. 他认为新政策将是有益的。|express, suggest, declare|—|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He opines that the economy will improve. = 他认为经济将会好转。
They opine that the project is feasible. = 他们认为这个项目是可行的。

一般过去时:
She opined that the painting was overpriced. = 她认为这幅画价格过高。
The professor opined that the student's work was excellent. = 教授认为学生的作业很优秀。

现在完成时:
Experts have opined that the situation is serious. = 专家们已经认为情况严重。
They have opined on the matter several times. = 他们已经多次就此事发表意见。

过去完成时:
Before the vote, he had opined that the bill would fail. = 在投票之前，他曾认为该法案将会失败。
She had opined against the proposal for many years. = 她多年来一直反对这项提案。

将来时:
He will opine on the matter at the next meeting. = 他将在下次会议上就此事发表意见。
They will opine that the changes are necessary. = 他们将会认为这些改变是必要的。

虚拟语气:
If he opined differently, we would reconsider. = 如果他持有不同意见，我们会重新考虑。
I wish he had opined more strongly against the plan. = 我希望他当时能更强烈地反对该计划。

被动语态:
It was opined by many that the decision was wrong. = 许多人认为这个决定是错误的。
The new policy was opined to be unfair. = 新政策被认为是不公平的。

疑问句:
What did he opine about the situation? = 他对这种情况有什么看法？
What do you opine about the new design? = 你对新的设计有什么看法？

否定句:
He did not opine that the evidence was conclusive. = 他不认为证据是确凿的。
They did not opine on the matter publicly. = 他们没有公开就此事发表意见。
