catchpoll: /ˈkætʃpɒl/ | n. |乡镇治安官，警察 | A catchpoll was a low-ranking peace officer, often responsible for apprehending criminals and maintaining order in a town or village.  The term is now largely archaic. | Example: The catchpoll pursued the fleeing thief through the cobbled streets.  乡镇治安官追捕逃犯穿过鹅卵石街道。 | 近义词：constable, bailiff, peace officer | 反义词：criminal, offender |  名词
