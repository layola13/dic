salmon:/ˈsæmən/ | n. |鲑鱼；三文鱼 | A salmon is a type of fish that lives in both salt water and fresh water.  They are known for their pinkish-orange flesh and are a popular food source. | Example: We had grilled salmon for dinner last night.  昨晚我们吃了烤鲑鱼。  |近义词：trout, fish | 反义词：None

She eats salmon regularly. = 她经常吃鲑鱼。

词源:

The word "salmon" comes from the Old French word "saumon," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "salmo."  The Latin word is believed to be of Celtic origin.
