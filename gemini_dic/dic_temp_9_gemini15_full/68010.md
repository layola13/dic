factoids:/ˈfæktɔɪdz/ | n. |小道消息；琐碎的“事实”；似是而非的事实 |  A factoid is a piece of information that is presented as a fact, but which is actually incorrect, misleading, or unsubstantiated.  They are often presented in a way that makes them seem credible, even if they are not.  Factoids can be spread through various media, including news reports, social media, and even academic publications. | Example: The claim that "most people only use 10% of their brain" is a persistent factoid, despite being demonstrably false.  | 近义词：rumor, hearsay, trivia, anecdote | 反义词：fact, truth, verified information |  名词
