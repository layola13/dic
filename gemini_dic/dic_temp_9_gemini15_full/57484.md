drag:/dræɡ/| v. n.|拖，拉，拽；沉闷乏味的人或事 | To pull (something) along forcefully, often with difficulty; something tedious or boring. |例句：He dragged the heavy box across the room. 他费力地把沉重的箱子拖过房间。  |例句：The meeting was a real drag.  这个会议真是枯燥乏味。|近义词：pull, haul, tow,  |反义词：push, propel


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drags the suitcase down the street. = 他把行李箱拖过街道。
She drags her feet when it comes to work. = 她工作的时候磨磨蹭蹭。

一般过去时:
They dragged the boat ashore. = 他们把船拖上岸。
I dragged myself out of bed this morning. = 今天早上我费力地起床。

现在完成时:
She has dragged her feet on this project for weeks. = 她这个项目已经拖延了好几个星期了。
He has dragged me into this argument. = 他把我卷入了这场争论。

过去完成时:
They had dragged the evidence from the crime scene. = 他们已经把证据从犯罪现场拖走了。
She had dragged herself to the finish line. = 她勉强坚持到了终点。

将来时:
We will drag the furniture into the new house tomorrow. = 我们明天要把家具搬进新房子。
He will drag his feet until the last minute. = 他会拖到最后一分钟。

虚拟语气:
If I dragged myself to the gym, I would feel better. = 如果我勉强自己去健身房，我会感觉更好。
I wish I hadn't dragged him into this mess. = 我真希望我没有把他卷入这场混乱。

被动语态:
The piano was dragged into the room. = 钢琴被拖进了房间。
The case was dragged out for years. = 这个案子被拖延了好几年。

疑问句:
Are you dragging your feet on purpose? = 你是不是故意拖延？
What are you dragging behind you? = 你拖着什么东西？

否定句:
Don't drag your feet! = 别磨蹭！
He didn't drag the topic any further. = 他没有继续拖延这个话题。
