semislaves:/ˈsɛmiˌsleɪvz/| n.|半奴隶；准奴隶 | A person who is in a state of servitude that is less than full slavery but still involves significant restriction of freedom and exploitation.  This term often refers to historical situations where individuals lacked full legal rights and were subject to harsh working conditions, though not technically considered slaves under the law. | Example:  Many indentured servants were essentially semislaves, bound to their masters for years with little hope of escape.  许多契约仆人基本上是半奴隶，多年来被束缚在主人身边，几乎没有逃脱的希望。| 近义词：indentured servant, bonded laborer, serf | 反义词：freeman, free person
