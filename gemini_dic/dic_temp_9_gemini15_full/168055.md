privatises:/ˈpraɪvətaɪzɪz/ | v. | 私有化 | To transfer (a business, industry, or service) from public or state ownership to private ownership and control.  |The government is planning to privatise the railway system. 政府计划将铁路系统私有化。| nationalise, commercialise | socialise, collectivise | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government privatises several state-owned companies each year. = 政府每年私有化几家国有公司。
He privatises his business to increase efficiency. = 他将他的企业私有化以提高效率。


一般过去时:
The utility company was privatised in the 1980s. = 这家公用事业公司在20世纪80年代被私有化。
They privatised the airline last year. = 他们去年将这家航空公司私有化了。


现在完成时:
Many industries have been privatised since the 1990s. = 自20世纪90年代以来，许多行业已被私有化。
The government has privatised the postal service. = 政府已经将邮政服务私有化了。


过去完成时:
Before the reforms, the company had never been privatised. = 在改革之前，该公司从未被私有化过。
They had already privatised the bank before the announcement. = 他们在宣布之前就已经将银行私有化了。


将来时:
The government will privatise the energy sector next year. = 政府明年将私有化能源部门。
They will likely privatise the water company soon. = 他们很可能会很快将水务公司私有化。


虚拟语气:
If the government privatised the healthcare system, it could lead to higher costs. = 如果政府将医疗体系私有化，可能会导致成本上升。
It would be better if they hadn't privatised the education system. = 如果他们没有将教育体系私有化就好了。


被动语态:
The airport was privatised in 2005. = 机场于2005年被私有化。
The telecoms industry is being privatised gradually. = 电信行业正在逐步私有化。


疑问句:

否定句:
