jinxes:/ˈdʒɪŋksɪz/| n. v.|厄运，坏运气；带来厄运，使倒霉 |A jinx is a person, object, or event that is believed to bring bad luck.  To jinx someone or something is to bring them bad luck, often unintentionally. |例句：She felt she was jinxed after failing three exams in a row. 她连续三次考试不及格后，感觉自己很倒霉。

He jinxed the whole team by mentioning the winning streak. 他提到连胜纪录，给整个球队带来了厄运。|近义词：curse, hex, ill omen |反义词：blessing, good luck charm


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jinxes every project he touches. = 他碰到的每一个项目都会倒霉。
She jinxes her friends unintentionally. = 她无意中给朋友们带来了厄运。


一般过去时:
The broken mirror jinxed their trip. = 那面破碎的镜子给他们带来了旅途中的厄运。
He jinxed the game with his careless comment. = 他因为不经意的评论而给比赛带来了厄运。


现在完成时:
I think I've jinxed myself by bragging about my good fortune. = 我想我因为吹嘘自己的好运而给自己带来了厄运。
She has jinxed every single one of her relationships. = 她毁掉了她所有的感情。


过去完成时:
They had jinxed the deal by rushing into it. = 他们因为操之过急而毁掉了这笔交易。
I had jinxed my chances of winning by doubting myself. = 我因为怀疑自己而毁掉了自己获胜的机会。


将来时:

I'm afraid that talking about it will jinx our plans. = 我担心谈论这件事会打乱我们的计划。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't jinxed it, I would have won the competition. = 如果我没有带来厄运，我就会赢得比赛。
I wish I hadn't jinxed my chances of success. = 我真希望我没有给自己带来厄运。


被动语态:
The team felt jinxed by the opposing team's strong performance. = 该队感觉被对方强大的实力压制了。
The project was jinxed by unforeseen circumstances. = 该项目因不可预见的情况而失败了。


疑问句:

Have you ever felt jinxed before? = 你以前有没有觉得倒霉过？


否定句:
I didn't mean to jinx you. = 我不是故意要给你带来厄运的。
They didn't believe in jinxes. = 他们不相信厄运。
