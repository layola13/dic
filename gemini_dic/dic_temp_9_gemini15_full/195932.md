sensitivities: /ˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪtiz/ | n. |敏感性；过敏反应；顾虑；体贴；细微差别 |  The quality or state of being sensitive;  a feeling of being easily offended or upset;  acute awareness or responsiveness.  It can also refer to a person's awareness of and reaction to the feelings of others, or to the specific things that a person is sensitive to (allergies, for example). |


例句：

He showed great sensitivity to her feelings.  他对她很体贴。

The plant has sensitivities to frost.  这种植物怕霜冻。


近义词：susceptibility, responsiveness, delicacy, consideration, awareness


反义词：insensitivity, callousness, indifference, bluntness


词性：名词

词源:

The word "sensitivities" derives from the adjective "sensitive," which ultimately comes from the Latin word "sentire," meaning "to feel" or "to perceive."  The suffix "-ity" is added to create the noun form, indicating a state or quality.
