know-it-all:/ˈnoʊɪtɔːl/| n. |自以为是的人，什么都懂的人|A know-it-all is someone who acts as if they know everything and are unwilling to listen to others' opinions or admit they don't know something. They often interrupt and correct others, showing off their supposed knowledge.  |例句：He's such a know-it-all; he never listens to anyone else's ideas.|近义词：smart aleck, wise guy, egghead |反义词：humble, unassuming


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He acts like a know-it-all. = 他表现得像个自以为是的人。
She thinks she's a know-it-all. = 她自以为什么都懂。


一般过去时:
He behaved like a know-it-all at the meeting. = 他在会议上表现得像个自以为是的人。
She acted like a know-it-all yesterday. = 昨天她表现得像个自以为是的人。


现在完成时:
He has always been a know-it-all. = 他一直都是个自以为是的人。
She has become a know-it-all since she went to university. = 自从她上大学以来，她就变得自以为是了。


过去完成时:
He had already established himself as a know-it-all before joining the team. = 在加入团队之前，他已经树立了自己自以为是的形象。
She had always acted like a know-it-all, so nobody listened to her. = 她总是表现得像个自以为是的人，所以没有人听她的。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish she weren't such a know-it-all. = 我希望她不要那么自以为是。


被动语态:

She wasn't considered a know-it-all until recently. = 直到最近，她才被认为是自以为是的。


疑问句:

Is she always a know-it-all, or is it just a phase? = 她一直都是个自以为是的人吗，还是这只是个阶段？


否定句:
He is not a know-it-all, he's just very knowledgeable. = 他不是个自以为是的人，他只是非常博学。
She's not really a know-it-all; she just likes to share her knowledge. = 她其实并不是个自以为是的人；她只是喜欢分享她的知识。
