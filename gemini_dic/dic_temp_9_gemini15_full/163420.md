preconstructions: /ˌpriːkənˈstrʌkʃənz/ | n. | 预先构建，预制结构 | Structures or components that are built or assembled beforehand, typically off-site, and then transported and installed at their final location.  They are often used in construction projects to save time and improve efficiency. | Example: The preconstructions for the new bridge were completed in a nearby factory. 新桥的预制构件是在附近工厂完成的。 | 近义词: prefabrications, preassemblies | 反义词: on-site constructions
