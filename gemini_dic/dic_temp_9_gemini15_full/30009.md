cawing:/ˈkɔːɪŋ/| v.|乌鸦叫声；呱呱叫|The sound made by a crow or similar bird.|例句：I woke up to the cawing of crows. 我被乌鸦的叫声吵醒了。|例句：The cawing of the crows echoed through the valley. 乌鸦的叫声在山谷中回荡。|近义词：croaking, caw, squawking|反义词：无


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The crows are cawing loudly. = 乌鸦叫得很响。
He often caws in the morning. = 他经常在早晨呱呱叫。


一般过去时:
The crows were cawing all night. = 乌鸦整夜都在叫。
The raven cawed loudly before flying away. = 那只渡鸦大声叫了一声后飞走了。


现在完成时:
The crows have been cawing since dawn. = 从黎明开始乌鸦就一直在叫。
They have cawed incessantly all day. = 他们整天都在不停地叫。


过去完成时:
The crows had cawed before the sun rose. = 太阳升起之前乌鸦就已经叫了。
The birds had cawed a warning before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，鸟儿们已经呱呱叫着发出警告。


将来时:
The crows will caw at sunrise tomorrow. = 明天日出时乌鸦会叫。
I think the crow will caw again soon. = 我想乌鸦很快还会再叫。


虚拟语气:
If the crows were cawing, I would know they were nearby. = 如果乌鸦在叫，我就知道它们在附近。
I wish the crows wouldn't caw so early in the morning. = 我希望乌鸦不要那么早叫。


被动语态:
(Cawing is rarely used passively.)  乌鸦叫声很少用被动语态。
A warning was given by the cawing of a distant bird.  一只远处的鸟的叫声发出了警告。(被动语态的例子，但焦点在警告，而非叫声本身)


疑问句:
Are the crows cawing? = 乌鸦在叫吗？
Why are they cawing so much? = 为什么它们叫得这么多？


否定句:
The crows weren't cawing this morning. = 今早乌鸦没叫。
The birds didn't caw at all last night. = 昨晚鸟儿们根本没有叫。


PHRASE->
(Cawing doesn't typically form common phrases.  It's primarily used as a verb describing the sound itself.)  呱呱叫通常不构成常用短语，它主要用作动词来描述声音本身。
The shrill cawing of the crows. = 乌鸦尖锐的叫声
A continuous cawing sound. = 连续不断的呱呱叫声
The loud cawing filled the air. = 响亮的叫声充满了空气
The eerie cawing in the night. = 夜晚怪异的呱呱叫声
The harsh cawing startled the children. = 刺耳的呱呱叫声吓到了孩子们
