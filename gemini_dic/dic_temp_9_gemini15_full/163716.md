predefeated:/ˌpriːdɪˈfiːtɪd/| adj.| 预先击败的；注定失败的 | Already defeated before the contest even begins; implying a lack of chance of success.  |例句：His arguments seemed predefeated from the start. 他的论点从一开始就显得站不住脚。  |例句：The team felt predefeated after their disastrous performance in the first game.  在第一场比赛中表现糟糕后，球队感觉注定要失败。|近义词：doomed, preordained to fail|反义词：victorious, assured of success
