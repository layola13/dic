headlinese:/ˈhɛdlaɪnziː/| n. |新闻标题体，耸人听闻的新闻报道风格|A style of writing characterized by short, sensational sentences often used in newspaper headlines.  It aims to grab the reader's attention quickly and concisely, sometimes at the expense of accuracy or nuance.  The style often uses strong verbs and avoids complex grammatical structures.|Example: "DOG BITES MAN" is a simple example of headlinese.  "Local Canine Attacks Resident, Leading to Minor Injuries" is a more detailed, less sensational version. |近义词：journalese, tabloid style|反义词：formal writing, academic writing
