confiding:/kənˈfaɪdɪŋ/| adj.|信任的；坦率的；秘密的|Trusting; frank; secret.  The adjective describes someone who is willing to share personal information or secrets, or a situation marked by trust and intimacy.|例句：She had a confiding nature and easily shared her deepest fears.|近义词：trusting, frank, open, intimate|反义词：secretive, reserved, closed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is confiding in his best friend. = 他在向他最好的朋友倾诉。
She is confiding her secrets to her diary. = 她正在向她的日记倾诉她的秘密。


一般过去时:
They confided their plans to me. = 他们把他们的计划告诉我了。
He confided in his priest about his troubles. = 他向他的牧师倾诉了他的烦恼。


现在完成时:
I have confided in you before. = 我以前向你倾诉过。
She has confided all her worries to her therapist. = 她已经把她所有的担忧都告诉了她的治疗师。


过去完成时:
He had confided in her many times before. = 他以前多次向她倾诉过。
They had confided their deepest secrets to each other. = 他们曾彼此倾诉过他们最深的秘密。


将来时:
I will confide in him about my problems. = 我将向他倾诉我的问题。
She will confide her feelings to her journal. = 她会把她的感受写进日记里。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would confide in someone. = 如果我是你，我会向某人倾诉。
I wish I could confide in my parents. = 我希望我能向我的父母倾诉。


被动语态:
His secrets were confided to a close friend. = 他的秘密被告诉了一个密友。
The information was confided only to a select few. = 该信息只被告知少数几个人。


疑问句:
Can I confide in you? = 我能相信你吗？
Whom did you confide in? = 你向谁倾诉了？


否定句:
She didn't confide in anyone about her fears. = 她没有向任何人倾诉她的恐惧。
He wouldn't confide in his colleagues. = 他不会向他的同事倾诉。
