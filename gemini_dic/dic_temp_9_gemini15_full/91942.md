hypermetropy:/ˌhaɪpəˈmɛtrəpi/| n. |远视；远视眼 |Hypermetropy, also known as farsightedness, is a refractive error of the eye where the eye does not focus light correctly on the retina. This results in blurry vision at near distances. |She was diagnosed with hypermetropy and needs glasses for reading. 她被诊断为远视，需要戴眼镜阅读。|近视(myopia), presbyopia|近视(myopia)
