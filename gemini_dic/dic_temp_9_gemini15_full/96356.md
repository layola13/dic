indirection:/ˌɪndɪˈrɛkʃən/| n. |间接；迂回；暗示 |The use of indirect methods or means;  avoiding direct mention or approach.  It often involves using a suggestion or implication rather than stating something directly. |例句：The poem uses indirection to express the speaker's grief. 这首诗用间接的方式表达了说话者的悲伤。

例句：He preferred indirection to a direct confrontation. 他更喜欢间接的方式而不是直接对抗。


近义词：circumlocution, circumvention, obliqueness, roundaboutness |反义词：directness


词源：from  in- + direction.  The prefix "in-" here means "not" or "opposite of," thus indicating the opposite of a direct approach or method.
