oralist:/ˈɔːrəlɪst/ | n. |口语者，口语教学支持者 | A person who advocates for or practices oralism, a method of teaching deaf people to speak and lip-read, rather than using sign language.  | Example:  Many oralists believe that lip-reading and speech are essential for deaf individuals' integration into society. 许多口语教学支持者认为唇读和说话对于聋哑人融入社会至关重要。 |近义词：speech therapist (in the context of teaching the deaf), proponent of oralism |反义词：manualist (supporter of sign language)
