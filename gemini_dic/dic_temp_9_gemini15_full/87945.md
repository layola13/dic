higgler:/ˈhɪɡlər/| n. |小贩；挑剔的人；讨价还价的人 |A higgler is a person who travels around selling small goods, often food, especially in a small-scale or informal way. They are often associated with peddling and hawking their wares.  The term can also carry a connotation of being fussy or picky, or someone who haggles over prices. |例句：The higgler walked through the market, shouting about his fresh produce.  这个小贩穿过市场，大声叫卖他的新鲜农产品。

The old woman was a tough higgler, never paying full price.  这位老妇人是个精明的讨价还价者，从不付全价。|近义词：peddler, hawker, vendor, retailer |反义词：buyer (in the context of haggling), customer (in the context of being picky)
