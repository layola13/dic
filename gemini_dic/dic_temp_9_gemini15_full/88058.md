highhanded: /ˌhaɪˈhændɪd/ | adj. | 傲慢的；专横的；盛气凌人的 |  Characterized by a domineering or arrogant disregard of others.  It implies a superior attitude and a dismissive treatment of others' feelings or opinions. |

例句：

The manager's highhanded treatment of his employees led to low morale.  = 经理对员工的傲慢态度导致士气低落。

His highhanded dismissal of their concerns was unacceptable. = 他傲慢地驳回了他们的担忧，这是不可接受的。


近义词：autocratic, dictatorial, imperious, arrogant, overbearing, domineering, condescending

反义词：humble, modest, considerate, respectful, deferential


词源：

The word "highhanded" literally refers to a hand held high, often in a gesture of superiority or command.  This figurative sense developed over time to describe behavior characterized by such an attitude.  The first recorded use in this sense dates back to the 16th century.
