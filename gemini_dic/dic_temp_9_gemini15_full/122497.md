mock at: /mɒk æt/ | vt. | 嘲笑；愚弄 | To laugh at someone or something in a way that shows you think they are stupid or ridiculous.  |例句：They mocked at his efforts to learn the language. 他们嘲笑他学习这门语言的努力。


例句：Don't mock at her; she's doing her best.  别嘲笑她；她正在尽力而为。


近义词：ridicule, scoff at, sneer at, jeer at, deride


反义词：praise, commend, applaud


词性: 动词

USAGE->

We mock at his clumsy dance. = 我们嘲笑他笨拙的舞蹈。

词源:
The verb "mock" ultimately derives from the Old French word "mocquer," meaning "to deride, jeer, scoff."  This, in turn, has roots in the Vulgar Latin word "moccare," which is likely of Germanic origin.  The "at" in "mock at" is a preposition indicating the target of the mocking.
