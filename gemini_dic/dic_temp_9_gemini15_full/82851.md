gull:/ɡʌl/| n.|海鸥|A gull is a type of seabird that is often seen near the coast. They are known for their scavenging behavior and their distinctive cry.  |Example:  A flock of gulls circled overhead.  一群海鸥在头顶盘旋。

The gulls scavenged for food on the beach. 海鸥在沙滩上觅食。|近义词：seagull, seabird|反义词：None specifically

词源:
The word "gull" comes from the Old English word *gūl, which was already used to refer to this type of bird.  Its origins are ultimately uncertain, but may be related to words describing gulping or swallowing, referring to the bird's feeding habits.
