hyperphalangism:/ˌhaɪpərˈfælənˌdʒɪzəm/ | n. |多指(趾)症 |A congenital condition characterized by an excessive number of phalanges (fingers or toes).  This condition can affect one or more digits and vary in severity.  It's often associated with other skeletal abnormalities. |Example: The child was diagnosed with hyperphalangism in his left hand, resulting in an extra finger.  孩子被诊断为左手多指症，导致多长了一个手指。|近义词：polydactyly (often used interchangeably, though polydactyly can also refer to extra digits entirely) |反义词：oligodactyly (having too few fingers or toes)
