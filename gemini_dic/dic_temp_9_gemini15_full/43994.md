criminalising: /ˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 刑事犯罪化；定为犯罪 | To make something illegal.  | The government is considering criminalising the use of certain types of pesticides. 政府正在考虑将某些类型的杀虫剂定为非法。 |  illegality, prohibition | legalization, decriminalization | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are criminalising drug use in the city. = 他们在城市中将吸毒定为犯罪。
The government criminalises possession of illegal weapons. = 政府将持有非法武器定为犯罪。


一般过去时:
They criminalised gambling in that state last year. = 他们去年在那个州将赌博定为犯罪。
The country criminalised same-sex marriage decades ago. = 这个国家几十年前将同性婚姻定为犯罪。


现在完成时:
They have criminalised the production of counterfeit goods. = 他们已经将假冒商品的生产定为犯罪。
Many countries have criminalised hate speech online. = 许多国家已经将网络仇恨言论定为犯罪。


过去完成时:
They had criminalised abortion before the new law was passed. = 在新法律通过之前，他们已经将堕胎定为犯罪。

将来时:

The new law will criminalise certain forms of cyberbullying. = 新法律将把某些形式的网络欺凌定为犯罪。


虚拟语气:
If they criminalised hate speech, online abuse would decrease. = 如果他们将仇恨言论定为犯罪，网络辱骂就会减少。

被动语态:

否定句:
