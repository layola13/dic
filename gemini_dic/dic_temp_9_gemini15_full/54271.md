disgruntled:/dɪsˈɡrʌntld/ | adj. | 不高兴的；不满的；愠怒的 | Feeling or showing annoyance or discontent.  | 例句：The disgruntled employee filed a complaint with HR.  不满的员工向人力资源部投诉了。

The disgruntled customers left negative reviews online. 不满的顾客在网上留下了负面评价。


近义词：annoyed, displeased, dissatisfied, unhappy, frustrated, resentful | 反义词：pleased, satisfied, content, happy


词源:  The word "disgruntled" comes from the Middle English word "disgrunt," meaning "to grumble or complain."  The prefix "dis-" adds a negative connotation. "Grumble" itself has roots in Old French and ultimately possibly Germanic origins related to sounds of grumbling.
