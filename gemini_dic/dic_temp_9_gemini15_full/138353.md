notices:/ˈnoʊtɪsɪz/| n.|通知；布告；注意；察觉|Information or a warning that is given to someone.|The company posted notices about the job openings.|公告；启事；通知| announcements, notifications, warnings| indifference, unawareness|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He notices the mistake. = 他注意到错误了。
She notices every detail. = 她注意到每一个细节。


一般过去时:
I noticed a change in his behavior. = 我注意到他的行为改变了。
They noticed the problem immediately. = 他们立刻注意到了问题。


现在完成时:
Have you noticed any improvement? = 你注意到任何改善了吗？
We have noticed a significant increase in sales. = 我们注意到销售额显著增长。


过去完成时:
I had noticed the pattern before. = 我之前就注意到了这种模式。
She had noticed the signs of trouble. = 她已经注意到了麻烦的迹象。


将来时:
I will notice any changes. = 我会注意到任何变化。
They will notice the difference soon. = 他们很快就会注意到区别。


虚拟语气:
If I noticed a problem, I would report it. = 如果我注意到问题，我会报告它。
I wish I had noticed the warning signs earlier. = 我希望我早点注意到警告信号。


被动语态:
The problem was noticed by the manager. = 经理注意到了这个问题。
The changes will be noticed by everyone. = 每个人都会注意到这些变化。


疑问句:
Did you notice anything unusual? = 你注意到什么异常了吗？
Have you noticed the new decorations? = 你注意到新的装饰了吗？


否定句:
I didn't notice anything wrong. = 我没有注意到任何错误。
She didn't notice the car approaching. = 她没有注意到汽车靠近。


PHRASE->

take notice of = 注意
give notice = 通知；发出预告
at short notice = 仓促；紧急通知
on notice = 被警告；处于被解雇的风险中
public notice = 公告
