effectuously: /ɪˈfɛktʃuəli/ | adv. |有效地；结果很好地 |  In a way that is effective; successfully.  It emphasizes the successful outcome or the completeness of the effect. |

例句：

The new marketing campaign was effectuously implemented, leading to a significant increase in sales. = 新的营销活动得到了有效的实施，导致销售额大幅增长。

She dealt with the difficult situation effectuously, calming everyone down and finding a solution. = 她有效地处理了这个棘手的情况，让每个人都平静下来并找到了解决办法。


近义词：effectively, efficiently, successfully, powerfully

反义词：ineffectively, unsuccessfully, fruitlessly


词源：

From "effectual" + "-ly".  "Effectual" comes from the Middle English *effectuel*, from Old French *effectif*, from Latin *effectīvus*, from *effectus* (past participle of *efficere*) meaning "to accomplish, effect."


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't have verb conjugations like "what" in the example.  It modifies verbs and describes how an action is performed.  There is no tense variation for the adverb itself.


PHRASE->

There are no common set phrases specifically using "effectuously". Its usage is generally within a sentence modifying a verb.  Examples of sentences including phrases:

Effectuously handling the crisis:  Successfully managing the difficult situation.
Effectuously solving the problem:  Finding a complete and effective solution.
Effectuously addressing the issue:  Dealing with the problem in a way that produces a positive outcome.
Effectuously implementing the plan:  Successfully putting the plan into action.
Effectuously communicating the message:  Getting the message across clearly and successfully.
