pungency:/ˈpʌndʒənsi/| n. |辛辣；刺激性；尖刻 |The sharp, biting quality of something, especially a taste or smell; also, the quality of being forceful and effective in expression.  It can refer to a strong, sharp sensation, often unpleasant but sometimes pleasantly stimulating. |例句：The pungency of the garlic was almost overwhelming. 大蒜的辛辣味几乎让人难以忍受。

例句：The pungency of her criticism stung him deeply.  她尖锐的批评深深地刺痛了他。


近义词：acridness, sharpness, spiciness, tartness, poignancy (in the sense of emotional impact)


反义词：mildness, blandness


词源：From Middle French *pungence, from Latin pungens, present participle of pungere "to prick, sting".  The word ultimately derives from the Proto-Indo-European root *penk- "to prick, pierce".
