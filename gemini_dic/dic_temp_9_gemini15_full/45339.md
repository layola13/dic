cumulatist: /ˈkjuːmjʊlətɪst/ | n. |累积主义者，积累论者 |A cumulatist is someone who believes in or practices cumulativism, the view that scientific knowledge accumulates over time, building upon previous discoveries and theories.  They emphasize the progressive nature of science and the importance of building on existing knowledge. | Example:  The physicist argued as a cumulatist, emphasizing the continuous progress of scientific understanding.  这位物理学家以累积论者的身份进行论证，强调科学理解的持续进步。|近义词：progressivist (in the context of science)|反义词：epistemological nihilist (someone who believes that true knowledge is impossible), revolutionary (in the context of scientific paradigm shifts) |名词
