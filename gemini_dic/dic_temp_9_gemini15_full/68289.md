falcated:/ˈfælkeɪtɪd/| adj.|镰刀状的；弯刀形的 | Having a curved shape like a sickle or scythe.  The word describes something that is curved and has a sharp, pointed end, similar to the shape of a farmer's sickle. |例句：The falcated moon hung low in the sky.  镰刀形的月亮低垂在天空。

The leaves of the plant were falcated.  这种植物的叶子是镰刀形的。


近义词：sickle-shaped, curved, crescent-shaped | 反义词：straight, linear


词源:  From Latin *falcatus, past participle of falcare "to bend like a sickle," from falx (genitive falcis) "sickle."  The word's ultimate origin lies in Proto-Indo-European *gʷelǵ- "to strike, beat."
