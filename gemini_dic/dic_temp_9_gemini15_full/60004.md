editorializes:/ˌɛdɪˈtɔːriəlaɪzɪz/ | v. |发表社论；表达个人观点；带有评论性地叙述 | To express opinions or bias in a way that resembles an editorial.  The reporter editorialized throughout his article, clearly favoring one side of the issue. |近义词：comment, opine, expound |反义词：report objectively, remain neutral


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He editorializes frequently in his columns. = 他经常在他的专栏里发表评论。
The newspaper editorializes against the proposed tax increase. = 这家报纸发表社论反对提议的税收增加。


一般过去时:
The commentator editorialized heavily during the debate. = 评论员在辩论中发表了大量的评论。
She editorialized in her speech, voicing her strong disapproval. = 她在演讲中表达了自己的强烈反对意见。


现在完成时:
The blogger has editorialized extensively on the subject of climate change. = 该博主已就气候变化问题发表了大量的评论。
He has editorialized throughout his book, expressing personal views. = 他在书中表达了自己的个人观点。


过去完成时:
The author had editorialized subtly throughout the earlier chapters. = 作者在前面的章节中含蓄地表达了自己的观点。
The journalist had editorialized on the topic before, expressing similar sentiments. = 这名记者之前就此事发表过评论，表达了类似的观点。


将来时:
The professor will editorialize extensively in his upcoming lecture. = 教授将在即将到来的演讲中发表大量的评论。
She will editorialize in her article, giving her perspective on the issue. = 她将在她的文章中发表评论，阐述她对这个问题的看法。


虚拟语气:
If he editorialized less, his reports would be more objective. = 如果他少发表一些评论，他的报道就会更客观。
I wish the news anchor wouldn't editorialize so much during the broadcast. = 我希望新闻主播在直播时不要发表太多评论。


被动语态:
The article was editorialized to favor a particular candidate. = 这篇文章带有倾向性，是为了偏袒某个候选人。
His opinions were editorialized in a way that misrepresented the facts. = 他的观点被带有评论性地表达出来，歪曲了事实。


疑问句:
Does the writer editorialize too much in this piece? = 作者在这篇文章中是否评论过多？
Does the report editorialize or present the facts neutrally? = 这篇报道是发表评论还是客观地呈现事实？


否定句:
The news reporter did not editorialize in her coverage. = 新闻记者在报道中没有发表评论。
The textbook does not editorialize, presenting information objectively. = 这本教科书没有发表评论，而是客观地呈现信息。
