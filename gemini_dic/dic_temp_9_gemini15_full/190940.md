scalability:/ˌskeɪləˈbɪləti/ | n. |可扩展性，可伸缩性 |The ability of a system, network, or process to handle a growing amount of work, or its potential to be enlarged to accommodate that growth.  It refers to how well a system can adapt to increasing demands without significant performance degradation.  This can involve adding more resources (hardware, software, personnel) or optimizing existing resources to handle the increased load. |例句：The scalability of the cloud platform allows us to easily handle increasing numbers of users. 云平台的可扩展性使我们能够轻松处理越来越多的用户。

The database's scalability was a major factor in our decision to adopt it. 数据库的可扩展性是我们决定采用它的一个主要因素。


近义词：extensibility, expandability, adaptability |反义词：inflexibility, rigidity
