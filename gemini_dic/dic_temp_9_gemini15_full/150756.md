/ˌpærəˈdæktɪləm/| n. |并指（畸形）|Paradactyly refers to a congenital anomaly characterized by the fusion of two or more digits, most commonly affecting the fingers or toes.  It can vary in severity, from a slight webbing to a complete fusion of the digits.  The cause is often unknown, but genetic factors are suspected to play a role. |The child was born with paradactyly of the third and fourth fingers.  孩子天生第三和第四根手指并指。|syndactyly, webbed fingers/toes|normal digits, separate fingers/toes|名词
