flip-flapping:/flɪpˈflæpɪŋ/| adj.| 扑腾的；飘动的；反复无常的 |Describes something moving rapidly up and down or back and forth in a light, irregular way, often suggesting instability or nervousness.  It can also refer to something changing erratically. |例句：The bird's flip-flapping wings beat against the wind.  这只鸟扑腾的翅膀拍打着逆风。

The flag was flip-flapping wildly in the strong breeze. 旗子在强风中剧烈地飘动。


近义词：fluttering, flapping, wavering, erratic

反义词：steady, stable, still


词性:形容词

词源:
The word is a combination of "flip" (a quick, light movement) and "flapping" (a repeated movement of something flat).  Both words have Germanic roots.
