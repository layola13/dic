heterodactylous: /ˌhɛtərəˈdæktələs/ | adj. | 异趾的，对趾的 | Having toes arranged in an opposing manner, such as two toes pointing forward and two backward.  This arrangement is common in birds of prey and some other animals, providing a better grip. | Example:  The owl's heterodactylous feet allow it to grasp its prey firmly.  = 猫头鹰的异趾足使其能够牢牢抓住猎物。 | 近义词：zygodactyl (in reference to specific arrangements) | 反义词：homodactylous
