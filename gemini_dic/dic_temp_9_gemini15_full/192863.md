/ˈsiːsɪkɪst/| adj. |最晕船的；最严重的晕船|Most severely affected by seasickness.  It describes the superlative degree of seasickness.|例句：After a rough crossing, she was the seasickest passenger on the ferry.  渡海后，她是渡轮上最晕船的乘客。
例句：The seasickest people were those who had eaten a big meal before the voyage.  航行前吃大餐的人是最晕船的。|近义词：most nauseous, most queasy|反义词：unaffected by seasickness, perfectly well


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is the seasickest person I know. = 他是我认识的最晕船的人。
The seasickest passengers are often those who are sensitive to motion. = 最容易晕船的乘客通常是对动作敏感的人。

一般过去时:
She was the seasickest she had ever been. = 她经历了有史以来最严重的晕船。
They were the seasickest during the storm. = 暴风雨期间他们晕船最厉害。

现在完成时:
He has been the seasickest person on every cruise he's been on. = 他每次乘船游览都是最晕船的人。
I have never felt so seasickest. = 我从未感到如此晕船。


过去完成时:
By the time we reached the shore, she had been the seasickest she'd ever been in her life. = 等我们到达岸边时，她经历了她一生中最严重的晕船。
Before the storm hit, they hadn't been the seasickest. = 在暴风雨来临之前，他们并没有很晕船。


将来时:
He will be the seasickest person on the boat if the sea is rough. = 如果海况恶劣，他将会是船上最晕船的人。
I think I will be the seasickest person on this journey. = 我想我会是这次旅行中最晕船的人。


虚拟语气:
If I were the seasickest person, I would stay in bed. = 如果我是最晕船的人，我会待在床上。
I wish I weren't the seasickest person on this boat. = 我希望我不是船上最晕船的人。


被动语态:  This word, as a superlative adjective, is not typically used in the passive voice.


疑问句:
Who was the seasickest person on the trip? = 这趟旅行中最晕船的人是谁？
Is he the seasickest person you know? = 他是你认识的最晕船的人吗？


否定句:
She wasn't the seasickest person on the boat. = 她不是船上最晕船的人。
They weren't the seasickest; several others felt worse. = 他们并不是最晕船的；还有几个人感觉更糟。



PHRASE->
There are not common idiomatic phrases using "seasickest".  The word itself functions as a descriptive adjective.  Phrases would be constructed around it, such as:


the seasickest passenger
the seasickest voyage
the seasickest feeling
feeling seasickest
become seasickest


词源:
seasick (adj.) + -est (superlative suffix)

seasick itself comes from sea (n.) + sick (adj.)
