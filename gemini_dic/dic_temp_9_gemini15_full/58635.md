dumbed-down: /ˌdʌmdˈdaʊn/ | adj. | 简化的；通俗化的；降低智力水平的 |  Simplified; made easier to understand, often to the point of being overly simplistic or lacking in intellectual depth.  It implies a reduction in complexity or sophistication to make something more accessible to a wider audience, potentially at the expense of accuracy or thoroughness. |

例句：
The news report was dumbed-down for a wider audience. = 新闻报道为了更广泛的受众而进行了简化。

The textbook was so dumbed-down that it lacked any real intellectual challenge. = 这本教科书过于简单化，缺乏任何真正的智力挑战。


近义词：simplified, simplified, popularized, watered-down, vulgarized

反义词：intellectualized, sophisticated, complex, challenging


词源：The term is a relatively recent coinage, likely arising from the combination of "dumb" (meaning stupid or unintelligent) and "down" (meaning to reduce or lower). The implication is that something has been made less intellectually demanding.
