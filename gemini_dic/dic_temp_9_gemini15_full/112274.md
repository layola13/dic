lophosteon:/ˈlɒfoʊstiːɒn/| n. |胸椎骨|A lophosteon is a type of vertebra (bone of the spine) characterized by a prominent neural spine that is elongated and often bifurcated (split into two branches). It's commonly found in certain extinct fishes and other aquatic vertebrates.  The term combines "lophos" (crest or ridge) and "osteon" (bone).|The fossil record reveals several species with distinctive lophosteons, allowing paleontologists to classify and study their evolutionary relationships.|近义词: vertebra (general term)|反义词:None|名词
