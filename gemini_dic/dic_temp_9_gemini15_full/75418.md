frugalness:/ˈfruːɡəlˌnəs/ | n. |节俭；俭朴 | The quality of being frugal; economy in use or expenditure; thrift.  It emphasizes a careful and economical approach to resource management, often implying wisdom and avoidance of waste. |例句：Her frugalness allowed her to save enough money for a down payment on a house. = 她的节俭使她攒够了足够的钱付首付买房子。

例句：His frugalness extended to all aspects of his life, from his clothing to his food. = 他的节俭贯穿了他生活的方方面面，从衣着到饮食。


近义词：thrift, economy, spareness, saving, parsimony


反义词：extravagance, profligacy, wastefulness, lavishness


词源：源自于中古英语 *frugal,  最终源自于拉丁语 *frugalis “bearing fruit, fruitful, productive; economical,”  来自 *frux, frugis “fruit.”  The sense shifted from "productive" to "economical" because being productive means efficiently using resources.
