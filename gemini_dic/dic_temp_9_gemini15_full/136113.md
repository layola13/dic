nonreducibility:/ˌnɒnˌrɪdjuːsəˈbɪləti/ | n. |不可约性，不可简化性 |The inherent inability of something to be reduced or simplified further.  It often refers to a concept, system, or problem that cannot be broken down into simpler components without losing essential meaning or functionality.  |Example: The nonreducibility of consciousness to purely physical processes is a major topic in philosophy of mind.  意识不能简化为纯粹的物理过程，这是心智哲学中的一个主要课题。|近义词：irreducibility, unreducibility |反义词：reducibility
