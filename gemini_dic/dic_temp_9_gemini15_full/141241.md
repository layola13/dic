op-artists:/ˈɑːp ˈɑːrtɪsts/| n. |欧普艺术家|Optical artists, also known as Op artists, are artists who create works of art that exploit the viewer's visual perception through optical illusions.  Their art often features bold geometric patterns, contrasting colors, and other visual tricks to create a sense of movement, depth, or distortion.  The term "Op Art" is a shortened form of "optical art".|The Op artists of the 1960s, such as Victor Vasarely and Bridget Riley, revolutionized the way people perceived art.  Their paintings often appear to vibrate or shift before the viewer's eyes.|近义词：optical artists|反义词：无
