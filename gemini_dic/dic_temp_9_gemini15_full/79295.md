glaze-ice:/ɡleɪz aɪs/| n.|雨凇，冰凌|A glaze of ice is a coating of ice formed on surfaces, typically by freezing rain or drizzle.  It's different from frost, which forms from water vapor. Glaze ice is usually transparent and smooth, often making surfaces appear wet even though they are covered in ice.|例句：The roads were treacherous due to a thick glaze of ice. 道路因厚厚的雨凇而变得危险。 The trees were beautifully coated with a glaze of ice. 树木上覆盖着一层美丽的冰凌。|近义词：ice coating, freezing rain, glazed frost|反义词：frost (in the sense of a different ice formation), dry ice|名词
