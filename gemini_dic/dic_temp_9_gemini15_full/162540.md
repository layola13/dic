preallegations: /ˌpriːælɪˈɡeɪʃənz/ | n. | 预先指控；未经证实的指控 |  Claims or accusations made before formal charges are filed.  These are often preliminary statements or reports that suggest wrongdoing but lack sufficient evidence for a full-fledged legal case.  They serve as a basis for further investigation. | The preallegations of fraud led to a thorough audit of the company's finances.  预先的欺诈指控导致了对公司财务的彻底审计。|  allegations, accusations, claims |  exonerations, refutations | 名词
