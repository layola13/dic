outlier:/ˈaʊtˌlaɪər/ | n. | 异常值；离群值；例外 | An outlier is a data point that significantly differs from other observations in a dataset.  It lies an abnormal distance from other values in a random sample from a population. Outliers can be caused by measurement error, data entry errors, or simply by the presence of unusual data points in the population.  |  例句：The unusually high temperature reading was an outlier.  这个异常高的温度读数是一个异常值。


例句：The extremely wealthy individual in our sample is considered an outlier.  在我们样本中的极度富有的人被认为是一个异常值。


近义词：aberration, anomaly, exception, deviation


反义词：norm, average, typical, mean
