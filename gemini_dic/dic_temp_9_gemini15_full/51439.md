detesting:/dɪˈtɛstɪŋ/| v. |厌恶；憎恨；极不喜欢|The act of feeling extreme dislike or disgust towards something or someone.  It implies a strong and often intense feeling of aversion.|例句：She detested the smell of onions. 她讨厌洋葱的味道。

例句：I detested his arrogant behavior. 我讨厌他傲慢的行为。


近义词：abhorring, loathing, hating, despising|反义词：loving, adoring, cherishing|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He detests public speaking. = 他讨厌公开演讲。
She detests the cold weather. = 她讨厌寒冷的天气。

一般过去时:
They detested the movie. = 他们讨厌那部电影。
I detested his lies. = 我讨厌他的谎言。

现在完成时:
I have detested that song since I was a child. = 从小我就讨厌那首歌。
She has detested him for years. = 她多年来一直讨厌他。

过去完成时:
He had detested her since their argument. = 自从他们争吵后，他就一直讨厌她。
We had detested the food before we even tasted it. = 我们甚至还没尝就讨厌那食物了。

将来时:
I will detest this job if I have to do it for long. = 如果我不得不长期做这份工作，我会讨厌它的。
She will detest her new neighbor if they keep making noise. = 如果邻居继续吵闹，她会讨厌她的新邻居的。

虚拟语气:
If I knew he was like that, I would have detested him from the start. = 如果我知道他是那样的人，我一开始就会讨厌他。
I wish I hadn't detested him so much; he wasn't so bad. = 我真希望我没有那么讨厌他；他并没有那么坏。

被动语态:
His behavior was detested by everyone. = 他的行为遭到所有人的厌恶。
The new policy is detested by many employees. = 许多员工讨厌这项新政策。

疑问句:
Do you detest anything in particular? = 你特别讨厌什么吗？
Does he detest cats? = 他讨厌猫吗？

否定句:
I don't detest classical music. = 我不讨厌古典音乐。
She doesn't detest spicy food. = 她不讨厌辛辣的食物。


PHRASE->
detest doing something: 讨厌做某事
detest the thought of: 讨厌想到...
detest to admit: 不得不承认
detest the idea of: 讨厌...的想法
detest with a passion:  极度厌恶
