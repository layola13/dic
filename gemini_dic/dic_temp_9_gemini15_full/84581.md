hammocks:/ˈhæməks/ | n. |吊床 |A hammock is a sling made of fabric, rope, or netting, suspended between two points, used as a bed or resting place.  |例句：We relaxed in the hammocks under the shade of the palm trees. 我们在棕榈树荫下的吊床上放松身心。

例句：The gentle breeze swayed the hammock back and forth. 轻柔的微风使吊床来回摇摆。


近义词：swing bed, hanging bed |反义词：N/A


词性：名词


词源:  The word "hammock" ultimately derives from the Taino word *hamaka*, meaning "fish net." The Taino were the indigenous people of the Caribbean islands, and their use of nets for various purposes, including sleeping, is reflected in the word's etymology.  The word entered English via Spanish.
