pairing:/ˈpɛərɪŋ/| n.|配对；组合；配对物|The act of pairing two or more things together; a set of two things that are paired together.  It can refer to people, objects, or ideas that are connected or associated. |例句：The pairing of red wine with cheese is a classic combination. 红酒配奶酪是经典的搭配。

例句：The successful pairing of the two actors led to a box office hit. 这两位演员的成功配对导致了票房大卖。


近义词：coupling, combination, match, duo, team|反义词：separation, division, disjunction|词性：名词


词源：源自古英语 *par, “pair”，意思是“一对”，来自原始日耳曼语 *pari, “pair”。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The chef is pairing the fish with a lemon sauce. = 厨师将鱼与柠檬酱搭配。
This wine pairs well with spicy food. = 这款酒与辛辣食物很相配。


一般过去时:
They paired the students for the project. = 他们将学生们分成小组进行项目。
She paired her red shoes with a black dress. = 她将红色的鞋子与黑色的裙子搭配。


现在完成时:
We have paired all the socks. = 我们已经把所有的袜子都配对了。
The company has paired its new product with a strong marketing campaign. = 公司已将其新产品与强有力的营销活动结合起来。


过去完成时:
They had paired off before the dance began. = 舞蹈开始之前，他们就已经两两配对好了。
She had paired the ingredients carefully before starting to cook. = 在开始烹饪之前，她已经仔细地将配料搭配好了。


将来时:
They will pair the winning team with a strong competitor. = 他们将把获胜的队伍与一个强大的竞争对手配对。
I will pair my new jeans with a simple white shirt. = 我将我的新牛仔裤与一件简单的白色衬衫搭配。


虚拟语气:
If I paired the colors differently, the result might be better. = 如果我将颜色搭配得不一样，结果可能会更好。
I wish I had paired the wine with a different dish. = 我真希望我把酒和不同的菜搭配。


被动语态:
The contestants were paired randomly. = 参赛者被随机配对。
The images are paired according to their themes. = 图片根据主题配对。


疑问句:
How many people are paired in this competition? = 这场比赛有多少人配对？
Which colors should be paired together? = 哪些颜色应该搭配在一起？


否定句:
The colors don't pair well together. = 这些颜色搭配不好。
He didn't pair the right tools for the job. = 他没有为这项工作选择合适的工具。
