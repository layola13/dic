ability: /əˈbɪləti/ | n. |能力，才能| The power or skill to do something.  It refers to a person's capacity to perform a task or function successfully. |例句：He has the ability to learn quickly. 他有快速学习的能力。|近义词：capacity, capability, talent, aptitude |反义词：inability, incapacity


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has excellent communication abilities. = 他有极好的沟通能力。
She shows great leadership ability. = 她展现出极强的领导能力。

一般过去时:
He demonstrated remarkable problem-solving ability last year. = 他去年展现了非凡的解决问题的能力。
She lacked the necessary skills and abilities. = 她缺乏必要的技能和能力。

现在完成时:
She has developed her musical abilities over many years. = 她多年来培养了自己的音乐才能。
He has honed his writing ability since he started his blog. = 自从他开始写博客以来，他的写作能力得到了磨练。

过去完成时:
By the time he graduated, he had acquired many valuable abilities. = 到他毕业时，他已经获得了许多宝贵的技能。
She had demonstrated her leadership ability in previous roles. = 她之前在担任的其他角色中已经展现了她的领导能力。

将来时:
He will need strong analytical abilities for this job. = 这份工作需要他具备很强的分析能力。
She will develop more abilities with more experience. = 她会有更多经验来发展更多能力。

虚拟语气:
If he had the ability, he would have helped us. = 如果他有能力，他会帮助我们的。
I wish I had the ability to speak fluent Spanish. = 我希望我有说流利西班牙语的能力。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
