dividend:/ˈdɪvɪdend/| n. |股息，红利，被除数|A dividend is a sum of money paid regularly (typically quarterly) by a company to its shareholders out of its profits (or reserves).  It represents a share of the company's earnings distributed to investors. |Example: The company announced a generous dividend payout to its shareholders.  这家公司宣布向股东支付丰厚的股息。

The company declared a dividend of $1 per share. 公司宣布每股派息1美元。|近义词：profit share, distribution, payout |反义词：loss, deficit


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company pays a dividend every quarter. = 公司每季度支付股息。
Investors receive dividends on their shares. = 投资者从他们的股票中获得股息。


一般过去时:
The company paid a large dividend last year. = 公司去年支付了大笔股息。
They received a dividend check in the mail. = 他们收到了一张邮寄的股息支票。


现在完成时:
The company has paid out significant dividends this year. = 公司今年已支付了大量的股息。
She has received her dividend payments regularly. = 她定期收到股息支付。


过去完成时:
Before the merger, the company had paid consistent dividends for decades. = 在合并之前，该公司几十年来一直支付稳定的股息。
They had already received their dividends for the quarter. = 他们已经收到了本季度的股息。


将来时:
The board will decide on the dividend payment next month. = 董事会将在下个月决定股息支付。
Investors expect a higher dividend next year. = 投资者预计明年股息会更高。


虚拟语气:
If the company were more profitable, it would pay a larger dividend. = 如果公司更有利可图，它将支付更高的股息。
I wish the dividend payout were higher. = 我希望股息支付更高。


被动语态:
Dividends are paid out to shareholders. = 股息支付给股东。
The dividend was declared last week. = 股息在上周宣布。


疑问句:
What is the dividend yield on this stock? = 这只股票的股息收益率是多少？
When will the dividend be paid? = 股息何时支付？


否定句:
The company did not pay a dividend this quarter. = 公司本季度没有支付股息。
They haven't received their dividend yet. = 他们还没有收到他们的股息。


PHRASE->

dividend yield: 股息收益率
dividend payout: 股息支付
dividend policy: 股息政策
cash dividend: 现金股息
stock dividend: 股票股息


词源:

The word "dividend" ultimately derives from the Latin verb "dividēre," meaning "to divide" or "to separate."  It entered English through the Old French "dividende."  The sense of sharing profits among shareholders developed over time.
