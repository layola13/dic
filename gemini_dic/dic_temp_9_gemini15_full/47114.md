daunt:/dɔːnt/| vt.|使气馁；使畏缩；使胆怯|to discourage or intimidate (someone), causing them to lose confidence or enthusiasm.|The difficult task did not daunt her.  艰巨的任务并没有使她气馁。|The high mountain daunted the inexperienced climbers.  高山使那些缺乏经验的登山者望而却步。|discourage, intimidate, dismay|encourage, embolden|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The upcoming exam daunts many students. =  即将到来的考试使许多学生感到害怕。
This challenge does not daunt him. = 这个挑战并没有使他气馁。


一般过去时:
The news daunted her plans. = 这个消息使她的计划泡汤了。
The storm daunted the sailors. = 风暴使水手们胆怯。


现在完成时:
The harsh criticism has daunted his confidence. = 严厉的批评打击了他的信心。
Nothing has daunted her spirit. = 没有什么能够吓倒她。


过去完成时:
The previous failure had daunted them from trying again. = 之前的失败使他们不敢再次尝试。
She had been daunted by the sheer scale of the project. = 她被这个项目的巨大规模吓倒了。


将来时:
The upcoming competition will daunt some athletes. = 即将到来的比赛会使一些运动员感到害怕。
I don't think anything will daunt him. = 我认为没有什么能吓倒他。


虚拟语气:
If the task weren't so daunting, I would have tried. = 如果任务没有那么艰巨，我会尝试的。
I wish the difficulty wouldn't daunt me so much. = 我希望困难不会让我如此害怕。


被动语态:
He was daunted by the prospect of public speaking. = 他害怕公开演讲。
The team was daunted by the unexpected setback. = 团队被意外的挫折吓倒了。


疑问句:
Does the thought of failure daunt you? = 失败的想法会让你害怕吗？
What daunts you the most in this project? = 在这个项目中，什么最让你害怕？


否定句:
The difficulties did not daunt her. = 困难并没有使她气馁。
He was not daunted by the challenges. = 他没有被挑战吓倒。


PHRASE->
daunting task = 艰巨的任务
daunting challenge = 令人畏惧的挑战
daunting prospect = 令人气馁的前景
feel daunted = 感到气馁
be daunted by = 被…吓倒
