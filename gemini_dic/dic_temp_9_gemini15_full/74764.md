freeze:/friːz/| /friːz/| v. n. |冻结；凝固；僵硬；使停止|To change from a liquid to a solid state due to cold; to become immobile or inactive.|The water froze overnight.  水一夜之间结冰了。|The fear froze her to the spot.  恐惧使她僵在当场。|近义词：congeal, solidify, harden|反义词：melt, thaw


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The river freezes every winter. = 这条河每年冬天都会结冰。
He freezes his vegetables for later use. = 他把蔬菜冷冻起来以备后用。


一般过去时:
The lake froze over last night. = 湖昨晚结冰了。
The engine froze because of the cold weather. = 由于天气寒冷，发动机冻住了。


现在完成时:
It has frozen solid. = 它已经冻结成固体了。
The pipes have frozen again. = 水管又冻住了。


过去完成时:
The ground had frozen before the snow fell. = 下雪前地面就已经冻住了。
The food had frozen solid in the freezer. = 食物在冰箱里已经冻得硬邦邦的了。


将来时:
The water will freeze if the temperature drops below zero. = 如果气温降到零度以下，水就会结冰。
They will freeze the assets of the company. = 他们将冻结该公司的资产。


虚拟语气:
If the temperature dropped below zero, the water would freeze. = 如果气温降到零度以下，水就会结冰。
I wish my bank account hadn't frozen. = 我真希望我的银行账户没有被冻结。


被动语态:
The meat is frozen for preservation. = 肉被冷冻起来以防腐。
The project has been frozen indefinitely. = 项目已被无限期搁置。


疑问句:
Does water freeze at 0 degrees Celsius? = 水在0摄氏度结冰吗？
Has the account been frozen? = 账户被冻结了吗？


否定句:
The water didn't freeze last night. = 水昨晚没有结冰。
The government won't freeze wages. = 政府不会冻结工资。


PHRASE->
freeze out: 排挤；冷落
freeze up: 冻结；卡住；紧张
freeze over: 全部结冰
freeze to death: 冻死
deep freeze: 深冷冻


词源:
From Old English *frēosan, from Proto-Germanic *frēusan, from Proto-Indo-European *(g)hrew- "to freeze".  Cognates include German frieren and Dutch vriezen.
