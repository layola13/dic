reshuffle:/ˌriːˈʃʌfəl/| vt. n.|改组；改换；洗牌|To reorganize something, especially a group of people.  It often implies a minor change or rearrangement rather than a complete overhaul.|例句：The cabinet was reshuffled after the election.  选举后内阁进行了改组。|例句：The teacher reshuffled the desks to create more space.老师重新安排课桌以腾出更多空间。|近义词：reorganize, rearrange, reorganise|反义词：maintain, keep the same


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company reshuffles its management team every year. = 公司每年都会改组管理团队。
He reshuffles the deck of cards before each game. = 他每次游戏前都会洗牌。

一般过去时:
They reshuffled the schedule after the storm. = 暴风雨过后他们重新安排了日程。
The government reshuffled its cabinet last month. = 政府上个月改组了内阁。

现在完成时:
The team has reshuffled its lineup several times this season. = 这个赛季球队已经几次调整了阵容。
She has reshuffled her priorities since having a baby. =自从有了孩子，她已经重新安排了她的优先级。

过去完成时:
They had reshuffled the furniture before the party. = 他们聚会前已经重新安排了家具。
He had reshuffled his investments before the market crash. = 在市场崩盘前他已经重新调整了他的投资。

将来时:
The company will reshuffle its marketing strategy next quarter. = 公司下个季度将调整其营销策略。
The coach will reshuffle the team's positions for the next match. = 教练将在下一场比赛中调整球队的阵容。

虚拟语气:
If the manager reshuffled the team, we might win. = 如果经理调整球队，我们可能会赢。
I wish they had reshuffled the seating arrangement. = 我希望他们能重新安排座位。

被动语态:

The schedule was reshuffled due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，日程被重新安排。

疑问句:

否定句:
