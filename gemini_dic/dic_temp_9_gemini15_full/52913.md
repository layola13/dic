dimorphous:/daɪˈmɔːrfəs/ | adj. | 双形的；二形的 | Existing or occurring in two distinct forms.  This term is often used in biology and crystallography to describe substances or organisms that exist in two different forms.  For example, a dimorphic fungus might exist as both a yeast and a mold.  A dimorphic crystal might have two different crystal structures. | Example: The fungus exhibits dimorphic growth, existing as both yeast and hyphae.  真菌表现出双形生长，既以酵母形式存在，也以菌丝形式存在。


Example:  The mineral is dimorphous, crystallizing in two distinct systems.  这种矿物是双形的，结晶成两种不同的体系。

近义词：biformal, dual-formed

反义词：monomorphic
