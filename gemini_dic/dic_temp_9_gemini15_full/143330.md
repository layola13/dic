outed:/ˈaʊtɪd/| v.|公开的；揭露；驱逐；|to reveal someone's secret, especially their sexual orientation; to force someone to leave a place or group|例句：He was outed as a gay man. 他被公开出柜了。|例句：The protesters were outed from the building. 抗议者被赶出了大楼。|近义词：revealed, exposed, expelled, evicted|反义词：concealed, hidden, protected


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They out him as a spy. = 他们揭露他是一个间谍。
She outs her colleagues' secrets. = 她揭露同事们的秘密。


一般过去时:
The newspaper outed the politician's affair. = 报纸揭露了这位政治家的婚外情。
He was outed by his own family. = 他被自己的家人揭露了。


现在完成时:
The company has outed several fraudulent employees. = 公司已经揭露了几名欺诈员工。
She has been outed as a whistleblower. = 她已被揭露为举报人。


过去完成时:
The truth had been outed before the trial began. = 在审判开始之前真相就已经被揭露了。
They had outed the thief before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，他们已经揭露了小偷。


将来时:
The media will out the scandal soon. = 媒体很快就会揭露这个丑闻。
I think they will out him eventually. = 我认为他们最终会揭露他。


虚拟语气:
If he were outed, his career would be ruined. = 如果他被揭露，他的事业就会毁了。
I wish I hadn't outed him. = 我真希望我没有揭露他。


被动语态:
He was outed in the press. = 他在媒体上被揭露了。
The secret was outed by an anonymous source. = 这个秘密被一个匿名消息来源揭露了。


疑问句:
Was he ever outed? = 他曾经被揭露过吗？
Has she been outed yet? = 她已经被揭露了吗？


否定句:
They didn't out him, despite knowing the truth. = 尽管知道真相，他们并没有揭露他。
The company tried to keep the scandal from being outed. = 公司试图阻止丑闻被揭露。


PHRASE->
be outed as = 被揭露为…
outed by = 被…揭露
outed to = 向…揭露
outed from = 被赶出…
came out (in the context of being outed) =公开出柜 (指性取向被公开)
