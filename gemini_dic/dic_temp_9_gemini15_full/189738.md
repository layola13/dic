saltigrade:/ˈsɔːltɪɡreɪd/| n. |盐分梯度；耐盐生物|A saltigrade is an organism that can tolerate or thrive in environments with high salt concentrations.  Saltigrades are often found in hypersaline lakes, salt marshes, and other salty habitats. They have developed various physiological and biochemical adaptations to survive in these challenging conditions.|Example:  Many extremophiles, including certain types of bacteria and archaea, are saltigrades.|近义词：halophile (嗜盐生物)|反义词：None specifically, but organisms that cannot tolerate high salinity could be considered the opposite.|名词
