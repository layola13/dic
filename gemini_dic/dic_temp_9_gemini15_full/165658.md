premodified:/ˌpriːˈmɒdɪfaɪd/|/ˌpriːˈmɑːdɪfaɪd/| adj. | 前置修饰的 |  Describes a word, phrase, or clause that precedes and modifies another element in a sentence, typically a noun.  It's used to describe the grammatical structure where the modifier comes before the word it modifies.  | Example:  The *bright red* car sped down the street. (Here, "bright red" is a premodified phrase modifying "car"). |近义词:  antecedent, preceding, foregoing |反义词: postmodified
