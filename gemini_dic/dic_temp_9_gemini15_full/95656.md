WORD ORIGIN->
The word "incomprehensibility" is derived from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not"), the verb "comprehend" (meaning "to understand"), and the suffix "-ibility" (meaning "the quality or state of being").  Therefore, it literally means "the state of not being understandable."  The root word "comprehend" traces back to the Latin "comprehendere," composed of "com-" (together) and "prehendere" (to seize, grasp).


USAGE->
This word is primarily used as a noun and describes the quality or state of something being difficult or impossible to understand.  It is typically used in formal contexts. There are no verb conjugations or tenses associated with "incomprehensibility" as it is a noun.
