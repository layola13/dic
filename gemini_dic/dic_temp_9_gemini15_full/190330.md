sarcoma:/sɑːrˈkoʊmə/|/ˈsɑːkəʊmə/| n. |肉瘤|A malignant tumor of connective tissue origin. Sarcomas are less common than carcinomas (cancers of epithelial origin), but they can be very aggressive and difficult to treat.  They develop from the mesenchymal cells, which are the cells that form the body's connective tissues, including bone, cartilage, fat, muscle and blood vessels.  |例句：The doctor confirmed the diagnosis of a sarcoma in his leg. 医生证实了他的腿部患有肉瘤。

The patient is undergoing chemotherapy for a rare type of soft tissue sarcoma.  这位病人正在接受化疗以治疗一种罕见的软组织肉瘤。|近义词：cancer, tumor, neoplasm|反义词：benign tumor
