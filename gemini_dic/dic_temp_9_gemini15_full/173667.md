quasi-legal:/ˈkwɑːziˈliːɡəl/ | adj. |准法律的；似是而非的法律的 | Seemingly or partially legal; resembling or having the nature of law but not fully conforming to its requirements.  It often describes actions, procedures, or systems that operate outside the strict confines of formal legal processes but nevertheless have some legal force or effect.  | Example: The company's internal dispute resolution process was considered quasi-legal, as it lacked the formal structure of a court but still bound employees to its decisions.  The arbitrator's ruling, while not legally binding, had a quasi-legal effect on the negotiations. |近义词：semi-legal, extralegal, informal, unofficial | 反义词：legal, formal, official
