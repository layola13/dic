put-through:/pʊt θruː/| vt.|使接通；使通过；完成；使经历|To connect (someone) by telephone. To cause (something) to pass through or across something. To complete (a task or process). To cause (someone) to undergo or experience something.|例句：The operator put me through to the manager. 接线员帮我接通了经理。|例句：The bill was put through parliament. 这项法案已通过议会。|近义词：connect, complete, accomplish, undergo|反义词：disconnect, interrupt, halt


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The operator puts me through to the right department. = 接线员把我转接到正确的部门。
They put the new policy through the board. = 他们将新政策提交董事会审核。


一般过去时:
She put the call through immediately. = 她立即接通了电话。
He put his plan through despite the opposition. = 尽管遭到反对，他还是完成了他的计划。


现在完成时:
We have put the project through successfully. = 我们已经成功完成了项目。
They have put many obstacles through their journey. = 在他们的旅途中，他们克服了许多障碍。


过去完成时:
She had put the proposal through before the deadline. = 她在截止日期前完成了提案。
They had put the children through a lot of hardship. = 他们让孩子们经历了很多苦难。


将来时:
I will put you through to his extension. = 我将帮你转接到他的分机。
The company will put the new product through rigorous testing. = 公司将对新产品进行严格的测试。


虚拟语气:
If they had put more effort, they would have put the project through on time. = 如果他们多花些力气，他们就能按时完成项目。
I wish they had put the plan through more carefully. = 我希望他们更仔细地执行计划。


被动语态:
I was put through to the wrong person. = 我被转接给了错误的人。
The proposal was put through with some amendments. = 该提案经过一些修改后通过了。


疑问句:

否定句:
