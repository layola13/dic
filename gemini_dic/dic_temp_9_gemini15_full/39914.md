constrained:/kənˈstreɪnd/ | adj. | 受约束的；受限制的；勉强的 |  Limited or restricted; forced or compelled.  It implies a lack of freedom or spontaneity. |例句：His movements were constrained by the tight bandages.他的动作被绷带紧紧束缚着。

例句：The constrained budget forced us to cut some features from the project.  有限的预算迫使我们从项目中削减了一些功能。


近义词：restricted, limited, confined, hampered, inhibited, compelled, forced


反义词：free, unrestricted, unconstrained, open, liberated


词源：From Old French *constreindre, from Latin *constringere, from com- + stringere "to bind, draw tight".


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He feels constrained by his responsibilities. 他感到责任束缚着他。
The rules constrain our actions.  规则约束着我们的行为。


一般过去时:
The heavy rain constrained our travel plans. 大雨阻碍了我们的旅行计划。
She felt constrained to apologize. 她觉得不得不道歉。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

The company had been constrained to reduce its workforce. 公司被迫裁员。


将来时:
Future regulations will constrain industrial emissions. 未来的法规将限制工业排放。

虚拟语气:
If we weren't constrained by time, we could finish the project earlier. 如果不受时间限制，我们可以更早完成项目。

被动语态:

否定句:
