fasces:/ˈfæsiːz/| n.|法西斯束；权力象征|A bundle of rods traditionally bound together with an axe.  In ancient Rome, fasces were carried by lictors as a symbol of the magistrate's power. The image is often used to represent authority, power, and sometimes tyranny.|The fasces on the Roman official's throne indicated his authority.  罗马官员宝座上的法西斯束象征着他的权力。|近义词：symbol of power, emblem of authority|反义词：powerlessness, weakness
