infusory:/ɪnˈfjuːzəri/| n. |注入器；灌输器；（旧称）原生动物|An infusory is an obsolete term for a microscopic organism, typically a ciliate, found in infusions of organic matter.  The term is now largely of historical interest, having been replaced by more precise classifications in microbiology.|Example:  Early microscopists often observed infusoria in pond water.|近义词: microorganism, protozoan|反义词: None specifically, but the term is outdated and lacks modern precise antonyms.
