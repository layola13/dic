crusaders:/kruˈseɪdərz/| n. |十字军战士；倡导者|People who participated in the Crusades, a series of religious wars between Christians and Muslims in the Middle East from the 11th to the 13th centuries.  The term can also refer to anyone who champions a cause with zeal and determination.|例句：The crusaders fought for the control of the Holy Land. 十字军战士为争夺圣地而战。

The crusaders’ journey was long and arduous. 十字军战士的旅程漫长而艰辛。


近义词：campaigners, fighters, advocates |反义词：opponents, adversaries
