piffling: /ˈpɪflɪŋ/ | adj. | 微不足道的，琐碎的，不重要的 |  Something piffling is small, insignificant, or trifling; it lacks importance or value.  It often suggests a feeling of contempt or dismissal for the thing described. | 例句：The amount of money he offered was piffling. 他提供的钱少得可怜。

The repair was a piffling matter, easily fixed. 修理是个微不足道的问题，很容易解决。


近义词：trivial, insignificant, negligible, trifling, petty, paltry, inconsequential | 反义词：significant, important, substantial, considerable


词源:  The word likely originated in the 17th century. Its etymology is uncertain, but it might be related to a now-obsolete word "piffle," meaning nonsense or foolish talk.  The "-ing" suffix transforms it into an adjective.
