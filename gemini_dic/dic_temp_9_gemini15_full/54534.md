disloyal:/dɪsˈlɔɪəl/| adj.|不忠诚的，背叛的|Not loyal; faithless; treacherous.  Disloyal behavior is characterized by a breach of trust or allegiance.|例句：His disloyal actions led to the downfall of the company. 他的不忠行为导致公司垮台。|例句：She felt betrayed by her disloyal friend. 她觉得被她不忠的朋友背叛了。|近义词：faithless, treacherous, unfaithful, disobedient|反义词：loyal, faithful, true, trustworthy|形容词

词源:
dis- (否定前缀) + loyal (忠诚的)  The word derives from the Old French word "leial," meaning "loyal," which in turn originates from the Latin word "legalis," meaning "lawful, belonging to law."  The prefix "dis-" adds the sense of negation or opposition.
