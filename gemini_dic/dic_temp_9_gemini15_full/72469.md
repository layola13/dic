flunkey's: /ˈflʌŋkiːz/ | n. | 谄媚者（的）；马屁精（的） | A flunkey is a servile and obsequious person, typically one who holds a position of low rank.  The term often carries a connotation of social climbing and excessive eagerness to please those in higher positions.  The possessive form "flunkey's" indicates that something belongs to or is associated with a flunkey. | Example: The flunkey's obsequious smile didn't fool anyone.  谄媚者的谄媚笑容骗不了任何人。  The flunkey's polished shoes reflected the harsh lights of the ballroom. 谄媚者的擦得锃亮的鞋子反射着舞厅刺眼的光线。|近义词：lackey, toady, sycophant, yes-man |反义词：leader, independent person, rebel

词源:

The word "flunkey" originates from the Dutch word "fluik," meaning "a servant" or "a groom," possibly via a Low German intermediary.  It entered English in the 18th century and evolved to carry its current derogatory connotations.
