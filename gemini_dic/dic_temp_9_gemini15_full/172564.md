pyrolignic:/ˌpaɪroʊˈlɪɡnɪk/| adj.|焦木的；木焦油的|Relating to or derived from the destructive distillation of wood, yielding pyroligneous acid.  The term refers to the substances produced when wood is heated in the absence of air, a process called pyrolysis. These substances include acetic acid, methanol, and other organic compounds.|The pyrolignic acid obtained from the process was used to preserve meat.|近义词：wood-derived, empyreumatic|反义词：None readily available.
