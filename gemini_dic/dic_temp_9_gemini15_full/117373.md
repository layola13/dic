mediatization:/ˌmiːdiətaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |媒介化；媒体化 |The process by which media permeate and reshape various aspects of society and culture.  It involves the increasing influence of media on our understanding of the world, social interactions, political processes, and even our personal identities.  This process is often seen as a complex interplay between media, society, and individuals. |例句：The mediatization of politics has led to shorter soundbites and a focus on image over substance. 政治的媒体化导致了更短的新闻片段以及对形象而非实质内容的关注。

The increasing mediatization of our lives raises concerns about the erosion of privacy.  我们生活中日益增长的媒体化引发了对隐私侵蚀的担忧。|近义词：media influence,  media saturation,  cultural transformation|反义词：de-mediatization (less media influence) |名词
