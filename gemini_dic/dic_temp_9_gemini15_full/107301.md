lamarckianism:/ləˈmɑːrkɪənɪzəm/| n. |拉马克主义|The theory of inheritance of acquired characteristics, developed by Jean-Baptiste Lamarck, stating that changes acquired during an organism's lifetime can be passed on to its offspring.  This theory is now largely discredited in favor of Darwinian evolution.|Example:  Lamarckianism posited that giraffes developed long necks through stretching to reach high branches, and this trait was then inherited by their offspring.|近义词：Lamarckism|反义词：Darwinism, neo-Darwinism
