contiguity:/ˌkɒntɪˈɡjuːɪti/| /ˌkɑːntɪˈɡjuːɪti/ | n. |邻接；连续；接近；紧密联系 |The state of being contiguous; proximity or closeness.  It often refers to things that are next to or touching each other, or events that happen closely together in time or space. |例句：The contiguity of the two houses made it easy for the families to socialize.  两座房子相邻，使得两家人很容易交往。

例句：The contiguity of events in the plot kept the reader engaged. 情节中事件的紧密衔接使读者始终保持兴趣。


近义词：proximity, adjacency, closeness, nearness, contiguousness


反义词：distance, remoteness, separation, discontinuity


词性：名词


词源：
From Middle French *contiguïté, from Latin contiguus ("touching, adjoining"), from con- ("together") + tangere ("to touch").  The suffix "-ity" is a common noun-forming suffix indicating a state or quality.
