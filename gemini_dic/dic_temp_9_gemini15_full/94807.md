impulsive: /ɪmˈpʌlsɪv/ | adj. | 冲动的；易冲动的 | Acting or done without forethought;  characterized by sudden, unthinking actions or decisions.  | 例句：He's impulsive and often regrets his decisions later. 他很冲动，经常后悔自己的决定。

例句：Her impulsive purchase of a new car left her with little money. 她冲动地买了一辆新车，导致她几乎没钱了。


近义词：rash, impetuous, reckless, hasty | 反义词：deliberate, cautious, thoughtful, premeditated | 词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is impulsive in his spending. = 他花钱很冲动。
She is always impulsive when she shops. = 她购物时总是很冲动。


一般过去时:
He was impulsive and bought a motorcycle without thinking. = 他很冲动，没经过思考就买了一辆摩托车。
She was impulsive and quit her job without a plan. = 她很冲动，没计划就辞职了。


现在完成时:
He has been impulsive throughout his life. = 他一生都非常冲动。
She has been impulsive in her relationships. = 她处理感情时一直很冲动。


过去完成时:
He had been impulsive in the past, so he tried to be more careful. = 他过去很冲动，所以他试图更小心。
She had been impulsive before, so this time she thought it over carefully. = 她以前很冲动，所以这次她仔细考虑了。


将来时:
He will likely be impulsive again in the future. = 他将来很可能会再次冲动。
She will probably be impulsive when faced with that offer. = 面对那个提议时，她可能会很冲动。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't been so impulsive, he wouldn't have lost his job. = 如果他不那么冲动，他就不会丢掉工作了。
If she weren't so impulsive, she would have made a better decision. = 如果她不那么冲动，她会做出更好的决定。


被动语态:
His actions were seen as impulsive by others. = 他的行为被别人认为是冲动的。
Her decision was considered impulsive by her friends. = 她朋友认为她的决定很冲动。


疑问句:
Is he always so impulsive? = 他总是这么冲动吗？
Was her behavior impulsive? = 她的行为是冲动的吗？


否定句:
He wasn't impulsive; he thought carefully before acting. = 他并不冲动；他在行动前仔细考虑了。
She wasn't impulsive; she made a well-considered decision. = 她并不冲动；她做了一个深思熟虑的决定。


PHRASE->
impulsive behavior: 冲动行为
impulsive buying: 冲动性购买
impulsive decision: 冲动决定
impulsive reaction: 冲动反应
highly impulsive: 非常冲动


词源:
The word "impulsive" comes from the Latin word "impulsus," which means "a push" or "a driving force."  It entered English in the 17th century.
