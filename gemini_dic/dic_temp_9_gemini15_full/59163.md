dyscrasia: /dɪsˈkreɪʒə/ | n. | 异常；不良状态；血液病 | An abnormal state, typically a medical condition.  It often refers to a disorder of the blood or blood-forming organs.  | Example:  The patient presented with a blood dyscrasia characterized by an abnormally low white blood cell count.  这位病人出现了一种血液病，其特征是白细胞计数异常低下。 | 近义词：disorder, abnormality, disease | 反义词：normality, health
