quasi-revolutionized: /ˈkwɑːzaɪˌrɛvəˈluːʃənaɪzd/ | adj. | 准革命性的，近乎革命的 |  Describes something that has brought about significant, though not entirely transformative, change; a near-revolution. It suggests a substantial shift but perhaps lacking the complete and sweeping impact of a true revolution. | 例句：The new software quasi-revolutionized the company's workflow, streamlining many processes but not completely overhauling the entire system.  = 新软件使公司的流程发生了准革命性的变化，简化了许多流程，但并没有完全彻底改变整个系统。


The introduction of the internet quasi-revolutionized communication, connecting people across the globe but also creating new challenges. = 互联网的引入使通讯发生了近乎革命性的变化，将世界各地的人们联系起来，但也带来了新的挑战。


近义词：transformative, groundbreaking, revolutionary (to a lesser extent), significantly changed


反义词：unchanged, insignificant, incremental


词性：形容词

词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi* meaning "as if," "almost," or "like."
* **revolutionized**: From "revolution" (a fundamental change in power or organization) + "-ized" (made or became).

The word combines the sense of "almost" or "nearly" with the impact of a revolution, creating a nuanced term for significant but incomplete transformation.
