shot-blast:/ˈʃɒtˌblæst/ | n. v. |喷丸；喷砂|The process of cleaning or surface treating a metal object by blasting it with small abrasive particles (shot).  It's used to remove rust, scale, paint, or other surface contaminants, and can also improve the surface finish for better adhesion. |例句：The steel components were shot-blasted to remove surface imperfections before painting. 钢铁部件在喷漆前经过喷丸处理以去除表面缺陷。

The engine block underwent a thorough shot-blast cleaning. 发动机缸体进行了彻底的喷砂清洗。|近义词：abrasive blasting, sandblasting |反义词：coating, painting

词源:
The word "shot-blast" is a compound word. "Shot" refers to the small abrasive particles used in the process, often made of steel or other metal. "Blast" refers to the forceful projection of these particles onto the surface of the object being treated.  The term thus describes the action of blasting the surface with shot.
