nodule:/ˈnɒdʒuːl/ /ˈnɑːdʒuːl/| n. |小瘤；结节；小结；(植物的)小球|A nodule is a small swelling or lump, especially one that is felt under the skin.  It can be caused by various factors, including inflammation, infection, or a tumor.  Nodules can occur in many different parts of the body. |例句：The doctor discovered a small nodule on her lung during the examination.  医生在检查中发现她肺部有一个小结节。

The patient presented with multiple skin nodules. 病人出现了多个皮肤结节。


近义词：lump, bump, swelling, tubercule |反义词：None specifically; antonyms would depend on the context of the nodule's cause. |词性:名词


词源:  From Late Latin *nodulus, diminutive of nodus "knot," from Proto-Indo-European *neud- "to bind, tie."  The connection to "knot" reflects the appearance of a nodule as a small, rounded mass.
