professorial: /ˌprɒfəˈsɔːriəl/ /ˌprɑːfəˈsɔːriəl/ | adj. | 教授的；教授似的；学究气的 | Relating to or characteristic of a professor; scholarly or academic in manner.  It often implies a certain formality, authority, and sometimes pedantry. |

例句：

He adopted a professorial air, lecturing us on the finer points of the debate. 他摆出一副教授的架势，向我们讲解辩论的细微之处。

Her professorial demeanor intimidated some students. 她教授般的气质让一些学生感到害怕。


近义词：academic, scholarly, learned, intellectual, didactic

反义词：unscholarly, unintellectual, informal, casual

词源:
From professor + -ial.  "Professor" originates from the Latin *professōr, from *profiteri "to declare publicly, profess," from pro- "forth" + fateri "to confess, speak."  The suffix "-ial" denotes "relating to" or "characteristic of."
