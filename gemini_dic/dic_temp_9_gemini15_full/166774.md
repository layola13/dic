presocialists: /ˌpriːˈsoʊʃəlɪsts/ | adj. | 前社会主义者，准社会主义者 |  Describing individuals or groups who hold beliefs or advocate policies that are partially or tentatively socialist, but not fully committed to a complete socialist system.  They might embrace some socialist principles while retaining aspects of capitalism or other ideologies.  The term often implies a transitional or evolving position. | Example: Some scholars argue that certain early 20th-century reformers were presocialists, advocating for social reforms while stopping short of full-blown revolution.  一些学者认为某些20世纪早期的改革者是前社会主义者，他们主张社会改革，但没有采取彻底的革命。| 近义词: proto-socialist, socialist sympathizer | 反义词: anti-socialist, capitalist |  adj.
