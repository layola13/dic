词源:
The word "laid-out" comes from the phrasal verb "lay out," which means to arrange or plan something in a particular way.  "Lay" originates from the Old English verb *lecgan*, meaning "to place" or "to put."  The "out" component adds the sense of spatial arrangement or distribution.
