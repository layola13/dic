non-sustainability:/ˌnɒnˌsʌsteɪnəˈbɪləti/ | n. |不可持续性，非永续性 |The inability to maintain or support a particular state or level over a prolonged period.  It often refers to practices or systems that deplete resources or damage the environment, making them unsustainable in the long term. |例句：The non-sustainability of current agricultural practices is leading to soil degradation. 当前农业生产方式的不可持续性导致土壤退化。

The non-sustainability of the current economic model is causing widespread social unrest.  当前经济模式的不可持续性正在导致广泛的社会动荡。|近义词：unsustainability, instability, depletion |反义词：sustainability, permanence, endurance
