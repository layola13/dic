retraversing: /ˌriːˈtrævərsɪŋ/ | v. | 再测量；复测；重新追踪 | To measure (a line, etc.) again; to traverse again.  The act of surveying or measuring a line a second time to check the accuracy of the first measurement. | 例句：The surveyor is retraversing the boundary line to ensure accuracy.  测量员正在重新测量边界线以确保准确性。

The team retraversed the trail to look for lost equipment.  团队重新追踪小路寻找丢失的设备。


近义词：resurveying, rechecking, remeasuring | 反义词：initial surveying, first measurement

词源:
The word "retraversing" comes from the prefix "re-" (again) and "traversing," which ultimately derives from the Old French word "traverser" (to cross).  Thus, "retraversing" literally means "to cross again."  The surveying usage is a specialized application of this more general meaning.
