post-conquest: /ˌpoʊstˈkɒŋkwest/ | adj. | 后征服的，征服后的 | Relating to the period or events following a conquest.  This adjective typically refers to a specific historical conquest, often implied by the context.  For example, "post-conquest England" refers to the period after the Norman Conquest of 1066. | Example: The post-conquest landscape of England saw significant changes in architecture and language.  = 征服后的英格兰在建筑和语言方面发生了显著变化。  The post-conquest era witnessed the rise of feudalism. = 征服后的时代见证了封建制度的兴起。 | 近义词: post-invasion, post-occupation | 反义词: pre-conquest
