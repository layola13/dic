canonised:/ˈkænənaɪzd/| vt.|封圣，尊为圣徒|To officially declare someone a saint in a religious ceremony, typically in the Catholic Church.|The Pope canonised the nun after a lengthy process of investigation into her life and miracles.  教皇在对这位修女的一生和奇迹进行了漫长的调查后，为她封圣。|beatified, sanctified|uncanonised|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The Church canonises saints based on evidence of their holiness. = 教会根据其圣洁的证据为圣徒封圣。
They canonise individuals who have shown exceptional piety. = 他们为那些表现出非凡虔诚的人封圣。


一般过去时:
The Pope canonised her last year. = 教宗去年为她封圣。
They canonised many martyrs during that period. = 他们在那个时期为许多殉道者封圣。


现在完成时:
Many saints have been canonised throughout history. = 历史上许多圣徒都被封圣。
The Church has canonised several new saints recently. = 教会最近封圣了几位新圣徒。


过去完成时:
Before the canonisation, a thorough investigation had been conducted. = 在封圣之前，已经进行了彻底的调查。
By the time he died, he had already been canonised. = 到他死的时候，他已经封圣了。


将来时:
They will canonise the missionary next year. = 他们将在明年为这位传教士封圣。
The process of canonisation will likely take many years. = 封圣的过程很可能需要很多年。


虚拟语气:

I wish they would canonise her quickly. = 我希望他们能尽快为她封圣。
