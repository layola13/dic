slander:/ˈslændər/| n. v.|诽谤；中伤|The act of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation.|例句：He was accused of slander after spreading false rumors about his colleague. 他在散布关于同事的谣言后被指控诽谤。|近义词：defamation, calumny, libel|反义词：praise, commendation|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slanders his opponents. = 他诽谤他的对手。
She slanders people behind their backs. = 她背后诽谤别人。


一般过去时:
They slandered him in the newspaper. = 他们在报纸上诽谤他。
He slandered her reputation. = 他诽谤了她的名誉。


现在完成时:
They have slandered me repeatedly. = 他们反复诽谤我。
She has slandered many people in her career. =  她在职业生涯中诽谤过很多人。


过去完成时:
He had slandered her before the trial. = 他在审判前就诽谤了她。
They had slandered the company for years. = 他们诽谤这家公司多年了。


将来时:
They will slander him in the election campaign. = 他们在竞选活动中将诽谤他。
She will slander her rivals mercilessly. = 她会无情地诽谤她的竞争对手。


虚拟语气:
If he slandered me, I would sue him. = 如果他诽谤我，我会起诉他。
I wish he hadn’t slandered my family. = 我真希望他没有诽谤我的家人。


被动语态:
He was slandered by his enemies. = 他被他的敌人诽谤了。
The company was slandered in the media. = 这家公司在媒体上被诽谤了。


疑问句:
Did he slander her? = 他诽谤她了吗？
Who slandered the witness? = 谁诽谤了证人？


否定句:
He did not slander anyone. = 他没有诽谤任何人。
She wouldn't slander her friend. = 她不会诽谤她的朋友。


PHRASE->
a slanderous remark = 一句诽谤的话
a slanderous attack = 一次诽谤的攻击
to be slandered = 被诽谤
to slander someone's name = 诽谤某人的名声
a campaign of slander = 一场诽谤运动
