confrontations:/ˌkɒnfrʌnˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 对抗；冲突；面对 | A confrontation is a hostile or argumentative meeting or situation between opposing parties.  It often involves a direct and challenging encounter. |例句：The two leaders avoided a direct confrontation. 两位领导人避免了直接对抗。

例句：The police were called to break up a violent confrontation between rival gangs. 警方被叫去制止敌对帮派之间的暴力冲突。


近义词：conflict, clash, encounter, dispute, showdown | 反义词：agreement, harmony, accord, peace


词性: 名词


词源:  From the verb "confront," which comes from the Old French "confronter," meaning "to bring face to face."  The prefix "con-" means "together," and the root "-front" relates to the idea of "facing" or "forefront."  The suffix "-ation" forms nouns of action or process.
