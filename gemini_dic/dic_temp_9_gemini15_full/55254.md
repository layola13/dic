dissimulative: /dɪˈsɪmjuleɪtɪv/ | adj. | 掩饰的；虚伪的；伪装的 |  Characterized by or tending toward dissimulation; concealing or disguising the truth.  It implies an intent to deceive or mislead. |

例句：

His dissimulative behavior aroused suspicion among his colleagues.  他的虚伪行为引起了同事们的怀疑。

The dissimulative nature of the politician's statements made it hard to believe anything he said.  这位政治家声明的掩饰性使得人们很难相信他说的话。


近义词：deceitful, deceptive, misleading, hypocritical, false, disingenuous

反义词：honest, truthful, genuine, frank, sincere, candid


词源:

From Latin *dissimulatus, past participle of dissimulare "to disguise, dissemble," from dis- "apart" + simulare "to imitate, feign."
