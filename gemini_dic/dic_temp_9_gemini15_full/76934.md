gapes:/ɡeɪps/| v.|张开嘴；裂开；目瞪口呆|To open one's mouth wide, especially in surprise or wonder; to stare with one's mouth open.  Also, to open wide; to be wide open.|例句：He gaped at the amazing sight. 他目瞪口呆地看着这令人惊叹的景象。
The crack in the wall gaped open. 墙上的裂缝张开了。|近义词：yawn, stare, gape|反义词：close, shut


USAGE->

He gapes at the television all evening. = 他整个晚上都对着电视发呆。
She gapes in astonishment. = 她惊讶地张大了嘴巴。

一般过去时:
The audience gaped in disbelief. = 观众们难以置信地张大了嘴巴。
He gaped at the enormous building. = 他看着那座巨大的建筑物，目瞪口呆。

词源:
Old English *gæpian, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch gapen and German gaffen.  The sense development is from "to yawn" to "to stare with open mouth".
