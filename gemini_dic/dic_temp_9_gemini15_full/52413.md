didelphous: /daɪˈdɛlfəs/ | adj. | 双室的；(指子房)有二心皮的 |  Having two carpels; referring to a gynoecium with two separate carpels.  The term is primarily used in botany to describe the structure of a pistil (female reproductive organ) in a flower. A didelphous gynoecium has two separate carpels that are not fused together.  | Example:  The flower exhibited a didelphous gynoecium, with two distinct carpels easily visible.  该花展现出双室的雌蕊，两个不同的心皮很容易看见。 | 近义词: bicarpellate (sometimes used interchangeably but emphasizes the two carpels rather than the separation) | 反义词: syncarpous (having fused carpels) |  词性: adjective
