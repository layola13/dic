coddling:/ˈkɒdlɪŋ/ | /ˈkɑːdlɪŋ/ | n. v. | 溺爱；娇惯；悉心照料 |  The act of treating someone too carefully or protectively, often to the point of hindering their independence and development.  It can also refer to the gentle simmering of food. |

例句：
Parents who coddle their children may create overly dependent adults. = 溺爱孩子的父母可能会养育出过度依赖的成年人。

She coddled her sick child, providing constant care and attention. = 她悉心照料生病的孩子，给予持续的关怀和照顾。


近义词：pampering, spoiling, indulging, cherishing (in the sense of caring for),  simmering (in the culinary sense)

反义词：neglecting, ignoring, abandoning, harsh treatment (in the sense of parenting)


词性:
名词 (n.):  coddling (the act of coddling)
动词 (v.):  to coddle (to treat someone too protectively; to simmer food gently)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He coddles his son excessively. = 他过分溺爱他的儿子。
She coddles the baby all day. = 她整天都娇惯着婴儿。


一般过去时:
They coddled the injured bird until it recovered. = 他们悉心照料受伤的小鸟直到它康复。
My grandmother coddled me when I was sick. = 我生病的时候奶奶细心照料我。


现在完成时:
She has coddled him throughout his life. = 她一生都溺爱着他。
They have coddled their child too much. = 他们把孩子娇惯坏了。


过去完成时:
She had coddled him since he was a child. = 她从他小时候起就一直溺爱他。
They had coddled the plant in the greenhouse. = 他们一直在温室里精心呵护那株植物。


将来时:
They will coddle their newborn baby. = 他们将细心照料他们的新生儿。
I will coddle myself with a hot bath. = 我要好好泡个热水澡犒劳自己。


虚拟语气:
If I coddled him more, he might be happier. = 如果我更溺爱他，他可能会更快乐。(This is less common with coddle in this sense)
I wish I had coddled her more when she was young. = 我真希望在她年轻的时候更呵护她一些。


被动语态:
The child was coddled by his parents. = 这个孩子被他的父母溺爱着。
The soup was gently coddled over low heat. = 汤在小火上慢慢地炖着。


疑问句:
Do you coddle your children? = 你溺爱你的孩子吗？
Does she coddle her plants? = 她细心照料她的植物吗？


否定句:
They don't coddle their employees. = 他们不溺爱他们的员工。
He didn't coddle the situation. = 他没有娇惯现状。



PHRASE->
coddle along = 慢慢地进行
coddle up = 偎依；依偎
coddle up to = 讨好；奉承
coddle oneself = 纵容自己
coddle the problem = 掩盖问题 (Less common, implies avoiding dealing with a problem properly)
