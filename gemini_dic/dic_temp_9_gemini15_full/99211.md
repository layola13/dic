interclusion:/ˌɪntərˈkluʒən/| n. |包含，插入；中断|The interclusion of a new paragraph broke the flow of the narrative.|近义词：insertion, interruption|反义词：exclusion, continuation|名词


英文解析:The act of inserting something into something else, or the state of being inserted.  It can also refer to an interruption or break in a continuous process or sequence.


例句:

The interclusion of a new paragraph broke the flow of the narrative.
插入新段落打断了叙事的流畅。


The sudden interclusion of the storm disrupted our plans.
暴风雨的突然来袭扰乱了我们的计划。


词源:

From Latin *intercludere, from inter- "between, among" + claudere "to shut, close".  The sense evolved from "to shut off" to "to interrupt" and then to the broader meaning of insertion.


用法说明:

虽然interclusion在现代英语中并不常用，但它在某些专业领域，特别是印刷术和语言学中，可能出现。  它通常指一种插入或中断，强调插入物对原有事物的影响。


近义词：

insertion, interruption, inclusion, intervention


反义词：

exclusion, omission, continuation
