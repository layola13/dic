hangovers: /ˈhæŋˌoʊvər/ | n. |宿醉；后遗症|

英文解析：An unpleasant physical feeling, such as headache or nausea, that you have the day after drinking too much alcohol.  It can also refer to the lingering negative effects of a past event or experience.

例句：
I woke up with a terrible hangover after the party.  我参加派对后醒来时宿醉得很厉害。

He's still suffering from the hangover of his failed business venture. 他仍然受着他的生意失败带来的后遗症的折磨。


近义词：aftereffects, repercussions, consequences, residual effects

反义词：None directly opposite, but antonyms might relate to the absence of negative effects, such as "refreshment," "recovery," or "well-being."
