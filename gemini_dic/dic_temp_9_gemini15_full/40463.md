contredanse:/ˈkɒntrədɑːns/| n. |康特雷丹斯舞 |A contradance is a type of folk dance, typically done in long lines of couples, with couples progressing down the line and changing partners.  It originated in France and spread throughout Europe and later to America.  The dance involves a series of figures and steps, often accompanied by music. |Example:  The village celebrated the harvest with a lively contredanse. 村庄用一场热闹的康特雷丹斯舞庆祝丰收。|近义词：country dance, square dance|反义词：solo dance, ballroom dance|名词
