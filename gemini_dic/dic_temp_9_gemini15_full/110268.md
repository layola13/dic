lifted:/ˈlɪftɪd/| v. adj.|举起，抬起；被提升的；情绪高涨的 | To raise something to a higher position;  also used figuratively to describe an improvement in mood or spirits, or something being stolen.|例句：He lifted the heavy box. 他搬起了沉重的箱子。 |She felt lifted after a good night's sleep.  睡了一夜好觉后，她感觉精神焕发了。|近义词：raised, elevated, hoisted, boosted |反义词：lowered, dropped, depressed


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He lifts weights every day. = 他每天举重。
She lifts her chin proudly. = 她骄傲地扬起了下巴。


一般过去时:
They lifted the piano with great effort. = 他们费了好大劲才把钢琴搬起来。
The crane lifted the building materials to the top floor. = 起重机把建筑材料吊到了顶楼。


现在完成时:
He has lifted his game significantly this season. = 这个赛季他的竞技状态有了显著提高。
She has lifted the spirits of the team with her inspiring speech. = 她鼓舞人心的演讲鼓舞了全队的士气。


过去完成时:
They had lifted the ban before he arrived. = 他到达之前他们就已经取消了禁令。
She had lifted the veil of mystery surrounding the case. = 她揭开了围绕此案的神秘面纱。


将来时:
They will lift the restrictions next month. = 他们下个月将取消限制。
The workers will lift the new equipment into place tomorrow. = 工人们明天将把新设备安装到位。


虚拟语气:
If he lifted a finger, he could have helped. = 如果他稍微动动手，他就能帮忙。
If only she had lifted her spirits sooner. = 但愿她早点振作起来。


被动语态:
The car was lifted by the jack. = 汽车被千斤顶顶起来了。
The restrictions were lifted unexpectedly. = 限制出乎意料地被取消了。


疑问句:
Did he lift the package? = 他搬起包裹了吗？
Can you lift that heavy suitcase? = 你能搬起那个沉重的行李箱吗？


否定句:
He couldn't lift the stone. = 他搬不动那块石头。
She didn't lift a finger to help. = 她没有帮一点忙。
