schedules:/ˈʃɛdʒuːlz/| n.|时间表；进度表；安排|A plan that lists all the times that things will happen.  It can refer to a timetable, a program, or a planned sequence of events.|例句：I need to check my schedules to see if I'm free. 我需要查看我的日程安排看看我是否有空。

例句：The project schedules are very tight.  这个项目的进度表非常紧张。|近义词：timetable, program, plan, itinerary|反义词：disorder, chaos|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He schedules his work carefully. = 他仔细安排他的工作。
They schedule meetings every week. = 他们每周安排会议。


一般过去时:
She scheduled a doctor's appointment for tomorrow. = 她预约了明天的医生。
We scheduled a trip to Europe last year. = 我们去年安排了一次欧洲之旅。


现在完成时:
They have scheduled all the events for the conference. = 他们已经安排好了会议的所有活动。
I have scheduled a meeting with my manager. = 我已经安排好了与经理的会议。


过去完成时:
He had scheduled the meeting before the announcement. = 他在宣布之前就安排好了会议。
We had scheduled our holiday before the school term started. = 我们在学期开始之前就安排好了假期。


将来时:
She will schedule a meeting next week. = 她下周将安排一次会议。
They will schedule the project launch for next month. = 他们将把项目启动安排在下个月。


虚拟语气:
If I had scheduled the appointment earlier, I wouldn't be so busy. = 如果我早点预约，我就不会这么忙了。
I wish I had scheduled a vacation. = 我真希望我安排了一个假期。


被动语态:
The meeting was scheduled for 2 pm. = 会议安排在下午两点。
The presentation has been scheduled for next Friday. = 演示已安排在下周五。


疑问句:
When is the meeting scheduled? = 会议什么时候安排？
Has the concert been scheduled yet? = 音乐会安排好了吗？


否定句:
I haven't scheduled any appointments this week. = 我本周没有安排任何预约。
They didn't schedule a meeting for today. = 他们今天没有安排会议。
