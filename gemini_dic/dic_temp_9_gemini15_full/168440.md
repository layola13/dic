probabilism:/ˈprɒbəbɪlɪzəm/| n. |或然论，概率论|The theory that in the absence of certainty, the most probable opinion should be followed.  It's a philosophical approach emphasizing probability and likelihood in decision-making, especially in moral theology.|Example:  The court's decision was based on probabilism, weighing the available evidence to determine the most likely scenario.  法庭的判决基于或然论，权衡现有证据以确定最可能的场景。|近义词：probabilistical reasoning, likelihood, plausibility |反义词：certainty, determinism
