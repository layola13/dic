gobbledygook:/ˈɡɑːblədɪɡʊk/| n. |难以理解的话；胡言乱语；废话|Unintelligible jargon; nonsense talk.  It refers to speech or writing that is confusing and meaningless. |例句：The politician's speech was nothing but gobbledygook.  那位政治家的讲话全是废话。|例句：I couldn't understand a word of his gobbledygook. 我一句他的胡言乱语都听不懂。|近义词：nonsense, jargon, gibberish, balderdash |反义词：clarity, sense, lucidity |名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun, so verb conjugations don't apply.  The usage focuses on how it's used in sentences.

Example 1: The contract was full of legal gobbledygook. (合同充满了法律术语，让人难以理解。)
Example 2: He spoke in a stream of gobbledygook that left everyone confused. (他讲了一大堆让人摸不着头脑的废话，把大家都弄糊涂了。)


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "gobbledygook".  The word itself acts as a phrase describing unintelligible language.
