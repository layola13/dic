/ˈduːəlɪst/| adj.|二元论者，二元论的|A dualist is someone who believes in dualism, a philosophical concept that posits the existence of two fundamental, distinct entities or principles, such as mind and body, or good and evil.  The term can also describe something having two parts or aspects.|Examples:  He is a staunch dualist, believing that the mind and body are entirely separate entities.  The painting presents a dualistic view of nature, showing both its beauty and its destructive power. |近义词：dichotomist,binaristic|反义词：monist,unitary
