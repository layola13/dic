reconfirmation:/ˌriːkɒnfɜːˈmeɪʃən/ | n. | 再确认；再次确认 | The act of confirming something again; a second confirmation.  It often implies a previous confirmation that needs to be verified or strengthened.  | Example: The airline requires reconfirmation of your flight 24 hours before departure.  航空公司要求您在出发前24小时再次确认航班。


The reconfirmation of the booking is essential.  再次确认预订至关重要。| confirmation, verification | denial, cancellation | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The hotel requires reconfirmation of the reservation. = 酒店需要再次确认预订。
We reconfirm our commitment to the project. = 我们再次确认我们对项目的承诺。


一般过去时:
He reconfirmed his attendance at the meeting. = 他再次确认了他参加会议。
She reconfirmed the details of the contract. = 她再次确认了合同的细节。


现在完成时:
They have reconfirmed their support for the initiative. = 他们已经再次确认了他们对这项倡议的支持。
We have reconfirmed the delivery date. = 我们已经再次确认了交货日期。


过去完成时:
They had reconfirmed the plans before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前已经再次确认了计划。
She had reconfirmed the address several times. = 她已经多次再次确认了地址。


将来时:
He will reconfirm his flight booking tomorrow. = 他明天将再次确认他的航班预订。
We will reconfirm the details with the client. = 我们将与客户再次确认细节。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would reconfirm the appointment. = 如果我是你，我会再次确认预约。
It is essential that you reconfirm your participation. = 你必须再次确认你的参与。


被动语态:
The booking was reconfirmed by the customer. = 预订已由客户再次确认。
The information needs to be reconfirmed. = 信息需要再次确认。


疑问句:
Have you reconfirmed your travel arrangements? = 你是否再次确认了你的旅行安排？
Did they reconfirm the delivery time? = 他们是否再次确认了交货时间？


否定句:
He did not reconfirm his reservation. = 他没有再次确认他的预订。
The client did not reconfirm the order. = 客户没有再次确认订单。
