prevaricator:/ˈprɛvərɪkeɪtər/| n. |支吾其词者；说谎者；闪烁其词者 |A person who speaks evasively or misleadingly, often to avoid telling the truth.  They often use ambiguous language or delay giving a direct answer. | The prevaricator avoided answering the question directly, offering instead a series of vague statements.  支吾其词者直接回避了这个问题，转而给出一些含糊不清的陈述。| equivocator, tergiversator, liar |truth-teller, honest person |名词

一般现在时:
He prevaricates when asked difficult questions. 他被问到难题时就支吾其词。
The witness prevaricates constantly during the trial.  证人在审判期间不断地闪烁其词。


一般过去时:
He prevaricated during the interrogation. 他在审讯中闪烁其词。
The politician prevaricated about his involvement in the scandal.  这位政治家对他卷入丑闻一事闪烁其词。


现在完成时:
He has prevaricated on this issue for years.  多年来，他一直在这个问题上闪烁其词。
The company has prevaricated about the safety of its product.  这家公司一直在含糊其辞，不愿说明其产品的安全性。


过去完成时:
He had prevaricated so much that no one believed him anymore. 他说得太多太含糊了，以至于没有人再相信他了。
They had prevaricated for so long that the truth was almost impossible to uncover. 他们隐瞒了这么久，真相几乎不可能被揭露了。


将来时:
He will prevaricate again if confronted. 如果再次被质问，他还会闪烁其词。
The suspect will likely prevaricate during the investigation.  嫌疑人很可能会在调查期间闪烁其词。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't prevaricated, the situation might have been different. 如果他不闪烁其词，情况可能会有所不同。
I wish he hadn't prevaricated; now we're all confused.  我希望他不要闪烁其词；现在我们都糊涂了。


被动语态:
His lies were prevaricated upon by the media.  他的谎言被媒体添油加醋地报道了。(Note: This use is less common and might sound awkward.  A more natural phrasing would focus on the act of prevarication itself, rather than the lies being the subject of the passive voice.)
The truth was skillfully prevaricated around. 真相被巧妙地回避了。


疑问句:
Does he often prevaricate? 他经常闪烁其词吗？
Did the witness prevaricate during his testimony? 证人在证词中是否闪烁其词？


否定句:
He didn't prevaricate; he told the truth. 他没有闪烁其词；他说的是实话。
She didn't prevaricate; she answered directly. 她没有闪烁其词；她直接回答了。
