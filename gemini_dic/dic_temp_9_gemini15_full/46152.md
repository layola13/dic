cymule:/ˈsɪmjuːl/| n. |小伞形花序|A cymule is a type of inflorescence (flower cluster) in which the flowers are arranged in a condensed, umbel-like cluster, with the individual flowers arising from a common point. Unlike a true umbel, the pedicels (flower stalks) of a cymule are not all of equal length.  It's a type of cyme, characterized by a determinate growth pattern where the central flower opens first.|Example: The inflorescence of the plant was identified as a cymule.|近义词：cyme, umbel (loosely)|反义词：raceme (indeterminate inflorescence)
