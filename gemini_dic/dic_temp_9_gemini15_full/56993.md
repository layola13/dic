double-density:/ˈdʌbəl ˈdensɪti/| adj.|双密度|Describes a storage medium, typically a floppy disk or magnetic tape, that can store twice the amount of data as a standard-density medium. This is achieved by increasing the density of data bits recorded on the surface.|例句：The double-density floppy disk could hold 1.2MB of data, compared to the 360KB of a single-density disk. 双密度软盘可以存储1.2MB的数据，而单密度软盘只能存储360KB。|近义词：high-density|反义词：single-density


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms that follow standard grammatical tense variations.  It describes a characteristic, not an action.  Therefore, tenses like past, present, future, etc., are not applicable.  The use is straightforward:

Example 1: "The computer used a double-density disk drive."  = 这台电脑使用双密度磁盘驱动器。
Example 2:  "Double-density floppies were common in the 1980s." = 双密度软盘在20世纪80年代很常见。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases specifically using "double-density".  Its usage is highly technical and limited to the context of data storage.
