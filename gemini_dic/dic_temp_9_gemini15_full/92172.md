hyperspaces: /ˈhaɪpərspeɪsɪz/ | n. |超空间；高维空间 | A region beyond the normal three dimensions of space, often considered in science fiction and theoretical physics.  It's a concept where additional spatial dimensions exist beyond our everyday perception.  Hyperspaces are often depicted as allowing for faster-than-light travel or other phenomena impossible within the constraints of our known universe. | Example:  In the science fiction novel, the spaceship jumped to hyperspace to escape the enemy. 在科幻小说中，宇宙飞船跃迁到超空间以躲避敌人。

| Example:  The theoretical physicist proposed a model of the universe that incorporates hyperspace.  这位理论物理学家提出了一个包含超空间的宇宙模型。|近义词：higher dimension, extra dimension |反义词：normal space, three-dimensional space |名词
