organizationist: /ˌɔːrɡənəˈzeɪʃənɪst/ | n. | 机构论者；组织理论家 | A person who studies or advocates for organizational theory and practice.  Someone who believes in the importance of organizations and their impact on society. They often focus on the structures, processes, and behaviors within organizations. | Example:  The organizationist presented a compelling case for restructuring the company.  机构论者提出了一个令人信服的公司重组案例。 | 近义词: organizational theorist, management theorist | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but could be contrasted with someone who emphasizes individual action over organizational structures) |  词性:名词
