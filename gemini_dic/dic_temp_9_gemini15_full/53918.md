discouraging: /dɪˈskʌrɪdʒɪŋ/ | adj. |令人沮丧的，使人灰心的|  causing someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm; disheartening. |例句：The discouraging news made her want to give up.  令人沮丧的消息让她想要放弃。

The constant failures were discouraging.  持续的失败令人灰心。|近义词：depressing, disheartening, dispiriting, discouraging, demoralizing |反义词：encouraging, inspiring, uplifting, heartening


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The weather is discouraging. = 天气令人沮丧。
This task seems discouraging. = 这项任务看起来令人沮丧。


一般过去时:
His words were discouraging. = 他的话令人沮丧。
The results of the experiment were discouraging. = 实验的结果令人沮丧。


现在完成时:
The setbacks have been discouraging. = 挫折一直令人沮丧。
The lack of progress has been discouraging. = 缺乏进展令人沮丧。


过去完成时:
The previous failures had been discouraging. = 之前的失败令人沮丧。
His past experiences had been discouraging. = 他过去的经历令人沮丧。


将来时:
The forecast predicts discouraging weather. = 预报预测天气令人沮丧。
The upcoming challenges will be discouraging. = 即将来临的挑战将令人沮丧。


虚拟语气:
It would be discouraging if we failed. = 如果我们失败了，那将令人沮丧。
It would have been discouraging if we hadn't tried. = 如果我们没有尝试，那将令人沮丧。

疑问句:

否定句:
