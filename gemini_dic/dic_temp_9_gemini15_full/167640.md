price-indexes:/ˈpraɪs ˈɪndɛksɪz/| n. |价格指数|A price index is a normalized average (typically a weighted average) of prices for a basket of selected goods and services in a given geographic area during a given period of time.  It is used to track changes in the relative cost of goods and services over time.  A price index of 100 indicates that the price level in the base period is the same as the price level in the period of interest.  A price index above 100 indicates that the average price level has increased since the base period, while a price index below 100 indicates that the average price level has decreased. |The Consumer Price Index (CPI) is a widely used price index that tracks changes in the cost of living.  The Producer Price Index (PPI) tracks changes in the prices received by domestic producers for their output. |近义词：cost of living index, inflation rate indicator |反义词：无  |名词
