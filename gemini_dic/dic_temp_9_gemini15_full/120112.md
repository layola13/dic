mimical:/ˈmɪmɪkəl/| adj.| 模仿的；滑稽模仿的 | Imitative; relating to mimicry; characterized by mimicry.  It often suggests a playful or humorous imitation, rather than a serious or exact replication. |例句：Her mimical skills were evident in her portrayal of the teacher.  她的模仿技巧在她对老师的刻画中显而易见。

例句：The comedian's mimical performance had the audience in stitches.  这位喜剧演员的模仿表演让观众捧腹大笑。

近义词： imitative, mimicking, mock, burlesque

反义词：original, genuine, authentic


词源:  From "mimic" + "-al," a suffix forming adjectives. "Mimic" itself comes from the Greek word "mimos," meaning "mime," "actor," or "imitator."  The Greek word is ultimately related to the verb "mimeisthai," meaning "to imitate."
