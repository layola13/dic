odd:/ɒd/ /ɑːd/| adj.|奇怪的，奇数的，临时的|Strange or unusual; not easily explained.  An odd number is not divisible by two.|例句：That's an odd coincidence.  = 那真是个奇怪的巧合。

That's an odd smell. = 那是一种奇怪的气味。|近义词：strange, peculiar, unusual, quirky|反义词：even, normal, usual, typical


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The number 7 is odd. = 数字7是奇数。
He has an odd habit of collecting bottle caps. = 他有一个收集瓶盖的怪癖。


一般过去时:
The results of the experiment were odd. = 实验的结果很奇怪。
She felt an odd sensation in her stomach. = 她感到胃里有一种奇怪的感觉。


现在完成时:
I've had an odd feeling all day. = 我一整天都感觉很奇怪。
Something odd has happened to my computer. = 我的电脑发生了一些奇怪的事情。


过去完成时:
I had an odd dream last night. = 我昨晚做了一个奇怪的梦。
They had an odd relationship, to say the least. = 至少可以说，他们之间关系很奇怪。


将来时:
I think something odd will happen tomorrow. = 我认为明天会发生一些奇怪的事情。
The weather forecast predicts odd conditions for next week. = 天气预报预测下周天气状况异常。


虚拟语气:
If it were not for this odd circumstance, we would have succeeded. = 如果不是因为这个奇怪的情况，我们本来会成功的。
I wish I didn't have such an odd name. = 我真希望我没有这么奇怪的名字。


被动语态:
The odd results were explained by the scientist. = 科学家解释了这些奇怪的结果。
His odd behavior was often overlooked by his colleagues. = 他的古怪行为经常被同事忽视。


疑问句:
Is there anything odd about this situation? = 这种情况有什么奇怪的地方吗？
Does that seem odd to you? = 你觉得那奇怪吗？


否定句:
There's nothing odd about his behavior. = 他的行为没有什么奇怪的。
It wasn't an odd request at all. = 这根本不是一个奇怪的要求。
