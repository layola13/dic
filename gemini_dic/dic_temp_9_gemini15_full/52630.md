digging:/ˈdɪɡɪŋ/| n. v.|挖掘；探索；刨根问底|The act of excavating or the process of searching for information.|例句：The archaeological dig was uncovering fascinating artifacts. 考古挖掘发现了迷人的文物。
例句：Her digging revealed a scandalous secret.  她的调查揭露了一个丑闻。|近义词：excavating, exploring, investigating|反义词：filling, burying, concealing|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are digging a new well. = 他们在挖一口新井。
He digs for gold in the mountains. = 他在山上淘金。

一般过去时:
We dug a hole in the garden. = 我们在花园里挖了一个洞。
She dug up some old photos. = 她挖出了一些旧照片。

现在完成时:
They have dug a lot of potatoes. = 他们已经挖了很多土豆。
He has been digging for hours. = 他已经挖了几个小时了。

过去完成时:
They had dug the foundation before the rain started. = 下雨前他们已经挖好了地基。
She had dug up all the information she needed. = 她已经找到了所有需要的信息。

将来时:
We will dig a new pond next spring. = 我们明年春天将挖一个新的池塘。
They will be digging the tunnel for months. = 他们将要挖掘隧道数月。

虚拟语气:
If we dug deeper, we might find more. = 如果我们挖得更深，可能会发现更多。
I wish I had dug a deeper hole. = 我真希望我挖个更深的洞。

被动语态:
The well was dug last year. = 这口井是去年挖的。
The evidence is being dug up by the police. = 证据正在被警方挖掘。

疑问句:
Are they digging a new road? = 他们正在挖一条新路吗？
What are you digging for? = 你在挖什么？

否定句:
They didn't dig the hole deep enough. = 他们挖的洞不够深。
We haven't dug up anything interesting yet. = 我们还没有挖到任何有趣的东西。
