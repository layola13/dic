slic:/slaɪs/| n. vt.|薄片，切片；一小部分|A slice is a flat, thin piece of something, cut from a larger piece.  It can also refer to a portion or section of something.|例句：I had a slice of pizza for lunch. 我午餐吃了一片披萨。|例句：The cake was cut into eight slices. 蛋糕被切成了八片。|近义词：piece, portion, section|反义词：whole


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slices the bread thinly. = 他把面包切成薄片。
She slices the tomatoes for the salad. = 她切番茄做沙拉。


一般过去时:
I sliced the cake into six pieces. = 我把蛋糕切成了六块。
He sliced the onion very finely. = 他把洋葱切得很细。


现在完成时:
She has sliced all the vegetables. = 她已经把所有蔬菜都切好了。
They have sliced the watermelon into triangles. = 他们已经把西瓜切成了三角形。


过去完成时:
He had sliced the bread before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，他已经把面包切好了。
She had sliced the cucumbers for the sandwiches. = 她已经为三明治切好了黄瓜。


将来时:
I will slice the ham for sandwiches tomorrow. = 我明天将切火腿做三明治。
She will slice the cake for the birthday party. = 她将为生日派对切蛋糕。


虚拟语气:
If I had a sharper knife, I would slice the meat more easily. = 如果我有一把更锋利的刀，我会更容易切肉。
I wish I had sliced the potatoes thinner. = 我真希望我把土豆切得薄一些。


被动语态:
The bread was sliced thinly. = 面包被切得很薄。
The vegetables are being sliced for the stir-fry. = 蔬菜正在被切丝准备炒菜。


疑问句:
How many slices of pizza did you eat? = 你吃了多少片披萨？
Will you slice the cheese for me? = 你能帮我切奶酪吗？


否定句:
I didn't slice the onions because I was crying. = 我没有切洋葱，因为我哭了。
He doesn't slice the meat very well. = 他不太会切肉。
