manics:/ˈmeɪniks/ | adj. | 狂躁的；躁狂的 | Relating to or characteristic of mania; exhibiting or characterized by excessive excitement, energy, and elation.  |  He was exhibiting manic behavior, talking rapidly and excitedly. 他表现出狂躁的行为，快速而兴奋地说话。  | 近义词：frantic, frenzied, wild, excited | 反义词：depressed, lethargic, calm, subdued | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The patient is manic today. = 病人今天很狂躁。
His behavior seems manic. = 他的行为似乎很狂躁。

一般过去时:
She was manic during the final stages of her project. = 在她项目的最后阶段，她非常狂躁。
He became manic after he stopped taking his medication. = 他停药后变得狂躁。

现在完成时:
He has been manic for several weeks. = 他狂躁了好几周了。
She has never felt so manic before. = 她以前从未感到如此狂躁。

过去完成时:
He had been manic for months before seeking treatment. = 在寻求治疗之前，他已经狂躁了几个月。
She had never experienced such manic episodes before the accident. = 在事故发生之前，她从未经历过如此狂躁的发作。

将来时:
I fear he will become manic again if he stops his medication. = 我担心如果他停药，他会再次变得狂躁。
She expects to feel manic after the stressful event. = 她预计在压力事件后会感到狂躁。


虚拟语气:
If he were manic, he would need immediate medical attention. = 如果他狂躁了，他需要立即就医。
I wish I hadn't been so manic during that presentation. = 我真希望在那个演示中我没有那么狂躁。


被动语态:
His condition was diagnosed as manic. = 他的病情被诊断为狂躁症。
The symptoms were described as manic. = 这些症状被描述为狂躁的。


疑问句:
Is he manic? = 他狂躁吗？
Does she seem manic to you? = 她看起来对你来说很狂躁吗？


否定句:
He is not manic; he's just very energetic. = 他不是狂躁；他只是精力充沛。
The doctor said she wasn't manic. = 医生说她没有狂躁。


PHRASE->
manic depression: 狂躁抑郁症
manic episode: 狂躁发作
manic-depressive: 狂躁抑郁症患者
manic phase: 狂躁期
hypomanic: 轻躁狂的 (related phrase, not a direct phrase with "manic")
