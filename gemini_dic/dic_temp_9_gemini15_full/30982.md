chaldean:/ˈkældiən/| n. adj.|迦勒底人；迦勒底的；|Chaldean refers to the people of ancient Babylonia, specifically the inhabitants of Chaldea, a southern region of Babylonia.  The term is also used to describe their language and culture.  In later usage, it sometimes refers to a  religious sect or to certain forms of astrology.|例句：The Chaldean king Nebuchadnezzar II is famous for his conquests and building projects. 迦勒底王尼布甲尼撒二世以其征服和建筑项目而闻名。


The ancient Chaldeans were renowned for their astronomical observations. 古代迦勒底人以其天文观测而闻名。|近义词：Babylonian (in some contexts)|反义词:None specifically.|名词，形容词
