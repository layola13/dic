honky-tonk:/ˈhɑːŋkiˌtɑːŋk/ | n. adj. |乡村酒吧；乡村音乐；庸俗的；俗气的 | A honky-tonk is a type of bar, typically found in the American South and West, that features country music, often with a lively and sometimes rowdy atmosphere.  It can also refer to the music itself, characterized by a raw, energetic style.  As an adjective, it describes something as being gaudy, cheap, or low-class. |例句：The band played honky-tonk music all night long. 乐队整夜演奏乡村音乐。 The honky-tonk was filled with boisterous patrons. 那家乡村酒吧里挤满了喧闹的顾客。|近义词：dive, roadhouse, honky-tonk music,  (adj.)tacky, cheap, gaudy |反义词：upscale, classy, sophisticated |名词，形容词
