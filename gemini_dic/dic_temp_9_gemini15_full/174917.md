raddle: /ˈrædəl/ | n., v. | 耙；涂抹；使激动；闲聊 |

英文解析：As a noun, a raddle refers to a tool used for spreading plaster or other material.  As a verb, it means to spread (a substance) with a raddle; to confuse or excite; or to talk idly or gossipy. The word's meanings are connected by the idea of spreading or distributing something, whether it's plaster or gossip.

例句：

(Noun) The farmer used a raddle to spread the lime across the field. = 农民用耙子把石灰撒在田里。

(Noun)  The artist used a raddle to apply the paint to the canvas. = 艺术家用耙子将颜料涂抹在画布上。


(Verb)  He raddled the plaster onto the wall. = 他用耙子把灰泥抹到墙上。

(Verb) The news raddled the community. = 这个消息使社区骚动不安。


近义词：

(Noun)  brush, spreader, applicator

(Verb)  stir, agitate, excite, chatter, gossip


反义词：

(Verb)  calm, soothe, pacify


词源：

The word "raddle" comes from the Old English word *rædel, meaning "a tool for spreading plaster". The verb usage derives from this original noun meaning.
