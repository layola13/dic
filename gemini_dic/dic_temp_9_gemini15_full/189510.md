saladin:/ˈsælədɪn/| n. |萨拉丁|Saladin (Salah ad-Din Yusuf ibn Ayyub) was a 12th-century Kurdish Muslim leader who is celebrated as a great military leader and statesman. He is most famous for his role in leading the Muslim forces against the Crusaders during the Third Crusade. His reputation is largely shaped by both Muslim and Western historical accounts, which often portray him as a chivalrous and honorable warrior, despite the context of war.  He is remembered for his military prowess, political acumen, and, according to some accounts, his relative tolerance and fairness towards conquered populations.| 例句：Saladin's victories against the Crusaders are still studied in military academies. 萨拉丁对抗十字军东征的胜利仍在军事学院学习。|近义词：leader, commander, sultan |反义词:N/A
