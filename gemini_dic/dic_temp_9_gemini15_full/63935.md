epipolism:/ɪˈpɪpəlɪzəm/| n. |附带语，叠句法|The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences, often used for emphasis or rhetorical effect.  Epipolism is a figure of speech where the same word or phrase is repeated at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences, creating a rhythmic and memorable effect.  It’s a tool often used in speeches and literature to emphasize a point or create a sense of urgency or build momentum. |Example:  "We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds, we shall fight in the fields and in the streets, we shall fight in the hills."  This is an example of epipolism, with the repetition of "we shall fight" at the beginning of each clause. |近义词：anaphora, parallelism|反义词：None specifically, but techniques that avoid repetition could be considered opposites.|名词
