mooned:/muːnd/| adj. v.| 月亮的；痴迷的；神魂颠倒的 |  Describes someone who is lost in thought, often in a dreamy or lovesick way; also can refer to something resembling the moon. |例句：He sat there, mooned, staring out the window. 他坐在那里，魂不守舍地盯着窗外。  She was completely mooned over him. 她完全迷恋他了。|近义词：dreamy, lovesick, infatuated |反义词：focused, alert, indifferent

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He moons over her every day. = 他每天都痴迷于她。
She moons about all day. = 她整天魂不守舍。


一般过去时:
He mooned over her all night. = 他整夜都在想她。
They mooned around the park. = 他们漫无目的地在公园里闲逛。


现在完成时:
He has mooned over her for months. = 他已经迷恋她好几个月了。
She has been mooning about since he left. = 自从他离开后，她一直魂不守舍。


过去完成时:
He had mooned over her for years before finally confessing his feelings. = 在最终表白感情之前，他已经迷恋她多年了。
They had mooned around aimlessly before finding their way. = 他们漫无目的地闲逛了一阵子才找到方向。


将来时:
He will moon over her until she notices him. = 他会一直迷恋她，直到她注意到他。
She will continue mooning about if he doesn't come back. = 如果他不回来，她会继续魂不守舍。


虚拟语气:
If I had known, I wouldn't have mooned over her so much. = 如果我知道的话，我就不会这么迷恋她了。
I wish I hadn't mooned about so much after the breakup. = 我真希望分手后我没有那么魂不守舍。


被动语态:
(The verb "moon" is not typically used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
Is he still mooning over his ex-girlfriend? = 他还在痴迷于他的前女友吗？
Was she mooning around when you saw her? = 你看到她的时候，她是不是魂不守舍？


否定句:
He didn't moon over her for long. = 他并没有迷恋她很久。
She didn't moon about after the good news. = 好消息传来后，她并没有魂不守舍。
