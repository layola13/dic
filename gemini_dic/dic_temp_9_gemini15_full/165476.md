preliteratures:/ˌpriːˈlɪtərətʃərz/| n. |前文学，前文学作品|The term "preliteratures" refers to oral traditions and forms of cultural expression that existed before the development of writing systems in a particular society.  These traditions often include myths, legends, folktales, songs, and rituals passed down through generations by word of mouth.  The study of preliteratures helps us understand the cultural and intellectual history of societies before they developed writing. |Example:  Scholars are increasingly recognizing the importance of studying preliteratures to understand the rich cultural heritage of many indigenous communities.  学者们越来越认识到研究前文学以了解许多土著社区丰富的文化遗产的重要性。|近义词：oral traditions, unwritten literature|反义词：literatures
