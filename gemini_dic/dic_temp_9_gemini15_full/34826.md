clinger:/ˈklɪŋər/| n. v.|粘着的人；依附者；依恋的人|One who clings; someone who is emotionally dependent on another person.|The child was a clinger, always wanting to be held.  这个孩子很粘人，总是想让人抱着。|He's become a clinger since his wife died. 他妻子去世后变得很依赖别人。|dependent, adherent|independent, self-reliant|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He clings to his old beliefs. 他坚持他的旧观念。
She clings to her childhood memories. 她依恋着童年回忆。

一般过去时:
The climber clung to the rock face. 登山者紧紧抓住岩石。
The baby clung to its mother. 婴儿紧紧抱着母亲。

现在完成时:
He has clung to power for too long. 他已经掌握权力太久了。
She has clung to hope despite the setbacks.  尽管遭遇挫折，她仍然抱有希望。

过去完成时:
He had clung to the hope of rescue. 他一直抱有获救的希望。
She had clung to her ideals throughout her life. 她一生都坚持自己的理想。

将来时:
I will cling to my principles, no matter what. 无论如何，我都会坚持我的原则。
She will cling to the railing as the boat rocks. 船摇晃时，她会抓住栏杆。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would cling to this opportunity. 如果我是你，我会抓住这个机会。
I wish I hadn't clung to those outdated ideas. 我真希望我没有坚持那些过时的想法。

被动语态:
The child was clung to by his mother.  孩子被母亲紧紧抱着。
The climber was clung to the rock face. 登山者紧紧地抓住岩石。

疑问句:
Does he still cling to his old ways? 他是否仍然坚持他的老习惯？
What is she clinging to? 她紧紧抓住的是什么？

否定句:
He doesn't cling to his beliefs as strongly as he used to. 他不像以前那样坚定地坚持自己的信仰。
She didn't cling to her anger for long. 她没有长时间地生气。


PHRASE->
cling to (something): 坚持；依恋
cling to life: 顽强地活着
cling to hope: 抱有希望
cling to one's beliefs: 坚持信仰
cling to the past: 念念不忘过去
