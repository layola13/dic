counterscale: /ˈkaʊntərskeɪl/ | n. | 反比例尺；反向比例尺 | A counterscale is a scale used in cartography or other fields where a smaller scale represents a larger area, and vice versa, in contrast to a typical scale where a larger scale represents a larger area.  It's often used to represent features that are too small to be easily shown on a standard map at a reasonable scale. | Example: The tiny details of the microchip were shown on a counterscale map, where a small distance on the map represented a large distance on the actual chip. | 近义词: inverse scale, reciprocal scale | 反义词: standard scale, direct scale | 名词
