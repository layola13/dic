phlegmatic: /flɛɡˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 冷静的；迟钝的；淡漠的 |  Characterized by a slow and unemotional temperament; apathetic.  A phlegmatic person is calm, not easily excited, and not easily upset. They may be seen as unemotional or even apathetic. |

例句：He remained phlegmatic in the face of the crisis.  | 他在危机面前依然保持冷静。

例句：Her phlegmatic nature made her a good mediator in disputes. | 她冷静的性格使她成为纠纷中一位优秀的调解人。


近义词：calm, composed, apathetic, stoic, impassive |

反义词：excitable, emotional, passionate, volatile, impulsive |

词源：From Late Latin *phlegmaticus, from Greek phlegmatikos, from phlegma "phlegm," from phlegein "to burn." The original connection was to the bodily humor phlegm, which was associated with a calm temperament.
