chiefrie:/ˈtʃiːfriː/| n. |首领的权力；领主的地位；领地|A chiefrie is the right or privilege of a chief or lord, often associated with land ownership and the authority over tenants or subjects.  It often involved the collection of dues or services from those under the chief's authority.  The term is primarily used in historical contexts, particularly referring to feudal systems. |Example:  The chiefrie granted him control over a significant tract of land and the loyalty of its inhabitants.  首领的权力使他控制了一大片土地及其居民的忠诚。|近义词：lordship, seigniory, domain|反义词：vassalage, servitude|名词
