extravagance:/ˈɛkstrævəɡəns/| n.|奢侈；浪费；铺张 |The excessive spending of money.  It often implies a lack of restraint and a disregard for the value of money. |例句：Her extravagance led to financial ruin.  她的奢侈导致了经济破产。

例句：The extravagance of the wedding was breathtaking. 婚礼的奢华令人叹为观止。


近义词：luxury, lavishness, profligacy, prodigality |反义词：frugality, thrift, economy, spareness|词性:名词

词源:
From Middle French *extravagance, from Latin *extravagantia, from extravagans, present participle of extravagari "to wander beyond," from extra "beyond" + vagari "to wander."  The sense development is from "wandering beyond bounds" to "exceeding reasonable limits" in spending.
