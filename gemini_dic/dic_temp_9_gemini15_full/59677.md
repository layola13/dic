echolocation:/ˌɛkoʊloʊˈkeɪʃən/| n.|回声定位|The process by which animals emit sounds and then use the echoes to locate objects and navigate. This is particularly common in bats, dolphins, and some birds.  It involves emitting sound waves and interpreting the returning echoes to determine the distance, direction, size, and shape of objects.| Example: Bats use echolocation to navigate in the dark and find prey.  蝙蝠利用回声定位在黑暗中导航并寻找猎物。|sonar, biosonar|None|名词
