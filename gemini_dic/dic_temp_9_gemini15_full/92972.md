idioblast:/ˈɪdiəʊblæst/| n. |特异细胞，异形细胞|A cell that is structurally different from the surrounding cells in a tissue or organ.  Idioblasts often contain specialized substances, such as crystals, tannins, or oils, and may have a variety of functions, including defense, storage, or secretion. |Example: Calcium oxalate idioblasts are common in many plant species.  许多植物物种中普遍存在草酸钙特异细胞。

The presence of idioblasts significantly altered the leaf's microstructure. 特异细胞的存在显著改变了叶片的微观结构。|近义词：specialized cell, aberrant cell|反义词：typical cell, normal cell
