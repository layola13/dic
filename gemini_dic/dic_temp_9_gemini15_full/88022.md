high-ups:/ˈhaɪ ʌps/| n. |要员；高层人士|Important people in an organization or government.  The term implies a position of power and influence. |例句：The high-ups in the company decided to merge with a competitor. 公司高层决定与竞争对手合并。

例句：The high-ups are meeting to discuss the new policy. 高层正在开会讨论新政策。


近义词：bigwigs, top brass, executives, leaders, officials|反义词：low-level employees, underlings


词源:  The word is a compound of "high" (meaning elevated or superior in rank) and "ups" (a shortened form of "upsiders" or referring to those in higher positions).  It's informal and colloquial.
