downsizing:/ˈdaʊnˌsaɪzɪŋ/ | n. |公司裁员；缩减规模；减少规模 | The process of reducing the size of something, especially a company or organization, typically by reducing the number of employees.  | Example: The company announced a major downsizing, resulting in hundreds of job losses. 公司宣布大规模裁员，导致数百人失业。 |近义词：reduction, cutback, streamlining |反义词：expansion, growth


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company is downsizing its workforce. = 公司正在裁员。
They are downsizing their operations. = 他们正在缩减业务规模。


一般过去时:
The factory downsized last year. = 工厂去年进行了裁员。
They downsized their marketing department. = 他们缩减了市场营销部门的规模。


现在完成时:
The company has downsized significantly in recent years. = 近年来，该公司已大幅度缩减规模。
They have downsized their product line. = 他们已经精简了产品线。


过去完成时:
The company had downsized before the merger. = 在合并之前，该公司已经进行了裁员。
They had downsized their staff before the economic downturn. = 经济衰退之前，他们已经裁减了员工。


将来时:
The company will downsize its operations next year. = 公司明年将缩减业务规模。
They will downsize their workforce to cut costs. = 他们将裁员以削减成本。


虚拟语气:
If the company downsized, it would save money. = 如果公司裁员，它就能省钱。
I wish the company hadn't downsized. = 我希望公司没有裁员。


被动语态:
The workforce was downsized by the company. = 公司裁减了员工。
The project was downsized due to budget constraints. = 由于预算限制，项目规模被缩减了。


疑问句:
Is the company downsizing? = 公司正在裁员吗？
Will they be downsizing soon? = 他们很快会裁员吗？


否定句:
The company is not downsizing. = 公司没有裁员。
They did not downsize their department. = 他们没有裁减他们部门的规模。


PHRASE->
downsizing the workforce = 裁员
downsizing the budget = 削减预算
downsizing the company = 公司缩减规模
downsizing the operation = 缩减业务
downsizing the product line = 精简产品线
