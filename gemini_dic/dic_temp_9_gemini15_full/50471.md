depressants:/dɪˈprɛsənts/ | n. |镇静剂；抑制剂 | Substances that reduce arousal or stimulation, typically affecting the central nervous system. They can slow down brain activity, resulting in effects such as relaxation, drowsiness, or slowed reflexes.  Examples include alcohol, opioids, and benzodiazepines. | Example: Alcohol and barbiturates are common depressants. 酒精和巴比妥类药物是常见的镇静剂。|近义词：sedatives, tranquilizers |反义词：stimulants
