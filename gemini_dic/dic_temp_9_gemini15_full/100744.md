/ɪnˈtrænzɪtɪv/| adj. |不及物的| intransitive means (of a verb) not having a direct object.  An intransitive verb describes an action or state of being that does not affect anything else.  For example, the verb "sleep" is intransitive because the subject (the person sleeping) is not acting upon anything else. |The bird sings beautifully.|近义词：non-transitive|反义词：transitive|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sleeps soundly. = 他睡得很香。
The sun rises in the east. = 太阳从东方升起。

一般过去时:
She walked quickly. = 她走得很快。
The rain fell heavily. = 雨下得很大。

现在完成时:
They have lived here for ten years. = 他们在这里住了十年了。
The birds have flown south. = 鸟儿们飞到南方去了。

过去完成时:
He had slept for eight hours. = 他睡了八个小时。
The meeting had ended before I arrived. = 我到达之前会议已经结束了。

将来时:
We will travel to Europe next year. = 我们明年将去欧洲旅行。
It will rain tomorrow. = 明天会下雨。

虚拟语气:
If I were a bird, I would fly. = 如果我是一只鸟，我就会飞。
I wish I could sleep longer. = 我希望我能睡久一点。

被动语态:
(Intransitive verbs generally cannot be used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
Does she sing? = 她唱歌吗？
Where did they go? = 他们去哪儿了？


否定句:
He doesn't sleep well. = 他睡不好。
The plants didn't grow well this year. = 今年植物长得不好。


PHRASE->
(Intransitive verbs don't typically form phrases in the same way transitive verbs do.  Phrases involving intransitive verbs often include adverbials describing the manner, time, or place of the action.)
