dug: /dʌɡ/ | v. | 挖掘；挖出；探索；深入研究 | To excavate or remove earth; to search thoroughly; to investigate deeply. | 例句：The dog dug a hole in the garden. = 狗在花园里挖了个洞。

The archaeologists dug up an ancient artifact. = 考古学家挖出了古代文物。


近义词：excavate, unearth, delve, explore | 反义词：fill, bury, cover


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He digs a hole every day. = 他每天挖一个洞。
They dig for gold in the mountains. = 他们在山上淘金。


一般过去时:
She dug a deep trench. = 她挖了一条深沟。
We dug up the potatoes yesterday. = 我们昨天挖了土豆。


现在完成时:
They have dug a well in the desert. = 他们在沙漠里挖了一口井。
The dog has dug up all my plants! = 狗把我的所有植物都挖出来了！


过去完成时:
They had dug the foundation before the rain started. = 在下雨之前他们已经挖好了地基。
We had dug all the holes before sunset. = 我们在日落之前已经挖好了所有洞。


将来时:
They will dig a new canal next year. = 他们明年将开凿一条新运河。
We will dig deeper to find the treasure. = 我们将更深入地挖掘以寻找宝藏。


虚拟语气:
If I had a shovel, I would dig a bigger hole. = 如果我有一把铲子，我会挖一个更大的洞。
I wish I had dug deeper. = 我真希望我挖得更深一点。


被动语态:
The hole was dug by the children. = 这个洞是孩子们挖的。
The treasure was dug up last week. = 宝藏是上周挖出来的。


疑问句:
Did they dig the tunnel? = 他们挖了隧道吗？
Where did you dig for clams? = 你在哪里挖蛤蜊？


否定句:
They didn't dig the well. = 他们没有挖井。
He didn't dig up any evidence. = 他没有挖出任何证据。


PHRASE->
dig in: 开始吃饭；努力工作
dig up: 挖掘；找出；发现
dig into: 深入研究；探究
dig oneself out of: 摆脱困境
dig one's heels in: 固执己见
