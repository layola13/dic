moderating:/ˈmɒdəreɪtɪŋ/ /ˈmɑːdəreɪtɪŋ/| v.| 调解；缓和；主持；节制 |  The act of making something less extreme, intense, or severe; controlling or regulating something.  It can also refer to chairing a meeting or discussion. | 例句：The government is moderating its economic policies. 政府正在缓和其经济政策。

The teacher is moderating the class discussion. 老师正在主持课堂讨论。


近义词：regulating, controlling, tempering, mediating, chairing

反义词：exacerbating, intensifying, inflaming, unrestrained


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is moderating the debate. 他正在主持辩论。
She moderates her spending. 她节制开支。


一般过去时:
The mediator moderated the conflict between the two parties. 调解员调解了双方之间的冲突。
The chairman moderated the meeting effectively. 主席有效地主持了会议。


现在完成时:
They have moderated their tone in recent communications.  他们在最近的沟通中缓和了语气。
She has moderated her diet to lose weight. 她已经节制饮食以减肥。


过去完成时:
He had moderated his expectations before the meeting. 他在会议前已经降低了预期。
The committee had moderated the proposal before submitting it.  委员会在提交提案之前已经对其进行了修改。


将来时:
The judge will moderate the proceedings. 法官将主持诉讼程序。
He will moderate his alcohol consumption. 他将节制饮酒。


虚拟语气:
If he moderated his language, the argument would have ended sooner. 如果他语气缓和一些，争论就会早点结束。
I wish she had moderated her criticism. 我希望她能缓和她的批评。


被动语态:
The discussion was moderated by a neutral party. 讨论由中立方主持。
The temperature was moderated by the cool breeze.  凉爽的微风使温度降低了。


疑问句:
Who is moderating the online forum? 谁在主持在线论坛？
Is the government moderating its stance on immigration? 政府是否正在缓和其对移民的立场？


否定句:
He did not moderate his anger. 他没有控制住他的怒火。
They failed to moderate the speed of the vehicle.  他们未能控制车辆的速度。

词源:
The word "moderating" comes from the Latin word "moderāri," meaning "to restrain, regulate, or keep within bounds."  It entered English through Old French.
