overrating:/ˌoʊvərˈreɪtɪŋ/ | n. |过高评价，高估|The act of rating something too highly; assigning excessive value or importance to something.  It often implies a misjudgment or lack of objectivity.|例句：The overrating of the film led to disappointed audiences.  这部电影的过高评价导致观众失望。 |例句：His overrating of his own abilities ultimately led to his failure. 他对自身能力的过高评价最终导致了他的失败。|近义词：overestimation, exaggeration, inflation|反义词：underrating, underestimation

词源:
The word "overrating" comes from the verb "overrate," which is formed by adding the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") to the verb "rate."  "Rate" itself has origins in Old French and ultimately Latin, referring to the act of assessing or valuing something.
