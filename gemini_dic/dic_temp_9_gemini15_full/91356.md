hydrorhiza:/ˈhaɪdroʊraɪzə/| n.| 水根；匍匐根状茎|The hydrorhiza is a creeping, root-like structure found in certain aquatic organisms, such as hydroids and some sea anemones.  It serves to anchor the organism and spread it across a substrate. |The hydrorhiza of the hydroid colony anchored itself to the rock. 水螅群体的水根固定在岩石上。 The extensive hydrorhiza allowed the anemone to cover a large area of the reef. 广泛的水根使海葵覆盖了大片礁石区域。| rhizome, stolon|  (反义词较难定义，因为其功能本身没有直接的反义)
