/ˌnɒnˈsɪbɪlənts/ | /ˌnɑːnˈsɪbələnts/ | n. |非塞擦音|Nonsibilants are consonants that are not sibilants.  They are produced without the use of a groove in the tongue, which is characteristic of sibilants.  Examples include /p/, /b/, /t/, /d/, /k/, /ɡ/, /m/, /n/, /ŋ/, /l/, /r/, and /w/.|The study of phonetics often distinguishes between sibilants and nonsibilants.|近义词：non-fricatives (in some contexts)|反义词：sibilants
