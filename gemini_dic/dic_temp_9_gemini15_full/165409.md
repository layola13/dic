prelanguage:/ˈpriːlæŋɡwɪdʒ/| n. |前语言；前语言阶段|A hypothetical stage of language development preceding the emergence of fully developed human language.  It refers to the communicative systems used by pre-human ancestors or very early humans, which lacked the complexity and structure of modern languages. These systems might have included gestures, calls, and proto-linguistic vocalizations.|Example:  The study of primate communication offers clues to understanding the potential characteristics of prelanguage.|近义词：protolanguage, early communication system|反义词：language
