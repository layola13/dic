externalism:/ɪkˈstɜːrnəlɪzəm/ | n. | 外在主义；外部论 | The philosophical view that mental states are determined by external factors, such as the environment and social context, rather than solely by internal factors, such as beliefs and desires.  It contrasts with internalism. | Example:  Externalism is a prominent theory in the philosophy of mind.  外部论是心智哲学中的一个重要理论。| 近义词：environmentalism (in some contexts) | 反义词：internalism |
