词源:
From Late Latin *freneticus, from Greek phrenētikos, from phrēn "mind, diaphragm," related to phren "mind, heart."  The original meaning was "mad," "insane."  The current meaning evolved to refer to intense excitement and agitation, still retaining a sense of being somewhat uncontrolled.
