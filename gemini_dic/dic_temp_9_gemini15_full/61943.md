emulsic:/ɪˈmʌlsɪk/| n.|乳化剂|An emulsifier is a substance that stabilizes an emulsion, preventing the two liquids from separating. Emulsifiers are commonly used in food processing to create smooth, stable mixtures of oil and water, such as mayonnaise and ice cream.|Example:  Lecithin is a common emulsifier used in many food products. 卵磷脂是许多食品中常用的乳化剂。|近义词：emulsifying agent, stabilizer|反义词：None specifically, but substances that *prevent* emulsification could be considered antonyms.|名词
