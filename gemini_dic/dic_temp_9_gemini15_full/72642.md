flysch:/flɪʃ/| n.|弗利施(一种沉积岩)|Flysch is a thick succession of sedimentary rocks, predominantly turbidites, that are typically found in geosynclines.  It is characterized by rhythmic layering and rapid deposition.  The layers often show graded bedding, with coarser material at the bottom and finer material at the top. This indicates deposition from turbidity currents.|The flysch deposits in the Alps are famous for their impressive thickness and complex structure.|近义词：turbidite sequence|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with  "molasses" (another type of sedimentary sequence)|名词
