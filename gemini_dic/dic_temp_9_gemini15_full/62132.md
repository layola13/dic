encirclement: /ɪnˈsɜːrkəlmənt/ | n. |包围，围困，环绕 | The act of surrounding something or someone completely; the state of being surrounded.  It often implies a military or strategic context, but can also be used more generally. |例句：The enemy's encirclement of the city led to its surrender.  敌人的包围导致了城市的投降。

The encirclement of the protestors by police prevented further demonstrations. 警方的包围阻止了进一步的示威活动。


近义词：surround, siege, blockade, envelopment, investment | 反义词：liberation, release, escape


词源:  From the verb "encircle" + "-ment" (a suffix forming nouns that signify the action, process, state, or result of). "Encircle" itself comes from "en-" (meaning "in" or "into") + "circle".
