intercedent:/ˌɪntərˈsiːdnt/| n.| 介入者；调解人；代祷者 | An intercedent is a person who intervenes on behalf of another.  They act as a mediator or go-between, often to plead a case or resolve a conflict.  This role can be formal, like a lawyer or diplomat, or informal, like a friend mediating a dispute. | Example: The union leader acted as an intercedent in the labor dispute, helping to negotiate a compromise between the workers and management. |近义词：mediator, arbitrator, advocate, intermediary |反义词：adversary, opponent
