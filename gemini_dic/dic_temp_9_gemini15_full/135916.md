nonpuerile: /ˌnɒnpˈjuːraɪl/ | adj. | 不幼稚的；成熟的 | Not childish; mature.  The novel's themes were surprisingly nonpuerile for a writer so young.  | 近义词：mature, adult, sophisticated, grown-up | 反义词：childish, immature, puerile


英文解析：The word "nonpuerile" is formed by adding the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") to the adjective "puerile," which means childish or immature. Therefore, "nonpuerile" signifies the absence of childishness or immaturity; it implies a level of maturity and sophistication.


例句：

His nonpuerile understanding of complex issues impressed everyone. = 他对复杂问题的成熟理解给每个人留下了深刻的印象。

The film's nonpuerile approach to its subject matter made it appealing to a wider audience. = 这部电影对主题的成熟处理使其吸引了更广泛的观众。


词源:

* **non-:**  A prefix meaning "not."  Derived from Latin.
* **puerile:** From Latin *puer* meaning "boy" or "child."  The suffix *-ile* forms adjectives.


用法说明：

"Nonpuerile" is primarily used as an adjective to describe things or people that lack childishness. It suggests a degree of intellectual or emotional maturity, often contrasting with something expected to be immature or simple-minded.  It's a relatively formal word and might be less common in everyday conversation than synonyms like "mature" or "sophisticated."
