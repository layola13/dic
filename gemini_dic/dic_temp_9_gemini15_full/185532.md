revaluation:/ˌriːvæljuˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 再估价；重新评估；重新评价 | The act of reassessing or reevaluating something, typically its worth or value.  This can apply to assets, currencies, or even ideas and beliefs. |例句：The company underwent a revaluation of its assets after the merger. 合并后，该公司对其资产进行了重新估价。


例句：The government announced a revaluation of the currency. 政府宣布对货币进行重新估价。


近义词： reassessment, reevaluation, reappraisal | 反义词：devaluation


词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government conducts regular revaluations of its assets. = 政府定期对其资产进行重新估价。
A revaluation of the property is necessary before the sale. = 销售前有必要对房产进行重新估价。


一般过去时:
The bank revalued its holdings last quarter. = 银行上个季度对其资产进行了重新估价。
They revalued the painting after discovering it was an original. = 他们在发现这幅画是原作后对其进行了重新估价。


现在完成时:
The company has revalued its stock several times this year. = 今年该公司已经多次对其股票进行了重新估价。
The economists have revalued the impact of the new policy. = 经济学家们已经重新评估了新政策的影响。


过去完成时:
The auditors had revalued the accounts before submitting their report. = 审计员在提交报告之前已经对账目进行了重新估价。
They had revalued their investment strategy after the market crash. = 市场崩盘后，他们已经重新评估了他们的投资策略。


将来时:
The company will revalue its properties next year. = 公司明年将对其房产进行重新估价。
The central bank will likely revalue the currency soon. = 中央银行可能会很快重新估价货币。


虚拟语气:
If they had revalued the property earlier, they would have made more money. = 如果他们早些对房产进行重新估价，他们就会赚更多钱。
I wish they had revalued the situation before acting. = 我希望他们在采取行动之前已经重新评估了局势。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
The government did not revalue the currency. = 政府没有对货币进行重新估价。
