serai:/sərˈaɪ/| n.| 驿站；客栈；宫殿；（尤指印度的）官邸 | A serai is a type of building, often found in the Middle East and India, that served as a rest stop for travelers, often including accommodation and sometimes stables for animals.  Historically, they were often built by governments or wealthy individuals to provide shelter and hospitality. In modern usage, the term can also refer to a large and opulent house or palace, particularly in India. | Example: The weary travelers sought refuge in the ancient serai along the Silk Road.  疲惫的旅行者在丝绸之路上的古老驿站寻求庇护。  The maharaja's serai was renowned for its lavish gardens and intricate architecture.  大君的官邸以其奢华的花园和复杂的建筑而闻名。|近义词：caravansary, inn, hostel, palace |反义词：None directly applicable.
