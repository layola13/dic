mummies:/ˈmʌmiz/ | n. |木乃伊；干尸 |A mummy is a dead body that has been preserved, usually by embalming, especially in ancient Egypt.  |例句：The museum has a fascinating collection of Egyptian mummies.  博物馆收藏了令人着迷的埃及木乃伊。

例句：Scientists are studying the DNA of ancient mummies to learn more about their lives. 科学家们正在研究古代木乃伊的DNA，以了解更多关于他们生活的信息。


近义词：corpse, remains

反义词：living, alive


词源：From Middle English *mumie, from Old French momie, from Late Latin mumia, from Arabic mūmiyā "bitumen," "mummy," ultimately of uncertain origin, perhaps from Persian mum "wax."  The original sense referred to a kind of bitumen used medicinally, which later became associated with embalmed bodies.
