enactments:/ɪˈnæktmənts/ | n. | 法令；法规；制定；颁布 | The new enactments aim to improve road safety.  新的法令旨在改善道路安全。 | 近义词：ordinances, statutes, laws, regulations | 反义词：repeals, abolitions


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government enacts new laws every year. = 政府每年都制定新的法律。
These rules are enacted to protect the environment. = 制定这些规章是为了保护环境。

一般过去时:
The parliament enacted the bill last week. = 议会在上周通过了该法案。
They enacted a new tax law to increase revenue. = 他们颁布了一项新的税法以增加收入。

现在完成时:
They have enacted several important reforms recently. = 他们最近实施了几项重要的改革。
The legislature has enacted many environmental protection laws. = 立法机关已经颁布了许多环境保护法。

过去完成时:
By the time the crisis hit, they had enacted emergency measures. = 当危机来临时，他们已经颁布了紧急措施。
Before the scandal, they had enacted strict rules against bribery. = 在丑闻爆发之前，他们已经颁布了严格的反贿赂规定。


将来时:
The council will enact new building codes next month. = 理事会将于下个月颁布新的建筑规范。
They will enact stricter penalties for traffic violations. = 他们将对交通违规行为处以更严厉的处罚。

虚拟语气:
If they enacted stricter laws, crime rates might fall. = 如果他们制定更严格的法律，犯罪率可能会下降。
I wish they had enacted this policy sooner. = 我希望他们早点实施这项政策。

被动语态:
New regulations are being enacted to control pollution. = 正在颁布新的规章以控制污染。
The law was enacted quickly to address the emergency. = 该法案迅速获得通过以应对紧急情况。

疑问句:
What enactments are currently under consideration? = 目前正在审议哪些法令？
When will the new enactments come into effect? = 新的法令何时生效？

否定句:
The government did not enact the proposed tax increase. = 政府没有颁布提议的税收增加。
They have not yet enacted the necessary reforms. = 他们尚未实施必要的改革。
