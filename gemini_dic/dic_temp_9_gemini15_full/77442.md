gawkiness:/ˈɡɔːkɪnəs/| n. |笨拙；尴尬；傻气 |A lack of grace; awkwardness.  It often refers to clumsy movements or social awkwardness. |例句：His gawkiness made him the target of teasing. 他的笨拙使他成为嘲弄的目标。

例句：There was a certain gawkiness in his attempt to dance. 他试图跳舞时显得有些笨拙。


近义词：awkwardness, clumsiness, ungainliness, gracelessness |反义词：grace, elegance, dexterity, sophistication |词性：名词


词源:  The noun "gawkiness" derives from the verb "gawk," meaning to stare openly and stupidly.  "Gawk" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old English or Old Norse words suggesting staring or gaping.  The "-iness" suffix adds the quality or state of being gawky.
