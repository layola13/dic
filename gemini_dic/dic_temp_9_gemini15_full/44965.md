crystallized:/ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 已结晶的；明确的；定型的 |  Describes something that has become solid and formed crystals, or something that has become fixed or definite in form.  | Example: The sugar solution crystallized into beautiful rock candy. 糖溶液结晶成美丽的冰糖。  The artist's style crystallized over many years. 艺术家的风格经过多年才定型。|近义词：solidified, defined, fixed | 反义词：amorphous, undefined, fluid |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sugar crystallizes quickly in cold weather. = 糖在寒冷的天气里结晶很快。
This idea crystallizes my thoughts on the subject. = 这个想法使我对这个问题的看法清晰起来。


一般过去时:
The solution crystallized overnight. = 溶液一夜之间结晶了。
His vision for the company crystallized after years of planning. = 他对公司愿景经过多年的规划后才最终确定。


现在完成时:
The scientists have crystallized the protein for further study. = 科学家们已经将蛋白质结晶化以便进一步研究。

过去完成时:

By the end of the meeting, their plan had crystallized. = 到会议结束时，他们的计划已经确定下来。

His ideas will crystallize as he gains more experience. = 随着他获得更多经验，他的想法将会更加清晰。
