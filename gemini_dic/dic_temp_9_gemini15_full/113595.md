/ˌmæsɪˈdoʊniənɪzəm/ | n. | 马其顿主义 | The ideology and political movement advocating for the rights and interests of Macedonians, often involving claims to a distinct Macedonian national identity and territory.  It encompasses various viewpoints on Macedonian history, language, culture, and political aspirations, sometimes overlapping with or diverging from Pan-Slavism or other regional ideologies.  The term can be used neutrally or pejoratively depending on context. | The rise of Macedonianism in the late 19th and early 20th centuries led to the creation of a separate Macedonian identity distinct from Bulgarian, Greek, or Serbian identities. | 近义词：Macedonian nationalism, Macedonian identity | 反义词： None directly opposite, but could be considered opposed to ideologies denying Macedonian distinctness, such as certain interpretations of Bulgarian or Greek nationalism.
