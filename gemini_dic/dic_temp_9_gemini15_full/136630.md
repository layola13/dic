/nɒnˈskærsɪti/ | n. |非匮乏；充足；充裕 |The concept refers to a situation where there is an abundance of resources or goods, such that they are readily available and not in short supply.  It contrasts with scarcity, which implies limited availability and competition for resources.  Nonscarcity often implies a system of production or distribution that ensures sufficient supply to meet demand. |The abundance of food in the supermarket demonstrated a nonscarcity of resources.  超市里食物的丰富显示了资源的充裕。| abundance, plenty, superfluity | scarcity, shortage, deficiency | 名词
