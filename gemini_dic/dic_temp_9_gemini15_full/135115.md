nonobviously:/ˌnɒnˈɒbviəsli/ | adv. | 不明显地，并非显而易见地 |  It describes something that is not readily apparent or easily understood.  It implies a degree of hiddenness or subtlety. | The solution to the problem was nonobviously linked to the initial data.  这个问题的解决方法与初始数据并非显而易见地相关。  | obviously, clearly, evidently | obviously, clearly, evidently |


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and as such doesn't have tense conjugations like verbs.  Its usage is primarily descriptive, modifying a verb, adjective, or another adverb.


例句1: He nonobviously hinted at his dissatisfaction with the project. 他含蓄地暗示他对这个项目的不满。

例句2: The connection between the two events was nonobviously subtle.  这两个事件之间的联系微妙得并不明显。


PHRASE->
This word does not readily form common phrases in the same way as verbs or nouns.  Its function is primarily to modify existing phrases.  Examples of its use within phrases:


例句1: A nonobviously effective strategy. 一个并非显而易见但行之有效的策略。

例句2: The nonobviously superior choice.  一个并非显而易见但更优的选择。

例句3:  Nonobviously connected ideas.  并非显而易见地相关的想法。

例句4: It was nonobviously better than the first design. 它比第一个设计要好，但这并非显而易见。

例句5:  A nonobviously simple solution. 一个看似不简单但实际上简单的解决方案。
