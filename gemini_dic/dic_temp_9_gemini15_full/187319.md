rocketed:/ˈrɒkɪtɪd/ /ˈrɑːkɪtɪd/| v.|飞速上升；迅速增长；发射|To move or increase very quickly.|The company's profits rocketed after the launch of their new product. 公司在推出新产品后利润飞速增长。|increased rapidly, soared, surged|decreased, declined, plummeted|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The price of gas rockets every year. = 天然气价格每年都在飞涨。
His career rockets upwards. = 他的事业扶摇直上。


一般过去时:
The stock market rocketed after the good news. = 好消息传来后，股市飙升。
Sales rocketed during the holiday season. = 假日期间销售额激增。


现在完成时:
The value of my investments has rocketed this year. = 我今年的投资价值已经大幅上涨。
The popularity of that band has rocketed since their last album. = 自从他们发行上一张专辑以来，该乐队的知名度已经迅速蹿升。


过去完成时:
The temperature had rocketed before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，温度已经飙升。
The number of applicants had rocketed before the deadline. = 截止日期前申请人数激增。


将来时:
The price of oil is predicted to rocket next year. = 预测明年油价将飞涨。
The company's profits are expected to rocket in the coming quarter. = 预计公司利润将在下一个季度大幅增长。


虚拟语气:
If the economy improved, the stock market would rocket. = 如果经济好转，股市将会飙升。
I wish my salary would rocket! = 我希望我的工资能大幅上涨！


被动语态:
The rocket was rocketed into space. = 火箭被发射到太空。 (This example uses the word as both verb and noun, showing the unusual reflexive usage.)
The sales figures were rocketed by the new marketing campaign. = 新的营销活动使销售数据大幅增长。(This example uses a passive voice to emphasize the effect on sales.)


疑问句:
Did the price of bitcoin rocket last year? = 比特币价格去年飙升了吗？
Will his fame rocket after the movie release? = 电影上映后他的名气会大增吗？


否定句:
The company's share price did not rocket as expected. = 公司股价并未如预期般飙升。
The number of tourists did not rocket last summer. = 去年夏天游客数量并没有激增。
