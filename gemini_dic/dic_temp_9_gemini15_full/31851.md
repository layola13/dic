cheered:/tʃɪrd/| v.|欢呼；喝彩；使高兴|To shout with joy or approval; to make someone happy or excited.|例句：The crowd cheered wildly when the home team scored.  观众在主队得分时疯狂欢呼。|例句：The good news cheered her up.  好消息让她高兴起来。|近义词：applauded, acclaimed, celebrated|反义词：booed, jeered


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cheers for his favorite team every game. = 他每场比赛都为最喜欢的球队加油。
They cheer loudly at the concert. = 他们在音乐会上大声欢呼。


一般过去时:
The audience cheered enthusiastically after the performance. = 演出结束后，观众热情地鼓掌欢呼。
She cheered up when she received the gift. = 收到礼物后她高兴起来。


现在完成时:
The fans have cheered their team to victory many times. = 粉丝们多次为他们的球队欢呼庆祝胜利。
We have cheered ourselves hoarse shouting.  我们喊哑了嗓子。


过去完成时:
They had cheered before the final whistle blew. = 在终场哨响之前，他们就已经欢呼了。
She had cheered herself up before going to the party. = 她去参加聚会之前已经让自己高兴起来了。


将来时:
The crowd will cheer when the singer comes on stage. = 当歌手上台时，人群将会欢呼。
I think they will cheer the news. = 我认为他们会为这个消息欢呼。


虚拟语气:
If the team won, the fans would cheer. = 如果球队赢了，球迷们就会欢呼。
I wish they would cheer me on. = 我希望他们能为我加油。


被动语态:
The team was cheered on by their fans. = 球队受到了球迷的加油助威。
The actor was cheered by the audience. = 演员受到了观众的热烈欢呼。


疑问句:
Did the crowd cheer? = 人群欢呼了吗？
Will they cheer for the underdog? = 他们会为弱者加油吗？


否定句:
The crowd didn't cheer at all. = 人群根本没有欢呼。
He didn't cheer her up. = 他没有让她高兴起来。
