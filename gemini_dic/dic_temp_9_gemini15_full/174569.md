quipu:/ˈkiːpuː/| n. |基普，结绳记事法 |A quipu is an ancient Andean device used for record-keeping.  It consists of colored strings knotted in various patterns to represent numbers, quantities, and possibly even words or concepts.  The precise method of interpretation remains debated by scholars. | Example:  The Inca civilization used quipu to record census data, agricultural yields, and taxes.  印加文明用基普记录人口普查数据、农业产量和税收。 |近义词：knotted record, mnemonic device |反义词：written record, digital record
