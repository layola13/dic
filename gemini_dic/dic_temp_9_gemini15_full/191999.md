scoring:/ˈskɔːrɪŋ/ | n. |得分，记分；评分；刻痕 |The act of assigning points or marks, or the points or marks assigned.  It can also refer to the process of making marks or scratches on a surface. |例句：The scoring of the exam was very strict. 考试评分非常严格。

The referee's scoring was controversial. 裁判的评分是有争议的。


近义词：marking, rating, assessment | 反义词：N/A


词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The team is scoring well. = 这支球队得分很高。
He is scoring points for his team. = 他正在为他的球队得分。


一般过去时:
She scored many goals in the match. = 她在比赛中进了很多球。

词源:
The word "scoring" comes from the verb "score," which ultimately derives from the Old Norse word "skor," meaning "notch" or "cut."  The meaning evolved to include the act of making notches to keep count, hence its use in keeping track of points in games and assessments.
