masterpiece:/ˈmɑːstəpiːs/| /ˈmæstəpiːs/| n. |杰作，名作 |A masterpiece is a creative work, such as a painting, novel, or piece of music, that is considered to be of exceptionally high quality and great skill.  It is often a product of the artist's greatest talent and effort, and it typically endures through time. | Shakespeare's Hamlet is considered a masterpiece of English literature.  莎士比亚的《哈姆雷特》被认为是英国文学的杰作。

The Mona Lisa is a masterpiece of Renaissance art.  蒙娜丽莎是文艺复兴艺术的杰作。

|近义词：classic, magnum opus, triumph |反义词：failure, flop, botch

词源:
The word "masterpiece" originates from the Old French word "maistrise piece," which literally translates to "master's piece."  The "master" refers to a skilled craftsman or artist, and "piece" refers to a work of art or creation.  Therefore, a masterpiece was originally understood as a work of exceptional quality produced by a master of their craft.
