plantership:/ˈplæntərʃɪp/| n. |种植园主身份；种植园制度；种植园主与种植园的关系|The relationship between a planter and his plantation, including the social and economic aspects of plantation life.  Plantership refers to the system of plantation agriculture and the role of the planter within that system. It often carries connotations of colonial history and the exploitation of labor.|例句：The plantership in the Caribbean relied heavily on enslaved labor. 加勒比海地区的种植园制度严重依赖奴隶劳动。|例句：The study explored the complexities of plantership in the Southern United States. 这项研究探讨了美国南部种植园制度的复杂性。|近义词：plantation system, colonial system|反义词：None specifically, but concepts like abolition or worker empowerment could be considered contrasting.
