corroborative:/kəˈrɒbəreɪtɪv/ | adj. | 确证的；证实的；支持的 |  Giving support or evidence to a statement, theory, or finding; confirming.  | 例句：The witness's testimony provided corroborative evidence for the prosecution's case.  证人的证词为检方的案情提供了确证证据。 | 例句：The results of the experiment were corroborative of the original hypothesis. 实验结果证实了最初的假设。 | 近义词：confirmatory, supportive, confirming, supportive, substantiative | 反义词：contradictory, refuting, disproving


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The evidence is corroborative of his alibi. = 证据证实了他的不在场证明。
This finding is corroborative of previous research. =这一发现证实了先前的研究。


一般过去时:
The witness's statement corroborated the suspect's story. =证人的陈述证实了嫌疑人的说法。
The historical documents corroborated the legend. =历史文献证实了这个传说。


现在完成时:
Several studies have corroborated the findings of this research. =几项研究已经证实了这项研究的结果。
Recent discoveries have corroborated the theory. =最近的发现证实了这个理论。


过去完成时:
The additional evidence had corroborated the initial findings. =额外的证据已经证实了最初的发现。
By then, several studies had corroborated his hypothesis. =到那时，几项研究已经证实了他的假设。


将来时:
Further testing will corroborate the results. =进一步的测试将证实结果。
We expect that future research will corroborate our findings. =我们预计未来的研究将证实我们的发现。


虚拟语气:
If more evidence corroborated the theory, it would be widely accepted. =如果更多证据证实了该理论，它将被广泛接受。
I wish more studies had corroborated the results. =我希望有更多研究证实这些结果。


被动语态:
His alibi was corroborated by several witnesses. =他的不在场证明得到了几位证人的证实。
The theory is corroborated by recent research. =这一理论得到了近期研究的证实。


疑问句:
Does this evidence corroborate the defendant's claim? =这证据是否证实了被告的主张？
What evidence corroborates this theory? =什么证据证实了这个理论？


否定句:
The new evidence did not corroborate the original hypothesis. =新的证据并没有证实最初的假设。
The witness's testimony failed to corroborate the defendant's story. =证人的证词未能证实被告的说法。


PHRASE->
corroborative evidence = 确证证据
corroborative data = 佐证数据
corroborative testimony = 证实证词
corroborative findings = 确证结果
corroborative study = 确证性研究

词源:
From Latin *corroboratīvus, from corroborare "to strengthen," from cor- "together" + robur "strength, oak."
