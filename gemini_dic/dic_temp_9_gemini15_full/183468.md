repudiated:/rɪˈpjuːdieɪtɪd/ | v. | 否认；拒绝；抛弃；与…断绝关系 | To refuse to accept or be associated with; to reject; to disown.  | The politician repudiated the allegations of corruption.  这位政治家否认了腐败指控。 |  deny, reject, renounce, disavow | accept, acknowledge, embrace, approve | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He repudiates his past actions. = 他否认他过去的行动。
She repudiates the claims made against her. = 她否认了针对她的指控。

一般过去时:
The government repudiated the treaty. = 政府拒绝承认那项条约。
He repudiated his former beliefs. = 他放弃了他以前的信仰。

现在完成时:
They have repudiated their earlier promises. = 他们已经违背了他们先前的承诺。
She has repudiated the accusations against her. = 她已经否认了针对她的指控。

过去完成时:
He had repudiated his family before he left. = 他离开之前已经与家人断绝关系了。
The company had repudiated the contract before the deadline. = 公司在截止日期前已经拒绝承认该合同。

将来时:
She will repudiate the offer. = 她将拒绝这个提议。
He will repudiate the agreement. = 他将拒绝这项协议。

虚拟语气:
If he repudiated the deal, he would face serious consequences. = 如果他拒绝这笔交易，他将面临严重的后果。
I wish he had repudiated the rumors earlier. = 我希望他早点否认那些谣言。

被动语态:
The treaty was repudiated by the government. = 这项条约被政府否决了。

疑问句:

否定句:
He did not repudiate the evidence. = 他没有否认证据。
She has not repudiated her support for the project. = 她并没有撤回对该项目的支持。
