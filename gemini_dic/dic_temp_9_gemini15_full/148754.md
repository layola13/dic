overture:/ˈoʊvərˌtʃər/| n.|序曲，开场白，前奏|An overture is an introductory piece of music, especially one at the beginning of an opera or oratorio.  It can also refer to a preliminary step or action that suggests what is to follow.|例句：The orchestra played a dramatic overture before the opera began.  乐队在歌剧开始前演奏了一首戏剧性的序曲。

The company's new marketing campaign is an overture to a major product launch.  公司的新的市场营销活动是重大产品发布的序曲。|近义词：prelude, introduction, preface|反义词：conclusion, finale

The orchestra plays a beautiful overture. = 乐队演奏一首优美的序曲。
This action serves as an overture to further negotiations. = 此举是进一步谈判的序曲。


一般过去时:
The conductor led the orchestra in a powerful overture. = 指挥带领乐队演奏了一首气势磅礴的序曲。
He made an overture of peace to his enemy. = 他向他的敌人发出了和平的倡议。


现在完成时:

She has made several overtures to join the club. = 她已经多次表示想加入俱乐部。


过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
If the overture were longer, the audience might be bored. = 如果序曲更长，观众可能会感到厌倦。
