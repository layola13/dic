invariant:/ɪnˈveriənt/ | adj. | 不变的；恒定的 |  An invariant is something that remains unchanged under certain transformations or conditions.  It is a constant or a property that does not vary.  For example, in geometry, the angles of a triangle always add up to 180 degrees, regardless of the size of the triangle. This is an invariant property of triangles. | The speed of light is an invariant in Einstein's theory of relativity.  光速是爱因斯坦相对论中的一个不变量。 The laws of physics are assumed to be invariant under certain transformations.  人们假设物理定律在某些变换下是不变的。 | constant, unchanging, stable | variant, changeable, mutable |  adj.
