hunkering:/ˈhʌŋkərɪŋ/| v.|蹲伏；蜷缩；顽强抵抗|The act of crouching or squatting low to the ground, often for protection or warmth.  It can also imply perseverance or resistance in the face of difficulty.|例句：The hikers hunkered down behind a rock to avoid the storm.  徒步旅行者们躲在一块岩石后面躲避暴风雨。

例句：Despite the setbacks, the team hunkered down and continued working towards their goal. 尽管遭遇挫折，团队仍然坚持不懈地努力实现目标。|近义词：crouching, squatting, squatting down,  sheltering,  persevering|反义词：standing, rising, surrendering

USAGE->

一般现在时:
He hunkered down by the fire. = 他在火炉旁蹲下。
They hunker down during storms. = 他们在暴风雨期间躲起来。

一般过去时:
She hunkered down behind a tree. = 她躲在一棵树后面。
We hunkered down in the basement. = 我们躲在地下室里。

现在完成时:
He has hunkered down all day. = 他整天都蹲在那里。
They have hunkered down and waited patiently. = 他们已经蹲下耐心地等待了。

过去完成时:
They had hunkered down before the rain started. = 在雨开始下之前，他们就已经蹲下了。
She had hunkered down for hours before help arrived. = 在得到帮助之前，她已经蹲了几个小时了。


将来时:
We will hunker down if the weather turns bad. = 如果天气变坏，我们将躲起来。
They will hunker down to weather the crisis. = 他们将坚持度过危机。


虚拟语气:
If it started to rain, we would hunker down immediately. = 如果开始下雨，我们将立即躲起来。
I wish I had hunkered down earlier. = 我真希望我早点躲起来。


被动语态:
(Hunker is rarely used in the passive voice because it is an intransitive verb.)


疑问句:
Did they hunker down during the attack? = 他们在袭击期间躲起来了吗？
Will you hunker down if it snows? = 如果下雪，你会躲起来吗？


否定句:
They didn't hunker down during the fire. = 他们在火灾期间没有躲起来。
He wouldn't hunker down, even when told to. = 即使被告知，他也不肯躲起来。


PHRASE->

hunker down: 躲起来；坚持不懈
hunker down for the winter: 为过冬做准备
hunker down and wait: 蹲下等待
hunker down in the trenches: 在战壕里坚守
hunker down to work: 埋头苦干


词源:

The word "hunker" likely originated as a dialectal variant of "hooch" or "hunch," both referring to postures of bending or crouching.  The suffix "-er" transforms the word into a verb.  Its precise etymology is still debated, but the connection to crouching and bending is clear.
