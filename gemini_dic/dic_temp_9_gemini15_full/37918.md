complementizers:/ˌkɒmplɪˈmɛntəz/| n. | 补语成分，连接词 | Complementizers are words or phrases that introduce a complement clause.  They function as subordinating conjunctions, linking a dependent clause to a main clause. Common examples include "that," "whether," "if," "for," and "because."  They signal the type of complement clause following them (e.g., declarative, interrogative).|  He said that he was tired. 他说他累了。|  I wonder whether it will rain.  我想知道是否会下雨。|  近义词：subordinating conjunctions | 反义词：N/A
