cognatus:/kɒɡˈneɪtəs/| n.|同源词，同族词，血亲|A cognate is a word that has a common ancestor with another word in a different language.  It's often used in linguistics to describe words that share a common etymological origin and often have similar meanings. |Example: The English word "brother" and the German word "Bruder" are cognates.|近义词：relative (in the context of language), kin (in the context of language)|反义词：N/A
