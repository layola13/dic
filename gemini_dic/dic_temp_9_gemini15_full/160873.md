post-Cartesian:/ˌpoʊst kɑːrˈtiʒən/| adj.|后笛卡尔的；后笛卡尔主义的 |  Referring to philosophical perspectives and approaches that developed after the influential work of René Descartes.  These perspectives often challenge, revise, or build upon Cartesian ideas, particularly concerning the mind-body problem, epistemology, and metaphysics. | Example: Post-Cartesian philosophers explored alternative models of consciousness beyond the mind-body dualism. 后笛卡尔哲学家探索了超越身心二元论的意识替代模型。 | 近义词：post-dualist, anti-Cartesian | 反义词：Cartesian
