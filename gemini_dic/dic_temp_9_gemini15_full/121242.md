miserhood:/ˈmaɪzərhʊd/ | n. |吝啬; 贪婪; 卑鄙 |The state or quality of being a miser; extreme stinginess.  It often implies not only a lack of generosity but also a preoccupation with accumulating wealth and a reluctance to spend money even on necessities. |例句：His miserhood was legendary in the town; he wouldn't even spend a penny on a warm meal.  他的吝啬在镇上是出了名的；他甚至舍不得花一分钱买一顿热饭。

His miserhood prevented him from enjoying the simple pleasures of life. 他的吝啬使他无法享受生活中的简单快乐。|近义词：stinginess, avarice, parsimony, greed |反义词：generosity, munificence, philanthropy |词性 n.
