pauperization:/ˌpɔːpəraɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |贫困化；使成贫民；使穷困 |The process of becoming impoverished or the act of making someone impoverished.  It often refers to a large-scale societal phenomenon where a significant portion of the population falls into poverty. |例句：The rapid industrialization led to widespread pauperization in the urban areas.  快速的工业化导致城市地区普遍贫困化。

例句：The economic crisis resulted in the pauperization of many families. 经济危机导致许多家庭贫困化。


近义词：impoverishment, impoverishing, destitution, deprivation

反义词：enrichment, prosperity, affluence


词源:  From "pauper" + "-ization". "Pauper" originates from the Old French word "pauvre," meaning "poor," which itself comes from the Latin word "pauper," also meaning "poor."  The suffix "-ization" indicates the process of becoming or making something.
