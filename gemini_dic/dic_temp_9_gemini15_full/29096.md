caryopsis:/kæriˈɑːpsɪs/| n. |颖果；谷粒|A caryopsis is a type of simple dry fruit in which the pericarp (fruit wall) is fused to the single seed.  It's characteristic of grasses (Poaceae) and is commonly known as a grain.  Examples include wheat, rice, corn, and barley.|The wheat caryopsis is a staple food for billions of people. |grain, kernel|  (no direct antonym)|名词
