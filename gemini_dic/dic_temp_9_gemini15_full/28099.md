CAPITALISED:/ˈkæpɪtəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 大写的；资本化的 |  Having initial capital letters; converted into capital.  | Example: The title of the book was capitalised. 这本书的标题是大写的。 |近义词：uppercase, uppercased | 反义词：lowercase, lowercased


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The word is capitalised. = 这个词是大写的。
He capitalises on his strengths. = 他利用自己的优势。


一般过去时:
The sentence was capitalised incorrectly. = 这句话的大写不正确。
She capitalised on the opportunity. = 她抓住了这个机会。


现在完成时:
They have capitalised all the proper nouns. = 他们已经将所有专有名词都大写了。
The company has capitalised on the new technology. = 公司已经利用了这项新技术。


过去完成时:
He had capitalised the first letter before submitting the document. = 他在提交文件之前已经将首字母大写了。
The investors had capitalised on the market downturn. = 投资者已经利用了市场低迷。


将来时:
She will capitalise the title of her essay. = 她将把她的论文标题大写。
They will capitalise on the growing market. = 他们将利用不断增长的市场。


虚拟语气:
If I capitalised the word, it would look better. = 如果我把这个词大写，看起来会更好。
I wish I had capitalised the proper nouns. = 我希望我之前已经把专有名词大写了。


被动语态:
The word was capitalised by the editor. = 这个词是由编辑大写的。
The opportunity was capitalised upon by the entrepreneur. = 这个机会被企业家利用了。


疑问句:
Is the word capitalised correctly? = 这个词的大写正确吗？
Was the investment capitalised effectively? = 这项投资是否有效地进行了资本化？


否定句:
The word is not capitalised. = 这个词没有大写。
He did not capitalise on the situation. = 他没有利用这种情况。


PHRASE->
capitalised cost = 资本化成本
fully capitalised = 完全资本化的
capitalised expenditure = 资本性支出
capitalised value = 资本化价值
to capitalise on something = 利用某事
