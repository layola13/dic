rise:/raɪz/ | vi. | 上升，上涨，起床 | to move upwards; to increase in amount, value, or level; to get out of bed | The sun rises in the east. 太阳从东方升起。| The price of oil has risen sharply. 油价大幅上涨。|近义词：ascend, climb, increase | 反义词：fall, descend, decrease | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun rises in the east every day. = 太阳每天都在东方升起。
The water level rises during the rainy season. = 雨季水位上涨。


一般过去时:
The balloon rose into the sky. = 气球升上了天空。
He rose from his chair to greet the guests. = 他从椅子上站起来迎接客人。


现在完成时:
The temperature has risen significantly today. = 今天的温度已经显著上升了。
Sea levels have risen due to global warming. = 由于全球变暖，海平面上升了。


过去完成时:
The water had risen to dangerous levels before the dam broke. = 在水坝决堤之前，水位已经上升到危险的程度。
By the time I arrived, the crowd had risen to their feet. = 当我到达的时候，人群已经起立了。


将来时:
The stock prices will rise next week, hopefully. = 希望下周股价会上涨。
The river will rise after the heavy rain. = 大雨过后河水将会上涨。


虚拟语气:
If the temperature rose any higher, it would be unbearable. = 如果温度再高一些，就无法忍受了。
I wish the sun would rise earlier in winter. = 我希望冬天太阳能早点升起。


被动语态:
The flag was risen at dawn. = 旗帜在黎明时分升起。
The dough was risen after two hours.  = 面团在两个小时后发酵起来了。


疑问句:
Does the sun rise in the west? = 太阳在西方升起吗？
Will the price of gas rise soon? = 油价很快会上涨吗？


否定句:
The balloon didn't rise high enough. = 气球没有升得足够高。
The tide didn't rise as much as expected. = 潮水涨幅不如预期。


PHRASE->
rise and fall = 起伏
rise to the occasion = 应对挑战
rise above = 克服
rise and shine = 早起
rise to power = 上台掌权
