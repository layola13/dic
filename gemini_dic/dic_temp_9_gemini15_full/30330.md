censurer: /ˈsɛnzə(r)/ | n. | 责备者；批评者 | A person who censures or criticizes severely.  | The censurer was relentless in his attacks on the politician's policies.  批评者对这位政治家的政策进行了无情的攻击。 | critic, reprover, condemner | supporter, praiser | 名词

一般现在时:
The professor censures students for plagiarism. = 教授因剽窃而责备学生。
He censures his friend's bad behavior. = 他责备朋友的不良行为。


一般过去时:
The newspaper censured the mayor for his handling of the crisis. = 报纸因市长处理危机的方式而谴责他。
She censured him for his rudeness. = 她因他的粗鲁而责备他。


现在完成时:
The committee has censured the company for its unethical practices. = 委员会因其不道德行为而谴责该公司。
He has censured her many times for the same mistake. = 他已经多次因同样的错误而责备她。


过去完成时:
The board had censured him before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，董事会已经谴责了他。
They had censured her work numerous times before she finally improved. = 在她最终改进之前，他们已经多次批评了她的作品。


将来时:
The judge will censure the lawyer for his unprofessional conduct. = 法官将因律师的不专业行为而谴责他。
We will censure him for his unacceptable behavior. = 我们将因他不可接受的行为而谴责他。


虚拟语气:
If he had been more careful, he wouldn't have been censured. = 如果他更仔细一些，就不会受到谴责。
I wish they hadn't censured her so harshly. = 我希望他们不要对她如此严厉地批评。


被动语态:
The politician was censured by the public for his lies. = 这位政治家因说谎而受到公众的谴责。
His work was censured by the critics for its lack of originality. = 他的作品因缺乏原创性而受到评论家的批评。


疑问句:
Was the teacher censured for her actions? = 老师因为她的行为而受到谴责吗？
Will he be censured for his misconduct? = 他会因自己的不当行为而受到谴责吗？


否定句:
The committee did not censure the employee for his mistake. = 委员会没有因为员工的错误而谴责他。
She wasn't censured for her outspoken remarks. = 她并没有因为直言不讳的言论而受到谴责。
