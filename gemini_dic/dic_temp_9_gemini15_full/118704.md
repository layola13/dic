metacism:/ˈmɛtəˌsɪzəm/| n. | 变音，音变 | The substitution of one sound for another, especially a change in the pronunciation of a word or syllable, often involving a shift in the place or manner of articulation.  This can be a systematic change in a language over time, or a casual error in speech. | Example: The pronunciation of "pin" as "bin" is an example of metacism.  元音/辅音的音位替换，常指发音变化，例如把“pin”读成“bin”。|近义词：sound change, phonetic change, pronunciation variation | 反义词：No direct antonym, but the opposite would be consistent pronunciation.
