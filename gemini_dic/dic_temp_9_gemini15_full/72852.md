folkland:/ˈfoʊklænd/| n.|全民土地，公地|Land held in common by a folk or community, typically under customary law, as opposed to privately owned land (bocland).  In Anglo-Saxon England, it referred to land belonging to the community and available for use by freemen, rather than being under the direct ownership of the king or the church.|The villagers relied on the folkland for grazing their animals and growing crops.|近义词：common land, communal land|反义词：bocland, private land
