semigovernmentally: /ˌsɛmiɡʌvərˈmɛntəli/ | adv. | 半官方地，半政府地 |  Relating to or characteristic of something that is partly government-controlled or operated.  It implies a level of involvement or influence from a government entity, but not complete control. |

例句：

The semigovernmentally funded research institute published its findings.  = 这个半官方资助的研究机构发表了它的研究结果。

The organization operates semigovernmentally, receiving both public and private funding. = 该组织以半官方的方式运作，同时接受公共和私人资金。


近义词：quasi-officially, partly officially, partially governmentally

反义词：privately, independently, fully publicly


词源:

* **semi-**:  A prefix meaning "half," "partly," or "partially."  Derived from Latin *semi*.

* **governmentally**: An adverb derived from "government," ultimately from Old French *gouvernment*, from Latin *gubernaculum* ("helm, rudder").  The suffix "-ally" indicates manner or characteristic.
