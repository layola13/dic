re-identification:/ˌriːˌaɪdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |重新识别，再识别 |The process of identifying a person or object again, often after a period of time or after some change in appearance or context.  This is commonly used in surveillance, security, and data privacy contexts. For example, re-identifying an individual from anonymized data or matching a face to a previous sighting. |Example: The police conducted a re-identification of the suspect using facial recognition technology.  警方使用面部识别技术对嫌疑人进行了重新识别。

The suspect was successfully re-identified through his unique gait. 嫌疑人通过其独特的步态被成功地重新识别。|近义词：re-recognition, identification (in some contexts)|反义词：anonymization, de-identification

WORD ORIGIN->
The word "re-identification" is a compound word formed from the prefix "re-" (meaning "again" or "anew") and the noun "identification" (the act of identifying someone or something). "Identification" itself comes from the verb "identify," derived from the Late Latin word "identificare," meaning "to make identical."  The Latin roots are "idem" (same) and "facere" (to make).
