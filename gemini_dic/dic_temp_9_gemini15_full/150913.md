parallelism:/ˈpærəˌlɛlɪzəm/ | n. | 平行；并列；相似；对称 | The quality or state of being parallel;  a similarity or correspondence between two things. It often refers to the grammatical or structural similarity between sentences or parts of sentences.  In literature, it's a stylistic device used for emphasis, rhythm, and clarity. | Example:  "He came, he saw, he conquered." (This sentence demonstrates parallelism through the repetition of the subject-verb structure.) | 近义词：similarity, correspondence, analogy, symmetry | 反义词：dissimilarity, contrast, asymmetry
