caryatid: /ˈkæriətaɪd/ | n. | 女像柱；負重女神像 | A sculpted female figure serving as an architectural support, typically a column or pillar.  Caryatids are often depicted in flowing robes and are used as decorative elements in classical architecture.  They represent a supporting female figure, often a mythological being. | The Erechtheion on the Acropolis in Athens features iconic caryatids.  雅典卫城上的埃雷赫提翁神庙以其标志性的女像柱而闻名。|  column, pillar, support |  (no direct antonym, but could be contrasted with a male figure acting as support, or a purely architectural support like a column without figurative representation)| 名词
