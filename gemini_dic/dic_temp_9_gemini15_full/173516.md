quasi-fully:/ˈkwɑːziˌfʊli/ | adj. | 准完全的；几乎完全的 |  Describes something that is almost completely or nearly fully something, but not quite entirely.  It implies a degree of incompleteness or imperfection. | The system is quasi-fully automated, with only minor manual intervention required.  这个系统几乎是全自动的，只需要少量人工干预。|近义词：almost fully, nearly complete, largely complete | 反义词：incomplete, partially, wholly

词源:
The word "quasi" is derived from the Latin word "quasi," meaning "as if," "as it were," or "almost."  "Fully" is a straightforward English word meaning "completely" or "entirely."  The combination creates a nuanced adjective indicating near-completion but not absolute completeness.
