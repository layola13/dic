quasi-honorable: /ˈkwɑːziˈɒnərəbl/ /ˈkwɑːziˈɑːnərəbl/ | adj. | 准荣誉的；似是而非的荣誉的 |  Almost but not quite honorable; seeming honorable but lacking true honor.  It suggests a pretense or superficiality of honor. |例句：His actions, while seemingly beneficial, were deemed quasi-honorable at best, raising questions about his true motives.  他的行为虽然看似有益，但充其量只能算作准荣誉的，让人质疑他的真实动机。

His quasi-honorable discharge from the army left him feeling bitter and unappreciated. 他从军队获得的准荣誉退伍令他感到痛苦和不被赏识。


近义词：seemingly honorable, nominally honorable, pseudo-honorable | 反义词：dishonorable, shameful, disgraceful


词源:  The word is a combination of "quasi" (meaning "seemingly," "apparently," or "to a certain extent") and "honorable" (meaning "worthy of respect and admiration").  "Quasi" comes from Latin, while "honorable" has Old French roots.
