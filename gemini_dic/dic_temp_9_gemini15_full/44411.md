cross-line:/ˈkrɒsˌlaɪn/ | n. |交叉线，跨线，越界 | A line that intersects another; a transgression of a boundary or limit.  | Example: The cross-line on the map indicated the border between two countries. 地图上的交叉线表示两国之间的边界。  |近义词：intersection, crossing, boundary, transgression |反义词：parallel line, conformity


USAGE->

The cross-line shows the division. = 交叉线显示了分界线。
This project crosses lines of authority. = 这个项目越过了权限界限。

一般过去时:
The runner crossed the finish line. = 跑步者越过了终点线。

现在完成时:
She has crossed the line several times. = 她已经几次越界了。
