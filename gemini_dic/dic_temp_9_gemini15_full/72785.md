foils:/fɔɪlz/ | n. v. | 箔；挫败；衬托 |

英文解析：
As a noun, "foil" refers to a very thin sheet of metal, often used in packaging or decoration.  It can also refer to a person or thing that contrasts with and so emphasizes the qualities of another. As a verb, "foil" means to prevent (something considered wrong or undesirable) from succeeding.

例句：
The chocolate was wrapped in aluminum foil.  = 巧克力用铝箔纸包裹着。

The detective's quick thinking foiled the robber's plan. = 侦探的快速思考挫败了强盗的计划。


近义词：
(n.) leaf, sheet, thwart, contrast, setback
(v.) thwart, frustrate, prevent, counter, defeat


反义词：
(n.)  enhancement, support, complement
(v.) assist, aid, enable
