crestfallen: /ˈkrestˌfɔːlən/ | adj. | 垂头丧气的；沮丧的 | dejected and depressed in spirit |

英文解析: Crestfallen describes someone who is very disappointed and discouraged, often because of a failure or setback.  It suggests a visible display of dejection, often involving a drooping posture or a downcast expression. The word evokes a feeling of being let down or defeated.

例句:

He was crestfallen after losing the championship game. = 他在输掉冠军赛后非常沮丧。

She looked crestfallen when she didn't get the promotion. = 她没有得到晋升时看起来很沮丧。


近义词: dejected, despondent, dispirited, discouraged, downcast, disheartened

反义词: elated, cheerful, joyful, triumphant, buoyant


词源:  The word comes from the image of a bird with its crest (a tuft of feathers on its head) fallen or drooping.  This visual metaphor perfectly captures the feeling of dejection and low spirits associated with being crestfallen.  The word's origin is a combination of "crest" (meaning the top of a bird's head or a wave) and "fallen" (meaning having dropped or slumped).
