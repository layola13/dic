passed-on:/pæstˈɔːn/| adj. v. | 传下来的，去世的，传递 | Passed-on refers to something that has been transmitted from one person or generation to another, or to someone who has died.  It can function as an adjective describing something inherited or a verb describing the act of transmitting something or the act of dying. |例句：The family's traditions have been passed on for generations.  这个家族的传统已经传承了几代。

The old man passed on peacefully in his sleep. 老人安详地睡梦中去世了。|近义词：transmitted, inherited, deceased, departed |反义词：newly acquired, living, extant


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He passes on his wisdom to his students. 他把他的智慧传授给他的学生们。
She passes on the information to her colleagues. 她把信息传递给她的同事们。

一般过去时:
They passed on the good news to their parents. 他们把好消息告诉了他们的父母。
He passed on last night. 他昨晚去世了。

现在完成时:
The legacy has been passed on through many years. 这份遗产已经传承多年。
She has passed on the family recipes. 她已经把家里的食谱传下来了。

过去完成时:
The story had been passed on from generation to generation. 这个故事一代一代地流传下来。
Before he passed on, he had made his will.  在他去世之前，他已经立下了遗嘱。

将来时:
I will pass on the message as soon as possible. 我会尽快转达这个消息。
He will pass on soon, the doctor said. 医生说他很快就会去世。

虚拟语气:
If he had passed on the information earlier, the problem could have been avoided. 如果他早点传递信息，问题就可以避免了。
I wish I hadn't passed on that opportunity. 我真希望我没有错过那个机会。

被动语态:
The secret was passed on to only a few people. 这个秘密只传给了少数人。
The family heirloom will be passed on to the eldest son. 这件传家宝将会传给长子。

疑问句:
Has the news been passed on yet? 消息已经传达了吗？
Did he pass on peacefully? 他是安详地去世的吗？

否定句:
He didn't pass on the important details. 他没有传递重要的细节。
She didn't want to pass on her responsibilities. 她不想把责任传给别人。
