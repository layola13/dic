colloquy: /ˈkɒləkwi/ | n. | 谈话；对话；讨论 | A formal conversation or discussion; a dialogue.  | The colloquy between the two leaders lasted for over an hour.  两位领导人的谈话持续了一个多小时。 | dialogue, conversation, discussion |  monologue, silence | noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
A lively colloquy occurs between the two scholars. = 两位学者之间进行了一场热烈的讨论。
The students engage in a polite colloquy after class. = 课后学生们进行了一次礼貌的谈话。


一般过去时:
A significant colloquy took place at the summit. = 峰会上进行了一次重要的对话。
The two friends held a lengthy colloquy about their future plans. =  两位朋友就他们的未来计划进行了长时间的讨论。


现在完成时:
They have had several colloquies about the project's feasibility. = 他们就该项目的可行性进行过几次讨论。
The committee has completed a series of colloquies on the proposed legislation. = 委员会已经就拟议中的立法完成了一系列的讨论。


过去完成时:

After the incident, the police had a colloquy with the suspect. = 事故发生后，警方与嫌疑人进行了一次谈话。


将来时:
They will have a colloquy on the matter tomorrow. = 他们明天将就此事进行一次谈话。
The panel will hold a colloquy to discuss the new policy. =  小组将举行一次讨论会来讨论新的政策。


虚拟语气:
If only there had been a more open colloquy. = 要是有更公开的对话就好了。
It would have been helpful if they'd had a preliminary colloquy. = 如果他们事先进行了一次预备性会谈，那就有帮助了。


被动语态:
The colloquy was recorded for posterity. =  这次谈话被记录下来以供后世参考。

疑问句:

否定句:
