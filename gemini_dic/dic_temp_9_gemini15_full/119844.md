mildewy:/ˈmɪldiː/| adj.|发霉的，霉臭的|Covered with or affected by mildew; musty.  The adjective describes something that has mildew on it or shows the effects of mildew, often with an unpleasant smell.|例句：The old book smelled mildewy. 这本旧书闻起来有霉味。

The damp basement had a distinctly mildewy odor.潮湿的地下室有一种明显的霉味。|近义词：moldy, musty, fusty|反义词：fresh, clean


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bread is mildewy. = 面包发霉了。
The towels smell mildewy. = 毛巾闻起来有霉味。

一般过去时:
The clothes were mildewy after being left in the rain. = 衣服淋雨后发霉了。
The old painting was mildewy and faded. = 那幅旧画发霉了而且褪色了。

现在完成时:
The basement has become mildewy since the leak. = 自从漏水以来，地下室变得发霉了。
The shoes have gotten mildewy from being stored improperly. = 鞋子因为储存不当而发霉了。

过去完成时:
The walls had been mildewy for years before we renovated. = 在我们装修之前，墙壁已经发霉多年了。
The food had become mildewy before we even opened the package. = 我们还没打开包装，食物就已经发霉了。

将来时:
The clothes will be mildewy if you don't dry them properly. = 如果你不把衣服晒干，它们就会发霉。
The stored grain will likely become mildewy in this humid climate. = 在这种潮湿的气候下，储存的谷物很可能会发霉。

虚拟语气:
If the room were properly ventilated, it wouldn't be so mildewy. = 如果房间通风良好，就不会这么发霉了。
I wish the damp cellar wasn't so mildewy. = 我希望潮湿的地窖不要这么发霉。

被动语态:
The leather was mildewed by the damp air. = 皮革被潮湿的空气弄发霉了。
The books were found to be mildewed after being stored in the attic for years. = 这些书被发现多年储存在阁楼后已经发霉了。

疑问句:
Is the bread mildewy? = 面包发霉了吗？
Are the old books mildewy? = 这些旧书发霉了吗？

否定句:
The new furniture isn't mildewy. = 新家具没有发霉。
The stored goods were not mildewy because they were properly packaged. = 储存的货物没有发霉，因为它们包装得很好。

词源:
From Middle English *mildiw, from Old English *mildew, of unknown origin; possibly related to  *mild, "gentle," due to the soft appearance of mildew.  The modern spelling and pronunciation evolved over time.
