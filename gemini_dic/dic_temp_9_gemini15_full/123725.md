moons:/muːnz/ | n. | 月亮；卫星；（尤指）月球 |  A celestial body that revolves around a planet.  It can also refer to a period of time or a phase of life. |例句：The moon shone brightly in the night sky. 月亮在夜空中明亮地照耀着。

The harvest moon is a beautiful sight.  丰收月是一个美丽的景象。


近义词：satellite, orb | 反义词：sun (in the context of celestial bodies)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The moon shines brightly tonight. = 今晚月亮很明亮。
The moons of Jupiter are fascinating to study. = 木星的卫星非常有趣。


一般过去时:
The moon rose at 7 pm last night. = 昨晚七点月亮升起来了。
We saw a full moon last night. = 昨晚我们看到了满月。


现在完成时:
The astronauts have walked on the moon. = 宇航员已经登上月球了。
Scientists have discovered many moons orbiting other planets. = 科学家们已经发现了围绕其他行星运行的许多卫星。


过去完成时:
The tide had already turned before the moon set. = 月落之前潮水就已经退了。
They had never seen such a large moon before. = 他们以前从未见过这么大的月亮。


将来时:
The moon will be full next week. = 下周将是满月。
We will see the moon eclipse next month.  = 下个月我们将看到月食。


虚拟语气:
If the moon were closer, the tides would be higher. = 如果月亮更近，潮汐就会更高。
I wish the moon would stay out longer tonight. = 我希望今晚月亮能待久一点。


被动语态:
The moon's surface has been photographed extensively. = 月球表面已被广泛拍摄。
The moon's gravitational pull is felt on Earth. = 地球感受到月球的引力。


疑问句:
Is the moon visible tonight? = 今晚能看到月亮吗？
How many moons does Saturn have? = 土星有多少卫星？


否定句:
