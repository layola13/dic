primping:/ˈprɪmpɪŋ/| n. |打扮；梳妆打扮 |The act of spending time making oneself look attractive, typically by adjusting one's appearance.  |例句：She spent an hour primping before the party. 她花了1个小时在派对前打扮自己。
|例句：His primping before the mirror was quite a spectacle. 他对着镜子打扮的样子真是奇观。|近义词：preening, grooming, beautifying |反义词：neglecting, slovenliness |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She primps every morning before work. = 她每天早上上班前都会打扮一番。
He primps his hair before every date. = 他每次约会前都会整理头发。

一般过去时:
She primped for hours before the wedding. = 婚礼前她打扮了好几个小时。
He primped himself in front of the mirror. = 他对着镜子打扮自己。

现在完成时:
She has primped herself for the big interview. = 她为了重要的面试已经打扮好了自己。
He has primped and preened all morning. = 他整个上午都在打扮和梳理。

过去完成时:
She had primped meticulously before going onstage. = 上台前她已经细致地打扮好了自己。
He had primped and polished his shoes before the meeting. = 会议前他已经擦亮了鞋子并打扮好了自己。

将来时:
She will primp for the gala tonight. = 今晚她将为晚会打扮自己。
He will primp and preen before meeting his new boss. = 他将在会见新老板之前打扮一番。

虚拟语气:
If she had more time, she would primp more. = 如果她有更多时间，她会打扮得更漂亮。
I wish I had time to primp before the meeting. = 我希望我有时间在会议前打扮一下。

被动语态:
(Primping is rarely used in the passive voice because it is usually an active process.)

疑问句:
Is she primping for a special occasion? = 她是在为某个特殊场合打扮吗？
Does he usually primp this much? = 他通常会打扮这么多吗？

否定句:
She didn't primp at all before going to the grocery store. = 去杂货店之前她根本没有打扮。
He doesn't usually primp before going to work. = 他通常上班前不会打扮。
