preinsinuations: /ˌpriːɪnˌsɪnjuˈeɪʃənz/ | n. | 预先暗示；暗中提示；先兆 | Hints or suggestions made beforehand, often subtly or indirectly, implying something negative or unpleasant.  Preinsinuations often create a negative atmosphere or prejudice before a full explanation or event.  | The preinsinuations about the project's failure created a sense of doom among the team.  项目失败的预先暗示给团队带来了一种厄运感。 | implications, suggestions, foreshadowings | open declarations, direct statements | 名词
