rummer:/ˈrʊmər/| n.|谣言，传闻|A rumour is a piece of information that is not confirmed to be true. It is often spread from person to person, and it can be about anything.|例句：I heard a rumour that he's leaving the company. 我听说他将要离开公司。|近义词：gossip, report, hearsay|反义词：fact, truth|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rumour spreads quickly. = 谣言传播很快。
A new rumour circulates among the employees. = 一个新的谣言在员工中流传。


一般过去时:
The rumour spread like wildfire. = 谣言像野火一样迅速蔓延。
She denied the rumour yesterday. = 她昨天否认了这个谣言。


现在完成时:
The rumour has reached many people. = 这个谣言已经传到了很多人那里。
Have you heard the rumour about the merger? = 你听说过关于合并的谣言吗？


过去完成时:
The rumour had already spread before the official announcement. = 在官方宣布之前，谣言就已经传开了。
They had dismissed the rumour as unfounded. = 他们已经驳斥了这个谣言，认为它没有根据。


将来时:
The rumour will likely reach the media soon. = 这个谣言很可能会很快传到媒体上。
I think the rumour will die down eventually. = 我认为这个谣言最终会平息。


虚拟语气:
If the rumour were true, it would be devastating. = 如果谣言是真的，那将是毁灭性的。
I wish the rumour wasn't spreading so fast. = 我希望这个谣言不要传播得这么快。


被动语态:
The rumour was spread by a disgruntled employee. = 这个谣言是由一个不满的员工散布的。
The rumour is being investigated by the authorities. = 这个谣言正在由当局调查。


疑问句:
Is there any truth to the rumour? = 这个谣言有几分真实性吗？
What is the rumour about? = 这个谣言是关于什么的？


否定句:
The rumour is not true. = 这个谣言不是真的。
I don't believe that rumour. = 我不相信那个谣言。
