consonant:/ˈkɒnsənənt/ /ˈkɑːnsənənt/| n. |辅音|A consonant is a speech sound that is not a vowel.  It is produced by partially or completely blocking the airflow through the vocal tract.  Consonants are characterized by features such as voicing (whether the vocal cords vibrate), place of articulation (where in the vocal tract the sound is produced), and manner of articulation (how the airflow is obstructed).|The letter 'b' is a consonant.|近义词：stop (in phonetics)|反义词：vowel|名词
