merger:/ˈmɜːrdʒər/ | n. | 合并；兼并 | The combination of two or more commercial companies.  A merger often involves one company absorbing another, with the surviving entity continuing operations under its own name or a newly created name.  This process can be driven by various factors, including achieving economies of scale, expanding market share, accessing new technologies or resources, or eliminating competition. | Example: The merger of the two banks created the largest financial institution in the country.  = 两家银行的合并创造了该国最大的金融机构。


The proposed merger will significantly increase our market share. = 这项拟议中的合并将显著提高我们的市场份额。


近义词：consolidation, amalgamation, union | 反义词：division, separation, split | 词性:名词

词源:

The word "merger" emerged in the late 19th century. It's derived from the verb "merge," which itself comes from the Old French word "merger," meaning "to plunge, sink, or immerse."  The sense of combining or uniting gradually developed from this original meaning.
