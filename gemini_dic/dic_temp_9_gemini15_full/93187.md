ignominy:/ˈɪɡnəmɪni/| n. |耻辱；不名誉|The state of being disgraced or dishonored; shame.  It often refers to public disgrace or humiliation. |例句：His ignominy was complete when he was publicly expelled from the club. 他被俱乐部公开除名，彻底蒙受了耻辱。

例句：The ignominy of defeat was hard to bear.  失败的耻辱难以承受。


近义词：dishonor, disgrace, shame, humiliation, opprobrium|反义词：honor, glory, prestige


词源:
From Latin *ignominia, from in- "not" + nomen "name"  (originally meaning "without a name," implying lack of reputation or recognition, leading to disgrace).  The loss of a good name signifies a loss of honor and respect.
