centralize:/ˈsɛntrəlaɪz/| vt. |使集中；中央集权化 |To bring or concentrate (power, control, etc.) into one central place or authority.|The company decided to centralize its operations in Chicago. 公司决定将其业务集中在芝加哥。|decentralize, disperse|localize, decentralize|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They centralize all their marketing efforts. = 他们把所有的市场营销工作都集中起来。
The government centralizes its power. = 政府权力集中。


一般过去时:
The company centralized its production last year. = 公司去年将生产集中起来。
The empire centralized its administration in the capital. = 帝国将行政管理中心设在首都。


现在完成时:
The government has centralized control over the media. = 政府已经控制了媒体。
Many businesses have centralized their data storage. = 许多企业已经将其数据存储集中起来。


过去完成时:
The kingdom had centralized its power before the war. = 王国在战争爆发前已经集权。
Before the merger, the companies hadn't centralized their operations. = 在合并之前，这些公司没有集中其业务。


将来时:
They will centralize their resources for the new project. = 他们将把资源集中到新的项目中。
The company will centralize its customer service department next year. = 公司明年将把客户服务部门集中起来。


虚拟语气:
If they centralized their efforts, they would achieve more. = 如果他们集中精力，他们会取得更大的成就。
It would be better if the company centralized its operations. = 如果公司业务集中起来会更好。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
