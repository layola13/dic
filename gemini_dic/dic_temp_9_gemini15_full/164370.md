preemotional:/ˌpriːɪˈmoʊʃənəl/ | adj. | 前情绪的；情感之前的；先于情绪的 |  Describes a state or process that occurs before the emergence of fully developed emotions.  It often refers to the physiological or neurological precursors to emotional experience.  | Example:  The baby exhibited preemotional behaviors such as increased heart rate and facial tension before crying.  婴儿在哭泣之前表现出心跳加快和面部紧张等前情绪行为。|近义词:protoemotional, pre-feeling|反义词:emotional


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "What is/was/will be..."  The usage is descriptive, modifying a noun.

Example 1:  The infant showed preemotional signs of distress. 婴儿表现出痛苦的前情绪迹象。
Example 2: Researchers are studying the preemotional brain activity associated with fear. 研究人员正在研究与恐惧相关的脑部前情绪活动。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases using "preemotional".  The word itself is relatively specialized and used primarily in academic or scientific contexts.
