monometallism:/ˌmɒnəˈmɛtəlɪzəm/| n. |单金属本位制|A monetary system in which the value of the currency is based on a single metal, usually gold or silver.  This system contrasts with bimetallism, which uses two metals.  Historically, monometallism often led to debates about the appropriate choice of metal and its impact on the economy.|Example: The gold standard is a classic example of monometallism.|近义词：single-metal standard|反义词：bimetallism
