presold:/priːˈsoʊld/| adj.|预售的，预先售出的|Already sold before being officially released or offered for sale.  This term typically refers to tickets, products, or services that have been sold in advance of their official release date or availability.|例句：The concert tickets were presold within hours.  这场音乐会的门票在几个小时内就预售一空了。|例句：Many of the new houses were presold before construction even began. 许多新房子甚至在动工前就被预售出去了。|近义词：pre-sold, advanced sold|反义词：unsold

USAGE->

The tickets are presold. = 门票已预售。
That product is presold. = 那款产品已预售。

一般过去时:
The album was presold last year. = 专辑去年就预售了。
The seats were presold quickly. = 座位很快就被预售光了。

词源:
The word "presold" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the past participle of the verb "sell" ("sold").  It indicates that something has been sold before a specific time or event.
