pillages:/ˈpɪlɪdʒɪz/| n.|掠夺；抢劫|The act of looting or plundering, especially in wartime.  It often involves taking goods by force.|例句：The invading army committed many pillages in the conquered territory.  侵略军在被征服的领土上进行了许多抢劫。|例句：The museum was the target of a daring pillages, with numerous artifacts stolen. 博物馆成为了一次大胆抢劫的目标，许多文物被盗。|近义词：plunder, loot, sack, raid|反义词：donation, contribution


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The army pillages villages. = 军队洗劫村庄。
He pillages the museum for artifacts. = 他洗劫博物馆以获取文物。

一般过去时:
The Vikings pillaged the coastal towns. = 维京人洗劫了沿海城镇。
The bandits pillaged the caravan. = 强盗洗劫了商队。

现在完成时:
They have pillaged the entire city. = 他们已经洗劫了整个城市。
The invaders have pillaged countless homes. = 侵略者已经洗劫了无数的房屋。

过去完成时:
The villagers had pillaged the enemy camp before dawn. = 村民们在黎明前洗劫了敌营。
The thieves had pillaged the bank vault before the police arrived. = 窃贼在警察到达之前已经洗劫了银行金库。

将来时:
They will pillage the next town tomorrow. = 他们明天将洗劫下一个城镇。
The rebels will pillage the capital if they win. = 如果叛军获胜，他们将洗劫首都。

虚拟语气:
If they had not pillaged the village, the people would have survived. = 如果他们没有洗劫村庄，人们就能活下来。
I wish they hadn't pillaged the museum. = 我真希望他们没有洗劫博物馆。

被动语态:
The village was pillaged by the invaders. = 村庄被侵略者洗劫了。
The museum was pillaged last night. = 博物馆昨晚被洗劫了。

疑问句:
Were the houses pillaged? = 房屋被洗劫了吗？

否定句:

They didn't pillage the church. = 他们没有洗劫教堂。
