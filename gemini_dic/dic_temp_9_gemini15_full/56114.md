doctrinairism:/ˌdɒktrɪˈneərɪzəm/ | n. |教条主义；空洞理论；教条作风 |The rigid adherence to a set of doctrines or principles, often without regard to practical considerations or the nuances of specific situations.  It often implies an inflexible and dogmatic approach, lacking in flexibility and adaptability. |例句：His doctrinaire approach to economics ignored the realities of the market.  他的经济学教条主义方法忽略了市场的现实。

例句：The party's doctrinaire policies alienated many potential supporters. 该党的教条主义政策疏远了许多潜在的支持者。


近义词：dogmatism, rigidity, inflexibility, formalism

反义词：pragmatism, flexibility, adaptability, open-mindedness
