malformations:/ˌmælˌfɔːrˈmeɪʃənz/| n. |畸形，先天性缺陷 |Abnormalities in the structure or function of a body part, usually present at birth.  Malformations can range from minor cosmetic issues to severe conditions affecting vital organs.  They are often caused by genetic factors, environmental influences during pregnancy, or a combination of both. |例句：The baby was born with several serious malformations of the heart. 这个婴儿出生时心脏有多处严重的畸形。

The study examined the incidence of neural tube malformations in the population. 这项研究调查了该人群中神经管畸形的发生率。


近义词：deformities, birth defects, anomalies, congenital defects |反义词：normality, perfection, typical development |词性:n.
