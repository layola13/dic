memorialize:/məˈmɔːriəlaɪz/| vt.|纪念，使不朽|To commemorate or keep the memory of someone or something alive, typically through a monument, ceremony, or written work.|The city plans to memorialize its fallen heroes with a new statue.|近义词：commemorate, celebrate, immortalize|反义词：forget, ignore


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They memorialize the victims every year. = 他们每年都纪念遇难者。
We memorialize our ancestors through storytelling. = 我们通过讲故事来纪念我们的祖先。

一般过去时:
The family memorialized their grandmother with a private ceremony. =  这个家庭通过一个私人仪式来纪念他们的祖母。
The artist memorialized the event in a powerful painting. =  这位艺术家在一幅强有力的画作中纪念了这一事件。

现在完成时:
They have memorialized the fallen soldiers with a moving tribute. = 他们已经用一个感人的悼念来纪念阵亡的士兵。
The museum has memorialized the history of the city through various exhibits. =  博物馆通过各种展览来纪念这座城市的历史。

过去完成时:
By the time the monument was unveiled, they had already memorialized the victims in a book. = 在纪念碑揭幕的时候，他们已经在一本书中纪念了受害者。
Before the ceremony, they had carefully memorialized the details of the incident. = 在仪式之前，他们已经仔细地记录了事件的细节。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If we had a suitable location, we would memorialize the event with a larger statue. = 如果我们有一个合适的地方，我们会用一个更大的雕像来纪念这个事件。
