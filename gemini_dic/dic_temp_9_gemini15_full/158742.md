plowboy:/ˈplaʊbɔɪ/| n. |犁地农夫，乡下人 |A plowboy is a boy or young man who works in the fields, typically plowing land with a plow.  The term often implies a low social status and lack of education. |例句：He was a plowboy from a small farming village. 他是一个来自一个小农业村庄的犁地农夫。  The novel depicts the harsh life of a plowboy in 19th-century America. 这部小说描绘了19世纪美国犁地农夫的艰苦生活。|近义词：farmhand, peasant, agricultural laborer |反义词：landowner, gentleman, aristocrat

词源:

The word "plowboy" is a straightforward compound word.  "Plow" comes from Old English *plōh,  referring to the agricultural implement. "Boy" retains its standard meaning of a male child or youth.  Thus, "plowboy" literally means a boy who uses a plow.
