pluck:/plʌk/ | vt., n. | 采摘；拔；弹奏；勇气；胆量 | To pull or pick something, especially something such as a flower, fruit, or feather, from where it is growing or fixed.  To pull out or remove something forcefully.  To play a stringed instrument by pulling the strings. Courage or determination. | 例句：She plucked a rose from the bush. 她从灌木丛中摘了一朵玫瑰。 He plucked up his courage and asked her to dance. 他鼓起勇气邀请她跳舞。|近义词：pull, pick, pluck up courage, nerve | 反义词：plant, sow, cowardice


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He plucks the guitar strings gently. = 他轻轻地拨动吉他弦。
She plucks fresh herbs from the garden every morning. = 她每天早上都从花园里采摘新鲜的香草。


一般过去时:
I plucked a chicken for dinner. = 我拔了一只鸡准备晚餐。
The farmer plucked all the ripe apples from the tree. = 农民摘下了树上所有成熟的苹果。


现在完成时:
She has plucked all the weeds from her flowerbed. = 她已经把花坛里的所有杂草都拔掉了。
They have plucked many beautiful flowers for the wedding. = 他们为婚礼摘了很多漂亮的花。


过去完成时:
He had plucked the feathers from the bird before cooking it. = 他在烹饪鸟之前已经拔掉了它的羽毛。
They had plucked the ripe mangoes from the tree before the storm. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们已经从树上摘下了成熟的芒果。


将来时:

She will pluck the flowers for her hair before the party. = 派对前她会摘花装饰头发。


虚拟语气:

I wish I had plucked that flower before it wilted. = 我真希望在我之前摘下那朵花，在那之前它就枯萎了。

否定句:
