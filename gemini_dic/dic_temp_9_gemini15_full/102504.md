jacobitism:/ˈdʒeɪkəbɪtɪzəm/| n. |雅各比主义|The political movement supporting the claim of the Stuarts, particularly James II and his descendants, to the British throne after the Glorious Revolution of 1688.  It involved a series of uprisings and conspiracies aimed at restoring the Catholic Stuart monarchy. |The Jacobite risings were a series of attempts to restore the Stuart dynasty to the British throne.  |近义词：Stuart cause,  counter-revolution |反义词：Whigism, Glorious Revolution
