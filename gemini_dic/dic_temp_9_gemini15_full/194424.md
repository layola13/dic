self-referentiality: /ˌsɛlf ˌrɛfəˈrɛnʃiˈælɪti/ | n. | 自指性，自我指涉性 | The quality or state of being self-referential; referring to itself or its own properties.  It often describes systems, statements, or concepts that directly or indirectly refer to themselves. For example, a sentence that mentions itself within its own structure exhibits self-referentiality.  | Example: The sentence "This sentence is false" is a classic example of self-referentiality, creating a paradox.  | 近义词: reflexivity, self-reference | 反义词: objectivity, extrinsicality |  词性: noun
