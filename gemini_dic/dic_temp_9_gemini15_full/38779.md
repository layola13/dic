conduciveness:/kənˈduːsɪvnəs/| n. |促成；有利因素；适宜性 |The conduciveness of the environment to learning is crucial. 环境对学习的适宜性至关重要。|近义词：suitability, aptness, favorableness |反义词：unsuitability, hindrance, impediment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The quiet atmosphere is conducive to study. = 安静的氛围有利于学习。
His calm demeanor is conducive to a productive meeting. = 他平静的态度有助于高效的会议。


一般过去时:
The warm weather was conducive to a good harvest. = 温暖的天气有利于丰收。
The supportive environment was conducive to her success. = 支持性的环境促成了她的成功。


现在完成时:
The changes have been conducive to improved efficiency. = 这些变化促进了效率的提高。
His efforts have been conducive to the project's success. = 他的努力促成了项目的成功。


过去完成时:
The previous policies had not been conducive to economic growth. = 先前的政策不利于经济增长。
The prior conditions had not been conducive to a peaceful resolution. = 先前的条件不利于和平解决。


将来时:
The new measures will be conducive to better health outcomes. = 新措施将有助于改善健康状况。
The planned reforms will be conducive to increased productivity. = 计划中的改革将有助于提高生产力。


虚拟语气:
If the conditions were more conducive, we would have succeeded. = 如果条件更适宜，我们就会成功。
If the environment were more conducive to creativity, we would have more innovative ideas. = 如果环境更有利于创造力，我们将会有更多创新的想法。


被动语态:
A conducive environment is considered essential for success. = 有利环境被认为是成功的关键。
The situation was deemed not conducive to further negotiations. = 局势被认为不利于进一步谈判。


疑问句:
Is this environment conducive to a productive discussion? = 这个环境有利于进行富有成效的讨论吗？
Are the current conditions conducive to achieving our goals? = 目前的条件有利于实现我们的目标吗？


否定句:
The noisy environment was not conducive to concentration. = 嘈杂的环境不利于集中注意力。
The tense atmosphere was not conducive to open communication. = 紧张的气氛不利于公开沟通。
