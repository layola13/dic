damning:/ˈdæmɪŋ/| adj.| 谴责的；无可辩驳的；足以定罪的 |strongly suggesting guilt or error; condemning.  The evidence against him was damning.| The evidence against him was damning.  他的罪证确凿。 | condemning, incriminating, inculpatory | exculpatory, exonerating | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The evidence is damning. = 证据是确凿的。
This report is damning for the company. = 这份报告对公司来说是致命的。


一般过去时:
The witness's testimony was damning. = 证人的证词是具有毁灭性的。
The judge found the evidence damning. = 法官认定证据确凿。


现在完成时:
The investigation has yielded damning evidence. = 调查已经产生了确凿的证据。
The new information has proven damning to his case. = 新信息已被证明对其案件不利。


过去完成时:
The jury had already heard the damning testimony. = 陪审团已经听取了具有毁灭性的证词。
The police had gathered enough damning evidence to arrest him. = 警方已经收集了足够的证据来逮捕他。


将来时:
The upcoming report will be damning for the administration. = 即将发布的报告将对政府不利。
This new evidence will be damning for the defendant. = 这项新的证据将对被告不利。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were damning, he would be convicted. = 如果证据确凿，他会被定罪。
I wish the evidence wasn't so damning against my client. = 我希望证据不要对我委托人如此不利。


被动语态:
The suspect was found guilty based on damning evidence. = 基于确凿的证据，嫌疑人被判有罪。
The politician's reputation was damaged by damning accusations. = 政治家的声誉因令人谴责的指控而受损。


疑问句:
Is the evidence against him damning enough for a conviction? = 反对他的是否足以定罪？
Isn't the testimony damning? = 证词难道不足以定罪吗？


否定句:
The evidence was not damning enough to convict him. = 证据不足以定罪。
The defense argued that the evidence was not damning. = 防御方辩称证据不足以定罪。
