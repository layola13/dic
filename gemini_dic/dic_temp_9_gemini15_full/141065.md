onomasticon:/ˌɒnəˈmæstɪkɒn/| n. |专名词典，人名地名词典|A book containing a list of names, especially proper names (of people and places).  It is often used in ancient texts to provide context and explanation for names that might be unfamiliar to the reader.|Example: The ancient Greek onomasticon helped scholars understand the mythology behind the characters in Homer's epics.|近义词：lexicon, glossary, gazetteer|反义词：None
