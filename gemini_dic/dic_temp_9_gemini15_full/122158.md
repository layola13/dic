mistrustful: /ˌmɪsˈtrʌstfəl/ | adj. | 不信任的，猜疑的 |  Characterized by or showing a lack of trust; suspicious.  |例句：He gave her a mistrustful look. 他给了她一个不信任的眼神。

例句：Her mistrustful nature prevented her from forming close relationships.  她不信任的性格使她无法建立密切的关系。


近义词：suspicious, skeptical, distrusting, wary

反义词：trusting, confident, believing


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is mistrustful of strangers. 他不信任陌生人。
She is always mistrustful of new ideas. 她总是对新想法抱有怀疑态度。


一般过去时:
He was mistrustful of her motives. 他不信任她的动机。
They were mistrustful of the government's promises. 他们不信任政府的承诺。


现在完成时:
I have been mistrustful of him ever since that incident. 从那件事以来，我一直不信任他。
She has always been mistrustful of politicians. 她一直对政客持怀疑态度。


过去完成时:
He had been mistrustful of her for years. 他多年来一直不信任她。
They had been mistrustful of the deal from the start. 从一开始他们就对这笔交易持怀疑态度。


将来时:
I will be mistrustful of anyone who approaches me like that. 我会不信任任何以那种方式接近我的人。
They will likely be mistrustful of his claims. 他们很可能会怀疑他的说法。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would be mistrustful of his offer. 如果我是你，我会怀疑他的提议。
I wish I hadn't been so mistrustful of her. 我真希望我当时不要对她那么不信任。


被动语态:
His motives were mistrustfully examined. 他的动机受到了怀疑的审查。
The proposal was viewed mistrustfully by the board.  董事会对该提案持怀疑态度。


疑问句:
Are you mistrustful of the new CEO? 你对新任首席执行官不信任吗？
Were they mistrustful of the evidence presented? 他们是否怀疑所提供的证据？


否定句:
I am not mistrustful of everyone. 我并非不信任所有人。
She is not mistrustful by nature. 她天生并非不信任他人。


PHRASE->

be mistrustful of: 不信任……

look mistrustful: 看起来不信任
become mistrustful: 变得不信任
a mistrustful glance: 一种不信任的眼神


词源:

The word "mistrustful" is derived from the prefix "mis-" (meaning "wrong" or "bad") and the word "trustful," which itself is derived from the noun "trust."  Therefore, "mistrustful" literally means "not trustworthy" or "lacking trust."
