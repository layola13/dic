parading:/pəˈreɪdɪŋ/| v.|游行，炫耀，展示 |The act of marching in a procession, often in a formal or celebratory way; also, to show off ostentatiously. |例句：The soldiers were parading through the streets. 士兵们正在街上游行。

The children were parading their new toys. 孩子们正在炫耀他们的新玩具。|近义词：marching, demonstrating, flaunting|反义词：hiding, concealing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They parade through the town every year. = 他们每年都在镇上游行。
The model parades the latest fashions. = 模特展示最新的时尚服装。

一般过去时:
The band paraded down Main Street. = 乐队沿着主要街道游行。
She paraded her new diamond ring. = 她炫耀她新的钻戒。

现在完成时:
They have paraded their support for the cause. = 他们已经公开表示支持这项事业。
The team has paraded its trophies. = 球队已经展示了他们的奖杯。

过去完成时:
The army had paraded before the king. = 军队曾在国王面前游行。
She had paraded her achievements to impress him. = 她曾炫耀她的成就以给他留下深刻印象。

将来时:
The school will parade its students at graduation. = 学校将在毕业典礼上让学生们游行。
He will parade his wealth to everyone. = 他将向所有人炫耀他的财富。

虚拟语气:
If they paraded their skills, they might get hired. = 如果他们展示他们的技能，他们可能会被雇用。
I wish they would parade their new products. = 我希望他们能展示他们的新产品。

被动语态:
The new uniforms were paraded at the ceremony. = 新制服在仪式上被展示。
His achievements are being paraded in the media. = 他的成就正在媒体上被宣传。

疑问句:
Will the team parade its victory? = 球队会庆祝他们的胜利吗？
Are they parading their influence? = 他们在炫耀他们的影响力吗？

否定句:
They didn't parade their wealth openly. = 他们没有公开炫耀他们的财富。
The soldiers did not parade in the rain. = 士兵们没有在雨中游行。
