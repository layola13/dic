iding:/ˈaɪdɪŋ/| v.|骑马；乘骑|The act of riding, especially on horseback.  It implies being mounted on an animal or vehicle and controlling its movement.|例句：He enjoys riding his horse through the fields. 他喜欢骑马穿过田野。|例句：She learned to ride a bicycle when she was six. 她六岁时学会了骑自行车。|近义词：riding, mounting, driving, cycling|反义词：walking, running


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rides his bike to work every day. = 他每天骑自行车上班。
She rides her horse in the park. = 她在公园里骑马。


一般过去时:
They rode their horses across the desert. = 他们骑马穿过沙漠。
He rode his bicycle to school yesterday. = 昨天他骑自行车去上学。


现在完成时:
I have ridden a horse many times. = 我骑过很多次马了。
She has ridden her bike across the country. = 她骑自行车横跨了整个国家。


过去完成时:
He had ridden his bike for hours before he stopped. = 他骑了几个小时的自行车才停下来。
They had ridden to the top of the mountain before nightfall. = 他们在天黑之前骑到了山顶。


将来时:
We will ride our bikes along the beach tomorrow. = 我们明天将在海滩上骑自行车。
They will ride horses in the rodeo next week. = 他们下周将在牛仔竞技表演中骑马。


虚拟语气:
If I had a horse, I would ride it every day. = 如果我有一匹马，我每天都会骑它。
I wish I could ride a motorcycle. = 我真希望我能骑摩托车。


被动语态:
The horse was ridden by a skilled rider. = 这匹马由一名熟练的骑手骑乘。
The bicycle was ridden hard and fast. = 这辆自行车被骑得又快又猛。


疑问句:
Do you ride a horse? = 你骑马吗？
Did they ride their bikes to the party? = 他们骑自行车去参加聚会了吗？


否定句:
He doesn't ride a motorcycle. = 他不骑摩托车。
She didn't ride her horse today. = 她今天没骑马。
