cabinet: /ˈkæbɪnət/ | n. |内阁；橱柜；箱子 | A cabinet is a group of ministers who advise a head of state or government.  It can also refer to a cupboard or a small, usually wooden, container with drawers or shelves for storing things. |例句：The cabinet will meet tomorrow to discuss the new budget.  内阁将于明天开会讨论新的预算。|例句：She keeps her jewelry in a small wooden cabinet. 她把珠宝放在一个小木柜里。|近义词：council, ministry, cupboard, chest |反义词： (无直接反义词，需根据语境选择)

词源:
The word "cabinet" comes from the Old French word "cabane," meaning "hut" or "small room."  This evolved through Middle French "cabinet," referring to a small private room or closet, and ultimately to the modern English meaning of a piece of furniture or a governmental body.
