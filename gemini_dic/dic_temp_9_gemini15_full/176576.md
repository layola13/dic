re-win:/ˌriːˈwɪn/| v.|重新赢得，再次获得|To win back something that was lost or previously won.  It implies a previous loss or competition.|例句：The team re-won the championship after a disappointing season.  球队在经历了一个令人失望的赛季后重新赢得了冠军。

例句：She re-won her confidence after overcoming her fear. 她克服恐惧后重新获得了自信。


近义词：regain, recover, recapture|反义词：lose, forfeit


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They re-win the contract every year. = 他们每年都重新赢得合同。
We re-win the trust of our customers. = 我们重新赢得了顾客的信任。


一般过去时:
The company re-won the bid last year. = 公司去年重新赢得了投标。
He re-won the election by a landslide. = 他以压倒性优势重新赢得了选举。


现在完成时:
She has re-won the hearts of the public. = 她重新赢得了公众的心。
They have re-won their place in the league. = 他们重新赢得了他们在联赛中的位置。


过去完成时:
After a period of decline, they had re-won their market share. = 在一段时间的衰退之后，他们重新赢得了市场份额。
He had re-won her affection after months of apology.  在几个月的道歉后，他重新赢得了她的好感。


将来时:
We will re-win the game next time. = 我们下次会重新赢得比赛。
I believe he will re-win the election. = 我相信他会重新赢得选举。


虚拟语气:
If we had tried harder, we might have re-won the game. = 如果我们更努力一些，我们可能就重新赢得了比赛。
If he worked harder, he could re-win his customers trust. 如果他更努力工作，他就能重新赢得顾客的信任。


被动语态:
The championship was re-won by the underdog team. = 冠军被弱势球队重新赢得。
The contract was re-won by our company. = 合同被我们公司重新赢得。


疑问句:

否定句:
They did not re-win the contract. = 他们没有重新赢得合同。
