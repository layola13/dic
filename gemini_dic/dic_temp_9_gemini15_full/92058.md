hyperproducers:/ˌhaɪpərprəˈduːsərz/ | n. |高产者，过度生产者 |A hyperproducer is an individual or entity that produces significantly more of a particular substance or product than is considered typical or average. This term is often used in the context of biology, medicine, and manufacturing.  For example, in microbiology, a hyperproducer strain of a microorganism might produce a significantly larger quantity of a desired metabolite (like an antibiotic or enzyme). In medicine, it can refer to someone with an overactive organ or gland. In manufacturing, it may refer to a highly productive factory or worker. |例句：The new strain of bacteria is a hyperproducer of penicillin. 新菌株是青霉素的高产菌株。  The factory is known as a hyperproducer of car parts. 这家工厂以汽车零件的高产量而闻名。|近义词：high-yielders, overproducers |反义词：underproducers, low-yielders
