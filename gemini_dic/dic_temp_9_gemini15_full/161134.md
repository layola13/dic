/pɒstɪɡˈzaɪlɪən/ | adj. | 流亡后的；流放归来后的 | Post-exilic refers to the period in Jewish history following the Babylonian exile (approximately 539 BCE), when the Jews returned to Judah and rebuilt the Temple in Jerusalem.  It describes the events, culture, and literature that emerged during this era.  | Example: Post-exilic Judaism saw a significant shift in religious practice and beliefs.  流亡后的犹太教经历了宗教实践和信仰的重大转变。 | 近义词: post-captivity, post-return | 反义词: pre-exilic
