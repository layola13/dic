exemplarist: /ɪɡˈzɛmplərɪst/ | adj. | 模范主义的；典范主义的 | Relating to or characterized by exemplarism, a philosophical theory that emphasizes the importance of examples and models in understanding and justifying moral and other normative judgments.  It suggests that we learn morality not through abstract principles, but by observing and emulating exemplary individuals or actions. |  The exemplarist approach to ethics focuses on the role of exemplary individuals. | 近义词：model-based, example-driven | 反义词：principle-based, rule-oriented | 形容词
