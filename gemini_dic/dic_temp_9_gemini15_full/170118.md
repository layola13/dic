protochlorophyll:/ˌproʊtoʊˈklɔːrəfɪl/ | n. |原叶绿素|A precursor to chlorophyll, a green pigment found in plants and other photosynthetic organisms.  It is a pigment that is converted into chlorophyll during the process of plant development, especially in the presence of light. Protochlorophyll is a magnesium-containing porphyrin compound that absorbs light energy, but it doesn't efficiently perform photosynthesis until it is converted to chlorophyll.|Plants synthesize protochlorophyll in the dark, and it is then converted to chlorophyll upon exposure to light.|近义词：chlorophyll precursor|反义词：chlorophyll (in the sense of the final product)
