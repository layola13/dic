ornithoidichnite:/ˈɔːrnɪθɔɪdaɪknaɪt/| n. |鸟迹化石|A fossil footprint or trackway made by a bird.  Ornithoidichnites are often used to infer the presence and behavior of extinct birds in ancient environments.  The study of ornithoidichnites helps paleontologists understand the evolution of avian locomotion and the paleoecology of past ecosystems.|The discovery of ornithoidichnites in the sandstone provided strong evidence of bird presence during the Cretaceous period.|近义词：bird track, avian footprint,  |反义词：mammalian footprint, reptilian track.
