castigates:/ˈkæstɪɡeɪt/| vt.|严厉批评；惩罚|To criticize severely; to punish.|The teacher castigated the student for cheating on the exam. 老师严厉批评了那个考试作弊的学生。|近义词：rebuke, reprimand, chastise|反义词：praise, commend

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He castigates his employees for minor mistakes. 他因小错而严厉批评他的员工。
The principal castigates students who break the rules. 校长严厉批评违规的学生。


一般过去时:
The judge castigated the defendant for his crimes. 法官严厉谴责被告的罪行。
She castigated him for his rudeness. 她严厉批评了他的粗鲁行为。


现在完成时:
The media has castigated the politician for his lies. 媒体因其谎言而严厉批评了这位政治家。
He has castigated himself for his mistakes. 他因自己的错误而自责。


过去完成时:
The professor had castigated the student for plagiarism before. 教授之前就因剽窃而严厉批评过这位学生。
They had castigated her for her irresponsible behavior. 他们因她不负责任的行为而严厉批评了她。


将来时:
The coach will castigate the team for their poor performance. 教练将因他们糟糕的表现而严厉批评球队。
She will castigate him if he continues to be late. 如果他继续迟到，她将严厉批评他。


虚拟语气:
If he were to make such a mistake again, his boss would castigate him severely. 如果他再次犯这样的错误，他的老板会严厉批评他。
I wish he hadn't castigated me so harshly. 我真希望他不要那么严厉地批评我。


被动语态:
The student was castigated for his disruptive behavior in class. 学生因在课堂上扰乱秩序而受到严厉批评。
His actions were castigated by the public. 他的行为受到公众的严厉批评。


疑问句:
Will the manager castigate the employees for the missed deadline? 经理会因为错过截止日期而严厉批评员工吗？
Why was he castigated by his parents? 他为什么受到父母的严厉批评？


否定句:
The teacher did not castigate the student, but instead offered guidance. 老师没有严厉批评学生，而是提供了指导。
She didn't castigate him, but simply explained the consequences. 她没有批评他，只是解释了后果。
