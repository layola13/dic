laconian:/ləˈkeɪniən/| adj.| 简洁的；简明的；言简意赅的 |Laconic comes from Laconia, a region in ancient Greece known for its concise and terse speech.  It describes speech or writing that is brief and to the point, sometimes to the point of seeming rude or abrupt. |His laconic reply, "No," ended the conversation.  他简短的回答“不”，结束了谈话。|succinct, terse, brief, concise |verbose, rambling, prolix, diffuse|形容词
