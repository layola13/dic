/əˈpoʊn/ | v. | 反对，抵制 | To disagree with something or someone; to be against something or someone.  | He opposed the new law. 他反对这项新法律。 She opposed his plans. 她反对他的计划。 | support, resist, withstand |  agree, approve, favor | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They oppose the government's policies. = 他们反对政府的政策。
He opposes any form of violence. = 他反对任何形式的暴力。

一般过去时:
She opposed the marriage. = 她反对这桩婚事。
The workers opposed the factory closure. = 工人们反对工厂关闭。

现在完成时:
We have opposed this project from the beginning. = 我们从一开始就反对这个项目。
They have consistently opposed the war. = 他们一直反对这场战争。

过去完成时:
He had opposed the idea before the meeting. = 他在会议之前就反对过这个主意。
She had already opposed the changes when the proposal was made. =  提案提出时，她就已经反对这些改变了。

将来时:
They will oppose the bill in parliament. = 他们将在议会中反对该法案。
We will continue to oppose this injustice. = 我们将继续反对这种不公正行为。

虚拟语气:
If he opposed the plan, he would have to give reasons. = 如果他反对这个计划，他必须给出理由。
I wish he had not opposed my suggestion. = 我真希望他不要反对我的建议。

被动语态:
The proposal was opposed by many people. = 这个提案遭到许多人的反对。
His ideas were strongly opposed. = 他的想法受到了强烈反对。

疑问句:
Does he oppose the new regulations? = 他反对新规吗？
Why do they oppose this plan? = 他们为什么反对这个计划？

否定句:
She did not oppose the decision. = 她没有反对这个决定。
He doesn't oppose the idea in principle. = 他原则上并不反对这个主意。


PHRASE->
be opposed to = 反对
in opposition to = 与...相反
oppose oneself to = 反对
set oneself in opposition to = 反对
strongly opposed to = 强烈反对
