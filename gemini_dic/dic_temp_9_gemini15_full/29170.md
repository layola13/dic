cashableness:/ˈkæʃəbəlnəs/| n. |可兑换性，可变现性 |The cashableness of an asset refers to how easily and quickly it can be converted into cash without significant loss of value.  This is a crucial factor in assessing liquidity and financial stability.  For example, a highly liquid asset like a money market account offers high cashableness, while a piece of real estate might have lower cashableness due to the time and effort required to sell it. |例句：The high cashableness of government bonds makes them attractive to investors seeking short-term liquidity. = 政府债券的高可变现性使其对寻求短期流动性的投资者具有吸引力。


The cashableness of the company's inventory is a key factor in its credit rating. = 公司库存的可变现性是其信用评级的关键因素。


近义词：liquidity, marketability, convertibility |反义词：illiquidity, unmarketability
