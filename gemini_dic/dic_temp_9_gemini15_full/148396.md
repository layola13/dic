overstaffs:/ˌoʊvərˈstæf/ | vt. |人员过剩，雇用过多员工| To employ more staff than are needed.  The company overstaffed during the peak season. 公司在旺季雇佣了过多的员工。|近义词：overman, overemploy |反义词：understaff


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The restaurant overstaffs during the lunch rush. = 餐厅在午餐高峰期雇佣了过多的员工。
They consistently overstaff their customer service department. = 他们始终在客户服务部门雇佣过多的员工。

一般过去时:
The factory overstaffed last year, leading to budget overruns. = 工厂去年雇佣了过多的员工，导致预算超支。
They overstaffed the project and ended up wasting resources. = 他们在这个项目中雇佣了过多的员工，最终浪费了资源。

现在完成时:
The company has overstaffed for the past three years. = 公司过去三年一直雇佣过多的员工。
They have overstaffed in anticipation of increased demand. = 他们为了应对需求增长而雇佣了过多的员工。

过去完成时:
The airline had overstaffed before the pandemic hit. = 在疫情爆发之前，航空公司就已经雇佣了过多的员工。
They realized they had overstaffed after the project was completed. = 项目完成后，他们才意识到自己雇佣了过多的员工。
