compartmentalism:/kəmˈpɑːrtmənˌtælɪzəm/| n. |隔间化；划分；专业化；(思想或活动的) 分隔 |The practice of dividing something into separate compartments.  This can refer to the organization of a physical space, a system, or even one's thoughts and activities. It often implies a lack of integration or communication between the different parts. |例句：The company's compartmentalism hindered effective communication between departments. 公司的部门分割阻碍了部门间的有效沟通。

例句：His compartmentalism of his personal and professional life led to a sense of isolation. 他将个人生活和职业生活隔离开来，导致了一种孤立感。


近义词：division, segregation, specialization, isolation, fragmentation


反义词：integration, unification, holism, interconnectedness


词源:  The word "compartmentalism" is derived from the noun "compartment," which comes from the Old French word "compartiment," meaning "partition," "division."  The suffix "-alism" indicates a system, practice, or doctrine.  The root is ultimately from the Latin "compartire," meaning "to share, divide."
