pitfalling: /ˈpɪtfɔːlɪŋ/ | n. | 陷阱；隐患；易犯的错误 |  A pitfall is a hidden or unexpected danger or difficulty.  It refers to a potential problem or mistake that is easily overlooked or underestimated.  The word emphasizes the concealed nature of the danger, implying that one might fall into it unknowingly. |

例句：

One pitfall of working from home is the blurring of boundaries between work and personal life. =  在家工作的陷阱之一是工作和个人生活界限的模糊。

The company encountered several pitfalls during the launch of its new product. =  该公司在新产品发布期间遇到了几个陷阱。


近义词：danger, hazard, snare, trap, drawback, impediment, stumbling block

反义词：advantage, benefit, boon, blessing, help

词源:

The word "pitfall" originates from the literal meaning of a covered pit or hole in the ground that someone could unexpectedly fall into.  The "pit" part refers to the hole, and "fall" describes the action of falling into it.  The figurative usage extended to encompass any hidden danger or unexpected problem.
