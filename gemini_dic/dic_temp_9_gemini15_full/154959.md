pestilentially:/ˌpɛstɪˈlɛnʃəli/| adj.| 瘟疫似的；有害的；致命的 |  In a pestilential manner; causing or tending to cause pestilence; destructive; deadly.  It describes something that is harmful, destructive, or deadly, often in a widespread or pervasive way, similar to a pestilence (a deadly epidemic disease). | Example: The pestilentially hot summer led to widespread crop failure.  这个瘟疫般炎热的夏天导致了广泛的农作物歉收。


The air was thick with a pestilentially sweet odor.  空气中弥漫着一种瘟疫般甜腻的气味。|近义词：deadly, destructive, pernicious, noxious, baneful |反义词：beneficial, harmless, salutary

词源:
From Middle English *pestilencien, from Old French pestilentiel, from Late Latin pestilentialis, from pestilentia "pestilence," from pestilens, present participle of pestilere "to be affected by pestilence," from pestis "pestilence."  The suffix "-ential" forms adjectives from nouns.
