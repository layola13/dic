impeaches:/ɪmˈpiːtʃɪz/ | vt. | 指控；弹劾；怀疑；损害 | To accuse (someone, especially a public official) of wrongdoing; to challenge the validity of.  |例句：The House of Representatives impeached the president.众议院弹劾了总统。

The evidence impeaches his testimony.证据使他的证词受到质疑。


近义词：accuse, indict, charge, challenge | 反义词：acquit, exonerate | 词性: Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The committee impeaches the governor for misconduct. = 委员会指控州长行为不端。
He frequently impeaches the credibility of his opponents. = 他经常质疑对手的可信度。


一般过去时:
The Senate impeached the judge last year. = 参议院去年弹劾了这位法官。
She impeached the accuracy of the report. = 她质疑了报告的准确性。


现在完成时:
They have impeached several officials this year. = 他们今年已经弹劾了几位官员。
The defense has impeached the witness's testimony. = 防御方已经质疑了证人的证词。


过去完成时:
The court had already impeached the verdict before the appeal. = 在上诉之前，法院已经推翻了判决。
History had impeached the king's legitimacy long before the rebellion. = 早在叛乱之前，历史就已经质疑了国王的合法性。


将来时:
The opposition party will likely impeach the president next year. = 反对党很可能会在明年弹劾总统。
The new evidence will probably impeach the defendant's alibi. = 新证据可能会推翻被告的不在场证明。


虚拟语气:
If they had more evidence, they would impeach the mayor. = 如果他们有更多证据，他们就会弹劾市长。
If the witness's testimony were impeached, the case would collapse. = 如果证人的证词被质疑，案件就会崩溃。


被动语态:
The president was impeached by the House. = 总统被众议院弹劾。
His claims were impeached by contradictory evidence. = 他的说法被相互矛盾的证据所驳斥。


疑问句:
Will the Senate impeach the judge? = 参议院会弹劾这位法官吗？
Has the prosecution successfully impeached the witness's credibility? = 控方是否成功地质疑了证人的可信度？


否定句:
The court did not impeach the jury's decision. = 法院没有推翻陪审团的决定。
The defense lawyer failed to impeach the key witness. = 辩护律师未能质疑关键证人。


PHRASE->
impeach for misconduct = 因行为不端而弹劾
impeach a witness's testimony = 质疑证人的证词
impeach the validity of = 质疑…的有效性
impeach the credibility of = 质疑…的可信度
impeach a judgment = 推翻判决


词源:
From Old French *empecher (to prevent, hinder), from empechier (to prevent), from Late Latin impedire (to hinder), from Latin im- (in, on, upon) + pedire (to fetter, bind).  The modern sense of "to accuse formally" developed in the 16th century.
