hyperintellectually: /ˌhaɪpərɪnˌtɛləkʃuəli/ | adv. | 超级聪明地，极度理性地 |  In a manner characterized by extreme intellectual ability and often excessive rationalization.  It implies an almost superhuman level of intelligence and a tendency to overthink or over-analyze. | 例句：He approached the problem hyperintellectually, dissecting every detail until he lost sight of the bigger picture. 他以极其聪明的方式处理这个问题，仔细分析每一个细节，直到他忽略了更大的图景。

The task was tackled hyperintellectually, leading to an overly complex solution. 这项任务是以极其聪明的方式完成的，导致了一个过于复杂的解决方案。| 近义词：exceedingly intellectually, supremely rationally, brilliantly analytically | 反义词：stupidly, unintelligently, irrationally, intuitively


词源:

* **hyper-**:  Prefix meaning "excessive," "over," or "beyond."  Derived from Greek ὑπέρ (hupér), meaning "over," "above," or "beyond."

* **intellect-**: Root referring to the ability to understand and reason.  Derived from Latin *intellectus*, meaning "understanding," "perception," from *intellegere*, "to understand."

* **-ually**: Adverbial suffix indicating "in a ... manner." Derived from Latin *-aliter*.
