hobbled:/ˈhɑːbəld/ /ˈhɒbəld/| vt. |跛行；使跛行；妨碍；束缚|To walk in an awkward way, usually because of an injury to a leg or foot; to restrict the movement or progress of something. |例句：The injured runner hobbled across the finish line. 受伤的跑步者一瘸一拐地跑过终点线。

The new regulations hobbled the company's growth. 新的法规阻碍了公司的发展。|近义词：limp, cripple, hamper, hinder |反义词：stride, advance, accelerate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hobbles around the house after his surgery. = 他手术后在家中一瘸一拐地走动。
The strict rules hobble the company's innovation. = 严格的规定阻碍了公司的创新。

一般过去时:
She hobbled home after twisting her ankle. = 她扭伤脚踝后一瘸一拐地回家了。
The economic crisis hobbled the country's development. = 经济危机阻碍了国家的进步。

现在完成时:
He has hobbled around since breaking his leg. = 自从摔断腿后，他一直一瘸一拐地走动。
The new policy has hobbled the efficiency of the organization. = 新的政策已经阻碍了该组织的效率。

过去完成时:
She had hobbled for miles before reaching help. = 在得到帮助之前，她已经一瘸一拐地走了几英里。
The government had hobbled the opposition party's campaign. = 政府已经阻碍了反对党的竞选活动。

将来时:
He will hobble for several weeks after the operation. = 手术后，他将一瘸一拐地走几周。
Increased regulations will hobble the industry's progress. = 越来越多的规定将阻碍该行业的进步。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't injured his leg, he wouldn't hobble. = 如果他没有伤到腿，他就不会跛行。
If the laws hadn't been so restrictive, the business wouldn't have been hobbled. = 如果法律没有那么严格，这项业务就不会受到阻碍。


被动语态:
His progress was hobbled by unexpected delays. = 他的进展因意外延误而受阻。
The project was hobbled by a lack of funding. = 该项目因缺乏资金而受阻。

疑问句:
Does he still hobble after the accident? = 事故后他是否仍然跛行？
Will the new rules hobble free speech? = 新的规定会限制言论自由吗？

否定句:
He doesn't hobble anymore. = 他不再跛行了。
The regulations did not hobble the company's expansion. = 这些规定并没有阻碍公司的扩张。
