eurocentric:/ˌjʊərəʊˈsɛntrɪk/| adj.|欧洲中心主义的，以欧洲为中心的|  Eurocentric refers to the tendency to view the world from a European perspective, often prioritizing European culture, values, and history while marginalizing or ignoring other cultures.  It implies a belief in European superiority or dominance. |例句：The eurocentric curriculum neglected the contributions of non-European civilizations.  欧洲中心主义的课程忽略了非欧洲文明的贡献。

The history textbook presented a distinctly eurocentric view of world events. 这本历史教科书对世界事件的看法明显带有欧洲中心主义色彩。|近义词：ethnocentric, occidentalist|反义词：multicultural, global, world-centric

词源:
The word "eurocentric" is a modern coinage, combining the prefix "euro-" (from "Europe") with the suffix "-centric" (meaning "centered on").  It emerged in the late 20th century as a critical term to describe biased viewpoints.
