intail:/ɪnˈteɪl/| v.|牵连，导致；继承(财产等)|to involve as a necessary consequence; to inherit (property, etc.)|The decision to expand the factory will entail significant costs.  这个扩建工厂的决定将带来巨大的成本。

The inheritance entailed a large old house and a considerable debt.  这笔遗产包括一栋很大的老房子和一大笔债务。|entail, involve, imply|disengage, separate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This project entails a lot of work. = 这个项目需要大量的工作。
The new policy entails significant changes. = 新政策需要重大的改变。


一般过去时:
The war entailed great suffering. = 那场战争带来了巨大的痛苦。
His decision entailed serious consequences. = 他的决定带来了严重的后果。


现在完成时:
The renovations have entailed unexpected expenses. = 装修带来了意想不到的费用。
Our actions have entailed unforeseen consequences. = 我们的行为带来了意想不到的后果。


过去完成时:
The investigation had entailed many hours of work. = 调查花费了大量时间。
The accident had entailed significant property damage. = 事故造成了巨大的财产损失。


将来时:
The new law will entail stricter regulations. = 新法律将带来更严格的法规。
The merger will entail job losses. = 并购将导致失业。


虚拟语气:
If we proceed, it would entail considerable risk. = 如果我们继续，将会承担相当大的风险。
I wish the job didn't entail so much travel. = 我希望这份工作不需要那么多出差。


被动语态:
Significant costs were entailed by the project. = 这个项目带来了巨大的成本。
Many changes were entailed by the new system. = 新系统带来了许多变化。


疑问句:
What does this job entail? = 这份工作都包括些什么？
Does this decision entail any risks? = 这个决定会带来任何风险吗？


否定句:
The job doesn't entail any overnight travel. = 这份工作不需要任何夜间旅行。
The plan doesn't entail any significant changes. = 这个计划不需要任何重大改变。
