re-granted:/ˌriːˈɡræntɪd/| v. |重新授予，再次给予 |To grant something again; to renew a grant.|The company re-granted the patent after a legal challenge. 公司在法律挑战后重新授予了专利。|近义词：renewed, reinstated, reissued |反义词：revoked, cancelled


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government re-grants the funding annually. = 政府每年重新拨款。
They re-grant the licenses to qualified applicants. = 他们向合格的申请人重新发放许可证。

一般过去时:
The judge re-granted the motion for a new trial. = 法官重新批准了新的审判动议。
The university re-granted her scholarship after her appeal. = 大学在她上诉后重新批准了她的奖学金。

现在完成时:
The committee has re-granted the permission to hold the event. = 委员会已经重新批准了举办该活动的许可。
The board has re-granted him the access to the system. = 董事会已经重新允许他访问该系统。


过去完成时:
They had re-granted the contract before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期前已经重新批准了合同。
The authority had re-granted the building permit after the inspection. = 当局在检查后已经重新批准了建筑许可证。


将来时:
The council will re-grant the planning permission next month. = 理事会下个月将重新批准规划许可。
They will re-grant the loan if he meets the new requirements. = 如果他满足新的要求，他们将重新发放贷款。


虚拟语气:
If the appeal were successful, the court would re-grant the bail. = 如果上诉成功，法院将重新批准保释。
