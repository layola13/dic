outpricing: /ˈaʊtpraɪsɪŋ/ | n. | 超价销售，高于市场价出售 | The act of pricing goods or services higher than competitors.  This strategy can be used to create a perception of higher quality or exclusivity. However, it can also alienate price-sensitive customers. | Example:  The boutique hotel engaged in outpricing, charging significantly more than comparable hotels in the area. = 这家精品酒店采用超价销售策略，收费远高于该地区同类酒店。


The company's outpricing strategy backfired, resulting in lower sales. = 该公司的超价销售策略适得其反，导致销售额下降。|近义词:overpricing, premium pricing |反义词:underpricing, discounting

词源:

The word "outpricing" is a relatively modern term, formed by combining "out-" (meaning to surpass or exceed) with "pricing" (the process of setting prices).  It's a straightforward combination reflecting its meaning.
