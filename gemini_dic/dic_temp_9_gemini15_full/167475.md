preventionism: /prɪˈvɛnʃənɪzəm/ | n. | 预防主义 | The policy or practice of prioritizing the prevention of problems, especially diseases or social issues, over treatment or remediation.  A focus on proactive measures to avoid negative outcomes. |  The government's preventionism led to increased funding for public health initiatives.  政府的预防主义导致公共卫生事业的资金增加。  | 近义词：prophylaxis, precaution, safeguard | 反义词：reactivism, remediation, treatment |  名词
