short-circuited:/ʃɔːrtˈsɜːkɪtɪd/ | adj. | 短路的；被短路的；思路短路的 |  Describes a situation where an electrical circuit is unintentionally completed through a path of lower resistance than intended, often causing a malfunction or damage.  It can also figuratively describe a situation where a process or train of thought is interrupted or prematurely concluded. |例句：The appliance malfunctioned because it was short-circuited.  这台电器因为短路而发生故障。

The negotiations were short-circuited by a sudden disagreement.  谈判因突然的意见分歧而中断。

近义词：bypassed, disrupted, interrupted, cut short | 反义词：completed, uninterrupted, continued
