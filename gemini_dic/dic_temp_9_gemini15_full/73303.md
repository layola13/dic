forecasting:/ˈfɔːrkæstɪŋ/ | v. n. | 预报；预测 | The act of predicting future events or trends, typically based on available data and analysis.  It can refer to the process itself or the predicted outcome. | 例句：The weather forecasting suggests rain tomorrow.  天气预报说明明天有雨。

The company's sales forecasting indicated a significant increase next quarter. 公司的销售预测显示下个季度将大幅增长。


近义词：prediction, projection, prognostication, estimation

反义词：hindsight (for the opposite of predicting the future)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We forecast the weather daily. = 我们每天预报天气。
The analysts forecast strong economic growth. = 分析师预测经济强劲增长。

一般过去时:
They forecast a successful product launch. = 他们预测产品发布会将成功。
The meteorologist forecast heavy snow. = 气象学家预报了暴雪。

现在完成时:
They have forecast a market downturn. = 他们已经预测了市场下滑。
We have forecast the project's completion date. = 我们已经预测了项目的完成日期。

过去完成时:
They had forecast lower profits than expected. = 他们预测的利润比预期低。
The economists had forecast a recession. = 经济学家们已经预测到经济衰退。

将来时:
We will forecast the next quarter's earnings. = 我们将预测下个季度的收益。
The experts will forecast the election results. = 专家们将预测选举结果。

虚拟语气:
If we had forecast the crisis, we could have prepared better. = 如果我们预测到这场危机，我们可以做得更好。
I wish we had forecast the market crash. = 我希望我们预测到市场崩盘。

被动语态:
The weather was forecast to be sunny. = 天气预报为晴天。
The election results were forecast accurately. = 选举结果被准确预测。

疑问句:
What is being forecast for the stock market? = 股票市场预测如何？
How is the weather forecast looking? = 天气预报怎么样？

否定句:
They did not forecast the sudden change in the market. = 他们没有预测到市场突然变化。
The forecast did not predict the storm. = 预报没有预测到风暴。
