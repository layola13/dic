dog days:/ˈdɒɡ deɪz/| n. |三伏天，酷暑期 |The dog days are the hottest days of summer, traditionally considered to be the period between early July and mid-August.  This period is associated with the rising of the star Sirius, also known as the Dog Star, which was believed by the ancients to add to the summer heat.  The term is now used more generally to refer to any period of intense heat and discomfort. |例句：The dog days of summer are finally over. 夏天的酷暑终于过去了。
The heat during the dog days can be unbearable. 三伏天的酷暑难以忍受。|近义词：heat wave, summer solstice |反义词：winter, cold spell
