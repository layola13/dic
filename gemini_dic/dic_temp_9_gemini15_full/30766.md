cessions:/ˈsɛʃənz/ | n. | 割让；让步；放弃 | The act of giving up something, typically territory or rights, especially under pressure.  It can also refer to the things given up. |例句：The treaty involved significant territorial cessions.  该条约涉及大片领土的割让。

例句：The company made several concessions to the union during negotiations.  公司在谈判中对工会做出了一些让步。


近义词：concessions, surrenders, relinquishments, yields | 反义词：acquisitions, gains, retentions | 词性：名词


词源:  From Old French *cession, from Latin cessio, from cedere "to yield, go, withdraw".


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government makes concessions to avoid strikes. = 政府做出让步以避免罢工。
Our company makes regular concessions to our clients. = 我们公司定期向客户让步。


一般过去时:
The losing country ceded territories after the war. = 战败国在战争后割让了领土。
They made significant concessions to reach an agreement. = 为了达成协议，他们做出了重大让步。


现在完成时:
The company has made several concessions to improve its image. = 公司已经做出了一些让步以改善其形象。
The government has ceded control over several industries. = 政府已经放弃了对几个行业的控制。


过去完成时:
Before the treaty, the nation had already made several territorial cessions. = 在条约签订之前，这个国家已经进行了多次领土割让。
They had made numerous concessions, yet the deal still fell through. = 他们已经做了很多让步，但交易仍然失败了。


将来时:
The government will make further concessions if protests continue. = 如果抗议继续，政府将做出进一步的让步。
We will likely cede some market share to our competitors. = 我们可能会将部分市场份额让给竞争对手。


虚拟语气:
If they made more concessions, the negotiations would have been successful. = 如果他们做出更多让步，谈判就会成功。
I wish the company had made more concessions to its employees. = 我希望公司能给员工更多让步。


被动语态:
Several concessions were made by both sides to secure peace. = 双方都做出了几项让步以确保和平。
Control of the island was ceded to the United Nations. = 岛屿的控制权被移交给联合国。


疑问句:

否定句:
The government refused to make any concessions. = 政府拒绝做出任何让步。
No further cessions of land will be tolerated. = 不再容忍任何土地割让。
