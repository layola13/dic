electrotyping:/ɪˌlɛkˈtroʊtaɪpɪŋ/ | n. | 电铸，电版制作 | The process of reproducing printing plates or other objects by electrolysis.  It involves depositing a layer of metal, usually copper, onto a mold of the original object using an electric current. This creates a durable and accurate copy. | Example:  Electrotyping was a crucial technique in the mass production of books before the advent of offset printing. 电铸是印刷术兴起之前书籍大量生产中一项至关重要的技术。


近义词：electroforming (更广泛的术语，包含电铸)
反义词：无直接反义词，但可以考虑与传统手工印刷方法对比
