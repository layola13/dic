gaekwar:/ɡeɪkˈwɑːr/ | n. |盖夸尔（印度古吉拉特邦的一个王公称号） | A title used for the rulers of certain princely states in Gujarat, India.  The term is derived from the Sanskrit word "gaekwad," which literally translates to "bullock-herder."  It was originally a hereditary title held by a Maratha family who rose to prominence and power in the region. | The Gaekwar of Baroda ruled a large and influential state.  | 近义词: maharaja (a more general term for a great king), raja (king) | 反义词:  None directly applicable, as it's a title not a descriptive word. | 名词
