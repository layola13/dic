presaged:/ˈprɛsɪdʒd/| v.|预示，预兆|To indicate or suggest beforehand; to foreshadow.|The dark clouds presaged a storm.  乌云预示着一场暴风雨。 The economic indicators presaged a recession. 经济指标预示着经济衰退。|foreshadow, foretell, augur, portend|misinterpret, overlook|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The economist presages a period of economic uncertainty.  经济学家预示着一段经济不确定时期。
The dark clouds presage a storm. 乌云预示着暴风雨。


一般过去时:
The strange events presaged the disaster. 奇怪的事件预示着灾难。
The writer's early works presaged his later success. 作家的早期作品预示着他后来的成功。


现在完成时:
Recent events have presaged a change in government policy. 最近的事件预示着政府政策的改变。
The rising sea levels have presaged the devastating effects of climate change. 海平面上升预示着气候变化的灾难性影响。


过去完成时:
The ominous silence had presaged the explosion. 不祥的寂静预示着爆炸。
The gathering storm clouds had presaged the heavy rainfall. 聚集的乌云预示着大雨。


将来时:
Analysts predict that the upcoming elections will presage significant political shifts. 分析师预测即将到来的选举将预示着重大的政治转变。
Experts believe that these new technologies will presage a revolution in healthcare. 专家们认为这些新技术将预示着医疗保健领域的革命。


虚拟语气:
If the signs had been correctly interpreted, the disaster could have been presaged. 如果正确解读了这些迹象，灾难本来是可以预见的。
If only we had heeded the warnings, the crisis could have been presaged. 如果我们能重视警告，这场危机本来是可以预见的。


被动语态:
The impending crisis was presaged by several warning signs. 迫在眉睫的危机由几个警告信号预示。
A significant economic downturn was presaged by falling stock prices. 股价下跌预示着严重的经济衰退。


疑问句:
Did the initial tremors presage a larger earthquake?  最初的地震是否预示着更大的地震？
Does this unusual behavior presage a more serious problem?  这种异常行为是否预示着更严重的问题？


否定句:
The initial reports did not presage the magnitude of the damage. 初步报告并没有预示到损失的规模。
The calm before the storm did not presage the ensuing chaos. 风暴前的平静并没有预示随后而来的混乱。


PHRASE->
presage disaster 预示灾难

presage the future 预示未来

词源:
From Old French *presager, from Latin praesagire "to foresee, divine," from prae- "before" + sagire "to perceive acutely, divine."
