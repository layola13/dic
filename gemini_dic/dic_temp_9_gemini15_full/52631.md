digging-out: /ˈdɪɡɪŋ aʊt/ | v. | 挖掘出来；摆脱困境；找出；（非正式）逃离 |

英文解析:  Digging-out refers to the action of excavating something from the ground or a similar enclosed space. It can also be used figuratively to describe the process of overcoming a difficult situation or finding something that is hard to locate. In informal contexts, it can mean escaping or fleeing a place.


例句:

The archaeologists spent months digging-out ancient artifacts from the site.  考古学家们花了几个月的时间从遗址中挖掘出古代文物。

He's been digging-out of debt for years. 他多年来一直在摆脱债务困境。

I finally dug-out the information I needed for the report. 我终于找到了报告所需的资料。

They dug-out of the city before the storm hit. 暴风雨来临之前，他们逃离了城市。


近义词: excavating, unearthing, extricating, escaping, retrieving, finding


反义词: burying, concealing, trapping, staying


词性: Verb (动词)
