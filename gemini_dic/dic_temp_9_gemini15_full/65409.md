euphonism:/ˈjuːfənɪzəm/ | n. |委婉语；委婉说法 |A euphonism is a mild or indirect word or expression substituted for one considered to be too harsh or blunt when referring to something unpleasant or embarrassing.  It's used to make something sound more pleasant or less offensive. | Example:  "Passed away" is a euphonism for "died."  "Let go" is a euphonism for "fired." |近义词：euphemism, polite term, soft word |反义词：dysphemism, blunt term


例句：

The company used the euphonism "right-sizing" instead of "layoffs" to soften the blow to its employees. 公司用“精简人员”这个委婉语代替“裁员”，以减轻对员工的打击。


Using the euphonism "passed away" instead of "died" can be more comforting to those grieving. 用“去世”代替“死亡”这样的委婉语，对那些正在悲伤的人来说可能更安慰。
