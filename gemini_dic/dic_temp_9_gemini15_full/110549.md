limerick:/ˈlɪmərɪk/| n. |五行打油诗|A limerick is a humorous five-line poem with a specific rhyming scheme (AABBA) and a particular rhythm.  The first, second, and fifth lines rhyme with each other and have three strong stresses; the third and fourth lines rhyme and have two strong stresses.  Limericks often have a nonsensical or absurd nature.|Example: There once was a fellow named Kyle, Whose jokes would make everyone smile. He'd tell them with glee, For all eyes to see, Then promptly would vanish a mile. |近义词：five-line poem, humorous verse|反义词：sonnet, epic poem


USAGE->
Limericks are primarily used for comedic effect and are often used to tell short, humorous stories or anecdotes.  They can be used in a variety of contexts, from casual conversation to formal literary settings (though less commonly in the latter).

Example 1:
He wrote a limerick once, and it was quite funny.
他写了一首五行打油诗，非常有趣。

Example 2:
The children enjoyed listening to the limerick.
孩子们喜欢听这首五行打油诗。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly involving the word "limerick" beyond its use as a noun referring to the poem form itself.  However, phrases describing or relating to limericks could include:

"a clever limerick",  "a silly limerick", "a bawdy limerick", "write a limerick", "recite a limerick".
