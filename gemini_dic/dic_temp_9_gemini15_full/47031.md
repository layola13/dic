/dæt/ | det. | 那，那个；什么 |  That refers to a specific person, place, thing, or idea that has already been mentioned or is easily understood from the context.  It can also be used as a pronoun, adjective, or adverb. | Example: That is my car. 那是我那辆车。That's a great idea!  那是个好主意！|近义词：this, which, those | 反义词:this


USAGE->
一般现在时:
That is my book. = 那是我那本书。
That sounds interesting. = 那听起来很有趣。

一般过去时:
That was a great movie. = 那是一部很棒的电影。
That happened last week. = 那发生在上周。

现在完成时:
That has been a long day. = 今天过得很漫长。
That has helped me a lot. = 那给了我很大帮助。

过去完成时:
That had already happened before I arrived. = 那在我到达之前就已经发生了。
That had been my plan all along. = 那一直是我的计划。

将来时:
That will be a problem. = 那将是一个问题。
That will happen tomorrow. = 那将会在明天发生。

虚拟语气:
If that were true, I would be surprised. = 如果那是真的，我会很惊讶。
I wish that were possible. = 我希望那是可能的。

被动语态:
That was decided yesterday. = 那件事昨天决定了。
That is being considered now. = 那正在被考虑。

疑问句:
Is that your bag? = 那是你的包吗？
What is that? = 那是什么？

否定句:
That is not my pen. = 那不是我的钢笔。
That wasn't the answer I expected. = 那不是我预期的答案。

PHRASE->
