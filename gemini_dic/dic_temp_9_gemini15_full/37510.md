commodities:/kəˈmɑːdətiz/ | n. | 商品，日用品 | Raw materials or primary agricultural products that can be bought and sold, such as copper or coffee.  These are usually traded on exchanges. |  The price of commodities like oil and wheat fluctuates greatly.  商品例如石油和小麦的价格波动很大。|  goods, wares, merchandise | luxury goods, services |  n.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The price of commodities affects the economy. = 商品的价格影响着经济。
We trade various commodities internationally. = 我们在国际上交易各种商品。

一般过去时:
Commodity prices fell sharply last year. = 去年商品价格大幅下降。
They exported a large quantity of commodities. = 他们出口了大量的商品。

现在完成时:
Commodity markets have become increasingly volatile. = 商品市场变得越来越动荡。
Investors have profited from commodity trading. = 投资者从商品交易中获利。

过去完成时:
Before the crisis, commodity prices had been rising steadily. = 危机爆发前，商品价格一直在稳步上涨。
They had already invested heavily in commodities. = 他们之前已经大量投资于商品。

将来时:
Commodity prices will likely increase in the coming months. = 未来几个月商品价格可能会上涨。
The company will diversify its commodity portfolio. = 公司将使其商品投资组合多样化。

虚拟语气:
If the price of commodities were lower, we would buy more. = 如果商品价格更低，我们会购买更多。
It is essential that commodities be traded fairly. = 商品交易必须公平。

被动语态:
Commodities are traded on global exchanges. = 商品在全球交易所交易。
Many commodities are affected by weather conditions. = 许多商品受天气状况影响。

疑问句:
What commodities are traded on the NYMEX? = 在纽约商业交易所交易哪些商品？
Which commodities are most affected by inflation? = 哪些商品受通货膨胀的影响最大？

否定句:
This company does not trade in agricultural commodities. = 这家公司不交易农产品。
These products are not considered primary commodities. = 这些产品不被认为是一级商品。
