lithesome:/ˈlaɪðsəm/| adj.|轻盈的；柔软的；灵活的| supple and graceful; moving or bending easily.  The dancer's lithesome movements captivated the audience.  |近义词：supple, agile, graceful, flexible|反义词：stiff, rigid, inflexible, clumsy

例句：

The lithesome dancer moved across the stage with effortless grace.  轻盈的舞者优雅地穿梭于舞台上。

Her lithesome body bent easily to pick up the fallen book. 她柔软的身子轻松地弯下拾起掉落的书。


词源：

The word "lithesome" originates from the Old English word "líð", meaning "yielding, pliant, gentle".  The suffix "-some" was added later to create the adjective, signifying "full of" or "characterized by".  Therefore, "lithesome" literally means "full of yieldingness" or "characterized by pliancy".
