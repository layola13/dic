high-mindednesses: /ˈhaɪˈmaɪndɪdnɪsəz/ | n. | 高尚情操；崇高思想；高傲自负 | The plural form of "high-mindedness," referring to instances or examples of noble principles, lofty ideals, or moral superiority. It can also carry a negative connotation, suggesting arrogance or condescension. |


例句：

His high-mindednesses were often misinterpreted as arrogance.  = 他的高尚情操常常被误解为傲慢。

The politician's speeches were full of high-mindednesses about social justice. = 这位政治家的演讲充满了关于社会正义的高尚情操。


近义词：ideals, principles, lofty aspirations, moral superiority (positive connotations);  pretentiousness, arrogance, condescension (negative connotations)


反义词：lowliness, baseness, meanness, pettiness, self-interest


词性：名词 (plural)
