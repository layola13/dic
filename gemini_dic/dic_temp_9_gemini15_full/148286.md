oversocial: /ˌoʊvərˈsoʊʃəl/ | adj. | 过度社交的，社交过度的 | Excessively sociable; tending to socialize too much.  Characterized by an excessive need for social interaction and stimulation, often to the detriment of other aspects of life. |


例句：

He's become oversocial lately, neglecting his work and family.  他最近变得过度社交，忽略了工作和家庭。

She worries she's becoming oversocial and needs to find a better balance. 她担心自己变得过度社交，需要找到更好的平衡。


近义词：gregarious, extroverted, sociable (but with a stronger emphasis on excess)


反义词：asocial, antisocial, introverted, withdrawn


词性：形容词

词源:

The word "oversocial" is a relatively modern formation. It combines the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") with the adjective "social" (relating to society or socializing).  The word reflects a growing awareness and concern about the potential negative consequences of excessive social engagement in modern society.
