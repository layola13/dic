distresses:/dɪˈstrɛsɪz/ | vt. n.|使痛苦；使忧虑；危难；不幸 | To cause someone to feel extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain.  |例句：The news of the accident deeply distressed her.  事故的消息使她非常痛苦。|例句：The constant noise distressed the baby.  持续的噪音让婴儿烦躁不安。|近义词：upset, trouble, worry, anguish |反义词：comfort, soothe, relieve


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bad news distresses me. = 这个坏消息使我苦恼。
Loud noises distress the dog. = 噪音让狗烦躁不安。

一般过去时:
The loss of her job distressed her greatly. = 失去工作让她非常痛苦。
The harsh words distressed the child. = 粗暴的话语让孩子很难过。

现在完成时:
This problem has distressed me for weeks. =  这个问题已经让我苦恼了好几个星期了。
The constant arguing has distressed the neighbours. =  不断的争吵让邻居们心烦意乱。

过去完成时:
She had been distressed by the news before she heard it officially. = 在她正式听到这个消息之前，她就为此感到不安了。
He had distressed himself worrying about the exam. = 他因为考试而过度担忧，让自己很痛苦。


将来时:
The news will distress many people. = 这个消息会让很多人心烦。
The upcoming exam will distress him. =  即将到来的考试会让他紧张不安。


虚拟语气:
I would be distressed if I lost my job. = 如果我丢了工作，我会非常痛苦。
She would be distressed if she knew the truth. = 如果她知道真相，她会很痛苦。


被动语态:
Many people were distressed by the violence. = 很多人被暴力事件弄得很痛苦。
He was distressed by the criticism. = 他被批评弄得很沮丧。


疑问句:
Does this situation distress you? =  这种情况让你苦恼吗？
What distresses you the most about this situation? =  关于这种情况，什么让你最苦恼？


否定句:
The news didn't distress him as much as I thought. =  这个消息并没有像我想象的那样让他痛苦。
This situation doesn't distress me at all. =  这种情况一点也不让我苦恼。
