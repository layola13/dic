slippy:/ˈslɪpi/| adj.| 滑的，湿滑的 | slippery describes a surface that is difficult to walk or move on because it is wet, oily, or icy.  It can also refer to something that is not reliable or trustworthy. |例句：The road was slippery after the rain. 雨后路面很滑。The ice made the pavement slippery. 冰使人行道很滑。|近义词：slippery,slick,smooth,greasy |反义词：grippy,rough,dry


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The floor is slippery. = 地面很滑。
The road is slippery today. = 今天路面很滑。

一般过去时:
The pavement was slippery yesterday. = 昨天人行道很滑。
The steps were very slippery. = 台阶非常滑。

现在完成时:
The floor has become slippery. = 地板变得很滑了。
It has been slippery all day. = 一整天都很滑。

过去完成时:
The path had been slippery before the snow cleared. = 在雪融化之前，小路一直很滑。
The stairs had become slippery after the rain. = 雨后楼梯变得很滑。

将来时:
The ice will make the roads slippery. = 冰会使道路变得很滑。
The path will be slippery tomorrow. = 明天小路会很滑。

虚拟语气:
If the road were slippery, I wouldn't drive. = 如果路滑，我就不开车了。
I wish the stairs weren't so slippery. = 我希望楼梯不要那么滑。

被动语态:
The floor is often made slippery by spilled water. = 地板经常因为洒水而变得很滑。
The road was made slippery by the ice. = 道路被冰弄得很滑。


疑问句:
Is the floor slippery? = 地板滑吗？
Are the roads slippery today? = 今天路面滑吗？

否定句:
The floor isn't slippery. = 地板不滑。
The road wasn't slippery this morning. = 今早路面不滑。
