inveiglement:/ɪnˈveɪɡlmənt/ | n. | 诱骗；巧言诱哄；欺骗 | The act of persuading someone to do something by using deception or flattery.  It often implies a gradual or subtle process of persuasion, rather than outright coercion. | 例句：His inveiglement into the scheme involved months of subtle manipulation.  他的被诱骗参与这个计划涉及数月的巧妙操纵。

The inveiglement of the young heiress was a carefully planned operation. 年轻女继承人的受骗是一个精心策划的行动。| 近义词：enticement, seduction, persuasion, deception, trickery | 反义词：honesty, frankness, straightforwardness

词源:
The word "inveiglement" comes from the verb "inveigle," which ultimately derives from the Old French word "enveogler," meaning "to entice" or "to allure."  The Old French word itself likely has Germanic roots.
