frontbench: /ˈfrʌntbentʃ/ | n. |前排座位；(尤指议会的)前排议员席位 |The seats occupied by the principal members of a legislative body, especially those in power.  The term usually refers to the leading members of the government or the opposition in a parliament. |例句：The frontbench ministers were grilled by the opposition. 前排部长们受到了反对派的严厉质询。


例句：The frontbench spokespeople delivered their party's policy statements. 前排发言人发表了他们党的政策声明。


近义词：front row, leadership (in the context of a political party)


反义词：backbench (the seats occupied by less important members)


词性：名词
