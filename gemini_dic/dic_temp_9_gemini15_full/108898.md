led-out:/ˌledˈaʊt/ | v. | 带出；引导；发出；泄露 | To guide or escort someone out of a place; to cause something to be revealed or known. | 例句：The police led the suspect out of the building.  警察把嫌疑犯带出了大楼。


例句：The company led out a new marketing campaign. 公司推出了一项新的营销活动。


近义词：escorted, guided, conducted, revealed, disclosed | 反义词：kept in, concealed, hidden | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They lead the tourists out of the museum. = 他们把游客带出了博物馆。
The company leads out a new product every year. = 公司每年都会推出一个新产品。

一般过去时:
The guide led us out of the forest. = 导游把我们带出了森林。

The investigation has led out some surprising information. = 调查已经披露了一些令人惊讶的信息。

过去完成时:
He had led out the horses before the race. = 他在比赛前已经把马带了出来。
The evidence had led out the truth about the accident. = 证据已经揭示了事故的真相。

将来时:
They will lead out the parade tomorrow. = 他们明天将带队游行。

虚拟语气:
