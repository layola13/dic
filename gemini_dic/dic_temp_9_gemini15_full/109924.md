libelled:/ˈlaɪbəld/ | v. |诽谤；中伤；污蔑 | To make a false and damaging statement about someone, especially in writing.  | The newspaper libelled the politician by publishing a false story about his private life.  这家报纸刊登了关于这位政客私生活的虚假报道，从而诽谤了他。| defame, slander, calumniate | praise, commend |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He libels his opponents regularly. = 他经常诽谤他的对手。
She libels her colleagues in her blog. = 她在博客中诽谤她的同事。

一般过去时:
They libelled the company in a newspaper article. = 他们在报纸文章中诽谤了该公司。
He libelled his former friend in a letter. = 他在一封信中诽谤了他的前朋友。

现在完成时:
The journalist has libelled many public figures. = 这名记者诽谤了许多公众人物。
She has libelled her rival in several interviews. = 她曾在几次采访中诽谤她的竞争对手。

过去完成时:
They had libelled the professor before he could sue them. = 他们在教授起诉他们之前就诽谤了他。
The newspaper had libelled the mayor for years before the truth emerged. = 在真相大白之前，这家报纸多年来一直在诽谤市长。

将来时:
They will libel him again if given the chance. = 如果有机会，他们会再次诽谤他。
She will libel her competitor in the next election campaign. = 她将在下届竞选活动中诽谤她的竞争对手。

虚拟语气:
If he libelled me, I would sue him. = 如果他诽谤我，我会起诉他。
I wish they hadn't libelled my reputation. = 我真希望他们没有诽谤我的名誉。

被动语态:
He was libelled by the tabloid press. = 他被小报诽谤了。
The company was libelled in a series of online posts. = 这家公司在一系列在线帖子中受到了诽谤。

疑问句:
Was he libelled by the article? = 他被这篇文章诽谤了吗？
Have they been libelled in the past? = 他们过去被诽谤过吗？

否定句:
He did not libel her, despite the rumors. = 尽管有谣言，但他并没有诽谤她。
They have not libelled anyone in their publications. = 他们的出版物中没有诽谤任何人。
