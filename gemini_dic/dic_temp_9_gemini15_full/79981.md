go-down:/ɡoʊ daʊn/ | v. n. |下降；倒塌；被理解；（非正式）派对；仓库 |  to move or fall to a lower level; to collapse; to be understood or accepted; (informal) a party; a warehouse | 近义词：descend, collapse, sink, party, storehouse | 反义词：ascend, rise, elevate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun goes down at 7 pm. = 太阳在下午7点落下。
The price of oil is going down. = 油价正在下降。


一般过去时:
The building went down in the earthquake. = 这座建筑在地震中倒塌了。
He went down with the flu. = 他得了流感。


现在完成时:
The temperature has gone down significantly. = 气温已经显著下降了。
The popularity of that band has gone down. = 那个乐队的受欢迎程度下降了。


过去完成时:
The stock market had gone down sharply before the news broke. = 在消息传出之前，股市已经暴跌了。
She had gone down with a bad cold before the trip. = 在旅行之前，她已经得了重感冒。


将来时:
The plane will go down if the engine fails. = 如果引擎故障，飞机将会坠毁。
The interest rates are expected to go down next month. = 预计下个月利率将会下降。


虚拟语气:
If the price went down, I would buy it. = 如果价格下降，我会买它。
I wish the noise would go down. = 我希望噪音能小一些。


被动语态:
The plane was brought down by enemy fire. = 飞机被敌人的炮火击落。
The building was going to be gone down for reconstruction. = 这座建筑将被拆除重建。


疑问句:
Does the price go down on weekends? = 周末价格会下降吗？
What time does the sun go down today? = 今天太阳几点落下？


否定句:
The plane didn't go down. = 飞机没有坠毁。
The inflation rate didn't go down as expected. = 通货膨胀率没有像预期的那样下降。



PHRASE->
go down well/badly:  被…接受/不被…接受  (The new policy didn't go down well with the employees. = 新政策没有得到员工的认可。)
go down in history:  载入史册 (His achievements will go down in history. = 他的成就将载入史册。)
go down with: 染上（疾病） (He went down with a bad cold. = 他得了重感冒。)
go down on:  (非正式) 口交 (This is inappropriate for this context.)
go down to:  去… (We went down to the beach. = 我们去了海滩。)
