noncircumstantial: /ˌnɒnˌsɜːrkəmˈstænʃəl/ | adj. |非偶然的；非情况性的；直接的；确凿的 | Not based on circumstantial evidence; direct and conclusive.  It emphasizes a direct and undeniable connection, lacking ambiguity or indirect inference.  |


例句：

The noncircumstantial evidence proved his guilt beyond a reasonable doubt.  = 非偶然证据证明了他的罪行，毫无疑问。

The detective needed noncircumstantial proof before making an arrest. = 侦探需要确凿的证据才能逮捕他。


近义词：direct, conclusive, definitive, undeniable, explicit

反义词：circumstantial, indirect, ambiguous, inferential


词源：

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not."
* **circumstantial**: Adjective derived from "circumstance," ultimately from Latin *circumstantia* ("standing around," "surrounding circumstances").


用法说明：

由于该词是形容词，它主要用来修饰名词，例如证据、事实、证明等。它通常与circumstantial (情况性的，间接的)  形成对比，强调证据的直接性和确定性。  在法律和侦探小说中，这个词经常出现，用来描述直接指向罪犯或事件的证据，而不是依赖于推断和间接证据。
