exhaled:/ɪkˈseɪld/| v.|呼气，吐气|To breathe out.  The act of breathing out air from the lungs.|例句：He exhaled slowly, his shoulders slumping with relief. 他慢慢地呼气，肩膀放松地松懈下来。
例句：After the race, she exhaled deeply, feeling the cool air fill her lungs.  赛跑后，她深深地呼气，感到凉爽的空气充满了她的肺部。|近义词：breathed out, expelled (air)|反义词：inhaled|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exhales deeply before each dive. = 他每次潜水前都会深呼吸。
She exhales a sigh of relief. = 她舒了一口气。

一般过去时:
He exhaled a plume of smoke. = 他吐出一缕烟。
She exhaled sharply when she heard the news. = 听到这个消息，她猛地吸了一口气。

现在完成时:
I have exhaled all the air from my lungs. = 我已经呼出了肺部所有的空气。
He has exhaled a cloud of dust. = 他已经呼出一团灰尘。

过去完成时:
She had exhaled all her breath before she could speak. = 在她说话之前，她已经呼出了所有的气息。
He had exhaled a long sigh of exhaustion. = 他已经深深地叹了一口气，表示疲惫。

将来时:
I will exhale slowly and calmly. = 我会慢慢地、平静地呼气。
She will exhale the smoke once she's finished. = 她完成后会吐出烟。

虚拟语气:
If I were calmer, I would exhale more easily. = 如果我更冷静，我会更容易呼气。
I wish I could exhale all my stress. = 我希望我能释放我所有的压力。

被动语态:
Air is exhaled from the lungs. = 空气从肺部呼出。
The smoke was exhaled slowly and deliberately. = 烟雾被慢慢地、故意地呼出。

否定句:
