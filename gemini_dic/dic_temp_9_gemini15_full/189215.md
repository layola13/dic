sacrilege:/ˈsækrɪlɪdʒ/| n.|亵渎；渎圣|The act of violating or profaning something sacred; an act of disrespect towards something considered sacred.  |例句：The destruction of the ancient temple was considered a sacrilege.  古代寺庙的破坏被认为是亵渎神灵的行为。|例句：To steal from a church is a sacrilege. 从教堂偷东西是亵渎神灵的行为。|近义词：desecration, profanation, blasphemy|反义词：reverence, veneration, sanctification|名词

He commits sacrilege by disrespecting religious symbols. = 他不尊重宗教象征，犯下了亵渎罪。

否定句:

词源:
From Old French *sacrilege, from Latin sacrilegium, from sacer "sacred" + legere "to gather, collect".  The original meaning referred to the theft of sacred objects.  The meaning has broadened over time to include any act of disrespect towards something sacred.
