naming:/ˈneɪmɪŋ/ | n. |命名；名称；提名 |The act of giving a name to something or someone.  It can also refer to the process of choosing a name, or the name itself. |例句：The naming of the new baby was a joyous occasion.  新婴儿的命名是一个令人高兴的场合。|例句：The naming ceremony was held in the church.  命名仪式在教堂举行。|近义词：nomination, designation, christening |反义词：unnaming (less common)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company is considering new naming conventions. = 公司正在考虑新的命名约定。
She is naming her daughter after her grandmother. = 她以祖母的名字为女儿命名。

一般过去时:
They named their son John. = 他们给他们的儿子取名约翰。
The project was officially named "Project X". = 该项目正式命名为“X计划”。

现在完成时:
The committee has completed the naming process. = 委员会已经完成了命名过程。
They have already named their new product. = 他们已经为他们的新产品命名了。

过去完成时:
Before the launch, they had already named the product. = 在发布之前，他们已经为产品命名了。
She had carefully considered various names before naming her pet. = 在为她的宠物命名之前，她已经仔细考虑了各种名字。


将来时:
We will name our new dog later this week. = 我们将在本周末晚些时候为我们的新狗命名。
The board will announce the official naming next month. = 董事会将在下个月宣布正式命名。


虚拟语气:
If I were naming the company, I would choose a different name. = 如果我来命名公司，我会选择一个不同的名字。
I wish I had named my cat something more original. = 我希望我给我的猫取个更原创的名字。


被动语态:
The new building was named after a famous scientist. = 这座新建筑以一位著名科学家的名字命名。
The product is being named by a marketing team. = 产品正在由一个营销团队命名。


疑问句:
What are you naming your child? = 你要给你孩子取什么名字？
What will the new software be named? = 新软件将被命名为什么？


否定句:
They did not name their child until he was a year old. = 直到他一岁的时候，他们才给他取名字。
The company has not yet decided on a name for the new product. = 公司尚未决定新产品的名称。
