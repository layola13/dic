posed:/poʊzd/| /pəʊzd/ | v. |摆姿势；提出；造成；假定 |to assume a particular posture, typically one that is considered attractive or impressive; to present (a question or problem) for consideration; to cause (a problem or difficulty); to assume something as a fact or basis for reasoning |例句：She posed for a photograph. 她摆好姿势拍照。 He posed a difficult question. 他提出了一个难题。|近义词：present, propose, suggest, assume |反义词：answer, solve


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He poses for photos every day. = 他每天都摆姿势拍照。
She poses a serious threat to our team. = 她对我们团队构成严重威胁。

一般过去时:
The model posed gracefully for the camera. = 模特优雅地为摄影机摆姿势。
He posed the question to the audience. = 他向观众提出了这个问题。

现在完成时:
The artist has posed the model in several different positions. = 艺术家已经让模特摆了几个不同的姿势。
She has posed a challenge to the current system. = 她向现行体制提出了挑战。

过去完成时:
The photographer had posed the subject before the lighting was set up. = 摄影师在灯光设置好之前就已经让拍摄对象摆好姿势了。
They had posed the problem for weeks before finding a solution. = 他们提出这个问题已经几个星期了，才找到解决方案。

将来时:
The actress will pose for the magazine cover tomorrow. = 女演员明天将为杂志封面摆姿势。
The professor will pose a series of questions during the lecture. = 教授将在讲座期间提出一系列问题。

虚拟语气:
If she posed for the painting, it would be a masterpiece. = 如果她为这幅画摆姿势，它将是一部杰作。
If he had posed the question differently, he might have received a better response. = 如果他把问题问得不一样，他可能会得到更好的回应。

被动语态:

The model was posed in a dramatic light. = 模特被安排在一个戏剧性的光线下摆姿势。

疑问句:

否定句:
