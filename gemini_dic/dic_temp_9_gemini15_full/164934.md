pregratifications: /ˌpriːˌɡrætifiˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 预先满足；提前满足 |  The act of satisfying oneself before a desired event or experience; anticipatory gratification.  This word is rarely used and is considered a neologism or a less common coinage. It implies a satisfaction derived from the *anticipation* of a future pleasure, rather than the pleasure itself. | Example:  His pregratifications in planning the trip overshadowed the actual journey. 他在计划旅行过程中的预先满足感掩盖了旅行本身的乐趣。 | 近义词: anticipation, foretaste, prelibation | 反义词: disappointment, frustration |  词性: noun
