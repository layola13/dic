USAGE->
This word is an adjective, and as such, it doesn't have tenses like verbs.  It describes a noun.  We can use it in various sentence structures.


Example Sentences with "nonclaimable":

Affirmative Sentence:
The expenses incurred during the trip were deemed nonclaimable. 旅行期间发生的费用被认为是不可报销的。

Negative Sentence:
The damage wasn't nonclaimable; we received full compensation.  损坏并非不可赔付；我们获得了全额赔偿。

Question Sentence:
Is this loss nonclaimable under our current policy?  根据我们目前的政策，这笔损失是否不可索赔？

Word Origin:

The word "nonclaimable" is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the word "claimable."  "Claimable" itself derives from the verb "claim," meaning to state that something is true or that something belongs to you.  The suffix "-able" indicates that something can be done or is capable of being done. Therefore, "nonclaimable" signifies something that cannot be claimed.
