destining:/ˈdɛstɪnɪŋ/| v.|注定，预定；指定；使注定|To determine or decide beforehand; to be destined for a particular fate or purpose.  It implies an inevitable or predetermined course of events.|例句：The stars seemed to be destining him for greatness.  星辰似乎注定他要成就一番伟业。

例句：They were destining the new recruit to a challenging assignment. 他们把这项具有挑战性的任务指派给了新兵。|近义词：ordaining, appointing, predetermining|反义词：leaving to chance, leaving undecided|词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is destining his life to helping others. = 他将自己的一生奉献给了帮助他人。
She destines her spare time to painting. = 她把空闲时间都用来绘画。


一般过去时:
They destined the land for agricultural use. = 他们将这块土地指定用于农业用途。
The ancient prophecy destined the hero to defeat the dragon. = 古代预言注定英雄将打败恶龙。


现在完成时:
Fate has destined me to a life of hardship. = 命运注定我要过一种艰苦的生活。
We have destined a significant portion of our budget for research and development. = 我们已经将预算中很大一部分拨给了研发。


过去完成时:
They had destined the entire inheritance to their only son. = 他们已经将全部遗产指定给了他们的独子。
History had destined them to be rivals. = 历史注定他们成为对手。


将来时:
The company will be destining more resources to its online marketing. = 公司将把更多资源投入到在线营销中。
I believe this path will destine me to success. = 我相信这条道路将使我走向成功。


虚拟语气:
If fate had not destined him to this path, he might have lived a different life. = 如果命运没有注定他走这条路，他或许会过着不同的生活。
I wish I had not destined myself to such a difficult task. = 我真希望自己没有给自己安排这么困难的任务。


被动语态:
The award was destined for the most innovative project. = 该奖项被指定授予最具创新性的项目。
His future was destined to be filled with challenges. = 他的未来注定充满挑战。


疑问句:
What destiny does the oracle destine for me? = 预言家为我预言了什么命运？
Is this path destined to lead to success? = 这条道路注定通向成功吗？


否定句:
They did not destine their fortune to charity. = 他们没有将自己的财富捐献给慈善机构。
He was not destined for a life of luxury. = 他并非注定要过奢侈的生活。


PHRASE->

destined to succeed = 注定要成功
destined to meet = 注定要相遇
self-destined = 自我决定; 注定
