/praɪzɪz/| n.|奖品；奖赏；战利品|Prizes are awarded to the winners of the competition. 奖品颁发给比赛的获胜者。|奖赏，报酬，奖励|rewards, awards, recompense|punishments, penalties|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company prizes innovation. = 公司重视创新。
He prizes his family above all else. = 他把家人看得比什么都重要。

一般过去时:
They prized the antique vase highly. = 他们非常珍视那个古董花瓶。
The Romans prized gold and silver. = 罗马人珍视黄金和白银。

现在完成时:
She has prized her education throughout her life. = 她一生都珍视她的教育。
We have prized his contributions to the team. = 我们一直珍视他对团队的贡献。

过去完成时:
They had prized the artifact before its discovery. = 他们早在发现之前就珍视这件文物了。
He had prized his freedom more than anything else. = 他比任何东西都更珍视他的自由。

将来时:
Future generations will prize this artwork. = 后代将珍视这件艺术品。
They will prize the opportunity to learn. = 他们将珍惜学习的机会。

虚拟语气:
If I were to win, I would prize the recognition more than the money. = 如果我赢了，我会更看重认可而不是金钱。
I wish I had prized my time more when I was younger. = 我希望我年轻的时候更珍惜我的时间。

被动语态:
Innovation is prized by this company. = 这家公司重视创新。

疑问句:

否定句:
