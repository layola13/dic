go-on-the-stage:/ɡoʊ ɑːn ðə steɪdʒ/ | phr. v. | 上台表演；登台演出 | To perform or appear on a stage.  This phrase describes the action of a performer going onto a stage to perform. | Example:  She nervously went on the stage to deliver her speech. 她紧张地上台发表演讲。 He went on the stage and sang a beautiful song. 他上台唱了一首优美的歌曲。 | 近义词: take the stage, appear on stage, perform | 反义词: leave the stage, exit the stage


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They go on the stage every night. = 他们每晚都上台表演。
The band goes on the stage at 8 pm. = 乐队晚上8点上台。


一般过去时:
She went on the stage and sang a song. = 她上台唱了一首歌。
The actors went on the stage after the curtain rose. = 幕布升起后，演员们上台了。


现在完成时:
He has gone on the stage many times. = 他已经多次上台表演了。
The singer has never gone on the stage without being nervous. =这位歌手上台表演从没有不紧张过。


过去完成时:
By the time the music started, she had already gone on the stage. = 音乐开始的时候，她已经上台了。
Before he went on the stage, he had practiced for hours. = 上台前，他已经练习了几个小时。


将来时:
She will go on the stage next week. = 她下周将上台表演。
The comedian will go on the stage after the opening act. = 喜剧演员将在开场表演之后上台。


虚拟语气:
If I were brave enough, I would go on the stage. = 如果我足够勇敢，我会上台。
I wish I could go on the stage and sing. = 我希望我能上台唱歌。


被动语态: *(This phrase is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes an active action.)*


疑问句:
Will they go on the stage tonight? = 他们今晚会上台表演吗？
When does the main act go on the stage? = 主角什么时候上台？


否定句:
He didn't go on the stage because he was sick. = 他因为生病而没有上台。
She refused to go on the stage. = 她拒绝上台。


PHRASE->
go on the stage and sing = 上台唱歌
go on the stage to perform = 上台表演
go on the stage with confidence = 充满自信地上台
go on the stage to speak = 上台演讲
go on the stage alone = 单独上台
