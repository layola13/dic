peert:/pɪər/| n. |同龄人，伙伴|A person of the same age, status, or ability as another.|The children played happily with their peert.|近义词：comrade, equal, companion|反义词：senior, superior, inferior|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a peer of mine. = 他是我的同龄人。
They are peers in the company. = 他们在公司是同事。


一般过去时:
She was a peer of the princess. = 她是公主的同龄人。
He was a peer in the senate. = 他是参议院的议员。


现在完成时:
He has been a peer of mine for many years. = 他多年来一直是我的同龄人。
She has always been a peer to be reckoned with. = 她一直是一位值得尊敬的同龄人。


过去完成时:
He had been a peer of the deceased. = 他曾是死者的同龄人。
She had been a peer in the field before she retired. = 在她退休之前，她一直是该领域的专家。


将来时:
He will be a peer in the new company. = 他将成为这家新公司的同事。
She will remain a peer in the industry. = 她将继续在该行业中保持同等地位。


虚拟语气:
If he were my peer, I would trust him more. = 如果他是我的同龄人，我会更信任他。
I wish I had a peer to confide in. = 我希望我有一个可以信赖的同龄人。


被动语态:
He was considered a peer among his colleagues. = 他被认为是同事中的同等地位者。
She was appointed as a peer in the House of Lords. = 她被任命为上议院议员。


疑问句:
Is he a peer of yours? = 他是你的同龄人吗？
Are they peers in the same profession? = 他们在同一个行业中是同等地位吗？


否定句:
He is not a peer of mine. = 他不是我的同龄人。
They are not peers in terms of experience. = 就经验而言，他们并非同等地位。
