preconsigning: /ˌpriːkənˈsaɪnɪŋ/ | v. | 预先托运，预先交付 |  The act of sending goods or information ahead of time, typically for logistical purposes.  It often involves arranging for transportation or storage before the goods are ready for shipment. | 例句：The company is preconsigning the shipment to ensure timely delivery. = 公司正在预先托运货物以确保及时送达。

The exporter preconsigned the documents to the importer's agent. = 出口商将文件预先交付给进口商的代理人。


近义词：pre-shipment, advance shipment, forward shipment | 反义词：post-consigning (not a standard word)


词源:  The word is a combination of "pre-" (meaning before) and "consigning" (the act of entrusting something to someone's care, typically for transportation or sale).  "Consigning" originates from the Old French word "consigner," meaning "to entrust" or "to sign over."
