indoctrinations:/ɪnˌdɒktrɪˈneɪʃənz/ | n. |灌输；教条灌输 |The process of teaching someone to accept a set of beliefs uncritically.  It often implies a forceful or manipulative method. |例句：The indoctrination of children into extremist ideologies is a serious concern. 儿童被灌输极端思想是一个严重的问题。


例句：The cult's indoctrination techniques were designed to break down individual will. 该邪教的灌输技术旨在摧毁个人的意志。


近义词：brainwashing, proselytization, inculcation, conditioning


反义词：open-mindedness, critical thinking, education (in the sense of fostering critical analysis)


词源:  From "indoctrinate," which itself comes from the Latin "in-" (into) + "doctrina" (teaching, doctrine).  The suffix "-ation" forms a noun, indicating the action or process.
