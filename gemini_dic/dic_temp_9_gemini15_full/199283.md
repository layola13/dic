silverfishes: /ˈsɪlvərˌfɪʃɪz/ | n. | 蠹鱼；衣鱼 | Small, wingless insects with silvery scales, often found in damp places, feeding on paper, starch, and other organic materials. They are considered pests in homes and libraries.  | Example:  I found several silverfish in the old book I was reading. 我在我读的那本旧书里发现了几只衣鱼。

The presence of silverfish in my pantry indicates a moisture problem. 我储藏室里出现衣鱼表明存在潮湿问题。 | 近义词：fishmoths (less common) | 反义词：None (no direct antonym)

词源:
The word "silverfish" is a descriptive name referring to the insect's silvery, fish-like appearance.  "Silver" refers to the color of their scales, and "fish" refers to their elongated, somewhat fish-like body shape.  The word is a compound word, combining "silver" and "fish".
