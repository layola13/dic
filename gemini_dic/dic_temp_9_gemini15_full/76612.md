gallnut:/ˈɡɔːlnʌt/| n. | 栎实，五倍子 | A gallnut is an abnormal growth on a plant, typically a tree such as an oak, caused by the presence of a gall wasp larva.  These growths are often round and hard, and can vary in size and color. They have been historically used in various applications, including tanning leather and making ink. | Example: The ancient Egyptians used gallnuts to create durable inks for their scrolls.  = 古埃及人用五倍子制作了耐用的墨水用于他们的卷轴。  The artisan carefully selected the gallnuts for their size and color before using them in the tanning process. = 工匠在鞣制过程中仔细挑选了大小和颜色合适的五倍子。 |近义词: gall, oak apple | 反义词:  (没有直接的反义词，可以考虑与其作用相反的事物，例如：normal growth)
