layabout:/ˈleɪəbaʊt/| n. adj.|懒汉，游手好闲的人；懒惰的，游手好闲的| A layabout is a person who is lazy and avoids work.  They are often idle and unproductive.|He's become a complete layabout since he lost his job. 他自从失业后就变成了一个十足的懒汉。|近义词：loafer, idler, sluggard, good-for-nothing|反义词：hard worker, diligent, industrious


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is a layabout. = 他是个懒汉。
She is always a layabout. = 她总是懒惰的。


一般过去时:
He was a layabout in his youth. = 他年轻时是个懒汉。
They were layabouts, always avoiding work. = 他们都是懒汉，总是逃避工作。


现在完成时:
He has been a layabout for years. = 他多年来一直是个懒汉。
She has become a layabout since the accident. = 自从事故发生后，她变得懒惰了。


过去完成时:
He had been a layabout before he found his passion. = 在他找到自己的热情之前，他一直是个懒汉。
They had been layabouts for so long that it was hard for them to change. = 他们当了这么久的懒汉，很难改变。


将来时:
He will remain a layabout unless he changes his ways. = 除非他改变自己的方式，否则他将继续做一个懒汉。
She will likely become a layabout if she continues to avoid responsibilities. = 如果她继续逃避责任，她很可能会变成一个懒汉。


虚拟语气:
If he weren't such a layabout, he would have a better life. = 如果他不那么懒惰，他会过上更好的生活。
I wish he weren't such a layabout. = 我希望他不那么懒惰。


被动语态:
(Layabout is rarely used in the passive voice because it's a noun and an adjective, not a verb.)


疑问句:
Is he a layabout? = 他是个懒汉吗？
Are they layabouts? = 他们是懒汉吗？


否定句:
He is not a layabout; he's just tired. = 他不是懒汉；他只是累了。
They are not layabouts; they just need more motivation. = 他们不是懒汉；他们只是需要更多动力。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases specifically using "layabout."  The word itself functions as a complete descriptor.
