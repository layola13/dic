lacquers:/ˈlækərz/ | n. v. |清漆；漆器；用漆涂饰 |A lacquer is a type of paint or varnish that dries to a hard, shiny surface.  It can be used to coat various materials, including wood, metal, and ceramics.  The verb "to lacquer" means to coat something with lacquer. |例句：The antique table was covered in a beautiful, red lacquer. 这张古董桌子涂着美丽的红色清漆。

The artist lacquered the wooden bowl with several coats for a durable finish. 艺术家给木碗涂了好几层漆，以获得耐用的表面。|近义词：varnish, paint, enamel |反义词：unvarnished, unpainted

词源:
The word "lacquer" comes from the Hindi word "lakh," referring to the resinous substance secreted by the lac insect.  This resin was historically a key ingredient in lacquers.  The word entered English through French and Portuguese.
