dissimule:/dɪˈsɪmjʊl/ | vt. |掩饰；隐藏；伪装 | To hide or disguise one's true feelings or intentions. |例句：He dissimulated his anger, but I could see it in his eyes. 他掩饰了他的怒火，但我从他的眼神中看出来了。|例句：She dissimulated her fear by putting on a brave face. 她强作镇定来掩饰她的恐惧。|近义词：conceal, mask, disguise, feign |反义词：reveal, expose, show

词源:
From Old French dissimuler, from Latin dissimulare, from dis- ("not") + simulare ("to imitate, feign").  The word ultimately derives from the idea of "not showing one's true self," hence "to disguise" or "to conceal."
