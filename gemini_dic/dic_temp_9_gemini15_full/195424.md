seminationalism:/ˌsɛmɪˈneɪʃənəlɪzəm/| n. |半民族主义；准民族主义|Seminationalism refers to a political ideology or movement that exhibits some, but not all, characteristics of nationalism.  It might prioritize certain aspects of national identity or interests while remaining less focused on or rejecting other aspects typically associated with full-blown nationalism.  This could manifest in various ways, such as a focus on regional identity within a larger nation,  a selective embrace of national symbols or traditions, or a prioritization of economic interests over strict adherence to nationalist principles.  The term often implies a less aggressive or exclusionary form of nationalism than its more extreme counterparts.|The rise of seminationalism in the region reflects a complex interplay of cultural and economic factors.|近义词：regionalism, subnationalism, localism|反义词：globalism, internationalism, cosmopolitanism
