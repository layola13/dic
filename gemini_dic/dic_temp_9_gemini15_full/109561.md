let-down:/ˈlɛtdaʊn/| n. v.|令人失望的事；使失望|A letdown is something that is disappointing because it is not as good as you expected or hoped for. It can refer to a situation, an event, or a person's behavior.  As a verb, it means to disappoint someone.|例句：The party was a real letdown.  派对真是令人失望。

The new software turned out to be a huge letdown.  新软件结果非常令人失望。


近义词：disappointment, disillusionment, disappointment, failure|反义词：success, fulfillment, delight
