gannet:/ˈɡænɪt/| n. |塘鹅|A large seabird with a long, pointed bill, short legs, and webbed feet, known for its spectacular diving for fish.  Gannet colonies are often found on cliffs and islands. They are skilled fliers and divers, capable of plunging into the water at high speeds to catch fish. |The gannet plunged from a great height to catch a fish.  塘鹅从高空俯冲下来捕鱼。  |  A large group of gannets was spotted on the cliff.  一群塘鹅被发现栖息在悬崖上。|  petrel, albatross |  (no direct antonym)
