drafted:/ˈdræftɪd/ | v. | 起草；选拔；征募 | To prepare a written document; to select someone for a team or position; to forcibly enroll someone into military service. |  例句：The lawyer drafted a contract for the sale of the house. 律师起草了一份房屋买卖合同。|  例句：He was drafted into the army. 他被征入伍。| 近义词：composed, formulated, drawn up, recruited, conscripted | 反义词：finalized, completed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They draft a new policy every year. = 他们每年起草一项新政策。
The team drafts new players annually. = 球队每年选拔新球员。


一般过去时:
She drafted a letter to her friend. = 她给朋友起草了一封信。
The company drafted a new marketing plan. = 公司起草了一个新的营销计划。


现在完成时:
They have drafted the final version of the report. = 他们已经起草了报告的最终版本。
The government has drafted many new laws recently. = 政府最近起草了许多新法律。


过去完成时:
He had drafted the speech before the meeting. = 他在会议前已经起草好了演讲稿。
The team had drafted three players before the season started. = 球季开始前，球队已经选拔了三名球员。

虚拟语气:

If they had drafted a stronger defense, they might have won the game. = 如果他们制定了更强的防守策略，他们也许会赢得比赛。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
