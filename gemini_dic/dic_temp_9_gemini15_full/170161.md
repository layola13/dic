protoorganism:/ˌproʊtoʊˈɔːrɡənɪzəm/| n. |原生物，原始生物|A hypothetical very early form of life, preceding the development of cells with a nucleus (eukaryotes).  It is believed to be a simple self-replicating entity, possibly a self-assembling protein or RNA molecule.  The exact nature of protoorganisms remains a subject of ongoing scientific research. |Example: The study of protoorganisms helps us understand the origin of life on Earth.  原生物的研究帮助我们理解地球上生命的起源。

The concept of protoorganisms is essential to understanding abiogenesis, the process by which life arose from non-living matter.  |近义词：precursor, ancestor, early life form |反义词：eukaryote (in the context of cellular organization)
