fail:/feɪl/| v. n. adj.|失败；不及格；衰竭| to be unsuccessful in achieving or completing something; to be insufficient or inadequate.  |例句：He failed his driving test. 他驾驶考试不及格。

例句：The engine failed.  引擎失灵了。


近义词：  miscarry,  flunk,  flop


反义词： succeed,  pass,  thrive


词性：动词，名词，形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fails to understand the instructions. 他理解不了这些说明。
The system fails regularly. 系统经常故障。


一般过去时:
She failed her exam. 她考试不及格。
My attempt to bake a cake failed miserably. 我尝试烤蛋糕惨遭失败。


现在完成时:
He has failed to meet his deadlines. 他没有按时完成最后期限的任务。
The project has failed to achieve its objectives.  该项目未能实现其目标。


过去完成时:
I had failed to notice the warning sign. 我没有注意到警告标志。
They had failed to anticipate the problem. 他们没有预料到这个问题。


将来时:
The mission will likely fail.  任务很可能会失败。
If you don't study, you will fail the test.  如果你不学习，你考试会不及格。


虚拟语气:
If I had studied harder, I wouldn't have failed the exam. 如果我学习更努力，我就不会考试不及格。
I wish I hadn't failed to tell him the truth. 我真希望我没有忘记告诉他真相。


被动语态:
The plan was failed by lack of funding.  由于缺乏资金，计划失败了。
The experiment was deemed to have failed.  实验被认为失败了。


疑问句:
Did you fail the test? 你考试不及格了吗？
Will the project fail?  项目会失败吗？


否定句:
He did not fail to impress us. 他确实给我们留下了深刻印象。
The system has not failed yet.  系统还没有出现故障。


PHRASE->
fail to:  未能
fail safe:  安全失效
fail-safe mechanism:  安全机制
fail miserably:  惨败
fail in: 在...方面失败


词源:
The word "fail" originates from Old French *faillir, meaning "to fail, to err, to be lacking," and ultimately from Latin *fallere, meaning "to deceive, to disappoint."  The sense of "not succeeding" evolved from the earlier meaning of "to be deficient" or "to be wanting."
