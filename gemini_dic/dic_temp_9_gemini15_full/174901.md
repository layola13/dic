racoons:/ræˈkuːnz/| n. |浣熊|A raccoon is a medium-sized mammal with grayish-brown fur, a black mask around its eyes, and a bushy tail. They are known for their intelligence and dexterity, often using their paws to manipulate objects.  They are omnivores and are found in North America, Central America, and parts of South America.|Example:  The raccoons raided the garbage cans last night.  昨晚浣熊袭击了垃圾桶。

The raccoons were skillfully opening the jar. 浣熊熟练地打开了罐子。|近义词：procyon (学名)|反义词：无
