hyperconformists: /ˌhaɪpərˈkɒnfɔːmɪsts/ | n. |过度顺从者，极端墨守成规者 | Individuals who excessively adhere to social norms and expectations, often to the point of suppressing their individuality and independent thought. They demonstrate an extreme level of conformity, exceeding what is typically considered socially acceptable.  | Example:  The hyperconformists in the group stifled any dissenting opinions, ensuring unanimous agreement regardless of individual beliefs.  过度顺从的群体成员压制了任何异议，确保了一致的意见，无论个人的信仰如何。 |近义词:  conformists, yes-men, followers |反义词: nonconformists, rebels, individualists
