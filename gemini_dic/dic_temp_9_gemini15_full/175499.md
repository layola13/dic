ramrodding: /ræmˈrɒdɪŋ/ | v. |  用力塞入；强行推进；粗暴地强迫 | The act of forcefully pushing or shoving something into a confined space, often implying a lack of care or finesse. It often refers to the process of pushing a ramrod down a firearm barrel to seat a charge.  | Example:  They ramrodded the legislation through parliament without proper debate.  他们不经适当辩论就强行通过了这项法案。  |近义词: jamming, forcing, shoving, pushing | 反义词: gently inserting, carefully placing
