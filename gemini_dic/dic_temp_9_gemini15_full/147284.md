overmobilized: /ˌoʊvərˈmoʊbəlaɪzd/ | adj. |过度动员的，动员过度 |  Describes a situation where resources, personnel, or capabilities have been mobilized to an excessive or unnecessary degree, often leading to inefficiency or counterproductive results.  It implies that the mobilization effort was too large, too intense, or misdirected. |

例句：The company's overmobilized response to the minor software glitch caused more problems than it solved. 公司对微小软件故障做出的过度反应反而导致了更多问题。

例句：The army was overmobilized for the relatively small-scale conflict, resulting in logistical nightmares and wasted resources.  军队为这场相对小规模的冲突进行了过度动员，导致后勤噩梦和资源浪费。

近义词：overextended, overstretched, overcommitted, excessively mobilized

反义词：undermobilized, insufficiently mobilized


词源:  The word is formed by combining the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") with the verb "mobilize" (to prepare and organize for action or war).  "Mobilize" itself originates from the French word "mobiliser," ultimately deriving from the Latin word "mobilis" meaning "movable."
