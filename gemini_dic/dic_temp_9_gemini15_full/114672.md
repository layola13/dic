malign:/məˈlaɪn/| adj., v.|恶意的；诽谤；中伤 | to speak about (someone) in a way that is intended to harm their reputation;  to speak ill of; to represent in a bad light. |例句：He was maligned by the press. 他受到媒体的恶意中伤。  The politician's opponents tried to malign his character.  这位政治家的对手试图诽谤他的品格。|近义词：defame, slander, vilify, traduce|反义词：praise, commend, laud


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often maligns his rivals. = 他经常诽谤他的对手。
They malign their competitors. = 他们中伤他们的竞争对手。

一般过去时:
She maligned him in the newspaper. = 她在报纸上诽谤了他。
The article maligned the company's reputation. = 这篇文章损害了公司的声誉。

现在完成时:
His enemies have maligned him for years. = 他的敌人多年来一直在诽谤他。
The press has maligned the innocent man. = 媒体诽谤了这个无辜的人。

过去完成时:
They had maligned her before she even spoke. = 甚至在她开口之前，他们就已经诽谤她了。
The rumors had maligned his family name for generations. = 流言蜚语几代以来一直玷污着他的家族声誉。

将来时:
They will malign him in the upcoming election. = 他们在即将到来的选举中会诽谤他。
The media will likely malign her campaign. = 媒体很可能会诽谤她的竞选活动。

虚拟语气:
If they maligned him, he would sue them. = 如果他们诽谤他，他会起诉他们。
I wish they hadn't maligned her reputation. = 我真希望他们没有诽谤她的名誉。

被动语态:
He was maligned by the media. = 他被媒体诽谤了。
The candidate was maligned by his opponents. = 该候选人被对手诽谤了。

疑问句:
Were they maligned by the press? = 他们被媒体诽谤了吗？
Has he been maligned unjustly? = 他是否受到了不公平的诽谤？

否定句:
They did not malign his character. = 他们没有诽谤他的品格。
She would never malign her friends. = 她永远不会诽谤她的朋友。
