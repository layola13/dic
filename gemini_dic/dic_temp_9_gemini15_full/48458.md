deemed:/diːmd/ | v. |认为，视为，相信|To consider or judge something in a particular way.  It often implies a formal or official judgment.|例句：The building was deemed unsafe and was subsequently demolished. 这座建筑被认为不安全，随后被拆除。 The plan was deemed impractical and abandoned.  该计划被认为不切实际而被放弃。 |近义词：considered, judged, regarded, held, estimated |反义词：disregarded, overlooked, ignored |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deems it necessary to attend the meeting. = 他认为参加会议是必要的。
She deems her work to be of high quality. = 她认为自己的工作质量很高。


一般过去时:
The committee deemed the proposal unacceptable. = 委员会认为该提案不可接受。
The judge deemed the evidence insufficient. = 法官认为证据不足。


现在完成时:
They have deemed the situation to be critical. = 他们已经认为情况危急。
We have deemed it appropriate to inform you immediately. = 我们认为立即通知你是合适的。


过去完成时:
The court had deemed the contract void. = 法院已判定该合同无效。
She had deemed it unwise to invest in that company. = 她曾认为投资那家公司不明智。


将来时:
The board will deem the matter at their next meeting. = 董事会将在下次会议上对此事做出裁决。
He will likely deem it inappropriate to comment further. = 他很可能会认为进一步评论是不合适的。


虚拟语气:
If the court deemed the evidence credible, the defendant would be convicted. = 如果法院认为证据可信，被告将被判有罪。
I wish they deemed my application worthy of consideration. = 我希望他们认为我的申请值得考虑。


被动语态:
The proposal was deemed to be flawed. = 该提案被认为有缺陷。
His actions were deemed unacceptable by many. = 他的行为被许多人认为是不可接受的。


疑问句:
Do you deem this a sufficient explanation? = 你认为这解释足够吗？
Does he deem himself qualified for the job? = 他认为自己有资格胜任这份工作吗？


否定句:
They did not deem it necessary to consult us. = 他们认为没有必要咨询我们。
I do not deem your suggestion feasible. = 我认为你的建议不可行。


PHRASE->
