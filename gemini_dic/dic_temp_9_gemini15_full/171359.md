publicly:/ˈpʌblɪkli/| adv. |公开地；公然地 |openly; in a way that can be seen or known by the public|The company publicly apologized for its mistake.  这家公司公开为其错误道歉。|The information was publicly available online.  这些信息可以在网上公开获取。|privately, secretly |secretly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They publicly support the new policy. = 他们公开支持这项新政策。
We publicly announce our engagement. = 我们公开宣布我们的订婚。


一般过去时:
She publicly denounced the government's actions. = 她公开谴责政府的行动。
He publicly revealed his identity. = 他公开透露了他的身份。


现在完成时:
They have publicly expressed their concerns. = 他们已经公开表达了他们的担忧。
The company has publicly committed to sustainability. = 这家公司已公开承诺可持续发展。


过去完成时:
They had publicly stated their opposition before the vote. = 他们在投票前就公开声明了他们的反对立场。
The artist had publicly displayed his work several times. =这位艺术家曾多次公开展示他的作品。


将来时:
They will publicly release the report next week. = 他们将在下周公开发布这份报告。
She will publicly address the issue tomorrow. = 她将在明天公开讨论这个问题。


虚拟语气:
If they publicly apologized, it might help. = 如果他们公开道歉，可能会有所帮助。
I wish they had publicly acknowledged their mistake. = 我希望他们能公开承认他们的错误。


被动语态:
The decision was publicly criticized. = 该决定受到了公开批评。
The scandal was publicly exposed. = 这场丑闻被公开了。


疑问句:
Was the information publicly disclosed? = 信息是否被公开披露？
Will the results be publicly announced? = 结果会公开宣布吗？


否定句:
The company did not publicly comment on the allegations. = 该公司没有公开评论这些指控。
He did not publicly endorse the candidate. = 他没有公开支持这位候选人。
