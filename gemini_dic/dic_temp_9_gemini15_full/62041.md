encamped:/ɪnˈkæmpt/| v. |扎营；宿营 | To have set up camp; to be situated in a camp.  |例句：The soldiers encamped on the hillside.  士兵们在山坡上扎营。

例句：The travelers encamped by the river for the night. 旅行者们在河边宿营过夜。

近义词：camped, pitched camp | 反义词：departed, moved on | 词性:动词


USAGE->

They encamp near the river every summer. = 他们每年夏天都在河边扎营。
The nomads encamp wherever they find water. = 游牧民族在哪里找到水就在哪里扎营。

一般过去时:
We encamped under the stars last night. = 我们昨晚在星空下扎营。

词源:
The word "encamped" comes from the Old French "encamper," which combines the prefix "en-" (in) and "camper" (to lodge, dwell).  The ultimate origin lies in the Latin "campus," meaning "field," reflecting the historical practice of setting up temporary settlements in open fields.
