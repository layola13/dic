distinguished:/dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt/| adj.|杰出的，卓越的，著名的|  Distinguished means to be successful, respected, and admired for one's achievements or qualities. It implies a high level of accomplishment and recognition.  |The distinguished professor gave a fascinating lecture.  这位杰出的教授做了一个精彩的演讲。


He is a distinguished scientist in his field. 他是该领域一位杰出的科学家。|eminent, prominent, renowned, celebrated|ordinary, undistinguished, unknown|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is distinguished for his contributions to science. 他因对科学的贡献而闻名。
The company is distinguished by its innovative products. 这家公司以其创新产品而著称。


一般过去时:
The general was distinguished by his bravery.  这位将军以其勇敢而闻名。
The artist was distinguished for his unique style.这位艺术家以其独特的风格而闻名。


现在完成时:
He has distinguished himself through hard work and dedication. 他通过努力工作和奉献精神而成就非凡。
She has distinguished herself in the field of medicine. 她已在医学领域取得了显著成就。


过去完成时:
By the time he retired, he had distinguished himself as a leading expert.在他退休时，他已经成为一位领先的专家。
Before the award ceremony, she had already distinguished herself as a talented writer.在颁奖典礼之前，她已经以才华横溢的作家而闻名。


将来时:
His future work will undoubtedly distinguish him as a pioneer in his field. 他的未来工作无疑会使他成为该领域的先驱。
This new invention will distinguish the company from its competitors. 这项新发明将使这家公司区别于其竞争对手。


虚拟语气:
If he had distinguished himself earlier, he would have received more recognition.如果他早些出名，他会得到更多的认可。
I wish I had distinguished myself in my studies. 我希望我在学习中能有更好的表现。


被动语态:
He was distinguished for his exceptional talent. 他因其杰出的才华而闻名。
The project was distinguished by its innovative approach. 该项目以其创新的方法而著称。


疑问句:
What distinguishes this product from its competitors?  是什么使这款产品区别于竞争对手？
What distinguished the winning team from the others?  是什么使获胜队与其他队伍不同？


否定句:
His work was not distinguished by its originality.  他的作品并不以其独创性而闻名。
The design was undistinguished and lacked character.  该设计平庸且缺乏特色。
