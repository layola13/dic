eaglestone: /ˈiːɡəlstoʊn/ | n. |鹰石，老鹰石| A concretion, usually a type of septarian nodule, found in various parts of the world.  These stones are often characterized by their unique shape and coloration, sometimes resembling an eagle's head or claw.  They are often collected as curiosities or used in folk medicine and jewelry-making.  The name derives from a belief that eagles used these stones to sharpen their talons. | 例句：The farmer found an interesting eaglestone while plowing his field.  这位农民在耕地时发现了一块有趣的鹰石。


The legend surrounding eaglestones claims eagles used them to sharpen their talons.  围绕鹰石的传说声称老鹰用它们来磨砺爪子。| 近义词：nodule, concretion | 反义词：无
