non-segmentation: /ˌnɒnˌseɡmenˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 非分割，不分段 | The absence of segmentation; a continuous, unbroken state or process.  It often refers to the continuous nature of something, as opposed to being divided into distinct parts or segments. For example, in linguistics, it might describe speech that lacks clear boundaries between words or phrases. In image processing, it might refer to an image without distinct regions or objects. | Example: The non-segmentation of the data made analysis more challenging.  = 数据的不分段使得分析更具挑战性。 |近义词：continuity, unbrokenness |反义词：segmentation, division, partitioning |
