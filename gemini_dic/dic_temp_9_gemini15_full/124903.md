muddling:/ˈmʌdliŋ/| v.| 混乱地做事；笨手笨脚地处理；使混乱；含糊不清地说 | To do something in a confused or ineffective way; to make something confused or unclear. | 例句：He muddled through the exam, barely passing. 他勉强通过了考试。 | 例句：Don't muddle the instructions; they're quite clear. 不要把说明弄乱；它们很清楚。| 近义词：messing up, bungling, botching | 反义词：organizing, clarifying, streamlining


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He muddles through his work every day. = 他每天都笨手笨脚地完成工作。
They are muddling the important documents. = 他们在把重要的文件弄乱。

一般过去时:
She muddled the recipe and the cake was a disaster. = 她把食谱弄乱了，蛋糕做得很糟糕。
He muddled his way through the maze. = 他笨手笨脚地走出了迷宫。

现在完成时:
I have muddled through this project somehow. = 我不知怎么地完成了这个项目。
They have muddled the financial records. = 他们把财务记录搞乱了。

过去完成时:
She had muddled the instructions before she started. = 她开始之前就把说明弄乱了。
They had muddled their way to success despite many challenges. = 尽管面临许多挑战，他们还是笨手笨脚地取得了成功。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish I hadn't muddled my words during the speech. = 我真希望我在演讲中不要说得含糊不清。

被动语态:
The instructions were muddled and difficult to follow. = 说明很混乱，很难理解。
The situation was further muddled by his actions. = 他的行为使情况更加混乱。

疑问句:
Are you muddling the facts? = 你在混淆事实吗？
Did he muddle the answer on purpose? = 他是故意把答案弄混的吗？

否定句:
He didn't muddle the instructions; he followed them precisely. = 他没有把说明弄乱；他严格按照说明做了。
They didn't muddle the situation; they handled it effectively. = 他们没有把情况弄糟；他们有效地处理了它。


PHRASE->

muddling about = 瞎忙活
muddling over = 仔细考虑(通常指考虑时间长但效果不佳)
