propping:/ˈprɑːpɪŋ/| v.|支撑；支持；维持；|to support or hold something up from falling or collapsing; to keep something going or functioning|例句：He propped the ladder against the wall. 他把梯子靠在墙上。

He propped himself up on his elbows. 他用胳膊肘支撑着身体。


近义词：supporting, sustaining, bolstering|反义词：toppling, collapsing, destroying|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are propping up the falling roof. = 他们正在支撑快要倒塌的屋顶。
We prop up the economy with government spending. = 我们用政府支出支撑经济。


一般过去时:
She propped the painting against the sofa. = 她把画靠在沙发上。
The government propped up the failing bank. = 政府支撑着即将倒闭的银行。


现在完成时:
They have propped up the tent with stakes. = 他们用桩子支撑起了帐篷。
We have propped up the business for years. = 我们多年来一直支撑着这家企业。


过去完成时:
They had propped up the old house with beams before the storm. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们用横梁支撑起了老房子。
The company had propped itself up with loans for months. = 公司几个月来一直靠贷款支撑。


将来时:
We will prop up the shelves with more books. = 我们会用更多的书来支撑书架。
They will prop up the sagging fence tomorrow. = 他们明天会支撑起下垂的栅栏。


虚拟语气:
If we propped the table properly, it wouldn't wobble. = 如果我们把桌子支好，它就不会晃动了。
If they had propped the roof better, it wouldn't have collapsed. = 如果他们把屋顶支撑得更好，它就不会倒塌了。


被动语态:

The failing company was propped up by investors. = 即将倒闭的公司得到了投资者的支持。


疑问句:

否定句:
They didn't prop up the plant properly, so it wilted. = 他们没有好好支撑植物，所以它枯萎了。
He didn't prop the door open, so it slammed shut. = 他没有把门撑开，所以门砰地关上了。
