co-opt:/ˌkoʊˈɑːpt/| vt.| 吸收；利用；篡夺；|To take or adopt (an idea, method, etc.) from another source. To adopt (a person) into a group, especially by election.  |例句：The company co-opted many ideas from its competitors. 这家公司借鉴了许多竞争对手的理念。

The rebels co-opted several prominent members of the community to bolster their cause.  叛乱者吸收了社区中几位知名人士来支持他们的事业。

近义词：adopt, incorporate, usurp, commandeer |反义词：reject, abandon, relinquish
|动词|

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They co-opt new members every year. = 他们每年都吸收新成员。
The company co-opts innovative ideas from startups. = 公司吸收初创公司的创新理念。


一般过去时:
The government co-opted the opposition leader. = 政府收买了反对派领袖。
The committee co-opted a new chairman. = 委员会选了一个新的主席。


现在完成时:
The movement has co-opted many mainstream ideas. = 这个运动已经吸收了许多主流思想。
They have co-opted several experts to advise them. = 他们已经吸收了几位专家来为他们提供建议。


过去完成时:
The party had co-opted several prominent figures before the election. = 该党在选举前已经吸收了几位知名人士。
She had co-opted her brother's ideas for her project. = 她已经借用了她哥哥的创意来做她的项目。


将来时:

The company will co-opt the technology into its new product. = 公司将把这项技术整合到它的新产品中。


虚拟语气:
If they co-opted more young people, the organization would be more dynamic. = 如果他们吸收更多年轻人，这个组织就会更有活力。

被动语态:

否定句:
