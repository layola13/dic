metempirical: /ˌmɛtɛmˈpɪrɪkəl/ | adj. | 超经验的；经验之外的 |

英文解析：Relating to or denoting knowledge or a method of inquiry that transcends empirical observation or experience; going beyond the limits of experience.  It suggests a method of gaining knowledge that is not solely reliant on sensory experience but incorporates other factors, such as reason, intuition, or revelation.

例句：

The philosopher proposed a metempirical argument for the existence of God, based on logical necessity rather than empirical evidence.  哲学家提出了一个超经验的论证来证明上帝的存在，其基础是逻辑上的必然性而不是经验性的证据。


Some spiritual practices claim to provide access to metempirical realities beyond the grasp of ordinary senses.  一些灵修实践声称可以让人接触到超越普通感官所能感知的超经验现实。


近义词：transcendental, metaphysical, a priori

反义词：empirical, experiential, a posteriori


词性：形容词
