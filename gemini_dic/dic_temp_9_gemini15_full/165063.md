preimagine:/ˌpriːɪˈmædʒɪn/| vt.|预想，预见，事先想象|To form a mental image of something before it actually happens; to anticipate mentally.|例句：I preimagined the difficulties of the project before I started. 我在开始项目之前就预想到了项目的困难。

例句：She preimagined a future where she would be successful and happy. 她预想了一个她会成功快乐的未来。


近义词：anticipate, foresee, envision, envisage|反义词：misjudge, overlook|词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I preimagine the outcome of the meeting.  = 我预想会议的结果。
They preimagine the challenges ahead. = 他们预想未来的挑战。


一般过去时:
I preimagined a successful career. = 我曾经预想一个成功的职业生涯。
She preimagined a peaceful retirement. = 她曾经预想一个平静的退休生活。


现在完成时:
I have preimagined many scenarios. = 我已经预想过许多情景。
We have preimagined the worst-case scenario. = 我们已经预想过最坏的情况。


过去完成时:
I had preimagined the questions he would ask. = 我已经预想过他会问的问题。
She had preimagined the trip in great detail. = 她已经详细地预想过这次旅行。


将来时:
I will preimagine the possibilities. = 我将预想各种可能性。
They will preimagine the consequences of their actions. = 他们将预想他们行为的后果。


虚拟语气:
If I had preimagined the consequences, I would have acted differently. = 如果我预想到了后果，我就会采取不同的行动。
I wish I had preimagined the difficulties. = 我希望我预想到了困难。


被动语态:
The potential risks were preimagined by the team. = 团队预想到了潜在的风险。
The future was preimagined in vivid detail. = 未来被生动地预想出来。


疑问句:
Did you preimagine the outcome? = 你预想到了结果吗？
Have they preimagined the potential problems? = 他们预想到了潜在的问题吗？


否定句:
I didn't preimagine the level of difficulty. = 我没有预想到困难的程度。
They hadn't preimagined the unexpected twist. = 他们没有预想到意外的转折。


PHRASE->
