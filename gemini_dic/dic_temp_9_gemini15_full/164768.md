preferentialists: /ˌprɛfəˈrɛnʃəlɪsts/ | n. | 偏好主义者，优惠待遇支持者 | Supporters of preferential treatment or policies, often based on factors like race, gender, or ethnicity.  These individuals believe that certain groups deserve advantages to compensate for past or present disadvantages. | The preferentialists argued for affirmative action policies to address historical injustices. 偏好主义者主张采取平权行动政策来解决历史上的不公正现象。 | proponents of affirmative action, advocates of preferential policies | opponents of meritocracy, critics of equal opportunity. | 名词
