proxyship:/ˈprɒksɪʃɪp/ | n. | 代理权；代理；代表身份 | The state or fact of acting as a proxy or substitute for someone else; the authority or power to act on behalf of another.  It often implies a temporary or specific delegation of authority, rather than a permanent position. | Example:  The company's CEO granted me proxyship to negotiate the contract. 公司首席执行官授权我谈判合同。|近义词：representation, agency, delegation |反义词：self-representation, direct action
