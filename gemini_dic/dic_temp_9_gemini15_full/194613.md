self-taxation:/ˌsɛlfˈtækeɪʃən/| n.|自我征税；自行纳税|The act of a group of people imposing taxes on themselves, typically for a specific purpose, without the intervention of a larger governing body.  This often occurs in situations where there is no central authority or where people want more control over how their funds are used.|例句：The villagers practiced self-taxation to fund the construction of a new school. 村民们实行自我征税来资助新建学校。

例句：The community decided on self-taxation to improve local infrastructure. 社区决定通过自我征税来改善当地基础设施。


近义词： voluntary taxation, community taxation |反义词： compulsory taxation, forced taxation


词性:名词
