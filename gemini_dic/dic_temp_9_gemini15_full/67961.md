facilitates:/fəˈsɪlɪteɪts/ | vt. | 促进；使便利；帮助 | To make (an action or process) easy or easier.  |例句：The new software facilitates communication between departments. 新软件促进了部门之间的沟通。

例句：The government's policies facilitate economic growth.  政府的政策促进了经济增长。


近义词：assist, aid, help, ease, expedite, promote


反义词：hinder, impede, obstruct, hamper


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher facilitates learning through interactive activities. = 老师通过互动活动来促进学习。
This tool facilitates data analysis. = 这个工具有助于数据分析。


一般过去时:
The meeting facilitated a productive discussion. = 会议促成了一次富有成效的讨论。
The new bridge facilitated transportation across the river. = 新桥便利了跨河交通。


现在完成时:
The changes have facilitated better communication between teams. = 这些变化促进了团队之间更好的沟通。
This program has facilitated access to education for many students. = 这个项目为许多学生提供了接受教育的机会。


过去完成时:
The prior investment had facilitated the project's rapid completion. = 先前的投资促使了项目的快速完成。
The earlier agreement had facilitated a smooth negotiation. = 早期达成的协议促成了顺利的谈判。


将来时:
The new technology will facilitate faster data processing. = 新技术将促进更快的数据处理。
This initiative will facilitate the integration of the two companies. = 这项倡议将促进两家公司的整合。


虚拟语气:
If we had better resources, it would facilitate the research. = 如果我们有更好的资源，它将有助于研究。
If the government facilitated trade, the economy would grow faster. = 如果政府促进贸易，经济增长将会更快。


被动语态:
The process was facilitated by the use of new technology. = 这个过程得益于新技术的运用。
Progress is being facilitated by a team of experts. = 一组专家正在促进进展。


疑问句:
How can we facilitate the process? = 我们如何才能促进这个过程？
What measures will facilitate the solution to the problem? = 什么措施将有助于解决这个问题？


否定句:
The lack of funding did not facilitate the project's success. = 资金不足并没有促进项目的成功。
The bureaucratic process did not facilitate efficient operations. = 官僚程序并没有促进高效运作。


PHRASE->
facilitate communication:促进沟通
facilitate learning:促进学习
facilitate growth:促进增长
facilitate understanding:促进理解
facilitate progress:促进进步


词源:
From Latin *facilitāre, from facilis ("easy").  The word ultimately derives from the Latin verb *facere, meaning "to do" or "to make".  The suffix "-ate" is a verb-forming suffix.
