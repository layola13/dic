pygopodes:/ˈpɪɡəˌpoʊdz/| n.| 趾足，后肢|  Pygopodes are birds with short legs situated far back on the body, adapted for swimming and diving.  They are characterized by their webbed feet and streamlined bodies.  This adaptation is especially useful for their lifestyle in aquatic environments.|Example: Penguins are a well-known example of pygopodes.|近义词：web-footed birds|反义词：None specifically, but birds with legs positioned more centrally could be considered the opposite.|名词
