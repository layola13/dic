petiolated:/ˈpɛtɪəleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 有叶柄的；具柄的 | Having a petiole; stalked.  A petiole is the stalk that joins a leaf blade to the stem.  The term describes leaves, flowers, or other plant structures attached to a stem by a stalk. | Example: The petiolated leaves of the maple tree are easily recognizable.  枫树的叶柄叶很容易辨认。The beautifully petiolated flowers swayed gently in the breeze.  美丽的有柄花朵在微风中轻轻摇曳。|近义词：stalked, pedunculate | 反义词：sessile
