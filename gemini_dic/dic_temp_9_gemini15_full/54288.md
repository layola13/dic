disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/| n., v.|厌恶；令人厌恶的事物；使厌恶|a feeling of strong dislike or disapproval; to cause someone to feel strong dislike or disapproval.|The sight of the rotting food filled me with disgust.  看到腐烂的食物让我感到恶心。|The smell of the garbage disgusted me. 垃圾的气味让我恶心。|revulsion, aversion, loathing|delight, pleasure|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The smell disgusts me. = 这气味让我恶心。
He disgusts me with his behavior. = 他的行为让我恶心。


一般过去时:
The movie disgusted me. = 这部电影让我恶心。
The food disgusted her. = 这食物让她恶心。


现在完成时:
That incident has disgusted me for years. = 那件事多年来一直让我反感。
His actions have disgusted many people. = 他的行为让很多人感到厌恶。


过去完成时:
I had been disgusted by his behaviour before that incident. = 在那件事之前，我就已经对他行为感到厌恶了。
She had already been disgusted by his lies. = 她早就对他的谎言感到厌恶了。


将来时:
That will disgust many people. = 那将会让很多人感到恶心。
The news will disgust the public. = 这个消息将会令公众感到厌恶。


虚拟语气:
I would be disgusted if I saw that. = 如果我看到那样的事情，我会感到恶心。
If I were you, I would be disgusted by his actions. = 如果我是你，我会对他行为感到厌恶。


被动语态:
Many people were disgusted by his actions. = 许多人对他行为感到厌恶。
She was disgusted by the news. = 她对这个消息感到厌恶。


疑问句:
Does this smell disgust you? = 这个气味让你恶心吗？
Does his behavior disgust you? = 他的行为让你恶心吗？


否定句:
That doesn't disgust me. = 那并没有让我恶心。
I wasn't disgusted by the movie. = 我对这部电影并不感到恶心。
