self-ridicule: /ˌsɛlfˈrɪdɪkjuːl/ | n. | 自嘲；自我解嘲 |  The act of making fun of oneself, often in a humorous or self-deprecating way. It can be a coping mechanism, a way to connect with others, or a form of humor. |例句：Her self-ridicule was a way to disarm her critics and make them laugh.  她的自嘲是一种化解批评、逗乐别人的方式。


例句：His stand-up routine was filled with self-ridicule, poking fun at his own shortcomings. 他的单口喜剧节目充满了自嘲，拿自己的缺点开玩笑。


近义词：self-deprecation, irony, sarcasm (when directed at oneself) | 反义词：self-aggrandizement, self-importance


词源:  The word is a compound of "self-" (oneself) and "ridicule" (to make fun of). "Ridicule" ultimately derives from the Latin word "ridiculus," meaning "laughable" or "absurd."
