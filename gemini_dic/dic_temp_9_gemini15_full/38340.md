conceived:/kənˈsiːvd/| vt.|构思，设想；怀孕 | To form an idea, plan, or design in the mind; to become pregnant. |例句：She conceived the idea for a new kind of computer. 她构思了一种新型电脑的想法。
She conceived a child. 她怀孕了。|近义词：imagine, devise, invent, create |反义词：misconceive, abandon


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He conceives innovative solutions. 他构思创新的解决方案。
They conceive of the project as a long-term investment. 他们将该项目视为一项长期投资。


一般过去时:
The artist conceived a masterpiece. 这位艺术家构思了一部杰作。
She conceived her first child at the age of 25.  她在25岁时怀上了她的第一个孩子。


现在完成时:
They have conceived a plan to revitalize the city center. 他们已经构思了一个振兴市中心的计划。
She has conceived three children. 她已经生了三个孩子。


过去完成时:
He had conceived the idea before the meeting. 他在会议之前就有了这个主意。
They had conceived the plan for years before it was implemented.  他们在实施这个计划之前已经构思了多年。


将来时:
They will conceive a new marketing strategy next month. 他们下个月将构思一个新的营销策略。
She hopes to conceive a child soon. 她希望很快能怀孕。


虚拟语气:
If I had conceived a better plan, things would have gone differently. 如果我构思了一个更好的计划，事情就会不一样了。
I wish I had conceived the idea earlier. 我真希望我早点想到这个主意。


被动语态:
The plan was conceived by a team of experts. 这个计划是由一个专家团队构思的。
The project was conceived in 1998.  这个项目始于1998年。


疑问句:
How did he conceive such a brilliant idea? 他是怎么想到这么好的主意？
When did she conceive? 她是什么时候怀孕的？


否定句:
They did not conceive of the difficulties involved.  他们没有想到其中涉及的困难。
She hadn't conceived yet. 她还没有怀孕。



PHRASE->
be conceived in = 以...为基础构思
conceived of = 构思，设想
conceived as = 被认为是
highly conceived = 构思精妙的
newly conceived = 新近构思的
