clear-headedness: /ˌklɪrˈhedɪdnəs/ | n. |头脑清晰；清醒；明智 | The ability to think clearly and rationally, especially in a difficult or stressful situation.  It implies a state of mental alertness, sound judgment, and freedom from confusion or emotional distress. |  例句：Her clear-headedness in the crisis saved the day.  她的头脑清醒挽救了局面。

例句：His clear-headedness allowed him to make a sound decision under pressure. 他的头脑清晰使他能够在压力下做出正确的决定。


近义词：sanity, lucidity, rationality, composure, presence of mind, levelheadedness

反义词：confusion, bewilderment, disorientation, irrationality, muddleheadedness


词源:  The word is a compound of "clear-headed," meaning having a clear and rational mind, and the suffix "-ness," which forms nouns denoting a state, quality, or condition. "Clear" derives from Old English *clǣre, meaning "bright, shining," and "headed" refers to the head, the seat of intellect.
