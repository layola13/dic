salifiable: /ˈsælɪfaɪəbl/ | adj. | 可成盐的，可盐化的 | Capable of forming a salt.  This term refers to a substance that can react with an acid or a base to form a salt.  It often describes organic compounds containing acidic or basic functional groups.  | The carboxylic acid is salifiable, readily reacting with sodium hydroxide to produce the corresponding sodium salt.  可盐化的羧酸很容易与氢氧化钠反应生成相应的钠盐。 |  salinable,  acidifiable |  insalifiable


USAGE->
This word is primarily used in a chemical context and doesn't lend itself to typical grammatical tense variations like "hello".  The following examples demonstrate its use in different sentence structures:


Affirmative Sentence:
Many organic acids are salifiable. = 许多有机酸是可盐化的。
This compound is readily salifiable. = 此化合物易于盐化。


Negative Sentence:
This compound is not salifiable under normal conditions. = 在正常条件下，此化合物不可盐化。
The substance proved to be nonsalifiable. = 该物质证明是不可盐化的。


Question Sentence:
Is this molecule salifiable? = 这个分子是可盐化的吗？
Are all amines salifiable? = 所有胺类都是可盐化的吗？


Passive Voice:
The compound was found to be easily salifiable. = 发现该化合物易于盐化。
The salt was produced by salifying the acid. = 通过使酸盐化生产出盐。


PHRASE->
There aren't common idiomatic phrases using "salifiable".  Its usage is highly technical and within the realm of chemistry.
