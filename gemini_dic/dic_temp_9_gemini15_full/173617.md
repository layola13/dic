quasi-innumerable: /ˈkwɑːziɪˈnjuːmərəbl/ | adj. | 几乎无数的，难以计数的 |  Nearly innumerable; so numerous as to be almost impossible to count. This word combines "quasi" (meaning "seemingly" or "apparently") with "innumerable" (meaning "too many to be counted"). It suggests a vast quantity that while not truly infinite, is practically so. | The stars in the night sky seem quasi-innumerable.  = 夜空中星星的数量似乎难以计数。 The grains of sand on the beach are quasi-innumerable. = 海滩上的沙粒几乎数不清。| innumerable, countless, myriad | finite, countable, few |  形容词
