cazic:/ˈkæzɪk/| n. |卡西克（古巴印第安人首领）|A cazic was a chief or leader among certain indigenous groups in the Caribbean, particularly those in Cuba and Hispaniola (modern-day Haiti and the Dominican Republic).  They held significant political and social power within their communities.  The term is derived from the Taíno language.| Example: The Spanish conquistadors encountered many cazics during their exploration of the Caribbean islands.  西班牙征服者在探索加勒比海岛屿期间遇到了许多卡西克。|近义词: chief, chieftain, leader |反义词: follower, subject
