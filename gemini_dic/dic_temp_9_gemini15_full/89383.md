homophony:/ˈhoʊmɒfəni/| /ˈhoʊməˌfoʊni/ | n. |同音异义现象|The property of having the same sound but different meanings.  Homophony refers to words that are pronounced identically but have different meanings and often different spellings. For example, "to," "too," and "two" are homophones.|The words "there," "their," and "they're" are a classic example of homophony, causing confusion for many English learners. |近义词：sound alike|反义词：homonymy (in the sense of different sounds, same spelling)
