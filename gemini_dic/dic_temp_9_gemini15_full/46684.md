dampen:/ˈdæmpən/| vt.|使潮湿；抑制；减弱|to make something slightly wet; to make something less strong or effective|例句：The rain dampened the ground. 雨淋湿了地面。The news dampened our enthusiasm. 这个消息使我们热情减退。|近义词：moisten, dampen, depress, subdue|反义词: dry, invigorate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rain dampens the spirits of the hikers. = 雨水打压了徒步旅行者的兴致。
He dampens the fire with water. = 他用水浇灭了火。


一般过去时:
The cold wind dampened my enthusiasm. = 冷风使我热情减退。
She dampened the cloth before applying it to the wound. = 她把布弄湿后才敷在伤口上。


现在完成时:
The recent economic downturn has dampened consumer spending. = 最近的经济衰退抑制了消费者支出。
The bad weather has dampened our holiday plans. = 坏天气打乱了我们的假期计划。


过去完成时:

将来时:
The coming storm will dampen the spirits of the festival goers. = 即将来临的风暴将会打击节日参加者的兴致。
The heavy rain will dampen the ground significantly. = 大雨会使地面严重潮湿。


虚拟语气:
If it rained, it would dampen the picnic. = 如果下雨，野餐就会受影响。
I wish the bad news hadn't dampened our spirits so much. = 我真希望坏消息没有这么打击我们的士气。


被动语态:
Our enthusiasm was dampened by the unexpected delay. = 我们因为意外的延误而热情减退。
The fire was dampened by the firefighters quickly. = 消防队员迅速地将火扑灭。


疑问句:
Will the rain dampen the outdoor concert? = 雨会影响露天音乐会吗？
Does the humidity dampen the mood? = 潮湿的天气会影响心情吗？


否定句:
The good news didn't dampen our spirits at all. = 好消息丝毫没有打击我们的士气。
The dry weather didn't dampen the ground. = 干燥的天气没有使地面变湿。
