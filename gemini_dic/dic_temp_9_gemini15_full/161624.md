poultices:/ˈpʊltɪsɪz/| n.|药膏；热敷剂|A poultice is a soft, moist mass of material, typically herbs or other substances, applied to the body to relieve pain or inflammation.  It's often warm and applied directly to the skin.|例句：The doctor recommended applying a warm poultice to the injured area. 医生建议在受伤部位敷上热敷剂。

例句：She made a poultice of herbs to soothe the burn. 她用草药做成药膏来缓解烧伤。|近义词：compress, cataplasm|反义词：None specifically.

词源:
The word "poultice" comes from the Old French word "poulte," meaning "pulp" or "soft mass."  This ultimately derives from the Latin word "puls," meaning "porridge" or "gruel," reflecting the consistency of many traditional poultices.
