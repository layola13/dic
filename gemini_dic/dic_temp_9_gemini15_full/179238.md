redisposition:/ˌriːdɪspəˈzɪʃən/| n. |重新安排；重新部署；改变倾向 |The act of arranging or placing something again; a new arrangement or placement; a change in attitude, opinion, or feeling.|例句：The company's redisposition of its resources led to increased efficiency. 公司重新配置资源提高了效率。

例句：The general ordered a redisposition of troops before the anticipated attack. 将军下令在预期攻击前重新部署部队。


近义词：rearrangement, reorganization, realignment, reorientation | 反义词：maintenance, stagnation


词性：名词


词源：re-(again) + disposition (arrangement, tendency)  源自于古法语 disposicion，最终源于拉丁语 disponere (to arrange, to dispose).
