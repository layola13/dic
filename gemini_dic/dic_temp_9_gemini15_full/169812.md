proscribe:/ˈproʊˌskraɪb/ | vt. | 禁止；谴责；摒弃 | To forbid something, especially by law; to denounce or condemn. | 例句：The sale of alcohol was proscribed in the town. = 该镇禁止销售酒精。

The government proscribed the use of that dangerous chemical. = 政府禁止使用那种危险的化学品。


近义词：forbid, ban, prohibit, condemn | 反义词：allow, permit, sanction


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The school proscribes the use of cell phones. = 学校禁止使用手机。
The doctor proscribes certain foods for his patient. = 医生禁止病人食用某些食物。

一般过去时:
The king proscribed the practice of witchcraft. = 国王禁止巫术的实践。
The committee proscribed the proposed changes. = 委员会否决了提议的修改。

现在完成时:
They have proscribed the use of that software. = 他们已经禁止使用该软件。
The organization has proscribed violence in all its forms. = 该组织已禁止一切形式的暴力。

过去完成时:
The church had proscribed the book for centuries. = 教会几个世纪以来一直禁止这本书。
Before the new law, they had proscribed public gatherings. = 在新法律颁布之前，他们禁止公众集会。

将来时:
The council will proscribe the construction of new buildings. = 理事会将禁止建造新建筑物。
The government will proscribe the import of certain goods. = 政府将禁止进口某些商品。

虚拟语气:
If they proscribed the activity, it would be a great loss. = 如果他们禁止这项活动，将是一个巨大的损失。
I wish they had not proscribed that beautiful old building. = 我真希望他们没有禁止那座美丽的旧建筑。

被动语态:
The practice was proscribed by the authorities. = 这种做法被当局禁止了。
The new regulations were proscribed by the government. = 新规章被政府禁止了。

疑问句:
Does the law proscribe the sale of fireworks? = 法律是否禁止销售烟花爆竹？
Will the judge proscribe the defendant's actions? = 法官会谴责被告的行为吗？


否定句:
The rule doesn't proscribe the use of computers. = 规则并没有禁止使用电脑。
The court did not proscribe the journalist's report. = 法院没有禁止记者的报道。


PHRASE->
proscribe against = 明令禁止
proscribe from = 禁止...
proscribe the use of = 禁止使用
proscribe something as = 将某事物视为…而加以禁止
proscribe something to = 将…禁止用于


词源:
From Middle English *proscriben, from Old French proscrire, from Latin prōscrībere, from prō- ("before, forth") + scrībere ("to write").  The original meaning referred to writing something up as outlawed, hence the modern meaning of forbidding.
