pre-recognition:/ˌpriːˌrekəɡˈnɪʃən/| n. |预识别，预先识别|The process of identifying something before full conscious recognition.  It often involves subconscious processing of sensory information.  For example, in facial recognition, pre-recognition might involve detecting a familiar face before the brain fully processes it as a specific person.|The system demonstrated impressive pre-recognition capabilities, alerting the user to potential threats even before they were fully visible.|近义词：prior identification, preliminary detection|反义词：post-recognition,  full recognition|名词
