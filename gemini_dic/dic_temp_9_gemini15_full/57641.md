dravidian:/dræˈvɪdiən/| adj. n.|达罗毗荼语系的；达罗毗荼人|Of or relating to the Dravidian languages or peoples.  The Dravidian languages are a family of languages spoken primarily in southern India and parts of eastern and central India, as well as in Sri Lanka, Pakistan, and Bangladesh.  The term also refers to the people who speak these languages.|例句：Tamil and Telugu are Dravidian languages.  泰米尔语和泰卢固语是达罗毗荼语系的语言。

The Dravidian culture is rich and diverse. 达罗毗荼文化丰富多样。|近义词：South Indian (in some contexts)|反义词：Indo-Aryan (in the context of language families)
