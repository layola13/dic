kabbalahs:/kəˈbɑːləz/| n. |卡巴拉（犹太神秘主义）|Kabbalahs are systems of Jewish mysticism that attempt to understand the nature of God and the universe through esoteric interpretations of the Hebrew Bible and Jewish tradition.  They often involve complex symbolic systems and mystical practices. |The study of Kabbalah has fascinated scholars and spiritual seekers for centuries.  The various Kabbalahs offer diverse perspectives on the divine.|近义词：Jewish mysticism, esotericism |反义词：None directly applicable.  Perhaps rationalism, literalism.
