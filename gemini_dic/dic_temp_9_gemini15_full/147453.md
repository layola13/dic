overoffended: /ˌoʊvərəˈfɛndɪd/ | adj. | 过度敏感的，易被冒犯的 | Excessively sensitive and easily offended; taking offense where none was intended. | He's so overoffended; he takes everything personally. 他太敏感了，凡事都往自己身上套。 | 近义词: hypersensitive, touchy, thin-skinned | 反义词: thick-skinned, insensitive, tolerant |


词源:  The word is a compound of "over-" (meaning excessively or to an excessive degree) and "offended" (past participle of "offend," meaning to cause someone to feel hurt, annoyed, or resentful).  Therefore, it literally means "excessively offended."


用法说明:


This word is primarily used as an adjective to describe someone who is overly sensitive and prone to taking offense, often disproportionately to the situation. It implies that the person's reaction is exaggerated or unreasonable.  It is often used informally.


例句:


He's so overoffended by the slightest criticism. 他对最轻微的批评都过度敏感。

She gets overoffended if you don't agree with her every opinion. 如果你不同意她的每一个观点，她就会过度敏感。


PHRASE->
There are no common set phrases using "overoffended". The word itself functions as a stand-alone descriptor.
