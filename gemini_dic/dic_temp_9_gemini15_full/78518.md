ghettoing:/ˈɡetəʊɪŋ/ | v. | 把…隔离；把…变成贫民窟；排斥 | To restrict or confine a group of people to a particular area or situation, often one characterized by poverty or social disadvantage; to treat someone or something as if they belong to a lower social class or are unworthy of consideration.  | Example: The new housing development was criticized for ghettoing low-income families in a single area. 新建的住宅区因将低收入家庭隔离在一个区域而受到批评。 |近义词：segregating, isolating, marginalizing, ostracizing | 反义词：integrating, including, welcoming


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are ghettoing the affordable housing projects. = 他们正在将经济适用房项目隔离起来。
The company is constantly ghettoing its employees based on race. = 这家公司不断地基于种族隔离员工。


一般过去时:
The city government ghettoed the minority community. = 市政府将少数民族社区隔离起来。
The school ghettoed the students with disabilities. = 学校将残疾学生隔离起来。


现在完成时:
They have ghettoed the poor into a small area of the city. = 他们已将穷人隔离在城市的一个小区域内。
The policy has effectively ghettoed a whole generation. = 这一政策实际上已经将整整一代人隔离起来。


过去完成时:
The developers had already ghettoed the low-income families before the protest started. = 在抗议开始之前，开发商已经将低收入家庭隔离起来。
The community had been ghettoed for decades before any action was taken. = 在采取任何行动之前，这个社区已经被隔离了几十年。


将来时:
They will ghetto the immigrants in the outskirts of the city. = 他们将在城市的郊区隔离移民。
The new zoning laws will likely ghetto the less wealthy. = 新的区域划分法可能会将较不富裕的人隔离起来。


虚拟语气:
If they continue this policy, they would be ghettoing an entire community. = 如果他们继续这项政策，他们将隔离整个社区。
I wish they hadn’t ghettoed the artists in that part of town. = 我真希望他们没有把艺术家隔离在城镇的那一部分。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
