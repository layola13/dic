gimmicks:/ˈɡɪmɪks/| n.|噱头，花招，诡计 |A gimmick is a trick or device intended to attract attention, especially one that is considered to be gimmicky or not very effective.  It often refers to something used in advertising or sales to attract customers. |例句：The advertisement used flashy graphics and catchy slogans as gimmicks to sell the product. 这则广告使用了炫目的图形和朗朗上口的口号作为噱头来销售产品。

例句：They employed various gimmicks to boost sales, but none proved particularly successful. 他们采用了各种花招来提高销量，但都没有取得特别的成功。


近义词：trick, stunt, ploy, gadget, device |反义词：genuineness, sincerity, honesty


词源:  The word "gimmick" likely originated in the early 20th century, possibly from a dialectal variation of "gimcrack," meaning a showy but worthless object.  The exact etymology is uncertain, but its connection to "gimcrack" suggests a history of describing things that are flashy but ultimately superficial.
