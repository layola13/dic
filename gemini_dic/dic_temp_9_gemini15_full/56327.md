dogmatician: /ˌdɒɡˈmætɪʃən/ | n. | 教条主义者；顽固坚持己见者 | A person who is stubbornly attached to their own doctrines or beliefs, often to the exclusion of others' viewpoints.  They tend to be inflexible and unwilling to consider alternative perspectives.  The term often carries a negative connotation, suggesting close-mindedness and an unwillingness to engage in reasoned discourse. |  He's a real dogmatician when it comes to politics, never willing to consider any other viewpoint. 他在政治上是个真正的教条主义者，从不愿考虑任何其他观点。  | 近义词：dogmatist, doctrinaire, bigot | 反义词：open-minded, tolerant, flexible | 名词
