municipalities:/ˌmjuːnɪsɪˈpælɪtiz/ | n. | 市政当局；自治市；市辖区 |  A municipality is a city or town that has its own local government.  It refers to the administrative body governing a specific area, often a city or town, but can also encompass larger regions depending on the legal framework.  The term often implies a degree of self-governance and autonomy within a larger political entity (like a state or country). |


例句：Many municipalities are struggling to balance their budgets. 许多市政当局都在努力平衡预算。

例句：The municipalities in this region are collaborating on a new waste management program. 这个地区的市政当局正在合作制定一项新的废物管理计划。


近义词：cities, towns, boroughs, counties (depending on context)


反义词：None (antonyms would depend on the contrasting concept, e.g.,  "rural areas" if contrasting with urban municipalities)


词性：名词


词源：源自拉丁语"municipium,"  meaning "a town enjoying the rights of Roman citizenship," ultimately derived from "munus" (duty, service) and "civis" (citizen).  The meaning evolved over time to encompass various forms of local government.
