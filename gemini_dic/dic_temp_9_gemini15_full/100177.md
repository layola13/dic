interregnums: /ˈɪntərˌreɡnəmz/ | n. | 权力真空期；过渡时期 | A period of time between two reigns or regimes during which there is no ruler.  It often refers to a period of instability or uncertainty. | Example: The death of the king plunged the country into a period of interregnum, marked by political maneuvering and social unrest.  The company experienced an interregnum after the CEO's sudden resignation. | 近义词：gap, interval, hiatus, interim | 反义词：continuity, reign, rule
