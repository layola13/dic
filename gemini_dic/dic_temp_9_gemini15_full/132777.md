nondualism:/ˈnɒnˈdʒuːəlɪzəm/|/ˈnɑːnˈduːəlɪzəm/ | n. |非二元论；不二法门|The philosophical concept that reality is not ultimately composed of two separate, independent entities or principles, such as mind and matter, subject and object, or good and evil.  It emphasizes the interconnectedness and interdependence of all things.|Example:  Many Eastern philosophies, such as Buddhism and Advaita Vedanta, embrace nondualism.  许多东方哲学，例如佛教和阿德瓦达吠檀多，都接受非二元论。|近义词：unity, oneness, monism|反义词：dualism, dichotomy, pluralism
