cutup:/kʌtˈʌp/| n. v.| 切碎；捣碎；古怪的人；（非正式）胡闹；|英文解析：As a noun, "cutup" refers to someone who is playful, mischievous, and often a bit disruptive. As a verb, it means to cut something into small pieces. |例句：He's such a cutup; he's always playing pranks. 他真是个捣蛋鬼，总是恶作剧。  The chef expertly cut up the vegetables for the stew. 厨师熟练地切碎了炖菜的蔬菜。|近义词：(n.) prankster, clown, goofball; (v.) chop, mince, dice |反义词：(n.) serious person, bore; (v.)  None specifically.
