catholicos:/ˌkæθˈɒlɪkɒs/ | n. |天主教领袖，大主教 | A catholicos is the title of the head of certain autocephalous Eastern Orthodox churches, particularly within the Oriental Orthodox and Eastern Catholic traditions.  The term is often used interchangeably with "patriarch," but it carries a distinct historical and theological significance. It reflects an ancient lineage and authority within specific church jurisdictions. | The Catholicos of All Armenians is a highly respected figure within the Armenian Apostolic Church. | Patriarch, primate |  (No direct antonym, as it's a specific title) | noun
