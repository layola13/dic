impounds:/ɪmˈpaʊndz/ | vt. |扣押；没收；（水）积聚 | To seize and hold (something, especially property) under the authority of a law.  This typically involves taking control of something because of a legal issue, debt, or violation.  |例句：The police impounded his car after he was caught drunk driving. =  警察在他酒后驾车被捕后扣押了他的车。

The authorities impounded the illegal drugs. = 当局没收了非法毒品。


近义词：confiscate, seize, impound

反义词：release, return


词源:  From Old French *empoindre, from em- + poindre "to seize, to grasp, to sting".
