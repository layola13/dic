detester:/dɪˈtɛstər/| n. |厌恶者，憎恨者 |A detester is someone who intensely dislikes or hates something or someone.  |例句：He's a detester of all things modern. 他是一个厌恶所有现代事物的人。|例句：She's become a detester of loud music. 她已经变成一个厌恶吵闹音乐的人。|近义词：hater, enemy, opponent |反义词：admirer, lover, enthusiast |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The detester avoids the object of their hatred. = 厌恶者避免与他们憎恨的对象接触。
He is a detester of hypocrisy. = 他是一个厌恶虚伪的人。


一般过去时:
She was a detester of injustice. = 她是一个厌恶不公正的人。
The detester left the room angrily. = 厌恶者愤怒地离开了房间。


现在完成时:
He has been a detester of that politician for years. = 多年来，他一直厌恶那位政客。
She has become a detester of social media. = 她已经变成了一个厌恶社交媒体的人。


过去完成时:
He had been a detester of the regime long before the revolution. = 早在革命之前，他就一直厌恶这个政权。
They had been detesters of the war for many years. = 他们多年来一直厌恶这场战争。


将来时:
She will remain a detester of cruelty. = 她将继续厌恶残酷的行为。
He will become a detester of this new policy. = 他将成为这项新政策的厌恶者。


虚拟语气:
If he were a detester of violence, he wouldn't have done that. = 如果他厌恶暴力，他就不会那样做了。
I wish I weren't a detester of public speaking. = 我真希望我不那么讨厌公开演讲。


被动语态:
The new policy is detested by many. = 许多人厌恶这项新政策。(注意：这里是被动语态，主语是政策，而不是detester)
His methods were detested by his colleagues. = 他的方法遭到同事们的厌恶。(注意：这里是被动语态，主语是方法，而不是detester)


疑问句:
Is he a detester of cats? = 他是讨厌猫的人吗？
Are you a detester of reality TV? = 你讨厌真人秀吗？


否定句:
He is not a detester of dogs. = 他并不讨厌狗。
She wasn't a detester of art; she simply didn't understand it. = 她并不讨厌艺术；她只是不懂艺术。

词源:
The word "detester" is derived from the verb "detest," which comes from the Latin word "detestari," meaning "to be a witness against," "to bear witness against," or "to protest against."  The prefix "de-" intensifies the meaning, implying strong dislike or hatred.
