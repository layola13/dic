rip-off: /ˈrɪp ɒf/ | n., v. | 敲竹杠；欺诈；劣质商品 |

英文解析:A rip-off is something that is much more expensive than it should be, or of much lower quality than expected,  implying unfair or dishonest pricing or quality. As a verb, it means to cheat someone financially.

例句:

That restaurant was a total rip-off; the food was terrible and the prices were outrageous. = 那家餐馆完全是敲竹杠；食物很糟糕，价格也离谱。

They totally ripped me off on that car; it broke down a week later. = 他们完全在车子上骗了我；一周后它就坏了。


近义词: swindle, fraud, cheat, overcharge, scam, exploitation

反义词: bargain, steal (in the sense of a great deal), fair price


词性:  Noun and Verb
