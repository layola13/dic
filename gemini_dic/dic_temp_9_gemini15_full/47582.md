debasements:/dɪˈbeɪsmənts/ | n. | 贬低；堕落；降低；贬值 | The act of debasing something or someone; the state of being debased.  It refers to lowering the quality, value, or moral character of something or someone.  |例句：The debasements of the currency led to high inflation. 通货贬值导致通货膨胀。

The debasements of his character were evident in his dishonest dealings. 他品格的堕落在他不诚实的交易中显而易见。


近义词：degradation, depreciation, deterioration, corruption, debasement


反义词：elevation, enhancement, improvement, upliftment


词源:  From "debase" + "-ment" (noun suffix). "Debase" itself comes from Old French *debaisser (to lower, humble), from *de- (down) + *baisser (to lower, from Vulgar Latin *bassare, related to Latin *bassus (low)).
