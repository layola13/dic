/ˈpaɪəɪst/| n. |虔诚的教徒；虔信派信徒 |A member of a Protestant group emphasizing personal piety and intense religious experience.  Pietists often focus on a deep personal relationship with God and a life of fervent devotion.  They often stress the importance of Bible study, prayer, and charitable works. |The Pietist movement began in the late 17th century as a reaction against the perceived formalism and lack of spiritual vitality in some established churches.  |近义词：devout, religious, godly |反义词：atheist, secularist, irreligious |名词
