caltrap: /ˈkæltræp/ | n. | 陷阱；蒺藜 | A sharp, pointed object, often made of metal or wood, scattered on the ground to injure the feet of people or animals.  Caltraps are typically four-pointed, with each point designed to lie upwards when dropped. | Example: The soldiers scattered caltrops around the castle to impede the enemy's advance.  士兵们在城堡周围散布了蒺藜以阻止敌人的前进。|近义词：spike trap, punji stick | 反义词：safe passage, clear path
