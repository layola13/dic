crocky:/ˈkrɒki/| adj.|破旧的；肮脏的；不健康的|Describes something that is in poor condition, dirty, or unhealthy.  It often implies a slightly humorous or informal tone.|例句：The old house was a bit crocky, but it had charm. 这栋老房子有点破旧，但很有魅力。
例句：The food looked a bit crocky, so I didn't eat it. 食物看起来有点不干净，所以我没吃。|近义词：shabby, dilapidated, rickety, dodgy,  decrepit|反义词：new, clean, pristine, healthy
