proportionated: /prəˌpɔːrʃənˌeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 成比例的；相称的；匀称的 | Having or showing the correct proportions; well-balanced.  The design is well-proportionated, with harmonious relationships between its parts.  | The building's design is well-proportionated. 这座建筑的设计比例协调。 The vase is beautifully proportionated.  这花瓶比例匀称优美。|近义词：balanced, symmetrical, harmonious | 反义词：disproportionate, unbalanced, asymmetrical


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The parts of the machine are proportionated correctly. 机器部件比例正确。
The ingredients are proportionated to create the perfect blend. 配料比例精确，以创造完美的混合物。


一般过去时:
The architect proportionated the windows and doors carefully. 建筑师仔细地设计了窗户和门的大小比例。
The sculptor proportionated the figure to make it look realistic. 雕塑家对雕像进行了比例调整使其看起来更逼真。


现在完成时:
The artist has proportionated the painting beautifully. 艺术家已经将这幅画的比例完美地协调起来。
They have proportionated the budget to cover all expenses. 他们已经对预算进行了比例分配以支付所有费用。


过去完成时:
He had proportionated the ingredients before he started cooking. 他在开始烹饪之前已经准备好配料的比例。
The team had proportionated the workload before they began the project. 团队在开始项目之前已经分配好工作量。


将来时:
She will proportionate the funds among the different departments. 她将把资金分配给不同的部门。
They will proportionate the ingredients according to the recipe. 他们将根据食谱调整配料比例。


虚拟语气:
If he had proportionated the materials correctly, the building would be stronger. 如果他正确地计算了材料的比例，建筑物就会更坚固。
If I were to proportionate the recipe, I would use less sugar. 如果我要调整食谱的比例，我会少用糖。


被动语态:
The resources were proportionated fairly among the teams. 资源在各团队之间被公平地分配。
The design was proportionated to fit the space perfectly. 设计比例协调，完美地契合空间。


疑问句:
Are the components proportionated correctly in the mixture? 混合物中的成分比例是否正确？
Has the budget been proportionated effectively across departments? 预算是否已在各部门之间有效分配？


否定句:
The sculpture was not proportionated correctly; the head was too large. 这座雕塑比例不正确；头部太大。
The ingredients were not proportionated properly, resulting in a poor mixture. 配料比例不当，导致混合物质量差。


PHRASE->

out of proportion:不成比例


词源:  From Middle French *proportioné, past participle of proportionner "to proportion," from Latin *proportiōnāre, from proportiō "proportion."  The ultimate origin is from Latin *pro "forth, forward" + *portiō "a part, share," from *partīre "to divide."
