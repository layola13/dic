have a short fuse: /ˌhæv ə ʃɔːrt fjuːz/ | adj. phr. | 脾气暴躁的，易怒的 |  To have a short fuse means to become angry very easily and quickly.  It's an idiom that uses the analogy of a fuse on a bomb or firework, which burns quickly and explodes.  | 例句：He has a short fuse and often loses his temper over minor things. 他脾气暴躁，经常因为小事发脾气。

He has such a short fuse that even a minor inconvenience can set him off. 他脾气太火爆了，即使是很小的不便也会让他发火。


近义词：irritable, quick-tempered, hot-headed, choleric, easily angered | 反义词：patient, even-tempered, mild-mannered, placid


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has a short fuse. = 他脾气暴躁。
She has a short fuse and often shouts. = 她脾气火爆，经常大喊大叫。


一般过去时:
He had a short fuse when he was younger. = 他年轻的时候脾气很暴躁。
She had a short fuse, which caused many problems in her relationships. = 她脾气火爆，这给她的人际关系带来了很多问题。


现在完成时:
He has always had a short fuse. = 他一直脾气暴躁。
She has had a short fuse since childhood. = 她从小脾气就火爆。


过去完成时:
He had had a short fuse for years before seeking therapy. = 在寻求治疗之前，他脾气暴躁已经很多年了。
She had had a short fuse all her life, making it hard to maintain friendships. = 她一生都脾气火爆，很难维持友谊。


将来时:
He will likely still have a short fuse even after anger management. = 即使在接受愤怒管理治疗后，他可能仍然脾气暴躁。
She will probably have a short fuse until she learns to manage her stress. = 在学会控制压力之前，她可能会一直脾气火爆。


虚拟语气:
If he didn't have such a short fuse, he would have more friends. = 如果他不那么暴躁，他就会有更多朋友。
If she had a longer fuse, she wouldn't argue so much. = 如果她脾气好一点，她就不会那么多争吵了。


被动语态:  (This idiom is not typically used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
Does he have a short fuse? = 他脾气暴躁吗？
Does she have a short fuse; is that why she’s so angry? = 她脾气火爆吗？这就是她为什么这么生气的原因吗？


否定句:
He doesn't have a short fuse; he's usually very calm. = 他脾气不暴躁；他通常很冷静。
She doesn't have a short fuse; she's surprisingly patient. = 她脾气不火爆；她出奇地有耐心。
