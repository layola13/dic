electrodynamics:/ˌɪˌlɛktrəʊdaɪˈnæmɪks/| n. |电动力学|The branch of physics that deals with the interaction of electric and magnetic fields and their effects on matter.  It's a fundamental part of classical physics, describing phenomena like electromagnetic radiation and the forces between charged particles.|Example:  Maxwell's equations are fundamental to electrodynamics. 麦克斯韦方程组是电动力学的基础。
The study of electrodynamics requires a strong foundation in calculus and vector analysis.  学习电动力学需要扎实的微积分和向量分析基础。|近义词:electromagnetism |反义词: (no direct antonym, but concepts like statics could be considered contrasting)|名词
