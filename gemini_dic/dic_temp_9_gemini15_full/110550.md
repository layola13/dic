limericks:/ˈlɪmərɪks/ | n. |五行打油诗，五音步诗 |A limerick is a humorous five-line poem with a specific rhyming scheme and rhythm.  It's typically nonsensical or bawdy in nature.  |Example: There once was a fellow named Kyle, /Whose jokes made the audience smile. /He'd say with a grin, /“Let the laughter begin!” /And then he would bow with great style.|近义词：five-line poem, humorous verse |反义词：serious poem, sonnet
