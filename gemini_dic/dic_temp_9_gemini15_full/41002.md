cop-outs:/ˈkɑːpˌaʊts/| n.|逃避责任的行为；借口；敷衍了事|An easy way to avoid a difficult situation or responsibility; an excuse.  A cop-out is something done to avoid responsibility or difficult work.|例句：His explanation was just a cop-out; he clearly wasn't telling the truth.他的解释只是一个借口；他显然没有说实话。

例句：Instead of facing the problem, he chose to offer a cop-out.与其面对问题，他选择逃避责任。


近义词：excuse, evasion, avoidance, pretext, subterfuge

反义词：responsibility, accountability, facing up to, confrontation


词源：The term "cop-out" likely emerged in the mid-20th century as a slang term.  "Cop" in this context doesn't refer to a police officer but is a shortened form of "cop out," meaning "to escape" or "to evade."  The addition of "-out" emphasizes the completeness of the avoidance.
