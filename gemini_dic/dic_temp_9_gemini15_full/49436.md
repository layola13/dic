delphian:/dɛlˈfaɪən/| adj.|德尔斐的；德尔斐神谕的；神秘的；含糊其辞的 | Relating to Delphi, the ancient Greek city famous for its oracle.  The adjective often implies something mysterious, ambiguous, or enigmatic, reflecting the often obscure nature of the oracle's pronouncements. |例句：The Delphic pronouncements were often open to multiple interpretations.  德尔斐的神谕常常有多种解读。

The Delphic method encourages questioning assumptions. 德尔斐方法鼓励质疑假设。


近义词：oracular, enigmatic, ambiguous, cryptic |反义词：clear, explicit, unambiguous, straightforward|词性：adj.


词源:  From Delphi, the ancient Greek city renowned for its oracle of Apollo.  The oracle's pronouncements were often veiled in ambiguity, contributing to the modern usage of "Delphian" to describe something mysterious or unclear.
