monitorship: /ˈmɒnɪtəʃɪp/ | n. | 监控；监察；监视职位 | The act or process of monitoring; the position or function of a monitor.  It can also refer to the period of time someone holds a monitoring position. |

例句：

The company implemented a new monitorship program to improve product quality.  = 公司实施了一个新的监控计划来提高产品质量。

Her monitorship of the project lasted for six months. = 她对项目的监控持续了六个月。


近义词：supervision, oversight, surveillance, monitoring

反义词：neglect, disregard, oversight (in the sense of failure to oversee)


词源:

From "monitor" + "-ship",  "-ship" is a suffix forming nouns denoting state, condition, quality, or art.  "Monitor" itself originates from Latin "monēre" (to warn, remind), reflecting the function of a monitor to keep watch and alert others to potential problems.
