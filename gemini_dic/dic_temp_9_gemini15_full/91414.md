hyemation:/haɪˈeɪmeɪʃən/| n. |冬眠；蛰伏|The process by which an animal or plant survives unfavorable environmental conditions, such as cold temperatures or lack of food, by entering a state of dormancy.  It is characterized by reduced metabolic rate, slowed heart rate, and lowered body temperature.|例句：Bears undergo hyemation during the winter months.  熊在冬季进行冬眠。|例句：The plant's hyemation allowed it to survive the harsh drought. 植物的蛰伏使它挺过了严酷的干旱。|近义词：hibernation, dormancy, diapause|反义词：activity, arousal
