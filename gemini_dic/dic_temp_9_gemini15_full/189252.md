saddle:/ˈsæd(ə)l/| n. v.|鞍，马鞍；（使）承担重负|A saddle is a seat for a rider on a horse or other animal.  It can also refer to a similar structure on a bicycle or motorcycle.  As a verb, it means to put a saddle on (an animal) or to burden or weigh down.|例句：He carefully adjusted the saddle on his horse. 他仔细地调整了他马上的鞍子。

The mechanic saddled the bicycle with a heavy rack. 技师给自行车加装了一个沉重的货架。|近义词：seat, burden|反义词：unburden


USAGE->

He saddles his horse every morning. = 他每天早上给他的马装鞍。
They saddle their bikes with extra bags for the trip. = 他们为旅行给他们的自行车装上额外的包。

The extra work saddled him with a lot of stress. = 额外的工作给他带来了很多压力。

虚拟语气:

If she hadn't saddled herself with so much work, she would have had more free time. = 如果她没有给自己安排这么多工作，她就会有更多空闲时间。

否定句:

词源:
The word "saddle" comes from the Old English word "sadel," which is believed to be of Germanic origin.  The Germanic root likely reflects an image of the saddle's shape and function.
