pressurises: /ˈprɛʃəraɪz/ | vt. |使增压；使承受压力；对…施压 | To subject to pressure; to put pressure on.  |例句：The company pressurised its employees to work overtime. 公司强迫员工加班。  |近义词：pressurize, force, coerce |反义词：relieve, ease


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government pressurises businesses to reduce emissions. = 政府向企业施压，要求减少排放。
He pressurises his son to study harder. = 他逼迫儿子更加努力学习。


一般过去时:
They pressurised him into accepting the deal. = 他们向他施压，让他接受这笔交易。
She pressurised me to make a quick decision. = 她逼我快速做决定。


现在完成时:
They have pressurised the authorities into changing the law. = 他们已经向当局施压，要求更改法律。
The media has pressurised the company to reveal more information. = 媒体已经向公司施压，要求披露更多信息。


过去完成时:
They had pressurised him for weeks before he finally agreed. = 在他最终同意之前，他们已经对他施压了好几个星期。
The committee had pressurised the mayor to resign. = 委员会此前曾向市长施压，要求他辞职。


将来时:
They will pressurise the board to approve the project. = 他们将向董事会施压，以批准该项目。
The union will pressurise the management to increase wages. = 工会将向管理层施压，要求提高工资。


虚拟语气:
If they pressurised him more, he might agree. = 如果他们对他施压更多，他可能会同意。
I wish they hadn't pressurised me so much. = 我真希望他们不要对我施压那么多。


被动语态:
The employees were pressurised into working overtime. = 员工被迫加班。
The decision was pressurised by external forces. = 这个决定受到了外部势力的压力。


疑问句:
Are they pressurising you to leave your job? = 他们是否在逼你离职？
Were you pressurised into signing that contract? = 你是被迫签署那份合同的吗？


否定句:
They didn't pressurise her to make a choice. = 他们没有强迫她做出选择。
The manager didn't pressurise his staff to work late. = 经理没有强迫他的员工加班。



PHRASE->
pressurise someone into doing something  = 迫使某人做某事
pressurise a system = 对系统加压
pressurise a container = 对容器加压
pressurise a cabin = 对客舱增压
under pressure = 在压力下


词源:
From "pressure" + "-ise" (suffix forming verbs).  "Pressure" itself originates from Old French *pressurer, from Latin pressura "a pressing, pressure," from pressus, past participle of premere "to press."
