malacopterygii:/ˌmælæˈkɒptərɪdʒiː/| n. |软鳍鱼类|A subclass of ray-finned fishes characterized by having soft, jointed, and often spiny rays in their fins.  They lack the hard, bony spines found in some other fish groups.  The group includes many familiar fish such as eels, herring, and cod.|Example:  Many commercially important fish species belong to the Malacopterygii subclass. 许多重要的商业鱼类都属于软鳍鱼亚纲。|近义词：Teleostei (a broader group including Malacopterygii)|反义词：Acanthopterygii (spiny-rayed fishes)|名词
