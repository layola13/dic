反义词：unnatural, stilted, formal, literal


词源:

The word "idiomatical" is derived from the noun "idiom," which comes from the Greek word "idioma" (ἴδιωμα), meaning "peculiar expression," "private property," or "peculiarity."  The suffix "-al"  transforms the noun into an adjective, indicating a quality or characteristic of something.
