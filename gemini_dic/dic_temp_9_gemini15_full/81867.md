grief-stricken: /ˈɡriːfˌstrɪkən/| adj. | 悲痛欲绝的，极度悲伤的 | Extremely sad because of a loss.  |例句：She was grief-stricken after the death of her husband. 她丈夫去世后悲痛欲绝。

例句：The grief-stricken parents mourned the loss of their child. 悲痛欲绝的父母为失去孩子而哀悼。


近义词：heartbroken, devastated, inconsolable, distraught

反义词：joyful, elated, cheerful, happy

词性: adjective


词源:

* **grief:**  Old English *grief*, meaning "grievance, sorrow, injury."  Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *greipa- ("to seize, grasp"), reflecting the feeling of being emotionally seized or overwhelmed by sorrow.

* **stricken:** Past participle of the verb "to strike," meaning to affect suddenly and forcefully.  In this context, "stricken" implies being powerfully affected by grief.
