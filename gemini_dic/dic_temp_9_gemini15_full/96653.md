industrialisms: /ɪnˈdʌstriəlɪzəm/ | n. | 工业化；工业制度 | The complex of social, economic, and political relationships associated with industrial production.  It encompasses the transformation of societies from agrarian to industrial economies, characterized by mass production, technological innovation, urbanization, and the rise of factories.  | Example: The rapid industrialisms of the 19th century transformed the social landscape of many nations. = 19世纪快速的工业化改变了许多国家的社会景象。  The consequences of unchecked industrialisms are devastating to the environment. = 不受控制的工业化的后果对环境具有破坏性。 | 近义词：industrialization, mechanization | 反义词：deindustrialization, agrarianism |  名词
