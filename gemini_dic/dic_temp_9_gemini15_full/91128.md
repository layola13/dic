hybridises:/ˈhaɪbrɪdaɪzɪz/ | v. | 杂交；混合；使杂交 | To produce a hybrid by combining elements from different sources.  This often refers to the process in biology where different species or varieties are crossed to create offspring with combined traits. It can also be used more broadly to describe the blending of different things, such as ideas or styles. | Example:  The scientists hybridised different strains of rice to produce a more resilient crop. 科学家们将不同品种的水稻杂交，以培育出更抗旱的作物。

The roses were hybridised to create a new colour.  这些玫瑰进行了杂交，培育出了新的颜色。


近义词：crossbreed, interbreed, blend, mix, combine | 反义词：separate, isolate
