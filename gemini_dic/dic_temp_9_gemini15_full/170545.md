pseudepigraphous: /ˌsjuːdəˈpɪɡrəfəs/ | adj. |伪经的；伪托之名的 |  Relating to or characteristic of pseudepigrapha; falsely attributed to an author or period.  The term describes writings falsely attributed to a well-known author or historical figure, often to lend authority or credibility to the work. These works often contain religious or philosophical ideas. | 例句：The discovery of the pseudepigraphous gospel challenged the accepted chronology of early Christianity.  发现伪经福音挑战了早期基督教的公认年代顺序。

The analysis of the pseudepigraphous texts revealed a complex interplay of religious and political motivations. 伪经文本的分析揭示了宗教和政治动机之间的复杂互动。| 近义词：spurious, apocryphal, forged, fictitious | 反义词：authentic, genuine, canonical
