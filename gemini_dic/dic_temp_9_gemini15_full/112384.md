loss-leader:/ˈlɒsˌliːdər/ | n. |亏本商品，赔本促销商品 | A loss leader is a product sold at a price below its market cost to stimulate sales of other, more profitable goods or services.  Businesses often use loss leaders to attract customers to their stores, hoping that these customers will also purchase other items at regular prices.  | Example: The supermarket offered milk as a loss leader to attract shoppers, knowing they would buy other groceries as well. 超市提供牛奶作为亏本商品来吸引顾客，他们知道顾客还会购买其他杂货。|近义词：bait, lure |反义词：profit-maker, high-margin item |名词
