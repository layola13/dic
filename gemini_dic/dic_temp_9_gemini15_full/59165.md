dyscrasy: /ˈdɪskrəsi/ | n. | 异常；不良状态；不和谐；血液病 | An abnormal or unhealthy state; a morbid condition, especially of the blood.  The term often implies an imbalance or disorder within a system. |  例句：The patient presented with a dyscrasy of the blood, requiring immediate medical attention.  患者出现血液异常，需要立即就医。

例句：The political dyscrasy led to widespread unrest and social upheaval. 政治上的不和谐导致了广泛的动荡和社会动乱。


近义词：imbalance, disorder, abnormality, discrepancy, disease


反义词：harmony, balance, health, normalcy


词性：名词


词源：From Late Latin dyscrasia, from Greek dys- (“bad”) + krasis (“mixture,” “temperament”).  The original Greek meaning referred to a bad mixture or temperament of bodily humors.
