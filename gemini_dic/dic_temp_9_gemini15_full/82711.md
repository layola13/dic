guid:/ɡaɪd/| n. |全局唯一标识符|A globally unique identifier (GUID), also known as a universally unique identifier (UUID), is a 128-bit integer used to identify information in computer systems.  It is designed to ensure that every identifier is unique across all space and time, even if generated on different systems.  This uniqueness is crucial for various applications, preventing conflicts and ensuring data integrity.|Example: Each user account on the system is assigned a unique GUID.  每个用户帐户都被分配了一个唯一的GUID。|近义词：UUID, unique identifier|反义词：N/A
