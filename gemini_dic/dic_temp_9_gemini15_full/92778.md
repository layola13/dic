icicle: /ˈaɪsɪkəl/ | n. | 冰垂，冰柱 | A cone-shaped piece of ice hanging from a surface, formed by the freezing of dripping water.  | Example:  The icicles hung from the roof like glittering daggers. 冰柱像闪闪发光的匕首一样悬挂在屋顶上。  The children were fascinated by the icicles forming on the fence.孩子们对围栏上形成的冰柱很着迷。| 近义词：ice spike, frozen drip | 反义词：None  |  词性: 名词

Icicles form on the eaves in winter. 冬天屋檐上会结冰柱。

词源:
The word "icicle" comes from the Middle English word "ysicle," which itself is derived from the Old English "īsīcel," a compound of "īs" (ice) and "īcel" (a small piece or drop).  The suffix "-icle" indicates a small thing, similar to its use in words like "particle" or "molecule".
