expose:/ɪkˈspoʊz/ | /ɪkˈspəʊz/ | vt. n. | 暴露；揭露；使曝光；使面临 | To reveal something that is usually hidden or kept secret; to make something known publicly. |  例句：The journalist exposed the politician's corruption.  记者揭露了这位政治家的腐败行为。

例句：The photographer exposed the film to sunlight. 摄影师将胶卷曝光在阳光下。


近义词：reveal, uncover, disclose, unveil | 反义词：conceal, hide, cover, mask | 词性: Verb (vt.) and Noun (n.)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exposes the truth to the public. 他向公众揭露真相。
The sun exposes the land to intense heat. 太阳使土地暴露在强烈的热量下。


一般过去时:
She exposed the company's unethical practices. 她揭露了这家公司的非道德行为。
The experiment exposed the mice to a new drug.  实验让老鼠接触一种新药。


现在完成时:
The investigation has exposed a vast network of crime.  调查已经揭露了一个庞大的犯罪网络。
We have exposed the children to various cultures. 我们让孩子们接触了各种文化。


过去完成时:
They had exposed the conspiracy before the trial began. 审判开始前，他们已经揭露了阴谋。
The scientist had exposed the bacteria to radiation. 科学家已将细菌暴露在辐射下。


将来时:
The report will expose the government's incompetence.  这份报告将揭露政府的不称职。
They will expose the paintings to the public next month. 他们下个月将向公众展出这些画作。


虚拟语气:
If we exposed the problem, they would have to address it. 如果我们揭露这个问题，他们就必须处理它。
I wish they had exposed the truth sooner. 我希望他们早点揭露真相。


被动语态:
The scandal was exposed by a whistleblower.  这个丑闻被一个举报人揭露了。
The film was exposed for too long. 胶卷曝光时间过长。


疑问句:
Did the media expose the truth? 媒体是否揭露了真相？
How was the secret exposed? 这个秘密是如何被揭露的？


否定句:
The company did not expose its financial problems. 这家公司没有公开其财务问题。
They refused to expose the criminal's identity. 他们拒绝透露罪犯的身份。


PHRASE->
expose oneself to: 使自己暴露于…
expose to: 使…暴露于
expose the truth: 揭露真相
expose a scandal: 揭露丑闻
expose shortcomings: 暴露缺点


词源:
From Old French *exposer, from Latin exponere, from ex- “out” + ponere “to place”.  The sense development is from “to place out” to “to lay open” to “to reveal”.
