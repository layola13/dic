maims:/meɪmz/| vt.|使残废；使严重受伤；严重损坏|To inflict serious damage on someone or something, especially to injure a person so severely that a limb or other part of the body is lost or disabled.|The accident maimed him for life.  事故使他终身残废。|The storm maimed the coastal towns. 暴风雨严重破坏了沿海城镇。|cripple, disable, injure|heal, repair


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The war maims many innocent people. = 战争使许多无辜的人致残。
He maims his opponents with his vicious attacks. = 他用恶毒的攻击使对手严重受伤。


一般过去时:
The explosion maimed several bystanders. = 爆炸使几名旁观者受伤致残。
The fall maimed her leg. = 那次跌倒使她的腿受伤致残。


现在完成时:
The accident has maimed him for life. = 事故使他终身残废。
Years of war have maimed countless civilians. = 多年的战争使无数平民伤残。


过去完成时:
Before the attack, the soldier had already been maimed in a previous battle. = 在袭击之前，这位士兵已经在之前的战斗中受了重伤致残。
The car accident had maimed her before she even reached adulthood. = 那场车祸在她成年之前就已经使她残疾。


将来时:
If the bomb explodes, it will maim many people. = 如果炸弹爆炸，它将会使许多人致残。
The reckless driving may maim someone someday. = 鲁莽驾驶迟早会伤及他人。


虚拟语气:

I wish that the war hadn't maimed so many innocent children. = 我希望战争没有使那么多无辜的孩子致残。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
