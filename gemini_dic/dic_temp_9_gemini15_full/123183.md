monoamine:/ˈmɒnoʊæmiːn/|/ˈmɒnəʊæmiːn/| n. |单胺|A monoamine is a compound containing one amine group.  They are neurotransmitters and hormones in the body, playing crucial roles in various physiological processes.  Examples include dopamine, norepinephrine, and serotonin.|Example:  Disruptions in monoamine neurotransmission are implicated in several neurological and psychiatric disorders.|近义词: biogenic amine|反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it's a class of compounds)|
