ogygian:/oʊˈdʒɪdʒiən/| adj.|奥吉吉亚的；史前的；极其古老的|Of or relating to Ogyges, a legendary king of Boeotia, whose reign was traditionally placed at the beginning of the second millennium BC.  The word is often used figuratively to describe something extremely ancient or primordial. |例句：The myths spoke of an ogygian age, before the rise of civilizations.  神话故事讲述了一个奥吉吉亚时代，那是文明兴起之前的时代。
