refractors:/rɪˈfræktərz/| n. |折射望远镜；折射器|A refractor is a type of optical telescope that uses a lens to collect and focus light.  It works by bending (refracting) the light as it passes through the lens, forming an image. Refractors are known for their sharp images and ease of maintenance. | Example:  The observatory boasts several large refractors for astronomical observation.  这个天文台拥有几台大型折射望远镜用于天文观测。|近义词：telescope, optical instrument|反义词：reflector (反射望远镜)|名词
