sight-readers:/ˈsaɪtˌriːdərz/| n.|视奏者，乐谱朗读者|Those who can read and play music at sight, without prior rehearsal.  They possess the ability to interpret and perform musical notation immediately. |例句：The orchestra needs more sight-readers for the upcoming concert.  乐团需要更多视奏者来参加即将到来的音乐会。  The conductor was impressed by the sight-readers' skill and accuracy. 指挥对视奏者的技巧和准确性印象深刻。|近义词：musicians, performers|反义词：rehearsers (in the context of playing music from memory or after rehearsal)
