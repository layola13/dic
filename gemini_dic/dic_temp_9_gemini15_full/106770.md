labarum:/ˈlæbərəm/| n. |军旗，皇帝的军旗|A labarum was a personal standard of the Roman emperors, typically featuring a depiction of Christ and the Chi Rho symbol.  It was adopted by Constantine the Great and became a significant symbol of the Christian Roman Empire.|The labarum of Constantine inspired awe and loyalty among his troops.|  近义词：standard, banner, ensign | 反义词：None specifically.
