reprobated:/ˈrɛprəbeɪtɪd/| adj. v.|谴责的；被斥责的；|Rejected, condemned, or disapproved of.  The reprobated plan was abandoned.|The committee reprobated his actions.  委员会谴责了他的行为。|近义词：condemned, denounced, disapproved, rejected|反义词：approved, accepted, praised


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reprobates their behavior. = 他谴责他们的行为。
The council reprobates the use of violence. = 理事会谴责使用暴力。


一般过去时:
They reprobated the proposal. = 他们否决了这个提案。
The judge reprobated the defendant's actions. = 法官谴责了被告的行为。


现在完成时:
The church has reprobated this practice for centuries. = 教会几个世纪以来一直谴责这种做法。
Society has reprobated his immoral conduct. = 社会谴责了他的不道德行为。


过去完成时:
They had reprobated his ideas before he even presented them. = 甚至在他提出他的想法之前，他们就已经否决了。
The committee had reprobated similar proposals in the past. = 委员会过去曾否决过类似的提案。


将来时:
They will reprobate the new policy. = 他们将谴责这项新政策。
The public will likely reprobate such a decision. = 公众很可能会谴责这样的决定。


虚拟语气:
If they knew the truth, they would reprobate his actions. = 如果他们知道真相，他们就会谴责他的行为。
I wish they would reprobate such behavior. = 我希望他们能谴责这种行为。


被动语态:
His actions were reprobated by the community. = 他的行为受到社区的谴责。
The plan was reprobated by the board of directors. = 该计划遭到董事会的否决。


疑问句:
Will his actions be reprobated? = 他的行为会被谴责吗？
Were their methods reprobated? = 他们的方法被谴责了吗？


否定句:
They did not reprobate his efforts. = 他们没有谴责他的努力。
The committee did not reprobate the new rules. = 委员会没有否决新规。

词源:
From Middle French *réprobé, from Latin reprobatus, past participle of reprobare "to disapprove, reject," from re- "again" + probare "to test, prove."  The original sense involved testing and finding wanting.
