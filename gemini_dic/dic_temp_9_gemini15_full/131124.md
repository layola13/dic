nonblack: /ˌnɑːnˈblæk/ | adj. | 非黑人的；非黑种人的 |  Describing people or things that are not of Black African descent.  This term is often used in contexts discussing race and ethnicity,  and its usage can be sensitive depending on the context. The term is increasingly viewed as problematic due to its broadness and potential for exclusion. More specific terms are often preferred. |  例句：The survey showed a disparity in outcomes between Black and nonblack participants.  这项调查显示，黑人和非黑人参与者之间的结果存在差异。|近义词：non-African American (in some contexts), people of color (when referring to a group that includes people of other races besides black),  other races. |反义词：Black, African American | 词性: adjective
