run-ins:/ˈrʌnɪnz/| n.|小冲突，争吵，偶然相遇 |A brief encounter or argument; a clash.|例句：He had a few run-ins with the law in his youth. 他年轻时几次与法律发生冲突。|例句：Their first run-in was at a conference. 他们第一次相遇是在一个会议上。|近义词：disputes, altercations, encounters, clashes|反义词：agreements, harmonies


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often has run-ins with his neighbours. = 他经常和邻居发生冲突。
They frequently have run-ins at work. = 他们在工作中经常发生冲突。

一般过去时:
We had a run-in at the supermarket yesterday. = 昨天我们在超市发生了一次冲突。
She had several run-ins with her boss last month. = 上个月她和老板发生了好几次冲突。

现在完成时:
I've had a few run-ins with that guy. = 我和那家伙发生过几次冲突。
They haven't had any run-ins since the incident. = 自那件事以来他们再也没有发生过冲突。

过去完成时:
He had already had several run-ins with the police before the arrest. = 在被捕之前，他已经和警察发生过几次冲突。
She had never had a run-in with the law before. = 她以前从未与法律发生过冲突。

将来时:
I think we'll have a run-in with them sooner or later. = 我想我们迟早会和他们发生冲突。
They might have a run-in if they continue to argue. = 如果他们继续争吵，他们可能会发生冲突。


虚拟语气:
If I had known he was so aggressive, I would have avoided a run-in. = 如果我知道他那么咄咄逼人，我就会避免与他发生冲突。
I wish I hadn't had that run-in with my landlord. = 我真希望我没有和我的房东发生那次冲突。

被动语态:
The new policy was expected to cause run-ins between management and staff. = 预计新政策会导致管理层和员工之间发生冲突。
Run-ins with the police are often reported in this area. = 在这个地区经常有与警察发生冲突的报告。

疑问句:
Have you ever had a run-in with the law? = 你曾经和法律发生过冲突吗？
Did they have a run-in at the party? = 他们在派对上发生冲突了吗？

否定句:
We didn't have any run-ins during our trip. = 我们在旅行期间没有发生任何冲突。
They haven't had a run-in with him for years. = 他们多年来没有和他发生过冲突。
