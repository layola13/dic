monoecism:/ˈmɒnoʊɪsɪzəm/|/ˈmɒnəʊɪsɪzəm/| n. |雌雄同株；雌雄同体|The state of having both male and female reproductive organs in the same individual plant or animal.  This contrasts with dioecism, where the sexes are separate.  In botany, monoecism refers to plants that bear separate male and female flowers on the same plant.  In zoology, it refers to animals that have both male and female reproductive organs. |例句：Many flowering plants exhibit monoecism, with separate male and female flowers on the same individual.  许多开花植物表现出雌雄同株，同一植株上有单独的雄花和雌花。

The corn plant is a classic example of monoecism; it has separate male (tassel) and female (ear) flowers on the same plant. 玉米植株是雌雄同株的典型例子；它在同一植株上具有单独的雄花（雄穗）和雌花（雌穗）。|近义词：hermaphroditism (in zoology)|反义词：dioecism
