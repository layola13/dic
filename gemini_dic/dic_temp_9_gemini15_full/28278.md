captive:/ˈkæptɪv/ | adj., n. | 俘虏的；被囚禁的；被迷住的 |  A captive is a person who is imprisoned or held against their will.  An adjective describing something held or controlled. |例句：The captive bird sang sadly in its cage.  被囚禁的小鸟在笼子里悲鸣。

The soldiers took many captives during the battle.  士兵们在战斗中俘获了许多俘虏。


近义词：prisoner, hostage, enslaved | 反义词：free, liberated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bird remains captive in its cage. = 这只鸟仍被囚禁在笼子里。
He keeps his emotions captive. = 他压抑着自己的情感。


一般过去时:
The army kept many captives after the war. = 战争结束后，军队俘虏了许多人。
They held him captive for days. = 他们将他囚禁了好几天。


现在完成时:
The rebels have held the journalist captive for weeks. = 反叛分子已经将记者囚禁数周了。
She has been captive to her fears for years. = 多年来，她一直被恐惧所束缚。


过去完成时:
The police had kept the suspect captive before the trial. = 在审判之前，警方将嫌疑犯拘留了。
They had been captive in the burning building. = 他们曾被困在燃烧的建筑物里。


将来时:
They will keep the criminal captive until the trial. = 他们将在审判前将罪犯关押起来。
The magician will hold the audience captive with his performance. = 魔术师将以他的表演吸引住观众。


虚拟语气:
If he were captive, he would be very unhappy. = 如果他被囚禁，他会非常不高兴。
I wish I were not captive to my addiction. = 我真希望自己不被我的瘾所困扰。


被动语态:
The hostages were kept captive for months. = 人质被囚禁了几个月。
The audience was held captive by the speaker's eloquence. = 观众被演讲者的雄辩所吸引。


疑问句:
Is the animal captive or wild? = 这动物是圈养的还是野生的？
Are the thoughts captive to the body? = 思想是被禁锢在肉体里吗？


否定句:
The bird is not captive, it's free. = 这只鸟不是被囚禁的，它是自由的。
His spirit is not captive to despair. = 他的精神没有被绝望所束缚。
