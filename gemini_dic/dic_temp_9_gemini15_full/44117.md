crisping: /ˈkrɪspɪŋ/ | v. | 使变脆；使变酥脆；(使)发出轻微的噼啪声 |  The act of making something crisp or become crisp;  the sound of something crisping.  |  例句：The bacon was crisping nicely in the pan. = 培根在锅里煎得正酥脆。


The sun crisped the leaves. = 太阳晒得树叶变脆了。

近义词：frying, browning, crackling | 反义词：softening, wilting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bacon is crisping nicely. = 培根煎得正酥脆。
She is crisping the potatoes in the oven. = 她正在烤箱里烤土豆，使其变脆。


一般过去时:
The chef crisped the onions before adding them to the soup. = 厨师在把洋葱加入汤中之前先把它们煎脆。
He crisped the lettuce leaves for the salad. = 他把生菜叶煎脆做沙拉。


现在完成时:
The sun has crisped the leaves on the trees. = 太阳已经把树叶晒脆了。
I have crisped the potatoes, they're ready to serve. = 我已经把土豆煎脆了，可以上菜了。


过去完成时:

They had crisped the chicken skin to a golden brown. = 他们已经把鸡皮煎成金黄色，酥脆可口。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish I had crisped the potatoes more. = 我希望我把土豆煎得更脆一些。


被动语态:
The potatoes are crisped to perfection. = 土豆煎得非常完美。
The lettuce will be crisped in a hot pan. = 生菜会在热锅里煎脆。


疑问句:
Are the potatoes crisping enough? = 土豆煎得够脆了吗？

否定句:
