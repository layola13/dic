overproving: /ˌoʊvərˈpruːvɪŋ/ | v. | 过度证明；过度验证 | The act of providing more evidence or proof than is necessary to establish a fact or conclusion.  It can also refer to exceeding the expected or required level of proof in a specific context, such as baking or chemical analysis. | Example:  The lawyer's overproving of the defendant's guilt actually weakened their case.  |近义词: over-demonstration, excessive verification | 反义词: underproving, insufficient proof |
