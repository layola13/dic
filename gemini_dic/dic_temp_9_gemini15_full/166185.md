prepsychological: /ˌpriːˌsaɪkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl/ | adj. |心理学之前的；先于心理学的 |  Describing something that existed or occurred before the development of modern psychology as a scientific discipline.  It often refers to explanations or understandings of behavior and mental processes that predate psychological theories and methods. | Example:  His prepsychological understanding of human nature relied heavily on religious dogma.  他的先于心理学的人性理解严重依赖于宗教教条。| 近义词:  antecedent, prior, pre-scientific | 反义词: psychological, post-psychological
