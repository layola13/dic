commensals:/ˈkɒmənˌsælz/| n.|共栖生物；共栖者|Organisms of different species that live in close association with one another, often with mutual benefit, but without a strict symbiotic relationship.  One organism may benefit while the other is neither harmed nor helped. |Example:  Many bacteria living in the human gut are commensals. 许多生活在人体肠道中的细菌是共栖生物。|近义词：symbionts (in some contexts), associates |反义词：parasites, predators
