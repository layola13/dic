hypnotising: /ˈhɪpnətaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 催眠的；使着迷的 |  To hypnotize; to fascinate or captivate completely.  | 例句：The magician was hypnotising the audience with his incredible tricks.  魔术师用他不可思议的戏法催眠了观众。


The rhythmic music was hypnotising, lulling her into a deep sleep.  有节奏的音乐催眠了她，使她进入了深度睡眠。| 近义词：mesmerizing, captivating, enchanting | 反义词：boring, dull, uninspiring | 动词

词源:
The word "hypnotising" comes from "hypnosis," which itself originates from the Greek word "hypnos," meaning "sleep."  The "-ising" suffix indicates the present participle, signifying an ongoing action.  Therefore, "hypnotising" describes the process of inducing or experiencing hypnosis.
