mights:/maɪts/| v.|可能，也许|The third person singular present indicative form of may, expressing possibility or uncertainty. It suggests a less certain possibility than "will" or "would."|例句：It might rain later. = 今天晚些时候可能下雨。

例句：She might be at home. = 她可能在家。

近义词：may, could, possibly, perhaps

反义词：certainly, definitely, undoubtedly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
It might rain tomorrow. = 明天可能下雨。
He might be interested in the job. = 他可能对这份工作感兴趣。


一般过去时:
It might have rained yesterday. = 昨天可能下雨了。
She might have been at home. = 她可能在家。


现在完成时:
It might have rained already. = 可能已经下过雨了。
He might have finished the work. = 他可能已经完成了工作。


过去完成时:
It might have rained before we arrived. = 我们到达之前可能已经下过雨了。
She might have left before I called. = 我打电话之前她可能已经离开了。


将来时:
It might rain next week. = 下周可能下雨。
He might be promoted next year. = 他明年可能被提升。


虚拟语气:
If it might rain, we would stay inside. = 如果可能下雨，我们就待在里面。
I wish it might snow tomorrow. = 我希望明天能下雪。


被动语态:
The project might be completed on time. = 项目可能按时完成。
The meeting might be cancelled. = 会议可能被取消。


疑问句:
Might it rain tomorrow? = 明天可能下雨吗？
Might she be interested in the job? = 她可能对这份工作感兴趣吗？


否定句:
It might not rain tomorrow. = 明天可能不下雨。
He might not be interested in the job. = 他可能对这份工作不感兴趣。


PHRASE->
might as well:不妨
might have done:本来可以...
just might:可能，也许
it might be that:可能...
that might be:那可能是...


词源:
The word "mights" originates from the Old English verb "mōtan," meaning "to be permitted," "to be allowed," or "to be able."  Over time, its meaning evolved to include the sense of possibility and probability.  The "-s" ending is the third-person singular present indicative form of the verb.
