hastinapura: /ˌhɑːstɪnəˈpʊrə/ | n. | 哈斯提那普尔 | Hastinapura is an ancient city mentioned in the Mahabharata, an epic poem from ancient India.  It is believed to have been located near modern-day Meerut, Uttar Pradesh, India. The city is significant in Hindu mythology and is associated with the Kuru Kingdom and the events narrated in the Mahabharata. Archaeological evidence suggests the existence of a large settlement in the area, though the exact location and extent of Hastinapura remain debated among scholars. | The Mahabharata describes Hastinapura as a prosperous and powerful city, the capital of the Kuru Kingdom.  | 近义词：Indraprastha (another ancient city in the Mahabharata) | 反义词：无
