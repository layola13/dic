/ɪˈfɛktər/ | vt. | 引起；实现；导致；完成 | To bring about or cause something to happen; to produce or accomplish something.  The new policy effected significant changes in the company. 新政策对公司造成了重大变化。 | 近义词：cause, produce, achieve, accomplish | 反义词：prevent, hinder, obstruct | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He effects a cure for the disease. = 他找到了治疗这种疾病的方法。
The new law effects major changes in tax laws. = 新法律对税法作出了重大改变。


一般过去时:
The medicine effected a rapid recovery. = 这药使他很快康复了。
She effected a reconciliation between her parents. = 她促成了她父母的和解。


现在完成时:
The changes have effected a marked improvement in the situation. = 这些变化使情况有了显著改善。
His efforts have effected a positive impact on the community. = 他的努力对社区产生了积极的影响。


过去完成时:
The government had effected several reforms before the election. = 政府在大选前已经实施了几项改革。
They had effected a change in strategy before the final battle. = 在最后的战斗之前，他们已经改变了策略。


将来时:
The new technology will effect a revolution in the industry. = 新技术将给这个行业带来一场革命。
We will effect changes to improve efficiency. = 我们将进行变革以提高效率。


虚拟语气:
If he were to effect a change, it would be significant. = 如果他能带来改变，那将意义重大。
I wish he could effect a better solution to the problem. = 我希望他能找到更好的解决办法。


被动语态:
Significant changes were effected by the new policy. = 新政策带来了重大的变化。
A compromise was effected after long negotiations. =经过长时间的谈判后，达成了妥协。


疑问句:
Can he effect a solution in time? = 他能及时找到解决方案吗？
How will they effect the changes they promised? = 他们将如何实施他们承诺的改变？


否定句:
He couldn't effect a change in the situation. = 他无法改变现状。
The government failed to effect the necessary reforms. = 政府未能实施必要的改革。


PHRASE->
effect a change = 带来改变
effect a cure = 治愈
effect a compromise = 达成妥协
effect a reconciliation = 促成和解
effect an arrest = 逮捕


词源:
源自中古英语 *effekten，来自古法语 *efecter，最终源自拉丁语 *efficere，意思是“完成，实现”。
