saraband:/ˈsærəbænd/| n.|萨拉班德舞曲|A saraband is a slow, stately dance in triple time, typically in 3/4 time,  popular in the Baroque period.  It is characterized by its graceful, flowing movements and often has a melancholic or serious tone.  The dance originated in Spain and spread throughout Europe. |例句：The orchestra played a beautiful saraband, its rhythm both elegant and sorrowful.  乐队演奏了一首优美的萨拉班德舞曲，其节奏既优雅又悲伤。

The dancers performed a graceful saraband, their movements slow and deliberate. 舞蹈演员们表演了一支优雅的萨拉班德舞曲，他们的动作缓慢而谨慎。|近义词：pavane, courante, allemande |反义词：jig, hornpipe
