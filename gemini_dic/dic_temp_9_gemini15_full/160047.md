polytonic:/ˌpɒliˈtɒnɪk/| adj. |多音调的；多音节的 |Having or involving more than one tone or accent.  The term is primarily used in linguistics and music, referring to systems with multiple musical tones or accents in a syllable or word. In linguistics, it often refers to ancient Greek writing, where multiple diacritical marks indicated pronunciation. | Example: Ancient Greek was a polytonic language, with accents indicating pitch.  古代希腊语是一种多音调语言，重音标示音高。|近义词：multitonic, polysyllabic (in some contexts)|反义词：monotonic
