ducking: /ˈdʌkɪŋ/ | v. | 躲避；闪避；迅速低下（头） | To lower one's head or body quickly, or to avoid something. | 例句：He ducked to avoid the flying ball. 他低头躲避飞来的球。

例句：She ducked behind the tree to hide from the rain. 她躲到树后避雨。


近义词：avoiding, dodging, evading | 反义词：facing, confronting


词源:  源自古英语 *ducian，意思是“潜水”。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ducks every time he sees the boss. = 他每次看到老板就躲起来。
They duck under the low bridge. = 他们躲过了低矮的桥。


一般过去时:
She ducked her head to avoid the branch. = 她低下头避开了树枝。

现在完成时:
He has ducked many difficult questions in the interview. = 他在采访中躲避了许多棘手的问题。
They have ducked responsibility for the failure. = 他们逃避了失败的责任。


过去完成时:
She had ducked behind the curtain before he entered the room. = 他进房间之前，她已经躲到了窗帘后面。
The thief had ducked into an alleyway to escape the police. = 小偷躲进一条小巷逃脱了警察的追捕。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
