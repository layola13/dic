quasi-conscientiously: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ kɒnʃiˈɛnʃəsli/ | adj. |似是而非地认真地; 貌似尽责地 |

英文解析:  The word "quasi-conscientiously" describes actions or behavior that appear to be done with a sense of responsibility and moral awareness, but are actually lacking in genuine conscientiousness. It suggests a superficial or pretended effort to do what is right.  The prefix "quasi-" means "seemingly" or "apparently," while "conscientiously" means "in a careful and thorough manner, with a sense of moral obligation."

例句:

He completed the assignment quasi-conscientiously, just enough to avoid any serious consequences. = 他似是而非地认真地完成了作业，只是为了避免严重的后果。


She attended the meetings quasi-conscientiously, rarely contributing anything meaningful. = 她貌似尽责地参加了会议，却很少做出有意义的贡献。


近义词: superficially, perfunctorily, nominally, seemingly diligently


反义词: conscientiously, diligently, scrupulously, thoroughly


词性: adjective (形容词)
