napoleon:/nəˈpoʊliən/ | n. |拿破仑；(指)雄心勃勃的人|Napoleon was a French military and political leader who rose to prominence during the French Revolution and led numerous successful military campaigns across Europe.  He is considered one of history's greatest military commanders and his Napoleonic Code significantly influenced legal systems worldwide.  |例句：Napoleon Bonaparte's ambition led him to conquer much of Europe.  拿破仑·波拿巴的野心使他征服了大部分欧洲。

Napoleon's defeat at Waterloo marked the end of his reign. 拿破仑在滑铁卢战役中的失败标志着他统治的结束。


近义词：emperor, leader, conqueror | 反义词：defeated, conquered

词源:
The name "Napoleon" is derived from the Italian name "Napoleone," a variant of "Nabuchodonosor" (Nebuchadnezzar), a name associated with ancient Babylonian kings known for their power and ambition.  The name gained popularity in Italy during the Renaissance and was later adopted by Napoleon Bonaparte's family.
