shattering: /ˈʃætərɪŋ/ | adj. |令人震惊的；极度令人不安的；极具破坏性的 | Extremely shocking or upsetting; causing great destruction. |

英文解析:  The word "shattering" describes something that causes intense emotional distress or significant physical damage. It implies a sudden and complete destruction or disruption of something previously intact.  It suggests a sense of devastation and irretrievable loss.


例句:

The news of his death was shattering.  = 他去世的消息令人震惊。

The earthquake caused shattering damage to the city. = 地震给这座城市造成了巨大的破坏。


近义词: devastating, shocking, appalling, traumatic, crushing, heartbreaking

反义词: comforting, reassuring, uplifting, insignificant, minor


词性:形容词
