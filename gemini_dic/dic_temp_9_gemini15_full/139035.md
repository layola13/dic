oakums:/ˈoʊkəm/| n.| 填塞料；旧麻绳；|Loose strands of hemp or jute fiber, used for caulking seams in ships and other vessels to prevent leaks.  It's also used in other applications requiring a sealant.|例句：The shipwright used oakum to caulk the seams of the wooden hull.  造船工人用填塞料堵塞木船体的缝隙。

The sailors spent hours packing oakum into the cracks of the old ship. 水手们花了数小时将填塞料塞进旧船的裂缝里。|近义词：caulking, packing, sealant|反义词：N/A

词源:
From Middle English *okam, from Old English *acumba, from Latin *acu-m, related to *acus "needle".  The original sense referred to the coarse fibers that were pulled apart to make caulking material.  The connection to "needle" likely refers to the way the fibers were separated and pulled through seams.
