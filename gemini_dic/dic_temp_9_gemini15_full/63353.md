entombments:/ɪnˈtuːmbmənts/ | n. | 埋葬；葬礼；墓穴 | The act of placing a dead body in a tomb or grave; the state of being buried.  It can also refer to the tombs or graves themselves, collectively. | Example: The ancient entombments were discovered during the archaeological dig.  古代墓葬是在考古发掘中发现的。  The entombments in the valley were remarkably well-preserved. 山谷里的墓葬保存得非常好。|近义词：burials, interments, sepulchers |反义词：exhumations
