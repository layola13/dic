precomprehension: /ˌpriːkɒmprɪˈhɛnʃən/ | n. |预理解，预先理解|The process of activating prior knowledge and making predictions before reading or listening to a text.  It involves using what you already know to anticipate the content and meaning of the upcoming material.  This can significantly improve comprehension and retention.| Example:  The teacher encouraged precomprehension activities, such as brainstorming and previewing vocabulary, before students started reading the chapter.  老师鼓励学生在阅读章节之前进行预理解活动，例如集思广益和预习词汇。|近义词：anticipation, preparation, preview |反义词：misunderstanding, misinterpretation |名词
