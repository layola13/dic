epizootic:/ˌɛpɪzuˈɒtɪk/| adj.|动物传染病的；动物疫病的 | An epizootic is a disease that spreads rapidly through a population of animals.  It refers to a widespread outbreak of disease among animals within a particular region or area.  The term is often used in veterinary medicine and epidemiology. | Example: The epizootic rapidly decimated the local cattle population.  动物疫病迅速地消灭了当地牛群。 The recent epizootic of avian flu caused significant economic losses to poultry farmers.  最近的禽流感疫情给家禽养殖户造成了巨大的经济损失。|近义词：epidemic (among humans), pandemic (widespread epidemic)|反义词：endemic (constantly present in a population)
