post-Confucian: /ˌpoʊst kənˈfjuːʃən/ | adj. | 后儒家的；后孔子的 |  Post-Confucian refers to the philosophical and cultural trends and developments that emerged in East Asia after the classical Confucian period, often characterized by reinterpretations, critiques, and syntheses of Confucian thought with other philosophical traditions.  It encompasses a wide range of thinkers and movements, and isn't a single unified school of thought. |  The post-Confucian thinkers often engaged with Buddhism and Daoism, leading to syncretic philosophies. | 近义词: Neo-Confucian (but distinct in its emphasis on a later period and broader range of influences) | 反义词: Classical Confucian
