repurchases:/ˈriːpɜːrtʃəsɪz/| n. |回购；再购|The act of buying back something that has already been sold, often securities or company stock.  This can be done for various reasons, such as to reduce the number of outstanding shares, to increase share price, or to take advantage of a perceived undervaluation.|例句：The company announced a significant share repurchases program to boost its stock price. 公司宣布了一项重大的股票回购计划以提高股价。|例句：The investor made several repurchases of the undervalued stock.  投资者对被低估的股票进行了多次回购。|近义词：buyback, reacquisition|反义词：selling, disposal|名词
