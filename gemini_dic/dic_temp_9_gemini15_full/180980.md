reimplantations:/ˌriːɪmˈplænˈteɪʃənz/| n. |再植；再植术|The process of surgically replacing a removed body part, such as a tooth or organ, back into its original position.  This often involves complex procedures to ensure successful reattachment and functionality.|例句：The success rate of dental reimplantations is significantly higher now than it was in the past.  牙再植的成功率现在比过去高得多。

The hospital has a specialized unit for limb reimplantations. 这家医院有一个专门的肢体再植科室。|近义词：replantation, transplantation, grafting|反义词：explantation, removal
