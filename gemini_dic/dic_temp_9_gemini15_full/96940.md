inextirpable: /ˌɪnɪkˈstɜːrpəbl/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的；无法根除的；无法消灭的 | Incapable of being extirpated or eradicated; impossible to remove or destroy completely.  | The problem of poverty is almost inexpugnable in some areas.  一些地区的贫困问题几乎是无法根除的。|  intractable, ineradicable, ingrained | eradicable, removable  | 形容词

This inexpugnable belief affects his judgment. = 这种根深蒂固的信念影响了他的判断。

一般过去时:
The old habits were inexpugnable. = 老习惯是根深蒂固的。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:
By then, his reputation for dishonesty had become inexpugnable. = 到那时，他欺骗的名声已经根深蒂固。
It had become inexpugnable that he was guilty. = 他有罪的事实已经无法改变。


将来时:
His influence will be inexpugnable for years to come. = 他的影响在未来几年都将根深蒂固。
The damage to the environment will be inexpugnable for generations. = 对环境的破坏将在几代人中都无法根除。


虚拟语气:
If the problem were inexpugnable, we wouldn't even try to solve it. = 如果这个问题无法根除，我们甚至不会尝试解决它。
I wish this negative attitude were not so inexpugnable. = 我希望这种消极态度不会如此根深蒂固。


被动语态:

疑问句:
Is this feeling of hopelessness inexpugnable? = 这种绝望感是无法根除的吗？
Are the effects of climate change truly inexpugnable? = 气候变化的影响真的无法根除吗？


否定句:
The problem is not inexpugnable; it can be solved. = 这个问题并非无法根除；它是可以解决的。
The damage is not completely inexpugnable; some recovery is possible. = 损害并非完全无法弥补；一些恢复是可能的。

词源:
The word "inextirpable" is derived from the Latin prefix "in-" (not), "extirpare" (to root out, to eradicate), and the suffix "-able" (capable of being).  Thus, it literally means "not capable of being rooted out."
