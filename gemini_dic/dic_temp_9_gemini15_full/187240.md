robing:/ˈroʊbɪŋ/| n. |抢劫；掠夺；穿长袍的动作|The act of robbing or the state of being robbed; also, the act of putting on a robe.|例句：The robing of the bank resulted in a large-scale manhunt. 银行抢劫案导致了大规模的搜捕行动。|例句：The quiet robing of the monk before prayer was a peaceful sight.  僧侣祈祷前安静地穿上长袍，是一幅祥和的景象。|近义词：plundering, looting, pillaging, dressing|反义词：giving, donating


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He robs banks. = 他抢劫银行。
She is robbing the store. = 她正在抢劫商店。


一般过去时:
They robbed the museum last night. = 他们昨晚抢劫了博物馆。
He robbed me of my wallet. = 他抢走了我的钱包。


现在完成时:
The thief has robbed several houses. = 窃贼已经抢劫了几户人家。
They have been robbing cars for months. = 他们已经偷车好几个月了。


过去完成时:
They had robbed the train before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，他们已经抢劫了火车。
She had been robbed twice before. = 她以前被抢过两次。


将来时:
He will rob another store tomorrow. = 他明天将抢劫另一家商店。
They will have robbed all the houses by morning. = 到早上他们将把所有房子都抢劫一空。


虚拟语气:
If he robbed the bank, he would be in big trouble. = 如果他抢劫银行，他会有大麻烦。
I wish I hadn't robbed that store. = 我真希望我没有抢劫那家商店。


被动语态:
The bank was robbed yesterday. = 银行昨天被抢劫了。
The cars are being robbed every night. = 汽车每晚都被偷。


疑问句:
Did he rob the store? = 他抢劫了商店吗？
Was the museum robbed? = 博物馆被抢劫了吗？


否定句:
He didn't rob the bank. = 他没有抢劫银行。
She hasn't robbed anyone. = 她没有抢劫任何人。
