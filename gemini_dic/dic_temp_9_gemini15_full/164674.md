preexplosion: /ˌpriːɪkˈspləʊʒən/ | n. | 爆炸前，预爆 | The events leading up to the explosion, including the preexplosion warnings.  |  近义词：antecedent, prelude | 反义词： aftermath, consequence | 名词


英文解析:The period of time or events immediately preceding an explosion.  It often refers to the detectable signs or conditions that indicate an imminent explosion is likely to occur.

例句:

The investigators meticulously examined the preexplosion data to determine the cause of the blast.  调查人员仔细检查了爆炸前的资料以确定爆炸的原因。


The preexplosion sounds were described as a series of increasingly loud pops. 爆炸前的声响被描述为一系列越来越大的砰砰声。
