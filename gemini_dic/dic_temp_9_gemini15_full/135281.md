nonpartial: /ˌnɒnˈpɑːrʃəl/ | adj. | 公正的，无偏见的 | Not partial; unbiased; fair.  It implies a lack of favoritism or prejudice in judgment or treatment. | 例句：The judge was known for his nonpartial rulings.  法官以其公正的裁决而闻名。

例句：The jury aimed for a nonpartial verdict, considering all the evidence impartially. 陪审团力求做出公正的裁决，公正地考虑所有证据。


近义词：impartial, unbiased, objective, fair, equitable, disinterested


反义词：biased, prejudiced, partial, unfair, one-sided


词源：The word is a combination of "non-" (meaning "not") and "partial" (meaning "biased" or "showing favoritism").  The root of "partial" traces back to the Latin word "pars," meaning "part."  A partial person favors one part over another, while a nonpartial person treats all parts equally.
