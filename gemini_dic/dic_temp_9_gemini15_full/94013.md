impalpably:/ɪmˈpælpəbli/ | adj. | 难以察觉地；无法触及地 | Incapable of being felt; imperceptible.  The change was so gradual and impalpable that it went unnoticed for years.  | 近义词：imperceptibly, subtly, undetectably | 反义词：palpably, noticeably, perceptibly


词源:

* **im-**:  prefix indicating negation or reversal (similar to "un-").
* **palp-**: from Latin "palpare," meaning "to touch" or "to feel."
* **-able**: suffix meaning "capable of being."
* **-ly**: adverbial suffix.


Therefore, "impalpably" literally means "not capable of being felt."


例句：

The shift in the political landscape was impalpable at first, only becoming apparent months later. = 政治格局的转变起初难以察觉，几个月后才变得明显。

The air hung heavy with an impalpable tension. = 空气中弥漫着一种难以察觉的紧张气氛。


USAGE->

This word is an adjective, so it doesn't have verb conjugations.  It modifies nouns and describes their state.  The grammatical functions are demonstrated in the examples above.  It can be used in various sentence structures:

* **Declarative sentences:** The change was impalpable.
* **Interrogative sentences:** Was the shift impalpable?
* **Exclamatory sentences:** How impalpable the transition was!


PHRASE->

There are no common idiomatic phrases using "impalpably." Its usage is typically straightforward and descriptive within sentences.
