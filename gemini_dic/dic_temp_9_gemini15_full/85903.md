headhunting:/ˈhedˌhʌntɪŋ/ | n. |猎头；挖角 |The act of actively seeking out and recruiting skilled employees from other companies.  This often involves approaching individuals directly, even if they are not actively looking for a new job. |Example:  The company engaged in aggressive headhunting to fill its senior management positions. 这家公司积极进行猎头活动以填补其高级管理职位。|近义词：executive search, talent acquisition |反义词：in-house recruitment

一般现在时:
Headhunting is a common practice in the tech industry. = 猎头在科技行业是一种常见的做法。
They headhunt top talent from competitor firms. = 他们从竞争对手公司挖角顶尖人才。


一般过去时:
The firm headhunted several key engineers last year. = 该公司去年挖走了几名关键工程师。
She was headhunted by a major investment bank. = 她被一家大型投资银行挖角。


现在完成时:
They have headhunted extensively for this role. = 他们为这个职位进行了广泛的猎头活动。
We have headhunted several candidates, but none are suitable. = 我们已经挖角了好几位候选人，但都没有合适的。


过去完成时:
Before joining our company, he had been headhunted multiple times. = 加入我们公司之前，他已经多次被猎头公司挖角。
The company had already headhunted the candidate before the interview. = 在面试之前，公司已经对这位候选人进行了猎头活动。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
