pre-disclosure: /ˌpriːdɪˈskloʊʒər/ | n. | 预先披露；提前公开 | The act of revealing information before it is officially or publicly released.  It often involves sharing sensitive or confidential information in advance, typically for review, feedback, or to prepare stakeholders.  |  例句：The pre-disclosure of the merger plans caused a significant stock market fluctuation. 预先披露的并购计划导致股市大幅波动。


例句：The company engaged in pre-disclosure of its financial results to its major investors. 公司向主要投资者预先披露了财务业绩。


近义词：advance notice, prior notification, early release, heads-up


反义词：secrecy, confidentiality, concealment


词性: noun
