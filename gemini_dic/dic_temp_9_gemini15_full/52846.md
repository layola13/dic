dimensionalizing: /ˌdaɪmɛnʃənəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |赋予维度；使具有维度 | To give something dimensions; to express something in terms of dimensions.  The process involves assigning dimensions (length, width, height, etc.) to a quantity or concept that may not inherently possess them. This is often done in physics and engineering to make calculations and analyses easier.  | Example: We need to dimensionalize the model to accurately simulate the real-world system. 我们需要赋予模型维度以便准确模拟现实世界系统。|近义词：scaling, quantifying|反义词：de-dimensionalizing (not a common word)
