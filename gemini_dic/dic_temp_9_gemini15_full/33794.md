circumduce:/ˈsɜːrkəmˌdjuːs/ | vt. | 围绕；环绕；诱骗；欺骗 | To lead around; to encompass; to deceive or mislead.  The word often implies a subtle or indirect form of deception. | 例句：The magician circumduced the audience with his illusions. 魔术师用他的幻觉迷惑了观众。

The government attempted to circumduce the public into accepting the new policy. 政府试图诱导公众接受这项新政策。


近义词：encircle, surround, mislead, deceive, dupe | 反义词：clarify, enlighten, inform honestly


词源:
From Latin *circumducere, from circum "around" + ducere "to lead".  The meaning evolved from the literal sense of "leading around" to the figurative sense of "misleading" or "deceptively guiding".
