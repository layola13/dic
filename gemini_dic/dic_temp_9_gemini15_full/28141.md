capitulary:/kəˈpɪtʃələri/ | n. | 章程；教规；法规汇编 | A capitulary is a collection of laws or decrees, especially one issued by a medieval ruler or ecclesiastical council.  It often refers to a chapter or section of a larger legal document. | Example: The Frankish capitularies covered a wide range of topics, from taxation to church administration.  法兰克的教规涵盖了从税收到教会管理的广泛主题。|近义词：decree, edict, ordinance, statute |反义词：N/A
