luddite:/ˈlʌdaɪt/| n. adj.|卢德分子；反对技术进步者； | A Luddite is a person who is opposed to new technology or industrial methods.  The term originates from the Luddite movement in 19th-century England, where textile workers destroyed machinery in protest against industrialization and its impact on their jobs.  |例句：He's a bit of a Luddite; he refuses to use a computer. 他有点像卢德分子；他拒绝使用电脑。


The Luddites were a group of English textile workers who protested against the introduction of new machinery in the early 19th century, believing it would lead to job losses. |例句：Some people criticize the rapid adoption of AI, labeling them as modern-day Luddites. 一些人批评人工智能的快速采用，称他们为现代的卢德分子。|近义词：reactionary, traditionalist, conservative |反义词：innovator, progressive, futurist |名词，形容词

一般过去时:
The workers were Luddites who destroyed the machines. = 工人们是摧毁机器的卢德分子。
He was a Luddite in his approach to technology. = 他在对待技术方面像个卢德分子。


现在完成时:
They have been Luddites for many years. = 他们多年来一直是卢德分子。
She has become a Luddite since the factory closed. = 自从工厂倒闭后，她变成了一个卢德分子。


过去完成时:
The movement had been largely suppressed by the time the new law was passed. = 新法律通过时，这场运动已被很大程度上压制了。
Before the industrial revolution, they had not been considered Luddites. = 在工业革命之前，他们不被认为是卢德分子。


将来时:

They will become Luddites if their jobs are threatened by automation. = 如果他们的工作受到自动化威胁，他们将会变成卢德分子。
