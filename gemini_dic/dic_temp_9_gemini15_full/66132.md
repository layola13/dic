exception:/ɪkˈsɛpʃən/ | n. |例外，异常；异议 | A person or thing that is excluded from a general statement or does not conform to a general rule.  An unusual or unexpected event, especially one that causes difficulty. |例句：He is an exception to the rule. 他是个例外。

There was an exception to the policy. 这项政策有例外。


近义词：abnormality, anomaly, deviation, irregularity | 反义词：rule, norm, regularity


词源：From Old French excepcion, from Latin exceptio, from excipere "to take out, except," from ex- "out" + capere "to take."
