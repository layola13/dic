ruffles:/ˈrʌfəlz/ | n. v. |褶边；弄皱；激怒 |

英文解析:
Noun:  A ruffle is a gathered or pleated strip of fabric used as a trimming.  It can also refer to a state of being disordered or agitated.

Verb: To ruffle means to make something wrinkled or uneven; to disturb the smoothness of something; or to upset or irritate someone.


例句:

Noun:
The dress had pretty lace ruffles at the sleeves. = 这件裙子袖子上装饰着漂亮的蕾丝褶边。

The wind ruffled her hair. = 风弄乱了她的头发。


Verb:
She ruffled the pages of the book, searching for the passage. = 她翻动书页，寻找那段话。

His criticism ruffled her feathers. = 他的批评让她恼火。


近义词:
Noun: frills, flounces, gathers, pleats (for fabric); commotion, disturbance (for state)
Verb: wrinkle, crease, crinkle, agitate, irritate, annoy


反义词:
Noun: smoothness, calm, order
Verb: smooth, calm, soothe


词性:
Noun, Verb
