rhapsode:/ˈræpsəʊd/| n. |吟诵者；（古代希腊的）抒情诗人；热情洋溢的演说家 | A rhapsode was a professional performer in ancient Greece who recited epic poems, often improvising and adding their own embellishments.  They were highly skilled in the art of oral performance and could captivate audiences with their dramatic delivery.  The term often implies a passionate and eloquent speaker or writer. | Example: The poet, a true rhapsode, delivered his work with such feeling that the audience was moved to tears.  |近义词：reciter, orator, performer, poet | 反义词：mute, silent, unemotional
