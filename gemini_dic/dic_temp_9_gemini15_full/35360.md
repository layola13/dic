co-inhabitant:/ˌkoʊˈɪnˌhæbɪtənt/| n. |同居者，同住者 |A person or animal that lives in the same place or area as another.  |例句：The rabbits and squirrels are co-inhabitants of the forest. 兔子与松鼠是森林的同居者。
例句：The two families were co-inhabitants of the same apartment building. 这两家人是同一栋公寓楼的同住者。|近义词：roommate, housemate, fellow occupant |反义词：stranger, outsider |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rabbits and squirrels are co-inhabitants of the forest. = 兔子与松鼠是森林的同居者。
These two families are co-inhabitants of the same apartment building. = 这两家人是同一栋公寓楼的同住者。


一般过去时:
We were co-inhabitants of that small apartment for two years. = 我们在那间小公寓里同住了两年。
The birds and insects were co-inhabitants of the old tree. = 鸟类和昆虫是那棵老树的同住者。


现在完成时:
They have been co-inhabitants of this city for over a decade. = 他们在这个城市共同生活了十多年。
We have been co-inhabitants of this office since the company moved. = 自从公司搬迁以来，我们一直是这个办公室的同住者。


过去完成时:
Before the fire, they had been co-inhabitants of the same house. = 在火灾发生之前，他们曾是同一栋房子的同住者。
Before the new tenant arrived, they had been co-inhabitants of a small apartment. = 在新租户到来之前，他们曾是同一间小公寓的同住者。


将来时:
We will be co-inhabitants of this new house next month. = 我们下个月将成为这栋新房子的同住者。
They will be co-inhabitants of the same research lab for the next year. = 他们将在未来一年成为同一个研究实验室的同住者。


虚拟语气:
If they were co-inhabitants, they would share the rent. = 如果他们是同住者，他们就会分摊房租。
If we had been co-inhabitants, we would have shared the responsibilities. = 如果我们是同住者，我们就会分担责任。


被动语态:
The house was inhabited by co-inhabitants from different backgrounds. = 这所房子住着来自不同背景的同住者。
The apartment was shared by co-inhabitants who respected each other's privacy. = 这间公寓由互相尊重隐私的同住者共同使用。


疑问句:
Are they co-inhabitants of the same building? = 他们是同一栋楼的同住者吗？
Were they co-inhabitants before the argument? = 他们吵架之前是同住者吗？


否定句:
They are not co-inhabitants; they just happen to live in the same street. = 他们不是同住者；他们只是碰巧住在同一条街上。
We were not co-inhabitants; we only shared a common area. = 我们不是同住者；我们只共用一个公共区域。
