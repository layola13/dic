retool:/ˌriːˈtuːl/| vt.|重新装备；改造；改组|To equip (a factory, etc.) with new machinery or tools, or (a person) with new skills.  To change or modify something, especially a system or process, in order to improve it or make it more efficient.|例句：The factory is being retooled to produce a new line of products. 工厂正在重新装备以生产新的产品线。

例句：The company is retooling its marketing strategy to attract younger customers.  公司正在调整其营销策略以吸引年轻客户。


近义词：refit, revamp, renovate, modernize|反义词：deteriorate, decay


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company retools its production line every year. = 公司每年都会重新调整生产线。
They retool their skills to meet the demands of the market. = 他们重新学习技能以满足市场的需求。


一般过去时:
The factory retooled its machinery last month. = 工厂上个月重新装备了机器。
She retooled her resume to apply for a new job. = 她修改了简历以申请一份新工作。


现在完成时:
They have retooled their entire manufacturing process. = 他们已经彻底改造了整个制造流程。
The company has retooled its marketing team. = 公司已经改组了营销团队。


过去完成时:
They had retooled the equipment before the inspection. = 他们在检查之前已经重新装备了设备。
She had retooled her presentation before the conference. = 她在会议之前已经修改了她的演示文稿。


将来时:
The plant will retool its operations next quarter. = 该工厂将在下个季度调整其运营。
He will retool his approach to the problem. = 他将调整解决这个问题的方法。


虚拟语气:
If they retooled their processes, they would be more efficient. = 如果他们改造了流程，他们将会更高效。
I wish I had retooled my skills earlier. = 我希望我早点重新学习技能。


被动语态:

The factory was retooled by a team of engineers. = 该工厂由一个工程师团队进行了重新装备。

否定句:
