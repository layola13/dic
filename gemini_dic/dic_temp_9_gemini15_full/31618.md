chasuble:/ˈtʃæzəbl/| n. |祭衣|A chasuble is a liturgical vestment worn by a priest during the celebration of Mass in the Catholic Church and some other Christian denominations.  It is a sleeveless outer garment, usually richly decorated, that falls to the knees or below.  |The priest wore a beautiful chasuble embroidered with biblical scenes.  祭司穿着一件绣有圣经场景的漂亮祭衣。|  vestment, cope |  --- | n.
