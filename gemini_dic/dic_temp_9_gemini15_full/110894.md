lip-sync: /ˈlɪpˌsɪŋk/ | v. | 对口型，假唱 | To move one's lips in synchronization with a recorded voice or soundtrack.  This is often done in music videos, films, or live performances where the performer's vocals are pre-recorded. | 例句：The singer lip-synced to the backing track during the concert.  歌手在演唱会期间对口型演唱伴奏。

The actress lip-synced perfectly to the character's lines in the movie.  女演员在电影中完美地对上了角色的台词。


近义词：mime (to mimic the actions of speaking without actually speaking),  mouth the words


反义词：sing live (to perform vocals without pre-recording)


词性: verb

词源:
The word "lip-sync" is a compound word formed from "lip" and "sync" (short for synchronize).  It emerged in the mid-20th century, reflecting the increasing use of pre-recorded audio in media.
