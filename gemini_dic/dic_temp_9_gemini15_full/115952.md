married:/ˈmærid/| adj.|已婚的，结婚的|Being united in marriage; joined in matrimony.|例句：They have been married for twenty years. 他们结婚二十年了。

例句：She is married to a doctor. 她嫁给了一位医生。


近义词：wedded, espoused| 反义词：single, unmarried


词源:
The word "married" comes from the Old English word "maried," which itself is derived from the verb "marien," meaning "to marry."  This verb ultimately traces back to the Latin word "maritare," meaning "to bestow in marriage," which is related to the Latin word "maritus," meaning "husband."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are married. = 他们结婚了。
He is happily married. = 他过着幸福的婚姻生活。


一般过去时:
They got married last year. = 他们去年结婚了。
She was married when she was 25. = 她25岁时结婚了。


现在完成时:
They have been married for five years. = 他们结婚五年了。
She has been married twice. = 她结过两次婚。


过去完成时:
They had been married for ten years when they divorced. = 他们离婚时已经结婚十年了。
She had already been married once before she met him. = 在她遇到他之前已经结过一次婚了。


将来时:
They will be married next month. = 他们下个月结婚。
We will get married in the spring. = 我们将在春天结婚。


虚拟语气:
If I were married, I would have children. = 如果我结婚了，我会生孩子。
I wish I were married. = 我希望我已经结婚了。


被动语态:
(The word "married" is rarely used in the passive voice.  The action of getting married is typically expressed actively.)


疑问句:
Are they married? = 他们结婚了吗？
Is she married? = 她结婚了吗？


否定句:
They are not married. = 他们没有结婚。
He is not married yet. = 他还没有结婚。
