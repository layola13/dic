preppiest: /ˈprɛpiːɪst/ | adj. | 最时髦的，最讲究的，最整洁的 | Most stylish and neat; conforming to the style of preppy fashion.  |例句：Her preppiest outfit was a navy blazer, crisp white shirt, and pleated khakis. 她最时髦的装扮是一件海军蓝外套、一件干净的白色衬衫和褶皱卡其裤。

例句：He always looks preppiest in his button-down shirts and loafers. 他穿着纽扣衬衫和便鞋时总是显得最讲究。


近义词：stylish, chic, neat, smart, dapper | 反义词：sloppy, unkempt, disheveled, scruffy


词源:  The word "preppiest" is the superlative form of "preppy,"  which is a shortened form of "preparatory school," referring to the style of dress and demeanor associated with students from elite private schools in the United States, particularly those in the Northeastern United States. The style emphasizes neatness, classic clothing items (such as button-down shirts, khakis, and blazers), and a generally well-put-together appearance.

用法说明:

"Preppiest" is an adjective, and it is used to describe the person, thing, or style that is the most preppy.  It implies a high degree of conformity to the preppy style.  It's often used in a playful or slightly ironic way, acknowledging the sometimes stereotypical nature of the preppy aesthetic.  The word carries a connotation of affluence and social status, though this is not always the case.
