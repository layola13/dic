firkins:/ˈfɜːrkɪnz/| n. |小桶；四加仑桶|A firkin is an old English unit of liquid measure, typically equivalent to nine gallons (approximately 41 liters).  It was commonly used for beer and butter. The word is now largely archaic, used mainly in historical contexts or for a quaint effect.|The brewer used firkins to store his finest ale. = 酿酒师用小桶储存他最好的啤酒。
