monetaristic: /ˌmʌnɪˈtærɪstɪk/ | adj. |货币主义的；重货币政策的|Relating to or characteristic of monetarism, an economic theory that emphasizes the role of money supply in influencing economic activity.|The monetaristic policies implemented by the government led to a decrease in inflation. 政府实施的货币主义政策导致通货膨胀下降。|近义词：monetary, fiscal (in contrast)|反义词：Keynesian


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The monetaristic approach focuses on controlling the money supply. = 货币主义方法侧重于控制货币供应量。
Monetarists believe that inflation is primarily caused by excessive money growth. = 货币主义者认为通货膨胀主要是由过度的货币增长引起的。

一般过去时:
The government pursued monetaristic policies in the 1980s. = 政府在20世纪80年代推行了货币主义政策。
Economists debated the effectiveness of monetaristic measures during the crisis. = 经济学家在危机期间争论了货币主义措施的有效性。

现在完成时:
Monetarism has influenced macroeconomic policy for decades. = 货币主义几十年来一直影响着宏观经济政策。
Central banks have adopted some monetaristic principles in their operations. = 中央银行在其运作中采用了一些货币主义原则。

过去完成时:
Before the crisis, many economists had dismissed monetaristic theories. = 在危机之前，许多经济学家都摒弃了货币主义理论。
They had already implemented monetaristic policies before the inflation surged. = 在通货膨胀飙升之前，他们已经实施了货币主义政策。

将来时:
The central bank will likely continue to use monetaristic tools to manage inflation. = 中央银行可能会继续使用货币主义工具来管理通货膨胀。
Economists predict that monetaristic policies will remain influential in the future. = 经济学家预测，货币主义政策在未来仍将具有影响力。

虚拟语气:
