put:/pʊt/ | vt. |放置，摆放；表达；使处于某种状态 | to place someone or something in a particular position |He put the book on the table. 他把书放在桌子上。|  She put her coat on. 她穿上了外套。| place, set, lay | remove, take away | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I put the milk in the fridge. = 我把牛奶放进冰箱。
He puts his shoes on before leaving. = 他出门前穿上鞋子。

一般过去时:
She put the flowers in a vase. = 她把花插进花瓶里。
They put the furniture in the new house. = 他们把家具搬进了新房子。

现在完成时:
I have put the dishes away. = 我已经把盘子收起来了。
She has put her heart into her work. = 她全身心地投入到工作中。

过去完成时:
He had put the car in the garage before it rained. = 下雨前他已经把车放进了车库。
They had put the children to bed before going out. = 他们出门前已经哄孩子们睡觉了。

将来时:
I will put the cake on the table later. = 我晚点会把蛋糕放在桌子上。
She will put the matter to a vote. = 她将把此事付诸表决。


虚拟语气:
If I had put more effort, I would have passed the exam. = 如果我更加努力，我就通过考试了。
I wish I had put my money in the bank. = 我真希望我把钱存进了银行。

被动语态:
The books were put on the shelf. = 书被放在书架上了。
The matter will be put to a vote. = 这件事将被付诸表决。

疑问句:
Where did you put your keys? = 你把钥匙放在哪里了？
Where shall we put the table? = 我们应该把桌子放在哪里？

否定句:
Don't put your feet on the table. = 不要把脚放在桌子上。
He didn't put the letter in the mailbox. = 他没有把信投入邮箱。


PHRASE->
put on: 穿上，上演
put off: 推迟，延期
put up with: 忍受，容忍
put down: 放下，记下
put forward: 提出
