pickpocket: /ˈpɪkˌpɒkɪt/ | n. |扒手；窃贼 |A pickpocket is a thief who steals from people's pockets, typically in crowded places.  Pickpockets are skilled at distracting their victims and moving quickly to take their valuables. |例句：The pickpocket deftly snatched the woman's wallet from her purse.  这个扒手熟练地从女人的钱包里偷走了她的钱包。

The police arrested several pickpockets at the festival.  警方在节日里逮捕了几名扒手。|近义词：thief, robber, pilferer |反义词：police officer, security guard

WORD ORIGIN->
The word "pickpocket" is a compound word, combining "pick" (meaning to steal something quickly and cleverly) and "pocket" (the place from which the theft is committed).  It arose in the 16th century.
