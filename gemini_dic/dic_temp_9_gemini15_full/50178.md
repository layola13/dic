deontology:/ˌdiːɒnˈtɒlədʒi/| n. |道义论，义务论|The study of duty and obligation; a moral theory that emphasizes the importance of moral rules and duties.  Deontology focuses on the inherent rightness or wrongness of actions, regardless of their consequences.|Example:  Kantian deontology emphasizes the importance of acting according to universal moral principles.  康德的道义论强调根据普遍道德原则行动的重要性。|近义词：moral philosophy, ethics|反义词：consequentialism
