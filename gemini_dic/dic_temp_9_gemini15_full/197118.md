shamrocks:/ˈʃæmˌrɒks/| n.|三叶草|A shamrock is a young sprig of clover, typically with three leaflets, that is considered a national symbol of Ireland.|例句：The Irish celebrate St. Patrick's Day with parades featuring shamrocks.|近义词：clover, trefoil|反义词:None.

词源:
The word "shamrock" comes from the Irish word "seamróg," which is a diminutive of "seamróg," meaning "little clover".  The word's usage became widespread after its association with St. Patrick's use of the three-leaf clover to illustrate the Holy Trinity.
