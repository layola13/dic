prelabial: /ˌpriːˈleɪbiəl/ | adj. |唇前的，唇之前的|  Relating to or situated in front of the lip.  This term is primarily used in phonetics and anatomy to describe sounds or structures located before the lips. For example, in phonetics, a prelabial sound is produced with the tongue approaching the lips from the front. In anatomy, prelabial refers to the area before the lip, such as the skin or tissues surrounding the lips.| The prelabial muscles help shape the mouth for speech.  唇前的肌肉有助于塑造说话的嘴型。|近义词:  antelabial (less common)|反义词: postlabial
