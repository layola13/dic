polarize:/ˈpoʊləraɪz/| /ˈpəʊləraɪz/| vt. n.|使极化；使两极分化；使偏振|To cause (something) to become polarized.  To divide into sharply opposing factions. To cause (light or other radiation) to vibrate in a single plane.|The political debate polarized the nation, dividing people into two opposing camps.  政治辩论使全国两极分化，将人们分成两个对立的阵营。|The sunglasses polarized the light, reducing glare. 这副太阳镜使光线偏振，减少了眩光。|unify, unite|moderate, unify|

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

This issue has polarized the electorate. = 这个问题使选民两极分化。


过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If the issue were not so divisive, it wouldn't polarize the public. = 如果这个问题没有那么具有争议性，它就不会使公众两极分化。
If the media hadn't interfered, the situation wouldn't have polarized so quickly. = 如果媒体没有干预，情况就不会这么快就两极分化。


被动语态:
