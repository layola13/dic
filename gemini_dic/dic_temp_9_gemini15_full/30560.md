/sɜːr/| n.|先生；阁下|A title of respect used to address a man.|Example: Yes, sir. 是的，先生。|近义词：mister, gentleman, |反义词:madam, miss.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The waiter serves the sir a cup of coffee. = 服务员给先生端了一杯咖啡。
Sir, would you like some more tea? = 先生，您还要茶吗？


一般过去时:
The manager called him "sir" respectfully. = 经理恭敬地称呼他为“先生”。
He addressed the old man as "sir". = 他尊称那位老人为“先生”。


现在完成时:
He has always been addressed as "sir" by his staff. = 他的员工一直尊称他为“先生”。
They have shown him the utmost respect, always calling him "sir". = 他们对他表示了最大的尊重，总是称呼他为“先生”。


过去完成时:
The children had already called the teacher "sir" before the class started. = 课前孩子们就已经称呼老师为“先生”了。
Before he left, he had thanked the guard, calling him "sir". = 在他离开之前，他已经感谢了保安，并称他为“先生”。


将来时:
He will be addressed as "sir" throughout the meeting. = 在整个会议期间，他都将被称为“先生”。
The students will call their professor "sir" with respect. = 学生们将尊敬地称呼他们的教授为“先生”。


虚拟语气:
If he were a sir, he would have more privileges. = 如果他是一位先生，他将拥有更多特权。
I wish I had addressed him as "sir" earlier. = 我真希望我早点称他为“先生”。


被动语态:
He was addressed as "sir" by all the guests. = 所有客人都尊称他为“先生”。
The title "sir" was used respectfully in the letter. = 在信中尊敬地使用了“先生”这个称呼。


疑问句:
Are you a sir or a madam? = 你是先生还是女士？
Excuse me, sir, could you help me? = 对不起，先生，您能帮我一下吗？


否定句:
He is not a sir, he is just a commoner. = 他不是先生，他只是一个普通人。
I didn't mean to offend you, sir. = 先生，我不是有意冒犯您的。
