prespeculating: /ˌpriːˈspɛkjuleɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 预先推测；事先猜测 |  Engaging in speculation before having all the necessary information or evidence.  It implies making assumptions or predictions based on incomplete data, often leading to potential inaccuracies. | 例句：They were prespeculating about the outcome of the election before the polls even closed. = 投票站甚至还没关闭，他们就开始预先推测选举结果了。  |  近义词：anticipating, forecasting, predicting, conjecturing | 反义词： verifying, confirming, observing
