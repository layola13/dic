nontautologically:/ˌnɒntɔːtɒˈlɒdʒɪkli/ | adv. | 非同义反复地；非循环论证地 |  Not in a tautological manner; not relying on a tautology.  It describes a statement or argument that is not self-evidently true or circular in reasoning.  It implies a genuine and independent support for the conclusion rather than simply restating the premise. | Example:  The argument was presented nontautologically, with evidence drawn from various independent sources.  这个论点并非循环论证，而是从不同的独立来源收集证据提出的。|近义词：logically, soundly, validly | 反义词：tautologically, circularly
