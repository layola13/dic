indulge:/ɪnˈdʌldʒ/ | vt. | 沉溺；纵容；满足；迁就 | to allow oneself to enjoy a particular pleasure, especially one that is considered harmful or undesirable | He indulged in a long hot bath. 他享受了一个长时间的热水澡。 |近义词：pamper, gratify, spoil | 反义词：deny, restrain, resist | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I indulge myself with a cup of coffee every morning. = 我每天早上都用一杯咖啡来犒劳自己。
They indulge in gossip every afternoon. = 他们每天下午都沉溺于八卦。


一般过去时:
She indulged her sweet tooth with a chocolate cake. = 她用一块巧克力蛋糕来满足她对甜食的渴望。
He indulged in excessive drinking last night. = 他昨晚酗酒。


现在完成时:
I have indulged in too many sweets lately. = 最近我吃太多甜食了。
She has indulged her children too much. = 她太惯着她的孩子们了。


过去完成时:
He had indulged in gambling before he lost all his money. = 他沉迷于赌博，然后输掉了所有的钱。
They had indulged in a lavish meal before the ceremony. = 仪式前他们享用了一顿丰盛的晚餐。


将来时:
I will indulge in a spa day next weekend. = 我下周末会去享受水疗日。
They will indulge their passion for travel this year. = 他们今年将会放纵他们对旅行的热情。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would indulge in a long novel. = 如果我有更多时间，我会沉迷于阅读长篇小说。
I wish I could indulge in a vacation right now. = 我真希望现在能去度假。


被动语态:
His desires were indulged by his parents. = 他的愿望被他的父母满足了。
Her whims were often indulged. = 她的任性经常被迁就。


疑问句:
Do you often indulge in unhealthy snacks? = 你经常吃不健康的零食吗？
Does he indulge in excessive spending? = 他经常过度消费吗？


否定句:
I don't indulge in smoking. = 我不抽烟。
She doesn't indulge her children's every whim. = 她不会满足她孩子们每一个突发奇想。


PHRASE->
indulge in = 沉溺于
indulge oneself = 放纵自己
indulge a whim = 满足一时兴起
indulge one's appetite = 满足食欲
indulge one's passion = 满足热情
