guaiacol:/ˈɡwaɪəˌkɒl/ | n. |愈创木酚|A colorless to yellowish liquid with a smoky, medicinal odor, found in various plant materials, especially creosote and beechwood tar. It's used in the production of vanillin and other flavorings, as well as in some pharmaceuticals. |Example: Guaiacol is a component of creosote, a wood preservative. 愈创木酚是木防腐剂——杂酚油的一种成分。 |近义词: methylcatechol, 2-methoxyphenol |反义词:  (no direct antonym)|
