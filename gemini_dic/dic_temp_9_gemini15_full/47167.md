dawn-on:/dɔːn ˈɒn/| phrasal verb| 逐渐明白；开始理解| To dawn on someone means that someone gradually begins to understand or realize something.  It implies a slow process of understanding, not an immediate grasp.| It finally dawned on me that he was lying. |近义词：occur to, strike, come to light|反义词：be obvious, be clear


USAGE->
一般现在时:
It dawns on me that I've made a mistake. = 我逐渐明白我犯了一个错误。
The truth is slowly dawning on her. = 真相正在慢慢地让她明白。


一般过去时:
It dawned on him that he was lost. = 他逐渐意识到自己迷路了。
The implications of the decision dawned on them later. = 他们后来才逐渐明白这个决定的含义。


现在完成时:
It has dawned on me that I need to change my approach. = 我已经逐渐意识到我需要改变我的方法。
It has finally dawned on them that their plan won't work. = 他们终于意识到他们的计划行不通了。


过去完成时:
It had dawned on her that she shouldn't have trusted him. = 她已经意识到她不应该相信他了。
By then, it had dawned on everyone that the situation was serious. = 到那时，每个人都意识到情况严重了。


将来时:
It will dawn on him eventually that he needs help. = 他最终会意识到自己需要帮助。
I think it will soon dawn on them that they are in danger. = 我认为他们很快就会意识到自己处于危险之中。


虚拟语气:
If only it had dawned on me sooner! = 要是我早点意识到就好了！
I wish it had dawned on me earlier that he was dishonest. = 我真希望我早点意识到他不诚实。


被动语态:
(This phrasal verb is rarely used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
When did it dawn on you that something was wrong? = 你是什么时候意识到有什么不对劲的？
Has it dawned on you yet that we need to act quickly? = 你已经意识到我们需要迅速行动了吗？


否定句:
It didn't dawn on him until much later. = 直到很久以后他才意识到。
It hadn't dawned on me that he was so talented. = 我之前没有意识到他如此有天赋。


PHRASE->
dawn on someone that... = 某人逐渐意识到……
dawn on someone gradually = 某人逐渐明白
dawn on someone slowly = 某人慢慢明白
dawn on someone suddenly = 某人突然明白
dawn on someone eventually = 某人最终明白
