hazards:/ˈhæzərdz/| n.|危险，危害|Something that can cause harm or damage.|The construction site is full of hazards, including exposed wires and heavy machinery. 建筑工地到处都是危险，包括裸露的电线和重型机械。|dangers, risks, perils|safety, security|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
There are many hazards associated with this job. = 这份工作有很多危险。
The company assesses hazards regularly. = 公司定期评估危险。


一般过去时:
The workers faced significant hazards during the storm. = 暴风雨期间，工人们面临着巨大的危险。
The report highlighted the hazards of smoking. = 报告强调了吸烟的危害。


现在完成时:
We have identified several hazards in the factory. = 我们已经在工厂里发现了几处危险。
The company has mitigated many of the hazards. = 公司已经减轻了许多危险。


过去完成时:
They had underestimated the hazards of the mountain climb. = 他们低估了登山的危险。
The engineers had failed to account for the hazards of the terrain. = 工程师们没有考虑到地形的危险。


将来时:
The project will present many unforeseen hazards. = 该项目将带来许多不可预见的危险。
We will assess the hazards before starting work. = 我们将在开始工作前评估危险。


虚拟语气:
If we had known about the hazards, we wouldn't have gone. = 如果我们知道这些危险，我们就不会去了。
It's important to act as if all hazards are present. = 将所有危险都视为存在是很重要的。


被动语态:
The hazards were identified during the safety inspection. = 在安全检查中发现了这些危险。
The risks and hazards are being carefully managed. = 风险和危险正在得到妥善管理。


疑问句:
What are the main hazards of this activity? = 这项活动的主要危险是什么？
Are there any potential hazards we should be aware of? = 我们应该注意哪些潜在的危险？


否定句:
There were no significant hazards reported. = 没有报告重大的危险。
The area is relatively free from hazards. = 该地区相对没有危险。
