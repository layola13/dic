callousing: /ˈkɔːləsɪŋ/ | v. | 起老茧；使…变得冷漠 | The process of developing calluses; becoming hardened or insensitive. | Example:  Constant friction from the guitar caused callousing on his fingertips. = 吉他不断摩擦导致他的指尖起了老茧。  The years of hardship calloused her heart. =  多年的苦难使她的心变得冷漠。 | 近义词: hardening, toughening, desensitizing | 反义词: softening, sensitizing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is callousing his hands by working in the garden. = 他在花园里干活，手磨出了茧子。
The constant criticism is callousing her to the opinions of others. =  持续不断的批评让她对别人的意见变得冷漠。

一般过去时:
Years of hard labor calloused his hands. =  多年的辛劳使他的双手长满了老茧。
The repeated rejections calloused his spirit. =  反复的拒绝使他的精神变得麻木。

现在完成时:
She has calloused her hands from years of playing the violin. = 她拉了多年的琴，手磨出了茧子。
He has calloused himself to the suffering of others. = 他对别人的痛苦变得麻木不仁。

过去完成时:
By the time the marathon ended, he had calloused his feet. = 马拉松结束时，他的脚已经磨出了厚厚的茧子。
She had calloused her heart to the point where she couldn't feel empathy. = 她已经变得冷漠无情，无法产生同理心。

将来时:

Continuous exposure to negativity will callouse her soul. = 持续接触负面情绪会使她的灵魂变得麻木。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
