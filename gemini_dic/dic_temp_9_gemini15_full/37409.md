commerciality: /ˌkəːməˈʃæliti/ | n. | 商业性；商业气息；赢利性 | The quality of being commercial; the extent to which something is concerned with or motivated by profit.  It often implies a focus on profit over artistic merit or other values. | 例句：The film lacked artistic merit, sacrificed to commerciality. 这部电影缺乏艺术价值，为了商业性而牺牲了。

The increasing commerciality of the festival has alienated some of its traditional supporters.  节日日益浓厚的商业气息疏远了一些传统支持者。| 近义词：profitability, marketability, commercialism | 反义词：artfulness, artistry, non-profitability


词源:  Derived from "commercial" + "-ity" (suffix forming abstract nouns). "Commercial" itself comes from the Old French *comercial, from Latin *commercium ("commerce," "trade"), from com- ("together") + mercari ("to trade").
