measure:/ˈmɛʒər/ | n., v. | 测量；措施；程度；尺寸 | To ascertain the size, amount, or degree of something; a unit or system of units used for measuring; a course of action intended to achieve a particular purpose. | 例句：The tailor measured me for a new suit. (裁缝为我量体裁衣。)  They took measures to prevent further accidents. (他们采取措施防止进一步的事故。) | 近义词：gauge, assess, determine, action, step | 反义词：estimate (in some contexts)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He measures the fabric carefully. = 他仔细地测量布料。
We measure success by different standards. = 我们用不同的标准衡量成功。


一般过去时:
She measured the ingredients before baking. = 烘焙前她测量了配料。
They measured the room to buy new furniture. = 他们测量了房间以便购买新家具。


现在完成时:
We have measured the impact of the policy. = 我们已经评估了这项政策的影响。
They have measured the distance accurately. = 他们已经精确地测量了距离。


过去完成时:
She had measured the ingredients before the class began. = 课程开始前她已经测量好配料了。
They had measured the land before the construction started. = 施工开始前他们已经测量过土地了。


将来时:
They will measure the temperature every hour. = 他们将每小时测量一次温度。
We will measure the effectiveness of the treatment. = 我们将衡量治疗的有效性。


虚拟语气:
If I had a ruler, I would measure the length. = 如果我有一把尺子，我会测量长度。
It would be better if we measured the risk more carefully. = 如果我们更仔细地衡量风险就好了。


被动语态:
The temperature is measured daily. = 温度每天都被测量。
The impact of the drug was measured in clinical trials. = 该药物的影响在临床试验中被测量。


疑问句:
How do you measure success? = 你如何衡量成功？
What instrument do you use to measure the pressure? = 你用什么仪器测量压力？


否定句:
They did not measure the depth of the water. = 他们没有测量水的深度。
We cannot accurately measure the effect of the variable. = 我们无法准确地测量该变量的影响。


PHRASE->

to measure up = 达到标准
