patriarchs:/ˈpeɪtriɑːrks/ | n. |族长；家长；（尤指）教会领袖 |  The word "patriarchs" refers to the male head of a family or tribe, especially in the early history of a people or culture.  It can also refer to the founding fathers of a religion, like Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob in the Abrahamic religions, or to high-ranking church officials.  |例句：The patriarch of the family made all the important decisions.  这个家族的族长做所有重要的决定。

The biblical patriarchs are revered figures in Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.圣经中的族长在犹太教、基督教和伊斯兰教中都被尊为重要人物。


近义词：forefathers, founders, elders, leaders | 反义词：None directly opposite, but could be considered opposite to concepts like matriarchs (female heads of family) or subordinates.


词性：名词
