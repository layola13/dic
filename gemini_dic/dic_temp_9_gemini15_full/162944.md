prechoice: /ˈpriːtʃɔɪs/ | n. | 预先选择；预先挑选 | A prechoice is a choice made in advance, often to simplify or expedite a later decision.  It often involves selecting options beforehand, reducing the number of choices to be made at a critical moment.  | Example: The airline offered a prechoice of meals for passengers on long-haul flights.  航空公司为长途航班的乘客提供预选餐食。  The committee made a prechoice of candidates before the formal interview process. 委员会在正式面试过程之前预先挑选了候选人。| 近义词：preselection, advance selection | 反义词：last-minute choice, spur-of-the-moment decision | 名词
