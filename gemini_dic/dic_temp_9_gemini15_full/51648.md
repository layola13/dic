deviates:/ˈdiːvieɪts/| v.|偏离；背离；违反|To move or turn away from a course, direction, or standard.|The path deviates sharply to the left.  这条路急剧向左偏离。|The company's actions deviate from its stated principles. 这家公司的行为与其既定的原则相悖。|conform, adhere|conform to, adhere to|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deviates from the plan. = 他偏离了计划。
The needle deviates slightly. = 指针略微偏离。

一般过去时:
The train deviated from its schedule. = 火车误点了。
She deviated from the main topic. = 她偏离了主要话题。

现在完成时:
The river has deviated from its course. = 河流已经改道了。
He has deviated significantly from the original design. = 他已经大大偏离了原始设计。

过去完成时:
They had deviated from the agreed-upon route. = 他们已经偏离了商定的路线。
The plane had deviated course before the crash. = 飞机在坠毁前已经偏离航向。

将来时:
The project will deviate from the budget. = 项目将超出预算。
The path will deviate near the river. = 小路将在河边偏离。

虚拟语气:
If he deviated, he would be punished. = 如果他偏离了，他会受到惩罚。
I wish he hadn't deviated from the instructions. = 我希望他没有偏离指示。

被动语态:
The plan was deviated from. = 计划被偏离了。

疑问句:

Does his behaviour deviate from the norm? = 他的行为偏离常态了吗？

否定句:
The data did not deviate significantly. = 数据没有显著偏离。
The car did not deviate from the highway. = 汽车没有偏离高速公路。
