nitroform:/ˈnaɪtrəfɔːrm/| n. |三硝基甲烷| Trinitromethane, a highly explosive and acidic compound. It is a pale yellow, crystalline solid that is soluble in water and organic solvents.  It is used in the synthesis of other explosives and is also a powerful oxidizing agent. | The nitroform anion is a strong base, making nitroform a relatively weak acid.  |  Examples: Nitroform is highly sensitive to shock and friction.
