pemmican:/ˈpɛmɪkən/| n. |肉干；压扁的肉干|A concentrated, high-energy food prepared traditionally by Native Americans and others.  It typically consists of dried lean meat (often bison or venison), mixed with fat and sometimes berries or other ingredients, then pounded into a solid mass. Pemmican was a vital food source for long journeys and harsh winters, providing sustenance and energy.|The explorers relied on pemmican to survive their arduous journey across the plains. = 探险家们依靠肉干在穿越平原的艰苦旅程中生存下来。|preserved meat, jerky|fresh meat, perishable food|名词
