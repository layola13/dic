photochromotypy:/ˌfoʊtoʊˈkroʊmətaɪpi/| n. |彩色照片印刷术|A photographic process for producing colored prints, typically using a three-color separation technique.  It involves creating separate negatives for each primary color (cyan, magenta, yellow) and then combining these to create a full-color image.|Example: The museum showcased early examples of photochromotypy, highlighting the vibrant colors achieved through this intricate process.|近义词：color photography, chromolithography|反义词：monochrome printing
