re-endorse:/ˌriːɪnˈdɔːrs/ | vt. |再次认可；再次背书 | To endorse again; to give one's support or approval to something a second time.  |例句：The board decided to re-endorse the project after reviewing the new data. 董事会决定在审查了新的数据后再次批准该项目。
例句：The candidate was re-endorsed by the party after his successful primary campaign.  候选人在初选成功后再次得到该党的支持。|近义词： reaffirm, support, approve |反义词：reject, disapprove, denounce |动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They re-endorse the policy every year. = 他们每年都重新认可这项政策。
The company re-endorses its commitment to sustainability. = 公司再次承诺致力于可持续发展。

一般过去时:
The committee re-endorsed the proposal after further discussion. = 委员会在进一步讨论后再次认可了该提案。
The mayor re-endorsed the police chief despite the recent controversy. = 尽管最近有争议，市长还是再次认可了警察局长。

现在完成时:
The organization has re-endorsed its support for the cause. = 该组织已经再次表示支持这一事业。
The senator has re-endorsed the bill since the amendments were added. = 自从增加了修正案以来，参议员再次认可了该法案。

过去完成时:
They had re-endorsed the plan before the final vote. = 他们在最终投票前已经再次认可了该计划。
The board had re-endorsed the CEO's strategy before the financial crisis. = 在金融危机爆发前，董事会已经再次认可了首席执行官的战略。

将来时:
The party will re-endorse the current president next year. = 该党明年将再次认可现任总统。
We believe the council will re-endorse the development project after the public hearing. = 我们相信市议会在公众听证会之后将再次认可该发展项目。

虚拟语气:
If they re-endorsed the initiative, it would have a significant impact. = 如果他们再次认可这项倡议，它将产生重大影响。
I wish the government would re-endorse the environmental protection program. = 我希望政府能够再次认可环境保护项目。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The board refused to re-endorse the controversial policy. = 董事会拒绝再次认可有争议的政策。
