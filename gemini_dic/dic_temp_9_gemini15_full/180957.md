reimagining:/ˌriːɪˈmædʒɪnɪŋ/| v.|重新想象，重新构想 |The act of imagining something again, often with a new perspective or approach. It involves reconsidering existing ideas, concepts, or objects and creating new interpretations or representations.  |例句：The company is reimagining its brand to appeal to a younger audience.  这家公司正在重新构想其品牌以吸引更年轻的受众。


例句：The artist's reimagining of the classic fairy tale was both surprising and captivating.  这位艺术家对经典童话故事的重新诠释既令人惊讶又引人入胜。

近义词：reconceiving, rethinking, reinterpreting, reinventing

反义词：maintaining, preserving, upholding


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are reimagining the city's public spaces. = 他们正在重新构想城市的公共空间。
We reimagine the future of work every day. = 我们每天都在重新构想工作的未来。

一般过去时:
She reimagined the story from a different character's perspective. = 她从不同人物的角度重新构想故事。
The designers reimagined the classic car design. = 设计师们重新设计了经典的汽车设计。

现在完成时:
They have reimagined the museum's exhibits. = 他们已经重新设计了博物馆的展品。
We have reimagined our marketing strategy. = 我们已经重新构想我们的营销策略。

过去完成时:
They had reimagined the play before the premiere. = 他们在首映之前已经重新构想了戏剧。
She had reimagined her life after the accident. = 事故之后，她已经重新构想了她的人生。

将来时:
They will reimagine the entire production process. = 他们将重新构想整个生产流程。
We will reimagine our approach to customer service. = 我们将重新构想我们处理客户服务的方式。

虚拟语气:

I wish they had reimagined the website design. = 我希望他们重新设计了网站。

被动语态:
