schisma:/ˈskɪzmə/| n.|分裂；教会分裂；不和谐 | A schisma is a division or separation, especially within a religious organization or a system of belief.  It often refers to a formal split, leading to the creation of distinct factions or groups.  The term can also be used more broadly to describe a division or lack of harmony in any context. | Example: The Great Schism of 1054 divided the Eastern Orthodox Church and the Roman Catholic Church.  东正教和罗马天主教的分裂发生于1054年。 |近义词：division, split, rift, separation, discord |反义词：unity, harmony, concord, agreement |名词
