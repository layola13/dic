miraculous: /mɪˈrækjʊləs/ | adj. | 奇迹般的；不可思议的 |

英文解析:  Extraordinary and wonderful; seeming to be caused by a miracle.  Something miraculous is so improbable or unexpected that it seems to defy natural laws or ordinary explanations.

例句:

The doctor described the patient's recovery as miraculous.  医生将病人的康复描述为奇迹般的。

His escape from the burning building was a truly miraculous event. 他从燃烧的建筑物中逃脱是一个真正不可思议的事件。


近义词:  marvelous, wonderful, amazing, astonishing, supernatural, extraordinary

反义词:  ordinary, commonplace, unexceptional, normal

词源:

Miraculous originates from the Late Middle English word "miracous,"  derived from the Latin word "mirac(ul)osus," meaning "wonderful," "marvelous," or "miraculous".  The root is "miraculum," meaning "miracle."
