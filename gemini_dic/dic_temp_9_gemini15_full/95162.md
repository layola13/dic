inarticulate:/ˌɪnɑːrˈtɪkjuleɪt/| adj.|表达不清的；口齿不清的；不会说话的|Unable to speak or express oneself clearly; lacking the power of speech.  The baby, still inarticulate, could only cry to express its needs.  |近义词：unclear, speechless, incommunicative|反义词：articulate, eloquent, fluent|


英文解析:The word "inarticulate" means unable to speak or express oneself clearly.  It suggests a lack of fluency or clarity in speech, either due to physical limitations or emotional difficulty. It can also describe something that is not expressed clearly or effectively.


例句:

He was inarticulate with grief, unable to speak properly. 他悲痛欲绝，说不出话来。

Her feelings were inarticulate, a jumble of emotions she couldn't express. 她的感受难以言表，是一团她无法表达的情绪。


词源:

The word comes from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") and "articulate," which derives from the Latin "articulatus," meaning "jointed" or "clearly expressed."  The original sense referred to a lack of clear joints or articulation in speech sounds.  Over time, the meaning broadened to encompass a lack of clarity in expression in general.
