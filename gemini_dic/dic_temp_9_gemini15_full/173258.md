quasi-conscious: /ˈkwɑːzaɪˈkɒnʃəs/ | adj. |似有意识的，半意识的 |  Partially conscious; having a low level of awareness; existing between full consciousness and unconsciousness.  It suggests a state where awareness is dim or unclear, unlike full consciousness but also distinct from complete unconsciousness. |


例句：

The patient was in a quasi-conscious state after the accident, responding only weakly to stimuli.  病人事故后处于半昏迷状态，对刺激反应很微弱。

She drifted in and out of a quasi-conscious dream, unable to fully wake or fully sleep. 她处于半梦半醒之间，无法完全清醒也无法完全入睡。


近义词：semiconscious, half-conscious, drowsy, lethargic


反义词：conscious, fully awake, alert


词性：形容词


词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "as it were," or "almost."  This prefix indicates a resemblance or approximation to something.

* **conscious**: From Latin *conscius*, meaning "knowing with," "sharing knowledge," ultimately derived from *con-* (together) + *scire* (to know).
