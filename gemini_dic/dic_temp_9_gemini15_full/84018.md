half-drowned: /ˌhɑːfˈdraʊnd/ | adj. | 半淹死的；几乎淹死的 |  Nearly drowned; partially submerged in water.  The adjective describes a state where someone or something has been submerged in water but not completely drowned. It suggests a near-drowning experience or a state of being partially underwater. | 例句：The half-drowned dog was pulled from the river by a kind stranger. =一只几乎淹死的狗被一位好心人从河里救了出来。

The ship, half-drowned in the storm, sent a distress signal. = 在暴风雨中，半沉没的船只发出了求救信号。


近义词：submerged, partially drowned, near-drowned | 反义词：undrowned, completely dry


词源：half (一半) + drowned (淹死的，过去分词)  The word is a compound adjective formed by combining "half," indicating incompleteness, and the past participle "drowned," signifying the act of being submerged in water to the point of near-death.
