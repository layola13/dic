laggard:/ˈlæɡɑːrd/| n. adj.|落后者，迟缓者；迟缓的，缓慢的|A person who makes slow progress or falls behind others.  Someone or something that is slow or late.|The project is behind schedule because of laggards. 项目进度落后是因为一些落后者。 The laggard economy is struggling to recover. 迟缓的经济正在努力复苏。|近义词：straggler, slowpoke, dawdler |反义词：leader, pioneer, pacesetter


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is a laggard in his studies. = 他学习很落后。
The laggard economy shows little sign of recovery. = 迟缓的经济几乎没有复苏迹象。


一般过去时:

词源:
The word "laggard" comes from the Middle English word "laggen," meaning "to lag" or "to fall behind."  This in turn is likely related to Old Norse words related to  "lying down" or "loitering".
