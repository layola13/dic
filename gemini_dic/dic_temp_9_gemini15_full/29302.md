casted:/ˈkæstɪd/| v.| 浇铸；扮演；投射；| to shape (molten metal, plaster, etc.) by pouring it into a mold; to assign (an actor) a role; to throw or fling (something); to cause to fall into a particular state or condition |例句：The statue was casted from bronze.  这尊雕像是青铜铸成的。  The director casted him as the lead role. 导演让他扮演主角。|近义词：mold, form, pour, shape, assign, throw, project |反义词：destroy, break, dismantle


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They cast the bronze every day. = 他们每天都铸造青铜。
The director casts actors carefully. = 导演仔细挑选演员。


一般过去时:
He casted the fishing line into the river. = 他把鱼线抛进河里。
They casted their votes for the best candidate. = 他们把选票投给了最佳候选人。


现在完成时:
They have casted the votes and are now ready to announce the winner. = 他们已经统计完选票，现在准备宣布获胜者。
The foundry has casted thousands of parts this year. = 这个铸造厂今年已经铸造了数千个零件。


过去完成时:
The play had already been casted before the auditions began. = 试镜开始前，戏已经选好演员了。
The metal had been casted and cooled before it was inspected. = 金属铸造冷却后才进行检查。


将来时:
They will cast the statue tomorrow. = 他们明天将铸造雕像。
The movie will be casted next month. = 这部电影下个月将选角。


虚拟语气:
If they casted the net wider, they would catch more fish. = 如果他们撒网更广，就能捕到更多鱼。
I wish they had casted a more experienced actor for that role. = 我希望他们为那个角色选一个更有经验的演员。


被动语态:
The roles were casted by the director himself. = 角色是由导演本人挑选的。

疑问句:

否定句:
