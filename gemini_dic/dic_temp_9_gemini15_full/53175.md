dipping:/ˈdɪpɪŋ/| v.|浸，蘸；下降；减少|To submerge (something) partially or briefly in a liquid.  To decrease slightly.|例句：She dipped her biscuit in her tea. 她把饼干蘸到茶里。

The price of oil has dipped recently. 近期油价有所下降。|近义词：immerse, submerge, lower, decrease|反义词：rise, increase, elevate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dips his fries in ketchup. = 他把薯条蘸番茄酱。
She dips her toes in the water. = 她把脚趾浸在水里。

一般过去时:
The temperature dipped below freezing. = 气温降到了冰点以下。
He dipped his hand into the cool stream. = 他把手伸进清凉的小溪里。

现在完成时:
The sales have dipped significantly this quarter. = 本季度销售额大幅下降。
The bird has dipped its beak into the river. = 鸟儿把喙浸到河里了。

过去完成时:
The stock price had dipped sharply before the announcement. = 在公告发布之前，股价已经大幅下跌。
She had dipped her brush in paint before starting the picture. = 她开始画画之前已经把画笔蘸了颜料。

将来时:
The sun will dip below the horizon soon. = 太阳很快就会落到地平线以下。
The economy is expected to dip next year. = 预计明年经济将会下滑。

虚拟语气:
If the temperature dipped below zero, the pipes would freeze. = 如果气温降到零度以下，管道就会冻结。
I wish the unemployment rate hadn't dipped so low. = 我希望失业率没有下降那么多。

被动语态:
The bread was dipped in butter. = 面包被蘸了黄油。
The cookies were dipped in chocolate. = 饼干被蘸了巧克力。

疑问句:
Does he dip his cookies in milk? = 他把饼干蘸牛奶吗？
Did the plane dip unexpectedly during the flight? = 飞机在飞行过程中意外下降了吗？

否定句:
She didn't dip her fries in the sauce. = 她没有把薯条蘸酱。
The sales figures didn't dip as much as predicted. = 销售数字没有像预测的那样下降那么多。


PHRASE->

dip into: 匆匆浏览；略微涉猎
dip in and out of:  时不时地参与
take a dip: 洗个澡，游泳
