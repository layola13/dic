predelaying: /ˌpriːdɪˈleɪɪŋ/ | v. | 预先延迟；提前推迟 |  The act of delaying something before it is scheduled to occur.  It implies a proactive decision to postpone, rather than a reactive delay due to unforeseen circumstances. | 例句：The project manager decided to predelay the launch to address some critical bugs. = 项目经理决定提前推迟发布，以解决一些严重的错误。

The airline predelayed several flights due to anticipated bad weather. = 航空公司由于预料到恶劣天气而提前推迟了几趟航班。


近义词：postpone, defer, delay, advance (in the sense of moving something to an earlier time, but with a negative connotation of postponing) | 反义词：expedite, accelerate, hasten | 词性：动词
