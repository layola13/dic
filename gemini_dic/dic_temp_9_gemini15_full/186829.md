ringbill: /ˈrɪŋbɪl/ | n. |环嘴鸟 | A bird with a ring-like marking around its bill.  The term is often used to refer to several species of birds, especially those in the family Anatidae (ducks, geese, and swans), that possess this characteristic.  | Example: The Australasian shoveler is a type of ringbill.  澳大利亚铲嘴鸭是一种环嘴鸟。 | 近义词:  (depending on the specific species) shoveler, whistling duck | 反义词: None directly applicable.
