glitters:/ˈɡlɪtərz/| v.|闪光；闪烁；光彩夺目|To shine with many small, bright points of light.|The diamonds glittered on her necklace.  她的项链上的钻石闪闪发光。The city glittered at night. 夜晚的城市灯火辉煌。|sparkle, shimmer, gleam|dull, matte|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The jewellery glitters in the sunlight. = 珠宝在阳光下闪闪发光。
The stars glitter in the night sky. = 星星在夜空中闪烁。

一般过去时:
The disco ball glittered throughout the party. = 整个派对期间，迪斯科球闪闪发光。
Her eyes glittered with excitement. = 她兴奋地眼睛闪闪发光。

现在完成时:
The snow has glittered all day. = 雪已经闪耀了一整天。
The frost has glittered on the windowpanes. = 窗玻璃上结的霜闪闪发光。

过去完成时:
The chandelier had glittered before the power went out. = 在停电之前，枝形吊灯闪闪发光。
The jewels had glittered in the museum case. = 宝石在博物馆的陈列柜里闪闪发光。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If the sun shone, the snow would glitter. = 如果阳光照耀，雪就会闪闪发光。
I wish the diamonds glittered more brightly. = 我希望钻石能更明亮地闪耀。

疑问句:
Does the ice glitter in the morning sun? = 冰在早晨的阳光下闪闪发光吗？
Do the sequins glitter under the stage lights? = 亮片在舞台灯光下闪闪发光吗？

否定句:
The dull metal did not glitter. = 那块暗淡的金属没有闪光。
The surface of the water didn't glitter today. = 水面今天没有闪光。
