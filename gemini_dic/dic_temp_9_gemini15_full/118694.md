metacarpus:/ˌmɛtəˈkɑːrpəs/| n. |掌骨；掌骨集合|The metacarpus is the part of the hand between the wrist and the fingers, consisting of five long bones.  It's responsible for the hand's flexibility and strength.  The metacarpal bones articulate with the carpal bones of the wrist and the phalanges of the fingers.|例句：The fracture involved the metacarpus of his left hand.  他的左手掌骨骨折了。|例句：He suffered a severe injury to his metacarpus during the game. 他在比赛中严重损伤了掌骨。|近义词：carpus (hand),  |反义词:phalanges (fingers), carpals (wrist bones).
