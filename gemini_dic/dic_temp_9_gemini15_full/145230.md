overbid:/ˌoʊvərˈbɪd/| vt.|出价过高；高估|To bid more than something is worth or more than one is willing to pay.  It implies offering a price that is excessively high, often resulting in a loss or an unfavorable outcome.|例句：He overbid on the antique chair, paying twice its market value. 他对那把古董椅出价过高，付了其市场价的两倍。

例句：The company overbid on the contract, leading to significant financial losses. 那家公司对合同出价过高，导致了巨大的经济损失。


近义词：overvalue, overestimate, exceed

反义词：underbid, undervalue
