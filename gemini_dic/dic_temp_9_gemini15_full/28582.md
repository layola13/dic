careers:/kəˈrɪrz/ | n. |职业；事业；生涯 |  The word "careers" refers to the progression of a person's professional life, encompassing their jobs, achievements, and overall path in their chosen field. It often implies a sense of purpose and advancement over time.  |  He's had a distinguished career in law. 他在法律领域拥有杰出的职业生涯。 |  jobs, professions, vocations |  unemployment, idleness | noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My career is going well. = 我的职业生涯进展顺利。
What career path are you considering? = 你正在考虑什么职业道路？


一般过去时:
Her career ended abruptly. = 她的职业生涯突然结束了。
He pursued a successful career in music. = 他在音乐方面追求了一份成功的职业生涯。


现在完成时:
I have had a varied career. = 我有过多种职业。
She has built a successful career in marketing. = 她建立了一个成功的市场营销职业生涯。


过去完成时:
By the age of 30, he had already established a thriving career. = 到30岁时，他已经建立了蓬勃发展的职业生涯。
Before starting her own business, she had a long career in finance. = 在创业之前，她在金融领域有很长的职业生涯。


将来时:
I will pursue a career in medicine. = 我将从事医学职业。
What career will you have in the future? = 你将来会从事什么职业？


虚拟语气:
If I had pursued a different career, I would be happier. = 如果我从事不同的职业，我会更快乐。
I wish I had chosen a career I enjoyed more. = 我希望我选择了更喜欢的职业。


被动语态:
His career was ruined by scandal. = 他的职业生涯被丑闻毁了。
Many career paths are influenced by family background. = 许多职业道路都受到家庭背景的影响。


疑问句:

否定句:
