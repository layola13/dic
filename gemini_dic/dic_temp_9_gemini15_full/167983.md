prisage:/ˈpraɪzɪdʒ/| n. |徵税；税款；特权；|A right or privilege granted by a sovereign to collect a tax or duty, especially on goods imported or exported.  Historically, it referred to the right to levy a tax on certain goods, often granted by a monarch to a particular individual or group.|Example: The king granted a prisage to the port officials, allowing them to collect a tax on all wine imported into the kingdom.  国王授予港口官员征税权，允许他们对进口到王国的全部葡萄酒征税。|近义词：duty, tax, impost, toll|反义词：exemption, freedom
