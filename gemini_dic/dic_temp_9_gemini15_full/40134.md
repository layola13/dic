contemplator:/ˈkɒntɛmpleɪtə(r)/| n. |沉思者，冥想者 | A person who contemplates; one given to contemplation.  |例句：The old monk was a wise contemplator, spending hours in silent reflection.  这位老和尚是一位睿智的沉思者，常常静坐冥想数小时。|近义词：meditator, thinker, philosopher |反义词：doer, actor


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He contemplates the meaning of life. = 他在思考人生的意义。
She contemplates her future career. = 她正在思考未来的职业。


一般过去时:
He contemplated the beautiful sunset. = 他沉思着美丽的日落。
She contemplated her options carefully. = 她仔细地考虑了她的选择。


现在完成时:
He has contemplated this problem for days. = 他已经思考这个问题好几天了。
She has contemplated leaving her job. = 她一直在考虑辞职。


过去完成时:
He had contemplated quitting his job before the promotion. = 在晋升之前，他曾考虑过辞职。
She had contemplated the consequences before acting. = 她行动前已经考虑过后果。


将来时:
He will contemplate the offer carefully. = 他会仔细考虑这个提议。
She will contemplate her next move. = 她将会仔细考虑她的下一步行动。


虚拟语气:
If I had contemplated the risks, I would have acted differently. = 如果我当时考虑过风险，我会采取不同的行动。
I wish I had contemplated the consequences more carefully. = 我希望我当时更仔细地考虑过后果。


被动语态:
The problem was contemplated by the committee. = 这个问题受到了委员会的仔细考虑。
The proposal is being contemplated by the board. = 董事会正在考虑这个提案。


疑问句:
What are you contemplating? = 你在思考什么？
What did they contemplate before making the decision? = 在做决定之前，他们考虑了什么？


否定句:
He did not contemplate the possibility of failure. = 他没有考虑失败的可能性。
She did not contemplate the long-term implications. = 她没有考虑长远的影响。
