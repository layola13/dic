panhellenism:/ˌpænˈhɛləˌnɪzəm/| n. |泛希腊主义|The political ideology advocating for the unification or cooperation of all Greek people and territories.  It was a significant movement during the 19th century, aiming to create a single, unified Greek nation. The concept has historical roots stretching back to antiquity, but its modern manifestation was largely a response to Ottoman rule.|The rise of panhellenism in the 19th century led to several wars of independence and territorial expansion for Greece.| 近义词：Greek nationalism, Hellenism (in some contexts)| 反义词：Balkan fragmentation, Ottomanism
