cornered:/ˈkɔːrnərd/ | adj. v. |被逼到角落的；走投无路的；被困住的 |  Surrounded and trapped, especially in a metaphorical sense.  |例句：The thief was cornered by the police. 小偷被警察逼到了角落。

例句：The politician felt cornered by the press's relentless questioning.  这位政治家感到被媒体无情的质问逼到了绝境。


近义词：trapped, surrounded, hemmed in, boxed in

反义词：free, open, unconfined

词性: Adjective (形容词), Verb (动词)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The animals are cornered. 动物们被逼到了角落。
He is constantly cornered by his creditors. 他不断地被债主逼债。


一般过去时:
The mouse was cornered in the cupboard. 老鼠被逼到了橱柜的角落。
The suspect was cornered in an alleyway. 嫌疑犯在一个小巷里被逼到了绝境。


现在完成时:
The police have cornered the suspect. 警方已经将嫌疑犯逼到了角落。
They have been cornered into making a difficult decision. 他们被迫做出了一个艰难的决定。


过去完成时:
The robbers had been cornered before the police arrived. 在警察到达之前，强盗已经被逼到了角落。
The debaters had cornered their opponent with a series of tough questions.  辩论者们用一系列尖锐的问题把对手逼到了绝境。


将来时:
The police will corner the criminal soon. 警察很快就会抓住罪犯。
We will be cornered if we don't find a solution quickly. 如果我们不迅速找到解决方案，我们将陷入困境。


虚拟语气:
If we cornered him, he would confess. 如果我们把他逼到绝境，他会坦白的。
If I had been cornered like that, I would have panicked. 如果我像那样被逼到绝境，我会恐慌的。


被动语态:
The lion was cornered by the hunters. 狮子被猎人逼到了绝境。

疑问句:

否定句:
