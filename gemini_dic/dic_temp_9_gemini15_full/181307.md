reinterring: /ˌriːɪnˈtɜːrɪŋ/ | v. |重新下葬；重新安葬 | The act of burying someone or something again.  This often implies a reburial after exhumation, perhaps for a more permanent or fitting location, or due to a change in circumstances. |例句：After the discovery of a new grave site, the family decided to reinter their ancestor's remains.  重新下葬先祖遗体。

The family considered reinterring their loved one closer to their current home.  家人考虑将他们心爱的人重新安葬在离他们家更近的地方。| 近义词：reburial, reburying | 反义词：exhuming, disinterring | 动词
