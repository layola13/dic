muscarine:/ˈmʌskərɪn/| n. |毒蕈碱|A toxic alkaloid found in certain poisonous mushrooms, especially the fly agaric (Amanita muscaria), that acts as a parasympathomimetic agent.  It stimulates the parasympathetic nervous system, causing effects such as slowed heart rate, increased salivation, and constriction of pupils.|Example:  Muscarine poisoning can lead to serious cardiovascular complications.|近义词：None, as it's a specific alkaloid.|反义词：None,  though its effects can be counteracted by anticholinergic agents.|名词
