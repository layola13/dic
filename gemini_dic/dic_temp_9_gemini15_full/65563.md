eutychianism:/juːˈtɪkiənɪzəm/ | n. |优提奇主义 |The theological doctrine of Eutyches, which asserted the single nature of Christ, resulting from the complete absorption of the human nature by the divine nature.  This view denied the distinctness of the human and divine natures in Christ. |The Council of Chalcedon (451 AD) condemned Eutychianism as heretical.  优提奇主义被公元451年的迦克墩公会议定为异端。|Monophysitism|Nestorianism|名词
