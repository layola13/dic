humoralist: /ˈhjuːmərəlɪst/ | n. |体液论者；幽默家 |  A person who subscribes to the humoral theory of medicine, believing that health and disease are determined by the balance of bodily humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile).  Also, a person with a humorous disposition or style. |  例句：In ancient Greece, many physicians were humoralists, believing imbalances in the four humors caused illness.  在古希腊，许多医生都是体液论者，他们认为四种体液的不平衡会导致疾病。

例句：The comedian is a renowned humoralist, known for his witty observations on everyday life. 这名喜剧演员是一位著名的幽默家，以其对日常生活的机智见解而闻名。


近义词： (as a medical theorist)  medical traditionalist,  (as a humorous person) humorist, comedian, wit

反义词： (as a medical theorist)  modernist,  (as a humorous person)  serious, humorless
