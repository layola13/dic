/ˈoʊldɪst/| adj. |最老的，最古老的 |Oldest refers to the superlative degree of old, indicating the greatest age among a group or within a context. It can be used to describe people, objects, or events.  |This is the oldest house on the street. 这条街上最古老的房子。  The oldest member of the club is over ninety.  俱乐部里最年长的成员超过九十岁了。|elder, senior |youngest, newest |形容词


USAGE->

The oldest tree in the park is over 100 years old. = 公园里最古老的树超过100岁了。
This is the oldest building in the city. = 这是这座城市最古老的建筑。

词源:

The word "oldest" derives from the Old English "yldest," the superlative form of "eald," meaning "old."  This Old English root is related to Proto-Germanic *ald-iz and further back to Proto-Indo-European *h₂el- meaning "to grow old."
