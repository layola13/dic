nanometre:/ˈnænəˌmiːtər/ | n. |纳米 | A nanometre is a unit of length equal to one billionth of a metre (10⁻⁹ m).  It's commonly used to measure extremely small things, such as the wavelengths of light or the size of atoms. |The diameter of a human hair is about 80,000 nanometres. 人类头发的直径大约是80000纳米。

The wavelength of visible light ranges from 400 to 700 nanometres. 可见光的波长范围为400到700纳米。|micrometer, micron|millimeter|
