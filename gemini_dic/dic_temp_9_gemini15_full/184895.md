retainer:/rɪˈteɪnər/| n. | 保留者；保持器；定金；律师费；固定工资 | A retainer is something that retains or holds something else in place, or a fee paid to someone to retain their services.  It can also refer to a person who is retained, especially a lawyer. |例句：He paid a large retainer to ensure the lawyer's availability. 他支付了一大笔律师费以确保律师随时待命。

例句：The bridge's retainer held the bridge deck firmly in place. 桥梁的支座牢固地支撑着桥面。


近义词：fee, payment, deposit, engagement,  retainer fee


反义词：dismissal, release, discharge


词性: 名词


USAGE->

The company retains a large team of lawyers. = 公司保留了一支庞大的律师团队。

现在完成时:

词源:
The word "retainer" comes from the Old French word "retenir," meaning "to retain," which in turn comes from the Latin word "retinēre," also meaning "to retain."  The meaning evolved from the act of holding something to include the fee paid to ensure someone's continued service.
