restructurer:/ˌriːˈstrʌktʃərər/ | n. | 重组者，改组者 | A person or organization that reorganizes a business, system, or structure.  | The company hired a restructuring expert to improve its efficiency. 这家公司聘请了一位重组专家来提高效率。 | reorganizer, reformer | disorganizer, destroyer | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The restructuring team works diligently. = 重组团队勤奋工作。
He regularly restructures his investment portfolio. = 他定期调整他的投资组合。


一般过去时:
They restructured the company last year. = 他们去年重组了公司。
The government restructured the healthcare system. = 政府重组了医疗体系。


现在完成时:
The company has restructured its operations to cut costs. = 公司已经重组了运营以降低成本。
She has restructured her debt multiple times. = 她已经多次重组了她的债务。


过去完成时:
They had restructured the department before the merger. = 在合并之前，他们已经重组了部门。
The bank had restructured its loans to struggling businesses. = 银行已经为陷入困境的企业重组了贷款。


将来时:
The CEO will restructure the management team next quarter. = 首席执行官将在下个季度重组管理团队。
They will restructure the curriculum to better meet student needs. = 他们将重组课程以更好地满足学生的需要。


虚拟语气:
If they restructured the company, it might be more profitable. = 如果他们重组公司，它可能会更赚钱。
I wish they had restructured the organization sooner. = 我希望他们早点重组组织。


被动语态:

否定句:
