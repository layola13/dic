ectozoic:/ˌɛktoʊˈzoʊɪk/| adj.| 外生的；体外生的 | Living or occurring on the surface of an organism or outside of its body.  The term is often used in biology to describe parasites that live on the surface of their host, as opposed to endoparasites, which live inside the host's body. | Example:  Many ectozoic parasites, such as lice and fleas, feed on the blood of their hosts.  许多外寄生虫，例如虱子和跳蚤，以宿主的血液为食。|近义词：epizoic (for animals), epiphytic (for plants)|反义词：endozoic
