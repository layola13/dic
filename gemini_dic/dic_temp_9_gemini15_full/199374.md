simpered:/ˈsɪmpərd/ | v. | 傻笑；假笑；谄媚地笑 | To smile in a shy, nervous, or insincere way. |例句：She simpered at the compliment. 她听到恭维话后傻笑了一下。

例句：He simpered nervously as he awaited his turn. 他紧张地傻笑着等待轮到他。


近义词：smiled weakly, grinned, smirked, fawned | 反义词：glowered, scowled


词源:  The word "simpered" comes from the Middle English word "simpren," meaning "to be simple or foolish."  Over time, the meaning shifted to imply a shy or affected smile, often associated with a lack of genuine feeling.  The connection lies in the idea that a simple or foolish person might display such an insincere smile.
