cocoons:/kəˈkuːnz/| n.|茧；保护层|A cocoon is a protective casing, usually of silk, spun by the larva of an insect, such as a moth or butterfly, before it pupates.  It provides shelter and protection during the transformation into an adult insect.  The term can also be used metaphorically to describe a protective or secluded environment.|例句：The caterpillar spun a silken cocoon around itself. 毛毛虫把自己裹进一个丝茧里。

The artist retreated to her studio, her own personal cocoon of creativity. 艺术家退回到她的工作室，她自己创造力的保护壳。|近义词：casing, shell, covering, shroud|反义词：exposure, openness
