sabianism:/ˈseɪbiənɪzəm/| n. |萨比教；星教|The religious practices and beliefs of the Sabians, a religious group mentioned in the Quran.  Sabianism is characterized by a syncretic blend of various religious elements, often including elements of Christianity, Judaism, Zoroastrianism, and pagan beliefs.  Scholars debate the historical accuracy and precise nature of Sabianism, with different interpretations existing regarding their origins and practices. |The historical evidence for Sabianism is complex and often debated among scholars. Some scholars argue that Sabianism was a genuine religious tradition with a long history, while others suggest that the term was used to refer to various groups with diverse beliefs.  |近义词：star-worship (in some contexts)|反义词：None specifically.
