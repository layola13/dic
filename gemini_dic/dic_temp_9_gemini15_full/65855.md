evocate:/ˈɛvoʊkeɪt/ | vt. |唤起，引起，使想起 | To evoke something means to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind.  | The music evoked a sense of longing. 这音乐唤起了渴望之情。 The photograph evoked memories of her childhood. 这张照片唤起了她童年时的回忆。 |  provoke, elicit, summon | suppress, repress


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The painting evokes a sense of peace. = 这幅画唤起了一种平静的感觉。
The scent of rain evokes happy memories. = 雨水的香味唤起了快乐的回忆。


一般过去时:
The speech evoked strong emotions in the audience. = 这篇演讲在听众中唤起了强烈的情感。
The old house evoked a feeling of mystery. = 那座老房子唤起了一种神秘感。


现在完成时:
Her words have evoked a great deal of discussion. = 她的话语引发了大量的讨论。
The film has evoked strong reactions from critics. = 这部电影激起了评论家们的强烈反响。


过去完成时:
The news had evoked a sense of dread in the community. = 这个消息曾在社区中引起了一种恐惧感。
The photograph had evoked a flood of memories. = 这张照片曾唤起了一连串的回忆。


将来时:
The new campaign will evoke strong feelings of patriotism. = 新的竞选活动将唤起强烈的爱国主义情感。
The play will evoke laughter and tears. = 这部戏剧将引起欢笑和泪水。


虚拟语气:
If the music evoked stronger emotions, the performance would have been more successful. = 如果音乐能唤起更强烈的情感，演出就会更成功。
I wish the painting evoked a more powerful response. = 我希望这幅画能唤起更强烈的反响。


被动语态:
A sense of nostalgia was evoked by the old song. = 那首老歌唤起了怀旧之情。
Strong emotions were evoked by the speaker's words. = 演讲者的言语唤起了强烈的情感。


疑问句:
What emotions does this poem evoke in you? = 这首诗在你心中唤起了什么情感？
Does the novel evoke a sense of place? = 这部小说是否唤起了一种地域感？


否定句:
The film did not evoke any strong feelings in me. = 这部电影在我心中没有唤起任何强烈的情感。
The painting failed to evoke the desired response. = 这幅画未能引起预期的反应。
