distraction:/dɪˈstrækʃən/ | n. | 分心，干扰 | The loud music was a distraction during the exam.  吵闹的音乐在考试中造成了干扰。| 近义词：interruption, diversion | 反义词：concentration, focus |

英文解析：Something that distracts attention or prevents concentration.  Anything that takes one's attention away from what one is concentrating on.

例句：

The constant ringing of the phone was a major distraction. 手机不停地响声是一个主要的干扰。

Driving in heavy traffic can be a significant distraction.  在交通繁忙的路况下驾驶会是很大的分心。


词源：

From Middle English *distraccion, from Old French *distraccion, from Latin distractio, from distractus, past participle of distrahere "to pull apart, draw away, distract," from dis- "apart" + trahere "to pull, drag."
