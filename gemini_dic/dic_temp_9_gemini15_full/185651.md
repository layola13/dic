reverend:/ˈrevərənd/| adj. n.|尊敬的；牧师；教士|Reverend is a title of respect used before the names of clergymen, especially Christian ministers.  It can be used as an adjective or a noun. As an adjective, it means deserving respect or veneration. As a noun, it refers to a clergyman.|例句：The Reverend John Smith will be leading the service. 史密斯牧师将主持仪式。 The reverend father gave a moving sermon. 尊敬的父亲做了一个感人的布道。|近义词：minister, priest, cleric, pastor|反义词：irreverent, disrespectful


USAGE->

The reverend delivers a powerful sermon every Sunday. = 牧师每周日都会发表一篇有力的布道。
He is the reverend of this church. = 他是这个教堂的牧师。

一般过去时:
The reverend blessed the couple before their wedding. = 牧师在他们婚礼前为这对夫妇祝福。
He served as a reverend for over thirty years. = 他做了三十多年的牧师。

现在完成时:
The reverend has served this community for many years. = 牧师多年来一直为这个社区服务。
He has been a reverend since his ordination. = 自从他受任为牧师以来，他就一直是牧师。

过去完成时:
The congregation had already assembled before the reverend arrived. = 在牧师到达之前，会众已经聚集了。
The reverend had prepared his sermon meticulously. = 牧师一丝不苟地准备了他的布道。

将来时:

否定句:

PHRASE->
Reverend Father = 尊敬的父亲 (天主教)
Reverend Doctor = 尊敬的博士 (拥有神学博士学位)
Reverend Mother = 尊敬的母亲 (修女)
The Reverend Mr./Ms. [Name] = 尊敬的先生/女士 [姓名]
The Reverend [Name] = 尊敬的 [姓名]


词源:
The word "reverend" comes from the Old French word "reverent," meaning "respectful" or "venerable."  This in turn derives from the Latin word "reverentem," the present participle of "revereri," meaning "to stand in awe," "to revere," or "to respect."
