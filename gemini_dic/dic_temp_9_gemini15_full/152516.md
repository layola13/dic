payout:/ˈpeɪaʊt/| n.|支付，收益，支出|The act of paying out money;  a sum of money paid out.|例句：The payout for winning the lottery was substantial. 彩票中奖的奖金非常可观。
例句：The company announced a significant payout to its shareholders. 公司宣布向股东支付巨额股息。|近义词：payment, disbursement, distribution|反义词：income, revenue


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company makes regular payouts to its employees. = 公司定期向员工支付工资。
Large payouts are common in this industry. = 在这个行业，巨额支付很常见。

一般过去时:
The investor received a large payout last year. = 投资者去年收到了一笔巨额款项。
They made a payout to the victims of the accident. = 他们向事故受害者进行了赔偿。

现在完成时:
The company has made several payouts this month. = 公司本月已经进行了几次支付。
We have not yet received our payouts. = 我们还没有收到我们的付款。


过去完成时:
The company had already made its payouts before the scandal broke. = 丑闻爆发前，公司已经完成了支付。
He had never received such a large payout before. = 他以前从未收到过这么大的款项。

将来时:
The lottery will have a large payout next week. = 彩票下周将有巨额奖金。
They will make the payout as soon as possible. = 他们会尽快付款。

虚拟语气:
If the project succeeds, the payout will be enormous. = 如果项目成功，收益将是巨大的。
I wish I had received a larger payout. = 我希望我收到更多的钱。

被动语态:
The payouts were delayed due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，付款被推迟了。
Payouts are often made at the end of the month. = 付款通常在月底进行。

疑问句:
What is the payout for this competition? = 这次比赛的奖金是多少？
When will the payouts be made? = 什么时候付款？

否定句:
There were no payouts for the unsuccessful applicants. = 不成功的申请者没有获得任何付款。
The company did not make the promised payout on time. = 公司没有按时支付承诺的款项。
