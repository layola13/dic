metallicity:/ˌmɛtəlˈɪsəti/| n. |金属丰度|The abundance of elements heavier than hydrogen and helium in a star or other celestial object.  It's a key parameter in astronomy, reflecting the history of star formation and galactic evolution.  High metallicity indicates a higher proportion of heavier elements, while low metallicity suggests a predominantly hydrogen and helium composition. | The high metallicity of this star suggests it formed in a region with previous generations of stars. 这颗恒星的高金属丰度表明它形成于存在先前恒星世代的区域。 |  abundance, heavy element content | low metallicity | 名词
