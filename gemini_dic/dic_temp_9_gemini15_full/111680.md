lockdowns:/ˈlɒkdaʊnz/| n.|封锁，禁闭|The act of imposing restrictions on movement and activities within a defined area, often in response to a crisis or emergency, such as a pandemic or riot.  This typically involves closing non-essential businesses, restricting travel, and encouraging people to stay home.|例句：The city imposed strict lockdowns to control the spread of the virus.  这座城市实施了严格的封锁以控制病毒的传播。|例句：The lockdowns had a significant impact on the economy.  封锁对经济产生了重大影响。|近义词：curfews, quarantines, shutdowns|反义词：openings, freedoms
