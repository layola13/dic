newspeak:/ˈnjuːspiːk/| n. |新话|A deliberately ambiguous and deceptive language used in George Orwell's novel *Nineteen Eighty-Four* to manipulate thought and control the population.  It is characterized by the reduction of vocabulary, the invention of euphemisms for unpleasant concepts, and the suppression of words that might express dissent or independent thought. |The Party's manipulation of language in *Nineteen Eighty-Four* is chillingly depicted through Newspeak.  |近义词：doublespeak, jargon, propaganda |反义词：clear language, honest communication |名词
