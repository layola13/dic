dogmatic:/dɒɡˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 教条的；独断的；武断的 |  Characterized by or given to the expression of opinions or beliefs as if they were facts; stubbornly adhering to one's beliefs.  |例句：His dogmatic approach to management alienated many of his staff. 他独断专行的管理方式疏远了许多员工。

例句：The professor's dogmatic teaching style left little room for student questions. 教授教条式的教学风格使得学生几乎没有提问的空间。


近义词：opinionated, authoritarian, doctrinaire, inflexible | 反义词：open-minded, flexible, tolerant, compromising


词源：From Late Latin dogmaticus, from Greek dogmatikos, from dogma "opinion," "doctrine," from dokein "to seem," "to think," "to appear."  The word's etymology reflects its core meaning: presenting beliefs as undeniable truths.
