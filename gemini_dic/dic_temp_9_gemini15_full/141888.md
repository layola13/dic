ordaining:/ɔːˈdeɪnɪŋ/ | v. | 授权；任命；规定；注定 | The act of ordaining, conferring holy orders, or appointing someone to a position, often with religious or official authority.  It can also refer to establishing something as a rule or decree. |例句：The bishop will be ordaining three new priests this Sunday. 主教将在本周日任命三位新牧师。

The church ordained him a deacon. 教堂任命他为执事。

近义词：appointing, commissioning, consecrating, establishing


反义词：removing, dismissing, abolishing


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ordains new priests every year. 他每年都任命新的牧师。
The government ordains new regulations frequently. 政府经常颁布新的规章。

一般过去时:
The king ordained a new law to protect the people. 国王颁布了一项新法律来保护人民。
They ordained him as the new leader of the group.  他们任命他为该组织的新领导人。

现在完成时:
The council has ordained several new policies this year. 理事会今年已经颁布了几项新政策。
The church has ordained many new ministers since its founding. 自成立以来，教堂已经任命了许多新的牧师。

过去完成时:
Before the war, the emperor had ordained a strict code of conduct. 在战争之前，皇帝已经颁布了一套严格的行为准则。
They had ordained him to be the head of the department before he took the position. 在他担任这个职位之前，他们已经任命他为部门主管。

将来时:
The archbishop will ordain several deacons next month. 大主教下个月将任命几位执事。
The committee will ordain a new set of rules for the competition. 委员会将为比赛制定一套新的规则。

虚拟语气:
If the council ordained a new law, it would help solve the problem. 如果理事会颁布一项新法律，将有助于解决这个问题。
I wish the government ordained better environmental policies. 我希望政府颁布更好的环保政策。

被动语态:
He was ordained a priest last year. 他去年被任命为牧师。
New laws were ordained to improve safety standards.  颁布了新的法律以提高安全标准。

疑问句:
When will the new bishop be ordained? 新任主教何时将被任命？
Has the committee ordained a solution to the issue? 委员会是否已经对这个问题制定了解决方案？

否定句:
They did not ordain him to that position.  他们没有任命他担任那个职位。
The council did not ordain any new regulations this year. 理事会今年没有颁布任何新的规章。

词源:
源自古法语 *ordener，源于拉丁语 ordināre，意思是“安排，排列”。  This reflects the sense of establishing order or appointing someone to a specific place within an established order.
