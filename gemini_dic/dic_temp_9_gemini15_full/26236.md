buttermilk:/ˈbʌtərˌmɪlk/| n. |酪乳，脱脂乳|A slightly acidic dairy drink that is a byproduct of churning butter. It's traditionally made by agitating cream to separate the butterfat, leaving behind the remaining liquid.  Now, it's often cultured with bacteria to give it a tangy flavor. It's lower in fat than milk and often used in baking and cooking. |例句：She used buttermilk in her pancakes for a fluffy texture. 她用酪乳做煎饼，使其口感蓬松。

例句：The recipe called for buttermilk, so I went to the store to buy some. 食谱需要酪乳，所以我去了商店买了一些。


近义词：cultured milk, soured milk |反义词：None.

词源:
The word "buttermilk" is a compound word.  "Butter" refers to the butterfat that is separated out during its production. "Milk" refers to the remaining liquid.  Therefore, buttermilk literally means "the milk left after butter is made".
