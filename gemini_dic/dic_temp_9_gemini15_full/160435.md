popularised:/ˈpɒpjʊləraɪzd/ /ˈpɑːpjʊləraɪzd/| vt.|普及；推广；使流行|To make (something) popular or widely known.|The internet has popularised many new ideas and trends.互联网普及了许多新的思想和潮流。|近义词：popularize, disseminate, spread|反义词：suppress, obscure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He popularises his new book through social media. 他通过社交媒体推广他的新书。
The company popularises its products through advertising.  这家公司通过广告推广其产品。


一般过去时:
The invention of the printing press popularised books. 印刷术的发明使书籍普及。
The Beatles popularised a new style of music. 甲壳虫乐队推广了一种新的音乐风格。


现在完成时:
Social media has popularised many new slang terms. 社交媒体普及了许多新的网络俚语。
This technology has popularised remote working.这项技术普及了远程办公。


过去完成时:
Before the internet, the government had popularised the use of bicycles. 在互联网出现之前，政府已经普及了自行车的使用。
By the 1950s, television had popularised many forms of entertainment. 到20世纪50年代，电视已经普及了许多娱乐形式。


将来时:
The new app will popularise a new way of communication. 这个新的应用程序将普及一种新的沟通方式。
This new technology will popularise sustainable living practices. 这项新技术将普及可持续的生活方式。


虚拟语气:
If they had popularised their product earlier, they would have made more money. 如果他们早点推广他们的产品，他们就会赚到更多钱。
I wish they had popularised this technology sooner. 我希望他们早点推广这项技术。


被动语态:
The new idea was popularised by a prominent scientist. 这个新想法是由一位杰出的科学家推广的。
His theories were popularised through numerous articles and books. 他的理论通过无数的文章和书籍而广为人知。


疑问句:
How did they popularise their brand? 他们是如何推广他们的品牌的？
What methods did they use to popularise their product? 他们使用了什么方法来推广他们的产品？


否定句:
They didn't popularise their product effectively. 他们没有有效地推广他们的产品。
The company failed to popularise its new invention. 公司未能推广其新发明。
