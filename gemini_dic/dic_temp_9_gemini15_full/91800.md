hyperfederalists: /ˌhaɪpərˈfedərəlɪsts/ | n. | 超联邦主义者 | Supporters of hyperfederalism, a political ideology advocating for a system of governance where subnational units (states, provinces, etc.) hold significantly more power than the central government.  They believe in maximizing the autonomy and self-governance of these subnational entities. |  The hyperfederalists argued for a complete overhaul of the existing power balance, granting states near-complete sovereignty. | 近义词: decentralists, states' rightists | 反义词: centralists, federalists (in the traditional sense)
