self-easing:/ˌsɛlfˈiːzɪŋ/ | adj. | 自行缓解的，自动减轻的 |  Describes something that reduces or alleviates its own effect or intensity without external intervention.  This term is often used in engineering and technical contexts to describe systems or mechanisms that automatically adjust to mitigate a problem. | Example: The self-easing brake system gradually reduced the speed of the vehicle.  自行缓解的制动系统逐渐降低了车辆的速度。The self-easing mechanism in the machine prevented further damage. 机器中的自缓机制防止了进一步的损坏。 | 近义词：self-regulating, self-adjusting, automatic | 反义词：self-aggravating, escalating | 词性：形容词
