caimacam:/kaɪˈmækæm/| n.| 副王，总督（西班牙殖民时期）|A caimacam was a deputy governor or lieutenant governor, particularly in the Ottoman Empire and Spanish colonial territories.  The term derives from Turkish.  It held varying levels of authority depending on the specific context and time period.|Example: The caimacam ruled the province in the absence of the governor.  副王在总督缺席期间统治着这个省份。|近义词：lieutenant governor, deputy governor, viceroy|反义词：governor (in the sense of superior)|名词
