called:/kɔːld/| v.|被称为，称呼，叫|To give a name to; to refer to something or someone by a particular name.|例句：The book is called "Pride and Prejudice". 这本书被称为《傲慢与偏见》。|例句：They called him John. 他们叫他约翰。|近义词：named, termed, designated|反义词：unnamed, anonymous|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They call him Tom. = 他们叫他汤姆。
I call my dog Lucky. = 我把我的狗叫幸运。


一般过去时:
She called me yesterday. = 她昨天给我打电话了。
They called the police. = 他们报了警。


现在完成时:
He has called several times. = 他已经打了好几次电话了。
We have called for help. = 我们已经求救了。


过去完成时:
They had called before we arrived. = 我们到达之前他们已经打过电话了。
She had called him many times. = 她已经给他打过很多次电话了。


将来时:
I will call you later. = 我晚些时候会给你打电话。
He will call his mother tomorrow. = 他明天会给他妈妈打电话。


虚拟语气:
If I called him, he would answer. = 如果我给他打电话，他会接的。
I wish I had called her. = 我真希望我给她打过电话。


被动语态:
The meeting was called to order. = 会议开始。
The game was called off due to rain. = 比赛因雨取消。


疑问句:
What is this plant called? = 这种植物叫什么名字？
What did you call her? = 你叫她什么？


否定句:
I didn't call him. = 我没有给他打电话。
They didn't call for help. = 他们没有求救。


PHRASE->
called for: 需要
called off: 取消
called in: 召集
called up: 打电话
called out: 斥责
