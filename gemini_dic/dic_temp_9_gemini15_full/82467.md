grumpy:/ˈɡrʌmpi/| adj.|脾气坏的，易怒的，不高兴的|easily annoyed and irritable; bad-tempered.|例句：He was grumpy because he hadn't had his coffee yet. 他还没喝咖啡，所以脾气很坏。|例句：Don't talk to him; he's in a grumpy mood today.  别和他说话；他今天心情不好。|近义词：irritable, surly, crotchety, ill-tempered|反义词：cheerful, happy, pleasant, good-humored|


词源:
The word "grumpy" is of uncertain origin, but likely developed from the earlier word "grum," meaning "sullen" or "gloomy."  The suffix "-y" was added to create the adjective "grumpy," signifying a state or quality of being grum.  The connection to "grumble" (to complain in a low voice) is also likely, suggesting a connection between complaining and a bad mood.
