mandarinship: /ˈmændərɪnʃɪp/ | n. | 统治；官僚政治；达官显贵；精通普通话的能力 | The skill or art of governing like a mandarin; the system or methods of government employed by mandarins; also, the body of officials, or officialdom.  It can also refer to proficiency in Mandarin Chinese. |


例句：

The emperor's mandarinship was absolute, brooking no dissent.  = 皇帝的统治是绝对的，不容许任何异议。

His years of study culminated in a remarkable mandarinship of Mandarin Chinese. = 他多年的学习最终使他精通普通话。


近义词：governance, administration, officialdom, mastery (in the context of language proficiency)


反义词：anarchy, chaos, insubordination (in the context of governance); illiteracy (in the context of language proficiency)


词性：名词


词源：源自"mandarin" (n.)，指中国高级官员，最终源自葡萄牙语“mandarim”，来自马来语“menteri”，意为“大臣”。 "-ship" 是一个名词后缀，表示状态、技能、身份或品质。  因此，"mandarinship" 结合了“高级官员”和“状态/技能/身份”的意思。
