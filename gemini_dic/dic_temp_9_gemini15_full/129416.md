nominates:/ˈnɒmɪneɪts/ /ˈnɑːmɪneɪts/| vt.|提名，推荐，任命|To officially suggest someone for a position or award.|The committee nominates candidates for the award.  委员会提名候选人角逐奖项。

The academy nominated her for best actress.  学院提名她为最佳女演员。


近义词：appoint, propose, suggest
反义词：reject, disqualify

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He nominates a new candidate every year. 他每年都提名一位新的候选人。
The party nominates its leader in a secret ballot.  该党通过无记名投票提名其领导人。

一般过去时:
They nominated him for the presidency. 他们提名他为总统。
The board nominated a new CEO last week.  董事会在上周提名了一位新的首席执行官。

现在完成时:
She has nominated several people for the scholarship. 她已经提名了几个人获得奖学金。
The committee has already nominated its candidates. 委员会已经提名了候选人。

过去完成时:
He had nominated her before the deadline. 他在截止日期前已经提名了她。
They had nominated three candidates before the meeting. 会议前他们已经提名了三位候选人。

将来时:
They will nominate him for the prize next year. 他们明年将提名他角逐该奖项。
The party will nominate its candidate next month.  该党下个月将提名其候选人。

虚拟语气:
If he were nominated, he would accept the position. 如果他被提名，他会接受这个职位。
I wish I had been nominated for the award. 我希望我被提名获得该奖项。

被动语态:
He was nominated for an Oscar. 他被提名奥斯卡奖。
She was nominated for the position but didn't get it. 她被提名担任该职位，但没有得到。

疑问句:
Who did they nominate for the job? 他们提名谁担任这份工作？
Has she been nominated yet? 她已经被提名了吗？

否定句:
They didn't nominate him for the position. 他们没有提名他担任该职位。
The committee did not nominate anyone this year.  委员会今年没有提名任何人。


PHRASE->
nominate someone for something: 提名某人做某事
nominate someone to something: 提名某人担任某职位
self-nominate: 自荐
nominate a successor: 提名继任者
nominate a candidate: 提名候选人
