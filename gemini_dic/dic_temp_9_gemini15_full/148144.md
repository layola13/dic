oversensitivities:/ˌoʊvərˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪtiz/ | n. | 过度敏感性；过敏反应 |  Exaggerated sensitivity or reactivity, often to stimuli that most people wouldn't react to strongly. This can manifest physically (allergies) or emotionally (easily offended).|例句：Her oversensitivities to criticism made it difficult to give her constructive feedback.  她的过度敏感使得很难给她建设性的反馈。

例句：His oversensitivities to certain foods resulted in frequent allergic reactions. 他对某些食物的过敏反应导致了频繁的过敏反应。


近义词：hypersensitivities, allergic reactions (for physical manifestations), emotional fragility (for emotional manifestations) | 反义词：insensitivity, thick-skinnedness, resilience | 词性:名词


词源:

* **over-**:  prefix indicating excess or to a greater degree than normal. (源自古英语，表示超过，过度)
* **sensitiv-**: from "sensitive,"  relating to the senses or feeling. (源自拉丁语 *sentire*,  meaning "to feel")
* **-ity**: suffix forming nouns of state, quality, or condition. (源自法语，构成名词后缀，表示状态，性质或状况)
* **-ies**: plural suffix. (源自古英语，表示复数)


因此，"oversensitivities"字面意思是"过度敏感的状态或情况的复数形式"。
