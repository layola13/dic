hocus-pocus: /ˈhoʊkəs ˈpoʊkəs/ | n. v. | 障眼法；戏法；胡言乱语 |

英文解析：Hocus-pocus is a nonsensical phrase used to describe magic tricks or sleight of hand, often implying deception or trickery.  It can also refer to something done quickly and secretly, or to meaningless or deceptive talk. As a verb, it means to perform magic tricks or to deceive someone.

例句：

The magician performed a dazzling display of hocus-pocus, making the rabbit disappear.  魔术师表演了一场令人眼花缭乱的障眼法，让兔子消失了。

He accused the politician of hocus-pocus and misleading the public. 他指责这位政客玩弄花招并误导公众。


近义词：trickery, deception, illusion, conjuring, charlatanry

反义词：truth, honesty, reality, genuineness

词性：Noun and Verb

词源: The origin of "hocus-pocus" is uncertain, but it's likely a corruption of a Latin phrase used in the Catholic Mass, possibly "hoc est corpus" ("this is the body"),  with the playful addition of "pocus". The word gained popularity in the 16th century and has since evolved to describe magical illusions and deceptive practices.
