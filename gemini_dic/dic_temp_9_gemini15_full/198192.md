short-lived:/ʃɔːrtˈlɪvd/ | adj. |短暂的，短命的| lasting only a short time; ephemeral. |例句：His joy was short-lived; the promotion was quickly rescinded.他的喜悦是短暂的；晋升很快就被撤销了。|例句：The short-lived success of the product was due to poor marketing. 产品短暂的成功是由于营销不力造成的。|近义词：brief, fleeting, transient, ephemeral |反义词：long-lasting, enduring, permanent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project is short-lived. = 这个项目是短暂的。
His fame is short-lived. = 他的名声是短暂的。


一般过去时:
The meeting was short-lived. = 会议很短暂。
Their happiness was short-lived. = 他们的幸福是短暂的。


现在完成时:
The excitement has been short-lived. = 兴奋是短暂的。
His career has proved short-lived. = 他的职业生涯证明是短暂的。


过去完成时:
The celebration had been short-lived. = 庆祝活动是短暂的。
Their honeymoon had been short-lived. = 他们的蜜月是短暂的。


将来时:
The effect will be short-lived. = 效果将会是短暂的。
The program will likely be short-lived. = 该项目可能寿命短暂。


虚拟语气:
If the project were short-lived, we would need to find a replacement. = 如果项目是短暂的，我们就需要找到替代方案。
I wish my happiness weren't short-lived. = 我希望我的快乐不是短暂的。


被动语态:
The victory was seen as short-lived. = 胜利被认为是短暂的。
The benefits of the treatment were reported as short-lived. = 该疗法的益处据报道是短暂的。


疑问句:
Is the effect of the medicine short-lived? = 这种药的效果是短暂的吗？
Was the success of the film short-lived? = 这部电影的成功是短暂的吗？


否定句:
The impact was not short-lived. = 影响并非短暂的。
The relationship was not short-lived; they stayed together for many years. = 这段关系并非短暂的；他们在一起很多年了。
