neutrality:/nuːˈtræliti/ | n. | 中立；中性；不参与 | The state of not supporting or opposing any side in a conflict or dispute.  It often implies a deliberate choice to remain uninvolved. | Example:  The country declared its neutrality in the ongoing war.  这个国家宣布在持续的战争中保持中立。

The government maintained a strict neutrality throughout the crisis. 政府在整个危机期间都保持严格的中立。|近义词：impartiality, nonalignment, indifference |反义词：partisanship, bias, involvement

词源:
The word "neutrality" originates from the Latin word "neutralitas," which itself comes from "neutrālis," meaning "neither," "belonging to neither side," or "neutral."  The suffix "-ity" denotes a state or quality.  Therefore, "neutrality" literally means "the state of being neutral."
