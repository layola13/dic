secularisms:/ˈsɛkjʊləˌrɪzəm/| n. |世俗化；非宗教性；世俗主义|The belief that religion should not play a role in government, education, or other public aspects of life.  It emphasizes reason, evidence, and human experience over religious dogma.  Different forms of secularism exist, ranging from complete separation of church and state to a more nuanced approach where religious beliefs are tolerated but don't dominate public life.|例句：The rise of secularism in Europe led to significant changes in social and political structures.  欧洲世俗主义的兴起导致社会和政治结构发生了重大变化。|例句：  Many argue that secularism is essential for a just and equitable society. 许多人认为世俗主义对于一个公正平等的社会至关重要。|近义词：non-religiousness, irreligion, laicism|反义词：theocracy, religious fundamentalism|名词
