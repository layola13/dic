reduplication:/ˌriːdjuːplɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 重复；叠词法 | The repetition of a word, syllable, or phrase, often to create emphasis or a specific effect.  This linguistic process is found across many languages. Examples include "bye-bye," "night-night," and "choo-choo."  | Example: The child's use of reduplication, such as "mama" and "dada," is a common stage in language development.  |近义词：repetition, reiteration | 反义词：uniqueness, singularity |
