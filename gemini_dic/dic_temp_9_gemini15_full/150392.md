panopticon: /pænˈɒptɪkɒn/| n. |全景监狱；全景式监控系统 |A circular prison with a central watchtower, enabling the surveillance of all prisoners without the prisoners being able to know whether they are being watched.  The term is also used more broadly to refer to any system of surveillance that allows a central authority to monitor the activities of a large population. |例句：The prison was designed as a panopticon, allowing guards to observe all inmates simultaneously.  这个监狱被设计成一个全景监狱，允许警卫同时观察所有囚犯。


The concept of a panopticon was developed by Jeremy Bentham in the late 18th century as an ideal prison design.  |近义词：surveillance system, monitoring system,  observatory |反义词：privacy, secrecy, anonymity
