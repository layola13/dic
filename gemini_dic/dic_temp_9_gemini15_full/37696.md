companioning:/kəmˈpænɪənɪŋ/| v.|陪伴；作伴；同行 |The act of accompanying someone or something.  It implies a shared journey or experience, often suggesting mutual support or companionship. |例句：She enjoyed companioning her grandmother on her daily walks. = 她喜欢每天陪她祖母散步。

例句：The guide companioned the tourists through the ancient ruins. = 导游陪同游客参观了古老的遗址。


近义词：accompanying, escorting, attending,  going with

反义词：abandoning, deserting, leaving alone


词性：动词


USAGE->

He companioned his friend on the trip. = 他在旅途中陪伴着他的朋友。
They companion each other through thick and thin. = 他们互相陪伴，风雨同舟。

词源:  The word "companioning" is derived from the noun "companion," which ultimately traces back to the Old French word "compaignon," meaning "fellow soldier" or "associate."  The "-ing" suffix transforms the noun into a gerund, signifying the act of being a companion.
