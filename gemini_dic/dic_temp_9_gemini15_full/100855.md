introspective: /ˌɪntrəˈspektɪv/ | adj. | 内省的，沉思的 | Given to examining one's own thoughts and feelings.  Characterized by introspection. | 例句：She is an introspective young woman who enjoys spending time alone, reflecting on her life. 她是一位内省的年轻女子，喜欢独自一人度过时光，反思自己的人生。

例句：His introspective nature led him to write a deeply personal journal.  他内省的天性使他写了一本日记，内容非常个人化。


近义词：reflective, contemplative, self-examining, thoughtful | 反义词：shallow, superficial, extroverted

词源:
The word "introspective" comes from the Latin prefix "intro-" (meaning "within" or "inward") and the verb "specere" (meaning "to look" or "to see").  The suffix "-ive" indicates an adjective. Therefore, "introspective" literally means "looking within" or "inward-looking".
