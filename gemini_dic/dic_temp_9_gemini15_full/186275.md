rhizophorous:/ˌraɪˈzɒfərəs/| adj. |生有支柱根的；红树林的|Producing or characteristic of rhizophores; relating to or characteristic of mangrove swamps.  The term describes plants, especially mangroves, that possess rhizophores, which are specialized roots that arise from the stem or branches and support the plant in soft substrates. These roots help anchor the plant and allow it to thrive in intertidal zones.| Example: The rhizophorous mangrove forests are vital habitats for many species.  生有支柱根的红树林对许多物种至关重要。|近义词：mangrove (as a descriptor), root-bearing|反义词：rootless
