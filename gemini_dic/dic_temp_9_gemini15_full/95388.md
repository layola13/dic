inched:/ɪntʃt/| v.|慢慢移动；逐渐前进|To move slowly or gradually; to advance by inches.|The snail inched its way across the leaf.  蜗牛慢慢地爬过叶子。|The price of gas inched upward.  汽油价格逐渐上涨。|crept, crawled, progressed|receded, retreated|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He inches closer to the finish line. 他慢慢靠近终点线。
The plant inches towards the sunlight. 植物慢慢地朝阳光生长。

一般过去时:
The car inched forward in the heavy traffic.  汽车在拥堵的交通中缓慢前进。
She inched the box across the floor. 她一点一点地把箱子移过地板。

现在完成时:
The temperature has inched up to 30 degrees. 温度已经慢慢升到30度。
The stock market has inched higher this week.  本周股市略有上涨。

过去完成时:
The hiker had inched his way up the steep mountain.  远足者艰难地攀登陡峭的山峰。
By the time we arrived, the sun had inched below the horizon.  在我们到达的时候，太阳已经慢慢地落到地平线以下了。

将来时:
The project will inch forward slowly but surely.  这个项目将缓慢但肯定地推进。
The economy is expected to inch upwards next year. 预计明年经济将小幅增长。

虚拟语气:
If the negotiations inched forward, a deal could be reached. 如果谈判有所进展，就能达成协议。
I wish the project would inch along faster. 我希望项目进展能更快一些。

被动语态:
