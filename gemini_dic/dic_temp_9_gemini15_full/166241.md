preraphaelite:/ˌpriːræfəˈlaɪt/| adj. |拉斐尔前派的；|The term "Preraphaelite" refers to the artists, works, and style associated with the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood, a group of English painters, poets, and critics founded in 1848.  They reacted against the academic art of their time, seeking inspiration in the art of the period before Raphael (hence the name). Their work is characterized by intense colors, detailed realism, moral themes, and often symbolic or allegorical content. They frequently depicted biblical, literary, and mythological subjects, along with romantic landscapes and portraits.|The Pre-Raphaelite paintings are known for their vibrant colors and detailed depictions of nature. |近义词：Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood (as a noun), Romantic (in relation to style)|反义词：Academic (in relation to art style)
