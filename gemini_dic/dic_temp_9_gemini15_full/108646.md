leafcup:/ˈliːfkʌp/| n.|叶杯；叶状杯|A leafcup is a cup-shaped structure formed by the leaves of a plant, often used to collect water or other substances.  It is often a naturally occurring structure, but can also be artificially created.  The term is mainly used in botany and related fields. |Example: The pitcher plant uses a modified leafcup to trap insects.  捕虫草利用改造后的叶杯来捕捉昆虫。 The children fashioned leafcups from large leaves to collect rainwater. 孩子们用大叶子做成叶杯收集雨水。|近义词：leaf-shaped container, natural cup|反义词：无|名词
