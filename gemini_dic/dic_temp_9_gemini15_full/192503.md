scrupling: /ˈskruːplɪŋ/ | n. |顾虑；犹豫；不安 |  A feeling of doubt or hesitation, especially about the morality or propriety of a course of action.  |例句：He acted without a scruple. 他毫不犹豫地行动了。|  例句：Her scruples prevented her from accepting the bribe.  她的顾虑阻止了她接受贿赂。| 近义词：hesitation, doubt, qualm, misgiving | 反义词：certainty, confidence, assurance |  名词

Their scruples prevent them from telling lies. = 他们的顾虑阻止他们说谎。
