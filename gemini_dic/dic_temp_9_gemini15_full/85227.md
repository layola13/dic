harlequinade:/ˌhɑːrliˈkweɪneɪd/| n. |滑稽戏；谐剧；杂耍剧|A type of comedy, originating in Italian commedia dell'arte, characterized by slapstick humor, exaggerated characters (particularly Harlequin), and improbable situations.  It often involves disguises, mistaken identities, and romantic entanglements.|The harlequinade was a highlight of the pantomime, full of chases and silly antics.|近义词：pantomime, farce, buffoonery |反义词：tragedy, drama
