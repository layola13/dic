silicas:/ˈsɪlɪkəz/ | n. | 二氧化硅（复数） | Silicas are chemical compounds consisting of silicon and oxygen atoms.  They are commonly found in nature as various forms of quartz, sand, and other minerals.  The term often refers to a group or collection of silica particles, rather than a single molecule. | Example: The experiment used various sizes of silicas to study their catalytic activity.  这个实验使用了不同尺寸的二氧化硅来研究它们的催化活性。|近义词： silica (单数), quartz, sand |反义词：无直接反义词
