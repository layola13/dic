paramedic:/ˌpærəˈmɛdɪk/| n. |护理人员；(尤指)急救医护人员 |A paramedic is a person who is trained to provide emergency medical care.  Paramedics are highly skilled medical professionals who often respond to emergency calls before an ambulance arrives, providing immediate treatment and stabilization to patients. They work under the direction of physicians and can administer medications, perform advanced life support procedures, and transport patients to hospitals. |例句：The paramedic arrived quickly and stabilized the patient before transporting them to the hospital. 急救人员迅速赶到，在将病人送往医院之前对其进行了稳定处理。

例句：A team of paramedics responded to the car accident. 一组护理人员对车祸进行了响应。


近义词：emergency medical technician (EMT), first responder |反义词：无
