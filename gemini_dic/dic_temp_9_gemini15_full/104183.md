junketing:/ˈdʒʌŋkɪtɪŋ/| n. v.|享乐旅行；公款旅游；|The act of taking a pleasure trip, especially one paid for by public funds.  It often implies an element of extravagance or impropriety.|例句：The senators were accused of junketing around Europe at taxpayer expense. 参议员们被指控用纳税人的钱在欧洲四处享乐旅行。|例句：The company's junketing policy was criticized for its lack of transparency. 公司的公款旅游政策因缺乏透明度而受到批评。|近义词：pleasure trip, excursion, outing|反义词：hard work, austerity

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are junketing in Hawaii. = 他们在夏威夷享受旅行。
He frequently junkets to exotic locations. = 他经常去异国他乡享受旅行。

一般过去时:
We junketted in the Caribbean last year. = 我们去年在加勒比海享受旅行。

现在完成时:
