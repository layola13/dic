setting:/ˈsɛtɪŋ/ | n. |布景；环境；背景；设定；装置；地点 | The arrangement of scenery and properties to represent the place where a play or movie is enacted.  It also refers to the time and place in which a story takes place, or the environment in which something exists or happens. |例句：The play's setting was a bustling marketplace in ancient Rome.  这部戏的背景设定是古罗马一个繁华的市场。

例句：The novel's setting is a remote island in the Pacific. 这部小说的背景设定在太平洋上的一个偏远岛屿。


近义词：background, environment, context, circumstance, configuration |反义词：foreground, absence


词源:  The word "setting" originates from the Old English word "settan," meaning "to set," "to place," or "to put."  This verb evolved into the noun "setting," referring to the act of placing or arranging something, which then extended to its current meanings related to location, environment, and configuration.
