pelta:/ˈpɛltə/| n.|小盾牌；盾形物|A pelta was a small, crescent-shaped shield used by ancient Greek and Thracian warriors.  Unlike the heavier, round shields of the hoplites, the pelta was lighter and more easily maneuvered, allowing for greater speed and agility on the battlefield.|Example: The Thracian peltast was renowned for his speed and skill in using his pelta and javelins.|近义词：shield, buckler|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with larger shields like the hoplon.
