rainfall:/ˈreɪnfɔːl/| n. |降雨，雨量|The amount of rain that falls in a particular place or period of time. Rainfall is essential for agriculture and many other aspects of life. |例句：The rainfall this year has been exceptionally heavy. 今年的降雨量异常大。

例句：We measured the rainfall with a rain gauge. 我们用雨量计测量了降雨量。


近义词：precipitation, shower, downpour |反义词：drought


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rainfall is heavy today. = 今天的降雨量很大。
Rainfall provides water for plants. = 降雨为植物提供水分。


一般过去时:
The rainfall was light yesterday. = 昨天的降雨量很小。
There was little rainfall last month. = 上个月降雨量很少。


现在完成时:
There has been a lot of rainfall this year. = 今年降雨量很大。
We have received sufficient rainfall for the crops. = 我们已经收到了足够的雨水来满足作物生长。


过去完成时:
There had been no rainfall for weeks before the flood. = 洪水来临前几周都没有降雨。
The drought had intensified due to lack of rainfall. = 由于缺乏降雨，干旱加剧了。


将来时:
The forecast predicts heavy rainfall tomorrow. = 预报明天将有大雨。
Rainfall is expected to increase next week. = 预计下周降雨量将会增加。


虚拟语气:
If there were more rainfall, the drought would end. = 如果降雨更多，干旱就会结束。
I wish there had been more rainfall last summer. = 我希望去年夏天降雨更多。


被动语态:
The crops were damaged by the heavy rainfall. = 作物被大雨破坏了。
The region is often affected by unpredictable rainfall. = 该地区经常受到不可预测的降雨的影响。


疑问句:
What is the average annual rainfall in this area? = 这个地区的年平均降雨量是多少？
Has there been any significant rainfall recently? = 最近有没有明显的降雨？


否定句:
There was no rainfall during the entire trip. = 整个旅程都没有降雨。
The lack of rainfall caused the crops to fail. = 缺乏降雨导致作物歉收。
