hurl:/hɜːrl/| /hɝl/ | vt.  |猛投；猛掷；大声喊叫；呕吐 | To throw something with great force.  Also, to utter something forcefully or vehemently. | 例句：He hurled the stone across the field. 他用力把石头扔过了田野。

He hurled insults at his opponent. 他向对手破口大骂。|近义词：throw, fling, cast, shout, yell |反义词：receive, catch, whisper


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hurls the ball with great force. = 他用力地投掷球。
She hurls insults at her rivals. = 她对她的竞争对手破口大骂。

一般过去时:
He hurled the rock at the window. = 他把石头扔向窗户。
The protesters hurled stones at the police. = 抗议者向警察投掷石块。

现在完成时:
She has hurled herself into her work. = 她全身心地投入到工作中。
He has hurled many accusations at his enemies. = 他向他的敌人发出了许多指控。

过去完成时:
He had hurled the weapon before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，他已经扔掉了武器。
They had hurled themselves into the fight. = 他们已经奋不顾身地投入战斗。


将来时:
He will hurl the javelin in the competition. = 他将在比赛中投掷标枪。
She will hurl accusations at him during the debate. = 她将在辩论中对他进行指控。


虚拟语气:
If he hurled the stone harder, it might have broken the window. = 如果他更用力地扔石头，可能会打破窗户。
I wish I hadn't hurled those insults at him. = 我真希望我没有对他破口大骂。

被动语态:
The stone was hurled across the field. = 石头被扔过了田野。
Insults were hurled at the politician. = 对这位政治家进行了辱骂。

疑问句:
Did he hurl the ball? = 他扔球了吗？
Why did she hurl the book at him? = 她为什么把书扔向他？

否定句:
He didn't hurl the stone intentionally. = 他不是故意扔石头的。
She didn't hurl any insults during the meeting. = 她在会议上没有进行任何辱骂。


PHRASE->
hurl abuse at someone = 对某人破口大骂
hurl oneself at something = 奋不顾身地投入某事
hurl a challenge = 发出挑战
hurl insults = 辱骂
hurl a spear = 投掷标枪

词源:
Old English *hyrlan, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch hurlen and German hurle.  The meaning developed from "to whirl" or "to spin" to "to throw violently."
