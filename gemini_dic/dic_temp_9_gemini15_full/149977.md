palmed:/pɑːmd/| vt.| 瞒骗；暗中给予；用掌心遮盖 | To conceal something in the palm of one's hand; to deceive or trick someone. |例句：He palmed the card skillfully. 他熟练地把牌藏在手里。

例句：The magician palmed the coin and made it disappear. 魔术师用手掌遮住硬币，使它消失了。


近义词：concealed, hid, tricked, deceived | 反义词：revealed, exposed, showed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He palms the money secretly. 他偷偷地藏起钱。
She palms the ball during the game.  她在比赛中暗中藏球。


一般过去时:
He palmed the letter into his pocket. 他把信塞进口袋里。
The pickpocket palmed my wallet. 扒手偷走了我的钱包。


现在完成时:
He has palmed off many fake paintings. 他已经卖掉了许多假画。(palmed off: 骗卖)
She has palmed the evidence. 她已经藏匿了证据。


过去完成时:
He had palmed the key before the police arrived. 警察到达之前，他已经藏好钥匙了。
She had palmed the winning lottery ticket. 她已经藏好中奖彩票了。


将来时:
He will palm the money to his accomplice. 他将把钱交给他的同伙。
She will palm off the faulty goods. 她将会把有瑕疵的商品卖掉。


虚拟语气:
If he had palmed the evidence successfully, he wouldn't be in jail. 如果他成功地藏匿了证据，他就不会坐牢。
I wish I had palmed that valuable painting. 我真希望我当时把那幅名贵的画藏起来。


被动语态:
The money was palmed by the thief. 钱被小偷藏了起来。
The evidence was palmed expertly. 证据被巧妙地藏了起来。


疑问句:
Did he palm the money? 他把钱藏起来了吗？
Was the evidence palmed successfully? 证据成功地藏起来了吗？


否定句:
He didn't palm the money. 他没有藏钱。
She didn't palm off the defective product. 她没有把有缺陷的产品卖掉。


PHRASE->
palm off: 骗卖；蒙混过关
palm something on someone: 把某物强加于某人
palm oneself off as: 冒充
palm a card: 藏牌
to palm a lie: 撒谎


词源:
源自古英语 *palme,  最终源自拉丁语 palma (palm of the hand).  最初指手掌，后来引申出其隐蔽和欺骗的含义。
