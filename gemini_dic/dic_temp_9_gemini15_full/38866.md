confederationisms:/ˌkənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪzəm/| n.|邦联主义；联盟主义|The study or advocacy of confederation.  It refers to political philosophies and systems that favor a confederation as a form of government.  This often involves a strong emphasis on states' rights and decentralized power.|例句：The rise of confederationisms in the early 19th century led to significant debates about the balance of power between states and the federal government.  邦联主义在19世纪初的兴起导致了关于州与联邦政府权力平衡的重大争论。|近义词：federalism (but with a stronger emphasis on state autonomy), decentralization|反义词：centralization, unitary government|名词
