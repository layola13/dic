saxonism:/ˈsæksənɪzəm/| n. |萨克森语；盎格鲁-撒克逊习语；古英语特征|A word, phrase, or grammatical construction characteristic of Old English or Anglo-Saxon.  It often refers to words or expressions considered archaic or dialectal in Modern English.|The use of "thou" instead of "you" is a clear example of Saxonism.|近义词：archaism, old English usage|反义词：modernism, neologism
