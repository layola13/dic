disconnect:/dɪsˈkənɛkt/ | vt. n. |断开连接；使分离；切断；使脱节 |To separate or sever a connection; to interrupt the continuity of something.  |例句：Please disconnect the power before working on the appliance. 请在修理电器前断开电源。|例句：The sudden disconnect between the two friends surprised everyone.  两个朋友之间的突然疏远让每个人都感到惊讶。|近义词：sever, separate, detach, disjoin |反义词：connect, join, link, unite |动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disconnects the internet every night. = 他每天晚上都断开互联网连接。
She disconnects from work to relax. = 她下班后会放松一下，让自己脱离工作。

一般过去时:
I disconnected the phone. = 我挂断了电话。
They disconnected the electricity supply. = 他们切断了电力供应。

现在完成时:
We have disconnected the old system. = 我们已经断开了旧系统。
She has disconnected herself from the drama. = 她已经让自己远离了这场闹剧。

过去完成时:
He had disconnected the battery before starting the repair. = 他在开始修理之前已经断开了电池。
They had disconnected the phone line earlier that day. = 他们当天早些时候已经断开了电话线。

将来时:
I will disconnect the internet after midnight. = 我会在午夜后断开互联网连接。
They will disconnect the power supply tomorrow. = 他们明天将切断电源。

虚拟语气:
If I disconnected the internet, I would have more time to study. = 如果我断开互联网，我就会有更多时间学习。
I wish I had disconnected my phone earlier. = 我真希望我早点关掉手机。

被动语态:
The internet was disconnected due to a storm. = 由于暴风雨，互联网被断开了。
The power supply is being disconnected for maintenance. = 电力供应正在被切断以进行维护。

疑问句:

否定句:
