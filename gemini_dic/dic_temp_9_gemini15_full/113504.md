m'lud:/mˈlʌd/| n.|法官大人，阁下|A respectful form of address for a judge in a British court.  It is a contraction of "my lord" (used for male judges) or "my lady" (used for female judges).|Example: "M'lud, I object to the question."  法官大人，我反对这个问题。|近义词: Your Honor, My Lord, My Lady|反义词: N/A


USAGE->
This word is not used in typical grammatical structures like other verbs or nouns. Its usage is restricted to legal contexts as a form of address.  Therefore, standard grammatical tense examples are not applicable.

PHRASE->
There are no common phrases using "m'lud" as it is a complete address in itself.
