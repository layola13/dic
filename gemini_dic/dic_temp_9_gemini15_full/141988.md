organicism:/ɔːrˈɡænɪsɪzəm/| n.|有机论；整体论；有机体论|The philosophical theory that emphasizes the interconnectedness and interdependence of parts within a whole, particularly in living organisms and societies.  It contrasts with mechanism, which views things as assemblages of independent parts.| Example:  His sociological work is informed by a strong belief in organicism. 他的社会学著作深受有机论思想的影响。|近义词：holism, systemism |反义词：mechanism, reductionism
