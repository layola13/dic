embalms:/ɪmˈbɑːmz/| vt.| 覆盖防腐剂；保存（尸体）|To preserve (a body) from decay by embalming.  The process typically involves removing internal organs, treating the body with preservatives, and then wrapping it in cloth.|例句：The funeral home embalmed the body before the viewing. = 殡仪馆在瞻仰遗容之前对遗体进行了防腐处理。
The ancient Egyptians embalmed their pharaohs to preserve them for the afterlife. = 古埃及人对法老进行防腐处理，以使其在来世得以保存。|近义词：preserve, conserve |反义词：decay, decompose
