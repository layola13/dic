large:/lɑːrdʒ/| adj.|大的，巨大的|The adjective "large" describes something of considerable size or extent. It can refer to physical dimensions, quantity, or degree.  |例句：The elephant is a large animal.  大象是一种大型动物。

The company has a large market share. 这家公司拥有很大的市场份额。


近义词：big, great, extensive, considerable, substantial|反义词：small, little, tiny, minute|词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The box is large. = 这个盒子很大。
The city is large and sprawling. = 这个城市很大且面积广阔。

一般过去时:
The house was large enough for our family. = 这房子对我们家来说足够大了。
The crowd was larger than expected. = 人群比预期的要多。

现在完成时:
The problem has become larger than we anticipated. = 问题比我们预料的要严重。
The company has grown large and influential. = 这家公司已经发展壮大并很有影响力。

过去完成时:
The building had been large, but it was later demolished. = 这栋建筑以前很大，但后来被拆除了。
The task had seemed large at first, but we managed to complete it. = 这项任务起初看起来很大，但我们还是完成了。

将来时:
The city will become even larger in the future. = 未来这座城市将会变得更大。
The project will require a large amount of funding. = 该项目需要大量的资金。

虚拟语气:
If the house were larger, we could have more guests. = 如果房子更大些，我们就能招待更多客人。
I wish I had a larger apartment. = 我希望我有一套更大的公寓。

被动语态:
The room is considered large for a hotel room. = 这个房间对于酒店房间来说被认为很大。
The area was described as large and sparsely populated. = 该地区被描述为面积广阔且人口稀少。

疑问句:
Is the table large enough? = 这张桌子够大吗？
Is the problem too large to solve easily? = 这个问题太大了，难以轻易解决吗？

否定句:
The room isn't large enough to hold the party. = 这个房间不够大，无法举办派对。
The sample size wasn't large enough for statistically significant results. = 样本量不足以得出具有统计意义的结果。
