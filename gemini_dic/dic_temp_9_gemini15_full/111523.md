loather:/ˈləʊðər/| n.|厌恶者，憎恨者|Someone who intensely dislikes something or someone.  A person who feels loathing.|He's a loather of all things political.|近义词：detester, hater, abhorrer|反义词：admirer, lover


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He loathes public speaking. = 他讨厌公开演讲。
She loathes the smell of fish. = 她讨厌鱼腥味。

一般过去时:
I loathed my job, so I quit. = 我讨厌我的工作，所以我辞职了。
They loathed the bitter taste of the medicine. = 他们讨厌那药的苦味。

现在完成时:
He has loathed that man ever since their argument. = 从他们争吵以来，他就一直讨厌那个人。
She has loathed spinach her entire life. = 她一辈子都讨厌菠菜。

过去完成时:
I had loathed the idea before even considering it. = 我甚至在考虑之前就已经讨厌这个主意了。
They had loathed the movie before it even started. = 在电影开始之前他们就已经讨厌这部电影了。

将来时:
I will loathe this task until it is complete. = 我会讨厌这项任务，直到它完成。
She will loathe the long commute once she gets the job. = 一旦她找到这份工作，她就会讨厌漫长的通勤。

虚拟语气:
If I were her, I would loathe the constant interruptions. = 如果我是她，我会讨厌不断的打扰。
I wish I didn't loathe the sound of drills. = 我真希望我不讨厌钻头的声音。

被动语态: (Loathe is rarely used passively)  This section is omitted because a passive construction with "loathe" sounds unnatural.

疑问句:
Do you loathe the cold weather? = 你讨厌寒冷的天气吗？
Does he loathe his new neighbor? = 他讨厌他的新邻居吗？

否定句:
I don't loathe all vegetables. = 我并非讨厌所有蔬菜。
She doesn't loathe cats, she's just allergic. = 她不讨厌猫，她只是过敏。


PHRASE->
a loather of crowds = 讨厌人群的人
a loather of hypocrisy = 讨厌虚伪的人
a loather of injustice = 讨厌不公正的人
a confirmed loather = 一个坚决的厌恶者
a lifelong loather = 一生的厌恶者
