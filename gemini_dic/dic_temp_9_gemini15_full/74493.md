frankpledge:/ˈfræŋkplɛdʒ/ | n. | 十人组互保制度；乡镇互保制度 | A system of mutual responsibility for maintaining order and apprehending criminals, prevalent in medieval England.  Each group of ten men (a tithing) was responsible for the conduct of its members.  Failure to produce a member accused of a crime resulted in collective punishment. | Example: The frankpledge system was a key element of Anglo-Saxon law enforcement.  十人组互保制度是盎格鲁-撒克逊执法体系的关键组成部分。|近义词：mutual responsibility, collective surety | 反义词：individual responsibility,  personal accountability|  词性: 名词
