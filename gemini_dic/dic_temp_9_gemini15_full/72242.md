floridly:/ˈflɒrɪdli/| adv. |华丽地；绚烂地；过分装饰地 | excessively ornate; flowery in style.  It describes something that is excessively decorated or embellished, often to the point of being gaudy. |例句：The author described the scene floridly, using excessive metaphors and adjectives.  作者华丽地描写了这个场景，使用了过多的隐喻和形容词。

The painting was floridly decorated with gold leaf and vibrant colours. 这幅画以金箔和鲜艳的色彩进行了华丽的装饰。

|近义词：ornately, elaborately, extravagantly, gaudily |反义词：plainly, simply, austerely


WORD ORIGIN->
From Middle English *florid, from Old French florit, from Latin floridus "flowering, blooming," from flos (genitive floris) "flower." The sense development is from "flowering" to "having many colors" to "highly decorated" and finally to "excessively ornate."


USAGE->

This word is an adverb, so it modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.  There's no specific tense conjugation associated with it as it doesn't act as a verb.  It's usage is consistent across tenses.

Example Sentences demonstrating its use:

He spoke floridly about his accomplishments. 他夸夸其谈地讲述了他的成就。

The room was floridly decorated for the party. 房间为派对进行了过度华丽的装饰。

She dressed floridly for the occasion. 她为了这次场合穿着打扮得过于华丽。

The prose was floridly written, full of unnecessary adjectives.  这篇文章写得过于华丽，充满了不必要的形容词。

The politician spoke floridly, avoiding direct answers.  这位政客说话华丽其辞，回避直接回答。


PHRASE->

There are no common set phrases specifically using "floridly."  Its usage is primarily as a standalone adverb to describe the manner in which something is done or presented.  However, it could be used in phrases like:

floridly described: 华丽地描述
floridly painted: 华丽地绘画
