outlaws:/ˈaʊtlɔːz/| n.|逃犯；歹徒；被逐出境者|People who have broken the law and are hiding from the authorities; those who live outside the law.|The outlaws hid in the mountains to escape capture. 逃犯躲在山里以躲避抓捕。|criminals, fugitives, bandits|law-abiding citizens, police

一般现在时:
The sheriff hunts down outlaws. = 警长追捕逃犯。
Outlaws often operate in remote areas. = 逃犯通常在偏远地区活动。

一般过去时:
The posse captured the outlaws. =  posse 抓住了逃犯。
The outlaws robbed the stagecoach. = 逃犯抢劫了驿马车。

现在完成时:
The police have apprehended several outlaws. = 警方已逮捕了几名逃犯。
They have been outlaws for many years. = 他们多年来一直是逃犯。

过去完成时:
The sheriff had already tracked the outlaws before they reached the border. = 警长在他们到达边境之前就已经追踪到逃犯了。
The townspeople had lived in fear of the outlaws for years. = 镇上的人们多年来一直生活在对逃犯的恐惧中。

将来时:
The authorities will pursue the outlaws relentlessly. = 当局将无情地追捕逃犯。
The outlaws will likely flee to another state. = 逃犯很可能会逃到另一个州。

虚拟语气:
If they were not outlaws, they could live normal lives. = 如果他们不是逃犯，他们就可以过正常的生活。
I wish the outlaws would surrender peacefully. = 我希望逃犯能和平投降。

被动语态:

疑问句:
