disobeys:/ˌdɪsoʊˈbeɪz/ | vt. |不服从；违抗 | To fail to obey; to act contrary to (a command, rule, or law).  | 例句：The child disobeys his parents' rules frequently.  孩子经常违抗父母的规矩。

The dog disobeys the command to "stay".  这条狗不服从“待着”的命令。


近义词：defy, disobey, flout, ignore, violate | 反义词：obey, comply with, conform to | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disobeys the rules every day. = 他每天都违反规定。
She disobeys her boss's instructions. = 她违抗老板的指示。


一般过去时:
He disobeyed his parents' orders. = 他违抗了父母的命令。
The soldiers disobeyed the general's command. = 士兵们违抗了将军的命令。


现在完成时:
She has disobeyed the law several times. = 她已经违法好几次了。
He has disobeyed his doctor's advice. = 他不听从医生的建议。


过去完成时:
They had disobeyed the captain's orders before the accident. = 事故发生前，他们已经违抗了船长的命令。
She had disobeyed her mother's warning. = 她没听她母亲的警告。


将来时:
He will disobey the rules again. = 他还会再次违反规定的。
They will disobey the orders if they are not convinced. = 如果他们没有被说服，他们将会违抗命令。


虚拟语气:
If he disobeyed the law, he would face serious consequences. = 如果他违法，他将面临严重后果。
I wish he hadn't disobeyed my instructions. = 我真希望他没有违抗我的指示。


被动语态:
The rules were disobeyed repeatedly. = 规则被反复违反。

疑问句:

否定句:
