cephalous:/ˈsɛfələs/| adj.| 头部的；有头的 |Having a head or head-like structure.  The term often appears in compound words to describe organisms or structures with specific head characteristics.  |Example:  Many arthropods are cephalothoracic, meaning their head and thorax are fused.|近义词：headed, capitate |反义词：headless, acephalous


USAGE->
This word is primarily used as an adjective within compound words, not in isolation. Therefore, grammatical tenses and other typical usages are not applicable in the same way as with a standard verb or noun.  The usage is always descriptive.


PHRASE->
cephalothorax:  (head + thorax)  The fused head and thorax of certain arthropods such as crustaceans and arachnids.
cephalocaudal:  (head + tail)  Relating to the longitudinal axis of the body, from head to tail.
encephalitis: (brain + inflammation)  Inflammation of the brain.
hydrocephalus: (water + head)  A condition where there is an abnormal accumulation of cerebrospinal fluid in the ventricles of the brain.
acrania: (without + skull) The absence of a skull.
