scale:/skeɪl/| n. v.|等级；规模；比例；鳞；刻度；天平；量程；攀登；衡量|A system or series of steps or degrees; the size or extent of something; a graduated series of markings, such as on a thermometer; a thin plate or flake, as of a fish; to climb or ascend; to measure or weigh.|The architect used a scale model to show the client the building's design. 建筑师用比例模型向客户展示了建筑的设计。  He scaled the cliff with ease. 他轻松地攀登了悬崖。|size, magnitude, extent, proportion|small, tiny, insignificant|名词，动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company scales its operations to meet market demand. = 公司调整其运营规模以满足市场需求。
The fish scales shimmered in the sunlight. = 鱼鳞在阳光下闪闪发光。


一般过去时:
She scaled the mountain in record time. = 她创纪录地攀登了这座山。
The artist scaled down the image for the brochure. = 艺术家为宣传册缩小了图像。


现在完成时:
They have scaled the heights of success in their field. = 他们在各自领域达到了成功的巅峰。
He has scaled the fish and is preparing it for cooking. = 他已经去鳞了，正在准备烹饪。


过去完成时:
She had scaled the wall before the guards arrived. = 警卫到达之前，她已经翻过了墙。
They had scaled down their project due to budget constraints. = 由于预算限制，他们已经缩减了项目规模。


将来时:

She will scale the mountain next week. = 她下周将攀登这座山。


虚拟语气:
If I scaled the mountain, I would feel a sense of accomplishment. = 如果我攀登了这座山，我会感到一种成就感。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
