rattle: /ˈrætl/ | n., v. | 嘎嘎声；喋喋不休；使发出嘎嘎声；使震动 |  A rattle is a series of short, sharp sounds, often made by knocking hard objects together. As a verb, it means to make or cause something to make such sounds, or to shake or move something vigorously.  |

例句：

The old car made a loud rattle as it went down the road. = 这辆旧车行驶在路上发出很大的嘎嘎声。

The baby's rattle amused her for hours. = 婴儿的拨浪鼓逗乐了她几个小时。


He rattled off the names of all fifty states. = 他快速地背诵出所有五十个州的名字。(v.  快速地说出)

The earthquake rattled the windows. = 地震震动了窗户。(v.  使震动)



近义词：clatter, clatter, shake, tremble, jangle, chatter


反义词：silence, stillness, quiet


词性：名词(n.)，动词(v.)


词源：

Middle English: from Old Norse *hraðla, of imitative origin.
