monologues: /ˈmɒnəlɒɡz/ /ˈmɑːnəlɒɡz/ | n. | 独白；独角戏 | A monologue is a long speech by one person.  It's often used in plays or other forms of performance.  It can also refer to a person's private thoughts or internal dialogue. | Example:  The actor delivered a powerful monologue about the loss of his family.  演员表演了一个关于失去家人的有力独白。

The play features several lengthy monologues. 这部戏包含几个冗长的独白。


近义词：soliloquy, speech, address | 反义词：dialogue, conversation | 词性:名词


词源:  The word "monologue" comes from the French word "monologue," which in turn originates from the Greek words "monos" (single) and "logos" (speech, word).  Thus, it literally means "single speech."
