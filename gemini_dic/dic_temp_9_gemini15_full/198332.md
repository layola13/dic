shoving:/ˈʃʌvɪŋ/| v.|猛推，挤，塞|The act of pushing someone or something forcefully.|例句：He was shoving his way through the crowded street. 他在拥挤的街道上奋力挤过。|例句：Stop shoving!  别推了！|近义词：pushing, thrusting, jostling|反义词：pulling, drawing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He shoves his way through the crowd every day. 他每天都挤过人群。
They shove the boxes into the van. 他们把箱子塞进货车。

一般过去时:
He shoved the door open angrily. 他生气地推开了门。
She shoved the papers into her bag. 她把文件塞进包里。

现在完成时:
He has shoved all the furniture into the corner. 他已经把所有的家具都塞到了角落里。
They have shoved their way to the front of the line. 他们已经挤到了队伍的最前面。

过去完成时:
He had shoved the evidence under the carpet. 他已经把证据藏到了地毯下面。
She had shoved the letter into her pocket. 她已经把信塞进了口袋。

将来时:
He will shove the blame onto someone else. 他会把责任推卸给别人。
They will shove the boxes into the truck tomorrow. 他们明天会把箱子塞进卡车。

虚拟语气:
If he had shoved harder, he might have opened the door. 如果他再用力推，也许就能打开门了。
I wish he hadn't shoved me. 我真希望他没有推我。

被动语态:
The box was shoved under the bed. 箱子被塞到了床底下。
The crowd was shoved aside by the police. 人群被警察推开了。

疑问句:
Who shoved you? 谁推了你？
What are you shoving in your pocket? 你口袋里塞的是什么？

否定句:
He didn't shove the door open. 他没有推开门。
She didn't shove him. 她没有推他。


PHRASE->
shove it:  滚开！
shove off:  走开！
shove aside:  把...推开
shove down:  咽下；强迫接受
shove something on/upon someone: 把...强加于某人
