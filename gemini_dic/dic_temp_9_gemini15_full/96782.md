inequivalve:/ˌɪnɪˈkwɪvælv/| adj.|不等瓣的；不等壳的|Having shells or valves of unequal size or shape.  The term is most commonly used in describing bivalve mollusks (like clams and oysters) where the two shells are not mirror images of each other.|Example: The inequivalve shells of the oyster are a characteristic feature.  牡蛎不等壳是其特征之一。 The fossil shows inequivalve shell morphology.  化石显示不等瓣壳形态。|近义词：unequal, dissimilar|反义词：equivalve
