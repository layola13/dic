impudency:/ˈɪmpjuːdənsɪ/ | n. | 厚颜无耻；鲁莽；放肆 |  The quality of being impudent; boldness or effrontery.  It often implies a lack of respect or disregard for social norms. | 例句：Her impudency in demanding a raise after only a month on the job was shocking.  她只工作了一个月就要求加薪，真是厚颜无耻。

例句：His impudency towards his elders was frowned upon. 他对长辈的无礼行为令人皱眉。


近义词：cheek, insolence, audacity, effrontery, impertinence | 反义词：respectfulness, politeness, deference, humility


词源:  From Middle English *impudence, from Old French *impudence, from Latin *impudentia, from impudens "shameless," from in- "not" + pudens "shy, modest," from pudēre "to be ashamed."
