generates:/ˈdʒɛnəreɪts/| vt.|产生；生成；引起；创造|To produce or create something.  It can refer to physical things, ideas, energy, or emotions.|例句：The new factory generates hundreds of jobs. = 这家新工厂创造了数百个就业机会。|例句：The storm generated strong winds and heavy rain. = 暴风雨带来了强风和暴雨。|近义词：produces, creates, causes, yields|反义词：destroys, eliminates, consumes


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun generates heat and light. = 太阳产生光和热。
This company generates a lot of revenue. = 这家公司创造了大量的收入。

一般过去时:
The artist generated a lot of interest in her work. =这位艺术家对她的作品产生了浓厚的兴趣。
The engine generated enough power to move the train. =发动机产生了足够的动力来驱动火车。

现在完成时:
The project has generated significant profits. =该项目已产生了可观的利润。
She has generated a lot of support for her cause. =她为自己的事业赢得了大量的支持。

过去完成时:
The earthquake had generated a tsunami. =地震引发了海啸。
They had generated a lot of excitement before the game. =比赛前他们制造了很多兴奋。

将来时:
The new technology will generate many new jobs. = 新技术将创造许多新的就业机会。
The company hopes to generate more sales next year. =该公司希望明年能获得更多销售额。

虚拟语气:
If the plant generated more energy, we could reduce our carbon footprint. =如果工厂能产生更多的能量，我们就能减少碳足迹。
I wish this machine generated less noise. =我希望这台机器噪音小一些。

被动语态:
Electricity is generated by power plants. =电力是由发电厂产生的。
A lot of controversy was generated by the politician's speech. =这位政治家的讲话引发了很多争议。

疑问句:
How does this machine generate electricity? =这台机器是如何发电的？
What factors generate customer loyalty? =什么因素能产生客户忠诚度？

否定句:
The experiment did not generate the expected results. =实验没有产生预期的结果。
The campaign failed to generate enough support to win the election. =这场竞选活动未能获得足够的支持以赢得选举。
