hydrozoal:/ˈhaɪdrəzoʊəl/| n. |水螅动物|A hydrozoan is a member of the class Hydrozoa, a large class of mostly marine, invertebrate animals belonging to the phylum Cnidaria.  They are characterized by a relatively simple body plan, often exhibiting both polyp and medusa forms in their life cycle.  Many hydrozoans are colonial, with individual polyps specialized for different functions such as feeding, reproduction, and defense.|Example: The vibrant colors of the coral reef are partly due to the presence of numerous hydrozoan species.|近义词：cnidarian (broader term)|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with animals lacking cnidocytes (stinging cells).|名词
