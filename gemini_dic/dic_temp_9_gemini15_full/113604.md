maceration:/ˌmæsəˈreɪʃən/| n. |浸渍；软化；捣烂|The process of softening or breaking down a substance by soaking it in liquid, often used in cooking, medicine, or other processes.  This often involves the use of water, sometimes with added substances like enzymes or acids.  |例句：The chef used maceration to soften the fruit for his jam. 大厨用浸渍法软化水果做果酱。

例句：The pharmacist prepared the medicine through maceration of herbs. 药剂师通过浸泡草药来制备药物。


近义词：soaking, steeping, infusion,  pounding (for the physical breakdown aspect) |反义词：drying, desiccation


词源: From Latin *macerare "to make thin, to soften," from macer "lean, thin."  The meaning shifted to encompass the process of softening by soaking.
