scoffed:/skɒft/| /ˈskɑːft/| vt.|嘲笑；轻蔑地拒绝；狼吞虎咽|to speak to someone or about something in a way that shows you think it is ridiculous or of no value; to refuse something offered to one in a scornful way; to eat something quickly and eagerly|例句：He scoffed at my suggestion. 他嘲笑我的建议。

She scoffed at the idea of taking a taxi. 她轻蔑地拒绝了坐出租车的建议。


近义词：deride, mock, sneer, jeer, disdain|反义词：praise, admire, applaud, commend|词性:动词

词源:
The word "scoff" comes from the Middle English word "scof," which meant "to mock" or "to jeer."  This in turn has roots in Old Norse and Old English terms with similar meanings.  The sense of "eating quickly and greedily" developed later.
