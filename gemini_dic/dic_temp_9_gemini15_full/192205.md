scratched:/skræʧt/ | v. | 刮擦；抓伤；挠；勾画 | To rub or scrape a surface with something sharp or rough.  |例句：The cat scratched the furniture. 猫抓坏了家具。  |例句：He scratched his head in confusion. 他困惑地挠了挠头。 |近义词：scrape, scratch at, claw, scrape, mar |反义词：polish, smooth


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scratches the table. 他抓桌子。
The dog scratches frequently. 这只狗经常抓挠。

一般过去时:
I scratched my arm yesterday. 我昨天抓伤了我的胳膊。
She scratched the lottery ticket. 她刮开了彩票。

现在完成时:
He has scratched the car. 他已经抓伤了汽车。
The cat has scratched the sofa several times. 猫已经抓过沙发好几次了。

过去完成时:
I had scratched the surface before I realized it was valuable. 我在意识到它很珍贵之前就已经刮花了表面。
They had scratched their heads over the problem for hours. 他们已经为此问题苦思冥想了几个小时。

将来时:
I will scratch the paint off. 我会把油漆刮掉。
She will scratch the itch. 她会挠痒痒。


虚拟语气:
If I scratched the lottery ticket, I might win. 如果我刮开彩票，我可能会中奖。
I wish I hadn't scratched the car. 我真希望我没有刮花车。

被动语态:
The car was scratched by a tree branch. 汽车被树枝刮花了。
The painting was scratched during transportation. 这幅画在运输过程中被刮花了。

疑问句:
Did the dog scratch the couch? 狗抓沙发了吗？
Have you ever scratched a car? 你有没有刮花过车？

否定句:
I didn't scratch the wall. 我没有抓墙。
The cat didn't scratch the carpet. 猫没有抓地毯。

词源:
The word "scratch" comes from the Old English word *scræccean, meaning "to claw" or "to scrape."  This word is related to other Germanic words with similar meanings.  The modern English word retains much of the original meaning and usage.
