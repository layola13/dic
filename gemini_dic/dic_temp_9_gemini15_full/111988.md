lone:/loʊn/| adj.|孤独的，单独的，唯一的| solitary, single, without companions; being the only one of its kind. |例句：She felt lone and isolated in the big city.她在繁华都市中感到孤独和孤立。|例句：He was the lone survivor of the plane crash.他是飞机失事中唯一的幸存者。|近义词：solitary, isolated, single, only |反义词：accompanied, together, numerous


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He feels lone tonight. 他今晚感到孤独。
The lone tree stands on the hill.  那棵孤零零的树矗立在山丘上。

一般过去时:
She felt lone after her friend moved away.  朋友搬走后，她感到孤独。
The lone wolf hunted alone in the forest. 那只孤狼独自在森林里狩猎。

现在完成时:
He has felt lone since his dog died. 自从他的狗死了以后，他就一直感到孤独。
This painting has remained the lone masterpiece of its kind. 这幅画一直是同类作品中唯一的一件杰作。

过去完成时:
She had felt lone before meeting her husband. 在遇到她丈夫之前，她一直感到孤独。
The castle had stood lone for centuries. 这座城堡孤零零地矗立了几个世纪。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish I weren't so lone. 我真希望我不那么孤独。


被动语态:
(Lone is rarely used in the passive voice.)  (孤独很少用被动语态)


疑问句:

否定句:

They are not lone; they are together. 他们并不孤独；他们在一起。
