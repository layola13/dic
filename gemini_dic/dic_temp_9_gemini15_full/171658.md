pulsars:/ˈpʌlsɑːrz/ | n. |脉冲星|A pulsar is a highly magnetized, rotating neutron star that emits beams of electromagnetic radiation.  These beams sweep across the sky like a lighthouse beam, and we detect them as pulses of radiation. Pulsars are formed when massive stars collapse at the end of their lives. |Example: Astronomers discovered a new pulsar emitting radio waves. 天文学家发现了一个发出无线电波的新脉冲星。|近义词：neutron star |反义词：无
