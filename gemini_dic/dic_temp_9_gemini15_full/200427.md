slackers:/ˈslækərz/| n.|懒鬼；游手好闲的人|A slacker is someone who avoids work or responsibilities. They are often perceived as lazy and unproductive.|例句：He's become a total slacker since he lost his job. 他自从失业后就变成了一个十足的懒鬼。|例句：Don't be such a slacker; get your work done! 不要那么懒惰；把你的工作做完！|近义词：loafer, idler, shirker|反义词：hard worker, diligent person


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slacks off at work. = 他在工作中偷懒。
They slack off during the meeting. = 他们在会议期间偷懒。

一般过去时:
He slacked off all afternoon. = 他整个下午都在偷懒。
They slacked off on their studies. = 他们学习懈怠了。

现在完成时:
He has slacked off on his responsibilities. = 他对自己的责任懈怠了。
She has slacked off since the promotion. = 自从升职以来，她工作懈怠了。

过去完成时:
He had slacked off for months before he got fired. = 他在被解雇之前已经偷懒了好几个月了。
They had slacked off on their training. = 他们训练懈怠了。

将来时:
He will slack off if he isn't supervised. = 如果没有人监督，他会偷懒。
They will slack off if the deadline is too far away. = 如果截止日期太远，他们会偷懒。

虚拟语气:
If he weren't such a slacker, he would have finished the project. = 如果他不那么懒惰，他早就完成项目了。
I wish I hadn't slacked off during my studies. = 我真希望我学习期间没有懈怠。

被动语态:
Work is often slacked off by employees. = 工作经常被员工懈怠。
The project was slacked off on by the team. = 项目被团队成员懈怠了。

疑问句:
Are you slacking off on your work? = 你工作懈怠了吗？
Were they slacking off during the presentation? = 他们在演示期间偷懒了吗？

否定句:
He doesn't slack off at work. = 他在工作中不偷懒。
She didn't slack off on her promise. = 她没有违背自己的承诺。


PHRASE->
slack off:  偷懒，放松
slacker mentality: 懒散的心态
chronic slacker: 惯性懒惰者
slacker attitude: 懒散的态度
become a slacker: 变成懒鬼
