camp:/kæmp/ |/kæmp/| n., v.|营地；露营；(尤指政治的)阵营；|A place where people live in tents or other temporary shelters, often for recreation or a specific purpose.  A group of people with shared ideas or beliefs, often political.|例句：We went camping in the mountains last weekend. = 我们上周末去山里露营了。

The political camps are deeply divided on this issue. = 在这个问题上，政治派别严重分裂。|近义词：encampment,  group, faction|反义词：solitude, isolation

一般现在时:
They camp near the river every summer. = 他们每年夏天都在河边露营。
The political party camps against the proposed law. = 该政党阵营反对拟议的法律。


一般过去时:
We camped under the stars last night. = 我们昨晚在星空下露营。
The two groups camped on opposite sides of the issue. = 这两组人对这个问题持截然相反的立场。


现在完成时:
They have camped in this area for many years. = 他们在这个地区露营多年了。
The opposition has camped out in protest against the government's decision.  = 反对派一直在抗议政府的决定。


过去完成时:
They had camped there before, so they knew the area well. = 他们以前在那里露营过，所以他们很了解那个地区。
Before the election, both candidates had camped out in key swing states. = 大选前，两位候选人都扎营在关键的摇摆州。


将来时:
We will camp in Yosemite National Park next month. = 我们下个月将在约塞米蒂国家公园露营。
The media predicts that the two parties will camp on their respective positions during the debate. = 媒体预测，两党将在辩论中坚持各自的立场。


虚拟语气:
If we had more time, we would camp for a longer period. = 如果我们有更多时间，我们会露营更长时间。
If they had camped further away, they might have avoided the noise. = 如果他们扎营远一点，或许就能避免噪音。


被动语态:
The campsite was carefully chosen for its safety and beauty. = 营地是精心挑选的，因为它安全又美丽。
The debate was dominated by the two opposing camps. = 辩论由两个对立阵营主导。


疑问句:
Where will you camp this summer? = 今年夏天你们会在哪里露营？
Which political camp does he belong to? = 他属于哪个政治阵营？


否定句:
We didn't camp last weekend because of the rain. = 我们上周末因为下雨没有去露营。
He doesn't belong to either political camp. = 他不属于任何一个政治阵营。
