scalabilities:/ˌskeɪləˈbɪlətiz/| n. |可扩展性；可伸缩性|The ability of a system, network, or process to handle a growing amount of work, or its potential to be enlarged to accommodate that growth.  This often involves adding resources (e.g., servers, bandwidth, processing power) to meet increased demand without significant performance degradation.|例句：The cloud platform offers excellent scalabilities, allowing us to easily handle increasing user traffic. 云平台具有极佳的可扩展性，使我们能够轻松处理不断增长的用户流量。

The company's new software boasts impressive scalabilities, adapting seamlessly to a growing user base. 该公司的新软件拥有令人印象深刻的可扩展性，能够无缝适应不断增长的用户群。|近义词：extensibility, scalability, expandability |反义词：limited capacity, inflexibility
