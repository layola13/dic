polyp:/ˈpɒlɪp/ /ˈpɑːlɪp/| n. |息肉；水螅；珊瑚虫 |A polyp is a small, soft-bodied animal that usually lives attached to a surface, such as a rock or a coral reef.  Many polyps live in colonies, while some live alone. They are characterized by a simple body structure with a single opening serving as both mouth and anus.  |例句：The diver discovered a vibrant colony of polyps on the coral reef.  潜水员在珊瑚礁上发现了一个充满活力的珊瑚虫群落。

The doctor removed a polyp from his colon during the colonoscopy. 医生在结肠镜检查期间切除了他结肠中的息肉。
