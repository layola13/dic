countryman:/ˈkʌntriˌmæn/| n. |同胞，同乡，乡下人 |A fellow native or inhabitant of one's country; a person from one's own country.  A countryman can refer to someone from the same country, region, or even just sharing a similar rural background.|例句：He felt a strong sense of camaraderie with his countrymen. 他对他的同胞们感到强烈的同志情谊。

例句：The visiting statesman was warmly welcomed by his countrymen.  访问的政治家受到了他同胞们的热烈欢迎。


近义词： compatriot, fellow citizen, native


反义词： foreigner, alien


词源:  The word "countryman" is a compound word. "Country" comes from the Old French "contre," meaning "opposite," and later referring to a region or land. "Man" comes from the Old English "mann," meaning "human being" or "person."  Therefore, "countryman" literally means "man of the country."
