cobweb: /ˈkɒbwɛb/ /ˈkɑːbwɛb/ | n. |蜘蛛网|A web spun by a spider, typically in a corner or other undisturbed place.  It consists of sticky threads used to trap insects.|The old house was filled with cobwebs.  这栋老房子里布满了蜘蛛网。|The attic was covered in thick cobwebs.  阁楼上覆盖着厚厚的蜘蛛网。|近义词：spiderweb, web|反义词：None|名词

词源:
The word "cobweb" comes from the Middle English "coppewebbe," a compound of "coppe" (meaning "spider") and "webbe" (meaning "web").  The "coppe" element is related to Old English "cop," meaning spider.  Thus, the word's origin directly reflects its meaning: a spider's web.
