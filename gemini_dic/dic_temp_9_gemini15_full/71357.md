flacking: /ˈflækɪŋ/ | v. | 大肆宣传；猛烈抨击；吹嘘 |  To promote or publicize aggressively; to criticize harshly.  |  例句：The company was flacking its new product heavily. 公司正在大力宣传其新产品。


例句：The politician was flacking his opponent relentlessly.  这位政客无情地抨击他的对手。


近义词：promoting, publicizing, hyping, slamming, criticizing


反义词：ignoring, praising, supporting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are flacking their new movie. = 他们在大力宣传他们的新电影。
The critics are flacking the author's latest novel. = 批评家们正在猛烈抨击这位作者的最新小说。


一般过去时:
The band flacked their concert on social media. = 乐队在社交媒体上宣传了他们的演唱会。
She flacked her competitor's business practices. = 她抨击了竞争对手的商业行为。

词源:
The word "flack" originates from the slang term for a press agent or publicist.  Its usage as a verb, "flacking," evolved to describe the aggressive or excessive actions of such agents.
