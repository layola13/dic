knocks:/nɒks/| /nɑːks/| vt. n.|敲打；碰撞；批评；打击|To strike a surface with a sharp blow; to hit repeatedly; to criticize severely; a sudden, unexpected impact or event.|例句：He knocks on the door. 他敲门。
He knocks on the door three times. 他敲了三次门。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He knocks on the door every morning. 他每天早上都敲门。
She knocks over the vase accidentally. 她不小心碰倒了花瓶。

一般过去时:
The boxer knocked out his opponent in the first round. 拳击手在第一回合击倒了他的对手。
The storm knocked down several trees. 暴风雨吹倒了几棵树。

现在完成时:
The continuous rain has knocked down the power lines.  持续的降雨已经导致电力线中断。
The scandal has knocked his reputation.  丑闻损害了他的名誉。

过去完成时:
The earthquake had knocked down many buildings before the rescue team arrived. 在救援队到达之前，地震已经摧毁了许多建筑物。
They had knocked off early to celebrate the holiday. 他们提前下班去庆祝节日。

将来时:
The wind will probably knock down the fence. 风很可能会把栅栏吹倒。
I think the bad news will knock her down. 我觉得这个坏消息会把她击垮。

虚拟语气:
If he had knocked harder, someone would have answered the door. 如果他敲得更用力一些，就会有人开门。
I wish the storm hadn't knocked down the tree in my garden. 我希望暴风雨没有把我家花园里的树吹倒。

被动语态:
The door was knocked loudly. 门被大声敲响了。
The vase was knocked over by the cat. 花瓶被猫打翻了。

疑问句:
Who knocked at the door? 谁敲门？
What knocked that picture off the wall? 什么东西把那幅画从墙上撞掉了？


否定句:
He didn't knock before entering the room. 他没有敲门就进来了。
The argument didn't knock her off her stride.  那场争论并没有让她失去节奏。


PHRASE->
knock down:  拆除；击倒
knock off:  下班；快速完成
knock out:  击倒；使失去知觉
knock up:  敲醒；使筋疲力尽
knock on wood:  （为了避免厄运）祈祷，轻敲木头


近义词：rap, strike, hit, thump, impact

反义词：push, pull


词性：动词 (vt.)  名词 (n.)
