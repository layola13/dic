preinscribing: /ˌpriːɪnˈskraɪbɪŋ/ | v. | 预先铭刻；预先规定 |  The act of writing or marking something beforehand; to prescribe or determine something in advance.  This word is quite rare and mainly found in specialized contexts.  It implies a prior action of inscription or determination. |


例句：

The treaty preinscribed the boundaries of the newly formed nation. = 条约预先规定了新成立国家的边界。

The architect preinscribed the building's structural features before construction began. = 建筑师在施工开始前预先规定了建筑物的结构特征。



近义词：predesignate, preordain, predetermine


反义词：postscribe (though not a direct opposite, it signifies writing after, rather than before)


词性: 动词
