savaging:/ˈsævɪdʒɪŋ/| n. v.| 猛烈抨击；粗暴对待；严重破坏 | The act of violently attacking or criticizing something or someone;  to severely damage or destroy something. |例句：The critics savaged his latest novel. 批评家们猛烈抨击了他的最新小说。

The storm savaged the coastline.  暴风雨严重破坏了海岸线。|近义词：attack, assault, criticize, demolish, devastate |反义词：praise, compliment, protect, preserve, repair


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The critics savage his every performance. 批评家们对他每一次演出都进行猛烈抨击。
He savages his opponents in every debate. 他在每一次辩论中都猛烈抨击他的对手。


一般过去时:
The reviewers savaged the new film. 影评人猛烈抨击了这部新电影。
The hurricane savaged the coastal towns. 飓风摧毁了沿海城镇。


现在完成时:
The media has savaged the politician's reputation. 媒体已经严重损毁了这位政治家的名誉。
The years have savaged the old house. 岁月已经严重损坏了这座老房子。


过去完成时:
The public had savaged the company's image before the crisis. 在危机爆发前，公众就已经严重损毁了该公司的形象。
The vandals had savaged the park before the police arrived.  破坏分子在警察到达之前已经严重破坏了公园。


将来时:
The press will savage the government's new policy.  媒体将会猛烈抨击政府的新政策。
The storm is expected to savage the region. 预计风暴将严重破坏该地区。


虚拟语气:
If they released that statement, the public would savage them. 如果他们发布那份声明，公众将会猛烈抨击他们。
I wish they hadn't savaged the antique furniture. 我真希望他们没有毁坏那些古董家具。


被动语态:
His latest book was savaged by the reviewers. 他的最新著作遭到评论家的猛烈抨击。
The forest was savaged by the fire. 森林被大火严重破坏。


疑问句:
Was his reputation savaged by the scandal? 他的名誉是否被丑闻严重损毁？
Will the building be savaged by the earthquake? 这座建筑物会在地震中被严重破坏吗？


否定句:
The audience didn't savage the performance; they actually enjoyed it. 观众并没有猛烈抨击这场演出；他们实际上很享受。
The storm didn't savage the town as much as they feared. 风暴并没有像他们担心的那样严重破坏小镇。
