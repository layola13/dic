prechoosing: /ˌpriːˈtʃuːzɪŋ/ | v. | 预先选择，预先挑选 | The act of choosing something in advance.  It implies a selection made before a specific need or event. | 例句：The committee was busy prechoosing the candidates for the award. 委员会正忙于预先挑选获奖候选人。

The company's prechoosing of suppliers ensured a smooth production process.  公司的预先选择供应商确保了生产过程的顺利进行。 | 近义词：preselecting, selecting beforehand, advance selection | 反义词：post-selecting, choosing afterward

词源:

The word "prechoosing" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the verb "choosing."  It's a relatively modern word formed by adding the prefix to an existing verb, reflecting the growing need to describe actions performed in advance of another event.
