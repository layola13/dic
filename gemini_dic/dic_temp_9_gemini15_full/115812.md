marked-down:/mɑːrktˈdaʊn/| adj.|打折的，减价的|Reduced in price.  The adjective "marked-down" describes items that have had their price reduced, typically in a sale.|例句：The marked-down clothes were a steal.  打折的衣服真便宜。|例句：Many marked-down items are available during the holiday season.  节日期间有很多打折商品。|近义词：discounted, reduced, sale-priced |反义词：full-priced, original-priced


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The store marks down its summer clothes. = 这家商店正在打折夏季服装。
These items are marked down for the sale. = 这些商品为促销而降价。

一般过去时:
They marked down the price of the TV. = 他们降低了电视机的价格。
The shop marked down all its winter coats last week. = 商店上周把所有冬衣都打折了。

现在完成时:
The prices have been marked down significantly. = 价格已经大幅下降。
The store has marked down all its inventory. = 商店已经对所有库存商品进行了降价。

过去完成时:
The item had been marked down before I saw it. = 我看到那件商品之前，它就已经打折了。
They had marked down the price several times before it finally sold. = 在最终售出之前，他们已经几次降价了。

将来时:
They will mark down the prices next week. = 他们下周将降价。
The store will mark down its seasonal items soon. = 商店很快就会对其季节性商品进行降价。

虚拟语气:
If they marked down the price, I would buy it. = 如果他们降价，我会买。
I wish they had marked down the price sooner. = 我希望他们早点降价。

被动语态:
The prices were marked down by 20%. = 价格降低了20%。
The items are being marked down for the clearance sale. = 这些商品正在为清仓销售而降价。

疑问句:
Were the prices marked down? = 价格下降了吗？
Have the items been marked down yet? = 商品已经打折了吗？

否定句:
The prices were not marked down. = 价格没有下降。
They didn't mark down the price enough. = 他们降价幅度不够。
