cirrhosis:/səˈroʊsɪs/| n. |肝硬化|A late stage of scarring (fibrosis) of the liver caused by many forms of liver diseases and conditions.  It is characterized by the replacement of normal liver tissue with scar tissue, which blocks the flow of blood through the liver and prevents the liver from functioning properly.|Example:  His years of heavy drinking led to cirrhosis of the liver.  他多年的酗酒导致肝硬化。


The excessive alcohol consumption eventually resulted in cirrhosis.  过量饮酒最终导致肝硬化。|近义词：liver disease, liver failure, hepatic fibrosis|反义词：healthy liver, normal liver function

词源:

The word "cirrhosis" comes from the Greek word "kirrhos," meaning "yellowish," referring to the yellowish discoloration of the liver often associated with the condition.  The suffix "-osis" is a Greek suffix indicating a disease or abnormal condition.
