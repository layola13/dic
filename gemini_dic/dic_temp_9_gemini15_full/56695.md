doomed:/duːmd/| adj.| 注定的，命定的；失败的，注定要失败的 | Certain to fail, suffer, die, or experience something unpleasant.  It implies an unavoidable negative outcome. |例句：The project was doomed from the start.  这个项目从一开始就注定要失败。

The company's doomed to bankruptcy if they don't change their strategy.  如果他们不改变策略，这家公司注定要破产。|近义词：fated, destined, condemned, accursed |反义词：blessed, fortunate, lucky


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project is doomed. = 这个项目注定要失败。
He is doomed to fail. = 他注定要失败。


一般过去时:
The plan was doomed from the outset. = 这个计划从一开始就注定要失败。
The ship was doomed to sink. = 那艘船注定要沉没。


现在完成时:
The relationship has been doomed since the beginning. = 这段关系从一开始就注定要失败。
His career has been doomed by his own mistakes. = 他的事业因他自己的错误而毁了。


过去完成时:
We had already realised the project was doomed. = 我们已经意识到这个项目注定要失败。
They had been doomed to poverty from birth. = 他们从出生就注定要贫困。


将来时:
The city will be doomed if the dam breaks. = 如果大坝决堤，这座城市将面临毁灭。
The mission is doomed to fail without reinforcements. = 没有增援，任务注定会失败。


虚拟语气:
If he continued this way, he would be doomed. = 如果他继续这样下去，他将注定失败。
I wish they weren't doomed. = 我希望他们不会失败。


被动语态:
The town was doomed by the flood. = 这个城镇被洪水摧毁了。
The mission was doomed to failure. = 任务注定要失败。


疑问句:
Is the plan doomed? = 这个计划注定要失败吗？
Are we doomed? = 我们注定要失败吗？


否定句:
The project is not doomed, yet. =  这个项目现在还没有注定失败。
He is not necessarily doomed. = 他不一定会失败。


PHRASE->
be doomed to: 注定要...
doomed from the start: 从一开始就注定要失败
doomed to failure: 注定要失败
a doomed enterprise: 注定失败的事业
doomed love: 注定的悲恋
