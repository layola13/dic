graybearded: /ˈɡreɪˈbɪərdɪd/ | adj. | 灰白胡须的，长着灰白胡子的 | Having a gray or white beard.  The term describes someone with a beard that is gray or white, often implying age and wisdom or sometimes a gruff appearance. | 例句：The graybearded old man sat by the fire, telling stories to the children.  灰白胡须的老人坐在火炉旁，给孩子们讲故事。

The graybearded professor lectured on ancient history.  这位灰白胡须的教授正在讲授古代历史课。


近义词：white-bearded, silver-bearded | 反义词：beardless, young


词性: 形容词


词源:  The word is a compound of "gray" (meaning grey or grayish-white) and "bearded" (meaning having a beard).  "Gray" originates from Old English *grāg,  while "bearded" derives from "beard" (Old English *beard),  ultimately from Proto-Germanic *bartą.
