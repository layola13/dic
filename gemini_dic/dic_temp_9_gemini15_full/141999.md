organising: /ˈɔːɡənaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |组织；筹备 |The act of organizing something.  It involves arranging, coordinating, and managing resources to achieve a specific goal.|例句：She's organising a fundraising event for the charity. 她正在为慈善机构组织一场募捐活动。|近义词：arranging, planning, coordinating |反义词：disorganising, disrupting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is organising a conference next month. 他下个月正在组织一个会议。
They are organising a trip to Europe. 他们在组织一次欧洲之旅。

一般过去时:
She organised a surprise party for her friend. 她为她的朋友举办了一个惊喜派对。
We organised the books by author. 我们按作者整理书籍。

现在完成时:
They have organised everything for the wedding. 他们已经为婚礼安排好了一切。
I have organised my schedule for the week. 我已经安排好了本周的日程。

过去完成时:
She had organised the meeting before he arrived.  在她到达之前，她已经组织好了会议。
They had organised the logistics before the trip began. 旅行开始之前，他们已经安排好了后勤工作。

将来时:
He will organise a team meeting next week. 他下周将组织一次团队会议。
We will organise a picnic in the park. 我们将在公园组织一次野餐。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would organise a bigger party. 如果我有更多时间，我会组织一个更大的派对。
I wish I had organised my notes better. 我希望我笔记整理得更好。

被动语态:
The event was organised by a local committee.  这个活动是由当地委员会组织的。
The meeting is being organised right now. 会议正在组织中。

疑问句:
Are you organising the party? 你在组织派对吗？
Who is organising the project? 谁在组织这个项目？

否定句:
I'm not organising anything this weekend. 我这个周末什么也不组织。
He didn't organise the trip properly. 他没有妥善组织这次旅行。
