remarks:/rɪˈmɑːrks/| n.|评论；意见；谈话；备注|Comments, observations, or statements expressing an opinion or reaction.  The speaker's remarks were met with applause.|近义词：comments, observations, statements, opinions|反义词：silence


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His remarks often cause controversy. = 他的言论经常引起争议。
Her remarks are always insightful. = 她的评论总是很有见地。


一般过去时:
The judge made some critical remarks about the defendant. = 法官对被告做了一些批评性评价。
He offered some helpful remarks on my presentation. = 他就我的演讲提出了一些有益的建议。


现在完成时:
She has made several remarks about the new policy. = 她对新政策发表了几点意见。
The committee has issued a statement with further remarks. = 委员会发表了一份包含进一步评论的声明。


过去完成时:
He had made several remarks before the meeting ended. = 会议结束前他已经发表了几点意见。
They had already exchanged remarks before I joined them. = 我加入他们之前他们已经互相交流过意见了。


将来时:
She will make her remarks at the end of the meeting. = 她将在会议结束时发表讲话。
The president will make important remarks concerning the economy. = 总统将就经济问题发表重要讲话。


虚拟语气:
If he had made different remarks, the outcome might have been better. = 如果他发表不同的言论，结果可能会更好。
I wish he had made more positive remarks. = 我希望他发表更多积极的评论。


被动语态:

The remarks were misinterpreted by the media. = 媒体误解了他的言论。


疑问句:

What remarks did she make about the situation? = 她对这种情况有什么看法？


否定句:
