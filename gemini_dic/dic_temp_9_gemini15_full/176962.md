reallocating:/ˌriːˈæləkeɪtɪŋ/| v.|重新分配，重新配置 |The act of assigning or distributing something again.  It often implies a change in the original allocation, perhaps due to new priorities or circumstances. |例句：The company is reallocating resources to its new product line. 公司正在将其资源重新分配给新的产品线。
例句：The government is reallocating funds to address the growing homelessness crisis. 政府正在重新分配资金以解决日益严重的无家可归危机。|近义词：reassigning, redistributing, reallotment|反义词：allocating (in its initial sense), concentrating


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are reallocating funds to the marketing department. = 他们正在将资金重新分配给市场部。
We reallocate resources based on performance. = 我们根据业绩重新分配资源。

一般过去时:
The manager reallocated the tasks among the team members. = 经理将任务重新分配给了团队成员。
They reallocated the budget after reviewing the project's progress. = 他们在审查项目进度后重新分配了预算。

现在完成时:
We have reallocated the server resources to improve performance. = 我们已经重新分配了服务器资源以提高性能。
The company has reallocated its staff to meet the increased demand. = 公司已经重新分配了员工以满足日益增长的需求。

过去完成时:
They had reallocated the funds before the project started. = 项目开始前他们已经重新分配了资金。
The team had reallocated their priorities after the emergency. = 紧急情况发生后，团队重新调整了优先级。

将来时:
We will reallocate the budget next quarter. = 我们将在下个季度重新分配预算。
The government will reallocate funds to disaster relief efforts. = 政府将重新分配资金用于救灾工作。

虚拟语气:
If we had more resources, we would reallocate them to research and development. = 如果我们有更多资源，我们会将其重新分配给研发。
It would be helpful if they reallocated some of their staff. = 如果他们重新分配一些员工，将会有所帮助。

被动语态:
