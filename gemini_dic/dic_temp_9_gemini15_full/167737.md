priggish:/ˈprɪɡɪʃ/| adj.|自以为是的；假道学的；装腔作势的 |Someone who is priggish is annoyingly self-righteous and superior. They often act in a way that shows they think they are morally better than other people.  They may be overly concerned with proper behavior and etiquette. |例句：He's a bit priggish, always correcting people's grammar. 他有点自以为是，总是纠正别人的语法。

例句：She found his priggish attitude irritating. 她觉得他自以为是的态度很烦人。


近义词：self-righteous, sanctimonious, smug, holier-than-thou, affected

反义词：humble, modest, unassuming, down-to-earth


词源：源于19世纪早期，可能与“prig”（爱卖弄小聪明的人）有关。  “Prig”的词源尚不明确，但可能与古法语或中古英语中的某个词有关，暗示着夸夸其谈或炫耀的行为。
