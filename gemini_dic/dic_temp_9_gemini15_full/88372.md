hipsters:/ˈhɪp.stɚz/ | n. |嬉皮士，潮人 |  A hipster is a subculture member who rejects mainstream or commercial culture.  They often adopt a distinctive style of dress, music, and art, valuing independent and alternative expressions. Hipsters are known for their ironic detachment, appreciation of vintage and retro items, and an interest in independent coffee shops, craft beer, and indie music.  The term can be used both descriptively and pejoratively, often implying a certain level of pretentiousness or affectation. | 例句：Many young people identify as hipsters, embracing independent music and vintage fashion.  许多年轻人认为自己是嬉皮士，他们喜欢独立音乐和复古时尚。

The term "hipster" originally referred to hep cats of the 1940s, who were known for their cool, rebellious attitude. The word "hip" comes from a Black American English slang term, possibly derived from the phrase "be hip to" (be aware of, understand).  The modern usage of "hipster" emerged in the early 2000s, signifying a new wave of youth counterculture. |近义词：indie, alternative, bohemian, trendy |反义词：mainstream, conventional, conformist
