panpsychists:/ˈpænˈsaɪkɪsts/| n. |泛心论者|Believers in panpsychism, a philosophical viewpoint that suggests consciousness is a fundamental and ubiquitous property of the universe, present in all matter to varying degrees. Panpsychists argue that consciousness is not an emergent property of complex systems, but rather an intrinsic feature of reality itself.  |  Many panpsychists believe that even seemingly inanimate objects possess some form of rudimentary consciousness. |近义词:  universalists (in a philosophical context), idealists (depending on the specific panpsychist view) |反义词:materialists, physicalists
