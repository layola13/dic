rapport:/ræˈpɔːr/| n. |友好关系；和谐；融洽| a close and harmonious relationship in which the people or groups concerned understand each other's feelings or ideas and communicate well.  |例句：The teacher established a good rapport with her students.  老师和学生建立了良好的师生关系。

例句：Building a strong rapport with clients is crucial for success in sales.  与客户建立良好关系对于销售成功至关重要。


近义词：harmony, understanding, affinity, connection, bond |反义词：estrangement, antagonism, discord, conflict, tension |词性：名词


词源：法语，源自拉丁语 *rapporter “to bring back, report”, from ad- “to” + *rapportare “to bring near, relate”


USAGE->

We maintain a good rapport with our suppliers. = 我们与供应商保持着良好的关系。
They have a strong rapport with their customers. = 他们与客户有着牢固的关系。
