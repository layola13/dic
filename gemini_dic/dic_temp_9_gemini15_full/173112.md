quasi-absolutely:/ˈkwɑːziˌæbsəluːtli/ | adv. |几乎绝对地，近乎绝对地 |  The adverb "quasi-absolutely" means almost or nearly absolutely; it suggests a high degree of certainty or truth, but not complete, unqualified certainty. It implies a qualification or slight reservation. |  The evidence quasi-absolutely proves his guilt.  证据几乎绝对证明了他的罪行。  The experiment's results quasi-absolutely confirm the hypothesis. 实验结果几乎完全证实了该假设。| 近义词: almost absolutely, virtually absolutely, practically absolutely | 反义词: conditionally, relatively, doubtfully |  无


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It doesn't have tenses or moods.  Its usage is primarily in declarative sentences to modify a verb or adjective.

Declarative Sentences:
The judge ruled quasi-absolutely in favor of the plaintiff. = 法官几乎绝对地支持原告。
His testimony was quasi-absolutely convincing. = 他的证词几乎绝对令人信服。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases specifically using "quasi-absolutely."  Its usage is fairly limited to academic or formal writing.
