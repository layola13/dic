promorphology:/ˌproʊmɔːrˈfɒlədʒi/ | n. | 前形态学 | The study of the earliest stages of morphological development, often focusing on the pre-linguistic or proto-linguistic aspects of form and meaning.  It explores how basic concepts and structures might have emerged before fully developed language systems.  This field frequently overlaps with other disciplines like evolutionary linguistics, cognitive science, and developmental psychology. | Example: Research in promorphology attempts to reconstruct the pre-linguistic communication systems that may have preceded the emergence of modern human language.  | 近义词:protolinguistics, prelinguistic communication studies | 反义词:None directly applicable, but contrasts with mature morphology studies. | noun
