/priːˈkæptʃərz/ | n. | 预先捕获；预先捕捉 | The term "precaptures" refers to instances where something is captured or recorded before a specific event or process.  It often implies a proactive measure to gather data or information in anticipation of future needs.  This term is not widely used and is often found in specialized contexts, such as computer science or data analysis.  | Example: The system performs precaptures of network traffic to facilitate later analysis of security incidents.  系统执行网络流量预先捕获以方便以后的安全事件分析。 | 近义词: pre-recording, advance capture, prior acquisition | 反义词: post-capture, retrospective capture | 名词
