fallowness's:/ˈfæləʊnəsɪz/| n. |休耕状态的；荒芜的；贫瘠的；不活跃的|The fallowness of the field was evident in the lack of crops. 田地的休耕状态显而易见，因为没有庄稼。|近义词：barrenness, sterility, inactivity |反义词：fertility, productivity, activity

英文解析：Fallowness refers to the state of being fallow, meaning left unplanted or uncultivated for a period of time to allow the soil to regain fertility. It can also be used figuratively to describe a period of inactivity, dormancy, or lack of productivity in other contexts.  The 's' at the end indicates possessive case, meaning it belongs to or is associated with fallowness.  For example, "the field's fallowness" indicates the fallowness of a specific field.


例句：

The fallowness's impact on soil health was significant. 休耕状态对土壤健康的影响是显著的。

The artist's fallowness lasted for several years before his inspiration returned.  这位艺术家的创作低潮持续了好几年，直到他的灵感回归。


词源：

From fallow + -ness (suffix denoting state or condition).  "Fallow" itself comes from Old English *fealu "pale yellow, reddish-yellow,"  later referring to the pale, reddish-yellow color of uncultivated land.  The connection is the color of the land left to rest.


用法说明：


由于fallowness's本身是名词所有格形式，因此其用法主要体现在所有格的用法上：


表示所属：The farm's fallowness was a temporary measure.  (农场休耕的状态是一种临时措施。)
表示关联：The project's fallowness signaled a lack of funding. (项目的停滞不前预示着资金不足。)


需要注意的是，由于fallowness本身并不常用，其所有格形式fallowness's更少见。  在大多数情况下，可以使用更简洁的表达方式，例如将所有格改为介词短语：The fallowness of the farm... (农场的休耕状态...)
