preassurances:/ˌpriːəˈʃʊərənsɪz/| n.| 事先保证；预先保证 |  Prior assurances or guarantees given before an event or action.  These are promises or guarantees made in advance to alleviate concerns or build confidence.  They are not necessarily legally binding. |例句：The company offered preassurances of safety before the launch of the new product. = 在新产品发布之前，该公司提供了安全方面的预先保证。

The government gave preassurances that the project would be completed on time. = 政府预先保证该项目将按时完成。


近义词：prior guarantees, advance assurances, prior commitments | 反义词：post-event assurances, after-the-fact guarantees


词性：名词
