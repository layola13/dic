overstoring:/ˌoʊvərˈstɔːrɪŋ/ | v. |过度储存；囤积 |The act of storing excessive amounts of goods, often beyond what is needed or can be effectively managed. This can lead to waste, spoilage, and increased storage costs.  |例句：The company's overstoring of inventory led to significant losses due to product expiration. 公司过度储存库存导致产品过期造成重大损失。
例句：Overstoring food during times of plenty can prevent shortages later, but careful planning is essential to avoid spoilage.  丰收时节储存过多的食物可以防止以后的短缺，但必须仔细规划以避免腐烂。|近义词：hoarding, stockpiling, accumulation |反义词：understocking, depletion
