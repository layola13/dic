inaugurates:/ɪˈnɔːɡjʊreɪts/| vt.| 开始；为…举行就职典礼；开创；创立 | To begin something officially or formally; to install someone into an office or position with a formal ceremony.  |例句：The president inaugurates a new era of peace. 总统开启了一个和平的新时代。
The new mayor was inaugurated yesterday. 新市长昨天就职了。|近义词：begin, commence, initiate, install|反义词：conclude, end, terminate


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The ceremony inaugurates the new museum. = 典礼为新博物馆揭幕。
He inaugurates a new project every year. = 他每年都会启动一个新的项目。


一般过去时:
They inaugurated the new bridge last week. = 他们上周为新桥举行了开通仪式。
The company inaugurated its new headquarters. = 公司为其新的总部举行了落成典礼。


现在完成时:
The government has inaugurated several new policies. = 政府已经启动了几项新政策。
She has inaugurated a new chapter in her life. = 她已经开启了她人生的新篇章。


过去完成时:
They had inaugurated the building before the earthquake. = 地震发生前，他们已经为大楼举行了落成典礼。
Before the speech, he had inaugurated the conference. = 在演讲之前，他已经为会议致开幕词。


将来时:
The university will inaugurate the new academic year next month. = 大学将于下个月开学。
They will inaugurate a new statue next week. = 他们下周将为一座新雕像举行揭幕仪式。


虚拟语气:
If they inaugurated the project earlier, it would have been completed by now. = 如果他们早点启动这个项目，它现在就已经完成了。
I wish they had inaugurated the program sooner. = 我真希望他们能早点启动这个项目。


被动语态:
The museum was inaugurated by the mayor. = 博物馆由市长揭幕。
The new law will be inaugurated next month. = 新法令将于下个月正式实施。


疑问句:
When will the new stadium be inaugurated? = 新体育馆什么时候启用？
Who will inaugurate the conference? = 谁将为会议致开幕词？


否定句:
The project was not yet inaugurated. = 项目尚未启动。
They did not inaugurate the new building on time. = 他们没有按时为新大楼举行落成典礼。
