rechanneled:/ˌriːˈtʃænld/ | v. |重新引导；重新分配；改变方向 | To redirect or divert something, typically a flow of something such as water, funds, or information, into a new channel or course.  | Example: The river was rechanneled to prevent flooding. 河流被重新引导以防止洪水。 The company rechanneled its resources into research and development. 公司将其资源重新分配到研发部门。|近义词：redirect, reroute, divert |反义词：obstruct, block


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company rechannels its profits into new projects. = 公司将利润重新投入新的项目。
He rechannels his energy into his work. = 他将精力重新投入到工作中。


一般过去时:
They rechanneled the water flow to irrigate the fields. = 他们重新引导水流来灌溉田地。
The government rechanneled funds to support education. = 政府重新分配资金以支持教育。
