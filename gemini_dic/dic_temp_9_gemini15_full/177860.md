receptor:/rɪˈsɛptər/ | n. |感受器；受体；接受器 | A specialized cell or group of cells that receives stimuli from the external or internal environment and transmits signals to sensory nerves or other cells.  Receptors are crucial for various biological processes, including vision, hearing, taste, smell, and touch, as well as hormone action and neurotransmission. | Example:  The receptor cells in the eye detect light.  眼睛里的感光细胞探测光线。

The pain receptors in the skin send signals to the brain. 皮肤中的疼痛感受器向大脑发送信号。

|近义词：sensor, receiver | 反义词：transmitter, effector | 名词
