impetuousness: /ɪmˈpɛtʃuəsnəs/ | n. | 冲动；鲁莽；性急 | The quality or state of being impetuous; rashness; impulsiveness.  It often involves acting on a sudden urge without considering the consequences. |

例句：

Her impetuousness led her to quit her job without having another one lined up. = 她的冲动让她在没有找到另一份工作的情况下辞职了。

His impetuousness in investing caused him significant financial losses. = 他投资时的鲁莽给他带来了巨大的经济损失。


近义词：impulsiveness, rashness, recklessness, hastiness, precipitateness

反义词：deliberation, carefulness, thoughtfulness, caution


词源:

The word "impetuousness" is derived from the Latin word "impetus," meaning "attack," "force," or "impulse."  The suffix "-ous" creates the adjective "impetuous," meaning "characterized by sudden force or passion."  The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective into the noun "impetuousness," signifying the quality or state of being impetuous.


用法说明:

Impetuousness is typically used to describe behavior characterized by sudden and forceful action, often without careful thought or planning. It carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting a lack of control and potential for negative consequences.  It's often applied to actions, decisions, or even personality traits.
