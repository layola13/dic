inelastic:/ˌɪnɪˈlæstɪk/| adj. |非弹性的，缺乏弹性的；无弹力的|Not easily altered or changed; inflexible.  Describes something that does not readily respond to changes in price or other economic factors.|The demand for gasoline is relatively inelastic; even if prices rise, people still need to buy it to drive their cars.|近义词：rigid, inflexible, unyielding, stiff|反义词：elastic, flexible, pliable

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The demand for salt is inelastic. = 食盐的需求是非弹性的。
This material is inelastic and doesn't stretch. = 这种材料是非弹性的，不会伸展。


一般过去时:
The price increase had little effect because demand was inelastic. = 价格上涨影响很小，因为需求是非弹性的。
The economy remained inelastic during the recession. = 在经济衰退期间，经济仍然缺乏弹性。

词源:
The word "inelastic" comes from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") and the word "elastic," which itself derives from the Greek word "elastikos" (meaning "capable of being drawn out").  Therefore, "inelastic" literally means "not capable of being drawn out" or "not easily stretched or altered."
