clanking:/ˈklæŋkɪŋ/| v. n. adj.|铿锵声，当啷声；发出铿锵声的 |The sound of metal hitting metal, often repeatedly and loudly.|例句：The chain clanked as the gate swung open.  链条在门打开时发出当啷声。
The clanking of the machinery was deafening. 机器铿锵作响，震耳欲聋。


近义词：rattling, banging, clattering | 反义词：silent, quiet | 词性:动词，名词，形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The pots and pans are clanking in the kitchen. 锅碗瓢盆在厨房里叮当作响。
The old engine clanks constantly. 这台旧发动机一直发出当当的响声。


一般过去时:
The metal bucket clanked against the stone wall. 金属桶撞击石墙发出当啷声。
The train clanked along the tracks. 火车沿着铁轨哐当哐当的行驶。


现在完成时:
The wind has been clanking the loose shutters all night.  风整夜都在使松动的百叶窗发出哐当声。
The old bell has clanked countless times over the years. 这口旧钟多年来敲响了无数次。


过去完成时:
The workers had clanked the heavy machinery into place before the inspection. 工人们在检查前已将重型机械哐当哐当地安装到位。
The storm had clanked the loose window panes all night.暴风雨整夜都在使松动的窗格发出哐当声。


将来时:
The construction will be clanking and banging all day tomorrow.  明天的施工将会整天发出哐当和砰砰的声响。
The new machinery will likely clank when it starts up. 新机器启动时可能会发出当当声。


虚拟语气:

词源:
The word "clank" comes from  the Middle English word "clanken," which is an imitative word, meaning it's a word that sounds like the noise it describes.  This in turn is likely related to words of Germanic origin describing similar sounds.
