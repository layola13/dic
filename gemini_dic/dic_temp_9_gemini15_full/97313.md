informant:/ɪnˈfɔːrmənt/ | n. |线人，告密者 | A person who gives secret information to another person or group, especially to the police or a government. |The informant provided crucial evidence that led to the arrest of the criminal.  线人提供了导致罪犯被捕的关键证据。| spy, whistleblower | accomplice, confidant | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The informant provides valuable intelligence to the police. 线人向警方提供宝贵的情報。
He often acts as an informant for the government. 他经常充当政府的线人。


一般过去时:
The informant gave the police a detailed description of the suspect. 线人向警方提供了嫌疑人的详细描述。
She was a key informant in the investigation. 她是调查中的关键线人。


现在完成时:
The informant has already given all the information he possesses. 线人已经提供了所有他掌握的信息。
The police have relied on this informant for years. 警方多年来一直依赖这位线人。


过去完成时:
The police had already received information from the informant before they raided the building. 警方在突击搜查大楼之前就已经收到了线人的情报。
They had known the informant for a long time before he betrayed them. 在他背叛他们之前，他们已经认识这位线人很久了。


将来时:
The informant will testify in court next week. 线人下周将在法庭上作证。
He will continue to provide information to the authorities. 他将继续向当局提供信息。


虚拟语气:
If the informant hadn't come forward, the case would have been unsolved. 如果线人没有站出来，这个案子就不会被侦破。
I wish the informant had been more reliable. 我希望线人更可靠一些。


被动语态:
The informant was threatened by the criminals. 线人受到罪犯的威胁。
The information was provided by a reliable informant. 信息是由一个可靠的线人提供的。


疑问句:
Who is the informant? 线人是谁？
Is the informant trustworthy? 线人可靠吗？


否定句:
He wasn't a willing informant; he was forced to cooperate. 他不是自愿的线人；他是被迫合作的。
She is not a known informant to the police. 她不是警方已知的线人。
