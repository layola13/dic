词源:
The word is formed from the prefix "over-" (meaning excessively or to a degree exceeding what is normal or desirable),  "obedient" (meaning willing to obey), and the adverbial suffix "-ly".  "Obedient" itself comes from the Old French *obeissant*, from Latin *obēdiēns*, present participle of *obēdīre* "to obey," from *ob- "to" + *audīre* "to hear, obey."
