/ˈkaʊntərˌwɔːk/ | n. |逆行；反向行走 |A counter-walk is the act of walking in the opposite direction to the usual or expected flow of traffic or movement.  It can refer to walking against the flow of people on a sidewalk, or against traffic in a designated walking area.  The term is often used in specific contexts, such as in pedestrian traffic management. |例句：Walking against the flow of people on the crowded street was a counter-walk and quite risky.  逆着拥挤街道上的人群行走是一种逆行，而且相当危险。|近义词：backtrack, retrograde movement |反义词：follow, conform to the flow
