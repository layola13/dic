megalomaniacs:/ˌmɛɡəlɵˈmeɪniˌæks/| n. |妄自尊大狂；夸大狂；自大狂|People with megalomania suffer from a delusion of grandeur, an inflated sense of their own importance and power. They often exhibit narcissistic traits and a disregard for the feelings and needs of others.  The term is often used informally and can be derogatory.|例句：The dictator's megalomaniacal tendencies led to a devastating war.  独裁者的狂妄自大导致了一场毁灭性的战争。

The company's CEO, with his megalomaniacal schemes, nearly bankrupted the firm. 公司的首席执行官，凭借其狂妄自大的计划，几乎使公司破产。


近义词：egomaniacs, narcissists, narcissist, megalomaniacs,  delusional, grandiose


反义词：humble, modest, self-effacing


词性:名词


词源:
The word "megalomaniac" is derived from the Greek words "mega" (meaning "great" or "large") and "mania" (meaning "madness" or "insanity").  The suffix "-iac" indicates a person suffering from a particular condition. Therefore, a megalomaniac is literally someone who is "mad with greatness," or who suffers from a delusion of grandeur.
