ragging:/ˈræɡɪŋ/| v.|戏弄；取笑；严厉批评；(尤指大学新生)欺凌|To tease or bully someone, especially playfully.  It can also refer to harsh criticism or severe reprimand. In a university context, it often refers to the hazing of freshmen.|例句：The older boys were ragging the new student.  (那些大男孩在戏弄新生。)  The professor's ragging really demoralized the students. (教授的严厉批评真的让学生们士气低落。) |近义词：teasing, bullying, heckling, reprimanding |反义词：praising, encouraging, supporting |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are ragging him mercilessly. = 他们正在无情地戏弄他。
He rags his little brother all the time. = 他总是戏弄他弟弟。


一般过去时:
They ragged him about his new haircut. = 他们取笑他新的发型。
The older students ragged the freshmen relentlessly. = 高年级的学生无情地欺凌新生。

过去完成时:
