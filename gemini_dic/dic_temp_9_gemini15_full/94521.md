impose:/ɪmˈpoʊz/ | vt. | 强加；施加；征税 | To force (something unwelcome or unfamiliar) to be accepted or put in place.  |例句：The government imposed a new tax on fuel. 政府对燃油征收新税。|例句：Don't impose your views on others. 不要把你的观点强加于他人。|近义词：enforce, inflict, levy |反义词：remove, abolish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They impose strict rules on students. = 他们对学生实行严格的规定。
The government imposes high taxes. = 政府征收高额税款。

一般过去时:
The teacher imposed a punishment on the student. = 老师惩罚了那个学生。
The company imposed a new dress code. = 公司实施了新的着装规范。

现在完成时:
They have imposed many new regulations. = 他们已经实施了许多新的规章制度。
The judge has imposed a heavy fine. = 法官判处了巨额罚款。

过去完成时:
The king had imposed his will on the people. = 国王已经将他的意志强加于人民。
Before the meeting, they had imposed a deadline. = 会议之前，他们已经设定了最后期限。


将来时:
The new law will impose stricter environmental standards. = 新法将实施更严格的环境标准。
The authorities will impose a curfew. = 当局将实施宵禁。

虚拟语气:
If they imposed stricter rules, the situation would improve. = 如果他们实施更严格的规定，情况就会好转。
I wish they wouldn't impose their beliefs on me. = 我希望他们不要把他们的信仰强加于我。

被动语态:
New taxes were imposed on the citizens. = 新税被强加于公民。
A curfew was imposed by the military. = 宵禁是由军队实施的。

疑问句:
Will the government impose new restrictions? = 政府会实施新的限制吗？
What penalties will be imposed for violating the law? = 违反法律将面临什么处罚？

否定句:
The school does not impose uniforms. = 学校不强制穿校服。
They didn't impose their ideas on the team. = 他们没有把他们的想法强加于团队。


PHRASE->
impose on =  打扰；利用
impose oneself on =  强加于
impose a burden on =  给…带来负担
impose restrictions on =  对…施加限制
impose a penalty on =  对…处以罚款
