hoists:/hɔɪst/| vt. n.|提升；吊起；举起|To lift or raise something heavy, typically using a machine.|The workers hoisted the heavy beam into place using a crane. 工人们用起重机将沉重的梁吊装到位。|raise, lift, elevate|lower, drop|动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hoists the flag every morning. 他每天早上升旗。
They hoist the sails to catch the wind. 他们升起帆来捕捉风。

一般过去时:
The crane hoisted the container onto the ship. 起重机将集装箱吊到船上。
We hoisted the anchor and set sail. 我们起锚启航。

现在完成时:
They have hoisted the new sign above the shop. 他们已将新标牌悬挂在商店上方。
The sailors have hoisted the mainsail. 水手们已经升起了主帆。

过去完成时:
They had hoisted the tent before the rain started. 下雨前他们已经支起了帐篷。(这里hoist指支起帐篷，并非严格意义上的提升重物)
The workers had hoisted the equipment before the inspection. 检查前工人们已经吊起了设备。


将来时:
They will hoist the new billboard tomorrow. 他们明天将竖起新的广告牌。
The crew will hoist the lifeboat into position. 船员们将把救生艇吊放到位。


虚拟语气:
If they had a crane, they could hoist the heavy stone. 如果他们有起重机，他们就能吊起那块巨石。
I wish I could hoist that heavy box myself. 我真希望我能自己搬起那个沉重的箱子。(这里hoist更接近于“搬起”)


被动语态:
The cargo was hoisted onto the ship. 货物被吊上了船。
The flag was hoisted at sunrise. 旗帜在日出时升起。


疑问句:
Can the crane hoist such a heavy weight? 这台起重机能吊起这么重的重量吗？
How high can the crane hoist? 这台起重机能吊起多高？


否定句:
They couldn't hoist the engine without a crane. 他们没有起重机无法吊起发动机。
He didn't hoist the sail because of the lack of wind. 由于缺乏风，他没有升起帆。


PHRASE->
hoist the sails 升起帆
hoist the flag 升旗
hoist the anchor 起锚
hoist oneself up  拉自己起来
hoist with one's own petard 自食其果 (成语，比喻自作自受)
