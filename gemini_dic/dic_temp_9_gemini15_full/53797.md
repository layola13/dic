disconcert:/dɪsˈkɑːnsərt/ | vt. |使不安；使困惑；使狼狈 |To disturb the composure or self-possession of; unsettle.  |The unexpected news disconcerted her.  意外的消息让她不安。|The sudden noise disconcerted the speaker. 突如其来的噪音使演讲者慌乱。| confuse, unsettle, perturb | compose, calm, reassure | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news disconcerts him. = 这个消息让他不安。
Loud noises disconcert her. = 吵闹的声音让她不安。

一般过去时:
The unexpected question disconcerted me. = 那个意外的问题让我不知所措。
His rude behavior disconcerted his guests. = 他粗鲁的行为让他的客人感到不安。

现在完成时:
Nothing has disconcerted him so far. = 到目前为止，没有什么能让他不安。
The continuous rain has disconcerted our plans. = 连续的雨水打乱了我们的计划。

过去完成时:
I had been disconcerted by his earlier comments. = 我被他之前的评论弄得不安。
She had already been disconcerted by his silence. = 她已经被他的沉默弄得不安了。

将来时:
The announcement will disconcert many people. = 这一宣布将会让许多人不安。
The bad weather will disconcert our plans for the picnic. = 恶劣的天气将会打乱我们野餐的计划。

虚拟语气:
If I knew it would disconcert him, I would not have said it. = 如果我知道这会让他不安，我就不会说了。
I wish the unexpected visit hadn't disconcerted me so much. = 我希望那次意外的拜访没有让我这么不安。

被动语态:
He was disconcerted by the criticism. = 他被批评弄得不安。
Their plans were disconcerted by the storm. = 他们的计划被暴风雨打乱了。

疑问句:
Does the thought of public speaking disconcert you? = 公开演讲的想法是否让你不安？
What disconcerted you about the meeting? = 会议上什么让你不安？

否定句:
The news did not disconcert her. = 这个消息并没有让她不安。
The unexpected guest didn't disconcert them. = 意外的客人并没有让他们不安。


PHRASE->
be disconcerted by = 被…弄得不安
feel disconcerted = 感到不安
become disconcerted = 变得不安
utterly disconcerted = 非常不安
seriously disconcerted = 非常不安
