overinvestments: /ˌoʊvərɪnˈvɛstmənts/ | n. |过度投资 | Excessive investments that exceed the optimal level, often leading to diminishing returns or losses.  Overinvestments can occur in various contexts, such as financial markets, business operations, or even personal finances. They often result from overly optimistic projections, a lack of proper risk assessment, or emotional decision-making. |例句：The company's overinvestments in new technology ultimately led to its bankruptcy.  这家公司在新型技术上的过度投资最终导致其破产。

The continuous overinvestments in the real estate market caused a bubble that eventually burst.  房地产市场的持续过度投资导致了最终破裂的泡沫。


近义词：Excessive investments,  excessive capital expenditure,  misallocation of resources |反义词：Underinvestment,  optimal investment, efficient allocation of resources |词性：名词
