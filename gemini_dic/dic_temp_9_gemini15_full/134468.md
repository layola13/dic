nonjuring: /ˈnɒnˌdʒʊərɪŋ/ | adj. |  不效忠的；拒绝宣誓效忠的 | Refusing to swear allegiance, especially to the established church or government.  The term typically refers to those who refused to swear allegiance to the Church of England after the Glorious Revolution of 1688. |  The Nonjurors were a group of clergy who refused to accept the new government. = 不效忠者是一群拒绝接受新政府的牧师。


The Nonjuring movement significantly impacted the religious and political landscape of 17th- and 18th-century England. = 不效忠运动极大地影响了17和18世纪英国的宗教和政治格局。


近义词: disloyal, rebellious, defiant, recalcitrant | 反义词: loyal, obedient, compliant, submissive


词源:  The word is a compound of "non-" (meaning "not") and "juring" (from "jure," meaning "to swear allegiance").  Therefore, it literally means "not swearing allegiance."  The term emerged in the late 17th century in the context of the English religious and political conflicts following the Glorious Revolution.
