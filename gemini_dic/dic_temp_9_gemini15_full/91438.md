hygienism:/ˈhaɪdʒiənɪzəm/| n. |卫生主义；卫生学说|The belief that the maintenance of good health is primarily achieved through the practice of hygiene.  This often involves an emphasis on cleanliness, sanitation, and the prevention of disease through environmental control and individual practices. It can sometimes extend to extreme practices or beliefs about purity and health.|The rise of hygienism in the 19th century led to significant improvements in public health.|近义词：sanitationism, public health movement|反义词：unsanitariness, neglect of hygiene|
