harassing:/həˈæsɪŋ/ | v. | 骚扰，困扰 |  To harass means to persistently annoy or trouble someone.  It often involves unwanted and aggressive behavior that can be emotionally or physically distressing. | Example:  He was harassing his neighbors with loud music. 他用大声的音乐骚扰他的邻居。

He's been harassing her with phone calls. 他一直在用电话骚扰她。


近义词：pester, badger, annoy, bother, torment | 反义词：soothe, comfort, appease | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They harass him daily. = 他们每天骚扰他。
She harasses her colleagues with constant criticism. = 她不断批评同事，骚扰他们。

一般过去时:
The bullies harassed him relentlessly. = 那些恶霸无情地骚扰他。
She harassed me with unwanted attention. = 她用不受欢迎的关注骚扰我。

现在完成时:
He has harassed her for months. = 他已经骚扰她几个月了。
They have harassed us with their complaints. = 他们一直在用他们的抱怨骚扰我们。

过去完成时:
The company had harassed the whistleblower before he went public. = 在举报人公开之前，公司已经骚扰了他。
She had harassed him for years before he finally reported it. = 在他最终举报之前，她已经骚扰了他多年。

将来时:
