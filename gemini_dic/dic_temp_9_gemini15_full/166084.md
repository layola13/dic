preportrayed:/ˌpriːpɔːrˈtreɪd/ | adj. | 预先描绘的；预先刻画的 |  Describes something that has been depicted or portrayed before an event or situation actually occurs.  It suggests a pre-existing image or representation that might influence how the event or situation is subsequently perceived. | The preportrayed villain of the novel turned out to be surprisingly sympathetic.  = 小说的预先描绘的反派人物最终出人意料地令人同情。 The preportrayed disaster scenario helped prepare the city for the actual hurricane. = 预先描绘的灾难情景帮助城市为实际的飓风做好了准备。 | foreshadowed, prefigured, previsualized |  unportrayed, unforeseen | adj.
