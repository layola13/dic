restrictionary: /rɪˈstrɪkʃənəri/ | adj. | 限制性的，约束性的 |  Relating to or characterized by restriction; tending to restrict.  It implies a limitation or constraint, often imposed by authority or regulation. |

例句：
The government imposed restrictionary measures on imports to protect domestic industries.  = 政府实施了限制性措施来保护国内产业。

The restrictionary clauses in the contract limited the company's flexibility. = 合同中的限制性条款限制了公司的灵活性。


近义词：restrictive, limiting, confining, restraining

反义词：liberal, permissive, unrestricted, open


词源:  Derived from "restriction" + "-ary" (forming adjectives).  "Restriction" itself comes from the verb "restrict," ultimately from Latin "restringere" (to bind back, tighten).
