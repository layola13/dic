histamine:/ˈhɪstəmiːn/ | n. | 组胺 | A compound that is released by cells in response to injury and in allergic and inflammatory reactions, causing contraction of smooth muscle and dilation of capillaries.  It is involved in many physiological processes and is a key mediator of allergic reactions. | Example: The release of histamine causes the symptoms of an allergic reaction, such as sneezing and itching.  组胺的释放会导致过敏反应的症状，例如打喷嚏和瘙痒。  |近义词：None (unique compound)| 反义词：None (no direct opposite)| 名词
