scallops:/ˈskæləps/| n.|扇贝，干贝|The edible part of a scallop, a type of shellfish.  Scallops are prized for their sweet flavor and tender texture. They are often served seared, grilled, or fried.|例句：We had pan-seared scallops for dinner last night. 昨晚我们晚餐吃了煎扇贝。

例句：The restaurant is famous for its delicious scallops and seafood risotto. 这家餐厅以其美味的扇贝和海鲜烩饭而闻名。

词源:
The word "scallop" comes from the Old French word "escalope," which referred to the meat of a scallop.  The ultimate origin is likely from the Late Latin word "scaloppa," which may be related to the word "scalp" due to a perceived resemblance in shape.
