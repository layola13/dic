reflorescence:/ˌriːflɔːˈrɛs(ə)ns/ | n. | 复苏；重新繁荣；再次开花 | The resurgence or renewed growth or flourishing of something.  It often refers to a revival after a period of decline or dormancy, particularly in the context of nature (plants reblooming) or society (a cultural revival). | Example:  The reflorescence of the arts after the war was remarkable.  战后艺术的复兴令人瞩目。


The reflorescence of the garden after the spring rains was a beautiful sight.  春雨过后花园的再次繁茂是一幅美丽的景象。| 近义词：revival, resurgence, rebirth, renaissance | 反义词：decline, decay, deterioration | 名词

词源:
The word "reflorescence" is derived from the Latin prefix "re-" (again) and the word "florescence," which itself comes from the Latin "florere" (to bloom, flourish).  Therefore, it literally means "to bloom again" or "to flourish again".
