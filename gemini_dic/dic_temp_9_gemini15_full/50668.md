derive:/dɪˈraɪv/ | vt. |获得，取得；推断；源于 | to obtain or receive something from a source; to deduce or infer something; to originate from |  He derived great pleasure from his work. 他从工作中获得了极大的乐趣。 |  obtain, gain, deduce, infer |  lose, forfeit, miss


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They derive energy from the sun. = 他们从太阳获取能量。
We derive meaning from our experiences. = 我们从经验中获得意义。


一般过去时:
She derived much satisfaction from volunteering. = 她从志愿工作中获得了很大的满足感。
Scientists derived a new theory from the data. = 科学家们从数据中推导出一个新的理论。


现在完成时:
He has derived significant benefits from the program. = 他从该项目中获得了显著的益处。
They have derived their conclusions from extensive research. = 他们从广泛的研究中得出了结论。


过去完成时:
She had derived her inspiration from ancient myths. = 她从古代神话中获得了灵感。
He had derived his understanding from years of study. = 他多年的学习使他获得了理解。


将来时:
We will derive a lot of knowledge from this course. = 我们将从这门课程中获得很多知识。
They will derive the answer from the equation. = 他们将从方程式中推导出答案。


虚拟语气:
If we followed this method, we would derive a better result. = 如果我们采用这种方法，我们将获得更好的结果。
I wish I could derive more happiness from my life. = 我希望我能够从生活中获得更多快乐。


被动语态:
The formula can be derived from the equation. = 这个公式可以从方程式中推导出来。
Energy is derived from various sources. = 能量来自各种来源。


疑问句:
Where do you derive your inspiration from? = 你的灵感来自哪里？
What conclusions can be derived from this study? = 从这项研究中可以得出什么结论？


否定句:
They did not derive any benefit from the investment. = 他们从这项投资中没有获得任何好处。
She could not derive the meaning of the passage. = 她无法理解这段话的意思。



PHRASE->
derive from = 源于；来自

derive pleasure from = 从……中获得乐趣
derive satisfaction from = 从……中获得满足
derive inspiration from = 从……中获得灵感
