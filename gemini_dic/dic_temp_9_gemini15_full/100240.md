interrupt:/ˌɪntəˈrʌpt/ | vt. |打断；中断；干扰 | To break the continuity of (a process, activity, or train of thought). |例句：Please don't interrupt me while I'm speaking.  请在我说话的时候不要打断我。|例句：The power was interrupted for several hours. 电力中断了好几个小时。|近义词：disrupt, hinder, impede |反义词：continue, resume


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He interrupts frequently during meetings. 他在会议期间经常打断别人。
The noise interrupts my concentration. 噪音打断了我的注意力。


一般过去时:
She interrupted him in the middle of his sentence. 她在他句子中间打断了他的话。
The storm interrupted our journey. 暴风雨打断了我们的旅程。


现在完成时:
They have interrupted the project several times. 他们已经几次打断了这个项目。
The constant interruptions have frustrated him.  不断的打断让他感到沮丧。


过去完成时:
He had interrupted the speaker before, so he hesitated to do it again. 他以前打断过演讲者，所以他犹豫要不要再打断。
The rain had interrupted the game for an hour. 雨已经中断比赛一个小时了。


将来时:
I don't think he will interrupt us again. 我认为他不会再打断我们了。
They will interrupt the service for maintenance. 他们将中断服务进行维护。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't interrupted, I would have finished my speech. 如果他没有打断，我就已经完成了我的演讲。
I wish he wouldn't interrupt so much. 我希望他不打断那么多。


被动语态:
The meeting was interrupted by a phone call. 会议被一个电话打断了。
The broadcast was interrupted due to technical difficulties. 由于技术问题，广播中断了。


疑问句:
Did they interrupt the presentation? 他们打断演示了吗？
Was the performance interrupted? 演出被打断了吗？


否定句:
Please don't interrupt. 请不要打断。
He didn't interrupt once during the lecture.  他在讲座期间一次也没有打断。


PHRASE->
interrupt someone's speech 打断某人的讲话

interrupt the flow 打断流程
