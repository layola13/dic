quasi-good:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ɡʊd/ | adj. | 准好的，似好的，近乎好的 |  Describes something that possesses some, but not all, of the characteristics of something good; it falls short of being truly good but is better than bad.  It often implies a compromise or a less-than-ideal situation that is still acceptable. | Example: The quasi-good solution involved some compromises, but it was the best we could achieve under the circumstances.  | 近义词: acceptable, tolerable, adequate, passable | 反义词: bad, terrible, awful, excellent (in the sense of being truly good)
