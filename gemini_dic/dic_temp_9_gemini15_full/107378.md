lamented:/ləˈmɛntɪd/| v.| 哀悼；痛惜；惋惜 | To express grief or sorrow for; mourn.  It often implies a feeling of regret or sadness over something that has happened or been lost. |例句：She lamented the loss of her childhood home. 她为失去儿时的家而悲伤。

例句：He lamented the passing of his old friend. 他为老朋友的去世而哀悼。


近义词：mourned, bewailed, grieved, regretted, deplored

反义词：celebrated, rejoiced, exulted


词源:
The word "lamented" comes from the Latin word "lamentari," meaning "to wail, lament, or mourn."  This in turn is related to the Latin word "lamentum," meaning "a wailing, lament, or song of mourning."  The word's journey through the languages shows its long-standing association with expressions of sorrow and grief.
