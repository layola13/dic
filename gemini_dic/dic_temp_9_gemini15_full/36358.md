coincided:/ˌkoʊɪnˈsaɪdɪd/| v.|同时发生，一致|To occur at the same time; to be in agreement or harmony.|例句：Their birthdays coincided.  他们的生日是同一天。

例句：Her account of the events coincided with his. 她的事件描述与他的相符。

近义词：occurred together, agreed, corresponded|反义词：differed, conflicted|词性:动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
Their schedules coincide. = 他们的日程安排一致。
His story coincides with the evidence. = 他的说法与证据相符。


一般过去时:
The eclipse coincided with the festival. = 日食与节日同时发生。
Our opinions coincided on that point. = 在那一点上，我们的意见一致。


现在完成时:
Many significant events have coincided this year. = 今年许多重要的事件同时发生。
Our interests have coincided surprisingly often. = 我们的兴趣惊人地经常一致。


过去完成时:
The two events had coincided by chance. = 这两个事件偶然同时发生。
His arrival had coincided with the beginning of the meeting. = 他的到达与会议的开始同时发生。


将来时:
The conference will coincide with the holiday season. = 会议将与假日季同时举行。
Their plans will coincide, hopefully. =  希望他们的计划能够一致。


虚拟语气:
If the meetings coincided, it would be very convenient. = 如果会议同时举行，那就非常方便。
I wish my vacation coincided with yours. = 我希望我的假期与你的假期同时。


被动语态:
The dates were made to coincide. = 日期被安排成一致的。
The two events were found to have coincided. = 发现这两个事件同时发生。


疑问句:
Do your plans coincide with mine? = 你的计划与我的计划一致吗？
Does the concert coincide with the football game? = 音乐会与足球比赛同时举行吗？


否定句:
Their accounts of the incident did not coincide. = 他们对事件的描述并不一致。
The dates of the two events did not coincide. = 这两个事件的日期不一致。


PHRASE->
coincide with: 与…同时发生
coincide in: 在…方面一致
roughly coincide: 大致相同
closely coincide: 非常接近
perfectly coincide: 完全一致

词源:
The word "coincide" comes from the Latin words "cum" (with) and "incidere" (to fall upon).  The original meaning referred to two things falling upon the same point.  Over time, the meaning broadened to include the simultaneous occurrence of events and the agreement of opinions or facts.
