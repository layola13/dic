placeboes:/pləˈsiːboʊz/ | n. |安慰剂；无效治疗 | A placebo is a substance or treatment that has no therapeutic effect, often used as a control in clinical trials. It's given to a control group to compare the effects of a real treatment against. The psychological effect of believing one is receiving treatment can sometimes lead to improvement in symptoms, even with a placebo. | The doctor gave him placeboes during the study. 医生在研究期间给他服用安慰剂。 |  placebo, sham, dummy |  cure, treatment
