distrust:/dɪsˈtrʌst/| n. v.|不信任，怀疑|The feeling of not trusting someone or something; lack of confidence or faith.|例句：Her distrust of politicians is well known.  她的不信任政客是众所周知的。|例句：I distrust his motives. 我怀疑他的动机。|近义词：suspicion, doubt, skepticism|反义词：trust, confidence, faith|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He distrusts the government. = 他不信任政府。
They distrust each other. = 他们彼此不信任。

一般过去时:
She distrusted his promises. = 她不相信他的承诺。
I distrusted my own judgment. = 我怀疑自己的判断。

现在完成时:
We have distrusted him since the incident. = 自那件事以来，我们就不再信任他了。
They have distrusted the company's claims. = 他们一直对公司的说法表示怀疑。

过去完成时:
He had distrusted her for years. = 他多年来一直不信任她。
I had distrusted his intentions from the start. = 从一开始我就怀疑他的意图。

将来时:
I will distrust him until he proves his innocence. = 直到他证明自己的清白，我才会信任他。
They will distrust the new system until it is fully tested. = 在新系统完全测试之前，他们将对其持怀疑态度。

虚拟语气:
If I didn't distrust him, I would lend him the money. = 如果我不怀疑他，我会把钱借给他。
I wish I didn't distrust her so much. = 我希望我不那么不信任她。

被动语态:
His motives were distrusted by many. = 许多人怀疑他的动机。
The new policy is distrusted by the public. = 新政策不被公众信任。

疑问句:
Do you distrust your boss? = 你不信任你的老板吗？
Does she distrust the evidence? = 她怀疑证据吗？

否定句:
I don't distrust her completely. = 我并不完全不信任她。
He didn't distrust her judgment at first. = 他一开始并不怀疑她的判断。


PHRASE->
a distrust of authority = 对权威的不信任
a feeling of distrust = 不信任感
inspire distrust = 激起不信任
object to distrust = 反对不信任
cause distrust = 引起不信任
