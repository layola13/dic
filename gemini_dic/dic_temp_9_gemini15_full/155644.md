phlebolite:/ˈfliːbaɪt/| n. |静脉石|A phlebolite is a small, calcified vein, often found in the pelvis or legs.  They are usually harmless and asymptomatic.  They are formed when blood clots within a vein become calcified over time.| Example: The X-ray showed several phlebolites in the patient's pelvic region.
