kantianism:/ˈkæntiənɪzəm/| n. |康德主义|The philosophical system of Immanuel Kant, emphasizing reason, morality, and the limits of human knowledge.  It centers on the concepts of the categorical imperative, transcendental idealism, and the distinction between phenomena and noumena.|Example:  Kantianism profoundly influenced subsequent ethical and epistemological thought.  康德主义深刻地影响了后来的伦理学和认识论思想。|近义词：transcendentalism, idealism|反义词：empiricism, skepticism|名词
