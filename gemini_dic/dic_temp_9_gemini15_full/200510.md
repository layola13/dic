slanter:/ˈslæntər/| n. v.|歪曲者；歪曲报道；使倾斜|A slanter is someone who presents information in a biased or misleading way.  It can also refer to the act of presenting information in a biased way, or something that is slanted or inclined. |例句：The newspaper article was a blatant slanter, ignoring key facts to support its agenda. 这篇报纸文章是赤裸裸的歪曲，忽略关键事实以支持其议程。The reporter was accused of being a slanter, manipulating the facts to fit his narrative. 记者被指控歪曲事实，以符合他的叙述。|近义词：distorter, propagandist, bias, manipulator|反义词：truth-teller, impartial reporter, objective observer|名词，动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He slants the news to favor his political party. = 他歪曲新闻以偏袒他的政党。
The building slants dangerously to one side. = 这栋建筑物危险地向一边倾斜。


一般过去时:
She slanted the story to make the hero look better. = 她歪曲了故事，使英雄看起来更好。
The old tree slanted over the road. = 老树倾斜在路边。


现在完成时:
They have slanted the debate with misleading statistics. = 他们用误导性的统计数据歪曲了辩论。
The tower has slanted noticeably over the years. = 塔多年来明显倾斜了。


过去完成时:
The media had slanted the coverage before the election. = 大选前媒体已经歪曲了报道。
The leaning tower had slanted further before the repairs. = 在维修之前，倾斜塔更加倾斜了。


将来时:
He will slant the report to protect his friend. = 他会歪曲报告来保护他的朋友。
The strong winds will slant the tall trees. = 强风会使高大的树木倾斜。


虚拟语气:
If he slanted the truth, people would lose trust in him. = 如果他歪曲事实，人们就会失去对他的信任。
I wish they hadn’t slanted the article so much. = 我希望他们没有那么歪曲这篇文章。


被动语态:
The facts were slanted to support the government's position. = 事实被歪曲以支持政府的立场。
The fence was slanted by the recent storm. = 栅栏被最近的风暴吹斜了。


疑问句:
Did he slant the evidence to win the case? = 他是否歪曲证据来赢得案件？
Is the tower slanting dangerously? = 塔是否危险地倾斜？


否定句:
He did not slant the information; he reported it accurately. = 他没有歪曲信息；他准确地报道了它。
The picture was not slanted; it was perfectly straight. = 图片没有倾斜；它是完全笔直的。
