pre-Hispanic: /ˌpriː hɪˈspænɪk/ | adj. | 前西班牙时期(的) |  Relating to the period in the history of the Americas before the arrival of the Spanish.  This term is primarily used in reference to the cultures, civilizations, and societies that existed in the Americas before the arrival of European colonizers, particularly the Spanish.  The term encompasses a vast range of indigenous cultures, each with its unique characteristics and history. |


例句：

Pre-Hispanic art often features intricate designs and symbolic imagery. = 前西班牙时期的艺术作品通常具有错综复杂的图案和象征性意象。

Many pre-Hispanic sites in Mexico are now UNESCO World Heritage sites. = 墨西哥许多前西班牙时期的遗址现在是联合国教科文组织的世界遗产。


近义词：pre-Columbian (更广泛，指哥伦布到达美洲之前)


反义词：post-Hispanic (西班牙殖民时期及之后)


词性:形容词
