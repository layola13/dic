overrationalization: /ˌoʊvərˌræʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 过度合理化；过度解释 | The act of excessively explaining or justifying something, often to avoid confronting uncomfortable emotions or truths.  It involves creating elaborate rationales that may not accurately reflect the situation.  | Example:  His overrationalization of his failure to get the promotion masked his underlying insecurity. 他对未能晋升的过度合理化掩盖了他潜在的不安全感。 |近义词: overexplanation, rationalization, justification | 反义词: emotional acceptance, intuitive understanding |  词性: 名词
