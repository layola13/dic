pewed:/pjuːd/| n.|教堂的长椅；（尤指）祈祷座位|A long bench, especially in a church, for people to sit on, typically in rows.|The congregation sat in the pews during the sermon. 教堂会众在布道期间坐在长椅上。|seat, bench|standing area|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The family sits in the same pew every Sunday. = 这家人每个星期天都坐在同一个长椅上。
That pew is usually occupied. = 那排长椅通常是坐满了的。


一般过去时:
They sat quietly in the pew, listening to the choir. = 他们静静地坐在长椅上，听着唱诗班唱歌。
We found a pew near the back of the church. = 我们在教堂后面找到一个座位。


现在完成时:
We have sat in this pew for many years. = 我们多年来一直坐在这个座位上。
The pew has been recently repaired. = 这个长椅最近刚刚维修过。


过去完成时:
They had already taken their seats in the pews before the service began. = 在仪式开始之前，他们已经坐在长椅上了。
We had never sat in such a comfortable pew before. = 我们以前从未坐过这么舒服的长椅。


将来时:
We will sit in the front pew next Sunday. = 我们下个星期天将坐在前排的座位上。
They will find a pew somewhere in the church. = 他们会在教堂里找到一个座位。


虚拟语气:
If I had a pew to myself, I would be more comfortable. = 如果我有一个单独的座位，我会更舒服。
I wish we had reserved a pew closer to the altar. = 我希望我们预订了离祭坛更近的座位。


被动语态:
The pews are cleaned regularly. = 长椅定期清洁。
