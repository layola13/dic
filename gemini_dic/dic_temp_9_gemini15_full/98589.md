/ɪnstɪˈtuːtskɑːjə/ | n. |研究院街，研究院路|Institutskaya is a Russian word typically referring to a street or avenue named after an institute or academy.  It's a common toponymic element in many Russian cities and towns. The word itself is formed by combining the word "institut" (институт, institute) with the suffix "-skaya" (-ская), a feminine possessive suffix often used to form names of places. | Example:  We walked down Institutskaya Street to reach the university. 我们沿着研究院街走到大学。|近义词: Academy Street, Institute Avenue |反义词: (no direct antonym, as it's a proper noun)|名词
