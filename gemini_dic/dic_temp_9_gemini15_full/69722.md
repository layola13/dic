feodality: /ˌfiːoʊˈdæləti/ | n. | 封建制度，封建主义 | The system of feudalism, characterized by a hierarchical structure of land ownership and loyalty obligations.  It involved a complex network of relationships between lords, vassals, and serfs, based on the holding of land in exchange for military service or other forms of loyalty. | The decline of feodality in Europe marked a significant transition in social and political structures.  | 近义词：feudalism, manorialism | 反义词：democracy, egalitarianism
