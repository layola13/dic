jeopardize:/ˈdʒepərdaɪz/ | vt. | 危及；使处于危险境地；损害 | To put something or someone at risk of being harmed, damaged, or destroyed.  |  例句：His reckless driving jeopardized the lives of others.  他的鲁莽驾驶危及了他人的生命。

例句：The new policy could jeopardize the company's future.  这项新政策可能会危及公司的未来。


近义词：endanger, threaten, imperil, risk, compromise | 反义词：protect, safeguard, secure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jeopardizes his health by smoking. = 他吸烟危害了他的健康。
The project jeopardizes the environment. = 这个项目危及环境。


一般过去时:
The storm jeopardized the harvest. = 暴风雨危及了收成。
His careless mistake jeopardized the whole plan. = 他粗心的错误危及了整个计划。


现在完成时:
His actions have jeopardized his reputation. = 他的行为已经损害了他的名誉。
The scandal has jeopardized the company's image. = 这场丑闻已经损害了公司的形象。


过去完成时:
They had jeopardized their position by refusing to cooperate. = 他们因为拒绝合作而危及了自己的地位。
The delay had jeopardized the success of the mission. = 延误已经危及了任务的成功。


将来时:
The flood will jeopardize the safety of the town. = 洪水将危及城镇的安全。
Further delays will jeopardize the project's completion. = 进一步的延误将危及项目的完成。


虚拟语气:
If he continues to drink, he will jeopardize his health. = 如果他继续喝酒，他会危及自己的健康。
If they had acted sooner, they would not have jeopardized the deal. = 如果他们早点采取行动，他们就不会危及这笔交易了。


被动语态:
The project was jeopardized by the unexpected rain. = 该项目因突降大雨而受损。
His career was jeopardized by the scandal. = 他的职业生涯因丑闻而受损。


疑问句:
Does this decision jeopardize our chances of success? = 这个决定会危及我们成功的几率吗？
Will the new regulations jeopardize the small businesses? = 新规会危及小型企业吗？


否定句:
We must ensure that the project is not jeopardized. = 我们必须确保项目不会受损。
The company took steps to prevent their reputation from being jeopardized. = 公司采取措施防止其声誉受损。


PHRASE->

jeopardize public safety = 危及公共安全
jeopardize the negotiations = 危及谈判


词源:
The word "jeopardize" ultimately derives from the Old French phrase "jeu parti," meaning "game played," or "stake placed."  This referred to a situation where something was at risk or in play.  Over time, this evolved into the modern English usage.
