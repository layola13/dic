lycanthropous:/ˌlaɪkænˈθrɒpəs/ | adj. | 狼人般的；患有狼人症的 |  Characterized by or relating to lycanthropy; suffering from lycanthropy.  The term refers to the transformation of a human into a wolf, either literally or metaphorically.  It often implies a supernatural or mythical element. | 例句：The villagers whispered of a lycanthropous curse affecting the shepherd. 村民们窃窃私语，说牧羊人受到了狼人诅咒。


The legend speaks of a lycanthropous beast that roams the forest at night. 传说中有一只在夜间在森林里游荡的狼人野兽。


近义词：werewolfish, lupine (in the sense of wolf-like characteristics) | 反义词：human, normal (in the sense of lacking lycanthropy)


词源:  From  Ancient Greek λύκος (lýkos, “wolf”) + ἄνθρωπος (ánthrōpos, “human being”).  The suffix "-ous" denotes "full of" or "having the qualities of".  Therefore, lycanthropous literally means "wolf-human-like".
