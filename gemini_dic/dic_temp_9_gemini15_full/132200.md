nondeafening: /ˌnɑːnˈdɛfənɪŋ/ | adj. | 不震耳欲聋的，不刺耳的 | Not loud enough to cause deafness or discomfort.  It describes a sound that is pleasant to the ear and not overwhelming. | 例句：The music was nondeafening, allowing for conversation. 音乐声不震耳欲聋，可以边听边聊天。

The concert had a nondeafening volume; we could easily talk to each other. 音乐会音量适中，我们很容易互相交谈。


近义词：soft, quiet, subdued, gentle, low, muted | 反义词：deafening, loud, boisterous, deafening, raucous


词源:  The word is a combination of "non-" (meaning "not") and "deafening" (meaning "extremely loud, capable of causing deafness").  "Deafening" itself derives from "deaf" (unable to hear), ultimately from Old English *deaf.
