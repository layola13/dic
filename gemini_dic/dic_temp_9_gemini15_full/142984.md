outbargain:/ˌaʊtˈbɑːrɡən/| vt.| 胜过…的讨价还价；比…更精于讨价还价 |To get a better price or deal than someone else in a negotiation.  |例句：I outbargained the vendor and got the rug for half the asking price. 我比卖方更精于讨价还价，以半价买到了地毯。

例句：She completely outbargained him at the flea market.她在跳蚤市场上完全比他更精于讨价还价。


近义词：outmaneuver, outsmart, outwit | 反义词：be outbargained


词性: 动词

词源:
The word "outbargain" is a combination of the prefix "out-" (meaning "to surpass" or "to exceed") and the verb "bargain" (meaning "to negotiate").  It's a relatively straightforward construction reflecting the action of surpassing someone in a bargaining situation.
