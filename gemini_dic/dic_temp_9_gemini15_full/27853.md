canonize:/ˈkænənaɪz/| vt.|封圣；尊为圣人；奉为经典|To officially declare someone a saint; to regard something as authoritative or classic.|The Church canonized her after a long investigation into her life and miracles. 教会经过对其生平和奇迹的长期调查后将她封圣。  The author's works have been canonized as essential reading for students of literature.  该作者的作品已被奉为文学专业的学生必读经典。|exalt, sanctify, enshrine|profane, desecrate|动词

一般现在时:
The Church canonizes saints after a rigorous process. = 教会经过严格的程序才封圣。
They canonize his work as a masterpiece. = 他们奉他的作品为杰作。


一般过去时:
The Pope canonized the new saint last year. = 教宗去年封圣了这位新圣人。
Critics canonized her novel as a landmark achievement. = 批评家们将她的小说奉为里程碑式的成就。


现在完成时:
The Catholic Church has canonized many figures throughout history. = 天主教教会历史上已经封圣了许多人物。
Literary scholars have canonized Shakespeare's plays as timeless classics. = 文学学者们已经将莎士比亚的戏剧奉为永恒的经典。


过去完成时:
Before the canonization, many miracles had been attributed to her. = 在封圣之前，许多奇迹都被归功于她。
By the time the book was published, it had already been canonized by many reviewers. = 到这本书出版的时候，它已经被许多评论家奉为经典。


将来时:
The committee will canonize the new member next month. = 委员会下个月将吸收新成员。
The academy will likely canonize his contributions to science in the future. = 科学院很可能会在未来肯定他对科学的贡献。


虚拟语气:
If they canonized him, it would be a great honor. = 如果他们封他为圣人，那将是莫大的荣誉。
I wish they would canonize her work for its impact on society. = 我希望他们能因为她对社会的影响而肯定她的作品。


被动语态:
He was canonized by the Pope in 1950. = 他在1950年被教宗封圣。
Her poems were canonized by generations of readers. = 她的诗歌被几代读者奉为经典。


疑问句:

否定句:
The Church did not canonize him despite his devout life. = 尽管他生活虔诚，教会还是没有将他封圣。
The critics did not canonize his latest novel. = 批评家们并没有将他的最新小说奉为经典。
