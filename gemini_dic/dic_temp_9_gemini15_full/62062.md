encashed:/ɪnˈkæʃt/| v.|兑现；兑换成现金|To convert a check or other negotiable instrument into cash.|例句：I encashed my paycheck at the bank. 我在银行兑现了我的工资支票。|例句：She encashed a traveler's check for spending money. 她兑换了一张旅行支票作为零花钱。|近义词：cashed, redeemed|反义词：deposited


USAGE->

He encashes his checks every Friday. = 他每个星期五都兑现支票。
She encashes her savings bonds. = 她兑现她的储蓄债券。


一般过去时:
I encashed the check yesterday. = 我昨天兑现了支票。
They encashed their winning lottery ticket. = 他们兑现了他们中奖的彩票。

词源:
The word "encashed" comes from the verb "cash," meaning to convert into money, and the prefix "en-," which intensifies the meaning.  "Cash" ultimately derives from the Old French "caisse," meaning "box" or "chest," referring to a strongbox used to keep money.
