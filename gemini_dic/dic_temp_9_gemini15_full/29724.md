catilinarian:/ˌkætɪlɪˈneəriən/| adj.|卡提林式的；煽动叛乱的；诽谤的| relating to or characteristic of Catiline, a Roman aristocrat who conspired to overthrow the Roman Republic in the 1st century BC; hence,  characterized by conspiracy, incitement to violence, or revolutionary fervor.  It often implies vehement denunciation or inflammatory speech.|The senator's catilinarian speech against the proposed tax increase incited outrage among his constituents.  参议员针对拟议的增税发表的煽动性讲话激怒了他的选民。|近义词：seditious, incendiary, inflammatory, subversive|反义词：pacific, conciliatory, appeasing
