deurbanization:/ˌdiːˌɜːbənaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 城市人口外流；逆城市化；非都市化 | The process by which people move from urban areas to rural areas, resulting in a decline in the population of cities.  This can be driven by various factors, including economic opportunities, lifestyle choices, and environmental concerns. | Example: The deurbanization of the Rust Belt in the US led to significant economic challenges for many cities.  美国“锈带”地区的逆城市化导致许多城市面临重大的经济挑战。|近义词：ruralization, suburbanization (partially), counter-urbanization | 反义词：urbanization
