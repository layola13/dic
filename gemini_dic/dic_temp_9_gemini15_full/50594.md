/dɪˈreɪndʒər/ | n. |使精神错乱的人；扰乱者|Someone who causes mental disturbance or chaos; a person who disrupts or upsets the mental state of others.  |The deranger left a trail of destruction in his wake.|近义词：disruptor, troublemaker, agitator |反义词：peacemaker, stabilizer |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deranges his schedule frequently. = 他经常打乱自己的日程安排。
The noise deranges my concentration. = 噪音扰乱了我的注意力。


一般过去时:
The news deranged her completely. = 这个消息把她彻底搞垮了。
The storm deranged the traffic system. = 暴风雨扰乱了交通系统。


现在完成时:
Stress has deranged his mental state. = 压力已经扰乱了他的精神状态。
The scandal has deranged the political landscape. = 这场丑闻扰乱了政治格局。


过去完成时:
The argument had deranged her composure. = 那场争论已经扰乱了她的镇定。
They had deranged the entire plan before I arrived. = 我到达之前他们就已经把整个计划搞乱了。


将来时:

He believes that the new technology will derange the industry. = 他相信这项新技术将会扰乱这个行业。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:
His mind was deranged by grief. = 他的精神被悲伤摧垮了。
The system was deranged by the cyberattack. = 系统被网络攻击扰乱了。


疑问句:

否定句:
The incident did not derange the overall progress. = 这件事并没有扰乱整体进度。
He did not derange the furniture. = 他没有弄乱家具。
