scarcely:/ˈskɛrsli/| adv.|几乎不，简直不，仅仅|It means "only just" or "hardly". It indicates a small amount or degree of something, often implying near absence.  It's often used to emphasize the smallness or insignificance of something.|例句：I scarcely knew him. 我几乎不认识他。
He scarcely had time to eat. 他几乎没时间吃饭。|近义词：barely, hardly, barely, seldom|反义词: abundantly, plentifully, copiously


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scarcely eats meat. 他几乎不吃肉。
She scarcely ever goes out. 她几乎从不出门。

一般过去时:
They scarcely noticed the mistake. 他们几乎没注意到那个错误。
I scarcely slept last night. 我昨晚几乎没睡。

现在完成时:
I have scarcely seen him since then. 从那以后我几乎没见过他。
She has scarcely had time to relax. 她几乎没有时间放松。

过去完成时:
We had scarcely arrived when it began to rain. 我们刚到就开始下雨了。
They had scarcely begun to work when the boss called them. 他们刚开始工作老板就叫他们了。

将来时:
We will scarcely have time to finish the project. 我们几乎没时间完成这个项目。
They will scarcely be able to afford the trip. 他们几乎负担不起这次旅行。

虚拟语气:
If I had scarcely any money, I would ask for help. 如果我几乎没有钱，我会寻求帮助。
I wish I had scarcely any homework. 我希望我的家庭作业几乎没有。


被动语态:
The problem was scarcely addressed at the meeting. 会议上几乎没有解决这个问题。
The damage was scarcely noticeable.  损坏几乎不明显。

疑问句:
Did you scarcely understand the explanation? 你几乎没理解解释吗？
Have you scarcely any money left? 你几乎没钱剩下了吗？

否定句:
I don't scarcely know her. 我几乎不认识她。(注意：这句语法上略显累赘，更自然的表达是 I hardly know her. 或者 I scarcely know her.)
He didn't scarcely eat anything. 他几乎什么都没吃。(同样，更自然的表达是 He hardly ate anything. 或者 He scarcely ate anything.)


PHRASE->
scarcely...when...: 刚...就...
scarcely...before...: 还没...就...
scarcely a... : 几乎没有...
scarcely enough: 几乎不够
scarcely any: 几乎没有
