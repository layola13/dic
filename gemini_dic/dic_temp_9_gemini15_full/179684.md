reenlightens:/ˌriːɪnˈlaɪtnz/| vt.|重新启迪；使重新明白；使重新认识到|To enlighten someone again; to cause someone to understand something more clearly or completely after a period of misunderstanding or lack of clarity.|The teacher reenlightened the students on the complex topic after their initial confusion.|近义词：re-educate, re-explain, clarify|反义词：confuse, obfuscate


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The professor reenlightens his students on a weekly basis. = 教授每周都会重新启迪他的学生。
She reenlightens me on the importance of patience. = 她让我重新认识到耐心的重要性。


一般过去时:
The experience reenlightened him on the value of friendship. = 那次经历让他重新认识到友谊的价值。
He reenlightened her on the details of the project. = 他向她重新解释了项目的细节。


现在完成时:
This book has reenlightened me on the subject of history. = 这本书让我对历史有了新的认识。
Recent events have reenlightened the public on the dangers of misinformation. = 最近发生的事件让公众重新认识到虚假信息的危害。


过去完成时:
She had reenlightened him on the matter before the meeting. = 在会议之前，她已经向他重新解释了这件事。
They had reenlightened us on the new rules before we started. = 在我们开始之前，他们已经向我们重新解释了新规则。

虚拟语气:
