neocolonialism:/ˌniːoʊkəˈloʊniəlɪzəm/| n. |新殖民主义|The indirect or subtle subjugation of a nation or people by another, often through economic or political means, rather than direct military or political control.  This often involves maintaining exploitative economic relationships, political interference, and cultural influence.  Neocolonialism is characterized by the continuation of colonial power structures and inequalities even after formal political independence has been achieved.| The legacy of colonialism continues to affect many developing nations through neocolonialism, hindering their economic growth and political stability.  Many argue that multinational corporations exert neocolonial influence over less developed countries.|近义词：imperialism, exploitation, domination|反义词：self-determination, independence, liberation|名词
