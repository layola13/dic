get-out: /ɡɛtˈaʊt/ | phrasal verb | 离开；逃脱；出版；说出来 |  A phrasal verb meaning to leave a place, to escape from a situation, to be published, or to reveal something.  | 例句：It's time to get out of here. = 该走了。  | 例句：He managed to get out of the burning building. = 他设法从燃烧的建筑物中逃了出来。 | 近义词：leave, escape, depart, publish, reveal | 反义词: enter, arrive, remain, conceal, keep secret |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They get out of bed early every morning. = 他们每天早上很早就起床。
We get out of the office at 5 pm. = 我们下午五点下班。


一般过去时:
She got out of the car and ran. = 她下了车，跑了起来。
He got out of trouble easily. = 他很容易就摆脱了麻烦。


现在完成时:
They have got out of the habit of reading newspapers. = 他们已经戒掉了读报的习惯。
I have got out of debt finally. = 我终于摆脱了债务。


过去完成时:
She had got out of the house before the storm broke. = 暴风雨来临之前，她已经离开了家。
He had got out of the habit of exercising regularly. = 他已经很久没有坚持定期锻炼了。


将来时:
We will get out of this town as soon as possible. = 我们会尽快离开这个小镇。
They will get out of the hospital next week. = 他们下周将出院。


虚拟语气:
If I had known it was dangerous, I would have got out of there sooner. = 如果我知道那很危险，我会更早离开那里。
I wish I could get out of this meeting. = 我真希望我能逃过这个会议。


被动语态:
The truth will eventually get out. = 真相最终会大白于天下。
The news got out quickly. = 消息传得很快。


疑问句:
When do you usually get out of work? = 你通常几点下班？
How do we get out of this mess? = 我们该如何摆脱这个困境？


否定句:
I can't get out of this commitment. = 我无法摆脱这个承诺。
He couldn't get out of the contract. = 他无法摆脱合同。



PHRASE->

get something out of something = 从…中得到…
