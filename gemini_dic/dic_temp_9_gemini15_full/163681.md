predecision:/ˌpriːdɪˈsɪʒən/| n. |预先决定；事先决定|The act of making a decision before all the facts are known or before a situation has fully developed.  It often implies a decision made with incomplete information or under pressure.|例句：The predecision to invest heavily in that technology proved to be a mistake.  预先决定大力投资这项技术证明是一个错误。|例句：Her predecision to quit her job before finding a new one caused her financial hardship. 她未找到新工作就预先决定辞职导致了她经济上的困难。|近义词：predetermination, prearrangement, advance decision|反义词：postdecision, improvisation, spontaneity|名词
