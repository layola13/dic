homologoumena:/hɒˈmɒləɡuːmɪnə/| n.| 承认的著作；被接受的作品 |  Texts or writings accepted as genuine or authentic, especially of a particular author.  The term is often used in biblical studies to refer to the books of the Bible that are universally accepted as canonical. | Example:  The homologoumena of the New Testament are generally undisputed. 新约的公认正典著作通常没有争议。 | 近义词: accepted works, canonical writings, authentic texts | 反义词: apocrypha, disputed texts, spurious writings
