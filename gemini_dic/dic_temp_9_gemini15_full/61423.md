emboldens:/ɪmˈboʊldənz/ | vt. |使大胆；使有勇气；加粗（字体） | To give (someone) courage or confidence. To make (something, such as text) bolder or more prominent. | 例句：The support of her friends emboldened her to speak out against injustice.  她的朋友的支持使她大胆地站出来反对不公正。

The speaker emboldened the text to highlight key points. 演讲者加粗了文本以突出重点。


近义词：encourage, inspire, strengthen, intensify | 反义词：discourage, intimidate, weaken


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He emboldens his arguments with strong evidence. = 他用有力的证据来增强他的论点。
The new policy emboldens small businesses to invest. = 新政策使小型企业更有信心进行投资。


一般过去时:
The victory emboldened the team to pursue further goals. = 胜利使球队更有信心去追求更高的目标。
Her success emboldened her to try again. = 她的成功使她更有勇气再试一次。


现在完成时:
The recent developments have emboldened the rebels. = 最近的事态发展壮大了叛军的胆量。
His training has emboldened him to face his fears. = 他的训练使他更有勇气面对恐惧。


过去完成时:
Having won the first round, they were emboldened to fight harder. = 赢得第一轮后，他们更有勇气更加努力地战斗。
She had been emboldened by her previous success, so she took on a more challenging project. = 她之前的成功使她更有信心，所以她承担了一个更具挑战性的项目。


将来时:
This new technology will embolden future generations of artists. = 这项新技术将使未来几代艺术家更加大胆。
The promised support will embolden them to continue their struggle. = 承诺的支持将使他们更有勇气继续他们的斗争。


虚拟语气:
If I were emboldened by my success, I would pursue a higher position. = 如果我的成功使我更有胆量，我会追求更高的职位。
If only he were emboldened to speak his mind. = 但愿他有勇气说出自己的想法。


被动语态:
The soldiers were emboldened by the arrival of reinforcements. = 士兵们在援军到来后士气大振。
The small business was emboldened by the government’s loan program. = 小型企业受到政府贷款项目的鼓舞。


疑问句:
What emboldened you to take such a risk? = 什么使你敢冒如此大的风险？
Will this new evidence embolden the prosecution? = 这项新的证据会使检方更有信心吗？


否定句:
The lack of support did not embolden them. = 缺乏支持并没有使他们更有勇气。
His fear did not embolden him to speak up.  他的恐惧并没有使他敢于大声说话。
