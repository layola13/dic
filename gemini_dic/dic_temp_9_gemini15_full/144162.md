outshouldering: /ˌaʊtˈʃoʊldəriŋ/ | v. | 超出…的肩膀；胜过；压倒 |  To surpass or excel someone or something; to outdo. This word is a relatively rare and somewhat archaic formation, primarily used in figurative contexts. It combines "out-" (meaning "to a greater extent than") with "shouldering" (referring to the action of bearing a burden or responsibility).


例句：

He outshouldered his rivals in the debate, presenting a far more compelling argument. = 他在辩论中胜过对手，提出了更有说服力的论点。

Her dedication outshouldered the challenges, leading to her eventual success. = 她的奉献精神克服了挑战，最终取得了成功。


近义词：outperform, surpass, excel, overshadow, outstrip

反义词：underperform, be surpassed by, be overshadowed by


词性：动词
