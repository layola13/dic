precipitated: /prɪˈsɪpɪteɪtɪd/ | v. adj. |  促成；使沉淀；使突然发生；仓促的；突发的 |

英文解析:As a verb, "precipitated" means to cause something to happen suddenly or prematurely.  As an adjective, it describes something that is sudden, unexpected, or hasty.  The word often implies a negative consequence due to the suddenness or lack of planning.  In chemistry, it refers to the process of a solid forming from a solution.

例句:

The unexpected news precipitated a crisis in the company. =  意外的消息导致公司陷入危机。

The rain precipitated the landslide. =  雨水引发了山体滑坡。

His rash decision precipitated a major argument. = 他鲁莽的决定导致了一场大争吵。


近义词: caused, triggered, spurred, hastened, abrupt, sudden, hasty, rash


反义词: delayed, postponed, gradual, deliberate, planned


词性: Verb (past participle), Adjective
