mugging:/ˈmʌɡɪŋ/ | n. v. |抢劫；行凶|The act of attacking and robbing someone, typically in a public place.|例句：He was the victim of a mugging last night. 他昨晚遭到抢劫。|例句：The police are investigating a series of muggings in the park. 警方正在调查公园里发生的一系列抢劫案。|近义词：robbery, assault, attack |反义词：donation, giving


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He mugs people in the street. = 他在街上抢劫路人。
She often mugs her friends for money. = 她经常向朋友骗钱。

一般过去时:
They mugged him and stole his wallet. = 他们抢劫了他并偷走了他的钱包。
The thief mugged the old lady. = 小偷抢劫了那位老太太。


现在完成时:
I have never been mugged before. = 我以前从未被抢劫过。
The police have mugged up on the case. = 警方已经仔细研究了这个案子。(非一般意义的mugging)

过去完成时:
He had been mugged twice before that incident. = 在那件事发生之前，他已经两次被抢劫了。
She had never been mugged in her life. = 她一生中从未被抢劫过。


将来时:
I hope I won't be mugged on my way home. = 我希望我回家路上不会被抢劫。
They will mug anyone who looks vulnerable. = 他们会抢劫任何看起来弱不禁风的人。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't walk alone at night; you might get mugged. = 如果我是你，我晚上不会独自步行；你可能会被抢劫。
I wish I hadn't been mugged last night. = 我真希望我昨晚没有被抢劫。


被动语态:
He was mugged near the station. = 他在车站附近被抢劫了。
Many people have been mugged in this area recently. = 最近这个地区很多人被抢劫了。


疑问句:
Have you ever been mugged? = 你曾经被抢劫过吗？
Was he mugged by a single attacker? = 他是被一个袭击者抢劫的吗？


否定句:
He wasn't mugged; he lost his wallet somewhere else. = 他没有被抢劫；他把钱包丢在别的地方了。
They haven't mugged anyone recently. = 他们最近没有抢劫任何人。

mug someone = 欺骗某人
