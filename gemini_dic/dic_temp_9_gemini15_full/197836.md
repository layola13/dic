shintiism:/ˈʃɪntiɪzəm/| n. |神道教 |Shintiism is a term sometimes used to refer to the indigenous religion of Japan, often called Shinto.  However, it's not a widely accepted or preferred term.  The term is considered less precise and potentially misleading compared to "Shinto."|Example: Some older texts might use "Shintiism," but "Shinto" is the more accurate and commonly used term.|近义词:Shinto|反义词:None.  It's not appropriate to assign an antonym to a religion.
