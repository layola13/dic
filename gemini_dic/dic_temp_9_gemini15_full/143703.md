outlet: /ˈaʊtlet/ | n. | 出口；商店；(情感等的)发泄途径 | An outlet is a place where something is sold, or a way of expressing strong emotions.  It can also refer to a place where something flows out. |


例句：

The mall has many clothing outlets. = 这个商场有很多服装专卖店。

She uses painting as an outlet for her anger. = 她用绘画来发泄怒气。


近义词：shop, store, vent, release

反义词：inlet, source (in some contexts)


词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The outlet sells shoes and bags. = 这家商店卖鞋子和包包。
This outlet provides a way to express emotions. = 这个途径可以表达情绪。


一般过去时:
The outlet closed down last year. = 这家商店去年倒闭了。

现在完成时:
The company has opened several new outlets this year. = 公司今年已经开设了几家新店。
She has finally found a healthy outlet for her stress. = 她终于找到了一个健康的减压方式。


过去完成时:

He had sought an outlet for his creativity for years before he found painting. = 在找到绘画之前，他已经多年寻求创造力的表达方式了。


将来时:
They will open a new outlet next month. = 他们下个月将开一家新店。
He will find an outlet for his grief eventually. = 他最终会找到发泄悲伤的方式。


虚拟语气:
If I had more money, I would open my own outlet. = 如果我有更多钱，我会开一家自己的店。
If he had a better outlet for his anger, he wouldn’t be so violent. = 如果他能更好地发泄怒气，他就不会那么暴力了。


被动语态:

疑问句:
