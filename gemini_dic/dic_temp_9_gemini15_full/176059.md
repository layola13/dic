ravin:/ˈrævɪn/| n. | 沟壑；峡谷；山涧 | A deep, narrow gorge worn by running water.  Ravines are often found in mountainous or hilly areas. | Example: The hikers lost their way in the deep ravine.  徒步旅行者在深深的沟壑中迷路了。|近义词：gorge, gully, chasm |反义词：plain, plateau


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The ravine cuts through the mountain. = 这条沟壑穿过山脉。
Water flows swiftly in the ravine. = 水快速地流过沟壑。


一般过去时:
The hikers fell into the ravine. = 徒步旅行者掉进了沟壑。
A landslide blocked the ravine. = 山体滑坡堵塞了沟壑。


现在完成时:
Rain has eroded the ravine. = 雨水侵蚀了沟壑。
The ravine has become deeper over the years. = 这些年来沟壑变得更深了。


过去完成时:
The bridge had collapsed before they reached the ravine. = 在他们到达沟壑之前，桥已经倒塌了。
They had never seen such a deep ravine before. = 他们以前从未见过如此深的沟壑。


将来时:
The ravine will be filled with water after the rain. = 雨后沟壑将充满水。
They will explore the ravine tomorrow. = 他们明天将探索这条沟壑。


虚拟语气:
If the ravine were wider, they could cross it easily. = 如果沟壑更宽，他们就能轻松地穿过它。
I wish the ravine were less dangerous. = 我希望沟壑不那么危险。


被动语态:
The ravine was carved by the river. = 沟壑是由河流雕刻而成的。
The ravine is being studied by geologists. = 沟壑正在被地质学家研究。


疑问句:
How deep is the ravine? = 沟壑有多深？
What animals live in the ravine? = 什么动物住在沟壑里？


否定句:
The ravine isn't safe to cross. = 这条沟壑不宜穿越。
He didn't see the ravine until it was too late. = 直到太晚了他才看到沟壑。
