open-mouthed: /ˈoʊpənmaʊθt/ | adj. | 张着嘴的，目瞪口呆的 |  Describes a person with their mouth open, often expressing surprise, astonishment, or shock. It can also imply a state of speechless amazement. |


例句：

He stood there open-mouthed, speechless with surprise.  = 他张着嘴站在那里，惊讶地说不出话来。

The audience watched open-mouthed as the magician pulled a rabbit from his hat. = 观众们张着嘴看着魔术师从帽子里变出一只兔子。


近义词：astonished, amazed, speechless, dumbfounded, aghast

反义词：composed, calm, unfazed


词源:

The word is a compound adjective formed from "open" (meaning not closed) and "mouthed" (referring to the mouth).  "Mouthed" is the past participle of the verb "mouth," meaning "to form words with the mouth."  The combination describes a state where the mouth is in an open position.


用法说明：

open-mouthed is primarily used as an adjective to describe a person's facial expression or posture.  It is not typically used to describe inanimate objects. The context usually implies a strong emotional reaction.  It is often used with verbs that describe reactions to surprising or shocking events, such as "stare," "watch," "gape," and "stand."
