colonized:/ˈkɑːlənaɪzd/ | /ˈkɒlənaɪzd/| v. |殖民；使殖民化 |To establish control over (a place or people) and settle there, typically with the aim of exploiting it economically.  The process involves establishing political, economic, and social dominance over an indigenous population.  This often leads to cultural assimilation and the suppression of local traditions. |例句：The European powers colonized much of Africa and Asia. 欧洲列强殖民了非洲和亚洲的大部分地区。

The British colonized India for many years.  英国殖民印度多年。


近义词：occupied, settled, conquered |反义词：decolonized, liberated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The empire colonizes new territories. = 这个帝国正在殖民新的领土。
They colonize the minds of the people. = 他们在殖民人们的思想。


一般过去时:
Spain colonized much of South America. = 西班牙殖民了南美洲的大部分地区。
The Romans colonized Britain. = 罗马人殖民了不列颠。


现在完成时:
European powers have colonized vast areas of the world. = 欧洲列强已经殖民了世界的大片地区。
They have colonized the island for centuries. = 他们已经殖民了这个岛屿几个世纪了。


过去完成时:
Before the arrival of the Europeans, the indigenous people had not been colonized. = 在欧洲人到来之前，当地人民没有被殖民。
The country had been colonized for over a hundred years. = 这个国家已经被殖民了一百多年了。


将来时:

The company plans to colonize the market. = 这家公司计划垄断市场。(引申义)


虚拟语气:
If they had colonized the area earlier, they would have controlled the resources. = 如果他们早些殖民该地区，他们就会控制资源。

被动语态:

The resources were colonized by the big corporations. = 资源被大公司垄断了。(引申义)


疑问句:

否定句:

They did not successfully colonize the new territory. = 他们没有成功殖民新的领土。
