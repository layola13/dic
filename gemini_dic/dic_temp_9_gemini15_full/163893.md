predetachment: /ˌpriːdɪˈtætʃmənt/ | n. |预先分离；预先拆卸；提前脱离| The act of detaching something beforehand;  the state of being detached before a main event. It often implies a planned separation or removal in advance of a larger operation or process. |例句：The predetachment of the auxiliary fuel tanks improved the aircraft's maneuverability. = 预先分离副油箱提高了飞机的机动性。

The predetachment of the troops from the main force allowed for a rapid response to the emergency. = 部队提前脱离主力部队，从而能够快速应对紧急情况。|近义词：preliminary separation, prior detachment, advance removal |反义词：post-detachment, attachment
