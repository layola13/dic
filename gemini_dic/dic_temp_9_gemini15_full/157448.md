词源:
From French *pirouette*, diminutive of *pirouette*, of unknown origin, possibly onomatopoeic (imitating the sound).  The word entered English in the late 18th century, initially referring to a quick, playful turn or a prank.  Its meaning later evolved to focus on its ballet usage.
