hyperimmunities:/ˌhaɪpərɪˈmjuːnɪtiz/| n. |超免疫性，过度免疫|  A state of having an abnormally high level of immunity, often resulting from exposure to a large amount of antigen or through artificial means.  This can be beneficial in some cases (e.g., providing protection against specific diseases), but it can also lead to adverse effects like autoimmune disorders. |Example:  The patient's hyperimmunities were a result of repeated exposure to the pathogen.  这位病人的超免疫性是反复接触病原体造成的。|近义词：excessive immunity, heightened immunity|反义词：immunodeficiency, immunosuppression
