diwali:/dɪˈwɑːliː/ | n. |排灯节 |Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is a festival of lights celebrated by Hindus, Jains, Sikhs, and some Buddhists.  It is celebrated in autumn every year and is usually five days long.  The festival signifies the victory of good over evil, light over darkness, knowledge over ignorance, and hope over despair.  Celebrations include lighting diyas (small oil lamps), offering prayers, sharing sweets, wearing new clothes, and bursting fireworks. | Example:  We celebrated Diwali with our family, lighting diyas and sharing sweets. 我们和家人一起庆祝排灯节，点亮油灯并分享糖果。


近义词：Deepavali (another name for the festival)
反义词：无
