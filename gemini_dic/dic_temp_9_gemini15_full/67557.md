extraordinarily:/ɪkˈstrɔːrdɪnərəli/| adv.|非常地，极其地|extremely; to a very great extent.  It emphasizes the degree or extent of something.|例句：He was extraordinarily talented. 他才华横溢。
例句：The view from the mountaintop was extraordinarily beautiful. 山顶的景色极其美丽。|近义词：exceptionally, remarkably, unusually, incredibly|反义词：ordinarily, commonly, typically

USAGE->
一般现在时:
She works extraordinarily hard. = 她工作非常努力。
He speaks extraordinarily well. = 他说得非常好。

一般过去时:
The movie was extraordinarily moving. = 这部电影感人至深。
They behaved extraordinarily politely. = 他们的举止异常有礼。

现在完成时:
She has performed extraordinarily well this season. = 她这个赛季表现出色。
He has improved extraordinarily quickly. = 他进步得非常快。

过去完成时:
They had already accomplished extraordinarily much by then. = 到那时他们已经取得了巨大的成就。
She had worked extraordinarily long hours to meet the deadline. = 她为了赶上截止日期加班加点工作。

将来时:
The technology will become extraordinarily advanced in the future. = 未来这项技术将变得极其先进。
He will extraordinarily help us. = 他将非常热心地帮助我们。

虚拟语气:
If he were extraordinarily talented, he would be famous. = 如果他很有天赋，他就会成名。
I wish I were extraordinarily rich. = 我希望我非常富有。

被动语态:
The project was managed extraordinarily efficiently. = 这个项目管理得非常高效。
The problem was handled extraordinarily well. = 这个问题处理得非常好。

疑问句:
Is it extraordinarily difficult to understand? = 理解起来是不是非常困难？
Was the performance extraordinarily impressive? = 表演极其令人印象深刻吗？

否定句:
The food wasn't extraordinarily delicious. = 这食物并不特别美味。
He did not behave extraordinarily. = 他表现得并不特别。


PHRASE->
extraordinarily well = 非常好地
extraordinarily talented = 才华横溢的
extraordinarily beautiful = 极其美丽的
extraordinarily difficult = 极其困难的
extraordinarily successful = 非常成功的


词源：
extraordinarily 来自于 extraordinary + ly。extraordinary  由 extra- (表示“超出，格外”) + ordinary (表示“普通的，平常的”)构成，因此字面意思是“超出寻常的”。  -ly 是副词后缀。
