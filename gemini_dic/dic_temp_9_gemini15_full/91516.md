/haɪoʊˈɡlɒsəs/ | n. |舌骨舌肌|The hyoglossus is a thin, quadrilateral muscle of the tongue.  It originates from the greater cornu and body of the hyoid bone and inserts into the side of the tongue. Its primary function is to depress and retract the tongue.  It plays a crucial role in swallowing and speech. | The hyoglossus muscle helps to depress and retract the tongue. | 近义词: genioglossus (partially), styloglossus (partially) | 反义词: None directly,  but muscles with opposing actions (e.g., genioglossus for protrusion) could be considered functionally antagonistic. | 名词
