peer:/pɪr/| n., v.| 同龄人；仔细看；凝视 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "peer" refers to a person who is equal to another in social standing, age, ability, etc.  As a verb, it means to look closely or intently.

例句:
He's a peer of mine at university. = 他是我的大学同龄人。

She peered into the darkness. = 她仔细地凝视着黑暗。


近义词:
(n.) equal, companion, colleague, contemporary
(v.) glance, gaze, stare, scrutinize


反义词:
(n.) superior, inferior, subordinate
(v.)  (no direct antonyms, but opposites in meaning could be words like "ignore" or "overlook")


词源:
From Old French *per, from Latin par, "equal". The verb sense evolved from the noun sense, referring to looking intently at someone of equal standing.
