juramentum: /ˌdʒuːrəˈmɛntəm/ | n. |誓言，宣誓；宣誓书 | A solemn oath or promise, especially one made under oath in a court of law.  |例句：The defendant took the juramentum before giving his testimony.  被告在作证前宣了誓。


例句：He broke his juramentum to his king. 他违背了他对国王的誓言。


近义词：oath, vow, pledge, affidavit


反义词：breach of oath, perjury


词性:名词


USAGE->
(Juramentum, being a noun, doesn't have verb conjugations.  The following examples show how it's used in different sentence structures.)

简单句:
The soldier swore a juramentum of loyalty.  士兵宣誓效忠。
The judge administered the juramentum. 法官主持宣誓。


复合句:
Before giving evidence, the witness had to take a sacred juramentum. 在作证前，证人必须宣读神圣的誓言。
Because of the gravity of the situation, the juramentum was administered with great solemnity. 由于情况严重，宣誓仪式庄严肃穆。


疑问句:
Did the accused take the juramentum truthfully?  被告是否诚实地宣誓？
Was the juramentum legally binding?  宣誓在法律上是否具有约束力？


否定句:
The witness did not take the juramentum seriously.  证人没有认真对待宣誓。
He denied breaking his juramentum. 他否认违背了他的誓言。

词源:
From Late Latin *iuramentum, from iurare "to swear" (from ius "law, right").  The word ultimately derives from Proto-Indo-European *h₂yeu- "to join, unite," reflecting the binding nature of oaths.
