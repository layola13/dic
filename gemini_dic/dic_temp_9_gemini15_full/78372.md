gesticulators: /ˈdʒɛstɪkjʊleɪtərz/ | n. | 手势表达者；使用手势的人 | A person who uses gestures, especially excessively.  | The professor was a renowned gesticulator, his lectures filled with dramatic hand movements.  这位教授是一位著名的运用手势的人，他的讲座充满了戏剧性的手势。 |  animator, mimicker |  (无明确反义词，可考虑：reserved person, taciturn person) | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gesticulates wildly when he's excited. = 他兴奋时会激动地做手势。
The conductor gesticulates to the orchestra. = 指挥对着乐队做手势。


一般过去时:
She gesticulated angrily at the driver. = 她生气地朝司机做手势。
He gesticulated to emphasize his point. = 他做手势以强调他的观点。


现在完成时:
The speaker has gesticulated throughout his presentation. = 演讲者在整个演讲过程中一直在做手势。
She has gesticulated so much her arms are tired. = 她做了很多手势，胳膊都累了。


过去完成时:
He had gesticulated for several minutes before anyone understood. = 他做了几分钟的手势才有人明白。
They had gesticulated frantically to attract attention. = 他们疯狂地做手势以引起注意。


将来时:

否定句:

She doesn't usually gesticulate. = 她通常不做手势。
