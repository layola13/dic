confidentialities:/ˌkɒnfɪdɛnʃiˈælɪtiz/ | n. |机密性，保密性 |The plural form of confidentiality, referring to multiple instances or aspects of keeping information secret. It emphasizes the various ways in which secrecy is maintained and the importance of protecting sensitive data. |例句：The company's confidentialities were compromised by a data breach.  公司的数据泄露损害了其多方面的保密性。

The numerous confidentialities surrounding the project required strict security measures. 项目涉及的诸多机密信息都需要严格的安全措施。|近义词：secrecies, privacies, secrets |反义词：publicity, openness


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense examples are not applicable.  The usage focuses on how the noun functions in different sentence structures.

Example Sentences:

Singular:  The confidentiality of the client's information is paramount. (客户信息的保密性至关重要。)

Plural: The confidentialities surrounding the merger negotiations were carefully protected. (围绕并购谈判的各项机密信息都得到了妥善保护。)

In a question:  Were all the confidentialities maintained throughout the investigation? (在整个调查过程中，所有机密信息都得到维护了吗？)

In a negative sentence: The company failed to uphold its confidentialities regarding employee data. (公司未能维护其关于员工数据的保密性。)

In a passive sentence:  The confidentialities were leaked to the press. (机密信息被泄露给了媒体。)



PHRASE->
Maintain confidentialities: 保持机密
Breach of confidentialities: 违反保密
Disclosure of confidentialities: 泄露机密
Protect confidentialities: 保护机密
Respect confidentialities: 尊重机密


WORD ORIGIN->
The word "confidentialities" is derived from the adjective "confidential," which in turn comes from the Latin word "confidentia," meaning "trust, confidence."  The suffix "-alities" creates a plural noun form indicating multiple instances of confidentiality.
