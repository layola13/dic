predictable:/prɪˈdɪktəbəl/ | adj. | 可预测的，意料之中的 |  easily foreseen or known in advance.  Something that is predictable happens or is likely to happen as expected. | The ending of the movie was predictable.  这部电影的结局是可以预测的。 |  expected, foreseeable, anticipated | unpredictable, surprising, unexpected |  形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The weather is predictable today. = 今天的天气是可以预测的。
His behavior is predictable. = 他的行为是可以预测的。


一般过去时:
The outcome of the election was predictable. = 大选的结果是可以预测的。
Her reaction was predictable. = 她的反应是可以预料的。


现在完成时:
The results have been predictable. = 结果是可以预料的。
Everything has been predictably dull. = 一切都意料之中的乏味。


过去完成时:
It had been predictable that he would fail. = 他会失败是可以预料的。

将来时:
The weather will be predictable tomorrow. = 明天天气将会是可预测的。

虚拟语气:
If the weather were predictable, we would plan a picnic. = 如果天气可以预测，我们会计划野餐。
If his actions were predictable, we could avoid trouble. = 如果他的行为可以预测，我们可以避免麻烦。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
The ending was not predictable. = 结局不可预测。
His actions were not predictable. = 他的行为不可预测。

词源:
The word "predictable" comes from the verb "predict," which itself comes from the Latin word "praedicere," meaning "to say beforehand" or "to foretell."  The suffix "-able" indicates capability or possibility.  Therefore, "predictable" literally means "capable of being predicted."
