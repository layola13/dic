groundswells:/ˈɡraʊndswɛlz/ | n. |涌浪，长浪 |Long, powerful ocean waves generated by distant storms.  These waves can travel vast distances and are characterized by their long wavelength and period.  They are distinct from wind waves, which are created by local wind conditions.|例句：The surfers were waiting for the groundswells to arrive.冲浪者们在等待涌浪到来。

The groundswells pounded the shore relentlessly. 涌浪无情地拍打着海岸。


近义词：swell, long wave | 反义词：ripples, chop


词源:  The word "groundswell" is a compound word. "Ground" refers to the bottom or base, in this case, the distant origin of the waves. "Swell" refers to a rise or increase in the level of something, in this context, the rising and falling of ocean water in wave form.  The combination suggests waves originating from a distant source, moving across large expanses of water.
