serialised: /ˈsɪəriəlaɪzd/ | v. | 序列化；连载；分期付款 | To convert data into a sequence of bytes or other units suitable for storage or transmission, or to publish (a story etc) in a series of installments. |

例句：

The data was serialised before being sent over the network.  = 数据在通过网络发送之前被序列化了。

The novel was serialised in a magazine over several months. = 这部小说在几个月的时间里分期连载在杂志上。


近义词：serialize,  serialized (American spelling)


反义词：deserialise (反序列化), publish as a whole (完整出版)


词性:动词

词源:
The word "serialised" comes from the word "serial," meaning "consisting of, or arranged in, a series."  The suffix "-ised" (or "-ized" in American English) is a verb suffix indicating the process of making something into a series.  The root traces back to the Latin word "series," meaning "a succession, series."
