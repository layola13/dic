ichthyophagous: /ɪkθiˈɒfəɡəs/ | adj. | 食鱼的，以鱼为食的 |  Eating fish; fish-eating.  The term refers to animals whose diet consists primarily or significantly of fish.  | 例句：Many seabirds are ichthyophagous. 许多海鸟都是食鱼的。


Many dolphins are ichthyophagous, relying on fish as their main food source.  许多海豚是食鱼的，鱼是它们的主要食物来源。


词源:  From Greek  ἰχθύς (ikhthús, "fish") + -phagous (from φαγεῖν (phagein), "to eat").


近义词: piscivorous


反义词:  There isn't a direct antonym, but words describing diets that exclude fish, such as herbivorous or carnivorous (excluding fish) could be considered contextual opposites.
