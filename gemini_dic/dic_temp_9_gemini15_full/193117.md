secularizations: /ˌsɛkjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. | 世俗化（过程）；脱教权化 | The process of becoming secular; the removal of something from religious or ecclesiastical influence or control.  It often refers to the historical process in which religion loses its social and cultural significance.  The plural form indicates multiple instances of this process. |

例句：

The secularizations of European societies in the 19th and 20th centuries led to significant changes in social structures.  = 19 世纪和 20 世纪欧洲社会的世俗化导致了社会结构的重大变化。

The rapid secularizations of education systems in many countries have been a subject of debate. = 许多国家教育体系的快速世俗化一直是一个争论的话题。


近义词：desacralization, secularization processes, laicization

反义词：sacralization, clericalization, theocratization


词性：名词(plural)
