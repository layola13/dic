dumbfounders:/dʌmˈfaʊndərz/| vt.|使惊呆；使目瞪口呆|To make someone speechless with astonishment.|The unexpected news dumbfounded her.|近义词：astonish, amaze, bewilder|反义词：enlighten, clarify

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The magician's trick dumbfounds the audience. = 魔术师的戏法使观众惊呆了。
His sudden outburst dumbfounds everyone present. = 他突然的爆发使在场的所有人都惊呆了。

一般过去时:
The shocking revelation dumbfounded the investigators. = 令人震惊的揭露使调查人员大吃一惊。
Her incredible talent dumbfounded the judges. = 她令人难以置信的天赋使评委们惊呆了。

现在完成时:
The scale of the disaster has dumbfounded the world. = 灾难的规模震惊了全世界。
The unexpected results have dumbfounded the scientists. = 出乎意料的结果使科学家们惊呆了。

过去完成时:
I had been dumbfounded by the news before I could react. = 在我做出反应之前，我已经对这个消息感到震惊了。
They had been dumbfounded by the complexity of the problem. = 他们已经被问题的复杂性弄糊涂了。

将来时:
The announcement will undoubtedly dumbfound many people. = 这一声明无疑会使许多人感到震惊。
The results of the experiment will dumbfound the scientific community. = 实验的结果将使科学界大吃一惊。

虚拟语气:

I wish I hadn't been dumbfounded; I should have asked more questions. = 我希望我没有那么震惊；我应该多问几个问题。

被动语态:

The judge was dumbfounded by the defendant's confession. = 审判员被被告的供词惊呆了。


疑问句:
Was he dumbfounded by the news? = 他被这个消息惊呆了吗？
Were they dumbfounded by the sudden change of plan? = 他们被计划的突然改变惊呆了吗？


否定句:
The news did not dumbfound her; she remained calm. = 这个消息并没有让她惊呆；她保持冷静。
He was not dumbfounded by the criticism; he expected it. = 他并没有被批评惊呆；他预料到了。

词源:
The word "dumbfounder" is a combination of "dumb" (meaning silent or speechless) and "founder" (meaning to sink or collapse, in this context, to overwhelm).  It's a relatively late entry into the English language, likely emerging in the 16th or 17th century.
