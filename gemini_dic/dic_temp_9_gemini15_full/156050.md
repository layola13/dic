photoorganotrophy: /ˌfoʊtoʊˌɔːrɡænəˈtrɒfi/ | n. | 光能有机营养 | The process by which organisms obtain energy from light and use organic compounds as a carbon source.  This metabolic strategy combines phototrophy (light energy harvesting) and organotrophy (organic carbon utilization). | Example: Many purple non-sulfur bacteria exhibit photoorganotrophy, using light energy and organic molecules for growth.  许多不产硫紫细菌表现出光能有机营养，利用光能和有机分子生长。 | 近义词: photoheterotrophy | 反义词: chemoautotrophy, chemolithotrophy
