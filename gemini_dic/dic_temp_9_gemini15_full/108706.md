lean-up-on:/ˌliːnˈʌpɒn/ | v. |依靠；依赖；凭借 | To rely on someone or something for support, help, or guidance.  |例句：I lean-up-on my friends for emotional support. 我依靠我的朋友寻求情感支持。
He leaned up on the counter, exhausted. 他靠在柜台上，精疲力尽。|近义词：rely on, depend on, count on |反义词：ignore, neglect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I lean on my family for support. = 我依靠我的家人来获得支持。
They lean on their experience to make decisions. = 他们依靠他们的经验来做决定。


一般过去时:
She leaned on her cane to walk. = 她拄着拐杖走路。
He leaned on his friend for advice. = 他向朋友寻求建议。


现在完成时:
I have leaned on my faith throughout difficult times. = 在艰难时期，我一直依靠我的信仰。
We have leaned on each other for years. = 多年来，我们一直互相依靠。


过去完成时:
He had leaned on his parents for financial help before finding a job. = 在找到工作之前，他曾依靠父母的经济资助。
She had leaned on her colleagues for support during the project. = 在项目期间，她曾依靠同事的支持。


将来时:
I will lean on my mentors for guidance in my career. = 我将在职业生涯中依靠我的导师的指导。
They will lean on technology to improve efficiency. = 他们将依靠技术来提高效率。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would lean on your strengths. = 如果我是你，我会依靠你的优势。
I wish I had leaned on my friends sooner. = 我希望我早点依靠我的朋友。


被动语态:  (This verb is rarely used passively)
The decision was heavily leaned upon by the committee. = 委员会非常依赖这个决定。(This sentence is awkward and unnatural.)


疑问句:
Who do you lean on in times of crisis? = 在危机时刻你依靠谁？
What resources do you lean on to complete the project? = 你依靠什么资源来完成项目？


否定句:
I don't lean on anyone for help. = 我不依靠任何人来帮忙。
They didn't lean on their intuition in that decision. = 他们在这个决定中没有依靠直觉。


PHRASE->
lean on someone for support = 依靠某人支持
lean on someone for help = 依靠某人帮助
lean on experience = 依靠经验
lean on one's strength = 依靠某人的优势
lean on the evidence = 依靠证据
