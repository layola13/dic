redeed:/riːˈdiːd/| n.|复立契约；再次授予；重新授予|A redeed is a legal document that confirms or re-establishes a right or title, often relating to land ownership.  It essentially repeats or reaffirms a previous deed.  It might be used to correct errors in a previous deed, to clarify ownership, or to transfer ownership again after a prior transfer.|The old deed was flawed, so they executed a redeed to correct the errors.  旧契约有缺陷，所以他们执行了一份新的契约来纠正错误。|reconfirmation, reaffirmation|cancellation, revocation|名词
