ruining:/ˈruːɪnɪŋ/ | v. |毁坏；破坏；糟蹋 |  The act of causing serious damage or destruction to something; making something worse.  |例句：He's ruining his health by smoking. 他吸烟正在毁坏自己的健康。


例句：The rain is ruining the picnic.  雨正在毁了野餐。


近义词：damaging, destroying, spoiling, wrecking


反义词：improving, repairing, restoring, enhancing


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is ruining his chances. 他正在毁掉他的机会。
They are ruining the environment.  他们在破坏环境。


一般过去时:
The storm ruined the crops. 暴风雨毁坏了庄稼。
She ruined her dress. 她把她的裙子弄坏了。


现在完成时:
The scandal has ruined his reputation. 这场丑闻毁了他的名声。
Years of neglect have ruined the building. 多年的忽视毁坏了这栋建筑。


过去完成时:
He had ruined his career before he was 30. 他30岁之前就毁了他的事业。
They had ruined their lives through gambling. 他们因赌博而毁掉了自己的生活。


将来时:
This policy will ruin the economy.这项政策将会毁掉经济。
The new law might ruin small businesses. 新法可能会毁掉小型企业。


虚拟语气:
If he continued to drink, he would ruin his health. 如果他继续喝酒，他会毁掉自己的健康。
If she had been more careful, she wouldn't have ruined her chances. 如果她更小心点，她就不会毁掉自己的机会了。


被动语态:
His reputation was ruined by the scandal. 他的名声被丑闻毁了。
The crops were ruined by the frost. 庄稼被霜冻毁了。


疑问句:
Is he ruining his life? 他正在毁掉自己的生活吗？
Are they ruining the project? 他们正在破坏这个项目吗？


否定句:
He isn't ruining his health. 他没有在毁掉他的健康。
They didn't ruin the party. 他们没有毁掉派对。


PHRASE->
ruin one's life: 毁掉某人的一生
ruin one's reputation: 毁掉某人的名誉
ruin someone's plans: 打乱某人的计划
ruin the mood: 破坏气氛
fall into ruin: 衰败；破败


词源:
The word "ruin" ultimately derives from the Latin word "ruina," meaning "fall," "ruin," or "destruction."  This Latin word has roots in the verb "ruere," meaning "to fall" or "to rush." Over time, the word evolved through Old French ("ruine") before entering English.
