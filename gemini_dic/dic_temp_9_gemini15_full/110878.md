lionizer: /ˈlaɪənaɪzər/ | n. | 崇拜者；吹捧者 | One who lionizes; one who treats someone as a celebrity.  | The young writer was a lionizer of famous artists, constantly seeking their attention and approval.  年轻的作家是著名艺术家的崇拜者，不断寻求他们的关注和认可。| adulator, flatterer, idolater | critic, detractor | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He lionizes famous actors. = 他崇拜著名演员。
She lionizes every successful entrepreneur. = 她崇拜每一个成功的企业家。


一般过去时:
They lionized the athlete after his victory. = 他们在运动员获胜后崇拜他。
The media lionized the pop star. = 媒体吹捧这位流行歌星。


现在完成时:
The town has lionized the local hero for years. = 这个镇多年来一直崇拜这位当地英雄。
She has lionized him since his first book was published. = 自从他的第一本书出版以来，她就一直崇拜他。


过去完成时:
They had lionized him before he even achieved anything significant. = 甚至在他取得任何重大成就之前，他们就已经崇拜他了。
The critics had lionized her work before the public even knew her name. = 在公众甚至不知道她的名字之前，评论家们就已经赞扬她的作品了。


将来时:
They will lionize him for his contribution to science. = 他们将因他对科学的贡献而崇拜他。
The public will lionize her after her remarkable achievement. = 在她取得非凡成就之后，公众将崇拜她。


虚拟语气:
If he were a famous artist, people would lionize him. = 如果他是一位著名的艺术家，人们就会崇拜他。
I wish they would stop lionizing him so much. = 我希望他们能停止如此崇拜他。


被动语态:
He was lionized by the press after his speech. = 演讲后，他受到媒体的吹捧。
The young artist was lionized by critics. = 这位年轻艺术家受到评论家的推崇。


疑问句:
Who are they lionizing this year? = 今年他们崇拜谁？
Why are people lionizing him so much? = 为什么人们如此崇拜他？


否定句:
They did not lionize him. = 他们没有崇拜他。
The public did not lionize the politician's efforts. = 公众没有赞扬这位政治家的努力。
