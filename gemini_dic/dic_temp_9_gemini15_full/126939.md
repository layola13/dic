narrate:/nəˈreɪt/| vt.|叙述，讲述|To tell a story or give an account of an event or series of events.|例句：He narrated the story of his childhood. 他讲述了他童年的故事。|近义词：relate, recount, describe|反义词：listen


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He narrates the story with great passion. = 他充满激情地讲述着这个故事。
She narrates her experiences in a captivating way. = 她以引人入胜的方式讲述着她的经历。


一般过去时:
The author narrated the events leading up to the war. = 作者叙述了导致战争发生的事件。
He narrated his adventures in the Amazon rainforest. = 他讲述了他亚马逊雨林探险的经历。


现在完成时:
She has narrated several best-selling novels. = 她已经讲述过几部畅销小说。
He has narrated his life story in his autobiography. = 他在他的自传中讲述了他的生活故事。


过去完成时:
She had narrated the incident to the police before they arrived. = 在警察到达之前，她已经向警方讲述了这起事件。
He had narrated the whole story several times. = 他已经多次讲述了整个故事。


将来时:
He will narrate the tale to the children tomorrow. = 他明天将向孩子们讲述这个故事。
She will narrate her journey across the country in her upcoming book. = 她将在即将出版的书中讲述她横跨国家的旅程。


虚拟语气:
If he narrated the truth, people might believe him. = 如果他讲述的是真相，人们可能会相信他。
I wish he narrated the story more clearly. = 我希望他更清晰地讲述这个故事。


被动语态:
The story was narrated with great skill. = 这个故事讲述得很巧妙。
The events of the day were narrated in the newspaper. = 当天的事件在报纸上被报道。


疑问句:
Who narrated the documentary? = 谁讲述了这部纪录片？
Will you narrate the story for us? = 你会为我们讲述这个故事吗？


否定句:
He did not narrate the events accurately. = 他没有准确地叙述事件。
She refused to narrate the details of the accident. = 她拒绝讲述事故的细节。


PHRASE->
narrate a story = 讲述一个故事
narrate an event = 描述一个事件
narrate one's experience = 讲述某人的经历
narrate the facts = 陈述事实
narrate from memory = 从记忆中讲述
