decarbonizer: /ˌdiːˈkɑːrbənˌaɪzər/ | n. | 脱碳剂，减碳装置 | A decarbonizer is a substance or device used to remove carbon or carbon dioxide from something, typically to mitigate climate change.  It can refer to technologies or processes aimed at reducing carbon emissions or removing existing carbon from the atmosphere or other systems. | Example: The company is investing heavily in the development of new decarbonizers for industrial processes. 这家公司正在大力投资开发用于工业过程的新型脱碳剂。 | 近义词: carbon remover, carbon capture technology | 反义词: carbon emitter, carbon intensifier |
