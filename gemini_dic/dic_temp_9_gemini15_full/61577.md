emerge:/ɪˈmɜːrdʒ/| /ɪˈmɝːdʒ/ | vi. |出现；浮现；兴起；摆脱 | to come out of something or come into view from a hidden or enclosed place; to become known or apparent; to develop or arise. |例句：The sun emerged from behind the clouds. 太阳从云后出现。
The truth eventually emerged. 真相最终大白。

近义词：appear, arise, come out, surface | 反义词：disappear, vanish, sink

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun emerges from behind the clouds every morning. 太阳每天早晨从云层后面升起。
New challenges emerge constantly in our rapidly changing world. 在我们快速变化的世界中，新的挑战不断涌现。

一般过去时:
The seedlings emerged from the soil after a week. 幼苗一周后从土壤中冒出来。
A new understanding of the problem emerged during the discussion. 在讨论中，对这个问题有了新的理解。

现在完成时:
Many new technologies have emerged in the past decade. 在过去的十年里，出现了许多新技术。
The truth has finally emerged after years of investigation. 多年调查后，真相终于大白。

过去完成时:
By the time I arrived, the winner had already emerged. 当我到达时，获胜者已经产生了。
It had emerged that the witness was unreliable. 已经发现证人不可靠。


将来时:
New opportunities will emerge as the market expands. 随着市场的扩大，新的机会将会出现。
We believe that a solution will eventually emerge. 我们相信最终会找到解决方案。


虚拟语气:
If more evidence emerged, the case would be reopened. 如果出现更多证据，案件将被重新审理。
I wish a better solution would emerge soon. 我希望很快能出现更好的解决方案。


被动语态:
The details of the accident were slowly emerging. 事故的细节正在慢慢浮出水面。
The new design was finally emerging after months of work. 新的设计经过几个月的努力终于问世了。


疑问句:
When will the results emerge? 结果何时会出来？

否定句:
