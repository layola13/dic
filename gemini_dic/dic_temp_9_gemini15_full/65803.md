evident:/ˈɛvɪdənt/| adj.|明显的，显然的 |Clearly seen or understood; obvious.  |The evidence is evident that he is guilty.  证据表明他是有罪的。|obvious, clear, apparent, manifest|hidden, obscure, unclear, ambiguous

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The evidence is evident. = 证据是明显的。
It's evident that he's lying. = 很明显他在说谎。

一般过去时:
The results were evident. = 结果是明显的。
It was evident that she was upset. = 很明显她很难过。


现在完成时:
It has become evident that we need more help. = 我们需要更多帮助，这一点已经很明显了。
It has been evident for some time that the project is failing. = 项目失败已经有一段时间很明显了。


过去完成时:
It had become evident that the plan was flawed. = 计划有缺陷这一点已经很明显了。
It had been evident to everyone that he was unhappy. = 他不高兴这一点对每个人来说都很明显。


将来时:
It will be evident soon whether the treatment is effective. = 治疗是否有效很快就会很明显。
It will become evident that his actions were wrong. = 他的行为是错误的这一点将变得很明显。


虚拟语气:
If it were evident, we would act immediately. = 如果很明显，我们会立即采取行动。
It would be evident that he is guilty if we had more evidence. = 如果我们有更多证据，他是有罪的这一点将是显而易见的。


被动语态:
The truth was made evident by the investigation.  真相通过调查变得很明显。
The problem was evident to everyone present. = 这个问题在场的所有人都看得出来。


疑问句:
Is it evident that he is the culprit? = 很明显他是罪犯吗？
Isn't it evident that she's talented? = 她很有天赋，不是吗？


否定句:
It was not evident what caused the accident. = 事故的起因尚不清楚。
It isn't evident to me why he did that. = 我不明白他为什么要那样做。


PHRASE->
It is evident that... = 很明显……
It is quite evident that... = 很明显……
It becomes evident that... = 越来越明显……
It was made evident that... = ……被证明很明显
Self-evident = 不言而喻的
