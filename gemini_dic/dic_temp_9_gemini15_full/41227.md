coralligenous: /ˌkɒrælˈɪdʒənəs/ | adj. |珊瑚礁的，珊瑚生成的 | Relating to or characteristic of coralligenous habitats, which are biogenic reefs formed by the accumulation of calcareous skeletons of corals and other organisms.  These habitats are typically found in relatively deep, clear waters. | Example: The coralligenous reefs off the coast of the Mediterranean are incredibly diverse ecosystems. 珊瑚地中海沿岸的珊瑚礁是极其多样化的生态系统。 |近义词：reefal, coralline | 反义词：None specifically, but words suggesting absence of coral reefs could be considered antonyms, like "sandy," "muddy," or "plain".
