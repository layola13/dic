hunch:/hʌntʃ/ | n. v. | 预感，直觉；弯腰驼背 |  A hunch is a feeling or guess based on intuition rather than facts.  To hunch is to bend the upper body forward. |例句：I had a hunch that he was lying. = 我预感他在撒谎。

I hunch over my desk when I work. = 我工作时总是弯腰驼背地伏在桌子上。

近义词：feeling, intuition, suspicion, hunchback | 反义词：certainty, fact, proof


USAGE->

一般现在时:
I have a hunch he'll be late. = 我预感他会迟到。
She often hunches over her books. = 她经常弯腰驼背地看书。


一般过去时:
I had a hunch it would rain. = 我预感会下雨。
He hunched his shoulders against the cold. = 他耸起肩膀以抵御寒冷。


现在完成时:
I've had a hunch about this for weeks. = 我对这件事已经预感了好几个星期了。
She's hunched over her computer all day. = 她整天都弯腰驼背地对着电脑。


过去完成时:
I had had a hunch that something was wrong. = 我已经预感到有什么不对劲了。
He had hunched over his work for hours. = 他已经伏案工作了好几个小时。


将来时:
I think I'll have a hunch about the results. = 我想我会对结果有所预感。
He will hunch over his desk again tomorrow. = 他明天还会再次弯腰驼背地伏在桌子上。


虚拟语气:
If I had a hunch, I would tell you. = 如果我有预感，我会告诉你的。
If he hadn't hunched over so much, his back wouldn't hurt. = 如果他不那么驼背，他的背就不会疼了。


被动语态:
His back was hunched from years of bad posture. = 他多年的不良姿势导致他驼背。
(The feeling of a hunch cannot be easily passivized)


疑问句:
Do you have a hunch about who did it? = 你对是谁干的有什么预感吗？
Does he always hunch over like that? = 他总是那样驼背吗？


否定句:
I don't have a hunch about the future. = 我对未来没有预感。
He doesn't hunch over when he's working. = 他工作时不驼背。


PHRASE->
have a hunch = 有预感

a hunch that = ...的预感


词源:

The noun "hunch" ultimately derives from the Old Norse word "hǫnk," meaning "hook" or "crook."  This refers to the physical posture of hunching over, which is a bent or hooked shape. The verb sense developed later from the visual image of the bent posture.
