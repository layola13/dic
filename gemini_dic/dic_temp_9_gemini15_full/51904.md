dhobi:/ˈdəʊbiː/| n. | 洗衣工；洗衣店 | A dhobi is a person whose job is to wash and iron clothes, often working independently or as part of a dhobi ghat (a communal laundry).  The term is primarily used in South Asia. |  The dhobi collected the clothes early in the morning.  洗衣工一大早就来收衣服了。 | laundromat, washerman |  (no direct antonym)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dhobi washes clothes every day. = 洗衣工每天洗衣服。
He works as a dhobi in the city. = 他在城里当洗衣工。

PHRASE->
dhobi ghat (洗衣场): A communal laundry area.
dhobi's charges (洗衣费): The cost of having clothes laundered by a dhobi.
dhobi ka kaam (洗衣工的工作): The work of a dhobi.
hire a dhobi (雇佣洗衣工): To employ a dhobi to wash clothes.
dhobi's services (洗衣服务): The services provided by a dhobi.
