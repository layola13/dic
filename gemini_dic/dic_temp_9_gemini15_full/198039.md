shoe:/ʃuː/| n.|鞋|A covering for the foot, typically made of leather, and having a sole and heel.|例句：I need to buy a new pair of shoes. 我需要买一双新鞋。|近义词：boot, slipper, sandal|反义词:barefoot


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He wears shoes every day. 他每天都穿鞋。
She often buys expensive shoes. 她经常买昂贵的鞋。


一般过去时:
I bought new shoes yesterday. 我昨天买了双新鞋。
They wore their best shoes to the wedding. 他们穿着最好的鞋子去参加婚礼。


现在完成时:
I have worn these shoes for years. 我已经穿这些鞋子好几年了。
She has bought three pairs of shoes this month. 这个月她已经买了三双鞋。


过去完成时:
I had worn out my old shoes before I bought new ones. 在我买新鞋之前，我的旧鞋已经穿坏了。
They had polished their shoes before the ceremony. 典礼前他们已经擦亮了鞋子。


将来时:
I will buy new shoes next week. 我下周会买新鞋。
She will wear comfortable shoes for the hike. 她会穿舒适的鞋去远足。


虚拟语气:
If I had more money, I would buy those shoes. 如果我有更多钱，我会买那些鞋。
I wish I had bought those shoes when they were on sale. 我真希望我在打折的时候买了那些鞋。


被动语态:
The shoes were made in Italy. 这些鞋是意大利制造的。
The shoes are being repaired now. 鞋子正在修理中。


疑问句:
Do you like my new shoes? 你喜欢我的新鞋吗？
What kind of shoes are you looking for? 你在找什么样的鞋？


否定句:
I don't need new shoes. 我不需要新鞋。
She didn't wear her shoes inside. 她没有在室内穿鞋。


PHRASE->

in someone's shoes = 设身处地
step into someone's shoes = 接替某人的工作
on the shoestring = 勉强维持；省吃俭用
be in someone's shoes = 处在某人的境地
