fragment:/ˈfræɡmənt/ | n. |碎片；片段；部分 | A fragment is a small part broken off or separated from something larger.  It can refer to a piece of something broken, a short passage from a book, a piece of music, or a portion of a larger whole. |例句：The archaeologist carefully pieced together the fragments of pottery. 考古学家小心翼翼地拼凑起陶器的碎片。

例句：He only read a fragment of the novel before falling asleep. 他只读了一小部分小说就睡着了。

近义词：piece, part, bit, section | 反义词：whole, entirety


词源:  From Old French *fragment, from Latin fragmentum "a broken piece," from frangere "to break."


用法说明:

名词用法:

单数形式：a fragment, the fragment
复数形式：fragments

可以修饰fragment的形容词：small, large, broken, incomplete, significant, important, interesting, etc.


例句:

1. The vase shattered into many fragments.  花瓶碎成许多碎片。
2.  She only remembered fragments of her childhood. 她只记得童年的一些片段。
3. The composer used a fragment of a folk song in his symphony.  作曲家在他的交响乐中使用了民间歌曲的片段。
4. This is just a fragment of the evidence we have. 这只是我们掌握证据的一小部分。
5. The police recovered several fragments of the broken window. 警察找回了几块破碎窗户的碎片。


PHRASE->
