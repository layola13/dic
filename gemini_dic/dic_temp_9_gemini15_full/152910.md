pedestaled: /ˈpɛdəstæld/ | v. |  使…放在基座上；抬高；推崇 | To place on a pedestal; to elevate to a position of high regard. | The statue was pedestaled in the town square.  这座雕像被安放在城镇广场的基座上。  He pedestaled her as the perfect woman. 他把她奉为完美的女性。| exalt, elevate, glorify | denigrate, belittle, humiliate | Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They pedestal the artist's work. = 他们推崇这位艺术家的作品。
He pedestals his mother above all others. = 他把母亲看得高于一切。


一般过去时:
The museum pedestaled the ancient artifact. = 博物馆将这件古代文物放在基座上。
She pedestaled him as a hero after his brave act. = 他的英勇行为之后，她把他奉为英雄。


现在完成时:
They have pedestaled the new CEO. = 他们已经将新任首席执行官捧上了高位。
We have pedestaled this issue for too long. = 我们已经过分抬高这个问题太久了。


过去完成时:
They had pedestaled the leader before his downfall. = 在他垮台之前，他们已经把他捧上了领导人的位置。
She had pedestaled him in her mind for years. = 多年来，她在心里一直把他奉为神明。


将来时:
They will pedestal the winner of the competition. = 他们将把比赛的获胜者推崇备至。
The company will pedestal its new product. = 公司将大力推广其新产品。


虚拟语气:
If they pedestaled him, he would be pleased. = 如果他们推崇他，他会很高兴。
I wish they had not pedestaled that idea so much. = 我真希望他们没有那么推崇那个想法。


被动语态:
The sculpture was pedestaled in the gallery. = 雕塑在画廊里被摆放在基座上。
His ideas were pedestaled by his colleagues. = 他的想法受到了同事们的推崇。


疑问句:
Was the painting pedestaled in the exhibition? = 这幅画在展览会上被放在基座上吗？
Are they pedestaling the wrong person? = 他们是不是在推崇错误的人？


否定句:
They did not pedestal his achievements. = 他们没有推崇他的成就。
She did not want to be pedestaled. = 她不想被奉为神明。


PHRASE->
be pedestaled = 被推崇
highly pedestaled = 被高度推崇
falsely pedestaled = 被虚假地推崇
unnecessarily pedestaled = 被不必要地推崇
publicly pedestaled = 公开地推崇

词源:
源自pedestal (基座) + -ed (动词后缀)，字面意思是“放在基座上”，引申为“推崇，抬高”。
