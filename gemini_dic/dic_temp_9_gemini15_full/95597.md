incommodate: /ɪnˈkɒmədeɪt/ | vt. | 使…不舒服；造成不便；打扰 | To cause inconvenience or discomfort to.  | The noise from the construction site incommoded the residents.  施工现场的噪音扰乱了居民。 | inconvenience, disturb, bother | comfort, please | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud music incommodes me. = 吵闹的音乐使我不舒服。
This schedule incommodes my plans. = 这个时间安排打乱了我的计划。


一般过去时:
The unexpected rain incommoded our picnic. = 突如其来的雨打乱了我们的野餐。
The traffic jam incommoded his journey to work. = 交通堵塞使他上班的行程很不方便。


现在完成时:
The constant interruptions have incommoded my work. = 持续不断的打断已经影响了我的工作。
The recent changes have incommoded many employees. = 最近的变动已经给许多员工带来了不便。


过去完成时:
The broken lift had incommoded the tenants for days. = 电梯坏了几天，给租户带来了不便。
The prior commitments had incommoded her ability to attend the conference. = 先前的承诺使她无法参加会议。


将来时:
The planned roadworks will incommode drivers for several weeks. = 计划中的道路工程将使司机在几周内感到不便。
The flight delays will incommode many passengers. = 航班延误将给许多乘客带来不便。


虚拟语气:
If the weather were better, it wouldn't incommode our trip. = 如果天气好一点，就不会影响我们的旅行。
I wish the noise wouldn't incommode my sleep. = 我希望噪音不会影响我的睡眠。


被动语态:
We were incommoded by the unexpected guest. = 我们被意外的客人打扰了。
The residents were incommoded by the lack of parking spaces. = 居民因缺乏停车位而感到不便。


疑问句:
Does the new policy incommode you in any way? = 新政策在任何方面都给您带来不便吗？
Will the changes incommode the workflow? = 这些改变会影响工作流程吗？


否定句:
The new system doesn't incommode the users. = 新系统不会给用户带来不便。
The hotel staff tried their best not to incommode the guests. = 酒店员工尽力避免打扰客人。


PHRASE->
incommode sb/sth: 打扰某人/某事

highly incommoded: 非常不舒服
seriously incommoded: 严重的不便
greatly incommoded: 极度的不便

词源:
Comes from the Old French word "incommoder," which means "to be inconvenient" or "to trouble".  The prefix "in-" means "in" or "into," and "commoder" is related to the Latin word "commodus," meaning "convenient" or "suitable."  Therefore, "incommodate" literally means "to make inconvenient."
