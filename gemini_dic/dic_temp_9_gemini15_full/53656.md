disheveled:/dɪˈʃɛvəld/| adj. |蓬乱的；凌乱的 |Untidy and disarranged in appearance.  Typically used to describe hair or clothing. |例句：Her hair was disheveled from the wind.  她的头发被风吹乱了。|例句：He looked disheveled and exhausted after the long journey. 长途旅行后，他看起来蓬乱而疲惫。|近义词：rumpled, tousled, unkempt, messy |反义词：neat, tidy, orderly

词源:
The word "disheveled" comes from the Old French word "descheveler," meaning "to uncomb the hair."  The prefix "dis-" indicates negation or reversal, and "-chevel" relates to the hair.  Therefore, the word's original meaning was literally "to un-hair" or "to make the hair loose and unkempt."
