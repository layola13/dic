misguided:/ˌmɪsˈɡaɪdɪd/| adj.|被误导的；错误的；盲目的|Based on or resulting from a misunderstanding or a mistaken belief.  It suggests a lack of proper guidance or understanding, leading to flawed actions or judgments.|例句：His misguided attempt to help actually made things worse.  他的好心办坏事，反而使情况更糟了。

例句：Her misguided loyalty to her friend led her to make a terrible mistake. 她对朋友的盲目忠诚导致她犯了一个严重的错误。


近义词：erroneous, mistaken, misguided, flawed, faulty, ill-advised, wrongheaded, ill-conceived

反义词：well-guided, insightful, well-informed, judicious, sensible


词源: mis- (wrongly, badly) + guide (to lead or direct) + -ed (past participle suffix)  The word combines the prefix "mis-" indicating something wrong or bad, with the verb "guide," resulting in an adjective describing something or someone led astray.
