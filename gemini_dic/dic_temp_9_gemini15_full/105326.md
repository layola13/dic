kick:/kɪk/| vt. n.|踢，猛击；反冲；刺激；活力|To strike or propel with the foot.  It can also refer to a sudden increase or boost, or a strong influence.  |例句：He kicked the ball across the field. 他把球踢过了球场。

例句：The new marketing campaign gave sales a real kick. 新的营销活动给销售带来了真正的刺激。|近义词：strike, punt, propel, boost|反义词：pull, push (in some contexts)

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He kicks the ball every day. = 他每天都踢球。
She kicks the can down the road. = 她把罐子踢到路边去了。

一般过去时:
I kicked the door open. = 我一脚踢开了门。
They kicked the tires before buying the car. = 他们在买车之前踢了踢轮胎。

现在完成时:
He has kicked the habit of smoking. = 他已经戒掉了抽烟的习惯。
She has kicked several goals in the match. = 她在这场比赛中已经踢进了好几个球。

过去完成时:
He had kicked the bucket before the doctor arrived. = 在医生到达之前他已经去世了。
She had kicked the habit long before she met him. = 在她遇见他很久以前她就戒掉了这个习惯。

将来时:
I will kick the ball to you. = 我会把球踢给你。
They will kick off the meeting at 2 pm. = 他们将在下午两点开始会议。

虚拟语气:
If I kicked that ball harder, it might have gone further. = 如果我踢得更用力一点，球可能飞得更远。
I wish I had kicked that goal. = 我真希望我进了那个球。

被动语态:
The ball was kicked into the net. = 球被踢进了球门。
The door was kicked in by the burglar. = 门被窃贼踢坏了。

疑问句:
Did he kick the ball? = 他踢球了吗？
Can you kick a ball very far? = 你能把球踢得很远吗？

否定句:
He didn't kick the ball hard enough. = 他踢球不够用力。
She doesn't kick the ball very often. = 她不经常踢球。


PHRASE->
kick off: 开始，启动
kick back: 放松，休息
kick in: 开始生效，起作用
kick around: 讨论，考虑
kick the bucket: 死去
