conjugations:/ˌkɒndʒuˈɡeɪʃənz/ | /ˌkɑːndʒuˈɡeɪʃənz/ | n. | 变化形式；词形变化；动词变位 | The different forms of a verb showing tense, mood, person, and number.  For example, the conjugations of the verb "to be" include "am," "is," "are," "was," "were," etc.  | 例句：Understanding verb conjugations is crucial for mastering a language.  理解动词变位对于掌握一门外语至关重要。


例句：The Spanish conjugations for the verb "hablar" (to speak) are quite complex. 西班牙语动词“hablar”（说）的变位相当复杂。


近义词：inflections, verb forms, declensions (if referring to nouns or adjectives) | 反义词：None (it's a process, not something with an opposite) | 词性：名词


词源:  From Latin *coniugātiō, from coniugāre "to join together, marry," from con- "together" + iugāre "to yoke."  The original sense relates to the joining together of different forms of a verb to create a complete system.
