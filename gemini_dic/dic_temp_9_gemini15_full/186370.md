rhysimeter:/raɪˈsɪmɪtər/| n. |雨量计|A rhysimeter is an instrument used to measure the rate of water infiltration into the soil.  It's typically used in hydrology and soil science to study soil water movement and assess soil properties related to water absorption and drainage.|Example: The researchers used a rhysimeter to determine the infiltration rate of the sandy loam soil.|近义词:infiltrometer|反义词:|None specifically.  The opposite concept would be something measuring water runoff, not infiltration.
