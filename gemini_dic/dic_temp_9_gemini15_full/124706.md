The word "mower" does not exist in standard English.  It's likely a misspelling of "mower".  I will provide the analysis for "mower".

mower:/ˈmoʊər/ | n. | 割草机；收割机 | A machine or person that mows grass or other crops. |  例句：He used a riding mower to cut the large lawn. 他用骑式割草机来割大草坪。 | 近义词：cutter, reaper | 反义词：planter (in the context of planting, not the opposite action of mowing)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He mows the lawn every Saturday. = 他每个星期六都割草。
The farmer mows the wheat field. = 农民收割小麦田。


一般过去时:
She mowed the grass yesterday. = 她昨天割了草。
They mowed the hay last week. = 他们上周割了干草。


现在完成时:
He has mowed the lawn already. = 他已经割过草了。
They have mowed the entire field. = 他们已经割完了整个田地。

We had mowed the lawn before the rain started. = 下雨开始前我们已经割过草了。
The farmer had mowed the majority of the crop before the storm. = 暴风雨来临之前，农民已经收割了大部分庄稼。


将来时:
I will mow the lawn tomorrow. = 我明天会割草。

词源:
The word "mower" comes from the Old English word "mawan," meaning "to mow."  The "-er" suffix is a common agentive suffix in English, indicating a person or thing that performs the action.
