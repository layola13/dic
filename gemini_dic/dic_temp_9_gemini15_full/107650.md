languid:/ˈlæŋɡwɪd/| adj.|倦怠的；懒洋洋的；迟缓的；没精打采的| characterized by lack of energy or enthusiasm; slow and relaxed.  It suggests a pleasant, dreamy state, but can also imply weakness or inactivity.|

例句：

The afternoon sun cast a languid spell over the town.  下午的阳光给小镇笼罩上了一层倦怠的氛围。

She moved with a languid grace. 她以懒洋洋的优雅姿态移动着。


近义词：listless, lethargic, sluggish, indolent, dreamy

反义词：energetic, vigorous, lively, active


词性：形容词

词源:

From Middle English *langwis, from Old French languissant, present participle of languir “to languish,” from Latin langueō “to be faint, weak.”  The word ultimately derives from the Latin root "langueō," meaning "to be faint, weak."  The sense of "slow and relaxed" developed later.
