clamber:/ˈklæmbər/| v.|攀登；攀爬；挣扎着前进 |To climb or move with difficulty or effort, using hands and feet.  |例句：The children clambered over the rocks.孩子们在岩石上攀爬。

例句：She clambered into the crowded bus. 她费力地挤上了拥挤的公共汽车。


近义词：climb, scramble, ascend, struggle |反义词：descend, slide


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They clamber over the fence. = 他们翻过栅栏。
He clambers up the mountain every weekend. = 他每个周末都爬山。

一般过去时:
We clambered up the steep hill. = 我们费力地爬上了陡峭的小山。
She clambered out of the car. = 她从车里爬了出来。

现在完成时:
They have clambered to the top of the mountain. = 他们已经爬到了山顶。
He has clambered over many obstacles in his life. = 他一生中克服了许多障碍。

过去完成时:
They had clambered up the tree before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们已经爬上了树。
She had clambered onto the roof to fix the antenna. = 她已经爬上屋顶去修理天线了。

将来时:
We will clamber up the cliff tomorrow. = 我们明天将攀爬悬崖。
They will clamber aboard the ship. = 他们将爬上船。

虚拟语气:
If I were stronger, I would clamber up that wall. = 如果我更强壮，我会爬上那堵墙。
I wish I could clamber over that mountain without getting tired. = 我希望我能不累地爬过那座山。

被动语态:
The rocks were clambered over by the children. = 孩子们爬过了岩石。（很少用被动语态）
The wall can be clambered over easily. = 这堵墙很容易翻越。（很少用被动语态）

疑问句:
Did they clamber over the wall? = 他们翻过了墙吗？
Will you clamber up the tree? = 你会爬树吗？

否定句:
He didn't clamber up the mountain because of the bad weather. = 由于天气不好，他没有爬山。
They couldn't clamber over the high fence. = 他们无法翻过高高的栅栏。


PHRASE->
clamber up = 爬上
clamber down = 爬下
clamber over = 爬过
clamber onto = 爬到...上面
clamber through = 爬过（例如丛林）

词源:
The word "clamber" ultimately derives from the Old Norse word *klambra, meaning "to cling," or "to climb."  It entered Middle English around the 14th century.
