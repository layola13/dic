hybodus:/ˈhaɪbədəs/| n. | 肋肋鲨属 | Hybodus is an extinct genus of hybodont sharks that lived from the Late Triassic to the Late Cretaceous periods. They were characterized by their heterodont dentition (different types of teeth), with some teeth being sharp and pointed for catching prey, and others being flatter and broader for crushing shells.  Hybodus fossils have been found worldwide. | Example:  Fossil evidence suggests that Hybodus was a successful predator in various marine environments.  = 化石证据表明，肋肋鲨是各种海洋环境中成功的捕食者。|近义词:  (extinct shark genera) |反义词: (none applicable)| noun
