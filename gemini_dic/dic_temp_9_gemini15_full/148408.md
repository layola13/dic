overstating:/ˌoʊvərˈsteɪtɪŋ/| v.|夸大；过分强调|To exaggerate or represent something as greater or more important than it really is.|The advertisement overstated the benefits of the product. 这则广告夸大了该产品的益处。|近义词：exaggerate, inflate, magnify|反义词：understate, downplay


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He overstates his accomplishments. 他夸大了自己的成就。
She overstates the risks involved. 她夸大了所涉及的风险。

一般过去时:
The speaker overstated the impact of the event. 演讲者夸大了事件的影响。
He overstated his case in the meeting. 他在会议上夸大了自己的说法。

现在完成时:
They have overstated their capabilities for years. 他们多年来一直夸大自己的能力。
She has overstated the urgency of the situation. 她夸大了情况的紧迫性。

过去完成时:
He had overstated his qualifications on his resume. 他在简历上夸大了自己的资历。
The report had overstated the financial losses. 报告夸大了财务损失。

将来时:
He will overstate his contributions to the project. 他会夸大他对项目的贡献。
They will likely overstate the positive outcomes. 他们可能会夸大积极的结果。

虚拟语气:
If he overstated his case, he would lose credibility. 如果他夸大了自己的说法，他会失去可信度。
I wish I hadn't overstated my confidence. 我真希望我没有夸大自己的信心。

被动语态:
The importance of the issue was overstated by the media. 媒体夸大了这个问题的重要性。
The benefits of the program have been overstated. 该项目的益处被夸大了。

疑问句:
Did he overstate the facts? 他是否夸大了事实？
Is she overstating the problem? 她是否夸大了问题？

否定句:
He did not overstate his concerns. 他没有夸大他的担忧。
She didn't overstate the difficulties. 她没有夸大困难。
