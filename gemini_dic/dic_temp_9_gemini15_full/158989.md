plutonism:/ˈpluːtəˌnɪzəm/| n. | 侵入说；深成岩浆作用说 | The theory that igneous rocks are formed from magma that has cooled and solidified beneath the Earth's surface.  This contrasts with the older neptunism, which held that all rocks were formed from water. | Example:  Plutonism explains the formation of granite and other intrusive igneous rocks.  侵入说解释了花岗岩和其他侵入性火成岩的形成。|近义词：intrusionism, igneous activity |反义词：neptunism
