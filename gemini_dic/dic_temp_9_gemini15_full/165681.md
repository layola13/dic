premonition:/ˌpriːməˈnɪʃən/| n. |预感，预兆|A premonition is a feeling that something, especially something unpleasant, is going to happen.  It's an intuitive sense of the future, often involving a sense of unease or foreboding. |例句：She had a premonition of disaster. 她有一种灾难将要发生的预感。|例句：A strange premonition kept her awake all night.  一种奇怪的预感让她整夜睡不着。|近义词：foreboding, presentiment, hunch|反义词：certainty, assurance|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has a premonition of danger. = 他有一种危险的预感。
She often experiences premonitions before big events. = 她在大事件发生前经常会有预感。

一般过去时:
I had a premonition that something bad would happen. = 我预感到不好的事情会发生。
They said they had a premonition about the accident. = 他们说他们对事故有一种预感。

现在完成时:
Have you ever had a premonition that came true? = 你曾经有过预感成真的经历吗？
She has had several premonitions this week. = 她本周已经有几次预感了。

过去完成时:
I had had a premonition that he was in trouble. = 我预感到他遇到了麻烦。
Before the earthquake, many people had had premonitions of disaster. = 地震前，许多人都预感到灾难会发生。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish I had heeded my premonition about the investment. = 我真希望我当时注意到了我对投资的预感。

被动语态:
