clappers:/ˈklæpərz/| n.|拨浪鼓；拍板；（尤指）教堂钟声的敲击器|The parts of a bell or other instrument that strike against each other to produce a sound.  Typically used in pairs.  Also refers to someone who claps their hands repeatedly. |例句：The church bells' clappers rang out across the valley. 教堂钟声的敲击器在山谷回响。

The children used clappers to make rhythmic sounds during the performance. 孩子们在表演中用拨浪鼓发出有节奏的声音。|近义词：beaters, strikers,  rattles|反义词：silence
