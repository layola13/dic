self-named:/ˌselfˈneɪmd/| adj.| 自称的；自命的 |  Describes something that has been named by itself or its creator, often implying a lack of external validation or authority.  It suggests a name chosen without official recognition or widespread acceptance. | Example:  The self-named "King of the Hill" ruled his small kingdom with an iron fist.  = 自称“山之王”的他用铁腕统治着他的小王国。|近义词：self-proclaimed, self-styled |反义词：officially named, formally titled
