preconsecrated:/ˌpriːˈkɒnsɪkreɪtɪd/|/ˌpriːˈkɑːnsɪkreɪtɪd/| adj.| 预先奉献的；预先祝圣的 | Previously consecrated; dedicated or set apart for sacred use in advance.  The term typically refers to objects or elements used in religious ceremonies, particularly in the Eucharist. |例句：The preconsecrated hosts were carefully stored in the tabernacle. 预先祝圣的圣体饼被小心地储存在圣体柜中。


The preconsecrated wafers were used in the Mass. 预先祝圣的薄饼被用于弥撒。


近义词：hallowed, sanctified, blessed, dedicated |反义词：unhallowed, profane, secular


词源:

* **pre-**:  prefix meaning "before" or "in advance."
* **consecrate**:  from Latin *consecrare* meaning "to make sacred," from *con-* (together) + *sacrare* (to make sacred).
