crispness:/ˈkrɪspnəs/| n. |脆度；干脆；清晰度 |The quality of being crisp; a state of being firm, brittle, and easily broken.  It can also refer to the clarity and distinctness of something, such as an image or sound. |例句：The crispness of the autumn air was invigorating. 秋天空气中的清爽令人振奋。

例句：The crispness of the photograph was remarkable, even after years of storage. 照片的清晰度非常出色，即使保存了多年也是如此。

近义词：firmness, sharpness, clarity, crispness, distinctness

反义词：softness, mushiness, blurriness, vagueness


词源:  Derived from the adjective "crisp," ultimately from Old French *crispe, from Latin crispus "curled, wavy," of uncertain origin.  The sense of "brittle" developed later.
