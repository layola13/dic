lacuna:/ləˈkuːnə/| n. |空白；缺口；脱漏 |A lacuna is a gap or missing part, especially in a manuscript, text, or sequence.  It can refer to a missing piece of information or a break in continuity. |例句：There are several lacunae in our knowledge of his early life.  我们的早期生活中对他有几处空白。|例句：The historian attempted to fill the lacunae in the historical record. 历史学家试图填补历史记录中的空白。|近义词：gap, void, hiatus, omission |反义词：completeness, fullness


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense, voice, etc., are not applicable.  However, we can show how it's used in different sentence structures.

In a declarative sentence:
The manuscript contained several lacunae.  = 这份手稿包含几个空白。

In an interrogative sentence:
Are there any lacunae in your research? = 你的研究中是否存在任何空白？

In an exclamatory sentence:
What a lacuna!  = 多么大的一个空白！


In a sentence with a relative clause:
The lacunae that remain in the story are significant. = 故事中剩下的空白很重要。


In a sentence using a prepositional phrase:
The lacunae in the argument were glaring.  = 论证中的缺陷是显而易见的。

PHRASE->
fill a lacuna = 填补空白
