daydream:/ˈdeɪdriːm/| n. v.| 白日梦；幻想；做白日梦 | A pleasant thought or fantasy that distracts one's attention from the present;  to indulge in daydreams. |例句：She was lost in a daydream. 她沉浸在白日梦中。

例句：I often daydream about traveling the world. 我经常做环游世界的白日梦。|近义词：fantasy, reverie, vision |反义词：reality, actuality|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He daydreams during class. = 他上课时做白日梦。
She daydreams about her future. = 她幻想她的未来。


一般过去时:
I daydreamed about my childhood. = 我回想起我的童年。
They daydreamed all afternoon. = 他们整个下午都在做白日梦。


现在完成时:
She has daydreamed about becoming a doctor. = 她一直梦想着成为一名医生。
He has daydreamed about this moment for years. = 他多年来一直梦想着这一刻。


过去完成时:
I had daydreamed about this trip for months before I went. = 在我去旅行之前，我已经梦想着这次旅行好几个月了。
They had daydreamed of success before they started their business. = 在他们开始创业之前，他们就已经幻想过成功了。


将来时:
I will daydream about my vacation later. = 我稍后会幻想我的假期。
She will daydream about her wedding day. = 她会幻想她的婚礼。


虚拟语气:
If I had the time, I would daydream more often. = 如果我有时间，我会更经常地做白日梦。
I wish I could daydream without feeling guilty. = 我希望我可以毫无负罪感地做白日梦。


被动语态:
His ideas were often dismissed as mere daydreams. = 他的想法常常被斥为仅仅是白日梦。
The project was initially considered a daydream. = 这个项目最初被认为是一个幻想。


疑问句:
Do you often daydream? = 你经常做白日梦吗？
What do you usually daydream about? = 你通常幻想什么？


否定句:
He doesn't daydream very often. = 他不经常做白日梦。
She didn't daydream during the meeting. = 她开会时没有做白日梦。
