homoplasty:/ˈhoʊməˌplæsti/| n. |同形性，趋同进化|The resemblance of structures that have different evolutionary origins.  Homoplasy results from convergent evolution, where similar environmental pressures lead to the development of similar traits in unrelated organisms.  For example, the wings of birds and bats are homoplastic; they have similar functions and appearances but evolved independently. |The streamlined bodies of sharks and dolphins exemplify homoplasy.  鲨鱼和海豚流线型的身体是同形性的例子。|近义词：convergent evolution, analogy|反义词：homology
