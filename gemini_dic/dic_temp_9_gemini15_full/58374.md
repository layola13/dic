dualistically:/ˌduːəˈlɪstɪkli/| adv.|二元论地，对立地|Adverb describing something done or viewed from a dualistic perspective, emphasizing the division into two opposing forces or principles.|The philosopher analyzed the problem dualistically, considering both good and evil as fundamental forces.|近义词：dichotomously,  binary|反义词：holistically, monistically

USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and as such, it doesn't have tense variations like verbs. Its usage depends on the context of the sentence.  It describes *how* something is done or perceived.

Example 1:
He interpreted the poem dualistically, focusing on the conflict between light and darkness. = 他用二元论的视角解读这首诗，重点关注光明与黑暗之间的冲突。

Example 2:
The artist's work is often viewed dualistically, with critics highlighting the tension between realism and abstraction. = 艺术家的作品常常被二元地看待，评论家们强调了现实主义和抽象主义之间的张力。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "dualistically".  Its usage is primarily within philosophical or analytical contexts.  However, phrases that relate to the concept of duality can be used:

Phrase 1:  black and white thinking = 非黑即白的思维方式
Phrase 2:  either/or proposition = 非此即彼的主张
Phrase 3:  in terms of duality = 从二元论的角度
Phrase 4:  a dual approach = 双重方法
Phrase 5:  seeing things in two ways = 用两种方式看待事物
