europeanization:/ˌjʊərəpiənaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |欧洲化；使欧洲化 | The process of adopting European customs, practices, or characteristics.  This can refer to the spread of European culture, languages, institutions, or political systems to other parts of the world, or the adoption of such elements within a specific region or country.  It often involves a complex interplay of cultural exchange, political influence, and economic integration. | Example: The eastern expansion of the European Union has led to significant europeanization in formerly communist countries.  东欧国家的欧洲化进程在欧盟东扩后显著加快。  The europeanization of colonial societies was a gradual and uneven process. 殖民地社会的欧洲化是一个渐进且不均衡的过程。 | 近义词：westernization, modernization | 反义词：de-europeanization, localization
