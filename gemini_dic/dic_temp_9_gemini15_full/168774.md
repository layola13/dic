prodigy:/ˈprɒdɪdʒi/ /ˈprɑːdɪdʒi/| n. |天才；奇才；奇迹|A person, especially a young one, with exceptional qualities or abilities.  A wonderful or marvelous thing. |例句：Mozart was a musical prodigy. 莫扎特是一位音乐神童。

The technological advancements of the 21st century are nothing short of a prodigy. 21世纪的技术进步简直就是一个奇迹。

|近义词：genius, wonder, marvel |反义词：dunce, mediocrity |名词


USAGE->

He is a child prodigy. = 他是一个神童。
She shows the signs of a prodigy. = 她展现出天才的迹象。


一般过去时:
He was a mathematical prodigy in his youth. = 他年轻时是个数学天才。
