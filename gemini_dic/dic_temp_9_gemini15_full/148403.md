overstate:/ˌoʊvərˈsteɪt/ | vt. |夸大；夸张 | To represent (something) as being larger, better, or worse than it really is. |例句：He overstated the risks involved in the project. 他夸大了项目中涉及的风险。|例句：Don't overstate your qualifications in your resume.  不要在简历中夸大你的资历。|近义词：exaggerate, magnify, amplify |反义词：understate, downplay


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He overstates his accomplishments. = 他夸大了自己的成就。
She overstates the importance of this issue. = 她夸大了这个问题的重要性。


一般过去时:
The media overstated the impact of the storm. = 媒体夸大了风暴的影响。
He overstated his case in the court. = 他在法庭上夸大了自己的说法。


现在完成时:
They have overstated their financial position for years. = 他们多年来夸大了自己的财务状况。
She has overstated the difficulty of the task. = 她夸大了这项任务的难度。


过去完成时:
He had overstated his abilities in the interview. = 他在面试中夸大了自己的能力。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he were to overstate his qualifications, he would not get the job. = 如果他夸大自己的资历，他就不会得到这份工作。
I wish I hadn't overstated my confidence. = 我真希望我没有夸大自己的信心。


被动语态:

His contributions to the project were overstated in the final report. = 他对项目的贡献在最终报告中被夸大了。


疑问句:

否定句:
