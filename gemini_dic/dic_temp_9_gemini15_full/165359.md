prejudging: /ˌpriːˈdʒʌdʒɪŋ/ | v. | 预先判断，先入为主 | To judge or form an opinion of (someone or something) before having sufficient knowledge.  | 例句：Prejudging a book by its cover is unwise. =  以貌取人是不明智的。


例句：He was prejudging the outcome of the election before the votes were even counted. =  在选票甚至还没有统计之前，他就预先判断了选举的结果。


近义词：preconceived notion, prejudgment, bias, preconception, stereotyping


反义词：objective judgment, open-mindedness, impartiality


词性：动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They prejudge people based on their appearance. = 他们根据外貌对人先入为主。
We prejudge the quality of a product by its packaging. = 我们根据产品的包装来预先判断其质量。

一般过去时:
She prejudged the situation and made a hasty decision. = 她对形势先入为主，做出了仓促的决定。
The judge prejudged the defendant's guilt. = 法官对被告的罪行先入为主。

现在完成时:
They have prejudged the contest before it even started. = 比赛还没开始，他们就先入为主地做了判断。
I have prejudged many movies based on trailers. = 我根据预告片对许多电影都先入为主地做了判断。

过去完成时:
He had prejudged her character based on a single incident. = 他根据一件事情就对她的品格先入为主地做了判断。
The jury had prejudged the case due to media coverage. = 由于媒体的报道，陪审团对案件先入为主。

将来时:
She will prejudge the winner based on past performance. = 她将根据过去的表现来预先判断获胜者。
They will prejudge the results of the experiment before they have all the data. = 在获得所有数据之前，他们将预先判断实验的结果。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't prejudged him, I might have been a better friend. = 如果我没有对他先入为主，我可能会成为一个更好的朋友。
I wish I hadn't prejudged the situation. = 我真希望我没有对形势先入为主。

被动语态:
The candidate was prejudged by the media. = 候选人受到了媒体的先入为主的评价。
The new policy was prejudged as ineffective. = 新政策被预先判断为无效。

疑问句:
Are you prejudging the situation? = 你对形势先入为主了吗？

否定句:
Don't prejudge the situation until you have all the facts. = 在掌握所有事实之前，不要对形势先入为主。
We did not prejudge the applicant's qualifications. = 我们没有预先判断申请人的资格。
