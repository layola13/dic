dictated:/ˈdɪkteɪtɪd/| v. | 听写；口述；命令；支配 | to speak or read aloud (words to be written down by another person)  | The teacher dictated the spelling test to the class. 老师给全班同学听写了拼写测验。  |  |  |verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dictates his letters to his secretary. 他把信口述给他的秘书。
She dictates the terms of the agreement. 她口述协议条款。


一般过去时:
The lawyer dictated the will to his client. 律师向他的委托人宣读了遗嘱。
The general dictated the terms of surrender. 将军口述了投降的条款。


现在完成时:
He has dictated several chapters of his new book. 他已经口述了他新书的几章。
She has dictated all the necessary information. 她已经口述了所有必要的信息。


过去完成时:
The witness had dictated his statement before the trial. 证人在审判前已经口述了他的证词。
They had dictated the conditions for the peace treaty before the signing ceremony. 他们在签字仪式之前已经口述了和平条约的条件。


将来时:
The professor will dictate the lecture notes to his students. 教授将把讲义口述给他的学生。
She will dictate her wishes to her lawyer. 她将把她的愿望口述给她的律师。


虚拟语气:
If he dictated the terms, we would agree. 如果他口述条款，我们会同意。
If she had dictated the rules clearly, there would have been less confusion. 如果她清楚地口述了规则，就不会有那么多的混乱。


被动语态:
The speech was dictated to the stenographer. 演讲被口述给速记员。
The terms of the contract were dictated by the stronger party. 合同条款是由实力较强的一方决定的。


疑问句:
Was the letter dictated or written? 这封信是口述的还是写的？
Will the instructions be dictated or printed? 指令是口述的还是打印的？


否定句:
He did not dictate the letter himself. 他没有自己口述这封信。
The judge did not dictate the sentence. 法官没有宣判。


PHRASE->

dictate one's will | 决定某人的意愿
