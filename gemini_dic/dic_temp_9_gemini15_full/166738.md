presider:/prɪˈzaɪdər/| n. |主席；主持者；主导者 |A presider is a person who presides over a meeting, ceremony, or other event. They typically have authority and responsibility for managing the proceedings.  |The judge presided over the trial.  法官主持了审判。| chairperson, moderator, chairman, superintendent | subordinate, participant | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The judge presides over the court. = 法官主持法庭。
She presides over the meeting with grace. = 她优雅地主持会议。

一般过去时:
He presided over the committee for many years. = 他多年来一直担任委员会主席。
The mayor presided at the ceremony. = 市长主持了仪式。


现在完成时:
She has presided over several important conferences. = 她主持过几次重要的会议。
He has presided at many meetings. = 他主持过许多会议。


过去完成时:
Before the new CEO arrived, the CFO had presided over the company. = 在新任首席执行官到来之前，首席财务官一直负责公司。
They had presided over the organization for decades before retiring. = 他们在退休前几十年一直领导着这个组织。


将来时:
The new chairman will preside over the next board meeting. = 新任主席将主持下一次董事会会议。
She will preside at the graduation ceremony. = 她将主持毕业典礼。


虚拟语气:
If he were to preside, the meeting would be more efficient. = 如果他来主持，会议会更高效。
I wish a more experienced person would preside over this project. = 我希望一个更有经验的人来主持这个项目。


被动语态:
The meeting was presided over by the CEO. = 会议由首席执行官主持。
The ceremony was presided over by a respected elder. = 典礼由一位受人尊敬的长者主持。


疑问句:
Who will preside at the wedding? = 谁将主持婚礼？
Who presided over the last meeting? = 谁主持了上次会议？


否定句:
He did not preside over the meeting; someone else did. = 他没有主持会议；别人主持的。
She will not preside at the event. = 她不会主持这个活动。


PHRASE->

presides over: 主持；负责

presiding judge: 主审法官
presidential preside: 总统主持
