candelabrum: /ˌkændəˈleɪbrəm/ | n. |烛台；枝状烛台 | A candelabrum is a branched candlestick, often ornately decorated, designed to hold several candles. It is typically used as a decorative element, often in formal settings.  | The dining room was illuminated by a magnificent silver candelabrum.  餐厅里由一个华丽的银烛台照明。  | candlestick, branched candlestick |  (无明确反义词，可考虑 less ornate candlestick) | 名词

词源:
From Late Latin *candelabrum, from Latin candela "candle" + -brum (suffix used to form nouns).  The word ultimately traces its roots back to the Proto-Italic *kandelā, from the Proto-Indo-European root *kend-, meaning "to shine" or "to burn".
