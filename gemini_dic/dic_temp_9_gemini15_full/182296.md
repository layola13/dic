remuneration:/ˌrɛmjʊnəˈreɪʃən/| n. |报酬，酬金，薪水 |Payment for work or services; compensation.  It encompasses salary, wages, bonuses, and other forms of payment. |The company offered a generous remuneration package to attract top talent. 这家公司提供丰厚的薪酬待遇以吸引顶尖人才。  |compensation, payment, salary, wages|penalty, punishment|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His remuneration is quite high. = 他的报酬相当高。
What is your expected remuneration for this job? = 你对这份工作的预期报酬是多少？


一般过去时:
Her remuneration for the project was substantial. = 她完成该项目的报酬相当可观。
The workers received their remuneration on Friday. = 工人们在星期五领到了报酬。


现在完成时:
They have received their remuneration for the past month. = 他们已经收到了上个月的报酬。
She has not yet received her full remuneration. = 她尚未收到全部报酬。


过去完成时:
He had already received his remuneration before leaving the company. = 他离开公司之前已经收到了他的报酬。
The employees had not received their remuneration on time. = 员工们没有按时收到报酬。


将来时:
She will receive her remuneration at the end of the month. = 她将在月底收到报酬。
They will be paid their remuneration according to their performance. = 他们将根据业绩获得报酬。


虚拟语气:
If I were offered a higher remuneration, I would accept the job. = 如果给我更高的报酬，我会接受这份工作。
I wish my remuneration was higher. = 我希望我的报酬更高。


被动语态:
Remuneration is paid on a monthly basis. = 报酬按月支付。
The employees' remuneration was calculated based on their work hours. = 员工的报酬是根据他们的工作时间计算的。


疑问句:
What is the remuneration for this position? = 这个职位的报酬是多少？
How much remuneration will I get for this task? = 完成这项任务我将获得多少报酬？


否定句:
The company did not offer sufficient remuneration. = 公司没有提供足够的报酬。
He didn't receive his remuneration on time. = 他没有按时收到报酬。
