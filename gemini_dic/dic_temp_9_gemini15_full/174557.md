quip:/kwɪp/ | n. v. |俏皮话，妙语；说俏皮话 | A witty remark.  |例句：He made a quick quip about the politician's speech. 他对这位政治家的演讲说了句俏皮话。
例句：She quipped that the meeting was as exciting as watching paint dry. 她俏皮地说，这次会议就像看油漆干一样令人兴奋。|近义词：joke, witticism, jest |反义词：serious remark, dull comment |词性：名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often quips about his boss. = 他经常拿他的老板开玩笑。
She quips during her stand-up routine. = 她在单口喜剧表演中妙语连珠。


一般过去时:
He quipped that the food was terrible. = 他俏皮地说这食物糟透了。
She quipped a witty remark about the situation. = 她对这种情况说了句机智的俏皮话。


现在完成时:
He has quipped many times about the same topic. = 他已经多次就同一话题说过俏皮话了。
She has quipped several times about his new haircut. = 她已经几次拿他的新发型开玩笑。


过去完成时:

She had quipped several times about his behavior before he noticed. = 他注意到之前，她已经几次拿他的行为开玩笑了。


将来时:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "quip" ultimately derives from the Old French word "quipper," meaning "to cut, to snip," or "to exchange sharp words."  This suggests a connection to the quick, sharp, and often cutting nature of a witty remark.
