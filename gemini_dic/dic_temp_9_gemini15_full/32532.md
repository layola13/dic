chip's:/tʃɪps/| n. |薯条；碎片；芯片|The word "chip's" is a possessive form of "chip," indicating ownership or belonging.  It can refer to multiple meanings depending on the context.  It most commonly refers to "potato chips," but can also describe small pieces of something broken off, or integrated circuits in electronics. |Example:  The chip's flavor was delicious. (薯条的味道很好吃。)  The computer chip's performance was impressive. (电脑芯片的性能令人印象深刻。) |近义词:fries(薯条), fragments(碎片), microchips(微芯片)|反义词:whole(完整的), intact(完整的)|名词
