endict: /ɪnˈdaɪkt/ | vt. | 控告；起诉 | To formally accuse someone of a crime. | The grand jury indicted him on charges of murder. 大陪审团以谋杀罪起诉他。| accuse, charge | acquit, exonerate | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The prosecutor indicts the defendant. = 检察官起诉被告。
They indict him for fraud. = 他们以欺诈罪起诉他。

一般过去时:
The grand jury indicted him last week. = 大陪审团上周起诉了他。
She was indicted for tax evasion. = 她因逃税被起诉。

现在完成时:
He has been indicted on multiple counts of theft. = 他已被指控多项盗窃罪。
The company has been indicted for environmental violations. = 该公司因违反环境法规而被起诉。

过去完成时:
The case had been indicted before the evidence was presented. = 在证据提交之前，案件已被起诉。
They had already been indicted when the new evidence emerged. = 当新的证据出现时，他们已经被起诉了。

将来时:
The court will indict him next month. = 法院下个月将起诉他。
They will likely be indicted for conspiracy. = 他们很可能会因共谋罪被起诉。

虚拟语气:
If they had more evidence, they would indict him. = 如果他们有更多证据，他们就会起诉他。
I wish the judge would indict the corrupt officials. = 我希望法官能起诉那些腐败官员。

被动语态:
He was indicted on charges of bribery. = 他被指控犯有贿赂罪。
The suspect was indicted by the grand jury. = 嫌疑人被大陪审团起诉。

疑问句:
Will he be indicted? = 他会被起诉吗？
Has she been indicted yet? = 她已经被起诉了吗？

否定句:
The judge did not indict the defendant. = 法官没有起诉被告。
They were not indicted due to lack of evidence. = 由于缺乏证据，他们没有被起诉。


PHRASE->
indict for (a crime) = 因（某种罪行）而起诉
indict on (charges) = 就（指控）起诉
true bill of indictment = 正式起诉书
indictment of = 对…的控告
bring an indictment against = 对…提起诉讼
