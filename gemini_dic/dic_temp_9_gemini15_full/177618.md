rebuked:/rɪˈbjuːkt/| vt.|斥责；谴责；训斥|To express sharp disapproval of; reprimand.|The teacher rebuked the student for talking in class.老师因学生在课堂上讲话而斥责了他。|近义词：reprimand, reproach, scold, upbraid|反义词：praise, commend


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rebukes his children for bad behavior. 他因孩子的不良行为而斥责他们。
The manager rebukes employees who are late. 经理斥责迟到的员工。


一般过去时:
She rebuked him for his rudeness. 她因他的粗鲁而斥责了他。
The principal rebuked the student for cheating. 校长因作弊而斥责了学生。


现在完成时:
He has rebuked me several times this week.  本周他已经几次斥责我了。
The boss has rebuked her for her mistakes. 老板因她的错误而斥责了她。


过去完成时:
She had rebuked him before he even apologized.  在他道歉之前，她就已斥责了他。
The teacher had rebuked the class for their disruptive behavior before the lesson began.  上课之前，老师已经因班级的扰乱行为而斥责了他们。


将来时:
He will rebuke her if she does it again.如果她再这样做，他会斥责她。
The judge will rebuke the lawyer for his inappropriate comments. 法官会因律师的不当言论而斥责他。


虚拟语气:
I wish he hadn't rebuked me so harshly.  我希望他不要那么严厉地斥责我。
If I were you, I wouldn't rebuke him in front of everyone. 如果我是你，我不会当着所有人的面斥责他。


被动语态:
He was rebuked for his poor performance. 他因表现不佳而受到斥责。
The proposal was rebuked by the committee. 该提案遭到委员会的斥责。


疑问句:
Was he rebuked for his actions? 他因自己的行为而受到斥责了吗？
Has she ever been rebuked by her superiors? 她有没有被上司斥责过？


否定句:
He did not rebuke her for her mistake. 他没有因她的错误而斥责她。
The manager didn't rebuke the employee, despite his lateness. 尽管员工迟到了，经理也没有斥责他。


PHRASE->
rebuke sharply  严厉斥责
rebuke publicly 公开谴责
receive a rebuke 受到斥责
a gentle rebuke 轻微的责备
a severe rebuke 严厉的斥责
