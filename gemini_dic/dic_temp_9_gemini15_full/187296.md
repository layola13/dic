rock-and-roll: /ˌrɒk ænd ˈroʊl/ | n. |摇滚乐|A genre of popular music originating in the United States in the late 1940s and early 1950s, characterized by a strong, usually amplified beat, and often featuring electric guitars, bass, and drums. It's a fusion of various musical styles like blues, rhythm and blues, country, and gospel.  Rock and roll revolutionized popular music and culture, influencing countless artists and generations. |例句：The concert featured legendary rock-and-roll bands from the 60s and 70s.  这场音乐会以60年代和70年代的传奇摇滚乐队为特色。


例句：Many consider Elvis Presley the King of Rock and Roll.  许多人认为猫王是摇滚乐之王。


近义词：rock music, pop music | 反义词：classical music, folk music
