presuffered:/prɪˈsʌfərd/| adj.| 预先遭受的；预先经受的 |  This word describes something that has been suffered or experienced before a particular point in time.  It implies a prior experience of hardship or difficulty.  The word is relatively rare and often found in more formal or literary contexts. | Example:  The presuffered hardships of the journey made the final destination seem even more rewarding.  = 旅途中的预先经历的艰难困苦，使最终的目的地显得更加令人欣慰。| 近义词:pre-experienced, beforehand experienced | 反义词: subsequently experienced, post-experienced |  形容词
