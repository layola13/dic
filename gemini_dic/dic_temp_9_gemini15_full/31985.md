chemosmotic: /ˌkɛmoʊˈsɒtɪk/ | adj. | 化学渗透的 | Relating to or denoting the generation of ATP (adenosine triphosphate) by the movement of protons across a membrane during cellular respiration or photosynthesis.  It describes the coupling of chemical reactions with osmotic pressure gradients across a membrane. | The chemosmotic theory explains how ATP is synthesized in mitochondria and chloroplasts. | 近义词：proton motive force-related | 反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with other ATP generation mechanisms.
