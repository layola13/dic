messengers:/ˈmɛsɪndʒərz/ | n. |信使；使者；传递信息者 | People or things that carry messages. Messengers can be individuals, animals, or even inanimate objects used to convey information from one place to another.  |例句：The messengers delivered the urgent message to the king. 信使们将紧急信息送达国王。

例句：Birds can sometimes act as messengers of nature. 鸟类有时可以充当自然的信使。


近义词：emissaries, couriers, bearers | 反义词：recipients, receivers | 词性：名词

词源:
The word "messenger" comes from the Old French word "messager," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "missarius," meaning "one who is sent."  The suffix "-er" indicates an agent or doer of an action.  Thus, a messenger is someone who is sent to carry a message.
