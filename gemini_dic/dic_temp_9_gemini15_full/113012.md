lumbers:/ˈlʌmbərz/ | v. |笨重地移动；蹒跚；发出隆隆声 |To move in a slow, heavy, awkward way.  |例句：The old man lumbered across the room.  老人笨拙地穿过房间。|例句：The truck lumbered down the hill. 卡车隆隆地下了山。|近义词：trudge, plod, stumble,  creak |反义词：glide, skim, dart


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He lumbers around the house. = 他在房子周围笨拙地走动。
The old engine lumbers continuously. = 这台旧发动机持续地发出隆隆声。

一般过去时:
The bear lumbered through the forest. = 熊笨拙地穿过森林。
The train lumbered to a stop. = 火车隆隆地停了下来。

现在完成时:
The elephant has lumbered across the field. = 大象已经笨拙地穿过田野了。
The old car has lumbered along for years. = 这辆旧车已经用了好多年了，并且发出隆隆声。

过去完成时:
The horse had lumbered into the stable before the storm broke. = 马在暴风雨来临之前已经笨拙地走进了马厩。
The ship had lumbered across the ocean for days. = 船在海上已经隆隆地航行了好几天了。


将来时:
The walker will lumber slowly along the trail. = 行人将沿着小路缓慢地笨拙地走动。
The engine will lumber to life. = 发动机会隆隆地启动。

虚拟语气:
If he were younger, he wouldn't lumber so much. = 如果他年轻一些，他就不会这么笨拙地移动了。
If the truck were lighter, it wouldn't lumber down the hill so slowly. = 如果卡车轻一些，它就不会这么慢地隆隆地下山了。

被动语态:
The heavy boxes were lumbered into the truck. = 沉重的箱子被笨拙地搬进了卡车。 (This usage is less common)
The logs were lumbered down the river.  原木被顺着河流笨拙地运送。 (This usage is more about the process of moving logs)

疑问句:
Does he always lumber like that? = 他总是这么笨拙地走动吗？
Does the engine always lumber so loudly? = 这台发动机总是发出这么大的隆隆声吗？

否定句:
He doesn't lumber around as much anymore. = 他现在不怎么笨拙地走动了。
The engine didn't lumber as much after the repairs. = 修理后，发动机没有那么大的隆隆声了。

lumber to a halt = 缓慢而笨拙地停下
