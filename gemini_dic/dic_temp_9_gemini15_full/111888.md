logon:/ˈlɒɡɒn/| /ˈlɑːɡɑːn/| v.|登录，签入|To gain access to a computer system or network by entering a username and password.|例句：You need to log on to access your email. 你需要登录才能访问你的邮箱。|例句：I logged on to the server and checked the files. 我登录到服务器并检查了文件。|近义词：sign in, access, connect|反义词：log off, sign out, disconnect|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
I log on to my computer every morning. = 我每天早上都登录我的电脑。
He logs on to the network to check his emails. = 他登录网络查看邮件。

一般过去时:
I logged on to the system yesterday. = 我昨天登录了系统。
She logged on to her account and updated her profile. = 她登录了她的账户并更新了她的个人资料。

现在完成时:
I have logged on to the system three times today. = 我今天已经登录系统三次了。
He has logged on to the network since this morning. = 他从今天早上开始就登录了网络。

过去完成时:
I had logged on before the meeting started. = 会议开始前我已经登录了。
She had logged on to her account before the deadline. = 截止日期前她已经登录了她的账户。

将来时:
I will log on to the server later tonight. = 我今晚晚些时候会登录服务器。
He will log on to the system when he gets to the office. = 他到办公室后会登录系统。

虚拟语气:
If I had logged on earlier, I would have seen the message. = 如果我早点登录，我就看到了信息。
I wish I had logged on to check my email yesterday. = 我真希望昨天登录查看邮件。

被动语态:
The system can be accessed by logging on. = 通过登录可以访问系统。
The server was logged on to remotely. = 服务器被远程登录了。

疑问句:
Have you logged on yet? = 你登录了吗？
Did he log on to the system? = 他登录系统了吗？

否定句:
I couldn't log on because I forgot my password. = 我因为忘记密码而无法登录。
He didn't log on to the network today. = 他今天没有登录网络。


PHRASE->
log on to: 登录到...
log on to a website: 登录一个网站
log on to a network: 登录到一个网络
log on to a server: 登录到一个服务器
log on to an account: 登录一个帐户
