crowned:/kraʊnd/| vt.|加冕，戴冠于；使…加冕；成就，使完美|To place a crown on someone's head, especially as a ceremony of making them a king or queen; to achieve success or recognition.|例句：The king was crowned in a magnificent ceremony. 国王在一个盛大的仪式上加冕。

例句：Her efforts were crowned with success. 她的努力最终获得成功。|近义词：coronate, invest|反义词：depose, dethrone|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He crowns the cake with cherries. = 他用樱桃装饰蛋糕。
They crown the champion with a laurel wreath. = 他们用桂冠为冠军加冕。


一般过去时:
The queen was crowned in Westminster Abbey. = 女王在威斯敏斯特教堂加冕。
He crowned his career with a final, triumphant performance. = 他以最后一次辉煌的演出结束了自己的职业生涯。


现在完成时:
She has crowned her years of hard work with a prestigious award. = 她多年的辛勤工作最终获得了享有盛誉的奖项。
They have crowned their efforts with success. = 他们的努力最终取得了成功。


过去完成时:
They had crowned him king before the rebellion started. = 反叛开始之前，他们已经将他加冕为国王。
She had crowned her culinary masterpiece with a delicate sauce. = 她用精致的酱汁完成了她的烹饪杰作。


将来时:
They will crown the new Miss Universe next month. = 下个月他们将为新的环球小姐加冕。
He will crown his efforts with a book launch next year. = 他将在明年通过书籍发布来完成他的努力。


虚拟语气:
If he were crowned king, he would change the laws. = 如果他加冕为王，他会改变法律。
I wish I could have crowned my efforts with victory. = 我希望我能以胜利来完成我的努力。


被动语态:
The new king was crowned last week. = 新国王上周加冕。
The cake will be crowned with fresh berries. = 蛋糕将用新鲜浆果装饰。


疑问句:
Will he be crowned tomorrow? = 他明天会加冕吗？
Was she crowned queen in 1990? = 她是在1990年加冕为女王的吗？


否定句:
They did not crown him king. = 他们没有将他加冕为王。
His efforts were not crowned with success this time. = 他的努力这次没有成功。
