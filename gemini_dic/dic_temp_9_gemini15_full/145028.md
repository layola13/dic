overaffirming: /ˌoʊvərəˈfɜːrmɪŋ/ | v. | 过度肯定；过度确认 |  The act of excessively or inappropriately affirming something, often to the point of being untrue or misleading.  It implies a strong and possibly unwarranted assertion of something's truth or validity.  |


例句：

He was overaffirming his abilities, leading the employer to have unrealistic expectations. = 他过度夸耀自己的能力，导致雇主对他抱有不切实际的期望。

Her constant overaffirming of her child's every action created an unhealthy sense of entitlement. = 她不断地过度肯定孩子的一举一动，造成了孩子不健康的优越感。


近义词：overstating, exaggerating, hyperbolizing, overselling


反义词：understating, downplaying, minimizing


词源:

The word is a combination of "over-" (meaning excessively or to a greater degree than is necessary) and "affirming" (meaning to state positively; to confirm).  The root of "affirm" traces back to the Latin "affirmare,"  composed of "ad-" (to) and "firmare" (to strengthen, make firm).
