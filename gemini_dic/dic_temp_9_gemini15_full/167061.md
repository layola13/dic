presumption:/prɪˈzʌmpʃən/| n. |推测；假定；傲慢；放肆|The act of presuming something; an assumption; an instance of being presumptuous.|例句：His presumption that I would help him was irritating. 他认为我会帮助他，这让我很恼火。

例句：The presumption of innocence is a fundamental principle of law.  无罪推定是法律的基本原则。


近义词：assumption, supposition, hypothesis|反义词：proof, certainty


词源：From Old French *presomption, from Latin præsūmptiōn-, præsūmptiō "a taking beforehand," from præsūmere "to take beforehand," from prae- "before" + sūmere "to take."


用法说明:

名词用法：

1. 推测，假定：指基于不充分证据或理由的假设。

例句：There's a presumption that he's guilty until proven innocent.  在证明无罪之前，人们假定他是罪犯。

例句：The presumption is that the meeting will be held on Friday.  人们假定会议将在星期五举行。


2. 傲慢，放肆：指不顾事实或礼仪而做出某些举动。

例句：Her presumption in asking for a raise after only a month was shocking. 她仅仅工作一个月就要求加薪，真是令人震惊。

例句：It was sheer presumption for him to assume he could win the election. 他认为自己能赢得选举，这纯粹是自以为是。


3. (法律)推定：指法律上的一种推定，根据一定的证据推断出某一事实。

例句：The presumption of innocence is a cornerstone of the justice system. 无罪推定是司法制度的基石。

例句：There is a legal presumption against self-incrimination.  法律推定反对自我指控。


PHRASE->
on the presumption that = 假定…
under the presumption that = 以…为前提
raise a presumption = 产生推定
rebut the presumption = 反驳推定
legal presumption = 法律推定
