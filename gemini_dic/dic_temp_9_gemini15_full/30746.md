/tʃeˈzɑːroʊ/| n. |切萨罗|Cesàro summation (or Cesàro mean) is a method of assigning a value to an infinite series.  It is a method of summation that can sometimes assign values to series that are divergent in the usual sense.  It is named after Ernesto Cesàro.|Example: The Cesàro sum of the Grandi's series (1 − 1 + 1 − 1 + · · ·) is 1/2.|近义词：Abel summation|反义词：无
