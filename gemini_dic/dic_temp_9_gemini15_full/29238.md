cassavas: /kəˈsɑːvəz/ | n. | 木薯 | The plural form of cassava, a starchy tuberous root native to South America, widely cultivated as a staple food in tropical regions. It is a major source of carbohydrates and is used to make various food products, including flour, starch, and tapioca.  | Example: Cassavas are a crucial part of the diet in many developing countries.  木薯是许多发展中国家饮食的重要组成部分。 |近义词: yuca (in some regions) | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, as it refers to a specific plant)
