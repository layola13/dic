contingentness:/kənˈtɪndʒəntnəs/ | n. |偶然性；依赖性；可能性 | The state or fact of being contingent; dependence on chance or on other factors.  It refers to something that is dependent on or subject to other things;  a lack of certainty or inevitability. | Example: The success of the project is contingent on securing funding.  项目的成功取决于获得资金。 The contingentness of the weather meant the picnic was cancelled. 天气的偶然性导致野餐被取消。 |近义词：dependency, dependence, chance, uncertainty, probability | 反义词：certainty, inevitability, necessity


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The success of the plan is contingent on several factors. = 计划的成功取决于几个因素。
Their happiness is contingent upon good health. = 他们的幸福取决于良好的健康。


一般过去时:
The outcome of the election was contingent on the final vote count. =  选举的结果取决于最终的投票计数。
His promotion was contingent upon his completing the project. = 他的晋升取决于他完成项目。


现在完成时:
The decision has been contingent upon further investigation. =  该决定一直取决于进一步调查。
Everything has been contingent on the weather forecast. = 一切都取决于天气预报。


过去完成时:
The meeting had been contingent upon the arrival of the CEO. = 会议取决于CEO的到来。
The trip had been contingent upon securing visas. = 旅行取决于获得签证。


将来时:
The trip will be contingent on the weather conditions. = 旅行将取决于天气状况。
Our plans will be contingent upon the availability of funding. = 我们的计划将取决于资金的可获得性。


虚拟语气:
If the success were contingent upon his efforts alone, it would be a difficult task. = 如果成功仅仅取决于他一个人的努力，那将是一项艰巨的任务。
I wish the outcome weren't so contingent on external factors. = 我希望结果不那么依赖于外部因素。


被动语态:
The decision was contingent upon factors outside our control. =  该决定取决于我们无法控制的因素。
The whole project is contingent upon the government's approval. =  整个项目取决于政府的批准。


疑问句:
Is the success of this project contingent upon market demand? = 这个项目的成功是否取决于市场需求？
Is the plan contingent upon their participation? = 计划是否取决于他们的参与？


否定句:
The outcome is not contingent on luck alone. = 结果不仅仅取决于运气。
The decision was not contingent upon his opinion. = 该决定并不取决于他的意见。
