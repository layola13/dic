marauders:/məˈrɔːdərz/| n.| 掠夺者，抢劫者 |People who raid and plunder, typically in a group.  They often engage in violent acts and are characterized by their destructive and lawless behavior. |例句：The marauders pillaged the village, leaving behind only destruction. 掠夺者洗劫了村庄，只留下破坏。
The marauding army terrorized the countryside. 肆虐的军队使农村地区陷入恐怖之中。|近义词：raiders, looters, pillagers, plunderers |反义词：benefactors, protectors, saviors


词源:

The word "marauder" comes from the Old French word "marauder," which itself is derived from the Old French verb "marauder," meaning "to wander about."  This sense of wandering evolved to imply wandering with malicious intent, leading to the modern meaning of raiding and plundering.


用法说明:

marauders is a noun and is always plural in form.  It refers to a group of people engaged in raiding and plundering.  The action of marauding is inherently violent and destructive.  The term often carries a connotation of savagery and lawlessness.

There is no verb form of "marauders".  To describe the action of marauding, one would use the verb "to maraud" or related verbs like "to raid," "to plunder," or "to pillage."
