flop's: /flɑːps/ | n. | 失败；扑通声 | a complete failure; a heavy fall or collapse | The play was a flop. 这部戏彻底失败了。  His attempt to climb the mountain ended in a flop. 他试图攀登那座山，结果以失败告终。 |近义词：failure, collapse, fiasco |反义词：success, triumph |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His new business is a flop. = 他的新生意失败了。
The cake flops onto the floor. = 蛋糕扑通一声掉在地上。


一般过去时:
The party was a flop. = 派对很失败。
He flopped onto the bed, exhausted. = 他筋疲力尽地扑倒在床上。


现在完成时:
His latest novel has been a flop. = 他最新的那部小说已经失败了。
The company has flopped in the market. = 这家公司在市场上失败了。


过去完成时:
The project had been a flop before they even finished it. = 他们还没完成项目就已经失败了。
He had flopped down on the sofa before realizing it was dirty. = 他在意识到沙发脏之前就已经扑倒在沙发上了。


将来时:
I think his new movie will be a flop. = 我认为他的新电影将会失败。
He will flop down on the couch as soon as he gets home. = 他一到家就会扑倒在沙发上。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't rushed the project, it wouldn't have been such a flop. = 如果他不仓促完成项目，就不会这么失败了。
If I could, I would flop onto that comfy sofa. = 如果可以的话，我会扑倒在那张舒适的沙发上。


被动语态:
The plan was flopped by the unexpected crisis. = 计划因突发危机而失败了。
The fish was flopped onto the ground. = 鱼被摔在了地上。


疑问句:
Was the concert a flop? = 音乐会失败了吗？

否定句:
The movie wasn't a flop; it was a huge success. = 这部电影并没有失败，而是获得了巨大的成功。
He didn't flop down on the floor; he sat gently on the chair. = 他没有扑倒在地上，而是轻轻地坐在椅子上。
