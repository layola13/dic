ostensibility: /ˌɒstɛnˈsɪbɪləti/ | n. |表面性；虚饰；假象 | The outward appearance or semblance of something; the state or quality of being ostensible.  It refers to something that is professed or appears to be true, but may not actually be true. |例句：The ostensibility of his generosity was quickly revealed when he refused to donate to charity.  他的慷慨表面现象很快就被揭露了，因为他拒绝向慈善机构捐款。

例句：Despite the ostensibility of peace talks, tensions remained high between the two countries. 尽管和平谈判表面上看起来很顺利，但两国之间的紧张关系依然很高。


近义词：semblance, appearance, pretense, show, facade

反义词：reality, truth, genuineness, authenticity


词源:  From ostensible + -ity.  "Ostensible" ultimately derives from the Latin word *ostendere*, meaning "to show" or "to display."
