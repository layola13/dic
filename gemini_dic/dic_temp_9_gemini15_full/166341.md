prerelationship:/ˌpriːrɪˈleɪʃənʃɪp/| n. |恋爱前关系，关系萌芽期|The period of time before a romantic relationship officially begins, often characterized by getting to know someone, flirting, and building attraction.  It's a stage of exploration and uncertainty before commitment.|Example: They were in a prerelationship for several months before deciding to become an official couple.  他们恋爱前相处了几个月才决定正式成为情侣。|近义词：courtship, dating (early stages), getting to know each other|反义词：relationship (committed), marriage


USAGE->
This word is a noun, and thus doesn't have verb conjugations.  The usage focuses on its function in a sentence.

Example 1:  The prerelationship felt exciting and nerve-wracking. 恋爱前的关系既令人兴奋又令人紧张。
Example 2:  His prerelationship with Sarah lasted for six weeks. 他和莎拉的恋爱前关系持续了六周。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly using "prerelationship".  However, phrases describing activities within a prerelationship are common:


getting to know someone  认识某人
building attraction  培养吸引力
casual dating  随意约会
flirting  调情
hanging out  一起出去玩
