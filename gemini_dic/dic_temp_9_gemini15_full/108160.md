latitudinarian:/ˌlætiˈtuːdɪnəriən/| n. adj.|宽容的；自由主义者；持宽容态度者|A person who is tolerant of the views or practices of others. Someone who believes in freedom of thought and action.|例句：His latitudinarian views on religion were shocking to some.|近义词：liberal, tolerant, broad-minded|反义词：intolerant, rigid, strict|名词，形容词

词源:
From Latin *latitudo "breadth, width," from latus "broad," + -arian.  The original sense referred to breadth of opinion or belief, evolving into the current meaning of tolerance.
