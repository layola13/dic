exploded:/ɪkˈspləʊdɪd/|/ˈɛksploʊdɪd/| v.|爆炸；爆发；迅速增长|To burst or cause to burst violently as a result of internal pressure; to suddenly increase rapidly.|The bomb exploded with a deafening roar. 炸弹发出震耳欲聋的巨响爆炸了。|The company's profits exploded after the launch of their new product. 公司在推出新产品后利润迅速增长。|burst, erupted, detonated|imploded, shrank, decreased|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The volcano explodes violently. = 火山猛烈爆发。
The population explodes in this city. = 这个城市人口激增。


一般过去时:
The balloon exploded. = 气球爆炸了。
The news exploded across the internet. = 这个消息在互联网上迅速传播。


现在完成时:
Three bombs have exploded in the city center. = 市中心已经爆炸了三枚炸弹。
The popularity of the song has exploded. = 这首歌的流行程度已经爆炸式增长。


过去完成时:
The pressure had exploded before the engineer noticed. = 工程师注意到之前压力就已经爆表了。
The building had exploded before the firefighters arrived. = 消防员到达之前，建筑物已经爆炸了。


将来时:
The bomb will explode at midnight. = 炸弹将在午夜爆炸。
The demand for this product will explode next year. = 明年对这种产品的需求将会激增。


虚拟语气:
If the boiler exploded, there would be a big disaster. = 如果锅炉爆炸，将会是一场巨大的灾难。
I wish the rumour hadn’t exploded like that. = 我真希望这个谣言没有这样迅速传播。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
