drop-a-brick: /drɒp ə brɪk/ | phrasal verb | 犯大错；说错话；出丑 | To make a serious mistake, often a social blunder; to say or do something incredibly foolish or embarrassing. | 例句：He really dropped a brick when he asked her about her ex-husband at the party. 他在派对上问她前夫的事，真是犯了个大错。  He dropped a brick by mentioning her weight gain. 他提到她体重增加了，真是说错话了。 | 近义词：blunder, make a fool of oneself, put one's foot in it | 反义词：excel, succeed, shine


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drops a brick every time he opens his mouth. = 他每次开口说话都会犯错。
She drops a brick at every meeting. = 她每次会议都会出丑。

一般过去时:
He dropped a brick at the interview. = 他在面试中犯了一个大错。
She dropped a brick when she mentioned his ex-girlfriend. = 她提到他的前女友，真是说错话了。

现在完成时:
He has dropped a brick so many times, I've lost count. = 他犯了这么多次错，我都数不清了。
She has dropped a brick by revealing the company secret. = 她泄露了公司秘密，犯了个大错。

过去完成时:
He had dropped a brick before he even started the presentation. = 他甚至还没开始演讲就犯了一个大错。
She had dropped a brick earlier in the conversation, and the atmosphere was awkward. = 她早些时候在谈话中犯了个错，气氛很尴尬。

将来时:
He will drop a brick if he keeps talking about politics. = 如果他继续谈论政治，他一定会犯错。
She will drop a brick if she isn't careful during her speech. = 如果她演讲时不小心，她会出丑。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't dropped a brick, he would have gotten the job. = 如果他没有犯错，他就会得到这份工作。
If she hadn't dropped a brick, the meeting would have gone smoothly. = 如果她没有出丑，会议就会顺利进行。

被动语态:
A brick was dropped by him during the important meeting.  = 在重要的会议上，他犯了一个大错。
Many bricks were dropped by the new employee during his first week. = 新员工在第一周犯了很多错。

疑问句:
Did he drop a brick during his speech? = 他在演讲中犯错了吗？
Has she ever dropped a brick in front of her boss? = 她有没有在她老板面前出过丑？

否定句:
He didn't drop a brick during the interview. = 他在面试中没有犯错。
She didn't drop a brick at the dinner party. = 她在那次晚宴上没有出丑。
