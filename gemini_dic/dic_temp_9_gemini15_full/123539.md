monotypic:/ˌmɒnəˈtɪpɪk/| adj. | 单型的；单类型的 |  Containing or consisting of only one type or species.  A monotypic taxon is a taxonomic group (like a genus or family) that contains only one subordinate taxon. For example, a monotypic genus contains only one species. | Example: The genus *Magnolia* is monotypic in some classifications, meaning it contains only one species.  这个属在某些分类中是单型的，意味着它只包含一个物种。 |近义词：single-type, unispecific | 反义词：polytypic
