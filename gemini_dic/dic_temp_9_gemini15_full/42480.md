counterflory: /ˈkaʊntərflori/ | adj. | 反向的，相对的（指旗帜或纹章）|  Describes a heraldic charge (a design on a coat of arms) where two or more fleurs-de-lis (lily flowers) are arranged so that their stems point in opposite directions, creating a counter or opposing effect.  It often implies opposition or contrast.  |Example: The shield displayed a counterflory arrangement of golden fleurs-de-lis on a blue field.  盾牌在一个蓝色底面上展示了一个金色百合花反向排列的图案。| 近义词: opposed, reversed, contrary | 反义词: concordant, aligned, same
