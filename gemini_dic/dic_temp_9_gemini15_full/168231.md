pro-gothic:/ˌproʊˈɡɒθɪk/| adj.| 前哥德式的，哥德式风格之前的 |  Relating to the architectural style that preceded the Gothic style in Europe, typically characterized by Romanesque features.  This term often refers to the transitional period between Romanesque and Gothic architecture, exhibiting a blend of both styles. | Example: The church's pro-Gothic architecture shows a fascinating mix of Romanesque arches and pointed Gothic windows.  这座教堂的前哥德式建筑展现了罗曼式拱门和哥特式尖窗的迷人融合。|近义词：pre-Gothic, Romanesque (in some contexts)|反义词：Gothic
