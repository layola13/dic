hypocaust:/ˈhaɪpəˌkɔːst/| n. |古代罗马地暖系统|A system of underfloor heating in ancient Roman buildings, consisting of a network of flues and chambers beneath the floor through which hot air circulated.  The hot air was typically generated by a furnace located outside or below the structure.|The hypocaust system provided efficient heating for Roman bathhouses and villas.  The hypocaust was a remarkable feat of Roman engineering. |近义词：underfloor heating (现代等效词)|反义词：None (no direct opposite)|名词
