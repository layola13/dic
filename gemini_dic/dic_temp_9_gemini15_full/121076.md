misconvey:/ˌmɪskənˈveɪ/ | vt. | 传达错误；误传 | To communicate something incorrectly or unclearly, leading to a misunderstanding. | 例句：He completely misconveyed my intentions. 他完全误解了我的意图。

例句：The politician's statement misconveyed the facts of the situation. 这位政治家的声明曲解了事实真相。


近义词：misinterpret, misrepresent, distort, miscommunicate | 反义词：convey accurately, communicate clearly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He misconceives my message. 他误解了我的意思。
They misconvey the information to the public. 他们向公众传达了错误的信息。

一般过去时:
She misconveyed the meaning of the poem. 她误解了这首诗的意思。
The journalist misconveyed the facts in his report.  记者在他的报道中歪曲了事实。

现在完成时:
The company has misconveyed its marketing strategy. 这家公司已经误传了它的营销策略。
He has misconveyed the importance of the meeting. 他已经误传了会议的重要性。


过去完成时:
They had misconveyed the instructions before starting the project. 他们在项目开始前就已经误解了指示。
I realised I had misconveyed my feelings to her. 我意识到我之前对她表达了错误的感情。


将来时:
She will misconvey the information to the board. 她将把信息误传给董事会。

虚拟语气:
If he had not misconveyed the instructions, the accident would not have happened. 如果他没有误传指示，事故就不会发生了。
I wish I hadn't misconveyed my intentions to her. 我真希望我没有对她表达错误的意图。


被动语态:
The message was misconveyed by the messenger. 信息被信使误传了。
The instructions were misconveyed, leading to confusion. 指示被误传，导致了混乱。


疑问句:
Did he misconvey the purpose of the meeting? 他是否误传了会议的目的？

否定句:
He did not misconvey his feelings. 他没有误传他的感情。
The teacher made sure not to misconvey the lesson's main points. 老师确保没有误传课程的要点。
