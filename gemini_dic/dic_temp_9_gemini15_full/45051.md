cubbyhole:/ˈkʌbiˌhoʊl/| n. |壁橱；小隔间；小房间|A small, enclosed space, often a cupboard or recess, used for storage.  It can also refer to a small, cramped room or office.|例句：He keeps his tools in a cubbyhole in the garage. 他把工具放在车库的一个小隔间里。

例句：The office was just a tiny cubbyhole, barely big enough for a desk and chair. 办公室只是一个狭小的隔间，勉强够放一张桌子和一把椅子。

近义词：compartment, recess, nook, closet

反义词：large room, spacious area


词性：名词


词源:  The word "cubbyhole" is a compound word. "Cubby" is a diminutive of "cub," referring to something small and snug.  "Hole" refers to a space or opening. The combination suggests a small, enclosed space.  The etymology traces back to the 19th century, with the earliest recorded usage appearing in the late 1800s.  The exact origin of the "cubby" part remains somewhat uncertain, but its diminutive nature is clear.
