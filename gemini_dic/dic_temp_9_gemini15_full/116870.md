maxwells:/ˈmækswɛlz/ | n. |麦克斯韦(复数)；麦克斯韦方程组|Maxwell's equations are a set of four equations that describe how electric and magnetic fields are generated and altered by each other and by charges and currents.  They are fundamental to the understanding of electromagnetism and have far-reaching applications in various fields of physics and engineering.  The plural form "maxwells" refers to either multiple instances of Maxwell's equations or, less commonly, to units of magnetic flux (now obsolete).|The Maxwell's equations elegantly unify electricity and magnetism. |近义词: (No direct synonyms, as it refers to a specific scientific concept)|反义词: (No direct antonyms)|名词
