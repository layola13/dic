parables:/ˈpærəblz/ | n. | 寓言；比喻 |A parable is a simple story used to illustrate a moral or spiritual lesson.  Parables often use everyday situations and characters to convey complex ideas in an accessible way.  They are commonly found in religious texts, but can also be used in secular contexts. | Example: Jesus often used parables to teach his disciples about the kingdom of God.  耶稣经常用寓言来教导他的门徒关于上帝的国度。  |近义词：allegory, fable, analogy |反义词：None specifically, but perhaps a direct explanation could be considered an antonym in the context of teaching a moral lesson. |名词
