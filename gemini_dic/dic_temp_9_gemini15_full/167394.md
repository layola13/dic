preundertook:/ˌpriːˈʌndərtuːk/| v.| 预先承担；预先着手 | To undertake something before it is officially or formally begun.  It implies a proactive and possibly unofficial commitment. |例句：The team preundertook the project, securing necessary materials before the official start date.  团队在项目正式启动前就预先着手，确保了必要的材料。


The company preundertook some market research to gauge public interest. 公司预先进行了一些市场调查以评估公众兴趣。|近义词：preempt, anticipate, initiate, commence |反义词：neglect, postpone, defer |动词
