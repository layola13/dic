macro-evolution:/ˌmækroʊˌɛvəˈluːʃən/ | n. |宏观进化；大进化 | The large-scale evolutionary changes that take place over long periods, resulting in the formation of new taxonomic groups.  It encompasses the processes that lead to the diversification of life and the emergence of new species, genera, families, and higher taxonomic ranks.  It contrasts with microevolution, which focuses on smaller-scale changes within populations. | Example: The fossil record provides strong evidence for macro-evolutionary trends, such as the gradual increase in brain size in hominids.  宏观进化趋势（例如，人科动物脑容量的逐渐增大）的化石记录提供了强有力的证据。 |近义词：large-scale evolution, major evolutionary change |反义词：microevolution |名词
