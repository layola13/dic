contend:/kənˈtɛnd/| vt. vi. n.|竞争；争论；主张；坚决认为|to struggle to overcome a difficulty or an opponent; to argue or disagree strongly; a point of contention|例句：She had to contend with her boss's bad temper. 她不得不应付老板的坏脾气。

例句：He contended that the election was unfair. 他认为选举不公平。


近义词：compete, struggle, dispute, assert, maintain|反义词：yield, concede, acquiesce|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He contends that the earth is flat. = 他认为地球是平的。
They contend for the championship title. = 他们争夺冠军头衔。


一般过去时:
She contended with numerous challenges in her career. = 她在事业上克服了许多挑战。
They contended fiercely over the inheritance. = 他们为遗产激烈争夺。


现在完成时:
We have contended with this problem for years. = 我们多年来一直在努力解决这个问题。
He has contended throughout his life for social justice. = 他一生都在为社会正义而奋斗。


过去完成时:
They had contended for hours before reaching an agreement. = 他们在达成协议之前争论了几个小时。
She had contended with similar situations before. = 她以前也遇到过类似的情况。


将来时:
We will contend with the consequences of our actions. = 我们将承担我们行为的后果。
They will contend against a formidable opponent in the final. = 他们在决赛中将与强大的对手对抗。


虚拟语气:
If I were to contend with him, I would certainly lose. = 如果我和他竞争，我肯定会输。
I wish I hadn't contended with him so aggressively. = 我真希望我没有那么咄咄逼人地和他争论。


被动语态:
The issue was contended by both sides. = 双方都对这个问题进行了争论。
The championship will be contended for by many strong teams. = 许多强大的球队将争夺冠军。


疑问句:
Will you contend for the position? = 你会竞争这个职位吗？
What points are they contending over? = 他们在争论什么问题？


否定句:
He did not contend with the difficulties. = 他没有克服困难。
They did not contend for the prize. = 他们没有争夺奖品。


PHRASE->
contend with = 与...竞争/对抗
contend for = 为...而竞争
contend against = 对抗...
contend that = 主张...
contend over = 为...而争论

词源:
源自拉丁语 *contendere，意思是“拉紧，努力”。  它包含词根 *con- (together) 和 *tendere (to stretch)。  因此，其本义与“努力争取”相关。
