nonsibilancies:/ˌnɒnˈsɪbɪlənsɪz/ | n. |非咝音|Nonsibilancies refer to sounds in speech that are not sibilants.  Sibilants are consonants characterized by a hissing or buzzing sound produced by forcing air through a narrow channel in the vocal tract.  Nonsibilancies encompass all other consonant and vowel sounds.  The term is primarily used in phonetics and phonology. |The study focused on the acoustic properties of nonsibilancies in different languages.  研究重点是不同语言中非咝音的声学特性。|近义词:non-sibilants|反义词:sibilants|名词
