self-exposed:/ˌsɛlfɪkˈspoʊzd/ | adj. | 自己暴露的；自我揭露的 |  Describes something or someone that has revealed themselves or their information, often unintentionally or against their will.  This often implies a vulnerability or a negative consequence. | Example: The self-exposed documents revealed a major scandal.  这些自我暴露的文件揭露了一场重大丑闻。  The self-exposed hacker faced severe legal consequences.  这位自我暴露的黑客面临着严重的法律后果。 |近义词：revealed, unveiled, disclosed, unmasked | 反义词：concealed, hidden, secret, undisclosed |


USAGE->

The website self-exposes user data. = 该网站会自行暴露用户数据。
This action self-exposes their weaknesses. = 这个举动暴露了他们的弱点。
