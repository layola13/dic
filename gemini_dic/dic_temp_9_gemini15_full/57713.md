dread:/drɛd/ | n., v. | 恐惧，担忧；害怕；使恐惧 | To feel extreme apprehension or fear; to anticipate with great dislike or fear.  |例句：The thought of the exam filled me with dread.  考试的想法让我充满了恐惧。

I dread going to the dentist. 我害怕去看牙医。|近义词：fear, terror, apprehension, horror |反义词：joy, delight, pleasure |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I dread public speaking. = 我害怕公开演讲。
She dreads the upcoming surgery. = 她害怕即将到来的手术。


一般过去时:
He dreaded the consequences of his actions. = 他害怕他行为的后果。
They dreaded the long journey ahead. = 他们害怕漫长的旅程。


现在完成时:
I have dreaded this day for weeks. = 我几周来一直害怕这一天。
She has dreaded telling him the bad news. = 她一直害怕告诉他这个坏消息。


过去完成时:
I had dreaded the meeting all week. = 我整个星期都在害怕这个会议。
They had dreaded the possibility of failure. = 他们一直害怕失败的可能性。


将来时:
I will dread the day I have to leave. = 我将害怕我不得不离开的那一天。
She will dread facing her boss after the mistake. = 她将害怕在犯错后面对她的老板。


虚拟语气:
I would dread to think what would happen if... = 我不敢想象如果……会发生什么。
If I were you, I would dread that possibility. = 如果我是你，我会害怕那种可能性。


被动语态:
The task was dreaded by everyone. = 这项任务让每个人都害怕。
The upcoming exam is dreaded by the students. = 即将到来的考试让学生们害怕。


疑问句:
What do you dread most about the job? = 你最害怕这份工作的什么？
Do you dread anything in particular? = 你有什么特别害怕的吗？


否定句:
I don't dread the work itself, but the deadlines. = 我并不害怕工作本身，而是截止日期。
She doesn't dread challenges; she embraces them. = 她不怕挑战；她拥抱挑战。


PHRASE->
in dread of = 害怕…
with dread = 害怕地
full of dread = 充满恐惧的
a dread disease = 一种可怕的疾病
dreadful sight = 可怕的景象
