drowned:/draʊnd/| vt.|淹死；使沉没；使泛滥；|to die under water; to cover completely with water; to overwhelm or suppress|例句：The child drowned in the river.  孩子在河里淹死了。|例句：The heavy rain drowned the crops. 大雨淹没了庄稼。|近义词：submerge, inundate, suffocate|反义词：save, rescue


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The river often drowns low-lying areas. = 这条河经常淹没低洼地区。
He drowns his sorrows in alcohol. = 他借酒消愁。

一般过去时:
The ship drowned in the storm. = 船在暴风雨中沉没了。
They drowned their anxieties in work. = 他们埋头工作以消除焦虑。

现在完成时:
The heavy rain has drowned the fields. = 大雨淹没了田地。
He has drowned his grief in drink. = 他借酒消愁。

过去完成时:
The village had been drowned by the flood. = 村庄已经被洪水淹没了。
They had drowned their sorrows in a night of drinking. = 他们彻夜狂饮以消愁解闷。

将来时:
The rising tide will drown the coastal towns. = 上涨的潮水将会淹没沿海城镇。
He fears the upcoming work will drown him. = 他担心即将到来的工作会压垮他。

虚拟语气:
If the dam had broken, the whole valley would have been drowned. = 如果大坝决堤，整个山谷都会被淹没。
If I hadn't worked so hard, I would have been drowned by my responsibilities. = 如果我不那么努力工作，我就会被责任压垮。

被动语态:
Many houses were drowned by the flood. = 许多房屋被洪水淹没了。
His voice was drowned out by the noise.  他的声音被噪音盖住了。

疑问句:
Was the cat drowned? = 猫被淹死了吗？
Were the crops drowned by the rain? = 庄稼被雨水淹了吗？

否定句:
The boat didn't drown. = 船没有沉没。
He didn't drown his worries in alcohol. = 他没有借酒消愁。
