disproportionately: /ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːʃənətli/ | adv. |不成比例地，不相称地 |  The adverb disproportionately means "to a degree that is out of proportion" or "unevenly." It describes a situation where something affects one group or aspect more significantly than others, not in a balanced or fair way.  |  例句：Minorities are disproportionately represented in prison. 少数族裔在监狱中的比例过高。

The impact of the storm was disproportionately felt by coastal communities.  风暴的影响对沿海社区的影响尤为严重。


近义词：unevenly, unfairly, inequitably, disproportionately, in a biased manner


反义词：proportionately, equitably, fairly, evenly


词源:

* **dis-:**  prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of"
* **proportion:**  from Old French *proportion*, from Latin *proportio*, from *pro* ("forward, forth") + *portio* ("portion, part").  Meaning a comparative relation between things or magnitudes.
* **-ate:** suffix forming adverbs from adjectives.  (e.g., fortunate -> fortunately)
* **-ly:** adverbial suffix.
