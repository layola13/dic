consumer-price-indexes:/kənˈsjuːmər praɪs ˈɪndɪsiːz/| n. |消费者物价指数|Consumer Price Indexes (CPI) are measures that examine the weighted average of prices of a basket of consumer goods and services, such as transportation, food, and medical care.  It is calculated by government statistical agencies to track changes in the price level of consumer goods and services purchased by households.  The CPI is a key indicator of inflation and is used by governments, businesses, and individuals to make economic decisions.|The CPI rose by 2% last year. 去年消费者物价指数上涨了2%。|Inflation rate, cost of living index|Producer Price Index (PPI), Deflation|
