laid-back: /ˌleɪdˈbæk/ | adj. |轻松的，悠闲的，不拘束的 | Relaxed and easygoing; not worried or stressed.  |例句：He has a laid-back attitude towards work. 他对工作态度很轻松。

例句：She's got a really laid-back style. 她有一种非常轻松随意的风格。


近义词：relaxed, casual, easygoing, unhurried, nonchalant

反义词：stressed, tense, anxious, uptight, rigid


词源:  The term "laid-back" originated in the mid-20th century, likely emerging from surfer culture. The "laid" part refers to the relaxed posture often adopted while sitting or lying down,  and "back" indicates the relaxed, reclined position. Over time, it evolved to describe a general attitude of relaxation and unconcern.
