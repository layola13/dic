counterfacts:/ˈkaʊntərfækts/| n. |反事实|A counterfactual is a statement that describes a situation that did not happen but could have happened.  It often involves imagining a different outcome to a past event.  Counterfactuals are frequently used in historical analysis, scientific inquiry, and everyday thinking to explore alternative possibilities and understand cause and effect.|Example: "If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam." (This describes a situation that didn't happen – the speaker did not pass the exam – but could have happened if they had studied more.)|近义词：hypothetical, speculative, what-if scenario |反义词：fact, reality
