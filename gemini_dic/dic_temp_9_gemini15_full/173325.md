quasi-dejected:/ˈkwɑːziːdɪˈdʒektɪd/| adj.|略感沮丧的，有点沮丧的|Slightly dejected; somewhat disheartened.  The word combines "quasi," meaning "seemingly" or "to a certain extent," with "dejected," meaning "sad and depressed." It implies a milder form of dejection, not a full-blown state of sadness.|例句：He felt quasi-dejected after the interview, not entirely hopeless but certainly disappointed.|近义词：dispirited, downcast, disheartened, slightly depressed|反义词：elated, cheerful, joyful, upbeat
