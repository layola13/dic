indiscriminative: /ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv/ | adj. |不加选择的；不加区别的；不加鉴别的 | Not making or based on careful distinctions; lacking discrimination.  Showing a lack of careful judgment or distinction. | 例句：The indiscriminate use of pesticides is harming the environment.  滥用杀虫剂正在危害环境。

例句：His indiscriminate spending habits led to financial ruin. 他的不加节制的消费习惯导致了经济破产。


近义词：undiscriminating, unselective, haphazard, random, careless


反义词：discriminating, selective, discerning, judicious, careful


词源：
* in- (prefix): not
* dis- (prefix): not, opposite of
* crimin- (root): to separate, distinguish (from Latin *cernere, "to separate, sift")
* -ative (suffix): tending to, given to

She is indiscriminate in her friendships. = 她在友谊上不加区别。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

They had been indiscriminate in their hiring practices. = 他们过去招聘时不加选择。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
