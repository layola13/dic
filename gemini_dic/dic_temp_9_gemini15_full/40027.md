consumerization:/ˌkənˌsuːməraɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |消费化；消费者化；民用化|The process by which goods and services that were once only available to businesses or specific groups become widely available to consumers.  This often involves a shift in technology and business models.  For example, the consumerization of IT refers to the trend of employees using their personal devices (smartphones, tablets) for work purposes.|Example: The consumerization of cloud computing has led to a significant increase in the adoption of cloud-based services by individuals.|近义词：commodification, popularization |反义词：professionalization, specialization
