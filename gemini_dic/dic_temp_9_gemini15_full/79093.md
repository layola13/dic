glaciers:/ˈɡleɪʃərz/| n. |冰川|Large masses of ice that slowly move down mountains or across land. Glaciers are formed by the accumulation and compaction of snow over many years. They are a significant feature of many mountainous regions and polar areas, and play a crucial role in the Earth's climate system.  |Example: The majestic glaciers of Alaska are a breathtaking sight.  阿拉斯加雄伟的冰川令人叹为观止。  The melting glaciers are a clear indication of climate change.  冰川融化是气候变化的明显迹象。|近义词：ice sheet, ice cap |反义词：None (Antonyms are not directly applicable as it describes a natural phenomenon)|名词

Scientists have observed changes in glacier movement. = 科学家们已经观察到冰川运动的变化。

过去完成时:

将来时:
