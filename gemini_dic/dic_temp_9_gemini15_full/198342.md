show-stopper:/ˈʃoʊˌstɑːpər/| n.| 令人震惊的事物；极精彩的表演；阻碍进展的事物 |Something or someone that is so impressive or exciting that it stops everything else from being noticed.  It can also refer to something that prevents progress. |例句：Her performance was a real show-stopper. 她的表演真是令人惊艳。

例句：The software bug proved to be a show-stopper, delaying the project by weeks.  这个软件漏洞证明是一个阻碍性问题，使项目推迟了数周。|近义词：spectacle, sensation, highlight, obstacle, impediment |反义词：dullness, bore, triviality

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The singer's voice is a show-stopper. = 歌手的歌声令人惊艳。
That new design is a real show-stopper. = 那个新的设计真是令人眼前一亮。


一般过去时:
His dance routine was a show-stopper. = 他的舞蹈表演令人惊叹。
The unexpected rain was a show-stopper for the outdoor concert. = 突如其来的雨使得户外音乐会无法进行。


现在完成时:
Her latest painting has been a show-stopper at the gallery. = 她的最新画作在画廊里引起了轰动。
The technical difficulties have been a show-stopper for the entire project. = 技术难题一直是整个项目中的阻碍。


过去完成时:
The lead actor's illness had been a show-stopper for the play's opening night. = 男主角的生病曾经是该剧首映之夜的阻碍。
The unexpected snowstorm had been a show-stopper for our travel plans. = 突如其来的暴风雪曾经打乱了我们的旅行计划。


将来时:
The new product launch will be a show-stopper. = 新产品的发布会将令人惊艳。
The lack of funding will be a show-stopper for the research. = 资金短缺将阻碍这项研究。


虚拟语气:
If her dress were a show-stopper, it would be featured in Vogue. = 如果她的衣服很惊艳，它将会被刊登在《Vogue》杂志上。
If the budget cuts hadn't been a show-stopper, the project would have been completed on time. = 如果预算削减不是一个阻碍，项目就能按时完成了。


被动语态:
The performance was deemed a show-stopper by all the critics. = 这场表演被所有评论家认为是令人惊艳的。
The project was delayed because a critical bug was a show-stopper. = 项目被延误，因为一个严重的漏洞是一个阻碍。


疑问句:

Is the lack of resources a show-stopper for the project? = 资源短缺是这个项目的阻碍吗？


否定句:
His presentation wasn't a show-stopper. = 他的演讲并不惊艳。
The minor glitches weren't show-stoppers for the software launch. = 轻微的故障并未阻碍软件发布。
