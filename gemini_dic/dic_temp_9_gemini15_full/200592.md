slave:/sleɪv/ | n. |奴隶，奴仆 | A person who is the legal property of another and is forced to obey them.  Slaves were historically subjected to harsh treatment and denied basic human rights.  The institution of slavery has been abolished in most countries, but its legacy continues to affect societies worldwide. | Example:  He was sold into slavery as a young boy. 他小时候被卖为奴隶。  The escaped slaves found refuge in the Underground Railroad. 逃亡的奴隶在“地下铁路”找到了避难所。|近义词：bondservant, serf, captive |反义词：master, owner
