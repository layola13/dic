sinecurist:/ˈsɪnɪkjuərɪst/| n. |享有俸禄而无须工作的人；闲差人员 | A sinecurist is a person who holds a position requiring little or no work but providing a salary or other benefits.  The term often implies a sense of unfairness or privilege. | Example:  The scandal revealed that several high-ranking officials were essentially sinecurists, drawing large salaries while contributing little to the organization.  = 丑闻揭露了几位高级官员实际上是闲差人员，领取高薪却对组织贡献甚微。 |近义词：placeman, officeholder (in a derogatory sense) |反义词：hard worker, diligent employee |名词
