marginals:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəlz/ | adj. |边缘的；微不足道的；不重要的；边际的；（经济学）边际的 | Relating to or situated at the edge or margin of something.  In economics, relating to incremental changes. |例句：The marginal notes in the book were helpful. 书中的边注很有帮助。


The marginal cost of producing one more unit was low. 生产多一单位的边际成本很低。|近义词：peripheral, minor, insignificant, trifling |反义词：central, major, significant, important |


USAGE->

These marginal changes don't significantly affect the outcome. = 这些微小的变化不会显著影响结果。

一般过去时:
The marginal cost was calculated based on the last unit produced. = 边际成本是根据生产的最后一个单位计算的。

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
