/ˈmɛləˌnaɪd/| n. |黑色素瘤样病变|A mellonide is a lesion that resembles a melanoma but is not cancerous. It's characterized by its dark pigmentation and often irregular borders, mimicking the appearance of a malignant melanoma.  However,  a proper diagnosis requires a biopsy to distinguish it from a true melanoma.  Further research is needed to fully understand the etiology and pathogenesis of mellonides.|Example: The dermatologist carefully examined the lesion, determining it to be a mellonide rather than a melanoma.|近义词：pigmented lesion, nevus|反义词：melanoma (in terms of malignancy)|名词
