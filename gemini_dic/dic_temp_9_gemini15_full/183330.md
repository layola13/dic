reprobation:/ˌrɛprəˈbeɪʃən/| n. |谴责；斥责；非难|The act of expressing strong disapproval or condemnation; the state of being condemned or disapproved of.  It often implies a feeling of moral outrage or disgust. |例句：His actions met with widespread reprobation.  他的行为受到了广泛的谴责。


例句：The thief faced the reprobation of the entire community.  这个贼受到了整个社区的谴责。


近义词：condemnation, censure, disapproval, denunciation, blame


反义词：approval, praise, commendation, acceptance


词性：名词

词源:
From Old French *reprobation, from Latin reprobātiōn-, from reprobāre "to disapprove, reject," from re- "again" + probāre "to test, prove."  Ultimately related to the English word "prove."
