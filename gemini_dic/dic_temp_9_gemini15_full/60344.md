egophony:/ˈɛɡəfəni/| n. |语音共振，自体语音增强|A phenomenon where a patient's voice sounds louder and clearer to the listener when auscultated over a consolidated area of the lung (e.g., due to pneumonia or pleural effusion). This happens because sound waves are better transmitted through a denser medium.  It is a clinical sign used in physical examination of the lungs.|例句：The doctor detected egophony during the lung auscultation, suggesting a possible consolidation.  医生在肺部听诊时发现了语音共振，提示可能存在实变。|近义词：bronchophony, whispered pectoriloquy|反义词：normal breath sounds
