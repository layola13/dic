rhyncholite:/ˈrɪŋkoʊlaɪt/| n. |喙石，鸟嘴石|A fossil of a bird's beak or a similar structure, particularly those found in ancient reptile beaks.  These fossils often represent only the hardened beak-like structure and not the surrounding soft tissue. They are commonly found in marine sediments.|Example:  The paleontologist carefully examined the rhyncholite, hoping to determine the species of extinct reptile it belonged to.  古生物学家仔细检查了喙石，希望能确定它所属的灭绝爬行动物种类。|近义词：beak fossil, rostral fossil |反义词：None specifically, but words implying a complete skull or skeleton could be considered contrasting.
