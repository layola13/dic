secrets: /ˈsiːkrɛts/ | n. | 秘密，机密 |  Something kept hidden or unknown.  A secret is something that is intentionally kept hidden from others. It can be a piece of information, a plan, or an event.  Secrets can be personal, involving only a few people, or they can be larger, impacting many.  | 例句：She confided her deepest secrets to her diary. 她把最深的秘密告诉了她的日记。

He kept the secret of his wealth from his family. 他对家人隐瞒了他的财富。


近义词：mystery, enigma, hidden information | 反义词：openness, transparency, public knowledge
