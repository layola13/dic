expatriated:/ɪkˈspætrieɪtɪd/ | adj. v. | 移居国外的；被驱逐出境的； |  Expatriated refers to someone who has left their native country to live in another, often for an extended period.  As a verb, it means to force someone to leave their native country. | Example (adj.):  Many expatriated artists found inspiration in their new homes.  许多移居国外的艺术家在他们的新家找到了灵感。 Example (v.): The dictator expatriated his political opponents. 独裁者驱逐了他的政治对手。|近义词 (adj.):  exiled, foreign, overseas | 反义词 (adj.): repatriated, native | 词性: adjective, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He expatriates himself to find a better life. = 他移居国外寻找更好的生活。
They expatriate talented individuals for their own benefit. = 他们为了自身利益而驱逐有才能的人。


一般过去时:
She expatriated herself to Paris many years ago. = 多年前她移居巴黎。
The government expatriated the dissidents. = 政府驱逐了异议人士。


现在完成时:
He has expatriated himself to Canada. = 他已经移居加拿大。
The company has expatriated several employees to different countries. = 公司已经将几名员工派驻到不同的国家。


过去完成时:
She had expatriated herself before the war broke out. = 在战争爆发之前，她已经移居国外了。
The king had expatriated his enemies long before the rebellion. = 国王早在叛乱之前就驱逐了他的敌人。


将来时:
They will expatriate him if he continues his activities. = 如果他继续他的活动，他们将会驱逐他。
She will expatriate herself to pursue her dreams. = 她将移居国外去追求她的梦想。


虚拟语气:
If I had the chance, I would expatriate myself to a tropical island. = 如果我有机会，我会移居到一个热带岛屿。
If he were to expatriate himself, he would miss his family. = 如果他移居国外，他会想念他的家人。


被动语态:
He was expatriated for political reasons. = 他因为政治原因被驱逐出境。
Many people were expatriated during the conflict. = 冲突期间许多人被驱逐出境。


疑问句:
Has he expatriated himself yet? = 他已经移居国外了吗？
Was she expatriated from her homeland? = 她是否被驱逐出她的祖国？


否定句:
He did not expatriate himself, he immigrated. = 他没有移居国外，他是移民。
She was not expatriated; she left voluntarily. = 她没有被驱逐出境；她是自愿离开的。
