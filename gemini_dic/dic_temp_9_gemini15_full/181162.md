reing:/reɪŋ/ | v. | 统治；支配；(尤指国王)当政 |  reign means to rule as a monarch or sovereign. It often implies a period of power and authority.  |例句：Queen Victoria reigned for over 60 years. 维多利亚女王统治了60多年。 |例句：His reign as champion ended last year. 他作为冠军的统治在去年结束。|近义词：rule, govern, preside, dominate |反义词：be ruled, be governed, be subjected


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The king reigns over the kingdom. = 国王统治着王国。
She reigns supreme in the fashion world. = 她在时尚界占据主导地位。


一般过去时:
The emperor reigned for many decades. = 这位皇帝统治了几十年。
The dynasty reigned for centuries. = 这个王朝统治了几个世纪。


现在完成时:
The queen has reigned for fifty years. = 女王已经统治了五十年。
He has reigned over the company since 2000. = 自2000年以来，他一直领导着这家公司。


过去完成时:
Before the new king reigned, there was a period of unrest. = 新国王统治之前，曾有一段动荡时期。
The previous ruler had reigned for only five years. = 前任统治者只统治了五年。


将来时:
The prince will reign after his father's death. = 王子将在父亲死后继位。
She will likely reign for many years to come. = 她很可能会统治很多年。


虚拟语气:
If he reigned wisely, his people would have prospered. = 如果他英明统治，他的子民就会繁荣。
I wish the previous king had reigned longer. = 我希望前任国王统治的时间更长一些。


被动语态:
The country was reigned over by a just king. = 这个国家由一位公正的国王统治。
The kingdom was effectively reigned over by the prime minister. = 王国实际上是由首相统治的。


疑问句:
How long did the Roman emperors reign? = 罗马皇帝统治了多久？
Who reigns over that small country? = 谁统治着那个小国？


否定句:
The tyrant did not reign justly. = 暴君统治不公正。
The new leader failed to reign effectively. = 新领导人未能有效地统治。
