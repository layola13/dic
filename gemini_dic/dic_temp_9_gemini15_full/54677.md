disorder:/dɪsˈɔːrdər/| n. v.|紊乱；疾病；扰乱；使混乱|a state of confusion or lack of order; a disturbance of the peace;  an illness or medical condition |例句：The room was in complete disorder.  房间里一片混乱。|The doctor diagnosed a serious disorder. 医生诊断出一种严重的疾病。|近义词：chaos, confusion, turmoil, illness, disease |反义词：order, peace, calm, health


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The noise disorders the meeting. = 噪音扰乱了会议。
She disorders her room every day. = 她每天都把房间弄乱。


一般过去时:
The riot disordered the city. = 暴乱扰乱了城市。
He disordered the files on his computer. = 他弄乱了他电脑上的文件。


现在完成时:
The storm has disordered the landscape. = 暴风雨已经破坏了风景。
Years of neglect have disordered the building. = 多年的忽视使这座建筑物变得破败不堪。


过去完成时:
The earthquake had disordered the town before the rescue team arrived. = 在救援队到达之前，地震已经摧毁了小镇。
They had disordered the evidence before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前，他们已经毁掉了证据。


将来时:
The coming storm will disorder the traffic. = 即将来临的风暴将扰乱交通。
He will disorder the whole plan if he keeps doing this. = 如果他继续这样做，他会打乱整个计划。


虚拟语气:
If he had not disordered the documents, we would have finished the report on time. = 如果他没有弄乱文件，我们就能按时完成报告。
I wish he hadn't disordered my schedule. = 我真希望他没有打乱我的日程安排。


被动语态:
The city was disordered by the flood. = 城市被洪水弄得一片混乱。
The meeting was disordered by the late arrival of the chairman. = 主席迟到扰乱了会议。


疑问句:

否定句:
The government didn't disorder the economy. = 政府并没有扰乱经济。
He didn't disorder the files. = 他没有弄乱文件。
