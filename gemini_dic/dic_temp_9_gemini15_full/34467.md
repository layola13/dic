claw: /klɔː/ | n., v. | 爪；抓；挠 |

英文解析:A claw is a sharp, curved nail on the foot of an animal, especially a bird or mammal.  It can also refer to the pincer-like appendage of some animals, such as crabs and lobsters. As a verb, it means to scratch, scrape, or pull at something with claws or fingernails.

例句:
The cat sharpened its claws on the scratching post. = 猫在抓猫柱上磨爪子。

The eagle used its powerful claws to grasp its prey. = 老鹰用它强壮的爪子抓住猎物。


近义词: talon, nail, hook, scratch

反义词:  (无直接反义词，但可以考虑与“gentle touch”相对)


词性:
名词 (n.): claw
动词 (v.): claw


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The cat claws the furniture. = 猫抓坏家具。
Birds claw at the ground to find food. = 鸟儿扒着地面找食物。

一般过去时:
The bear clawed at the tree trunk. = 熊抓挠树干。
She clawed her way to the top of the mountain. = 她艰难地爬上了山顶。

现在完成时:
The dog has clawed at the door all morning. = 这狗整个早上都在抓门。
He has clawed his way to success. = 他凭借自己的努力获得了成功。

过去完成时:
The thief had clawed his way through the window. = 小偷已经从窗户爬进来了。
The cat had clawed at the curtains before we got home. = 我们回家之前，猫已经抓破窗帘了。

将来时:
The kitten will claw at your clothes if you tease it. = 如果你逗弄小猫，它会抓你的衣服。

虚拟语气:

I wish I hadn't clawed the car. = 我真希望我没有抓坏汽车。

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "claw" comes from Old English *clawu, related to Dutch klauw, German Klaue, and ultimately Proto-Germanic *klawō.  The word's origins are likely onomatopoeic, meaning it imitates the sound of scratching.
