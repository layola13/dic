quasi-determining: /ˈkwɑːzi dɪˈtɜːrmɪnɪŋ/ | adj. | 准决定性的，近似决定性的 |  Describes a situation or factor that has a strong influence on an outcome but does not entirely determine it.  It suggests a high degree of probability or influence, but acknowledges the possibility of other contributing factors. | Example:  The initial conditions were quasi-determining; while they strongly suggested a particular outcome,  minor variations could have led to different results.  | 近义词: largely determining, predominantly influencing, heavily impacting | 反义词: indeterminate, random, chance
