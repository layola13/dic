misdemeanour:/ˌmɪsdɪˈmiːnə(r)/| n. |轻罪；不端行为；轻微的过失|A misdemeanour is a minor crime, less serious than a felony.  It typically involves a less severe punishment than a felony. |例句：He was arrested for a misdemeanour. 他因犯轻罪被捕。

例句：Driving without a license is a common misdemeanour. 无证驾驶是一种常见的轻罪。


近义词：minor offense, petty crime, infraction, delinquency |反义词：felony, crime (in the sense of a serious crime) |词性：名词


词源:

The word "misdemeanour" comes from the Old French word "mesdemener," which means "to mismanage" or "to behave badly."  The prefix "mis-" indicates wrong or bad, and "demeanour" refers to conduct or behavior.  Over time, the word evolved to specifically refer to a less serious crime.
