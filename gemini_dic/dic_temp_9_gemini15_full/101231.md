inviter:/ɪnˈvaɪtər/ | n. | 邀请者，邀请人 | A person who invites someone to an event or place.  | Example: The inviter sent out personalized invitations.  邀请者寄出了个性化的邀请函。|近义词: host, guest (in some contexts)|反义词:拒絶者 (no direct antonym)|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The inviter sends out emails. = 邀请者发送电子邮件。
The inviter greets the guests. = 邀请者问候客人。


一般过去时:
The inviter sent out the invitations last week. = 邀请者上周寄出了请柬。
The inviter welcomed everyone warmly. = 邀请者热情地欢迎了每个人。


现在完成时:
The inviter has already sent out all the invitations. = 邀请者已经寄出了所有请柬。
The inviter has arranged everything for the party. = 邀请者已经为派对安排好了一切。


过去完成时:
The inviter had already prepared the venue before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，邀请者已经准备好了场地。
The inviter had sent a reminder email the day before. = 邀请者前一天发送了一封提醒邮件。

虚拟语气:

I wish the inviter had more time to spend with the guests. = 我希望邀请者有更多时间与客人相处。


被动语态:
The guests were invited by the inviter. = 客人是由邀请者邀请的。
The invitations were sent out by the inviter. = 请柬是由邀请者寄出的。


疑问句:

否定句:

The inviter wasn't able to attend the event. = 邀请者无法参加活动。

词源:
The word "inviter" is derived from the verb "invite," which comes from the Old French word "inviter," meaning "to call in, invite."  The suffix "-er" is an agent suffix, indicating the person who performs the action.
