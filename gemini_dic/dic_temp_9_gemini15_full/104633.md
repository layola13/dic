karakul: /ˈkærəkʊl/ | n. | 卡拉库尔羊皮；卡拉库尔羔羊皮 |  Karakul refers to a type of sheepskin, specifically the pelt of a karakul lamb.  This lambskin is known for its distinctive tightly curled, glossy fleece.  The pelts are used to make coats, hats, and other garments.  The term may also refer to the breed of sheep itself. | Example:  Her luxurious coat was made of exquisite karakul.  她的奢华外套是用精美的卡拉库尔羊皮制成的。 | 近义词: astrakhan (similar type of lambskin) | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but perhaps smooth leather) |  n.
