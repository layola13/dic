dashing:/ˈdæʃɪŋ/ | adj. | 潇洒的；英俊的；充满活力的 | Stylish and attractive; full of energy and spirit.  The word often implies a certain boldness or flair. | 例句：He was a dashing young officer with a charming smile. 他是一位潇洒的年轻军官，带着迷人的微笑。

例句：The dashing hero rescued the princess from the dragon's lair.  英勇潇洒的英雄从巨龙的巢穴中救出了公主。


近义词：stylish, handsome, spirited, dashing, spirited, gallant, debonair


反义词：slovenly, dowdy, drab, unstylish


词性：形容词


词源：源自动词“dash”，意为“猛冲，飞奔”。  最初形容以一种快速、充满活力和自信的方式行动的人，后来引申为形容外貌和举止的潇洒和英俊。  The original meaning relates to the swift and energetic movements of a dashing person.  The sense of attractive style developed later.
