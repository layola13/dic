dorks:/dɔːrks/| n.|笨蛋，傻瓜|A dork is a person considered to be foolish or socially inept.  The term is often used informally and can be considered offensive.|例句：He's such a dork; he wears mismatched socks.|近义词：nerd, geek, goofball, klutz|反义词：genius, superstar, cool person


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a total dork. = 他是个十足的笨蛋。
They are such dorks. = 他们真是些笨蛋。

一般过去时:
He was a dork in high school. = 他高中时是个笨蛋。
She acted like a dork at the party. = 她在派对上表现得像个傻瓜。

现在完成时:
He has always been a dork. = 他一直是个笨蛋。
She has become a bit of a dork since she started playing video games. = 自从她开始玩电子游戏以来，她有点傻了。

过去完成时:
He had been a dork for years before he finally got a girlfriend. = 他做了好几年的笨蛋，才终于交到女朋友。
She had never acted like a dork before. = 她以前从未像个傻瓜那样行事。

将来时:
He will always be a dork. = 他将永远是个笨蛋。
She will probably act like a dork again at the next party. = 她很可能在下一次派对上再次表现得像个傻瓜。

虚拟语气:
If he were not such a dork, he would have more friends. = 如果他不那么笨，就会有更多朋友。
I wish I weren't such a dork sometimes. = 有时候我希望自己不要那么傻。

被动语态:
(This word is rarely used passively)  He was considered a dork by his classmates. = 他被他的同学们认为是个笨蛋。
(This word is rarely used passively)  The behavior was seen as dorky. = 这种行为被认为很傻气。

疑问句:
Is he a dork? = 他是笨蛋吗？
Are they dorks? = 他们是笨蛋吗？

否定句:
He is not a dork. = 他不是笨蛋。
They are not dorks. = 他们不是笨蛋。
