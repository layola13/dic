deemphasizing: /ˌdiˈɛmfəsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |  弱化；淡化；不强调 |  The act of giving less importance or prominence to something.  It involves reducing the emphasis or significance placed on a particular aspect, idea, or element. |


例句：

The report deemphasized the company's financial losses. = 报告淡化了公司财务损失。

They were deemphasizing the role of the military in the conflict. = 他们正在淡化军事在冲突中的作用。


近义词：downplaying, underplaying, minimizing, playing down


反义词：emphasizing, highlighting, stressing, accentuating


词性：动词 (现在分词)


USAGE->

The company deemphasizes its past mistakes. = 公司淡化了它过去的错误。

过去完成时:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:  The word "deemphasizing" is formed from the prefix "de-" (meaning "to remove or reverse") + "emphasizing" (the act of giving emphasis).  "Emphasizing" ultimately derives from the Greek word "emphasis," meaning "stress" or "importance."
