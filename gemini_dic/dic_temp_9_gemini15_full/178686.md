recriminatory: /rɪˌkrɪmɪˈnɔːtəri/ | adj. | 互相指责的；反驳的；责备的 | Accusatory; characterized by or given to recrimination.  It suggests a mutual exchange of accusations and blames, often in a heated or angry manner. |

例句：

The meeting descended into a recriminatory shouting match, with each side blaming the other for the failure.  = 会议演变成互相指责的大喊大叫，双方互相指责对方的失败。

Their relationship was marked by constant recriminatory arguments, making it difficult to resolve their issues. = 他们的关系充满了不断的互相指责的争吵，使得解决问题变得困难。


近义词：accusatory, blaming, reproachful, censorious

反义词：exculpatory, forgiving, apologetic


词源:

From Latin *recriminatōrius, from recriminari "to accuse in return," from re- "back, again" + criminari "to accuse," from crimen "charge, accusation."
