correlations:/ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃənz/ | n. | 相关性；相互关系 |  Correlations refer to the statistical relationships between two or more variables.  A correlation indicates the degree to which two or more variables tend to vary together.  A positive correlation means that as one variable increases, the other tends to increase as well. A negative correlation means that as one variable increases, the other tends to decrease.  There can also be no correlation, meaning there is no relationship between the variables. | Example 1:  The study found a strong positive correlation between smoking and lung cancer.  研究发现吸烟和肺癌之间存在很强的正相关关系。| Example 2: There is a negative correlation between exercise and weight gain. 运动和体重增加呈负相关。|近义词: relationships, connections, associations |反义词: independence, disassociations |名词
