/ˌkænɪˈbeɪliən/ | adj. | 食人族的；残暴的 | Relating to or characteristic of cannibals; savagely cruel or violent. |The cannibalean rituals of the tribe were shocking to outsiders.  食人族的仪式让外人感到震惊。 |近义词：anthropophagous, savage, ferocious | 反义词：civilized, humane


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't readily lend itself to the grammatical structures shown in the example.  The usage is limited to describing things as relating to or having the characteristics of cannibalism.  Therefore, tense changes (present, past, future etc) are not applicable in the same way as with verbs.

例句一：The novel depicted a cannibalean society where survival depended on consuming the weak. 这部小说描绘了一个食人族社会，在那里，生存取决于吃掉弱者。
例句二：His cannibalean rage was terrifying to witness. 他的暴怒令人恐惧。

PHRASE->
由于cannibalean本身是一个形容词，它很少与其他词构成固定词组。  可以考虑与名词搭配，例如：cannibalean practices（食人习俗）, cannibalean feast（食人盛宴）, cannibalean tribe（食人部落）, cannibalean history（食人历史）, cannibalean act（食人行为）。
