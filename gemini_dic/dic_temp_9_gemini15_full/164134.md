predisponency: /ˌpriːdɪˈspɒnənsi/ | n. | 预先倾向；素质；癖性 | A predisposition or tendency towards something.  It often refers to a susceptibility to a particular disease or condition.  The word suggests an inherent or inborn inclination rather than a learned one. |  His family history predisposed him to heart disease. 他家族病史使他易患心脏病。


Her predisponency towards anxiety made public speaking difficult.  她易焦虑的性格使得公开演讲对她来说很困难。 | 近义词：predisposition, proclivity, propensity, tendency | 反义词：resistance, immunity, aversion
