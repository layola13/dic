photolithotrophy:/ˌfoʊtoʊlɪθˈɑːtrəfi/| n. |光合自养 |The process by which certain organisms use light energy to convert inorganic carbon sources into organic matter. This process is distinct from photoautotrophy, which uses organic carbon sources.  Photolithotrophy is a type of lithotrophy, meaning it utilizes inorganic electron donors.  |Example: Many bacteria engage in photolithotrophy, using sunlight and inorganic compounds like hydrogen sulfide to produce energy and biomass.  许多细菌进行光合自养，利用阳光和硫化氢等无机化合物来产生能量和生物质。|近义词：photoautotrophy (in some contexts, as both use light), lithotrophy |反义词：heterotrophy, organotrophy
