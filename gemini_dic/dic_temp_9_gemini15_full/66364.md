excuse:/ɪkˈskjuːs/| n., v.|借口，原谅；免除|

英文解析:As a noun, "excuse" refers to a reason or explanation given to justify a fault or mistake. As a verb, it means to forgive someone for something or to release someone from a duty or obligation.

例句:
Noun:  He gave a flimsy excuse for his lateness. 他为他的迟到找了一个牵强的借口。

Noun: I need an excuse to leave early. 我需要一个早退的理由。


Verb: Please excuse my interruption. 请原谅我的打断。

Verb:  I'm sure she'll excuse your behavior. 我相信她会原谅你的行为。


近义词:Noun: reason, justification, pretext; Verb: pardon, forgive, exempt

反义词:Noun: truth, reality; Verb: blame, condemn


词性:Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I excuse your rudeness. 我原谅你的粗鲁。
He excuses his mistakes easily. 他很容易原谅自己的错误。


一般过去时:
She excused herself from the meeting. 她从会议上离开了。
They excused his absence. 他们原谅了他的缺席。


现在完成时:
I have excused myself for the night. 我已经告辞休息了。
He has excused many students from the exam. 他已经免除许多学生的考试。


过去完成时:
She had excused herself before the announcement.  她在公告之前就已经告辞了。
They had excused him from the work due to his illness. 由于他的疾病，他们已经免除他的工作。


将来时:
I will excuse you this time. 我这次会原谅你。
He will excuse her from the chores. 他将免除她的家务。


虚拟语气:
I would excuse him if he apologized. 如果他道歉，我会原谅他。
I wish I had excused myself earlier. 我希望我早点离开就好了。


被动语态:
His absence was excused. 他的缺席得到了原谅。
The students were excused from class. 学生们被允许不上课。


疑问句:
Will you excuse me? 你能原谅我吗？

否定句:
I cannot excuse your behavior. 我不能原谅你的行为。
He wouldn't excuse her lateness. 他不会原谅她的迟到。
