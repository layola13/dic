intrusions:/ɪnˈtruːʒənz/| n. |入侵；侵扰；干扰 | The act of intruding or the state of being intruded upon; an unwelcome intrusion into a person's privacy, affairs, or property.  |例句：The constant intrusions of the media made her life unbearable. 媒体不断的入侵使她的生活难以忍受。

例句：His phone calls were unwelcome intrusions on her quiet evening.  他的电话打扰了她宁静的夜晚。


近义词：invasions, encroachments, infringements, interruptions


反义词：departures, exits, withdrawals


词性：名词


词源:  From the verb "intrude," which comes from the Latin word "intrudere," meaning "to thrust in," "to push in," or "to force in."  This is formed from "in-" (into) + "trudere" (to thrust).  The "-sion" suffix indicates the act or process.
