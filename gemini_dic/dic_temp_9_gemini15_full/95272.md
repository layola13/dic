incapacitate:/ɪnˈkæpəseɪt/| vt.|使无能力；使丧失能力；使无法工作|To deprive of power, ability, or strength; to disable.|The accident incapacitated him for life.  事故使他终身残疾。|The heavy rain incapacitated the transportation system. 大雨使交通系统瘫痪。|disable, cripple, debilitate|enable, empower|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The virus incapacitates many people every year.  病毒每年使许多人丧失能力。
This medication incapacitates the bacteria.  这种药物能使细菌失去活性。


一般过去时:
The injury incapacitated him for several weeks.  这次受伤使他丧失能力好几个星期。
The storm incapacitated the power grid.  暴风雨使电网瘫痪。


现在完成时:
The illness has incapacitated her for months.  这场病让她丧失能力好几个月了。
The accident has incapacitated him from driving.  这场事故使他无法驾驶。


过去完成时:
He had been incapacitated by illness before the competition.  比赛前他就因为生病而丧失了能力。
The company had been incapacitated by the scandal. 这家公司因丑闻而元气大伤。


将来时:
The surgery will temporarily incapacitate him.  手术会暂时使他失去行动能力。
This new policy will incapacitate small businesses.  这项新政策将使小企业失去竞争力。


虚拟语气:
If the drug incapacitated him, the surgery would be unnecessary. 如果药物能使他失去能力，手术就不必要了。

被动语态:

The factory was incapacitated by the fire.  工厂被大火烧毁，无法运转。


疑问句:

否定句:

The regulations won't incapacitate the company.  这些规定不会使公司失去能力。
