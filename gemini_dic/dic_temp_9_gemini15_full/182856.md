repels:/rɪˈpɛlz/| vt.|排斥；驱逐；使厌恶|To drive or force back; to cause aversion or disgust in.|例句：Magnets repel each other. 磁铁互相排斥。|例句：The smell repelled him.  这气味使他作呕。|近义词：rebuff, drive away, disgust |反义词：attract, allure, charm


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The magnet repels the iron filings. = 磁铁排斥铁屑。
This idea repels many people. = 这个想法让很多人反感。

一般过去时:
The strong smell repelled me. = 强烈的味道让我反感。
The force repelled the attacker. = 这股力量击退了袭击者。

现在完成时:
The government's policy has repelled many investors. = 政府的政策已经吓退了许多投资者。
His arrogance has repelled many potential friends. = 他的傲慢已经得罪了许多潜在的朋友。

过去完成时:
The shield had repelled numerous attacks before it finally broke. = 盾牌在最终破裂之前已经抵挡了无数次攻击。
His previous behavior had repelled her from ever trusting him again. = 他之前的行为让她再也不相信他了。

将来时:
This new technology will repel future attacks. = 这项新技术将抵御未来的攻击。
I believe this plan will repel any further criticism. = 我相信这个计划会抵制任何进一步的批评。

虚拟语气:
If only the repellent had repelled the mosquitoes! = 要是驱蚊剂能驱赶蚊子就好了！
If the proposal repelled investors, the project would fail. = 如果提案吓退了投资者，项目就会失败。

被动语态:
The invaders were repelled by the strong defense. = 侵略者被强大的防御击退了。
His arguments were repelled by the opposing counsel. = 他的论点被对方律师驳斥了。

疑问句:
Does this material repel water? = 这种材料防水吗？
Why does he repel people? = 他为什么让人反感？

否定句:
The force did not repel the projectile. = 这股力量没有击退弹丸。
The charm of the city did not repel the tourists. = 城市的魅力并没有让游客反感。
