pauciloquent:/pɔːˈsɪləkwənt/| adj.|寡言的，沉默的，话少的 |  A person who is pauciloquent speaks very little.  It suggests a habitual reticence rather than a temporary silence.  The word emphasizes the scarcity of words spoken. |例句：He was a pauciloquent man, rarely engaging in conversation. 他是个寡言少语的人，很少参与谈话。

例句：The pauciloquent witness provided little information to the police.  寡言的证人向警方提供的证据很少。


近义词： taciturn, reticent, laconic, reserved, uncommunicative

反义词： loquacious, garrulous, voluble, talkative


词源：  From Latin *pauciloquus, from paucus "few" + loquor "I speak".  The suffix "-ent" creates an adjective.

USAGE->
This word is an adjective and does not have verb conjugations or tense changes like "What".  It describes a person or their speech.  Therefore, tense information is not applicable.
