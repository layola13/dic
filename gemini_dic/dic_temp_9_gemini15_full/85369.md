harrumph:/ˈhɑːrʌmp/| int.|哼，一声不满的哼声|An interjection expressing disapproval, dissatisfaction, or disdain; a sound of disdainful or contemptuous rejection.  It often implies a feeling of self-importance or superiority.|例句：He harrumphed and turned away, refusing to answer. 他哼了一声，转过身去，拒绝回答。  She harrumphed at the suggestion, clearly unimpressed. 她对这个建议哼了一声，显然很不以为然。|近义词：humph, snort, grunt|反义词：approval, agreement
