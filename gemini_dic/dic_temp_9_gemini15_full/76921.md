gaol:/dʒeɪl/ | n. |监狱 | A gaol is a prison.  The term is now largely archaic or regional, more common in British English. | He was sent to gaol for theft. 他因盗窃被送进监狱。| prison, jail | freedom, liberty | 名词


USAGE->
This word is a noun, so it doesn't conjugate like verbs.  The following examples show how "gaol" is used in different sentence structures:

Simple Present Tense:
The gaol is overcrowded. = 监狱人满为患。
That gaol holds dangerous criminals. = 那座监狱关押着危险的罪犯。


Simple Past Tense:
The prisoner escaped from the gaol. = 囚犯从监狱逃跑了。
He spent five years in that gaol. = 他在那座监狱里度过了五年。


Present Perfect Tense:
They have been released from gaol. = 他们已经被释放出狱了。
He has never been to gaol before. = 他以前从未进过监狱。


Past Perfect Tense:
The judge had already sentenced him to gaol. = 法官已经判处他入狱。
She had been in gaol for months. = 她已经在监狱里待了几个月了。


Future Tense:
He will go to gaol if he's found guilty. = 如果他被判有罪，他将入狱。
They will likely remain in gaol for many years. = 他们很可能在监狱里待很多年。


Passive Voice:
The gaol was built in the 19th century. = 这座监狱建于19世纪。
The prisoners are being held in the gaol. = 囚犯们被关押在监狱里。
