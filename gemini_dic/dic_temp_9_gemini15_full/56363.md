doing-up: /ˈduːɪŋ ʌp/ | v. | 修缮；打扮；扣上；整理；(非正式) 欺骗 |

英文解析:  The phrasal verb "doing up" has several meanings depending on the context. It can refer to renovating or repairing a building, improving one's appearance, fastening something (like buttons or a zipper), or tidying something.  In informal contexts, it can also mean cheating or deceiving someone.

例句:

They're doing up the old house. = 他们在翻修那栋旧房子。

She spent hours doing herself up for the party. = 她花了几个小时打扮自己去参加派对。


近义词: renovate, refurbish, repair,  dress up, tidy, fasten, cheat (informal)

反义词: neglecting, destroying, letting deteriorate, undressing, messing, unfastening
