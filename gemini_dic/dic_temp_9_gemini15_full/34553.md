clear-off:/klɪər ˈɒf/| phrasal verb| 走开；离开；清除；消失 | To leave quickly; to remove something completely; to disappear. |例句：The police told the protestors to clear off.  警察让抗议者离开。|例句：We need to clear off all the rubbish before the party. 我们需要在派对前清除所有垃圾。|近义词：leave, depart, remove, vanish |反义词：arrive, appear, stay, remain

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They clear off every evening. = 他们每天晚上都离开。
He clears off his desk before leaving. = 他离开前会收拾桌子。

一般过去时:
The crowd cleared off after the concert. = 音乐会结束后，人群散去了。
She cleared off the dishes after dinner. = 晚饭后她收拾了碗碟。

现在完成时:
They have cleared off all the snow. = 他们已经清除了所有的雪。
He has cleared off all his debts. = 他已经还清了他所有的债务。

过去完成时:
They had cleared off before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前他们就离开了。
She had cleared off the table before her guests arrived. = 客人到达之前她已经收拾好桌子了。

将来时:
We will clear off as soon as the meeting is over. = 会议一结束我们就离开。
They will clear off the debris after the accident. = 事故发生后他们会清理残骸。

虚拟语气:
If it rained, we would clear off. = 如果下雨，我们就离开。
I wish I had cleared off earlier. = 我真希望我早点离开。

被动语态:
The mess was cleared off by the cleaners. = 混乱被清洁工清理干净了。
The snow will be cleared off by the morning. = 雪会在早上被清扫干净。

疑问句:
Will they clear off soon? = 他们很快就会离开吗？
Have they cleared off the rubbish yet? = 他们已经清理垃圾了吗？

否定句:
They didn't clear off until late. = 直到很晚他们才离开。
He didn't clear off the spilled milk. = 他没有擦掉洒出的牛奶。
