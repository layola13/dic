infectiousness: /ɪnˈfɛkʃəsnəs/ | n. | 传染性，感染性 | The ability of a disease or pathogen to spread from one individual to another.  It refers to the ease with which an infectious agent can be transmitted from an infected host to a susceptible host.  This involves factors like the mode of transmission (e.g., airborne, contact, vector-borne), the infectious dose, and the duration of shedding.  High infectiousness means a disease spreads rapidly, while low infectiousness means it spreads slowly. | 例句：The high infectiousness of the virus led to a rapid spread of the disease.  高度的病毒传染性导致疾病迅速传播。

The infectiousness of measles is significantly higher than that of chickenpox. 麻疹的传染性远高于水痘。


近义词：contagiousness, transmissibility | 反义词：resistance to infection, immunity
