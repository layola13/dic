downfalls:/ˈdaʊnfɔːlz/ | n. | 缺点；衰落；垮台 | The inherent weaknesses or defects of something; a sudden loss of power or status. | 例句：One of the downfalls of the project was its unrealistic budget.  = 该项目的一个缺点是其预算不切实际。

The company's downfall was caused by poor management. =  公司的垮台是由管理不善造成的。| 近义词：shortcomings, flaws, failures, setbacks | 反义词：strengths, advantages, successes
