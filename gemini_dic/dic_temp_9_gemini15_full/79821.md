glycosaminoglycan:/ˌɡlaɪkoʊˌsæmɪnəˈɡlɪkən/| n. |糖胺聚糖|A long, unbranched polysaccharide consisting of repeating disaccharide units.  These disaccharides typically contain an amino sugar (usually glucosamine or galactosamine) and a uronic acid (such as glucuronic acid or iduronic acid). Glycosaminoglycans are major components of the extracellular matrix and play crucial roles in various biological processes, including cell adhesion, tissue hydration, and signal transduction.|例句：The cartilage is mainly composed of collagen and glycosaminoglycans.  软骨主要由胶原蛋白和糖胺聚糖组成。

例句：Defects in glycosaminoglycan metabolism can lead to various connective tissue disorders. 糖胺聚糖代谢缺陷会导致各种结缔组织疾病。

反义词：None (no direct antonym)
