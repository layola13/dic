injunction:/ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən/ | n. | 法令；禁令；强制令 | A court order requiring a person to do or cease doing a specific action.  | The court issued an injunction to prevent the demolition of the historic building. 法院发布禁令以阻止拆除历史建筑。 | prohibition, mandate, order | permission, allowance | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The judge issues an injunction against the company. = 法官对该公司发布禁令。
An injunction prevents further construction. = 禁令阻止进一步施工。


一般过去时:
The court issued an injunction last week. = 法院上周发布了一项禁令。
They obtained an injunction to stop the pollution. = 他们获得了禁令来阻止污染。


现在完成时:
The company has received an injunction. = 该公司已收到禁令。
The protestors have been served with an injunction. = 抗议者已收到禁令。


过去完成时:
The work had stopped after the injunction was issued. = 在发布禁令后，工作已经停止了。
They had already violated the injunction before it was served. = 在禁令送达之前，他们就已经违反了禁令。


将来时:
The judge will issue an injunction tomorrow. = 法官明天将发布禁令。
They will appeal the injunction next week. = 他们下周将对禁令提出上诉。


虚拟语气:
If the court had issued an injunction sooner, the damage would have been less. = 如果法院早些发布禁令，损失就会减少。
I wish they had obtained an injunction earlier. = 我希望他们早点获得禁令。


被动语态:
The injunction was issued to protect the environment. = 发布禁令是为了保护环境。
The injunction is being challenged in court. = 该禁令正在法庭上受到质疑。


疑问句:
Has the injunction been served yet? = 禁令已经送达了吗？
What type of injunction was issued? = 发布的是什么类型的禁令？


否定句:
The court did not issue an injunction. = 法院没有发布禁令。
There was no injunction to prevent the action. = 没有禁令可以阻止该行为。


PHRASE->
a temporary injunction  临时禁令
a permanent injunction 永久禁令
a preliminary injunction 初步禁令
to seek an injunction  寻求禁令
to violate an injunction 违反禁令
