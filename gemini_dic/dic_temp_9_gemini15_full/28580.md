careering:/kəˈrɪərɪŋ/| adj.| 猛冲的；飞驰的；狂奔的 | characterized by haste and lack of control; rushing headlong.  |例句：The car came careering around the corner.  这辆车飞速地转过弯来。

例句：He was careering down the mountainside on his bike. 他骑着自行车飞速地冲下山坡。


近义词：rushing, speeding, dashing, hurtling |反义词：slow, deliberate, cautious


词源:  The word "careering" comes from the noun "career," which ultimately derives from the Old French word "carriere," meaning "racecourse" or "road."  The sense of rapid, uncontrolled movement developed from the imagery of a horse racing wildly along a track.

被动语态:  The word "careering" is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes an active, uncontrolled movement.  A possible, albeit awkward, construction might be:  The car was careering down the street (by the wind). = 汽车被风吹着飞驰在街上（由风造成）。
