reregulates: /ˌriːˈreɡjuleɪts/ | vt. |重新规范；再次调节| To regulate again or anew; to adjust or control something again.| The government reregulates the market to prevent monopolies. 政府重新规范市场以防止垄断。|近义词： readjust, re-adjust, regulate anew, control again | 反义词：deregulate, disorganize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company reregulates its pricing strategy every year. = 公司每年都会重新调整其定价策略。
He reregulates his sleep schedule after a long trip. = 长途旅行后，他重新调整了自己的睡眠时间表。


一般过去时:
The council reregulated the traffic flow after the accident. = 事故发生后，委员会重新规范了交通流量。
She reregulated the thermostat to a more comfortable temperature. = 她把恒温器重新调节到更舒适的温度。


现在完成时:
The authorities have reregulated the fishing quotas to protect the fish population. = 当局已经重新规范了捕捞配额以保护鱼类种群。
They have reregulated the water supply system to improve efficiency. = 他们已经重新规范了供水系统以提高效率。


过去完成时:
The company had reregulated its production process before the inspection. = 公司在检查之前已经重新规范了其生产流程。
They had reregulated the chemical discharge before the environmental agency arrived. = 在环保机构到达之前，他们已经重新规范了化学物质的排放。


将来时:
The government will reregulate the banking industry next year. = 政府明年将重新规范银行业。
The school will reregulate its disciplinary policy next semester. = 学校下学期将重新规范其纪律政策。


虚拟语气:
If the government reregulated the industry, the market would be more stable. = 如果政府重新规范该行业，市场将会更加稳定。
I wish they had reregulated the noise levels earlier. = 我希望他们早点重新规范噪音水平。


被动语态:

The water supply system is being reregulated to ensure its safety. = 供水系统正在被重新规范以确保其安全。


疑问句:
