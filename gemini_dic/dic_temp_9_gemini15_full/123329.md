monographs:/ˈmɒnəɡrɑːfs/| n. |专著，论文集 |A monograph is a scholarly work of writing on a single subject or a specific aspect of a subject, typically longer and more detailed than an article or essay. It often presents original research or a comprehensive review of existing literature.  |Example:  He wrote a monograph on the history of the Roman Empire. 他写了一部关于罗马帝国历史的专著。

The professor published a monograph detailing her research on ancient civilizations. 教授发表了一篇专著，详细介绍了她对古代文明的研究。|近义词：treatise, dissertation, study |反义词：article (in the sense of being shorter and less comprehensive)
