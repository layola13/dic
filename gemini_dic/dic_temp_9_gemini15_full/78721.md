gigged:/ɡɪɡd/ | v. |演出；被临时雇佣 |  To have performed a gig (a short-term job or performance, especially a musical one).  This word is the past participle of "gig."|例句：The band gigged at a small club last night. 乐队昨晚在一个小俱乐部演出。

例句：He got gigged to paint a mural for a local business. 他被临时雇佣为当地一家企业绘制壁画。


近义词：performed, played, worked, hired


反义词：N/A (The antonym would depend on the context;  e.g., if referring to a performance, "failed" might be an antonym; if referring to employment, "unemployed" might be an antonym.)


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gigs regularly at the local pub. 他经常在当地酒吧演出。
She gigs as a freelance photographer. 她以自由摄影师的身份做演出。

一般过去时:
They gigged in several cities on their tour. 他们在巡演中在几个城市演出。
He gigged for a small company last week. 他上周为一家小公司工作。

现在完成时:
The band has gigged all over Europe. 乐队已经在欧洲各地演出过了。
She has gigged at many prestigious events. 她已经在许多重要的活动上演出过了。

过去完成时:
They had gigged extensively before releasing their first album. 他们在发行第一张专辑之前已经广泛地演出过了。
He had gigged for that company before, so he knew the people there. 他以前曾在那家公司工作过，所以认识那里的人。

将来时:
The band will gig in London next month. 乐队下个月将在伦敦演出。
She will gig at the festival this summer. 她今年夏天将在音乐节上演出。

虚拟语气:
If they had gigged more often, they would have gained more experience. 如果他们更经常演出，他们就会获得更多经验。
I wish I had gigged more when I was younger. 我真希望我年轻的时候多演出一些。

被动语态:
They were gigged for a corporate event. 他们被雇佣参加公司活动。
He was gigged to write a song for the movie. 他被雇佣为电影创作歌曲。

疑问句:
Did they gig last night? 他们昨晚演出了吗？
Has she ever gigged in America? 她曾在美国演出过吗？

否定句:
The band didn't gig last weekend. 乐队上周末没有演出。
They haven't gigged in a while. 他们有一段时间没演出了。
