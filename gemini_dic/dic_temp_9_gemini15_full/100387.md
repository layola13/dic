interstructures:/ˈɪntərˌstrʌktʃərz/ | n. | 互相作用的结构；相互关联的结构 |  The term "interstructures" refers to the interconnected and interdependent structures within a system or organization. It emphasizes the relationships and interactions between different components rather than focusing solely on individual elements.  The concept is often used in fields like sociology, architecture, and systems theory to describe complex systems where elements influence each other significantly. | Example: The study explored the interstructures of power, knowledge, and discourse in shaping social identities.  这项研究探讨了权力、知识和话语在塑造社会身份方面的相互作用结构。 |近义词:networks, systems, frameworks |反义词:isolated structures, independent components | 名词
