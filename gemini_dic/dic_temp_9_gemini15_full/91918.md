hypermanic: /ˌhaɪpərˈmeɪniæk/ | adj. | 极度躁狂的；狂躁的 | Extremely manic; characterized by an abnormally elevated mood, increased energy, and racing thoughts.  This term describes a state beyond typical mania, indicating a severe and potentially dangerous level of mood elevation. | 例句：His hypermanic episode required immediate hospitalization.  他的极度躁狂发作需要立即住院治疗。

His behavior was so erratic; it suggested a hypermanic state. 他的行为如此古怪，暗示他处于极度躁狂状态。


近义词：extremely manic, intensely manic, wildly euphoric, deliriously happy | 反义词：depressed, lethargic, apathetic


词源:  The word "hypermanic" is a combination of "hyper-" (meaning "excessive" or "above normal") and "manic" (referring to mania, a state of abnormally elevated mood).  "Manic" itself originates from the Greek word "mania," meaning "madness" or "frenzy."
