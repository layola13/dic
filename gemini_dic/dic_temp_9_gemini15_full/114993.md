mandamus:/mænˈdeɪməs/| n. |命令令状；强制令 | A writ issued by a court ordering a person or body to perform a specified act.  It is a discretionary remedy, and the court will only issue it if it is satisfied that the applicant has no other adequate remedy available.  | 例句：The court issued a writ of mandamus to compel the government to release the documents. = 法院发布命令令状强制政府公开文件。


The court ordered a mandamus to the city council to hold new elections. = 法院命令市议会举行新的选举。(命令令状)


近义词：injunction, writ, order | 反义词：prohibition, restraint | 词性：名词


词源:  From Medieval Latin *mandamus (first person plural present active indicative of *mandare "to command, entrust"), from Latin *mandāre (to entrust, order, command), from manus ("hand") + dare ("to give").  The legal term reflects the court's command to a lower court or official.
