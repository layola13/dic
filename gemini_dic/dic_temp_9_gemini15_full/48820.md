defocalization: /ˌdiːˈfoʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |散焦，焦外模糊| The blurring of an image due to a shift in focus.  It is a photographic and artistic technique used to create a shallow depth of field, drawing attention to the subject in sharp focus while softening the background. | Example: The photographer achieved a beautiful defocalization effect, making the portrait's subject stand out against a dreamy, out-of-focus background. | 近义词：blurring, softening, out-of-focus, bokeh | 反义词：sharpening, focusing, in-focus | 名词
