peripteral: /ˌpɛrɪˈptɛrəl/ | adj. | 周廊式的；环柱式的 |  Relating to or denoting a building or part of a building that has a colonnade around its exterior.  The term often refers to ancient Greek and Roman temples. |  The Parthenon is a famous example of a peripteral temple.  周殿神庙就是一个著名的周廊式神庙的例子。 The new museum boasts a peripteral design, allowing for ample natural light. 新博物馆采用环柱式设计，让充足的自然光线进入。|近义词：columned, colonnaded | 反义词：uncolumned, enclosed

词源:
From Greek περίπετρος (perípētros), from περί (perí) "around" + πετρος (pétrŏs) "stone".  Originally referring to a building surrounded by a stone wall, the term evolved to describe structures with surrounding colonnades.
