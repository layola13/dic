notwithstanding:/ˌnɒtwɪðˈstændɪŋ/ | adv. conj.|尽管；虽然；即使 | Despite; in spite of; although.  The word functions as both an adverb and a conjunction, indicating that something is true despite the existence of another fact. |例句：Notwithstanding the evidence, the jury found him not guilty. 尽管有证据，陪审团还是判决他不罪。


例句：Notwithstanding his age, he remains active. 尽管年纪大了，他仍然很活跃。


近义词：despite, in spite of, although, even though, regardless of


反义词：because of, due to, owing to


词性：adverb, conjunction

USAGE->

词源:
The word "notwithstanding" is a compound word formed from "not" and "withstanding." "Withstand" comes from the Old English "wiþstandan," composed of "wiþ" (against) and "standan" (to stand).  Therefore, "notwithstanding" literally means "not standing against" or "in spite of."
