clam-up:/klæm ʌp/| v.| 闭嘴；变得沉默寡言； |to become suddenly unwilling to talk, usually because one is embarrassed or upset|例句：He clammed up when I asked him about his past.  当我问起他的过去时，他闭口不言了。|例句：She clammed up after the accident, refusing to discuss it with anyone. 事故发生后，她变得沉默寡言，拒绝与任何人讨论此事。|近义词：shut up, become silent, fall silent|反义词：open up, speak freely


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He clams up whenever he's nervous. = 他一紧张就闭口不言。
They clam up when the boss is around. = 老板在的时候他们就闭嘴不说话。

一般过去时:
She clammed up when I mentioned her ex-boyfriend. = 当我提到她前男友时，她闭口不言了。
He clammed up during the interrogation. = 在审讯期间，他闭口不言。

现在完成时:
He has clammed up since the incident. = 自从那件事发生后，他就一直沉默寡言。
She has clammed up about her plans for the future. = 她对未来的计划一直守口如瓶。

过去完成时:
He had clammed up before I could ask him anything. = 我还没来得及问他什么，他就已经闭口不言了。
They had clammed up about the scandal for weeks. = 他们对那个丑闻已经沉默了几周了。

将来时:
He will clam up if you press him too hard. = 如果你逼得太紧，他会闭口不言的。
She will probably clam up if you ask her about her personal life. = 如果你问她私生活，她可能会闭口不言。

虚拟语气:
If he weren't so embarrassed, he wouldn't clam up. = 如果他不那么尴尬，他就不会闭口不言了。
I wish she wouldn't clam up whenever I try to talk to her. = 我希望她不要每次我试图和她说话时都闭口不言。

被动语态: (This verb is rarely used in the passive voice)
The witness was clammed up by fear. = 证人被恐惧吓住了，不敢说话。(This is less common and sounds unnatural)
His mouth was clammed shut by fear.  = 他被恐惧吓得嘴巴闭得紧紧的。(More natural alternative)


疑问句:
Does he always clam up when you ask personal questions? = 你问他私人问题的时候，他总是闭口不言吗？
Why did she clam up during the interview? = 为什么她在面试中闭口不言？

否定句:
He didn't clam up; he simply had nothing to say. = 他不是闭口不言，他只是没什么好说的。
She didn't clam up; she explained everything clearly. = 她并没有闭口不言，她把一切都解释得很清楚。


PHRASE->
clam up completely = 完全闭嘴
clam up tight = 紧紧闭嘴
clam up and say nothing = 闭嘴不说话
clam up like an oyster = 像牡蛎一样紧闭
clam up in fear = 害怕地闭嘴
