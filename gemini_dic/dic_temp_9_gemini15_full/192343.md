scrimmages:/ˈskrɪmɪdʒɪz/| n.|混战；练习赛；短暂的争斗 | A scrimmage is a practice game or a short, disorganized fight or struggle.  It often involves a lot of pushing and shoving, but it's usually not very violent. |例句：The football team engaged in a series of intense scrimmages before the season started.  橄榄球队在赛季开始前进行了一系列激烈的练习赛。


The two teams had a brief scrimmage before the main game. 两队在大赛前进行了一场简短的练习赛。


近义词：scuffle, tussle, melee, brawl |反义词：orderly match, planned encounter |词性:名词


Etymology:  The word "scrimmage" ultimately derives from the Old French word "escrime," meaning "fencing" or "swordsmanship."  Over time, the meaning shifted to refer to a less formal and more chaotic type of fighting or contest, eventually settling on its current usage.
