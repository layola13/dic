eugenics:/juːˈdʒɛnɪks/| n. |优生学|The study of how to improve the qualities of the human population, especially by such means as discouraging people with undesirable traits from reproducing and encouraging those with desirable traits to reproduce.|例句：The eugenics movement of the early 20th century led to forced sterilizations and other discriminatory practices.|近义词：selective breeding, racial hygiene|反义词：dysgenics
