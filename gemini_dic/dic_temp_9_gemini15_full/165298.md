preinterpretations:/ˌpriːɪntɜːrprɪˈteɪʃənz/| n. |预先解释；事先解读|Interpretations made before something is fully understood or experienced.  These interpretations often involve speculation or assumptions based on limited information.  Preinterpretations can be helpful in forming hypotheses or initial understandings, but they should be revised as more information becomes available. |The preinterpretations of the ancient text were largely speculative, based on limited knowledge of the language and context.  古代文本的预先解释很大程度上是推测性的，基于对语言和语境的有限了解。|近义词：preconceptions, anticipations, preliminary interpretations|反义词：postinterpretations, reinterpretations
