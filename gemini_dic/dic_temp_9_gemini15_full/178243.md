recompenses:/ˈrɛkəmpɛnsɪz/ | n. | 报酬，补偿 |  Something given or received as payment or reparation, especially for loss or injury.  It often implies a sense of justice or fairness in the exchange. |例句：The company offered generous recompenses to its employees for their hard work. 公司为员工的辛勤工作提供了丰厚的报酬。

例句：He received a substantial recompense for the damage to his property. 他因财产损失收到了巨额赔偿。


近义词：reward, compensation, amends, restitution | 反义词：penalty, punishment | 词性:noun
