shall:/ʃæl/| modal v.|将要；应该；必须|Shall is a modal verb used primarily in British English. It expresses future tense, obligation, suggestion, or determination.  It's less common in American English, where "will" is generally preferred.|例句：Shall we go for a walk? 我们去散步好吗？|例句：Shall I help you with that? 我帮你做那个好吗？|近义词：will, should, must|反义词：need not, must not


USAGE->
一般将来时:
Shall we go to the cinema tonight? = 我们今晚去看电影好吗？

Shall I open the window for you? = 我帮你打开窗户好吗？


疑问句(表示建议或请求):
Shall we have dinner together? = 我们一起吃晚饭好吗？

Shall I help you with your homework? = 我帮你做作业好吗？


正式场合的将来时:
The meeting shall commence at 2 p.m. = 会议将于下午两点开始。

The rules shall be strictly enforced. = 规定必须严格执行。


命令或规定(较为正式):
You shall obey the law. = 你必须遵守法律。

Visitors shall not enter without permission. = 未经许可，访客不得入内。


虚拟语气(罕见):
If I should have known, I would have told you. = 如果我知道的话，我会告诉你的。(书面语，较少使用shall在此情境)

Should you require assistance, please contact us. = 如需帮助，请联系我们。(should更常用)


PHRASE->
Shall we? = 好吗？/行吗？
What shall we do? = 我们该怎么办？
As it shall be. = 就这样吧。
It shall be done. = 这事我会办妥。
So be it. (类似shall的用法, 表示顺从或接受)= 就这样吧。
