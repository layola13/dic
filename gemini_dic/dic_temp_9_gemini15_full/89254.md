hominids:/ˈhɒmɪnɪdz/ /ˈhɑːmənɪdz/| n. | 人科动物；人类及其祖先 |  Members of the family Hominidae, including all modern and extinct great apes (orangutans, gorillas, chimpanzees, bonobos, and humans).  Hominids are characterized by their relatively large brain size, bipedal locomotion (in most species), and complex social behavior. |  Fossil evidence suggests that hominids originated in Africa millions of years ago.  化石证据表明，人科动物起源于数百万年前的非洲。

The discovery of new hominid fossils is constantly reshaping our understanding of human evolution.  新的类人猿化石的发现不断重塑着我们对人类进化的理解。|近义词：great apes, primates |反义词：None (no direct antonyms, but could be contrasted with other primate families)
