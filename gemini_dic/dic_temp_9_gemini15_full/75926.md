fungi:/ˈfʌŋɡaɪ/| n.|真菌，菌类|A fungus or fungi are any of a large group of spore-producing organisms feeding on organic matter, including molds, yeast, mushrooms, and toadstools. They lack chlorophyll and are classified in the kingdom Fungi.|例句：Mushrooms are a type of fungi.  蘑菇是一种真菌。|例句：Many fungi are beneficial to the environment. 许多真菌对环境有益。|近义词：mycetes, molds, yeasts|反义词:plants (in terms of chlorophyll and photosynthesis)

词源:
The word "fungi" is the plural of the Latin word "fungus," meaning "mushroom."  The term ultimately derives from ancient Greek words related to the concept of "sponge," likely due to the porous nature of many fungi.

用法说明:
fungi作为名词，通常用作复数形式。单数形式为fungus。
