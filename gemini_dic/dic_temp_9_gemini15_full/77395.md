/ɡɔːtəməˈbʊdə/ | n. | 乔达摩·佛陀 | Gautama Buddha refers to Siddhartha Gautama, the historical founder of Buddhism.  He is revered as the Buddha, meaning "the awakened one," after achieving enlightenment.  His teachings, which emphasize compassion, mindfulness, and the path to liberation from suffering, form the basis of Buddhist philosophy and practice. | The teachings of Gautama Buddha have influenced billions of people across the globe. |  Siddhartha Gautama, the historical Buddha |  No direct antonyms; concepts like atheism or nihilism offer contrasting worldviews. | n.
