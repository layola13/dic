nonturbulent: /ˌnɑːnˈtɜːrbjələnt/ | adj. | 平静的；无湍流的；稳定的 | Not turbulent; smooth; steady.  The term describes a flow of fluid (like air or water) that is smooth and predictable, lacking the chaotic eddies and swirls characteristic of turbulence.  It can also be used more broadly to describe a situation or environment that is calm and stable. | 例句：The flight was nonturbulent, allowing passengers to relax and enjoy the scenery.  = 飞行很平稳，乘客可以放松身心，欣赏风景。

The atmosphere was nonturbulent, a welcome change after the stormy weather. =  暴风雨天气过后，平静的大气环境令人欣慰。| 近义词：calm, smooth, tranquil, steady, stable | 反义词：turbulent, chaotic, unsteady, erratic, stormy

词源:
The word is formed by combining the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") with the adjective "turbulent."  "Turbulent" originates from the Latin word "turbulentus," meaning "disturbed," "tumultuous," or "violent."  Therefore, "nonturbulent" literally means "not disturbed" or "not turbulent."
