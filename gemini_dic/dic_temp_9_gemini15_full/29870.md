causeway:/ˈkɔːzweɪ/| n. |堤道；高架路；路基|A raised road or path, especially one across marshy ground or water.|例句：The causeway was flooded after the heavy rain.  暴雨过后，堤道被淹了。|例句：They built a causeway to connect the island to the mainland.他们修建了一条堤道连接岛屿和大陆。|近义词：road, path, embankment, viaduct |反义词：None specifically, but could be considered the opposite of a low-lying, flooded area.

词源:
The word "causeway" comes from the Old French "chaussée," meaning "paved road" or "highway."  The "chausser"  in Old French means "to shoe" (as in, to provide with shoes or covering), reflecting the idea of a paved or improved road, hence a raised and strengthened path.
