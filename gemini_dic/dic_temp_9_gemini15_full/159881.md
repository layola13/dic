polymetre:/ˈpɒlimiːtə(r)/|/ˈpɑːlimiːtər/| n. |复调，多节奏|The coexistence of two or more metrical systems in a single musical composition.  It is a compositional technique where different sections or layers of music operate with different meters simultaneously or in alternation.  This creates a complex and often unpredictable rhythmic texture.|Example:  The composer employed polymetre to achieve a dissonant and captivating effect in the symphony's finale.  =  作曲家在交响乐的终曲中运用复调以达到不和谐而迷人的效果。|近义词：heterometry|反义词：monometry|名词
