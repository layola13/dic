immortalize: /ɪˈmɔːrtəlaɪz/ | vt. |使不朽；使永垂不朽 | To make someone or something famous or remembered forever.  |例句：Shakespeare immortalized many characters and phrases in his plays.  莎士比亚在他的戏剧中使许多人物和短语永垂不朽。

例句：Her bravery immortalized her name in history. 她的勇敢使她的名字永载史册。


近义词：memorialize, perpetuate, eternize | 反义词：obliterate, destroy, forget


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He immortalizes his heroes in his novels. 他在他的小说中使他的英雄们永垂不朽。
The artist immortalizes the beauty of nature in his paintings.  这位艺术家在他的画作中使大自然的美丽永垂不朽。

一般过去时:

现在完成时:
She has immortalized herself through her charitable work. 她通过慈善工作使自己名垂青史。

过去完成时:
The ancient Greeks had immortalized their gods in their myths and legends. 古希腊人已经在他们的神话和传说中使他们的神明永垂不朽。
Before her death, she had immortalized her family's history in a detailed memoir. 在她去世之前，她已经在一本详细的回忆录中使她家族的历史永垂不朽。

将来时:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
immortal (adj.) + -ize (suffix)

immortal 源自拉丁语 *inmortalis,  in- "not" + mortalis "mortal, subject to death," from mors (genitive mortis) "death."  "-ize" 是一个动词后缀，表示“使成为……；使……化”。
