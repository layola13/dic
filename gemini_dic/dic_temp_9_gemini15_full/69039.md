fatwa:/ˈfæt.wɑː/|/ˈfæt.wə/| n. |法特瓦（伊斯兰教法令）|A fatwa is a non-binding Islamic legal opinion.  It is issued by a qualified scholar of Islamic law (a mufti) in response to a question concerning Islamic law.  While not legally binding in the same way as a court ruling, fatwas can carry significant religious and social weight, and can influence the behavior of Muslims.  They can cover a wide range of topics, from personal conduct to matters of public policy.|The mufti issued a fatwa forbidding the consumption of alcohol.  穆夫提发布了一道法令，禁止饮酒。|religious edict, Islamic ruling|court order, secular law
