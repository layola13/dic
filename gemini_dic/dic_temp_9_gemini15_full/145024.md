/ˌoʊvərəˈfɜːrm/ | vt. |过度肯定，过分确信 | To overaffirm means to state something with excessive or unwarranted confidence; to assert something too strongly or repeatedly.  It implies a lack of nuance or consideration of alternative possibilities. |  He overaffirmed his position, failing to acknowledge any potential weaknesses in his argument. 他过分肯定自己的立场，没有承认论点中任何潜在的弱点。  She overaffirmed her ability to handle the project, leading to unexpected difficulties. 她过分肯定自己能处理好这个项目，导致了意想不到的困难。 | reaffirm, assert, confirm | doubt, question, deny | 动词
