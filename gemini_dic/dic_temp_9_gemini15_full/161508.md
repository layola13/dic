potentiometrically:/ˌpoʊtɛnʃiəˈmɛtrɪkli/| adv.| 电位法地；电势测定地 |  Describes a process or measurement done using potentiometry, a method of electrochemical measurement that determines the potential difference (voltage) between two electrodes in an electrochemical cell. This voltage is then related to the concentration or activity of a specific ion or substance. | The pH of the solution was determined potentiometrically using a glass electrode.  溶液的pH值是用玻璃电极电位法测定的。 |  electrochemically, voltammetrically |  (No direct antonym, as it describes a method, not a quality.) | 副词

USAGE->
This word is an adverb and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It describes how an action is performed.  There are no tenses to consider for this word.

EXAMPLE SENTENCES->
The concentration of ions was measured potentiometrically. 离子的浓度是用电位法测量的。
The experiment was conducted potentiometrically to avoid interference from other factors. 实验是用电位法进行的，以避免其他因素的干扰。
