outrode:/ˌaʊtˈroʊd/| v.|超过，胜过；骑马超过|To surpass or exceed, especially in riding a horse.  It implies exceeding someone's speed or endurance on horseback.|例句：He outrode all the other competitors in the race. 他在比赛中超过了所有其他选手。

例句：The experienced rider easily outrode the novice.  经验丰富的骑手轻松地超过了新手。


近义词：outspeed, outstrip, surpass, exceed|反义词：be outridden by, be surpassed by|词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He outrides everyone in the competition. = 他在比赛中超过了所有人。
She outrides her brother in the daily rides. = 她在日常骑行中超过了她的兄弟。


一般过去时:
He outrode all his opponents in the race. = 他在比赛中超过了所有对手。
They outrode the storm by seeking shelter. = 他们通过寻求庇护而躲过了风暴。(Note: this usage is less common and uses "outride" figuratively)


现在完成时:
She has outridden many challenges in her career. = 她职业生涯中克服了许多挑战。(Note: this usage is figurative)
He has outridden his competitors in the marathon. = 他在马拉松比赛中超过了竞争对手。(Note: less common, often uses "outspeed" instead)


过去完成时:
He had outridden the pursuing soldiers before reaching the forest. = 他到达森林前已经甩掉了追兵。(Note: less common in this tense)
She had outridden her fatigue before completing the journey.  = 完成旅程前，她已经克服了疲劳。(Figurative use)


将来时:
He will outride the storm. = 他将会度过难关。(Figurative use)
They will outride their rivals in the next race. = 他们在下一场比赛中将超过他们的对手。


虚拟语气:
If he had trained harder, he would have outridden his opponent. = 如果他训练更努力，他就会超过对手。
If she were faster, she would outride the others. = 如果她速度更快，她就能超过其他人。


被动语态:
He was outridden by a younger, faster competitor. = 他被一个更年轻、速度更快的竞争对手超过了。
The storm was outridden by the experienced sailors. = 经验丰富的海员们顶住了风暴。(Figurative use)


疑问句:
Can he outride the current champion? = 他能超过目前的冠军吗？
Did she outride her fear? = 她克服恐惧了吗？(Figurative use)


否定句:
He didn't outride the other racers. = 他没有超过其他赛车手。
She couldn't outride the strong headwinds. = 她无法克服强烈的逆风。(Figurative use)


PHRASE->
outride a storm = 渡过难关
outride a difficulty = 克服困难
outride a crisis = 渡过危机
outride one's fear = 克服恐惧
outride the competition = 超过竞争对手


词源:
The word "outrode" is the past tense of the verb "outride," formed by combining the prefix "out-" (meaning "to surpass" or "to exceed") with the verb "ride."  The root "ride" has Old English origins.
