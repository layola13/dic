coax:/koʊks/| vt.|劝诱；哄骗；诱导|To persuade or gently urge (someone) to do something.|例句：He coaxed the shy child to sing a song. 他哄着害羞的孩子唱了一首歌。|近义词：persuade, urge, tempt |反义词：force, compel


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I coax my dog to sit. = 我哄我的狗坐下。
She coaxes her son to eat his vegetables. = 她哄她的儿子吃蔬菜。


一般过去时:
He coaxed the information out of her. = 他从她口中套出了信息。
They coaxed the fire to life with kindling. = 他们用引火物哄着火燃起来。


现在完成时:
I have coaxed the plant to grow. = 我已经哄着这株植物生长了。
She has coaxed a confession from the suspect. = 她已经从嫌疑人那里套取了供词。


过去完成时:
He had coaxed the engine to start before the race. = 他在比赛前已经哄着发动机启动了。
They had coaxed the reluctant witness to testify. = 他们已经哄着那个不愿作证的证人作证了。


将来时:
I will coax him to come to the party. = 我将哄他来参加派对。
She will coax her daughter to try new foods. = 她将哄她的女儿尝试新的食物。


虚拟语气:
If I could coax him, he would help us. = 如果我能说服他，他会帮助我们。
I wish I could coax her to forgive me. = 我希望我能哄她原谅我。


被动语态:
The child was coaxed into eating his dinner. = 孩子被哄着吃了晚饭。
The information was coaxed from the witness. = 信息是从证人那里套取的。


疑问句:
Can you coax the cat down from the tree? = 你能把猫从树上哄下来吗？
Could you coax him to change his mind? = 你能说服他改变主意吗？


否定句:
I couldn't coax him to admit his mistake. = 我无法哄他承认他的错误。
She didn't coax him into doing anything he didn't want to do. = 她没有哄他做任何他不愿意做的事情。


PHRASE->
coax into doing something = 哄骗做某事
coax out of someone = 从某人那里套取
coax a smile from someone = 从某人脸上哄出微笑
coax a confession = 套取口供
coax someone to sleep = 哄某人睡觉
