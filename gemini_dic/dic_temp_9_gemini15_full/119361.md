microcolony: /ˈmaɪkroʊkɑːləni/ | n. | 微菌落 | A small, visible cluster of microorganisms, typically bacteria or fungi, growing on a solid surface.  It represents an early stage of colony development before it becomes macroscopically visible. | Example: The researchers observed the formation of microcolonies on the agar plate after 24 hours of incubation.  研究人员在培养24小时后观察到琼脂平板上形成了微菌落。 | 近义词:  colony (but emphasizing small size), cluster | 反义词: macrocolony
