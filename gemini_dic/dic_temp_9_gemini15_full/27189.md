calliper:/ˈkælɪpər/| n.|卡尺，测径器|A calliper is an instrument used to measure the diameter or thickness of an object.  It typically consists of two legs with jaws that can be adjusted to fit the object being measured.  There are various types of callipers, including vernier callipers and digital callipers, offering different levels of precision.|例句：The machinist used a calliper to measure the diameter of the shaft. 技工用卡尺测量轴的直径。

例句：We used a digital calliper to ensure precise measurements for the project. 我们使用数显卡尺来确保项目的精确测量。|近义词：gauge, dividers, compass|反义词:|

词源:
From  Middle French *caliper, from Old French *calebre, from Medieval Latin *calibra ("caliber").  The word's evolution reflects its use in measuring, initially likely referring to the diameter or bore of a cannon.
