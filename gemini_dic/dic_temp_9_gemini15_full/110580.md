limited companies: /ˈlɪmɪtɪd ˈkʌmpəniz/ | n. | 有限公司 |  A limited company is a type of company where the liability of the shareholders is limited to the amount they have invested. This means that if the company goes bankrupt, the shareholders' personal assets are protected.  There are different types of limited companies, depending on the jurisdiction.  For example, in the UK, there are private limited companies (Ltd) and public limited companies (PLC).  | 例句：Many small businesses operate as limited companies to protect their personal assets.  许多小型企业以有限公司的形式运营，以保护其个人资产。


Many large corporations are public limited companies, listed on the stock exchange. 许多大型公司是上市的公众有限公司。


近义词：corporation, firm, business | 反义词：unlimited company, sole proprietorship | 词性：noun
