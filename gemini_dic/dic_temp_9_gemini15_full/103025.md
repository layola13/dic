jejunenesses:/dʒɪˈdʒuːnəsɪz/ | n. |空洞；缺乏趣味；枯燥乏味 |The jejunenesses of his writing made it difficult to read.  他的文章空洞乏味，难以阅读。|近义词：banalities, vapidness, insipidity |反义词：depth, richness, vitality


英文解析:The plural form of "jejuneness," referring to instances or examples of being jejune (lacking interest or substance; dull).  It highlights multiple occurrences of a lack of depth, originality, or interest.

例句:

The professor pointed out several jejunenesses in the student's essay, noting a lack of insightful analysis and original thought. 教授指出了学生论文中的一些空洞之处，指出缺乏深刻的分析和原创性思想。


The numerous jejunenesses in the politician's speech left the audience unimpressed.  这位政治家演讲中大量的空洞之处让听众印象不佳。


词源:  Derived from the adjective "jejune," ultimately from the Latin "jejunus," meaning "empty," "fasting," or "lean."  The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective into a noun signifying the state or quality of being jejune.
