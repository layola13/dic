presanctifies: /ˌpriːˈsæŋktɪfaɪz/ | v. | 预先圣化；预先奉献 | To make holy or sacred beforehand; to consecrate in advance.  This word is primarily used in theological contexts, referring to the act of setting something apart as holy before it is officially used or dedicated.  It often relates to the Eucharist (Holy Communion) in Christian theology. | The priest presanctifies the bread and wine before the mass. =  牧师在弥撒前预先圣化了面包和酒。


The practice of presanctification is especially associated with Eastern Orthodox traditions where the Eucharist may be reserved for later distribution to the sick or homebound. | 近义词: consecrate, sanctify, hallow | 反义词: profane, desecrate
