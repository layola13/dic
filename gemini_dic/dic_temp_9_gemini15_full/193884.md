self-dissatisfactions:/ˌsɛlfˌdɪsˌsætɪsˈfækʃənz/| n. |自我不满，自我厌恶 |Self-dissatisfactions refer to feelings of unhappiness or discontent with oneself, one's accomplishments, or one's character.  These feelings can stem from various sources, including unmet expectations, perceived failures, or a general sense of inadequacy.  They can manifest in different ways, ranging from mild dissatisfaction to debilitating self-criticism. |例句：Her self-dissatisfactions stemmed from a relentless pursuit of perfection. = 她的自我不满源于对完美的无情追求。

Her constant self-dissatisfactions led her to seek professional help. = 她持续的自我不满让她寻求专业帮助。|近义词：self-criticism, self-loathing, discontent, dissatisfaction, unhappiness |反义词：self-acceptance, self-love, contentment, satisfaction
