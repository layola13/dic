cadges:/kædʒ/| vt.|乞讨；骗取；诱捕|to beg persistently for something; to obtain something by persistent requests or trickery. |例句：He cadged a cigarette from a stranger. 他向陌生人讨了一支烟。

He tried to cadge a ride home. 他试图骗取搭车回家。|近义词：beg, solicit, sponge, wheedle |反义词：give, donate, bestow


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cadges money from his friends. 他向朋友们乞讨钱财。
She cadges rides to work every day. 她每天都骗取搭车上班。

一般过去时:
He cadged a meal from his neighbor. 他向邻居讨了一顿饭。
They cadged tickets to the concert. 他们骗取了演唱会的门票。

现在完成时:
He has cadged enough money for a new bike. 他已经乞讨到足够买一辆新自行车的钱了。
She has cadged favors from her colleagues for years.  她多年来一直从同事那里骗取好处。

过去完成时:
He had cadged a lift before the rain started.  在下雨之前他已经骗到了一程车。
They had cadged information from several sources. 他们已经从多个来源骗取了信息。

将来时:
He will cadge a cigarette from someone at the party. 他会在派对上向别人讨烟抽。
She will cadge a ride to the airport tomorrow. 她明天会想办法搭车去机场。

虚拟语气:
If he needed money, he would cadge it from his family. 如果他需要钱，他会向家人乞讨。
I wish I hadn't cadged that money from him. 我真希望我没有向他讨钱。

被动语态:
A ride was cadged from a passing motorist. 一辆车是被从一位过路司机那里骗来的。
Money was cadged from unsuspecting tourists.  钱是从毫无戒心的游客那里骗来的。

疑问句:
Did he cadge money from you? 他向你讨钱了吗？
Is she cadging rides again? 她又在骗取搭车了吗？

否定句:
He didn't cadge anything from me. 他没有向我讨要任何东西。
She wouldn't cadge a favor from anyone. 她不会向任何人讨要恩惠。
