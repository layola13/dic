geode:/ˈdʒioʊd/ | n. | 晶洞；结核 | A geode is a hollow, roughly spherical rock that is lined with crystals.  Geodes are formed when minerals precipitate out of water within a cavity in a rock.  They are often found in sedimentary and volcanic rocks. | Example: The geologist carefully cracked open the geode, revealing a dazzling array of amethyst crystals.  这个地质学家小心地敲开了晶洞，露出了令人眼花缭乱的紫水晶。| 近义词: cavity, nodule | 反义词:  (无明确反义词，可考虑与massive rock 对比) | 名词
