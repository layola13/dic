hit the books:/hɪt ðə bʊks/| phrasal verb|努力学习，用功读书|To study diligently and intensively.|I have a big exam next week, so I need to hit the books.|近义词：study hard, burn the midnight oil, knuckle down|反义词：slack off, goof off


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They hit the books every night before a big test. = 他们每次大考前都会每天晚上努力学习。
He hits the books diligently to prepare for the upcoming exam. = 他为了准备即将到来的考试而努力学习。


一般过去时:
She hit the books all weekend to prepare for her presentation. = 她整个周末都在努力学习以准备她的演讲。
We hit the books hard after realizing how difficult the exam would be. = 在意识到考试有多难之后，我们努力学习。


现在完成时:
I have hit the books all semester and I'm finally ready for finals. = 我整个学期都在努力学习，现在终于准备好期末考试了。
They have hit the books so hard lately; they deserve a break. = 他们最近学习非常努力，应该休息一下。


过去完成时:
She had hit the books for months before she felt confident enough for the competition. = 在她感觉自己有足够的信心参加比赛之前，她已经学习了好几个月了。
He had hit the books intensely for years before finally publishing his masterpiece. = 在最终出版他的杰作之前，他已经刻苦学习多年了。


将来时:
I will hit the books all week to prepare for the interview. = 我将一整周努力学习以准备面试。
We will hit the books tonight to cram for the quiz tomorrow. = 我们今晚将努力学习，为明天的测验突击复习。


虚拟语气:
If I had hit the books earlier, I would have passed the exam. = 如果我早点努力学习，我就通过考试了。
I wish I had hit the books more diligently during the semester. = 我希望我在这个学期学习更努力些。


被动语态: (This phrase is rarely used in passive voice.)
The books were hit (by him) all night. = 书本 (被他) 整晚都在被学习。 (This is unnatural and not recommended)


疑问句:
Will you hit the books tonight? = 你今晚会努力学习吗？
Are you going to hit the books this weekend? = 你这个周末打算努力学习吗？


否定句:
He didn't hit the books at all before the test. = 他在考试前根本没怎么学习。
They didn't hit the books enough to pass the exam. = 他们学习不够，没有通过考试。


PHRASE->
hit the books hard = 努力学习
hit the books late into the night = 挑灯夜战
hit the books before the exam = 考前突击
hit the books for hours = 学习好几个小时
hit the books consistently = 持续努力学习
