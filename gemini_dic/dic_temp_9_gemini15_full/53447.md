disapprobations:/ˌdɪsˌæprəˈbeɪʃənz/ | n. | 不赞成；反对；非难 | The expression of disapproval; strong condemnation or censure.  It often refers to a feeling or an action showing disapproval. |例句：His actions met with widespread disapprobation. 他的行为遭到广泛谴责。

例句：The disapprobation of his parents was a heavy burden on him. 他父母的反对对他来说是个沉重的负担。


近义词：condemnation, censure, disapproval, objection, criticism

反义词：approval, praise, commendation, acceptance


词源:

* **dis-**:  prefix indicating negation or reversal (as in "dislike," "disorder")
* **approb-**: from Latin "approbare," meaning "to approve"
* **-ation**: suffix forming nouns of action or process
