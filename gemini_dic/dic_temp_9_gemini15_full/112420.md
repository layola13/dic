lotophagi:/loʊˈtɒfədʒaɪ/| n. |食莲人；食莲花者|The Lotophagi were a mythical people mentioned by Homer in the Odyssey, who lived on a diet primarily consisting of the lotus fruit. Their consumption of the lotus was said to induce a state of blissful forgetfulness and apathy.  The word itself combines the Greek words "lotus" (λωτός) and "phagein" (φαγεῖν), meaning "to eat".| Example: The story of the Lotophagi serves as a cautionary tale about the dangers of hedonistic indulgence.  食莲人的故事是对享乐放纵危险的警示。|近义词：lotus-eaters|反义词：无
