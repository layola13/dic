calls:/kɔːlz/ | /kɔːlz/ | n., v. |  称呼；呼叫；要求；电话 |  The word "calls" can function as both a noun and a verb. As a noun, it refers to an act of calling or a demand. As a verb, it means to shout, to make a telephone call, or to summon. |

例句：

The doctor's calls are always urgent.  医生的电话总是很紧急。

She calls her mother every Sunday. 她每个星期天都给她母亲打电话。


近义词：shouts, cries, summons, demands, rings (phone)

反义词：answers (in the sense of responding to a call), ignores


词性：Noun, Verb


USAGE->

一般现在时:

He calls his mother every day. 他每天都给他母亲打电话。

She calls for help. 她呼救。


一般过去时:

He called me last night. 他昨晚给我打了电话。

The teacher called on the student to answer the question. 老师叫学生回答问题。


现在完成时:

She has called several times this morning. 今天早上她已经打了好几次电话了。

He has called for a meeting. 他已经要求开会。


过去完成时:

I had called before he arrived.  在他到达之前我已经打过电话了。

She had called the police but they hadn't arrived yet. 她已经报警了，但是他们还没到。


将来时:

I will call you tomorrow. 我明天会给你打电话。

He will call for a taxi. 他会叫一辆出租车。


虚拟语气:

If he called, I would answer. 如果他打电话，我会接。

I wish he called more often. 我希望他经常打电话。


被动语态:

The meeting was called off. 会议取消了。

The police were called to the scene. 警察被叫到了现场。


疑问句:

Who called? 谁打的电话？

Did he call you back? 他给你回电话了吗？


否定句:

He didn't call me. 他没有给我打电话。

She didn't call for help. 她没有呼救。



PHRASE->

calls for: 需要；要求  (The situation calls for immediate action.  情况需要立即采取行动。)

calls on: 拜访；号召 (He calls on his clients regularly. 他定期拜访他的客户。)

calls off: 取消 (They called off the meeting. 他们取消了会议。)

calls up: 打电话给；使想起 (I'll call up my friend. 我会给我朋友打电话。)

calls in:  召集；请来 (The boss called in all the staff for a meeting. 老板召集所有员工开会。)
