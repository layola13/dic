orbital:/ˈɔːrbɪtl/ /ˈɔːrbɪdəl/| n. |轨道；眼窝；(原子)轨道|A path of a celestial body or satellite around a star, planet, or moon.  An orbital is also a region of space around an atomic nucleus where there is a high probability of finding an electron. |例句：The Earth's orbital path around the sun is elliptical. 地球绕太阳运行的轨道是椭圆形的。

The electron occupies a specific orbital within the atom. 电子占据原子内的特定轨道。|近义词：orbit, path, trajectory |反义词：center, nucleus (in the context of atomic orbitals)

词源:

The word "orbital" derives from the Latin word "orbita," meaning "track," "wheel rut," or "circuit."  This reflects the circular or elliptical path associated with orbital motion.  The suffix "-al" denotes an adjective or noun related to the base word.
