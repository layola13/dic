commands:/kəˈmændz/ | n. |命令，指令；掌握，控制 | Commands are instructions given to someone to do something.  Commands can also refer to the power to control or influence people or things. |  The captain gave the command to fire. (船长下令开火。) The general has command of the army. (将军统率军队。) | instructions, orders, directives | chaos, disorder | noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher gives commands to her students. = 老师对学生发出命令。
The general issues commands to his troops. = 将军向他的部队下达命令。

一般过去时:
The officer gave commands clearly. = 军官清晰地发出命令。
They followed the commands without question. = 他们毫无疑问地服从命令。

现在完成时:
The commander has given many commands during the battle. = 司令官在战斗中已经下达了许多命令。
She has obeyed all the commands she received. = 她服从了她收到的所有命令。


过去完成时:
He had given the commands before the troops moved. = 部队行动之前，他已经下达了命令。
They had already received the commands before the meeting. = 会议之前，他们已经收到了命令。


将来时:
The captain will give commands soon. = 船长很快就会下达命令。
The government will issue new commands next week. = 政府下周将发布新的命令。


虚拟语气:
If he gave the command, they would obey. = 如果他下达命令，他们会服从。
I wish he had given the command earlier. = 我希望他早点下达命令。


被动语态:
The commands were given loudly and clearly. = 命令大声而清晰地发出。
The new commands are being implemented this week. = 新命令本周正在实施。


疑问句:
What commands did you receive? = 你收到了什么命令？
Were the commands understood by everyone? = 每个人都理解这些命令了吗？


否定句:
The soldiers did not obey the commands. = 士兵们没有服从命令。
He didn't give any commands at all. = 他根本没有下达任何命令。
