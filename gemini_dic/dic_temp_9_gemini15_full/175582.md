randomizers:/ˈrændəmaɪzərz/| n. |随机器，随机化装置|A randomizer is a device or process that introduces randomness into a system or process.  It can be used to create unpredictable outcomes, ensure fairness, or add variety. Examples include algorithms that shuffle data, hardware devices that generate random numbers, or even simple methods like drawing names from a hat.|例句：The software uses a randomizer to assign participants to different treatment groups.  该软件使用随机器将参与者分配到不同的治疗组。

The experiment employed a sophisticated randomizer to prevent bias.  实验采用了一种复杂的随机器来防止偏差。|近义词：random number generator, shuffler|反义词：determiner, organizer

The word "randomizer" is derived from the word "random," which ultimately comes from the Old French word "randon," meaning "a violent rush or impetus."  The suffix "-izer" is a common English suffix that turns a noun or adjective into a verb or noun denoting a person or thing that performs the action or has the quality of the base word.  Therefore, a randomizer is something that makes things random.
