consanguinity:/ˌkɒnsæŋˈɡwɪnɪti/| n. |血亲关系；同族；近亲结婚|The state of being related by blood; kinship.  Consanguinity refers to the biological relationship between individuals who are descended from a common ancestor.  It often implies a close relationship, though the degree of closeness varies.  In some cultures, consanguineous marriages (marriages between close relatives) are common, while in others they are taboo due to the increased risk of genetic disorders in offspring. |Example: The close consanguinity between the two families led to many social interactions.|近义词：kinship, blood relationship, family ties|反义词： unrelatedness
