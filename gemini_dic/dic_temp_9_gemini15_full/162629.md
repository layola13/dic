preappoint:/ˌpriːəˈpɔɪnt/| vt.|预先任命，提前指定|To appoint beforehand; to decide or arrange something in advance.|The committee preappointed a chairman for the next meeting.  委员会提前指定了下次会议的主席。


They preappointed her to the position. 他们提前任命她担任这个职位。|appoint, nominate, designate|dismiss, remove


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They preappoint a new manager every year. = 他们每年都预先任命一位新经理。
The company preappoints its interns for the summer program. = 公司为暑期项目预先安排实习生。

一般过去时:
The board preappointed John as CEO last month. = 董事会于上个月预先任命约翰为首席执行官。
She preappointed her successor before her retirement. = 她在退休前预先指定了她的继任者。

现在完成时:
They have preappointed three candidates for the scholarship. = 他们已经为奖学金预先选定了三名候选人。
The university has preappointed the guest speakers for the conference. = 大学已为会议预先选定演讲嘉宾。

过去完成时:
He had preappointed a replacement before leaving for vacation. = 他在休假前已预先指定了替补人员。
They had preappointed the judges for the competition weeks in advance. = 他们几周前就已预先选定了比赛的评委。

将来时:
The government will preappoint a new ambassador to China next year. = 政府明年将预先任命一位新的驻华大使。
We will preappoint the team leaders for the project before it starts. = 我们将在项目开始前预先任命团队领导。

虚拟语气:
If they had preappointed a mediator, the conflict might have been avoided. = 如果他们预先指定了一名调解人，冲突本可以避免。
I wish they had preappointed someone to handle the logistics. = 我希望他们预先指定了人来处理后勤工作。

被动语态:
