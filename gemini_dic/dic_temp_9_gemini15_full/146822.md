overimposing: /ˌoʊvərɪmˈpoʊzɪŋ/ | adj. |  过度强加的；过于强调的 |  The act of imposing something excessively or inappropriately; placing or laying something over something else to an excessive degree. It suggests an unwanted or forceful imposition. |

例句：

The overimposing of new regulations stifled innovation.  过度强加的新规扼杀了创新。

His overimposing personality made it difficult to work with him. 他过于强势的个性使得与他共事变得困难。


近义词：oppressive, domineering, overpowering, excessive, superimposing


反义词：submissive, yielding, gentle, understated


词性：形容词


词源：

* **over-**:  prefix indicating excess or to a great extent. (来自表示过度或很大程度的前缀)
* **impose**: verb meaning to force something on someone or something. (动词，意为强加于某人或某事物)

  * **im-**: prefix indicating "in" or "on". (前缀，表示“在…里”或“在…上”)
  * **pose**:  verb related to placing or setting something. (动词，与放置或设定某物有关)  Ultimately derived from Latin "ponere" (to place). (最终源于拉丁语"ponere"，意为放置)
