closures: /ˈkloʊʒərz/ | n. |闭包；结束；封闭；完结 |The act of closing something or the state of being closed.  In computer science, a closure is a function that has access to variables from its surrounding scope, even after that scope has finished executing. |例句：The closures of the wounds were carefully monitored.  伤口闭合的情况受到了仔细监测。

例句：The JavaScript closures allow us to create private variables. JavaScript闭包允许我们创建私有变量。


近义词：conclusion, ending, termination, completion |反义词：opening, beginning, commencement


词源:  From Middle English *closur, from Old French closüre, from Old French clos "enclosed, shut," from Vulgar Latin *clausum, past participle of claudere "to shut, close."  The computer science meaning is a relatively recent development, deriving from the mathematical concept of closures.
