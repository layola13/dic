complexioned:/kəmˈplekʃənd/ | adj. | 肤色的，脸色…的 | Having a particular complexion.  This word describes someone's skin tone or coloring. It's often used to specify a person's skin color, especially in a descriptive way. | He is dark-complexioned. 他皮肤黝黑。  She is fair-complexioned. 她的肤色白皙。 | dark-skinned, light-skinned, fair, olive-skinned |  pale, swarthy (depending on context) | adj.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is dark-complexioned. = 他皮肤黝黑。
She is fair-complexioned and has beautiful eyes. = 她皮肤白皙，眼睛很漂亮。


一般过去时:
The old painting depicted a pale-complexioned woman. = 那幅旧画描绘了一个脸色苍白的女子。
He was olive-complexioned when he was younger. = 他年轻的时候皮肤是橄榄色的。


现在完成时:
She has always been light-complexioned. = 她一直皮肤白皙。
He has become darker-complexioned since he moved to the tropics. = 自从他搬到热带地区后，他的肤色就变深了。


过去完成时:
They had been fair-complexioned before their long journey in the sun. = 他们在长时间的阳光照射下旅行之前，皮肤是白皙的。
She had never seen someone so ruddy-complexioned before. = 她以前从未见过肤色如此红润的人。


将来时:
He will likely be tanned and darker-complexioned after his vacation. = 假期过后，他可能会晒黑，肤色也会更深。
She will probably become more fair-complexioned after winter. = 冬天过后，她可能会变得更白皙。


虚拟语气:
If he were lighter-complexioned, he might look different. = 如果他的肤色更浅，他看起来可能会有所不同。
I wish I were olive-complexioned like my sister. = 我真希望我的肤色像我姐姐一样是橄榄色的。


被动语态:

His complexion is often described as being ruddy-complexioned. = 他的肤色经常被描述为红润的。


疑问句:

否定句:
