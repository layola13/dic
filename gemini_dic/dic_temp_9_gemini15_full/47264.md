de-americanization: /ˌdiːˌæmɛrɪkənɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |去美国化；摆脱美国影响 | The process of removing or reducing American influence or characteristics from something.  This can refer to cultural practices, political systems, economic structures, or any other aspect of life.  It often implies a rejection of American values or a move towards greater independence from the United States. | Example: The country's de-americanization policy led to the removal of many American-owned businesses. 这个国家的去美国化政策导致许多美国企业撤资。 | 近义词:  denationalization (if referring to removing national characteristics),  indigenization (if referring to promoting local culture)| 反义词: americanization
