out-tell:/ˌaʊtˈtɛl/| vt.|胜过；超过；比…好|To surpass or exceed in telling or recounting; to excel in narrative skill.|例句：His stories out-tell all others. 他的故事胜过所有其他的故事。|例句：Her account of the incident out-told everyone else's. 她对事件的描述比其他任何人的都好。|近义词：surpass, exceed, excel, outdo|反义词：understate, underestimate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His stories out-tell all others. = 他的故事胜过所有其他的故事。
This version out-tells the original. = 这个版本比原版更好。

一般过去时:
Her account out-told everyone else's. = 她对事件的描述比其他任何人的都好。
The witness's testimony out-told the suspect's alibi. = 证人的证词驳斥了嫌疑人的不在场证明。

现在完成时:
His tales have out-told many a legend. = 他的故事已经胜过许多传说。
This new evidence has out-told the previous findings. = 这项新证据已经超过了之前的调查结果。

过去完成时:
The old sailor's yarn had out-told every other story at the tavern. = 老水手的航海故事在那家酒馆里已经胜过所有其他故事了。
By the end of the debate, her arguments had out-told her opponent's. = 辩论结束时，她的论点已经胜过了她的对手。

将来时:
His new book will out-tell all his previous works. = 他的新书将会胜过他以前的所有作品。
I predict that this technology will out-tell its predecessors. = 我预测这项技术将胜过其前身。

虚拟语气:
If only his evidence out-told the prosecution's case! = 但愿他的证据能胜过检方的指控！
If his story out-told hers, he might win the prize. = 如果他的故事比她的好，他可能会赢得奖品。

被动语态:
His achievements were out-told by the media. = 他的成就被媒体夸大了。(这里被动语态略显牵强，因为out-tell本身强调主动胜过)
The truth was out-told by rumors. = 真相被谣言掩盖了。(这里被动语态也略显牵强，但可以理解为真相被谣言“胜过”在传播方面)


疑问句:
Does his version out-tell the original? = 他的版本比原版更好吗？
Will the new findings out-tell the old research? = 新的发现会超过旧的研究吗？

否定句:
His explanation didn't out-tell the facts. = 他的解释没有胜过事实。
Her account did not out-tell the other witnesses' testimonies. = 她的说法没有胜过其他证人的证词。


PHRASE->
out-tell a story =  讲述一个精彩的故事
out-tell the truth = 比真相更精彩
out-tell the competition =  胜过竞争对手
out-tell the rumors =  胜过谣言
out-tell expectations = 超出预期


词源:
The word "out-tell" is a compound verb formed from the prefix "out-" (meaning "to a greater extent" or "surpassing") and the verb "tell" (meaning to narrate or relate).  It's a relatively infrequent word, more common in literary contexts than everyday speech.
