commensurable: /kəˈmɛnzərəbl/ | adj. | 可通约的；可测量的；相称的 |

英文解析：Capable of being measured by a common standard; having a common measure.  Something is commensurable with another if there exists a unit that divides both evenly.  This concept applies not only to numbers but also to lengths, quantities, and sometimes more abstract concepts.

例句：

The two lengths are commensurable because they are both divisible by 5.  = 这两条长度是可通约的，因为它们都可以被5整除。

Their ambitions are commensurable with their abilities. = 他们的抱负与他们的能力相称。


近义词：proportionate, comparable, measurable, congruous

反义词：incommensurable, disproportionate, immeasurable


词源：

Comes from the Latin word "commensurabilis," which is formed from "com-" (together, with) + "mensura" (measure) + "-bilis" (capable of).  Therefore, it literally means "capable of being measured together."
