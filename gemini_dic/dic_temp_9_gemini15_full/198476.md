shriller:/ˈʃrɪlər/| adj.|刺耳的，尖锐的，令人不快的|Having a high-pitched and unpleasant sound.|The shriller the sound, the more irritating it became.|近义词：piercing, strident, shrill|反义词：soft, mellow


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The alarm clock has a shriller ring than before. = 闹钟的铃声比以前更刺耳了。
Her voice is shriller than usual today. = 她今天的声音比平时更尖锐。


一般过去时:
The whistle shrieked a shriller sound. = 口哨发出更尖锐的声音。
The child cried with a shriller voice. = 孩子用更尖锐的声音哭喊。


现在完成时:
The music has become shriller and more intense. = 音乐变得更尖锐、更强烈了。
The wind has shrieked a shriller cry. = 风发出更刺耳的呼啸声。


过去完成时:
The singer's voice had become shriller after the illness. = 歌手生病后，声音变得更尖锐了。
The siren's shriek had been shriller and more urgent. = 警报器的尖叫声变得更尖锐、更紧急了。


将来时:

The concert will feature a shriller and more powerful performance. = 音乐会将呈现更尖锐、更强大的表演。


虚拟语气:
If the sound were shriller, I would cover my ears. = 如果声音更尖锐，我会捂住耳朵。
I wish the noise wasn't so shriller. = 我希望噪音不要那么刺耳。


被动语态:
The sound was described as shriller and more unpleasant. = 声音被描述为更尖锐、更令人不快。
Her voice was considered shriller by the audience. = 她的声音被观众认为过于尖锐。


疑问句:
Is the sound of the bird shriller than normal? = 鸟叫声比平时更尖锐吗？
Isn't her voice a little shriller than usual? = 她的声音是不是比平时尖锐了一点？


否定句:
The music wasn't shriller; it was actually quite soft. = 音乐并不尖锐，实际上相当柔和。
The sound wasn't shriller than I expected. = 声音并没有比我预期的更尖锐。
