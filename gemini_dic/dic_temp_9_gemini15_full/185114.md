retinalite:/ˌrɛtɪˈnaɪt/| n. |视网膜炎|Retinalite is an inflammation of the retina, the light-sensitive tissue lining the back of the eye.  It can be caused by various factors, including infections, autoimmune diseases, and certain medications. Symptoms can include blurred vision, floaters, and photophobia (sensitivity to light).|The patient was diagnosed with retinalite after experiencing sudden vision loss.|近义词：retinopathy (a broader term encompassing various retinal diseases)|反义词：无|名词
