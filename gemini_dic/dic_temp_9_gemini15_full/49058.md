dehumanises:/ˌdiːˈhjuːmənaɪz/ | vt. | 使失去人性；使没有人性；使非人化 | To deprive of human qualities or attributes; to treat or regard as less than human.  |例句：The factory work dehumanised the workers, reducing them to mere cogs in a machine. 工厂的工作使工人失去了人性，使他们沦为机器上的齿轮。
The brutal regime systematically dehumanised its political opponents. 残暴的政权系统性地使政治对手非人化。| 近义词：dehumanize, depersonalize, brutalize, objectify | 反义词：humanize, personalize, empathize

词源:
The word "dehumanises" is derived from the word "human" and the prefix "de-," meaning "to remove" or "to deprive of."  The suffix "-ise" (or "-ize" in American English) indicates the action of making something. Therefore, dehumanise means "to deprive of human qualities."
