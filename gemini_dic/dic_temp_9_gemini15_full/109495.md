leptomeningitis:/ˌlɛptoˌmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs/| n. |  leptomeningitis | 脑膜炎 |  Leptomeningitis is an inflammation of the leptomeninges, the delicate membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. It's typically caused by infection (viral, bacterial, fungal, or parasitic). Symptoms can vary widely depending on the cause and severity, but often include headache, fever, stiff neck, and sensitivity to light.  | The patient was diagnosed with bacterial leptomeningitis and immediately started on antibiotics.  这位病人被诊断出患有细菌性脑膜炎，并立即开始服用抗生素。| encephalitis, meningitis |  (no direct antonym,  but conditions *without* leptomeningeal inflammation could be considered opposite in a limited sense)
