ecstatics:/ɛkˈstætɪks/| adj.| 狂喜的，欣喜若狂的 | Extremely happy and excited.  |例句：She was ecstatic about winning the lottery. 她对赢得彩票欣喜若狂。|例句：The ecstatic fans cheered wildly after the game.  比赛结束后，狂喜的球迷们疯狂欢呼。|近义词：rapturous, overjoyed, thrilled, delighted |反义词：dejected, depressed, miserable, despondent

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is ecstatic about his promotion. = 他对自己的晋升感到欣喜若狂。
She is ecstatic to see her family. = 她看到家人非常高兴。


一般过去时:
They were ecstatic when they heard the good news. = 听到好消息时，他们欣喜若狂。
He was ecstatic after scoring the winning goal. = 在攻入制胜球后，他欣喜若狂。


现在完成时:
I have been ecstatic ever since I got engaged. = 从我订婚以来，我一直欣喜若狂。
She has been ecstatic about her new job. = 她一直对她的新工作感到兴奋。


过去完成时:
They had been ecstatic to finally reach the summit. = 他们终于到达山顶时欣喜若狂。
She had been ecstatic about the prospect of traveling abroad. = 她对出国旅行的前景感到非常兴奋。


将来时:
I will be ecstatic when I graduate. = 我毕业时会欣喜若狂。
They will be ecstatic to see you. = 他们会非常高兴见到你。


虚拟语气:

I wish I could be ecstatic about my work. = 我希望我能对我的工作感到兴奋。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
