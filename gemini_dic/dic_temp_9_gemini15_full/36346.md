coiling:/ˈkɔɪlɪŋ/ | v. | 盘绕；卷曲 | The act of winding something into a coil or spiral; the state of being coiled.  | Example: The snake was coiling itself around the branch.  这条蛇正盘绕在树枝上。 |近义词：winding, spiraling, twisting | 反义词：uncoiling, unwinding | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The vine is coiling around the tree. = 葡萄藤缠绕着树木。
The spring is constantly coiling and uncoiling. = 弹簧不断地卷曲和伸展。


一般过去时:
The rope coiled neatly on the deck. = 绳子整齐地卷在甲板上。
She coiled her hair into a bun. = 她把头发盘成一个发髻。


现在完成时:
The plant has coiled itself around the support. = 植物已经缠绕在支撑物上了。
The wire has coiled and become tangled. = 电线已经缠绕在一起，变得纠结了。


过去完成时:
The snake had coiled itself before striking. = 蛇在攻击之前已经盘好了身子。
The spring had coiled tightly before releasing its energy. = 弹簧在释放能量之前已经紧紧地卷了起来。


将来时:
The smoke will coil lazily into the air. = 烟雾将懒洋洋地盘旋在空中。
He will coil the hose after using it. = 他用完水管后会把它卷起来。


虚拟语气:
