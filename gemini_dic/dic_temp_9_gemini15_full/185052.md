/rɛˈtɔːrɪʃən/ | n. |修辞学家；雄辩家 | A rhetorician is a person skilled in the art of rhetoric, which is the art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing, especially the use of figures of speech and other compositional techniques.  Rhetoricians study and practice the techniques of persuasion and argumentation. They analyze how language is used to influence audiences, and they may also teach these techniques to others. | Example:  Aristotle was a highly influential rhetorician.  亚里士多德是一位极具影响力的修辞学家。|近义词：orator, speaker, debater |反义词：None directly applicable, but perhaps "inarticulate" or "unpersuasive" could be considered antonyms in a broad sense. | n.
