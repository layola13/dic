co-pilot:/ˈkoʊˌpaɪlət/| n.|副驾驶，辅助驾驶员|A co-pilot is a pilot who assists the main pilot of an aircraft.  They share responsibilities and duties during the flight. |例句：The co-pilot monitored the instruments while the captain handled communication. 副驾驶监控仪表，机长负责通讯。

例句： During the emergency landing, the co-pilot played a crucial role in assisting the captain. 紧急着陆期间，副驾驶在协助机长方面发挥了至关重要的作用。


近义词：second pilot, assistant pilot|反义词：captain, pilot-in-command|词性：名词

词源:
The word "co-pilot" is a compound word.  "Co-" is a prefix meaning "together" or "jointly," and "pilot" refers to a person who operates an aircraft.  Therefore, a co-pilot is someone who pilots an aircraft jointly with another pilot (typically the captain).
