foretold:/fɔːrˈtoʊld/ | v. | 预言，预告 | To state, tell about, or predict (something that will happen in the future).  It is the past participle of the verb "foretell". | 例句：The fortune teller foretold a bright future for me.  算命先生预言我会有一个光明的未来。

The ancient prophecies foretold the coming of a great king.  古老的预言预示着一位伟大君王的到来。


近义词：predict, prophesy, foresee, prefigure | 反义词：mispredict

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He foretells the future. = 他预言未来。
She foretells the weather. = 她预报天气。

一般过去时:
The prophet foretold the disaster. = 先知预言了这场灾难。
They foretold a successful outcome. = 他们预言了一个成功的结果。

The seers have foretold many events. = 预言家们已经预言了许多事件。

过去完成时:

The ancient texts had foretold the rise of a new empire. = 古代文本预言了一个新帝国的崛起。

将来时:

Scientists will foretell the path of the hurricane. = 科学家们将预言飓风的路径。
