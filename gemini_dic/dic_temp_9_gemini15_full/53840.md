disconsolation: /ˌdɪskɒnsəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 悲伤；沮丧；失意 |  A state of great sadness and hopelessness.  It implies a profound sense of loss and despair, often stemming from a significant disappointment or bereavement. |  例句：Her disconsolation was palpable after the loss of her beloved pet.  她失去心爱的宠物后，悲伤之情溢于言表。


例句：The news of the failed project filled him with disconsolation.  项目失败的消息让他感到非常沮丧。


近义词：despair, dejection, misery, despondency


反义词：joy, happiness, elation, jubilation


词源:  The word comes from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") and "consolation," which derives from the Latin word "consolari" (to comfort). Therefore, disconsolation literally means "the absence of comfort" or "lack of solace."
