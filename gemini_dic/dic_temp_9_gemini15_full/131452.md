noncogent:/nɒnˈkoʊdʒənt/| adj. | 不确凿的；论证无效的；不合逻辑的 |  lacking the force or cogency to compel acceptance; not convincing.  A noncogent argument may have true premises and a valid structure, but it fails to persuade because the premises are irrelevant to the conclusion or otherwise weak. | Example: His argument, while seemingly logical, was ultimately noncogent due to a flawed premise.  他的论点虽然看似合乎逻辑，但由于前提错误，最终站不住脚。|近义词： unconvincing, invalid, illogical, fallacious | 反义词： cogent, convincing, valid, sound
