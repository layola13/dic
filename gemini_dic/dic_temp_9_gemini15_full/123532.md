monotreme:/ˈmɒnəˌtriːm/| n. |单孔类动物|A monotreme is a mammal that lays eggs instead of giving birth to live young.  Monotremes are characterized by having a cloaca, a single opening for the urinary, reproductive, and digestive tracts.  They are also unique among mammals for possessing a pouch in which they raise their young.  The only living monotremes are the echidnas and the platypus.|例句：The platypus is a fascinating example of a monotreme, a unique group of mammals that lay eggs.|近义词：egg-laying mammal|反义词：marsupial, placental mammal
