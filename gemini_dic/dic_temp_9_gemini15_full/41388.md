corms:/kɔːrmz/| n.|球茎，块茎|A corm is a short, thickened, underground stem that serves as a storage organ for nutrients.  It is different from a bulb in that it is solid and does not have fleshy leaves. Corms are commonly found in plants like gladioli and crocuses. They are used by plants to survive unfavorable conditions and to reproduce vegetatively. | Example: Gladioli reproduce through corms.  郁金香通过球茎繁殖。  The gardener carefully separated the corms before planting them. 园丁在种植之前仔细地分开了球茎。|近义词：bulb (though structurally different), tuber|反义词：无
