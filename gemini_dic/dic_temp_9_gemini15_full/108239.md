laughings:/ˈlæfɪŋz/| n.|笑声，笑；(尤指)连续不断的笑声|The sound of continuous laughter;  a series of laughs.  It often implies a somewhat uncontrolled or unrestrained laughter.|例句：The laughings of the children filled the air. 孩子们的笑声充满了空气。
例句：Her laughings echoed through the empty hall. 她的笑声在空旷的大厅回荡。|近义词：laughter, chuckles, guffaws|反义词：crying, sobbing

laugh:/læf/| v. n.|笑，大笑|To make the sounds and movements of the face and body that express amusement or happiness.|例句：The joke made everyone laugh.  笑话让每个人都笑了。
例句：She laughed heartily at the comedian's jokes. 她开怀大笑，听着喜剧演员讲笑话。|近义词：chuckle, giggle, smile|反义词：cry, weep

一般现在时:
I laugh every day. = 我每天都笑。
They laugh at his jokes. = 他们嘲笑他的笑话。

一般过去时:
I laughed until my sides hurt. = 我笑到肚子疼。
He laughed at my mistake. = 他嘲笑我的错误。

现在完成时:
I have laughed so hard today. = 我今天笑得很开心。

过去完成时:

They had laughed at him mercilessly. = 他们无情地嘲笑他。

将来时:
