permafrost: /ˈpɜːrməfrɒst/ | n. | 永久冻土 | Permanently frozen subsoil found in very cold regions, typically at high latitudes or altitudes.  It consists of soil, sediment, and rock cemented together by ice.  The active layer, the upper layer that thaws seasonally, sits atop the permafrost.  The depth of permafrost can vary greatly depending on location and climate. | The melting permafrost is causing significant changes to the landscape and releasing greenhouse gases.  永久冻土的融化正在对地貌造成重大改变并释放温室气体。|近义词：permanently frozen ground, ice-bound soil | 反义词：unfrozen ground
