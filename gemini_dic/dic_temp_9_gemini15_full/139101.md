obduracy:/ˈɒbdʒʊrəsi/| /ˈɑːbdʒʊrəsi/ | n. | 顽固；固执；执拗 |  The quality of being stubbornly persistent in wrongdoing;  unyielding adherence to an opinion, purpose, or course of action in the face of reason, argument, or persuasion. It implies a refusal to change one's mind or behavior, even when faced with evidence or strong arguments to the contrary.  |  His obduracy in the face of overwhelming evidence was astonishing.  他的顽固不化，即使面对压倒性的证据也令人惊讶。

The government's obduracy on the issue led to widespread protests. 政府在这个问题上的顽固不化导致了大规模抗议。 |  stubbornness, inflexibility, intransigence, pertinacity |  yielding, flexibility, compliance, pliability | 名词
