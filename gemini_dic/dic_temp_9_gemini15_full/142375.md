orthopteran:/ɔːrˈθɒptərən/| n. |直翅目昆虫|A member of the insect order Orthoptera, characterized by having chewing mouthparts and usually two pairs of wings, the forewings being leathery and the hindwings membranous.  Many orthopterans are known for their stridulation (sound production by rubbing body parts together), such as crickets and grasshoppers. |Example: Grasshoppers and crickets are common examples of orthopterans.  直翅目昆虫的常见例子包括蚱蜢和蟋蟀。|近义词：insect (general term) |反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with insects from other orders.
