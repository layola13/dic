coreferential:/ˌkɔːrɪˈfɛrənʃəl/ | adj. | 指称相同的，共指的 |  Referring to or denoting two or more linguistic expressions that refer to the same entity.  This term is primarily used in linguistics and discourse analysis to describe the relationship between words or phrases that point to the same person, place, thing, or idea.  | Example: In the sentence "John saw Mary, and he waved at her," "John" and "he" are coreferential, as are "Mary" and "her."  | 近义词：co-referential, identical in reference | 反义词：non-coreferential, distinct in reference
