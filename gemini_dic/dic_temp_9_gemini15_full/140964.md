one-upsmanship: /ˌwʌnˈʌpmənʃɪp/ | n. | 炫耀才能；技高一筹；争胜心理 | The practice of always trying to surpass someone else's achievement or claim.  It involves subtly attempting to make oneself appear superior. | 例句：His constant one-upsmanship made him unpopular with his colleagues. 他总是想压倒别人，这让他在同事中不受欢迎。

例句：The conversation quickly devolved into a tiresome display of one-upsmanship.  谈话很快变成了令人厌烦的炫耀才能的表演。


近义词：competitive spirit,  braggadocio, boasting, showing off, outdoing

反义词：humility, modesty, self-deprecation


词源:  The word is a blend of "one-up" (to surpass) and "manship" (skill or expertise).  It emerged in the mid-20th century.
