obliged:/əˈblaɪdʒd/| adj.|感激的；不得不的；有义务的|Feeling grateful or indebted; being under a moral or legal obligation to do something.|例句：I'm obliged to you for your help.  = 我非常感谢你的帮助。|例句：We are obliged to follow the rules. = 我们有义务遵守规章制度。|近义词：grateful, indebted, thankful, bound|反义词：ungrateful, unwilling, unobligated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I am obliged to you for your kindness. = 我感激你的好意。
He is obliged to attend the meeting. = 他必须参加会议。


一般过去时:
I was obliged to cancel my trip. = 我不得不取消我的旅行。
She was obliged to work overtime. = 她不得不加班。


现在完成时:
I have been obliged to borrow money. = 我不得不借钱。
They have been obliged to postpone the project. = 他们不得不推迟项目。


过去完成时:
I had been obliged to leave early. = 我不得不早点离开。
She had been obliged to make a difficult decision. = 她不得不做出一个艰难的决定。


将来时:
I will be obliged to explain my actions. = 我将不得不解释我的行为。
He will be obliged to pay a fine. = 他将不得不支付罚款。


虚拟语气:
I would be obliged if you could help me. = 如果你能帮我，我将不胜感激。
She would have been obliged to resign if she hadn't apologized. = 如果她没有道歉，她就不得不辞职了。


被动语态:
I was obliged to leave. = 我被迫离开。（被动语态，强调被迫）
He was obliged to pay the debt. = 他被迫偿还债务。（被动语态，强调被迫）


疑问句:
Are you obliged to do that? = 你必须那样做吗？
Was he obliged to attend the ceremony? = 他必须参加典礼吗？


否定句:
I am not obliged to help him. = 我没有义务帮助他。
She was not obliged to follow his instructions. = 她没有义务遵从他的指示。


PHRASE->
be obliged to do sth. = 不得不做某事
feel obliged to do sth. = 感到有义务做某事
much obliged = 非常感谢
I'd be much obliged = 我将非常感激
under an obligation = 有义务


词源:
obliged 源自于法语单词 "obligé,"  最终源于拉丁语 "obligāre,"  意为 "to bind" 或 "to tie."  因此，"obliged" 的本义是受到约束或义务的。
