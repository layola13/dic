disinclined:/ˌdɪsɪnˈklaɪnd/| adj.| 不愿意的；不情愿的；反感的 | unwilling; reluctant; averse |  He was disinclined to help. 他不愿意帮忙。 I'm disinclined to believe him. 我不太相信他。 |近义词：reluctant, unwilling, averse, loath |反义词：inclined, willing, eager


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is disinclined to answer questions. = 他不愿意回答问题。
She is disinclined to participate in the project. = 她不愿意参与这个项目。


一般过去时:
They were disinclined to share their ideas. = 他们不愿意分享他们的想法。
He was disinclined to go out in the rain. = 他不愿意冒雨出门。


现在完成时:
I have been disinclined to exercise lately. = 最近我一直不太想运动。
She has been disinclined to socialize since the incident. = 自从那件事发生后，她就不太愿意社交了。


过去完成时:
He had been disinclined to believe the news, but then he saw the evidence. = 他之前一直不愿意相信这个消息，但后来他看到了证据。
They had been disinclined to invest in the company, until they saw the profit projections. = 在看到利润预测之前，他们一直不愿意投资这家公司。


将来时:
I will be disinclined to travel during the peak season. = 我将在旺季不愿旅行。
She will likely be disinclined to cooperate after the argument. = 争吵之后，她很可能不愿意合作。


虚拟语气:
If I were disinclined to help, I wouldn't have offered. = 如果我不愿意帮忙，我就不会主动提出。
I wish I weren't so disinclined to early mornings. = 我真希望自己对早起没那么反感。


被动语态:  (The adjective "disinclined" is rarely used passively.)


疑问句:
Are you disinclined to accept the offer? = 你不愿意接受这个提议吗？
Is he disinclined to discuss the matter further? = 他不愿意进一步讨论这件事吗？


否定句:
He is not disinclined to help, if asked nicely. = 如果好好请求，他并不反对帮忙。
She is not entirely disinclined to consider the proposal. = 她并非完全不愿意考虑这个提案。


PHRASE->
be disinclined to  = 不愿意...
feel disinclined to = 感到不愿意...
seem disinclined to = 看起来不愿意...
show oneself disinclined to = 表现出不愿意...
become disinclined to = 变得不愿意...
