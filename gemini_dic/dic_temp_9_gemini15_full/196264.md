serenading: /ˌsɛrəˈneɪdɪŋ/ | v. |  serenading |  serenade 的现在分词形式，意为“夜间在…窗下唱歌（尤指为讨好某人）” |  To sing a serenade to someone, especially under their window at night. |


例句：

He was serenading his girlfriend under her balcony. = 他在她阳台下为他的女朋友唱歌。

The musicians were serenading the guests with beautiful melodies. = 音乐家们正在为宾客们演奏优美的旋律。


近义词：singing to, wooing (in a romantic context)

反义词：shouting at, ignoring


词性：动词 (verb)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He serenades her every night. = 他每晚都为她唱歌。
They serenade their friends with guitar music. = 他们用吉他音乐为朋友们演奏。

一般过去时:
He serenaded her with a beautiful song. = 他为她唱了一首美丽的歌。
The band serenaded the crowd after the concert. = 音乐会在结束后，乐队为人群演奏。

现在完成时:
He has serenaded her many times. = 他已经为她唱过很多次歌了。
The group has serenaded audiences all over the world. = 这个乐队在世界各地为观众演出过。

过去完成时:
He had serenaded her before he proposed. = 在他求婚之前，他已经为她唱过歌了。
The singers had serenaded the king before the banquet. = 在宴会前，歌手们为国王演唱过。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
