fajitas:/fəˈhiːtəz/| n.|墨西哥肉片|Strips of grilled meat (usually beef, chicken, or pork) served with warm tortillas, typically with various toppings such as cheese, lettuce, tomatoes, and salsa.  Fajitas are a popular Tex-Mex dish.|例句：We ordered chicken fajitas and shared them family-style. 我们点了鸡肉卷饼，然后像家人一样分享。

词源:
The word "fajitas" is derived from the Spanish word "faja," meaning "belt" or "band." This refers to the strips of meat that are grilled and resemble strips of fabric or a belt.  The word evolved in the Tex-Mex culinary tradition.
