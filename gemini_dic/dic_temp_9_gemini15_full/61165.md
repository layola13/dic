elucidate:/ɪˈluːsɪdeɪt/ | vt. |阐明；解释；说明 | To make something clear; to explain something in a way that is easy to understand.  | The professor elucidated the complex theory with simple examples. 教授用简单的例子阐明了复杂的理论。 | explain, clarify, illuminate, explicate | confuse, obscure | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He elucidates the concept clearly. 他清晰地阐述了这个概念。
The teacher elucidates the grammar rules patiently. 老师耐心地解释语法规则。

一般过去时:
She elucidated her position on the issue. 她阐明了她在这个问题上的立场。
The scientist elucidated the findings of his research. 科学家阐明了他的研究结果。

现在完成时:
He has elucidated the mystery surrounding the disappearance. 他已经阐明了围绕失踪案的谜团。
The author has elucidated the complex plot of the novel. 作者已经阐明了这部小说的复杂情节。

过去完成时:
They had elucidated the problem before the meeting. 他们在会议前已经阐明了这个问题。
The detective had elucidated the clues before arresting the suspect. 侦探在逮捕嫌疑人之前已经阐明了线索。

将来时:
The speaker will elucidate the main points of his presentation. 演讲者将阐明他演讲的要点。
She will elucidate the benefits of the new policy. 她将阐明这项新政策的好处。

虚拟语气:
If he elucidated the instructions more clearly, we wouldn't be so confused. 如果他更清楚地解释说明书，我们就不会这么困惑。
I wish he had elucidated the process more thoroughly. 我希望他更彻底地解释这个过程。

被动语态:
The theory was elucidated by the renowned physicist. 这个理论是由著名的物理学家阐明的。
The complex issue was elucidated in the report. 这个复杂的问题在报告中得到了阐明。

疑问句:
Can you elucidate this point further? 你能进一步阐明这一点吗？
How did the author elucidate the theme of the story? 作者是如何阐明故事主题的？

否定句:
He did not elucidate his reasoning sufficiently. 他没有充分阐明他的推理。
The speaker failed to elucidate the key concepts. 演讲者未能阐明关键概念。

词源:
From Latin *elucidāre, from ē- "out" + lucīdāre "to make bright," from lūx, lūcis "light."  The word ultimately derives from the root meaning "light".
