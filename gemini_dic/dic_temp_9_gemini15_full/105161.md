kettledrums:/ˈketəldrʌmz/| n. |定音鼓；铜鼓|Kettle drums are a type of percussion instrument consisting of two large, bowl-shaped drums, typically tuned to different pitches. They are used in orchestras, bands, and other musical ensembles to provide a deep, resonant sound.  |例句：The orchestra began with a powerful roll of the kettledrums. 乐队以强劲的定音鼓声开始演奏。

The kettledrums provided a dramatic backdrop to the singer's performance. 定音鼓为歌手的表演提供了戏剧性的背景。|近义词：timpani|反义词:None.

词源:
The word "kettledrum" is a compound word. "Kettle" refers to the bowl-shaped drum, and "drum" refers to the percussion instrument.  The origin of "kettle" as a container is from Old English *cetel, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *katilō. "Drum" ultimately comes from  Old French *drume, from Old High German *trumma.
