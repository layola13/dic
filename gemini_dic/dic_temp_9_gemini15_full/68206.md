fair-trading: /ˌfeərˈtreɪdɪŋ/ | n. |公平交易；公平贸易 | The practice of ensuring that businesses and consumers engage in ethical and equitable transactions. This includes practices like fair pricing, honest advertising, and consumer protection.  | Example:  The government is promoting fair-trading practices to protect consumers from exploitation.  政府正在推广公平交易行为以保护消费者免受剥削。


The new regulations aim to improve fair trading within the industry. 新规旨在改善行业内的公平交易。| 近义词：ethical trading, honest dealing | 反义词：unfair trading, exploitation
