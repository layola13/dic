guelf:/ɡwɛlf/| n. |盖尔弗派, 吉贝林派的支持者 |A supporter of the Guelphs, a political faction in medieval Italy.  The Guelphs were originally supporters of the papacy and opposed the Holy Roman Emperors. The term is often used historically to denote a supporter of papal authority.|The Guelphs and Ghibellines fought for centuries.|近义词：papist (in historical context), supporter of papal authority|反义词：Ghibelline
