quasi-prosperously:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈprɒspərəsli/| adj.|表面繁荣地，看似兴旺地| seemingly prosperous; giving a deceptive impression of prosperity.  The town appeared quasi-prosperously, with newly painted buildings masking underlying economic struggles.|近义词：apparently prosperous, seemingly affluent|反义词：clearly impoverished, demonstrably failing


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, describing how something appears to be.  Therefore, grammatical tenses and voice are applied to the verb it modifies, not to the adverb itself.  Examples below show usage with various verb tenses.

一般现在时:
They live quasi-prosperously, hiding their debts. = 他们表面上过着富足的生活，掩盖着他们的债务。
The business appears quasi-prosperously, but its profits are dwindling. = 这家公司表面上看起来很兴旺，但利润却在减少。


一般过去时:
The family lived quasi-prosperously for many years before their investments failed. = 这家人多年来表面上过着富足的生活，直到他们的投资失败。
The country presented a quasi-prosperous image, masking widespread poverty. = 这个国家呈现出表面繁荣的景象，掩盖了普遍存在的贫困。


现在完成时:
They have lived quasi-prosperously for years, maintaining a facade of wealth. = 他们多年来一直表面上过着富足的生活，维持着财富的假象。
The nation has appeared quasi-prosperously despite rising unemployment. = 尽管失业率上升，这个国家仍然显得表面繁荣。


过去完成时:
They had lived quasi-prosperously until the economic downturn hit. = 直到经济衰退来临，他们才过着表面繁荣的生活。
The town had presented a quasi-prosperous facade before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，这个小镇一直呈现着表面繁荣的景象。


将来时:
They will continue to live quasi-prosperously, hoping to avoid detection. = 他们将继续表面上过着富足的生活，希望能避免被发现。
The city will likely appear quasi-prosperously for a few more years before the bubble bursts. = 在泡沫破裂之前的几年里，这座城市很可能会显得表面繁荣。


被动语态:
Their lifestyle was perceived as quasi-prosperously until the truth emerged. = 直到真相大白，他们的生活方式才被认为是表面繁荣的。
The situation was presented as quasi-prosperously by the government, misleading the public. = 政府将情况描绘成表面繁荣的，误导了公众。


PHRASE->
This word is not typically used in common phrases.  Its usage is as a descriptive adverb modifying verbs that describe appearance or state of being.  Examples of such verbs would include: appear, seem, look, present, function, operate, etc.
