pillories:/ˈpɪləriz/| n. v.| 颈手枷；示众；公开谴责 | A pillory is a wooden structure with holes for the head and hands, used formerly to punish offenders by exposing them to public ridicule.  The verb "to pillory" means to expose someone to public ridicule or criticism. | 例句：The politician was pilloried in the press for his scandalous behavior.  这位政客因其丑闻行为而遭到媒体的公开谴责。

The criminal was put in the pillory for his crimes.  罪犯因其罪行而被置于颈手枷中。

近义词：publicly shame, ridicule, denounce, condemn | 反义词：praise, commend, applaud


词源:
The word "pillory" comes from the Old French word "pilori," which itself derives from the Latin word "pilorium."  The origin of the Latin word is uncertain, but it may be related to the word "pila," meaning "pillar" or "post," reflecting the structure's resemblance to a post.
