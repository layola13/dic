clumsier:/ˈklʌmziər/| adj.|更笨拙的，更笨手笨脚的 |More awkward or clumsy.  It describes a greater degree of clumsiness than "clumsy."|例句：He was even clumsier than usual, knocking over a vase and spilling his drink. 他比平时更笨手笨脚，打翻了一个花瓶，还洒了饮料。


例句：Her movements became clumsier as the day wore on. 随着一天的过去，她的动作越来越笨拙。


近义词：awkwarder, more ungainly, more maladroit


反义词：graceful, deft, skillful, adroit


词性：形容词


词源:  Derived from "clumsy" + "-er" (comparative suffix). "Clumsy" itself originates from the Middle English word "clumsey," possibly related to words suggesting "slow" or "heavy" movement.  The exact etymology is debated, but the association with awkwardness and lack of dexterity is clear throughout its history.
