pantographs: /ˈpæn.tə.ɡræfs/ | n. |万向节，多向节，缩放仪|A pantograph is a mechanical linkage that allows for the reproduction of a drawing or design at a different scale. It consists of a framework of parallel bars connected by joints, allowing for the precise copying of movements.  |Example: The architect used a pantograph to enlarge the blueprint. 建筑师用万向节放大蓝图。|近义词：copying device, duplicator |反义词：None specifically, but words suggesting limitations on scaling or copying could be considered opposites in context. |名词
