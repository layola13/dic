lido's:/ˈliːdoʊz/| n. |利多海滩（所有格）；利多（复数）|The word "lido" refers to a public beach or bathing place, often associated with a swimming pool or similar facility.  The possessive form "lido's" indicates belonging to a specific lido.  It can also be the plural of "lido", referring to multiple such places.|例句：We spent the afternoon at the lido's beach. 我们在利多海滩度过了下午。


例句：Many families enjoyed the sunshine at the lido's during the summer. 许多家庭在夏天享受利多的阳光。


近义词：beach, bathing place, swimming pool (depending on context) |反义词：None specifically. |词性: Noun (possessive/plural)


词源: From Italian *lido*, meaning "shore," "beach," or "coast."  The word entered English in the early 20th century, likely through the popularity of the Lido in Venice, Italy, a famous beach area.
