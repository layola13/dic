reharmonized:/ˌriːˈhɑːrmənaɪzd/| v. |重新和声化，重新编配和弦|To reharmonize a piece of music is to change its harmony, often to create a new or different effect.  This usually involves replacing some or all of the original chords with different ones while maintaining the melody.  It's a common technique in jazz, pop, and classical music arrangements. | Example: The jazz musician reharmonized the standard ballad, giving it a modern twist.  重新编配了这首标准的抒情歌，赋予它一种现代的风格。|近义词：rearrange, re-arrange, adapt|反义词：leave unchanged, preserve original harmony|动词
