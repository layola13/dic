isographic:/ˌaɪsoʊˈɡræfɪk/| adj. | 等图的，等形的 | Relating to or denoting a system of writing in which each character represents a morpheme.  Isographic writing systems are characterized by the use of logograms or ideograms, where each symbol directly represents a word or concept, rather than a sound.  This contrasts with alphabetic writing systems, which use symbols to represent sounds. |Example:  The ancient Egyptian hieroglyphic system is a well-known example of an isographic writing system.  埃及象形文字系统就是一个著名的等图文字系统的例子。|近义词: logographic, ideographic |反义词: alphabetic, phonetic
