cilia:/ˈsɪliə/| n.|纤毛，（复数）|Minute hair-like organelles, found on the surface of certain cells, that beat rhythmically to move fluids or particles.  They are involved in various biological processes, including locomotion, sensory perception, and clearing debris.|例句：The cilia in the respiratory tract help to remove mucus and debris.  呼吸道中的纤毛有助于清除粘液和碎屑。|The paramecium uses its cilia for movement.  草履虫利用纤毛运动。|近义词：microvilli (in some contexts)|反义词:None
