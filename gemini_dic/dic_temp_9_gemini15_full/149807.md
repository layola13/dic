paleobotany:/ˌpeɪliəˈbɒtəni/ | n. |古植物学 |The study of fossil plants.  Paleobotany uses fossils to reconstruct past plant life and ecosystems, contributing to our understanding of evolution, climate change, and the history of life on Earth. | Example:  Paleobotanical evidence suggests that flowering plants evolved much earlier than previously thought. 古植物学证据表明，开花植物的进化时间比之前认为的要早得多。|近义词：paleontology (but with a broader scope)|反义词：None
