destabilized:/ˌdiːˈsteɪbəlaɪzd/| v. |使不稳定；动摇；扰乱|To make something unstable or insecure; to cause something to lose its stability or equilibrium.  The political reforms destabilized the government.  | 近义词：unsettled, disrupted, undermined, weakened | 反义词：stabilized, strengthened, secured


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant changes destabilize the market. = 持续不断的变化使市场动荡不安。
Poor management destabilizes the company's performance. = 糟糕的管理使公司的业绩不稳定。


一般过去时:
The earthquake destabilized the region's infrastructure. = 地震使该地区的基建不稳定。
The unexpected news destabilized her emotional state. = 突如其来的消息扰乱了她的情绪。


现在完成时:
Years of conflict have destabilized the entire nation. = 多年的冲突已经使整个国家动荡不安。
The economic crisis has destabilized many businesses. = 经济危机已经使许多企业陷入困境。


过去完成时:
The government had destabilized the economy before the new policies were implemented. = 政府在实施新政策之前就已经动摇了经济。
The rumors had destabilized the stock market before the official announcement. = 在官方宣布之前，谣言已经扰乱了股市。


将来时:
The upcoming election will destabilize the political landscape. = 即将到来的选举将使政治格局动荡不安。
Further economic sanctions will destabilize the already fragile economy. = 进一步的经济制裁将使本已脆弱的经济更加不稳定。


虚拟语气:
If the government hadn't intervened, the situation would have destabilized further. = 如果政府不干预，情况会进一步恶化。
If they had acted sooner, they could have prevented the situation from destabilizing. = 如果他们早点采取行动，他们本可以阻止局势恶化。


被动语态:
The region was destabilized by the war. = 该地区因战争而动荡不安。
The government was destabilized by the opposition's actions. = 政府因反对派的行动而动荡不安。


疑问句:
What factors destabilized the peace process? = 哪些因素使和平进程不稳定？
How did the new legislation destabilize the industry? = 新法案是如何使该行业动荡不安的？


否定句:
The reforms did not destabilize the country as predicted. = 与预测不同，改革并没有使国家动荡不安。
The new technology did not destabilize the existing market. = 新技术并没有动摇现有的市场。
