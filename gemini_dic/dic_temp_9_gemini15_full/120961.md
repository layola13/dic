mischaracterize:/ˌmɪskærəktəraɪz/ | vt. |歪曲；误解；描写不准确 | To represent (someone or something) in a way that is inaccurate or unfair.  |例句：The media mischaracterized the politician's statement, leading to public outrage.媒体歪曲了这位政治家的声明，导致了公众的愤怒。

例句：He felt the article mischaracterized his intentions. 他觉得这篇文章歪曲了他的意图。


近义词：distort, misrepresent, misinterpret, falsify | 反义词：represent accurately, portray truthfully | 词性:动词

He often mischaracterizes his opponent's arguments. = 他经常歪曲对手的论点。

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:
The newspaper had mischaracterized the situation before the facts were known. = 在事实真相大白之前，报纸就歪曲了情况。

虚拟语气:

否定句:

词源:
mis- (错误地) + characterize (描述，刻画)  The prefix "mis-" indicates a wrong or bad action.  "Characterize" comes from the Middle French word "caracteriser"  and ultimately from the Greek "charaktēr" (mark, stamp, character).
