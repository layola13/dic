preexpressing:/ˌpriːɪkˈspresɪŋ/| v. |预先表达；提前表达|The act of expressing something before it actually happens or is fully developed.  It often involves anticipating future events or feelings and communicating them beforehand.  The word is relatively uncommon and often found in academic or technical contexts.|例句：The artist's preexpressing of his emotions in the painting's early sketches was evident.|近义词：prefiguring, foreshadowing, anticipating|反义词：post-expressing (not a common word)|动词
