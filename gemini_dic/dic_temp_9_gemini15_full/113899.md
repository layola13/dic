madrigal:/ˈmædrɪɡəl/ | n. | 牧歌；抒情歌曲 | A madrigal is a polyphonic vocal composition, usually unaccompanied, of the Renaissance period, characterized by its expressive and often pastoral or amorous nature.  It typically features several voices singing in counterpoint, creating a rich and harmonious sound.  |  Example: The choir sang a beautiful Renaissance madrigal.  合唱团演唱了一首美丽的文艺复兴时期牧歌。  | 近义词：song, ballad, chanson | 反义词：None (no direct antonym)
