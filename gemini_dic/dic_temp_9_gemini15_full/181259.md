reinstation:/ˌriːɪnˈsteɪʃən/| vt.|恢复职位；使恢复原状|To restore to a former position or state; to reinstate someone is to put them back in their job or position after they have been removed.|The company reinstated him after they realized their mistake. 公司意识到错误后恢复了他的职位。|近义词：restore, reinstate, reestablish|反义词：dismiss, remove, terminate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reinstates his employees after a period of suspension. 他在停职一段时间后恢复了员工的职位。
The government reinstates the old policy. 政府恢复了旧政策。


一般过去时:
They reinstated the old system last year. 他们去年恢复了旧制度。
The judge reinstated the verdict after the appeal. 上诉后法官恢复了判决。


现在完成时:
The court has reinstated the original decision. 法院已经恢复了最初的决定。
The principal has reinstated the student after the investigation. 校长在调查后恢复了该学生的学籍。


过去完成时:
They had reinstated the manager before the crisis deepened. 在危机加剧之前，他们已经恢复了经理的职位。
The committee had reinstated the rule before the meeting. 委员会在会议前已经恢复了该规定。


将来时:
The company will reinstate the workers once the strike is over. 一旦罢工结束，公司将恢复工人的工作。
The board will reinstate the funding next year. 董事会明年将恢复拨款。


虚拟语气:
If they had apologized, she would have reinstated them. 如果他们道歉了，她就会恢复他们的职位。
I wish the judge had reinstated my license. 我希望法官恢复我的执照。


被动语态:
He was reinstated to his position after the investigation. 调查后，他被恢复了职位。
The old rules were reinstated by the committee. 委员会恢复了旧规则。


疑问句:
Will they reinstate the program? 他们会恢复这个项目吗？
Has she been reinstated to her former position? 她是否被恢复到以前的职位？


否定句:
The company did not reinstate him after the incident. 事故后，公司没有恢复他的职位。
They refused to reinstate the old regulations. 他们拒绝恢复旧规章。
