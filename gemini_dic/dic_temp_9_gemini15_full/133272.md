nonexistentialisms: /ˌnɒnˌɛɡzɪsˈtɛnʃəlɪzəm/ | n. |非存在主义|The philosophical position that denies or questions the existence of essential properties or essences.  It often contrasts with essentialism, which holds that things have inherent, defining characteristics.  Nonexistentialism might explore the idea that identity is fluid and constructed rather than fixed and predetermined.  It is a relatively less common term and often appears in academic discussions of philosophy and ontology.|例句：The professor's lecture explored various philosophical positions, including nonexistentialisms and their implications for our understanding of self.|近义词：anti-essentialism, nominalism|反义词：essentialism
