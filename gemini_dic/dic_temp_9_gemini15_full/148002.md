overrigid:/ˌoʊvərˈrɪdʒɪd/| adj. |过于僵硬的，死板的 | excessively rigid; inflexible; lacking adaptability.  The overrigid rules stifled creativity.  |近义词：inflexible, unyielding, rigid, inflexible, stiff |反义词：flexible, adaptable, pliable


例句：

The overrigid structure of the company prevented it from adapting to the changing market.  公司的僵化结构使其无法适应不断变化的市场。

His overrigid adherence to the rules made him unpopular with his colleagues. 他过于死板地遵守规章制度，使他在同事中不受欢迎。


词源:

* **over-**:  prefix indicating excess or to a great degree. (表示过度或很大程度的)
* **rigid**:  adjective meaning stiff, inflexible, or unyielding. (形容词，意为僵硬的，不灵活的或不屈服的)  This word ultimately derives from Latin *rigidus*, meaning "stiff."


用法说明:

Overrigid is primarily used as an adjective to describe something that is excessively inflexible or stiff. It implies a negative connotation, suggesting that the rigidity is hindering progress, adaptability, or creativity.  It is often used to describe rules, systems, structures, or people.
