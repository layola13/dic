lackland:/ˈlæklænd/| adj.|缺乏土地的；无地可耕的|Lackland refers to someone or something lacking land or territory.  It is often used in historical contexts, particularly in relation to the surname of King John of England, who was known as "John Lackland" because he initially inherited less land than his brothers.|例句：King John, initially known as John Lackland, eventually became one of England's most powerful monarchs.|近义词：landless, propertyless|反义词：land-rich, wealthy
