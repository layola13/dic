icicle: /ˈaɪsɪkəl/| n. |冰柱|A pendent piece of ice formed by the freezing of dripping water.  Ice crystals adhere to and build upon each other, forming a long, tapering structure. Icicles are commonly seen hanging from roofs, trees, and other surfaces during cold weather.|例句：Sharp icicles hung from the eaves of the roof.  屋檐下垂下尖利的冰柱。

例句：The children were fascinated by the glistening icicles.孩子们对闪闪发光的冰柱很着迷。


近义词：ice dagger, ice spike|反义词：None (Antonyms are not readily applicable to a natural phenomenon like an icicle)|词性：名词


词源：源自古英语 *īsīcel, 由 īs (“ice”) 和 īcel (“icicle”) 组成。
