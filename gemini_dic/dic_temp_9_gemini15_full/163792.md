predemocracies: /ˌpriːdɪˈmɒkrəsiːz/ | n. | 前民主社会，民主之前的社会 | Societies or political systems existing before the establishment of democracy.  The term often refers to periods characterized by authoritarian rule, oligarchy, or other non-democratic forms of governance.  It highlights the historical context leading up to the adoption of democratic principles and institutions. |  Example:  Historians study predemocracies to understand the evolution of political thought and practice.  历史学家研究前民主社会以了解政治思想和实践的演变。  | 近义词: authoritarian regimes, autocracies, oligarchies | 反义词: democracies | 名词
