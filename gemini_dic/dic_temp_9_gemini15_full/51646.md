deviate:/ˈdiːvieɪt/|/ˈdiːviˌeɪt/| v.|偏离；背离；离题|To depart from an established course or norm; to stray; to diverge.|The train deviated from its usual route due to the storm. 火车由于暴风雨而偏离了原路线。 The speaker deviated from his prepared remarks. 演讲者偏离了他准备好的发言稿。|stray, diverge, digress|conform, adhere, comply|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deviates from the rules. 他违反规定。
The path deviates sharply to the left. 小路急转弯向左。


一般过去时:
The plane deviated from its planned course. 飞机偏离了预定航线。
She deviated from the recipe and added extra spices. 她偏离了食谱，加了额外的香料。


现在完成时:
The company has deviated significantly from its original goals. 公司已经严重偏离了其最初的目标。
He has deviated from the truth in his testimony. 他在证词中偏离了事实真相。


过去完成时:
They had deviated from the plan before realizing their mistake. 他们在意识到错误之前就已经偏离了计划。
The river had deviated its course after the flood. 河流在洪水过后改变了河道。


将来时:
The project will deviate from the budget if we don't control costs. 如果我们不控制成本，项目将超出预算。
The train will deviate from the schedule due to unexpected delays. 火车由于意外延误将延误行程。


虚拟语气:
If he deviated from the plan, he would be in trouble. 如果他偏离计划，他就会有麻烦。
I wish he hadn't deviated from the instructions. 我真希望他没有偏离指示。


被动语态:
The road was deviated due to construction. 道路因施工而改道。
The flight path was deviated because of bad weather. 由于天气恶劣，飞行路线发生了改变。


疑问句:
Does the path deviate at this point? 小路在这一点上是否偏离？
Did the car deviate from the highway? 汽车是否偏离了高速公路？


否定句:
The needle did not deviate from the zero mark. 指针没有偏离零刻度。
The results did not deviate significantly from expectations. 结果与预期没有显著偏差。


PHRASE->
