ionization:/ˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 电离；离子化 | The process of converting an atom or molecule into an ion by adding or removing charged particles.  It is a fundamental process in many areas of science and technology, including chemistry, physics, and materials science. | Example: The ionization of air molecules by lightning causes the characteristic crackling sound.  空气分子被闪电电离会发出特有的噼啪声。|近义词：ionisation (英式拼写)|反义词：neutralization|名词
