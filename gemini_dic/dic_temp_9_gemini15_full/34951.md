clocking-in: /ˈklɒkɪŋ ɪn/ | v. |打卡上班，签到|The act of recording one's arrival at work, typically by using a time clock.  This term often implies using a physical or digital system to register one's presence at the start of a workday.|例句：Employees are expected to clocking-in by 9 am. 员工应在上午 9 点前打卡上班。

例句：He forgot to clocking-in this morning and got a warning. 他今天早上忘记打卡上班，受到了警告。


近义词：signing in, punching in, registering|反义词：clocking-out, signing out


词源:  The word "clocking-in" is a compound of "clock" and "in."  "Clock" in this context refers to a time clock, a device used to record the time of arrival and departure of employees. "In" indicates the action of recording one's arrival.  The origin of the time clock itself traces back to the late 19th and early 20th centuries, a time of increasing industrialization and the need for precise tracking of employee hours.
