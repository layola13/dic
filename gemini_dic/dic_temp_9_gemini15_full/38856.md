confederacy:/kənˈfɛdərəsi/| n.|邦联，联盟，同谋集团|A confederacy is an alliance or league, especially of states or nations.  It can also refer to a secret group working together for a particular purpose, often illegal.|例句：The Confederate States of America formed a confederacy during the American Civil War. 美国内战期间，美利坚联盟国组成了一个邦联。|例句：The conspirators formed a secret confederacy to overthrow the government. 阴谋者们组成了一个秘密联盟来推翻政府。|近义词：alliance, league, federation, coalition|反义词：independence, separation, disunion|名词

词源:
The word "confederacy" comes from the Old French "confédération," ultimately deriving from the Latin "confoederatio," formed from "con-" (together) and "foederatio" (a league or treaty).  This reflects the core meaning of a union or alliance formed by agreement.
