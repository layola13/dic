nonpalatalization:/ˌnɒnˌpælətaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |非颚化；非软腭化|The process by which a sound loses its palatal articulation.  It refers to the phonetic change where a sound that was previously produced with the tongue near the hard palate (palatalization) is no longer produced in that manner.  This often involves a shift in the articulation point towards the front or back of the mouth.|The nonpalatalization of /k/ in certain dialects of English results in the pronunciation of "keep" as [kip] instead of [kʲip].|近义词：depalatalization|反义词：palatalization
