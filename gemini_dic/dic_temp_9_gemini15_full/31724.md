cheapen:/ˈtʃiːpən/ | vt. |使变廉价；贬低；降低…的价值 | To reduce the price or value of something; to make something seem less valuable or important. |例句：The mass production of the product cheapened it considerably. 大规模生产使该产品价格大幅下降。

例句：His constant boasting cheapened his achievements. 他不断的吹嘘贬低了他的成就。


近义词：devalue, depreciate, belittle, demean | 反义词：enhance, elevate, appreciate, value


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cheapens his own work by selling it so cheaply. = 他以如此低廉的价格出售自己的作品，贬低了自己的作品价值。
They cheapen the brand by using low-quality materials. = 他们使用劣质材料降低了品牌的价值。


一般过去时:
The scandal cheapened the reputation of the company. = 那次丑闻损害了公司的声誉。
The flood cheapened the value of the land. = 洪水降低了土地的价值。


现在完成时:
Mass production has cheapened many goods. = 大规模生产已经使许多商品价格下降。
His actions have cheapened the meaning of true heroism. = 他的行为已经贬低了真正英雄主义的意义。


过去完成时:
The government's policies had cheapened the currency before the crisis. = 政府的政策在危机爆发前已经使货币贬值。
They had cheapened the experience by rushing through it. = 他们仓促行事，贬低了这次经历的价值。


将来时:
The new technology will cheapen the cost of production. = 新技术将降低生产成本。
Increased competition will cheapen the price of this product. = 激烈的竞争将会降低这种产品的价格。


虚拟语气:
If they lowered their prices, they would cheapen their products. = 如果他们降低价格，他们就会使他们的产品贬值。
I wish they hadn't cheapened the ceremony with their inappropriate behavior. = 我真希望他们没有用不当的行为贬低这次仪式。


被动语态:
The product was cheapened by the influx of cheaper imports. = 该产品因大量廉价进口商品涌入而价格下降。
The brand's image was cheapened by the negative publicity. = 该品牌的形象因负面宣传而受损。


疑问句:
Does mass production always cheapen a product? = 大规模生产是否总是会使产品贬值？
Will this decision cheapen the company's image? = 这个决定会损害公司的形象吗？


否定句:
The high quality of the materials did not cheapen the product. = 材料的高质量并没有使产品贬值。
They refused to cheapen their standards to meet the market demand. = 他们拒绝为了满足市场需求而降低自己的标准。

词源:
源自中古英语 *chepen，意为“买卖，交易”。最终源自古英语 *ceapian，意为“买卖，交换”。  与cheap (廉价的) 词源相关。
