pedlers:/ˈpɛdlərz/| n.|小贩，流动商贩|A peddler is a person who travels from place to place selling goods, often small items.  They typically sell their wares directly to consumers, often at a lower price than established shops.|例句：The peddler sold colorful scarves and trinkets in the town square.  这个小贩在城镇广场出售色彩鲜艳的围巾和小饰品。

The old peddler carried his wares in a large pack on his back.  这位老小贩背着一个大包，里面装着他的货物。|近义词：vendor, hawker, itinerant merchant|反义词：retailer (in a fixed location)
