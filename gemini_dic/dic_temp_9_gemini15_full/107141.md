ladyship: /ˈleɪdiʃɪp/ | n. | 女士；夫人；（对贵妇的尊称）|  The term "ladyship" is a title of respect used to address or refer to a woman of noble rank, or more generally, a woman of high social standing. It is a more formal and respectful alternative to "madam" or "ma'am."  | Example: Her Ladyship requested a private carriage.  她的夫人生请一辆私人马车。  |近义词:madam, ma'am, mistress (in certain contexts) |反义词:  None directly opposite, but less formal terms such as "woman", "girl" could be considered less formal alternatives.
