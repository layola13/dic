petty:/ˈpɛti/| adj.|琐碎的；小气的；卑鄙的 |  Of little importance; trivial. Characterized by an excessive concern with minor details;  mean or ungenerous. |例句：He got upset over such petty things. 他为如此琐碎的事情而生气。

He's too petty to admit he was wrong. 他太小气了，不肯承认自己错了。


近义词：trivial, insignificant, trifling, mean, small-minded |反义词：important, significant, generous, magnanimous


词源:  From Old French *petis, from Latin *petitus, past participle of petere "to seek, to strive for, to attack".  The sense development is from "sought after, desired" to "small, insignificant" to "mean, ungenerous".


用法说明:

一般现在时:
His complaints are usually petty. = 他的抱怨通常是琐碎的。
She considers this a petty issue. = 她认为这是一个无关紧要的问题。


一般过去时:
The argument was about a petty disagreement. = 那场争论是关于一个微不足道的分歧。
He made a petty remark about her dress. = 他对她的衣服作了轻蔑的评论。


现在完成时:
He has always been petty with his money. = 他一直对钱很小气。
She has shown petty behavior toward her colleagues. = 她对同事表现出小气行为。


过去完成时:
They had already discussed petty details before the meeting. = 会议前他们已经讨论过一些琐碎的细节。
He had been petty towards his brother for years. = 他多年来一直对他兄弟很小气。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
