semiconductor:/ˈsɛmɪkənˈdʌktər/ | n. | 半导体 | A substance whose electrical conductivity is intermediate between that of a conductor and an insulator; its conductivity increases with temperature.  Semiconductors are essential components in modern electronics, used in transistors, integrated circuits, and many other devices. | Example: Silicon is a common semiconductor used in computer chips.  硅是一种常用的用于计算机芯片的半导体。 |近义词:  None (it's a specific class of material) |反义词: conductor, insulator |

词源:
The word "semiconductor" is a combination of "semi-" (meaning "half" or "partly") and "conductor" (a substance that allows electricity to flow easily).  It reflects the intermediate conductivity between conductors and insulators.
