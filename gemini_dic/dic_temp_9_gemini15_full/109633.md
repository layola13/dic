letting:/ˈlɛtɪŋ/| v.|允许，让；出租；泄露|The act of allowing something to happen or someone to do something; the act of renting out property; the act of revealing something that was previously secret.|例句：Letting the cat out of the bag revealed the surprise.  泄露秘密揭开了惊喜的面纱。|例句：The letting of the apartment was handled by a real estate agent.  公寓的出租由房地产经纪人处理。|近义词：permitting, allowing, renting, revealing|反义词：prohibiting, forbidding, concealing


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He lets his children watch TV after dinner. = 他允许孩子们晚饭后看电视。
She lets her apartment to students. = 她把公寓租给学生。

一般过去时:
They let me borrow their car. = 他们让我借他们的车。
The landlord let the property for a good price. = 房东以好价钱把房产租了出去。

现在完成时:
We have let the dog off its leash. = 我们已经把狗从绳索上解开了。
They have let the news slip to the press. = 他们已经向媒体透露了这个消息。

过去完成时:
She had let her emotions get the better of her. = 她已经让自己的情绪控制了她。
They had let the opportunity slip away. = 他们已经错失了机会。

将来时:
I will let you know as soon as I have the information. = 我一得到信息就会告诉你。
We will be letting the property in the spring. = 我们将在春天出租房产。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would let him go. = 如果我是你，我会让他走。
I wish I had let her know earlier. = 我真希望我早点让她知道。

被动语态:
The dog was let out of its cage. = 狗被放出了笼子。
The apartment was let quickly. = 公寓很快就被租出去了。

疑问句:
Will you let me come with you? = 你会让我和你一起去吗？
Are they letting anyone else into the club? = 他们还让其他人进入俱乐部吗？

否定句:
She didn't let him see the letter. = 她没让他看信。
They won't let us enter without tickets. = 他们不会让我们没票就进去。


PHRASE->
letting go = 放手
letting down = 辜负
letting in = 允许进入
letting off = 放过
letting slip = 无意中透露
