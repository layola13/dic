mandoline:/ˈmændəˌliːn/ | n. |曼陀林（一种拨弦乐器）|A mandolin is a stringed musical instrument, typically with a pear-shaped body and a fretted neck, played by plucking the strings with a plectrum.  It is known for its bright, ringing tone. |She played a beautiful melody on her mandolin. 她用曼陀林演奏了一首优美的旋律。|lute, ukulele|  (无明显反义词)|名词

词源:

The word "mandoline" comes from the Italian word *mandolino*, a diminutive of *mandora*, ultimately derived from the Greek word *pandoura*, a type of ancient lute.  The instrument itself evolved over centuries from various lute-like instruments in the Mediterranean region.
