predispositions:/ˌpriːdɪsˌpəˈzɪʃənz/ | n. | 倾向；素质；预先倾向 | A predisposition is an inclination or tendency towards a particular behavior or condition.  It often refers to a genetic or inherent vulnerability to something, but can also be learned or developed through experience.  It suggests a likelihood or increased susceptibility, not a certainty. | Example:  He had a predisposition to heart disease due to family history. 他由于家族病史而有患心脏病的倾向。

His childhood experiences gave him a predisposition towards anxiety. 他童年的经历使他容易焦虑。


近义词：tendency, inclination, proneness, susceptibility, vulnerability | 反义词：resistance, immunity, imperviousness

一般过去时:

Her predispositions towards risk-taking were evident in her youth. = 她冒险的倾向在她年轻时就显而易见。


现在完成时:

Her predispositions towards helping others have resulted in a rewarding career. = 她助人为乐的倾向让她拥有了有意义的事业。


过去完成时:

将来时:
His genetic predispositions may increase his risk of developing diabetes. = 他的基因倾向可能会增加他患糖尿病的风险。
Her predispositions towards leadership will likely lead to a successful career. = 她对领导的倾向很可能会让她拥有成功的事业。


虚拟语气:
If he didn't have a predisposition to laziness, he would be more successful. = 如果他不懒惰，他会更成功。(This example uses "predisposition" in a slightly different sense, referring to a character trait)
If she hadn't had a predisposition to anxiety, she would have handled the situation better. = 如果她没有焦虑倾向，她会更好地处理这种情况。


被动语态:
The patient's predisposition to allergies was considered during treatment. = 在治疗过程中考虑了患者的过敏倾向。
His predisposition to depression was diagnosed by a specialist. = 他对抑郁症的倾向被专家诊断出来。


疑问句:

否定句:
