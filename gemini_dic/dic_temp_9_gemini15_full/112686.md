lowlanders:/ˈloʊlændərz/| n. |低地居民；平原居民|People who live in low-lying areas, typically referring to a region in contrast to highlands or mountains.  The term often carries cultural and historical connotations, referring to a specific group of people with shared traditions and possibly a distinct identity from those in higher elevations.| 例句：The lowlanders celebrated their unique harvest festival, different from the highland traditions. 低地居民庆祝他们独特的丰收节，这与高地传统不同。 The conflict between the highlanders and the lowlanders lasted for centuries. 高地居民和低地居民之间的冲突持续了几个世纪。|近义词：plain dwellers, valley dwellers|反义词：highlanders
