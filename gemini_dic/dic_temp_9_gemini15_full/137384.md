nontemptation: /ˌnɒntemptˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 不诱惑，无诱惑力 | The lack of temptation; the state of not being tempted.  It refers to a situation or circumstance where there's no urge or desire to do something considered wrong or inappropriate.  | Example:  The nontemptation to cheat on the exam was due to his strong moral compass.  考试作弊的诱惑对他来说不存在，这源于他坚定的道德原则。 | 近义词: resistance to temptation, lack of enticement, immunity to temptation | 反义词: temptation, enticement, allure


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense considerations are not applicable.  The usage revolves around its function in a sentence as a noun.

Example 1:  The monk found solace in the nontemptation of worldly pleasures.  这位僧侣在不受世俗享乐诱惑中找到了慰藉。

Example 2:  Her nontemptation to spend impulsively saved her from financial ruin. 她没有冲动消费的诱惑，这使她免于财务破产。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "nontemptation."  The word itself is relatively uncommon and often replaced with phrases expressing the same idea.  For example, instead of "He showed great nontemptation," one might say "He strongly resisted temptation" or "He was immune to temptation."
