fecund:/ˈfiːkʌnd/| adj.|多产的；肥沃的；富饶的 |Fruitful; productive; abundantly yielding.  The fecund land produced bountiful harvests.  |The fertile land produced abundant crops. Barren, sterile, unproductive |形容词


例句：

The fecund river valley supported a large population.  肥沃的河谷养活了大量人口。

Her fecund imagination produced a wealth of creative ideas. 她丰富的想象力产生了大量的创意。


近义词：

fertile, productive, prolific, fruitful, teeming

反义词：

barren, sterile, unproductive, infertile, arid


词源:

From Latin *fecundus, from *fec-, past participle stem of facere "to make," + -undus, a suffix of adjectives denoting capacity or tendency.  The word's roots trace back to the idea of "making" or "producing" abundantly.


用法说明：

fecund primarily functions as an adjective, describing things capable of producing abundantly. This can refer to:

* **Biological fertility:**  Referring to the ability to produce offspring abundantly (e.g., a fecund animal, a fecund woman).
* **Agricultural productivity:** Describing land or soil that is very fertile and produces plentiful crops.
* **Creative abundance:**  Describing a person's mind that is full of ideas, or a work that is rich in detail or ideas.


需要注意的是，虽然fecund可以指人的生育能力，但使用时应谨慎，避免造成冒犯。  在现代英语中，更常用的词语是fertile或prolific来描述人的生育能力。  fecund更常用于描述土地、动物或创意的丰富性。
