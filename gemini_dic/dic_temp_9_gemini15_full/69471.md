feisty: /ˈfiːsti/ | adj. | 泼辣的；好斗的；精力充沛的 |  Energetic, spirited, and sometimes aggressive.  A feisty person is lively, determined, and not easily intimidated. They might be quick-tempered or argumentative, but their energy and passion are usually viewed positively. |

例句：

The feisty puppy nipped at my heels.  = 那只泼辣的小狗咬了我的脚后跟。

She's a feisty negotiator, always getting the best deals. = 她是一位泼辣的谈判者，总是能达成最优交易。


近义词：spirited, spirited, lively, spirited, vivacious, fiery, gutsy, tenacious, mettlesome


反义词：submissive, docile, meek, timid, mild-mannered


词源:  The word "feisty" is believed to have originated in the early 20th century, possibly from a dialectal variant of "fist," referring to a person who is quick to fight or use their fists.  Its exact etymology remains debated, but the connection to fighting and spiritedness is clear.
