irredeemably:/ˌɪrɪˈdiːməbli/| adj.|不可挽回地；无法补救地|Beyond redemption; incapable of being rectified or improved.  It suggests a state of permanent damage or ruin.|The company's reputation was irredeemably damaged by the scandal.  这次丑闻不可挽回地损害了公司的声誉。| hopelessly, irretrievably, incurably| redeemable, recoverable, repairable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The damage is irredeemably done. = 损坏已无法挽回。
He is irredeemably committed to this project. = 他已无法挽回地投入到这个项目中。


一般过去时:
The situation was irredeemably lost. = 局势已无法挽回。
His reputation was irredeemably tarnished. = 他的名誉已无法挽回地受损。


现在完成时:
The situation has become irredeemably compromised. = 局势已变得无法挽回。
They have irredeemably alienated their supporters. = 他们已无法挽回地疏远了他们的支持者。


过去完成时:
By then, the situation had become irredeemably broken. = 到那时，情况已无法挽回地破裂了。
He had irredeemably damaged his credit rating. = 他已无法挽回地损害了他的信用评级。


将来时:
The damage will be irredeemably done by then. = 到那时，损坏将无法挽回。
His career will be irredeemably ruined if this continues. = 如果这种情况持续下去，他的职业生涯将被彻底毁掉。


虚拟语气:
If he had acted sooner, the damage might not have been irredeemably done. = 如果他早点采取行动，损害可能就不会无法挽回。
I wish I hadn't made that mistake; it has irredeemably damaged our relationship. = 我真希望我没有犯那个错误；它已经无法挽回地损害了我们的关系。


被动语态:
The situation was irredeemably damaged by his actions. = 他的行为使情况无法挽回地受损。
The project was considered irredeemably flawed from the start. = 从一开始，这个项目就被认为是无法挽回地存在缺陷。


疑问句:
Is the situation irredeemably lost? = 局势是否已无法挽回？
Is his reputation irredeemably damaged? = 他的名誉是否已无法挽回地受损？


否定句:
The situation is not irredeemably lost; there is still hope. = 局势并非无法挽回；仍然有希望。
His actions, while serious, did not irredeemably damage his career. = 他的行为虽然严重，但并没有无法挽回地损害他的职业生涯。
