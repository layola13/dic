ne'er-do-well:/ˈnɛːrduːˈwɛl/| n. |不务正业的人，游手好闲的人 |A ne'er-do-well is a person who is lazy and avoids work. They are often irresponsible and unreliable.  The term carries a strong connotation of disapproval. |例句：He's a ne'er-do-well who spends his days loafing around.|近义词：good-for-nothing, wastrel, loafer, idler|反义词：hard worker, achiever
