preter:/ˈpriːtər/ | adj. |过去的，以前的| Relating to or denoting the past tense or a past time.  It is often used in grammatical contexts to describe the past tense of verbs.|The preterite tense describes actions completed in the past. |近义词：past, bygone |反义词：future, present


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He preaches every Sunday. = 他每周日布道。
She works at a bank. = 她在银行工作。


一般过去时:
He preached last Sunday. = 他上周日布道。
She worked at a bank before she retired. = 她退休前在银行工作。


现在完成时:
He has preached many times this year. = 他今年已经布道很多次了。
She has worked hard all her life. = 她一生都努力工作。


过去完成时:
He had preached before the king arrived. = 国王到达之前他已经布道了。
She had worked there for ten years before she was promoted. = 她升职之前在那里工作了十年。


将来时:
He will preach next Sunday. = 他下周日将布道。
She will work on that project tomorrow. = 她明天将从事那个项目。


虚拟语气:
If he had preached more clearly, people would have understood. = 如果他布道更清晰一些，人们就会理解。
If she had worked harder, she would have succeeded. = 如果她更努力工作，她就会成功。


被动语态:
The sermon was preached by the pastor. = 这篇布道是由牧师讲的。
The report was worked on by several people. = 这份报告是由几个人完成的。


疑问句:
Did he preach yesterday? = 他昨天布道了吗？
Did she work late last night? = 她昨晚加班了吗？


否定句:
He did not preach yesterday. = 他昨天没有布道。
She did not work on the weekend. = 她周末没有工作。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "preter" as it's primarily a grammatical term.  The usage examples above illustrate its function within phrases.
