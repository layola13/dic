preobserves:/ˌpriːəbˈzɜːrvz/| v.| 预先观察；事先观察 | To observe or examine something beforehand.  It implies a careful and often systematic observation made in advance of some other event or action. |例句：The scientist preobserves the specimen before conducting the experiment. 科学家在进行实验之前预先观察了样本。

The team preobserves the weather patterns before launching the rocket.  团队在发射火箭之前预先观察了天气模式。| 近义词：foresee, anticipate, pre-examine | 反义词：ignore, overlook, neglect |动词
