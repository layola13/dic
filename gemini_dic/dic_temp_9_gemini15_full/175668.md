ranty:/ˈrænti/| adj.|喋喋不休的，易怒的，激烈的|Characterized by ranting; tending to rant.  A ranty person is someone who talks angrily and at length.|例句：He delivered a ranty speech filled with accusations. 他发表了一篇充满指控的激昂演讲。

例句：Her ranty email made me feel uncomfortable.  她的喋喋不休的邮件让我感到不舒服。


近义词：angry, irate, furious, vociferous,  long-winded,  exclamatory


反义词：calm, placid, serene, tranquil, composed


词源:  Derived from "rant," meaning to speak in a loud, angry, or uncontrolled way.  The suffix "-y" adds an adjectival quality, meaning "full of" or "characterized by."  The word ultimately traces back to Old French *ranter, of uncertain origin, possibly from a Germanic source.
