scofflaw:/ˈskɒflɔː/| n. |蔑视法律者，违法者 |A person who flouts the law, especially habitually and openly.  |例句：He was branded a scofflaw for repeatedly ignoring parking regulations. 他因屡次无视停车规定而被贴上蔑视法律者的标签。
例句：The company was fined heavily for being a scofflaw and polluting the river. 这家公司因蔑视法律污染河流而被处以巨额罚款。|近义词：lawbreaker, outlaw, delinquent, offender |反义词：law-abiding citizen


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scoffs at the law. = 他蔑视法律。
She scoffs at rules and regulations. = 她蔑视规章制度。


一般过去时:
They scoffed at the speed limit. = 他们无视超速限制。

现在完成时:
He has scoffed at the law for years. = 他多年来一直蔑视法律。

过去完成时:

She had scoffed at the rules, leading to her downfall. = 她无视规则，最终导致了她的失败。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "scofflaw" is a blend of "scoff" (to mock or scorn) and "law".  It was coined in the early 20th century, likely in the United States, to describe those who openly and contemptuously disregard the law.
