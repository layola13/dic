proromanticism: /ˌproʊroʊˈmænɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 前浪漫主义 |  A period in the late 18th century, preceding the Romantic movement, characterized by a growing interest in the sublime, the irrational, and the emotional, often manifested in literature, art, and philosophy. It foreshadowed the full-blown Romantic movement but lacked its fully developed characteristics. | The Sturm und Drang movement in German literature is often considered a key example of proromanticism.  Its emphasis on emotion and individualism paved the way for the Romantic era. | 近义词: pre-romanticism, proto-romanticism | 反义词: N/A
