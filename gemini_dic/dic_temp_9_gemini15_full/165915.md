preorder:/ˈpriːɔːrdər/| n. v.|预购，预定；先期订购|To order (something) before it is available for sale or delivery.  A pre-order is an order placed for a product or service before it is released or available.|例句：I preordered the new iPhone. 我预购了新款iPhone。

例句：Many fans preordered tickets to the concert months in advance. 许多粉丝提前几个月预购了演唱会的门票。


近义词：pre-purchase, advance order|反义词：postorder, backorder|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They preorder their groceries online every week. = 他们每周都在网上预购杂货。
We preorder books from our favorite authors. = 我们预购我们最喜欢的作家的书。

一般过去时:
She preordered a limited edition watch. = 她预购了一块限量版手表。
He preordered the game and received it on launch day. = 他预购了游戏，并在发行当天收到了。

现在完成时:
I have preordered the new album. = 我已经预购了新专辑。
They have preordered tickets for the show and are excited. = 他们已经预购了演出的门票并且很兴奋。

过去完成时:
We had preordered a table at the restaurant, so we didn't have to wait. = 我们已经预订了餐厅的桌子，所以不用等。
She had preordered the cake for her daughter's birthday party. = 她已经为女儿的生日派对预订了蛋糕。

将来时:
I will preorder the next book in the series. = 我将预购该系列的下一本书。
They will preorder the tickets as soon as they go on sale. = 门票一开售，他们就会预购。

虚拟语气:
If I had more money, I would preorder that luxury car. = 如果我有更多钱，我会预购那辆豪华轿车。
I wish I had preordered the concert tickets earlier; they are sold out now. = 我真希望我早点预购演唱会门票；现在已经卖完了。

被动语态:
The new video game was preordered by millions of fans. = 数百万粉丝预购了这款新的电子游戏。
The limited edition shoes were preordered within hours of announcement. = 限量版鞋子在发布后的几个小时内就被预购一空。

疑问句:
Have you preordered the new phone? = 你预购了新手机吗？
Did they preorder the tickets for the event? = 他们预购了活动的票吗？

否定句:
I didn't preorder the album; I'll buy it in the store. = 我没有预购专辑；我会在商店买。
They didn't preorder the tickets and missed out on the early bird discount. = 他们没有预购门票，错过了早鸟折扣。
