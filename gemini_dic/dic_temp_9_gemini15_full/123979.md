mormondom:/ˈmɔːrməndɒm/| n. |摩门教世界；摩门教徒社会|The term "Mormondom" refers to the social and cultural world of members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (often called Mormons). It encompasses the beliefs, practices, institutions, and communities associated with Mormonism.  The term can be used neutrally, but it can also carry either positive or negative connotations depending on the speaker's perspective.|Example: Understanding Mormondom requires studying its history, theology, and social structures.  理解摩门教世界需要研究其历史、神学和社会结构。|近义词：Mormon culture, Mormon community|反义词：None directly, but could be contrasted with non-Mormon societies or cultures.
