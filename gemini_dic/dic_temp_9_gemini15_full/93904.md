immortals:/ɪˈmɔːrtəlz/| n.|不朽者；神仙；永生者|People or beings who are immortal; often used in reference to gods or mythical creatures possessing eternal life.|Example: The immortals of Mount Olympus ruled over the ancient Greeks. 奥林匹斯山上的众神统治着古希腊人。|近义词：gods, deities, the undying|反义词：mortals, the dead

词源:
The word "immortals" comes from the prefix "im-" (not) + "mortal" (subject to death).  Therefore, it literally means "not subject to death."  The root "mort" comes from the Latin "mors," meaning "death."
