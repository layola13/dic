indulgence:/ɪnˈdʌldʒəns/| n.|纵容；放纵；沉溺；嗜好|The act of indulging or giving way to one's desires or impulses; excessive self-gratification. It can refer to a specific instance of indulging or a general tendency to do so. |例句：Her indulgence in chocolate was legendary.  她的巧克力嗜好是出了名的。

例句：He showed great indulgence towards his spoiled child. 他对被宠坏的孩子非常纵容。


近义词：pampering, gratification, self-indulgence, overindulgence|反义词：self-denial, abstinence, restraint, discipline|词性：名词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
His indulgence in video games is a problem. = 他沉迷于电子游戏是个问题。
Her daily indulgence of a large dessert is excessive. = 她每天吃大量的甜点太过分了。


一般过去时:
Their indulgence at the buffet was obvious. = 他们在自助餐上的放纵很明显。

现在完成时:

She has shown considerable indulgence towards her grandchildren. = 她对她的孙子孙女表现出了相当的纵容。


过去完成时:
