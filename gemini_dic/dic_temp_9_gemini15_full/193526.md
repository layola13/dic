self-abuser: /ˌsɛlf əˈbjuːzər/ | n. | 自虐者，性自虐者 | A person who engages in self-abuse, often referring to sexual self-abuse or other forms of self-harm.  The term is often used in a clinical or legal context and can be considered stigmatizing. | 例句：The therapist worked with the self-abuser to develop healthier coping mechanisms.  治疗师帮助这位自虐者培养更健康的应对机制。

The term is rarely used in a positive context and should be approached with sensitivity.


近义词：self-harmer, abuser (in the context of self-abuse) | 反义词：N/A (no direct antonym, but implies the opposite of self-care and healthy behavior)


词性: 名词

词源:
The word is a compound of "self," meaning oneself, and "abuser," derived from the verb "abuse," meaning to misuse or maltreat.  The term reflects the act of harming oneself.  The specific meaning related to sexual self-abuse developed over time as clinical understanding of the behavior evolved.
