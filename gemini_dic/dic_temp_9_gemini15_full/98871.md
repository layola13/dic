intended:/ɪnˈtɛndɪd/| adj.|预期的；打算的；计划中的|Something that is intended is planned or meant to be a particular way.  It refers to a purpose or goal that someone has in mind.|例句：The intended recipient of the letter was his mother. 这封信的收信人是他的母亲。

例句：The intended use of this product is for cleaning purposes only. 此产品的预期用途仅限于清洁。


近义词：planned, designed, meant, purposed|反义词：unintended, accidental, unplanned|词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The intended outcome is a successful project. = 预期的结果是一个成功的项目。
His intended action is to leave early. = 他打算早点离开。


一般过去时:
The intended destination was Paris, but we ended up in Rome. = 预定的目的地是巴黎，但我们最终到了罗马。
Her intended gift was a surprise. = 她打算送的礼物是一个惊喜。


现在完成时:
The intended changes have been implemented. = 预期的更改已实施。
Everything has gone according to the intended plan. = 一切都按计划进行。


过去完成时:
The intended meeting had been cancelled due to the storm. = 由于暴风雨，预定的会议已被取消。
I had intended to go, but I fell ill. = 我本来打算去的，但我病倒了。


将来时:
The intended launch date is next month. = 预计的发布日期是下个月。
We intend to finish the project by the end of the year. = 我们打算在年底前完成项目。


虚拟语气:
I had intended to help, but I was too busy. = 我本来打算帮忙的，但我太忙了。
If I had known, I would have changed my intended course of action. = 如果我知道的话，我会改变我的计划。


被动语态:
The intended message was not clearly conveyed. = 预期的信息没有被清晰地传达。
The project was intended to improve efficiency. = 该项目旨在提高效率。


疑问句:
Was that the intended effect? = 那是预期的效果吗？
Is this the intended meaning? = 这是预期的意思吗？


否定句:
The intended result was not achieved. = 没有达到预期的结果。
That was not my intended meaning. = 那不是我想要表达的意思。
