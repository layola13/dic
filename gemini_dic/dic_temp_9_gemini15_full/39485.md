connotation:/ˌkɒnəˈteɪʃən/ /ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃən/ | n. |内涵，含义；暗示 |The word "home" has warm connotations for most people.  这个词“家”对大多数人来说有着温暖的含义。| implication, suggestion, innuendo | denotation |名词


例句：

The word "home" has warm connotations for most people.  这个词“家”对大多数人来说有着温暖的含义。

The connotations of the word "cheap" are negative.  “便宜”这个词的含义是负面的。


近义词：

implication, suggestion, innuendo, implication, overtone


反义词：

denotation (字面意义)


词源：

From Middle French *connotation, from Latin connotare "to mark in addition," from com- "together" + notare "to mark."


用法说明：

Connotation refers to the extra meanings, feelings, or ideas that are associated with a word or phrase, beyond its literal definition (denotation). These associations can be cultural, emotional, or personal, and can vary depending on context and individual interpretation.  内涵指的是与词语或短语相关的额外含义、情感或想法，超越其字面定义（外延）。这些关联可能是文化上的、情感上的或个人的，并且可能因上下文和个人解释而异。
