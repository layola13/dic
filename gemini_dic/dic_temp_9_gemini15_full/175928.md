rationalising: /ˈræʃənəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使合理化；解释…的理由；使合理 | To make or attempt to make (something) seem reasonable.  | The company rationalised its operations by closing down several factories. 公司通过关闭几家工厂使其运营合理化。 | justify, explain, vindicate | illogical, unreasonable | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rationalises his behaviour by claiming it was necessary. = 他声称这样做是必要的，以此来为自己行为辩解。
She rationalises her spending habits by saying she deserves it. = 她声称自己应得的，以此来为自己的消费习惯辩解。


一般过去时:
They rationalised the production process to increase efficiency. = 他们使生产流程合理化以提高效率。
He rationalised his decision after facing criticism. = 他在面临批评后为自己的决定辩解。


现在完成时:
The government has rationalised its welfare system. = 政府已经使其福利制度合理化。
She has rationalised her approach to the problem. = 她已经使她解决问题的方法合理化。


过去完成时:
They had rationalised their investment strategy before the market crash. = 在市场崩盘之前，他们已经使其投资策略合理化。
He had rationalised his fears before confronting the issue. = 在面对这个问题之前，他已经使其恐惧合理化。


将来时:
The company will rationalise its workforce to cut costs. = 公司将精简员工以降低成本。
She will rationalise her argument before presenting it. = 她将在陈述她的论点之前使其合理化。


虚拟语气:
If I rationalised my spending, I would have more savings. = 如果我使我的支出合理化，我就会有更多的储蓄。
I wish I had rationalised my investment choices earlier. = 我希望我早点使我的投资选择合理化。


被动语态:
The company's operations were rationalised to improve profitability. = 公司的运营被合理化以提高盈利能力。
His decision was rationalised by his colleagues. = 他的决定得到了同事们的辩解。


疑问句:
How can we rationalise this unexpected outcome? = 我们如何解释这个意外的结果？
Have you rationalised your reasons for leaving the job? = 你是否已经为离开工作找到了合理的理由？


否定句:
He didn't rationalise his actions; he simply apologised. = 他没有为自己的行为辩解；他只是道歉了。
She couldn't rationalise the illogical decision made by the management. = 她无法解释管理层做出的不合逻辑的决定。


PHRASE->

rationalise a decision = 为决定辩护
rationalise the process = 使流程合理化
