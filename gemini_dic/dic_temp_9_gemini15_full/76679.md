galvanographic:/ˌɡælvnəˈɡræfɪk/ | adj. | 电镀的；电刻的 | Relating to or denoting the process of producing relief prints or engravings by electrotyping.  The process uses electricity to deposit a layer of metal onto a mold, creating a durable printing plate. | The museum showcased a collection of beautifully preserved galvanographic prints from the 19th century.  |  electrotyped, electrographic |  (No direct antonym, but a contrasting term could be "hand-engraved") |  形容词

词源:

The word "galvanographic" is a combination of "galvan" (from Luigi Galvani, the Italian scientist who discovered animal electricity) and "graphic" (from Greek "graphein," meaning "to write").  Therefore, it literally means "related to writing or printing using electricity."
