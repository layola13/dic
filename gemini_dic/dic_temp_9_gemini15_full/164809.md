/ˈpriːfiksɪz/ | n. | 前缀 | Prefixes are groups of letters placed at the beginning of a word to modify its meaning.  They are often used to create new words or to change the grammatical function of a word. For example, "un-" is a prefix that means "not," as in "unhappy."  | The prefix "pre-" means "before" or "in front of." | 近义词：affix, word part | 反义词：suffix
