malignify:/məˈlɪɡnɪfaɪ/ | vt. | 诽谤；中伤；使恶化 | To speak evil of; to defame; to represent in a bad light; to make or become malignant.  | 例句：He tried to malignify his opponent's character during the debate. 他试图在辩论中诽谤对手的品格。 | 例句：The constant negativity malignified the team's morale.  持续的消极情绪恶化了团队士气。| 近义词：defame, slander, vilify, smear | 反义词：praise, commend, extol


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He malignifies his rivals constantly. = 他不断地诽谤他的对手。
The media malignifies the situation. = 媒体歪曲了事态。


一般过去时:
She malignified him in public. = 她在公众场合诽谤他。
The article malignified the company's reputation. = 这篇文章损害了公司的声誉。


现在完成时:
They have malignified his good name. = 他们已经诽谤了他的名誉。
The rumors have malignified the truth. = 谣言已经歪曲了真相。


过去完成时:
He had malignified her for years. = 他多年来一直诽谤她。
The press had malignified the facts before the trial. = 在审判之前，媒体已经歪曲了事实。


将来时:
They will malignify her character in the upcoming election. = 他们在即将到来的选举中将诽谤她的品格。
The opposition party will malignify the government's policies. = 反对党将诽谤政府的政策。


虚拟语气:
If he malignified her, she would sue him. = 如果他诽谤她，她会起诉他。
I wish they hadn't malignified the scientist's work. = 我希望他们没有诽谤这位科学家的工作。


被动语态:
His reputation was malignified by the media. = 他的名声被媒体诽谤了。
The truth was malignified by the biased reporting. = 真相被有偏见的报道歪曲了。


疑问句:
Did he malignify her intentionally? = 他故意诽谤她吗？
Were the accusations malignifying the innocent? = 这些指控是在诽谤无辜者吗？


否定句:
He did not malignify her; he simply stated the facts. = 他没有诽谤她；他只是陈述了事实。
The report did not malignify the company's performance. = 报告并没有歪曲公司的业绩。


PHRASE->
malignify someone's reputation = 诽谤某人的名誉
malignify the truth = 歪曲真相
malignify the facts = 歪曲事实
malignify someone's character = 诽谤某人的品格
malignify a situation = 恶化局势


词源:
From Middle English *malignien, from Old French *malignier, from maligne (feminine of maligne) "malignant, evil," from Latin malignus "evil, malignant."  The suffix "-ify" is from Old French -ifier, from Latin -ficare "to make."
