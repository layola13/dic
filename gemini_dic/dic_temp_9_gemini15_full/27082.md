calibrating:/ˈkælɪbreɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 校准；校正；测定…的口径 | To adjust (an instrument or apparatus) so that it shows accurate readings, or to determine the actual value of something by comparing it to a standard.  | 例句：The technician is calibrating the instruments before the experiment. 技术员在实验前正在校准仪器。 | 例句：We need to calibrate the scale to ensure accurate weight measurements. 我们需要校准秤以确保重量测量的准确性。 | 近义词：adjusting, rectifying, standardizing | 反义词：misaligning, deranging

词源:
源自于拉丁语 "calibrare"，意思是“确定…的口径”。  最终来自古法语 "calibre" (口径)，这又源自阿拉伯语 "qālib" (模具,模型)。
