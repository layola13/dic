paleotype:/ˈpeɪliəʊtaɪp/| n. |古音标法，古语音学符号系统|A system of phonetic notation devised by Alexander John Ellis in the 19th century to represent the sounds of various languages, especially English dialects.  It was an attempt to create a more precise and consistent system than existing orthographies.  It used many symbols not found in standard alphabets.|The paleotype transcription of the word "bath" varies depending on the dialect.|近义词：phonetic transcription, phonetic notation|反义词：standard spelling
