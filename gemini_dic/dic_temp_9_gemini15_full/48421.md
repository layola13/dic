deducibility:/ˌdɪˌdjuːsəˈbɪləti/| n. |可推论性，可推导性 |The quality of being deducible; the ability to be deduced or inferred from other information.  It refers to the logical relationship between premises and a conclusion, where the conclusion necessarily follows from the premises.  |Example: The high level of deducibility in the argument made it easy to follow the reasoning.  The deducibility of the theorem from the axioms was demonstrated by the mathematician.|近义词: inferability, derivability |反义词:  uninferability, inexplicability
