collationer: /ˌkɒləˈeɪʃənər/ | n. | 校对员，整理员 | A person who collates; one who assembles things in order.  A collationer is responsible for checking the accuracy and completeness of a document or set of documents, often by comparing multiple versions or sources. They ensure that all pages are in the correct order, that there are no missing pages or sections, and that the text is consistent throughout.  | Example: The publishing house hired a meticulous collationer to ensure the accuracy of the new textbook. 出版社雇佣了一位细心的校对员来确保新教科书的准确性。|近义词：proofreader, editor, verifier | 反义词：None specifically, but antonyms could describe actions opposite to collation, such as disorganizer or destroyer.
