demagoged:/ˈdɛməɡɑːɡd/| v. |煽动；蛊惑；利用群众情绪进行政治操纵 | To use popular prejudices and false claims to gain power.  It implies manipulative and dishonest rhetoric, often exploiting emotions rather than reason. | The politician demagoged the crowd into a frenzy of anger against the opposing party.  政治家煽动人群对反对党产生愤怒狂潮。|近义词：incite, agitate, manipulate, inflame |反义词：reason, persuade, conciliate

一般现在时:
He demagogues the public with his inflammatory speeches. 他用煽动性言论蛊惑公众。
The speaker demagogues the audience with promises of easy solutions. 演讲者向听众承诺轻松解决问题以蛊惑他们。


一般过去时:
The dictator demagoged the population into supporting his war. 独裁者煽动人民支持他的战争。
She demagoged the voters with misleading information about her opponent. 她用关于对手的误导性信息来蛊惑选民。


现在完成时:
He has demagoged his way to power by exploiting people's fears. 他通过利用人们的恐惧而煽动上位。
The media has demagoged the public opinion on this issue. 媒体在这个问题上煽动公众舆论。


过去完成时:
They had demagoged the masses before the revolution. 他们在革命之前就煽动了群众。
The candidate had demagoged the electorate for years before winning the election.  这位候选人在赢得选举之前的几年里一直在蛊惑选民。


将来时:
He will demagogue the voters with empty promises. 他将用空洞的承诺来蛊惑选民。
They will demagogue the public with fear-mongering tactics. 他们将用危言耸听的策略来煽动公众。


虚拟语气:
If he demagoged the people, they would have revolted. 如果他煽动人民，他们就会反抗。
If she had demagoged the crowd, the rally would have been a disaster. 如果她煽动人群，集会就会是一场灾难。


被动语态:
The public was demagoged into supporting the unpopular policy. 公众被煽动支持这项不受欢迎的政策。
The election was demagoged by both sides.  选举双方都进行了煽动。


疑问句:
Was the crowd demagoged by the speaker's rhetoric? 演讲者的言辞是否煽动了人群？
Did the politician demagogue the voters effectively? 这位政治家是否有效地煽动了选民？


否定句:
He did not demagogue the people; he reasoned with them. 他没有煽动人民；他与他们讲道理。
The candidate refused to demagogue the electorate with false claims.  候选人拒绝用虚假说法来蛊惑选民。
