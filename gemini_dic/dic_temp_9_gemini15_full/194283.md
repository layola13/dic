self-operators:/ˈsɛlf ˈɑːpəreɪtərz/| n. |自操作符，自身操作符|Self-operators are operators that operate on a single operand, modifying it in place.  They are commonly used in programming languages to perform actions directly on a variable without needing to assign the result back to the variable. For example, `++` (increment) and `--` (decrement) are common self-operators. |Example:  `x++;` (increments x by 1)  This is equivalent to `x = x + 1;`, but more concise. Another example: `list.append(item)` appends an item to the list, modifying the list itself.|近义词：unary operators|反义词：binary operators, ternary operators
