indictments:/ɪnˈdaɪtmənts/| n. |起诉书(复数) |正式指控某人犯罪的书面控告；对某人提出多项指控的行为。Indictments are formal accusations of a crime, presented by a grand jury, leading to a trial.  |例句：The indictments against the former CEO were widely publicized. = 对前任首席执行官的起诉受到了广泛关注。

The multiple indictments detail a range of financial crimes. = 多项起诉书详细描述了一系列金融犯罪行为。


近义词：accusations, charges, arraignments| 反义词：acquittals


词源:  The word "indictment" comes from the Old French "enditement," meaning "to indict" or "to present a charge."  This in turn originates from the Latin "indictamentum," a compound of "indicare" (to point out, to show) and the suffix "-mentum" (action or result of).
