paucities:/ˈpɔːsətiz/| n. |缺乏；不足；少量 |A paucity of resources hampered the project's progress. 资源匮乏阻碍了项目的进展。|近义词：scarcity, shortage, dearth, deficiency |反义词：abundance, plenty, surplus |名词


英文解析：Paucity refers to a smallness of number; scarcity; scantiness. It implies a lack or deficiency of something, often something essential or desirable.  The word often suggests a regrettable insufficiency.

例句：

The paucity of evidence made it difficult to convict the suspect.  证据不足使得难以定罪嫌疑犯。

The paucity of rainfall led to a severe drought.  降雨稀少导致了严重的干旱。


词源:

The word "paucities" is the plural form of "paucity," derived from the Latin word "paucitas," meaning "smallness," "fewness," or "scarcity."  The Latin root is "paucus," meaning "little," "few," or "small."
