doddered:/ˈdɒdəd/ | v. | 蹒跚；衰老；枯萎 | To walk or move unsteadily, especially because of old age or weakness; to become weak or feeble from age.  |  The old man doddered down the street, leaning heavily on his cane.  这位老人拄着拐杖，蹒跚地走下街道。


The old woman doddered in her twilight years, her once-sharp mind becoming clouded.  这位老妇人在暮年变得衰弱，她曾经敏锐的头脑变得迟钝了。|近义词： totter, wobble, falter, weaken | 反义词：strengthen, flourish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dodders around the house all day. 他整天在房子周围蹒跚地走动。
The plant dodders along the fence.  植物沿着栅栏枯萎生长。


一般过去时:
She doddered across the room. 她蹒跚地走过房间。
The vine doddered and died. 藤蔓枯萎而死。


现在完成时:
He has doddered ever since his stroke. 自从中风后，他就一直行动迟缓。
The flower has doddered in the harsh sun.  这朵花在强烈的阳光下枯萎了。


过去完成时:
She had doddered for years before she finally fell. 在她最终跌倒之前，她已经行动迟缓了很多年。
The plant had doddered before we noticed it.在我们注意到之前，植物就已经枯萎了。


将来时:
He will dodder more as he gets older. 他年纪越大，行动会越迟缓。
The leaves will dodder and fall from the tree. 叶子会枯萎并从树上掉下来。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't fallen, he wouldn't have doddered so much. 如果他没有摔倒，他也不会这么行动迟缓。
If the plant had received more water, it wouldn't have doddered. 如果植物得到更多水，它就不会枯萎。


被动语态:  (This verb is rarely used passively)  被动语态很少用
