catarrhs:/kəˈtɑːrz/ | n. |卡他, 鼻塞, 黏液分泌过多 | A catarrh is an inflammation of the mucous membrane, typically in the nose and throat, producing a discharge of mucus.  It is often associated with a common cold or other respiratory infections. | Example: The patient presented with symptoms of a catarrh, including a runny nose and sore throat.  患者出现了卡他症状，包括流鼻涕和喉咙痛。|近义词：cold, coryza, rhinitis |反义词：None specifically, but "health" or "absence of infection" could be considered antonyms in a broad sense.
