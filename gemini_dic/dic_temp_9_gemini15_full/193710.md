self-confirmations: /ˌsɛlfˌkɑːnfərˈmeɪʃənz/ | n. | 自我肯定；自我确认 | Repeated positive statements made to oneself to reinforce beliefs and build self-esteem.  Self-confirmations are a technique used in self-help and positive psychology to improve mental and emotional well-being. They involve regularly repeating affirmations to oneself, often in writing or verbally.  | Example:  "Daily self-confirmations helped her overcome her fear of public speaking." = “每日的自我肯定帮助她克服了对公开演讲的恐惧。”


Example: "He used positive self-confirmations to boost his confidence before the job interview." = “他在面试前用积极的自我肯定来增强信心。”


近义词：affirmations, positive self-talk, self-encouragement | 反义词：negative self-talk, self-criticism
