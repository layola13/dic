metazoic:/ˌmɛtəˈzoʊɪk/ | adj. | 后生动物的；后生代的 | Relating to the Metazoa, the group of animals that are multicellular and have differentiated tissues.  The term can also refer to the period of geological time when metazoans were abundant. | Example: The Cambrian explosion marks the beginning of a rapid diversification of metazoic life.  寒武纪生命大爆发标志着后生动物生命快速多样化的开始。| 近义词：multicellular (in the biological sense) | 反义词：prokaryotic (in the biological sense), azoic (in the geological sense)
