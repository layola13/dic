deadheading:/ˈdedˌhedɪŋ/| v.|  去除枯萎的花或枝；（火车、公共汽车等）空车行驶；（尤指）不载客或货物的行驶|  The process of removing dead or spent flowers and foliage from a plant to encourage further blooming or to improve the plant's overall appearance; or the act of a vehicle, especially a train or bus, traveling without passengers or cargo. |例句：I spent the afternoon deadheading roses in the garden. 我下午都在花园里剪除枯萎的玫瑰花。

例句：The bus was deadheading back to the depot. 这辆公共汽车空车返回车库。|近义词：pruning, trimming (for plants);  running empty (for vehicles)|反义词：载客行驶, carrying passengers/freight
