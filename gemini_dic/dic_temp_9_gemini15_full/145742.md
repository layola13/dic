overcrowded:/ˌoʊvərˈkraʊdɪd/| adj. |拥挤的，人满为患的 | excessively crowded; filled beyond capacity.  The adjective "overcrowded" describes a place or situation where there are too many people or things in a limited space, leading to discomfort and inconvenience.  It implies a level of crowding that is beyond what is comfortable or manageable. |例句：The train was overcrowded, and I had to stand for the entire journey.  火车非常拥挤，我不得不一路站着。

The concert hall was overcrowded; many people couldn't find seats. 音乐厅人满为患，许多人都找不到座位。|近义词：packed, crammed, congested, crowded |反义词：empty, sparse, uncrowded
