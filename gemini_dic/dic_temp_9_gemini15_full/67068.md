explode:/ɪkˈsploʊd/ /ɪkˈspləʊd/ | vt. vi. n.|爆炸；爆发；激增；快速发展|To burst violently as a result of internal pressure; to suddenly increase rapidly.|The bomb exploded with a deafening roar. 炸弹发出震耳欲聋的巨响爆炸了。 The volcano exploded, sending ash and rock into the air. 火山爆发，喷出火山灰和岩石。 |erupt, burst, detonate|implode, collapse|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The pressure cooker explodes if the steam valve is blocked.  如果蒸汽阀堵塞，压力锅就会爆炸。
The business explodes with new customers every month. 每月都有大量新客户涌入这家公司。


一般过去时:
The balloon exploded when it hit the ceiling. 气球碰到天花板就爆了。
The population exploded in the 1960s.  20世纪60年代人口激增。


现在完成时:
The situation has exploded since the news broke.  自从新闻爆出以来，情况已经失控了。
Three new factories have exploded onto the market this year. 今年有三个新工厂迅速进入市场。


过去完成时:
The pipe had exploded before the firemen arrived.  消防员到达之前，管道已经爆裂了。
The demand for the product had exploded before the marketing campaign began.  营销活动开始之前，对该产品的需求就已经激增了。


将来时:
The rocket will explode in five seconds.  火箭将在五秒钟后爆炸。
The company’s profits will explode next quarter. 公司的利润将在下个季度激增。


虚拟语气:
If the bomb exploded, many people would be hurt. 如果炸弹爆炸，许多人将会受伤。
If the economy exploded, we would be very rich. 如果经济迅速发展，我们将非常富有。


被动语态:
The building was exploded by a terrorist group. 这座建筑物被恐怖组织炸毁了。
The market was exploded by the introduction of a new technology. 新技术的引进使市场迅速扩张。


疑问句:
Did the bomb explode? 炸弹爆炸了吗？
Will the business explode next year?  这家公司明年会迅速发展吗？


否定句:
The bomb didn't explode. 炸弹没有爆炸。
The party didn't explode as we expected.  聚会并没有像我们预期的那样热闹。
