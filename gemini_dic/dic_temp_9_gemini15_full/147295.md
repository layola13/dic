overmodestly:/ˌoʊvərˈmɑːdəstli/ | adv. | 过分谦虚地，过谦地 |  The adverb "overmodestly" describes an action or statement characterized by excessive modesty or humility, often to the point of being self-deprecating or insincere. It suggests a degree of humility that goes beyond what is considered appropriate or natural. | He overmodestly attributed his success to luck, downplaying his own considerable talent and effort. 他过分谦虚地将自己的成功归功于运气，轻描淡写了自己的才能和努力。| modestly, humbly | boastfully, arrogantly | 副词


USAGE->

This word is an adverb, so it modifies verbs and doesn't have tenses like nouns and verbs.  Examples will show its usage in different sentence structures.

Declarative Sentence:
He spoke overmodestly about his achievements. = 他过分谦虚地谈论了他的成就。
She overmodestly declined the award. = 她过分谦虚地谢绝了奖项。


Interrogative Sentence:
Did he overmodestly refuse the promotion? = 他是否过分谦虚地拒绝了晋升？

Exclamatory Sentence:
How overmodestly he accepted the praise! = 他接受赞扬时多么过分谦虚！
Overmodestly, she deflected all credit to her team! = 她多么过分谦虚地把所有的功劳都归于她的团队！


Negative Sentence:

She didn't overmodestly hide her accomplishments. = 她没有过分谦虚地隐藏自己的成就。
