ordained:/ɔːˈdeɪnd/ | adj. v. |被任命的；被圣职授任的；注定发生的 |  To have been officially appointed to a position, especially a religious one; decreed or destined by fate. |例句：He was ordained a priest last year. 他去年被任命为牧师。

The newly ordained minister delivered a powerful sermon. 新任命的牧师发表了一篇充满力量的布道。


近义词：appointed, consecrated, decreed, fated

反义词：prohibited, forbidden, prevented


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ordains new priests every year. 他每年都任命新的牧师。
The church ordains candidates after rigorous training. 教会经过严格的培训后才任命候选人。


一般过去时:
The bishop ordained him a deacon. 主教任命他为执事。
My grandfather was ordained in 1960. 我的祖父在1960年被任命。


现在完成时:
He has been ordained for over twenty years. 他已被任命超过二十年了。
Many priests have been ordained since the new cathedral opened. 自从新大教堂开放以来，许多牧师已被任命。


过去完成时:
He had already been ordained before he moved to the new parish. 他搬到新的教区之前就已经被任命了。
They had been ordained for many years before retiring. 他们在退休前已经被任命多年了。


将来时:
He will be ordained next month. 他下个月将被任命。
The seminary will ordain five new priests this spring. 神学院今年春天将任命五位新的牧师。


虚拟语气:
If he were ordained, he would have more influence. 如果他被任命，他将会有更大的影响力。
I wish I had been ordained earlier in my life. 我希望我早些时候就被任命了。


被动语态:
He was ordained into the priesthood. 他被任命为神职人员。
New ministers are ordained every year. 每年都有新的部长被任命。


疑问句:
Was he ordained a priest? 他被任命为牧师了吗？
When will she be ordained? 她什么时候会被任命？


否定句:
He was not ordained until he was thirty. 直到他三十岁才被任命。
She had not yet been ordained at the time of the ceremony. 在仪式举行时，她还没有被任命。


PHRASE->
ordained minister: 被任命的牧师
ordained priest: 被任命的牧师
ordained deacon: 被任命的执事
ordained bishop: 被任命的主教
divinely ordained: 天意注定


词源:
源自古法语 *ordener,  由拉丁语 *ordināre 派生而来，意思是“安排，排列”。  其含义逐渐演变为“任命”，特别是指宗教职位的任命。
