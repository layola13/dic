plebbish:/ˈplɛbɪʃ/| adj.|平民的；粗俗的；粗鲁的；下流的|Characterized by or suggestive of the manners or speech of the common people; vulgar; coarse; crude.  The word often carries a negative connotation, implying a lack of refinement or sophistication.|例句：His plebbish behavior at the formal dinner was quite embarrassing. 他在正式晚宴上的粗俗行为非常令人尴尬。

例句：The plebbish graffiti marred the beauty of the ancient building.  那些粗俗的涂鸦破坏了古建筑的美观。


近义词：vulgar, coarse, uncouth, boorish, loutish, common


反义词：refined, cultured, sophisticated, elegant, polished


词源:源自古法语 *plebeius,  最终源于拉丁语 plebeius, 指罗马平民。  The word reflects a historical class distinction, where "plebeians" were contrasted with the patricians (the aristocracy).
