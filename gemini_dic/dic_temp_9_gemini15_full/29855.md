causativity:/ˌkɔːzəˈtɪvɪti/| n. |致因性，使役性 |The quality or state of causing something to happen; the property of a verb that expresses the causing of an action or state.  Causativity refers to the relationship between a cause and an effect, or the act of causing something to happen.  It's often studied in linguistics and grammar, particularly in relation to verbs. |The teacher's encouragement caused the student to improve.  老师的鼓励导致学生进步了。 The heavy rain caused the river to flood.  大雨导致河水泛滥。| agency, effectivity |passivity, inactivity|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun causes the plants to grow. 太阳使植物生长。
Her actions cause me to worry. 她的行为让我担心。


一般过去时:
The storm caused the trees to fall. 暴风雨导致树木倒塌。
His words caused her to cry. 他的话让她哭了。


现在完成时:
The drought has caused the crops to fail. 干旱导致庄稼歉收。
His negligence has caused many problems. 他的疏忽导致了很多问题。


过去完成时:
The delay had caused them to miss the train. 延误导致他们错过了火车。
Her illness had caused her to cancel the trip. 她的病让她取消了旅行。


将来时:
The new policy will cause businesses to adapt. 新政策将导致企业适应。
The heat will cause the ice to melt. 热量会使冰融化。


虚拟语气:
If the medicine caused no side effects, I would take it. 如果这种药没有副作用，我会服用。
If he had studied harder, he would have passed the exam. 如果他学习更努力，他就会通过考试。


被动语态:
The damage was caused by the earthquake.  损害是由地震造成的。
The problem is caused by a software bug. 问题是由软件错误引起的。


疑问句:
What caused the accident?  是什么导致了事故？
What caused the delay?  是什么导致了延误？


否定句:
The rain didn't cause the flood. 雨水并没有导致洪水。
His words didn't cause her any harm. 他的话没有对她造成任何伤害。


PHRASE->
