preempted:/prɪˈɛmptɪd/| v.|抢先；先发制人；占据；取消（原定节目）|To take action before someone else in order to prevent them from doing something; to seize control of something before someone else can; to replace a scheduled television program with another.|例句：The government preempted the opposition's attack by releasing its own report. 政府抢先发布了自己的报告，从而化解了反对派的攻击。

例句：The emergency broadcast preempted all other scheduled programming. 紧急广播取代了所有其他预定节目。|近义词：anticipate, forestall, supersede, displace |反义词：postpone, delay


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The network preempts its prime-time schedule for breaking news. =  电视台为了突发新闻而抢占黄金时段的节目安排。
He preempts any criticism by addressing the issue first. = 他先处理了这个问题，从而避免了任何批评。


一般过去时:
The meeting was preempted by an unexpected phone call. = 会议被一个意外的电话打断了。
She preempted his question by answering it herself. = 她抢先回答了他的问题。


现在完成时:
The new regulations have preempted the old ones. = 新规章已经取代了旧规章。
They have preempted the market by launching the product early. = 他们通过提前推出产品来抢占市场。


过去完成时:
The storm had preempted our plans for a picnic. = 暴风雨打乱了我们野餐的计划。
He had preempted the argument by presenting his evidence first. = 他先出示了证据，从而避免了争论。


将来时:
The company will preempt any potential legal challenges. = 公司将先发制人，应对任何潜在的法律挑战。
They will preempt the broadcast with a special announcement. = 他们将用特别公告来取代广播。


虚拟语气:
If they had preempted the crisis, the situation would have been better. = 如果他们预先阻止了危机，情况就会更好。
I wish I had preempted his rude remark. = 我真希望我预先阻止了他无礼的评论。


被动语态:
The show was preempted by a special report. = 节目被特别报道取代了。
His plans were preempted by unforeseen circumstances. = 他的计划被不可预见的情况打乱了。


疑问句:

Was the meeting preempted? = 会议被取消了吗？


否定句:
The company did not preempt the competitor's move. = 公司没有抢先竞争对手一步。
They did not preempt the crisis. = 他们没有预先阻止危机。
