contraceptives:/ˈkɒntrəˌsɛptɪvz/| n. |避孕药具；避孕措施 |Substances or devices used to prevent pregnancy.  Contraceptives work through various mechanisms, including preventing ovulation, blocking sperm from reaching the egg, or preventing a fertilized egg from implanting in the uterus.|例句：Many different types of contraceptives are available, including pills, condoms, and IUDs. 许多不同类型的避孕药具可用，包括药丸、避孕套和宫内节育器。

例句：The effectiveness of contraceptives varies depending on the method used and user compliance. 避孕药具的有效性取决于所使用方法和用户的依从性。


近义词：birth control, family planning methods |反义词：fertility drugs

词源:
The word "contraceptives" is derived from the Latin prefix "contra-" (against) and the verb "cipere" (to take).  The suffix "-ive" denotes something that has the power or tendency to do something.  Therefore, "contraceptives" literally means "things that act against conception".
