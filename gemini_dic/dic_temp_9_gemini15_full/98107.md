inroads:/ˈɪnˌroʊdz/ | n. | 侵蚀； inroads (复数) |  A road or path leading into a place;  progress made against something, especially a problem or difficulty.  The plural form "inroads" is most commonly used and refers to significant progress or advances made into something. |


例句：

The new marketing campaign has made significant inroads into the target market.  = 新的营销活动已在目标市场上取得了显著进展。

They've made some inroads into solving the problem of homelessness. = 他们在解决无家可归者问题方面取得了一些进展。


近义词：progress, advance, inroads, encroachment, penetration

反义词：setback, regression, decline


词性:名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company makes inroads into new markets every year. = 公司每年都在开拓新市场。
Their efforts are making inroads into poverty. = 他们的努力正在减少贫困。

一般过去时:
The army made significant inroads into enemy territory. = 军队深入敌方领地。
Last year's campaign made little inroads against the disease. = 去年运动对这种疾病收效甚微。

现在完成时:
We have made considerable inroads into reducing our carbon footprint. = 我们在减少碳足迹方面取得了相当大的进展。
They have made little inroads into solving the problem. = 他们在解决这个问题上几乎没有取得什么进展。

过去完成时:
Before the new treatment, they had made few inroads against the cancer. = 在新的治疗方法出现之前，他们对癌症几乎没有取得什么进展。
By the end of the year, they had made substantial inroads into the project. = 到年底，他们在这个项目上取得了重大进展。

将来时:
We will make inroads into the problem of unemployment. = 我们将努力解决失业问题。
The new technology will help us make inroads into the market. = 新技术将帮助我们进军市场。

虚拟语气:
If we worked harder, we would make more inroads into the project. = 如果我们更努力工作，我们就能在这个项目上取得更大的进展。
I wish we had made more inroads into the problem earlier. = 我希望我们早点在这个问题上取得更多进展。


被动语态:
Significant inroads were made into the enemy's defenses. = 敌人的防御工事遭到严重破坏。
Inroads have been made in the fight against hunger. = 在抗击饥饿的斗争中取得了进展。

疑问句:
What inroads have been made in the research? = 这项研究取得了什么进展？
Have they made any inroads into the problem? = 他们在解决这个问题上取得了什么进展？

否定句:
The negotiations made no inroads. = 谈判没有取得任何进展。
They failed to make any inroads into the market. = 他们未能进军市场。
