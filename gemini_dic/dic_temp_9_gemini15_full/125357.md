multilobular:/ˌmʌltiˈloːbjʊlə(r)/| adj.|多叶的，多小叶的|Having or composed of many lobes or lobules.  This term is often used in medical contexts to describe organs or structures with multiple lobes.  For example, a multilobular lung describes a lung with multiple sections or lobes.|例句：The tumor was described as multilobular, indicating its complex structure.  肿瘤被描述为多叶的，表明其结构复杂。

The patient's liver showed a multilobular pattern on the scan.  患者的肝脏在扫描中显示出多叶的模式。|近义词：multilobed, lobed|反义词：unilobular, single-lobed

WORD ORIGIN->
The word "multilobular" is derived from the Latin words "multus" (many) and "lobulus" (a small lobe).  "Lobulus" itself comes from "lobus" (lobe), referring to a rounded projection or division of an organ.
