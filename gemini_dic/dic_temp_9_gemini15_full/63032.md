enrapt:/ɪnˈræpt/| vt.|使狂喜；使着迷|To fill (someone) with great delight; to enchant or fascinate.|The audience was enraptured by the singer's performance.  观众被歌手的表演迷住了。|近义词：captivate, enchant, enthrall, fascinate|反义词：bore, disenchant


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The music enrapts the listener. = 音乐使听者着迷。
The beautiful scenery enrapts the tourists. = 美丽的风景使游客着迷。

一般过去时:
The story enraptured me. = 这个故事让我着迷。
The magician's trick enraptured the children. = 魔术师的戏法让孩子们着迷。

现在完成时:
The beauty of the sunset has enraptured me. = 日落的美丽让我着迷。
The play has enraptured audiences for years. = 这部戏多年来一直让观众着迷。

过去完成时:
I had been enraptured by her beauty before I even spoke to her. = 在我跟她说话之前，我就已经被她的美貌迷住了。
The audience had been enraptured by the performance before the final act. = 在最后一幕之前，观众已经被演出迷住了。

将来时:
The new movie will enrapture audiences worldwide. = 这部新电影将迷住全世界的观众。
The concert will enrapture everyone who attends. = 音乐会将使所有参加的人着迷。

虚拟语气:
If the music were better, it would enrapture the crowd. = 如果音乐更好，它就会迷住人群。
I wish the book had enraptured me more. = 我希望这本书能让我更着迷。

被动语态:
The audience was enraptured by the performance. = 观众被演出迷住了。
He was enraptured by her singing. = 他被她的歌声迷住了。

疑问句:
Was the audience enraptured by the play? = 观众被这出戏迷住了吗？
Will the story enrapture the reader? = 这个故事会让读者着迷吗？

否定句:
The movie did not enrapture me. = 这部电影并没有让我着迷。
The lecture failed to enrapture the students. = 讲座未能吸引学生。
