raging:/ˈreɪdʒɪŋ/| adj. | 狂暴的；猛烈的；激烈的 | violently angry; extremely intense or severe. |例句：The raging storm prevented us from leaving.  狂风暴雨阻止了我们离开。|例句：He was filled with raging anger. 他充满了愤怒。|近义词：furious, violent, intense, furious, tempestuous |反义词：calm, gentle, mild, peaceful


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The fire is raging. = 火势凶猛。
The river is raging. = 河水奔腾咆哮。

一般过去时:
The storm raged all night. = 暴风雨肆虐了一整夜。
The debate raged on for hours. = 辩论持续了好几个小时。

现在完成时:
The fire has been raging for hours. = 大火已经燃烧了好几个小时了。
The controversy has been raging for weeks. = 争议已经持续了好几周了。

过去完成时:
The wind had raged before the storm broke. = 在暴风雨来临之前，风已经狂暴了一阵子。
The argument had raged for days before they finally reached a compromise. = 争论持续了好几天，他们才最终达成妥协。

将来时:
The storm will rage throughout the night. = 暴风雨将持续整夜。
The debate will rage on for many years to come. = 这场辩论还会持续很多年。

虚拟语气:
If the storm were raging, we wouldn't go out. = 如果暴风雨肆虐，我们就不会出去了。
I wish the anger wasn't raging inside me. = 我希望我内心的愤怒不要如此强烈。

被动语态:
The city was raged by the fire. = 城市被大火吞噬。
The field was raged by the storm.  = 田地被暴风雨摧毁。


疑问句:
Is the fire still raging? = 火势还在猛烈燃烧吗？
Is the debate still raging? = 辩论还在激烈进行吗？

否定句:
The fire wasn't raging when I arrived. = 我到达时，火势并不猛烈。
The argument wasn't raging when I entered the room. = 我进房间时，争吵并没有很激烈。
