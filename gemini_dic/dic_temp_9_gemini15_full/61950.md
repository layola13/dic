emulsifier:/ɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪər/| n. |乳化剂|A substance that stabilizes an emulsion, preventing the liquids from separating. Emulsifiers work by reducing the surface tension between the two liquids, allowing them to mix more easily. They are commonly used in food, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals.  | Example:  Lecithin is a common emulsifier used in mayonnaise to prevent the oil and water from separating.  蛋黄素是一种常用的蛋黄酱乳化剂，可以防止油和水分离。|近义词：emulsifying agent |反义词：None (反义词概念与乳化剂功能相反，而非词语本身)|名词
