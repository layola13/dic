prejudgements:/ˌpriːˈdʒʌdʒmənts/| n. |偏见；先入之见；预先判断|Prejudgements are opinions or conclusions formed before having sufficient evidence or knowledge. They often stem from biases, stereotypes, or limited information and can lead to unfair or inaccurate assessments.|例句：His prejudices against immigrants stemmed from unfounded prejudgements. 他的反移民偏见源于毫无根据的先入之见。
例句：The judge warned the jury to avoid prejudgements about the defendant's guilt or innocence.  法官告诫陪审团避免对被告是否有罪做出预先判断。|近义词：biases, preconceptions, preconceived notions, assumptions|反义词：objectivity, impartiality, fairness
