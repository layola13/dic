quasi-lawfully:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈlɔːfəli/| adj.|准合法地，似合法地|Approaching or resembling legality but lacking complete legal validity or authorization.  It suggests an action or state that is close to being lawful but doesn't fully meet all legal requirements.|The company operated quasi-lawfully, skirting regulations but avoiding outright illegality.|近义词：legally questionable, semi-legal, borderline legal|反义词：lawful, illegal, legitimate


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "is," "was," "will be," etc.  Instead, it modifies nouns.  Therefore, tenses are reflected in the verb of the sentence it modifies.

Present Perfect: The government has allowed some quasi-lawfully activities to continue. = 政府允许一些准合法活动继续进行。
Past Perfect:  They had believed their actions were quasi-lawfully until the investigation began. = 在调查开始之前，他们认为他们的行为是准合法的。
Future Simple: The court will decide whether the business operated quasi-lawfully. = 法院将裁决这家企业是否以准合法的方式经营。


Passive Voice:

PHRASE->
There are no common multi-word phrases directly using "quasi-lawfully".  The word itself is used to modify a noun describing an action or state.
