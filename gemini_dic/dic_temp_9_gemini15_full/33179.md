chromoblast:/ˈkroʊməˌblæst/| n.|色素芽生菌；色素性芽生菌病|A chromoblast is a type of fungus that causes a chronic, localized skin infection called chromoblastomycosis.  It's characterized by the formation of sclerotic bodies (brownish, thick-walled cells) in the infected tissue.|Example: The doctor diagnosed the patient with chromoblastomycosis, a fungal infection caused by a chromoblast.|近义词: Fonsecaea, Phialophora (specific chromoblast-causing fungi)|反义词: None specifically, but terms related to non-fungal skin infections could be considered contrasting.
