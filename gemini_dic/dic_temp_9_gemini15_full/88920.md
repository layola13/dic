hold-up: /ˈhoʊld ʌp/ | n., v. | 抢劫；耽搁；支撑；阻碍 |

英文解析：As a noun, "hold-up" refers to a robbery, typically at gunpoint.  As a verb, it means to delay or impede progress, or to support or sustain something.

例句 (Noun):

The bank was the target of a brazen hold-up yesterday.  = 昨天银行成为了一起明目张胆的抢劫案的目标。

Two masked men carried out the hold-up and escaped with a large sum of money. = 两个蒙面男子实施了抢劫并携巨款逃脱。


例句 (Verb - delay):

The traffic hold-up caused me to be late for work. = 交通阻塞导致我上班迟到。

Bad weather caused a significant hold-up in the construction project. = 恶劣天气导致建筑项目严重延误。


例句 (Verb - support):

The pillars hold up the roof of the cathedral. = 柱子支撑着大教堂的屋顶。

The strong wooden beams hold up the weight of the second floor. = 坚固的木梁支撑着二楼的重量。


近义词 (Noun): robbery, heist, mugging, delay

近义词 (Verb - delay): delay, postpone, impede, obstruct

近义词 (Verb - support): support, sustain, uphold


反义词 (Noun):  (No direct opposite, but the opposite action would be successful completion of a transaction without robbery)

反义词 (Verb - delay):  expedite, accelerate

反义词 (Verb - support): collapse, weaken


词性: Noun, Verb
