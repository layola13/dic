It refers to a single consonant sound, as opposed to a geminate consonant, which is a doubled consonant sound.  For example, in the word "better," the "t" is nongeminate, while in the word "batter" the "t" sound is usually considered geminate in some analyses, though the degree of gemination can vary across languages and dialects. | The word "better" has a nongeminate /t/ sound, unlike "better" in some dialects. | 近义词：single, simple | 反义词：geminate, double
