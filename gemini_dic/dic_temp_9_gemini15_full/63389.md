entosternum: /ˌɛntoʊˈstɜːrnəm/ | n. |胸骨柄|The entosternum is the most anterior part of the sternum, a bone located in the anterior midline of the thorax. It is also known as the manubrium.  It articulates with the clavicles and the second costal cartilages.  |The entosternum is a crucial part of the skeletal structure, providing attachment points for muscles and protecting vital organs. |近义词:manubrium|反义词:xiphoid process|名词
