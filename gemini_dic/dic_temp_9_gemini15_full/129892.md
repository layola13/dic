non-inverted:/ˌnɒnɪnˈvɜːtɪd/| adj. |非倒装的|Not inverted; in the normal word order.  This term is primarily used in linguistics, particularly in the context of sentence structure.  It describes a sentence that follows the standard Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) word order, as opposed to an inverted sentence where the word order is altered, often for stylistic or grammatical reasons (e.g., in questions or exclamations).|The sentence "The cat sat on the mat" is non-inverted.|近义词：standard, normal, uninverted |反义词：inverted
