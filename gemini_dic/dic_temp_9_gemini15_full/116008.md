marshel:/ˈmɑːʃəl/| n. v.|元帅，指挥官；整理，排列；引导|  A marshal is a high-ranking officer in the armed forces, often a general or admiral.  The word can also describe someone who organizes or controls something, or someone who guides or leads people. |例句：He was promoted to field marshal. 他被晋升为陆军元帅。The police marshaled the crowd. 警察引导人群。|近义词：general, commander, arrange, guide|反义词：disorder, confuse
