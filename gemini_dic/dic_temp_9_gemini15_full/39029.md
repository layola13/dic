conflates:/kənˈfleɪts/| vt.|合并，混淆|To combine (two or more sets of things) into one.|The article conflates several different issues. 这篇文章将几个不同的问题混为一谈。|近义词：merge, combine, fuse, amalgamate|反义词：separate, distinguish, differentiate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He conflates the two concepts in his argument. = 他在他的论证中混淆了这两个概念。
The author conflates fact and fiction in her novel. = 作者的小说中混淆了事实和虚构。


一般过去时:
The media conflated the two events, causing confusion. = 媒体将这两件事混为一谈，造成了混乱。
She conflated her personal feelings with her professional judgment. = 她将个人感情与职业判断混淆了。


现在完成时:
The historian has conflated several different historical periods in his analysis. = 这位历史学家在他的分析中混淆了几个不同的历史时期。
They have conflated their individual goals with the team's objectives. = 他们将个人目标与团队目标混淆了。


过去完成时:
He had conflated the two theories before realizing his mistake. = 他在意识到自己的错误之前就已经混淆了这两个理论。
The critic had conflated the author's style with that of another writer. = 批评家将作者的风格与另一位作家的风格混淆了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish the newspaper hadn't conflated the two stories. = 我希望报纸没有将这两个故事混为一谈。


被动语态:
The two concepts were conflated in the report. = 这两个概念在报告中被混淆了。
His ideas were conflated by his critics. = 他的想法被他的批评者混淆了。


疑问句:
Does the author conflate cause and effect in this section? = 作者在这部分中是否混淆了因果关系？
Does the study conflate correlation with causation? = 这项研究是否将相关性与因果关系混淆了？


否定句:
The researcher carefully avoided conflating the variables. = 研究人员仔细避免混淆变量。
He did not conflate the two distinct issues. = 他没有混淆这两个截然不同的问题。

词源:
From Latin *conflāre, from com- + flāre "to blow".  The original sense referred to the melting down of metals, implying a blending or fusing.  The modern meaning developed from this, extending to the metaphorical blending of ideas or concepts.
