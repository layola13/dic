pseudohypertrophy:/ˌsuːdoʊhaɪpərˈtrɒfi/| n. |假性肥大 |Apparent increase in the size of an organ or tissue, due to an increase in the size of cells (hypertrophy) or to an abnormal accumulation of interstitial fluid (edema), but without a true increase in the number or size of functional cells.  It often occurs in muscular dystrophy, where muscle tissue is replaced by fat and connective tissue, giving the appearance of increased muscle mass. |The boy's calves showed pseudohypertrophy, a hallmark of Duchenne muscular dystrophy.  这个男孩的小腿显示出假性肥大，这是杜氏肌营养不良症的标志。|hypertrophy, enlargement |atrophy, wasting|名词
