route:/ruːt/|ˈraʊt/| n. v.|路线，路程；途径；方法；（尤指）航线；规划路线；发送（信息等）|

英文解析：A route is a way or course taken in getting from a starting point to a destination.  It can refer to a planned path of travel, a regular pathway, or a method of achieving something. As a verb, it means to send something via a particular path or channel.

例句：
The best route to the city center is via the highway. = 到市中心的最佳路线是高速公路。

We need to find a new route around the road closure. = 我们需要找到一条绕过道路封闭的新路线。


近义词：path, way, course, itinerary, method, channel

反义词：detour (in the sense of a deviation from a planned route)


词性：名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bus takes this route every day. = 这辆公共汽车每天都走这条路线。
This route avoids the heavy traffic. = 这条路线避开了交通拥堵。


一般过去时:
We followed the old Roman route. = 我们沿着古老的罗马道路行进。
They routed the message through a secure server. = 他们通过安全服务器发送了消息。


现在完成时:
We have found a shorter route. = 我们找到了一条更短的路线。
They have routed all the emails to the spam folder. = 他们已将所有电子邮件发送到垃圾邮件文件夹。


过去完成时:
They had already planned the route before the journey. = 他们在旅程开始前就已经规划好了路线。
The company had routed the shipment before the strike began. = 公司在罢工开始之前就已经发送了货物。


将来时:
We will take the scenic route tomorrow. = 我们明天将走风景优美的路线。
They will route the call to the appropriate department. = 他们会将电话转接到相应的部门。


虚拟语气:
If we took a different route, we would arrive earlier. = 如果我们走一条不同的路线，我们会早点到达。
If they had routed the package differently, it would have arrived on time. = 如果他们采用不同的运输路线，包裹就会准时到达。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
