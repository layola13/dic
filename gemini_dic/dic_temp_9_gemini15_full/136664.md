nonseasoned:/ˌnɑːnˈsiːznd/| adj. |未经调味的；清淡的 |Not seasoned; lacking seasoning or flavor.  It refers to food that hasn't been enhanced with spices, herbs, or salt. |例句：The chicken was nonseasoned and rather bland.  这鸡没放调料，相当清淡。

The vegetables were served nonseasoned, allowing their natural flavors to shine.  蔬菜没有调味，让其天然风味得以展现。


近义词：unseasoned, bland, tasteless, flavorless |反义词：seasoned, flavorful, spicy, savory


词源：
The word "nonseasoned" is a combination of the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "seasoned."  "Seasoned" in this context derives from the verb "to season," meaning to add spices, herbs, or salt to food to enhance its flavor.  The ultimate origin traces back to Old French "saisnir" (to salt, pickle).
