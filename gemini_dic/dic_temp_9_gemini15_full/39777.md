consonances:/ˈkɒnsənənsɪz/ | /ˈkɑːnsənənsɪz/ | n. | 辅音谐音；和谐；一致 | The repetition of consonant sounds, especially at the beginning of words, creates consonance, adding a musical quality to the text.  Consonance is a literary device used to create a sense of unity and flow in writing. It can also be used to emphasize certain words or phrases.  | 例句：The repeated "s" sounds in "sing a song of sixpence" create a pleasing consonance.  重复出现的“s”音在“sing a song of sixpence”中创造了一种令人愉悦的辅音谐音。


The alliteration in "Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers" is a type of consonance. “Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers”中的头韵是一种辅音谐音。 | 近义词：harmony, agreement, accord | 反义词：discord, dissonance, disagreement | 名词
