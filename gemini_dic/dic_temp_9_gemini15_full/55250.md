dissimulating: /dɪˈsɪmjuleɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 掩饰；伪装；假装 | To hide or disguise one's true feelings or intentions.  | 例句：He was dissimulating his anger by forcing a smile. 他强作欢笑掩饰怒气。 | 近义词：concealing, masking, feigning, pretending | 反义词：revealing, disclosing, admitting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dissimulates his true feelings. = 他掩饰自己的真实感情。
She dissimulates her anxiety well. = 她把焦虑掩饰得很好。


一般过去时:
He dissimulated his nervousness during the interview. = 面试期间，他掩饰了自己的紧张情绪。
She dissimulated her disappointment at the news. = 她掩饰了听到这个消息后的失望。


现在完成时:
He has dissimulated his intentions throughout the negotiation. = 在整个谈判过程中，他一直在掩饰自己的意图。

过去完成时:

She had dissimulated her true feelings until the very end. = 直到最后，她才表现出自己的真实情感。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
Is he dissimulating his intentions? = 他是在掩饰自己的意图吗？

否定句:

She did not dissimulate her anger; she expressed it openly. = 她没有掩饰自己的愤怒，而是公开表达了出来。

词源:
From Latin dissimulāre "to disguise, conceal," from dis- "apart" + simulare "to imitate, pretend."  The word essentially means "to pretend not to be what one is."
