reimpose:/ˌriːɪmˈpoʊz/ | vt. |重新实施，再次强加 | To impose something again; to reinstate a law, rule, tax, etc. after it has been removed or suspended. |例句：The government decided to reimpose the travel restrictions due to the rising number of COVID-19 cases. = 由于新冠肺炎病例数量的上升，政府决定重新实施旅行限制。

例句：After a period of relaxation, the school reimposed strict dress code rules. = 在一段时间的宽松之后，学校重新实施了严格的着装规定。


近义词：reinstate, re-establish, reintroduce | 反义词：abolish, repeal, remove


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government reimposes new taxes every year. = 政府每年都会重新征税。
They reimpose the curfew every weekend. = 他们每个周末都会重新实施宵禁。

一般过去时:
The judge reimposed the sentence after the appeal. = 上诉后，法官重新判决。
The school reimposed the uniform policy last year. = 学校去年重新实施了校服政策。

现在完成时:
The company has reimposed its stricter hiring standards. = 公司已经重新实施了更严格的招聘标准。
They have reimposed restrictions on public gatherings. = 他们已经重新限制了公众集会。

过去完成时:
The dictator had reimposed martial law before the rebellion. = 在叛乱之前，独裁者已经重新实施了戒严令。
The principal had reimposed the ban on cell phones after several incidents. = 在发生几起事件后，校长已经重新禁止使用手机。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If the situation worsened, they would reimpose the lockdown. = 如果情况恶化，他们将重新实施封锁。
