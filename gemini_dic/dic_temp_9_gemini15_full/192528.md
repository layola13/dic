scrutinizing: /ˈskruːtənaɪzɪŋ/ | v. (现在分词) | 仔细检查；细察；严格审查 | Examining or inspecting closely and thoroughly.  |例句：The detective scrutinized the evidence carefully.  侦探仔细检查了证据。

例句：She scrutinized the contract before signing it.  她在签署合同之前仔细审查了合同。


近义词：examining, inspecting, investigating, studying, analyzing | 反义词：ignoring, overlooking, neglecting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scrutinizes every detail of the report. 他仔细检查报告的每一个细节。
They scrutinize the candidates' qualifications. 他们仔细审查候选人的资格。


一般过去时:
The judge scrutinized the witness's testimony. 法官仔细审查了证人的证词。
She scrutinized the painting for any signs of forgery. 她仔细检查画作是否有伪造的迹象。


现在完成时:
The committee has scrutinized the proposals thoroughly. 委员会已经彻底审查了这些提案。
He has scrutinized the data for hours, looking for patterns. 他已经仔细研究数据数小时，寻找模式。


过去完成时:
They had scrutinized the documents before making their decision. 他们在做出决定之前已经仔细审查了文件。
The investigators had scrutinized all the evidence before concluding their report. 调查人员在完成报告之前已经仔细审查了所有证据。


将来时:
The auditors will scrutinize the company's financial records. 审计人员将仔细审查公司的财务记录。
We will scrutinize the results of the experiment before publishing them. 我们将在发表实验结果之前仔细审查结果。


虚拟语气:
If I were to scrutinize the contract, I might find a loophole. 如果我要仔细审查合同，我可能会发现漏洞。
