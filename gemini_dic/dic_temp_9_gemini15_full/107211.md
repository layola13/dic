laid:/leɪd/| v. |放置；产卵；铺设；制定；摆放|The past tense of "lay".  It means to put something down gently or carefully. It can also refer to a hen laying eggs, or to the act of placing bricks or tiles to create a surface.|例句：She laid the baby gently in the crib. 她轻轻地把婴儿放在婴儿床里。|例句：The hen laid an egg this morning. 母鸡今天早上产了一个蛋。|近义词：placed, put, set, established|反义词：lifted, removed, unearthed


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He lays the book on the table every day. = 他每天都把书放在桌子上。
The hen lays eggs regularly. = 母鸡定期下蛋。

一般过去时:
I laid the flowers on her grave. = 我把花放在她的坟墓上。
She laid the blame on her brother. = 她把责任推给了她哥哥。

现在完成时:
They have laid the foundation for the new building. = 他们已经为新建筑奠定了基础。
The bird has laid three eggs this week. = 这只鸟本周下了三个蛋。

过去完成时:
She had laid the groundwork for her success years ago. = 多年前她就为自己的成功奠定了基础。
Before the storm, they had laid down all the loose objects. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们已经把所有松散的物品都放好了。

将来时:
They will lay the carpet tomorrow. = 他们明天将铺地毯。
She will lay her cards on the table. = 她将摊牌。

虚拟语气:
If I had laid the plans properly, the project would have been successful. = 如果我计划得当，项目就会成功。
I wish I had laid the groundwork earlier. = 我希望我早点打好基础。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
