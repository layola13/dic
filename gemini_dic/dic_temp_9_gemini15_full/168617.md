proclaims:/prəˈkleɪmz/ | vt. |宣布；声明；表明 | To announce officially or publicly; to declare; to make known.  | 例句：The president proclaimed a state of emergency. 总统宣布进入紧急状态。

The government proclaimed its commitment to human rights. 政府宣布其致力于人权。


近义词：announce, declare, proclaim, promulgate | 反义词：conceal, hide, suppress | 词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The mayor proclaims a new policy every year. = 市长每年都宣布一项新政策。
She proclaims her innocence loudly. = 她大声宣称自己的清白。


一般过去时:
He proclaimed his love for her publicly. = 他公开宣称爱她。
They proclaimed the winner of the competition. = 他们宣布了比赛的获胜者。


现在完成时:
The company has proclaimed its financial success. = 公司已经宣布了它的财务成功。
She has proclaimed her intention to run for office. = 她已经宣布了竞选公职的意图。


过去完成时:
After the investigation, they proclaimed that he was innocent. = 调查后，他们宣布他是无辜的。
He proclaimed that he had already finished the work before the deadline. = 他宣称在截止日期前他已经完成了工作。


将来时:
The judge will proclaim the verdict tomorrow. = 法官明天将宣布判决。
They will proclaim the results of the election next week. = 他们下周将宣布选举结果。


虚拟语气:
If he proclaimed his guilt, he would be punished. = 如果他承认有罪，他将受到惩罚。
I wish he had proclaimed his love for me earlier. = 我希望他早点向我表白。


被动语态:
The new law was proclaimed yesterday. = 新法律昨天公布了。
His innocence was proclaimed by the court. = 法院宣布了他的清白。


疑问句:
What did the speaker proclaim at the meeting? = 说话者在会议上宣布了什么？
Will the government proclaim any new measures? = 政府会宣布任何新措施吗？


否定句:
He did not proclaim his intentions. = 他没有宣布他的意图。
The judge did not proclaim a guilty verdict. = 法官没有宣布有罪判决。


PHRASE->

词源:
From Old French *proclamer, from Latin proclamare "to call out loudly," from pro- "forth" + clamare "to shout."
