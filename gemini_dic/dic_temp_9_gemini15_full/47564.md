debacle:/ˈdɛbəkəl/| n. |惨败；彻底失败；溃败 |A debacle is a great disaster or complete failure, especially a sudden and ignominious one. It often refers to a situation where a plan or project collapses dramatically. |例句：The launch of the new product was a complete debacle. 新产品的发布彻底失败了。

例句：The peace negotiations ended in a debacle. 和平谈判以失败告终。


近义词：catastrophe, disaster, fiasco, failure |反义词：success, triumph, victory |词性：名词

词源:
The word "debacle" comes from the Old French word "débâcle," which means "a breaking up,"  "a breaking of ice,"  or "a sudden violent rush of water."  This refers to the breaking up of ice on a river.  The metaphorical sense of a sudden and complete collapse evolved from this original meaning.
