espouse:/ɪˈspaʊz/ | vt. | 支持，拥护，嫁娶 | To support, advocate, or champion a cause, belief, or person; to marry. | 例句：He espoused the cause of human rights. 他拥护人权事业。  She espoused a new theory of physics. 她支持一种新的物理理论。 | 近义词：advocate, champion, support, marry | 反义词：oppose, reject, repudiate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He espouses conservative values. = 他拥护保守的价值观。
She espouses a vegan lifestyle. = 她提倡素食生活方式。

一般过去时:
They espoused the principles of democracy. = 他们拥护民主原则。
He espoused her in a small church ceremony. = 他在一个小型教堂仪式上娶了她。

现在完成时:
She has espoused the cause of environmental protection for many years. = 她多年来一直致力于环保事业。
They have espoused a more liberal approach to education. = 他们采取了更自由的教育方法。

过去完成时:
He had espoused the cause of social justice before he entered politics. = 他在从政之前就一直倡导社会正义。
She had espoused the new technology before it became widely adopted. = 新技术广泛采用之前，她就已支持它。

将来时:
They will espouse a new strategy to increase sales. = 他们将采取一项新的策略来增加销售额。
He will espouse the principles of equality in his upcoming speech. = 他将在即将发表的演讲中倡导平等的原则。

虚拟语气:
If he espoused those ideals, he would be a better leader. = 如果他拥护这些理想，他将成为一个更好的领导者。
I wish he had espoused a more conciliatory approach. = 我希望他采取更温和的方式。

被动语态:
These views were espoused by many intellectuals. = 这些观点被许多知识分子所支持。
The new policy was espoused by the government. = 政府支持这项新政策。

疑问句:
Does he espouse any particular political party? = 他支持哪个政党？
What principles does she espouse? = 她支持什么原则？

否定句:
He did not espouse violence as a means of achieving his goals.  = 他并没有主张使用暴力来实现他的目标。
She does not espouse the use of nuclear weapons. = 她不赞成使用核武器。


PHRASE->
espouse a cause = 拥护一项事业
espouse a belief = 相信某种信仰
espouse a theory = 支持某种理论
espouse a lifestyle = 采取某种生活方式
to espouse marriage = 结婚


词源:
From Old French *espouser, from Vulgar Latin *exsponsare, from Latin sponsus "betrothed," from spondere "to promise."  The sense of "to marry" developed first, with the sense of "to support" arising later.
