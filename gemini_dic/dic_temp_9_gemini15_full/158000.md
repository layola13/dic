planner:/ˈplænər/ | n. |计划者；计划本；日程安排簿 | A planner is a person who makes plans, or a book used for recording appointments and plans.  |  She's a meticulous planner, always prepared for any eventuality.  她是一位一丝不苟的计划者，总是为任何意外情况做好准备。He uses a digital planner to keep track of his appointments. 他用电子日程本记录约会。 | scheduler, organizer, diary | haphazard, disorganized | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He plans his day meticulously. 他一丝不苟地计划他的一天。
She plans her meals in advance. 她提前计划她的膳食。


一般过去时:
They planned a surprise party for her. 他们为她策划了一个惊喜派对。
He planned his route carefully. 他仔细地规划了他的路线。


现在完成时:
She has planned her entire wedding. 她已经计划好了整个婚礼。
We have planned a trip to Europe. 我们已经计划了一次欧洲之旅。


过去完成时:
They had planned to go to the beach, but it rained. 他们原计划去海滩，但是下雨了。
She had planned everything perfectly. 她已经把一切都计划得完美无缺。


将来时:
I will plan a vacation next year. 我明年将计划一次假期。
They will plan the company's next marketing campaign. 他们将计划公司的下一个营销活动。


虚拟语气:
If I had planned better, I wouldn't have been so stressed. 如果我计划得更好，我就不会这么紧张了。
I wish I had planned this trip earlier. 我希望我早点计划这次旅行。


被动语态:
The event was planned by a professional event planner.  这个活动是由一位专业的活动策划人策划的。
The meeting was planned for next week. 会议计划在下周举行。


疑问句:
Who planned this party? 谁策划的这个派对？
Have you planned your weekend yet? 你计划好你的周末了吗？


否定句:
I didn't plan to stay so long. 我并没有打算待这么久。
They didn't plan on attending the conference. 他们没打算参加会议。
