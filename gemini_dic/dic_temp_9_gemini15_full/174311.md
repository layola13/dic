quibbling:/ˈkwɪblɪŋ/| v. |吹毛求疵；挑剔；争论细枝末节 | To argue over insignificant points; to find fault with minor details. |例句：He's always quibbling about the price. 他总是对价格吹毛求疵。|例句：Stop quibbling over such small details; the overall plan is good. 不要再为这些小细节争论了；总体计划很好。|近义词：nitpicking, caviling, carping |反义词：accepting, agreeing


USAGE->

He quibbles about everything. = 他对什么都吹毛求疵。

一般过去时:
She quibbled with the waiter about the bill. = 她和服务员为账单争论不休。
We quibbled over the details for hours. = 我们就细节争论了几个小时。

现在完成时:

They have quibbled over every single point. = 他们对每一个点都吹毛求疵。

She had quibbled about the price before finally agreeing to buy it. = 她对价格吹毛求疵了一番才最终同意购买。

词源:
The word "quibble" ultimately derives from the Middle Dutch word "kwibbelen," meaning "to exchange words," "to wrangle," or "to squabble."  It entered English around the 16th century.
