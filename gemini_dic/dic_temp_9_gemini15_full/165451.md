preliberal: /ˌpriːˈlɪbərəl/ | adj. | 前自由主义的；自由主义之前的 |  Describing ideas, systems, or periods that existed before the rise of liberalism as a dominant political ideology.  It often refers to a time characterized by different political and social structures, such as monarchies, aristocracy, or theocratic rule, where individual rights and freedoms were not central tenets.  The exact historical period referenced varies depending on context, but generally points to a time before the 18th and 19th centuries when liberal thought began to gain significant traction. |  The preliberal era saw widespread social hierarchies and limited individual liberties. | 近义词：anti-liberal (in some contexts), traditional, authoritarian | 反义词：liberal, progressive
