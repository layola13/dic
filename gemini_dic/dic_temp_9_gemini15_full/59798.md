ecphasis:/ˈɛkfəsɪs/ | n. |强调，重音 |Emphasis is the act of giving special importance or prominence to something.  It can refer to the stress placed on a syllable or word in speech, or to the highlighting of a particular point in writing or discussion. | Example: The speaker placed strong emphasis on the need for immediate action.  演讲者强烈强调需要立即采取行动。|近义词：stress, accent, highlight |反义词：downplay, understate |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher places emphasis on grammar. = 老师强调语法。
He puts emphasis on the importance of education. = 他强调教育的重要性。


一般过去时:
She emphasized the urgency of the situation. = 她强调了情况的紧急性。
The report emphasized the risks involved. = 报告强调了所涉及的风险。


现在完成时:
They have emphasized the need for reform. = 他们一直强调改革的必要性。
We have emphasized the importance of teamwork. = 我们一直强调团队合作的重要性。


过去完成时:
He had emphasized the dangers before the trip. = 他在旅行前就强调了危险性。
She had emphasized the need for caution earlier. = 她之前就强调了谨慎的必要性。


将来时:
They will emphasize the benefits of the new policy. = 他们将强调新政策的好处。
She will emphasize the importance of punctuality. = 她将强调准时的重要性。


虚拟语气:
If he emphasized his points more clearly, he would have a better presentation. = 如果他更清晰地强调他的观点，他的演讲就会更好。
I wish she had emphasized the safety precautions. = 我希望她当时强调了安全预防措施。


被动语态:
The importance of exercise is emphasized by many doctors. = 许多医生都强调运动的重要性。
The need for change was emphasized in the meeting. = 会议上强调了改变的必要性。


疑问句:
What did he emphasize in his speech? = 他在演讲中强调了什么？
What aspects of the project are being emphasized? = 项目的哪些方面正在被强调？


否定句:
He did not emphasize the negative aspects. = 他没有强调消极方面。
The report did not emphasize the long-term consequences. = 报告没有强调长远的影响。
