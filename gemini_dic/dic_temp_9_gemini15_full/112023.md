long-serving: /ˌlɒŋˈsɜːvɪŋ/ | /ˌlɔːŋˈsɝːvɪŋ/ | adj. | 资深；长期服务的 |  Describes someone who has held a position or been involved in something for a long period of time. It emphasizes the duration and often implies experience and dedication. |


例句：
The long-serving mayor finally retired after 20 years in office.  = 这位长期担任市长的官员在任职20年后终于退休了。

She is a long-serving member of the board, known for her wise counsel. = 她是一位资深的董事会成员，以其明智的建议而闻名。


近义词：veteran, experienced, established, seasoned


反义词：newly appointed, inexperienced, junior


词性:形容词
