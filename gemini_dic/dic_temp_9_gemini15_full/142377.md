orthopyroxene: /ˌɔːrθoʊˈpaɪrəksiːn/ | n. | 正斜方辉石 | A common rock-forming mineral belonging to the pyroxene group, characterized by its orthorhombic crystal structure and chemical composition. It is typically found in igneous and metamorphic rocks.  | Example: Orthopyroxene is a key mineral in many basalts and gabbros. 正斜方辉石是许多玄武岩和辉长岩中的关键矿物。 | 近义词: clinopyroxene (斜方辉石) | 反义词:  None (no direct antonym, but contrasting terms include clinopyroxene, which has a different crystal structure) | 名词

词源:

* **ortho-**:  From Greek *orthos*, meaning "straight," "right," or "correct," referring to the orthorhombic crystal system.
* **pyroxene**: From Greek *pyr* ("fire") and *xenos* ("stranger"), originally referring to a mineral found in volcanic rocks.  The name alludes to its occurrence in volcanic rocks, though the name is now applied to a broader group of minerals.
