co-optive:/koʊˈɑːptɪv/ | adj. | 合作的；联合的；吸收同化的 |  To make something part of a larger system or group, often by absorbing or incorporating it.  It suggests a process of assimilation, sometimes subtly or strategically, rather than through force.  This can apply to ideas, people, or organizations. |


例句：

The company's co-optive strategy involved acquiring smaller competitors to expand its market share. = 这家公司的合作策略包括收购较小的竞争对手以扩大其市场份额。

The government's co-optive approach to dissent involved incorporating some opposition leaders into the ruling party. = 政府处理异见的合作方式包括将一些反对派领导人纳入执政党。


近义词：

incorporative, assimilative, integrative, appropriative


反义词：

divisive, exclusive, segregative


词性:

adjective
