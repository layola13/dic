exocarp:/ˈɛksəʊkɑːrp/ | n. | 外果皮 | The exocarp is the outermost layer of a fruit, also known as the skin or peel.  It protects the inner parts of the fruit and often contributes to its flavor and texture.  | Example: The exocarp of an orange is easily peeled.  橙子的外果皮很容易剥开。  |近义词：peel, rind, skin | 反义词：endocarp, mesocarp
