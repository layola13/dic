columbuss:/kəˈlʌmbəs/| n. |哥伦布（人名）|Columbus is a proper noun referring to Christopher Columbus, the Italian explorer who is credited with completing the first European voyages across the Atlantic Ocean, opening the way for the extensive transoceanic colonization of the Americas.|例句：Christopher Columbus sailed the ocean blue in 1492.  哥伦布在1492年横渡了浩瀚的海洋。|近义词：None (proper noun)|反义词：None (proper noun)|名词

词源:  The name "Columbus" is derived from the Latin word "columbus," meaning "dove."  It is a common name in Italy and Spain.  It is believed to be given to Christopher Columbus due to the dove's symbolism of peace or the Holy Spirit, although the connection isn't definitively proven.
