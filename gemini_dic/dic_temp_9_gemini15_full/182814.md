repealed:/rɪˈpiːld/| v.|废除，撤销|To repeal a law or act means to officially cancel or abolish it.|The government repealed the unpopular tax law.|近义词：abolish, revoke, annul|反义词：enact, institute, establish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government repeals outdated laws. = 政府废除过时的法律。
They repeal the old regulation every year. = 他们每年都废除旧规章。

一般过去时:
Congress repealed the prohibition law. = 国会废除了禁酒法。
The court repealed its previous decision. = 法院撤销了之前的判决。

现在完成时:
They have repealed several discriminatory laws. = 他们已经废除了几项歧视性法律。
The legislature has repealed the controversial bill. = 立法机关已经废除了有争议的法案。

过去完成时:
The law had been repealed before the incident occurred. = 该法律在事件发生前已被废除。
They had repealed the tax before the election. = 他们在选举前已经废除了税收。

将来时:
The Senate will repeal the act next month. = 参议院下个月将废除这项法案。
They will repeal the outdated policy soon. = 他们很快就会废除过时的政策。

虚拟语气:
If they repealed the law, many people would be happy. = 如果他们废除这项法律，很多人都会高兴。
I wish they would repeal that unfair rule. = 我希望他们能废除那条不公平的规定。

被动语态:
The law was repealed last year. = 该法律去年被废除了。
The regulation is being repealed as we speak. = 正如我们所说，该规章正在被废除。

疑问句:
Has the law been repealed? = 这项法律被废除了吗？
Will the new policy be repealed? = 新政策会被废除吗？

否定句:
The government did not repeal the unpopular policy. = 政府没有废除那项不受欢迎的政策。
They have not yet repealed the outdated law. = 他们尚未废除过时的法律。


PHRASE->
