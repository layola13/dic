rhapsodize:/ˈræpsədaɪz/| vt.|狂想；热情地谈论；赋诗于|To speak or write in an enthusiastic and effusive manner; to express oneself in a rhapsody.  |例句：He rhapsodized about the beauty of the sunset. 他热情洋溢地谈论着日落的美丽。

例句：She rhapsodized about her travels in Italy. 她热情地描述了她意大利的旅行。


近义词：rave, enthuse, extol, eulogize|反义词：criticize, condemn, denounce|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rhapsodizes about his childhood memories. 他热情地回忆着童年往事。
She rhapsodizes about the joys of motherhood. 她热情洋溢地谈论着为人母亲的喜悦。


一般过去时:
He rhapsodized about the concert for hours. 他兴奋地谈论了几个小时的音乐会。
She rhapsodized about the delicious food she had eaten. 她兴高采烈地谈论着她吃过的美味佳肴。


现在完成时:
He has rhapsodized about that painting ever since he saw it.  自从他看到那幅画以来，他就一直对它赞不绝口。
She has rhapsodized about her new job to everyone she knows. 她已经向所有认识的人热情洋溢地描述了她的新工作。


过去完成时:
He had rhapsodized about the book before he even finished reading it.  甚至在他读完书之前，他就已经对这本书赞不绝口了。
She had rhapsodized about the trip for weeks leading up to it.  在旅行开始前的几周里，她一直兴高采烈地谈论着这次旅行。


将来时:
He will rhapsodize about his success once he achieves it.  一旦他成功了，他就会滔滔不绝地谈论他的成功。
She will rhapsodize about her wedding day for years to come.  在未来的几年里，她都会兴高采烈地谈论她的婚礼。


虚拟语气:
If he had seen the sunset, he would have rhapsodized about its beauty. 如果他看到了日落，他会热情洋溢地谈论它的美丽。
If she had tasted the wine, she would have rhapsodized about its exquisite flavor. 如果她尝过这种酒，她会赞不绝口地谈论它那绝妙的风味。


被动语态:
His travels were rhapsodized about in the newspaper article. 报纸文章热情地描述了他的旅行。
The beauty of the landscape was rhapsodized about by the tourists.  游客们热情地赞美了这片美丽的风景。


疑问句:
Does he often rhapsodize about his experiences? 他经常热情地谈论他的经历吗？
Will she rhapsodize about the concert tonight? 她今晚会热情地谈论这场音乐会吗？


否定句:
He didn't rhapsodize about the movie; he found it boring. 他并没有对这部电影赞不绝口；他觉得它很无聊。
She didn't rhapsodize about the food; she thought it was bland. 她并没有对食物赞不绝口；她认为它很清淡。


PHRASE->
rhapsodize about something: 热情地谈论某事
rhapsodize on a theme:  围绕某个主题热情地表达
rhapsodize in prose/verse: 用散文/诗歌热情地表达
rhapsodize with delight:  兴高采烈地表达
rhapsodize eloquently:  雄辩地、热情地表达


词源:
From the Greek word "rhapsody," meaning "stitching together," referring to the way ancient Greek poets combined different poetic fragments.  The word later came to mean a lyrical, often ecstatic, composition.
