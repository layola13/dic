relessor: /rɪˈlɛsɔːr/ | n. |出租人，租赁人 | The person who leases property to another.  A lessor is the one who grants a lease; the lessee is the one who receives it. This term is commonly used in legal and real estate contexts.  |Example: The lessor increased the rent after the first year of the lease. 出租人第一年租期结束后提高了租金。|近义词：landlord, landlord, owner |反义词：lessee


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "to be".  Therefore, tense variations are not applicable.  The grammatical functions relate to its use in sentences.


Example sentences demonstrating different grammatical functions:

Subject: The lessor was contacted about the repair. 出租人被联系到修理的事宜。

Object: The tenant notified the lessor of the damage. 租户通知了出租人损坏的情况。

Appositive:  John, the lessor, inspected the property.  约翰，出租人，检查了房产。

Prepositional Phrase:  The agreement was made between the lessor and lessee.  协议是在出租人和承租人之间达成的。
