crackdowns:/ˈkrækdaʊnz/| n.|镇压；严厉打击|The act of suppressing something, especially a social movement or activity, with force or authority.  It often involves strict enforcement of laws and regulations.|例句：The government launched a crackdown on drug trafficking.  政府对贩毒进行了严厉打击。|例句：There has been a recent crackdown on illegal immigration. 近期对非法移民进行了严厉打击。|近义词：suppression, repression, clampdown|反义词：leniency, tolerance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government cracks down on illegal activities. = 政府严厉打击非法活动。
Police crack down on speeding drivers. = 警察严厉打击超速驾驶。

一般过去时:
They cracked down on protests last week. = 他们上周镇压了抗议活动。
The authorities cracked down on dissent. = 当局镇压了异见。

现在完成时:
The police have cracked down on organized crime. = 警察已经严厉打击了有组织犯罪。
Authorities have cracked down on corruption. = 当局已经打击了腐败。

过去完成时:
They had cracked down on freedom of speech before the election. = 他们在选举前就已经压制了言论自由。
The regime had cracked down on any opposition. = 该政权已经镇压了任何反对势力。

将来时:
The government will crack down on tax evasion. = 政府将严厉打击逃税行为。
They will crack down on illegal gambling. = 他们将严厉打击非法赌博。

虚拟语气:
If they cracked down harder, the crime rate might decrease. = 如果他们更严厉打击犯罪，犯罪率可能会下降。
I wish the government would crack down on pollution. = 我希望政府能够严厉打击污染。

被动语态:
Illegal activities are being cracked down on. = 非法活动正在受到严厉打击。
Protests were cracked down on violently. = 抗议活动遭到了暴力镇压。

疑问句:
Will the government crack down on this issue? = 政府会对这个问题采取严厉措施吗？
Are they going to crack down on the protesters? = 他们会镇压抗议者吗？

否定句:
The government did not crack down on the protestors. = 政府没有镇压抗议者。
They haven't cracked down on corruption effectively. = 他们没有有效地打击腐败。
