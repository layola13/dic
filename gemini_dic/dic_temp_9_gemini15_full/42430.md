countercultures:/ˈkaʊntərkʌltʃərz/| n.| 反文化；对立文化 | A counterculture is a subculture whose values and norms of behavior differ significantly from the mainstream culture.  It often arises as a reaction against or rejection of the dominant culture's values.  Countercultures can be associated with social movements, artistic expression, and political activism. | Example: The hippie movement of the 1960s was a significant counterculture, rejecting materialism and embracing peace and love.  The punk rock movement of the 1970s and 80s also represented a counterculture, challenging societal norms through music and fashion. | 近义词：subculture, alternative culture | 反义词：mainstream culture, dominant culture | 名词
