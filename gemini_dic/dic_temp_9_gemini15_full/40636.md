conventionalisms:/kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪzəm/| n.|习俗；常规；传统；墨守成规|The adherence to or practice of conventional ways and ideas;  the established practices of a society or group.  It often implies a rigid or unthinking adherence to these practices, rather than a thoughtful consideration of their merits.|例句：He challenged the conventionalisms of Victorian society. 他挑战了维多利亚社会习俗。|例句：Her writing broke free from the conventionalisms of her time. 她的作品摆脱了那个时代的传统束缚。|近义词：traditions, customs, norms, formalities|反义词：unconventionality, originality, innovation|名词
