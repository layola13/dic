sharpest:/ʃɑːrpɪst/ | adj. | 最锋利的；最敏锐的；最尖锐的；最剧烈的 |  The sharpest knife in the drawer is the best for cutting tomatoes.  抽屉里最锋利的刀最适合切西红柿。  His sharpest criticism was reserved for his own mistakes. 他最尖锐的批评留给了他自己的错误。|  keen, acute, incisive | blunt, dull, obtuse | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The knife is sharpest. 这把刀最锋利。
His mind is sharpest in the morning. 他早上思维最敏捷。

一般过去时:
The knife was sharpest yesterday. 这把刀昨天最锋利。
Her eyesight was sharpest when she was younger. 她年轻时视力最好。

现在完成时:
This has been the sharpest winter in decades. 这是几十年来最寒冷的冬天。
The pain has been sharpest in the last hour.  过去一小时疼痛最剧烈。


过去完成时:
The pain had been sharpest before the medication kicked in.  药物起效之前疼痛最剧烈。
The blade had been sharpest before it was used to cut the rope. 刀片在用来割绳子之前最锋利。

将来时:
The competition will be sharpest next year. 明年的竞争将更加激烈。
The image will be sharpest with the highest resolution setting.  在最高分辨率设置下图像将最清晰。


虚拟语气:
If the knife were sharpest, it would cut through the meat easily. 如果刀最锋利，它就能很容易地切过肉。
If my hearing were sharpest, I could hear the bird's song. 如果我的听力最敏锐，我就能听到鸟鸣声。

被动语态:
The picture is considered the sharpest in the exhibition. 这幅画被认为是展览会上最清晰的。

疑问句:
Which knife is sharpest? 哪把刀最锋利？
Whose eyesight is sharpest? 谁的视力最敏锐？


否定句:
The image isn't sharpest at the edges. 图像边缘不够清晰。
His senses aren't sharpest after a long day.  经过漫长的一天后，他的感官不再敏锐。

词源:
源自古英语 *scearp,  与古高地德语 *scharp, 古弗里西亚语 *skerp, 古萨克森语 *skerp, 荷兰语 scherp, 古诺尔斯语 *skarpr, 德语 scharf 同源，其含义均为“锋利的，尖锐的”。  "-est" 是英语最高级后缀。
