enslavements:/ɪnˈsleɪvmənts/| n. |奴役；奴隶制度|The state of being enslaved; the act of enslaving others.  It refers to the condition of being deprived of freedom and forced into servitude, often involving coercion and exploitation.  This can encompass various forms of oppression and control, both historical and contemporary.|例句：The historical enslavements of Africans had a devastating impact on generations.  历史上非洲人的奴役对几代人都产生了毁灭性的影响。

The continued enslavements of people in modern forms of human trafficking are a global crisis.  现代人口贩卖中持续存在的奴役是全球性的危机。|近义词：bondage, servitude, captivity, subjugation |反义词：freedom, liberty, emancipation
