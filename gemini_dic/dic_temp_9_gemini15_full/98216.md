insecure:/ˌɪn.sɪˈkjʊr/| adj.| 不安全的；缺乏自信的；不稳定的 | lacking self-confidence or assurance; not safe or secure. |例句：He felt insecure about his ability to do the job. 他对自己完成这项工作的能力感到没有把握。|例句：The house is insecure, needing better locks on the doors. 这房子不安全，门上需要更好的锁。|近义词：anxious, apprehensive, unsure, unstable |反义词：confident, secure, stable, safe


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He feels insecure about his future. 他对未来感到没有安全感。
She is insecure in her relationships.  她在人际关系中缺乏安全感。

一般过去时:
I felt insecure after the presentation. 演讲结束后，我感到没有安全感。
They were insecure about their financial situation. 他们对自己的财务状况感到不安。

现在完成时:
He has been insecure ever since the accident. 自从那次事故以来，他一直缺乏安全感。
She has felt insecure around tall people her whole life. 她一生都对高个子人感到不安。


过去完成时:
I had felt insecure before meeting my new boss. 在见到我的新老板之前，我感到没有安全感。
She had been insecure about her singing ability for years. 她多年来一直对自己的歌唱能力缺乏自信。

将来时:
I will feel insecure if I don't get the job. 如果我没有得到这份工作，我会感到没有安全感。
She will probably be insecure about her new role at first. 她一开始可能会对她的新角色感到不安。

虚拟语气:
If I were more secure, I wouldn't be so insecure. 如果我更有安全感，我就不会这么没有自信了。
I wish I weren't so insecure about my appearance. 我希望我不那么在意我的外表。
