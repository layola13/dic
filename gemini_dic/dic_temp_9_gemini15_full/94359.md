implacability:/ɪmˌplækəˈbɪləti/ | n. |不宽容；不屈服；坚决；顽固 |The implacability of the judge was evident in his harsh sentence.  法官的不宽容在他的严厉判决中显而易见。|近义词：unyieldingness, inflexibility, relentlessness |反义词：leniency, mercy, forgiveness |名词


英文解析:Implacability refers to the inability or unwillingness to be appeased, pacified, or moved from a position.  It suggests a firm, unyielding, and often inflexible attitude.  This characteristic can be applied to people, situations, or even natural forces.


例句:

The implacability of the storm prevented any rescue attempts.  暴风雨的无情阻止了任何救援尝试。

Her implacability in her pursuit of justice was admirable, even if it made her unpopular. 她对正义的执着追求令人钦佩，即使这让她不受欢迎。


词源:

The word "implacability" derives from the Latin word "placare," meaning "to appease" or "to calm." The prefix "im-" indicates negation or lack of, thus "implacability" means "the state of being unappeasable."
