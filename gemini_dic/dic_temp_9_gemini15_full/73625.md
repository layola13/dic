foreworld:/ˈfɔːrˌwɜːrld/| n. |前世界，过去的世界，旧世界 |The term "foreworld" refers to a past era or world, often one that is considered simpler or less technologically advanced than the present. It can evoke a sense of nostalgia or a contrast to the current state of affairs. The usage is primarily found in literature and philosophical discussions.  There is no standard, widely accepted definition, and its meaning depends heavily on context. | Example:  The author depicted a foreworld where life moved at a slower pace and technology was minimal.  作者描绘了一个生活节奏缓慢、科技落后的前世界。|近义词:past, bygone era, olden times |反义词:future, tomorrow, coming age |名词
