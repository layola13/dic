mummifying: /ˈmʌmɪfaɪɪŋ/ | v. | 埃及式防腐；制作木乃伊 | The process of preserving a body after death, typically by removing the internal organs, treating the body with natron (a naturally occurring salt), and wrapping it in linen bandages.  This practice was common in ancient Egypt. | Example: The ancient Egyptians were skilled at mummifying their dead.  古代埃及人在制作木乃伊方面技艺精湛。

The process of mummifying a body involved several steps, including evisceration and the application of resins.  制作木乃伊的过程包括几个步骤，包括掏空内脏和涂抹树脂。


近义词: embalming (though embalming uses different methods and is not restricted to ancient Egyptian techniques)
反义词: decomposing, decaying

词源:
From "mummy," ultimately from the Arabic word "mumiyā," which originally referred to a bitumen-like substance used medicinally, later applied to the preserved bodies themselves.  The suffix "-fying" indicates the process of making something into a mummy.
