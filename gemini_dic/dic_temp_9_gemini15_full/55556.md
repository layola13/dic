distrusted:/dɪsˈtrʌstɪd/| adj. | 不信任的，怀疑的 |  Feeling or showing a lack of trust; suspecting deceit or treachery.  |例句：He felt distrusted by his colleagues after the incident. 他在那件事发生后感到同事们不信任他。  |近义词：suspicious, doubtful, skeptical, unbelieving |反义词：trusting, confident, believing |形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They distrust their new boss. = 他们不信任他们的新老板。
He distrusts politicians in general. = 他普遍不信任政客。


一般过去时:
I distrusted his motives. = 我不相信他的动机。
She distrusted her instincts in this matter. = 在这件事上，她不相信自己的直觉。


现在完成时:
We have distrusted him since the scandal. = 自从丑闻发生以来，我们就不再信任他了。
They have distrusted the company's promises for years. = 多年来，他们一直不相信这家公司的承诺。


过去完成时:
I had distrusted him even before the evidence emerged. = 甚至在证据出现之前，我就已经不信任他了。
She had distrusted his accounts of the events for a long time. = 她很久以前就不相信他对事件的描述了。


将来时:
They will distrust him even more after this. = 之后他们会更加不信任他。
I will distrust anyone who behaves like that. = 我会不信任任何行为像那样的人。


虚拟语气:
If I had distrusted him earlier, I wouldn't have lost so much money. = 如果我早点不信任他，我就不会损失那么多钱了。
I wish I had distrusted his promises. = 我真希望我当时不相信他的承诺。


被动语态:
His claims were distrusted by many. = 他的说法被许多人不相信。
His leadership is being distrusted by the team. = 他的领导能力正受到团队的质疑。


疑问句:
Do you distrust your partner? = 你不相信你的伴侣吗？
Are they distrusted by their neighbors? = 他们被邻居们不信任吗？


否定句:
I don't distrust her completely. = 我并不完全不信任她。
They didn't distrust the initial reports. = 他们并没有不相信最初的报告。


PHRASE->
feel distrusted = 感到不被信任
be distrusted by = 被…不信任
deeply distrusted = 深度不信任
widely distrusted = 广泛不被信任
generally distrusted = 通常不被信任
