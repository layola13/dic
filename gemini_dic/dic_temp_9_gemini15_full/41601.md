corpuscle: /ˈkɔːrpʌsəl/ | n. | 微粒；血细胞 | A corpuscle is a small unattached cell, especially a blood cell.  It refers to any small particle or body.  The term is often used in biology and medicine to describe cells, particularly blood cells. | Example: Red blood corpuscles carry oxygen throughout the body.  红血球在全身输送氧气。 |  Example: The scientist observed the corpuscles under the microscope. 科学家在显微镜下观察微粒。 |近义词：cell, particle, granule | 反义词：N/A
