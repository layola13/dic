non-Malthusians:/ˌnɒnˈmælθuːziənz/ | n. |非马尔萨斯主义者 |Those who reject or oppose Malthusian theories, particularly the belief that population growth will inevitably outstrip resources, leading to widespread famine and misery.  They often advocate for technological advancements, improved resource management, or other solutions to address potential population-resource imbalances. |Example:  Many non-Malthusians believe that technological innovation can overcome resource limitations imposed by population growth.  许多非马尔萨斯主义者认为，技术创新可以克服人口增长带来的资源限制。|近义词:anti-Malthusians |反义词:Malthusians |名词
