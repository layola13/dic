/ˈkaʊntərˌkwɛstʃən/ | n. | 反问；反诘 | A question asked in response to another question, often to challenge or refute the original question.  It can be used to deflect, clarify, or even attack the initial question. | Example: "Why did you lie?" "Why would I lie?" | 近义词：rebuttal, counter-argument | 反义词：answer, response (in the sense of a direct and unchallenging reply)
