kiding: /ˈkɪdɪŋ/ | v. |开玩笑，戏弄 |To say something that is not true, often in a playful way.  |例句：Are you kidding me?  你在开玩笑吗？|例句：He was only kidding when he said he was quitting his job. 他说他要辞职只是开玩笑。|近义词：joking, teasing, jesting |反义词：serious, earnest


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I'm kidding you. = 我在跟你开玩笑。
He's kidding around with his friends. = 他在和朋友们开玩笑。

一般过去时:
She kidded me about my new haircut. = 她取笑我的新发型。
They kidded around all afternoon. = 他们整个下午都在开玩笑。

现在完成时:
I have been kidding you all along. = 我一直在跟你开玩笑。
They have kidded him relentlessly. = 他们没完没了地取笑他。

过去完成时:
I hadn't realized he was kidding until after he left. = 直到他离开后我才意识到他在开玩笑。
She had kidded him about it many times before. = 她之前已经多次取笑他这件事了。

将来时:
I will kid you later about that. = 我以后会拿这个跟你开玩笑。
He will probably kid her about her driving. = 他可能会取笑她的驾驶技术。

虚拟语气:
If I were kidding, I wouldn't be so serious. = 如果我在开玩笑，我就不会这么认真了。
I wish I hadn't kidded him so much. = 我真希望我没有那么取笑他。

被动语态:
He was kidded mercilessly by his classmates. = 他被他的同学们无情地取笑了。
She wasn't easily kidded. = 她不容易被开玩笑。

疑问句:
Are you kidding? = 你在开玩笑吗？
Were they kidding when they said that? = 他们说那话的时候是在开玩笑吗？

否定句:
I wasn't kidding when I said I was leaving. = 我说我要离开的时候不是在开玩笑。
He wasn't kidding; he was really upset. = 他不是开玩笑；他真的很难过。



PHRASE->
kid around = 开玩笑

just kidding = 开玩笑的
no kidding = 说真的
kid oneself = 自欺欺人
