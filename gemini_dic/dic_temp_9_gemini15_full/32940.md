chopped:/tʃɑːpt/| v. |切碎的，剁碎的|To cut something into small pieces, usually with a knife or other sharp instrument.  |例句：She chopped the vegetables for the soup. 她把蔬菜切碎做汤。 |例句：The butcher chopped the meat into small pieces.  屠夫把肉切成小块。|近义词：minced, diced, sliced|反义词：whole, uncut


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He chops vegetables every day. = 他每天都切菜。
She chops the onions finely. = 她把洋葱切得很细。

一般过去时:
I chopped the wood for the fire. = 我砍柴生火。
They chopped down the old tree. = 他们砍倒了那棵老树。

现在完成时:
She has chopped all the carrots. = 她已经把所有的胡萝卜都切好了。
He has chopped enough wood for the winter. = 他已经砍好了够过冬的柴火。

过去完成时:
We had chopped the vegetables before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，我们已经把蔬菜切好了。
They had chopped down the tree before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们已经砍倒了那棵树。

将来时:
I will chop the herbs for the pasta. = 我将为意大利面切碎香草。
She will chop the vegetables tomorrow. = 她明天将切菜。

虚拟语气:
If I had a sharper knife, I would chop the meat more easily. = 如果我有一把更锋利的刀，我会更容易切肉。
I wish I had chopped the onions earlier. = 我真希望我早点把洋葱切了。

被动语态:
The vegetables were chopped finely. = 蔬菜被切得很细。
The wood was chopped by the lumberjack. = 木材被伐木工砍倒了。

疑问句:
Have you chopped the garlic yet? = 你切好蒜了吗？
Did he chop the carrots? = 他切胡萝卜了吗？

否定句:
I didn't chop the onions. = 我没有切洋葱。
She hasn't chopped the parsley yet. = 她还没切香菜。
