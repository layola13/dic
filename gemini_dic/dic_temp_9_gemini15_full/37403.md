commercialising: /ˌkɔːməˈsælaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 商业化；使商业化 | The act of making something commercial or adapting something for commercial purposes.  It involves turning something that may not have been originally intended for profit into a product or service that can be sold for profit. | 例句：The company is commercialising its research findings by developing and marketing new products. 公司正在通过开发和销售新产品来实现其研究成果的商业化。


The university is commercialising its technology to generate revenue. 大学正在将其技术商业化以产生收入。

近义词：monetizing, marketing, profiting from, exploiting commercially | 反义词：decommercializing, non-profit, philanthropic | 动词

词源:
The word "commercialising" is derived from the word "commercial," which comes from the Middle French word "commercial," ultimately from the Latin word "commercium," meaning "trade" or "commerce."  The suffix "-ising" is a present participle ending indicating the process of making something commercial.
