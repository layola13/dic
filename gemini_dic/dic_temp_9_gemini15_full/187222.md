robbing:/ˈrɒbɪŋ/|/ˈrɑːbɪŋ/| v.|抢劫；剥夺；偷窃|The act of taking something by force or unlawfully.  It often implies violence or threat of violence, but can also refer to stealing without such threats.|例句：The robbers were robbing the bank. 抢劫犯正在抢劫银行。

例句：He was robbing Peter to pay Paul. 他是拆东墙补西墙。


近义词：stealing, plundering, looting, pilfering, thieving|反义词：giving, donating, contributing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are robbing the store. = 他们在抢劫商店。
He robs banks for a living. = 他靠抢劫银行为生。


一般过去时:
The thief robbed the house last night. = 窃贼昨晚抢劫了这所房子。
She robbed me of my dignity. = 她剥夺了我的尊严。


现在完成时:
They have robbed several banks this year. = 今年他们已经抢劫了几家银行。

过去完成时:
The police discovered that he had robbed the museum before. = 警方发现他以前曾抢劫过博物馆。

将来时:
They will rob the train tomorrow. = 他们明天将抢劫火车。
The new policy will rob the poor of their benefits. = 新政策将剥夺穷人的福利。


虚拟语气:

I wish they hadn't robbed me of my money. = 我真希望他们没有抢走我的钱。


被动语态:
