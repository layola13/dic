nonenclosure: /ˌnɒnɪnˈkleʊʒə(r)/ | n. | 非封闭；未封闭 | The lack of physical barriers or boundaries;  something that is not enclosed.  The term often implies a lack of containment or protection.  It can refer to physical spaces, systems, or even concepts. | 例句：The open-plan office design promotes a sense of nonenclosure. = 开放式办公室设计营造了一种非封闭感。


The company's nonenclosure approach to data security is concerning. = 公司对数据安全的非封闭式方法令人担忧。


近义词：openness, unconfinement, exposure | 反义词：enclosure, containment, confinement | 词性：名词

词源:  The word "nonenclosure" is a compound word formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the noun "enclosure" (meaning "the act of enclosing or the state of being enclosed").  "Enclosure" itself derives from the Old French "enclos,"  ultimately from Latin roots meaning "to shut in."
