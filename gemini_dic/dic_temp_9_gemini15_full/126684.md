namby-pambyish: /ˈnæmbi ˈpæmbiʃ/ | adj. | 懦弱的；无力的；过于甜腻的；矫揉造作的 |  Characterized by excessive sentimentality, weakness, or effeminacy; lacking force or spirit.  It suggests a lack of decisiveness and strength, often associated with overly delicate or sentimental behavior. |


例句：

His namby-pambyish poem lacked the strength and conviction of his earlier work.  他的过于甜腻的诗歌缺乏他早期作品的力量和说服力。

The namby-pambyish leader failed to inspire his troops.  这个懦弱的领导人未能鼓舞他的军队。


近义词：weak, feeble, effeminate, sentimental, mawkish, wishy-washy, insipid


反义词：strong, forceful, resolute, masculine, decisive, spirited


词源:

The term "namby-pamby" itself originated as a playful nickname, possibly from a children's rhyme.  Its first recorded use was in the early 18th century,  attributed to writer  Jonathan Swift who used it to mock the sentimental style of writing of Ambrose Philips.  The "-ish" suffix simply turns the noun into an adjective.
