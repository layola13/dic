glauberite:/ˈɡlɔːbəraɪt/| n.|硫酸钠钙石|Glauberite is a naturally occurring sulfate mineral composed of sodium and calcium sulfate.  It's typically found in evaporite deposits, often associated with other sulfate minerals like anhydrite and gypsum. Its crystal structure is monoclinic, and it can exhibit various colors, including colorless, white, yellow, or gray.
