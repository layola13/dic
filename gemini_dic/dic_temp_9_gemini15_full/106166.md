knock-down:/ˈnɒkdaʊn/| /ˈnɑːkdaʊn/| adj. n. v.|击倒的；廉价的；拆卸式的；击倒；拆卸|

英文解析:
As an adjective, "knock-down" describes something that is easily defeated or dismantled, or sold at a very low price. As a noun, it refers to the act of knocking someone down or a structure that is easily knocked down. As a verb, it means to knock someone or something down.


例句:
adj. (easily defeated):  He delivered a knock-down argument that silenced his opponent. 他提出了一个令人信服的论点，让对手哑口无言。

adj. (cheap): They offered a knock-down price for the used car. 他们为这辆二手车提供了低廉的价格。

n. (the act of knocking down): The knock-down of the building took several days. 拆除这栋建筑花了几天时间。

v. (to knock down): The boxer knocked his opponent down in the first round. 拳击手在第一回合就击倒了他的对手。


近义词:
adj. (easily defeated): overwhelming, decisive, crushing
adj. (cheap): inexpensive, bargain, cheap
n. (the act of knocking down): demolition, destruction, collapse
v. (to knock down): fell, topple, demolish


反义词:
adj. (easily defeated): difficult, challenging, invincible
adj. (cheap): expensive, costly, pricey
v. (to knock down): erect, build, construct

词源:
The word "knock-down" is a compound word formed from the verb "knock" and the adverb "down."  "Knock" comes from Old English *cnocian, meaning "to strike". "Down" is also of Old English origin, meaning "from a higher to a lower position".  The combined meaning developed naturally from the literal sense of physically knocking something down.
