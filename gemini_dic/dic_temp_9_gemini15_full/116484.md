materialization: /məˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 物质化；实现；具体化 | The process of becoming physical or real; the act of giving something a physical form.  It can also refer to the manifestation of something abstract into a concrete reality. |

例句：

The materialization of her dreams into a successful business was a testament to her hard work.  她的梦想变成了成功的企业，这是她努力工作的证明。

The sudden materialization of the ghost frightened everyone.  鬼魂的突然出现吓坏了所有人。


近义词：realization, manifestation, embodiment, concretization

反义词：abstraction, idealization


词性: 名词


词源:

The word "materialization" comes from the verb "materialize," which itself is derived from the noun "material" and the suffix "-ize," meaning "to cause to become."  "Material" ultimately comes from the Latin word "materia," meaning "matter" or "substance." Therefore, "materialization" literally means the act of becoming matter or substance.
