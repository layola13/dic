morpheme:/ˈmɔːrfiːm/| n. |语素|The smallest unit of language that carries meaning.  A morpheme can be a word (e.g., "cat") or a part of a word (e.g., the prefix "un-" in "unhappy" or the suffix "-ing" in "running").  Morphemes are the building blocks of words. | Example: The word "unbreakable" contains three morphemes: "un-" (meaning not), "break" (meaning to separate), and "-able" (meaning capable of being). |近义词:root, base, element|反义词:N/A|

词源:

The word "morpheme" comes from the Greek words *morphē* (μορφή), meaning "form," and *-ēma* (-ήμα), a suffix indicating "result" or "that which."  Therefore, "morpheme" literally means "that which is formed" or "the smallest unit of form."
