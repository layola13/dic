overspecializing: /ˌoʊvərˈspeʃəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |过度专业化；过于专门化 | The act of focusing too narrowly on a specific skill or area of expertise, often to the detriment of broader knowledge or adaptability.  It implies a negative consequence resulting from excessive specialization. | 例句：Overspecializing in a single technology can leave you vulnerable to market shifts. = 过度专注于单一技术可能会让你容易受到市场变化的影响。


Overspecializing in one field can limit career opportunities. = 过度专攻一个领域可能会限制职业机会。


近义词：hyper-specialization, over-qualification (in context), narrow focus


反义词：generalization, diversification, broad-based training


词源:  The word is a combination of "over-" (meaning excessively or to an excessive degree) and "specializing" (the act of specializing).  "Specializing" itself derives from "specialize," which comes from the late Middle English word "special," ultimately from the Medieval Latin "specialis," meaning "of or pertaining to a species."
