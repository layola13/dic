name-dropping: /ˈneɪmˌdrɑːpɪŋ/ | n. | 炫耀式地提及名人 |  The act of casually mentioning famous people's names in conversation to impress others or to appear more important or knowledgeable.  It often involves exaggerating the connection or relationship with the mentioned celebrities.


例句：

His constant name-dropping about his connections to Hollywood celebrities grew tiresome. = 他不断炫耀自己与好莱坞名人的关系，让人厌烦。

She was guilty of name-dropping, mentioning famous authors as if they were close personal friends. = 她犯了炫耀式提及名人的毛病，说起著名作家好像他们是亲密的朋友一样。


近义词：showing off, boasting, bragging, grandstanding

反义词：humility, modesty, self-effacement


词源:

The term is a compound of "name" and "dropping," reflecting the action of casually "dropping" or mentioning famous names into a conversation.  The term's origin is not precisely documented, but its usage reflects a long-standing social phenomenon.


用法说明:

Name-dropping is typically used in a negative context, implying insincerity and an attempt to gain social status through association.  It is often perceived as pretentious and arrogant.  However, in some contexts (e.g., a humorous self-deprecating way), it can be used ironically.
