overinclination: /ˌoʊvərɪnklɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. | 倾向过度；过度爱好；偏向 |  An excessive inclination or tendency; a leaning too far in one direction.  It suggests an imbalance or disproportionate focus on something. | The overinclination towards instant gratification is detrimental to long-term goals.  过度追求即时满足不利于长远目标。 |近义词：predilection, bias, partiality, propensity | 反义词：indifference, impartiality, objectivity |  名词

Word Origin:

The word is a combination of "over-" (meaning excessive or too much) and "inclination" (a tendency or leaning).  "Inclination" itself comes from the Latin word "inclinare," meaning "to bend or lean."
