ritualised:/ˈrɪtʃuəlaɪzd/| adj.|仪式化的；例行化的；程式化的 |  transformed into or characterized by a ritual;  following a set of established procedures or practices.  |例句：The ceremony was highly ritualised, with each step precisely defined.  仪式非常程式化，每个步骤都精确定义。

The wedding was a highly ritualised affair. 婚礼是一场高度仪式化的事件。


近义词：formalized, standardized, conventionalized, stereotyped | 反义词：improvised, spontaneous, informal, unconventional


USAGE->

词源:
From "ritual" (a religious or ceremonial act or practice) + "-ised" (past participle suffix indicating to make or become something).  The word ultimately traces back to Latin "ritus" (ritual, rite).
