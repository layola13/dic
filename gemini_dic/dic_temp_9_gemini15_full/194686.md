self-violent: /ˌsɛlfˈvaɪələnt/ | adj. | 自残的；自我暴力的 |  Self-violent refers to behavior that involves harming oneself intentionally. This can manifest in various ways, including physical self-harm (cutting, burning, hitting), self-neglect (failure to meet basic needs), and self-destructive behaviors (substance abuse, reckless driving). It's often a symptom of underlying mental health conditions like depression, anxiety, or PTSD.  | Example:  The patient exhibited self-violent tendencies, requiring close monitoring and therapeutic intervention.  患者表现出自残倾向，需要密切监控和治疗干预。 | 近义词: self-harming, self-destructive, automutilative | 反义词: self-caring, self-nurturing
