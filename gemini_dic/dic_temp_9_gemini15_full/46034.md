cycad:/ˈsaɪkæd/| n. |苏铁；铁树 |A cycad is a type of long-lived gymnosperm plant with a stout trunk and large, stiff, evergreen leaves. They are often mistaken for palms or ferns, but are more closely related to conifers. Cycads are ancient plants, with some species dating back to the Mesozoic Era.  They are found in tropical and subtropical regions around the world. |Example: The botanical garden boasts a rare collection of cycads, some of which are centuries old.  苏铁花园拥有珍稀的苏铁品种，有些已有数百年历史。|近义词：palm (in appearance only), fern (in appearance only) |反义词：None specifically, but perhaps "angiosperm" (flowering plant) could be considered a contrasting term. |名词
