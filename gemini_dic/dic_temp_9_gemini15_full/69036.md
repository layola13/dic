fatuousness:/ˈfætuəsnəs/| n. |愚蠢；轻浮；自以为是|The fatuousness of his remarks was evident to everyone.  他的话语的愚蠢显而易见。|近义词：silliness, foolishness, absurdity, vapidity|反义词：wisdom, intelligence, seriousness


英文解析:Fatuousness refers to a state of being foolish or silly, often in a self-satisfied or complacent way. It implies a lack of intelligence or good judgment, combined with an air of smugness or self-importance.  The person exhibiting fatuousness may be unaware of their own foolishness.


例句:

His fatuous grin annoyed everyone in the room. 他那傻乎乎的傻笑惹恼了房间里的每个人。

The professor's fatuous pronouncements on the subject were met with widespread skepticism. 教授关于这个主题的轻浮言论遭到了普遍的怀疑。


词源:

The word "fatuousness" is derived from the Middle English word "fatuous," which in turn comes from the Latin word "fatuus," meaning "foolish" or "silly."  The suffix "-ness" adds the quality or state of being.
