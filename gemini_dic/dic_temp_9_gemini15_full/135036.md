/ˌnɒnˈnaɪəlɪzəm/ | n. |非虚无主义|The philosophical position that rejects nihilism, affirming the existence of objective meaning, value, or purpose in life. It actively opposes the nihilistic view that life is inherently meaningless.  Nonnihilism encompasses a wide range of beliefs, from religious faith to secular humanism, all united by their rejection of nihilism's core tenets.| The rise of nonnihilism in the late 20th century suggests a growing dissatisfaction with the perceived meaninglessness of modern life. |近义词：meaningfulism, existentialism (partially)|反义词：nihilism
