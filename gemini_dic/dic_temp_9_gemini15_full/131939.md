noncontractual: /ˌnɒnkənˈtræktʃuəl/ | adj. | 非合同的，非契约的 | Not forming part of or covered by a contract; not legally binding.  |例句：The agreement is noncontractual and therefore not legally enforceable.  这份协议是非合同性的，因此在法律上不可强制执行。

The understanding between the two parties was noncontractual.  双方之间的谅解是非合同性的。


近义词：informal, unofficial, voluntary, unenforceable | 反义词：contractual, binding, obligatory


词源：
* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not"
* **contractual**:  adjective derived from "contract," ultimately from Old French *contracter* "to contract, draw together," from Latin *contrahere* "to draw together, contract," from *con-* "together" + *trahere* "to draw."


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms in typical usage.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  The following examples demonstrate its usage in different sentence structures:

肯定句:
The arrangement is purely noncontractual. = 这项安排纯粹是非合同性的。
His involvement was on a noncontractual basis. = 他的参与是非合同性的。


否定句:
The agreement wasn't noncontractual; it was legally binding. = 这份协议并非非合同性的；它是具有法律约束力的。
The relationship between the two companies is not noncontractual. = 这两家公司之间的关系并非非合同性的。


疑问句:
Is the agreement noncontractual? = 这份协议是非合同性的吗？
Was the arrangement entirely noncontractual? = 这项安排完全是非合同性的吗？


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "noncontractual."  Its usage is typically standalone within sentences.
