climacterical:/ˌklaɪmækˈtɛrɪkəl/ | adj. |  climacteric的形容词形式，更年期的；转折点的；决定性的 | Relating to or characteristic of the climacteric, especially the female climacteric (menopause).  The term can also describe a crucial or decisive point or stage in a process. |例句：She experienced several climacterical symptoms, including hot flashes and mood swings. 她经历了一些更年期症状，包括潮热和情绪波动。


例句：The climacterical period of a company's development often involves significant restructuring.  一个公司发展的关键时期通常伴随着重大的重组。


近义词：critical, crucial, decisive, turning-point | 反义词：insignificant, inconsequential, minor


词源:  From "climacteric" + "-al".  "Climacteric" itself comes from Greek  κλιμακτηρικός (klimaktērikós), meaning "relating to a ladder or rung," referring to the critical stages or turning points in life, often associated with age and physical changes.
