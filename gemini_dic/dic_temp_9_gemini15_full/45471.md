curb:/kɜːrb/ /kɝb/| n. v.|路边石；抑制；控制；约束| A curb is a stone or concrete edge at the side of a road or pavement.  It can also mean to restrain or control something.|例句：The city council plans to install new curbs along Main Street.  市议会计划在主街上安装新的路边石。 The government needs to curb inflation.  政府需要控制通货膨胀。|近义词：restraint, check, control, limit|反义词：acceleration, encouragement, stimulation|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They curb their spending. = 他们控制他们的开支。
We curb the dog every evening. = 我们每天晚上都拴住那条狗。


一般过去时:
He curbed his anger. = 他抑制了他的怒火。
The police curbed the illegal activity. = 警察制止了非法活动。


现在完成时:
They have curbed their spending habits. = 他们已经控制了他们的消费习惯。
We have curbed the spread of the disease. = 我们已经控制了疾病的传播。


过去完成时:
She had curbed her appetite before the party. = 她在聚会前已经控制了她的食欲。
The government had curbed inflation before the election. = 政府在大选前已经控制了通货膨胀。


将来时:
They will curb their emissions. = 他们将减少排放。
We will curb the dog's tendency to chase cars. = 我们将控制那条狗追逐汽车的倾向。


虚拟语气:
If they curbed their spending, they would save more money. = 如果他们控制开支，他们就能省更多钱。
If the government had curbed inflation earlier, the economy would be stronger now. = 如果政府早些控制通货膨胀，现在的经济状况就会更好。


被动语态:
Spending should be curbed. = 开支应该受到控制。
The spread of the disease was curbed by strict quarantine measures. = 疾病的传播通过严格的隔离措施得到了控制。


疑问句:
How can we curb this problem? = 我们如何才能控制这个问题？
Will they curb their power? = 他们会控制他们的权力吗？


否定句:
They did not curb their enthusiasm. = 他们没有抑制他们的热情。
The government failed to curb the rising crime rate. = 政府未能控制不断上升的犯罪率。
