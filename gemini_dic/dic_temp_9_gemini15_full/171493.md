/pʌf/| n. |噗的一声；微风；少量；|A sudden puff of wind blew my hat off.一阵突如其来的微风吹掉了我的帽子。|近义词：gust, breeze, puff of wind |反义词：gale, storm


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The baker puffs the dough. = 面包师将面团吹起。
A gentle breeze puffs the sails. = 轻柔的微风吹鼓着船帆。


一般过去时:
He puffed on his pipe. = 他抽着他的烟斗。
The wind puffed the clouds across the sky. = 风把云吹过天空。


现在完成时:
She has puffed up her cheeks. = 她已经鼓起了她的脸颊。
The child has puffed out his chest with pride. = 孩子骄傲地挺起了胸膛。


过去完成时:
He had puffed himself out before the race. = 他在比赛前已经让自己累得气喘吁吁。
The wind had puffed the leaves into a pile. = 风已经把树叶吹成一堆了。


将来时:
She will puff out the candles. = 她将会把蜡烛吹灭。
The strong wind will puff the boat along. = 强风会把船吹动。


虚拟语气:
If he puffed harder, he might blow out the candle. = 如果他更用力地吹，他可能会把蜡烛吹灭。
I wish I hadn't puffed so much on that cigarette. = 我真希望我没有抽那么多烟。


被动语态:
The balloons were puffed up with air. = 气球被吹胀了。
The sails were puffed out by the wind. = 船帆被风吹鼓起来。


疑问句:
Did you puff out your chest when you received the award? = 你领奖时是不是挺起了胸膛？
Does he often puff on his electronic cigarette? = 他经常抽电子烟吗？


否定句:
He didn't puff out his cheeks in anger. = 他没有生气地鼓起脸颊。
She did not puff the smoke away. = 她没有把烟吹走。
