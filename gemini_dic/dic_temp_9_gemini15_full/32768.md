chock-a-block:/tʃɒk ə blɒk/| adj. |塞满的；挤满的；充满的 |Completely full; crowded.  The phrase originated from the nautical term "chock," meaning a wedge used to secure something in place.  When things are "chock-a-block," it implies they are tightly packed, like wedges filling a space. |例句：The market was chock-a-block with shoppers. 市场挤满了购物者。

The train was chock-a-block, and I could barely find a seat. 火车里挤满了人，我几乎找不到座位。|近义词：packed, crammed, crowded, full, brimming |反义词：empty, sparse, vacant
