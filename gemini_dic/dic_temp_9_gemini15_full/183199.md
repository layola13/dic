/rɛˈprɛz/ | v. | 代表；象征；表现；再现 | To stand for or be a sign of; to embody or express; to portray or depict accurately.  | The flag represents the nation.  这面旗帜代表这个国家。 | The symbol represents peace. 这个符号代表和平。 | embody, symbolize, signify | misrepresent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He represents his country in the Olympics. 他代表他的国家参加奥运会。
This painting represents the artist's emotions. 这幅画代表了艺术家的情感。


一般过去时:
She represented her company at the conference. 她代表公司参加了会议。
The actor represented the character convincingly. 这位演员令人信服地扮演了这个角色。


现在完成时:
He has represented this district for many years. 他多年来一直代表这个地区。
The artist has successfully represented the beauty of nature.  这位艺术家成功地展现了大自然的美丽。


过去完成时:
They had represented their views before the meeting. 他们在会议之前已经表达了他们的观点。
The evidence had clearly represented the defendant's guilt. 证据清楚地表明了被告的罪行。


将来时:
She will represent her school in the debate. 她将代表学校参加辩论。
The play will represent a turning point in the author's career. 这部戏将代表作者职业生涯的转折点。


虚拟语气:
If he represented the facts honestly, the situation would be different. 如果他诚实地陈述事实，情况就会不同。
I wish the painting better represented the original scene. 我希望这幅画能更好地展现原景。


被动语态:
The country is well represented in the international forum. 这个国家在国际论坛上有很好的代表。
His ideas were represented in the final report. 他的想法在最终报告中得到了体现。


疑问句:
Does this symbol represent anything specific? 这个符号代表什么具体的东西吗？
Who represents your interests in this matter?  在这件事情上谁代表你的利益？


否定句:
The data does not represent the true situation. 数据不能代表真实情况。
The painting failed to represent the subject accurately. 这幅画未能准确地描绘主题。



PHRASE->
represent oneself  :  自我介绍
represent a challenge :  构成挑战
represent a threat : 构成威胁
represent the interests of : 代表...的利益
be well represented : 有充分的代表
