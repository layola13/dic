self-formations:/selfˌfɔːrˈmeɪʃənz/| n.| 自我形成，自我构造 | Structures or processes that arise spontaneously from within a system, without external guidance or imposition.  This term is often used in various fields like linguistics, geology, and psychology to describe the emergence of patterns or structures without external intervention.  |Example: The intricate self-formations in the sand dunes were a testament to the power of natural processes.  沙丘中复杂的自我构造证明了自然过程的力量。

|近义词: self-organization, spontaneous generation, self-assembly |反义词: externally imposed structures, artificial constructs|名词
