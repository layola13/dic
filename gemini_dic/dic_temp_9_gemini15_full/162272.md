preabundant: /ˌpriːəˈbʌndənt/ | adj. | 非常丰富的，极其丰富的 | Extremely abundant; more than abundant.  The word suggests an abundance that surpasses the usual or expected level. |  例句：The preabundant harvest allowed for generous donations to the food bank.  非常丰收使得向食物银行慷慨捐赠成为可能。
例句：The preabundant resources of the region fueled its rapid economic growth.  该地区极其丰富的资源推动了其经济的快速增长。| 近义词：superabundant, overflowing, profuse, copious | 反义词：scarce, deficient, meager, scant | 词性：形容词


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and thus doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  The usage is straightforward; it modifies a noun to describe an exceptionally high quantity.

一般现在时:
The harvest is preabundant this year. = 今年的收成非常丰富。
The resources of the company are preabundant. = 公司的资源极其丰富。

一般过去时:
The harvest was preabundant last year. = 去年收成极其丰富。
The oil reserves were preabundant before the depletion. = 在枯竭之前，石油储备非常丰富。

现在完成时:
The region has been preabundant in natural resources. = 该地区一直自然资源丰富。
The farmer has had a preabundant crop this season. = 这位农民本季获得了丰收。

过去完成时:
The country had been preabundant in resources before the war. = 在战争爆发前，这个国家资源丰富。
The lake had been preabundant with fish before pollution. = 在污染之前，湖里鱼很多。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
