dividing: /dɪˈvaɪdɪŋ/ | v. | 分割，划分，分开 | The act of separating something into parts or sections.  It often implies a process of separating something into distinct groups or categories. | 例句：The wall divides the two rooms. 这堵墙隔开了两个房间。

The river divides the city into two parts. 这条河将城市分成了两部分。


近义词：separating, splitting, partitioning, sharing, distributing | 反义词：uniting, joining, combining, merging


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is dividing the cake into four pieces. 他正在把蛋糕分成四块。
She divides her time between work and family. 她把时间分配在工作和家庭之间。


一般过去时:
They divided the land among themselves. 他们把土地分给了自己。
We divided the tasks equally. 我们平均分配了任务。


现在完成时:
She has divided her inheritance among her children. 她已将遗产分给了她的孩子们。
They have divided the company into several departments. 他们已将公司分成几个部门。


过去完成时:
Before the war, the country had divided into two factions. 战争爆发前，国家已分裂成两个派系。
They had divided the spoils before I arrived. 我到达之前他们就已经瓜分了战利品。


将来时:
We will divide the profits equally. 我们将平均分配利润。
The judge will divide the property between the two parties. 法官将把财产分配给双方。


虚拟语气:
If we divided the work more evenly, we would finish sooner. 如果我们更平均地分配工作，我们会更快完成。
I wish I had divided my time more wisely. 我希望我以前能更明智地分配时间。


被动语态:
The cake was divided into six slices. 蛋糕被切成了六片。
The class was divided into smaller groups for the activity.  为了这次活动，班级被分成了更小的组。


疑问句:
How will you divide the responsibilities? 你将如何分配责任？
How is the land divided? 土地是如何划分的？


否定句:
They did not divide the money fairly. 他们没有公平地分配这笔钱。
He didn't divide the tasks efficiently. 他没有有效地分配任务。
