imprescriptible: /ˌɪmˈskriptibl/ | adj. | 不可诉讼的；无法获得时效的；永久的 |  Incapable of being extinguished by the lapse of time; not subject to prescription.  This term often relates to legal rights or claims that cannot be lost through inaction or the passage of time.  It implies a right that is permanently vested and cannot be challenged based on the passage of time.


例句：

The right to life is considered imprescriptible. = 生命权被认为是不可剥夺的。

His claim to the land was imprescriptible due to ancient inheritance. = 由于古老的继承权，他对这块土地的所有权是不可否认的。


近义词：inalienable, perpetual, everlasting, permanent

反义词：prescriptible, forfeitable, waivable


词源:

The word "imprescriptible" is derived from the Latin prefix "im-" (not) + "prescriptible" (capable of being prescribed or limited by prescription).  "Prescription" in this context refers to the legal principle of acquiring rights or losing them through the passage of time and inaction.
