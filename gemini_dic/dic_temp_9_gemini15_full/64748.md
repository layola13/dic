escout:/eˈskaʊt/| n. |侦察兵，童子军|A scout is a person, especially a member of the Scouts, who explores or investigates an area; a boy scout.|例句：He was a scout in the army. 他是军队里的侦察兵。|例句：The scouts are learning survival skills. 童子军们正在学习生存技能。|近义词：explorer, pathfinder, scout leader|反义词：N/A|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scouts the area for enemies. 他侦察该地区是否有敌人。
She scouts for talent in the city. 她在城市里寻找人才。


一般过去时:
They scouted the route before the march. 他们在行军前侦察了路线。
He scouted the forest for berries. 他在森林里寻找浆果。


现在完成时:
We have scouted several locations for the new office. 我们已经为新办公室勘察了几个地点。
She has scouted many potential candidates for the job. 她已经物色了许多潜在的求职者。


过去完成时:
They had scouted the area thoroughly before the attack. 他们在攻击前已经彻底侦察了该地区。
She had scouted the market for weeks before making her investment.  她在投资前已经考察了市场数周。


将来时:
We will scout the terrain tomorrow. 我们明天将侦察地形。
They will scout the competition before launching their product. 他们在推出产品之前会调查竞争对手。


虚拟语气:
If I were a scout, I would be more careful. 如果我是侦察兵，我会更加小心。
I wish I had scouted the area better before we started. 我希望我们在出发前能更好地侦察该地区。


被动语态:
The area was scouted by the soldiers. 该地区由士兵们侦察过。
The candidates were scouted by the company's recruiters. 候选人被公司的招聘人员物色过。


疑问句:
Did you scout the area before you went in? 你进去之前有侦察过该地区吗？
Has she scouted any suitable locations yet? 她已经找到任何合适的地点了吗？


否定句:
They did not scout the area properly. 他们没有正确地侦察该地区。
She hasn't scouted the market well enough. 她对市场的调查还不够充分。


PHRASE->
scout around:四处寻找
scout for:寻找，物色
scout out:侦察，勘探
boy scout:童子军
girl scout:女童子军
