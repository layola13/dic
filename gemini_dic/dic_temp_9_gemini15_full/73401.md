foreignly:/ˈfɒrənli/ /ˈfɔːrənli/ | adv. | 外国地；外国式地 | In a foreign manner; characteristic of a foreigner.  This word describes something done or existing in a way that is characteristic of a foreign country or people. It often implies a lack of familiarity or understanding of local customs or norms.  | He spoke foreignly, with a heavy accent. 他说着一口浓重的外语口音。

She dressed foreignly, in brightly colored clothes unlike anything seen locally. 她穿着异域风格的鲜艳衣服，与当地人的穿着截然不同。

|近义词：extraneaously, internationally (in a broader sense)|反义词：locally, domestically, naturally|副词|

USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and thus doesn't conjugate like verbs or nouns.  The usage is always related to describing the manner in which an action is performed or a state exists.  Examples are provided above in the definition.


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases using "foreignly."  The word is generally used straightforwardly to describe a manner.
