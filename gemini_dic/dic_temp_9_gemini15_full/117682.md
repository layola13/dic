meistersinger:/ˈmaɪstəˌsɪŋər/| n. |德语歌咏比赛的歌手；纽伦堡行会歌手；大师歌手|A meistersinger was a member of a singing guild in medieval Germany, particularly active in Nuremberg.  They composed and performed songs according to strict rules and conventions.  The term evokes a sense of tradition, craftsmanship, and adherence to established forms.  The word is often used figuratively to refer to someone who displays similar dedication and adherence to rules within their own field.|The Nuremberg Meistersingers were known for their strict adherence to poetic and musical rules.|近义词：guild singer, artisan singer|反义词：improviser, free-style singer
