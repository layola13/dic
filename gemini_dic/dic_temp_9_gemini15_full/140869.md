on-the-rocks:/ˌɒn ðə rɒks/|adj.|加冰的；（指境况）非常糟糕的；|on the rocks means with ice added, typically referring to a drink. It can also mean in a very difficult or precarious situation.|例句：I'll have a whiskey on the rocks. = 我要一杯加冰的威士忌。
例句：Their marriage is on the rocks. = 他们的婚姻正处于危机之中。|近义词：with ice, precarious, troubled, difficult|反义词：smooth, easy, stable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He always orders his scotch on the rocks. = 他总是点加冰的苏格兰威士忌。
The economy is currently on the rocks. = 目前经济状况非常糟糕。

一般过去时:
She had her gin on the rocks last night. = 她昨晚喝了加冰的杜松子酒。
The company was on the rocks before the merger. = 在合并之前，公司情况非常糟糕。

现在完成时:
He has always preferred his drinks on the rocks. = 他一直喜欢喝加冰的饮料。
The project has been on the rocks since the funding was cut. = 自从资金被削减以来，该项目一直进展得很不顺利。

过去完成时:
The relationship had been on the rocks for months before they separated. = 在他们分开之前，他们的关系已经糟糕了好几个月了。
The business had been on the rocks long before the bankruptcy. = 破产很久以前，这家公司就已经经营困难了。

将来时:
I think this deal will be on the rocks if we don't reach an agreement soon. = 我认为如果我们不尽快达成协议，这笔交易将会失败。
Their marriage will likely be on the rocks if they continue to argue. = 如果他们继续争吵，他们的婚姻很可能会破裂。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't invested wisely, his business would be on the rocks now. = 如果他没有明智地投资，他的生意现在就会一团糟。
If they had communicated better, their marriage might not be on the rocks. = 如果他们沟通得更好，他们的婚姻可能不会走到尽头。

被动语态:
The negotiation was on the rocks for several weeks before a compromise was reached. = 在达成妥协之前，谈判僵持了好几个星期。
The relationship was already on the rocks before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，这段关系就已经岌岌可危了。

疑问句:
Is your relationship on the rocks? = 你的关系是不是很糟糕？
Is the company on the rocks financially? = 公司的财务状况是不是很糟糕？

否定句:
Their marriage isn't on the rocks; they're very happy. = 他们的婚姻并没有问题，他们非常幸福。
The project isn't on the rocks; it's progressing well. = 该项目进展顺利，没有遇到什么问题。
