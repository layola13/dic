disjuncture:/dɪsˈdʒʌŋk(tʃ)ər/| n. |脱节；不连贯；中断 | A lack of connection or continuity; a break or interruption.  It often refers to a disconnect between different elements, ideas, or systems. |例句：The disjuncture between theory and practice is a major problem. 理论与实践脱节是一个主要问题。

The economic downturn caused a disjuncture in the company's growth. 经济衰退导致公司发展中断。


近义词：discontinuity, gap, break, interruption, disconnect, inconsistency

反义词：continuity, connection, coherence, consistency


词源:  From Middle French *disjoncture, from Latin dis- ("apart") + junctura ("a joining").  The Latin root "jungere" means "to join".
