pentamerus:/pɛnˈtæmərəs/| n. |五聚体；(古生物)五射珊瑚属|A pentamerus is a type of extinct brachiopod, a marine invertebrate with a shell composed of two parts.  The name refers to the five-rayed structure often found in its shell.  In modern usage, "pentamerus" can also refer to any molecule or structure composed of five subunits.|The fossil record shows a wide distribution of pentamerus during the Ordovician and Silurian periods.|近义词：brachiopod (in a broader sense)|反义词：无
