popularize:/ˈpɑːpjʊləraɪz/| vt. |普及；推广；使流行|To make (something) popular or widely known.  The process involves making something accessible and appealing to a larger audience. This often involves simplification, adaptation, and effective communication strategies.|例句：The scientist popularized his theory through a series of accessible articles. 科学家通过一系列通俗易懂的文章普及了他的理论。

例句：The government is trying to popularize the use of renewable energy. 政府正在努力推广可再生能源的使用。


近义词：promote, disseminate, spread, publicize, popularise (British spelling)


反义词：suppress, obscure, conceal, suppress


词性：动词

词源:
Popularize comes from the word "popular," meaning "of the people," combined with the suffix "-ize," meaning "to make" or "to cause to become."  Therefore, popularize means "to make popular."  The word's roots trace back to the Latin word "populus," meaning "people."
