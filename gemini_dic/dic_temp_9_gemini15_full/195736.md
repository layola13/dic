Semites:/ˈsɛmaɪts/| n.|闪米特人；闪族人|A member of a group of related peoples, including the Jews, Arabs, and Assyrians, who speak Semitic languages and share a common cultural heritage.  The term is often used in a broad sense to refer to speakers of Semitic languages, but it can also be used in a more narrow sense to refer to specific ethnic groups.|Example:  The history of the Semites is long and complex, marked by both periods of great achievement and profound suffering. 闪米特人的历史悠久而复杂，既有辉煌成就的时期，也有深重苦难的时期。|近义词：Semitic people|反义词：None specifically, but terms like "non-Semitic" or descriptions of specific non-Semitic groups could be considered opposites.
