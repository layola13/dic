might:/maɪt/| modal v.|可能，也许|Might indicates possibility or uncertainty, often expressing a less certain possibility than "may" or "could".  It can also express a polite request or suggestion. |例句：It might rain later.  =  一会儿可能下雨。  

例句：Might I borrow your pen? = 我可以借一下你的钢笔吗？|近义词：may, could, possibly, perhaps|反义词：certainly, definitely, must


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I might go to the park later. = 我待会儿可能去公园。
She might be at home. = 她可能在家。


一般过去时:
I might have gone to the park yesterday, but I didn't. = 我昨天可能去公园了，但是我没有去。
He might have been ill, but he didn't see a doctor. = 他可能病了，但他没去看医生。


现在完成时:
They might have finished the project by now. = 他们现在可能已经完成了项目。
She might have already left for work. = 她可能已经去上班了。


过去完成时:
I might have known better than to trust him. = 我本来应该知道不要相信他。
We might have seen the movie already, but I don't remember. = 我们可能已经看过这部电影了，但我记不清了。


将来时:
We might go to the beach next weekend. = 我们下周末可能去海滩。
It might snow tomorrow. = 明天可能下雪。


虚拟语气:
I might have done better if I had studied harder. = 如果我学习更努力的话，我可能会做得更好。
She might have won the race if she hadn't fallen. = 如果她没摔倒的话，她可能赢得了比赛。


被动语态:
The problem might have been solved already. = 这个问题可能已经被解决了。
The report might be finished by tomorrow. = 报告可能在明天完成。


疑问句:
Might I ask you a question? = 我可以问你一个问题吗？
Might it be possible to reschedule the meeting? = 是否有可能重新安排会议？


否定句:
I might not go to the party. = 我可能不去参加派对。
He might not have seen the email. = 他可能没有看到邮件。


PHRASE->
might as well: 不妨
might just as well: 也许还不如
it might be said that: 可以说
there might be: 可能有
might as well have: 倒不如
