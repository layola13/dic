coincidence:/koʊˈɪnsɪdəns/| n. |巧合，偶然的相似|The striking similarity between the two events is purely coincidental.  两件事之间惊人的相似之处纯粹是巧合。|chance, accident, fortuity|design, intention, inevitability|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This is no coincidence; it's planned. = 这不是巧合；这是计划好的。
Coincidence plays a big role in our lives. = 巧合在我们的生活中扮演着重要的角色。


一般过去时:
It was a strange coincidence that we met in Paris. = 我们在巴黎相遇是一个奇怪的巧合。
Their meeting was purely coincidental. = 他们的相遇纯属巧合。


现在完成时:
There have been several coincidences recently. = 最近发生了几件巧合的事情。
Many coincidences have led me to believe in fate. = 许多巧合让我相信命运。


过去完成时:
It was a coincidence that he had already left before I arrived. = 我到达之前他已经离开了，真是巧合。
By coincidence, they had already discussed the matter. = 巧合的是，他们已经讨论过这件事了。


将来时:
It will be a coincidence if we both choose the same restaurant. = 如果我们都选择同一家餐厅，那将是巧合。
I don't think it will be a coincidence. = 我认为这不会是巧合。


虚拟语气:
If it weren't for coincidence, we wouldn't have met. = 如果不是巧合，我们就不会相遇了。
I wish it weren't just a coincidence. = 我希望这不仅仅是巧合。


被动语态:
The similarities were considered a coincidence. = 这些相似之处被认为是巧合。
The meeting was described as a pure coincidence. = 会议被描述为纯粹的巧合。


疑问句:
Is it just a coincidence? = 这仅仅是巧合吗？
Was it a coincidence or something else? = 这是巧合还是其他什么？


否定句:
It's not a coincidence; it's destiny. = 这不是巧合；这是命运。
It wasn't a coincidence that they both wore the same dress. = 他们俩都穿同样的衣服，这并非巧合。
