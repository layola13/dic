microclimate:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌklaɪmət/| n. |微气候|A small-scale climate that differs from the surrounding area, typically due to local factors such as topography, vegetation, or human activity.  A microclimate exists within a larger climate zone and is characterized by its unique temperature, humidity, wind, and sunlight conditions.|The valley floor had a unique microclimate, warmer and more humid than the surrounding hills. 山谷底部拥有独特的微气候，比周围的山丘更温暖潮湿。

The city experienced a microclimate of higher temperatures due to the urban heat island effect.  城市由于城市热岛效应而经历了更高的气温微气候。|mesoclimate, local climate|macroclimate|
