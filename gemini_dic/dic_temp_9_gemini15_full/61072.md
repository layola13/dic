ellipsis:/ɪˈlɪpsɪs/ | n. |省略号；省略；遗漏 |The omission of a word or phrase that is grammatically necessary but can be understood from the context.  It is often represented by three dots (...).|例句：The sentence ended with an ellipsis, leaving the reader to imagine the rest.  这句话以省略号结尾，让读者自行想象剩下的内容。

例句：Her account of the event contained significant ellipses; crucial details were missing. 她的事件描述有明显的遗漏；关键细节缺失。


近义词：omission, omission of words, suspension, dots |反义词：completeness, explicitness

词性：名词
