freshet:/ˈfreʃɪt/| n.|溪流；涨水；洪水 |A freshet is a sudden increase in the water level of a stream, river, or other body of water, typically caused by heavy rain or melting snow.  It is usually a temporary phenomenon, unlike a long-lasting flood. |例句：The recent heavy rains caused a freshet in the river, making it dangerous to cross. 近日的大雨导致河水暴涨，使得过河十分危险。


The spring freshet brought new life to the valley. 春汛为山谷带来了新的生机。|近义词：flood, spate, inundation |反义词：drought, dryness |名词
