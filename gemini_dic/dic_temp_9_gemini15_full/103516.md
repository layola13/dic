joculator:/ˈdʒɒkjʊleɪtə(r)/| n. |滑稽演员；说笑话的人；戏弄者|A joculator was a professional jester or entertainer in the Middle Ages, often employed by royalty or nobility to provide amusement through jokes, songs, and acrobatic feats.  They were known for their wit, quick thinking, and ability to improvise.  |Example: The king's joculator kept the court entertained with his witty remarks and playful antics.  国王的滑稽演员以其诙谐的言辞和滑稽的动作逗乐了整个宫廷。|近义词：jester, buffoon, comedian |反义词：serious person, somber individual
