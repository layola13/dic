cornage:/ˈkɔːrnɑːʒ/| n.|号角声；猎号；（旧时）一种地租|A cornage is a rent paid in the form of blowing a horn.  It was a feudal tenure in which the tenant was obligated to perform the service of blowing a horn to warn of the approach of an enemy.|Example: The lord demanded his cornage from the tenant, a sound that echoed through the valley.  领主向佃户索要号角税，号角声回荡在山谷中。|近义词：horn-blowing, horn-call|反义词：silence, quiet
