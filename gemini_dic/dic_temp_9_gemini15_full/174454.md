quincunx:/ˈkwɪnkʌŋks/| n. |梅花形排列；五点式；棋盘式排列|A quincunx is an arrangement of five objects where four objects are positioned at the corners of a square, and the fifth object is placed in the center.  It's often used to describe the arrangement of trees or plants in a garden, or the pattern on dice.  The term also has historical significance in Roman surveying and land division. |The trees were planted in a quincunx pattern across the field.  树木以梅花形排列种植在田野里。|arrangement, configuration, pattern | disorder, chaos |名词
