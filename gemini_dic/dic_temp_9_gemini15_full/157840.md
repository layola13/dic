plagioclase:/ˈplædʒiˌoʊkleɪs/| n. |斜长石|A group of rock-forming minerals that are feldspars,  characterized by a specific crystal structure and chemical composition, typically containing sodium, calcium, and aluminum silicates.  Plagioclase feldspars are common constituents of igneous and metamorphic rocks.|Example: The granite sample contained a high percentage of plagioclase feldspar.|近义词：feldspar (a broader term)|反义词：N/A (no direct antonym, but other feldspar types like alkali feldspar could be considered contrasting)|名词
