offal: /ˈɒfəl/ /ˈɔːfəl/ | n. |下水，内脏 |The internal organs of an animal, especially those used as food.  This term often refers to the parts of an animal not typically eaten as prime cuts of meat, such as the heart, liver, kidneys, and intestines.  The perception of offal as food varies greatly across cultures.  | Example:  Many cultures utilize offal in their traditional cuisine.  许多文化在其传统菜肴中使用下水。
