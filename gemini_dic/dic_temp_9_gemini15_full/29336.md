casting: /ˈkɑːstɪŋ/ /ˈkæstɪŋ/ | n. v. | 铸造；浇铸；演员阵容；选角 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "casting" refers to the process of shaping molten metal or other material by pouring it into a mold. It can also refer to the actors selected for roles in a play, film, or television program.  As a verb, "casting" means to throw or fling something forcefully; to select actors for parts in a play, film etc; or to shape (metal, etc.) by pouring while molten into a mold.

例句:

The bronze statue was created through a complex casting process.  青铜雕像是通过复杂的铸造过程制作的。

The director is still casting for the lead role in the upcoming film.  导演仍在为即将上映的电影选角。

The casting of the play was exceptional; every actor was perfectly suited to their role.  这部戏的演员阵容非常出色；每位演员都非常适合自己的角色。

He was casting a fishing line into the lake. 他正在向湖里抛鱼线。


近义词:
Noun: molding, founding (for metal casting),  ensemble (for actors), selection (for casting actors)
Verb: throwing, flinging,  selecting,  founding,  molding


反义词:
There isn't a direct antonym for "casting" in all its senses.  However, depending on the context, opposites could include:  for the metalworking sense, "machining" or "forging"; for the acting sense, "rejection" (of actors).
