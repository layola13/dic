reevaluated:/ˌriːɪˈvæljuˌeɪtɪd/| v. |重新评估；重新评价 |To assess or judge (something) again, often with a different perspective or new information.|The committee reevaluated the project's budget after discovering unforeseen costs.  委员会在发现意外成本后重新评估了项目的预算。| reassessed, reappraised | accepted, approved | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reevaluated the situation every week. = 他们每周都会重新评估局势。
We reevaluated our marketing strategy. = 我们重新评估了我们的营销策略。

一般过去时:
The company reevaluated its hiring process last year. = 公司去年重新评估了它的招聘流程。
She reevaluated her life choices after the accident. = 事故之后她重新评估了她的人生选择。


现在完成时:
They have reevaluated their priorities since the pandemic. = 自疫情以来，他们已经重新评估了他们的优先事项。
We have reevaluated the risks involved. = 我们已经重新评估了所涉及的风险。


过去完成时:
They had reevaluated the plan before presenting it to the board. = 他们在向董事会提交计划之前已经重新评估了该计划。
She had reevaluated her options before making a final decision. = 她做出最终决定之前已经重新评估了她的选择。


将来时:
They will reevaluated the data next week. = 他们下周将重新评估数据。
We will reevaluated the contract before signing it. = 我们会在签署合同之前重新评估合同。


虚拟语气:
If they had reevaluated the evidence earlier, they might have reached a different conclusion. = 如果他们早些时候重新评估证据，他们可能会得出不同的结论。
It's important that they reevaluated their assumptions. = 重要的是他们要重新评估他们的假设。


被动语态:

否定句:
