persiflages:/ˈpɜːrsɪfleɪʒz/| n. |戏谑；嘲弄；挖苦 |Light and playful mockery; teasing; often witty and ironic criticism.  It's a form of satire that uses humor to gently ridicule or criticize. |例句：His speech was filled with persiflages of the government's policies. 他的演讲充满了对政府政策的戏谑。

例句：The comedian's act consisted of persiflages of current events.  这位喜剧演员的表演由对时事的戏谑组成。


近义词：mockery, satire, ridicule, teasing, raillery, banter


反义词：praise, commendation, flattery, compliment


词源：From French *persiflage*, from *persiffler* "to jeer, mock," of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old French *sifler* "to whistle."  The word entered English in the late 19th century.
