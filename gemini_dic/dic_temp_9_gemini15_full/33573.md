ciliata:/ˌsɪliˈeɪtə/| n. |纤毛虫；纤毛动物|Ciliates are a large group of single-celled eukaryotic organisms that are characterized by the presence of cilia, which are short, hair-like structures that beat rhythmically to propel the organism through its environment. They are found in a wide variety of aquatic habitats, and some species are also found in moist soil.  Many ciliates are predators, feeding on other microorganisms, while others are filter feeders. |Example: Paramecium is a well-known genus of ciliates.  纤毛虫的一个著名属是草履虫。|近义词：protozoan (原生动物)|反义词：None|Noun
