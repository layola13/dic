gossiping:/ˈɡɑːsɪpɪŋ/ | v. |说闲话；传播流言蜚语 | The act of engaging in gossip; talking about other people's lives, often in a casual and informal way, sometimes spreading rumors or untrue information. |例句：They were gossiping about their neighbors. 他们在说邻居的闲话。

例句：Stop gossiping! It's not nice.  别再八卦了！这不好。


近义词：whispering, chattering, tattling, scandal-mongering | 反义词：being silent, being discreet


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are gossiping about the new teacher. = 他们在说新老师的闲话。
We gossip every afternoon. = 我们每天下午都闲聊。


一般过去时:
She gossiped with her friends yesterday. = 她昨天和朋友们聊八卦。
They gossiped about the party all night. = 他们整晚都在八卦派对的事。


现在完成时:
They have gossiped about this issue for weeks. = 他们已经就此事八卦了好几周了。
She has gossiped too much recently. = 她最近八卦太多了。


过去完成时:
They had gossiped about her before she arrived. = 她来之前他们就已经在说她的闲话了。
I had gossiped with my colleagues before the meeting. = 我在会议前已经和同事们聊过天了。


将来时:
They will gossip about this at the next meeting. = 他们下次会议上会讨论这件事。
She will probably gossip about it all day. = 她可能会整天都在八卦这件事。


虚拟语气:
If they gossiped less, they would have more friends. = 如果他们少说闲话，就会有更多朋友。
I wish they wouldn't gossip so much. = 我希望他们不要说那么多闲话。


被动语态:
The incident was gossiped about widely. = 这件事被广泛传播。
The news was gossiped throughout the town. = 这个消息在镇上到处都在传。


疑问句:
Were they gossiping about me? = 他们是在说我闲话吗？
Are you gossiping again? = 你又在说闲话了？


否定句:
They don't gossip much. = 他们不太说闲话。
She didn't gossip about it to anyone. = 她没有对任何人说这件事。


PHRASE->
gossiping about = 说...的闲话
gossiping with = 和...说闲话
enjoy gossiping = 喜欢八卦
avoid gossiping = 避免说闲话
