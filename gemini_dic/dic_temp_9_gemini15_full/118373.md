merits:/ˈmɛrɪts/ | n. |优点，功绩，价值 | The merits of the plan are obvious.  该计划的优点显而易见。|近义词：advantages, virtues, strengths |反义词：demerits, flaws, shortcomings |


英文解析：Merits refer to the good qualities or advantages of something.  It can also refer to deserving reward or praise for something accomplished.

例句：

The judge considered the merits of the case before making a decision.  法官在做出决定之前考虑了案件的利弊。

His years of hard work finally earned him the merits he deserved. 他多年的辛勤工作最终为他赢得了应得的荣誉。


词源:

From Old French *merite, from Latin meritum "reward, desert, what one deserves," from merēre "to earn, deserve, gain."  The word's evolution reflects a shift from the idea of deserved reward to the qualities that earn that reward.

一般过去时:

现在完成时:
His work has merited numerous awards. = 他的作品获得了无数奖项。
The project has clearly merited the investment. = 该项目显然值得投资。


过去完成时:
The decision had already been made based on the merits of the case. = 决定已经根据案件的优缺点做出了。
They had previously discussed the merits of different strategies. = 他们之前已经讨论过不同策略的优缺点。


将来时:

The new product will merit a strong marketing campaign. = 新产品值得开展强有力的营销活动。


虚拟语气:

The proposal's merits were discussed extensively. = 提议的优点被广泛讨论。
The case was judged on its merits. = 该案是根据其自身优点来判断的。


疑问句:

否定句:
