repercussions:/ˌriːpərˈkʌʃənz/| n.| 后果，反响，影响 | The consequences or effects of an action or event, especially bad ones.  |例句：The repercussions of the scandal were far-reaching. 这场丑闻的影响深远。

例句：The government's decision had unforeseen repercussions. 政府的决定产生了意想不到的后果。


近义词：consequences, effects, ramifications, aftermath, backlashes|反义词：causes, origins


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The repercussions of his actions are clear. = 他行为的后果很明显。
These policies have serious repercussions for the economy. = 这些政策对经济有严重的影响。


一般过去时:
The earthquake had devastating repercussions. = 地震产生了毁灭性的后果。
The company felt the repercussions of the market crash. = 公司感受到了市场崩盘的影响。


现在完成时:

His actions have had serious repercussions for his career. = 他的行为对他事业造成了严重的后果。


过去完成时:

By then, the repercussions of the war were already being felt. = 到那时，战争的后果已经开始显现。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

The company was heavily impacted by the repercussions of the lawsuit. = 公司受到了诉讼后果的严重影响。

否定句:
