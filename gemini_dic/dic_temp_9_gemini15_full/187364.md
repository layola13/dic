roded:/roʊd/| v.|骑乘；(使)颠簸前进|The word "roded" is the past tense of "rode," which means to sit on and control the movement of a horse or other animal, or to travel on something.  It is an irregular verb.|例句：He roded his horse across the field. 他骑马穿过田野。

She roded her bike up the steep hill. 她骑自行车上了陡峭的山坡。


近义词：ridden,mounted|反义词：walked,ran


USAGE->

一般现在时:
I ride my bike to work. = 我骑自行车上班。
They ride horses on the ranch. = 他们在牧场上骑马。

一般过去时:
He rode his bicycle yesterday. = 他昨天骑了他的自行车。
She rode a horse for the first time. = 她第一次骑马。

现在完成时:
I have ridden this horse many times. = 我骑过这匹马很多次了。
They have ridden their bikes across the country. = 他们骑自行车横穿了全国。

过去完成时:
He had ridden the horse before the race. = 他在比赛前骑过那匹马。
She had ridden her bike for hours before she stopped. = 她骑了几个小时的自行车才停下来。

将来时:
I will ride my bike tomorrow. = 我明天将骑自行车。
We will ride horses in the mountains next week. = 我们下周将在山上骑马。

虚拟语气:
If I had a horse, I would ride it every day. = 如果我有一匹马，我会每天都骑它。
I wish I could ride a motorcycle. = 我真希望我能骑摩托车。

被动语态:
The horse was ridden by a young girl. = 那匹马被一个年轻女孩骑着。
The bike was ridden hard and put away wet. = 这辆自行车骑得很猛，而且湿着就收起来了。


疑问句:
Did you ride your bike today? = 你今天骑自行车了吗？
Have they ever ridden a camel? = 他们曾经骑过骆驼吗？

否定句:
I didn't ride my bike today. = 我今天没骑自行车。
He hasn't ridden a horse in years. = 他好几年没骑马了。

PHRASE->
rode hard and put away wet：骑得很猛，而且湿着就收起来了
rode shotgun：坐在副驾驶座上
rode herd on：看管；监督
rode the rails：搭乘火车旅行（尤指免费）
rode into the sunset：骑马走向日落（象征美好结局）

词源:
The word "rode" is the past tense of the verb "ride," which has Old English roots dating back to the Proto-Germanic word *rīdan.  The meaning has remained largely consistent over time, referring to the act of sitting and controlling the movement of an animal or vehicle.
