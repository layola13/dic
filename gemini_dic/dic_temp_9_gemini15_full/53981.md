discretion:/dɪˈskreʃən/| n.|谨慎；酌情决定；自由支配；秘密|The quality of being careful about what you say or do, so that you do not offend or upset anyone or reveal any secrets; the right to decide something. |例句：The information is being held in the strictest discretion.  这些信息正被严格保密。

例句：He was given discretion in how to spend the money. 他被赋予了如何支配这笔钱的自由。

近义词：prudence, caution, judgment, secrecy

反义词：indiscretion, recklessness, imprudence

词源：源自古法语 *discrecion，来自拉丁语 *discernere  “区分，辨别”，由 dis- “分开” + cernere “分开，区别”构成。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He uses discretion in his dealings with others. 他在与他人交往中谨慎行事。
She shows great discretion in her choice of words. 她在选择措辞方面非常谨慎。


一般过去时:
The judge used his discretion to reduce the sentence.  法官运用他的酌情权减轻了刑罚。
She exercised discretion and didn't reveal the secret. 她谨慎地没有泄露秘密。


现在完成时:
The company has shown discretion in handling the crisis.  公司在处理危机时表现出了谨慎。
He has used his discretion wisely in this matter. 他在这件事上明智地行使了他的自由裁量权。


过去完成时:
They had exercised discretion and avoided a public confrontation.  他们事先谨慎行事，避免了公开冲突。
The police had shown discretion in not arresting him. 警方谨慎地没有逮捕他。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
