slobbiest: /ˈslɒbiɪst/ | adj. | 最邋遢的，最不整洁的 | Most untidy or careless.  The slobbiest person I know leaves their clothes all over the floor.  | 近义词：untidiest, messiest, dirtiest, laziest | 反义词：neatest, tidiest, cleanest


例句：

His apartment was the slobbiest I've ever seen. = 他的公寓是我见过的最邋遢的。

She's the slobbiest student in the class; her notes are a mess. = 她是班上最邋遢的学生；她的笔记乱七八糟。


词源:

Derived from "slob," a word of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle Dutch "slobbe," meaning "to slobber" or "to daub," suggesting a connection to messiness and lack of neatness. The "-iest" suffix is a superlative ending, indicating the highest degree.


用法说明:

* **作为形容词:**  `Slobbiest` is the superlative form of the adjective "sloppy," meaning extremely untidy, careless, or lazy. It's used to describe a person, place, or thing that exhibits the highest degree of sloppiness.  It often implies a lack of care or attention to detail.


* **语法功能:** It functions as a predicate adjective (e.g., "He is the slobbiest") or an attributive adjective (e.g., "the slobbiest room").


* **语体:** It is generally considered informal.  Avoid using it in formal writing.
