It refers to the ease with which a piece of text can be paraphrased without losing its essential meaning.  A high degree of paraphrasability indicates that the meaning is clear and can be expressed in multiple ways. | The high paraphrasability of the instructions made them easy to understand for users of different linguistic backgrounds.  The low paraphrasability of the poem made it difficult to translate accurately. | syn: rewordability, expressiveness | ant: unintelligibility, ambiguity | n.
