interpolation:/ɪnˌtɜːrpəˈleɪʃən/ | n. |内插法；插值；(数据的)内插 |The process of estimating values within the range of a discrete set of known data points.  It involves constructing new data points within the range of a discrete set of known data points.  This is often done using mathematical formulas or algorithms. |例句：Linear interpolation is a simple method for estimating values between known data points. 线性插值是一种估计已知数据点之间值的简单方法。

例句：We used spline interpolation to create a smooth curve through the scattered data points. 我们使用样条插值法创建穿过分散数据点的平滑曲线。


近义词：estimation, approximation, extrapolation (for values outside the known range) |反义词：extrapolation |词性：名词


词源:  The word "interpolation" comes from the Latin prefix "inter-" (meaning "between") and the verb "polare" (meaning "to polish" or "to smooth").  The original sense suggested smoothing or filling in the gaps between known points.
