indissipable: /ˌɪnˈdɪsɪpəbl/ | adj. | 不可驱散的；无法消除的 | Incapable of being dispelled or removed; persistent.  | The indissoluble bond of friendship between them was evident to all.  他们之间牢不可破的友谊是显而易见的。 |近义词：unyielding, ineradicable, indelible, permanent | 反义词：dissipatable, removable, transient


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The smell of the perfume is indissipable. = 香水的气味挥之不去。
This feeling of grief is indissipable. = 这种悲伤的感觉挥之不去。


一般过去时:
The stain was indissipable, despite repeated attempts to remove it. = 尽管反复尝试去除污渍，但污渍仍然无法去除。
Her memory of that day remained indissipable. = 她对那一天的记忆挥之不去。


现在完成时:
The problem has proven indissipable, despite many attempts at a solution. = 尽管做了很多尝试来解决问题，但问题已被证明是无法解决的。
The effects of the trauma have been indissipable. = 创伤的影响一直挥之不去。


过去完成时:
The damage had been indissipable, leaving a lasting scar. = 损伤是无法消除的，留下了永久的伤疤。
The rumor had proven indissipable, spreading throughout the town. = 谣言已被证明是无法消除的，在整个城镇蔓延开来。


将来时:
The effects of climate change will be indissipable for generations to come. = 气候变化的影响将在未来几代人中挥之不去。
The impact of this decision will likely be indissipable. = 这个决定的影响很可能无法消除。


虚拟语气:
If the stain were indissipable, we would need a stronger cleaning agent. = 如果污渍无法去除，我们就需要更强的清洁剂。
If the problem were indissipable, further investigation would be necessary. = 如果问题无法解决，就需要进一步调查。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "indissipable" is derived from the prefix "in-" (not), the verb "dissipare" (to scatter, dispel), and the suffix "-able" (capable of).  It ultimately traces back to Latin roots.
