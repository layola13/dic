pre-Indian: /ˌpriːˈɪndiən/ | adj. | 前印第安人的；印第安人之前的 |  Referring to the period of time or the cultures that existed in the Americas before the arrival of European colonists.  This term is often used in archaeological and historical contexts to describe artifacts, settlements, or cultures that predate European contact.  The term is somewhat controversial due to its potential to erase Indigenous histories and perspectives, with some preferring terms like "pre-Columbian" or more specific regional designations. |  Example: Pre-Indian cultures in North America developed sophisticated agricultural techniques. 前北美印第安文化的社会发展出了复杂的农业技术。 | 近义词: pre-Columbian, pre-contact | 反义词: post-colonial, post-contact |  无词性变化
