embitterment: /ɪmˈbɪtərmənt/ | n. |痛苦;怨恨;积怨 | The feeling of deep-seated resentment or displeasure caused by past experiences.  It often involves a sense of injustice and lasting negativity. |例句：Years of hardship and disappointment led to a deep embitterment.  多年来的苦难和失望导致了他深深的怨恨。

The embitterment between the two brothers was palpable. 两兄弟之间的怨恨显而易见。


近义词：resentment, bitterness, rancor, acrimony, animosity | 反义词：contentment, happiness, joy, satisfaction


词源：源自动词embitter，em- (表示“使……”) + bitter (苦的，痛苦的)。
