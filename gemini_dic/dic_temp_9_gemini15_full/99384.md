interdicted:/ˌɪntərˈdɪktɪd/| adj.|禁止的；被禁止的；被阻止的 | Officially forbidden; prohibited; prevented.  The area was interdicted to all but authorized personnel.  | The shipment was interdicted by customs officials. 海关官员禁止了该批货物的运输。 The general interdicted all troop movements. 将军禁止所有部队调动。|近义词：prohibited, forbidden, banned, embargoed |反义词：permitted, allowed, authorized


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The government interdicts the sale of illegal drugs. = 政府禁止销售非法药物。
This area is interdicted to civilians. = 这个区域禁止平民进入。


一般过去时:
The captain interdicted the use of personal electronic devices during takeoff. = 机长在飞机起飞期间禁止使用个人电子设备。
The court interdicted the publication of the sensitive documents. = 法院禁止出版这些敏感文件。


现在完成时:
The authorities have interdicted several shipments of contraband this year. = 今年当局已查禁了几批走私货物。
They have interdicted access to the classified information. = 他们已经禁止访问机密信息。


过去完成时:
The judge had interdicted the defendant from contacting the witness. = 法官此前已禁止被告与证人联系。
Before the storm hit, they had interdicted all boat traffic. = 在风暴来临之前，他们已经禁止所有船只通行。


将来时:
The military will interdict any attempts to cross the border illegally. = 軍方將禁止任何非法越境企圖。
The new law will interdict the use of harmful chemicals in manufacturing. = 新法律将禁止在制造业中使用有害化学品。


虚拟语气:
If the government interdicted the sale of firearms, crime rates might decrease. = 如果政府禁止销售枪支，犯罪率可能会下降。
I wish they had interdicted the construction of that building. = 我希望他们当时就禁止建造那栋建筑。


被动语态:
The import of certain goods was interdicted by the government. = 某些商品的进口被政府禁止了。
Access to the server has been interdicted due to a security breach. = 由于安全漏洞，服务器访问已被禁止。


疑问句:
Were the flights interdicted due to bad weather? = 由于天气恶劣航班被禁飞了吗？
Has the transportation of these materials been interdicted? = 这些材料的运输已被禁止了吗？


否定句:
The authorities did not interdict the protest march. = 当局没有禁止游行示威。
The company has not been interdicted from operating in this country. = 这家公司没有被禁止在这个国家经营。

词源:

From Latin *interdicere, from inter- ("between") + dicere ("to say, speak").  The original meaning implied "to forbid" or "to pronounce a prohibition".
