constrainers:/kənˈstreɪnərz/ | n. |约束者；限制因素 |Those who or that which imposes limitations or restrictions.  Constrainers can be people, rules, systems, or circumstances that limit freedom or possibilities.  |例句：The new regulations are seen by many as unnecessary constrainers on business.|近义词：restrictors, limiters, restrainers |反义词：enablers, liberators


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The regulations constrain business growth. = 这些规章制度限制了企业的发展。
These rules constrain our choices. = 这些规则限制了我们的选择。


一般过去时:
The old laws constrained trade between nations. = 旧的法律限制了国家间的贸易。
Financial difficulties constrained their travel plans. = 经济困难限制了他们的旅行计划。


现在完成时:
The government has constrained spending in recent years. = 近年来，政府已经限制了支出。
Rising oil prices have constrained economic growth. = 油价上涨已经制约了经济增长。


过去完成时:
The company had been constrained by outdated technology. = 这家公司一直受到过时技术的限制。
They had already been constrained by the budget. = 他们已经被预算限制住了。


将来时:
New laws will constrain the power of corporations. = 新法律将限制公司的权力。
The drought will constrain agricultural production. = 干旱将限制农业生产。


虚拟语气:
If there were no constrainers, we could achieve more. = 如果没有限制，我们可以取得更大的成就。
I wish there weren't so many constrainers on our creativity. = 我希望我们的创造力不要受到那么多的限制。


被动语态:
Businesses are constrained by regulations. = 企业受到规章制度的约束。
Our actions are constrained by ethical considerations. = 我们的行动受到道德考虑的约束。


疑问句:
What factors constrain economic development? = 哪些因素制约了经济发展？
What constrainers prevent the project from progressing? = 什么限制因素阻碍了项目的进展？


否定句:
The company is not constrained by its budget. = 这家公司不受预算的限制。
Their creativity is not constrained by convention. = 他们的创造力不受传统观念的限制。


PHRASE->
major constrainers = 主要限制因素
external constrainers = 外部约束因素
