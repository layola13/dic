insinuatory: /ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪtəri/ | adj. | 暗示的；影射的；含沙射影的 |  Suggesting something unpleasant or offensive in an indirect way; hinting at something bad.  |

例句：

His insinuatory remarks about her honesty were deeply offensive.  = 他对她诚实的影射性言论非常令人反感。

The insinuatory tone of the article suggested corruption within the government. = 这篇文章含沙射影的语气暗示政府内部存在腐败。


近义词：suggestive, hinting, implying, allusive, suggestive, innuendo


反义词：direct, frank, open, straightforward


词源:

From "insinuate" + "-ory".  "Insinuate" comes from the Latin "insinuāre," meaning "to wind in," "to insinuate," or "to introduce gradually."  This in turn is from "in-" (into) + "sinuāre" (to bend, to curve). The "-ory" suffix creates an adjective.
