misleading:/ˌmɪsˈliːdɪŋ/ | adj. | 误导的，欺骗性的 |  Giving the wrong idea or impression; deceptive.  | 例句：The advertisement was misleading; the product was not as described.  这则广告具有误导性；产品并非如描述的那样。 | 近义词：deceptive, deceptive, confusing, inaccurate | 反义词：accurate, truthful, clear


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sign is misleading. = 这个标志具有误导性。
His words are misleading. = 他的话具有误导性。


一般过去时:
The map misled us. = 地图误导了我们。
The instructions were misleading. = 说明书具有误导性。


现在完成时:
The campaign has misled many voters. = 这场运动已经误导了许多选民。
His actions have misled the public. = 他的行为已经误导了公众。


过去完成时:
I realized that I had been misled. = 我意识到我被误导了。
They discovered they had been misled by false information. = 他们发现他们被虚假信息误导了。


将来时:
This information will mislead many people. = 这个信息将会误导许多人。
The new policy may mislead the investors. = 新的政策可能会误导投资者。


虚拟语气:
If the instructions hadn't been misleading, we wouldn't have made that mistake. = 如果说明书没有误导我们，我们就不会犯那个错误。
If the advertisement hadn't been misleading, I wouldn't have bought the product. = 如果广告没有误导我，我就不会买这个产品。


被动语态:
Many people were misled by the rumours. = 许多人被谣言误导了。
The public is being misled by the media. = 公众正被媒体误导。


疑问句:
Is this information misleading? = 这个信息具有误导性吗？
Are the instructions misleading? = 说明书具有误导性吗？


否定句:
The report was not misleading. = 报告没有误导性。
The evidence was not misleading. = 证据没有误导性。


PHRASE->
highly misleading = 非常具有误导性
potentially misleading = 潜在地具有误导性
extremely misleading = 极度具有误导性
intentionally misleading = 故意误导
seriously misleading = 严重误导
