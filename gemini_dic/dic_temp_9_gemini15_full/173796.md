quasi-philosophically:/ˈkwɑːzaɪˌfɪləˈsɒfɪkli/ | adv. | 准哲学地；似是而非地哲学地 |  Approaching or treating a subject in a manner resembling philosophical inquiry, but lacking the rigor or depth of true philosophical analysis.  It often implies a superficial or somewhat pretentious engagement with philosophical ideas. | 例句：He discussed the problem of free will quasi-philosophically, offering some interesting insights but ultimately lacking a coherent argument. 他似是而非地哲学地讨论了自由意志的问题，提出了一些有趣的见解，但最终缺乏连贯的论证。


例句：She approached the topic of human nature quasi-philosophically, weaving together personal anecdotes and vague allusions to philosophical concepts. 她以一种准哲学的方式探讨了人性的主题，将个人轶事和对哲学概念含糊的暗示交织在一起。


近义词：speculatively, abstractly, theoretically, metaphysically (in a less rigorous sense)


反义词：practically, empirically, concretely, scientifically


词性：副词
