renavigations:/ˌriːˌnævɪˈɡeɪʃənz/ | n. |重新导航；重新规划航线 |The process of planning or executing a new course of action, often involving a change of direction or strategy.  It often implies a previous navigation attempt that was unsuccessful or needs adjustment. |例句：The company underwent several renavigations of its marketing strategy after the initial campaign failed. 公司在最初的广告活动失败后，对其营销策略进行了多次重新规划。

The ship required renavigations due to unexpected storms.  由于意外的风暴，船只需要重新规划航线。


近义词：re-routings, replannings, reorientations |反义词：stagnation, persistence (in a previously chosen course)
