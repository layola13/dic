contextualist: /kənˈtɛkʃuəlɪst/ | adj., n. |语境主义者；语境论者 |  A contextualist is a person who believes that the meaning of a word or phrase depends heavily on the context in which it is used.  Contextualism, as a philosophical position, emphasizes the importance of context in determining meaning, truth, and justification.  This applies across various fields, including linguistics, philosophy, and literary criticism.  | The contextualist approach to language emphasizes the role of the social and situational factors in shaping meaning. | 近义词：situationist, relativist | 反义词：absolutist, essentialist
