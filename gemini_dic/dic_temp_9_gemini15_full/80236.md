goer:/ˈɡoʊər/| n. | 常去的人；参加者；旅行者 | A person who frequents a particular place or participates in a particular activity.  |He's a regular goer at the pub. 他是这家酒吧的常客。| frequent visitor, participant, attendee |stay-at-home, recluse| 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a frequent goer to the museum. 他是博物馆的常客。
She is a keen goer to concerts. 她是一个热衷于去音乐会的常客。


一般过去时:
He was a regular goer to that cafe. 他以前经常去那家咖啡馆。
She was a dedicated goer to the gym. 她过去是健身房的常客。


现在完成时:
He has been a goer to that club for years. 他已经去那个俱乐部很多年了。
She has been a committed goer to yoga classes. 她一直坚持参加瑜伽课。


过去完成时:
He had been a goer to the library since childhood. 他从小就是图书馆的常客。
She had been a regular goer to the theatre before the pandemic. 大流行之前，她经常去剧院。


将来时:
He will be a goer to the conference next week. 他下周将参加会议。
She will be a first-time goer to the festival. 她将第一次参加这个节日。


虚拟语气:
If he were a regular goer to the gym, he would be fitter. 如果他经常去健身房，他会更健康。
If she were a goer to more social events, she would meet more people. 如果她参加更多社交活动，她会认识更多的人。


被动语态:
(The word "goer" is rarely used in the passive voice.  A passive construction would be awkward and unnatural.)


疑问句:
Is he a goer to the beach this summer? 他今年夏天会去海滩吗？(This uses "goer" in a slightly informal and idiomatic way.)
Are you a regular goer to this restaurant? 你是这家餐馆的常客吗？


否定句:
He is not a goer to the opera. 他不喜欢歌剧。
She is not a frequent goer to parties. 她不常参加派对。


PHRASE->
