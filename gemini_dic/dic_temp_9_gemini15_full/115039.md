mandolin: /ˈmændəlɪn/ | n. |曼陀铃 | A mandolin is a stringed musical instrument, typically with four pairs of strings, played by plucking.  It is known for its bright, ringing tone. |  例句：He played a beautiful melody on his mandolin. 他用曼陀铃演奏了一首优美的旋律。

例句：The band featured a mandolin player who added a unique sound to their music. 乐队里有一位曼陀铃演奏家，为他们的音乐增添了独特的音色。

近义词：lute (but with a different tone and construction)  | 反义词：无

词源:
The word "mandolin" comes from the Italian word *mandolino*, a diminutive of *mando*, meaning "mandola".  The mandola itself is derived from earlier lute-like instruments.  Ultimately, the word's roots trace back to Greek and possibly even further.
