chlorhydrin:/ˈklɔːrhaɪdrɪn/ | n. |氯醇 |A chlorhydrin is an organic compound containing both a hydroxyl group (-OH) and a chlorine atom (-Cl) on adjacent carbon atoms.  They are typically formed by the reaction of an alkene with hypochlorous acid (HOCl).  Chlorhydrins are often intermediates in the synthesis of other organic compounds, particularly epoxides. |Example: Epichlorohydrin, a common chlorhydrin, is used in the production of epoxy resins.  环氧氯丙烷，一种常见的氯醇，用于生产环氧树脂。|近义词:chloroalcohol |反义词:None specifically.
