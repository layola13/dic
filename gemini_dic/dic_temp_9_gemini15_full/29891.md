cauterised:/ˈkɔːtəraɪzd/ | v. | 烧灼；止血；使…失去活力 | To burn the surface of (a wound) with a hot instrument or chemical to stop bleeding and prevent infection.  The term also implies a destructive or numbing effect. | Example: The surgeon cauterised the bleeding wound to stop the blood flow. 外科医生用烧灼法止住了伤口出血。  The experience cauterised his youthful idealism.  这次经历磨灭了他年轻时的理想主义。|近义词：burn, sear, sear, stigmatize | 反义词：heal, soothe

词源:

The word "cauterise" comes from the Late Latin word *cauterizare*, which in turn derives from the Greek word καυτηρίασις (kautēriasis), meaning "burning, cauterization."  The Greek word is based on καῦσις (kausis), meaning "burning," from καίειν (kaiein), meaning "to burn."
