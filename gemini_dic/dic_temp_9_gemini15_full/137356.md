nonteachability: /ˌnɒnˌtiːtʃəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 不可教性，难以教导 | The inherent difficulty or impossibility of teaching something.  This refers to a concept, skill, or subject matter that resists effective teaching methods, often due to its complexity, abstract nature, or the learner's limitations.  | Example: The nonteachability of certain abstract concepts in mathematics is a challenge for educators.  不可教性，某些抽象的数学概念难以教授对教育工作者来说是一个挑战。 | 近义词: untrainability, ineducability | 反义词: teachability,  learnability | 名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "teach" does.  The usage revolves around describing the state of being unteachable.


Example sentences illustrating different grammatical contexts:

Affirmative sentence:

The study highlighted the nonteachability of empathy through rote memorization. 这项研究强调了通过死记硬背来培养同理心的不可教性。


Negative sentence:

The nonteachability of this skill is not a reason to give up.  这项技能的不可教性并不是放弃的理由。


Interrogative sentence:

Is the nonteachability of this subject matter a valid concern?  这个学科的不可教性是一个合理的担忧吗？


PHRASE->

There aren't common phrases directly using "nonteachability".  However, phrases that express similar ideas could include:

*  difficult to teach:  难以教授
*  impossible to learn:  无法学习
*  beyond comprehension:  难以理解
*  incapable of learning:  无法学习
*  resistant to instruction:  抗拒指导


The word "nonteachability" is relatively uncommon and usually appears in academic or specialized contexts discussing educational theory or the limitations of teaching certain subjects.
