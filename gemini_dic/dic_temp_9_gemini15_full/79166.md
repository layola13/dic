glamorizations:/ˌɡlæməraɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. |魅力化；美化；渲染；|The process of making something seem more attractive, exciting, or glamorous than it really is.  It often involves exaggerating positive aspects and ignoring negative ones.|例句：The glamorizations of war in movies often fail to depict the horrors of combat.  电影中对战争的渲染往往未能展现战斗的恐怖。|The glamorizations of celebrity life often lead to unrealistic expectations. 名人生活的渲染往往导致不切实际的期望。|近义词：idealizations, romanticizations, embellishments |反义词：denigrations, criticisms, exposés |名词
