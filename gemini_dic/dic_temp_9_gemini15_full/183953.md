resettlements:/ˌriːˈsɛtəlmənts/| n. |重新安置；迁移安置 |The act of resettling people, typically after displacement or disaster.  It involves providing new homes, jobs, and infrastructure.  This often happens after natural disasters, wars, or government-led relocation projects.|例句：The government initiated large-scale resettlements after the devastating earthquake.  政府在地震后启动了大规模的重新安置工作。

The ongoing refugee crisis has led to many forced resettlements.  持续的难民危机导致了许多被迫的重新安置。|近义词：relocations, transplantations|反义词：evictions, displacements|名词
