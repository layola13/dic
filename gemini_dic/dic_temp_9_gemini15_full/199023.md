signally:/ˈsɪɡnəli/| adv. |显著地；非常地；尤其地|The project signally failed to meet its objectives.  这个项目显著地没有达到其目标。|remarkably, notably, significantly, especially|insignificantly, trivially|副词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He signally improves his work. 他显著地提高了他的工作水平。
The new policy signally affects the economy.  新政策显著地影响了经济。


一般过去时:
The experiment signally failed. 实验彻底失败了。
She signally outperformed her colleagues. 她显著地超过了她的同事。


现在完成时:
The new system has signally improved efficiency. 新系统已显著提高了效率。
His efforts have signally failed to achieve the desired result. 他的努力并没有显著地达到预期效果。


过去完成时:
They had signally warned him of the dangers. 他们曾明确地警告过他危险。
The company had signally underestimated the market demand. 公司曾显著低估了市场需求。


将来时:
The new technology will signally change the industry. 新技术将显著改变该行业。
His actions will signally impact the outcome. 他的行动将显著影响结果。


虚拟语气:
If he had signally improved, he would have been promoted. 如果他显著进步，他就会被提升。
I wish the project had signally succeeded. 我希望这个项目已经取得显著成功。


被动语态:
The problem was signally ignored. 这个问题被显著地忽视了。
His contribution was signally recognized. 他的贡献得到了显著的认可。


疑问句:
Did the plan signally fail? 计划彻底失败了吗？
Does this decision signally impact the future? 这个决定会显著地影响未来吗？


否定句:
The attempt was not signally successful.  这次尝试并不显著成功。
The changes did not signally alter the situation.  这些变化并没有显著改变局面。


PHRASE->
signally important: 极其重要
signally deficient: 严重不足
signally honored: 非常荣幸
signally defeated:  惨败
signally distinguished:  非常杰出


词源:
From Old French *signalement "sign, signal," from signal "sign, signal" (see signal).  The adverbial use developed in Middle English.
