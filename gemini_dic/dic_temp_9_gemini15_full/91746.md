/ˌhaɪpərˈdɪvɪʒən/ | n. | 超分，过度分割 | The process of dividing something into excessively many parts or categories, often leading to fragmentation and a loss of overall coherence.  This term is primarily used in fields like data analysis, computer science, and image processing, where over-partitioning data can hinder analysis or processing efficiency. | Example:  The hyperdivision of the market into niche segments made it difficult for the company to target its advertising effectively.  =  市场被过度细分到各个利基市场，这使得公司难以有效地定位其广告。 | 近义词: overpartitioning, excessive subdivision, fragmentation | 反义词: aggregation, consolidation, synthesis |  n.
