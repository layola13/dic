put a crimp in: /pʊt ə krɪmp ɪn/ | phrasal verb | 使受挫折；阻碍；妨碍 | To hinder or impede the progress or success of something.  |例句：The sudden rain put a crimp in our picnic plans.  = 突如其来的雨水打乱了我们的野餐计划。

The rising cost of materials put a crimp in the company's profits. = 材料价格上涨影响了公司的利润。


近义词：hamper, hinder, obstruct, impede, thwart, mar


反义词：facilitate, aid, assist, promote


PHRASE->
put a crimp in one's style = 打扰某人的计划或风格
put a crimp in the proceedings = 扰乱进程
put a crimp in someone's plans = 打乱某人的计划
put a crimp in the economy = 损害经济
put a crimp in the celebrations = 影响庆祝活动
