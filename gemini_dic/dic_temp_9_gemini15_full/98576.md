institutionalizations: /ˌɪnstɪˌtjuːʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. |体制化；制度化；使制度化 | The process of embedding something within an institution or making it part of a structured system.  This often involves formalizing practices, procedures, or beliefs. It can refer to both positive and negative aspects, depending on the context.  For example, the institutionalization of a new technology can lead to widespread adoption and efficiency gains, while the institutionalization of discrimination can perpetuate inequality. |例句：The institutionalization of the new policy has led to significant improvements in efficiency. 新政策的制度化带来了显著的效率提升。

The institutionalization of racism within the justice system is a serious problem. 司法系统内部种族主义的制度化是一个严重的问题。


近义词：formalization, systematization, standardization, establishment | 反义词：informalization, decentralization, abolition
