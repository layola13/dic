scandalizes:/ˈskændəlaɪzɪz/| vt.|使震惊；使愤慨；使丑闻缠身|To shock or offend someone by doing something considered morally wrong or improper; to cause scandal.|The politician's actions scandalized the public.  这位政治家的行为激怒了公众。|His behavior scandalized his family. 他的行为令他的家人感到震惊。|offends, shocks, appalls|pleases, delights|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The news scandalizes many people. = 这个消息令许多人震惊。
His behavior scandalizes his colleagues. = 他的行为令他的同事们感到震惊。

一般过去时:
The scandal scandalized the entire nation. = 这场丑闻震惊了全国。
The revelation scandalized the community. = 这一爆料令整个社区震惊。

现在完成时:
The recent revelations have scandalized the public. = 最近的揭露使公众感到震惊。
His actions have scandalized his friends and family. = 他的行为令他的朋友和家人感到震惊。

过去完成时:
They had already scandalized the town before the new evidence emerged. = 在新的证据出现之前，他们就已经令全镇震惊了。
The rumors had scandalized the family for years. = 多年来，这些谣言一直令这个家庭蒙羞。

将来时:
His upcoming speech will scandalize many. = 他即将发表的演讲将令许多人震惊。
The truth, when revealed, will scandalize everyone. = 事实一旦揭露，将会令所有人震惊。

虚拟语气:
If he knew the truth, he would be scandalized. = 如果他知道真相，他会感到震惊。
I wish my actions hadn't scandalized you. = 我真希望我的行为没有让你感到震惊。

被动语态:
The public was scandalized by the politician's dishonesty. = 公众被这位政治家的不诚实行为激怒了。
The town was scandalized by the recent events. = 这个小镇被最近发生的事件震惊了。

疑问句:
Does his behavior scandalize you? = 他的行为是否让你感到震惊？
Will the upcoming report scandalize the public? = 即将来临的报告会激怒公众吗？

否定句:
The news did not scandalize her. = 这个消息并没有让她震惊。
His actions did not scandalize everyone. = 他的行为并没有让所有人感到震惊。
