crimea:/kraɪˈmiːə/| n. |克里米亚|A peninsula in southern Ukraine, historically significant and currently a subject of geopolitical dispute.  The region is known for its diverse history, geography, and culture, having been under various empires and influences throughout its history. It has a significant Russian-speaking population. The peninsula's strategic location on the Black Sea has made it a vital area for trade and military control for centuries.|例句：The annexation of Crimea by Russia in 2014 led to international sanctions.  2014年俄罗斯吞并克里米亚导致国际制裁。|近义词：peninsula|反义词：无
