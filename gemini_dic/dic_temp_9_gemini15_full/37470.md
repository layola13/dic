commissioning:/kəˈmɪʃənɪŋ/ | v. | 委托；正式启用；交付使用 | The act of officially putting something into service or operation, often after a period of testing and preparation.  It can also refer to the act of giving someone the authority to do something. | 例句：The new power plant is undergoing commissioning. 新电厂正在进行调试。
The artist received a commission to paint a portrait.  艺术家接到一幅肖像画的委托。| 近义词：authorization, activation, inauguration | 反义词：decommissioning

一般现在时:
They commission new software every year. = 他们每年都会委托开发新的软件。
The company commissions market research regularly. = 这家公司定期委托进行市场调研。

一般过去时:
The government commissioned a study on climate change. = 政府委托进行了一项关于气候变化的研究。
The museum commissioned a new sculpture for its entrance. = 博物馆委托为其入口处创作了一座新的雕塑。

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
