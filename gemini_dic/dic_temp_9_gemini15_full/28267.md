captivate:/ˈkæptɪveɪt/| vt.|使着迷；迷住；吸引|To fascinate or enchant someone, holding their attention completely. |例句：The magician captivated the audience with his amazing tricks. 魔术师以他令人惊叹的魔术技巧迷住了观众。 |例句：Her beauty captivated him instantly. 她的美貌立刻吸引了他。|近义词：enthrall, fascinate, enchant, charm |反义词：bore, repel


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The music captivates the listener. = 音乐使听者着迷。
His charm captivates everyone he meets. = 他的魅力吸引了他遇到的每一个人。


一般过去时:
The story captivated me from beginning to end. = 这个故事从头到尾都让我着迷。
The dancer's performance captivated the audience. = 舞者的表演迷住了观众。


现在完成时:
Her performance has captivated audiences worldwide. = 她的演出已迷住了全世界的观众。
The novel has captivated readers for generations. = 这部小说几代人都被它迷住了。


过去完成时:
The ancient city had captivated explorers for centuries. = 这个古老的城市几个世纪以来一直吸引着探险家们。
By the time the play ended, the audience had been completely captivated. = 到戏剧结束时，观众已经被完全迷住了。


将来时:
I believe this new technology will captivate consumers. = 我相信这项新技术将会吸引消费者。
The upcoming exhibition is expected to captivate art lovers. = 预计即将举行的展览会吸引艺术爱好者。


虚拟语气:
If only I could captivate her attention. = 要是我能吸引她的注意就好了。
I wish the movie had captivated me more. = 我希望这部电影更吸引我一些。


被动语态:
The audience was captivated by the singer's voice. = 观众被歌手的歌声迷住了。
He was captivated by her intelligence and wit. = 他被她的智慧和才智所吸引。


疑问句:
What aspects of the game captivated you the most? = 游戏的哪些方面最吸引你？
Does the novel truly captivate readers as it claims? = 这部小说真的像它声称的那样吸引读者吗？


否定句:
The film failed to captivate the audience. = 这部电影未能吸引观众。
Unfortunately, the presentation did not captivate the board members. = 不幸的是，演示没有吸引住董事会成员。
