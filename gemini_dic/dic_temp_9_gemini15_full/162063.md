pre-British:/ˌpriːˈbrɪtɪʃ/| adj.| 前英国的，英国之前的 |  Describes something that existed or occurred before the period of British influence or rule in a particular place or context.  It often implies a time before significant British colonization, settlement, or political control. | Example: The pre-British history of India is rich and complex.  印度在英国统治之前的历史丰富而复杂。| 近义词: ante-British, precolonial | 反义词: post-British
