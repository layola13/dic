/ˈplʊərəlʌɪzərz/ | n. | 复数形式|  A pluralizer is a linguistic term referring to a process or element that converts a word or grammatical element into its plural form.  This can involve adding suffixes, changing internal vowels, or employing entirely different words.  It focuses on the mechanisms and rules involved in creating plural forms in a language. |  English has various pluralizers; for instance, adding "-s" or "-es" is a common pluralizer.  The word "children" is formed by a different pluralizer than simply adding "-s" to "child". | 近义词: pluralization, plural form creator | 反义词: singularizer (not a commonly used word)
