cutting:/ˈkʌtɪŋ/| n., adj., v.|切割；剪裁；削减；尖刻的；有力的；（植物的）插枝|

英文解析:
As a noun, "cutting" refers to the action or process of cutting something, or the piece that is cut off. As an adjective, it describes something that is sharp, critical, or effective.  As a verb, it means to use a sharp instrument to divide something. In horticulture, it refers to a piece of a plant used for propagation.


例句:
The cutting of the cake was the highlight of the party. = 切蛋糕是派对的高潮。

The chef's cutting skills were impressive. = 厨师的刀工令人印象深刻。


近义词:
(n.) slice, section, trimming, clipping, excerpt, cutting remark
(adj.) sharp, incisive, trenchant, pointed, severe, forceful
(v.) slicing, severing, trimming, snipping, hewing


反义词:
(n.) joining, mending, repair
(adj.) blunt, mild, gentle, kind, soft


词性:
Noun, Adjective, Verb
