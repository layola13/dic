ethereal:/ɪˈθɪəriəl/| adj.|轻飘飘的；超凡脱俗的；天体的；精致的 |extremely delicate and light in a way that seems too perfect for this world;  suggestive of heaven or a spirit world.  |例句：The ethereal beauty of the ballerina captivated the audience.  舞者的空灵之美迷住了观众。

例句：Her ethereal voice filled the concert hall. 她空灵的声音充满了音乐厅。


近义词：celestial, heavenly, airy, delicate, gossamer

反义词：earthly, mundane, material, substantial, coarse


词源:  From Middle English *etherial, borrowed from Late Latin *aethereus, from Greek aithēr "upper air," "clear sky," "the highest region of the heavens,"  related to aithō "I burn," "I blaze." The concept of "ether" as a fifth element (after earth, air, fire, and water) was prevalent in ancient Greek philosophy.
