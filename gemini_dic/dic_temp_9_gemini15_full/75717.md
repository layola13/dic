full of beans:/ˌfʊl əv biːnz/ | adj. |精力充沛的，充满活力的|Full of beans is an informal idiom used to describe someone who is energetic, lively, and enthusiastic.  It implies a playful and sometimes mischievous energy.|例句：The children were full of beans after their trip to the zoo.  孩子们在动物园游玩后精力充沛。|例句：He's usually full of beans in the mornings, but today he seems tired. 他早上通常精力充沛，但今天看起来很累。|近义词：energetic, lively, spirited, enthusiastic, bouncy|反义词：lethargic, sluggish, tired, apathetic


USAGE->
This idiom, being an adjective phrase, doesn't conjugate like a verb.  It describes a state of being.  Therefore, there are no tenses to illustrate.


PHRASE->
There are no common verb phrases built around "full of beans". The phrase itself acts as a complete descriptive unit.
