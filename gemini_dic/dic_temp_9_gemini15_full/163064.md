preclusion:/prɪˈkluːʒən/| n. |阻止；排除；妨碍|The act of preventing something from happening or being done; the state of being prevented or excluded.  It often implies a deliberate or formal act of prevention.|例句：The preclusion of women from voting was a major injustice.  妇女被剥夺投票权是一场严重的社会不公。

例句：His criminal record led to the preclusion of his job application. 他的犯罪记录导致了他的求职申请被拒绝。


近义词：prevention, exclusion, prohibition, barring, impediment

反义词：permission, allowance, admittance


词源：From Latin *praeclusio, from praecludere "to shut out, close up," from prae- "before" + claudere "to shut."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rule precludes any further discussion. = 这条规则排除了任何进一步的讨论。
His illness precludes him from attending the meeting. = 他的疾病使他无法参加会议。


一般过去时:
The court precluded the evidence from being admitted. = 法院阻止了证据的提交。
The heavy rain precluded our picnic. = 大雨阻止了我们的野餐。


现在完成时:
The new law has precluded many unfair practices. = 新法律已经阻止了许多不公平的做法。
His injury has precluded him from playing this season. = 他的伤势使他本赛季无法参赛。


过去完成时:
The previous agreement had precluded any further negotiations. = 先前的协议已经排除了任何进一步的谈判。
His prior commitments had precluded him from joining us. = 他先前的承诺使他无法加入我们。


将来时:
The storm will preclude the flight from taking off. = 暴风雨将阻止飞机起飞。
The lack of funding will preclude the project from going ahead. = 资金短缺将阻止项目继续进行。


虚拟语气:
If the weather were better, it wouldn't preclude our outing. = 如果天气更好，它就不会阻止我们的郊游。
If he hadn't been injured, nothing would have precluded his success. = 如果他没有受伤，什么都不会阻止他的成功。


被动语态:
Further discussion was precluded by the chairman. = 主席阻止了进一步的讨论。
The application was precluded due to insufficient qualifications. = 由于资格不足，申请被拒绝了。


疑问句:
Does the contract preclude us from expanding the business? = 合同是否阻止我们扩大业务？
Will the new regulations preclude the use of pesticides? = 新规章是否会禁止使用农药？


否定句:
The law does not preclude the possibility of appeal. = 法律并不排除上诉的可能性。
Nothing precluded him from achieving his goals. = 没有什么能阻止他实现目标。
