dawned:/dɔːnd/| v.|开始出现；被理解；破晓| To begin to be understood or realised; to become evident; to become light at the beginning of the day. |例句：The truth finally dawned on him. 真相最终被他明白了。
The sun dawned over the horizon. 太阳从地平线上升起。|近义词：appeared, emerged, broke, brightened|反义词：set, darkened


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The truth dawns on me slowly. = 真相慢慢地被我理解了。
Understanding dawns gradually. = 理解逐渐到来。


一般过去时:
It dawned on me that I had made a mistake. = 我意识到我犯了一个错误。
The solution dawned on her after much thought. = 经过深思熟虑后，她找到了解决办法。


现在完成时:
It has dawned on me that I need to change my ways. = 我意识到我需要改变我的生活方式。
The importance of the matter has dawned on them. = 他们已经意识到了这件事的重要性。


过去完成时:
It had dawned on him that he was in danger. = 他已经意识到自己处于危险之中。
By then, it had dawned on her that he was lying. = 到那时，她已经意识到他在撒谎。


将来时:
It will dawn on him eventually. = 他最终会明白的。
Hopefully, the truth will dawn on them soon. = 希望真相很快就会大白于天下。


虚拟语气:
If it dawned on him sooner, he might have acted differently. = 如果他早点意识到，他可能会采取不同的行动。
I wish it had dawned on me earlier. = 我希望我早点意识到。


被动语态:
The meaning of the poem was not easily dawned upon. = 这首诗的意思不容易被理解。
The solution was eventually dawned upon by the team. = 这个解决方案最终被团队找到了。


疑问句:
When did it dawn on you? = 你是什么时候意识到的？
Has it dawned on you yet? = 你已经意识到了吗？


否定句:
It didn't dawn on him immediately. = 他没有立即意识到。
The significance of the event didn't dawn on them until later. = 直到后来他们才意识到这一事件的重要性。
