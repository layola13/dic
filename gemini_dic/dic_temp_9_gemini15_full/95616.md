incomparable:/ɪnˈkɒmpərəbl/ | adj. | 无与伦比的，无可比拟的 |  Beyond comparison; superior to all others.  It suggests that something is so extraordinary that it cannot be compared to anything else. |例句：Her beauty is incomparable.  她的美貌无与伦比。

例句：The view from the mountaintop was incomparable. 从山顶看到的景色是无与伦比的。


近义词：unparalleled, matchless, unsurpassed, peerless, unequalled

反义词：ordinary, commonplace, mediocre, average


词源：The word "incomparable" comes from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") and the word "comparable," which itself is derived from the Latin word "comparare" (to compare). Thus, "incomparable" literally means "not comparable."

用法说明:

incomparable 主要用作形容词，修饰名词。它强调的是某种事物独一无二的卓越性，其优越性无法与其他任何事物相比较。  它通常用于描述具有极高价值、美丽、技能或重要性的东西。


例句：

1.  His talent is incomparable; he's a true genius. 他的天赋无与伦比；他是一位真正的天才。
2. The artist's skill is incomparable; his paintings are breathtaking. 艺术家的技艺是无与伦比的；他的画作令人叹为观止。
3.  The chef's culinary creations are incomparable; a true feast for the senses.  这位厨师的烹饪作品是无与伦比的；一场真正的感官盛宴。
4. The beauty of the sunset was incomparable; a spectacle to behold. 日落的美丽是无与伦比的；一场值得一看的奇观。
5. The quality of this wine is incomparable; it's simply the best. 这款酒的品质是无与伦比的；它就是最好的。
