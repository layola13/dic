dyad:/ˈdaɪæd/| n.|二元组，二元体，对子|A dyad is a pair or couple, often implying a close or interdependent relationship.  It can refer to a group of two, a pair of related things, or a two-element set. In mathematics, it specifically refers to a second-order tensor.|例句：The teacher divided the class into dyads for the collaborative project.老师把班级分成两人小组进行合作项目。  The musical piece featured a compelling dyad of violins. 这首乐曲以令人印象深刻的小提琴二重奏为特色。|近义词：pair, couple, duo, twosome|反义词：group (large), multitude, myriad
