paroxysms:/ˈpærəkˌsɪzəmz/| n. |一阵一阵的剧烈发作；突发；狂暴|A sudden attack or violent expression of a particular emotion or activity.|例句：She suffered paroxysms of coughing. 她一阵一阵地剧烈咳嗽。|例句：He was overcome by paroxysms of rage. 他被一阵阵的愤怒所压倒。|近义词：fit, seizure, attack, spasm|反义词：calmness, tranquility, peace

词源:
From Late Latin *paroxysmus, from Greek παροξυσμός (paroxysmos), from παροξύω (paroxýō) "to sharpen, to exasperate," from παρά (pará) "beside, beyond" + ὀξύς (oxýs) "sharp, acute."  The original sense referred to a crisis or exacerbation of a disease.
