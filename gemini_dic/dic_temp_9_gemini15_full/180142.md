reforests:/ˌriːˈfɒrɪsts/ | vt. |重新造林；使重新长满树木 |To plant trees on land that has been cleared of trees.  This verb implies a large-scale effort to restore a forested area. |例句：The government is planning to reforest the deforested areas. 政府计划对砍伐森林的地区进行重新造林。

例句：The organization is working to reforest the mountainsides that were ravaged by wildfires.  该组织正在努力对野火肆虐的山坡进行重新造林。


近义词：afforest, replant, regenerate


反义词：deforest, deforest


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reforest the hillsides every year. = 他们每年都在山坡上重新造林。
The company reforests damaged areas after logging. = 伐木后，公司会对受损地区进行重新造林。

一般过去时:
They reforested the area after the fire. = 火灾之后，他们对该地区进行了重新造林。
The government reforested millions of hectares of land. = 政府重新造林数百万公顷土地。

现在完成时:
They have reforested a significant portion of the land. = 他们已经对很大一部分土地进行了重新造林。
The project has successfully reforested the barren hills. = 该项目成功地使荒凉的山丘重新长满了树木。

过去完成时:
Before the initiative, they had reforested only a small area. = 在这项倡议之前，他们只对一小片区域进行了重新造林。
By the end of the decade, they had reforested most of the damaged forest. = 到十年结束时，他们已经重新造林了大部分受损的森林。

将来时:
They will reforest the damaged areas next year. = 他们明年将对受损地区进行重新造林。
The plan is to reforest the entire region within the next twenty years. = 计划在未来二十年内使整个区域重新长满树木。

虚拟语气:
If we had more resources, we would reforest the entire mountain range. = 如果我们有更多资源，我们将对整个山脉进行重新造林。
It is essential that they reforest the area to prevent erosion. = 他们必须对该地区进行重新造林以防止水土流失。
