obliquity:/əˈblɪkwəti/| n. |倾斜；偏斜；不正；斜度 |The state of being oblique; deviation from a right, straight, or normal course.  It refers to a slanting or deviation from a vertical or horizontal line, or a deviation from a standard or norm.  This can be literal, referring to a physical angle, or figurative, referring to a deviation from accepted norms or truth. |例句：The obliquity of the sun's rays is responsible for the seasons. 太阳光线的倾斜造成了四季。

The obliquity of his moral compass was evident in his actions. 他道德指南针的偏差在他的行为中显而易见。|近义词：slant, inclination, deviation, obliqueness|反义词：straightness, perpendicularity, directness
