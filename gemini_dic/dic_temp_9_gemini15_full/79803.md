glycine:/ˈɡlaɪsiːn/ | n. |甘氨酸 |Glycine is the simplest of the 20 amino acids that are the building blocks of proteins.  It is a non-essential amino acid, meaning the body can synthesize it. It plays a crucial role in various metabolic processes and has a sweet taste. |Example: Glycine is an important component of collagen, a major structural protein in connective tissues. 甘氨酸是胶原蛋白的重要组成部分，胶原蛋白是结缔组织中的主要结构蛋白。|近义词：amino acid (broad term) |反义词：None (no direct antonym) |名词
