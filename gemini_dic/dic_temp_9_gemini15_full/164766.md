preferentialisms: /ˌprɛfəˈrɛnʃəlɪzəm/ | n. | 偏好主义；优惠待遇制度 | The system of giving preferential treatment to certain groups or individuals.  It often refers to policies that favor specific groups, often at the expense of others, based on factors like ethnicity, nationality, or political affiliation.  This can lead to unfair competition and inequality. | Example: The preferentialisms shown towards domestic industries hindered the growth of foreign businesses.  偏向国内产业的优惠待遇制度阻碍了外国企业的增长。|近义词：favoritism, bias, discrimination | 反义词：impartiality, fairness, equality | 名词
