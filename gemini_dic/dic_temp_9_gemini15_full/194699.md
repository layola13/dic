self-whippers: /ˌsɛlfˈwɪpərz/ | n. | 自虐者 | People who inflict pain or punishment upon themselves, often for religious or masochistic reasons.  The term is often used in a derogatory or judgmental way. | 例句：The cult leader encouraged his followers to become self-whippers as a form of spiritual purification.  = 教主鼓励他的追随者进行自虐作为一种精神净化方式。

The term "self-whippers" is a compound word combining "self" and "whippers".  "Self" refers to oneself, and "whippers" refers to those who whip, in this context, whipping oneself.  There isn't a direct etymological history for the exact term "self-whippers" as it's a relatively modern colloquialism, but its components are derived from Old English: "self" from *self, and "whip" likely from Proto-Germanic *hwipō.  The "-er" suffix is a common agentive suffix indicating a person who performs the action.

反义词:  There isn't a direct antonym, but terms like self-care advocates or those who practice self-compassion could be considered indirect opposites.
