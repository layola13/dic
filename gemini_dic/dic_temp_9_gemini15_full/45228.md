culminations:/ˌkʌlmɪˈneɪʃənz/ | n. | 顶点；高潮；结果 | The highest or climactic point of something, especially as attained after a long time.  |例句：The culmination of years of research resulted in a groundbreaking discovery.  多年研究的顶点最终导致了一项突破性发现。

例句：The series of events culminated in a dramatic showdown.  一系列事件最终以一场戏剧性的对决告终。


近义词：apex, climax, peak, zenith | 反义词：beginning, start, commencement | 词性：名词


词源:  From Latin *culmen (“summit, top”), the past participle of *culminare (“to reach the summit”).


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The culmination of their efforts is a successful project. = 他们努力的顶点是一个成功的项目。
His hard work culminates in a promotion. = 他的努力最终以晋升告终。


一般过去时:
The festival culminated in a spectacular fireworks display. = 节日以壮观的烟火表演达到高潮。
The negotiations culminated in a signed agreement. = 谈判最终达成了一份签署的协议。


现在完成时:
The project has culminated in a remarkable achievement. = 该项目最终取得了非凡的成就。
Years of training have culminated in her Olympic victory. = 多年的训练最终让她获得了奥运冠军。


过去完成时:
By the time they arrived, the storm had culminated in a devastating flood. = 当他们到达时，暴风雨已经演变成一场毁灭性的洪水。
The argument had culminated in a bitter fight. = 争论最终演变成一场激烈的争吵。


将来时:
The concert will culminate in a performance by a famous singer. = 音乐会将以一位著名歌手的表演达到高潮。
Their efforts will culminate in the launch of a new product. = 他们的努力将最终导致一款新产品的推出。


虚拟语气:
If only his efforts had culminated in success. = 如果他的努力最终获得成功就好了。
I wish the project had culminated in a more satisfying result. = 我希望这个项目能有一个更令人满意的结果。


被动语态:
The event was culminated by a grand banquet. =  活动以盛大的宴会作为结束。 (Note:  "culminate" is less commonly used in the passive voice)
The celebration was culminated with a breathtaking display. = 庆祝活动以令人叹为观止的表演结束。(Note:  "culminate" is less commonly used in the passive voice)


疑问句:
What will culminate the conference? =  什么将作为会议的结束？
How did the conflict culminate? = 冲突是如何结束的？


否定句:
The negotiations did not culminate in an agreement. = 谈判并没有达成协议。
His efforts did not culminate in the desired outcome. = 他的努力并没有达到预期的结果。


PHRASE->

culmination of: …的顶点/高潮
