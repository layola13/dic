carta: /ˈkɑːrtə/| n. |宪章；证书；纸张 | A formal document, especially one granted by a ruler or government, conferring rights, privileges, or status.  |例句：The Magna Carta is a cornerstone of English law.  《大宪章》是英国法律的基石。

例句：The university granted him a carta of commendation for his research.  大学授予他一份表彰其研究的证书。


近义词：charter, deed, document, writ | 反义词：None specifically.  | 词性：名词


词源：From Medieval Latin *carta ("paper, charter"), from Latin charta ("papyrus, paper").  The word ultimately derives from Greek khartēs ("papyrus sheet").
