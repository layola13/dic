chronicities:/krɒnɪˈsɪtiz/ | n. |慢性病；长期病症 |The plural form of "chronicity," referring to the state of being chronic or long-lasting, especially in relation to diseases or conditions.  It often implies a persistent and ongoing nature,  requiring long-term management. |例句：The study focused on the prevalence of chronicities among the elderly population. 这项研究的重点是老年人群中慢性病的患病率。

The increasing prevalence of chronicities is placing a strain on healthcare systems.  慢性病的患病率不断上升，给医疗系统带来了压力。 |近义词：long-term illnesses, persistent conditions, enduring ailments |反义词：acute conditions, short-term illnesses


词源:  Derived from the word "chronic," which comes from the Late Latin word "chronicus," meaning "relating to time."  The suffix "-ities" denotes a state or quality.
