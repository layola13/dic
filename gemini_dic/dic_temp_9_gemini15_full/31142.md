chancing:/ˈtʃɑːnsɪŋ/ | v. | 碰巧；冒险；尝试；偶然发生 | Taking a risk or trying something; happening by chance. | 例句：He's chancing his arm by applying for that job. 他申请那份工作是在冒险。


例句：I chanced upon an old friend at the market. 我在市场上偶然碰到了一个老朋友。


近义词：risking, venturing, attempting, happening

反义词：avoiding, shunning, guaranteeing


词性:动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He chances his luck every day. = 他每天都在碰运气。
She chances upon new discoveries frequently. = 她经常偶然发现新的东西。

一般过去时:
They chanced a meeting with the CEO. = 他们冒险与CEO会面。
I chanced to see him at the party. = 我碰巧在派对上看到他。

现在完成时:
He has chanced upon a rare antique. = 他偶然发现了一件稀有的古董。
She has chanced her career several times. = 她已经几次改变了职业。

过去完成时:
They had chanced their fortune on a risky investment. = 他们把财富押在了一项风险投资上。
I had chanced to meet her before. = 我以前碰巧见过她。

将来时:
We will chance a visit to the museum tomorrow. = 我们明天将冒险去博物馆参观。
He will chance upon a solution eventually. = 他最终会偶然找到一个解决方案。

虚拟语气:
If I chanced to win the lottery, I would travel the world. = 如果我碰巧中了彩票，我会周游世界。
I wish I had chanced upon that opportunity. = 我希望我曾经抓住那个机会。

被动语态:
The meeting was chanced upon by the reporter. = 记者偶然碰到了这次会议。
The outcome was chanced by fate. = 结果是命运决定的。

疑问句:
Did you chance to see the movie? = 你碰巧看过这部电影吗？
Will you chance it and try again? = 你会冒险再试一次吗？

否定句:
He didn't chance a look at the difficult problem. = 他不敢看那个难题。
They didn't chance meeting the president. = 他们没有碰巧遇到总统。
