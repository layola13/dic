liberalized:/ˈlɪbərəlaɪzd/ | v. | 使自由化；放松管制 | To make something more liberal or less restrictive.  This often refers to economic policies, social norms, or legal frameworks. | Example: The government liberalized its trade policies, leading to increased foreign investment. 政府放宽了贸易政策，导致外国投资增加。|近义词：deregulated, loosened, eased | 反义词：restricted, tightened, constrained | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government liberalizes its immigration policies. = 政府正在放宽其移民政策。
They liberalize their approach to education. = 他们正在放宽其教育方法。


一般过去时:
The country liberalized its economy in the 1990s. = 这个国家在20世纪90年代放宽了经济管制。
The company liberalized its dress code. = 公司放宽了着装规定。


现在完成时:
The reforms have liberalized the media. = 改革使媒体更加自由。
They have liberalized their visa requirements. = 他们已经放宽了签证要求。


过去完成时:
The market had already liberalized before the new regulations were introduced. = 新规实施之前，市场已经放宽管制了。
They had liberalized their hiring practices before the lawsuit. = 诉讼发生前，他们已经放宽了招聘制度。


将来时:
The government will liberalize its environmental regulations. = 政府将放宽其环境法规。
The company will liberalize its parental leave policy. = 公司将放宽其陪产假政策。


虚拟语气:
If the government liberalized the trade barriers, the economy would grow faster. = 如果政府放宽贸易壁垒，经济将会增长更快。
I wish they had liberalized the rules earlier. = 我希望他们早点放宽规则。


被动语态:
The market was liberalized by the government. = 市场由政府放宽管制。
The regulations were liberalized in response to public pressure. = 为了回应公众压力，法规得到了放宽。


疑问句:
Has the government liberalized its stance on same-sex marriage? = 政府是否已经放宽了对同性婚姻的立场？
Will the new law liberalize the banking sector? = 新法律会使银行业更加自由化吗？


否定句:
The government did not liberalize its drug laws. = 政府没有放宽其毒品法律。
The company has not yet liberalized its hiring practices. = 公司尚未放宽其招聘制度。
