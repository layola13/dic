renotarized:/ˌriːˈnoʊtəraɪzd/ | v. |重新公证;重新认证 |  To notarize again; to have a document notarized a second time.  This usually happens when there's an error in the original notarization or when additional signatures are needed. |The contract needed to be renotarized after a clause was amended.|近义词：recertified, revalidated | 反义词：denoted


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They renotarize documents regularly. = 他们定期重新公证文件。
He renotarizes contracts for clients. = 他为客户重新公证合同。

The lawyer renotarized the will after the error was discovered. = 律师在发现错误后重新公证了遗嘱。
She renotarized the deed to correct the address. = 她重新公证了契约以更正地址。
