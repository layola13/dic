prepositor:/ˌprepəˈzɪtər/| n. |前置词词组；前置词使用者| A prepositor is a word or phrase that is used before a noun, pronoun, or noun phrase to show its relationship to another word in the sentence.  It's essentially a more formal and less common term for a prepositional phrase. | Example: "The book is on the table."  "On the table" acts as a prepositor, showing the location of the book. |近义词:prepositional phrase|反义词:None|名词
