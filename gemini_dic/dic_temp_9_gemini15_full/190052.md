sandpiper:/ˈsændˌpaɪpər/ | n. |矶鹬，滨鹬 | A small wading bird with a long, slender bill, typically found near water's edge.  Sandpipers are known for their distinctive foraging behavior, often probing the mud or sand for food. |例句：We saw a flock of sandpipers foraging along the shoreline. 我们看到一群矶鹬在海岸线上觅食。


例句：The least sandpiper is a tiny bird, easily overlooked.  小滨鹬是一种非常小的鸟，很容易被忽视。


近义词：shorebird, wader
