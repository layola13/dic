indicter:/ɪnˈdaɪtər/ | n. | 起诉人，告发者 | A person who indicts someone.  Someone who formally accuses someone of a crime. | The indicter presented compelling evidence to the grand jury.  起诉人向大陪审团提交了令人信服的证据。| accuser, prosecutor | defendant, acquitted party | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The indicter presents evidence to the court. = 起诉人向法庭提交证据。
He indicts the defendant for fraud. = 他以诈骗罪起诉被告。


一般过去时:
The indicter presented strong evidence against the accused. = 起诉人提供了强有力的证据反对被告。
She indicted him for theft last year. = 她去年以盗窃罪起诉了他。


现在完成时:
The grand jury has indicted the suspect on multiple charges. = 大陪审团已对嫌疑人提出多项指控。
They have indicted several individuals in the corruption scandal. = 他们在腐败丑闻中已经起诉了几个人。


过去完成时:
The judge had already indicted the defendant before the new evidence emerged. = 在新的证据出现之前，法官已经起诉了被告。
The prosecution had indicted the CEO prior to the public announcement. = 在公开宣布之前，检方已经起诉了首席执行官。


将来时:
The prosecutor will indict the suspect tomorrow. = 检察官明天将起诉嫌疑人。
They will indict him on charges of conspiracy. = 他们将以共谋罪名起诉他。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, the indicter would indict him without hesitation. = 如果证据更充分，起诉人会毫不犹豫地起诉他。
I wish the indicter had indicted the true perpetrator. = 我希望起诉人已经起诉了真正的罪犯。


被动语态:
The suspect was indicted on charges of murder. = 嫌疑人被指控犯有谋杀罪。
He was indicted by a grand jury. = 他被大陪审团起诉。


疑问句:
Who indicted the defendant? = 谁起诉了被告？
Has the grand jury indicted anyone yet? = 大陪审团是否已经起诉任何人？


否定句:
The prosecutor did not indict the suspect due to insufficient evidence. = 由于证据不足，检察官没有起诉嫌疑人。
The grand jury refused to indict him. = 大陪审团拒绝起诉他。
