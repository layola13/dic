projecting:/prəˈdʒektɪŋ/ | v. | 计划；预测；突出；投射；表现 |  To plan or design something for the future; to estimate or calculate something will happen in the future; to make something stick out; to cast or throw something onto a surface; to express or convey a particular feeling or image. |

例句：

The architect is projecting a new skyscraper. = 建筑师正在设计一座新的摩天大楼。

They are projecting a 5% increase in sales next year. = 他们预计明年销售额将增长 5%。


例句：

The building projects a long shadow in the afternoon. = 这座建筑物在下午投射出很长的阴影。

The film projects images onto the screen. = 电影将图像投射到屏幕上。


例句：

He projected an air of confidence. = 他表现出自信的样子。

Her paintings project a sense of tranquility. = 她的画作展现出一种宁静感。


近义词：planning, forecasting, extending, throwing, expressing, displaying


反义词：retreating, withdrawing, concealing, obscuring


词性：动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He projects his voice to the back of the room. = 他把声音投射到房间的后面。
She projects a positive image to her clients. = 她向她的客户展现积极的形象。

一般过去时:
The company projected a loss for the quarter. = 公司预测本季度亏损。
The speaker projected his slides onto the screen. = 演讲者将幻灯片投射到屏幕上。

现在完成时:
They have projected the completion date for the project. = 他们已经预测了项目的完成日期。
The artist has projected his emotions onto the canvas. = 艺术家已经将他/她的情感投射到画布上。

过去完成时:
They had projected a successful outcome, but things went wrong. = 他们曾预料到一个成功的结果，但事情出了差错。
She had projected her anxieties onto her friend. = 她曾将她的焦虑投射到她的朋友身上。


将来时:
We will project the financial statements next week. = 我们下周将预测财务报表。
He will project the film onto the large screen. = 他将把电影投射到大的屏幕上。

虚拟语气:
If they projected accurately, the bridge would be completed on time. = 如果他们的预测准确，桥梁就能按时完工。
I wish I had projected the consequences of my actions. = 我希望我曾预料到我行为的后果。


被动语态:
The image was projected onto the wall. = 图像被投射到墙上。
The film is being projected in the cinema. = 电影正在电影院上映。


疑问句:
What are you projecting onto the screen? = 你正在屏幕上投射什么？
How far does the building project a shadow? = 这座建筑物的阴影能投射多远？


否定句:
The data did not project a positive trend. = 数据没有显示积极的趋势。
He didn't project his voice well enough to be heard. = 他的声音投射得不够好，听不见。
