roaringly:/ˈrɔːrɪŋli/ | adv. | 轰鸣地；咆哮地；喧闹地 | In a roaring manner; loudly and forcefully.  |例句：The engine roared as the car sped away.  引擎轰鸣着，汽车飞驰而去。

The crowd roared its approval. 人群大声喝彩。


近义词：loudly, noisily, thunderously, boisterously | 反义词：quietly, softly, silently


词源:  Derived from the word "roar,"  meaning a loud, deep sound,  plus the adverbial suffix "-ly."  The word "roar" itself has Old English roots, possibly connected to words meaning "to cry" or "to bellow."

用法说明:

roaringly is an adverb that describes the manner in which something happens. It typically describes a loud, forceful, and often uncontrolled sound or action.  It implies a great deal of energy and volume.  It can be used to describe the sound of machines, animals, or even crowds.

3. The wind roared roaringly through the trees. 风呼啸着穿过树林。

4. The fire roared roaringly in the fireplace. 火在壁炉里熊熊燃烧。

5. The train roared roaringly past the station. 火车轰鸣着驶过车站。
