interrelation:/ˌɪntərɪˈleɪʃən/| n. |相互关系；相互关联|The complex interrelation of factors influencing climate change makes it difficult to predict future trends.  相互影响气候变化的多种因素错综复杂，难以预测未来的趋势。|近义词：correlation, connection, interdependence|反义词：independence, separation|名词


USAGE->
This word is a noun, and thus doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It doesn't have tenses or voice changes.  Its usage focuses on its function within a sentence.

Example sentences demonstrating different grammatical functions:

Subject of a sentence:
The interrelation of economics and politics was the focus of the lecture. 经济与政治的相互关系是讲座的重点。

Object of a preposition:
The study explored the interrelation between diet and health.  这项研究探讨了饮食与健康之间的相互关系。

Object of a verb:
Researchers investigated the interrelation of various social factors. 研究人员调查了各种社会因素之间的相互关系。

Part of a noun phrase:
A detailed analysis of the interrelation of cause and effect is needed. 需要对因果关系的相互关系进行详细分析。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "interrelation".  However, phrases illustrating the concept include:

reciprocal relationship: 相互关系
interconnectedness: 相互关联
mutual dependence: 相互依赖
complex interaction: 复杂互动
dynamic interplay: 动态互动
