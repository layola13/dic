exceeding:/ɪkˈsiːdɪŋ/| adj.|超过的；极度的| exceeding something means being greater than it in amount, degree, or intensity.  It emphasizes a significant surplus or intensity.| The cost of the project was exceeding expectations.  这个项目的成本超出了预期。|  extreme, excessive, surpassing | insufficient, inadequate


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The temperature is exceeding 30 degrees Celsius. = 温度超过30摄氏度。
His performance exceeds expectations. = 他的表现超出了预期。


一般过去时:
The speed of the car exceeded the limit. = 车速超过了限制。
The demand exceeded the supply. = 需求超过了供应。


现在完成时:
The project has exceeded its budget. = 这个项目已经超支了。
The success has exceeded our wildest dreams. = 成功超出了我们最狂野的梦想。


过去完成时:
The damage had exceeded what we initially thought. = 造成的损害超出了我们最初的估计。
The deadline had already been exceeded. = 截止日期已经被超过了。


将来时:
The population will exceed one billion. = 人口将超过十亿。
The costs are likely to exceed the budget. = 成本可能会超过预算。


虚拟语气:
If the temperature exceeded 40 degrees, we would have canceled the event. = 如果温度超过40度，我们就取消活动了。
If only his ambition hadn't exceeded his ability. = 要是他野心不大于能力就好了。


被动语态:
The speed limit was exceeded by many drivers. = 许多司机都超速了。
The budget was exceeded by a considerable margin. = 预算被超支了一大笔。


疑问句:
Does the amount exceeding the limit require special approval? = 超过限额的部分是否需要特别批准？
Is the temperature exceeding the safe operating range? = 温度是否超过了安全操作范围？


否定句:
The profits did not exceed expectations. = 利润没有超出预期。
The rainfall did not exceed the average for this time of year. = 降雨量没有超过这个季节的平均水平。


PHRASE->

exceeding speed: 超速
exceeding authority: 滥用职权
