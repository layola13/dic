doldrums:/ˈdɒldrəmz/| n.|萧条时期；低迷状态；忧郁；|a period or state of inactivity, stagnation, or depression.|The economy is in the doldrums. 经济处于低迷状态。|近义词：depression, slump, stagnation|反义词：boom, prosperity


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The economy remains in the doldrums. = 经济仍然处于低迷状态。
She is always in the doldrums. = 她总是情绪低落。


一般过去时:
The market was in the doldrums last year. = 去年市场处于低迷状态。
He was in the doldrums after losing his job. = 他失业后情绪低落。


现在完成时:
The company has been in the doldrums for months. = 公司已经低迷了好几个月了。
She has been in the doldrums since her breakup. = 自从分手以来，她一直情绪低落。


过去完成时:
The project had been in the doldrums before they injected new funding. = 在他们注资之前，该项目一直处于低迷状态。
He had been in the doldrums for weeks before finally seeking help. = 他情绪低落了好几个星期才最终寻求帮助。


将来时:
The industry will likely remain in the doldrums for the next quarter. = 该行业在下个季度可能会继续低迷。
I fear she will be in the doldrums for a long time. = 我担心她会情绪低落很长时间。


虚拟语气:
If the economy weren't in the doldrums, we would have more jobs. = 如果经济不低迷，我们将会有更多工作。
If she weren't always in the doldrums, she would be more fun to be around. = 如果她不总是情绪低落，和她相处会更有趣。


被动语态:
The situation was described as being in the doldrums. = 这种情况被描述为低迷状态。
His spirits were left in the doldrums after the defeat. = 失败后，他的情绪低落。


疑问句:
Is the music industry still in the doldrums? = 音乐产业仍然低迷吗？
Is he always in the doldrums? = 他总是情绪低落吗？


否定句:
The market is not in the doldrums anymore. = 市场不再低迷了。
She is not always in the doldrums; she has her good days. = 她并不总是情绪低落；她也有好日子。


PHRASE->
fall into the doldrums = 陷入低迷
out of the doldrums = 摆脱低迷
in the doldrums of = 在...的低迷时期
the shipping doldrums = 海运业的低迷时期
economic doldrums = 经济低迷
