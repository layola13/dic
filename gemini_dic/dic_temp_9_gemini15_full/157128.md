pinches:/ˈpɪntʃɪz/| n./v.|捏，少量，掐|To take hold of something between the thumb and finger and press it.  Also, a small amount. |例句：He pinched the child's cheek. 他捏了孩子的脸颊。


例句：She pinched a small amount of salt from the container. 她从容器里捏了一小撮盐。


近义词：squeeze, grip, tweak, a bit, a little


反义词：abundant, plenty, large amount


词性：Noun and Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pinches her cheek playfully. = 他开玩笑地捏了捏她的脸颊。
She pinches pennies to save money. = 她省吃俭用攒钱。


一般过去时:
The cook pinched some herbs to add flavor. = 厨师捏了一些香草来调味。
He pinched the thief's wallet. = 他偷了小偷的钱包。


现在完成时:

I have pinched myself to make sure I'm not dreaming. = 我掐了自己一下，以确保自己不是在做梦。


过去完成时:
