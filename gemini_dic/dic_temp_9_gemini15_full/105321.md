kibosh:/ˈkaɪbɒʃ/ | n. |彻底破坏；终止；阻止 | To put a kibosh on something means to put a stop to it, to end it decisively.  It's often used informally. | The sudden rain put a kibosh on our picnic.  突如其来的雨让我们野餐泡汤了。 |近义词：end, stop, finish, halt |反义词：start, begin, commence


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bad weather puts a kibosh on our plans. = 糟糕的天气打乱了我们的计划。
He kiboshes every new idea. = 他否决每一个新想法。


一般过去时:
The manager kiboshed the project. = 经理终止了这个项目。
The unexpected snowstorm kiboshed our trip. = 突如其来的暴风雪使我们的旅行泡汤。


现在完成时:
They have kiboshed the deal. = 他们已经终止了这笔交易。
She has kiboshed all my attempts to help. = 她已经挫败了我所有想要帮忙的尝试。


过去完成时:
