hoodwink:/ˈhʊdˌwɪŋk/| vt. |欺骗，哄骗 |To deceive or trick someone. |例句：The salesman hoodwinked the elderly woman into buying a useless product.  售货员欺骗了这位老太太，让她买了一件无用的产品。


例句：He tried to hoodwink me into giving him my money. 他试图欺骗我，让我把钱给他。


近义词：deceive, trick, cheat, swindle, dupe

反义词：enlighten, inform, truthfully


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hoodwinks his customers regularly. 他经常欺骗他的顾客。
They hoodwink investors with false promises.  他们用虚假承诺欺骗投资者。


一般过去时:
The magician hoodwinked the audience with his illusions. 魔术师用他的幻象欺骗了观众。
She hoodwinked her parents about her whereabouts. 她欺骗了她的父母关于她的行踪。


现在完成时:
He has hoodwinked many people throughout his career.  他职业生涯中欺骗过很多人。
They have hoodwinked the public for years. 他们多年来一直在欺骗公众。


过去完成时:
The con artist had hoodwinked several victims before being caught. 骗子在被抓之前已经欺骗了好几个受害者。
I realized that I had been hoodwinked after it was too late. 我意识到自己被骗了，为时已晚。


将来时:
He will hoodwink them again if given the chance.  如果给他机会，他会再次欺骗他们。
They will hoodwink the voters with their empty slogans. 他们将用空洞的口号欺骗选民。


虚拟语气:
If he had not hoodwinked me, I would have invested. 如果他没有欺骗我，我就投资了。
I wish I had not been hoodwinked by him.  我希望我没有被他欺骗。


被动语态:
Many people were hoodwinked by his charming demeanor. 很多人被他迷人的举止所欺骗。
The investors were hoodwinked out of their savings. 投资者被骗走了他们的积蓄。


疑问句:
Was he hoodwinked by the advertisement? 他被广告欺骗了吗？
Were the customers hoodwinked into buying faulty goods? 顾客是被诱骗购买有缺陷的商品吗？


否定句:
He did not hoodwink anyone; he told the truth. 他没有欺骗任何人；他说了实话。
The company did not hoodwink the public; the product was genuinely innovative. 公司没有欺骗公众；该产品确实是创新的。
