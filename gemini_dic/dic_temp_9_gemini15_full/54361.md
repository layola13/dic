dishonoured: /dɪsˈɒnəd/ | /dɪsˈɑːnərd/ | adj. v. | 丢脸的；被拒绝的；未被兑现的 |  Disgraced; deprived of honor; refused payment on (a check or bill of exchange).  |


英文解析:
The word "dishonoured" can function as both an adjective and a verb. As an adjective, it describes someone or something that has lost honor or respect. As a verb, it typically refers to the act of refusing to accept a check or bill of exchange due to insufficient funds or other reasons.  The root "honor" implies a positive connotation, while the prefix "dis-" negates it, creating a sense of shame or disgrace.

例句:

As an adjective:
The dishonoured general was stripped of his rank. =  那个丢脸的将军被剥夺了军衔。

He felt dishonoured by his actions. = 他为自己的行为感到羞耻。


As a verb:
The bank dishonoured the check because of insufficient funds. = 由于资金不足，银行拒付了支票。

His request for a loan was dishonoured. = 他的贷款申请被拒绝了。


近义词:
(adjective) disgraced, shamed, humiliated, disrespected; (verb) rejected, refused, declined


反义词:
