categoricalness:/ˌkætɪˈɡɔːrɪkəlnəs/| n. |绝对性；明确性；肯定性 |The categoricalness of his denial surprised everyone.  他的断然否认让每个人都感到惊讶。|近义词：definiteness, certainty, positiveness |反义词：ambiguity, uncertainty, vagueness

英文解析:The quality of being categorical; the state of being absolute, unconditional, or without exception.  It implies a firm and unwavering declaration or belief, leaving no room for doubt or ambiguity.


例句:

Her categoricalness in her opinions often made her unpopular.  她观点的绝对性常常使她不受欢迎。


The categoricalness of the judge's verdict left no room for appeal.  法官判决的绝对性使得上诉毫无余地。


词源:

Derived from the adjective "categorical," which comes from the Middle French "categorique" and ultimately from the Late Latin "categoriacus," related to the Greek "katēgorikós," meaning "declarative," "affirmative," or "predicative."  The suffix "-ness" is a common English suffix forming nouns of quality or state.
