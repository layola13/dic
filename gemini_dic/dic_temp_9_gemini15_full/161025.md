postcava: /ˈpoʊstˌkeɪvə/ | n. |后腔静脉|The postcava is a large vein that returns blood from the lower body to the heart.  It is formed by the union of the common iliac veins.  In humans, it is also known as the inferior vena cava. |Example: The surgeon carefully repaired the damage to the postcava during the operation.  外科医生在手术中仔细修复了后腔静脉的损伤。|近义词：inferior vena cava (in humans)|反义词：N/A|名词
