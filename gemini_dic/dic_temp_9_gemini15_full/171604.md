pulmonata:/ˌpʌlmoʊˈneɪtə/| n. |肺螺类(软体动物)|Pulmonata is a taxonomic order within the Gastropoda, a large group of mollusks that includes snails and slugs.  Members of this order are characterized by their possession of a lung for respiration, typically a modified mantle cavity.  They are mostly terrestrial or freshwater species, although some may live in brackish water.  Many pulmonates are hermaphrodites, meaning they possess both male and female reproductive organs.|Example: Many land snails belong to the order Pulmonata.|近义词: Gastropoda (broader term)|反义词:  (No direct antonym, but Opistobranchia represents a contrasting order with different respiratory systems.)|名词
