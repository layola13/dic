hallelujah: /ˌhæləˈluːjə/ | int. |哈利路亚（赞美上帝）| An exclamation of praise or joy to God; often used in religious contexts.  It is a transliteration of the Hebrew phrase הַלְלוּיָהּ (halleluyah), meaning "praise ye the Lord". | Example: The choir sang "Hallelujah" at the end of the service.  唱诗班在礼拜结束时唱了“哈利路亚”。| 近义词：praise, hosanna, amen | 反义词：无 |  感叹词


USAGE->
This word is primarily an interjection and doesn't conjugate like verbs or nouns.  It doesn't have tenses or grammatical variations in the standard sense.  Its usage is limited to expressing praise or joy, typically in a religious context.


PHRASE->
Hallelujah chorus: 哈利路亚合唱
Sing hallelujah: 唱哈利路亚
A hallelujah moment:  一个值得赞美的时刻
Give hallelujah: 赞美上帝
Shout hallelujah: 高呼哈利路亚
