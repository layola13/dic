roseworm:/ˈroʊzwɜːrm/ | n. | 玫瑰虫，线虫 | A roseworm is a type of worm, often a nematode, that infests rose plants, feeding on their roots, stems, or leaves, causing damage and potentially killing the plant.  The term is not a formally classified scientific name, but rather a common name used to describe various worm species that affect roses. | Example: The rose bushes were severely damaged by roseworms, leaving them with wilting leaves and stunted growth.  |近义词: rose nematode, rootworm (in a broader sense) | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but the absence of roseworms would be a positive condition for rose health) | 词性:名词
