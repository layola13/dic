end up:/ɛnd ˈʌp/| v.|最终成为；最终处于某种状态|to reach or arrive at a particular place or situation, especially one that is unexpected|例句：We ended up staying much longer than we had planned. 我们最终停留的时间比我们计划的要长得多。

例句：After many setbacks, she ended up running her own successful business.  在经历了许多挫折之后，她最终经营了自己的成功企业。


近义词：finish, conclude, result in, culminate in|反义词：begin, start, commence|词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I usually end up working late. = 我通常最终加班到很晚。
They often end up arguing. = 他们经常以争吵告终。

一般过去时:
We ended up getting lost. = 我们最终迷路了。
He ended up failing the exam. = 他最终考试不及格。

现在完成时:
I've ended up owing a lot of money. = 我最终欠了很多钱。
She's ended up living in a small village. = 她最终住在一个小村庄里。

过去完成时:
They had ended up exhausted after the marathon. = 马拉松结束后，他们已经筋疲力尽了。
We had ended up missing the train. = 我们最终错过了火车。

将来时:
I think we'll end up being friends. = 我想我们最终会成为朋友。
You'll end up regretting this. = 你最终会后悔的。

虚拟语气:
If you keep driving like that, you'll end up crashing. = 如果你继续那样开车，你最终会撞车。
If she hadn't studied hard, she would have ended up failing. = 如果她不努力学习，她最终会不及格。

被动语态:
The project ended up being cancelled. = 项目最终被取消了。
The meeting ended up being postponed. = 会议最终被推迟了。

疑问句:
Where did you end up last night? = 你昨晚最终去了哪里？
How did you end up in this situation? = 你怎么会陷入这种境地的？

否定句:
I didn't end up going to the party. = 我最终没有去参加聚会。
She didn't end up winning the competition. = 她最终没有赢得比赛。



PHRASE->
