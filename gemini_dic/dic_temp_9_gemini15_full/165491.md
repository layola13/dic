prelosses:/ˈpriːlɒsɪz/ | n. | 预期损失；先前损失 | Losses that are expected to occur in the future, often used in financial contexts to estimate potential liabilities.  It refers to losses that have not yet happened but are anticipated based on past data, models, or projections. | Example: The company's prelosses for the next quarter are estimated at $1 million.  这家公司预计下一季度的预期损失为100万美元。  |近义词:anticipated losses, projected losses, potential losses | 反义词: profits, gains | 名词
