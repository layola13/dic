octateuch:/ˈɒkteɪjuːk/| n. |五经前七卷书|The Octateuch is a term used in some biblical scholarship to refer to the first eight books of the Hebrew Bible (or Old Testament): Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy, Joshua, Judges, and Ruth. These books are considered to form a cohesive narrative unit, particularly concerning the history of the Israelites from creation to the establishment of the monarchy.  The term is less common than Pentateuch (the first five books).| The study of the Octateuch provides insight into the development of Israelite religion and society. |近义词: Pentateuch (for the first five books)|反义词:|None.
