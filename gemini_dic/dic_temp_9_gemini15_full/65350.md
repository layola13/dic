eulogism:/ˈjuːlədʒɪzəm/ | n. |颂词；赞美；悼词 | A speech or writing in praise of someone or something, especially a eulogy.  | Example: The eulogism delivered at the funeral was both moving and heartfelt.  这次葬礼上的颂词既感人又真挚。|近义词：praise, commendation, tribute, panegyric |反义词：criticism, condemnation, denunciation |名词

词源:
Eulogism comes from the Greek word "eulogia" (εὐλογία), meaning "good speech" or "praise".  The "-ism" suffix indicates a system, practice, or doctrine.  Therefore, eulogism refers to the practice or instance of delivering a speech of praise.
