prespecifications: /ˌpriːˌspesɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 预先规范；预先说明 | Specifications made in advance; preliminary specifications.  These often serve as a starting point for further refinement and detailed planning.  They might include general goals, requirements, or constraints. |


例句：

The prespecifications for the new software included ease of use and compatibility with existing systems. = 新软件的预先规范包括易用性和与现有系统的兼容性。

The project's prespecifications were reviewed and revised before the detailed design phase began. = 在开始详细设计阶段之前，对项目的预先规范进行了审查和修改。

反义词：final specifications, post-specifications (this is not a common term)

The word is a combination of "pre-" (meaning before or in advance) and "specifications" (detailed descriptions of requirements, dimensions, or other characteristics).  "Specifications" itself comes from the Latin "speciēs" (appearance, kind) and the act of specifying, or making something specific.
