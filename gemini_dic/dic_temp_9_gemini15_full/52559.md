disfranchise:/dɪsˈfrænˌtʃaɪz/| vt.|剥夺…的公民权；取消…的权利|To deprive someone of a right or privilege, especially the right to vote.|The government disfranchised many citizens during the period of political unrest.  政府在政治动荡时期剥夺了许多公民的权利。|近义词：disenfranchise, deprive, disempower|反义词：enfranchise, empower

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They disfranchise voters who fail to register. = 他们剥夺未登记选民的投票权。
The law disfranchises anyone convicted of a felony. = 法律剥夺任何被判重罪者的权利。


一般过去时:
The ruling party disfranchised its political opponents. = 执政党剥夺了其政治对手的权利。
Many women were disfranchised before the suffrage movement. = 在争取妇女选举权的运动之前，许多妇女被剥夺了投票权。


现在完成时:
Recent electoral reforms have disfranchised a significant portion of the population. = 最近的选举改革已经剥夺了相当一部分人口的权利。
The new legislation has disfranchised thousands of voters due to strict ID requirements. = 新的立法由于严格的身份证要求而剥夺了成千上万选民的投票权。


过去完成时:
They had disfranchised the minority groups long before the protests began. = 在抗议活动开始很久以前，他们就已经剥夺了少数群体的权利。
The government had disfranchised the opposition party for years.  = 政府多年来一直剥夺反对党的权利。


将来时:
The proposed changes will disfranchise many elderly voters. =  拟议中的变化将剥夺许多老年选民的投票权。
They will disfranchise anyone found guilty of voter fraud. = 他们将剥夺任何被发现犯有选民欺诈罪的人的权利。


虚拟语气:
If they passed this law, it would disfranchise millions. = 如果他们通过这项法律，它将剥夺数百万人的权利。
I wish they hadn't disfranchised so many people. = 我真希望他们没有剥夺这么多人的权利。


被动语态:
Many were disfranchised during the military coup. =  在军事政变期间，许多人被剥夺了权利。
Thousands of voters have been disfranchised by the new regulations. =  成千上万的选民因新的规章制度而被剥夺了权利。


疑问句:
Were any citizens disfranchised by the new law? = 新法律是否剥夺了任何公民的权利？
Will this policy disfranchise a large number of people? =这项政策会剥夺大量人民的权利吗？


否定句:
The government did not disfranchise any voters in this election. = 政府在此次选举中没有剥夺任何选民的权利。
They would not disfranchise anyone without due process. = 他们不会在没有正当程序的情况下剥夺任何人的权利。
