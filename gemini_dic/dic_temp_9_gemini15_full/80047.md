goads:/ɡoʊdz/| n. v.|刺激；激励；驱使；尖锐的刺激物|A goad is something that encourages or urges someone to do something.  It can be a physical object used to prod animals, or a more metaphorical stimulus.|例句：His criticism served as a goad to greater effort.  他的批评促使他更加努力。|例句：The farmer used a goad to urge the oxen forward.  农夫用尖竿驱赶牛前进。|近义词：spur, incentive, stimulus, prod|反义词：discouragement, deterrent|名词,动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He goads his team to success. = 他激励他的团队取得成功。
The constant criticism goads him. = 持续的批评刺激着他。

一般过去时:
She goaded him into arguing. = 她激他争论。
The coach goaded the players to victory. = 教练激励球员取得胜利。

现在完成时:
His ambition has goaded him on. = 他的雄心一直激励着他。
They have goaded me into making a decision. = 他们一直怂恿我做决定。

过去完成时:
He had goaded her into a fight. = 他之前激怒她让她打架。
The media had goaded the public into a frenzy. = 媒体之前煽动公众陷入狂热。

将来时:
I will goad him into action. = 我将激励他采取行动。
The news will goad them to protest. = 这个消息将激励他们抗议。

虚拟语气:
If I goaded him, he might help. = 如果我激励他，他可能会帮忙。
I wish I hadn't goaded him. = 我真希望我没有激怒他。

被动语态:
He was goaded into making a mistake. = 他被激怒而犯了一个错误。
The team was goaded to greater efforts. = 球队被激励做出更大的努力。

疑问句:
What goads them to act? = 什么激励他们行动？
Does this criticism goad you? = 这种批评会激励你吗？

否定句:
I will not goad him. = 我不会激怒他。
Nothing goaded her into a response. = 没有什么能激起她的回应。


PHRASE->
goad someone into doing something = 怂恿某人做某事
goad someone on = 鼓励某人继续
goad someone to action = 激励某人采取行动
a goad to action = 行动的激励
under the goad of = 在…的驱使下


词源:
From Old English *gādian (“to goad, to prick”), from Proto-Germanic *gadizō (“to goad”), from Proto-Indo-European *gʰedʰ- (“to strike, to stab”).  The word's evolution reflects its original use as a pointed stick for prodding animals.
