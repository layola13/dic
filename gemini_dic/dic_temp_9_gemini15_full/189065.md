saboteur: /ˈsæbəˌtʊər/ | n. |破坏分子，捣乱者 | A person who engages in sabotage; one who commits acts of destruction or disruption, often secretly or deliberately.  |The saboteur planted a bomb in the factory.  这个破坏分子在工厂里安放了一枚炸弹。 The government accused him of being a saboteur. 政府指控他是破坏分子。| subversive, vandal, troublemaker | supporter, builder, constructor | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He sabotages the project. 他破坏了这个项目。
She sabotages the meeting. 她破坏了会议。

一般过去时:
They sabotaged the pipeline. 他们破坏了管道。
He sabotaged the election. 他破坏了选举。

现在完成时:
The workers have sabotaged the machinery. 工人们已经破坏了机器。
She has sabotaged our plans. 她破坏了我们的计划。

过去完成时:
They had sabotaged the bridge before the army arrived. 军队到达之前，他们已经破坏了桥梁。
The spies had sabotaged the communication system. 间谍们已经破坏了通讯系统。

将来时:
They will sabotage the negotiations. 他们将破坏谈判。
She will sabotage his career. 她将破坏他的事业。

虚拟语气:
If they sabotaged the system, we would be in trouble. 如果他们破坏了系统，我们将陷入困境。
I wish they hadn't sabotaged the experiment. 我真希望他们没有破坏实验。

被动语态:
The equipment was sabotaged by a rival company. 设备被竞争对手公司破坏了。
The election was sabotaged by a group of extremists.  选举被一群极端分子破坏了。

疑问句:

Did anyone sabotage the computer network? 有人破坏了电脑网络吗？

否定句:
They did not sabotage the operation. 他们没有破坏行动。
She wouldn't sabotage her own team. 她不会破坏她自己的团队。
