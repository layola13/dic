mediant:/ˈmiːdiənt/| n. |中音，中音程|In music theory, a mediant is the interval of a third above or below the tonic or root note of a scale or chord.  It refers to the third degree of a major or minor scale.|例句：The mediant of C major is E.  C大调的中音是E。|近义词：third (in the context of musical intervals)|反义词：None specifically, but the opposite concept would be the tonic or root.|名词
