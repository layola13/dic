disrupt:/dɪsˈrʌpt/ | vt. |扰乱；中断；破坏；扰动 | to interrupt (an event, activity, or process) by causing a disturbance or problem.  |例句：The demonstration disrupted the traffic. 示威游行扰乱了交通。

例句：The snowstorm disrupted air travel for days.暴风雪扰乱了数天的空中旅行。


近义词：interrupt, interfere with, hinder, impede, upset |反义词：order, organize, facilitate


词源:  The word "disrupt" comes from the Latin prefix "dis-" (meaning "apart," "away," or "not") and the Latin verb "rumpere" (meaning "to break").  Therefore, the word literally means "to break apart" or "to break up."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The construction work disrupts the traffic flow. = 建设工程扰乱了交通流量。
She disrupts the class with her constant talking. = 她不停地说话扰乱了课堂秩序。


一般过去时:
The power outage disrupted the computer system. = 停电打断了计算机系统。
The strike disrupted production at the factory. = 罢工扰乱了工厂的生产。


现在完成时:
The pandemic has disrupted global supply chains. = 疫情扰乱了全球供应链。
Technological advancements have disrupted many traditional industries. = 技术进步已经扰乱了许多传统行业。


过去完成时:
The heavy rain had disrupted the train schedule. = 大雨扰乱了火车时刻表。
The previous accident had disrupted the highway for hours. = 先前的意外事故使高速公路瘫痪了数小时。


将来时:

The new policy may disrupt the existing market balance. = 新政策可能会扰乱现有的市场平衡。


虚拟语气:
If the storm hadn't disrupted the flight, we would have arrived on time. = 如果暴风雨没有扰乱航班，我们就按时到达了。
I wish the traffic hadn't disrupted my schedule. = 我真希望交通没有打乱我的计划。


被动语态:
The meeting was disrupted by a sudden power cut. = 会议因突然停电而被打断。
The production line was disrupted by a machine malfunction. = 生产线因机器故障而中断。

否定句:
