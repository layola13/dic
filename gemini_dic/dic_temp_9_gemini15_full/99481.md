interferometer:/ˌɪntərˈfɛrɒmɪtər/| n. |干涉仪|An instrument that uses the interference of waves (usually light waves) to make precise measurements of distance, wavelength, or refractive index.  It works by splitting a beam of light into two or more beams, sending them along different paths, and then recombining them. The interference pattern created reveals information about the paths the beams have taken.|Example:  Astronomers use interferometers to create images of distant stars with incredibly high resolution. 天文学家使用干涉仪来创建具有极高分辨率的遥远恒星图像。|近义词：interferometer (no true synonyms, but devices with similar functions could be mentioned, such as spectroscope)|反义词：N/A
