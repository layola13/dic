counterintuitively:/ˌkaʊntərɪnˈtʃuɪtɪvli/ | adv. | 反直觉地；出乎意料地 | Contrary to what intuition or common sense would suggest.  It describes something that happens or is true in a way that seems opposite to what one would expect. |  The solution, counterintuitively, was to add more water to the mixture.  反直觉的是，解决方法是往混合物中加更多的水。 | unexpectedly, surprisingly, paradoxically | intuitively | adv.


例句：

Counterintuitively, the best way to lose weight is to eat more frequently, but smaller portions. 反直觉的是，减肥的最佳方法是更频繁地进食，但每次少吃。

The team, counterintuitively, performed better under pressure.  反直觉的是，这个团队在压力下表现得更好。


近义词：

unexpectedly, surprisingly, paradoxically, surprisingly


反义词：

intuitively, expectedly


词源:

The word is formed from the prefix "counter-" (meaning "against" or "opposite"), the noun "intuition" (meaning "instinct" or "gut feeling"), and the adverbial suffix "-ly".  Therefore, it literally means "in a way that is against intuition".
