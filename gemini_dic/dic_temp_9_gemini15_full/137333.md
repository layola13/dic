词源:

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not" or "without."
* **tarnish**:  Verb meaning "to lose luster or shine, especially as a result of oxidation."  It comes from Old French *ternir*, meaning "to dull, darken." Ultimately, the root is likely Proto-Indo-European *ter-,* related to words meaning "to rub" or "to wear."
* **-able**: Suffix meaning "capable of being" or "worthy of being."

Therefore, "nontarnishable" means "not capable of being tarnished."
