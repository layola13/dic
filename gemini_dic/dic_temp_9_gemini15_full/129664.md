non-defining:/ˌnɒn dɪˈfaɪnɪŋ/| adj. |非限定的，非限制性的 |  Describes a clause or phrase that adds extra information to a sentence but is not essential to the meaning of the sentence. It is set off by commas.  | Example: My brother, who lives in London, is a doctor. (The clause "who lives in London" is non-defining because it adds extra information but is not essential to understanding that my brother is a doctor.)|近义词：additional, supplementary |反义词：defining, restrictive


USAGE->
This word is an adjective,  therefore it doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  It modifies nouns.  Example usages will show how it functions in different sentence structures.

Simple Sentence:
The book, which was quite old, was filled with fascinating illustrations.  = 那本书，相当古老，充满了迷人的插图。

Complex Sentence:
The concert, that took place in Central Park, was attended by thousands. = 在中央公园举行的音乐会，吸引了成千上万的人参加。


Compound Sentence:
My car, which is a bright red, is very reliable; however, my bicycle, which is old and rusty, is not. = 我的车，鲜红色，非常可靠；然而，我的自行车，又旧又生锈，不可靠。


Parallel Structure:
The students, who were excited, and the teachers, who were equally enthusiastic, celebrated the school's success. =  兴奋的学生和同样热情的老师们一起庆祝学校的成功。


Conditional Sentence:
If the information, which is crucial to the investigation, is withheld, the case will remain unsolved. = 如果对调查至关重要的信息被隐瞒，案件将无法侦破。


Passive Voice:
The report, which was compiled last week, was presented to the board. = 上周编写的报告已提交给董事会。
