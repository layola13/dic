preextending: /ˌpriːɪkˈstɛndɪŋ/ | v. | 预先延长；提前延长 |  To extend something beforehand; to lengthen something in advance.  This word is not a standard dictionary entry and is likely a newly coined or rarely used word, formed by combining "pre-" (before) and "extending".  Its meaning is derived directly from the components. |

The company is preextending its warranty program to appease customers. = 公司正在提前延长其保修计划以安抚客户。

They decided to preextend the deadline for the project to allow more time. = 他们决定提前延长项目的截止日期以争取更多时间。
