miscreated:/ˌmɪsˈkreɪtɪd/| adj.|被错误创造的，畸形的，怪诞的|Something miscreated is wrongly or badly made or formed, often implying a sense of unnaturalness or imperfection. It suggests a flaw in the creation process, leading to a distorted or flawed outcome.  The term often carries a slightly negative or unsettling connotation.|例句：The miscreated creature terrorized the village.  那个被错误创造的生物吓坏了村庄。

The sculptor's miscreated statue was a grotesque parody of human form. 雕塑家的畸形雕像是对人形的怪诞模仿。


近义词：malformed, deformed, monstrous, aberrant, flawed, imperfect|反义词：perfectly formed, well-made, flawless, natural


词源:  The word "miscreated" is formed by combining the prefix "mis-" (meaning "wrongly" or "badly") with the past participle of the verb "create" ("created").  Therefore, its roots lie in the Old French "creer" (to create) which ultimately originates from the Latin "creare."
