raggy:/ˈræɡi/| adj.|蓬乱的；破旧的；粗糙的；劣质的 |  Describes something that is unkempt, worn-out, rough, or of poor quality. It often refers to clothing, hair, or surfaces. |例句：The dog's fur was all raggy after its romp in the woods. = 这只狗在树林里玩耍后，毛发都蓬乱了。

The old rug was raggy and worn thin in places. = 这块旧地毯又旧又破，有些地方磨薄了。|近义词：shabby, tattered, rough, unkempt |反义词：neat, tidy, smooth, pristine


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The old coat is raggy. = 这件旧外套很破旧。
His hair looks raggy today. = 他今天的头发看起来很蓬乱。


一般过去时:
The rug was raggy after years of use. = 这块地毯用了多年后变得很破旧。
Her clothes were raggy and stained. = 她的衣服又脏又破。


现在完成时:
The constant rain has made the path raggy. = 持续的雨水使这条路变得坑坑洼洼。
Years of neglect have made the building look raggy. = 多年的忽视使这栋建筑看起来破旧不堪。


过去完成时:
The car had become raggy before they decided to sell it. = 在他们决定卖掉这辆车之前，它就已经变得破旧不堪了。
The once-beautiful dress had become raggy and faded. = 这件曾经美丽的连衣裙已经变得破旧褪色了。


将来时:
The carpet will become raggy if we don't clean it regularly. = 如果我们不定期清洁地毯，它会变得很脏。
That shirt will look raggy after just one wash. = 那件衬衫洗一次就会看起来很破旧。


虚拟语气:
If I had a new coat, I wouldn't look so raggy. = 如果我有一件新外套，我就不会看起来这么邋遢了。
I wish my hair wasn't so raggy. = 我希望我的头发不要这么蓬乱。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
