cornering: /ˈkɔːrnərɪŋ/ | n. |拐弯；垄断；逼迫；控制 | The act of turning a corner;  the act of forcing someone into a difficult situation; gaining control of a market. | 例句：The sharp cornering of the car nearly caused an accident.  这辆车的急转弯差点造成事故。  The company achieved cornering of the market for their product. 这家公司实现了对其产品的市场垄断。 |近义词：rounding, monopolizing, controlling, maneuvering | 反义词：straightening, releasing, yielding


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The driver is cornering too fast. = 司机转弯速度太快。
He is cornering the market on rare stamps. = 他正在垄断稀有邮票的市场。


一般过去时:
The cyclist cornered sharply at the bend. = 骑自行车的人在弯道处急转弯。
They cornered the suspect in a dark alley. = 他们在黑暗的小巷里逼住了嫌疑人。


现在完成时:
She has cornered the market in organic produce. = 她已经垄断了有机农产品的市场。
The police have cornered the escaped prisoner. = 警察已经将逃犯逼到了绝境。


过去完成时:
The car had cornered poorly before the accident. = 事故发生前，这辆车转弯很糟糕。
The investors had cornered the market before the price surge. = 在价格飙涨之前，投资者已经控制了市场。


将来时:
He will corner the market on new technology. = 他将垄断新技术的市场。
The police will corner the robbers soon. = 警察很快就会将抢劫犯逼入绝境。


虚拟语气:
If he cornered the market, he would be very wealthy. = 如果他垄断了市场，他就会非常富有。
I wish I had cornered the market on that stock. = 我真希望我当时垄断了那种股票。


被动语态:
The suspect was cornered by the police. = 嫌疑人被警察逼到了绝境。
The market is being cornered by a few large companies. = 市场正被几家大公司垄断。


疑问句:
Is he good at cornering at high speeds? = 他擅长高速转弯吗？
Are they trying to corner the market? = 他们试图垄断市场吗？


否定句:
He didn't corner the market; his competitor did. = 他没有垄断市场；他的竞争对手垄断了。
The driver didn't corner smoothly; he almost lost control. = 司机转弯不平稳；他差点失去控制。
