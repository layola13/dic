clavichord:/ˈklævɪkɔːrd/| n. |古钢琴 |A clavichord is a stringed keyboard instrument that produces sound when brass tangents, attached to the keys, strike the strings.  It was popular in the Baroque period and known for its quiet, intimate sound.  Unlike the harpsichord or piano, the volume of the clavichord is controlled by the force with which the keys are pressed. |The clavichord was a favorite instrument of many composers of the Baroque period. | harpsichord, pianoforte |  | n.
