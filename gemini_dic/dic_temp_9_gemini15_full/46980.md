darwinism:/ˈdɑːrwɪnɪzəm/| n. |达尔文主义|The theory of evolution by natural selection, proposed by Charles Darwin.  It explains the diversity of life on Earth through the process of adaptation and the inheritance of advantageous traits.  Darwinism emphasizes the role of natural selection, variation, and inheritance in shaping the evolution of species. |The theory of Darwinism revolutionized biology. 达尔文主义理论彻底改变了生物学。|evolutionary theory, natural selection|creationism|名词
