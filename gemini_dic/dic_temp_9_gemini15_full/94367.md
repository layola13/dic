implant:/ɪmˈplænt/ | n., vt. | 植入物；移植；植入 | To insert (something) into a part of the body, especially by surgery.  | 例句：The surgeon successfully implanted a pacemaker.  外科医生成功地植入了起搏器。

例句：They implanted a microchip into the animal for tracking purposes. 他们为了追踪目的向动物植入了微型芯片。


近义词：insert, embed, graft, engraft | 反义词：remove, extract, excise


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The doctor implants the device carefully. = 医生小心地植入该装置。
She implants new ideas into her students' minds. = 她将新的思想植入学生的头脑中。


一般过去时:
They implanted the new tooth yesterday. = 他们昨天植入了新牙。
The scientist implanted the gene into the plant. = 科学家将基因植入植物中。


现在完成时:
He has implanted several pacemakers in his career. = 他在他的职业生涯中植入了好几个起搏器。
She has implanted a strong sense of responsibility in her children. = 她已在她的孩子们心中灌输了强烈的责任感。


过去完成时:

The researchers had implanted the sensors before the experiment began. = 研究人员在实验开始之前已经植入了传感器。


将来时:

The company plans to implant the software in all its systems. = 公司计划将其软件植入所有系统中。


虚拟语气:
If I were a doctor, I would implant the latest technology. = 如果我是一名医生，我会植入最新的技术。
It's as if they had implanted a false memory. = 就好像他们植入了虚假的记忆一样。


被动语态:

疑问句:

词源:
The word "implant" comes from the Old French word "implenter," which means "to plant in."  This in turn derives from the Latin prefix "im-" (in, into) and "plantare" (to plant).
