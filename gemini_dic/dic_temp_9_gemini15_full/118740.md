metalanguage:/ˈmɛtəlæŋɡwɪdʒ/| n. |元语言，超语言|A language used to talk about another language; a system of terms, notations, or signs used to describe or analyze another system.  It's used to discuss the features, structure, and use of a language. For example, grammatical terms like "noun," "verb," and "adjective" are part of the metalanguage used to describe the English language.|Linguists use metalanguage to describe the rules and structures of a language.  For example, when discussing the grammar of a sentence, they might use terms such as "subject," "predicate," and "object," which are part of the metalanguage.|近义词：descriptive language, analytic language|反义词：object language
