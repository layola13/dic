descendant: /dɪˈsɛndənt/ | n. | 后代，子孙 | A person, plant, or animal that is descended from a particular ancestor.  |例句：She is a direct descendant of Queen Victoria. 她维多利亚女王的直系后裔。 |例句：The modern apple is a descendant of the wild crabapple. 现代苹果是野苹果的后代。|近义词：offspring, progeny, successor |反义词：ancestor, forebear

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a descendant of a long line of kings. = 他是世袭君主家族的后代。
The plant is a descendant of a rare species. = 这种植物是稀有物种的后代。


一般过去时:
She was a direct descendant of the famous painter. = 她是那位著名画家的直系后代。
The breed was a descendant of ancient wolves.  = 这个品种是古代狼的后代。


现在完成时:
Scientists have identified the descendant species. = 科学家们已经确定了后代物种。
Many families have traced their descendants through genealogy. = 许多家庭通过家谱追溯了他们的后代。


过去完成时:
By the time the war ended, many descendants had lost their homes. = 到战争结束时，许多后代已经失去了家园。
The historian had already identified the descendants of the original settlers. = 历史学家已经确定了最初定居者的后代。


将来时:
Future generations will be the descendants of our actions today. = 子孙后代将是我们今天行动的后代。
Researchers will continue to study the descendants of this unique species. = 研究人员将继续研究这个独特物种的后代。


虚拟语气:
If he were a descendant of royalty, he would have a title. = 如果他是皇室后代，他将会有一个头衔。
If they were descendants of Vikings, they would have a stronger seafaring tradition. = 如果他们是维京人的后代，他们将会有更强的航海传统。


被动语态:

否定句:
