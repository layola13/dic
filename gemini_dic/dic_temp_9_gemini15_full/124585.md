mouse-like: /ˈmaʊsˌlaɪk/ | adj. | 像老鼠的；胆小的；懦弱的 | Resembling a mouse, especially in being small, timid, or shy.  |例句：The small, mouse-like creature scurried away when it heard a noise.  那只像老鼠一样的小动物听到声音就赶紧跑开了。

例句：He had a mouse-like quality about him, always seeming to shrink away from confrontation. 他有一种胆小的样子，总是似乎在躲避冲突。


近义词：timid, shy, cowardly, faint-hearted, meek

反义词：bold, brave, courageous, fearless, assertive


词源:  The word is a compound adjective formed from "mouse" and "-like," a suffix indicating resemblance.  "Mouse" comes from Old English *mūs, related to other Germanic words for "mouse" (e.g., German Maus). "-like" is derived from Old English -līc, meaning "like" or "similar to."
