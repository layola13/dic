noncoinage:/ˈnɒnkɔɪnɪdʒ/| adj. |非铸造的；非造币的|Not made by minting or stamping metal; not of the nature of coined money.|The museum displayed several noncoinage forms of currency, including shells and beads.|近义词：uncoined, non-minted|反义词：coined, minted


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective, and thus doesn't have verb conjugations.  The usage focuses on describing things that aren't coins or haven't been produced through the minting process.  Therefore, tense-based conjugations are not applicable.

Example 1:
The archaeologist found several noncoinage artifacts at the dig site. = 考古学家在挖掘现场发现了几件非铸币文物。


Example 2:
Historians are increasingly interested in studying noncoinage forms of currency in ancient societies. = 历史学家越来越关注古代社会中非铸币货币形式的研究。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "noncoinage".  The word is used in a descriptive, rather than idiomatic, context.
