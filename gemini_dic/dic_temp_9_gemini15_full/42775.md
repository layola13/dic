counts:/kaʊnts/ |/kaʊnts/| n. v.|计数，次数，重要性；认为重要，有价值|The number of times something happens or exists.  To consider something important or significant.|例句：The teacher counts the students.老师数着学生人数。

The project counts for a large proportion of the company's revenue. 这个项目占公司收入的大部分。


近义词：matters, signifies,  numbers,  totals| 反义词：insignificant, unimportant| 词性：Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He counts the money every day. 他每天都数钱。
The professor counts attendance regularly. 教授定期点名。


一般过去时:
She counted the sheep before she fell asleep. 她睡前数羊。
They counted the votes after the election. 他们选举后清点选票。


现在完成时:
I have counted to a hundred. 我已经数到一百了。
We have counted all the apples in the basket. 我们已经数清了篮子里所有的苹果。


过去完成时:
They had counted the ballots before the announcement. 他们在宣布结果之前已经清点过选票了。
She had counted on his help, but he didn't come. 她指望过他的帮助，但他没有来。


将来时:
I will count the stars tonight. 今晚我将数星星。
They will count the votes tomorrow. 他们明天将清点选票。


虚拟语气:
If I counted correctly, there are twenty apples. 如果我数得没错，有二十个苹果。
I wish I had counted the money before leaving. 我希望离开前我已经数过钱了。


被动语态:
The votes are being counted now. 选票正在被清点。
The students were counted by the teacher. 学生们被老师数过了。


疑问句:
How many people are counted in the census? 人口普查统计了多少人？
How many times have you counted them? 你数过几次了？


否定句:
He didn't count the change. 他没有数零钱。
They didn't count on his support. 他们没有指望他的支持。


PHRASE->
count on/upon  依靠，指望
count down 倒计时
count in 包含，算上
count out 排除，不算
count one's blessings 感激所拥有的一切

词源:
The word "count" comes from the Old French word "conter," meaning "to tell" or "to relate."  This ultimately derives from the Latin word "computare," meaning "to compute" or "to reckon."  The sense of counting objects developed from the original meaning of recounting or narrating.
