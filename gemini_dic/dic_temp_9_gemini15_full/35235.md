clumpiest:/ˈklʌmpiɪst/ | adj. | 最笨拙的，最粗笨的 | Most awkward or clumsy.  It describes the superlative degree of clumsiness.  |例句：He tripped over the rug, proving himself to be the clumpiest person in the room. 他绊倒在地毯上，证明自己是屋里最笨拙的人。

例句：Of all the dancers, she was by far the clumpiest, constantly bumping into other performers. 在所有舞者中，她无疑是最笨拙的，不断地撞到其他表演者。


近义词：awkwardest, most ungainly, most clumsy, most ungraceful | 反义词：most graceful, most elegant, most deft


词源:  Derived from "clump," which originally referred to a shapeless mass or a group of things clustered together.  The sense of awkward movement likely developed from the imagery of someone moving in a clumsy, uncoordinated way, like a shapeless clump. The "-iest" suffix denotes the superlative form, indicating the highest degree of clumsiness.


用法说明：

clumpiest is an adjective, and it's used to describe a person, an animal, or an object that exhibits the highest degree of clumsiness or awkwardness.  It's typically used in comparative or superlative constructions.
