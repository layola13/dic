clinged:/klɪŋd/| v.|粘住；依附；坚持|The past tense of "cling". It describes the action of holding on tightly to something or someone, or adhering closely to a surface.  |The child clung to his mother's leg.  孩子紧紧抓住母亲的腿。|The wet clothes clung to his skin. 湿衣服贴在他的皮肤上。|adhere, stick, hold on|detach, let go|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He clings to his beliefs. 他坚持他的信仰。
The ivy clings to the wall. 常春藤攀附在墙上。

一般过去时:
She clung to the railing as the boat rocked. 船摇晃时，她紧紧抓住栏杆。
He clung to hope despite the setbacks. 尽管遭受挫折，他仍然抱有希望。

现在完成时:
They have clung to their traditions for centuries. 他们几百年来一直坚持他们的传统。
The scent of rain has clung to the earth all day. 雨水的味道整天都弥漫在大地上。

过去完成时:
She had clung to the memory of her childhood. 她一直珍藏着她童年的回忆。
He had clung to that belief until the very end. 他直到最后都坚持那个信念。

将来时:
She will cling to her principles no matter what. 不管发生什么，她都会坚持她的原则。
The climber will cling to the rock face for dear life. 攀登者将紧紧抓住岩壁，保住性命。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would cling to your dreams. 如果我是你，我会坚持你的梦想。
I wish I had clung to that opportunity. 我真希望我当时抓住了那个机会。

被动语态:
The climber was clung to by a rescue rope. 攀登者被一根救援绳索紧紧抓住。
The scent was clung to the air. 气味弥漫在空中。

疑问句:
Did he cling to his ideals? 他坚持他的理想吗？
Why did she cling to him so tightly? 她为什么那样紧紧地抱着他？

否定句:
He didn't cling to the branch. 他没有抓住树枝。
She didn't cling to her anger for long. 她没有长时间地生气。
