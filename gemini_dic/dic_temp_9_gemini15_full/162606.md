preannounced:/ˌpriːəˈnaʊnst/| adj.|预先宣布的；预告的|Previously announced; made known in advance.|The preannounced price increase caused some customers to postpone their purchases. 预先宣布的价格上涨导致一些顾客推迟了购买。|announced beforehand, foreshadowed|unannounced, surprising|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company preannounces its earnings every quarter. = 公司每个季度都会预先公布其收益。
This event is preannounced on the website. = 此活动已在网站上预先公布。


一般过去时:
The concert was preannounced a month in advance. = 音乐会提前一个月就预先宣布了。
They preannounced the product launch to generate hype. = 他们预先宣布了产品发布以制造宣传。


现在完成时:
The government has preannounced several policy changes. = 政府已预先宣布了几项政策变化。
They have preannounced the winner of the competition. = 他们已经预先宣布了比赛的获胜者。


过去完成时:
The news had been preannounced before the official statement. = 在官方声明之前，这个消息就已经预先宣布了。
The results had been preannounced, so the announcement was anticlimactic. = 结果已经预先公布了，所以公告显得有些虎头蛇尾。


将来时:
The company will preannounce its next product line next week. = 公司下周将预先公布其下一条产品线。
They will preannounce the changes to the schedule. = 他们将预先宣布日程安排的变更。


虚拟语气:
If they had preannounced the changes, we would have been better prepared. = 如果他们预先宣布了这些变化，我们就会有更好的准备。
I wish they had preannounced the meeting. = 我希望他们预先宣布了会议。


被动语态:
The price increase was preannounced to the public. = 价格上涨已预先向公众宣布。
The decision was preannounced by the committee. = 该决定已由委员会预先宣布。


疑问句:
Was the meeting preannounced? = 会议预先宣布了吗？
Has the new policy been preannounced? = 新政策已经预先宣布了吗？


否定句:
The changes were not preannounced. = 这些变化没有预先宣布。
The decision was not preannounced to the employees. = 该决定没有预先通知员工。
