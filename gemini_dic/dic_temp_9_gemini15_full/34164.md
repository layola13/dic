clambering: /ˈklæmbərɪŋ/ | v. |攀爬；攀登；挣扎 |  The act of climbing awkwardly or with difficulty, often using hands and feet. It implies a struggle or effort.  | 例句：The children were clambering over the rocks.孩子们在岩石上攀爬。

The monkeys were clambering through the trees.猴子们在树林里攀爬。


近义词：climbing, scrambling, scrambling up, scaling | 反义词：descending, sliding down

词源:
The word "clamber" likely originates from the Old French word "clambrer," meaning "to climb."  The suffix "-ing" forms the present participle, indicating an ongoing action.
