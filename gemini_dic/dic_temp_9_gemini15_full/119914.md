militias: /məˈlɪʃəz/ | n. | 民兵组织；民兵 | Unorganized groups of citizens who are trained and equipped for military service, especially in times of emergency.  They are often distinct from a nation's regular armed forces. |


例句：

The state governor called up the militias to help control the riots. = 州长召集民兵来帮助控制骚乱。

Several local militias joined forces to defend the border. = 几个地方民兵组织联合起来保卫边境。


近义词：citizen army, armed citizenry, volunteer army

反义词：regular army, standing army

词源:

The word "militia" comes from the Late Latin word *militia, meaning "military service," which in turn derives from the Latin word miles, meaning "soldier."  The concept of citizen-soldiers has ancient roots, but the modern English word emerged during the Middle Ages.
