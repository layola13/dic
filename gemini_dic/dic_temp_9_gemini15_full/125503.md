/ˌmʌltiˈriːflɛksɪz/ | n. |多重反射; 多重反射现象 | Multiple reflexes are involuntary responses involving more than one reflex arc.  This term is less common in everyday language and more frequently used in specialized fields like neurology and physiology.  It often refers to a complex series of reflexes triggered by a single stimulus, or the interaction of multiple reflexes simultaneously or sequentially.   | Example:  The study investigated the multireflexes elicited by sudden loud noises in infants.  这项研究调查了突然的巨响在婴儿身上引起的多种反射。 |近义词: complex reflexes, chain reflexes |反义词: single reflex, simple reflex |名词
