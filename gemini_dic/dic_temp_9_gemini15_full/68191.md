fair:/feər/| adj., n., v.|公平的；展览会；公平对待；|

英文解析:As an adjective, "fair" means just, impartial, and equitable.  It can also describe something that is pleasing or satisfactory, or something of average quality. As a noun, it refers to a gathering of people for amusement or entertainment, often involving games and competitions, or a market. As a verb, it means to give someone a fair chance or to treat someone justly.


例句:

adj. 公平的:
The judge made a fair decision. = 法官做出了公平的判决。

The weather was fair and sunny. = 天气晴朗宜人。


n. 展览会:
We went to the county fair. = 我们去了县里的展览会。

There's a job fair next week. = 下周有一个招聘会。


v. 公平对待:
It's important to fair with everyone. = 公平对待每个人很重要。

She tried to fair the deal between the parties. = 她试图公平地处理各方之间的交易。
