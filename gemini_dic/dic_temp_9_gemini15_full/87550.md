heteromorphism:/ˌhɛtərəˈmɔrfɪzəm/| n. |异形性，异态性 |The existence of different forms in the same species.  This can refer to different forms within a single generation (polymorphism) or different forms across generations (alternation of generations). Examples include the different forms of some insects (e.g., larvae and adults) or the alternation of generations in plants. |The dimorphism of the butterfly is a striking example of heteromorphism.  蝴蝶的二态性是异形性的一个显著例子。


The phenomenon of heteromorphism is common in many plant and animal species. 异形性现象在许多动植物物种中很常见。|近义词：polymorphism, dimorphism|反义词：homomorphism
