digressed:/daɪˈɡrɛst/| v.|离题，偏离主题|To stray from the main subject in speaking or writing.|The speaker digressed from his prepared remarks to answer a question from the audience.  演讲者偏离了准备好的发言稿来回答观众的提问。|近义词：deviate, wander, ramble|反义词：focus, concentrate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He digresses frequently in his lectures. = 他在讲座中经常离题。
She digresses from the main point of her argument. = 她偏离了她论点的主要内容。


一般过去时:
The professor digressed from the topic of the war. = 教授偏离了战争这个话题。
He digressed into a long story about his childhood. = 他扯到关于他童年的长篇故事上去了。


现在完成时:
He has digressed several times during this presentation. = 在这次演示中，他已经多次离题了。
She has digressed so much that we've lost track of the original subject. = 她已经离题太多，以至于我们忘记了最初的话题。


过去完成时:
By that point, he had digressed so far that no one remembered the initial question. = 到那时，他已经离题太远，以至于没有人记得最初的问题了。
They had digressed from the agenda and were discussing personal matters. = 他们已经偏离了议程，开始讨论私事了。


将来时:
I think he will digress again if you ask that question. = 我想如果你问那个问题，他会再次离题。
She will probably digress when she gets to that part of the story. = 当她讲到故事的那一部分时，她可能会离题。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't digressed, the lecture would have been more focused. = 如果他没有离题，讲座就会更有重点。
I wish he hadn't digressed so much; I didn't understand the conclusion. = 我真希望他没有离题这么多；我不理解结论。


被动语态:
The conversation was digressed from the planned topic. = 会话偏离了计划中的话题。
The meeting was digressed from its original purpose by irrelevant issues. = 会议因无关紧要的问题而偏离了其最初的目的。


疑问句:
Did he digress during his speech? = 他在演讲中离题了吗？
Has she digressed from the main argument? = 她已经偏离了主要的论点了吗？


否定句:
He did not digress from his prepared speech. = 他没有偏离他准备好的演讲。
She didn't digress at all during the interview. = 她在面试期间根本没有离题。
