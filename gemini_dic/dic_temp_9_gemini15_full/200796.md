slicking: /ˈslɪkɪŋ/ | v. | 使光滑；使顺滑；打扮；欺骗 | Making something smooth or shiny;  grooming or styling hair;  acting in a deceptively smooth or charming manner. | 例句：He was slicking back his hair. 他正在把头发梳向脑后。

例句：She spent hours slicking her hair for the party. 她花了几个小时为派对打扮头发。


近义词：smoothing, polishing, grooming,  conning (欺骗)


反义词：roughening, matting (头发)


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slicks his hair every morning. = 他每天早上都梳理头发。
She slicks her presentation with impressive data. = 她用令人印象深刻的数据使她的演示文稿看起来很出色。


一般过去时:
He slicked back his hair before the interview. = 面试前他把头发梳到后面。
The salesman slicked his way into a big deal. = 销售员巧妙地达成了一笔大交易。


现在完成时:
She has slicked her resume to make it more appealing. = 她已经润色了她的简历，使其更具吸引力。
He has slicked his way to the top of the company. = 他已凭借手段爬到了公司的顶端。


过去完成时:
They had slicked the car before showing it to the potential buyer. = 在向潜在买家展示汽车之前，他们已经把汽车擦亮了。
She had slicked her hair up in a sophisticated bun. = 她已经把头发盘成一个精致的发髻。


将来时:
He will slick his hair before the wedding. = 他将在婚礼前梳理头发。
They will slick the floor for the dance. = 他们将为跳舞而擦亮地板。


虚拟语气:

疑问句:
