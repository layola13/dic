reforecasts:/ˌriːˈfɔːrˌkæsts/| n. |再预测，重新预测|A reforecast is a new forecast made using the same model and data but with updated information or a different approach.  It's often used to improve the accuracy of weather forecasts or other predictions by incorporating new observations or correcting errors in the initial data. |Example: The meteorologists conducted reforecasts to refine their predictions of the hurricane's path.  气象学家进行了重新预测以改进他们对飓风路径的预测。|近义词：refined forecast, revised prediction |反义词：initial forecast
