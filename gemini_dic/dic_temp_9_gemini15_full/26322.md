buyout:/ˈbaɪaʊt/| n. |收购；买断；买断协议|The act of purchasing a company's outstanding shares to take it private, or purchasing a significant portion of a company's assets.|例句：The company announced a management buyout.  这家公司宣布了一项管理层收购。|例句：They offered a buyout to all employees over 50. 他们向所有50岁以上的员工提供了买断补偿。|近义词：acquisition, takeover, purchase|反义词：sellout, divestiture


USAGE->

一般现在时:
A private equity firm often conducts buyouts. 私募股权公司经常进行收购。
He considers a buyout of his competitor. 他正在考虑收购他的竞争对手。


一般过去时:
The company underwent a leveraged buyout last year. 该公司去年经历了一次杠杆收购。
They completed the buyout of the smaller firm. 他们完成了对这家规模较小公司的收购。


现在完成时:
They have just completed a major buyout. 他们刚刚完成了一次重大收购。
The investors have successfully executed several buyouts this year. 投资者今年成功地进行了多次收购。


过去完成时:
Before the merger, the company had already planned a buyout. 在合并之前，该公司已经计划了一次收购。
The shareholders had considered a buyout before accepting the merger offer. 在接受合并要约之前，股东们曾考虑过收购。


将来时:
The firm will likely pursue several buyouts next year. 该公司明年可能会进行几次收购。
They will announce the details of the buyout soon. 他们很快就会宣布收购的细节。


虚拟语气:
If they had more capital, they would attempt a larger buyout. 如果他们有更多资金，他们会尝试一次更大的收购。
I wish we could have afforded the buyout. 我希望我们能够负担得起这次收购。


被动语态:
The company was bought out by a larger corporation. 这家公司被一家更大的公司收购了。
The buyout is being finalized this week. 收购将于本周完成。


疑问句:
Is this a hostile buyout or a friendly one? 这是敌意收购还是友好收购？
Will the buyout impact the employees' jobs? 收购会影响员工的工作吗？


否定句:
The buyout did not go through as planned. 收购没有按计划进行。
They decided against the buyout due to unforeseen circumstances. 由于不可预见的情况，他们放弃了收购。
