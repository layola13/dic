preconsumptions:/ˌpriːkənˈsʌmpʃənz/| n. |预先消费；预先假设|The act of consuming something before it is officially available or before one has the right to do so;  assumptions made in advance.  It often implies an assumption about future needs or actions. |例句：The preconsumptions of resources led to a shortage later in the project.  预先消耗资源导致项目后期短缺。

The company's preconsumptions about market demand proved inaccurate. 公司对市场需求的预先假设被证明是不准确的。|近义词：anticipation, assumption, presupposition |反义词：postconsumption, reality, fact |名词
