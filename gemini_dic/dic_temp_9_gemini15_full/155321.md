pharaonic: /ˌfærəˈɒnɪk/ | adj. | 法老的；古埃及的 | Relating to the pharaohs or ancient Egypt.  The word evokes a sense of grandeur, power, and ancient civilization.  It is often used to describe things that are monumental, impressive, or characteristic of ancient Egyptian culture. |

例句：

The pharaonic tombs are marvels of engineering. = 法老陵墓是工程奇迹。

The museum's collection includes many pharaonic artifacts. = 博物馆的收藏包括许多法老时期的文物。


近义词：Egyptian, ancient Egyptian


反义词：modern, contemporary


词源:  Derived from "Pharaoh," the title of the ancient Egyptian rulers.  "Pharaoh" itself has a complex etymology, ultimately deriving from an ancient Egyptian word, likely *pr-ꜥꜣ ("great house").  The "-ic" suffix is added to create an adjectival form.
