instruct:/ɪnˈstrʌkt/| vt.| 指示；命令；教育；指导 | To give someone instructions or directions on what to do.  |例句：The teacher instructed the students to write an essay. 老师指示学生们写一篇论文。|例句：The doctor instructed the patient to take the medicine three times a day. 医生指示病人一天服用三次药。|近义词：direct, order, teach, guide |反义词：mislead, confuse


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher instructs the students daily. = 老师每天都指导学生们。
He instructs his team on the new project. = 他指导他的团队进行新的项目。


一般过去时:
She instructed me to wait outside. = 她指示我在外面等候。
The police officer instructed the driver to pull over. = 警官指示司机靠边停车。


现在完成时:
I have instructed them to proceed with caution. = 我已经指示他们谨慎行事。
She has instructed her assistant on the new procedures. = 她已经指导她的助手新的流程。


过去完成时:
He had instructed his staff before the meeting. = 他在会议之前已经指示了他的员工。
They had instructed the workers to stop the machine. = 他们已指示工人停止机器。


将来时:
I will instruct you when to begin. = 我会指示你何时开始。
She will instruct the class on the new material. = 她将指导班级学习新材料。


虚拟语气:
If I instructed them properly, they would have succeeded. = 如果我正确地指导他们，他们就会成功。
I wish I had instructed them more clearly. = 我希望我当时能更清楚地指导他们。


被动语态:
The students were instructed to complete the assignment. = 学生们被指示完成作业。
The workers were instructed by the supervisor. = 工人们受到了主管的指示。


疑问句:
What did the manual instruct you to do? = 手册指示你做什么？
How did she instruct them to finish the task? = 她如何指示他们完成任务？


否定句:
He didn't instruct them to do it that way. = 他没有指示他们那样做。
She didn't instruct me clearly enough. = 她没有足够清晰地指导我。


PHRASE->
instruct someone to do something = 指示某人做某事
instruct someone in something = 指导某人学习某事
instruct someone on something = 指导某人关于某事
follow instructions = 遵循指示
clear instructions = 清晰的指示
