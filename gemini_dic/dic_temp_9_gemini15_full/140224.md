officiating:/əˈfɪʃieɪtɪŋ/| v.|主持，担任裁判，执行公务|The act of performing a ceremony or function, especially in a religious or official capacity; also, the act of acting as a referee or umpire in a game or sport.|例句：The priest officiated at the wedding ceremony.  牧师主持了婚礼仪式。|例句：The referee officiated fairly despite the pressure. 尽管压力很大，裁判还是公平地执法。|近义词：presiding, conducting, administering, refereeing, umpiring|反义词：spectating, observing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He officiates at many weddings each year. 他每年主持许多婚礼。
She officiates at the local church.  她在当地教堂做礼拜仪式。


一般过去时:
The judge officiated at the trial. 法官主持了审判。
He officiated the game with great skill. 他以精湛的技巧执法比赛。


现在完成时:
He has officiated at hundreds of ceremonies. 他主持过数百场仪式。
She has officiated at several major sporting events. 她主持过几场大型体育赛事。


过去完成时:
The priest had officiated at many weddings before retiring.  这位牧师退休前主持过很多婚礼。
The referee had officiated many games before this controversial match.  这位裁判在这场有争议的比赛之前执法过很多场比赛。


将来时:
He will officiate at my sister's wedding next month. 他下个月将主持我姐姐的婚礼。

虚拟语气:
If he were to officiate, the ceremony would be flawless. 如果他来主持，仪式将会完美无瑕。
I wish he would officiate at our wedding. 我希望他能主持我们的婚礼。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
