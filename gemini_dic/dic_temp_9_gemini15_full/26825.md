caging:/ˈkeɪdʒɪŋ/| n. |关在笼子里；囚禁；限制|The act of confining or keeping something or someone in a cage or other enclosure.  It can also refer to the feeling of being trapped or restricted.|例句：The caging of birds is a controversial practice.  鸟类关笼是一种有争议的做法。

例句：The constant surveillance felt like a form of caging.  持续的监控让人感觉像是一种囚禁。


近义词：confinement, imprisonment, restriction, enclosure

反义词：freedom, liberty, release


词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bird enjoys its caging. = 这只鸟喜欢待在笼子里。(This example is unusual as it implies a positive view of caging, which is rare.)
Caging animals is often criticized. = 关养动物经常受到批评。

过去完成时:

The protesters had protested against the caging of animals for years. = 抗议者多年来一直在抗议关养动物。
