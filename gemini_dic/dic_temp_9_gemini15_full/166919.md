prestigiator:/ˌprɛstɪˈdʒɪeɪtər/| n. |魔术师；江湖骗子|A person who performs magic tricks, especially as a form of entertainment.  The term often carries a connotation of deception or trickery, suggesting that the magician's skill is more about illusion than genuine supernatural power.  |例句：The prestigiator amazed the audience with his incredible card tricks.  魔术师以他令人难以置信的纸牌戏法让观众惊叹不已。

The prestigiator's disappearing act was nothing short of spectacular.  魔术师的消失表演简直令人叹为观止。


近义词：magician, illusionist, conjurer, charlatan |反义词：None directly, but words implying honesty and truthfulness would be antonymic in the context of the deceptive aspects of the word.|词性:名词


词源:  The word "prestigiator" is derived from the Latin word "praestigiae," meaning "illusion," "jugglery," or "trickery."  It entered English through French.
