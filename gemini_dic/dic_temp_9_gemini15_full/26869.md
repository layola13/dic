cajoles:/kəˈdʒoʊlz/| vt.| 哄骗；劝诱；讨好 | To persuade someone to do something by using flattery or gentle persuasion.  |例句：He cajoled her into lending him the money. 他哄骗她借给他钱。

例句：She cajoled him into going to the party. 她哄骗他去参加聚会。


近义词：coax, persuade, wheedle, sweet-talk | 反义词：coerce, force, compel | 动词


词源:  The word "cajole" comes from the Old French word "cajouler," which possibly derives from a Vulgar Latin word meaning "to chatter" or "to babble."  The exact etymology is uncertain.

一般现在时:
He cajoles his friends into playing video games. = 他哄骗他的朋友们玩电子游戏。
She cajoles her boss for a raise. = 她哄骗她的老板给她加薪。

一般过去时:
He cajoled her into believing his lies. = 他哄骗她相信他的谎言。
They cajoled the children into eating their vegetables. = 他们哄骗孩子们吃蔬菜。

现在完成时:
He has cajoled his way into a promotion. = 他通过哄骗获得了晋升。
She has cajoled many people to donate to her charity. = 她已经哄骗许多人向她的慈善机构捐款。

过去完成时:
He had cajoled the information out of his reluctant source. = 他已经从他那不情愿的消息来源处哄骗出了信息。
She had cajoled her parents into letting her go on the trip. = 她已经哄骗她的父母让她去旅行。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he cajoled her enough, she might agree. = 如果他足够哄骗她，她可能会同意。
I wish he hadn't cajoled me into that investment. = 我真希望他没有哄骗我进行那项投资。


被动语态:
He was cajoled into signing the contract. = 他被哄骗签署了合同。
The audience was cajoled into a frenzy of applause. = 观众被哄骗得热烈鼓掌。


疑问句:

否定句:
He didn't cajole her; he simply asked politely. = 他没有哄骗她；他只是礼貌地询问。
She wouldn't be cajoled into giving up her principles. = 她不会被哄骗放弃她的原则。
