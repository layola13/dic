nonlocalizable: /ˌnɒnˈloʊkəlaɪzəbl/ | adj. | 不可本地化的 |  Unable to be localized or adapted for a specific region or language.  This term often applies to software, content, or data that contains elements which cannot be easily translated or modified to suit different cultural contexts. This might be due to hardcoded values, dependencies on specific formats, or reliance on culturally specific assumptions. | Example:  The core algorithm of the software is nonlocalizable, requiring significant re-engineering for international markets.  核心算法是不可本地化的，需要对国际市场进行大量重新设计。|近义词: untranslatable, unadaptable | 反义词: localizable, adaptable |
