man-up: /mæn ʌp/ | v. | 勇敢起来；振作起来；承担责任 | To behave bravely and responsibly, especially in a difficult situation.  It often implies overcoming fear or emotional weakness. The term is considered informal and sometimes considered offensive. |

例句：
He needs to man up and face his problems.  = 他需要勇敢起来面对他的问题。

You need to man up and tell her the truth. = 你需要勇敢地告诉她真相。


近义词：be brave, be courageous, toughen up, grow a pair (informal and potentially offensive), pull yourself together


反义词：give up, quit, surrender, cower,  lose heart


词源:  The phrase is a relatively recent colloquialism, a combination of "man" and "up."  The "up" implies an increase in strength or courage.  Its origins are difficult to pinpoint exactly, but it likely emerged in the latter half of the 20th century reflecting changing societal attitudes towards masculinity.

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:
He had manned up and solved the problem before anyone noticed. = 他在任何人注意到之前就已经勇敢地解决了这个问题。
She had manned up and finished the project ahead of schedule. = 她已经勇敢地提前完成了项目。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态: (Not typically used in passive voice)  The phrase is inherently active.


疑问句:

否定句:
