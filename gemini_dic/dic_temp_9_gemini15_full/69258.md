feather-brained: /ˈfɛðərbreɪnd/ | adj. |  轻浮的；愚蠢的；头脑简单的 |  Describes someone who is frivolous, scatterbrained, and lacking in intelligence or good judgment.  It suggests a lack of seriousness and practicality. | 例句：He's so feather-brained, he forgot his own birthday! 他太轻浮了，竟然忘了自己的生日！


He made a feather-brained decision that cost the company thousands of dollars. 他做了一个轻率的决定，导致公司损失了数千美元。| 近义词：silly, frivolous, flighty, scatterbrained, giddy, airheaded | 反义词：sensible, practical, intelligent, shrewd, wise |  词性：形容词

词源:
The word combines "feather," suggesting lightness and airiness, with "brained," referring to the brain and intellect. The implication is that the person's brain is as light and flighty as a feather, lacking substance and seriousness.
