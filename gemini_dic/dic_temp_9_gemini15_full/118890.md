metatarsals:/ˌmɛtəˈtɑːrsəlz/| n. |跖骨|The metatarsals are the five long bones in the foot that are located between the tarsal bones of the hindfoot and the phalanges of the toes. They are numbered from one to five, starting from the medial side of the foot (the side closest to the big toe).  The metatarsals play a crucial role in weight-bearing, locomotion, and balance.  Injuries to the metatarsals are common, particularly stress fractures and fractures from direct trauma.|The dancer fractured one of her metatarsals during rehearsal.  这位舞者在排练中骨折了一根跖骨。

The metatarsals articulate with the tarsal bones proximally and with the phalanges distally. 跖骨近端与跗骨相连，远端与趾骨相连。|近义词：foot bones (general term)|反义词：N/A
