reappointing:/ˌriːəˈpɔɪntɪŋ/| v.|重新任命|The act of appointing someone to a position again.  It implies a previous appointment to the same role.|例句：The board voted to reappoint the CEO for another five years.  董事会投票决定再次任命首席执行官五年。

例句：After a successful first term, the mayor was reappointed unanimously.  在成功的第一任期之后，市长获得了一致的再次任命。


近义词：re-electing, reinstating, reappointing


反义词：dismissing, removing, firing


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reappoint the judge annually. = 他们每年重新任命这位法官。
The company reappoints its CEO every three years. = 公司每三年重新任命一次首席执行官。

一般过去时:
The president reappointed the ambassador last week. = 总统上周重新任命了大使。
The committee reappointed her to the position. = 委员会再次任命她担任这个职位。

现在完成时:
The school has reappointed Mr. Smith as principal. = 学校已经再次任命史密斯先生为校长。
They have reappointed the same team for the project. = 他们已经为该项目重新任命了同一个团队。

过去完成时:
The board had reappointed him before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，董事会已经再次任命了他。
Before the new evidence emerged, they had reappointed the suspect as head of security. = 在新的证据出现之前，他们已经再次任命了嫌疑人为安全主管。

将来时:
They will reappoint the head coach next month. = 他们下个月将重新任命主教练。
The company will likely reappoint her to the board. = 公司很可能会再次任命她进入董事会。

虚拟语气:
If they had more evidence, they would reappoint him. = 如果他们有更多证据，他们就会再次任命他。
I wish they would reappoint her to the committee. = 我希望他们能再次任命她进入委员会。

被动语态:
The director was reappointed by the board. = 主任被董事会重新任命。
He was reappointed despite the controversy. = 尽管存在争议，他还是被重新任命了。

疑问句:

Was she reappointed after the investigation? = 调查后她被重新任命了吗？

否定句:
They did not reappoint him to the committee. = 他们没有再次任命他进入委员会。
The company will not reappoint the failing CEO. = 公司不会重新任命表现不佳的首席执行官。
