impetus:/ˈɪmpɪtəs/| n. | 推动力；刺激；冲动 |  Impetus is the force or energy with which a body moves. It can also refer to something that makes something happen or happen faster.  | The recent economic downturn gave impetus to calls for reform. 近期经济衰退推动了改革呼声。

The discovery of gold provided the impetus for the westward expansion. 黄金的发现为西进扩张提供了动力。


近义词：incentive, stimulus, motivation, impulse

反义词：hindrance, obstacle, impediment


词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new policy provides impetus for economic growth. = 新政策为经济增长提供了动力。
This project needs a strong impetus to succeed. = 这个项目需要强大的推动力才能成功。


一般过去时:
The invention of the printing press gave impetus to the Renaissance. = 印刷术的发明推动了文艺复兴。
His anger gave him the impetus to act rashly. = 他的愤怒促使他鲁莽行事。


现在完成时:
The rising cost of living has given impetus to calls for higher wages. = 生活成本的上升推动了提高工资的呼声。
Recent events have given impetus to the debate on climate change. = 最近的事件推动了关于气候变化的辩论。


过去完成时:
The previous success had given them the impetus to try again. = 此前的成功给了他们再次尝试的动力。
His early failures had given him the impetus to work harder. = 他早期的失败给了他更加努力工作的动力。


将来时:
The upcoming election will give impetus to political campaigning. = 即将到来的选举将推动政治竞选。
Technological advancements will give impetus to further innovation. = 技术进步将推动进一步创新。


虚拟语气:
If we had more impetus, we could finish the project earlier. = 如果我们有更大的动力，我们就能早点完成项目。
If only I had the impetus to start exercising! = 我多么希望自己有开始锻炼的动力啊！


被动语态:
The project was given impetus by a large grant. = 该项目因获得一笔巨额拨款而获得了动力。
The reform was spurred by an impetus from the public. = 这项改革受到了公众的推动。


疑问句:
What is the main impetus behind this movement? = 推动这一运动的主要动力是什么？
What gave impetus to the development of this technology? = 什么推动了这项技术的发展？


否定句:
Lack of funding provided no impetus for further research. = 缺乏资金并没有为进一步的研究提供动力。
The absence of support gave no impetus to the project. = 缺乏支持并没有推动这个项目。

词源:
The word "impetus" comes from the Latin word "impetus," meaning "attack," "onset," or "violent force."  It entered English in the 14th century.
