clerisy:/ˈklɛrəsi/| n. |知识分子阶层；文化精英；学者阶层|The body of learned men and women in a society, especially those who are influential in shaping its cultural and intellectual life.  They are often seen as a separate class or group, distinct from the general population. The term often carries a connotation of intellectual elitism and sometimes a sense of detachment from practical concerns. |The French Revolution saw a concerted attack on the established clerisy, whose privileges and influence were seen as obstacles to progress.|近义词：intelligentsia, elite, learned class |反义词：masses, common people, laity
