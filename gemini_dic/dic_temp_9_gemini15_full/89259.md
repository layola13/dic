hominoid:/ˈhɒmɪnɔɪd/ /ˈhɑːmɪnɔɪd/ | n. |类人猿；人猿总科动物 | A hominoid is any member of the superfamily Hominoidea, which includes humans, chimpanzees, gorillas, orangutans, and gibbons.  They are characterized by their relatively large brain size, lack of a tail, and specialized adaptations for brachiation (arm swinging). | Example:  Orangutans are fascinating hominoids known for their arboreal lifestyle.  =  猩猩是令人着迷的类人猿，以其树栖生活方式而闻名。

The great apes (gorillas, chimpanzees, orangutans) are all hominoids. = 大猩猩、黑猩猩、猩猩都是类人猿。


近义词：ape (in some contexts), primate


反义词：There isn't a direct antonym, but one could contrast hominoids with other primates like monkeys or lemurs.


词性:名词
