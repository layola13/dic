plutoid:/ˈpluːtɔɪd/| n. | 类冥天体 | A plutoid is a celestial body that is in a direct orbit around the Sun, is massive enough for its own gravity to have overcome rigid body forces so that it assumes a hydrostatic equilibrium (nearly round) shape, and has not cleared the neighborhood around its orbit.  | Example: Pluto is a well-known example of a plutoid.  冥王星就是一个众所周知的类冥天体。|近义词：dwarf planet (矮行星) |反义词：planet (行星)
