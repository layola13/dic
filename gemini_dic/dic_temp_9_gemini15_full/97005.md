infarction:/ɪnˈfɑːkʃən/| n. |梗塞；(尤指)心肌梗塞|The sudden blockage of an artery, typically by a blood clot or air bubble, resulting in a lack of blood supply to the affected tissue.  This often leads to tissue death (necrosis). Infarction is most commonly associated with heart attacks (myocardial infarction), but can occur in other organs as well.|Example:  He suffered a myocardial infarction, commonly known as a heart attack. 他遭受了心肌梗塞，俗称心脏病发作。

The blockage of blood supply to an area of the brain resulted in a cerebral infarction. 脑部某区域血液供应中断导致脑梗塞。|近义词：obstruction, blockage, occlusion |反义词：perfusion, reperfusion
