mates:/meɪts/| n.|伙伴；朋友；同事|Companions; friends; colleagues.  The word refers to a group of people who are close friends or who share a common interest or activity.|例句：He and his mates went fishing.他和他的伙伴们去钓鱼了。|近义词：friends, companions, buddies, colleagues|反义词：enemies, adversaries


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My mates play football every Saturday. = 我的伙伴们每个星期六都踢足球。
These mates help each other with their projects. = 这些伙伴互相帮助完成项目。

一般过去时:
My mates went to the pub last night. = 我的伙伴们昨晚去了酒吧。
We had a great time with our mates at the party. = 我们在派对上和伙伴们玩得很开心。

现在完成时:
I've known my mates for many years. = 我认识我的伙伴们很多年了。
My mates have just finished their exams. = 我的伙伴们刚刚完成考试。

过去完成时:
I had already met his mates before the party. = 在派对之前我已经见过他的伙伴们了。
They had known each other for years before they started working together. = 在他们开始一起工作之前，他们已经认识很多年了。

将来时:
My mates will be here soon. = 我的伙伴们很快就会到。
We will meet our mates at the airport tomorrow. = 我们明天将在机场与伙伴们会面。

虚拟语气:
If I had more mates, I wouldn't feel so lonely. = 如果我有更多伙伴，我就不会感到那么孤独。
I wish my mates were here with me right now. = 我希望我的伙伴们现在能和我在一起。

被动语态:
The project was completed by my mates and me. = 这个项目由我和我的伙伴们完成。
The team was formed by mates with shared interests. = 这个团队是由志同道合的伙伴们组成的。

疑问句:
Are these your mates? = 这些是你的伙伴们吗？
Do you still hang out with your old mates? = 你还和以前的伙伴们一起出去玩吗？

否定句:
I don't have many mates. = 我没有很多伙伴。
He didn't invite his mates to the wedding. = 他没有邀请他的伙伴们参加婚礼。
