/fɔːrtiˈɔːri/ | adv. | 更不必说，何况 |  From the Latin phrase "a fortiori," meaning "from the stronger reason," this adverb is used to indicate that if something is true in a particular case, then it is even more likely to be true in a similar but stronger or more extreme case. It implies a stronger argument based on a previously established premise. |  If she can't lift that small box, then a fortiori she can't lift the larger one.  如果她搬不动那个小箱子，那么她更搬不动那个大箱子了。|  moreover, especially, all the more, much less |  n/a |  adv.
