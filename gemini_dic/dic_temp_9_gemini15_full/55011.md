disputatious:/ˌdɪspjuˈteɪʃəs/| adj.| 好争论的；爱争辩的；好辩的 |  Given to dispute or argument; contentious.  |  He's known for his disputatious nature and often gets into heated debates. 他以好争论的性格而闻名，经常陷入激烈的辩论。  |近义词：argumentative, contentious, combative, litigious |反义词：conciliatory, agreeable, peaceful

一般现在时:
He is disputatious in his approach to negotiations. = 他在谈判中喜欢争论。
She is always disputatious with her colleagues. = 她总是和同事们争论不休。


一般过去时:

They were disputatious about the terms of the contract. = 他们就合同条款争论不休。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

She had been disputatious throughout the discussions, making little progress. = 在整个讨论过程中，她一直好争论，进展甚微。

虚拟语气:
