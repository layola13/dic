gurnard:/ˈɡɜːrnɑːrd/| n. |红娘鱼，鼠头鱼 |A gurnard is a type of fish with large, bony head and large pectoral fins.  They are found in the Atlantic and Mediterranean seas.  Many species are edible. |例句：The fisherman caught a large red gurnard.  渔夫钓到了一条大红娘鱼。

例句：We had gurnard for dinner, it was delicious. 我们晚饭吃了红娘鱼，味道很好。


近义词：sea robin (though not a direct synonym, it refers to a similar-looking fish)


反义词：None directly applicable.


词性：名词


词源：The word "gurnard" comes from the Old French word "gournard," which is believed to be of imitative origin, possibly onomatopoeic, reflecting the sound the fish makes.  The ultimate etymology is uncertain, but the connection to sounds associated with the fish is likely.
