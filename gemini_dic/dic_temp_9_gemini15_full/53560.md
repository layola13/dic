disbase: /dɪsˈbeɪs/ | v. | 贬低；破坏；使丢脸 | To deprive (something) of its base or foundation; to degrade or debase; to dishonor or disgrace.  | 例句：The constant criticism disbased his confidence.  持续的批评贬低了他的自信。 | 例句：His actions disbased the reputation of the entire company.  他的行为损害了整个公司的声誉。 | 近义词：degrade, devalue, dishonor, discredit | 反义词：elevate, enhance, honor, uphold | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disbases his opponent's arguments in the debate. = 他在辩论中贬低了对手的论点。
The company's unethical practices disbase its brand image. = 该公司的违反道德行为损害了其品牌形象。

一般过去时:
The scandal disbased the politician's career. = 这场丑闻毁掉了这位政治家的职业生涯。
The harsh words disbased her self-esteem. = 那些刻薄的话语贬低了她的自尊。


现在完成时:
His actions have disbased the family's honor. = 他的行为已经玷污了家族的荣誉。
Repeated failures have disbased his confidence in himself. = 反复的失败已经贬低了他对自己的信心。


过去完成时:
They had disbased the currency before the economic crisis. = 他们在经济危机之前已经贬低了货币。
The rumors had disbased her reputation before the truth emerged. = 在真相大白之前，谣言已经损害了她的名誉。


将来时:
His reckless behavior will disbase his chances of success. = 他鲁莽的行为将会毁掉他成功的几率。
The new policy will likely disbase the value of their investments. = 新政策可能会贬低他们投资的价值。


虚拟语气:
If he had not acted so recklessly, he would not have disbased his reputation. = 如果他不那么鲁莽行事，他就不会毁掉自己的名誉。
I wish they hadn't disbased the sacred tradition. = 我真希望他们没有贬低神圣的传统。


被动语态:
His arguments were disbased by the opposing team. = 他的论点被对方团队驳斥了。
The value of the currency was disbased by inflation. = 通货膨胀贬低了货币的价值。


疑问句:

How did the company disbase its reputation? = 公司是如何损害其声誉的？


否定句:
He did not disbase his commitment to the project. = 他并没有放弃他对项目的承诺。
The evidence did not disbase their claims. = 证据并没有驳斥他们的说法。
