quipping:/ˈkwɪpɪŋ/| v.|俏皮地说；挖苦；打趣|The act of making witty or sarcastic remarks; jesting.|例句：He spent the evening quipping with his friends. 他整个晚上都在和朋友们开玩笑。  例句：Her quipping kept the audience entertained. 她的俏皮话逗乐了观众。|近义词：joking, jesting, teasing|反义词：seriousness, solemnity


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He quips about the weather every morning. = 他每天早上都拿天气开玩笑。
She quips with her colleagues during lunch breaks. = 她在午餐休息时间和同事们开玩笑。

一般过去时:
He quipped that the meeting was a waste of time. = 他俏皮地说这次会议浪费时间。
She quipped about his new haircut. = 她对他的新发型开玩笑。

现在完成时:
He has quipped about the same thing several times. = 他已经多次说过同样的事情了（开玩笑）。
She has quipped so much today that everyone is laughing. = 她今天说了很多笑话，大家都笑了起来。

过去完成时:
He had quipped about the situation before anyone else dared. = 在其他人敢之前，他已经对这种情况开过玩笑了。
She had quipped something similar earlier in the conversation. = 在之前的谈话中，她说过类似的话。


将来时:
He will quip about the boss's new suit. = 他将会拿老板的新西装开玩笑。
She will quip about his clumsy dance moves.  = 她将会拿他笨拙的舞姿开玩笑。

虚拟语气:
If he were here, he would quip about the situation. = 如果他在这儿，他会对这种情况开玩笑。
I wish I had the courage to quip back at him. = 我真希望我有勇气回敬他一句俏皮话。

被动语态:
His jokes were often quipped at the expense of others. = 他的笑话经常以取笑他人为代价。
The situation was quipped about relentlessly by the media. = 媒体对这种情况无情地进行调侃。

疑问句:
Is he always quipping like that? = 他总是那样开玩笑吗？
Was she quipping when she said that? = 她那样说的时候是在开玩笑吗？

否定句:
He didn't quip at all during the entire meeting. = 在整个会议期间，他一句话也没开玩笑。
She didn't quip, but simply nodded in agreement. = 她没有开玩笑，只是简单地点了点头表示同意。
