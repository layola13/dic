redefine:/ˌriːˈfaɪn/ | vt. |重新定义；重新解释 | To define something again or in a new way.  This often involves changing the existing understanding or boundaries of a concept, term, or object. | 例句：The company redefined its brand image to attract a younger audience.  公司重新定义了其品牌形象以吸引更年轻的受众。

例句：The discovery of new evidence forced scientists to redefine their theory. 新证据的发现迫使科学家们重新定义他们的理论。


近义词：revise, reinterpret, reformulate, reconceptualize | 反义词：maintain, uphold, preserve (in the sense of not changing the definition) | 词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They redefine their goals every year. = 他们每年都会重新定义他们的目标。
We redefine success in our own terms. = 我们用自己的方式重新定义成功。

一般过去时:
The court redefined the law on privacy. = 法院重新定义了隐私权法。
Scientists redefined the boundaries of the known universe. = 科学家们重新定义了已知宇宙的边界。

现在完成时:
They have redefined their marketing strategy. = 他们已经重新定义了他们的营销策略。
The company has redefined its corporate culture. = 公司已经重新定义了其企业文化。

过去完成时:
They had redefined their priorities before the crisis hit. = 在危机来临之前，他们已经重新定义了他们的优先事项。
The government had redefined its economic policies by then. = 到那时，政府已经重新定义了其经济政策。

将来时:

The new technology will redefine how we communicate. = 新技术将重新定义我们的沟通方式。

虚拟语气:
If we redefined our approach, we might succeed. = 如果我们重新定义我们的方法，我们可能会成功。
I wish they had redefined their objectives earlier. = 我希望他们早点重新定义目标。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
