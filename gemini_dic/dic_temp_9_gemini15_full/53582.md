disbranch:/dɪsˈbrændʃ/| vt.|  除去分支；切断分支 | To remove a branch or branches from something, especially a tree or organization.  |例句：The gardener disbranched the overgrown rose bush. 园丁修剪了过度生长的玫瑰丛。
例句：The company decided to disbranch its failing overseas operations. 公司决定砍掉其在海外亏损的业务。|近义词：prune, trim, lop, sever|反义词：branch, grow


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The gardener disbranches the rose bush every spring. = 园丁每年春天都修剪玫瑰丛。
They disbranch their unsuccessful projects regularly. = 他们定期砍掉不成功的项目。

一般过去时:
He disbranched the apple tree last autumn. = 他去年秋天修剪了苹果树。
