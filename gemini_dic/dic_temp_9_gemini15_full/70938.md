fired:/faɪərd/| vt. adj. n.|解雇；点燃；发射；|激动的；兴奋的；被解雇的；|To dismiss someone from their job. To ignite something. To shoot a projectile.|例句：He was fired for incompetence. 他因不称职而被解雇。The rocket was fired into space. 火箭发射到太空。|近义词：dismiss, discharge, ignite, launch, shoot|反义词：hire, employ, extinguish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fires the employees who are late. = 他解雇迟到的员工。
She fires up the engine every morning. = 她每天早上启动引擎。


一般过去时:
They fired him last week. = 他们上周解雇了他。
He fired a shot into the air. = 他朝空中开了一枪。


现在完成时:
They have fired several employees this year. = 今年他们已经解雇了几名员工。
She has fired the oven and the pizza is almost ready. = 她已经把烤箱预热好了，披萨快好了。


过去完成时:
He had fired the gun before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，他已经开枪了。
She had fired the kiln before going home. = 她回家之前已经把窑烧好了。


将来时:
They will fire him if he continues to be late. = 如果他继续迟到，他们将会解雇他。
He will fire the cannon at noon. = 他将在中午发射大炮。


虚拟语气:
If I were the boss, I would fire him immediately. = 如果我是老板，我会立即解雇他。
If I had known better, I wouldn't have fired that shot. = 如果我早知道，我就不会开那枪了。


被动语态:
He was fired for his misconduct. = 他因行为不端而被解雇。
The cannon was fired to celebrate the victory. = 大炮被发射以庆祝胜利。


疑问句:
Were you fired from your last job? = 你被上一份工作解雇了吗？
Was the gun fired intentionally? = 这枪是故意开的吗？


否定句:
They did not fire him despite his mistakes. = 尽管他犯了错误，他们也没有解雇他。
The engine didn't fire up this morning. = 引擎今天早上没有启动。
