pseudorepublican:/ˌsjuːdoʊrɪˈpʌblɪkən/| adj. |伪共和主义的；假共和主义的 |Appearing to support republicanism but actually lacking its genuine principles or characteristics.  A pseudorepublican government might hold elections but lack genuine democratic freedoms or operate under authoritarian control. |例句：The regime, though holding elections, was widely considered a pseudorepublican dictatorship. 这个政权虽然举行选举，却被普遍认为是一个伪共和主义的独裁统治。


例句：His actions, while outwardly supporting the party platform, revealed a pseudorepublican stance motivated by self-interest. 他的行为表面上支持党的纲领，但却暴露出一种出于私利的伪共和主义立场。


近义词：quasi-republican, sham-republican, facade-republican |反义词：genuinely republican, truly democratic


词源:  The word is a combination of "pseudo-" (meaning false or pretended) and "republican" (relating to a republic or its principles).  The prefix "pseudo-" comes from the Greek word "pseudēs," meaning "false" or "lying."  "Republican" originates from the Latin "res publica," meaning "public thing" or "commonwealth."
