etruscans:/ɪˈtrʌskənz/| n.|伊特鲁里亚人；伊特鲁里亚语|The Etruscans were a people who inhabited Etruria (modern Tuscany) in ancient Italy.  Their civilization flourished from the 8th to the 2nd century BC, leaving behind a rich legacy of art, architecture, and culture.  They were known for their sophisticated metalworking, distinctive funerary practices, and unique alphabet. Their language remains largely undeciphered, posing a significant linguistic puzzle.|The Etruscan tombs contained elaborate frescoes and artifacts.|近义词：ancient Italians|反义词：无
