词源:

The word "nontransportation" is a compound word formed by combining the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") with the noun "transportation".  "Transportation" itself comes from the Old French word "transportacion," derived from the Latin word "transportare," meaning "to carry across."  Therefore, "nontransportation" literally means "not the carrying across" or "not related to carrying or moving things."
