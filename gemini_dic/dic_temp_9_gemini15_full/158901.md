plunders:/ˈplʌndərz/| v.|掠夺；抢劫；洗劫|to steal goods violently from a place, especially during a war or other disorder; to take something valuable from someone by force|例句：The invading army plundered the city, leaving it in ruins.  入侵的军队洗劫了这座城市，把它夷为平地。

例句：Pirates plundered the merchant ship and stole all its cargo. 海盗洗劫了商船，偷走了所有货物。


近义词：loot, ransack, rob, despoil

反义词：enrich, protect, preserve

词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The army plunders villages regularly.  军队经常洗劫村庄。
He plunders the internet for information. 他在互联网上搜集信息。


一般过去时:
The Vikings plundered the coast. 维京人掠夺了海岸。
The thieves plundered the museum. 窃贼洗劫了博物馆。


现在完成时:
The rebels have plundered many towns. 反叛者已经洗劫了许多城镇。
They have plundered the national treasury. 他们已经洗劫了国库。


过去完成时:
The city had been plundered before the army arrived. 在军队到达之前，这座城市已经被洗劫一空了。
The villagers had plundered the abandoned houses. 村民们已经洗劫了那些废弃的房屋。


将来时:
The invaders will plunder the countryside. 侵略者将掠夺乡村。

虚拟语气:
