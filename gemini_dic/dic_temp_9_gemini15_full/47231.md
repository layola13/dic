dazzled: /ˈdæzəld/ | vt. adj. |使眼花缭乱；使惊奇；耀眼的；眼花缭乱的 |To impress or overwhelm with brilliance or splendor; to be greatly impressed or amazed.  |例句：The fireworks dazzled the crowd. 烟花让人群眼花缭乱。 The dazzling performance left the audience speechless.  令人眼花缭乱的表演让观众说不出话来。 |近义词： amazed, astonished, impressed, blinded |反义词： unimpressed, bored, uninspired |形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bright lights dazzle me. = 耀眼的光线让我眼花缭乱。
The magician's tricks dazzle the audience. = 魔术师的戏法让观众眼花缭乱。

一般过去时:
The sunset dazzled her. = 日落让她眼花缭乱。
He was dazzled by her beauty. = 他被她的美貌迷住了。

现在完成时:
The new technology has dazzled the world. = 新技术让全世界为之惊叹。
I have been dazzled by the possibilities. = 我被这些可能性迷住了。

过去完成时:
I had been dazzled by his charm before I realised his true nature. = 在我意识到他本性之前，我已经被他迷住了。
She had been dazzled by the promise of wealth. = 她曾被财富的承诺所迷惑。


将来时:
The new product will dazzle consumers. = 新产品将使消费者眼花缭乱。
The upcoming show will dazzle the audience with its special effects. = 即将来临的演出将以其特效让观众眼花缭乱。


被动语态:
We were dazzled by the beauty of the scenery. = 我们被风景的美景所震撼。
The audience was dazzled by the singer's performance. = 观众被歌手的表演所倾倒。

疑问句:
Were you dazzled by the fireworks display? = 你被烟火表演迷住了吗？
Is he easily dazzled by superficial things? = 他容易被表面现象迷惑吗？

否定句:
The presentation didn't dazzle the investors. = 这次演示并没有打动投资者。
The movie didn't dazzle me as much as I expected. = 这部电影并没有像我预期的那样让我惊艳。


PHRASE->
dazzle with: 以...使眼花缭乱
dazzle someone: 使某人眼花缭乱
be dazzled by: 被...迷住
dazzling beauty: 令人惊艳的美貌
a dazzling display: 令人眼花缭乱的展示


词源:
源自古法语 *dazeller，意思是“闪耀，炫耀”，可能与古法语 *dazel  “眩晕”有关，最终源自日耳曼语系。
