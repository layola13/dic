deforming:/dɪˈfɔːrmɪŋ/ | v. | 变形；使变形 |  The process of changing the shape or form of something, often due to external forces or internal stresses.  It can refer to a gradual or sudden change. | 例句：The intense heat deformed the metal.  = 剧烈的热量使金属变形。

The earthquake deformed the landscape. = 地震改变了地貌。


近义词：distorting, warping, misshaping, contorting | 反义词：shaping, forming, molding


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The heat is deforming the plastic. = 热量正在使塑料变形。
The pressure deforms the rock. = 压力使岩石变形。


一般过去时:
The accident deformed the car's frame. = 事故使汽车的框架变形。
The sculptor deformed the clay to create a unique shape. = 雕塑家改变了粘土的形状以创造一个独特的形状。


现在完成时:
The constant pressure has deformed the pipeline. = 持续的压力使管道变形了。
Years of sun exposure have deformed the paintwork. = 多年的日晒使油漆变形了。


过去完成时:
The bridge had deformed significantly before the engineers noticed. = 在工程师们注意到之前，桥梁已经严重变形了。
The building had deformed subtly over many decades. = 这座建筑物在几十年里已经微妙地变形了。


将来时:
The intense cold will deform the concrete. = 强烈的寒冷会使混凝土变形。
Continued weight will deform the structure. = 持续的重量会使结构变形。


虚拟语气:
If the temperature were higher, the material would deform more easily. = 如果温度更高，材料会更容易变形。
I wish the metal hadn't deformed so badly. = 我希望金属没有变形得这么严重。


被动语态:
The metal was deformed by the impact. = 金属因撞击而变形。
The structure is being deformed by the wind. = 结构正在被风力变形。


疑问句:
Has the material deformed at all? = 材料是否变形了？
Is the pressure sufficient to deform the object? = 压力是否足以使物体变形？


否定句:
The wood didn't deform under the load. = 木材在负载下没有变形。
The metal did not deform significantly during the test. = 金属在测试期间没有发生明显的变形。
