mislead:/ˌmɪsˈliːd/ | vt. | 误导；使误信 | To guide or direct wrongly; to lead astray.  |例句：The advertisement misled consumers into believing the product was more effective than it actually was.  广告误导消费者相信该产品比实际效果更好。

The politician's statements misled the public about his true intentions. 这位政治家的声明误导了公众对其真实意图的了解。|近义词：deceive, delude, confuse,  misinform |反义词：guide, enlighten, inform, clarify |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often misleads his friends with false information. = 他经常用虚假信息误导他的朋友。
The advertisement misleads consumers about the product's quality. = 这则广告误导消费者对产品质量的认知。


一般过去时:
The map misled us, and we got lost. = 地图误导了我们，我们迷路了。
His words misled me into believing him. = 他的话让我误以为他可信。


现在完成时:
They have misled the investors with their fraudulent scheme. = 他们用欺诈计划误导了投资者。
The company has been misled by false reports. = 这家公司被虚假报告误导了。


过去完成时:
I had been misled by the rumors before I learned the truth. = 在我了解真相之前，我已经受到了谣言的误导。
The detective realized he had been misled by the suspect. = 侦探意识到自己被嫌疑人误导了。


将来时:
The misleading campaign will mislead many voters. = 这场误导性的竞选活动将会误导许多选民。
I fear that his actions will mislead the children. = 我担心他的行为会误导孩子们。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't been misled, I would have made a different decision. = 如果我没有被误导，我会做出不同的决定。
I wish I hadn't been misled by his promises. = 我真希望我没有被他那些诺言所误导。


被动语态:
Many people were misled by the fake news. = 许多人被假新闻误导了。
The public was misled by the government's propaganda. = 公众被政府的宣传误导了。


疑问句:
Were you misled by the instructions? = 你被说明书误导了吗？
Has the company been misled by fraudulent activities? = 这家公司是被欺诈活动误导了吗？


否定句:
The guide did not mislead us; we found our way easily. = 向导没有误导我们；我们很容易就找到了路。
He tried to mislead me, but I saw through his lies. = 他试图误导我，但我识破了他的谎言。


PHRASE->
mislead someone into doing something = 诱使某人做某事
be misled by something = 被某事误导
mislead someone about something = 在某事上误导某人
intentionally mislead = 故意误导
grossly mislead = 严重误导


词源:
mis- (wrongly) + lead (to guide)
