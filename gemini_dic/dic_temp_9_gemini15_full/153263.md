penalise:/ˈpiːnəlaɪz/|/ˈpiːnəlaɪz/| vt.|惩罚；处罚|To punish or inflict a penalty on someone or something for a fault, mistake, or crime.|The team was penalised for a foul.  球队因为犯规被罚。

The driver was penalised for speeding. 司机因超速而受罚。|punish, sanction, fine|reward, pardon|动词
USAGE->
一般现在时:
They penalise late submissions. 他们惩罚迟交作业。
The referee penalises fouls consistently. 裁判始终如一地惩罚犯规。

一般过去时:
The judge penalised the defendant for perjury. 法官因伪证罪惩罚被告。
The teacher penalised him for cheating on the exam. 老师因考试作弊而惩罚他。

现在完成时:
The team has been penalised several times this season. 这个赛季球队已经被罚款好几次了。
She has been penalised for her irresponsible behaviour. 她因不负责任的行为而受到惩罚。

过去完成时:
The player had been penalised before the final whistle. 在终场哨响之前，这位球员已经被罚了。
They had been penalised heavily for their breach of contract. 他们因违反合同而受到严厉的处罚。

将来时:
The company will penalise employees for excessive absenteeism. 公司将惩罚员工旷工过多。
He will be penalised if he breaks the rules again. 如果他再次违反规定，他将受到处罚。

虚拟语气:
If they had followed the rules, they wouldn't have been penalised. 如果他们遵守规则，就不会受到惩罚。
I wish I hadn't been penalised for that mistake. 我真希望我没有因为那个错误而受到惩罚。

被动语态:
Late submissions are penalised. 迟交作业将受到惩罚。
The player was penalised for unsportsmanlike conduct. 该球员因不文明行为而受到处罚。

疑问句:
Will they penalise us for being late? 他们会因为我们迟到而惩罚我们吗？
Are students penalised for missing deadlines? 学生因错过截止日期而受到惩罚吗？

否定句:
The company does not penalise employees for minor infractions. 公司不会因为轻微违规而惩罚员工。
They were not penalised for their actions. 他们的行为没有受到惩罚。

词源:
From Latin *poena (“penalty, punishment”) +‎ -al +‎ -ise.  源于拉丁语 poena（惩罚）+‎ -al +‎ -ise.
