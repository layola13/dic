gaudier:/ˈɡɔːdiər/| adj. |过于艳丽的；俗丽的；炫耀的 |More showy or flamboyant than is considered tasteful; excessively ornate.  |The gaudier colors of the carnival costumes contrasted sharply with the muted tones of the city buildings.|近义词：flashy, ostentatious, flamboyant, showy |反义词：subtle, understated, muted, plain


USAGE->

The painting is gaudier than I expected. = 这幅画比我预期的更艳丽。
Her dress is too gaudier for the occasion. = 她的衣服对于这个场合来说过于艳丽。


一般过去时:
The decorations were gaudier last year. = 去年的装饰更艳丽。
His style was much gaudier in his youth. = 他年轻时的风格要艳丽得多。


现在完成时:
The city has become gaudier over the years. = 多年来，这座城市变得越来越艳丽。
Their taste has become gaudier since they moved. = 他们搬家后品味变得更俗气了。

否定句:

词源:
From French *gaudy*, from Old French *gaude*, from Medieval Latin *gaudium* ("joy").  The sense shifted from "bright" (associated with joy) to "showy and tasteless".
