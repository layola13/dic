close-set: /ˈkloʊs sɛt/ | adj. | 靠得很近的，密集的 |  Describes things that are placed or positioned very near to each other, often implying a lack of space between them.  This can refer to physical objects or abstract concepts. | 例句：The houses in the close-set neighborhood were almost touching.  这个紧密相邻的社区里的房子几乎挨在一起。

The close-set eyes of the owl gave it a wise appearance. 猫头鹰紧凑的眼睛使它看起来很聪明。| 近义词：compact, crowded, dense, clustered, tight | 反义词：spaced out, widely separated, dispersed, scattered |  词性：形容词
