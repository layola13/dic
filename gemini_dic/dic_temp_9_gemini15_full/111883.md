logography:/ləˈɡɑːɡrəfi/ | n. | 语素文字；表意文字 | The system of writing in which symbols represent words or morphemes rather than sounds.  Logography is a writing system where symbols represent whole words or morphemes, unlike alphabets which represent sounds.  Examples include ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs and Chinese characters. | Example:  Ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs are a well-known example of logography.  古代埃及象形文字是语素文字的一个著名例子。| 近义词：ideography, word-writing | 反义词：alphabetic writing, phonetic writing |  名词
