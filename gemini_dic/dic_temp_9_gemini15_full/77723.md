gemmiparity:/ˌdʒɛmiˈpærəti/| n. |双子胎生；双生繁殖|The production of twins, especially in animals.  This term is used primarily in zoology and biology to describe the reproductive process where two offspring are produced simultaneously from a single pregnancy or reproductive event.|Example:  Gemmiparity is a common reproductive strategy in some species of invertebrates.|近义词：biparental reproduction, twinning|反义词：uniparity (producing a single offspring at a time)
