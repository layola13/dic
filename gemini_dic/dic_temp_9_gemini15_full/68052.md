factualnesses:/ˈfæktʃuəlˌnɛsɪz/ | n. | 事实性；真实性 | The factualnesses of the report were questioned.  报告的事实性受到了质疑。 | 近义词：truthfulness, accuracy, verifiability | 反义词：falsity, inaccuracy, unreliability


词源：

* **factual:**  源自拉丁语 *factum* (something done, deed, fact), 过去分词 *factus*.  表示“事实的”。
* **-ness:**  英语后缀，表示“性质”、“状态”。


英文解析：

Factualnesses refers to the state or quality of being factual; the degree to which something is based on facts and is therefore true or accurate. It emphasizes the plurality of factual aspects or instances.  The word itself is relatively uncommon, often replaced by simpler terms like "accuracy," "truthfulness," or "verifiability."

例句：

The judge carefully considered the factualnesses presented by both sides before making his ruling.  法官在做出裁决之前仔细考虑了双方提出的各项事实。


The multiple factualnesses of the historical account added to its credibility. 历史叙述中的多个事实增加了它的可信度。
