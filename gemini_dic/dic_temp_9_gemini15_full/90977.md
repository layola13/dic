hurrying:/ˈhʌriɪŋ/| v.|匆忙，赶快 |The act of moving or acting quickly.  It often implies a sense of urgency or pressure to complete something quickly.|例句：He was hurrying to catch the train. 他在赶火车。|例句：She hurried through her breakfast. 她匆匆忙忙地吃着早餐。|近义词：rushing, hastening, speeding |反义词：dawdling, delaying, lingering


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is hurrying to work. = 他正在匆忙赶去上班。
They are hurrying to finish the project. = 他们在匆忙完成项目。

一般过去时:
I hurried home after work. = 我下班后匆忙回家了。
She hurried to help her friend. = 她匆忙去帮助她的朋友。

现在完成时:
We have hurried through the meeting. = 我们匆匆忙忙地开了个会。
They have hurried to get everything done on time. = 他们已经匆忙完成了所有事情以赶上时间。

过去完成时:
He had hurried to the airport, but still missed his flight. = 他匆忙赶到了机场，但还是错过了航班。
She had hurried to finish the report before the deadline, but it wasn't perfect. = 她匆忙赶在截止日期前完成了报告，但它并不完美。

将来时:
I will hurry to the station. = 我将匆忙赶往车站。
They will hurry to complete the task before the deadline. = 他们将会匆忙完成任务以赶上截止日期。

虚拟语气:
If I had hurried, I would have caught the bus. = 如果我当时快点，我就赶上公交车了。
I wish I hadn't hurried so much; I made a mistake. = 我真希望我没有那么匆忙；我犯了一个错误。

被动语态:
The project was hurried to completion. = 项目被匆忙完成。
The decision was hurried, and it was a mistake. = 决定过于仓促，这是一个错误。

疑问句:
Are you hurrying somewhere? = 你是在赶去什么地方吗？
Why are they hurrying? = 他们为什么这么匆忙？

否定句:

He didn't hurry, so he missed the train. = 他不着急，所以错过了火车。
