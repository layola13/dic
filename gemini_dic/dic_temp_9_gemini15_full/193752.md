self-criticism: /ˌsɛlfˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 自我批评 | The act of criticizing oneself; self-evaluation often involving negative judgments.  It can be a healthy mechanism for personal growth or a destructive pattern leading to low self-esteem. | 例句：He engaged in rigorous self-criticism after the presentation, identifying areas for improvement. 他在演示结束后进行了严格的自我批评，找出需要改进的地方。


例句：Her constant self-criticism prevented her from enjoying her successes. 她不断的自我批评让她无法享受成功的喜悦。


近义词：self-reproach, introspection, self-assessment, self-condemnation


反义词：self-congratulation, self-admiration, self-satisfaction


词性：名词

词源:
The word "self-criticism" is a compound word.  "Self" is an Old English word meaning "own" or "same," while "criticism" comes from the Greek "kritikos," meaning "able to judge."  Therefore, self-criticism literally means "the act of judging oneself."
