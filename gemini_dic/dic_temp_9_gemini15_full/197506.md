sheitan:/ʃeɪˈtɑːn/| n. |撒旦，魔鬼|The word "sheitan" is primarily used in Arabic and related languages to refer to Satan or the Devil, a powerful evil entity. It represents the personification of evil, temptation, and opposition to God.  The term carries strong religious connotations within Islamic and other Abrahamic faith contexts. |例句：In many Islamic traditions, sheitan is believed to whisper temptations into people's ears.  在许多伊斯兰传统中，人们相信撒旦会向人们耳边低语诱惑。|近义词：devil, satan, Iblis (Arabic)|反义词：God, angel, Allah|名词
