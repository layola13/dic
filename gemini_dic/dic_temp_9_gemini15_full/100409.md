intertex:/ˈɪntərˌtɛks/| n.|互文性；文本间的相互关联|The concept of intertextuality refers to the way texts relate to each other.  Intertextuality acknowledges that texts are not created in isolation but are constantly in dialogue with other texts.|例句：The novel's intertextuality with classic fairy tales is evident in its characters and plot.|近义词：interrelation, connection, relationship|反义词：isolation, independence
