redacts:/rɪˈdækts/| vt.|涂黑；删除；编辑；修改|To redact something means to remove parts of it, especially so that it can be published or made available without causing problems.  This often involves removing sensitive or confidential information.|The government redacted sensitive details from the report before releasing it to the public. = 政府在向公众发布报告之前删除了敏感细节。|The editor redacted several passages from the manuscript. = 编辑从手稿中删除了几段文字。|近义词：expunge, censor, delete, edit|反义词：reveal, disclose, publish


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They redact sensitive information from the document. = 他们从文件中删除敏感信息。
The editor redacts the article before publication. = 编辑在发表文章之前对其进行编辑。


一般过去时:
He redacted the names of the witnesses from the report. = 他从报告中删除了证人的姓名。
She redacted the offensive language from the email. = 她从电子邮件中删除了冒犯性语言。


现在完成时:
They have redacted all the confidential details. = 他们已经删除了所有机密细节。
The agency has redacted the report for public release. = 该机构已编辑该报告以供公开发布。


过去完成时:

They had redacted the names to protect their privacy. = 他们已经删除了姓名以保护他们的隐私。
