dearticulate:/diːˈɑːrtɪkjuleɪt/| vt.|使不清楚；使含糊；使表达不清 |To make (something, especially speech) unclear or indistinct.|The speaker's nervousness dearticulated his message.  演讲者的紧张使他的讲话不清楚。|近义词：mumble, slur, garble|反义词：articulate, clarify


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dearticulates his words when he's nervous. = 他紧张的时候说话含糊不清。
The fog dearticulates the distant sounds. = 雾使远处的声响变得模糊不清。


一般过去时:
The strong wind dearticulated her voice. = 大风使她的声音变得模糊不清。
The poor connection dearticulated the phone call. = 糟糕的连接使电话变得含糊不清。


现在完成时:
The years have dearticulated his once-clear speech. = 岁月使他曾经清晰的讲话变得含糊不清。
Stress has dearticulated her ability to express herself clearly. = 压力使她清晰表达自己的能力下降了。


过去完成时:
The excessive alcohol had dearticulated his ability to speak coherently. = 过量的酒精已经使他无法连贯地说话。
The long illness had dearticulated his thoughts, making them difficult to understand. = 长期的疾病使他的想法变得模糊不清，难以理解。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

If she weren't so tired, she wouldn't dearticulate her words. = 如果她不那么累，她就不会说话含糊不清。


被动语态:
