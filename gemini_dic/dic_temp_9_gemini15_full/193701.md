self-confessedly:/ˌsɛlf kənˈfɛstɪdli/ | adv. | 自认地，自己承认地 |  The adverb "self-confessedly" means in a way that is openly admitted by oneself. It implies a degree of self-awareness and possibly even a lack of defensiveness about the admission.  | Example: He self-confessedly enjoyed the thrill of the illegal activity. 他自认享受非法活动的刺激。

Example: She self-confessedly struggled with procrastination. 她自认与拖延症作斗争。


近义词: openly, frankly, admittedly, avowedly

反义词: secretly, clandestinely, covertly


词源:

* **self-**:  from Old English *self, *sylf, meaning "self, same."
* **confessed**: from the verb "confess,"  originating from Old French *confesser, from Latin *confiteri, meaning "to acknowledge, own up to."  This in turn is formed from *com- ("together") + *fateri ("to say, speak").
* **-ly**:  an adverbial suffix, indicating manner or state.


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.  It doesn't have tenses like verbs.  Therefore, examples will show its usage in different sentence structures.
