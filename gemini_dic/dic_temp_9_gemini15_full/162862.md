preceded:/prɪˈsiːdəd/ | v. |在…之前发生；先于 | To come before something or someone in time or order.  |例句：The meeting was preceded by a period of intense negotiation.  会议之前进行了一段时间的紧张谈判。|例句：The storm preceded the arrival of the rescue team. 暴风雨发生在救援队到达之前。|近义词：precede, antecede, forerun |反义词：follow, succeed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The event precedes the festival. = 这个活动在节日之前举行。
He precedes me in the line. = 他排在我前面。


一般过去时:
The announcement preceded the launch. = 公告在发布之前发布。
She preceded him into the room. = 她在他之前进入房间。


现在完成时:
The rain has preceded the storm. = 雨水已经先于暴风雨到来。
He has preceded me in completing the project. = 他已经先于我完成了项目。


过去完成时:
The preparations had preceded the actual event. = 准备工作在实际活动之前就已经完成了。
They had preceded us to the restaurant. = 他们已经先于我们到达了餐厅。


将来时:
The concert will precede the fireworks display. = 音乐会将在烟火表演之前举行。
He will precede her in the presentation. = 他将在演示中先于她发言。


虚拟语气:
If the warning had preceded the incident, the damage could have been avoided. = 如果警告在事件发生之前发出，损害本来是可以避免的。
I wish the email had preceded the phone call. = 我希望邮件能够在电话之前发出。


被动语态:
The meeting was preceded by a prayer. = 会议在祈祷之后举行。
The decision was preceded by extensive research. = 该决定是在广泛研究之后做出的。


疑问句:
What preceded the explosion? = 爆炸前发生了什么？
Did anything precede the accident? = 事故发生前有什么事情发生吗？


否定句:
Nothing preceded the sudden change. = 突然的变化没有任何先兆。
The announcement did not precede the event. = 公告并没有在事件之前发布。


PHRASE->
be preceded by =  以...为先导
preceded by a period of = 在一段...时期之后
immediately preceded by = 直接在...之前
preceded the development of = 在...的发展之前
preceded the introduction of = 在...的引进之前
