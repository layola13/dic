corkscrewed:/ˈkɔːrkskruːd/ | v. | 螺旋状移动；扭曲；拧紧 | To move or twist in a corkscrew-like manner; to become twisted or distorted. |例句：The road corkscrewed down the mountain. 这条路蜿蜒向下盘旋至山底。

The wind corkscrewed the kite into the air. 风把风筝螺旋状地送上了天空。


近义词：twisted, spiraled, wound | 反义词：straightened, untwisted | 词性:动词

词源:
The word "corkscrewed" comes from the noun "corkscrew," which refers to a tool used to remove corks from bottles.  The tool's twisting, spiral motion is what gives the verb its meaning.  The "-ed" suffix indicates the past participle or past tense form of the verb.
