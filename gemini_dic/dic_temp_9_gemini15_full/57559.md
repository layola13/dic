dramaticism: /ˈdræməˌtɪzəm/ | n. |戏剧性；戏剧效果；夸张 |The quality or state of being dramatic; the use of dramatic techniques or effects.  It often implies exaggeration or sensationalism. |例句：The dramaticism of the scene was heightened by the sudden thunderstorm.  这场面的戏剧性因突如其来的雷暴而增强。

The novel lacked dramaticism; the plot was predictable and the characters underdeveloped. 这部小说缺乏戏剧性；情节可预测，人物刻画也不够到位。|近义词：spectacle, theatricality, sensationalism |反义词：understatement, realism, plainness |
