omniferous: /ɒmˈnɪfərəs/ | adj. | 杂食的，无所不吃的 | All-consuming; devouring everything.  The word implies a voracious appetite and a lack of discrimination in what is consumed.  It's often used figuratively to describe something that consumes everything in its path. |  The omniferous fire swept through the forest, leaving nothing but ash.  | 近义词：voracious, insatiable, ravenous | 反义词：selective, fastidious |  形容词

词源:

The word "omniferous" comes from the Latin words "omni-" meaning "all" and "-ferous" meaning "bearing" or "producing".  Therefore, literally it means "all-bearing" or "producing everything," although its usage is more focused on the all-consuming aspect.
