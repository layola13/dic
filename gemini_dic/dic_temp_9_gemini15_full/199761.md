serious: /ˈsɪəriəs/ | adj. | 严肃的；认真的；严重的；危急的 |  Serious means showing or feeling a great deal of concern or worry. It can also refer to something that is important and needs attention or something that is potentially dangerous or harmful. | 例句：He has a serious look on his face. 他脸上带着严肃的表情。

She is serious about her work. 她对她的工作很认真。


近义词：grave, solemn, earnest, critical, severe | 反义词：frivolous, lighthearted, trivial, unimportant, insignificant |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The situation is serious. = 情况很严重。
He is serious about his studies. = 他学习很认真。


一般过去时:
The accident was serious. = 事故很严重。
She was serious about her relationship. = 她对她的感情很认真。


现在完成时:
Things have become serious. = 事情已经变得严重了。
She has been serious about losing weight. = 她一直认真地想减肥。


过去完成时:
The problem had become serious before we noticed it. = 我们注意到这个问题之前，它已经变得很严重了。
I had been serious about quitting smoking for years. = 多年来我一直认真地想戒烟。


将来时:
The situation will become serious if we don't act now. = 如果我们现在不采取行动，情况将会变得严重。
He will be serious about finding a new job. = 他将会认真地寻找新工作。


虚拟语气:
If the situation were serious, we would take action. = 如果情况严重，我们会采取行动。
I wish I had been more serious about my education. = 我希望我以前对我的教育更认真一些。


被动语态:
The matter is taken seriously. = 这件事被认真对待。
The situation was considered serious. = 情况被认为很严重。


疑问句:
Is the situation serious? = 情况严重吗？
Are you serious about this proposal? = 你对这个提议认真吗？


否定句:
The situation is not serious. = 情况并不严重。
He is not serious about his commitments. = 他对自己的承诺并不认真。


PHRASE->
