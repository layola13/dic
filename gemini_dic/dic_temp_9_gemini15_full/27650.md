candidacies:/ˈkændɪdəsiːz/| n. |候选人资格；候选人名单|The noun "candidacies" refers to the state of being a candidate, or the collection of candidatures. It often implies a competitive process where multiple individuals are vying for a position or office.  The plural form indicates multiple instances of candidacy.|例句：The election saw a record number of candidacies.  这次选举出现了创纪录数量的候选人。


The committee reviewed all the candidacies before making a decision.  委员会在做出决定之前审查了所有候选人的资格。|近义词：applications, nominations |反义词: None directly, but "lack of candidacy" could be considered an antonym.

词源:
From "candidate," ultimately from Latin *candidatus, the past participle of *candidare "to be dressed in white," from candidus "white, shining, pure,"  referring to the white togas worn by Roman candidates for office.  The "-acies" suffix creates a noun of abstract qualities.
