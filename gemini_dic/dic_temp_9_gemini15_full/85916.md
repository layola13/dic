headland:/ˈhedlænd/| n.|岬，海角|A headland is a narrow piece of land that sticks out into the sea.  It is often formed by erosion and is usually high and rocky.|例句：The lighthouse sits on a dramatic headland overlooking the ocean. 灯塔坐落在一个俯瞰大海的壮丽海角上。 The ship rounded the headland and sailed into the bay. 船绕过海角驶入海湾。|近义词：promontory, cape, point|反义词：inlet, bay
