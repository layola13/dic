cubits:/ˈkjuːbɪts/ | n. |肘尺；古代长度单位 | A cubit is an ancient unit of length, approximately equal to the length of a forearm from the elbow to the tip of the middle finger.  Different cultures had slightly varying definitions of a cubit.  The term is still used sometimes in historical or biblical contexts. | The ancient Egyptians measured the pyramids in cubits. 古代埃及人用肘尺测量金字塔。  The ark was 300 cubits long. 方舟长300肘尺。 | 近义词：span, fathom (though these are also units of length, not direct synonyms) | 反义词：N/A
