martyrized:/ˈmɑːrtəraɪzd/| v. |殉道；迫害；使受苦 |To kill or persecute someone because of their beliefs.  The word implies suffering for a cause, often religious or political. |例句：The early Christians were martyrized for their faith. 早期基督徒因信仰而殉道。


The early reformers were martyrized for their beliefs. 早期的改革家们因他们的信仰而受迫害。|近义词：persecuted,  tormented, victimized |反义词：venerated, celebrated, protected

WORD ORIGIN->
From the name of the Christian saint, Saint Martyr, who suffered death for his faith.  The word ultimately derives from the Greek word μάρτυς (martys), meaning "witness".  The implication is that martyrs "witness" to their beliefs through their suffering and death.
