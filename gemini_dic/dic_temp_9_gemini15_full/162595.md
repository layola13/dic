preambulate:/ˈpriːæmbjuleɪt/ | v. | 开场白；漫步；前言；序言 | To walk or move about in a leisurely manner; to introduce something with a preliminary statement.  |例句：The professor preambulated around the room before beginning his lecture. 教授在开始讲课前在房间里踱步。|例句：The treaty preambulated with a statement of shared goals.  条约以一份共同目标声明作为开场白。 |近义词：amble, stroll, preface, introduce |反义词：conclude, finish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He preambulates before every meeting. = 他每次会议前都会先闲逛一下。
She preambulates the report with a summary. = 她用摘要作为报告的开场白。


一般过去时:
They preambulated through the park yesterday. = 他们昨天在公园里散步。
He preambulated his speech with a joke. = 他用一个笑话开始了他的演讲。


现在完成时:
She has preambulated the entire city. = 她已经逛遍了整个城市。
The author has preambulated the novel with a lengthy introduction. = 作者在小说中写了一个冗长的引言。


过去完成时:
They had preambulated for hours before finding the trail. = 他们走了几个小时才找到小路。
He had preambulated the topic extensively before presenting his findings. = 在陈述他的发现之前，他已广泛地探讨过这个话题。


将来时:
We will preambulate the garden after dinner. = 我们晚饭后会在花园里散步。
She will preambulate her presentation with a brief overview. = 她将在她的演示文稿中做一个简短的概述。


虚拟语气:
If I had time, I would preambulate the countryside. = 如果我有时间，我会在乡下散步。
If she had prepared better, she wouldn't have had to preambulate so much during her presentation. = 如果她准备得更好，在演示文稿中就不需要那么多铺垫了。


被动语态:
The topic was preambulated with a historical overview. = 这个话题以历史概述作为开场白。
The meeting was preambulated by a lengthy discussion of procedural matters. = 会议以冗长的程序性问题讨论作为开场白。


疑问句:
Does he usually preambulate before speaking? = 他通常在说话前会先踱步吗？
Will the speaker preambulate with an anecdote? = 演讲者会用一个轶事作为开场白吗？


否定句:
He did not preambulate; he went straight to the point. = 他没有闲逛；他直接切入主题。
The report did not preambulate; it got right to the conclusions. = 报告没有前言；它直接得出了结论。


PHRASE->
preambulate around = 闲逛
preambulate through = 漫步穿过
preambulate to = 引向；作为...的开场白
preambulate with = 以...开始；用...作为开场白
preambulate upon = 详述；详细论述


词源:
From Latin *praeambulare, from prae- ("before") + ambulare ("to walk").  The sense of "introductory remarks" developed later.
