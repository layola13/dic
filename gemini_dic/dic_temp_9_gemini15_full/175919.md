ration:/ˈræʃən/| n. vt.|定量供应；配给；口粮|The act of supplying or distributing something, especially food or other essential goods, in limited quantities, often during times of scarcity or emergency.  It can also refer to the amount supplied in this way. |例句：The government imposed strict food rations during the war. = 战争期间政府实行严格的粮食配给。

例句：During the drought, farmers received rations of water for their crops. = 干旱期间，农民们得到用于农作物的配水定量。


近义词：allowance, allotment, quota, supply |反义词：abundance, surplus, excess |词性：n. vt.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government rations essential goods during emergencies. = 政府在紧急情况下对必需品进行配给。
They ration their resources carefully. = 他们仔细地分配资源。


一般过去时:
The army rationed food and water to the soldiers. = 军队向士兵配给食物和水。
They rationed their fuel to make it last longer. = 他们对燃料进行配给以使其持续更久。


现在完成时:
They have rationed supplies since the hurricane. = 自飓风以来，他们一直在配给物资。
The country has rationed petrol for months. = 这个国家已经实行汽油配给好几个月了。


过去完成时:
They had rationed the food before the troops arrived. = 在部队到达之前，他们已经对食物进行了配给。
The city had rationed water for weeks before the rain came. = 在下雨之前，城市已经进行了几周的限水。


将来时:
The government will ration electricity if the power grid fails. = 如果电网瘫痪，政府将实行限电。
We will have to ration our food if the winter is severe. = 如果冬天很寒冷，我们将不得不对食物进行配给。


虚拟语气:
If we had rationed our resources better, we wouldn't be in this situation. = 如果我们能更好地分配资源，就不会陷入这种境地。
It would have been wise to ration the supplies before the journey. = 在旅行前对物资进行配给本来是明智之举。


被动语态:
Food was rationed to every household during the siege. = 围城期间，每户人家都得到了食物配给。
Supplies are being rationed to ensure everyone has enough. = 正在对物资进行配给，以确保每个人都有足够的物资。


疑问句:
Are we going to be rationed again this winter? = 今年冬天我们还要再次实行配给吗？
Will the government ration fuel again this year? = 政府今年还会再次对燃料进行配给吗？


否定句:
The government did not ration sugar during the peacetime. = 和平时期政府没有对糖进行配给。
We didn't ration the water, and we ran out. = 我们没有对水进行配给，结果用完了。

词源:
The word "ration" comes from the Old French word "racion," meaning "portion" or "share," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "ratio," meaning "reason" or "calculation."  The sense of dividing something fairly or systematically is inherent in the root.
