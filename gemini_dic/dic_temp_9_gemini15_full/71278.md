fixations:/ˌfɪkˈseɪʃənz/ | n. | 固定观念；迷恋；注视；(精神分析) 停滞 |  A fixation is a persistent focus on a particular object or idea, often to an unhealthy degree.  It can refer to a strong emotional attachment, an obsessive preoccupation, or a point of visual focus. In psychoanalysis, it refers to a persistent attachment to an earlier stage of psychosexual development. |

例句：

His fixations on cleanliness bordered on obsessive-compulsive disorder.  | 他对清洁的迷恋近乎强迫症。

The child's fixations on the toy were almost unnerving. | 孩子对玩具的迷恋几乎令人不安。


近义词：obsessions, preoccupations, attachments, fascinations

反义词：indifference, detachment, apathy


词源：源自fixate (v.)  + -ion (名词后缀)；fixate  最终源于拉丁语fixare "to fasten, fix"


用法说明：


复数形式: fixations

常用搭配：

1. unhealthy fixations: 不健康的迷恋
2. sexual fixations: 性欲停滞
3. childhood fixations: 童年时期的心理停滞
4. have a fixation on: 对...有迷恋
5. overcome fixations: 克服迷恋
