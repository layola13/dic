comport:/kəmˈpɔːrt/| v.|举止，行为；相符；适应|To behave in a particular way, especially in a way that is consistent with a particular situation or role; to be in agreement or harmony; to be suitable or fitting.|例句：He comported himself with dignity throughout the ordeal. 他在整个磨难中都保持着尊严。|近义词：behave, conduct, comport oneself|反义词：misbehave, misconduct


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He always comports himself with grace. = 他总是举止优雅。
How does she comport herself in public? = 她如何在公众场合表现自己？


一般过去时:
She comported herself well under pressure. = 她在压力下表现良好。
The two stories did not comport with each other. = 这两个故事相互矛盾。


现在完成时:
He has always comported himself with respect. = 他一直以来都表现得很尊重。
How has the new system comported with expectations? = 新系统与预期相符吗？


过去完成时:
The evidence had not comported with his claims. = 证据与他的说法不符。
She had comported herself admirably throughout the crisis. = 在整个危机中，她的表现令人钦佩。


将来时:
He will comport himself appropriately at the meeting. = 他将在会议上表现得体。
How will the new policy comport with existing regulations? = 新政策将如何与现有法规相符？


虚拟语气:
If he comported himself better, he would be more respected. = 如果他表现得更好，他会得到更多尊重。
I wish he would comport himself more professionally. = 我希望他能更专业地表现自己。


被动语态:
His behavior was not well comported. = 他的行为举止不好。
The results were not comported with the predictions. = 结果与预测不符。


疑问句:
How did he comport himself at the interview? = 他在面试中表现如何？
Does this plan comport with our company's values? = 这个计划符合我们公司的价值观吗？


否定句:
He did not comport himself properly at the dinner. = 他在晚餐上表现不当。
The findings do not comport with the initial hypothesis. = 研究结果与最初的假设不符。


PHRASE->
