hyperdicrotism:/ˌhaɪpərˈdaɪkrəˌtɪzəm/| n. |双峰搏动，大脉压|A condition characterized by abnormally large pulse pressure, resulting in a double peak in the arterial pulse wave. This is often associated with aortic regurgitation or other valvular heart diseases.|Example: The patient's hyperdicrotism was indicative of severe aortic regurgitation.|近义词:  wide pulse pressure|反义词:  narrow pulse pressure
