flap: /flæp/ | n., v. | 拍打；飘动；（尤指飞机的）襟翼；兴奋；（尤指鸟类的）翅膀；（衣服等的）下摆 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "flap" refers to a broad, flat piece of something that hangs loosely, or a sudden or forceful movement of something.  It can also refer to a state of excitement or agitation. As a verb, "flap" means to move up and down or back and forth with a light, rapid motion.


例句:
The bird flapped its wings and flew away.  = 鸟拍打着翅膀飞走了。

The flag flapped in the wind. = 旗子在风中飘动。


近义词:
(n.)  wing, cover,  piece,  agitation,  excitement
(v.)  beat,  flutter,  wave,  thrash


反义词:
(n.)  calm,  tranquility
(v.)  (no direct opposite, context-dependent)


词性:
Noun, Verb

词源:
From Middle English flappen, of imitative origin.  The word's sound mimics the action it describes.
