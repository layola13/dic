hypercoagulabilities:/ˌhaɪpərˌkoʊæɡjʊləˈbɪlətiz/| n. |高凝状态，高凝性|The tendency for blood to clot more readily than normal.  This can lead to serious health problems such as thrombosis (blood clots in the veins) and embolism (blood clots traveling through the bloodstream).|Example:  Inherited hypercoagulabilities can significantly increase the risk of deep vein thrombosis.|近义词：thrombophilia, procoagulant state|反义词：hypocoagulability
