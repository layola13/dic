preadmonished:/priːədˈmɒnɪʃt/| adj.|预先警告的，事先告诫的|Previously warned or cautioned.  The word implies a warning given before an event or action, often to prevent negative consequences.|例句：He felt preadmonished about the dangers of the expedition. 他预感到探险的危险。|例句：Having been preadmonished about the slippery conditions, she walked cautiously. 因为事先被警告了路况湿滑，她小心翼翼地走着。|近义词：forewarned, pre-warned, cautioned|反义词：unwarned, unprepared
