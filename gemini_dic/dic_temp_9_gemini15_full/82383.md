grubbed:/ɡrʌbd/| v.| 费力地挖；勉强获得；粗略地吃；|To dig or pull up (something) from the ground, often with difficulty; to obtain something with difficulty or by unfair means; to eat greedily or in a messy way.|例句：They grubbed up the old tree roots.  他们费力地挖掉了老树根。|近义词：dug, unearthed, scrounged, scavenged |反义词：planted, sowed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grubs for worms in the garden. = 他在花园里费力地挖蚯蚓。
They grub around for information. = 他们四处寻找信息。


一般过去时:
She grubbed up all the weeds. = 她把所有的杂草都拔掉了。
We grubbed around for a solution to the problem. = 我们四处寻找问题的解决办法。


现在完成时:
They have grubbed out the old foundations. = 他们已经挖掉了旧的地基。
He has grubbed enough money to buy a car.  = 他已经攒够了买车的钱。


过去完成时:
She had grubbed for food all day. = 她已经找了一整天的食物了。
They had grubbed out the evidence before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前，他们已经销毁了证据。


将来时:
We will grub up the potatoes tomorrow. = 我们明天将把土豆挖出来。
They will grub for answers to the mystery. = 他们将努力寻找这个谜团的答案。


虚拟语气:
If I had grubbed harder, I might have found it. = 如果我更努力地挖，我可能会找到它。
I wish I had grubbed enough money for the trip. = 我真希望我为这次旅行攒够了钱。


被动语态:
The old building was grubbed out to make way for a new one. = 旧建筑被拆除以腾出空间建造新建筑。
The information was grubbed from unreliable sources. = 信息是从不可靠的来源获得的。


疑问句:

否定句:

He didn't grub around for excuses. = 他没有找借口。
