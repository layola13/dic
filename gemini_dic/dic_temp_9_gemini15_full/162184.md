pre-messianic: /ˌpriːˌmɛsiˈænɪk/ | adj. |弥赛亚之前的，先弥赛亚时代的 | Relating to the period before the arrival or expected arrival of the Messiah.  This adjective describes events, beliefs, or conditions that existed before the time when a Messiah was believed to have appeared or would appear. | Example:  The pre-messianic era was characterized by a longing for a savior. = 弥赛亚之前的时代以对救世主的渴望为特征。  The pre-messianic prophecies foretold the coming of a great leader. = 弥赛亚之前的预言预示着一位伟大领袖的到来。 | 近义词: ante-messianic, pre-Christ, pre-Christian | 反义词: post-messianic
