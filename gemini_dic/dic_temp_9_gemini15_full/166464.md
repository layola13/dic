prerevival: /ˌpriːrɪˈvaɪvəl/ | adj. | 复兴之前的；预兆复兴的 |  Describing a period or event that comes before a revival or resurgence of something, often suggesting a period of decline or stagnation preceding the revival.  It implies anticipation or foreshadowing of the revival. | Example: The prerevival period saw a decline in religious fervor, but the seeds of a great awakening were being sown.  复兴之前的时期见证了宗教热情下降，但伟大觉醒的种子正在播种。 | 近义词: precursory, antecedent, preliminary | 反义词: post-revival
