mouthy:/ˈmaʊθi/| adj.|喋喋不休的；夸夸其谈的；傲慢无礼的|Given his mouthy nature, he often gets into arguments.|由于他喋喋不休的性格，他经常发生争吵。|近义词：talkative, garrulous, loquacious, impertinent|反义词：quiet, reserved, taciturn

例句：

He's a bit mouthy, always arguing with everyone. 他有点喋喋不休，总是和每个人争吵。

The mouthy teenager refused to listen to his parents' advice.  那个喋喋不休的青少年拒绝听从父母的建议。


词源:

The word "mouthy" originates from the noun "mouth," meaning the opening in the face through which an animal or person takes in food and speaks.  The suffix "-y" transforms nouns into adjectives, often implying an excessive or characteristic quality.  Therefore, "mouthy" literally means "having the characteristics of a mouth" in an excessive way, leading to its current meaning of being excessively talkative or argumentative.


用法说明:

"Mouthy" is primarily used as an adjective to describe someone who talks too much, often in a boastful, aggressive, or disrespectful manner. It carries a negative connotation, suggesting that the person's talkativeness is annoying or offensive.  It's often used informally.
