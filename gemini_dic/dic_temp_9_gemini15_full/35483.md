coact:/koʊˈækt/| vt.|强制;强迫;胁迫|To compel or force (someone) to do something.|The government coacted the company into releasing the documents. 政府强迫该公司公开文件。|近义词：compel, force, oblige|反义词：allow, permit

词源:
From Latin *co- "together" + agere "to do, drive, act".  The prefix "co-" suggests a joint or combined action, but in this case, it's a joint action of forcing or compelling.
