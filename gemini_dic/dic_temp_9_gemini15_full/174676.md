quolls:/kwɒlz/| n. |袋狸|A quoll is a carnivorous marsupial mammal native to Australia and New Guinea.  They are known for their agile movements and varied diet, which includes insects, small mammals, birds, and reptiles.  Quolls are facing threats from habitat loss and introduced predators. |  There are several species of quolls, each with slightly different characteristics and habitats.  The eastern quoll is considered critically endangered.  |近义词: marsupial, carnivore |反义词:  (no direct antonym, but could consider herbivore as a contrasting term)|名词
