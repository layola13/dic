quasi-appealingly: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ əˈpiːlɪŋli/ | adv. | 模糊地吸引人地，似是而非地吸引人地 |  The word combines "quasi" (meaning seemingly, apparently, or to a certain extent) with "appealingly" (meaning in an attractive or pleasant manner). It describes something that is somewhat attractive or appealing, but not entirely or genuinely so; it might be subtly charming or have a hint of appeal, but lacks complete attractiveness.  | 例句：The painting was quasi-appealingly abstract, with hints of recognizable forms but ultimately defying easy categorization.  这幅画模糊地吸引人，有着一些可辨认的形状，但最终无法轻易分类。


The advertisement's message was quasi-appealingly vague, leaving the viewer with a sense of intrigue but little concrete information. 广告信息模棱两可，引人入胜，但缺乏具体的资料。| 近义词：slightly attractive, vaguely alluring, mildly charming | 反义词：repulsive, unattractive, unappealing


词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "as it were," "almost," or "seemingly."  This prefix indicates something that resembles or partially fulfills the qualities of the word it modifies.

* **appealingly**: From "appeal" (from Old French *apeler, from Latin *appellare, "to call to," "to address"),  plus the adverbial suffix "-ly."  "Appealing" means attractive or pleasing.

用法说明:

This word is an adverb, modifying a verb to describe how an action or state is performed.  It's used to describe something that has a limited or questionable appeal, often suggesting a subtle or indirect attractiveness.  It carries a connotation of something that is not wholly appealing, perhaps because it's ambiguous, incomplete, or slightly flawed.  It's a relatively uncommon word, used in more formal or literary contexts.


PHRASE->

There are no common set phrases with "quasi-appealingly".  Its usage is primarily as a single descriptive adverb.
