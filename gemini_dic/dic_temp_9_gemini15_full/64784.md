eskimos:/ˈɛskɪmoʊz/ | n. |爱斯基摩人（旧称，现多用Inuk或Inuit）|A term formerly used to refer to various indigenous peoples inhabiting the Arctic regions of North America, Greenland, and Siberia.  The term is now considered offensive by many, as it is believed to have been derived from a word meaning "eaters of raw meat."  The preferred terms are Inuk (singular) and Inuit (plural) which are self-designations. | The Eskimos lived in igloos during the winter.  爱斯基摩人在冬天住在冰屋里。  Many Eskimos rely on hunting and fishing for survival.  许多爱斯基摩人依靠狩猎和捕鱼为生。|Inuk, Inuit, Arctic people|None (the word itself is considered offensive)|n.
