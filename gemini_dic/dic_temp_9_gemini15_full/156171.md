photozincography:/ˌfoʊtoʊzɪŋˈkɑːɡrəfi/| n. |照相锌刻术|A photographic process for reproducing images on zinc plates, used in the 19th century for printing.  It involved using light-sensitive chemicals to create an image on a zinc plate, which was then etched to produce a printing surface. |The museum showcased several prints created using photozincography.  博物馆展示了几幅采用照相锌刻术制作的版画。|  lithography, collotype |  (no direct antonym)| 名词
