fall-offs:/ˈfɔːlˌɔfs/| n.|下降；减少；脱落|A fall-off refers to a decrease or decline in something, often a quantity, level, or rate.  It can also refer to something that falls off, such as a piece of clothing or a cliff.|例句：There has been a significant fall-off in sales this quarter.  本季度销售额大幅下降。|例句：The hiker suffered a serious fall-off the cliff.  远足者从悬崖上严重跌落。|近义词：decline, decrease, drop, reduction|反义词：increase, rise, growth|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The number of customers shows a fall-off every winter. = 每到冬天顾客数量就会下降。
There is a gradual fall-off in the quality of the product. = 产品质量正在逐渐下降。


一般过去时:
A fall-off in donations forced the charity to close. = 捐款减少迫使慈善机构关闭。
The bridge experienced a fall-off in its structural integrity. = 桥梁的结构完整性出现了下降。


现在完成时:
There has been a fall-off in the company's stock price recently. = 公司股价最近有所下跌。
We have seen a fall-off in the number of applicants for the job. = 我们已经看到申请这份工作的申请人数减少了。


过去完成时:
By the end of the year, there had been a significant fall-off in production. = 到年底，产量已经大幅下降。
Before the new regulations, there had been a fall-off in environmental protection efforts. = 在新的规章制度出台之前，环境保护工作已经有所减少。


将来时:
Experts predict a fall-off in tourism next year. = 专家预测明年旅游业将下降。
We anticipate a fall-off in the demand for this product. = 我们预计对这种产品的需求将会下降。


虚拟语气:
If there were a fall-off in support, the project would fail. = 如果支持减少，项目就会失败。
I wish there weren't such a fall-off in the quality of education. = 我希望教育质量不会下降这么多。


被动语态:
The decline in profits was attributed to a fall-off in demand. = 利润下降归因于需求减少。
The decrease in student enrollment is partly due to a fall-off in financial aid. = 学生入学人数减少部分是由于经济援助减少。


疑问句:

Have you noticed a fall-off in the quality of the service? = 你注意到服务质量下降了吗？


否定句:
There was no fall-off in the team's performance. = 球队的表现没有下降。
There hasn't been a fall-off in the popularity of this book. = 这本书的人气没有下降。
