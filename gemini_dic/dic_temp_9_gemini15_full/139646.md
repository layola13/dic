occultish:/ɒˈkʌltɪʃ/ | adj. |神秘的；奥秘的；超自然的 |Relating to or characteristic of occult practices or beliefs; mysterious, supernatural, or magical.  The adjective describes something connected to the occult, suggesting secrecy, mystery, and often supernatural elements. It implies a hidden or esoteric knowledge.  |例句：The occultish rituals involved chanting and strange symbols.  神秘的仪式包括吟唱和奇怪的符号。

The novel explored occultish themes of reincarnation and astral projection. 这部小说探讨了轮回和星体投射等神秘主义主题。


近义词： mystical, supernatural, magical, esoteric, arcane |反义词：rational, scientific, mundane, commonplace


词源：From "occult," from Latin *occultus, past participle of occulēre "to hide," from ob "over" + -culēre (related to celare "to hide").  The suffix "-ish" adds a slightly derogatory or uncertain quality.
