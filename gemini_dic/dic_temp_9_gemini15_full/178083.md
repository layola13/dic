reclines:/rɪˈklaɪnz/ | v. | 躺下；倚靠；放松；倾斜 | To lean back and relax; to assume a resting position with the body supported, often at an angle.  |  He reclined in his armchair and read a book. 他躺在扶手椅上看书。  She reclined against the pillows, feeling tired. 她靠着枕头，感到很累。 | lean back, lounge, rest | stand, sit upright | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reclines on the sofa every evening. 他每天晚上都躺在沙发上。
She reclines gracefully in her chair. 她优雅地斜倚在椅子上。

一般过去时:
He reclined in the hammock all afternoon. 他整个下午都躺在吊床上。
She reclined her head on his shoulder. 她把头靠在他的肩膀上。

现在完成时:
He has reclined in that chair for hours. 他在那把椅子上躺了几个小时了。
She has reclined by the pool all day. 她整天都躺在泳池边。

过去完成时:
He had reclined for a while before the guests arrived. 客人到达之前，他已躺了一会儿。
She had reclined on the beach before she felt the sun's harshness.  她在感到阳光强烈之前，一直在海滩上躺着。

将来时:
He will recline by the fireplace after dinner. 晚饭后他会在壁炉旁躺下。
She will recline on the deck and watch the sunset. 她会躺在甲板上欣赏日落。

虚拟语气:
If I were tired, I would recline on the couch. 如果我累了，我就躺在沙发上。
I wish I could recline on a beach right now. 我真希望现在能躺在沙滩上。

被动语态:
The chair is designed to recline comfortably. 这把椅子设计得很舒适，可以让人舒服地躺下。
The seats in the theatre can be reclined. 剧院里的座位可以放倒。

疑问句:
Does he often recline in front of the TV? 他经常躺在电视机前吗？
Will she recline in the sun all day? 她会整天躺在阳光下吗？

否定句:
He doesn't recline often. 他不经常躺着。
She didn't recline because she was too busy. 她因为太忙而没有躺下。
