ranted:/ˈræntɪd/| v.| 喋喋不休地抱怨；咆哮；激昂地演说 | to speak or shout at length in a wild, excited, or angry way | He ranted about the injustice of it all. 他喋喋不休地抱怨这一切的不公正。|近义词：rave, fulminate, declaim |反义词：whisper, murmur


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rants about politics every day. = 他每天都喋喋不休地谈论政治。
She rants and raves whenever she's stressed. = 她一有压力就又喊又叫。


一般过去时:
He ranted at the referee after the game. = 赛后他对着裁判大发雷霆。
She ranted and raved about the poor service. = 她对糟糕的服务大发牢骚。


现在完成时:
He has ranted and raved at me for hours. = 他对我大发雷霆好几个小时了。
She has ranted about the unfairness of the system many times. = 她多次抱怨这个制度的不公平。


过去完成时:
He had ranted for twenty minutes before anyone intervened. = 他已经咆哮了二十分钟，才有人出来干预。
She had ranted about the noise all evening. = 她整个晚上都在抱怨噪音。


将来时:
He will rant and rave if he doesn't get his way. = 如果他不遂心愿，他就会大发雷霆。
She will rant about the traffic again tomorrow. = 她明天还会抱怨交通状况。


虚拟语气:
If he knew the truth, he wouldn't rant like that. = 如果他知道真相，他就不会那样大发雷霆了。
I wish he wouldn't rant so much. = 我希望他不那么喋喋不休。


被动语态:
His complaints were often ranted about by the media. = 他的抱怨经常被媒体大肆报道。
The injustice was ranted against by many people. = 许多人对这种不公正行为表示强烈抗议。


疑问句:
Was he ranting again last night? = 他昨晚又在咆哮了吗？
Why is she ranting and raving? = 她为什么又喊又叫？


否定句:
He didn't rant or rave at the meeting. = 他在会议上没有大发雷霆。
She didn't rant about the situation. = 她没有抱怨这种情况。


PHRASE->
rant and rave = 大发雷霆，大吵大闹
rant about something = 对某事大发牢骚
rant against something = 痛斥某事
rant on (about something) = 喋喋不休地谈论（某事）
let someone have it (rant at someone) = 对某人大发雷霆


词源:
The word "rant" ultimately derives from the Middle Dutch word "ranten," meaning "to rave, to roar."  This word entered English in the 16th century and has since evolved to encompass its modern meanings.
