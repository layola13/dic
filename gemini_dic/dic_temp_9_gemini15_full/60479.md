ejection:/ɪˈdʒɛkʃən/ | n. |喷射；弹出；驱逐；解雇 | The act of ejecting or being ejected; the state of being ejected.  It can refer to the forceful expulsion of something from a confined space, the removal of someone from a place or position, or the act of discharging something. |例句：The ejection of the pilot from the fighter jet saved his life.  飞行员从战斗机中弹射出来挽救了他的生命。

The sudden ejection of the cork from the champagne bottle surprised everyone. 香槟瓶塞的突然弹出让每个人都大吃一惊。


近义词：expulsion, expulsion, dismissal, removal |反义词：insertion, admission, retention

词源:
The word "ejection" comes from the Latin word "ejectus," the past participle of "eicere," meaning "to throw out" or "to expel."  The prefix "e-" indicates "out" and "ject" is from the Latin "jacere," meaning "to throw."
