shekinah: /ʃəˈkiːnə/ | n. | 弥赛亚荣耀，神临在 | The Shekinah is a manifestation of God's presence, often described as a luminous cloud or light.  It's a concept primarily found in Kabbalistic Jewish mysticism and some Christian traditions.  The Shekinah represents God's immanent presence and active engagement with the world. Unlike the transcendent, distant God of some theological perspectives, the Shekinah emphasizes God's closeness and involvement in creation. |  The Shekinah glory filled the Tabernacle.  弥赛亚的荣耀充满了会幕。  |  Divine presence, glory, immanence |  Absence, distance | noun
