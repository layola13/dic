finding:/ˈfaɪndɪŋ/| n.|发现；调查结果；调查报告|The act of discovering something or someone; the result of an inquiry or investigation; a reported discovery.|例句：The police are still investigating the crime, and we await their findings.  警方仍在调查这起犯罪案件，我们等待他们的调查结果。

The most significant finding of the study was the link between diet and heart disease.  这项研究最重要的发现是饮食与心脏病之间的联系。


近义词：discovery, conclusion, result, determination|反义词：loss, failure

USAGE->

一般现在时:
The scientist is finding new evidence. = 科学家正在发现新的证据。
He finds joy in helping others. = 他从帮助他人中找到快乐。


一般过去时:
They found a lost dog yesterday. = 他们昨天找到了一只丢失的狗。
I found the answer to the puzzle. = 我找到了谜题的答案。


现在完成时:
We have found many interesting fossils. = 我们已经发现了许多有趣的化石。
She has found her lost keys. = 她已经找到了她丢失的钥匙。


过去完成时:
He had found the treasure before the others arrived. = 他在其他人到达之前就已经找到了宝藏。
They had found a solution before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期之前已经找到了解决方案。


将来时:
They will find a cure for this disease eventually. = 他们最终会找到这种疾病的治疗方法。
We will find out the truth soon. = 我们很快就会查明真相。


虚拟语气:
If I found a million dollars, I would travel the world. = 如果我找到一百万美元，我会周游世界。
I wish I had found the answer sooner. = 我希望我早点找到答案。


被动语态:
The lost child was found safe and sound. = 走失的孩子被安然无恙地找到了。
The solution to the problem has not yet been found. = 这个问题的解决方法尚未找到。


疑问句:
Have you found your glasses? = 你找到你的眼镜了吗？
What did you find in the box? = 你在盒子里找到了什么？


否定句:
I cannot find my keys. = 我找不到我的钥匙。
They did not find any evidence. = 他们没有发现任何证据。
