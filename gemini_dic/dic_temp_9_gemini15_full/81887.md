grievously:/ˈɡriːvəsli/| adj. |极其严重地；令人痛苦地；悲痛地 |extremely serious or severe; causing or expressing great sorrow or distress|The accident grievously injured three people. 这起事故严重地伤害了三个人。|近义词：severely, seriously, painfully, sadly |反义词：slightly, mildly, insignificantly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grievously misinterprets the data. 他严重误解了这些数据。
She grievously offends her colleagues with her remarks. 她的话严重冒犯了她的同事。


一般过去时:
The company grievously underestimated the market demand. 公司严重低估了市场需求。
The war grievously affected the nation's economy. 战争严重影响了国家的经济。


现在完成时:
They have grievously wronged him. 他们严重冤枉了他。
The drought has grievously damaged the crops. 干旱严重损坏了庄稼。


过去完成时:
She had grievously disappointed her parents. 她之前严重地让她的父母失望了。
He had grievously erred in his judgment. 他在判断上犯了严重的错误。


将来时:
The storm will grievously damage the coastal areas. 暴风雨将严重破坏沿海地区。
His actions will grievously impact his reputation. 他的行为将严重影响他的声誉。


虚拟语气:
If he had acted differently, he wouldn't have grievously harmed his career. 如果他采取不同的行动，就不会严重损害他的事业了。
I wish I hadn't grievously insulted him. 我真希望我没有严重侮辱他。


被动语态:
His reputation was grievously damaged by the scandal. 他的声誉因丑闻而严重受损。
The city was grievously affected by the earthquake.  城市受到了地震的严重影响。


疑问句:
Was he grievously injured in the accident? 他在事故中受重伤了吗？

否定句:
The damage wasn't grievously extensive. 损害并没有严重到无法挽回的地步。
He wasn't grievously hurt in the fall. 他摔倒时伤得不严重。
