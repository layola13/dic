preinvocation:/ˌpriːɪnˈvɔːkeɪʃən/| n. |预先调用，预先调用过程 |The act of invoking something before it is actually needed;  preparation or setting up before an invocation.  It often refers to the steps taken before a function or subroutine is called in a program.  This might involve setting up arguments or initializing variables.|例句：The preinvocation phase of the database query involved checking for data integrity.|近义词：preparation, setup, initialization |反义词：post-invocation
