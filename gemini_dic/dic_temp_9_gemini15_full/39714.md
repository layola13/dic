consignification:/ˌkɒnˌsɪɡnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. | 共指；共同指涉 | The process by which different signs or symbols come to be associated with each other and acquire a shared meaning.  It refers to the way meanings are created through the relationships between signs within a system, rather than the inherent meaning of individual signs.  For example, the words "red" and "rose" might acquire a consignification of romance through repeated cultural association. | The painting's use of red and black created a powerful consignification of danger and passion.  |  co-signification, connotation, implication | denotation | 名词
