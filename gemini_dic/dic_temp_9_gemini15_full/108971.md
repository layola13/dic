leetman: /ˈliːtˌmæn/ | n. | 网络高手，黑客 | A person highly skilled in computer programming, especially one who is adept at exploiting security vulnerabilities.  Often associated with the use of "leetspeak," a cryptic writing style using numbers and symbols in place of letters. | Example:  He's a real leetman; he can crack any password. 他是个真正的网络高手；他能破解任何密码。 | 近义词：hacker, programmer, coder, cracker | 反义词：noob, beginner, unskilled user

词源:

The term "leetman" is a combination of "leet" (referencing "leetspeak," a slang used by hackers and online communities) and "man," implying a skilled individual within that community.  The term emerged from online communities and gaming culture, reflecting the admiration for highly skilled individuals in these spaces.  It carries a slightly informal and often playful tone.
