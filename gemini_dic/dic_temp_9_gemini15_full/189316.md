safeguarding: /ˈseɪfɡɑːrdɪŋ/ | v. | 保护；维护；保障 | The act of protecting something from harm or damage.  It emphasizes the proactive and ongoing nature of protection. | Example: The company has implemented new measures for safeguarding its intellectual property. 公司已经实施了新的措施来保护其知识产权。  The government is committed to safeguarding the rights of its citizens. 政府致力于保障公民的权利。|近义词：protecting, preserving, shielding, securing |反义词：endangering, jeopardizing, risking


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We are safeguarding our environment. = 我们正在保护我们的环境。
They safeguard the interests of their clients. = 他们维护客户的利益。


一般过去时:
The police safeguarding the event. = 警察保障了这次活动的顺利进行。

现在完成时:
We have safeguarded our data against unauthorized access. = 我们已经保护了我们的数据免受未经授权的访问。

过去完成时:

词源:
The word "safeguarding" is derived from the Old English word "sælig" meaning "safe" or "blessed," combined with the Old English word "wærdian" meaning "to guard" or "to protect."  The modern meaning and usage evolved gradually through Middle English and into Modern English.
