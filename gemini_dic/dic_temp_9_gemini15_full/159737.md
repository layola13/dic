polycracy: /pɑːˈlɪkrəsi/ | n. | 寡头政治；多头政治 | Government by many; rule by a multitude of persons or groups.  This often implies a lack of central authority or effective governance, leading to inefficiency and instability.  The term can sometimes be used to describe a system where power is fragmented among various competing factions or elites, resulting in a stalemate or chaotic situation. | Example: The city's governance devolved into a polycracy, with various interest groups vying for control and little progress being made on crucial issues. | 近义词：oligarchy, plutocracy, factionalism | 反义词：autocracy, monarchy, dictatorship |  名词
