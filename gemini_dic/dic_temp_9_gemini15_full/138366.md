notion:/ˈnoʊʃən/| n. |概念，想法，意图，打算 |a conception of or belief about something.  It can refer to a general understanding or a vague idea. |He had a notion to visit his aunt.|近义词：concept, idea, belief, thought |反义词：reality, fact


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His notion of fairness is different from mine. = 他对公平的观念与我不同。
What is your notion of success? = 你对成功的概念是什么？

一般过去时:
Her notion of a perfect vacation involved relaxing on a beach. = 她对完美假期的想法是躺在海滩上放松。
I had a sudden notion to call my parents. = 我突然想给我父母打电话。

现在完成时:
His notions about politics have changed considerably. = 他对政治的看法已经发生了很大的变化。
Have you had any notions about what to do next? = 你对下一步该做什么有什么想法吗？

过去完成时:
I had already formed a notion of what the job entailed before I started. = 在我开始工作之前，我已经对这份工作的内容有了一个概念。
She had no notion that her secret would be discovered. = 她根本没想到她的秘密会被发现。

将来时:
I will have a better notion of the situation after I've spoken to her. = 我和她谈话后，对情况会有更好的了解。
What notion will you have regarding the new project? = 你对新项目有什么想法？

虚拟语气:
If I had a notion of how difficult it would be, I wouldn't have started. = 如果我知道这有多难，我一开始就不会做了。
I wish I had a notion of where he's gone. = 我真希望知道他去哪儿了。

被动语态:
The notion of individual liberty was not widely accepted. = 个人自由的概念并没有被广泛接受。
His notion was quickly dismissed by the others. = 他的想法很快就被其他人否决了。

疑问句:
What's your notion of a good life? = 你认为美好生活是什么样的？
Do you have any notion where the keys are? = 你知道钥匙在哪儿吗？

否定句:
I have no notion what he's talking about. = 我不知道他在说什么。
She had no notion of the danger she was in. = 她不知道自己有多危险。
