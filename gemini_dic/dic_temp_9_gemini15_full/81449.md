gratin:/ˈɡræˈtæ̃/|/ˈɡrætɪn/| n. | gratin, 烤菜|A gratin is a dish of food, typically vegetables or pasta, that is topped with breadcrumbs or cheese and then baked until golden brown.  It's characterized by its browned, crispy topping. | Example:  The potato gratin was creamy and delicious, with a perfectly crisp top.  土豆 gratin 又香又滑，顶部酥脆可口。|近义词：au gratin, scalloped|反义词：无

词源:
The word "gratin" comes from the French word "gratter," meaning "to scrape" or "to grate." This refers to the process of grating cheese or breadcrumbs onto the top of the dish before baking.  The dish's name reflects this crucial preparation step.
