mendiant:/ˈmɛndiænt/| n. |乞丐；施舍者|A mendicant is a person who begs for a living, often relying on charity from others.  They may be homeless or impoverished, and their begging is often a necessity for survival.  The term can also refer to a member of a religious order that relies on alms.|例句：The mendicant stood on the street corner, holding out his hat.  这位乞丐站在街角，伸出他的帽子。|The starving mendicant accepted the offered bread gratefully. 挨饿的乞丐感激地接受了递来的面包。|近义词： beggar, pauper, vagrant |反义词： millionaire, philanthropist
