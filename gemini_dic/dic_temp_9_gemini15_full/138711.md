nullifying: /ˈnʌlɪfaɪɪŋ/ | v. | 使无效；取消；废除 | To make something legally or officially ineffective or void.  | The court nullified the contract due to fraud. 法院因欺诈而使合同无效。 | invalidate, annul, negate | validate, confirm, ratify | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He nullifies the contract. = 他使合同无效。
The new law nullifies the old regulations. = 新法律使旧规失效。


一般过去时:
The judge nullified the verdict. = 法官推翻了判决。
The amendment nullified the previous agreement. = 修正案使之前的协议失效。


现在完成时:
They have nullified the election results. = 他们已经宣布选举结果无效。
The government has nullified the trade deal. = 政府已经废除了贸易协定。


过去完成时:
The higher court had nullified the lower court's decision. = 上级法院已经推翻了下级法院的裁决。
She had nullified her previous statement. = 她已经撤回了之前的声明。


将来时:
The changes will nullify the existing system. = 这些改变将会使现有系统失效。
He will nullify the contract if he finds evidence of fraud. = 如果他发现有欺诈证据，他将使合同无效。


虚拟语气:
If they signed the contract, it would nullify their previous agreement. = 如果他们签署了这份合同，那将使他们之前的协议无效。
It would nullify the entire process if we made that mistake. = 如果我们犯了那个错误，整个过程都会失效。


被动语态:
The contract was nullified by the court. = 合同被法院宣布无效。
The agreement will be nullified if the conditions aren't met. = 如果不满足条件，协议将被宣布无效。


疑问句:
Will this action nullify the warranty? = 这一行为会使保修失效吗？
Does the new policy nullify the previous one? = 新政策是否使之前的政策失效？


否定句:
The agreement was not nullified. = 协议没有被宣布无效。
The court did not nullify the contract. = 法院没有使合同无效。
