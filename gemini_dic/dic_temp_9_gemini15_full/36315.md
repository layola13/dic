coholder: /ˈkoʊhoʊldər/ | n. | 共同持有人 | A coholder is a person who holds something jointly with another or others.  This term is often used in legal and financial contexts, referring to individuals who share ownership or responsibility for something.  For example, coholders of a bank account have equal access and authority over the funds. | Example:  The siblings are coholders of their late parents' estate.  = 兄弟姐妹是他们已故父母遗产的共同持有人。


The coholders of the property agreed to sell it. = 这处房产的共同持有人同意出售它。


近义词: joint holder, sharer, co-owner | 反义词: sole holder, individual owner | 词性: noun
