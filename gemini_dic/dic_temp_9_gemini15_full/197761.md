shilly-shallyer: /ˈʃɪliˈʃæliər/ | n. |犹豫不决的人；拖延者 | A person who is indecisive and procrastinates.  |He's such a shilly-shallyer; he can never make up his mind. 他真是个犹豫不决的人，他总是下不了决心。|近义词：procrastinator, waffler, dilly-dallier |反义词：decisive person, go-getter


USAGE->
This word is a noun, so it doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  However, it can be used in different sentence structures:

Simple Present Tense:
He is a shilly-shallyer. = 他是一个犹豫不决的人。
The committee members are shilly-shallyers. = 委员会成员都是犹豫不决的人。


Simple Past Tense:
He was a shilly-shallyer in his youth. = 他年轻的时候优柔寡断。
The project was delayed because of the shilly-shallyers. = 项目因为那些犹豫不决的人而被耽搁了。


Present Perfect Tense:
He has always been a shilly-shallyer. = 他一直都是个犹豫不决的人。
They have become shilly-shallyers in their old age. = 他们上了年纪后变得优柔寡断了。


Future Tense:
He will remain a shilly-shallyer, I fear. = 我担心他仍然会优柔寡断。
The negotiations will likely fail because of the shilly-shallyers involved. = 谈判很可能会因为涉及到的犹豫不决的人而失败。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "shilly-shallyer".  However, the concept of indecisiveness can be expressed with phrases like:

to shilly-shally = to hesitate or procrastinate (动词词组)
to be on the fence = to be undecided (形容词词组)
to waffle = to speak or act evasively (动词词组)
to hem and haw = to hesitate or be indecisive in speech (动词词组)
to beat around the bush = to avoid directly addressing an issue (动词词组)


词源:
The word "shilly-shally" (from which "shilly-shallyer" is derived) is of uncertain origin, possibly an onomatopoeia representing the sound of hesitant speech.  It first appeared in the 16th century.  The "-er" suffix is a common English suffix used to form nouns indicating a person or thing that performs an action.
