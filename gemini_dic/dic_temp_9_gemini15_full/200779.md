slice:/slaɪs/| n., vt.|薄片；切片；部分；份额|A slice is a flat, relatively thin piece of something, often cut from a larger piece.  It can also refer to a portion or share of something.|例句：She cut a slice of cake. 她切了一块蛋糕。

例句：That's a large slice of the market. 那是市场很大的一部分。


近义词：piece, portion, section, segment|反义词：whole


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slices the bread thinly. = 他把面包切得很薄。
She slices the tomatoes for the salad. = 她把西红柿切成片做沙拉。


一般过去时:
I sliced the lemon into wedges. = 我把柠檬切成角。
They sliced the pizza into eight pieces. = 他们把披萨切成八块。


现在完成时:
He has sliced all the onions. = 他已经把所有洋葱都切好了。
She has sliced the cake for the party. = 她已经把蛋糕切好准备派对了。


过去完成时:
They had sliced the vegetables before they started cooking. = 他们开始做饭之前已经把蔬菜切好了。
She had sliced the bread before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，她已经把面包切好了。


将来时:
I will slice the watermelon later. = 我晚点会切西瓜。
We will slice the cake for the birthday party. = 我们会为生日派对切蛋糕。


虚拟语气:
If I had a sharper knife, I would slice the bread more easily. = 如果我有一把更锋利的刀，我就能更容易地切面包。
I wish I could slice this meat more thinly. = 我希望我能把这肉切得更薄。


被动语态:
The bread was sliced thinly. = 面包被切得很薄。
The pizza was sliced into six pieces. = 比萨被切成了六块。


疑问句:
How many slices of pizza do you want? = 你想要几片披萨？
Have you sliced the bread yet? = 你切面包了吗？


否定句:
