digresses:/daɪˈɡrɛsɪz/| v.|离题；偏离主题；扯远了|To stray from the main subject in speaking or writing.|例句：He constantly digresses in his lectures. 他在讲课时经常离题。|例句：The speaker digressed from his prepared remarks to answer a question. 演讲者偏离了准备好的发言来回答一个问题。|近义词：deviate, wander, stray|反义词：focus, concentrate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He digresses frequently during his speeches. = 他在演讲中经常离题。
She digresses often when telling stories. = 她讲故事时经常跑题。


一般过去时:
The professor digressed from the topic yesterday. = 昨天教授偏离了主题。
He digressed into a long anecdote about his childhood. = 他扯到一段关于他童年的长篇轶事。


现在完成时:
He has digressed several times already in this presentation. = 在这次演示中他已经几次离题了。
She has digressed so much that we've lost track of the main point. = 她已经跑题太多，以至于我们弄丢了主题。


过去完成时:
Before he concluded, he had digressed extensively on the history of the subject. = 在他总结之前，他已经广泛地谈论了该主题的历史。
They had digressed so far from the original agenda that the meeting ran overtime. = 他们已经偏离了原定的议程太远，以至于会议超时了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish he wouldn't digress every five minutes. = 我希望他不要每五分钟就离题。
