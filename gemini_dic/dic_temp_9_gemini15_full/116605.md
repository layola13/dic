matrimoine:/ˈmætɹɪmɔɪn/| n. |母系遗产；母系传承|Matrimoine refers to the inheritance or legacy passed down through the female line of a family, encompassing cultural practices, traditions, knowledge, and possessions.  It contrasts with patrimony, which focuses on the paternal line.  It's a relatively new term gaining traction in feminist and anthropological studies to highlight the often-overlooked contributions of women in shaping family history and cultural identity.|The matrimoine of the family included intricate weaving techniques passed down for generations.|近义词：heritage, legacy, inheritance|反义词：patrimony
