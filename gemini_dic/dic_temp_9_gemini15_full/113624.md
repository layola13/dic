machiavellian:/ˌmækɪəˈvɛliən/| adj.|马基雅维利的；权谋的；狡诈的|Characterized by cunning, deceit, and a lack of moral principles in political activities.  It refers to the principles of governance advocated by Niccolò Machiavelli in his book "The Prince," which often involve the use of manipulation and deception to achieve political goals.  |The CEO's Machiavellian tactics allowed him to outmaneuver his competitors and secure the lucrative contract.|近义词：cunning, devious, scheming, manipulative, unscrupulous|反义词：honest, upright, virtuous, ethical, principled|
