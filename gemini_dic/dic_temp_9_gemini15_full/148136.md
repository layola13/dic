overselling:/ˌoʊvərˈsɛlɪŋ/ | n. | 过度销售；吹嘘； |The act of selling more of something than is available or desirable, or of exaggerating its qualities to make it seem more attractive than it actually is.  |例句：The company was accused of overselling its new product, leading to customer dissatisfaction.  这家公司被指控夸大其词地宣传其新产品，导致客户不满。|近义词：hype, exaggeration, puffery |反义词：underselling, downplaying


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company oversells its products every year. = 公司每年都过度销售其产品。
He oversells his abilities in job interviews. = 他在求职面试中夸大了自己的能力。


一般过去时:
They oversold the tickets for the concert. = 他们过度销售了音乐会的门票。
She oversold the benefits of the new software. = 她夸大了新软件的优点。


现在完成时:
The airline has overbooked and oversold its flights. = 这家航空公司超额预订并过度销售了航班。
We have oversold our inventory this quarter. = 本季度我们过度销售了库存。


过去完成时:
They had oversold their capacity before the peak season. = 他们在旺季之前就已经过度销售了他们的产能。
The salesman had overpromised and oversold the features of the car. = 销售员夸大了汽车的功能，过度销售了。


将来时:
They will oversell the tickets if they don't increase the capacity. = 如果他们不增加产能，他们将会过度销售门票。
The marketing team anticipates they will oversell the new phone. = 营销团队预计新款手机将会过度销售。


虚拟语气:
If they had not oversold the tickets, there would have been enough seats for everyone. = 如果他们没有过度销售门票，每个人都会有足够的座位。
I wish they hadn't oversold the product's capabilities. = 我真希望他们没有夸大产品的性能。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
