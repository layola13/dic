rack-up:/ˈrækaʊp/| vt.|累积；达到；获得；(非正式)痛击；|to accumulate or achieve a large amount or number of something.  It can also mean to inflict a defeat on someone.|例句：The company racked up record profits last year.  这家公司去年获得了创纪录的利润。|例句：He racked up three victories in a row. 他连续赢得了三场胜利。|近义词：accumulate, amass, achieve, gain, score|反义词：lose, decrease, diminish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They rack up points in the game. = 他们在游戏中累积积分。
She racks up frequent flyer miles. = 她积累了大量的飞行里程。


一般过去时:
The team racked up a decisive victory. = 这支队伍取得了决定性的胜利。
He racked up a huge debt. = 他欠下了巨额债务。


现在完成时:
We have racked up significant progress. = 我们已经取得了显著的进步。
She has racked up many awards. = 她已经获得了许多奖项。


过去完成时:
They had racked up considerable losses before the merger. = 在合并之前，他们已经积累了相当大的损失。
The athlete had racked up several injuries before the competition. = 运动员在比赛前已经积累了几处伤病。


将来时:
We will rack up more sales next quarter. = 我们下个季度将获得更多销售额。
They will rack up a lot of experience this summer. = 他们今年夏天将积累很多经验。


虚拟语气:
If he played better, he would rack up more points. = 如果他打得更好，他会得到更多积分。
If she had worked harder, she would have racked up more achievements. = 如果她更努力工作，她本可以取得更多成就。


被动语态:
Many points were racked up by the winning team. = 获胜的队伍积累了很多积分。
Significant losses were racked up by the company last year. = 公司去年累计了巨大的损失。


疑问句:
How many points did they rack up? = 他们积累了多少积分？
How much debt has he racked up? = 他欠了多少钱？


否定句:
They didn't rack up enough votes to win. = 他们没有获得足够的选票来获胜。
The company didn't rack up the expected profits. = 公司没有获得预期的利润。
