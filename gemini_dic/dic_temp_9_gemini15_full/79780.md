gluts:/ɡlʌts/| n.|供应过剩；暴饮暴食|An excessive supply or amount of something.|The market is glutted with cheap imports. 市场充斥着廉价进口商品。|近义词：surplus, excess, plethora|反义词：shortage, scarcity


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The market gluts with cheap goods. = 市场充斥着廉价商品。
This restaurant gluts its customers with food. = 这家餐馆让顾客吃撑了。


一般过去时:
The harvest glutted the market with apples. = 收获使市场苹果供应过剩。
He glutted himself on sweets. = 他暴饮暴食糖果。


现在完成时:
The government has glutted the market with subsidized products. = 政府用补贴产品充斥了市场。
She has glutted herself on chocolate. = 她吃撑了巧克力。


过去完成时:
The previous year's harvest had glutted the market, leading to low prices. = 前一年的收成导致市场供应过剩，从而导致价格低廉。
He had glutted himself before the party, so he didn't eat much. = 他在聚会前已经吃撑了，所以没吃多少。


将来时:
The new factory will glut the market with cheap toys. = 新工厂将用廉价玩具充斥市场。
I think I will glut myself on pizza tonight. = 我想今晚我要痛快地吃比萨。


虚拟语气:
If the factory opened, it would glut the market. = 如果工厂开业，它将使市场供应过剩。
I wish I hadn't glutted myself on that cake. = 我真希望我没有吃那么多蛋糕。


被动语态:
The market was glutted with low-quality goods. = 市场充斥着低质量的商品。
He was glutted with information. = 他被信息淹没了。


疑问句:
Is the market glutted with oil? = 市场石油供应过剩吗？
Did he glut himself at the buffet? = 他在自助餐上吃撑了吗？


否定句:
The market isn't glutted with fresh produce. = 市场并非新鲜农产品供应过剩。
He didn't glut himself at the feast. = 他在盛宴上没有吃撑。
