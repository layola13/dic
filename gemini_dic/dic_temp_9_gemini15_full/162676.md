preassumptions:/ˌpriːəˈsʌmpʃənz/| n. |预设，预先假设|Assumptions made before any evidence is available.  These assumptions often influence how we interpret subsequent information.  Preassumptions can be helpful in guiding investigations but can also lead to bias if not carefully considered.|例句：The researchers' preassumptions about the effectiveness of the new drug influenced their interpretation of the trial results.  研究人员对新药有效性的预设影响了他们对试验结果的解读。|近义词：presuppositions, preconceptions, prior assumptions|反义词：objective observations, empirical evidence
