overanxiety: /ˌoʊvəræŋˈzaɪəti/ | n. |过度焦虑，忧虑过度 | Excessive anxiety; worry to an excessive degree.  It often manifests as a feeling of being overwhelmed by worry and concern. |例句：Her overanxiety about the exam prevented her from sleeping the night before.  她对考试的过度焦虑让她前一晚睡不着觉。

例句：His overanxiety about his job performance led to burnout. 他对工作表现的过度焦虑导致了倦怠。


近义词：hyperanxiety, excessive worry, undue concern, apprehension, nervousness

反义词：calmness, composure, serenity, peace of mind


词源:  The word "overanxiety" is a compound word formed from "over-" (meaning "excessively" or "too much") and "anxiety" (a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease, typically about an imminent event or something with an uncertain outcome).  "Anxiety" itself originates from the Latin word "anxietas," meaning "uneasiness" or "trouble."


用法说明：

Overanxiety is typically used as a noun and describes a state of being.  It's often used to describe a condition where worry is excessive and negatively impacts a person's well-being.  It's not commonly used as a verb or adjective.  The word implies that the anxiety is beyond a normal or healthy level.
