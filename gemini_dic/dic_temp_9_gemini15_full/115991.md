marshaler:/ˈmɑːrʃələr/ | n. | 司仪；整理者；调解人 | A person who marshals or arranges something, especially a parade or ceremony; someone who organizes or controls a process or system. | The parade marshaler ensured that the floats were in the correct order.  阅兵司仪确保花车按正确的顺序排列。 | organizer, coordinator, arranger | disorganizer, disruptor | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The marshaler organizes the parade every year. = 司仪每年都组织游行。
He marshals the resources efficiently. = 他有效地调配资源。

一般过去时:
The marshaler organized the event flawlessly. = 司仪完美地组织了这次活动。
She marshaled her arguments convincingly. = 她令人信服地陈述了她的论点。

现在完成时:
The marshaler has marshaled many successful events. = 司仪已经组织了许多成功的活动。
He has marshaled his evidence carefully. = 他仔细地整理了他的证据。

过去完成时:
The marshaler had already marshaled the troops before the signal. = 司仪在信号发出之前就已经整顿好了军队。
She had marshaled her thoughts before presenting her proposal. = 在提交提案之前，她已经整理好了思路。
