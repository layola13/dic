eudemonist:/juːˈdiːmənɪst/| n. |幸福论者，追求快乐主义者 |A eudemonist is someone who believes that happiness or well-being is the highest good and the ultimate aim of human life.  Eudemonists focus on living a virtuous life to achieve eudaimonia, often translated as flourishing or living well.  Their philosophy emphasizes living in accordance with virtue and reason to achieve a fulfilling life. |Example: Many ancient Greek philosophers, such as Aristotle, were eudemonists who believed that virtue was essential for achieving eudaimonia.  许多古代希腊哲学家，例如亚里士多德，都是幸福论者，他们认为美德对于实现幸福至关重要。|近义词：hedonist (享乐主义者),  |反义词：nihilist (虚无主义者), ascetic (禁欲主义者)
