prepose:/priːˈpəʊz/| vt.|提前提出；预先提出；把…放在前面|To put or place before; to propose beforehand; to suggest or offer beforehand.|例句：He preposed the idea of a meeting next week. 他提前提出了下周开会的想法。|例句：The architect preposed several design options. 建筑师预先提出了几个设计方案。|近义词：propose, suggest, antepose|反义词：postpose


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He preposes a new plan every month. = 他每个月都提出一个新计划。
They prepose their concerns to the manager. = 他们向经理提出了他们的担忧。

一般过去时:
She preposed a solution to the problem. = 她提出了一项解决问题的方案。
The committee preposed a new set of rules. = 委员会提出了一套新的规章制度。

现在完成时:
He has preposed several amendments to the bill. = 他已经为该法案提出了几项修正案。
They have preposed a significant change in strategy. = 他们已经提出了重大的战略变化。

过去完成时:
She had preposed the topic for discussion before the meeting. = 她早在会议之前就提出了讨论的话题。
They had preposed a compromise before negotiations began. = 在谈判开始之前，他们已经提出了一个妥协方案。

将来时:
He will prepose a new marketing campaign next quarter. = 下个季度他将提出一个新的市场营销活动。
They will prepose a revised budget at the next meeting. = 他们将在下次会议上提出修改后的预算。

虚拟语气:
If he preposed a better solution, they would have accepted it. = 如果他提出一个更好的解决方案，他们就会接受它。
I wish he had preposed this idea earlier. = 我希望他早点提出这个主意。

被动语态:
A new policy was preposed by the government. = 政府提出了一项新政策。
Several options were preposed for consideration. = 提出了几个备选方案供考虑。

疑问句:
What did he prepose at the meeting? = 他在会议上提出了什么？
What will they prepose as a solution? = 他们会提出什么解决方案？

否定句:
He did not prepose any changes to the original plan. = 他没有对原计划提出任何修改。
They didn't prepose a different approach. = 他们没有提出不同的方法。
