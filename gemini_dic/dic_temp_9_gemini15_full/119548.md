microspectroscope:/ˌmaɪkroʊˈspɛktrəskoʊp/| n. |显微光谱仪|A microspectroscope is a device that combines a microscope with a spectroscope, allowing for the analysis of the spectral properties of microscopic samples.  It is used to identify the chemical composition and structure of very small samples by analyzing the light they absorb, transmit, or reflect. |例句：Researchers used a microspectroscope to analyze the pigments in a single pollen grain. 研究人员用显微光谱仪分析了一粒花粉中的色素。

The microspectroscope allowed them to determine the precise chemical makeup of the ancient artifact. 显微光谱仪使他们能够确定古代文物的精确化学成分。|近义词：micro-spectrometer, spectroscopic microscope|反义词：无
