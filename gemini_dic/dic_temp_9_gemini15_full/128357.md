neutrino:/nuːˈtriːnoʊ/| n. | 微中子 | A neutrino is a subatomic particle that has very little mass and no electric charge.  It interacts very weakly with matter, making it extremely difficult to detect. Neutrinos are produced in a number of ways, including nuclear reactions in the sun and other stars, and in nuclear reactors.  | Example:  Scientists are studying neutrinos to learn more about the universe.  科学家们正在研究中微子以了解更多关于宇宙的知识。  |近义词: lepton (a broader category)|反义词: None (no direct opposite)|
