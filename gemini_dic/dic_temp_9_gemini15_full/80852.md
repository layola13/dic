gourds:/ɡɔːrdz/| n. |葫芦；瓢瓜 |The word "gourds" refers to the fruit of various plants in the family Cucurbitaceae, typically having a hard rind and often used for containers or decoration.  They are diverse in size, shape, and color.  Many are edible, while others are grown primarily for their ornamental value. |例句：Many different kinds of gourds were displayed at the autumn festival. 秋季节日展出了许多不同种类的葫芦。|例句：She carved beautiful faces into the dried gourds. 她把干葫芦雕刻成美丽的脸庞。|近义词：melons, pumpkins (in some contexts) |反义词：None specifically, but "gourds" could be contrasted with fruits lacking a hard rind.
