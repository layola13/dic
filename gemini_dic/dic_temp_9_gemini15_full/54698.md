disorganize:/dɪsˈɔːrɡənaɪz/| vt.|使混乱；使无组织；扰乱|To throw into disorder; to deprive of organization or system.  It implies a disruption of an existing order or structure. |例句：The sudden storm disorganized the team's schedule. 突如其来的暴风雨打乱了团队的行程。
例句：Years of neglect had disorganized the once-efficient filing system. 多年的忽视使曾经高效的归档系统变得混乱不堪。|近义词：disarrange, upset, jumble, confuse|反义词：organize, arrange, order, systematize|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disorganizes his work space every day. = 他每天都把他的工作空间弄得乱七八糟。
They disorganize the meeting by constantly interrupting. = 他们不断打断会议，使会议混乱不堪。


一般过去时:
The unexpected power outage disorganized the entire production line. = 意外的停电打乱了整个生产线。
She disorganized her notes when she panicked. = 她惊慌失措时把笔记弄乱了。


现在完成时:
The constant changes have disorganized the company's marketing strategy. =  不断的变化已经打乱了公司的营销策略。
Years of procrastination have disorganized his life. = 多年的拖延已经打乱了他的生活。


过去完成时:
They had disorganized the evidence before the police arrived. = 警方到达之前，他们已经把证据弄乱了。
He had disorganized his files, making it difficult to find the important document. = 他把文件弄乱了，很难找到重要的文件。


将来时:
The upcoming storm will disorganize air travel. = 即将来临的风暴将会扰乱空中交通。
I fear that the new software will disorganize our workflow. = 我担心新软件会扰乱我们的工作流程。


虚拟语气:
If he had not disorganized the files, we would have found the report easily. = 如果他没有把文件弄乱，我们就能很容易找到报告。
I wish I hadn't disorganized my desk; now I can't find my keys. = 我真希望我没有把我的桌子弄乱；现在我找不到我的钥匙了。


被动语态:
The schedule was disorganized by the unexpected delay. =  计划由于意外延误而被打乱。
The team's efforts were disorganized by the lack of clear communication. = 团队的努力由于缺乏清晰的沟通而变得混乱。


疑问句:
Did the change in leadership disorganize the company? = 领导层的变动是否打乱了公司？
Will the new policy disorganize the current system? = 新政策会打乱当前的系统吗？


否定句:
The manager ensured that the project was not disorganized. = 经理确保项目没有被打乱。
We tried our best to not disorganize the meeting. = 我们尽力不让会议混乱。
