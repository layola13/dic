pretesting: /ˈpriːˌtɛstɪŋ/ | n. | 预测试，试运行 | The act of testing something before its official launch or use to identify and fix potential problems.  It is often used in research, marketing, and software development. | 例句：The company conducted extensive pretesting of the new software before its release. 公司在发布新软件之前对其进行了广泛的预测试。

例句：We're pretesting the survey questions to ensure clarity and accuracy. 我们正在预测试调查问卷问题以确保清晰度和准确性。


近义词：pilot testing, trial run, dry run, pre-testing


反义词：post-testing


词性：名词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The team is pretesting the new marketing campaign. = 团队正在预测试新的营销活动。
They pretest all their products before launch. = 他们在发布所有产品之前都会进行预测试。

一般过去时:
We pretested the website design last week. = 我们上周对网站设计进行了预测试。
The researchers pretested the questionnaire with a small group. = 研究人员用一小群人对问卷进行了预测试。

现在完成时:
They have pretested the software several times. = 他们已经对软件进行了数次预测试。
We have pretested the marketing materials and are ready to launch. = 我们已经预测试了营销材料，并准备发布。

过去完成时:
They had pretested the prototype before presenting it to the board. = 他们在向董事会展示原型之前已经对其进行了预测试。
The developers had pretested the app extensively before its release. = 开发人员在发布应用程序之前对其进行了广泛的预测试。


将来时:
We will pretest the new feature next month. = 我们下个月将对新功能进行预测试。
They will pretest the campaign in a few key markets. = 他们将在几个主要市场预测试该活动。


虚拟语气:
If we had pretested the system, we would have found the bug earlier. = 如果我们对系统进行了预测试，我们就会更早地发现错误。
It would have been better if they had pretested the product more thoroughly. = 如果他们对产品进行了更彻底的预测试，那就更好了。

被动语态:
The survey was pretested with a diverse sample group. = 调查是用一个多样化的样本组进行预测试的。
The new software is being pretested by the development team. = 新软件正在由开发团队进行预测试。


疑问句:
Has the product been pretested? = 产品是否已经过预测试？
Will the questionnaire be pretested before distribution? = 问卷在分发之前会进行预测试吗？


否定句:
They did not pretest the software before releasing it. = 他们在发布软件之前没有进行预测试。
The company didn't pretest the marketing campaign properly. = 公司没有正确地预测试营销活动。


PHRASE->

词源:  The word "pretesting" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the verb "testing."  It emerged as a technical term in the mid-20th century, particularly in fields like market research and experimental psychology.
