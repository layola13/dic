unstable:/ʌnˈsteɪbəl/| adj. |不稳定的，不稳固的 |lacking stability; likely to change or fail; easily upset emotionally.  |例句：The chair is unstable and likely to topple over. 这把椅子不稳，容易倒。

The political situation in the country is highly unstable.  该国的政治局势极不稳定。

近义词：unsteady, shaky, precarious, erratic, volatile |反义词：stable, steady, firm, secure, constant


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The bridge is unstable. = 这座桥不稳定。
The economy is currently unstable. = 目前经济不稳定。

一般过去时:
The building was unstable after the earthquake. = 地震后，这座建筑物不稳定。
His mental state was unstable during that period. = 在那段时间里，他的精神状态不稳定。

现在完成时:
The situation has become unstable recently. = 最近情况变得不稳定了。
The government has been unstable for years. = 政府多年来一直不稳定。

过去完成时:
The ground had become unstable before the landslide. = 山体滑坡前，地面已经变得不稳定了。
The relationship had been unstable for months before they broke up. = 在他们分手前的几个月里，他们的关系一直不稳定。

将来时:
The weather forecast predicts that the weather will become unstable tomorrow. = 天气预报预测明天天气将变得不稳定。
The company's position will become unstable if they don't address the issues. = 如果他们不解决这些问题，公司的处境将会变得不稳定。

虚拟语气:
If the structure were unstable, it would collapse. = 如果结构不稳定，它就会倒塌。
I wish the political climate weren't so unstable. = 我希望政治气候不要这么不稳定。

被动语态:
The building was deemed unstable by the inspectors. = 这座建筑物被检查员认定为不稳定。
The situation is being monitored for signs of instability. = 正在监测情况，以寻找不稳定的迹象。

疑问句:
Is the situation unstable? = 情况不稳定吗？
Is the ground unstable here? = 这儿的地面不稳定吗？

否定句:
The chair is not unstable; it's quite sturdy. = 这把椅子不稳固；它相当结实。
The government is not unstable; it's quite strong. = 政府并不动荡；它相当强大。
