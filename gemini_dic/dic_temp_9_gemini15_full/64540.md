errantia: /ɛˈrænʃiə/ | n. | 游走生物；漂游生物 |  Errant animals, especially those that wander or roam. The term is primarily used in zoology to describe a group of invertebrates, particularly within the phylum Annelida (segmented worms) and other phyla, characterized by their free-living and wandering lifestyle. They lack a permanent attachment to a substrate or burrow.  | Example: Many polychaete worms are errantia, actively searching for food in the ocean.  许多多毛类蠕虫是游走生物，在海洋中积极寻找食物。| 近义词: wanderer, rover, vagrant | 反义词: sedentary, sessile
