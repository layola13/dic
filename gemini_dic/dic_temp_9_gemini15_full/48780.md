deflationists: /dɪˈfleɪʃənɪsts/ | n. | 通货紧缩论者，紧缩主义者 |  Those who believe in or advocate deflation as a desirable economic policy. They often argue that deflation can lead to greater economic stability and efficiency by curbing inflation and encouraging saving.  | The deflationists argued for a reduction in the money supply to combat rising prices. 通货紧缩论者主张减少货币供应量以对抗物价上涨。  | inflationists (反通货膨胀论者) | inflationists (通货膨胀论者)
