disperse:/dɪˈspɜːrs/| vt. vi. n.|驱散，散布，分散|to cause something to spread over a wide area or to become less concentrated; to move apart and go in different directions|例句：The police dispersed the crowd.  警察驱散了人群。

The seeds were dispersed by the wind.  种子被风吹散了。


近义词：scatter, spread, dissipate, diffuse|反义词：gather, collect, concentrate|词性:动词

词源:
The word "disperse" comes from the Latin word "dispersus," the past participle of "dispergere," meaning "to scatter."  "Dis-" is a prefix meaning "apart" or "away," and "-pers-" is related to the Latin word "spargere," meaning "to scatter" or "to sprinkle."
