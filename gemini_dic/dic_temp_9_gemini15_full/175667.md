rants:/rænts/| n. v.| 咆哮；抱怨；激烈的演说 |  A rant is a long, angry speech of criticism or complaint.  It can be a noun referring to the speech itself, or a verb describing the act of giving such a speech. | 例句：He went on a rant about the unfairness of the system. 他开始喋喋不休地抱怨制度的不公平。

He ranted and raved about the terrible service. 他愤怒地咆哮着糟糕的服务。|近义词：diatribe, tirade, harangue |反义词：praise, compliment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rants about politics every day. = 他每天都对政治大发牢骚。
She often rants to her friends about her boss. = 她经常向朋友们抱怨她的老板。


一般过去时:
He ranted at the meeting yesterday. = 他昨天在会议上大发雷霆。
She ranted about the injustice of the situation. = 她对事情的不公正大加指责。


现在完成时:
He has ranted about this issue for years. = 他多年来一直在抱怨这个问题。
She has ranted and raved at every opportunity. = 她抓住每一个机会大发雷霆。


过去完成时:
He had ranted for an hour before anyone intervened. = 他已经抱怨了一个小时，然后才有人介入。
She had ranted and stormed before finally calming down. = 她发脾气并大吵了一顿，最后才平静下来。


将来时:
He will rant and rave if he doesn't get his way. = 如果他不遂愿，他会大发雷霆。
She will likely rant about the movie later. = 她很可能会在稍后抱怨这部电影。


虚拟语气:
If he ranted less, people would listen more. = 如果他少抱怨一些，人们就会多听一些。
I wish he wouldn't rant so much. = 我希望他不至于抱怨那么多。


被动语态:
His complaints were often ranted about by the media. = 他的抱怨经常被媒体大肆报道。
The issue was ranted about endlessly online. = 这个问题在网上被没完没了地抱怨。


疑问句:
Is he ranting again? = 他又在抱怨了吗？
Why is she ranting so loudly? = 她为什么这么大声地抱怨？


否定句:
He didn't rant at all during the meeting. = 他在会议上根本没有抱怨。
She didn't rant, but she expressed her displeasure clearly. = 她没有大发雷霆，但她清楚地表达了不满。


PHRASE->
go on a rant = 开始大发牢骚
rant and rave = 大声抱怨；怒吼
rant about something = 抱怨某事
a drunken rant = 酒后胡言乱语
a political rant = 政治上的激烈言论
