conglutinative:/kənˈɡluːtɪnətɪv/| adj.|胶合的；凝集的；粘合的|  Relating to or characterized by the process of conglutination, the act of gluing or sticking together; tending to stick together. The word often describes substances that clump or adhere to each other, or processes that cause this adhesion.|The conglutinative properties of the glue made it ideal for repairing broken pottery.|近义词：adhesive, agglutinative, cohesive|反义词：separative, dispersive

The conglutinative nature of the clay made it difficult to separate the individual pieces.  粘土的胶合性使得难以分离各个碎片。

词源:

From conglutinate + -ive.  Conglutinate comes from Latin *conglutinare, from con- "together" + glutinare "to glue," from gluten "glue."
