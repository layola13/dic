derogations:/ˌdɛrəˈɡeɪʃənz/| n.|  derogation 的复数形式；减损；贬低；例外；违反 |  The plural form of derogation, referring to instances of lessening, belittling, or exceptions to a rule or law.  It can imply a lowering of status, value, or dignity, or a departure from an established standard. |例句：The government's numerous derogations from the treaty caused international concern. = 政府对条约的多次违反引起了国际关注。


The numerous derogations from the company's ethical code led to a loss of public trust. = 公司对道德准则的多次违反导致公众信任的丧失。|近义词：exceptions, infringements, violations, diminutions|反义词：affirmations, enhancements, upholdings|名词

词源:
From the Latin word "derogatio," meaning "a taking away," "a lessening."  It combines "de-" (away, down) and "rogatio" (asking, proposal).  The sense of a lessening or exception developed from the original meaning of a formal act of repealing or modifying a law.
