entryways:/ˈɛntriˌweɪz/| n.| 入口处；通道；入口 |  Places where one enters a building or area.  Entryways can be hallways, doorways, or even open spaces leading into a room or building. | Example: The entryways to the museum were crowded with visitors.  博物馆的入口挤满了游客。 |近义词：entrances, access points, gateways |反义词：exits, outlets


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The entryway leads to the living room. = 入口通往客厅。
There are two entryways to the building. = 这栋楼有两个入口。

一般过去时:
The entryway was blocked by a fallen tree. = 入口被倒下的树木挡住了。
We entered through the back entryway. = 我们从后门进入。

They have redesigned the entryway to the store. = 他们已经重新设计了商店的入口。
We have never used this entryway before. = 我们以前从未使用过这个入口。
