exclusionary:/ɪkˈskluːʒənəri/ | adj. | 排斥的；排外的；不包括的 |  Excluding or tending to exclude certain people or groups.  It implies a deliberate act of keeping someone or something out. |

例句：

The club's exclusionary membership policies led to protests.  俱乐部的排斥性会员政策导致了抗议。

His exclusionary remarks alienated many of his colleagues. 他排斥性的言论疏远了他的许多同事。


近义词：discriminatory, restrictive, sectarian, elitist

反义词：inclusive, welcoming, open, broad-minded


词源:

From "exclusion" + "-ary".  "Exclusion" comes from the Latin *excludere, meaning "to shut out," from *ex- ("out") + *claudere ("to shut").  The suffix "-ary" forms adjectives.
