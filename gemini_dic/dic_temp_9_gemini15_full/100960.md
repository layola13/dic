inures:/ɪˈnjʊərz/ | vt. | 使习惯；使适应；使不受影响 | To become used to something unpleasant or difficult.  | 例句：He inured himself to the cold by spending hours outdoors each day. 他通过每天在户外待数小时来使自己适应寒冷。  | 例句：Years of hardship had inured her to pain. 多年的苦难使她对痛苦习以为常。| 近义词：accustom, habituate, harden | 反义词：sensitize, weaken | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He inures himself to the cold. = 他使自己习惯寒冷。
The company inures its employees to long working hours. = 公司使员工习惯长时间工作。

一般过去时:
She inured herself to the harsh conditions. = 她使自己习惯了恶劣的环境。
The soldiers inured themselves to the dangers of war. = 士兵们使自己习惯了战争的危险。

现在完成时:
He has inured himself to criticism. = 他已经习惯了批评。
She has inured herself to the pain of loss. = 她已经习惯了失去的痛苦。

过去完成时:
They had inured themselves to the rigors of the journey. = 他们已经习惯了旅途的艰辛。
He had inured himself to the loneliness of his work. = 他已经习惯了他工作的孤独。

将来时:
She will inure herself to the demands of her new job. = 她将使自己适应新工作的要求。
They will inure themselves to the heat of the desert. = 他们将使自己适应沙漠的炎热。

虚拟语气:
If I were to live in the Arctic, I would inure myself to the cold. = 如果我要住在北极，我将使自己习惯寒冷。
I wish I could inure myself to the stress of my job more quickly. = 我希望我能更快地使自己适应工作的压力。

被动语态:
He was inured to hardship by his upbringing. = 他从小就习惯了艰苦。
The troops were inured to the harsh climate. = 部队已经习惯了恶劣的气候。

疑问句:

否定句:
