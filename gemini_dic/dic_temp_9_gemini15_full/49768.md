demonetization: /ˌdiːˌmɒnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | /ˌdiːˌmɑːnɪtəˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |货币贬值；停止流通；废除货币| The act of stripping a currency unit of its status as legal tender.  This often involves replacing old banknotes and coins with new ones, or removing a denomination from circulation altogether.  It can be done for various reasons, such as combating inflation, preventing counterfeiting, or cleaning up the monetary system. |例句：The government announced a surprise demonetization of the 500 and 1000 rupee notes. = 政府宣布意外废除500和1000卢比的纸币。

The demonetization policy led to long queues at banks. = 货币贬值政策导致银行排起了长队。


近义词：currency reform, monetary reform, withdrawal of currency | 反义词：monetization


词源:  From "demonetize" + "-ation". "Demonetize" is formed from the prefix "de-" (meaning "to remove" or "to deprive of") + "monetize" (from "money" + "-ize," a suffix forming verbs).  Ultimately, the word traces back to the Latin word "moneta," referring to a Roman mint and hence, money.
