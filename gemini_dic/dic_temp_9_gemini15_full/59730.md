ECMO:/ˈɛkmoʊ/| n. |体外膜肺氧合|Extracorporeal membrane oxygenation; a life support system that does the work of the heart and lungs.  It temporarily replaces the function of these organs by removing blood from the body, adding oxygen, and removing carbon dioxide before returning the oxygenated blood to the body.|例句：The patient was placed on ECMO after suffering a massive heart attack.  病人遭受严重心脏病发作后被置于ECMO支持下。|近义词：cardiopulmonary bypass (CPB), extracorporeal life support (ECLS)|反义词：None (ECMO is a life support technology, not something with a direct opposite)|名词
