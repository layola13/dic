sabre-rattling: /ˈseɪbərˌrætlɪŋ/ | n. | 炫耀武力；战争威胁；恐吓；虚张声势 | The act of making aggressive or threatening statements or actions, especially in international relations, to intimidate or provoke a reaction. It often involves a display of military power without actual intention of using it.  |例句：The sabre-rattling from both countries increased tensions in the region.  两国间的炫耀武力加剧了该地区的紧张局势。

例句：The recent military exercises were seen as a clear act of sabre-rattling. 最近的军事演习被视为赤裸裸的战争威胁。


近义词：intimidation, threat, bluster, posturing, hectoring, bullying

反义词：peacemaking, appeasement, conciliation, diplomacy


词源:  The term comes from the image of rattling a sabre (a cavalry sword) as a demonstration of aggression or readiness for combat.  "Sabre" comes from the French word "sabre," ultimately of Eastern origin. "Rattling" refers to the shaking or noisy movement.
