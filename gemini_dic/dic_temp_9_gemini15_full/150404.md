panproctocolectomy:/ˌpænˌprɒktoʊkəˈlɛktəmi/ | n. | 全直肠切除术 | The complete surgical removal of the rectum and colon.  This procedure is typically performed for severe inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), such as ulcerative colitis or Crohn's disease, or for colorectal cancer.  |  The surgeon performed a panproctocolectomy to remove the cancerous tumor. = 外科医生进行了全直肠切除术以切除癌性肿瘤。

The patient underwent a panproctocolectomy due to severe ulcerative colitis. = 由于严重的溃疡性结肠炎，患者接受了全直肠切除术。


近义词: total proctocolectomy, colectomy with proctectomy | 反义词: None (it's a surgical procedure, not something with an antonym) | 词性: noun

词源:
* **pan-**:  Greek prefix meaning "all," "entire," or "complete."
* **procto-**: Greek  "proktos," meaning "rectum."
* **cole-**: Greek "kolon," meaning "colon."
* **-ectomy**:  Greek suffix "-ektomē," meaning "surgical excision" or "removal."

Therefore, panproctocolectomy literally means the complete surgical removal of the rectum and colon.
