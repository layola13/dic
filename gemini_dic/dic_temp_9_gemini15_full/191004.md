scalping:/ˈskælpɪŋ/| n. v.|恶意抬高价格；剥头皮；投机倒卖|The act of buying and selling something quickly to profit from price fluctuations, often involving high risk.|例句：He made a fortune through scalping tickets to the concert. 他通过倒卖演唱会门票赚了一大笔钱。|近义词：profiteering, speculation, gouging|反义词：fair pricing, reasonable pricing


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He scalps tickets to popular events. = 他倒卖热门活动的票。
They scalp stocks to make quick profits. = 他们炒股以快速获利。


一般过去时:
She scalped the tickets and resold them at a higher price. = 她倒卖了门票，并以更高的价格转售。
The investors scalped the stock market and lost everything. = 投资者炒股，结果赔光了所有钱。


现在完成时:
He has scalped hundreds of tickets this year. = 他今年已经倒卖了数百张票。
They have scalped their way to financial ruin. = 他们通过投机倒卖走向了经济破产。


过去完成时:
The police had already caught the scalpers before the game started. = 在比赛开始之前，警方已经抓到了黄牛党。
He had scalped many shares before the crash. = 在股市崩盘之前，他已经倒卖了许多股票。


将来时:
They will scalp the new iPhone when it is released. = 新iPhone发布时，他们会进行倒卖。
He will scalp the shares and make some money. = 他将倒卖股票并赚一些钱。


虚拟语气:
If he had more money, he would scalp more tickets. = 如果他更有钱，他会倒卖更多门票。
If I were him, I would not scalp tickets. = 如果我是他，我不会倒卖门票。


被动语态:
The tickets were scalped before they could be purchased legally. = 门票在合法购买之前就被黄牛倒卖了。

疑问句:
Is he scalping tickets again? = 他又在倒卖门票了吗？

否定句:
He doesn’t scalp tickets; he buys them legitimately. = 他不倒卖门票；他合法购买。
They didn’t scalp the shares, they invested long-term. = 他们没有炒股，而是进行长期投资。
