impliable:/ɪmˈplaɪəbl/ | adj. | 可暗示的；可暗指的 |  capable of being implied; capable of being inferred or suggested without being explicitly stated.  This word describes something that can be understood or suggested indirectly, rather than being stated directly.  |The contract contained several impliable terms.  合同包含一些可以暗指的条款。|  implicit, inferable, suggested | explicit, stated directly |  形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The contract implies several responsibilities. = 合同暗示着几项责任。
This situation implies a lack of trust. = 这种情况暗示着缺乏信任。

一般过去时:
His silence implied consent. = 他的沉默暗示着同意。
The evidence implied his guilt. = 证据暗示了他的罪行。


现在完成时:
The study has implied a need for further research. = 这项研究暗示需要进一步的研究。
Recent events have implied a change in policy. = 最近的事件暗示了政策的改变。


过去完成时:
The clues implied that the crime had been planned carefully. = 线索暗示犯罪已被精心策划。
Her behavior implied that she had known the truth all along. = 她的行为暗示她一直都知道真相。


将来时:
The results will imply a significant breakthrough. = 结果将暗示一项重大突破。
Future trends will imply a change in consumer behavior. = 未来的趋势将暗示消费者行为的改变。


虚拟语气:
If the results implied a different outcome, we would have to reconsider our approach. = 如果结果暗示不同的结果，我们将不得不重新考虑我们的方法。
If this statement implied a threat, I would take action. = 如果这个声明暗示威胁，我会采取行动。


被动语态:
It was implied that he was responsible. = 暗示他负有责任。
A need for reform was implied by the report. = 报告暗示了改革的必要性。


疑问句:
Does this statement imply anything else? = 这句话暗示着其他什么吗？
What does the author imply by this metaphor?  = 作者用这个隐喻暗示了什么？


否定句:
The statement did not imply any criticism. = 该声明没有暗示任何批评。
The evidence does not imply his involvement. = 证据并没有暗示他的参与。


PHRASE->
imply that... = 暗示...
imply something to someone = 向某人暗示某事
imply a meaning = 暗示一个意思
imply a consequence = 暗示一个后果
to imply strongly = 强烈暗示
