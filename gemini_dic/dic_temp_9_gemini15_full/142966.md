outawing: /ˈaʊtˌlɔːɪŋ/ | v. |  宣布为非法；禁止；驱逐出境 |  The act of making something illegal or prohibiting it;  expelling someone from a place or group. |


英文解析:  "Outawing" is the gerund form of the verb "outlaw," meaning to declare something illegal or to banish someone.  It implies a formal or official act of prohibition.  The word carries a strong sense of making something forbidden and potentially punishable.


例句:

The government is considering outawing the sale of certain weapons.  = 政府正在考虑禁止销售某些武器。

The tribe outawing him for his actions. = 部落因他的行为而将他驱逐。


近义词: prohibiting, banning, forbidding, making illegal,  expelling


反义词: legalizing, permitting, allowing, authorizing


词性: Verb (gerund)
