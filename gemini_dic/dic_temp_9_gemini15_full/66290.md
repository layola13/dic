excoriate:/ɪkˈskɔːrieɪt/ | vt. | 严厉批评；痛斥；剥皮；擦伤 | To criticize severely; to denounce; to flay the skin from; to abrade | 例句：The critic excoriated the author's new novel. = 批评家严厉批评了作者的新小说。


The senator excoriated his opponent for accepting illegal campaign donations. = 参议员痛斥他的对手接受非法竞选捐款。


近义词：castigate, rebuke, lambaste, denounce, flay | 反义词：praise, commend, applaud

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The critic excoriates the new film. = 批评家痛斥这部新电影。
He excoriates his employees for their mistakes. = 他因员工的错误而严厉批评他们。


一般过去时:
The newspaper excoriated the politician's actions. = 报纸严厉批评了这位政治家的行为。
She excoriated him for his dishonesty. = 她因他不诚实而痛斥他。


现在完成时:
The public has excoriated the company for its unethical practices. = 公众因该公司的违反道德的行为而对其进行了严厉批评。
The media has excoriated the government's response to the crisis. = 媒体严厉批评了政府对危机的应对措施。


过去完成时:
The judge had excoriated the defendant for his lies. = 法官因被告说谎而严厉批评了他。
They had excoriated the plan before it was even implemented. = 在计划实施之前，他们就对其进行了痛斥。


将来时:
The professor will excoriate the student for plagiarism. = 教授将因剽窃而严厉批评这位学生。
The shareholders will excoriate the CEO for the company's losses. = 股东们将因公司的损失而严厉批评首席执行官。


虚拟语气:
If I were to excoriate him, he wouldn't understand. = 如果我要严厉批评他，他也不会理解。
I wish I hadn't excoriated her so harshly. = 我真希望我没有那么严厉地批评她。


被动语态:
The policy was excoriated by the opposition party. = 这项政策受到了反对党的严厉批评。
His behavior was excoriated by the media. = 他的行为受到了媒体的严厉批评。


疑问句:
Will the committee excoriate the candidate for his gaffe? = 委员会会因为他的失误而严厉批评这位候选人吗？
Did the boss excoriate you for being late? = 老板因为你迟到而严厉批评你了吗？


否定句:
The review did not excoriate the performance, but it wasn't exactly positive either. = 这篇评论并没有严厉批评这场演出，但它也不是完全正面评价。
The manager didn't excoriate the team, but he expressed his disappointment. = 经理没有严厉批评团队，但他表达了他的失望。


PHRASE->

词源:
From Latin *excoriāre, from ex- "out of" + corium "skin, hide."  The sense of "to criticize severely" developed later.
