sciatherical: /ˌsaɪəˈθɛrɪkəl/ | adj. | 阴凉的；喜阴的 | Relating to or preferring shade or partial shade.  The term is primarily used in botany and horticulture, referring to plants that thrive in shady conditions.  | 例句：Many woodland plants are sciatherical, requiring protection from direct sunlight.  许多林地植物是喜阴的，需要避免阳光直射。


The word sciatherical is less common than its synonym "shade-loving". |近义词：shade-loving, sciophilous, umbrophilic |反义词：heliophilic, sun-loving


词源:  The word is derived from Greek:  "skia" (σκιά) meaning "shade" and "theros" (θερος) meaning "summer" (though its application extends beyond summer). The suffix "-ical" indicates an adjective.
