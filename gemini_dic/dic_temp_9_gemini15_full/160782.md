positioning:/pəˈzɪʃənɪŋ/ | n. |定位，布局，策略性安排 |The act of establishing or defining the position of something or someone.  It often refers to marketing and branding strategies, but can also apply to physical placement or strategic placement in a broader sense. |例句：The company's successful positioning as a luxury brand helped it command higher prices. 公司成功地将自己定位为奢侈品牌，这帮助它获得了更高的价格。

例句：The careful positioning of the furniture maximized the space in the small apartment. 家具的精心摆放使小公寓的空间最大化。


近义词：placement, location, situation, strategy |反义词：misplacement, disorientation


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The marketing team carefully considers the product positioning. = 营销团队仔细考虑产品的定位。
Our current positioning strategy emphasizes quality. = 我们目前的定位策略强调质量。


一般过去时:
They repositioned the brand to attract a younger audience. = 他们重新定位了品牌以吸引更年轻的受众。
The company's initial positioning failed to resonate with consumers. = 公司最初的定位未能引起消费者的共鸣。


现在完成时:
We have successfully positioned ourselves as market leaders. = 我们已经成功地将自己定位为市场领导者。
The brand has undergone several repositioning exercises. = 该品牌已经进行了多次重新定位。


过去完成时:

The company realized that they had misjudged their market positioning. = 公司意识到他们误判了他们的市场定位。


将来时:

We will be focusing on improving our brand positioning. = 我们将专注于改进我们的品牌定位。


虚拟语气:
If we had better positioning, we would have higher sales. = 如果我们的定位更好，我们的销售额就会更高。
It would be beneficial if we repositioned our product line. = 如果我们重新定位我们的产品线，将会有益。


被动语态:
The product is being positioned as eco-friendly. = 该产品被定位为环保产品。
The brand's image was carefully positioned to appeal to a specific demographic. = 该品牌的形象被精心定位以吸引特定的人群。


疑问句:
