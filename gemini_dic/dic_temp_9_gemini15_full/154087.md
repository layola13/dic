perichondritis:/ˌpɛriˌkɒnˈdraɪtɪs/| n. |软骨膜炎|Inflammation of the perichondrium, the membrane covering cartilage, most commonly in the ear.  This inflammation can be caused by infection (bacterial, viral, or fungal), trauma, or autoimmune diseases. Symptoms include pain, swelling, redness, and tenderness to the touch.  |The patient presented with perichondritis of the ear, characterized by significant swelling and erythema.|近义词：ear infection (if caused by infection)|反义词：None specifically, but the absence of inflammation would be the opposite.
