/praɪz/| n. |奖赏，奖品；奖金|A prize is something valuable that is given to someone as a reward for winning a competition or achieving something significant.  It can be an object, money, or recognition.|She won first prize in the science fair. = 她获得了科学展览会的一等奖。|The company offered a large prize for solving the problem. = 公司为解决这个问题提供了一笔巨额奖金。|reward, award|punishment|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company offers a prize for the best design. = 公司为最佳设计提供奖品。
This competition has many valuable prizes. = 这个比赛有很多有价值的奖品。

一般过去时:
He won a prize for his outstanding work. = 他因杰出的工作获得了奖项。
She received a prize at the school ceremony. = 她在学校典礼上获得了一个奖项。

现在完成时:
She has won several prizes this year. = 她今年已经赢得了几个奖项。
They have already announced the prize winners. = 他们已经宣布了获奖者。


过去完成时:
He had already won three prizes before he entered the final competition. = 在他参加决赛之前，他已经赢得了三个奖项。
She had never won a prize so big before. = 她以前从未赢得过这么大的奖项。

将来时:
The winner will receive a cash prize. = 获胜者将获得现金奖励。
They will announce the prizes next week. = 他们将在下周公布获奖名单。

虚拟语气:
If I won the prize, I would travel around the world. = 如果我赢得奖品，我会环游世界。
I wish I had won the prize. = 我真希望我赢得了奖品。

被动语态:
The prize was awarded to the most talented student. = 奖品颁给了最有天赋的学生。
The prizes are being judged at the moment. = 奖项正在评审中。

疑问句:
What is the prize for winning the contest? = 赢得比赛的奖品是什么？
Who won the first prize? = 谁获得了第一名？

否定句:
He didn't win a prize this time. = 他这次没有获奖。
She didn't receive any prize money. = 她没有收到任何奖金。
