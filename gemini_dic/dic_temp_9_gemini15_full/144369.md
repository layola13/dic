outstanding:/ˌaʊtˈstændɪŋ/| adj.|杰出的，突出的，未解决的|excellent; remarkably good; exceptionally good.  Something outstanding is very noticeable because it is extremely good or bad.  It can also mean something that has not been dealt with or settled.|例句：Her performance was outstanding. 她的表演非常出色。

His debts are outstanding.  他的债务未偿还。|近义词：excellent, exceptional, remarkable, prominent, unresolved|反义词：ordinary, mediocre, insignificant, settled


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project is outstanding. = 这个项目很杰出。
This problem is outstanding. = 这个问题未解决。


一般过去时:
His work was outstanding last year. = 他去年的工作非常出色。
The issue remained outstanding until the meeting. = 这个问题一直持续到会议才解决。


现在完成时:
The debt has been outstanding for several months. = 这笔债务已经拖欠了几个月了。
Many questions have remained outstanding after the lecture. = 讲座结束后，许多问题仍然悬而未决。


过去完成时:
The report had been outstanding for two weeks before the deadline. = 报告在截止日期前两周就已完成（并被提交）。
Several tasks had been outstanding before the new manager arrived. = 新经理到来之前，几项任务尚未完成。


将来时:
The results will be outstanding, if all goes well. = 如果一切顺利，结果将非常出色。
The payment will remain outstanding until next month. = 付款将拖延到下个月。


虚拟语气:
If the work were outstanding, he would have been promoted. = 如果他的工作很出色，他就会被提升了。
If the issue had been outstanding, they would have postponed the decision. = 如果问题未解决，他们就会推迟决定。


被动语态:
The problem was outstanding for a long time. = 这个问题长期未解决。
The award was given to the outstanding employee. = 该奖项颁给了杰出的员工。


疑问句:
Are there any outstanding issues? = 还有未解决的问题吗？
Is his performance outstanding? = 他的表现出色吗？


否定句:
The results were not outstanding. = 结果并不出色。
The account is not outstanding. = 账户没有未结余额。
