discredits:/dɪsˈkrɛdɪts/| vt.|使失去名誉；使受怀疑；使不可信；驳斥|To damage the reputation or credibility of someone or something.|The scandal discredited the politician.  这场丑闻使这位政客声名扫地。|His lies discredited his testimony. 他的谎言使他的证词失去了可信度。|近义词：disgrace, discredit, dishonor, shame|反义词：credit, honor, validate


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The media often discredits public figures. = 媒体经常败坏公众人物的名声。
This evidence discredits the defendant's alibi. = 这证据使被告的不在场证明不可信。


一般过去时:
The experiment discredited the old theory. = 这个实验驳斥了旧理论。
His actions discredited the entire organization. = 他的行为使整个组织蒙羞。


现在完成时:
Recent revelations have discredited the company's claims. = 最近的揭露使这家公司的说法失去了可信度。
The scandal has discredited the mayor's administration. = 这场丑闻使市长的政府名誉扫地。


过去完成时:
The evidence had already discredited the witness's testimony before the trial. = 在审判之前，证据已经使证人的证词失去了可信度。
They had discredited the opponent's argument before he could finish. = 在对方说完话之前，他们就已经驳斥了他的论点。


将来时:
This new evidence will discredit the prosecution's case. = 这项新证据将使检方的案情失去可信度。
The upcoming report will likely discredit the government's claims. = 即将来临的报告可能会使政府的说法失去可信度。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, it would discredit the defense's argument. = 如果证据更充分，它就会驳斥辩方的论点。
I wish this report hadn't discredited our research. = 我真希望这份报告没有败坏我们的研究成果。


被动语态:
The politician was discredited by the scandal. = 这位政客因丑闻而名誉扫地。
His theories were discredited by subsequent research. = 他的理论被随后的研究驳斥了。


疑问句:
Will this new evidence discredit the witness? = 这项新证据会使证人失去可信度吗？
Does this information discredit the company's image? = 这个信息会败坏公司的形象吗？


否定句:
The study did not discredit the established theory. = 这项研究并没有驳斥已建立的理论。
Nothing could discredit his unwavering dedication. = 没有什么能败坏他坚定不移的奉献精神。


PHRASE->

fall into discredit = 声名扫地
