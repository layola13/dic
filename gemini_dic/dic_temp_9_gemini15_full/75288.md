frontbenchers:/ˈfrʌntˌbɛntʃərz/| n. |前排议员，要员|Members of a parliament or other legislative body who sit on the front benches, typically representing the government or the official opposition.  They are usually senior members of their party and hold important positions.|Example: The frontbenchers engaged in a heated debate on the new bill. 前排议员就新法案进行了激烈的辩论。|近义词：leading members, senior politicians, key figures|反义词：backbenchers
