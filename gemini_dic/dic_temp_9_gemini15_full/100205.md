interrhyme:/ˈɪntərˌraɪm/| n. |内韵，内部押韵|Internal rhyme, also known as inner rhyme, is a literary device that involves the rhyming of words within a single line of verse, or within multiple lines close together.  It creates a musical effect and adds emphasis to certain words or phrases. Unlike end rhyme, which occurs at the end of lines, interrhyme occurs within a line or within closely positioned lines.|Example:  "The fair breeze blew, the white foam flew," (The words "blew" and "flew" are interrhymes).|近义词：inner rhyme, internal rhyme|反义词：end rhyme
