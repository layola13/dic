hatchet-faced:/ˈhætʃɪtfeɪst/| adj.| 鹰钩鼻的，刻薄相的 | Having a thin, sharp face, often suggesting harshness or ill temper.  The term evokes a face with a prominent, sharp nose and often angular features.  It implies a lack of warmth or friendliness. | Example: The hatchet-faced woman glared at the children.  那个鹰钩鼻的女人怒视着孩子们。  The witness gave a hatchet-faced description of the suspect. 目击者对嫌疑人的描述刻薄尖锐。 | Near synonyms: sharp-featured, severe-looking, grim-faced | Antonyms: pleasant-faced, kind-faced, amiable-looking |  adj.
