hypochondriasis:/ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪəsɪs/| n. | 疑病症 |  A psychological disorder characterized by the persistent belief that one is suffering from a serious illness, despite medical reassurance to the contrary.  People with hypochondriasis may misinterpret normal bodily sensations as symptoms of a disease.  They often spend a significant amount of time and energy seeking medical attention, and may become preoccupied with their health concerns to the detriment of their daily life. | He suffers from hypochondriasis and constantly worries about his health, even after numerous doctor visits. 他患有疑病症，即使看了很多次医生，也总是担心自己的健康。


|近义词：health anxiety, illness anxiety disorder |反义词：None (It's a disorder, not a state that has a direct opposite.)|
