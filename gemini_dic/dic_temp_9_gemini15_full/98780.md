integrable: /ɪnˈtɛɡrəbl/ | adj. | 可积的，可整合的 | Capable of being integrated or integrated; able to be expressed as an integral.  This term is used in mathematics, particularly in calculus and analysis, to describe functions or forms that can be integrated.  | Example: The function f(x) = x² is integrable over any finite interval.  该函数f(x) = x²在任何有限区间上都是可积的。|近义词：integrable, summable | 反义词：non-integrable

词源:
The word "integrable" is derived from the Latin word "integer," meaning "whole" or "untouched."  This relates to the concept of integration as a process of finding a whole from parts.  The suffix "-able" indicates capability or possibility.
