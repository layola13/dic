countermobilization:/ˌkaʊntərˌmoʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |反动员；对动员的抵抗|The organized effort to resist or counteract a social movement or political campaign.  It involves the mobilization of resources and people to oppose the goals and actions of a particular group.  For example, countermobilization might involve lobbying efforts, public protests, or the spread of counter-narratives.|Example: The government's new policy faced strong countermobilization from environmental groups.  政府的新政策遭到了环保组织的强烈反动员。|近义词：opposition, resistance, counter-movement|反义词：mobilization, support
