self-indulger: /ˌsɛlfɪnˈdʌldʒər/ | n. |自我放纵者，纵欲者 | A person who habitually indulges their desires and whims without regard for the consequences.  Someone who prioritizes their own pleasure and gratification above other considerations. | Example: He was a self-indulger, spending lavishly on himself despite his mounting debts.  他是个自我放纵的人，尽管债台高筑，却依然挥霍无度。  |近义词：hedonist, pleasure-seeker, libertine |反义词：ascetic, self-denier, disciplinarian |


词源:

* **self-**:  Prefix meaning "of or relating to oneself."  源自古英语 *self*.
* **indulge**:  Verb meaning "to allow oneself to enjoy the pleasure of."  源自古法语 *endulger*,  最终源自拉丁语 *indulgere* (to be kind, to grant).
* **-er**:  Suffix forming nouns denoting agents or doers of an action.


用法说明:

这个词主要用作名词，指那些习惯性地放纵自己欲望和怪癖的人，而不考虑后果。它通常带有负面含义，暗示缺乏自律和责任感。


例句：

1.  He became a self-indulger after winning the lottery.  赢得彩票后，他变得自我放纵起来。
2.  Many critics saw the character in the novel as a self-indulger.  许多评论家认为小说中的人物是一个自我放纵的人。


(由于“self-indulger”本身就是一个名词，所以不适用动词的各种时态变化。以下部分，以其相关的动词"indulge"为例进行说明。)


USAGE->

一般现在时:
I indulge myself with a cup of coffee every morning. = 我每天早上都用一杯咖啡来犒劳自己。
She indulges in expensive hobbies. = 她沉迷于昂贵的爱好。


一般过去时:
He indulged in chocolate cake after dinner. = 晚饭后他吃了一块巧克力蛋糕。
They indulged their children too much. = 他们对孩子过于溺爱。


现在完成时:
I have indulged myself too much lately. = 我最近太放纵自己了。
She has indulged in several glasses of wine. = 她已经喝了好几杯酒了。


过去完成时:
He had indulged in gambling for years before he lost everything. = 他沉迷于赌博多年，直到输光了一切。
They had indulged their cravings before starting their diet. = 在开始节食之前，他们已经满足了自己的欲望。


将来时:
I will indulge in a long bath tonight. = 今晚我要好好泡个澡。
They will indulge themselves with a luxurious vacation. = 他们将享受一次奢华的假期。


虚拟语气:
If I had the time, I would indulge in a good book. = 如果我有时间，我会沉迷于一本好书。
I wish I could indulge in a long holiday. = 我真希望我能休个长假。


被动语态:
His desires were indulged by his parents. = 他的愿望被父母满足了。
The children were indulged with gifts. = 孩子们得到了很多礼物。


疑问句:
Do you often indulge in sweets? = 你经常吃甜食吗？
Does she indulge herself with expensive clothes? = 她经常买昂贵的衣服来犒劳自己吗？


否定句:
I don't indulge in gossip. = 我不参与八卦。
She doesn't indulge her whims. = 她不放纵自己的怪癖。


PHRASE->

indulge in: 沉迷于；放纵
indulge oneself:  纵容自己
give oneself up to: 沉溺于
treat oneself to:  犒劳自己
yield to: 屈服于
