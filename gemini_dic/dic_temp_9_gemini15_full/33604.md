cinch:/sɪntʃ/| n., v.|轻而易举的事；轻松地完成|Something easily done; to fasten or secure something.|The exam was a cinch for her. 她考试轻而易举就通过了。He cinched the deal with a firm handshake. 他以坚定的握手完成了这笔交易。|近义词：snap, breeze, piece of cake|反义词：struggle, difficulty

USAGE->
一般现在时:
It's a cinch to operate this machine. = 操作这台机器很容易。
This task is a cinch for him. = 这项任务对他来说易如反掌。

一般过去时:
She cinched the belt around her waist. = 她把腰带系在腰上。
He cinched the deal by offering a lower price. = 他通过提供更低的价格敲定了这笔交易。

现在完成时:
He has cinched the championship title. = 他已经获得了冠军头衔。
She has cinched her seatbelt for safety. = 为了安全，她已经系好了安全带。

过去完成时:
He had cinched the contract before the deadline. = 他在截止日期前已经签署了合同。
She had cinched the bag tightly to prevent spillage. = 她已经紧紧地系好了袋子以防止溢出。


将来时:
They will cinch the victory with their superior skills. = 他们将凭借高超的技能赢得胜利。
I will cinch my shoes before the race. = 我将在比赛前系好我的鞋带。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I could cinch the project. = 如果我有更多的时间，我就能轻松完成这个项目。
If he had practiced more, he could have cinched the first place. = 如果他多练习一下，他就能轻松获得第一名。

被动语态:
The package was cinched with a strong rope. = 包裹是用结实的绳子捆绑的。
The deal was cinched after long negotiations. = 在长时间谈判后，交易达成了。


疑问句:
Can you cinch this rope for me? = 你能帮我系一下这条绳子吗？
Is this task a cinch for you? = 这项任务对你来说很容易吗？

否定句:
The knot wasn't cinched tightly enough. = 这个结系得不紧。
He couldn't cinch the deal because of the competition. = 由于竞争，他无法达成交易。


PHRASE->
cinch a deal = 达成协议
cinch a victory = 赢得胜利
cinch a belt = 系紧腰带
a cinch to do sth = 做某事很容易
a cinch for sb = 对某人来说很容易
