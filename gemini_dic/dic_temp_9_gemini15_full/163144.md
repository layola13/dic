precommanding:/ˌpriːkəˈmændɪŋ/| adj.| 预先指挥的；先发制人的；先期命令的 |  Describing an action or order given in advance of another action or event, often to prepare for it or to gain an advantage. It implies a proactive and often controlling approach.  |例句：The precommanding of troops ensured a swift and decisive victory.  预先调遣军队确保了迅速而决定性的胜利。


The general's precommanding strategy involved a surprise attack.  将军的先发制人战略包括一次突然袭击。 |近义词：proactive, anticipatory, preparatory, preliminary |反义词：reactive, belated, delayed, impromptu
