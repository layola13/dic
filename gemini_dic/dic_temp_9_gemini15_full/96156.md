indentureship:/ɪnˈdɛntʃəʃɪp/ | n. | 契约奴役；学徒期； indenture 的名词形式 | A binding agreement, especially one that bound a person to service for a specified term.  It often refers to a system where young people were bound to a master to learn a trade or profession.  The term can also refer to the period of time served under such an agreement. | Example:  His indentureship lasted seven years, after which he became a fully qualified carpenter.  他的学徒期持续了七年，之后他成为了一名合格的木匠。 | 近义词：apprenticeship, servitude | 反义词：freedom, independence
