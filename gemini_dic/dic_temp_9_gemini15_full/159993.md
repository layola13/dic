polysemy:/pɒliˈsiːmi/| /ˈpɑːliˌsiːmi/| n.|多义性；一词多义|The existence of more than one possible meaning for a word or phrase.|The word "bank" has polysemy, as it can refer to a financial institution or the land alongside a river.|近义词：ambiguity, multiple meanings|反义词：monosemy, univocity


The word "polysemy" comes from the Greek words "poly" (πολύς), meaning "many," and "sēma" (σῆμα), meaning "sign" or "mark."  Therefore, it literally means "many signs" or "many meanings."
