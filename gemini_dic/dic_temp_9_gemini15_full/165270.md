preinsuring: /ˌpriːɪnˈʃʊərɪŋ/ | v. | 预先保险，提前投保 |  To insure something before it is officially needed or before a risk is fully known.  This often involves obtaining insurance coverage for a future event or period. | 例句：The company decided to preinsure the new building before construction even began. = 公司决定在建筑工程开始之前就为新建筑投保。


The construction company preinsured the project to mitigate potential financial losses. = 建筑公司为项目预先投保以减轻潜在的财务损失。


近义词：advance insurance, prior insurance | 反义词：post-insuring (this term is rarely used) | 词性：动词
