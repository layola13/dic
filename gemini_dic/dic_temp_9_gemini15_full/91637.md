hyperbolic:/ˌhaɪpərˈbɒlɪk/ | /ˌhaɪpərˈbɑːlɪk/ | adj. | 双曲线的；夸张的；极度的 | Exaggerated; characterized by exaggeration; of or relating to a hyperbola.  |例句：His claims were wildly hyperbolic.  他的说法极其夸张。

例句：The hyperbolic curve on the graph represents the relationship between the two variables. 图表上的双曲线表示这两个变量之间的关系。

近义词：exaggerated, inflated, overstated | 反义词：understated, moderate, literal

词源:
From the Greek word "hyperbolē," meaning "excess," "exaggeration."  The geometric term "hyperbola" was derived from this, referring to a type of conic section. The adjective "hyperbolic" then followed from the geometric term.
