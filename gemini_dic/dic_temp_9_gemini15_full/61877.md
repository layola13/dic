empresses:/ˈɛmprəsɪz/| n. |女皇们，皇后们 |The word "empresses" is the plural form of "empress," referring to multiple female monarchs or rulers.  It denotes women holding supreme power in an empire or kingdom. |例句：Throughout history, many empresses have wielded significant political power. 纵观历史，许多女皇都拥有强大的政治权力。

The reign of several empresses marked a golden age for the empire.  几个女皇的统治时期标志着帝国的黄金时代。|近义词：queens (if referring to queens of kingdoms, not necessarily empires) |反义词：subjects, commoners

词源:
Empress comes from the Old French "empresse," which in turn derives from the Latin "imperatrix," the feminine form of "imperator," meaning "commander" or "emperor."  The suffix "-ess" denotes the feminine gender.
