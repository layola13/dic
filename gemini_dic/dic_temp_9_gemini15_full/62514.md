endpapers:/ˈɛndˌpeɪpərz/ | n. |衬页|The blank or decorated leaves of paper at the beginning and end of a book, often pasted to the inside of the cover.  They are sometimes used for preliminary matter (such as a title page, copyright page, or dedication) or for supplementary material.| Example: The endpapers of the antique book were beautifully illustrated. 这本古书的衬页插图精美。  The endpapers were used to display a map relevant to the story. 衬页被用来展示与故事相关的的地图。|近义词：flyleaves (less common)|反义词：None
