preoperators:/ˌpriːˈɒpəreɪtərz/| n. |预操作符，前置操作符|Operators that appear before the operand.  They are used in programming languages to perform operations on a single operand. Examples include the increment operator (++), the decrement operator (--), and the unary minus operator (-).|The program uses preoperators to increment the counter before each loop iteration.|近义词：prefix operators|反义词：postoperators
