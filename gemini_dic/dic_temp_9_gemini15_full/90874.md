hunkerish: /ˈhʌŋkərɪʃ/ | adj. | 矮胖的；蹲伏的；蜷缩的 |  Characterized by a hunched or crouched posture; squat and sturdy.  It suggests a feeling of being close to the ground, possibly for warmth or protection. The word implies a somewhat ungainly or awkward appearance. | 例句：The hunkerish dog huddled by the fireplace for warmth.  = 那只矮胖的狗蜷缩在壁炉旁取暖。


The old woman, hunkerish and weathered, sat by the window. =  那位矮胖且饱经风霜的老妇人坐在窗边。


近义词：squat, stocky, dumpy,  crouching,  bowed


反义词：tall, slender, lanky, upright

词源:  Derived from the verb "hunker," meaning to crouch or squat down.  The suffix "-ish" adds a somewhat informal and slightly less precise quality to the meaning.  The root likely relates to Old English words associated with crouching or bending.
