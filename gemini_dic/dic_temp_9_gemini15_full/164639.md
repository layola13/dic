preexpectation:/ˌpriːˌɛkspekˈteɪʃən/| n. |预先期望，预期 |An expectation formed before an event or experience.  It refers to a belief or anticipation about something that is expected to happen in the future, often based on prior knowledge or experience.  This anticipation can influence how an individual perceives and interprets subsequent events. |Example:  The preexpectation of a difficult exam led to increased anxiety among the students. 预先对考试难度有预期导致学生焦虑增加。|近义词：anticipation, foreboding, premonition|反义词：surprise, unexpectedness |名词
