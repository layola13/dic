nondomesticating: /ˌnɑːnˌdəʊmɛstɪkeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 非驯化的；未被驯化的 | Not domesticated; not tamed or cultivated.  The term often refers to plants and animals that have not been selectively bred by humans for specific traits. |


例句：

The nondomesticating species of wild rice requires specific environmental conditions to thrive. = 非驯化种类的野生稻需要特定的环境条件才能茁壮成长。

The study focused on the behavior of nondomesticating animals in their natural habitats. = 这项研究的重点是非驯化动物在其自然栖息地的行为。


近义词：wild, untamed, uncultivated, feral


反义词：domesticated, tamed, cultivated


词源：

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of"
* **domesticating**:  present participle of the verb "domesticate," meaning "to tame (an animal) and keep it as a pet or for work; to cultivate (a plant) for human use."  "Domesticate" ultimately derives from the Latin word "domesticus," meaning "of the house" or "belonging to the home."
