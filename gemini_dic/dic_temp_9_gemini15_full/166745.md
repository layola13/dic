presidio:/prɪˈsɪdioʊ/ | n. |要塞，军事基地 | A military post or fort, especially one in Spanish California.  The word derives from the Spanish word "presidio," meaning "outpost" or "garrison."  It often refers to a fortified settlement established by the Spanish in the Americas. | Example: The Presidio of San Francisco is a popular historical landmark.  旧金山普雷西迪奥是一个受欢迎的历史地标。|近义词：fort, garrison, outpost |反义词：None specifically, but words implying civilian settlement could be considered antonyms.
