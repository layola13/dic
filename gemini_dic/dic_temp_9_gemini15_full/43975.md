crier:/ˈkraɪər/ | n. | 叫卖者；哭喊者；报幕员 | A crier is a person who announces something publicly, often in a loud voice, or someone who cries out or weeps.  This word's meaning has evolved over time, reflecting its usage in different historical contexts. | Example: The town crier announced the king's arrival. 城中报信人宣布国王驾到。  The child was a crier, wailing inconsolably. 这个孩子是个爱哭鬼，哭得撕心裂肺。 | 近义词：announcer, proclaimer, wailer | 反义词：silent person


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The town crier announces the news daily. = 城中报信人每天都宣布新闻。
He cries out in pain. = 他痛苦地喊叫。


一般过去时:
The crier announced the winning lottery numbers. = 报幕员宣布了彩票中奖号码。
She cried out when she saw the accident. = 她看到事故时大声喊叫。


现在完成时:
The town crier has announced the royal wedding. = 城中报信人已经宣布了皇室婚礼。
He has cried many times since the loss of his dog. = 自从他的狗去世后，他已经哭过很多次了。


过去完成时:
The crier had already announced the results before the crowd arrived. = 在人群到达之前，报幕员已经宣布了结果。
She had cried herself to sleep the night before. = 前一天晚上她哭着睡着了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
