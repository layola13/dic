describer: /dɪˈskraɪbər/ | n. | 描绘者，描述者 | A person who describes things. Someone who gives a detailed account of something.  | Example: The describer painted a vivid picture of the scene.  描述者生动地描绘了场景。 |近义词：narrator, commentator, reporter |反义词：None specifically, but antonyms could be related to actions opposite of describing, such as obfuscator or concealer.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The describer explains the process clearly. = 描述者清楚地解释了这个过程。
He describes the painting beautifully. = 他漂亮地描述了这幅画。


一般过去时:
The describer recounted the events accurately. = 描述者准确地叙述了这些事件。
She described the accident in detail. = 她详细地描述了事故。


现在完成时:
The describer has already described the main points. = 描述者已经描述了要点。
He has described many different cultures. = 他已经描述了许多不同的文化。


过去完成时:
The describer had already finished his description before the meeting. = 描述者在会议开始前已经完成了他的描述。
She had described the situation to the police. = 她已经向警方描述了情况。


将来时:
The describer will describe the experiment results tomorrow. = 描述者明天将描述实验结果。
He will describe his journey in his book. = 他将在他的书中描述他的旅程。


虚拟语气:
If the describer were more descriptive, the audience would understand better. = 如果描述者更具描述性，观众会更容易理解。
I wish the describer had used more vivid language. = 我希望描述者使用更生动的语言。


被动语态:
The scene was vividly described by the describer. = 场景被描述者生动地描述了。
The process is being described in the manual. = 该过程在手册中被描述。


疑问句:
Who is the describer of this event? = 谁是这次事件的描述者？
What exactly did the describer say? = 描述者到底说了什么？


否定句:
The describer did not provide enough detail. = 描述者没有提供足够的细节。
He did not describe the problem clearly. = 他没有清楚地描述这个问题。
