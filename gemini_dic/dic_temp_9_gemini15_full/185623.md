revenue's:/ˈrevənjuːz/| n. |收入的；收益的 |Revenue's meaning refers to the income generated from business activities or investments.  It specifically indicates possession or relationship with revenue.  For example, "the company's revenue's growth" indicates that the growth is related to the company's revenue.|例句：The company's revenue's growth is impressive this year.  今年公司收入的增长令人印象深刻。|近义词：income's, earnings' , profit's|反义词：expenditure's, cost's, loss's|名词所有格


USAGE->
This is not applicable to 'revenue's' as it is a possessive form of a noun, not a verb.  The usage examples are relevant to the word "revenue" itself, not its possessive form.


一般现在时:
Our company's revenue is high this quarter. = 我们公司本季度的收入很高。
Their revenue consistently exceeds expectations. = 他们的收入始终超过预期。


一般过去时:
Last year's revenue was lower than projected. = 去年的收入低于预期。
The company's revenue decreased significantly in the third quarter. = 公司第三季度的收入大幅下降。


现在完成时:
Revenue has increased steadily since the new product launch. = 自从新产品推出以来，收入稳步增长。
The company has seen a significant rise in revenue this year. = 今年公司收入大幅增长。


过去完成时:
Before the merger, their revenue had already plateaued. = 在合并之前，他们的收入已经趋于平稳。
By the end of the year, the revenue had exceeded our initial target. = 到年底，收入已经超过了我们的初始目标。


将来时:
We project that next year's revenue will be much higher. = 我们预计明年的收入将会高得多。
The company anticipates a substantial increase in revenue next quarter. = 公司预计下季度收入将大幅增长。


虚拟语气:
If our revenue were higher, we could afford new equipment. = 如果我们的收入更高，我们就能买得起新设备。
I wish our revenue was more stable. = 我希望我们的收入更稳定一些。


被动语态:
Revenue is often affected by market fluctuations. = 收入经常受到市场波动的影响。
The company's revenue was negatively impacted by the economic downturn. = 公司的收入受到了经济衰退的负面影响。


疑问句:
What is the company's total revenue for the year? = 公司全年的总收入是多少？
How much revenue did they generate last month? = 上个月他们创造了多少收入？


否定句:
The company's revenue did not meet our expectations. = 公司的收入没有达到我们的预期。
Their revenue was not as high as it had been in previous years. = 他们的收入不如前几年那么高。
