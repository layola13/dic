chiliarch:/ˈkɪliˌɑːrk/| n.|千夫长，指挥一千人的军官|A chiliarch was a military commander in ancient Greece and Rome, responsible for a unit of approximately 1,000 soldiers.  The term is often used in historical contexts, particularly relating to the Roman army and the New Testament.|Example: The chiliarch, a seasoned veteran, led his legion with unwavering courage.  千夫长，一位经验丰富的退伍军人，以坚定不移的勇气领导着他的军团。|近义词：tribune, commander, prefect|反义词：private, soldier
