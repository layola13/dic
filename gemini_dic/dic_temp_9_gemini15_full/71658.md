flatters:/ˈflætərz/| vt.|奉承；谄媚；使高兴|To praise someone excessively or insincerely, especially to gain their favor.  It often implies a degree of falseness or self-interest in the praise.|例句：He flattered the boss in order to get a promotion. 他奉承老板以求升职。|例句：Her compliments, while flattering, felt insincere. 她的赞美虽然令人高兴，但却感觉不真诚。|近义词：praise, compliment, cajole, fawn over|反义词：insult, offend, criticize, belittle|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flatters his superiors to get ahead. = 他奉承上司以求晋升。
She often flatters her friends to make them feel good. = 她经常奉承朋友以使他们感觉良好。


一般过去时:
He flattered the professor to get a better grade. = 他奉承教授以获得更好的成绩。
She flattered her way into the exclusive club. = 她通过奉承的方式进入了那个高级俱乐部。


现在完成时:
He has flattered his way into a high-ranking position. = 他通过奉承获得了高级职位。
She has flattered many people throughout her career. = 她职业生涯中奉承过很多人。


过去完成时:
He had flattered the CEO before asking for a raise. = 他在请求加薪之前就奉承了首席执行官。
She had flattered the artist before requesting a portrait. = 在请求画像之前，她奉承了这位艺术家。


将来时:
He will flatter the client to secure the contract. = 他将奉承客户以确保合同。
She will flatter her colleagues to gain their support. = 她将奉承同事以获得他们的支持。


虚拟语气:
If he flattered her more, she might give him the job. = 如果他更奉承她，她可能会给他这份工作。
I wish I hadn't flattered him so much; it made me look weak. = 我真希望我没有那么奉承他；那让我看起来很软弱。


被动语态:
The politician was flattered by the enthusiastic crowd. = 这位政治家受到了热情的群众的奉承。
His work was flattered by critics, but it was not genuine praise. = 他的作品受到了评论家的赞扬，但这并非真诚的赞美。


疑问句:
Does he flatter everyone he meets? = 他会奉承他遇到的每个人吗？
Do you think she's flattering me? = 你觉得她在奉承我吗？


否定句:
He didn't flatter her; he spoke his mind honestly. = 他没有奉承她；他诚实地表达了自己的想法。
She doesn't flatter people; she's always direct. = 她不奉承人；她总是直截了当。
