mazdeism:/ˈmɑːzdeɪɪzəm/| n. |拜火教，琐罗亚斯德教|Mazdeism, also known as Zoroastrianism, is an ancient Iranian religion founded by the prophet Zoroaster (Zarathushtra) in ancient Persia (modern-day Iran). It is a monotheistic religion centered on the worship of Ahura Mazda, the supreme god, and the struggle between good and evil.  Followers believe in a final judgment and the eventual triumph of good over evil. The religion emphasizes ethical behavior, truthfulness, and good thoughts, words, and deeds.|The ancient Persian empire flourished under the influence of Mazdeism. |近义词：Zoroastrianism|反义词：无
