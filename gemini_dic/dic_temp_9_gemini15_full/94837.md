imputation:/ˌɪmpjuˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 归咎；归因；(数据的)填补 | The act of imputing something to someone or something; attribution.  It can also refer to the process of replacing missing values in a dataset with estimated values. | Example: The imputation of blame to the wrong person led to a miscarriage of justice.  错误地将责任归咎于他人导致了司法不公。  The data analysis relied on imputation to handle missing values. 数据分析依赖于填补缺失值。 |近义词：attribution, assignment, imputation of data |反义词：exculpation, exoneration

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The manager imputes the failure to poor planning. = 经理将失败归咎于糟糕的计划。
He imputes his success to hard work and dedication. = 他将自己的成功归因于努力工作和奉献精神。


一般过去时:
They imputed the delay to unforeseen circumstances. = 他们将延误归咎于不可预见的情况。
She imputed the anonymous letter to her rival. = 她将匿名信归咎于她的竞争对手。


现在完成时:
Researchers have imputed missing data points using various statistical methods. = 研究人员已经使用各种统计方法填补了缺失的数据点。
The team has imputed the cause of the accident to human error. = 该团队已将事故的原因归咎于人为错误。


过去完成时:
They had imputed the loss to a natural disaster before the investigation began. = 在调查开始之前，他们已经将损失归咎于自然灾害。
The investigators had imputed the crime to a known gang before new evidence emerged.  调查人员在出现新的证据之前，已经将罪行归咎于一个已知的帮派。


将来时:
The committee will impute responsibility based on the evidence presented. = 委员会将根据提交的证据来追究责任。
Scientists will impute the missing data using a sophisticated algorithm. = 科学家们将使用复杂的算法来填补缺失的数据。


虚拟语气:
If they imputed the problem to the software, they would have wasted valuable time. = 如果他们将问题归咎于软件，他们就会浪费宝贵的时间。
I wish they hadn't imputed such bad motives to my actions. = 我真希望他们不要对我采取的行动妄加揣测。


被动语态:
The responsibility was imputed to the project manager. = 责任被归咎于项目经理。
Missing values were imputed using a mean imputation method. = 使用均值填补法填补缺失值。


疑问句:
To whom do you impute this failure? = 你把这个失败归咎于谁？
What method was used to impute the missing data? = 使用什么方法填补缺失数据？


否定句:
The court did not impute guilt to the defendant. = 法院没有认定被告有罪。
We cannot impute the results solely to chance. = 我们不能仅仅将结果归咎于运气。

词源:
From Latin *imputationem (nominative *imputatio), from imputare "to charge, impute," from in- "in, on" + putare "to think, reckon, prune."
