oversold:/ˌoʊvərˈsoʊld/ | adj. | 卖得过多的；供过于求的；(股票等)被高估的 |  Something that is oversold has been promoted or marketed too much, often leading to disappointment or a lack of genuine value.  It can also refer to a situation where the demand exceeds supply, such as an airline flight.  In the context of stocks, it means the price is higher than its intrinsic value. |例句：The new phone was heavily oversold and many customers were disappointed with its performance. 这款新手机被过度宣传，许多顾客对其性能感到失望。

The concert was oversold, and many people were turned away at the door. 音乐会门票销售过度，许多人被拒之门外。


近义词：overhyped, overpriced, overvalued | 反义词：undervalued, undersold
