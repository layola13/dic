cenotaph:/ˈsɛnəˌtæf/| n. |无名冢，纪念碑 |A cenotaph is a monument erected in memory of a person or group of people whose remains are elsewhere.  It's often built when the body cannot be recovered or the burial site is unknown. |例句：A cenotaph was erected in the town square to honor the soldiers who died in the war.  这个小镇广场上竖立着一座纪念碑，以纪念在战争中牺牲的士兵。|近义词：monument, memorial, mausoleum|反义词：grave (in the sense of containing the actual remains)
