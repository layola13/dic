predisappointment: /ˌpriːˌdɪs.əˈpɔɪnt.mənt/ | n. | 事先的失望；预期的失望 |  A feeling of disappointment that occurs before an event actually happens, often due to anticipation of a negative outcome.  It's the feeling of knowing something might go wrong before it even begins. |  例句：The predisappointment was palpable; everyone knew the project was doomed from the start. 事先的失望显而易见；每个人都知道这个项目从一开始就注定要失败。

例句：Her predisappointment about the interview was so strong that she almost didn't go.  她对面试的预期失望如此强烈，以至于她差点不去。


近义词：anticipation of failure, foreboding, apprehension, premonition (of disappointment) | 反义词：optimism, expectation of success, confidence
