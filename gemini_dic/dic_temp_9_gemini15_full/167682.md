prickpunch: /ˈprɪkpʌntʃ/ | n. |冲压机；冲孔机 | A prickpunch is a tool used to create small indentations in metal, typically to mark a location for drilling or other machining operations.  It's a small, hardened steel punch with a pointed end.  | Example: The machinist used a prickpunch to mark the center of the hole before drilling.  机械师在钻孔前用冲头标记孔的中心。 | 近义词: center punch, prick punch | 反义词:  (lacks a direct antonym, but could be considered opposite to a tool that smooths or fills a surface)
