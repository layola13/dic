evangelize:/ɪˈvændʒəlaɪz/ | vt. | 传福音；布道；使…基督教化 | To preach the gospel or Christian doctrine.  To convert or attempt to convert someone to Christianity.  It can also refer to spreading a belief system or idea enthusiastically. | 例句：Many missionaries travelled overseas to evangelize.  许多传教士前往海外传教。

The church actively evangelizes in the local community. 教堂积极在当地社区传教。


近义词：preach, proselytize, proselyte, convert, missionarize | 反义词：suppress (beliefs), secularize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They evangelize in the streets. = 他们在街上布道。
The church evangelizes the poor. = 教堂向穷人传教。


一般过去时:
He evangelized throughout the country. = 他在全国各地传教。
The missionaries evangelized the natives. = 传教士向土著人传教。


现在完成时:
They have evangelized for many years. = 他们传教多年了。
She has evangelized in several countries. = 她曾在几个国家传教。


过去完成时:
He had evangelized in Asia before moving to Africa. = 他在搬到非洲之前曾在亚洲传教。
They had evangelized for decades before retiring. = 他们在退休前已经传教了几十年。


将来时:
We will evangelize in the new city. = 我们将在新城市传教。
She will evangelize to the young people. = 她将向年轻人传教。


虚拟语气:
If I were to evangelize, I would choose a peaceful approach. = 如果我要传教，我会选择和平的方式。
I wish I had evangelized sooner in my life. = 我希望我早点开始传教。


被动语态:
The gospel was evangelized across the empire. = 福音在整个帝国传播开来。
The new ideas were evangelized through the media. = 新思想通过媒体传播开来。


疑问句:
How do they evangelize? = 他们如何传教？

否定句:
He did not evangelize aggressively. = 他没有咄咄逼人地传教。
They do not evangelize without respect for other beliefs. = 他们传教时尊重其他信仰。
