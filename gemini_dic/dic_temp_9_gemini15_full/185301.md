retrench:/rɪˈtrentʃ/ | v. | 削减；紧缩；退却；减少开支 | To reduce expenses or spending; to cut back; to withdraw or retreat. |例句：The company had to retrench after losing a major contract. 公司在失去一份主要合同后不得不裁员节支。|例句：The army retrenched to a more defensible position. 军队退守到一个更易防守的位置。|近义词：reduce, cut back, curtail, economize |反义词：expand, increase, augment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company retrenches every year to improve profitability. = 公司每年都削减开支以提高盈利能力。
He retrenches his spending on entertainment. = 他减少了在娱乐上的支出。

一般过去时:
They retrenched their operations after the market downturn. = 市场低迷后，他们缩减了业务规模。
The government retrenched spending on social programs. = 政府削减了社会福利项目的支出。

现在完成时:
The company has retrenched significantly since the recession. = 自经济衰退以来，该公司已大幅裁员节支。
We have retrenched our marketing efforts to focus on key areas. = 我们已经缩减了营销工作，重点关注关键领域。

过去完成时:
They had retrenched their workforce before the merger. = 他们曾在合并之前裁减了员工。
The company had retrenched its overseas operations before the crisis. = 在危机爆发之前，该公司已经缩减了海外业务。

将来时:
The company will retrench if profits continue to fall. = 如果利润继续下降，该公司将削减开支。
We will retrench our spending on non-essential items. = 我们将减少在非必需品上的支出。

虚拟语气:
If the company had retrenched earlier, it might have avoided bankruptcy. = 如果公司早点削减开支，它也许就能避免破产。
I wish we had retrenched our spending last year. = 我真希望我们去年就减少了开支。

被动语态:
Spending was retrenched by the government to control inflation. = 政府削减开支以控制通货膨胀。
The workforce was retrenched due to economic difficulties. = 由于经济困难，员工被裁员。

疑问句:
Will the company retrench this year? = 公司今年会削减开支吗？
Did they retrench their marketing budget? = 他们削减了营销预算吗？

否定句:
The company did not retrench despite the losses. = 尽管亏损，公司并没有削减开支。
They did not retrench their staff even during the recession. = 即使在经济衰退期间，他们也没有裁员。


PHRASE->

retrenchment program = 节约计划
retrenchment measures = 紧缩措施
