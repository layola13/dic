lectern:/ˈlɛktərn/| n. |讲台；读经台|A lectern is a stand, typically with a slanted top, used to hold a book or manuscript, especially when reading aloud to an audience, often in a church, lecture hall, or other public space.  A lectern allows the speaker to stand comfortably while reading and facing the audience.|Example: The priest stood at the lectern to read the gospel.|近义词：podium, pulpit |反义词：None.
