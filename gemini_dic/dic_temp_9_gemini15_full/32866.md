词源:
The word "cholo" originates from the Quechua word "chulu," meaning "man," "person," or "individual" in the Andes region of South America.  Over time, its meaning shifted, particularly in the context of colonial and post-colonial societies, acquiring negative connotations associated with marginalized groups.  Its current usage is highly context-dependent and often carries strong emotional weight.
