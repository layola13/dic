post-Elizabethan:/ˌpoʊstˌɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən/| adj.|伊丽莎白时代以后的；后伊丽莎白时代的|Relating to the period in English history and culture following the reign of Queen Elizabeth I (1558-1603).  This era is characterized by significant shifts in literary styles, political landscapes, and social structures.  The transition from the Renaissance to the Baroque is often associated with this period.|例句：Shakespeare's works mark the end of the Elizabethan era, while the post-Elizabethan period saw the rise of Jacobean drama.  莎士比亚的作品标志着伊丽莎白时代的结束，而后伊丽莎白时代则见证了雅各布戏剧的兴起。

The post-Elizabethan era witnessed a decline in the popularity of the sonnet and a rise in the popularity of the masque. 后伊丽莎白时代见证了十四行诗流行程度的下降和面具剧流行程度的上升。|近义词：Jacobean, Stuart, post-Renaissance|反义词：Elizabethan
