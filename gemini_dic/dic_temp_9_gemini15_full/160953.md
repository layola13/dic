post-romanticism:/ˌpoʊst roʊˈmænɪsɪzəm/| n. |后浪漫主义|Post-romanticism is a broad term referring to various artistic, literary, and philosophical movements that emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, reacting against or extending upon the ideals and aesthetics of Romanticism.  It encompasses diverse styles and approaches, often characterized by a more complex and nuanced view of human nature, emotion, and the relationship between art and reality. |The late 19th and early 20th centuries saw the rise of post-romanticism in literature, marked by authors grappling with the disillusionment following the Romantic era.  |近义词：Late Romanticism, Modernism (in some contexts)|反义词：Romanticism
