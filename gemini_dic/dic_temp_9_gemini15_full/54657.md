disobey:/ˌdɪsoʊˈbeɪ/ | vt. |不服从；违抗 | to refuse to obey a person, rule, or law | 例句：He disobeyed the order and was punished. 他违抗命令受到了惩罚。| 例句：The children disobeyed their parents and went out late. 孩子们不听父母的话，很晚才回家。| 近义词：defy, flout, disregard | 反义词：obey, comply, conform | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They disobey the rules regularly. = 他们经常违反规定。
He disobeys his parents' wishes. = 他违背父母的意愿。

一般过去时:
She disobeyed her teacher's instructions. = 她违抗了老师的指示。

现在完成时:
He has disobeyed the law several times. = 他已经违法好几次了。
They have disobeyed their parents' wishes for years. = 他们多年来一直违背父母的意愿。

过去完成时:
The child had disobeyed his parents many times before they took action. = 在父母采取行动之前，孩子已经多次不听话了。
She had disobeyed the rules so often that she was expelled. = 她多次违反规定，最终被开除了。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
