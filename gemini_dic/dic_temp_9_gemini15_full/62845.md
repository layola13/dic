enjoin:/ɪnˈdʒɔɪn/ | vt. | 命令；吩咐；禁止；劝告 | To order or command someone to do something; to prohibit or forbid something; to urge or advise someone to do something. | 例句：The judge enjoined the defendant from contacting the witness. = 法官命令被告不得联系证人。  The law enjoins us to respect the rights of others. = 法律告诫我们尊重他人的权利。| 近义词：command, order, forbid, urge, advise | 反义词：permit, allow


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The court enjoins the company to cease polluting the river. = 法院命令该公司停止污染河流。
She enjoins her children to be respectful to their elders. = 她告诫她的孩子们要尊敬长辈。

一般过去时:
The doctor enjoined him to give up smoking. = 医生劝他戒烟。
The teacher enjoined the students from talking during the exam. = 老师禁止学生在考试期间说话。

现在完成时:
The judge has enjoined the publication of the documents. = 法官已禁止出版这些文件。
She has enjoined her son to study hard for his exams. = 她已告诫她的儿子要为考试努力学习。

过去完成时:
The principal had enjoined the students from leaving the campus without permission. = 校长此前已禁止学生未经许可离开校园。
He had enjoined his assistant to finish the report before the deadline. = 他此前已吩咐他的助手在截止日期前完成报告。

将来时:
The court will enjoin the construction of the new building. = 法院将禁止建造新建筑。
She will enjoin her daughter to be careful when crossing the street. = 她将告诫她的女儿过马路时要小心。


虚拟语气:
If the judge enjoined the defendant from speaking, the trial would be fairer. = 如果法官禁止被告发言，审判将会更加公平。
I wish the government would enjoin the use of harmful chemicals. = 我希望政府能禁止使用有害化学品。

被动语态:
The company was enjoined from dumping waste into the ocean. = 该公司被禁止将废物倾倒入海。
He was enjoined to appear in court. = 他被命令出庭。


疑问句:
Does the law enjoin us to pay taxes? = 法律是否命令我们纳税？
Will the court enjoin the demolition of the historical building? = 法院会禁止拆除那座历史建筑吗？

否定句:
The judge did not enjoin the defendant from speaking to the press. = 法官没有禁止被告与媒体交谈。
The school does not enjoin students from using their phones during breaks. = 学校不禁止学生在课间使用手机。


PHRASE->
enjoin upon/on = 郑重嘱咐；命令
be enjoined to = 被命令…
enjoin oneself to =  致力于…
enjoin silence = 命令保持沉默
enjoin obedience = 命令服从


词源:
From Old French *enjoindre, from Latin *in-iungere, from in- "in" + iungere "to join".  The original meaning was "to join in," "to unite," which evolved into the sense of "to order" or "to command".
