natively:/ˈneɪtɪvli/ | adv. | 本地地；天生地；天然地 |  Naturally or originally; in a native manner.  | 例句：She speaks English natively. = 她英语说得像母语一样流利。

例句：He natively understands the nuances of the local dialect. = 他天生就理解当地方言的细微之处。


近义词：naturally, inherently, originally, intrinsically

反义词：artificially, acquiredly, non-natively


词源:  Derived from the adjective "native," meaning belonging to a place by birth; indigenous.  The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective into an adverb, indicating a manner or way.  The word's roots trace back to the Latin word "nasci," meaning "to be born."
