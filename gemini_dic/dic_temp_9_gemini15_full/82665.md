guesser:/ˈɡɛsər/| n. |猜测者，推测者|A person who guesses, especially one who makes a living by guessing.  Someone who tries to predict something without firm evidence.|He's a good guesser; he often predicts the lottery numbers correctly.|近义词：conjecturer, speculator|反义词：knower, expert


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He guesses the answer every time. = 他每次都猜测答案。
She guesses the meaning of the word. = 她猜测这个词的意思。


一般过去时:
He guessed the riddle correctly. = 他正确地猜中了谜语。
She guessed the weight of the cake. = 她猜对了蛋糕的重量。


现在完成时:
He has guessed the password correctly. = 他已经猜对了密码。
She has guessed all the answers. = 她已经猜对了所有答案。


过去完成时:
He had guessed the ending of the movie. = 他已经猜到了电影的结局。
She had guessed the thief's identity. = 她已经猜到了小偷的身份。


将来时:
He will guess the winner of the game. = 他将猜出比赛的获胜者。
She will guess the number I'm thinking of. = 她将猜出我想的数字。


虚拟语气:
If I were a better guesser, I would win the lottery. = 如果我是一个更好的猜谜者，我会赢得彩票。
I wish I had guessed the correct answer. = 我希望我猜对了答案。


被动语态:
The answer was guessed by him. = 答案被他猜出来了。
The winner was guessed correctly by many people. = 很多人都正确地猜到了获胜者。


疑问句:
Who guessed the answer? = 谁猜对了答案？
Can you guess what I'm thinking? = 你能猜到我在想什么吗？


否定句:
He didn't guess the correct answer. = 他没有猜对答案。
She couldn't guess the mystery. = 她猜不出这个谜。


PHRASE->
a good guesser: 一个很好的猜测者
skilled guesser: 熟练的猜测者
intuitive guesser: 直觉敏锐的猜测者
educated guesser:  有根据的猜测者
potential guesser:  潜在的猜测者
