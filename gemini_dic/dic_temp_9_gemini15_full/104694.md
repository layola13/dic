kasbah:/ˈkæzbɑː/| n. |卡斯巴, 堡垒 |A kasbah is a citadel, a fortified town, or a large house, especially in North Africa.  The term often refers to the old city or fortified quarter of a North African town.  It's characterized by narrow, winding streets, high walls, and often a complex layout designed for defense. |例句：The old kasbah of Marrakech is a labyrinth of narrow alleys and bustling souks.  摩洛哥马拉喀什的古卡斯巴是一个狭窄小巷和熙攘集市的迷宫。

The kasbah offered refuge from the desert sun and potential attackers.  这个卡斯巴为人们提供了躲避沙漠烈日和潜在袭击者的庇护。|近义词：citadel, fortress, stronghold |反义词：open area, undefended settlement
