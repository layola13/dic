epitomising: /ɪˈpɪtəmaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 作为…的典型；是…的缩影；体现 |  To be a perfect example of something; to embody or represent the essential characteristics of something. |


例句：

His work epitomises the spirit of the 1960s. = 他的作品体现了20世纪60年代的精神。

The building epitomises modern architecture. = 这座建筑是现代建筑的典范。


近义词：embody, represent, exemplify, symbolize, typify


反义词：misrepresent, contradict, belie


词性：动词 (现在分词形式)


词源:  The word "epitomising" comes from the noun "epitome," which originates from the Greek word "epitome" (ἐπιτομή), meaning "a cutting short, abridgment, summary."  The verb form then developed to signify something representing the essence of a larger whole.
