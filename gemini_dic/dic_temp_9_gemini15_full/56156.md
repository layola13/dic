dodded:/dɒdɪd/ | v. | 过去式和过去分词 | 蹒跚而行；摇摇晃晃地走；（尤指年老或疲惫时）步履蹒跚 | The old man dodded along the path.  这位老人沿着小路蹒跚而行。|近义词：staggered, tottered, shuffled | 反义词：walked briskly, strode


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dodders around the house. = 他在房子周围蹒跚而行。
She dodders when she walks. = 她走路时步履蹒跚。

一般过去时:
He doddered down the stairs. = 他蹒跚地走下楼梯。
She doddered into the room. = 她摇摇晃晃地走进房间。

现在完成时:
He has doddered all the way to the shop. = 他一路蹒跚地走到商店。
She has been doddering since her illness. = 自从她生病以来，她就一直在步履蹒跚。


过去完成时:
He had doddered for years before his fall. = 在摔倒之前，他已经步履蹒跚了很多年。
She had doddered through the park before reaching the bench. = 她蹒跚地穿过公园才到达长椅。


将来时:
He will dodder more as he gets older. = 他年纪越大，就会越蹒跚。
She will likely dodder if she doesn't get help. = 如果得不到帮助，她很可能会步履蹒跚。


虚拟语气:
If he were younger, he wouldn't dodder so much. = 如果他年轻点，就不会这么蹒跚了。
If she had exercised more, she wouldn't dodder now. = 如果她多锻炼，现在就不会步履蹒跚了。


被动语态:
(This verb is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes an action done by the subject, not to the subject.)


疑问句:

Does she dodder when she's tired? = 她累的时候会步履蹒跚吗？


否定句:
He doesn't dodder anymore. = 他不再步履蹒跚了。
She didn't dodder before her accident. = 她事故发生前没有步履蹒跚。
