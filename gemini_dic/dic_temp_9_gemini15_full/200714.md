sleepwalks: /ˈsliːpwɔːks/ | v. |梦游；(喻)漫不经心地行动|  To walk while asleep.  This usually occurs during the deepest stages of sleep and the person is unaware of their actions.  It can be a symptom of sleep disorders or other underlying medical conditions. |例句：He sleepwalks every night. 他每晚都梦游。

The child sleepwalked down the stairs and into the kitchen.  孩子梦游着下楼走到厨房。


近义词：somnambulate


反义词：无


词性：动词

词源:
The word "sleepwalks" comes from the combination of "sleep" and "walks."  "Sleep" is from Old English *slæp,  while "walks" is from Old English *walcan, meaning "to roll, to go, to move."  Therefore, "sleepwalks" literally means "to walk while sleeping."
