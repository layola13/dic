schisms:/ˈsɪzəm(z)/| n. |分裂；教会的分裂；意见分歧 | A schism is a split or division between strongly opposed sections or parties, caused by differences in opinion or belief.  It often refers to a division within a religious organization, but can also apply to political parties or other groups. | Example: The church suffered a major schism in the 16th century.  十六世纪教会经历了一次严重的分裂。  The political party is facing a deep schism over its leader's policies.  该政党在其领导人的政策问题上正经历着深刻的分裂。|近义词：division, split, rift, fracture, discord |反义词：unity, harmony, concord, agreement |名词
