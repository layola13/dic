hit-and-running:/ˌhɪt ænd ˈrʌnɪŋ/| adj. | 肇事逃逸的；打一下就跑的 |  Describes an accident, typically a traffic accident, in which the person responsible for causing the accident leaves the scene without providing assistance or identifying themselves to the authorities or the injured parties. It can also be used more generally to describe any action where someone causes harm and then flees.  |例句：The police are investigating a hit-and-run accident that injured a pedestrian.  警方正在调查一起造成行人受伤的肇事逃逸事故。

The driver was charged with hit-and-run after leaving the scene of the accident.  司机在事故现场逃逸后被指控犯有肇事逃逸罪。


近义词：肇事逃逸，逃逸事故，逃之夭夭 | 反义词：responsible, accountable, staying at the scene

词源:

The term is a compound of "hit" (to strike or collide) and "running" (fleeing or escaping).  The phrase's meaning evolved naturally from its literal description of the action to its current use as a legal term.
