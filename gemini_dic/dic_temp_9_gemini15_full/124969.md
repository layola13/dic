muggar:/ˈmʌɡər/| n.| 歹徒，强盗 | A mugger is a person who attacks and robs someone, especially in a public place.  |例句：He was mugged in broad daylight. 他在光天化日之下遭到抢劫。|例句：The police are investigating a series of muggings in the park. 警方正在调查公园里发生的一系列抢劫案。|近义词：robber, bandit, thief |反义词：victim, law-abiding citizen |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The mugger demands money from the victim. = 歹徒向受害者索要钱财。
He often mugs people near the station. = 他经常在车站附近抢劫他人。

一般过去时:
The mugger attacked the woman from behind. = 歹徒从背后袭击了该女子。
They mugged him and stole his wallet. = 他们抢劫了他并偷走了他的钱包。

现在完成时:
He has been mugged twice this year. = 他今年已被抢劫两次。
The police have arrested the mugger. = 警方已经逮捕了歹徒。

过去完成时:
I had never been mugged before that incident. = 在那件事发生之前，我从未被抢劫过。
They had mugged several people before being caught. = 他们在被捕之前已经抢劫了多人。


将来时:
He will mug someone again if he isn't caught. = 如果他不被抓住，他会再次抢劫他人。
The police believe he will mug more people. = 警方认为他会抢劫更多的人。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't been so careless, he wouldn't have been mugged. = 如果他不那么粗心，就不会被抢劫了。
I wish I hadn't been mugged last night. = 我真希望昨晚我没有被抢劫。

被动语态:
The victim was mugged in the street. = 受害者在街上被抢劫了。
Several people have been mugged recently. = 最近有几个人被抢劫了。

疑问句:

Have you ever been mugged? = 你曾经被抢劫过吗？

否定句:
