incriminate:/ɪnˈkrɪmɪneɪt/| vt.|使负罪；控告；牵连|To accuse someone of a crime or wrongdoing; to make someone appear guilty.  |例句：The evidence incriminates him directly.  证据直接证明他犯了罪。

例句：The witness's testimony incriminated the defendant. 目击证人的证词使被告受到指控。


近义词：accuse, charge, implicate, indict| 反义词：exonerate, absolve, vindicate| 词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The prosecutor attempts to incriminate the suspect. = 检察官试图使嫌疑犯认罪。
Does this evidence incriminate anyone? = 这证据会牵连到任何人吗？


一般过去时:
The police incriminated him with forged documents. = 警方用伪造的文件控告了他。
The witness's statement incriminated the CEO. = 证人的陈述使首席执行官受到牵连。


现在完成时:
The investigation has incriminated several high-ranking officials. = 调查已经牵连到几名高级官员。
The new evidence has incriminated the former president. = 新证据使前任总统受到牵连。


过去完成时:
They had incriminated him before the trial even started. = 他们甚至在审判开始之前就将他定罪了。
The police had incriminated the suspect based on circumstantial evidence. = 警方根据间接证据指控了嫌疑人。


将来时:
The lawyer believes the new evidence will incriminate his client. = 律师相信新的证据将会使他的当事人受到指控。
They fear that the upcoming testimony will incriminate more people. = 他们担心即将到来的证词将会牵连更多的人。


被动语态:
He was incriminated by a false confession. = 他因虚假供词而被控罪。
The suspect was incriminated by the forensic evidence. = 嫌疑人因法医证据而被控罪。


疑问句:
Does this statement incriminate the accused? = 这份声明会使被告受到指控吗？
Would further investigation incriminate others? = 进一步调查会牵连其他人吗？


否定句:
The defense lawyer tried to avoid incriminating his client. = 辩护律师试图避免使他的当事人受到指控。
The witness refused to say anything that could incriminate anyone. = 证人拒绝说任何可能使任何人受到指控的话。


PHRASE->
incriminate oneself = 自证其罪
incriminate another = 牵连他人
incriminate unintentionally = 无意中使...负罪
incriminate by association = 因关系而牵连
incriminate falsely = 诬陷


词源:
From Latin *incriminare, from in- "in, into" + criminare "to accuse."
