grasping:/ˈɡræspɪŋ/| adj. v-ing|贪婪的；渴望的；抓住的；理解的|Having or showing a strong desire to get or keep something, especially money or power;  characterized by or given to seizing or holding fast; understanding or comprehending something.|例句：His grasping nature alienated many of his colleagues.他的贪婪本性疏远了许多同事。

例句：The child had a firm grasping of the toy.孩子紧紧抓住玩具。


近义词：greedy, avaricious, covetous, acquisitive, clutching|反义词：generous, unselfish, altruistic


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is grasping for power. = 他渴望权力。
She is grasping at straws. = 她正在做徒劳的努力。


一般过去时:
He was grasping at opportunities. = 他抓住机会。
She was grasping for air. = 她大口喘气。


现在完成时:
He has been grasping at shadows. = 他一直在追求虚无缥缈的东西。

过去完成时:
He had been grasping for years before he finally succeeded. = 在他最终成功之前，他已经苦苦追求了很多年。
She had grasped the meaning of the poem. = 她已经理解了这首诗的意思。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
