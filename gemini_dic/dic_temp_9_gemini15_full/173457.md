quasi-expensive:/ˈkwɑːziɪkˈspɛnsɪv/ | adj. |准贵的；有点贵的 |  Relatively expensive; not quite expensive but more costly than average or typical.  The term implies a degree of expensiveness that is not extreme or exorbitant, but still noticeable. |例句：The restaurant was quasi-expensive; the meal was good, but we could have found cheaper options. 这家餐厅有点贵；饭菜不错，但我们可以找到更便宜的选择。

The new phone is quasi-expensive, more than I typically spend but worth it for the features. 这部新手机有点贵，比我通常花的钱多，但物有所值。

近义词：moderately priced, somewhat expensive, pricey, costly | 反义词：cheap, inexpensive, affordable, budget-friendly

词源:

* **quasi-**:  This prefix comes from Latin, meaning "as if," "seemingly," or "apparently." It indicates something that resembles or partially possesses a particular quality.

* **expensive**: This word comes from the Old French "expensive," derived from the Latin "expensīvus," meaning "costly."  "Ex-" means "out" and "pensus" relates to "weighing" or "spending."
