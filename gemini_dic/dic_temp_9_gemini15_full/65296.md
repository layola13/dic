eudaemonism:/juːˈdiːmənɪzəm/| n. |幸福论，快乐主义|The philosophical belief that happiness or flourishing is the highest good.  Eudaemonism emphasizes living a virtuous life in accordance with reason and nature to achieve eudaimonia (human flourishing).|例句：Aristotle's ethics is often described as a form of eudaemonism, focusing on the development of virtue and the achievement of a fulfilling life.|近义词：hedonism (though with important distinctions), well-being philosophy|反义词：nihilism, pessimism
