quasi-provincial: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ prəˈvɪnʃəl/ | adj. | 具有省份特色的；带有地方色彩的；思想狭隘的 |

英文解析：Quasi-provincial describes something that has characteristics of a province, often implying a limited perspective or narrow-mindedness. It suggests a lack of cosmopolitanism or a restricted outlook, similar to the views one might find in a smaller, less diverse region.  The word combines "quasi," meaning seemingly or partially, with "provincial," referring to a province or its characteristics.

例句：

His quasi-provincial views on art were considered outdated by the international art community.  他的艺术观点带有浓厚的地方色彩，在国际艺术界被认为过时了。


Her quasi-provincial upbringing led her to initially struggle to adapt to the fast-paced city life. 她从小在偏远地区长大，起初很难适应快节奏的城市生活。


近义词：insular, parochial, narrow-minded, small-town, limited, restricted


反义词：cosmopolitan, broad-minded, open-minded, worldly, international


词性: adjective
