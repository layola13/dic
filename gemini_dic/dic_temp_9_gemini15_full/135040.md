nonnobilities:/ˌnɒnˈnəʊbɪlətiz/ | n. |非贵族；平民；非贵族阶层 | The word "nonnobilities" refers to the collective body of people who are not of noble birth or rank; the commoners.  It emphasizes the absence of noble status.  The term is less common than its antonym, "nobility," and often used in a comparative or contrasting context. | Example: The chasm between the nobilities and the nonnobilities was stark in the 18th century. 18世纪贵族和非贵族之间的差距是巨大的。 | 近义词：commoners, plebeians, the masses | 反义词：nobility, aristocracy
