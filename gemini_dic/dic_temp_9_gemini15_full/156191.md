phratry:/ˈfreɪtri/| n. |宗族；部落；氏族 |A phratry is a social group in some societies, typically consisting of several clans or lineages united by a common ancestor or belief.  It often plays a significant role in social organization, kinship, and ritual practices.  The term is frequently used in anthropology and sociology to describe kinship systems in various cultures. |Example: The ancient Greek city-state was often divided into phratries, which played a crucial role in political and religious life.  希腊古代城邦通常被划分为若干个宗族，这些宗族在政治和宗教生活中扮演着至关重要的角色。|近义词：clan, tribe, lineage |反义词：individual, family (in the nuclear sense)
