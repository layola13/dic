connives:/kəˈnaɪvz/| v.|纵容；默许；密谋|To connive means to secretly allow something bad to happen or to secretly plan something bad with someone else.  It often implies a sense of guilty knowledge or participation. |例句：The principal connived at the cheating students. 校长纵容那些作弊的学生。

例句：They connived to overthrow the government. 他们密谋推翻政府。


近义词：abet, collude, conspire, tolerate, overlook|反义词：oppose, prevent, obstruct
