rhombogene:/ˈrɒmbədʒiːn/ | n. |菱形基因|A rhombogene is a gene expressed in the rhombomeres, segments of the developing hindbrain.  These genes play crucial roles in patterning the hindbrain and the structures that arise from it, such as cranial nerves and parts of the brain stem.  Their expression helps define the boundaries and identities of the rhombomeres, influencing the development of specific neuronal populations and their connections.|The expression patterns of rhombogenes are vital in understanding the formation of the hindbrain and the development of various neural structures.  Mutations in rhombogenes can lead to severe craniofacial abnormalities. |近义词：Hox gene (partially, as rhombogenes are a subset of Hox genes involved in hindbrain development)|反义词：无|
