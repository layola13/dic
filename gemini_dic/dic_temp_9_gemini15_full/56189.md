dodge:/dɑːdʒ/| /dɒdʒ/| vt. n.|躲避；闪避；逃避；欺骗；便宜货|to move quickly and suddenly to one side in order to avoid being hit by something or someone; to avoid something unpleasant or unwanted|例句：He dodged the blow. 他躲开了那一击。
The driver swerved to dodge a cyclist. 司机为了躲避一名骑自行车的人而突然转向。|近义词：avoid, evade, shun|反义词：confront, face


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dodges the questions skillfully. 他巧妙地回避了这些问题。
She dodges traffic skillfully. 她熟练地躲避交通。


一般过去时:
I dodged a bullet yesterday. 我昨天躲过了一劫。
The cat dodged the falling vase. 猫躲开了掉下来的花瓶。


现在完成时:
He has dodged the issue for years. 他多年来一直回避这个问题。
She has dodged responsibility for the mistake. 她一直逃避对这个错误的责任。


过去完成时:
He had dodged the police for months before he was finally caught. 他被捕前已经躲避警方数月之久。
She had dodged the question several times before finally answering. 她几次躲避这个问题后才最终回答。


将来时:
I will dodge the incoming ball. 我将会躲开飞来的球。
They will dodge the responsibility. 他们将会逃避责任。


虚拟语气:

If I were you, I'd dodge that conversation. 如果我是你，我会回避那段对话。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
