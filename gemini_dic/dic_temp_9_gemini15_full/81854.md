griddling:/ˈɡrɪdlɪŋ/| n. | 煎烤；用烤盘煎 | The act of cooking food on a griddle.  Griddling involves cooking food on a flat, heated surface, often resulting in nicely browned and slightly crispy edges. | Example: The aroma of onions griddling filled the kitchen.  煎洋葱的香味充满了厨房。|近义词：pan-frying, broiling|反义词：boiling, steaming

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He griddles pancakes every Sunday. = 他每个星期天都煎薄煎饼。
She griddles vegetables for a healthy side dish. = 她煎蔬菜作为健康的配菜。

一般过去时:
I griddled the steak until it was perfectly seared. = 我把牛排煎到完美焦香。
They griddled the fish with herbs and spices. = 他们用香草和香料煎鱼。

现在完成时:
We have griddled many burgers this summer. = 今年夏天我们已经煎了很多汉堡。
She has never griddled tofu before. = 她以前从未煎过豆腐。

过去完成时:
I had griddled the mushrooms before adding them to the pasta. = 我在把蘑菇添加到意面之前已经煎过了它们。
They had griddled the peppers and onions for the fajitas. = 他们已经为卷饼煎好了青椒和洋葱。

将来时:
I will griddle some sausages for breakfast tomorrow. = 我明天早餐会煎一些香肠。
She will griddle the chicken breast with lemon and garlic. = 她会用柠檬和大蒜煎鸡胸肉。

虚拟语气:
If I had a griddle, I would griddle some delicious vegetables. = 如果我有一个烤盘，我会煎一些美味的蔬菜。
I wish I had griddled the fish longer; it was slightly undercooked. = 我希望我把鱼煎久一点；它有点没熟透。

被动语态:

否定句:
