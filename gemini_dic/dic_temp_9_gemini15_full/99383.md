interdict: /ˈɪntərˌdɪkt/ | v. n. | 禁止；阻止；法院禁止令 |

英文解析:To officially forbid something, especially by law;  an official order forbidding something.  As a noun, it refers to the act of forbidding or the order itself.

例句:

The government interdicted the sale of the dangerous drug. = 政府禁止销售这种危险药物。

The court issued an interdict preventing the demolition of the historic building. = 法院发布了禁止拆除历史建筑的法令。


近义词:prohibit, forbid, ban, injunction

反义词:permit, allow, authorize


词性:Verb, Noun

现在完成时:

词源:
From Old French *interdire, from Latin interdicere "to forbid, prohibit," from inter- "between, among" + dicere "to say, speak."  The noun use developed later.
