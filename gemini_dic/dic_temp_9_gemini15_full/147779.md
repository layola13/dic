overpromoting:/ˌoʊvərprəˈmoʊtɪŋ/ | v. | 过度推销，晋升过快 |  The act of promoting something or someone excessively, often to the point of being ineffective or counterproductive.  It can refer to marketing a product too aggressively, or advancing someone in their career too rapidly. |例句：The company's overpromoting of its new product led to disillusionment among consumers. 公司对其新产品的过度推销导致消费者幻灭。

The aggressive overpromoting of the film resulted in a backlash from critics. 这部电影的过度宣传导致评论家们强烈反弹。

近义词：hype, overselling, pushing too hard,  excessive promotion |反义词：underselling, downplaying, suppressing

词源:
The word "overpromoting" is a combination of the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") and the verb "promoting" (the act of advancing or raising the status of something or someone).  Therefore, it literally means "promoting too much".
