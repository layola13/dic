inflexibility: /ˌɪnˌflɛksəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 不灵活；僵硬；顽固 | Lack of flexibility; rigidity; stubbornness.  The inflexibility of the rules made it difficult to adapt to changing circumstances.  | 近义词：rigidity, stiffness, inflexibility | 反义词：flexibility, adaptability, pliability

例句：

The inflexibility of the school's curriculum prevented students from exploring their interests. 学校课程的僵化阻碍了学生探索兴趣。

His inflexibility in negotiations led to a breakdown in communication. 他在谈判中的不灵活导致沟通中断。


词源：

* **in-:**  prefix meaning "not" or "without"
* **flexibility:**  from Latin *flexibilis*, meaning "capable of being bent."  This in turn derives from *flectere*, meaning "to bend."
* **-ity:** suffix forming nouns of quality or state.


USAGE->

This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  There are no tenses to illustrate.  However, we can show how it's used in different sentence structures:


肯定句:

The company's inflexibility is hindering its growth.  公司的僵化阻碍了它的发展。

His inflexibility of opinion made compromise impossible. 他意见的顽固使得妥协成为不可能。


否定句:

The new management showed that inflexibility wasn't necessary for success. 新管理层证明成功并不需要僵化。

There was no inflexibility in their approach to problem-solving. 他们在解决问题的方法上没有僵化。


疑问句:

Is the inflexibility of the system to blame for the delays? 系统的僵化是否应该为延误负责？

Does the inflexibility of the rules limit creativity? 规则的僵化是否限制了创造力？


PHRASE->

There are not common idiomatic phrases directly using "inflexibility."  However, we can show phrases that express similar concepts:

* **Lack of flexibility:** This is a more direct and common way to express the same idea.
* **Rigid adherence to:**  This phrase describes someone sticking stubbornly to rules or principles.
* **Unwillingness to compromise:** This highlights the refusal to bend one's position.
* **Inability to adapt:** This focuses on the lack of ability to change in response to new situations.
* **Set in their ways:** This describes people resistant to change.
