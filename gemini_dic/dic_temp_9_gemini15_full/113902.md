madrigalian:/ˌmædrɪˈɡeɪliən/| adj.|  牧歌体的；牧歌风格的 | Relating to or characteristic of a madrigal; having the qualities of a madrigal.  The term often describes music or poetry that is characterized by its sweetness, grace, and intricacy.  |例句：The madrigalian style of the poem is evident in its delicate imagery and intricate rhyme scheme. 这首诗歌的牧歌风格在其细腻的意象和复杂的韵律中显而易见。

例句：His compositions displayed a distinctly madrigalian charm, blending lyrical melodies with complex harmonies. 他的作品展现出一种独特的牧歌魅力，将抒情的旋律与复杂的和声融为一体。


近义词：pastoral, lyrical, melodious, sweet, graceful


反义词：harsh, discordant, jarring, rough, ungraceful


词源：From *madrigal* + *-ian*.  The word "madrigal" itself originates from the Italian word *madrigale*, possibly derived from the Provençal *madrigal*,  ultimately of uncertain origin, but possibly related to the Old French word *madris*, meaning "of the morning."
