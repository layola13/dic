languisher:/ˈlæŋɡwɪʃər/| n. | 衰弱者；憔悴者；苦苦挣扎的人 | A languisher is someone or something that is weak, fading, or declining.  It often implies a state of neglect or suffering. | Example:  After months in prison, he was a mere languisher, his spirit broken.  几个月监禁后，他变得憔悴不堪，精神崩溃。  |近义词：sufferer, victim, debilitated person | 反义词：thriver, flourishing person | 名词

一般现在时:
The plant languishes in the shade. = 植物在阴凉处枯萎。
He languishes in his current job. = 他对现在的工作感到厌倦。

一般过去时:
The prisoner languished in his cell for years. = 囚犯在牢房里 languished 多年。
She languished for weeks after the surgery. = 她手术后 languished 了几周。

现在完成时:
He has languished in poverty for too long. = 他贫困潦倒太久了。
The business has languished since the economic downturn. = 自经济衰退以来，这家企业一直不景气。

过去完成时:
The project had languished for months before they secured new funding. = 在他们获得新的资金之前，该项目已经 languished 了几个月。
She had languished in obscurity before her sudden rise to fame. = 在突然成名之前，她默默无闻了很久。

将来时:
Without proper care, the garden will languish next summer. = 如果不精心照料，花园明年夏天将会枯萎。
If he continues like this, he will languish in his career. = 如果他继续这样下去，他的事业将会停滞不前。


虚拟语气:
If he had received proper treatment, he wouldn't have languished. = 如果他接受了适当的治疗，就不会 languished 了。
I wish I hadn't languished in that dead-end job for so long. = 我真希望我没有在那份没有前途的工作上 languished 了那么久。


被动语态:
The plan was languished due to lack of funding. = 由于缺乏资金，该计划被搁置了。
The project has been languishing for years. = 这个项目已经 languished 了好几年了。


疑问句:
Is the economy languishing? = 经济是否正在衰退？
Why is he languishing in his career? = 为什么他的事业停滞不前？


否定句:
The business is not languishing; it's thriving. = 这家公司并没有衰退；它正在蓬勃发展。
He didn't languish in despair; he fought back. = 他并没有绝望地 languished；他奋起反抗。
