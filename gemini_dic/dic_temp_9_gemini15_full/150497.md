pantographic:/ˌpæn.təˈɡræf.ɪk/| adj.| 自动描图的；万向节的 | Relating to or using a pantograph.  A pantograph is a mechanical linkage that allows the reproduction of a drawing or image at a different scale. The term often describes devices used for copying or enlarging drawings, but can also refer to the mechanism itself. | Example: The engineer used a pantographic machine to enlarge the blueprint. 工程师用一台自动描图机放大蓝图。 | Near synonyms: copying, duplicating, enlarging | Antonyms:  reducing (in scale)
