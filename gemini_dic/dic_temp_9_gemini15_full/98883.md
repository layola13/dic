intends:/ɪnˈtɛndz/| v.|打算；计划；意图|To have in mind as a purpose or intention; plan.|例句：She intends to visit her parents next week. 她打算下周去看望父母。  例句：He intends to finish the project by Friday. 他打算在星期五之前完成这个项目。|近义词：plans, purposes, aims, proposes|反义词: neglects, ignores


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He intends to buy a new car. = 他打算买一辆新车。
She intends to travel to Europe next year. = 她打算明年去欧洲旅行。

一般过去时:
They intended to go to the beach, but it rained. = 他们本来打算去海滩，但是下雨了。
He intended to call you, but he forgot. = 他本来打算给你打电话，但是他忘了。

现在完成时:
I have intended to write to you for weeks, but I haven't had the time. = 我好几个星期都想给你写信，但是我一直没时间。
She has intended to quit her job for months, but she hasn't yet. = 她几个月来一直想辞职，但是她还没辞职。

过去完成时:
We had intended to leave early, but we missed the bus. = 我们本来打算早点离开，但是我们错过了公共汽车。
They had intended to finish the project by Friday, but they were delayed. = 他们本来打算在星期五之前完成项目，但是他们被耽搁了。

将来时:
He will intend to start his own business next year. = 他明年打算开始自己的事业。(注意：将来时态下，intend本身用将来时态较少见，通常用will+动词原形来表达未来的打算)
They will probably intend to attend the conference. = 他们可能会打算参加这次会议。(注意：将来时态下，intend本身用将来时态较少见，通常用will+动词原形来表达未来的打算)


虚拟语气:
I intended to go, but I was sick. = 我本来打算去的，但是我病了。(与过去事实相反)
If I had intended to hurt you, I would have apologized. = 如果我本来打算伤害你，我会道歉的。(与过去事实相反)


被动语态:
It was intended that the project should be completed by the end of the year. = 项目原本计划在年底完成。
The meeting was intended to resolve the conflict. = 会议的目的是为了解决冲突。


疑问句:
Do you intend to go to the party? = 你打算去参加聚会吗？
What does she intend to do with the money? = 她打算怎么用这笔钱？


否定句:
I do not intend to answer any more questions. = 我不打算再回答任何问题了。
He did not intend to cause any trouble. = 他并没有打算惹麻烦。



PHRASE->
intend to do something = 打算做某事
intend for somebody/something = 为某人/某物而准备
be intended to do something = 旨在做某事
intend on doing something = 打算做某事
have no intention of doing something = 没有打算做某事
