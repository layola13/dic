rappel:/ræˈpɛl/ | v. | 绳降；垂降 | To descend a steep cliff or rock face by using a rope.  | Example: The climbers rappelled down the mountain. 登山者沿山绳降而下。 | 近义词： descend, abseil | 反义词：ascend, climb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rappels down the cliff every weekend. = 他每个周末都沿悬崖绳降。
She rappels with great skill and precision. = 她绳降技术娴熟精准。


一般过去时:
We rappelled down the mountain yesterday. = 我们昨天沿山绳降而下。
The rescue team rappelled to the injured hiker. = 救援队绳降到受伤的徒步旅行者身边。


现在完成时:
They have rappelled many times before. = 他们以前绳降过很多次。
She has never rappelled before, but she's eager to try. = 她以前从未绳降过，但她渴望尝试。


过去完成时:
We had rappelled half way down before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，我们已经绳降到中途了。
They had never rappelled from such a high cliff before. = 他们以前从未从这么高的悬崖上绳降过。


将来时:
We will rappel down the waterfall tomorrow. = 我们明天将沿瀑布绳降而下。
They will rappel into the cave to explore it. = 他们将绳降进入洞穴进行探险。


虚拟语气:
If I had the right equipment, I would rappel down that cliff. = 如果我有合适的装备，我会沿那悬崖绳降而下。
I wish I could rappel as expertly as she does. = 我希望我能像她一样熟练地绳降。


被动语态:
The rescue operation was completed by rappelling down the building. = 救援行动是通过沿建筑物绳降完成的。
The equipment is regularly checked before rappelling. = 在绳降之前，设备会定期检查。


疑问句:
Have you ever rappelled before? = 你以前绳降过吗？
Will they rappel down the face of the mountain? = 他们会沿山崖绳降而下吗？


否定句:
I didn't rappel down the cliff; I climbed down. = 我没有沿悬崖绳降；我是爬下来的。
They will not rappel without proper safety equipment. = 没有合适的安全设备，他们不会绳降。


PHRASE->

rappel line = 绳降绳索


词源:
The word "rappel" is borrowed from French, where it means "to lower (oneself) by a rope."  The French word itself likely comes from an earlier form related to the verb "raper," meaning "to scrape" or "to rub," hinting at the friction involved in the descent.
