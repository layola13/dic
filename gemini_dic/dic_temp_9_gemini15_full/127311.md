naw:/nɑː/| int.|不，才不|A shortened, informal version of "no."  Often used in casual conversation to express disagreement or refusal.  It is primarily used in spoken English and is considered informal.|例句： "Want to go out tonight?" "Naw, I'm tired."  今晚想出去吗？“不，我累了。”|近义词：no, nope|反义词：yes, yeah


USAGE->
This word is an interjection and doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what."  It's used in simple, informal sentences.  

PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically built around "naw," as it is a very informal word used mostly on its own.
