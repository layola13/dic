segregates:/ˈsɛɡrɪɡeɪts/| v.|隔离；分离；种族隔离|To separate or set apart from others or from a main body.  This often implies a forced or discriminatory separation, particularly based on race, religion, or other group affiliations.|例句：The school segregated students by race. 学校按种族隔离学生。|例句：The prisoners were segregated according to the severity of their crimes. 囚犯们根据罪行的严重程度被隔离。|近义词：separate, isolate, divide, partition|反义词：integrate, unite, unify


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government segregates funding based on need. = 政府根据需要分配资金。
He segregates his recycling diligently. = 他认真地分类回收垃圾。


一般过去时:
The company segregated its employees into different departments. = 公司将其员工分到了不同的部门。
They segregated the wheat from the chaff. = 他们把麦粒和糠分离了。


现在完成时:
The city has segregated its waste collection system. = 该城市已经对其垃圾收集系统进行了分类。
South Africa has largely overcome the legacy of racial segregation. = 南非已在很大程度上克服了种族隔离的遗留问题。


过去完成时:
Before the Civil Rights Movement, many Southern states had long segregated their schools. = 在民权运动之前，许多南方州长期以来一直对其学校实行种族隔离。
They had already segregated the contaminated samples from the clean ones. = 他们已经将受污染的样本与清洁样本分开了。


将来时:
The new law will likely segregate certain types of hazardous waste. = 新法律可能会对某些类型的危险废物进行隔离。
The company plans to segregate its marketing efforts by region. = 公司计划按地区划分其营销工作。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:
