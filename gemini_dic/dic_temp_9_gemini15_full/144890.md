over-seed:/ˈoʊvərsiːd/ | vt. | 补播种子；再播种 | To sow seed on top of existing vegetation.  This is often done to thicken a lawn, improve its quality, or establish a new type of grass. | Example: We over-seeded the lawn in the fall to improve its density. 我们在秋天补播了草坪种子以增加其密度。|近义词：reseed, top-dress (with seed) | 反义词：None explicitly, but the opposite action would be to remove existing seeds or plants.
