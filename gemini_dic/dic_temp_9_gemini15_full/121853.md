misrepresentation:/ˌmɪsˌreprɛzənˈteɪʃən/ | n. |不当陈述；歪曲；虚假表示 |The act of misrepresenting something or someone; a false or misleading statement.  It involves presenting something in a way that is untrue or inaccurate, often to deceive or mislead. |例句：His misrepresentation of the facts led to a lot of confusion.  他的事实歪曲导致了很多混乱。

例句：The company was accused of misrepresentation in its advertising. 这家公司被指控在其广告中做了虚假陈述。


近义词：distortion, falsification, misstatement, deception |反义词：truth, accuracy, honesty |词性：名词

词源:
mis- (错误的，坏的) + representation (陈述，描述)  The prefix "mis-" indicates a wrong or faulty action. "Representation" comes from "represent," meaning to depict or describe something. Therefore, "misrepresentation" signifies a wrong or inaccurate depiction or description.
