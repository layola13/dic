disabuse:/ˌdɪs.əˈbjuːz/ | vt. | 使某人摆脱错误观念；纠正；使醒悟 | To free someone from a misconception or error. | 例句：He tried to disabuse her of the notion that all men are unreliable. 他试图让她摆脱所有男人都不可靠的观念。

例句：The evidence disabused me of my earlier belief. 证据让我放弃了之前的信念。


近义词：correct, enlighten, undeceive | 反义词：misinform, mislead, deceive | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disabuses his students of their misconceptions. = 他纠正学生们的错误观念。
She disabuses her friend of the rumor. = 她让她的朋友认清谣言的真相。


一般过去时:
The teacher disabused the students of their misunderstanding. = 老师纠正了学生们的误解。
The article disabused me of my prejudice. = 这篇文章让我摆脱了我的偏见。


现在完成时:
I have disabused him of the idea that he is perfect. = 我已经让他明白他并非完美无缺。
She has disabused many people of their false beliefs. = 她已经纠正了许多人的错误观念。


过去完成时:
I had disabused myself of the notion that he was honest. = 我已经认清了他并不诚实的事实。
They had disabused him of his unrealistic expectations. = 他们已经让他认清了他不切实际的期望。


将来时:

The meeting will disabuse them of their complacency. = 会议将使他们摆脱自满情绪。


虚拟语气:
If I could disabuse him of his prejudices, I would. = 如果我能让他摆脱偏见，我会这么做的。
I wish I could disabuse her of that dangerous belief. = 我真希望我能让她摆脱那个危险的信念。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
