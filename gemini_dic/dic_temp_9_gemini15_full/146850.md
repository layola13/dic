overindividualizations: /ˌoʊvərˌɪndɪvɪdʒuəlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. |过度个人化；过分强调个人化 | The excessive focus on individual needs and experiences to the detriment of collective goals or social cohesion.  This often manifests as a disregard for shared values and responsibilities, prioritizing personal preferences above all else.  It is a complex sociological concept with implications for social structures, communities, and political systems. | Example: The overindividualizations of modern society have led to a decline in community engagement and social trust.  过分强调个人化的现代社会导致了社区参与度和社会信任度的下降。 | 近义词: hyperindividualism, individualism taken to extremes, egoism, selfishness | 反义词: collectivism, communalism, altruism
