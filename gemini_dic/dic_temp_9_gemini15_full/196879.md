shackled:/ˈʃækl/| v. adj. |  束缚；囚禁；镣铐 | To restrain with shackles; to be burdened by something. |例句：The prisoner was shackled to the wall. 囚犯被铐在墙上。The country was shackled by economic hardship.  这个国家被经济困境所束缚。|近义词：chained, bound, fettered, confined |反义词：freed, liberated, unfettered


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He shackles the prisoners every night. 他每天晚上都给囚犯上镣铐。
They shackle their creativity with rigid rules. 他们用僵化的规则束缚了他们的创造力。

一般过去时:
The police shackled the suspect before taking him to the station. 警察在带嫌疑犯去警局之前给他戴上了手铐。
The heavy debt shackled the family for years.  沉重的债务多年来束缚着这个家庭。

现在完成时:
They have shackled the criminal and are transporting him now. 他们已经把罪犯铐了起来，现在正在押送他。
Years of poverty have shackled her spirit. 多年的贫困已经束缚了她的精神。

过去完成时:
The rebels had shackled the governor before the army arrived.  叛军在军队到达之前已经把州长绑了起来。
She had been shackled by doubt for weeks before making a decision. 在做出决定之前，她几周来一直被怀疑所束缚。

将来时:
The authorities will shackle the protesters if they become violent. 如果抗议者变得暴力，当局将逮捕他们。
The new regulations will shackle the growth of small businesses. 新的规章制度将限制小型企业的增长。

虚拟语气:
If they shackled him, he wouldn't be able to escape. 如果他们把他铐起来，他就无法逃脱。
I wish they hadn't shackled him so tightly. 我真希望他们没有把他绑得那么紧。

被动语态:
The prisoner was shackled and taken away. 囚犯被铐起来带走了。
His movements were shackled by his injuries. 他的行动受到了伤势的限制。

疑问句:
Were the prisoners shackled before the trial? 审判前囚犯被戴上手铐了吗？
Are their ambitions shackled by lack of funds? 他们的雄心壮志是否因为缺乏资金而受限？

否定句:
They didn't shackle the witness.  他们没有给证人戴上手铐。
The company is not shackled by outdated technology.  这家公司不受过时技术的束缚。


PHRASE->

词源:
From Middle English *shakel, from Old English *sceacul, from Proto-Germanic *skakulō ("fetter"), from Proto-Indo-European *skeg- ("to bind").  Ultimately related to words like "shake."  The original meaning referred to a shackle or fetter, a physical restraint.  The verb sense developed later.
