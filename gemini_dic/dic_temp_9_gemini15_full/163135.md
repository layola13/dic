precombinations:/ˌpriːkɒmbɪˈneɪʃənz/ | n. |预先组合，预先搭配 |A precombination refers to a combination or arrangement of things that is prepared or determined in advance.  It implies a planned selection or grouping before a specific application or event. This term is less common than its constituent parts and often used in specialized contexts. |Example: The software allows for precombinations of settings to streamline the workflow.  软件允许预先组合设置以简化工作流程。|近义词：preparatory combinations, advance arrangements |反义词：improvisation, spontaneity
