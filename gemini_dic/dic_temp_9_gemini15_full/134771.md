nonmetal:/ˈnɒnˌmɛtəl/ | n. |非金属|A chemical element that lacks the characteristics of a metal, typically being a poor conductor of heat and electricity, and readily forming negative ions.| Examples include oxygen, carbon, and sulfur.  Nonmetals are often brittle and lack ductility and malleability.|近义词: metalloid (sometimes), element (in the context of chemical elements)|反义词: metal
