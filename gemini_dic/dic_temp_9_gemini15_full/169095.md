proheme:/ˈproʊhiːm/| n. |序言，前言|A proheme is an introductory statement or passage, especially one preceding a literary or musical composition.  It's often used to set the scene or establish the theme.|Example: The author included a lengthy proheme to his novel, outlining the historical context.  作者在他的小说中加入了一段冗长的序言，概述了历史背景。|近义词：preface, introduction, prelude, prologue|反义词：conclusion, epilogue


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "what".  Therefore, grammatical tenses are not applicable.  The usage focuses on its function within a text.


Example 1: The proheme of the symphony was surprisingly melancholic.  交响乐的序言出人意料地忧郁。

Example 2:  The lengthy proheme delayed the start of the main narrative.  冗长的序言延缓了主要叙事的开始。
