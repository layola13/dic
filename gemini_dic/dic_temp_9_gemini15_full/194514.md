self-satisfiedly:/ˌsɛlfˈsætɪsfaɪdəli/| adv.| 自鸣得意地；沾沾自喜地 |  In a self-satisfied manner; with an air of smug complacency.  It describes the way someone behaves when they are excessively pleased with themselves and their achievements. |例句：He self-satisfiedly announced his promotion. 他自鸣得意地宣布了他的晋升。

He smiled self-satisfiedly after winning the game.  他赢得比赛后自鸣得意地笑了。


近义词：complacently, smugly, contentedly (but with a negative connotation) |反义词：humbly, modestly, sheepishly


词源:

* **self-satisfied:** This is a compound adjective formed from:
    * **self-**:  A prefix indicating something is related to oneself.
    * **satisfied:** Past participle of the verb "satisfy," meaning to fulfill a need or desire.  "Satisfy" ultimately derives from the Latin *satisfacere*, meaning "to make enough" or "to satisfy."  *Satis* means "enough," and *facere* means "to make" or "to do."

* **-ly:** This is an adverbial suffix, transforming the adjective "self-satisfied" into an adverb.


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it modifies verbs.  It doesn't have tenses like verbs do.  However, it can be used in various sentence structures.

例句1：He spoke self-satisfiedly about his accomplishments. 他自鸣得意地谈论着自己的成就。
例句2：She smiled self-satisfiedly, ignoring the criticism. 她自鸣得意地笑了笑，无视了批评。


PHRASE->
There are no common set phrases specifically using "self-satisfiedly."  However, it often appears in phrases like:

* self-satisfiedly declared

* self-satisfiedly explained
