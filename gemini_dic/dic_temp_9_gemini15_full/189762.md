saltwort:/ˈsɔːltwɜːrt/| n. |盐生植物 |A succulent plant that grows in salty or alkaline soil, often near the coast.  Many species belong to the family Chenopodiaceae.  They are often characterized by their ability to tolerate high salt concentrations. |Example:  The saltwort thrived in the harsh, saline environment of the coastal dunes.  盐生植物在沿海沙丘恶劣的盐碱环境中茁壮成长。|近义词：halophyte |反义词： (lacks a direct antonym, could use a plant that thrives in fresh water)
