/kaɪˈæzmɪ/| n. | 交叉；(修辞)交叉对应；(解剖)交叉 | A chiasm is a rhetorical or literary figure of speech in which two or more clauses are balanced against each other by the reversal of their structures in order to produce an artistic effect.  It can also refer to a crisscross or intersection, particularly in anatomy (e.g., the optic chiasm). | Example: "Ask not what your country can do for you—ask what you can do for your country." (John F. Kennedy)  这是一个交叉对应结构的例子，前半句和后半句结构相反，但意思相关。|近义词：cross, intersection, antithesis |反义词：parallelism
