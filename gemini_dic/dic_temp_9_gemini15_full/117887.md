/ˈmiːloʊ/| n. |芫菁属昆虫|A meloe is a type of beetle belonging to the family Meloidae, commonly known as blister beetles.  They are characterized by their soft bodies, often with bright warning coloration, and are known for producing cantharidin, a toxic substance that causes blisters upon contact with skin.  Some species are also known for their unusual life cycle involving hyperparasitism. |Example: The farmer found a meloe crawling on his crops.|近义词: blister beetle |反义词:|
