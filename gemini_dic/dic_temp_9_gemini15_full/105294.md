kibbitzed:/ˈkɪbɪtzt/| v.|喋喋不休地提建议；插嘴；瞎掺和|To offer unsolicited advice or comments, often in a meddling or intrusive way.  |例句：He constantly kibbitzed during the game, offering unhelpful suggestions. 他在比赛中不断地插嘴，提出毫无帮助的建议。

例句：  Don't kibbitz while I'm trying to concentrate.  别在我试图集中注意力的时候插嘴。


近义词：butt in, interfere, meddle, intervene, intrude


反义词：remain silent, mind one's own business


词性:动词

词源:
The word "kibbitz" likely comes from Yiddish קיבוץ (kibbuts), meaning "gathering" or "group."  In Yiddish, kibbitzing originally referred to friendly chatting or gossiping within a group.  Over time, the meaning shifted to its current connotation of intrusive and unsolicited advice.
