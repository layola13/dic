preambition:/ˌpriːæmˈbɪʃən/| n.| 预先的雄心；早期的抱负 |  A preambition is an ambition or aspiration that is formed or conceived before something else happens or is achieved.  It often refers to a goal or desire that precedes a major accomplishment or event.  The term highlights the planning and foresight involved in achieving success. | Example: His preambition to become a renowned surgeon drove him through years of rigorous medical training.  他的成为著名外科医生的预先雄心驱使他经过了数年的严格医学训练。|近义词：early aspiration, prior ambition,  forethought|反义词： afterthought,  unforeseen goal | 名词
