misrhymed:/ˌmɪsˈraɪmd/ | adj. | 押韵错误的，不押韵的 |  Describes words or lines of verse that appear to rhyme but do not actually rhyme perfectly, either because of slight differences in pronunciation or because of using near rhymes (also called slant rhymes or half rhymes).  | Example: "The poem had a few misrhymed couplets, jarring the reader's sense of rhythm." | 近义词：ill-rhymed, poorly rhymed, off-rhyme, near rhyme (as a noun)| 反义词：perfectly rhymed, true rhyme
