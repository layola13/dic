presignals:/ˈpriːsɪɡnəlz/| n.|预兆，前兆|Signs or indications that something is likely to happen in the future.  Presignals often provide warnings or clues about impending events.  They are not definitive predictions but rather suggestive pointers.|例句：The unusual bird migration patterns were presignals of the approaching hurricane.  异常的候鸟迁徙模式是即将到来的飓风的预兆。

The drop in stock prices served as presignals of a coming recession.  股价下跌是即将到来的经济衰退的预兆。|近义词：forewarnings, harbingers, omens, portents|反义词：aftereffects, consequences, results
