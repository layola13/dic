lucking-out: /ˈlʌkiŋ aʊt/ | v. | 意外地成功；侥幸成功 | To experience unexpected good fortune or success; to have a stroke of luck. | 例句：I was lucky to get the job, I was really lucking out. 我很幸运得到了这份工作，我真的很走运。  They lucked out and found a parking spot right in front of the building.他们很幸运，在建筑物正前方找到一个停车位。 | 近义词：getting lucky, striking it rich, hitting the jackpot | 反义词：being unlucky, failing, suffering misfortune |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He's lucking out in his career. = 他的事业很顺利。
They are lucking out with the weather for their picnic. = 他们野餐的天气很好。


一般过去时:
We lucked out and found a great deal on a hotel. = 我们很幸运，找到了一家物美价廉的酒店。
She lucked out on the exam, even though she didn't study much. = 尽管她没怎么学习，但她考试却很幸运地考得不错。


现在完成时:
I've been lucking out lately with job interviews. = 最近我的求职面试都很顺利。
They have lucked out with the weather this summer. = 今年夏天他们的天气一直很好。


过去完成时:
We had lucked out by finding a table at the restaurant without a reservation. = 我们很幸运，在餐厅没预订的情况下找到了一张桌子。
She had lucked out on several occasions before finally securing her dream job. = 在最终获得梦想工作之前，她已经好几次走运了。


将来时:
I hope we'll luck out and win the lottery! = 我希望我们会幸运地赢得彩票！
They think they'll luck out and find a perfect apartment. = 他们认为他们会幸运地找到一套完美的公寓。


虚拟语气:
If we lucked out, we could afford a new car. = 如果我们走运的话，就能买得起一辆新车。
I wish I had lucked out in the job market. = 我希望我在就业市场上能走运。


被动语态: *(This verb is rarely used in the passive voice.)*
The team were lucked out by a fortunate turn of events.  =  这个团队因为幸运的事件而走运了。(较为生硬，不常用)
The project was lucked out by the unexpected support from investors. = 这个项目因为投资者意外的支持而获得成功。(较为生硬，不常用)


疑问句:
Did they luck out with their investment? = 他们的投资顺利吗？
Will we luck out and find tickets to the concert? = 我们会幸运地买到演唱会的门票吗？


否定句:
We didn't luck out with the weather for our hike. = 我们徒步旅行的天气不好。
They didn't luck out on the test and failed. = 他们考试没考好，不及格了。


PHRASE->
luck out on something  = 在某事上走运
luck out with something  = 在某事上走运
luck out in something  = 在某方面走运
to luck out big time  = 非常幸运
just lucked out  = 纯粹是运气好
