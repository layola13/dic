pre-positioning:/ˌpriːpəˈzɪʃənɪŋ/| n. |预先定位；预先放置|The act of placing something in a position beforehand.  It often implies preparation or anticipation.  This term is less common than other ways of expressing the same idea. |例句：The pre-positioning of troops ensured a swift victory.  预先部署军队确保了迅速的胜利。|近义词：pre-placement, advance positioning|反义词：post-positioning


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company is undertaking pre-positioning of its resources. = 公司正在进行资源的预先配置。
We are pre-positioning supplies for the upcoming storm. = 我们正在为即将到来的风暴预先储备物资。


一般过去时:
They pre-positioned the equipment before the earthquake. = 他们在地震前预先放置了设备。
The army pre-positioned its forces near the border. = 军队在边境附近预先部署了兵力。


现在完成时:
The organization has pre-positioned aid for the disaster-stricken area. = 该组织已为灾区预先配置了援助物资。
We have pre-positioned our marketing materials for the product launch. = 我们已经为产品发布预先准备了营销材料。


过去完成时:
They had pre-positioned the rescue teams before the flood hit. = 洪水来临之前，他们已经预先部署了救援队。
The company had pre-positioned its inventory in anticipation of increased demand. = 公司预料到需求增加，已经预先储备了库存。


将来时:
We will pre-position the cameras for the event. = 我们将为活动预先放置摄像机。
The government will pre-position emergency supplies in case of a hurricane. = 政府将预先储备紧急物资以防飓风来袭。


虚拟语气:
If we had pre-positioned the supplies, the relief effort would have been more effective. = 如果我们预先储备了物资，救援工作会更有效。
I wish we had pre-positioned the equipment; it would have saved us time. = 我希望我们预先放置了设备；那会节省我们的时间。


被动语态:
The materials were pre-positioned at the warehouse. = 材料已预先放置在仓库中。
The troops were pre-positioned strategically across the region. = 部队已在该地区战略性地预先部署。


疑问句:
Were the supplies pre-positioned correctly? = 物资是否被正确地预先放置？
Have the emergency teams been pre-positioned? = 紧急救援队是否已预先部署？


否定句:
The relief supplies were not pre-positioned effectively. = 救援物资没有得到有效地预先配置。
They did not pre-position enough resources for the project. = 他们没有为该项目预先配置足够的资源。
