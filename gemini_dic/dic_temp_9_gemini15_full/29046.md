cartulary:/ˈkɑːrtʃʊləri/ | n. | 契约集；文书簿；登记簿 | A cartulary is a collection of charters and other legal documents, typically kept by a monastery or other institution.  It served as a record of land ownership, privileges, and other important matters. | Example: The monastery's cartulary detailed centuries of land transactions and legal disputes.  修道院的契约集详细记录了几个世纪以来的土地交易和法律纠纷。 | 近义词：register, archive, record | 反义词：None
