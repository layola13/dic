idol:/ˈaɪd(ə)l/| n. |偶像，崇拜对象 |A person or thing that is greatly admired, loved, or revered.  Idols can be celebrities, religious figures, or even objects. |例句：She's an idol to many young girls. 她对许多年轻女孩来说是偶像。|例句：He made an idol out of his late father. 他把他已故的父亲视为偶像。|近义词：icon, hero, role model |反义词：villain, enemy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is my idol. = 他是我的偶像。
They admire their idols. = 他们崇拜他们的偶像。

一般过去时:
She was a pop idol in the 1990s. = 她90年代是流行偶像。
He idolized his older brother. = 他崇拜他的哥哥。

现在完成时:
I have always admired her as an idol. = 我一直把她视为偶像。
Many people have followed this idol's career. = 许多人追随这位偶像的职业生涯。

过去完成时:
He had already been an idol for many years before he retired. = 他在退休前已经做了很多年的偶像。
They had never met their idol before that concert. = 在那场音乐会之前，他们从未见过他们的偶像。

将来时:
She will be a great idol in the future. = 她将来会成为伟大的偶像。
He will continue to be an idol for many years to come. = 他在未来很多年里都将继续成为偶像。

虚拟语气:
If I met my idol, I would be so excited. = 如果我遇到我的偶像，我会非常兴奋。
I wish I could meet my idol someday. = 我希望有一天能见到我的偶像。

被动语态:
He was idolized by millions of fans. = 他被数百万粉丝崇拜。
She is considered an idol by many people. = 她被许多人视为偶像。

疑问句:
Who is your idol? = 你最崇拜谁？
Is she really your idol? = 她真的是你的偶像吗？

否定句:
He is not my idol. = 他不是我的偶像。
She wasn't a very good role model for the youth. = 她并非一个很好的青少年榜样。(此例说明并非所有偶像都是好的榜样)


PHRASE->

idol worship = 偶像崇拜
