ludditism:/ˈlʌdaɪtɪzəm/| n. |卢德主义；反对技术进步|The rejection of new technology or technological change, often associated with violent or destructive actions.  This term derives from the Luddites, a group of 19th-century English textile workers who protested against the introduction of new machinery that they believed threatened their jobs.|例句：The rise of automation has led to fears of a new wave of Ludditism.  自动化技术的兴起导致人们担心会出现新一轮的卢德主义运动。
例句：His Ludditism prevented him from adopting more efficient methods. 他的卢德主义思想阻止了他采用更高效的方法。|近义词：technophobia, anti-technology sentiment |反义词：technophilia, innovation

USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb, adjective, or adverb forms in common usage.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  However, we can illustrate its usage in various sentence structures.

In a declarative sentence:
His Ludditism was rooted in a fear of unemployment. = 他的卢德主义根源于对失业的恐惧。
The Ludditism of the early industrial revolution is a cautionary tale. = 工业革命早期的卢德主义是一个警示性的故事。


In an interrogative sentence:
Is this company's resistance to change a form of Ludditism? = 这家公司对变革的抵制是一种卢德主义吗？
Was the destruction of the machinery an act of Ludditism? = 破坏机器的行为是卢德主义行为吗？


In an exclamatory sentence:
The sheer Ludditism of their response was astonishing! = 他们回应中纯粹的卢德主义令人震惊！
Such blatant Ludditism is unacceptable in today's world! = 在当今世界，如此公然的卢德主义是不可接受的！


In a sentence with a modifying phrase:
The Ludditism, fueled by economic hardship, spread rapidly. = 由经济困难推动的卢德主义迅速蔓延。
A well-reasoned critique of technology is not the same as Ludditism, characterized by violence and destruction. = 对技术的合理批判与以暴力和破坏为特征的卢德主义不同。
