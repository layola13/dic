necropolises:/nɪˈkrɒpəliːz/| n. |死城；墓地群 |A necropolis is a large, designed cemetery, often of historical or archaeological significance, containing many tombs and other funerary structures.  Necropolises can range in size from a small collection of tombs to vast complexes spanning many acres. They often reflect the cultural and religious beliefs of the people who built them. |The ancient Egyptian necropolis of Giza is famous for its pyramids and Sphinx.  埃及吉萨的古埃及墓地以其金字塔和狮身人面像而闻名。|cemeteries, burial grounds, burial sites| (近义词无明确反义词，可考虑“living areas”, “inhabited places”)
