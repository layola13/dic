quasi-defeated:/ˈkwɑːziˌdiːˈfiːtɪd/| adj. |几近失败的，惨败的，几乎被打败的 | Almost defeated; on the verge of defeat; suffering a near-defeat.  The term implies a close call, a situation where defeat was narrowly avoided. |例句：The army was quasi-defeated, losing most of its supplies and a significant number of soldiers but managing to escape with its core leadership.|近义词：nearly defeated, almost defeated, badly beaten |反义词：victorious, triumphant
