chancre:/ˈʃæŋkər/| n. |硬下疳|A chancre is a painless sore, typically the first sign of syphilis. It's a firm, round, and usually painless ulcer that appears at the site of infection.  Chancres can appear on the genitals, mouth, or anus. They usually heal within 3 to 6 weeks, even without treatment, but the underlying syphilis infection persists. |The patient presented with a chancre on his penis, indicative of primary syphilis.  这位病人阴茎上长了一个硬下疳，表明是梅毒初期。|ulcer, sore |healthy skin|名词
