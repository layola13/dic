shudder:/ˈʃʌdə(r)/| v.|颤抖，哆嗦，不寒而栗|To tremble convulsively, typically as a result of fear, revulsion, or cold.  |例句：She shuddered at the thought of the operation. 她一想到手术就打了个寒颤。

例句：A shudder ran through her as she heard the news.  听到这个消息，她浑身一颤。


近义词：tremble, shiver, shake, quiver|反义词：calm, still


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He shudders at the sight of blood. = 他看到血就颤抖。
The old house shudders in the wind. = 老房子在风中摇晃。


一般过去时:
She shuddered when she saw the snake. = 她看到蛇时打了个寒颤。
The ground shuddered violently during the earthquake. = 地震时地面剧烈震动。


现在完成时:
I have shuddered at the thought of it ever since. = 从那以后，我一想到它就害怕。
The building has shuddered several times due to the recent construction. = 由于最近的施工，这座建筑物已经摇晃了好几次。


过去完成时:
She had shuddered at the sight of the accident before she fainted. = 在她晕倒之前，她已经对事故的景象感到害怕。
The car had shuddered violently before it finally stopped. = 汽车剧烈震动后才最终停了下来。


将来时:

The bridge will shudder under the weight of the heavy truck. = 大桥将在重型卡车的重量下颤抖。


虚拟语气:

I wish I hadn't shuddered so much; it made me look weak. = 我真希望自己没有那么害怕，那样看起来就不会那么软弱。


被动语态:

否定句:
