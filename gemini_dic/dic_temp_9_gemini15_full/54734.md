disparages:/dɪˈspærɪdʒ/ | vt. |贬低；轻蔑；诋毁 | To speak of or treat (someone or something) in a way that shows a lack of respect or approval.  |例句：He disparaged her efforts, saying they were insignificant. 他贬低了她的努力，说它们微不足道。

例句：Don't disparage his achievements; he worked very hard for them. 不要贬低他的成就；他为此付出了很大的努力。


近义词：belittle, denigrate, demean, slight, scorn, underestimate |反义词：praise, commend, extol, laud

词源：源自古法语 *desparager，由des-（否定前缀）和parager（比较，匹配）构成，字面意思为“使不相称”。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disparages his opponent's ideas in every debate. 他在每一次辩论中都贬低对手的观点。
She disparages the efforts of her team members regularly. 她经常贬低团队成员的努力。


一般过去时:
He disparaged her singing ability last night. 他昨晚贬低了她的歌唱能力。
The critic disparaged the actor's performance in the play. 批评家贬低了这位演员在该剧中的表演。


现在完成时:
He has disparaged her work throughout the project. 他在整个项目中一直贬低她的工作。
She has disparaged her colleagues' contributions to the report. 她一直贬低同事对报告的贡献。


过去完成时:
He had disparaged her intelligence before she even started speaking.  在她开始说话之前，他就已经贬低了她的智力。
They had disparaged the company's new product before it even launched.  产品发布之前，他们就贬低了公司的新品。


将来时:
He will disparage her ideas in the upcoming meeting. 他将在即将举行的会议上贬低她的想法。
She will disparage her competitor's products in her advertisement. 她将在广告中贬低竞争对手的产品。


虚拟语气:
If he disparaged her work, she would be very upset. 如果他贬低她的工作，她会非常难过。
I wish he hadn't disparaged her efforts.  我希望他不要贬低她的努力。


被动语态:
Her ideas were disparaged by the committee. 她的想法被委员会贬低了。
His contributions were disparaged by his supervisor. 他的贡献被他的主管贬低了。


疑问句:
Does he disparage others to make himself feel better? 他是否贬低别人来让自己感觉更好？
Did she disparage his work on purpose? 她是有意贬低他的工作吗？


否定句:
He does not disparage his colleagues' work. 他不贬低同事的工作。
She did not disparage his efforts, instead she offered constructive criticism. 她没有贬低他的努力，而是提出了建设性批评。
