idolize:/ˈaɪdəlaɪz/| vt.|崇拜；偶像化 |To admire and respect someone very much, often more than is realistic.|例句：Many young people idolize famous singers and actors.  许多年轻人崇拜著名的歌手和演员。
例句：She idolized her older sister growing up. 她从小就崇拜她的姐姐。|近义词：worship, adore, revere|反义词：despise, scorn, disdain|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He idolizes his favorite sports star. = 他崇拜他最喜欢的体育明星。
They idolize their parents. = 他们崇拜他们的父母。

一般过去时:
I idolized that band when I was a teenager. = 我十几岁的时候崇拜那个乐队。
She idolized her grandfather. = 她崇拜她的祖父。

现在完成时:
He has idolized this musician for years. = 他多年来一直崇拜这位音乐家。
She has idolized that actress since childhood. = 她从小就崇拜那位女演员。

过去完成时:
I had idolized her for so long that meeting her was surreal. = 我崇拜了她这么久，见到她感觉很不真实。
They had idolized the leader before the truth about him was revealed. = 在真相大白之前，他们一直崇拜着这位领导人。

将来时:
I think he will continue to idolize her. = 我认为他会继续崇拜她。
They will still idolize him for his accomplishments. = 他们仍然会因为他的成就而崇拜他。

虚拟语气:
If I were to idolize anyone, it would be her. = 如果我要崇拜任何人，那将是她。
I wish I hadn't idolized him so blindly. = 我真希望我没有那么盲目地崇拜他。

被动语态:
He was idolized by millions of fans. = 他被数百万粉丝崇拜。
That singer is idolized by young people worldwide. = 那位歌手受到全世界年轻人的崇拜。

疑问句:
Do you idolize anyone? = 你崇拜任何人吗？
Whom did they idolize in their youth? = 他们年轻时崇拜谁？

否定句:
I don't idolize celebrities. = 我不崇拜名人。
They didn't idolize anyone in particular. = 他们并没有特别崇拜任何人。


PHRASE->
idolatrous devotion = 狂热的崇拜
blindly idolize = 盲目崇拜
cease to idolize = 不再崇拜
begin to idolize = 开始崇拜
idols to idolize = 崇拜的偶像

词源:
From the word "idol," meaning an image or representation of a god, combined with the suffix "-ize," meaning "to make or become."  The word thus literally means "to make into an idol" or "to treat as an idol."  The root traces back to Greek εἰδωλον (eidōlon), meaning "image, phantom."
