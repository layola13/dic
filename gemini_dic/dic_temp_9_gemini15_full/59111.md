dyking:/ˈdaɪkɪŋ/| n.|筑堤；筑堤工程|The act of building or constructing dikes, typically to control flooding or water levels.  It often involves the use of earth, rock, or other materials to create a barrier. |例句：The dyking project successfully protected the farmland from the rising river.  筑堤工程成功地保护了农田免受河水上涨的侵害。

The dyking along the coast is crucial for preventing coastal erosion.沿海的筑堤工程对于防止海岸侵蚀至关重要。|近义词：embankment, damming|反义词：breaching (a dike), flooding|名词

词源:
The word "dyking" comes from the Middle Dutch word "dijken," meaning "to build a dike."  This ultimately derives from the Proto-Germanic *dīkō, meaning "ditch" or "dike."  The modern English word "dike" itself is also a direct descendant of this Middle Dutch word.
