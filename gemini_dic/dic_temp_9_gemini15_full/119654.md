middlemanship: /ˈmɪdlʃɪpmənʃɪp/ | n. | 中间商业务；中间人的作用 | The act or practice of acting as a middleman; intermediation.  It often implies a degree of control or manipulation by the middleman.  | 例句：The company avoids middlemanship by dealing directly with producers. 这家公司通过直接与生产商交易来避免中间商环节。


The excessive middlemanship in the supply chain drove up prices. 供应链中过多的中间商环节推高了价格。| 近义词：intermediation, brokerage, agency | 反义词：direct dealing,  producer-consumer relationship |  词性：名词
