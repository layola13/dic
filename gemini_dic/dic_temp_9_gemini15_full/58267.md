drunk:/drʌŋk/| adj. n. v.|喝醉的；醉汉；使喝醉|

英文解析:
As an adjective, "drunk" describes someone who has consumed too much alcohol and is intoxicated. As a noun, it refers to a person who is intoxicated. As a verb (less common), it means to make someone drunk by giving them alcohol.

例句:
He was too drunk to drive home. 他醉得无法开车回家。

The drunk man stumbled down the street.  那个醉汉摇摇晃晃地走下街道。


近义词:
intoxicated, inebriated, tipsy, soused, plastered

反义词:
sober


词性:
adjective, noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is drunk. 他喝醉了。
They are often drunk at parties. 他们在派对上经常喝醉。

一般过去时:
He was drunk last night. 他昨晚喝醉了。
She got drunk at the wedding. 她在婚礼上喝醉了。

现在完成时:
He has been drunk several times this month. 这个月他已经喝醉好几次了。
She has never been drunk before. 她以前从未喝醉过。

过去完成时:
He had been drunk the previous night, so he couldn't remember anything. 他前一天晚上喝醉了，所以什么都不记得了。
They had already gotten drunk before the party even started. 派对还没开始，他们就已经喝醉了。

将来时:
He will be drunk if he keeps drinking. 如果他继续喝下去，他会喝醉的。
I think they will get drunk at the concert. 我认为他们在音乐会上会喝醉。

虚拟语气:
If he weren't drunk, he would drive us home. 如果他没有喝醉，他会开车送我们回家。
I wish I hadn't gotten so drunk last night. 我真希望昨晚我没有喝得那么醉。

被动语态:
He was gotten drunk by his friends. 他被他的朋友灌醉了。(比较少用)
The partygoers were drunk by the end of the night. 到晚上结束时，参加派对的人都喝醉了。(比较少用)


疑问句:
Are you drunk? 你喝醉了吗？
Was he drunk when he fell? 他摔倒的时候喝醉了吗？

否定句:
He is not drunk. 他没有喝醉。
She wasn't drunk; she was just tired. 她没有喝醉；她只是累了。



PHRASE->

词源:
The word "drunk" comes from the Old English word "druncen," which is the past participle of the verb "drinkan" (to drink).  The meaning evolved from simply having drunk something to specifically meaning intoxicated by alcohol.
