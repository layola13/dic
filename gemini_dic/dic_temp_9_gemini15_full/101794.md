irrigate:/ˈɪrɪɡeɪt/| vt.|灌溉；冲洗；冲净 |To supply water to (land) artificially.  It also refers to washing or flushing out a bodily part or wound.|例句：Farmers irrigate their fields to ensure a good harvest. 农民灌溉他们的田地以确保获得好收成。  The doctor irrigated the wound to remove debris.医生冲洗伤口以清除碎屑。|近义词：water, flush, flush out|反义词：dehydrate, dry out|动词


USAGE->

They irrigate their crops daily. = 他们每天灌溉庄稼。
The farmer irrigates his fields using a sprinkler system. = 农民使用喷灌系统灌溉他的田地。

词源:
From Latin *irrigare, from in- "in" + rigare "to water".  The word ultimately derives from Proto-Indo-European *h₂reǵ- "to make straight, to rule, to direct."  The connection lies in the idea of directing water to a specific area.
