reciter:/rɪˈsaɪtər/ | n. |朗诵者，背诵者 | A person who recites something, especially poetry or prose.  |例句：The reciter's voice was clear and resonant.  朗诵者的声音清晰而洪亮。 |例句：She is a gifted reciter, capable of captivating her audience. 她是一位有天赋的朗诵者，能够吸引她的观众。|近义词：reader, performer, speaker |反义词：listener, audience


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He recites poetry every evening. = 他每天晚上朗诵诗歌。
She recites the alphabet to her students. = 她给学生们朗诵字母表。

一般过去时:
He recited a Shakespearean sonnet. = 他朗诵了一首莎士比亚十四行诗。
She recited the poem from memory. = 她背诵了这首诗。

现在完成时:
He has recited this poem many times. = 他已经朗诵过这首诗很多次了。
She has recited at several public events. = 她已经在几个公共场合朗诵过。

过去完成时:
He had recited the poem before the audience arrived. = 在观众到来之前，他已经朗诵了这首诗。
She had recited the entire play before the final act. = 在最后一幕之前，她已经朗诵了整部戏剧。

将来时:
He will recite at the school's talent show. = 他将在学校的才艺表演中朗诵。
She will recite a new poem she wrote. = 她将朗诵一首她新写的诗。

虚拟语气:
If he recited more clearly, the audience would understand better. = 如果他朗诵得更清晰，观众就会更容易理解。
I wish she had recited the poem more slowly. = 我希望她朗诵这首诗时能慢一些。

被动语态:

The speech was recited flawlessly.  演讲被完美地朗诵出来。

疑问句:

否定句:
