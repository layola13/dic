hideyoshi:/ˌhiːdəˈjoʊʃi/| n. |丰臣秀吉|Hideyoshi, also known as Toyotomi Hideyoshi, was a powerful Japanese military commander, statesman, and the de facto ruler of Japan in the late 16th century.  He rose from humble beginnings to unify Japan after a long period of civil war. He is known for his military genius, his ambitious land reforms, and his attempts to establish a centralized government.
