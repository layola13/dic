quasi-socialistic:/ˈkwɑːziˌsoʊʃəlɪstɪk/ | adj. |准社会主义的；似社会主义的 |  Approaching or resembling socialism but not completely or fully implementing its principles.  It suggests a system with some socialist characteristics but also significant elements of capitalism or other economic models. | Example: The country's economic policies are described as quasi-socialistic, with strong state intervention but also a significant private sector.  这个国家的经济政策被描述为准社会主义的，国家干预强烈，但也存在重要的私营部门。| 近义词：socialist-leaning, semi-socialist | 反义词：capitalistic, free-market

词源：
The word "quasi" is derived from the Latin word "quasi," meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."  "Socialistic" is derived from "socialism," which itself originates from the Latin word "socius" (companion) and the suffix "-ism" (denoting a doctrine or system). Therefore, "quasi-socialistic" combines the idea of something resembling socialism without being fully or purely socialist.
