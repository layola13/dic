pyroacid:/paɪroʊˈæsɪd/| n. |焦酸|A pyroacid is an acid anhydride formed by the removal of water from two molecules of an oxyacid.  Pyroacids are often formed by heating the corresponding oxyacid. For example, heating phosphoric acid (H₃PO₄) can produce pyrophosphoric acid (H₄P₂O₇).|Example:  Heating sulfuric acid can lead to the formation of a pyroacid.|近义词：condensation product (in this specific chemical context)|反义词：无
