detraction:/dɪˈtrækʃən/| n. |诋毁；贬低；减损；减低|The act of taking away or reducing something; the act of diminishing the value, importance, or reputation of something or someone.  Detraction can refer to taking something away physically, or figuratively, by belittling or criticizing.|例句：His constant detractions undermined her confidence. 他不断的诋毁破坏了她的自信。

例句：The constant noise was a detraction from the peaceful atmosphere.  持续的噪音破坏了宁静的氛围。


近义词：criticism, disparagement, belittling, denigration, defamation, diminution|反义词：praise, commendation, enhancement, elevation|词性:noun


词源:  From the Latin *detractio*, from *detractus*, past participle of *detrahere* "to draw away, drag down, detract," from *de-* "down, away" + *trahere* "to draw, pull."

过去完成时:
