indigestible: /ˌɪnˈdɪdʒɛstəbəl/ | adj. |难以消化的；难以理解的 |  Difficult or impossible to digest; unable to be digested by the body.  It also refers to something that is difficult to understand or accept. | 例句：The steak was indigestible.  = 那块牛排难以消化。

The complex argument was indigestible to the average reader. =  这个复杂的论点对于普通读者来说难以理解。| 近义词：unpalatable, undigestible, incomprehensible | 反义词：digestible, palatable, understandable | 词性: adjective


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This food is indigestible. = 这食物难以消化。
That explanation is indigestible. = 那个解释难以理解。


一般过去时:
The meal was indigestible. = 那顿饭难以消化。
His theory proved indigestible to his colleagues. = 他的理论被他的同事证明难以理解。


现在完成时:
I have found this chapter indigestible. = 我发现这一章难以理解。
She has always found spicy food indigestible. = 她一直觉得辛辣的食物难以消化。


过去完成时:
The cake had become indigestible after sitting out all day. = 蛋糕放在外面一整天后变得难以消化了。
He realized that his argument had been indigestible to the audience. = 他意识到他的论点让听众难以理解。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "indigestible" comes from the prefix "in-" (not) + "digestible" (able to be digested).  "Digestible" itself derives from the Latin word "digerere," meaning "to digest, distribute, arrange."
