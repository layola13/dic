nickeled-and-dimed:/ˈnɪkəld ænd daɪmd/| adj.|勉强糊口，入不敷出，为小钱操心|The term describes a situation where someone is barely able to make ends meet, constantly struggling with small expenses and facing financial hardship.  It implies a life characterized by financial instability and a constant worry about money. The imagery evokes the small change (nickels and dimes) that barely covers essential needs.|例句：After losing her job, she found herself nickeled and dimed, constantly worrying about how to pay her bills.  =  失业后，她发现自己入不敷出， постоянно беспокоится о том, как оплатить счета。

She's been nickeled and dimed to death by unexpected car repairs. = 她被意料之外的汽车修理费弄得焦头烂额。|近义词：financially strapped, impoverished, hard-up, broke|反义词：wealthy, affluent, well-off, prosperous|形容词
