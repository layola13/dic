gladiators:/ˈɡlædieɪtərz/| n.|角斗士，斗士|A gladiator was a person trained to fight other people to the death in public entertainment in ancient Rome.  They were often slaves or criminals, but sometimes also free men who chose this dangerous profession for fame and fortune.|例句：The gladiators fought fiercely in the arena. 角斗士们在竞技场里激烈地搏斗。

例句：The movie depicted the brutal lives of Roman gladiators.  这部电影描绘了罗马角斗士残酷的生活。


近义词：fighter, combatant, warrior|反义词：spectator, observer


词源：源自拉丁语 *gladiātor,  来自 gladius （短剑）。字面意思是“佩剑者”。
