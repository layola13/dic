generationism:/ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃənɪzəm/| n. |世代论，世代主义|The belief that each generation has its own distinct characteristics and values, influencing its actions and perspectives.  This theory emphasizes the impact of generational differences on society and culture.  It often involves analyzing historical events and societal trends through the lens of generational cohorts. | Example:  The rise of social media is often attributed to generationism, with younger generations shaping its development and usage.  世代论认为每一代人都有其独特的特征和价值观，这些特征和价值观会影响他们的行为和观点。这个理论强调了代际差异对社会和文化的影响。它经常通过代际群体的视角来分析历史事件和社会趋势。 例如：社交媒体的兴起通常归因于世代论，年轻一代塑造了它的发展和使用方式。|近义词：generational difference, cohort analysis |反义词：universalism (the belief in universal values and experiences across generations)
