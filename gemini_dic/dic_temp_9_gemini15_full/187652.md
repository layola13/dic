rontgen:/ˈrɛntɡən/ | n. |伦琴；X射线 | X-rays; a form of electromagnetic radiation.  The term is often used interchangeably with "X-ray."  The word is named after Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen, the German physicist who discovered them. | Example: The doctor ordered a rontgen to examine the broken bone.  医生开了个伦琴射线检查断骨。 | 近义词:X-ray, radiograph | 反义词:None


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  The usage revolves around its function as a noun referring to X-rays.
