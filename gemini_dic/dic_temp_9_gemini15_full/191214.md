scarpered:/ˈskɑːpəd/| v.| 匆忙逃走，溜走 | To run away quickly; to flee.  |例句：The thief scarpered when he saw the police.  小偷看到警察就逃跑了。|例句：When the bell rang, the children scarpered from the classroom.  铃响了，孩子们从教室里逃跑了。|近义词：flee, bolt, dash, run away |反义词：approach, arrive, come, enter


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scarpers every time he sees me. = 他每次看到我就跑。
They always scarper when the boss arrives. = 老板一来他们就总是溜走。

一般过去时:
He scarpered when the lights went out. = 灯灭了，他跑了。
The suspect scarpered down the alley. = 嫌疑犯沿着小巷逃跑了。

现在完成时:
He has scarpered and we don't know where he is. = 他已经逃跑了，我们不知道他在哪里。
They have scarpered off to the beach. = 他们已经偷偷溜到海滩去了。

过去完成时:
He had scarpered before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前他已经逃跑了。
They had scarpered away before we could catch them. = 我们还没来得及抓住他们，他们就已经逃跑了。

将来时:
He will scarper if he sees the police. = 如果他看到警察，他会逃跑的。
I think they will scarper as soon as they can. = 我想他们会尽快逃走的。

虚拟语气:

I wish he hadn't scarpered. = 我真希望他没有逃跑。


被动语态:
(Scarpered is rarely used in the passive voice because it implies active fleeing.)

疑问句:
Did he scarper? = 他逃了吗？
Where did they scarper to? = 他们逃到哪里去了？

否定句:
He didn't scarper; he stayed and faced the music. = 他没有逃跑；他留下来承担后果。
They didn't scarper, they just left quietly. = 他们没有逃跑，只是静静地离开了。
