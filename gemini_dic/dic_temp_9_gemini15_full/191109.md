scapegoater: /ˈskeɪpˌɡoʊtər/ | n. |替罪羊；找替罪羊的人 | A person who is blamed for the wrongdoings, mistakes, or failures of others.  |例句：He became the scapegoater for the company's financial problems. 他成了公司财务问题的替罪羊。|近义词：fall guy, patsy, whipping boy |反义词：innocent party, culprit


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The manager scapegoats his employees for his own mistakes. = 经理为自己的错误而让他的员工当替罪羊。
She often scapegoats her children for her bad mood. = 她经常因为自己的坏情绪而让她的孩子当替罪羊。


一般过去时:
They scapegoated the intern for the data breach. = 他们让实习生为数据泄露事件当替罪羊。
The politician scapegoated the minority group for the economic crisis. = 这位政治家将经济危机归咎于少数民族群体。
