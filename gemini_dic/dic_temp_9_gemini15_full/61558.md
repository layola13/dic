emend:/ɪˈmɛnd/ | vt. | 修订；改正；改进 | To correct or improve (something, especially a text) by making small changes.  | The editor carefully emended the manuscript before publication. 编辑在出版前仔细地修改了手稿。 | improve, correct, revise, rectify | worsen, damage, spoil | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He amends his report every day. = 他每天修改他的报告。
She amends the document to be more concise. = 她修改文件使其更简洁。

一般过去时:
The author amended the errors in his book. = 作者修改了他书中的错误。
They amended the law to address the issue. = 他们修改法律以解决这个问题。

现在完成时:
He has amended his statement several times. = 他已经修改了他的声明几次了。
She has amended the contract to reflect the new agreement. = 她已经修改了合同以反映新的协议。

过去完成时:
The committee had amended the proposal before the meeting. = 委员会在会议前已经修改了提案。
They had amended the rules several times before the game started. = 比赛开始前，他们已经修改了规则几次。

将来时:
He will amend his application before submitting it. = 他会在提交申请前修改它。
She will amend the document to include the new information. = 她将修改文件以包含新的信息。

虚拟语气:
If he were to amend the contract, he would need their approval. = 如果他要修改合同，他需要他们的批准。
I wish he had amended his behavior earlier. = 我希望他早点改正他的行为。

被动语态:
The document was amended by the committee. = 文件由委员会修改。
The law was amended last year. = 这项法律在去年被修改了。

疑问句:
Has the text been amended? = 这篇文章被修改了吗？
Will the proposal be amended? = 提案会被修改吗？

否定句:
He did not amend his report. = 他没有修改他的报告。
They did not amend the contract.  = 他们没有修改合同。
