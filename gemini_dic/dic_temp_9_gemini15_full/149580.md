paid-for:/peɪd fɔːr/| adj.| 付费的，已付费的 | Paid-for content refers to content that has been purchased or for which a fee has been paid.  It contrasts with free or sponsored content. | Example:  The paid-for advertising campaign was very successful.  付费广告活动非常成功。


Example: This is a paid-for app, unlike the free version. 这是一款付费应用，与免费版本不同。|近义词：purchased, bought, commercial |反义词：free, complimentary, sponsored


PHRASE->
paid-for content: 付费内容
paid-for services: 付费服务
paid-for advertising: 付费广告
paid-for subscription: 付费订阅
paid-for access: 付费访问

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The software is paid-for.  这个软件是付费的。
That service is paid-for. 那项服务是付费的。


一般过去时:
We paid for the tickets. 我们买了票。
They paid for the dinner. 他们付了晚餐的钱。


现在完成时:
I have paid for the subscription. 我已经支付了订阅费用。
She has paid for the course. 她已经支付了课程费用。


过去完成时:
He had already paid for the flight before he got the cancellation notice. 他在收到取消通知之前已经支付了航班费用。
They had paid for the hotel room a month in advance. 他们一个月前就预付了酒店房间的费用。


将来时:
We will pay for the repairs tomorrow. 我们明天将支付修理费用。
They will pay for the new car next month. 他们下个月将支付新车的费用。


虚拟语气:
If I had more money, I would pay for the concert tickets. 如果我有更多钱，我会买演唱会门票。
I wish I had paid for the insurance. 我真希望我买了保险。


被动语态:
The software is paid for by the company. 该软件由公司付费。
The service is paid for monthly. 该服务按月付费。


疑问句:
Is this a paid-for service? 这是付费服务吗？
Are the tickets paid for? 门票付了吗？


否定句:
The article is not paid-for. 这篇文章不是付费的。
The event was not paid for by the government. 此活动并非政府付费。
