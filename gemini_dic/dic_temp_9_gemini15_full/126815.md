napalms:/ˈnæpɑːmz/| n. |凝固汽油弹；燃烧剂|A highly flammable substance used in incendiary bombs and weapons.  It consists of a gasoline-based mixture thickened with a gelling agent, often a soap-like material. Napalm adheres to surfaces and burns intensely, making it a devastating weapon. |The use of napalm in the Vietnam War caused widespread controversy. 越南战争中使用凝固汽油弹引发了广泛争议。The devastating effects of napalm are well documented. 凝固汽油弹的破坏性后果已被充分记录。|incendiary bombs, flamethrowers|water, fire suppressants|
