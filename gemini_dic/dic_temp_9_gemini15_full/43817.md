creole:/ˈkriːoʊl/| adj. n.|克里奥尔语的；克里奥尔人；克里奥尔语|Creole refers to a language that develops from a mixture of languages, typically a pidgin that has become the native language of a community.  It can also refer to people of mixed European and African or other non-European ancestry, particularly in the Caribbean and Louisiana.  The term can also describe foods or cultures unique to a specific region. |例句：Louisiana Creole cuisine is known for its rich flavors. 路易斯安那克里奥尔菜以其丰富的风味而闻名。The Creole language of Mauritius is a mix of French and African languages. 毛里塔尼亚的克里奥尔语是法语和非洲语言的混合体。|近义词：pidgin, hybrid, mixed|反义词：pure, unmixed
