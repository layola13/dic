sahib:/ˈsɑːb/| n.|先生，老爷；主人|A respectful term of address used in South Asia, particularly towards men of higher social standing or authority. It is derived from the Hindi and Urdu word साहिब (sāhib), ultimately from the Arabic word صاحب (ṣāḥib), meaning "companion," "owner," or "master."  The term carries connotations of respect and deference. |例句：The sahib addressed the villagers.  老爷训斥了村民们。


The sahib gave orders to his servants.  老爷吩咐他的仆人们。

近义词：sir, master, gentleman |反义词：servant, slave
