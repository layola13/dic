mumble:/ˈmʌmbəl/ | v. |咕哝，含糊地说 |To speak in a low, indistinct tone; to utter words unclearly.  |He mumbled something about being tired. 他含糊地说自己累了。|He mumbled a response, barely audible. 他咕哝着回答，几乎听不见。 |speak indistinctly, mutter |shout, speak clearly |verb

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He mumbles his words. = 他含糊不清地说。
She mumbles when she's nervous. = 她紧张的时候会咕哝。

一般过去时:
He mumbled a reply. = 他咕哝着回答。
She mumbled her thanks. = 她含糊地说谢谢。

现在完成时:
He has mumbled throughout the entire meeting. = 他在整个会议上都在咕哝。
She has mumbled so much I can't understand her. = 她咕哝了这么多，我听不懂她在说什么。

过去完成时:
He had mumbled something about leaving early. = 他曾含糊地说要早点离开。
She had mumbled apologies before hanging up. = 她挂电话前含糊地道歉了。


将来时:
He will mumble his excuses again. = 他会再次含糊地解释。
She will probably mumble something unintelligible. = 她可能会咕哝一些听不懂的东西。

虚拟语气:
If he mumbled less, people would understand him better. = 如果他不那么含糊，人们会更好地理解他。
I wish she wouldn't mumble so much. = 我希望她不要那么含糊。

被动语态:
His words were mumbled so low that I couldn't hear them. = 他的话咕哝得很低，我听不见。
The answer was mumbled inaudibly. = 答案含糊不清，听不见。

疑问句:
Was he mumbling to himself? = 他是在自言自语吗？
Is she always mumbling like that? = 她总是那样含糊不清吗？

否定句:
He didn't mumble; he spoke clearly. = 他没有咕哝；他说话很清楚。
She didn't mumble a word. = 她一句话也没咕哝。


PHRASE->
mumble something to oneself = 自言自语

mumble and grumble = 咕哝抱怨
