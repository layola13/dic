The term also refers to the Southern United States, especially during the pre-Civil War era. |例句：The band played a lively dixieland tune. 乐队演奏了一首欢快的迪克西兰爵士乐曲。

The term "Dixieland" is believed to have originated as a slang term for New Orleans, Louisiana, during the 19th century.  Its exact etymology is uncertain, but it likely derives from a combination of "Dixie," a popular nickname for the Southern states, and "land," indicating a place or region. The specific origin and evolution of the term remain debated among scholars.
