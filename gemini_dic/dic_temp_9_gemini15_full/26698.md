caddish:/ˈkædɪʃ/| adj.|粗鲁的；卑鄙的；无赖的|Unpleasant, ungentlemanly, or dishonorable.  A caddish act is one that shows a lack of consideration for others, often involving selfishness or unfairness. |例句：He showed a caddish disregard for her feelings. 他表现出对她的感情的粗鲁漠视。

例句：It was a caddish thing to do, leaving her stranded like that.  那样把她丢在那儿不管，真是卑鄙的行为。


近义词：ungentlemanly, boorish, loutish,  inconsiderate, selfish, mean,  scoundrelly

反义词：gentlemanly, courteous, considerate, kind, honorable


词源：源自早期现代英语单词“cad”，意为“一个不体面的男人；一个无赖”。  "Cad" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly a shortening of "cadet,"  but its meaning as a term of reproach for a man of low character developed independently.
