representationalist: /ˌreprɛzɛnˈteɪʃənəlɪst/ | adj. n. | 表象主义者；表现主义者 |  A representationalist is a person who believes in or practices representationalism, a philosophical position holding that our mental states are fundamentally representational.  It emphasizes the idea that our minds contain internal representations of the external world. These representations, like images or symbols, mediate our interaction with reality.  Representationalists often contrast their view with eliminative materialism or other non-representational theories of mind.  | The philosopher argued convincingly for a representationalist theory of mind. |近义词: mentalist, cognitivist | 反义词: eliminativist
