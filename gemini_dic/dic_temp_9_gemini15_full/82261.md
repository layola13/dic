grounding:/ˈɡraʊndɪŋ/| n. v.|接地，基础，基本原理；使接地，使着陆，训斥|

英文解析:
As a noun, grounding refers to the connection of something to the earth, providing a path for electrical current to flow. It can also mean the basic principles or fundamental facts of a subject.  As a verb, grounding means to connect something to the earth, to force an aircraft to land, or to reprimand someone firmly.

例句:
The electrician ensured the proper grounding of the electrical system. = 电工确保了电力系统的正确接地。

The grounding of the aircraft was due to bad weather. = 飞机着陆是由于恶劣天气造成的。


近义词:
earthing (n.), foundation (n.), basis (n.), reprimand (v.), rebuke (v.)

反义词:
insulation (n., opposite of grounding in electrical context),  exaltation (n., opposite of reprimand)


词性:
noun, verb
