noncognate:/ˈnɑːnˌkɑːɡneɪt/| adj.|非同源词的；非同源语的 |A noncognate word is a word in one language that does not have a common ancestor with a word in another language, despite possible semantic similarity.  It implies that the words developed independently, rather than through shared linguistic history.|  The English word "dog" and the French word "chien" are noncognates, even though they both refer to the same animal.  Their similarities are coincidental, not inherited from a common ancestor.|近义词： unrelated, dissimilar |反义词：cognate
