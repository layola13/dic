sensitive: /ˈsɛnsɪtɪv/ | adj. | 敏感的；易受影响的；体贴的 | Easily affected by external stimuli; readily responsive to external influences or impressions; showing awareness and understanding of the feelings of others. |

例句：
He is sensitive to criticism.  他很敏感，容易受批评影响。

She is a sensitive person and easily hurt by others' words. 她是一个敏感的人，很容易被别人的话伤害到。


近义词：responsive, susceptible, delicate, perceptive, thoughtful

反义词：insensitive, callous, thick-skinned, indifferent


词源:
源自于拉丁语 *sentire "to feel,"  其含义发展演变至今。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is sensitive to light. 他对光线很敏感。
She is sensitive to the needs of others. 她很能体察别人的需求。

一般过去时:
He was sensitive about his height. 他对自己的身高很敏感。
The equipment was sensitive to changes in temperature.  该设备对温度变化很敏感。

现在完成时:
She has become more sensitive to the plight of the homeless. 她对无家可归者的困境越来越敏感。
They have always been sensitive to the environment.  他们一直都很关注环境。

过去完成时:
He had been sensitive to her feelings throughout their relationship. 在他们恋爱期间，他一直都很体谅她的感受。
The market had been sensitive to global economic conditions. 市场一直对全球经济形势很敏感。


将来时:
The technology will be more sensitive to small changes. 这项技术将对微小的变化更加敏感。
I think she will be sensitive to your concerns. 我认为她会理解你的担忧。

虚拟语气:
If he were more sensitive, he would have understood her feelings. 如果他更敏感一些，他就会理解她的感受了。

被动语态:

The instrument is sensitive to changes in pressure. 仪器对压力的变化很敏感。

疑问句:

否定句:
