scumbling:/ˈskʌmblɪŋ/| n. |粗略涂抹；轻擦；(绘画技法) 擦笔法 |The technique of applying paint thinly and unevenly, creating a textured effect.  It's often used to suggest a hazy or indistinct quality. |例句：The artist used scumbling to create a misty atmosphere in the landscape painting.  这位艺术家用擦笔法在风景画中营造出朦胧的氛围。

The artist achieved a soft, luminous quality through skillful scumbling.  这位艺术家通过熟练的擦笔法获得了柔和、明亮的质感。


近义词：dry brushing, stippling, dabbing |反义词：smooth blending, glazing |词性：名词

词源:  The word "scumbling" likely originates from the Old English word "scumlian," meaning "to skim" or "to rub lightly." This reflects the technique's process of lightly applying paint, akin to skimming or rubbing.  Over time, the meaning evolved to specifically describe the artistic technique.
