debilitated:/dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪd/ | adj. |虚弱的；衰弱的；无力的 |Weakened, especially in terms of strength or energy.  The illness left him debilitated and unable to work. |The long illness left her debilitated.|weakened, enfeebled, incapacitated, prostrated |strong, robust, healthy, vigorous |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is debilitated by the illness. = 他因病而虚弱。
The heat debilitates many people. = 炎热使许多人感到虚弱。

一般过去时:
The surgery debilitated him for several weeks. = 手术使他虚弱了好几周。
The marathon race debilitated many runners. = 马拉松比赛使许多跑步者感到虚弱。

现在完成时:
The illness has debilitated him considerably. = 这场病使他相当虚弱。
Years of hard work have debilitated her. = 多年的辛勤工作使她变得虚弱。

过去完成时:
He had been debilitated by the illness for months before he sought medical help. = 他生病虚弱了好几个月才去看医生。
She had been debilitated by grief following her husband's death. = 在丈夫去世后，悲伤使她虚弱不堪。

将来时:
The treatment will likely debilitate him for a short time. = 治疗可能会使他暂时虚弱。
Prolonged exposure to the sun will debilitate you. = 长时间暴露在阳光下会使你虚弱。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't been debilitated, he would have won the race. = 如果他不虚弱，他就能赢得比赛。
I wish I weren't so debilitated by this cold. = 我真希望我不那么虚弱。

被动语态:
He was debilitated by the accident. = 他在事故中受了重伤变得虚弱。
Many people are debilitated by chronic illnesses. = 许多人因慢性病而虚弱。

疑问句:
Is he debilitated by the disease? = 他因病而虚弱吗？
Are you feeling debilitated after the long journey? = 长途旅行后你感到虚弱吗？

否定句:
He is not debilitated by the medication. = 他服用药物后并没有虚弱。
She was not debilitated by the physical challenges. = 她并没有因体力挑战而虚弱。


PHRASE->
be debilitated by = 因…而虚弱
severely debilitated = 严重虚弱
become debilitated = 变得虚弱
feel debilitated = 感到虚弱
mentally debilitated = 精神虚弱


词源:
From Late Latin *debilitatus, past participle of debilitare "to weaken," from Latin de- "down, away" + *habilis "able, skillful."  The sense shifted from "to deprive of ability" to "to weaken."
