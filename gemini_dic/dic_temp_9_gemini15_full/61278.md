embrace:/ɪmˈbreɪs/ | vt. n. |拥抱；欣然接受；包含；围绕 |To hold (someone) closely in one's arms, especially as a sign of affection.  To accept (something readily or willingly).|例句：She embraced her daughter warmly. 她热情地拥抱了她的女儿。

The company embraced new technologies. 这家公司欣然接受了新技术。|近义词：hug, clasp, accept, welcome |反义词：reject, shun, spurn, avoid

词源:
The word "embrace" comes from the Old French "embracer," meaning "to clasp in the arms," which ultimately derives from the Latin "bracchium," meaning "arm."  The prefix "em-" is from Old French, reinforcing the idea of encompassing or surrounding.
