margrave:/ˈmɑːɡreɪv/| n. |侯爵；边疆伯爵|A margrave is a nobleman in the Holy Roman Empire and other parts of Europe, historically ruling over a march or border region.  The title is equivalent to a marquess.  The term derives from the Old High German words *mark* (border region) and *graf* (count).|The margrave defended the kingdom's eastern border against invaders.|近义词：marquess, landgrave|反义词：vassal, subject
