self-interpreting: /ˌsɛlfɪnˈtɜːrpətiŋ/ | adj. | 自解释的；自我诠释的 |  Able to explain or understand itself; containing within itself the means of its own interpretation or explanation.  This term is often used in the context of computer programs or codes that contain within themselves the information needed to understand and execute their own instructions.  | 例句：The program was self-interpreting, meaning it didn't require a separate interpreter to run.  这个程序是自解释的，这意味着它不需要单独的解释器来运行。

The self-interpreting code was able to dynamically analyze and execute its own instructions.  这个自解释代码能够动态分析并执行其自身的指令。


近义词：self-explanatory, self-contained, autonomous  | 反义词：opaque, ambiguous, requiring external interpretation | 词性: adjective


USAGE->

This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms in standard usage.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  The following examples illustrate its usage in different sentence structures:

词源:

The word is a compound word formed from "self-" (meaning "of, by, or for oneself") and "interpreting" (the present participle of the verb "to interpret," meaning "to explain the meaning of").  Therefore, its meaning is literally "interpreting itself."
