cowering: /ˈkaʊərɪŋ/ | adj. | 畏缩的；胆怯的 |  Showing fear and trying to make oneself smaller.  It describes someone who is shrinking back in fear, often physically curling up or hiding. |例句：The small dog cowered behind its owner, afraid of the thunderstorm. 小狗害怕雷雨，畏缩在主人身后。

The children cowered in fear as the bully approached. 孩子们害怕地畏缩起来，因为恶霸走近了。


近义词：cringing, shrinking, trembling, quaking | 反义词：brave, bold, defiant, courageous | 词性:形容词

词源:
The word "cower" comes from the Old English word *cūgan, meaning "to crouch" or "to shrink."  This reflects the physical posture associated with fear and submission.
