rubber-stamp:/ˈrʌbərˌstæmp/| n. vt. |橡皮图章；例行公事；批准|A rubber stamp is a stamp made of rubber used to make impressions, often of signatures or official seals.  As a verb, it means to approve something routinely without proper consideration. |例句：The proposal was rubber-stamped by the committee without discussion.  这个提案在委员会未经讨论就被批准了。


The manager rubber-stamped all the applications. 经理例行公事地批准了所有申请。|近义词：approve, ratify, endorse, authorize|反义词：reject, deny, veto, oppose|
