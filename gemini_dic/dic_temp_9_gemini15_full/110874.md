lionization: /ˌlaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 尊崇；奉为偶像；赞扬；推崇 | The act of treating someone as a celebrity or important figure.  It often involves public admiration, praise, and attention. |The lionization of the author after the release of his best-selling novel was overwhelming. 作者的畅销小说出版后，他受到了极大的推崇。|  glorification, idolization, elevation, aggrandizement | denigration, vilification, condemnation, criticism | 名词

被动语态:

She was lionized for her humanitarian work. = 她因其人道主义工作而受到赞扬。


疑问句:

否定句:
His work was not lionized during his lifetime. = 他生前并没有受到推崇。
They did not lionize him for his controversial actions. = 他们并没有因为他的争议行为而赞扬他。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "lionization".  The word itself is relatively formal and less frequently used in phrasal constructions.  Instead, related phrases are used:
to be lionized by the press = 受媒体推崇
to receive lionization = 获得推崇
to be the subject of lionization = 成为推崇的对象
to undergo lionization = 经历推崇
to deserve lionization = 值得推崇


词源:
From the verb "lionize," meaning "to treat as a lion," implying great respect and admiration.  This ultimately derives from the metaphorical association of lions with strength, power, and majesty.
