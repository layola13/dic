nidus:/ˈnaɪdəs/| n. | 病灶；中心；起源 | A nidus is a place where something, especially a disease or something unpleasant, begins to develop or is concentrated.  It often refers to a focus of infection or a site of origin for something harmful. | Example: The surgeon carefully removed the nidus of the infection. 外科医生仔细地清除了感染的病灶。The small town served as a nidus for the revolutionary movement.  这个小镇成了革命运动的中心。 | 近义词：focus, center, source, origin | 反义词：periphery, edge, end
