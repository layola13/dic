enthusiasm:/ɪnˈθuːziæzəm/ | n. | 热情；热心 | Enthusiasm is a feeling of excitement and strong interest in something.  It is characterized by a strong positive feeling and a desire to be involved. |  Her enthusiasm for the project was infectious. 她对这个项目的热情极具感染力。  The team's enthusiasm was evident in their hard work.  团队的热情在他们的辛勤工作中显而易见。 | zeal, passion, fervor | apathy, indifference, boredom | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
His enthusiasm inspires others. = 他的热情鼓舞着他人。
She shows great enthusiasm for her work. = 她对工作表现出极大的热情。


一般过去时:
Their enthusiasm waned after the initial success. = 他们最初的成功之后，热情减退了。
He recalled his enthusiasm for learning as a child. = 他回忆起孩童时代对学习的热情。


现在完成时:
Her enthusiasm has never flagged. = 她的热情从未减退。
We have witnessed their growing enthusiasm for the cause. = 我们见证了他们对这项事业日益增长的热情。


过去完成时:
His enthusiasm had already diminished by the time the project ended. = 到项目结束时，他的热情已经减退了。
They realized their enthusiasm had been misplaced. = 他们意识到他们的热情用错了地方。


将来时:
His enthusiasm will surely carry him through. = 他的热情一定会让他坚持下去。
We anticipate great enthusiasm for the upcoming event. = 我们预计即将到来的活动会引起极大的热情。


虚拟语气:
If only he had more enthusiasm for his studies! = 但愿他对学习更有热情！
I wish she showed more enthusiasm for her job. = 我希望她对工作表现出更多热情。


被动语态:
His enthusiasm was rewarded with success. = 他的热情获得了成功。
The project benefited from the enthusiasm shown by the team. = 该项目受益于团队所展现的热情。


疑问句:

否定句:

The lack of enthusiasm was disheartening. = 热情的缺乏令人沮丧。
