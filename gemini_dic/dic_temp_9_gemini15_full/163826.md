/ˌpriːdɪˈpɒzɪts/ | n. | 预先存款；预付定金 | A predeposit is a payment made in advance, typically as a security deposit or partial payment for goods or services.  It reduces the amount owed later. | Example: The tenant made a predeposit of one month's rent.  租客预付了一个月的房租。 |近义词：advance payment, down payment, deposit |反义词：postpayment, arrears


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company requires a predeposit for all custom orders. = 公司要求所有定制订单预付定金。
He always makes a predeposit on his annual vacation package. = 他总是预付年度度假套餐的费用。


一般过去时:
She predeposited $1000 for the new car. = 她为新车预付了1000美元。
They predeposited funds into their children's education accounts. = 他们向子女的教育账户预存了资金。


现在完成时:
We have predeposited enough money for the concert tickets. = 我们已经为演唱会门票预存了足够的钱。
She has predeposited the down payment on the house. = 她已经预付了房子的首付。


过去完成时:
They had predeposited the money before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期之前已经预存了这笔钱。
He had predeposited a significant sum for his retirement. = 他为退休预存了一大笔钱。


将来时:
I will predeposit the full amount next week. = 我下周将预付全部款项。
They will predeposit the funds into the escrow account. = 他们将资金预存到托管账户。


虚拟语气:
If I had predeposited the money, I wouldn't be in this situation now. = 如果我预存了这笔钱，我现在就不会处于这种境地了。
I wish I had predeposited more for the trip. = 我真希望我为这次旅行预存更多钱。


被动语态:

否定句:
He did not predeposit the funds. = 他没有预付资金。
