expurgated: /ˈɛkspɜːrɡeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 删去不当部分的；净化了的 | purged of objectionable material; cleansed.  The expurgated edition removed all offensive language.  |近义词：redacted, sanitized, bowdlerized | 反义词：unabridged, complete


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The publisher expurgates offensive content from the book. = 出版商从书中删除了冒犯性的内容。
This version is expurgated to be suitable for young readers. = 为了适合年轻读者，这个版本经过了删改。


一般过去时:
They expurgated the manuscript before publication. = 他们在出版前删改了手稿。
The censors expurgated the play to remove objectionable scenes. = 检查员删改了戏剧以去除令人反感的场景。


现在完成时:
The government has expurgated many historical records. = 政府已经删改了许多历史记录。
The editor has expurgated the text to meet the requirements. = 编辑已经删改了文本以满足要求。


过去完成时:
They had expurgated the letter before sending it. = 他们在寄出信之前已经删改了它。
The author had expurgated the novel several times before its final publication. = 作者在小说最终出版之前已经删改过几次了。


将来时:

The committee will expurgate all inappropriate content from the website. = 委员会将从网站上删除所有不当内容。


虚拟语气:
If they expurgated the book, it would be more acceptable to a wider audience. = 如果他们删改这本书，它将会被更广泛的读者接受。
I wish they had expurgated the offensive parts of the speech. = 我希望他们已经删掉了演讲中冒犯的部分。


被动语态:

疑问句:

Has the original version been expurgated? = 原版是否经过删改？


否定句:
The original manuscript was not expurgated. = 原稿没有经过删改。
They did not expurgate the controversial passages. = 他们没有删改有争议的段落。

词源:
From Latin *expurgāre "to cleanse, purify," from ex- "out, from" + purgāre "to cleanse, purify."
