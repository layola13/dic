reluctant:/rɪˈlʌktənt/| adj. | 不情愿的，勉强的 | unwilling and hesitant; disinclined.  The adjective "reluctant" describes someone who is unwilling or hesitant to do something. It implies a feeling of resistance or reservation. |例句：He was reluctant to admit his mistake. 他不情愿承认自己的错误。

He was reluctant to leave the party. 他不情愿离开聚会。


近义词：hesitant, unwilling, resistant, loath, averse | 反义词：eager, willing, enthusiastic, keen


词源：From the Latin word "reluctari," meaning "to struggle against," "to resist."  The word's history reflects its core meaning of resisting or being unwilling.
