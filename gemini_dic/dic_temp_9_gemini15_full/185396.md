retroreflection:/ˌrɛtroʊrɪˈflɛkʃən/| n. |逆反射；反光；回射|The reflection of light or other radiation back to its source.  Retroreflection occurs when light strikes a surface that is designed to reflect the light back in the direction from which it came. This is in contrast to specular reflection, where light is reflected at an angle.  Many road signs use retroreflective materials to make them visible at night. |The cat's eyes reflected the car's headlights with remarkable retroreflection.|近义词：backscattering, reflection|反义词：transmission
