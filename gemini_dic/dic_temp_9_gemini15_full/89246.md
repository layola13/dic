hominem:/ˈhɒmɪnɛm/ | adj. |针对个人的；人身攻击的 |  Referring to an argument that attacks the person making the argument rather than addressing the argument itself; a personal attack.  | Example:  He resorted to *ad hominem* attacks, insulting his opponent's character instead of debating the policy. 他诉诸人身攻击，侮辱对手的品格而不是辩论政策。| 近义词：personal, abusive, insulting | 反义词：objective, rational, fair


USAGE->

This word is an adjective, and thus doesn't have verb conjugations like "what" does.  The usage focuses on its application in describing arguments or attacks.

Example 1 (in a sentence):  The debate became unproductive when it devolved into *ad hominem* attacks.
Example 1 (Translation):  当辩论演变成人身攻击时，它变得没有成效。

Example 2 (in a sentence): His criticism was purely *ad hominem*; he offered no substantive arguments against the proposal.
Example 2 (Translation): 他的批评纯粹是人身攻击；他没有提出反对该提案的任何实质性论据。


PHRASE->

There are no common phrases specifically using "hominem" besides the Latin phrase "ad hominem."  The phrase itself is the key usage.
