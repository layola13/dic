post-Victorian: /ˌpoʊst vɪkˈtɔːriən/ | adj. | 维多利亚时代以后的；后维多利亚时代的 | Relating to the period of time after the Victorian era (1837-1901), characterized by changes in social, political, and cultural norms.  It often implies a reaction against or a departure from Victorian values and aesthetics. |

例句：

The post-Victorian era saw a rise in feminist movements challenging traditional gender roles.  维多利亚时代以后，女权运动兴起，挑战传统的性别角色。

Post-Victorian literature often explored themes of disillusionment and social change. 后维多利亚时期的文学作品常常探讨幻灭和社会变革的主题。


近义词：Edwardian (if referring specifically to the Edwardian period), late 19th-century, early 20th-century


反义词：Victorian


词源:

The term is a compound word.  "Post" means "after" or "following," and "Victorian" refers to the reign of Queen Victoria of the United Kingdom (1837-1901).  The term thus literally means "after the Victorian era."
