depulp:/dɪˈpʌlp/| vt.| 去除果肉；除去髓；使失去活力 | To remove the pulp from something, especially fruit.  The term can also refer to removing the soft central part of something or to the process of making something less energetic or vital. |例句：The machine depulps the oranges before juicing them. 这台机器在榨汁前去除橘子的果肉。

The factory depulped the logs to prepare them for processing. 工厂将原木的髓部去除，以便进行加工。


近义词：pulp,remove,extract|反义词：None specifically, but the opposite action would be adding pulp or restoring vitality. |动词

词源:
The word "depulp" is a combination of the prefix "de-" (meaning "to remove" or "to deprive of") and "pulp," referring to the soft, fleshy part of a fruit or vegetable.  Therefore, it literally means "to remove the pulp."
