fossil fuel:/ˈfɑːsɪl fjuːəl/| n. |化石燃料|Fossil fuels are fuels formed by natural processes such as anaerobic decomposition of buried dead organisms.  They contain energy originating in ancient photosynthesis.  The age of the organisms and their resulting fossil fuels is typically millions of years.  Examples include coal, petroleum, and natural gas.|The power plant relies heavily on fossil fuels for energy generation. 发电厂严重依赖化石燃料发电。|Renewable energy sources are increasingly replacing fossil fuels. 可再生能源日益取代化石燃料。|近义词：non-renewable energy, hydrocarbon fuels|反义词：renewable energy, sustainable energy
