doctorates:/ˈdɒktəreɪts/| n. | 博士学位（复数）|The plural form of "doctorate," referring to multiple doctoral degrees.  Doctorates are advanced academic degrees conferred after significant research and the completion of a dissertation.|例句：Many universities offer a wide range of doctorates in various fields. 许多大学提供各种领域的博士学位。|例句：She holds two doctorates, one in physics and one in mathematics. 她拥有两个博士学位，一个是物理学，一个是数学。|近义词：doctoral degrees, PhDs (in specific contexts)|反义词：bachelor's degrees, master's degrees|名词

WORD ORIGIN->
The word "doctorates" is derived from the Latin word "doctor," meaning "teacher."  The suffix "-ate" indicates a noun of status or rank.  Therefore, "doctorate" signifies the status of being a doctor (in the academic sense) and "doctorates" is its plural.
