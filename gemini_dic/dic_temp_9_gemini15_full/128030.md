nerd:/nɜːrd/| n. |书呆子，电脑迷|A person who is very interested in technical or academic subjects, especially computers, and who is often considered to be socially awkward.  Nerds are often seen as intelligent and knowledgeable but lacking in social skills. |He's a computer nerd who spends hours coding. 他是个电脑迷，花好几个小时写代码。|geek, dweeb, wonk|social butterfly, popular person|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a nerd. = 他是个书呆子。
She nerds out over video games. = 她沉迷于电子游戏。


一般过去时:
He was a nerd in high school. = 他高中时是个书呆子。
They nerded out over the new Star Wars movie. = 他们对新的星球大战电影非常兴奋。


现在完成时:
He has always been a nerd. = 他一直是个书呆子。
She has nerded out over that band for years. = 她迷恋那个乐队很多年了。


过去完成时:
He had been a nerd since childhood. = 他从小就是个书呆子。
They had nerded out about the concert before it even started. = 演唱会还没开始，他们就已经兴奋不已了。


将来时:
He will always be a nerd. = 他将永远是个书呆子。
They will nerd out when they see the new technology. = 当他们看到新技术时，他们会非常兴奋。


虚拟语气:
If he were a nerd, he would understand this. = 如果他是个书呆子，他会明白这个。
I wish I were a nerd so I could fix this computer. = 我希望我是一个书呆子，这样我就能修好这台电脑了。


被动语态:
He is often called a nerd. = 他经常被称为书呆子。
The new technology was nerded out over by everyone. = 新技术让每个人都兴奋不已。


疑问句:
Is he a nerd? = 他是书呆子吗？
Are they nerding out over the new game? = 他们对这款新游戏很兴奋吗？


否定句:
He is not a nerd. = 他不是书呆子。
They didn't nerd out over the presentation. = 他们对演示文稿并不兴奋。


PHRASE->
nerd out:  变得非常兴奋或痴迷于某事
total nerd:  十足的书呆子
computer nerd: 电脑迷
science nerd: 科学迷
social nerd:  (讽刺)在社交方面很笨拙的人
