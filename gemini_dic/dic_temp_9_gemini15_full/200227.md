skippership:/ˈskɪpʃɪp/| n.|跳船；逃船；弃船|A skippership is the act of abandoning a ship, often illegally or without permission.  It can also refer to a person who abandons a ship. This word is quite uncommon and rarely used in modern English.  |例句：The crew were accused of skippership after the vessel ran aground. 船只搁浅后，船员被指控弃船。 The captain faced charges related to skippership and desertion. 船长面临与弃船和逃亡相关的指控。|近义词：desertion, abandonment, absconding|反义词：remaining onboard, staying with the ship|名词
