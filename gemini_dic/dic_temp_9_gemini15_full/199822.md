sit:/sɪt/ | vi. | 坐，栖息 | To rest one's weight on one's buttocks; be seated.  |  例句：Please sit down. 请坐下。 | 例句：The bird sat on the branch. 鸟栖息在树枝上。 | 近义词：perch, settle, squat | 反义词：stand, rise


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I sit here every day. = 我每天都坐在这里。
They sit at the back of the classroom. = 他们坐在教室的后面。

一般过去时:
He sat on the park bench. = 他坐在公园的长凳上。
She sat quietly and listened. = 她安静地坐着听着。

现在完成时:
I have sat here for hours. = 我已经坐在这里几个小时了。
We have sat through three long meetings. = 我们已经参加了三个冗长的会议。

过去完成时:
He had sat there for a while before she arrived. = 她到达之前他已经在那里坐了一会儿了。
They had sat down to dinner when the phone rang. = 他们刚坐下吃饭，电话就响了。

将来时:
I will sit by the window. = 我将坐在窗边。
She will sit and watch the sunset. = 她将坐着观看日落。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would sit down and relax. = 如果我是你，我会坐下放松一下。
I wish I could sit still for five minutes. = 我真希望我能安静地坐五分钟。

被动语态:
The chair was sat on by several people. = 那把椅子被几个人坐过。
The seats are usually sat in quickly. = 这些座位通常很快就被坐满了。

疑问句:
Where do you usually sit? = 你通常坐在哪里？
May I sit here? = 我可以坐在这里吗？

否定句:
He doesn't sit still for long. = 他坐不住多久。
She didn't sit down all day. = 她一整天都没坐下。


PHRASE->
sit down = 坐下
sit up = 坐直;熬夜
sit back = 放松
sit tight = 保持不动；耐心等待
sit around = 闲坐着


词源:
The word "sit" originates from the Old English word "sittan," which has cognates in other Germanic languages.  Its ultimate origin is Proto-Germanic *sittan, and further back to Proto-Indo-European *sed- ("to sit").
