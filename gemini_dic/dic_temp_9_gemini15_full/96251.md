indicator: /ˈɪndɪkeɪtər/ | n. | 指标；指示器；标志 |

英文解析:Something that indicates or points out something else; a sign or symptom.  It can be a device that shows a measurement or a fact that suggests something else.

例句：

The rising unemployment rate is a key indicator of economic trouble.  不断上升的失业率是经济陷入困境的一个关键指标。

The needle on the gauge serves as an indicator of fuel level.  仪表盘上的指针是油量指示器。


近义词：sign, signal, marker, index, gauge, measure, symptom

反义词：N/A (No direct opposite, but antonyms might be related to the opposite of what the indicator shows)


词性：名词


词源:  From Late Middle English *indicatour*, from Old French *indicateur*, from Latin *indicatōr*, agent noun from *indicāre* "to point out, show".
