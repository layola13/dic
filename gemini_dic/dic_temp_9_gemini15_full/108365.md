lavishness:/ˈlævɪʃnəs/| n. |挥霍；奢华；浪费 |The lavishness of the banquet was breathtaking.  宴会之奢华令人叹为观止。|近义词：extravagance, profusion, opulence |反义词：frugalit, sparseness, economy |


英文解析：Lavishness refers to the quality of being extremely generous or extravagant in giving or using something.  It often implies an excessive or wasteful use of resources, especially money or time.  It can describe a lavish lifestyle, a lavish party, or a lavish gift.


例句：

The lavishness of their wedding was evident in the expensive decorations and the gourmet food.  他们婚礼的奢华从昂贵的装饰和美味佳肴中可见一斑。


Her lavishness with her money led to her financial ruin. 她挥霍无度最终导致了经济破产。


词源:

The word "lavishness" is derived from the Old French word "laviss," meaning "to wash, to bathe,"  which ultimately comes from the Latin word "lavare," meaning "to wash."  The connection lies in the idea of abundance and profusion, as if something is so plentiful it's like being washed over by it.  The sense evolved to imply abundance and extravagance in spending or giving.
