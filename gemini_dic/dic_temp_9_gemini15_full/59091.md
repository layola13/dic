dyed:/daɪd/| v.|染色；染成…颜色|To color (a fiber, yarn, fabric, etc.) by immersion in or application of a dye.  This process changes the color of the material permanently. |例句：She dyed her hair red. 她把头发染成了红色。

例句：He dyed the fabric a deep blue. 他把布料染成了深蓝色。


近义词：colored, stained, tinted|反义词：bleached, uncolored


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I dye my hair every month. = 我每个月都染头发。
She dyes fabrics for a living. = 她靠染色织物为生。


一般过去时:
I dyed my shirt blue last week. = 我上周把衬衫染成了蓝色。
They dyed the wool before spinning it. = 他们在纺纱前对羊毛进行了染色。


现在完成时:
I have dyed my hair many times. = 我染过很多次头发了。
She has dyed several scarves this week. = 她这周已经染了几条围巾了。


过去完成时:
I had dyed my hair before the party. = 我在聚会前已经染了头发。
They had dyed the fabric before cutting it. = 他们在剪裁之前已经染了布料。


将来时:
I will dye my hair blonde next week. = 我下周要将头发染成金色。
She will dye the silk a vibrant green. = 她将会把丝绸染成鲜艳的绿色。


虚拟语气:
If I dyed my hair green, everyone would laugh. = 如果我把头发染成绿色，大家都会笑。
I wish I had dyed my hair a different color. = 我真希望我把头发染成不同的颜色。


被动语态:
The fabric was dyed a beautiful shade of purple. = 布料被染成了美丽的紫色。
The wool is being dyed in the factory. = 羊毛正在工厂里染色。


疑问句:
Did you dye your own hair? = 你自己染的头发吗？

否定句:

They haven't dyed the curtains yet. = 他们还没染窗帘。

词源:
The word "dyed" comes from the Old English word "dygian," meaning "to dye."  This in turn has roots in Proto-Germanic and ultimately Proto-Indo-European, connecting it to words with similar meanings in other languages.
