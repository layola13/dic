daredevil:/ˈdɛərˌdɛvəl/ | n. adj. | 胆大妄为的人；鲁莽的；大胆的 | A daredevil is someone who takes unnecessary risks, often for the thrill of it.  A daredevil act is one that is dangerous and reckless. |例句：He's a daredevil who enjoys skydiving. 他是一个喜欢跳伞的胆大妄为的人。

例句：That was a daredevil stunt, driving that fast around the corner. 那是一个鲁莽的举动，以那么快的速度转弯。


近义词：adventurer, risk-taker, dare, reckless, audacious | 反义词：cautious, timid, careful, prudent | 词性：名词，形容词


词源:
The word "daredevil" is a compound word.  It combines "dare," meaning to challenge or provoke, and "devil," referring to the spirit of evil or mischief. This suggests someone who boldly confronts danger, acting as if they were possessed by a devilish courage. The combination emphasizes the reckless and audacious nature of their actions.
