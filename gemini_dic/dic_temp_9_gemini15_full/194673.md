self-valuing: /ˈsɛlfˈvæljuɪŋ/ | adj. | 自我价值的；重视自我的 |  Having or showing a high regard for oneself and one's abilities.  This adjective describes someone who has a strong sense of self-worth and believes in their own capabilities.  It can also describe actions or behaviors that reflect this self-regard. |

例句：

Her self-valuing attitude helped her overcome many obstacles.  她的重视自我的态度帮助她克服了许多障碍。

He demonstrated a self-valuing approach by setting firm boundaries. 他通过设定明确的界限展现了一种重视自我的态度。


近义词：self-respecting, confident, self-assured, assertive

反义词：self-deprecating, insecure, self-doubting, submissive


词性：形容词

USAGE->  (This word is an adjective, so it doesn't have verb conjugations like "what".  The following shows how it's used in different sentence structures.)


肯定句:

He is a self-valuing individual. 他是一个重视自我的人。

She showed a self-valuing demeanor throughout the negotiation.  她在整个谈判过程中都表现出重视自我的风度。


否定句:

He is not a self-valuing person; he constantly puts himself down. 他不是一个重视自我的人；他总是贬低自己。

Their self-valuing approach wasn't appreciated by the team. 他们的重视自我态度并没有得到团队的赏识。


疑问句:

Is she a self-valuing person? 她是一个重视自我的人吗？

Are these self-valuing behaviors sustainable in the long run?  这些重视自我的行为从长远来看是否可持续？


比较级和最高级: (Self-valuing doesn't have inherent comparative or superlative forms.  You would use adverbs to modify the degree of self-valuing.)

She is the most self-valuing person I know. 她是我认识的最重视自己的人。(Using "most" to modify the adjective)
