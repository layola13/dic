graphophone:/ˈɡræfəfoʊn/| n. |留声机|A graphophone is an early type of phonograph that used a wax cylinder to record and reproduce sound.  It was developed by Alexander Graham Bell and Chichester Bell.  The graphophone improved upon the phonograph's design, notably its use of a wax cylinder instead of tinfoil.| The graphophone was a significant step in the development of sound recording technology.  Many early recordings were made using a graphophone.|近义词：phonograph|反义词：None.
