cuts:/kʌts/| n. v.|切割；削减；伤口；减少|to separate or divide something, especially with a sharp instrument; a wound caused by a sharp object; a reduction in something.|例句：He cuts the bread with a knife. 他用刀切面包。

The deep cuts on his arm needed stitches. 他胳膊上的深伤口需要缝针。


近义词：slices, trims, reduces, decreases|反义词：joins, unites, increases


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cuts the grass every week. = 他每周都割草。
She cuts the vegetables for the soup. = 她切菜做汤。

一般过去时:
I cut my finger yesterday. = 我昨天割破了手指。
They cut the cake into eight pieces. = 他们把蛋糕切成八块。

现在完成时:
He has cut his hair short. = 他把头发剪短了。
She has cut down on her spending. = 她减少了开支。

过去完成时:
The chef had already cut the meat before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前厨师已经切好了肉。
She had cut her hair before the wedding. = 婚礼前她剪了头发。

将来时:
I will cut the flowers tomorrow. = 我明天会剪花。
They will cut the ribbon at the opening ceremony. = 他们将在开幕式上剪彩。

虚拟语气:
If I had a sharper knife, I would cut the vegetables more easily. = 如果我有一把更锋利的刀，我会更容易切菜。
I wish I hadn't cut myself. = 我真希望我没有割伤自己。

被动语态:
The cake was cut into slices. = 蛋糕被切成片。
The budget has been cut significantly. = 预算被大幅削减。

疑问句:
Did you cut the pie? = 你切派了吗？
Where did you cut yourself? = 你在哪里割伤自己的？

否定句:
I didn't cut the cake. = 我没有切蛋糕。
He doesn't cut his own hair. = 他不自己剪头发。
