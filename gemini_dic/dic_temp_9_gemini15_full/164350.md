preembarrass: /ˌpriːɪmˈbærəs/ | adj. | 预先感到尴尬的；事先感到难堪的 |  Describes a feeling of embarrassment experienced *before* an embarrassing situation actually occurs. It anticipates the potential for embarrassment.  |  The thought of giving the speech preembarrassed him.  他一想到要发表演讲就感到事先尴尬。 He felt preembarrassed about the upcoming family reunion. 他对即将到来的家庭聚会感到事先难堪。|  None (it's a newly coined word, lacking direct synonyms)|  None (antonyms would describe the opposite of feeling embarrassment)| Adjective
