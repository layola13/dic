pre-chilling:/ˌpriːˈtʃɪlɪŋ/| v. |预冷；预先冷却 |To pre-chill something means to cool it down beforehand, typically to a specific temperature, before a further process or use.  This is often done to improve the quality, efficiency, or outcome of a subsequent step. For example, pre-chilling ingredients before cooking can help maintain texture and prevent overcooking.  |例句：We pre-chilled the champagne before serving it. 我们在开香槟之前先把它预冷了。
The chef pre-chilled the plates to keep the dessert cold longer.  厨师预先冷却了盘子以保持甜点更长时间的冰冷。|近义词：refrigerate beforehand, cool down in advance |反义词：pre-heating
