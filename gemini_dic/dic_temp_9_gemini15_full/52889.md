diminutivenesses:/ˌdɪmɪˈnuːtɪvnəsɪz/ | n. | 微小性；细小之处（复数） | The multiple diminutivenesses of the miniature painting were astonishing.  |  近义词：smallnesses, littlenesses,  minutness | 反义词：largenesses, immensities


英文解析：The plural form of "diminutiveness," referring to the state or quality of being small or insignificant; instances or examples of smallness.  It emphasizes the multiplicity of small aspects or features.


例句：

The numerous diminutivenesses of the model car made it incredibly realistic.
= 模型车的诸多微小之处使其显得非常逼真。


The artist meticulously captured the numerous diminutivenesses of the landscape.
= 艺术家一丝不苟地捕捉了风景画的诸多细微之处。


词源：

* **diminutiveness:**  Derived from the adjective "diminutive," which ultimately comes from the Latin verb *minuere* ("to lessen, diminish"). The suffix "-ness" is added to form a noun indicating a state or quality.  The plural "diminutivenesses" simply adds the plural "-es" to the noun.
