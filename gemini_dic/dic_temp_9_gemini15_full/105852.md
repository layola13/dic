kite:/kaɪt/| n.|风筝|A toy consisting of a light frame with thin material stretched over it, flown in the wind at the end of a long string.  |例句：The children were flying kites in the park.孩子们在公园里放风筝。|例句：My kite is shaped like a bird.我的风筝形状像一只鸟。|近义词：flyer|反义词:无


USAGE->
This word is a noun, so it doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  The usage changes based on the sentence structure.

Example Sentences (showing different grammatical roles):

Subject:  The kite soared high above the trees.  风筝飞上了树梢之上。
Object:  He launched the kite into the air. 他把风筝放飞到空中。
Possessive: That's my kite.那是我的风筝。
Appositive:  A colorful kite, it danced in the breeze.  一个色彩鲜艳的风筝，它在微风中飞舞。
Part of a prepositional phrase:  The string of the kite snapped. 风筝的线断了。


PHRASE->
box kite:  盒式风筝
delta kite: 三角翼风筝
kite surfing: 风筝冲浪
flying a kite: 放风筝
make a kite: 制作风筝


词源:
The word "kite" comes from the Old English word "cyta,"  meaning "bird of prey."  The resemblance of the flying toy to a bird in flight likely led to the adoption of this word.  The connection to birds of prey also suggests a sense of soaring and speed.
