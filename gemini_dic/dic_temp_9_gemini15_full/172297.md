put-out-to-sea:/pʊt aʊt tə siː/| phrasal verb| 送出海；抛弃；|This phrase describes the act of sending something (usually a ship or person) out to sea, often implying a sense of abandonment or irreversible departure.  It can also suggest launching something new and uncertain into the world.|例句：The old fishing boat was put out to sea, never to return.  这艘旧渔船被送出海，再也没有回来。
The young man, full of dreams, put himself out to sea, leaving his family behind.  怀揣梦想的年轻人独自远航，离开了家人。|近义词：send out to sea, abandon, cast adrift|反义词：bring back, rescue, reclaim


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They put their boat out to sea every morning. = 他们每天早上把船送出海。
The company puts new products out to sea every year. = 这家公司每年都推出新产品。

一般过去时:
The sailors put their ship out to sea before the storm hit. = 水手们在暴风雨来临之前把船送出海。
He put his hopes and dreams out to sea, trusting in fate. = 他把希望和梦想托付给命运，送向大海。


现在完成时:
They have put many ships out to sea over the years. = 多年来，他们已经送出许多船只。
She has put her past out to sea and started anew. = 她已经放下过去，重新开始。


过去完成时:
They had put the life raft out to sea before the ship sank. = 在船沉之前，他们已经把救生筏送出海。
He had put all his savings out to sea on a risky venture. = 他已经把所有积蓄都投入到一个冒险的项目中。


将来时:
We will put the research vessel out to sea next week. = 我们下周将把这艘研究船送出海。
The captain will put the damaged boat out to sea, hoping for rescue. = 船长将把受损的船只送出海，希望能获救。


虚拟语气:
If we had put the boat out to sea earlier, we would have avoided the storm. = 如果我们早点把船送出海，就能避免这场暴风雨。
I wish I had put my fears out to sea long ago. = 我真希望我很久以前就能放下恐惧。


被动语态:
The small boat was put out to sea by the strong current. = 小船被强劲的洋流送出海。
The new policy was put out to sea, regardless of public opinion. = 新政策被推出，不管公众的意见如何。


疑问句:
When will the fishing fleet be put out to sea? = 渔船队什么时候出海？
Why was the damaged ship put out to sea? = 为什么受损的船只被送出海？


否定句:
They did not put the boat out to sea because of the bad weather. = 由于天气恶劣，他们没有把船送出海。
The company decided not to put the new product out to sea until next year. = 公司决定新产品推迟到明年再推出。
