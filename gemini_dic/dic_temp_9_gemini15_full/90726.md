humiliated:/ˈhjuːmɪlieɪtɪd/| adj.|受羞辱的；蒙受耻辱的| feeling ashamed and degraded.  The experience left him feeling deeply humiliated.  |近义词：mortified, ashamed, disgraced, humbled|反义词：honored, glorified, exalted|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is humiliated by his failure. = 他因为失败而感到羞愧。
She feels humiliated when people laugh at her. = 当人们嘲笑她时，她感到羞愧。


一般过去时:
I was humiliated by his public criticism. = 我被他公开的批评而感到羞辱。
They were humiliated by the team's loss. = 球队输了，他们感到很丢脸。


现在完成时:
He has been humiliated many times in his life. = 他一生中多次受过羞辱。
She has never felt so humiliated before. = 她以前从未感到如此羞愧。


过去完成时:
I had been humiliated before, so I was prepared for it. = 我以前受过羞辱，所以我做好了准备。
They had been humiliated repeatedly, leading to low self-esteem. = 他们屡次受辱，导致自尊心低下。


将来时:
He will be humiliated if he doesn't apologize. = 如果他不道歉，他将会颜面尽失。
She will feel humiliated if her secret is revealed. = 如果她的秘密被揭露，她会感到羞愧。


虚拟语气:
I would be humiliated if I failed the exam. = 如果考试不及格，我会感到羞愧。
She wished she hadn't acted in a way that humiliated her. = 她真希望自己没有做出让她丢脸的事情。


被动语态:
He was humiliated in front of everyone. = 他当着所有人的面受了羞辱。
She was humiliated by the sarcastic remarks. = 她被讽刺的话语羞辱了。


疑问句:

Is she humiliated by her mistake? = 她因为自己的错误而感到羞愧吗？


否定句:
He wasn't humiliated by the rejection.  = 他并没有因为被拒绝而感到羞愧。
She wasn't humiliated; she simply felt disappointed. = 她没有感到羞辱，只是感到失望。
