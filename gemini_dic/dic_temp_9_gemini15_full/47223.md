dazed:/deɪzd/ | adj. | 茫然的；昏昏沉沉的；受惊的 |  Feeling stunned or confused, typically as a result of a shock or sudden event.  |例句：After the car accident, she was dazed and confused.  车祸后，她感到茫然和困惑。 |例句：The blow to the head left him dazed.  头部遭到重击后，他感到头晕目眩。|近义词：stunned, bewildered, disoriented, confused |反义词：alert, aware, clear-headed


词源:  The word "dazed" comes from the Old English word "dæsan," meaning "to stun" or "to daze."  This is related to the Old High German "tezan," meaning "to strike" or "to beat."


用法说明:


一般现在时:
He is dazed by the news. 他对这个消息感到茫然。
She is always dazed after a long flight. 她长途飞行后总是昏昏沉沉的。


一般过去时:
The boxer was dazed by a powerful punch. 拳击手被一记重拳打得头晕眼花。
I was completely dazed after the accident. 事故后我完全懵了。


现在完成时:
He has been dazed since the accident. 自从事故发生后，他就一直处于茫然状态。
She has been dazed by the intensity of the situation. 她被局势的紧张程度搞得晕头转向。


过去完成时:
He had been dazed for hours after the fall. 他摔倒后昏迷了几个小时。
They had been dazed by the unexpected announcement. 他们被突如其来的公告搞得不知所措。


将来时:
The impact will likely daze him for a few minutes.  这次撞击可能会让他晕个几分钟。
I think the news will daze her. 我想这个消息会让她震惊。


虚拟语气:
If he had not been dazed, he might have avoided the accident. 如果他没有晕头转向，他本可以避免事故。
I wish I hadn't been so dazed during the exam. 我真希望考试时我没有那么昏昏沉沉。


被动语态:
He was dazed by the bright light. 他被强光晃得眼花缭乱。
She was dazed by the sudden noise. 她被突如其来的噪音弄得晕头转向。


疑问句:
Was he dazed after the fall? 他摔倒后是不是昏过去了？
Are you dazed by the complexity of the problem? 你是不是被问题的复杂性搞糊涂了？


否定句:
He wasn't dazed by the criticism. 他并没有被批评弄得晕头转向。
She wasn't dazed by the loud music. 她并没有被大声的音乐弄得头晕目眩。
