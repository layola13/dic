logjammed:/ˈlɒɡdʒæmd/| adj.| 阻塞的；停滞的；受阻的 |  Blocked or obstructed, like a river or a process;  characterized by a standstill due to blockage.  |例句：The legislative process is completely logjammed, with no bills making progress. 立法程序完全停滞不前，没有任何法案取得进展。

The negotiations are logjammed due to irreconcilable differences. 由于无法调和的分歧，谈判陷入了僵局。|近义词：blocked, stalled, gridlocked, deadlocked |反义词：flowing, progressing, unblocked, moving freely


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The project is logjammed due to funding issues. = 由于资金问题，该项目陷入了僵局。
The traffic is logjammed because of the accident. = 由于事故，交通堵塞了。


一般过去时:
The river was logjammed by fallen trees. = 河流被倒下的树木阻塞了。
The negotiations were logjammed until a compromise was reached. = 直到达成妥协，谈判才得以解冻。


现在完成时:
The pipeline has been logjammed for weeks. = 管道已经堵塞了几周了。
The discussions have been logjammed by disagreements over key issues. = 由于在关键问题上的分歧，讨论陷入了僵局。


过去完成时:
The project had been logjammed for months before a new manager arrived. = 在新经理到来之前，该项目已经停滞了几个月。
The negotiations had been logjammed for days, leading to frustration among participants. = 谈判已经僵持了几天，导致参与者感到沮丧。


将来时:
The construction project will be logjammed unless we get more funding. = 除非我们获得更多资金，否则建筑项目将会停滞。
The traffic will be logjammed during the rush hour. = 在高峰时段，交通将会堵塞。


虚拟语气:
If the budget hadn't been cut, the project wouldn't have been logjammed. = 如果预算没有被削减，项目就不会停滞。
If we had anticipated the problem, the negotiations wouldn't have been logjammed. = 如果我们预料到这个问题，谈判就不会陷入僵局。


被动语态:
The system was logjammed by a software glitch. = 系统由于软件故障而瘫痪。
The progress was logjammed by bureaucratic red tape. = 由于官僚主义的繁文缛节，进展受到了阻碍。


疑问句:
Is the pipeline logjammed again? = 管道又堵塞了吗？
Is the legislative process logjammed by political maneuvering? = 立法程序是否因为政治策略而受阻？


否定句:
The process is not logjammed; it's just slow. = 流程并没有停滞，只是速度很慢。
The negotiations were not logjammed; they proceeded smoothly. = 谈判并没有停滞，进展顺利。


PHRASE->
a logjam of problems = 一堆问题
break a logjam = 打破僵局

economic logjam = 经济僵局

词源:
The word "logjam" comes from the literal image of logs (pieces of wood) jamming a river, creating a blockage.  The "jam" part refers to the act of being tightly packed or wedged together, causing obstruction.
