scombroid:/ˈskɒmbrɔɪd/|/ˈskɑːmbrɔɪd/| n. |鲭科鱼类；（指鱼类）鲭状的|Scombroid refers to fish belonging to the family Scombridae, which includes tuna, mackerel, and bonito.  These fish are characterized by their fusiform (torpedo-shaped) bodies, forked tails, and often, streamlined bodies adapted for fast swimming. The term can also be used to describe something resembling these fish in shape or form.|Example:  Many commercially important fish are scombroids, providing a valuable source of protein globally.  许多重要的商业性鱼类都属于鲭科鱼类，为全球提供了宝贵的蛋白质来源。|近义词：mackerel-like, tuna-like|反义词：无


Etymology:
The word "scombroid" derives from the genus name *Scomber*, which is the type genus of the Scombridae family.  *Scomber* itself is ultimately of Greek origin, possibly related to words meaning "to wander" or "to roam," reflecting the migratory nature of many scombroid fish.
