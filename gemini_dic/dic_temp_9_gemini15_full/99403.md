interdisciplinary:/ˌɪntərˈdɪsəplɪneri/ | adj. |跨学科的，多学科的 | Relating to more than one branch of knowledge.  Interdisciplinary studies integrate knowledge and methods from multiple disciplines to address complex problems or questions. | Example: The research project adopted an interdisciplinary approach, drawing on expertise from sociology, psychology, and economics.  这个研究项目采用了跨学科的方法，汲取了社会学、心理学和经济学的专业知识。|近义词：multidisciplinary, transdisciplinary |反义词：disciplinary, single-discipline

Word Origin:

The word "interdisciplinary" is formed from the prefix "inter-" (meaning "between" or "among"), the word "discipline" (referring to a branch of knowledge or study), and the suffix "-ary" (forming adjectives).  Therefore, it literally means "between disciplines" or "relating to multiple disciplines."
