obser:  /ˈɒbzɜː(r)/ | /əbˈzɜːrv/ | n. | 观察者 | An observer is a person who watches something or someone carefully.  Observers are often present at scientific experiments or important events to record data or witness proceedings. | Example:  The scientists were careful observers, noting every detail of the experiment. 科学家们是细心的观察者，记录实验的每一个细节。 |近义词: viewer, spectator, onlooker |反义词: participant, actor


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The observer watches the birds. = 观察者观察着鸟类。
He observes the traffic carefully. = 他仔细观察交通情况。


一般过去时:
The observer watched the experiment closely. = 观察者仔细观察了实验。
She observed a strange phenomenon. = 她观察到一种奇怪的现象。


现在完成时:
The observer has observed many interesting events. = 观察者已经观察到许多有趣的事件。
We have observed a significant change in the data. = 我们已经观察到数据发生了显著变化。


过去完成时:
The observer had observed the pattern before the experiment concluded. = 在实验结束之前，观察者已经观察到这种模式。
They had observed similar results in previous studies. = 他们在之前的研究中观察到类似的结果。


将来时:
The observer will observe the celestial event tonight. = 观察者今晚将观察天象。
She will observe the patients' reactions to the new medication. = 她将观察病人对新药的反应。


虚拟语气:
If the observer were closer, he would see more detail. = 如果观察者更近一些，他会看到更多细节。
I wish the observer had been more attentive. = 我希望观察者当时更专心一些。


被动语态:
The experiment was observed by several scientists. = 实验被几位科学家观察到。
The changes were observed and recorded meticulously. = 这些变化被仔细地观察和记录。


疑问句:
Who observed the incident? = 谁观察到了这一事件？
What did the observer see? = 观察者看到了什么？


否定句:
The observer did not see anything unusual. = 观察者没有看到任何异常情况。
They did not observe any significant differences. = 他们没有观察到任何显著差异。
