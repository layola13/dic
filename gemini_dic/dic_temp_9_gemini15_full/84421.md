halls:/hɔːlz/ | n. |大厅；礼堂；走廊；住宅 | Large rooms used for public gatherings or ceremonies, or long narrow passages in buildings.  |例句：The concert was held in the grand halls of the city.  这场音乐会在城市的大厅举行。

例句：We walked down the long, dimly lit halls of the old castle. 我们走过古堡长长的、昏暗的走廊。


近义词：rooms, corridors, auditoriums, chambers | 反义词：None specifically, but  "small room" could be considered an antonym in some contexts. | 词性:名词


词源:  From Old English *halle, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch hal and German Halle, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *hallō.  The word's evolution reflects its long history as a significant architectural feature in various cultures.
