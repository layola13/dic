inklings:/ˈɪŋklɪŋz/| n. |迹象；预感；模糊的暗示 | A slight suggestion or indication; a vague feeling or understanding.  |例句：I had an inkling that something was wrong. 我隐隐觉得有什么不对劲。

例句：She gave me an inkling of her plans. 她给了我一些关于她计划的暗示。


近义词：hint, suggestion, suspicion, feeling, premonition

反义词：certainty, assurance, knowledge


词源：源自古英语 *inclingan，意为“ to make known, announce”。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I have an inkling of what's going on. = 我对正在发生的事情有点感觉。
He gives no inkling of his intentions. = 他没有透露他的意图。


一般过去时:
I had an inkling that she was unhappy. = 我隐隐觉得她不高兴。
They gave no inkling of their plans. = 他们没有透露他们的计划。


现在完成时:
I've had an inkling that something bad might happen. = 我隐隐觉得可能会发生不好的事情。
Have you had any inkling of their intentions? = 你对他们的意图有任何感觉吗？


过去完成时:
I'd had an inkling that the project was failing. = 我已经隐隐觉得这个项目要失败了。
She hadn't given any inkling that she was leaving. = 她没有透露她要离开的消息。


将来时:

Will you give me an inkling of your decision? = 你会给我暗示你的决定吗？


虚拟语气:
