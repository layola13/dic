lowbrow:/ˈloʊbraʊ/| adj.|低俗的；庸俗的；智力水平低的 |Having or appealing to a low level of intellectual or cultural taste.  It refers to things considered unrefined, commonplace, or lacking in sophistication.|例句：He enjoys lowbrow comedies. 他喜欢低俗喜剧。  The magazine is considered lowbrow entertainment. 这本杂志被认为是低俗的娱乐读物。|近义词：vulgar, common, unsophisticated, plebeian|反义词：highbrow, cultured, sophisticated, refined|词性:形容词

词源:

The term "lowbrow" emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.  It's a figurative expression that uses "brow" as a metaphor for intellect.  A "highbrow" person was considered intelligent and cultured, while a "lowbrow" person was seen as having less refined tastes.  The term reflects social stratification and judgments about cultural preferences.
