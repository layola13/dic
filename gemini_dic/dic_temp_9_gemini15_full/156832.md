piggybacked:/ˈpɪɡiˌbækt/ | v. | 搭便车；附加；顺带捎带 | To attach or add something to something else, especially in a way that is convenient or opportunistic.  | 例句：They piggybacked the new software onto the existing system.  他们将新软件附加到现有系统上。

例句：We piggybacked our marketing campaign onto a larger event. 我们将我们的营销活动附加到一个更大的活动上。


近义词：attached, added, appended, combined | 反义词：detached, separated, removed | 词性:动词

词源:
The term "piggyback" originates from the image of a child riding on the back of another person, like a pig riding on a back.  The figurative use extends this imagery to describe adding something to something else in a dependent or secondary manner.
