outsources:/ˈaʊtsɔːrsɪz/ | vt. | 外包；外判 | To obtain (goods or a service) from an outside or foreign supplier, in particular in place of an internal source.  |例句：Many companies outsource their manufacturing to countries with lower labor costs. 许多公司将其制造业务外包给劳动力成本较低的国家。

例句：The company outsources its customer service to a call center in India.  这家公司将其客户服务外包给印度的一家呼叫中心。


近义词：contract out, subcontract, delegate


反义词：insource


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company outsources its IT services. = 公司将IT服务外包。
We outsource our marketing to a specialist agency. = 我们将营销外包给一家专业机构。


一般过去时:
They outsourced their data entry last year. = 他们去年将数据录入外包了。
The project was outsourced to a third-party vendor. = 该项目外包给了第三方供应商。


现在完成时:
The firm has outsourced several departments since 2020. = 自2020年以来，该公司已将多个部门外包。
We have outsourced our accounting for many years. = 我们多年来一直将会计工作外包。


过去完成时:
They had already outsourced the project before the meeting. = 会议之前他们已经将项目外包了。
The company had outsourced its printing before the new regulations came into effect. = 新规定生效前，该公司已将印刷业务外包。


将来时:
We will outsource our production to China next year. = 我们明年将把生产外包给中国。
The company plans to outsource its human resources department in the future. = 公司计划将来将其人力资源部门外包。


虚拟语气:
If we outsourced the work, we would save money. = 如果我们将工作外包，我们将节省资金。
It would be better if they outsourced their customer support. = 如果他们将客户支持外包会更好。


被动语态:
The work was outsourced to a cheaper provider. = 这项工作外包给了更便宜的供应商。
The project is being outsourced to a team in another country. = 项目正在外包给另一个国家的团队。


疑问句:
Does your company outsource any of its functions? = 贵公司是否将任何职能外包？
Will they outsource the software development? = 他们会将软件开发外包吗？


否定句:
We do not outsource our core business functions. = 我们不会将核心业务职能外包。
The company decided not to outsource its research and development. = 公司决定不将其研发部门外包。


PHRASE->
outsource manufacturing = 外包制造
outsource production = 外包生产
outsource services = 外包服务
outsource to a third party = 外包给第三方
outsource IT = 外包IT


词源:
The word "outsource" is a relatively modern coinage, a blend of "out" (meaning outside) and "source" (meaning origin or supplier).  It reflects the practice of obtaining goods or services from external providers.
