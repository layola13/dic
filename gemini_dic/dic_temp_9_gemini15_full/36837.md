colonizability:/ˌkɒlənaɪzəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可殖民化性，易于被定殖 | The ability of a microorganism to establish itself and multiply in a particular environment or host. This often depends on factors like the microorganism's virulence, the host's immune system, and the environmental conditions.  | Example: The high colonizability of *Staphylococcus aureus* in hospital settings contributes to its prevalence as a nosocomial pathogen.  医院环境中金黄色葡萄球菌的高可定殖性导致其作为医院感染病原体的普遍存在。 |近义词：inhabitability, susceptibility | 反义词：resistance, immunity | 名词
