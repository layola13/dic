exuviation:/ˌɛksjuˈveɪʃən/| n. |蜕皮；蜕壳；脱落|The shedding of an outer layer, such as an insect's exoskeleton or a snake's skin.  This process is crucial for growth and development in many arthropods and reptiles. |例句：The snake's exuviation was a spectacular sight.  这条蛇蜕皮的景象非常壮观。

例句：The cicada's exuviation left its empty shell clinging to the tree.  蝉蜕下的空壳粘在树上。


近义词：molting, shedding, sloughing|反义词：retention, growth (in the context of the new layer replacing the old)|词性:名词
