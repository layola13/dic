retainership: /rɪˈteɪnərʃɪp/ | n. | 保留；委托；常年聘用；酬金 | The state of being retained;  the act of retaining someone. It often refers to a contractual agreement where a client retains the services of a professional (like a lawyer or consultant) on a regular basis for a set fee. |


英文解析:A retainership is a contractual agreement under which a client hires a professional (such as a lawyer, consultant, or other expert) on a continuing basis for a predetermined fee.  This fee is paid regularly, usually monthly or annually, and guarantees the professional's availability and services to the client, even if specific tasks aren't always immediately available.  The retainership ensures a prioritized relationship with the professional.


例句:

The law firm secured a lucrative retainership with a major corporation. = 这家律师事务所与一家大公司签署了一份利润丰厚的常年聘用合同。

Our company has a retainership agreement with a marketing consultant. = 我们公司与一位市场顾问签订了常年聘用协议。


近义词:retainer, engagement, employment, contract


反义词: termination, dismissal, release


词源:  Derived from the verb "retain," meaning "to keep possession of,"  combined with the suffix "-ership," indicating a state, condition, or quality.  The word's roots trace back to Old French "retenir" and ultimately to Latin "retinere."
