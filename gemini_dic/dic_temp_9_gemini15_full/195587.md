semiproof:/ˈsɛmiˌpruːf/| adj.|半证明，不完全证明|A semiproof is an argument or demonstration that falls short of a complete or rigorous proof but provides strong evidence or a high degree of plausibility for a proposition. It might rely on assumptions, approximations, or incomplete data, yet still offers convincing support for the claim.  |例句：The physicist presented a semiproof of his theory, acknowledging some gaps in the mathematical formalism. 物理学家提出了他理论的一个半证明，承认数学形式化中存在一些差距。|近义词：partial proof, suggestive evidence, plausible argument|反义词：full proof, rigorous proof, irrefutable evidence|形容词
