hit the road:/hɪt ðə roʊd/| phrasal verb|动身；出发；上路|To begin a journey, especially a long one by car or other vehicle.  This idiom emphasizes the act of starting a journey immediately.|例句：It's getting late, we should hit the road.  该晚啦，我们该上路了。

例句：We hit the road at dawn to avoid the traffic.  为了避免交通拥堵，我们在黎明时分就出发了。


近义词：set off, depart, leave, start out

反义词：arrive, return


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They hit the road every weekend. = 他们每个周末都上路。
We hit the road early to avoid the rush hour. = 我们早点出发以避免高峰时段。


一般过去时:
They hit the road last night. = 他们昨晚出发了。
We hit the road after breakfast. = 我们早餐后就出发了。


现在完成时:
They have hit the road several times this month. = 这个月他们已经出发好几次了。
We have hit the road to many different places. = 我们已经去过很多不同的地方了。


过去完成时:
They had hit the road before the storm hit. = 在暴风雨来临之前，他们已经出发了。
We had hit the road before we realized we forgot something. = 我们在意识到忘记了什么东西之前就已经出发了。


将来时:
They will hit the road tomorrow morning. = 他们明天早上会出发。
We will hit the road as soon as the rain stops. = 雨停了我们就出发。


虚拟语气:
If they had hit the road earlier, they would have arrived on time. = 如果他们早点出发，他们就会准时到达。
I wish we had hit the road sooner. = 我真希望我们早点出发。


被动语态: (This idiom is rarely used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
Will they hit the road soon? = 他们很快就会出发吗？
When are you going to hit the road? = 你打算什么时候出发？


否定句:
They didn't hit the road until noon. = 他们直到中午才出发。
We won't hit the road until we finish packing. = 我们要等到打包完毕才出发。


PHRASE->
hit the road hard = 加速行驶
hit the open road = 驶上开阔的道路
hit the road running = 一开始就全力以赴
hit the road again = 再次出发
hit the road jack = (俚语)滚蛋


词源:
The phrase "hit the road" likely originated in the early days of automobile travel. The image of a car literally "hitting" or driving onto the road vividly conveys the act of starting a journey.  The use of "hit" adds a sense of energy and decisiveness to the departure.
