monopolise: /məˈnɒpəlaɪz/ | /məˈnɑːpəlaɪz/ | vt. |垄断；独占；霸占 | To control something completely and prevent anyone else from sharing it. |例句：He tried to monopolise the conversation. 他试图独占谈话。 |例句：The company monopolised the market for years. 这家公司垄断了市场多年。|近义词：dominate, control, corner, hoard |反义词：share, distribute, relinquish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He monopolises the conversation. = 他垄断了谈话。
She monopolises the company's resources. = 她独占了公司的资源。

一般过去时:
They monopolised the market. = 他们垄断了市场。
He monopolised her attention. = 他独占了她的注意力。

现在完成时:
They have monopolised the industry for decades. = 他们几十年来一直垄断着这个行业。
She has monopolised the conversation all evening. = 她整个晚上都在独占谈话。

过去完成时:
The company had monopolised the market before the new competitors arrived. = 在新的竞争对手到来之前，这家公司已经垄断了市场。
He had monopolised the discussion, leaving no room for others to speak. = 他独占了讨论，没有给其他人留下发言的空间。

将来时:
He will monopolise the resources if he gets the promotion. = 如果他升职，他会独占资源。
They will likely monopolise the technology in the future. = 未来他们很可能会垄断这项技术。

虚拟语气:
If he monopolised the market, he would become very wealthy. = 如果他垄断了市场，他就会变得非常富有。
I wish he wouldn't monopolise the conversation. = 我希望他不独占谈话。

被动语态:
The market was monopolised by a single company. = 市场被一家公司垄断。
The resources are being monopolised by the management. = 资源正在被管理层独占。

疑问句:
Does he monopolise the conversation? = 他是不是独占了谈话？
Is the company monopolising the market unfairly? = 这家公司是否不正当地垄断了市场？

否定句:
He didn't monopolise the discussion. = 他没有独占讨论。
She doesn't want to monopolise your time. = 她不想占用你的时间。
