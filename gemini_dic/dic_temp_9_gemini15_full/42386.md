counterblockading: /ˌkaʊntərblɒˈkeɪdɪŋ/ | v. | 反封锁 | The act of establishing a blockade to counter or oppose an existing blockade.  It involves intercepting or preventing the movement of supplies or personnel intended for a blockaded area or entity.  This is often a retaliatory measure in times of conflict or political tension. | Example:  The country responded to the naval blockade by initiating a counterblockading operation, disrupting the enemy's supply lines.  = 该国通过发起反封锁行动来回应海军封锁，扰乱了敌人的供应线。 | 近义词：counter-blockade,  opposition blockade | 反义词：lifting a blockade,  unblockading
