foreplane:/ˈfɔːrpleɪn/ | n. |前缘，前平面，(飞机的)前翼| A foreplane is a surface, typically on an aircraft or other flying vehicle, located at the front edge of the wing or similar lifting surface. It is often used to control pitch (the up-and-down motion) and can be adjusted to improve stability and maneuverability.  In older contexts, it might also refer to a carpenter's tool. | Example: The pilot adjusted the foreplane to maintain a steady altitude.  飞行员调整前缘以保持稳定高度。  | 近义词：leading edge, horizontal stabilizer (in aircraft context) | 反义词：rear plane, aft plane
