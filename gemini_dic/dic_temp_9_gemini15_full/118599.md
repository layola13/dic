mess:/mes/| n., v.| 混乱；脏乱；困境；弄乱；搞糟 |A state of disorder or confusion.  |例句：The room was in a complete mess.  房间里一片混乱。

The project is a complete mess.  这个项目一团糟。|近义词：disorder, chaos, muddle, jumble |反义词：order, tidiness, neatness |名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The children make a mess every day. = 孩子们每天都弄得乱七八糟。
This situation is a real mess. = 这种情况真是糟糕透顶。


一般过去时:
He messed up the kitchen. = 他把厨房弄乱了。
They made a terrible mess of the party. = 他们把聚会搞砸了。


现在完成时:
She has made a mess of her life. = 她把自己的生活搞得一团糟。
They have messed up the garden. = 他们把花园弄得乱七八糟了。


过去完成时:
He had already messed up the report before the deadline. = 他在截止日期之前就已经把报告搞砸了。
She had made a mess of her hair before she went out. = 她出门前就已经把头发弄乱了。


将来时:
He will mess up the interview if he doesn't prepare. = 如果他不准备，他将会把面试搞砸。
They will make a mess of the painting if they are not careful. = 他们如果不仔细的话，将会把画弄脏。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't been so careless, he wouldn't have made such a mess. = 如果他不那么粗心，就不会弄得这么乱。
I wish I hadn't made such a mess of the cake. = 我真希望我没有把蛋糕弄得这么糟糕。


被动语态:
The room was messed up by the children. = 房间是被孩子们弄乱的。
The project was a complete mess, and it was never finished. = 这个项目一团糟，而且从未完成。


疑问句:
What's the mess all about? = 这到底是怎么回事？
Is this mess your doing? = 这是你弄的吗？


否定句:
He didn't mean to make a mess. = 他不是故意要弄乱的。
She didn't mess up the presentation. = 她没有把演示搞砸。


PHRASE->
make a mess = 弄乱；搞砸
in a mess = 一团糟；乱七八糟
clean up the mess = 收拾烂摊子
a right mess = 一大堆烂摊子
a hot mess =  一团糟（尤指人或情况）

词源:
The word "mess" originates from Old French "mes," meaning "portion of food,"  and ultimately from the Latin "missa," meaning "sent,"  referring to a portion of food sent or served. Over time, its meaning evolved to encompass a state of disorder or confusion.
