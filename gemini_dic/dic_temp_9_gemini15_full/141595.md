oppugn:/əˈpʌɡn/| vt.| 反驳；驳斥；攻击 | To attack someone's opinions or arguments.  To oppose or resist something. |例句：He was not afraid to oppugn the views of his superiors. 他不怕反驳上司的观点。

例句：The senator oppugned the president's economic policies. 参议员驳斥了总统的经济政策。


近义词：challenge, contest, refute, deny, oppose |反义词：support, endorse, agree with, confirm |词性：动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He oppugns the theory. = 他反驳这个理论。
They oppugn the government's decision. = 他们反对政府的决定。

一般过去时:
She oppugned his arguments effectively. = 她有效地反驳了他的论点。
The lawyer oppugned the witness's testimony. = 律师驳斥了证人的证词。

现在完成时:
He has oppugned the prevailing wisdom on many occasions. = 他多次驳斥了流行的观点。
They have oppugned the proposed legislation. = 他们反对拟议中的立法。

过去完成时:
She had oppugned his claims before the debate. = 辩论之前，她就驳斥了他的说法。
They had oppugned the ruling party's policies for years. = 他们多年来一直反对执政党的政策。

将来时:
He will oppugn the findings of the report. = 他将反驳报告的调查结果。
They will oppugn the new regulations. = 他们将反对新的规章制度。

虚拟语气:
If he were to oppugn the judge's ruling, he would face serious consequences. = 如果他敢于驳斥法官的裁决，他将面临严重后果。
I wish I had the courage to oppugn his authority. = 我真希望我有勇气反对他的权威。

被动语态:

The new policy is being oppugned by many citizens. = 许多公民正在反对这项新政策。

疑问句:
Will he oppugn the decision? = 他会反对这个决定吗？
Does she dare oppugn his authority? = 她敢于挑战他的权威吗？

否定句:
He did not oppugn the verdict. = 他没有反对判决。
She would not oppugn the prevailing opinion. = 她不会反对普遍的意见。


PHRASE->
oppugn a theory = 反驳一种理论
oppugn a decision = 反对一个决定
oppugn someone's authority = 挑战某人的权威
oppugn the evidence = 反驳证据
oppugn a statement = 反驳一个说法


词源:
From Latin *oppugnare, from ob- ("against") + pugnare ("to fight").  The word's history reflects its meaning of actively fighting against or challenging something.
