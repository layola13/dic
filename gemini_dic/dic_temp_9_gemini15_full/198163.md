shorebirds:/ˈʃɔːrˌbɜːrdz/ | n. |滨鸟，岸鸟 |Shorebirds are birds that live near the shore, often feeding on invertebrates in the intertidal zone.  They are typically characterized by long legs and bills, adapted for their foraging habits.  Many species are migratory. |例句：Many shorebirds migrate long distances each year to breed and feed.  许多滨鸟每年迁徙很长的距离来繁殖和觅食。

例句：We saw a variety of shorebirds on the beach, including sandpipers and plovers.我们在沙滩上看到了各种各样的滨鸟，包括滨鹬和鸻。


近义词：coastal birds, waders


反义词：None (no direct antonym)


词性：noun
