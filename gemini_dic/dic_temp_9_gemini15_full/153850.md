percepts:/ˈpɜːsɛpts/ | n. |知觉；感知；感官印象 |The mental representations of sensory experience.  Percepts are the basic units of perception, formed by the brain's interpretation of sensory input. They are subjective and can vary between individuals. |例句：Our percepts of the world are shaped by our past experiences and cultural background. = 我们对世界的感知受到我们过去经验和文化背景的影响。

例句：The artist skillfully manipulated light and shadow to create striking percepts in his paintings. =艺术家巧妙地运用光影，在他的画作中创造出令人印象深刻的知觉效果。


近义词：sensations, perceptions, impressions |反义词：hallucinations (in the sense of inaccurate perceptions)


词源：源自拉丁语“percipere”，意思是“感知”或“察觉”。  It combines the prefix "per-" (thoroughly) and "capere" (to take or seize).
