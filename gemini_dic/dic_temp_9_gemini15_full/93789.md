immerge:/ɪˈmɜːdʒ/ | v. | 出现；浮现；显露；沉浸；使沉浸 | To come out of something that covers or surrounds something else; to become apparent, known, or prominent.  |例句：The sun emerged from behind the clouds. 太阳从云后出现。

The truth eventually emerged.  真相最终大白。


近义词：appear, emerge, surface, rise | 反义词：submerge, disappear, sink


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun emerges from behind the clouds every morning. 太阳每天早上从云层后面出现。
He always emerges victorious from any challenge. 他总是能从任何挑战中取得胜利。


一般过去时:
The swimmer emerged from the pool, shivering. 游泳者从游泳池里出来，浑身发抖。
After the storm, the truth emerged about the damage. 暴风雨过后，损害的真相浮出水面。


现在完成时:
The truth has emerged about the scandal.  丑闻的真相已经大白。
New evidence has emerged that suggests he is innocent.  出现的新证据表明他是无辜的。


过去完成时:
By the time I arrived, the winner had already emerged. 当我到达时，获胜者已经产生了。
After the investigation had emerged, several people were arrested. 调查结束后，有几个人被捕。


将来时:
The results of the election will emerge later today. 选举结果将在今天晚些时候揭晓。
A new leader will emerge from the chaos. 一个新的领导者将从混乱中脱颖而出。


虚拟语气:
If only the truth had emerged sooner! 要是真相早点大白就好了！
I wish a solution would emerge from this deadlock. 我希望这个问题能从僵局中找到解决办法。


被动语态:
The problem was eventually emerged as a major challenge.  这个问题最终被认定为一个重大挑战。(This is less common; "was identified" or "was revealed" would be more natural)
New findings will be emerged to the public soon. 新发现很快就会公之于众。(This is less common; "will be revealed" or "will be announced" would be more natural)


疑问句:
When will the winner emerge? 获胜者将在何时产生？
Will a solution ever emerge to this problem? 这个问题最终会有解决办法吗？


否定句:
The truth did not emerge until much later. 真相直到很久以后才大白。
No clear winner emerged from the contest. 比赛中没有出现明显的赢家。


PHRASE->
emerge victorious: 赢得胜利
emerge unscathed: 安然无恙
emerge from: 从...出现
emerge as: 成为...
emerge into: 进入...


词源:
The word "emerge" comes from the Old French word "emerger," which in turn derives from the Latin word "emergere," meaning "to rise out of," "to come forth," or "to rise to the surface."  The Latin word is formed from the prefix "e-" (out) and "mergere" (to plunge or dip).
