phosphene:/ˈfɒsfɪn/ /ˈfɑːsfɪn/| n. |磷光，幻视|A phosphene is a visual phenomenon characterized by the experience of seeing light without light actually entering the eye.  This can be caused by various factors, including pressure on the eyes, electrical stimulation of the visual cortex, or certain drugs.  Phosphenes can appear as flashes, spots, or other patterns of light.|Example:  He experienced bright phosphenes after rubbing his eyes hard. 他用力揉眼睛后看到了明亮的磷光。

The patient reported seeing phosphenes during the migraine. 病人在偏头痛期间报告看到磷光。|近义词：flash, spark, glimmer|反义词：无|
