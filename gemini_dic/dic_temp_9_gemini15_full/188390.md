rubric:/ˈruːbrɪk/ | n. |评分标准；标题；(章节等的)小标题；规则；(书写材料的)红色标题| A rubric is a set of instructions or guidelines that explain how to do something, often used in academic settings to explain how a piece of work will be graded.  It can also refer to a heading or title, especially one printed in red. | Example: The rubric for the essay clearly outlined the criteria for grading.  评分标准清楚地概述了论文的评分标准。

The exam rubric detailed the point allocation for each section.  考试评分标准详细说明了各部分的计分分配。

| 近义词：criteria, guidelines, instructions, heading, title | 反义词：None directly applicable |  词性：名词


ETYMOLOGY->
From Latin *rubrica ("red ocher; red heading"), from ruber ("red").  The original rubrics were written in red ink.
