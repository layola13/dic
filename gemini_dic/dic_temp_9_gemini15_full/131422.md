nonclimbable: /ˌnɑːnˈklaɪməbəl/ | adj. | 不可攀登的，无法攀爬的 |  Not able to be climbed.  This word describes something that is too steep, slippery, or otherwise impossible to ascend. |


例句：

The sheer cliff face was nonclimbable. =  陡峭的悬崖峭壁是不可攀登的。

The icy mountain slope proved nonclimbable, even for experienced climbers. = 冰冻的山坡证明是不可攀登的，即使对于经验丰富的登山者也是如此。


近义词：unclimbable, inaccessible, impassable

反义词：climbable, accessible, scalable


词源：

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "without".
* **climbable**: adjective derived from the verb "climb," meaning "able to be climbed."  "Climb" ultimately derives from Old English *climban*.


用法说明：

由于`nonclimbable`是一个形容词，它在句子中主要做定语修饰名词。  它没有动词形式，也不存在时态变化。


PHRASE->

There are no common phrases specifically using "nonclimbable".  The word itself is used directly to describe a characteristic.
