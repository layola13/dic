occasioning: /əˈkeɪʒənɪŋ/ | v. | 导致；引起；造成 |  To cause or give rise to (something, especially something bad).  | 例句：The storm occasioning widespread flooding. = 暴风雨导致了大范围的洪涝。

The heavy rain occasioning a delay in the game. = 大雨导致比赛延误。


近义词：causing, producing, resulting in, bringing about | 反义词：preventing, avoiding, averting | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud music is occasioning complaints from the neighbours. = 吵闹的音乐正在引起邻居的抱怨。
His actions are occasioning a lot of trouble. = 他的行为正在造成很多麻烦。


一般过去时:
The fire occasioned significant damage to the building. = 大火给建筑物造成了严重的损害。
Her carelessness occasioned a serious accident. = 她的粗心导致了一场严重的事故。


现在完成时:
The recent drought has occasioned food shortages in many areas. = 最近的旱灾已导致许多地区粮食短缺。
His impulsive decision has occasioned much regret. = 他冲动的决定给他带来了许多后悔。


过去完成时:
The lack of planning had occasioned many problems during the project. = 缺乏计划在项目期间造成了许多问题。
The heavy snowfall had occasioned the closure of the highway. = 大雪导致了高速公路的关闭。


将来时:
The new policy will occasion some changes in the workplace. = 新政策将会引起工作场所的一些变化。
The upcoming election will occasion much debate. = 即将到来的选举将会引起许多争论。


虚拟语气:
If he had been more careful, he would not have occasioned such a disaster. = 如果他更小心一点，他就不会造成如此大的灾难。
If she had studied harder, she would not have occasioned failure in the exam. = 如果她学习更努力，她就不会考试不及格。


被动语态:
Many problems were occasioned by the lack of communication. = 许多问题是由缺乏沟通造成的。
The delay was occasioned by unforeseen circumstances. = 延误是由不可预见的情况造成的。


疑问句:
What occasioned the sudden change of plans? = 什么导致了计划的突然改变？
What occasioned the argument between them? = 什么导致了他们之间的争吵？


否定句:
The good weather did not occasion any problems for the picnic. = 好天气并没有给野餐带来任何问题。
His efforts did not occasion any improvement in the situation. = 他的努力并没有改善这种情况。


PHRASE->
occasioning damage = 造成损害
occasioning delays = 造成延误
occasioning trouble = 造成麻烦
occasioning concern = 引起担忧
occasioning hardship = 造成困苦


词源:
源自古法语 *ocasioner，源自拉丁语 occasio, occasionem "a falling, a chance, an opportunity,"  字面意思为“a falling,”  引申为“a happening, an event,” 然后发展为“cause, reason.”
