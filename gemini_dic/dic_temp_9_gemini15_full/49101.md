deign:/deɪn/| vt.|屈尊；俯就；认为值得；同意 |To condescend to do something; to think something worthy or suitable.  It implies a sense of superiority or reluctance on the part of the subject. |例句：Would she deign to accept his invitation? = 她会屈尊接受他的邀请吗？

例句：The queen deigned to speak to the commoner. = 女王屈尊与平民说话。


近义词：condescend, stoop, vouchsafe |反义词：refuse, decline, disdain


词源：源自古法语deigner，意为“认为值得”，最终源自拉丁语dignari，“认为有尊严，认为值得”。
