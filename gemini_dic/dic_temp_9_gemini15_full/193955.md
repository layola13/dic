self-evaluate:/ˌsɛlfɪˈvæljuˌeɪt/| vt.|自我评价，自我评估|To make a judgment about one's own abilities, performance, or character.  It involves critically examining one's strengths and weaknesses.|例句：I need to self-evaluate my performance before the annual review. 我需要在年度考核前对自己的表现进行自我评估。

例句：She self-evaluated her presentation skills as needing improvement. 她对自己演讲技巧的自我评价是需要改进。


近义词：assess oneself, appraise oneself, judge oneself|反义词：N/A|词性:verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I self-evaluate my work regularly. = 我定期对自己的工作进行自我评估。
He self-evaluates his progress every week. = 他每周都会对自己取得的进展进行自我评估。


一般过去时:
She self-evaluated her project last night. = 她昨晚对自己负责的项目进行了自我评估。
They self-evaluated their teamwork after the presentation. = 演示结束后，他们对自己团队合作进行了自我评估。


现在完成时:
I have self-evaluated my strengths and weaknesses. = 我已经对自己的优势和劣势进行了自我评估。
She has self-evaluated her performance several times this year. = 今年她已经几次对自己表现进行了自我评估。


过去完成时:

They had self-evaluated their contributions to the team before the meeting. = 会议之前，他们已经对自己对团队的贡献进行了自我评估。


将来时:

She will self-evaluate her performance after the deadline. = 截止日期之后，她将对自己表现进行自我评估。


虚拟语气:
If I were to self-evaluate honestly, I would admit my mistakes. = 如果我要诚实地进行自我评估，我会承认自己的错误。
I wish I had self-evaluated my options more carefully. = 我真希望我当时更仔细地权衡过自己的选择。


被动语态:
