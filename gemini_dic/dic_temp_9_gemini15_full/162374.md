preacquit:/ˌpriːəˈkwɪt/| v.|预先开释，提前释放|To release or discharge someone from a charge or obligation before a formal trial or judgment.  This is a relatively rare word and is often used in a legal context. It implies a preemptive action, preventing a formal trial or process.|例句：The judge decided to preacquit the defendant due to lack of sufficient evidence. = 由于证据不足，法官决定提前释放被告。The prisoner was preacquitted after new evidence emerged. = 新证据出现后，囚犯被提前释放。|近义词：exonerate, absolve, discharge, release|反义词：convict, indict, imprison
