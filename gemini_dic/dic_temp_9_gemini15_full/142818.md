ousts:/aʊsts/| vt.|罢黜；驱逐；挤掉|To remove someone forcefully from a position of power or authority.|The rebels ousted the dictator from power. 反叛者罢黜了独裁者。|近义词：remove, displace, expel, dethrone|反义词：install, enthrone, appoint


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ousts his rivals from the competition. 他将竞争对手逐出比赛。
The new CEO ousts underperforming employees. 新任首席执行官解雇表现不佳的员工。


一般过去时:
The general ousted the president in a coup. 将军在政变中罢黜了总统。
She was ousted from her job for misconduct. 她因行为不端而被解雇。


现在完成时:
The team has ousted its opponents in every match. 该队在每一场比赛中都击败了对手。
The scandal has ousted several high-ranking officials. 丑闻导致几名高级官员下台。


过去完成时:
They had ousted the previous management before implementing their reforms. 他们在实施改革之前已经罢黜了之前的管理层。
The company had ousted him from his position before the news broke. 公司在消息传出之前已经将他解雇了。


将来时:
The upcoming election will likely oust the incumbent mayor.  即将到来的选举很可能会罢免现任市长。
They predict that the new technology will oust traditional methods. 他们预测这项新技术将取代传统方法。


虚拟语气:
If the opposition party won, they would oust the current government. 如果反对党获胜，他们就会罢黜现政府。
I wish they would oust that corrupt official. 我希望他们能罢黜那个腐败的官员。


被动语态:
The mayor was ousted by a vote of no confidence. 市长因信任投票而被罢免。
He was ousted from the club for breaking the rules. 他因为违反规定而被逐出俱乐部。


疑问句:
Will the challenger oust the champion? 挑战者会击败冠军吗？
Was the CEO ousted due to the recent scandal? 首席执行官是因为最近的丑闻而被罢免的吗？


否定句:
The rebels failed to oust the government. 反叛者未能推翻政府。
They did not oust him despite his many mistakes. 尽管他犯了很多错误，他们也没有罢免他。


PHRASE->
oust from power  罢黜权力

oust someone from their position  将某人从职位上罢免
oust a rival  击败对手
oust competition  战胜竞争
