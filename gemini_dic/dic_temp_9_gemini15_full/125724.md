munificently:/məˈnɪfɪsntli/| adv. |慷慨地；大方地； щедро| generously and abundantly; in a munificent manner.  The philanthropist munificently donated a large sum of money to the charity. |近义词：generously, lavishly, bountifully, liberally |反义词：stingily, meagerly, sparingly


例句:

He munificently endowed the university with a new library. 他慷慨地为大学捐赠了一个新的图书馆。

The king munificently rewarded his loyal subjects. 国王慷慨地奖赏了他忠诚的臣民。


词源:

From Middle English *munificentliche, from Old French *munificenchement, from Latin munificentia, from munificus "generous, bountiful," from munus "gift, duty" + facere "to make, do."


用法说明:

munificently is an adverb that describes the manner in which something is done. It emphasizes the generosity and abundance of an action, particularly in giving or bestowing something.  It is often used to describe acts of charity or generosity on a grand scale.  The word carries a strong positive connotation.
