coauthoring:/koʊˈɔːθəriŋ/| v.| 合著，共同创作 | The act of two or more people writing something together.  |例句：They are coauthoring a book on artificial intelligence.  他们正在合著一本关于人工智能的书。|近义词：collaborating, joint authorship |反义词：sole authorship


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They coauthor papers regularly. = 他们定期合著论文。
We are coauthoring a research article. = 我们正在合著一篇研究论文。

一般过去时:
They coauthored a groundbreaking study last year. = 他们去年合著了一项开创性的研究。
We coauthored a book on history. = 我们合著了一本历史书。

现在完成时:
They have coauthored several books together. = 他们一起合著了几本书。
We have coauthored numerous articles on this topic. = 我们在这个主题上合著了许多文章。

过去完成时:
They had coauthored two papers before they met. = 在他们见面之前，他们已经合著了两篇论文。
Before the conference, we had coauthored a presentation. = 在会议之前，我们已经合著了一个演示文稿。

将来时:
They will coauthor another article next month. = 他们下个月将合著另一篇文章。
We will be coauthoring a chapter in the upcoming textbook. = 我们将在即将出版的教科书中合著一章。

虚拟语气:
If they had more time, they would coauthor a novel. = 如果他们有更多时间，他们会合著一部小说。
I wish I could coauthor a book with my favorite author. = 我希望我能和我最喜欢的作者合著一本书。

被动语态:
The article was coauthored by three leading experts. = 这篇文章由三位主要专家合著。
The research paper is being coauthored by a team of scientists. = 这篇研究论文正在由一个科学家团队合著。

疑问句:

否定句:
They did not coauthor that article. = 他们没有合著那篇文章。
We are not coauthoring anything at the moment. = 我们目前没有合著任何东西。
