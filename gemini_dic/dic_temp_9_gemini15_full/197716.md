shift:/ʃɪft/| n. v.|转移；轮班；转变；移动|The act of moving something from one place to another, or a change in position, direction, or condition.|The factory workers work in shifts.  工厂工人轮班工作。|The wind shifted direction. 风向变了。|近义词：move, change, alteration, transfer|反义词：stay, remain, maintain|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He shifts the boxes every day. 他每天都搬动箱子。
The wind shifts direction frequently. 风向经常改变。


一般过去时:
She shifted her weight from one foot to the other. 她把体重从一只脚移到另一只脚。
The company shifted its focus to new markets. 公司将重点转向了新的市场。


现在完成时:
They have shifted their strategy several times. 他们已经几次改变了策略。
The government has shifted its policy on immigration. 政府已经改变了其移民政策。


过去完成时:
He had shifted the blame onto his colleague. 他已经把责任推到了同事身上。
The company had shifted its production to a new factory. 公司已将其生产转移到新的工厂。


将来时:
They will shift the furniture tomorrow. 他们明天将移动家具。
The company will shift its headquarters to a new city. 公司将把总部迁往新的城市。


虚拟语气:
If I shifted my priorities, I could finish the project. 如果我调整我的优先级，我就能完成这个项目。
I wish I had shifted my focus earlier. 我希望我早点把注意力转移到别的地方。


被动语态:
The blame was shifted onto the junior employee. 责任被推到了初级员工身上。
The focus has been shifted to sustainable development. 重点已经转向了可持续发展。


疑问句:
Did you shift the gears? 你换挡了吗？
When will the company shift its operations? 公司什么时候会转移业务？


否定句:
He didn't shift his position on the issue. 他在这个问题上没有改变立场。
The company hasn't shifted its marketing strategy. 公司没有改变其营销策略。


PHRASE->
night shift 夜班
day shift 白班
shift gears 换挡
power shift 权力转移
a paradigm shift 范式转换

词源:
Old English *sciftan, from Proto-Germanic *skipjan (source also of Old High German *sciften, Old Norse *skipta, Dutch schiften, German schiften), from Proto-Indo-European *s(k)eip- (“to cut, divide, separate”).  The meaning evolved from “divide, share” to “move, change.”
