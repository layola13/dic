rought:/rɔːt/ | adj. |粗糙的，粗略的，未经加工的 |Rough surfaces are difficult to paint. 粗糙的表面很难粉刷。 The carpenter made a rough sketch of the design. 木匠画了一个粗略的设计草图。 |近义词：uneven, bumpy, coarse, crude |反义词：smooth, polished, refined


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The road is rough. = 这条路很粗糙。
The texture of the fabric feels rough. = 这块布料的质地感觉粗糙。

一般过去时:
The journey was rough. = 这趟旅程很艰辛。
He had a rough childhood. = 他有一个坎坷的童年。

现在完成时:
The weather has been rough lately. = 最近天气一直很恶劣。
She has had a rough time at work. = 她最近工作很辛苦。

过去完成时:
The terrain had been rough before the road was built. = 在修路之前，地形很崎岖。
He had already had a rough day when the accident happened. = 事故发生时，他已经度过了艰难的一天。

将来时:
The seas will be rough tomorrow. = 明天大海将会波涛汹涌。
The next few weeks will be rough for the company. = 未来几周对公司来说将会很艰难。

虚拟语气:
If the terrain were rougher, we wouldn't have been able to pass. = 如果地形更崎岖，我们就无法通过了。
I wish the situation wasn't so rough. = 我希望情况不要这么糟糕。

被动语态:
The surface was roughed up by the wind and rain. = 表面被风和雨刮粗糙了。
The edges were roughed up to improve grip. = 边缘被磨粗糙以提高抓地力。

疑问句:
Is the surface rough? = 表面粗糙吗？
Is the ride going to be rough? = 这趟旅程会颠簸吗？

否定句:
The surface is not rough. = 表面不粗糙。
The journey wasn't rough at all. = 这趟旅程一点也不颠簸。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "rough" originates from Old English *ruhg, meaning "rough, shaggy."  It's related to other Germanic words with similar meanings.
