down-and-out: /ˌdaʊn ænd aʊt/ | adj. | 穷困潦倒的，失意的 |  形容处于贫困和绝望状态的人，通常指经济上和精神上都处于低谷。它强调的是一种全面的困境，不仅仅是贫穷，也包括缺乏希望和自尊。 | Example: He was down-and-out after losing his job and his apartment. 他失业又没了公寓，变得穷困潦倒。 | 近义词: impoverished, destitute, ruined, dejected, despairing | 反义词: affluent, prosperous, successful, fortunate, happy |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is down-and-out and needs help. = 他穷困潦倒，需要帮助。
They are down-and-out, living on the streets. = 他们穷困潦倒，流落街头。


一般过去时:
He was down-and-out after the business failed. = 他生意失败后变得穷困潦倒。
She felt down-and-out after the breakup. = 分手后她感到失意和沮丧。


现在完成时:
He has been down-and-out for years. = 他穷困潦倒多年了。
She has felt down-and-out since losing her job. = 自从失业以来，她一直感到很沮丧。


过去完成时:
He had been down-and-out before he met his benefactor. = 在他遇到他的恩人之前，他一直穷困潦倒。
She had felt down-and-out for months before seeking help. = 在寻求帮助之前，她已经感到沮丧好几个月了。


将来时:
He will be down-and-out if he loses this job. = 如果他丢了这份工作，他就会穷困潦倒。
She fears she will be down-and-out without a steady income. = 她担心如果没有稳定的收入，她会穷困潦倒。


虚拟语气:
If he had been more careful, he wouldn't be down-and-out now. = 如果他更小心点，他现在就不会穷困潦倒了。
If she had received support, she wouldn't have felt so down-and-out. = 如果她得到支持，她就不会感到那么沮丧了。


被动语态: (This adjective is rarely used in the passive voice.)  This phrase is not typically used passively.


疑问句:
Is he down-and-out? = 他穷困潦倒吗？
Are they feeling down-and-out lately? = 他们最近感到沮丧吗？


否定句:
He is not down-and-out; he's just having a rough patch. = 他并没有穷困潦倒；他只是经历了一段艰难时期。
She is not down-and-out; she's resilient and will overcome this. = 她并没有失意；她很有韧性，会克服这个困难的。
