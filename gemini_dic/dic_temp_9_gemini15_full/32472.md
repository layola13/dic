chinatowns:/ˈtʃaɪnəˌtaʊnz/| n. |唐人街|A Chinatown is a neighborhood, typically located in a city in a non-Chinese-speaking country, with a high concentration of Chinese residents and businesses.  It often features Chinese restaurants, shops, cultural centers, and other establishments reflecting Chinese culture and traditions.  The term can also refer to similar ethnic enclaves of Chinese people in other countries.|例句：San Francisco's Chinatown is one of the oldest and largest in North America. 旧金山的唐人街是北美最古老和最大的唐人街之一。|例句：Many Chinatowns around the world offer a vibrant and unique cultural experience. 世界各地的许多唐人街都提供充满活力和独特的文化体验。|近义词：Chinese quarter, ethnic enclave|反义词：无
