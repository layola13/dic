inspector:/ɪnˈspɛktər/| n.|检查员，督察|A person who officially examines something to check its quality, condition, or that it is being done correctly.|The building inspector found several code violations. 建筑检查员发现了几项违规行为。|examiner, supervisor, investigator| saboteur, destroyer |名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The inspector inspects the building regularly. 检查员定期检查建筑物。
He inspects the goods before they are shipped. 他在货物装运前检查货物。


一般过去时:
The inspector inspected the factory last week. 检查员上周检查了工厂。
She inspected the documents carefully. 她仔细检查了文件。


现在完成时:
The inspector has inspected all the rooms. 检查员已经检查了所有房间。
They have inspected the evidence thoroughly. 他们已经彻底检查了证据。


过去完成时:
The inspector had inspected the site before the accident. 事故发生前，检查员已经检查过现场。
He had inspected the car, and found nothing wrong. 他检查了汽车，没有发现任何问题。


将来时:
The inspector will inspect the bridge tomorrow. 检查员明天将检查桥梁。
She will inspect the work in progress next week. 她下周将检查正在进行的工作。


虚拟语气:
If the inspector were here, he would see the problem. 如果检查员在这里，他会看到这个问题。
I wish the inspector had inspected the machine earlier. 我希望检查员早点检查机器。


被动语态:
The building was inspected by the inspector. 建筑物由检查员检查。
The documents are being inspected by the authorities. 文件正在由当局检查。


疑问句:
Has the inspector inspected the factory yet? 检查员已经检查过工厂了吗？
Will the inspector inspect the new equipment? 检查员会检查新设备吗？


否定句:
The inspector did not inspect the warehouse. 检查员没有检查仓库。
She didn't find anything wrong when she inspected it. 她检查时没有发现任何问题。

词源:

The word "inspector" comes from the Latin word "inspicere," meaning "to look into" or "to examine."  The suffix "-or" denotes an agent or doer of the action.  Therefore, an inspector is someone who performs the action of inspection.
