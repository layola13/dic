funnelling: /ˈfʌnəlɪŋ/ | v. | 漏斗状输送；引导；集中；整合 | The act of guiding or channeling something into a narrower course or system.  It often implies a process of restriction, concentration, or streamlining. |  例句：The company is funnelling its resources into its new product line.  公司正在将其资源投入新的产品线。

例句：The government is funnelling money into education. 政府正在向教育投入资金。


近义词：channeling, directing, guiding, concentrating, streamlining

反义词：dispersing, scattering, diffusing


词源:  From "funnel," ultimately from Old French *funell, diminutive of *fune, from Vulgar Latin *fūnis, "rope," referring to the rope-like shape of a funnel.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They funnel resources into research. = 他们将资源投入研究。
The system funnels customer data to the marketing department. = 该系统将客户数据传输到市场部门。

一般过去时:
The government funnelled aid to the disaster-stricken area. = 政府向灾区输送了援助。
She funnelled her energy into finishing the project. = 她集中精力完成项目。

现在完成时:
They have funnelled millions of dollars into the campaign. = 他们已经向该活动投入了数百万美元。
The company has funnelled its profits back into the business. = 公司已将其利润重新投入到业务中。

过去完成时:
They had funnelled all the information into a single report. = 他们已将所有信息整合到一份报告中。
The organization had funnelled donations to various charities. = 该组织已将捐款捐赠给各个慈善机构。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
