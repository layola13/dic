dogmas:/ˈdɒɡməz/ | n. |教条；信条；教义 |  A dogma is a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true.  Dogmas are often associated with religious beliefs, but can also apply to any system of thought or belief.  They are typically accepted without question or critical examination. |Example:  The church's dogmas were strictly enforced.  这个教会的教条被严格执行。

The scientific community rejects the dogma of creationism. 科学界拒绝创造论的教条。|近义词：doctrine, tenet, creed, belief |反义词：heresy, skepticism, doubt |名词
