scupper:/ˈskʌpər/ | n., vt. | 泄水管；破坏；使失败 |  A scupper is a small opening in the side of a ship's deck to allow water to drain away. As a verb, it means to sink or destroy something, or to ruin a plan or scheme. |例句：The leak in the scupper caused the boat to sink. 泄水管的漏洞导致船沉没。 The storm scuppered our plans for a picnic. 暴风雨破坏了我们野餐的计划。 |近义词：(n.) drain, outlet; (vt.) sink, ruin, wreck, thwart |反义词：(vt.) save, succeed, rescue
