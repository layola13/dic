conjuring:/ˈkɒndʒərɪŋ/ /ˈkɑːndʒərɪŋ/| n. v.|变戏法；魔法；幻想|The act of performing magic tricks or illusions; the practice of magic; the evocation of something (especially something supernatural) seemingly from nothing.|例句：The magician's conjuring act amazed the audience. 魔术师的变戏法让观众惊叹不已。  The conjuring of spirits is a dangerous practice. 招魂是一种危险的行为。|近义词：magic, illusion, sorcery, incantation|反义词：reality, fact

一般现在时:
He conjures rabbits from hats. = 他从帽子里变出兔子。
The magician conjures illusions on stage. = 魔术师在舞台上变幻幻象。

一般过去时:
She conjured a delicious meal from simple ingredients. = 她用简单的食材变出了一顿美味佳肴。
The wizard conjured a protective shield around the castle. = 巫师在城堡周围变出了一个保护罩。

现在完成时:
He has conjured up a plan to solve the problem. = 他已经想出了一个解决问题的方案。
The artist has conjured a breathtaking landscape on canvas. = 艺术家在画布上描绘了一幅令人叹为观止的风景。

过去完成时:

The witch had conjured a powerful storm to hinder their journey. = 女巫已经变出一场强大的风暴来阻碍他们的旅程。

将来时:

The chef will conjure a feast for the royal wedding. = 厨师将为皇室婚礼准备盛宴。

虚拟语气:
If I could conjure a wish, I would wish for world peace. = 如果我能许愿，我会许愿世界和平。
I wish I could conjure up the courage to speak my mind. = 我真希望我能鼓起勇气说出我的想法。

疑问句:
