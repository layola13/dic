last-minute: /ˌlɑːstˈmɪnɪt/ | adj. | 最后一刻的；临时的 |  Done or happening at the very last possible moment.  Something done at the last minute is done very close to the deadline or at the very end of a period of time. |

例句：

He made a last-minute decision to go to the party. 他临时决定去参加派对。

We booked our flights last minute and got a great deal. 我们在最后一刻预订了航班，并获得了很大的优惠。


近义词：late, eleventh-hour,  at the last moment


反义词：early, timely, well in advance


词源:

The term "last-minute" is a straightforward compound of "last" and "minute."  "Last" refers to the final or concluding instance, while "minute" in this context denotes a short period of time, often referring to the final moments before a deadline.  The combination emphasizes the immediacy and brevity of the timeframe.


用法说明:

last-minute通常用作形容词，修饰名词，例如：last-minute changes (最后一刻的改变), last-minute booking (最后一刻的预订), last-minute rush (最后一刻的匆忙).  它也可以用在名词短语中，例如 a last-minute decision (一个临时的决定).  它通常带有紧迫感和仓促感。
