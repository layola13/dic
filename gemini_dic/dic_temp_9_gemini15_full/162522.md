preagitation: /ˌpriːˌæɡɪˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 预先搅拌；预先煽动 | The act of agitating something beforehand, often to enhance a process or reaction.  It can refer to the physical mixing of substances before a main process, or to the stirring up of emotions or opinions before a main event. | 例句：The preagitation of the chemicals ensured a more complete reaction. 预先搅拌化学物质确保了更完全的反应。 The preagitation of the crowd before the rally was evident in their heightened energy.  集会前的民众预先煽动在他们增强的能量中显而易见。 | 近义词：premixing, preliminary stirring, incitement | 反义词：passivity, quiescence
