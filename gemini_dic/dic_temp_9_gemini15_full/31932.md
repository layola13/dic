cheirotherium:/kaɪroʊˈθɪəriəm/| n. |手兽，掌形兽| An extinct genus of large, quadrupedal reptiles, known from their characteristic handprints and footprints found in Triassic rocks.  These footprints are remarkably well-preserved and show clear evidence of five fingers with claws, suggesting a reptilian origin.|  The discovery of *Cheirotherium* footprints provided crucial early evidence for the existence of large reptiles during the Triassic period. |近义词： (no direct synonyms, other Triassic reptile genera could be mentioned depending on context)|反义词： (no direct antonyms)|名词
