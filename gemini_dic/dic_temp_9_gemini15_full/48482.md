deep-sixing: /ˌdiːpˈsɪksɪŋ/ | v. | 故意抛弃；暗中处理；销毁 | To deliberately discard or get rid of something, often secretly or unceremoniously.  It often implies getting rid of something unwanted or problematic. |  The company deep-sixed the project after realizing it was unprofitable. 公司在意识到该项目无利可图后将其搁置。


The evidence was deep-sixed to protect the guilty party.  证据被销毁以保护罪犯。


近义词：discard, ditch, scrap, jettison, suppress, eliminate | 反义词：preserve, retain, save, keep
