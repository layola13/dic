heliacally:/ˌhiːliˈækəli/| adv.|  日出时，当太阳升起时|Adverb describing something happening at or around the time of the heliacal rising or setting of a star or planet.  This means the first or last visibility of a celestial body just before sunrise or after sunset, respectively, as it emerges from or disappears into the sun's glare.|The star appeared heliacally just before sunrise.|近义词：at dawn, at sunrise|反义词：at night
