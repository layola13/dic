fomented:/foʊˈmɛntɪd/| v. |煽动；激起；酿成 |To incite or stir up (an undesirable feeling or situation).  It implies a gradual process of instigation, often leading to unrest or violence. |例句：The rumors fomented unrest among the soldiers. 谣言在士兵中煽动了骚乱。

例句：He fomented a rebellion against the king. 他煽动了一场反对国王的叛乱。


近义词：incite, instigate, stir up, provoke, agitate |反义词：quell, suppress, calm, pacify |词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The media often foments social division. = 媒体经常煽动社会分裂。
He constantly foments trouble at work. = 他不断在工作中制造麻烦。


一般过去时:
The politician fomented dissent among the people. = 这位政客煽动了人民之间的不满。
Their actions fomented a crisis in the company. = 他们的行为在公司引发了危机。


现在完成时:
They have fomented hatred between the two groups for years. = 他们多年来一直在两组人之间煽动仇恨。
The government has fomented a climate of fear. = 政府制造了一种恐惧的气氛。


过去完成时:
The rebels had fomented a coup before the king's arrival. = 在国王到来之前，叛军已经策划了一场政变。
She had fomented resentment for years before finally speaking out. = 在最终说出来之前，她已经积怨多年了。


将来时:
They will foment a revolution if their demands are not met. = 如果他们的要求得不到满足，他们将发动革命。
He will continue to foment discord among his colleagues. = 他将继续在他同事之间煽动不和。


虚拟语气:
If he had not fomented rebellion, many lives would have been saved. = 如果他没有煽动叛乱，许多人的生命就会得到拯救。
I wish they hadn't fomented such division. = 我真希望他们没有煽动这种分裂。


被动语态:
Discontent was fomented by the rising prices. = 不满情绪是被不断上涨的价格所煽动的。
Rebellion was fomented by the oppressive regime. = 叛乱是被压迫的政权所煽动的。


疑问句:
Who fomented this conflict? = 谁煽动了这场冲突？
What actions fomented the riot? = 什么行为煽动了这场暴乱？


否定句:
The government did not foment the protests. = 政府没有煽动抗议活动。
He refused to foment trouble among his friends. = 他拒绝在他朋友中制造麻烦。


PHRASE->
foment trouble = 制造麻烦
foment discord = 煽动不和
foment unrest = 煽动骚乱
foment rebellion = 煽动叛乱
foment hatred = 煽动仇恨


词源:
源自古法语 *fomenter，意为“施肥，滋养”，最终源自拉丁语 *fomentare，意为“施温热，熏蒸”。  其意义演变与“滋养”某种负面情绪或情况有关，从而发展出“煽动”的含义。
