decollectivizing: /ˌdiːkəˈlɛktɪvaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 去集体化；取消集体所有制 | The process of changing from a system of collective ownership or organization to one of individual ownership or organization.  This often involves the privatization of state-owned assets and the dismantling of collective farms or industries. | 例句：The government initiated a policy of decollectivizing agriculture, leading to the emergence of private farms. = 政府启动了农业去集体化的政策，导致了私人农场出现。

The ongoing decollectivizing of the housing market has increased homeownership rates. = 住房市场持续的去集体化提高了房屋拥有率。| 近义词：privatize, decentralize | 反义词：collectivize, socialize
