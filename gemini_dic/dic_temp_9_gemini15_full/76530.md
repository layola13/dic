词源:

The word "galimatias" entered English from French.  The French word itself is derived from the Italian word "galimattia."  The ultimate origin is uncertain, but one theory traces it to a medieval Italian word possibly referring to a confused jumble of sounds or words.  Another theory suggests a connection to Arabic words referring to confused speech.


用法说明:

Galimatias is used to describe speech or writing that is so confused, unclear, or nonsensical as to be unintelligible. It implies a lack of coherence and meaning, often suggesting an excessive use of jargon or technical terms inappropriately.  It carries a negative connotation, indicating that the communication is frustrating and unproductive.


例句:

His explanation was pure galimatias; I couldn't understand a word.  他的解释完全是胡言乱语，我一个字都听不懂。

The contract was filled with legal galimatias that made it difficult to understand the terms.  合同充满了难以理解的法律术语，使得理解条款变得困难。
