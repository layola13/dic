shuck:/ʃʌk/ | vt., n. | 去壳；脱粒；欺骗；骗取；廉价货 | To remove the shell, husk, or covering from (something, especially an ear of corn or an oyster).  Also, to deceive or cheat someone. As a noun, it refers to the outer covering of something, or something of low quality or value. | 例句：He shucked the corn before boiling it. 他在煮玉米之前剥去了玉米皮。  She felt shucked out of her money by the dishonest salesman. 她觉得被那个不诚实的推销员骗走了钱。| 近义词：shell, husk, peel, swindle, cheat | 反义词：preserve, protect
