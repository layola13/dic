humanist:/ˈhjuːmənɪst/| n. adj. |人道主义者；人文主义者；人本主义者 |A humanist is a person who emphasizes human values and concerns.  A humanist approach to something prioritizes human experience and reason. |例句：Many Renaissance artists were humanists.  许多文艺复兴时期的艺术家都是人文主义者。|例句：The humanist tradition values critical thinking and intellectual freedom. 人文主义传统重视批判性思维和思想自由。|近义词：humanitarian, rationalist |反义词：dogmatist, authoritarian

词源:

The word "humanist" comes from the Latin word "humanus," meaning "human," "humane," or "cultivated."  The "-ist" suffix denotes a person who follows a particular doctrine or system of thought.  The term developed during the Renaissance, referring to scholars who emphasized classical studies and human potential over religious dogma.
