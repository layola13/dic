legitimator: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmeɪtər/ | n. | 使合法者；合法化者 | One that legitimizes or makes legitimate.  Someone or something that gives something authority or validity.  | The government's new policies aim to be a legitimator of the current social order.  政府的新政策旨在使现有的社会秩序合法化。|近义词：authorizer, validator, ratifier | 反义词：invalidator, disprover


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new law acts as a legitimator of their actions. = 这项新法律使他们的行为合法化。
He is a legitimator of the company's questionable practices. = 他使公司有问题的做法合法化。


一般过去时:
The court decision legitimized their claim to the property. = 法院的判决使他们对该财产的主张合法化。
The king's signature acted as a legitimator of the document. = 国王的签名使该文件合法化。


现在完成时:
The recent election has legitimized the new government's power. = 最近的选举使新政府的权力合法化。
This evidence has legitimized the scientist's theory. = 这一证据使这位科学家的理论合法化。


过去完成时:
The prior agreement had legitimized their involvement in the project. = 先前的协议使他们参与该项目合法化。
His previous work had legitimized his current position. = 他以前的工作使他目前的职位合法化。


将来时:
The upcoming legislation will legitimize same-sex marriage. = 即将来临的立法将使同性婚姻合法化。
This discovery will legitimize their research findings. = 这一发现将使他们的研究结果合法化。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, it would legitimize their claims. = 如果证据更充分，它将使他们的主张合法化。
I wish the process would legitimize my efforts more quickly. = 我希望这个过程能更快地使我的努力合法化。


被动语态:
Their actions were legitimized by a recent Supreme Court ruling. = 他们的行为得到了最近最高法院裁决的合法化。
The treaty was legitimized by international consensus. = 这项条约得到了国际共识的合法化。


疑问句:
What legitimizes this controversial decision? = 什么使这个有争议的决定合法化？
What will legitimize their actions in the eyes of the public? = 在公众眼中，什么会使他们的行为合法化？


否定句:
The lack of evidence did not legitimize their accusations. = 缺乏证据并没有使他们的指控合法化。
His explanation did not legitimize his behavior. = 他的解释并没有使他的行为合法化。
