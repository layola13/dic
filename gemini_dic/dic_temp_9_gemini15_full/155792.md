phonophobia:/ˌfoʊnoʊˈfoʊbiə/| n. |恐音症，声音恐惧症 |An extreme or irrational fear of sounds or noises.  This phobia can manifest in various ways, from mild anxiety to panic attacks triggered by specific sounds or general auditory stimuli.  The severity varies greatly among individuals. |Example: Her phonophobia made everyday life extremely challenging, as even common sounds like traffic or conversations could trigger intense anxiety.  |近义词：acousticophobia, sonophobia |反义词：None (phobias don't typically have direct antonyms)
