embarrassment:/ɪmˈbærəsmənt/ | n. |窘迫；尴尬；难堪 | The feeling of being ashamed, nervous, or uncomfortable because of something you have done or because of something that has happened to you.  It often involves a sense of exposure or vulnerability. |例句：Her embarrassment was evident as she blushed and stammered.  她脸红结巴，显然很尴尬。

例句：The public speaking caused him considerable embarrassment.  公开演讲让他相当尴尬。


近义词：shame, humiliation, awkwardness, discomfiture | 反义词：pride, composure, self-assurance | 词性：名词
