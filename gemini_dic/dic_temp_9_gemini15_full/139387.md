observance:/əbˈzɜːrvəns/ | n. |遵守；庆祝；纪念；观察 | The act of complying with a law, rule, custom, or practice; the celebration of a religious or other special day; the act of carefully watching or noticing something. | 例句：The strict observance of the rules is essential. 严格遵守规章制度至关重要。


The observance of Christmas varies across cultures.  圣诞节的庆祝方式因文化而异。

近义词：compliance, celebration, commemoration, monitoring, keeping

反义词：violation, disregard, neglect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He observes the speed limit. = 他遵守限速。
She observes the traditions of her family. = 她遵守家族传统。


一般过去时:
They observed the holiday with a feast. = 他们用盛宴庆祝了节日。
He observed the birds in the trees. = 他观察树上的鸟儿。


现在完成时:
We have observed a significant increase in sales. = 我们观察到销售额显著增长。
She has observed the rules all her life. = 她一生都遵守规则。


过去完成时:
They had observed the warning signs before the accident. = 事故发生前他们已经注意到了警告标志。
He had observed a pattern in the data. = 他已经观察到数据中的模式。


将来时:
We will observe the anniversary next week. = 我们下周将纪念周年纪念日。
They will observe the election results closely. = 他们将密切关注选举结果。


虚拟语气:
If they observed the rules, they wouldn't be in trouble. = 如果他们遵守规则，就不会惹上麻烦。
I wish they observed the rules more carefully. = 我希望他们更仔细地遵守规则。


被动语态:
The rules are observed strictly in this company. = 公司严格遵守这些规定。
The anniversary was observed with a grand ceremony. = 周年纪念日以盛大的仪式庆祝。


疑问句:
Do you observe any changes? = 你观察到任何变化吗？
Did they observe the silence? = 他们遵守沉默了吗？


否定句:
He did not observe the traffic light. = 他没有遵守交通信号灯。
They did not observe the deadline. = 他们没有遵守最后期限。
