license:/ˈlaɪs(ə)ns/| n. v.|许可证；执照；授权；许可|A license is an official document that gives you permission to do something.  It can refer to a driver's license, a marriage license, a business license, etc.  As a verb, it means to grant permission.|例句：I need to get a driver's license. 我需要考取驾照。

例句：The company licensed its technology to other firms. 这家公司将其技术许可给其他公司。|近义词：permit, authorization, permit, warrant|反义词：prohibition, ban, restriction|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He licenses his software to many companies. 他把他的软件授权给许多公司。
She holds a valid driver's license. 她持有有效的驾照。


一般过去时:
They licensed the song for use in the film. 他们许可这首歌用于电影中。
He received his pilot's license last year. 他去年获得了飞行员执照。


现在完成时:
The university has licensed the technology to several businesses.  大学已将这项技术许可给几家企业。
She has held a teaching license for over ten years. 她拥有超过十年的教师资格证。


过去完成时:
They had already licensed the music before the concert.  他们在音乐会之前已经取得了音乐版权。
Before the trip, he had ensured he had a valid driving license. 出行前，他已确保自己持有有效的驾照。


将来时:
We will license the new software next year. 我们明年将授权新的软件。
She will apply for her professional license next month. 她下个月将申请专业执照。


虚拟语气:
If I had a fishing license, I could go fishing. 如果我有捕鱼许可证，我就可以去钓鱼。
I wish I had licensed the software earlier. 我真希望我早点授权这个软件。


被动语态:
The software is licensed to users worldwide. 该软件已授权给全球用户。
The technology was licensed to a major corporation. 这项技术被授权给一家大型公司。


疑问句:
Do you have a driving license? 你有驾照吗？
Is this software legally licensed? 这个软件有合法授权吗？


否定句:
He doesn't have a license to practice medicine. 他没有行医执照。
The company did not license the technology to its competitors.  该公司没有将其技术授权给竞争对手。
