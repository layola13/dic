jolt's:/dʒoʊlt/| n.|猛推；震动；电击|A sudden, violent, or jarring movement or impact.|The earthquake gave the city a severe jolt. 地震使该城市遭受了剧烈的震动。|近义词：shock, shake, bump|反义词：smoothness, calmness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The machine jolts violently. = 机器剧烈震动。
This news jolts me. = 这个消息让我震惊。


一般过去时:
The car jolted to a stop. = 车子猛地停了下来。
The sudden noise jolted him awake. = 突然的噪音把他惊醒了。


现在完成时:
The news has jolted the market. = 这则消息震动了市场。
The experience has jolted her confidence. = 这段经历让她信心受损。


过去完成时:
The news had jolted him before he heard the details. = 在他听到细节之前，这个消息已经让他震惊了。
She had jolted herself awake with a sudden noise. = 她被一声巨响惊醒了。


将来时:
The announcement will jolt the industry. = 这则公告将震动整个行业。
The impact of the news will jolt the financial markets. = 这则消息的影响将震动金融市场。


虚拟语气:
If the news were to jolt the market, the stock prices would drop. = 如果消息震动市场，股价就会下跌。
If I were to jolt him, I might hurt him. = 如果我猛推他，可能会伤到他。


被动语态:
The car was jolted off the road. = 车子被震出路面。
He was jolted awake by the loud thunder. = 他被巨大的雷声惊醒。


疑问句:
Was the car jolted by the bump? = 车子是被颠簸震的吗？
Did the news jolt the investors? = 这个消息让投资者震惊了吗？


否定句:
The news did not jolt him. = 这个消息并没有让他震惊。
The earthquake didn't jolt the building. = 地震没有震动这座建筑物。


PHRASE->

give someone a jolt = 给某人一个冲击
a jolt to the system = 对系统的冲击
a jolt of caffeine = 一阵咖啡因的刺激
