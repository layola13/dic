kicking:/ˈkɪkɪŋ/| v.|踢，猛击，抨击|The act of kicking involves propelling something with the foot. It can be a forceful action or a gentle one, depending on the context.  It can also be used metaphorically to describe strong criticism or opposition.|例句：He kicked the ball across the field. 他把球踢过了球场。


例句：The team is kicking off their new season with a challenging game.  球队在新赛季以一场充满挑战的比赛开始。


近义词：striking, hitting, punting, booting


反义词：pulling, receiving


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He kicks the ball every day. = 他每天都踢球。
She kicks her feet against the wall. = 她用脚踢墙。


一般过去时:
He kicked the ball into the goal. = 他把球踢进了球门。
They kicked the door down. = 他们踢破了门。


现在完成时:
I have kicked that can down the street three times. = 我已经把那个罐子踢到街对面三次了。
She has kicked the habit of smoking. = 她已经戒掉了抽烟的习惯。


过去完成时:
He had kicked the ball before the whistle blew. = 在哨子吹响之前他已经踢了球。
She had kicked off her shoes before entering the house. = 她在进屋之前已经脱掉了鞋子。


将来时:
He will kick the ball to his teammate. = 他将把球踢给他的队友。
She will kick the door open tomorrow. = 她明天会把门踢开。


虚拟语气:
If I kicked that ball, it would probably go out of bounds. = 如果我踢那个球，它可能会出界。
I wish I hadn't kicked that dog. = 我真希望我没有踢那条狗。


被动语态:
The ball was kicked far down the field. = 球被踢到了很远的球场。
The door was kicked in by the robbers. = 门被强盗踢开了。


疑问句:
Did he kick the ball? = 他踢球了吗？
Why did you kick the dog? = 你为什么要踢那条狗？


否定句:
He didn't kick the ball hard enough. = 他踢球不够用力。
She doesn't kick the ball very often. = 她不经常踢球。


PHRASE->
kicking and screaming:  又哭又闹地
kicking the bucket: 死去
kicking off: 开始
kicking oneself: 后悔
kicking up a fuss: 大吵大闹
