extensional:/ɪkˈstɛnʃənəl/ | adj. | 外延的；外延性的 | Relating to or denoting the extension of a concept, that is, the set of all things to which the concept applies.  It contrasts with intensional, which focuses on the meaning or definition of a concept. | Example:  The extensional meaning of "dog" refers to all dogs in the world.  The intensional meaning defines what characteristics make something a dog. |近义词:denotative |反义词:intensional |

词源:
The word "extensional" is derived from the word "extension," which comes from the Latin word "extensio," meaning "stretching out" or "extension." The "-al" suffix indicates an adjectival form.  The term gained prominence in logic and philosophy in relation to the study of concepts and their application to the real world.
