contractibility:/ˌkɒn træk təˈbɪləti/| /ˌkɑːnˈtræktəˈbɪləti/| n. |可收缩性，收缩力|The inherent ability of a muscle or other tissue to shorten and thicken, thus producing movement.  This property is essential for many biological functions, including locomotion, digestion, and circulation.  |例句：The contractibility of the heart muscle is crucial for maintaining blood flow. 心肌的收缩力对于维持血流至关重要。

例句：The experiment measured the contractibility of the isolated muscle fibers. 实验测量了分离的肌纤维的收缩力。
