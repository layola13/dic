rapporteurs:/ˌræpɔːrˈtɜːrz/| n. |报告员，记录员|A rapporteur is a person who reports on the proceedings of a meeting, conference, or other gathering.  They typically summarize discussions, decisions, and recommendations. |The rapporteur summarized the key findings of the committee's investigation.  报告员总结了委员会调查的主要发现。|reporter, summarizer, recorder|  (无明确反义词,可考虑non-reporter)|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rapporteur summarizes the meeting. = 报告员总结会议。
He reports on the progress of the project. = 他汇报项目的进展。


一般过去时:
The rapporteur summarized yesterday's meeting. = 报告员总结了昨天的会议。
She reported on the findings of the study. = 她汇报了研究结果。


现在完成时:
The rapporteur has summarized all the key points. = 报告员已经总结了所有要点。
He has reported the incident to the authorities. = 他已将事件报告给当局。


过去完成时:
The rapporteur had already summarized the discussion before the meeting ended. = 在会议结束之前，报告员已经总结了讨论内容。
She had reported the problem to her supervisor earlier that day. = 她当天早些时候已经向她的主管报告了这个问题。


将来时:
The rapporteur will summarize the findings tomorrow. = 报告员明天将总结调查结果。
He will report to the board next week. = 他下周将向董事会汇报。


虚拟语气:
If the rapporteur were here, he would summarize the discussion. = 如果报告员在这里，他会总结讨论内容。
I wish the rapporteur had reported the incident sooner. = 我希望报告员早点报告这件事。


被动语态:
The meeting was summarized by the rapporteur. = 会议由报告员进行了总结。
The incident was reported to the police. = 事件已向警方报告。


疑问句:
Who is the rapporteur for this meeting? = 这次会议的报告员是谁？
Has the rapporteur submitted the report yet? = 报告员是否已提交报告？


否定句:
The rapporteur did not summarize the meeting effectively. = 报告员没有有效地总结会议。
He did not report the findings accurately. = 他没有准确地报告调查结果。
