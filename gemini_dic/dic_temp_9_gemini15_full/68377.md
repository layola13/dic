falloff:/ˈfɔːlɔːf/| n.|下降；衰落；减少；(悬崖等的)陡坡|a decline or decrease in the amount, number, or rate of something.|The falloff in sales was unexpected. 销售额下降是意料之外的。|The cliff had a dangerous falloff.悬崖有一个危险的陡坡。|decline, decrease, drop, reduction|increase, rise, growth|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The falloff in productivity is concerning.  生产率下降令人担忧。
There is a significant falloff in the number of applicants.申请人数大幅下降。


一般过去时:
A sharp falloff in profits occurred last quarter. 上个季度利润急剧下降。
The falloff in attendance at the concerts was noticeable.音乐会出席人数的下降很明显。


现在完成时:
There has been a steady falloff in the quality of their products. 他们产品的质量一直在下降。
We have seen a considerable falloff in customer satisfaction. 我们看到顾客满意度大幅下降。


过去完成时:
By the end of the year, there had been a dramatic falloff in investment. 到年底，投资已经大幅下降。
Before the new regulations, there had been a gradual falloff in pollution levels. 在新的规章出台之前，污染水平一直在逐渐下降。


将来时:
Experts predict a falloff in tourism next year. 专家预测明年旅游业将下降。
We anticipate a falloff in demand for our services. 我们预计对我们服务的 demand 将会下降。


虚拟语气:
If there were a falloff in the economy, many people would lose their jobs.如果经济下滑，许多人将失业。
I wish there wasn't such a falloff in public support for the arts.我希望公众对艺术的支持不会如此下降。


被动语态:
The falloff in the stock market was widely reported. 股市下跌被广泛报道。

疑问句:

否定句:
There was no significant falloff in the company's performance. 公司业绩没有明显下降。
The project did not experience a falloff in funding. 项目资金没有减少。
