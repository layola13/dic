semi-nomad:/ˈsɛmɪˌnoʊmæd/| adj.|半游牧的，部分游牧的|Partially nomadic;  describing a lifestyle that combines settled periods with seasonal or periodic migration.  It implies a less fully nomadic existence than a true nomad.|例句：Many pastoralist communities are semi-nomadic, moving their herds seasonally to find fresh pasture. 许多牧民是半游牧的，他们会根据季节迁移牧群以寻找新鲜牧场。|例句：The tribe maintains a semi-nomadic lifestyle, spending winters in villages and summers in mountain pastures.  该部落保持着半游牧的生活方式，冬天住在村庄里，夏天住在山区牧场上。|近义词：partially nomadic, seasonally migratory|反义词：settled, sedentary

词源:

semi- :  From Latin *semi-, meaning "half," "partly," or "partially."

nomad: From Greek νομάς (nomás), meaning "one who wanders," "pastoral people."  Ultimately related to νέμω (némō), meaning "to distribute," "to pasture," or "to allot."
