fade:/feɪd/ | v. n. | 褪色；逐渐消失；衰弱 | To gradually lose brightness, strength, or intensity.  | The flowers began to fade. 花开始凋谢。 The sound of the music faded into the distance. 音乐声逐渐消失在远处。 | wither, diminish, weaken | brighten, intensify, strengthen | 动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The color of the shirt fades slowly in the sun.  = 这件衬衫的颜色在阳光下慢慢褪色。
The music fades out at the end of the song. = 歌曲结尾处音乐逐渐淡出。


一般过去时:
The photograph faded over time. = 照片随着时间的推移褪色了。
His anger faded after he calmed down. = 他冷静下来后怒气消退了。


现在完成时:
The paint has faded considerably since it was applied. = 油漆自从涂上以来已经褪色不少了。
His memory of the event has faded. = 他对这件事的记忆已经淡忘了。


过去完成时:
The flowers had faded before I could take a picture. = 我还没来得及拍照，花就谢了。
His enthusiasm had faded by the end of the project. = 到项目结束时，他的热情已经消退了。


将来时:
The sun will fade as the day ends. = 白天结束时太阳将会落下。
His influence will fade over time. = 他的影响力会随着时间的推移而减弱。


虚拟语气:
If the sun were to fade, the earth would be in danger. = 如果太阳消失，地球将面临危险。
I wish my memories of childhood wouldn’t fade. = 我希望我童年时的记忆不会消逝。


被动语态:
The image was faded by the sunlight. = 图像被阳光晒褪色了。
The old photograph had been faded by years of exposure to light. = 这张旧照片由于多年的光照而褪色了。


疑问句:
Does the paint fade easily? = 油漆容易褪色吗？
Is his memory fading? = 他的记忆力衰退了吗？


否定句:
The colors didn't fade despite the strong sunlight. = 尽管阳光强烈，颜色也没有褪色。
His determination didn't fade even after repeated setbacks. = 即使屡遭挫折，他的决心也没有动摇。
