righteous:/ˈraɪtʃəs/ | adj. | 正义的；正直的；虔诚的 |  morally right or justifiable; virtuous.  Characterized by righteousness; upright and morally correct. |例句：He was a righteous man who always stood up for what he believed in. 他是一个正直的人，总是坚持自己的信仰。

例句：Her righteous anger at the injustice was palpable. 她对不公正的义愤填膺是显而易见的。


近义词：upright, virtuous, just, holy, pious | 反义词：wicked, sinful, unjust, immoral, unrighteous


词源:  The word "righteous" originates from the Old English word *rihtwīs*,  a compound of *riht* ("right") and *wīs* ("wise").  It reflects the historical connection between moral correctness and wisdom.  The modern spelling evolved over time through Middle English.


用法说明:

一般现在时:
He is righteous in his dealings. = 他在处理事情上是正直的。
She is always righteous in her judgments. = 她总是以正直的态度进行判断。

一般过去时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

The decision was deemed righteous by the court. = 法院认为该决定是正义的。

疑问句:
Is he truly righteous in his actions? = 他的行为真的正直吗？

否定句:
He is not righteous in his dealings. = 他在处理事情上不正直。
Her actions were not righteous. = 她的行为是不正义的。
