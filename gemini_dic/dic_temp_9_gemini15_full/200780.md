sliced:/slaɪst/| adj. |切片的；薄片的|Cut into slices.  Describes something that has been cut into thin, flat pieces.|The sliced bread was perfect for toast. 切片面包非常适合做吐司。  He served sliced tomatoes with the sandwich. 他用切片西红柿搭配三明治。|近义词：cut, sectioned|反义词：whole, uncut

USAGE->

一般现在时:
The chef slices the vegetables thinly. = 厨师把蔬菜切得很薄。
She slices the cake into eight pieces. = 她把蛋糕切成八块。


一般过去时:
He sliced the lemon for his tea. = 他切了柠檬片泡茶。
They sliced the pizza into six slices. = 他们把披萨切成六片。


现在完成时:
I have sliced all the onions for the soup. = 我已经把所有洋葱都切好准备做汤了。
She has sliced the cucumbers for the salad. = 她已经把黄瓜切好准备做沙拉了。


过去完成时:
He had sliced the bread before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，他已经把面包切好了。
They had sliced the cake before the party started. = 派对开始之前，他们已经把蛋糕切好了。


将来时:
I will slice the tomatoes for the pasta. = 我会把西红柿切好准备做意大利面。

虚拟语气:
If I had a sharper knife, I would slice the meat more easily. = 如果我有一把更锋利的刀，我会更容易切肉。
I wish I had sliced the potatoes thinner. = 我希望我把土豆切得更薄一些。


被动语态:
The bread is sliced thinly. = 面包切得很薄。
The vegetables were sliced before cooking. = 蔬菜在烹饪前被切好了。


疑问句:

否定句:
