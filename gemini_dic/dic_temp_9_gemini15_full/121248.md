miserly:/ˈmaɪzərli/| adj.|吝啬的，守财奴的|Characterized by or indicative of miserliness; stingy.  The miserly old man refused to give even a penny to charity.|近义词：stingy, parsimonious, frugal (in a negative sense), niggardly|反义词：generous, charitable, munificent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is miserly with his money. = 他对钱很吝啬。
She is always miserly in her spending habits. = 她的消费习惯总是很吝啬。


一般过去时:
He was miserly in his youth. = 他年轻时很吝啬。
They were miserly with their praise. = 他们吝于赞美。


现在完成时:
He has been miserly all his life. = 他一辈子都吝啬。
She has always been miserly with compliments. = 她总是吝于赞美。


过去完成时:
He had been miserly even before he became wealthy. = 他甚至在富有之前就已很吝啬。
They had been miserly with resources throughout the project. = 他们整个项目期间都吝啬资源。


将来时:
He will be miserly even when he wins the lottery. = 即使他中了彩票，他也会很吝啬。
They will likely be miserly with information. = 他们很可能会吝啬信息。


虚拟语气:
If he were not so miserly, he would have more friends. = 如果他不那么吝啬，他就会有更多朋友。
I wish he weren't so miserly with his time. = 我希望他不那么吝啬他的时间。


被动语态:
His behavior is often described as miserly. = 他的行为通常被形容为吝啬的。
The company's approach to funding was considered miserly. = 该公司的资金投入方式被认为是吝啬的。


疑问句:
Is he always so miserly? = 他总是这么吝啬吗？
Are they being miserly on purpose? = 他们故意吝啬吗？


否定句:
He is not miserly at all. = 他一点也不吝啬。
She was not miserly with her help. = 她并不吝啬她的帮助。

词源:
源自古法语 *mesire,  最终源于拉丁语 *miser ("wretched, unhappy"),  尽管其现代含义与原始含义不同，但最初指那些因过度专注于财富而使自己变得不幸的人。
