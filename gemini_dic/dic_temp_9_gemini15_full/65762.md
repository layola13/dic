every-inch:/ˈɛvri ɪntʃ/| adj.|每一英寸的；十足的；完全地|Completely; in every respect.  The phrase emphasizes the totality or completeness of a quality or characteristic. |例句：He's every inch a gentleman. 他十足一位绅士。|近义词：completely, totally, utterly, perfectly|反义词：partially, incompletely, slightly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is every inch a soldier. = 他十足一个军人。
She is every inch a queen. = 她完全像个女王。

一般过去时:
He was every inch the hero. = 他完全是个英雄。
She was every inch a scholar. = 她十足一位学者。

现在完成时:
He has been every inch the professional. = 他一直都非常专业。
She has remained every inch the artist. = 她始终保持着艺术家的风范。

过去完成时:
He had been every inch a leader. = 他一直都是个领导者。
She had always been every inch a lady. = 她一直都是个淑女。


将来时:
He will be every inch the champion. = 他将成为一个名副其实的冠军。
She will prove herself every inch a fighter. = 她将证明自己是一个斗士。

虚拟语气:
If he were every inch a gentleman, he wouldn't have done that. = 如果他真是个绅士，他就不会那样做了。
If she were every inch a scholar, she would have known that. = 如果她真是个学者，她就会知道那件事了。

被动语态:
(This phrase is rarely used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
Is he every inch a scholar? = 他真的是个学者吗？
Is she every inch the leader? = 她真的是个领导者吗？

否定句:
He is not every inch a gentleman. = 他并非十足的绅士。
She wasn't every inch the professional. = 她并非完全的专业人士。
