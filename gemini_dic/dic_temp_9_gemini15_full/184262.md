resonated:/ˈrezəneɪtɪd/| v.|引起共鸣；产生回响；回荡|To evoke a feeling of strong agreement or approval; to produce a strong feeling or response in someone.|The speaker's words resonated with the audience.  演讲者的言辞引起了听众的共鸣。|The singer's voice resonated throughout the hall. 歌手的歌声在整个大厅回荡。|echo, reverberate, vibrate|dissonated, clashed


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The music resonates with the listeners.  音乐引起听众的共鸣。
His ideas resonate with many people. 他的想法与许多人产生共鸣。

一般过去时:
The message resonated deeply with the community.  这条信息深深地引起了社区的共鸣。
Her speech resonated strongly with the voters. 她的演讲强烈地引起了选民的共鸣。

现在完成时:
His work has resonated with audiences worldwide. 他的作品在世界各地引起了共鸣。
The film's themes have resonated with critics and audiences alike.  这部电影的主题引起了评论家和观众的一致共鸣。

过去完成时:
The artist's earlier works had already resonated with a small but dedicated following. 艺术家早期的作品已经引起了一小部分但忠实的追随者的共鸣。
By the end of the concert, the music had resonated throughout the entire city. 音乐会在结束时，音乐已经回荡在整个城市。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:
