shake-up: /ˈʃeɪkʌp/ | n., vt. | 震动；改组；彻底改革 | A shake-up is a reorganization or restructuring, often involving significant changes in personnel or methods.  It can also refer to a sudden and violent disturbance. |近义词：reorganization, restructuring, overhaul, upheaval |反义词：stability, stagnation, maintenance of the status quo


例句(名词):

The company underwent a major shake-up after the CEO resigned. = 公司在首席执行官辞职后进行了大规模的改组。

The political shake-up resulted in several cabinet ministers losing their jobs. = 政治上的震荡导致几位内阁部长丢了工作。


例句(动词):

They decided to shake up the management team to improve efficiency. = 他们决定改组管理团队以提高效率。

The new manager shook up the department by introducing new policies. = 新经理通过引入新政策来彻底改革了部门。

一般现在时:

The CEO shakes up the company every few years. = 首席执行官每隔几年就会对公司进行一次改组。

He often shakes up the ingredients before adding them to the cake batter. = 他经常在把配料添加到蛋糕糊中之前先把它们摇匀。


一般过去时:

The government shook up the education system last year. = 政府去年改革了教育体系。

She shook up the bottle of soda before opening it. = 她打开苏打水瓶之前摇晃了一下。


现在完成时:

The company has shaken up its marketing strategy. = 公司已经调整了其营销策略。

He has shaken up the drink and served it to the customers. = 他已经摇匀了饮料并把它端给了顾客。


过去完成时:

They had shaken up the team before the crucial game. = 他们在关键比赛之前已经调整了队伍。

The chef had shaken up the dressing before adding the salad. = 厨师在添加沙拉之前已经摇匀了调味汁。


将来时:

The board will shake up the company's structure next quarter. = 董事会将在下个季度改组公司的结构。

I will shake up this bottle of juice before I drink it. = 我喝果汁之前会摇晃一下这瓶果汁。


虚拟语气:
