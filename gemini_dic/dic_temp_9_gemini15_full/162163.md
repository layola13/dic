pre-interest:/ˌpriːˈɪntrəst/| adj.|利息前的，预先利息|Pre-interest refers to the period or amount before interest is calculated or added.  It often relates to financial calculations where the principal amount is considered before any interest accrues.  For example, the pre-interest balance on a loan is the amount owed before interest is added.|The pre-interest balance on my loan was $10,000.|近义词:principal, initial, before-interest|反义词:post-interest, after-interest
