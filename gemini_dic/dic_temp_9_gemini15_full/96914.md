inexplicable:/ˌɪnɪkˈsplɪkəbl/| adj.|无法解释的，不可思议的|incapable of being explained or accounted for.  Something inexplicable is so strange or unusual that it is impossible to understand or explain.|The disappearance of the painting remained inexplicable.|近义词：unaccountable, incomprehensible, unfathomable|反义词：explainable, understandable


词源:

* **in-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "without"
* **ex-**: prefix meaning "out" or "from"
* **plic-**: root derived from the Latin word "plicare," meaning "to fold" or "to intertwine."  This refers to the idea of something being folded or intertwined in a complex and difficult-to-understand way.
* **-able**: suffix indicating capability or possibility.  In this case, it means "capable of being explained." The "in-" prefix negates this capability.


用法说明:

Inexplicable is primarily used as an adjective to describe something that is beyond explanation or understanding.  It often implies a sense of mystery or wonder.


例句：

The sudden change in weather was inexplicable.  = 天气的突然变化是无法解释的。

His behavior was completely inexplicable to everyone. = 他的行为对每个人来说都完全无法理解。


近义词：

unaccountable, incomprehensible, unfathomable, mysterious, enigmatic, puzzling


反义词:

explainable, understandable, comprehensible, clear, obvious


词性:

形容词 (adjective)
