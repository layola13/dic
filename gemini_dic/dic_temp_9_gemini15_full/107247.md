laissez:/lɛˈseɪ/| interj. |听其自然；放任；自由放任|  From the French verb "laisser," meaning "to leave" or "to let,"  "laissez-faire"  reflects a policy of minimal governmental intervention in the economic affairs of individuals and society.  It emphasizes individual freedom and self-reliance. | Example:  The government adopted a laissez-faire approach to the economy. 政府对经济采取了自由放任的政策。|近义词：nonintervention, free rein|反义词：intervention, regulation, control| interj.
