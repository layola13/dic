out-of-work: /ˌaʊt əv ˈwɜːrk/ | adj. | 失业的，待业的 |  Unemployed; without a job.  | Example: He's been out of work for six months. 他失业六个月了。  |近义词：unemployed, jobless | 反义词：employed, working


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is out of work. = 他失业了。
She is currently out of work and looking for a new job. = 她目前失业了，正在找新工作。

一般过去时:
He was out of work last year. = 他去年失业了。
They were out of work for a short period after the factory closed. = 工厂倒闭后，他们失业了一段时间。

现在完成时:
She has been out of work since last spring. = 从去年春天开始她就失业了。
He has been out of work for so long that he is starting to lose hope. = 他失业了这么久，开始失去希望了。

过去完成时:
He had been out of work for two years before he finally found a new job. = 他失业了两年后才最终找到了一份新工作。
Before she got this job, she had been out of work for several months. = 在她得到这份工作之前，她已经失业了几个月。

将来时:
He will be out of work if the company closes down. = 如果公司倒闭，他就会失业。
She will likely be out of work for a while after her contract expires. = 她的合同到期后，她很可能会失业一段时间。

虚拟语气:
If I were out of work, I would look for a new job immediately. = 如果我失业了，我会立即找一份新工作。
I wish I were not out of work. = 我希望我没有失业。

被动语态:
(This adjective doesn't typically use passive voice)


疑问句:
Is he out of work? = 他失业了吗？
Are many people in this area out of work? = 这个地区很多人失业了吗？


否定句:
He is not out of work; he has a very demanding job. = 他没有失业；他有一份非常 demanding 的工作。
She is not out of work; she's currently employed as a teacher. = 她没有失业；她目前是一名教师。
