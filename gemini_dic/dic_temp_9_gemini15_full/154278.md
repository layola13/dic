perissodactyla: /ˌpɛrɪsɵʊˈdæktɪlə/ | n. |奇蹄目动物|  A taxonomic order of mammals characterized by having an odd number of toes on each foot, with the central toe being the largest and bearing the most weight.  This order includes horses, rhinoceroses, and tapirs.  They have a specialized digestive system adapted to their herbivorous diet. |Example:  Horses, zebras, and rhinoceroses are all examples of perissodactyls.  马、斑马和犀牛都是奇蹄目的例子。| 近义词: ungulates (a broader term)| 反义词: artiodactyla (even-toed ungulates)
