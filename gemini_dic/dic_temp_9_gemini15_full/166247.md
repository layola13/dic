prerealization:/ˌpriːˌriːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |预先实现; 预见; 预感|The act or process of realizing something before it actually happens; a premonition or anticipation.  It often involves a sense of knowing something before it is explicitly revealed or experienced.  This word is relatively rare and often found in academic or philosophical contexts.|例句：Her prerealization of the impending danger saved her life. = 她预感到即将到来的危险而救了自己一命。
The artist's prerealization of the finished painting was remarkably accurate. = 艺术家对完成画作的预见非常准确。|近义词：premonition, anticipation, foreboding, presentiment |反义词：surprise, unexpectedness |名词
