relubricating: /ˌriːˈluːbrɪkeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 再润滑；重新润滑 | The act of lubricating something again.  It implies a previous lubrication that has worn off or become insufficient. | 例句：The mechanic is relubricating the engine parts. = 机械师正在给发动机零件重新润滑。

The machine requires relubricating after heavy use. = 机器在重度使用后需要重新润滑。| 近义词：re-oiling, greasing, oiling | 反义词：drying, dehydrating |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They relubricate the machinery regularly. = 他们定期对机器进行再润滑。
He relubricates his bicycle chain every week. = 他每周都给自行车链条重新上油。

一般过去时:
The engineer relubricated the bearings before restarting the machine. = 工程师在重新启动机器之前对轴承进行了重新润滑。
She relubricated the hinges to quiet the squeaking sound. = 她给铰链重新上油以消除吱吱作响的声音。

现在完成时:
They have relubricated the equipment to prevent further damage. = 他们已经对设备进行了重新润滑以防止进一步损坏。
The mechanic has relubricated the car's engine, and it runs smoothly now. = 机械师已经对汽车发动机进行了重新润滑，现在它运行平稳了。

过去完成时:
The machine had already been relubricated before the inspection. = 在检查之前，机器已经过重新润滑。
They had relubricated the parts before assembling the machine. = 他们在组装机器之前已经对零件进行了重新润滑。

虚拟语气:

词源:
re- (prefix indicating again or anew) + lubricate (to apply a lubricant to, making something smooth and less resistant to motion)  Lubricate itself comes from Latin *lubricāre "to make slippery," from lubricus "slippery."
