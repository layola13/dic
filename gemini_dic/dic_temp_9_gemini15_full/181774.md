religions:/rɪˈlɪdʒənz/| n. |宗教（复数）|The word "religions" refers to various systems of faith and worship. It encompasses a wide range of beliefs, practices, and organizations centered around spiritual and moral values.  Religions often involve a belief in a supernatural power or powers, rituals, and a code of conduct. |Example: The world's major religions include Christianity, Islam, Buddhism, and Hinduism. 世界主要宗教包括基督教、伊斯兰教、佛教和印度教。

The study of religions is called comparative religion.  对宗教的研究被称为比较宗教学。|近义词：belief systems, faiths, creeds|反义词：atheism, secularism
