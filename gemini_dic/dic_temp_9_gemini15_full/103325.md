jiggler:/ˈdʒɪɡlər/| n. |摆动器；摇晃物；不安分的人|A jiggler is something that jiggles or moves slightly and repeatedly.  It can refer to a physical object or a person who is restless or fidgety.  |例句：The loose doorknob acted as a jiggler, rattling constantly.  门把手松动，不断地摇晃。

The child was a little jiggler, unable to sit still for more than a minute.  这个孩子很不安分，坐不住超过一分钟。|近义词：wiggler, shaker, fidgeter |反义词：stillness, calm, quiet person

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loose tile jiggles constantly. = 这块松动的瓷砖一直在晃动。
He jiggles his leg nervously. = 他紧张地抖动着腿。


一般过去时:
The earthquake jiggled the building. = 地震使建筑物摇晃。
She jiggled the handle to open the door. = 她摇晃把手以打开门。


现在完成时:
The car has jiggled loose. = 车子已经摇晃松动了。
He has jiggled the machine all day without success. = 他整天都在摇晃机器，但没有成功。


过去完成时:
The wind had jiggled the leaves from the trees. = 风已经把树叶摇落了。
She had jiggled the lock several times before it finally opened. =  她摇晃了锁好几次才最终打开。


将来时:
The machine will jiggle if it's not properly balanced. = 如果机器没有平衡好，它就会晃动。
The strong wind will jiggle the windows. = 大风会使窗户摇晃。


虚拟语气:
If I jiggled the handle harder, it might open. = 如果我用力摇晃把手，它可能会打开。
I wish the loose tooth wouldn't jiggle so much. = 我希望这颗松动的牙齿不要晃动得这么厉害。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The door didn't jiggle when I pushed it. = 当我推门时，门没有晃动。
