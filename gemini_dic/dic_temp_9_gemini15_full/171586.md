pulls:/pʊlz/ | v. |拉，拖，拽 | To exert force on (something) so as to cause it to move towards oneself.  |例句：He pulls the rope. 他拉绳子。 |例句：The car pulled away from the curb.  汽车驶离路边。|近义词：tug, haul, drag |反义词：push


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pulls the wagon. = 他拉手推车。
She pulls her hair back in a ponytail. = 她把头发扎成马尾辫。

一般过去时:
I pulled a muscle lifting that box. = 我搬那个箱子时拉伤了肌肉。
The train pulled into the station. = 火车驶入车站。

现在完成时:
She has pulled all-nighters to finish the project. = 为了完成项目，她熬了好几个通宵。
They have pulled together to overcome the difficulties. = 他们齐心协力克服困难。

过去完成时:
He had pulled the plug before the machine overheated. = 在机器过热之前，他已经拔掉了插头。
After she had pulled the lever, the door opened. = 她拉动杠杆后，门开了。

将来时:
I will pull out all the stops to help you. = 我将竭尽全力帮助你。
The company will pull its investment from the project. = 公司将撤回对该项目的投资。

虚拟语气:
If he pulled the lever, the machine would start. = 如果他拉动杠杆，机器就会启动。
I wish I had pulled myself together earlier. = 我真希望我早点振作起来。


被动语态:
The rope was pulled taut. = 绳子被拉紧了。
The plug was pulled from the socket. = 插头从插座上拔掉了。

疑问句:
Can you pull that heavy box for me? = 你能帮我拉那个重箱子吗？
Who pulled the fire alarm? = 谁拉响了火警？

否定句:
He couldn't pull the heavy cart. = 他拉不动那辆重车。
She didn't pull any punches in her criticism. = 她批评时毫不留情。


PHRASE->

pull off: 成功完成
