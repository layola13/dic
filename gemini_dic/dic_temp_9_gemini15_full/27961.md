cantor:/ˈkæntər/| n.|康托尔；唱诗班领唱；（集合论中的）康托尔|The term "cantor" has two main meanings.  In music, it refers to a singer, typically a male, who leads the singing of a choir or congregation. In mathematics, it refers to Georg Cantor, the founder of set theory, or to concepts within set theory. |例句：The cantor led the congregation in a beautiful hymn. 唱诗班领唱带领会众唱了一首优美的圣歌。|近义词：chorister, precentor, cantor (in math: set theorist)|反义词：None directly applicable.
