precomparison: /ˌpriːkəmˈpærɪsən/ | n. |预先比较；事前比较 | A precomparison is a comparison made before a main comparison or analysis. It's often used to establish a baseline or context for the primary comparison.  Precomparisons can help to refine the focus of a subsequent comparison, identify potential biases, or eliminate irrelevant data. | Example: The researchers conducted a precomparison of the two groups' baseline characteristics before starting the main study.  研究人员在开始主要研究之前对两组的基线特征进行了预先比较。 |近义词： preliminary comparison, prior comparison, antecedent comparison |反义词：postcomparison, subsequent comparison
