simoom:/sɪˈmuːm/| n. |西沐风，热风 |A simoom is a hot, dry wind that blows across the deserts of North Africa and Arabia.  It is characterized by its intense heat, strong gusts, and often carries sand or dust.  The simoom can be extremely dangerous, causing dehydration, heatstroke, and even death. |Example: The travelers were caught in a fierce simoom and struggled to find shelter.  旅行者们被一场猛烈的西沐风困住了，努力寻找庇护所。|近义词：khamsin (埃及热风), haboob (苏丹热风)|反义词：无直接反义词，可考虑凉爽的风，如breeze (微风)|名词
