monophysitical:/ˌmɒnəˈfɪzɪtɪkəl/| adj. |单性论的；单一神性论的 |Relating to or characteristic of Monophysitism, a Christological doctrine that holds that Christ had only one nature, the divine.  The term emphasizes the unity of Christ's divine nature, often to the exclusion or subordination of his human nature.  This doctrine was a significant point of contention in early Christianity, leading to various schisms and theological debates. |The Monophysitical churches of the East maintained their distinct theological identity despite centuries of conflict. |近义词：unipersonal (in this context) |反义词：dyophysite, Nestorian
