recolonization: /ˌriːkɒlənaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 再殖民化；重新殖民 | The act or process of re-establishing colonial control over a territory or people previously under colonial rule.  It often involves the resettlement of colonists and the imposition of new political and economic systems.  This can be a controversial and complex process, with lasting impacts on the colonized population. | Example: The recolonization of the island after the war led to widespread resentment among the indigenous population.  再殖民化战争后岛屿的再殖民化导致当地居民普遍怨恨。| 近义词：reconquest, reoccupation | 反义词：decolonization, independence
