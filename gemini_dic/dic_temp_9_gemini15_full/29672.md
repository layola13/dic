cathead:/ˈkætˌhed/| n.|猫头；（船上的）起重机；（旧称）绞盘；|A cathead is a type of crane or hoist, typically found on ships, used for lifting heavy objects, especially anchors.  The term can also refer to a person's head, especially if likened to a cat's head, or to a type of wooden pulley used on older ships. |例句：The sailors used the cathead to raise the anchor. 水手们用起重机升起了锚。The cathead on the old sailing ship was made of sturdy oak. 那艘旧帆船上的绞盘是用坚固的橡木制成的。|近义词：crane, hoist, winch|反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "cathead" likely derives from the resemblance of the wooden structure to a cat's head, particularly earlier versions of the device.  The "cat" part may also relate to the word "cat" meaning a "block and tackle," a system of pulleys.
