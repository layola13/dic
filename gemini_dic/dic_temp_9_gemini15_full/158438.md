plebeians:/plɪˈbiːənz/ | n. |平民，庶民 |The plebeians formed the common people of ancient Rome, distinct from the patricians or aristocracy.  They lacked the political power and social status of the patricians. |例句：In ancient Rome, the plebeians struggled for political rights against the powerful patricians.  = 在古罗马，平民与强大的贵族斗争争取政治权利。

The plebeians eventually gained some political influence through the establishment of the Tribune of the Plebs. = 平民最终通过设立护民官获得了一些政治影响力。|近义词：commoners, common people, masses |反义词：patricians, aristocracy, elite
