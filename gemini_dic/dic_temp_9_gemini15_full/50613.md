deregulation: /ˌdiːˌreɡjuˈleɪʃən/ | n. |放松管制；解除管制 |The process of removing or reducing state regulations typically in a particular industry.  It aims to increase competition and efficiency by allowing businesses more freedom to operate. However, it can also lead to negative consequences such as market instability and exploitation. | Example: The deregulation of the airline industry led to lower fares but also increased flight delays.  放松航空业的管制导致票价下降，但也导致航班延误增加。|近义词： liberalization, decontrol |反义词：regulation, control
