canonizers:/ˈkænənaɪzərz/ | n. | 承认…为圣徒者；尊奉为经典者 | Those who canonize, officially declaring someone a saint or something a classic work.  They elevate individuals or works to a position of high esteem and authority. |The Church canonizers declared her a saint after reviewing her life and miracles.|近义词：sanctifiers, enshriners |反义词：decanonizers, denouncers

一般现在时:
The church canonizes saints based on evidence of their piety. = 教会根据圣徒虔诚的证据来承认他们为圣徒。
They canonize only those who meet strict criteria. = 他们只承认符合严格标准的人为圣徒。


一般过去时:
The committee canonized the author's works as classics. = 委员会将作者的作品尊奉为经典。
The pope canonized the martyr after centuries of requests. = 教皇在几个世纪的请求之后，封圣了殉道者。


现在完成时:
The Vatican has canonized many significant figures throughout history. = 梵蒂冈已经将许多重要人物列入圣徒。
The literary world has canonized Shakespeare's plays as masterpieces. = 文学界已将莎士比亚的戏剧尊奉为杰作。


过去完成时:

By the 19th century, they had canonized most of the early church fathers. = 到19世纪，他们已经将大多数早期教会教父尊奉为经典。


将来时:
The church will canonize her next year if the evidence is sufficient. = 如果证据充分，教会将于明年封圣她。
Scholars predict that future generations will canonize this author's work. = 学者预测，后代将尊奉这位作者的作品。


虚拟语气:
