nonfinitely:/ˌnɒnˈfaɪnaɪtli/ | adv. | 非限定地 | Not in a finite form; without expressing tense or person. This term usually describes verbs or verb phrases.  |  The verb "to run" is used nonfinitely in the phrase "I want to run." | 近义词： infinitely | 反义词：finitely


USAGE->

This word is an adverb describing the state of a verb or verb phrase, not a verb itself, so it doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, standard verb tense examples are not applicable.  Instead, examples showing the contrast between finite and nonfinite verb forms are provided.

Finite Verb (expressing tense and person):

I run every day. = 我每天都跑步。
She ran yesterday. = 她昨天跑了。


Nonfinite Verb (not expressing tense or person):

To run is good exercise. = 跑步是很好的锻炼。 (Infinitive)
Running is good exercise. = 跑步是很好的锻炼。(Gerund)
I want him to run. = 我想让他跑步。(Infinitive)


PHRASE->

There are no common idiomatic phrases using "nonfinitely".  The word is primarily used in grammatical analysis.
