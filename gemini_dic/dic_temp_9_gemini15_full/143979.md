outraging: /aʊtˈreɪdʒɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人愤慨的；令人震惊的 | Extremely shocking and unacceptable; causing strong indignation. |

例句：

The outraging news of the massacre shocked the world.  = 大屠杀的令人震惊的消息震惊了世界。

His outraging behavior at the party was unacceptable. = 他在聚会上令人愤慨的行为是不可接受的。


近义词：scandalous, shocking, outrageous, appalling, offensive, insulting

反义词：acceptable, pleasing, agreeable, inoffensive


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The news is outraging many people. = 这个消息激怒了许多人。
His actions are outraging to the public. = 他的行为激怒了公众。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:
The incident has outraged the entire nation. = 这起事件激怒了整个国家。
His behavior has outraged his family and friends. = 他的行为激怒了他的家人和朋友。


过去完成时:
They had been outraged by the injustice. = 他们对这种不公正感到愤怒。
The public had been outraged by the corruption scandal. = 公众对腐败丑闻感到愤怒。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish the news hadn't outraged so many people. = 我希望这个消息没有激怒这么多人。


被动语态:
Many people were outraged by the decision. = 许多人对这个决定感到愤怒。
The public is being outraged by the government's inaction. = 公众对政府的不作为感到愤怒。


疑问句:

What outraged you the most about the situation? = 在这种情况下，什么最让你愤怒？


否定句:
I wasn't outraged by his remarks. = 我并没有被他讲话激怒。
She wasn't outraged by the news. = 她并没有被这个消息激怒。

词源:

The word "outraging" comes from the verb "outrage," which itself comes from the Old French word "outrager," meaning "to do violence to,"  "to assault," or "to injure."  The prefix "out-" intensifies the meaning, suggesting a degree of violence or offense that goes beyond the ordinary.
