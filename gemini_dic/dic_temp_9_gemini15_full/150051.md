palpitating:/ˈpæl.pɪ.teɪ.tɪŋ/| adj.| 心跳加速的；悸动的；兴奋的 | Feeling a rapid or irregular heartbeat.  The word often describes a feeling of nervousness, excitement, or fear. |例句：My heart was palpitating with excitement. 我的心跳因兴奋而加速。

例句：She felt a palpitating fear as she waited for the results. 她等待结果时感到一阵阵的恐惧。


近义词：throbbing, fluttering, quivering, pounding

反义词：calm, still, tranquil

词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His heart is palpitating. = 他的心跳加速。
The engine is palpitating. = 发动机在悸动。


一般过去时:
My heart palpitated with fear. = 我害怕得心跳加速。
The bird's wings palpitated rapidly. = 鸟的翅膀快速地扇动。


现在完成时:
My heart has been palpitating all day. = 我整天心跳都加速。
The machine has been palpitating since it started. = 机器自从启动以来就一直悸动。


过去完成时:
My heart had palpitated before the interview. = 面试前我的心跳就加速了。
The old car had palpitated its last moments before it died. = 旧车在报废前一阵阵地悸动着。


将来时:
My heart will palpitate when I see her. = 我看到她的时候心跳会加速。
The engine will palpitate under heavy load. = 发动机在重载下会悸动。


虚拟语气:
If I saw a spider, my heart would palpitate. = 如果我看到蜘蛛，我的心跳会加速。
If the machine were faulty, it would palpitate irregularly. = 如果机器有故障，它会不规则地悸动。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
My heart wasn't palpitating. = 我的心跳没有加速。

PHRASE->

词源:
The word "palpitating" comes from the Latin word "palpitatum," the past participle of "palpitare," meaning "to throb" or "to flutter."  This ultimately relates to the word "palpitate," meaning to beat rapidly or irregularly, especially of the heart.
