hoards:/hɔːrdz/| n. v.|囤积；宝藏；大量|A large accumulation of something, typically something valuable or precious.  The act of accumulating such things.|例句：The dragon hoarded gold in its cave.  龙在它的洞穴里囤积着黄金。
The miser hoarded his money.守财奴囤积了他的钱财。|近义词：accumulate, amass, stockpile|反义词：disperse, distribute, squander|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hoards old coins. 他囤积旧硬币。
She hoards information. 她囤积信息。

一般过去时:
They hoarded food during the war. 战争期间他们囤积食物。
He hoarded his feelings. 他压抑着自己的感情。

现在完成时:
They have hoarded enough supplies for the winter. 他们已经囤积了足够的过冬物资。
She has hoarded her emotions for too long. 她压抑情绪太久了。

过去完成时:
The squirrels had hoarded nuts before the winter. 松鼠在冬天来临之前已经囤积了坚果。
He had hoarded his secrets for years. 他隐瞒秘密多年了。

将来时:
They will hoard resources in anticipation of the drought. 他们将囤积资源以应对旱灾。
She will hoard her energy for the marathon. 她将为马拉松储备能量。

虚拟语气:
If he hoarded less, he wouldn't be so stressed. 如果他不那么囤积东西，就不会那么压力大了。
I wish I hadn't hoarded so much junk. 我真希望我没有囤积那么多垃圾。

被动语态:
Resources were hoarded by the government during the crisis. 危机期间，政府囤积了资源。
Information is often hoarded by companies. 信息常常被公司囤积。

疑问句:
Does he hoard anything unusual? 他囤积什么不寻常的东西吗？

否定句:
He doesn't hoard stamps. 他不收集邮票。
