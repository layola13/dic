rushing:/ˈrʌʃɪŋ/| v. adj. n.| 冲; 匆忙; 奔流 |The act of moving or acting quickly; a rapid flow or rush.  It can be used as a noun, verb, or adjective. |例句：He was rushing to catch the train. 他在赶火车。  The rushing river was dangerous to cross.  奔腾的河流难以渡过。|近义词：hurrying, hastening, speeding, surging |反义词：dawdling, delaying, lingering


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is rushing to finish his work. = 他正在匆忙完成他的工作。
They are rushing through the city streets. = 他们正快速穿过城市街道。


一般过去时:
She rushed to the hospital. = 她匆忙赶往医院。
We rushed through our dinner. = 我们匆匆忙忙地吃了晚饭。


现在完成时:
He has rushed through three projects this month. = 这个月他已经匆匆忙忙地完成了三个项目。
They have rushed to judgment. = 他们草率地下了结论。


过去完成时:
I had rushed to the airport, only to find my flight delayed. = 我匆忙赶到机场，却发现我的航班延误了。
She had rushed through her preparations, so she missed some important steps. = 她仓促地完成了准备工作，所以错过了一些重要的步骤。


将来时:
They will rush to help when they hear about the accident. = 他们听到事故的消息后会立即赶去帮忙。
We will rush to finish the report before the deadline. = 我们将赶在截止日期前完成报告。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I wouldn't rush. = 如果我有更多时间，我就不会匆忙了。
I wish I hadn't rushed into that decision. = 我真希望我没有那么仓促地做出那个决定。


被动语态:
The project was rushed to completion. = 项目被仓促完成。
The decision was rushed through parliament. = 该决议被匆促通过议会。


疑问句:
Are you rushing? = 你在赶时间吗？
Why are they rushing? = 他们为什么这么匆忙？


否定句:
He didn't rush. = 他不慌不忙。
They didn't rush into a decision. = 他们没有草率地做出决定。
