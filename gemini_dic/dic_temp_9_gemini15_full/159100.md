pockmarked:/ˈpɑːkˌmɑːrkt/| adj.|麻脸的，坑坑洼洼的|Covered with pocks or small depressions, resembling the surface of the moon.  The word often describes skin, but can also be used to describe other surfaces.|例句：His face was pockmarked from a childhood illness. 他脸上因为儿时患病而留下了麻子。

例句：The old building's walls were pockmarked with bullet holes.  这栋老建筑的墙壁上布满了弹孔。


近义词：pitted, scarred, cratered

反义词：smooth, unblemished


词源:  The word "pockmarked" derives from "pock," meaning a small mark or scar left by smallpox or another disease. The suffix "-marked" indicates the presence of these marks.  The word "pock" itself originates from the Middle English word "pok," ultimately from a Proto-Germanic root.
