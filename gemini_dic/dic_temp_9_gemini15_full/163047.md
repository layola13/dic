preclerical: /ˌpriːˈklɛrɪkəl/ | adj. |教士职务之前的；在神职人员任职之前的|  Relating to or characteristic of the period before someone enters the clergy; preceding a clerical career or position.  This word is quite rare and largely found in specialized historical or academic contexts.  It describes something that happened or existed before someone became a member of the clergy.

例句：His preclerical life was marked by a strong interest in literature and philosophy. = 他在成为神职人员之前的生命中对文学和哲学有着浓厚的兴趣。

His preclerical activities included teaching and writing. = 他在成为神职人员之前的活动包括教学和写作。


近义词：antecedent, prior, preceding

反义词：postclerical


词性：形容词
