hi-hats:/ˈhaɪ hæts/| n.| 踩镲 |A pair of thin, usually metallic, cymbals played by a drummer by pressing a pedal with the foot and striking them with a stick or beater.|例句：The drummer's hi-hats provided a steady beat for the song.  鼓手的踩镲为歌曲提供了稳定的节奏。

例句：The jazz musician skillfully manipulated his hi-hats to create a complex rhythmic pattern.  这位爵士乐手熟练地操控他的踩镲，创造出复杂的节奏型。
