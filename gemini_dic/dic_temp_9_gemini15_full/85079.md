hard-row-to-hoe:/ˌhɑːrdˈroʊtəhoʊ/| adj. phr.| 费力的差事；艰难困苦|A difficult and unpleasant task or situation requiring considerable effort.|例句：Raising six children alone is a hard row to hoe.  独自抚养六个孩子是一件非常艰难的事情。|例句：He's facing a hard row to hoe in his attempt to reform the company. 他试图改革公司，面临着重重困难。|近义词：arduous task, uphill battle, tough challenge|反义词：easy task, smooth sailing


USAGE->
This phrase is an idiom and doesn't have verb conjugations like "to be", "to go" etc.  It functions as an adjective phrase describing a task or situation.  Therefore, typical verb tense examples are not applicable.


PHRASE->
a hard row to hoe for someone: 对某人来说是一件难事
a hard row to hoe in: 在...方面面临巨大挑战
have a hard row to hoe: 面临巨大的挑战
it's a hard row to hoe: 这是一件难事
that's a hard row to hoe: 那是一件难事
