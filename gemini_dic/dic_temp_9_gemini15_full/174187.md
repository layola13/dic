queering:/ˈkwɪərɪŋ/ | v. | 使成酷儿；质疑异性恋规范；挑战性别规范 | The act of questioning or challenging heteronormative assumptions and practices, often with the aim of creating more inclusive and diverse spaces.  It can involve questioning gender roles, sexual orientations, and societal expectations. | Example:  The play queered traditional gender roles by portraying a strong female lead who rejects societal expectations.  这部戏剧通过刻画一位拒绝社会期望的强势女性主角来挑战传统的性别角色。


Example: The artist's work queers the notion of beauty by showcasing diverse body types and expressions.这位艺术家的作品通过展示不同的体型和表达方式来挑战美的概念。


近义词：challenging, questioning, subverting, disrupting | 反义词：affirming, upholding, reinforcing, conforming

词源:
The verb "queering" derives from the noun "queer," which initially meant "strange" or "odd."  Over time, it has been reclaimed by the LGBTQ+ community as a label and a source of empowerment. The verb reflects this reclamation, signifying the act of challenging and disrupting established norms.
