hyphens: /ˈhaɪfən/ | n. | 连字符 | A punctuation mark (-) used to connect words or parts of words, or to divide words into syllables.  It's used to create compound words, to join prefixes or suffixes, to avoid ambiguity, and to show a break in a word across lines. | Example:  The word "well-being" uses a hyphen to connect "well" and "being". Another example: self-esteem, mother-in-law. | 近义词：dash (in some contexts) | 反义词：None
