hyperidealistic: /ˌhaɪpərˌaɪdiˈælɪstɪk/ | adj. | 过于理想主义的，空想的 | Excessively idealistic; impractical and unrealistic.  Characterized by or exhibiting an extreme or impractical degree of idealism. |  His hyperidealistic plans for world peace were ultimately doomed to fail. 他过于理想主义的世界和平计划最终注定要失败。  The hyperidealistic approach to the problem ignored the practical difficulties.  这种过于理想主义的解决方法忽略了实际困难。 |近义词: utopian, unrealistic, impractical, visionary |反义词: realistic, pragmatic, practical, down-to-earth

词源:
The word is a combination of "hyper-" (meaning excessive or beyond) and "idealistic" (relating to idealism, a philosophical theory emphasizing the importance of ideas and ideals).  Therefore, "hyperidealistic" signifies an extreme or excessive form of idealism.
