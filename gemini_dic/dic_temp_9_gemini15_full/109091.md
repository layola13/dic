lege:/ˈliːdʒ/ | n. | 法律权利；特权；特许；法定权利 | The right to do or have something, especially by law.  |  He had the lege to vote. 他有投票权。 | 近义词：right, privilege, entitlement | 反义词：obligation, duty


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has the lege to speak freely. = 他有自由发言的权利。
They enjoy the lege of free education. = 他们享有免费教育的权利。

一般过去时:
She lost her lege to practice law. = 她失去了执业律师的权利。
We did not have the lege to enter the building. = 我们没有进入大楼的权利。

现在完成时:
They have had the lege to own property for years. = 他们拥有房产的权利已经有很多年了。
She has gained the lege to represent her country. = 她获得了代表她国家的权利。

过去完成时:
He had already lost his lege to vote before the election. = 在选举之前他已经失去了投票权。
They had never had the lege to travel abroad before. = 他们以前从未有过出国旅行的权利。

将来时:
She will have the lege to appeal the decision. = 她将有权对该决定提出上诉。
They will gain the lege to access classified information. = 他们将获得访问机密信息的权利。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
