conestoga:/kɒnɪˈstoʊɡə/| n. |篷车；康尼斯托加式马车| A large covered wagon used by pioneers in the 18th and 19th centuries to transport goods and people across the American West.  It was characterized by its distinctive curved, canvas-covered body and broad wheels. |例句：The pioneers trekked westward in their Conestoga wagons.  拓荒者们乘坐他们的康尼斯托加篷车向西进发。
