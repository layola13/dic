gloomed:/ɡluːmd/| v.|变得忧郁；使忧郁|To become gloomy; to make gloomy.  The news gloomed his spirits. |近义词：depressed, saddened, dejected |反义词：cheered, brightened, elated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bad weather glooms the city. = 糟糕的天气使这座城市阴沉。
The news often glooms her. = 这个消息经常让她忧郁。


一般过去时:
The rain gloomed the afternoon. = 雨使下午变得阴沉。
His failure gloomed his prospects. = 他的失败使他的前景黯淡。


现在完成时:
The continuous rain has gloomed the entire week. = 持续的雨水使整个星期都阴沉。
The loss of his job has gloomed him considerably. = 失去工作使他相当沮丧。


过去完成时:
The argument had gloomed the atmosphere before the meeting even started. = 争吵甚至在会议开始之前就已经使气氛低沉了。
She had gloomed for days after receiving the bad news. = 在收到坏消息后，她沮丧了好几天。


将来时:
The approaching storm will gloom the sky. = 即将到来的暴风雨将使天空阴沉。
I fear that the news will gloom him deeply. = 我担心这个消息会让他非常沮丧。


虚拟语气:
If the sun didn't come out, the day would gloom. = 如果太阳不出来，这一天就会阴沉。
I wish the news wouldn't gloom my spirits. = 我希望这个消息不会让我沮丧。


被动语态:
His face was gloomed by the sad news. = 他悲伤的消息使他的脸阴沉下来。
The party was gloomed by the unexpected argument. = 意外的争吵使聚会气氛低沉。


疑问句:
Does this weather gloom you? = 这种天气让你感到沮丧吗？
Does the thought of the exam gloom you? = 想到考试让你沮丧吗？


否定句:
The bright sunshine didn't gloom the day. = 明媚的阳光并没有使这一天阴沉。
The good news didn't gloom her spirits. = 好消息并没有让她沮丧。
