dramatises:/ˈdræmətaɪz/|/ˈdrɑːmətaɪz/| vt. |戏剧化；使戏剧性；夸张地表现|To represent or portray (something) in a dramatic or exaggerated way.  It often implies an overemphasis or sensationalizing of events.|

例句：
The media dramatises the situation, making it seem far worse than it actually is. = 媒体戏剧化地报道了这一情况，使其看起来比实际情况糟糕得多。

The play dramatises the struggles of a young woman in 19th-century London. = 这部戏剧戏剧化地展现了一位19世纪伦敦年轻女性的挣扎。


近义词：exaggerate, sensationalize, overemphasize, dramatize


反义词：understate, downplay, minimize


词性：动词

词源:
From "drama" + "-ise" (a suffix forming verbs).  "Drama" ultimately derives from the Greek word "δρᾶμα" (dragma), meaning "action" or "deed," referring to a play or performance.  The "-ise" suffix is of French origin, similar to the English "-ize".
