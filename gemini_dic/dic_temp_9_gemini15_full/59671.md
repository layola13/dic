echinozoa:/ˌɛkɪnəˈzoʊə/| n. |棘皮动物|Echinozoa is a class of marine invertebrates belonging to the phylum Echinodermata.  They are characterized by their radial symmetry and spiny skin.  Examples include sea urchins and sand dollars.|The echinozoa are a diverse group found in various marine habitats.|Sea urchins are a common example of echinozoa.|近义词：echinoderm (broader term)|反义词：无
