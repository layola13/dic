pressures:/ˈprɛʃərz/| n.|压力，压强|Forces that tend to compress or deform an object.  The word can refer to physical pressure, as well as social, emotional, or mental pressures. |例句：The pressures of modern life can be overwhelming. 现代生活的压力可能会让人难以承受。|例句：The air pressure is high today. 今天的气压很高。|近义词：stress, strain, burden, weight, force|反义词：relief, ease, comfort


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The job puts a lot of pressure on him. = 这份工作给他带来了很大的压力。
Financial pressures are affecting many families. = 经济压力正在影响许多家庭。


一般过去时:
She felt immense pressure to succeed. = 她感到巨大的成功压力。
The company faced intense pressures during the recession. = 公司在经济衰退期间面临着巨大的压力。


现在完成时:
Recent events have put increased pressure on the government. = 最近发生的事件给政府带来了更大的压力。
They have been under considerable pressure to meet the deadline. = 他们一直承受着很大的压力来赶上最后期限。


过去完成时:
He had felt the pressure building for weeks before the exam. = 在考试前的几周，他已经感到压力越来越大了。
The negotiations had reached a point of intense pressure. = 谈判已经达到了极其紧张的压力点。


将来时:
The increasing population will put more pressure on resources. = 人口增长将给资源带来更大的压力。
We anticipate that the project will put pressure on our budget. = 我们预计该项目将给我们的预算带来压力。


虚拟语气:
If there were less pressure, she would be happier. = 如果压力小一些，她会更快乐。
I wish there weren't so many pressures in my life. = 我希望我的生活中没有这么多压力。


被动语态:

The team was under pressure to perform well. = 该团队承受着表现良好的压力。


疑问句:

否定句:
