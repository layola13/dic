mildews:/ˈmɪlduːz/| n. |霉菌；霉；(动植物的)霉病 |A mildew is a fungus that grows on plants, food, or other organic matter, often appearing as a fuzzy or powdery coating.  It thrives in damp, warm conditions.  Mildews can cause discoloration, damage, and unpleasant odors.|The bread developed mildew after being left out in the humid air. 这面包放在潮湿的空气中后长了霉。 The old book was covered in mildew. 那本旧书长满了霉。|mold, fungus, blight |sterile, clean|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
Mildew often grows in damp places. = 霉菌通常生长在潮湿的地方。
The bread mildews quickly in this humid weather. = 在这种潮湿的天气里，面包很快就发霉了。


一般过去时:
The walls mildewed after the flood. = 洪水过后，墙壁长了霉。
The old clothes mildewed in the damp basement. = 旧衣服在潮湿的地下室里发霉了。


现在完成时:
The leather has mildewed due to the lack of ventilation. = 由于通风不良，皮革已经发霉了。
The painting has mildewed because it was stored improperly. = 这幅画因为保存不当而发霉了。


过去完成时:
The books had mildewed before we discovered them. = 我们发现这些书之前，它们就已经发霉了。
The food had mildewed by the time we got back from vacation. = 等我们度假回来的时候，食物已经发霉了。


将来时:
The clothes will mildew if left in this damp closet. = 如果把衣服留在这么潮湿的衣橱里，它们会发霉的。
The fruit will likely mildew if not refrigerated. = 水果如果不冷藏，很可能会发霉。


虚拟语气:
If the room were drier, the walls wouldn't mildew. = 如果房间更干燥，墙壁就不会发霉了。
I wish the clothes hadn't mildewed before the trip. = 我真希望衣服在旅行前没有发霉。


被动语态:
The walls were mildewed by the constant humidity. = 墙壁由于持续潮湿而发霉了。
The bread was mildewed and had to be thrown away. = 面包发霉了，不得不扔掉。


疑问句:

否定句:
The clothes didn't mildew because they were properly stored. = 衣服因为保存得当所以没有发霉。
The bread hasn't mildewed yet, but it might soon. = 面包还没发霉，但可能很快就会发霉。
