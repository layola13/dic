humiliative: /hjuːˈmɪliətɪv/ | adj. | 令人羞辱的；使人丢脸的 | Causing or tending to cause humiliation; shameful.  | The humiliating experience left him feeling small and insignificant.  这次令人羞辱的经历让他感觉渺小而微不足道。 | shameful, degrading, mortifying, embarrassing | honorable, uplifting, glorious | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The situation is humiliating for everyone involved. =  这种情况对所有参与者来说都非常丢脸。
This comment is humiliating to her. = 这句话让她感到很羞辱。


一般过去时:
The defeat was a humiliating experience for the team. = 这次失败对球队来说是一次耻辱的经历。
He felt humiliated after the public criticism. = 公开批评后，他感到很羞愧。


现在完成时:
His actions have humiliated his family. = 他的行为已经让他家人蒙羞。
She has been humiliated by her boss's behavior. = 她受到了老板行为的羞辱。


过去完成时:
I had already been humiliated before he even started speaking. = 他还没开始说话，我已经先感到羞辱了。
They had humiliated him in front of everyone. = 他们当着所有人的面羞辱了他。


将来时:
This decision will humiliate the company. = 这个决定将会让公司蒙羞。
His actions will likely humiliate him in the future. = 他的行为很可能会让他将来蒙羞。


虚拟语气:
If I did that, I would be humiliated. = 如果我那样做，我会感到羞愧。
I wish I hadn't said that; I feel humiliated. = 我真希望我没有那样说；我感到很羞愧。


被动语态:
He was humiliated by the public announcement. = 他被公开的声明羞辱了。
She was humiliated in front of her colleagues. = 她被同事们羞辱了。


疑问句:
Is this situation humiliating to you? = 这种情况让你感到羞辱吗？

否定句:

I didn't feel humiliated, just a little embarrassed. = 我并没有感到羞辱，只是有点尴尬。
