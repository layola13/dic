heptastich: /ˈheptəstɪk/ | n. |七行诗 | A heptastich is a poem or stanza consisting of seven lines.  It's less common than other poetic forms like couplets or quatrains.  The term itself highlights the number seven. | Example:  While not a famous example, a simple heptastich could be:  The sun shines bright, / The birds all sing, / A perfect day, / Joy it does bring, / Happiness flows, / Like a gentle stream, / A blissful dream. | 近义词: septet (although septet can refer to other musical forms) | 反义词: None directly applicable, as it relates to the number of lines. | 词性: noun
