permeable:/ˈpɜːrmɪəbl/ | adj. | 可渗透的；可透过的 | Able to be passed or soaked through.  A permeable membrane allows liquids or gases to pass through it. | The soil is permeable, allowing water to drain easily. 这土壤是可渗透的，允许水轻松排出。 The cell membrane is permeable to some substances but not others. 细胞膜对某些物质是可渗透的，而对另一些物质则不是。 |  porous, penetrable | impermeable, impervious


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The membrane is permeable to water. = 这层膜可以透水。
This material is permeable to air. = 这种材料可以透气。


一般过去时:
The dam was permeable after the flood. = 洪水过后，水坝变得可渗透了。
The fabric was permeable to light. = 这布料可以透光。


现在完成时:
The soil has become more permeable after the rain. = 雨后土壤变得更透水了。
The membrane has proven to be permeable to gases. = 这层膜已被证明对气体具有渗透性。


过去完成时:
The wall had become permeable before the collapse. = 在倒塌之前，墙体已经变得可渗透了。
The rock had been permeable for centuries. = 这块岩石几个世纪以来都是可渗透的。


将来时:
The new material will be permeable to water. = 新材料将可以透水。
The soil will become more permeable after the irrigation. = 灌溉后土壤会变得更透水。


虚拟语气:
If the membrane were permeable, the fluid would flow through. = 如果膜是可渗透的，液体就会流过。
I wish the soil were more permeable. = 我希望土壤更透水。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
