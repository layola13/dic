cussedness:/ˈkʌsɪdnəs/| n. |固执；倔强；顽固|The stubborn refusal to accept help or advice.  It's a trait often associated with strong-willed individuals, but can also be seen as a negative quality when it leads to unnecessary conflict or suffering.|例句：Her cussedness prevented her from admitting she was wrong.  她的固执阻止了她承认自己错了。

例句：His cussedness in refusing to compromise led to a breakdown in negotiations. 他顽固地拒绝妥协，导致谈判破裂。|近义词：stubbornness, obstinacy, perversity, mulishness|反义词：compliance, yielding, docility


词源：源自中古英语 *cussed，意为“被诅咒的”，后来演变为形容词“cussed”，意为“顽固的，倔强的”。  最终演变为名词“cussedness”。
