plunged:/plʌndʒd/|/ˈplʌndʒd/| vt.  |投入；骤降；使陷入；|to jump or dive into something, especially water; to suddenly become worse|The swimmer plunged into the pool. 游泳者跳入游泳池。The country plunged into recession.国家陷入经济衰退。|  dive, immerse, submerge |climb, ascend, rise|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He plunges into his work every day. 他每天都投入工作。
She plunges her hands into the cold water. 她把手伸进冷水中。

一般过去时:
The temperature plunged overnight.气温一夜之间骤降。
He plunged the knife into the cake. 他把刀插进蛋糕里。

现在完成时:
The stock market has plunged since the news broke.  自从消息传出以来，股市暴跌。
She has plunged herself into her studies. 她全身心地投入到学习中。

过去完成时:
The economy had plunged into a deep crisis before the government intervened.  政府干预之前，经济已经陷入严重的危机。
They had plunged into debt after buying the house. 他们买房后债台高筑。

将来时:
The company's profits are expected to plunge next quarter. 预计公司下一季度的利润将暴跌。
They will plunge into the icy water. 他们将跳入冰冷的水中。

虚拟语气:
If he plunged into the river, he would be in danger. 如果他跳进河里，他将处于危险之中。
I wish I hadn't plunged into that risky investment. 我真希望我没有投入那项高风险的投资。

被动语态:
The city was plunged into darkness by the power outage. 停电使城市陷入黑暗。
The country was plunged into war. 国家陷入战争。

疑问句:
Did the stock prices plunge yesterday?  股票价格昨天暴跌了吗？
Will the swimmer plunge into the deep end? 游泳者会跳进深水区吗？

否定句:
He didn't plunge into the project without careful planning. 他并没有在没有周密计划的情况下就投入这个项目。
The sales figures didn't plunge as dramatically as predicted. 销售额并没有像预测的那样大幅下降。
