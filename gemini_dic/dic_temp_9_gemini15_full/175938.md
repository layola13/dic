rationalization:/ˌræʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n.|合理化；解释；找借口|The act of attempting to justify or explain away one's behavior or actions.  It often involves finding seemingly logical reasons for actions that are actually motivated by other, less acceptable factors. |例句：His rationalization for missing the deadline was unconvincing.  他的错过截止日期的解释令人难以信服。

例句：The company's rationalization of its workforce led to many layoffs. 公司对员工队伍的合理化导致了许多裁员。


近义词：justification, explanation, excuse, vindication |反义词：condemnation, criticism, censure


词源:  From "rationalize,"  which comes from the word "rational," ultimately derived from the Latin word "ratio," meaning "reason" or "reckoning."  The "-ization" suffix indicates the process or act of making something rational.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rationalizes his bad behavior. = 他为自己的不良行为找借口。
She rationalizes her spending habits. = 她为自己的消费习惯找借口。

一般过去时:
They rationalized their decision after the fact. = 他们事后为自己的决定找了借口。
He rationalized his mistakes to his boss. = 他向老板为自己的错误找了借口。

现在完成时:
She has rationalized her actions to herself. = 她已经为自己做过的事情找了借口。
They have rationalized their investment strategy. = 他们已经为自己的投资策略找了借口。

过去完成时:
He had rationalized his fear before confronting the problem. = 在面对问题之前，他已经为自己害怕的情绪找了借口。
She had rationalized her anger before speaking to her friend. = 在跟朋友说话之前，她已经为自己生气的情绪找了借口。

将来时:
He will rationalize his actions to avoid blame. = 他将会为自己的行为找借口以避免责备。
She will rationalize her decision later. = 她稍后会为自己的决定找借口。

虚拟语气:
If I were to fail, I would rationalize it as a learning experience. = 如果我失败了，我会把它合理化成一次学习的经验。
I wish I hadn't rationalized my procrastination. = 我真希望我没有为自己的拖延找借口。

被动语态:
His actions were rationalized by his supporters. = 他的行为得到了支持者的合理化解释。
The company's restructuring was rationalized as a necessary measure. = 公司的重组被合理化为必要的措施。

疑问句:

否定句:
He didn't rationalize his behavior.  = 他没有为自己的行为找借口。
She refused to rationalize her mistakes. = 她拒绝为自己的错误找借口。
