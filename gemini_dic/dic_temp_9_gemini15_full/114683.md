maligning:/məˈlaɪnɪŋ/| v.|诽谤；中伤；诋毁 | To speak badly of someone or something; to defame; to speak ill of.  |例句：He was accused of maligning his former employer. 他被指控诽谤他的前雇主。|例句：The article malignes the reputation of the company. 这篇文章损害了公司的声誉。|近义词：slander, defame, vilify, denigrate |反义词：praise, commend, extol


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are maligning her character. = 他们在诽谤她的品格。
He constantly maligns his rivals. = 他不断地诽谤他的竞争对手。

一般过去时:
She maligns him in her book. = 她在书中诽谤了他。
The newspaper maligns the politician's actions. = 这家报纸诽谤了这位政治家的行为。

现在完成时:
They have been maligning him for years. = 他们多年来一直在诽谤他。
She has maligned his reputation. = 她已经损害了他的名誉。

过去完成时:
He had maligned her to her friends before the incident. = 在事件发生之前，他已经向她的朋友诽谤她了。
The media had maligned the athlete’s performance. = 媒体已经诽谤了这位运动员的表现。

将来时:
They will malign her in the upcoming election. = 他们在即将到来的选举中将诽谤她。
He will continue to malign his opponents. = 他将继续诽谤他的对手。

虚拟语气:
If they maligns her again, I'll sue them. = 如果他们再次诽谤她，我会起诉他们。
I wish they hadn't maligned his good name. = 我希望他们没有诽谤他的好名声。

被动语态:
He was maligined by the press. = 他被媒体诽谤了。
Her reputation was maligined by false accusations. = 她的名誉被不实的指控所损害。

疑问句:
Are they maligning him again? = 他们又在诽谤他了吗？
Was he maligined by the media? = 他被媒体诽谤了吗？

否定句:
They did not malign her. = 他们没有诽谤她。
He would not malign his friends. = 他不会诽谤他的朋友。
