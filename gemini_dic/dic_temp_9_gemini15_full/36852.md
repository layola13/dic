colony:/ˈkɒləni/| /ˈkɑːləni/ | n. |殖民地；群体；菌落 |A colony is a group of people from one country who have settled in another, usually a distant land, often under the rule of their original country.  It can also refer to a group of animals or plants living together, or a group of bacteria or other microorganisms. |例句：The British established many colonies in North America. 英国在北美建立了许多殖民地。

例句：A large colony of ants lived under the stone. 一大群蚂蚁住在石头底下。|近义词：settlement, community, group |反义词：mother country, metropolis
