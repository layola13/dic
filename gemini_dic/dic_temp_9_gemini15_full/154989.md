petalody:/ˈpɛtəlˌoʊdi/| n. |花瓣化|The transformation of stamens or other floral organs into petals.  This phenomenon is often observed in flowers and results in an increase in the number of petals or petal-like structures.  The cause can be genetic mutations or environmental factors.|The rose exhibited petalody, with many stamen-like structures transformed into additional petals.|近义词：petaloidy (less common)|反义词：staminody (transformation into stamens)
