hypostatic:/ˌhaɪpəˈstætɪk/| adj.| 本体论的；假定的；依附于个体的；| Relating to or of the nature of hypostasis; especially : of or relating to the doctrine of the Trinity in which three persons (Father, Son, and Holy Spirit) are united in one divine substance or essence.  The word often implies a distinction between the substance and the persons within that substance. | The hypostatic union is a central tenet of Christian theology. 本体论的结合是基督教神学的一个核心信条。|The hypostatic interpretation of the Trinity suggests a distinction between persons and substance.  对三位一体的本体论解释表明了位格和本体之间的区别。|近义词：essential, inherent, intrinsic |反义词：nonessential, extrinsic, accidental|
