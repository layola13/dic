/ˈpænˈsaɪkɪst/| n. |泛心论者，泛灵论者 |A panpsychist is someone who believes that consciousness is a fundamental and ubiquitous property of the universe, present in all matter to some degree.  Panpsychism is a philosophical position that contrasts with materialism, which holds that only physical matter exists. |Example:  Many prominent philosophers have been panpsychists throughout history, proposing that even seemingly inanimate objects possess some level of consciousness.  许多历史上著名的哲学家都是泛心论者，他们认为即使是看似无生命的物体也具有一定的意识。|近义词：idealist (in some contexts)|反义词：materialist
