faint:/feɪnt/ | adj., v., n. | 微弱的；昏厥；昏倒 |  to become unconscious for a short time, or to almost disappear | 近义词：weak, dim, swoon | 反义词：strong, bright, conscious


英文解析:
As an adjective, "faint" means weak, indistinct, or barely perceptible.  As a verb, it means to lose consciousness briefly. As a noun, it refers to the act of losing consciousness.


例句(adj.):
The faint sound of music drifted from the distance.  = 远处传来微弱的音乐声。

The light was too faint to read by. = 光线太暗，无法看书。


例句(v.):
She fainted from the heat. = 她热晕过去了。

He nearly fainted when he heard the news. = 听到这个消息他差点晕过去。


例句(n.):
She had a faint and fell to the ground. = 她晕倒在地。

The doctor attributed her condition to a faint. = 医生认为她的情况是由于晕厥造成的。
