imagined:/ɪˈmædʒɪnd/| v. |想象的；设想的| To form a mental image or concept of something; to conceive of something in the mind.  It implies the creation of something in the mind, often without evidence or proof. |例句：I imagined a beautiful beach with white sand and clear blue water. 我想象着一片美丽的海滩，上面有白色的沙子清澈的蓝水。

She imagined a life far away from her current circumstances. 她想象着远离她现在境况的生活。

近义词：conceived, envisioned, pictured, visualized |反义词：real, actual, factual |词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I imagine things differently than you do. = 我想象事物的方式和你不同。
He imagines himself to be a great musician. = 他想象自己是一位伟大的音乐家。


一般过去时:
We imagined a better future for our children. = 我们想象着孩子们拥有更美好的未来。
She imagined that she was flying. = 她想象自己正在飞翔。


现在完成时:
I have imagined many scenarios in my head. = 我脑海中已经构想了许多场景。
She has imagined a world without war. = 她已经想象了一个没有战争的世界。


过去完成时:
He had imagined that the trip would be easier. = 他曾经想象这次旅行会更容易。
They had imagined a different outcome. = 他们曾想象过不同的结果。


将来时:
I will imagine the possibilities. = 我将想象各种可能性。
She will imagine a new design for the dress. = 她将构想一件新设计的裙子。


虚拟语气:
If I imagined it, I could create it. = 如果我能想象到它，我就能创造它。
I wish I could imagine a solution to this problem. = 我希望我能想象出这个问题的解决办法。


被动语态:
The future was imagined differently by each of them.  = 未来在他们每个人心中被想象得不同。
A world of peace is imagined by many. = 许多人想象着一个和平的世界。


疑问句:
What do you imagine will happen next? = 你想象接下来会发生什么？
What did he imagine he saw? = 他想象他看到了什么？


否定句:
I cannot imagine how it happened. = 我无法想象这是怎么发生的。
She didn't imagine the consequences. = 她没有预料到后果。
