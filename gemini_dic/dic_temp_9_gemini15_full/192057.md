scotoma:/skoʊˈtoʊmə/|/skəˈtəʊmə/| n. |视野缺损，暗点 |A scotoma is an area of partial or complete blindness in an otherwise normal visual field. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including damage to the retina, optic nerve, or visual cortex.  Scotomas can be temporary or permanent, and their size and location can vary. | Example:  The patient reported experiencing a scotoma in their right eye after the accident.  事故后病人报告其右眼出现视野缺损。  |近义词：blind spot, visual field defect |反义词：clear vision
