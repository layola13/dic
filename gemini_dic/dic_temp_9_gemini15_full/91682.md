hypercoagulable: /ˌhaɪpərkoʊˈæɡjələbl/ | adj. | 高凝状态的，易于凝血的 |  Describing a condition where the blood is more likely to clot than normal. This increased clotting tendency can lead to various health problems, including thrombosis (blood clot formation in a blood vessel) and embolism (a blood clot that travels to and blocks a blood vessel). The condition can be inherited or acquired due to various factors. | 例句：The patient's hypercoagulable state increased the risk of pulmonary embolism. 患者的高凝状态增加了肺栓塞的风险。


The patient presented with symptoms consistent with a hypercoagulable disorder.  患者出现的症状与高凝状态疾病一致。


词源:
* **hyper-**:  from Greek, meaning "over," "above," or "beyond."
* **coagulable**: from Latin "coagulare," meaning "to curdle" or "to clot."  This is related to the verb "coagulate."


近义词: thrombotic, prothrombotic


反义词: hypocoagulable (having a decreased tendency to clot)
