rangers:/ˈreɪndʒərz/| n.|护林员；游骑兵；侦察兵；（尤指）美国国家公园的护林员|Members of a group of people who protect and manage a natural area, such as a forest or national park. They often have law enforcement powers.  Rangers also refers to a military unit, a scouting unit, or a group of explorers.|例句：The park rangers rescued the lost hikers. 公园护林员救出了迷路的徒步旅行者。

例句：The Texas Rangers are a famous baseball team.德州游骑兵队是一支著名的棒球队。


近义词：park keeper, warden, scout, patrol, conservationist |反义词：None specifically, but words implying destruction or disregard for nature could be considered antonyms.


词源:The word "ranger" comes from the Old French word "ranger," meaning "to range" or "to roam."  This reflects the historical role of rangers as individuals who patrolled and protected large areas of land.  The word's evolution reflects a shift in meaning from simply roaming to specifically managing and protecting natural resources.
