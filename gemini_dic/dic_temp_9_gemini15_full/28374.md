/kɑːrˈbænɪl/| n. |卡巴尼尔；苯胺基；氨基苯基|Carbanil is a chemical term referring to the phenylcarbamoyl group (-NHCO-C6H5) or, less commonly, to phenyl isocyanate (C6H5NCO).  It's derived from the combination of "carbamoyl" and "phenyl".  The term is used in organic chemistry to describe compounds containing this functional group. |The synthesis of carbanilates often involves the reaction of isocyanates with alcohols. |近义词: phenylcarbamoyl (more precise) |反义词:  None specifically, as it describes a functional group.
