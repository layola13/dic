overgrossness: /ˌoʊvərˈɡroʊsnəs/ | n. |过度肥大；过分粗俗；极度粗糙 | Excessive size or bulk; excessive coarseness or vulgarity.  The term is rare and often used in a figurative sense to describe something excessively large, coarse, or vulgar.  It lacks common usage and lacks readily available synonyms or antonyms.


例句：

The overgrossness of the building's design clashed with the delicate landscaping.  = 建筑设计的过度肥大与精致的景观格格不入。

The overgrossness of his language offended many guests. = 他语言的粗俗冒犯了许多客人。


近义词:  (depending on context)  excessiveness, coarseness, vulgarity, crudeness, grossness (a less intense form)


反义词: (depending on context)  refinement, delicacy, subtlety, elegance


词性: 名词 (n.)


词源:  Derived from "over-" (excessive) + "gross" (large, coarse, vulgar).  The "-ness" suffix denotes a state or quality.  The word is a relatively modern formation, arising from a combination of existing morphemes rather than having a traceable historical root like many older words.
