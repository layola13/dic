mercantilism:/ˈmɜːkəntaɪlɪzəm/| n. |重商主义|An economic system (especially prevalent in Europe from the 16th to 18th centuries) in which a nation's power is directly related to its wealth, usually measured in gold and silver.  Mercantilism emphasizes exporting more than importing, building a large treasury, and establishing colonies to secure resources and markets.  It promotes government intervention in the economy to achieve these goals. |The rise of mercantilism in the 17th century led to intense competition between European powers for colonial possessions.  |近义词：economic nationalism, protectionism |反义词：free trade, laissez-faire economics
