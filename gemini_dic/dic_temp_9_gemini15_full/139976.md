odoriferous:/ˌoʊdəˈrɪfərəs/ | adj. | 芬芳的；有气味的；臭的 | Having or emitting a strong smell; often implies a pleasant smell, but can also refer to an unpleasant one.  The word's connotation depends heavily on context. | 例句：The odoriferous roses filled the garden with their sweet fragrance. 芬芳的玫瑰花充满了花园的甜蜜芬芳。

The odoriferous swamp was avoided by all who knew its reputation.  臭气熏天的沼泽地被所有知道它名声的人避开了。| 近义词：fragrant, aromatic, scented, redolent (pleasant smells); fetid, malodorous, foul-smelling (unpleasant smells) | 反义词：odorless, scentless, inodorous

WORD ORIGIN->
The word "odoriferous" is derived from the Latin words "odor" (smell) and "ferre" (to bear or carry).  Therefore, it literally means "smell-bearing" or "carrying a smell."
