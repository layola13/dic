equivalent:/ɪˈkwɪvələnt/ | adj., n. | 等价的；等量的；对等的 |  An equivalent amount, value, or meaning.  Something that is equal to something else in value, amount, meaning, function, or effect. |

例句：

The two sums are equivalent. = 这两个数额相等。

This new car is equivalent to the old model in terms of fuel efficiency. = 这辆新车在燃油效率方面与旧款车型相当。


近义词：equal, comparable, similar, corresponding

反义词：different, unlike, dissimilar


词源：

From Old French *equivalent, from Latin aequivalēns, present participle of aequivalēre "to be of equal value," from aequus "equal" + valēre "to be strong, be worth."


用法说明:

作为形容词:

*  It describes something that is equal in value, amount, meaning, or function to something else.

作为名词:

* It refers to something that is equal to something else.  It can be a specific amount, value, or thing.


例句 (更多):

There is no equivalent in English for this French expression. =  这个法语表达在英语中没有等价的词语。

The company offered him an equivalent position in a different department. = 公司为他提供了另一个部门的同等职位。

He received the equivalent of $1000 in goods. = 他收到了价值1000美元的商品。

This medication has no known equivalent. = 此药物没有已知的等效药物。
