conceptualised: /kənˈsɛptʃuəlaɪzd/ | v. | 概念化；构想 | To form a concept or idea of something; to imagine or visualize something.  | The architect conceptualised a futuristic building design. 建筑师构想了一个未来主义的建筑设计。 | imagined, envisioned, conceived, formulated | unconceptualised,  unimagined | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He conceptualises new marketing strategies every year. 他每年都会构想新的营销策略。
The artist conceptualises her work through dreams.  这位艺术家通过梦境来构想她的作品。


一般过去时:
She conceptualised a brilliant solution to the problem. 她构想出了一个解决这个问题的绝妙方案。
They conceptualised the project over several meetings. 他们在几次会议上构想出了这个项目。


现在完成时:
The team has conceptualised a new product line. 团队已经构想出一条新的产品线。
We have conceptualised several different scenarios. 我们已经构想了几种不同的情景。


过去完成时:
They had conceptualised the entire campaign before the meeting. 他们在会议之前就已经构想出了整个活动方案。
She had conceptualised a novel approach to teaching. 她之前已经构想了一种新颖的教学方法。


将来时:
The company will conceptualise its new advertising campaign next month. 公司将于下个月构想它的新广告活动。
We will conceptualise a plan for sustainable development. 我们将制定可持续发展的计划。


虚拟语气:
If they had conceptualised the project differently, it might have been more successful. 如果他们当初对项目的构想有所不同，它可能会更成功。
I wish I had conceptualised a better solution. 我希望我当初能构想出一个更好的解决方案。


被动语态:
The project was conceptualised by a team of experts.  这个项目是由一个专家团队构想的。
The new design was conceptualised over a period of several months. 新的设计是在几个月的时间里构想出来的。


疑问句:
How did they conceptualise the idea? 他们是如何构想出这个想法的？

否定句:
