permutations:/ˌpɜːmjuˈteɪʃənz/ | n. |排列；组合；置换 |The arrangement of objects or elements in a specific order.  Permutations consider the order of the elements. For example, the permutations of the letters ABC are ABC, ACB, BAC, BCA, CAB, CBA.  There are different types of permutations, including permutations with repetitions and permutations without repetitions.  Understanding permutations is crucial in probability, statistics, and combinatorics. |Example:  The number of permutations of the letters in the word "MATH" is 24.  数学单词“MATH”中字母的排列组合共有24种。


There are 6 permutations of the letters A, B, and C. A, B, 和 C 这三个字母有 6 种排列组合。


近义词：arrangements, combinations (though combinations don't consider order), sequences |反义词：None directly opposite, but the concept of a single, unordered set is a contrast.
