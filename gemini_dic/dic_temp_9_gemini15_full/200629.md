slavophile:/ˈslæ vəˌfaɪl/| n. |斯拉夫爱好者；斯拉夫主义者 |A person who admires and supports the culture, traditions, and political systems of Slavic peoples, often advocating for their independence and national identity.|The Slavophile movement in 19th-century Russia emphasized the unique spiritual and cultural values of the Russian people and opposed Westernization.|近义词：Russophile (specifically for Russia)|反义词：Westernizer
