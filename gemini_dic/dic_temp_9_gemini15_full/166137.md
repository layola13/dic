prepricing:/ˈpriːpraɪsɪŋ/| n. |预定价，预先定价|The process of determining the price of a product or service before it is officially launched or offered to the market.  This often involves market research, cost analysis, and competitive analysis to arrive at an optimal price point.  It aims to maximize profitability while considering consumer perceptions of value. |例句：The company conducted extensive prepricing research before launching its new smartphone. 公司在推出新智能手机之前进行了广泛的预定价研究。

例句：Accurate prepricing is crucial for a successful product launch.  准确的预定价对于产品成功发布至关重要。


近义词：price estimation, preliminary pricing, advance pricing


反义词：post-pricing (定价后)


词性：名词
