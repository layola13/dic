confuser:/kənˈfjuːzər/| n. |使人困惑的事物；令人迷惑的人|Something or someone that causes confusion.|The complex instructions were a real confuser.|近义词：puzzle, enigma, perplexity |反义词：clarifier, elucidator


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The confusing instructions confuse many users. = 令人困惑的说明使许多用户感到困惑。
This confusing situation confuses me. = 这种混乱的局面让我感到困惑。


一般过去时:
The complicated rules confused me yesterday. = 复杂的规则昨天让我很困惑。
That ambiguous statement confused the audience. = 那句模棱两可的陈述使听众感到困惑。


现在完成时:
The conflicting reports have confused the investigation. = 互相矛盾的报告使调查工作陷入了混乱。
This new technology has confused many older people. = 这项新技术让许多老年人感到困惑。


过去完成时:
I had been confused by his contradictory explanations. = 我被他自相矛盾的解释弄糊涂了。
The conflicting evidence had confused the jury. = 互相矛盾的证据让陪审团感到困惑。


将来时:
The new system will confuse many users initially. = 新系统最初会让许多用户感到困惑。
This complicated problem will confuse many students. = 这个复杂的问题将会让许多学生感到困惑。


虚拟语气:
If the instructions were clearer, they wouldn't confuse so many people. = 如果说明更清晰，就不会让这么多人感到困惑。
I wish the new software didn't confuse me so much. = 我希望新软件不会让我这么困惑。


被动语态:
Many users were confused by the software update. = 许多用户因为软件更新而感到困惑。
The audience was confused by the speaker's ambiguous language. = 听众被演讲者含糊不清的语言弄糊涂了。


疑问句:
Does this complex problem confuse you? = 这个复杂的问题让你困惑吗？
What confuses you about this explanation? = 关于这个解释，什么让你感到困惑？


否定句:
The instructions did not confuse me. = 说明并没有让我感到困惑。
The simple design did not confuse the users. = 简洁的设计并没有让用户感到困惑。
