sandbanks:/ˈsændˌbæŋks/| n. |沙洲；沙滩 |A sandbank is a long, narrow ridge of sand formed in the sea or a river.  They are often found near the coast and can be submerged at high tide.  Sandbanks are dynamic features, constantly changing shape and location due to tides and currents.|例句：The boat ran aground on a sandbank.  船搁浅在沙洲上。

The children loved building sandcastles on the sandbanks. 孩子们喜欢在沙洲上堆沙堡。|近义词：sandbar, shoal|反义词：deep water, open sea
