junkie:/ˈdʒʌŋki/| n.|瘾君子，迷恋者|A person who is addicted to a particular substance, especially a drug, or who is excessively addicted to something.|He became a heroin junkie. 他成了海洛因瘾君子。|近义词：addict, fiend, devotee|反义词:non-addict


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a junkie. = 他是个瘾君子。
She is a social media junkie. = 她是个社交媒体迷。


一般过去时:
He was a junkie in his youth. = 他年轻时是个瘾君子。
She was a work junkie before she retired. = 她退休前是个工作狂。


现在完成时:
He has been a junkie for many years. = 他吸毒多年了。
She has become a fitness junkie recently. = 她最近成了健身狂。


过去完成时:
He had been a junkie before he went to rehab. = 他戒毒前是个瘾君子。
She had been a coffee junkie for years before she cut it out. = 在戒掉咖啡之前，她多年来一直是个咖啡瘾君子。


将来时:
He will remain a junkie unless he seeks help. = 除非他寻求帮助，否则他将继续吸毒。
She will likely become a video game junkie if she keeps playing. = 如果她继续玩游戏，她很可能会变成游戏迷。


虚拟语气:
If he were not a junkie, he would have a better life. = 如果他不是瘾君子，他会过得更好。
If she weren't a work junkie, she would have more time for her family. = 如果她不是个工作狂，她就会有更多时间陪伴家人。


被动语态:
He was often called a junkie by his peers. = 他经常被同龄人称为瘾君子。
The term "junkie" is sometimes considered offensive. = “瘾君子”这个词有时被认为是冒犯性的。


疑问句:
Is he a junkie? = 他是瘾君子吗？
Is she a chocolate junkie? = 她是个巧克力迷吗？


否定句:
He is not a junkie; he's just a heavy user. = 他不是瘾君子；他只是个大量使用者。
She is not a junkie; she simply enjoys video games. = 她不是瘾君子；她只是喜欢玩电子游戏。
