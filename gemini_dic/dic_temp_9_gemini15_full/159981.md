polypus:/ˈpɒlɪpʌs/ /ˈpɑːlɪpʌs/| n.|息肉；水螅；(某些动物的)多足动物|A polypus is a growth of tissue from a mucous membrane, typically in the nose, intestine, or uterus.  It can be benign or malignant.|例句：The doctor discovered a polypus in his nose during the examination.  医生在检查中发现他鼻子里长了一个息肉。


The polyp was successfully removed during a minor surgical procedure.  息肉在一次小手术中被成功切除。|近义词：tumor, growth, mass|反义词：None specifically, but the antonym would relate to the absence of a growth.

词源:
From Late Latin *polypus, from Greek πολύπους (polypous), from πολύς (polys, “many”) + πούς (pous, “foot”).  The Greek word referred to an octopus or similar creature, due to its many arms or tentacles. The medical term evolved to describe the many finger-like projections of the growth.
