nauseates:/ˈnɔːzieɪts/| vt.|使恶心；使反感|To cause someone to feel nausea; to disgust or sicken someone.  The smell of the rotting fish nauseated him. 那条腐烂的鱼的气味使他恶心。 The politician's speech nauseated many in the audience.  这位政治家的演讲让许多听众感到反感。|近义词：disgust, sicken, revolt|反义词：delight, please


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The smell of the garbage nauseates me. = 垃圾的气味让我恶心。
This movie nauseates many viewers. = 这部电影让许多观众感到反胃。


一般过去时:
The strong perfume nauseated her. = 那浓烈的香水让她恶心。
The sight of blood nauseated him. = 看到血让他恶心。


现在完成时:
That experience has nauseated me for years. = 那次经历多年来一直让我感到反胃。
The constant negativity has nauseated her to the point of illness. = 持续的消极让她恶心到生病的地步。


过去完成时:
The spoiled food had already nauseated him before he even took a bite. =  在他甚至还没吃一口之前，变质的食物就已经让他恶心了。
She had been nauseated all day before she finally vomited. = 在她最终呕吐之前，她已经恶心了一整天。
