monocarbonate:/ˌmɒnəˈkɑːrbənˌeɪt/|/ˌmɑːnəˈkɑːrbənˌeɪt/| n. |一碳酸盐|A salt or ester of carbonic acid containing only one carbonate group.  Monocarbonates are less common than dicarbonates.  They are often found in specific chemical compounds or as intermediates in reactions.|Example:  The synthesis of this specific polymer involved the use of a monocarbonate intermediate.|近义词:  (Depending on context)  carbonate (in a broad sense),  bicarbonate (in some contexts indicating a single carbonate group),  alkali metal carbonate|反义词:  (No direct antonym, but)  dicarbonate, polycarbonates
