deserver:/dɪˈzɜːrvər/ | n. |应得者，值得拥有的人|A person or thing that deserves something.  A deserving person or thing. |例句：He's a deserver of the award for his outstanding contribution. 他因杰出贡献而值得获得该奖项。|近义词：recipient, beneficiary |反义词：undeserver


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deserves a promotion. = 他值得升职。
She deserves all the praise she receives. = 她应得所有她收到的赞扬。

一般过去时:
They deserved to win the game. = 他们理应赢得比赛。
He deserved a better fate. = 他应该拥有更好的命运。

现在完成时:
She has deserved her success through hard work. = 她通过努力工作赢得了成功。
They have deserved a rest after such a long journey. = 经过这么长的旅程，他们应该休息一下。

过去完成时:
He had deserved the punishment for his actions. = 他因为自己的行为而应该受到惩罚。
She had deserved better treatment from her employer. = 她应该从雇主那里得到更好的待遇。

将来时:
You will deserve a reward for your efforts. = 你将因为你的努力而得到奖励。
They will deserve to be commended for their bravery. = 他们将因为他们的勇敢而值得赞扬。

虚拟语气:
If he had worked harder, he would have deserved a better grade. = 如果他学习更努力，他本应该得到更好的成绩。
She deserved to have been given more opportunities. = 她应该得到更多机会。

被动语态:
The award was deservedly given to him. = 奖项理所当然地颁给了他。
The punishment was deserved by the criminal. = 惩罚是罪犯应得的。

疑问句:
Does she deserve this criticism? = 她值得这种批评吗？
Do they deserve our help? = 他们值得我们帮助吗？

否定句:
He doesn't deserve such harsh treatment. = 他不值得如此严厉的待遇。
They don't deserve to be blamed for the accident. = 他们不应为事故受责备。
