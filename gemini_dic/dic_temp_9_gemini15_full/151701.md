parton:/ˈpɑːrtɒn/| n. |组成部分；（尤指）夸克；帕顿；|A parton is a constituent of a hadron, such as a proton or neutron.  In the standard model of particle physics, partons are quarks and gluons.  The term "parton" is also used more generally to refer to any constituent of a composite particle.|例句：Protons and neutrons are composed of partons. 质子和中子是由夸克组成的。（此处例句略有不严谨，为方便理解而使用）|近义词：constituent, component |反义词：whole, entirety

词源:
The word "parton" is a blend of "particle" and "part," reflecting its role as a fundamental constituent of hadrons.  It was coined by Richard Feynman in the context of his parton model, which provided a successful description of high-energy hadron scattering experiments.
