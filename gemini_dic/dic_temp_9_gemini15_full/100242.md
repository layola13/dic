interrupted:/ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪd/ | adj. |被打扰的，中断的 |  Describes something that has been stopped or broken before it was finished.  |  The interrupted meeting was rescheduled for the next day.  会议被打扰了，重新安排在了第二天。 | unfinished, broken off, halted | continued, completed | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The phone interrupts my work frequently. = 电话经常打断我的工作。
Noise interrupts the concert. = 噪音打断了音乐会。


一般过去时:
The sudden noise interrupted his sleep. = 突然的噪音打断了他的睡眠。
My train journey was interrupted by a signal failure. = 我的火车旅程因为信号故障而中断。


现在完成时:
The project has been interrupted several times. = 这个项目已经被打断了好几次。
Our plans have been interrupted by unexpected events. = 我们的计划被突发事件打断了。


过去完成时:
The game had been interrupted by rain before the final score was announced. = 在最终比分公布之前，比赛已经被雨水打断了。
They had already been interrupted twice when the fire alarm went off. = 火灾警报响起时，他们已经被打断了两次。


将来时:
The meeting will be interrupted if the boss doesn't arrive on time. = 如果老板没有准时到达，会议将会被打断。
I fear my presentation will be interrupted by technical difficulties. = 我担心我的演示文稿会因技术问题而中断。


虚拟语气:
If only they hadn't interrupted, I could have finished my speech. = 如果他们没有打断，我就能完成我的演讲了。
I wish I hadn't been interrupted during the crucial moment. = 我真希望在关键时刻没有被打断。


被动语态:
The broadcast was interrupted by a breaking news report. = 节目被突发新闻报道打断了。
Our conversation was interrupted by a loud crash. = 我们的谈话被一声巨响打断了。


疑问句:
Was your presentation interrupted? = 你的演讲被打断了吗？
Were they interrupted during their important discussion? = 他们在重要的讨论期间被打断了吗？


否定句:
The lecture wasn't interrupted. = 讲座没有被打断。
My work wasn't interrupted at all yesterday. = 昨天我的工作根本没有被打断。
