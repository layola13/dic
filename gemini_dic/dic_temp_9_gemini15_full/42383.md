counterblockade:/ˈkaʊntəˈblɒkeɪd/| n. |反封锁，对封锁的抵抗|A counterblockade is an action taken to oppose or counteract a blockade.  It often involves establishing alternative trade routes or supplying the blockaded area with goods and resources.  This strategy aims to neutralize the effects of the blockade and maintain economic activity. |Example: During the war, the allied nations launched a counterblockade to circumvent the enemy's naval restrictions.  二战期间，盟国发动了反封锁以规避敌人的海军限制。|近义词：counteraction, opposition, resistance |反义词：blockade, embargo
