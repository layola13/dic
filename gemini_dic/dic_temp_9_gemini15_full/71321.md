fizzling: /ˈfɪzəlɪŋ/ | adj. | 逐渐失败的；逐渐消失的；嘶嘶作响的 | Gradually failing; losing force; making a hissing sound. |

例句：

The party was fizzling out by midnight.  = 派对在午夜时分逐渐冷清下来。

His career is fizzling. = 他的事业正在衰败。


近义词：failing, waning, petering out, sputtering

反义词：thriving, flourishing, booming


词性：形容词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The project is fizzling. = 项目正在失败。
The sound is fizzling out. = 声音正在逐渐消失。


一般过去时:
The experiment fizzled. = 实验失败了。
The excitement fizzled out quickly. = 兴奋很快消退了。


现在完成时:
The party has fizzled out. = 派对已经结束。
His enthusiasm has fizzled. = 他的热情已经消退了。


过去完成时:
The negotiations had fizzled before they even began. = 谈判甚至还没开始就失败了。
The fire had fizzled down to embers. = 火已经燃成余烬。


将来时:
The deal will likely fizzle. = 这笔交易很可能会失败。
The protests will fizzle out soon. = 抗议活动很快就会平息。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't made that mistake, the deal wouldn't have fizzled. = 如果他没有犯那个错误，这笔交易就不会失败了。
I wish my plans hadn't fizzled. = 我真希望我的计划没有失败。


被动语态:
The project was fizzled by lack of funding. = 项目因缺乏资金而失败。
The argument was fizzled out by the intervention of a third party. = 第三方的介入使争论平息。


疑问句:
Is the campaign fizzling? = 活动正在失败吗？
Is the battery fizzling out? = 电池快没电了吗？


否定句:
The plan didn't fizzle; it was a complete success. = 计划没有失败；它完全成功了。
The negotiations didn't fizzle out; they reached an agreement. = 谈判没有破裂；他们达成了协议。


PHRASE->

fizzle with excitement =兴奋地颤抖
