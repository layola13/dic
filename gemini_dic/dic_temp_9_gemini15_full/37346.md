commendatory: /ˌkɒmənˈdeɪtəri/| /ˌkɑːmənˈdeɪtəri/ | adj. |赞美的；称赞的；值得称赞的 | Expressing or conveying praise or commendation.  |例句：The article contained commendatory remarks about the new policy. 这篇文章包含了对新政策的赞扬。

例句：He received a commendatory letter for his outstanding work. 他因杰出工作而收到一封表扬信。


近义词：approving, praising, laudatory, complimentary

反义词：critical, disapproving, condemnatory


词源：源自 commend (v.)  + -atory (adj. suffix)  "tending to," "of the nature of,"  最终源自拉丁语 commendāre "to entrust, commit, recommend, praise."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The review is commendatory of the author's style. = 这篇评论赞扬了作者的文风。
His work is commendatory of his dedication. = 他的工作值得称赞，体现了他的奉献精神。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:

Critics have been commendatory of her latest performance. = 批评家们一直赞扬她最近的演出。


过去完成时:

The reviewers had been commendatory of the book's originality. = 评论家们在之前就一直赞扬这本书的原创性。


将来时:
The panel will be commendatory of the innovative approach. = 专家组将对这种创新方法表示赞扬。

虚拟语气:
If the report were more commendatory, it might be better received. = 如果报告更正面一些，可能会更好地被接受。
I wish the feedback were more commendatory. = 我希望反馈更正面一些。


被动语态:
His actions were commendatory of his character. = 他的行为值得称赞，体现了他的品格。
The project's success was widely commendatory of its design. = 该项目的成功广受赞誉，证明了其设计方案的成功。


疑问句:

否定句:
The response was not commendatory, rather critical. = 反馈并非赞扬，而是批评。
