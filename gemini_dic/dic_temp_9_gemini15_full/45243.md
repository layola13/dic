culs-de-sac:/ˌkʊl dəˈsæk/| n.| 死胡同；尽头；闭塞的局面 | A street or passage that is closed at one end.  It is often used metaphorically to describe a situation with no obvious way out. |例句：We live on a cul-de-sac, so there's very little through traffic. 我们住在死胡同里，所以过往车辆很少。

例句：The negotiations reached a cul-de-sac, with neither side willing to compromise.  谈判陷入了僵局，双方都不愿妥协。


近义词：dead end, impasse, bottleneck | 反义词：through street, open road


词源:
The term "cul-de-sac" is borrowed from French.  "Cul" means "bottom" or "end," and "de sac" means "of a sack" or "of a bag."  Therefore, a literal translation would be "bottom of a sack," referring to the closed end of a sack.
