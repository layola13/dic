legalitarianism: /ˌliːɡəlɪˈteəriənɪzəm/ | n. | 法治主义；法律至上主义 | The belief that law should be the supreme authority in a society, overriding other considerations such as morality or social custom.  It emphasizes the rule of law, equal application of the law, and the protection of individual rights through legal processes.  |  Example:  Some argue that strict legalitarianism can stifle innovation and flexibility in governance.  一些人认为严格的法治主义可能会扼杀治理中的创新和灵活性。


Example: The judge's decision reflected a strong commitment to legalitarianism, prioritizing the letter of the law over any perceived equitable outcome.  法官的判决反映了对法治主义的坚定承诺，优先考虑法律条文而不是任何被认为公平的结果。


近义词：rule of law, nomocracy, juridicism


反义词：arbitrary rule, tyranny, authoritarianism


词性: 名词
