hyperbatic:/ˌhaɪpərˈbætɪk/ | adj. |  颠倒语序的，倒装的 |  Involving the inversion of normal word order, especially in a literary context. This is a rhetorical device used to create emphasis, surprise, or a particular stylistic effect.  | Example:  "Home went I" is a hyperbatic sentence, inverting the standard subject-verb-object order.  | 近义词：inverted, transposed | 反义词：normal, standard
