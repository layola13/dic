necker:/ˈnɛkər/| n.|内克尔立方体|A Necker cube is an ambiguous two-dimensional representation of a three-dimensional cube.  It's famous for its ability to cause a perceptual shift, where the observer's interpretation of which face is "front" and which is "back" spontaneously reverses.  This is a classic example of bistable perception.|Example:  The Necker cube is a well-known example of how our brains interpret ambiguous visual information.|近义词：ambiguous figure, bistable image|反义词：unambiguous image
