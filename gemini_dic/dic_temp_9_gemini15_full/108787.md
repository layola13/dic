/liːsts/ | n. | 最少的；最小的 | least指的是最小的数量或程度，是little和few的最高级形式。它通常用作形容词或名词，也可以用作副词。 |  He has the least money of all. 他是所有人中钱最少的。 The least I can do is help you. 我至少可以帮你。 | fewer, less | most, more |  名词，形容词，副词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The least you can do is apologize. = 你至少应该道歉。
We have the least amount of supplies. = 我们拥有最少的物资。


一般过去时:
She had the least experience in the team. = 她在团队中经验最少。
They produced the least amount of goods last year. = 他们去年生产的产品最少。


现在完成时:
He has done the least work out of everyone. = 他做的工作比任何人都少。
She has received the least amount of support. = 她得到的支持最少。


过去完成时:
They had accomplished the least before the deadline. = 截止日期前，他们完成的工作最少。
We had the least information available. = 我们当时掌握的信息最少。


将来时:
He will have the least influence on the decision. = 他对决定的影响最小。
They will likely produce the least amount of products next quarter. = 下个季度他们很可能生产的产品最少。


虚拟语气:
I wish I had the least bit of worry. = 我希望我一点也不担心。
If I had the least chance, I would try. = 如果我有一点点机会，我会尝试。


被动语态:
The least important tasks were done last. = 最不重要的任务最后完成。
The least amount of effort is being put into this project. = 这个项目投入的努力最少。


疑问句:
What is the least you can do? = 你至少能做什么？
Which project requires the least amount of time? = 哪个项目最省时间？


否定句:
I have no least concern about this matter. = 我对此事毫不担心。
He showed not the least sign of guilt. = 他没有表现出丝毫的内疚。



PHRASE->
at least = 至少
least of all = 尤其不；最不
the least of my worries = 我最不担心的问题
to the least degree = 至少
not in the least = 一点也不
