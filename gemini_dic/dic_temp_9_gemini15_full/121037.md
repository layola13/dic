misconceiving:/ˌmɪskənˈsiːvɪŋ/| v.|误解，误认为 | To have a wrong idea or understanding of something.  | 例句：He was misconceiving the meaning of the poem. 他误解了这首诗的意思。| 例句：Many people misconceive the role of government in the economy. 很多人误解了政府在经济中的作用。|近义词：misunderstanding, misinterpreting, mistaking |反义词：understanding, comprehending


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He misconceives the instructions regularly. = 他经常误解指示。
They misconceive the impact of their actions. = 他们误解了他们行为的影响。


一般过去时:
I misconceived the purpose of the meeting. = 我误解了会议的目的。
She misconceived the severity of the situation. = 她误解了局势的严重性。


现在完成时:
We have misconceived the problem from the start. = 我们从一开始就误解了这个问题。
They have misconceived the nature of the task. = 他们误解了任务的性质。


过去完成时:
He had misconceived the plan before starting the project. = 他在开始项目之前就误解了计划。
They had misconceived the deadline, resulting in delays. = 他们误解了截止日期，导致延误。


将来时:
I fear he will misconceive my intentions. = 我担心他会误解我的意图。
She will misconceive the instructions unless they are clarified. = 除非说明清楚，否则她会误解指示。


虚拟语气:

I wish I hadn't misconceived the importance of the meeting. = 我真希望我没有误解会议的重要性。


被动语态:
The instructions were misconceived by many participants. = 许多参与者都误解了指示。
The significance of the event was misconceived by the media. = 事件的重要性被媒体误解了。


疑问句:
Did he misconceive my message? = 他是否误解了我的信息？

否定句:
I did not misconceive his meaning; I understood him perfectly. = 我没有误解他的意思；我完全理解他。
They did not misconceive the risks involved; they were fully aware. = 他们并没有误解所涉及的风险；他们完全意识到。
