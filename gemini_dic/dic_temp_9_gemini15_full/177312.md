reassured:/ˌriːəˈʃʊrd/| v. |使安心；使确信；使放心 | To restore confidence or courage to; to free from fear or anxiety.  |  The doctor's words reassured the anxious patient. 医生的话使焦虑的病人安心了。|近义词：comfort, soothe, allay |反义词：alarm, frighten, unsettle


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reassures his clients regularly. 他定期地让他的客户安心。
The news reassures me. 这个消息让我安心。


一般过去时:
She reassured her son that everything would be alright. 她向儿子保证一切都会好起来的。
The police reassured the witnesses. 警察让证人们安心。


现在完成时:
This information has reassured me considerably. 这个信息让我安心不少。
The success of the project has reassured the investors.  项目的成功让投资者们放心了。


过去完成时:
Having reassured herself, she proceeded with the plan. 安慰好自己之后，她继续执行计划。
She had reassured him many times before. 她之前已经多次让他安心了。


将来时:
I will reassure you that everything will be fine. 我会让你放心，一切都会好起来的。
The manager will reassure the staff about the future of the company. 经理会让员工们对公司的未来放心。


虚拟语气:
If only I could reassure her! 要是我能让她安心就好了！
I wish I could have reassured him sooner. 我真希望我能早点让他安心。


被动语态:
The anxious parents were reassured by the teacher's explanation. 焦虑的父母被老师的解释打消了顾虑。
The public were reassured by the government's promise. 公众被政府的承诺所打消顾虑。


疑问句:
Can anything reassure her? 有什么能让她安心吗？
What can I do to reassure him? 我能做什么来让他安心？


否定句:
Nothing could reassure her.  什么都不能让她安心。
The news didn't reassure her at all.  这个消息一点都没让她安心。
