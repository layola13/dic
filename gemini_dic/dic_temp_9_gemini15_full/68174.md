fainted:/ˈfeɪntɪd/| v.|昏倒，晕倒|To lose consciousness briefly because of a loss of blood supply to the brain.  This can be caused by various factors, including shock, stress, exhaustion, or underlying medical conditions.|例句：She fainted from the heat. 她热晕过去了。|例句：He fainted dead away when he heard the news. 他听到这个消息后当场昏了过去。|近义词：passed out, blacked out, swooned|反义词：remained conscious


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He faints easily. = 他容易晕倒。
She faints when she sees blood. = 她看到血就晕倒。


一般过去时:
I fainted during the marathon. = 我在马拉松比赛中晕倒了。
He fainted at the sight of the accident. = 他看到事故现场就晕倒了。


现在完成时:
She has fainted twice this week. = 她本周已经晕倒了两次。
He has never fainted before. = 他以前从未晕倒过。


过去完成时:
I had fainted before they could help me. = 在他们能帮我之前我已经晕倒了。
She had fainted from hunger. = 她因为饥饿而晕倒了。


将来时:
I think I will faint if I see that again. = 我想如果再看到那个，我会晕倒的。
He will probably faint if he stands up too quickly. = 如果他站起来太快，他可能会晕倒。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't had some water, I would have fainted. = 如果我没有喝水，我就已经晕倒了。
I wish I hadn't fainted in front of everyone. = 我真希望我没有在大家面前晕倒。


被动语态:
He was fainted by the sudden shock. = 他被突如其来的冲击弄晕了。(不太常用，通常用主动语态)
The patient was found fainted on the floor. = 病人被发现晕倒在地板上。(强调状态)


疑问句:
Did she faint? = 她晕倒了吗？
Has he ever fainted before? = 他以前晕倒过吗？


否定句:
I didn't faint. = 我没有晕倒。
She didn't faint despite the pain. = 尽管很痛，她也没有晕倒。


PHRASE->
faint away = 晕倒过去

faint from hunger = 饿晕了
faint with fear = 吓得晕倒了
