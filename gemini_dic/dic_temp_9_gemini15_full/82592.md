guardianship:/ˈɡɑːrdɪənʃɪp/ | n. |监护；保护；保管 |The legal right and responsibility to care for a minor or incapacitated person.  Guardianship involves making decisions about the ward's well-being, including their finances, health care, and education. |例句：The court awarded guardianship of the child to her aunt. 法院判决孩子的监护权归她姑姑所有。|例句：The museum takes great pride in the guardianship of its priceless artifacts.  博物馆为守护其无价的文物而感到自豪。|近义词：custody, protection, care, tutelage |反义词：neglect, abandonment
