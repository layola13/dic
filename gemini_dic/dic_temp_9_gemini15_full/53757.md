discomfiters: /dɪsˈkʌmfɪtərz/ | n. | 使人不安的人或事物 | Things or people that cause discomfort or embarrassment.  | The constant criticism from her colleagues were major discomfiters.  她的同事不断批评，让她非常难受。 |  annoyances, embarrassments, irritants | comforters, reassurers |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant noise discomfits me. = 持续的噪音让我不舒服。
These insects are discomfiting the animals. = 这些昆虫让动物们感到不安。

一般过去时:
The news discomfited him greatly. = 这个消息让他非常不安。
The unexpected guest discomfited the hostess. = 不速之客让女主人感到尴尬。

现在完成时:
His behavior has discomfited many people. = 他的行为让很多人感到不安。
The sudden change in plans has discomfited the team. = 计划的突然改变让团队感到不安。

过去完成时:
I had been discomfited by his aggressive tone before he even began speaking. =  在他开始说话之前，他已经咄咄逼人的语气就让我感到不安了。
They had been discomfited by the unexpected turn of events. = 他们对事情出乎意料的转变感到不安。


将来时:
The upcoming exam will discomfit many students. =  即将到来的考试会让很多学生感到不安。
I think the long flight will discomfit the children. = 我认为漫长的飞行会让孩子们感到不舒服。

虚拟语气:
If only the situation weren't so discomfiting. = 如果情况没那么令人不安就好了。
I wish my boss's criticism wouldn't discomfit me so much. = 我希望老板的批评不会让我这么不安。

被动语态:
He was discomfited by her sharp words. = 他被她尖刻的话语弄得不安。
The team was discomfited by the unexpected loss. =  团队因意外的失败而感到沮丧。

疑问句:
Does this situation discomfit you? =  这种情况让你不安吗？
What discomfits you the most about this job? = 这份工作最让你不安的是什么？

否定句:
The news didn't discomfit her at all. = 这个消息一点也没有让她不安。
The presentation didn't discomfit him; he remained calm. = 演示并没有让他不安；他保持冷静。
