flying-out: /ˈflaɪɪŋ aʊt/ | v. |飞出，出发 | The act of leaving by air; departing on a flight.  It describes the process of departing from a place via air travel. | 例句：The family is flying out to Hawaii tomorrow. = 这家人明天将飞往夏威夷。

The plane is flying out at 6:00 AM. = 飞机将于早上6点起飞。


近义词：departing, leaving, taking off, setting off | 反义词：flying in, arriving


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They fly out every Tuesday. = 他们每周二都飞出去。
We fly out on business trips regularly. = 我们定期出差。


一般过去时:
They flew out yesterday. = 他们昨天飞走了。
We flew out last week for a holiday. = 我们上周出去度假了。


现在完成时:
They have flown out already. = 他们已经飞走了。
I have flown out to see my family several times this year. = 今年我已经飞出去见过几次家人了。


过去完成时:
They had flown out before I called. = 我打电话之前他们已经飞走了。
She had flown out to London before the meeting. = 会议之前她已经飞往伦敦了。


将来时:
They will fly out next week. = 他们下周将飞出去。
We will fly out as soon as the tickets are confirmed. = 一旦机票确认，我们就飞出去。


虚拟语气:
If I had the money, I would fly out to see her. = 如果我有钱，我会飞去看她。
I wish I could fly out right now. = 我真希望我现在就能飞出去。


被动语态:
The flight was flown out on time. = 飞机准时起飞了。(This usage is less common for "flying out" which is more often active.)
The passengers were flown out to a safer location after the storm. = 暴风雨过后，乘客们被空运到一个更安全的地方。(This highlights the action of being transported by flight)


疑问句:
When are they flying out? = 他们什么时候飞出去？
Are you flying out this weekend? = 你这个周末飞出去吗？


否定句:
They are not flying out today. = 他们今天不飞出去。
We did not fly out because of the bad weather. = 由于天气不好，我们没有飞出去。
