hallow:/ˈhæləʊ/ | vt. n. adj. | 使神圣；尊敬；圣洁的；万圣节前夜 | to make holy; to consecrate; to honor as holy;  the evening before All Saints' Day | 例句：The priest hallowed the ground. 牧师使这块土地神圣化了。The hallowed halls of learning.  知识的圣殿。  | 近义词：consecrate, sanctify, bless | 反义词：desecrate, profane


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hallows the Sabbath. = 他遵守安息日。
We hallow the memory of our ancestors. = 我们崇敬祖先的记忆。


一般过去时:
They hallowed the new church. = 他们使新的教堂神圣化。
The king hallowed the treaty. = 国王使条约神圣化。


现在完成时:
She has hallowed the place with her presence. = 她的到来使这个地方变得神圣。
They have hallowed the tradition for generations. = 他们世世代代传承着这个传统。


过去完成时:
The people had hallowed the site long before the church was built. = 在教堂建成很久以前，人们就已将这处地点视为圣地。
They had hallowed the agreement with a solemn oath. = 他们用庄严的誓言使协议神圣化。

We will hallow this day with a special ceremony. = 我们将以特殊的仪式来纪念这一天。


虚拟语气:
If we hallowed this tradition, we would preserve our heritage. = 如果我们遵守这个传统，我们就能保护我们的遗产。
I wish they had hallowed their promise. = 我真希望他们遵守了他们的诺言。


被动语态:
