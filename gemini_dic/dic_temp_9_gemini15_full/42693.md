counterterm: /ˈkaʊntərtɜːrm/ | n. |反项；对立词；对抗性术语| A counterterm is a term that opposes or contradicts another term, often used in legal or technical contexts to describe a provision or clause that negates or limits the effect of another provision.  It's often used in contracts, legislation, or technical specifications to balance or limit the scope of a previously stated term. | Example: The contract included a counterterm that limited the company's liability in case of unforeseen circumstances.  这个合同包含一个反项条款，限制了公司在不可预见的情况下承担的责任。|近义词：contradictory term, opposing term, qualifying term |反义词：confirming term, supporting term |名词
