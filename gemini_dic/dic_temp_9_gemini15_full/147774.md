overpromises:/ˌoʊvərˈprɑːmɪsɪz/ | v. |  许诺过多；夸大其词 | To promise more than one can deliver or is likely to deliver.  This often involves exaggerating one's capabilities or the likelihood of success. |  He overpromises and underdelivers, which has damaged his credibility. 他夸下海口却做不到，这损害了他的信誉。


The company overpromises on its new product's capabilities. 这家公司对其新产品的性能夸夸其谈。 |近义词: exaggerate, boast, hyperbolize |反义词: underpromise, be realistic

词源:
The word "overpromise" is a combination of the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") and the verb "promise."  It's a relatively modern word, emerging as a natural extension of the existing vocabulary.
