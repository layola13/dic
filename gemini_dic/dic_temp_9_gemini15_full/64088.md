epode:/ˈiːpoʊd/| n.| 尾声；第三段；(诗歌中的)第三部分 |A concluding part of an ode or other poem, typically shorter than the preceding strophes and of a different meter.  An epode is often a reflective or summarizing section that follows a more lyrical or dramatic part.  In classical Greek lyric poetry, an epode was the third part of a triadic structure (strophe, antistrophe, epode).|The poem's epode offered a poignant reflection on the events described in the preceding stanzas.|近义词：conclusion, coda, postlude|反义词：introduction, prelude
