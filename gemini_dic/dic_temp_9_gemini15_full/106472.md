kowtowing: /ˈkaʊˌtoʊɪŋ/ | v. | 叩头；卑躬屈膝；奴颜婢膝 |  To show exaggerated deference or servility; to act in an excessively submissive way.  | 例句：He was constantly kowtowing to his boss, hoping for a promotion. 他不断地对老板卑躬屈膝，希望能得到晋升。

例句：The company's kowtowing to the demands of its shareholders ultimately hurt its long-term prospects. 公司对股东要求的卑躬屈膝最终损害了它的长期前景。


近义词：fawning, cringing, groveling,  obsequiousness, servility

反义词：defiance,  rebellion,  insubordination, assertiveness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He kowtows to his superiors. = 他对上司卑躬屈膝。
They kowtow to the market trends. = 他们对市场趋势唯命是从。


一般过去时:
He kowtowed to the emperor. = 他向皇帝叩头。
She kowtowed to pressure from her family. = 她屈服于家庭的压力。


现在完成时:
They have kowtowed to every whim of the client. = 他们一直对客户的每一个要求都唯命是从。
He has kowtowed to authority all his life. = 他一生都对权威卑躬屈膝。


过去完成时:
He had kowtowed to the dictator for years. = 他多年来一直对独裁者卑躬屈膝。
They had kowtowed to the demands of the powerful before. = 他们以前曾屈服于强权的要求。


将来时:
He will kowtow to his new boss. = 他将对他的新老板卑躬屈膝。
She will not kowtow to unfair treatment. = 她不会屈服于不公平的待遇。


虚拟语气:
If he wanted the job, he would kowtow to them. = 如果他想要这份工作，他会对他们卑躬屈膝。
I wish I hadn't kowtowed to their demands. = 我真希望我没有屈服于他们的要求。


被动语态:
His ideas were kowtowed to by the committee. = 他的想法被委员会采纳了（可以理解为一种过度顺从）。
The company's decisions were kowtowed to by its employees. = 公司的决定受到了员工的过度顺从。


疑问句:
Does he kowtow to anyone? = 他对谁卑躬屈膝？
Why does she kowtow to their unreasonable demands? = 她为什么对他们不合理的要求卑躬屈膝？


否定句:
He doesn't kowtow to anyone. = 他不向任何人卑躬屈膝。
She didn't kowtow to the pressure. = 她没有屈服于压力。


PHRASE->
kowtowing to authority = 对权威卑躬屈膝
kowtowing to pressure = 屈服于压力
kowtowing to the market = 迎合市场
kowtowing to public opinion = 迎合公众舆论
kowtowing to fashion = 盲目追求时尚

词源:
From Chinese  磕头 (kētóu), literally "knock head".  The word entered English in the 19th century, reflecting the practice of bowing deeply with the forehead touching the ground as a sign of respect or submission in Chinese culture.
