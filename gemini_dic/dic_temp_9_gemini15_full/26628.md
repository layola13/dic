cackle: /ˈkækəl/ | v., n. |咯咯笑；（尤指鸡的）咯咯叫声 | To make a series of short, sharp, high-pitched sounds, typically those of a hen or a person laughing.  |近义词：chuckle, giggle, crow, squawk | 反义词：sob, weep


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The hens cackle loudly in the morning. = 母鸡早上大声咯咯叫。
She cackles whenever she hears a joke. = 她每当听到笑话就咯咯地笑。


一般过去时:
The chickens cackled all night. = 鸡整夜都在咯咯叫。
She cackled with delight at the news. = 她听到这个消息高兴地咯咯笑。


现在完成时:
The old woman has cackled her way through life. = 这位老妇人一生都咯咯地笑着度过。
They have cackled about the incident for hours. = 他们就这件事咯咯地笑了好几个小时。


过去完成时:
The children had cackled before they fell asleep. = 孩子们睡着前咯咯地笑了一阵。
She had cackled at his clumsy attempt. = 她对他笨拙的尝试咯咯地笑了起来。


将来时:
The audience will cackle at the comedian's jokes. = 观众会对着喜剧演员的笑话咯咯地笑。
The children will cackle with glee when they receive their presents. = 孩子们收到礼物时会高兴地咯咯笑。


虚拟语气:
If she told that joke, they would cackle. = 如果她讲那个笑话，他们会咯咯地笑。
I wish I could cackle like that. = 我真希望我能像那样咯咯地笑。


被动语态:
Her jokes were cackled at by everyone. = 她的笑话让每个人都咯咯地笑。
(Note:  Passive voice is less common with "cackle".)


疑问句:
Did you hear the hens cackling? = 你听到母鸡咯咯叫了吗？
Why are they cackling so loudly? = 他们为什么这么大声地咯咯叫？


否定句:
The audience didn't cackle at his jokes. = 观众没有因为他讲的笑话而咯咯地笑。
She didn't cackle; she just smiled politely. = 她没有咯咯地笑；她只是礼貌地笑了笑。


PHRASE->
a cackle of laughter = 一阵咯咯的笑声
dry cackle = 干巴巴的笑声
nervous cackle = 紧张的咯咯笑

hysterical cackle =歇斯底里的咯咯笑


词源:
The word "cackle" comes from the Middle English word *kakelen*, which is an onomatopoeia—a word that imitates the sound it represents.  Its ultimate origins are likely Proto-Germanic.
