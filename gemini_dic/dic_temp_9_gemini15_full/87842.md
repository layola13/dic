hide-and-seek:/haɪd ænd siːk/| n.|捉迷藏|A children's game in which one or more players hide and the others search for them.  This game involves concealing oneself and then being found.|例句：The children were playing hide-and-seek in the park.孩子们在公园里玩捉迷藏。

例句：My little sister loves playing hide-and-seek with me. 我妹妹喜欢和我玩捉迷藏。


近义词：hide-and-go-seek| 反义词：None| 词性:Noun

词源:
The term "hide-and-seek" is a straightforward compound of the verbs "hide" and "seek," reflecting the core actions of the game.  "Hide" originates from Old English *hidan, meaning "to conceal," while "seek" comes from Old English *sēcan, meaning "to search for."  The combination naturally evolved to describe this popular children's game.
