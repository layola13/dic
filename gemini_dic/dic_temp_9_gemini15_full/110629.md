limpin: /ˈlɪmpɪn/ | adj. | 跛行的；蹒跚的 |  Describes a manner of walking that is uneven and unsteady, typically due to injury or weakness in a leg or foot.  | Example: The dog limped painfully after the accident.  这只狗事故后疼痛地跛行。  |近义词: lame, halting, hobbling | 反义词:  striding, walking normally


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He limps every time he walks. = 他每次走路都跛行。
The horse is limping after the race. = 赛马之后，马跛了。


一般过去时:
She limped home after twisting her ankle. = 她扭伤脚踝后一瘸一拐地回家了。
The injured soldier limped through the forest. = 受伤的士兵一瘸一拐地穿过森林。


现在完成时:
He has limped since the fall. = 他从摔倒后就一直跛着脚。
She has been limping for days. = 她已经跛了好几天了。


过去完成时:
He had limped for miles before he found help. = 他在找到帮助之前已经跛行了数英里。
The runner had limped across the finish line. = 赛跑者一瘸一拐地跑过终点线。


将来时:
I think he will limp for a few weeks. = 我想他会跛几周。
She will likely limp until the swelling goes down. = 她可能会跛行直到肿胀消退。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't injured his leg, he wouldn't be limping now. = 如果他没有伤到腿，他现在就不会跛了。
I wish I wasn't limping so much. = 我希望我不那么跛。


被动语态: (很少用被动语态)
His gait was described as limping. = 他的步态被形容为跛行。
The limp was noticed by everyone.  = 大家都注意到了他的跛行。


疑问句:
Is he limping because of his injured knee? = 他是因为膝盖受伤才跛的吗？
Why is the dog limping? = 为什么狗在跛行？


否定句:
He doesn't limp anymore. = 他不再跛了。
She didn't limp after her recovery. = 她康复后就不再跛了。


PHRASE->
limp along: 艰难地前进
limp home: 一瘸一拐地回家
limp with pain: 痛苦地跛行
a limping gait: 跛行步态
limp through: 艰难地完成


词源:
The word "limp" ultimately derives from the Old English word "limpan," meaning "to be weak, flabby, soft."  Over time, its meaning shifted to describe a weakened or unsteady gait.
