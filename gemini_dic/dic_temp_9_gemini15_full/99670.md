interlinearly:/ˌɪntərˈlɪniərli/| adv. |逐行地；行间地|In linguistics, interlinearly refers to a method of presenting a text where a translation or gloss is placed directly below or above each line of the original text. This allows for a direct comparison between the original and the translated version, making it easier to understand the meaning and structure of the text.  |例句：The ancient text was translated interlinearly, with the original language above and the English translation below.|近义词：line by line, word for word|反义词：None directly applicable.  The opposite would be a non-linear or parallel translation presentation.


USAGE->
This adverb does not readily lend itself to the typical tense variations of verbs like "to be" or "to do."  Its usage is primarily descriptive of a method of translation or presentation.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  The following examples illustrate its usage in different sentence structures:

Declarative Sentences:
The professor presented the text interlinearly. = 教授逐行地呈现了文本。
They translated the poem interlinearly for easier comprehension. = 为了更容易理解，他们逐行地翻译了这首诗。


Negative Sentences:
The document was not translated interlinearly. = 这份文件没有逐行翻译。
The students did not present their work interlinearly. = 学生们没有逐行展示他们的作品。


Question Sentences:
Was the passage translated interlinearly? = 这段文字是逐行翻译的吗？
Did they present the data interlinearly? = 他们逐行呈现数据了吗？



PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "interlinearly." Its usage is highly specific to linguistic analysis and translation.
