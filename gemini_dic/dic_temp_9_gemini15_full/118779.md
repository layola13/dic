metalloidal:/ˌmɛtəˈlɔɪdəl/ | adj. |类金属的；准金属的 |  Possessing properties intermediate between those of metals and nonmetals. Metalloids, also known as semimetals, are elements that exhibit characteristics of both metals and nonmetals.  They are typically semiconductors and have variable electrical conductivity depending on factors like temperature and pressure. |  Silicon is a metalloidal element crucial in electronics.  |近义词: semimetallic | 反义词: metallic, nonmetallic
