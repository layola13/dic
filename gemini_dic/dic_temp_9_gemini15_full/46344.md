dactyl:/ˈdæktɪl/ | n. |音步(诗歌韵律单位)；指节|A metrical foot consisting of one stressed syllable followed by two unstressed syllables.  It is a common metrical foot in poetry.  For example, the word "poetry" itself is a dactyl. | Example: The poem's rhythm is based on a pattern of dactyls. 这首诗的节奏基于一系列的音步。|近义词:  trochee (反义词)|反义词: spondee, iamb


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb or adjective forms.  Therefore, typical verb tenses, passive voice, etc., are not applicable.  The usage focuses on its application in describing poetic meter.

Example 1:  The line "Highly important" is a dactylic line.  这句“高度重要”是一句达克提尔诗句。

Example 2:  Analyzing the poem's metrical structure revealed a preponderance of dactyls. 分析这首诗的韵律结构揭示了大量音步的使用。

PHRASE->
dactylic hexameter:  达克提尔六步格 (a line of verse with six dactyls)
dactylic rhythm:  达克提尔节奏 (rhythmic pattern based on dactyls)
dactylic verse: 达克提尔诗歌 (verse written in dactyls)
dactyllic tetrameter: 达克提尔四步格 (a line of verse with four dactyls)
dactylic pentameter: 达克提尔五步格 (a line of verse with five dactyls)
