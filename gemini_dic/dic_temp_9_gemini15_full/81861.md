gridlock:/ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk/|/ˈɡrɪdˌlɑːk/| n. |交通堵塞；僵局；停滞 |A state of total standstill, typically of traffic, caused by congestion.  It can also refer to a situation where no progress can be made due to disagreement. |例句：The city center was in gridlock during rush hour.  = 高峰时段市中心交通严重堵塞。

The negotiations reached a gridlock; neither side was willing to compromise. = 谈判陷入僵局；双方都不愿妥协。|近义词：traffic jam, standstill, deadlock, impasse |反义词：free flow, progress, resolution |名词
