holisms:/ˈhəʊlɪzəm/| n. |整体论，整体主义|The philosophical concept that emphasizes the importance of considering the whole rather than its individual parts.  Holism stresses that the properties of a system are not simply the sum of its parts, but emerge from the interactions between them.  It is often applied to various fields, including biology, ecology, psychology, and philosophy.|例句：The study adopted a holistic approach, considering both the individual's mental and physical health.|近义词：wholeness, integration, entirety |反义词：reductionism, atomism
