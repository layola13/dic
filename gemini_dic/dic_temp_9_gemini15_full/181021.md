rein: /reɪn/ | n., v. | 缰绳；控制；统治；抑制 |

英文解析：A rein is a strap attached to a bit in a horse's mouth, used by a rider to control the horse.  As a verb, it means to control or restrain something or someone.

例句：
The rider pulled on the reins to stop the horse. = 骑手拉紧缰绳以阻止马匹前进。

He tried to rein in his anger. = 他试图控制自己的怒火。


近义词： (n.) bridle, strap; (v.) control, curb, restrain, govern

反义词： (v.) unleash, release, free


词源：From Old French *reine, from Vulgar Latin *retena, diminutive of Latin retinere "to hold back".
