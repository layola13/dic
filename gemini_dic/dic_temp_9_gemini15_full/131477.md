noncoincidence:/ˌnɒnkəʊˈɪnsɪdəns/| n. |非巧合；并非偶然 |The striking similarities between the two events were clearly a noncoincidence, suggesting a deliberate connection.  两起事件之间惊人的相似之处显然并非巧合，表明存在某种故意联系。|近义词：coincidence,  correlation,  connection |反义词：coincidence, accident, chance


USAGE->

This word is primarily a noun and doesn't lend itself to the grammatical tenses and moods in the same way as verbs.  The following examples demonstrate its usage in different sentence structures:


Declarative Sentence:
The discovery was a noncoincidence; it was the result of meticulous planning.  = 这一发现并非偶然；它是精心策划的结果。
Their meeting wasn't a noncoincidence; they had arranged it beforehand. = 他们的相遇并非偶然；他们事先安排好了。


Interrogative Sentence:
Was the timing of the events a noncoincidence? = 这些事件的时间安排是巧合吗？（反问，暗示并非巧合）
Could the similarities be explained as anything other than a noncoincidence? = 这些相似之处能否解释为非巧合？


Exclamatory Sentence:
What a noncoincidence! They both chose the same restaurant! = 真巧！他们俩都选择了同一家餐馆！（表达惊讶，暗示并非偶然的巧合）
A noncoincidence!  The evidence points to a conspiracy. =  并非巧合！证据指向阴谋。


PHRASE->

There aren't common phrases specifically using "noncoincidence," as it's not a frequently used word.  However, we can create phrases that convey similar meanings:

A planned event: 一个计划好的事件。
