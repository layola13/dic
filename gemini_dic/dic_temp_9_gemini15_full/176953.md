realleges:/riˈælɪdʒɪz/ | vt. | 再指控；重新指控 | To accuse someone again of something.  This implies a previous accusation, either by the same person or a different one.  The act of re-alleging often happens after new evidence emerges or the initial accusation was dismissed. |例句：The prosecutor realleges the defendant's involvement in the robbery after finding new evidence.  检察官在发现新证据后再次指控被告参与了抢劫案。

The victim realleged the assault, providing more detailed information to the police. 受害者再次指控袭击事件，并向警方提供了更详细的信息。


近义词：re-accuse, reiterate the accusation, reaffirm the charge | 反义词：acquit, exonerate | 词性:动词

词源:  The word "realleges" is formed by adding the prefix "re-" (meaning "again" or "anew") to the verb "alleges," which comes from the Old French "aleger" (to allege) and ultimately from the Latin "ad-legare" (to appoint, to send). Therefore, "realleges" literally means "to allege again."
