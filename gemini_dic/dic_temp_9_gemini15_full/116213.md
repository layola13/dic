masochists:/ˈmæsəkɪsts/| n. |受虐狂；自虐者 |A masochist is a person who derives sexual gratification from suffering pain or humiliation.  The term often extends beyond sexual contexts to describe someone who enjoys activities that cause them discomfort or pain. |例句：Some people mistakenly believe all masochists are sexually motivated, but the term can describe anyone who enjoys self-inflicted pain. 一些人错误地认为所有受虐狂都是出于性动机，但这个词可以用来形容任何喜欢自虐的人。

例句：He's a masochist; he enjoys the pressure of working long hours under intense deadlines. 他是个受虐狂；他喜欢在紧张的最后期限下长时间工作带来的压力。


近义词：self-punisher, sadist (in a specific, reciprocal context)

反义词：sadist


词源:The word "masochist" is derived from the name of Leopold von Sacher-Masoch (1836-1895), an Austrian novelist whose works frequently featured characters who derived pleasure from pain and humiliation.  His name became synonymous with this type of behavior.
