formost:/ˈfɔːrməʊst/| adj.|最重要的；最显著的；最先的 |most important or prominent; foremost in importance or position|The foremost expert in the field.  这位领域的首席专家。


The foremost runner crossed the finish line first. 最先的跑步者第一个冲过终点线。

近义词：leading, principal, chief, primary, main

反义词：least important, last, secondary


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is foremost among his peers. 他在同龄人中首屈一指。
This issue is foremost in our minds. 这个问题是我们最关心的。


一般过去时:
She was foremost in the fight for women's rights. 她始终走在争取妇女权利的最前列。
He was foremost in his class. 他是班上最优秀的学生。


现在完成时:
He has been foremost in promoting the project. 他一直是这个项目最主要的推动者。
She has remained foremost in her field. 她一直是她所在领域的佼佼者。


过去完成时:
He had been foremost in the debate before the final decision. 在最终决定之前，他一直是辩论中的主要人物。
They had been foremost in supporting the cause. 在支持这项事业方面，他们一直走在最前列。


将来时:
She will be foremost in the upcoming election. 她将在即将到来的选举中处于领先地位。
He will foremost among the candidates. 他将在候选人中名列前茅。


虚拟语气:
If he were foremost in his field, he would have more opportunities. 如果他在他的领域处于领先地位，他就会有更多机会。
If she were foremost in the competition, she would win. 如果她在比赛中名列前茅，她就会获胜。


被动语态:
He was considered foremost among the scientists. 他被认为是科学家中的佼佼者。
The issue was deemed foremost by the committee. 委员会认为这个问题最为重要。


疑问句:
Is this project foremost in your priorities? 这个项目是你优先考虑的事项吗？
Who is foremost among the contenders? 谁是竞争者中的佼佼者？


否定句:
This concern is not foremost on our agenda. 这个问题不在我们议程的首位。
He was not foremost in the race. 他在比赛中没有领先。
