dueler:/ˈduːlər/| n. |决斗者，争斗者 |A person who engages in a duel.  Someone who enjoys or is skilled in argument or competition. |例句：The two duelers faced each other, pistols drawn.  两位决斗者面对面站着，拔出了手枪。

The skilled debater was a dueler with words.  这位熟练的辩论家是一位言语上的斗士。|近义词：combatant, fighter, antagonist |反义词：peacemaker, mediator


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He duels with his rivals regularly. = 他经常和他的对手决斗。
She duels with her colleagues over ideas. = 她经常和同事就想法展开争论。


一般过去时:
They dueled at dawn. = 他们在黎明时分决斗。
He dueled with his opponent for hours. =他和他的对手争论了几个小时。


现在完成时:
He has dueled many times in his life. = 他一生中决斗过很多次。
She has dueled with the same opponent many times. = 她已经和同一个对手争论过很多次了。


过去完成时:
They had dueled before, so they knew each other's fighting styles. = 他们以前就决斗过，所以他们了解彼此的战斗风格。
She had dueled with him for years before they finally agreed. = 在他们最终达成一致之前，她和他争论了多年。


将来时:
They will duel tomorrow morning. = 他们明天早上将决斗。
He will duel with his arch-rival in the upcoming debate. = 他将在即将到来的辩论中与他的死对头进行辩论。


虚拟语气:
If he were to duel, he would choose a rapier. = 如果他要决斗，他会选择一把细剑。

被动语态:

疑问句:
