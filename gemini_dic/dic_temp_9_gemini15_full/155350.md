pharmacologist:/ˌfɑːrməˈkɒlədʒɪst/|/ˈfɑːrməkɑːlədʒɪst/| n. |药理学家|A pharmacologist is a scientist who studies the effects of drugs on living organisms.  They conduct research on how drugs are absorbed, distributed, metabolized, and excreted by the body, as well as their mechanisms of action and therapeutic effects.  Pharmacologists also investigate the adverse effects of drugs and develop new drugs and therapies.|Example: The pharmacologist presented her findings on the new drug's efficacy at the conference.  药理学家在会议上介绍了她对这种新药疗效的研究结果。
