hyperintellectualness: /ˌhaɪpərɪnˌtɛləkˈtʃuələnəs/ | n. | 过度卖弄知识；卖弄学问；炫耀智力 |  The excessive display or ostentatious use of intellectual knowledge or ability, often to an irritating or pretentious degree. It suggests an overemphasis on intellect to the point of being artificial or unnatural.  |  His hyperintellectualness made him unpopular with his colleagues. 他过度的卖弄学问使他在同事中不受欢迎。

词源:

The word is a modern coinage, formed by combining:

* **hyper-**:  A prefix meaning "excessive," "over," or "beyond."
* **intellectual**:  Relating to the intellect or the ability to think and reason.
* **-ness**: A suffix forming nouns of state or condition.

Therefore, "hyperintellectualness" literally means "the state or condition of being excessively intellectual."
