panders:/ˈpændərz/| vt. n.| 迎合；逢迎；拉皮条；| To gratify or indulge the desires of (someone), especially in a way that is morally wrong or base.  To act as a panderer; procure. | 例句：The magazine panders to the lowest instincts. 这本杂志迎合最低级的本能。

He was arrested for pandering to underage girls. 他因拉皮条给未成年女孩而被捕。


近义词：cater to, indulge, gratify, appease,  (for the n. meaning) procurer, pimp

反义词：resist, oppose, defy,  (for the n. meaning)  None directly opposite, but words suggesting moral integrity could be used in contrast.


词性:动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He panders to the wealthy. = 他迎合富人。
The newspaper panders to its readers' prejudices. = 这家报纸迎合读者的偏见。


一般过去时:
The politician pandered to the public's fear. = 这位政客迎合了公众的恐惧。
She pandered to his every whim. = 她满足了他的每一个怪癖。


现在完成时:
The company has pandered to the market demands for years. = 多年来，该公司一直在迎合市场需求。
He has pandered to his own vanity. = 他一直迎合自己的虚荣心。


过去完成时:
They had pandered to the dictator's ego for decades. = 几十年来，他们一直在迎合独裁者的自负。
The media had pandered to the public's sensationalism. = 媒体迎合了公众的猎奇心理。


将来时:
She will pander to the crowd's desire for entertainment. = 她将迎合人群对娱乐的渴望。
The government will pander to the demands of big business. = 政府将迎合大企业的需求。


虚拟语气:
If the candidate pandered to the voters, he might win the election. = 如果候选人迎合选民，他可能会赢得选举。
I wish the media wouldn't pander to violence. = 我希望媒体不要迎合暴力。


被动语态:
The public's desires are pandered to by the media. = 公众的愿望被媒体迎合了。
His vanity was pandered to by his entourage. = 他的虚荣心被他的随从们迎合了。


疑问句:

Does he pander to the elite? = 他是否迎合精英？


否定句:
The artist doesn't pander to popular taste. = 这位艺术家不迎合大众口味。
The journalist refused to pander to the government's propaganda. = 记者拒绝迎合政府的宣传。


PHRASE->
pander to the masses: 迎合大众

词源:
The word "pander" ultimately derives from the Greek word "pandēros," which referred to a procurer or pimp in ancient Greek literature.  The word's evolution through various languages eventually led to its modern English usage.
