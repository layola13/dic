revalued:/ˌriːˈvæljuːd/| v. |重新估价；重新评价|To revalue something means to assess its worth again, often due to changed circumstances or new information. This can apply to assets, currencies, or even personal values.  |例句：The company revalued its assets after the market downturn. 公司在市场低迷后重新评估了其资产。

The government revalued the currency to combat inflation. 政府重新评估货币以对抗通货膨胀。|近义词： reassess, reappraise, reevaluated |反义词：devalue

一般现在时:
The company revalues its assets annually. = 公司每年重新评估其资产。
He revalues his priorities every few years. = 他每隔几年重新评估他的优先事项。

一般过去时:

过去完成时:
