cripplers: /ˈkrɪplərz/ | n. | 使残废的人或事物；严重削弱…的人或事物 | Something or someone that cripples; something that severely weakens or disables.  |例句：The new regulations are considered cripplers to small businesses.  新的规章制度被认为严重损害了小型企业。|近义词：disablers, debilitators, wreckers, destroyers |反义词：enhancers, strengtheners, supporters


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The economic sanctions cripple the country's economy. = 经济制裁使该国经济瘫痪。
These policies cripple the ability of small businesses to thrive. = 这些政策削弱了小型企业蓬勃发展的能力。


一般过去时:
The accident crippled him for life. = 那次事故使他终身残疾。
The storm crippled transportation across the region. = 暴风雨使该地区的交通瘫痪。


现在完成时:
Years of war have crippled the nation's infrastructure. = 多年的战争已经严重破坏了国家的基础设施。
The drought has crippled the agricultural sector. = 干旱已经严重打击了农业部门。


过去完成时:
The company had been crippled by debt before the merger. = 在合并之前，该公司已经被债务拖垮了。
The repeated attacks had crippled the enemy's defenses. = 反复的攻击已经摧毁了敌人的防御工事。


将来时:
The new tax law will cripple many small businesses. = 新的税法将使许多小型企业瘫痪。
The proposed cuts will cripple essential public services. = 拟议中的削减将使重要的公共服务瘫痪。


虚拟语气:
If the project was not properly funded, it would be crippled. = 如果该项目没有得到适当的资金支持，它就会瘫痪。
I wish the new regulations hadn't crippled our business. = 我真希望新的规章制度没有损害我们的生意。


被动语态:
The city's infrastructure was crippled by the earthquake. = 城市的基础设施在地震中被摧毁了。
The team's performance was crippled by injuries. = 球队的表现因伤病而受损。


疑问句:
What crippled the negotiations? = 什么导致谈判破裂？
What factors are crippling the economy? = 哪些因素正在严重损害经济？


否定句:
The reforms did not cripple the healthcare system. = 改革并没有使医疗体系瘫痪。
The new software did not cripple the computer's performance. = 新软件并没有影响计算机的性能。
