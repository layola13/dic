presentment:/prɪˈzentmənt/ | n. |呈递；提交；提出；票据；（尤指对犯罪的）指控 |The formal act of presenting something, especially a document or accusation.  It can also refer to a bill of exchange or other negotiable instrument. |例句：The presentment of the evidence was crucial to the case.  证据的呈递对案件至关重要。

The presentment of the cheque was delayed. 支票的提交被延迟了。


近义词：submission, presentation, indictment, accusation | 反义词：withdrawal, retraction


词源：源自古法语 *presentment，意为“呈现”，来自拉丁语 *praesentare，由 *prae-（在…之前）和 *praesentare（呈现）构成。


用法说明：


名词用法：


1. 提交、呈递：指正式地提交文件、证据或其他物品。


例句：The jury heard the presentment of the prosecution's case.  陪审团听取了检方陈述案情。


例句：The bank refused the presentment of the forged cheque. 银行拒收伪造支票。


2. 提出（建议、观点等）：指正式地提出建议、观点或意见。


例句：The committee considered the presentment of several new proposals.  委员会考虑了几个新的提案。


例句：Her presentment of the problem was clear and concise. 她对问题的陈述清晰简洁。


3. （尤指对犯罪的）指控：指控某人犯有罪行。


例句：The grand jury returned a presentment against the defendant. 大陪审团对被告人提出了起诉。


例句：The presentment detailed the charges against the accused. 起诉书详细列明了对被告的指控。


4. 票据（尤指汇票）：指一种可以转让的票据，通常用于商业交易。


例句：The presentment of the bill of exchange was due on the 15th. 汇票到期日为15日。


例句：He accepted the presentment of the bill without question. 他毫无疑问地接受了汇票。


注意：在法律语境中，“presentment”通常指大陪审团对犯罪的指控，而不是指控本身。  在商业语境中，它通常指商业票据的提交。
