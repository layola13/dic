reductionisms:/rɪˈdʌkʃənɪzəm/| n.|还原论；简化论|The approach of reducing complex systems to simpler components to understand them.  It often involves explaining a phenomenon in terms of its constituent parts and their interactions, neglecting emergent properties.|Example:  His analysis of the social issue was criticized for its reductionism, ignoring the complex interplay of factors.|近义词：simplification, oversimplification|反义词：holism, comprehensiveness
