satirist:/ˈsætərɪst/| n. |讽刺作家；讽刺家 |A satirist is a writer who uses satire, a literary genre using humor, irony, exaggeration, or ridicule to expose and criticize folly or vice.  Satirists often target political figures, social institutions, or human behavior. | Example:  Jonathan Swift was a renowned satirist known for his biting wit and social commentary.  乔纳森·斯威夫特是一位以其辛辣的智慧和社会评论而闻名的著名讽刺作家。  |近义词：humorist, wit, caricaturist |反义词：praiser, flatterer |名词

词源:
The word "satirist" is derived from the Latin word "satura," meaning "a medley" or "a mixed dish."  This reflects the diverse elements often found in satirical works.  The word evolved through French "satirique" before entering English.
