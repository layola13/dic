cashflow:/ˈkæʃfloʊ/ | n. |现金流，现金流量 |The movement of money into and out of a business.  It refers to the difference between cash coming into and going out of a business during a specific period.  Positive cash flow indicates more money coming in than going out, while negative cash flow indicates the opposite. |例句：The company's cash flow improved significantly after the new product launch.  这家公司的新产品发布后，现金流有了显著改善。

例句：  We need to improve our cash flow to avoid financial difficulties. 我们需要改善现金流以避免财务困难。


近义词：cash movement, cash receipts and disbursements | 反义词：cash deficit, negative cash flow | 词性：名词

词源:
The word "cash flow" is a compound noun.  "Cash" refers to readily available money, and "flow" refers to the continuous movement of something. The term arose naturally from the need to describe the continuous movement of money in and out of a business.  There is no single etymological event marking its origin; rather, it's a semantic development reflecting the increasing importance of financial management in business.
