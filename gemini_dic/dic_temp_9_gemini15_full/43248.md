cracks:/kræk/| n. v.|裂缝；破裂；爆裂声；（声音）变得沙哑；（嗓子）嘶哑；玩笑；尝试；破解；|a line on a surface along which something has broken but not separated completely; a sudden, sharp sound, typically made by something breaking;  to break or cause to break|例句：There were cracks in the wall. 墙上出现了裂缝。The vase cracked when it fell. 花瓶掉下来的时候碎了。|近义词：fissure, split, fracture, snap, break|反义词：mend, repair, fix


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The vase cracks under pressure. = 花瓶在压力下破裂。
He cracks jokes all the time. = 他总是开玩笑。


一般过去时:
The ice cracked under his weight. = 冰在他体重下裂开了。
She cracked her knuckles before starting to type. = 她开始打字前掰了掰手指关节。


现在完成时:
The old building has cracked in several places. = 这座旧建筑物在几个地方都裂开了。
He has cracked the code. = 他破译了密码。


过去完成时:
The foundation had cracked before the earthquake. = 地震前地基就已开裂了。
She had cracked under the pressure of the job. = 她无法承受工作压力而崩溃了。


将来时:

I think the deal will crack soon. = 我认为这笔交易很快就会达成。


虚拟语气:

If he had tried harder, he might have cracked the problem. = 如果他再努力一些，他也许就能解决这个问题了。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:

The word "crack" comes from the Old English word *cracian, meaning "to creak" or "to crackle".  It's related to words in other Germanic languages with similar meanings.  The sense of "to break" developed later, but is now the most common usage.
