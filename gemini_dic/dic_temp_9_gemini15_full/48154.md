decommercialization: /ˌdiːkəˈmɜːrsəlʌɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 去商业化；非商业化 | The process of removing something from commercial influence or reducing its commercial aspects.  It often involves shifting focus from profit maximization to other values, such as social good or environmental protection. | Example: The decommercialization of healthcare aims to make essential medical services accessible to everyone regardless of their ability to pay.  去商业化医疗保健旨在使每个人都能获得必要的医疗服务，而不管他们的支付能力如何。


近义词：discommercialization, non-commercialization

反义词：commercialization


词性：名词
