enhearten:/ɪnˈhɑːrtən/| vt.|鼓励；使振奋|To give courage or hope to; encourage.  The good news enheartened the team.|近义词：encourage, inspire, embolden|反义词：discourage, dispirit, depress|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news enheartens me. = 这个消息使我振奋。
His support enheartens her to pursue her dreams. = 他的支持鼓励她追求梦想。


一般过去时:
The coach's words enheartened the players before the game. = 教练的话在比赛前鼓舞了队员们。
The unexpected victory enheartened the entire community. = 这场意外的胜利鼓舞了整个社区。


现在完成时:
His unwavering belief has enheartened me throughout the difficult times. = 他坚定不移的信念在我度过难关的过程中一直鼓励着我。
Recent breakthroughs in research have enheartened scientists working on a cure. = 最近的研究突破鼓舞了致力于寻找治愈方法的科学家们。


过去完成时:
The previous success had enheartened them to take on a more challenging project. = 此前的成功鼓舞了他们去承担一个更具挑战性的项目。
Knowing that others had overcome similar difficulties had enheartened her. = 知道其他人克服了类似的困难使她感到鼓舞。


将来时:
This new strategy will enhearten the troops. = 这项新战略将鼓舞士气。
The upcoming conference will enhearten the participants. = 即将召开的会议将鼓舞与会者。


虚拟语气:
If only his words had enheartened me earlier. = 要是他早点鼓励我该多好。
I wish the positive feedback had enheartened them more. = 我希望积极的反馈能更多地鼓励他们。


被动语态:
The team was enheartened by the coach's speech. = 球队受到了教练讲话的鼓舞。
The struggling entrepreneur was enheartened by the investor's interest. = 这位苦苦挣扎的企业家受到了投资者兴趣的鼓舞。


疑问句:
Does his support enhearten you? = 他的支持是否让你振奋？
Will this news enhearten the public? = 这个消息会鼓舞公众吗？


否定句:
The criticism didn't enhearten him. = 批评并没有鼓励他。
The lack of progress didn't enhearten the team. = 缺乏进展并没有鼓舞团队。


PHRASE->
be enheartened by = 被…鼓舞
enhearten oneself = 鼓起勇气
enhearten the troops = 鼓舞士气
enhearten the spirit = 振奋精神
enhearten the soul = 鼓舞人心


词源:
The word "enhearten" comes from the Old English "on-heortan," meaning "to put heart into."  The prefix "en-" means "to cause to be," and "hearten" derives from "heart," referring to courage and spirit.
