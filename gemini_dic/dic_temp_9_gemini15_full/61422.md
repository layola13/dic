emboldening: /ɪmˈbəʊldənɪŋ/ | v. | 使大胆；使有勇气 |  The act of making someone bolder or more confident.  It implies giving someone the courage to do something they might not otherwise do. | 例句：Her encouragement emboldened him to speak his mind.  她的鼓励使他大胆地说出了自己的想法。


例句：The positive feedback from the audience emboldened the young singer to perform even better.  观众的积极反馈使得这位年轻的歌手更有勇气，表现得更好。


近义词：encouraging, heartening, inspiring, empowering


反义词：discouraging, disheartening, intimidating


词性：动名词 (gerund)

USAGE->
一般现在时:
His words embolden me daily. = 他的话每天都给我鼓舞。
The teacher's praise emboldens her students. = 老师的赞扬鼓励了她的学生们。


一般过去时:
The success of her first novel emboldened her to write a second. = 她第一部小说的成功使她更有勇气写第二部。
His father's support emboldened him to pursue his dreams. = 他父亲的支持使他更有勇气去追求他的梦想。


现在完成时:
The recent victory has emboldened the team. = 最近的胜利使球队士气大振。
Positive reviews have emboldened the artist to experiment with new techniques. = 积极的评价使这位艺术家更有勇气尝试新的技巧。


过去完成时:
Having won the first round, he was emboldened to face the champion. = 赢得了第一轮比赛后，他更有勇气面对冠军了。
She had been emboldened by her previous successes. = 她之前的成功给了她勇气。


将来时:
This new evidence will embolden the investigators. = 这项新证据将使调查人员更有勇气。
The upcoming election will embolden the opposition party. = 即将到来的选举将使反对党更有胆量。


虚拟语气:
If I were emboldened, I would confront him. = 如果我有勇气，我会去面对他。
I wish I were emboldened enough to speak my mind. = 我希望我有足够的勇气说出我的想法。


被动语态:
He was emboldened by his friends' support. = 他受到了朋友的支持而变得更有勇气。
She felt emboldened by the positive response to her work. = 她对自己的作品得到积极回应而感到鼓舞。


疑问句:
What emboldened you to take such a risk? = 是什么让你敢冒这么大的风险？
What will embolden him to speak the truth? = 什么会让他有勇气说出真相？


否定句:
The criticism did not embolden him; instead, it discouraged him. = 批评并没有使他更有勇气，反而让他泄气了。
Her fear prevented her from being emboldened. = 她的恐惧使她无法鼓起勇气。


PHRASE->
emboldened by = 因…而大胆
emboldened to = 大胆地…
feel emboldened = 感到大胆
be emboldened to do something = 有胆量做某事
emboldened action = 大胆的行动
