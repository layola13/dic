buttercups: /ˈbʌtərkʌps/ | n. |毛茛属植物，金凤花 | Bright yellow flowers, often found in meadows and fields.  They are characterized by their cup-shaped blooms and are known for their vibrant color.  The name likely comes from the butter-yellow color of the petals. |例句：The children picked a bunch of buttercups in the meadow.孩子们在草地上摘了一束金凤花。

例句：The field was ablaze with a carpet of buttercups. 田野里一片金凤花，鲜艳夺目。


近义词：meadow flowers, goldcups |反义词：无


词源:  The word "buttercup" originated in Middle English, combining "butter" (referring to the butter-yellow color of the flowers) and "cup" (describing the shape of the flower).  The etymology reflects the flower's striking visual characteristics.
