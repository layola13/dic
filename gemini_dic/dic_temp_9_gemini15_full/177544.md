rebottled:/ˌriːˈbɑːtəld/ | v. | 再装瓶；重新装瓶 | To bottle something again.  This usually refers to liquids, like wine or other beverages, that have been previously bottled and are being bottled again, perhaps after being processed or stored. | Example: The wine was rebottled after aging in oak barrels.  重新装瓶的葡萄酒在橡木桶中陈酿后。The old juice was rebottled into smaller containers for better sales. 旧果汁被重新装入较小的容器中以更好地销售。| 近义词：repackaged, refilled | 反义词：unbottled
