expressed:/ɪkˈsprɛst/ | v. | 表达；表示；挤出 | To convey a thought, feeling, or idea; to force something out.  |例句：He expressed his gratitude for their help. 他表达了对他们帮助的感激之情。 |例句：The artist expressed her emotions through her paintings.艺术家通过绘画表达了她的情感。|近义词：conveyed, communicated, articulated |反义词：suppressed, concealed, hidden |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She expresses her opinions freely. = 她自由地表达自己的观点。
They express their concerns to the manager. = 他们向经理表达了他们的担忧。

一般过去时:
He expressed his disappointment with the results. = 他表达了对结果的失望。
She expressed her love for her family in her letter. = 她在她信中表达了她对家人的爱。

现在完成时:
They have expressed their interest in the project. = 他们已经表达了对这个项目的兴趣。
She has expressed her intention to resign. = 她已经表达了她辞职的意图。

过去完成时:
He had expressed his concerns before the meeting. = 他在会议之前已经表达了他的担忧。
They had expressed their dissatisfaction with the service. = 他们已经表达了对服务的 不满。

将来时:
She will express her views at the next meeting. = 她将在下次会议上表达她的观点。
He will express his apologies to her. = 他将向她道歉。

虚拟语气:
If he expressed his feelings, she might understand. = 如果他表达了他的感受，她可能会理解。
I wish I had expressed my thanks properly. = 我希望我当时能恰当地表达我的谢意。

被动语态:
His opinions were expressed clearly. = 他的观点表达得很清楚。
The concerns were expressed to the higher management. = 担忧已向高层管理人员表达。

疑问句:
How did she express her feelings? = 她是怎么表达她的感受的？
Did he express any concerns about the plan? = 他对这个计划表达了任何担忧吗？

否定句:
He didn't express his anger openly. = 他没有公开表达他的愤怒。
She didn't express her opinion on the matter. = 她没有就此事表达她的意见。
