delusiveness:/dɪˈluːsɪvnəs/ | n. |妄想；错觉；虚幻；欺骗性 |The state or quality of being delusional; the holding of false beliefs despite evidence to the contrary.  It often involves a disconnect from reality and can be a symptom of mental illness. |例句：His delusiveness led him to believe he was a powerful king. 他的妄想让他相信自己是一位强大的国王。

例句：The delusiveness of his promises became apparent when he failed to deliver on any of them.  他那些虚假的承诺在他未能兑现任何承诺时就变得显而易见了。


近义词：fantasy, illusion, hallucination, delusion, deception

反义词：reality, truth, sanity, soundness


词源:  Derived from the adjective "delusive," which comes from the Latin word "deludere," meaning "to deceive" or "to mock."  The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective into a noun, indicating a state or quality.
