siderostat:/ˈsaɪdərəstæt/| n. |太阳跟踪器，定日镜|A siderostat is a type of heliostat that uses a single mirror to reflect sunlight onto a fixed point.  It differs from a heliostat in that the mirror rotates about a single axis, typically a vertical axis, to track the sun's movement across the sky. This allows a fixed telescope or instrument to receive consistent sunlight. |Example: The solar furnace utilizes a large siderostat to concentrate sunlight onto the target.  太阳能熔炉利用大型定日镜将阳光集中到目标上。|近义词：heliostat (but with a key difference in axis of rotation)|反义词：无
