pentecostalist:/ˌpɛntəˈkɒstəlɪst/| n. |五旬节教徒；五旬宗信徒|A follower of Pentecostalism, a renewal movement within Protestant Christianity that emphasizes a direct personal experience of God through the baptism of the Holy Spirit, often manifested in speaking in tongues (glossolalia), divine healing, and prophecy.|例句：Many pentecostalist churches hold services that include speaking in tongues and ecstatic worship.|近义词：Pentecostal, charismatic Christian|反义词:None specifically.
