roil:/rɔɪl/| vt. n.|使翻腾；搅动；使不安；使混乱|To make (a liquid) turbid or muddy by stirring up sediment.  To disturb or disquiet. |例句：The storm roiled the sea.  暴风雨使大海翻腾。 The news roiled the markets.  这消息扰乱了市场。|近义词：agitate, churn, disturb, unsettle |反义词：calm, soothe, pacify


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The wind roils the lake. = 风使湖水翻腾。
He roils the conversation with his negativity. = 他用消极的态度扰乱谈话。


一般过去时:
The earthquake roiled the earth. = 地震使大地翻腾。
Her unexpected departure roiled the team. = 她意外的离开使团队不安。


现在完成时:
The scandal has roiled the political landscape. = 这场丑闻已经扰乱了政治局面。
Years of conflict have roiled the region. = 多年的冲突已经使该地区陷入混乱。


过去完成时:
The news had roiled the investors before the market opened. = 在市场开盘前，这个消息已经让投资者不安了。
The rumor had roiled the community for weeks. = 这个谣言已经扰乱了社区好几个星期了。


将来时:
The impending storm will roil the ocean. = 即将来临的风暴将使海洋翻腾。
His announcement will roil the shareholders. = 他的声明将使股东们不安。


虚拟语气:
If he spoke, he would roil the waters. = 如果他说话，他会搅乱局面。
I wish the news wouldn't roil the public. = 我希望这个消息不会扰乱公众。


被动语态:
The water was roiled by the storm. = 水被风暴搅动了。
The political party was roiled by internal disputes. = 这个政党被内部纠纷弄得混乱不堪。


疑问句:
Does the thought of public speaking roil you? = 公开演讲的想法是否让你不安？
Will this decision roil the employees? = 这个决定会让员工不安吗？


否定句:
The calm waters were not roiled by the gentle breeze. = 平静的水面没有被微风搅动。
The announcement did not roil the market as much as expected. = 这则公告并没有像预期的那样扰乱市场。
