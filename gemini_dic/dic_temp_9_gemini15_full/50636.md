derides:/dɪˈraɪdz/| vt.|嘲笑；嘲弄；奚落|To express contempt for; ridicule.  The speaker derided the opposing party's policies. |The critics derided his new play as childish and amateurish.|近义词：mock, scoff at, jeer at, ridicule, scorn|反义词：praise, commend, applaud


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He derides his opponent's ideas in every debate. = 他在每一次辩论中都嘲笑对手的观点。
She derides the fashion trends of the younger generation. = 她嘲笑年轻一代的时尚潮流。


一般过去时:
The comedian derided the politician's speeches. = 喜剧演员嘲笑了这位政治家的演讲。
They derided his efforts as futile. = 他们嘲笑他的努力是徒劳的。


现在完成时:
The media has derided the government's response to the crisis. = 媒体已经嘲笑了政府对危机的回应。
He has often derided his brother's ambition. = 他经常嘲笑他弟弟的野心。


过去完成时:
They had derided his proposal before he even finished presenting it. = 甚至在他完成陈述之前，他们就已经嘲笑他的提案了。
She had derided his singing for years. = 她多年来一直嘲笑他的歌声。


将来时:
They will deride his attempts to win the election. = 他们将会嘲笑他试图赢得选举。
She will likely deride his artistic expression. = 她很可能会嘲笑他的艺术表达。


虚拟语气:
If he knew better, he wouldn't deride others so easily. = 如果他懂得更好，他就不会那么轻易地嘲笑别人。
I wish he wouldn't deride my work so often. = 我希望他不那么经常嘲笑我的工作。


被动语态:
His ideas were derided by the critics. = 他的想法受到评论家的嘲笑。
His efforts were derided as meaningless. = 他的努力被嘲笑为毫无意义。


疑问句:
Does she often deride her colleagues? = 她经常嘲笑她的同事吗？
Why did they deride his efforts? = 他们为什么嘲笑他的努力？


否定句:
He did not deride her accomplishments. = 他并没有嘲笑她的成就。
They would not deride his attempts to help. = 他们不会嘲笑他试图帮忙的举动。


PHRASE->
deride someone's efforts = 嘲笑某人的努力
deride someone's appearance = 嘲笑某人的外表
deride someone's intelligence = 嘲笑某人的智力
deride someone's ambition = 嘲笑某人的雄心壮志
deride a proposal/plan = 嘲笑一项提案/计划

词源:
源自于古法语 *derider，意为“嘲笑”。  最终源自于拉丁语 *deridere，由 *de-（“向下”）和 *ridere（“笑”）构成。
