scaffolds:/ˈskæfəʊldz/ | n. | 脚手架；支架；临时结构；（比喻）支持；基础 |  A temporary structure used for supporting workmen and materials during the erection or repair of a building; a temporary or makeshift support or framework.  | Example: The builders erected scaffolds around the building for repairs. 建筑工人为维修在建筑物周围搭建了脚手架。

The scaffolds were removed after the painting was completed. 油漆完成后，脚手架被拆除了。


近义词：framework, support, staging, platform | 反义词：foundation (in a figurative sense, scaffolds are temporary, while foundations are permanent) | 词性:名词


词源:

The word "scaffold" ultimately derives from the Old North French word "eschafaut," which itself comes from the Old Italian "scaffale," meaning "shelf" or "stage."  The evolution reflects the original use of scaffolds as elevated platforms.
