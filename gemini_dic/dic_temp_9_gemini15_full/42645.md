countersigns: /ˈkaʊntərsaɪnz/ | n. | 副署；暗号；密语 | A countersign is a password or other secret sign used to identify someone as being trustworthy or belonging to a particular group.  It can also refer to the act of signing something in addition to another signature, often for verification or approval. | Examples:  The soldiers exchanged countersigns before allowing each other to pass.  士兵们交换暗号后才互相放行。 The document requires countersignature from the CEO. 这份文件需要CEO的副署。 | 近义词：password, code word, secret signal, endorsement | 反义词：open sign, public signal |  词性: Noun
