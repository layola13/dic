folksy:/ˈfoʊksi/| adj.|乡土的；朴实的；友好的；通俗的|Characterized by or suggesting the qualities of folk or folks; simple, unsophisticated, and friendly.  It can also refer to something that is plain and unpretentious, or to a style of music or art that is rooted in folk traditions.|例句：He had a folksy charm that made him instantly likable. 他有一种乡土魅力，使人立刻喜欢上他。

例句：The band played folksy music with a modern twist.  乐队演奏带有现代风格的乡土音乐。


近义词：rustic, homespun, down-to-earth, folksy, informal, friendly, unpretentious|反义词：sophisticated, pretentious, artificial, formal


词源：From folk + -sy.  "Folk" ultimately derives from Old English *folc, meaning "people." The suffix "-sy" adds a diminutive or descriptive quality.
