jot:/dʒɒt/| /dʒɑːt/ | vt. n.|记下，写下；少量；笔记 | To write something quickly or briefly.  |例句：I jotted down his phone number. 我记下了他的电话号码。|例句： I need to jot down a few notes for the meeting. 我需要为会议记下一些笔记。 |近义词：note, scribble, write down |反义词：elaborate, expound


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jots down important information. = 他记下重要信息。
She jots ideas in her notebook. = 她把想法记在笔记本上。

一般过去时:
I jotted down the address. = 我记下了地址。
They jotted notes during the lecture. = 他们在讲座期间做了笔记。

现在完成时:
She has jotted down all the key points. = 她已经记下了所有要点。
He has jotted a short message. = 他已经写了一条简短的短信。

过去完成时:
I had jotted the main points before the meeting. = 我在会议之前已经记下了要点。
She had jotted a reminder on the calendar. = 她已经在日历上记下了一条提醒。

将来时:
I will jot down the recipe. = 我会记下食谱。
He will jot the details later. = 他稍后会记下细节。

虚拟语气:
If I had time, I would jot down everything. = 如果我有时间，我会把所有东西都记下来。
I wish I had jotted down the directions. = 我真希望我记下了路线。

被动语态:
The address was jotted down incorrectly. = 地址记错了。
The notes were jotted down hastily. = 笔记匆匆记下。

疑问句:
Did you jot down the address? = 你记下地址了吗？
Will you jot down the details? = 你会记下细节吗？

否定句:
I didn't jot down the details. = 我没有记下细节。
He didn't jot anything down. = 他什么也没记下。
