mesmerise: /ˈmɛzməraɪz/ | vt. | 使着迷；使神魂颠倒 | To hold the attention of (someone) completely as if by magic; to fascinate. | 例句：The magician mesmerised the audience with his incredible tricks. 魔术师以他不可思议的戏法迷住了观众。  The beautiful scenery mesmerised me.  美丽的风景让我着迷。| 近义词：hypnotise, captivate, fascinate, enchant | 反义词：bore, repel


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The music mesmerises me. = 这音乐让我着迷。
His performance mesmerises the crowd. = 他的表演迷住了人群。

一般过去时:
The story mesmerised me. = 这个故事让我着迷。
The speaker mesmerised the audience with his oratory skills. = 演讲者以其雄辩的技巧迷住了听众。

现在完成时:
The painting has mesmerised art critics for decades. = 这幅画几十年来一直让艺术评论家着迷。
That view has mesmerised me since I first saw it. = 从我第一次看到那个景色起，我就被它迷住了。

过去完成时:
I had been mesmerised by his charm before I realised his deception. = 在我意识到他的欺骗之前，我已经被他迷住了。
She had been mesmerised by the storyteller's tales before she fell asleep. = 在她睡着之前，她已经被讲故事的人的故事迷住了。

将来时:
The new exhibit will mesmerise visitors with its interactive displays. = 新展览将以其互动展示迷住参观者。
I am sure the sunset will mesmerise you. = 我相信日落会让你着迷。

虚拟语气:
If I had a magic wand, I could mesmerise everyone into peace. = 如果我有一根魔杖，我就能让每个人都平静下来。 (此处"mesmerise"略带夸张修辞)
If only I could mesmerise him into telling the truth. = 但愿我能让他说出真相。(此处"mesmerise"略带夸张修辞)

被动语态:
The audience was mesmerised by the performance. = 观众被表演迷住了。
The crowd was mesmerised by the breathtaking view. = 人群被令人叹为观止的景色迷住了。

疑问句:
Does his charisma mesmerise you? = 他的魅力让你着迷吗？
What aspects of the presentation mesmerised you the most? = 演示文稿的哪些方面最让你着迷？

否定句:
The movie didn't mesmerise me. = 这部电影并没有让我着迷。
His speech failed to mesmerise the audience. = 他的演讲未能迷住听众。
