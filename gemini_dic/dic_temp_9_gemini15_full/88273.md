hint:/hɪnt/| n. v. |暗示；提示；线索| a suggestion or piece of information that helps you to guess something, or a small piece of something that shows what the rest is like.  |例句：He gave me a hint about the answer. 他给了我关于答案的提示。|例句：The cake had a hint of cinnamon. 蛋糕有一丝肉桂味。|近义词：clue, suggestion, indication |反义词：explanation, disclosure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hints at the truth. 他暗示了真相。
She hints at her dissatisfaction. 她暗示了她的不满。


一般过去时:
He hinted at the problem yesterday. 他昨天暗示了这个问题。
She hinted that she was leaving. 她暗示她要离开了。


现在完成时:
They have hinted at a possible solution. 他们已经暗示了一个可能的解决方案。
He has hinted at his intentions several times. 他已经多次暗示了他的意图。


过去完成时:
She had hinted at her unhappiness before she quit her job. 在她辞职之前，她已经暗示过她的不快乐。
He had hinted at the surprise before the party. 他在派对之前就暗示了这个惊喜。


将来时:
She will hint at the news in her next speech. 她将在她的下次演讲中暗示这个消息。
He will hint at his feelings later tonight. 他今晚晚些时候会暗示他的感受。


虚拟语气:
If he hinted at the truth, we might have understood. 如果他暗示了真相，我们也许就明白了。
I wish he had hinted at the danger sooner. 我希望他早点暗示危险。


被动语态:
The answer was hinted at in the previous paragraph. 答案在上一段中有所暗示。
The problem was hinted at, but not directly addressed. 问题被暗示了，但没有直接解决。


疑问句:
Did he hint at anything important? 他暗示了什么重要的事情吗？
What did she hint at in her email? 她在邮件里暗示了什么？


否定句:
He didn't hint at the reason for his resignation. 他没有暗示他辞职的原因。
She didn't hint at any problems with the project. 她没有暗示项目有任何问题。


PHRASE->
