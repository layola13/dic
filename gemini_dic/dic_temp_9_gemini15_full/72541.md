fluted:/ˈfluːtɪd/ | adj. | 有凹槽的；有槽纹的；音调圆润的 | Having a series of vertical grooves or channels.  The term often refers to decorative grooves, but can also describe functional channels. |

例句：The fluted columns of the Greek temple are impressive. = 希腊神庙的凹槽柱令人印象深刻。

例句：She admired the fluted edges of the antique vase. = 她欣赏古董花瓶上凹槽的边缘。


近义词：grooved, channeled, ribbed, furrowed

反义词：smooth, plain


词性:形容词

词源:
From Middle English *flutyd, from Old French fluté, from flûte "flute," from Vulgar Latin *fluta, diminutive of Latin flūtus "pipe, flute."  The sense development is from "shaped like a flute" to "having grooves like a flute."
