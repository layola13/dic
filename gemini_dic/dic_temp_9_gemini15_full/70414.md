fightability:/ˌfaɪtəˈbɪləti/| n. |战力；战斗力；可战性 |The capacity or potential for fighting effectively; the degree to which something or someone is capable of fighting.  This term often refers to the ability of a military force, weapon system, or individual to engage in combat successfully. It encompasses factors like training, equipment, morale, and strategy. |例句：The army's fightability was significantly reduced by the lack of supplies.  军队因缺乏补给而战斗力大减。

The new fighter jet boasts superior fightability compared to its predecessor.新型战斗机的战斗力比其前身强得多。|近义词：combat effectiveness, fighting capacity, military strength |反义词：inability to fight, weakness, vulnerability
