retainers:/rɪˈteɪnərz/ | n. | 保持者；(法律)代理人；(牙科)保持器；随从 |  A retainer is a person who is kept in employment, especially a lawyer or other professional person. It can also refer to a device used to maintain something in place, such as a dental retainer.  Additionally, it can mean a person in the service of another, especially one of a retinue. |例句：He hired a high-powered retainer to handle the case. 他雇了一位实力雄厚的律师来处理这个案子。


The law firm has several retainers on long-term contracts. 这家律师事务所与几位律师签订了长期合同。


近义词：attorney, lawyer, agent, servant, follower, supporter | 反义词：employer, client, principal


词源:  The word "retainer" originates from the Old French word *retenir*, meaning "to retain,"  which itself comes from the Latin *retinēre*, a compound of *re-* (back, again) and *tenēre* (to hold).  The sense of "keeping someone in employment" developed from the idea of "holding onto" or "keeping" their services.
