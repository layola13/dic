castrato:/kəˈstrɑːtoʊ/| n. |阉伶，阉人|A castrato was a male singer who had been castrated before puberty, resulting in a high-pitched voice that retained the clarity and power of a boy's voice but with greater resonance and range.  Castrati were highly valued in opera during the Baroque and Classical periods.|  Farinelli, a famous castrato, captivated audiences throughout Europe with his extraordinary vocal abilities.  |近义词：eunuch (though not always a singer)|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with a male singer with a normal voice.
