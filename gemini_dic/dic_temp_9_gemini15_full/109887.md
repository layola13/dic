liaise: /liˈeɪz/ | v. | 联系；联络；协调 | To work with someone from another organization to achieve a shared goal.  |例句：We need to liaise closely with the marketing department to ensure a successful product launch. 我们需要与市场部密切合作以确保产品发布成功。

例句：The project manager will liaise with the various teams to coordinate their efforts. 项目经理将与各个团队联系以协调他们的工作。


近义词：coordinate, cooperate, collaborate, communicate | 反义词：isolate, disconnect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They liaise with the suppliers regularly. = 他们定期与供应商联系。
The two departments liaise on a weekly basis. = 这两个部门每周进行联络。


一般过去时:
The team liaised with the client to resolve the issue. = 团队与客户联系以解决问题。
She liaised with the authorities to obtain the necessary permits. = 她与当局联系以获得必要的许可证。


现在完成时:
We have liaised with several potential investors. = 我们已经与几位潜在投资者联系过了。
They have liaised effectively throughout the project. = 在整个项目中，他们都进行了有效的沟通协调。


过去完成时:
They had liaised with the engineers before making the final decision. = 他们做出最终决定之前已经与工程师们联系过了。
The company had liaised extensively with the community prior to construction. = 在施工前，公司与社区进行了广泛的沟通。


将来时:
We will liaise with our partners to develop a new strategy. = 我们将与我们的合作伙伴联系以制定新的战略。
The team will liaise regularly to monitor progress. = 团队将定期联络以监督进度。


虚拟语气:
If we liaised more effectively, we could avoid these problems. = 如果我们更有效地沟通协调，就能避免这些问题。
It would be helpful if they liaised with the local community. = 如果他们能与当地社区联系就好了。


被动语态:
The information was liaised to the relevant departments. = 信息已与相关部门沟通。
The details were liaised through several channels. = 详情已通过多个渠道沟通。


疑问句:
Does your department liaise with other departments regularly? = 你的部门是否定期与其他部门联系？
Have you liaised with the relevant authorities? = 你是否已经与相关部门联系？


否定句:
We did not liaise with the competitor's company. = 我们没有与竞争对手公司联系。
The team failed to liaise effectively with the other teams. = 团队未能与其他团队有效地沟通协调。


PHRASE->
liaise with = 与…联络
liaise closely = 密切联系
liaise effectively = 有效沟通
liaise directly = 直接联系
liaise regularly = 定期联系


词源:
The word "liaise" is derived from the French word "liaison," meaning "link" or "connection."  The verb form entered English in the early 20th century.
