creditably:/ˈkrɛdəbli/| adj.|值得称赞地；令人信服地；令人满意地 |Admirably; in a way that deserves praise or approval.  It suggests that something was done well and effectively, meeting expectations or exceeding them. |例句：He handled the difficult situation creditably. 他出色地处理了这个棘手的情况。

例句：She performed creditably in the competition, despite the pressure.  她尽管承受着压力，在比赛中表现出色。


近义词：satisfactorily, commendably, respectably, admirably, acceptably

反义词：unsatisfactorily, discreditably, poorly, inadequately


词源:
From credit (n.) + -ably (suffix).  The noun "credit" ultimately derives from the Latin word "credere," meaning "to believe" or "to trust."  The suffix "-ably" forms adverbs and adjectives meaning "in a ... manner" or "to a ... degree."

用法说明:

Creditably is an adverb that modifies a verb, describing how an action was performed. It emphasizes the positive aspect of the action, highlighting its success, competence, or worthiness of praise.  It doesn't just mean "okay" or "sufficient"; it implies a level of excellence or achievement.
