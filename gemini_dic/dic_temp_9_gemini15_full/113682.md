mackerel:/ˈmækərəl/ | n. | 鲭鱼 | A saltwater fish of the family Scombridae, typically having a streamlined body and dark bluish-green back.  Mackerel are important food fish. | Example: We had grilled mackerel for dinner last night.  昨晚我们吃了烤鲭鱼。  The fisherman caught a large school of mackerel. 渔民捕到了一大群鲭鱼。 | 近义词： tuna, herring | 反义词：  (无直接反义词，可根据语境考虑如 freshwater fish 淡水鱼) | 名词

词源:
The word "mackerel" comes from the Old English "makrele," which itself is of uncertain origin, possibly of Germanic origin related to spotted or speckled appearance.  Some theories connect it to words meaning "spotted" or "marked."
