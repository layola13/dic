choked: /tʃoʊkt/ | vt.  | 哽住；窒息；阻塞；抑制；充满；使充满 | to have breathing stopped by something blocking the windpipe; to suppress a feeling or reaction; to fill or block something. | 近义词：strangled, suffocated, stifled | 反义词：unblocked, opened


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He chokes on his food sometimes. = 他有时会食物哽住。
The smoke chokes the air. = 烟雾使空气窒息。


一般过去时:
I choked on a piece of bread. = 我被一片面包噎住了。
The heavy traffic choked the city streets. = 交通拥堵阻塞了城市街道。


现在完成时:
The dust has choked the engine. = 灰尘已经堵塞了发动机。
He has choked back his tears. = 他已经忍住了眼泪。


过去完成时:
She had choked back a sob before speaking. = 她说话前已经忍住了一声抽泣。
The river had choked with silt over the centuries. = 这条河几个世纪以来已经被淤泥堵塞了。


将来时:
The rising tide will choke the harbor. = 上涨的潮水将阻塞港口。
I think I will choke on this dry bread. = 我想我会被这块干面包噎住的。


虚拟语气:
If I ate too fast, I might choke. = 如果我吃得太快，我可能会噎住。
If he hadn't choked back his anger, the situation would have been worse. = 如果他不压制自己的怒火，情况会更糟。


被动语态:
The pipe was choked with debris. = 管子被碎片堵塞了。
His voice was choked with emotion. = 他的声音因激动而哽咽。


疑问句:
Did the smoke choke you? = 烟呛到你了吗？
Is the drain choked? = 下水道堵塞了吗？


否定句:
He didn't choke on the food. = 他没有被食物噎住。
The government didn't choke off free speech. = 政府没有压制言论自由。


PHRASE->
choke back (tears, laughter) = 忍住（眼泪，笑声）
choke down (food) = 硬咽下（食物）
choke off (supply) = 切断（供应）
choke up (with emotion) = 感情激动得哽咽
choke to death = 噎死
