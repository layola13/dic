scotomy:/skoʊˈtoʊmi/| n. |视野缺损，暗点|A scotoma is a partial loss of vision or a blind spot in an otherwise normal visual field.  It can be caused by a variety of factors, including damage to the retina, optic nerve, or brain.  The size and location of the scotoma can vary depending on the underlying cause. |Example: The patient reported experiencing a scotoma in their right eye, making it difficult to read.  患者报告右眼出现视野缺损，导致阅读困难。|近义词：blind spot, visual field defect|反义词：normal vision
