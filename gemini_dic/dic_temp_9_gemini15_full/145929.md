overdiscipline: /ˌoʊvərˈdɪsəplɪn/ | n. adj. |过度管教；过分严格的训练 | Excessive discipline;  the act of disciplining someone too much or too harshly. It can refer to a state of being excessively disciplined, or the act of imposing excessive discipline. |

The children suffered from overdiscipline, becoming timid and lacking self-confidence.  孩子们遭受了过度管教，变得胆小且缺乏自信。

His overdiscipline in his training regime led to burnout and injury. 他训练中过度严格的纪律导致了倦怠和受伤。

词源:

The word "overdiscipline" is a compound word formed from the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") and the noun "discipline". "Discipline" itself comes from the Old French word "discipline," ultimately derived from the Latin word "disciplina," meaning "instruction, training, teaching."  Therefore, "overdiscipline" literally means "excessive instruction, training, or teaching."
