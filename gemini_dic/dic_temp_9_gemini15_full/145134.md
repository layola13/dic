overarticulated:/ˌoʊvərɑːrˈtɪkjuleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 过分清晰发音的；咬字过分的 | Excessively clear and precise in pronunciation; overly distinct articulation of speech sounds.  It often implies a degree of artificiality or affectation. | 例句：His overarticulated speech sounded unnatural.  他的发音过于清晰，听起来不自然。

His overarticulated pronunciation made him sound like a robot. 他过于清晰的发音让他听起来像个机器人。


近义词：affected, precise, distinct, pedantic | 反义词：slurred, mumbled, indistinct, imprecise


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "over-" (meaning excessively or too much),  "articulate" (to pronounce clearly), and the suffix "-ed" (past participle, forming an adjective). "Articulate" itself comes from the Latin word "articulatus," meaning "jointed," referring to the separate sounds joined together in speech.
