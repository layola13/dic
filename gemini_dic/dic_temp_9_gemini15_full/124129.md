mortgages:/ˈmɔːrɡɪdʒɪz/ | n. |抵押贷款；抵押 |A mortgage is a loan secured by a property.  The borrower agrees to pay back the loan with interest over a specified period, and the lender has the right to seize the property if the borrower defaults. |例句：They took out a mortgage to buy their first home. =他们贷款买了他们的第一套房子。

例句：The high interest rates made it difficult to get a mortgage. =高利率使得获得抵押贷款变得困难。


近义词：loan, lien |反义词：None  |词性:Noun

词源:
The word "mortgage" comes from Old French *mort gage*, literally meaning "dead pledge".  The "dead" refers to the fact that the property is tied up as collateral until the loan is repaid.  The word entered English in the 15th century.
