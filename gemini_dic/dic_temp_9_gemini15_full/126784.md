nanoparticle: /ˈnænəʊˌpɑːrtɪkl/ | n. | 纳米粒子 | A nanoparticle is a tiny particle with at least one dimension sized from 1 to 100 nanometers.  These particles exhibit unique properties due to their small size, often differing significantly from the bulk material. They are used in various applications, from medicine to electronics. | Example:  Scientists are researching the use of nanoparticles in targeted drug delivery. 科学家们正在研究纳米粒子在靶向药物递送中的应用。  |近义词: nanocluster, ultrafine particle | 反义词:macromolecule
