misassigning:/ˌmɪs.əˈsaɪ.nɪŋ/ | v. |错误分配，误派 | The act of assigning something or someone incorrectly.  It implies a mistake in judgment or procedure leading to an inappropriate allocation of resources, tasks, or responsibilities. | Example:  The manager was criticized for misassigning the most experienced employee to the least challenging project.  经理因将经验最丰富的员工分配到最不具挑战性的项目而受到批评。|近义词：misallocating, misdirecting, wrongly assigning | 反义词：assigning correctly, properly allocating
