re-emission:/ˌriːɪˈmɪʃən/| n. |再发射；再辐射|The process by which previously absorbed or emitted radiation is subsequently emitted again.  This often happens with a change in wavelength or direction.  For example, sunlight absorbed by the Earth's surface is re-emitted as infrared radiation.|Example: The re-emission of light from a fluorescent material is a key part of its operation. 再发射光线是荧光材料运作的关键部分。The re-emission of heat from the Earth's surface contributes to the greenhouse effect. 地球表面的热量再辐射导致温室效应。|近义词：secondary emission, re-radiation|反义词：absorption|名词
