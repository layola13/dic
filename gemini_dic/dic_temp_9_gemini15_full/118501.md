mesmerizing: /ˈmɛzməraɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人着迷的，使人神魂颠倒的 |  Extremely captivating or fascinating; enchanting.  It describes something so compelling that it holds one's attention completely. |例句：The mesmerizing sunset painted the sky with vibrant colors.  令人着迷的日落用鲜艳的色彩描绘了天空。

The mesmerizing performance left the audience speechless.  令人着迷的表演让观众说不出话来。


近义词：captivating, enchanting, fascinating, hypnotizing, spellbinding | 反义词：boring, dull, uninteresting, tedious


词源:  Derived from Franz Mesmer (1734-1815), an Austrian physician who developed a theory of "animal magnetism," which involved inducing a trance-like state in patients. While his methods were later discredited, the term "mesmerize" came to mean to fascinate or hypnotize.
