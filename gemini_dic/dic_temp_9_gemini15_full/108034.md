lat:/læt/| adj.| 迟的；近来的；已故的 | Late means happening or done after the expected, usual, or planned time. It can also refer to something that is recent or deceased. |例句：The train was late. 火车晚点了。|例句：He's a late riser. 他是个晚起的人。|近义词：tardy, delayed, recent, deceased |反义词：early, prompt, ancient


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is always late for work. = 他总是上班迟到。
The movie starts late tonight. = 电影今晚很晚才开始。


一般过去时:
We arrived late at the party. = 我们晚到了聚会。
She was late for her appointment. = 她约会迟到了。


现在完成时:
He has been late several times this week. = 他这周已经迟到好几次了。
They have only recently arrived. = 他们最近才到达。


过去完成时:
I had already been late when the boss called. = 老板打电话时我已经迟到了。
The train had already left when we got to the station. = 我们到达车站时，火车已经开走了。


将来时:
The meeting will be late tomorrow. = 明天的会议将会很晚。
I will be late if the traffic is bad. = 如果交通堵塞，我会迟到。


虚拟语气:
If I had left earlier, I wouldn't have been late. = 如果我早点出发，就不会迟到了。
I wish I hadn't been so late. = 我真希望我没有那么晚。


被动语态:
The letter was late in arriving. = 这封信到得很晚。
The project was completed late. = 项目完成得很晚。


疑问句:
Is the train late? = 火车晚点了吗？
Was the meeting late yesterday? = 昨天的会议晚了吗？


否定句:
The train wasn't late. = 火车没有晚点。
He is never late. = 他从不迟到。


PHRASE->
of late = 最近
late at night = 深夜
sooner or later = 迟早
better late than never = 亡羊补牢
the late Mr./Ms. X = 已故的X先生/女士
