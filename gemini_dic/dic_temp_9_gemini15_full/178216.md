recommission:/ˌriːkəˈmɪʃən/| vt.|重新委托；再次启用；重新委任 |To put something back into service or commission after it has been decommissioned or out of service.  It also means to officially authorize someone to do a job again. |例句：The old warship was recommissioned after extensive repairs.  老军舰经过大修后重新服役了。

The bridge was recommissioned after a year of renovations.  桥梁经过一年的翻修后重新启用。


近义词：reactivate, reinstate, renew |反义词：decommission

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They recommission the old factory. = 他们重新启用旧工厂。
The company recommissions its employees regularly. = 公司定期重新委任其员工。


一般过去时:
The government recommissioned the investigation. = 政府重新委托了这项调查。
The museum recommissioned the artist to create a new sculpture. = 博物馆再次委托这位艺术家创作一件新的雕塑。

过去完成时:
