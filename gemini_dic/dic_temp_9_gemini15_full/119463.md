micromere:/ˈmaɪkrəʊmɪər/| n. |小球，小细胞|A micromere is a small cell, especially one of the small cells formed by unequal cleavage of a fertilized ovum.  Micromeres are typically found in the animal pole of the developing embryo and often give rise to ectodermal tissues. |Example: The micromeres in this sea urchin embryo are noticeably smaller than the macromeres.  这个海胆胚胎中的小球明显小于大球。|近义词：small cell, blastomere (in the context of cleavage)|反义词：macromere
