rookie:/ˈrʊki/ | n. | 新手，菜鸟 | A rookie is a person who is new to a job or activity.  | Example: He's a rookie police officer. 他是个新手警察。 | 近义词：beginner, novice, newcomer | 反义词：veteran, expert


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a rookie in this company. = 他是这家公司的新人。
She is a rookie basketball player. = 她是位篮球新手。


一般过去时:
He was a rookie last year. = 他去年还是个新手。
She was a rookie journalist when she started. = 她刚开始工作时是个新手记者。


现在完成时:
He has been a rookie for only six months. = 他当新人只有六个月了。
She has only recently become a rookie in the field. = 她最近才在这个领域成为新手。


过去完成时:
He had been a rookie for only a year before he got promoted. = 他晋升之前只当了一年的新人。
She had been a rookie teacher for two years when she decided to quit. = 她当了两年新手老师后决定辞职。


将来时:
He will be a rookie next season. = 他下个赛季将是新人。
She will be a rookie programmer after graduation. = 她毕业后将成为一名新手程序员。


虚拟语气:
If he were a rookie, he would be more cautious. = 如果他是新手，他会更谨慎。
If she were a rookie again, she would do things differently. = 如果她再次成为新手，她会做不同的事情。


被动语态:
He was considered a rookie by his colleagues. = 他被同事们认为是新手。
She was treated as a rookie despite her experience. = 尽管她有经验，但她还是被当作新手对待。


疑问句:
Is he a rookie in this field? = 他在这个领域是新手吗？
Are they rookies in the industry? = 他们在业界是新手吗？


否定句:
He is not a rookie anymore. = 他不再是新手了。
She is not a rookie; she's very experienced. = 她不是新手，她很有经验。


PHRASE->
rookie mistake = 新手错误

rookie season = 新秀赛季
rookie cop = 新手警察
