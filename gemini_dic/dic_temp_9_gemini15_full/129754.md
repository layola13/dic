non-exceptional: /ˌnɒnɪkˈsɛpʃənəl/ | adj. |非例外的；普通的；一般的 | Not exceptional; ordinary; typical.  | The results were non-exceptional, falling within the expected range.  结果很普通，在预期范围内。|  ordinary, typical, average, unexceptional | exceptional, extraordinary, remarkable, unusual


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The weather is non-exceptional today. = 今天的天气很普通。
His performance is non-exceptional. = 他的表现很普通。


一般过去时:
The project's results were non-exceptional. = 该项目的成果很普通。
Her grades were non-exceptional last semester. = 她上学期成绩很普通。


现在完成时:
Nothing non-exceptional has happened recently. = 最近没有什么特别的事情发生。
We have seen non-exceptional growth in the market. = 我们看到市场增长平平。


过去完成时:
The situation had been non-exceptional until the recent incident. = 直到最近的事件发生前，情况一直很普通。
Before the storm, the weather had been non-exceptional. = 暴风雨来临之前，天气一直很普通。


将来时:
The forecast predicts non-exceptional weather for next week. = 预报显示下周天气将很普通。
We expect non-exceptional sales figures for the next quarter. = 我们预计下个季度的销售额将很普通。


虚拟语气:
If the results were non-exceptional, we would continue with the plan. = 如果结果很普通，我们将继续执行计划。
I wish the traffic wasn't so non-exceptional today. = 我希望今天的交通状况不要这么普通（堵车）。


被动语态:
The case was deemed non-exceptional by the court. = 法院认为此案并非例外。
His contribution was considered non-exceptional by his colleagues. = 他的贡献在他同事看来很普通。


疑问句:
Is the situation non-exceptional? = 情况是否很普通？
Are these results non-exceptional? = 这些结果是否很普通？


否定句:
The situation was not non-exceptional; it was extraordinary. = 情况并非普通；而是非同寻常的。
His talent was not non-exceptional; he was a genius. = 他并非没有天赋；他是个天才。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "non-exceptional" is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "exceptional" (meaning "unusually good; outstanding").  Therefore, it literally means "not exceptional".
