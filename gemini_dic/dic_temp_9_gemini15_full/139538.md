obtruded:/əbˈtruːdəd/| v.|突出；强加；闯入|To force or impose (something) on someone; to thrust oneself or itself into a place or situation.  |例句：He obtruded his opinions on the meeting. 他在会议上强加自己的意见。|例句：The mountain obtruded itself into the landscape.  山峦突兀于景色之中。|近义词：impose, thrust, intrude, force|反义词：withdraw, retreat, recede


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He obtrudes his opinions on others. = 他强加他的意见给他人。
The building obtrudes on the skyline. = 这座建筑物突兀于天际线。

一般过去时:
She obtruded her concerns into the conversation. = 她把她的担忧强加到谈话中。
The noise obtruded on our peaceful evening. = 噪音扰乱了我们平静的夜晚。

现在完成时:
He has obtruded himself into our lives. = 他已闯入我们的生活。
The new regulations have obtruded upon our freedoms. = 新规章限制了我们的自由。

过去完成时:
They had obtruded their views before anyone else could speak. = 在其他人发言之前，他们已经强加了他们的观点。
The unexpected guest had obtruded upon their private dinner. = 不速之客打断了他们的私人晚餐。

将来时:
She will obtrude her ideas on the committee. = 她将把她的想法强加给委员会。
The new highway will obtrude on the natural beauty of the area. = 新高速公路将破坏该地区的自然美景。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't obtruded his advice, we might have succeeded. = 如果他没有强加他的建议，我们也许会成功。
I wish she hadn't obtruded herself into our private affairs. = 我真希望她没有干涉我们的私事。

被动语态:
His opinions were obtruded upon the group. = 他的意见被强加给了小组。
Their privacy was obtruded upon by the paparazzi. = 狗仔队侵犯了他们的隐私。

疑问句:
Was his presence obtruded upon the meeting? = 他的出现是不是强加于会议？
Are our rights being obtruded upon by the government? = 我们的权利是否正受到政府的侵犯？

否定句:
He did not obtrude his opinions during the meeting. = 他在会议上没有强加自己的意见。
She tried not to obtrude on their conversation. = 她尽量不去打扰他们的谈话。



PHRASE->
obtrude oneself = 擅自介入

obtrude into = 闯入；插入
obtrude on = 妨碍；打扰
obtrude with = 以…强加于
