hypergenetic: /ˌhaɪpərˈdʒɛnɪtɪk/ | adj. | 超成因的；超越成因的 |  Relating to or denoting processes or features that are formed or modified after the initial formation of a rock or other geological feature.  It often implies a secondary or superimposed origin.  The term is frequently used in geology and geomorphology. | 例句：The hypergenetic alteration of the basalt resulted in the formation of clay minerals.  超成因蚀变玄武岩导致了粘土矿物的形成。

The hypergenetic weathering patterns on the cliff face were strikingly different from the primary structures.  悬崖峭壁上的超成因风化模式与原生构造截然不同。


近义词：secondary, superimposed, epigenetic (in some contexts) | 反义词：primary, autochthonous, syngenetic


词源:  From "hyper-" (beyond, above, over) + "genetic" (relating to origin or formation).  The prefix "hyper-" indicates that the process or feature goes beyond or transcends the initial genetic process.
