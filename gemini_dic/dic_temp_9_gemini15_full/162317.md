preaccusations: /ˌpriːˌækjuˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. | 预先指控；提前指控 |  Preaccusations are accusations made before formal charges are filed.  They often serve as a warning or a prelude to a more serious legal action.  This term is less common than "accusations" and usually implies an informal or preliminary nature.  | The preaccusations against the CEO were leaked to the press before the official investigation began. = 对CEO的预先指控在正式调查开始前就被泄露给了媒体。


The company faced numerous preaccusations of unethical practices before the lawsuit. = 在诉讼之前，该公司面临着许多关于不道德行为的预先指控。| 近义词：allegations, preliminary charges, accusations | 反义词：exculpations, vindications | 名词
