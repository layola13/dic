delicacy:/ˈdɛləkəsi/| n.|精致；美味佳肴；微妙之处；纤细；虚弱|A delicacy is something considered rare and exquisite, often a food item.  It can also refer to something requiring careful handling or skill, or a subtle point.  |例句：Foie gras is considered a delicacy in many countries.鹅肝酱在许多国家被认为是美味佳肴。

例句：The negotiation required great delicacy and tact.  谈判需要非常细致和技巧。


近义词：rare treat, dainty, refinement, subtlety|反义词：roughness, crudeness, coarseness|名词

词源:
From Old French *delicatesse, from Latin delicates (plural of delicatus), from delicāre "to allure, entice, charm," from dē- "away, down" + līcāri "to entice."  The sense evolution is from "something alluring" to "something exquisite" to "subtlety".
