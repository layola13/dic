scoop:/skuːp/| n., vt.| 铲；勺；独家新闻；抢先报道；|A scoop is a tool used for digging or lifting things, often made of a bowl-shaped container attached to a handle.  It can also refer to a piece of news published before anyone else.|例句：He used a scoop to serve the ice cream. 他用勺子盛冰淇淋。|例句：The newspaper got a scoop on the election results. 这家报纸抢先报道了选举结果。|近义词：shovel, ladle, scoopful, exclusive|反义词：N/A


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scoops ice cream every day. = 他每天都舀冰淇淋。
She scoops the dirt from the garden. = 她把花园里的泥土铲起来。

一般过去时:
I scooped the snow off the driveway. = 我把车道上的雪铲掉了。
They scooped up the spilled sugar. = 他们把洒出来的糖铲起来。


现在完成时:
The journalist has scooped the story. = 这位记者获得了这条独家新闻。
He has scooped out the pumpkin for a jack-o'-lantern. = 他已经挖空了南瓜做成南瓜灯。


过去完成时:
She had scooped the sand into a bucket before the tide came in. = 在潮水涨起来之前，她已经把沙子铲到桶里了。
They had scooped all the ice cream before I arrived. = 我到之前他们已经把所有的冰淇淋都舀完了。


将来时:
I will scoop the dog's food into his bowl. = 我会把狗粮舀到它的碗里。
The reporter will scoop the competition with this breaking news. = 记者将凭借这条突发新闻击败竞争对手。


虚拟语气:
If I had a bigger scoop, I would be able to scoop more ice cream. = 如果我有一个更大的勺子，我就能舀更多的冰淇淋。
If he had scooped the news earlier, he would have been more successful. = 如果他早点获得这则新闻，他会更成功。


被动语态:
The ice cream was scooped into the cones. = 冰淇淋被舀进甜筒里。
The snow was scooped from the sidewalk. = 人行道上的雪被铲掉了。


疑问句:
What are you scooping? = 你在铲什么？
Did he scoop all the ice cream? = 他把所有的冰淇淋都舀了吗？


否定句:
I didn't scoop the snow. = 我没有铲雪。
She didn't scoop the news. = 她没有获得这条新闻。
