criminalised:/ˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪzd/| v.| 宣布...为犯罪；定为犯罪 | To declare (an action or activity) illegal or punishable by law.  | Example: The possession of certain drugs was criminalised in 1971.  1971年，持有某些毒品被定为犯罪。 |近义词：outlawed, prohibited, banned |反义词：legalised, permitted, allowed |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government criminalises drug trafficking. = 政府将贩毒定为犯罪。
They criminalise acts of violence against women. = 他们将对妇女的暴力行为定为犯罪。


一般过去时:
The law criminalised gambling in this city last year. = 去年，法律将这个城市的赌博定为犯罪。
They criminalised the possession of unregistered firearms. = 他们将持有未登记的枪支定为犯罪。


现在完成时:
The country has criminalised hate speech in recent years. = 近年来，这个国家已将仇恨言论定为犯罪。
Society has criminalised certain forms of discrimination.  = 社会已经将某些形式的歧视定为犯罪。


过去完成时:
Before the new law, they hadn't criminalised online harassment. = 在新法律颁布之前，他们还没有将网络骚扰定为犯罪。
The authorities had already criminalised the practice long before the public outcry. = 在公众抗议之前很久，当局就已经将这种做法定为犯罪了。


将来时:
They will criminalise the use of AI in autonomous weapons. = 他们将把在自主武器中使用人工智能定为犯罪。
The new legislation will criminalise certain types of cybercrime. = 新立法将把某些类型的网络犯罪定为犯罪。


虚拟语气:
If they criminalised this behavior, it might deter others. = 如果他们将这种行为定为犯罪，可能会阻止其他人。
It would be better if they criminalised the spread of misinformation. = 如果他们将虚假信息的传播定为犯罪，那就更好了。


被动语态:
Drug use was criminalised decades ago. = 数十年前，吸毒就被定为犯罪了。
The practice has been criminalised under the new law. = 根据新法律，这种做法已被定为犯罪。


疑问句:
Has the government criminalised the distribution of this material? = 政府是否已将这种材料的传播定为犯罪？
Will they criminalise the use of this technology? = 他们会将这项技术的应用定为犯罪吗？


否定句:
The court did not criminalise the defendant's actions. = 法院没有将被告的行为定为犯罪。
They haven't yet criminalised the use of this substance. = 他们尚未将这种物质的用途定为犯罪。


PHRASE->
criminalise the act of = 将…的行为定为犯罪
