counterraiding: /ˌkaʊntərˈreɪdɪŋ/ | v. | 反袭击；反突袭 | The act of conducting a raid in response to a previous raid, often with the aim of disrupting or retaliating against the original attackers.  It involves a swift, aggressive counterattack to neutralize the initial threat.  | Example:  The rebels launched a counterraiding operation after the government forces attacked their base.  反政府武装在政府军袭击他们的基地后发起了反击行动。


近义词: counterattack, reprisal, retaliation, counteroffensive


反义词: surrender, retreat, capitulation


USAGE->

The army counterraids enemy positions regularly. = 军队定期对敌方阵地进行反袭击。
They counterraid any attempt to infiltrate their territory. = 他们反击任何试图渗透他们领土的企图。
