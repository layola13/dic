faucet: /ˈfɔːsɪt/ /ˈfɔːsət/ | n. | 水龙头；龙头 | A faucet is a device that controls the flow of liquid, typically water, from a pipe.  It usually consists of a valve and a spout. | Example:  The faucet in the kitchen is leaking. 厨房的水龙头漏水了。  I turned on the faucet to fill the sink. 我打开水龙头来装满水槽。|近义词：tap, spigot |反义词：None

词源:
The word "faucet" comes from the Old French word "faucett," a diminutive of "fausset," meaning "spigot" or "small cask."  The ultimate origin is likely from the Latin word "faux," meaning "false," referring perhaps to a false bottom or a secondary opening in a container.
