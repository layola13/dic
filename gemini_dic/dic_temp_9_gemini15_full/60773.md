electrolyzer:/ɪˈlɛktrəlaɪzər/| n. |电解槽，电解器|An electrolyzer is a device that uses electricity to drive a chemical reaction that splits water (H₂O) into hydrogen (H₂) and oxygen (O₂).  This process is called electrolysis.  Electrolyzers are used in various applications, including hydrogen production for fuel cells and industrial processes.|例句：The electrolyzer efficiently converts electricity into hydrogen fuel.  电解槽有效地将电力转化为氢燃料。

The company is investing in a new electrolyzer to increase its hydrogen production capacity.该公司正在投资新的电解槽以提高其制氢能力。|近义词：electrolytic cell|反义词：fuel cell (in the context of energy conversion)|名词
