over-aptnesses:/ˈoʊvərˈæptnɪsəz/ | n. | 过于恰当；过分精明；过分巧妙 |  The word "over-aptnesses" describes instances where something is excessively or inappropriately apt, clever, or skillful. It suggests a negative connotation, implying that the aptness is excessive or out of place, potentially leading to undesirable consequences.  This word is rarely used and may be considered somewhat archaic or overly formal.  |  The over-aptnesses of his remarks only served to highlight his lack of genuine empathy.  他的话语过于巧妙，反而突显了他缺乏真正的同理心。 The over-aptnesses of the lawyer's arguments made the jury suspicious. 律师论证过于巧妙，令陪审团起了疑心。 | 近义词: excessive cleverness, undue skill, inappropriate aptitude | 反义词: inaptitude, clumsiness, awkwardness | 名词
