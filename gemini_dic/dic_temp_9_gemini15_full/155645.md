phlebolith:/ˈflɛboʊlɪθ/| n. |静脉石|A phlebolith is a small, calcified concretion (a mass of mineral salts) that forms within a vein.  They are usually asymptomatic and are commonly found incidentally on imaging studies such as X-rays or CT scans.  They are most often found in the pelvic veins.  |Example: The X-ray revealed several small phleboliths in the patient's pelvic area.  X光显示患者盆腔区域有几颗小的静脉石。|近义词：vein calcification|反义词：无
