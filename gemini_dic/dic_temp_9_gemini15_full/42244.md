countdowns:/ˈkaʊntdaʊnz/| n.|倒计时，倒数|A countdown is a sequence of numbers counting down to zero, often used to mark the time remaining before an event.  It can refer to the process itself or the numbers being counted.|例句：The countdown to the launch of the rocket began. 火箭发射的倒计时开始了。|例句：The final countdown before the fireworks display was thrilling. 烟火表演前的最后倒计时令人激动。|近义词：countback, timer|反义词: countup


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The countdown begins at ten. = 倒计时从十开始。
We countdown to the new year every year. = 我们每年都会倒计时迎接新年。

一般过去时:
The countdown ended at zero. = 倒计时在零结束。
They did a countdown before the race started. = 比赛开始前他们进行了倒计时。

现在完成时:
The countdown has finished. = 倒计时结束了。
We have completed the countdown and the event is about to begin. = 我们已经完成了倒计时，活动即将开始。

过去完成时:
The countdown had already begun before we arrived. = 我们到达之前倒计时已经开始了。
They had finished the countdown before the announcement. = 他们在宣布之前就已经完成了倒计时。

将来时:
The countdown will begin in five minutes. = 倒计时将在五分钟后开始。
We will countdown to the moment the new product is revealed. = 我们将倒计时到新产品发布的时刻。

虚拟语气:
If we had more time, we would have a longer countdown. = 如果我们有更多时间，我们会进行更长时间的倒计时。
