carbonatation:/ˌkɑːrbənəˈteɪʃən/| n. |碳酸化；碳化作用|The process of converting a substance into a carbonate or of combining it with carbon dioxide.  This is often used in the context of geology, food science, and materials science.  For example, the carbonation of limestone creates caves, while carbonation of water creates fizzy drinks. |例句：The carbonatation of concrete increases its strength and durability.  混凝土的碳酸化增加了它的强度和耐久性。

The carbonatation of the soda water gave it its characteristic fizz. 苏打水的碳酸化赋予了它特有的气泡。


近义词：carbonation, mineralization (in some contexts)|反义词：decarbonation
