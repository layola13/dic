pre-deposit:/ˌpriːdɪˈpɒzɪt/| n.|预付定金，预存款|A pre-deposit is a payment made in advance, before the main payment is due.  It secures a place, service, or good. |Example:  The hotel requires a pre-deposit of $100 to guarantee your reservation.  酒店需要您预付100美元作为预订保证金。|近义词：down payment, advance payment, deposit, retainer|反义词：post-payment, final payment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The customer makes a pre-deposit for the new furniture. = 顾客为新家具预付定金。
We require a pre-deposit for all large orders. = 所有大订单都需要预付定金。

一般过去时:
She made a pre-deposit for her flight tickets last week. = 她上周为机票预付了定金。
They pre-deposited $500 for the concert tickets. = 他们为演唱会门票预付了500美元。

现在完成时:
He has pre-deposited the money for the down payment on the house. = 他已经预付了房屋首付的钱。
We have pre-deposited funds for the project. = 我们已经为项目预付了资金。

过去完成时:
They had pre-deposited the full amount before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期前已经预付了全部款项。
She had pre-deposited the money for the course before starting. = 她开始课程前已经预付了课程费用。


将来时:

They will pre-deposit the required amount next week. = 他们下周将预付所需金额。
