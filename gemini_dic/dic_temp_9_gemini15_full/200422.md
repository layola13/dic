slackened:/ˈslækənd/| v.|放松；减弱；减缓 | To become looser or less tight; to decrease in intensity, speed, or force. |例句：The rope slackened and the bucket fell. 绳子松了，桶掉下来了。  

例句：The wind slackened in the evening.  傍晚风力减弱了。


近义词：relaxed, loosened, eased, slowed, weakened |反义词：tightened, strengthened, intensified, accelerated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rope slackens under the weight. = 绳子在重量下松弛了。
His grip slackens as he gets tired. = 他累了，抓握也松了。

一般过去时:
The wind slackened after the storm. = 暴风雨过后，风力减弱了。
He slackened his pace as he approached the finish line. = 当他接近终点线时，他放慢了脚步。

现在完成时:
The tension has slackened since the negotiations began. = 自谈判开始以来，紧张局势有所缓和。
The market has slackened recently. = 最近市场低迷。

过去完成时:
The rope had slackened before he could secure it. = 绳子在他能固定它之前就已经松了。
The economy had slackened considerably before the government intervened. = 在政府干预之前，经济已经大幅减缓。


将来时:
The wind will slacken by tomorrow. = 到明天风就会变小。
I think the rain will slacken soon. = 我想雨很快就会变小。

虚拟语气:
If the rope had not slackened, the climber would not have fallen. = 如果绳子没有松动，攀登者就不会摔倒。
If the traffic had slackened, I would have arrived earlier. = 如果交通不拥挤，我会早到。

被动语态:
The pace was slackened to avoid accidents. = 为避免事故，步伐放慢了。
The rules were slackened to attract more tourists. = 规则放宽以吸引更多游客。

疑问句:
Has the pace slackened? = 步伐减慢了吗？
Did the tension slacken after the agreement? = 达成协议后紧张局势是否缓和？

否定句:
The rope did not slacken. = 绳子没有松动。
The market hasn't slackened despite the bad news. = 尽管有坏消息，市场并没有低迷。
