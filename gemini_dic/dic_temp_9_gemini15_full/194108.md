self-imposed:/ˌsɛlfɪmˈpoʊzd/| adj. | 自我强加的，自己造成的 |  imposed upon oneself;  resulting from one's own actions or decisions.  |例句：He suffered from self-imposed exile. 他遭受着自我放逐的痛苦。|例句：Her self-imposed silence was agonizing to witness.  目睹她自我强加的沉默令人痛苦。|近义词：voluntary, self-inflicted, chosen |反义词：imposed, forced, involuntary |形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rule is self-imposed. = 这条规则是自己制定的。
This hardship is self-imposed. = 这种苦难是自己造成的。


一般过去时:
The deadline was self-imposed. = 截止日期是自己设定的。
The isolation was self-imposed as a form of punishment. = 这种隔离是自己作为一种惩罚而强加的。


现在完成时:
He has self-imposed a strict diet. = 他为自己强加了严格的节食。
She has self-imposed a writing schedule. = 她给自己制定了一个写作计划。


过去完成时:
He had self-imposed a ban on social media before the exam. = 考试前，他已经给自己禁止使用社交媒体了。
They had self-imposed a strict code of conduct. = 他们已经给自己制定了一套严格的行为准则。


将来时:
She will self-impose a daily exercise routine. = 她将给自己制定每日锻炼的例行程序。
He will self-impose a media blackout. = 他将自我禁绝媒体。


虚拟语气:
If he had self-imposed stricter limits, he would have succeeded. = 如果他给自己设定更严格的限制，他就会成功。
I wish I hadn’t self-imposed so much pressure. = 我真希望我没有给自己施加这么大的压力。


被动语态:  (Self-imposed is rarely used in the passive voice because it is inherently active.)  N/A


疑问句:
Is this a self-imposed restriction? = 这是自我强加的限制吗？
Was the delay self-imposed or due to external factors? = 延迟是自己造成的还是由于外部因素？


否定句:
The punishment was not self-imposed; it was ordered by the court. = 惩罚不是自己强加的；它是法院判决的。
The limitations were not self-imposed, but rather externally mandated. = 这些限制不是自己强加的，而是外部强制规定的。
