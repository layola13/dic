procurement: /prəˈkjʊrmənt/ | n. | 采购；获得；取得 | The process of obtaining goods, services, or works from an external source.  It involves identifying needs, selecting suppliers, negotiating contracts, and managing the delivery and payment. | 例句：The procurement process for this project was lengthy and complex.  这个项目的采购过程漫长而复杂。


The company's procurement department is responsible for sourcing all materials. 公司的采购部门负责所有材料的采购。


近义词：acquisition, purchasing, sourcing, supply, provision | 反义词：disposal, sale


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company procures all its equipment from a single supplier. = 公司从单一供应商处采购所有设备。
Our team procures the necessary resources for each project. = 我们的团队为每个项目采购必要的资源。


一般过去时:
They procured the land for the new factory last year. = 他们去年购得了新工厂的土地。
The government procured millions of doses of the vaccine. = 政府采购了数百万剂疫苗。


现在完成时:
We have procured all the materials needed for the construction. = 我们已经采购了建筑所需的所有材料。
The organization has procured funding for its new initiative. = 该组织已经为其新计划筹集了资金。


过去完成时:
They had procured the necessary permits before starting construction. = 他们动工前已取得了必要的许可证。
The company had procured the equipment before the project began. = 该公司在项目开始前已采购了设备。


将来时:
We will procure the software next month. = 我们下个月将采购该软件。
The school will procure new computers for the students. = 学校将为学生采购新的电脑。


虚拟语气:
If we had more budget, we would procure a more advanced system. = 如果我们有更多预算，我们会采购更先进的系统。
I wish we could procure the materials more quickly. = 我希望我们能够更快地采购材料。


被动语态:
The materials were procured from a reputable supplier. = 材料是从信誉良好的供应商处采购的。
The contract was procured through a competitive bidding process. = 合同是通过竞争性投标程序获得的。


疑问句:
Where did you procure those rare books? = 你从哪里采购到那些珍稀书籍？
How will the company procure the necessary funding? = 公司将如何获得必要的资金？


否定句:
They did not procure the necessary licenses. = 他们没有获得必要的许可证。
The company failed to procure the equipment on time. = 公司未能按时采购设备。


PHRASE->

procurement officer: 采购员
procurement cost: 采购成本

词源:
From Old French *procurement, from procurer "to procure," from Latin procurare, from pro "forth" + curare "to care for."
