cajoler:/kəˈʒoʊlər/| vt.| 哄骗；劝诱；讨好 | To coax or persuade someone gently or subtly, often by flattery or deception. | 例句：He tried to cajole her into accepting the job. 他试图哄骗她接受这份工作。

例句：She cajoled him into giving her a raise. 她哄骗他给她加薪。


近义词：coax, persuade, wheedle, entice, sweet-talk | 反义词：coerce, force, compel, intimidate


词源：源自古法语 *cajouler，可能与古法语 *cajol “石头”有关，暗示以甜言蜜语或讨好方式“抚平”或“软化”某人。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cajoles his clients into buying more products. = 他哄骗他的客户购买更多产品。
She cajoles her children into eating their vegetables. = 她哄骗她的孩子们吃蔬菜。


一般过去时:
The salesman cajoled the customer into buying a new car. = 销售员哄骗顾客买了一辆新车。
He cajoled his way into the party. = 他用甜言蜜语混进了派对。


现在完成时:
She has cajoled him into agreeing to the deal. = 她已经哄骗他同意这笔交易了。
They have cajoled their way to the top. = 他们通过甜言蜜语爬到了顶峰。


过去完成时:
He had cajoled her into believing his lies. = 他曾经哄骗她相信他的谎言。
They had cajoled the information from the suspect. = 他们已经从嫌疑人那里套取了信息。


将来时:
She will cajole him into changing his mind. = 她将哄骗他改变主意。
He will cajole his way out of trouble. = 他将用甜言蜜语摆脱困境。


虚拟语气:
If I cajoled him enough, he might agree. = 如果我足够哄骗他，他可能会同意。
I wish I could cajole her into helping me. = 我希望我能哄骗她帮助我。


被动语态:
He was cajoled into signing the contract. = 他被哄骗签了合同。
The information was cajoled from the witness. = 信息是从证人那里套取的。


疑问句:
Did he cajole her into doing that? = 他是否哄骗她那样做的？
How did he cajole them into giving him money? = 他是如何哄骗他们给他钱的？


否定句:
He didn't cajole her; he simply persuaded her. = 他没有哄骗她；他只是说服了她。
She wouldn't be cajoled into revealing her secrets. = 她不会被哄骗透露她的秘密。
