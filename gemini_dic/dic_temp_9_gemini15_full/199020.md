signalled: /ˈsɪɡnəld/ | vt. | 发信号，表明，预示 | To give a signal; to indicate or suggest something. | 例句：The police signalled for the car to stop. = 警察示意汽车停车。


The red light signalled danger. = 红灯预示危险。|近义词：indicated, showed, suggested, implied | 反义词：concealed, masked, hid | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He signals his intentions clearly. = 他清楚地表达了他的意图。
The flag signals a message to the ships. = 旗子向船只发出信号。

一般过去时:
She signalled to me from across the street. = 她从街对面向我示意。
The bell signalled the end of the class. = 铃声预示下课了。

现在完成时:
They have signalled their agreement to the proposal. = 他们已经表示同意该提案。
The company has signalled its intention to expand. = 公司已表示有意扩张。

过去完成时:
He had signalled his discontent before the meeting. = 他在会议之前就表达了他的不满。
The doctor had signalled the need for immediate surgery. = 医生已经表示需要立即手术。

将来时:
They will signal their arrival by phone. = 他们将通过电话通知到达。
The train will signal its approach with a whistle. = 火车将用汽笛声预示其即将到达。

虚拟语气:
If he had signalled his concerns earlier, things might have been different. = 如果他早点表达他的担忧，情况可能会有所不同。
I wish he had signalled his intention to leave. = 我希望他早点表示他打算离开。

被动语态:
The danger was signalled by a loud siren. = 危险由响亮的警笛声预示。
Their arrival was signalled by a flash of light. = 他们的到来被一道闪光所预示。

疑问句:
Did he signal for help? = 他是否示意求救？
What did the flashing lights signal? = 闪烁的灯光预示着什么？

否定句:
He didn't signal his intention to quit. = 他没有表示要辞职的意图。
The driver didn't signal before turning. = 司机转弯前没有打转向灯。
