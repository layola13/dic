decuman:/ˈdɛkjʊmən/| adj.| 十的；十倍的；  |From Latin *decumanus, meaning "tenth," from decem "ten."  It refers to something related to the number ten, often in the context of size or quantity. In ancient Roman military camps, the decumanus maximus was the main east-west street, supposedly ten times the width of a normal street.  The term also appears in some botanical contexts, referring to a type of arrangement of leaves or branches. | The decuman gate was the largest gate in the Roman camp.  罗马营地最大的门是十门。|  tenfold, ten times |  single, one
