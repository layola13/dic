promulgate:/ˈprɒmʌlɡeɪt/ /ˈprɑːmʌlɡeɪt/ | vt. |颁布；公布；宣传 |To make (a law, etc.) known officially or publicly; to spread an idea or belief widely.  |例句：The government will promulgate new environmental regulations next month.  政府将于下个月颁布新的环保法规。

例句：The university is trying to promulgate its new vision for the future. 大学正试图宣传其新的未来愿景。


近义词：announce, proclaim, publish, disseminate, propagate | 反义词：suppress, conceal, withhold


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government promulgates new laws regularly. = 政府定期颁布新法律。
She promulgates her ideas through her writings. = 她通过写作传播她的思想。

一般过去时:
The king promulgated a new decree. = 国王颁布了一道新的法令。
They promulgated the good news to everyone. = 他们向大家宣布了这个好消息。

现在完成时:
The organization has promulgated its mission statement. = 该组织已经公布了它的使命宣言。
They have promulgated their findings widely. = 他们已经广泛传播了他们的研究结果。

过去完成时:
The church had promulgated its doctrines for centuries. = 教会几个世纪以来一直宣扬其教义。
Before the war, they had promulgated a message of peace. = 在战争爆发前，他们曾宣扬和平的信息。

将来时:
The company will promulgate its new marketing strategy next quarter. = 公司将在下个季度公布其新的营销战略。
They will promulgate the results of the study soon. = 他们很快就会公布这项研究的结果。

虚拟语气:
If the government promulgated stricter laws, pollution might decrease. = 如果政府颁布更严格的法规，污染可能会减少。
I wish they would promulgate the truth instead of hiding it. = 我希望他们能公布真相而不是隐瞒它。

被动语态:
New regulations were promulgated by the council. = 新规章由议会颁布。
The results of the experiment were widely promulgated. = 实验结果被广泛传播。

疑问句:
When will the new policy be promulgated? = 新政策什么时候公布？
Has the company promulgated its annual report yet? = 公司是否已经公布年度报告？

否定句:
The government did not promulgate the new tax laws until next year. = 政府直到明年才颁布新的税法。
They chose not to promulgate the findings of their research. = 他们选择不公布他们的研究结果。


PHRASE->
promulgate a law = 颁布法律
promulgate a doctrine = 传播教义
promulgate a theory = 传播理论
promulgate a message = 传播信息
widely promulgate = 广泛传播

词源:
From Latin *promulgare, from pro- ("forth") + vulgare ("to make public").  The word's history reflects its core meaning: making something public or known.
