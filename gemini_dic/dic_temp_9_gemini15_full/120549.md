miraculous:/mɪˈrækjʊləs/ | adj. | 奇迹般的；不可思议的；非凡的 |  extraordinary or wonderful; seeming to be caused by a miracle.  |例句：Her recovery was miraculous.  她的康复是奇迹般的。

例句：The view from the mountaintop was absolutely miraculous. 山顶的景色绝对令人惊叹。


近义词：amazing, astonishing, marvelous, wonderful, extraordinary, supernatural

反义词：ordinary, commonplace, mundane, unexceptional


词源：源自中古英语 *miraculos,  来自拉丁语 *miracūlōsus，由miraculum（奇迹）派生而来，miraculum  来自mirārī（惊奇，惊叹）。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The recovery is miraculous.  康复是奇迹般的。
This technology seems miraculous. 这项技术看起来不可思议。


一般过去时:
The escape was miraculous.  逃脱是奇迹般的。
His survival was miraculous. 他的生存是奇迹般的。


现在完成时:
The results have been miraculous. 结果是奇迹般的。
Scientists have achieved miraculous breakthroughs. 科学家们取得了奇迹般的突破。


过去完成时:
The success had been miraculous. 成功是奇迹般的。（指过去某时已完成的奇迹般的事件）
It had been a miraculous escape from the fire.  那是一次奇迹般的逃火。


将来时:
The cure will be miraculous. 治疗将会是奇迹般的。
The new technology promises a miraculous future. 新技术预示着奇迹般的未来。


虚拟语气:
If the treatment were miraculous, it would cure the disease. 如果治疗是奇迹般的，它就能治愈这种疾病。
I wish the outcome had been miraculous. 我希望结果是奇迹般的。


被动语态:
The patient was declared miraculously recovered. 病人被宣布奇迹般地康复。
The problem was miraculously solved. 问题奇迹般地解决了。


疑问句:
Is his recovery truly miraculous?  他的康复真是奇迹般的吗？
Was the event miraculous or just lucky?  那件事是奇迹般的还是只是运气好？


否定句:
The situation was not miraculous, but rather the result of hard work.  情况并非奇迹般，而是努力工作的结果。
The results were not miraculous, despite the initial hope.  结果并非奇迹般，尽管最初抱有希望。
