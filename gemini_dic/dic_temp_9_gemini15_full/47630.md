debilitating: /dɪˈbɪlɪteɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 使衰弱的；使虚弱的；严重的 | Weakening or impairing strength; causing debilitation.  It describes something that significantly weakens or disables a person or thing, often in a physical or mental sense.  |  例句：The debilitating effects of the illness kept him bedridden for weeks.  = 疾病的严重影响使他卧床数周。

The debilitating heat forced us to postpone the outdoor event. = 令人虚弱的酷暑迫使我们推迟了户外活动。


近义词：weakening, enervating, incapacitating, disabling, crippling

反义词：strengthening, invigorating, empowering


词源：From "debilitate" (verb), which comes from Latin *debilitāre, from *dē- (down, away) + *habilis (able, fit).  The "-ating" suffix indicates a present participle, making it an adjective.
