buttress:/ˈbʌtrəs/ | n. v. |扶壁；支持；加固 | A projecting support of stone or brick built against a wall.  A structure built against a wall for support.  To support or strengthen with a buttress. To bolster or reinforce. |例句：The cathedral's massive buttresses are a testament to medieval engineering. 这座大教堂巨大的扶壁是中世纪工程技术的证明。

The old wall was buttressed with additional supports to prevent collapse. 这堵旧墙加固了额外的支撑以防止倒塌。|近义词：support, prop, reinforce, strengthen |反义词：weaken, undermine, destabilize


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The buttresses support the cathedral walls. = 扶壁支撑着大教堂的墙壁。
He buttresses his arguments with solid evidence. = 他用确凿的证据来支持他的论点。


一般过去时:
The builders buttressed the crumbling wall. = 建筑工人加固了摇摇欲坠的墙壁。
She buttressed her claim with irrefutable facts. = 她用无可辩驳的事实来支持她的说法。


现在完成时:
They have buttressed the bridge with steel beams. = 他们用钢梁加固了桥梁。
The government has buttressed the economy with new policies. = 政府用新政策来支撑经济。


过去完成时:
The engineers had buttressed the dam before the flood. = 工程师在洪水到来之前加固了水坝。
She had buttressed her position with years of experience. = 她凭借多年的经验巩固了自己的地位。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

His claims were buttressed by numerous witnesses. = 他的说法得到了许多证人的证实。


疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->

buttress against =  抵御…；防止…
buttress up = 加固；支持
buttress with = 用…支撑；用…加强
economic buttress = 经济支柱

词源:

From Old French *boteresse, from boter "to thrust," from Vulgar Latin *bottare, frequentative of battuere "to beat."  The original meaning referred to a structure that "beat back" or resisted pressure.
