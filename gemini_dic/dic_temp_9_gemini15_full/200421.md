slacken:/ˈslækən/| vt. vi.|放松；减慢；松弛；懈怠|to become looser or less tight, or to make something looser or less tight|The rope slackened and he fell.|绳子松了，他摔了下去。
The pace of the meeting slackened after lunch. |午饭后会议节奏慢了下来。|loosen, relax, ease|tighten, strengthen, accelerate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rope slackens under the weight. = 绳子在重量下松弛了。
He slackens his efforts. = 他放松了努力。

一般过去时:
The wind slackened in the evening. = 晚上风小了。
She slackened her grip on the steering wheel. = 她放松了对方向盘的握力。

现在完成时:
The tension has slackened considerably. = 紧张气氛已大大缓和。
The market has slackened recently. = 最近市场疲软。

过去完成时:
The storm had slackened before we reached the shore. = 我们到达海岸之前风暴已经减弱了。
By the time we arrived, the traffic had slackened. = 我们到达时，交通已经缓解了。

将来时:

I think the wind will slacken by morning. = 我想早上风会变小。

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:
