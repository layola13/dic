planners:/ˈplænərz/| n.|计划者；策划者；日历；记事本|A planner is a person who makes plans, or a tool used for planning.|例句：She's a meticulous planner, always prepared for any eventuality. 她是一位细心的计划者，总是为任何意外情况做好准备。|例句：I use a digital planner to schedule my appointments. 我用电子记事本安排我的约会。|近义词：organizer, scheduler, calendar, diary|反义词：procrastinator

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He plans his day carefully. = 他仔细地计划他的一天。
They plan their vacation every year. = 他们每年都计划他们的假期。

一般过去时:
She planned a surprise party for her friend. = 她为她的朋友策划了一个惊喜派对。
We planned to go to the beach, but it rained. = 我们计划去海滩，但是下雨了。

现在完成时:
They have planned a fundraising event. = 他们已经计划了一个筹款活动。
She has planned her career path meticulously. = 她已经一丝不苟地规划了她的职业道路。

过去完成时:
I had planned to visit my parents, but I got sick. = 我原本计划去看望我的父母，但我病倒了。
They had planned the trip for months. = 他们已经计划了这次旅行几个月了。

将来时:
We will plan our wedding next year. = 我们明年将计划我们的婚礼。
He will plan his retirement carefully. = 他将仔细计划他的退休生活。

虚拟语气:
If I had planned better, I wouldn't have been so stressed. = 如果我计划得更好，我就不会这么紧张了。
I wish I had planned my trip more thoroughly. = 我希望我更周全地计划我的旅行。

被动语态:
The event was planned by a professional event planner. = 这个活动是由一位专业的活动策划人策划的。
The meeting was planned for next week. = 会议计划在下周举行。

疑问句:
Have you planned your weekend yet? = 你计划好周末了吗？
What are you planning to do today? = 你今天计划做什么？

否定句:
I haven't planned anything for the summer. = 我还没有计划好夏天的任何事情。
They didn't plan their route properly. = 他们没有正确地规划路线。
