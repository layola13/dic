independent: /ˌɪndɪˈpɛndənt/ | adj. | 独立的；自主的；自立的 | Free from outside control; not subject to another's authority.  | 例句：She is an independent woman who makes her own decisions. 她是一位独立自主的女性，自己做决定。 | 例句：The two countries are politically independent. 这两个国家在政治上是独立的。 | 近义词：autonomous, self-reliant, self-sufficient | 反义词：dependent, reliant, subordinate |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is independent in his thinking. = 他在思想上是独立的。
She is independent financially. = 她在经济上是独立的。

一般过去时:
The colony became independent in 1960. = 这个殖民地于1960年独立。
He was independent from a young age. = 他从小就独立。

现在完成时:
She has become independent since she moved out. = 自从她搬出去后，她就变得独立了。
They have remained independent throughout the conflict. = 在整个冲突中，他们一直保持独立。

过去完成时:
The country had been independent for many years before the war. = 在战争爆发前，这个国家已经独立多年了。
He had been independent since his graduation. = 他自从毕业后就一直独立。

将来时:
The new nation will be independent next year. = 新国家明年将独立。
She will be financially independent by the time she's 30. = 到她30岁的时候，她将在经济上独立。

虚拟语气:
If I were independent, I would travel the world. = 如果我独立，我就会环游世界。
I wish I were independent of my parents' financial support. = 我希望我不再依赖父母的经济资助。

被动语态:
The country was declared independent. = 该国被宣布独立。
His independence was admired by everyone. = 他的独立受到了所有人的赞赏。

疑问句:
Is he independent enough to live alone? = 他够独立可以独自生活吗？
Are they truly independent from foreign influence? = 他们真的不受外国影响吗？

否定句:
The child is not yet independent. = 这个孩子还不独立。
The company is not independent; it's controlled by a larger corporation. = 这家公司不是独立的；它受一家更大的公司控制。
