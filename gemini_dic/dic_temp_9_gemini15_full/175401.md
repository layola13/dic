ramayana:/rɑːˈmɑːjənə/| n. |罗摩衍那|The Ramayana is one of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India, narrating the life of Prince Rama.  It's a long narrative poem that explores themes of dharma (righteousness), karma (action and consequence), and the battle between good and evil. The epic is attributed to the sage Valmiki and is considered a foundational text of Hinduism.  It has significantly influenced the culture, literature, and art of South and Southeast Asia.|Example: The Ramayana is a story of love, duty, and the triumph of good over evil.  罗摩衍那是一个关于爱情、责任和正义战胜邪恶的故事。|近义词：Mahabharata (another major Indian epic)|反义词：无
