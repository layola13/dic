hyperchlorination: /ˌhaɪpərˌklɔːrɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. |过度加氯；氯气超量处理 | The process of adding an excessive amount of chlorine to water or other substances, often to disinfect but potentially leading to undesirable side effects.  This is frequently used in water treatment to eliminate pathogens, but overdoing it can create harmful byproducts. | Example: The hyperchlorination of the swimming pool water caused several swimmers to experience skin irritation.  过度加氯的泳池水导致几名游泳者出现皮肤刺激。


The hyperchlorination of the drinking water supply was necessary after a contamination incident.饮用水供应的过度加氯在污染事件后是必要的。|近义词: overchlorination, excessive chlorination |反义词: underchlorination, insufficient chlorination


词源:

* **hyper-**:  From Greek "hyper," meaning "over," "above," or "beyond."
* **chlor-**: From Greek "chloros," meaning "greenish-yellow," referring to the color of chlorine gas.
* **-ination**: A suffix indicating the process of adding or treating with something.
