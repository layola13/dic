noncognates:/ˈnɑːnˌkɑːɡneɪts/ | n. | 非同源词 | Words in two languages that do not share a common ancestor.  They are not related etymologically.  They may sound similar or have similar meanings by chance, but their origins are different. | Example: The English word "bank" (financial institution) and the German word "Bank" (bench) are noncognates, despite their similar spelling and pronunciation; they have completely different origins. | 近义词：false friends, unrelated words | 反义词：cognates
