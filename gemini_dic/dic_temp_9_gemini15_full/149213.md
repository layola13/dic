oxyhemoglobin:/ˌɒksiˈhiːməʊɡləʊbɪn/ | n. | 氧合血红蛋白 | Oxygenated hemoglobin; hemoglobin combined with oxygen.  It is the bright red, oxygen-carrying form of hemoglobin found in the arteries. | Example:  The bright red color of arterial blood is due to the presence of oxyhemoglobin.  动脉血鲜红色的原因是含有氧合血红蛋白。|近义词: oxygenated hemoglobin | 反义词: deoxyhemoglobin

词源:
* **oxy-**:  From Greek *oxys* meaning "sharp," "acid," "pointed," later used to mean "oxygen."
* **hemo-**: From Greek *haima* meaning "blood."
* **-globin**: From Greek *globos* meaning "sphere," referring to the globular protein structure of hemoglobin.

Therefore, oxyhemoglobin literally means "oxygen-blood-sphere protein".
