mycetozoa:/ˌmaɪsɪˈtoʊzə/| n.|粘菌；菌状原生生物|Mycetozoa are a group of eukaryotic organisms that exhibit characteristics of both fungi and animals. They are amoeboid in their feeding stage and produce spores in their reproductive stage.  They are often found in moist, shady environments.  |Example: The slime mold is a type of mycetozoa.|近义词：slime mold|反义词：无
