denuding: /dɪˈnuːdɪŋ/ | v. |  剥光；使裸露；使荒芜 | The act of removing the covering of something, especially vegetation or soil from land.  It can also refer to the act of stripping someone of their clothes or possessions. |

例句：

The deforestation denuded the hillsides. =  森林砍伐使山坡变得光秃秃的。

The harsh winter denuded the landscape of its color. =  严酷的冬天使风景失去了色彩。


近义词：stripping, exposing, uncovering, baring, despoiling


反义词：clothing, covering, concealing, protecting, enriching


词性：动词

现在完成时:

The prolonged drought has denuded the land of its vegetation. = 长期的干旱使土地失去了植被。


过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:
The farmer did not denude his fields. = 这位农民没有使他的田地荒芜。
They will not denude the mountain of its trees. = 他们不会使山上的树木消失。


PHRASE->

词源:  Comes from the Latin word "denudare," meaning "to make bare" or "to uncover."  It's a combination of "de-" (meaning "away from" or "off") and "nudare" (meaning "to make naked").
