hit-it-off:/ˌhɪt ɪˈtɑːf/| v. |一见如故，相处融洽|To develop a good rapport or friendship quickly and easily.  It implies an immediate and natural connection between people.|They hit it off immediately and became best friends. 他们一见如故，成为了最好的朋友。|get along, connect, bond|clash, dislike, disagree|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They hit it off well. = 他们相处得很好。
We hit it off instantly. = 我们一见如故。

一般过去时:
They hit it off at the party. = 他们在派对上一见如故。
We hit it off during our first meeting. = 我们在第一次见面时就相处融洽。

现在完成时:
We've hit it off since we first met. = 从我们第一次见面起，我们就相处得很好。
They've hit it off remarkably well. = 他们相处得非常好。

过去完成时:
I hadn't realized they'd hit it off so well before. = 我之前没有意识到他们相处得这么好。
By the end of the trip, they had hit it off wonderfully. = 到旅行结束时，他们相处得非常融洽。

将来时:
I think we'll hit it off. = 我想我们会相处融洽的。
I predict they will hit it off splendidly. = 我预测他们会相处得非常出色。

虚拟语气:
If we'd had more time, we might have hit it off better. = 如果我们有更多的时间，我们可能会相处得更好。
I wish we had hit it off better. = 我希望我们相处得更好。

被动语态: (This phrase is rarely used in the passive voice.)  The passive voice is grammatically possible but unnatural.

疑问句:
Do you think we'll hit it off? = 你认为我们会相处融洽吗？
Did they hit it off at the conference? = 他们在会议上相处融洽吗？

否定句:
They didn't hit it off at all. = 他们一点也不合得来。
We didn't hit it off, unfortunately. = 不幸的是，我们相处得不融洽。
