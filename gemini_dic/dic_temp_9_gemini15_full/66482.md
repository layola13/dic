exerted:/ɪɡˈzɜːrtɪd/ | v. |施加；运用；努力 | to make a great effort; to use power, influence, etc. to produce a particular effect. |例句：She exerted all her strength to open the door. 她使出全身力气打开门。

例句：The government exerted pressure on the company to change its policies. 政府向公司施压，要求其改变政策。

近义词：applied, employed, used, put forth, strained

反义词：relaxed, neglected, ignored


USAGE->

He exerts considerable influence over his employees. = 他对他员工施加相当大的影响力。
She exerts herself to meet deadlines. = 她努力赶上截止日期。

一般过去时:
The hurricane exerted a devastating force on the coastline. = 飓风对海岸线施加了毁灭性的力量。

现在完成时:
They have exerted themselves to achieve this success. = 他们为了取得这个成功而付出了巨大的努力。
She has exerted considerable pressure on him to change his mind. = 她对他施加了很大的压力来改变他的想法。

过去完成时:

The company had exerted its influence to secure the contract. = 公司已经运用其影响力来确保合同。

将来时:

The new manager will exert more control over the team. = 新经理将对团队施加更多控制。

虚拟语气:

More effort should be exerted to improve the situation. = 应该付出更多努力来改善这种情况。

疑问句:
