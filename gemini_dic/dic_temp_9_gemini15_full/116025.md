marsupial:/mɑːrˈsuːpiəl/| n. |有袋动物|A marsupial is a mammal that carries its young in a pouch.  Marsupials are characterized by their unique reproductive system, where the young are born in a relatively undeveloped state and complete their development within the mother's pouch.  |Example: Kangaroos and koalas are examples of marsupials.|近义词：pouched mammal|反义词：placental mammal
