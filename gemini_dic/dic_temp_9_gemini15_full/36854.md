colophany:/kəˈlɒfəni/| n. |松香；古希腊松脂|A resin obtained from pine trees, especially from the sap of various species of Pinus. It is a hard, brittle, transparent substance, yellowish or brownish in color, and has a characteristic odor.  It is used in various applications, including varnishes, adhesives, and fluxes in soldering.|The violin maker used colophony on the bow to improve grip and sound.|rosin, resin|—|n.
