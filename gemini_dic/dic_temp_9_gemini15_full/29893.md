cauterising: /ˈkɔːtəraɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 烧灼；止血 | The act of burning tissue to destroy it or to stop bleeding.  It involves using heat, chemicals, or electricity to destroy the affected area. | 例句：The surgeon cauterised the wound to stop the bleeding. = 外科医生烧灼伤口以止血。  The doctor used a cautery instrument to cauterise the bleeding vessel. = 医生使用烧灼器来烧灼出血的血管。 | 近义词：searing, burning, scarifying | 反义词：healing, soothing

词源:

From  Late Latin *cauterizare, from Latin cautērium "hot iron," from cautere "to burn," from *caudere "to burn."  The word reflects the historical method of using a heated iron to cauterize wounds.
