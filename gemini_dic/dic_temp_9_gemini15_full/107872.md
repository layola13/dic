largesses:/ˈlɑːrdʒɪsɪz/| n.|慷慨的赠与；施舍；赏赐| generous gifts; donations; benefactions.  The word implies a large or extravagant gift, often given by someone in a position of power or wealth.|The king showered his loyal subjects with largesses.|近义词：gifts, donations, benefactions, grants|反义词：stinginess, miserliness

词源:
From Old French *largesse, from large "broad, wide, ample," from Latin largus.  The meaning shifted from "abundance" to "generous gift" over time.
