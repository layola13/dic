identifiably: /aɪˌdɛntɪˈfaɪəbli/ | adv. | 可辨认地，可识别地 |  Clearly able to be identified or recognized.  | The painting was identifiably  a Monet. 这幅画明显是莫奈的。  | recognizably, discernibly | indistinguishably, unidentifiably | 副词


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and therefore doesn't have tenses like verbs.  It describes how an action is performed or a state exists.

Example sentences demonstrating its usage in different sentence structures:


Simple sentence:
The bird was identifiably a robin. = 那只鸟明显是知更鸟。


Complex sentence:
Because the signature was identifiably forged, the document was deemed invalid. = 由于签名明显是伪造的，该文件被认为无效。

词源:

The word "identifiably" derives from the verb "identify," which comes from the Latin words "idem" (the same) and "facere" (to make).  The suffix "-ably" indicates the possibility or capability of being identified.
