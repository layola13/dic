quasi-dangerous:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈdeɪndʒərəs/| adj.|准危险的，潜在危险的 |  Almost but not completely dangerous; presenting a degree of risk or threat.  It suggests a situation that has the potential to be dangerous but isn't necessarily so at the present moment.  The word implies a level of uncertainty or ambiguity regarding the actual level of danger. | 例句：The abandoned mine shaft was quasi-dangerous;  it wasn't immediately life-threatening, but there were risks of collapse and injury.  废弃的矿井准危险；它不是立即危及生命的，但存在坍塌和受伤的风险。

The situation is quasi-dangerous, requiring careful monitoring. 情况比较危险，需要仔细监控。| 近义词：potentially dangerous, somewhat dangerous, risky, hazardous| 反义词：safe, secure, harmless
