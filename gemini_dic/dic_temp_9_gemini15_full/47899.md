decent:/ˈdiːsnt/ | adj. | 体面的；像样的；相当好的 |  Decent means of acceptable quality or standard; fairly good.  It can also refer to moral behavior. |

例句：

He's a decent guy. = 他是个好人。

She received a decent salary for the job. = 她这份工作拿到了不错的薪水。


近义词：proper, respectable, adequate, satisfactory, fair, reasonable

反义词：improper, indecent, inadequate, unsatisfactory, poor, bad


词源:

源自古法语 *desent,  由拉丁语 *decēns 派生而来，意思是“适合的，得体的”。


USAGE->

一般现在时:

He is a decent person. = 他是一个体面的人。

This restaurant serves decent food. = 这家餐馆供应不错的食物。


一般过去时:

The movie was decent. = 这部电影还不错。

They had a decent meal. = 他们吃了一顿像样的饭。


现在完成时:

I have had decent experiences with this company. = 我和这家公司有过不错的经历。

We have done a decent job. = 我们做得不错。


过去完成时:

They had already eaten a decent dinner before the party. = 他们在聚会之前已经吃过一顿像样的晚餐了。

She had finished a decent amount of work before the deadline. = 在截止日期之前，她已经完成了一定数量的工作。


将来时:

We will have a decent holiday. = 我们将拥有一个愉快的假期。

The project will be decent if we work hard. = 如果我们努力工作，这个项目将会做得不错。


虚拟语气:

If he had a decent job, he wouldn't be so stressed. = 如果他有一份像样的工作，他就不会这么压力大了。

I wish I had a decent car. = 我希望我有一辆像样的车。


被动语态:

A decent price was offered. = 一个不错的价格被开出了。

疑问句:

否定句:

His behavior wasn't decent. = 他的行为不体面。
