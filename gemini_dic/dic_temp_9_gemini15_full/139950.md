odontocete:/oʊˈdɒntəˌsiːt/| n. |齿鲸|A toothed whale.  Odontocetes are a suborder of cetaceans characterized by their possession of teeth, used for capturing prey.  They are highly intelligent and diverse, including dolphins, porpoises, and other toothed whales.  They echolocate to navigate and hunt. |Example: Dolphins are a well-known example of an odontocete.  海豚是齿鲸的一个众所周知的例子。  Many odontocetes are highly social animals. 许多齿鲸是高度群居的动物。|近义词:toothed whale|反义词:mysticetes (baleen whale)
