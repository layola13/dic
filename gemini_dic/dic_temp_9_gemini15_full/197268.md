sharpen:/ˈʃɑːrpən/| vt. |削尖；使敏锐；使清晰；磨快|To make or become sharp or sharper.|The carpenter sharpened his chisel before starting work. 木匠在开始工作之前磨快了他的凿子。  He sharpened his wit in the debate. 他在辩论中磨练了他的才智。| hone, refine, enhance | blunt, dull |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sharpens his pencils every day. 他每天都削铅笔。
The teacher sharpens the students' minds with challenging questions. 老师用具有挑战性的问题来磨练学生的头脑。

一般过去时:
I sharpened my knife before cutting the vegetables. 我切菜之前磨了刀。
She sharpened her presentation skills by practicing her speech. 她通过练习演讲来提高她的演示技巧。

现在完成时:
He has sharpened his skills through years of practice. 他通过多年的练习磨练了他的技能。
The company has sharpened its focus on its core products. 公司已经更加关注其核心产品。

过去完成时:
She had sharpened her pencils before the exam. 她在考试前已经削好了铅笔。
They had sharpened their arguments before the meeting. 他们在会议前已经完善了他们的论点。

将来时:
I will sharpen my axe before chopping wood. 我砍柴之前会磨斧头。
The company will sharpen its competitive edge by investing in new technology. 公司将通过投资新技术来增强其竞争优势。

虚拟语气:
If I had sharpened my knife, I wouldn't have had so much trouble cutting the bread. 如果我磨过刀，就不会在切面包时这么费劲了。
I wish I had sharpened my pencils before the test. 我希望我在考试前把铅笔削好了。

被动语态:
The knives are sharpened regularly in the kitchen. 厨房里的刀具定期磨刀。
His arguments were sharpened by the opposing counsel. 他的论点被对方律师强化了。

疑问句:

否定句:
He didn't sharpen his pencils before the exam. 他考试前没有削铅笔。
The company did not sharpen its focus on its core business. 公司没有专注于其核心业务。
