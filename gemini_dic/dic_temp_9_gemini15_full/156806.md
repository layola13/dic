pigeonholers: /ˈpɪdʒənˌhoʊlər/ | n. | 把…装进鸽舍的人；（贬义）按类型划分的人；墨守成规的人 |  Someone who pigeonholes things or people.  The term often carries a negative connotation, implying a rigid and unthinking categorization of individuals or ideas.  |  He's a notorious pigeonholer, always trying to fit people into neat little boxes. 他是个臭名昭著的按类型划分的人，总是试图把人们归入整齐的小类别。  |近义词: categorizer, classifier, typer | 反义词: open-minded person, freethinker


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He pigeonholes every new employee. = 他把每个新员工都归类了。
They pigeonhole artists based on their style. = 他们根据艺术家的风格对他们进行分类。


一般过去时:
She pigeonholed the documents by subject. = 她按主题将文件分类。
The professor pigeonholed the students into different learning groups. = 教授把学生分成不同的学习小组。


现在完成时:
He has pigeonholed himself into a specific niche. = 他把自己归入了一个特定的领域。
The company has pigeonholed its products into different market segments. = 公司已将其产品细分为不同的细分市场。


过去完成时:
They had pigeonholed the evidence before the trial. = 审判前他们已对证据进行了分类。
She had pigeonholed him as a troublemaker before even meeting him. = 甚至在见面之前，她就把他归类为捣乱分子。


将来时:
They will pigeonhole the applicants based on their qualifications. = 他们将根据申请人的资格对他们进行分类。
He will pigeonhole the new research findings into existing theories. = 他将把新的研究成果归入现有的理论中。


虚拟语气:
If I were him, I wouldn't pigeonhole my colleagues like that. = 如果我是他，我不会那样给我的同事分类。
I wish they wouldn't pigeonhole me as just a writer. = 我希望他们不要把我仅仅归类为一个作家。


被动语态:
The new ideas were pigeonholed and ignored. = 新的想法被归类并被忽略了。
The students are often pigeonholed by their teachers. = 学生们经常被老师们归类。


疑问句:
Why do you pigeonhole people so readily? = 为什么你这么轻易地给人们分类？
Does she pigeonhole all her clients in the same way? = 她是否以同样的方式对所有客户进行分类？


否定句:
I don't pigeonhole people based on their appearance. = 我不会根据外表来给人们分类。
They didn't pigeonhole the innovative ideas. = 他们没有把创新的想法归类。


PHRASE->
pigeonhole someone as... = 把某人归类为...
pigeonhole something into... = 把某物归类到...
pigeonhole a problem = 将问题分类
be pigeonholed = 被归类
get pigeonholed = 被归类
