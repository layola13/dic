prevaricators:/prɪˈværɪkeɪtərz/| n.|支吾其辞者，闪烁其词者|A person who prevaricates; someone who avoids giving a direct answer to a question.  They often use evasive language or misleading statements to conceal the truth or avoid committing themselves.|例句：The prevaricators in the trial made it difficult for the jury to reach a verdict. 审判中的支吾其辞者使陪审团难以达成裁决。


The defense lawyer's prevarications about his client's alibi raised serious doubts.  辩护律师对其当事人不在场证明的闪烁其词引起了严重的怀疑。|近义词：equivocator, tergiversator, obfuscator|反义词：truth-teller, straightforward person|名词

词源:
From Latin *praevaricārī, from prae- ("before") + varicārī ("to straddle, to go astray").  The original meaning related to deviating from a straight path, which evolved into the sense of evasiveness and dishonesty in speech.
