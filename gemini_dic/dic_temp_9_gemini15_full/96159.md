indep: /ɪnˈdɛp/ | adj. | 独立的；自主的 | Independent means free from outside control or influence; self-governing.  It suggests autonomy and self-reliance. |例句：She is an independent woman and makes her own decisions. 她是一位独立女性，自己做决定。|例句：The country declared its independence from colonial rule.  这个国家宣布脱离殖民统治，独立了。|近义词：autonomous, self-reliant, self-sufficient, sovereign |反义词：dependent, reliant, subordinate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is independent in his thinking. = 他思想独立。
The two countries maintain independent policies. = 这两个国家保持独立的政策。

一般过去时:
She was independent from a young age. = 她从小就独立。
The company became independent after the merger. = 并购后，公司获得了独立性。

现在完成时:
They have become increasingly independent over the years. = 这些年来，他们变得越来越独立。
The project has achieved independence from external funding. = 该项目已摆脱外部资金的依赖。

过去完成时:
He had been independent for many years before he married.  = 他结婚前已经独立生活多年了。
The nation had already achieved independence before the war ended. = 在战争结束之前，这个国家就已经独立了。

将来时:
She will be independent when she turns eighteen. = 她十八岁时将会独立。
The region will soon gain independence. = 该地区很快就会获得独立。

虚拟语气:
If she were independent, she would travel the world. = 如果她独立自主，她就会周游世界。
I wish I were more independent. = 我希望我更独立一些。

被动语态:
The country was declared independent in 1947. = 这个国家于1947年宣布独立。
His decision was made independently of external pressure. = 他的决定不受外界压力影响而独立做出。

疑问句:
Is he truly independent in his actions? = 他的行动真的独立吗？
Are these two events truly independent of each other? = 这两件事真的彼此独立吗？

否定句:
He is not independent enough to live alone. = 他不够独立，无法独自生活。
The two factors are not independent; they influence each other. = 这两个因素不是独立的；它们相互影响。
