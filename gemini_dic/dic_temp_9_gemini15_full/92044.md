hyperphoria:/ˌhaɪpərˈfɔːriə/ | n. |垂直性斜视，上隐斜 | A condition in which one eye tends to turn upward, causing double vision.  It is a type of heterophoria, a latent deviation of the eyes that is only present when binocular fusion is broken. | Example: The patient presented with symptoms of diplopia and was diagnosed with right hyperphoria.  患者出现复视症状，被诊断为右眼上隐斜。 | 近义词：vertical heterophoria, superior rectus muscle weakness | 反义词：hypophoria
