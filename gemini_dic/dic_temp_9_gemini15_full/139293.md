oblivious:/əˈblɪviəs/ | adj. | 不知道的；未察觉的；遗忘的 | Not aware of or concerned about what is happening around one.  | 例句：He seemed oblivious to the noise and confusion around him. 他似乎没有注意到周围的喧嚣和混乱。

例句：She was completely oblivious of the danger. 她完全没有意识到危险。


近义词：unaware, unconscious, insensible, inattentive | 反义词：aware, conscious, mindful


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is oblivious to the mess. = 他没有注意到这烂摊子。
They are oblivious of the risks. = 他们没有意识到风险。


一般过去时:
She was oblivious to his feelings. = 她没有察觉到他的感情。
He remained oblivious to the changes. = 他对变化浑然不觉。


现在完成时:
I have been oblivious to the news. = 我一直没有注意到这个消息。
They have remained oblivious to the problem. = 他们一直没有注意到这个问题。


过去完成时:
She had been oblivious to his suffering. = 她一直没有注意到他的痛苦。
They had remained oblivious to the warning signs. = 他们一直没有注意到警告信号。


将来时:
He will be oblivious to the consequences. = 他将不会意识到后果。
She will remain oblivious to the truth. = 她将依然对真相一无所知。


虚拟语气:
If he were not oblivious, he would have reacted differently. = 如果他没有视而不见，他会做出不同的反应。
I wish I hadn't been so oblivious to her needs. = 我真希望我以前没有对她那么冷漠无视。


被动语态:
The danger was largely oblivious to the public. = 公众基本上没有意识到危险。
The problem was completely oblivious to the authorities. = 当局完全没有注意到这个问题。


疑问句:
Are you oblivious to the fact that he lied? = 你没注意到他撒谎了吗？
Was she oblivious to the criticism? = 她没有注意到批评吗？


否定句:
He was not oblivious to the criticism. 他并非没有注意到批评。
She wasn't oblivious to the implications. = 她并非没有意识到其含义。


PHRASE->
