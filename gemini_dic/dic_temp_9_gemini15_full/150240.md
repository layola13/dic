pandered:/ˈpændərd/| v.| 迎合；逢迎；曲意逢迎 | To cater to or exploit the weaknesses or desires of others. To act in a subservient manner in order to please someone, often in a way that is morally questionable. | 例句：The politician pandered to the prejudices of his constituents to win their votes.  这位政治家迎合选民的偏见以赢得选票。

The magazine pandered to the public's fascination with celebrity gossip. 这本杂志迎合了公众对名人八卦的兴趣。


近义词：appease, indulge, cater to,  flatter,  kowtow

反义词：oppose, defy, resist, antagonize,  ignore


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He panders to the wealthy. = 他逢迎富人。
The company panders to consumer demand. = 这家公司迎合消费者的需求。


一般过去时:
She pandered to his every whim. = 她百依百顺地满足他的每一个愿望。
The government pandered to the media's demands. = 政府迎合了媒体的要求。


现在完成时:
They have pandered to the public's fear of crime. = 他们一直迎合公众对犯罪的恐惧。
The media has pandered to sensationalism. = 媒体一直迎合耸人听闻的新闻。


过去完成时:
He had pandered to the crowd for years before his downfall. = 在他垮台之前，他已经多年来迎合大众了。
The artist had pandered to the tastes of the wealthy patrons. =  这位艺术家多年来一直迎合富有的赞助人的口味。


将来时:
She will pander to the crowd to win their support. = 她将迎合大众以赢得他们的支持。
The company will pander to the latest trends. = 公司将迎合最新的潮流。


虚拟语气:
If he pandered to the public, he would win the election. = 如果他迎合公众，他就会赢得选举。
I wish the media wouldn't pander to violence. = 我希望媒体不要迎合暴力。


被动语态:
His ideas were pandered to by the media. = 他的想法被媒体迎合了。
The public's desires were pandered to by the marketing campaign. = 公众的愿望被营销活动迎合了。


疑问句:
Does he pander to the public's opinion? = 他是否迎合公众的意见？
Did the author pander to popular taste? = 作者是否迎合了大众口味？


否定句:
He did not pander to the pressure. = 他没有屈服于压力。
The artist refused to pander to commercial interests. = 这位艺术家拒绝迎合商业利益。


PHRASE->

pander to the lowest common denominator:  迎合最低的共同点
pander to someone's vanity: 奉承某人的虚荣心
pander to the masses:  迎合大众
pander to the demand: 迎合需求
pander to prejudice:  迎合偏见

词源:

源自法语“pandre,”  最终源自希腊语“pandēros,” 指的是一种妓院的老板或中间人。  其含义的演变反映了迎合他人欲望以谋取私利的行为。
