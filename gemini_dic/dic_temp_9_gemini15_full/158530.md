pleonasm:/ˈpliːənæzəm/| n. |累赘;冗余;语义重复|The use of more words than are necessary to convey a meaning; redundancy in speech or writing.  It often involves using two words or phrases with nearly identical meanings.  While sometimes considered a stylistic flaw, pleonasm can also be used for emphasis or clarity.|例句：The phrase "free gift" is a pleonasm because all gifts are free.  (免费礼物这个短语是累赘，因为所有的礼物都是免费的。)  The expression "a burning fire" is another example. (熊熊大火也是一个例子。) |近义词：redundancy, tautology, superfluity|反义词：conciseness, brevity
