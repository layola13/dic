pseudo-Victorian: /ˌsuːdoʊvɪkˈtɔːriən/ | adj. | 假维多利亚式，仿维多利亚风格的 |

英文解析：The term "pseudo-Victorian" describes something that imitates or styles itself after the Victorian era (roughly 1837-1901) in Britain, but without genuine historical authenticity.  It might adopt visual elements like ornate furniture, dark wood, or certain fashion styles, but lack the underlying social, political, or technological context of the actual Victorian period.  It can be used ironically or derisively, suggesting a superficial or kitschy imitation.


例句：

The restaurant's décor was pseudo-Victorian, with heavy drapes and dark wood, but the menu was decidedly modern. = 这家餐馆的装饰是仿维多利亚风格的，有厚重的窗帘和深色的木头，但菜单却绝对是现代的。


The novel's plot, while set in the Victorian era, felt pseudo-Victorian in its overly romanticized and simplistic depiction of the period. = 这部小说的情节虽然设定在维多利亚时代，但在其过于浪漫化和简单化的时代描写中，感觉像是假维多利亚风格的。


近义词：faux-Victorian, imitation Victorian, mock-Victorian


反义词：authentic Victorian, genuine Victorian, truly Victorian


词性：形容词
