complexions:/kəmˈplɛkʃənz/ | n. |面色，肤色；外观；局面 |The natural appearance of a person's skin, especially as an indication of health or feelings.  It can also refer to the general appearance or character of something. |例句：Her complexion improved after a week at the seaside.  她海边度假一周后，气色好了。

例句：The complexion of the political situation is changing rapidly.  政治局势的面貌正在迅速变化。

近义词：appearance, hue, coloring, aspect

反义词：N/A (Not applicable;  it's not a word that has a direct opposite)


词源:
From Middle English *complexioun, from Old French complexion, from Latin complexio "combination, structure," from  complectere "to entwine, embrace," from com- "together" + plectere "to weave, braid."  The sense evolved from the physical interweaving of humors in the body to the resulting outward appearance of the skin.
