diamond:/ˈdaɪəmənd/| n. |钻石；菱形；金刚石 |A diamond is a precious stone consisting of a clear and colorless crystalline form of pure carbon. It is the hardest naturally occurring substance.  Diamonds are highly valued for their beauty, rarity, and durability, and are often used in jewelry. |例句：She received a diamond ring as a gift. 她收到一枚钻石戒指作为礼物。

例句：The baseball diamond was freshly painted. 棒球场上的菱形区域刚被重新粉刷。|近义词：gem, jewel |反义词：N/A
