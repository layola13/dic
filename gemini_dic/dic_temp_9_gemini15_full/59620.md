ecchymosis:/ˌɛkiˈmoʊsɪs/| n.|瘀斑，紫癜|A discoloration of the skin resulting from bleeding underneath, typically caused by trauma.  It is a larger area of bleeding under the skin than a petechia.|例句：The patient presented with ecchymosis on his left arm after the fall. 患者摔倒后左臂出现瘀斑。

例句：The doctor noted extensive ecchymosis around the impact site. 医生注意到撞击部位周围有广泛的瘀斑。


近义词：bruise, contusion, hematoma|反义词：None specifically, but terms describing unblemished skin could be considered antonyms.


词源: From Late Latin *ecchymosis, from Greek ἐγχύμωσις (ekchýmōsis), from ἐγχέω (ekhéō) "to pour in," from ἐν (en) "in" + χέω (chéō) "to pour."  The word literally means "a pouring in."
