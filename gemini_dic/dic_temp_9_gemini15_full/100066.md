interpolater: /ɪnˈtɜːrpəleɪtər/ | n. | 内插器；插值器 | A device or program that performs interpolation.  It estimates values within a range of known data points.  | Example: The interpolater smoothed the jagged lines on the graph, creating a more accurate representation of the data.  内插器使图表上的锯齿线变得平滑，从而更准确地表示数据。 |近义词: interpolator, estimator | 反义词:  extrapolator (for values outside the known range)

词源:  The word "interpolater" is derived from the verb "interpolate," which comes from the Latin words "inter" (between) and "polare" (to polish or smooth).  The original meaning referred to inserting something into a text or conversation.  In mathematics and computer science, the meaning evolved to refer to estimating values between known data points.
