redargue:/ˌriːˈɑːrɡjuː/| vt.| 反驳；辩驳 |To argue again or in a different way; to dispute or contest.  It implies a more forceful and detailed counter-argument than simply disagreeing.|例句：He redargued the judge's ruling, citing new evidence. 他以新的证据反驳了法官的裁决。|例句：The defense lawyer redargued the prosecution's case, pointing out inconsistencies in their testimony.  辩护律师反驳了检方的说法，指出了他们证词中的矛盾之处。|近义词：rebut, refute, contest, dispute |反义词：agree, concede, accept |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He redargues the points raised in the meeting. = 他反驳了会议上提出的论点。
They redargue the validity of the contract. = 他们辩驳合同的有效性。


一般过去时:
