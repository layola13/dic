prerepresentation:/ˌpriːˌrɛprɪzenˈteɪʃən/| n. |预先呈现；预演；预表 |The act or process of presenting something beforehand, often as a preparation or rehearsal for a later, more formal presentation.  It can also refer to something that symbolizes or foreshadows something else. |例句：The prerepresentation of the play allowed the actors to work out any kinks before opening night. 预演让演员们能在开幕夜之前解决任何问题。

The prerepresentation of the data showed a clear trend. 数据的预先呈现显示了一个清晰的趋势。|近义词：preview, rehearsal, prefiguration, foreshadowing |反义词：postpresentation, aftermath
