metascientifically: /ˌmɛtəˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪkli/ | adv. | 元科学地 | In a manner relating to metascience, the study of the underlying assumptions, methods, and implications of science.  It examines the philosophical and sociological aspects of scientific practice. | Example: The philosopher analyzed the scientific findings metascientifically, questioning the underlying assumptions and potential biases.  |近义词: philosophically (regarding science), epistemologically (regarding knowledge) | 反义词: empirically (based on observation), practically (based on practice)


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and thus doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  Its usage is consistent; it modifies a verb to indicate a metascientific approach.

Example 1:  The research was evaluated metascientifically, considering its methodology and philosophical implications. = 这项研究是从元科学的角度进行评估的，考虑到了其方法论和哲学意义。

Example 2:  He interpreted the data metascientifically, focusing on the social context of the experiment. = 他从元科学的角度解读了数据，关注实验的社会背景。


PHRASE->

Phrases using "metascientifically" are less common due to the word's specialized nature.  However, one could construct phrases like:

1. metascientifically analyzed: 元科学地分析
2. metascientifically examined: 元科学地检查
3. metascientifically investigated: 元科学地调查
4. metascientifically evaluated: 元科学地评估
5. metascientifically approached: 元科学地方法
