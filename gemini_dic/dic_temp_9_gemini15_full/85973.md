heads-up:/ˈhedz ʌp/| n.|预先警告，提示|A heads-up is a warning or piece of information given in advance.|例句：Give me a heads-up before you come over. 来之前先告诉我一声。|例句：I appreciate the heads-up about the meeting. 我感谢你提前告知会议的消息。|近义词：warning, alert, notice|反义词：surprise, ambush


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He gives me a heads-up before every meeting. 他每次会议前都会给我预先警告。
She always gives her team a heads-up about deadlines. 她总是提前告知她的团队截止日期。


一般过去时:
He gave me a heads-up about the traffic jam. 他提前告知我交通堵塞的情况。
She gave us a heads-up before the big announcement.  她在重大宣布之前给我们预先做了提示。


现在完成时:
They have given us a heads-up about the changes in policy. 他们已经提前告知我们政策上的变化。
She has given me a heads-up on several occasions. 她已经多次给我预先警告了。


过去完成时:
He had given me a heads-up, so I was prepared. 他已经提前告诉我了，所以我做好了准备。
They had given us a heads-up about the potential problems. 他们已经提前告知我们潜在的问题。


将来时:
He will give me a heads-up when he arrives. 他到达时会提前通知我。
She will give the team a heads-up before the project starts. 项目开始前她会提前告知团队。


虚拟语气:
If he had given me a heads-up, I wouldn't have been so surprised. 如果他提前告诉我，我就不会这么惊讶了。
I wish she had given me a heads-up about the exam. 我希望她能提前告诉我考试的事情。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
I didn't get a heads-up about the cancellation. 我没有收到关于取消的预先通知。
