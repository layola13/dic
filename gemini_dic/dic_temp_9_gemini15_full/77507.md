gazing:/ˈɡeɪzɪŋ/| v.|凝视，注视 |To look steadily and intently, often with wonder or admiration.  |例句：She was gazing at the beautiful sunset. 她凝视着美丽的日落。

词源:
The word "gaze" ultimately derives from the Old French word "gazer," meaning "to look," which in turn has roots in the Germanic languages.  The exact Germanic origin is debated, but possible connections exist to words implying "to see" or "to watch."
