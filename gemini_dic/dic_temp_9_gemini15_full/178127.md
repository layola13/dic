recognising: /ˈrekəɡnaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 认出；承认；认可 | Identifying someone or something that you have seen, heard, or experienced before.  It also implies acknowledging or accepting the truth or existence of something. | 例句：I recognised her from the photograph. = 我从照片上认出了她。


例句：The government is recognising the importance of renewable energy. = 政府正在认识到可再生能源的重要性。


近义词：identifying, acknowledging, appreciating, realising


反义词：ignoring, overlooking, denying, disregarding


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I am recognising her face now. = 我现在认出她的脸了。
He recognises the need for change. = 他认识到改变的必要性。

一般过去时:
I recognised him immediately. = 我立刻认出了他。
She recognised her mistake. = 她意识到了自己的错误。

现在完成时:
I have recognised her from previous meetings. = 我从以前的会议上就认出她了。
We have recognised the problem for some time.  = 我们已经认识到这个问题有一段时间了。

过去完成时:
I had recognised his voice before he spoke. = 他还没说话我就认出了他的声音。
They had recognised the danger before it was too late. = 危险发生之前他们就已经意识到了。

将来时:
I will recognise her when I see her. = 我看到她的时候就会认出她。
We will recognise his contribution to the project. = 我们将认可他对项目的贡献。

虚拟语气:
If I recognised him, I would have said hello. = 如果我认出他了，我会和他打招呼的。
I wish I had recognised the warning signs. = 我真希望我当时能意识到警告信号。

被动语态:
His talent was recognised by the judges. = 他的才能被评委们认可了。
The problem is being recognised by more people. = 这个问题正被越来越多的人认识到。

疑问句:
Do you recognise that song? = 你认得这首歌吗？
Does he recognise the gravity of the situation? = 他认识到局势的严重性吗？

否定句:
I didn't recognise him at first. = 我一开始没认出他。
They didn't recognise their own mistakes. = 他们没有认识到自己的错误。
