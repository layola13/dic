hinderest: /ˈhɪndərɪst/ | v. | 阻碍(古英语) | To obstruct or impede; to prevent from progressing. This word is archaic and rarely used in modern English. It is the second-person singular present subjunctive form of the verb "hinder." | Example:  If thou hinderest this good work, God will punish thee.  (如果你阻碍这项善举，上帝会惩罚你。) | 近义词：obstruct, impede, hamper, obstruct, prevent | 反义词：aid, assist, facilitate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hinders my progress. = 他阻碍我的进步。
The heavy rain hinders our travel plans. = 大雨阻碍了我们的旅行计划。

一般过去时:
The storm hindered our journey. = 暴风雨阻碍了我们的旅程。
He hindered the investigation. = 他妨碍了调查。


现在完成时:
The snow has hindered travel for days. = 大雪已经阻碍了多日的出行。
They have hindered the development of the project. = 他们阻碍了项目的进展。


过去完成时:
The lack of funding had hindered the project's progress. = 资金不足已经阻碍了项目的进展。
The previous administration had hindered efforts to reform the system. = 前一届政府阻碍了改革体系的努力。


将来时:
The bad weather will hinder the flight. = 恶劣的天气将阻碍航班起飞。
Increased regulations will hinder economic growth. = 越来越严格的法规将阻碍经济增长。


虚拟语气:
If he hindered the investigation, he would be punished. = 如果他妨碍调查，他将受到惩罚。
If they had not hindered the project, it would have been completed on time. = 如果他们没有阻碍项目，项目就会按时完成。


被动语态:

His career was hindered by his lack of experience. = 他的事业因缺乏经验而受阻。


疑问句:

否定句:
This plan does not hinder our operation. = 这个计划并没有阻碍我们的运作。
Nothing can hinder our determination. = 没有什么能够阻碍我们的决心。
