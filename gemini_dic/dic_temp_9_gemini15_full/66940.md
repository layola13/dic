expensive:/ɪkˈspɛnsɪv/| adj.|昂贵的，价格高的|Costing a lot of money.  Something is expensive if it requires a large amount of money to purchase or obtain.|That diamond necklace is expensive.|近义词：costly, pricey, dear|反义词：cheap, inexpensive, affordable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
That car is expensive. = 那辆车很贵。
These shoes are expensive. = 这些鞋子很贵。

一般过去时:
The hotel was expensive. = 这家酒店很贵。
The concert tickets were expensive. = 音乐会的门票很贵。

现在完成时:
Gas has become expensive recently. = 最近汽油变得很贵了。
This painting has proven to be expensive to maintain. = 这幅画的维护费用很高。

过去完成时:
The trip had already proven to be expensive before we even left. = 我们出发前，这次旅行就已经证明很贵了。
I had realized the antique was expensive after I bought it. = 我买下古董后才意识到它很贵。

将来时:
Travel will likely be expensive next year. = 明年旅行可能会很贵。
That new technology will probably be expensive at first. = 那项新技术一开始可能会很贵。

虚拟语气:
If it weren't so expensive, I would buy it. = 如果它不是那么贵，我会买的。
I wish this house weren't so expensive. = 我真希望这房子不要这么贵。

被动语态:
The item is considered expensive by many. = 这件商品被许多人认为很贵。
The project was deemed too expensive to continue. = 该项目被认为过于昂贵而无法继续进行。

疑问句:
Is this too expensive? = 这个太贵了吗？
Are these services expensive? = 这些服务贵吗？

否定句:
The dress isn't expensive. = 这件衣服不贵。
That restaurant isn't as expensive as I thought. = 那家餐厅不像我想象的那么贵。


PHRASE->

prohibitively expensive = 贵得令人难以承受
