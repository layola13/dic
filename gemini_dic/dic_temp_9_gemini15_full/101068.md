inventions:/ɪnˈvɛnʃənz/ | n. |发明；创造 |  Inventions are new things or processes that have been created.  They can range from simple tools to complex technologies. The invention of the printing press revolutionized communication. | 近义词：discoveries, innovations, creations | 反义词：destruction, demolition


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His inventions improve people's lives. = 他的发明改善了人们的生活。
Many companies are working on new inventions. = 许多公司正在研发新的发明。


一般过去时:
Edison invented the light bulb. = 爱迪生发明了灯泡。
The Wright brothers invented the airplane. = 莱特兄弟发明了飞机。


现在完成时:
Scientists have made many important inventions this century. = 科学家们在本世纪取得了许多重要的发明。
She has patented several of her inventions. = 她已经为她的几项发明申请了专利。


过去完成时:
Before the invention of the internet, communication was much slower. = 在互联网发明之前，通讯要慢得多。
They had already completed several inventions before securing funding. = 他们在获得资金之前已经完成了多项发明。


将来时:
Future inventions will likely change the world dramatically. = 未来的发明可能会极大地改变世界。
They will announce their new invention next week. = 他们下周将宣布他们的新发明。


虚拟语气:
If I had invented something useful, I would be very proud. = 如果我发明了什么有用的东西，我会非常自豪。
I wish I had invented the smartphone. = 我真希望是我发明了智能手机。


被动语态:
The telephone was invented by Alexander Graham Bell. = 电话是由亚历山大·格雷厄姆·贝尔发明的。
Many inventions are being developed in secret. = 许多发明正在秘密研发中。


疑问句:
What inventions are you working on? = 你正在研究什么发明？
What was the most important invention of the 20th century? = 20 世纪最重要的发明是什么？


否定句:

They haven't announced their new inventions yet. = 他们还没有宣布他们的新发明。
