ferret-out: /ˈfɛrɪt aʊt/ | vt. | 搜出；查出；发现；揭露 | To search for and find something hidden or concealed, often through diligent investigation.  | 例句：The detectives ferreted out the truth behind the seemingly simple accident. = 警探们查出了这起看似简单的意外背后的真相。

例句：The journalist ferreted out a scandalous secret about the politician. = 记者挖出了关于这位政治家的一个丑闻秘密。


近义词：uncover, discover, unearth, expose, reveal | 反义词：conceal, hide, obscure, suppress


词源:  The word "ferret" comes from the Old French word "furet," meaning "weasel."  Ferrets are small, slender mammals known for their ability to squeeze into tight spaces and find things.  This association with searching and finding gave rise to the phrasal verb "ferret out."
