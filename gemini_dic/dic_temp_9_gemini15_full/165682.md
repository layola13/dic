premonstrator: /ˌpriːmɒnˈstreɪtə(r)/ | n. | 预兆者；先兆；预示者 | A premonstrator is someone or something that serves as a warning or indication of something that will happen in the future.  It often implies a strong feeling or intuition of a future event, rather than a clear prediction. | The strange silence in the forest was a premonstrator of the impending storm. 森林中奇怪的寂静预示着即将到来的暴风雨。  The recurring nightmare served as a premonstrator of the accident. 反复出现的噩梦预示着那场事故。 | harbinger, omen, portent |  (反义词较少，因为其本身表达的是一种预示，没有直接的反义词，可以考虑用表示“没有预兆”或“出乎意料”的词语来表达反义) unexpected, unforeseen | 名词
