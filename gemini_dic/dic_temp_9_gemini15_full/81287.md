granting:/ˈɡræntɪŋ/ | v. | 准予；授予；承认；给予 | To allow someone to have something, or to give something to someone. |例句：The government is granting a large sum of money to the project. 政府正在为该项目拨款。|例句：Granting that he is guilty, what punishment should he receive? 就算他是有罪的，他应该受到什么惩罚？|近义词：bestowing, awarding, conceding, admitting |反义词：denying, refusing, withholding


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are granting him permission to leave early. = 他们正在准许他提前离开。
The company is granting bonuses to its employees. = 公司正在给员工发放奖金。


一般过去时:
The university granted him a scholarship. = 大学给他提供了一笔奖学金。
She granted my request. = 她同意了我的请求。


现在完成时:
They have granted the visa applications. = 他们已经批准了签证申请。
We have granted him access to the data. = 我们已经允许他访问数据。


过去完成时:
They had granted him a leave of absence. = 他们已经批准了他的休假申请。
She had granted him permission before he left. = 她在他离开之前已经允许了他。


将来时:
The committee will grant the award next week. = 委员会下周将颁发该奖项。
They will grant him asylum. = 他们将给他提供政治庇护。


虚拟语气:
If they granted my wish, I would be very happy. = 如果他们满足我的愿望，我会非常高兴。
I wish they granted me more time. = 我希望他们给我更多时间。


被动语态:
The permit was granted without any problems. = 许可证顺利获得了批准。
The request was granted immediately. = 请求立即得到了批准。


疑问句:
Will they grant the application? = 他们会批准申请吗？
Were they granted permission to enter? = 他们被允许进入了吗？


否定句:
They did not grant his request. = 他们没有答应他的请求。
The judge did not grant the motion. = 法官没有批准动议。


PHRASE->
taking into account/granting that = 考虑到/即使
granting assumption = 假设

granting powers = 授予权力
