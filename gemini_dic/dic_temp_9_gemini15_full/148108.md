oversecularization: /ˌoʊvərˈsɛkjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |过度世俗化 | The process by which secular values and institutions become dominant to the extent that they suppress or displace religious values and institutions.  This often involves a rejection of traditional religious authority and practices, and a shift towards a more materialistic and individualistic worldview.  | Example: The rapid economic growth in the country led to an oversecularization of society, diminishing the influence of religious institutions. | 近义词:  extreme secularization, secularism,  de-religiousation | 反义词:  religious revival, theocracy,  sacralization
