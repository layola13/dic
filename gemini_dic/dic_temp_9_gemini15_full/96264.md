indictable:/ɪnˈdaɪtəbl/ | adj. | 可起诉的，可控告的 | Capable of being indicted; subject to indictment.  This refers to a crime that is serious enough to warrant a formal accusation by a grand jury. |  The evidence presented was strong enough to make the suspect indictable.  证据充分，足以对嫌疑人提起诉讼。


The crime was indictable under state law.  根据州法律，该罪行是可以被起诉的。 | indictable offence,  chargeable, prosecutable |  unindictable

词源:
From "indict" + "-able".  "Indict" comes from Old French "enditer" (to dictate, compose), ultimately from Latin "dictare" (to dictate, say).  The "-able" suffix indicates capability or possibility.
