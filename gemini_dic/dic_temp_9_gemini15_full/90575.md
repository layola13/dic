hulch:/hʌltʃ/| v.| 笨拙地移动；踉跄；呕吐 | To move clumsily or awkwardly; to stumble; to vomit. | 例句：He hulched his way through the crowded room. 他笨拙地穿过拥挤的房间。  | 例句：The sick child hulched and retched.  生病的孩子呕吐不止。|近义词：lurch, stagger, heave|反义词：glide, float


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hulches along the street. = 他在街上笨拙地走着。
She hulches her food down. = 她狼吞虎咽地吃着食物。

一般过去时:
He hulched forward in his seat. = 他向前踉跄地移动座位。
She hulched after a particularly rough ride. = 剧烈颠簸后，她呕吐了。

现在完成时:
He has hulched his way through the entire marathon. = 他笨拙地完成了整个马拉松比赛。
She has hulched several times since becoming ill. = 她自从生病以来已经呕吐了好几次。

过去完成时:
He had hulched against the wall, trying to hide. = 他佝偻着身子靠着墙，试图躲藏。
She had hulched repeatedly before collapsing. = 在晕倒之前，她反复呕吐。

将来时:
He will hulch across the finish line. = 他将踉踉跄跄地穿过终点线。
She will likely hulch if the boat continues rocking. = 如果船继续摇晃，她可能会呕吐。

虚拟语气:
If he had not hulched, he might have won the race. = 如果他没有踉跄，他可能赢得了比赛。
If she hadn't hulched, she could have enjoyed the evening. = 如果她没有呕吐，她本可以享受夜晚。

被动语态:  (很少用被动语态)
The food was hulched down quickly. = 食物被迅速地狼吞虎咽。
(This is awkward and unnatural)

疑问句:
Did he hulch when he fell? = 他摔倒时是否踉跄了？
Will she hulch on the long car journey? = 在漫长的汽车旅途中她会呕吐吗？

否定句:
He didn't hulch despite the rough terrain. = 尽管地形崎岖，他也没有踉跄。
She didn't hulch even after eating the spoiled food. = 即使吃了变质的食物，她也没有呕吐。
