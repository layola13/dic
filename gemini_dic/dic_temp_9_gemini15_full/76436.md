gainer:/ˈɡeɪnər/| n.|获利者，受益者|A gainer is a person or thing that gains something, such as profit, advantage, or weight.|例句：He was a big gainer from the merger. 他是这次合并的大赢家。|例句：The stock market gainers outnumbered the losers. 股市上涨的股票多于下跌的股票。|近义词：beneficiary, winner, profiteur |反义词：loser, sufferer


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company is a gainer in this market. = 这家公司在这个市场上获利。
She is a gainer of weight recently. = 她最近体重增加了。

一般过去时:
He was a gainer in the lottery. = 他在彩票中赢得了奖金。
The team was a gainer from the new strategy. = 这个团队从新的策略中受益。

现在完成时:
The investor has been a gainer from the rising stock prices. = 投资者因股价上涨而获利。
She has been a gainer in terms of experience. = 就经验而言，她受益匪浅。

过去完成时:
He had been a gainer in the previous year's competition. = 他在去年的比赛中获胜。
The company had been a gainer before the economic downturn. = 在经济衰退之前，该公司一直盈利。

将来时:
They will be gainers from the new policy. = 他们将从这项新政策中受益。
The project will be a gainer if we invest more. = 如果我们投入更多资金，这个项目将获利。

虚拟语气:
If he had invested earlier, he would have been a bigger gainer. = 如果他早点投资，他会获得更大的收益。
If she had studied harder, she would have been a gainer in the exam. = 如果她学习更努力，考试成绩会更好。

被动语态:
The company was viewed as a gainer by the analysts. = 分析师认为该公司是获利者。
He was considered a gainer in the negotiations. = 他被认为在谈判中获益。

疑问句:
Who are the gainers in this situation? = 在这种情况下，谁是受益者？
Is he a gainer from this deal? = 他从这笔交易中获益了吗？

否定句:
He wasn't a gainer in the competition. = 他在比赛中没有获胜。
The company wasn't a gainer from the marketing campaign. = 公司的营销活动没有带来利润。
