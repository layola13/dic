chasubles:/ˈtʃæzəblz/| n.|祭衣|A chasuble is a liturgical vestment worn by a priest or deacon during the celebration of the Eucharist in some Christian denominations, particularly the Catholic Church. It is a sleeveless outer garment, often richly ornamented, that falls over the shoulders and down the back.  |Example: The priest wore a beautiful chasuble embroidered with gold thread during the mass.  祭司在弥撒中穿着一件用金线绣制的美丽的祭衣。|近义词：vestment, alb (though alb is worn underneath)|反义词：N/A
