prokaryotes:/proʊˈkæriˌoʊts/| n. |原核生物|Prokaryotes are single-celled organisms that lack a membrane-bound nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles.  They are typically much smaller than eukaryotes and are found in a wide range of environments.  Examples include bacteria and archaea. |Bacteria are prokaryotes that play vital roles in various ecosystems.|近义词：monera (旧分类法)|反义词：eukaryotes|名词
