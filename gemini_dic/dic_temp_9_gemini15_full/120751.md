misanthropos:/ˌmɪzˈænθrəpɒs/| n. |厌世者，遁世者|A misanthrope is a person who dislikes humankind and avoids human society.  They often exhibit cynicism and distrust towards others. |例句：He became a misanthrope after a series of betrayals by close friends. 他在经历了一系列被密友背叛后变成了一个厌世者。

例句：The hermit lived the life of a misanthrope, shunning all contact with the outside world.  这位隐士过着厌世者的生活，避免与外界的一切接触。|近义词：cynic, recluse, hermit|反义词：philanthropist, altruist|名词


词源：

The word "misanthrope" comes from the Greek words "misos" (μίνος), meaning "hatred," and "anthropos" (ἄνθρωπος), meaning "human being" or "mankind."  Therefore, a misanthrope literally means someone who hates humanity.


用法说明:

misanthrope 主要用作名词，通常指那些厌恶人类，并因此避免与他人交往的人。  他们通常表现出愤世嫉俗和对他人不信任的态度。  这个词通常带有负面含义，尽管有些情况下，它也可能被用来形容一个选择独处的人，而不是一个积极地憎恨人类的人。


例句：

1.  He's not exactly a misanthrope, but he prefers the company of books to people. 他并非严格意义上的厌世者，但他更喜欢书籍的陪伴而不是人。


2.  Some interpret his solitary lifestyle as evidence of misanthropy, while others see it as a simple preference for solitude. 有些人认为他隐居的生活方式是他厌世的证据，而另一些人则认为这只是他单纯喜欢独处而已。


近义词：

* Cynic:  A person who believes that people are motivated purely by self-interest; distrustful of human sincerity or integrity. 愤世嫉俗者
* Recluse: A person who lives a solitary life and tends to avoid other people. 隐士
* Hermit: A person who lives a solitary life, often for religious reasons.  隐士


反义词：

* Philanthropist: A person who seeks to promote the welfare of others, especially by donating money to good causes. 慈善家
* Altruist: A person who is unselfish and concerned with the welfare of others. 利他主义者


注意：虽然misanthrope可以描述那些避免社交的人，但它比recluse和hermit更强调对人类的厌恶感。  recluse和hermit更强调的是独处的生活方式，而misanthrope更强调对人类的负面情感。
