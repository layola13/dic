monophysite:/ˈmɒnəfaɪsaɪt/| n. |单性论者；单性论派信徒|A monophysite is a member of a Christian sect that believes that Christ had only one nature, the divine, not two natures (divine and human).  This belief originated in the Christological controversies of the 5th and 6th centuries. Monophysites rejected the Council of Chalcedon (451 AD), which defined Christ as having two natures united in one person. |The Coptic Church is a prominent example of a Monophysite church. |近义词：Miaphysite (often used interchangeably, but with subtle distinctions)|反义词：Diophysite
