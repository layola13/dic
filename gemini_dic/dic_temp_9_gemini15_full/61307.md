embarkment:/ɪmˈbɑːkmənt/ | n. | 堤岸；路堤；栈桥 | A raised structure, typically of earth or stone, built to carry a road, railway, or canal, or to prevent flooding.  | Example: The old embankment was crumbling after years of neglect.  这条古老的堤岸由于多年的忽视而坍塌了。|近义词：bank, levee, dike, causeway | 反义词：None specifically, but "low-lying area" could be considered an antonym in terms of function.
