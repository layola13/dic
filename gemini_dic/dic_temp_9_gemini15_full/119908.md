militating:/ˈmɪlɪteɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 反对；起作用；导致 | Acting against; working against; having an effect on.  The word often implies an ongoing or persistent influence. | 例句：His prejudices were militating against his better judgment.  他的偏见妨碍了他的明智判断。

例句：The lack of funding is militating against the success of the project. 资金不足阻碍了项目的成功。


近义词：counteracting, opposing, hindering, working against, influencing

反义词：supporting, aiding, assisting, promoting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The high cost is militating against widespread adoption. 高昂的成本阻碍了广泛采用。
His laziness is militating against his chances of success. 他的懒惰妨碍了他成功的几率。


一般过去时:
His fears militated against his taking action. 他的恐惧阻止了他采取行动。
The bad weather militated against a successful harvest. 恶劣的天气影响了收成。


现在完成时:
Their disagreements have militated against team cohesion. 他们的分歧一直影响着团队凝聚力。
Economic hardship has militated against social progress. 经济困难一直阻碍着社会进步。


过去完成时:
His lack of experience had militated against his promotion. 他的缺乏经验曾阻碍了他的晋升。
The previous government's policies had militated against economic growth. 前政府的政策曾阻碍经济增长。


将来时:
The new regulations will militate against small businesses. 新规将不利于小型企业。
Climate change will militate against future food security. 气候变化将威胁未来的粮食安全。


虚拟语气:
If only his pride hadn't militated against seeking help.  如果他的骄傲没有阻止他寻求帮助就好了。
If the lack of resources hadn't militated against the project, it would have succeeded. 如果资源不足没有阻碍该项目，它本来会成功的。


被动语态:
His arguments were militated against by the opposing counsel. 他的论点遭到了对方律师的反驳。(Note:  Passive voice is less common with "miltiate")
The decision was militated against by unforeseen circumstances.  这个决定受到了不可预见情况的影响。(Note:  Passive voice is less common with "miltiate")


疑问句:
What factors are militating against the success of this initiative?  什么因素正在阻碍这项倡议的成功？
Is anything militating against your completing the task on time? 有什么事情阻碍你按时完成任务吗？


否定句:
Nothing militated against her achieving her goals. 没有什么能阻止她实现目标。
His determination militated against any doubts he might have had. 他的决心战胜了他可能有的任何疑虑。(Note: This uses "militated against" in a slightly different sense implying a victory over a negative influence)


PHRASE->
miltiate against success  妨碍成功
miltiate against progress  阻碍进步
miltiate against efficiency  影响效率
miltiate against the interests of...  损害...的利益
miltiate against a fair trial  妨碍公正审判

词源:
来自拉丁语 *militāre “to serve as a soldier,” from miles “soldier.”  The sense development is from "serving in a military manner" (i.e., actively working against something) to the modern meaning.
