prewarrants:/ˌpriːˈwɒrənts/ | n. |战前授权；战前批准 |  A prewarrant refers to an authorization or approval given before a war or conflict.  It typically involves actions taken in anticipation of or preparation for the war, such as the allocation of resources or the mobilization of troops. The term is less common and often found in historical or legal contexts. | Example: The prewarrants for the construction of new military bases were issued months before the invasion. = 战前建设新军事基地的授权书在入侵前几个月就发放了。|近义词:prior authorization, advance approval |反义词:postwar authorization
