permit:/pəˈmɪt/| n./v.|许可证；允许；许可|a written or verbal authorization allowing someone to do something.|The building permit allowed them to start construction. 建筑许可证允许他们开始施工。|license, authorization, consent|prohibit, forbid, deny|n./v.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He permits smoking in his office. 他允许在他的办公室吸烟。
The city permits the construction of a new park.  城市允许建造一个新的公园。


一般过去时:
They permitted the children to stay up late. 他们允许孩子们熬夜。
The teacher permitted him to leave early. 老师允许他早退。


现在完成时:
The government has permitted the import of new technology. 政府已经允许进口新技术。
She has permitted her son to drive her car. 她允许她儿子开她的车。


过去完成时:
He had permitted the use of his tools before the project started.  项目开始前，他已允许使用他的工具。
They had permitted the meeting to go on longer than scheduled. 他们允许会议持续的时间比预定的时间长。


将来时:
The authorities will permit the demonstration to proceed. 当局将允许示威进行。
She will permit her daughter to travel abroad. 她将允许她的女儿出国旅行。


虚拟语气:
If they permitted it, we would attend the party. 如果他们允许，我们会参加派对。
I wish they permitted dogs in this park. 我希望他们允许在这个公园里遛狗。


被动语态:
The application was permitted by the authorities. 申请得到了当局的批准。
Smoking is not permitted in this building.  此建筑物内禁止吸烟。


疑问句:
Does the law permit this action? 法律允许这种行为吗？
Will they permit us to enter? 他们会允许我们进入吗？


否定句:
They do not permit visitors after 5 pm. 他们下午5点后不允许访客。
The rules do not permit the use of cell phones. 规则不允许使用手机。


PHRASE->
work permit 工作许可证
building permit 建筑许可证
hunting permit 狩猎许可证
parking permit 停车许可证
fishing permit 钓鱼许可证


词源:
From Old French *permettre, from Latin permittere, from per- ("through, thoroughly") + mittere ("to send, let").  The word's history reflects its meaning: to allow something to pass through or proceed.
