epictetus:/ˌɛpɪkˈtiːtəs/| n. |埃皮克泰德|Epictetus was a prominent Stoic philosopher who lived in the first and second centuries CE.  His teachings emphasized inner peace and self-control through acceptance of what we cannot change.  His teachings are primarily known through the writings of his student Arrian.| Epictetus's philosophy focused on the dichotomy of control, distinguishing between things we can control (our thoughts and actions) and things we cannot (external events).|  He believed that true freedom lies in focusing on what we can control and accepting what we cannot. |近义词:None (unique historical figure)|反义词:None (unique historical figure)|名词
