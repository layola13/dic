gasping:/ˈɡæspɪŋ/| v. |喘息；气喘吁吁|The act of breathing with difficulty, often due to exertion, shock, or lack of air.  It often implies a sudden or forceful intake of breath.|例句：He was gasping for air after running the marathon. 他跑完马拉松后大口喘气。

例句：The drowning man was gasping his last breaths. 溺水的人正在喘着最后一口气。

近义词：panting, wheezing, struggling for breath|反义词：breathing easily, calm breathing|词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gasps for breath. = 他喘不过气来。
She gasps in surprise. = 她惊奇地倒吸了一口气。


一般过去时:
He gasped when he saw the accident. = 他看到事故时倒吸了一口冷气。
She gasped at the beauty of the scenery. = 她被景色的美丽惊呆了，倒吸了一口气。


现在完成时:
He has gasped for breath all morning. = 他整个上午都在喘不过气来。
She has gasped with laughter several times today. = 她今天已经好几次笑得喘不过气来了。


过去完成时:
He had gasped before he fainted. = 他昏倒前已经喘不过气来了。
She had gasped in horror at the sight. = 她看到那个景象时，吓得倒吸了一口气。


将来时:
He will gasp when he hears the news. = 他听到这个消息会大吃一惊的。
She will gasp for air after climbing the mountain. = 她爬山后会喘不过气来的。


虚拟语气:
If he ran a marathon, he would gasp for breath. = 如果他跑马拉松，他会喘不过气来的。
I wish I hadn't gasped so loudly; it startled everyone. = 我真希望我没有那么大声地喘气；这吓到了所有人。


被动语态:  (Gasping is rarely used in the passive voice.)  The passive voice is less common with this verb.


疑问句:
Is he gasping for air? = 他是不是喘不过气来了？
Was she gasping when she saw the spider? = 她看到蜘蛛的时候是不是喘不过气来了？


否定句:
He didn't gasp when he heard the news. = 他听到这个消息并没有倒吸一口冷气。
She didn't gasp; she remained calm. = 她没有喘气；她保持冷静。
