keratins:/ˈkɛrətɪnz/| n. |角蛋白|A family of fibrous structural proteins. Keratins are the key structural components of hair, skin, and nails.  They are also found in other epithelial tissues.  They provide strength and protection.|例句：Hair and nails are primarily composed of keratins. 头发和指甲主要由角蛋白组成。

例句：The skin's strength and resilience are due in large part to the keratins within it. 皮肤的强度和弹性很大程度上取决于其中的角蛋白。

词源:
The word "keratin" comes from the Greek word "keras," meaning "horn." This reflects the fact that keratin is a major component of horns, hooves, and other hard, protective structures in animals.
