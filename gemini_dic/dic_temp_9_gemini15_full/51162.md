despotism: /ˈdɛspəˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 专制统治；暴政 |  The exercise of absolute power, especially in a cruel or oppressive way.  It is characterized by the concentration of power in the hands of a single person or a small group, without effective checks or balances. |  例句：The people suffered under years of despotic rule.  人民在多年的暴政统治下受苦。

The king's despotism led to widespread discontent and rebellion. 国王的暴政导致了广泛的不满和叛乱。


近义词：tyranny, autocracy, dictatorship, absolutism | 反义词：democracy, freedom, liberty


词源:  From Middle French *despotisme, from Late Latin despotĭsmus, from Greek despōtēs "master," "lord," "tyrant."  The Greek word ultimately derives from a Persian word meaning "lord" or "master."
