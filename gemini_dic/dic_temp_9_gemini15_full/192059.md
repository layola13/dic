scotoscope:/ˈskoʊtəskoʊp/| n. |暗视器，暗适应计|An instrument used to measure the ability of the eye to adapt to darkness or low light conditions. It typically involves presenting stimuli of varying brightness to the eye and measuring the subject's response time or threshold.  |Example: The ophthalmologist used a scotoscope to assess the patient's night vision. 眼科医生用暗视器评估患者的夜视能力。|近义词：dark adaptation meter |反义词：photometer (measures brightness)|名词
