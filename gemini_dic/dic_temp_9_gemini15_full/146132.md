overemploy:/ˌoʊvərɪmˈplɔɪ/| vt. |过度雇用，雇用过多的人员|To employ more people than are needed or than can be productively used.  This often leads to inefficiency and reduced productivity per employee.|例句：The company was criticized for overemploying staff during the economic downturn.  公司因在经济低迷时期过度雇用员工而受到批评。

例句：The project was overemployed, leading to duplicated efforts and wasted resources.  项目过度雇员，导致工作重复和资源浪费。


近义词：overstaff, overman|反义词：understaff, underman|词性:动词

词源:
The word "overemploy" is a combination of the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") and the verb "employ" (meaning "to give someone a job").  Therefore, it literally means "to employ excessively."
