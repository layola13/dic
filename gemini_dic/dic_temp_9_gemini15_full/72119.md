flogger:/ˈflɒɡər/|/ˈflɑːɡər/| n. |鞭打者；鞭子；严厉批评者|A flogger is a person who uses a whip or flog to punish someone. It can also refer to a whip itself, or, figuratively, someone who harshly criticizes or reprimands others.  |例句：The cruel flogger beat the slaves mercilessly.  残酷的鞭打者无情地鞭打奴隶。

The teacher, though not a flogger in the literal sense, was a flogger of students who made mistakes. 虽然这位老师并非字面意义上的鞭打者，但他对犯错的学生却非常严厉。


近义词：whipper, scourger, castigator, critic, censurer |反义词：praiser, flatterer, defender

一般现在时:
He flogs the horse. = 他鞭打马匹。
She flogs her students with extra homework. = 她用额外的作业来“鞭打”她的学生。


一般过去时:
The farmer flogged his mule. = 农夫鞭打了他的骡子。
The critic flogged the new movie in his review. = 批评家在他的评论中严厉批评了这部新电影。


现在完成时:
He has flogged many horses in his life. = 他一生中鞭打过许多马匹。
She has flogged herself to get ahead in her career. = 她为了在事业上取得成功而拼命努力。


过去完成时:
He had flogged the horse before the race. = 他在比赛前鞭打了那匹马。
The newspaper had flogged the issue for weeks before the election. = 报纸在选举前几周就一直在大肆渲染这个问题。


将来时:
He will flog the lazy worker. = 他将鞭打懒惰的工人。
The media will flog the scandal until everyone knows about it. = 媒体会炒作这个丑闻，直到每个人都知道为止。


虚拟语气:
If he flogged that horse again, it would die. = 如果他再次鞭打那匹马，它就会死。
If they flogged the story more, more people would pay attention. = 如果他们更大力地宣传这个故事，更多的人会关注。


被动语态:
The horse was flogged by the farmer. = 这匹马被农夫鞭打了。
The politician was flogged by the press for his lies. = 这位政治家因为说谎而受到媒体的严厉批评。


疑问句:
Did he flog the dog? = 他鞭打那条狗了吗？
Will they flog the story on TV? = 他们会在电视上大肆渲染这个故事吗？


否定句:
He did not flog the horse. = 他没有鞭打那匹马。
They did not flog the issue to the public. = 他们没有向公众大肆渲染这个问题。
