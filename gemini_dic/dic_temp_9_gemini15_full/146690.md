overheartily: /ˌoʊvərˈhɑːrtɪli/ | adv. | 过分热情地，过度热情地 | excessively enthusiastically; with excessive heartiness |  He overheartily embraced his old friend. 他热情地拥抱了他的老朋友。(This implies an embrace that was perhaps too enthusiastic or prolonged for the situation.)  |近义词：excessively, effusively, warmly (but with a negative connotation)|反义词：reservedly, coldly, apathetically

词源:

The word is a combination of "over-" (meaning excessively or too much) and "heartily" (meaning in a hearty or enthusiastic manner).  "Heartily" itself comes from "heart" + "-ily" (an adverbial suffix).  Thus, "overheartily" describes an action done with excessive enthusiasm or warmth.
