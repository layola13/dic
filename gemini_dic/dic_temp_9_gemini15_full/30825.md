词源:
The word "cha-cha-chaed" is derived from the name of the dance, "cha-cha-chá," which itself is of Cuban origin.  The repetitive "cha-cha-cha" onomatopoeically represents the sound of the dance steps.  The "-ed" ending is the past tense and past participle marker in English verbs.
