rushes:/ˈrʌʃɪz/ | n. v. | 冲，奔涌；匆忙；急流 |  A sudden forceful movement; to move or act with great speed or haste. |  例句：The river rushes towards the sea. 河水奔腾入海。  The children rushed into the house. 孩子们冲进屋里。 | 近义词：hurries, dashes, hastens | 反义词：crawls, creeps, dawdles |

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rushes to work every morning. = 他每天早上都匆忙上班。
The river rushes constantly. = 河水持续奔流。


一般过去时:
She rushed to catch the train. = 她赶火车去了。
They rushed through their work. = 他们匆忙完成了工作。


现在完成时:
I have rushed through my homework. = 我匆匆忙忙地完成了我的家庭作业。
He has rushed into several bad decisions. = 他仓促做出了几个糟糕的决定。


过去完成时:
They had rushed to the hospital before the ambulance arrived. = 救护车到达之前，他们已经匆忙赶到了医院。
She had rushed to finish the report before the deadline. = 截止日期之前，她已经匆匆忙忙地完成了报告。


将来时:
I will rush to the airport to catch my flight. = 我将赶往机场赶飞机。
We will rush to help if you need assistance. = 如果你需要帮助，我们会立即赶来。


虚拟语气:
If I had rushed, I would have missed the bus. = 如果我当时匆忙的话，就会错过公交车。
I wish I hadn't rushed into that investment. = 我真希望我没有仓促投资。


被动语态:
The project was rushed to completion. = 这个项目被仓促完成。
The decision was rushed through parliament. = 这个决定被匆忙通过议会。


疑问句:
Are you rushing? = 你在赶时间吗？
Why are they rushing? = 他们为什么这么匆忙？


否定句:
Don't rush! = 不要着急！
He didn't rush into the decision. = 他没有仓促做出决定。
