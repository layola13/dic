phanariote:/fænˈæriˌoʊt/| n. |法纳里奥特人（指15世纪至19世纪在奥斯曼帝国统治下的罗马尼亚和希腊的希腊裔贵族）| A Phanariote was a member of a Greek family from the Phanar district of Constantinople (now Istanbul) who held high administrative positions in the Ottoman Empire during the 15th to 19th centuries.  They often served as hospodars (princes) of Wallachia and Moldavia.  Their influence was significant, but their power was ultimately dependent on the Ottoman Sultan. | The Phanariotes played a crucial role in Ottoman administration, but their privileged position sometimes led to resentment from other groups. | 近义词：Greek nobleman (in the Ottoman context) | 反义词：Ottoman peasant, commoner
