caryatids:/ˈkæriæˌtɪdz/ | n. |女像柱，负重女像 | Sculptures of women used as architectural supports, typically in classical Greek architecture.  They are usually depicted as draped figures, bearing the weight of an entablature.  | Example: The temple was renowned for its exquisitely carved caryatids.  这座寺庙以其精美雕刻的女像柱而闻名。|近义词：female figures, supporting columns |反义词：male figures (as architectural supports), columns (non-figurative)
