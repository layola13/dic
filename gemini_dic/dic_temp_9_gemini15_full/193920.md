self-election: /ˌsɛlfɪˈlɛkʃən/ | n. | 自我选举，自行选择 | The act of choosing oneself for a position or role, often without formal nomination or election.  It implies a sense of self-confidence and initiative, but can also suggest arrogance or a lack of proper process.  | Example:  The committee criticized the CEO's self-election to the board, arguing it was undemocratic.  委员会批评首席执行官自行当选董事会成员的行为，认为这是不民主的。


|近义词: self-appointment, self-nomination | 反义词: appointment by others, formal election |  词性: noun
