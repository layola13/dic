introduce:/ˌɪntrəˈduːs/| vt.|介绍，引进，提出|To make someone or something known to someone else; to bring something into use or existence.|例句：Let me introduce you to my friend, John.  让我来介绍我的朋友约翰给你认识。|例句：The company introduced a new product last year. 公司去年推出了一款新产品。|近义词：present, acquaint, launch|反义词：withdraw, remove


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I introduce my friends to each other. = 我把我朋友们互相介绍给彼此。
She introduces new ideas into the discussion. = 她向讨论中引入了新的想法。


一般过去时:
He introduced me to his family. = 他把我介绍给了他的家人。
They introduced a new policy last month. = 他们上个月推行了一项新政策。


现在完成时:
I have introduced myself to the group. = 我已经向小组做了自我介绍。
They have introduced several successful products this year. = 他们今年已经推出几款成功产品了。


过去完成时:
She had already introduced him to her parents before he proposed. = 在他求婚之前，她已经把他介绍给了她的父母。
The company had introduced that technology before the competitor. = 这家公司在竞争对手之前就已经引进了这项技术。


将来时:
I will introduce you to my boss tomorrow. = 我明天会把你介绍给我的老板。
They will introduce a new software next week. = 他们下周将推出一个新软件。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would introduce myself more confidently. = 如果我是你，我会更有自信地做自我介绍。
I wish I had introduced myself earlier. = 我真希望我早点做自我介绍。


被动语态:
He was introduced to the audience as a famous scientist. = 他被介绍给观众，是一位著名的科学家。
The new technology was introduced gradually to the market. = 新技术逐渐被引入市场。


疑问句:
May I introduce myself? = 我可以自我介绍一下吗？
Who introduced this topic? = 谁提出了这个话题？


否定句:
I didn't introduce myself properly. = 我没有好好地做自我介绍。
They haven't introduced the new system yet. = 他们还没有推出新系统。


PHRASE->
introduce oneself = 自我介绍
introduce a bill = 提出法案
introduce a topic = 提出一个话题
introduce evidence = 提出证据
introduce a character = 介绍一个人物
