formful:/ˈfɔːrmfʊl/ | adj. | 富有形式感的；形式丰富的；讲究形式的 | Full of form or shape; having a definite and often elaborate form.  The word implies a richness or complexity of form, often suggesting elegance or artistry. |例句：The formful sculpture captivated the audience with its intricate details.  富有形式感的雕塑以其复杂的细节吸引了观众。

The architect's designs were known for their formful elegance. 建筑师的设计以其形式上的优雅而闻名。


近义词：formal, ornate, elaborate, stylish, graceful | 反义词：formless, shapeless, plain, simple


词源:  The word "formful" is a relatively straightforward combination of "form" and the suffix "-ful," meaning "full of."  The root "form" derives from Old French "forme" and ultimately from Latin "forma," meaning "shape, form, figure." The suffix "-ful" is a common English suffix adding the sense of "full of" or "characterized by."
