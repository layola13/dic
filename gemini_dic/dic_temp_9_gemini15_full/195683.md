semisocialism: /ˌsɛmiˈsoʊʃəlɪzəm/ | n. | 半社会主义；部分社会主义 |

英文解析：A political and economic system that combines elements of socialism and capitalism.  It typically involves government intervention in certain sectors of the economy, such as healthcare or education, while leaving other sectors to private enterprise.  The degree of government involvement varies greatly depending on the specific implementation.

例句：

Some argue that many Western European countries operate under a form of semisocialism.  一些人认为许多西欧国家都在某种半社会主义制度下运作。

The debate over semisocialism often centers on the optimal balance between government regulation and free markets.  关于半社会主义的辩论往往集中在政府监管和自由市场之间的最佳平衡点上。


近义词：mixed economy, social democracy, welfare state

反义词：pure capitalism, laissez-faire capitalism

词源:

The word "semisocialism" is a modern coinage, combining the prefix "semi-" (meaning "half" or "partly") with "socialism."  The term reflects the blending of socialist and capitalist principles in certain economic and political systems.  The origin of "socialism" itself traces back to the early 19th century, stemming from the French word "socialisme," reflecting ideas about social ownership and control of the means of production.
