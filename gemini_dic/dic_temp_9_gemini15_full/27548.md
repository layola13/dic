Canaanite:/ˈkeɪnənʌɪt/| n. |迦南人|A member of the ancient Semitic people who inhabited Canaan, the land between the Mediterranean Sea and the Jordan River.  The Canaanites were known for their sophisticated city-states, advanced metalworking, and religious practices involving various deities.  Their culture significantly influenced later civilizations in the region.|The archaeological evidence suggests that the Canaanites were skilled artisans and traders.|近义词：Canaanite (adj.)|反义词：无
