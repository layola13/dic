hail-mary-pass:/ˈheɪlˌmɛriˌpæs/| n. |孤注一掷的尝试；绝望之举|A hail Mary pass is a long, desperation pass in American football, typically thrown when the team is losing and time is running out.  It's a high-risk, high-reward play with a low probability of success.  The term is now used more broadly to describe any last-ditch effort in any situation.|例句：The team tried a hail Mary pass in the final seconds of the game, but it fell incomplete.  球队在比赛最后几秒钟尝试了一次孤注一掷的长传，但是没有成功。

The company made a hail Mary pass by launching a completely new product line in a declining market. 这家公司在市场下滑的情况下，通过推出全新的产品线做了一次孤注一掷的尝试。


近义词：last resort, long shot, desperate attempt |反义词：sure thing, guaranteed success|名词
