coruscation:/ˌkɒrʌsˈkeɪʃən/| n. |闪耀；闪烁；灿烂 |A flash of brilliant light; a glittering or sparkling display.  The coruscation of the fireworks illuminated the night sky.  |近义词：flash, gleam, glitter, sparkle |反义词：dullness, dimness


英文解析:A coruscation is a sudden flash or blaze of light, often associated with something bright and sparkling. It can also refer to a brilliant display of wit or intelligence.


例句:

The coruscation of the lightning momentarily blinded the hikers. =  闪电的闪光瞬间使徒步旅行者失明。

The coruscation of her wit captivated the entire audience. = 她才智的闪耀迷住了全场观众。


词源:

From Latin *coruscare "to glitter," from corusc-, stem of coruscus "glittering," of uncertain origin.
