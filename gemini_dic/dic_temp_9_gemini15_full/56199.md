dodges:/dɒdʒ/| /dɑːdʒ/ | vt. & n. |躲避；闪避；巧妙地避开；诡计；骗局 |To avoid something unpleasant or unwanted, especially by a quick movement.|例句：He dodged the blow with a quick movement. 他迅速地躲开了那一击。He dodged his responsibilities. 他逃避责任。|近义词：evade, avoid, circumvent |反义词：confront, face


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dodges the ball skillfully. = 他熟练地躲避了球。
She dodges questions about her past. = 她巧妙地回避了关于她过去的问题。


一般过去时:
The driver dodged a car that suddenly pulled out. = 司机躲开了一辆突然驶出的汽车。
He dodged the responsibility for his actions. = 他逃避了他行为的责任。


现在完成时:
She has dodged the media all week. = 她整个星期都在躲避媒体。
He has dodged paying taxes for years. = 他多年来一直逃税。


过去完成时:
He had dodged the issue for months. = 他几个月来一直回避这个问题。
They had dodged several attempts to catch them. = 他们躲过了几次抓捕他们的企图。


将来时:
I will dodge the traffic by taking a shortcut. = 我将抄近路以避开交通拥堵。
She will dodge the questions if she can. = 她如果能的话会躲避这些问题。


虚拟语气:
If I had known he was there, I would have dodged him. = 如果我知道他在那里，我就躲开他了。
I wish I could dodge the upcoming meeting. = 我真希望我能躲开即将到来的会议。


被动语态:
The question was dodged by the politician. = 这个问题被这位政治家回避了。
The bullet was dodged by a hair's breadth. = 子弹擦身而过。


疑问句:
Can you dodge the question? = 你能回避这个问题吗？
Did he dodge the traffic successfully? = 他成功地躲避了交通吗？


否定句:
He didn't dodge the issue. = 他没有回避这个问题。
She couldn't dodge the responsibility. = 她无法逃避责任。
