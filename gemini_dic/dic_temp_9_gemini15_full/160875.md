post-chlorination: /ˌpoʊstˌklɔːrɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. | 次氯化消毒 | The process of adding chlorine to water or wastewater after other treatment processes, typically to ensure disinfection and to remove residual organic matter.  Post-chlorination is a common final step in water treatment to kill remaining harmful microorganisms. | Example: Post-chlorination is essential to guarantee the safety of drinking water. 次氯化消毒对于保证饮用水的安全至关重要。|近义词: final disinfection, terminal disinfection |反义词: pre-chlorination
