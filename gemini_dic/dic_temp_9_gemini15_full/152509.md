payoff:/ˈpeɪɔːf/ | n., v. |回报；结果；贿赂；付清；获得成功|The result or consequence of an action or process; the act of paying someone, especially a large sum of money, to influence their actions; the act of paying off a debt. |近义词：reward, outcome, bribe, settlement,  result |反义词：failure, loss


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The payoff for hard work is often success. = 努力工作的回报往往是成功。
This strategy promises a high payoff. = 这种策略承诺高回报。


一般过去时:
The payoff for his risky investment was huge. = 他冒险投资的回报巨大。
They finally paid off their mortgage. = 他们终于还清了抵押贷款。


现在完成时:
The company has paid off all its debts. = 公司已经还清了所有债务。
His efforts have paid off handsomely. = 他的努力获得了丰厚的回报。


过去完成时:
They had paid off the loan before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期前已经还清了贷款。
She had received a significant payoff for her invention. = 她因她的发明获得了可观的回报。


将来时:
This investment will pay off in the long run. = 这项投资从长远来看将会有回报。
We will pay off the car loan next year. = 我们明年将还清汽车贷款。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
