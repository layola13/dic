misgauging:/ˌmɪsˈɡeɪdʒɪŋ/| v.|误判，错误估计|To judge or assess incorrectly or wrongly.|The company misgauged the public's reaction to their new product.|近义词：misjudge, miscalculate, underestimate|反义词：accurately assess, correctly gauge


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They misgauge the market demand every year. = 他们每年都误判市场需求。
He misgauges his opponent's strength. = 他误判了对手的实力。


一般过去时:
The government misgauged the severity of the crisis. = 政府误判了危机的严重性。
We misgauged the distance and ran out of fuel. = 我们误判了距离，导致燃料耗尽。


现在完成时:
