seapods:/ˈsiːpɒdz/| n. |海豆，海鞘|Seapods are marine invertebrates belonging to the subphylum Tunicata. They are filter feeders that attach themselves to rocks, pilings, or other submerged surfaces.  They come in various shapes and sizes and are often found in colonies.| Example: The divers observed a vibrant colony of seapods clinging to the underwater rocks.  潜水员观察到一群鲜艳的海豆附着在水下岩石上。|近义词：sea squirts, tunicates|反义词：无
