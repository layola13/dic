postremogeniture: /ˌpɒstrəʊmoʊˈdʒɛnɪtʃər/ | n. | 末子继承制 |  The practice of inheritance where the youngest child, typically a son, inherits the family estate.  This is in contrast to primogeniture, where the eldest inherits.  Postremogeniture is a less common system of inheritance than primogeniture, and was historically more prevalent in certain parts of Europe. |  In some parts of Europe, postremogeniture was practiced to ensure that the youngest child, often responsible for caring for aging parents, was provided for. | 近义词:  youngest child inheritance | 反义词: primogeniture |  词性: 名词
