predelineate:/ˌpriːdɪˈlɪnieɪt/ | vt. | 预先描绘；预先勾画；预先确定 | To describe, mark, or outline something in advance.  It often implies a detailed or precise description before the actual event or creation. | 例句：The architect predelineated the building's structure before construction began. = 建筑师在施工前预先勾勒了建筑物的结构。

The city planners predelineated the zones for residential and commercial development. = 城市规划者预先划定了住宅和商业开发区。


近义词：prefigure, foreshadow, sketch out, outline, predefine | 反义词：improvise, ad-lib


词源：
pre- (前缀，表示“预先”) + delineate (描绘，勾勒)  Delineate itself comes from the Latin words "de-" (down) and "linea" (line), meaning "to trace the lines of."
