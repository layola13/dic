equinox:/ˈiːkwɪnɒks/| /ˈiːkwɪnɑːks/| n.|春分，秋分|The time or date (twice each year) at which the sun crosses the celestial equator, when day and night are of equal length (about September 22 and March 20).|例句：The autumnal equinox falls around September 22nd. 秋分大约在9月22日。|例句：The vernal equinox marks the beginning of spring. 春分标志着春天的开始。|近义词：equilux|反义词：solstice|

词源:
The word "equinox" comes from the Latin words "aequus" (equal) and "nox" (night), referring to the roughly equal length of day and night on these dates.
