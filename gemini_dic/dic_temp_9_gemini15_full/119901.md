militarizing: /ˈmɪlɪtəraɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使军事化；赋予军事特征 |  The process of giving military characteristics to something that is not inherently military, such as a policy, organization, or area.  This can involve the deployment of military forces, the adoption of military strategies, or the use of military technology. |


例句：

The government is accused of militarizing the police force. = 政府被指控将警察部队军事化。

The country's increasing militarizing of its borders is causing concern among its neighbors. = 该国日益增强的边境军事化正在引起邻国的担忧。


近义词：weaponizing, fortifying, arming


反义词：demilitarizing, pacifizing


词性：动词

词源:

The word "militarizing" is derived from the word "military," ultimately from the Latin word "militaris," meaning "of or belonging to soldiers."  The suffix "-izing" indicates the process of making something military in nature.
