huckstering:/ˈhʌk.stər.ɪŋ/| n. v.| 兜售；叫卖；欺骗；夸大宣传 | The act of aggressively promoting or selling something, often with exaggerated claims or deceptive practices. It can also refer to the practice of engaging in such activities. |例句：The huckstering of miracle cures is illegal in many countries.  许多国家禁止兜售包治百病的灵丹妙药。

The constant huckstering on television is annoying. 电视上没完没了的推销广告真烦人。


近义词：peddling, hawking, touting, racketeering,  salesmanship (in a negative context) |反义词：honesty, integrity, truthfulness

词性:名词和动词


USAGE->

词源:
The word "huckster" ultimately derives from the Middle Dutch word "hokster," meaning "peddler" or "hawker."  This term entered English around the 16th century. The "-ing" suffix transforms the noun into a gerund, indicating the action or process of huckstering.
