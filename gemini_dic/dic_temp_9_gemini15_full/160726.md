portrayers:/pɔːrˈtreɪərz/ | n. | 画像者；扮演者；描写者 | Those who portray, either visually or through acting.  People who depict or represent something or someone. | The museum showcased portraits painted by various portrayers.  博物馆展出了不同画像者的肖像画。  The play's portrayers brought the characters to life. 这部剧的扮演者们将角色演绎得栩栩如生。 |depicters, actors, artists, interpreters |  (无明确反义词，可考虑：misrepresenters) | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The portrayers depict the characters accurately. = 画像者准确地描绘了人物。
These actors portray diverse roles. = 这些演员扮演各种不同的角色。


一般过去时:
The artists portrayed the scenery beautifully. = 艺术家们美丽地描绘了风景。
The actor portrayed Hamlet convincingly. = 这位演员令人信服地扮演了哈姆雷特。


现在完成时:
She has portrayed many strong female characters in her career. = 她在她的事业中塑造了许多强大的女性角色。
The director has portrayed complex themes in his films. = 导演在他的电影中描绘了复杂的主题。


过去完成时:
They had portrayed the historical event with great detail before the exhibition. = 他们在展览前已经非常详细地描绘了这一历史事件。
The playwright had portrayed the protagonist's inner conflict effectively. = 剧作家已经有效地刻画了主人公的内心冲突。


将来时:
He will portray a villain in the upcoming movie. = 他将在即将上映的电影中扮演反派。
She will portray the lead role in the new play. = 她将在新剧中扮演主角。


虚拟语气:
If she portrayed the role differently, the audience might react differently. = 如果她以不同的方式扮演这个角色，观众的反应可能会不同。
I wish he had portrayed the character with more depth. = 我希望他能够更深入地刻画这个人物。


被动语态:
