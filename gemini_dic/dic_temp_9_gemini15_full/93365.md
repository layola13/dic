illegitimately:/ɪˌlɪˈdʒɪtɪmətli/| adv. |不正当地；非法地；私生地 | done in a way that is not legal or morally acceptable.  It implies a lack of proper authorization, legality, or justification. |例句：He illegitimately gained access to the confidential files. 他不正当地获取了机密文件。

例句：The company was accused of illegitimately using its employees' personal information. 这家公司被指控不正当地使用员工的个人信息。


近义词：unlawfully, improperly, unfairly, unjustly


反义词：legitimately, rightfully, lawfully, properly


词源：The word "illegitimately" is derived from the word "legitimate," which comes from the Latin word "legitimus," meaning "lawful," "according to law," or "born in wedlock."  The prefix "il-" (or "in-") indicates negation, creating the opposite meaning.
