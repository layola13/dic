hypercyanotic: /ˌhaɪpərˌsaɪəˈnɒtɪk/ | adj. |紫绀过度的；极度发绀的 | Extremely cyanotic; characterized by excessive cyanosis (a bluish discoloration of the skin due to insufficient oxygen).  The term often refers to severe cyanosis, especially in newborns with congenital heart defects.  | 例句：The infant presented with hypercyanotic spells, requiring immediate medical intervention.  婴儿出现紫绀发作，需要立即进行医疗干预。

The baby's hypercyanotic condition was a cause for serious concern. 婴儿的严重紫绀情况令人担忧。


近义词：severely cyanotic, profoundly cyanotic | 反义词：well-oxygenated, pink


词源:  The word is a combination of "hyper-" (meaning excessive or above normal) and "cyanotic" (relating to cyanosis).  "Cyanosis" comes from the Greek word "kyanos," meaning "dark blue."
