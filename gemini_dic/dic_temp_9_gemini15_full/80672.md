gorebill:/ˈɡɔːrˌbɪl/| n. |血腥的法案；残酷的法律|A gorebill is a law or act considered excessively cruel or unjust, often involving bloodshed or violence.  The term evokes a strong image of brutality and suffering. It's not a commonly used word. |Example:  The new tax laws were criticized as a modern-day gorebill, leaving many families struggling to make ends meet. = 新的税法被批评为现代版的血腥法案，导致许多家庭难以维持生计。|近义词：cruel law, oppressive legislation, tyrannical decree |反义词：just law, humane legislation, equitable ruling |名词
