metacarpals:/ˌmɛtəˈkɑːrpəlz/ | n. |掌骨 |The metacarpals are the five long bones in the hand located between the wrist and fingers. They are numbered I-V, starting from the thumb side.  They articulate proximally with the carpal bones of the wrist and distally with the proximal phalanges of the fingers.  Fractures of the metacarpals are common injuries, often resulting from sports or trauma. |The boxer fractured two metacarpals in his right hand. 拳击手右手骨折了两根掌骨。 The X-ray showed a clear break in one of the metacarpals. X光片显示其中一根掌骨清晰地断裂了。|carpus, phalanges |tarsals (foot bones) |名词
