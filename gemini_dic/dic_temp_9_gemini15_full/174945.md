radiant: /ˈreɪdiənt/ | adj. | 容光焕发的；光芒四射的；辐射的 | Shining brightly; emitting or reflecting light or heat.  | The radiant smile on her face lit up the room. 她脸上灿烂的笑容照亮了整个房间。 | luminous, bright, glowing | dull, dim, dark |  形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun is radiant today. = 今天阳光灿烂。
Her face is radiant with happiness. = 她脸上洋溢着幸福的光彩。


一般过去时:
The newlyweds looked radiant. = 新婚夫妇看起来容光焕发。
The fire was radiant with heat. = 火光四射，热力十足。


现在完成时:
She has looked radiant ever since she got engaged. =自从她订婚以来，她就一直容光焕发。
The sun has been radiant all day. = 阳光灿烂了一整天。


过去完成时:
The painting had been radiant before it faded. = 这幅画在褪色之前曾是那么光彩夺目。
They had felt radiant after their victory. = 他们胜利后感到容光焕发。


将来时:
The newly painted walls will look radiant. = 新刷的墙壁将会显得光彩照人。
The star will be radiant in the night sky. = 这颗星星将在夜空中闪耀。


虚拟语气:
If the sun were radiant, we could go for a walk. = 如果阳光灿烂，我们就可以去散步。
I wish my skin were more radiant. = 我希望我的皮肤更光彩照人。


被动语态:
The room was radiant with candlelight. = 房间里烛光闪耀。
The subject was radiant in the photograph. = 照片中的人物容光焕发。


疑问句:
Is the diamond radiant enough? = 这颗钻石够闪耀吗？
Does the flower look radiant? = 这朵花看起来很漂亮吗？


否定句:
The room wasn't radiant; it was rather dark. = 房间并不明亮，而是相当昏暗。
Her face wasn't radiant; she looked tired. = 她脸色不好看，看起来很疲惫。

词源:
From Middle French *radiant, from Latin radiāns, present participle of radiāre "to shine," from radius "rod, ray, spoke of a wheel."  Ultimately related to the Proto-Indo-European root *h₂reh₂- "to stretch, reach."
