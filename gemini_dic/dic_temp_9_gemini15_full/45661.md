curtails:/kərˈteɪlz/ | vt. | 缩减；削减；限制 | To cut short or reduce in extent or quantity.  | The government curtailed funding for the arts. 政府削减了对艺术的资助。  |  reduce, shorten, limit, restrict | extend, expand, increase


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He curtails his spending. = 他缩减了他的开支。
The company curtails its production. = 公司缩减了产量。

一般过去时:
They curtailed the project. = 他们缩减了项目。
She curtailed her holiday. = 她缩短了她的假期。

现在完成时:
We have curtailed our expenses. = 我们已经削减了开支。
The drought has curtailed the harvest. = 干旱已经减少了收成。

过去完成时:
They had curtailed their travel plans. = 他们已经缩减了他们的旅行计划。
The company had curtailed its investment. = 公司已经减少了投资。

将来时:
They will curtail the meeting. = 他们将会缩短会议。
The government will curtail public spending. = 政府将会削减公共开支。

虚拟语气:
If they curtailed the budget, they could save money. = 如果他们削减预算，他们就能省钱。
I wish they had curtailed the meeting earlier. = 我希望他们早点结束会议。

被动语态:
The project was curtailed due to lack of funding. = 项目由于缺乏资金而被缩减。
Spending will be curtailed by the government. = 开支将由政府削减。

疑问句:
Will the government curtail the program? = 政府会削减这个项目吗？
Did they curtail the meeting? = 他们缩短了会议吗？

否定句:
They did not curtail their holiday. = 他们没有缩短他们的假期。
The company did not curtail its investment. = 公司没有减少投资。


PHRASE->
curtail spending = 削减开支
curtail rights = 限制权利
curtail freedom = 限制自由
curtail debate = 缩短辩论
curtail production = 减少产量

词源:
From Old French *curteler, from curt "short".  The word ultimately derives from Latin "curtus," meaning "shortened, cut short."
