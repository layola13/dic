salmonellae:/ˌsæl.məˈnɛ.liː/| n.|沙门氏菌属|Plural of Salmonella, a genus of rod-shaped, Gram-negative, facultative anaerobic bacteria.  Salmonellae are responsible for causing salmonellosis, a foodborne illness characterized by diarrhea, fever, and abdominal cramps.  The bacteria are commonly found in raw or undercooked meat, poultry, eggs, and unpasteurized milk. Proper food handling and cooking are essential to prevent infection.|例句：The outbreak of food poisoning was traced back to contaminated eggs containing salmonellae.  这次食物中毒的爆发追溯到含有沙门氏菌的受污染鸡蛋。|近义词：Salmonella (singular)|反义词：N/A
