nontransference:/ˌnɒnˈtrænsfərəns/| n. |非转移；非迁移|The absence of transference in a therapeutic relationship;  the failure to project feelings or experiences onto another person, particularly the therapist.  This often refers to a situation where a patient does not unconsciously transfer past feelings or relationships onto the therapist. |Example: The therapist noted the patient's remarkable nontransference, a rare occurrence in their practice.|近义词:lack of transference, absence of projection|反义词:transference
