haematoplast:/ˈhiːmætəplæst/| n. |造血细胞,血小板母细胞|A haematoplast is a cell that gives rise to blood platelets (thrombocytes).  It's an older term, less frequently used in modern hematology.  The term is largely superseded by megakaryocyte.|The study of haematoplasts was crucial in early understanding of platelet formation.|近义词：megakaryocyte |反义词：无
