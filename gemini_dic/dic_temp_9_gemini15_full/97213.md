inflate:/ɪnˈfleɪt/ | vt.  |膨胀；充气；使通货膨胀；夸大 | to increase the size or volume of something by filling it with air or gas; to increase the supply of money and credit, leading to a rise in prices; to exaggerate or overstate something |例句：He inflated the balloon. 他吹起了气球。

The government's spending inflated the national debt. 政府的开支使国债膨胀。


近义词：expand, swell, bloat, exaggerate | 反义词：deflate, shrink, contract


词源:  From Latin *inflare "to blow into," from in- "in" + flare "to blow."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He inflates the tires every week. = 他每周都给轮胎充气。
The central bank tries to control inflation. = 中央银行试图控制通货膨胀。


一般过去时:
She inflated the beach ball. = 她吹起了沙滩球。
The news report inflated the risks involved. = 新闻报道夸大了所涉及的风险。


现在完成时:
They have inflated the price of their products. = 他们已经抬高了产品价格。
The government has inflated the currency. = 政府已经使货币贬值（通货膨胀）。


过去完成时:
The stock market had inflated before the crash. = 股市在崩盘前已经膨胀了。
They had inflated the significance of the event. = 他们已经夸大了事件的重要性。


将来时:
He will inflate the tires before the long drive. = 长途驾驶前，他会给轮胎充气。
The rising energy prices will inflate the cost of living. = 上涨的能源价格将推高生活成本。


虚拟语气:
If they inflated the balloon more, it might burst. = 如果他们再给气球充更多气，它可能会爆裂。
If he had not inflated the figures, the report would have been more accurate. = 如果他没有夸大数字，报告会更准确。


被动语态:
The balloon was inflated by the child. = 气球是被孩子吹大的。
The prices were inflated by the high demand. = 价格被高需求推高了。


疑问句:
Is the tire fully inflated? = 轮胎充气了吗？
Were the statistics inflated to mislead the public? = 这些统计数据是否被夸大以误导公众？


否定句:
He did not inflate the tires properly. = 他没有正确地给轮胎充气。
The company did not inflate its earnings. = 公司没有夸大其收益。


PHRASE->

inflate the economy: 刺激经济
inflate prices: 推高物价
inflate one's ego: 夸大自己
