deluges:/ˈdɛljuːdʒ/| n., v.| 洪水；暴雨；泛滥；涌来 | A deluge is a large amount of something, especially water, that falls or flows rapidly and powerfully.  As a verb, it means to overwhelm with a large amount of something. |例句：The heavy rain caused a deluge in the city streets.  这场大雨导致城市街道被洪水淹没。

The recent deluge of emails overwhelmed my inbox. 最近邮件如潮水般涌来，淹没了我的收件箱。|近义词：flood, inundation, torrent, downpour |反义词：drought, dryness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rain deluges the streets every afternoon. = 下午雨水总是淹没街道。
The news deluges us with information. = 新闻以信息轰炸我们。


一般过去时:
The city was deluged by the unexpected storm. = 城市被突如其来的暴风雨淹没了。
The audience deluged the speaker with questions. = 听众向演讲者提出了很多问题。


现在完成时:
The region has been deluged by heavy rainfall this year. = 今年该地区遭受了暴雨的袭击。
The company has been deluged with applications for the job. = 公司收到了大量的求职申请。


过去完成时:
The dam had been deluged by the rising waters before it burst. = 水位上涨之前，大坝已经被洪水淹没了。
They had been deluged with complaints before the product launch. = 产品发布前，他们收到了大量的投诉。


将来时:
The forecast predicts that the area will be deluged by a hurricane. = 预报显示该地区将遭受飓风的袭击。
We expect to be deluged with orders once the product is released. = 我们预计产品发布后订单会如潮水般涌来。


虚拟语气:
If the dam had not been strong, it would have been deluged by the flood. = 如果大坝不够坚固，它就会被洪水冲垮。
If it deluged, we would need to take precautions. = 如果暴雨成灾，我们就需要采取预防措施。


被动语态:
The streets were deluged by the storm. = 街道被暴风雨淹没了。
The server was deluged with requests. = 服务器被大量的请求淹没了。


疑问句:
Will the city be deluged by the approaching typhoon? = 即将到来的台风会淹没城市吗？
Is the inbox being deluged with spam emails? = 收件箱是否被垃圾邮件淹没了？


否定句:
The desert was not deluged by rain; it remained dry. = 沙漠并没有被雨水淹没；它仍然干燥。
The website was not deluged with traffic after the launch. = 网站发布后流量并没有激增。


PHRASE->
a deluge of  =  大量的...
deluge with = 用...淹没
deluge of requests = 大量的请求
deluge of information = 信息过载
deluge of criticism = 铺天盖地的批评


词源:
From Old French *deluge, from Latin diluvium ("a flood, a deluge"), from diluere ("to wash away"), from dis- ("away") + luere ("to wash").
