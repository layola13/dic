salutes:/səˈluːts/| n. v.|敬礼；致敬；祝贺|The act of saluting; an expression of respect, admiration, or congratulations.  It can refer to a military salute, a formal greeting, or an expression of approval.|例句：The soldiers saluted the flag.  士兵向国旗敬礼。

The audience saluted the performers with a standing ovation.  观众起立鼓掌向表演者致敬。|近义词：greetings, homage, accolades, cheers|反义词：insults, disrespect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He salutes his superior officer every morning. 他每天早上都向他的上司敬礼。
She salutes her colleagues with a friendly smile. 她用友好的微笑向同事们致意。


一般过去时:
The soldiers saluted the general as he passed by. 士兵们在他经过时向将军敬礼。
He saluted her bravery and courage. 他赞扬了她的勇敢和勇气。


现在完成时:
The troops have saluted the commander. 部队已经向指挥官敬礼。
She has saluted the efforts of the entire team. 她已经对整个团队的努力表示赞扬。


过去完成时:
They had saluted the flag before the ceremony began. 仪式开始前他们已经向国旗敬礼。
He had saluted her intelligence and quick thinking. 他已经赞扬了她的智慧和敏捷的思维。


将来时:
The soldiers will salute the new president. 士兵们将向新总统敬礼。

虚拟语气:
If I were to meet the queen, I would certainly salute her. 如果我见到女王，我一定会向她敬礼。
