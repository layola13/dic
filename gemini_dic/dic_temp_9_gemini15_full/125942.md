music:/ˈmjuːzɪk/| n.|音乐|The art of arranging sounds in time so as to produce a continuous, unified composition, as through melody, harmony, rhythm, and timbre; an artistic form of auditory communication.|例句：I love listening to classical music. 我喜欢听古典音乐。
例句：The music was so beautiful that it moved me to tears. 音乐如此优美，让我感动得流泪。|近义词：sound, melody, harmony|反义词：silence, noise


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I listen to music every day. = 我每天都听音乐。
He plays music in a band. = 他在一个乐队演奏音乐。

一般过去时:
She played the piano at the concert last night. = 她昨晚在音乐会上弹钢琴。
We listened to some jazz music yesterday. = 我们昨天听了些爵士乐。

现在完成时:
I have listened to that song many times. = 我已经听过那首歌很多次了。
They have composed several beautiful pieces of music. = 他们已经创作了几首优美的乐曲。


过去完成时:

将来时:
We will go to a concert next week. = 我们下周去看音乐会。

虚拟语气:
