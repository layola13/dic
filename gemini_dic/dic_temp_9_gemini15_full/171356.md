publicized:/ˈpʌblɪsaɪzd/ | v. | 公开宣传；广而告之 | To make something known publicly.  | The company publicized its new product through television commercials. 公司通过电视广告宣传其新产品。 | advertised, promoted, announced | concealed, hidden, suppressed | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company publicizes its new products regularly. = 公司定期宣传其新产品。
They publicize their events on social media. = 他们在社交媒体上宣传他们的活动。


一般过去时:
The band publicized their tour extensively. = 乐队广泛宣传了他们的巡演。
She publicized her book launch on her blog. = 她在博客上宣传了她的新书发布会。


现在完成时:
They have publicized the event to a wide audience. = 他们已经向广大观众宣传了这一活动。
The museum has publicized its new exhibit online. = 博物馆已经在网上宣传了它的新展览。


过去完成时:
They had publicized the concert before the tickets went on sale. = 门票开售前，他们就已经宣传了这场音乐会。
The artist had publicized her work through various galleries. = 这位艺术家之前通过各种画廊宣传过她的作品。


将来时:
They will publicize the results of the study next week. = 他们下周将公布这项研究的结果。
The school will publicize the upcoming fundraiser. = 学校将宣传即将到来的筹款活动。


虚拟语气:
If they had publicized the event earlier, more people would have attended. = 如果他们早些宣传活动，会有更多人参加。
I wish they had publicized the change in policy more effectively. = 我希望他们能更有效地宣传政策的改变。


被动语态:
The new policy was publicized throughout the company. = 新政策在公司内部得到广泛宣传。
The findings of the research were publicized in several journals. = 这项研究的结果发表在几家期刊上。


疑问句:
How did they publicize the product launch? = 他们是如何宣传产品发布的？
Has the company publicized its new initiative? = 公司是否宣传了它的新计划？


否定句:
The company did not publicize the safety concerns. = 公司没有宣传安全隐患。
They haven't publicized the details of the agreement yet. = 他们还没有公布协议的细节。
