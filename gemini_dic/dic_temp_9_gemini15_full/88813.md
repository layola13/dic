hog:/hɒɡ/|ˈhɑːɡ/| n. vt.|猪；贪婪的人；大量占用；霸占|A hog is a domestic pig, especially a male one.  The word can also refer to a greedy or selfish person, or to someone who takes more than their fair share. As a verb, it means to take or use excessively.|例句：He's such a hog, always eating the largest portion. 他真是个贪婪鬼，总是吃最大份的。

The farmer raises hogs for meat. 这位农民养猪卖肉。


近义词：pig, glutton, grabber,  monopolize|反义词：generous, unselfish, sharing

词源:

The word "hog" comes from the Old English word *hōg*, which is related to the Old High German *hūc*.  These words ultimately derive from Proto-Germanic *khūkōn-, meaning "pig".  The use of "hog" to refer to a greedy person is a metaphorical extension of the pig's reputation for gluttony.
