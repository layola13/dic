libriform:/ˈlɪbraɪfɔːrm/| adj.| 书本形状的；叶状的 |  Referring to a type of cell or tissue that is elongated and flattened, resembling the shape of a book or leaf.  The term is primarily used in botany and anatomy to describe certain types of cells or fibers. | Example: The libriform fibers in the wood provided strength and support to the tree.  书材中的纤维状细胞为树木提供了强度和支撑。|近义词：fibrous, elongated, flattened |反义词：rounded, isodiametric
