huckstered:/ˈhʌkstərd/ | vt. |兜售；叫卖；推销；(尤指)欺骗性地推销 | To promote or sell (something, especially aggressively or dishonestly).  |例句：He huckstered his wares loudly in the marketplace. 他在市场上大声叫卖他的商品。

例句：The salesman huckstered a miracle cure for baldness, making exaggerated claims about its effectiveness.  那个推销员兜售一种治疗秃头的灵丹妙药，夸大其功效。


近义词：peddle, hawk, tout, vend,  push (informal)

反义词：give away (for free), donate


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hucksters his goods on the street corner. = 他在街角兜售他的货物。
They huckster their services to unsuspecting clients. = 他们向毫无戒心的客户兜售他们的服务。


一般过去时:
He huckstered his wares at the fair. = 他在集市上兜售他的商品。
She huckstered dubious investment schemes. = 她兜售可疑的投资计划。


现在完成时:
He has huckstered his way to success. = 他靠兜售商品获得了成功。(隐含不光彩手段)
They have huckstered their beliefs to the masses. = 他们向大众兜售他们的信仰。(隐含不实宣传)


过去完成时:
He had huckstered his paintings for years before finding recognition. = 在获得认可之前，他已经兜售他的绘画多年了。
She had huckstered false promises to gain their trust. = 她曾以虚假的承诺来获得他们的信任。


将来时:

They will huckster their ideas to potential investors. = 他们将向潜在投资者推销他们的想法。


虚拟语气:
If he huckstered his products more effectively, he would make more money. = 如果他更有效地推销他的产品，他会赚更多钱。
I wish he hadn't huckstered such a misleading product. = 我真希望他没有兜售这么误导人的产品。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
He doesn't huckster his services; he provides them honestly. = 他不兜售他的服务；他诚实地提供它们。
She didn't huckster her beliefs; she shared them openly. = 她没有兜售她的信仰；她公开地分享了它们。

词源:
The word "huckster" ultimately derives from the Middle Dutch word "hokster," meaning "peddler" or "hawker."  This term evolved through Middle English, where it took on its current meaning of someone who aggressively sells goods, often with questionable methods.
