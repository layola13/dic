melodramatist:/ˌmɛləˈdræməṭɪst/| n. | 情节剧作家，煽情剧作家 | A melodramatist is a writer of melodramas, which are dramatic works characterized by exaggerated emotions, sensationalism, and often a moralistic tone.  They often feature stereotypical characters and plots designed to evoke strong emotional responses from the audience. |  Shakespeare, though not solely a melodramatist, incorporated elements of melodrama into some of his plays.莎士比亚虽然并非纯粹的情节剧作家，但他的一些戏剧中融入了情节剧的元素。The famous melodramatist wrote several successful plays that captivated audiences with their dramatic twists.这位著名的情节剧作家创作了几部成功的戏剧，其戏剧性的转折深深吸引了观众。| playwright, dramatist |  realist, naturalist | 名词
