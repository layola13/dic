diamante:/ˌdiəˈmɑːnti/ | n. |钻石；菱形；诗歌形式 |A diamante is a type of poem that is shaped like a diamond.  It consists of seven lines, with the number of syllables in each line following a specific pattern: 1-2-3-4-3-2-1.  The poem often uses contrasting words or ideas.  It is also a precious stone, a hard, crystalline form of pure carbon. |例句：The necklace featured a large, sparkling diamante. 这条项链上有一颗闪闪发光的钻石。


例句：She wrote a diamante poem about love and loss. 她写了一首关于爱与失去的菱形诗。


近义词：diamond, gem, jewel


反义词：None directly applicable, but  "dull stone" could be considered an antonym in the context of preciousness.


词源：From the Middle French *diamant*, from Old French *diamant*, from Medieval Latin *diamant-, *diamans*, from Late Greek *ἀδάμας (adámas) "unconquerable, unyielding, adamant," ultimately of Indo-European origin.  The word's evolution reflects its historical association with hardness and unyielding strength.
