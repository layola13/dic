diathetic:/ˌdaɪəˈθɛtɪk/ | adj. | 体质的；气质的；与体质有关的 | Relating to or affecting a person's physical constitution or temperament.  It often refers to a predisposition to certain illnesses or a particular way of reacting to stimuli. The term is used particularly in medicine and psychology. | Example:  The doctor noted the patient's diathetic predisposition to allergies. 医生注意到病人对过敏的体质倾向。

|近义词: constitutional, temperamental, dispositional |反义词:  N/A (no direct antonym, but could be contrasted with terms like "resilient" or "unaffected") |词性: adjective


词源:  From Greek *dia-*, "through," + *thesis*, "placing, position," referring to a person's inherent physical or mental makeup.  The term evolved through medical and philosophical discourse relating to body and mind.
