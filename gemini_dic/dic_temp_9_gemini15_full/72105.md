flock:/flɒk/| /flɑːk/ | n. v. |一群；聚集 |A group of birds, sheep, or other animals of one kind that are together.  Also used figuratively to describe a large group of people. |例句：A flock of sheep grazed peacefully in the meadow. 一群羊在草地上安静地吃草。 A flock of tourists gathered around the famous landmark. 一群游客聚集在著名的地标周围。|近义词：crowd, herd, group, throng |反义词：individual, singleton


USAGE->
一般现在时:
A flock of birds flies south for the winter. = 一群鸟飞往南方过冬。
The sheep flock together for protection. = 羊群聚集在一起以寻求保护。


一般过去时:
A large flock of geese flew overhead. = 一大群鹅飞过头顶。
The birds flocked together in the trees. = 鸟儿们成群结队地聚集在树上。


现在完成时:
Many birds have flocked to the lake this spring. = 今年春天许多鸟类聚集在湖边。
Thousands of people have flocked to the city for the festival. = 数千人涌入城市参加节日。


过去完成时:
The birds had flocked to the warmer climate before the first frost. = 在第一次霜冻之前，鸟类已经飞往温暖的气候。
The villagers had flocked to the town square before the announcement. = 在宣布之前，村民们已经聚集在镇广场。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If a flock of birds appeared, it would be a beautiful sight. = 如果一群鸟出现，那将是一幅美丽的景象。
I wish a flock of butterflies would visit my garden. = 我希望一群蝴蝶能来我的花园。


被动语态:
