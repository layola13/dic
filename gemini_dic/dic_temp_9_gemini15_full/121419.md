misinform:/ˌmɪsɪnˈfɔːrm/ | vt. | 误传；向…提供错误信息 | To give someone false or inaccurate information. | 例句：The newspaper misinformed the public about the accident. 报纸误传了事故的消息。

例句：The campaign deliberately misinformed voters about their opponent's policies.  这场竞选故意向选民误传对手的政策。


近义词：deceive, mislead, delude,  disinform

反义词：inform accurately, enlighten,  clarify


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He misinforms his clients regularly. 他经常向他的客户提供错误信息。
They misinform the public about the true facts. 他们向公众误传事实真相。


一般过去时:
The company misinformed the investors about their financial situation. 这家公司向投资者误传了他们的财务状况。
She misinformed her colleagues about the deadline. 她向她的同事误传了截止日期。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

词源:
mis- (错误的) + inform (告知)  The prefix "mis-" indicates a wrong or bad action, modifying the verb "inform" to mean giving incorrect information.
