mineralogist:/ˌmɪnəˈrælədʒɪst/| n. |矿物学家|A scientist who studies minerals.  A mineralogist identifies, classifies, and analyzes minerals, often using techniques like X-ray diffraction and optical microscopy.  They may work in universities, museums, government agencies, or mining companies.|例句：The mineralogist carefully examined the sample under a microscope.  这位矿物学家在显微镜下仔细检查了样本。

The renowned mineralogist published a groundbreaking paper on the formation of quartz crystals.  这位著名的矿物学家发表了一篇关于石英晶体形成的开创性论文。


近义词：geologist (if their work overlaps significantly with geology), petrologist (if focusing on rocks) |反义词：None (no direct antonym exists)|词性：n.
