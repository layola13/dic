cephalopods:/ˈsɛfələpɒdz/| n. |头足类动物|Cephalopods are a class of marine molluscs characterized by their prominent heads and arms or tentacles. They include octopuses, squids, cuttlefish, and nautiluses.  They are highly intelligent and possess sophisticated nervous systems.| Example:  Giant squid are among the largest invertebrates, and are fascinating examples of cephalopods. 巨型鱿鱼是最大的无脊椎动物之一，是头足类动物的迷人例子。|近义词: mollusks (a broader term)|反义词:None.
