purposefully:/ˈpɜːrpəsfʊli/| adv.|故意地，有意地| done in a way that was planned, not by chance or accident.  It implies intention and a conscious decision to act in a particular way.|例句：He purposefully ignored her questions. 他故意无视她的问题。

例句：She purposefully chose a dress that would stand out. 她特意选择了一件引人注目的裙子。


近义词：intentionally, deliberately, designedly, on purpose|反义词：accidentally, unintentionally, inadvertently, by chance


词源:

* **purpose (n.)**  Old French *purpos*, from Latin *propositum*, past participle of *proponere* "to propose, put forward," from *pro-* "forward" + *ponere* "to put."

* **-fully (adv. suffix)**  From Old English *-lice*, *-lic*, from *lic* "like," from Proto-Germanic *-lika-.
