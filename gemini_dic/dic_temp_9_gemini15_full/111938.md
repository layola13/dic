lollers:/ˈlɒləz/| n.|懒散的人；闲荡的人；（尤指）无所事事的人|A loller is someone who spends their time relaxing or idling, often in a leisurely or unproductive way.  They might be lounging around, daydreaming, or simply avoiding work or responsibilities. The term often carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting a lack of ambition or drive.|例句：He spent the afternoon as a loller, watching clouds drift by. 他整个下午都无所事事地躺着看云飘过。


The teenagers were lollers, spending their summer days playing video games and avoiding chores. 这些少年是懒散的人，他们的夏天都在玩电子游戏和逃避家务。|近义词：idler, lounger, slacker, dawdler|反义词：hard worker, go-getter, hustler|名词


词源:  The word "loller" derives from the verb "loll," meaning to sit, lie, or hang down in a relaxed or lazy manner.  The verb "loll" itself has origins in Middle English, possibly related to Old Norse or Old French words suggesting similar meanings of relaxation and hanging.
