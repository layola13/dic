landgrave:/ˈlændɡreɪv/| n.|方伯；领主|A landgrave was a medieval German nobleman, ranking below a prince and above a count, who ruled over a landgraviate.  The title derived from the Old High German words "land" (land) and "graf" (count).|The landgrave ruled his territory with considerable autonomy.  |近义词：prince, count, marquis|反义词：subject, peasant|名词
