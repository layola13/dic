hiccuping:/ˈhɪkʌpɪŋ/| v. |打嗝；抽噎|The act of having hiccups; characterized by involuntary spasms of the diaphragm.|例句：The baby was hiccuping uncontrollably. 婴儿一直在不受控制地打嗝。|例句：She was hiccuping nervously before the presentation. 她在演示之前紧张地打嗝。|近义词：spasming, gasping|反义词:None

词源:
The word "hiccup" comes from the Middle English word "hik," which is of uncertain origin, possibly onomatopoeic (imitating the sound).  The "-ing" suffix indicates the present participle, describing the act of hiccuping.
