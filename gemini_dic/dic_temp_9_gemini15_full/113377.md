lycopod:/ˈlaɪkəpɒd/| n. |石松，卷柏|Lycopods are a group of vascular plants that are characterized by their small size, simple leaves, and branching stems.  They are often found in moist, shady environments.  They represent an ancient lineage of plants, with some species dating back to the Carboniferous period.|Example:  Many lycopods are epiphytes, growing on other plants.|近义词：club moss (非严格近义词，但指同一类植物)|反义词：无
