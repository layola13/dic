forebode:/fɔːˈboʊd/| /fɔːˈbəʊd/ | vt. |预示，预兆；预感|To forebode means to have a feeling that something bad will happen in the future; to indicate or suggest beforehand.  It often implies a sense of unease or apprehension. |例句：The dark clouds foreboded a storm.  乌云预示着一场暴风雨。

The ominous silence foreboded trouble.  不祥的寂静预示着麻烦。


近义词：predict, foreshadow, portend, augur, presage |反义词：assure, guarantee


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dark clouds forebode rain. = 乌云预示着下雨。
His silence forebodes trouble. = 他的沉默预示着麻烦。

一般过去时:
The old woman foreboded disaster. = 老妇人预感到了灾难。
The strange noises foreboded an accident. = 奇怪的声音预示着一场事故。

现在完成时:
The signs have foreboded a difficult winter. = 迹象预示着一个艰难的冬天。
Recent events have foreboded a major economic downturn. = 最近发生的事件预示着经济将出现重大衰退。

过去完成时:
She had foreboded the loss of her job. = 她预感到了自己会失业。
They had foreboded the outcome of the negotiation. = 他们预感到了谈判的结果。

将来时:
The fortune teller forebodes a happy future for her. = 算命先生预言她会有一个幸福的未来。(Note: Future tense use is less common with "forebode" which is more about a feeling of impending doom)
Experts forebode a surge in inflation next year.  专家预言明年通货膨胀将会飙升。(Note: Future tense use is less common with "forebode" which is more about a feeling of impending doom)

虚拟语气:
If I had heeded the signs, I would have foreboded the accident. = 如果我注意到了这些迹象，我就会预感到那场事故。 (Note:  Hypothetical situations are possible but less frequent)
If only I had foreboded the consequences! = 要是我能预感到后果就好了！(Note:  Hypothetical situations are possible but less frequent)

被动语态:
A storm was foreboded by the dark clouds. = 乌云预示着一场暴风雨。(less common)
Disaster was foreboded by the unsettling news. = 令人不安的消息预示着灾难。(less common)

疑问句:
Does this omen forebode anything bad? = 这个预兆预示着什么不好的事情吗？
What does the rising tide forebode for the coastal towns? = 涨潮预示着沿海城镇将会发生什么？


否定句:
Nothing foreboded the sudden change of events. = 没有什么预示着事件的突然变化。
The early signs did not forebode such a rapid decline. = 早期的迹象并没有预示着如此迅速的衰退。

词源:
Old English *for-bodian "to announce beforehand," from for- "before" + bodian "to announce, proclaim."  The modern meaning shifted to emphasize a sense of foreboding.
