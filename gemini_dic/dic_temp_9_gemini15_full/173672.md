quasi-legitimate: /ˈkwɑːzi lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət/ | adj. |似是而非的，表面上合法的 | Seemingly or apparently legitimate, but not truly or fully so; questionable in terms of legality or authenticity.  It suggests a situation or action that has some, but not all, of the characteristics of legitimacy. |例句：The company's practices, while not strictly illegal, were considered quasi-legitimate, raising ethical concerns.  这家公司的做法虽然不完全违法，却被认为似是而非，引发了道德方面的担忧。


The government's actions, though technically legal, were viewed as quasi-legitimate due to their lack of transparency. 政府的行为虽然在技术上合法，但由于缺乏透明度，被认为是似是而非的。|近义词：dubious, questionable, suspect, borderline, marginal |反义词：legitimate, genuine, authentic, valid

词源:
The word is a combination of "quasi," meaning "seemingly" or "apparently," and "legitimate," meaning "conforming to the law or rules."  Therefore, "quasi-legitimate" describes something that appears to be legal or valid but falls short of genuine legitimacy.
