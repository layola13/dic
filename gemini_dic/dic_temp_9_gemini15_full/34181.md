clamorer:/klæˈmɔːrər/ | v. |喧嚣；吵闹；强烈要求 | To make a loud and confused noise; to demand something loudly and persistently. | 例句：The crowd clamored for the speaker to begin.  人群吵着要演讲者开始。

The protesters clamored for the government to resign. 抗议者强烈要求政府辞职。


近义词：shout, yell, roar, demand, clamor, protest | 反义词：whisper, murmur, be silent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The crowd clamors for justice. = 人群吵着要正义。
They clamor for change. = 他们强烈要求改变。

一般过去时:
The students clamored for a longer break. = 学生们吵着要更长的休息时间。
The audience clamored for an encore. = 观众们吵着要返场演出。

现在完成时:
The public has clamored for answers. = 公众一直在强烈要求得到答案。
They have clamored for reform for years. = 他们多年来一直强烈要求改革。

过去完成时:
The workers had clamored for better wages for months. = 工人们几个月来一直在强烈要求更高的工资。
The fans had clamored for their favorite band to return. = 粉丝们一直在强烈要求他们最喜欢的乐队回归。

将来时:
The citizens will clamor for new policies. = 公民们将会强烈要求新的政策。
We expect that they will clamor for immediate action. = 我们预计他们将会强烈要求立即采取行动。

虚拟语气:
If they clamored louder, they might have been heard. = 如果他们喊得更大声，他们也许会被听到。
I wish they hadn’t clamored so much. = 我希望他们不要吵得这么厉害。

被动语态:
The reforms were clamored for by the people. = 人民强烈要求改革。
A new policy is being clamored for by the community. = 社区正在强烈要求一项新政策。

疑问句:
Why are they clamoring so loudly? = 他们为什么吵得这么大声？
What are they clamoring for? = 他们强烈要求什么？

否定句:
The protestors did not clamor for violence. = 抗议者并没有鼓吹暴力。
They didn't clamor for his resignation. = 他们并没有强烈要求他辞职。

词源:
The word "clamor" ultimately derives from the Latin word "clamare," meaning "to shout" or "to cry out."  The modern English word retains this core meaning of loud and insistent noise or demand.
