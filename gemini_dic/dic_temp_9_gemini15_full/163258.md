preconcessions:/ˌpriːkənˈseʃənz/ | n. | 预先让步；先发制人式妥协 |  Preconcessions refer to concessions made before negotiations formally begin, often to establish a more cooperative atmosphere or to gain an advantage in the subsequent discussions.  They are strategic moves designed to influence the negotiation process.  | Example: The company made preconcessions on the issue of working hours to encourage a smoother negotiation on salary increases. 公司在工作时间问题上做出了预先让步，以促进薪资增长谈判的顺利进行。| 近义词：preemptive concessions, anticipatory concessions | 反义词：unyielding stance, intransigence | 名词
