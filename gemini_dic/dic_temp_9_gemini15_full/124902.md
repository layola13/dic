muddles:/ˈmʌdəlz/ | v. n. | 弄乱；使糊涂；混乱；困境 | To make something wet and dirty, or to make something confused and unclear.  |例句：He muddled the papers together. 他把文件弄得乱七八糟。  The evidence muddled the case. 证据使案件变得扑朔迷离。|近义词：confuse, bewilder, mess up |反义词：clarify, organize, straighten


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He muddles his thoughts. = 他把自己的想法弄得一团糟。
She muddles the ingredients together. = 她把配料混在一起。


一般过去时:
I muddled my speech. = 我把我的演讲搞砸了。
The storm muddled the landscape. = 暴风雨使景色变得模糊不清。


现在完成时:
