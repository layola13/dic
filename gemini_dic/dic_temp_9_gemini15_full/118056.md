mendaciousness: /mɛnˈdeɪʃəsnəs/ | n. | 虚假；谎话连篇；欺骗性 | The quality or state of being mendacious; untruthfulness; dishonesty.  It refers to a habitual tendency to lie or deceive. |例句：His mendaciousness was eventually exposed when his lies were contradicted by evidence.  他的虚伪最终被证据揭穿了。

The blatant mendaciousness of his claims shocked everyone. 他说法中赤裸裸的虚假震惊了所有人。


近义词：deceitfulness, dishonesty, falseness, duplicity, prevarication | 反义词：truthfulness, honesty, veracity


词源:  Derived from the adjective "mendacious," which comes from the Latin word "mendax," meaning "lying" or "false."  The suffix "-ness" is added to form the noun, indicating a state or quality.
