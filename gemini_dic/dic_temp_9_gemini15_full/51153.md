despondency: /dɪˈspɒndənsi/ | n. |沮丧，失望 | A state of low spirits caused by loss of hope or courage.  It often involves feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and dejection.  |例句：His despondency was evident in his slumped posture and downcast eyes. 他的沮丧从他无精打采的姿势和低垂的眼睛中显而易见。

例句：After failing the exam, she succumbed to a deep despondency. 考试失败后，她陷入了深深的沮丧。


近义词：dejection, despair, depression, discouragement | 反义词：elation, optimism, hope, cheerfulness


词性：名词
