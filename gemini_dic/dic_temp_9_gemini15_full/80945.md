grabbing:/ˈɡræbɪŋ/ | v. | 抓取；抢夺；吸引 | To seize or snatch something quickly.  Also, to attract attention strongly. |例句：He grabbed the phone and answered it. 他抓起电话并接听了。

例句：The new advertisement is grabbing everyone's attention. 这则新广告吸引了所有人的注意。

近义词：seizing, snatching, catching, attracting

反义词：releasing, letting go, ignoring


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grabs the newspaper every morning. 他每天早上都抓起报纸。
She grabs a quick lunch before work. 她上班前快速吃午餐。

一般过去时:
I grabbed my keys and ran out the door. 我抓起钥匙就跑出门外。
They grabbed the opportunity to travel. 他们抓住了旅行的机会。

现在完成时:
He has grabbed the last piece of cake. 他已经抢走了最后一块蛋糕。
She has grabbed my attention with her presentation. 她用她的演讲吸引了我的注意。

过去完成时:
I had grabbed my coat before the rain started. 下雨开始之前我已经拿好外套了。
They had grabbed the best seats before the concert began. 音乐会开始之前他们已经抢到了最好的座位。

将来时:
I will grab a bite to eat later. 我一会儿会吃点东西。
She will grab the chance to speak to the CEO. 她会抓住机会和首席执行官谈话。

虚拟语气:
If I had grabbed the opportunity, I would have succeeded. 如果我抓住了机会，我就会成功。
I wish I had grabbed that amazing deal. 我真希望我当时抓住了那个绝佳的交易。

被动语态:
The phone was grabbed by a thief. 电话被小偷抢走了。
His attention was grabbed by the loud noise. 他的注意力被巨大的噪音吸引住了。

疑问句:
Did you grab the tickets? 你拿到票了吗？
Will you grab me a coffee? 你能帮我拿杯咖啡吗？

否定句:
I didn't grab the chance to ask him. 我没有抓住机会问他。
She didn't grab the book from the shelf. 她没有从架子上拿书。


PHRASE->
grab a bite to eat:  吃点东西
grab a drink:  喝一杯
grab some sleep:  小睡一会儿

grab attention: 吸引注意力
