carlovingian:/ˌkærəlɪnˈdʒiən/| adj.| 卡洛林王朝的；卡洛林复兴的 |Of or relating to the Carolingian dynasty or its time (roughly 750-987 AD), a period of relative stability and cultural flourishing in Western Europe. The Carolingian Renaissance saw advancements in art, literature, and education.  |The Carolingian dynasty played a crucial role in shaping early medieval Europe. |近义词： early medieval, franco-saxon |反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with terms suggesting decline or chaos.
