prefroze:/ˌpriːˈfroʊz/| v.|预先冻结，提前冻结|To freeze something before it is needed or used.  This is a relatively uncommon word, often used in specific contexts like food preservation or industrial processes.|例句：The company prefroze the berries to ensure consistent quality throughout the year.  这家公司预先冷冻浆果以确保全年产品质量的一致性。
The chef prefrozed the ingredients for the upcoming catering event.  厨师预先冷冻了即将到来的餐饮活动的食材。|近义词：freeze beforehand, pre-chill|反义词：thaw, unfreeze|动词
