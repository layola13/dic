caveator: /ˈkævɪˌeɪtər/ | n. | 提出警告的人；附加条件的人 | A person who issues a caveat; one who enters a caveat.  Someone who provides a warning or caution, often about a potential problem or limitation. | Example: The lawyer acted as a caveator, warning his client of the potential legal pitfalls.  律师充当警告者，警告他的客户潜在的法律陷阱。| 近义词：warner, cautioner, admonisher | 反义词：approver, endorser


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The lawyer caveats his client about the risks. = 律师告诫他的客户风险。
She caveates her agreement with several conditions. = 她对她的协议附加了一些条件。


一般过去时:
He caveated his statement with a disclaimer. = 他在声明中加上了免责声明。
They caveated their support for the project. = 他们对该项目的支持附带条件。


现在完成时:
She has caveated her investment strategy with a risk assessment. = 她已经用风险评估来限定她的投资策略。
He has caveated his opinion with a careful explanation. = 他已经用仔细的解释来限定他的意见。


过去完成时:

They had caveated their agreement with a detailed contract. = 他们已经用一份详细的合同来限定他们的协议。

虚拟语气:
