faunus:/ˈfɔːnəs/| n. |森林之神，牧神|A Roman god of the woods, fields, and flocks, often depicted with horns and a goat's legs.  He is associated with fertility and abundance. Faunus is the Roman equivalent of the Greek Pan. |Example: The statue depicted Faunus with his usual goat legs and horns, a symbol of his pastoral nature.|近义词：Pan (Greek equivalent)|反义词：None.
