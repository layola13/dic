list: /lɪst/ | n. | 列表，清单 | A list is a series of names, items, or other things written or printed one after another.  | Example: I made a list of things to buy at the grocery store. 我列了一个在杂货店要买的东西清单。 |近义词：catalogue, inventory, schedule | 反义词：无


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The list contains five items. = 这个清单包含五个项目。
This list shows all the participants. = 这个清单显示了所有参与者。


一般过去时:
She made a shopping list yesterday. = 她昨天列了一个购物清单。
He read the list aloud. = 他大声朗读了清单。


现在完成时:
They have completed the list. = 他们已经完成了清单。
I have checked the list twice. = 我已经检查过两次清单了。


过去完成时:
He had already prepared the list before the meeting. = 他在会议前就已经准备好了清单。
We had finished the list by noon. = 我们在中午之前就完成了清单。


将来时:
She will send the list tomorrow. = 她明天会发送清单。
We will create a new list next week. = 我们下周会创建一个新的清单。


虚拟语气:
If I had a list, I would know what to buy. = 如果我有一个清单，我就知道该买什么了。
I wish I had a more detailed list. = 我希望我有一个更详细的清单。


被动语态:
The list was prepared by John. = 清单是由约翰准备的。
The list is being updated. = 清单正在更新。


疑问句:
What is on the list? = 清单上有什么？
Is everything on the list? = 清单上所有东西都在吗？


否定句:
The list does not include that item. = 清单不包含那件物品。
He didn't have a list. = 他没有清单。
