quasi-commercialized: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ kəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪzd/ | adj. |准商业化；半商业化 |  Describes something that has some characteristics of commercial activity but is not fully or officially commercial.  It often implies a transition stage towards full commercialization, or a system that blends commercial and non-commercial aspects.  | Example: The university's research lab was quasi-commercialized, receiving funding from both government grants and private companies. 大学的研究实验室是准商业化的，既接受政府拨款，也接受私营公司的资助。| 近义词：semi-commercial, partly commercialized | 反义词：fully commercialized, non-commercial
