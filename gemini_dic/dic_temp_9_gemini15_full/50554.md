deputing:/dɪˈpjuːtɪŋ/| v.|代理；代表；临时担任；|Acting as a deputy or substitute; temporarily filling a position.|例句：She's deputing for the manager while he's on leave.  她正在代理经理的职务，因为经理休假了。|例句：He's deputing as chairman for the meeting. 他正在临时担任会议主席。|近义词：representing, substituting, acting for |反义词：leading, managing (in the sense of permanently holding the position)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is deputing for the CEO. = 他正在代理首席执行官。
She deputes for her colleague on Tuesdays. = 她星期二代理她的同事。

一般过去时:
He deputed for his boss during the conference. = 他在会议期间代理了他的老板。
She deputed her responsibilities to her assistant. = 她把她的责任委托给了她的助理。

现在完成时:
He has deputed for the manager several times this year. = 今年他已经几次代理经理了。
She has deputed her tasks efficiently. = 她有效地完成了她的任务(此处depute指“委托”)。

过去完成时:
He had deputed his duties before leaving for vacation. = 他在休假前已经委托了他的职责。
She had deputed the work to a junior colleague before the deadline. = 在截止日期之前，她已将工作委托给了一位年轻的同事。

将来时:
He will deputation for the team leader next week. = 他下周将代理团队领导。
She will deputation for the absent teacher. = 她将代理缺席的老师。

虚拟语气:
If he were to deputation, he would need additional training. = 如果他要代理，他需要额外的培训。
I wish he had deputed his tasks more effectively. = 我希望他更有效地委托了他的任务。

被动语态:
The tasks were deputed to the most experienced staff member. = 任务被委托给了经验最丰富的员工。
His duties were deputed to his assistant while he was ill. = 他生病期间，他的职责被委托给了他的助理。

疑问句:

否定句:
He did not deputation for the director. = 他没有代理主管。
She will not be deputing for him on that matter. = 在那件事上她不会代理他。
