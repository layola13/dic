sacks:/sæks/ | n. |麻袋；大量；解雇 | A sack is a large bag made of strong material, often used for carrying or storing things.  It can also refer to a large quantity or number of something, or the act of dismissing someone from their job. |例句：He carried a sack of potatoes. 他提着一袋土豆。

The farmer filled the sacks with grain. 农民用谷物装满了麻袋。


近义词：bag,  container, dismissal, firing | 反义词：hiring, employment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sacks the potatoes. = 他把土豆装进麻袋。
She sacks the grain. = 她把谷物装进麻袋里。


一般过去时:
They sacked the old building. = 他们拆除了旧建筑。
The company sacked ten employees. = 公司解雇了十名员工。


现在完成时:
He has sacked all the old furniture. = 他已经把所有旧家具都扔掉了。
The manager has sacked several staff members this year. = 经理今年已经解雇了几名员工。


过去完成时:
They had sacked the city before the army arrived.  = 军队到达之前，他们已经洗劫了这座城市。
The company had sacked him before the scandal broke. =丑闻爆发前，公司已经解雇了他。


将来时:

The boss will sack him if he's late again. = 如果他再次迟到，老板将会解雇他。


虚拟语气:
If he were late again, he would be sacked. = 如果他再次迟到，他就会被解雇。

被动语态:
The old building was sacked. = 旧建筑被拆除了。

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "sack" ultimately derives from the Old Norse word "sakkr," meaning "bag."  This Old Norse word entered Middle English, evolving into its current form and expanding its meanings over time.
