lipoproteins:/ˈlɪpoʊˌproʊtiːnz/| n.|脂蛋白|Lipoproteins are complex particles that are composed of lipids (fats) and proteins. They transport lipids throughout the bloodstream.  Different types of lipoproteins exist, each with a different density and function.  For example, low-density lipoproteins (LDL) are often referred to as "bad cholesterol," while high-density lipoproteins (HDL) are known as "good cholesterol."|  High levels of LDL cholesterol are linked to an increased risk of heart disease.  Conversely, high levels of HDL cholesterol are associated with a reduced risk of heart disease. The liver plays a crucial role in lipoprotein metabolism.| LDL cholesterol, HDL cholesterol, chylomicrons|  (No direct antonym, but low lipoprotein levels can be considered the opposite of high lipoprotein levels)| noun
