crosses:/ˈkrɒsɪz/ | n. v. |交叉；十字架；穿过；横过 | The act of moving from one side of something to the other; a structure formed by two intersecting lines or pieces; to move or extend across something.  |例句：The road crosses the river. 这条路穿过河流。  The two lines intersect and form a cross. 两条线相交形成一个十字。 |近义词：intersects, traverses, passes |反义词：parallels


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He crosses the street every day. = 他每天都过马路。
She crosses her legs while sitting. = 她坐着的时候跷着二郎腿。

一般过去时:
The bird crossed the sky. = 鸟儿飞过了天空。
I crossed the finish line first. = 我第一个冲过终点线。

现在完成时:
They have crossed the border. = 他们已经越过边境了。
He has crossed the bridge many times. = 他已经过过这座桥很多次了。

过去完成时:
She had crossed the river before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，她已经过了河。
They had crossed the mountains before nightfall. = 他们在夜幕降临前翻过了山。

将来时:
We will cross the ocean next year. = 我们明年将横渡大洋。
They will cross the desert in the spring. = 他们将在春天穿越沙漠。

虚拟语气:
If I crossed the street, I might see him. = 如果我过马路，我可能会看到他。
I wish I had crossed the finish line earlier. = 我真希望我早点冲过终点线。


被动语态:
The river is crossed by many people daily. = 这条河每天都被很多人穿过。
The bridge will be crossed when we reach it. = 我们到达那里时就会过桥。


疑问句:
Does this road cross the railway? = 这条路是否与铁路交叉？
Where does the path cross the stream? = 小路在哪里与小溪交叉？


否定句:
The plane did not cross the border. = 飞机没有越过边境。
He doesn't cross the street without looking. = 他不过马路不看路。


PHRASE->
cross the line = 越界
cross one's fingers = 祈祷好运
cross your heart = 发誓
cross-cultural = 跨文化的
cross-examination = 盘问


词源:
From Old English *cris, from Latin crux, meaning "cross".  The meaning evolved to include the action of crossing over or intersecting.
