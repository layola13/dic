self-impairable: /ˌsɛlf ɪmˈpɛərəbl/ | adj. | 自我损害的；无法自我修复的 |  This word describes something that is incapable of repairing itself. It suggests an inherent inability to overcome damage or deterioration without external intervention. The "self-" prefix emphasizes the inability to self-heal.  | The software has a self-impairable bug that requires a complete system reinstall to fix.  | irreparable, unrepairable, unfixable | repairable, fixable |  形容词


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "what" in the example.  The usage is limited to descriptive sentences.


例句1: The self-impairable nature of the damage necessitated a complete replacement of the component. = 损坏的自我不可修复的特性使得必须更换组件。

例句2:  His self-impairable emotional wounds prevented him from forming healthy relationships. = 他自我无法修复的情感创伤阻止了他建立健康的关系。


PHRASE->
This word doesn't readily form common phrases. Its usage is primarily standalone.  Examples of phrases it could be used in, but aren't common phrases in themselves, include:

self-impairable systems
self-impairable damage
self-impairable condition
inherently self-impairable
potentially self-impairable


词源:
The word is formed from the prefix "self-" (meaning "of, by, or for oneself"), the verb "impair" (meaning "to weaken or damage"), and the suffix "-able" (meaning "capable of being").  "Impair" itself originates from Old French *empeirir ("to worsen").
