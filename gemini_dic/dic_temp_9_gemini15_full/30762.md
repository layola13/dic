cesser: /ˈsɛsər/ | n. |停止；终止；停止令 |The act of ceasing; a stopping or ending.  It often refers to a formal or legal cessation.  The word is less common in everyday conversation and more often found in legal or technical contexts.|例句：The cessation of hostilities was announced.  停火令已经发布。|The company announced the cessation of production. 公司宣布停产。|近义词：stoppage, termination, discontinuance, end |反义词： commencement, beginning, start | 名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  Therefore, typical verb tenses (present, past, future, etc.) are not applicable.  Its usage revolves around its noun function within sentences.

Example sentences illustrating different grammatical structures:

Simple Sentence:
The cessation of rain was a relief. 雨停了真是令人欣慰。


Sentence with a prepositional phrase:
After the cessation of the war, rebuilding began.  战争结束后，重建开始了。


Sentence with a relative clause:
The cessation of operations, which was unexpected, caused concern.  意想不到的停业引起了担忧。


Sentence with an adjective:
The abrupt cessation of funding jeopardized the project.  资金的突然停止危及了项目。


Sentence with a participial phrase:
With the cessation of hostilities, peace talks resumed.  随着敌对行动的停止，和平谈判恢复了。
