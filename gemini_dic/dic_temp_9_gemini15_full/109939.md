liberalising: /ˈlɪbərəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 自由化；放宽管制 | The act of making something more liberal; removing or relaxing restrictions or controls.  |例句：The government is liberalising the economy by reducing regulations. 政府正在通过减少管制来使经济自由化。

例句：The country is undergoing a period of rapid liberalising in its media laws.  这个国家正在经历媒体法律快速自由化的时期。


近义词：deregulating, freeing, opening up, reforming | 反义词：restricting, controlling, tightening, regulating | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government is liberalising trade policies. = 政府正在放宽贸易政策。
They are liberalising their immigration laws. = 他们正在放宽移民法。


一般过去时:
The company liberalised its dress code last year. = 公司去年放宽了着装规定。
The reforms liberalised the financial market. = 改革使金融市场自由化。


现在完成时:
They have liberalised the visa requirements for tourists. = 他们已经放宽了游客的签证要求。
The country has liberalised its censorship laws. = 这个国家已经放宽了审查制度。


过去完成时:
Before the revolution, they had liberalised some aspects of the legal system. = 在革命之前，他们已经放宽了法律体系的某些方面。
The government had liberalised the media before the protests began. = 在抗议活动开始之前，政府已经放宽了媒体管制。


将来时:
The government will liberalise the telecommunications sector next year. = 政府明年将开放电信行业。
They will liberalise their trade agreements with other countries. = 他们将与其他国家放宽贸易协定。


虚拟语气:
If they liberalised the market, the economy would grow faster. = 如果他们使市场自由化，经济增长将会更快。
I wish they would liberalise the immigration policies. = 我希望他们能放宽移民政策。


被动语态:
The economy was liberalised by the government's reforms. = 经济因政府的改革而自由化。
Trade restrictions are being liberalised gradually. = 贸易限制正在逐步放宽。


疑问句:
Is the government planning to liberalise the education system? = 政府是否计划使教育体系自由化？
Will they liberalise the laws regarding data privacy? = 他们会放宽关于数据隐私的法律吗？


否定句:
The government has not yet liberalised the banking sector. = 政府尚未开放银行业。
They did not liberalise the market quickly enough. = 他们没有足够快地使市场自由化。
