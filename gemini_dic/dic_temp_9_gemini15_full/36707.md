collision:/kəˈlɪʒən/ | n. |碰撞；冲突；抵触 | The impact resulting from two or more objects striking against each other.  It can also refer to a clash of opposing ideas or interests. |例句：The collision of the two cars caused a major traffic jam.  两辆车的相撞导致了严重的交通堵塞。
例句：There was a collision of opinions at the meeting. 会议上意见相左。|近义词：crash, impact, encounter, clash |反义词：agreement, harmony


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The cars collide frequently at this intersection.  这些车在这个十字路口经常相撞。
A disagreement often collides with their plans.  分歧经常与他们的计划相冲突。

一般过去时:
The two trains collided violently. 两列火车猛烈相撞。
Their views collided during the debate.  他们在辩论中观点相冲突。

现在完成时:
Several accidents have collided with his life lately. 最近几起事故影响了他的生活。
Many challenges have collided with their goals.  许多挑战与他们的目标相冲突。


过去完成时:
The car had collided with a tree before it caught fire. 汽车撞到树上之后才着火。
Their perspectives had collided long before the meeting.  他们的观点在会议开始之前就已经冲突了。

将来时:
The two ships will collide if they don't change course. 如果他们不改变航向，这两艘船将会相撞。
Their interests will collide sooner or later.  他们的利益迟早会发生冲突。

虚拟语气:
If the two particles collided, a new element might form. 如果这两个粒子相撞，可能会形成一种新的元素。
If their opinions had collided less, they might have reached an agreement.如果他们的意见冲突较少，他们可能已经达成一致。

被动语态:

His plans were collided with by unforeseen circumstances.他的计划被不可预见的情况打乱了。

疑问句:
Did the two objects collide? 这两个物体相撞了吗？

否定句:
The two cars did not collide. 两辆车没有相撞。
Their beliefs did not collide.  他们的信仰没有冲突。
