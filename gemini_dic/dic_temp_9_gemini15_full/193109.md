secularising: /ˌsɛkjʊləraɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 世俗化 |  The process of making something less religious or more concerned with worldly matters.  It involves removing something from religious or ecclesiastical influence or control.  This can apply to institutions, societies, or individuals. |


例句：

The government is secularising the education system by removing religious instruction from schools. = 政府正在通过从学校移除宗教教育来世俗化教育体系。

The secularising influence of modern science has challenged traditional religious beliefs. = 现代科学的世俗化影响力挑战了传统的宗教信仰。


近义词：desacralizing, laicizing, worldly


反义词：sacralizing, sanctifying, clericalizing


词性：动词

词源:  From "secular" + "-ising" (suffix indicating the process of making something).  "Secular" itself comes from the Latin "saecularis," meaning "of the age" or "worldly," ultimately derived from "saeculum" (age, century, generation).
