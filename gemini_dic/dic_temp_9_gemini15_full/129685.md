non-dimensional:/ˌnɒn daɪˈmɛnʃənəl/ | adj. | 无量纲的；无因次的 |  Lacking dimensions; having no units.  A non-dimensional quantity is a pure number, without any physical units attached.  This is often achieved by dividing a quantity with units by another quantity with the same units.  Non-dimensional quantities are useful in scaling and simplifying equations in physics and engineering. | Example: The Reynolds number is a non-dimensional quantity used in fluid mechanics to characterize the flow regime.  雷诺数是一个用于流体力学中表征流动状态的无量纲量。  | 近义词：unitless, dimensionless | 反义词：dimensional
