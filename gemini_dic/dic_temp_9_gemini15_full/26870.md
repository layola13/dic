cajoling:/kəˈdʒoʊlɪŋ/ | v. | 哄骗；劝诱；讨好 |  The act of persuading someone to do something by flattery or coaxing.  It often implies a degree of manipulation or insincerity. | 例句：He spent the afternoon cajoling his daughter into cleaning her room. 他花了一下午的时间哄女儿打扫房间。

He was cajoling her into accepting his proposal. 他正哄着她接受他的求婚。


近义词：coaxing, persuading, wheedling, sweet-talking | 反义词：coercing, forcing, compelling


词源：源自中古英语 *cajollen，可能源自古法语 cajoler（“欺骗，哄骗”），其词源尚不明确。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cajoles his friends into doing his work. = 他哄骗朋友帮他做工作。
She constantly cajoles her boss for a raise. = 她不断地向老板讨要加薪。


一般过去时:
The salesman cajoled the customer into buying the expensive car. = 销售员哄骗顾客买下了那辆昂贵的车。
They cajoled him into signing the contract. = 他们哄骗他签了合同。


现在完成时:
He has cajoled his way into a promotion. = 他通过哄骗获得了晋升。
She has cajoled her parents into letting her go to the concert. = 她通过哄骗父母让她去参加音乐会。


过去完成时:
He had cajoled her into believing his lies. = 他已经哄骗她相信他的谎言了。
They had cajoled the information out of him. = 他们已经哄骗他透露了信息。


将来时:
She will cajole him into changing his mind. = 她将会哄骗他改变主意。
He will cajole the investors into providing more funding. = 他将哄骗投资者提供更多资金。


虚拟语气:
If I cajoled him enough, he might agree. = 如果我足够哄他，他可能会同意。
I wish I could cajole her into helping me. = 我希望我能哄她帮我。


被动语态:
He was cajoled into accepting the job. = 他被哄骗着接受了这份工作。
The children were cajoled into eating their vegetables. = 孩子们被哄着吃蔬菜。


疑问句:
Does he often cajole people into doing things for him? = 他经常哄骗别人为他做事吗？
Were they cajoled into making that decision? = 他们是被哄骗着做出那个决定的吗？


否定句:
He didn't cajole her; he simply asked politely. = 他没有哄她；他只是礼貌地问了。
She wouldn't be cajoled into doing anything she didn't want to do. = 她不会被哄骗去做任何她不想做的事情。


PHRASE->
cajole someone into doing something = 哄骗某人做某事
cajole someone out of something = 哄骗某人放弃某事
cajole someone into agreement = 哄骗某人同意
cajole a confession from someone = 从某人那里哄骗出供词
cajole a secret from someone = 从某人那里哄骗出秘密
