nondexterity: /ˌnɒnˈdɛkstərɪti/ | n. |笨拙；不灵巧；缺乏技巧|Lack of dexterity; clumsiness.  The surgeon's nondexterity led to a complication during the operation.  |近义词：awkwardness, clumsiness, ineptitude |反义词：dexterity, skill, adroitness |

词源:  The word "nondexterity" is a combination of the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the noun "dexterity" (meaning "skill in performing tasks, especially with the hands").  "Dexterity" itself originates from the Latin word "dexter," meaning "right hand" (as in the right hand being often associated with skill).  Therefore, nondexterity literally means "not having skill or dexterity."


用法说明:

例句:

His nondexterity made it difficult for him to assemble the furniture.  他的笨拙使他难以组装家具。

The pianist's nondexterity was evident in his uneven playing.  这位钢琴家的不灵巧在他的演奏不均匀中显而易见。
