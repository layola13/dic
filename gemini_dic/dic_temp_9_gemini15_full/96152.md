indents:/ɪnˈdɛnts/ | n. |缩进，凹痕 | An indent is a space left at the beginning of a line of text, usually to show that it is part of a paragraph or list.  It can also refer to a notch or indentation in a surface. | Example:  The essay required a half-inch indent for each paragraph. 这篇文章要求每段开头缩进半英寸。 The coin had a small indent on its edge. 这枚硬币边缘有一个小凹痕。 |近义词：indentation, recess, notch |反义词：projection, protrusion |名词
