entrapment:/ɪnˈtræpmənt/| n. |圈套；诱捕；陷害 |The act of trapping someone, especially by inducing them to commit a crime that they would not otherwise have committed.  It involves the use of deception or coercion to persuade someone to engage in illegal activity.  |例句：The police were accused of entrapment after they supplied the drugs to the suspect.  警方被指控栽赃陷害，因为他们向嫌疑人提供了毒品。

例句：He claimed he was a victim of entrapment, saying the undercover officer had pressured him into committing the crime. 他声称自己是栽赃陷害的受害者，说卧底警官强迫他犯罪。


近义词：decoy, snare, trap, enticement, seduction, lure

反义词：release, liberation, acquittal
