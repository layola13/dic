distinguishes: /dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃɪz/ | v. | 区分，辨别 | To recognize or treat (someone or something) as different.  |例句：His talent distinguishes him from other artists. 他的天赋使他与其他艺术家区别开来。

例句：What distinguishes this brand from its competitors is its superior quality.  这个品牌与竞争对手的区别在于其卓越的品质。


近义词：differentiates, separates, identifies, discerns | 反义词：confuses, blends, obscures | 词性: 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He distinguishes between right and wrong. = 他明辨是非。
The professor distinguishes the different theories. = 教授区分了不同的理论。


一般过去时:
She distinguished herself through hard work. = 她通过努力工作而脱颖而出。
The detective distinguished the suspect's lie. = 侦探识破了嫌疑人的谎言。


现在完成时:
Scientists have distinguished several new species. = 科学家们已经区分出几种新物种。
He has distinguished himself in his field. = 他在他的领域里获得了很高的成就。


过去完成时:
Before the experiment, she had distinguished the variables. = 在实验之前，她已经区分了变量。
They had distinguished the authentic painting from the forgery. = 他们已经区分了真迹和赝品。


将来时:
Researchers will distinguish the impact of climate change. = 研究人员将区分气候变化的影响。
The judge will distinguish between the two cases. = 法官将区分这两个案例。


虚拟语气:
If she distinguished the problem earlier, it wouldn't have been so severe. = 如果她早点发现问题，情况就不会这么严重了。
I wish I could distinguish the sounds more clearly. = 我希望我能更清晰地区分这些声音。


被动语态:
The two products are distinguished by their packaging. = 这两种产品通过其包装区分开来。
The winner will be distinguished by a special award. = 获胜者将获得一个特别奖项。


疑问句:
What distinguishes you from your peers? = 你与同龄人有什么不同？
How can we distinguish fact from fiction? = 我们如何区分事实和虚构？


否定句:
He couldn't distinguish the twins. = 他分不清这对双胞胎。
I didn't distinguish any difference between the two samples. = 我没有发现这两个样本之间有任何区别。


PHRASE->
distinguish oneself =  脱颖而出
distinguish between = 区分…和…
distinguish A from B = 将A与B区分开来
be distinguished for = 因…而著名
distinguishable from = 可与…区分开


词源:
源自古法语 *distinguir,  来自拉丁语 distinguere,  由 dis- ("apart") 和 stinguere ("to prick, to goad") 组合而成。字面意思是“刺穿，区分”。  这暗示着通过“刺穿”或“区分”来分开不同的东西。
