repoliticizing: /ˌriːpɒləˈtɪsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |  使重新政治化；使重新具有政治意义 |  The act of making something political again, or giving it a renewed political significance after a period of depoliticization.  This often involves bringing political ideologies, agendas, or conflicts back into a previously non-political or neutralized context. | 例句：The recent protests have repoliticized the issue of healthcare reform. 近期抗议活动使医疗改革问题重新政治化了。The government's decision to cut funding for education has repoliticized the debate. 政府削减教育经费的决定使这场辩论重新政治化了。| 近义词：reactivate, revitalize (in a political context) | 反义词：depoliticize, neutralize

词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "re-" (again), "politic" (relating to politics), and the suffix "-izing" (making or causing to become).  It's a relatively modern coinage reflecting the dynamic nature of political influence.
