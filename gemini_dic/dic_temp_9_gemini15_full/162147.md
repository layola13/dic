pre-Gothic:/ˌpriːˈɡɒθɪk/| adj.| 前哥特式的 |  Describing the architectural style that preceded the Gothic style in Europe, typically characterized by Romanesque features.  It often refers to the transition period between Romanesque and Gothic architecture. | Example:  The church exhibits some pre-Gothic features in its round arches and massive walls. 这座教堂在其圆形拱门和厚重的墙壁中展现出一些前哥特式的特色。 | 近义词：Romanesque, proto-Gothic | 反义词：Gothic
