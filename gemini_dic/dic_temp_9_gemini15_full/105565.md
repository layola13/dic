kinaesthesis:/ˌkaɪniˈsθiːsɪs/ | n. | 本体感觉，动觉 | The sense of body position and movement, derived from sensory receptors in muscles, tendons, and joints.  It allows us to know where our body parts are in space without looking at them. | Example:  Maintaining balance while walking relies heavily on kinaesthesis.  保持平衡行走很大程度上依赖于本体感觉。|近义词： proprioception | 反义词： None specifically;  perhaps terms implying lack of awareness of body position.
