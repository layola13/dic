servant:/ˈsɜːrvənt/ | n. |仆人，佣人，雇员 |A servant is a person employed to work in another's household.  Servants historically performed a wide range of duties, from cooking and cleaning to childcare and personal assistance.  The term often carries connotations of a hierarchical social structure. |例句：In the 19th century, many wealthy families employed several servants. = 在19世纪，许多富裕家庭雇佣了好几个仆人。

The old manor house was once staffed by a large number of servants. = 这座古老的庄园曾经雇佣了大量的仆人。

近义词：employee, attendant, domestic, help, retainer |反义词：employer, master


词源:
Old French *servant*, from *servir* "to serve," from Latin *servīre*.  The word reflects a long history of employment based on service.


用法说明:

一般现在时:
The servant cleans the house every day. = 仆人每天打扫房子。
He serves his master faithfully. = 他忠实地服侍他的主人。


一般过去时:
The servant prepared a delicious meal. = 仆人准备了一顿美味的饭菜。
She served the family for many years. = 她为这个家庭服务了很多年。


现在完成时:
The servant has cleaned all the rooms. = 仆人已经打扫了所有房间。
They have served their time faithfully. = 他们忠实地完成了他们的工作。


过去完成时:
The servant had already prepared the breakfast before they woke up. = 他们醒来之前，仆人已经准备好了早餐。
The family had been served by this servant for decades. = 这个家庭几十年来一直由这个仆人服务。


将来时:
The servant will polish the silver tomorrow. = 仆人明天将擦拭银器。
He will serve the guests at the banquet. = 他将在宴会为客人服务。


虚拟语气:
If the servant were here, he would help us. = 如果仆人在这儿，他会帮助我们。
I wish the servant had arrived earlier. = 我希望仆人早点到。


被动语态:
The house is cleaned by the servant. = 房子是由仆人打扫的。
The guests were served by the servant. = 客人由仆人招待。


疑问句:
Is the servant available? = 仆人有空吗？
Did the servant complete the task? = 仆人完成了任务吗？


否定句:
The servant did not arrive on time. = 仆人没有准时到达。
She will not serve tea this afternoon. = 她今天下午不供应茶。
