contemplatives:/kənˈtɛmplətɪvz/| adj. n.|沉思的；冥想的；沉思者|Relating to or characterized by contemplation; given to or characterized by deep thought.  The word often refers to people or things that are quiet and reflective, engaged in deep thought or prayer.  It can also describe a style of art or writing that emphasizes reflection and introspection.|例句：The monks led a contemplative life of prayer and meditation.  僧侣们过着沉思默想的祈祷生活。

The artist's work is characterized by its contemplative mood. 艺术家的作品以其沉思的气氛为特征。|近义词：meditative, reflective, thoughtful, pensive|反义词：active, restless, impulsive

词源:
From Middle English *contemplatif, from Old French contemplatif, from Latin contemplativus, from contemplari "to contemplate," from com- "together" + templum "temple" (originally a place for observing the stars; hence, a place for observation and reflection).
