lensings: /ˈlɛnzɪŋz/ | n. |透镜效应，引力透镜现象 | The bending of light from a distant object as it passes through a gravitational field, creating multiple images or distortions.  This phenomenon is a prediction of Einstein's general theory of relativity. | Example: Gravitational lensings can magnify distant galaxies, making them appear brighter and larger than they actually are. 引力透镜效应可以放大遥远的星系，使它们看起来比实际亮度和尺寸更大。 | 近义词: gravitational lensing, light bending | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but the absence of lensing effects could be described as)  unlensed, undistorted
