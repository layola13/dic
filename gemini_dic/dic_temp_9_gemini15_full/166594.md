presecular: /ˌpriːˈsɛkjʊlə(r)/ | adj. | 前世俗的，先于世俗化的 |  Describing something that existed or occurred before the widespread influence of secularism or before a society became predominantly secular.  It implies a time period or state of affairs characterized by religious or spiritual dominance, which later gave way to secular values and institutions. |  The presecular era saw a strong intertwining of religious and political power.  | 近义词:  pre-modern,  religious,  traditional | 反义词: secular, modern, post-secular
