disoblige:/ˌdɪs.əˈblaɪdʒ/ | vt. |使不高兴；得罪；违背；不顺从 | To cause someone to feel unhappy or annoyed; to offend; to fail to comply with a request or expectation. | 例句：I wouldn't want to disoblige my host by refusing their offer. 我不想因为拒绝主人的邀请而得罪他们。  | 例句：He disobliged his parents by dropping out of college. 他退学让父母很生气。 | 近义词：offend, annoy, displease, slight | 反义词：please, gratify, oblige


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disobliges his friends frequently. = 他经常得罪他的朋友们。
She disobliges her boss by being late. = 她迟到惹恼了她的老板。

一般过去时:
They disobliged the customer by ignoring his complaint. = 他们忽视了顾客的投诉，使顾客不高兴。
He disobliged his promise to help. = 他违背了帮助的承诺。

现在完成时:
I have disobliged my teacher by not doing my homework. = 我因为没做作业而得罪了老师。
She has disobliged her family by her actions. = 她的行为让她的家人很不高兴。

过去完成时:
They had disobliged the guests by serving cold food. = 他们因为上了冷菜而得罪了客人。
He had disobliged the rules and been punished. = 他违反了规定并受到了惩罚。

将来时:
I will disoblige my parents if I fail the exam. = 如果考试不及格，我会让我父母失望。
They will disoblige their contract by not delivering the goods. = 他们不交付货物将违反合同。

虚拟语气:
I would disoblige my principles if I betrayed my friends. = 如果我背叛了我的朋友，我会违背我的原则。
If I were you, I wouldn't disoblige him so easily. = 如果我是你，我不会轻易得罪他。

被动语态:
The customer felt disobliged by the poor service. = 顾客对糟糕的服务感到不满。
His request was disobliged by the manager. = 他的请求被经理拒绝了。

疑问句:
Would you disoblige me by helping me with this? = 你能帮我这个忙吗？（委婉说法）
Did I disoblige you in any way? = 我有什么地方得罪你了吗？

否定句:
I wouldn't disoblige my family for anything. = 我不会为了任何事情得罪我的家人。
He tried not to disoblige anyone at the meeting. = 他尽量在会议上不冒犯任何人。


PHRASE->

disoblige by omission = 因疏忽而得罪
disoblige unintentionally = 无意中得罪
disoblige through neglect = 因疏忽而得罪
