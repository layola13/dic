词源:

The word is a combination of "over-" (meaning excessively or too much) and "opinionated" (having strong opinions and unwilling to change them).  "Opinionated" itself comes from "opinion" + "-ated" (a suffix indicating having the quality of).  The root of "opinion" traces back to the Latin "opinio," meaning "belief," "thought," or "judgment."
