signpost:/ˈsaɪnpoʊst/| n. |路标；标志；征兆 | A signpost is a post with signs that give directions or information. It can also refer to something that indicates or suggests something else.  |Example: The signpost pointed towards the village. 路标指向村庄。

The signpost of his success was his hard work. 他成功的标志是他努力工作。|近义词：marker, indicator, signal, guide |反义词：misdirection, confusion


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The signpost indicates the right path. = 路标指示正确的道路。
This signpost shows the way to the beach. = 这个路标显示通往海滩的路。


一般过去时:
The old signpost pointed to the abandoned town. = 旧路标指向废弃的城镇。
The signpost directed us to the nearby lake. = 路标引导我们去了附近的湖泊。


现在完成时:
The signpost has been damaged by the storm. = 路标已被暴风雨损坏。
That signpost has guided many travelers. = 那块路标指引了许多旅行者。


过去完成时:
The signpost had already been knocked down before we arrived. = 我们到达之前，路标已经被撞倒了。
They had followed the signpost before getting lost. = 他们在迷路之前曾沿着路标走过。


将来时:
The new signpost will be installed next week. = 新的路标将在下周安装。
We will follow the signpost to reach our destination. = 我们将按照路标到达目的地。


虚拟语气:
If there were a signpost, we wouldn't have gotten lost. = 如果有路标，我们就不会迷路了。
I wish there were a clear signpost to guide us. = 我希望有一个清晰的路标来引导我们。


被动语态:
The signpost was damaged in the accident. = 路标在事故中受损。
The signposts are regularly maintained by the council. = 路标定期由市议会维护。


疑问句:
Does this signpost show the way to the station? = 这个路标显示去车站的路吗？
Which way does the signpost point? = 路标指向哪个方向？


否定句:
There was no signpost to indicate the correct direction. = 没有路标指示正确的方向。
The signpost didn't give clear instructions. = 路标没有给出清晰的指示。
