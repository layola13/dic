fascism:/ˈfæʃɪzəm/| n. |法西斯主义|A political ideology characterized by dictatorial power, forcible suppression of opposition, and strong regimentation of society and the economy.|例句：The rise of fascism in the 1930s led to World War II.  二三十年代法西斯主义的兴起导致了第二次世界大战。|例句：He argued that fascism was a direct response to the failures of liberal democracy. 他认为法西斯主义是对自由民主失败的直接回应。|近义词：totalitarianism, authoritarianism, autocracy|反义词：democracy, liberalism, anarchism

词源:  The term "fascism" derives from the Italian word *fascio*, meaning "bundle" or "group," referring to the *fasci di combattimento* (combat squads) formed by Benito Mussolini in 1919.  The term evolved to describe the political movement and ideology he established.
