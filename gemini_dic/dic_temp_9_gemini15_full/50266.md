depersonalizes:/ˌdiːˈpɜːrsənəlaɪz/ | vt. |使去个性化；使非人性化 | To remove the personal characteristics or individuality from something or someone; to make impersonal or objective.  |例句：The bureaucratic process depersonalizes the experience of applying for a loan.  官僚流程使贷款申请体验去个性化。 |例句：The factory assembly line depersonalizes the work, making it feel repetitive and meaningless. 工厂流水线使工作去个性化，使其感觉重复且毫无意义。|近义词：dehumanize, impersonalize, objectify |反义词：personalize, humanize

词源:
The word "depersonalizes" is derived from the prefix "de-" (meaning "to remove" or "to deprive of"), the word "personal" (referring to an individual's characteristics), and the suffix "-ize" (meaning "to make" or "to cause to become").  Therefore, it literally means "to remove the personal qualities from."
