radio-dating: /ˈreɪdioʊˌdeɪtɪŋ/ | n. |放射性测年法|  The process of determining the age of an object by measuring the amount of a radioactive isotope it contains. This technique is commonly used in archaeology, geology, and other fields to date materials containing organic matter or certain minerals.  It relies on the principle of radioactive decay, where unstable isotopes transform into stable isotopes at a known rate. By comparing the ratio of the radioactive isotope to its stable counterpart, scientists can estimate the age of the sample. | Scientists used radio-dating to determine the age of the ancient artifact. 科学家利用放射性测年法确定了古代文物的年代。  | radiometric dating, carbon dating |  (no direct antonym, but could be considered opposite of methods that don't rely on radioactive decay)| noun
