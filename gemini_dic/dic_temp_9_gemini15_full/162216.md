pre-Raphaelite:/ˌpriːˈræfəˌeɪlaɪt/| adj.| 拉斐尔前派(的)|  Relating to or denoting the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood, a group of 19th-century English painters who rejected the academic art of their time and sought to revive the artistic principles and techniques of the period before Raphael (1483-1520). Their work is characterized by intense color, detailed realism, and a focus on moral and symbolic themes.  |The Pre-Raphaelite paintings are known for their vibrant colors and detailed depictions of nature.|近义词：Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood (名词)|反义词：Post-Raphaelite (a term rarely used)|形容词
