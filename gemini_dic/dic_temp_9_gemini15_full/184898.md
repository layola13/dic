retainerships:/rɪˈteɪnərʃɪps/ | n. | 保留金制度；长期聘用关系 | A retainership is an agreement where a client pays a professional, such as a lawyer or consultant, a regular fee in exchange for their services on an ongoing basis, or for their availability should their services be required.  This ensures consistent access to the professional's expertise and avoids the need for hourly billing for smaller tasks. | The law firm offers retainerships to its high-profile clients. 这家律师事务所为其高端客户提供保留金制度。  She secured a retainership with a large corporation. 她与一家大公司达成了长期聘用协议。 |  long-term contract, ongoing engagement | termination of contract
