lollard:/ˈlɒləd/| n.| लोलाड़, 循道宗信徒 |A follower of John Wycliffe, a 14th-century English theologian who challenged the authority of the Catholic Church. Lollards were known for their belief in the Bible as the ultimate authority and for their emphasis on personal piety and simple living. They were often persecuted for their beliefs.|The Lollards were persecuted for their religious beliefs.|近义词：heretic, dissenter|反义词：conformist
