horripilation:/ˌhɒrɪpɪˈleɪʃən/| n.|起鸡皮疙瘩；毛骨悚然|The involuntary erection of the hairs of the skin, especially as a reaction to cold, fear, or excitement.  This physiological response is often accompanied by a feeling of unease or discomfort.|例句：The chilling story induced horripilation in the audience.  令人毛骨悚然的故事情节让观众起了一身鸡皮疙瘩。

例句：A sudden gust of wind caused a horripilation on my arms.  一阵冷风让我胳膊上起了鸡皮疙瘩。


近义词：goosebumps, goose flesh, piloerection|反义词：None (the opposite would be smooth skin)|词性：名词


词源:  From Latin *horripilare, from *horrere "to bristle, stand on end" + pilus "hair".  The word reflects the physical manifestation of the reaction (hairs standing on end) as well as the emotional experience.
