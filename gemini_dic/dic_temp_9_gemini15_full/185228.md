retractions:/rɪˈtræktʃənz/ | n. | 撤回；收回；缩回 | The act of withdrawing or taking back something that has been said, written, or done.  This often refers to statements, publications, or claims that are later found to be inaccurate or untrue.  It can also refer to a physical retraction, like the pulling back of a body part. |

例句：

The journal published retractions of several articles due to scientific misconduct. = 由于学术不端行为，该期刊撤回了多篇文章。

The politician issued a retraction of his earlier inflammatory remarks. = 该政治家撤回了之前煽动性的言论。


近义词：withdrawals, cancellations, recantations

反义词：affirmations, confirmations, endorsements

词源：

From the verb "retract," which comes from the Latin "retrāctus,"  a past participle meaning "drawn back."  The prefix "re-" means "back" or "again," and "trāctus" is related to the verb "trahere," meaning "to draw" or "to pull."

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

The article was retracted by the journal. = 这篇文章被期刊撤回了。
His claims were retracted after further investigation. = 经过进一步调查后，他的说法被撤回了。
