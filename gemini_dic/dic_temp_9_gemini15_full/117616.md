megapode:/ˈmɛɡəˌpoʊd/| n. |巨鷊，冢鸟|A megapode is a bird of the family Megapodiidae, characterized by their unique breeding behavior of burying their eggs in mounds of soil, decaying vegetation, or sand.  These birds are found in tropical regions of Australia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.|Example: The male megapode meticulously constructed a large mound to incubate his eggs.|近义词：mound-builder (in reference to their nesting behavior)|反义词：无
