interlockers:/ˌɪntərˈlɒkərz/ | n. |互锁装置；连锁装置 | Interlockers are mechanisms that prevent something from operating unless certain conditions are met, often used for safety purposes.  They are commonly found in machinery and systems requiring sequential operation or safety interlocks. For example, a machine might have interlockers to prevent it from starting unless the safety guard is in place. |The machine's interlockers prevented it from starting until the safety guard was closed. 这台机器的互锁装置防止机器在安全防护装置关闭之前启动。 The train's interlockers ensured that only one train could occupy a section of track at a time. 火车的互锁装置确保一次只有一列火车可以占据一段轨道。 |safety devices, interlocking mechanisms |disabling devices, unlocking mechanisms|名词
