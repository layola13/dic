repossession's:/ˌriːpəˈzeʃənz/ | n. | 再拥有；收回；赎回 | The act of taking back possession of something, usually property, due to non-payment or breach of contract.  It often refers to the legal process involved. | 例句：The bank initiated repossession's of the car after the owner failed to make payments.  银行在车主未能付款后启动了汽车收回程序。

The repossession's of the house left the family homeless.  房屋收回使这个家庭无家可归。


近义词：recovery, reclaim, seizure | 反义词：release, relinquish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bank repossesses many cars each month. = 银行每个月收回许多汽车。
Repossession often causes financial hardship. = 收回财产通常会导致经济困难。


一般过去时:
They repossessed the property last week. = 他们上周收回该房产。
The company repossessed the equipment due to non-payment. =  由于未付款，该公司收回设备。


现在完成时:
The landlord has repossessed several apartments this year. =  房东今年已经收回了几套公寓。
The lender has repossessed the property because of default. = 由于违约，贷款人已经收回该房产。


过去完成时:
The bank had already repossessed the vehicle before he contacted them. =  在他联系银行之前，银行已经收回车辆了。
They had repossessed the equipment several times before they finally sold it. = 在他们最终出售设备之前，他们已经收回了几次设备。


将来时:

The company will repossess the machinery if the contract is breached. = 如果违反合同，公司将收回机器。


虚拟语气:

If he had paid on time, they wouldn't have repossessed his home. = 如果他按时付款，他们就不会收回他的房子。


被动语态:
