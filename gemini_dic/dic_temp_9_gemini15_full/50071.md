dentalism:/ˈdɛntəlɪzəm/| n. |齿音化，齿音过多|The excessive use of dental sounds in speech, often resulting in a lisp or other speech impediment.  Dentalism can be a characteristic of certain dialects or a symptom of a speech disorder.  It involves the articulation of sounds with the tongue making contact with the teeth. | Example:  His speech exhibited a noticeable dentalism, making it difficult to understand some of his words. 他说话明显齿音化，使得一些词语难以理解。|近义词:lisp, dental articulation|反义词: (lack of) clear articulation
