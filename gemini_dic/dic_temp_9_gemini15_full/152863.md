pedaling:/ˈpɛdəlɪŋ/| v.|踩踏板；骑车|The act of operating pedals, typically on a bicycle or similar machine.  It can also refer to the continuous process of pushing something forward with effort.|例句：He was pedaling furiously up the hill. 他奋力地踩着自行车上坡。|例句：The government is pedaling a new economic policy. 政府正在推行一项新的经济政策。|近义词：cycling, riding, propelling|反义词： coasting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pedals his bike to work every day. = 他每天骑自行车上班。
She pedals slowly uphill. = 她缓慢地踩着自行车上坡。

一般过去时:
I pedaled as fast as I could. = 我尽可能快地骑车。
They pedaled through the countryside. = 他们骑车穿过乡村。

现在完成时:
He has pedaled over 100 miles this week. = 他本周已经骑了100多英里。
She has been pedaling for hours. = 她已经骑了好几个小时了。

过去完成时:
We had pedaled for hours before we reached the summit. = 我们到达山顶之前已经骑了好几个小时了。
They had pedaled all day in the rain. = 他们一整天都在雨中骑车。

将来时:
I will pedal to the store later. = 我晚点会骑车去商店。
We will be pedaling along the coast tomorrow. = 我们明天将在沿海骑车。

虚拟语气:
If I had a bike, I would pedal to work. = 如果我有一辆自行车，我会骑车上班。
I wish I could pedal faster. = 我希望我能骑得更快。

被动语态:
The bike was pedaled hard by the racer. = 比赛选手用力地踩踏自行车。
The new policy is being pedaled by the administration. (Figurative use) = 政府正在大力推行这项新政策。


疑问句:
Are you pedaling uphill? = 你是在上坡骑车吗？
How long have you been pedaling? = 你骑车多久了？

否定句:
He didn't pedal fast enough to win the race. = 他骑车不够快，没能赢得比赛。
She wasn't pedaling hard enough to keep up. = 她骑车不够用力，跟不上。
