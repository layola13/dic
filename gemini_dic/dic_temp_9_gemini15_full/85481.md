hassle:/ˈhæsəl/ | n., vt. | 麻烦；纠缠；与…争吵 | a difficult or troublesome situation; to annoy or bother persistently |例句：Moving house was a real hassle.  搬家真是一件麻烦事。  |近义词：trouble, bother, problem, difficulty |反义词：ease, simplicity


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The paperwork is a hassle. = 这些文书工作真麻烦。
This job is more of a hassle than I expected. = 这份工作比我预想的麻烦得多。


一般过去时:
Getting a visa was a real hassle. = 获取签证真是一件麻烦事。
The meeting was a huge hassle. = 那次会议非常麻烦。


现在完成时:
I've had enough hassles today. = 我今天已经受够麻烦了。
We've had several hassles with the neighbors lately. = 最近我们和邻居发生了好几次冲突。


过去完成时:
I had already anticipated the hassle of finding parking. = 我已经预料到找停车位会很麻烦。
They had avoided a lot of hassle by booking in advance. = 他们提前预订避免了很多麻烦。


将来时:
I predict there will be a hassle finding a hotel room. = 我预测找酒店房间将会很麻烦。
Finding transportation will be a hassle. = 找到交通工具将会很麻烦。


虚拟语气:
If I knew it would be such a hassle, I wouldn't have gone. = 如果我知道会这么麻烦，我就不会去了。
I wish this project wasn't such a hassle. = 我希望这个项目不要这么麻烦。


被动语态:
The project was hassled by unexpected delays. = 项目因为意料之外的延误而变得麻烦。
He was hassled by the police for questioning. = 他被警察盘问，很麻烦。


疑问句:
Is getting tickets a hassle? = 买票很麻烦吗？
Will finding a parking space be a hassle? = 找停车位会很麻烦吗？


否定句:
It wasn't a hassle at all. = 一点也不麻烦。
The process wasn't as much of a hassle as I'd feared. = 这个过程并没有我担心的那么麻烦。
