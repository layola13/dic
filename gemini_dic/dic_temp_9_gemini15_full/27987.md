canvass:/ˈkænvəs/ | v., n. |游说；细查；(油画)画布 |

英文解析:As a verb, to canvass means to solicit votes or opinions; to examine or discuss thoroughly. As a noun, it refers to a thorough examination or survey; the act of soliciting votes; a strong, closely woven fabric used for painting.


例句(动词):

The candidate canvassed the neighborhood for votes.  |  候选人挨家挨户地拉选票。

The committee canvassed the issue before making a decision. |  委员会在做出决定之前仔细研究了这个问题。


例句(名词):

The election canvass showed a close race. |  选举调查显示这是一场势均力敌的竞争。

The artist stretched the canvas before beginning to paint. |  艺术家在开始作画之前绷紧了画布。


近义词: (verb) solicit, survey, poll, examine; (noun)  survey, poll, scrutiny,  (for the fabric) cloth


反义词: (verb) ignore, neglect; (noun)  N/A


词性: verb, noun
