reevaluate:/ˌriːɪˈvæljuˌeɪt/ | vt. |重新评估；重新评价 | To evaluate something again, often with more information or a different perspective.  |例句：The company decided to reevaluate its marketing strategy after poor sales figures. 公司在销售业绩不佳后决定重新评估其营销策略。

例句：We need to reevaluate the risks involved before proceeding. 在继续之前，我们需要重新评估所涉及的风险。


近义词： reassess, reappraise, reconsider | 反义词：accept, confirm


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reevaluate the project every quarter. = 他们每季度都会重新评估这个项目。
We reevaluate our goals annually. = 我们每年都会重新评估我们的目标。


一般过去时:
The committee reevaluated the proposal last week. = 委员会上周重新评估了该提案。
She reevaluated her career path after the layoff. = 在裁员后，她重新评估了自己的职业道路。


现在完成时:
They have reevaluated the situation several times. = 他们已经多次重新评估了这种情况。
We have reevaluated our budget and found some savings. = 我们已经重新评估了我们的预算，并发现了一些节省之处。


过去完成时:
The manager had reevaluated the employee's performance before the meeting. = 经理在会议前已经重新评估了员工的表现。
After the incident, they had reevaluated their security protocols. = 事故发生后，他们重新评估了自己的安全协议。


将来时:
The board will reevaluate the investment next month. = 董事会将在下个月重新评估这项投资。
We will reevaluate the plan after receiving the new data. = 我们将在收到新数据后重新评估该计划。


虚拟语气:
If they reevaluated their priorities, they might be more successful. = 如果他们重新评估自己的优先级，他们可能会更成功。
I wish I had reevaluated my decision sooner. = 我希望我早点重新评估我的决定。


被动语态:
The project was reevaluated by the team. = 该项目由团队重新评估。
The policy will be reevaluated next year. = 该政策将于明年重新评估。


疑问句:
Will the company reevaluate its position on the issue? = 公司会重新评估其在这个问题上的立场吗？
Have they reevaluated the cost-effectiveness of the project? = 他们是否已经重新评估了该项目的成本效益？


否定句:
The government did not reevaluate its policies despite public outcry. = 尽管公众强烈抗议，政府也没有重新评估其政策。
They refused to reevaluate their strategy, even in the face of failure. = 即使面对失败，他们也拒绝重新评估他们的策略。


PHRASE->
reevaluate the situation = 重新评估局势
reevaluate the data = 重新评估数据
reevaluate the options = 重新评估选择
reevaluate the risk = 重新评估风险
reevaluate the impact = 重新评估影响

词源:
re- (prefix indicating repetition or again) + evaluate (from Latin *aestimare "to estimate")
