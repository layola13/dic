self-consoling: /ˌsɛlf kənˈsoʊlɪŋ/ | adj. | 自我安慰的；自我宽慰的 |  Describes the act of comforting oneself, especially in times of distress or disappointment. It implies finding solace and reassurance within oneself, without external help. |

例句：

She engaged in self-consoling behaviors after the breakup, rereading old letters and listening to sentimental music.  = 分手后，她进行了自我安慰的行为，重读旧信，听伤感音乐。

His self-consoling monologue only served to prolong his misery. = 他自我安慰的独白只会延长他的痛苦。


近义词：self-soothing, self-comforting, solace-seeking

反义词：self-flagellating, self-recriminating

词源:

The word "self-consoling" is a compound word.  "Self" is a prefix meaning "of or relating to oneself," and "consoling" is the present participle of the verb "console," meaning "to comfort."  The verb "console" ultimately derives from the Latin word *consolari*, meaning "to comfort" or "to strengthen."
