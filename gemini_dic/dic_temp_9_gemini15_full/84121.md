half-mad:/ˌhɑːfˈmæd/ | adj. | 半疯的，有点疯的 | Slightly or partially insane; eccentric or irrational.  The term suggests a degree of madness, but not complete insanity. It often implies unpredictable behavior or odd beliefs. |例句：He's a bit half-mad, always talking to squirrels. 他有点疯疯癫癫的，总是跟松鼠说话。


例句：She acted half-mad after hearing the news.  听到这个消息后，她表现得有点疯疯癫癫的。


近义词：eccentric, slightly crazy, unbalanced, deranged (less common in this context),  unhinged (stronger connotation)


反义词：sane, rational, composed, level-headed


词源:  The word is a compound of "half" meaning "partially" and "mad" meaning "insane".  The meaning is straightforward and reflects the literal combination of the two words.  "Mad" itself has a complex etymology tracing back to Old English and Proto-Germanic roots related to concepts of anger, frenzy, and mental illness.
