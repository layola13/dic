hemselve:/hɛmˈsɛlvz/| pron.|他们自己|The reflexive pronoun "themselves" refers to a plural group of people doing something to or for themselves.  It is used as the object of a verb or preposition, indicating the action affects the subject.  |They hurt themselves playing football. 他们踢足球时受伤了。|They completed the project themselves. 他们自己完成了这个项目。|themselves, each other|others, others


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They hurt themselves. = 他们伤了自己。
They dress themselves. = 他们自己穿衣服。


一般过去时:
They helped themselves to the food. = 他们自己拿食物吃。
They blamed themselves for the mistake. = 他们为这个错误责备自己。


现在完成时:
They have exhausted themselves. = 他们已经筋疲力尽了。
They have prepared themselves for the exam. = 他们已经为考试做好了准备。


过去完成时:
They had already prepared themselves before the meeting started. = 会议开始前他们已经做好了准备。
They had convinced themselves it was the right decision. = 他们已经说服自己这是正确的决定。


将来时:
They will reward themselves after finishing the work. = 完成工作后，他们将奖励自己。
They will find themselves in trouble if they continue this way. = 如果他们继续这样下去，他们会自找麻烦。


虚拟语气:
If they were to help themselves, they would feel better. = 如果他们帮助自己，他们会感觉更好。
I wish they could pull themselves together. = 我希望他们能振作起来。


被动语态:
(Note:  Reflexive pronouns are rarely used in the passive voice.)  While grammatically possible in some extremely rare constructions, it's unnatural and generally avoided.


疑问句:
Did they hurt themselves badly? = 他们伤得很重吗？
Did they do it all by themselves? = 他们完全是自己做的吗？


否定句:
They didn't hurt themselves. = 他们没有伤到自己。
They didn't help themselves to the cake. = 他们没有自己吃蛋糕。
