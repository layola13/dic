outguard:/ˈaʊtɡɑːrd/| n.|前哨，守卫，警戒部队|A detachment of troops stationed at an advanced position to guard against attack or provide early warning.  It refers to a group of people or a unit positioned at the perimeter to protect something or someone.|例句：The outguard reported the enemy's approach. 前哨报告了敌人的逼近。|例句：The castle's outguard was heavily armed. 城堡的前哨部队装备精良。|近义词：advance guard, vanguard, picket, outpost|反义词：rear guard, interior defense.
