consortship:/ˈkɒnsɔːtʃɪp/| n. |伴侣关系；配偶关系；合作关系|The state of being a consort; companionship; association; partnership.  It often implies a close and often formal relationship, especially between a ruler and their spouse, or between partners in a business or endeavor.|Example: The consortship between the king and queen was a cornerstone of the kingdom's stability. 国王和王后的伴侣关系是王国稳定的基石。|Example:  Their successful business was built on a strong consortship of shared ideas and complementary skills. 他们成功的生意是建立在强烈的共享理念和互补技能的合作关系之上。|近义词：companionship, partnership, alliance, association|反义词：estrangement, separation, isolation
