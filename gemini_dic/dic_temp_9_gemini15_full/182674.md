reoutlining: /ˌriːˈaʊtlaɪnɪŋ/ | v. | 重新概述；重新列提纲 | The act of outlining something again, often with revisions or improvements.  It implies a previous outline existed and is being reworked.  This could apply to a plan, an essay, a speech, or any structured piece of work. | Example:  After receiving feedback, she spent the afternoon reoutlining her presentation. = 在收到反馈后，她花了一下午的时间重新整理她的演示文稿。


近义词: revising, restructuring, reorganizing, re-planning


反义词: finalizing, completing


词性: 动词
