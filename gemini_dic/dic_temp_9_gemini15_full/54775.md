dispelling: /dɪˈspelɪŋ/ | v. |驱散；消除；打破(迷信等)|The act of dispelling something, typically something intangible like fear, doubt, or a misconception. It involves removing or overcoming something that is causing confusion, uncertainty, or negativity. |例句：The rising sun dispelled the fog.  旭日驱散了雾霾。

例句：The evidence dispelled any doubts about his guilt.  证据消除了对他有罪的任何怀疑。


近义词：dispersing, scattering, eliminating, removing, refuting

反义词：creating, fostering, encouraging, perpetuating

词源:
The word "dispelling" comes from the Middle English word "dispelen," which itself comes from the Old French word "despeler."  The prefix "dis-" means "away" or "apart," and the root "-pel-" comes from the Latin word "pellere," meaning "to drive" or "to push."  Therefore, "dispelling" literally means "driving away" or "pushing apart."
