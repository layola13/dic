scurry:/ˈskʌri/ | v. | 疾走；慌忙移动 | To move quickly and hurriedly, especially in a way that suggests nervousness or fear. | 例句：The mice scurried away when the cat appeared.  老鼠看到猫出现就赶紧跑开了。

例句：The children scurried to their rooms when they heard their mother's footsteps. 孩子们听到母亲的脚步声就赶紧跑回自己的房间。


近义词：rush, dash, scram, scamper | 反义词：saunter, stroll, dawdle


词源:  The word "scurry" likely originates from a combination of Old Norse and Old English roots.  While a precise etymology is debated, it's believed to relate to words suggesting quick or hurried movement. The sound symbolism also contributes to its meaning, reflecting the quick, light steps involved in scurrying.
