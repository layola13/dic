prefinite:/ˈpriːˌfaɪnaɪt/| adj. | 前限定的，前限定词的 |  Relating to or denoting a word or phrase that precedes and modifies a noun phrase, such as a determiner or an adjective.  A prefinite element is a word or phrase that comes before the finite verb in a clause. This term is primarily used in linguistics and grammatical analysis. | The prefinite element in the sentence "The big red ball bounced" is "The big red". | 近义词: preceding, antecedent | 反义词: postfinite
