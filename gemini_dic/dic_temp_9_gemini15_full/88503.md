/hɪˌstɔːriˈɒnəmər/ | n. |历史学家|A historiographer is a scholar who studies the writing of history, including its methods, sources, and biases.  Historiographers analyze how historical narratives are constructed and interpreted, often focusing on the influence of social, political, and cultural contexts. They are interested in the way history is written and presented, not necessarily in writing new history itself. |The historiographer meticulously analyzed the primary sources to determine the accuracy of the prevailing historical narrative.  历史学家仔细分析了主要来源，以确定流行的历史叙事的准确性。|historian, chronicler|  (no direct antonym, but could be considered opposite to someone who deliberately distorts historical facts)|名词
