resubstantiation:/ˌriːˌsʌbstænʃiˈeɪʃən/| n. |复质化；重新赋予实质|The theological concept of resubstantiation refers to the belief that during the Eucharist, the substance of bread and wine is not merely symbolically transformed but is actually changed into the body and blood of Christ, while retaining its outward appearance.  This differs from transubstantiation, where the substance is entirely transformed. |The debate about resubstantiation versus transubstantiation has been a central point of theological discussion for centuries.|近义词：re-sacralization (in certain contexts)|反义词：iconoclasm, annihilation|名词
