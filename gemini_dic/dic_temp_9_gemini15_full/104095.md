jump-on: /dʒʌmp ˈɒn/ | phrasal verb | 斥责；批评；抓住机会；突然袭击 | To criticize someone severely or to take advantage of an opportunity quickly.  |例句：The boss jumped on him for being late. 老板因为他的迟到而狠狠地批评了他。|近义词：criticize, reprimand, seize, exploit |反义词：praise, commend, ignore, miss


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jumps on every opportunity to advance his career. 他抓住每一个机会来提升他的事业。
They jump on anyone who makes a mistake. 他们抓住任何犯错的人进行批评。


一般过去时:
She jumped on the chance to travel abroad. 她抓住了出国旅行的机会。
The team jumped on the opposing team's weakness.  球队抓住了对方球队的弱点。


现在完成时:
He has jumped on several promising projects this year. 他今年已经参与了好几个有前景的项目。
They have jumped on me for every little thing lately. 最近他们因为任何小事都批评我。


过去完成时:
She had jumped on the bandwagon before it became popular.  她在它变得流行之前就加入了潮流。
They had jumped on the problem before it escalated. 他们在问题恶化之前就解决了它。


将来时:
He will jump on any opportunity to make money. 他会抓住任何赚钱的机会。
I will jump on that offer if they make it. 如果他们提出那个提议，我会抓住。


虚拟语气:
If I saw a chance like that, I would jump on it. 如果我看到那样一个机会，我会抓住它。
If he had seen the opportunity, he would have jumped on it. 如果他看到了机会，他就会抓住它。


被动语态:
The employee was jumped on for his poor performance.  这位员工因为表现糟糕而受到了严厉批评。
The idea was jumped on by investors immediately. 这个想法立即被投资者抓住。


疑问句:
Will you jump on this project? 你会参与这个项目吗？（抓住这个项目的机会）
Why did he jump on me like that? 他为什么那样斥责我？


否定句:
He didn't jump on the chance to speak. 他没有抓住发言的机会。
They didn't jump on the mistake immediately.  他们没有立即抓住那个错误。


PHRASE->
jump on the bandwagon: 赶时髦，跟风
jump to conclusions: 妄下结论
jump for joy: 欣喜若狂
jump the gun: 操之过急
jump ship: 弃船逃跑/辞职
