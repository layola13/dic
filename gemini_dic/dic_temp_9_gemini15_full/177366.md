reattributions: /ˌriːˌætrɪˈbjuːʃənz/ | n. | 再归因；重新归属；重新分配 | The act of attributing something again, often after a previous attribution has been questioned or changed.  It involves re-examining the source or origin of something and assigning it to a different source or owner. This is common in academic research, copyright law, and intellectual property discussions. |  例句：The reattributions of the paintings to a lesser-known artist sparked a debate among art historians.  重新将这些画归属于一位鲜为人知的艺术家引发了艺术史学家之间的争论。


例句：The newly discovered evidence led to the reattributions of several historical events. 新发现的证据导致对几起历史事件的重新归属。


近义词：reassignment, reapportionment, reallocation


反义词：original attribution, initial assignment


词性：名词
