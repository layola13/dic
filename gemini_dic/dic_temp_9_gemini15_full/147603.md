/ˌoʊvərˈplɔːzəbli/ | adj. | 过分可信地；过于合理地 | Excessively plausible; seemingly more believable than it actually is.  It suggests a level of believability that is exaggerated or unconvincing due to its very completeness or lack of flaws.  The word implies a sense of artificiality or manipulation in achieving this plausibility. | The witness's testimony, while overplausibly detailed, contained several inconsistencies.  目击证人的证词虽然细节过分可信，但包含一些前后矛盾之处。


近义词:  implausibly, unconvincingly, unrealistically,  unbelievably (in a negative context)

反义词: implausibly, unconvincingly, dubiously, questionably

词性: adjective (形容词)


例句:

He explained the incident overplausibly, making it seem too perfect to be true. 他对事件的解释过于完美，让人觉得难以置信。


The suspect's alibi, while overplausibly constructed, fell apart under scrutiny. 嫌疑人的不在场证明虽然编造得过于完美，但在仔细审查下还是露出了破绽。
