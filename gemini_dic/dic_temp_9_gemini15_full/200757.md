slep:/slɛp/| n.|睡眠，睡着|sleep refers to the natural periodic state of rest for the body and mind, characterized by the suspension of consciousness and relative inactivity.  It is essential for physical and mental restoration.|例句：I need eight hours of sleep every night. 我每天晚上需要睡八个小时。

例句：The baby slept soundly through the night.  婴儿整夜睡得很香。


近义词： slumber, doze, nap|反义词：wakefulness, insomnia


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sleeps eight hours a day. 他每天睡八个小时。
She usually sleeps well. 她通常睡得很好。


一般过去时:
I slept badly last night. 我昨晚睡得很不好。
They slept through the storm. 他们睡过了暴风雨。


现在完成时:
I haven't slept well recently. 我最近睡得不好。
She has slept for ten hours. 她已经睡了十个小时。


过去完成时:
I had slept for only an hour when the phone rang.  电话响的时候，我只睡了一个小时。
They had slept soundly before the earthquake. 地震前他们睡得很香。


将来时:
I will sleep better tonight. 今晚我会睡得更好。
We will be sleeping by 11 pm. 我们晚上11点就会睡觉。


虚拟语气:
If I had slept more, I wouldn't be so tired. 如果我睡得多一点，我就不会这么累了。
I wish I could sleep longer. 我希望我能睡久一点。


被动语态:
The baby was slept soundly.  婴儿睡得很香。(被动语态在此例中不太常用)
The problem was not slept on; it was addressed immediately.  这个问题没有被搁置，而是立即得到了解决。(被动语态用于比喻)


疑问句:
Did you sleep well? 你睡得好吗？
How long did you sleep? 你睡了多久？


否定句:
I didn't sleep a wink last night. 我昨晚一点也没睡。
She couldn't sleep because of the noise. 因为噪音，她睡不着。


PHRASE->
fall asleep = 入睡
go to sleep = 去睡觉
sleep tight = 睡个好觉
sleep soundly = 睡得很香
sleep in = 睡懒觉
