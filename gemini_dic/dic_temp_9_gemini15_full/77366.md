gauges:/ɡeɪdʒ/| n. v.|测量仪器；标准；判断；估计|A gauge is an instrument used for measuring something.  It can also refer to a standard or a way of judging or estimating something.|The mechanic used a gauge to check the tire pressure. 机械师用压力表检查轮胎气压。|The gauge on the dashboard shows the fuel level. 仪表盘上的仪表显示油量。|meter, measure, indicator|uncalibrated, inaccurate|n.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The gauge measures the water level accurately.  = 测量仪精确地测量水位。
This device gauges the pressure of the gas. = 这个装置测量气体的压力。


一般过去时:
The mechanic gauged the tire pressure before the race. = 赛前，技师测量了轮胎气压。
She gauged the depth of the pool before jumping in. = 她跳进游泳池前估量了一下水深。


现在完成时:
We have gauged the public's reaction to the new product. = 我们已经评估了公众对新产品的反应。
The scientists have gauged the impact of the earthquake. = 科学家们已经评估了地震的影响。


过去完成时:
They had gauged the distance before setting off. = 他们在出发前已经估算过距离。
She had gauged the mood of the room before making her speech. = 她在发表演讲前已经观察了房间里的气氛。


将来时:
We will gauge the effectiveness of the new marketing campaign next month. = 我们下个月将评估新营销活动的有效性。
They will gauge the public's opinion through surveys. = 他们将通过调查来衡量公众的意见。


虚拟语气:
If I had a more accurate gauge, I would have known the exact pressure. = 如果我有一个更精确的测量仪，我就会知道确切的压力。
I wish I had gauged the situation more carefully. = 我真希望我当时更仔细地判断了形势。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
