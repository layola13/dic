non-vintage: /ˌnɒnˈvɪntɪdʒ/ | adj. | 非年份的，不标年份的 | Not indicating or of a specified year of production, especially of wine.  This term often implies a blend of wines from different years, lacking the characteristics associated with a specific vintage. |  例句：This wine is non-vintage, a blend of grapes from several harvests. 这款酒是非年份酒，是几茬葡萄的混合酿造。


This non-vintage champagne is surprisingly good value. 这款非年份香槟物超所值，令人惊讶。


近义词：unspecified year, blended, mixed | 反义词：vintage, single-vintage


词源:  The word is a compound of "non-" (meaning "not") and "vintage."  "Vintage" ultimately derives from the Old French word "vendage," meaning "grape harvest" or "vintage."  Thus, "non-vintage" literally means "not of a specific harvest year."
