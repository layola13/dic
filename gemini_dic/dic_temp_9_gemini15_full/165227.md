preinsertion: /ˌpriːɪnˈsɜːrʃən/ | n. | 预先插入；提前插入 | The act of inserting something before something else, especially in a sequence or process.  It often refers to the addition of something before a specific point in time or a predetermined event. | 例句：The preinsertion of the prologue added depth to the play. =  序言的预先插入增加了戏剧的深度。

The preinsertion of a new paragraph disrupted the flow of the article. =  新段落的提前插入打乱了文章的流程。

| 近义词：prior insertion, anteposition | 反义词：postinsertion |  名词
