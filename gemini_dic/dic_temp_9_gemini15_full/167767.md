primary-care: /ˈpraɪməri kɛər/ | n. |初级保健，基本医疗护理 | Primary care refers to the initial point of contact a person has with the healthcare system, usually involving a general practitioner or family doctor.  It focuses on preventative care, managing common health problems, and referring patients to specialists when necessary. | Example:  Many people rely on their primary-care physician for annual checkups and treatment of minor illnesses.  许多人依靠他们的初级保健医生进行年度体检和治疗轻微疾病。 | 近义词：general practice, primary medical care | 反义词：specialized care, tertiary care |  词性：名词
