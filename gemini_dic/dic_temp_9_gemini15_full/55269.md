dissipater: /ˈdɪsɪpeɪtər/ | n. | 消散者；挥霍者 | A person or thing that dissipates something, especially energy or money.  Someone who wastes resources or money. |例句：The government's new policies are seen by some as a dissipater of public funds.  政府的新政策被一些人视为挥霍公共资金的行为。


The irresponsible gambler was a dissipater of his family's fortune.  那个不负责任的赌徒挥霍了他家的财产。


近义词：waster, squanderer, spendthrift | 反义词：saver, conserver


词源:  From the verb "dissipate," which comes from the Latin "dissipare," meaning "to scatter," "to disperse," or "to waste."  The suffix "-er" denotes an agent or doer of the action.

USAGE->

The sun dissipates the morning mist. = 太阳驱散了早晨的薄雾。
He dissipates his energy on trivial matters. = 他把精力浪费在琐事上。

虚拟语气:
