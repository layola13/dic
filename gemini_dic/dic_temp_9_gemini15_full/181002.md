reimpositions:/ˌriːɪmˌpoʊˈzɪʃənz/| n. |重新实施；再次施加|The reimpositions of sanctions led to widespread protests. 重新实施制裁导致了大规模抗议。|近义词：reinstatement, renewal, resumption |反义词：removal, abolition, repeal |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government reimposes taxes annually. = 政府每年重新征税。
Reimposition of restrictions is under consideration. = 正在考虑重新实施限制。


一般过去时:
They reimposed the curfew after the riots. = 暴乱后他们重新实施了宵禁。
The company reimposed its dress code last month. = 公司上个月重新实施了着装规范。


现在完成时:
They have reimposed travel restrictions due to the new variant. = 由于新的变种病毒，他们已经重新实施了旅行限制。
The authorities have reimposed a ban on public gatherings. = 当局已经重新禁止公众集会。


过去完成时:
They had reimposed the rules before the incident occurred. = 事件发生前他们已经重新实施了这些规定。
The school had reimposed the uniform policy after several complaints. = 在收到几起投诉后，学校已经重新实施了校服政策。


将来时:
They will reimpose the tariffs next year. = 他们明年将重新实施关税。
The court will likely reimpose the sentence. = 法院可能会重新判处该刑罚。


虚拟语气:
If they reimposed the tax, there would be a public outcry. = 如果他们重新征税，将会引发公众强烈抗议。
I wish they hadn't reimposed the restrictions. = 我希望他们没有重新实施这些限制。


被动语态:
The old laws were reimposed by the new government. = 旧法律被新政府重新实施了。
Further restrictions are being reimposed due to rising cases. = 由于病例增加，正在重新实施进一步的限制。


疑问句:
Will the sanctions be reimposed? = 制裁会重新实施吗？
Were the travel restrictions reimposed last year? = 旅行限制去年重新实施了吗？


否定句:
The government did not reimpose the fines. = 政府没有重新实施罚款。
They haven't reimposed any new regulations yet. = 他们还没有重新实施任何新的规章制度。
