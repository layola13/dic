plaister: /ˈpleɪstər/ | n. v. | 灰泥；膏药；敷药；涂抹 | A plaster is a substance used to coat a surface, such as a wall, or a medicinal preparation applied to the skin.  The verb form means to cover or repair something with plaster. | 例句：He used plaster to repair the damaged wall. 他用灰泥修补了损坏的墙壁。

The doctor applied a plaster to the wound. 医生在伤口上敷了药膏。


近义词：mortar, bandage, ointment | 反义词：remove, uncover


词源:  From Old French *plastre, from Latin emplastrum, from Greek ἐμπλάστρον (emplástron), from ἐμπλάσσειν (emplássein) "to daub, plaster".
