forbidden:/fərˈbɪdn/| adj.|被禁止的，禁止的|Something that is forbidden is not allowed.  It is against the rules or the law.  The act of forbidding something is often done by an authority figure.|例句：Smoking is forbidden in this building.  这个建筑物内禁止吸烟。|例句：The forbidden fruit in the Garden of Eden tempted Adam and Eve.  伊甸园里禁果诱惑了亚当和夏娃。|近义词：prohibited, banned, illegal, disallowed|反义词：permitted, allowed, legal


USAGE->
一般现在时:
That action is forbidden. = 那个行为是被禁止的。
This area is forbidden to the public. = 这个区域禁止公众进入。

一般过去时:
The king forbade the marriage. = 国王禁止了这桩婚事。
The teacher forbade the students from talking. = 老师禁止学生们说话。

现在完成时:
Access to the files has been forbidden. = 访问这些文件已经被禁止了。
The use of that software has been forbidden by the company. = 公司已经禁止使用该软件。


过去完成时:
He had forbidden me from seeing her. = 他已经禁止我见她了。
They had forbidden the use of cameras. = 他们已经禁止使用照相机了。

将来时:
The government will forbid the import of certain goods. = 政府将禁止进口某些商品。
They will forbid us from entering the area. = 他们将禁止我们进入该区域。


虚拟语气:
If only it weren't forbidden, I would do it. = 如果它不被禁止就好了，我会做的。
I wish smoking weren't forbidden in this restaurant. = 我希望这家餐厅不要禁止吸烟。


被动语态:
The book was forbidden by the church. = 这本书被教会禁止了。
Entrance to the site is forbidden. = 禁止进入该地点。


疑问句:
Is this area forbidden? = 这个区域禁止通行吗？
Are these activities forbidden? = 这些活动被禁止了吗？


否定句:
That's not forbidden. = 那没有被禁止。
The rule isn't forbidden. = 那条规则没有被禁止。
