entitlements:/ɪnˈtaɪtlmənts/ | n. | 权利；应得的待遇；福利；津贴 | Rights to benefits; a feeling of being entitled to something.  | Example:  The new law expands entitlements for the elderly. 这项新法律扩大了老年人的福利。

Example: She felt a sense of entitlement, believing she deserved special treatment. 她有一种优越感，认为自己应该受到特殊待遇。


近义词：rights, benefits, privileges, claims

反义词：deprivation, lack, absence


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He feels entitled to a promotion. 他觉得自己应该升职。
They believe they have entitlements to better healthcare. 他们相信自己有权获得更好的医疗保健。


一般过去时:
She felt entitled to a raise after all her hard work.  经过多年的努力，她觉得自己应该加薪。
They claimed their entitlements to unemployment benefits. 他们申请了失业救济金。


现在完成时:
He has felt entitled to success since his early years. 从小他就觉得自己应该成功。
They have received their entitlements in full. 他们已经完全收到了应得的款项。


过去完成时:
She had felt entitled to the inheritance. 她一直认为自己有权继承遗产。
They had not yet claimed their entitlements. 他们还没有申请他们的应得福利。


将来时:
He will be entitled to a pension when he retires. 他退休后将有权领取养老金。
They will receive their entitlements on schedule. 他们将按时收到他们的应得福利。


虚拟语气:
If he worked harder, he would be entitled to a bonus. 如果他工作更努力，他将有资格获得奖金。
I wish I were entitled to a longer vacation. 我希望我有资格休更长的假。


被动语态:
The benefits are entitled to all employees. 所有员工都有权享受这些福利。
The entitlements were granted to the veterans. 这些应得的福利被授予退伍军人。


疑问句:
Are you entitled to a discount? 你有资格享受折扣吗？
What entitlements are available to low-income families? 低收入家庭可以获得哪些福利？


否定句:
He is not entitled to a refund. 他无权获得退款。
They were not entitled to the property. 他们无权拥有该房产。
