kingdom: /ˈkɪŋdəm/ | n. |王国，领域，界 |

英文解析：A kingdom is a country ruled by a king or queen.  It can also refer to a particular area or domain of activity or interest.

例句：

The United Kingdom is made up of four countries.  = 英国由四个国家组成。

The animal kingdom is incredibly diverse. = 动物王国极其多样化。


近义词：realm, domain, region, empire

反义词：republic (in the sense of a form of government)


词源：

Old English *cynerīce, from cyning "king" + rīce "realm, power, rule".  The word ultimately derives from Proto-Germanic *kuningaz "king" (related to Old Norse konungr, Old High German kuning) and a Proto-Indo-European root *kwen- "to beget, generate".  The suffix -dom is of Germanic origin, related to Old English -dōm, meaning "state, condition, power".
