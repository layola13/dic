cross-row:/ˈkrɒsˌroʊ/ | n. | 横排；识字课；初级读物 | A cross-row is a line of letters or words arranged across a page, often used in teaching children to read.  It can also refer to the elementary reading lesson itself. | Example:  The teacher used a cross-row to teach the alphabet. 教师用横排字母教字母表。 The children struggled with their cross-row. 孩子们在识字课上苦苦挣扎。 | 近义词：alphabet chart, primer, reading lesson | 反义词：advanced reading, higher-level text |  名词
