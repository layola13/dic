consignations:/ˌkɒnsɪɡˈneɪʃənz/ | n. | 交付；托运；委托；寄售 | The act of consigning something; the state of being consigned.  It often refers to the process of entrusting goods or property to another person or entity for sale, shipment, or storage. | 例句：The consignations of goods to the warehouse were delayed due to the storm.  暴风雨导致货物交付仓库延误。

The consignations of art to the auction house were carefully documented.  送往拍卖行的艺术品都经过仔细登记。


近义词：shipments, deliveries, entrustments, consignments

反义词：retentions, withdrawals


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company makes regular consignations of goods to its distributors. = 公司定期向经销商交付货物。
He consignes his paintings to the gallery for sale. = 他将自己的画寄售给画廊。


一般过去时:
They consigned the package to the shipping company yesterday. = 他们昨天将包裹托付给了货运公司。
The artist consigned his artwork to a reputable gallery. = 艺术家将他的作品寄售给了一家信誉良好的画廊。


现在完成时:
We have consigned all the necessary documents to the lawyer. = 我们已将所有必要文件委托给了律师。
The exporter has consigned several shipments to different countries. = 出口商已向不同国家托运了几批货物。


过去完成时:
They had consigned the goods before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期前已交付了货物。
The company had consigned the majority of its products before the market crash. = 在市场崩盘之前，该公司已交付了大部分产品。


将来时:
She will consign the furniture to the moving company next week. = 她下周将家具托付给搬家公司。
We will consign the goods via air freight. = 我们将通过空运交付货物。


虚拟语气:
If I were to consign my car, I would choose a reputable dealer. = 如果我要寄售我的车，我会选择一家信誉良好的经销商。
I wish I had consigned the paintings sooner; they would have sold better. = 我真希望我早点把画寄售出去；那样它们就能卖得更好。


被动语态:
The goods were consigned to the wrong address. = 货物寄送到了错误的地址。
The shipment was consigned by sea. = 货物是通过海运交付的。


疑问句:
Where were the goods consigned? = 货物寄送到哪里了？
To whom were the documents consigned? = 文件委托给了谁？


否定句:
The artist did not consign his works to any gallery this year. = 这位艺术家今年没有将他的作品寄售给任何画廊。
They haven't yet consigned the shipment. = 他们还没有托运货物。


PHRASE->

consign to oblivion:  遗忘；使湮没
