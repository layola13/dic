forfeits:/ˈfɔːrfɪts/ | n. v. | 丧失；罚金；被没收的东西 |  To lose or give up something as a penalty or because of a fault; something that is lost or given up as a penalty. |

例句：

He forfeited his right to vote. = 他丧失了他的投票权。

The team forfeited the game after several players were injured. =  几名球员受伤后，球队放弃了比赛。


近义词：lose, surrender, relinquish

反义词：gain, win, acquire


词性：

名词：a penalty for breaking a rule or law.  (违反规章制度或法律的惩罚)

动词：to lose or be deprived of (something) as a penalty for wrongdoing. (因犯错而失去或被剥夺某物)


词源：

Old French *forfet, from *forfaire, from *for- (intensive prefix) + faire "to do, make".  The original sense was "to do wrong," hence "to forfeit."
