demarcate:/ˈdiːmɑːrkeɪt/ | vt. |划定界限；区分；标示界限 | To set the boundaries or limits of; to mark or distinguish | The new fence clearly demarcates the property line. 新的栅栏清楚地划定了地界。  The different colored flags demarcate the different teams' areas. 不同颜色的旗帜划分了不同队伍的区域。 | delineate, define, separate | blur, confuse, merge | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The council demarcates the areas for construction.  市议会划定建筑区域。
They demarcate their responsibilities clearly. 他们清楚地划分了各自的职责。

一般过去时:
The treaty demarcated the borders between the two countries.  条约划定了两国之间的边界。
The teacher demarcated the sections of the exam. 老师划分了考试的各个部分。

现在完成时:
The government has demarcated several protected areas. 政府已划定了几个保护区。
Scientists have demarcated the different phases of the project. 科学家们已经划分了项目的不同阶段。

过去完成时:
They had demarcated the playing field before the game started. 比赛开始前，他们已经划定了比赛场地。
The committee had demarcated the roles of each member before the meeting. 委员会在会议前已经划分了每个成员的角色。

将来时:
The surveyors will demarcate the land for the new housing development. 测量员将为新的住宅开发项目划定土地。
The team will demarcate the research areas for each student. 团队将为每个学生划分研究领域。

虚拟语气:
If they had demarcated the zones more clearly, there wouldn't have been so much confusion. 如果他们更清晰地划定区域，就不会有那么多混乱。
It would be helpful if they demarcated the different stages of the process. 如果他们能划分出流程的不同阶段，那就太好了。

被动语态:
The boundaries were demarcated by the surveyors. 边界由测量员划定。

疑问句:

否定句:
