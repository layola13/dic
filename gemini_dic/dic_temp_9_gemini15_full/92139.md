hypersensitiveness: /ˌhaɪpərsɛnsɪˈtɪvnɪs/ | n. |过敏性；过度敏感；神经质 |  An excessive sensitivity to stimuli;  an abnormally heightened sensitivity, often resulting in exaggerated reactions to external or internal factors.  This can manifest physically (allergies), emotionally (overreacting to criticism), or mentally (extreme anxiety). |例句：Her hypersensitiveness to criticism made it difficult to give her constructive feedback.  她的过度敏感让她难以接受建设性批评。


Her hypersensitiveness to loud noises caused her significant distress. 她对大声噪音的过度敏感让她非常痛苦。


近义词：oversensitivity, overreaction,  exaggerated sensitivity,  irritability,  reactivity | 反义词：insensitivity,  callousness,  apathy,  indifference


词源:  The word is formed by combining "hyper-" (meaning "excessive" or "above normal") with "sensitiveness" (meaning the quality or state of being sensitive). "Sensitive" originates from the Latin word "sentire," meaning "to feel."
