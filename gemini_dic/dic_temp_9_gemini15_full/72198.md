flops:/flɑːps/| /flɒps/| n. v.|失败；拍打声；笨拙的动作 | to fail; to fall or move heavily and clumsily; a sound made by something heavy falling or flapping |The new product was a complete flop.  这个新产品彻底失败了。  |The bird's wings flopped uselessly.  鸟的翅膀无力地拍打着。| success, hit | success, triumph | n. v.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often flops down on the sofa after work. 他下班后经常瘫倒在沙发上。
The fish flops around in the bucket. 鱼在桶里扑腾。


一般过去时:
The play flopped badly. 这出戏惨败了。
She flopped down onto the bed, exhausted. 她筋疲力尽地倒在床上。


现在完成时:
The company has flopped in the market. 这家公司在市场上失败了。
The plan has flopped, and we need a new one. 计划失败了，我们需要一个新的。


过去完成时:
The project had flopped before they even finished it.  他们在项目完成之前就已经失败了。
They had flopped several times before they finally succeeded. 他们在最终成功之前已经失败了几次。


将来时:
I think the movie will flop. 我认为这部电影会失败。
If we don't advertise, the product will flop. 如果我们不做广告，产品就会失败。


虚拟语气:
If the play hadn't flopped, the theatre would still be open. 如果那出戏没有失败，剧院现在还会开着。
I wish my business hadn't flopped. 我希望我的生意没有失败。


被动语态:
The project was flopped by the inexperienced team. 这个项目被缺乏经验的团队搞砸了。
The new song was flopped by the critics.  这首新歌被评论家批评了。


疑问句:
Did the movie flop? 这部电影失败了吗？
Will the new restaurant flop? 新餐厅会失败吗？


否定句:
The product didn't flop; it was a huge success.  产品没有失败；它非常成功。
The cake didn't flop; it was delicious. 蛋糕没有失败；它很好吃。
