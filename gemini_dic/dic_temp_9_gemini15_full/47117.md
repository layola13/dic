daunting: /ˈdɔːntɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人畏惧的；使人胆怯的；艰巨的 |  Something daunting is difficult, challenging, or frightening in a way that discourages or intimidates. It suggests a task or situation that seems overwhelming or impossible to handle. |

例句：

The task of writing a novel seemed daunting at first.  =  一开始写小说这项任务看起来令人畏惧。

The mountain climb was a daunting challenge. =  攀登这座山是一个艰巨的挑战。


近义词：intimidating, formidable, challenging, discouraging, overwhelming, disheartening

反义词：easy, simple, straightforward, encouraging, manageable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project seems daunting. = 这个项目看起来令人畏惧。
Climbing that mountain is daunting. = 攀登那座山令人望而却步。

一般过去时:
The exam was daunting. = 那次考试令人畏惧。
The news was initially daunting. = 这个消息最初令人沮丧。

现在完成时:
The task has proven more daunting than anticipated. = 这项任务已被证明比预期的更具挑战性。
The journey has been daunting, but rewarding. = 这段旅程很艰辛，但也很有意义。

过去完成时:
I had found the previous task daunting, so I was hesitant. = 我之前觉得任务很艰巨，所以我很犹豫。
They had already faced a daunting challenge. = 他们之前已经面临过一个艰巨的挑战。

将来时:
The upcoming meeting will be daunting. = 即将到来的会议将令人畏惧。
The future seems daunting. = 未来看起来令人畏惧。

虚拟语气:
If the task weren't so daunting, I would start immediately. = 如果任务不是那么艰巨，我会立即开始。
I wish the problem weren't so daunting. = 我希望问题不那么令人畏惧。

被动语态:
The climbers were daunted by the sheer height of the mountain. = 登山者被山的陡峭高度吓住了。
He was daunted by the complexity of the problem. = 他被问题的复杂性吓倒了。

疑问句:

Does the prospect of public speaking seem daunting? = 公开演讲的前景看起来令你害怕吗？

否定句:
The challenge wasn't as daunting as I'd expected. = 挑战并不像我预期的那样艰巨。
The problem wasn't daunting at all, it was quite simple. =  这个问题一点也不难，很简单。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "daunting" comes from the past participle of the verb "daunt," meaning "to overcome with fear; to intimidate."  "Daunt" itself has origins in the Old French word "donter," meaning "to tame, subdue."
