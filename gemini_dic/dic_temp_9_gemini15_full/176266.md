re-cost:/ˌriːˈkɒst/| v.|重新估价，重新计算成本|To calculate the cost of something again, often because of changed circumstances or new information.|例句：We need to re-cost the project after the unexpected increase in material prices. 我们需要在材料价格意外上涨后重新计算项目的成本。|例句：The architect had to re-cost the building plans after the client requested additional features. 建筑师在客户要求增加功能后不得不重新计算建筑图纸的成本。|近义词：recalculate, re-evaluate, reassess|反义词：estimate (initial cost)|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They re-cost the project every quarter. = 他们每季度都会重新计算项目的成本。
The company re-costs its products annually. = 公司每年都会重新计算其产品的成本。


一般过去时:
They re-cost the project last month. = 他们上个月重新计算了项目的成本。
The contractor re-costed the job after discovering hidden damage. = 承包商在发现隐藏损坏后重新计算了工程的成本。


现在完成时:
They have re-costed the project twice this year. = 他们今年已经两次重新计算了项目的成本。
The team has re-costed the proposal to reflect the updated specifications. = 团队已重新计算了提案的成本以反映更新的规格。


过去完成时:
They had re-costed the project before presenting the final budget. = 他们在提交最终预算之前已经重新计算了项目的成本。
The client had re-costed the renovations before agreeing to the contract. = 客户在同意合同之前已经重新计算了装修的成本。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If the materials cost more, we would re-cost the entire project. = 如果材料成本更高，我们将重新计算整个项目的成本。
I wish we had re-costed the project before starting. = 我希望我们在开始之前就重新计算了项目的成本。

否定句:
