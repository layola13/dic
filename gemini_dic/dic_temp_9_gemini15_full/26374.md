by-law:/ˈbaɪˌlɔː/|/ˈbaɪˌlɔː/| n. |内部规章；法规；地方法规 |A by-law is a rule made by a local authority or corporation.  These rules are usually less significant than national laws but still carry legal weight within their jurisdiction. They often govern matters such as parking, noise levels, and building codes. |Example: The town council passed a new by-law restricting the use of fireworks.  镇议会通过了一项限制燃放烟花的新规章。 The condominium association has a strict by-law regarding pet ownership.  这个公寓协会关于宠物所有权有一项严格的内部规章。|近义词：regulation, ordinance, rule |反义词：national law, federal law
