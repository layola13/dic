curs:/kɜːrs/| v.|诅咒；咒骂；使受苦；掠过；|to express a strong feeling of anger and dislike towards someone or something, typically by using unpleasant words:  |例句：He cursed his bad luck. 他咒骂自己的运气不好。|例句：The old woman cursed the thief. 老妇人诅咒小偷。|近义词：damn, imprecate, execrate |反义词：bless, praise


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He curses his enemies. = 他诅咒他的敌人。
She curses under her breath. = 她低声咒骂。

一般过去时:
He cursed the darkness. = 他诅咒黑暗。
They cursed their fate. = 他们诅咒他们的命运。

现在完成时:
He has cursed me for years. = 他诅咒了我很多年。
She has cursed her bad luck repeatedly. = 她反复诅咒自己的坏运气。

过去完成时:
He had cursed her before she left. = 他在她离开之前就诅咒了她。
They had cursed their situation many times. = 他们已经多次诅咒他们的处境。

将来时:
He will curse you if you do that. = 如果你那样做，他会诅咒你。
She will curse the day she met him. = 她会诅咒她遇见他的那天。

虚拟语气:
If I cursed him, I would feel guilty. = 如果我诅咒他，我会感到内疚。
I wish I hadn't cursed at him. = 我真希望我没有骂他。

被动语态:
He was cursed by the witch. = 他被女巫诅咒了。
The land was cursed with drought. = 这片土地遭受了干旱的诅咒。

疑问句:
Does he curse often? = 他经常诅咒吗？
Did she curse anyone? = 她诅咒过任何人吗？

否定句:
He doesn't curse easily. = 他不会轻易诅咒。
She didn't curse at the driver. = 她没有骂司机。
