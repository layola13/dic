daimyo:/ˈdaɪmjoʊ/| n. |大名（日本封建时代的大诸侯）|A daimyo (大名) was a powerful feudal lord in Japan during the Edo period (1603-1868).  They controlled vast territories and commanded large armies.  Their power was second only to the shogun.|例句：The daimyo controlled their own armies and levied taxes on their domains.  大名控制着他们自己的军队，并向他们的领地征税。

The daimyo often competed with each other for power and influence, resulting in occasional conflicts. 大名经常为了权力和影响力而互相竞争，导致偶尔发生冲突。|近义词：feudal lord, baron, noble|反义词：peasant, commoner
