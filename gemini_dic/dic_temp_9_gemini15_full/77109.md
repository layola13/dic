garter:/ˈɡɑːrtər/| n.|吊袜带；丝带；勋章带|A garter is a band or strap worn around the leg, typically to hold up a stocking or sock.  It can also refer to a ceremonial band worn as part of an order of knighthood. |例句：She wore a delicate lace garter. 她戴着一条精致的蕾丝吊袜带。

例句：The Order of the Garter is a British order of chivalry.  嘉德勋章是英国的一个骑士勋章。


近义词：ligature, band, strap

反义词：N/A


词源:The word "garter" comes from the Old French word "gartere," meaning "to bind" or "to fasten."  This ultimately derives from the Proto-Germanic root "*gart-" meaning "to gird" or "to surround."  The modern English word reflects the object's function of binding or fastening a stocking or sock to the leg.
