federacy:/ˈfɛdərəsi/| n. |联邦，联盟|A confederacy is a group of people, countries, or organizations that are joined together in an alliance or league.  It often implies a loose association, unlike a federation which usually has a stronger central government.  The term often carries connotations of temporary or unstable alliances. |Example: The states formed a loose confederacy before adopting the Constitution.  各州在采纳宪法之前组成了一个松散的联盟。


The thirteen colonies formed a confederacy before the adoption of the Constitution. 十三个殖民地在采纳宪法之前组成了一个联盟。|近义词：alliance, league, federation, coalition |反义词：independent states, single entity |名词
