forecasted:/ˈfɔːrkæstɪd/| v. |预报，预测|To predict or estimate (a future event or trend) based on available information.  It is the past participle of the verb "forecast".|例句：The weather forecasted heavy rain for tomorrow.  明天天气预报有暴雨。

例句：The analysts forecasted a strong economic growth for next year. 分析师预测明年经济将强劲增长。


近义词：predicted, projected, anticipated, estimated

反义词：None directly opposite, but "unexpected" or "unpredicted" could be considered contrasting.

词性:动词(过去分词)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The weatherman forecasts rain. =  天气预报员预报有雨。
They forecast a successful product launch. = 他们预测产品发布会将会成功。

一般过去时:
The meteorologist forecasted sunny skies. = 气象学家预测天气晴朗。
The company forecasted higher profits. = 公司预测利润将会更高。

现在完成时:
They have forecasted a market downturn. = 他们已经预测了市场低迷。
We have forecasted the project's completion date. = 我们已经预测了项目的完成日期。

过去完成时:
They had forecasted a rise in interest rates. = 他们先前已经预测到利率将会上升。
The economists had forecasted a recession before it happened. = 经济学家在经济衰退发生前就预测到了。

将来时:
They will forecast the election results. = 他们将会预测选举结果。
The experts will forecast the impact of the new policy. = 专家们将预测新政策的影响。

虚拟语气:
If they had forecasted the storm, we would have stayed home. = 如果他们预测到暴风雨，我们就不会出门了。
I wish they had forecasted the market crash. = 我真希望他们预测到市场崩盘。

被动语态:
The weather was forecasted to be clear. = 天气预报显示天气晴朗。
The economic growth was forecasted to be slow. = 经济增长被预测将会缓慢。

疑问句:
Was the earthquake forecasted? = 地震被预测到了吗？
Have the sales figures been forecasted accurately? = 销售数据被准确预测了吗？

否定句:
The storm was not forecasted. = 暴风雨没有被预测到。
The increase in inflation was not forecasted. = 通货膨胀的增加没有被预测到。
