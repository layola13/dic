pegged:/pɛɡd/| v.|钉住；确定；估价；|to fix something firmly in place, or to describe something as having a particular value or quality|The exchange rate was pegged to the dollar. 汇率与美元挂钩。|近义词：fix, fasten, attach, estimate, assess|反义词：unfix, detach, release


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pegs the price of the house at $500,000. = 他将这栋房子的价格定为50万美元。
They peg their hopes on a successful outcome. = 他们把希望寄托在成功的结果上。

一般过去时:
The government pegged the interest rate at 2%. = 政府将利率设定为2%。
She pegged him as a liar from the start. = 她从一开始就认定他是骗子。

现在完成时:
They have pegged the inflation rate for the year. = 他们已经确定了今年的通货膨胀率。
The company has pegged its marketing budget at $1 million. = 公司已将其营销预算确定为100万美元。

过去完成时:
They had pegged the value of the shares before the market crash. = 在市场崩盘之前，他们已经估算了股票的价值。
He had pegged her as a potential candidate for the job. = 他之前已经把她认定为该职位的潜在候选人。

将来时:
The central bank will peg the currency to the euro. = 中央银行将把货币与欧元挂钩。
They will peg their marketing campaign to the release of the new product. = 他们将把营销活动与新产品的发布联系起来。

虚拟语气:
If the government pegged the price of oil, it might help stabilize the market. = 如果政府限定石油价格，可能会帮助稳定市场。
I wish they had pegged the budget more accurately. = 我希望他们当初更准确地制定预算。

被动语态:
The exchange rate was pegged to the US dollar. = 汇率与美元挂钩。
The price of the product was pegged at $10. = 产品价格定为10美元。

疑问句:
Was the price pegged to the cost of materials? = 价格是根据材料成本确定的吗？
How was the value of the asset pegged? = 资产的价值是如何确定的？

否定句:
The government did not peg the price of bread. = 政府没有限定面包的价格。
They did not peg their expectations too high. = 他们没有把期望定得太高。


PHRASE->
peg down = 确定；查明
peg away at = 坚持不懈地做
peg out = 累垮；死亡
peg something to something = 将某物与某物挂钩
peg one's hopes on = 把希望寄托于
