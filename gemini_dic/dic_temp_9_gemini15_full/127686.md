negationists:/ˌnɛɡəˈʃənɪsts/| n. |否认者，否定论者|People who deny the existence or importance of something, often historical events or scientific facts.  They actively reject widely accepted evidence or conclusions.|Example: Holocaust negationists continue to spread misinformation despite overwhelming historical evidence.  大屠杀否认者继续散布虚假信息，尽管有压倒性的历史证据。|近义词：deniers, revisionists|反义词：affirmers, acceptanceists
