sheepishness:/ˈʃiːpiʃnəs/ | n. |羞怯；局促不安；羞愧 | A feeling of embarrassment or shyness.  It often arises from a sense of having done something wrong or foolish.  | Example:  Her sheepishness was evident as she mumbled an apology.  她的局促不安显而易见，她结结巴巴地道歉。


Example: The sheepishness on his face was undeniable after he tripped and fell. 他绊倒后脸上羞愧的表情是不可否认的。


近义词: shyness, embarrassment, shame, awkwardness, bashfulness

反义词: confidence, boldness, assurance, bravado


词源:  Derived from the adjective "sheepish," which ultimately comes from the Old English word "scēap," meaning "sheep."  The connection lies in the perceived meekness and timidity often associated with sheep.  The "-ish" suffix adds a qualitative or adjectival sense.  The noun form "-ness"  indicates a state or quality.
