gradualisms:/ˈɡrædʒuəlɪzəm/ | n. |渐变论；渐进主义 |The theory or practice of gradual change, especially in political or social reform.  It emphasizes incremental adjustments rather than sudden, revolutionary transformations. |Example: The government adopted a policy of gradualism in its economic reforms, introducing changes slowly to minimize disruption.  政府在经济改革中采取了渐进主义政策，缓慢地引入变革以尽量减少混乱。|近义词：incrementalism, evolution, gradual development |反义词：revolution, radicalism, abrupt change |名词
