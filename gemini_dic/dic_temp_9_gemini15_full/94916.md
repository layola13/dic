in-house: /ˌɪnˈhaʊs/ | adj. |内部的；公司内部的 | Existing or happening within a company or organization.  |例句：We decided to use in-house designers for the project. 我们决定使用公司内部的设计师来做这个项目。 |近义词：internal, domestic |反义词：external, outsourced


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company employs many in-house lawyers. = 这家公司雇佣了许多公司内部的律师。
Our in-house training program is very successful. = 我们的内部培训项目非常成功。


一般过去时:
They had an in-house celebration for the company anniversary. = 他们为公司周年庆举办了一场内部庆祝活动。
The project was completed by the in-house team. = 这个项目是由公司内部团队完成的。


现在完成时:
We have developed several in-house software applications. = 我们已经开发了几款内部软件应用程序。
The company has always relied on its in-house expertise. = 公司一直以来都依赖其内部专业知识。


过去完成时:
They had already completed the in-house review before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前已经完成了内部审查。
The marketing team had finalized the in-house campaign before the launch. = 市场营销团队在发布之前已经完成了内部宣传活动。


将来时:
We will use in-house resources for the next project. = 我们将在下一个项目中使用内部资源。
The company will continue to invest in its in-house talent. = 公司将继续投资于其内部人才。


虚拟语气:
If we had more in-house expertise, we would have completed the project sooner. = 如果我们有更多内部专业知识，我们就能更快地完成项目。
It would be better if we used in-house staff for this task. = 如果我们为此任务使用内部员工会更好。


被动语态:
The design was handled entirely in-house. = 设计完全由公司内部处理。
The marketing materials were created in-house. = 营销材料是在公司内部制作的。


疑问句:
Is the software developed in-house? = 这个软件是公司内部开发的吗？
Will the training be conducted in-house? = 培训将在公司内部进行吗？


否定句:
The product wasn't developed in-house; it was outsourced. = 该产品并非公司内部开发的；它是外包的。
They didn't use in-house consultants for that project. = 他们那个项目没有使用公司内部的顾问。
