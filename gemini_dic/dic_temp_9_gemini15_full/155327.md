pharisee:/ˈfærɪsiː/| n. |法利赛人，伪君子|A member of an ancient Jewish sect that emphasized strict adherence to the law and ritual.  The Pharisees were a prominent group in Judea during the time of Jesus, and are often depicted in the New Testament as hypocritical and self-righteous.  The term is now often used to describe someone who is outwardly religious but inwardly hypocritical.|例句：The Pharisees were known for their strict observance of Jewish law. 法利赛人以严格遵守犹太教法而闻名。

The Pharisees were criticized by Jesus for their hypocrisy.  耶稣批评法利赛人的伪善。|近义词：hypocrite, sanctimonious, self-righteous|反义词：sincere, genuine, humble|
