cement:/sɪˈment/| n., v.|水泥；巩固；胶合| a substance that is used to bind other substances together, especially in building.  As a verb, it means to solidify or strengthen.|例句：The workers used cement to build the wall. 工人们用水泥砌墙。The agreement cemented their friendship. 这项协议巩固了他们的友谊。|近义词：concrete, consolidate, bind, strengthen|反义词：separate, weaken|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The cement hardens quickly. = 水泥凝固很快。
He cements the bricks together. = 他把砖块粘在一起。


一般过去时:
They cemented the cracks in the road. = 他们用水泥修补了路上的裂缝。
The treaty cemented peace between the two countries. = 条约巩固了两国之间的和平。


现在完成时:
The builders have cemented the foundation. = 建筑工人已经完成了地基的浇筑。
She has cemented her reputation as a leading expert. = 她已经巩固了她作为领先专家的声誉。


过去完成时:
They had cemented the deal before the meeting. = 他们会在会议前达成协议。
The artist had cemented the stones together to create a sculpture. = 艺术家已经把石头粘在一起创作了一个雕塑。


将来时:
We will cement the tiles tomorrow. = 我们明天将粘贴瓷砖。
The new policy will cement the company's position in the market. = 新政策将巩固公司在市场上的地位。


虚拟语气:
If we had more cement, we could finish the job. = 如果我们有更多水泥，我们就能完成这项工作。
If he cemented the relationship earlier, it wouldn't have ended. = 如果他早点巩固这段关系，它就不会结束了。


被动语态:
The wall was cemented together strongly. = 这堵墙被牢固地砌在一起。
The deal was cemented by both parties. = 这笔交易得到了双方的认可并巩固下来。


疑问句:
Is the cement dry yet? = 水泥干了吗？
Has the agreement been cemented? = 协议已经确定了吗？


否定句:
The cement didn't set properly. = 水泥没有完全凝固。
They didn't cement the relationship. = 他们没有巩固这种关系。
