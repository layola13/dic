repeating-decimals:/rɪˈpiːtɪŋ ˈdesɪməlz/| n.|循环小数|A repeating decimal is a decimal number that has a digit or a group of digits that repeats infinitely.  For example, 1/3 = 0.333... is a repeating decimal because the digit 3 repeats infinitely.  The repeating part is often indicated by a bar over the repeating digits (e.g., 0.3̅).|Example: The fraction 1/7 results in the repeating decimal 0.142857142857...|近义词：recurring decimal|反义词：terminating decimal
