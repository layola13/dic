collectivity: /kəˌlɛkˈtɪvɪti/ | n. | 集体；集体性；全体 | The state or quality of being collective; a group of people considered as a whole.  It refers to a group of individuals acting or being considered as a single unit.  This can involve shared goals, interests, or actions.  |例句：The collectivity of the villagers decided to build a new school.  村民全体决定建造一所新学校。

The strong sense of collectivity within the team led to their success.  团队内部强烈的集体感促成了他们的成功。


近义词：unity, group, aggregate, community, body

反义词：individuality, separateness, isolation


词性：名词

词源:
The word "collectivity" derives from the Late Latin word "collectivus," meaning "gathered together," which in turn originates from the Latin verb "colligere," meaning "to gather" or "to collect."  The suffix "-ity" forms abstract nouns indicating a state, quality, or condition.
