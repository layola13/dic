jaganatha: /ˌdʒɑːɡəˈnɑːθə/ | n. | 贾甘纳特 |  The name Jaganatha refers to a form of the Hindu deity Vishnu, particularly revered in Puri, Odisha, India.  It is a Sanskrit compound word meaning "Lord of the World." The deity is depicted as a dark-colored image, and the annual Ratha Yatra festival is a major event associated with his worship. |  The Jaganatha temple in Puri is a significant pilgrimage site for Hindus.  | 近义词:  Vishnu (in a specific context) | 反义词: None directly applicable |  名词
