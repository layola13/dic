gotten:/ˈɡɑːtn/| /ˈɡɒtn/| v.| 得到；获得；变成 |The past participle of "get". It is primarily used in American English.  In British English, "got" is more common.  It indicates possession, acquisition, or a resulting state. |例句：I've gotten a new job. = 我找到了一份新工作。

I had gotten everything ready before he arrived. = 他来之前我已经把一切都准备好了。


近义词：obtained, acquired, received, become

反义词：lost, missed, forfeited


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I get a letter every week. = 我每周都收到一封信。
They get along well. = 他们相处融洽。


一般过去时:
I got a new car last year. = 我去年买了一辆新车。
She got sick suddenly. = 她突然病了。


现在完成时:
I have gotten used to the noise. = 我已经习惯了噪音。
He has gotten much better at playing the piano. = 他弹钢琴的技术进步了很多。


过去完成时:
I had gotten ready before the guests arrived. = 客人到来之前我已经准备好了。
She had gotten lost before she found her way. = 在找到路之前她迷路了。


将来时:
I will get the book tomorrow. = 我明天会拿到那本书。
They will get married next month. = 他们下个月结婚。


虚拟语气:
If I had gotten there earlier, I would have seen him. = 如果我早点到那里，我就看到他了。
I wish I had gotten a better grade on the test. = 我希望我在考试中取得更好的成绩。


被动语态:
The job was gotten by my friend. = 这份工作是我朋友得到的。（较少用，更常用的是"The job was obtained by my friend."）
The problem was gotten rid of. = 问题解决了。（较少用，更常用的是"The problem was solved."）


疑问句:
Did you get my email? = 你收到我的邮件了吗？
What did you get for your birthday? = 你生日收到了什么礼物？


否定句:
I didn't get the job. = 我没有得到那份工作。
She didn't get enough sleep last night. = 她昨晚睡眠不足。


PHRASE->
get along = 相处
get over = 克服
get rid of = 摆脱
get away with = 逃脱惩罚
get back = 回来
