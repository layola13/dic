habilitation:/ˌhæbɪlɪˈteɪʃən/| n. |康复；资格认定；授权|The process of restoring someone to health or normal life through training and therapy; the action of officially granting someone a right or title.|例句：The rehabilitation center provided physical therapy and occupational therapy to help patients regain their independence. 康复中心提供物理治疗和职业治疗，以帮助病人恢复独立生活能力。|近义词：revalidation, reaccreditation, restoration|反义词：incapacitation, disablement

词源:
From Middle French *habilitation, from Latin habilitāre "to qualify, enable," from habilis "able, fit."  The English word retains the sense of enabling or qualifying, particularly in academic contexts (habilitation thesis).  The modern medical sense of restoring health developed later.
