imaginative: /ɪˈmædʒɪnətɪv/ | adj. | 富有想象力的；有创意的 |  Having or showing a lively imagination.  Characterized by or resulting from imagination. |

例句：

He's a very imaginative writer, creating fantastical worlds in his novels.  他是一位非常富有想象力的作家，在他的小说中创造了奇幻的世界。

Her imaginative solution to the problem impressed everyone. 她对这个问题富有想象力的解决方案给每个人留下了深刻的印象。


近义词：creative, inventive, original, innovative

反义词：unimaginative, unimaginative, prosaic, literal


词源:

From imaginative (adjective), from imagine + -ative.  The word "imagine" itself comes from the Latin "imaginari," meaning "to form a mental image."


用法说明:

imaginative作为形容词，主要用来描述人或事物具有丰富的想象力或创造力。它可以修饰人、物、作品、想法、方案等等。


例句补充：

(修饰人) He is an imaginative child, always coming up with new games. 他是一个富有想象力的孩子，总是想出新的游戏。

(修饰物) The film's imaginative set design was truly breathtaking. 电影富有想象力的布景设计真是令人叹为观止。

(修饰作品) The novel is full of imaginative characters and exciting plots. 这部小说充满了富有想象力的人物和精彩的情节。

(修饰想法/方案)  Her imaginative marketing strategy led to a significant increase in sales. 她富有想象力的营销策略导致销售额大幅增长。


PHRASE->

an imaginative solution 一个富有想象力的解决方案

an imaginative approach 一个富有想象力的方法

imaginative storytelling 富有想象力的讲故事

an imaginative design 富有想象力的设计

imaginative use of language 富有想象力的语言运用
