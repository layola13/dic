disowns: /dɪsˈoʊnz/ | vt. | 否认；与…断绝关系 | To refuse to acknowledge or accept as one's own; to renounce a connection or relationship with. | 例句：He disowned his son after the scandal.  他因丑闻而与儿子断绝关系。

例句：The wealthy businessman disowned his illegitimate child.  那位富有的商人否认了他私生子。


近义词：renounce, repudiate, reject, abjure


反义词：acknowledge, claim, accept, own


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disowns his past mistakes. 他否认他过去的错误。
She disowns her radical views. 她否认她激进的观点。


一般过去时:
My father disowned me when I married him. 我结婚时，我父亲与我断绝了关系。
The company disowned the faulty product. 公司否认了有缺陷的产品。


现在完成时:
He has disowned his family. 他已经和他的家人断绝了关系。
She has disowned all responsibility for the incident. 她已经否认对这一事件负有任何责任。


过去完成时:
He had disowned his inheritance before his death. 他死前已经放弃了他的遗产。
She had disowned her beliefs by that point. 到那时，她已经放弃了自己的信仰。


将来时:
I fear he will disown his son if he continues this behavior. 我担心如果他继续这种行为，他会与儿子断绝关系。
She will likely disown the project if it fails. 如果项目失败，她可能会放弃这个项目。


虚拟语气:
If he were to disown his family, it would break their hearts. 如果他与家人断绝关系，将会伤透他们的心。
I wish he hadn't disowned his sister. 我真希望他没有和他姐姐断绝关系。


被动语态:
His son was disowned by his father. 他的儿子被他父亲抛弃了。
The statement was disowned by the government.  政府否认了该声明。


疑问句:

否定句:
He did not disown his responsibilities. 他没有否认他的责任。
