non-orientable: /ˌnɒnˌɔːriˈɛntəbəl/ | adj. |不可定向的|  Describes a surface or space that cannot be consistently assigned an "inside" and "outside," or a surface where a continuous path can be traced that reverses orientation.  This means you can't consistently define a normal vector at every point on the surface.  A Möbius strip is a classic example. | The Klein bottle is another example of a non-orientable surface.  |近义词: untwisted (in a specific context)| 反义词: orientable |
