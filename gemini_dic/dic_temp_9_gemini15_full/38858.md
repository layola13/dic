confederate:/kənˈfɛdəreɪt/| n., v., adj.|同盟者；邦联成员；联合；结盟；同谋|

英文解析:
As a noun, a confederate refers to a person, state, or country that is joined with others in a league or alliance.  It can also refer to someone who is involved in a secret plot or conspiracy with others. As a verb, it means to unite or combine into a confederacy or league. As an adjective, it describes something related to or characteristic of a confederacy.

例句：

n. (名词):
The Confederate states fought against the Union during the American Civil War. = 南方邦联各州在南北战争期间与联邦对抗。

The two companies formed a confederate to share resources. = 这两家公司结成联盟以共享资源。


v. (动词):
Several nations confederated to form a powerful trading bloc. = 数个国家联合起来组成了一个强大的贸易集团。

The rebels confederated against the tyrannical government. = 反叛者联合起来对抗暴政政府。


adj. (形容词):
Confederate flags were a common sight in the South before the Civil War. = 内战前，南方经常可以看到邦联旗帜。


近义词:
Noun: ally, accomplice, partner, associate
Verb: unite, ally, combine, join
Adjective: allied, federated


反义词:
Noun: enemy, opponent, adversary
Verb: separate, divide, disunite
Adjective: opposing, antagonistic


词性:
noun, verb, adjective
