overadorning: /ˌoʊvərˈædɔːrnɪŋ/ | v. |过度装饰，装饰过度 | The act of decorating excessively;  excessive ornamentation.  It implies that the decoration is excessive or inappropriate, detracting from the overall effect rather than enhancing it. | Example: Overadorning the cake with frosting made it look gaudy and less appealing.  The overadorning of the Christmas tree hid the beauty of the tree itself. | 近义词: overdecoration, embellishment (in an excessive way), overloading | 反义词: simplicity, minimalism, understatement

词源:
The word "overadorning" is a combination of the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") and the verb "adorning" (meaning "to decorate").  "Adorning" itself comes from the Old French word "adorner,"  ultimately derived from Latin "adornāre," meaning "to adorn" or "to equip."
