divisibility:/ˌdɪvɪzəˈbɪləti/| n. |可除性，整除性|The property of a number being divisible by another number without leaving a remainder.  It refers to the ability of a number to be divided evenly by another number.  For example, the divisibility of 12 by 3 is easily demonstrated because 12 divided by 3 equals 4 with no remainder.|The divisibility of 12 by 3 is clear; 12/3 = 4.  However, the divisibility of 13 by 2 is not possible as it leaves a remainder of 1.|divisibility rules,  factors, multiples|indivisibility

词源:
The word "divisibility" is derived from the verb "divide," ultimately stemming from the Latin word "dividere," meaning "to divide" or "to separate." The suffix "-ibility" indicates the capacity or capability of being divided.
