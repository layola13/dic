ruffled:/ˈrʌf(ə)ld/| adj., v.| 褶皱的；烦躁的；弄乱的 |Disordered or slightly disarranged in appearance; agitated or disturbed.|The wind ruffled her hair. | 近义词：dishevelled, tousled, flustered | 反义词：smooth, calm, serene

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The wind ruffles her hair. = 风吹乱了她的头发。
He ruffles his feathers when he's angry. = 他生气时会竖起羽毛。


一般过去时:
The storm ruffled the lake's surface. = 暴风雨弄皱了湖面。
The news ruffled her composure. = 这个消息扰乱了她的平静。


现在完成时:
The children have ruffled the bedsheets. = 孩子们把床单弄皱了。
The argument has ruffled my feathers. = 那场争论让我心烦意乱。


过去完成时:
The cat had ruffled its fur before it attacked. = 猫在攻击前已经竖起了毛。
She had ruffled her dress before the interview. = 面试前她把衣服弄平整了。


将来时:
The strong wind will ruffle the sea. = 强风会使大海波涛汹涌。
I think the news will ruffle her feathers. = 我想这个消息会让她心烦。


虚拟语气:
If the wind blew harder, it would ruffle the leaves more. = 如果风吹得更猛烈，它会把树叶吹得更乱。
If I hadn't been so ruffled, I would have handled the situation better. = 如果我不那么烦躁，我会更好地处理这种情况。


被动语态:
Her hair was ruffled by the wind. = 她的头发被风吹乱了。
His feathers were ruffled by the criticism. = 他因为批评而感到不安。


疑问句:
Is your hair ruffled? = 你的头发乱了吗？
Was his composure ruffled by the news? = 他因为这个消息而心烦意乱吗？


否定句:
The wind didn't ruffle the calm surface of the lake. = 风没有吹皱平静的湖面。
The news didn't ruffle her at all. = 这个消息一点也没让她心烦。
