dyslysin:/ˈdɪslɪsɪn/| n. |脱氧胆酸|A secondary bile acid formed in the intestine by bacterial reduction of deoxycholic acid. It is a less potent detergent than deoxycholic acid and is largely eliminated in the feces.  |Dyslysin is a relatively inactive bile acid, unlike its precursor deoxycholic acid. |近义词：暂无精确近义词|反义词：暂无精确反义词|名词
