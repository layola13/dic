scorning: /ˈskɔːrnɪŋ/ | v. | 轻蔑；嘲笑；不屑 |  To treat with contempt; to scorn.  |例句：He scorned their offer of help. 他不屑接受他们提供的帮助。|  例句：She scorned the idea as ridiculous. 她认为这个主意荒谬可笑，不屑一顾。| 近义词：despising, contemning, sneering, mocking | 反义词：admiring, respecting, praising | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scorns their efforts. = 他轻蔑他们的努力。
She scorns dishonest behavior. = 她鄙视不诚实的行为。

一般过去时:
He scorned her advice. = 他不屑她的建议。
They scorned the warnings. = 他们无视警告。

现在完成时:
He has scorned every opportunity given to him. = 他不屑每一个给予他的机会。
She has scorned the rules her whole life. = 她一生都蔑视规则。

过去完成时:
He had scorned her feelings for years. = 多年来他一直轻视她的感情。
They had scorned the evidence before realizing its importance. = 在意识到证据的重要性之前，他们一直轻视它。

将来时:
He will scorn any attempt at reconciliation. = 他将蔑视任何和解的尝试。
She will scorn his advances. = 她将不屑他的追求。

虚拟语气:
If he knew better, he wouldn't scorn their help. = 如果他更了解情况，他就不会轻视他们的帮助。
I wish he wouldn't scorn my ideas so readily. = 我希望他不那么轻易地蔑视我的想法。

被动语态:
His efforts were scorned by his colleagues. = 他的努力遭到同事们的轻蔑。
Her suggestions were scornfully scorned. = 她的建议被轻蔑地拒绝了。

疑问句:
Does he scorn their help? = 他是否轻蔑他们的帮助？
Why do they scorn the poor? = 为什么他们轻视穷人？


否定句:
He doesn't scorn her advice. = 他并不轻视她的建议。
They didn't scorn the evidence. = 他们没有轻视证据。
