radicalization: /ˌrædɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |激进化；极端化 | The process by which an individual or group adopts increasingly extreme political, social, or religious ideologies.  This often involves a rejection of mainstream values and a commitment to radical action. | Example: The rapid radicalization of young people online is a serious concern.  网上年轻人的迅速激进化令人担忧。  The government is trying to combat the radicalization of terrorist groups. 政府正试图打击恐怖组织的激进化。 |近义词：extremification, polarization |反义词：moderate, de-radicalization
