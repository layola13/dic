droit:/drɔɪt/| n. |权利；法权；公正；正义|Right; entitlement; justice; law.  A legal right, especially one guaranteed by law.|The Universal Declaration of Human Rights proclaims the right to education. 世界人权宣言宣布受教育的权利。|近义词：right, privilege, claim, entitlement|反义词：wrong, injustice, illegality


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He has a right to speak. = 他有说话的权利。
She enjoys her right to vote. = 她享有投票权。


一般过去时:
They exercised their right to protest. = 他们行使了抗议的权利。
He lost his right to appeal. = 他失去了上诉的权利。


现在完成时:
We have asserted our rights for years. = 我们多年来一直在维护我们的权利。
She has finally obtained her legal rights. = 她最终获得了她的合法权利。


过去完成时:
They had already lost their right to property. = 他们已经失去了财产权。
He had never known his rights before. = 他以前从未了解过自己的权利。


将来时:
They will fight for their rights in the future. = 他们将来会为自己的权利而斗争。
She will claim her right to inheritance. = 她将要求继承权。


虚拟语气:
If I had known my rights, I would have acted differently. = 如果我知道我的权利，我会采取不同的行动。
I wish I had the right to refuse. = 我希望我有权拒绝。


被动语态:
Their rights were violated. = 他们的权利受到了侵犯。
The right to free speech is often protected. = 言论自由权经常受到保护。


疑问句:
What are your rights in this matter? = 在这件事上你的权利是什么？
Do you know what your rights are? = 你知道你的权利是什么吗？


否定句:
He doesn't have the right to do that. = 他没有权利那样做。
They were denied their basic rights. = 他们的基本权利被剥夺了。


PHRASE->
droit de seigneur:  (obsolete) Lord's right (to the first night with a bride).  封建领主初夜权 (已过时)
droit d'auteur: copyright 版权
droit civil: civil law 民法
droit international: international law 国际法
droit public: public law 公法
