contractors:/kənˈtræktərz/| n. |承包商，订约人 | A contractor is an individual or company that undertakes a contract to provide goods or services.  They often work on a project basis, agreeing to complete specific tasks for a set fee. | Example:  The construction company hired several subcontractors to complete different aspects of the building project. 这家建筑公司雇佣了几家分包商来完成建筑项目的不同方面。 The general contractor oversaw all the subcontractors. 总承包商负责监督所有分包商。|近义词：builders, suppliers, vendors |反义词：employees

词源:
The word "contractor" comes from the verb "contract," meaning "to enter into a formal agreement."  The suffix "-or" indicates the person or thing that performs the action.  The root of "contract" traces back to the Latin word "contrahere," meaning "to draw together," implying the bringing together of parties in an agreement.
