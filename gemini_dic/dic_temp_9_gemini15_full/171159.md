psychogalvanometer: /ˌsaɪkoʊɡælˈvænɒmɪtər/ | n. |心理电流计| A psychogalvanometer is a device used to measure changes in the electrical resistance of the skin, which are believed to reflect emotional or psychological states.  It is based on the principle that emotional arousal can cause changes in sweat gland activity, altering the skin's conductivity.  While once popular, its use has largely been superseded by more sophisticated techniques. | Example:  The researcher used a psychogalvanometer to measure the subject's emotional response to the stimuli.  研究人员用心理电流计来测量受试者对刺激的情绪反应。 | 近义词: galvanometer (more general term)| 反义词: None directly applicable |  词性: noun
