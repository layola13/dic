monotrophic:/ˌmɒnəˈtrɒfɪk/ | adj. | 单食性的；单营养的 | Relating to or characterized by feeding on a single type of food.  Monotrophic organisms, often found in specialized ecological niches, are highly adapted to their specific diet.  Their survival is directly linked to the availability of their preferred food source. This term is commonly used in ecology and zoology to describe the feeding habits of animals and sometimes plants. | Example:  The koala is a monotrophic marsupial, feeding exclusively on eucalyptus leaves.  = 考拉是一种单食性有袋动物，只吃桉树叶。 The study revealed a monotrophic diet in the insect larvae. = 这项研究揭示了昆虫幼虫的单一饮食。 | 近义词:  specialized, selective, restricted | 反义词: polyphagic, omnivorous
