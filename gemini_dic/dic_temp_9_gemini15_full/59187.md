dysmorphia:/dɪsˈmɔːrfɪə/| n. |形体变形症；变形恐惧症 |A psychological disorder characterized by a distorted body image and an obsessive preoccupation with perceived flaws in one's appearance.  People with body dysmorphic disorder (BDD) spend a lot of time worrying about their appearance and may engage in repetitive behaviors, such as checking their appearance in the mirror or comparing themselves to others.  | Example:  Her dysmorphia caused her to spend hours each day scrutinizing her reflection.  她患有形体变形症，每天花数小时仔细检查自己的倒影。

|近义词：body image distortion, BDD (Body Dysmorphic Disorder) |反义词：body acceptance, self-love |词性：名词
