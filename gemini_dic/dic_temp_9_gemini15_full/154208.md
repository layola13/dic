peripatetics: /ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪks/ | n. | 逍遥学派；巡回讲学；漫游 |  The philosophical school of Aristotle, characterized by its practice of teaching while walking.  Also refers to the practice of walking while teaching or studying, or the act of wandering or traveling. | Example:  Aristotle's peripatetics were renowned throughout Greece.  亚里士多德的逍遥学派在整个希腊都享有盛名。The professor's peripatetics around the campus were a familiar sight.  教授在校园里四处走动教学是很常见的景象。 | 近义词：Itinerant philosophy, ambulatory teaching, wandering | 反义词：sedentary study, stationary teaching
