jawbreaking:/ˈdʒɔːˌbreɪkɪŋ/| adj.|难以发音的，拗口的|Difficult to pronounce; extremely hard to articulate.  The word itself is often used to describe words that are difficult to pronounce due to their length or unusual sound combinations.|例句：The name "floccinaucinihilipilification" is quite jawbreaking.  这个词“floccinaucinihilipilification”很难发音。|例句：That technical term was so jawbreaking, I had to look it up.  那个专业术语太拗口了，我不得不查字典。|近义词：unpronounceable, difficult, hard to say|反义词：easy to pronounce, simple, clear


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "what".  It only describes the difficulty of pronunciation.  Therefore, tense and voice are not applicable.
