precriticisms:/ˌpriːˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/| n.|预先批评；事先批评|Precriticisms are criticisms made before something is released or completed, often focusing on potential flaws or weaknesses.  They are often used in the context of  works in progress, aiming to improve the final product.|例句：The film received harsh precriticisms before its release, leading to significant changes in post-production.  这部电影在上映前受到了严厉的预先批评，导致后期制作进行了重大修改。|近义词：advance criticism, preliminary criticism, prior critique|反义词：post-criticism, retrospective criticism
