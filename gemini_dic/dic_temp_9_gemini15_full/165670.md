premonitor: /ˌpriːˈmɒnɪtər/ | /ˌpriːˈmɑːnɪtɔːr/ | n. | 预兆；预感；预告者 | A premonition is a feeling that something, typically something bad, is going to happen.  It is an intuitive feeling of anticipation, often concerning an event that has not yet occurred.  A premonitor is a person or thing that gives a warning or indication of something to come. |例句：She had a premonition of disaster. 她有一种灾难将要发生的预感。

The dark clouds were a premonitor of the approaching storm.  乌云是即将到来的暴风雨的预兆。|近义词：foreboding, presentiment, omen, harbinger |反义词：certainty, assurance
