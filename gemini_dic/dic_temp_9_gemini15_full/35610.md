coasted:/ˈkoʊstɪd/| v.|滑行；轻松获胜；依赖；依靠 | to move along smoothly and easily, without much effort; to succeed without much effort; to rely on something or someone |例句：The car coasted to a stop.  汽车缓缓停下。|例句：He coasted to victory in the election. 他在选举中轻松获胜。|近义词：glided, drifted, cruised, relied on, depended on|反义词：struggled, fought, labored


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He coasts downhill on his bike. = 他骑着自行车沿山坡滑行。
She coasts through life without much effort. = 她不费吹灰之力就过着生活。


一般过去时:
The boat coasted gently into the harbor. = 船缓缓驶入港口。
He coasted to victory in the race. = 他在比赛中轻松获胜。


现在完成时:
They have coasted along for years without any real challenges. = 他们多年来一直过着轻松的生活，没有任何真正的挑战。
She has coasted through her career on her family's connections. = 她依靠家族关系在事业上轻松过关。


过去完成时:
The plane had coasted to a halt on the runway. = 飞机在跑道上滑行停了下来。
Before the final exam, he had coasted along without studying much. = 期末考试前，他一直没有怎么学习。


将来时:
The ship will coast along the shoreline. = 船将沿着海岸线航行。
I think he will coast to victory in the next election. = 我认为他将在下次选举中轻松获胜。


虚拟语气:
If he had studied harder, he wouldn't have had to coast through the exam. = 如果他学习更努力，他就不用在考试中轻松过关了。
If the wind had been stronger, the boat would have coasted faster. = 如果风更大一些，船就会滑行得更快。


被动语态:
The boat was coasted into the harbor by the experienced captain. = 经验丰富的船长将船缓缓驶入港口。（被动语态略显不自然，此句为勉强构造）
The victory was coasted to by the skillful athlete.（被动语态略显不自然，此句为勉强构造）= 高超的运动员轻松取得了胜利。


疑问句:
Did the car coast to a stop? = 车子是不是缓缓停下来的？
Does she usually coast through her work? = 她通常工作都很轻松吗？


否定句:
The car didn't coast to a stop; it braked suddenly. = 车子没有缓缓停下；而是突然刹车了。
He didn't coast through the exam; he studied hard. = 他没有在考试中轻松过关；他学习很努力。
