self-classifications:/ˌsɛlfˌklæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/| n. |自我分类；自我归类 |The process by which individuals or groups categorize themselves based on various characteristics. This can be based on factors such as race, gender, social class, profession, or any other attribute relevant to the individual or group's identity and understanding of themselves within a broader social context. Self-classifications are often influenced by social norms, cultural expectations, and personal experiences.  |Example:  The survey asked participants to provide their self-classifications regarding ethnicity and religious affiliation.  自我分类调查要求参与者提供他们关于种族和宗教信仰的自我分类。


|近义词：self-categorization, self-identification, self-designation |反义词：external classification, other-categorization |名词|
