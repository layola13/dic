orchestration:/ˌɔːrkiˈstreɪʃən/ | n. |编排；管弦乐队演奏；协调；组织|The skillful arrangement of musical parts in a composition; the act of coordinating or organizing something.|The orchestra's performance was a brilliant orchestration of sound and emotion. 这支管弦乐队的演奏是声音和情感的精彩编排。The successful launch of the product required careful orchestration of marketing and sales efforts. 产品的成功发布需要仔细协调营销和销售工作。|arrangement, coordination, organization |chaos, disorder|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The conductor carefully orchestrates the music. 指挥仔细地编排音乐。
She orchestrates her daily schedule with precision. 她精确地安排她的日常日程。


一般过去时:
The composer orchestrated the symphony beautifully. 作曲家精美地编排了这首交响乐。
He orchestrated a surprise party for his friend. 他为他朋友策划了一个惊喜派对。


现在完成时:
They have orchestrated a successful fundraising campaign. 他们已经策划了一个成功的筹款活动。
She has orchestrated several major events in the past year. 她在过去一年里策划了几场大型活动。


过去完成时:
