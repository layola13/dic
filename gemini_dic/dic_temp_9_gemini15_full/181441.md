rejection:/rɪˈdʒɛkʃən/| n. |拒绝；排斥；被抛弃的事物 |The act of rejecting something or someone; the state of being rejected.  It can refer to a refusal to accept something, a dismissal of an idea or person, or the state of being cast aside or unwanted. |例句：His application for the job received a prompt rejection.  他的求职申请很快就被拒绝了。

例句：The rejection of his theory led to years of further research. 他的理论被否决，导致了数年进一步的研究。


近义词：refusal, dismissal, repudiation, spurning |反义词：acceptance, approval


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company rejects many applications. = 公司拒绝了许多申请。
He rejects all offers of help. = 他拒绝所有帮助。


一般过去时:
They rejected my proposal. = 他们拒绝了我的提议。
She rejected his advances. = 她拒绝了他的追求。


现在完成时:
I have rejected several job offers. = 我已经拒绝了好几份工作邀请。
He has rejected all attempts at reconciliation. = 他拒绝了所有和解的尝试。


过去完成时:
They had rejected the plan before I arrived. = 我到达之前他们就已经否决了这个计划。
She had rejected his help several times. = 她已经拒绝过他好几次的帮助了。


将来时:
They will reject the proposal if it's not revised. = 如果提案没有修改，他们将会拒绝。
I will reject any unfair treatment. = 我将拒绝任何不公平的待遇。


虚拟语气:
If they rejected my idea, I would be disappointed. = 如果他们拒绝我的想法，我会很失望。
I wish they hadn't rejected my application. = 我真希望他们没有拒绝我的申请。


被动语态:
His application was rejected. = 他的申请被拒绝了。
The proposal was rejected by the committee. = 该提案被委员会否决了。


疑问句:

否定句:
