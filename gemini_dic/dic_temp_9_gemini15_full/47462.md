dealer:/ˈdiːlər/| n. |经销商，商人，发牌者|A person or business that buys and sells goods, especially in large quantities; someone who deals cards in a game.|例句：He's a car dealer. 他是汽车经销商。|例句：The dealer shuffled the cards. 发牌者洗牌。|近义词：trader, merchant, distributor |反义词：consumer, buyer


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dealer sells cars. = 经销商卖车。
He deals cards in the poker game. = 他在扑克游戏中发牌。

一般过去时:
The dealer sold a lot of cars last month. = 上个月经销商卖了很多车。
She dealt the cards quickly and efficiently. = 她快速有效地发了牌。

现在完成时:
The dealer has sold all the new models. = 经销商已经卖掉了所有新款车型。
He has dealt thousands of hands of poker. = 他已经发了成千上万副扑克牌。

过去完成时:
The dealer had already sold the car before I arrived. = 我到达之前经销商已经卖掉了那辆车。
Before the game started, she had dealt the cards. = 游戏开始前，她已经发了牌。

将来时:
The dealer will sell more cars next year. = 经销商明年会卖出更多汽车。
He will deal the cards fairly. = 他会公平地发牌。

虚拟语气:
If I were a dealer, I would sell only high-quality products. = 如果我是经销商，我只会出售高质量的产品。
If he had dealt the cards differently, the outcome of the game would have been different. = 如果他发牌方式不同，比赛结果就会不同。

被动语态:
The cars are sold by the dealer. = 车子由经销商出售。
The cards were dealt fairly. = 牌发得很公平。

疑问句:
Is he a car dealer? = 他是汽车经销商吗？
Who dealt the cards? = 谁发的牌？

否定句:
He isn't a drug dealer. = 他不是毒贩。
She didn't deal the cards properly. = 她没有正确地发牌。
