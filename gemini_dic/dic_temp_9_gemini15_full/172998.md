quantivalent:/ˈkwɒntɪveɪlənt/| adj.| 多价的；多化合价的 | Having or exhibiting more than one valence.  A quantivalent element can combine with other elements in more than one ratio. For example, iron (Fe) is quantivalent because it can exist in both the +2 (ferrous) and +3 (ferric) oxidation states. | Example:  Iron is a quantivalent element, existing in both ferrous and ferric forms.  铁是一种多价元素，以亚铁和铁两种形式存在。|近义词：polyvalent, multivalent | 反义词：univalent, monovalent
