overpublicizes:/ˌoʊvərˈpʌblɪsaɪzɪz/| v.|过度宣传，大肆宣扬|To promote or advertise something excessively or to an inappropriate degree.|The company overpublicized its new product, leading to inflated expectations and eventual disappointment.  这家公司对其新产品进行了过度宣传，导致期望值过高，最终令人失望。|近义词：hype, overpromote, oversell|反义词：undersell, downplay


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The media overpublicizes every minor celebrity scandal. = 媒体过度渲染每一个微不足道的名人丑闻。
He constantly overpublicizes his achievements. = 他不断地夸大自己的成就。


一般过去时:
They overpublicized the concert, resulting in a smaller crowd than expected. = 他们对这场音乐会进行了过度宣传，导致观众比预期少。
The politician overpublicized his charitable work to gain votes. = 这位政治家过度宣传了他的慈善工作以赢得选票。


现在完成时:
The company has overpublicized its new technology, creating unrealistic expectations. = 该公司已经对其新技术进行了过度宣传，造成了不切实际的期望。
She has overpublicized her involvement in the project, taking credit that wasn't entirely hers. = 她过度宣传了她参与该项目的情况，揽取了并非完全属于她的功劳。


过去完成时:
The marketing team had overpublicized the product launch, leading to initial disappointment. = 营销团队对产品发布进行了过度宣传，导致了最初的失望。
They had overpublicized the event, so many people were turned away at the door. = 他们对活动进行了过度宣传，所以许多人在门口被拒之门外。


将来时:
They will overpublicize the upcoming election, creating unnecessary tension. = 他们将过度宣传即将到来的选举，制造不必要的紧张气氛。
The company plans to overpublicize its new product line to dominate the market. = 该公司计划过度宣传其新的产品线以主导市场。


虚拟语气:
