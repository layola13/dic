poster:/ˈpoʊstər/| n.|海报，招贴|A poster is a large printed notice or advertisement, often with pictures or illustrations.|例句：They put up posters advertising the concert. 他们张贴了广告音乐会的海报。|近义词：bill, placard, notice|反义词:None


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The poster advertises a new movie. = 这张海报宣传一部新电影。
That poster depicts a beautiful landscape. = 那张海报描绘了一幅美丽的风景。


一般过去时:
He designed a poster for the school play. = 他为学校戏剧设计了一张海报。
They removed all the posters from the wall. = 他们把墙上的所有海报都取了下来。


现在完成时:
We have put up posters all over town. = 我们已经在镇上到处贴了海报。
She has designed many posters for various events. = 她为各种活动设计了很多海报。


过去完成时:
They had already put up the posters before the event started. = 活动开始前他们已经贴好了海报。
I had seen that poster many times before. = 我以前见过很多次那张海报。


将来时:
They will put up new posters next week. = 他们下周将张贴新的海报。
We will design a poster for the upcoming conference. = 我们将为即将到来的会议设计一张海报。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would design a more creative poster. = 如果我有更多时间，我会设计一张更有创意的海报。
I wish I had designed a better poster for the competition. = 我希望我为比赛设计了一张更好的海报。


被动语态:
The poster was designed by a famous artist. = 这张海报是由一位著名艺术家设计的。
The posters are being put up around the city. = 海报正在全城张贴。


疑问句:
What is on the poster? = 海报上是什么？
Who designed this poster? = 谁设计的这张海报？


否定句:
The poster doesn't show the price. = 海报没有显示价格。
He didn't create that poster. = 他没有创作那张海报。
