platitudinous:/ˌplætɪˈtjuːdɪnəs/ | adj. |陈腐的；平庸的；老生常谈的 |  Characterized by or expressing platitudes; commonplace, banal, and unoriginal.  The speaker's platitudinous remarks bored the audience.  | 近义词：banal, commonplace, trite, hackneyed, clichéd | 反义词：original, innovative, insightful, profound


例句：

His speech was filled with platitudinous statements about the importance of hard work and honesty.  他的演讲充满了关于努力工作和诚实重要性的陈词滥调。


The novel's plot was predictable and platitudinous, lacking any originality or depth. 这部小说的情节可预测且陈腐，缺乏任何原创性或深度。


词源：

Derived from "platitude" (a commonplace or trite remark), which itself comes from the French word "plat," meaning "flat." The sense is that platitudinous statements are flat, lacking depth or originality.


用法说明：

Platitudinous is primarily used as an adjective to describe something, usually speech or writing, that is filled with clichés, overused expressions, or banal statements.  It implies a lack of originality, insight, or depth.  It carries a negative connotation, suggesting that the subject matter is dull and uninspiring.
