cross-plough: /ˈkrɒs plaʊ/ | n. | 交叉犁耕 | A method of plowing land where furrows are plowed in two directions, typically at right angles to each other, to create a more finely tilled and level field. This technique helps to improve drainage, aeration, and weed control.  | Example:  The farmer used cross-ploughing to prepare the field for planting. = 农民使用交叉犁耕来准备播种的田地。  | 近义词: double plowing,  cross-tilling | 反义词: single plowing,  unidirectional plowing |  名词
