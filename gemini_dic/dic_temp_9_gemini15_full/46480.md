daimyo:/ˈdaɪmjoʊ/| n. |大名，日本封建时代的藩主|A daimyo was a powerful feudal lord in Japan, ruling over a large territory and possessing a private army.  They held significant political and military influence during the Edo period.  Their power was largely dependent on the size of their domains and the strength of their samurai armies.|例句：The daimyo held considerable sway over their domains.  大名对其领地拥有相当大的影响力。|例句：Many daimyo were involved in the political intrigues of the Edo period.  许多大名都卷入了江户时期的政治阴谋。|近义词：feudal lord, baron (less precise)|反义词：commoner, peasant|名词
