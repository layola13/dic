coinciding:/ˌkoʊɪnˈsaɪdɪŋ/| v. |同时发生，一致 |happening or existing at the same time.  |例句：The eclipse coincided with the festival. 日食与节日同时发生。

例句：His interests coincide with mine. 他的兴趣与我的相同。


近义词：corresponding, simultaneous, concurrent |反义词：conflicting, differing, disparate|词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The events coincide every year. = 这些事件每年都同时发生。
Her birthday coincides with mine. = 她的生日与我的生日是同一天。


一般过去时:
Their arrival coincided with the start of the meeting. = 他们的到来与会议的开始同时发生。
The two events coincided perfectly. = 这两件事完美地同时发生。


现在完成时:
Several factors have coincided to create this situation. = 几个因素共同导致了这种情况。
Many events have coincided to make this a momentous year. = 许多事件同时发生，使这一年成为具有里程碑意义的一年。


过去完成时:
The accident had coincided with a power outage. = 事故发生时正巧停电。
The discovery had coincided with a period of intense research. = 这一发现正值一个高强度的研究时期。


将来时:
The concert will coincide with the city's anniversary celebration. = 音乐会将与该市的周年庆典同时举行。
The launch date will coincide with a major marketing campaign. = 上市日期将与一次大型营销活动同时进行。


虚拟语气:
If the meetings coincided, we could combine them. = 如果会议时间相同，我们可以把它们合并。
I wish my vacation coincided with his. = 我希望我的假期能与他的假期重合。


被动语态:
The two events were found to have coincided. = 发现这两件事同时发生了。
The timing of the announcements was deliberately made to coincide. = 公告的时间安排是故意使其同时发布的。


疑问句:
Does your schedule coincide with the conference? = 你的日程安排与会议时间冲突吗？
Do the two dates coincide? = 这两个日期是否相同？


否定句:
The two events did not coincide. = 这两件事没有同时发生。
Their schedules did not coincide, preventing collaboration. = 他们的时间安排不一致，妨碍了合作。



PHRASE->

coincide in = 在...方面一致
coincide on = 在...方面意见一致
to largely coincide = 在很大程度上重合
