chromatophore:/ˈkroʊmətəˌfɔːr/|/ˈkrəʊmætəfɔːr/| n. |色素细胞|A chromatophore is a pigmented cell found in many organisms, such as amphibians, fish, reptiles, and cephalopods. These cells contain pigments that can be dispersed or concentrated, allowing the organism to change its color.  This color change can serve various functions, including camouflage, communication, and thermoregulation.| The chameleon's chromatophores allow it to change its skin color to match its surroundings. 变色龙的色素细胞使它能够改变皮肤颜色以匹配周围环境。  The squid uses its chromatophores to create dazzling displays to attract mates or intimidate rivals. 鱿鱼利用其色素细胞创造令人眼花缭乱的景象来吸引配偶或恐吓竞争对手。|pigment cell, color cell|  (no direct antonym, but opposite function could be described as  "non-pigmented cell" or "cell lacking color-changing ability")|名词
