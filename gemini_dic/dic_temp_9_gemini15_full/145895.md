overdifferentiation: /ˌoʊvərˌdɪfərɛnʃiˈeɪʃən/ | n. |过度分化；过度专业化 | The excessive specialization or differentiation of cells, tissues, or organs beyond what is necessary or functional.  This term is often used in biology and developmental biology to describe a situation where cells have become too specialized, leading to potential problems. It can also be used in a more general sense to refer to excessive specialization in other contexts, such as in organizational structures or social systems. | Example:  Overdifferentiation of cells in the embryo can lead to developmental abnormalities.  过度分化会导致胚胎发育异常。 |近义词：hyperdifferentiation, overspecialization | 反义词：underdifferentiation, generalization
