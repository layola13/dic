phlegmatism: /ˈflɛɡməˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 冷静；迟钝；冷漠；痰液过多 |  A temperament characterized by apathy, sluggishness, and indifference.  It refers to a calm, unemotional, and stoic disposition.  The term originally referred to an excess of phlegm, one of the four humors in ancient Greek medicine, which was believed to be associated with this temperament. |  His phlegmatism in the face of danger was remarkable.  他的临危不乱令人惊叹。

He showed remarkable phlegmatism during the crisis. 他在危机期间表现出非凡的冷静。


近义词：calmness, composure, stoicism, apathy, impassivity | 反义词：excitability, emotionalism, passion, impulsiveness


词源:  From Middle English *flegmatisme, from Old French flegmatisme, from Late Latin phlegmatismus, from Greek phlegmatikos, from phlegma "phlegm."  The Greek word itself derives from phlegein "to burn," referring to the inflammatory nature of certain bodily humors.  The association between phlegm and a calm temperament is a historical medical concept.
