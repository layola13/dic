militated:/ˈmɪlɪteɪtɪd/| v.|反对；起作用；导致|To work against; to be a powerful factor in hindering or preventing something.  It often implies a strong or forceful opposition.|例句：His lack of experience militated against his promotion.他的缺乏经验妨碍了他的晋升。

例句：The high cost of living militated against their decision to move to the city.高昂的生活成本妨碍了他们搬到城市的决定。


近义词：counteracted, opposed, hindered, impeded, obstructed

反义词：aided, assisted, facilitated, promoted


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His stubbornness militates against his success. = 他的顽固妨碍了他的成功。
The cold weather militates against outdoor activities. = 天气寒冷不利于户外活动。


一般过去时:
His fear militated against his taking action. = 他的恐惧阻止了他采取行动。
The lack of funding militated against the project's completion. = 资金不足阻碍了项目的完成。


现在完成时:
His prejudices have militated against his understanding of others. = 他的偏见一直妨碍着他对别人的理解。
The government's policies have militated against economic growth. = 政府的政策一直阻碍着经济增长。


过去完成时:
His doubts had militated against his accepting the offer. = 他的疑虑曾使他不愿接受这份工作。
The bad weather had militated against their plans for a picnic. = 恶劣的天气曾打乱了他们野餐的计划。


将来时:
The new regulations will militate against small businesses. = 新的法规将不利于小型企业。
His lack of preparation will militate against his chances of success. = 他的准备不足将不利于他的成功几率。


虚拟语气:
If his pride hadn't militated against it, he would have apologized. = 如果他的骄傲没有妨碍，他就会道歉了。
If the economic downturn hadn't militated against it, the project would have been completed on time. = 如果经济衰退没有阻碍，项目本来可以按时完成。


被动语态:
His application was militated against by his poor references. = 他的申请由于推荐信不好而被否决。
Their plans were militated against by unforeseen circumstances. = 他们的计划由于不可预见的情况而受阻。


疑问句:
Does his age militate against his chances of employment? = 他的年龄是否会影响他的就业机会？
Will the lack of resources militate against the project's success? = 资源匮乏是否会影响项目的成功？


否定句:
His hard work did not militate against his success. = 他的努力工作并没有妨碍他的成功。
The favorable weather conditions did not militate against their outdoor activities. = 良好的天气条件并没有妨碍他们的户外活动。
