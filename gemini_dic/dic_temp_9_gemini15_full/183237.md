repressing:/rɪˈprɛsɪŋ/ | v. |压制；抑制；压抑 |To repress something means to prevent it from being expressed or released.  It often refers to emotions or thoughts, but can also apply to actions or movements.  |例句：The government is repressing dissent.  政府正在压制异见。

The dictator tried to repress the people's anger. 独裁者试图压制人民的愤怒。


近义词：suppress, subdue, stifle, restrain |反义词：express, unleash, release, encourage


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He represses his feelings. 他压抑自己的感情。
They repress any sign of rebellion. 他们压制任何反抗的迹象。


一般过去时:
She repressed a sigh. 她强忍住一声叹息。
The regime repressed all opposition. 该政权压制了所有反对势力。


现在完成时:
He has repressed his anger for years. 他多年来一直压抑着自己的怒火。
They have repressed freedom of speech. 他们压制了言论自由。


过去完成时:
She had repressed her emotions for so long that they finally erupted. 她压抑了这么久的感情，最终爆发了。
The government had repressed any news of the protests. 政府压制了所有关于抗议的新闻。


将来时:
They will repress any future attempts at rebellion. 他们将压制任何未来的叛乱企图。
He will repress the urge to speak out. 他将压制住想大声说话的冲动。


虚拟语气:
If he didn't repress his feelings, he would be a happier person. 如果他不压抑自己的感情，他会更快乐。
I wish I hadn't repressed my creativity for so long. 我真希望我没有压抑我的创造力这么久。


被动语态:
Dissent is repressed in many countries. 在许多国家，异见都被压制。
All forms of protest were repressed by the authorities. 所有形式的抗议都被当局压制了。


疑问句:
Is he repressing his true feelings? 他是否在压抑自己的真实感情？
Are they repressing the truth? 他们是否在隐瞒真相？


否定句:
He doesn't repress his opinions. 他不会压抑自己的观点。
They didn't repress their laughter. 他们没有忍住笑。
