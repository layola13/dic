ferrotype:/ˈfɛroʊtaɪp/| n. |铁版照相；铁片照片|A ferrotype is a photographic print made on a thin, usually black-enameled, iron plate.  The process involves coating the plate with collodion, sensitizing it, exposing it in a camera, and then developing and fixing the image directly on the plate.  The resulting image is characterized by its glossy, almost mirror-like finish.|Example: My grandfather had a collection of ferrotypes, showing family portraits from the late 19th century.  我的祖父收藏了一批铁版照片，展示了19世纪后期的全家福照片。|近义词：tintype, melainotype|反义词：None.
