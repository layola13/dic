predeterminate:/ˌpriːdɪˈtɜːrmɪneɪt/| adj.| 预先决定的；预定的 | Predetermined means decided or settled in advance. It implies a lack of spontaneity or chance.  |The outcome of the experiment was predetermined by the flawed design.  实验结果由于设计缺陷而被预先决定了。  The predetermined route took them through the mountains. 预定的路线带他们穿过山区。| preordained, prearranged | random, spontaneous | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rules predetermine the game's outcome. = 规则预先决定了比赛的结果。
This system predetermines the allocation of resources. = 这个系统预先决定了资源的分配。


一般过去时:
They predetermined the meeting's agenda. = 他们预先确定了会议的议程。
The judge predetermined the defendant's guilt. = 法官预先认定被告有罪。


现在完成时:
The committee has predetermined the winners. = 委员会已经预先确定了获胜者。
We have predetermined the project's timeline. = 我们已经预先确定了项目的进度安排。


过去完成时:
They had predetermined the strategy before the battle. = 他们在战斗前就已经预先确定了战略。
The company had predetermined its marketing budget. = 公司已经预先确定了其市场营销预算。


将来时:
They will predetermine the selection criteria. = 他们将预先确定选择标准。
The government will predetermine the tax rates for the next fiscal year. = 政府将预先确定下一财政年度的税率。


虚拟语气:
If they had predetermined the risks, they might have avoided the disaster. = 如果他们预先确定了风险，他们也许就能避免这场灾难。
I wish they had predetermined a better solution. = 我希望他们预先确定了一个更好的解决方案。


被动语态:
The results were predetermined by the biased sample. = 结果被有偏差的样本预先决定了。
The winner was predetermined before the competition even started. = 比赛开始前就已经预先确定了获胜者。


疑问句:
Were the participants' choices predetermined? = 参与者的选择是预先决定好的吗？
Is the outcome predetermined by external factors? = 结果是由外部因素预先决定的吗？


否定句:
The results were not predetermined; they were random. = 结果不是预先确定的；它们是随机的。
The judges did not predetermine the rankings. = 评委们没有预先确定排名。


PHRASE->
predetermined outcome = 预先确定的结果
predetermined course = 预先确定的路线
predetermined plan = 预先制定的计划
predetermined response = 预先设定的反应
predetermined factor = 预先确定的因素

词源:
pre- (前缀，表示“在…之前”) + determine (动词，表示“决定”)  + -ate (形容词后缀)
