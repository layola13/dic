of-sorts:/əvˈsɔːrts/| adj.|  某种的；差不多；像……那样的 |  Used to describe something that is not exactly what it is called but is similar enough to be considered as such. It implies a degree of vagueness or imprecision. |例句：He's a musician of sorts. 他算是个音乐家。|近义词：kind of, sort of, somewhat, vaguely |反义词：precisely, exactly


USAGE->

He is a writer of sorts. = 他算是个作家。
This is a celebration of sorts. = 这算是个庆祝活动。


一般过去时:
She had a job of sorts. = 她曾经有过一份工作，说不上是什么工作。
It was a party of sorts. = 那算是场派对。
