non-Spartan: /ˌnɒnˈspɑːrtən/ | adj. |非斯巴达式的；奢侈的；享乐的 | Not austere or self-denying; characterized by luxury or self-indulgence.  The word contrasts with the qualities associated with the ancient Spartans, known for their discipline, frugality, and military prowess. |例句：Their non-Spartan lifestyle included lavish parties and expensive vacations.  他们的非斯巴达式生活方式包括奢华的派对和昂贵的假期。


Their non-Spartan approach to training surprised the coach. 他们非斯巴达式的训练方法让教练大吃一惊。


近义词：luxurious, lavish, indulgent, self-indulgent, hedonistic, comfortable


反义词：Spartan, austere, frugal, ascetic, self-denying, disciplined


词源：The word is a combination of "non-" (meaning "not") and "Spartan," which derives from Sparta, an ancient Greek city-state known for its rigorous military training and austere lifestyle.  The adjective "Spartan" has come to signify a life characterized by simplicity, frugality, and self-discipline.
