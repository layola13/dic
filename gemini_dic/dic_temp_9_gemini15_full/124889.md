muddle-headed: /ˈmʌdlˌhedɪd/ | adj. | 糊涂的；混乱的；头脑不清醒的 | Confused and muddled in one's thinking; lacking clarity or coherence.  |  例句：He's a rather muddle-headed fellow; he can never find his keys. 他是个相当糊涂的家伙；他总是找不到他的钥匙。

例句：Her muddle-headed explanation only confused me further. 她混乱的解释只会让我更加困惑。


近义词：confused, bewildered, disoriented, addled, befuddled, jumbled

反义词：clear-headed, lucid, sharp, astute, insightful

词源:
The word "muddle-headed" is a compound word.  "Muddle" comes from the Middle English word "muddelen," meaning to make a mess or confuse.  "-headed" is a suffix indicating the location of the quality, in this case, the head or mind.  Therefore, "muddle-headed" literally means having a confused or messy mind.
