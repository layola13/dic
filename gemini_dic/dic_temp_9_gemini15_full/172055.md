puritans:/ˈpjʊərɪtənz/ | n. |清教徒 |Members of a group of English Protestants of the late 16th and 17th centuries who regarded the Reformation of the Church of England under Elizabeth as incomplete and sought to simplify and regulate religious practices.  They were known for their strict moral code and their belief in predestination. |The Puritans fled England to escape religious persecution and establish a colony in America based on their beliefs.  清教徒逃离英格兰以逃避宗教迫害，并在美国建立了一个基于他们信仰的殖民地。|Pilgrims, Separatists |Catholics, nonconformists |名词
