contradictory:/ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪktəri/| adj. |矛盾的，前后不一的|Inconsistent; self-contradictory; containing opposing ideas.  The witness's statements were contradictory, making it difficult to determine the truth.|近义词：inconsistent, conflicting, opposing |反义词：consistent, compatible, harmonious


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His statements are contradictory. = 他的陈述自相矛盾。
The two theories are contradictory. = 这两种理论是相互矛盾的。

一般过去时:
Her actions were contradictory to her words. = 她的行为与她所说的话相矛盾。
The evidence presented was contradictory. = 提出的证据是相互矛盾的。

现在完成时:
The new findings have contradicted the earlier research. = 新发现与早期的研究结果相矛盾。
His explanations have been contradictory throughout the investigation. = 在整个调查过程中，他的解释一直前后矛盾。

过去完成时:
The witness's testimony had contradicted the defendant's alibi. = 目击证人的证词与被告的不在场证明相矛盾。
The previous studies had presented contradictory results. = 此前的研究结果相互矛盾。

将来时:
The results of the experiment will likely contradict current theories. = 实验结果可能会与目前的理论相矛盾。
His upcoming book will contradict many established beliefs. = 他即将出版的书将与许多既定的信仰相冲突。

虚拟语气:
If his statements were not contradictory, we could reach a conclusion. = 如果他的陈述不矛盾，我们就能得出结论。
I wish the information wasn't so contradictory. = 我希望信息不要那么矛盾。

被动语态:
His claims were contradicted by new evidence. = 他的说法被新的证据驳斥了。
The theory has been contradicted by recent studies. = 该理论已被最近的研究推翻。

疑问句:
Are his arguments contradictory? = 他的论点是否矛盾？
Is there any contradictory evidence? = 有任何相互矛盾的证据吗？

否定句:
The facts are not contradictory. = 事实并不矛盾。
Their accounts were not contradictory. = 他们的说法并不矛盾。

internally contradictory = 内在矛盾的
