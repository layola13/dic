exertion:/ɪɡˈzɜːrʃən/ | n. |努力；费力；运用；发挥 | The act of making a great effort; strenuous effort.  It can also refer to the use or application of something, such as strength or skill. |例句：The exertion of climbing the mountain left him exhausted.  爬山费力让他筋疲力尽。

例句：The exertion of his influence secured the deal. 他运用影响力确保了这笔交易。


近义词：effort, strain, struggle, endeavor, application | 反义词：rest, idleness, inactivity


词源:  From Old French *exercition, from Latin exercitātiōn-, exercitātiō "exercise, practice, training," from exercitāre "to train, exercise," from exercitus "army, force," from ex- "out" + arcere "to ward off, enclose, confine."  The sense evolved from "training" to "strenuous effort."
