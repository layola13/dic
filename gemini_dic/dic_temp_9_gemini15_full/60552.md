elapse:/ɪˈlæps/| v.|过去；消逝；流逝|To pass (of time)|例句：Five years elapsed before he returned.  五年后他才回来。|例句：Several months elapsed before we heard from him again.  几个月后我们才又收到他的来信。|近义词：pass, go by, lapse|反义词：begin, start


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Time elapses quickly when you are having fun. = 当你玩得开心的时候，时间过得很快。
The clock elapses seconds, minutes and hours. = 时钟记录着秒、分和小时的流逝。


一般过去时:
Several years elapsed before they met again. = 几年后他们才再次见面。
A long time elapsed before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前过了很久。


现在完成时:
Many years have elapsed since I last saw her. = 自从我上次见到她以来，已经过去了很多年。
A considerable amount of time has elapsed since the accident. = 事故发生后已经过去了相当长的时间。


过去完成时:
A considerable amount of time had elapsed before the rescue team arrived. = 救援队到达之前已经过去相当长的时间了。
Many years had elapsed since the war ended. = 战争结束已经过去了很多年。


将来时:
Several more years will elapse before the project is completed. = 项目完成还需要几年时间。
I don't know how long will elapse before the train arrives. = 我不知道火车还要多久才能到。


虚拟语气:
If a longer time elapsed, things would be different. = 如果时间再长一些，情况就会不同。
If only a few more years had elapsed, she would have been ready. = 如果再过几年就好了，她那时就准备好了。


被动语态:
(The verb "elapse" is rarely used in the passive voice because it is intransitive.)  (动词“elapse”很少用于被动语态，因为它是不及物动词。)


疑问句:
How much time will elapse before the results are released? = 结果发布还需要多长时间？
How long did it elapse before you understood? = 你理解花了多长时间？


否定句:
Not much time elapsed before the meeting began. = 会议开始没过多久。
Little time elapsed before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前时间很短。
