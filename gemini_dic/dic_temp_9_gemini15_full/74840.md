frenzy:/ˈfrɛnzi/| n.|狂暴；狂乱；激动|A state of uncontrolled excitement or wild behavior.|The crowd erupted in a frenzy of excitement after the winning goal.  人群在获胜进球后爆发出一阵狂喜。|The dog went into a frenzy when it saw the cat. 这只狗看到猫后就发狂了。|狂热，狂喜|calm, peace|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
A frenzy of activity fills the office every Friday afternoon.  每周五下午办公室里都充斥着疯狂的活动。
His constant worries keep him in a state of frenzy. 他不断的担忧使他处于狂乱的状态。


一般过去时:
A frenzy of buying swept the stock market last week. 上周一股抢购热潮席卷了股市。
The news sent the city into a frenzy.  这个消息让这座城市陷入疯狂。


现在完成时:
The artist's sudden fame has caused a frenzy of interest in his work. 艺术家突然成名引起了人们对其作品的狂热兴趣。
This announcement has created a frenzy among investors. 这个公告在投资者中引起了恐慌。


过去完成时:
After the announcement, a frenzy had already gripped the city. 公告发布后，这座城市已经陷入恐慌。
Before the concert, a frenzy had overtaken the fans. 音乐会开始前，歌迷们已经陷入狂热。


将来时:
The release of the new phone will likely cause a frenzy. 新手机的发布可能会引起一阵狂热。
The expected announcement will likely create a frenzy in the market. 预计的公告可能会在市场上引起恐慌。


虚拟语气:
If the news were true, it would cause a frenzy. 如果这个消息是真的，它会引起恐慌。
If the game were to be cancelled, it would cause a frenzy of protest. 如果比赛取消，将会引发抗议狂潮。


被动语态:
The city was gripped by a frenzy after the earthquake. 地震后，这座城市被恐慌所笼罩。
The market was thrown into a frenzy by the unexpected news.  意外的消息使市场陷入恐慌。


疑问句:
What caused this sudden frenzy?  是什么引起了这种突然的狂乱？
Is this frenzy justified?  这种狂热有道理吗？


否定句:
There was no frenzy at the concert. 音乐会上没有狂热。
The announcement didn't cause a frenzy.  这个公告并没有引起恐慌。
