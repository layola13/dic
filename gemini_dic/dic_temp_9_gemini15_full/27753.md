cannabene:/ˌkænəˈbiːn/| n. |大麻素|A cannabinoid is a group of diverse chemical compounds produced by the Cannabis sativa plant (marijuana and hemp).  These compounds interact with the endocannabinoid system (ECS) in the human body, which plays a role in regulating various physiological processes.  Cannabinoids can have a variety of effects, depending on the specific compound and the dosage. Some cannabinoids, like THC, have psychoactive effects, while others, like CBD, are non-psychoactive.  The study of cannabinoids and their effects is an ongoing field of research.|例句：Research on the therapeutic potential of cannabinoids is expanding rapidly.  大麻素的治疗潜力研究正在迅速发展。|近义词：cannabinoid (more common and scientifically preferred term)|反义词：无|名词
