excusably:/ɪkˈskjuːzəbli/ | adj. | 可原谅地；情有可原地 |  Adjective meaning capable of being excused; pardonable. It describes an action or behavior that is understandable and forgivable, given the circumstances. |


例句：

His lateness was excusably due to the heavy traffic. = 他的迟到是因为交通拥堵，情有可原。

Her outburst was excusably given the pressure she was under. = 考虑到她承受的压力，她的爆发是可以原谅的。


近义词：pardonably, forgivably, understandably


反义词：unforgivably, inexcusably


词源:

From "excuse" + "-ably".  "Excuse" originates from Old French "escuse," ultimately from Latin "excusare," meaning "to excuse," from "ex-" (out) + "causare" (to allege, plead).  The suffix "-ably" indicates capability or possibility.
