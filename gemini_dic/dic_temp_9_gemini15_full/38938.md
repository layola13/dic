confidency:/ˈkɒnfɪdənsi/| n. |自信，信任，秘密|Confidence is a feeling of self-assurance and belief in one's own abilities or qualities.  It can also refer to trust in another person or a secret shared between people. |例句：He has great confidence in his abilities. 他对自己的能力很有信心。|例句：She confided her secret to her best friend. 她向她最好的朋友吐露了她的秘密。|近义词：self-assurance, assurance, trust, faith, belief|反义词：doubt, uncertainty, distrust, suspicion

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He shows great confidence in his work. = 他在工作中表现出极大的信心。
Her confidence in her team is unwavering. = 她对团队的信心坚定不移。


一般过去时:
She had complete confidence in her doctor's judgment. = 她完全相信她医生的判断。
His confidence wavered after the setback. = 挫折之后，他的信心动摇了。


现在完成时:
I have gained more confidence since starting my new job. = 自从开始我的新工作以来，我变得更有信心了。
They have placed their confidence in the wrong person. = 他们把信任放在了错误的人身上。


过去完成时:
She had already lost confidence in her ability to succeed. = 她已经失去了成功的信心。
He had never before felt such complete confidence. = 他以前从未有过如此完全的信心。


将来时:
She will gain confidence as she progresses. = 随着她的进步，她会变得更有信心。
I believe he will regain his confidence eventually. = 我相信他最终会恢复信心。


虚拟语气:
If I had more confidence, I would try harder. = 如果我更有信心，我会更加努力。
I wish I had the confidence to speak up. = 我希望我有勇气说出来。


被动语态:
His confidence was shaken by the criticism. = 他的信心被批评动摇了。
Their confidence was betrayed by his actions. = 他的行为辜负了他们的信任。


疑问句:
Do you have confidence in your abilities? = 你对自己有信心吗？
Does she have any confidence in her partner? = 她对她的伙伴有信心吗？


否定句:
He lacks confidence in his own judgment. = 他缺乏对自身判断的信心。
She doesn't have the confidence to ask for a raise. = 她没有勇气要求加薪。
