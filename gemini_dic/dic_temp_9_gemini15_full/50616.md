deregulatory:/ˌdiːˈreɡjʊlətɔːri/| adj. |放松管制的；反规制的 |Removing or reducing the regulation of something, especially in the economy.|The government implemented deregulatory policies to stimulate economic growth.  政府实施放松管制的政策以刺激经济增长。|近义词：liberalizing, free-market, laissez-faire |反义词：regulatory, controlling, restrictive


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The government pursues a deregulatory approach to the energy sector. = 政府对能源部门采取放松管制的方法。
Deregulatory policies often lead to increased competition. = 放松管制的政策通常会导致竞争加剧。


一般过去时:
The previous administration adopted a strongly deregulatory stance. = 上届政府采取了强烈的放松管制立场。
The deregulation of the airline industry led to lower fares. = 航空业的放松管制导致票价下降。


现在完成时:
Recent years have seen a significant increase in deregulatory activities. = 近年来，放松管制活动显著增加。
The company has benefited greatly from the deregulatory environment. = 这家公司从放松管制的环境中获益匪浅。


过去完成时:

They had not anticipated the negative consequences of their deregulatory measures. = 他们没有预料到其放松管制措施的负面后果。


将来时:
The administration will likely continue its deregulatory agenda. = 政府可能会继续其放松管制计划。
Further deregulation will probably lead to job losses in some sectors. = 进一步放松管制可能会导致某些行业的失业。


虚拟语气:
If they had implemented more deregulatory policies, the economy might have grown faster. = 如果他们实施了更多放松管制的政策，经济增长可能会更快。
