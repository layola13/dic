oxbird:/ˈɒksbɜːrd/ | n. |牛鸟|A mythical bird, sometimes depicted as having the head of an ox and the body of a bird.  The term is not widely used and lacks a standardized definition; its meaning can vary depending on the context.  It's often found in folklore and fantasy literature. |例句：In the ancient legend, a fearsome oxbird guarded the sacred grove.  古代传说中，一只可怕的牛鸟守护着圣林。|近义词：griffin(only if described with a similar composite structure), chimera |反义词：无
