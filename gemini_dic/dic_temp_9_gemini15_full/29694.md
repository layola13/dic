cathode:/ˈkæθoʊd/| n. |阴极|The cathode is the negatively charged electrode in an electrochemical cell or vacuum tube. It attracts positively charged ions or electrons.  The term is derived from the Greek word "kathodos," meaning "descent" or "way down." This refers to the path of electrons moving through a circuit. |Example: In a battery, the cathode is the electrode where reduction occurs. 在电池中，阴极是发生还原反应的电极。

The cathode in this vacuum tube emits electrons when heated.  这个真空管中的阴极在加热时会发射电子。|近义词：negative electrode, electron emitter |反义词：anode
