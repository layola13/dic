masonries:/ˈmeɪsənriz/| n. |石工制品；石砌建筑物；砌体工程|The term "masonries" refers to structures or works constructed of stone, brick, or similar materials, often involving skilled craftsmanship.  It can encompass a range of projects, from walls and buildings to more intricate decorative elements.  The plural form emphasizes the multiplicity of such structures or the collective nature of the work.|例句：The ancient ruins showcased impressive masonries, testament to the builders' skill.  古代遗迹展示了令人印象深刻的石砌建筑，证明了建造者的技艺。


The intricate masonries of the cathedral took centuries to complete. 大教堂复杂的石工制品耗费了几个世纪才完成。|近义词：stonework, brickwork, masonry|反义词：woodwork, metalwork

词源:

The word "masonry" comes from the Old French "maçonerie," which derives from "maçon," meaning "mason."  The suffix "-ry" indicates a collective or craft.  Therefore, "masonries" is the plural form, referring to multiple instances of masonry work or structures.
