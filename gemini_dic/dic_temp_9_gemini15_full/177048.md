reannounce:/ˌriːəˈnaʊns/| vt.|重新宣布；再次宣布|To announce something again, often with more emphasis or to a wider audience.  The company reannounced its commitment to sustainability.|近义词：announce again, reiterate, restate|反义词：withdraw, retract


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company reannounces its new product line every quarter. = 公司每个季度都会重新发布其新的产品线。
They reannounce the policy changes at every staff meeting. = 他们在每次员工会议上都会重新宣布政策变更。

一般过去时:
The mayor reannounced the city's plans for the new park. = 市长再次宣布了城市新建公园的计划。
She reannounced her candidacy after a brief hiatus. = 在短暂休整后，她再次宣布竞选。


现在完成时:
The government has reannounced its commitment to reducing carbon emissions. = 政府已经再次承诺减少碳排放。
He has reannounced his support for the bill despite the opposition. = 尽管遭到反对，他仍然再次表示支持该法案。


过去完成时:
They had reannounced the concert date after the venue change. = 在场地变更后，他们已经重新宣布了音乐会的日期。
The school had reannounced the closure due to the severe weather. = 由于恶劣天气，学校已经再次宣布关闭。


将来时:
The president will reannounce his economic policies next week. = 总统下周将再次宣布其经济政策。
They will reannounce the winner of the competition after the verification process. = 他们将在核实过程结束后再次宣布比赛获胜者。


虚拟语气:
If they reannounced the sale, I would definitely buy it. = 如果他们再次宣布促销活动，我一定会购买。
I wish they had reannounced the deadline for the project. = 我希望他们已经重新宣布了项目的截止日期。


被动语态:
The new guidelines were reannounced to all employees. = 新指南已向所有员工重新宣布。
The decision to postpone the event was reannounced via email. = 推迟活动的决定已通过电子邮件再次宣布。


疑问句:
Will the company reannounce its plans for expansion? = 公司会重新宣布其扩张计划吗？
Have they reannounced the opening date of the museum? = 他们是否已经重新宣布了博物馆的开馆日期？


否定句:
The company did not reannounce the product recall. = 公司没有重新宣布产品召回。
They haven't reannounced the changes to the curriculum. = 他们还没有重新宣布课程的变更。
