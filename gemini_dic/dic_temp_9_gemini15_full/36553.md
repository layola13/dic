collapse:/kəˈlæps/ | /ˈkɒlæps/| v. n.|倒塌；崩溃；瓦解；衰竭|To fall down suddenly and completely; to break down or fail suddenly.|The building collapsed after the earthquake. 地震后，大楼倒塌了。|The negotiations collapsed. 谈判破裂了。|fall, crumble, cave in, give way|stand, endure, resist, thrive|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bridge collapses under heavy traffic.  桥梁在车流量大的情况下会坍塌。
He collapses under pressure. 他在压力下崩溃了。


一般过去时:
The tent collapsed in the storm. 帐篷在暴风雨中倒塌了。
The company collapsed due to mismanagement. 公司由于管理不善而倒闭了。


现在完成时:
The government has collapsed after the election.  选举后政府垮台了。
The stock market has collapsed. 股市崩盘了。


过去完成时:
The bridge had collapsed before the rescue team arrived.  救援队到达之前，桥梁已经坍塌了。
The talks had collapsed before they could reach an agreement.  谈判在他们达成协议之前就破裂了。


将来时:
The old house will collapse soon if it's not repaired.  如果这栋旧房子不维修，很快就会倒塌。
The economy is likely to collapse if inflation continues. 如果通货膨胀持续下去，经济很可能会崩溃。


虚拟语气:
If the dam collapsed, the town would be flooded. 如果水坝垮塌，城镇就会被淹没。
I wish the negotiations hadn't collapsed. 我真希望谈判没有破裂。


被动语态:
The building was collapsed by the explosion. 建筑物被爆炸摧毁了。
The deal was collapsed due to lack of funding.  由于缺乏资金，交易失败了。


疑问句:
Did the roof collapse? 屋顶塌了吗？
Has the system collapsed? 系统崩溃了吗？


否定句:
The building did not collapse. 建筑物没有倒塌。
The negotiations did not collapse. 谈判没有破裂。
