pigeonholing: /ˈpɪdʒənˌhoʊlɪŋ/ | v. | 把…归入类别；对…按类别处理；限制；束缚 | The act of assigning or confining someone or something to a particular category, often in a way that is too simplistic or restrictive.  |例句：He felt pigeonholed by his previous job title, limiting his career progression. 他觉得以前的工作头衔限制了他的职业发展。

例句：The company's pigeonholing of its employees based solely on age is discriminatory.  这家公司仅仅根据年龄对员工进行分类的做法是歧视性的。


近义词：categorizing, classifying, typecasting, stereotyping | 反义词：individualizing, personalizing


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They pigeonhole new employees based on their resumes. = 他们根据简历对新员工进行分类。
He pigeonholes every problem into a pre-defined solution. = 他把每个问题都归入预先定义的解决方案中。


一般过去时:
The professor pigeonholed the student's research into a specific field. = 教授将学生的科研成果归入一个特定的领域。
She felt pigeonholed by the media's portrayal of her. = 她感觉媒体对她的刻画让她受到了限制。


现在完成时:
They have pigeonholed her as just a "housewife." = 他们把她仅仅归类为“家庭主妇”。
The artist has been pigeonholed throughout his career. = 这位艺术家在他的整个职业生涯中都被归类了。


过去完成时:
The critics had already pigeonholed him as a romantic novelist. = 批评家们已经把他归类为浪漫小说家了。
Before the interview, she had already been pigeonholed as unqualified. = 面试前，她已经被认定为不合格了。


将来时:
The company will pigeonhole applicants based on their skills. = 公司将根据申请人的技能对他们进行分类。
I fear they will pigeonhole my ideas as unrealistic. = 我担心他们会认为我的想法不切实际。


虚拟语气:
If they didn't pigeonhole people so readily, there'd be more innovation. = 如果他们不那么轻易地对人进行分类，就会有更多的创新。
I wish they hadn't pigeonholed me as just a "salesperson". = 我希望他们没有把我仅仅归类为“销售人员”。


被动语态:
New ideas are often pigeonholed by established organizations. = 新想法常常被已有的组织归类。
He felt that he was being pigeonholed into a specific role. = 他感觉自己被归入一个特定的角色。


疑问句:

否定句:
We don't pigeonhole our students based on their backgrounds. = 我们不会根据学生的背景对他们进行分类。
The manager doesn't want to pigeonhole his team members. = 经理不想对他的团队成员进行分类。
