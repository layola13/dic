preunderstanding:/ˌpriːˌʌndərˈstændɪŋ/| n. |预先理解；预先领会；先有之见 |A preunderstanding is a prior understanding or preconceived notion that influences one's interpretation of new information.  It's a framework of knowledge and assumptions that shapes how someone approaches a new situation or concept.  This can be beneficial, providing a context for understanding, or detrimental, leading to biases and misinterpretations. |The professor's preunderstanding of the subject matter allowed him to quickly grasp the student's research. |prior understanding, preconception, prior knowledge|misunderstanding, ignorance|名词
