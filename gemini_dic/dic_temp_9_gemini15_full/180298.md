refrigerating: /rɪˈfrɪdʒəreɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 冷藏；制冷 |  The process of making or keeping something cold, typically food, to prevent spoilage.  |例句：We need to refrigerate the milk to keep it fresh. 我们需要把牛奶冷藏起来以保持新鲜。

例句：The restaurant uses advanced refrigerating techniques to maintain food quality. 这家餐馆采用先进的冷藏技术来保持食品质量。


近义词：chilling, cooling, preserving


反义词：heating, warming


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We refrigerate the leftovers every night. = 我们每晚都把剩菜冷藏起来。
They refrigerate the vaccines to maintain potency. = 他们冷藏疫苗以保持效力。


一般过去时:

词源:
The word "refrigerating" is derived from the Latin word "refrigerare," meaning "to cool" or "to refresh."  "Refrigerare" is composed of "re-" (again) and "frigere" (to be cold).  The word evolved through Middle English and Old French, eventually leading to the modern English "refrigerating."
