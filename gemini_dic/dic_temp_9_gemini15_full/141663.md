option:/ˈɒpʃən/ /ˈɑːpʃən/| n. |选择；选项；买卖权利|A choice between two or more possibilities.  An option gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a specified price on or before a specified date.|例句：We have several options for dinner tonight.  今晚我们有几种晚餐选择。|例句：The company offered him a stock option. 公司给他提供了股票期权。|近义词：choice, alternative, selection|反义词：necessity, requirement|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
What option do you prefer? = 你更喜欢哪个选项？
Which option is the best? = 哪个选项最好？

一般过去时:
What option did you choose? = 你选择了哪个选项？
What option did they offer you? = 他们给你提供了什么选择？

现在完成时:
What option have you selected? = 你选择了哪个选项？
What options have they considered? = 他们考虑了哪些选项？

过去完成时:
What option had you considered before making the decision? = 在做决定之前你考虑过哪个选项？
What option had she chosen before the deadline? = 在截止日期之前她选择了哪个选项？

将来时:
What option will you choose tomorrow? = 你明天会选择哪个选项？
What option will be available next week? = 下周会有什么选择？

虚拟语气:
If I had known about this option, I would have chosen it. = 如果我知道这个选项，我就会选择它。
I wish I had considered more options. = 我希望我考虑过更多选项。

被动语态:
What option was chosen? = 选择了什么选项？
Which option is being considered? = 正在考虑哪个选项？

疑问句:
What are the options? = 选项是什么？
What option is best suited for me? = 哪种选项最适合我？

否定句:
There is no other option. = 没有其他选择。
I don't have any other option. = 我没有其他选择。


PHRASE->
