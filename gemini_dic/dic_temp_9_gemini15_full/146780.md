overidentifications:/ˌoʊvərˌaɪdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/| n. |过度识别；过度认同；过度认定|The act of identifying or associating something with a particular category or group to an excessive degree, often leading to inaccurate or biased conclusions.  This is common in social science research where pre-conceived notions can lead to misinterpretations of data.  For example, seeing patterns where none exist, or forcing data to fit a pre-existing theory. |Example:  The researcher's overidentifications with the participants led to a biased interpretation of the results.  研究人员对参与者的过度认同导致了对结果的偏见解读。|近义词：misidentification, overinterpretation, bias|反义词：accurate identification, objective analysis|名词
