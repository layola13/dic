drew:/druː/ | v. | 画；拉；拖；吸引 | To make a picture with a pen, pencil, or crayon; to pull or drag something; to attract or fascinate. |例句：She drew a beautiful picture of a flower. 她画了一幅美丽的鲜花图画。|例句：The horse drew the cart. 马拉车。|近义词：sketch, pull, attract |反义词：push, repel


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He draws pictures every day. = 他每天都画画。
She draws inspiration from nature. = 她从大自然中汲取灵感。

一般过去时:
I drew a map of the city. = 我画了一张城市地图。
He drew the sword from its scabbard. = 他从剑鞘里拔出剑。


现在完成时:
She has drawn several portraits this year. = 她今年已经画了几幅肖像画。
The artist has drawn inspiration from various cultures. =这位艺术家从各种文化中汲取灵感。

过去完成时:
They had drawn their conclusions before the evidence was presented. = 证据提交之前，他们就已经得出了结论。
The children had drawn pictures for their parents. =孩子们已经为父母画了画。

将来时:
I will draw a plan for our new house. = 我将为我们的新房子画一个平面图。
She will draw the winning lottery ticket.  = 她将抽到中奖彩票。

虚拟语气:
If I could draw, I would illustrate my own books. = 如果我会画画，我就会为自己的书配图。
I wish I had drawn better in my youth. = 我希望我年轻时画得更好。

被动语态:
The winning ticket was drawn by a child. = 中奖彩票是由一个孩子抽到的。
The conclusion was drawn from the data analysis. = 结论是从数据分析中得出的。

疑问句:
Did you draw that picture yourself? = 那幅画是你自己画的吗？
What did you draw on the blackboard? = 你在黑板上画了什么？

否定句:
I didn't draw that picture. = 我没有画那幅画。
He didn't draw any conclusions from the meeting. = 他从会议中没有得出任何结论。
