overgrading: /ˌoʊvərˈɡreɪdɪŋ/ | n. | 打高分；给过高的分数；评级过高 | The act of assigning a higher grade to a student's work than it deserves.  This can be intentional or unintentional, and often stems from factors like leniency, pressure to raise class averages, or insufficient grading standards. | Example: The teacher was accused of overgrading, consistently giving students higher marks than their work warranted.  | 近义词: inflated grading, generous grading | 反义词: undergrading, strict grading
