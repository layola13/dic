irreparably: /ɪˈrepərəbli/ | adj. | 不可挽回地；无法弥补地 | Incapable of being repaired or rectified; beyond repair.  | The damage to the antique vase was irreparable.  古董花瓶的损坏是无法弥补的。|  irremediable, irretrievable | reparable, fixable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The damage is irreparable. = 损坏是不可挽回的。
This mistake irreparably harms our reputation. = 这个错误不可挽回地损害了我们的声誉。


一般过去时:
The relationship was irreparably damaged. = 这段关系受到了不可挽回的损害。
The accident irreparably harmed his health. = 这场事故严重损害了他的健康，无法恢复。


现在完成时:
The situation has been irreparably compromised. = 局势已经被不可挽回地危及。
We have irreparably damaged the ecosystem. = 我们已经对生态系统造成了不可挽回的破坏。


过去完成时:
By then, the damage had been irreparably done. = 到那时，损害已经无法弥补了。
They had irreparably broken the trust between them. = 他们之间已经彻底丧失了信任。


将来时:
This decision will irreparably harm the company. = 这个决定将不可挽回地损害公司。
The ongoing conflict will irreparably damage the environment. = 持续的冲突将不可挽回地破坏环境。


虚拟语气:
If we had acted sooner, the damage might not have been irreparable. = 如果我们早点行动，损害可能不会无法弥补。
I wish the damage wasn’t irreparable. = 我希望损坏不是无法弥补的。


被动语态:
The reputation of the company was irreparably damaged by the scandal. = 公司的声誉被丑闻不可挽回地损害了。
The ecosystem is being irreparably harmed by pollution. = 生态系统正遭受污染的不可挽回的破坏。


疑问句:
Is the damage irreparable? = 损坏是不可挽回的吗？
Is the situation irreparably damaged? = 情况是否已经无法挽回？


否定句:
The damage was not irreparable; it could be fixed. = 损坏并非不可挽回；它可以修复。
The problem is not irreparably damaged; we can still solve it. = 问题并非不可挽回；我们仍然可以解决它。
