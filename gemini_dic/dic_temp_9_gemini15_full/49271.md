deliberately:/dɪˈlɪbərətli/ | adv. |故意地，蓄意地 | done in a way that is planned or intended; on purpose | He deliberately ignored her. 他故意不理她。 | intentionally, purposely, consciously | accidentally, unintentionally, unconsciously | 副词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She deliberately avoids him. = 她故意避开他。
They deliberately mislead the public. = 他们故意误导公众。


一般过去时:
He deliberately broke the vase. = 他故意打碎了花瓶。
She deliberately chose to ignore the warning signs. = 她故意选择忽略警告标志。


现在完成时:
I have deliberately avoided contact with him. = 我故意避免和他联系。
They have deliberately concealed the truth. = 他们故意隐瞒了真相。


过去完成时:
She had deliberately hidden the letter. = 她故意藏起了那封信。
He had deliberately planted the evidence. = 他故意栽赃了证据。


将来时:
I will deliberately choose a different route. = 我将故意选择一条不同的路线。
They will deliberately ignore my advice. = 他们将故意无视我的建议。


虚拟语气:
If I had known, I would have deliberately avoided the situation. = 如果我知道的话，我会故意避免这种情况。
I wish I had deliberately chosen a different path. = 我希望我当时故意选择了另一条路。


被动语态:
The damage was deliberately inflicted. = 损害是故意造成的。
The meeting was deliberately scheduled for a late hour. = 会议故意安排在深夜。


疑问句:
Did she deliberately cause the accident? = 她是不是故意造成事故的？
Did they deliberately exclude him from the project? = 他们是不是故意将他排除在项目之外？


否定句:
He did not deliberately hurt her feelings. = 他并非故意伤害她的感情。
She did not deliberately lie to the police. = 她没有故意对警方说谎。
