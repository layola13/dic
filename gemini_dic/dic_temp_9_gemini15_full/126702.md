named:/neɪmd/ | adj. | 被命名的，有名的 |  Describes something that has been given a name.  It can also imply fame or recognition. | Example:  The newly named street is very quiet.  这条新命名的街道非常安静。 |近义词：called, designated, titled | 反义词：unnamed, anonymous | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dog is named Max. = 这只狗名叫麦克斯。
The project is named "Project X". = 这个项目名为“X计划”。

一般过去时:
The baby was named Lily. = 这个婴儿名叫莉莉。
The company was named after its founder. = 这家公司是以其创始人命名的。

现在完成时:
The book has been named a bestseller. = 这本书已被评为畅销书。
The award has been named after a famous scientist. = 该奖项是以一位著名科学家的名字命名的。

过去完成时:
The building had been named before the ceremony. = 这座建筑在仪式前就已经被命名了。
The street had been named after a historical figure. = 这条街是以一个历史人物的名字命名的。

将来时:
The new product will be named next week. = 新产品将于下周命名。
The stadium will be named after a local hero. = 体育场将以当地英雄的名字命名。

虚拟语气:
If the baby were a boy, he would be named after his grandfather. = 如果婴儿是男孩，他将以他祖父的名字命名。
I wish the painting had been named something more descriptive. = 我希望这幅画的名字更具描述性一些。

被动语态:
The bridge was named after a famous engineer. = 这座桥是以一位著名工程师的名字命名的。
The street is being named tomorrow. = 这条街明天将被命名。

疑问句:
What is the bird named? = 这只鸟叫什么名字？
What was the play named? = 这出戏叫什么名字？

否定句:
The project is not yet named. = 该项目尚未命名。
The child was not named after either parent. = 这孩子没有以任何一个父母的名字命名。


PHRASE->
well-named: 名副其实的
badly named: 名不副实的
newly named: 新命名的
highly named: 名声很高的
commonly named: 普遍被称作的
