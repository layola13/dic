non-belligerency: /ˌnɒn bɪˈlɪdʒərənsi/ | n. | 非交战状态；不参战；和平状态 | The state of not being at war; a policy of not engaging in war.  It often refers to a nation's official stance of neutrality during a conflict. | Example:  The country declared its non-belligerency in the ongoing conflict.  这个国家宣布在持续的冲突中保持中立。


近义词: neutrality, peace, non-combat, pacificism | 反义词: belligerency, warfare, hostility

词源:  The word "non-belligerency" is a compound word.  "Non-" is a prefix meaning "not," "belligerency" comes from "belligerent,"  ultimately deriving from the Latin word "belliger," meaning "waging war."  Therefore, non-belligerency literally means "not waging war" or "not being at war".
