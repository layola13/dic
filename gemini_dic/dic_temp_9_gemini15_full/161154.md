/ˌpoʊstˈfrækʃərz/ | n. |骨折后遗症，骨折后并发症 | Postfractures refers to the complications or sequelae that can occur after a bone fracture has healed.  These can include persistent pain, stiffness, limited range of motion, malunion (improper healing), nonunion (failure to heal), avascular necrosis (bone death due to lack of blood supply), and complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS).  |  The patient experienced significant postfractures pain despite the fracture healing properly. |  Complications, sequelae, after-effects |  (No direct antonym;  the antonym would be the absence of complications after a fracture heals) | n.
