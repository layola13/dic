misappraises:/ˌmɪsæˈpreɪz/| vt.|误评；错误估价|To judge or assess incorrectly or unfairly.  It implies a misunderstanding or a lack of proper evaluation, often leading to an inaccurate conclusion.|例句：The appraiser misappraised the antique chair, significantly undervaluing its worth.  估价师误判了那把古董椅的价值，大大低估了它的价值。

The company misappraised the market demand, leading to significant losses. 公司误判了市场需求，导致了巨大的损失。|近义词：misjudge, undervalue, overvalue, miscalculate|反义词：accurately assess, correctly evaluate
