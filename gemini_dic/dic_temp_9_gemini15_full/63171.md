entail:/ɪnˈteɪl/ | vt. | 意味；包含；需要；使…成为必要 | To involve (something) as a necessary consequence.  To necessitate or require (something). |例句：The job entails a lot of hard work. 这份工作需要付出很多努力。
The project will entail significant costs.  这个项目将需要大量的资金。

近义词：involve, necessitate, require, imply
反义词：exclude, preclude

USAGE->
一般现在时:
This decision entails certain risks. = 这个决定包含一定的风险。
The job entails long hours. = 这份工作需要长时间工作。

一般过去时:
The war entailed great suffering. = 那场战争带来了巨大的痛苦。
The renovation entailed unexpected expenses. = 装修带来了意想不到的费用。

现在完成时:
The project has entailed more work than we anticipated. = 这个项目比我们预期的花费了更多精力。
The changes have entailed significant adjustments. = 这些变化需要做出重大的调整。

过去完成时:
The decision had entailed considerable debate. =  这个决定之前经历了相当多的争论。
The accident had entailed a lengthy investigation. = 事故之后需要进行长时间的调查。


将来时:
The new policy will entail changes to our procedures. = 新政策将需要改变我们的程序。
The expansion will entail hiring more staff. = 扩张将需要招聘更多员工。


虚拟语气:
If the plan entailed too much risk, we would abandon it. = 如果这个计划风险太大，我们将放弃它。
I wish the project hadn't entailed so much paperwork. = 我希望这个项目不需要这么多文书工作。


被动语态:
Many unforeseen difficulties were entailed by the project. =  这个项目带来了许多无法预料的困难。
Significant costs are entailed in the development of the new product. = 新产品的开发需要花费大量的成本。


疑问句:
Does this decision entail any legal consequences? = 这个决定会带来任何法律后果吗？
What does this proposal entail? = 这个提议包含什么？


否定句:
The plan does not entail any major changes. = 这个计划不需要任何重大改变。
The job description doesn't entail working weekends. = 工作描述中不需要周末工作。


PHRASE->
entail upon:  强加于
entail in: 包含在…之中
entail with:  伴随…
entail oneself in: 积极参与
entailment of: …的必然结果
