precontract:/ˈpriːkɒntrækt/| n. |预备合同；初步协议|A precontract is an agreement made before a formal contract is signed, outlining the key terms and conditions that will be included in the final contract.  It's often used to establish a framework for negotiations and ensure both parties are on the same page before committing to a legally binding agreement. |Example:  The precontract for the property purchase detailed the price, deposit, and closing date.  预购房产的预备合同详细说明了价格、押金和成交日期。|近义词： preliminary agreement, memorandum of understanding, letter of intent |反义词：final contract, binding agreement |名词
