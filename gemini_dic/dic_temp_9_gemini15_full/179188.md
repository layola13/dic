redisbursing:/ˌriːˈdɪsbɜːrsɪŋ/| v.| 再分配，重新支付 | The act of disbursing funds again, often after an initial disbursement has been made.  This typically involves re-allocating resources or making further payments related to a previous transaction. | Example: The company is redisbursing funds to cover unexpected expenses. 公司正在重新分配资金以支付意外开支。|近义词：redistributing, reallocating |反义词：collecting, accumulating |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They redisburse the funds annually. = 他们每年重新分配资金。
The government redisburses the budget based on new priorities. = 政府根据新的优先事项重新分配预算。


一般过去时:
The bank redisbursed the mistakenly withdrawn funds. = 银行重新支付了被错误提取的资金。
They redisbursed the money to the correct recipients. = 他们将钱重新支付给了正确的收款人。


现在完成时:
The company has redisbursed profits to shareholders. = 公司已将利润重新分配给股东。
The organization has redisbursed its donations to support new projects. = 该组织已重新分配其捐款以支持新项目。


过去完成时:
After the audit, they had redisbursed the misallocated funds. = 审计之后，他们重新分配了错配的资金。
Before the project started, they had redisbursed the initial investment. = 项目开始之前，他们已经重新分配了最初的投资。


将来时:

The company will redisburse the bonus to its employees next month. = 公司下个月将把奖金重新分配给员工。


虚拟语气:
