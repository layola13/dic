cross-cousin: /ˈkrɒs kʌzən/ | n. |表兄弟姐妹（指父母的异性手足的子女） | A cross-cousin is a cousin who is the child of your parent's sibling of the opposite sex.  For example, your mother's brother's child is your cross-cousin, as is your father's sister's child.  This term is often used in anthropological studies of kinship systems. |  My mother's brother's daughter is my cross-cousin.  我的母亲的兄弟的女儿是我的表妹。

My father's sister's son is my cross-cousin. 我的父亲的姐妹的儿子是我的表哥。 | cousin, relative |  no direct antonym.
