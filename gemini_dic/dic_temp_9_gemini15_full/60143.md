词源:

The word "effectualness" comes from the adjective "effectual" + the suffix "-ness".  "Effectual" itself originates from the Old French "effectif," meaning "effective," which further derives from the Latin "effectus," the past participle of "efficere," meaning "to bring about, accomplish."  The "-ness" suffix is a common English suffix forming nouns of quality, state, or condition.
