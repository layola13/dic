good-natured: /ˈɡʊdˈneɪtʃərd/ | adj. | 温和的；善良的；好脾气的 | Having or showing a pleasant and kindly disposition.  A good-natured person is friendly, cheerful, and tolerant. They are generally easy to get along with and rarely get angry or upset. |


例句：

He has a good-natured smile that always brightens the room. = 他有一张温和的笑容，总是能照亮房间。

She's a good-natured soul, always willing to lend a hand. = 她心地善良，总是乐于助人。


近义词：kind, amiable, affable, genial, pleasant

反义词：ill-tempered, bad-tempered, grumpy, surly, irritable


词源：

The word "good-natured" is a compound adjective.  "Good" is an Old English word meaning "good, virtuous, suitable." "Natured" comes from the Old French "nature," ultimately from the Latin "natura," meaning "nature, kind, sort."  Therefore, "good-natured" literally means "having a good nature."
