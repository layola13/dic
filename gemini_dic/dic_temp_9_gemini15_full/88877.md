hoity-toity:/ˈhɔɪti ˈtoɪti/ | adj. | 傲慢的；自以为是的；装腔作势的 |  Characterized by or displaying affected elegance, superiority, or disdain; snobbish.  It suggests an air of affected refinement and a condescending attitude towards others.  | 例句：She acted all hoity-toity when she found out her new neighbor drove a used car. 她发现她的新邻居开的是旧车后，就摆出一副盛气凌人的样子。

She gave them a hoity-toity look and turned away. 她给了他们一个傲慢不屑的眼神，然后转身离开了。

| 近义词：snobbish, pretentious, supercilious, uppity, stuck-up | 反义词：humble, modest, unassuming, down-to-earth | 形容词


词源:

The origin of "hoity-toity" is uncertain, but it's believed to be of imitative origin,  likely arising from a combination of sounds suggesting affected speech or mannerisms. The exact etymology remains elusive, with no definitive tracing back to earlier words or phrases.  The term emerged in the 19th century and quickly gained popularity as a descriptive adjective.  Its structure, a repetition of similar-sounding words, contributes to its playful, yet critical, nature.
