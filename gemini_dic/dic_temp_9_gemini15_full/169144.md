prokaryotic:/ˌproʊkæriˈɒtɪk/| adj.|原核的，原核生物的| Relating to or characteristic of prokaryotes, organisms whose cells lack a defined nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles.  Prokaryotic cells are typically smaller and simpler than eukaryotic cells. | Example: Bacteria are prokaryotic organisms.  细菌是原核生物。|近义词：prokaryal|反义词：eukaryotic


USAGE->
This word is an adjective, so it doesn't have verb conjugations like "hello".  It describes nouns.  Here are examples using it in different sentence structures:

肯定句:
The prokaryotic cell structure is simpler than the eukaryotic one. = 原核细胞结构比真核细胞结构简单。
This bacterium is a prokaryotic organism. = 这种细菌是原核生物。


否定句:
This organism is not prokaryotic; it's eukaryotic. = 这个生物不是原核生物；它是真核生物。
The cells are not prokaryotic in nature. = 这些细胞的本质不是原核的。


疑问句:
Is this organism prokaryotic or eukaryotic? = 这个生物是原核生物还是真核生物？
Are these cells prokaryotic? = 这些细胞是原核细胞吗？
