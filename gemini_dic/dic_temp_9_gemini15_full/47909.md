decentralist: /ˌdiːˈsentrəlɪst/ | adj., n. | 分权主义者；地方分权主义者 | An advocate or supporter of decentralization; a person who believes that power should be distributed among many people or groups rather than concentrated in the hands of a few.  | 例句：Many decentralist thinkers believe that the concentration of power in a single government is inherently dangerous.  许多分权主义思想家认为权力集中在单一政府手中本质上是危险的。


The decentralist movement is gaining traction as people seek more autonomy. 分权运动正在兴起，因为人们寻求更大的自主权。| 近义词：federalist (in some contexts), autonomist | 反义词：centralist, authoritarian

词源:
The word "decentralist" is derived from "decentralize" + "-ist".  "Decentralize" combines the prefix "de-" (meaning "away from" or "reverse of") with "centralize".  "Centralize" comes from "central" (meaning "of or relating to a center") + "-ize" (a suffix forming verbs indicating to make or become something).  Therefore, "decentralist" ultimately traces back to the concept of moving away from a central point of power or authority.
