nonneutrally:/ˌnɑːnˈnjuːtrəli/| adv. |非中立地，偏向地|  Describes an action or statement that is not impartial or objective, showing favoritism or bias.  It implies a lack of neutrality. | Example: The article reported the events nonneutrally, clearly favoring one side.  这篇文章非中立地报道了事件，明显偏向一方。| 近义词：partially, biasedly, unfairly, subjectively | 反义词：neutrally, impartially, objectively | 副词


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and therefore doesn't have tense conjugations like verbs.  Its usage is primarily to modify verbs or entire clauses.

Example 1 (modifying a verb):  The journalist wrote nonneutrally about the controversial issue.  记者非中立地撰写了关于这个有争议的话题的文章。

Example 2 (modifying a clause):  The judge ruled nonneutrally, which led to widespread criticism.  法官做出了非中立的裁决，这导致了广泛的批评。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases specifically using "nonneutrally".  Its usage is straightforward and primarily descriptive.  However, it can be combined with other words to create phrases:

Example 1:  reported nonneutrally  非中立地报道
Example 2:  presented nonneutrally  非中立地呈现
Example 3:  interpreted nonneutrally  非中立地解读
Example 4:  discussed nonneutrally  非中立地讨论
Example 5:  covered nonneutrally  非中立地报道


词源:
The word is formed by the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") + "neutrally" (the adverbial form of "neutral").  "Neutral" originates from the Latin word "neutrālis," meaning "neither,"  "belonging to neither side," or "neither one nor the other."
