prohibitionism:/ˌproʊɪˈbɪʃənɪzəm/| n. |禁酒主义；禁令主义|The belief in or advocacy of the prohibition of alcohol.  It's a social and political movement that aims to control or ban the production, distribution, and consumption of alcoholic beverages.|The rise of prohibitionism in the early 20th century led to the passage of the 18th Amendment in the United States.|近义词：temperance movement, abstentionism|反义词：alcoholism, liberalization
