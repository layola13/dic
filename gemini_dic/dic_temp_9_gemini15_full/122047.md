mistaken:/mɪˈsteɪkən/| adj.|错误的，误会了的|Being wrong; incorrect in opinion or judgment.  It implies a misunderstanding or a wrong belief.|例句：I was mistaken about the time; the meeting is at 2 pm, not 3 pm.  我弄错了时间；会议在下午2点，而不是3点。

例句：He was mistaken in thinking she was angry. 他错以为她生气了。


近义词：wrong, incorrect, erroneous, fallacious|反义词：correct, right, accurate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The information is mistaken. = 信息是错误的。
His assumptions are mistaken. = 他的假设是错误的。


一般过去时:
I was mistaken about the date. = 我弄错了日期。
They were mistaken about the identity of the thief. = 他们弄错了小偷的身份。


现在完成时:
I have been mistaken in my judgment. = 我判断错误了。
She has been mistakenly identified as the suspect. = 她被误认为是嫌疑犯。


过去完成时:
I had been mistaken about his intentions. = 我一直误解了他的意图。
They had been mistakenly led to believe the rumor. = 他们一直被误导相信了这个谣言。


将来时:
I will be mistaken if I think he will agree. = 如果我认为他会同意，我就会错了。
They will be mistakenly accused of the crime. = 他们会被错误地指控犯下罪行。


虚拟语气:
If I were not mistaken, he would have arrived by now. = 如果我没有弄错的话，他现在应该已经到了。
I wish I hadn't been mistaken about her feelings. = 我真希望我没有误解她的感受。


被动语态:
The facts were mistaken for fiction. = 事实被误认为是虚构的。
The identity of the witness was mistakenly reported. = 目击者的身份被错误报道了。


疑问句:
Am I mistaken? = 我弄错了吗？
Are you mistaken about the address? = 你地址弄错了吗？


否定句:
I am not mistaken; I saw him myself. = 我没有弄错；我亲眼看见他了。
She is not mistaken about the consequences. = 她对后果没有弄错。
