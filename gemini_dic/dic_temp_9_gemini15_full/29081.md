carving:/ˈkɑːrvɪŋ/ | n. v. | 雕刻；雕刻品 | The act or process of cutting a design or figure on a hard material; a piece of work so produced. | 例句：The intricate carving on the cathedral's facade is breathtaking. 教堂立面的精细雕刻令人叹为观止。


The wood carving was a beautiful piece of art.  这件木雕是一件美丽的艺术品。


近义词：sculpture, engraving, etching | 反义词：smoothing, polishing | 词性：名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He carves wood every day. 他每天都雕刻木头。
She carves beautiful figures from stone. 她用石头雕刻出美丽的雕像。

一般过去时:
They carved a pumpkin for Halloween. 他们雕刻了一个南瓜来庆祝万圣节。
He carved his initials into the tree. 他把他的缩写刻在树上。

现在完成时:
She has carved many intricate designs. 她已经雕刻了许多复杂的图案。
They have carved their names on the mountainside. 他们已经在山坡上刻上了他们的名字。

过去完成时:
He had carved the statue before the exhibition. 他在展览之前就雕刻好了这尊雕像。
She had carved the wooden bird for her daughter. 她为女儿雕刻了一只木鸟。

将来时:
They will carve a new totem pole for the village. 他们将为村庄雕刻一个新的图腾柱。
He will carve the turkey for Thanksgiving. 他将在感恩节雕刻火鸡。

虚拟语气:
If I had the tools, I would carve a dragon. 如果我有工具，我会雕刻一条龙。
I wish I could carve like Michelangelo. 我希望我能像米开朗基罗那样雕刻。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
