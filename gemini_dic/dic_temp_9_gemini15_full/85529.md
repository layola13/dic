hatch: /hætʃ/ | n., v. | 舱口；孵化；策划 |

英文解析:
As a noun, a hatch refers to a small door or opening, especially in the deck of a ship or aircraft, or in the roof or floor of a building.  As a verb, it means to come out of an egg; to cause eggs to hatch; or to devise or plan something secretly.

例句:
The chickens hatched from their eggs. = 小鸡从蛋里孵出来了。

The submarine crew opened the hatch. = 潜艇船员打开了舱口。


近义词:
(n.) opening, door, portal; (v.) incubate, brood, plot, scheme

反义词:
(n.)  None directly; (v.)  None directly, but the opposite of hatching eggs might be destroying eggs.


词性:
Noun, Verb
