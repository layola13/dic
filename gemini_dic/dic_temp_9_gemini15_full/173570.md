quasi-ideal:/ˈkwɑːzaɪˌaɪdiəl/ | adj. | 准理想的；近似理想的 |  Approaching or approximating an ideal; having characteristics that resemble an ideal but are not perfectly so.  This term is often used in mathematics, physics, and other scientific fields to describe a system or structure that exhibits some, but not all, of the properties of an ideal system. | Example: The quasi-ideal crystal structure showed some deviations from perfect lattice arrangement.  准理想晶体结构显示出与完美晶格排列的一些偏差。|近义词:near-ideal, almost ideal, approximate | 反义词:ideal, perfect
