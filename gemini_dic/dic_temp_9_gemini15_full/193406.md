seismographies:/saɪzˈmɒɡrəfiz/| n. |地震图；地震记录|Records of seismic waves produced by seismographs, showing the time, amplitude, and other characteristics of ground motion during an earthquake or other seismic event.  Seismographies are crucial for understanding earthquake magnitudes, locations, and the Earth's internal structure.|The seismographies clearly indicated the epicenter of the earthquake was near the coast.  地震图清楚地表明地震震中靠近海岸。
