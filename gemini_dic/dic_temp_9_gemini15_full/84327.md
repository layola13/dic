halfcoaxingly: /ˌhæfˈkoʊksɪŋli/ | adv. | 半哄骗地；半劝诱地 |  Describes an action performed in a way that is partly coaxing and partly something else, often implying a degree of manipulation or insincerity. It suggests a gentle, persuasive approach that might be subtly manipulative.  | Example: He halfcoaxingly suggested she take the job, knowing it would be a significant challenge for her. 他半哄半劝地建议她接受这份工作，他知道这对她来说将是一项巨大的挑战。 | 近义词: persuasively, enticingly, wheedlingly, cajolingly | 反义词: forcefully, directly, commandingly | 副词


USAGE->

This word is an adverb and doesn't have verb conjugations like "What" which is a pronoun.  Therefore, verb tense examples are not applicable.  It modifies verbs.


例句1: She halfcoaxingly offered him a cookie to get him to clean his room.  她半哄半骗地给他一块饼干，让他去打扫房间。

例句2: The salesman halfcoaxingly described the car's features, hoping to close the deal. 销售员半哄半骗地描述了汽车的特性，希望能成交。

词源:

The word is a combination of "half" (meaning partly or to a limited extent) and "coaxingly" (an adverb derived from the verb "to coax," meaning to persuade gently and patiently).  The "-ingly" suffix indicates manner or way.  The word is a relatively modern formation, not found in older dictionaries.
