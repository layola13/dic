interjecting:/ˌɪntərˈdʒɛktɪŋ/| v.| 插嘴；突然插入 | The act of inserting something, especially a remark, abruptly into a conversation or activity.  |例句：He kept interjecting irrelevant comments during the lecture. 他在讲座期间不断插话，说了些无关紧要的话。|例句：The comedian interjected a funny story into his serious speech.  喜剧演员在他的严肃演讲中穿插了一个有趣的故事。|近义词：interrupting, butting in, chiming in |反义词：remaining silent, listening attentively


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He interjects comments frequently. = 他经常插嘴。
She interjects witty remarks into the conversation. = 她经常在谈话中插进一些俏皮话。

一般过去时:
He interjected a joke into the serious discussion. = 他在严肃的讨论中插了一句笑话。
She interjected her opinion during the meeting. = 她在会议期间插嘴表达了自己的意见。

现在完成时:
He has interjected his opinions several times already. = 他已经几次插嘴表达了自己的观点。
She has interjected herself into the argument repeatedly. = 她一再插手争论。


过去完成时:
He had interjected several times before the speaker finished. = 在演讲者结束之前，他已经插嘴好几次了。
She had interjected a correction before the error was noticed. = 在错误被发现之前，她已经插嘴进行了纠正。
