regrater:/rɪˈɡreɪtər/| n. |包销商；囤积居奇者；转卖者|A regrater is someone who buys goods in large quantities and then resells them at a higher price, often in the same market.  This practice was historically considered a form of market manipulation and was often subject to legal restrictions.  |例句：The regrater bought up all the wheat, driving up the price for the local farmers.  包销商买光了所有的麦子，导致当地农民的麦子价格上涨。

例句：The town council tried to prevent regraters from manipulating the price of essential goods.  镇议会试图阻止包销商操纵生活必需品的物价。


近义词：monopolizer, speculator, middleman |反义词：producer, consumer |词性 n.


词源:  The word "regrater" comes from the Anglo-French word "regrater," meaning "to scrape together, to hoard."  It is ultimately derived from the Old French verb "grater," meaning "to scratch" or "to scrape," referring to the practice of scraping together goods to resell at a profit.  The "re-" prefix intensifies the action.
