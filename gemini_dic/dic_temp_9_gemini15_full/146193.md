overexerts:/ˌoʊvərɪˈzɜːrts/| vt.|过度使用；使过度劳累|To exert oneself to an excessive degree; to strain beyond one's capacity.  It implies pushing oneself too hard, often resulting in fatigue or injury.|例句：He overexerts himself at work and often comes home exhausted. 他工作过度劳累，经常疲惫不堪地回家。

例句：She overexerted herself during the marathon and had to drop out. 她在马拉松比赛中过度劳累，不得不退出比赛。


近义词：overwork, strain, overtax, exhaust, fatigue


反义词：rest, relax, conserve energy


词性:动词

词源:
The word "overexerts" comes from the verb "exert," meaning "to make a vigorous effort," combined with the prefix "over-," indicating "too much" or "excessively."  "Exert" itself has roots in the Latin word "exertus," the past participle of "exergere," meaning "to raise up," "to lift."
