liquidators: /ˈlɪkwɪdeɪtərz/ | n. | 清算人；清算公司；处理人 |

英文解析: A liquidator is a person or company appointed to wind up the affairs of a company that is being dissolved or bankrupt.  They collect the assets, pay off debts, and distribute any remaining funds to shareholders or creditors.  In some contexts, it can also refer to someone who sells off assets quickly, often at a loss.

例句:

The company appointed liquidators after it filed for bankruptcy. = 公司在申请破产后任命了清算人。

The liquidators sold off the remaining inventory at a significant discount. = 清算人以大幅折扣的价格卖掉了剩余的库存。


近义词:  administrator, receiver, trustee (in bankruptcy)

反义词:  (No direct antonym, but the opposite concept would be someone who builds or expands a business.)

词性: noun
