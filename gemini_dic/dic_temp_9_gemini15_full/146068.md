overeasy: /ˌoʊvərˈiːzi/ | adj. | 煎得很嫩的（指鸡蛋）|  Describes an egg fried so that the white is set but the yolk remains runny.  It's a style of cooking eggs where the egg is fried on both sides, but the yolk is left soft. | Example: I'd like two overeasy eggs with my breakfast. 我早餐想吃两个煎得很嫩的鸡蛋。 |近义词: sunny-side up (only flipped once), runny | 反义词: over hard, over medium
