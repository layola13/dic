malabar:/ˈmæləbɑːr/| n. |马拉巴尔（地名）|Malabar refers to a region on the southwestern coast of India, historically known for its spices and trade.  It encompasses parts of present-day Kerala and Karnataka states. The name is of uncertain origin, possibly derived from the Malayalam word "malabar," meaning "mountain range" or "highland."|The Malabar Coast was famous for its spice trade for centuries. |近义词：Kerala (partially), Malabar Coast|反义词：无
