imposing: /ɪmˈpoʊzɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人印象深刻的；雄伟的；威严的 |  Impressive in appearance or manner; commanding attention.  | 例句：The imposing castle stood on the hill.  那座雄伟的城堡耸立在山上。

例句：The imposing speaker commanded the attention of the entire audience.  那位令人印象深刻的演讲者吸引了全场观众的注意力。


近义词： impressive, grand, majestic, awe-inspiring, commanding

反义词： insignificant, unimpressive, humble, meek


词源：

From Old French *imposer, from Latin imponere "to place upon," from in- "on, upon" + ponere "to place, put."  The sense development is from "to place upon" (as a burden or duty) to "to impose oneself" (to make one's presence felt) to the current adjectival sense of being impressive or commanding.
