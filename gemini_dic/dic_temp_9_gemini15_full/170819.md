pseudoetymological:/ˌsuːdoʊˌɛtɪməˈlɒdʒɪkəl/ | adj. | 假词源的；貌似词源的 |  Relating to or characteristic of a word or etymology that is falsely or mistakenly derived.  It implies a connection between a word's origin and meaning that isn't actually supported by historical evidence.  The word often seems plausible on the surface but is incorrect. | The word "hamburger" is often cited as an example of a pseudoetymological belief, with a common misconception linking it to Hamburg, Germany, although the actual etymology is more complex and less directly related. | 近义词: spurious, false, erroneous, mistaken | 反义词: authentic, genuine, true
