journalism:/ˈdʒɜːrnəlɪzəm/| n. |新闻业；新闻工作|The activity of gathering, assessing, creating, and presenting news and information. It includes the methods of inquiry employed, the way information is collected and verified, and the forms in which it is presented.  Journalism is often considered a public service, aiming to inform the public and hold power accountable.|Example:  She pursued a career in journalism after graduating from college. 她大学毕业后从事新闻工作。

Example: Investigative journalism is crucial for a healthy democracy.  调查性新闻对于健康的民主至关重要。


近义词：news reporting, news media, media work|反义词：censorship, propaganda
