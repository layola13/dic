cocultivations:/ˌkoʊkʌlˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 共同培养；共培养；混养 | The process of growing two or more different types of cells or organisms together in the same culture.  This technique is often used in cell biology and microbiology to study cell-cell interactions, symbiotic relationships, or the effects of one organism on another. | Example:  Researchers conducted cocultivations of human immune cells with tumor cells to investigate the immune response.  研究人员对人体免疫细胞和肿瘤细胞进行了共培养，以研究免疫反应。


Example: The cocultivation of bacteria and fungi revealed a synergistic effect on nutrient utilization.  细菌和真菌的共培养揭示了对营养利用的协同作用。


近义词：coculture, mixed culture | 反义词：monoculture (单一培养)
