second-guesser:/ˈsɛkəndˈɡɛsər/ | n. |事后诸葛亮，爱事后批评的人 | A person who criticizes or second-guesses decisions after they have been made.  |  He's a terrible second-guesser, always pointing out what we should have done differently. 他是个十足的事后诸葛亮，总是指出我们本应该怎么做不一样。|  Monday morning quarterback, armchair critic | supporter, advocate | noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He second-guesses every decision I make. = 他对我做的每一个决定都指指点点。
She constantly second-guesses her own choices. = 她不断地质疑自己的选择。

一般过去时:
He second-guessed my strategy after the game. = 比赛结束后，他批评了我的策略。
They second-guessed the coach's decision to substitute players. = 他们质疑教练换人 的决定。

现在完成时:
I've been second-guessed so many times, I'm losing confidence. = 我被指指点点过很多次，信心都快没了。
They have second-guessed the company's marketing campaign. = 他们一直在批评公司的营销活动。

过去完成时:
After the failure, they realized they had second-guessed the initial plan too much. = 失败之后，他们意识到他们对最初的计划过度批评了。
I wish I hadn't second-guessed my instincts. = 我真希望我没有怀疑自己的直觉。

将来时:
I know they will second-guess my decision to leave the company. = 我知道他们会批评我离开公司的决定。
He predicts that the public will second-guess the government's response to the crisis. = 他预测公众会批评政府对危机的应对。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't second-guessed myself, I might have succeeded. = 如果我没有怀疑自己，我或许会成功。
I wish people wouldn't second-guess my every move. = 我希望人们不要事事都批评我。

被动语态:
His decisions were constantly second-guessed by his colleagues. = 他的决定总是被他同事们所批评。
The team's strategy was second-guessed after their defeat. = 他们队在失败后，策略受到了批评。

疑问句:
Are you constantly second-guessing yourself? = 你是不是总是怀疑自己？
Will they second-guess my proposal? = 他们会批评我的提案吗？

否定句:
Don't second-guess your abilities. = 不要怀疑你的能力。
He didn't second-guess her judgment in this matter. = 在这件事上，他没有质疑她的判断。
