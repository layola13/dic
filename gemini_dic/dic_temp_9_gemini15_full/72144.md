floodplains:/ˈflʌdpleɪnz/ | n. | 洪泛平原 |  A floodplain is a relatively flat area of land next to a river or stream that is subject to flooding.  It is formed by the deposition of sediment during periods of high water. Floodplains are often fertile and productive agricultural land due to the nutrient-rich soil. | Example: The fertile floodplain of the Nile River supported ancient Egyptian civilization.  尼罗河肥沃的洪泛平原支撑着古埃及文明。  The river overflowed its banks, inundating the floodplain. 河水漫过河岸，淹没了洪泛平原。| 近义词：alluvial plain, river valley | 反义词：highland, upland
