kinetogenesis:/ˌkaɪnɪtoʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs/| n. | 运动发生；动力发生；运动成因 | The origin and development of movement.  Kinetogenesis refers to the biological processes that generate movement,  encompassing the interplay of neurological, muscular, and skeletal systems. It is a broad term often used in fields such as biomechanics, physiology, and developmental biology. | Example:  The study explored the kinetogenesis of swimming in larval amphibians.  这项研究探讨了蝌蚪游泳的运动发生机制。 | 近义词：motion generation, movement initiation | 反义词：motionlessness, immobility |  名词
