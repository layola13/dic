quasi-territorially: /ˈkwɑːzaɪˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəli/ | adv. | 准领土地；类似领土地 |  Adverbial form of "quasi-territorial," indicating a manner or condition resembling territoriality but not fully meeting its criteria. It suggests a partial or approximate claim to territory, or behavior that mimics territorial behavior without the full legal or biological definition.


例句：

The nomadic tribes maintained quasi-territorially control over their seasonal grazing lands. = 游牧部落对他们季节性的牧场保持着准领土控制。


The company operates quasi-territorially, assigning sales regions with overlapping responsibilities. = 这家公司以准领土的方式运营，分配的销售区域存在重叠的责任。


近义词：partially territorially, semi-territorially, nominally territorially


反义词：fully territorially, completely territorially, unequivocally territorially


词性：副词


词源：

* **quasi-**:  From Latin, meaning "as if," "seemingly," or "almost."
* **territorial**: From "territory," ultimately from Latin "territorium," meaning "land," "district," or "region."  This combines "terra" (earth) and "-orium" (a suffix indicating a place or area).
* **-ly**:  English suffix forming adverbs.


USAGE->  (Since this is an adverb, verb tenses don't directly apply to it.  The adverb modifies the verb of the sentence.)

Example Sentences illustrating different sentence structures:
