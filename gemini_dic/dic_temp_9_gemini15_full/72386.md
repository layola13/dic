fluctuability:/ˌflʌktʃuˈeɪbɪləti/| n. |波动性，易变性 |The capacity or tendency to fluctuate; instability.  Fluctuability in the stock market can cause significant financial risk.  股市波动可能导致巨大的金融风险。|近义词：variability, instability, changeability |反义词：stability, constancy, steadiness


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, typical verb tenses are not applicable.  Instead, we will show how to use it in different sentence structures.


In a simple sentence:
The fluctuability of the price of oil is a major concern.  石油价格的波动性是一个主要担忧。


In a complex sentence:
Because of the fluctuability of the weather, we decided to postpone the outdoor event. 由于天气的波动性，我们决定推迟户外活动。


In a sentence with a prepositional phrase:
The company's success depends on minimizing the fluctuability in its production.  公司的成功取决于最大限度地减少其生产的波动性。


In a sentence with a relative clause:
The market's fluctuability, which is a common characteristic, is difficult to predict.  市场的波动性，这是一个普遍特征，很难预测。


In a sentence with an adverbial clause:
Although the fluctuability is high, we remain confident in the long-term prospects. 尽管波动性很高，我们仍然对长期前景充满信心。
