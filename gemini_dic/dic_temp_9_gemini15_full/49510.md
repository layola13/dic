demagogues:/ˈdɛməɡɒɡz/| n.|煽动者，蛊惑民心的政客|A demagogue is a political leader who seeks support by appealing to the desires and prejudices of ordinary people rather than by using rational argument.  They often use emotionally charged language and promises to gain power and manipulate the public. |例句：The demagogue used fear-mongering tactics to rally support for his policies.  煽动者利用危言耸听的策略来争取对其政策的支持。


例句：His speeches were filled with demagogic rhetoric designed to inflame the passions of the crowd. 他的演讲充满了煽动性言论，旨在激起群众的激情。


近义词：agitator, rabble-rouser, inciter, firebrand|反义词：statesman, leader (in a positive sense), diplomat


词源:  The word "demagogue" comes from the Greek words "dēmos" (δήμος), meaning "people," and "agōgos" (ἀγωγός), meaning "leader" or "guide."  Therefore, a literal translation would be "leader of the people," but the modern connotation is strongly negative, implying manipulation and exploitation.
