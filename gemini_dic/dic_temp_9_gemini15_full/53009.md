dinosauria:/ˌdaɪnəˈsɔəriə/| n. |恐龙类，恐龙总目|The term "Dinosauria" refers to a diverse group of extinct reptiles that dominated terrestrial ecosystems for over 165 million years.  The name itself is derived from the Greek words "deinos" (terrible, fearfully great) and "sauros" (lizard).  Dinosauria is a clade, meaning it includes a common ancestor and all of its descendants.  This clade is further divided into various suborders and families, reflecting the vast diversity of dinosaur forms. |The discovery of *Tyrannosaurus rex* fossils significantly increased our understanding of Dinosauria.|近义词：dinosaurs|反义词：无
