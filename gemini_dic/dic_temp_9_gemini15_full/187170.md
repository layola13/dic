roadstead:/ˈroʊdˌsted/ | n. |停泊处，锚地 | A roadstead is a sheltered area of water, especially near the coast, where ships can anchor temporarily but is not a harbor with complete protection from the elements.  It offers some protection from winds and waves but lacks the extensive harbor facilities like docks and piers. | Example: The small fishing boats found shelter in the roadstead during the storm.  小型渔船在暴风雨中在停泊处找到了避难所。The ship waited in the roadstead for the pilot to arrive. 船在停泊处等待领航员到来。|近义词：anchorage, mooring, bay|反义词：harbor, port
