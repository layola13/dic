reck:/rek/| vt.|考虑；关心；认为；计算|To think carefully about something; to consider something important; to believe something to be true; to calculate.|He reckoned without the cost of the materials. 他没有算计到材料的成本。|consider, contemplate, calculate|ignore, disregard, neglect|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reckons he'll win the race. = 他认为他会赢得比赛。
She reckons the cost of the project carefully. = 她仔细计算了项目的成本。


一般过去时:
They reckoned they would be late. = 他们认为他们会迟到。
He reckoned the distance incorrectly. = 他错误地计算了距离。


现在完成时:
I've reckoned the total cost of the trip. = 我已经计算出了旅行的总成本。
She has reckoned with her mistakes. = 她已经正视了自己的错误。


过去完成时:
He had reckoned without the unexpected delay. = 他没有料到意外的延误。
They had reckoned that the project would be finished by now. = 他们原以为项目到那时就完成了。


将来时:
I reckon I will finish the work by tomorrow. = 我估计我明天能完成这项工作。
She reckons she will be home by six. = 她预计六点钟到家。


虚拟语气:
If I reckoned correctly, we're lost. = 如果我算得没错，我们迷路了。
I wish I had reckoned the risk beforehand. = 我真希望我事先考虑过风险。


被动语态:
The cost was reckoned to be too high. = 成本被认为太高了。
The distance was incorrectly reckoned. = 距离计算错误。


疑问句:
Do you reckon he'll come? = 你认为他会来吗？
What do you reckon the problem is? = 你认为问题是什么？


否定句:
I don't reckon he'll be happy about it. = 我认为他不会高兴的。
They didn't reckon with the opposition. = 他们没有考虑到反对意见。


PHRASE->
reckon with:  处理；对付；考虑到
reckon on: 依赖；指望
reckon without: 没有考虑到；没有预料到
reckon up: 计算；总结
reckon something to be: 认为某事是


词源:
Old English *reccan "to care for, to think," from Proto-Germanic *rekkōn- (source also of Old High German rehhōn "to count," Old Norse reikja "to count, reckon," Old Saxon rekkian "to reckon").  The sense evolved from "care" to "consider" to "count."
