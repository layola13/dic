reconstructionist: /ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪst/ | adj. n. | 重建主义者；重建论者 | Relating to or advocating reconstructionism, a movement emphasizing the importance of reconstructing or reinterpreting religious beliefs and practices in light of modern knowledge and experience. 词源:
The word "reconstructionist" is derived from the verb "reconstruct," meaning to build or put something together again.  The suffix "-ist" indicates a person who believes in or practices something.  The term arose in the context of religious movements that sought to reinterpret and reformulate traditional religious beliefs and practices to make them relevant to modern life and thought.
