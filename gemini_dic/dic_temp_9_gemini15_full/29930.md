cavaliers:/ˌkævəˈlɪərz/| n.| 骑士；宫廷侍从；(尤指英国内战中的)保皇党 |  A cavalier is a knight or a gentleman serving in a royal court, especially one of the supporters of King Charles I during the English Civil War.  The term also refers to a person who is nonchalant and carefree. |例句：The cavaliers rode bravely into battle.  骑士们勇敢地冲进了战场。

例句：He displayed a cavalier attitude towards his responsibilities. 他对自己的责任表现出漫不经心的态度。|近义词：gentleman, knight, royalist, nonchalant, carefree |反义词：roundhead, puritan, serious, careful
