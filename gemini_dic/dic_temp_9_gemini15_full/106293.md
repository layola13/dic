knuckling:/ˈnʌklɪŋ/| n. v.|指关节着地；屈服；|The act of resting on or hitting with the knuckles; yielding or submitting.|例句：He was knuckling under the pressure. 他屈服于压力之下。|近义词：yielding, submitting, bowing|反义词：resisting, rebelling, defying|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He knuckles under pressure. = 他屈服于压力。
She knuckles down to her studies. = 她认真学习。


一般过去时:
He knuckled under to his boss's demands. = 他屈服于老板的要求。
They knuckled down and finished the project on time. = 他们努力工作，按时完成了项目。


现在完成时:
He has knuckled under to the circumstances. = 他已经屈服于环境。
She has knuckled down and improved her grades significantly. = 她刻苦学习，成绩显著提高。


过去完成时:
He had knuckled under before the deadline. = 他在截止日期前就屈服了。
They had knuckled down, but it was still too late. = 他们努力过，但为时已晚。


将来时:
He will knuckle under if the pressure continues. = 如果压力持续下去，他会屈服。
They will knuckle down to complete the task. = 他们会努力完成任务。


虚拟语气:
If he knuckled under, he would succeed. = 如果他屈服，他会成功。
I wish he had knuckled down earlier. = 我希望他早点努力。


被动语态:
The deal was knuckled under by the opposing party.  = 这笔交易被对方方压垮了。(This example is less common,  the passive voice is not frequently used with this verb.)
The work will be knuckled down to by the team. = 这个工作将会被团队认真对待。(This example is less common,  the passive voice is not frequently used with this verb.)


疑问句:
Will he knuckle under to their demands? = 他会屈服于他们的要求吗？
Did she knuckle down and study hard for the exam? = 她为了考试而努力学习了吗？


否定句:
He didn't knuckle under to the threats. = 他没有屈服于威胁。
She didn't knuckle down and get the work done. = 她没有努力工作把事情做完。


PHRASE->
knuckle down: 认真努力
knuckle under: 屈服
knuckle up: 握紧拳头
knuckle under to: 屈服于
knuckles to the grindstone: 勤奋工作


词源:
The word "knuckle" comes from Middle English *knokkle, from Old English *cnucl, meaning "joint of a finger".  The sense of "yielding" or "submitting" developed later, likely from the image of someone hitting the ground with their knuckles in defeat or submission.
