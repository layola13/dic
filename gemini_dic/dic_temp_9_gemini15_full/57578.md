dramatizing: /ˈdræməˌtaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |戏剧化；使戏剧性；夸张地描述 | To represent or portray (something) in a dramatic way; to exaggerate or heighten the effect of.  |例句：He was dramatizing the situation to make himself look better. 他在夸大其词以使自己看起来更好。


例句：The news report dramatized the events of the disaster. 新闻报道戏剧化地报道了灾难事件。

近义词：exaggerate, sensationalize, heighten

反义词：downplay, understate, minimize


USAGE->

He dramatizes every little problem. 他把每一个小问题都戏剧化。
They dramatize their lives on social media. 他们在社交媒体上戏剧化地展现他们的生活。

一般过去时:

现在完成时:
He has dramatized his role in the company's success. 他夸大了自己在公司成功中的作用。

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
If he dramatized the story more, it would be more interesting. 如果他把故事演绎得更夸张一些，就会更有趣。

被动语态:
