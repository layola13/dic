phyllocyanin: /ˌfaɪloʊˈsaɪənɪn/ | n. |叶绿素类似物 | A pigment similar to chlorophyll, often found in certain bacteria and archaea.  It's characterized by a porphyrin ring structure, but differs in the substituents attached to the ring.  Phyllocyanins are involved in light-harvesting processes in these organisms. | Example:  The photosynthetic bacteria contained a unique phyllocyanin pigment that absorbed light at longer wavelengths than chlorophyll.  细菌体内含有独特的叶绿素类似物色素，其吸收的光波长比叶绿素更长。 | 近义词: chlorophyll (but with crucial structural differences) | 反义词: None specifically,  but could be contrasted with pigments that don't participate in photosynthesis. | 名词
