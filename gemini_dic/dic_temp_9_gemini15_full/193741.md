self-controlled: /ˌself kənˈtroʊld/ | adj. |自制的，克己的 | Able to control one's feelings and overcome impulsive behavior.  Someone who is self-controlled is able to manage their emotions and actions effectively, resisting temptations and staying calm under pressure. |例句：She remained remarkably self-controlled in the face of such provocation.  她面对如此挑衅却保持了惊人的自制力。

She demonstrated great self-control by resisting the urge to eat the entire cake. 她克制住吃掉整个蛋糕的冲动，展现了极强的自制力。


近义词：disciplined, restrained, composed, self-possessed |反义词：impulsive, uncontrolled, unrestrained, undisciplined


词源：The word is a compound of "self," referring to oneself, and "controlled," the past participle of the verb "control," meaning to exercise restraining or directing influence over.  The combination signifies having control over oneself.
