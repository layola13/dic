cockled:/ˈkɒkld/ | adj. | 波浪形的；起皱的；有褶皱的 |  Having a wrinkled or puckered surface; uneven and bumpy.  The word often describes a surface texture, similar to the shell of a cockle. |例句：The old leather jacket was cockled and worn.  这件旧皮夹克起皱且磨损。

The sea was cockled with whitecaps. 海面波浪起伏，浪花朵朵。


近义词：wrinkled, creased, crumpled, puckered | 反义词：smooth, flat, unwrinkled

词源:  The word derives from the noun "cockle," referring to a type of shellfish with a ridged shell. The adjective "cockled" adopted the shell's textured appearance to describe similar surfaces.  The association with the cockle's shell likely explains the bumpy or wrinkled connotation.
