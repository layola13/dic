learnednesses:/ˈlɜːrnɪdnɪsɪz/ | n. |学问；知识；学识 |The sum total of knowledge acquired through learning and experience.  It often implies a deep or profound understanding of a subject or subjects.  The plural form emphasizes multiple areas of expertise or a vast amount of knowledge. |例句：His learnednesses in both history and philosophy were impressive.  他的历史和哲学方面的学识令人印象深刻。

His numerous learnednesses spanned multiple disciplines, making him a true polymath. 他的学识涵盖多个学科，使他成为一位真正的博学家。


近义词：knowledge, erudition, scholarship, expertise  |反义词：ignorance, illiteracy


词性：名词


词源：源自动词"learn" (学习)，加上后缀"-ed" (过去分词形式，表示完成或被动)，再加后缀"-ness" (名词后缀，表示状态或性质)，最后加上复数后缀"-es"。  This reflects the accumulated state of having learned many things.
