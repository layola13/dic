pandarism:/ˈpændərɪzəm/| n. | 逢迎；谄媚；拉皮条 | The act of pandering;  servile flattery;  procuring prostitutes.  It often implies a willingness to exploit or compromise one's values for personal gain or favor. | Example:  His pandarism to the wealthy elite earned him a comfortable life but cost him his integrity.  他的谄媚讨好富人精英让他过上了舒适的生活，但却让他失去了正直。  |近义词：fawning, sycophancy,  procuring, pimping | 反义词：integrity, honesty, uprightness | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He panders to the desires of his wealthy clients. = 他迎合他富有的客户的欲望。
The politician panders to the public's fears. = 这个政客迎合公众的恐惧。

一般过去时:
She pandered to the whims of her boss to keep her job. = 为了保住工作，她一味迎合老板的喜好。
The newspaper pandered to the public's thirst for scandal. = 这家报纸迎合了公众对丑闻的渴望。

现在完成时:
He has pandered to the crowd for years, gaining popularity but losing respect. = 他多年来一直迎合大众，获得了人气却失去了尊重。
The company has pandered to the lowest common denominator in its marketing. = 这家公司在市场营销中迎合了最低的共同点。

过去完成时:
They had pandered to the dictator for years before his downfall. = 在独裁者垮台之前，他们多年来一直奉承他。
The artist had pandered to the critics' expectations in his previous works. = 在他之前的作品中，艺术家迎合了评论家的期望。

将来时:
She will pander to the media to gain positive coverage. = 她将迎合媒体以获得正面报道。
The government will pander to special interests to secure votes. = 政府将迎合特殊利益以争取选票。


虚拟语气:
If he pandered less, he might have more genuine friends. = 如果他不那么逢迎，他可能会拥有更多真正的朋友。
I wish the artist hadn't pandered to the popular taste. = 我希望艺术家没有迎合大众口味。


被动语态:
The public's opinions are often pandered to by politicians. = 政客们常常迎合公众的意见。
The movie was criticized for pandering to violent stereotypes. = 这部电影因迎合暴力刻板印象而受到批评。


疑问句:
Does he pander to the wealthy? = 他是否逢迎富人？
Is the media pandering to public sentiment? = 媒体是否迎合公众情绪？


否定句:
He does not pander to anyone. = 他不逢迎任何人。
She refused to pander to the demands of her critics. = 她拒绝迎合批评家的要求。
