interinhibition:/ˌɪntərɪnɪˈbɪʃən/| n. |相互抑制；交互抑制|The mutual inhibition of two or more processes or activities.  For example, in some neural networks, the activation of one neuron can suppress the activity of another.  This phenomenon is crucial for various biological processes and is also observed in other systems, such as chemical reactions.|Example:  The study demonstrated interinhibition between the two competing bacterial strains.  这项研究证明了两种竞争细菌菌株之间的相互抑制。|近义词:mutual inhibition, reciprocal inhibition|反义词:mutual excitation, synergism|无
