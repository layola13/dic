post-biblical: /ˌpoʊstbaɪˈbɪkl/ | adj. |圣经以后的；后圣经时代的 | Relating to the period after the completion of the biblical canon.  This term typically refers to the period and the literature produced after the formation of the Jewish and Christian biblical canons, covering a vast range of historical periods and religious expressions. The exact time frame is debated depending on the context, but generally encompasses the period from the end of biblical writings to the present day. | The post-biblical interpretations of the scriptures often differ widely.  后圣经时代的圣经解释常常大相径庭。 | 近义词：post-canonical, later, subsequent | 反义词：biblical, canonical
