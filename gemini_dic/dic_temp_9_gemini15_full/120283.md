miniaturization: /ˌmɪniətʃəraɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 小型化；微型化 | The process of making something smaller.  It often involves reducing the size of electronic components or systems. | Example: The miniaturization of computers has led to the development of smartphones.  小型化的电脑导致了智能手机的发展。The miniaturization of medical devices allows for less invasive procedures. 微型医疗设备使得微创手术成为可能。|近义词：reduction, downsizing |反义词：enlargement, magnification |

词源:
The word "miniaturization" is derived from "miniature," which ultimately comes from the Latin word "miniare," meaning "to paint in miniature."  Originally referring to the art of painting small portraits, the term evolved to encompass the general concept of making things smaller.  The suffix "-ization" denotes the process of making something.
