palaeographist:/ˌpælɪˈɒɡrəfɪst/|/ˈpælɪəɡræfɪst/| n. |古文字学家|A palaeographist is a scholar who studies ancient or historical handwriting and scripts. They analyze the physical characteristics of writing to determine its date, origin, and authorship, contributing to our understanding of history and culture. |The palaeographist meticulously examined the medieval manuscript, identifying subtle variations in the script.  古文字学家仔细检查了中世纪的手稿，识别出文字中细微的变化。|historian, epigrapher| (no direct antonym)|名词
