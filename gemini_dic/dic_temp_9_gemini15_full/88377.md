hiragana:/hɪrəˈɡɑːnə/|/ˈhɪrəɡɑːnɑː/| n. |平假名 | Hiragana is a Japanese syllabary, one of the three main writing systems of the Japanese language. It is a phonetic script, meaning each character represents a syllable.  It is mainly used for writing native Japanese words and grammatical particles.  |Example:  The word "hello" is written as "こんにちは" (konnichiwa) in hiragana.  |近义词:katakana(片假名)|反义词:N/A|名词
