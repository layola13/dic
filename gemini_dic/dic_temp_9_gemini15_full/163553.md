/ˌpriːˈkaʊnsəlɪŋ/ | n. |咨询前的辅导；术前咨询 | Precounselling refers to the guidance and information provided to individuals before they receive a particular service or undergo a specific procedure, often in a medical or psychological context.  It aims to prepare them for what to expect, address their concerns, and ensure informed consent.  |Example:  Precounselling for surgery included detailed explanations of the procedure, potential risks, and recovery process.  术前咨询包括对手术过程、潜在风险和恢复过程的详细解释。 |近义词：pre-consultation, prior briefing, preparation |反义词：postcounselling, aftercare
