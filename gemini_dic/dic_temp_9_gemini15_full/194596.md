self-suppressive:/ˌsɛlf səˈprɛsɪv/| adj.| 自我抑制的，自抑的 |  Describing something that inhibits or suppresses its own activity or growth.  This term often applies to systems or processes where an increase in the activity of the system leads to a decrease in its further activity.  | Example:  The self-suppressive mechanism prevented the runaway reaction.  自我抑制机制阻止了失控反应。  | 近义词：self-limiting, autoinhibitory | 反义词：self-reinforcing, self-accelerating |  形容词
