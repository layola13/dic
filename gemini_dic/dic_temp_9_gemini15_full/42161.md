couching:/ˈkaʊtʃɪŋ/| n. v.| 躺卧；表达方式；辅导；治疗；|The act of lying down or reclining; a particular manner or style of expressing something; the act of coaching or tutoring; therapy or treatment.|例句：He was couching on the sofa. 他躺在沙发上。The politician's couching of the issue was deliberately vague.  这位政治家对这个问题的表达方式故意含糊不清。|近义词：reclining, phrasing, coaching, tutoring, therapy|反义词：standing, upright, directness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He couches his words carefully. = 他仔细地措辞。
She couches her criticisms gently. = 她委婉地提出批评。

一般过去时:
He couched his response diplomatically. = 他外交辞令地作出了回应。
The doctor couched the bad news carefully. = 医生小心翼翼地告知了坏消息。

现在完成时:
They have couched their arguments effectively. = 他们有效地阐述了论点。
She has couched her resignation in polite terms. = 她以礼貌的措辞递交了辞呈。

过去完成时:
He had couched his apology in a formal letter. = 他在正式信函中表达了他的歉意。
They had couched their demands in strong terms. = 他们以强硬的措辞提出了要求。

将来时:
He will couch his presentation professionally. = 他将专业地进行演讲。
She will couch her refusal politely. = 她将委婉地拒绝。

虚拟语气:
If he couched his words differently, he would have been understood. = 如果他换一种说法，就会被理解了。
I wish she had couched her remarks more tactfully. = 我希望她说话更委婉一些。

被动语态:
The message was couched in ambiguous terms. = 这条消息措辞含糊不清。
The proposal was couched in diplomatic language. = 这份提案使用外交辞令。

疑问句:
How would you couch your request? = 你将如何表达你的请求？
How was the difficult news couched? = 那个坏消息是怎么说的？

否定句:
He didn't couch his anger appropriately. = 他没有恰当地表达自己的愤怒。
She didn't couch her opinion delicately enough. = 她表达自己的观点不够委婉。
