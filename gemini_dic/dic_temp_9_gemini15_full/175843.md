rastafarianism:/ˌrɑːstəˈfɛəriənɪzəm/ | n. |拉斯特法里教 | Rastafarianism is a religious movement that originated in Jamaica in the 1930s.  It is based on the deification of Haile Selassie I, the former Emperor of Ethiopia, who Rastafarians believe to be the Second Coming of Christ, or even God incarnate.  The movement emphasizes spiritual liberation, black pride, and the repatriation of the African diaspora to Africa.  It is characterized by its distinctive cultural practices, including the wearing of dreadlocks, the use of cannabis (ganja) for religious purposes, and the use of reggae music as a form of spiritual expression. | Many Rastafarians believe Haile Selassie I was the Messiah. | 近义词：None (unique religious movement) | 反义词：None (no direct opposite)
