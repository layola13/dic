kickstart: /ˈkɪkˌstɑːrt/ | vt. n. |  启动，促进，激发 | To give something a sudden increase in activity or progress; to start something suddenly and forcefully. |  例句：The new marketing campaign kickstarted sales. 新的营销活动促进了销售。  The government's investment kickstarted the local economy. 政府的投资启动了当地经济。 | 近义词：boost, jumpstart, initiate, launch | 反义词：halt, stop, hinder


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new project kickstarts innovation. = 新项目启动了创新。
They kickstart their day with a cup of coffee. = 他们以一杯咖啡开始新的一天。


一般过去时:
The new CEO kickstarted a restructuring process. = 新任CEO启动了重组流程。
The rain kickstarted the growth of the plants. = 雨水促进了植物的生长。


现在完成时:
The company has kickstarted several new initiatives this year. = 公司今年启动了几项新举措。
The advertising campaign has kickstarted a surge in brand awareness. = 广告活动引发了品牌知名度的激增。


过去完成时:
They had kickstarted the engine before the race began. = 比赛开始前他们已经启动了引擎。
The previous government had kickstarted a major infrastructure project. = 前一届政府启动了一个大型基础设施项目。


将来时:
The company will kickstart a new product line next quarter. = 公司下个季度将启动一个新的产品线。
We will kickstart the meeting with a brief overview. = 我们将以简短的概述开始会议。


虚拟语气:
If we had more funding, we could kickstart the project sooner. = 如果我们有更多资金，我们可以更快地启动项目。
I wish they would kickstart the reform process. = 我希望他们能启动改革进程。


被动语态:
The project was kickstarted by a generous donation. = 该项目由一笔慷慨的捐款启动。
The economy was kickstarted by government spending. = 经济得到了政府支出的推动。


疑问句:
Will the new policy kickstart economic growth? = 新政策能否刺激经济增长？
What measures can be taken to kickstart the stalled negotiations? = 可以采取什么措施来启动停滞的谈判？


否定句:
The lack of funding prevented them from kickstarting the project. = 资金不足阻止了他们启动项目。
The company failed to kickstart its new marketing campaign effectively. = 公司未能有效启动其新的营销活动。
