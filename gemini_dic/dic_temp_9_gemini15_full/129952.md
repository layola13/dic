non-market:/ˈnɑːnˈmɑːrkɪt/ | adj. |非市场的，非市场化的|  Relating to activities or transactions that are not conducted through market mechanisms, such as buying and selling in a competitive marketplace.  These activities might be driven by factors other than supply and demand, like government regulation, social norms, or altruism. |例句：The government provided non-market funding for the project. 政府为该项目提供了非市场化的资金。

The provision of healthcare is often considered a non-market activity.  医疗保健的提供通常被认为是非市场活动。|近义词：unmarketable, noncommercial |反义词：market-based, commercial
