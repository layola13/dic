daimio:/ˈdaɪmiəʊ/| n. |大名, 日本封建时代的诸侯|A daimyo was a powerful feudal lord in Japan.  They controlled vast territories and armies, and were second only to the Shogun in power.  The daimyo system played a crucial role in shaping Japanese history and society during the Edo period. |Example: The daimyo Oda Nobunaga unified much of Japan through military conquest.  大名织田信长通过军事征服统一了日本大部分地区。|近义词:feudal lord, baron,noble|反义词:peasant, commoner|名词
