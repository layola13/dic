significate:/ˈsɪɡnɪfɪkeɪt/| vt.| 意味；表示；象征 | To signify; to mean; to represent.  The symbol signifies peace.  这个符号象征和平。|近义词：indicate, denote, represent, imply | 反义词：obscure, conceal


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The symbol signifies peace. = 这个符号象征和平。
The gesture signifies agreement. = 这个手势表示同意。


一般过去时:
The painting signified a new era in art. = 这幅画标志着艺术新纪元的到来。
His silence signified his disapproval. = 他的沉默表示他的反对。


现在完成时:
The discovery has signified a major breakthrough in science. = 这一发现标志着科学上的一大突破。
Her actions have signified a change in her attitude. = 她的行为表明她的态度发生了变化。


过去完成时:
The ancient carvings had signified a powerful civilization. = 古代的雕刻象征着一个强大的文明。
His earlier writings had signified his growing discontent. = 他早期的著作表明了他日益增长的不满。


将来时:
The new law will signify a shift in government policy. = 新法将标志着政府政策的转变。
Her upcoming speech will signify her commitment to the cause. = 她即将发表的演讲将表明她对这一事业的承诺。


虚拟语气:
If this signified danger, we should have been warned. = 如果这意味着危险，我们应该得到警告。
If the results signified success, we would celebrate. = 如果结果意味着成功，我们将庆祝。


被动语态:
Peace was signified by the dove. =和平由鸽子象征。
The treaty was signified by the exchange of signatures. = 条约以交换签名的方式正式生效。


疑问句:
What does this gesture signify? = 这个手势意味着什么？
What does the rising flag signify? = 升起的旗帜意味着什么？


否定句:
The results did not signify a clear trend. = 结果并没有表明一个清晰的趋势。
His words did not signify his true intentions. = 他的话语并没有表达他真正的意图。


PHRASE->
signify one's approval/disapproval = 表示赞成/反对
signify one's intention = 表示意图
signify one's willingness = 表示意愿
signify one's consent = 表示同意
signify one's gratitude = 表示感谢
