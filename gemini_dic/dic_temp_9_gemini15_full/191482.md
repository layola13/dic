schlep:/ʃlɛp/ | v., n. | 费力地搬运；拖；艰难跋涉；讨厌的人或事 | To carry or drag something heavy or cumbersome; to travel or move something with difficulty; a tiresome or unpleasant person or thing. | 例句：I had to schlep all the groceries home. = 我不得不费力地把所有杂货搬回家。  He schlepped his way across the country. = 他艰难地跋涉穿越全国。 | 近义词：lug, haul, drag, trudge, burden | 反义词：glide, float, effortless


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He schleps his luggage to the airport. = 他把行李费力地搬到机场。
She schleps her heavy backpack to school every day. = 她每天费力地背着沉重的背包去上学。

一般过去时:
I schlepped the furniture up the stairs. = 我费力地把家具搬上楼梯。
They schlepped the equipment to the filming location. = 他们把设备拖到拍摄地点。

现在完成时:
He has schlepped all the boxes into the attic. = 他已经把所有的箱子都搬到阁楼上了。
She has schlepped through the snow to get here. = 她费力地穿过雪地来到这里。

过去完成时:
I had schlepped the groceries home before it started to rain. = 下雨前我已经把杂货搬回家了。
They had schlepped the heavy equipment for hours before taking a break. = 他们在休息之前已经费力地搬运重型设备好几个小时了。


将来时:
I will schlep the piano to the new apartment tomorrow. = 我明天要把钢琴搬到新公寓。
They will schlep the supplies to the remote village next week. = 他们下周要把物资运到偏远的村庄。

虚拟语气:
If I had a car, I wouldn't have to schlep all these bags. = 如果我有车，我就不用费力地搬运所有这些包了。
I wish I didn't have to schlep all these books to the library. = 我真希望不用费力地把所有这些书搬到图书馆。

被动语态:
The heavy boxes were schlepped to the storage unit. = 那些沉重的箱子被费力地搬运到储物单元。
The equipment will be schlepped to the construction site by truck. = 设备将由卡车运送到建筑工地。

疑问句:
Do you have to schlep all that stuff yourself? = 你必须自己费力地搬运所有那些东西吗？
Will someone schlep the groceries for me? = 会有人帮我搬杂货吗？

否定句:
I don't want to schlep all those bags. = 我不想费力地搬运所有那些包。
He didn't schlep the boxes himself; he hired someone. = 他没有自己搬箱子；他雇了人。

词源:
The word "schlep" is of Yiddish origin, derived from the verb "shlēpn," meaning "to drag" or "to carry."  It entered English in the 20th century and has become increasingly common, especially in informal settings.
