quarrels:/ˈkwɒrəl/| /ˈkwɔːrəl/| n. v.|争吵，口角|a noisy argument or disagreement, especially one between people who know each other well.|例句：They had a bitter quarrel about the inheritance. 他们为了遗产发生了激烈的争吵。|例句：The children were quarreling over a toy. 孩子们为了一个玩具而争吵。|近义词：dispute, argument, fight, squabble|反义词：agreement, harmony, concord|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They quarrel frequently. = 他们经常吵架。
We rarely quarrel. = 我们很少吵架。


一般过去时:
They quarreled loudly last night. = 他们昨晚大声吵了一架。
We quarreled over a silly matter. = 我们为一件小事争吵了。


现在完成时:
They have quarreled several times this week. = 他们本周已经吵了好几次架了。
We haven't quarreled in a long time. = 我们很久没吵架了。


过去完成时:
They had quarreled before I arrived. = 我到达之前他们就已经吵过架了。
We had never quarreled before that incident. = 在那件事发生之前我们从未吵过架。


将来时:
They will quarrel again, I'm sure. = 我肯定他们还会再吵。
We will avoid quarreling if possible. = 如果可能，我们会避免吵架。


虚拟语气:
If they hadn't quarreled, they would still be friends. = 如果他们没有吵架，他们仍然会是朋友。
I wish they wouldn't quarrel so much. = 我希望他们不要吵那么多架。


被动语态:
(The quarrel) was heard by the neighbors.  = 邻居们听到了争吵声。(Quarrel is rarely used passively)
(The matter) was quarreled over by the two brothers. = 这件事被两兄弟争吵过了。(More natural to use a different phrasing)


疑问句:
Are they quarreling again? = 他们又在吵架吗？
Why are you quarreling with him? = 你为什么要和他吵架？


否定句:
They don't quarrel often. = 他们不经常吵架。
We didn't quarrel at all. = 我们根本没有吵架。


PHRASE->
have a quarrel = 发生争吵
pick a quarrel = 找茬儿
quarrel with someone = 与某人争吵
quarrel about something = 为某事争吵
quarrel over something = 为某事争吵
