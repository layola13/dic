reflexity:/ˌriːflɛkˈsɪti/| n. |自反性；反身性；反射性 |The property of being reflexive; the state or quality of being self-referential or self-reflective.  Reflexivity involves a relationship between an entity and itself, often highlighting the circular or interconnected nature of things.  It's a key concept in various fields, including sociology, linguistics, and philosophy. | Example: The reflexivity of the text is evident in its constant referencing of its own creation and limitations.  这段文字的自反性体现在它不断地提及自身的创作和局限性。 |近义词：self-reference, introspection, self-reflection |反义词：objectivity, externality


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, typical verb tenses are not applicable.  Instead, we will illustrate its usage in different sentence structures.

Declarative Sentences:
The study highlighted the reflexivity of the social system.  这项研究突出了社会体系的自反性。

The author explores the reflexivity of identity formation in her work. 作者在她的作品中探讨了身份形成的自反性。


Interrogative Sentences:
Does the poem demonstrate reflexivity? 这首诗是否体现了自反性？

What aspects of the theory account for its reflexivity?  该理论的哪些方面解释了它的自反性？


Negative Sentences:
The experiment lacked reflexivity.  这个实验缺乏自反性。

The analysis failed to consider the reflexivity of the data.  该分析未能考虑数据的自反性。
