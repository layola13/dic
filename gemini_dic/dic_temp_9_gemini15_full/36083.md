coelenterata:/ˌsiːlənˈtɛrətə/| n. |腔肠动物门|The phylum Coelenterata, also known as Cnidaria, encompasses a diverse group of aquatic invertebrates characterized by radial symmetry, a gastrovascular cavity, and stinging cells called cnidocytes.  They include jellyfish, corals, sea anemones, and hydroids.  The term "coelenterata" is less frequently used in modern taxonomy, with "Cnidaria" being preferred.| Example:  The vibrant colors of the coelenterates on the coral reef captivated the divers.  腔肠动物在珊瑚礁上的鲜艳色彩吸引了潜水员。|近义词: Cnidaria |反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it's a taxonomic group)| n.
