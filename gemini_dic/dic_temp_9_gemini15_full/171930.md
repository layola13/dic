pupipara:/ˌpjuːpɪˈpærə/| n. |蛹寄生虫|A pupipara is a type of parasitic fly whose larvae develop within the body of the host, emerging as pupae.  These flies are characterized by their unique reproductive strategy, bypassing the free-living larval stage.  The term refers specifically to flies in the family Pupipara.|Example:  The sheep ked is a well-known example of a pupipara.|近义词： parasitic fly |反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with flies with free-living larvae.|名词
