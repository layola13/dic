post-punk:/ˌpoʊstˈpʌŋk/| adj. n.| 后朋克 | Post-punk is a genre of rock music that emerged in the late 1970s and early 1980s as a reaction against the perceived excesses of punk rock.  It's characterized by a more experimental and art-school approach, often incorporating elements of other genres like funk, dub, and avant-garde music. | Example: The band's music is often described as post-punk, blending dark melodies with driving rhythms.  后朋克乐队融合了黑暗的旋律和强劲的节奏。|近义词：new wave, alternative rock |反义词：punk rock (in the context of a reaction against it)
