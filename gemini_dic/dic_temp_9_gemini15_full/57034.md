doublethink:/ˈdʌbəlθɪŋk/| n.|双重思想，矛盾思想|The act of holding two contradictory beliefs simultaneously and accepting both as true, despite their logical incompatibility.  This is a concept from George Orwell's novel *Nineteen Eighty-Four*, where it is used to describe the mental gymnastics employed by citizens of Oceania to maintain loyalty to the Party.|例句：The Party's propaganda relies heavily on doublethink to control the population.  党派宣传严重依赖双重思想来控制人民。

例句：He simultaneously believed the war was necessary and that it was a terrible mistake, a perfect example of doublethink. 他同时认为战争是必要的，又认为它是一个可怕的错误，这是双重思想的完美例证。|近义词：hypocrisy, self-deception, cognitive dissonance|反义词：consistency, coherence, intellectual honesty|名词


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations.  It describes a state of mind or a process, not an action. Therefore, typical verb tenses are not applicable.
