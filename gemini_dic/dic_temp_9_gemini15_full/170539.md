pseudaesthesia:/ˌsuːdəsɛsˈθiːʒə/|/ˌsjuːdəsɛsˈθiːʒə/| n. |假感觉；感觉异常|A subjective sensation of tingling, pricking, or numbness, not due to external stimulation.  It's a sensory disorder where the patient experiences sensations that aren't caused by any external stimuli.  |Example: The patient complained of pseudaesthesia in her left arm, a feeling of pins and needles without any apparent physical cause.  患者抱怨她的左臂有假感觉，一种没有明显生理原因的针刺感。|近义词：paresthesia, dysesthesia|反义词：normal sensation, true sensation

USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense-related examples are not applicable.  The usage focuses on describing the experience of pseudaesthesia.


Example 1:  The doctor diagnosed the patient's numbness as pseudaesthesia, ruling out any neurological damage. 医生诊断病人的麻木为假感觉，排除了任何神经损伤。

Example 2:  After the surgery, the patient experienced temporary pseudaesthesia in her leg, a common side effect of the procedure.  手术后，病人腿部出现暂时性假感觉，这是手术的常见副作用。


PHRASE->
Phrases directly using "pseudaesthesia" are limited as it's a medical term.  Related phrases might focus on the symptoms:

feeling of pins and needles: 针刺感
tingling sensation: 刺痛感
numbness without cause: 无缘无故的麻木
sensory disorder: 感觉障碍
phantom limb sensation (in cases where it relates to missing limbs): 幻肢感
