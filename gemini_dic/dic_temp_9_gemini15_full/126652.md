nakba:/ˈnɑːkbə/ | n. |灾难；浩劫；大难|The Nakba refers to the 1948 Palestinian exodus, also known as the Palestinian catastrophe.  It marks the displacement of hundreds of thousands of Palestinians from their homes during the 1948 Arab–Israeli War. The term is highly charged and evokes strong emotions for Palestinians. |The Nakba is a defining event in Palestinian history. The Nakba led to the creation of the State of Israel and the displacement of a large portion of the Palestinian population. |近义词：catastrophe, calamity, disaster |反义词：blessing, salvation
