keels:/kiːlz/| n.|龙骨；船底；（船的）稳定性；|The keel is the central structural member of a ship, running lengthwise along the bottom.  It provides strength and stability.|The ship's keel struck a rock.|近义词：backbone, base|反义词：top, surface


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The keel supports the weight of the ship. = 龙骨支撑着船的重量。
The ship's keel runs the length of the hull. = 船的龙骨贯穿船体的全长。


一般过去时:
The keel of the boat was damaged in the storm. = 船的龙骨在暴风雨中受损。
The old ship's keel finally gave way. = 那艘老船的龙骨最终断裂了。


现在完成时:
The shipwrights have laid the keel for the new vessel. = 造船工已经为新船铺设了龙骨。
They have strengthened the keel to withstand rough seas. = 他们加固了龙骨以抵御汹涌的海浪。


过去完成时:
Before the launch, they had carefully inspected the keel. = 下水前，他们仔细检查了龙骨。
The keel had already been laid before the funding was secured. = 在资金到位之前，龙骨就已经铺设好了。


将来时:
They will lay the keel for the new yacht next month. = 他们下个月将为新游艇铺设龙骨。
The keel will provide stability even in heavy seas. = 龙骨即使在汹涌的海面上也能提供稳定性。


虚拟语气:
If the keel were stronger, the ship wouldn't have sunk. = 如果龙骨更坚固，船就不会沉了。
I wish the keel had been properly inspected before the voyage. = 我希望航行前龙骨能够得到适当的检查。
