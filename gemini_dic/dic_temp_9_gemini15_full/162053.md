pre-Aryan:/ˌpriːˈɛəriən/| adj.| 前雅利安人的；雅利安人之前的 | Relating to the period or people in Europe before the arrival of the Indo-European speakers generally referred to as Aryans.  The term is often used in discussions of archaeology, linguistics, and anthropology to describe cultures and languages predating the Indo-European expansion.  The exact nature and extent of Pre-Aryan cultures remains a subject of ongoing research and debate. | Example:  Pre-Aryan settlements have been discovered in various parts of Europe.  前雅利安人的定居点在欧洲各地被发现。|近义词：pre-Indo-European |反义词：Aryan, Indo-European
