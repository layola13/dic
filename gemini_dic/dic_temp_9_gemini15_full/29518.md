catastrophe:/kəˈtæstrəfi/| n. |大灾难；灾祸 | A sudden and widespread disaster.  A great and sudden misfortune; calamity. |例句：The earthquake was a catastrophe that devastated the city.  这场地震是一场摧毁了这座城市的巨大灾难。

例句：The failure of the harvest was a catastrophe for the farmers. 歉收对农民来说是一场灾难。

近义词：disaster, calamity, cataclysm, debacle

反义词：blessing, good fortune, success

The flood represents a catastrophe for the coastal communities. = 洪水对沿海社区来说是一场灾难。

一般过去时:

The eruption of Mount Vesuvius was a catastrophe that buried Pompeii.  = 维苏威火山的爆发是一场掩埋了庞贝城的灾难。

If precautions are not taken, a severe environmental catastrophe will result. = 如果不采取预防措施，就会导致严重的生态灾难。
