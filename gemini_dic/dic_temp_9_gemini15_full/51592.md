devaluing: /ˌdiːˈvæljuɪŋ/ | v. | 贬值；降低价值；轻视 |

英文解析：To devalue something means to reduce its value or importance.  This can refer to currency, assets, or even ideas and principles.

例句：

The government is accused of devaluing the local currency. = 政府被指控贬值当地货币。

They devalued his contributions to the project. = 他们贬低了他对项目的贡献。


近义词：depreciating, undermining, belittling, discounting, cheapening

反义词：appreciating, valuing, enhancing, elevating


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company is constantly devaluing its brand. = 公司不断地贬低其品牌价值。
He devalues other people's opinions. = 他贬低别人的意见。


一般过去时:
The central bank devalued the currency last year. = 央行去年贬值了货币。
She felt devalued after her boss ignored her suggestions. = 在老板忽视她的建议后，她感到自己的价值被贬低了。


现在完成时:
Inflation has devalued savings significantly. = 通货膨胀已大幅贬值储蓄。
They have devalued the importance of education in recent years. = 近年来，他们贬低了教育的重要性。


过去完成时:
The company had already devalued its assets before the merger. = 在合并之前，该公司已经贬值了其资产。
He felt that his work had been devalued by the company's new policies. = 他觉得公司的新政策贬低了他的工作价值。


将来时:
The government will likely devalue the currency to boost exports. = 政府可能会贬值货币以促进出口。
They predict that the new technology will devalue existing infrastructure. = 他们预测新技术将贬低现有基础设施的价值。


虚拟语气:
If they devalued the currency further, it would cause economic chaos. = 如果他们进一步贬值货币，将会导致经济混乱。
I wish they hadn't devalued my efforts. = 我真希望他们没有贬低我的努力。


被动语态:
The currency was devalued to improve the trade balance. = 货币被贬值以改善贸易平衡。
His ideas were devalued by his colleagues. = 他的想法被他的同事贬低了。


疑问句:

否定句:
