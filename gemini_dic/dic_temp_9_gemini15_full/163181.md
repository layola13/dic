precompensating: /ˌpriːˈkɒmpənseɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 预先补偿的，提前补偿的 |  Acting to compensate in advance for something expected to happen.  This term implies taking action beforehand to mitigate the effects of a future event.  | 例句：The precompensating strategy involved investing heavily in infrastructure before the anticipated population boom. = 预先补偿策略包括在预期人口激增之前大力投资基础设施。


The company implemented a precompensating policy for potential losses due to the upcoming lawsuit. = 公司实施了一项预先补偿政策，以应对即将到来的诉讼可能造成的损失。| 近义词：anticipatory, proactive, preventative | 反义词：reactive, belated, delayed |  词性：形容词
