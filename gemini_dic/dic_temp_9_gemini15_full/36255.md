cognoscibility:/ˌkɒɡnɒsɪˈbɪləti/ | n. |可知性，可认识性 |The ability or possibility of being known or understood.  It refers to the extent to which something can be grasped by the intellect.  The term often arises in discussions of epistemology and the limits of human knowledge. | Example: The cognoscibility of the universe is a question that has puzzled philosophers for centuries.  宇宙的可知性是几个世纪以来困扰哲学家的一个问题。|近义词：knowability, understandability |反义词：unknowability, incomprehensibility |名词
