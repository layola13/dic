quack:/kwæk/| n., v.| 骗子；庸医；鸭叫声；吹牛；发出鸭叫声 |A quack is someone who pretends to have medical skills or knowledge but does not.  It can also refer to a loud, harsh cry like that of a duck, or boastful talk. |例句：He was exposed as a quack doctor. 他被揭露为一个庸医。|例句：The duck quacked loudly. 鸭子大声嘎嘎叫。|近义词：charlatan, impostor, fraud, faker|反义词：expert, professional, genuine


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He quacks like a duck. = 他像鸭子一样嘎嘎叫。
That doctor is a quack; he doesn't know anything. = 那个医生是个庸医；他什么都不懂。


一般过去时:
The duck quacked all night. = 鸭子整夜嘎嘎叫。
He quacked his way to fame. = 他靠吹嘘成名。


现在完成时:
He has quacked about his accomplishments all day. = 他整天都在吹嘘他的成就。
She has discovered that he's a quack. = 她已经发现他是个骗子。


过去完成时:
They had quacked about the deal for weeks before it fell through. = 在交易失败之前，他们已经吹嘘了好几个星期。
I had never heard such quackery before. = 我以前从未听过如此荒谬的言论。


将来时:
He will quack when he sees the food. = 他看到食物就会嘎嘎叫。
They will surely expose him as a quack. = 他们一定会揭露他是庸医。


虚拟语气:
If he were a real doctor, he wouldn't quack so much. = 如果他是个真正的医生，他就不会夸夸其谈。
I wish he hadn't quacked so much about his supposed expertise. = 我真希望他不要夸夸其谈他的所谓专业知识。


被动语态:
His claims were quacked down by the medical community. = 他的说法被医学界驳斥了。
The quackery was exposed by an investigative journalist. = 骗局被一位调查记者揭露了。


疑问句:
Does he quack when he's happy? = 他高兴的时候会嘎嘎叫吗？
Is he really a quack or just a misunderstood genius? = 他真的是个骗子还是一个被误解的天才？


否定句:
The duck didn't quack this morning. = 鸭子今天早上没有嘎嘎叫。
He didn't quack about his success; he was modest. = 他没有吹嘘他的成功；他很谦虚。


PHRASE->
Quack doctor = 庸医
Quackery = 庸医行为；江湖骗术
Quacking duck = 嘎嘎叫的鸭子
To quack something up = (非正式) 随意地或不熟练地做某事
A quack remedy = 骗人的疗法
