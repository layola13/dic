molds:/moʊldz/| n. v.|模具；霉菌；塑造；使成形|

英文解析:
Noun: A mold is a hollow container used to give a specific shape to something, such as plastic or metal.  It can also refer to a fungus that grows in damp places, often appearing as a fuzzy growth on food or other organic matter. As a verb, it means to shape or form something into a particular shape.

例句:
The baker used a special mold to create the intricate cake design. = 面包师用特殊的模具制作了复杂的蛋糕设计。

The bread had started to grow mold. = 面包开始发霉了。

The sculptor molded the clay into a beautiful statue. = 雕塑家将粘土塑造成一尊美丽的雕像。

The hot metal was molded into car parts. = 滚烫的金属被铸造成汽车零件。


近义词:
(noun) forms, shapes, casts, fungi, mildew
(verb) shape, form, fashion, cast, shape


反义词:
(noun)  None directly opposite, but "cleanliness" or "sterility" are contrasting concepts in relation to mold as a fungus.
(verb)  destroy, dismantle, deconstruct


词性:
Noun, Verb
