habituation:/ˌhæbɪtʃuˈeɪʃən/ | n. |习惯化，适应 |The process of becoming accustomed to a stimulus.  It's a decrease in response to a stimulus after repeated presentations. This is a different process than sensory adaptation, which is a decrease in sensitivity to a stimulus.  |Example: Habituation to the loud noise of the city traffic allowed her to sleep through the night. 习惯城市交通的喧嚣声让她能够睡个整夜觉。|近义词：acclimation, adaptation |反义词：sensitization

USAGE->
一般现在时:
Habituation occurs gradually over time. = 习惯化会随着时间的推移逐渐发生。
My dog shows habituation to the doorbell. = 我的狗对门铃表现出习惯化。


一般过去时:
Habituation to the cold weather developed quickly. = 对寒冷天气的适应很快形成。
The patient showed habituation after several sessions of therapy. = 病人在几次治疗后表现出习惯化。


现在完成时:
The baby has shown habituation to the loud sounds. = 宝宝已经对响亮的声音表现出习惯化。
She has habituated to the long working hours. = 她已经适应了长时间的工作。


过去完成时:
He had habituated to the smells before moving in. = 他在搬进来之前已经适应了那些气味。
The animals had habituated to the presence of the researchers. = 动物们已经习惯了研究人员的存在。


将来时:
The new employee will habituate to the company culture soon. = 新员工很快就会适应公司文化。
They will habituate to the new schedule in a few weeks. = 他们几周后就会适应新的时间表。


虚拟语气:
If he had habituated to the environment, he would have been more comfortable. = 如果他已经适应了环境，他会更舒服。

被动语态:

否定句:

He hasn't habituated to the noise yet. = 他还没适应噪音。
