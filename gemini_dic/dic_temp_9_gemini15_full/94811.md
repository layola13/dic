impulsivist:/ɪmˈpʌlsɪvɪst/| n. |冲动主义者；强调冲动的人|A person who acts impulsively and believes that impulsive action is important or even morally superior.  An impulsivist prioritizes immediate gratification over long-term planning and consequences. They might argue that spontaneity and intuition are valuable qualities, outweighing the risks associated with unplanned behavior. | Example: Some philosophers are impulsivists, believing that acting on instinct is more authentic than deliberative reasoning.  一些哲学家是冲动主义者，他们认为凭直觉行事比深思熟虑更真实。|近义词：impulsive person, spontaneous person |反义词：deliberatist, planner
