sacrosanct:/ˈsækrəʊsæŋkt/ | adj. | 神圣不可侵犯的；极其神圣的 | Extremely sacred; inviolable.  The tradition is sacrosanct and must be preserved.  | 近义词：sacred, inviolable, hallowed, holy | 反义词：profane, unholy, desecrated


英文解析：The word "sacrosanct" signifies something that is so sacred or holy that it must not be touched, violated, or questioned. It implies a level of reverence and protection far exceeding mere sanctity.  It often refers to principles, beliefs, or traditions that are considered inviolable and beyond criticism or alteration.


例句：

The sanctity of the Sabbath is sacrosanct in many religions. = 许多宗教都认为安息日的圣洁是神圣不可侵犯的。

He treated his personal privacy as something sacrosanct. = 他把自己的个人隐私看作是神圣不可侵犯的。


词源:

The word "sacrosanct" is derived from Latin:

* *"sacer"* (sacred, holy)
* *"sanctus"* (holy, sacred)

The combination implies a double emphasis on holiness and inviolability.  It literally means "holy of holies".
