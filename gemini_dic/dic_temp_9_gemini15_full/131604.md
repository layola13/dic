noncomplaisant: /ˌnɒnkəmˈpleɪznt/ | adj. | 不讨好的；不顺从的；不乐意的 | Unwilling to comply or cooperate; not obliging; unaccommodating.  A noncomplaisant person is typically unresponsive to the requests or desires of others, often exhibiting a degree of stubbornness or resistance. | 例句：His noncomplaisant attitude made it difficult to work with him.  他的不合作态度使和他一起工作变得很困难。


例句：The noncomplaisant child refused to eat his vegetables. 这个不听话的孩子拒绝吃蔬菜。


近义词：uncooperative, unaccommodating, unresponsive, intractable, recalcitrant, disobliging


反义词：complaisant, obliging, accommodating, cooperative, amenable


词源:  The word is derived from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "complaisant," which ultimately comes from the Old French word "complaisant," meaning "pleasing" or "agreeable."  The Old French word itself stems from the verb "complaire," meaning "to please" or "to gratify."
