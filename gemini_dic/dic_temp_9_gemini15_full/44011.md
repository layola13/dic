criminative: /ˈkrɪmɪneɪtɪv/ | adj. | 犯罪的；谴责的；控告的 | Accusatory; tending to accuse or condemn; implying guilt.  The word suggests an act of accusing someone of a crime or wrongdoing, often with an implication of guilt.  It focuses on the act of accusation itself, rather than the crime committed. |

例句：

His criminative tone implied he believed his brother was guilty.  他的语气带有谴责意味，暗示他认为他兄弟有罪。

The criminative evidence presented in court was overwhelming. 法庭上提出的犯罪证据是压倒性的。


近义词：accusatory, incriminating, condemnatory, inculpatory

反义词：exculpatory, exonerative, justifying


词源:

The word "criminative" derives from the Latin word "crimen," meaning "crime" or "accusation."  The suffix "-ative" is added to form the adjective, indicating a tendency or quality of being related to crime or accusation.


用法说明:  The word "criminative" is primarily used as an adjective to describe something that accuses or implies guilt. It's less common than words like "accusatory" or "incriminating," but it carries a slightly stronger connotation of implying guilt or condemnation.  It's often used in formal writing or legal contexts.
