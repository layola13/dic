cognizably:/ˈkɒɡnɪzəbli/ | /ˈkɑːɡnɪzəbli/ | adj. | 可感知地；可认识地；可辨认地 |  Able to be perceived or recognized; noticeable.  | The difference between the two products is readily cognizably.  两个产品之间的差异很容易被察觉。 The damage to the car was clearly cognizably. 车辆的损坏是显而易见的。| noticeably, perceptibly, discernibly | imperceptibly, indiscernibly | 形容词


词源:

The word "cognizably" derives from the verb "cognize," which in turn comes from the Latin word "cognoscere," meaning "to know" or "to learn."  The suffix "-ably" indicates the possibility or capability of being done.


用法说明:

"Cognizably" is an adverb that describes something that is easily perceived or understood. It emphasizes the ability to recognize or become aware of something.  It often implies a degree of clarity or distinctness.


例句:

The improvement in her health was readily cognizably.  她的健康状况改善明显可见。

The error in the calculation was not immediately cognizably. 计算中的错误并非立即就能察觉。


近义词:

noticeably, perceptibly, discernibly, recognizably, appreciably


反义词:

imperceptibly, indiscernibly, unnoticeably, imperceivably
