listed:/ˈlɪstɪd/ | adj. | 列出的，登记的 |  Being included in a list; recorded or mentioned in a list. | Example: The listed companies are all financially sound.  列出的公司都财务稳健。|近义词：enumerated, cataloged, itemized | 反义词：unlisted, omitted


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The items are listed in alphabetical order. = 这些项目按字母顺序排列。
The ingredients are listed on the back of the package. = 配料列在包装的背面。


一般过去时:
He listed all his assets on the document. = 他在文件中列出了他的所有资产。
The names of the participants were listed on the whiteboard. = 参与者的姓名写在白板上。


现在完成时:
She has listed her house for sale. = 她已将她的房子挂牌出售。
They have listed all the potential risks in their report. = 他们在报告中列出了所有潜在的风险。


过去完成时:
He had listed his car before he decided to keep it. = 他在决定保留他的车之前已经把它列出来了。
The committee had listed several options before making a decision. = 委员会在做出决定之前列出了几个选项。


将来时:
We will list the new products on our website next week. = 我们下周将在我们的网站上列出新产品。
They will list the company's achievements in the annual report. = 他们将在年度报告中列出公司的成就。


虚拟语气:
If I listed all my problems, it would take all day. = 如果我把我的所有问题都列出来，那将要花一整天的时间。
I wish I had listed the ingredients before I started cooking. = 我希望我在开始做饭之前已经列出了配料。


被动语态:
The names were listed alphabetically. = 这些名字按字母顺序排列。
The company's products are listed on the stock exchange. = 该公司的产品在证券交易所上市。


疑问句:
Are all the participants listed? = 所有参与者都列出来了？
What items are listed on the agenda? = 议程上列出了哪些项目？


否定句:
The item wasn't listed in the catalog. = 该项目未在目录中列出。
His name was not listed among the winners. = 他的名字没有列在获奖者名单中。


PHRASE->
listed company = 上市公司
to be listed = 被列入
to list prices = 列出价格
short listed = 入围的
to list the pros and cons = 列出优缺点
