prerestraining: /ˌpriːrɪˈstreɪnɪŋ/ | adj. | 预先约束的；预先限制的 |  Describing something that is done before a restraint is imposed, often to prevent or mitigate a negative outcome. It implies a proactive measure taken in anticipation of a potential problem.  | Example: Prerestraining measures were put in place to prevent the spread of the virus. 预先采取的措施是为了防止病毒传播。 | 近义词：preemptive, preventative, prophylactic | 反义词：reactive, post-hoc
