forbade:/fərˈbeɪd/| vt.|禁止，不准|To forbid something means to order that it must not be done, or that it is not allowed.  It implies a strong prohibition, often by an authority figure.|The king forbade the marriage.|近义词：prohibit, prevent, ban|反义词：allow, permit


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The school forbids smoking on campus. = 学校禁止在校园内吸烟。
My parents forbid me from watching TV on weekdays. = 我父母禁止我在工作日看电视。


一般过去时:
The teacher forbade the students from leaving early. = 老师禁止学生提前离开。
He forbade his daughter to date that boy. = 他禁止他女儿与那个男孩约会。


现在完成时:
The government has forbidden the import of certain goods. = 政府已经禁止进口某些商品。
They have forbidden me from entering the building. = 他们已经禁止我进入大楼。


过去完成时:
He had forbidden her from seeing him again before he left. = 他离开前已经禁止她再次见他。
The law had forbidden the sale of alcohol to minors for many years. = 多年来，法律一直禁止向未成年人出售酒精。


将来时:
The council will forbid the construction of new high-rise buildings in the area. = 理事会将禁止在该地区建造新的高层建筑。
They will forbid us from accessing the classified documents. = 他们将禁止我们访问机密文件。


虚拟语气:
If he forbade me to go, I wouldn't go. = 如果他禁止我去，我就不去。
I wish he hadn't forbidden me from attending the party. = 我真希望他没有禁止我参加聚会。


被动语态:
Smoking is forbidden in this building. = 这栋楼禁止吸烟。
Access to the files was forbidden. = 访问这些文件是被禁止的。


疑问句:
Is smoking forbidden here? = 这里禁止吸烟吗？
Was she forbidden from attending the meeting? = 她被禁止参加会议了吗？


否定句:
The law doesn't forbid the use of mobile phones in classrooms. = 法律并没有禁止在教室使用手机。
He didn't forbid us to leave. = 他没有禁止我们离开。


PHRASE->
forbid someone from doing something = 禁止某人做某事
forbid the sale of something = 禁止销售某物
forbid entry = 禁止进入
forbid access = 禁止访问
forbid contact = 禁止接触
