firebreak:/ˈfaɪərbreɪk/| n.|防火线，隔离带|A firebreak is a gap made in vegetation or other combustible material to stop the spread of a fire.  It acts as a barrier, preventing flames from crossing and consuming large areas.  Firebreaks can be created by removing vegetation, plowing furrows, or creating other types of barriers.|例句：The firefighters created a firebreak to contain the wildfire. 消防队员创建了一条防火线来控制野火。

The farmers plowed a wide firebreak around their fields to protect their crops. 农民们在田地周围犁出了一条宽阔的防火线来保护他们的庄稼。|近义词：fire lane, fireguard|反义词：fuel, accelerant|名词
