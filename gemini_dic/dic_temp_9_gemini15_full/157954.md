planetoidal:/ˌplænɪˈtɔɪdəl/| n. |类行星的，小行星的 |A planetoid is a celestial body that is similar to a planet but is smaller and often irregular in shape.  It is often used interchangeably with the term "asteroid," though there can be subtle distinctions in scientific usage.  Planetoids are typically found in asteroid belts or orbiting other celestial bodies. |Example:  Many planetoids orbit the sun in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.  许多小行星在火星和木星之间的小行星带围绕太阳运行。|近义词：asteroid, minor planet |反义词：planet (in terms of size and established definition)
