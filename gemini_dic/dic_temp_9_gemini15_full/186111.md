rhabdophora:/ræbˈdɔːfərə/| n. |鞭毛纲动物|A class of annelids characterized by the presence of chaetae (bristles) arranged in bundles on parapodia (fleshy appendages).  They are mostly marine worms, exhibiting a wide range of lifestyles and morphologies.  |Example:  Many species within the Rhabdophora are ecologically important as benthic organisms.  许多鞭毛纲动物作为底栖生物在生态上很重要。|近义词:Polychaeta (a broader term encompassing Rhabdophora)|反义词:None (it's a taxonomic classification)|名词
