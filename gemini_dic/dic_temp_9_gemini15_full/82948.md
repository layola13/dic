gunboats:/ˈɡʌnˌboʊts/ | n. |炮艇 |A small warship with powerful guns, typically used for river or coastal patrol and bombardment.  Gunboats are designed for shallow-water operations and often have a shallow draft to navigate rivers and inlets. They are smaller and more maneuverable than larger warships. | Example:  The river was patrolled by several gunboats to prevent smuggling.  这条河由几艘炮艇巡逻以防止走私。


The imperial navy used gunboats to project power along the Yangtze River.  帝国海军利用炮艇在长江沿岸展示实力。|近义词：warship, patrol boat |反义词：battleship (in terms of size and capability)

词源:
The word "gunboat" is a compound word, combining "gun" (a weapon that fires projectiles) and "boat" (a small vessel).  Its origin can be traced back to the 19th century when such vessels were increasingly used for projecting military power, especially in colonial contexts.
