cashability:/ˌkæʃəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可兑现性，可变现性 | The ability of an asset to be quickly and easily converted into cash.  This refers to how readily something can be sold or exchanged for cash without significant loss of value.  It considers factors like liquidity, market demand, and transaction costs. | Example: The cashability of the bonds was a key factor in the investment decision.  债券的可兑现性是投资决策中的一个关键因素。  The high cashability of government bonds makes them attractive to investors. 政府债券的高可兑现性使其对投资者具有吸引力。| 近义词：liquidity, convertibility, negotiability | 反义词：illiquidity, non-convertibility | 名词
