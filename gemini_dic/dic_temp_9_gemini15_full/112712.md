loxodromism:/ˈlɒksədrɒmɪzəm/| n. |斜航，恒向航线|The science or practice of sailing or flying on a rhumb line (loxodrome).  It refers to the course of a vessel following a constant compass bearing, resulting in a curved path on a map projection.  This path is a logarithmic spiral that intersects all meridians at the same angle. |Example:  The navigator calculated the loxodromism to reach the destination efficiently, despite the resulting longer distance.  =  领航员计算了斜航路线以有效到达目的地，尽管这导致了更长的距离。|近义词：rhumb line navigation, constant bearing navigation|反义词：great-circle navigation|
