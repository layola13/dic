conduit:/ˈkɒndjuːt/ /ˈkɑːnduɪt/| n. |管道；导管；渠道；途径|A conduit is a pipe, tube, or channel through which something can pass.  It can refer to physical structures like pipes for water or electricity, or metaphorical channels for information or influence. |Example: The water flowed through the conduit. 水流经管道。The internet serves as a conduit for information.互联网是信息的渠道。|近义词：channel, duct, pipe, pipeline|反义词：barrier, obstruction


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The pipes conduit water to the building. = 管道将水输送到建筑物。
This river acts as a conduit for migrating fish. = 这条河是候鸟迁徙的通道。


一般过去时:
The old conduit leaked badly. = 旧管道严重漏水。
The company used a secret conduit to transfer funds. = 公司利用秘密渠道转移资金。


现在完成时:
They have installed a new conduit for the electrical wiring. = 他们已经安装了一条新的电线管道。
This organization has become a conduit for political influence. = 这个组织已经成为政治影响的渠道。


过去完成时:

The information had passed through several conduits before reaching the public. = 信息在到达公众之前已经通过多个渠道传播。


将来时:
