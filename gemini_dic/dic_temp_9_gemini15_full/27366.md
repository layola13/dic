cambrobriton:/ˌkæmbrəˈbrɪtən/| n. |不列颠-坎布里亚人，早期不列颠人|A term used to refer to the inhabitants of Britain during the Roman and post-Roman periods, encompassing both Britons (Celts) and the Romano-British population. The term reflects a blend of Celtic and Roman influences on the culture and identity of the people living in Britain at that time.|The study of Cambrobriton culture reveals a fascinating blend of Celtic traditions and Roman innovations.|近义词：Briton, Romano-British, early Briton|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with later groups like Anglo-Saxons.|名词
