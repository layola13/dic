mothers-in-law: /ˈmʌðərzɪnˌlɔː/ | n. |岳母；婆婆；丈母娘|

英文解析:A mother-in-law is the mother of one's spouse.  The term can refer to either a husband's mother (in the case of a wife) or a wife's mother (in the case of a husband).  The plural form "mothers-in-law" refers to multiple such relationships.

例句:

My mother-in-law is visiting next week. = 我的岳母下周要来访。

I get along well with both my mothers-in-law. =我和我的两位岳母相处融洽。


近义词: spouse's mother

反义词:  无直接反义词，但可以考虑与"daughter-in-law" 或 "son-in-law"  形成对比关系


词性: Noun (名词)
