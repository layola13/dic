dilucid: /dɪˈluːsɪd/ | vt. |阐明；解释；说明 | To explain something clearly; to make something clear. |例句：The professor lucidly elucidated the complex theory. 教授清晰地阐明了这个复杂的理论。

例句：The lawyer elucidated the points of the contract to the client. 律师向客户阐明了合同的要点。


近义词：explain, clarify, illuminate, explicate | 反义词：obscure, confuse, obfuscate | 词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He elucidates the problem every time. 他每次都阐明这个问题。
The teacher elucidates the new concept to her students.老师向学生阐明新的概念。


一般过去时:

The speaker elucidated his points with examples. 演讲者用例子阐明了他的观点。


现在完成时:
He has elucidated the mystery of the disappearance. 他已经阐明了失踪的谜团。
The scientist has elucidated the process of photosynthesis. 科学家已经阐明了光合作用的过程。


过去完成时:
They had elucidated the cause of the accident before the report was written. 他们在报告撰写之前已经阐明了事故的原因。
The detective had elucidated the case by the time the trial began.  侦探在审判开始时已经阐明了案件。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

词源:
From Latin *ēlūcīdāre, from ē- "out" + lūcīdāre "to make bright," from lūx, lūcis "light."  The word ultimately traces back to the Indo-European root *leuk-, meaning "light" or "bright."
