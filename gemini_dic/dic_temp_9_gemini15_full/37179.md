comed:/kʌmd/| v.|来过，来到；出现；开始|To have come; arrived; appeared; begun.  The word "comed" is an archaic or dialectal past tense form of "come," and is rarely used in modern English.|例句：He comed to the party late. 他来晚了的聚会。|近义词：arrived, appeared, began|反义词：left, departed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I come to school every day. = 我每天都来学校。
They come here often. = 他们经常来这里。

一般过去时:
He came to my house yesterday. = 他昨天来我家了。
She came home late last night. = 她昨晚回家很晚。


现在完成时:
I have come to understand the problem. = 我已经开始理解这个问题了。
They have come a long way. = 他们已经走了很长一段路。


过去完成时:
I had come to a decision before he arrived. = 他到达之前我已经做出了决定。
She had come to realize her mistake. = 她已经意识到自己的错误了。


将来时:
I will come to see you tomorrow. = 我明天来看你。
He will come to the meeting next week. = 他下周会来参加会议。


虚拟语气:
If he came tomorrow, I would be happy. = 如果他明天来，我会很高兴。
I wish she had come to the party. = 我希望她来参加聚会。


被动语态:
The solution was come upon unexpectedly. (This is an unusual usage, better phrasing would be "The solution was found unexpectedly") = 这个解决方案是意外发现的。
The decision was come to after much deliberation. (This is an unusual usage, better phrasing would be "The decision was made after much deliberation")=经过长时间的考虑后做出了决定。


疑问句:
Did he come? = 他来了吗？
When will they come? = 他们什么时候来？


否定句:
He did not come to the meeting. = 他没有来参加会议。
She didn't come home until late. = 她直到很晚才回家。

PHRASE->
come across: 偶然遇见
come about: 发生
come along: 来吧
come back: 回来
come down with: 染上（疾病）
