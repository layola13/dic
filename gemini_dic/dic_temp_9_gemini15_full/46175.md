cyperaceous: /ˌsaɪpəˈreɪʃəs/ | adj. |莎草科的；像莎草的 | Belonging to or characteristic of the Cyperaceae, a family of plants including sedges.  The term often describes plants with grass-like or rush-like appearance, but with triangular stems which distinguish them from true grasses.  |例句：The marshy area was characterized by its dense cyperaceous vegetation.  沼泽地以其茂密的莎草科植物为特征。

The cyperaceous plants thrived in the wet conditions. 莎草科植物在潮湿的环境中茁壮成长。


近义词：sedge-like, rush-like (when referring to appearance) |反义词：无


词性：形容词


词源:  From "Cyperaceae," the botanical name for the sedge family.  The suffix "-aceous" indicates "belonging to" or "resembling."  The name "Cyperaceae" ultimately derives from the Greek word "kyperos," referring to a type of sedge.
