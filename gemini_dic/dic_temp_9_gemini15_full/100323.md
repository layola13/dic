interspersals:/ˌɪntərˈspɜːrslz/| n.| 间杂；散布；点缀 | The insertion of something of a different kind among things of the same kind; a thing inserted among others.  |例句：The interspersed jokes lightened the serious tone of the lecture.  间杂的笑话减轻了讲座严肃的基调。

例句：The interspersed flowers added beauty to the garden. 散布的花朵增加了花园的美感。


近义词：intermingling, mixture, scattering, insertions | 反义词：segregation, separation, isolation | 词性：名词

词源:
The word "intersperse" is derived from the Latin prefix "inter-" (between, among) and "spargere" (to scatter, to sprinkle).  Therefore, it literally means "to scatter among."
