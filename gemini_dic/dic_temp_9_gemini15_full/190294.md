sarcasm:/ˈsɑːrkæzəm/|/ˈsɑːrkæzəm/| n. |讽刺；嘲讽 |The use of irony to mock or convey contempt.  Sarcasm often involves stating the opposite of what one means, typically to express contempt, disapproval, or frustration.  It relies heavily on context and tone of voice to be understood. |例句：He said, "Oh, fantastic," with heavy sarcasm, clearly meaning the opposite. 他说：“哦，太棒了”，语气充满了讽刺，显然意思相反。

例句：Her sarcastic remark about his "brilliant" idea made him blush. 她对他“绝妙”的主意所作的讽刺性评论使他脸红了。


近义词：irony, satire, mockery, taunt, gibe |反义词：praise, compliment, flattery


词性：名词
