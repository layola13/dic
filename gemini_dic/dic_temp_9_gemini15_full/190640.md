satyrs:/ˈseɪtɑːrz/| n. |萨提尔，森林之神|Mythological creatures, typically depicted as having the head, torso, and arms of a man and the legs and ears of a horse or goat, often associated with wildness, revelry, and lust.  They are frequently portrayed as companions of Dionysus. |The satyrs danced wildly around the bonfire. 萨提尔们在篝火旁狂野地跳舞。|fauns, sileni|—|名词
