degenerationist: /ˌdiːdʒɛnəˈreɪʃənɪst/ | n. | 退化论者；衰退论者 | A degenerationist is someone who believes in degeneration, particularly the belief that humanity or a particular race is declining in quality or ability.  They might subscribe to theories suggesting a decline from a previous golden age or a loss of inherent capabilities.  The term often carries a negative connotation, implying a pessimistic or even fatalistic view of progress. | Example:  Some 19th-century thinkers were degenerationists, fearing the decline of the aristocracy and the rise of the masses.  一些19世纪的思想家是退化论者，担心贵族阶层的衰落和大众的兴起。 | 近义词:  pessimist (regarding societal progress), decline theorist | 反义词:  progressionist, optimist (regarding societal progress)
