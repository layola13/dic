gaping:/ˈɡeɪpɪŋ/| adj.|张开的；目瞪口呆的；巨大的；令人震惊的|Wide open;  characterized by a wide opening;  causing astonishment or wonder.|例句：The gaping hole in the wall was a result of the explosion.  墙上那个大洞是爆炸造成的。

例句：He stood there, gaping in disbelief at the sight before him. 他站在那里，难以置信地目瞪口呆地看着眼前的景象。


近义词：open, wide, agape, astonished, amazed, stunned|反义词：closed, shut, narrow, unimpressed


词源：源自古英语 *gapian “to yawn, gape”，与古高地德语 *gāpian “to yawn” 和古诺尔斯语 *gapa “to gape” 相关。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The door is gaping open. = 门大开着。
The wound is gaping. = 伤口裂开了。

一般过去时:
The crowd gaped at the magician. = 人群目瞪口呆地看着魔术师。
He gaped at the incredible view. = 他目瞪口呆地看着令人难以置信的景色。


现在完成时:
The children have been gaping at the television all afternoon. = 孩子们一下午都在目瞪口呆地看着电视。
The hole has been gaping for weeks. =  那个洞已经裂开好几个星期了。


过去完成时:
They had gaped at the painting for a long time before realizing it was a forgery. = 他们在意识到那幅画是赝品之前，已经目瞪口呆地看了很久。
She had gaped at the sight of the accident, speechless. = 她目瞪口呆地看着事故现场，说不出话来。


将来时:
They will gape when they see the finished project. = 当他们看到完成的项目时，他们会目瞪口呆。

虚拟语气:
If I had seen that gaping hole, I would have warned them. = 如果我看到那个大洞，我会警告他们的。
If the wound hadn't been gaping, it would have healed faster. = 如果伤口没有裂开，它就会愈合得更快。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

He didn't gape at the news. = 他对这个消息没有感到惊讶。
