rendition:/rɛnˈdɪʃən/| n. |演绎；表演；翻译；版本 |A performance or interpretation, especially of a musical work or a dramatic role; a particular version or translation of something.  |例句：Her rendition of the song was breathtaking.  她的歌曲演绎令人叹为观止。

例句：The new translation offers a fresh rendition of the classic text.  新的译本提供了这部经典文本的新颖版本。


近义词：performance, interpretation, version, translation, rendering

反义词：original (in the sense of the first or unadapted version)


词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The singer gives a beautiful rendition of the song. = 歌手演唱这首歌很美。
His rendition of Hamlet is quite impressive. = 他对哈姆雷特的演绎相当令人印象深刻。


一般过去时:
The orchestra gave a memorable rendition of Beethoven's Fifth Symphony. = 管弦乐队演奏贝多芬第五交响曲令人难忘。
She delivered a powerful rendition of the poem. = 她朗诵这首诗歌很动人。


现在完成时:
They have given many renditions of this piece. = 他们已经演奏过这首乐曲很多次了。
The actor has given several acclaimed renditions of the role. = 这位演员已经演出过几次广受好评的角色。


过去完成时:
Before the concert, they had already rehearsed their rendition many times. = 音乐会之前，他们已经排练过很多次他们的演奏了。
She had perfected her rendition of the aria before the audition. = 在试镜之前，她已经完善了她对咏叹调的演绎。


将来时:
The band will give a special rendition of their hit song. = 乐队将演唱他们热门歌曲的一个特别版本。
He will give a moving rendition of the speech at the ceremony. = 他将在仪式上进行一次感人的演讲。


虚拟语气:
If he gave a better rendition, he might have won the competition. = 如果他的演绎更好，他可能会赢得比赛。
I wish I had heard his rendition of that song. = 我真希望我听到过他演唱的那首歌。


被动语态:

The play's most famous scene was given a compelling rendition by the actors. = 这部剧最著名的场景由演员们进行了引人入胜的演绎。


疑问句:

否定句:
