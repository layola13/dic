butanoic:/ˌbjuːtəˈnɔɪɪk/ | adj. | 丁酸的；丁酸根的 | Butanoic refers to a chemical compound containing four carbon atoms in a straight chain, specifically butanoic acid (also known as butyric acid).  It describes derivatives or related compounds of this acid. |  The rancid smell of butter is due to the presence of butanoic acid.  |  butyric, valeric (related acids) |  | adjective
例句：

The rancid smell of butter is due to the presence of butanoic acid. = 黄油的酸败气味是由于丁酸的存在。

Butanoic esters are commonly used as flavoring agents in food. = 丁酸酯通常用作食品中的调味剂。


词源:  The name is derived from "butyr-" (from Greek βούτυρον, *búturon, "butter"), referring to the source of butyric acid (butanoic acid), plus the suffix "-oic," a standard chemical suffix indicating a carboxylic acid.
