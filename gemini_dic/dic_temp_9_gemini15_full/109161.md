legitimated:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmeɪtɪd/| v. |使合法；使正当；承认…为合法 |To make something legal or acceptable.  To give official approval to something. |例句：The government legitimated the new law by passing it through parliament. 政府通过议会批准使这项新法律合法化。

The court legitimated his claim to the property. 法院承认他拥有该房产的权利。


近义词：authorize, validate, justify, ratify |反义词：invalidate, disapprove, repudiate

词源：
From Latin *legitimatus, past participle of legitimare "to declare lawful," from lex (genitive legis) "law" + -timare (frequentative suffix).  The sense development is from "to declare lawful" to "to make lawful" and then to "to give official recognition or approval to".
