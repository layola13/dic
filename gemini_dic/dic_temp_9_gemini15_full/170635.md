pseudo-literary: /ˈsuːdoʊˈlɪtəreri/ | adj. | 似文雅的；伪文学的；装腔作势的 |  Appearing to be literary or cultured, but actually not genuine or sincere; affectedly literary. It often describes writing or speech that attempts to imitate literary styles but lacks genuine depth, originality, or artistic merit.  |  The novel was criticized for its pseudo-literary language and contrived plot. 这部小说因其做作的文学语言和人为的故事情节而受到批评。 | 近义词：pretentious, affected, bombastic, highfalutin | 反义词：genuine, authentic, sincere, unpretentious

词源:

* **pseudo-**:  From Greek *pseudēs*, meaning "false," "lying," or "deceitful."
* **literary**: From Latin *litterarius*, meaning "of or pertaining to letters or literature."


Therefore, "pseudo-literary" means "falsely literary" or "pretending to be literary."
