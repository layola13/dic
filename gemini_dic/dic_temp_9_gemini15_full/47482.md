deambulate:/diˈæmbjuleɪt/| v.|四处走动；漫步|To walk about; stroll.|例句：The elderly gentleman deambulated slowly through the park.  这位年长的绅士在公园里慢慢地散步。|例句：After the long flight, I needed to deambulate to relieve my stiffness. 长途飞行后，我需要四处走动以缓解僵硬。|近义词：walk, stroll, wander, ramble|反义词：remain stationary, stay put


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deambulates in the park every morning. = 他每天早上都在公园里散步。
They deambulate through the city streets. = 他们漫步在城市街道上。

一般过去时:
She deambulated along the beach at sunset. = 她日落时在海滩上漫步。
We deambulated through the ancient ruins. = 我们漫步在古老的废墟中。

现在完成时:
He has deambulated for miles today. = 他今天已经走了几英里路了。
They have deambulated all over the countryside. = 他们已经走遍了整个乡村。

过去完成时:
She had deambulated for hours before she found her way. = 她走了好几个小时才找到路。
They had deambulated through the forest before it got dark. = 天黑之前，他们已经在森林里走了很久。

将来时:
We will deambulate through the market tomorrow. = 我们明天将在市场上闲逛。
He will deambulate along the riverbank after lunch. = 他午餐后将在河边散步。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would deambulate through the museum. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会在博物馆里四处走走。
I wish I could deambulate freely without pain. = 我希望我能自由自在地行走，而不会感到疼痛。

被动语态:  (Deambulate is rarely used in the passive voice because it is an intransitive verb.)


疑问句:
Does he often deambulate in the evenings? = 他经常在晚上散步吗？
Will they deambulate to the beach today? = 他们今天会去海滩散步吗？

否定句:
He did not deambulate because of his injury. = 因为他受伤了，所以他没有散步。
She will not deambulate alone at night. = 她晚上不会独自散步。

deambulate peacefully = 安静地散步
