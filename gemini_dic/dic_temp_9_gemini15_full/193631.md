self-blames:/ˈsɛlf bleɪmz/| v.|自责，责备自己|To hold oneself responsible for something bad that has happened.  It implies a feeling of guilt or regret.|例句：She self-blames for the accident, even though it wasn't her fault. 她为那场事故自责，即使那不是她的错。|例句：He constantly self-blames for his past mistakes, hindering his personal growth. 他不断地为过去的错误自责，阻碍了他的个人成长。|近义词：reproach oneself, blame oneself, criticize oneself|反义词：self-congratulate, excuse oneself


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He self-blames for every failure. = 他为每一次失败而自责。
She self-blames for her poor performance. = 她为自己的糟糕表现而自责。


一般过去时:
He self-blamed for the lost deal. = 他为失去的交易而自责。
She self-blamed after the argument. = 争吵后她自责了。

He has self-blamed since the incident. = 自那件事发生以来，他一直自责。

过去完成时:

She had self-blamed all night before finally falling asleep. = 她自责了一整夜才终于睡着。

虚拟语气:

I wish I hadn't self-blamed for something that wasn't my fault. = 我真希望我没有为不是我错的事情自责。


被动语态:  (The verb "self-blame" is not typically used in the passive voice because it is a reflexive verb.)
