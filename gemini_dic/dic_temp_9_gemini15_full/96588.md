inductees:/ɪnˌdʌkˈtiːz/| n. |被引进者；新成员；入选者|People who have been formally admitted to a group or organization, often with a ceremony.  For example, new members of the military, a hall of fame, or a society.|例句：The inductees into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame this year are incredibly diverse. 今年入选摇滚名人堂的新成员非常多元化。

The inductees were sworn in during a solemn ceremony.  入选者在一个庄严的仪式上宣誓就职。|近义词：recipients, initiates, recruits|反义词：graduates (in the sense of leaving an organization)
