officiate:/əˈfɪʃieɪt/| v.|主持，担任职务，履行职责|To perform the duties of an office or position, especially in a religious or official ceremony.|例句：The priest will officiate at the wedding.  牧师将主持婚礼。|例句：The judge will officiate at the trial.  法官将主持审判。|近义词：preside, conduct, administer|反义词：refrain, abstain


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He officiates at many weddings each year. 他每年主持许多婚礼。
The mayor officiates at the city council meetings. 市长主持市议会会议。

一般过去时:
She officiated at her sister's wedding last month. 她上个月主持了她妹妹的婚礼。
The referee officiated the football game fairly. 裁判公平地主持了足球比赛。

现在完成时:
He has officiated at hundreds of weddings in his career. 他在他的职业生涯中主持了数百场婚礼。
The judge has officiated in many important cases. 法官主持过许多重要的案件。

过去完成时:
Before the new judge arrived, the old judge had officiated for twenty years. 新法官到来之前，老法官已经做了二十年的法官。
They had officiated the ceremony before the guests arrived. 客人到达之前，他们已经主持了仪式。

将来时:
She will officiate at the graduation ceremony next week. 她下周将主持毕业典礼。
The priest will officiate the baptism ceremony. 牧师将主持洗礼仪式。

虚拟语气:
If I were a priest, I would officiate at your wedding. 如果我是牧师，我会主持你们的婚礼。
I wish I could officiate at the ceremony, but I am busy. 我真希望我能主持仪式，但我太忙了。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
He did not officiate at the meeting. 他没有主持会议。
She refused to officiate at the controversial ceremony. 她拒绝主持这场有争议的仪式。
