gilding:/ˈɡɪldɪŋ/| n. v.|镀金；虚饰|The act of covering something with a thin layer of gold;  a deceptive or superficial attractiveness.|The gilding on the frame was starting to wear off.  镀金画框开始磨损了。The politician's promises were mere gilding, masking his true intentions. 这位政治家的承诺不过是虚饰，掩盖了他的真实意图。|plating, ornamenting|unvarnished, plain|名词,动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The artist is gilding the statue. = 艺术家正在给雕像镀金。
They gild the lilies. = 他们画蛇添足。


一般过去时:
He gilded the picture frame. = 他给画框镀了金。
She gilded the pill. = 她粉饰太平。


现在完成时:
They have gilded the dome of the cathedral. = 他们已经给大教堂的圆顶镀了金。
They have gilded their own reputations. = 他们已经粉饰了自己的名声。


过去完成时:
The craftsman had gilded the entire altar before the ceremony. = 工匠在仪式之前已经给整个祭坛镀了金。
They had gilded the truth, making it less believable. = 他们已经粉饰了真相，使其不太可信。


将来时:
We will gild the edges of the book. = 我们将给书的边缘镀金。
She will gild the lily with excessive praise. = 她将用过度的赞美来画蛇添足。


虚拟语气:
If I had the gold, I would gild the entire room. = 如果我有金子，我会把整个房间都镀金。
If he gilded the truth, he would be less convincing. = 如果他粉饰真相，他将更没有说服力。


被动语态:
The frame was gilded by a skilled artisan. = 画框是由一位技艺精湛的工匠镀金的。
The truth was gilded by the media. = 真相被媒体粉饰了。


疑问句:
Is the statue gilded with real gold? = 这尊雕像是用真金镀的吗？
Was the information gilded to make it more appealing? = 信息是否经过粉饰以使其更具吸引力？


否定句:
The building wasn't gilded, it was painted gold. = 这座建筑物不是镀金的，而是涂成金色的。
He didn't gild the lily; he told the truth. = 他没有画蛇添足；他说了实话。
