flamboyance: /flæmˈbɔɪəns/ | n. |炫耀；华丽；艳丽；浮夸 |  A showy and flamboyant display of something, especially of one's wealth or success;  extravagant or unrestrained behaviour.  | 例句：The flamboyance of his personality was immediately apparent. 他的个性张扬显而易见。

The flamboyance of his clothes was quite shocking. 他的衣着过于华丽，令人震惊。


近义词：ostentation, showiness, extravagance, exuberance | 反义词：restraint, modesty, simplicity


词源:  From Middle French *flambe, from Old French *flambe (meaning "flame"), from Vulgar Latin *flamma (meaning "flame"). The association with "flame" is related to the vibrant, bright, and often fiery appearance associated with flamboyance.
