geordies:/ˈdʒɔːdiz/| n. |乔迪人（泰恩河畔纽卡斯尔及其周边地区的人）| Geordies are people from Newcastle upon Tyne and the surrounding area in North East England.  They are known for their distinctive accent, which is often considered to be quite strong and difficult for outsiders to understand.  The term can also refer to the dialect itself.|例句：He's a Geordie, so he speaks with a strong Northumbrian accent. 他是乔迪人，所以他说一口浓重的诺森伯兰郡口音。|例句：I found the Geordie accent quite challenging to understand at first. 起初我发现乔迪口音很难理解。|近义词：Newcastlian (although this term is less common and perhaps less widely understood than Geordie)|反义词：无|名词
