show:/ʃoʊ/ /ʃəʊ/| vt. n.|展示，演出，表明|to reveal or display something to others; a public performance or display.|例句：Please show me your ID. 请出示您的身份证。

例句：The magician showed us a breathtaking illusion. 魔术师给我们展示了一个令人叹为观止的幻象。


近义词：display, demonstrate, exhibit, present|反义词：hide, conceal, obscure|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He shows his artwork every year. 他每年都展示他的艺术作品。
They show great enthusiasm for the project. 他们对这个项目表现出极大的热情。


一般过去时:
She showed me her new dress. 她给我看她的新衣服。
The teacher showed the students how to solve the problem.老师向学生们展示了如何解决这个问题。


现在完成时:
He has shown remarkable improvement. 他已经展现出显著的进步。
They have shown their support for the cause. 他们已经表达了对这项事业的支持。


过去完成时:
She had shown me the photos before I left.在我离开之前，她已经给我看过照片了。
They had shown their willingness to cooperate.他们已经表现出合作的意愿。


将来时:
We will show you the best way to get there. 我们将向你展示到达那里的最佳路线。
They will show their skills at the competition. 他们将在比赛中展示他们的技能。


虚拟语气:
If he showed more effort, he would succeed. 如果他付出更多努力，他就会成功。
I wish she showed more understanding. 我希望她能表现出更多的理解。


被动语态:
The painting was shown at the gallery. 这幅画在画廊展出。
The results were shown to be inaccurate. 结果被证明是不准确的。


疑问句:
Will you show me how to do it? 你能教我怎么做吗？
What time does the movie show? 电影几点开始放映？


否定句:
He didn't show any interest. 他没有表现出任何兴趣。
She didn't show up for the meeting. 她没有参加会议。


PHRASE->
show off:炫耀
show up:出现
show around:带...参观
put on a show:上演演出
show one's hand:摊牌


词源:
The word "show" originates from Old English *sceawian, meaning "to look at, behold, observe," related to "sceawu," meaning "sight, appearance, show."  The modern sense evolved gradually from the idea of looking at something to the act of presenting or displaying something to be looked at.
