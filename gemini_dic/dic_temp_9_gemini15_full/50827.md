descriptiveness:/dɪˈskrɪptɪvnəs/ | n. |描述性，描绘性 |The quality of being descriptive; the extent to which something is descriptive.  It refers to the ability to convey information or create an image using vivid and detailed language.  |例句：The descriptiveness of the novel's prose brought the characters and setting to life. 小说的散文描述性使人物和场景栩栩如生。

The descriptiveness of her account left no doubt about what had happened.  她描述的生动性使发生的事情毫无疑问。


近义词：detail, vividness, graphicness, expressiveness | 反义词：vagueness, ambiguity, brevity


词源:  Derived from the adjective "descriptive," which comes from the verb "describe" (from Old French *descrivre, from Latin dēscribere, "to write down," from de- "down" + scribere "to write").  The suffix "-ness" is added to create the noun form, indicating a quality or state.
