deceptive:/dɪˈsɛptɪv/| adj.|欺骗性的，迷惑人的|giving an appearance or impression different from the true one; misleading.|The advertisement was deceptive, making false claims about the product's effectiveness.  这则广告具有欺骗性，对产品的有效性做出了虚假声明。|近义词：misleading, illusory, fraudulent|反义词：truthful, honest, genuine


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The salesman's words are deceptive. = 销售员的话是具有欺骗性的。
This situation is deceptively simple. = 这种情况表面上看起来很简单。


一般过去时:
His actions were deceptive. = 他的行为具有欺骗性。
The painting was deceptively realistic. = 这幅画具有迷惑性的真实感。


现在完成时:
His promises have been deceptive. = 他的承诺一直都是欺骗性的。
The simplicity of the instructions has been deceptive. = 指令的简单性具有迷惑性。


过去完成时:
I realized his smile had been deceptive. = 我意识到他的笑容一直是虚假的。
They had been deceptively quiet before the explosion. = 爆炸前他们一直异常安静，这具有迷惑性。


将来时:
His future plans may be deceptive. = 他未来的计划可能是欺骗性的。
The ease of the task will be deceptively simple. = 任务的轻松程度将具有迷惑性。


虚拟语气:
If his words were not deceptive, I would trust him. = 如果他的话不是欺骗性的，我会相信他。
I wish the situation were not so deceptively easy. = 我希望情况不是如此表面上容易。


被动语态:
The customer was deceived by the deceptive advertisement. = 客户被具有欺骗性的广告欺骗了。
The audience was deceived by the deceptively simple magic trick. = 观众被看似简单的魔术戏法所迷惑。


疑问句:
Is his behavior deceptive? = 他的行为具有欺骗性吗？
Are the statistics deceptively presented? = 这些统计数据是否具有迷惑性地呈现？


否定句:
His intentions were not deceptive. = 他的意图并非具有欺骗性。
The problem was not deceptively simple; it was very complicated. = 这个问题并非表面上简单；它非常复杂。
