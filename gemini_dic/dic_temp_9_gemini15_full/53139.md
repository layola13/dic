diplomat:/ˈdɪpləˌmæt/ | n. |外交官，外交家 | A person appointed by their government to represent it in foreign countries.  They negotiate treaties and agreements, promote their country's interests, and maintain relationships with other nations. |The diplomat skillfully negotiated a peace treaty between the warring factions.  这位外交官巧妙地协商了一项在交战各派之间停战的条约。 | envoy, ambassador, negotiator | antagonist, warmonger | 名词

词源:
From Old French *diplomate, from Medieval Latin diplomata, from Greek diplōma ("a document, letter, or decree folded double").  The modern meaning developed in the 19th century.
