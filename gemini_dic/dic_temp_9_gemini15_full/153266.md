penalising: /ˈpiːnəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |惩罚；处罚| The act of imposing a penalty or punishment.  It implies a negative consequence for a wrongdoing or failure to meet a requirement. |例句：Penalising late submissions discourages procrastination. = 惩罚迟交作业可以避免拖延。

The company is penalising employees for excessive absenteeism. = 公司正在惩罚旷工过多的员工。


近义词：punishing, sanctioning, reprimanding, chastising


反义词：rewarding, encouraging


词源:  From "penalty" + "-ing" (present participle suffix).  "Penalty" itself originates from Latin "poena," meaning "pain" or "punishment."

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

They had been penalised heavily for their mistakes. = 他们因为犯的错误受到了严重的处罚。

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:
