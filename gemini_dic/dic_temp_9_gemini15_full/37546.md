commonplace:/ˈkɒmənpleɪs/ /ˈkɑːmənpleɪs/| adj. n.|平庸的；普通的事物；老生常谈|Lacking originality or excitement; ordinary.  A commonplace remark or event is ordinary and not interesting.|The movie was a commonplace story of boy meets girl.|近义词：ordinary, mundane, banal, trite|反义词：unusual, exceptional, remarkable, extraordinary


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This opinion is commonplace among young people. = 这种观点在年轻人中很常见。
He makes commonplace remarks in meetings. = 他在会议上发表了一些平淡无奇的言论。


一般过去时:
Her ideas were commonplace in the past. = 她的想法在过去很普通。
The event was commonplace, nothing special happened. = 这件事很普通，没有什么特别的事情发生。


现在完成时:
Such issues have become commonplace in modern society. = 这些问题在现代社会已经变得司空见惯。
Many commonplace errors have been found in the report. = 报告中发现了许多常见的错误。


过去完成时:
It had become a commonplace occurrence by then. = 到那时，这已经成为一件习以为常的事情了。
The technology had already become commonplace before the launch of the product. = 在产品发布之前，这项技术就已经普及了。


将来时:
This technology will become commonplace in the near future. = 这项技术在不久的将来将会普及。
Artificial intelligence will become commonplace in various fields. = 人工智能将在各个领域变得司空见惯。


虚拟语气:
If it were commonplace, no one would care. = 如果这是司空见惯的事，没有人会关心。
I wish this problem were commonplace and easy to solve. = 我希望这个问题是常见的且容易解决的。


被动语态:
The idea was considered commonplace by the experts. = 专家们认为这个想法很普通。
These methods have been rendered commonplace by advancements in technology. = 这些方法由于技术的进步而变得司空见惯。


疑问句:
Is this a commonplace occurrence? = 这是常见的事情吗？
Is the design commonplace or unique? = 这个设计是普通的还是独特的？


否定句:
This is not a commonplace situation. = 这不是常见的情况。
His artistic style is anything but commonplace. = 他的艺术风格绝非平庸。
