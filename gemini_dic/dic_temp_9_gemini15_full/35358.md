co-host:/ˈkoʊhoʊst/| n. v.|联合主持人；共同主持|A co-host is a person who hosts an event or program together with another person.  It can also be used as a verb, meaning to host something together with someone else.|例句：She's the co-host of a popular morning radio show. 她是一位热门早间广播节目的联合主持人。|例句：They decided to co-host the charity gala. 他们决定共同主持慈善晚会。|近义词：joint host, partner host|反义词：sole host, single host|名词，动词


USAGE->

He co-hosts a podcast every week. = 他每周共同主持一个播客。
They co-host a cooking show on television. = 他们在电视上共同主持一个烹饪节目。

虚拟语气:

词源:
The word "co-host" is a compound word formed from the prefix "co-" (meaning "together" or "jointly") and the noun "host."  The prefix "co-" derives from Latin "com-" (together).  Therefore, the word's meaning directly reflects its components:  hosting something together.
