nonfragileness:/ˌnɒnˈfrædʒəlnɪs/ | n. |不易碎性，坚固性，耐用性 |The quality or state of being not fragile; robustness; resilience.  It refers to the ability of a system or object to withstand stress or damage without breaking down.  This concept is often used in engineering, technology, and even social sciences to describe systems that can adapt and survive in the face of adversity. |例句：The nonfragileness of the bridge was tested during the earthquake.  桥梁的坚固性在地震中受到了考验。


The nonfragileness of the economic system is crucial for long-term growth.  经济体系的韧性对于长期增长至关重要。


近义词：robustness, resilience, durability, strength, sturdiness |反义词：fragility, brittleness, vulnerability


词性:名词


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "What is..."  It's used descriptively. Examples are shown below using different sentence structures:


Declarative Sentence:
The product's nonfragileness is a key selling point. = 产品的耐用性是其主要卖点。
The building's impressive nonfragileness withstood the hurricane. = 建筑物令人印象深刻的坚固性经受住了飓风的考验。


Interrogative Sentence:
Does the design prioritize nonfragileness? = 该设计是否优先考虑了坚固性？
What factors contribute to the system's remarkable nonfragileness? = 哪些因素促成了该系统非凡的韧性？


Exclamatory Sentence:
The nonfragileness of that structure! = 那座建筑的坚固性啊！
What incredible nonfragileness! = 多么不可思议的坚固性！


PHRASE->
There are not common idiomatic phrases directly using "nonfragileness".  However, the concept can be expressed through phrases like:


high degree of resilience: 高度韧性
