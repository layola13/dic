ischaemia:/ɪˈskiːmiə/ | n. | 缺血 | Ischaemia is a restriction in blood supply to tissues, causing a shortage of oxygen and glucose needed for cellular function.  This can lead to tissue damage or death. | Example:  Prolonged ischaemia can lead to myocardial infarction. 长期缺血会导致心肌梗塞。|近义词：hypoperfusion, ishemia |反义词：perfusion


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, grammatical tenses, active/passive voice, etc. are not applicable.  The usage focuses on its application in different sentence structures.


Example Sentences:

Declarative Sentence:
Cerebral ischaemia is a serious medical condition. 脑缺血是一种严重的疾病。


Interrogative Sentence:
Could the patient's symptoms be attributed to peripheral ischaemia? 病人的症状可能是由周围缺血引起的？


Exclamatory Sentence:
The sudden onset of ischaemia shocked the medical team! 缺血的突然发作震惊了医疗团队！
