cataclysm:/ˈkætəˌklɪzəm/| n.|大灾难，巨变|a large-scale and violent event that causes great destruction and change.  A cataclysm can be a natural disaster, such as an earthquake or flood, or a social upheaval, such as a revolution or war. |例句：The earthquake was a cataclysm that devastated the city.  这场地震是一场摧毁了这座城市的巨大灾难。

The eruption of Mount Vesuvius was a cataclysm that buried Pompeii. 维苏威火山爆发是一场掩埋了庞贝城的巨大灾难。|近义词：catastrophe, disaster, calamity, upheaval |反义词：calm, peace, tranquility
