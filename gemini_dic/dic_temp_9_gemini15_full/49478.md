delusionist:/dɪˈluʒənɪst/| n. |妄想狂；骗子；幻想家 |A delusionist is someone who holds false beliefs, often in spite of evidence to the contrary.  They may be suffering from a mental disorder or simply be prone to fantastical thinking.  The term can also be used, more colloquially, to refer to someone who deceives others, especially through illusion or trickery. | Example: The magician, a master delusionist, amazed the audience with his impossible feats.  The delusional patient believed he was a king. |近义词：illusionist, deceiver, charlatan, fantasist |反义词：realist, truth-teller
