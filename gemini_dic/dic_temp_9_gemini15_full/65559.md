eutrophications:/juːtrɒfɪˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 富营养化 (复数) | The process by which a body of water becomes enriched in dissolved nutrients (such as phosphates) that stimulate the growth of aquatic plant life usually resulting in the depletion of dissolved oxygen.  This process can lead to the death of fish and other aquatic organisms. | 例句：Excessive fertilizer runoff can cause eutrophications in lakes and rivers.  过量的化肥径流会导致湖泊和河流富营养化。

The eutrophications of the coastal waters have resulted in massive algal blooms.  沿海海域的富营养化导致了大规模的藻类大量繁殖。

| 近义词：hypertrophication, nutrient enrichment | 反义词：oligotrophication |  词性:名词
