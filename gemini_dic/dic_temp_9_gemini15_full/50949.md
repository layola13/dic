designating: /ˈdɛzɪɡneɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 指定；指派；命名 |  The act of assigning a specific purpose, function, or meaning to something or someone.  It implies a formal or official act of selection or identification. |


例句：

The committee is designating funds for the new project.  委员会正在为新的项目指定资金。

The map designates the location of the museum.  地图标明了博物馆的位置。


近义词：appointing, assigning, naming, specifying, identifying


反义词：ignoring, neglecting, overlooking


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government designates certain areas as wildlife reserves. 政府将某些地区指定为野生动物保护区。
He designates his assistant to handle the matter. 他指定他的助手来处理这件事。

一般过去时:
They designated a spokesperson to address the media.  他们指定了一位发言人来向媒体发表讲话。
The company designated a new CEO last month. 上个月公司指定了一位新的首席执行官。


现在完成时:
The council has designated this building a historical landmark. 理事会已将这座建筑物指定为历史地标。
She has designated her will to her lawyer. 她已将她的遗嘱交给了她的律师。


过去完成时:
They had designated the meeting room before the conference began.  他们在会议开始之前就已经指定了会议室。
The committee had designated him as the project leader. 委员会已将他指定为项目负责人。


将来时:
The board will designate a new chairman next week.  董事会下周将指定一位新的主席。
We will designate a specific area for the picnic. 我们将指定一个特定的区域用于野餐。


虚拟语气:
If they designated more funds, the project would succeed. 如果他们拨款更多，项目就会成功。
I wish they had designated a better location for the event. 我希望他们为活动指定一个更好的地点。


被动语态:
The funds were designated for educational purposes.  这些资金被指定用于教育目的。
He was designated as the team captain. 他被指定为队长。


疑问句:
Has the committee designated a date for the meeting?  委员会是否已为会议指定了一个日期？
Who has been designated to lead the project? 谁被指定领导这个项目？


否定句:
The company did not designate a replacement for the retiring CEO.  公司没有指定退休首席执行官的接替者。
They have not yet designated a location for the new factory. 他们尚未为新工厂指定地点。


PHRASE->

designate something for something: 将某物指定用于某目的
designate someone as something: 将某人指定为某身份
designate something to someone: 将某物指定给某人
be designated as: 被指定为
designate a time/date/place: 指定时间/日期/地点
