nominalization:/ˌnɒmɪnəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 名词化；名词构成 | The process of turning a verb, adjective, or other word class into a noun.  This often leads to more formal and abstract writing.  Nominalizations can sometimes make writing less clear and more difficult to read. | Example:  The *investigation* of the crime took several months. (Nominalization of "investigate")  The *destruction* of the forest was devastating. (Nominalization of "destroy") | 近义词：noun formation, substantivization | 反义词：verbification (the opposite process)
