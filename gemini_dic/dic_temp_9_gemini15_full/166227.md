prequestioning:/ˌpriːˈkwɛstʃənɪŋ/| n. |预先提问；预先询问|The act of asking questions before an event or interview to gather information or prepare for it.  It is often used in research, journalism, and interviews to ensure clarity and efficiency. |Example:  Prequestioning the interviewees helped the journalist to focus the interview and avoid irrelevant questions. 预先向受访者提问帮助记者集中采访重点并避免不相关的问题。|近义词：preliminary questioning, advance questioning|反义词：post-questioning, follow-up questioning|名词
