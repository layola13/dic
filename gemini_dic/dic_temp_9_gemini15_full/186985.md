riskier:/ˈrɪskiər/| adj.|更危险的，风险更大的|More dangerous or hazardous than something else.  It implies a higher degree of potential harm or negative consequences. |例句：Investing in the stock market is riskier than putting money in a savings account. 投资股市比把钱存入储蓄账户更冒险。

例句： Mountain climbing is riskier than hiking on a flat trail. 登山比在平坦的小路上远足更危险。


近义词：more dangerous, more hazardous, more perilous, less safe

反义词：safer, less risky, secure


USAGE->

一般现在时:
This investment is riskier than that one. = 这项投资比那项投资风险更大。
That activity appears riskier than it actually is. = 那项活动看起来比实际情况更危险。


一般过去时:
The situation became riskier after the storm. = 暴风雨过后，情况变得更加危险。
The plan seemed riskier than we initially thought. = 这个计划似乎比我们最初想的更冒险。


现在完成时:
The project has become riskier than anticipated. = 这个项目比预期的更危险了。
Things have gotten riskier since the new regulations were implemented. = 自从实施了新规章以来，情况变得更加危险了。


过去完成时:
The situation had become riskier before they intervened. = 在他们介入之前，情况已经变得更加危险了。
We had realized the project was riskier than we initially assessed. = 我们已经意识到这个项目比我们最初评估的更危险。


将来时:
The journey will be riskier than we expected. = 这段旅程将比我们预期的更危险。
Investing in cryptocurrencies will likely be riskier in the future. = 未来投资加密货币可能会更加冒险。


虚拟语气:
If the plan were riskier, we would have reconsidered it. = 如果计划更冒险，我们会重新考虑。
I wish the situation wasn't riskier than it currently is. = 我希望情况不像现在这么危险。


被动语态:
The mission was considered riskier than initially reported. = 这项任务被认为比最初报道的更危险。
The new policy is being criticized for making the situation riskier. = 新政策因使情况更加危险而受到批评。


疑问句:
Is this approach riskier than the previous one? = 这种方法比之前的方法更冒险吗？
Isn't this venture riskier than you initially thought? = 这项投资不是比你最初想的更冒险吗？


否定句:
The investment isn't as riskier as it seems. = 这项投资并不像看起来那么冒险。
The new procedure isn't riskier than the old one. = 新程序并不比旧程序更危险。

词源:  Derived from the adjective "risky" (meaning involving or exposing to risk), adding the comparative suffix "-ier" to indicate a higher degree of risk.  "Risky" itself originates from the noun "risk," ultimately from Old French "risque" (meaning "risk, hazard").
