reenforcement:/ˌriːɪnˈfɔːrsmənt/| n. |强化；加固；增援；补充 |The act of strengthening or reinforcing something.  It can refer to strengthening a structure, adding support to an idea, or providing additional troops. |例句：The bridge requires reinforcement to withstand heavy traffic. 这座桥需要加固才能承受大量的交通流量。

例句：The teacher's positive reinforcement helped the student improve their grades. 教师的积极强化帮助学生提高了成绩。


近义词：strengthening, support, augmentation, bolstering |反义词：weakening, undermining, demolition


词源:
From re- + enforce + -ment.  "Re-" means again or anew. "Enforce" comes from Old French *enforcier, from en- + forcier ("to force"). "-ment" is a suffix forming nouns of action or process.  Therefore, the word literally means "to force again" or "to strengthen again."
