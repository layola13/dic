paratroopers:/ˌpærəˈtruːpərz/| n. |伞兵，空降兵 |Soldiers who are trained to parachute from airplanes.  Paratroopers are highly trained and specialized soldiers who deploy from aircraft to engage in combat or other military operations. They are known for their speed, precision, and ability to deploy behind enemy lines. |Example: The paratroopers landed silently behind enemy lines.  伞兵悄悄地降落在敌后。


The paratroopers secured the bridge before the enemy could reinforce it. 空降兵在敌人增援之前控制了桥梁。
