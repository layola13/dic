dictating: /dɪkˈteɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 听写；口授；命令；支配 |  To speak or read aloud (words to be written down by another person). To issue orders or instructions. To strongly influence or determine (something).|


例句：

The teacher is dictating a passage to the students. = 老师正在给学生们听写一段文章。

She dictated a letter to her secretary. = 她口述了一封信给她的秘书。


近义词：

reading aloud, dictating to, prescribing, commanding, governing

反义词：

writing, following, obeying


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dictates his letters to his assistant. = 他将他信件口述给助手。
She dictates the terms of the agreement. = 她口述协议条款。

一般过去时:
The boss dictated a memo to his staff. = 老板向他的员工口述了一份备忘录。
He dictated his will to his lawyer. = 他向他的律师口述了他的遗嘱。

现在完成时:
She has dictated all the necessary documents. = 她已经口述了所有必要的文档。
He has dictated the terms of the contract to the opposing party. = 他已经向对方口述了合同条款。

过去完成时:
The author had dictated the entire manuscript before his illness. = 作者在生病之前已经口述了整篇稿件。
They had dictated the rules of engagement before the battle. = 战斗前他们已经口述了交战规则。

将来时:
The speaker will dictate his speech to the transcriber. = 演讲者将把他的演讲口述给速记员。
She will dictate the terms of the surrender to the enemy. = 她将向敌人口述投降条款。

虚拟语气:
If he dictated the terms, they would accept. = 如果他口述条款，他们会接受。
If she had dictated her wishes clearly, there would be no confusion. = 如果她清楚地表达了她的意愿，就不会有混淆。

被动语态:
The letter was dictated to the secretary. = 这封信是口述给秘书的。
The terms of the agreement were dictated by the stronger party. = 协议条款是由实力较强的一方决定的。

疑问句:
Was the speech dictated or written? = 演讲是口述还是书写的？
Will the instructions be dictated? = 指示会被口述吗？

否定句:
He didn't dictate the letter himself. = 他没有自己口述这封信。
She didn't dictate the rules of the game. = 她没有规定比赛规则。


PHRASE->
dictate terms  = 规定条件
dictate policy = 制定政策

词源:

From Old French dicter ("to say, dictate, pronounce, speak"), from Latin dictāre ("to say, speak, pronounce, dictate").  The Latin word is related to the Latin dicere ("to say").
