fumblingness:/ˈfʌmblɪŋnəs/| n. |笨拙；慌乱；结巴 |The act or state of fumbling; clumsiness; awkwardness.  It often implies a lack of skill or dexterity, leading to mistakes or inefficiency. |例句：Her fumblingness with the new software was evident to everyone.  她的新软件操作非常笨拙，每个人都看得出来。

例句：The fumblingness of his explanation only served to confuse the audience further. 他解释得支支吾吾，更让听众感到困惑。


近义词：awkwardness, clumsiness, ineptness, maladroitness |反义词：dexterity, skill, adroitness, grace


词源:  Derived from the verb "fumble," which comes from the Middle English word "fumblen," possibly of imitative origin, suggesting the sound of clumsy handling.
