dantesque:/dænˈtɛsk/| adj.|但丁式的；地狱般的；可怕的；令人震惊的| Relating to or suggestive of the works of Dante Alighieri, especially *The Inferno*, characterized by graphic descriptions of suffering and violence.  The word often implies a sense of overwhelming horror or despair. |例句：The aftermath of the war was a dantesque scene of destruction and suffering.  战后是一幅但丁式的地狱景象，充满了破坏和苦难。

The description of the famine was dantesque, detailing unimaginable levels of starvation and death.  对饥荒的描述是地狱般的，详细描述了难以想象的饥饿和死亡。


近义词：infernal, hellish, horrific, nightmarish, apocalyptic|反义词：heavenly, idyllic, utopian, peaceful

词源:The word "dantesque" is derived from the name of the Italian poet Dante Alighieri (1265-1321), the author of the epic poem *The Divine Comedy*, particularly its first part, *Inferno*.  The *Inferno* vividly depicts the nine circles of Hell, filled with graphic descriptions of suffering and torment.  The adjective "dantesque" therefore evokes the imagery and atmosphere of Dante's hellish descriptions.
