hobbed:/hɒbd/ | /hɑːbd/ | v. |  过去式和过去分词 |  (使)成碎片；(使)凹凸不平；乱砍；粗制滥造 |

英文解析：To hobble means to make something rough, uneven, or damaged, often by chopping or hacking at it.  It can also imply a lack of skill or care in the process, resulting in a poorly finished product. The past tense and past participle "hobbed" indicates that this action has already been completed.

例句：

The sculptor hobbed the rough stone, shaping it into a crude form. = 雕塑家粗糙地凿着粗石，把它塑造成一个粗略的形状。

The old road was hobbed and rutted, making travel difficult. = 那条老路凹凸不平，坑坑洼洼的，使旅行变得困难。


近义词：damaged, roughened, mutilated, hacked, mangled

反义词：smoothed, polished, refined, perfected

词性：动词
