exposing:/ɪkˈspoʊzɪŋ/| v. |揭露；使暴露；使曝光 | To reveal something that is hidden or secret, or to make someone or something vulnerable. |例句：The journalist's article exposed corruption within the government.  记者的文章揭露了政府内部的腐败。

The investigation exposed the company's illegal activities.  调查揭露了该公司的非法活动。|近义词：revealing, uncovering, disclosing |反义词：concealing, hiding, covering up |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is exposing the truth. = 他正在揭露真相。
She exposes her paintings in the gallery. = 她在画廊展出她的画作。

一般过去时:
They exposed the scandal to the public. = 他们向公众揭露了丑闻。
The experiment exposed the plants to harmful chemicals. = 实验将植物暴露于有害化学物质中。

现在完成时:
The media has exposed many political secrets. = 媒体已经揭露了许多政治秘密。
He has exposed himself to the sun too long. = 他晒太阳晒得太久了。


过去完成时:
They had exposed the fraud before the trial. = 他们在审判前已经揭露了欺诈行为。
She had exposed her vulnerabilities to him. = 她已经向他展露了她的弱点。


将来时:
The reporter will expose the politician's lies. = 记者将揭露这位政治家的谎言。
The scientist will expose the bacteria to the new antibiotic. = 科学家将把细菌暴露在新抗生素中。


虚拟语气:
If he exposed the truth, he would be in danger. = 如果他揭露真相，他会处于危险之中。
I wish she hadn't exposed my secret. = 我真希望她没有暴露我的秘密。


被动语态:
The truth was exposed by the journalist. = 真相被记者揭露了。
The children were exposed to the cold weather. = 孩子们暴露在寒冷的天气中。


疑问句:
Will the investigation expose the truth? = 调查会揭露真相吗？
Was the evidence exposed during the trial? = 证据在审判中被揭露了吗？


否定句:
He didn't expose the criminal's identity. = 他没有揭露罪犯的身份。
They did not expose the problem to management. = 他们没有向管理层暴露这个问题。
