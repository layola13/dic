dicotyledons:/ˌdaɪkɒtɪˈliːdən/| n. |双子叶植物|Dicotyledons, also known as dicots, are flowering plants that have two cotyledons (embryonic leaves) in their seeds.  They are one of the two major groups of angiosperms (flowering plants), the other being monocotyledons. Dicots are characterized by a variety of features, including net-veined leaves, taproots, and flower parts usually in multiples of four or five.  Many common plants, such as roses, oaks, and sunflowers, are dicots.|Example: Roses and sunflowers are examples of dicotyledons.|近义词：dicots|反义词：monocotyledons
