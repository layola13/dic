pinched:/pɪntʃt/| v. adj.| 捏，掐；偷窃；吝啬的；（俚语）不舒服的； |to squeeze (something) between the thumb and finger; to steal something; feeling a sharp, brief pain;  |例句：He pinched the child's cheek. 他捏了孩子的脸颊。
She pinched a cookie from the jar. 她从罐子里偷了一块饼干。|近义词：squeeze, steal, nip,  stingy|反义词：generous, lavish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pinches my cheeks playfully. = 他顽皮地捏我的脸颊。
She pinches pennies. = 她很节俭。


一般过去时:
I pinched myself to make sure I wasn't dreaming. = 我掐了自己一下，以确保我不是在做梦。
The thief pinched my wallet. = 小偷偷了我的钱包。


现在完成时:
He has pinched several apples from the tree. = 他已经从树上偷了好几个苹果。
She has pinched her finger in the door. = 她的手指被门夹到了。


过去完成时:
I had pinched my finger before I started cooking. = 在我开始做饭之前，我已经把手指弄伤了。
She realized she had pinched the wrong button on the machine. = 她意识到她按错了机器上的按钮。


将来时:
He will pinch the dough to make dumplings. = 他将捏面团做饺子。
I think the dog will pinch you if you try to pet it. = 我想如果你想抚摸它，那狗会咬你。


虚拟语气:
If I pinched him, he would cry. = 如果我捏他，他会哭的。
I wish I hadn't pinched that cookie. = 我真希望我没有偷那块饼干。


被动语态:

疑问句:
Did he pinch her? = 他捏她了吗？

否定句:
He didn't pinch the baby. = 他没有捏婴儿。
She didn't pinch anything from the shop. = 她没有从商店偷任何东西。
