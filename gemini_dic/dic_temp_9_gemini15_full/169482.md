propagandistic: /ˌprɒpəˈɡændɪstɪk/ | adj. |宣传的；煽动的| Relating to or characteristic of propaganda; aiming to promote a particular doctrine or cause.  It often implies a manipulative or deceptive approach. |例句：The government's propagandistic campaign aimed to sway public opinion.  政府的宣传活动旨在影响公众舆论。


The speech was filled with propagandistic rhetoric. 这篇演讲充满了煽动性的言辞。


近义词：propaganda-like, promotional, persuasive, biased, manipulative |反义词：objective, unbiased, truthful, factual

词源:
From propaganda + -istic.  Propaganda itself originates from the Latin *propaganda fide*, meaning "propagation of the faith," referring to the Congregation for the Propagation of the Faith, a Vatican department established in 1622.
