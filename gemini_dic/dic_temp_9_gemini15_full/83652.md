haemorrhages:/ˈhɛmərɪdʒɪz/| n. |出血，大出血|The noun "haemorrhage" refers to a profuse flow of blood from a ruptured blood vessel.  It can also refer to a rapid loss or decline in something, such as a haemorrhage of talent from a company.|例句：The patient suffered a severe haemorrhage after the accident. 事故后病人严重出血。

The company experienced a haemorrhage of skilled workers after the merger. 并购后，公司损失了大量熟练工人。|近义词：bleeding, hemorrhage, effusion|反义词：healing, clotting
