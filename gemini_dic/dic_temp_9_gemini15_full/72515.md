flurrying:/ˈflʌriɪŋ/| n. |一阵纷飞；一阵忙碌|A flurry is a sudden, brief period of activity, excitement, or commotion, often involving many things happening at once. It can refer to things like a flurry of snow, a flurry of activity, or a flurry of questions.  |例句：A flurry of snow covered the ground. 一阵纷飞的雪覆盖了地面。  The announcement caused a flurry of excitement.  这个公告引起了一阵兴奋。|近义词：bustle, rush, flurry of activity|反义词：calm, stillness, quiet
