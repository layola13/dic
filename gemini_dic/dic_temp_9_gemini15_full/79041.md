give the nod to: /ɡɪv ðə nɒd tuː/ | phrasal verb |  批准；同意；首肯 | To give approval or consent to something or someone. It implies a silent or non-verbal agreement, often a subtle gesture like a nod. |

例句：
The committee gave the nod to the proposed budget. = 委员会批准了拟议的预算。

The CEO gave the nod to the new marketing campaign. = 首席执行官批准了新的营销活动。


近义词：approve, authorize, sanction, consent to, agree to

反义词：reject, refuse, deny, veto


词性: phrasal verb

词源:  The phrase originates from the simple act of nodding one's head to signify agreement or approval.  The "nod" itself is an ancient gesture of assent, and "give" simply means to bestow or grant.  The combination of "give" and "the nod" evolved into a common idiom to describe the act of granting approval, especially in formal settings.
