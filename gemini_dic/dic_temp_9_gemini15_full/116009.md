marshes:/ˈmɑːʃɪz/| n.|沼泽；湿地|Areas of low-lying land that are saturated with water, often characterized by vegetation adapted to wet conditions. Marshes are typically found near rivers, lakes, or coastlines.  They play a vital role in ecosystems, providing habitat for various plants and animals.|Example: The birds nested in the reeds of the marshes.  鸟儿在沼泽芦苇中筑巢。|近义词：swamps, bogs, wetlands|反义词：dryland, upland
