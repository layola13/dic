musclebound: /ˈmʌsəlbaʊnd/ | adj. |肌肉发达的；肌肉僵硬的 | Excessively muscular; having muscles that are so large and stiff as to impede movement.  |例句：He's become so musclebound he can barely lift his arms above his head. 他肌肉发达到几乎抬不起胳膊。  |近义词：overmuscled, bulky, hefty |反义词：weak, frail, slender


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is musclebound from years of weightlifting. = 他多年举重导致肌肉发达。
She is musclebound and struggles with simple movements. = 她肌肉发达，简单的动作都费力。


一般过去时:
He was musclebound after the competition. = 比赛后他肌肉僵硬。
She became musclebound due to lack of stretching. = 由于缺乏伸展运动，她变得肌肉僵硬。


现在完成时:
He has become musclebound since he started bodybuilding. = 自从开始健美以来，他肌肉变得非常发达。
She has been musclebound for years, limiting her flexibility. = 她多年来肌肉僵硬，限制了她的灵活性。


过去完成时:
He had been musclebound for so long that he needed physical therapy. = 他肌肉僵硬已久，需要进行物理治疗。
She had become musclebound before she realized the importance of stretching. = 在她意识到伸展运动的重要性之前，她就已经肌肉僵硬了。


将来时:
He will be musclebound if he continues training this way. = 如果他继续这样训练，他的肌肉会变得非常发达。
She will likely become musclebound without proper flexibility exercises. = 如果没有适当的柔韧性练习，她很可能会变得肌肉僵硬。


虚拟语气:

I wish I weren't so musclebound; it makes everyday tasks difficult. = 我希望我不那么肌肉发达；这使得日常工作变得困难。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "musclebound" is a compound word. "Muscle" comes from the Latin word "musculus," meaning "little mouse," a reference to the shape of some muscles.  "Bound" in this context means "tied up" or "restricted,"  referring to the restricted movement caused by excessively developed muscles.
