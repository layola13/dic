dims:/dɪmz/| v.|使变暗淡；使模糊；减弱|to make something less bright or clear; to reduce the intensity of something|The lights dimmed as the movie began. 电影开始时灯光暗了下来。|The rain dimmed the distant hills.  雨模糊了远处的山峦。|brighten, illuminate|brighten, enhance|


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The sun dims in the evening.  太阳傍晚时分变暗。
He dims the lights before watching TV. 他看电视前调暗灯光。


一般过去时:
She dimmed the headlights to avoid blinding the oncoming traffic. 她调暗车灯以免晃到迎面而来的车辆。
The old photograph had dimmed with age.  旧照片因年代久远而褪色。


现在完成时:
The bright colors have dimmed over time.  鲜艳的颜色随着时间的推移已经褪色。
The once vibrant painting has dimmed considerably.  曾经鲜艳的画作已经相当暗淡。


过去完成时:
The lights had dimmed before the performance began.  演出开始前灯光已经暗了下来。
The colors of the tapestry had dimmed significantly due to years of exposure to sunlight.  由于多年的阳光照射，挂毯的颜色已经严重褪色。


将来时:
The sun will dim as it sets.  太阳落山时会变暗。
They will dim the lights for the ceremony.  他们会为仪式调暗灯光。


虚拟语气:
If the lights dimmed, we would have a better view of the stars. 如果灯光变暗，我们就能更好地看到星星。
I wish the sunset would dim more slowly.  我希望日落能慢一些。


被动语态:
The lights were dimmed by the technician. 灯光由技术人员调暗。
The image was dimmed by the fog.  图像被雾气模糊了。


疑问句:

否定句:
