non-execution: /ˌnɑːnˌɛksɪˈkjuːʃən/ | n. |不执行；未执行；未履行 | The failure to carry out or complete something, such as a plan, task, or legal order.  This term often implies a deliberate or involuntary omission rather than an inability. | 例句：The non-execution of the contract resulted in significant financial losses. = 合同未履行导致了巨大的经济损失。

The non-execution of the project led to delays and budget overruns. = 项目未执行导致了延误和预算超支。


近义词：failure, default, omission, nonperformance, breach (in the context of a contract) | 反义词：execution, performance, fulfillment, completion | 词性: 名词


词源:

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of".  Derived from Old French *non* and ultimately from Latin *non*.

* **execution**: From Old French *execucioun*, from Latin *exsecutio*, from *exsequi* "to follow out, to perform," from *ex-* "out" + *sequi* "to follow".
