prime-ministerships: /ˈpraɪm ˈmɪnɪstərʃɪps/ | n. |首相任期；总理任期 |The periods of time during which someone serves as prime minister.  This word refers to the instances of holding the office, not the office itself. |例句：The two prime-ministerships of Margaret Thatcher were marked by significant economic reforms.  玛格丽特·撒切尔的两个首相任期都以重大的经济改革为标志。

例句：Historians often analyze the successes and failures across different prime-ministerships to understand long-term trends. 历史学家经常分析不同首相任期的成功和失败，以了解长期趋势。


近义词：terms of office (as prime minister), premierships


反义词：无


词性：名词
