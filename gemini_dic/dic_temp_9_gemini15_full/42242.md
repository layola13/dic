countably:/ˈkaʊntəbli/ | adj. | 可数的 |  Able to be counted; consisting of, or relating to, items that can be counted.  It implies that the items are discrete and individual, not continuous or forming a whole. |  The stars are countably infinite.  The apples in the basket are countably many. | countable, numerable | uncountable, innumerable


USAGE->

This word is an adjective and does not have verb conjugations like "What" in the example.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  Instead, here are examples demonstrating its use in different sentence structures:

Affirmative Sentences:
The sheep in the field are countably numerous. = 田野里的羊数量可数且众多。
There are countably many grains of sand on the beach. = 海滩上有许多可数的沙粒。


Negative Sentences:
The stars in the sky are not easily countably. = 天上的星星不容易数清。
The grains of sand on the beach are not countably finite. = 海滩上的沙粒数量不是有限可数的。


Interrogative Sentences:
Are the items in the box countably? = 箱子里的物品是可数的吗？
Is the number of students in the class countably? = 班级里学生的人数是可数的吗？
