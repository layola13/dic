reflexivization:/ˌriːflɛksɪvaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. | 反身化；反身性 | The process by which a pronoun refers back to its antecedent within the same clause or sentence.  It involves the use of reflexive pronouns (e.g., myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves) to indicate that the subject and object of a verb are the same entity.  | Example:  He hurt himself playing football. (他踢足球时伤了自己。) |近义词：self-reference, reflection | 反义词：non-reflexivity | 词性:名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a linguistic term and doesn't have typical grammatical tense conjugations like "What" in the example.  Instead, it describes a grammatical process.  Therefore, tense usage examples are not applicable.

Examples illustrating the concept of reflexivization:


Reflexive Pronoun Use:
He looked at himself in the mirror. = 他看着镜子里的自己。
She bought herself a new dress. = 她给自己买了一件新裙子。


Non-reflexive vs. Reflexive:
He washed the car. (Non-reflexive: the subject and object are different) = 他洗了车。
He washed himself. (Reflexive: the subject and object are the same) = 他洗了澡。


Obligatory Reflexivization:
She prides herself on her work. (Obligatory reflexive:  "pride oneself" requires a reflexive pronoun) = 她为自己的工作感到自豪。
The children amused themselves with toys. (Obligatory reflexive: "amuse oneself" requires a reflexive pronoun) = 孩子们玩玩具自娱自乐。


Reciprocal Reflexivization:
They looked at each other. (Reciprocal, indicating mutual action, similar to reflexivization) = 他们互相看着对方。
The siblings helped each other with homework. (Reciprocal, indicating mutual action, similar to reflexivization) = 兄弟姐妹互相帮助做作业。
