/pəˈrɪsæd/| n. |奇蹄目动物|Perissodactyla is an order of mammals characterized by having only one toe (or digit) bearing weight on each foot.  This contrasts with artiodactyls, which have two or more weight-bearing toes.  The order includes horses, rhinoceroses, and tapirs.|例句：Horses, rhinoceroses, and tapirs are all members of the perissodactyl order.  = 马、犀牛和貘都是奇蹄目动物。|近义词：ungulate (a more general term)|反义词：artiodactyl|名词
