nativization:/ˌnætɪvaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |本土化；归化；使…成为本地人|The process of adapting something (e.g., a word, a custom, or a person) to a local environment or culture.  This often involves changes in pronunciation, spelling, or meaning to make it more easily understood and used by the local population.|Example: The nativization of English vocabulary in Japan resulted in many loanwords being adapted to Japanese pronunciation.  日本英语词汇的本土化导致许多外来词被改编成日语发音。|近义词：adaptation, localization, acculturation|反义词：foreignization, alienation, exoticism|
