endemiology:/ˌɛndɪmiˈɒlədʒi/ | n. | 地方病学 | The branch of medicine and public health concerned with the study of endemic diseases, their distribution, and control.  It focuses on understanding the factors that contribute to the persistence of diseases within a specific geographic area or population.  | Example:  The study of endemiology is crucial for understanding the spread of malaria in certain regions. = 地方病学的研究对于理解疟疾在某些地区的传播至关重要。


The endemiology of this virus is still poorly understood, making it difficult to develop effective prevention strategies. =  这种病毒的地方病学特征仍然知之甚少，这使得制定有效的预防策略变得困难。


近义词: epidemiology (although epidemiology has a broader scope) | 反义词: None

词源:  From Greek *endemos (endemic) + -logia (study of).  "Endemic" itself comes from Greek *en (in) + *dēmos (people).  Therefore, endemiology literally means "the study of what is prevalent among a people (in a particular region)."
