proposal:/prəˈpoʊzl/ | n. | 建议；提议；求婚 |  A proposal is a plan or suggestion, especially a formal one, put forward for consideration. It can also refer to an offer of marriage.  |例句：He submitted a proposal for a new marketing strategy. 他提交了一份新的市场营销策略建议书。|例句：She accepted his marriage proposal. 她接受了他的求婚。|近义词：suggestion, offer, plan, proposition |反义词：rejection, refusal


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He proposes a new solution every week. = 他每周都提出一个新的解决方案。
The company proposes a new marketing plan. = 公司提议一个新的营销计划。

一般过去时:
She proposed a different approach to the problem. = 她对这个问题提出了不同的方法。
They proposed a toast to the happy couple. = 他们提议为这对幸福的夫妇敬酒。

现在完成时:
He has proposed several amendments to the bill. = 他已经对该法案提出了几项修正案。
The committee has proposed a new set of rules. = 委员会已经提出了一套新的规则。

过去完成时:
She had already proposed the idea before the meeting. = 会议之前她已经提出了这个想法。
They had proposed several solutions, but none were satisfactory. = 他们已经提出了几个解决方案，但没有一个令人满意。

将来时:
The government will propose new tax laws next year. = 政府明年将提出新的税法。
She will propose a motion at the next meeting. = 她将在下次会议上提出动议。

虚拟语气:
If he proposed a better plan, we might consider it. = 如果他提出一个更好的计划，我们可能会考虑。
I wish he had proposed a more realistic solution. = 我希望他提出一个更现实的解决方案。

被动语态:
A new budget was proposed by the finance committee. = 财政委员会提出了一份新的预算。
The changes were proposed by the employees themselves. = 这些改变是由员工自己提出的。

疑问句:
What did he propose at the meeting? = 他在会议上提出了什么？
Has she proposed a date for the wedding yet? = 她是否已经提出婚礼日期了？

否定句:
He did not propose any concrete solutions. = 他没有提出任何具体的解决方案。
They did not propose any changes to the existing system. = 他们没有建议对现有系统进行任何更改。
