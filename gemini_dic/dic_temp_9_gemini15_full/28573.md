careeners:/kəˈriːnərz/| n. v.|倾斜者；使倾斜；快速行驶|A careener is someone or something that leans or tilts sharply.  As a verb, it means to lean or tilt sharply, or to move swiftly and recklessly.|例句：The old ship careened dangerously in the storm.  古老的船在暴风雨中危险地倾斜。The car careened around the corner.  汽车快速地拐过弯角。|近义词：tilt, slant, list, veer, speed|反义词：stabilize, straighten, balance|名词，动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ship careens dangerously. = 船危险地倾斜。
He careens down the street. = 他飞快地冲下街道。

一般过去时:
The car careened off the road. = 汽车冲出了道路。
The drunken sailor careened into a lamppost. = 醉酒的水手撞到了一根灯柱上。

现在完成时:
The boat has careened several times in the rough seas. = 这艘船在汹涌的海面上已经倾斜了好几次了。
They have careened through the city streets. = 他们已经飞驰穿过城市街道。

过去完成时:
The plane had careened before crashing. = 飞机在坠毁前已经倾斜了。
The driver had careened several times before he stopped. = 司机在停车前已经好几次急转弯了。


将来时:
The ship will careen violently in the upcoming storm. = 这艘船将在即将到来的暴风雨中剧烈倾斜。
The driver will careen through the intersection. = 司机将会飞驰穿过十字路口。

虚拟语气:
If the captain had not acted quickly, the ship would have careened and sunk. = 如果船长没有迅速采取行动，船就会倾斜并沉没。
If he had not been drinking, he would not have careened into the wall. = 如果他不喝酒，他就不会撞到墙上了。


被动语态:
The car was careened into the ditch. = 汽车被撞进了沟里。(被动语态在此不太常用，常用主动语态)
The ship was careened by the strong winds. = 船被强风吹得倾斜。(被动语态在此不太常用，常用主动语态)


疑问句:
Will the boat careen in this strong wind? = 在这强风中船会倾斜吗？
Did the car careen before the accident? = 事故发生前汽车是否倾斜了？


否定句:
The ship did not careen during the voyage. = 船在航行过程中没有倾斜。
He did not careen into the other car. = 他没有撞到另一辆车。


PHRASE->
careen down (急速冲下)
careen off (撞离)
careen wildly (剧烈倾斜)
careen out of control (失控)
careen into (撞上)

词源:
The word "careen" ultimately derives from the Old French word "carène," referring to the keel of a ship.  The nautical sense of "to heel over" developed from this, later extending to the more general meaning of swerving or tilting sharply.
