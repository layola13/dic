pseudosematic:/ˌsuːdəʊsɪˈmætɪk/| adj.|似义的；貌似有意义的|Appearing to have meaning or conveying a message, but lacking genuine semantic content.  It often refers to elements in a system that resemble meaningful symbols but don't actually have established meaning within that system.  For example,  certain patterns or sounds in animal communication might appear purposeful to observers but lack a precise, consistent meaning. |Example: The markings on the butterfly's wings are pseudosematic; they look like eyespots to deter predators, but they don't convey any specific information.  |近义词：semblant, apparent, seeming |反义词：semantic, meaningful
