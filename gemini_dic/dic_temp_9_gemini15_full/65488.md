europeanizations:/ˌjʊərəpiənɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. |欧洲化；使欧洲化 | The process of adopting European customs, practices, or characteristics.  This often involves the spread of European culture, institutions, and values to other parts of the world.  It can be a complex and contested process, with both positive and negative consequences. | Example: The rapid europeanizations of Eastern Europe after the fall of the Soviet Union led to significant social and economic changes.  欧洲在苏联解体后迅速的欧洲化导致了重大的社会和经济变化。 The europeanizations of national legal systems is a significant aspect of EU integration. 国家法律体系的欧洲化是欧盟一体化的一个重要方面。 | 近义词：Europeanisation, westernization | 反义词：localization, indigenization
