ruble: /ˈruːbl/ | n. |卢布|The ruble is the basic monetary unit of Russia.  It's a currency used for transactions within Russia and is subject to fluctuations in the global market.  The ruble's value has historically been volatile, influenced by factors like oil prices and geopolitical events. | Example: The Russian ruble weakened against the dollar today.  今天卢布兑美元贬值了。|近义词：currency, monetary unit |反义词：None (as it's a unit of currency, not something that has inherent opposites)| noun

词源:
The word "ruble" comes from the Old East Slavic word  "rubl," meaning "cut" or "piece," referring to the fact that silver bars were cut into pieces to create currency.  This ultimately derives from the Proto-Slavic root *rubiti, meaning "to chop" or "to cut."
