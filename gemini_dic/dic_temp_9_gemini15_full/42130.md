cottonmouths:/ˈkɑːtnˌmaʊθz/ | n. |棉口蛇（复数） | A type of venomous snake found in the southeastern United States, known for its dark coloration and the white or pale interior of its mouth.  They are non-aggressive but will bite if threatened. | Example:  Several cottonmouths were sighted near the swamp.  几条棉口蛇在沼泽附近被发现。|近义词:water moccasin (same snake, different name) |反义词:  (no direct antonym, but non-venomous snakes could be considered opposite)|名词
