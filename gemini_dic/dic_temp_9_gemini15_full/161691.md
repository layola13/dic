pouts:/paʊts/| v.|撅嘴，生气地不说话 |To push one's lips together in a expression of annoyance, displeasure, or sadness. |例句：The child pouted when he didn't get the toy he wanted.  孩子没得到他想要的玩具，就撅起了嘴。|例句：She pouted for hours after the argument. 争吵之后，她生气地撅着嘴好几个小时。|近义词：sulks, sulk, mumbles|反义词：smiles, grins


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pouts when he's unhappy. = 他不开心的时候会撅嘴。
She often pouts after a disagreement. = 她争吵后经常撅嘴。

一般过去时:
The baby pouted after his nap. = 午睡后，宝宝撅起了嘴。
He pouted all day because he failed the test. = 他因为考试不及格而整天撅着嘴。

现在完成时:
She has pouted ever since she lost the game. = 自从输了比赛后，她就一直撅着嘴。
He has pouted all afternoon. = 他整个下午都在撅嘴。

过去完成时:
He had pouted for an hour before his mother finally gave in. = 他已经撅嘴一个小时了，他妈妈才最终妥协。
She had pouted all morning before she finally spoke. = 她早上一直在撅嘴，直到最后才开口说话。

将来时:
I think he will pout if he doesn't get his way. = 我想如果他不顺他的意，他会撅嘴的。
She will probably pout if you don't invite her. = 如果你不邀请她，她可能会撅嘴。


虚拟语气:
If he knew better, he wouldn't pout so much. = 如果他知道得更好，他就不会那么撅嘴了。
I wish he wouldn't pout all the time. = 我希望他不要总是撅嘴。

被动语态: (This verb is rarely used in the passive voice)
His pout was quite noticeable. = 他的撅嘴相当明显。(This focuses on the pout itself, not the action of pouting)
His unhappiness was expressed by a pout. = 他的不开心是通过撅嘴表达出来的。(This is a more natural way to express the passive idea)


疑问句:
Is he pouting again? = 他又在撅嘴了吗？
Why is she pouting? = 她为什么撅嘴？

否定句:
He didn't pout at all. = 他根本没有撅嘴。
She doesn't usually pout like that. = 她通常不会那样撅嘴。


PHRASE->
pout one's lips = 撅起嘴唇
pout and sulk = 撅嘴生气
pout about something = 为某事而撅嘴
pout at someone = 对某人撅嘴
pout silently = 静静地撅嘴
