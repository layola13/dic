nummulite:/ˈnʌmjuːlaɪt/| n. |货币虫；钱币虫|A nummulite is a large, single-celled organism (a foraminiferan) that had a lenticular or disc-shaped shell, common in the Eocene Epoch.  These fossils are often found in large numbers and are significant index fossils used in stratigraphy.  | Example: The limestone was rich in nummulites, indicating its Eocene age.  这块石灰岩富含货币虫，表明其为始新世时期。|近义词：foraminiferan (a more general term)|反义词：无
