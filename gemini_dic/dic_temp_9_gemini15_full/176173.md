re-adapting:/ˌriːəˈdæptɪŋ/ | v. |重新适应；再适应 |The process of adjusting or becoming accustomed to a new or changed situation again.  It implies a prior adaptation that is being modified or re-established. |例句：After his injury, he was re-adapting to a life without his full physical capabilities.  在他受伤后，他正在重新适应没有完全身体机能的生活。

例句：The company is re-adapting its business model to meet the changing market demands. 公司正在重新调整其商业模式以满足不断变化的市场需求。


近义词：readjusting, reacclimating, reorienting | 反义词：maladapting, resisting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are re-adapting to the new environment. = 他们在重新适应新的环境。
The team is re-adapting its strategy. = 团队正在重新调整策略。


一般过去时:
He re-adapted quickly to the new job. = 他很快适应了新工作。
The bird re-adapted to flying after its injury. = 那只鸟在受伤后重新适应了飞行。


现在完成时:
She has re-adapted well to life after retirement. = 她退休后生活适应得很好。
They have re-adapted their plans several times. = 他们已经几次重新调整了他们的计划。


过去完成时:
He had re-adapted to civilian life before the war ended. = 在战争结束之前，他已经重新适应了平民生活。
The refugees had re-adapted to a new culture by the time they settled. = 难民们在定居时已经重新适应了一种新的文化。


将来时:

The city will re-adapt its infrastructure to accommodate the growing population. = 该市将重新调整其基础设施以适应不断增长的人口。


虚拟语气:
If they had re-adapted sooner, the outcome would have been better. = 如果他们早点重新适应，结果会更好。
I wish I had re-adapted more easily to the change. = 我希望我更容易适应变化。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
