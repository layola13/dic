scalder:/ˈskɔːldər/| n. v.|烫伤者；使…烫伤的人或物|A scalder is a person or thing that causes a scald.  The word also functions as a verb, meaning to scald something or someone.|例句：The hot water was a scalder, leaving a nasty burn on his arm.  滚烫的水烫伤了他胳膊，留下了一个难看的伤痕。  He accidentally scalded the milk while making coffee. 他煮咖啡时不小心把牛奶烫了。|近义词：burner, scourger (as a verb)|反义词：healer, soother (as a verb)

词源:
The word "scalder" derives from the Old English word "scald," meaning to burn with hot liquid or steam. This in turn may relate to the Proto-Germanic word *skaldian.  The noun form "scalder" is a relatively modern development, arising from the verb's usage.
