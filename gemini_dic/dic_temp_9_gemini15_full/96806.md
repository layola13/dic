inertia:/ɪˈnɜːʃə/| n. |惯性，惰性，迟钝|The property of a body to remain at rest or to continue in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.  Inertia is also used metaphorically to describe resistance to change or action. |例句：The car's inertia kept it moving even after the brakes were applied. 汽车的惯性使得即使在刹车后它仍然在运动。

The inertia of the company prevented it from adapting to the changing market.  公司的惰性阻止了它适应不断变化的市场。|近义词：resistance, sluggishness, lethargy|反义词：activity, dynamism, momentum
