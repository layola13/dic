patentability: /ˌpætənˈtæbɪləti/ | n. | 可专利性 | The ability of an invention to be patented; the quality of being patentable.  It refers to whether an invention meets the legal requirements for patent protection. This includes novelty, utility, and non-obviousness. |  The patentability of the new drug is currently under review.  新的药物的可专利性目前正在审查中。The high court questioned the patentability of the software algorithm. 高等法院质疑该软件算法的可专利性。|  patenteability,  inventability | non-patentability

词源:
From *patent* (a government grant conferring a right or title) + *-ability* (suffix denoting capacity or fitness).  The word "patent" ultimately derives from the Latin *patere*, meaning "to lie open," referring to the open disclosure of an invention required for patent protection.
