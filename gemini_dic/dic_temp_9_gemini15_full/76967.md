garble:/ˈɡɑːrbl/| vt. |使含糊不清；歪曲；扰乱（声音）|To make something unclear or confusing, especially speech or a message.  It can also refer to the act of scrambling or distorting a signal, such as a radio transmission. |例句：The telephone line was garbled, making it difficult to understand the conversation. 电话线路杂音很大，使谈话难以理解。


The radio broadcast was badly garbled by static. 收音机广播因静电干扰而严重失真。|近义词：distort, mangle, jumble, confuse |反义词：clarify, elucidate

USAGE->

He garbles his words when he's nervous. 他紧张的时候说话含糊不清。
The machine garbles the data before transmission. 机器在传输数据之前会使其混乱。

一般过去时:
She garbled the message she received. 她把收到的信息弄混了。

过去完成时:
