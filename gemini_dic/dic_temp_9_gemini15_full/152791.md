pectate:/ˈpɛkteɪt/| n.|果胶酸盐|Pectate is the salt of pectic acid, a polysaccharide found in plant cell walls.  It's a key component contributing to the texture and properties of fruits and vegetables. Pectates are used in various food applications for their gelling and thickening properties.|例句：The high pectate content in apples contributes to their firmness.|近义词：pectin (though pectin is the acid itself, while pectate is a salt)|反义词：无|名词
