chancers:/ˈtʃɑːnsəz/| n.| 机会主义者，投机者 |People who take risky chances, often without much consideration for the consequences. They are often perceived as opportunistic and unreliable.|例句：He's a chancer, always looking for a quick buck. 他是个投机分子，总是想快速赚钱。

例句：Those chancers tried to swindle the old lady out of her savings. 那些投机分子试图骗取老太太的积蓄。


近义词：gamblers, speculators, opportunists, risk-takers|反义词：cautious people, planners, conservatives


词源:chance (n.) + -er (suffix denoting a person who does something)  源于古法语"chaunce,"  最终源于拉丁语"cadentia" (a fall).  The original meaning related to luck or fate, evolving to encompass those who rely on luck or take risks.
