capillaries:/ˈkæpɪleriz/| n.|毛细血管|The capillaries are the smallest blood vessels in the body, connecting arteries and veins. They are responsible for exchanging nutrients and waste products between the blood and the surrounding tissues.|例句：Oxygen and nutrients pass from the blood in the capillaries into the body's tissues. 氧气和营养物质从毛细血管中的血液进入身体组织。|The blood flows through the capillaries to deliver oxygen to the cells. 血液流经毛细血管，向细胞输送氧气。|近义词：capillary vessels|反义词：arteries, veins|名词
