slipping:/ˈslɪpɪŋ/| v. |滑倒；下降；偷偷溜走；|The act of sliding or losing one's footing; a gradual decline or deterioration; secretly leaving a place.|例句：He slipped on the ice and fell. 他在冰上滑倒了。|例句：The company's profits are slipping. 公司的利润正在下降。|近义词：sliding, sliding, gliding, dropping |反义词：advancing, improving, climbing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The vase is slipping from my grasp. = 花瓶从我的手中滑落。
He is slipping into a coma. = 他正在陷入昏迷。


一般过去时:
The thief slipped out of the building. = 小偷偷偷溜出了大楼。
She slipped and fell down the stairs. = 她滑倒了，从楼梯上摔了下来。


现在完成时:
My memory has been slipping lately. = 最近我的记忆力下降了。
Standards have been slipping in this school. = 这所学校的标准一直在下降。


过去完成时:
He had slipped away before I could stop him. = 我还没来得及阻止他，他就溜走了。
The company's market share had been slipping for months. = 公司的市场份额已经下降了好几个月了。


将来时:
The roof will be slipping soon if we don't repair it. = 如果我们不修理屋顶，它很快就会塌下来。
Prices are likely to slip further next year. = 明年价格可能会进一步下跌。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't slipped, I wouldn't have broken my arm. = 如果我没有滑倒，就不会摔断胳膊了。
If he had not slipped, he would have won the race. = 如果他没有失误，他就会赢得比赛了。


被动语态:
The secret was slipped to the press. = 这个秘密被泄露给了媒体。
The plan was slipped into the agenda without anyone noticing. = 计划被偷偷塞进了议程，没有人注意到。


疑问句:
Is he slipping? = 他是不是快不行了？(指健康状况)
Is the support slipping away? = 支持正在消失吗？


否定句:
He didn't slip up during the presentation. = 他在演示过程中没有出错。
She didn't slip on the wet floor. = 她没有在湿滑的地板上滑倒。


PHRASE->
slip away:溜走；逝去
slip up:出错；失误
slip through the cracks:被忽视；漏掉
slip of the tongue:口误
slip one's mind:忘记
