colicky:/ˈkɒlɪki/| adj.| 疝痛的；肠绞痛的；易患肠绞痛的 | Characterized by colic; suffering from or prone to colic.  Colicky pain is severe, intermittent pain in the abdomen, often associated with cramping.  |例句：The baby was crying inconsolably; he seemed to be colicky.  这个婴儿哭个不停；他好像肠绞痛。|例句：She experienced colicky pains after eating certain foods. 她吃了某些食物后会经历阵发性腹痛。|近义词：crampy, spasmodic |反义词：painless, comfortable

词源:
From "colic," ultimately from the Greek word  "kōlikos," meaning "relating to the colon."  The word "colic" originally referred to pain in the colon, but now often refers to abdominal pain in infants or to severe cramping pain in general.
