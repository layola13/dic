oxysalt:/ˈɒksɪsɔːlt/| n. |含氧酸盐|An oxysalt is a salt of an oxyacid.  Oxyacids are acids that contain oxygen.  Oxysalts are formed when the hydrogen atoms of an oxyacid are replaced by a metal or other cation.  Many common salts are oxysalts, including sulfates, nitrates, phosphates, and carbonates. |Example: Sodium sulfate (Na₂SO₄) is an oxysalt because it's derived from sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄).|近义词：salt (in a broader sense)|反义词：无
