purposedly:/ˈpɜːrpəs(ə)li/ | adv. |故意地，有意地 | done in a way that is intended or planned:  | 例句：He purposely ignored her question. 他故意忽略了她的问题。

例句：She purposely misled the police. 她故意误导了警察。


近义词：intentionally, deliberately, designedly, on purpose | 反义词：accidentally, unintentionally, inadvertently


词源:  The word "purposedly" comes from the adjective "purposed," which itself derives from the noun "purpose."  The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective into an adverb, indicating manner or way.  The root of "purpose" traces back to Old French "pourposer" and ultimately to Latin "propositum," meaning "something proposed or intended."


用法说明:

"Purposedly" emphasizes the intention behind an action.  It suggests a conscious decision to do something, often with a specific goal or effect in mind.  It's stronger than simply saying something was done "intentionally," implying a more calculated or deliberate act.  The action might be positive or negative, depending on the context.
