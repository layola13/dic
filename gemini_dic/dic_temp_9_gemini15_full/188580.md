ruled:/ruːld/ | v. | 统治；裁决；决定；划线 | To govern or control (a country, people, etc.); to make a formal decision in a court of law; to draw a line on something. |例句：The king ruled the country with an iron fist.  国王铁腕统治国家。|例句：The judge ruled in favor of the plaintiff.  法官判决原告胜诉。|近义词：govern, control, decide, decree |反义词：be ruled, be governed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rules the kingdom wisely. = 他明智地统治着王国。
The judge rules on the case. = 法官裁决此案。


一般过去时:
The queen ruled for many years. = 女王统治了很多年。
The referee ruled a touchdown. = 裁判判决达阵。


现在完成时:
She has ruled the company for decades. = 她几十年来一直掌管着这家公司。
The court has ruled against the defendant. = 法院已判决被告败诉。


过去完成时:
He had ruled the empire before the war. = 他在战争前统治着帝国。
The jury had ruled on the case before the judge's final decision. = 在法官最终裁决之前，陪审团已经对案件做出了裁决。


将来时:
They will rule the land with justice. = 他们将以正义统治这片土地。
The committee will rule on the proposal next week. = 委员会下周将对该提案做出裁决。


虚拟语气:
If he ruled justly, the people would be happy. = 如果他公正地统治，人民就会幸福。
If the judge had ruled differently, the outcome would have been different. = 如果法官做出不同的判决，结果就会不同。


被动语态:
The country was ruled by a dictator. = 这个国家由一个独裁者统治。
The case was ruled on quickly. = 此案很快就被裁决了。


疑问句:
Who ruled England in 1500? = 1500年谁统治英格兰？
How will the committee rule on this matter? = 委员会将如何裁决此事？


否定句:
He did not rule the country effectively. = 他没有有效地统治国家。
The judge did not rule in his favor. = 法官没有判决对他有利。


PHRASE->
ruled out: 排除
ruled in: 裁定为有效
ruled against: 裁定反对
ruled upon: 对…做出裁决
ruled by: 受…统治
