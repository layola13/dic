dreaded:/ˈdrɛdɪd/| adj.|令人恐惧的，可怕的，令人担忧的| causing or inspiring great fear or anxiety.  The dreaded exam loomed large in their minds.|近义词：feared, terrifying, awful, horrible|反义词：welcome, delightful, pleasant|形容词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The thought of public speaking is dreaded by many. = 许多人害怕公开演讲。
He dreads the upcoming surgery. = 他害怕即将到来的手术。


一般过去时:
She dreaded the moment she had to tell him the news. = 她害怕不得不告诉他这个消息的那一刻。
They dreaded the long journey ahead. = 他们害怕漫长的旅程。


现在完成时:
I have dreaded this day for weeks. = 我已经害怕这一天好几个星期了。
She has dreaded telling her parents the truth. = 她一直害怕告诉父母真相。


过去完成时:
He had dreaded the confrontation for days before it happened. = 在冲突发生前的几天里，他一直害怕这次冲突。
They had dreaded the possibility of failure. = 他们一直害怕失败的可能性。


将来时:
I will dread the day I have to leave. = 我将害怕不得不离开的那一天。
We will dread facing such a difficult challenge. = 我们将害怕面对如此艰难的挑战。


虚拟语气:
I would dread to imagine what would happen if... = 我不敢想象如果……会发生什么。
She wished she hadn't dreaded the trip so much; it turned out fine. = 她希望自己不要那么害怕这次旅行；结果还不错。


被动语态:
The upcoming test is dreaded by all the students. = 即将到来的考试让所有学生都感到害怕。
His arrival was dreaded by the whole family. = 他的到来让全家人都感到害怕。


疑问句:
Is the upcoming assignment dreaded by the class? = 班级里的人害怕即将到来的作业吗？
What aspect of the project is most dreaded by the team? = 团队最害怕项目的哪个方面？


否定句:
He didn't dread the meeting as much as he anticipated. = 他并没有像预料的那样害怕这次会议。
She didn't dread the challenge; she welcomed it. = 她并不害怕这个挑战；她欢迎它。


PHRASE->
the dreaded disease = 可怕的疾病

the dreaded moment = 可怕的时刻
dreaded consequences = 可怕的后果
dreaded task = 可怕的任务
