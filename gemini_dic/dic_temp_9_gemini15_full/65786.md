evesdropper:/ˈivzˌdrɑːpər/| n. |偷听者，窃听者 | A person who secretly listens to a private conversation.  |例句：He was an evesdropper, always trying to hear what others were saying. 他是个偷听者，总是试图偷听别人在说什么。

例句：The evesdropper was caught red-handed. 偷听者当场被抓住。

近义词：eavesdropper, listener, spy |反义词：speaker, talker


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The evesdropper listens to the conversation. = 偷听者在偷听谈话。
He eavesdrops on his neighbors regularly. = 他经常偷听邻居的谈话。


一般过去时:
She eavesdropped on their argument. = 她偷听了他们的争吵。
The detective eavesdropped on the suspects' conversation. = 侦探偷听了嫌疑人的谈话。


现在完成时:
I have eavesdropped on them several times. = 我已经偷听了他们好几次了。
He has been an evesdropper for years. = 他当了多年的偷听者。


过去完成时:

She had been eavesdropping for hours before she heard anything interesting. = 在她听到任何有趣的事情之前，她已经偷听了几个小时。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If I were an evesdropper, I would know their secret. = 如果我是个偷听者，我会知道他们的秘密。
