parashoth: /pəˈrɑːʃɔːθ/ | n. | 帕夏特（犹太教经文章节） |  Parashoth refers to the weekly sections into which the Torah (the first five books of the Hebrew Bible) is divided for reading in synagogues throughout the Jewish liturgical year. Each parashah contains a portion of the Torah text, typically read aloud over the course of a Sabbath service.  The division of the Torah into parashoth facilitates a systematic study and understanding of the sacred text over the course of a year. | Example:  The synagogue chanted this week's parashah beautifully.  本周会堂朗读了这周的帕夏特，读得非常优美。 | 近义词：Torah portion, sidra (similar term used in some Jewish communities) | 反义词：无 |  词性: 名词
