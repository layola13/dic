perambulation:/pəˌræm.bjuˈleɪ.ʃən/| n. |漫步；巡游；闲逛 |The act of walking around, especially for pleasure or to inspect something.  It often implies a leisurely and somewhat aimless stroll. |例句：The couple enjoyed a leisurely perambulation through the park. 这对夫妇在公园里享受了一次悠闲的漫步。

例句：The judge ordered a perambulation of the property boundaries. 法官下令对房产边界进行巡视。


近义词：stroll, walk, ramble, promenade, saunter |反义词：rush, sprint, dash |词性：名词


词源:

The word "perambulation" comes from the Latin word "perambulare," which is a combination of "per-" (through, throughout) and "ambulare" (to walk).  Therefore, the literal meaning is "to walk through" or "to walk about."  The word entered English during the Middle Ages.
