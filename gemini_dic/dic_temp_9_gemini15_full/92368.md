hypochondria: /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪə/ | n. | 疑病症，臆想病 |  An excessive preoccupation or worry about having a serious illness.  This is often based on a misinterpretation of normal bodily sensations.  People with hypochondria may repeatedly seek medical attention, even when reassured by doctors that they are healthy.  |  He suffers from hypochondria and is constantly worried about his health. 他患有疑病症，总是担心自己的健康。  She visits the doctor almost weekly with various imagined ailments, a clear sign of her hypochondria. 她几乎每周都去看医生，诉说各种各样的想象中的疾病，这清楚地表明了她患有疑病症。 |  health anxiety, illness anxiety disorder |  (反义词较难直接对应，可考虑healthy mindset, realistic appraisal of health) | 名词
