depletion:/dɪˈpliːʃən/| n. |损耗；枯竭；减少 |The gradual depletion of the ozone layer is a serious environmental concern.  臭氧层的逐渐损耗是一个严重的环境问题。|近义词：reduction, exhaustion, decrease |反义词：increase, replenishment


英文解析：The act of depleting or the state of being depleted; reduction in the number or quantity of something.  指耗尽或被耗尽的行为或状态；某事物数量或质量的减少。

例句：

The depletion of natural resources is a major problem facing the world. 自然资源的枯竭是世界面临的一个主要问题。

Years of overfishing have led to the depletion of fish stocks in many areas. 多年的过度捕捞导致许多地区的鱼类资源枯竭。


词源:

From the verb "deplete," which comes from the Old French word "depletir," meaning "to empty completely."  The prefix "de-" means "completely," and the root "-plete" is related to the Latin word "plenus," meaning "full."  Therefore, "depletion" literally means "the act of making completely empty."
