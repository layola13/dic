derives:/dɪˈraɪvz/ | vt. |获得；源于；导出；推断| To obtain something from a source; to trace the origin or derivation of something; to deduce or infer something.|例句：He derives great pleasure from his work. 他从工作中获得极大的乐趣。|例句：This word derives from Latin. 这个词源于拉丁语。|近义词：obtain, originate, deduce, infer |反义词：lose, give, create


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He derives his income from investments. = 他从投资中获得收入。
She derives satisfaction from helping others. = 她从帮助他人中获得满足感。

一般过去时:
They derived the formula from previous research. = 他们从之前的研究中推导出这个公式。
The word "alcohol" derived from Arabic. = “酒精”一词源于阿拉伯语。

现在完成时:
We have derived many benefits from this project. = 我们从这个项目中获得了许多益处。
Scientists have derived a new treatment from this plant. = 科学家们从这种植物中提取了一种新的疗法。

过去完成时:
They had derived their conclusions from insufficient data. = 他们根据不足的数据得出了结论。
She had derived a great deal of knowledge from her travels. = 她从旅行中获得了大量的知识。

将来时:
They will derive energy from the sun in the future. = 未来他们将从太阳中获取能量。
We will derive much benefit from this new technology. = 我们将从这项新技术中获得许多益处。

虚拟语气:
If we worked harder, we would derive greater rewards. = 如果我们更努力工作，我们将获得更大的回报。
I wish I could derive more enjoyment from my job. = 我希望我能从我的工作中获得更多乐趣。

被动语态:
The results were derived from a large-scale study. = 这些结果来自一项大规模的研究。
The conclusion can be derived from the evidence presented. = 从所提供的证据中可以得出结论。

疑问句:
Where does he derive his inspiration from? = 他的灵感来自哪里？
What can we derive from this experiment? = 我们能从这个实验中得到什么？

否定句:
He does not derive any satisfaction from his job. = 他从工作中得不到任何满足感。
They did not derive any meaningful results from the test. = 他们从测试中没有得到任何有意义的结果。
