houseboy: /ˈhaʊsˌbɔɪ/ | n. | 男佣，男仆 | A male domestic servant, typically in a wealthy household.  The term is now largely considered offensive due to its historical association with colonialism and racial inequality. | He worked as a houseboy for a wealthy family in the city. 他在一个富裕的城市家庭做男佣。 | domestic servant, manservant |  (no direct antonym, but terms implying independence or higher social standing could be considered contextual opposites) | 名词

PHRASE->

There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "houseboy".  The term itself is problematic and should be avoided.  Instead, more neutral and respectful phrasing like "domestic worker," "household employee," or "servant" should be used, depending on the context.  Phrases using these alternatives would be more appropriate. For example:

domestic worker's duties
household employee's schedule
a diligent servant
