photolability:/ˌfoʊtoʊlæˈbɪləti/ | n. |见光分解性，光敏性 |The tendency of a substance to decompose or change its chemical structure when exposed to light.  This is often observed in pharmaceuticals, dyes, and other light-sensitive materials.  Photolability can be a significant concern in the storage and handling of these materials, requiring protective measures like dark containers or UV-blocking packaging. |Example: The photolability of the drug necessitated storing it in amber glass vials.  见光分解性药物需要储存在琥珀色玻璃瓶中。|近义词：photosensitivity, light sensitivity |反义词：photostability
