preshadow:/prɪˈʃædəʊ/| vt.|预示，预兆|To foreshadow is to indicate or suggest something, usually something bad, that is going to happen in the future.  It often involves subtle hints or clues.  |例句：The dark clouds preshadowed a storm.  乌云预示着一场暴风雨。

例句：The author preshadowed the tragic ending with several ominous clues throughout the novel. 作者在小说中用几个不祥的线索预示了悲惨的结局。


近义词：foreshadow, foretell, portend, augur

反义词：obscure, conceal, hide

词性:动词
