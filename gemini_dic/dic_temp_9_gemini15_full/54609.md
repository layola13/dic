dismay:/dɪsˈmeɪ/| n. vt.|沮丧，灰心；使沮丧，使灰心|a feeling of shock and unhappiness caused by something unexpected|The news filled them with dismay.  这个消息使他们非常沮丧。|Her dismay was evident.  她的沮丧显而易见。|近义词：despair, disappointment, dejection|反义词：elation, joy, delight|名词,动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His actions dismay me. = 他的行为让我沮丧。
The news dismays the public. = 这个消息使公众沮丧。

一般过去时:
The bad news dismayed her. = 这个坏消息使她沮丧。
The results of the test dismayed the teacher. = 测试的结果让老师很沮丧。

现在完成时:
The constant failures have dismayed him. = 持续的失败让他灰心丧气。
Nothing has dismayed her spirit. = 没有什么能让她灰心丧气。

过去完成时:
He had been dismayed by the lack of support. = 他因为缺乏支持而感到沮丧。
They had been dismayed to find the house empty. = 他们发现房子空空如也，感到很沮丧。

将来时:
The upcoming exam will dismay many students. = 即将到来的考试会让许多学生感到沮丧。
The announcement will dismay investors. = 这一声明将会使投资者沮丧。

虚拟语气:
I would be dismayed if he failed the exam. = 如果他考试不及格，我会很沮丧。
She would have been dismayed if she had known the truth. = 如果她知道真相的话，她会很沮丧。

被动语态:
He was dismayed by the criticism. = 他对批评感到沮丧。
The audience was dismayed by the poor performance. = 观众对糟糕的表演感到沮丧。

疑问句:
Are you dismayed by the results? = 你对结果感到沮丧吗？
Was she dismayed by his behavior? = 她对他的行为感到沮丧吗？

否定句:
I wasn't dismayed by the rejection. = 我对被拒绝并不沮丧。
He wasn't dismayed by the challenges. = 他并没有被挑战吓倒。


PHRASE->
