preelect:/priːɪˈlɛkt/| v.|预先选举，提前选举|To elect someone or something before the official election.  This term is often used in the context of selecting a candidate or nominee before a formal vote takes place.  |例句：The party preelected a candidate for the upcoming mayoral race.  该党在即将到来的市长竞选中预先选举了一名候选人。|近义词：preselect, nominate, choose|反义词：post-elect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They preelect a new chairman every year. = 他们每年预选一位新主席。
The committee preelects its officers in the spring. = 委员会在春季预选其官员。


一般过去时:
The college preelected its student body president last month. = 该学院上个月预选了学生会主席。
The school preelected the head boy before the official election. = 学校在正式选举前预选了班长。

They have preelected a new team captain. = 他们已经预选了一位新的队长。


过去完成时:
