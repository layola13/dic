flatlining:/ˈflætˌlaɪnɪŋ/| v. n. adj.|平线, 骤停, 停滞不前|

英文解析:To flatline means to remain at zero or a very low level, often referring to a flat electrocardiogram (ECG) indicating cardiac arrest.  It can also be used metaphorically to describe a stagnant situation or a lack of progress. As a noun, it refers to the state of being flatlined. As an adjective, it describes something that is stagnant or unchanging.

例句:

动词用法：
The patient's heart was flatlining. = 病人的心跳骤停了。

The sales figures have been flatlining for months. = 几个月来销售额一直停滞不前。


名词用法：
The flatlining of the economy is a serious concern. = 经济的停滞不前是一个严重的问题。

After the initial excitement, the project fell into a flatlining period. = 最初的兴奋过后，项目进入了停滞期。


形容词用法：
The flatlining market showed no signs of recovery. = 停滞不前的市场没有显示出复苏的迹象。

His career is in a flatlining state. = 他的事业处于停滞状态。


近义词:
(verb) stagnate, plateau, remain unchanged, cease, stop
(noun) standstill, stagnation, plateau, lull
(adjective) stagnant, unchanging, static


反义词:
(verb) surge, increase, rise, improve, progress
(noun) upswing, boom, surge, improvement
(adjective) dynamic, thriving, booming, growing


词性:Verb, Noun, Adjective
