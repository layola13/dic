/ˌpriːˌrekəɡˈnɪʃənz/ | n. |预先识别；预先承认 |The process of identifying or acknowledging something before it happens or becomes fully apparent.  It often involves anticipation, prediction, or preparation based on prior knowledge or experience.  This term is less common and often used in specialized contexts such as artificial intelligence or cognitive psychology.|例句：The system's prerecognitions of potential threats allowed for preventative measures. 系统对潜在威胁的预先识别允许采取预防措施。

The company's prerecognitions of market trends led to its success.  公司的市场趋势预判使其获得了成功。|近义词：anticipation, prediction, foreknowledge, premonition |反义词：misrecognition, misunderstanding, misjudgment|
