enslavement:/ɪnˈsleɪvmənt/| n. |奴役；奴隶制度 |The state of being a slave; the act of enslaving someone.  It often involves forced labor, deprivation of freedom, and the denial of basic human rights.  |例句：The enslavement of Africans during the transatlantic slave trade was a horrific crime against humanity. 非洲人在跨大西洋奴隶贸易中的奴役是对人类的可怕罪行。

例句： Centuries of enslavement left deep scars on the social and economic fabric of the nation.几个世纪的奴役给国家的社会和经济结构留下了深深的伤痕。


近义词：bondage, servitude, captivity, thralldom, subjugation

反义词：freedom, liberation, emancipation


词源:  From "enslave" + "-ment," a suffix forming nouns that denote the action or state of being of the verb. "Enslave" itself comes from Old French "esclaver" (to enslave), ultimately from Latin "sclavus" (Slav, slave).  The word reflects the historical association of Slavic peoples with slavery in Europe.
