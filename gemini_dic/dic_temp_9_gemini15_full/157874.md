plainchants:/ˈpleɪnˌtʃænts/| n. |圣咏，单声部圣歌|Monophonic liturgical chants of the Western Christian Church, typically sung in Latin.  Plainchant is characterized by its unison singing and lack of harmony or accompaniment.  It is a major part of the musical heritage of the Catholic Church and other Western Christian traditions.|例句：The monks chanted plainchant during the morning service.  僧侣在早上的弥撒中唱圣咏。|近义词：Gregorian chant, liturgical music |反义词：polyphonic music, harmony
