discardable: /dɪˈskɑːrdəbl/ | adj. | 可丢弃的，可抛弃的 | Capable of being discarded; not worth keeping or saving.  Something that is discardable is considered unnecessary or unwanted and can be thrown away or gotten rid of. | Example:  The old furniture was discardable and replaced with new pieces. 旧家具是可以丢弃的，被换成了新的家具。 The broken vase was discardable.  那个破花瓶是可以丢弃的。| 近义词：disposable, expendable, dispensable | 反义词：essential, indispensable, necessary | 词性：形容词

词源:
The word "discardable" is derived from the verb "discard," which itself comes from the Old French word "descarder," meaning "to reject" or "to throw away."  The "-able" suffix indicates capability or suitability.
