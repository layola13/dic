collector:/kəˈlɛktər/| n. |收藏家，收集者，收款员|A collector is a person who collects things such as stamps, coins, or art.  It can also refer to a person or device that collects something, like a tax collector or a data collector.|例句：He is a passionate stamp collector. 他是一位狂热的集邮者。

例句：The tax collector came to collect the overdue taxes. 税务员来收取逾期的税款。


近义词：gatherer, accumulator, assembler |反义词：disperser, scatterer


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He collects stamps. = 他收集邮票。
She collects antique furniture. = 她收集古董家具。


一般过去时:
He collected many rare coins. = 他收集了许多稀有的硬币。
They collected donations for the charity. = 他们为慈善机构募捐。


现在完成时:
She has collected over a thousand books. = 她已经收集了超过一千本书。
He has collected all the necessary data. = 他已经收集了所有必要的数据。


过去完成时:
They had collected enough evidence before the trial. = 审判前他们已经收集了足够的证据。
She had collected all the ingredients before she started cooking. = 在开始烹饪之前，她已经收集了所有配料。


将来时:
He will collect the package tomorrow. = 他明天将取包裹。
They will collect the rubbish later. = 他们稍后将收集垃圾。


虚拟语气:
If I were a collector, I would collect classic cars. = 如果我是一个收藏家，我会收集老爷车。
I wish I had collected more stamps when I was younger. = 我希望我年轻时收集更多邮票。


被动语态:
The taxes are collected by the government. = 税款由政府征收。
The data is collected automatically by the system. = 数据由系统自动收集。


疑问句:
Does he collect anything? = 他收集什么东西吗？
What kind of art does she collect? = 她收集什么类型的艺术品？


否定句:
He doesn't collect stamps anymore. = 他不再收集邮票了。
She didn't collect anything valuable. = 她没有收集任何有价值的东西。
