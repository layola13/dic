nonerratically: /ˌnɒnˌɛrætɪkli/ | adv. | 不不稳定地；规律地；稳定地 |  Acting in a steady and predictable manner; without erratic behavior.  This word is formed by adding the prefix "non-" to the adverb "erratically".  It describes actions or processes that are consistent and predictable, lacking the unpredictable or irregular nature of erratic behavior. |

The machine operated nonerratically throughout the experiment.  = 机器在整个实验过程中运行稳定。

The pilot maintained nonerratic control of the aircraft during the turbulence. = 飞行员在颠簸中保持了飞机的稳定控制。


近义词: steadily, consistently, regularly, predictably, reliably

反义词: erratically, unpredictably, inconsistently, irregularly, unreliably


词源:

* **non-**:  A prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of".  It's derived from Latin.

* **erratically**: An adverb derived from "erratic," which comes from the Latin word "erraticus," meaning "wandering" or "straying."  This in turn relates to the verb "errare," meaning "to wander" or "to err."


用法说明：

nonerratically  primarily functions as an adverb modifying verbs, describing how an action is performed.  It emphasizes the absence of erratic or unpredictable behavior.  The word is relatively uncommon in everyday speech but is useful in technical or scientific writing where precision of description is important.
