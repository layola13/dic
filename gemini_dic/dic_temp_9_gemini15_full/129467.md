non-affluent: /ˌnɒnˈæfluənt/ | adj. | 不富裕的，贫穷的 | Not wealthy; lacking affluence.  The term describes individuals or groups who lack significant financial resources.  It's a more polite and less judgmental term than "poor." |

例句：

Many non-affluent families struggle to afford healthcare.  许多不富裕的家庭难以负担医疗费用。

The non-affluent neighborhoods often lack access to quality education. 不富裕的街区通常缺乏优质教育资源。


近义词：impoverished, poor, underprivileged, less well-off

反义词：affluent, wealthy, rich, well-to-do


词源:

The word is a combination of "non-" (meaning "not") and "affluent" (meaning "wealthy, rich").  "Affluent" itself comes from the Latin word "affluere," meaning "to flow to."  This refers to the flow of wealth or abundance.
