pulmonibranchiate:/ˌpʌlˈmoʊnɪbræŋkieɪt/| adj. |肺鳃类的；有肺鳃的|Having both lungs and gills.  This term describes certain amphibians and aquatic animals that possess both lungs for air breathing and gills for underwater respiration.  The possession of both respiratory systems often indicates a transitional stage in evolution from aquatic to terrestrial life.| Example:  The axolotl is a pulmonibranchiate amphibian.  / 钝口螈是一种肺鳃类两栖动物。|近义词:  (lacks direct synonyms, but could be described as 'lung-and-gill possessing') |反义词:  (lacks direct antonyms,  but could be described as 'exclusively lung-breathing' or 'exclusively gill-breathing') |


Etymology:  The word is a combination of "pulmo-" (lung, from Latin *pulmo) and "-branchiate" (gills, from Greek *branchia).  The suffix "-ate" indicates possession or having the characteristic of.
