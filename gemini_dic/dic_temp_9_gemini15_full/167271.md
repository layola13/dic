prethreatened:/ˌpriːˈθrɛtənd/| adj.|预先威胁的，预先警告的 |  Describes something that has been threatened beforehand, or a situation where a threat has been issued in advance.  It implies a prior warning or indication of a potential threat. |例句：The prethreatened strike caused widespread anxiety among workers. 预先警告的罢工导致工人之间普遍焦虑。  The prethreatened storm forced the cancellation of the outdoor concert.  预先警告的风暴迫使户外音乐会取消。|近义词：forewarned, pre-announced, anticipated|反义词：unwarned, unexpected
