/ˌnɒnˌɛkstɜːˈnælɪti/ | n. |非外部性|The absence of externalities; a situation where the actions of one economic agent do not affect the well-being of others.  It implies a complete internalization of costs and benefits.  This is a theoretical ideal rarely found in practice.|Example:  In a perfectly competitive market with complete information, nonexternality is assumed, though rarely achieved.  在一个信息完全的完全竞争市场中，假设不存在外部性，尽管这很少实现。|近义词: internalization, absence of spillover effects |反义词: externality
