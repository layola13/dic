moose's:/ˈmuːsɪz/| n.|驼鹿的；驼鹿的（所有格）|The possessive form of "moose," indicating belonging to a moose.  It's used to show ownership or relationship.|例句：The moose's antlers were magnificent. 驼鹿的鹿角非常壮观。|近义词：belonging to a moose|反义词:N/A


USAGE->
This word is a possessive noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "to be" or "to do".  It only shows possession.  Therefore, tense and voice are irrelevant.

PHRASE->
moose's tracks: 驼鹿的足迹
moose's call: 驼鹿的叫声
moose's habitat: 驼鹿的栖息地
the moose's size: 驼鹿的体型
the moose's behavior: 驼鹿的行为
