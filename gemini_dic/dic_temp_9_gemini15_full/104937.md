kelosis:/keɪˈloʊsɪs/ | n. | 胼胝形成；角化过度 |  Kelosis refers to the abnormal thickening of the skin, often resulting in a hardened, calloused area. This condition is characterized by the excessive accumulation of keratin, a protein that forms the skin's protective outer layer. Kelosis can occur anywhere on the body but is commonly seen on areas subjected to frequent friction or pressure.  | Example:  The prolonged exposure to the sun resulted in kelosis on her hands.  日晒时间过长导致她手上出现了胼胝。 | 近义词：hyperkeratosis, callus formation | 反义词：skin softening,  dermal thinning |  n.
