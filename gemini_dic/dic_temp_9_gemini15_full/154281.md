peristalses:/ˌpɛrɪˈstælsiːz/| n. |蠕动(复数)|The wavelike muscular contractions that propel food through the digestive tract.  Peristalses are essential for the movement of food, chyme, and feces through the gastrointestinal system.|Example:  The efficient peristalses in his digestive system ensured rapid food transit.|近义词：peristalsis (单数)|反义词：None directly applicable.  The opposite would be a lack of peristalsis (ileus).
