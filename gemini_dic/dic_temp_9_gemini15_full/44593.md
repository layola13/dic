croutons:/kruːˈtɒnz/| n. |烤面包丁，面包块 |Small, typically cubed pieces of bread that have been seasoned and usually toasted or fried until crisp.  They are often added to salads, soups, or other dishes to add texture and flavor. |Example: The Caesar salad was topped with crispy croutons.  凯撒沙拉上面撒着脆脆的面包丁。

The soup was garnished with croutons and fresh herbs. 汤里加了面包丁和新鲜香草作为装饰。|近义词：bread cubes, toasted bread, bread pieces |反义词：None.

词源:
The word "crouton" comes from the Old French word "croûton," which literally means "little crust."  The word evolved through Middle French and ultimately entered English in the 19th century.  The suffix "-ton" is a diminutive, indicating smallness.
