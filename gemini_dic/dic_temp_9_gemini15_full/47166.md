dawn:/dɔːn/| n. v.|黎明，拂晓；开始，来临|The beginning of daylight; the time when the sun first appears in the morning.  It can also refer to the beginning of something.|例句：Dawn broke over the hills. 黎明时分，阳光洒在山丘上。

The dawn of a new era is upon us.一个新时代的黎明已经到来。|近义词：sunrise, daybreak, beginning, onset|反义词：dusk, nightfall, end


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The sun dawns every morning. = 太阳每天早上升起。
Dawn breaks slowly in the winter. = 冬天黎明来得很慢。


一般过去时:
Dawn broke just as we reached the summit. = 我们到达山顶时，黎明刚刚破晓。
It dawned on me that I had forgotten my keys. = 我突然意识到我忘记带钥匙了。


现在完成时:
Dawn has broken, and the birds are singing. = 黎明已经破晓，鸟儿们正在歌唱。
It has dawned on me that I need to change my approach. = 我意识到我需要改变我的方法。


过去完成时:
Dawn had already broken when we started our hike. = 我们开始远足时，黎明已经破晓了。
It had dawned on him that his plan was flawed. = 他已经意识到他的计划有缺陷。


将来时:
Dawn will break soon. = 黎明很快就会到来。
It will dawn on them eventually. = 他们最终会明白的。


虚拟语气:
If dawn broke earlier, we could start sooner. = 如果黎明来得早一些，我们就能早些出发。
I wish dawn would break quickly so I could start my day. = 我希望黎明快点到来，这样我就能开始我的一天了。


被动语态:
Dawn is often greeted with birdsong. = 黎明常常伴随着鸟鸣声到来。
The truth was gradually dawned on the public. 真相逐渐被公众所知晓。


疑问句:
When will dawn break tomorrow? = 明天什么时候天亮？
Has it dawned on you yet? = 你明白了吗？


否定句:
Dawn hadn't broken yet when the alarm clock rang. = 闹钟响的时候，天还没有亮。
It didn't dawn on her that he was lying. = 她没有意识到他在撒谎。



PHRASE->

at dawn = 在黎明时分
before dawn = 在黎明之前
until dawn = 直到黎明
from dawn till dusk = 从黎明到黄昏
the dawn of = …的开始
