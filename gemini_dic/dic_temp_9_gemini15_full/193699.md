self-conditioning: /ˈsɛlf kənˈdɪʃənɪŋ/ | n. |自我调节，自我适应 |The process by which an organism or system adjusts to its environment or circumstances without external intervention.  It involves internal mechanisms that regulate and maintain stability in the face of changing conditions.  This can apply to biological systems (e.g., the body's regulation of temperature), psychological systems (e.g., coping mechanisms), or even technological systems (e.g., self-regulating machinery). |Example: The athlete's self-conditioning program helped him to manage stress and improve performance.  运动员的自我调节计划帮助他管理压力并提高成绩。|近义词：self-regulation, adaptation, adjustment |反义词：maladaptation, dysfunction |名词
