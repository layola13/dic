faring:/ˈfɛərɪŋ/ | v. | 进展；情况；生活境况 | To progress or get along; to fare.  It describes how someone or something is managing or doing in a particular situation. |例句：How are you faring in your new job? 你新工作进展如何？

How is your family faring? 你的家人情况如何？| 近义词：progressing, succeeding, thriving | 反义词：struggling, failing, suffering | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
How are you faring today? = 你今天过得怎么样？
How is the project faring? = 项目进展如何？


一般过去时:
How did you fare on your exam? = 你考试考得怎么样？
How did the team fare in the competition? = 球队在比赛中表现如何？


现在完成时:
How have you been faring since you moved? = 你搬家后过得怎么样？
How has the business fared this year? = 今年生意怎么样？


过去完成时:
How had the patient fared before the operation? = 患者手术前情况如何？
How had the plants fared during the drought? = 干旱期间植物情况如何？


将来时:
How will you fare in the interview? = 你面试会表现如何？
How will the crops fare with this weather? = 这种天气庄稼会怎么样？


虚拟语气:
If I had studied harder, I would have fared better on the test. = 如果我学习更努力，考试成绩会更好。
I wish I had fared better in the competition. = 我希望我在比赛中表现更好。


被动语态:
The project is faring well. = 项目进展顺利。(被动语态相对少见，通常用主动语态)
The situation is being fared cautiously by the government. = 政府谨慎地应对局面。(被动语态相对少见，通常用主动语态)


疑问句:
How is he faring? = 他情况如何？
How are things faring? = 事情进展如何？


否定句:
He isn't faring well. = 他情况不好。
The company isn't faring as well as expected. = 公司的业绩不如预期。


PHRASE->
fare well:  进展顺利
fare badly:  进展不顺利
fare forth: 出发；动身
fare better:  情况更好
fare worse: 情况更糟
