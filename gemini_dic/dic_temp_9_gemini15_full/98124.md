insanability: /ˌɪnsænˈæbɪliːti/ | n. |精神错乱；疯狂；无理智 | The state of being insane; unsoundness of mind.  It often refers to a legal determination of mental incompetence to stand trial or manage one's affairs. | 例句：The defendant's lawyer pleaded insanability to avoid a guilty verdict.  被告的律师以精神错乱为由请求免于判决有罪。

The court found him suffering from insanability and unfit to stand trial. 法院认定他患有精神错乱，不适合接受审判。


近义词：insanity, madness, lunacy, derangement, mental illness | 反义词：sanity, soundness of mind, rationality


词源:  Derived from "insane" + "-ability,"  meaning the state or quality of being insane.  "Insane" ultimately comes from the Latin "in-" (not) + "sānus" (sound, healthy, sane).
