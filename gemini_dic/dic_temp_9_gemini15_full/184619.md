restrains:/rɪˈstreɪnz/| vt. |抑制；约束；克制；阻止|To prevent someone or something from doing something; to limit or control the freedom of someone or something.|例句：The government is trying to restrain inflation. 政府正在努力抑制通货膨胀。|例句：He could barely restrain his anger. 他几乎无法抑制自己的怒火。|近义词：restrict, control, curb, suppress|反义词：release, unleash, liberate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He restrains his emotions. = 他克制自己的情绪。
The government restrains the power of the corporations. = 政府限制企业的权力。


一般过去时:
She restrained herself from crying. = 她忍住了哭泣。
The police restrained the suspect. = 警察控制住了嫌疑人。


现在完成时:
They have restrained their spending. = 他们已经控制了支出。
The dam has restrained the floodwaters. = 水坝控制了洪水。


过去完成时:
He had restrained his laughter until the meeting ended. = 他一直忍住笑，直到会议结束。
The security guards had restrained the protestors before they could cause any damage. =保安在抗议者造成任何破坏之前就控制住了他们。


将来时:
We will restrain the dog with a leash. = 我们将用皮带拴住狗。
The new law will restrain the spread of the disease. = 新法律将抑制疾病的传播。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would restrain myself. = 如果我是你，我会克制自己。
It would be better if you restrained your criticism. = 如果你能克制你的批评会更好。


被动语态:
His anger was restrained with difficulty. = 他的怒火好不容易才被抑制住。
The prisoner was restrained by the guards. = 囚犯被警卫控制住了。


疑问句:
Does this belt restrain the movement of the child? = 这条带子能限制孩子的活动吗？
What measures can restrain the spread of misinformation? = 采取什么措施可以抑制虚假信息的传播？


否定句:
He couldn't restrain his tears. = 他忍不住哭了。
The government failed to restrain the economic downturn. = 政府未能抑制经济下滑。
