英文解析:
As a noun, "shingle" refers to a small, thin piece of wood, slate, or other material used as a covering for roofs or walls.  It can also refer to a small, flat stone, especially one found on a beach.  In American slang, it can mean a barbershop. As a verb, "shingle" means to cover a roof or wall with shingles.

例句：
The old house needed new shingles on its roof. = 那栋老房子需要给屋顶换新的瓦片。

They used colorful shingles to give the building a unique look. = 他们用色彩鲜艳的瓦片使建筑物看起来独一无二。


近义词：
tile, slate (for roof covering); pebble, stone (for small stones)


反义词：
There isn't a direct antonym, but words like "bare" or "uncovered" could be used in contrast to a shingled roof.

词源:
The word "shingle" comes from the Middle English word "shingle," which in turn derives from Old English *scingel, meaning "a thin strip of wood."  The word's ultimate origins are likely Germanic, related to words meaning "to cleave" or "to split."
