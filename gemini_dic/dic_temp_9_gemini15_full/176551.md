re-seizing: /ˌriːˈsiːzɪŋ/ | v. |  重新夺取；再次抓住 | The act of seizing something again; regaining possession or control.  |例句：The government is focused on re-seizing control of the volatile situation. 政府正集中精力重新控制动荡的局势。


The police are attempting to re-seizing the stolen artifacts. 警方正试图重新夺回被盗文物。


近义词：recapturing, recovering, reclaiming | 反义词：releasing, surrendering, relinquishing

They re-seize the property every year. = 他们每年都重新夺回那处房产。
The company re-seizes control of the market. = 公司重新控制了市场。

一般过去时:
The army re-seized the city after a long battle. = 经过一场长时间的战斗后，军队重新夺回了这座城市。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

词源:  The word is a combination of the prefix "re-" (again) and the verb "seize" (to take hold of suddenly and forcibly).  "Seize" itself originates from Old French *seisir, from Vulgar Latin *exceptiare, from Latin *capere (to take).
