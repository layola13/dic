didst:/dɪdst/| v. | 你做了(古英语第二人称单数过去时)|The archaic second-person singular past tense form of "do". It is rarely used in modern English, primarily appearing in literature, poetry, and religious texts to create a formal or historical tone.  | Example: Didst thou see the light? = 你看见那道光了吗？|近义词:did (modern equivalent)|反义词:None, as it's a verb tense.
