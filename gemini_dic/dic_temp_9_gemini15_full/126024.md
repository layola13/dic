mussels:/ˈmʌsəlz/ | n. |贻贝，淡菜 | Edible saltwater bivalve mollusks, often eaten as seafood.  They are typically found attached to rocks or other hard surfaces in the ocean. |例句：We had mussels steamed with garlic and white wine for dinner.  我们晚餐吃了蒜蓉白葡萄酒蒸贻贝。

例句：The restaurant is famous for its delicious mussels in a creamy sauce. 这家餐馆以其美味的奶油酱贻贝而闻名。


近义词：shellfish, clams (部分情况下) | 反义词：无


词源:  The word "mussel" comes from Old English *mussele, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *muslō,  related to Old High German muschila "mussel".  The word's origins suggest a long history of mussel consumption in Europe.
