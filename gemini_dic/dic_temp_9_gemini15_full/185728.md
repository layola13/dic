revet:/rɪˈvɛt/| n. v.|护坡；加固；铆接|A revetment is a structure that is used to protect a slope or bank from erosion.  It can be made of various materials, such as stone, concrete, or timber.  As a verb, it means to protect (a slope or bank) with a revetment; to fasten (something) firmly. |例句：The riverbank was reinforced with a strong revetment. 河岸用坚固的护坡加固了。 The engineer decided to revet the unstable slope to prevent landslides. 工程师决定对不稳定的斜坡进行护坡以防止山体滑坡。|近义词：reinforce, strengthen, protect|反义词：weaken, erode, destabilize

词源:
From Old French *reveter, from re- + veter "to clothe, cover."  The original sense was to cover or clothe something, and this evolved to mean to protect or reinforce a surface, especially a slope or bank.
