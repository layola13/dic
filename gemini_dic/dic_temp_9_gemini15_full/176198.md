re-assembly's:/ˌriːəˈsɛmbliːz/| n. |重新组装的；重新组装的过程|The process of putting something back together again after it has been taken apart.  It often refers to the result of this process, the reassembled item itself, or its state.  The possessive 's' indicates ownership or relation.|例句：The re-assembly's complexity surprised the engineers. = 重新组装的复杂性让工程师们大吃一惊。

The re-assembly's success depended on careful attention to detail. = 重新组装的成功取决于对细节的仔细关注。|近义词：reconstruction, reinstallation, recomposition|反义词：disassembly, dismantling|名词
