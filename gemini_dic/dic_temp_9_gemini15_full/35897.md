cocooning: /kəˈkuːnɪŋ/ | n. | 茧居；隐居；自我封闭 | The act of staying at home and avoiding social contact, often for extended periods.  This behavior is often associated with relaxation and self-care, but can also indicate social anxiety or avoidance. | 例句：After a stressful week at work, she enjoyed a weekend of cocooning, watching movies and reading books.  周末工作压力很大后，她享受了一个周末的茧居时光，看电影和看书。

例句：He's been cocooning himself since the pandemic started, rarely leaving his apartment. 自从疫情开始以来，他就一直把自己封闭起来，很少离开公寓。


近义词：seclusion, withdrawal, isolation, self-imposed confinement


反义词：socializing, outgoingness, extroversion


词性:名词

词源:  The word "cocooning" is derived from the noun "cocoon," which refers to the protective covering spun by a moth larva.  The metaphorical extension to human behavior emphasizes the sense of protection, seclusion, and self-enclosure provided by the activity.
