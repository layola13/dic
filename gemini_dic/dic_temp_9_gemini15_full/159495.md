polish:/ˈpɒlɪʃ/ /ˈpɑːlɪʃ/| n., v.|抛光；擦亮；改进；波兰语|

英文解析:As a noun, polish refers to the act of making something smooth and shiny by rubbing it. As a verb, it means to make something smooth and shiny, or to improve something by refining or perfecting it.  It can also refer to the language spoken in Poland.

例句:

He used polish to shine his shoes. 他用鞋油擦亮鞋子。

The writer spent months polishing his manuscript.  这位作家花了几个月的时间润色他的手稿。


近义词:buff, shine, refine, improve, embellish

反义词:roughen, deteriorate, worsen

词源:
The word "polish" ultimately derives from the Old French word "poliss-, polir," meaning "to polish" or "to make smooth". This in turn has roots in the Latin word "polire," with the same meaning.  The meaning evolved to include the sense of refinement and improvement over time.
