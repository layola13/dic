ditching:/ˈdɪtʃɪŋ/| v.|抛弃；丢弃；放弃；排干（水）；|The act of getting rid of something or someone; abandoning something.|例句：He was ditching his old car for a new one. 他正把他的旧车换成一辆新车。

例句：The farmer is ditching the field to prevent flooding. 农民正在排干田里的水以防止洪水。


近义词：abandoning, discarding, relinquishing, scrapping|反义词：keeping, retaining, preserving|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are ditching their old plans. = 他们在放弃他们的旧计划。
He ditches his responsibilities. = 他逃避责任。


一般过去时:
She ditched her boyfriend. = 她甩了她的男朋友。
They ditched the broken car by the roadside. = 他们在路边抛弃了那辆坏车。


现在完成时:
We have ditched our unhealthy habits. = 我们已经改掉了不健康的习惯。
He has ditched his old friends. = 他已经抛弃了他的老朋友。


过去完成时:
They had ditched the evidence before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前，他们已经销毁了证据。
She had ditched her plans to go to the party. = 她已经放弃了去参加聚会的计划。


将来时:
I will ditch this project if it fails. = 如果这个项目失败，我将放弃它。
They will ditch their old house and buy a new one. = 他们将放弃他们的旧房子，买一栋新的。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would ditch that idea. = 如果我是你，我会放弃那个主意。
I wish I had ditched that job sooner. = 我真希望我早点离开那份工作。


被动语态:
The old car was ditched in the woods. = 那辆旧车被丢弃在树林里。
The plan was ditched because of funding issues. = 由于资金问题，计划被放弃了。


疑问句:

否定句:
