dreibund: /ˈdraɪbʊnd/ | n. |三国同盟|The Dreibund was a military alliance between Germany, Austria-Hungary, and Italy from 1882 to 1915.  It was a significant factor in the lead-up to World War I.  The alliance aimed to maintain a balance of power in Europe and protect each member's interests. However, the alliance ultimately fractured due to diverging national interests and ultimately led to Italy switching sides during the war.|The formation of the Dreibund marked a significant shift in European power dynamics.|近义词：triple alliance, military pact|反义词：N/A|名词
