craft:/kræft/ | /krɑːft/| n., v.| 手艺；工艺；技巧；制作；精巧地制作 | Skill in making or doing something, as a hobby or profession; an activity involving skill in making things.  |例句：She has a great craft in knitting. 她擅长编织。

The ancient Egyptians were masters of many crafts. 古埃及人是许多手艺的大师。


近义词：skill, art, trade, profession, handiwork | 反义词：clumsiness, awkwardness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He crafts beautiful pottery. = 他制作精美的陶器。
She crafts compelling narratives. = 她创作引人入胜的故事。


一般过去时:
They crafted a detailed plan. = 他们制定了一个详细的计划。
He crafted a beautiful wooden bird. = 他雕刻了一只精美的木鸟。


现在完成时:
She has crafted several award-winning films. = 她已经制作了几部获奖电影。
They have crafted a strong business strategy. = 他们制定了一个强大的商业战略。


过去完成时:
They had crafted a proposal before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前起草了一份提案。
She had crafted a stunning piece of jewelry.  = 她制作了一件令人惊艳的珠宝。


将来时:
He will craft a response to the letter. = 他将起草一封回信。
They will craft a new marketing campaign. = 他们将策划一场新的营销活动。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would craft a more detailed report. = 如果我有更多时间，我会撰写一份更详细的报告。
I wish I could craft a solution to this problem. = 我希望我能找到解决这个问题的方法。


被动语态:
The sculpture was carefully crafted. = 这尊雕塑是精心制作的。
The policy was crafted by a team of experts. = 这项政策是由一个专家团队制定的。


疑问句:
How was this beautiful vase crafted? = 这个漂亮的花瓶是怎么制作的？
What materials were used to craft this piece of furniture? = 制作这件家具用了什么材料？


否定句:
He didn't craft a convincing argument. = 他没有提出令人信服的论点。
She couldn't craft a solution to the problem. = 她无法找到解决这个问题的方法。


PHRASE->
handicraft: 手工艺

master craft:  大师级工艺


词源:
From Old English *cræft, from Proto-Germanic *kraptaz (“strength, power, skill”), from Proto-Indo-European *(k)kreh₁- (“to take, seize”).  The word's evolution reflects a shift from the original meaning of "strength, power" to the specialized sense of "skillful making" over time.
