embroil:/ɪmˈbrɔɪl/ | vt. |使卷入；使陷入；使混乱 | To involve someone or something in a difficult or complicated situation, especially a conflict or argument.  |例句：The scandal embroiled several prominent politicians.  这场丑闻使几位著名政客陷入其中。

例句：The family was embroiled in a bitter inheritance dispute. 这个家庭卷入了一场激烈的遗产纠纷。


近义词：involve, entangle, implicate | 反义词：extract, disentangle, extricate | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He embroils himself in every argument. 他总是卷入每一场争论。
The company embroils itself in unnecessary lawsuits. 这家公司总是卷入不必要的诉讼。


一般过去时:
The war embroiled many innocent civilians. 那场战争使许多无辜平民卷入其中。
The conflict embroiled the two neighboring countries. 这场冲突使这两个邻国陷入其中。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
