reorganises:/ˌriːˈɔːrɡənaɪzɪz/ | v. | 重组，改组 | To reorganize means to restructure or rearrange something, typically a system, organization, or process, in order to improve efficiency or effectiveness.  It implies a significant change in the structure or operation. | The company reorganised its sales department to improve performance. 公司为了提高业绩重组了销售部门。  | The government is reorganising its healthcare system. 政府正在重组医疗体系。 | restructure, revamp, reform | disorganize, disarrange | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company reorganises its structure annually. = 公司每年都会重组其结构。
He reorganises his files every month. = 他每个月都会整理他的文件。


一般过去时:
They reorganised the team after the merger. = 合并后，他们重组了团队。
She reorganised her schedule to fit in the new project. = 她重新安排了日程表以适应新的项目。


现在完成时:
The company has reorganised several times this decade. = 这十年来，公司已经重组了好几次。
He has reorganised his life priorities after the accident. = 事故后，他重新安排了他的生活优先级。


过去完成时:
They had reorganised the department before the new manager arrived. = 新经理到来之前，他们已经重组了部门。
She had reorganised her notes before the exam. = 考试前，她已经整理好笔记了。


将来时:
The firm will reorganise its operations next year. = 公司明年将重组其运营。
We will reorganise the meeting schedule after the holiday. = 假期过后，我们将重新安排会议日程。


虚拟语气:
