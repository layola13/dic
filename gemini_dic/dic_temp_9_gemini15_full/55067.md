disregard:/ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑːrd/| vt.|忽略，漠视，不理会|To ignore or pay no attention to something.|He disregarded the warnings and continued to drive recklessly. 他无视警告，继续鲁莽驾驶。|ignore, neglect, overlook|heed, consider, attend to|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They disregard the rules. = 他们无视规则。
He disregards my advice. = 他不理会我的建议。

一般过去时:
She disregarded his feelings. = 她忽视了他的感受。
I disregarded the email. = 我忽略了那封邮件。

现在完成时:
We have disregarded the evidence. = 我们已经忽略了证据。
They have disregarded the warnings repeatedly. = 他们一再无视警告。

过去完成时:
He had disregarded the danger signs. = 他之前忽略了危险信号。
She had disregarded her doctor's advice. = 她之前无视了医生的建议。

将来时:
I will disregard his comments. = 我将忽略他的评论。
They will disregard the contract. = 他们将无视合同。

虚拟语气:
If I disregarded the law, I would face the consequences. = 如果我违反法律，我将面临后果。
If she had disregarded the warning, she would have been injured. = 如果她无视警告，她就会受伤。

被动语态:
The warning was disregarded. = 警告被忽略了。
His advice was disregarded. = 他的建议被忽略了。

疑问句:
Did they disregard the instructions? = 他们是否忽略了指示？
Will they disregard the agreement? = 他们会无视协议吗？

否定句:
I didn't disregard his feelings. = 我没有忽视他的感受。
They didn't disregard the importance of the task. = 他们没有忽视这项任务的重要性。
