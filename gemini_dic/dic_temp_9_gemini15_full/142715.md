otiosities:/ˌoʊʃiˈɒsətiz/ | n. |冗长；赘述；无聊的事物 |  Unnecessary or excessive detail; something tiresome or tedious.  The lengthy descriptions in the novel were mere otiosities, distracting from the main plot.  |  近义词：verbosity, prolixity, superfluities, trivialities | 反义词：conciseness, brevity


例句：

His speech was filled with otiosities that bored the audience.  他的演讲充满了冗长的内容，让听众感到厌烦。

The book contained many otiosities that could have been easily omitted. 这本书包含许多可以轻松省略的冗余内容。


词源:

From Late Latin *otiositas, from otiosus "leisurely, idle," from otium "leisure."  The sense evolved from "idle things" to "unnecessary details."


用法说明：

otiosities 通常指不必要的细节或冗长乏味的陈述，这些内容通常会分散注意力，并使主要信息变得模糊不清。它通常用于描述书面或口头表达中过多的、不必要的细节。


注意：由于该词本身就包含负面含义，因此在使用时应谨慎，避免造成冒犯。  它通常用于评论性的语境中，而不是直接描述某人的言行。
