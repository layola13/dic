hickories:/ˈhɪkəriːz/ | n. |山核桃树（复数）；山核桃（复数）|The word "hickories" is the plural form of "hickory," referring to a type of tree in the genus Carya, known for its hard wood and edible nuts.  It can also refer to the nuts themselves. |例句：The forest was filled with towering hickories. 这片森林里长满了高大的山核桃树。

例句： We collected many hickories from the woods. 我们从树林里收集了很多山核桃。

|近义词：pecan (if referring to the nut), walnut (if referring to the wood)|反义词：无|名词

词源:
The word "hickory" comes from the Powhatan language, a Native American language spoken in Virginia.  Various spellings and variations existed before it settled into its current form.  The exact Powhatan word it derives from is uncertain, but it is connected to the tree and its nuts.
