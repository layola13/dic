deregulators: /ˌdiːˈreɡjuleɪtərz/ | n. | 去监管者；放松管制者 | Those who advocate for or implement policies that reduce government regulation.  They believe that less government intervention leads to greater economic efficiency and growth. | Example: The rise of deregulators in the financial industry led to increased risk-taking and ultimately contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.  去监管者在金融业的兴起导致了风险的增加，最终导致了2008年的金融危机。 | 近义词：liberalizers, free-marketeers | 反义词：regulators, interventionists | 名词
