infinitival: /ˌɪnfɪnɪˈteɪvəl/ | adj. | 不定式的；不定词的 | Relating to or of the nature of an infinitive.  An infinitival phrase is a phrase that begins with an infinitive (to + verb). |The infinitival phrase "to run a marathon" is ambitious.  不定式短语“跑马拉松”很雄心勃勃。|近义词：infinitive-based |反义词：finite (in the context of verb forms)


USAGE->
This word is primarily used to describe phrases, not in various grammatical tenses like "what".  Therefore, a traditional tense conjugation example is not applicable.  Instead, examples will show how "infinitival" describes different types of phrases.


Examples demonstrating the use of "infinitival":

Infinitival phrase as subject:

To err is human. = 犯错是人之常情。
To forgive is divine. = 宽恕是神圣的。


Infinitival phrase as object:

I want to travel the world. = 我想环游世界。
He decided to study abroad. = 他决定出国留学。


Infinitival phrase as complement:

My goal is to succeed. = 我的目标是成功。
His ambition was to become a doctor. = 他的志向是成为一名医生。


Infinitival phrase as adverbial modifier:

To be honest, I don't know. = 说实话，我不知道。
To make a long story short, he failed. = 长话短说，他失败了。


Infinitival phrase with a preposition:

He's looking forward to meeting you. = 他期待着见到你。
She's used to working long hours. = 她习惯于长时间工作。


PHRASE->
Infinitival phrase = 不定式短语
Infinitival clause = 不定式从句
Infinitival construction = 不定式结构
Subject to = 服从于；取决于
In order to = 为了
