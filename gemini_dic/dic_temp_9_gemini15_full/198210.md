shortchange:/ʃɔːrtˈtʃeɪndʒ/ | vt. | 欺诈；少给；使…失望 | To give someone less money than they are owed; to treat someone unfairly or less than they deserve.  | 例句：The cashier shortchanged me by five dollars. = 收银员少给了我五美元。

The store shortchanged me on my order, forgetting to include the promised free gift. = 商店在我的订单上少给了我东西，忘记包含承诺的免费赠品。

近义词：cheat, defraud, swindle, deprive, disappoint | 反义词：overpay, fairly compensate


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The shopkeeper shortchanges customers regularly. = 店主经常欺骗顾客。
He shortchanges his employees on their wages. = 他克扣员工的工资。


一般过去时:
The vendor shortchanged me at the market yesterday. = 卖主昨天在市场上少给了我钱。

现在完成时:

She has felt shortchanged by her education. = 她觉得她的教育让她失望了。


过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

疑问句:
