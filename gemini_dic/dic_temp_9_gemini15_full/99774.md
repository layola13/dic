intermediator:/ˌɪntərˈmiːdieɪtər/| n. |中间人，调解人，媒介|A person who acts as a link between people or groups, especially in order to bring about an agreement or compromise.  An intermediary facilitates communication and negotiation between parties who might otherwise struggle to interact directly. |例句：The lawyer acted as an intermediator between the two disputing parties. 律师充当了这两个争执方的中间人。|例句：A real estate agent often serves as an intermediator in property transactions. 房地产经纪人经常充当房地产交易的中间人。|近义词：mediator, go-between, intermediary, negotiator|反义词：direct participant, principal
