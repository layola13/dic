scurrying:/ˈskʌriɪŋ/| v.| 疾跑；慌忙移动 |  To move quickly and hurriedly, often in a panic or in a disorganized way. | 例句：The mice scurried away when the cat appeared.  老鼠看到猫出现后迅速逃跑了。


例句：The children scurried to their mother when they heard the thunderstorm. 孩子们听到雷雨声时迅速跑到母亲身边。


近义词：dashing, hurrying, rushing, scampering


反义词：dawdling, strolling, sauntering


词性：动词

They scurry to complete the task before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期前匆忙完成任务。

被动语态:  (Scurry is rarely used in the passive voice because it implies active movement.)


疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "scurry" likely originates from a now-obsolete Scottish word that referred to a quick movement or running. The exact etymology is unclear, but it's believed to have evolved from onomatopoeic roots, mimicking the sound of quick, hurried movements.
