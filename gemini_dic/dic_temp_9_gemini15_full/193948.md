self-esteem:/ˌsɛlfɪˈstiːm/| n. |自尊；自信 |Self-esteem refers to an individual's subjective evaluation of their own worth.  It encompasses beliefs about oneself (for example, "I am capable," "I am worthy"), as well as emotional states, such as triumph, despair, pride, and shame.  High self-esteem is generally associated with positive feelings and a sense of self-efficacy, while low self-esteem is often linked to negative emotions and feelings of inadequacy. |Example:  Building children's self-esteem is crucial for their healthy development.  = 建立孩子的自尊心对他们的健康发展至关重要。

|Example: Her self-esteem suffered after the job loss. = 她失业后自尊心受到了打击。|近义词: self-respect, confidence, self-worth |反义词: self-doubt, inferiority complex, low self-worth |名词


词源：

The word "self-esteem" is a compound word formed from "self" and "esteem."

* **Self:**  From Old English *self, *sylf, meaning "same, very." This reflects a sense of identity and individuality.

* **Esteem:** From Old French *estimer, from Latin *aestimare, meaning "to estimate, value, appraise."  This carries the sense of judging one's worth or value.
