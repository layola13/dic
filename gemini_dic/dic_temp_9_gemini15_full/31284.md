chaperon:/ˈʃæpərəʊn/| n. v.|监护人，伴侣；陪伴(尤指年轻女子)| A chaperon is a person, usually a mature woman, who accompanies and supervises young people, especially unmarried women, at social events to ensure their proper behavior.  The verb form means to act as a chaperon.|例句：Her aunt chaperoned her to the dance. 她姑妈陪她去跳舞了。
The teacher chaperoned the students on their field trip.老师在他们的实地考察中陪伴学生们。|近义词：escort, guardian, attendant|反义词：unattended


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She chaperones young girls at social events. = 她会在社交场合陪伴年轻女孩。
He chaperones his daughter to parties. = 他陪女儿去参加派对。


一般过去时:
My mother chaperoned me to my first prom. = 我妈妈陪我去参加我的第一次舞会。
They chaperoned the students during the school trip. = 他们在学校旅行期间陪伴学生们。


现在完成时:
She has chaperoned countless young women over the years. = 多年来，她已经陪伴过无数年轻女子。
We have chaperoned several school trips this year. = 今年我们已经陪同了几次学校旅行。


过去完成时:
She had chaperoned her niece at many events before this one. = 在这次活动之前，她已经陪同她的侄女参加过许多活动。
The teachers had chaperoned the students on several field trips before this one. = 在这次实地考察之前，老师们已经陪伴学生们进行过几次实地考察。


将来时:
I will chaperone my daughter to her graduation party. = 我将陪女儿参加她的毕业舞会。
They will chaperone the students to the museum next week. = 他们下周将陪学生们去博物馆。


虚拟语气:
If I had a chaperone, I would have felt much more comfortable at the party. = 如果我有一个监护人，我会在派对上感觉舒服得多。
I wish I had a chaperone at the concert. = 我希望我在音乐会上有个监护人。


被动语态:
The students were chaperoned by two teachers. = 学生们由两位老师陪同。
The young women were chaperoned throughout the evening. = 年轻女子们整个晚上都有人陪同。


疑问句:

Who will chaperone the group on the trip? = 谁会在旅行中陪伴这个小组？


否定句:
She did not chaperone her daughter to the party. = 她没有陪女儿去参加派对。
The school did not provide chaperones for the trip. = 学校没有为这次旅行提供监护人。
