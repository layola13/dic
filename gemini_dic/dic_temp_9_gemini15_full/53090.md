diphthong:/ˈdɪpθɒŋ/| /ˈdɪpθɔːŋ/| n. |双元音|A diphthong is a vowel sound that is produced by combining two vowel sounds in a single syllable.  The sound begins at one vowel and moves towards another, creating a gliding effect.  The tongue, lips, and jaw change position during the pronunciation.  Examples include the sounds in the English words "boy," "cow," and "pie."|The word "boy" contains a diphthong, a glide from /ɔɪ/.|近义词：glide, vowel combination|反义词：monophthong
