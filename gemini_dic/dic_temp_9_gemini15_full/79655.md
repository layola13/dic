glossolaly:/ˌɡlɒsəˈleɪli/| /ˌɡlɑːsəˈleɪli/| n.|说方言，说灵言，说天语|The utterance of speech that is unintelligible to the speaker and others, often associated with religious or spiritual experiences.  It is considered by some to be a divinely inspired gift, while others attribute it to psychological factors.|例句：Some Pentecostal Christians believe glossolaly is a sign of the Holy Spirit.  一些五旬节教徒相信说灵言是圣灵的标志。

例句：The researcher studied the neurological basis of glossolaly in different religious contexts. 研究人员研究了不同宗教背景下说灵言的神经学基础。


近义词：xenoglossy, speaking in tongues|反义词：clear speech, articulate speech


词源: From Greek glossa ("tongue") + lalein ("to speak").  The term combines the idea of "tongue" with the act of "speaking," referring to the seemingly foreign or unknown language produced.
