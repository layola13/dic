requisitioning: /ˌrɛkwɪˈzɪʃənɪŋ/ | v. | 征用；强征；正式请求 | The act of demanding or seizing something, especially property or services, by or for the government or an authorized body.  It often implies a compulsory taking, rather than a voluntary request. | 例句：The army requisitioned all available vehicles.  军队征用了所有可用的车辆。

例句：The government requisitioned the factory to produce war materials. 政府征用了这家工厂来生产军需物资。


近义词：commandeering, seizing, appropriating, demanding, requesting


反义词：releasing, returning, relinquishing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government requisitiones supplies regularly. = 政府定期征用物资。
The company requisitiones new equipment frequently. = 公司经常正式请求新设备。


一般过去时:
They requisitioned the building for use as a hospital. = 他们征用了那栋建筑作为医院。
The military requisitioned all the available fuel. = 军方征用了所有可用的燃料。


现在完成时:
The authorities have requisitioned several properties for public use. = 当局已征用了数处房产用于公共用途。
The company has requisitioned new software for its employees. = 公司已为员工正式请求了新的软件。


过去完成时:
They had requisitioned the warehouse before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们已经征用了仓库。
The general had requisitioned additional troops before the battle. = 将军在战斗前已正式请求增兵。


将来时:
The army will requisition more resources if the conflict escalates. = 如果冲突升级，军队将征用更多资源。
The company will requisition additional office space next year. = 公司明年将正式请求额外的办公空间。


虚拟语气:
If they requisitioned more equipment, the project would be finished sooner. = 如果他们征用更多设备，项目就能更快完成。
I wish they had requisitioned the supplies earlier. = 我真希望他们早点征用物资。


被动语态:
The building was requisitioned by the government. = 这栋建筑被政府征用了。
The resources were requisitioned for the emergency relief efforts. = 资源被征用用于紧急救济工作。


疑问句:
Were any supplies requisitioned during the emergency? = 紧急情况下征用了任何物资吗？
Has the company requisitioned the necessary materials? = 公司是否已正式请求必要的材料？


否定句:
The government did not requisition any private property. = 政府没有征用任何私人财产。
The company did not requisition additional funding for the project. = 公司没有为该项目正式请求额外的资金。

词源:
From Old French *requisition, from Latin requisitionem (nominative requisitio), from requirere "to demand, require," from re- "again" + quaerere "to seek."
