renormalizing: /ˌriːˈnɔːrməlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 再正规化；重新归一化 | The process of applying a renormalization procedure again, often to refine or improve a previous calculation or model.  It involves adjusting parameters or values in a system to account for previously ignored or underestimated factors, typically to maintain consistency or accuracy. | Example: The physicists were renormalizing the quantum field theory to account for the infinities appearing in the calculations.  物理学家正在重新归一化量子场论以解释计算中出现的无穷大。 | 近义词: recalibrating, readjusting, refining | 反义词: leaving unchanged, ignoring
