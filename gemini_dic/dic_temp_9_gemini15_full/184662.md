restrive:/rɪˈstrɪkt/| v.|限制，约束 |To limit the size, amount, or extent of something.|例句：The government has restricted the sale of alcohol. 政府已经限制了酒类的销售。|例句：His movements were restricted by his injuries. 他的行动受到了伤势的限制。|近义词：limit, confine, restrain|反义词：expand, enlarge, liberate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We restrict access to sensitive information. = 我们限制对敏感信息的访问。
The rules restrict what we can do. = 规则限制了我们能做什么。

一般过去时:
They restricted the number of participants. = 他们限制了参与者的数量。
The company restricted its spending. = 公司限制了它的支出。


现在完成时:
They have restricted access to the building. = 他们已经限制了进入大楼的权限。
We have restricted our use of plastic. = 我们已经限制了我们对塑料的使用。


过去完成时:
They had restricted the flow of traffic before the accident. = 事故发生前他们已经限制了交通流量。
The government had restricted imports before the crisis. = 危机爆发前政府已经限制了进口。


将来时:
We will restrict the use of this technology. = 我们将限制这项技术的应用。
The new law will restrict smoking in public places. = 新法将限制在公共场所吸烟。


虚拟语气:
If they restricted the budget, the project would succeed. = 如果他们限制预算，项目就能成功。
I wish they restricted the noise level. = 我希望他们能限制噪音水平。


被动语态:
Access to the website is restricted. = 网站访问受到了限制。
The sale of weapons is restricted by law. = 武器销售受法律限制。


疑问句:
Does the law restrict the use of drones? = 法律是否限制无人机的使用？
Are there any rules that restrict our activities? = 有什么规则限制我们的活动吗？


否定句:
The government did not restrict the flow of information. = 政府没有限制信息的流动。
We do not restrict access to our database. = 我们不限制对我们数据库的访问。


PHRASE->
restrict access = 限制访问

restrict growth = 限制增长
