reenslaving: /ˌriːˈɪnˌsleɪvɪŋ/ | v. | 再奴役；重新奴役 | The act of enslaving someone again after they have been freed.  This term highlights the repetition and perpetuation of slavery. It implies a return to a previous state of enslavement, often after a period of freedom or perceived liberation.  | Example:  The reenslaving of freed people after the Civil War was a horrific violation of human rights.  = 内战后对被解放人民的再次奴役是对人权的可怕侵犯。 | 近义词: re-enslavement, re-subjugation | 反义词: liberation, emancipation |
