ducker:/ˈdʌkər/| n. |躲闪者；鸭子；(尤指用于射击练习的) 靶鸭 | A person or thing that ducks or dives; a decoy duck used in shooting practice.  |例句：The boxer was a clever ducker, avoiding many punches. 拳击手是个灵巧的躲闪者，躲过了许多拳击。

例句：The hunter used a ducker to attract the ducks. 猎人用一个靶鸭来吸引野鸭。


近义词：diver, evader | 反义词：None specifically, context-dependent.


词源:  From *duck* (the bird) +‎ *-er (agent suffix).  The meaning extended from the action of a duck to a person who ducks or avoids something.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ducks when the ball comes. = 球来的时候他躲闪。
She ducks under the low branch. = 她躲过了低矮的树枝。


一般过去时:
He ducked to avoid the blow. = 他躲闪以避免打击。
She ducked her head as she entered the doorway. = 她进门时低下了头。


现在完成时:
He has ducked every question so far. = 到目前为止，他一直躲避着每一个问题。
She has ducked responsibility for her actions. = 她一直逃避对她行为的责任。


过去完成时:
He had ducked before the punch landed. = 拳击落地之前他已经躲闪了。
She had ducked several times to avoid being seen. = 她已经躲闪了几次以避免被看到。


将来时:
He will duck if he sees the ball coming. = 如果他看到球来了，他会躲闪。
She will duck under the table to hide. = 她会躲到桌子底下藏起来。


虚拟语气:
If he had ducked, he wouldn't have been hit. = 如果他躲闪了，他就不会被打中。
If she had ducked sooner, she would have avoided the collision. = 如果她早点躲闪，她就避免了碰撞。


被动语态:
The question was ducked by the politician. = 这个问题被政客回避了。
The ball was ducked expertly by the player. = 球被运动员巧妙地躲过了。


疑问句:

否定句:
He didn't duck; he stood his ground. = 他没有躲闪；他坚守阵地。
She didn't duck the responsibility. = 她没有逃避责任。
