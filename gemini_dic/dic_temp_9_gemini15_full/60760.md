electrography:/ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒɡrəfi/ | n. | 电子描记术；肌电图描记术 | The process of recording electrical activity, particularly in muscles or the brain.  It involves using electrodes to detect and record these signals, which are then displayed as waveforms.  This technique is used in various medical and scientific fields for diagnostic and research purposes. | Example: Electromyography (EMG), a type of electrography, is commonly used to diagnose muscle disorders. 肌电图（EMG），一种电子描记术，常用于诊断肌肉疾病。 | 近义词：electromyography (EMG), electroencephalography (EEG) | 反义词：N/A
