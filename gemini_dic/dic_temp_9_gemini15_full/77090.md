garrisons:/ˈɡærɪsnz/| n. |卫戍部队；驻军；兵营 | A garrison is a group of soldiers stationed in a particular place, especially a town or fort, to protect it.  It can also refer to the act of providing such troops or the building where they are stationed. |例句：The town was protected by a strong garrison.  这个城镇受到一支强大的卫戍部队的保护。

The enemy established a large garrison in the conquered city. 敌军在被征服的城市建立了一个大型驻军。


近义词：troops, military force, occupation force |反义词：None directly opposite, but could be contrasted with civilian population or unarmed citizens. |词性：名词
