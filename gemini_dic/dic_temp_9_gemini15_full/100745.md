intransitives: /ɪnˈtrænzɪtɪvz/ | n. |不及物动词|不及物动词；不带宾语的动词|Intransitive verbs are verbs that do not take a direct object.  They describe actions or states of being that do not directly affect anything else.  For example, "sleep," "run," and "exist" are intransitive verbs.|The bird sings beautifully.  鸟儿唱歌很美。

The sun sets in the west. 太阳在西方落下。
