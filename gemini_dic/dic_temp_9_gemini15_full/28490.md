carcinomata:/ˌkɑːrsɪˈnoʊmətə/| n. |癌瘤（复数）| Malignant neoplasms (tumors) of epithelial origin.  Carcinomata are cancers that arise from epithelial cells, which line the surfaces of organs and cavities throughout the body.  They are characterized by uncontrolled cell growth and the potential to invade surrounding tissues and metastasize (spread) to other parts of the body.  Different types of carcinomata are classified based on the specific type of epithelial cells from which they originate and their location in the body. | 例句：The patient's advanced carcinomata required aggressive treatment.  这位患者的晚期癌瘤需要积极治疗。

The biopsy revealed multiple carcinomata in the patient's lungs. 活检显示患者肺部有多处癌瘤。| 近义词：cancers, malignancies, neoplasms | 反义词：benign tumors, healthy tissue | 名词
