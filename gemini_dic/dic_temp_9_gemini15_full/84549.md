hammer-down: /ˈhæmərdaʊn/ | adj. |全力以赴的；彻底的；猛击的|

英文解析:The term "hammer-down" is an idiom that means to do something with maximum effort or intensity. It suggests a forceful and relentless action, like hitting something repeatedly with a hammer until it's completely broken or submitted.  It can also describe something being done comprehensively or thoroughly.

例句:

He pushed the car to the limit, a true hammer-down performance. 他把车开到了极限，一次真正全力以赴的表现。

The team implemented a hammer-down strategy to meet the deadline.  团队实施了全力以赴的策略以赶上最后期限。


近义词:full-throttle, all-out, relentless, thorough, complete, intense

反义词:gentle, gradual, hesitant, half-hearted, incomplete


词源:The term originates from the action of using a hammer to forcefully strike something.  The "hammer" part refers to the tool, and "down" indicates the downward force applied. The idiom extends this literal image to describe any forceful and determined action.
