ferrocalcite:/ˌfɛroʊˈkælˌsaɪt/| n. |铁方解石|A variety of calcite containing iron.  It's a carbonate mineral with iron impurities that affect its color and properties.  The iron content varies, leading to different shades ranging from yellowish-brown to dark brown or even black. |Example: The geologist identified the sample as ferrocalcite due to its characteristic brownish hue and iron content.|近义词: siderite (if iron content is significantly high)|反义词: calcite (pure, without significant iron)|名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense variations are not applicable.  The usage is descriptive, identifying a specific type of mineral.

Example 1:  The rock contained veins of ferrocalcite. = 这块岩石含有铁方解石脉。

Example 2:  Ferrocalcite is less common than pure calcite. = 铁方解石比纯方解石少见。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases associated with "ferrocalcite".  Its usage is limited to geological and mineralogical contexts.
