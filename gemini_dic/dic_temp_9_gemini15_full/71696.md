flavin:/ˈfleɪvɪn/| n.|黄素|A group of light-sensitive organic compounds that are widely distributed in nature, particularly in plants and microorganisms. They act as electron carriers in various metabolic processes, such as photosynthesis and respiration.  They are also precursors to various other biologically important molecules, such as riboflavin (vitamin B2).|Many flavins are fluorescent, exhibiting bright yellow-green fluorescence under UV light. The fluorescence properties of flavins are important in their various biological functions. The structure of flavins typically involves a three-ring isoalloxazine structure.  Flavin-containing enzymes are often involved in redox reactions.|Riboflavin is a flavin. |近义词：riboflavin (in some contexts)|反义词: none.
