scuffled:/ˈskʌfəld/| v.| 笨拙地行走；扭打；争吵 | To walk with short, quick steps, often making a shuffling sound.  To fight or struggle in a confused way. To engage in a minor argument or squabble. |例句：The children scuffled playfully in the playground. 孩子们在操场上嬉戏打闹。

The two men scuffled briefly before the police intervened.  两名男子在警察介入前短暂扭打了一番。


近义词：shuffle, struggle, grapple, bicker, squabble |反义词：glide, waltz, cooperate, agree

词源:
The word "scuffle" comes from the Middle English word "scufflen," meaning "to shuffle the feet," which itself has uncertain origins, possibly connected to an Old Norse or Dutch root implying a scraping or shuffling motion.
