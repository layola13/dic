hit the panic button: /hɪt ðə ˈpænɪk ˈbʌtən/ | phrasal verb |  惊慌失措；手忙脚乱；采取紧急措施 | To react to a problem or situation with excessive fear and anxiety, often leading to hasty and potentially ineffective actions.  It implies a loss of composure and rational thought in the face of stress. |

例句：

When the server crashed during the presentation, the presenter hit the panic button and started babbling incoherently. = 当演示文稿期间服务器崩溃时，演示者惊慌失措，开始语无伦次地说话。

The stock market plunged, causing many investors to hit the panic button and sell their shares. = 股市暴跌，导致许多投资者惊慌失措地抛售股票。


近义词：lose one's cool, freak out, overreact,  get flustered

反义词：remain calm, stay composed, keep one's head
