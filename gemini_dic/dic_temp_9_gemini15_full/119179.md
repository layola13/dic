mezzanine: /ˌmɛzəˈniːn/ | n. |夹层，半层| A mezzanine is an intermediate floor in a building, typically between two main floors.  It is usually smaller than the floors above and below it and is often used for offices, storage, or other auxiliary purposes.  The term often implies a less substantial structure than a full floor, often with an open balustrade or railing. | The hotel's mezzanine level housed a small bar and lounge.  这家酒店的夹层设有一个小型酒吧和休息室。
