ploughhead:/ˈplaʊhed/| n. |犁头 |The ploughhead is the metal part of a plough that cuts and turns the soil.  It's the crucial component for tilling the land. |例句：The farmer sharpened the ploughhead before beginning to plough the field.  农民在开始耕地之前磨快了犁头。|The ploughshare, share|No direct antonym, but  "unplowed land" could be considered a contextual opposite.|名词
