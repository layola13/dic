rousing: /ˈraʊzɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人振奋的；激动人心的；热烈的 |  Exciting and stimulating; causing great enthusiasm.  | Example:  The band gave a rousing performance. 乐队进行了令人振奋的演出。  |近义词: exciting, stimulating, thrilling, inspiring | 反义词: dull, boring, depressing |  形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The music is rousing the crowd. = 音乐正在激励人群。
The speech is rousing the audience. = 演讲正在激励听众。

一般过去时:
The news was rousing to the citizens. = 这个消息令市民振奋。
The victory rouses the team's spirit. = 胜利鼓舞了球队的士气。


现在完成时:
The campaign has roused considerable public support.  = 这场运动已经激起了相当大的公众支持。
His speech has roused many people to action. = 他的演讲激起了许多人的行动。


过去完成时:
The announcement had roused expectations to a fever pitch. = 这一宣布已将预期推向了高潮。
They had roused the sleeping children. = 他们已经叫醒了熟睡的孩子们。


将来时:
The upcoming concert will rouse the city. = 即将到来的音乐会将使这座城市充满活力。
The new policy will rouse strong opposition. = 新政策将激起强烈反对。


虚拟语气:
If the news were rousing enough, we would celebrate. = 如果消息足够振奋人心，我们会庆祝。
I wish the concert were more rousing. = 我希望音乐会更令人振奋。


被动语态:
The audience was roused by the speaker's passionate words.  = 听众被演讲者充满激情的言语所激励。
The crowd was roused to cheers. = 人群被激起欢呼。


疑问句:
Is the music rousing enough? = 音乐够令人振奋吗？
Does the story rouse your interest? = 这个故事能激起你的兴趣吗？


否定句:
The movie wasn't rousing at all. = 这部电影一点也不令人振奋。
The speech failed to rouse the audience. = 演讲未能激励听众。


PHRASE->

a rousing game 一场精彩的比赛
