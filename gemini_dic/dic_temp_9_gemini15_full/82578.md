guarded:/ˈɡɑːrdɪd/ | adj. | 有戒心的；谨慎的；受保护的；被看守的 |  Describes something or someone that is cautious, reserved, or protected. It implies a sense of suspicion, secrecy, or restriction. |例句：He gave a guarded answer, unwilling to reveal too much. 他给出了一个谨慎的回答，不愿意透露太多。

例句：The president's movements are closely guarded. 总统的行动受到严密的保护。


近义词：cautious, wary, reserved, protected, shielded | 反义词：open, frank, unguarded, exposed


词源: 源自古英语 *gegyrd,  "girded, surrounded," from *gierd, "girdle, belt," related to Old High German *gurti, "girdle."  The sense evolved from being physically surrounded or protected to being mentally or emotionally reserved.
