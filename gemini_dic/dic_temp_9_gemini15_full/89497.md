honeysucker:/ˈhʌniˌsʌkər/| n. |吸蜜鸟|A honeysucker is a bird of the family Meliphagidae, which are found in Australia, New Zealand, and surrounding islands. They are known for their long, slender beaks adapted for feeding on nectar from flowers.  They are also important pollinators.|Many species of honeysuckers have evolved specialized beaks to access nectar from different types of flowers.|Many species of honeysuckers are found in Australia. |近义词： nectarivore (a more general term for nectar-eating animals)|反义词：None specifically, but terms describing birds with different diets could be considered contrasting.|名词
