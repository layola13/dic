chinook:/ʃɪˈnʊk/ | n. | 奇努克风；奇努克语；奇努克人 | A warm, dry wind that blows down the eastern slopes of the Cascade Range in the Pacific Northwest of North America.  It is often associated with rapid temperature increases.  The term also refers to a Chinook language and the people who speak it.|例句：The chinook wind melted the snow in a matter of hours. 奇努克风在几个小时内融化了积雪。  The Chinook people have a rich cultural heritage. 奇努克人拥有丰富的文化遗产。|近义词：foehn (similar warm, dry wind)|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with cold winds like a blizzard. |名词
