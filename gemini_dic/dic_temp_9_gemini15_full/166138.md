preprimitive:/ˌpriːˈprɪmɪtɪv/| adj.|前原始的，更早期的，远古的|  Describing something that existed or developed before what is considered primitive;  often used in a comparative sense to denote an earlier stage of development. It implies a stage even earlier than what is typically termed "primitive".|例句：The preprimitive tools were made of rough stone and bone.  = 那些前原始的工具是用粗糙的石头和骨头制成的。The archaeological evidence suggests a preprimitive culture existed in the region. = 考古证据表明该地区存在着前原始文化。|近义词：proto-, early, antecedent, primordial|反义词：advanced, modern, developed
