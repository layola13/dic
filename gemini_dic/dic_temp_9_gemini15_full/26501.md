/kæbəˈlɛərz/ | n. |骑士们，武士们 |  The word "caballers" is the plural form of "caballer," which is a less common and somewhat archaic word meaning "knight" or "gentleman" in Spanish. It's essentially a more formal and perhaps slightly antiquated way of referring to a group of knights or gentlemen. The word carries a sense of chivalry, honor, and nobility associated with the medieval knightly class.  | Example: The caballers rode forth to defend the kingdom. 骑士们出发去保卫王国。 | 近义词：knights, gentlemen, esquires | 反义词：peasants, villains
