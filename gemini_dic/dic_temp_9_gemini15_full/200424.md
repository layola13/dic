slackening: /ˈslækənɪŋ/ | n. | 松弛；减弱；放慢 | The slackening of the rope caused the bucket to fall.  绳索松弛导致水桶掉落。|  weakening, lessening, deceleration | tightening, strengthening, acceleration | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The demand for the product is slackening. = 对该产品的需求正在减弱。
The pace of work is slackening. = 工作节奏正在放慢。

一般过去时:
The wind slackened in the evening. = 傍晚风力减弱了。
His grip slackened and he dropped the book. = 他握力减弱，书掉了。

现在完成时:
The economy has been slackening for several months. = 经济已经低迷了几个月了。
Interest in the project has slackened considerably. = 对该项目的兴趣已经大大减弱了。

过去完成时:
The rain had slackened before we reached home. = 我们到家之前雨已经小了。
His enthusiasm had slackened after the initial success. = 初步成功后，他的热情减退了。

将来时:
The storm is expected to slacken tomorrow. = 预计暴风雨明天会减弱。
Production will slacken due to the shortage of materials. = 由于材料短缺，产量将下降。

虚拟语气:
If the wind slackened, we could sail further. = 如果风力减弱，我们可以航行更远。
I wish the traffic would slacken so I could get home earlier. = 我希望交通能缓解，这样我就能早点回家了。

被动语态:
The rules were slackened to encourage investment. = 放宽了规则以鼓励投资。
The security measures were slackened, leading to a security breach. = 安全措施有所放松，导致安全漏洞。

疑问句:
Is the traffic slackening? = 交通是否正在缓解？
Has the tension slackened at all? = 紧张局势是否有所缓解？

否定句:
The flow of water did not slacken. = 水流并没有减弱。
The government did not slacken its efforts to fight inflation. = 政府并没有放松打击通货膨胀的努力。
