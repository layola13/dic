champlain:/ʃæmˈpleɪn/| n. |尚普蘭；尚普蘭湖| Champlain refers to Samuel de Champlain, a French explorer and cartographer who played a significant role in exploring and mapping parts of North America, particularly the St. Lawrence River and the Great Lakes region.  It also refers to geographical locations named after him, most notably Lake Champlain. |Example: Lake Champlain is a beautiful lake on the border between the United States and Canada. 尚普蘭湖是美國和加拿大邊界上的一個美麗的湖泊。|近义词: (geographical context) lake, sea, body of water|反义词: N/A
