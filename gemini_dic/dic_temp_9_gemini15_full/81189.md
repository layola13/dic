grandiloquent:/ɡrænˈdɪləkwənt/| adj.|夸夸其谈的；华而不实的；浮夸的 | characterized by lofty and pompous language; bombastic.  The politician's grandiloquent speech failed to impress the audience.  |近义词：bombastic, pompous, inflated, high-flown |反义词：simple, modest, humble, unassuming


例句：

His grandiloquent pronouncements were met with skepticism. 他的夸夸其谈的声明遭到了怀疑。

The author's grandiloquent style obscured the meaning of his work. 作者浮夸的文风掩盖了他作品的意义。


词源：

From the Latin *grandiloquus*, from *grandis* ("grand, great") + *loquor* ("I speak").  The word literally means "speaking grandly."


用法说明:

grandiloquent 主要用来形容语言或写作风格，而不是指人本身。虽然它可以暗示说话者或作者试图显得重要或有学问，但它本身并不直接评价说话者或作者的品格。  它的负面含义在于强调其语言的夸张和浮夸，而非其内容的实质。


PHRASE->

There are no common phrases directly using "grandiloquent".  The word itself functions as a descriptive adjective. However, phrases that could be modified by "grandiloquent" include:

grandiloquent pronouncements: 夸夸其谈的声明
grandiloquent style: 浮夸的风格
grandiloquent speech: 夸夸其谈的演讲
grandiloquent manner: 浮夸的态度
grandiloquent rhetoric: 华而不实的辞藻
