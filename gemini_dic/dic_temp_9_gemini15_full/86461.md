hegelism:/ˈheɪɡəlɪzəm/| n. |黑格尔主义|The philosophical system of Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel, emphasizing the dialectical process of thesis, antithesis, and synthesis in the development of ideas and history.  Hegelism often involves a belief in the inherent rationality of history and the unfolding of Geist (spirit or mind) through the conflict of opposing forces.|  Many scholars criticized Hegelism for its perceived idealism and its justification of the status quo. |近义词：Hegelianism|反义词：Nihilism (in a broad sense, as it rejects Hegel's belief in a rational order)
