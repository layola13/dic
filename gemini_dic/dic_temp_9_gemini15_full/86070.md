hearing:/ˈhɪərɪŋ/ | n. |听力，听觉；听证会；审讯 |The ability to perceive sound.  A formal process of listening to evidence and arguments. |例句：My hearing is not as good as it used to be. 我的听力不如以前好了。

例句：The hearing will commence tomorrow morning. 听证会将于明天上午开始。

|近义词：audition, listening, trial |反义词：deafness, silence |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hears the birds singing. 他听到鸟儿在唱歌。
She hears the news every day. 她每天都收听新闻。


一般过去时:
I heard a strange noise last night. 昨晚我听到一个奇怪的声音。
They heard the announcement. 他们听到了通知。


现在完成时:
I have heard that song many times. 我听过那首歌很多次了。
She has heard the news. 她已经听到了消息。


过去完成时:
I had heard the story before. 我以前听过这个故事。
They had heard the rumors. 他们已经听说了谣言。


将来时:
I will hear from him soon. 我很快就会收到他的来信。
We will hear the results tomorrow. 我们明天将听到结果。


虚拟语气:
If I heard him correctly, he said he was leaving. 如果我听正确的话，他说他要离开了。
I wish I hadn't heard that gossip. 我真希望我没有听到那个八卦。


被动语态:
The announcement was heard by everyone.  公告被大家听到了。
The music was heard throughout the house. 音乐声传遍了整个房子。


疑问句:
Do you hear me? 你听到我说话了吗？
Can you hear the music? 你能听到音乐吗？


否定句:
I didn't hear what you said. 我没听到你说什么。
She doesn't hear well. 她听力不好。


PHRASE->
within hearing = 在听力范围内
give someone a hearing = 认真倾听某人的意见
beyond hearing = 超出听力范围
lose one's hearing = 失去听力
hearing aid = 助听器

词源:
Old English *hēring, from Proto-Germanic *hausjanō, from Proto-Indo-European *(h)k<sup>w</sup>éws- (“to hear”). Cognate with Old High German hōren, Old Saxon hōrian, Old Norse heyra, Gothic hausjan.
