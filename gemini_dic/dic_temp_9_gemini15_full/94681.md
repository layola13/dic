imprisoned: /ɪmˈprɪzənd/ | adj. | 监禁的；被囚禁的 | Deprived of freedom; confined in prison.  |例句：The prisoner felt imprisoned by his circumstances. 囚犯感到他的环境使他被囚禁。

The wrongfully imprisoned man was finally released.  被冤枉监禁的人最终获释了。|近义词：confined, incarcerated, jailed, captive |反义词：free, liberated, released

一般现在时:
He is imprisoned for theft. 他因盗窃被监禁。
The bird is imprisoned in a cage.  鸟被关在笼子里。

一般过去时:
They imprisoned him without trial. 他们未经审判就将他监禁。
She was imprisoned for her political beliefs. 她因政治信仰而被监禁。

现在完成时:
He has been imprisoned for ten years. 他已被监禁十年了。
She has been unjustly imprisoned. 她被不公平地监禁了。

过去完成时:
He had been imprisoned before for the same crime. 他之前曾因同样的罪行被监禁过。
They had already imprisoned him when the new evidence surfaced. 新证据出现时，他们已经将他监禁了。

将来时:
They will imprison him if he is found guilty. 如果他被判有罪，他们将监禁他。
He will be imprisoned for life. 他将被终身监禁。

虚拟语气:
If he were imprisoned, his family would suffer greatly. 如果他被监禁，他的家人将遭受巨大的痛苦。
I wish he hadn't been imprisoned. 我真希望他没被监禁。

被动语态:
He was imprisoned for his actions. 他因自己的行为而被监禁。

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "imprisoned" comes from the Old French word "emprisoner," which itself is derived from the Latin word "in" (in, into) and "prisona" (prison).  The suffix "-ed" indicates the past participle form, signifying a state of being.
