get into:/ɡɛt ˈɪntuː/ | phrasal verb | 进入；陷入；开始从事 |  To enter or become involved in something.  This phrase can have many nuances depending on context. |


例句：

He got into the car and drove away. = 他上了车然后开车走了。

She got into trouble for lying. = 她因为撒谎而惹上了麻烦。


近义词：enter, embark on, engage in, become involved in

反义词：get out of, exit, leave, disengage from


USAGE->

一般现在时:
I get into the habit of reading before bed. = 我养成了睡前阅读的习惯。
They get into arguments frequently. = 他们经常争吵。


一般过去时:
We got into a heated discussion about politics. = 我们就政治问题进行了激烈的讨论。
He got into a car accident last week. = 他上周发生了一起车祸。


现在完成时:
I've gotten into yoga recently. = 我最近开始练习瑜伽。
She's gotten into a lot of debt. = 她欠了很多债。


过去完成时:
Before the exam, I had gotten into a state of panic. = 考试前，我陷入了恐慌状态。
They had gotten into a fight before I arrived. = 我到之前他们就已经打起来了。


将来时:
I will get into the details later. = 我稍后会详细说明。
We will get into serious trouble if we don't finish the project on time. = 如果我们不能按时完成项目，我们将面临严重的麻烦。


虚拟语气:
If I got into a better university, my life would be different. = 如果我进了更好的大学，我的生活就会不一样。
I wish I hadn't gotten into that argument. = 我真希望我没有卷入那场争论。


被动语态:  (The passive voice is less common with "get into")
The car was easily gotten into. = 这辆车很容易进去。
The argument was gotten into quickly and unexpectedly. = 争论很快且出乎意料地开始了。


疑问句:
How did you get into that mess? = 你是怎么陷入这种困境的？
Did you get into the concert easily? = 你很容易就进演唱会了吗？


否定句:
I didn't get into the party. = 我没有参加派对。
He didn't get into any trouble. = 他没有惹上任何麻烦。


PHRASE->
get into trouble = 惹麻烦
get into a habit = 养成习惯
get into debt = 负债
get into an argument = 发生争吵
get into shape = 恢复健康


词源:

The phrasal verb "get into" originates from the verb "get," meaning "to obtain" or "to become," combined with the preposition "into," indicating movement or a state of being inside something.  The meaning evolved over time to encompass a wider range of situations beyond simple physical entry.
