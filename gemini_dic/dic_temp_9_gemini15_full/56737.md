doormats: /ˈdɔːrmæts/ | n. |擦鞋垫，门垫 |A doormat is a piece of material placed at a doorway to wipe dirt and moisture from shoes.  It can also be used figuratively to describe someone who is easily taken advantage of. |例句：We need a new doormat; this one is worn out. = 我们需要一块新的擦鞋垫；这块已经磨损了。

The children left muddy footprints all over the doormat. = 孩子们把泥脚印弄得到处都是。


近义词：mat, rug (in the literal sense) |反义词：None (in the literal sense).  Figuratively, the antonym would relate to someone assertive and not easily manipulated.


词源: The word "doormat" is a compound word, combining "door" and "mat."  "Door" comes from Old English *doru, and "mat" comes from Old English *matte, meaning "a woven fabric." The meaning of "doormat" developed naturally from the object's placement and function.
