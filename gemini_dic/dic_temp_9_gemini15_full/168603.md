prochronize:/ˈproʊkroʊnaɪz/ | vt. |使提前；提前安排|To arrange events or information in a time order that is earlier than their actual occurrence.  This often involves deliberately misrepresenting chronology.|The historian was accused of prochronizing events to fit his narrative.|近义词：anachronize, pre-date|反义词：postdate, anachronism
