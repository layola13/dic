compounded:/kəmˈpaʊndɪd/ | v. adj. | 混合的；加重的；复杂的 | To combine or mix together; to increase or intensify something;  to make more complex or serious. |


例句：

The interest on the loan is compounded annually. = 贷款利息每年复利计算。

The problem was compounded by a lack of communication. = 缺乏沟通使问题更加复杂。


近义词：mixed, combined, intensified, aggravated, exacerbated

反义词：simplified, reduced, lessened, alleviated


词性：动词(v.)，形容词(adj.)


USAGE->

一般现在时:

The bank compounds the interest monthly. = 银行每月计算复利。

She compounds her herbal remedies with honey. = 她用蜂蜜混合她的草药疗方。


一般过去时:

They compounded the ingredients carefully. = 他们仔细地混合了配料。

The crisis was compounded by rising inflation. = 通货膨胀加剧了危机。


现在完成时:

The debt has compounded over the years. = 债务多年来不断累积。

The effects of pollution have compounded significantly. = 污染的影响已显著加剧。


过去完成时:

The situation had been compounded by a series of unfortunate events. = 一系列不幸的事件使情况更加复杂。

By the time the rescue arrived, the problems had compounded dramatically. = 等到救援到来时，问题已严重恶化。


将来时:

The company will compound the profits into a new venture. = 公司将把利润投入新的项目。

Rising temperatures will compound the effects of drought. = 气温上升将加剧干旱的影响。


虚拟语气:

If we compounded the two chemicals, it might explode. = 如果我们混合这两种化学物质，它可能会爆炸。

If the problem had not been compounded, it would have been easily solved. = 如果问题没有恶化，它本来很容易解决。


被动语态:

The situation was compounded by the government's inaction. = 政府的不作为使情况更加复杂。


疑问句:

Is the interest compounded daily or monthly? = 利息是每天复利还是每月复利？

否定句:

词源:

The word "compounded" comes from the Latin word "componere," meaning "to put together" or "to compose."  The prefix "com-" indicates "together," and "ponere" means "to place" or "to put."  Over time, the meaning evolved to include the senses of mixing, intensifying, and making complex.
