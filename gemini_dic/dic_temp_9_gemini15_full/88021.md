high-up: /ˌhaɪˈʌp/ | adj. | 职位高的；地位高的；上层人士的 |  Someone or something high-up is in a position of power or authority, usually within an organization. It emphasizes the elevated status and influence of the person or thing.  |例句：He's a high-up in the government. 他是政府里的高级官员。

例句：She networked with high-ups in the industry. 她与业内的高层人士建立了联系。


近义词：senior, important, influential, powerful, upper-echelon, bigwig

反义词：low-level, subordinate, junior, insignificant, powerless


词性：形容词
