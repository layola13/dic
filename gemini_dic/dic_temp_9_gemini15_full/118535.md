mesohippus:/ˌmɛsoʊˈhɪpəs/| n. | 中部马 |Mesohippus is an extinct genus of early horse that lived during the Oligocene epoch (approximately 33.9 to 23 million years ago). It was a relatively small horse, about the size of a modern sheep, with three toes on each foot.  Mesohippus is considered an important transitional form in the evolution of horses, showing features intermediate between earlier genera like Eohippus and later genera like Merychippus.  |The fossil remains of Mesohippus have been found in North America. |近义词: Miohippus (a closely related genus) |反义词: N/A
