flouted:/ˈflaʊtɪd/| v.|蔑视，嘲笑，公然违反|To treat with contempt; to disregard or violate (a rule, law, etc.) openly.|例句：They flouted the law by speeding. 他们超速行驶，公然违反了法律。|例句：He flouted convention by wearing jeans to the wedding. 他穿着牛仔裤去参加婚礼，公然违反了习俗。|近义词：defy, scorn, scoff at, mock|反义词：obey, respect, adhere to|动词

词源:
The word "flout" ultimately derives from the Old French word "flouter," meaning "to mock" or "to jeer."  This, in turn, has uncertain origins, possibly related to words signifying blowing or puffing.  The sense of "to treat with contempt" developed over time.
