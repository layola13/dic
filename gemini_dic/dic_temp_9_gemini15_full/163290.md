preconduction:/ˌpriːkənˈdʌkʃən/| n. |预传导，前导电|The process leading up to the conduction of an electrical impulse in a nerve or muscle fiber.  It involves the depolarization of the cell membrane and the opening of ion channels.  Preconduction is a crucial step in ensuring the efficient transmission of signals throughout the body.|Example:  Studies on preconduction in cardiac muscle cells have revealed important insights into the mechanisms of arrhythmias.  研究心肌细胞预传导过程揭示了心律失常机制的重要见解。|近义词:preactivation, initiation|反义词:postconduction, cessation|名词
