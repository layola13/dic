hypermodernities:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɒdərnɪtiz/| n. |超现代性；后现代性之后的阶段|Hypermodernities refer to the conditions and experiences of life in the late 20th and early 21st centuries, characterized by rapid technological advancements, globalization, and the fragmentation of traditional social structures.  It builds upon postmodernity but emphasizes the acceleration and intensification of its key features.|The rapid spread of social media is a key characteristic of hypermodernities.|近义词：late modernity, post-postmodernity|反义词：premodernity, tradition


USAGE->

This word is primarily used as a noun and doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs do.  Therefore, traditional tense examples are not applicable.  The usage focuses on its application in sentences to describe phenomena.

Example 1:  The anxieties surrounding climate change are a significant aspect of contemporary hypermodernities. = 气候变化带来的焦虑是当代超现代性一个重要的方面。

Example 2:  Many scholars debate the defining characteristics of hypermodernities and how they differ from postmodernity. = 许多学者正在辩论超现代性的定义特征以及它与后现代性的区别。

The term "hypermodernities" itself is a relatively recent scholarly term and doesn't have common phrasal pairings in the same way as common words. Instead, it's used within descriptive phrases or academic contexts:


Phrase 1:  characteristics of hypermodernities = 超现代性的特征
