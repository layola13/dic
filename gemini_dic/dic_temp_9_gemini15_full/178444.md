reconsolidating:/ˌriːkənˈsɑːlɪdeɪtɪŋ/| v. |重新巩固；重新整合|The act of strengthening or solidifying something again.  This often refers to memories, where the process of reconsolidation refers to the process by which a memory trace is destabilized and then restabilized, making it susceptible to modification or even erasure. It also applies to other areas where something previously consolidated needs to be strengthened or made more secure.|例句：The therapist is helping the patient reconsolidate their traumatic memories by using exposure therapy. 治疗师正在使用暴露疗法帮助病人重新巩固他们的创伤记忆。

例句：The government is attempting to reconsolidate its power base after the recent election. 政府在最近的选举后试图重新巩固其权力基础。

近义词：reinforce, strengthen, restabilize |反义词：weaken, destabilize, dismantle

词性：动词
