pre-liquidation:/ˌpriːˌlɪkwɪˈdeɪʃən/| adj.| 清算前的；破产前的 | Relating to the period of time before a business or company is liquidated.  It often refers to the actions and events leading up to liquidation, such as financial distress, declining performance, and attempts to restructure. | Example: Pre-liquidation negotiations between the company and its creditors failed to reach an agreement.  清算前的公司与债权人之间的谈判未能达成协议。|近义词：pre-bankruptcy, pre-insolvency |反义词：post-liquidation
