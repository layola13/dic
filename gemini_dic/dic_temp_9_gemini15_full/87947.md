high:/haɪ/| adj.|高的，高级的，高度的|  High refers to a large distance from the ground or a base point. It can also describe something of superior quality or rank. |例句：The mountain is very high. 这座山很高。 The company is hiring for a high-paying job. 这家公司正在招聘高薪工作。|近义词：tall, elevated, lofty, superior|反义词：low, short, inferior


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The sun is high in the sky. = 太阳高挂天空。
The price of gas is high this year. = 今年汽油价格很高。


一般过去时:
The plane flew high above the clouds. = 飞机飞得很高，在云层之上。
He scored high on the test. = 他考试考了高分。


现在完成时:
The temperature has been high all week. = 这周气温一直很高。
She has climbed high mountains. = 她攀登过高山。


过去完成时:
The water level had been high before the storm. = 暴风雨来临之前水位很高。
They had already achieved a high level of success. = 他们已经取得了很高的成就。


将来时:
The rocket will fly high into space. = 火箭将飞向高空。
The stock market is expected to be high next year. = 股市预计明年会走高。


虚拟语气:
If I were high enough, I could see the ocean. = 如果我够高，就能看到大海。
I wish I had a high paying job. = 我希望我有一份高薪的工作。


被动语态:
The flag was hoisted high. = 旗帜被高高升起。
The building is considered high-quality. = 这栋建筑被认为是高质量的。


疑问句:
Is the building high enough? = 这栋楼够高吗？
How high can you jump? = 你能跳多高？


否定句:
The bridge isn't high enough for large ships. = 这座桥不够高，大型船只过不去。
His grades weren't high enough to get into that university. = 他的成绩不够高，进不了那所大学。
