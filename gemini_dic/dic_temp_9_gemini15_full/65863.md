evoked:/ɪˈvoʊkt/ | vt. |唤起，引起，使想起 | To evoke something means to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind.  It suggests a strong and often emotional response. |The music evoked memories of her childhood.  这音乐唤起了她童年的回忆。|  provoked, stimulated, triggered | suppressed, ignored |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The painting evokes a sense of peace. = 这幅画唤起了一种平静的感觉。
The smell of rain evokes happy memories. = 雨的味道唤起了快乐的回忆。

一般过去时:
Her speech evoked strong emotions in the audience. = 她的演讲在观众中唤起了强烈的情感。
The photograph evoked a sense of nostalgia. = 这张照片唤起了一种怀旧之情。


现在完成时:
The author's words have evoked a powerful response from readers. = 作者的文字激起了读者强烈的反响。
His performance has evoked considerable debate. = 他的表演引发了相当大的争议。


过去完成时:
The old house had evoked a feeling of unease in him. = 那栋旧房子在他心中唤起了一种不安的感觉。
The news had evoked a wave of panic. = 这个消息引发了一阵恐慌。


将来时:
The upcoming exhibition will evoke a sense of wonder. = 即将举行的展览将唤起一种惊奇感。
The new movie is expected to evoke strong reactions. = 这部新电影预计会引起强烈反响。


虚拟语气:
If the music evoked stronger emotions, the performance would have been better. = 如果音乐能唤起更强烈的情感，演出就会更好。
I wish the book evoked more emotion in me. = 我希望这本书能在我心中激起更多的情感。


被动语态:
A sense of longing was evoked by the sunset. = 日落唤起了渴望之情。
Strong emotions were evoked by the story. = 故事唤起了强烈的情感。


疑问句:
What emotions does this painting evoke in you? = 这幅画在你心中唤起了什么情感？
What memories does this song evoke? = 这首歌唤起了什么回忆？


否定句:
The movie failed to evoke any real emotion. = 这部电影未能唤起任何真正的情感。
The presentation didn't evoke much interest. = 这次演示并没有引起多大兴趣。
