engender:/ɪnˈdʒɛndər/ | vt. | 产生；引起；导致 | To cause or give rise to (a feeling, situation, or condition).  |例句：His policies engendered much controversy. 他的政策引起了很多争议。
例句：The new law is intended to engender trust between the police and the community. 这项新法律旨在增进警察和社区之间的信任。 |近义词：cause, produce, generate, create |反义词：destroy, eliminate, suppress


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The war engenders fear and instability. = 战争产生恐惧和不稳定。
This policy engenders a sense of fairness. = 这项政策带来了公平感。


一般过去时:
The speech engendered a great deal of excitement. = 那次演讲引起了极大的兴奋。
The incident engendered suspicion among the team members. = 这件事在团队成员中引起了怀疑。


现在完成时:
His actions have engendered much criticism. = 他的行为招致了很多批评。
The reforms have engendered significant economic growth. = 改革带来了显著的经济增长。


过去完成时:
The previous government's policies had engendered widespread discontent. = 前政府的政策已引起广泛的不满。
The rumors had engendered fear and panic among the population. = 谣言已经在民众中引起了恐惧和恐慌。


将来时:
The proposed changes will engender significant improvements. = 提议的变革将带来显著的改进。
This new technology will engender many new opportunities. = 这项新技术将带来许多新的机遇。


虚拟语气:
If we were to adopt this policy, it would engender positive change. = 如果我们采用这项政策，它将会带来积极的改变。
I wish this project had not engendered so much conflict. = 我希望这个项目没有引起这么多的冲突。


被动语态:
Great changes were engendered by the revolution. = 革命带来了巨大的变化。
Much controversy is engendered by the new legislation. = 新法案引起了很大的争议。


疑问句:
What factors engender such strong reactions? = 什么因素会引起如此强烈的反应？
Does this approach engender positive results? = 这种方法会产生积极的结果吗？


否定句:
The meeting did not engender any significant progress. = 会议没有取得任何重大进展。
His attempts to improve morale did not engender trust among his employees. = 他试图改善士气，但并没有赢得员工的信任。
