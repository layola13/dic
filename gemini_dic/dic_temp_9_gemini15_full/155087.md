petroglyph:/ˈpɛtrəɡlɪf/| n. |岩刻，石刻|A petroglyph is a rock carving, made by removing part of a rock surface by incising, picking, carving, or abrading.  Petroglyphs are often found in areas with significant cultural or historical importance.|例句：The ancient petroglyphs depict scenes of hunting and daily life.  古代岩画描绘了狩猎和日常生活场景。

The petroglyphs on the canyon walls are thousands of years old.峡谷墙壁上的岩画已有数千年的历史。|近义词：rock carving, carving, inscription|反义词：painting (as a contrasting art form)|名词
