/ˈdɛstɪnəbli/ | adv. | 可预见地，注定地 | Inevitably; as if by fate.  The word suggests an outcome that is predetermined, often implying a sense of inevitability or fate.  It often carries a connotation of something being unavoidable or destined to happen. |


例句：

Destinably, they fell in love. =  他们注定要相爱。

He destinably failed the exam, having made no effort to study. = 他注定考试不及格，因为他根本没有努力学习。


近义词： inevitably, unavoidably, certainly, surely


反义词： unexpectedly, surprisingly, accidentally, coincidentally


词性：副词
