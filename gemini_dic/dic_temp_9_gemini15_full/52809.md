dillydally:/ˈdɪlɪˌdæli/| v.|磨蹭；拖延；浪费时间|To waste time; to dawdle; to be slow and inefficient.|例句：Stop dillydallying and get to work! 别磨蹭了，赶紧工作！

例句：Don't dillydally; we're already late.  别磨蹭了，我们已经迟了。


近义词：dawdle, procrastinate, delay, hesitate, tarry

反义词：hurry, rush, expedite

He dillydallies around all day. 他整天磨磨蹭蹭。
They always dillydally before making decisions. 他们做决定前总是拖拖拉拉。

词源:
The word "dillydally" is of uncertain origin, but it is likely a combination of two older words or phrases.  One theory links it to "dally," meaning to waste time, and an unknown word possibly related to "diddle" (to trifle or cheat).  The exact etymology remains debated among linguists.
