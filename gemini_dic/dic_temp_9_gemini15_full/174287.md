questor:/ˈkwɛstər/| n. |财务官；调查员；提问者| A questor was originally a Roman official responsible for managing public funds.  The term has evolved to also describe someone who investigates or inquires, or even someone who asks many questions. |The questor meticulously examined the financial records.|近义词：treasurer, investigator, inquirer|反义词：respondent, answerer


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The questor manages the company's finances. = 财务官管理着公司的财务。
He quests for answers to his questions. = 他寻求他问题的答案。

一般过去时:
The Roman questor oversaw the distribution of grain. = 罗马财务官监督粮食的分发。
She quested for information about her family history. = 她寻找关于她家族历史的信息。

现在完成时:
The questor has already audited the accounts. = 财务官已经审计了账目。
He has quested for the truth for many years. = 他多年来一直在寻求真相。

过去完成时:
The questor had already prepared the report before the meeting. = 财务官在会议前已准备好报告。
She had quested for answers, but found none. = 她一直在寻找答案，但没有找到。

将来时:
The questor will present the budget next week. = 财务官下周将提交预算。
He will quest for knowledge in distant lands. = 他将在遥远的国度寻求知识。

虚拟语气:
If he were a questor, he would have access to those records. = 如果他是财务官，他就能接触到那些记录。
I wish I were a questor so I could find the answers. = 我希望我是一个调查员，这样我就能找到答案。

被动语态:

The truth will be quested for by many. = 真相将被许多人追寻。

疑问句:
Is he a questor for this organization? = 他是这个组织的财务官吗？

否定句:
He is not a questor, but an accountant. = 他不是财务官，而是一个会计。
