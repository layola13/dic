recarving:/ˌriːˈkɑːrvɪŋ/ | n. | 再雕刻；重新雕刻 | The act of carving something again, often to improve it or create a new design.  It implies a previous carving existed. | Example: The sculptor undertook a recarving of the statue's face to correct a flaw. = 雕塑家对雕像的脸进行了重新雕刻以纠正一个缺陷。|近义词：re-sculpting, refinishing |反义词：destruction, demolition | 名词
