codon:/ˈkoʊdɑːn/| n. |密码子|A codon is a sequence of three nucleotides (a triplet) that forms a unit of genetic code in a DNA or RNA molecule.  Each codon specifies a particular amino acid during protein synthesis or signals the termination of the synthesis. |Example: The codon AUG codes for the amino acid methionine and also serves as a start codon in many organisms.  密码子AUG编码甲硫氨酸，在许多生物体中也充当起始密码子。|近义词：triplet (in this context)|反义词：无
