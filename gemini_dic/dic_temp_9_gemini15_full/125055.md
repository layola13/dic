muleteers:/ˌmjuːlɪˈtɪərz/| n.|骡夫；赶骡子的人|People who drive or lead mules, especially in mountainous or difficult terrain.  They often transport goods or people using mules as beasts of burden.|例句：The muleteers skillfully guided their mules along the treacherous mountain path.  骡夫熟练地引导他们的骡子沿着险峻的山路行走。


The muleteers, burdened with heavy packs, slowly descended the winding trail.  负重如山的骡夫们缓缓地沿着蜿蜒的小路下山。|近义词：mule drivers, pack-train drivers|反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "muleteers" comes from "mule" + "-eer", a suffix indicating a person who works with or handles something.  "Mule" itself derives from Latin "mulus," meaning "mule."  Therefore, "muleteers" literally means "those who work with mules."
