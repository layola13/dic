photomicrography: /ˌfoʊtoʊmaɪˈkrɒɡrəfi/ | n. |显微照相术|The technique of photographing objects or organisms through a microscope.  It involves using a microscope to magnify the subject and then capturing an image using a camera.  This technique is widely used in various scientific fields, such as biology, medicine, and materials science, to visualize microscopic structures and details. |The photomicrography revealed intricate details of the cell structure. 显微照相术揭示了细胞结构的复杂细节。


近义词:microphotography, photomicroscopy


反义词:macrophotography (photography of large objects)
