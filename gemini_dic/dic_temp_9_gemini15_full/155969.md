photograph:/ˈfoʊtəɡræf/| n. |照片；摄影作品 | A photograph is a picture made using a camera.  It captures a moment in time and can be a powerful form of visual communication. | Example: I took a photograph of the sunset. 我拍了一张日落的照片。|近义词：picture, image, snap, shot |反义词：N/A


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He photographs the birds every morning. 他每天早晨都给鸟儿拍照。
She photographs landscapes for a living. 她以拍摄风景为生。


一般过去时:
They photographed the accident scene. 他们拍摄了事故现场。
I photographed the Eiffel Tower last year. 我去年拍过埃菲尔铁塔的照片。


现在完成时:
She has photographed many famous people. 她已经拍摄过许多名人的照片。
He has photographed wildlife in Africa. 他在非洲拍摄过野生动物。


过去完成时:
They had photographed the building before it was demolished. 在大楼被拆除之前，他们已经拍过照片了。
We had photographed all the exhibits before the museum closed. 在博物馆关闭之前，我们已经拍完了所有展品的照。


将来时:
I will photograph the wedding. 我将拍摄婚礼。
She will photograph the event for the newspaper. 她将为报纸拍摄该活动。


虚拟语气:
If I had a better camera, I would photograph more often. 如果我有一台更好的相机，我会更频繁地拍照。
I wish I had photographed that amazing sunset. 我真希望我拍下了那令人惊叹的日落。


被动语态:
The building was photographed from many angles. 这座建筑从许多角度都被拍摄了。
The event was photographed by several professional photographers.  这次活动由几位专业摄影师拍摄。


疑问句:
Did you photograph the meeting? 你拍下了会议吗？
Have you ever photographed a celebrity? 你有没有拍过名人的照片？


否定句:
I didn't photograph the event. 我没有拍摄这个活动。
They didn't photograph the scenery. 他们没有拍摄风景。



PHRASE->

词源:
The word "photograph" comes from the Greek words "phos" (light) and "graphein" (to write).  It literally means "to write with light".  The word was coined in the 19th century, reflecting the newly developed process of capturing images using light-sensitive materials.
