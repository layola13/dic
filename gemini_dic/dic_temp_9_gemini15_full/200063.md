sketchier:/ˈskɛtʃiər/| adj.|粗略的；不精确的；可疑的；不靠谱的|lacking in detail or precision; unreliable or suspicious.|The sketchier parts of the story made me question its veracity. 故事中比较粗略的部分让我质疑其真实性。|近义词：rough, vague, unreliable, dubious, suspect|反义词：precise, accurate, reliable, trustworthy


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The plan is sketchier than we thought. = 这个计划比我们想象的还要粗略。
His explanation seems rather sketchy. = 他的解释似乎相当粗略。


一般过去时:
The details of the accident were sketchy. = 事故的细节很模糊。
Her memory of the event was sketchy at best. = 她对这件事的记忆充其量也只是支离破碎的。


现在完成时:
The evidence has been sketchy, making it difficult to reach a conclusion. = 证据一直很模糊，难以得出结论。
The information we have received has been rather sketchy. = 我们收到的信息相当粗略。


过去完成时:
The police report had been sketchy, leading to a flawed investigation. = 警方的报告很粗略，导致调查存在缺陷。
Our understanding of the situation had been sketchy before the meeting. = 会议之前我们对局势的了解很粗略。


将来时:
The forecast for tomorrow is sketchy. = 明天的天气预报不太准确。
The details of the upcoming project are still sketchy. = 未来项目的细节仍然不明确。


虚拟语气:
If the plan were less sketchy, we would have more confidence. = 如果计划不那么粗略，我们会更有信心。
If the evidence were not so sketchy, we could convict him. = 如果证据不是那么模糊，我们就能定他的罪。


被动语态:
The details of the case were sketched out rather vaguely. = 此案的细节描述相当模糊。
The plan was deemed sketchy by the committee. = 该计划被委员会认为不够完善。


疑问句:
Are the details of the report sketchy? = 报告的细节模糊吗？
Is your recollection of the events sketchy? = 你对事件的回忆模糊吗？


否定句:

The evidence presented wasn't sketchy, it was conclusive. = 提出的证据并非含糊不清，而是确凿的。
