preconceive:/ˌpriːkənˈsiːv/| vt.|预想；预先构想；先入为主|To form an opinion or conception of beforehand; to prejudge.  It often implies a negative or biased judgment formed before having sufficient evidence.|例句：I preconceived the meeting to be boring, but it turned out to be quite engaging. 我预想会议会很无聊，但结果却相当引人入胜。|例句：Don't preconceive the outcome before you even start the project.  在项目开始之前不要先入为主地预想结果。|近义词：prejudge, prejudge, anticipate, assume, suppose|反义词：understand, comprehend

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He preconceived the task to be difficult. = 他预想这项任务很难。
They preconceive the outcome of the election. = 他们预先设想选举的结果。

一般过去时:
She preconceived a negative opinion of him. = 她对他先入为主地有了负面评价。
We preconceived the trip to be exhausting. = 我们预想这次旅行会很累人。

现在完成时:

They have preconceived a solution to the problem. = 他们已经预想出了解决这个问题的方法。

过去完成时:

He had preconceived that the experiment would fail. = 他已经预想实验会失败。

将来时:
I will preconceive a plan before starting the project. = 我将在开始项目之前预想一个计划。
They will preconceive the consequences of their actions. = 他们将会预想他们行为的后果。

虚拟语气:
If I had preconceived the difficulties, I would have prepared better. = 如果我预先想到了困难，我就会准备得更好。
I wish I hadn't preconceived the outcome. = 我真希望我没有预先设想结果。

被动语态:
The task was preconceived to be impossible. = 这项任务被预想为不可能完成的。
The meeting was preconceived as a waste of time. = 会议被预想为浪费时间。

疑问句:

否定句:
I did not preconceive the success of the project. = 我没有预想这个项目会成功。
They did not preconceive the challenges they would face. = 他们没有预想到他们将面临的挑战。
