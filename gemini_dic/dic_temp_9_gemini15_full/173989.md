quasi-superficial: /ˈkwɑːziˌsuːpərˈfɪʃəl/ | adj. | 准表面的；似表面的 |

英文解析:  The word "quasi-superficial" describes something that appears superficial or only deals with the surface level, but is not entirely so. It implies a degree of depth or complexity hidden beneath a seemingly shallow exterior.  It suggests a partial or incomplete understanding or treatment of a subject.

例句:

His analysis, while seemingly quasi-superficial, revealed subtle insights into the underlying issues. = 他的分析虽然看似肤浅，但却揭示了潜在问题的微妙之处。

The quasi-superficial charm of the novel masked a complex exploration of human relationships. = 小说的表面魅力掩盖了对人际关系的复杂探索。


近义词: seemingly superficial, superficially, shallow, cursory, perfunctory

反义词: profound, thorough, in-depth, exhaustive

词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation, but not a complete identity.

* **superficial**: From Latin *superficialis*, meaning "on the surface," derived from *super-* (above) + *facies* (face, surface).
