half-civilized: /ˌhæfˈsɪvəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 半开化的；未完全文明的 | Partially civilized; showing some but not all the characteristics of a civilized society.  It often implies a lack of sophistication, refinement, or advanced social and cultural development. |例句：The tribes in the remote mountains were considered half-civilized by the colonial government.  偏远山区的部落被殖民政府认为是半开化的。


The explorers encountered a half-civilized people in the jungle. 探险家在丛林中遇到了一群半开化的居民。


近义词：partially civilized, underdeveloped, primitive, barbarous (negative connotation), unsophisticated


反义词：civilized, sophisticated, advanced, cultured


词源:  The word is a compound of "half" and "civilized."  "Half" indicates incompleteness or a partial state, while "civilized" derives from the Latin word "civilis," meaning "of or relating to citizens," later evolving to encompass the broader meaning of "cultured, refined, and advanced in social and cultural development."
