词源:
The word "glibness" derives from the adjective "glib," which has Old English roots, though its precise etymology is uncertain.  Some suggest a connection to the Old English word "glidan," meaning "to glide" or "to slip," reflecting the smooth, effortless quality associated with glib speech.  Others propose a connection to words suggesting slipperiness or lack of substance.  Regardless, the core meaning of ease and sometimes superficiality in speech has remained consistent.
