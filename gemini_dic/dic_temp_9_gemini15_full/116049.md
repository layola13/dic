martialist: /ˈmɑːʃəlɪst/ | n. |好战分子，尚武主义者 |A person who advocates or glorifies war or military power.  Someone who believes in the importance of military strength and preparedness, often to the exclusion of other considerations. | Example: The general was considered a martialist, always advocating for increased military spending and a strong defense.  这位将军被认为是一个好战分子，总是主张增加军费开支和加强国防。|近义词：militant, warmonger |反义词：pacifist
