informant:/ɪnˈfɔːrmənt/| n. |线人，告密者 |A person who gives information to another, especially to an authority or the media.  An informant often provides secret or confidential information. |The police relied heavily on the informant's testimony.  警方严重依赖线人的证词。

The informant’s tip led to the arrest of several suspects. 线人的举报导致几名嫌疑人被捕。|informer, source, whistleblower |collaborator, accomplice |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is an informant for the FBI. 他是联邦调查局的线人。
She acts as an informant for the local police. 她充当当地警方的线人。

一般过去时:
He was an informant during the investigation. 他在调查期间是线人。
She served as an informant in the past. 她过去曾担任线人。

现在完成时:
He has been an informant for many years. 他做了多年的线人。
She has provided valuable information as an informant. 她作为线人提供了宝贵的信息。

过去完成时:
He had been an informant before the operation began.  行动开始前他就是线人了。
She had already acted as an informant in similar cases. 她之前在类似案件中已经担任过线人。


将来时:
He will continue to be an informant for the agency. 他将继续担任该机构的线人。
She will likely become an informant in the future. 她将来很可能会成为线人。

虚拟语气:
If he were an informant, we would have more evidence. 如果他是线人，我们会得到更多证据。
I wish she had been an informant earlier. 我真希望她早点成为线人。

被动语态:
The informant was protected by the authorities. 线人受到当局的保护。
The information was given by a reliable informant. 信息是由可靠的线人提供的。

疑问句:
Is he a reliable informant? 他是一个可靠的线人吗？
Was she the informant who provided the crucial information? 提供关键信息的是她吗？


否定句:
He is not a reliable informant. 他不是一个可靠的线人。
She was not an informant in that case. 在那起案件中，她不是线人。
