dinornis:/ˈdaɪnɔːrnɪs/| n. |恐鸟属|Dinornis is a genus of extinct flightless birds that were endemic to New Zealand.  They were the largest birds ever to exist, with some species reaching heights of over three meters.  They were heavily built, with powerful legs and feet, and were herbivores.  Their extinction, which occurred relatively recently, is attributed to human hunting and habitat destruction.|The discovery of *Dinornis* bones revolutionized our understanding of New Zealand's prehistoric fauna.|近义词:Moa (a common name for various species within the Dinornis genus)|反义词:None|名词
