sanidine:/ˈsænɪdaɪn/| n. |透长石|A high-temperature potassium feldspar mineral, belonging to the alkali feldspar group.  It is characterized by its glassy luster and often occurs in volcanic rocks.  It is a relatively simple crystal structure with a high degree of disorder. |Example: Sanidine crystals are often found in rhyolite and trachyte lavas. 透长石晶体通常存在于流纹岩和粗面岩熔岩中。|近义词：orthoclase (at lower temperatures)|反义词：无|名词
