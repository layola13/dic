cold-turkeyed: /ˈkoʊldˈtɜːrkiːd/ | v. |突然戒掉（毒瘾、坏习惯等）|  To stop using an addictive substance or engaging in an addictive behavior suddenly and completely, without any gradual tapering or assistance.  This phrase alludes to the unpleasant and often difficult withdrawal symptoms experienced when quitting "cold turkey".|


例句：

He cold-turkeyed his smoking habit after his doctor's warning. = 他在医生的警告后突然戒掉了烟瘾。

She cold-turkeyed her caffeine addiction, experiencing severe headaches for several days. = 她突然戒掉了咖啡因成瘾，几天来一直头痛剧烈。


近义词：abruptly quit, suddenly cease, give up completely


反义词：gradually wean off, taper off


词性:动词


USAGE->

一般过去时:
He cold-turkeyed his addiction last month. = 他上个月突然戒掉了瘾。
They cold-turkeyed their sugar intake after the doctor's advice. = 医生建议后，他们突然戒掉了糖。


现在完成时:
She has cold-turkeyed smoking twice but relapsed both times. = 她两次突然戒烟，但两次都复发了。
He has cold-turkeyed his video game habit and is feeling much better. = 他已经戒掉了玩电子游戏的不良习惯，感觉好多了。


将来时:
I will cold-turkey my coffee habit next week. = 我下周将突然戒掉咖啡的习惯。
They plan to cold-turkey their alcohol consumption starting tomorrow. = 他们计划从明天开始突然戒酒。


过去完成时:
Before the rehab, he had cold-turkeyed several times without success. = 在康复治疗之前，他已经尝试过几次突然戒毒，但都没有成功。
She had cold-turkeyed her medication earlier that year. = 那年早些时候，她曾突然停药。



被动语态:
His addiction was cold-turkeyed with little support. = 他的成瘾几乎没有得到任何支持就突然戒掉了。
The treatment program did not advocate for having the habit cold-turkeyed. = 该治疗方案不主张突然戒除该习惯。
