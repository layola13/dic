petitenesses:/ˈpɛtɪtnɛsɪz/| n.|琐碎的事；微不足道；小气；小心眼|The author dismisses the pettiness of the characters' squabbles. 作者不屑一顾地dismisses角色争吵的琐碎。|近义词：trivialities, triviality, insignificance, minutiae|反义词：importance, significance


英文解析：The plural noun "petitenesses" refers to small, insignificant matters or details, often implying a lack of importance or triviality. It can also suggest pettiness or mean-spiritedness in behavior or attitude.  The word carries a slightly negative connotation, highlighting the unimportance or even unpleasantness of the things being discussed.


例句：

He was irritated by the endless petitenesses of office life. 他对办公室生活中无休止的琐碎小事感到恼火。

Her constant complaints about minor inconveniences revealed a surprising amount of pettiness. 她不断抱怨一些微不足道的不便，暴露了她令人惊讶的小气。


词源:

Derived from the French word "petit," meaning "small," "little," or "short."  The suffix "-ness" is added to create the noun form, indicating a quality or state.  The further addition of "-es" makes it plural.
