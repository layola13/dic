prohibits:/prəˈhɪbɪts/| vt.|禁止，阻止|to officially forbid something|The law prohibits smoking in public places. 这项法律禁止在公共场所吸烟。|The school prohibits the use of mobile phones. 学校禁止使用手机。|allows, permits|permits, allows|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The school prohibits the use of mobile phones. = 学校禁止使用手机。
The rules prohibit eating in the classroom. = 规定禁止在教室里吃东西。

一般过去时:
The government prohibited the sale of that drug. = 政府禁止销售那种药物。
They prohibited access to the building. = 他们禁止进入该建筑物。

现在完成时:
The city has prohibited the construction of new high-rises. = 该城市已禁止建造新的高层建筑。
They have prohibited smoking in all public spaces. = 他们已禁止在所有公共场所吸烟。

过去完成时:
They had prohibited alcohol before the game. = 他们在比赛前就禁止了酒精。
The council had prohibited parking in this area for years. = 委员会多年来一直禁止在这个区域停车。

将来时:
The council will prohibit the use of fireworks. = 理事会将禁止使用烟花。
The new law will prohibit the import of certain goods. = 新法律将禁止进口某些商品。


虚拟语气:
If they prohibited the event, many people would be disappointed. = 如果他们禁止了这次活动，许多人会感到失望。
I wish they prohibited smoking here. = 我希望他们在这里禁止吸烟。


被动语态:
Smoking is prohibited in this area. = 本区域禁止吸烟。
The use of cameras was prohibited at the event. = 活动中禁止使用相机。

疑问句:
Does the law prohibit the sale of alcohol to minors? = 法律禁止向未成年人出售酒精吗？
Does this sign prohibit parking here? = 这个标志禁止在此停车吗？

否定句:
The rule doesn't prohibit the use of laptops. = 规则并不禁止使用笔记本电脑。
The company doesn't prohibit employees from using social media during breaks. = 公司不禁止员工在休息时间使用社交媒体。


PHRASE->
prohibit from doing sth = 禁止做某事
strictly prohibit = 严禁
prohibit the entry of = 禁止...进入
prohibit the use of = 禁止使用
prohibit the sale of = 禁止出售
