opposit:/ˈɒpəzɪt/ | adj. | 对面的；相反的；对立的 |  Opposite means being on the other side, or being completely different. It can describe physical location or contrasting ideas or qualities. |例句：The bank is opposite the post office. 银行在邮局对面。 The two brothers have opposite personalities. 这两个兄弟性格相反。 | 近义词：contrary, reverse, converse | 反义词：same, identical, similar | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The library is opposite the park. = 图书馆在公园对面。
His opinion is opposite to mine. = 他的观点与我的相反。


一般过去时:
The house opposite was empty. = 对面的房子是空的。
Their views were diametrically opposite. = 他们的观点截然相反。


现在完成时:
The building opposite has been renovated. = 对面的建筑物已经翻新了。
Everything has turned opposite to what we expected. = 一切都和我们预料的相反。


过去完成时:
He had already walked to the opposite side of the street. = 他已经走到街对面了。
I had thought the opposite was true. = 我曾以为情况正好相反。


将来时:
The new school will be opposite the old one. = 新学校将在旧学校对面。
We will see the opposite effect soon. = 我们很快就会看到相反的效果。


虚拟语气:
If the situation were opposite, I would act differently. = 如果情况相反，我会采取不同的行动。
I wish the result were opposite. = 我希望结果相反。


被动语态:
The house opposite is being painted. = 对面的房子正在粉刷。
The opposite view is rarely being considered. = 相反的观点很少被考虑。


疑问句:
Is the shop opposite open? = 对面的商店开着吗？
Is the opposite true in this case? = 在这种情况下，相反的情况是否属实？


否定句:
The hotel isn't opposite the station. = 旅馆不在车站对面。
His argument isn't opposite to mine. = 他的论点与我的论点并不相反。


PHRASE->
