diptote:/ˈdɪptoʊt/| n. |双格词|A noun or adjective that has only two cases (typically the nominative and vocative, or the nominative and accusative).  It is a grammatical term referring to words with only two inflectional forms.  This is a relatively rare phenomenon in many languages. |Example:  In some ancient languages, certain pronouns might be diptotes, exhibiting only a nominative and accusative form.  The specific examples vary greatly depending on the language.|近义词: (No direct synonyms, as it describes a specific grammatical feature)|反义词: (No direct antonyms,  but words with many cases are the opposite concept)|n.
