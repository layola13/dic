losing: /ˈluːzɪŋ/ | v. | 丢失；失败；失去；输掉 |  The act of failing to keep possession of something; the act of being defeated or unsuccessful; the process of gradually decreasing or diminishing something. |


例句：

He's losing his hair. = 他正在秃顶。

She's losing her patience. = 她正在失去耐心。


近义词：mislaying, dropping, failing, forfeiting

反义词：winning, gaining, keeping, retaining


USAGE->

一般现在时:
I am losing my keys. = 我正在丢失我的钥匙。
We are losing the game. = 我们正在输掉比赛。


一般过去时:
I lost my wallet yesterday. = 我昨天丢了钱包。
They lost the election. = 他们输掉了选举。


现在完成时:
I have lost my appetite. = 我已经失去了食欲。
She has been losing weight recently. = 她最近一直在减肥。


过去完成时:
I had lost all my money before I arrived home. = 我回家前已经把所有的钱都花光了。
They had lost the battle before reinforcements arrived. = 增援部队到达之前，他们就已经输掉了战斗。


将来时:
I will be losing my job soon. = 我很快就要失业了。
We will lose the opportunity if we don't act fast. = 如果我们不迅速行动，我们将失去这个机会。


虚拟语气:
If I lost my job, I would be devastated. = 如果我失业了，我会非常沮丧。
I wish I hadn't lost my temper. = 我真希望我没有发脾气。


被动语态:
The game was lost due to poor teamwork. = 由于团队合作不好，比赛输了。
The election was lost by a narrow margin. = 选举以微弱劣势输掉了。


疑问句:
Are you losing your hearing? = 你听力下降了吗？
Is she losing interest in her work? = 她对工作失去兴趣了吗？


否定句:
I'm not losing hope. = 我没有失去希望。
He didn't lose his cool. = 他没有失去冷静。


PHRASE->

losing battle: 败局

losing ground: 失去优势

losing oneself: 迷失自我


词源:

The word "losing" comes from the Old English verb "losan," meaning "to perish, be lost, die."  This in turn is related to the Proto-Germanic root "*leusan," with similar meanings.  The modern sense of "losing" evolved over time from these earlier connotations of destruction and disappearance.
