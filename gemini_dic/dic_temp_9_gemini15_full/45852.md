cut-back:/kʌtˈbæk/ | n., v. | 削减；减少；倒退 |

英文解析：A cut-back refers to a reduction in something, typically spending, production, or activity.  As a noun, it's the reduction itself; as a verb, it's the act of making the reduction.

例句：

Noun:
The company announced a cut-back in production due to falling demand.  = 公司由于需求下降而宣布减产。

The government implemented a cut-back in military spending. = 政府削减了军费开支。


Verb:
We need to cut back on our expenses. = 我们需要削减开支。

They decided to cut back the number of employees. = 他们决定裁员。


近义词：reduction, decrease, curtailment, diminution, retrenchment

反义词：increase, augmentation, expansion


词性：Noun, Verb


USAGE->

一般现在时:
We cut back on sugar. = 我们减少糖的摄入。
The company cuts back its marketing budget. = 公司削减了营销预算。


一般过去时:
They cut back production last year. = 他们去年削减了产量。
She cut back on her caffeine intake. = 她减少了咖啡因的摄入。


现在完成时:
We have cut back on our travel expenses. = 我们已经削减了差旅费。
The government has cut back on social programs. = 政府已经削减了社会福利项目。


过去完成时:
They had cut back their workforce before the merger. = 他们在合并之前已经裁员了。
The company had cut back on research and development. = 公司已经削减了研发经费。


将来时:
We will cut back on energy consumption. = 我们将减少能源消耗。
The factory will cut back its operations next month. = 工厂下个月将减产。


虚拟语气:
If we cut back on spending, we would be able to save more money. = 如果我们削减开支，就能省下更多的钱。
I wish they had cut back on the noise. = 我希望他们能减少噪音。


被动语态:
Production was cut back due to the economic downturn. = 由于经济衰退，产量被削减了。
Spending will be cut back across all departments. = 所有部门的开支都将被削减。


疑问句:
Will they cut back on staff? = 他们会裁员吗？
Have you cut back on anything lately? = 你最近有没有减少什么？


否定句:
We didn't cut back on our holiday plans. = 我们没有削减我们的假期计划。
The company hasn't cut back on its advertising campaign. = 公司没有削减它的广告宣传活动。



PHRASE->
cut back on something = 减少…
cut back to something = 回到…
cut someone back = 阻碍某人
cut back the branches = 修剪树枝
cut it back = 减少它
