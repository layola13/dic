nominalizing:/ˈnɑːmɪnəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 名词化 | The process of converting a verb, adjective, or other part of speech into a noun.  This often involves adding suffixes like "-tion," "-ment," "-ance," "-ence," or "-ing." Nominalization can make writing more abstract and less direct. | The *investigation* revealed new evidence. (from "investigate")  His *commitment* to the project was impressive. (from "commit") |  verbification, substantiation |  (No direct antonym, but the opposite action would be "verbifying" or using verbs instead of nouns)| 动词
