discourtship:/dɪsˈkɔːrtʃɪp/| n. |绝交；不和；反目成仇|The state of being estranged or out of favor; a falling out or breach of friendship.  Discourtship often implies a prior period of friendship or amicable relations that has soured. | Example: The discourteship between the two families lasted for generations.  两个家族之间的不和持续了好几代。|近义词：estrangement, alienation, breach, rift |反义词：friendship, reconciliation, amity
