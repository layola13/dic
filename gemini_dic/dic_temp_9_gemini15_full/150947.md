paralytic: /ˌpærəˈlɪtɪk/ | adj., n. | 瘫痪的；麻痹的；醉醺醺的 |

英文解析:
As an adjective, "paralytic" means affected by paralysis, or completely unable to move a part or all of the body. It can also describe someone who is extremely intoxicated by alcohol, to the point of being unable to function normally. As a noun, it refers to a person suffering from paralysis, or a substance that causes paralysis.


例句:
The accident left him paralytic.  = 这场事故使他瘫痪了。

Her paralytic fear prevented her from moving. = 她极度的恐惧让她动弹不得。


近义词:
crippled, paralyzed, incapacitated, inebriated, intoxicated


反义词:
able-bodied, healthy, sober


词性:
adj., n.

词源:
From the Greek word "paralytikos," meaning "to loosen or relax."  This further derives from "para-" (beside, beyond) and "lyein" (to loosen).  The word reflects the historical understanding of paralysis as a loosening or loss of control over the body's functions.
