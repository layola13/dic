/ˌhaɪpəˈtæksɪs/| n. | 并列句 | Hypotaxis refers to a sentence structure where clauses are arranged in a hierarchical relationship, one clause being subordinate to another. This creates a complex sentence with dependent and independent clauses, contrasting with parataxis where clauses are arranged equally.  It involves the use of subordinating conjunctions or relative pronouns to link clauses, showing a clear relationship of dependence. | The complex sentence, "Because it was raining, the game was canceled," demonstrates hypotaxis, with "Because it was raining" being subordinate to the main clause "the game was canceled." | parataxis | parataxis | n.
