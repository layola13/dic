grabbing:/ˈɡræbɪŋ/| v.|抓取；抢夺；吸引；（非正式）很棒的；|To seize or snatch something quickly.  It can also refer to attracting attention or interest, or informally, something excellent.|例句：He grabbed the phone and answered it. 他抓起电话并接听了。

He was grabbing at straws, trying to find a solution. 他在试图找到解决方案，却抓不住重点。


近义词：seizing, snatching, catching, captivating,  (informal) awesome, amazing

反义词：releasing, letting go, ignoring, repelling


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grabs the newspaper every morning. 他每天早上都抓起报纸。
She grabs a quick bite before work. 她在上班前快速吃点东西。


一般过去时:
I grabbed my keys and ran out the door. 我抓起钥匙就跑出门了。
They grabbed the opportunity to travel. 他们抓住了旅行的机会。


现在完成时:
He has grabbed the lead in the race. 他在比赛中已经取得了领先地位。
She has grabbed several awards for her work. 她因其作品获得了多个奖项。


过去完成时:
I had grabbed my coat before the rain started. 下雨前我已经拿好外套了。
They had grabbed the best seats before the concert began. 音乐会开始前他们已经抢到了最好的座位。


将来时:
He will grab a sandwich for lunch. 他午餐将随便吃个三明治。
She will grab the chance to speak with the CEO. 她将抓住机会与首席执行官交谈。


虚拟语气:
If I had grabbed the opportunity, I would have been successful. 如果我抓住了机会，我就成功了。
I wish I had grabbed that promotion when it was offered. 我真希望我当时接受了晋升的机会。


被动语态:
The phone was grabbed by the child. 电话被孩子抢走了。
The opportunity was grabbed by a competitor. 机会被竞争对手抓住了。


疑问句:
Did he grab the package? 他拿包裹了吗？
What did she grab from the table? 她从桌子上拿了什么？


否定句:
He didn't grab the chance to speak. 他没有抓住机会发言。
She didn't grab the book from the shelf. 她没有从书架上拿书。


PHRASE->
grab a bite = 快速吃点东西
grab a drink = 随便喝点东西
grab attention = 吸引注意力
grab hold of = 抓住
grab the opportunity = 抓住机会
