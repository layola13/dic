mandolins: /ˈmændəˌliːnz/ | n. |曼陀林琴复数 | A mandolin is a stringed musical instrument of the lute family, typically having four pairs of strings, played with a plectrum.  It is known for its bright, ringing tone. | 例句：The band played a lively tune on their mandolins.乐队用他们的曼陀林演奏了一首欢快的曲子。


The orchestra included several mandolins in its string section.管弦乐队在其弦乐部分中包含了几把曼陀林。


近义词：lute (a more general term), ukulele (smaller, related instrument) | 反义词：None (no direct antonym)| 词性:名词


词源:  The word "mandolin" comes from the Italian word *mandolino*, ultimately derived from the Greek word  *mandoras* which was a type of lute.  The Italian word underwent various changes as it evolved into different languages.
