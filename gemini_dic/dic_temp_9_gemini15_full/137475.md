nontolerantly: /ˌnɒnˈtɒlərəntli/ | adv. | 不宽容地，不容忍地 |  Acting in a manner that shows intolerance or prejudice.  It describes behavior that is unwilling to accept differences in opinions, beliefs, or practices.  |

例句：He responded nontolerantly to the opposing viewpoint, dismissing it without consideration. 他不宽容地回应了相反的观点，毫不考虑地就将其驳回了。

例句：The government acted nontolerantly towards dissent, suppressing any opposition. 政府对异议采取了不宽容的态度，压制了任何反对意见。


近义词：intolerantly, bigotedly, prejudicedly

反义词：tolerantly, acceptingly


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adverb "tolerantly," which is derived from the adjective "tolerant."  "Tolerant" comes from the Latin word "tolerans," the present participle of "tolerare," meaning "to bear, endure, or tolerate."


USAGE->
This word is an adverb and thus doesn't have tense variations like verbs. It describes *how* an action is performed.  The tense is determined by the verb it modifies.
