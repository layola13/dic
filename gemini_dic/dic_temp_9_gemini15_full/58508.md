duels:/ˈduːəlz/ | n. |决斗；竞争 | A duel is a formal combat between two people, typically fought with weapons.  It often involves a prearranged agreement and witnesses.  In a broader sense, it can refer to any intense rivalry or competition. |例句：The two men fought a duel at dawn. = 两个男人在黎明时分决斗。

The company is locked in a fierce duel for market share. = 这家公司正卷入一场激烈的市场份额争夺战。|近义词：combat, contest, fight, competition |反义词：cooperation, collaboration, peace

词源:
The word "duel" comes from the Old French word "duelle," which in turn derives from the Latin word "duellum," meaning "a small war" or "a fight."  This reflects the serious and potentially lethal nature of a duel.
