mediocracy: /ˌmiːdiˈɒkrəsi/ | n. | 平庸的统治；庸才当政；平庸；平庸之辈 |

英文解析:Mediocrity is rule or influence by people of mediocre abilities or an environment characterized by mediocrity.  It suggests a system or situation where average or below-average individuals hold power or influence, leading to stagnation and lack of innovation.  It often implies a lack of excellence or ambition.

例句:

The company's mediocrity is evident in its lack of innovation and slow growth.  公司的平庸在其缺乏创新和缓慢增长中显而易见。

The election of a mediocracy to govern the city was a sign of public apathy. 选举一群平庸之辈来治理这座城市是公众冷漠的标志。


近义词: mediocrity, incompetence, ineptitude, second-rateness

反义词: excellence, brilliance, meritocracy


词性: 名词
