preexperiencing:/ˌpriːɪkˈspɪəriənsɪŋ/ | v. | 预先体验；预先经历 |  The act of experiencing something before it actually happens, often through imagination, simulation, or vicarious experience. It involves mentally preparing oneself for a future event by anticipating its sensations, emotions, and potential outcomes.  |  Example:  She was preexperiencing the joy of her upcoming wedding, visualizing the ceremony and reception in her mind. 她正在预先体验即将到来的婚礼的喜悦，在脑海中想象着仪式和招待会。 | 近义词：anticipating, foreshadowing, previsualizing | 反义词：reliving, reminiscing | 动词
