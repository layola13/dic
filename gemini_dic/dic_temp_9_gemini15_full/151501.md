parrot:/ˈpærət/| n. |鹦鹉|A bird with a hooked beak, strong legs, and usually brightly coloured feathers. Many parrots can mimic human speech.  |例句：The parrot squawked loudly. 这只鹦鹉大声叫喊。|例句：He owns a green parrot that can say "hello". 他养了一只绿鹦鹉，会说“你好”。|近义词：bird, macaw|反义词：无


USAGE->

Parrots are known for their bright colours. = 鹦鹉以其鲜艳的色彩而闻名。

词源:
The word "parrot" entered English from Old French "parot," which in turn came from the Latin "psittacus," itself borrowed from Greek ψιττακός (psittakos).  The Greek word's origin is uncertain but may be onomatopoeic, imitating the bird's call.
