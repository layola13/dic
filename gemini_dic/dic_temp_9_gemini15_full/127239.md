nauseously:/ˈnɔːziəsli/| adv.|令人作呕地；令人反胃地；恶心地 |In a way that causes or suggests nausea.  It describes something that is extremely unpleasant and makes one feel sick to the stomach.  |例句：The smell of the rotting fish was nauseously sweet.  腐烂鱼的臭味令人作呕地甜腻。

The food was cooked nauseously. 这食物煮得令人作呕。


近义词：sickeningly, disgustingly, repulsively|反义词：pleasantly, delightfully


词源:  Derived from the adjective "nauseous," meaning causing nausea or sickness. The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective into an adverb, indicating the manner in which something is done or experienced.  The word "nausea" itself originates from the Greek word "naus," meaning "ship," reflecting the historical association of seasickness with nausea.
