hexachord:/ˈhɛksəkɔːrd/| n. |六声音程；六声音阶|A hexachord is a six-note musical scale or segment of a scale.  It was a fundamental element in medieval musical theory and composition, particularly in the development of counterpoint and modal music.  Different hexachords were used to organize the notes of the musical system, and their relationships were crucial for understanding musical harmony. |Example: The study of hexachords was central to understanding medieval musical theory.|近义词：six-note scale, six-tone scale |反义词：无
