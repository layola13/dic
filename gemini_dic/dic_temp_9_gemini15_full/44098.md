/ˈkraɪsɪs/| n. |危机，紧要关头 |A crisis is a time of great difficulty, danger, or uncertainty.  It often requires immediate action and decisive solutions.  |例句：The country is facing a serious economic crisis. 这个国家正面临严重的经济危机。|例句：The doctor said the patient was in a critical crisis. 医生说病人正处于危急关头。|近义词：emergency, predicament, difficulty |反义词：calm, peace, stability |名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The crisis demands immediate action. = 危机需要立即采取行动。
This crisis affects many people. =这场危机影响了许多人。


一般过去时:
The 2008 financial crisis had a devastating impact. = 2008年的金融危机造成了毁灭性的影响。
The company overcame a major crisis last year. =该公司去年克服了一场重大危机。


现在完成时:
We have successfully navigated several crises. = 我们已经成功地渡过了几次危机。
The government has addressed the crisis effectively. = 政府已经有效地解决了这场危机。


过去完成时:
Before the intervention, the situation had already become a full-blown crisis. = 在干预之前，情况已经演变成一场全面的危机。

将来时:
A major crisis will likely occur if we don't act now. = 如果我们现在不采取行动，很可能会发生一场重大危机。
The experts predict that a new crisis will emerge next year. =专家预测明年将出现新的危机。


虚拟语气:
If we had acted sooner, the crisis might have been avoided. = 如果我们早点行动，这场危机本来是可以避免的。
It's crucial that we address the crisis before it escalates. =至关重要的是，我们在危机升级之前解决它。


被动语态:
The crisis was handled effectively by the emergency services. = 紧急服务部门有效地处理了这场危机。
The problem is being exacerbated by the current crisis. = 当前的危机正在加剧这个问题。


疑问句:
What caused this crisis? = 这次危机的原因是什么？
Is there a solution to this crisis? = 这场危机有解决办法吗？


否定句:
The situation is not yet a crisis. = 情况还不是危机。
