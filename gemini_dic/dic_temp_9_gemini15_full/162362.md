preacquainting: /ˌpriːəˈkweɪntɪŋ/ | v. | 预先认识，事先熟悉 |  The act of becoming familiar with something or someone before a formal introduction or meeting.  It implies a prior, perhaps indirect, knowledge or experience. | 例句：Preacquainting oneself with the company's culture before the interview helped the candidate feel more confident. 事先熟悉公司的文化帮助应聘者感觉更自信。  Preacquainting the team with the project goals ensured everyone was on the same page from the start.  事先让团队熟悉项目目标确保每个人从一开始就在同一页上。 | 近义词：familiarizing, acquainting, introducing | 反义词：unfamiliarizing, alienating
