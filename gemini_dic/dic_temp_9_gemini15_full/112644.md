lowballing:/ˈloʊˌbɔːlɪŋ/| n. v.|压价；低价报价；|The act of offering a significantly lower price than expected, often in a negotiation or bid.  This tactic aims to secure a deal at a favorable price for the offeror, potentially at the expense of the other party.|例句：He tried to lowball the seller on the house, offering far less than the asking price. 他试图压低房子的售价，出价远低于要价。

例句：The contractor was accused of lowballing his bid to win the project, intending to increase the cost later. 承包商被指控低价竞标以赢得项目，意图稍后再增加成本。


近义词：underbidding, undercutting, shortchanging |反义词：overpaying, overbidding
