post-war:/ˌpoʊstˈwɔːr/| adj.|战后的| Relating to the period after a war.  This adjective describes things, events, or situations that occurred or existed after a war has ended.  It often implies a significant impact or influence from the preceding war.|例句：Post-war reconstruction efforts were extensive. 战后重建工作非常广泛。

例句：The post-war baby boom led to significant population growth. 战后婴儿潮导致人口大幅增长。


近义词：after-war, postbellum (for American Civil War)


反义词：pre-war, antebellum (for American Civil War)


词性：形容词

词源:
The word "post-war" is a compound word formed from the prefix "post-" (meaning "after" or "following") and the noun "war".  Its usage emerged naturally following major conflicts, providing a concise way to refer to the period and its associated effects.
