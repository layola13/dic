over-securities:/ˌoʊvər sɪˈkjʊrətiz/| n. |过度证券化，过度抵押证券化|Over-securitization refers to the process of creating multiple layers of securities from a single underlying asset, often to a degree that amplifies risk rather than mitigating it.  This practice was a significant contributor to the 2008 financial crisis.  It involves creating complex financial instruments backed by pools of mortgages, loans, or other assets, often with inadequate due diligence or understanding of the underlying risk.|The over-securitization of subprime mortgages played a crucial role in the 2008 financial crisis. = 次贷抵押贷款的过度证券化在2008年金融危机中扮演了至关重要的角色。|Excessive securitization,  Hyper-securitization |Under-securitization| n.
