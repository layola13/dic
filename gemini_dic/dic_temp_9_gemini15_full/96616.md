indulging:/ɪnˈdʌldʒɪŋ/ | v. |沉溺；纵容；放纵 |The act of allowing oneself to enjoy a particular pleasure, often to excess.  It implies a yielding to one's desires or impulses, often with a sense of self-indulgence. |例句：He was indulging in a large piece of chocolate cake. 他正在吃一块很大的巧克力蛋糕。  She indulged her children with too many sweets. 她纵容孩子们吃了太多糖果。 |近义词：pampering, gratifying, satisfying, giving in to |反义词：restraining, denying, resisting, abstaining |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He indulges in video games every evening. = 他每天晚上沉迷于电子游戏。
She indulges her sweet tooth with daily ice cream. = 她每天都吃冰淇淋来满足自己的甜食瘾。

一般过去时:
They indulged in a luxurious spa day. = 他们享受了一天奢华的温泉疗养。
I indulged myself with a long nap after work. = 下班后我好好睡了一觉。

现在完成时:
He has indulged in too much junk food lately. = 最近他吃了太多垃圾食品。
She has indulged her passion for painting for many years. = 多年来她一直沉迷于绘画。

过去完成时:
They had indulged in several glasses of wine before dinner. = 晚餐前他们已经喝了几杯酒。
She had indulged her craving for chocolate before she went on her diet.  = 开始节食之前，她已经满足了自己的巧克力渴望。

将来时:
We will indulge in a celebratory dinner after the wedding. = 婚礼结束后我们将举行庆祝晚宴。
They will indulge themselves with a relaxing vacation. = 他们将享受轻松的假期。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would indulge in a hobby. = 如果我有更多时间，我会沉迷于一项爱好。
I wish I could indulge in a long weekend getaway. = 我真希望我能享受一个漫长的周末假期。

被动语态:
His desires were indulged by his parents. = 他的愿望得到了父母的纵容。
The children were indulged with expensive gifts. = 孩子们得到了昂贵的礼物。


疑问句:
Does he often indulge in unhealthy habits? = 他经常沉迷于不健康的习惯吗？
Will you indulge in some dessert after dinner? = 晚饭后你想吃些甜点吗？

否定句:
She doesn't indulge in gossip. = 她不参与八卦。
They didn't indulge their children's every whim. = 他们没有满足孩子们的每一个愿望。


PHRASE->
indulge in sth = 沉溺于某事
indulge oneself = 放纵自己
indulge a habit = 养成某种习惯
indulge one's appetite = 满足食欲
indulge a whim = 满足一时兴起
