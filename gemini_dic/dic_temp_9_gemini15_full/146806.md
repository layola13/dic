overimitations:/ˌoʊvərˌɪmɪˈteɪʃənz/ | n. |过度模仿|The act of imitating beyond what is necessary or typical;  excessive or unnecessary copying of observed behaviors, often seen in children's learning or in studies of animal behavior.  It refers to the replication of actions that are not functionally necessary to achieve a goal.  For example, a child might meticulously copy every irrelevant detail of an adult's action when only a core aspect is needed to accomplish the task. |例句：Studies have shown that children engage in overimitations, copying even irrelevant actions observed in a demonstration.  研究表明，儿童会进行过度模仿，即使在演示中观察到无关紧要的动作也会模仿。


The phenomenon of overimitation has been observed in various species beyond humans, suggesting potential evolutionary roots. 过度模仿现象已在人类以外的各种物种中观察到，这表明其可能具有进化根源。|近义词：excessive imitation, superfluous copying, redundant mimicry |反义词：selective imitation, minimal imitation, functional imitation

词源:

* **over-**:  prefix indicating excess or beyond a limit. (来自表示超过或超出限制的前缀)
* **imitation**:  from Latin *imitātiōn-, *imitātiō* "imitation," from *imitārī* "to imitate," from *aemulus* "rival, emulator." (来自拉丁语 *imitātiōn-, *imitātiō* “模仿”，来自 *imitārī* “模仿”，来自 *aemulus* “竞争者，效仿者”)


There is no verb form of "overimitations."  The verb would be "overimitate."
