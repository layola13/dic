overprovident: /ˌoʊvərˈprɑːvɪdənt/ | adj. | 过度节俭的；过度储蓄的 | Excessively provident; saving too much.  It implies a degree of anxiety or miserliness associated with excessive saving. |  例句：His overprovident nature prevented him from enjoying the fruits of his labor. = 他过度节俭的性格使他无法享受自己劳动成果。

例句：She was so overprovident that she had saved enough money to live comfortably for the next twenty years, yet she still worried about the future. = 她过于节俭，攒够了足够她舒适地生活二十年的钱，但她仍然担心未来。


近义词：miserly, frugal (to an extreme), thrifty (to an extreme), saving (to excess)

反义词：prodigal, extravagant, spendthrift, improvident


词源：

* **over-**:  prefix meaning "too much," "excessive," or "beyond."
* **provident**: from Latin *prōvidēns*, present participle of *prōvidēre* "to foresee, provide for," from *prō- "before" + *vidēre* "to see."
