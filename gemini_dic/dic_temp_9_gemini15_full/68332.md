fall-off: /ˈfɔːl ɒf/ /ˈfɔːl ɔːf/ | n. vi. |脱落；下降；减少；衰退 |

英文解析:To fall off means to detach or separate from something; to decrease or decline in amount, number, or quality; to become less successful or popular.  It can refer to a physical detachment, a decline in numbers, or a decrease in performance.

例句:
The paint is starting to fall off the wall. = 油漆开始从墙上脱落。

The number of students attending the class has fallen off significantly. = 上课的学生人数显著减少。


近义词: detachment, decrease, decline, drop, slump, reduction, deterioration

反义词: increase, rise, growth, improvement, upswing


词性:

Noun (名词):  The act of falling off; a sudden decrease.  This usage is less common.


Verb (不及物动词): To fall from a higher position; to decrease or decline. This is the more common usage.
