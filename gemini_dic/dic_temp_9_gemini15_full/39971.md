consubstantialist:/ˌkɒnsʌbˈstænʃəlɪst/| adj. | 共质论者；实在论者 | Relating to or supporting consubstantiation, the theological doctrine that the substance of the bread and wine in the Eucharist remains unchanged but Christ is present in a mystical way.  | The consubstantialist view of the Eucharist differs significantly from transubstantiation. 共质论对圣餐的观点与化体说大相径庭。|近义词：realist (in a theological context) |反义词：transubstantiationist
