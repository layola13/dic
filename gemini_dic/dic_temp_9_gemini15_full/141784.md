/ˈɔːreɪtərz/| n. |雄辩家；演说家 | Skilled, persuasive public speakers.  Orators are known for their eloquence, powerful delivery, and ability to captivate an audience.  | Example:  Martin Luther King Jr. was a powerful orator, whose speeches inspired millions.  马丁·路德·金博士是一位有力的演说家，他的演讲激励了数百万人。
|近义词：speaker, public speaker, rhetorician |反义词：mumbler, stammerer


/ˈɔːreɪtərz/| n. |雄辩家；演说家 |  Orators often use rhetorical devices to enhance their speeches and connect with their listeners on an emotional level. They may employ techniques like metaphors, similes, and repetition to emphasize key points and make their message memorable. | Example: Many great orators throughout history have used their skills to inspire social change and political action. 历史上许多伟大的演说家都利用他们的技巧来激励社会变革和政治行动。
|近义词：speaker, public speaker, rhetorician |反义词：mumbler, stammerer


词源：源自拉丁语 "orator,"  meaning "speaker," derived from "os," meaning "mouth."
