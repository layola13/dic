pallor:/ˈpælər/| n.|苍白；脸色苍白|An unnatural paleness of the skin, often indicating illness or fear.|例句：Her pallor suggested she was unwell.  她的脸色苍白表明她身体不适。|例句：The sudden pallor on his face alarmed us. 他脸上突然变得苍白让我们很担心。|近义词：paleness, wanness, ashenness|反义词：ruddy, flushed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The patient's pallor worries the doctor. = 病人的苍白让医生担心。
Her pallor is a sign of illness. = 她的苍白是生病的迹象。

一般过去时:
His pallor increased after the accident. = 事故后他的脸色更加苍白。
The witness's pallor gave away his lie. = 证人的苍白暴露了他的谎言。

现在完成时:
Her pallor has worsened since the surgery. = 她自从手术后脸色越来越苍白。
The prolonged illness has left him with a noticeable pallor. = 长期生病使他脸色明显苍白。

过去完成时:
His pallor had been a warning sign, but we ignored it. = 他的苍白本该是个警告信号，但我们忽略了。
She had never seen such a profound pallor before. = 她以前从未见过如此严重的苍白。

将来时:
His pallor will likely intensify if he doesn't get treatment. = 如果他不接受治疗，他的脸色可能会更加苍白。
The doctor predicts her pallor will improve with rest. = 医生预测她休息后脸色会好转。

虚拟语气:
