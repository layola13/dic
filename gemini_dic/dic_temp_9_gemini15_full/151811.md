passed:/pæst/ | /pɑːst/ | v. adj. | 通过；过去；逝世 | To move or proceed beyond a particular point in space or time.  To be approved or accepted. To die. |例句：He passed his driving test. 他通过了驾驶考试。
The years passed quickly.  岁月流逝得很快。

近义词：approved, succeeded, deceased, gone by
反义词：failed, rejected, alive, present

词性: 动词，形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He passes the ball well. = 他传球传得很准。
She passes the exam easily. = 她轻松地通过了考试。


一般过去时:
I passed my exams. = 我通过了考试。
The parade passed by our house.  游行队伍从我们家门口经过。


现在完成时:
She has passed her driving test. = 她已经通过了驾驶考试。
Many years have passed since then. 自那以后，许多年过去了。


过去完成时:
After he had passed his exams, he celebrated. = 他通过考试后就庆祝了。
The train had already passed when we arrived at the station. 我们到达车站时，火车已经开走了。


将来时:
I will pass the message to him. = 我会把消息转达给他。
The comet will pass close to the Earth next year.  这颗彗星明年将靠近地球。


虚拟语气:
If he had passed the exam, he would be very happy. = 如果他通过了考试，他会非常高兴。
I wish I had passed the test. 我真希望我通过了考试。


被动语态:
The bill was passed by the Senate. = 这项法案已由参议院通过。
The baton was passed to the next runner.  接力棒传给了下一名选手。


疑问句:
Did you pass the test? = 你考试及格了吗？
Has the train passed yet? 火车过去了没有？


否定句:
I didn't pass the exam. = 我考试没及格。
He hasn't passed his driving test yet. 他还没通过驾驶考试。
