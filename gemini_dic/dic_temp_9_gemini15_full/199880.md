six-figure: /sɪksˈfɪɡər/ | adj. | 六位数的；年薪达六位数的 |  Describes a number with six digits, typically used to refer to a large sum of money, especially salaries or financial transactions. It often implies a high level of wealth or income. |例句：He earns a six-figure salary. 他赚取六位数的薪水。

He's hoping to close a six-figure deal. 他希望达成一笔六位数的交易。


近义词：high-earning, well-paid (for salaries); substantial, considerable (for sums of money) | 反义词：low-paying, meager (for salaries); insignificant, paltry (for sums of money) | 词性:形容词
