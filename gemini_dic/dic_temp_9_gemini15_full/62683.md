engendered:/ɪnˈdʒɛndərd/| v. |产生；引起；导致 |To engender means to cause or give rise to (something).  It often refers to the creation of feelings, situations, or conditions. |His actions engendered distrust among his colleagues.  他的行为在同事中引起了不信任。

The policies engendered widespread anger.  这些政策引起了广泛的愤怒。


近义词：cause, produce, generate, create, bring about
反义词：destroy, eliminate, prevent, suppress


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new law engenders debate. = 新法律引发了辩论。
His speech engenders hope. = 他的演讲带来了希望。

一般过去时:
The war engendered much suffering. = 那场战争造成了许多苦难。
Her words engendered a sense of calm. = 她的话语带来了一种平静感。

现在完成时:
The project has engendered significant progress. = 该项目已经取得了显著进展。
His actions have engendered a lot of controversy. = 他的行为引起了很多争议。

过去完成时:
The rumors had engendered fear among the villagers. = 谣言已经在村民中引起了恐惧。
The previous administration had engendered economic instability. = 前一届政府已经导致了经济不稳定。

将来时:
The new technology will engender many new jobs. = 新技术将创造许多新的就业机会。
This decision will engender further complications. = 这个决定将导致进一步的复杂化。

虚拟语气:
If the policy were implemented, it would engender social unrest. = 如果实施这项政策，它将导致社会动荡。
I wish the meeting hadn't engendered so much conflict. = 我真希望会议没有造成如此多的冲突。

被动语态:
Much anger was engendered by the government's decision. = 政府的决定引起了很大的愤怒。
New ideas are engendered by creative thinking. = 创造性思维会产生新的想法。

疑问句:
What factors engendered this result? = 哪些因素导致了这个结果？
What will engender change in this company? = 什么会在这个公司引起变化？

否定句:
The event did not engender any positive feelings. = 这件事没有引起任何积极的情绪。
His speech failed to engender support among the audience. = 他的演讲未能赢得听众的支持。


PHRASE->
engender trust  = 产生信任
engender confidence = 增强信心
engender support = 获得支持
engender hostility = 引起敌意
engender conflict = 引起冲突
