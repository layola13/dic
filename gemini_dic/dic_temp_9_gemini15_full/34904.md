cliquish:/ˈklɪkɪʃ/| adj.|排他的，小圈子似的，拉帮结派的|Characterized by or given to the formation of exclusive cliques.  The adjective describes a group or individual who tends to exclude outsiders and favor only those within their inner circle.  It often implies a sense of exclusivity and a lack of welcoming behavior towards others.|例句：The office felt cliquish;  new employees were often ignored by established groups.  办公室气氛很排外；新员工经常被老员工忽视。

The department's cliquish atmosphere made it difficult for newcomers to integrate. 部门的排外氛围使新来者难以融入。|近义词：exclusive, sectarian, factional, in-groupy|反义词：inclusive, welcoming, open, gregarious

词源:
From clique (a small, exclusive group of people), ultimately from the French word "clique," of uncertain origin, possibly from the Middle Dutch word "klikle," meaning "claw," or a similar word.  The association might be to the idea of a group clinging together like claws.
