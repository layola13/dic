legions:/ˈliːdʒənz/| n.|军团，大批，众多 |A legion is a large military unit in ancient Rome, typically consisting of several thousand soldiers.  The word now also refers to a large number of people or things. |例句：The Roman legions conquered much of Europe. 罗马军团征服了大部分欧洲。

The legions of fans waited patiently for the concert to begin.  一大群歌迷耐心地等待演唱会开始。|近义词：army, multitude, host, throng |反义词：few, handful
