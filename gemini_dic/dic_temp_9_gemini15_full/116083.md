marty:/ˈmɑːrti/| n. |烈士，殉道者 |A martyr is a person who is killed because of their religious or other beliefs.  Martyrs are often seen as heroic figures who sacrificed their lives for a cause. |Example: Joan of Arc was a martyr for her faith.  圣女贞德为了她的信仰而成为烈士。|近义词：hero, saint, victim |反义词：persecutor, oppressor


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a martyr for the cause of freedom. 他是为自由事业而牺牲的烈士。
They are martyrs to their beliefs. 他们是为信仰献身的殉道者。

一般过去时:
She was a martyr for her country. 她为国捐躯，成为烈士。
He became a martyr for his convictions. 他为了自己的信念而成为烈士。

现在完成时:
Many have martyred themselves for a better future. 许多人为更美好的未来献出了生命。
She has become a martyr in the eyes of many. 在许多人眼中，她已成为烈士。

过去完成时:
They had already martyred several leaders before the revolution.  革命前他们已经杀害了几位领导人。
He had long been considered a martyr by his followers. 他早已被追随者视为烈士。

将来时:
He will be remembered as a martyr. 他将被人们铭记为烈士。
They will likely martyr more people to achieve their goals. 他们很可能会为了达到目的而杀害更多的人。

虚拟语气:
If he had not been a martyr, he might have lived a long life. 如果他没有成为烈士，他可能会有很长的人生。
I wish he hadn't become a martyr so young. 我真希望他不要这么年轻就成为烈士。


被动语态:
Many martyrs were killed for their beliefs. 许多烈士因信仰而被杀害。
He was martyred for his outspoken views. 他因直言不讳而被杀害。


疑问句:
Was he a martyr or a traitor? 他是烈士还是叛徒？
Were they truly martyrs for the cause? 他们真的是为事业而牺牲的烈士吗？


否定句:
He was not a martyr; he was a criminal. 他不是烈士，他是个罪犯。
They were not martyrs; they were terrorists. 他们不是烈士，他们是恐怖分子。
