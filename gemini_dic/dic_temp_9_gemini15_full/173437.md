quasi-established:/ˈkwɑːziɪˈstæblɪʃt/| adj.|准已建立的；似已确立的；半官方的 |  Describes something that is almost or partially established, but not fully or officially so. It suggests a state of being between established and not established, often implying a lack of complete acceptance or formal recognition.  |例句：The quasi-established nature of the organization means it lacks the full legal backing of a registered charity.  这个组织准已建立的性质意味着它缺乏注册慈善机构的充分法律支持。

The company's quasi-established market position makes it vulnerable to competitors. 这家公司似已确立的市场地位使其容易受到竞争对手的攻击。|近义词：partially established, semi-official, informally recognized, tentatively established |反义词：fully established, officially recognized, firmly established
