entailment:/ɪnˈteɪlmənt/ | n. | 蕴含；必然结果；牵连 | The logical consequence or implication of something.  It refers to something that is implied or follows necessarily from something else.  | Example:  The decision to build the new factory entails significant job creation.  这个决定建造新工厂必然会带来大量的就业机会。 |近义词：implication, consequence, involvement |反义词：inconsistency, irrelevance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project entails a lot of work. = 这个项目需要很多工作。
This decision entails significant risk. = 这个决定包含很大的风险。


一般过去时:
The war entailed immense suffering. = 那场战争带来了巨大的苦难。
Her illness entailed a long period of recovery. = 她的疾病需要很长的恢复期。


现在完成时:
His actions have entailed serious consequences. = 他的行为已经带来了严重的后果。
The reforms have entailed significant changes to the system. = 改革已经给系统带来了重大的变化。


过去完成时:
The investigation had entailed numerous interviews. = 调查已经进行了大量的访谈。
The accident had entailed considerable expense. = 事故已经带来了相当大的损失。


将来时:
The new regulations will entail further costs. = 新的法规将带来额外的成本。
The expansion will entail hiring more staff. = 扩张将需要雇佣更多员工。


虚拟语气:
If we proceed, it would entail considerable risk. = 如果我们继续进行，那将冒很大的风险。
If I accepted the offer, it would entail relocating. = 如果我接受这个提议，就需要搬家。


被动语态:
Significant changes were entailed by the new policy. = 新政策带来了重大的变化。
Further investigation is entailed by the recent events. = 最近的事件需要进一步调查。


疑问句:
What does this plan entail? = 这个计划包含什么？
Does this job entail travel? = 这份工作需要出差吗？


否定句:
The agreement did not entail any financial obligations. = 该协议不包含任何财务义务。
The proposal does not entail any major changes. = 该提案不包含任何重大改变。



PHRASE->
entail upon: 使承担；强加于
entailment relation: 蕴含关系
mutual entailment: 互蕴
logical entailment: 逻辑蕴含
textual entailment: 语义蕴含
