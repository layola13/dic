/krɛd/ | n. | 信用，贷款，信用卡 | Credit refers to the trust given to a borrower that they will repay a debt, or the money lent or borrowed. It can also refer to a method of payment, like a credit card, or an acknowledgment of someone's contribution. |例句：He has excellent credit. 他信用极佳。|例句：The company gave him credit for his hard work. 公司肯定了他的辛勤工作。|近义词：loan, trust, reputation |反义词：debt, debit


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gets credit for his work. = 他因他的工作而获得好评。
We use credit cards for online shopping. = 我们使用信用卡进行网上购物。


一般过去时:
They received credit for their invention. = 他们因他们的发明而获得认可。
She paid for the car using credit. = 她用信用贷款买了车。


现在完成时:
I have built up my credit score. = 我已经提高了我的信用评分。
They have used credit to buy a house. = 他们用贷款买了一栋房子。


过去完成时:
He had already established good credit before applying for the loan. = 他在申请贷款之前就已经建立了良好的信用记录。
She had used her credit card before it was cancelled. = 在信用卡被注销之前，她已经使用过了。


将来时:
They will use credit to finance their business. = 他们将使用贷款来为他们的业务融资。
We will check his credit history before lending him money. = 我们会在借钱给他之前检查他的信用记录。


虚拟语气:
If he had better credit, he could buy a house. = 如果他的信用更好，他就能买房子了。
I wish I had better credit. = 我希望我的信用更好。


被动语态:

The purchase was made using credit. = 这笔购买是用信用贷款支付的。


疑问句:

What kind of credit is available? = 有哪种类型的信用贷款？


否定句:
