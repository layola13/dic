demonstrabilities:/ˌdɛmənˈstreɪtəˈbɪlətiz/| n. |可证实性；可论证性|The demonstrabilities of the theory are still under debate.  该理论的可证实性仍在争论中。|近义词：provability, verifiability |反义词：unprovability, unverifiability


英文解析:The plural noun "demonstrabilities" refers to the qualities or characteristics that make something demonstrable or capable of being proven or shown to be true.  It emphasizes the capacity for evidence and proof.


例句:

The high demonstrabilities of the scientific findings convinced the skeptical scientists. 科学发现的高度可证实性说服了那些持怀疑态度的科学家们。

The court case hinged on the demonstrabilities of the presented evidence.  该案件的成败取决于所提供证据的可证实性。


词源:

Derived from "demonstrable," which comes from the verb "demonstrate," ultimately from Latin "demonstrare" (to point out, show).  The "-ilities" suffix denotes a quality or state.
