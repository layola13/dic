词源:

The word "impecuniousness" derives from the Latin word "pecunia," meaning "money."  The prefix "im-" indicates a negation or lack of something. Thus, "impecunious" means "lacking money," and "-ness" is a suffix that turns an adjective into a noun, resulting in "impecuniousness."
