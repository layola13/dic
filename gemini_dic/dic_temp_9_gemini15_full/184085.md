resigning:/rɪˈzaɪnɪŋ/| v. |辞职；放弃；顺从；听任 |The act of giving up a job, position, or responsibility.  It also implies accepting something unpleasant without protest. |例句：He is resigning from his position as CEO. 他正在辞去首席执行官的职位。

例句：She resigned herself to her fate. 她听天由命。

近义词：quitting, relinquishing, surrendering

反义词：accepting, embracing, retaining


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is resigning from his job next month. = 他下个月要辞职。
She is resigning herself to the inevitable. = 她听天由命。

一般过去时:
He resigned from his position last week. = 他上周辞职了。
She resigned herself to a life of poverty. = 她认命了，过着贫穷的生活。

现在完成时:
He has resigned from the company. = 他已经离开了公司。
She has resigned herself to the fact that she won't win. = 她已经接受了她不会赢的事实。

过去完成时:
He had resigned before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前他就辞职了。
She had resigned herself to failure before even trying. = 甚至还没尝试之前她就认定自己会失败了。

将来时:
He will resign if the situation doesn't improve. = 如果情况没有好转，他就会辞职。
She will resign herself to whatever happens. = 她会顺其自然。


虚拟语气:
I would resign if I were in his shoes. = 如果我是他，我就辞职了。
She would have resigned herself to it long ago if she had known. = 如果她早知道的话，她早就认命了。

被动语态:
His resignation was announced yesterday. = 他的辞职声明昨天发布了。 (This is less common for the "accepting" sense)


疑问句:
Is he resigning? = 他要辞职吗？
Has she resigned herself to the situation? = 她对这种情况已经认命了吗？

否定句:
He is not resigning yet. = 他还没辞职。
She did not resign herself to defeat. = 她没有认输。


PHRASE->
resign one's position = 辞职
resign from a job = 辞去工作
resign oneself to = 顺从；听任
resign one's commission = 辞去委托
resign one's office = 辞去公职
