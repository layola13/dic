compound-noun:/ˈkɒmpaʊnd naʊn/| n.|复合名词|A compound noun is a noun that is made up of two or more words. These words can be combined in several ways, such as open compounds (two words separated by a space), closed compounds (two words joined together without a space), or hyphenated compounds (two words joined together with a hyphen). Compound nouns function grammatically as single units, referring to a specific entity, concept, or thing.  | Example:  The "firetruck" raced to the scene. (closed compound)  He received a "high-five" from his teammate. (hyphenated compound) She works in the "customer service" department. (open compound)|近义词: combined noun, multiple-word noun |反义词: simple noun
