phonetician: /ˌfoʊniˈʃən/ | n. |语音学家| A phonetician is a scientist who studies the sounds of human speech.  They analyze the physical properties of speech sounds, how they are produced, and how they are perceived.  Phoneticians often work in universities, research labs, or speech therapy settings. | Example: The phonetician analyzed the pronunciation of the vowel sounds in different dialects. 语音学家分析了不同方言中元音发音。|近义词：linguist (if focusing on the linguistic aspects of speech sounds) |反义词：None specifically.
