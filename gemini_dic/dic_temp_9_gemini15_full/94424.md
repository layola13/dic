implies:/ɪmˈplaɪz/ | vt. |暗示；意味；表明 | to express (something) in an indirect way; to suggest (something) without saying or showing it plainly | 例句：His silence implies consent. 他的沉默意味着同意。
例句：The evidence implies that he was guilty.  证据暗示他是有罪的。|近义词：suggest, indicate, signify | 反义词：state explicitly, declare


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He implies that he is unhappy. = 他暗示他不开心。
Her tone implies disapproval. = 她的语气暗示着不满。

一般过去时:
The article implied a need for reform. = 这篇文章暗示需要改革。
She implied that she knew more than she was saying. = 她暗示她知道比说的更多。

现在完成时:
The study has implied a link between diet and disease. = 这项研究已经暗示了饮食和疾病之间的联系。
Recent events have implied a change in government policy. = 最近发生的事件暗示了政府政策的改变。

过去完成时:
His behaviour had implied a lack of confidence. = 他的行为暗示了他缺乏自信。
The letter had implied that they were in danger. = 这封信暗示他们处于危险之中。

将来时:
The report will imply a serious problem with the project. = 这份报告将暗示项目存在严重问题。
His actions will imply a change of heart. = 他的行为将暗示他改变了主意。

虚拟语气:
If his words implied a threat, I didn't notice it. = 如果他的话暗示了威胁，我没有注意到。
If the data implied a different conclusion, we would have to revise our hypothesis. = 如果数据暗示了不同的结论，我们将不得不修改我们的假设。

被动语态:
It was implied that the company was in financial trouble. = 暗示公司正面临财务困境。
The need for change was implied in the report. = 报告中暗示了改变的必要性。

疑问句:
What does his behaviour imply? = 他的行为暗示了什么？
Does this statement imply a threat? = 这句话暗示威胁吗？

否定句:
He didn't imply any criticism in his remarks. = 他在讲话中并没有暗示任何批评。
The evidence does not imply guilt. = 证据并不暗示有罪。


PHRASE->
imply that = 暗示…
imply something = 暗示某事
imply a meaning = 暗含某种意义
imply a threat = 暗示威胁
imply something indirectly = 间接暗示某事
