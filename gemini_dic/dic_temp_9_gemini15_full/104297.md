justificatory:/ˌdʒʌs·tɪˈfɪkəˌtɔːri/ | adj. |辩护的；证明正当的；为…辩护的 | Serving to justify or excuse.  It provides a reason or explanation for something, often something that might otherwise be considered wrong or questionable.  |例句：His justificatory remarks failed to convince the jury.他的辩解未能说服陪审团。

例句：The company offered a justificatory statement for its actions.该公司就其行为发表了一份辩护声明。


近义词：exculpatory, explanatory, apologetic | 反义词：incriminatory, condemnatory


词源:  Derived from the word "justify," which itself comes from the Old French "iustifier" and ultimately from the Latin "iustificare," meaning "to declare just or righteous."  The suffix "-tory" indicates something that has the function or quality of doing something.
