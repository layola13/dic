investiture:/ɪnˈvɛstɪtʃər/ | n. |授职仪式；授予；封授 |The formal act of investing someone with an office or dignity; the conferring of a rank, title, or office.  |Example: The investiture of the new king was a grand ceremony.  新的国王的授职仪式是一场盛大的典礼。 |近义词：installation, inauguration, induction |反义词：removal, dismissal


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company invests heavily in research and development. = 这家公司大力投资研发。
He invests his savings in the stock market. = 他把积蓄投资股市。


一般过去时:
The knight was invested with the Order of the Garter. = 骑士被授予嘉德勋章。
She invested her inheritance in real estate. = 她把继承的遗产投资房地产。


现在完成时:
They have invested millions of dollars in the project. = 他们已经在这个项目上投资了数百万美元。
He has invested wisely and built a substantial fortune. = 他投资得很明智，积累了可观的财富。


过去完成时:
Before the ceremony, the king had invested the nobleman with a dukedom. = 典礼之前，国王已将公爵爵位授予这位贵族。
She had invested all her money before the market crashed. = 在市场崩盘之前，她已经投资了所有的钱。


将来时:
They will invest more in renewable energy sources. = 他们将更多地投资可再生能源。
We will invest in new equipment next year. = 我们明年将投资新设备。


虚拟语气:
If he had invested earlier, he would be richer now. = 如果他早点投资的话，现在就会更富有。
I wish I had invested in that company. = 我真希望我当时投资了那家公司。


被动语态:
The new CEO was invested with full authority. = 新任首席执行官被赋予了全部权力。
The funds were invested in government bonds. = 这些资金投资于政府债券。


疑问句:
Where did they invest their money? = 他们把钱投资在哪里了？
Will you invest in this promising startup? = 你会投资这家有前途的初创公司吗？


否定句:
He did not invest in the stock market. = 他没有投资股市。
They will not invest any more money in this project. = 他们不会再在这个项目上投资任何资金。
