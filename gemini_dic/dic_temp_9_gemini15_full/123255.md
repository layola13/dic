monodelphia:/ˌmɒnəˈdɛlfiə/| n. | 单孔类动物；后兽亚纲 |  Monodelphia refers to a subclass of mammals characterized by having a single opening (cloaca) for the urinary, reproductive, and digestive tracts. This subclass encompasses marsupials and placentals.  | Example:  Kangaroos and humans are both examples of monodelphia.  袋鼠和人类都是单孔类的例子。|近义词：Metatheria (for marsupials), Eutheria (for placentals) |反义词：Prototheria (monotremes, which have a cloaca but are a different subclass) |名词
