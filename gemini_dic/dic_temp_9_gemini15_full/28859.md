carpetbagger:/ˈkɑːrpɪtˌbæɡər/ | n. |南部佬，投机政客 | A carpetbagger is a Northerner who went to the South after the American Civil War to take advantage of the political and economic instability there.  They were often seen as opportunistic and exploitative. The term is now used more broadly to refer to anyone who comes to a new place to exploit its resources or opportunities, often in a cynical or self-serving way. |Example:  After the war, many carpetbaggers flocked to the South, seeking to profit from the Reconstruction era.  战后，许多南部佬涌入南方，试图从重建时期中获利。 |近义词：opportunist, exploiter, profiteer |反义词：native, altruist
