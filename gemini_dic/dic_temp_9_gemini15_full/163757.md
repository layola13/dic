predelegate:/ˌpriːˈdɛlɪɡeɪt/| v.|预先委派，提前授权|To assign or delegate beforehand or in advance.|The board decided to predelegate the authority to negotiate the contract to the CEO.|近义词：delegate, authorize, empower|反义词：retain, withhold


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They predelegate responsibilities to their team members. = 他们预先将责任委派给团队成员。
The company predelegates certain tasks to external contractors. = 公司预先将某些任务委派给外部承包商。


一般过去时:
The manager predelegated the project to the new team leader. = 经理预先将项目委派给了新的团队领导。
She predelegated her voting rights to her spouse. = 她预先将其投票权委托给了她的配偶。


现在完成时:
The committee has predelegated the power to make decisions. = 委员会已经预先授权了决策权。
He has predelegated his responsibilities for the upcoming trip. = 他已经预先委托了他即将旅行期间的责任。


过去完成时:
They had predelegated the tasks before the meeting commenced. = 他们在会议开始之前就已经预先分配了任务。
The CEO had predelegated the authority to sign the documents. = 首席执行官已预先授权签署文件。


将来时:
The president will predelegate the signing of the treaty to the ambassador. = 总统将预先授权大使签署条约。
We will predelegate the work to ensure timely completion. = 我们将预先分配工作以确保及时完成。


虚拟语气:
If the board predelegated the authority, the negotiations would have gone smoother. = 如果董事会预先授权，谈判就会进行得更顺利。
I wish they had predelegated the responsibilities more clearly. = 我希望他们能更清晰地预先分配责任。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
