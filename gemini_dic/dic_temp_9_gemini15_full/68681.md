farcicality:/fɑːˈsɪkəlɪti/ | n. |滑稽性；荒谬性 |The absurdity or ludicrousness of a situation or event; the quality of being farcical.  It refers to the element of humor derived from the incongruity and improbability of events, often involving exaggerated characters and situations.|The farcicality of the situation was undeniable; a penguin in a tutu attempting to conduct an orchestra.  |近义词：absurdity, ludicrousness, buffoonery, silliness |反义词：seriousness, gravity, solemnity
