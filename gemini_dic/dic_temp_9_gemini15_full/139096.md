obdiplostemony: /ˌɒbdɪˈplɒstɪməni/ | adj. |  反花柱雄蕊的 |  Referring to flowers having two whorls of stamens, the outer whorl being opposite to the petals and the inner whorl alternate with the petals. This arrangement is characteristic of certain plant families, particularly some within the Primulaceae (primrose family) and other related groups.  | The flower exhibits obdiplostemony, a feature often cited in taxonomic keys for identifying certain plant genera. | 近义词:  (no direct synonym, as it describes a specific floral arrangement) | 反义词: (no direct antonym, but  "diplostemony" with the inner whorl opposite the petals could be considered a contrasting arrangement) |  形容词
