crumbles:/ˈkrʌmbəlz/| n. v.|碎屑；崩塌；（使）破碎|The word "crumbles" can function as both a noun and a verb. As a noun, it refers to small pieces of something, especially bread or cake.  As a verb, it means to break into small pieces or to fall apart. |例句：The cake crumbled when I cut it.  蛋糕在我切的时候碎了。The old building is crumbling.  那座旧建筑正在崩塌。|近义词：(n.) fragments, bits; (v.) disintegrates, collapses|反义词：(n.) whole, intact; (v.) solidifies, strengthens|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The cake crumbles easily. = 蛋糕很容易碎。
The empire crumbles slowly. = 这个帝国正在慢慢崩塌。

一般过去时:
The cookie crumbled in my hand. = 饼干在我手里碎了。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

否定句:
