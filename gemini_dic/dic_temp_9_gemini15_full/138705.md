nullification:/ˌnʌlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |废止；取消；无效化 |The act of making something legally void or ineffective.  It often refers to a state's attempt to invalidate a federal law within its borders.  |例句：The Supreme Court ruled against the state's attempt at nullification of the federal law. 最高法院裁决反对该州试图废除联邦法律的行为。


例句：The governor's attempt at nullification was ultimately unsuccessful. 州长的废止尝试最终失败了。


近义词：invalidation, annulment, repeal, abolition |反义词：ratification, confirmation, validation |词性：名词


词源:  From Latin *nullificatio, from nullificare "to make null," from nullus "nothing" + -ficare, a suffix forming verbs meaning "to make."
