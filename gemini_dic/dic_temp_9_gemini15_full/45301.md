culverts:/ˈkʌlvərts/| n.|涵洞，暗渠|A culvert is a structure that carries water under a road, railroad, trail, or other obstruction.  It's typically a pipe, box, or other conduit. |例句：The culvert under the highway was clogged with debris.  高速公路下的涵洞被碎石堵塞了。

例句：The new culverts improved drainage in the area. 新建的涵洞改善了该地区的排水。


近义词：conduit, drain, channel, waterway|反义词：bridge, overpass|词性:名词

词源:
The word "culvert" comes from the Old French word "culverta," which ultimately derives from the Latin words "culvertus," meaning "covered," and "vertere," meaning "to turn."  This reflects the function of a culvert as a covered passage for water to turn or flow underneath something.
