clumsiest: /ˈklʌmziːst/ | adj. | 最笨拙的，最笨手笨脚的 |  The superlative form of "clumsy," describing someone or something that is extremely awkward and ungainly in movement or handling things.  It emphasizes the highest degree of clumsiness. | Example: He was the clumsiest person I've ever met; he tripped over the air. 他是我见过的最笨拙的人；他连空气都能绊倒。  She dropped the vase, proving herself the clumsiest in the room. 她打碎了花瓶，证明自己是房间里最笨手笨脚的人。| 近义词：awkward, ungainly, gauche, inept | 反义词：graceful, dexterous, skillful, adroit |  形容词

词源:
The word "clumsiest" derives from the Middle English word "clumsen," meaning "awkward, clumsy." This in turn likely comes from an Old Norse word, though the exact etymology is debated.  The superlative "-est" suffix is a standard English grammatical addition to create the superlative degree of the adjective.
