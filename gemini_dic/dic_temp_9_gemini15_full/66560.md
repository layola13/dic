exhibitionism:/ˌɛksɪˈbɪʃənɪzəm/ | n. |炫耀癖；卖弄；展览主义 |The act of displaying one's body or behavior for attention or admiration, often in a way that is considered inappropriate or shocking.  It can manifest in various ways, ranging from subtle attention-seeking to overtly provocative behavior.  This can be driven by a deep-seated need for validation or a desire to overcome feelings of inadequacy.  In psychology, it's often associated with personality disorders. |例句：His constant boasting about his wealth is a clear sign of exhibitionism. 他不断炫耀自己的财富，这明显是炫耀狂的表现。

His flamboyant clothing and outrageous behavior were classic examples of exhibitionism. 他华丽的服装和古怪的行为是展览主义的典型例子。|近义词：ostentation, showmanship, boastfulness |反义词：modesty, humility, reserve |名词
