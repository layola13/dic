diptera: /ˈdɪptərə/ | n. | 双翅目昆虫 | Diptera refers to an order of insects characterized by having only two wings, as opposed to the four wings found in most other flying insects.  This order includes flies, mosquitoes, and gnats. The name comes from the Greek words "di" (two) and "pteron" (wing). | Example: Many diptera are important pollinators, while others are vectors for diseases.  许多双翅目昆虫是重要的传粉者，而另一些则是疾病的媒介。| 近义词:  flies (informal), two-winged insects | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but could be contrasted with insects with four wings, such as Hymenoptera)
