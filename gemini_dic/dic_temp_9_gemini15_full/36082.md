coelentera: /siːlənˈtɛrə/ | n. |腔肠动物；腔肠动物门 | Coelenterata (also known as Cnidaria) is a phylum containing over 10,000 aquatic invertebrate species, including jellyfish, corals, sea anemones, and hydroids. They are characterized by radial symmetry, a gastrovascular cavity, and stinging cells called nematocysts.  | Example:  The vibrant colors of the coelenterates on the reef were breathtaking.  腔肠动物在珊瑚礁上的鲜艳色彩令人叹为观止。 | 近义词: Cnidaria | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it refers to a taxonomic group)
