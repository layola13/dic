preextinguishment: /ˌpriːɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃmənt/ | n. | 预先灭火；预防性灭火 | The act or process of extinguishing a fire before it becomes widespread or causes significant damage.  It often involves proactive measures taken to prevent a fire from starting or spreading. | Example:  The preextinguishment system automatically sprayed water onto the potential ignition source, preventing a large fire. = 预先灭火系统自动向潜在的着火点喷水，防止大火发生。


The fire department conducted a preextinguishment inspection of the building to identify and mitigate potential hazards. = 消防部门对该建筑进行了预防性灭火检查，以识别和减轻潜在危害。| 近义词：fire prevention, fire suppression, preemptive fire control | 反义词：post-extinguishment, fire damage control
