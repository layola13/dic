nonmaterialistically:/ˌnɒnˌmætəriəˈlɪstɪkli/ | adv. |非物质主义地；精神上地 |  Adverb formed from "nonmaterialistic," indicating an approach or perspective that prioritizes non-material values over material possessions or wealth. It suggests a focus on spiritual, intellectual, or emotional aspects of life. |Example:  He lived nonmaterialistically, finding fulfillment in experiences rather than acquisitions. 他过着非物质主义的生活，从体验而不是从获得中获得满足。|近义词：spiritually, ideally, ethically |反义词：materialistically, greedily, avariciously

Etymology:

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of."
* **materialistic**: Adjective derived from "materialism,"  a philosophical viewpoint that emphasizes the importance of matter and physical reality over mind or spirit.  "Materialism" itself comes from "material" (matter) + "-ism" (a doctrine or belief system).
* **-ally**: Adverbial suffix, transforming the adjective "nonmaterialistic" into an adverb.
