hustles:/ˈhʌsəl/| v., n.|努力工作；奔波；欺骗；(非正式)忙碌；|英文解析：As a verb, "hustle" means to work hard and energetically; to act quickly and efficiently; or to cheat or swindle someone. As a noun, it refers to energetic activity; a way of making a living; or a swindle.  |例句：He hustles every day to make ends meet. 他每天努力工作以维持生计。He was accused of hustling money from elderly people. 他被指控骗取老年人的钱财。|近义词：scramble, struggle, rush, bustle, swindle, cheat|反义词：idle, loaf, relax, rest


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hustles every day. = 他每天都在努力工作。
She hustles to get everything done on time. = 她努力工作以按时完成所有事情。

一般过去时:
He hustled all night to finish the project. = 他整夜努力工作以完成项目。
They hustled to catch the train. = 他们匆忙赶火车。

现在完成时:
She has hustled her way to the top of her company. = 她通过努力工作到达了她公司的顶峰。
He has hustled all his life to provide for his family. = 他一生都在努力工作以养家糊口。

过去完成时:
They had hustled to get ready before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，他们已经忙着准备了。
She had hustled all week and was exhausted. = 她忙活了一周，筋疲力尽了。

将来时:
He will hustle to meet the deadline. = 他将努力赶上截止日期。
They will hustle to find a solution to the problem. = 他们将努力寻找问题的解决办法。

虚拟语气:
If I hustled harder, I might succeed. = 如果我更努力工作，我可能会成功。
I wish I had hustled more in my youth. = 我希望我年轻时更努力工作。

被动语态:
He was hustled out of the building. = 他被匆忙地赶出了大楼。(此处指被强行带走)
The deal was hustled through quickly. = 这笔交易被迅速匆忙地完成了。

疑问句:
Does he hustle every day? = 他每天都努力工作吗？

否定句:
He doesn't hustle enough. = 他不够努力。
They didn't hustle to get there on time. = 他们没有努力按时到达那里。
