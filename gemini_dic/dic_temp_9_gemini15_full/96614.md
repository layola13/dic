indulgence:/ɪnˈdʌldʒəns/ | n. | 纵容；放纵；沉溺；宽容；迁就 | The act of indulging or being indulgent; yielding to one's desires or whims.  It can refer to a specific instance of indulgence, or a general tendency towards it. |例句：Her indulgence in chocolate was legendary.  她的巧克力瘾是出了名的。

例句：The parents showed great indulgence towards their spoiled child. 父母对他们被宠坏的孩子非常纵容。


近义词：pampering, gratification, self-indulgence, leniency, permissiveness | 反义词：restraint, abstinence, self-discipline, strictness, severity | 词性:名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He indulges in video games every evening. 他每天晚上都沉迷于电子游戏。
She indulges her sweet tooth with frequent dessert. 她经常吃甜点来满足自己的嗜甜。


一般过去时:
They indulged in a luxurious vacation after years of hard work. 他们经过多年的努力后，享受了一次奢华的假期。
He indulged his anger by shouting at his boss. 他通过向老板大喊来发泄怒气。


现在完成时:
I have indulged myself with a long, hot bath. 我给自己泡了个长时间的热水澡来放松一下。
She has indulged in too much junk food lately. 她最近吃了太多垃圾食品。


过去完成时:
They had indulged in several glasses of wine before the argument started. 争吵开始之前，他们已经喝了几杯酒。
He had indulged his fantasies for years before finally taking action. 他多年来一直沉溺于幻想，然后才最终采取行动。


将来时:
We will indulge in a celebratory dinner after the wedding. 婚礼结束后，我们将举行庆祝晚宴。
They will indulge their children with many gifts for Christmas. 圣诞节他们将给孩子们很多礼物。


虚拟语气:
If I had the time, I would indulge in a relaxing weekend getaway. 如果我有时间，我会享受一个轻松的周末短途旅行。
I wish I could indulge in a long nap. 我真希望我能好好睡上一觉。


被动语态:
His bad habits were indulged by his parents. 他的坏习惯被父母纵容了。
Children should not be indulged in everything they want. 孩子们不应该被百依百顺。


疑问句:
Does he often indulge in unhealthy habits? 他经常沉迷于不健康的习惯吗？
Will you indulge my request? 你会满足我的要求吗？


否定句:
He doesn't indulge in gossip. 他不参与八卦。
She didn't indulge her cravings for chocolate. 她没有满足自己对巧克力的渴望。
