dicacity:/dɪˈkæsɪti/| n.|大胆；无畏；放肆；鲁莽|Audacity or boldness.  It suggests a spirited, even reckless disregard for consequences.  The word often carries a slightly negative connotation, implying a lack of judgment or excessive confidence.|例句：Her dicacity in challenging the CEO surprised everyone.  她的大胆举动，挑战首席执行官，让所有人都大吃一惊。

例句：His dicacity led him into trouble. 他的鲁莽使他陷入困境。


近义词：audacity, boldness, temerity, effrontery, nerve|反义词：timidity, cowardice, diffidence, humility|词性: 名词


词源:  The word "dicacity" derives from the Latin word "dicax," meaning "talkative," "witty," or "sharp-tongued." Over time, the meaning shifted to emphasize the boldness and sometimes reckless nature of speech, eventually evolving into its current meaning of audacity or boldness in action.
