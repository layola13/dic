presumably:/prɪˈzjuːməbli/ | adv. |大概，可能，据推测 |It is generally accepted that something is true, although it is not certain.  It expresses a belief based on what seems likely. |例句：Presumably, he'll be at the meeting.  他大概会在会上吧。 |例句：Presumably, the delay is due to bad weather.  推测延误是由于天气恶劣造成的。|近义词：probably, likely, supposedly |反义词：certainly, definitely


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He presumably works hard. = 他大概努力工作。
She presumably knows the answer. = 她大概知道答案。

一般过去时:
They presumably arrived late. = 他们大概迟到了。
He presumably missed the bus. = 他大概错过了公共汽车。

现在完成时:
She presumably has finished her work. = 她大概已经完成了她的工作。
They presumably have already left. = 他们大概已经离开了。

过去完成时:
He presumably had already eaten. = 他大概已经吃过了。
They presumably had known the truth. = 他们大概已经知道真相了。

将来时:
It will presumably rain tomorrow. = 明天大概会下雨。
They will presumably be there on time. = 他们大概会准时到那里。

虚拟语气:
He would presumably be happy if he knew. = 如果他知道的话，他大概会很高兴。
She would presumably have gone if invited. = 如果被邀请的话，她大概会去的。

被动语态:
The problem was presumably solved. = 问题大概解决了。
The decision was presumably made yesterday. = 决定大概昨天做出的。

疑问句:
Would he presumably be interested? = 他大概会感兴趣吗？
Is she presumably coming? = 她大概会来吗？

否定句:
It is presumably not true. = 这大概不是真的。
He presumably didn't mean to hurt her. = 他大概不是故意要伤害她的。
