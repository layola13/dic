insignificancy:/ˌɪnsɪɡˈnɪfɪkənsi/ | n. |无足轻重；微不足道；不重要 | The insignificancy of his role in the project was evident.  他的项目角色微不足道，这一点显而易见。|近义词：unimportance, triviality, nothingness |反义词：significance, importance


英文解析:The state or quality of being insignificant; lack of importance or value.  指不重要或缺乏价值的状态或品质。


例句:

His contributions were dismissed as insignificancy. 他的贡献被认为微不足道。

The insignificancy of the problem was soon forgotten.  这个问题的微不足道很快就被遗忘了。


词源:

* **in-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "without"  (否定前缀，意为“不”或“无”)
* **signific-**:  from Latin *significare*, meaning "to signify" or "to make a sign" (源于拉丁语 *significare*，意为“表示”或“做标记”)
* **-ancy**:  suffix indicating a state or quality (后缀，表示状态或品质)


Therefore, insignificancy literally means "the state of not signifying" or "the state of being without importance."  因此，insignificancy 字面意思是“不表示的状态”或“不重要的状态”。
