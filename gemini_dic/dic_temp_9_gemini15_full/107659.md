languishing:/ˈlæŋɡwɪʃɪŋ/| adj.| 衰弱的；萎靡的；被忽视的 |  Failing to make progress or be successful.  Characterized by a lack of energy, vitality, or growth.  Often implies a state of neglect or unhappiness. |例句：The economy is languishing. = 经济正在衰退。

The project has been languishing for months. = 这个项目已经搁置了几个月。


近义词：failing, declining, deteriorating, stagnating, withering, drooping

反义词：flourishing, thriving, prospering, advancing, progressing


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The business is languishing due to lack of investment. = 由于缺乏投资，这家公司正在衰退。
His career is languishing after years of inactivity. =  他多年无所事事，事业停滞不前。


一般过去时:
The plant languished after the drought. = 干旱过后，植物枯萎了。
The town languished for years after the factory closed. = 工厂倒闭后，这个小镇衰落了好几年。


现在完成时:
The economy has been languishing for the past decade. = 过去十年经济一直低迷。
The project has languished since the funding was cut. = 自从资金被削减以来，该项目就一直停滞不前。


过去完成时:
The company had languished for years before it finally went bankrupt. = 这家公司在最终破产之前已经衰落多年了。
The relationship had languished for months before they decided to separate. =  他们的关系已经持续低迷了几个月，然后他们决定分手。


将来时:
If we don't take action, the company will languish. = 如果我们不采取行动，公司将会衰落。
Without investment, the project will languish for years. =  如果没有投资，这个项目将会停滞多年。


虚拟语气:
If the government hadn't intervened, the industry would have languished. = 如果政府不干预，这个行业就会衰落。
I wish my creative skills weren't languishing. = 我真希望我的创造力不要停滞不前。


被动语态:
The proposal was languished in the committee for months. =  这个提案在委员会里搁置了几个月。
The neglected garden was languishing, full of weeds. = 被忽视的花园里杂草丛生，一片萧条。


疑问句:
Is the market languishing due to low demand? = 市场是因为需求低迷而低迷吗？
Are your talents languishing in this unchallenging job? =  你的才能在这个没有挑战性的工作中被埋没了吗？


否定句:
The business is not languishing; it's thriving. = 这家公司没有衰退，它正在蓬勃发展。
His career is not languishing; he's making great progress. =  他的事业没有停滞不前，他正在取得很大的进步。
