shakas:/ˈʃɑːkəz/| n. |沙卡手势；冲浪手势|A gesture made with the hand, typically with the thumb and pinky finger extended while the other fingers are curled, often used in surfing and Hawaiian culture as a sign of greeting, respect, or solidarity.  It is also known as the "hang loose" sign.|例句：He gave me a shaka as he paddled past. 他划桨经过时给了我一个沙卡手势。

例句：The surfers exchanged shakas after a successful wave. 冲浪者在成功冲浪后互相做了沙卡手势。|近义词：hang loose, hang ten (partial)|反义词:  (no direct antonym, context-dependent)|名词
