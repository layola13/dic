consimilitude:/kɒnsɪˈmɪlɪtjuːd/ | n. |相似，类似，相似之处 | resemblance or similarity; a likeness.  The consimilitude between the two paintings was striking.  |近义词：similarity, resemblance, likeness, analogy |反义词：dissimilarity, difference, contrast | 名词


英文解析:Consimilitude refers to a resemblance or similarity between things. It suggests a close likeness, often in appearance, character, or quality.  The word emphasizes a degree of correspondence or analogy, going beyond mere superficial resemblance.


例句:

The consimilitude between the two brothers was uncanny.  = 两兄弟之间的相似之处令人难以置信。

There is a remarkable consimilitude in the writing styles of the two authors. = 两位作者的写作风格有着惊人的相似之处。


词源:

The word "consimilitude" comes from the Late Latin word *consimilitudo*, a combination of the prefix *con-* (meaning "together" or "with") and *similitudo* (meaning "likeness" or "similarity").  *Similitudo* itself derives from the Latin word *similis* ("like," "similar") and the suffix *-tudo* (forming nouns of quality or state).


USAGE->  (Consimilitude, being a noun, doesn't have verb conjugations.  The following shows how it's used in different sentence structures.)

陈述句: The consimilitude of their voices was immediately apparent. = 他们声音的相似之处立刻显而易见。
The painting showed a striking consimilitude to the original. = 这幅画与原作有着惊人的相似之处。

疑问句: Is there any consimilitude between these two theories? = 这两种理论之间有什么相似之处吗？
What is the consimilitude between a metaphor and a simile? = 隐喻和明喻之间有什么相似之处？

否定句: There was no consimilitude between the two accounts of the accident. = 对事故的两种说法之间没有任何相似之处。
The expert denied any consimilitude between the suspect's alibi and the evidence. = 专家否认嫌疑人的不在场证明与证据之间有任何相似之处。
