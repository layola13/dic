crushed:/krʌʃt/| v. adj. n.|压碎的；受挫的；失败的；|to press or squeeze something with force so that it loses its shape or is broken into pieces; to defeat someone completely; feeling very unhappy because of a failure or disappointment |例句：The car was crushed in the accident.  这辆车在事故中被压扁了。The weight of the responsibility crushed him.  责任的重担压垮了他。|近义词：squashed, flattened, defeated, devastated |反义词：intact, whole, successful, triumphant


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He crushes the can. 他压扁易拉罐。
She crushes the grapes for wine. 她把葡萄压碎酿酒。


一般过去时:
The earthquake crushed the buildings. 地震摧毁了建筑物。
He felt crushed by the news. 他听到这个消息后感到非常沮丧。


现在完成时:
The economy has been crushed by the pandemic. 疫情重创了经济。
His spirit has been crushed by repeated failures. 他屡遭失败，意志消沉。


过去完成时:
The car had been crushed before the police arrived.  警察到达之前，车已经被压扁了。
He had felt crushed after losing the election. 选举失利后，他感到非常沮丧。


将来时:
The machine will crush the rocks. 机器将粉碎岩石。
This news will crush her. 这个消息会让她崩溃。


虚拟语气:
If I had known the truth, I wouldn’t have been so crushed. 如果我知道真相，我就不会这么沮丧了。
If only he hadn’t been crushed by the situation. 但愿他不会被这种情况击垮。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
