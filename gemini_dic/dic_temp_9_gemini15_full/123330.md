monography:/məˈnɒɡrəfi/| n. |专著，论文|A monograph is a scholarly work written on a single subject or a specific aspect of a larger subject.  It is typically longer and more detailed than an article, but shorter than a book.  Monographs often represent original research and provide in-depth analysis. |Example:  He wrote a monograph on the life and works of Shakespeare. 他写了一部关于莎士比亚生平和作品的专著。|近义词：treatise, dissertation, study|反义词：overview, summary|名词
