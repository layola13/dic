conodont:/ˈkɒnədɒnt/ | n. |牙形石 | Microscopic tooth-like fossils of extinct marine animals.  They are important index fossils used in stratigraphy to date sedimentary rocks. | Example: The discovery of conodonts in the limestone confirmed its age to the early Paleozoic.  牙形石在石灰岩中的发现证实了其属于早古生代的时代。|近义词: microfossil (a general term)|反义词:macrofossil
