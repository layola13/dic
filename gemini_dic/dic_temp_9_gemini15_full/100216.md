interrogates:/ɪnˈtɛrəɡeɪt/| vt.|讯问，审问|To ask someone a lot of questions, especially in a forceful way, in order to get information.  |例句：The police interrogated the suspect for hours. 警方讯问了嫌疑犯数小时。|例句：The journalist interrogated the politician about his controversial policies.  记者就其有争议的政策盘问了这位政客。|近义词：question, examine, quiz, cross-examine|反义词：release, free


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The police interrogates suspects regularly. = 警方定期讯问嫌疑人。
She interrogates her students to ensure they understand the material. = 她盘问她的学生以确保他们理解材料。

一般过去时:
They interrogated the witness for several hours. = 他们讯问了证人几个小时。
The detective interrogated the prime suspect last night. = 侦探昨晚讯问了主要嫌疑人。

现在完成时:
The authorities have interrogated all the potential witnesses. = 当局已经讯问了所有可能的证人。
The investigators have interrogated the suspect multiple times. = 调查人员多次讯问了嫌疑人。

过去完成时:
The police had interrogated the man before he confessed. = 警方在他认罪前已经讯问了他。
The detective had interrogated several people before identifying the culprit. = 侦探在确定罪犯之前已经讯问了几个人。

将来时:
The judge will interrogate the defendant tomorrow. = 法官明天将讯问被告。
The lawyer will interrogate the witness in court. = 律师将在法庭上盘问证人。

虚拟语气:
If the police interrogated him more carefully, they might find more evidence. = 如果警方更仔细地审问他，他们可能会找到更多证据。
I wish the detective had interrogated the witness more thoroughly. = 我希望侦探能更彻底地盘问证人。

被动语态:
The suspect was interrogated for hours. = 嫌疑人被讯问了数小时。
The witnesses were interrogated separately. = 证人们被分别讯问。

疑问句:
Will the police interrogate the suspect again? = 警方还会讯问嫌疑人吗？
Have they interrogated all the relevant people? = 他们已经讯问所有相关人员了吗？

否定句:
The police did not interrogate the witness. = 警方没有讯问证人。
They haven't interrogated the main suspect yet. = 他们还没有讯问主要嫌疑人。


PHRASE->
rigorous interrogation = 严格的审问
intense interrogation = 激烈的审问
lengthy interrogation = 漫长的审问
under interrogation = 在审问中
subject to interrogation = 受到审问


词源:
interrogate (v.)  late 14c., from Latin interrogatus, past participle of interrogare "to question," from inter- "between, among" + rogare "to ask."  The meaning "to question officially" is from 1590s.
