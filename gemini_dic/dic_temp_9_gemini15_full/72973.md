fooled:/fʊld/| vt.|愚弄，欺骗|To trick or deceive someone.  To make someone believe something that is not true.|例句：He fooled me into believing his story. 他骗我信了他的话。

例句：The magician fooled the audience with his clever tricks. 魔术师用他精巧的戏法愚弄了观众。


近义词：deceived, tricked, cheated, misled|反义词：enlightened, informed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fools everyone with his charm. 他用他的魅力愚弄每个人。
She fools around a lot. 她经常胡闹。


一般过去时:
I was fooled by his disguise. 我被他的伪装骗了。
They fooled the security guards. 他们骗过了保安。


现在完成时:
I have been fooled before. 我以前被骗过。
He has fooled us all. 他骗了我们所有人。


过去完成时:
I had been fooled many times before. 我以前被骗过很多次。
She had fooled her parents for years. 她欺骗了她父母多年。


将来时:
I will not be fooled again. 我不会再被骗了。
They will fool you if you are not careful. 如果你不小心，他们会欺骗你。


虚拟语气:
If I had known better, I wouldn't have been fooled. 如果我知道得更好，我就不会被骗了。
I wish I hadn't been fooled by him. 我真希望我没有被他骗。


被动语态:

The audience was fooled by the illusion. 观众被幻觉所迷惑。

否定句:
