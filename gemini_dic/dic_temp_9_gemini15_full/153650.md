pentosan:/ˈpɛntəsæn/| n. |戊聚糖|A type of polysaccharide composed of pentose sugars (five-carbon sugars).  It's often found in plants and some microorganisms, and is known for its various properties, including its use in some medical applications.|例句：Pentosans are a complex group of polysaccharides found in various plant cell walls. 戊聚糖是一类复杂的聚糖，存在于各种植物细胞壁中。|近义词：hemicellulose (部分近义词，因为戊聚糖是半纤维素的一种)|反义词：无|名词

词源:
The word "pentosan" is derived from "pentose" + "-an," a suffix used to form names of polysaccharides. "Pentose" itself comes from "pent-" (five) + "-ose" (sugar), referring to the five-carbon sugar units that make up the molecule.
