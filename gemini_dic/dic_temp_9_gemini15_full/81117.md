grammaticalness:/ˌɡræˈmætɪkəlnəs/ | n. | 语法性，合乎语法 | The degree to which a sentence or utterance conforms to the rules of grammar.  It refers to whether a sentence is well-formed and acceptable according to the grammatical rules of a language.  Grammaticality is distinct from acceptability, which considers factors beyond mere grammatical correctness, such as meaningfulness and naturalness. | The sentence "Colorless green ideas sleep furiously" is grammatically correct, but its meaning is nonsensical. | well-formedness, acceptability | ungrammaticality
