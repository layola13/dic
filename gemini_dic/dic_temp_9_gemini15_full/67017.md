expirers:/ɪkˈspaɪrərz/ | n. |到期者，失效者 | Those who or that which expires.  People or things that reach the end of their validity period.  | Example: The expirers of the contract are required to renew it. 合同到期者需要续约。  The list of expirers included several important medications. 到期清单中包含几种重要的药物。 |近义词: those whose validity has ended, those that have lapsed |反义词: renewers, those still valid


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The policy expires next month. = 保险单下个月到期。
The milk expires tomorrow. = 牛奶明天过期。


一般过去时:
The warranty expired last year. = 保修期去年到期了。
My passport expired six months ago. = 我的护照六个月前过期了。


现在完成时:
My subscription has expired. = 我的订阅已经过期了。
The offer has expired. = 促销活动已经结束了。


过去完成时:
The visa had expired before he arrived. = 他到达之前签证就已经过期了。
The contract had expired months ago. = 合同几个月前就已经过期了。


将来时:
The license will expire in a year. = 执照一年后到期。
His membership will expire next month. = 他的会员资格下个月到期。


虚拟语气:
If the contract had not expired, we would have renewed it. = 如果合同没有过期，我们会续约的。
If the milk hadn't expired, we could have used it. = 如果牛奶没过期，我们就可以用了。


被动语态:
The document was expired. = 文件已过期。
The products were expired and had to be discarded. = 产品已过期，必须丢弃。


疑问句:
Has the license expired? = 执照过期了吗？
When does the contract expire? = 合同什么时候到期？


否定句:
The agreement has not expired yet. = 协议还没有过期。
The medication did not expire until next year. = 药物直到明年才过期。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly using "expirers" as it is a noun referring to things that expire, rather than a verb or adjective that would readily form phrases.  Instead, phrases relate to the *process* of expiring:

expiry date: 到期日
date of expiry: 到期日
before expiry: 到期前
upon expiry: 到期时
after expiry: 到期后
