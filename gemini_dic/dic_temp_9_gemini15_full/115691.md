marginalises:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəlaɪz/ | vt. |边缘化；排斥；使…处于社会边缘 | To treat a person, group, or concept as insignificant or peripheral.  |The government's policies marginalized the poor and the elderly. 政府的政策使穷人和老年人边缘化。|  exclude, sideline, ostracize | integrate, include, empower |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They marginalize minority groups. = 他们边缘化少数群体。
The company marginalizes its employees' opinions. = 公司忽视员工的意见。


一般过去时:
The media marginalized the issue. = 媒体淡化了这个问题。
Society marginalized her because of her disability. = 社会因为她的残疾而排斥她。


现在完成时:
This policy has marginalized many talented individuals. =这项政策已经边缘化了许多有才华的人。
Their actions have marginalized the voices of the people. = 他们的行为使人民的声音被边缘化。


过去完成时:
The company had marginalized its research department before the crisis. = 公司在危机之前就已经边缘化了其研究部门。
They had marginalized her ideas for years. = 他们多年来一直忽视她的想法。


将来时:
This new law will marginalize small businesses. = 这项新法将使小型企业边缘化。
Their actions will further marginalize the vulnerable populations. = 他们的行动将进一步边缘化弱势群体。


虚拟语气:
If they continued this policy, they would marginalize the entire community. = 如果他们继续这项政策，他们将边缘化整个社区。
I wish they wouldn't marginalize the concerns of the workers. = 我希望他们不要忽视工人们的担忧。


被动语态:
The community was marginalized by the government's policies. = 社区因政府的政策而被边缘化。
His ideas were marginalized by the dominant group. = 他的想法被统治阶级边缘化了。


疑问句:
Are women still marginalized in this profession? = 在这个行业中，女性是否仍然处于边缘地位？
How can we prevent marginalized groups from being further disadvantaged? = 我们如何才能防止边缘化群体进一步受损？


否定句:
The company did not marginalize its employees' suggestions. = 公司并没有忽视员工的建议。
They refused to marginalize the voices of dissent. = 他们拒绝忽视异议的声音。


PHRASE->

economically marginalized = 经济边缘化

词源:
The word "marginalise" comes from the noun "margin," meaning the edge or border of something.  The "-alise" suffix is a verb-forming suffix, suggesting the act of making something marginal or placing it on the margin.  The ultimate root is likely Latin *margo, meaning "edge, boundary."
