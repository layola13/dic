quasi-medical:/ˈkwɑːziˈmɛdɪkəl/ | adj. |似医疗的，准医疗的 | seeming to be or be related to medicine but not fully or officially so.  This term describes something that resembles medical practice or treatment but lacks the full authority, credentials, or rigor of established medicine.  It often refers to practices or products that claim health benefits but haven't undergone rigorous scientific testing or approval. | Example:  The advertised "quasi-medical" device promised to cure back pain, but had no scientific evidence to support its claims.  =  广告中宣传的“似医疗的”设备承诺能治愈背痛，但没有科学证据支持其说法。| 近义词：paramedical, pseudo-medical | 反义词：medical, orthodox medical
