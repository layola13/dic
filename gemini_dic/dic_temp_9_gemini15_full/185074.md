reticent: /ˈrɛtɪsənt/ | adj. | 沉默寡言的；不爱说话的；含蓄的 |  Reluctant or unwilling to talk about something; reserved.  | He was reticent about his past. 他对自己的过去讳莫如深。|  reserved, taciturn, uncommunicative, quiet |  outspoken, garrulous, loquacious


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is reticent about his feelings. = 他对自己的感情很沉默。
She is reticent in social situations. = 她在社交场合很沉默寡言。

一般过去时:
He was reticent during the interrogation. = 在审讯期间，他沉默寡言。
She was reticent to share her personal information. = 她不愿分享她的个人信息。

现在完成时:
He has been reticent since the accident. = 自从事故发生后，他就一直沉默寡言。
She has remained reticent about the details of the case. = 她对案件的细节一直保持沉默。

过去完成时:
He had been reticent throughout the entire meeting. = 他在整个会议期间都一直沉默寡言。
She had remained reticent about her plans for the future. = 她对未来的计划一直保持沉默。

将来时:
He will be reticent to answer any questions. = 他将不愿回答任何问题。
She will likely remain reticent on the subject. = 她很可能会对这个话题保持沉默。

虚拟语气:
If he were less reticent, we would know more. = 如果他不那么沉默寡言，我们会知道更多。
I wish she weren't so reticent about her problems. = 我希望她不要对自己的问题如此沉默。

被动语态:
His reticence was noted by everyone. = 大家都注意到了他的沉默寡言。
The information was withheld, seemingly due to reticence on the part of the witness. = 信息被隐瞒了，这似乎是由于证人的沉默寡言。

疑问句:
Why is he so reticent today? = 他今天为什么这么沉默寡言？
Is she always so reticent? = 她总是这么沉默寡言吗？

否定句:
He was not reticent to express his opinions. = 他毫不犹豫地表达了自己的意见。
She was not at all reticent in describing the events. = 她描述事件时一点也不含糊。
