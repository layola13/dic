emboli: /ˈɛmbəlaɪ/ | n. | 栓塞（复数）; 栓子（复数） |  A mass of material (such as a blood clot, air bubble, or piece of fat) that travels through the bloodstream and lodges in a blood vessel, obstructing blood flow.  Emboli can be life-threatening, depending on their size and location. |

例句：

Pulmonary emboli are a serious complication of deep vein thrombosis. 肺栓塞是深静脉血栓形成的严重并发症。

Multiple emboli were found lodged in the coronary arteries. 多个栓子发现堵塞在冠状动脉中。


近义词：thrombus (单数栓子), occlusion

反义词：patent (畅通的), unobstructed


词性：名词 (复数)

词源:

From the New Latin *embolus, from Greek ἔμβολος (émbolos) "plug, piston, bolt."  The medical term refers to something that plugs or obstructs a vessel.
