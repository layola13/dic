deafen:/ˈdɛfən/| vt.|使聋; 使震聋; 使震惊|to make someone unable to hear, either temporarily or permanently, because of a very loud noise.  It can also figuratively mean to overwhelm or shock someone.|The loud music deafened me.  震耳欲聋的音乐让我听不见了。
The news deafened her with its shocking implications. 这个消息及其令人震惊的含义让她震惊不已。|stun, overwhelm|hear, be aware of|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud music deafens me. = 吵闹的音乐使我耳聋。
This constant noise deafens the workers. = 持续不断的噪音使工人们耳聋。

一般过去时:
The explosion deafened everyone nearby. = 爆炸使附近的人都耳聋了。
The thunder deafened the children. = 雷声震聋了孩子们。

现在完成时:
Years of working in a noisy factory have deafened him. = 多年在嘈杂的工厂工作使他耳聋了。
The continuous loud music has deafened my hearing. = 持续不断的吵闹音乐已经损伤了我的听力。

过去完成时:
He had been deafened by a previous explosion. = 他曾被之前的爆炸震聋了。
She had already been deafened before the concert even started. = 甚至在音乐会开始之前，她就已经被震聋了。

将来时:
The fireworks will deafen the neighborhood. = 烟花会震聋整个街区。
That rock concert will probably deafen you. = 那场摇滚音乐会可能会让你耳聋。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
