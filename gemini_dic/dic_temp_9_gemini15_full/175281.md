rainfalls:/ˈreɪnfɔːlz/ | n. |降雨，降水 |Rainfall refers to the amount of rain that falls in a particular area over a specific period. It is often measured in millimeters or inches.  |例句：The rainfalls have been heavy this year. 今年的降雨量很大。|例句：We experienced several days of continuous rainfalls last week. 上周我们经历了几天持续的降雨。|近义词：precipitation, showers, downpour |反义词：drought


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rainfall varies throughout the year. = 全年的降雨量各不相同。
Rainfall is essential for agriculture. = 降雨对农业至关重要。

一般过去时:
Heavy rainfalls caused flooding in the town. = 大雨导致该镇发生洪水。
The rainfall last month was significantly lower than average. = 上个月的降雨量明显低于平均水平。

现在完成时:
We have had unusually high rainfalls this season. = 这个季节我们经历了异常高的降雨量。
The total rainfall this year has exceeded expectations. = 今年的总降雨量超过了预期。

过去完成时:

They had not anticipated such heavy rainfalls. = 他们没有预料到会有这么大的降雨。

将来时:
Meteorologists predict heavy rainfalls next week. = 气象学家预测下周会有大雨。
The rainfall forecast for tomorrow is uncertain. = 明天的降雨预报不确定。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

The amount of rainfall is measured using a rain gauge. = 降雨量是用雨量计测量的。

疑问句:

否定句:
