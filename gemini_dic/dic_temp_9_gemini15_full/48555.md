defamed:/dɪˈfeɪmd/| vt.|诽谤；中伤；败坏名誉 | to harm someone's reputation by saying or writing bad things about them that are not true.  |例句：He was defamed by a rival company. 他被一家竞争公司诽谤了。|例句：The newspaper article defamed the politician's character. 这篇报纸文章败坏了这位政治家的名誉。|近义词：slander, libel, vilify, malign |反义词：praise, commend, extol


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He defames his opponents regularly. = 他经常诽谤他的对手。
The tabloid newspaper defames celebrities. = 这家小报诽谤名人。

一般过去时:
She defamed him in a public speech. = 她在公开演讲中诽谤了他。
The article defamed the company's reputation. = 这篇文章败坏了公司的声誉。

现在完成时:
They have defamed him repeatedly in the media. = 他们在媒体上多次诽谤他。
The blogger has defamed several public figures. = 该博主已诽谤了几位公众人物。

过去完成时:
The court found that he had defamed the witness. = 法院认定他诽谤了证人。
She realized that she had defamed her friend unintentionally. = 她意识到自己无意中诽谤了她的朋友。

将来时:
They will defame him again if given the chance. = 如果有机会，他们还会再次诽谤他。
The newspaper is likely to defame the candidate. = 这家报纸可能会诽谤这位候选人。

虚拟语气:
If he had not defamed her, she would not have sued him. = 如果他没有诽谤她，她就不会起诉他。
I wish they hadn't defamed my family. = 我希望他们没有诽谤我的家人。

被动语态:
He was defamed in the press. = 他在媒体上受到了诽谤。
The victim was defamed by false accusations. = 受害者因虚假指控而名誉受损。

疑问句:
Was he defamed by the journalist? = 他被记者诽谤了吗？
Have they been defamed by this false report? = 他们被这份虚假报告诽谤了吗？

否定句:
They did not defame him, despite his claims. = 尽管他声称，他们并没有诽谤他。
The company denies that it defamed its competitor. = 该公司否认诽谤其竞争对手。
