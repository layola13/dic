signified:/ˈsɪɡnɪfaɪd/ | v. | 意味；表示；象征 | To be an indication of; to represent; to mean.  | The dark clouds signified an approaching storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨即将来临。 The symbol signified peace. 这个符号象征和平。| indicated, represented, symbolized | insignificant |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The symbol signifies peace. = 这个符号象征和平。
This gesture signifies agreement. = 这个手势表示同意。


一般过去时:
The red light signified danger. = 红灯表示危险。
His silence signified his disapproval. = 他的沉默表示他的反对。


现在完成时:
The results have signified a major breakthrough. = 结果表明取得了重大突破。
Her actions have signified her commitment. = 她的行为表明了她的承诺。


过去完成时:
The old building had signified prosperity in the past. = 这座旧建筑过去象征着繁荣。
The cracked pavement had signified years of neglect. = 破裂的人行道表明多年来无人照管。


将来时:
The new law will signify a change in policy. = 新法将意味着政策的改变。
Rising sea levels will signify a worsening climate crisis. = 海平面上升将预示着气候危机日益恶化。


虚拟语气:

If the symbol signified hope, it would offer comfort. = 如果这个符号象征希望，它就能带来安慰。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
The painting did not signify anything specific. = 这幅画并没有表示任何具体的东西。
His words did not signify his true feelings. = 他的话语并没有表达他真实的想法。
