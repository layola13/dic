exploding:/ɪkˈspləʊdɪŋ/ | v. | 爆炸；激增；迅速发展 |  The verb "exploding" describes something bursting or breaking apart violently, often with a loud noise. It can also refer to a rapid increase or expansion. | 例句：The volcano exploded, sending ash and rocks into the air.  火山爆发，喷出火山灰和岩石。  The popularity of the new game exploded overnight.  这款新游戏的受欢迎程度一夜之间激增。 | 近义词：bursting, erupting, detonating, booming | 反义词：imploding, collapsing, shrinking


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bomb explodes every minute. = 炸弹每分钟爆炸一次。
The business is exploding with new clients. = 公司正在迅速发展壮大，新客户不断增加。

一般过去时:
The pressure cooker exploded. = 高压锅爆炸了。

现在完成时:
Three bombs have exploded in the city this week. = 本周城市里已经爆炸了三枚炸弹。
The popularity of that song has exploded across the globe. = 那首歌的流行程度席卷全球。


过去完成时:
The building had exploded before the firefighters arrived. = 消防员到达之前，大楼已经爆炸了。
The demand for the product had exploded before the company could meet it. = 在公司能够满足需求之前，对产品的需求就已经激增了。


将来时:
The firework will explode in a few seconds. = 烟花几秒钟后就会爆炸。
The company expects its profits to explode next quarter. = 公司预计下个季度的利润将大幅增长。


虚拟语气:

If the market had exploded earlier, we could have made more money. = 如果市场早些时候就出现爆炸式增长，我们就能赚更多钱。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
