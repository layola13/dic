elapses:/ɪˈlæps/| vi.|过去式elapsed, 过去分词elapsed|消逝；过去；流逝|To pass or go by (of time).|例句：Five years elapsed before we met again.  五年后我们才再次相遇。

例句：Much time elapsed before the rescue team arrived.  救援队到达之前过去了很长时间。


近义词：pass, go by, lapse


反义词：begin, start, commence

USAGE->
一般现在时:
Time elapses slowly when you're bored. = 当你无聊的时候，时间过得很慢。
The year elapses quickly. = 一年过得很快。


一般过去时:
Several hours elapsed before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前过去了几个小时。
A long time elapsed before he responded. = 他过了很久才回应。


现在完成时:
Many years have elapsed since we last saw each other. = 自从我们上次见面以来，已经过去了很多年。
Considerable time has elapsed since the accident. = 自事故发生以来已经过去了相当长的时间。


过去完成时:
Several days had elapsed before the news reached us. = 消息传到我们这里之前已经过去了几天。
A long period had elapsed since the last eruption. = 自上次喷发以来已经过去了很长一段时间。


将来时:
Another ten years will elapse before the project is completed. = 项目完成还需要十年时间。
A few more months will elapse before the decision is made. = 在做出决定之前还需要几个月的时间。


虚拟语气:
If a month elapsed without incident, they would celebrate. = 如果一个月平安无事地过去，他们就会庆祝。
If only a few more years had elapsed, they might have accomplished more. = 如果再多过几年，他们可能会有更多成就。


被动语态:
(The verb "elapse" is intransitive and cannot be used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
How much time elapsed before you noticed the problem? = 你多久才注意到这个问题？
How many years have elapsed since the war ended? = 自战争结束以来已经过去了多少年？


否定句:
Little time elapsed before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前几乎没有时间。
No time elapsed before the alarm went off. = 警报响起之前没有时间过去。


PHRASE->
let time elapse = 让时间过去
elapse into = 渐渐变成
time elapsed = 过去的时间
elapse quietly = 平静地过去
a period elapsed = 一段时间过去
