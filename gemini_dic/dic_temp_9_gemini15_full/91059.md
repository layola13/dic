hustler:/ˈhʌslər/| n. |骗子；努力工作的人；活跃的人|A hustler is someone who is quick-witted and resourceful, often in a slightly dubious or illegal way.  It can also refer to someone who works hard and persistently to achieve success. |例句：He was a smooth-talking hustler who always seemed to find a way to make money. 他是一个油嘴滑舌的骗子，总能找到赚钱的方法。

He's a hustler who built his empire from nothing. 他是一个白手起家的奋斗者。|近义词：con man, swindler, go-getter, grinder, entrepreneur|反义词：idler, loafer, slacker|名词

一般现在时:
The hustler scams tourists. = 骗子诈骗游客。
He hustles every day to make a living. = 他每天努力工作以谋生。

一般过去时:
That hustler conned me out of my money. = 那个骗子骗走了我的钱。
She hustled hard to get into the top universities. = 她努力学习，进入顶尖大学。

现在完成时:
He has hustled his way to the top. = 他通过努力奋斗达到了顶峰。
She has hustled for years to build her business. = 她为了发展自己的事业奋斗了很多年。

过去完成时:
The police had already caught the hustler before he could escape. = 警察在他逃跑之前就已经抓住了骗子。
He had hustled all day, so he was exhausted. = 他辛勤工作了一整天，所以筋疲力尽。

将来时:
He will hustle to finish the project on time. = 他将努力按时完成项目。
She will hustle to get a better job. = 她将努力争取一份更好的工作。

虚拟语气:
If he were a hustler, he would have made more money. = 如果他是一个骗子，他会赚更多钱。
I wish I had hustled harder in my younger years. = 我希望我年轻时更努力一些。

被动语态:

The project was completed because he hustled. = 项目得以完成是因为他努力工作。

疑问句:

否定句:
