isostemony:/ˌaɪsoʊˈstɛməni/ | n. | 同雄蕊数 |  Isostemony refers to the condition in flowers where the number of stamens (male reproductive organs) is equal to the number of petals or sepals.  This is a characteristic of many plant families.  | Example:  The lily exhibits isostemony, having six stamens and six tepals (petals and sepals). 百合花表现出同雄蕊数，有六个雄蕊和六个花被片（花瓣和萼片）。| 近义词：isomerous (in the context of floral parts) | 反义词：anisostemony (unequal number of stamens and petals/sepals) |  词性：名词
