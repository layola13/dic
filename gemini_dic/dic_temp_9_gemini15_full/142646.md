ostracizes:/ˈɒstrəsaɪzɪz/ | vt. |排斥，放逐，冷落 | To exclude someone from a society or group.  |例句：He was ostracized by his colleagues after he revealed the company's secrets. 他泄露了公司秘密后被同事们排斥了。 |例句：The new student felt ostracized because she didn't speak the local language. 新生因为不会说当地语言而感到被排斥。|近义词：exclude, shun, boycott, isolate |反义词：include, welcome, accept |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ostracizes anyone who disagrees with him. = 他排斥任何与他意见相左的人。
The community ostracizes those who break the rules. = 社区排斥那些违反规则的人。


一般过去时:
They ostracized him for his unconventional views. = 他们因为他非传统的观点而排斥他。
The villagers ostracized the family after the scandal. = 丑闻发生后，村民们排斥了那个家庭。


现在完成时:
She has been ostracized by her friends since the argument. = 自从那次争吵后，她一直被朋友们排斥。
The group has ostracized him for his betrayal. = 因为他的背叛，小组已经排斥了他。


过去完成时:
They had ostracized him long before the incident. = 在事件发生很久以前，他们就已经排斥他了。
The community had ostracized her family for generations. = 社区世世代代都排斥她的家族。


将来时:
They will ostracize him if he continues to behave badly. = 如果他继续表现不好，他们将会排斥他。
The school will ostracize students who cheat on exams. = 学校将会排斥考试作弊的学生。


虚拟语气:
If he were to speak out, he would be ostracized. = 如果他敢于直言，他将会被排斥。
I wish they hadn't ostracized her. = 我希望他们没有排斥她。


被动语态:
He was ostracized for his beliefs. = 他因为他的信仰而被排斥。
She felt ostracized by the group. = 她感到被小组排斥。


疑问句:
Was he ostracized because of his religion? = 他是因为宗教信仰而被排斥的吗？
Are they ostracizing him unfairly? = 他们不公平地排斥他吗？


否定句:
They did not ostracize him; they merely ignored him. = 他们并没有排斥他；他们只是忽视了他。
She wasn't ostracized; she chose to leave the group. = 她并没有被排斥；她选择离开小组。


PHRASE->
be ostracized from = 被逐出...

subject to ostracism = 受到排斥
risk ostracism = 冒被排斥的风险
cause ostracism = 导致排斥


词源:
From Ancient Greek ὀστρακισμός (ostrakismos), literally "potsherd," referring to the ancient Athenian practice of ostracism, where citizens could vote to exile a person by writing their name on a broken piece of pottery (ὀστρακον, ostrakon).
