narcissism:/ˈnɑːsɪsɪzəm/ | n. |自恋；自我陶醉 |  Narcissism is excessive self-admiration and a preoccupation with oneself and one's attributes.  It's characterized by an inflated sense of self-importance, a need for admiration, and a lack of empathy.  Severe narcissism can be a personality disorder. | 例句：His narcissism prevented him from seeing the impact of his actions on others. = 他的自恋使他无法看到他的行为对别人的影响。

His constant need for praise is a clear sign of narcissism. = 他不断需要赞扬，这清楚地表明了他自恋。


近义词：self-obsession, egotism, vanity, conceit | 反义词：humility, modesty, selflessness


词源:  The word "narcissism" comes from the Greek myth of Narcissus, a hunter who fell in love with his own reflection in a pool of water and eventually died because he could not tear himself away.  The myth represents the obsessive fascination with oneself that defines the condition.
