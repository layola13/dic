reviling:/rɪˈvaɪlɪŋ/| v.|辱骂；斥责；痛斥 |  To criticize or abuse (someone or something) harshly.  |例句：The crowd reviled the politician for his unpopular policies.  人群因他不受欢迎的政策而辱骂这位政治家。

例句：He spent the entire sermon reviling the sins of the congregation. 他在整个布道中都在痛斥会众的罪恶。


近义词：denounce, condemn, abuse, vilify, lambaste

反义词：praise, commend, applaud, flatter


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They revile him daily for his actions. = 他们每天都因他的行为而辱骂他。
The protestors revile the government's decision. = 抗议者痛斥政府的决定。


一般过去时:
She reviled her brother for his carelessness. = 她因她弟弟的粗心大意而斥责他。
The audience reviled the actor's poor performance. = 观众辱骂这位演员糟糕的表演。


现在完成时:
They have reviled him for years. = 他们多年来一直辱骂他。
The media has reviled the company's unethical practices. = 媒体一直谴责这家公司的违反道德的行为。


过去完成时:
The villagers had reviled the stranger before he even spoke. = 村民们在他开口说话之前就已经辱骂他了。
They had reviled the dictator for decades before he fell from power. = 在独裁者垮台之前，他们几十年来一直在辱骂他。


将来时:
They will revile him for his lies. = 他们将因他的谎言而辱骂他。
The public will revile the company's new policy. = 公众将痛斥公司的这项新政策。


虚拟语气:
If he knew what he did, they would revile him even more. = 如果他知道自己做了什么，他们会更加辱骂他。
I wish they wouldn't revile him so much. = 我希望他们不要那么辱骂他。


被动语态:
He was reviled by the public for his comments. = 他因自己的言论而受到公众的辱骂。
The new law was reviled by many citizens. = 这项新法律受到许多公民的痛斥。


疑问句:
Why do they revile him so fiercely? = 为什么他们如此激烈地辱骂他？
Will the people revile the decision of the court? = 人们会辱骂法院的决定吗？


否定句:
They did not revile him openly. = 他们没有公开辱骂他。
The crowd did not revile the speaker, instead they listened attentively. = 人群没有辱骂演讲者，而是专心致志地倾听。


PHRASE->
publicly revile = 公开辱骂
fiercely revile = 猛烈辱骂
constantly revile = 不断辱骂
bitterly revile = 痛苦地辱骂
secretly revile = 暗中辱骂


词源:
From Old French *reviller, from re- + viler "to be vile, worthless."  Ultimately from the Latin *vilis "cheap, worthless."
