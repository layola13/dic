janu:/ˈdʒænjuː/| n. |一月（古罗马月份名）|Janu is an ancient Roman name for the month of January, derived from Janus, the two-faced Roman god of beginnings, gates, transitions, time, duality, doorways, passages, frames, and endings. It's a less common variant of January.  |The month of Janu was a time of new beginnings in ancient Rome.|近义词：January|反义词：无
