grudging:/ˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ/| adj.|勉强同意的；不情愿的；吝啬的 |Reluctant or unwilling; given or done unwillingly.  It often implies resentment or a feeling of being forced. |例句：He gave a grudging apology. 他勉强地道歉了。

例句：She offered a grudging admission of her mistake. 她勉强承认了自己的错误。


近义词：reluctant, unwilling, reluctant, hesitant, unenthusiastic, stingy, miserly |反义词：willing, eager, generous, enthusiastic


词源:  The word comes from the Old English word "grūdian," meaning to murmur or grumble. This reflects the underlying feeling of resentment often associated with grudging actions or attitudes.  The -ing suffix transforms the verb into an adjective.


用法说明:

一般现在时:
He gives grudging praise to his competitor. 他勉强称赞了他的竞争对手。
They offer grudging support to the new policy. 他们勉强支持这项新政策。


一般过去时:
She gave a grudging consent to the plan. 她勉强同意了这个计划。
He offered a grudging handshake. 他不情愿地握了握手。


现在完成时:
He has given only grudging cooperation to the investigation. 他对调查只提供了勉强配合。
They have shown grudging respect for their opponent’s skills. 他们对对手的技能表现出勉强尊重。


过去完成时:
She had given a grudging acceptance of his explanation. 她勉强接受了他的解释。
They had offered grudging apologies for their behavior.  他们对自己的行为勉强道歉了。


将来时:
He will give only grudging approval to the project. 他只会勉强批准这个项目。
They will offer grudging assistance. 他们将勉强提供援助。


虚拟语气:
If he were to give a compliment, it would be grudging. 如果他要赞美的话，也会是勉强的。
I wish he would give a less grudging response. 我希望他能给出不那么勉强的回应。


被动语态:
His efforts were grudgingly acknowledged. 他的努力勉强得到了承认。
The new rules were grudgingly accepted. 新规则勉强被接受了。


疑问句:
Is his support for the project entirely grudging? 他对这个项目的支持完全是勉强的吗？
Was her apology truly grudging or sincere? 她的道歉是真的勉强还是真诚的？


否定句:
His support wasn't grudging at all; he was genuinely enthusiastic. 他的支持一点也不勉强；他是真心的热情。
Her agreement wasn't grudging; she was happy to help. 她的同意并非勉强；她乐于帮忙。
