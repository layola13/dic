milkshake:/ˈmɪlkʃeɪk/| n. |奶昔|A sweet, cold drink made from milk, ice cream, and often other flavorings.  It's a popular dessert or snack, especially among children.| 例句：I ordered a strawberry milkshake at the diner. 我在小餐馆点了一杯草莓奶昔。

例句：He blended the ingredients to make a delicious chocolate milkshake. 他把配料混合在一起做了一杯美味的巧克力奶昔。


近义词：smoothie (if made with fruit and less ice cream), shake (a more general term)

反义词：None (It's difficult to find a true antonym, as it depends on what aspect you're contrasting)

词性：名词

词源:  The word "milkshake" is a compound word combining "milk" and "shake."  "Shake" in this context refers to the act of mixing or blending ingredients, likely evolving from the action of shaking a container to mix the components before the advent of electric blenders. The word's origins are thus straightforward and reflect its preparation method.
