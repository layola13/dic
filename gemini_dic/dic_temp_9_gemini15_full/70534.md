filibustering: /ˈfɪlɪbʌstərɪŋ/ | n. | 冗长演说；阻挠议事；故意拖延 | The act of obstructing legislation by making long speeches.  It is a tactic used in legislative bodies to delay or prevent a vote on a bill. |例句：The senator engaged in a filibuster to prevent the bill from being passed.  参议员进行冗长演说以阻止法案通过。

The opposition party used a filibuster to delay the vote. 反对党利用冗长演说来拖延投票。


近义词：obstruction, delay, procrastination, stall | 反义词：expedition, efficiency, swiftness


词源:  The word "filibustering" ultimately derives from the Dutch word "vrijbuiter," meaning "freebooter" or "pirate."  In the 19th century, it was used to describe irregular warfare and adventurers.  Its application to legislative tactics reflects the similar nature of obstructing progress through unconventional means.
