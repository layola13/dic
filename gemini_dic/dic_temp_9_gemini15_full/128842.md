nigiri-zushi:/niˈɡɪri ˈzuːʃi/| n. |握寿司|Hand-pressed sushi, a type of sushi where vinegared rice is formed into an oblong shape and topped with a piece of seafood, typically raw fish.  It's characterized by its simple yet elegant presentation and the fresh, high-quality ingredients used.|  I ordered a plate of nigiri-zushi, including tuna, salmon, and shrimp. 我点了一盘握寿司，包括金枪鱼、三文鱼和虾。  I prefer nigiri-zushi to maki-zushi because I enjoy the clean flavor of the fish. 我更喜欢握寿司而不是卷寿司，因为我喜欢鱼的清香味道。|sashimi, sushi| (no direct antonym, but perhaps "cooked sushi" could be considered a contrasting term)|名词
