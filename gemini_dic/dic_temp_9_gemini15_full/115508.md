many-a-time:/ˈmɛni ə taɪm/| adv.|屡次；多次；好几次|Many-a-time is an archaic adverbial phrase meaning "many times" or "on many occasions".  It emphasizes the frequency of an event.  The phrase uses the archaic "many a" construction, where "many a" modifies a singular noun, but the verb agrees with the singular noun.  It's more common in literature than in everyday speech.|例句：Many-a-time have I walked this path.  我多次走过这条路。

Many-a-time I wished I had studied harder. 我多次希望我学习更努力。|近义词：often, frequently, repeatedly, numerous times|反义词：rarely, seldom, infrequently, once
