caesarean:/sɪˈzəriən/| n. adj.|剖腹产；凯撒的|The term "caesarean section" refers to a surgical procedure in which a baby is delivered through an incision in the mother's abdomen and uterus.  The term originates from the Roman emperor Julius Caesar, who was supposedly born this way.  However, the accuracy of this is debated. |例句：She had a caesarean section after experiencing complications during labor. 她分娩时出现并发症，做了剖腹产。|例句：The baby was delivered via caesarean.  这个婴儿是通过剖腹产出生的。|近义词：surgical delivery, abdominal delivery|反义词：vaginal delivery, natural childbirth|名词，形容词
