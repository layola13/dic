culmination:/ˌkʌlmiˈneɪʃən/| n. |顶点；高潮；结果|The highest or climactic point of something, especially as attained after a long time.|The culmination of years of research resulted in a groundbreaking discovery.  多年研究的最终成果是一项突破性发现。|The concert was the culmination of a successful tour.  这场音乐会是成功巡演的高潮。|climax, apex, peak, summit|anticlimax, nadir


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The culmination of their efforts is a successful project. = 他们努力的最终成果是一个成功的项目。
The event marks the culmination of a long process. = 此事件标志着一个漫长过程的结束。


一般过去时:
The film's climax was the culmination of a suspenseful plot. = 电影的高潮是悬念情节的最终结果。
The signing of the treaty was the culmination of years of negotiation. = 条约的签署是多年谈判的最终结果。


现在完成时:
The exhibition has been the culmination of months of hard work. = 展览是几个月辛勤工作的最终成果。
Her promotion has marked the culmination of her career. = 她的晋升标志着她职业生涯的顶峰。


过去完成时:
By then, the culmination of their efforts had become apparent. = 到那时，他们努力的最终结果已变得显而易见。
The award was the culmination of what had been a challenging year. = 获奖是充满挑战的一年的最终结果。


将来时:
The launch of the new product will be the culmination of years of development. = 新产品的发布将是多年发展的最终结果。
The completion of the project will be the culmination of our hard work. = 项目的完成将是我们辛勤工作的最终结果。


虚拟语气:
If only it were the culmination of our troubles! = 但愿这是我们麻烦的结束！
