英文解析:Glamor refers to an attractive or exciting quality that makes something or someone seem very interesting and desirable. It often involves elements of beauty, excitement, and luxury.  The word usually suggests a somewhat idealized or superficial attractiveness.


例句:

The city at night held a certain glamor. 夜晚的城市有一种特殊的魅力。

Her glamorous appearance captivated everyone in the room. 她迷人的外表吸引了房间里所有人的目光。


近义词：

allure, fascination, charm, enchantment, splendor, magnificence


反义词：

dullness, drabness, ordinariness, plainness, banality


词源:

The word "glamor" originates from the Scottish word "grammar," which referred to magic or enchantment. This was later influenced by the word "glamour," meaning a magical charm or illusion. Over time, the spelling shifted, and the meaning evolved to refer to the attractive or exciting quality of something or someone.  The association with magic and illusion hints at the somewhat idealized nature of glamor.
