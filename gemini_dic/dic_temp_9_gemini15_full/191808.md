scissure: /ˈsɪʒər/ | n. | 裂缝；裂隙；[解剖] 裂 | A natural or artificial crack, split, or fissure, especially in rock or the earth.  A narrow opening or groove; a cleft.  In anatomy, a deep groove or fissure, such as the fissure of Rolando (central sulcus) in the brain. |

例句：

The earthquake caused a deep scissure in the earth. = 地震在地面上造成了一条深深的裂缝。

The surgeon carefully examined the scissure in the patient's skull. = 外科医生仔细检查了病人颅骨上的裂缝。


近义词：crack, cleft, fissure, split, rift

反义词：seam, joint (in the sense of a solid, unbroken connection)


词源:

From Middle French *cisure, from Latin *cissura, from *cīdere "to cut".  The word shares etymological roots with words like "scissors" and "incision".
