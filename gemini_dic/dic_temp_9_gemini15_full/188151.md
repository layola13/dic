rousting: /ˈraʊstɪŋ/ | v. | 驱赶；吵醒；猛烈摇晃 | The act of forcibly waking someone up or driving someone out of a place.  It can also refer to the act of violently shaking something. |


英文解析:  To rouse someone means to wake them up, often forcefully or abruptly.  It can also mean to drive someone out of a place, usually forcefully.  The word implies a degree of suddenness and perhaps even harshness.

例句:

The early morning sun rousting the sleepy campers from their tents. =清晨的阳光把睡梦中的露营者从帐篷里赶了出来。

The police were rousting the protestors from the occupied building. =警察正在把抗议者从被占领的建筑物里驱赶出去。


近义词: awakening, stirring, dislodging, ejecting, shaking

反义词: calming, soothing, lulling


词性: 动词
