deputizing: /ˈdɛpjʊtaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 代理；临时代理；授权 | Acting or serving as a deputy; temporarily filling the position of someone else.  |  例句：The manager is deputizing for the CEO while she is on vacation. =经理在首席执行官休假期间代理她的职务。

例句：During the emergency, the junior officer was deputizing for the captain. = 在紧急情况下，下级军官代理了船长。


近义词：substituting, acting for, standing in for


反义词：leading, originating


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is deputizing for the absent teacher. = 他正在代理缺席的老师。
She deputizes for her boss frequently. = 她经常代理她的老板。


一般过去时:
The vice president deputized for the president at the meeting. = 副总裁在会议上代理了总统。
He deputized for his colleague while she was ill. = 他在他同事生病时代理了她。


现在完成时:
She has deputized for him on several occasions. = 她已经几次代理他了。
He has been deputizing for the manager all week. = 他整个星期都在代理经理。


过去完成时:
They had deputized for the team leader before the project started. = 项目开始前他们已经代理了团队领导。
She had deputized for her sister at the previous event. = 在之前的活动中，她已经代理了她的姐姐。


将来时:
He will be deputizing for the mayor next week. = 他下周将代理市长。
She will deputize for the director during his absence. = 他不在的时候她将代理主任。


虚拟语气:
If he were to be absent, someone would have to deputize for him. = 如果他缺席，就必须有人代理他。
I wish I could deputize for her, but I'm too busy. = 我希望我能代理她，但我太忙了。


被动语态:
A junior officer was deputized to lead the patrol. = 一名下级军官被委派领导巡逻。
He was deputized to handle the negotiations. = 他被授权处理谈判。


疑问句:
Who is deputizing for the principal today? = 今天谁代理校长？
Will anyone be deputizing for you during your leave? = 你休假期间会有人代理你吗？


否定句:
He will not be deputizing for the manager tomorrow. = 他明天不会代理经理。
She did not deputize for her colleague last week. = 她上周没有代理她的同事。


PHRASE->
deputize for someone = 代理某人
be deputized to do something = 被授权做某事
deputize in someone's absence = 在某人缺席期间代理
acting deputizing = 代理的
deputizing capacity = 代理身份
