excite:/ɪkˈsaɪt/ | vt. |使兴奋；使激动|To cause someone or something to feel very enthusiastic and eager.  It often involves a strong feeling of anticipation or pleasure.|例句：The news excited everyone.  这个消息使每个人都兴奋起来。 |例句：The prospect of travel excited her. 旅行的前景让她兴奋不已。|近义词：thrill, stimulate, arouse, enliven |反义词：bore, depress, calm, tranquilize |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The roller coaster excites me. = 过山车让我兴奋。
The new technology excites investors. = 新技术让投资者兴奋。

一般过去时:
The concert excited the audience. = 音乐会让观众兴奋。
The good news excited her immensely. = 这个好消息让她非常兴奋。

现在完成时:
The success of the project has excited the team. = 项目的成功让团队兴奋不已。
The unexpected gift has excited the children. = 这份意外的礼物让孩子们兴奋不已。

过去完成时:
I had been excited about the trip for weeks. = 我已经对这次旅行兴奋了好几个星期了。
They had been excited to see their family again. = 他们非常兴奋地再次见到家人。

将来时:
The new game will excite many players. = 新游戏将会让许多玩家兴奋。
The upcoming holiday will excite the children. = 即将来临的假期会让孩子们兴奋。

虚拟语气:
If I won the lottery, I would be excited. = 如果我中了彩票，我会很兴奋。
I wish I could be excited about my work. = 我希望我能对我的工作感到兴奋。

被动语态:
The crowd was excited by the performance. = 观众被演出激动了。
He was excited to hear the good news. 他听到这个好消息很兴奋。(被动语态略显不自然，主动语态更常用)


疑问句:
Does this idea excite you? = 这个主意让你兴奋吗？
What excites you about this opportunity? = 这个机会的什么地方让你兴奋？


否定句:
The movie didn't excite me at all. = 这部电影一点也不让我兴奋。
The slow pace of the meeting didn't excite the participants. = 会议的缓慢节奏没有让与会者兴奋。


PHRASE->
excite sb to do sth = 激励某人做某事
be excited about = 对...感到兴奋
get excited = 变得兴奋
feel excited = 感到兴奋
excite interest = 激发兴趣
