caviar:/ˈkæviɑːr/| n.|鱼子酱|Salted roe of sturgeon fish, typically eaten as a delicacy.  It is often served as an appetizer or hors d'oeuvre.|例句：The restaurant is famous for its exquisite caviar service. 这家餐厅以其精美的鱼子酱服务而闻名。

例句：We enjoyed a small portion of caviar with blinis and crème fraîche. 我们享用了一小份配有薄饼和酸奶油的鱼子酱。


近义词：roe, fish eggs


反义词：None (no direct antonym, but could consider inexpensive foods as a contrast)


词源:  The word "caviar" comes from the Turkish word "khavyar" (хавьяр), which ultimately derives from the Persian word "خاویار" (khāvyār).  The Persian word's root is "khāg," meaning "egg."  It entered English via various European languages.
