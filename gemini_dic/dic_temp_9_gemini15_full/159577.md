pollarding:/ˈpɒlədɪŋ/|/ˈpɑːlədɪŋ/| n. vt.|截顶；修剪树枝|The regular cutting back of the branches of a tree to promote bushy growth and prevent it from becoming too tall.|例句：Pollarding is a traditional method of managing trees, particularly willows and poplars.  截顶是管理树木的传统方法，尤其适用于柳树和杨树。

例句：The park's ancient oaks have been pollarded for centuries. 公园里的古老橡树已经截顶了几个世纪。


近义词：coppicing, topping, pruning|反义词：leaving to grow freely

词性：名词，动词

词源:
The word "pollarding" comes from the Old French word "poler," meaning "to lop" or "to prune," ultimately deriving from the Latin word "polare," with a similar meaning.  The "-ing" suffix indicates the act or process.
