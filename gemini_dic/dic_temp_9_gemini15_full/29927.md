cavalierness:/ˌkævəˈlɪərnəs/| n. |漫不经心；傲慢；轻率|A casual or offhand manner; nonchalance; disdainful disregard.  It suggests a lack of proper respect or consideration for others or for a situation.  |例句：Her cavalierness towards the rules angered the teacher.  她的漫不经心激怒了老师。

例句：His cavalierness in handling the important documents caused a major problem. 他处理重要文件的轻率态度造成了一个大问题。


近义词： nonchalance, indifference, insouciance, disregard, contempt

反义词： carefulness, seriousness, respectfulness, consideration, attentiveness


词源：源自法语词 *cavalier*,  意为“骑士”，后来引申出“傲慢的，轻率的”之意。  The sense of nonchalant disregard developed from the historical image of a knight's often arrogant and dismissive attitude.
