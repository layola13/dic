/kɒkəˈduːdəlduːɪŋ/ | v. | 咯咯啼叫（模仿公鸡叫声） |  The word "cock-a-doodle-dooing" is a verb formed by adding the "-ing" suffix to the onomatopoeic phrase "cock-a-doodle-doo," which imitates the sound a rooster makes. It describes the act of a rooster crowing or someone imitating that sound. | The rooster was cock-a-doodle-dooing at dawn.  公鸡在黎明时分咯咯啼叫。 The child was cock-a-doodle-dooing, mimicking the rooster.  孩子咯咯地叫着，模仿公鸡。 | crowing, clucking |  (No direct antonym, as it describes a specific sound) | v.
