fainting:/ˈfeɪntɪŋ/| n.|昏厥；晕倒|The sudden loss of consciousness, typically caused by a temporary reduction in blood flow to the brain.  It is often characterized by lightheadedness, dizziness, and a loss of strength before the actual loss of consciousness.|例句：She experienced a fainting spell after donating blood. 她献血后晕倒了。|例句：The heat caused him to faint. 炎热的天气让他晕倒了。|近义词：swooning, blacking out|反义词：consciousness, wakefulness|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
Fainting is a common symptom of low blood sugar. = 晕倒是一种低血糖的常见症状。
He often faints when he sees blood. = 他看到血经常会晕倒。


一般过去时:
She fainted during the marathon. = 她在马拉松比赛中晕倒了。
The patient fainted after the surgery. = 病人在手术后晕倒了。


现在完成时:
He has fainted several times this week. = 他本周已经晕倒好几次了。
She has never fainted before. = 她以前从未晕倒过。


过去完成时:
He had fainted before anyone could help him. = 在任何人能够帮助他之前，他已经晕倒了。
She had fainted twice before the doctor arrived. = 在医生到达之前，她已经晕倒了两次。


将来时:
I think he will faint if he sees that. = 我想如果他看到那样的话他会晕倒的。
She might faint from the pain. = 她可能会因为疼痛而晕倒。


虚拟语气:
If I had known she was going to faint, I would have helped her. = 如果我知道她要晕倒，我会帮她的。
I wish I hadn't seen her faint. = 我真希望我没有看到她晕倒。


被动语态:
He was fainted by the sudden shock. = 他被突如其来的震惊弄晕了。(被动语态在此处略显不自然，通常用主动语态)
The patient was found fainted on the floor. = 病人被发现晕倒在地板上。(被动语态在此处更常用)


疑问句:

Has he ever fainted before? = 他以前晕倒过吗？


否定句:
She didn't faint. = 她没有晕倒。
He hasn't fainted yet. = 他还没晕倒。
