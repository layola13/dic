coerced:/koʊˈɜːrst/ | vt. |强迫；胁迫；威逼 | To force someone to do something they don't want to do, typically by using threats or pressure. |例句：The confession was coerced by police.  警方强迫他认罪。

例句：He was coerced into signing the contract. 他被迫签署了合同。


近义词：compelled, forced, pressured, intimidated, blackmailed | 反义词：persuaded, encouraged


词源:  The word "coerced" comes from the Latin word "coergere," which means "to drive together," "to restrain," or "to compel."  The prefix "co-" indicates "together," and "ergere" is related to the word "work," suggesting the idea of forcing someone to work or act against their will.
