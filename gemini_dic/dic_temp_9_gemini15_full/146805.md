overimitation:/ˌoʊvərˌɪmɪˈteɪʃən/| n. |过分模仿|The act of imitating behaviors or actions beyond what is necessary or functional;  excessive or unnecessary imitation. This is particularly studied in child development and cognitive psychology, where children may copy irrelevant or inefficient actions observed in a model. |Example:  The child's overimitation of the researcher's unnecessary actions during the experiment demonstrated the phenomenon in question.  孩子在实验中对研究者不必要行为的过分模仿证明了所讨论的现象。|近义词：excessive imitation,  mimicry (in a negative context)|反义词：selective imitation, original behavior|
