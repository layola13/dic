sardius:/ˈsɑːrdiəs/| n. |红玉髓，赤玉髓 |A sard is a variety of chalcedony, a cryptocrystalline form of quartz, that is typically reddish-brown in color.  It was highly valued in antiquity for its color and was often used in jewelry and carvings. The name derives from Sardis, an ancient city in Lydia (modern-day Turkey), where it was mined. | Example: The ancient ring featured a beautiful sard, its rich red hue reflecting the light.  这枚古戒指镶嵌着一颗美丽的红玉髓，其浓郁的红色光泽反射着光线。 |近义词：carnelian, chalcedony |反义词：无
