mete-out:/miːtˈaʊt/| vt.| 分配；处以；给予 | to distribute or apportion (something); to administer or inflict (punishment, etc.) |例句：The judge meted out a harsh sentence. 法官判处了严厉的刑罚。
例句：The teacher meted out the rewards fairly among the students.老师公平地将奖励分配给学生们。|近义词：distribute, allot, administer, inflict |反义词：withhold, retain


USAGE->

mete out a blow = 痛击


词源:
The verb "mete out" originates from the Old English verb *mǣtan, meaning "to measure" or "to allot."  The "out" is an adverbial particle intensifying the action of measuring or distributing.  Over time, the meaning shifted to focus on the distribution of rewards or punishments.
