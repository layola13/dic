hyperflexibility: /ˌhaɪpərˌflɛksəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 超柔韧性，过度灵活 |  The state of having joints that bend or move further than is typical or considered normal. This can be a genetic trait, the result of specific training, or indicative of underlying medical conditions. It often manifests as an increased range of motion in joints, which can lead to instability and increased risk of injury.  | Example:  Her hyperflexibility allowed her to perform incredible contortionist feats, but also made her prone to dislocations.  她的超柔韧性让她能够表演令人难以置信的杂技表演，但也让她容易脱臼。


|近义词: excessive flexibility, extreme flexibility, hypermobility | 反义词: inflexibility, rigidity, stiffness | 词性: 名词
