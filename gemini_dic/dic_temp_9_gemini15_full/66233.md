exclaim:/ɪkˈskleɪm/| vt.|呼喊；惊叫；大声说|To cry out or speak suddenly and vehemently.|She exclaimed with delight at the unexpected gift.  她收到意外的礼物，高兴地惊呼起来。|He exclaimed in anger at the injustice. 他愤怒地大声喊叫着这种不公正。|shout, cry out, utter|whisper, murmur

一般现在时:
He exclaims every time he sees a spider. 他每次看到蜘蛛都会惊叫。
She exclaims her love for her family frequently. 她经常大声表达对家人的爱。


一般过去时:
He exclaimed in surprise when he saw the results. 他看到结果时惊讶地大叫。
She exclaimed that she couldn't believe it. 她惊呼说她简直不敢相信。


现在完成时:
He has exclaimed his opinion many times. 他已经多次大声表达了他的意见。
She has exclaimed at the beauty of the sunset many times. 她已经多次惊叹于日落的美丽。


过去完成时:
He had exclaimed his innocence before the trial. 他在审判前曾大声喊冤。

If he had exclaimed his concerns earlier, things might have been different. 如果他早些表达他的担忧，情况可能会有所不同。
