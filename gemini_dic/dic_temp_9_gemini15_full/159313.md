pointingstock: /ˈpɔɪntɪŋstɑːk/ | n. | 指向的股票；用于指示的股票 | A pointing stock is a stock, often a publicly traded company's stock, that is used as an example or illustration, often in a negative context, to highlight a particular trend or phenomenon.  It might be a company performing poorly, undergoing a scandal, or representing a specific market downturn. | Example: "The failing tech startup became a pointing stock for the dangers of over-expansion."  = “这家失败的科技初创公司成为了过度扩张危险的典型案例。” | 近义词：example, illustration, case study | 反义词: (No direct antonym, but the opposite would be a successful company used as a positive example) | 名词
