inter-class:/ˌɪntərˈklæs/| adj.|跨类的，类间的|Existing or occurring between classes or categories.  It often refers to relationships, comparisons, or interactions that transcend the boundaries of individual classes.|例句：The study examined inter-class differences in academic performance.  这项研究考察了不同班级之间在学业成绩上的差异。
例句：Inter-class rivalries were common among the students. 学生之间经常存在班级间的竞争。|近义词：cross-class, between-class|反义词：intra-class, within-class|


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms in the way "what" does. Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  The usage focuses on its descriptive function within sentences.

Example Sentences illustrating different contexts:


Comparative:
The research highlighted significant inter-class variations in income levels. 这项研究强调了收入水平上显著的跨阶级差异。

The inter-class differences in attitudes toward education were surprising.  对教育的态度在不同阶级间的差异令人惊讶。

Descriptive:
Inter-class mobility is a key factor in social stratification.  跨阶级流动是社会分层的一个关键因素。

The program aimed to reduce inter-class inequalities in healthcare access.  该项目旨在减少医疗保健获取方面的阶级差距。

PHRASE->
There aren't common idiomatic phrases directly using "inter-class."  Its usage is mainly in technical or academic writing.  However, we can show phrases that *relate* to the concept:


social mobility: 社会流动性
class conflict: 阶级斗争
economic inequality: 经济不平等
income disparity: 收入差距
social stratification: 社会分层
