recognises:/ˈrɛkəɡnaɪzɪz/ | v. | 认出；识别；承认；认可 | To identify someone or something or to accept something as true or valid. |例句：I recognise that man from the news. 我从新闻里认出了那个人。|例句：The government recognises the need for reform. 政府认识到改革的必要性。|近义词：identify, acknowledge, appreciate, admit |反义词：ignore, disregard, deny, overlook


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He recognises his mistakes. = 他认识到自己的错误。
She recognises the importance of education. = 她认识到教育的重要性。

一般过去时:
I recognised her from a photograph. = 我从照片上认出了她。
They recognised the danger immediately. = 他们立即意识到了危险。

现在完成时:
We have recognised the need for change. = 我们已经认识到改变的必要性。
She has recognised his talent for years. = 她多年来一直认可他的才能。

过去完成时:
I had recognised him before he spoke. = 他还没开口我就认出他来了。
They had recognised the pattern of the crime. = 他们已经认出了犯罪的模式。

将来时:
I will recognise him when I see him. = 我见到他就会认出他。
They will recognise the significance of this discovery. = 他们将会认识到这项发现的重要性。

虚拟语气:
If I recognised him, I would tell him. = 如果我认出他，我会告诉他。
I wish I recognised the signs earlier. = 我希望我早点认出这些迹象。

被动语态:
His contribution was recognised by the company. = 他的贡献得到了公司的认可。
The problem was recognised and addressed quickly. = 问题很快就被发现并解决了。

疑问句:
Do you recognise this song? = 你认得这首歌吗？
Does he recognise his own limitations? = 他认识到自己的局限性吗？

否定句:
I didn't recognise him at first. = 我一开始没认出他。
They didn't recognise the seriousness of the situation. = 他们没有认识到局势的严重性。
