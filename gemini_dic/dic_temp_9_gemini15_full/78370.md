gesticulation:/dʒɛsˌtɪkjuˈleɪʃən/| n. |手势；姿态；比划|The act of making gestures, especially expressive ones with hands or arms.  It often conveys meaning or emotion beyond spoken words.  |例句：Her animated gesticulation emphasized her point.  她的生动比划强调了她的观点。

例句：The speaker's gesticulation was so dramatic it almost overshadowed his words. 演讲者的夸张手势几乎盖过了他的话。


近义词：gesture,posturing,miming|反义词：stillness,immobility|词性：名词


词源：

From Late Latin *gesticulatio, from Latin *gesticulare "to make gestures," from gestus "gesture, posture, bearing," past participle of gerere "to carry, bear, do, perform."  The word ultimately relates to the same root as "gesture" itself.
