preemploys:/ˌpriːɪmˈplɔɪz/| v.|预先雇用，提前雇用 |To employ someone before they are needed.  This is often done to secure talent or to prepare for future projects. |例句：The company preemploys interns each summer to ensure a sufficient workforce for the busy season. 公司每年夏天都会预先雇用实习生，以确保旺季有足够的人手。

例句：The construction firm preemploys skilled laborers to begin work on the new bridge project as soon as permits are issued. 建筑公司预先雇用了熟练工人，以便在许可证发放后立即开始新桥项目的施工。


近义词：hire in advance, engage beforehand, recruit early


反义词：lay off, fire, dismiss


词性：动词
