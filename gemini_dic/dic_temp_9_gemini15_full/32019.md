cheques:/tʃɛks/| n. |支票|A written order directing a bank to pay money from a person's account to another person.|I paid the bill with a cheque. 我用支票付了账。|近义词：check (美式英语)|反义词:cash, debit card


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He writes cheques regularly. = 他定期开支票。
She pays her bills with cheques. = 她用支票支付账单。


一般过去时:
They wrote several cheques last week. = 他们上周写了几张支票。
He cashed the cheque yesterday. = 他昨天兑现了支票。


现在完成时:
I have written a cheque for the rent. = 我已经开了一张房租支票。
She has not yet received the cheque. = 她还没有收到支票。


过去完成时:
He had already written the cheque before the meeting. = 他在会议之前就已经写好了支票。
They had deposited the cheques before the bank closed. = 他们在银行关门前已经存了支票。


将来时:
I will write a cheque tomorrow. = 我明天会写一张支票。
She will pay you by cheque next week. = 她下周会用支票付你钱。


虚拟语气:
If I had a chequebook, I would pay you immediately. = 如果我有支票簿，我会立即付钱给你。
I wish I had a cheque to give you. = 我真希望有一张支票可以给你。


被动语态:
The cheque was deposited into my account. = 支票存入了我的账户。
The cheques are being processed. = 支票正在处理中。


疑问句:
Did you receive the cheque? = 你收到支票了吗？
Can I pay by cheque? = 我可以用支票支付吗？


否定句:
I did not write a cheque. = 我没有写支票。
He doesn't accept cheques. = 他不接受支票。
