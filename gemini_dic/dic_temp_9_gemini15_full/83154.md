gutter:/ˈɡʌtər/ | n. |排水沟；阴沟；低劣的东西 | A gutter is a channel at the edge of a roof for carrying off rainwater, or a channel at the side of a street to carry off surface water.  It can also refer to something of low quality or morality. | 例句：The rainwater flowed down the gutter. 雨水顺着排水沟流了下来。  The newspaper article was pure gutter journalism. 这篇报纸文章纯粹是低劣的新闻报道。 | 近义词：drain, channel, sewer, trough | 反义词：summit, peak, zenith

词源:

The word "gutter" ultimately derives from the Old French word "goutiere," meaning a water channel or spout. This, in turn, is related to the Latin word "gutta," meaning a drop.  The evolution reflects the original function of gutters as channels to collect and direct drops of water.
