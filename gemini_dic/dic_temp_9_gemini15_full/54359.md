dishonour: /dɪsˈɒnə(r)/ /dɪsˈɑːnər/ | n., vt. | 耻辱；丢脸；不光彩；拒绝接受（支票等）|

英文解析:  Dishonour refers to a state of disgrace or shame, often resulting from a wrongdoing or failure to meet expectations. As a verb, it means to bring shame or disgrace upon someone or something; or to refuse to accept (a bill of exchange, cheque, etc.).

例句:

The scandal brought dishonour to the entire family.  这场丑闻给整个家族带来了耻辱。

His refusal to pay his debts was a dishonour to his family name. 他拒绝偿还债务是对家族名誉的玷污。


近义词: shame, disgrace, disrepute, insult, disrespect

反义词: honour, glory, respect, credit

词性: noun, verb
