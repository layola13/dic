/ˌmʌltiˈdaɪəlektəlɪzəm/ | n. | 多方言现象 | The coexistence and use of multiple dialects within a speech community.  Multidialectalism is often a characteristic of societies with diverse populations or regions with distinct linguistic traditions. It can reflect social, geographical, or historical factors. | Example: The multilingual and multidialectal nature of Singaporean society reflects its diverse population and history.  新加坡社会的多种语言和方言性质反映了其多元化的人口和历史。|近义词：linguistic diversity, dialectal variation | 反义词：monolingualism, linguistic uniformity

词源:

* **multi-**:  prefix meaning "many" or "much"  (来自拉丁语)
* **dialect-**:  relating to a dialect (来自希腊语διάλεκτος, dialektos, "conversation, dialect")
* **-alism**: suffix forming nouns denoting doctrines, principles, or practices (来自希腊语)


因此，multidialectalism 是由 "multi-" (多), "dialect" (方言), 和 "-alism" (…现象) 组合而成，字面意思是“多方言现象”。
