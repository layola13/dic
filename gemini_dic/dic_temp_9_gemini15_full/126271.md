/ˈmaɪəlɒn/| n. |脊髓|The myelon, also known as the spinal cord, is the cylindrical structure of nervous tissue extending from the medulla oblongata in the brainstem to the lumbar region of the vertebral column. It is responsible for transmitting signals between the brain and the rest of the body.
