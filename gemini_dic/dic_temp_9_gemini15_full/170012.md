protectionism: /prəˈtɛkʃənɪzəm/ | n. | 保护主义 |

英文解析：The practice of shielding a country's domestic industries from foreign competition by taxing imports or establishing other barriers to trade.  It aims to protect local businesses and jobs from cheaper foreign goods, but often leads to higher prices for consumers and reduced overall economic efficiency.

例句：

The government implemented protectionist policies to safeguard its struggling textile industry.  政府实施了保护主义政策以保护其苦苦挣扎的纺织业。

Many economists argue that protectionism harms long-term economic growth. 许多经济学家认为保护主义损害了长期的经济增长。


近义词：trade protection, economic nationalism, import substitution

反义词：free trade, globalization, laissez-faire economics
