laryngealization: /ˌlærɪndʒiəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 声带紧闭 | The process of constricting the vocal cords during phonation, resulting in a creaky or breathy voice quality.  This is often used in descriptions of phonetics and speech sounds, referring to a voice quality where the vocal cords are partially or completely closed during voicing, creating a characteristic sound.  | Example: Laryngealization is a common feature in many languages, such as Arabic and some dialects of English.  声带紧闭是许多语言中的一种常见特征，例如阿拉伯语和某些英语方言。| 近义词: vocal fold constriction, creakiness, breathiness | 反义词: clear voicing
