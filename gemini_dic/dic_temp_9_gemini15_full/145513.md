overcoaching: /ˌoʊvərˈkoʊtʃɪŋ/ | n. | 过度训练；过度指导 | The act of coaching someone excessively, often to the detriment of their well-being, performance, or enjoyment.  This can manifest in various ways, such as excessive training volume, overly critical feedback, or a lack of focus on the athlete's individual needs. |  The young athlete suffered from overcoaching, leading to burnout and a loss of passion for the sport.  |  overtraining, excessive coaching | undercoaching | 名词
