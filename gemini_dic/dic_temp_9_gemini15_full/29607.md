categorised:/ˈkætəɡəraɪzd/ | v. | 分类，归类 | To place into a category or categories.  |The librarian categorised the books by author. 图书管理员按作者对书籍进行了分类。|  classify, sort, group |  uncategorised, unclassified | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They categorise the data by age. = 他们按年龄对数据进行分类。
We categorise our customers by their purchasing habits. = 我们根据客户的购买习惯对他们进行分类。

一般过去时:
She categorised the emails into urgent and non-urgent. = 她将电子邮件分为紧急和非紧急邮件。
The researcher categorised the responses into different themes. = 研究人员将回复归纳成不同的主题。

现在完成时:
He has categorised all the files on his computer. = 他已经将他电脑上的所有文件都分类好了。
We have categorised the products for easier browsing. = 我们已经对产品进行了分类，方便浏览。

过去完成时:
They had categorised the evidence before presenting their findings. = 他们在提交调查结果之前已经对证据进行了分类。
She had categorised the samples according to their chemical composition. = 她已经根据样品的化学成分对它们进行了分类。

将来时:
We will categorise the donations by the type of assistance offered. = 我们将按提供的援助类型对捐款进行分类。
They will categorise the applicants based on their qualifications. = 他们将根据申请人的资格对申请人进行分类。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would categorise all my photos. = 如果我有更多时间，我会把我的所有照片都分类。
It would be helpful if they categorised the information more clearly. = 如果他们能更清晰地分类信息，那就太好了。

被动语态:
The products are categorised by price and features. = 产品按价格和功能进行分类。
The data was categorised using a sophisticated algorithm. = 数据使用复杂的算法进行分类。

疑问句:
How are the students categorised? = 学生是如何分类的？
How were the survey responses categorised? = 问卷调查的回复是如何分类的？

否定句:
They did not categorise the data properly. = 他们没有正确地对数据进行分类。
The report did not categorise the different risk factors effectively. = 报告没有有效地对不同的风险因素进行分类。
