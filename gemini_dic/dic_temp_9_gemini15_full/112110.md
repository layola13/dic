longship:/ˈlɒŋʃɪp/| n. |长船；北欧海盗船 | A longship is a type of narrow, fast sailing ship used by the Vikings and other Norse peoples.  They were characterized by their long, narrow hulls, shallow drafts, and often featured a single mast with a square sail.  Longships were used for warfare, exploration, trade, and raiding. | Example: The Vikings sailed their longships across the ocean to explore new lands.  = 维京人驾驶他们的长船横渡海洋去探索新的土地。

The discovery of a well-preserved longship in a fjord provided valuable insights into Viking shipbuilding techniques. = 在峡湾发现一艘保存完好的长船，为了解维京人的造船技术提供了宝贵的见解。

近义词：Viking ship, Norse ship | 反义词：无
