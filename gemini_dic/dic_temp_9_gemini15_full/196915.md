shadowed:/ˈʃædəʊd/| adj.|阴暗的；遮蔽的；被阴影笼罩的；|Covered in shadow; dark or gloomy;  followed secretly or closely.|例句：The room was shadowed and mysterious.  房间阴暗而神秘。  The detective shadowed the suspect for days.  侦探跟踪了嫌疑犯好几天。|近义词：shaded, obscured, darkened, shadowed,  |反义词：bright, illuminated, sunlit

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The trees shadow the house. = 树木遮蔽了房子。
The clouds shadow the sun. = 云彩遮蔽了太阳。


一般过去时:
The mountains shadowed the valley. = 山峦笼罩着山谷。
He shadowed her movements closely. = 他密切地跟踪着她的行动。


现在完成时:
The scandal has shadowed his career. = 丑闻一直困扰着他的职业生涯。
Fear has shadowed her life for years. = 多年来，恐惧一直笼罩着她的生活。


过去完成时:
The past failures had shadowed her confidence. = 过去的失败曾让她缺乏自信。
The threat of war had shadowed the peace talks. = 战争的威胁曾笼罩着和平谈判。


将来时:
The upcoming election will shadow the economic debate. = 即将到来的选举将会影响经济辩论。
The bad weather will shadow our picnic plans. = 恶劣的天气将会影响我们的野餐计划。


虚拟语气:
If the sun were shadowed, it would be cooler. = 如果太阳被遮蔽，天气就会凉爽些。
I wish my past mistakes hadn't shadowed my present. = 我真希望我过去的错误没有影响我的现在。


被动语态:
His reputation was shadowed by the accusations. = 他的名声因指控而受损。
The evidence was shadowed by doubt. = 证据被怀疑所蒙蔽。


疑问句:
Is the path shadowed by the trees? = 小路被树木遮蔽了吗？
Were their actions shadowed by ulterior motives? = 他们的行动是出于不可告人的动机吗？


否定句:
The room wasn't shadowed; it was brightly lit. = 房间并不阴暗；它光线充足。
His past wasn't shadowed by any significant mistakes. = 他的过去没有任何重大的错误。
