co-sovereignty:/ˌkoʊsoʊˈvɛrənti/| n. |共主权；联合主权|The joint exercise of sovereignty by two or more entities over a territory or area, often involving a degree of shared authority and responsibility.  This arrangement can be established through treaties or agreements, and the precise division of powers can vary greatly depending on the context.  It is often employed in situations where there is a need to balance the interests of different entities with claims to the same territory or resources.|Example: The agreement established a co-sovereignty arrangement over the disputed islands, with both nations sharing responsibility for their management and defense.|近义词：shared sovereignty, joint sovereignty|反义词：absolute sovereignty, sole sovereignty|名词
