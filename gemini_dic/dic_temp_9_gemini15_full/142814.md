ousted: /ˈaʊstɪd/ | vt. |罢免；驱逐；逐出| to remove someone from a position of power, especially forcefully or unfairly.  |例句：The CEO was ousted in a boardroom coup.  首席执行官在一场董事会政变中被罢免。

例句：The dictator was ousted from power by the rebels.  独裁者被叛军赶下台。


近义词：remove, displace, depose, unseat, expel

反义词：install, appoint, reinstate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They oust corrupt officials. = 他们罢免腐败官员。
The company ousts underperforming employees. = 公司解雇表现不佳的员工。

一般过去时:
The president was ousted in a military coup. = 总统在军事政变中被罢免。
He was ousted from his position after the scandal. = 丑闻之后他被免职。

现在完成时:
She has been ousted from her leadership role. = 她已被免去领导职务。
The team has been ousted from the competition. = 该队已被淘汰出局。

过去完成时:
He had been ousted from the party before the election. = 大选前他已经遭到党内除名。
The old regime had been ousted by the revolutionaries. = 旧政权已被革命者推翻。

将来时:
They will likely be ousted in the next election. = 他们很可能会在下届选举中被赶下台。
The CEO will be ousted if the scandal is proven. = 如果丑闻被证实，首席执行官将被罢免。

虚拟语气:
If he had not been ousted, the company might have succeeded. = 如果他没有被罢免，公司也许会成功。
I wish I hadn't been ousted from the team. = 我真希望我没有被踢出球队。

被动语态:
The mayor was ousted by the voters. = 市长被选民罢免。
He was ousted from his job unfairly. = 他被不公平地解雇了。

疑问句:
Was the prime minister ousted? = 总理被罢免了吗？
Will the coach be ousted after the loss? = 球队失利后教练会被解雇吗？

否定句:
The dictator was not ousted easily. = 独裁者并非轻易被推翻。
They did not oust him from his position despite the allegations. = 尽管有指控，他们也没有将他免职。


PHRASE->
be ousted from = 被逐出…
oust somebody from something = 将某人从…中赶出去
oust somebody in favour of somebody =  为了某人而罢免某人
oust somebody from power = 将某人赶下台
oust somebody from office = 将某人免职
