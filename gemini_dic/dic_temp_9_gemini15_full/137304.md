nonsystemic: /ˌnɑːnˈsɪˈstɛmɪk/ | adj. | 非系统的，不成系统的 | Not forming or belonging to a system; not systematic.  It implies a lack of organization, order, or coherence.  | 例句：The approach to the problem was nonsystemic and ineffective.  = 解决这个问题的方法是非系统的且无效的。

The evidence suggests a nonsystemic error, not a flaw in the overall system. = 证据表明这是一个非系统性错误，而不是整个系统的缺陷。


近义词：unsystematic, disorganized, haphazard, random, chaotic | 反义词：systematic, organized, methodical, coherent


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "systemic" (relating to a system). "Systemic" itself derives from the noun "system," ultimately from the Ancient Greek word "systema," meaning "a whole composed of parts."
