headlights:/ˈhɛdlaɪts/ | n. | 车前灯，头灯 |  The lights at the front of a vehicle, used to illuminate the road ahead.  | The headlights of the car cut through the fog.  这辆车的车前灯穿透了雾。 |  front lights, lamps |  (反义词无直接对应词，可考虑用darkness 或 no lights)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The headlights illuminate the road. = 车前灯照亮了道路。
These headlights are very bright. = 这些车前灯非常明亮。


一般过去时:
The headlights flashed. = 车前灯闪烁了一下。
The headlights were on. = 车前灯是开着的。


现在完成时:
The headlights have been replaced. = 车前灯已经被更换了。
We have dimmed the headlights. = 我们已经调暗了车前灯。


过去完成时:
The headlights had failed before we reached the town. = 我们到达镇上之前，车前灯就已经坏了。
The headlights had been on all night. = 车前灯整夜都亮着。


将来时:
The headlights will be brighter. = 车前灯将会更亮。
The headlights will need replacing soon. = 车前灯很快就要更换了。


虚拟语气:
If the headlights worked, we could drive at night. = 如果车前灯能用，我们晚上就能开车。
I wish the headlights were brighter. = 我希望车前灯更亮一些。


被动语态:
The headlights were damaged in the accident. = 车前灯在事故中受损了。
The headlights are being repaired. = 车前灯正在维修中。


疑问句:
Are the headlights on? = 车前灯开了吗？
What's wrong with the headlights? = 车前灯出了什么问题？


否定句:
The headlights weren't working. = 车前灯坏了。
The headlights didn't illuminate the road properly. = 车前灯没有正确地照亮道路。
