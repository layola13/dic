mutual:/ˈmjuːtʃuəl/| adj.|相互的，彼此的|Existing between or shared by two or more parties.  It implies reciprocity and a feeling of shared understanding or benefit.|例句：We have a mutual respect for each other. 我们彼此尊重。

例句：Their success is based on mutual trust and cooperation. 他们的成功是建立在相互信任和合作的基础上。


近义词：reciprocal, shared, common, joint|反义词：unilateral, one-sided


词源：From Middle English *mutual, from Old French *mutuel, from Latin *mutuus (“borrowed, lent, exchanged”), from mutare (“to change”).


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They have a mutual understanding. = 他们之间有相互理解。
We share mutual interests. = 我们有共同的兴趣爱好。


一般过去时:
They had a mutual dislike. = 他们彼此讨厌。
We shared mutual concerns. = 我们分享了共同的担忧。


现在完成时:
They have developed a mutual respect. = 他们已经培养了相互尊重。
We have enjoyed mutual benefits from this partnership. = 我们从这次合作中获得了相互的利益。


过去完成时:
They had already established a mutual agreement. = 他们已经达成了一致协议。
We had shared mutual experiences before. = 我们之前有过共同的经历。


将来时:

被动语态:

疑问句:
Do you have any mutual friends? = 你有共同的朋友吗？

否定句:
