inviolable: /ɪnˈvaɪələbl/ | adj. | 不可侵犯的，神圣不可侵犯的 |  Not able to be violated or infringed; sacred.  Something inviolable is protected from being attacked or damaged.  |

例句：

The right to life is an inviolable human right.  = 生命权是不可侵犯的人权。

The treaty established an inviolable border between the two countries. = 条约确立了两国之间不可侵犯的边界。


近义词：sacred, untouchable, unassailable, immune, protected

反义词：vulnerable, assailable, violable


词源:

The word "inviolable" comes from the Old French word *inviolable*, which itself derives from the Latin word *inviolabilis*, a combination of the prefix *in-* (not) and *violare* (to violate).  The meaning therefore directly relates to "not able to be violated".
