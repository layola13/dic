protosurgeons:/ˈproʊtoʊˌsɜːrdʒən/| n. |史前外科医生；早期外科医生|Early practitioners of surgery, before the development of modern surgical techniques and understanding.  They lacked the advanced tools and knowledge of modern surgeons but performed rudimentary surgical procedures.|Example:  The protosurgeons of ancient Egypt used rudimentary tools to perform trepanation. 史前埃及的外科医生使用简陋的工具进行开颅手术。|近义词：early surgeons, primitive surgeons|反义词：modern surgeons
