excelling:/ɛkˈsɛlɪŋ/ | v. | 擅长；优秀；胜过 |  To surpass others in merit or skill; to be superior.  | He excelled in mathematics. 他擅长数学。  She excelled at sports. 她擅长体育运动。 | surpassing, outshining, surpassing, outperforming | failing, underperforming, lagging


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He excels at playing the piano. = 他擅长弹钢琴。
She excels in her studies. = 她学习成绩优秀。

一般过去时:
He excelled in his previous job. = 他在以前的工作中表现出色。
She excelled at the competition. = 她在比赛中表现出色。

现在完成时:
He has excelled in his career. = 他在事业上取得了巨大成功。
She has excelled in all her endeavors. = 她在她所有的努力中都取得了优异的成绩。

过去完成时:
He had excelled in school before he started his career. = 他在开始职业生涯之前在学校表现出色。
She had excelled in her previous role before taking on this new challenge. = 在承担这项新挑战之前，她在之前的角色中表现出色。

将来时:
He will excel in his new role. = 他将在新的角色中表现出色。
She will excel if she puts in the effort. = 如果她付出努力，她将会表现出色。

虚拟语气:
If he had practiced more, he would have excelled. = 如果他多练习一些，他就会表现出色。
I wish I had excelled in my studies. = 我希望我在学习中表现出色。

被动语态:
He was excelled by his rival in the race. = 在比赛中，他被他的对手超越了。(This usage is less common and often sounds unnatural)
The team was excelled by their opponents. = 这个团队被他们的对手超越了。(This usage is less common and often sounds unnatural)


疑问句:
Does she excel in any particular subject? = 她擅长哪一门科目？
Did he excel in the interview? = 他在面试中表现出色吗？

否定句:
He did not excel in sports. = 他不擅长体育运动。
She did not excel at her job. = 她工作表现不佳。


PHRASE->
excel at = 擅长于...
excel in = 在...方面表现出色
excel oneself = 超越自我
to excel in one's field = 在自己的领域里表现出色
excel beyond expectations = 超越预期
