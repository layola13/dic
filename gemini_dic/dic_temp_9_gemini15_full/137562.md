nontransitives:/ˌnɒnˈtrænzɪtɪvz/| n. |非及物动词（或其集合）|Nontransitive verbs are verbs that do not take a direct object.  They describe actions or states of being that do not directly affect another noun or pronoun. |The sun shines. (The sun is the subject; there's no direct object receiving the action of shining.)| intransitive verbs| transitive verbs| n.
