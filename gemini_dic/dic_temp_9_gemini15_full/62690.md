engender:/ɪnˈdʒɛndər/ | vt. | 产生；引起；导致 | To cause or give rise to (a feeling, situation, or condition).  |  His policies engendered a great deal of controversy. 他的政策引起了很大的争议。 The issue engendered much debate.  这个问题引起了很多争论。|  cause, produce, create, generate | suppress, prevent, stop | 动词

一般现在时:
The new law engenders hope for a better future. = 新法律带来了对更美好未来的希望。
His actions engender mistrust among his colleagues. = 他的行为在同事中引起了不信任。

一般过去时:
The speech engendered a sense of unity among the audience. = 这篇演讲在听众中产生了一种团结感。
The storm engendered widespread damage to the coastline. = 暴风雨对海岸线造成了广泛的破坏。

现在完成时:
The reforms have engendered significant economic growth. = 改革带来了显著的经济增长。
Her work has engendered a new wave of interest in the field. = 她的工作引发了该领域的新一轮兴趣。

过去完成时:
The previous government's policies had engendered widespread social unrest. = 前政府的政策已经引起了广泛的社会动荡。
His previous mistakes had engendered a lack of confidence in his leadership. = 他之前的错误导致了人们对其领导能力缺乏信心。

将来时:
The proposed changes will engender considerable debate. = 提出的变化将会引起相当大的争论。
The new technology will engender significant improvements in efficiency. = 新技术将带来效率的显著提高。

虚拟语气:
If the government acted decisively, it would engender public trust. = 如果政府果断行动，它就能赢得公众的信任。
I wish the project had engendered more enthusiasm among the team members. = 我希望这个项目能在团队成员中激发出更多的热情。

被动语态:
Much controversy was engendered by the politician's statement. = 这位政治家的声明引起了很大的争议。
Significant progress is being engendered by the new research. = 新的研究正在取得显著进展。

疑问句:

What will engender a positive change in the community? = 什么才能在社区中带来积极的改变？

否定句:
The program did not engender the expected results. = 该计划没有产生预期的结果。
His approach did not engender any support from his colleagues. = 他的方法没有得到同事的任何支持。
