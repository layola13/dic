coxcombical: /ˈkɒkskəʊmbɪkəl/ | adj. | 狂妄自大的；自鸣得意的；花里胡哨的 |  Affectedly foppish; vain and conceited.  Characterized by ostentatious display; showy. |

例句：

His coxcombical attire drew unwanted attention.  = 他那花里胡哨的穿着吸引了不必要的注意。

Her coxcombical boasting about her achievements annoyed everyone. = 她自吹自擂的炫耀自己的成就惹恼了所有人。


近义词：vain, conceited, pretentious, ostentatious, flamboyant, showy


反义词：humble, modest, unassuming, self-effacing


词源:

The word derives from "coxcomb," a historical term for a fool or a vain person, itself derived from the "cock's comb," a red crest on a rooster's head, symbolizing arrogance and vanity.  The suffix "-ical" transforms the noun into an adjective.
