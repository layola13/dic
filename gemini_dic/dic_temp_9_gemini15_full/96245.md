indicational:/ˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃənəl/ | adj. | 指示性的，表示的 | Relating to or of the nature of an indication; suggestive.  It implies something but doesn't state it directly. |  The slight tremor in his voice was indicational of his nervousness. 他声音轻微颤抖，暗示他很紧张。The cloudy sky was indicational of an impending storm.  阴沉的天空预示着暴风雨即将来临。| suggestive, symptomatic, indicative | conclusive, definitive | 形容词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The flickering light is indicational of a power problem. 闪烁的灯光表明电力有问题。
His hesitant speech is indicational of his uncertainty. 他犹豫的言语表明了他的不确定性。

现在完成时:

Recent studies have been indicational of a link between diet and disease. 最近的研究表明饮食与疾病之间存在联系。

虚拟语气:
If his behavior were indicational of his true character, we would be concerned. 如果他的行为能说明他的真实性格，我们会担心。
If the symptoms were indicational of a more serious illness, we would seek further medical advice. 如果症状暗示更严重的疾病，我们会寻求进一步的医疗建议。


被动语态:
His anxiety was indicated by his fidgeting. 他的焦虑通过他的不安表现出来。(注意：这里用indicated，更简洁直接)
The impending storm was indicated by the darkening sky. 即将来临的风暴由阴暗的天空预示。(注意：这里用indicated，更简洁直接)


疑问句:
Is this behavior indicational of a deeper problem? 这种行为是否暗示着更深层次的问题？
Are these symptoms indicational of a serious illness? 这些症状是否预示着严重的疾病？


否定句:
The initial findings were not indicational of any wrongdoing. 初步调查结果没有显示任何不当行为。
Her cheerful demeanor was not indicational of her inner turmoil. 她快乐的表情并没有表明她内心的不安。
