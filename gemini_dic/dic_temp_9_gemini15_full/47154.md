dawdled:/ˈdɔːdəld/| v.|磨蹭；虚度光阴；|to spend time idly; to move slowly and lazily.|例句：He dawdled over his breakfast, completely ignoring the time. 他磨磨蹭蹭地吃早餐，完全不理会时间。|例句：Don't dawdle; we're already late.  别磨蹭了，我们已经迟了。|近义词：delayed, lingered, procrastinated, idled|反义词：hurried, rushed, hastened

词源:
The word "dawdle" likely originated from the Middle Dutch word "dwadelen," meaning "to wander" or "to stray."  This suggests an etymological connection to the idea of aimless wandering, which aligns with the modern sense of the word.
