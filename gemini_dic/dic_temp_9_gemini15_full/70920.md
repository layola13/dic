firebomb:/ˈfaɪərˌbɒmb/ /ˈfaɪərˌbɑːm/ | n. vt. |燃烧弹；用燃烧弹攻击 | A firebomb is a bomb designed to start fires, typically by using incendiary materials.  It can also refer to the act of attacking something with a firebomb. |例句：The protesters threw firebombs at the police station.  抗议者向警察局投掷燃烧弹。

The building was destroyed by a firebomb. 这座建筑物被燃烧弹摧毁了。

近义词：incendiary device, Molotov cocktail |反义词：fire extinguisher, water |词性：n. vt.

词源:
The word "firebomb" is a compound word, combining "fire" (meaning "burning") and "bomb" (an explosive device).  Its usage emerged in the mid-20th century, coinciding with the widespread use of incendiary bombs during World War II.
