reliberated:/ˌriːˈlɪbəreɪtɪd/| adj.|重新解放的；再次解放的|  The word "reliberated" means freed again or set free a second time. It implies a previous liberation followed by a subsequent recapture and then a further release.  This word is relatively uncommon and often found in specific historical or political contexts. |例句：After years of oppression, the people were reliberated from the tyrannical regime.  多年压迫之后，人民从暴政中再次获得解放。The reliberated territories were quickly rebuilt.  再次解放的领土迅速重建。|近义词：reclaimed, emancipated, freed, liberated|反义词：enslaved, oppressed, subjugated, imprisoned|
