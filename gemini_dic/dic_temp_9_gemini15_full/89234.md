homies:/ˈhoʊmiz/| n. |好朋友，伙伴，哥们儿 |A term of address used among close friends, typically male. It often implies a sense of shared background or experience.  |例句：My homies and I are going to the game tonight.  今晚我和我的哥们儿要去看比赛。
例句：We’re all homies here, so relax and enjoy yourself. 我们都是朋友，所以放松点，好好享受吧。|近义词：buddies, pals, friends, mates |反义词：enemies, strangers


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My homies hang out at the park every day. = 我的哥们每天都在公园里闲逛。
These homies always support each other. = 这些哥们总是互相支持。

一般过去时:
My homies went to the concert last night. = 我的哥们昨晚去了音乐会。
We were all homies back in high school. = 我们高中时都是好朋友。

现在完成时:
I've known my homies for over ten years. = 我认识我的哥们超过十年了。
My homies have helped me through tough times. = 我的哥们帮我度过了艰难的时期。

过去完成时:
I had known my homies before I moved to this city. = 在我搬到这座城市之前就认识我的哥们了。
They had been homies since childhood. = 他们从小就是好朋友。

将来时:
My homies will be at the party tomorrow. = 我的哥们明天会在派对上。
We will always be homies, no matter what. = 无论发生什么，我们都会永远是朋友。


虚拟语气:
If I had more homies, I wouldn't feel so lonely. = 如果我有更多朋友，我就不会感到那么孤独。
I wish I had more homies to hang out with. = 我希望我有更多朋友一起出去玩。

被动语态:
(This word is rarely used in the passive voice)  这个词很少用被动语态。
Homies are often seen together.  哥们经常被看到在一起。

疑问句:
Are those your homies? = 那些是你的朋友吗？
Who are your homies? = 你的朋友是谁？

否定句:
Those aren't my homies. = 那些不是我的朋友。
I don't have many homies. = 我没有很多朋友。
