nobunaga: /ˌnoʊbʊˈnɑːɡə/ | n. | 织田信长 | Oda Nobunaga (1534-1582), a powerful Japanese daimyo (feudal lord) of the late Sengoku period, known for his military tactics, ambition, and role in unifying Japan.  He is often portrayed as a ruthless yet brilliant strategist who modernized warfare in Japan.  His legacy is complex and continues to be debated by historians. |  The historical drama depicts Nobunaga's rise to power and his impact on Japanese society.  历史剧描绘了信长崛起及其对日本社会的影响。 | 近义词：daimyo, warlord | 反义词： (no direct antonym, but concepts like "peaceful ruler" or "submissive lord" could be considered opposites in context) | 名词
