monographs:/ˈmɒnəɡræfs/ | n. | 专著，论文集 | A monograph is a scholarly work of writing on a single subject or an aspect of a subject. It is typically longer and more detailed than an article, but shorter than a book.  | Example:  She wrote a monograph on the life of Jane Austen. 她写了一本关于简·奥斯汀生平的专著。


Example: He published a monograph detailing his research findings. 他发表了一篇专著，详细介绍了他的研究成果。


近义词：treatise, dissertation, study


反义词：anthology (a collection of works by multiple authors)


词性：名词
