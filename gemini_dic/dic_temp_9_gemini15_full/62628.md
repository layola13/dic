enforcer:/ɪnˈfɔːrsər/ | n. |执行者，实施者，强制执行者 | A person or thing that enforces rules, laws, or agreements.  Someone or something that makes sure rules are followed. |例句：The police are the enforcers of the law.  警察是法律的执行者。|例句：The new software acts as an enforcer of company policy. 新软件充当公司政策的执行者。|近义词：executor, implementer, agent |反义词：violator, transgressor


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The police enforces the law. = 警察执行法律。
He enforces rules strictly. = 他严格执行规章。

一般过去时:
The judge enforced the court order. = 法官强制执行了法院命令。
They enforced a new policy last year. = 他们去年实施了一项新政策。

现在完成时:
They have enforced the new regulations since January. = 自一月以来，他们一直在执行新的规章。
The government has enforced stricter penalties for violations. = 政府对违规行为实施了更严格的处罚。

过去完成时:
The company had enforced the dress code before the meeting. = 公司在会议之前已经执行了着装规范。
She had enforced the rule many times before he finally obeyed. = 在他最终服从之前，她已经多次执行了这项规定。

将来时:
They will enforce the new law next month. = 他们下个月将执行新法律。
The court will enforce its decision. = 法院将执行其判决。

虚拟语气:
If they enforced the rules more strictly, there would be less crime. = 如果他们更严格地执行规章，犯罪就会减少。
I wish the government would enforce environmental regulations more effectively. = 我希望政府能更有效地执行环境法规。

被动语态:
The new rules are enforced by the security guards. = 新规章由保安人员执行。
The law was enforced strictly. = 法律被严格执行。

疑问句:
Who enforces these regulations? = 谁执行这些规章？
How will the new policy be enforced? = 新政策将如何执行？

否定句:
The school doesn't enforce the dress code. = 学校不执行着装规范。
The government failed to enforce the law effectively. = 政府未能有效执行法律。
