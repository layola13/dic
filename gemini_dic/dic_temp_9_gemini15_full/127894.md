neogrammarian:/ˈniːoʊˌɡræmɛəriən/| n. |新语法学家；新语法学派成员 |A member of a school of 19th-century linguists who emphasized the regularity of sound change in language evolution.  They believed that sound changes operate consistently across all words containing a particular sound, with few exceptions. |The Neogrammarians' work revolutionized the study of historical linguistics. |近义词：historical linguist |反义词：None specifically applicable.
