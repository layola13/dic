expediment:/ɪkˈspɛdɪmənt/ | n. | 妨碍；障碍；阻碍物 | An event, thing, or person that causes delay or difficulty; an obstacle. | The unexpected snowstorm was a major impediment to our travel plans.  意外的暴风雪严重阻碍了我们的旅行计划。| obstacle, hindrance, obstruction | assistance, help, facilitation | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The rocks are an impediment to the construction. = 这些岩石是施工的障碍。
This rule is an impediment to progress. = 这条规则阻碍了进步。


一般过去时:
The heavy rain was a major impediment to the game. = 大雨严重阻碍了比赛。
Lack of funds was a significant impediment to the project. = 资金不足是该项目的一大障碍。


现在完成时:
The bureaucratic processes have been a major impediment to efficiency. = 官僚程序一直是效率的一大障碍。
Numerous challenges have been impediments to our success. = 许多挑战都阻碍了我们的成功。


过去完成时:
The broken bridge had been an impediment to travel for months. = 那座断桥几个月来一直阻碍交通。
Previous failures had been impediments to their confidence. = 之前的失败已经阻碍了他们的信心。


将来时:
The expected strike will be a major impediment to production. = 预计中的罢工将严重阻碍生产。
Budget cuts will be an impediment to future research. = 预算削减将阻碍未来的研究。


虚拟语气:
If there were no impediments, we would finish the project on time. = 如果没有障碍，我们会按时完成项目。
I wish there were no impediments to our plans. = 我希望我们的计划没有障碍。


被动语态:
The project was impeded by unexpected delays. = 该项目因意外延误而受阻。
Our progress is being impeded by a lack of resources. = 我们的进展因缺乏资源而受到阻碍。


疑问句:
What are the main impediments to success? = 成功的主要障碍是什么？
Are there any impediments to your plan? = 你的计划有什么障碍吗？


否定句:
There were no impediments to their journey. = 他们的旅程没有任何障碍。
The smooth road presented no impediments to the driver. = 平坦的道路没有给司机带来任何阻碍。
