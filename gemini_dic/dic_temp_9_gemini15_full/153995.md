prefix:/ˈpriːfɪks/| n.|前缀|A prefix is a group of letters placed before the root of a word to modify its meaning.  Prefixes are often used to create new words or to change the grammatical function of a word.|例句：The prefix "un-" changes the meaning of "happy" to "unhappy". 前缀“un-”将“happy”的意思改为“unhappy”。|近义词：affix, foreword |反义词：suffix
