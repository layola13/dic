puttering:/ˈpʌtəriŋ/| v.| 慢吞吞地忙活；发出轻微的噼啪声 |  to do something slowly and inefficiently, often in a pleasant way; to make a series of soft popping or sputtering sounds. |例句：He spent the afternoon puttering around in the garden. 他在花园里慢吞吞地忙活了一下午。

例句：The old engine was puttering along, emitting a faint sputtering sound.  旧发动机慢吞吞地运转着，发出轻微的噼啪声。


近义词：fussing, pottering, tinkering, dawdling, fiddling


反义词：rushing, hurrying, speeding


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He putters around the house every Saturday. = 他每个星期六都在房子周围慢吞吞地忙活。
She putters in her garden every morning. = 她每天早上都在花园里慢吞吞地忙活。


一般过去时:
He puttered around the garage all afternoon. = 他整个下午都在车库里慢吞吞地忙活。
She puttered with her knitting for hours. = 她花了几个小时慢吞吞地织毛衣。


现在完成时:
He has been puttering around the garden all morning. = 他整个上午都在花园里慢吞吞地忙活。
She has puttered away at her project for weeks. = 她已经花了好几周时间慢吞吞地做她的项目了。


过去完成时:
He had been puttering around the house for hours before she arrived. = 在她到达之前，他已经在房子周围慢吞吞地忙活了好几个小时。
She had puttered with the recipe for days before finally baking the cake. = 在最终烘焙蛋糕之前，她已经花了好几天时间慢吞吞地研究食谱了。


将来时:
He will be puttering in his workshop tomorrow. = 他明天将在他的工作室里慢吞吞地忙活。
She will putter around the flowerbeds all afternoon. = 她整个下午都会在花坛周围慢吞吞地忙活。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would spend the day puttering in my garden. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会在花园里慢吞吞地忙活一整天。
I wish I could spend the afternoon just puttering around. = 我希望我能花整个下午慢吞吞地忙活。


被动语态: (Puttering is rarely used in the passive voice.)  慢吞吞地忙活很少用被动语态。
The old car was being puttered along the road. = 旧车正慢吞吞地沿着路行驶。(This is an unusual usage)


疑问句:
What are you puttering around with? = 你在慢吞吞地忙活什么？
Is he still puttering in the garage? = 他还在车库里慢吞吞地忙活吗？


否定句:
He wasn't puttering around; he was working hard. = 他不是在慢吞吞地忙活；他是在努力工作。
She didn't spend the day puttering; she had important things to do. = 她没有花一整天时间慢吞吞地忙活；她有重要的事情要做。

词源:
The word "putter" is of uncertain origin, possibly an onomatopoeic word imitating the sound of a soft engine or the activity of gentle work.  The verb form "puttering" developed naturally from the noun.
