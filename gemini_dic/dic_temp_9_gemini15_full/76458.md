gairfowl: /ˈɡɛərfaʊl/ | n. | 海雀 | A seabird of the family Alcidae, characterized by its short wings and diving ability.  Gairfowl are known for their dense plumage and compact bodies, which help them to withstand the cold waters of the North Atlantic. They are also known for their unique breeding habits and social behavior. Many species are now endangered or extinct due to overhunting and habitat loss. | The great auk, a type of gairfowl, was hunted to extinction in the 19th century.  | 近义词：auk, seabird | 反义词：None  |  名词
