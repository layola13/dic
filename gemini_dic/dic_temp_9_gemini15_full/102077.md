isophenomenal: /ˌaɪsoʊfɪˈnɒmɪnəl/ | adj. | 同现象的；同感现象的 |  Relating to or denoting phenomena that are subjectively similar despite objective differences.  It refers to experiences that feel the same, even if the underlying causes are different. | Example:  The pain from a migraine and the pain from a toothache, while having different physiological origins, might be isophenomenal for the patient.  | 近义词: subjectively similar, phenomenologically equivalent | 反义词: objectively different, dissimilar
