inevitable:/ɪˈnɛvɪtəbl/ | adj. | 不可避免的，必然的 | Certain to happen; unavoidable.  |例句：The accident was inevitable given the bad weather and the driver's recklessness.  这场事故在恶劣天气和驾驶员的鲁莽下是不可避免的。
例句：Failure is inevitable if you don't put in the effort.  如果你不努力，失败是不可避免的。|近义词：unavoidable, inescapable, certain, sure |反义词：avoidable, preventable, unlikely


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The outcome is inevitable. = 结果是不可避免的。
His success seems inevitable. = 他的成功似乎是不可避免的。

一般过去时:
The war was inevitable. = 战争是不可避免的。
Their breakup was inevitable. = 他们的分手是不可避免的。

现在完成时:
It has become inevitable that they will merge. = 他们将合并已成为不可避免的事实。
It has become inevitable that he will resign. = 他辞职已成定局。


过去完成时:
It had become inevitable that the company would fail. = 公司倒闭已成定局。
It had become inevitable that the project would be cancelled. = 项目取消已成定局。


将来时:

It will be inevitable that technology will advance. = 科技进步是不可避免的。

虚拟语气:
It would have been inevitable that they would lose. = 他们输掉比赛是不可避免的。
Even if he tried harder, failure would have been inevitable. = 即使他更加努力，失败也是不可避免的。

否定句:
