drawdown:/ˈdrɔːdaʊn/| n. v.|下降；减少；提取（资金）；耗尽|The reduction in the value of an asset from its peak to a trough.  It also refers to the withdrawal of funds from an account or the depletion of a resource.|例句：The drawdown in the stock market was significant.  股市的下跌幅度很大。  The company experienced a drawdown in its cash reserves. 这家公司经历了现金储备的减少。|近义词：decline, decrease, reduction, depletion|反义词：increase, rise, growth


USAGE->

The value of the investment experiences regular drawdowns. = 这项投资的价值会定期下跌。
He regularly makes drawdowns from his retirement account. = 他定期从退休账户中提取资金。
