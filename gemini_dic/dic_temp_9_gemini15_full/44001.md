criminalize:/ˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪz/| vt.|使犯罪化；定为犯罪|To declare something illegal; to make something a crime.|The government decided to criminalize the possession of unregistered firearms. 政府决定将持有未注册的枪支定为犯罪。|They are trying to criminalize homelessness.他们试图将无家可归者定为犯罪。|legalize, outlaw|decriminalize, legalize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government criminalizes drug use. = 政府将吸毒定为犯罪。
They criminalize certain forms of protest. = 他们将某些形式的抗议定为犯罪。

一般过去时:
The country criminalized same-sex marriage. = 该国过去将同性婚姻定为犯罪。
They criminalized free speech. = 他们过去将言论自由定为犯罪。

现在完成时:
Many countries have criminalized human trafficking. = 许多国家已经将人口贩运定为犯罪。
Society has criminalized certain behaviors. = 社会已经将某些行为定为犯罪。

过去完成时:
They had criminalized dissent before the revolution. = 他们在革命前已经将异议定为犯罪。
The regime had criminalized opposition parties. = 该政权已经将反对党定为犯罪。

将来时:
They will criminalize hate speech. = 他们将把仇恨言论定为犯罪。
The new law will criminalize unauthorized data collection. = 新法律将把未经授权的数据收集定为犯罪。

虚拟语气:
If they criminalized that behavior, there would be less violence. = 如果他们将那种行为定为犯罪，暴力就会减少。
I wish they would criminalize online harassment. = 我希望他们能将网络骚扰定为犯罪。

被动语态:
Drug use was criminalized in many countries. = 在许多国家，吸毒被定为犯罪。
Certain activities are being criminalized under the new law. = 在新法律下，某些活动正在被定为犯罪。

疑问句:
Will they criminalize this activity? = 他们会将这项活动定为犯罪吗？

否定句:
The government did not criminalize peaceful protest. = 政府没有将和平抗议定为犯罪。
They haven't criminalized that behavior yet. = 他们还没有将那种行为定为犯罪。
