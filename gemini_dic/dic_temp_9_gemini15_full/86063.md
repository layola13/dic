hear of:/hɪər ɒv/ | phrasal verb | 听说，听说过 | To have heard about something or someone.  It implies a less direct or detailed knowledge than "know". | I've heard of that band, but I haven't listened to their music. 我听说过那个乐队，但我还没听过他们的音乐。|  know about, have heard about | be unfamiliar with


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I hear of new restaurants opening all the time. = 我经常听说有新的餐馆开张。
They hear of this event through social media. = 他们通过社交媒体听说这件事。


一般过去时:
I heard of him through a mutual friend. = 我通过一个共同的朋友听说过他。
She heard of the accident on the news. = 她从新闻上听说这起事故。


现在完成时:
Have you heard of that new technology? = 你听说过那项新技术吗？
They have heard of the potential dangers. = 他们听说过潜在的危险。


过去完成时:
I had heard of the artist before I saw his work. = 在我看到他的作品之前，我就听说过这位艺术家了。
She had heard of the problem, but hadn't encountered it herself. = 她听说过这个问题，但自己并没有遇到过。


将来时:
I think you'll hear of him again soon. = 我想你很快就会再次听说他。
We will hear of the results next week. = 我们下周将听到结果。


虚拟语气:
If I had heard of the opportunity earlier, I would have applied. = 如果我早点听说这个机会，我就申请了。
I wish I had heard of this program sooner. = 我希望我早点知道这个项目。


被动语态:
The company is rarely heard of outside the industry. = 这家公司很少被业界以外的人听说。
His name was rarely heard of in those days. = 那些日子里很少有人听说过他的名字。


疑问句:
Have you ever heard of quantum physics? = 你听说过量子物理学吗？
Have you heard of this restaurant before? = 你以前听说过这家餐馆吗？


否定句:
I haven't heard of that band before. = 我以前没听说过那个乐队。
They hadn't heard of the changes in policy. = 他们没有听说过政策的变化。


PHRASE->
hear of something new = 听说新事物
hear of a scandal = 听说丑闻
hear of a person's achievements = 听说某人的成就
hear of a place = 听说一个地方
never heard of it = 从未听说过
