crumblies:/ˈkrʌmbliz/| n.|碎屑；易碎的东西|crumbly things,  easily broken into small pieces.|The cake was delicious, but a little crumbly.  蛋糕很好吃，但是有点碎。|近义词：friable, brittle|反义词：solid, firm|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:

The soil is crumbly and easy to work with. = 这土壤很松软，易于耕作。


一般过去时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

The old bridge will likely crumble under the weight. = 这座旧桥很可能承受不住重量而倒塌。


虚拟语气:
If the cake were less crumbly, it would be easier to eat. = 如果蛋糕不那么碎，就更容易吃了。
I wish the soil weren't so crumbly; it's difficult to plant in. = 我希望土壤不要这么松软；很难种植。


被动语态:

The building was crumbled by the earthquake. = 这座建筑被地震摧毁了。


疑问句:
Is the cake crumbly? = 蛋糕很碎吗？

否定句:
The soil isn't crumbly; it's very compact. = 土壤不松软；它很紧实。
The biscuit wasn't crumbly at all; it was quite firm. = 饼干一点也不松脆；它相当坚固。
