firebreaks: /ˈfaɪərbreɪks/ | n. |防火线，防火隔离带 | A firebreak is a gap made in vegetation or other combustible material to stop or slow the spread of a wildfire.  It acts as a barrier, preventing flames from jumping across it.  Firebreaks are often created by clearing vegetation, plowing furrows, or using other methods to create a wide, bare area. |Example:  The firefighters created a firebreak to contain the wildfire. 消防员开辟了一条防火线来控制野火。

The construction of firebreaks around the forest helped prevent the spread of the fire. 森林周围修建的防火线有助于阻止火势蔓延。|近义词：fire line, control line |反义词：fuel, kindling
