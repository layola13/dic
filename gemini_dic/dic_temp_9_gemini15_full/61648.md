emis:/ˈiːmɪs/| n. |排放物，发射；散发 | Emission refers to the act of releasing something, such as gas, light, or energy, or the substance released.  It often implies a release into the environment. |Example: Greenhouse gas emissions are a major concern for climate change.  温室气体排放是气候变化的主要担忧。  The sun's emission of light and heat is essential for life on Earth. 太阳的光和热的散发对于地球上的生命至关重要。|近义词：discharge, release, emanation |反义词：absorption, intake |名词

一般现在时:
The factory emits pollutants into the air. = 工厂向空气中排放污染物。
The sun emits light and heat. = 太阳散发光和热。


一般过去时:
The volcano emitted a large cloud of ash. = 火山喷发出大量的火山灰。
The car emitted a loud noise. = 汽车发出很大的噪音。


现在完成时:
The company has emitted more pollutants than allowed. = 该公司排放的污染物超过了允许的限量。
Scientists have emitted a warning about the dangers of climate change. = 科学家们就气候变化的危险发出了警告。


过去完成时:
The factory had emitted tons of pollutants before the regulations were implemented. = 在实施法规之前，这家工厂已经排放了数吨污染物。
The researchers had emitted a preliminary report on their findings. = 研究人员已经发布了关于他们研究结果的初步报告。


将来时:
The new power plant will emit less carbon dioxide. = 新发电厂将排放更少的二氧化碳。
They predict the volcano will emit more lava in the coming days. = 他们预测火山将在未来几天喷发出更多的熔岩。


虚拟语气:
If the factory emitted less pollution, the air would be cleaner. = 如果工厂排放的污染物更少，空气就会更清洁。
It is essential that the country emits fewer greenhouse gases. = 该国减少温室气体排放至关重要。


被动语态:
Pollutants are emitted by many industrial processes. = 许多工业过程都会排放污染物。
A warning about the risks was emitted by the government. = 政府发布了关于风险的警告。


疑问句:

否定句:
The new technology does not emit harmful gases. = 新技术不会排放有害气体。
The engine did not emit any smoke. = 发动机没有排放任何烟雾。
