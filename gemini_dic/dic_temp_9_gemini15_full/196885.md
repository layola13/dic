shackling: /ˈʃækəlɪŋ/ | v. n. | 束缚；镣铐；枷锁 | The act of restraining someone or something with shackles or chains;  the state of being restrained in this way.  It can also refer to something that restricts freedom or progress. |

英文解析:Shackling involves binding or fastening someone or something with shackles (metal restraints for the ankles or wrists) or chains.  Figuratively, it can describe any restriction of freedom, movement, or progress.

例句:
The prisoner was shackled to the wall. = 囚犯被铐在墙上。

They felt shackled by the rules and regulations. = 他们感到受到规章制度的束缚。


近义词:  restraining, confining, binding, fettering, chaining, restricting

反义词: liberating, freeing, releasing, unbinding


词性: Verb (transitive), Noun
