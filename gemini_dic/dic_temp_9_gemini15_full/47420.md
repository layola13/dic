deadlocks:/ˈdedlɒks/ | n. |死锁，僵局 |A deadlock is a situation where two or more processes are blocked indefinitely, waiting for each other to release resources that they need.  In a broader sense, it refers to any situation where progress is impossible because of mutual opposition. |例句：The negotiations reached a deadlock, with neither side willing to compromise.  谈判陷入僵局，双方都不愿妥协。

The peace talks ended in a deadlock. 和谈陷入僵局。|近义词：stalemate, impasse, standstill |反义词：resolution, breakthrough, agreement
