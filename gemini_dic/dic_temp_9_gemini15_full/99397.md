interdiffusion:/ˌɪntərˌdɪˈfjuːʒən/| n. |互扩散|The mutual diffusion of atoms or molecules between two or more phases, materials, or regions.  This process typically occurs when two substances are in contact and their atoms or molecules move across the interface, leading to a gradual mixing of the components. Interdiffusion is driven by the difference in chemical potential between the regions, aiming to achieve equilibrium.|Example: The interdiffusion of copper and nickel atoms in a copper-nickel alloy leads to a gradual change in composition from pure copper to pure nickel across the sample.|近义词：mutual diffusion, diffusional mixing|反义词：separation, segregation
