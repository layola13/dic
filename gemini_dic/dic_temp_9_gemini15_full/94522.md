imposed:/ɪmˈpoʊzd/ | vt. |强加；施加；征收 | to force someone or something to accept something, especially a belief, rule, or tax |例句：The government imposed a new tax on luxury goods. 政府对奢侈品征收新税。|例句：They imposed their will on the weaker nations. 他们把自己的意志强加于较弱的国家。|近义词：enforce, inflict, levy |反义词：remove, abolish

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government imposes new regulations every year. = 政府每年都实施新的规章制度。
He imposes his opinions on others. = 他把自己的观点强加于他人。

一般过去时:
They imposed a curfew after the riot. = 暴乱后他们实施了宵禁。
The judge imposed a heavy fine on the defendant. = 法官对被告处以巨额罚款。

现在完成时:
The company has imposed strict new safety measures. = 公司已经实施了严格的新安全措施。
She has imposed her ideas on the project. = 她已将自己的想法强加于这个项目。

过去完成时:
They had imposed a travel ban before the pandemic worsened. = 大流行恶化之前，他们已经实施了旅行禁令。
The dictator had imposed his rule through fear. = 独裁者通过恐惧来统治。


将来时:
The authorities will impose stricter penalties for this crime. = 当局将对这项罪行处以更严厉的惩罚。
They will impose a new set of rules next month. = 他们下个月将实施一套新的规则。


虚拟语气:
If they imposed stricter rules, the situation might improve. = 如果他们实施更严格的规定，情况可能会好转。
I wish they hadn't imposed such a heavy burden on us. = 我真希望他们没有给我们施加如此沉重的负担。


被动语态:
New taxes were imposed on small businesses. = 对小型企业征收了新税。
A new dress code was imposed by the school. = 学校实施了新的着装规定。


疑问句:
Will new restrictions be imposed on our travel? = 我们的出行将会受到新的限制吗？
What kind of penalties will be imposed? = 将会处以什么样的处罚？


否定句:
The government did not impose any new taxes this year. = 政府今年没有征收任何新税。
They did not impose their views on the discussion. = 他们没有把自己的观点强加于讨论。


PHRASE->
impose on:  强加于
impose a tax: 征税
impose restrictions: 施加限制
impose a penalty: 处罚
impose oneself: 自作主张
