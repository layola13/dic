rule:/ruːl/|n. v.|规则，规定；统治，支配|A rule is a statement that tells you what is or is not allowed in a particular situation.  To rule means to govern or control.|The school has a strict rule against chewing gum. 这所学校有严格的禁止嚼口香糖的规定。|The king ruled the country for many years. 国王统治这个国家多年。|regulation, law, guideline|anarchy, chaos|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rule states that we must wear uniforms. = 规则规定我们必须穿制服。
What rules govern this game? = 什么规则支配着这个游戏？


一般过去时:
The king ruled wisely and justly. = 国王明智而公正地统治。
They ruled that the defendant was guilty. = 他们裁定被告有罪。


现在完成时:
The new rules have been in effect for a month. = 新规已生效一个月了。
They have ruled against the motion. = 他们否决了这项动议。


过去完成时:
The old rules had been in place for decades. = 旧规则已经实施了几十年了。
Before the trial, they had already ruled out several possibilities. = 在审判之前，他们已经排除了几种可能性。


将来时:
The new regulations will rule out any further delays. = 新规将排除任何进一步的延误。
The committee will rule on the matter next week. = 委员会下周将对此事做出裁决。


虚拟语气:
If there were no rules, chaos would ensue. = 如果没有规则，就会出现混乱。
I wish the rules were simpler. = 我希望规则更简单一些。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

They did not rule out the possibility of an accident. = 他们没有排除发生事故的可能性。
