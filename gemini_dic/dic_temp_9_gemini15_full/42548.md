countermovements:/ˈkaʊntərˌmuːvmənts/| n. |反运动；对立运动|A countermovement is a social movement that arises in opposition to another social movement.  It often challenges the values, goals, or methods of the original movement.  Countermovements can be reactionary, seeking to restore a previous social order, or they can be progressive, offering alternative solutions to the problems addressed by the initial movement.  Examples include the anti-suffrage movement in response to the women's suffrage movement, or the anti-globalization movement as a response to globalization. |The anti-war movement saw the rise of several countermovements supporting military intervention.|近义词：opposition movements, counter-revolutions,  reactions |反义词：mainstream movements, dominant movements|名词
