re-rise:/ˌriːˈraɪz/| v.|再次上升；重新兴起|To rise again; to revive or reappear after a decline.|The stock market is expected to re-rise after the recent downturn.  股票市场预计将在近期低迷后再次上涨。|The phoenix re-rises from the ashes.  凤凰涅槃。|rise, ascend, revive|fall, decline, descend|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The sun re-rises every morning. = 太阳每天早上都会升起。
The company re-rises after the financial crisis. = 公司在金融危机后重新崛起。


一般过去时:
The bird re-rose into the sky. = 鸟儿再次飞上了天空。
The economy re-rose after a period of stagnation. = 经济在一段停滞期后再次复苏。


现在完成时:
The river has re-risen after the heavy rain. = 大雨过后，河水再次上涨。
His career has re-risen after a long break. = 他在长时间休息后事业再次兴起。


过去完成时:
The water level had re-risen before we could evacuate. = 我们撤离之前，水位已经再次上涨。
The population had re-risen to its pre-war level. = 人口已经恢复到战前水平。


将来时:
The prices will re-rise next year. = 明年价格将会再次上涨。
The band will re-rise to fame after their new album. = 乐队的新专辑发布后将会再次成名。


虚拟语气:
If the economy re-rose, many people would find jobs. = 如果经济复苏，许多人就能找到工作。
I wish the sun would re-rise tomorrow. = 我希望明天太阳能再次升起。


被动语态:
The issue was re-risen in the meeting. = 这个问题在会议上再次被提出。
The matter will be re-risen at the next session. = 这件事将在下一次会议上再次提出。


疑问句:
Will the prices re-rise soon? = 价格很快就会再次上涨吗？
Did the river re-rise after the flood? = 洪水过后河水再次上涨了吗？


否定句:
The sun did not re-rise immediately after the eclipse. = 日食之后太阳并没有立即再次升起。
The stock prices have not re-risen yet. = 股价还没有再次上涨。
