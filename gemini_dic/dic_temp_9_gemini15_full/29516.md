catastrophist:/kəˈtræstrəfɪst/ | n. | 灾难论者；灾变论者 | A catastrophist is someone who believes that sudden, large-scale events, rather than gradual change, are the primary drivers of geological and biological change. They often emphasize the role of catastrophic events like asteroid impacts or massive volcanic eruptions in shaping the Earth's history and the evolution of life. | Example: Some catastrophists argue that the extinction of the dinosaurs was caused by a large asteroid impact.  一些灾变论者认为恐龙的灭绝是由一次大型小行星撞击造成的。|近义词：catastrophism proponent, doomsayer (in some contexts)|反义词：gradualist, uniformitarian
