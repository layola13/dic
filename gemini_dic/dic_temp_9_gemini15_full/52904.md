dimming:/ˈdɪmɪŋ/| v. n. |变暗；使变暗；变模糊；减弱|The act of making something less bright or intense; the process of becoming less bright or intense.  It can also refer to a gradual decline or weakening.|例句：The lights began dimming. 灯光开始变暗。

The sound of the music was slowly dimming. 音乐声慢慢变弱。


近义词：fading, weakening, lessening, obscuring |反义词：brightening, intensifying, strengthening |词性:动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun is dimming behind the clouds.  = 太阳被云遮挡而变暗了。
He dims the lights before watching a movie. = 他看电影前会把灯调暗。


一般过去时:
The lights dimmed suddenly. = 灯光突然变暗了。
She dimmed the screen to protect her eyes. = 她调暗屏幕以保护眼睛。


现在完成时:
The star has dimmed considerably over the centuries. = 这颗星星几个世纪以来已经暗淡了许多。
The pain has dimmed somewhat since taking the medication. = 服药后疼痛有所减轻。


过去完成时:
The city had dimmed its lights in response to the blackout. = 该市为了应对停电而调暗了灯光。
The memory had dimmed with the passage of time. = 随着时间的推移，记忆已经变得模糊。


将来时:
The lights will dim automatically at sunset. = 日落时灯光会自动变暗。
The economy is expected to dim in the coming year. = 预计经济将在来年减弱。


虚拟语气:
If the lights dimmed any further, we would not be able to see. = 如果灯光再暗一些，我们就看不清了。
I wish the pain would dim a little. = 我希望疼痛能减轻一点。


被动语态:

The enthusiasm was dimmed by the bad news. = 坏消息使热情减弱了。


疑问句:

Is the signal dimming? = 信号在减弱吗？


否定句:
The lights did not dim. = 灯光没有变暗。
The prospect did not dim his hopes. = 这前景并没有使他灰心。
