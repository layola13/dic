guildry:/ˈɡɪldri/| n. |行会；同业公会；协会|A guildry is an association of craftsmen or merchants, often with a specific trade or craft.  It's a historical term, often referring to medieval organizations.  Guildries regulated their members' activities, set standards for quality, and sometimes held monopolies on certain trades. | Example: The silversmiths' guildry controlled the production and sale of silverware in the town.  这个银匠行会控制着城镇里银器的生产和销售。|近义词：guild, association, corporation, fraternity |反义词：None specifically.
