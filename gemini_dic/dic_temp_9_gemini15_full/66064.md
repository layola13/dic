exhortation: /ˌɛgzɔːrˈteɪʃən/ | n. |劝告；鼓励；训诫 |The act of exhorting; earnest advice or劝告; a strong urging.  It often implies a moral or religious tone, urging someone to do what is right or good. |例句：The priest's exhortation to his congregation was met with silence. 牧师对会众的劝告受到了沉默的回应。

例句：His exhortation to us to work harder inspired us to greater effort. 他鼓励我们更加努力工作的劝告激励了我们付出更大的努力。


近义词：encouragement, urging, admonition, advice, pleading

反义词：discouragement, dissuasion


词源:  From Middle English *exhortacioun, from Old French exhortation, from Latin exhortationem (nominative exhortatio), from exhortari "to encourage," from ex- "out" + hortari "to encourage," from hortus "garden, enclosure."  The sense development is thought to be from the idea of "calling forth" or "stirring up" from within, like cultivating a garden.
