self-accelerating: /ˌsɛlf əkˈsɛləreɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 自加速的，自行加速的 |  Describes a process or reaction that increases its own rate over time without external intervention.  This often happens due to the products of the reaction catalyzing further reaction. | 例句：The fire became a self-accelerating inferno, fueled by the wind.  这场大火在风的助长下，变成了自燃的烈火。


The self-accelerating decomposition of the chemicals led to an explosion. 化学物质的自加速分解导致了爆炸。


近义词：autocatalytic, self-sustaining | 反义词：self-inhibiting, self-regulating


词源:  The word is a compound of "self-" (meaning "by itself" or "of itself") + "accelerating" (the present participle of "accelerate," meaning "to increase in speed or rate").  The root of "accelerate" is the Latin word "accelerare,"  from "ad-" (to) + "celer" (swift).
