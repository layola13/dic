rockabilly:/ˌrɒkəˈbɪli/ | adj. n. |摇滚乡村音乐；摇滚乡村风格的 |A genre of music that combines rock and roll with country and western music.  It originated in the United States in the 1950s.  The term itself is a portmanteau of "rock and roll" and "hillbilly."  The music is characterized by a strong backbeat, often featuring a prominent upright bass, and lyrics that frequently dealt with themes of romance, rebellion, and teenage life. |例句：The band played a great set of rockabilly tunes.  乐队演奏了一套很棒的摇滚乡村音乐。

The energetic guitarist was known for his rockabilly style.  这位精力充沛的吉他手以其摇滚乡村风格而闻名。|近义词：rock and roll, rock 'n' roll, country |反义词：classical, jazz
