ex post facto:/ˌɛkspōstˈfæktō/| adj.| 事后制定的；追溯效力的 |  Relating to or denoting a law that applies to acts committed before its enactment.  It typically refers to a law that retroactively changes the legal consequences (or status) of actions that were committed, or relationships that existed, before the enactment of the law. | Example: The ex post facto law was challenged in court.  事后制定的法律受到了法庭的质疑。The government passed an ex post facto law punishing individuals for actions that were legal at the time they were committed. 政府通过了一项追溯效力的法律，惩罚那些在其行为发生时是合法的个人。| 近义词：retroactive, retrospective | 反义词：prospective
