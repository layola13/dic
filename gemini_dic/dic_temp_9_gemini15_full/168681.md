procrastinative: /prəˈkræstɪneɪtɪv/ | adj. | 拖延的，耽搁的 |  Characterized by procrastination; tending to postpone or delay.  It describes a person or behavior that is habitually delaying or postponing actions. |


例句：

He has a procrastinative nature, always leaving things until the last minute.  = 他性格拖延，总是把事情拖到最后一刻。

Her procrastinative approach to studying led to poor grades. = 她学习拖延的作法导致成绩差。


近义词：dilatory, delaying, tardy, slow, deferring


反义词：prompt, punctual, timely, expeditious


词源:

The word "procrastinative" is derived from the verb "procrastinate," which comes from the Late Latin word *procrastinare*.  This word itself is a combination of the prefix *pro-* (forward) and *crastinus* (of tomorrow).  Therefore, the literal meaning is "to put off until tomorrow."
