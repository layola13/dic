loiters:/ˈlɔɪtərz/| v.|闲逛；徘徊；磨蹭|To stand or wait around idly or without purpose.|例句：He loiters on the street corner every afternoon. 他每天下午都在街角闲逛。|例句：Don't loiter; we're late! 不要磨蹭，我们迟到了！|近义词：linger, dawdle, hang around, idle|反义词：hurry, rush


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He loiters around the park every evening. = 他每天晚上都在公园里闲逛。
They loiter in the shopping mall for hours. = 他们在购物中心闲逛了好几个小时。

一般过去时:
She loitered near the bus stop, waiting for her friend. = 她徘徊在公交车站附近，等她的朋友。
The children loitered by the river, throwing stones. = 孩子们在河边磨蹭，扔石头。

现在完成时:
He has loitered around here for too long. = 他在这里闲逛太久了。
They have loitered outside the cinema, waiting for the movie to finish. = 他们在电影院外面磨蹭，等电影结束。

过去完成时:
He had loitered in the library for hours before he finally found the book. = 他在图书馆磨蹭了好几个小时才最终找到那本书。
She had loitered on the way home, causing her to be late for dinner. = 她回家路上磨蹭，导致她晚饭迟到了。

将来时:
I will loiter around the city center after work. = 我下班后会在市中心闲逛。
They will loiter in the cafe for a while before going home. = 他们会在咖啡馆里闲逛一会儿再回家。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't loitered, I wouldn't have missed the train. = 如果我没有磨蹭，我就不会错过火车了。
I wish I hadn't loitered; I would have been on time. = 我真希望我没有磨蹭；我本来可以准时的。

被动语态:  (Loiter is rarely used passively)
The streets were often loitered in by young people. = 街道上经常有年轻人闲逛。(This sounds unnatural)
He was seen loitering near the bank. = 人们看见他在银行附近闲逛。(Focus is on being seen, not the act of loitering passively)

疑问句:
Does he often loiter in the streets? = 他经常在街上闲逛吗？
Were they loitering near the crime scene? = 他们在犯罪现场附近闲逛吗？

否定句:
He doesn't loiter around here anymore. = 他不再在这里闲逛了。
They didn't loiter; they went straight to the meeting. = 他们没有磨蹭；他们直接去了会议。


PHRASE->
loiter around = 闲逛
loiter about = 徘徊
loiter by = 在...附近徘徊
loiter near = 在...附近闲逛
loiter in = 在...里面闲逛
