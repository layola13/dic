dinarchy:/ˈdaɪnɑːrki/| n. |二元统治；双首统治|A system of government in which two people rule jointly, often with equal power.  This term often implies a division of power, possibly with some inherent conflict or tension.  It can also refer to a system where two distinct groups or entities share power.|Example: The small island nation operated under a dinarchy, with the chief elder and the high priest sharing equal authority.|近义词：dual monarchy, diarchy|反义词：monarchy, autocracy
