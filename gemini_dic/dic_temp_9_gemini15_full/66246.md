exclave:/ˈekskleɪv/| n.|飞地，海外领地|A portion of a country geographically separated from the main part by surrounding territory of another country or countries.  An exclave is a piece of land that is politically part of a country but is geographically separated from it by the territory of another country.|例句：Kaliningrad is a Russian exclave between Poland and Lithuania. 加里宁格勒是位于波兰和立陶宛之间的一个俄罗斯飞地。|例句：The territory is an exclave, completely surrounded by hostile nations. 这块领土是一个飞地，完全被敌对国家包围。|近义词：enclave (but with opposite meaning), outlier, detached territory|反义词: mainland, contiguous territory|名词
