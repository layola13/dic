reign:/reɪn/| v.|统治，支配，盛行|To rule as a monarch; to be prevalent or dominant.|例句：The queen reigned for over 50 years. 女王统治了50多年。|例句：Terror reigned in the streets after the earthquake. 地震后，恐怖统治着街道。|近义词：rule, govern, prevail|反义词：be ruled, be governed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The king reigns over the kingdom. = 国王统治着王国。
His influence reigns supreme in the company. = 他在公司里的影响力至高无上。


一般过去时:
Queen Victoria reigned for 63 years. = 维多利亚女王统治了63年。
A sense of peace reigned after the storm. = 风暴过后，一种平静感弥漫开来。


现在完成时:
She has reigned for ten years. = 她已经统治了十年。
Calm has reigned since the conflict ended. = 自冲突结束后，平静就一直持续着。


过去完成时:
Before the new emperor reigned, there was chaos. = 在新皇帝统治之前，一片混乱。
Peace had reigned for many years before the war broke out. = 在战争爆发前，和平持续了很多年。


将来时:
The prince will reign after his father's death. = 王子将在父亲去世后继位。
Order will reign once the crisis is over. = 一旦危机过去，秩序就会恢复。


虚拟语气:
If he reigned wisely, his people would prosper. = 如果他英明统治，他的子民就会繁荣。
I wish the good times would reign forever. = 我希望好时光永远持续下去。


被动语态:
The country was reigned over by a just ruler. = 这个国家由一位公正的统治者统治。
The area was reigned by fear after the incident. = 事件发生后，恐惧统治着这个地区。


疑问句:
Who reigned after King Henry VIII? = 亨利八世国王之后是谁统治的？
What kind of atmosphere reigned at the party? = 晚会的气氛如何？


否定句:
The emperor did not reign long. = 这位皇帝没有统治很久。
Peace did not reign in the city that day. = 那天城市里并没有和平。


PHRASE->
