prevailers:/prɪˈveɪlərz/| n. |战胜者；获胜者；占优势者|Those who prevail; those who are victorious or dominant.  The prevailers in the competition will receive prizes.  比赛的获胜者将获得奖品。|近义词：winners, victors, champions, conquerors |反义词：losers, vanquished, defeated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The team of prevailers celebrate their victory. = 获胜的队伍庆祝他们的胜利。
The current trends prevail in the fashion industry. = 当前的潮流在时尚界占据主导地位。


一般过去时:
The prevailers of the battle marched home triumphantly. = 战役的胜利者凯旋而归。
Last year's prevailers in the market lost their position this year. = 去年市场上的赢家今年失去了他们的地位。


现在完成时:
The prevailers have claimed their rewards. = 获胜者已经领取了他们的奖励。
The new ideas have prevailed over the old ones. = 新思想已经战胜了旧思想。


过去完成时:
By the time the war ended, the prevailers had suffered heavy losses. = 当战争结束时，胜利者已经遭受了重大损失。
Before the final decision, the prevailers had already secured their advantage. = 在最终决定之前，获胜者已经巩固了他们的优势。


将来时:
The prevailers will be announced at the end of the competition. = 比赛结束后将公布获胜者。
We believe that reason will prevail in the end. = 我们相信最终理性会占上风。


虚拟语气:
If only the forces of good had prevailed! = 但愿正义的力量已经获胜！
If the prevailers had been less arrogant, the outcome might have been different. = 如果获胜者不那么傲慢，结果可能会有所不同。


被动语态:
The prizes were awarded to the prevailers. = 奖品颁发给了获胜者。
The new policy was eventually prevailed upon by the opposition. = 新政策最终被反对派所压倒。


疑问句:
Who are the prevailers in this conflict? = 这场冲突中的获胜者是谁？

否定句:
