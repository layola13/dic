literacy:/ˈlɪtərəsi/| n. |识字；读写能力；文化水平 |The ability to read and write.  It also often refers to a broader concept including the ability to understand and use information. |例句：Improving literacy rates is crucial for national development.  提高识字率对国家发展至关重要。

例句：The program aims to promote literacy among adults.  该项目旨在提高成年人的识字率。


近义词：reading ability, writing ability, education, learning |反义词：illiteracy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Literacy is important for success. = 识字对成功很重要。
Good literacy skills are essential in today's world. = 良好的读写能力在当今世界至关重要。

一般过去时:
Literacy rates were lower in the past. = 过去识字率较低。
He achieved literacy at a young age. = 他很小就学会了识字。

现在完成时:
Many countries have improved their literacy rates. = 许多国家已经提高了识字率。
She has demonstrated high levels of literacy throughout her career. = 她在整个职业生涯中都展现出高水平的识字能力。

过去完成时:
Before the program, many people had lacked literacy. = 在项目开展之前，许多人缺乏识字能力。
They had already achieved basic literacy before starting secondary school. = 他们在上中学之前就已经具备了基本的识字能力。

将来时:
We will continue to invest in literacy programs. = 我们将继续投资于识字项目。
The government will improve literacy access in rural areas. = 政府将改善农村地区的识字机会。


虚拟语气:
