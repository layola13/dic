randomization:/ˌrændəmaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |随机化，随机分配 |The process of making something random; the act of assigning subjects to different treatment groups in a way that each subject has an equal chance of being assigned to any group.  This is crucial in experiments to minimize bias and ensure that results are not skewed by pre-existing differences between groups.| 例句：Randomization is a key element of a well-designed clinical trial.  随机化是精心设计的临床试验的关键因素。|  例句：The software uses randomization to generate unique passwords. 该软件使用随机化生成唯一的密码。|近义词：random selection, random assignment |反义词：determination, pre-selection
