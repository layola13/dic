herald:/ˈhɛrəld/ | n. | 预兆；先驱；通报者 | A person or thing that announces or signals the approach of someone or something else.  |例句：The robin's song is a herald of spring.  知更鸟的歌声是春天的预兆。|例句：The new discovery heralds a new era in medicine.  这一新发现预示着医学新时代的到来。|近义词：forerunner, precursor, harbinger |反义词：epilogue, aftermath


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The newspaper heralds the upcoming election. = 报纸预告了即将到来的选举。
This discovery heralds a new age of technology. = 这一发现预示着科技新时代的到来。

一般过去时:
The church bells heralded the arrival of the king. = 教堂的钟声预告了国王的到来。
The dark clouds heralded the coming storm. = 乌云预示着暴风雨的到来。

现在完成时:
Recent studies have heralded a new understanding of the disease. = 最近的研究已经预示着对这种疾病有了新的认识。
The invention has heralded a revolution in communication. = 这项发明预示着通讯领域的革命。

过去完成时:
The falling leaves had heralded the onset of winter. = 落叶已经预示着冬天的来临。
The strange sounds had heralded the approach of danger. = 奇怪的声音已经预示着危险的临近。

将来时:
Scientists believe this new drug will herald a breakthrough in cancer treatment. = 科学家相信这种新药将预示着癌症治疗的突破。
The new policy will herald a change in the country's economic direction. = 新政策将预示着国家经济方向的转变。

虚拟语气:
If the signs were correct, they would herald a period of peace. = 如果这些迹象是正确的，它们将预示着一个和平时期。
If this discovery were true, it would herald a revolution in our understanding of the universe. = 如果这一发现是真实的，它将预示着我们对宇宙认识的一场革命。

被动语态:
The coming of spring was heralded by the blossoming of the trees. = 春天的到来以树木开花来预示。
The success of the project was heralded by the media. = 项目的成功受到了媒体的预示。

疑问句:
Does the appearance of these birds herald the arrival of warmer weather? = 这些鸟的出现是否预示着温暖天气的到来？
What events herald the start of the festival? = 什么事件预示着节日的开始？


否定句:
The quiet morning did not herald any impending trouble. = 平静的早晨并没有预示任何即将到来的麻烦。
The calm before the storm did not herald the coming chaos. = 暴风雨前的平静并没有预示着即将到来的混乱。


PHRASE->

herald a new era = 预示一个新时代
herald the beginning of = 预示…的开始
herald the end of = 预示…的结束
herald a change = 预示一个变化
