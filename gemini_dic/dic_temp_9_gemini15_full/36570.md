collarbone: /ˈkɑːləbəʊn/ /ˈkɒləbəʊn/ | n. | 锁骨 | The collarbone, also known as the clavicle, is a long bone that connects the sternum (breastbone) to the scapula (shoulder blade). It plays a vital role in shoulder movement and provides structural support to the upper body.  |  He broke his collarbone in a skiing accident. 他在滑雪事故中锁骨骨折了。  She felt a sharp pain in her collarbone. 她感到锁骨一阵剧痛。 | clavicle |  (no direct antonym, but perhaps words describing its absence or damage could be considered contextual antonyms)
