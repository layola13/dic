collide: /kəˈlaɪd/ | v. | 碰撞；冲突；抵触 | To strike or come together with force; to clash or be in conflict. |


例句：

The two cars collided at the intersection. = 两辆车在十字路口相撞。

Their opinions collided sharply on the issue. = 他们在这个问题上的意见严重冲突。


近义词：crash, bump, clash, conflict


反义词：agree, harmonize, concur


词性：动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:

The planets collide regularly. = 行星定期碰撞。

Two ideas collide in his mind. = 他的脑海中产生了两种互相冲突的想法。


一般过去时:

The trains collided violently. = 火车猛烈相撞。

Their plans collided with reality. = 他们的计划与现实相冲突。


现在完成时:

The two companies have collided in the marketplace. = 两家公司在市场上发生了冲突。

Several cars have collided on the highway today. = 今天高速公路上已经发生了好几起车祸。


过去完成时:

Before the merger, the two companies had already collided on several occasions. = 在合并之前，这两家公司已经发生过几次冲突。

By the time he arrived, the arguments had already collided. = 当他到达时，争论已经爆发了。


将来时:

The two objects will collide in a few hours. = 这两个物体将在几小时内发生碰撞。

If we don't change course, our plans will collide with theirs. = 如果我们不改变方向，我们的计划就会与他们的计划冲突。


虚拟语气:

If the two particles collided, a new element would be created. = 如果这两个粒子相撞，就会产生一种新的元素。

If their views collided less, they would get along better.  = 如果他们的观点冲突少一些，他们相处会更好。


被动语态:

The cars were collided by a speeding truck. = 车子被一辆超速的卡车撞了。

The project was nearly abandoned when two different strategies collided. = 当两种不同的策略发生冲突时，该项目差点被放弃。


疑问句:

Did the two cars collide? = 两辆车相撞了吗？

Will their opinions collide during the debate? = 他们在辩论中观点会发生冲突吗？


否定句:

The two planes did not collide. = 两架飞机没有相撞。

Their schedules did not collide this week. = 他们的日程安排本周没有冲突。
