secretiveness:/ˈsiːkrətɪvnəs/| n. |神秘；隐秘；保密性 |The quality or state of being secretive; an inclination to keep things secret. It often implies a deliberate attempt to conceal information or activities.  |例句：Her secretiveness about her past fueled speculation among her colleagues.  她的过去对她秘而不宣，引发了同事们的猜测。

例句：The secretiveness surrounding the project raised concerns about transparency.  项目的神秘性引发了人们对透明度的担忧。


近义词：mystery, confidentiality, privacy, concealment, reticence

反义词：openness, frankness, transparency, candor


词源：源自secretive (adj.) + -ness (suffix indicating a state or quality).  Secretive itself comes from secret (n.) + -ive (suffix forming adjectives). Secret ultimately traces back to Old French "secret," from Latin "secretum," a "secret thing,"  a derivative of "secreare,"  "to separate," "to set apart."
