radiometal:/ˌreɪdiouˈmɛtəl/| n. |放射性金属|A radioactive metal, typically referring to those used in nuclear medicine and industrial applications.  Radiometals are characterized by their radioactive decay, emitting particles or energy that can be used for various purposes.  They are often incorporated into targeted therapies to deliver radiation directly to cancerous cells. | Example:  Technetium-99m is a commonly used radiometal in medical imaging.  技术钪-99m是医学影像中常用的放射性金属。|近义词：radionuclide (更广义的术语，包括非金属)|反义词：non-radioactive metal
