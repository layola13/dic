handful:/ˈhændfʊl/| n. |一把，少量；难以应付的人或事| A handful is a quantity that can be grasped by one hand.  It can refer to a small number or amount of something, or to a person or thing that is difficult to manage or control. |例句：He gave me a handful of coins. 他给了我一把硬币。

例句：She found managing the unruly children a real handful. 她发现管教那些不听话的孩子真是件棘手的事。|近义词：small amount, few, handful, difficult, troublesome|反义词：large quantity, many, easily managed
