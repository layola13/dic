predescriptions:/ˌpriːdɪˈskrɪpʃənz/| n. |预先描述；预先规定|A predescription is a description or specification made in advance.  It outlines something before it exists or happens, setting expectations or providing a framework.  The term often implies a degree of formality or authority. |例句：The architect provided predescriptions of the building's design before construction began. 建筑师在施工开始前提供了建筑物设计的预先描述。

The committee issued predescriptions for the project's deliverables.  委员会发布了项目交付成果的预先规定。|近义词：predetermination, advance specification, prior description |反义词：post-description, retrospective account
