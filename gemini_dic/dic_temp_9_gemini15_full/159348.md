poisony: /ˈpɔɪzənɪ/ | adj. | 有毒的，致命的 | Containing or relating to poison; extremely harmful or unpleasant. | 例句：The snake was poisony and could kill a person with one bite. 那条蛇是有毒的，一口就能杀死人。

The plant is poisony to cattle.  这种植物对牛有毒。


近义词：toxic, venomous, virulent, deadly | 反义词：harmless, innocuous


词源:  Derived from "poison" + "-y" (suffix indicating a quality or state).  "Poison" itself has roots in Latin "potio" (a draught, drink), ultimately related to the verb "potare" (to drink).  The "-y" suffix transforms the noun "poison" into an adjective.
