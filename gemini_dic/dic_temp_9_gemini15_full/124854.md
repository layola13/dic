mucor: /ˈmjuːkɔːr/ | n. | 毛霉属 | A genus of fungi that are common in soil, decaying vegetation, and other organic matter.  They are often white or grayish in color and have a characteristic cottony appearance. Some species are opportunistic pathogens, causing infections in immunocompromised individuals. | Example:  The bread mold was identified as a species of *Mucor*.  面包霉被鉴定为毛霉属的一种。 | 近义词: Rhizopus (another genus of bread mold) | 反义词:  None specifically, as it's a genus classification.
