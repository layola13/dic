nominative:/ˈnɒmɪnətɪv/| /ˈnɑːmɪnətɪv/ | adj. n. |主格的；主格|Relating to or denoting the case of a noun or pronoun that is the subject of a verb.|The nominative case is the grammatical case used for the subject of a verb.  In English, the nominative case is primarily marked by word order rather than inflection (changes in word form).|  The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence. For example, in the sentence "The dog barked," "dog" is in the nominative case.


例句：

The cat sat on the mat. (猫坐在垫子上。)  Here, "cat" is in the nominative case because it's the subject performing the action.

She is a doctor. (她是一名医生。)  "She" is in the nominative case as the subject of the verb "is".


近义词：subjective (in some contexts)


反义词：objective, accusative, genitive, dative


词性：adjective, noun
