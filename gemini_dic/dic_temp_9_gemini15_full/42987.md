covetousness: /ˈkʌvɪtəsnəs/ | n. |贪婪；贪心；渴望 |  An excessive or inordinate desire for wealth or possessions; greed.  It often involves a strong longing for something that belongs to someone else. |例句：His covetousness led him to steal from his friends. 他的贪婪导致他偷了朋友的东西。

例句：The covetousness of the king led to the downfall of his kingdom. 国王的贪婪导致了他的王国灭亡。


近义词：greed, avarice, rapacity, envy, longing


反义词：generosity, contentment, altruism


词源:  The word "covetousness" comes from the Middle English word "coveitise," which in turn derives from the Old French word "coveitise," meaning "desire, longing." This ultimately traces back to the Latin word "cupiditas," meaning "desire, lust, greed."  The suffix "-ness" indicates a state or quality.
