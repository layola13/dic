local-area-networks: /ˈloʊkəl ˈeriə ˈnɛtwɜːrks/ | n. |局域网| A local-area network (LAN) is a computer network that interconnects computers within a limited area such as a home, school, computer laboratory, or office building.  LANs provide a way to share resources such as printers, files, and Internet connections. | Example: Our office uses a LAN to share files and printers.  我们的办公室使用局域网来共享文件和打印机。|近义词: intranet |反义词: WAN (wide-area network)
