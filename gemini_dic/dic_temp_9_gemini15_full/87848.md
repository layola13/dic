hidebound:/ˈhaɪdbaʊnd/| adj.|思想僵化的；顽固的；墨守成规的|Having fixed opinions; unwilling or unable to change.  The hidebound policies of the company prevented it from adapting to the changing market.|近义词：dogmatic, inflexible, rigid, stubborn|反义词：open-minded, flexible, adaptable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is hidebound in his views. = 他的观点很顽固。
They are hidebound by tradition. = 他们受传统束缚。


一般过去时:
She was hidebound in her beliefs. = 她对自己的信仰很固执。
The old system was hidebound and inefficient. = 旧制度僵化且效率低下。


现在完成时:
He has been hidebound in his thinking for years. = 他多年来思想僵化。
The organization has become hidebound and resistant to change. = 该组织变得墨守成规，抗拒改变。


过去完成时:
They had been hidebound by their upbringing. = 他们的教养使他们思想僵化。
The company had been hidebound in its approach to marketing. = 公司在营销方法上一直很死板。


将来时:
He will likely remain hidebound in his opinions. = 他很可能仍然会固执己见。
The government will continue to be hidebound by bureaucracy. = 政府将继续受官僚主义的束缚。


虚拟语气:
If he weren't so hidebound, he would be more successful. = 如果他不那么顽固，他会更成功。
I wish they weren't so hidebound in their approach. = 我希望他们的方法不要那么死板。


被动语态:
His ideas were considered hidebound by his colleagues. = 他的想法被同事们认为是顽固的。
The project was hampered by a hidebound approach. = 该项目因方法僵化而受阻。


疑问句:
Is he hidebound in his political views? = 他的政治观点顽固吗？
Are they hidebound by outdated regulations? = 他们受过时的规章制度约束吗？


否定句:
He is not hidebound in his thinking; he's quite open-minded. = 他思想并不僵化；他相当开明。
They are not hidebound by tradition; they embrace innovation. = 他们不受传统束缚；他们拥抱创新。
