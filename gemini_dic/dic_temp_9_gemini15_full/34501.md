clean-cut: /ˌkliːnˈkʌt/ | adj. | 清晰的；整洁的；规矩的；一丝不苟的 |  Neat and tidy in appearance; conventionally respectable.  It implies a well-groomed and conventionally attractive appearance, often suggesting youthfulness and respectability. |


例句：

He had a clean-cut look, with neatly combed hair and a crisp shirt.  他穿着整洁，头发梳理得很整齐，衬衫也很挺括。

The clean-cut lines of the building impressed me. 这座建筑物清晰的线条给我留下了深刻的印象。


近义词：neat, tidy, well-groomed, respectable, polished, prim


反义词：sloppy, unkempt, disheveled, scruffy, rough, untidy


词源:

The word is a compound of "clean" and "cut,"  reflecting the image of something being sharply defined and free from irregularities.  The "cut" aspect implies a precise and well-defined appearance. The term evolved in the early 20th century and gained popularity as a descriptor of appearance and style.
