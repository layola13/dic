hypermodernists:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɒdənɪsts/| n. |超现代主义者|Those who advocate or practice hypermodernism, a style or movement in art, architecture, or other fields characterized by a rejection of established norms and a focus on extreme or paradoxical elements.|The hypermodernists challenged the very foundations of artistic expression, embracing irony and self-awareness.|近义词：postmodernists, avant-gardists|反义词：traditionalists, classicists
