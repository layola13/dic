hox:/hɒks/| n. |霍克斯基因|Hox genes are a group of related genes that control the body plan of an embryo along the anterior-posterior (head-tail) axis.  They are crucial for the development of many different body segments and structures.|Example:  The mutation in the hox gene resulted in a dramatic change in the fly's wing structure.  霍克斯基因的突变导致果蝇翅膀结构发生了巨大变化。|近义词:homeotic gene|反义词:无|名词
