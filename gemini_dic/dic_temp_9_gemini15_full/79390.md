gliders:/ˈɡlaɪdərz/ | n. |滑翔机；滑翔者 | A glider is an aircraft that flies without an engine, relying on air currents and the pilot's skill to stay aloft.  They are often launched by being towed by another aircraft or by being winched into the air. |例句：The gliders soared gracefully through the sky. 滑翔机优雅地翱翔于天空。  The glider pilots expertly navigated the thermals. 滑翔机飞行员熟练地驾驭着热气流。|近义词：sailplane |反义词：powered aircraft
