shmoozes:/ʃmuːz/| v.|闲聊；奉承；巴结 |To engage in casual, often flattering conversation, especially for social or professional advantage.  It implies a degree of insincerity or self-serving motivation.|例句：He spent the evening shmoozing with potential investors. 他整个晚上都在和潜在投资者闲聊。

例句：She shmoozed her way to the top of the company. 她通过奉承巴结的方式爬到了公司的高层。

近义词：network, socialize, schmooze,  hobnob,  cajole

反义词：ignore, shun, snub

词源:
The word "shmooze" is of Yiddish origin, likely derived from the Yiddish word  "shmuesen," meaning "to chat" or "to gossip." It entered English in the early 20th century and gained wider popularity in the mid-20th century.
