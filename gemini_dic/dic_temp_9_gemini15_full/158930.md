plurality:/plʊˈræləti/| n. |多数；复数；多种；多样性 |The existence of more than one; the state of being plural.  It often refers to the fact that more than one thing exists, or that something has multiple aspects or forms.  In politics, it specifically refers to the largest number of votes received by a candidate, even if that number is less than half of the total. |例句：The plurality of opinions made reaching a consensus difficult. 多种意见使得达成共识变得困难。

例句：The candidate won by a plurality of the votes, but not by a majority.  这位候选人以得票最多获胜，但并非多数票获胜。


近义词：majority (in some contexts), multiplicity, diversity, multitude

反义词：minority, singularity, unity


词源:  From Middle English pluralite, from Old French pluralité, from Late Latin pluralitas, from Latin pluralis ("plural").  The Latin root is *plus ("more").
