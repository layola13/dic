caucuses:/ˈkɔːkəsɪz/| n.|党团会议；核心小组；预选会议|A caucus is a meeting of members of a political party, typically to select candidates or to decide on policy.  It can also refer to a smaller group within a larger organization. |The Democratic caucus met to discuss the upcoming election. 民主党核心小组开会讨论即将到来的选举。 The Iowa caucuses are an important early event in the US presidential election cycle. 爱荷华州党团会议是美国总统大选周期中一个重要的早期活动。|conference, meeting, gathering|—|n.


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The party caucuses regularly to discuss strategy. = 该党定期举行党团会议讨论策略。
Our team caucuses before each project to assign roles. = 我们团队在每个项目之前都会召开小组会议分配角色。

一般过去时:
The senators caucuses last night to decide on the bill. = 参议员们昨晚举行党团会议决定法案。
The group caucuses before making a final decision. = 该小组在做出最终决定之前举行了会议。

现在完成时:
The committee has caucuses several times this month. = 委员会本月已经几次召开会议。
They have caucuses extensively on the proposed legislation. = 他们已经就拟议的立法进行了广泛的讨论。

过去完成时:
They had caucuses before the official announcement was made. = 在正式宣布之前，他们已经开过会了。
The delegates had caucuses for hours before reaching a consensus. = 代表们在达成共识之前进行了数小时的讨论。

将来时:

We will caucus after the presentation to discuss the findings. = 我们将在演示结束后召开会议讨论结果。

虚拟语气:

I wish we could caucus more frequently to address these issues. = 我希望我们能够更频繁地开会来解决这些问题。
