masques:/mæskz/| n.|面具；伪装；假面舞会|A masque is a type of entertainment popular in the 16th and 17th centuries, combining music, dance, poetry, and elaborate costumes and scenery.  It often involved allegorical or mythological themes.|The Renaissance masques were known for their spectacular displays.|近义词：mask, disguise, facade|反义词：reality, truth
