catalyst: /ˈkætəlɪst/ | n. |催化剂，刺激因素 | A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change.  Also, a person or thing that precipitates an event or process. |例句：Enzymes are biological catalysts. 酶是生物催化剂。

The new policy acted as a catalyst for economic growth. 这项新政策成为经济增长的催化剂。

近义词：accelerant, stimulant, impetus, trigger | 反义词：inhibitor, deterrent

词源:
The word "catalyst" comes from the Greek word "katályein," meaning "to dissolve" or "to loosen."  This refers to the way a catalyst seemingly "loosens" the chemical bonds in a reaction, allowing it to proceed more readily.  It entered English scientific vocabulary in the 19th century.
