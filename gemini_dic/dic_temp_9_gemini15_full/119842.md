mildewing:/ˈmɪlˌduːɪŋ/ | v. |发霉；长霉 | The process of becoming covered with mildew;  the state of being mildewed.  It often refers to the growth of mildew on surfaces like walls, fabrics, or food. |例句：The old book was mildewing in the damp cellar.  旧书在潮湿的地窖里发霉了。


The bread started mildewing after a few days in the humid weather.  面包在潮湿的天气里几天后就开始发霉了。|近义词：molding, rotting, decaying |反义词：drying, preserving


USAGE->

The old shoes are constantly mildewing in this climate. = 在这种气候下，旧鞋子总是发霉。

一般过去时:
The fruit mildewed quickly in the heat. = 水果在高温下很快发霉了。
The basement mildewed after the flood. = 洪水过后，地下室发霉了。

词源:
The word "mildewing" is derived from the noun "mildew," which comes from the Old English word *melugu, meaning "fine dust" or "powder."  This reflects the powdery appearance of mildew.  The "-ing" suffix indicates the present participle, describing an ongoing process.
