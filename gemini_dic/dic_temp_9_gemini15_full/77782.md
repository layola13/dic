generalissimo:/ˌdʒɛnəˈrælɪsimoʊ/| n. | 最高统帅；大元帅 | A generalissimo is the supreme commander of the armed forces of a nation.  It's a title, often associated with dictators or autocratic leaders, implying absolute military authority. The term suggests a level of power exceeding that of a mere general. | Example: Francisco Franco was the generalissimo of Spain during the Spanish Civil War.  弗朗西斯科·佛朗哥在西班牙内战期间是西班牙的最高统帅。  | 近义词：supreme commander, commander-in-chief | 反义词：private, soldier | 名词
