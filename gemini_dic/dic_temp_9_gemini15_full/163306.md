preconfession: /ˌpriːkənˈfɛʃən/ | n. | 预先忏悔；预先承认 | A preconfession is an act of confessing or admitting something before it is officially or formally confessed.  It might involve revealing a wrongdoing or mistake before being directly questioned or accused.  It often carries a sense of anticipation or preparation for a more formal confession. | Example:  His preconfession to the police saved him from a harsher sentence.  他的预先坦白使他免受更严厉的判决。 | 近义词：preliminary admission, anticipatory confession | 反义词：denial, concealment |  词性: noun
