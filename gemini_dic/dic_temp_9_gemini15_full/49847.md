demoralizer: /dɪˈmɔːrəlaɪzər/ | n. | 使士气低落的人或事物 | Something or someone that lowers morale or discourages.  A demoralizer can be a person, event, or situation that undermines confidence, hope, and enthusiasm. | The constant criticism from his boss acted as a demoralizer, making him lose his motivation.  这位老板不断的批评就像一个打击士气的人，让他失去了动力。|近义词：discourager, depressant, disheartener | 反义词：motivator, inspirer, encourager | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The constant negativity demoralizes the team. = 持续的消极情绪打击了团队的士气。
That project acts as a demoralizer for many employees. = 那个项目对许多员工来说是打击士气的。


一般过去时:
The unexpected defeat demoralized the troops. =出乎意料的失败使部队士气低落。
The harsh criticism demoralized the young artist. = 严厉的批评使这位年轻的艺术家士气低落。


现在完成时:

This series of failures has demoralized the entire team. = 这系列的失败已经让整个团队士气低落。


过去完成时:

将来时:

Continuous failures will demoralize the students. = 持续的失败将会让学生们失去信心。


虚拟语气:
If the management continued this way, it would demoralize the entire company. = 如果管理层继续这样下去，将会打击整个公司的士气。
I wish that the bad news hadn't demoralized the team so much. = 我真希望坏消息没有这么打击团队的士气。


被动语态:

否定句:
