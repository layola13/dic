clutching: /ˈklʌtʃɪŋ/ | v. | 紧抓；紧握；抓住 |  Holding something tightly, often because of fear, anxiety, or desperation.  | 例句：The drowning man was clutching at a piece of driftwood.  溺水的人紧紧抓住一块漂浮的木头。


例句：She was clutching her handbag tightly as she walked down the dark street. 她走下黑暗的街道时紧紧抓住她的手提包。


近义词：grasping, gripping, seizing, clinging


反义词：releasing, letting go


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is clutching the steering wheel. = 他紧握着方向盘。
She clutches her teddy bear every night. = 她每晚都紧紧抱着她的泰迪熊。

一般过去时:
He clutched the money tightly in his pocket. = 他紧紧地把钱攥在口袋里。
She clutched at his arm, begging for help. = 她抓住他的胳膊，乞求帮助。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

词源:
The word "clutch" comes from the Middle English word "clucchen," which in turn derives from the Old English word "clūcgan."  These words signified "to grasp" or "to seize."  The meaning and usage have evolved over time to encompass the emotional and physical aspects of holding something tightly.
