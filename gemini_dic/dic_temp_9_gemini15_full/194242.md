self-minded: /ˌsɛlfˈmaɪndɪd/ | adj. | 自以为是的；以自我为中心的 | Self-minded describes someone who is excessively preoccupied with their own thoughts, feelings, and interests, often to the exclusion of others' needs or perspectives.  They are often inconsiderate and self-centered. | 例句：He's become rather self-minded since his promotion.  他自从升职后变得相当自以为是了。

He is so self-minded that he never considers others' feelings. 他是如此以自我为中心，从不考虑别人的感受。


近义词：selfish, egotistical, self-centered, narcissistic, egocentric | 反义词：considerate, selfless, altruistic, empathetic


词源:  The word is a compound of "self-" (meaning oneself or one's own) and "minded" (meaning having a particular disposition or inclination).  The "-minded" suffix is frequently used to form adjectives describing someone's mental state or character.


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms. Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  Its usage is consistent across various sentence structures.

肯定句:
He is a self-minded individual. 他是一个自以为是的人。
She made a self-minded decision. 她做了一个自私的决定。

否定句:
He is not self-minded at all; he's very considerate. 他一点也不自私；他很体贴。
Their actions were not self-minded but rather altruistic. 他们的行为并非自私的，而是利他的。

疑问句:
Is he really that self-minded? 他真的那么自以为是吗？
Are her decisions always so self-minded? 她的决定总是那么自私吗？
