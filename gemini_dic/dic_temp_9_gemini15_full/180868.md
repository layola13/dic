rehiring: /ˌriːˈhaɪərɪŋ/ | n. | 重新雇用，再次聘用 | The act of hiring someone back who has previously worked for the company.  This often happens after a period of unemployment or layoff.  | Example: The rehiring of experienced staff boosted company morale.  重新雇用经验丰富的员工提升了公司士气。 | 近义词：reemployment, reinstatement | 反义词：firing, dismissal, layoff | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company is considering rehiring several laid-off employees. = 公司正在考虑重新雇用几名被解雇的员工。
Rehiring skilled workers is a priority for the project. = 重新雇用熟练工人是该项目的优先事项。


一般过去时:
They rehired him after a year's absence. =  一年后他们重新雇用了。
The factory rehired most of the workers after the strike ended. =罢工结束后，工厂重新雇用了大部分工人。


现在完成时:
The company has rehired over fifty employees this year. = 公司今年已经重新雇用了五十多名员工。
She has been rehired by her former employer. = 她已经被她的前任雇主重新雇用了。


过去完成时:
They had rehired him before the project started. = 项目开始之前，他们已经重新雇用了他。
The manager had rehired the team before the deadline. = 经理在截止日期之前已经重新雇用了团队。


将来时:
They will rehire him once the funding is secured. = 一旦资金到位，他们就会重新雇用他。
The company will rehire additional staff next quarter. = 公司下个季度将重新雇用更多员工。


虚拟语气:
If they rehired her, the project would be successful. = 如果他们重新雇用她，项目就会成功。

被动语态:
