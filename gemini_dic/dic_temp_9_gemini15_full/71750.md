flaying: /ˈfleɪɪŋ/ | v. | 剥皮；严厉批评；鞭打 | To strip the skin from; to severely criticize; to whip.  | The butcher was flaying the rabbit.  屠夫正在剥兔子的皮。 |近义词：skinning, castigating, scourging | 反义词：covering, praising


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flayers the animal. 他在剥动物的皮。
She flayers her opponent in the debate. 她在辩论中严厉批评她的对手。


一般过去时:
They flayed the hide from the carcass.  他们把皮从尸体上剥了下来。
The critic flayed the author's new novel. 批评家严厉批评了作者的新小说。

词源:
From Old English *flēan, meaning "to flay".  The word is related to the Old Norse word *flá, meaning "to flay" and the Old High German *flehan, also meaning "to flay."  The modern English word retains the core meaning from its Germanic roots.
