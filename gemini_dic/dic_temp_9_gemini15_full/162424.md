preadjustment: /ˌpriːəˈdʒʌstmənt/ | n. | 预先调整，预调 | The act of adjusting something beforehand; preparatory adjustment.  It involves making changes or alterations in advance to ensure optimal performance or to anticipate future needs. |例句：The preadjustment of the equipment ensured a smooth start to the experiment.  预先调整设备确保实验顺利开始。

The preadjustment of the camera settings was crucial for capturing the perfect shot. 相机的预先设置对于捕捉完美的画面至关重要。|近义词：presetting, precalibration, preparation |反义词：postadjustment, readjustment

词源:

The word "preadjustment" is a compound word formed by combining the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before" or "in advance") with the noun "adjustment" (meaning the act of adjusting something).  Therefore, it literally means "an adjustment made beforehand."
