chaffing: /ˈtʃæfɪŋ/ | n. v. |摩擦；恼怒；谷物脱粒；|

英文解析：
As a noun, "chaffing" refers to the action of rubbing against something, causing irritation or wear. It can also refer to the process of separating grain from its stalks.  As a verb, "chaffing" means to rub against something, causing irritation or wear, or to tease or annoy someone playfully (though sometimes aggressively).

例句：
The chaffing of the rope against the mast caused it to fray.  绳索与桅杆摩擦导致其磨损。

The constant chaffing of his criticisms was wearing her down. 他持续不断的批评让她受不了。


近义词：
(noun) abrasion, friction, irritation, annoyance, teasing
(verb) rubbing, abrading, irritating, teasing, annoying


反义词：
(noun) smoothness, comfort, soothing
(verb) soothing, comforting, pleasing


词性：
Noun, Verb


词源:
From Middle English *chaffen, from Old English *ċæfian "to rub, chafe," from Proto-Germanic *kʰapōną "to seize, grasp,"  related to Old Norse *kjafa "jaw," Old High German *chafo "jaw," and possibly to Old English *ċeāf "chaff."  The sense development is from "rubbing" to "irritating" to "teasing."
