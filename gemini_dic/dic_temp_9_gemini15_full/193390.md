seint:/seɪnt/| n.|圣人，圣徒|A saint is a person who is officially recognized as being exceptionally holy.  The term is often used in a religious context, particularly within Christianity.  Saints are often believed to have performed miracles or lived exceptionally virtuous lives.|例句：Saint Francis of Assisi was known for his love of animals.  圣方济各以热爱动物而闻名。|例句：Many saints are depicted in religious art. 许多圣徒被描绘在宗教艺术中。|近义词：holy person, blessed one, righteous one|反义词：sinner, wicked person, unholy person|名词

词源:
The word "saint" comes from the Old French word "seint," which itself derives from the Latin word "sanctus," meaning "holy."  The evolution reflects the spread of Christianity and its terminology across Europe.
