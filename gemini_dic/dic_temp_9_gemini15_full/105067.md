kerbs:/kɜːrbz/| n.|路缘石；(英式英语)人行道边缘|Curbs or kerbs are the raised edges of a road, typically made of stone or concrete, separating the pavement from the roadway.  They serve to guide traffic and protect pedestrians.|例句：The car mounted the kerb and crashed into a lamppost.  这辆车冲上了路缘石，撞到了一根灯柱。|例句： Cyclists should dismount and walk their bikes on the kerb.  骑自行车的人应该下车，在人行道边缘推着自行车走。|近义词：curb (美式英语), edge, border|反义词：roadway, carriageway
