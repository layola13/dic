mores:/ˈmɔːrɛz/| n.|风俗习惯，道德观念|The established customs, manners, and morals of a particular group.|例句：The mores of Victorian society were very strict. 维多利亚时代的社会风俗习惯非常严格。|近义词：customs, traditions, conventions|反义词：laws (in some contexts)


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  Therefore, tense examples are not applicable.  Instead, we'll show usage in different sentence structures.


Simple Sentence:
Their mores dictated a strong emphasis on family.  他们的风俗习惯强调家庭的重要性。

Complex Sentence:
Because their mores were so different, communication proved challenging. 因为他们的风俗习惯大相径庭，沟通证明很具有挑战性。

Compound Sentence:
The community's mores were changing, and younger generations were challenging the status quo. 社区的风俗习惯正在改变，年轻一代正在挑战现状。


PHRASE->
The word "mores" doesn't typically form common phrases in the same way as verbs or prepositions.  Instead, it's used in descriptive phrases.
