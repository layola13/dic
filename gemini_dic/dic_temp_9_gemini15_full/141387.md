operetta:/ˌɒpəˈretə/| n. |轻歌剧 |A short opera, typically a comic one, with spoken dialogue. Operettas are characterized by lighthearted plots, catchy melodies, and often include elements of dance and spectacle. They are generally less complex and demanding than full-length operas. | Example:  The Gilbert and Sullivan operetta "The Pirates of Penzance" is a classic example of the genre. 吉尔伯特和萨利文创作的轻歌剧《彭赞斯的海盗》是该类型的经典之作。|近义词：musical comedy, light opera |反义词：grand opera

词源:
The word "operetta" is a diminutive of "opera," formed by adding the Italian suffix "-etta," meaning "little."  Therefore, "operetta" literally means "little opera."  It reflects the smaller scale and lighter tone of this theatrical form compared to full-scale operas.
