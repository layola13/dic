hexateuch:/ˈhɛksətuːk/| n. |六经；摩西五经加约书亚记|The hexateuch is a term used in biblical studies to refer to the first six books of the Bible, comprising the Pentateuch (Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy) and the Book of Joshua.|The scholarly study of the hexateuch often involves analyzing the different sources and layers of authorship within the text.|近义词: Pentateuch (with Joshua) |反义词: None
