lungfishes: /ˈlʌŋfɪʃɪz/ | n. | 肺鱼 | Lungfishes are a group of six extant species of fish that possess both gills and lungs. They are considered living fossils, representing an ancient lineage of vertebrates. They are found in the freshwater habitats of Africa, South America, and Australia.  Their ability to breathe air makes them highly adaptable to environments with low oxygen levels.  | The Australian lungfish is critically endangered. | 近义词：air-breathing fish (广义) | 反义词：无
