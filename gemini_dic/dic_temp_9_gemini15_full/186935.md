ripoff:/ˈrɪpɒf/| n. v.|敲竹杠；欺诈；廉价劣质品 |A ripoff is something that is much more expensive than it should be, or of much lower quality than it should be.  It can refer to a dishonest action, often involving overcharging or selling something inferior. |例句：That restaurant is a ripoff; the food was awful and overpriced. 那家餐馆是黑店；食物很糟糕而且价格过高。

That concert ticket was a total ripoff -  the sound system was terrible and the band was late. 那张演唱会门票完全是敲竹杠——音响系统很糟糕，乐队还迟到了。


近义词：fraud, swindle, cheat, scam, overcharge |反义词：bargain, steal (in the sense of a great deal)
