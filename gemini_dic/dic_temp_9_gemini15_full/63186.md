entangledness: /ɪnˈtæŋɡldnəs/ | n. |纠缠；缠绕；复杂性 |The state of being entangled; a complex or interwoven relationship.  It often refers to the interconnectedness of things, often in a way that is difficult to separate or understand.  In physics, it specifically refers to a quantum phenomenon where two or more particles become linked in such a way that they share the same fate, regardless of the distance separating them. |例句：The entangledness of the political situation made it difficult to predict the outcome.  政治局势的复杂性使得难以预测结果。


The entangledness of their lives was evident in their shared history. 他们生活中的纠缠在他们共同的历史中显而易见。


近义词：intertwining, interconnectedness, complexity, involvement


反义词：separateness, independence, simplicity


词性：名词


词源:  The word "entangledness" is derived from the verb "entangle," which comes from the Old English "entanglian," meaning "to catch in a tangle."  The suffix "-ness" adds the meaning of "state or quality of."  Therefore, "entangledness" literally describes the state of being tangled or interwoven.
