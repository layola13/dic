pharaoh:/ˈfɛroʊ/| n. |法老|The Pharaoh was the ruler of ancient Egypt, considered a divine king.  Pharaohs held absolute power and were responsible for the well-being of the kingdom, overseeing religious practices, public works, and military campaigns. The title "Pharaoh" wasn't originally a personal name, but rather a title for the ruler, similar to "King" or "Emperor". Over time, it became associated with specific rulers and is now used to refer to the ancient Egyptian monarchs.|例句：The Pharaoh ruled Egypt with an iron fist. 法老铁腕统治埃及。|例句：Archaeologists unearthed the tomb of a powerful Pharaoh. 考古学家挖掘出一位强大的法老的陵墓。|近义词：king, emperor, sovereign, monarch|反义词：subject, peasant
