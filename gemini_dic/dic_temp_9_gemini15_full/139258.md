obligement:/əˈblaɪdʒmənt/ | n. |义务；恩惠；帮忙 |The word "obligement" is now largely archaic or obsolete in modern English. It means a favor or act of kindness; an obligation or duty. It implies a sense of being indebted to someone for their assistance.  |例句：He felt a deep obligement to his benefactor. 他对他恩人深感感激。(此处“感激”更贴切地表达了obligement的含义)  He acknowledged the obligement with a grateful nod. 他感激地点点头以示感谢。|近义词：favor, kindness, obligation, debt, service |反义词：disregard, neglect, refusal
