fretboard:/ˈfrɛtˌbɔːrd/ | n. |指板|The fretboard is the long, narrow strip of wood on the neck of a stringed instrument, such as a guitar or bass, with frets inlaid to define the notes.  It's where the musician presses down on the strings to change the pitch. |Example: He carefully cleaned the fretboard of his prized guitar. 他仔细清洁了他珍爱的吉他的指板。|近义词：fingerboard|反义词：无
