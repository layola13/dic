repolishing:/ˌriːˈpɑːlɪʃɪŋ/ | v. |重新抛光；再精加工 | The act of polishing something again, often to improve its appearance or functionality.  It implies a previous polishing effort. | Example:  The jeweler spent hours repolishing the antique brooch to restore its original luster. = 珠宝匠花了数小时重新抛光古董胸针，以恢复其原有的光泽。

The car needed repolishing after the accident. = 事故后，汽车需要重新抛光。|近义词：refinishing, rebuffing, renovating | 反义词：roughening, damaging
