facundious:/fəˈkʌndɪəs/ | adj. |雄辩的；健谈的；口才流利的 | Eloquent; fluent and persuasive in speaking or writing.  Characterized by great eloquence or persuasiveness. |例句：He delivered a facundious speech that captivated the audience. 他发表了一篇雄辩的演讲，迷住了听众。

The renowned orator was known for his facundious pronouncements.  这位著名的演说家以其雄辩的声明而闻名。


近义词：eloquent, articulate, fluent, silver-tongued, voluble, persuasive |反义词：taciturn, reticent, inarticulate, speechless


词源：From Latin *facundus ("eloquent, fluent"), from fari ("to speak").  The suffix -ious is a common adjectival suffix in English, derived from Latin -osus.


用法说明：

facundious主要用作形容词，修饰人或其言语，强调其表达能力的出色和令人信服。它通常用于正式语境中，表达对某人演讲或写作技巧的高度赞扬。  与eloquent相比，facundious更强调说话或写作的流畅性和说服力。
