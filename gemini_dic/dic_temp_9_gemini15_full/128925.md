nine-eleven:/ˌnaɪnɪˈlɛvən/| n. |九一一事件，9·11恐怖袭击|The term "nine-eleven" refers to the terrorist attacks that occurred on September 11, 2001, in the United States.  It is often used as shorthand for the events of that day, which involved the coordinated attacks on the World Trade Center in New York City and the Pentagon in Arlington, Virginia, as well as a failed attack on the U.S. Capitol. The attacks resulted in widespread devastation, loss of life, and significant changes to U.S. domestic and foreign policy.| Example: The events of nine-eleven changed the world.  九一一事件改变了世界。|近义词: September 11th attacks, 9/11 attacks |反义词: N/A | noun
