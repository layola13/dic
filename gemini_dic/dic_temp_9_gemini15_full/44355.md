cross-boundary: /ˈkrɒsˈbaʊndəri/ | adj. |跨界的，跨区域的 |  Relating to or involving the crossing of a boundary or boundaries, especially geographical, administrative, or jurisdictional boundaries.  It often implies interaction or cooperation between different entities or regions. |例句：The project involves cross-boundary collaboration between several universities. 这个项目涉及几所大学之间的跨界合作。

The research team conducted a cross-boundary study on air pollution. 研究团队对空气污染进行了跨界研究。


近义词：transboundary, transnational, interregional, international (depending on context) |反义词：within-boundary, local, confined

词源:

The word is a compound of "cross," meaning "to go across" or "extending across," and "boundary," referring to a line that marks the limits of something.  Both "cross" and "boundary" have Old English roots.
