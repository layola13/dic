journalist:/ˈdʒɜːrnəlɪst/| n. |记者，新闻工作者 |A journalist is a person who writes news stories for newspapers, magazines, or websites, or broadcasts news on television or radio.  |Example:  She's a journalist for a major national newspaper. 她是一家大型全国性报纸的记者。|Example: Many journalists covered the breaking news story. 许多记者报道了这则突发新闻。|近义词：reporter, newsman, correspondent |反义词：None specifically, but  "reader" might be considered an opposite in the sense of audience vs. creator. |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The journalist writes articles daily. = 这位记者每天写文章。
He reports on current events. = 他报道时事。


一般过去时:
The journalist wrote a compelling story. = 这位记者写了一个引人入胜的故事。
She interviewed the president yesterday. = 她昨天采访了总统。


现在完成时:
The journalist has covered many important stories. = 这位记者报道过许多重要的新闻。
She has worked for this newspaper for ten years. = 她为这家报纸工作了十年。


过去完成时:
The journalist had already filed her report before the deadline. = 在截止日期之前，这位记者已经提交了她的报告。
He had interviewed several witnesses before writing the article. = 在撰写文章之前，他已经采访了几位证人。


将来时:
The journalist will attend the press conference tomorrow. = 记者明天将参加新闻发布会。
She will be reporting live from the scene. = 她将从现场进行直播报道。


虚拟语气:
If the journalist were more experienced, she would have handled the situation better. = 如果这位记者更有经验，她会更好地处理这种情况。
I wish the journalist had been more careful with his facts. = 我希望这位记者对事实更谨慎一些。


被动语态:
The article was written by a young journalist. = 这篇文章是一位年轻记者写的。
The interview was broadcast live on television. = 采访在电视上进行了现场直播。


疑问句:
Is the journalist a member of the press association? = 这位记者是新闻协会的成员吗？
What did the journalist report? = 记者报道了什么？


否定句:
The journalist did not disclose her sources. = 这位记者没有透露她的消息来源。
He was not allowed to enter the press room. = 他不被允许进入新闻发布室。
