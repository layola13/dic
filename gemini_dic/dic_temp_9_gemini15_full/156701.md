piebald:/ˈpaɪbɔːld/| adj.| 花斑的，黑白杂色的 |Having patches of two or more colors, especially black and white.  This word typically describes the coat of an animal, but can also be used figuratively. |例句：The piebald horse galloped across the field. 这匹花斑马在田野里飞奔。

The cow was piebald, with large patches of black and white. 这头奶牛是黑白花斑的，有大块的黑斑和白斑。


近义词：dappled, mottled, speckled, particolored |反义词：uniformly colored, solid-colored


词源:  The word "piebald" comes from the Middle English word "pie," meaning "magpie," a bird known for its black and white plumage, combined with "bald," meaning "white" or "marked with white."  The original meaning referred specifically to the black and white markings, but the modern usage is broader to include other color combinations.
