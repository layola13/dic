posttarsal: /ˌpoʊstˈtɑːrsəl/ | adj. |跗节后的，后跗节的 | Relating to the part of a leg or appendage situated behind the tarsus (the segment of the leg between the tibia and the foot).  The term is primarily used in zoology and entomology, particularly in describing the anatomy of insects and other arthropods. | Example: The posttarsal claws of the insect provided excellent grip on the smooth surface. 跗节后的爪子使昆虫能够很好地抓握光滑的表面。| 近义词:metatarsal (if referring to a similar position on a vertebrate leg) | 反义词:pretarsal
