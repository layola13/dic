patronage:/ˈpeɪtrənɪdʒ/| n. | 赞助；惠顾；庇护；任命 | The support given by a patron.  It can refer to financial support,  support of an institution or cause, or the act of giving someone a job or position based on favoritism rather than merit. |例句：The museum relies heavily on private patronage.  博物馆严重依赖私人赞助。

The artist received patronage from a wealthy collector.这位艺术家得到了一位富有的收藏家的资助。


近义词：support, sponsorship, protection, favoritism |反义词：neglect, opposition, rejection |词性:名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The museum receives generous patronage from local businesses. = 博物馆收到当地企业的慷慨赞助。
His career advancement is due to his boss's patronage. = 他的事业进步是由于老板的提携。


一般过去时:
The theatre received considerable patronage during its early years. = 剧院在其早期获得了相当多的资助。
The king bestowed his patronage upon the young artist. = 国王将他的庇护给予了这位年轻的艺术家。


现在完成时:
The charity has benefited greatly from the patronage of several celebrities. = 慈善机构从几位名人的资助中受益匪浅。
The company has enjoyed considerable patronage from its loyal customers. = 公司一直享有其忠实顾客的大力支持。


过去完成时:
The artist had already received patronage from several prominent families before his exhibition. = 在展览之前，这位艺术家已经得到过几个显赫家族的资助。
The organization had relied on the patronage of a single wealthy donor until recently. = 直到最近，该组织一直依靠一位富有的捐助人的资助。


将来时:
The new museum hopes to attract patronage from both individuals and corporations. = 新博物馆希望吸引个人和公司的资助。
We anticipate increased patronage for the upcoming event. = 我们预计即将举行的活动将获得更多支持。


虚拟语气:
If only the artist had received more patronage during his lifetime. = 如果这位艺术家在他有生之年能得到更多资助就好了。
I wish the theatre had received more patronage in its early years. = 我希望剧院在其早期能得到更多资助。


被动语态:
The young writer was fortunate to receive patronage from a well-known publisher. = 这位年轻的作家幸运地得到了一位著名出版商的资助。
The project was abandoned due to a lack of patronage. = 该项目由于缺乏资金支持而被放弃。


疑问句:
Does the organization rely on government patronage? = 该组织是否依赖政府的资助？
What kind of patronage does the artist need to succeed? = 这位艺术家需要什么样的支持才能成功？


否定句:
The struggling artist received little patronage from the art community. = 这位苦苦挣扎的艺术家几乎没有得到艺术界的支持。
The new business lacked patronage and had to close. = 这家新企业缺乏顾客支持，不得不关闭。

词源:

Patronage comes from the Old French word *patronage*, meaning "protection, support," ultimately deriving from the Latin word *patronus*, meaning "protector, patron."  The sense of "support" developed from the original meaning of protection offered by a powerful individual to a less powerful one.
