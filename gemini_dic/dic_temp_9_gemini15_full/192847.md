seared:/sɪrd/ | vt. adj. | 烧焦的；烤焦的；使…焦；使…感到震惊 | To burn the surface of something with a sudden, intense heat.  Also, to inflict a sudden, intense emotional pain or shock. |例句：The steak was seared on both sides. = 牛排两面都煎焦了。

The news seared her heart. = 这消息让她心碎。|近义词：scorch, burn, sear, brand |反义词：unharmed, untouched


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He sears the vegetables before adding them to the soup. = 他在把蔬菜加到汤里之前先把它们煎一下。
The sun sears the skin in the midday heat. = 正午的烈日灼伤皮肤。


一般过去时:
She seared the salmon fillets until they were nicely browned. = 她把鲑鱼片煎至金棕色。
The criticism seared his pride. = 批评伤害了他的自尊。


现在完成时:
He has seared hundreds of steaks in his career as a chef. = 他在他厨师生涯中煎过数百块牛排。
The memory of the accident has seared itself into her mind. = 事故的记忆深深地刻在了她的脑海里。


过去完成时:
They had seared the peppers before adding them to the fajitas. = 他们在把辣椒添加到煎饼卷中之前已经把它们煎过了。
She had seared her reputation with her reckless behavior. = 她因鲁莽的行为而损害了自己的名誉。


将来时:
I will sear the chicken breasts before baking them. = 我将在烘烤鸡胸肉之前先煎一下。
The scandal will sear its way into public consciousness. = 这场丑闻将会深入公众意识。


虚拟语气:
If I seared the meat longer, it would be more flavorful. = 如果我把肉煎得更久一点，味道会更好。
I wish I hadn't seared the vegetables so much, they're too hard. = 我真希望我没有把蔬菜煎得太多，它们太硬了。


被动语态:
The steak was seared to perfection. = 牛排煎得恰到好处。
His reputation was seared by the scandal. = 他的名声因丑闻而受损。


疑问句:
Have you ever seared a fish? = 你曾经煎过鱼吗？
Was the meat properly seared? = 肉煎熟了吗？


否定句:
I didn't sear the onions long enough, they're still raw. = 我没有把洋葱煎够时间，它们还是生的。
The experience didn't sear her soul as she thought it would. = 这经历并没有像她想的那样让她心灵受到创伤。


PHRASE->

pan-seared: 煎的
