prefamiliar:/ˌpriːfəˈmɪliər/| adj. |预先熟悉的；先前熟悉的|  The word "prefamiliar" is a relatively rare and informal word, combining the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") with "familiar." It describes something that is familiar before a particular event or time.  It implies a prior acquaintance or knowledge, suggesting a sense of recognition or comfort even before a new encounter.  There isn't a direct equivalent single-word synonym, but the meaning can be conveyed through phrases. |The experience felt prefamiliar, as if I'd lived it before.|近义词：already known, previously encountered, vaguely familiar |反义词：unfamiliar, novel, strange
