contrarily:/ˈkɒntrɛrɪli/|/ˈkɑːntrɛrəli/| adv. |相反地；反之；矛盾地 |Contrary to expectations, he passed the exam.  |Conversely, on the contrary, in contrast, oppositely |similarly, likewise |副词

contrarily: This adverb indicates opposition or contrast to a previous statement or expectation. It implies a difference or contradiction.  It suggests a turn of events or a shift in perspective that is unexpected given the preceding information.


例句1:  He said he would come, but contrarily, he didn't show up. = 他说他会来，但相反地，他没有出现。

例句2:  The forecast predicted rain, but contrarily, the sun shone brightly all day. = 预报说会下雨，但相反地，阳光灿烂地照耀了一整天。


近义词: conversely, on the contrary, in contrast, oppositely

反义词: similarly, likewise


词源:  Derived from the adjective "contrary," ultimately from the Latin "contrarius" meaning "opposite" or "against." The "-ly" suffix transforms the adjective into an adverb.
