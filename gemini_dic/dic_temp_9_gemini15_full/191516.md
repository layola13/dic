schmooze:/ʃmuːz/| v.|闲聊；奉承；拉关系 | To chat informally and in a friendly way, often to gain an advantage.  It often implies insincere flattery or networking. | 例句：He spent the evening schmoozing with potential investors. 他整个晚上都在和潜在投资者闲聊。  He schmoozed his way to the top. 他通过拉关系爬到了顶峰。|近义词：network, socialize, flatter, ingratiate | 反义词：ignore, shun, alienate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He schmoozes with clients every day. = 他每天都和客户闲聊。
They schmooze at industry events. = 他们在行业活动上拉关系。

一般过去时:
She schmoozed the CEO at the conference. = 她在会议上讨好首席执行官。
They schmoozed all night at the party. = 他们在派对上聊了一整夜。

现在完成时:
He has schmoozed his way into a lucrative deal. = 他通过拉关系获得了一笔利润丰厚的交易。
She has schmoozed with many influential people. = 她已经和许多有影响力的人拉过关系。

过去完成时:
He had schmoozed the committee before presenting his proposal. = 他在提交提案之前已经和委员会成员套近乎了。
They had schmoozed the investors for months before securing funding. = 在获得资金之前，他们已经和投资者套近乎了好几个月。

将来时:
She will schmooze with the board members tomorrow. = 她明天将和董事会成员会面并套近乎。
They will schmooze at the networking event next week. = 他们下周将在社交活动上拉关系。

虚拟语气:
If he schmoozed more, he might get the promotion. = 如果他更会拉关系，他可能会得到晋升。
I wish I had schmoozed more effectively at the conference. = 我希望我在会议上更有效地拉关系。

被动语态:
He was schmoozed by the experienced salesman. = 他被经验丰富的销售员讨好了。
The new project was secured after much schmoozing. = 在一番拉关系之后，新的项目才得以落实。

疑问句:
Does he schmooze often? = 他经常拉关系吗？
Were they schmoozing at the party? = 他们在派对上拉关系吗？

否定句:
He doesn't schmooze; he prefers direct communication. = 他不拉关系；他更喜欢直接沟通。
They didn't schmooze with the important clients. = 他们没有和重要的客户套近乎。

词源:

The word "schmooze" is of Yiddish origin, likely derived from the Yiddish word *ʃmūzn*, meaning "to chat, gossip."  Its usage in English spread significantly in the mid-20th century.
