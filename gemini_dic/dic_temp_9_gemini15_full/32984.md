choreographers:/ˌkɔːriˈɒɡrəfərz/ | n. | 编舞者 | A choreographer is a person who creates and arranges dances and ballets.  They plan and organize the movements, steps, and formations of dancers in a performance. | The choreographer created a stunning ballet that captivated the audience.  这位编舞者创作了一部令人惊叹的芭蕾舞剧，深深吸引了观众。  The renowned choreographer is known for her innovative and expressive style.  这位著名的编舞家以其创新和富有表现力的风格而闻名。 |  dance director, ballet master |  (no direct antonym, but opposite concept could be a "stage manager" focusing on non-dance aspects) | noun
