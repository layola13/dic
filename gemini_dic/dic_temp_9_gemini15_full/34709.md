clevernesses:/ˈklɛvənəsɪz/ | n. |聪明才智；巧妙；机灵 |The plural form of cleverness, referring to multiple instances or types of cleverness, intelligence, skill, or ingenuity.  It emphasizes a variety of clever acts or ideas. |例句：Her clevernesses in solving the puzzle were truly impressive.  她的解谜技巧令人印象深刻。

例句：The professor marveled at the student's diverse clevernesses in approaching the problem. 教授对学生解决问题时展现出的各种巧妙方法感到惊叹。


近义词：ingenuity, skills, talents, wits, adroitness

反义词：stupidity, foolishness, incompetence, clumsiness


词源:  Derived from "clever," which comes from the Middle English "clevere," meaning "skillful," ultimately of unknown origin, possibly related to a Proto-Germanic root.  The suffix "-ness" is a common English noun-forming suffix indicating a state, quality, or condition.
