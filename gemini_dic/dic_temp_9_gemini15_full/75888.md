fundamentalistic: /ˌfʌndəˌmɛntəlˈɪstɪk/ | adj. | 原教旨主义的；基本主义的 |

英文解析:  Relating to or characteristic of fundamentalism, a movement in theology emphasizing the literal interpretation of religious texts and the strict adherence to traditional doctrines.  It can also refer to a belief in basic principles as the foundation of a system or theory.


例句:

His fundamentalistic views on religion led to conflict with more liberal members of the congregation.  他的原教旨主义宗教观导致了他与教会中较为自由派成员的冲突。

The professor's approach to teaching was fundamentally fundamentalistic, focusing solely on the core concepts. 教授的教学方法本质上是基本主义的，只关注核心概念。


近义词:  orthodox, traditionalist, literalist, conservative


反义词: liberal, progressive, modernist, secular


词源:  From "fundamentalism" + "-istic".  "Fundamentalism" itself derives from "fundamental," meaning basic or essential,  ultimately from Latin "fundamentum" (foundation).
