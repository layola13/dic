/ˌpriːdɪˈsaɪsɪv/| adj. |预先决定的；决定性的；先决的 |  Predecisive refers to something that determines or influences a decision before it's made. It suggests a preemptive or anticipatory influence on the outcome.  It's less common than words like "decisive" or "preliminary," but implies a significant impact on the final decision even before the decision-making process fully unfolds. |The predecisive factors in the election were the candidate's strong fundraising and effective media campaign.  |预选因素是候选人强大的资金筹集和有效的媒体宣传。|  influential, determining, crucial |indecisive, inconsequential, unimportant|
