declassification: /ˌdiːˌklæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |解密；解除保密 | The process of removing the classification from something, making it available to the public.  This often applies to government documents or information previously considered secret or confidential.  | The declassification of the documents revealed a shocking truth about the government's involvement.  解密文件揭示了政府参与其中令人震惊的真相。| classification, disclosure | classification, secrecy |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government declassifies documents regularly. = 政府定期解密文件。
This process declassifies sensitive information. = 这个过程解密敏感信息。


一般过去时:
They declassified the files last week. = 他们上周解密了这些文件。
The agency declassified the report after a long review. = 该机构经过长时间审查后解密了这份报告。


现在完成时:
The agency has declassified several important documents. = 该机构已经解密了几份重要文件。
They have declassified information related to the case. = 他们已经解密了与该案相关的资料。


过去完成时:
The information had been declassified before the press conference. = 在新闻发布会之前，信息已被解密。
The documents had already been declassified by the time the investigation began. = 在调查开始时，文件已经被解密了。


将来时:
They will declassify the remaining documents next year. = 他们将在明年解密剩余的文件。
The committee will declassify the report after further review. = 委员会将在进一步审查后解密报告。


虚拟语气:
If they declassified the files, the public would know the truth. = 如果他们解密这些文件，公众就会知道真相。
I wish they would declassify the documents related to this incident. = 我希望他们能解密与这一事件相关的文件。


被动语态:
The documents were declassified by the committee. = 文件由委员会解密。
The information is being declassified gradually. = 信息正在逐步解密。


疑问句:

否定句:
The government did not declassify the documents. = 政府没有解密这些文件。
