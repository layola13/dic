gunslinger:/ˈɡʌnslɪŋər/| n. |枪手，快枪手| A gunslinger is a person who is skilled in using guns, especially one who is quick to draw and shoot.  They are often depicted in Western films and novels as outlaws or lawmen who are experts in gunfights. |Example:  The gunslinger drew his pistol with lightning speed.  这个枪手以闪电般的速度拔出了他的手枪。

The legendary gunslinger was known throughout the Wild West.  这位传奇的枪手在整个狂野西部都很有名。|近义词：gunman, pistolero, sharp-shooter |反义词：civilian, pacifist

词源:
The word "gunslinger" is a compound word, combining "gun" (a firearm) and "slinger" (someone who throws or uses something skillfully).  The suffix "-er" indicates a person who performs the action.  The term arose in the American Old West, reflecting the era's prevalent gun culture.
