materialisms:/məˈtɪəriəlɪzəm/| n. |唯物主义；物质至上主义|The philosophical theory that all phenomena are ultimately physical; the view that physical matter is the only or fundamental reality.  It emphasizes the importance of material possessions and physical comfort over spiritual or intellectual values.  |例句：Marxism is a form of historical materialism. 马克思主义是一种历史唯物主义。

例句：His life is characterized by a crass materialism. 他的生活以粗俗的物质至上主义为特征。


近义词：physicalism, pragmatism, secularism |反义词：idealism, spiritualism, asceticism |词性：名词

词源:
The word "materialism" derives from the Latin word "materialis," meaning "relating to matter."  The suffix "-ism" indicates a doctrine, system, or practice.  Therefore, "materialism" signifies a philosophical system or belief emphasizing the importance of matter.
