settled:/ˈsɛtəld/ | adj., v. | 解决的；已定居的；平静的；安顿下来的 |  to put or come to a state of rest, quiet, or stability; to establish oneself in a place | 近义词：resolved, established, calm, quiet | 反义词：unsettled, unresolved, turbulent, restless


英文解析:

As an adjective, "settled" describes something that is established, decided, or no longer changing. It can also describe a person who has established a permanent home in a place.  As a verb, "settled" means to bring something to an end or to establish oneself in a place.


例句：

adj. (形容词):

The argument was finally settled.  = 争论最终解决了。

The family is settled in their new home. = 这家人在新家安顿下来了。


v. (动词):

They settled their differences amicably. = 他们友好地解决了分歧。

He settled down in a small village after retirement. = 他退休后在一个小村庄定居下来。


USAGE->

一般现在时:

The sun settles in the west. = 太阳在西方落下。

He settles his accounts every month. = 他每月结算账目。


一般过去时:

The dust settled after the explosion. = 爆炸后尘埃落定。

She settled the bill before leaving the restaurant. = 她离开餐馆前结了账。


现在完成时:

They have settled into their new routine. = 他们已经适应了新的生活节奏。

The dispute has been settled out of court. = 纠纷已庭外和解。


过去完成时:

The matter had been settled before he arrived. = 他到达之前此事已解决。

They had settled the issue before the meeting. = 会议前他们已解决了这个问题。


将来时:

We will settle the score someday. = 我们总有一天会算账的。

The dispute will be settled through arbitration. = 争议将通过仲裁解决。


虚拟语气:

If only I had settled my debts earlier! = 要是我早点还清债务就好了！

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
