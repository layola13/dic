judicially:/dʒuˈdɪʃəli/| adv.| 司法上地；审判上地；公正地 | In a manner pertaining to the administration of justice; according to the rules and principles of law.  | 例句：The case was handled judicially.  此案已依法办理。 The judge ruled judicially, based on the evidence presented.  法官根据提出的证据作出了公正的裁决。|近义词：legally, impartially, fairly |反义词：illegally, unfairly, unjustly

一般现在时:
The court judicially reviews all cases. = 法院对所有案件进行司法审查。
He judicially decides all disputes. = 他公正地裁决所有纠纷。


一般过去时:
The judge judicially reviewed the evidence. = 法官对证据进行了司法审查。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

The jury had judicially weighed the evidence before reaching a verdict. = 陪审团在作出裁决之前已经对证据进行了司法权衡。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If the evidence were presented judicially, the outcome might be different. = 如果证据是通过司法程序提交的，结果可能会不同。
I wish the case had been handled more judicially. = 我希望此案处理得更公正些。


被动语态:
The matter was judicially resolved. = 此事已得到司法解决。
The decision was judicially made. = 此决定是依法作出的。


疑问句:
Was the decision made judicially? = 这一决定是依法做出的吗？
Were the rights of the defendant judicially protected? = 被告的权利是否得到了司法保护？


否定句:
The case was not handled judicially. = 此案处理不公正。
The judge did not act judicially in this instance. = 法官在这种情况下没有公正行事。

词源:
From judicial + -ly.  Judicial comes from Late Latin *judicialis, from Latin iudicium "judgment," from iūdex "judge," from iūs "law" + dicere "to say."
