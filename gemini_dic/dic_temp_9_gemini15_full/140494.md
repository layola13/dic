older:/ˈoʊldər/| adj.|较老的，年纪较大的|Older refers to something or someone that is more advanced in age than something or someone else. It implies a relative comparison, not necessarily a great age. |例句：My older brother is a doctor.  我的哥哥是一位医生。

例句：This is an older model of car. 这是一款较老款的汽车。

近义词：elder, senior, more mature

反义词：younger, junior


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is older than his sister. 他比他妹妹大。
The older building is more charming.  那栋较老的建筑更迷人。


一般过去时:
She was older than she looked. 她看起来比实际年龄年轻。
The car was older than I thought. 那辆车比我想象的还要旧。


现在完成时:
He has become older since I last saw him. 自从我上次见到他以来，他变老了。
The technology has become older and less efficient. 这项技术已经过时且效率低下。


过去完成时:
She had been older than me for many years.  她比我大很多年了。
The painting had been older than the museum itself.  这幅画比博物馆本身还要古老。


将来时:
He will be older next year. 他明年会更大。
The machine will be older and require more maintenance. 这台机器会更旧，需要更多的维护。


虚拟语气:
If he were older, he would understand. 如果他年纪再大一些，他就会明白了。
I wish I were older and wiser. 我希望我更年长、更明智。


被动语态:
The older house was sold quickly. 那栋老房子很快就卖掉了。
The older technology is being replaced with new systems. 较旧的技术正在被新的系统所取代。


疑问句:
Is he older than you? 他比你大吗？
Which building is older? 哪栋建筑物更旧？


否定句:
He is not older than me. 他不比我大。
The device is not older than a year. 这设备还不满一年。
