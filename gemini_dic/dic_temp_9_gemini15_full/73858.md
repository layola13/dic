formulated:/ˈfɔːrmjuleɪtɪd/ | v. (past participle) | 制定的；构想的；表达的 | To create or develop something, typically a plan, system, or product, by careful thought and planning.  | The company formulated a new marketing strategy. 公司制定了一个新的营销策略。 | developed, devised, created, designed | haphazard, unplanned | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They formulate new policies every year. = 他们每年都制定新的政策。
The chef carefully formulates his recipes. = 厨师仔细地制定他的食谱。

一般过去时:
She formulated a plan to escape. = 她制定了一个逃跑计划。
The scientists formulated a hypothesis. = 科学家们制定了一个假设。

现在完成时:
They have formulated a response to the crisis. = 他们已经制定了应对危机的方案。
The team has formulated a comprehensive strategy. = 团队已经制定了一个全面的策略。

过去完成时:
They had formulated their arguments before the debate. = 他们在辩论前已经形成了自己的论点。
The committee had formulated a preliminary report. = 委员会已经制定了一份初步报告。

将来时:
We will formulate a new approach next week. = 我们下周将制定一种新的方法。
The government will formulate its budget soon. = 政府很快就会制定预算。

虚拟语气:
If they formulated a better plan, they would succeed. = 如果他们制定一个更好的计划，他们就会成功。
I wish they had formulated a clearer policy. = 我希望他们制定一个更清晰的政策。

被动语态:
The policy was formulated by the committee. = 该政策是由委员会制定的。
A new strategy is being formulated by the management. = 管理层正在制定一项新的战略。

疑问句:
How was the plan formulated? = 这个计划是怎么制定的？
Has a new marketing strategy been formulated yet? = 新的营销策略制定好了吗？

否定句:
They did not formulate a clear plan. = 他们没有制定一个清晰的计划。
The company has not yet formulated its response. = 公司尚未制定其回应。
