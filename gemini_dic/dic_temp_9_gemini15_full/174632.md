quivery:/ˈkwɪvəri/| adj.|颤抖的；发抖的；虚弱的|Trembling or shaking slightly; indicating nervousness, weakness, or fear.  |例句：Her voice was quivery with emotion.  她的声音因激动而颤抖。

例句：His hands were quivery as he held the fragile vase. 他拿着易碎的花瓶，双手颤抖。


近义词：trembling, shaky, shaky, quivering, unsteady,fluttering|反义词：steady, firm, stable|词性:形容词

词源:
The word "quivery" derives from the Old English word "cwic" meaning "alive, quick" and implying movement or vibration.  This evolved through Middle English forms and ultimately led to words like "quiver" (to tremble) and its adjectival form "quivery".
