constituent:/kənˈstɪtʃuənt/| n. adj.|成分；选民；构成要素；有构成作用的|A constituent part or element of something.  A voter who elects a representative. |例句：Water is a constituent of most living things.  水是大多数生物的组成部分。|例句：She's a constituent of the local MP. 她是当地国会议员的选民。|近义词：component, element, ingredient, voter|反义词：whole, entirety


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The water is a constituent of the solution. = 水是溶液的成分。
Sugar is a key constituent of many baked goods. = 糖是许多烘焙食品的关键成分。


一般过去时:
Hydrogen and oxygen were constituents of the water molecule. = 氢和氧是水分子的组成部分。
He was a loyal constituent of the senator for many years. = 多年来，他一直是这位参议员忠实的选民。

She has been a consistent constituent of the party for over two decades. = 二十多年来，她一直是该党的一位坚定选民。


过去完成时:

Before the election, he had always been a reliable constituent of the ruling party. = 在选举之前，他一直是执政党可靠的选民。
