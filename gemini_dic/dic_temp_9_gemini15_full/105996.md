knappish:/ˈnæpɪʃ/| adj.|暴躁的；易怒的；不友好的|Short-tempered; irritable; unfriendly.  The word suggests a tendency towards sudden outbursts of anger or displeasure, often in a somewhat petty or insignificant way.  It implies a general disposition rather than a single instance of bad temper.|例句：He was feeling rather knappish after a long day at work. 他在工作了一整天后感到相当暴躁。

例句：She’s usually quite pleasant, but she can be very knappish in the mornings. 她通常很讨人喜欢，但早上可能会非常暴躁。


近义词：irritable, grumpy, crotchety, testy, surly, snappish

反义词：pleasant, amiable, good-natured, cheerful, affable


词源:  The word's origin is uncertain, but it's likely related to the obsolete word "knapp," meaning "to snap" or "to bite," suggesting a sharp or sudden display of temper.  The "-ish" suffix indicates a quality or characteristic.
