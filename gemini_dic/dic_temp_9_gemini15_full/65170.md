ethnographically:/ˌɛθnəˈɡræfɪkli/| adv. |人种学地；民族志地|In an ethnographic manner; according to the methods of ethnography.  It involves studying people and cultures in their natural settings, using methods like participant observation, interviews, and analysis of artifacts.|例句：The researcher studied the community ethnographically, immersing herself in their daily lives. 研究人员以民族志的方式研究了这个社区，沉浸在他们的日常生活中。The tribe’s social structures were analyzed ethnographically. 该部落的社会结构是以人种学的方式进行分析的。|近义词：anthropologically, culturally|反义词：无
