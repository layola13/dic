quinquevir: /ˈkwɪŋkwɪvər/ | n. |五人委员会成员；五人执政官 | A member of a board or commission of five; specifically, a member of any of several Roman boards of five.  The word is derived from the Latin "quinque" (five) and "vir" (man). Quinqueviri were often appointed to handle specific tasks or responsibilities within the Roman government. For example, there were quinqueviri who oversaw the construction of public works. |The quinqueviri were responsible for overseeing the construction of the new aqueduct. 五人委员会负责监督新渡槽的建设。|近义词：commissioner, official |反义词： N/A
