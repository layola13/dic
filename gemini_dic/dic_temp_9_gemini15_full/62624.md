enforced:/ɪnˈfɔːrst/| v.|强制执行的；被强迫的 | to compel observance of or compliance with (a law, rule, etc.) | 例句：The new law was enforced strictly.  新法律被严格执行。 | 例句：He was enforced to work overtime. 他被迫加班。|近义词：imposed, implemented, mandated |反义词：ignored, neglected, disregarded |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government enforces strict environmental regulations. = 政府强制执行严格的环境法规。
They enforce the rules fairly. = 他们公平地执行规则。

一般过去时:
The police enforced the curfew. = 警察强制执行宵禁。
The judge enforced the contract. = 法官强制执行合同。

现在完成时:
The company has enforced a new dress code. = 公司已经实施了新的着装规范。
They have enforced the ban successfully. = 他们已经成功地实施了禁令。

过去完成时:
The authorities had enforced martial law before the riots. = 暴乱发生前，当局已经实施了戒严。
The school had enforced a strict no-phone policy. = 学校之前已经执行了严格的禁手机政策。

将来时:
The government will enforce stricter penalties. = 政府将实施更严格的处罚。
They will enforce the new regulations next month. = 他们将在下个月实施新的规章。

虚拟语气:
If they enforced the rules properly, there would be less crime. = 如果他们正确执行规则，犯罪就会减少。
I wish they enforced the speed limit more strictly. = 我希望他们更严格地执行限速规定。

被动语态:
The law was enforced effectively. = 这项法律得到了有效的执行。
The rules are enforced by the school administration. = 这些规则由学校管理部门执行。

疑问句:
Are the new regulations being enforced? = 新规章正在执行吗？

否定句:
