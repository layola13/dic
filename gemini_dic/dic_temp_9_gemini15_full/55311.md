dissolved:/dɪˈzɒlvd/| vt. |溶解；解散；消除；(感情等)破裂|To dissolve something means to make it become liquid by mixing it with a liquid, or to make something such as a company, parliament, or marriage officially end.  |例句：The sugar dissolved in the tea. 糖溶解在茶里了。  The parliament was dissolved. 国会解散了。|近义词：melt, break up, disperse, disband |反义词：solidify, form, unite, consolidate

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sugar dissolves quickly in hot water. = 糖在热水中迅速溶解。
The committee dissolves after each meeting. = 委员会每次会议后都解散。


一般过去时:
The company dissolved last year. = 公司去年解散了。
The snow dissolved in the warm sun. = 雪在温暖的阳光下融化了。


现在完成时:
The problem has dissolved itself. = 问题已经自行解决了。
The tension has dissolved since the agreement. = 达成协议后，紧张气氛已经消散了。


过去完成时:
The marriage had dissolved before he met her. = 在他遇到她之前，婚姻就已经破裂了。
The government had dissolved before the election. = 政府在选举前就已经解散了。


将来时:
The snow will dissolve as the temperature rises. = 随着气温上升，雪会融化。
The company will dissolve if it doesn't improve its performance. = 如果公司业绩不改善，它将会解散。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:
