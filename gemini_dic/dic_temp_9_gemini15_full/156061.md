photophthalmia: /ˌfoʊtoʊfˈθælmiə/ | n. | 光性眼炎 | Inflammation of the eye caused by exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation, such as from sunlight, welding arcs, or other intense light sources.  The condition is characterized by pain, redness, and photophobia (sensitivity to light).|  The welder suffered from photophthalmia after a long day working without proper eye protection. 电焊工在没有适当护目镜的情况下工作了一整天后患上了光性眼炎。|  keratitis, conjunctivitis |  (no direct antonym, but the absence of the condition could be described as "healthy eyes") | 名词
