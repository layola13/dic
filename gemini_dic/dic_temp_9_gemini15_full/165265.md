preinsurance: /ˌpriːɪnˈʃʊərəns/ | n. | 预保险；前期保险 | Insurance taken out before a major insurance policy.  It often covers potential losses during the period between application and acceptance of the main policy.  This can be particularly important for high-value assets or projects where a significant delay in obtaining full coverage could be financially damaging. | The company offered preinsurance to protect against potential losses during the lengthy approval process for the construction project. = 该公司提供预保险，以保护在建筑项目的漫长审批过程中可能造成的损失。

The construction company secured preinsurance to cover potential damages before the main policy was finalized. = 建设公司在主要保单最终确定之前获得了预保险以支付潜在损失。


近义词: preliminary insurance, interim insurance | 反义词: post-insurance (this is not a common term)

词源:
The word "preinsurance" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the noun "insurance."  It's a relatively modern term reflecting the increasing need for coverage during the often lengthy processes of securing major insurance policies.
