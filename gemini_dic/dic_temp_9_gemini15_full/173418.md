quasi-English:/ˈkwɑːziˈɪŋɡlɪʃ/| adj. |准英语；似英语；类英语|English that is not standard or correct, often containing elements of other languages or dialects, or using non-standard grammatical constructions.  It often refers to the simplified or non-native-like English used in specific contexts, such as pidgins or creoles, or in informal settings.|例句：The instructions were written in a kind of quasi-English that was difficult to understand.  这些说明是用一种难以理解的准英语写的。|近义词：pidgin, jargon, slang, broken English |反义词：standard English, formal English
