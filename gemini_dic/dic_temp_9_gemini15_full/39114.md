confronts:/kənˈfrʌnts/| vt.|面对；对抗；使面临；质问|to meet someone or something that is difficult or challenging; to deal with a problem or difficult situation; to challenge someone to a fight or argument; to accuse someone of something.|例句：He confronts his fears head-on. 他勇敢地面对自己的恐惧。|She confronted him with the evidence. 她用证据质问他。|近义词：face, encounter, challenge, oppose|反义词：avoid, evade, ignore|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He confronts his problems daily. = 他每天都面对他的问题。
The company confronts a significant challenge. = 公司面临着巨大的挑战。


一般过去时:
She confronted her boss about the unfair treatment. = 她向老板投诉不公平待遇。
The army confronted the enemy at dawn. = 军队在黎明时与敌人交战。


现在完成时:
We have confronted many obstacles in this project. = 在这个项目中，我们已经克服了许多障碍。
He has confronted his past mistakes and learned from them. = 他直面过去的错误并从中吸取教训。


过去完成时:
They had confronted similar situations before. = 他们以前曾面临过类似的情况。
She had confronted the thief before he could escape. = 在小偷逃跑前，她已经与之对质了。


将来时:
We will confront this issue at the next meeting. = 我们将在下次会议上讨论这个问题。
They will confront the difficulties with courage. = 他们将勇敢地面对困难。


虚拟语气:
If I confronted him, I would tell him the truth. = 如果我面对他，我会告诉他真相。
I wish I had confronted him sooner. = 我真希望我早点和他对质。


被动语态:
The problem was confronted by the team. = 这个问题由团队解决了。
The soldiers were confronted by a sudden attack. = 士兵们遭遇了突然袭击。


疑问句:
How will you confront this crisis? = 你将如何应对这场危机？
What challenges will we confront in the future? = 未来我们将面临哪些挑战？


否定句:
He didn't confront his fears. = 他没有面对他的恐惧。
She didn't confront him about his lies. = 她没有就他的谎言质问他。


PHRASE->
confront a problem = 面对问题
confront the truth = 面对真相
confront the enemy = 与敌人对抗
confront a challenge = 面对挑战
confront reality = 面对现实
