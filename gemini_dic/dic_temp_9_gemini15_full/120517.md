minutia:/mɪˈnjuːʃiə/| n. |细微之处，琐碎细节 |minute details; trivial matters;  The detective meticulously examined every minutia of the crime scene.  侦探一丝不苟地检查了犯罪现场的每一个细节。|近义词：detail, trifle, nicety |反义词：gist, essence


英文解析：Minutia refers to a very small or insignificant detail.  It often implies a large number of such details, collectively forming a complex picture.  The plural form is also "minutiae".


例句：

The report was filled with minutiae, making it difficult to discern the main points. 报告充满了琐碎的细节，难以辨别要点。

He obsessed over the minutiae of his work, often losing sight of the bigger picture. 他沉迷于工作的细枝末节，常常忽略了大局。


近义词：detail, trifle, nicety, particular, subtlety

反义词：gist, essence, core, main point


词源：The word "minutia" comes from the Latin word "minutus," meaning "small" or "minute."  It entered English in the 17th century.
