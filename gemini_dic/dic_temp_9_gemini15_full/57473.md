drafts:/drɑːfts/|ˈdræfts/| n. |草稿；汇票；通风；一股气流|a preliminary version of a piece of writing |例句：I've finished the first draft of my essay.  我已经完成了我的论文初稿。|例句：He paid for the goods by drafts. 他用汇票支付货款。|近义词：sketch, outline, preliminary version,  |反义词：final version, finished product


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The wind drafts through the broken window. = 风从破窗户吹进来。
He drafts a letter to his friend. = 他起草了一封信给他的朋友。


一般过去时:
They drafted a new constitution. = 他们起草了一部新宪法。

The committee has drafted a report on the issue. = 委员会已经起草了一份关于这个问题的报告。


过去完成时:
