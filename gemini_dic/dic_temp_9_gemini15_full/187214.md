robbed:/rɒbd/ /rɑːbd/ | vt. |抢劫；被剥夺|To take property unlawfully from (a person or place) by force or threat of force.  It can also mean to deprive someone of something valuable. |例句：The bank was robbed last night. = 银行昨晚被抢劫了。

The thief robbed me of my wallet. = 小偷抢走了我的钱包。|近义词：plunder, steal, burglarize |反义词：give, donate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He robs banks. = 他抢劫银行。
She robs the poor. = 她剥削穷人。

一般过去时:
They robbed the store. = 他们抢劫了商店。
The hurricane robbed them of their home. = 飓风夺走了他们的家园。

现在完成时:
They have robbed several houses this month. = 这个月他们已经抢劫了几所房子。
The drought has robbed the land of its fertility. = 干旱使这片土地失去了肥力。

过去完成时:
He had robbed the bank before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，他已经抢劫了银行。
The storm had robbed them of all their possessions. = 暴风雨夺走了他们所有的财产。

将来时:
They will rob the train tomorrow. = 他们明天将抢劫火车。
The new policy will rob many people of their rights. = 新政策将剥夺许多人的权利。

虚拟语气:
If they robbed the store, they would be arrested. = 如果他们抢劫商店，他们会被逮捕。
I wish they hadn't robbed me. = 我真希望他们没有抢劫我。

被动语态:
The bank was robbed last night. = 银行昨晚被抢劫了。
He was robbed of his inheritance. = 他被剥夺了继承权。

疑问句:
Were you robbed? = 你被抢劫了吗？
Has she been robbed of her dignity? = 她是否被剥夺了尊严？

否定句:
They didn't rob the bank. = 他们没有抢劫银行。
He wasn't robbed of his chance. = 他没有被剥夺机会。
