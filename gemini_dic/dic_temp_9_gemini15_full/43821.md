creolism:/ˈkriːəˌlɪzəm/| n. |克里奥尔语现象，克里奥尔文化|The study of creole languages and cultures.  Creolism refers to the linguistic and cultural processes involved in the development and use of creole languages. It encompasses the historical, social, and linguistic factors contributing to their formation and evolution.  |Example:  The research paper explores the creolism of the Caribbean islands, focusing on the interplay between colonial languages and indigenous dialects.  研究论文探讨了加勒比群岛的克里奥尔语现象，重点关注殖民语言和当地方言之间的相互作用。|近义词：Creole language development, Creolization|反义词：Standard language preservation
