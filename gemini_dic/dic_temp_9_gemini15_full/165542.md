premarkets:/ˈpriːmɑːrkɪts/| n. |盘前交易，市场开盘前交易|The period of trading before the official opening of a stock exchange.  Pre-market trading allows investors to buy and sell securities before the regular trading session begins.|例句：Premarket trading activity often provides clues about the day's market direction. 盘前交易活动通常能为当天的市场方向提供线索。|例句：The stock jumped 5% in premarkets after the announcement.  在公告发布后，该股票在盘前交易中上涨了5%。|近义词：before-market trading, early trading|反义词：after-hours trading, post-market trading|名词
