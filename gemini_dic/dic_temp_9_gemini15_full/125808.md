murky:/ˈmɜːrki/ | adj. | 昏暗的；模糊不清的；泥泞的 |  Dark, unclear, and difficult to see through; obscure or indistinct.  | The murky water made it difficult to see the bottom of the lake.  湖水浑浊，难以看到湖底。 The future of the company remains murky.  公司的未来仍然不明朗。 | cloudy, obscure, muddy, hazy | clear, bright, transparent, lucid


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The water is murky. = 水很浑浊。
The details of the plan are murky. = 计划的细节模糊不清。


一般过去时:
The river was murky after the storm. = 暴风雨过后，河水变得浑浊。
His explanation was murky and unconvincing. = 他的解释含糊不清且令人难以信服。


现在完成时:
The lake has become murky due to pollution. = 由于污染，湖水变得浑浊。
The situation has remained murky despite investigations. = 尽管进行了调查，情况仍然不明朗。


过去完成时:
The water had been murky for weeks before the cleanup began. = 在清理工作开始前的几周里，水一直很浑浊。
The evidence had become murky due to the passage of time. = 由于时间的流逝，证据变得模糊不清。


将来时:
The weather forecast predicts murky skies tomorrow. = 天气预报预测明天天空将阴沉。
The future of the project will likely remain murky for some time. = 项目的未来很可能在一段时间内仍然不明朗。


虚拟语气:
If the water were murky, we wouldn't swim. = 如果水浑浊，我们就不游泳。
If the situation were less murky, we could make a decision. = 如果情况不那么模糊，我们就能做出决定。


被动语态:
The details were made murky by the witness's evasiveness. = 证人的闪烁其词使细节变得模糊不清。
The view was made murky by the fog. = 大雾使视野变得模糊。


疑问句:
Is the water murky? = 水浑浊吗？
Is the future of the negotiations murky? = 谈判的未来不明朗吗？


否定句:
The water is not murky; it's crystal clear. = 水不浑浊；它是清澈见底的。
The evidence is not murky; it's quite clear. = 证据并非模糊不清；它相当清晰。
