hunches:/hʌntʃɪz/| n.|预感，直觉；驼背|A hunch is a feeling or guess based on intuition rather than on facts.  It can also refer to a rounded posture of the back. |例句：I had a hunch that he was lying. 我预感到他在说谎。

例句：He sat hunched over his desk. 他驼着背坐在书桌前。|近义词：feeling, intuition, suspicion, premonition|反义词：certainty, fact, proof


USAGE->

一般现在时:
I have a hunch that it will rain. = 我预感会下雨。
She often has hunches about people's true characters. = 她经常对人们的真实性格有预感。


一般过去时:
I had a hunch that something was wrong. = 我预感有什么不对劲。
He had a hunch the police were watching him. = 他预感警察在监视他。


现在完成时:
I've had a hunch about this project for weeks. = 我对这个项目已经有了几周的预感。
She's had many hunches that proved correct. = 她有过许多被证明正确的预感。


过去完成时:
I had had a hunch that he would quit his job. = 我之前就预感到他会辞职。
She had had a hunch the stock market would crash. = 她之前就预感到股市会崩盘。


将来时:
I think I'll have a hunch about the test results. = 我想我会对考试结果有所预感。
He believes he will have a hunch about the future. = 他相信他会对未来有所预感。


虚拟语气:
If I had a hunch, I would tell you. = 如果我有预感，我会告诉你的。
I wish I had a hunch about what to do next. = 我希望我对接下来该做什么有所预感。


被动语态:
(Hunches are rarely used in passive voice because it’s a feeling or intuition.)


疑问句:
Do you have a hunch about who did it? = 你对是谁干的有什么预感吗？
Have you ever had a hunch that turned out to be right? = 你曾经有过被证明正确的预感吗？


否定句:
I don't have a hunch about the answer. = 我对答案没有预感。
She didn't have a hunch about what would happen. = 她对将会发生什么没有预感。


PHRASE->
have a hunch = 有预感
follow your hunch = 相信你的直觉
gut hunch = 直觉
strong hunch = 强烈的预感
a hunch that = 一个预感……
