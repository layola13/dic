opisthobranchiate:/ɒpˈsɪθəʊbræŋkieɪt/| adj. |后鳃类的；后鳃亚纲的|Having gills located in the posterior part of the body.  Opisthobranchiate refers to a diverse group of sea slugs and other marine gastropods characterized by their gills located at the rear of their bodies. They are known for their striking colors and often unusual body forms.|Example: Many opisthobranchiate mollusks exhibit vibrant coloration as a form of aposematism.|近义词: opisthobranch (noun)|反义词:None.
