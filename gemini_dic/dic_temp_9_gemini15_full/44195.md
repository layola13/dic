croaky:/ˈkroʊki/| adj. |沙哑的，嘶哑的； 呱呱叫的 | Having a harsh, rough voice, like that of a frog.  The sound is often associated with illness or tiredness. |例句：His voice was croaky from shouting. 他喊哑了嗓子。|例句：The frog made a croaky sound. 青蛙发出呱呱的叫声。|近义词：raspy, hoarse, gravelly |反义词：clear, smooth


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His voice is croaky this morning. = 他今天早上声音沙哑。
The frog's croak is croaky. = 青蛙的叫声很沙哑。


一般过去时:
Her voice was croaky after the concert. = 音乐会后，她的声音沙哑了。
The old crow's croak was croaky and low. = 那只老乌鸦的叫声沙哑而低沉。


现在完成时:
My throat has been croaky all week. = 我整个星期嗓子都哑了。
He has been croaky since he caught a cold. = 他自从感冒后就一直声音沙哑。


过去完成时:
His voice had been croaky for days before he saw the doctor. = 在他去看医生之前，他的声音已经沙哑了好几天了。
The speaker's voice had been croaky throughout the presentation. = 演讲者在整个演讲过程中声音都是沙哑的。


将来时:
His voice will likely be croaky after the long speech. = 长时间的演讲后，他的声音可能会沙哑。
I think my voice will be croaky tomorrow after singing. = 我想明天唱歌后我的嗓子会哑。


虚拟语气:
If I had drunk more water, my voice wouldn't be so croaky. = 如果我多喝点水，我的声音就不会这么沙哑了。
I wish my voice weren't so croaky. = 我希望我的声音不要这么沙哑。


被动语态:
(Not naturally used in passive voice.  The adjective "croaky" describes a state, not an action performed upon something.)


疑问句:

否定句:
His voice wasn't croaky at all. = 他的声音一点也不沙哑。
