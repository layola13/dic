congregativeness:/ˌkɒŋɡrɪˈɡeɪtɪvnəs/ | n. | 群居性；聚集倾向 | The tendency to congregate or gather together, especially in groups.  This term often refers to social animals that live in groups for mutual benefit, such as protection or cooperation in finding food.  | Example: The high congregativeness of this species of bird makes them easy to study in large flocks.  这种鸟类的高度群居性使得它们很容易在大群中被研究。 | 近义词：gregariousness, sociability, flocking | 反义词：solitariness, isolation, seclusion


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "hello".  It describes a characteristic or tendency, not an action.  Therefore, typical verb tenses don't apply.  However, we can use it in different sentence structures:

Simple Present:  The sheep's congregativeness is evident in their grazing behavior.  羊群的群居性在其觅食行为中显而易见。
Present Continuous:  The researchers are studying the congregativeness of the penguins during breeding season. 研究人员正在研究企鹅在繁殖季节的群居性。


Past Simple: The surprising congregativeness of the usually solitary cats was noted by the observers.  观察者注意到通常独居的猫令人惊讶的群居性。
Past Continuous: The scientists were observing the increasing congregativeness of the migrating birds. 科学家们正在观察迁徙鸟类日益增长的群居性。


Present Perfect:  Scientists have observed a decrease in the congregativeness of this particular species.  科学家们已经观察到这个特定物种的群居性下降了。
Present Perfect Continuous: The change in habitat has been affecting the congregativeness of the local deer population.栖息地的变化一直在影响当地鹿群的群居性。


Future Simple:  We predict a higher congregativeness among the wolves during the winter months. 我们预测狼群在冬季的群居性会更高。
Future Continuous: They will be observing the congregativeness of the insects over the next few weeks. 他们将在接下来的几周内观察昆虫的群居性。


Conditional Sentences:  If the congregativeness of the herd increases, they will be more vulnerable to predators. 如果兽群的群居性增加，它们将更容易受到捕食者的攻击。
If the environment were more hospitable, the congregativeness of the animals might improve. 如果环境更适宜居住，动物的群居性可能会改善。
