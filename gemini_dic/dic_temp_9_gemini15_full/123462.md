monosaccharide:/ˌmɒnəˈsækəraɪd/| n. |单糖|A simple sugar that is the basic subunit of a carbohydrate.  It cannot be hydrolyzed to a simpler sugar. Monosaccharides are classified according to the number of carbon atoms they contain (triose, tetrose, pentose, hexose, etc.) and the position of their carbonyl group (aldose or ketose).|Example: Glucose, fructose, and galactose are examples of monosaccharides.|近义词：simple sugar|反义词：polysaccharide
