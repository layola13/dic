neocriticism:/ˌniːoʊˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/| n. |新批评; 新批评主义|A literary movement that emphasizes close reading of the text itself, without reference to the author's biography, historical context, or other external factors.  Neocriticism focuses on the text's internal structure, language, and meaning.|例句：Neocriticism championed the objective analysis of literary works. 新批评主义提倡对文学作品进行客观分析。

例句：The professor's lecture focused on the principles of neocriticism in analyzing poetry. 教授的讲座重点介绍了在新批评主义中分析诗歌的原则。


近义词：formalism, close reading, textual analysis |反义词：historical criticism, biographical criticism, reader-response criticism|词性:名词
