scorched:/ˈskɔːrtʃt/| adj. v.| 烧焦的；枯萎的；严厉批评的 |  To burn the surface of something with heat; to criticize severely.  |例句：The intense heat scorched the grass.  烈日把草烧焦了。

The chef scorched the onions to add flavor. 厨师把洋葱烤焦以增加风味。

近义词：burnt, singed, seared, charred, criticized harshly |反义词：unharmed, undamaged, praised

一般现在时:
The sun scorches the earth daily. = 太阳每天灼烧着大地。
He scorches the vegetables before adding them to the soup. = 他在把蔬菜加入汤中之前先将其烤焦。

一般过去时:
The fire scorched the wooden fence. = 大火烧焦了木栅栏。
She scorched the letter with her harsh words. = 她用尖刻的话语严厉批评了这封信。

现在完成时:
The drought has scorched the land. = 干旱已经使土地干枯。
His reputation has been scorched by the scandal. = 他的名誉因丑闻而受损。

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

疑问句:
Was the cake scorched? = 蛋糕烤焦了吗？

The cake wasn't scorched, it was perfectly browned. = 蛋糕没有烤焦，它烤得恰到好处。
