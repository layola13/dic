praemaxilla:/ˌpriːmækˈsɪlə/| n. |前上颌骨|The praemaxilla is a paired bone in the upper jaw of many vertebrates, particularly in fishes and amphibians. It is located at the very tip of the snout, anterior to the maxilla.  In humans and other mammals, the praemaxilla is fused with other bones of the upper jaw, forming part of the premaxillary bone.
