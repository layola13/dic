nonreasons:/ˈnɒnˈriːzənz/ | n. |非理由，伪理由，借口 |Things presented as reasons but which are not valid or genuine reasons.  They are often used to justify actions or decisions that are actually based on other, perhaps less defensible, motivations. |Example: He gave several nonreasons for missing the deadline, none of which were convincing. 他给出几个错过截止日期的非理由，没有一个令人信服。 |近义词：excuses, pretexts, rationalizations, specious arguments |反义词：reasons, justifications, valid arguments


USAGE->

This word, being a noun, doesn't conjugate like verbs.  The usage depends on the sentence structure and context.  Here are some examples showing different sentence structures with "nonreasons":

Simple Sentence:
His nonreasons were easily refuted. = 他的伪理由很容易被驳斥。

Complex Sentence:
Because of his nonreasons, the project was delayed. = 由于他的借口，项目被延误了。

Compound Sentence:
She offered several nonreasons, but I saw through them, and the truth came out. = 她提供了几个不成立的理由，但我识破了它们，真相大白了。

Sentence with a prepositional phrase:
The committee dismissed his arguments as mere nonreasons. = 委员会驳回了他的论点，认为那只是些借口。

Sentence with a relative clause:
The excuses, which were actually nonreasons, were not accepted. = 那些实际上是借口的理由并没有被接受。


PHRASE->

There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "nonreasons".  The word is used more in its standalone meaning.  However, we can show phrases that relate to the concept:

make excuses:  = 找借口
give a pretext: =  给出借口
under the pretext of: =  以...为借口
offer a rationalization: =  给出合理的解释（有时是伪装的）
a flimsy excuse: = 一个站不住脚的借口
