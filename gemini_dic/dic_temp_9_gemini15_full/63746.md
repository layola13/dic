epicedian:/ˌɛpɪˈdiːdʒən/ | n. |挽歌，悼词 | A poem or song of mourning, especially one for a deceased person of high rank or importance.  It often celebrates the life and achievements of the deceased. | Example: Tennyson wrote a famous epicedian for the death of Prince Albert.  田纳西为艾伯特亲王的去世写了一首著名的挽歌。 |近义词：elegy, lament, dirge |反义词：celebration, eulogy (while a eulogy can be celebratory, it's primarily a speech, not a poem)
