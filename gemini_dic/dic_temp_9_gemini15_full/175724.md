rapparee:/ˌræpəˈriː/| n.|游击队员，土匪|A rapparee was a member of a band of Irish robbers and outlaws active during the 17th and 18th centuries, particularly during periods of unrest and conflict.  They often operated in small, mobile groups, engaging in raids and ambushes. Their activities were characterized by violence, plunder, and a lack of respect for established authority.|Example: The rapparees harassed the English troops throughout the campaign.|近义词：guerrilla, outlaw, brigand|反义词：law-abiding citizen, soldier (in a regular army)
