climactic:/ˌklaɪˈmæktɪk/| adj.|达到高潮的；决定性的；顶点的| Forming a climax; relating to or of the nature of a climax.  The climactic scene in the movie was incredibly intense.  | The climactic battle determined the fate of the kingdom. |近义词：culminating, crucial, decisive|反义词：anticlimactic, insignificant


例句：

The climactic scene of the play was powerfully moving.  这部剧的高潮部分感人至深。

The climactic moment arrived when the hero finally confronted the villain. 英雄最终与反派对抗的时刻是高潮。


词源:

From the noun climax + -ic.  The noun climax itself originates from Greek κλῖμαξ (klîmax), meaning "ladder, stairs".  The metaphorical sense of "highest point" developed from this.


用法说明:


这个词主要用作形容词，描述达到高潮或顶峰的事件、时刻或场景。它强调的是一个过程中的关键点，通常是戏剧性或重要的转折点。  它也经常用于描述具有决定性意义的事件。


PHRASE->

a climactic moment  高潮时刻
a climactic event  决定性事件
a climactic scene  高潮场面
the climactic battle  决定性战役
climactic ending  高潮结尾
