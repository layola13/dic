cautionry: /ˈkɔːʃənəri/ | adj. | 警告的；警戒的；谨慎的 |  Relating to or expressing a warning or caution; characterized by caution.  | Example: He issued a cautionary tale about the dangers of drug abuse. 他讲述了一个关于吸毒危害的警示故事。 | 近义词：warning, advisory, admonitory | 反义词：encouraging, reassuring


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher gives cautionary advice to her students. = 老师给她的学生们提出了警告性的建议。
He offers cautionary remarks about the risks involved. = 他就其中涉及的风险提出了警告性意见。


一般过去时:
She gave a cautionary speech about the dangers of speeding. = 她发表了一篇关于超速危险的警告性演讲。
The captain issued a cautionary signal before the storm. = 船长在暴风雨来临之前发出警告信号。


现在完成时:
The government has issued numerous cautionary statements about the economic downturn. = 政府已经就经济衰退发出了许多警告性声明。
The scientists have repeatedly given cautionary warnings about climate change. = 科学家们已经多次就气候变化发出了警告。


过去完成时:
They had already received cautionary messages before the earthquake struck. = 地震发生之前，他们已经收到了警告信息。
The hikers had ignored the cautionary signs before getting lost. = 徒步旅行者在迷路之前忽略了警告标志。


将来时:
The meteorologist will issue cautionary alerts about the approaching hurricane. = 气象学家将发布关于即将到来的飓风的警告。
The company will likely send cautionary letters to its negligent employees. = 公司可能会向其疏忽大意的员工发送警告信。


虚拟语气:
If I had heeded the cautionary warnings, I wouldn't have made that mistake. = 如果我听了警告，我就不会犯那个错误了。
I wish I had listened to the cautionary advice given to me. = 我真希望我听了给我的警告建议。


被动语态:
Cautionary measures were taken to prevent further accidents. = 采取了警戒措施以防止进一步的事故。
The risks involved were clearly stated in a cautionary note. = 相关风险在一份警告说明中已明确说明。

否定句:
