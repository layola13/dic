national-socialists:/ˈnæʃənəl ˈsoʊʃəlɪsts/| n. |国家社会主义者，纳粹分子 |National Socialists, commonly known as Nazis, were members of the National Socialist German Workers' Party (NSDAP) in Germany during the 1920s and 1930s.  They advocated a totalitarian regime based on extreme nationalism, racism (especially antisemitism), and a belief in the superiority of the "Aryan race". Their ideology led to the Holocaust and World War II. |The national-socialists rose to power in Germany through propaganda and political maneuvering.|近义词：Nazis, Fascists|反义词：anti-fascists, communists, democrats
