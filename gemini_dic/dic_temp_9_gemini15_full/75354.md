frother:/ˈfrɒðər/ /ˈfrɒðɚ/| n. v.|起泡器；使起泡的人或物；使…起泡沫|A frother is a device or substance that creates foam or froth.  It can also refer to someone or something that causes excitement or agitation. |例句：The barista used a milk frother to create a creamy latte.  咖啡师用奶泡器制作了一杯奶油拉花拿铁。

The protesters were frothing at the mouth, angry about the government's decision.  抗议者们怒气冲冲，对政府的决定大发雷霆。|近义词：foam maker, agitator|反义词：flattener, calmer

词源:
The word "frother" comes from the verb "froth," meaning to form a froth or foam.  "Froth" itself has Old English roots, ultimately related to words meaning "foam" or "spume."  The "-er" suffix denotes an agent or instrument.
