liar:/ˈlaɪər/| n. |说谎者，骗子| A person who tells lies.  |He's a liar; I don't believe a word he says. 他是个骗子；我一句话也不相信他说的。| deceiver, fibber, fabricator |truth-teller, honest person|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He lies to his parents every day. = 他每天都骗他的父母。
She lies about her age. = 她谎报年龄。


一般过去时:
He lied to me about the accident. = 他向我撒谎了关于事故的事。
She lied under oath in court. = 她在法庭上做了伪证。


现在完成时:
He has lied to me repeatedly. = 他一再向我撒谎。
She has lied about her qualifications. = 她对自己的资历撒了谎。


过去完成时:
He had lied to the police before they caught him. = 他在被警察抓住之前就对警察撒了谎。
She had lied to her friends for years. = 她多年来一直在欺骗她的朋友。


将来时:
He will lie again if given the chance. = 如果有机会，他会再次撒谎。
She will lie to protect herself. = 她会撒谎来保护自己。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't lied, he wouldn't be in this trouble. = 如果他没有撒谎，他就不会有这样的麻烦。
I wish I hadn't lied to her. = 我真希望我没有对她撒谎。


被动语态:
The truth was lied about by the witness. = 证人对真相撒了谎。
Lies were told to cover up the scandal. = 谎言被用来掩盖丑闻。


疑问句:
Is he a liar? = 他是个说谎者吗？
Did she lie to you? = 她对你撒谎了吗？


否定句:
He doesn't lie often. = 他不常说谎。
She didn't lie to the police. = 她没有向警方撒谎。



PHRASE->
pathological liar = 惯性说谎者
habitual liar = 习惯性说谎者
compulsive liar = 强迫性说谎者
professional liar =职业说谎者
bald-faced liar = 厚颜无耻的说谎者
