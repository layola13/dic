deontologist:/ˌdiːɒnˈtɒlədʒɪst/| n. | 道德论者，义务论者 | A deontologist is someone who believes that the morality of an action should be judged by its adherence to rules and duties, rather than by its consequences.  Deontologists focus on the inherent rightness or wrongness of actions themselves, regardless of their outcomes. |  He is a strict deontologist who believes lying is always wrong, even if it would save a life. 他是一位严格的义务论者，他认为说谎总是错的，即使它能挽救生命。  She argued from a deontological perspective that the proposed policy violated basic human rights. 她从义务论的角度论证说，拟议中的政策侵犯了基本人权。 | consequentialist, utilitarian | amoralist, consequentialist (depending on context)
