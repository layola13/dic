consecration:/ˌkɒnsɪˈkreɪʃən/| /ˌkɑːnsɪˈkreɪʃən/| n.|奉献；祝圣；神圣化 |The act of making or declaring something sacred; the act of dedicating something to a religious or divine purpose.  It often involves a formal ceremony or ritual.| 例句：The consecration of the new church was a joyous occasion. 新教堂的祝圣是一个令人高兴的场合。

The consecration of his life to the service of others was evident in his actions. 他将一生奉献给他人的服务，这在他的行动中显而易见。


近义词：dedication, sanctification, hallowing | 反义词：desecration, profanation


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The priest consecrates the bread and wine. = 牧师将面包和葡萄酒圣化。
She consecrates her time to charity work. = 她把时间奉献给慈善事业。

一般过去时:
They consecrated the land for a new park. = 他们将这块土地奉献给新建的公园。
The king consecrated the temple. = 国王为寺庙举行了祝圣仪式。

现在完成时:
He has consecrated his life to scientific research. = 他已将自己的一生奉献给了科学研究。
The church has been consecrated for over a century. = 这座教堂已祝圣了一个多世纪。

过去完成时:
They had consecrated the building before the ceremony began. = 仪式开始前，他们已经将建筑物祝圣了。
The priest had consecrated the chalice before the mass. = 在弥撒之前，牧师已经将圣杯祝圣了。

将来时:
They will consecrate the new hospital next week. = 他们下周将为新医院举行祝圣仪式。
She will consecrate her talents to helping the needy. = 她将把自己的才能奉献给帮助穷人。

虚拟语气:
If they consecrated the land, we could start building. = 如果他们将土地奉献出来，我们就可以开始建造了。
I wish they had consecrated the temple earlier. = 我希望他们早点为寺庙举行祝圣仪式。

被动语态:
The land was consecrated for the construction of a school. = 这块土地被奉献用于建造学校。

疑问句:

否定句:
