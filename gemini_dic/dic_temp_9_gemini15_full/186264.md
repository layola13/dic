rhizodont:/ˈraɪzədɒnt/| n. |根齿动物|A rhizodont is an extinct genus of large, predatory, lobe-finned fish that lived during the Devonian period.  They were characterized by their large, powerful jaws and sharp teeth. |Example:  The fossil remains of the rhizodont were discovered in a Devonian-aged sedimentary rock formation.|近义词:  (lacks perfect synonyms due to extinction, but could consider related terms like)  osteolepiform,  sarcopterygian |反义词: (N/A)
