recomposition:/ˌriːkɒmpəˈzɪʃən/ | n. |重新组合；改写；改编 | The act of composing something again; a new arrangement or version of something.  It often implies a rearrangement or improvement of existing material. | Example: The recomposition of the orchestral score made it more accessible to a wider audience.  重新编排管弦乐谱使其更容易被更广泛的观众接受。  |近义词：rearrangement, reworking, revision, adaptation | 反义词：decomposition, destruction
