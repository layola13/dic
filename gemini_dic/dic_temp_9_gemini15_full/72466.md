flunked:/flʌŋkt/| vt.|不及格，考试失败|To fail to pass an examination or a test.|He flunked his math exam. 他数学考试不及格了。|She flunked out of college. 她大学没读完就退学了。|passed, succeeded|passed, succeeded


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flunks all his exams. 他所有考试都考砸了。
She flunks every test she takes. 她参加的每一次考试都失败了。


一般过去时:
I flunked my driving test. 我驾照考试不及格了。
He flunked out of school last year. 他去年被学校开除了。


现在完成时:
I have flunked three exams this semester. 这学期我已经有三门考试不及格了。
She has never flunked a test in her life. 她一生中从未考试不及格过。


过去完成时:
He had flunked all his previous attempts at the exam. 他之前几次考试都失败了。
She had flunked the test before, so she studied harder this time. 她之前考试不及格，所以这次学习更努力了。


将来时:
I think I will flunk this exam. 我觉得我会这次考试不及格。
If he doesn't study harder, he will flunk the next test. 如果他不努力学习，下次考试会不及格。


虚拟语气:
If I had studied harder, I wouldn't have flunked the exam. 如果我学习更努力，就不会考试不及格了。
I wish I hadn't flunked that important exam. 我真希望我没有那次重要的考试不及格。


被动语态:
The student was flunked for cheating. 那个学生因为作弊而考试不及格。
Many students were flunked in the difficult exam. 许多学生在这次很难的考试中不及格。


疑问句:
Did you flunk the exam? 你考试不及格了吗？
Will he flunk the final exam? 他期末考试会不及格吗？


否定句:
I didn't flunk the exam. 我考试没有不及格。
He didn't flunk any of his classes. 他没有哪门课不及格。


PHRASE->
flunk out: 退学，被开除
flunk a course: 某门课程不及格
flunk an exam: 考试不及格
flunk a test: 测试不及格
to flunk someone: 使某人不及格 (较少用)


词源:
The word "flunk" likely originated in American English slang in the late 19th century. Its etymology is uncertain, but it may be related to the German word "flunken," meaning "to fail," or possibly a variant of other words suggesting failure or deficiency.
