overpolishing: /ˌoʊvərˈpɑːlɪʃɪŋ/ | n. |过度抛光；矫枉过正| The act of polishing something excessively, often resulting in a negative outcome.  It can refer to the literal over-polishing of a physical object, or the metaphorical over-refinement of something like writing or a presentation.  The excessive effort detracts from the original quality or intention. |例句：The overpolishing of the wood dulled its natural beauty. = 过度抛光木材使其天然美观尽失。

The overpolishing of her prose made it seem stiff and unnatural. = 她文章的过度润色使其显得生硬而不自然。|近义词：over-refinement, overdoing, excessive polishing |反义词：under-polishing, neglecting, roughening |
