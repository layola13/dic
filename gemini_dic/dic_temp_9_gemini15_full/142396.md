orthotropic:/ˌɔːrθəˈtrɒpɪk/| adj.|正交异性的；正交各向异性的|Having different properties in different directions.  This is typically used to describe materials where properties like strength or stiffness vary depending on the direction of the applied force.  For example, wood is orthotropic because it is stronger along the grain than across it.|例句：The composite material exhibits orthotropic behavior due to the aligned fibers.  这种复合材料由于纤维排列整齐而表现出正交异性行为。|例句：The analysis of the orthotropic plate requires specialized techniques. 正交异性板的分析需要专门的技术。|近义词：anisotropic (but with specific directional properties)|反义词：isotropic
