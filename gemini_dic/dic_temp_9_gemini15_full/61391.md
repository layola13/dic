emblematical: /ˌɛmblɪˈmætɪkəl/ | adj. | 象征性的；有象征意义的 |  Representing something else; symbolic.  The lion is emblematical of courage.  | The dove is emblematical of peace. 白鸽象征和平。 |近义词：symbolic, representative, figurative | 反义词：literal, realistic


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The crown is emblematical of royalty. 皇冠象征着王权。
This painting is emblematical of the artist's inner turmoil. 这幅画象征着艺术家内心的骚动。


一般过去时:
The burning bush was emblematical of God's presence.  燃烧的荆棘象征着上帝的存在。
The red rose was emblematical of love in Victorian times. 维多利亚时代，红玫瑰象征着爱情。


现在完成时:
The broken vase has become emblematical of our failed relationship. 打碎的花瓶已经成为了我们失败关系的象征。
The artist's signature has become emblematical of his unique style. 艺术家的签名已经成为了他独特风格的象征。


过去完成时:
The ancient symbol had long been emblematical of fertility.  这个古老的符号早已象征着生育能力。
The fallen statue had once been emblematical of the empire's power. 倒塌的雕像曾经象征着帝国的权力。


将来时:
The new monument will be emblematical of national unity. 新的纪念碑将象征着民族团结。
The upcoming election will be emblematical of the country's political future.  即将到来的选举将象征着国家的政治未来。


虚拟语气:
If the dove were emblematical of something other than peace, it would be quite unsettling. 如果鸽子象征的不是和平，那将会非常令人不安。
I wish the company logo were more emblematical of our values. 我希望公司标志更能象征我们的价值观。


被动语态:
The flag is emblematical of the nation. 国旗象征着国家。
The color red is often emblematical of danger or passion. 红色常常象征着危险或激情。


疑问句:
Is the skull emblematical of death? 头骨象征着死亡吗？
What is this symbol emblematical of?  这个符号象征着什么？


否定句:
The image is not emblematical of happiness. 这幅图像并不象征着幸福。
The building is not emblematical of modern architecture. 这座建筑并不象征着现代建筑。

词源:
From Middle English *emblematical, from Old French *emblematical, from Latin *emblematicus, from Greek *emblematikos, from *emblema (emblem).
