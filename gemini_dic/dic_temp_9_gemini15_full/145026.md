overaffirmatively: /ˌoʊvərəˈfɜːrmətɪvli/ | adv. | 过分肯定地，过度肯定地 |  This word describes an action or statement that is excessively or unnecessarily affirmative. It implies an overemphasis on agreement or confirmation, potentially to the point of being insincere or unconvincing.  | 例句：He responded overaffirmatively to every suggestion, making it hard to gauge his true opinion. 他对每一个建议都过分肯定地回应，让人难以判断他的真实想法。  |近义词：excessively affirmatively, excessively positively, overenthusiastically | 反义词：negatively, cautiously, hesitantly |  词性: 副词

USAGE->
This word is an adverb and doesn't have verb conjugations like "what".  Its usage is straightforward: it modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb to describe the manner in which an action or statement is expressed.


例句1: She overaffirmatively agreed to the proposal, raising suspicions about her genuine enthusiasm. 她过分肯定地同意了这个提议，让人怀疑她是否真的热情。

例句2: He overaffirmatively declared his innocence, making his claim seem less believable. 他过分肯定地宣称自己无罪，反而使他的说法显得不太可信。


PHRASE->
This word doesn't have common phrases in the same way that "what" does.  Its usage is primarily as a single adverbial modifier.
