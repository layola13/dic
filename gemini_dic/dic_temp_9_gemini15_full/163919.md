predetermining:/ˌpriːdɪˈtɜːrmɪnɪŋ/| adj.| 预先决定的，预先确定的 | Determining or deciding something in advance.  It implies a decision made before an event or process occurs, often suggesting a lack of spontaneity or freedom. |例句：The predetermined outcome of the rigged election angered the public.  预先决定的舞弊选举结果激怒了公众。


The predetermined flight path avoided the storm. 预先确定的飞行路线避开了风暴。


近义词：preordained, prearranged, fixed, settled


反义词：uncertain, unpredictable, spontaneous, chance


词性：形容词

词源:
The word "predetermining" is formed by combining the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") with "determining" (from "determine," ultimately from Latin "determinare," meaning "to bound, limit, define"). Therefore, "predetermining" signifies "deciding or fixing beforehand."
