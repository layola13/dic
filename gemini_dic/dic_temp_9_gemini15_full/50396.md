deposing: /dɪˈpoʊzɪŋ/ | v. | 免职；罢黜；作证；陈述 |

英文解析:The act of removing someone from a position of power or authority, or the act of giving evidence under oath.  It can refer to the legal process of removing someone from office or a position of power, or the act of formally stating something, especially in a court of law.

例句:

The king was deposed by his rebellious son.  = 国王被他的叛逆儿子废黜了。

The witness deposed that she saw the defendant flee the scene. = 证人证实她看到被告逃离现场。


近义词: oust, dethrone, remove, testify, state

反义词: install, appoint, enthrone

词源:  From Old French *deposer, from Latin deponere, from de- ("from") + ponere ("to place").  The sense of "to remove from office" developed from the earlier sense of "to set down," "to lay down."
