guineas:/ˈɡɪniz/| n.|几尼,几内亚镑|A guinea is an old British gold coin worth one pound and one shilling (21 shillings).  It was formerly used in Britain, especially for professional fees.  The term is sometimes still used informally to refer to a sum of money, typically around £1. |例句：The doctor charged a guinea for his consultation. 医生收取了一几尼的诊金。

例句：He paid a few guineas for the antique book. 他花了几个几尼买了这本古董书。


近义词：pound (in the sense of a sum of money), sovereign


反义词：无


词性：名词
