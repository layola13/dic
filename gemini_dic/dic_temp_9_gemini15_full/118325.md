meretriciousness:/ˌmɛrɪˈtrɪʃəsnəs/| n. |俗丽；华而不实；媚俗；不正当；虚假 |The meretriciousness of the decorations clashed with the simplicity of the architecture.  装饰的俗丽与建筑的简洁格格不入。|近义词：showiness, tawdriness, gaudiness, superficiality |反义词：authenticity, genuineness, sincerity


英文解析:The quality of being superficially attractive but actually having little value or integrity; flashy or tawdry.  指的是表面上吸引人，但实际上缺乏价值或完整性；俗丽或廉价的品质。


例句:

The novel's plot, while exciting, was ultimately marred by its meretriciousness. 小说的情节虽然刺激，但最终却被其华而不实所破坏。


The excessive use of glitter and gold gave the design a feeling of meretriciousness. 过度使用亮片和黄金使设计显得俗丽。


词源:

From Middle French *meretricier (to prostitute) and Latin meretrix (prostitute).  词源来自中古法语 *meretricier（卖淫）和拉丁语 meretrix（妓女）。  The original sense implied the deceptive allure of a prostitute, which then broadened to encompass anything superficially attractive but ultimately lacking substance.  最初的含义暗示了妓女的欺骗性诱惑，然后扩展到包含任何表面上吸引人但最终缺乏实质的东西。
