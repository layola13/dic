decasyllabic:/ˌdekəˈsɪləbɪk/| adj. | 十音节的 | Having ten syllables.  A decasyllabic word or line of verse contains ten syllables. | Example:  The word "incomprehensibilities" is a decasyllabic word.  十音节词“incomprehensibilities”就是一个例子。| 近义词：ten-syllabled | 反义词：monosyllabic, disyllabic


词源:  From "deca" (ten) + "syllable" (a unit of pronunciation).  源自“deca”（十）+“syllable”（音节）。

用法说明:

This word is primarily used in the context of prosody (the study of poetic meter and rhythm) and linguistics.  It describes words or lines of verse that have ten syllables.  这个词主要用于韵律学（对诗歌韵律和节奏的研究）和语言学。它描述的是有十个音节的词或诗歌行。


例句:

He attempted to write a decasyllabic poem. 他试图写一首十音节的诗。

The line from the epic is decasyllabic.  这句史诗中的诗句是十音节的。


由于decasyllabic本身是一个描述性形容词，它没有常用的语法变化形式，例如一般现在时，一般过去时等。 它主要作为形容词修饰名词。  Because decasyllabic is a descriptive adjective, it does not have common grammatical variations like simple present tense, simple past tense, etc. It primarily functions as an adjective modifying nouns.
