indulger:/ɪnˈdʌldʒər/| n. |沉溺者，纵容者 |A person who indulges in something, especially excessive pleasure or self-indulgence.  Someone who allows themselves to enjoy pleasures freely. |He was a notorious indulger in rich foods and fine wines. 他是一个臭名昭著的美食美酒享乐者。|近义词：epicure, hedonist, pleasure-seeker |反义词：ascetic, puritan


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The child indulges in sweets every day. = 这个孩子每天都沉溺于甜食。
He indulges his every whim. = 他放纵自己的每一个怪念头。


一般过去时:
She indulged in a long hot bath after a hard day. = 辛苦了一天后，她享受了一次长时间的热水澡。
They indulged their children with too many toys. = 他们给孩子们太多的玩具，过于溺爱他们。


现在完成时:
He has indulged in too much alcohol lately. = 他最近饮酒过量了。
She has indulged her passion for painting. = 她一直沉溺于绘画的热情中。


过去完成时:
He had indulged in gambling before he lost all his money. = 他在输掉所有钱之前沉迷于赌博。
They had indulged in a massive feast before the journey. = 他们在旅程之前大吃了一顿。


将来时:
I will indulge myself with a vacation next month. = 我下个月要好好犒劳自己，去度假。
They will indulge in some retail therapy after the exam. = 考试结束后，他们会去购物放松一下。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would indulge in a hobby. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会沉迷于一项爱好。
I wish I could indulge in a long holiday. = 我真希望我能休个长假。


被动语态:
His desires were indulged by his parents. = 他的愿望被父母满足了。
Her every whim was indulged. = 她的每一个怪念头都被满足了。


疑问句:
Does he often indulge in fast food? = 他经常吃快餐吗？
Do you indulge yourself too much? = 你是不是太纵容自己了？


否定句:
She doesn't indulge in gossip. = 她不参与八卦。
He didn't indulge his anger. = 他没有放纵自己的怒火。


PHRASE->
indulge in: 沉溺于，享受
indulge oneself: 纵容自己
indulge a whim: 满足一个怪念头
indulge one's appetite: 满足食欲
indulge one's passion: 满足热情
