cower:/ˈkaʊər/| v.|畏缩；蜷缩；胆怯 | To crouch down in fear or shame.  |例句：The dog cowered under the table when the thunder started.  雷声响起时，狗吓得躲在桌子底下。|例句：She cowered in fear as the gunman approached.  枪手走近时，她吓得瑟瑟发抖。|近义词：shrink, cringe, flinch |反义词：stand tall, confront, defy |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cowers in the corner when he's scared. = 他害怕时就躲在角落里瑟瑟发抖。
The small animal cowers before the larger predator. = 小动物在大型捕食者面前畏缩不前。

一般过去时:
She cowered behind the sofa during the storm. = 暴风雨期间，她躲在沙发后面瑟瑟发抖。
The child cowered in fear when he saw the scary clown. = 孩子看到可怕的小丑时吓得瑟瑟发抖。


现在完成时:
He has cowered in fear every time he's heard that sound. = 每当听到那个声音，他都吓得瑟瑟发抖。
The witness has cowered before the intimidation tactics of the defendant. = 证人在被告的恐吓战术面前畏缩不前。


过去完成时:
They had cowered in the basement for hours before the rescue arrived. = 救援到来之前，他们在地下室躲藏了几个小时。
The villagers had cowered in their homes all night, fearing the approaching storm. = 村民们整夜都躲在家里，害怕即将到来的暴风雨。


将来时:
She will cower in fear if she sees a spider. = 如果她看到蜘蛛，她会吓得瑟瑟发抖。
The hostages will cower in fear if the kidnappers become violent.  人质如果绑匪变得暴力，就会吓得瑟瑟发抖。


虚拟语气:
If I saw a ghost, I would cower in terror. = 如果我看到鬼，我会吓得瑟瑟发抖。
I wish I hadn't cowered; I should have stood up to him. = 我真希望我没有畏缩；我应该勇敢地面对他。


被动语态:
(The verb "cower" is rarely used in the passive voice because it implies active fear.)  (动词“cower”很少用于被动语态，因为它暗示着主动的恐惧。)


疑问句:
Does he always cower when faced with authority? = 面对权威时，他总是畏缩不前吗？
Why did the cat cower under the bed? = 为什么猫躲在床底下？


否定句:
He did not cower; he stood his ground. = 他没有畏缩；他坚守阵地。
She refused to cower in the face of adversity. = 她拒绝在逆境面前畏缩。


PHRASE->
cower in fear = 吓得瑟瑟发抖
cower in shame = 羞愧地畏缩
cower before someone = 在某人面前畏缩
cower down = 蜷缩起来
cower away = 躲避


词源:
The word "cower" originates from the Old English word "cūgian," meaning "to crouch," "to bow," or "to stoop."  This word, in turn, has connections to Proto-Germanic roots.
