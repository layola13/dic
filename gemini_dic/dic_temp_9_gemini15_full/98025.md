inordinate:/ɪˈnɔːrdɪnət/| adj.|过度的；过分的；极大的|excessive, unreasonable, or beyond normal limits.  It implies an excess that is often considered unreasonable or excessive.|例句：He spends an inordinate amount of time on the internet. 他在互联网上花费了过多的时间。

例句：The project suffered from inordinate delays.  这个项目遭受了过度的延误。


近义词：excessive, extravagant, immoderate, exorbitant, undue

反义词：moderate, reasonable, restrained, limited, small


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project requires an inordinate amount of resources.  这个项目需要过多的资源。
He displays inordinate patience with his students. 他对他的学生表现出过度的耐心。


一般过去时:
The company incurred inordinate expenses last quarter.  公司上个季度产生了过多的开支。
She showed inordinate concern for her friend's well-being. 她对朋友的福祉表现出过度的关心。


现在完成时:
They have spent an inordinate amount of money on renovations. 他们已经花费了过多的钱用于装修。
He has demonstrated inordinate skill in his chosen profession. 他在他选择的职业中展现出过人的技能。


过去完成时:
The team had already experienced inordinate setbacks before the final deadline.  在最终期限之前，团队已经经历了过多的挫折。
She had consumed an inordinate quantity of coffee that day.  那天她喝了过量的咖啡。


将来时:
The new regulations will likely lead to inordinate paperwork.  新的规章制度可能会导致过多的文书工作。
We anticipate inordinate delays in the shipment. 我们预计发货会有过度的延误。


虚拟语气:
If he had not shown such inordinate confidence, he might have succeeded. 如果他没有表现出如此过度的自信，他也许会成功。
I wish I hadn't shown such inordinate haste in making that decision. 我真希望我当时在做决定时不要那么匆忙。


被动语态:
Inordinate pressure was placed on the employees to meet the deadline.  员工们承受着巨大的压力来赶上最后期限。
Inordinate amounts of time are often wasted on unproductive meetings.  通常会在没有成效的会议上浪费大量的时间。


疑问句:
Is there an inordinate amount of traffic today? 今天的交通是否异常拥堵？
Does he show inordinate favoritism toward certain employees? 他是否对某些员工表现出过度的偏袒？


否定句:
The amount of work was not inordinate; it was manageable. 工作量并不多，是可以应付的。
She did not display inordinate emotion during the difficult situation. 在困难的情况下，她没有表现出过度的感情。
