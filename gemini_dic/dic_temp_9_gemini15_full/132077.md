noncreativity: /ˌnɒnˌkriːeɪˈtɪvɪti/ | n. |缺乏创造力；没有创意| The lack of originality, inventiveness, or imagination.  It refers to an absence of creative thinking or the inability to produce something new or original. | The essay demonstrated a shocking lack of noncreativity; it was simply a rehash of well-known ideas. 这篇文章显示出令人震惊的缺乏创造力；它只是对一些知名观点的重复利用。 | creativity, originality, imagination |  ingenuity, innovation | n.


USAGE->
This word is a noun and does not conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense and voice are not applicable.

词源:
The word "noncreativity" is a modern coinage, formed by adding the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") to the noun "creativity."  "Creativity" itself originates from the Latin word "creare," meaning "to create,"  which evolved through Middle English and Old French.
