drill:/drɪl/| n. vt.|钻，训练，操练|A drill is a tool used to make holes, or the act of repeatedly practicing something.|例句：He used a drill to make a hole in the wall. 他用钻头在墙上钻了个洞。  The soldiers underwent rigorous drills. 士兵们进行了严格的训练。|近义词：exercise, practice, training, boring|反义词：relaxation, rest


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drills holes in the wood. = 他在木头上钻孔。
She drills her students every day. = 她每天都训练她的学生。

一般过去时:
They drilled a well last week. = 他们上周打了一口井。
The teacher drilled the class on grammar. = 老师给班级进行了语法练习。

现在完成时:
We have drilled several holes already. = 我们已经钻了好几个洞了。
She has drilled the same passage many times. = 她已经练习了同一段落很多遍了。

过去完成时:
They had drilled the holes before the pipes arrived. = 管子到达之前，他们已经钻好了孔。
The team had drilled extensively before the competition. = 比赛前，队伍进行了大量的训练。

将来时:
We will drill the new well next month. = 我们下个月将要打新井。
They will drill the students on vocabulary tomorrow. = 他们明天将要对学生进行词汇训练。

虚拟语气:
If I had a drill, I would fix the shelf. = 如果我有一把钻头，我就会修理架子。
If they had drilled more, they would have won. = 如果他们训练更多，他们就赢了。

被动语态:
The hole was drilled quickly. = 孔很快就被钻好了。
The students were drilled on pronunciation. = 学生们接受了发音训练。

疑问句:
What kind of drill do you use? = 你用什么类型的钻头？
How often do they drill the team? = 他们多久对队伍进行一次训练？

否定句:
He didn't drill the hole straight. = 他没有把洞钻直。
They didn't drill enough before the test. = 他们在考试前没有进行足够的训练。
