plerome:/ˈpliːroʊm/| n. |充满；完全；丰满；(植物)胚囊; |The plerome is the central cylinder of a plant stem or root, which develops into the vascular tissues.  It refers to the complete fulfillment of something, a state of being completely filled or satisfied.  In theological contexts, it can refer to the fullness of God's being or the totality of creation.|例句：The artist sought to achieve a plerome of color and texture in her latest painting.  艺术家力求在她最新的绘画中达到色彩和肌理的完美融合。|近义词：fullness, completeness, totality, plenitude |反义词：emptiness, deficiency, lack
