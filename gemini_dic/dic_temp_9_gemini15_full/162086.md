pre-compass's: /ˌpriːˈkʌmpəsɪz/ | adj. | 前指南针的；指南针之前的 |  Describing something that existed or occurred before the invention and widespread use of the magnetic compass for navigation.  This term is primarily used in historical contexts to refer to methods of navigation, cartography, or exploration that predate the compass. | Example: Pre-compass sailors relied heavily on celestial navigation and land markers. 前指南针时代的水手们严重依赖于天体导航和陆标。|近义词: pre-magnetic, ante-compass | 反义词: post-compass
