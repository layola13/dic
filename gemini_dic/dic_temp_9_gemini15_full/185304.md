retrench:/rɪˈtrentʃ/| vt. n.|紧缩；削减；退却；减少开支 |To reduce costs or spending, typically in a business or government.  |例句：The company had to retrench after losing a major contract. 公司在失去一份主要合同后不得不紧缩开支。  |例句：The government announced a program of severe retrenchment. 政府宣布了一项严厉的紧缩计划。|近义词：cut back, reduce, economize |反义词：expand, increase, augment |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company retrenches its spending every quarter. = 公司每个季度都会削减开支。
They retrench their workforce to improve profitability. = 他们裁员以提高盈利能力。

一般过去时:
The government retrenched significantly during the recession. = 政府在经济衰退期间大幅度紧缩开支。
The army retrenched after suffering heavy losses. = 军队在遭受重大损失后撤退了。

现在完成时:
The company has retrenched its operations in several countries. = 公司已在几个国家削减了业务。
They have retrenched their marketing budget due to low sales. = 由于销售额低迷，他们已经削减了营销预算。

过去完成时:
The company had retrenched before the financial crisis hit. = 在金融危机爆发之前，该公司就已经开始紧缩开支了。
The army had retrenched to a more defensible position. = 军队已经退到一个更易防守的位置。

将来时:
The company will retrench if profits continue to fall. = 如果利润继续下降，公司将采取紧缩措施。
The government is expected to retrench spending next year. = 预计政府明年将削减开支。

虚拟语气:
If they had retrenched sooner, they might have avoided bankruptcy. = 如果他们早点采取紧缩措施，他们也许就能避免破产。
It's a shame they didn't retrench their spending. = 真可惜他们没有削减开支。


被动语态:
Spending was retrenched by the government. = 政府削减了开支。
The project was retrenched due to lack of funding. = 由于资金不足，该项目被取消了。


疑问句:
Will the company retrench its workforce? = 公司会裁员吗？
Has the government retrenched its spending enough? = 政府削减的开支够了吗？


否定句:
The company did not retrench its marketing budget. = 公司没有削减其营销预算。
The army did not retrench despite heavy casualties. = 尽管伤亡惨重，军队也没有撤退。
