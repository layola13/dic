contendress:/kənˈtɛndərəs/| n.|竞争者，对手|A woman who contends or competes, especially in a contest or argument.|She is a formidable contendress in the political arena.  她是一位在政治领域中实力强大的竞争者。|近义词：competitor, rival, opponent|反义词：ally, supporter|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The athlete contends for the gold medal. = 运动员正在争夺金牌。
She contends that the results are inaccurate. = 她认为结果不准确。


一般过去时:
The two teams contended fiercely for victory. = 这两支队伍为胜利而激烈竞争。
He contended with many difficulties during his journey. = 他在旅途中克服了许多困难。


现在完成时:
She has contended with adversity throughout her life. = 她一生都在与逆境作斗争。
They have contended for the championship for many years. = 他们多年来一直在争夺冠军。


过去完成时:
He had contended with similar problems before. = 他以前曾遇到过类似的问题。
The company had contended against fierce competition. = 这家公司曾面临激烈的竞争。


将来时:
The team will contend for the title next season. = 该队将在下个赛季争夺冠军头衔。
She will contend that the evidence is insufficient. = 她将争辩说证据不足。


虚拟语气:
If she contended more effectively, she might win. = 如果她更有效地竞争，她可能会获胜。
I wish I had contended more strongly for my position. = 我希望我当时更强有力地为我的立场辩护。


被动语态:
The issue was contended by both sides. = 双方都对这个问题进行了争论。
The championship will be contended for by many athletes. = 许多运动员将争夺冠军。


疑问句:
Who will contend for the presidency? = 谁将竞选总统？
Does she contend that the claim is false? = 她认为这个说法是错误的吗？


否定句:
He did not contend with the challenges effectively. = 他没有有效地应对挑战。
She did not contend that the theory was correct. = 她并没有认为这个理论是正确的。
