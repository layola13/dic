sadden:/ˈsædən/| vt.|使悲伤；使忧愁|To cause someone to feel sadness or unhappiness.|The news saddened her deeply.  这个消息使她深感悲伤。|His death saddened all who knew him. 他的死使所有认识他的人都感到悲伤。|近义词：depress, grieve, upset|反义词：cheer, gladden, elate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bad news saddens me. = 这个坏消息使我悲伤。
His behavior saddens his parents. = 他的行为使他的父母悲伤。

一般过去时:
The loss of her pet saddened her greatly. = 她宠物的离去使她非常悲伤。
The rain saddened the picnic. = 雨水使野餐扫兴。

现在完成时:
The continuous bad weather has saddened everyone. = 持续不断的坏天气让每个人都感到沮丧。
Recent events have saddened the nation. = 最近发生的事件使全国人民感到悲痛。

过去完成时:
I had been saddened by the news before I received the letter. = 在收到信之前，我已经因为这个消息而感到悲伤了。
They had been saddened by the loss of their jobs before they found new ones. = 在找到新工作之前，他们因失业而感到悲伤。

将来时:
The news will sadden many people. = 这个消息将会使很多人感到悲伤。
The announcement will sadden his friends. = 这一宣布将会使他的朋友们感到悲伤。

虚拟语气:
It would sadden me to see him like that. = 看到他那样我会很难过。
It would have saddened me if he hadn't come. = 如果他没有来，我会很伤心。

被动语态:
She was saddened by the news. = 她听到这个消息后感到悲伤。
We were saddened by the outcome of the game. = 我们对比赛的结果感到失望。

疑问句:
Does this news sadden you? = 这个消息让你悲伤吗？
Does the situation sadden you? = 这种情况让你悲伤吗？

否定句:
The news didn't sadden her as much as I thought. = 这个消息并没有像我想象的那样让她悲伤。
The event didn't sadden him at all. = 那件事根本没有让他悲伤。


PHRASE->
be saddened by = 因…而悲伤
feel saddened = 感到悲伤
become saddened = 变得悲伤
deeply saddened = 深感悲伤
sadden one's heart = 使某人心痛
