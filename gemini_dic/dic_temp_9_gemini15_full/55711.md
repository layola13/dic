div:/dɪv/ | n. | 分隔，分歧，隔断 | A division or separation; a landmark separating two things.  | Example: The river forms a natural div between the two countries. 这条河形成了两国之间的天然分界线。 | 近义词：separation, partition, division | 反义词：union, joining, combination | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The wall divides the room into two sections. = 这堵墙把房间隔成两部分。
This issue divides public opinion. = 这个问题分裂了公众舆论。


一般过去时:
The company divided its assets among its shareholders. = 公司将其资产分配给股东。
The conflict divided the community. = 冲突分裂了社区。


现在完成时:
The researchers have divided the data into several categories. = 研究人员已将数据分成几类。
We have divided the work evenly among the team. = 我们已经平均分配了团队的工作。


过去完成时:
They had divided the cake before I arrived. = 我到达之前，他们已经分好了蛋糕。
The army had divided its forces before the battle. = 军队在战斗前已经分兵。


将来时:
We will divide the profits equally. = 我们将平均分配利润。
The company will divide into two separate entities. = 公司将分裂成两个独立的实体。


虚拟语气:
If they divided the resources fairly, there would be no conflict. = 如果他们公平地分配资源，就不会有冲突。
I wish they had divided the workload more evenly. = 我希望他们更平均地分配工作量。


被动语态:
The land was divided among the heirs. = 土地被分给了继承人。
The task was divided into smaller, manageable parts. = 任务被分成更小、更容易管理的部分。


疑问句:
How will the inheritance be divided? = 遗产将如何分配？
Into how many parts is the project divided? = 这个项目被分成多少部分？


否定句:
The team did not divide the responsibilities effectively. = 团队没有有效地分配责任。
The students did not divide their time wisely. = 学生们没有明智地分配时间。
