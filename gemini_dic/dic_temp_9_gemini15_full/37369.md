commensurately:/kəˈmɛnsərətli/ | adv. | 相称地；相应地；成比例地 | In proportion; correspondingly.  It implies a direct relationship between two things, where an increase or decrease in one leads to a similar change in the other. | 例句：His salary was increased commensurately with his increased responsibilities. = 他的薪水随着他增加的责任相应地提高了。


His efforts were commensurately rewarded. = 他的努力得到了相应的回报。


近义词：proportionately, correspondingly, proportionally, in proportion to | 反义词：disproportionately, incongruously


词源：源自commensurate (adj.) + -ly (adv. suffix).  Commensurate itself comes from Latin "commensuratus," the past participle of "commensurāre," meaning "to measure together," from "com-" (together) + "mensurāre" (to measure).  Thus, the word's root implies a shared measure or proportion.
