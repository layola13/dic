penchants:/ˈpɛntʃənts/| n. |偏好；嗜好；倾向 |A strong inclination or liking for something.|He has a penchant for spicy food. 他喜欢吃辣的食物。|近义词：preference, liking, fondness, inclination |反义词：dislike, aversion


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has a penchant for classic cars. = 他对经典汽车情有独钟。
She shows a penchant for detail in her work. = 她在工作中展现出对细节的偏好。

一般过去时:
He had a penchant for gambling in his youth. = 他年轻时嗜赌。
She had a penchant for collecting antique dolls. = 她过去喜欢收集古董娃娃。

现在完成时:
He has developed a penchant for gardening in his retirement. = 他退休后养成了园艺的嗜好。
She has always had a penchant for adventure. = 她一直都喜欢冒险。

过去完成时:
He had already developed a penchant for painting before he went to art school. = 在他上艺术学校之前就已经养成了绘画的嗜好。
She had always had a penchant for music, even before she started taking lessons. = 在她开始上课之前就一直对音乐情有独钟。

将来时:
He will likely develop a penchant for cooking once he moves out. = 他搬出去后很可能会养成烹饪的嗜好。
She will probably maintain her penchant for travel throughout her life. = 她可能会终身保持旅行的爱好。

虚拟语气:
If he had a penchant for sports, he would join a team. = 如果他喜欢运动，他会加入一个团队。
I wish I had a penchant for early rising. = 我希望我能养成早起的习惯。

被动语态:
His penchant for spicy food is well-known. = 他喜欢吃辣的食物是众所周知的。
Her penchant for collecting stamps was noted by many. = 很多人注意到她收集邮票的爱好。

疑问句:
What is his penchant? = 他有什么嗜好？
What is her particular penchant in art? = 她对艺术有什么特别的偏好？

否定句:
He doesn't have a penchant for classical music. = 他不喜欢古典音乐。
She doesn't show any penchant for hard work. = 她没有表现出任何努力工作的倾向。
