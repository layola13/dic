crackles: /ˈkrækəlz/ | n., v. |噼啪声，爆裂声 |发出噼啪声；爆裂 |近义词：sputters, snaps, pops |反义词：silence, stillness


英文解析：
The noun "crackles" refers to a series of short, sharp, crackling sounds, like those produced by fire, electricity, or the sound of a crisp object being broken. As a verb, "crackles" means to make these sounds.  The word often implies a lively or energetic feeling, particularly when referring to things like energy or atmosphere.


例句：

The fire crackled merrily in the hearth.  = 火在壁炉里愉快地噼啪作响。

The radio crackled with static. = 收音机发出噼里啪啦的静电声。


近义词：sputters, snaps, pops, crackles


反义词：silence, stillness


词性：名词，动词
