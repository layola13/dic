declaims:/dɪˈkleɪmz/| vt.|朗诵；雄辩地陈述；谴责|To speak or deliver (a speech or poem) in a formal and often theatrical manner; to express (something) forcefully or vehemently.|The actor declaimed the sonnet with great passion.  演员充满激情地朗诵十四行诗。|He declaimed against the injustices of the system. 他慷慨激昂地谴责制度的不公。|recite, pronounce, proclaim|accept, approve

一般现在时:
He declaims his poetry every Sunday. = 他每周日朗诵他的诗歌。
She declaims against the government's policies. = 她谴责政府的政策。

一般过去时:
The orator declaimed passionately about freedom. = 演说家激情洋溢地论述自由。
He declaimed his grievances to the assembled crowd. = 他向聚集的人群大声陈述了他的不满。
