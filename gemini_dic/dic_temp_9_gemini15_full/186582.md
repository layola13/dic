ridicule:/ˈrɪdɪkjuːl/| n. vt.|嘲笑；奚落；愚弄 |To make fun of someone or something in a cruel or unkind way, often by using mockery or sarcasm.  |例句：The children ridiculed him for his strange clothes. 孩子们因为他奇怪的衣服而嘲笑他。

例句：His ideas were ridiculed by his colleagues. 他的想法受到了同事们的嘲笑。


近义词：mock, deride, scorn, jeer, taunt |反义词：praise, commend, applaud


词性:
名词:  The ridicule he received was undeserved. 他受到的嘲笑是不应该的。
动词: They ridiculed his efforts to learn Spanish. 他们嘲笑他学习西班牙语的努力。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They ridicule his ideas. = 他们嘲笑他的想法。
She ridicules his attempts at humor. = 她嘲笑他尝试幽默的行为。


一般过去时:
They ridiculed his performance. = 他们嘲笑他的表演。
She ridiculed his clumsy dance moves. = 她嘲笑他笨拙的舞姿。


现在完成时:
They have ridiculed him relentlessly. = 他们无情地嘲笑了他。
She has ridiculed his efforts repeatedly. = 她一再嘲笑他的努力。


过去完成时:
They had ridiculed him before he even began. = 在他甚至开始之前，他们就已经嘲笑了他。
She had ridiculed his project long before its completion. = 在他的项目完成很久之前，她就嘲笑它了。

否定句:

词源:
