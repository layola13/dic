isodimorphic:/ˌaɪsoʊdaɪˈmɔːrfɪk/| adj. |同晶型的|Isomorphic refers to substances that have the same crystalline structure but different chemical compositions.  Isodimorphic describes minerals that can substitute for each other in a crystal lattice, exhibiting similar crystal forms despite having different chemical compositions.  This substitution is often partial, meaning the minerals can exist in a series of solid solutions, with varying proportions of the substituting elements. |Example:  The minerals olivine and pyroxene are isodimorphic, showing similar crystal structures despite their different chemical compositions.橄榄石和辉石是同晶型的，尽管它们的化学成分不同，但具有相似的晶体结构。|近义词：isostructural|反义词：polymorphic, allotropic|
