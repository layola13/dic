exonerative: /ɪɡˈzɒnəreɪtɪv/ | adj. | 证明无罪的；免除责任的 |  Exonerative evidence clears someone of blame or guilt.  It suggests innocence or freedom from guilt.  | The exonerative testimony from the witness helped to free the wrongly accused man. = 证人的免责证词帮助释放了被冤枉的人。

The judge presented exonerative evidence that proved the defendant's innocence. = 法官出示了证明被告无罪的证据。


近义词：acquitting, clearing, justifying, vindication | 反义词：incriminating, condemning, inculpating


词源:  From  exonerate + -ive.  Exonerate comes from the Latin "exonerare," meaning "to unload, disburden," from "ex-" (out) + "onus" (burden).  The "-ive" suffix creates an adjective.
