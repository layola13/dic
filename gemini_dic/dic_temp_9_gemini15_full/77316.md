gatherers:/ˈɡæðərərz/| n.|采集者，采摘者|People who collect things, especially food, from the wild.  They are often associated with hunter-gatherer societies.|例句：The ancient gatherers relied on berries and nuts for sustenance. 古代的采集者依靠浆果和坚果维持生计。|例句：The gatherers returned to the village with baskets full of mushrooms. 采集者们带着装满蘑菇的篮子回到了村庄。|近义词：collectors, harvesters|反义词：producers, cultivators|名词

词源:

The word "gatherers" comes from the verb "gather," which has Old English roots ("gaderian").  The suffix "-ers" indicates a person or thing that performs the action of the verb.  The basic meaning of collecting things together has remained consistent throughout its history.
