peer-to-peer:/pɪər.təˈpɪr/ | adj., n. |点对点；对等的 | Relating to or denoting a computer network or system in which each workstation has equal status and can act as both a client and a server.  Peer-to-peer networks are characterized by the absence of a central server.  As a noun, it refers to a system or network operating in this manner. | 例句：The file was shared via a peer-to-peer network.  这个文件是通过点对点网络共享的。

The software uses a peer-to-peer architecture. 这个软件使用点对点架构。| 近义词： decentralized, distributed | 反义词： centralized, client-server |

词源:
The term "peer-to-peer" is a compound of "peer" and "to-peer". "Peer" in this context means an equal, a person of the same status or ability.  The "to-peer" part indicates the direct connection or interaction between equals. The term emerged in the context of computer networking to describe systems without a central server.
