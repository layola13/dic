preauthorize: /ˌpriːˈɔːθəraɪz/ | vt. |预先授权，预先批准 | To authorize in advance.  This typically involves granting permission or approval before a specific action or transaction takes place.  |例句：The system preauthorizes payments for recurring subscriptions. = 系统预先授权定期订阅的付款。

The bank preauthorized the payment from my checking account. = 银行预先授权从我的支票账户付款。


近义词：authorize in advance, approve beforehand, preapprove


反义词：deny, disapprove, reject


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company preauthorizes all large purchases. = 公司预先批准所有大额采购。
They preauthorize access to the secure server. = 他们预先授权访问安全服务器。


一般过去时:
The manager preauthorized the project budget. = 经理预先批准了项目预算。
The credit card company preauthorized the hotel reservation. = 信用卡公司预先批准了酒店预订。


现在完成时:
The system has preauthorized the transaction. = 系统已经预先授权了交易。
We have preauthorized the shipment of the goods. = 我们已经预先批准了货物的运输。


过去完成时:
They had preauthorized the funds before the conference. = 他们在会议之前已经预先授权了资金。
She had preauthorized the withdrawal from her savings account. = 她已经预先授权从她的储蓄账户提款。


将来时:
The bank will preauthorize the loan payment. = 银行将预先授权贷款还款。
He will preauthorize the purchase of the new equipment. = 他将预先批准购买新设备。


虚拟语气:
If they preauthorized the payment, we would have received the goods. = 如果他们预先授权付款，我们就会收到货物。
I wish they had preauthorized the funds earlier. = 我希望他们早点预先授权资金。


被动语态:
The payment was preauthorized by the bank. = 付款已获得银行的预先授权。
The purchase order was preauthorized by the purchasing department. = 采购订单已获得采购部门的预先授权。


疑问句:

Was the transaction preauthorized? = 交易是否已获得预先授权？


否定句:
The system did not preauthorize the transaction. = 系统没有预先授权该交易。
The request was not preauthorized. = 请求未获得预先授权。


PHRASE->
preauthorize a payment = 预先授权付款
preauthorize a purchase = 预先授权购买
preauthorize a transaction = 预先授权交易
preauthorize access = 预先授权访问
preauthorize funds = 预先授权资金


词源:

The word "preauthorize" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the verb "authorize" (meaning "to give official permission or approval").  "Authorize" itself comes from the Old French word "autoriser," ultimately deriving from the Latin "auctor" (author, originator).
