ovalbumin:/ˌoʊvəlˈbjuːmɪn/ | n. | 卵清蛋白 | Ovalbumin is the major protein of egg white, making up approximately 55% of the total protein content.  It is a glycoprotein and a member of the serpin superfamily, although it lacks the typical serpin inhibitory activity. It's often used in research and various applications due to its abundance and relative ease of purification. |  The researchers analyzed the ovalbumin content in different egg samples. 研究人员分析了不同鸡蛋样品中的卵清蛋白含量。
