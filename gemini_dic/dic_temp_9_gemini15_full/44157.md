criticised:/ˈkrɪtɪsaɪzd/| v.|批评，指责|To express disapproval of someone or something.|The film was criticised for its violence.  这部电影因其暴力而受到批评。  The teacher criticised the student's poor grammar. 老师批评了学生的语法不好。|condemn, rebuke, censure|praise, commend|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He criticizes her work regularly. = 他经常批评她的工作。
They criticize the government's policies. = 他们批评政府的政策。

一般过去时:
She criticised his performance last night. = 她昨晚批评了他的表现。
The judge criticised the lawyer's arguments. = 法官批评了律师的论点。

现在完成时:
They have criticised the new law many times. = 他们多次批评了这项新法律。
She has criticised his behaviour on several occasions. = 她曾多次批评过他的行为。

过去完成时:
He had criticised her before, but she ignored him. = 他以前曾批评过她，但她不理他。
They had criticised the plan, but it was still implemented. = 他们曾批评过这个计划，但它仍然被实施了。

将来时:
He will criticise her again if she continues this behaviour. = 如果她继续这种行为，他会再次批评她。
They will criticise the proposal at the meeting tomorrow. = 他们将在明天的会议上批评这项提案。

虚拟语气:
I wish he hadn't criticised my work so harshly. = 我真希望他不要如此严厉地批评我的工作。
If she criticised him, he would be very upset. = 如果她批评他，他会非常难过。

被动语态:
His work was criticised by the committee. = 他的作品受到了委员会的批评。
The proposal was criticised for being unrealistic. = 该提案因不切实际而受到批评。

疑问句:
Did anyone criticise your presentation? = 有人批评你的演示文稿吗？
Has the government been criticised for its handling of the crisis? = 政府因其处理危机的方式而受到批评了吗？

否定句:
He didn't criticise her directly. = 他没有直接批评她。
They didn't criticise the decision publicly. = 他们没有公开批评这个决定。


PHRASE->
be criticised for = 因…而受到批评

level criticism at = 对…提出批评
subject to criticism = 容易受到批评
open to criticism =  经得起批评
