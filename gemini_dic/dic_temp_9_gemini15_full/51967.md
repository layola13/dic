diacoustics:/ˌdaɪəˈkjuːstɪks/| n. |建筑声学；声音传播学|The study of the acoustic properties of a building or enclosed space, including sound reflection, absorption, and diffusion.  It focuses on how sound behaves within a specific environment and how to optimize that environment for clear and pleasing sound. |Example:  The architect consulted a specialist in diacoustics to design the concert hall's acoustics. 建筑师咨询了一位建筑声学专家来设计音乐厅的音响效果。  |近义词：architectural acoustics, room acoustics|反义词：无
