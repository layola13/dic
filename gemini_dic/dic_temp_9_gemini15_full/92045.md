hyperphoric:/ˌhaɪpərˈfɔːrɪk/| adj.| 超指的，指涉超前的 | Referring to something beyond the current discourse or context; often used in linguistics to describe a pronoun or other element that refers to something that will be introduced later in the text.  | Example:  The hyperphoric pronoun "it" in the sentence "It will rain tomorrow, the forecast said so," refers to the upcoming prediction of rain. | 近义词：anaphoric (but opposite direction), cataphoric | 反义词：hypophoric
