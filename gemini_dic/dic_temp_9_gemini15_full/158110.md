plastidule:/ˈplæstɪdjuːl/| n. |叶绿体，质体|A plastidule is a small, immature plastid, an organelle found in plant cells and some other organisms.  Plastids develop from proplastids, which are undifferentiated precursors.  Plastidules are involved in the early stages of plastid biogenesis and differentiation, eventually maturing into various types of plastids, such as chloroplasts (for photosynthesis) or chromoplasts (for pigment storage).|Example: The proplastids in the meristematic cells differentiated into amyloplasts and plastidulles.|近义词：proplastid (前质体)|反义词：mature plastid (成熟质体)
