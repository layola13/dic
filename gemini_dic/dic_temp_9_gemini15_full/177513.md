rebloomed:/ˌriˈbluːmd/ | v. | 再度开花；重新繁盛 | To bloom again after a period of dormancy or decline.  The verb "rebloom" implies a renewed flowering or flourishing after a pause or setback. |  例句：After a harsh winter, the roses rebloomed in spectacular fashion.  严冬过后，玫瑰花盛开得格外艳丽。

The garden rebloomed after the summer rains. 夏季雨水过后，花园重新开花。| 近义词：reflourish, revive, regenerate | 反义词：wither, fade, die


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The roses rebloom every spring. = 玫瑰每年春天都会重新开花。
The plants rebloom beautifully in the warm weather. = 植物在温暖的天气里开得非常漂亮。


一般过去时:
The tulips rebloomed after we pruned them. = 我们修剪了郁金香后，它们又重新开花了。
The flowers rebloomed surprisingly quickly after the frost. = 霜冻过后，花朵出人意料地很快又重新开放了。


现在完成时:
The azaleas have rebloomed this year after a long drought. = 杜鹃花在经历了长时间的干旱后，今年又重新开花了。
The garden has rebloomed twice this summer. = 这个花园今年夏天已经重新开花两次了。


过去完成时:
The orchids had rebloomed before the exhibition. = 兰花在展览之前已经重新开花了。
The trees had rebloomed beautifully by the time of the festival. = 到节日的时候，树木已经开得非常漂亮了。


将来时:

I think the hydrangeas will rebloom if we give them enough water. = 我想如果我们给绣球花足够的水分，它们就会再次开花。


虚拟语气:

I wish the lilies would rebloom this season. = 我希望百合花这个季节能再次开花。


被动语态:
The flowers were rebloomed with the help of special fertilizer. = 在特殊肥料的帮助下，花朵重新开放了。(此用法较少见，通常用主动语态)
The plants can be rebloomed by providing sufficient sunlight. = 通过提供充足的阳光，植物可以重新开花。(此用法较少见，通常用主动语态)


疑问句:
Will the flowers rebloom this year? = 今年花还会开吗？
Do you think the shrubs will rebloom after being transplanted? = 你认为这些灌木移植后还会重新开花吗？


否定句:
The plants didn't rebloom this year because of the cold weather. = 由于天气寒冷，这些植物今年没有重新开花。
The roses failed to rebloom after the harsh winter. = 严冬过后，玫瑰花未能重新开放。
