sanhedrim:/ˈsænɪˌdrɪm/| n. |犹太公议会|The Sanhedrin was the supreme council of the ancient Jewish people, responsible for religious and civil matters. It consisted of 71 members, including the high priest and other prominent figures.  The Sanhedrin's authority varied throughout Jewish history, but it generally held considerable power in matters of law, religion, and governance.|The Sanhedrin condemned Jesus to death. 犹太公议会判处耶稣死刑。| council, assembly, synod | anarchy, chaos | 名词
