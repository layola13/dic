cosmo:/ˈkɒzməʊ/| /ˈkɑːzməʊ/ | n. |宇宙；秩序；世界；整洁|The totality of all things; the universe.  It can also refer to a particular ordered system or harmonious arrangement. |例句：The vastness of the cosmos is awe-inspiring. 宇宙的浩瀚令人敬畏。

The cosmos is a marvel of intricate design. 宇宙是一个错综复杂的设计奇迹。|近义词：universe, macrocosm, world |反义词：chaos, disorder

USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tenses, voice, and mood are not applicable.  However, we can show how it's used in different sentence structures.


Simple Sentence:
The cosmos is expanding. = 宇宙正在膨胀。
We observed a distant galaxy in the cosmos. = 我们在宇宙中观测到一个遥远的星系。


Compound Sentence:
The cosmos is vast and mysterious, and scientists continue to explore its secrets. = 宇宙浩瀚而神秘，科学家们仍在继续探索它的奥秘。
Many theories exist about the origin of the cosmos, but none are definitively proven. = 关于宇宙起源的理论有很多，但没有一个得到确切的证实。


Complex Sentence:
Because the cosmos is so immense, it's difficult to comprehend its scale. = 因为宇宙如此浩瀚，所以很难理解它的规模。
Although we have learned much about the cosmos, much remains unknown. = 虽然我们已经了解了很多关于宇宙的知识，但仍然有很多未知之谜。


PHRASE->
cosmic dust: 宇宙尘埃
cosmic rays: 宇宙射线
cosmic background radiation: 宇宙背景辐射
cosmic horror: 宇宙恐怖（科幻小说类型）
cosmic microwave background: 宇宙微波背景辐射


词源:
From Late Latin *cosmus, from Greek κόσμος (kosmos) "order, harmony, the world," related to κοσμέω (kosmeō) "to order, adorn, arrange."  The modern English word reflects the sense of "universe" derived from the Greek.
