sailing:/ˈseɪlɪŋ/ | n. /v.| 航海；航行；帆船运动 | The act of traveling by boat using sails; the sport of racing sailboats. | 例句：He enjoys sailing in the summer. 他喜欢夏天航海。

Sailing is a popular sport. 帆船运动是一项受欢迎的运动。


近义词：navigation, cruising, yachting | 反义词：None specifically, but words like "walking," "driving," or "flying" represent alternative modes of travel.


词性：名词(n.)，动词(v.)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sails every weekend. = 他每个周末都航海。
They sail across the ocean. = 他们横渡海洋。


一般过去时:
She sailed to Greece last year. = 她去年航行去了希腊。
We sailed the boat along the coast. = 我们沿着海岸航行。


现在完成时:
They have sailed around the world. = 他们已经环游世界了。
I have never sailed before. = 我以前从未航海过。


过去完成时:
He had sailed many miles before he reached the island. = 他到达岛屿之前已经航行了许多英里。
They had sailed for days before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们已经航行了好几天。


将来时:
We will sail to the Caribbean next month. = 我们下个月将航行到加勒比海。
She will sail her new boat tomorrow. = 她明天将驾驶她的新船。


虚拟语气:
If I had time, I would sail to the Bahamas. = 如果我有时间，我会航行到巴哈马群岛。
I wish I were sailing in the Mediterranean right now. = 我真希望现在正在地中海航行。


被动语态:
The boat was sailed by a skilled captain. = 这艘船由一位经验丰富的船长驾驶。
The race was sailed in strong winds. = 比赛在强风中进行。


疑问句:
Do you sail? = 你会航海吗？
Where did they sail to? = 他们航行到哪里去了？


否定句:
He doesn't sail very often. = 他不经常航海。
They didn't sail because of the bad weather. = 由于天气不好，他们没有航海。


PHRASE->
coastal sailing: 沿海航海
ocean sailing:  远洋航行
windward sailing: 逆风航行
sailing ship: 帆船

词源:
The word "sailing" derives from the Old English word "segel," meaning "sail."  This in turn has roots in Proto-Germanic and ultimately in Proto-Indo-European language families, reflecting the ancient importance of sailing as a mode of transport and trade. The "-ing" suffix denotes the act or process of.
