词源:
From Late Latin *pontificius, from Latin pontifex "pontiff," literally "bridge-maker," from pons "bridge" + facere "to make."  The term originally referred to the high priests in ancient Rome responsible for maintaining bridges and other public works, later evolving to refer to the Pope.
