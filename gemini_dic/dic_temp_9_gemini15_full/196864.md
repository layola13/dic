shabbiest:/ˈʃæbiːɪst/| adj.|最破旧的，最褴褛的|Most worn-out; in poor condition; dilapidated.  It describes something that is old, worn, and in poor condition, often showing signs of neglect.|The shabbiest part of town was near the docks.|近义词：worn-out, dilapidated, ragged, run-down, tattered|反义词：new, pristine, immaculate, spick-and-span

一般现在时:
The old house looks shabby. = 这栋旧房子看起来很破旧。
That coat is shabby. = 那件外套很破旧。

一般过去时:
The furniture was shabby before we renovated the house. = 我们装修房子之前，家具很破旧。
Her dress looked shabby after the party. = 派对后她的衣服看起来很破旧。

现在完成时:
The carpet has become shabby over the years. = 多年来，地毯已经变得破旧了。
The once-grand hotel has become shabby. = 那家曾经宏伟的酒店现在已经变得破旧了。

过去完成时:
The car had become shabby before they decided to sell it. = 在他们决定卖掉汽车之前，汽车已经变得破旧了。
The building had been shabby for decades. = 那栋建筑已经破旧了几十年了。

将来时:
The sofa will become shabby if we don't take care of it. = 如果我们不好好保养沙发，它会变得破旧。
The paint will become shabby with time. = 油漆会随着时间的推移而变得破旧。

虚拟语气:
If the house weren't so shabby, we would have bought it. = 如果房子不是那么破旧，我们就会买下来。
I wish my shoes weren't so shabby. = 我希望我的鞋子不要那么破旧。

被动语态:

The furniture has been left shabby by years of neglect. = 多年的忽视使家具变得破旧。

疑问句:
Is this coat too shabby to wear? = 这件外套太破旧不能穿了吗？
Is the car too shabby to sell? = 这辆车太破旧不能卖了吗？

否定句:
The hotel wasn't shabby at all; it was quite luxurious. = 酒店一点也不破旧；它相当豪华。
