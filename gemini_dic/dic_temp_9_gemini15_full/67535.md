extrafoliaceous:/ˌɛkstrəˌfoʊliˈeɪʃəs/| adj.|叶外的，叶片外的|Growing or situated outside the leaf.  This term is primarily used in botany to describe structures or growths that are located on the surface of a leaf, but not originating from the leaf tissue itself.|例句：The extrafoliaceous growths on the plant were identified as parasitic fungi. 植物上叶片外的生长物被鉴定为寄生真菌。


The extrafoliaceous appendages on the stem were unusual for this species.  茎上的叶片外附属物对这个物种来说是不寻常的。|近义词：external, superficial|反义词：intrafoliaceous


WORD ORIGIN->
The word "extrafoliaceous" is derived from:

* **extra-:**  A prefix meaning "outside" or "beyond."
* **foli-:** From the Latin "folium," meaning "leaf."
* **-aceous:** A suffix indicating "pertaining to" or "having the nature of."

Therefore, extrafoliaceous literally means "pertaining to being outside the leaf."


USAGE->
This word is primarily used in a descriptive context within botanical writing and scientific studies of plant morphology. It is not commonly used in everyday conversation.  There are no specific verb conjugations or tenses associated with it as it's an adjective.


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly incorporating "extrafoliaceous".  Its use is limited to its specific botanical meaning.
