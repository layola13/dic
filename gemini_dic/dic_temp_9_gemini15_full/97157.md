infinitives:/ɪnˈfɪnətɪvz/ | n. |不定式 |An infinitive is the base form of a verb, usually preceded by "to,"  expressing the action or state of being without specifying tense or subject.  It functions as a noun, adjective, or adverb. | 例句：I want to travel the world. (noun)  He is the person to ask. (adjective)  She studied hard to succeed. (adverb)|近义词：verbal noun (as a noun)|反义词：无


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I want to go to the park. = 我想去公园。
She needs to study for the exam. = 她需要为考试学习。


一般过去时:
He planned to visit his family. = 他计划去看望他的家人。
They hoped to win the game. = 他们希望赢得比赛。


现在完成时:
I have decided to quit my job. = 我已经决定辞职了。
She has promised to help us. = 她已经答应帮助我们。


过去完成时:
He intended to have finished the report by now. = 他本来打算现在已经完成报告了。
They had hoped to have arrived earlier. = 他们本来希望早点到达。


将来时:
We are going to travel to Europe next year. = 我们明年要去欧洲旅行。
She will try to finish the project on time. = 她将尽力按时完成项目。


虚拟语气:
It's important to be careful. = 小心很重要。
It would be nice to have a vacation. = 休假会很不错。


被动语态:
The book is difficult to understand. = 这本书很难理解。
The problem needs to be solved immediately. = 这个问题需要立即解决。


疑问句:
What do you want to do? = 你想做什么？
Where do you plan to go? = 你计划去哪里？


否定句:
I decided not to go. = 我决定不去。
She failed to complete the task. = 她没能完成任务。
