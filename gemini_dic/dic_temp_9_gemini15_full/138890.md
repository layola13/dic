词源:
The word "nut-like" is a compound adjective formed from the noun "nut" and the suffix "-like," meaning "resembling" or "similar to."  The word "nut" itself has origins in Old English and Proto-Germanic roots, referring to various kinds of edible seeds or nuts.  The "-like" suffix is of Old English origin as well.
