sirened:/ˈsaɪrənd/| v.|发出汽笛声；报警；诱惑|To make a sound like a siren; to warn with a siren; to attract irresistibly.|例句：The police car sirened past. 警车鸣笛驶过。

The ship sirened a warning to the approaching vessels. 船发出汽笛警告驶近的船只。|近义词：wailed, howled, blared|反义词：silenced, hushed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ambulance sirens every day. = 救护车每天都鸣笛。
The factory siren at 5 pm. = 工厂的汽笛在下午五点鸣响。


一般过去时:
The ship sirened loudly as it left the harbor. = 船离开港口时大声鸣笛。
The police car sirened past our house. = 警车鸣笛经过我们家。


现在完成时:
The factory has sirened at the end of the shift. = 工厂在轮班结束时鸣响了汽笛。
The lifeguard has already sirened a warning. = 救生员已经鸣笛发出警告。


过去完成时:
The captain had sirened before the storm hit. = 船长在暴风雨来临之前已经鸣笛了。
The train had sirened its approach just before the accident. = 火车在事故发生前刚刚鸣笛预告。


将来时:
The ship will siren when it enters the harbor. = 船进入港口时将会鸣笛。
The alarm will siren if there is a fire. = 如果发生火灾，警报将会鸣响。


虚拟语气:
If the ship had sirened earlier, the accident might have been avoided. = 如果船早点鸣笛，事故可能就会避免了。
I wish the factory hadn't sirened so loudly last night. = 我真希望工厂昨晚不要鸣笛这么大声。


被动语态:

The emergency siren was sirened throughout the city. = 紧急警报在整个城市鸣响。

否定句:
