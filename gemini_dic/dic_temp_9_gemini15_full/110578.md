limited's:/ˈlɪmɪtɪdz/| n.|有限的；受限制的 |The company's resources are limited.|公司的资源有限。|近义词：restricted, confined, circumscribed |反义词：unlimited, boundless, unrestricted


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company's resources are limited. = 公司的资源是有限的。
His knowledge of the subject is limited. = 他对这个主题的了解有限。


一般过去时:
The meeting was limited to only ten people. = 会议仅限于十人参加。
Their options were limited by the budget. = 他们的选择受到预算的限制。


现在完成时:
The project has been limited by unforeseen circumstances. = 该项目因不可预见的情况而受限。
Our progress has been limited by a lack of funding. = 我们的进展由于缺乏资金而受到限制。


过去完成时:
The damage had been limited by the quick response of the firefighters. = 消防员的快速反应将损失控制在最小范围内。
The investigation had been limited to a specific timeframe. = 调查仅限于特定时间段。


将来时:
The success of the plan will be limited by the available resources. = 计划的成功将受到可用资源的限制。
Their choices will be limited if the budget isn't increased. = 如果预算不增加，他们的选择将会受到限制。


虚拟语气:
If the budget were unlimited, we could do so much more. = 如果预算无限，我们可以做得更多。
I wish our time were not so limited. = 我希望我们的时间不是那么有限。


被动语态:
Access to the building was limited to authorized personnel. = 只有授权人员才能进入该建筑物。
The discussion was limited to specific topics. = 讨论仅限于特定主题。


疑问句:
Are our options limited? = 我们的选择有限吗？
Is the number of participants limited? = 参加人数有限制吗？


否定句:
The possibilities are not limited. = 可能性并非有限。
The damage was not limited to one area. = 损害并不局限于一个区域。
