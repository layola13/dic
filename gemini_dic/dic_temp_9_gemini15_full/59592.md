ebullient:/ɪˈbʊljənt/| adj.|热情洋溢的；兴高采烈的；充满活力的|Characterized by or expressing great enthusiasm and cheerfulness.|The children were ebullient after receiving their gifts.孩子们收到礼物后兴高采烈。|近义词：exuberant, enthusiastic, vivacious|反义词：apathetic, lethargic, depressed


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is ebullient about his new job. 他对他的新工作热情洋溢。
She remains ebullient despite the challenges.  尽管面临挑战，她依然热情满满。


一般过去时:

被动语态:

疑问句:
Is he usually so ebullient? 他通常这么热情吗？

否定句:
He wasn't ebullient at all; he seemed quite subdued. 他一点也不热情；他显得相当压抑。
The response to the announcement wasn't ebullient.  对公告的回应并不热情。

词源:
From Late Latin *ebullient-, present participle of ebullire "to boil up, bubble," from e- "out" + bullire "to boil."  最终源自拉丁语动词 *bullire "to boil"。  因此，字面意思与沸腾、冒泡有关，引申为热情洋溢、充满活力。
