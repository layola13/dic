pendulum: /ˈpɛndʒələm/ | n. | 钟摆；摇摆不定的事物 | A pendulum is a weight suspended from a pivot so that it can swing freely.  It is used in clocks to regulate their movement.  The term can also be used metaphorically to describe something that swings back and forth between two extremes. | Example: The grandfather clock's pendulum swung rhythmically.  爷爷钟的钟摆有节奏地摆动着。  The political climate is a pendulum swinging between hope and despair. 政治气候像钟摆一样在希望与绝望之间摇摆。 | 近义词：oscillator, swing | 反义词：static, immobile
