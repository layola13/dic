interweaves:/ˌɪntərˈwiːvz/| v.| 交织；交错；混合 | To combine or blend together in a way that is intricate or complex.  The threads of the narrative interweave seamlessly, creating a rich tapestry of events and emotions. | The author masterfully interweaves fact and fiction in his historical novel.  The different musical themes interweave beautifully in the symphony. |近义词：intertwine, blend, combine, mingle |反义词：separate, divide, segregate

词源:

The word "interweave" is a combination of the prefix "inter-" (meaning "between" or "among") and the verb "weave" (meaning "to create a fabric by interlacing threads").  Its origins trace back to Old English *wefan, meaning to weave.  The prefix "inter-" has Latin roots.
