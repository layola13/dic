cocultivating:/ˌkoʊˈkʌltɪveɪtɪŋ/| v.| 共同培养；共培养 | The process of growing two or more different types of cells or organisms together in the same culture.  This technique is used in various fields, including cell biology and microbiology, to study cell-cell interactions, symbiotic relationships, and other biological phenomena. | Example: Researchers are cocultivating human cells with bacterial cells to investigate the impact of the microbiome on human health.  研究人员正在共同培养人类细胞和细菌细胞，以研究微生物组对人类健康的影响。|近义词：cultivating together, growing together |反义词：separately culturing
