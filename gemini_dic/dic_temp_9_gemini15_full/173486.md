quasi-fatalistic: /ˈkwɑːzaɪˌfeɪtəlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 近乎宿命论的；似宿命论的 | Seemingly resigned to fate; exhibiting a passive acceptance of events as inevitable.  It suggests a belief that events are largely predetermined, but not with the absolute certainty of true fatalism.  There's a hint of resignation, but perhaps with a glimmer of agency or possibility remaining. |

例句：

He adopted a quasi-fatalistic attitude towards his failing business, neither actively fighting nor fully surrendering. = 他对日渐衰败的生意采取了一种近乎宿命论的态度，既没有积极地反抗，也没有完全放弃。


Her quasi-fatalistic approach to the crisis meant she didn't actively seek solutions, but neither did she despair completely. = 她对危机的近乎宿命论的态度意味着她并没有积极地寻求解决方案，但她也没有完全绝望。


近义词：passive, resigned, accepting, acquiescent


反义词：proactive, defiant, optimistic, hopeful


词性：形容词
