semicolons:/ˈsɛmɪˌkoʊlən/| n. |分号 |A semicolon is a punctuation mark (;) used to connect independent clauses that are closely related in thought.  It indicates a pause that is stronger than a comma but weaker than a period.  Semicolons are also used to separate items in a list when those items already contain commas. | Example: The storm raged; the trees bowed before its fury.  暴风雨肆虐；树木在暴风雨面前弯下了腰。  |近义词：colon (in some contexts) |反义词：comma (in terms of strength of pause)


USAGE->
There is no verb conjugation for semicolons as it is a punctuation mark, not a verb.  The usage examples below demonstrate how semicolons are used grammatically.

Joining Independent Clauses:
The rain was falling; the wind howled fiercely.  雨在下；风在猛烈地呼啸。

The game was over; the crowd dispersed slowly.  比赛结束了；人群慢慢散去。


Separating Items in a List with Internal Commas:
I have visited London, England; Paris, France; and Rome, Italy. 我去过英国伦敦；法国巴黎；和意大利罗马。

The guests included Mr. Smith, a lawyer; Ms. Jones, a doctor; and Dr. Brown, a professor.  来宾包括史密斯先生，一位律师；琼斯女士，一位医生；和布朗博士，一位教授。


Conjunctive Adverbs:
He was tired; however, he continued working. 他很累了；然而，他继续工作。

She studied hard; therefore, she passed the exam. 她努力学习；因此，她通过了考试。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases that specifically use "semicolon" as a part of the phrase.  The use of semicolons is purely grammatical.
