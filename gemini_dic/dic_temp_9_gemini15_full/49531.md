demarcating:/ˌdiːmɑːrˈkeɪtɪŋ/| v.|标明界限；划定界线 | To set the boundaries or limits of something.  The process of establishing clear distinctions or divisions. | 例句：The treaty demarcates the border between the two countries.  条约划定了两国之间的边界。

例句：The fence demarcates the property line.  栅栏标明了地界。


近义词：define, delimit, separate, distinguish, outline

反义词：blur, confuse, combine, merge

词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They demarcate the playing field. = 他们划定比赛场地。
The government demarcates the protected area. = 政府划定保护区。


一般过去时:
The explorers demarcated the new territory. = 探险家们划定了新的领土。
They demarcated the area for construction. = 他们划定了建筑区域。


现在完成时:
Scientists have demarcated several distinct zones. = 科学家们已经划定了几个不同的区域。
The committee has demarcated the responsibilities of each member. = 委员会已经划定了每个成员的职责。


过去完成时:
They had demarcated the plot before starting to build. = 他们在开始建造之前已经划定了地块。
The map had demarcated the ancient city walls. = 地图上已经标明了古代城墙。


将来时:

The surveyors will demarcate the land for the new housing development. = 测量员将为新的住宅开发项目划定土地。


虚拟语气:
If they demarcated the area clearly, there would be less confusion. = 如果他们清楚地划定区域，就不会有那么多的混乱。
I wish they had demarcated the boundaries more precisely. = 我希望他们能更精确地划定界限。


被动语态:
The area was demarcated by the local authorities. = 该区域由地方当局划定。
The boundaries are being demarcated using GPS technology. = 边界正在使用 GPS 技术进行划定。


疑问句:
How will they demarcate the different sections of the park? = 他们将如何划分公园的不同区域？
Have the boundaries been clearly demarcated? = 边界是否已明确划定？


否定句:
The map did not clearly demarcate the river's course. = 地图没有清楚地标明河流的走向。
They failed to demarcate the area effectively. = 他们未能有效地划定该区域。


PHRASE->

词源:
From the Latin word "demarcāre", meaning "to mark out" or "to set the limits of".  The prefix "de-" means "away" or "off", and "marcāre" means "to mark".
