sidles:/ˈsaɪdəlz/| vi.|悄悄地走；蹭着走；|to walk sideways or close to a wall or other object, especially in a shy or sneaky way.|例句：He sidled up to the bar and ordered a drink. 他悄悄地走到酒吧点了一杯酒。|例句：She sidled past him, avoiding eye contact. 她悄悄地从他身边走过，避免眼神接触。|近义词：inch, creep, shuffle|反义词：stride, march


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He sidles up to the counter. = 他悄悄地走到柜台旁。
She sidles through the crowd. = 她从人群中悄悄地挤过去。

一般过去时:
He sidled into the room. = 他悄悄地溜进了房间。
She sidled away from the argument. = 她悄悄地离开争吵现场。

现在完成时:
He has sidled into a position of influence. = 他已逐渐取得了有影响力的地位。
She has sidled her way into the company. = 她已设法进入那家公司。

过去完成时:
He had sidled out before I arrived. = 我到之前他已经悄悄地出去了。
She had sidled up to the boss before asking for a raise. = 她先悄悄地走到老板跟前，然后才要求加薪。

将来时:
He will sidle up to her and ask for a dance. = 他会悄悄地走到她跟前，邀请她跳舞。
She will sidle out of the meeting before the vote. = 她会在投票前悄悄地离开会议。

虚拟语气:
If he sidled closer, he might hear their conversation. = 如果他悄悄地靠得更近，他可能会听到他们的谈话。
I wish he hadn't sidled away from the discussion. = 我真希望他没有从讨论中悄悄地溜走。

被动语态:  (Sidle is an intransitive verb, it cannot be used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
Did he sidle away from the situation? = 他是不是悄悄地躲开了那个局面？
Will she sidle up to him at the party? = 她会在派对上悄悄地走到他身边吗？

否定句:
He didn't sidle up to the table, he walked straight there. = 他没有悄悄地走到桌子旁，而是径直走过去。
She didn't sidle out of the room; she left openly. = 她没有悄悄地溜出房间，而是公开地离开了。


PHRASE->
sidle up to someone = 悄悄地靠近某人
sidle into something = 悄悄地进入某种状态/情况
sidle away from something = 悄悄地避开某事
sidle past someone = 悄悄地从某人身边走过
sidle out of something = 悄悄地退出某事


词源:

The word "sidle" originates from the Old English word "sidlian," meaning "to go sideways."  It's related to the Old Norse word "síða," meaning "side."  The evolution of the word shows a clear link between its meaning and the physical action of moving sideways.
