pedantocracy: /ˌpedænˈtɒkrəsi/ | n. | 卖弄学问的统治；书呆子统治；学究政治 | Rule by pedants; a government or system of governance characterized by the dominance of pedants.  It implies an overly rigid, formal, and often ineffective system controlled by those who emphasize trivial details over substance. | The professor's pedantocracy in the department stifled creativity and innovation.  教授在系里的卖弄学问的统治扼杀了创造力和创新。 | 近义词：bureaucracy,  oligarchy | 反义词：democracy, meritocracy
