radio-label:/ˈreɪdioʊˌleɪbl/| n. |放射性标记物，放射性同位素标记|A radio-label is a molecule that has been tagged with a radioactive isotope to allow its detection and tracking in biological systems or chemical processes.  It's used in various scientific experiments to study the movement, distribution, and metabolism of substances within an organism or system.|Example: Researchers used a carbon-14 radio-label to trace the pathway of glucose metabolism in the cells.|近义词：tracer, radioactive tracer|反义词：unlabeled molecule
