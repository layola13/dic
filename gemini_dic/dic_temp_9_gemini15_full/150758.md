paraded:/pəˈreɪdɪd/| v.| 游行，炫耀，展示 | To march in a procession, often in a formal or celebratory way; to show off ostentatiously. |例句：The soldiers paraded through the streets. 士兵们在街上游行。  The model paraded her new designs. 模特展示了她新的设计。|近义词：marched, displayed, showed off |反义词：hid, concealed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They parade every Sunday. = 他们每个星期天都游行。
The company parades its achievements annually. = 公司每年都炫耀它的成就。


一般过去时:
We paraded through the town. = 我们在镇上游行。
She paraded her new jewelry at the party. = 她在派对上炫耀她的新首饰。


现在完成时:
They have paraded in this city many times. = 他们在城里游行过很多次。
He has paraded his wealth since winning the lottery. = 自从赢得彩票后，他就一直炫耀他的财富。


过去完成时:
The soldiers had paraded before the king arrived. = 国王到达之前，士兵们已经游行了。
She had paraded her skills before the interview. = 面试前，她已经展示了她的技能。


将来时:
We will parade next week. = 我们下周将游行。
The company will parade its new products at the exhibition. = 公司将在展览会上展示它的新产品。


虚拟语气:
If we had more time, we would have paraded longer. = 如果我们有更多的时间，我们会游行更久。
If she had more confidence, she would have paraded her talents. = 如果她更有自信，她就会展示她的才华。


被动语态:
The parade was watched by thousands of people. = 游行被成千上万的人观看。
The new collection was paraded at the fashion show. = 新系列在时装秀上被展示。


疑问句:
Will they parade tomorrow? = 他们明天会游行吗？
Were the designs paraded successfully? = 设计是否成功地被展示了？


否定句:
They did not parade in the rain. = 他们没有在雨中游行。
She didn't parade her achievements. = 她没有炫耀她的成就。
