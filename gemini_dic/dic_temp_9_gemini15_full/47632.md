debilitation: /ˌdɪˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən/ | n. |衰弱；虚弱；无力；使衰弱| The process of weakening or impairing; a state of being weakened or impaired, often physically or mentally.  |例句：The long illness resulted in severe debilitation. 长期疾病导致严重的虚弱。

例句：Prolonged exposure to the sun can lead to debilitation. 长时间暴露在阳光下会导致虚弱。


近义词：weakening, enfeeblement, incapacitation, prostration, impairment

反义词：strengthening, invigoration, empowerment


词源:  From Late Latin *debilis (weak) + -itation (act of).  The Latin root "debilis" is related to the Proto-Indo-European root "*dʰebh-", meaning "to bind, to join."  The sense of weakness likely developed from the idea of being bound or constrained.
