mistrusted:/mɪsˈtrʌstɪd/| v. |不信任，怀疑|The verb "mistrusted" is the past tense and past participle of the verb "mistrust," meaning to have no confidence or faith in someone or something; to suspect.  It implies a feeling of doubt or suspicion about the trustworthiness or reliability of a person, thing, or situation. |例句：He mistrusted the salesman's promises. 他不相信推销员的承诺。

He mistrusted her motives from the start. 他从一开始就怀疑她的动机。|近义词：distrusted, doubted, suspected, disbelieved |反义词：trusted, believed, confided in


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They mistrust his judgment. = 他们不信任他的判断。
We mistrust the information we received. = 我们不信任我们收到的信息。


一般过去时:
I mistrusted his intentions. = 我不信任他的意图。
She mistrusted his sudden generosity. = 她不信任他突然的慷慨。


现在完成时:
I have mistrusted him ever since that incident. = 从那件事起，我就一直不信任他。
They have mistrusted the company's leadership for years. = 多年来，他们一直不信任公司的领导层。


过去完成时:
I had mistrusted her before, so I was not surprised by her actions. = 我以前就不信任她，所以她的行为并不让我感到惊讶。
They had mistrusted his claims even before the evidence emerged. = 即使在证据出现之前，他们也不相信他的说法。


将来时:
I will mistrust his advice in the future. = 将来我将不相信他的建议。
We will mistrust any information coming from that source. = 我们将不信任来自该来源的任何信息。


虚拟语气:
If I had trusted him, I wouldn't be in this mess. = 如果我当时信任他，我就不会陷入这种困境。(注意：这里用的是trust，因为虚拟语气是对过去情况的假设)
I wish I hadn't mistrusted her judgment. = 我真希望我没有怀疑她的判断力。


被动语态:
His judgment was mistrusted by many. = 许多人不信任他的判断。
The information was mistrusted by the authorities. = 当局不信任这些信息。


疑问句:
Did you mistrust his explanation? = 你不相信他的解释吗？
Why did they mistrust her? = 他们为什么不信任她？


否定句:
I didn't mistrust her at first. = 我一开始并没有不信任她。
They didn't mistrust the evidence presented. = 他们并不怀疑提交的证据。


PHRASE->
