cold-turkey:/koʊldˈtɜːrki/| n. adj. phrasal verb|戒断;突然完全停止|To stop doing something suddenly and completely, especially something addictive, without any gradual reduction or assistance.|例句：He quit smoking cold turkey. 他突然戒了烟。 He went cold turkey on his drug habit. 他一下子戒掉了毒瘾。|近义词：abruptly, suddenly, immediately|反义词：gradually, tapering off


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He quits smoking cold turkey. = 他突然戒烟。
She goes cold turkey on her caffeine addiction. = 她突然戒掉了咖啡因成瘾。


一般过去时:
He quit smoking cold turkey. = 他突然戒了烟。
She went cold turkey on her sugar addiction. = 她突然戒掉了糖瘾。


现在完成时:
He has quit smoking cold turkey. = 他已经突然戒烟了。
She has gone cold turkey on her chocolate habit. = 她已经突然戒掉了巧克力习惯。


过去完成时:
He had quit smoking cold turkey a year ago. = 他一年前就突然戒烟了。
She had gone cold turkey on her alcohol consumption. = 她已经突然戒酒了。


将来时:
He will quit smoking cold turkey next week. = 他下周将突然戒烟。
She will go cold turkey on her gaming addiction. = 她将突然戒掉游戏成瘾。


虚拟语气:
If I were him, I would quit smoking cold turkey. = 如果我是他，我会突然戒烟。
I wish I could go cold turkey on my unhealthy eating habits. = 我希望我能突然戒掉不健康的饮食习惯。


被动语态:  (This idiom is rarely used passively)
The addiction was overcome by going cold turkey. = 通过突然戒断克服了成瘾。
The bad habit was abandoned cold turkey. = 这个坏习惯被突然放弃了。


疑问句:
Did he quit smoking cold turkey? = 他是突然戒烟的吗？
Will she go cold turkey on her coffee? = 她会突然戒掉咖啡吗？


否定句:
He didn't quit smoking cold turkey; he gradually reduced his intake. = 他不是突然戒烟的；他是逐渐减少吸烟量的。
She didn't go cold turkey on her medication; she followed her doctor's instructions for tapering off. = 她没有突然停药；她按照医生的指示逐渐减量。
