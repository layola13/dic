prevalidity:/ˌpriːˈvælɪdəti/| n. |预效度；预测效度|The degree to which a test or measure predicts future performance or behavior.  It assesses how well a test or instrument predicts an outcome of interest.|例句：The prevalidity of the new aptitude test was assessed by comparing scores to subsequent job performance.  新的能力倾向测试的预效度是通过将分数与随后的工作表现进行比较来评估的。|近义词：predictive validity, prognostic validity|反义词：None specifically defined, but low predictive power could be considered an antonym.
