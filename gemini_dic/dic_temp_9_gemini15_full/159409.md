polarising:/ˈpoʊləraɪzɪŋ/|/ˈpəʊləraɪzɪŋ/| adj. |使两极分化的；引起争议的；偏光的|  Causing strong opinions or reactions, often opposing ones;  also relating to the polarization of light.|The polarising issue of immigration sparked heated debates.  移民这个两极分化的议题引发了激烈的辩论。|His controversial views were highly polarising, alienating some while attracting others. 他有争议的观点极具争议性，疏远了一些人，同时也吸引了另一些人。|unifying, unifying, conciliatory|harmonious, agreeable|

一般现在时:
The politician's remarks are polarising the public. = 这位政治家的言论正在使公众两极分化。
This topic polarizes opinions across the country. = 这个话题正在全国范围内引起意见分歧。


一般过去时:
The election polarised the nation. = 那次选举使全国两极分化。
The debate polarised the community.  = 那场辩论使社区两极分化。


现在完成时:
The issue has polarised the electorate. =  这个问题已经使选民两极分化。
This controversy has polarised public opinion. =这场争议已经使公众意见两极分化。


过去完成时:
The media had polarised the public before the trial began. = 在审判开始之前，媒体已经使公众两极分化。
The previous government had polarised the country with its economic policies. = 前政府的经济政策使国家两极分化。


将来时:
This decision will further polarise the country. = 这个决定将进一步使国家两极分化。
The upcoming referendum will likely polarise public opinion. =  即将到来的公投可能会使公众意见两极分化。


虚拟语气:
If the government hadn't intervened, the situation would have polarised even further. = 如果政府不干预，情况本会更加两极分化。
If they hadn't taken such a divisive stance, the issue wouldn't have been so polarising. = 如果他们没有采取如此分裂的立场，这个问题就不会如此两极分化。


被动语态:
The public is being polarised by the media's biased reporting. = 公众正受到媒体有偏见报道的影响而两极分化。
Society was polarised by the events of that year. = 那一年发生的事件使社会两极分化。


疑问句:
Is this issue polarising the community? = 这个问题是否正在使社区两极分化？
Does this policy risk polarising voters? =这项政策是否有使选民两极分化的风险？


否定句:
The new policy did not polarise the population. = 新政策并没有使人口两极分化。
His speech did not polarise the audience as expected. = 他的演讲并没有像预期的那样使听众两极分化。
