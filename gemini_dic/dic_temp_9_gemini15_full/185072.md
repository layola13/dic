reticence: /ˈrɛtɪsəns/ | n. | 沉默寡言；含蓄；不轻易表达 |  A reluctance or unwillingness to speak about your thoughts or feelings.  |  Her reticence on the subject surprised us.  她的沉默寡言让我们感到惊讶。  |  reserve, taciturnity, silence |  volubility, loquacity, garrulity


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His reticence prevents him from sharing his feelings. = 他的沉默寡言使他无法分享自己的感受。
She shows reticence in public speaking. = 她在公开演讲中表现得很沉默寡言。


一般过去时:
His reticence during the interrogation was suspicious. = 他在审讯中的沉默寡言令人怀疑。
Her reticence about her past made her seem mysterious. = 她对过去的沉默寡言让她显得神秘莫测。


现在完成时:
Her reticence has made it difficult to understand her true intentions. = 她的沉默寡言使得很难理解她的真实意图。
His reticence has been a source of frustration for his family. = 他的沉默寡言一直是他家人沮丧的根源。


过去完成时:
His reticence had already created a distance between them. = 他的沉默寡言已经在他们之间制造了距离。
Her reticence had prevented her from seeking help sooner. = 她的沉默寡言让她无法早点寻求帮助。

虚拟语气:

否定句:

Her lack of reticence surprised everyone. = 她不沉默寡言的态度让每个人都感到惊讶。

词源:
From Latin *reticēre, from *retinent-, present active participle of retinēre "to hold back," from re- "back" + tenere "to hold."  The sense development is from "holding back" to "holding back speech."
