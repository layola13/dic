crozier:/ˈkroʊʒər/| n. |牧羊杖；主教权杖|A crozier is a shepherd's crook, or a ceremonial staff carried by a bishop or abbot as a symbol of his office.  It is often elaborately decorated.|例句：The bishop carried his crozier high as he processed down the aisle.主教在过道上走的时候高举着他的牧羊杖。

The abbot used his crozier to point out the important passages in the scripture.  方丈用他的牧羊杖指着经文中重要的段落。|近义词：pastoral staff, staff,  crook|反义词：None

词源:
From Old French *croc, meaning "hook," ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kruk- "hook, bend."  The -ier suffix is a common agent noun suffix.  The evolution reflects the crook shape of the staff.
