significant:/sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt/| adj.|重要的，显著的，意味深长的|important;noticeable;having a meaning|The results of the study were significant.  这项研究的结果意义重大。

His contribution to the project was significant. 他对项目的贡献是巨大的。

近义词：important, considerable, substantial, noteworthy, momentous|反义词：insignificant, unimportant, trivial, negligible


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The data is significant. = 数据很重要。
This detail is significant to the investigation. = 这个细节对调查很重要。


一般过去时:
The change was significant. = 变化很大。
His contribution was significant to the team's success. = 他的贡献对团队的成功至关重要。


现在完成时:
The impact has been significant. = 影响深远。
Researchers have found significant differences between the two groups. = 研究人员发现两组之间存在显著差异。


过去完成时:
The problem had become significantly worse. = 问题已经变得严重得多。
They had already made significant progress before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期之前已经取得了显著进展。


将来时:
The effect will be significant. = 效果将非常显著。
The new policy will have a significant impact on the economy. = 新政策将对经济产生重大影响。


虚拟语气:
If the results were significant, we would publish them. = 如果结果很重要，我们会发表它们。
It is significant that he didn't attend the meeting. = 他没有参加会议，这一点很重要。


被动语态:
The difference was considered significant. = 差异被认为是显著的。
The impact of the decision was widely considered significant. = 这一决定的影响被广泛认为是重大的。


疑问句:
Is this finding significant? = 这一发现重要吗？
Are the differences statistically significant? = 这些差异在统计上是否显著？


否定句:
The changes were not significant. = 变化不显著。
The effect of the drug was not statistically significant.  药物的效果在统计上并不显著。


PHRASE->
significant other = 重要他人
statistically significant = 统计显著的
highly significant = 非常重要的
of significant importance = 至关重要的
significantly different = 显著不同


词源:
significant源于拉丁语“significāre”，意为“to make a sign; to signify”。  "significāre"  由 "signum" (sign, mark) 和 "-ficare" (to make) 构成。  因此，significant 的核心含义与“标志”、“象征”和“表明”有关。  它逐渐演变为我们今天理解的“重要的”、“显著的”含义。
