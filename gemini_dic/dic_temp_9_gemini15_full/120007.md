millimetres: /ˈmɪlɪmiːtərz/ | n. |毫米|

英文解析:A millimetre (mm) is a unit of length in the metric system, equal to one thousandth of a metre (0.001 m).  It's commonly used for measuring small distances and objects.

例句:

The thickness of the paper is approximately 0.1 millimetres.  = 这张纸的厚度大约是0.1毫米。

The screw is 5 millimetres in diameter. = 这颗螺丝的直径是5毫米。


近义词: millimeter (American spelling)

反义词:  There isn't a direct antonym, as it's a unit of measurement.  One could use larger units like centimetres or metres to express a contrasting idea of scale.

词源:
