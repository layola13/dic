give-the-slip: /ɡɪv ðə slɪp/ | v. | 逃脱；摆脱；溜掉 | To escape from someone or something, especially secretly or cleverly.  | 例句：The thief gave the police the slip in the crowded market. 小偷在拥挤的市场中摆脱了警察。

The prisoner gave the guards the slip during the night. 囚犯在夜间逃脱了警卫的看守。


近义词：escape, evade, elude, get away from | 反义词：be caught, be apprehended


词性: 动词


USAGE->

过去完成时:

疑问句:
Did the prisoner give the guards the slip? = 囚犯逃脱了警卫的看守吗？

否定句:
He didn't give the police the slip; they caught him. = 他没有逃脱警察的追捕；他们抓住了他。
She couldn't give her anxiety the slip; it followed her everywhere. = 她无法摆脱焦虑；焦虑无处不在跟着她。

词源:
The phrase "give the slip" originates from the practice of slipping a piece of paper (a "slip") to someone, often secretly or discreetly.  This evolved to mean secretly leaving someone or something. The "give" part signifies the action of letting something go or escape.
