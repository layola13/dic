non-symmetrical: /ˌnɒn sɪˈmɛtrɪkəl/ | adj. | 非对称的 | Not symmetrical; lacking symmetry.  Describes something that doesn't have symmetry, meaning it doesn't have the same shape or form on both sides of a central line. | Example: The vase had a non-symmetrical design, with one side noticeably larger than the other.  花瓶的设计是不对称的，一边明显比另一边大。| 近义词：asymmetrical, lopsided, uneven | 反义词：symmetrical, balanced

词源:

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of". (源自拉丁语“non”)
* **symmetrical**:  Adjective meaning "having symmetry".  (源自希腊语“symmetria”,  "sym-" meaning "together" and "metron" meaning "measure")

Therefore, "non-symmetrical" literally means "not having the same measurements on both sides".
