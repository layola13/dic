manageability:/ˌmænɪdʒəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可管理性，易处理性 | The ease with which something can be managed or controlled.  It refers to the degree to which a system, process, or task can be effectively managed and controlled.  This can encompass aspects like simplicity, efficiency, and predictability. | Example: The manageability of the project improved significantly after we implemented new software.  这个项目的可管理性在我们实施新软件后得到了显著提高。  The manageability of the server farm was a major concern. 服务器群的可管理性是一个主要问题。 |近义词：controllability,  handleability,  tractability | 反义词：unmanageability, intractability


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, typical verb tenses are not applicable.  Instead, we'll show how manageability is used in different sentence structures.


肯定句:
The manageability of the network is excellent. = 网络的可管理性非常好。
High manageability is crucial for successful project implementation. = 高可管理性对于项目的成功实施至关重要。


否定句:
The poor manageability of the system led to many problems. = 系统糟糕的可管理性导致了许多问题。
Lack of manageability hindered the team's progress. = 可管理性的缺乏阻碍了团队的进展。


疑问句:
What is the manageability of this new software? = 这款新软件的可管理性如何？
Is the manageability of this task within our capabilities? = 这个任务的可管理性在我们能力范围内吗？


比较句:
The manageability of this system is far superior to the previous one. = 这个系统比以前的系统可管理性好得多。
Compared to other options, this solution offers better manageability.  = 与其他方案相比，此方案具有更好的可管理性。

词源:
The word "manageability" derives from the verb "manage,"  ultimately from Old French *managier (to handle, manage, train a horse), from Vulgar Latin *managiare (to handle, control), from manus (hand). The suffix "-ability" indicates the capacity or potential for something.
