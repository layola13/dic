grafting:/ˈɡræftɪŋ/ | n. vt. |嫁接；移植；（尤指不正当的）获取；安插 |

英文解析:The act or process of grafting; the joining of a scion to a stock.  It can also refer to the act of attaching something (usually something undesirable) onto something else. In a figurative sense, it refers to the dishonest acquisition of something, often a job or position, through improper means.


例句:

The gardener is grafting a new apple variety onto a rootstock.  园丁正在将一个新的苹果品种嫁接到砧木上。

They grafted a new section onto the existing building. 他们把一个新的部分嫁接到现有的建筑物上。


近义词:implantation, inoculation, insertion, transplanting (for the biological sense); acquisition, appropriation (for the figurative sense).


反义词:removal, excision, severance (for the biological sense); relinquishment, forfeiture (for the figurative sense).


词性:noun & verb
