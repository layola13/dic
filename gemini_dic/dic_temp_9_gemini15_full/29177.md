cashed in: /kæʃt ɪn/ | v. |兑现；获利；利用…获利| To convert something (such as an asset) into cash; to profit from something; to exploit something for personal gain. |例句：He cashed in his chips and retired early. 他兑现了他的筹码，提前退休了。

He cashed in on the popularity of the new technology. 他利用新技术的流行获利。

近义词：reaped, capitalized on, exploited | 反义词：wasted, squandered


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They cash in their savings bonds regularly. = 他们定期兑现他们的储蓄债券。
He cashes in on every opportunity. = 他抓住每一个机会获利。


一般过去时:
She cashed in her stocks last week. = 她上周卖掉了她的股票。
The company cashed in on the holiday season. = 公司利用节日季获利。


现在完成时:
They have cashed in all their investments. = 他们已经兑现了所有的投资。
He has cashed in big time on his new invention. = 他凭借他的新发明赚了一大笔钱。


过去完成时:
She had cashed in her lottery ticket before she went to the store. = 她去商店之前已经兑现了她的彩票。
The company had cashed in on the trend before its competitors. = 在竞争对手之前，该公司已经利用了这一趋势。


将来时:
They will cash in their bonds when they retire. = 他们退休时将兑现他们的债券。
He will cash in on the success of his latest book. = 他将利用他最新著作的成功获利。


虚拟语气:
If I had cashed in my stocks earlier, I would have made more money. = 如果我早点兑现我的股票，我会赚更多钱。
I wish I had cashed in on that opportunity. = 我真希望我当时抓住了那个机会。


被动语态:
The bonds were cashed in by the bank. = 债券被银行兑现了。
The opportunity was cashed in by a rival company. = 机会被一家竞争公司利用了。


疑问句:
When will you cash in your savings? = 你什么时候兑现你的储蓄？
Did they cash in on the public's fears? = 他们是否利用了公众的恐惧？


否定句:
He didn't cash in his winnings. = 他没有兑现他的奖金。
They didn't cash in on the trend. = 他们没有利用这一趋势。


PHRASE->

cash in a check = 兑现支票
