diamide:/ˈdaɪæmaɪd/| n. |二酰胺|A diamide is an organic compound containing two amide groups.  Diamides are used in various applications, including as ligands in coordination chemistry and as building blocks in the synthesis of polymers and other complex molecules.  They often exhibit diverse biological activities.|Example: Oxamide is a simple diamide with two amide groups connected to a central carbon atom. 草酰胺是一种简单的二酰胺，其两个酰胺基团连接到中心碳原子。|近义词:  diacylamide (有时)|反义词:  N/A
