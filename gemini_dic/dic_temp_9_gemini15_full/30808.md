cezar:/ˈsiːzɑːr/| n. |凯撒，恺撒 |The word "cezar" is a variant spelling of "Caesar," the name of a prominent Roman family and specifically Julius Caesar, a renowned Roman general and statesman.  The name itself is of Roman origin, and its etymology is uncertain. One theory suggests it derives from a word meaning "hairy," referring to the family's supposed ancestor. Another connects it to a word for "grey eyes."  The name became synonymous with imperial power and authority. |例句：Julius Caesar conquered Gaul.  朱利叶斯·凯撒征服了高卢。 |近义词：emperor, leader, ruler |反义词：subject, citizen
