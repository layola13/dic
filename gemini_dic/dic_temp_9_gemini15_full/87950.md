high-class: /ˌhaɪˈklæs/ | adj. | 高级的；上流社会的；高雅的 |  Of or relating to the highest social class; elegant and expensive.  It implies superior quality, refinement, and often exclusivity. | 例句：That's a high-class restaurant; the food is exquisite and the service impeccable. 那是一家高级餐厅；食物精美，服务周到。


The hotel offers high-class accommodations and amenities. 这家酒店提供高级住宿和设施。


近义词：elite, upscale, luxurious, exclusive, refined, sophisticated | 反义词：low-class, common, cheap, ordinary, vulgar


词源:  The term "high-class" is a compound word. "High" signifies elevated status or position, while "class" refers to a social stratum or category.  Its origins trace back to the development of stratified societies where social rank was a significant factor. The combination emphasizes the superior position of a particular social group or the superior quality of a product or service.
