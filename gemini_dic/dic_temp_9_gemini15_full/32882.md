chondrite:/ˈkɒndraɪt/| n. |球粒陨石|A stony meteorite containing small, spherical inclusions called chondrules, which are believed to have formed in the early solar system.  Chondrites are the most common type of meteorite found on Earth.  They are considered to be relatively unaltered samples of the early solar system. |Example:  The researchers analyzed the composition of the chondrite to learn more about the formation of planets.  研究人员分析了球粒陨石的成分，以了解更多关于行星形成的信息。|近义词: meteorite (but not all meteorites are chondrites)|反义词: achondrite (a type of meteorite lacking chondrules)
