go-up:/ɡoʊ ʌp/| phrasal verb| 上升；上涨；增长；进步；被提升| To move to a higher position, level, or value; to increase in amount or degree; to improve or progress; to be promoted.  |例句：The price of gas went up last week.  上周汽油价格上涨了。|例句：He went up in the company and became a manager. 他在公司里升职了，成为了经理。|近义词：rise, increase, ascend, improve, advance|反义词：go down, decrease, descend, decline, worsen


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The temperature goes up in the afternoon. = 下午气温上升。
The company's profits go up every year. = 公司的利润每年都在增长。


一般过去时:
The balloon went up into the sky. = 气球飞上了天空。
The stock market went up sharply yesterday. = 股市昨天大幅上涨。


现在完成时:
The value of the house has gone up significantly. = 房子的价值已经大幅上涨了。
My English has gone up since I started taking classes. = 自从我开始上课以来，我的英语水平提高了。


过去完成时:
The river had gone up several feet before the flood. = 在洪水来临之前，河水已经上涨了好几英尺。
The company's share price had gone up considerably before the announcement. = 在公告发布之前，公司股价已经大幅上涨了。


将来时:
The price of oil will go up next year. = 明年油价将会上涨。
I think my salary will go up after my performance review. = 我认为绩效考核后我的薪水会涨。


虚拟语气:
If the temperature went up any higher, I would faint. = 如果气温再高一些，我会晕倒。
If the sales went up, we would get a bonus. = 如果销售额上升，我们会得到奖金。


被动语态:
The volume was gradually going up. = 音量正在逐渐增大。
The building is going up quickly. = 这栋楼正在迅速建造中。


疑问句:
Did the cost of living go up this year? = 今年生活成本上涨了吗？
Will the interest rates go up soon? = 利率很快会上升吗？


否定句:
The number of students didn't go up this semester. = 本学期学生人数没有增加。
The plane didn't go up because of the bad weather. = 由于天气恶劣，飞机没有起飞。


PHRASE->

go up against:对抗，与...竞争
go up to:走到...跟前
go up to someone:走近某人
go up against the odds:逆境而行
