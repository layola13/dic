caucus:/ˈkɔːkəs/| n. v.|党团会议；核心小组；秘密会议；参加党团会议|A caucus is a meeting of members of a political party, typically to select candidates or decide policy.  It can also refer to a smaller group within a larger organization.|例句：The Democratic caucus met to discuss their strategy for the upcoming election.  民主党核心小组会面讨论即将到来的选举策略。

The senators held a private caucus to decide on their next move. 参议员们举行了一次秘密会议来决定他们的下一步行动。|近义词：meeting, conference, convention|反义词：None specifically.  The antonym would depend on the context (e.g.,  public meeting if referring to a secret caucus).|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The party caucuses regularly. =  该党定期举行党团会议。
The committee caucuses to make decisions. = 委员会召开核心小组会议来做决定。


一般过去时:
The Democrats caucused last night. = 民主党人昨晚举行了党团会议。
They caucused before the vote. = 他们在投票前进行了核心小组会议。


现在完成时:
The party has caucused several times this year. =  该党今年已经举行过几次党团会议了。
They have caucused on this issue extensively. = 他们已经就这个问题进行了广泛的讨论。


过去完成时:
They had caucused before the announcement was made. =  在宣布之前他们已经开过会了。
The group had caucused extensively before reaching a conclusion. = 该小组在得出结论之前进行了广泛的讨论。


将来时:
The committee will caucus next week. = 委员会下周将举行会议。
The party will caucus to nominate a candidate. = 该党将召开党团会议提名候选人。


虚拟语气:
If they caucused more frequently, they might be more effective. = 如果他们更频繁地举行党团会议，他们可能会更有效率。
It would have been helpful if they had caucused beforehand. = 如果他们事先举行过党团会议，那就有帮助了。


被动语态:
The issue was caucused on extensively. =  这个问题受到了广泛的讨论。
A decision was reached after the group caucused. = 在小组会议之后达成了决定。(Note:  Passive voice is less common with "caucus".)


疑问句:
Did they caucus before voting? = 他们在投票前开过会吗？
Will the party caucus this week? = 该党本周会开会吗？


否定句:
They did not caucus before making a decision. = 他们在做决定之前没有开会。
The group will not caucus until next month. = 该小组直到下个月才会开会。
