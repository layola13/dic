retessellation:/ˌriːˈtesəleɪʃən/ | n. | 重新镶嵌；重新铺设 | The process of re-arranging tiles or other shapes to create a new tessellation.  This often involves changing the arrangement, orientation, or even the shapes themselves, while maintaining the overall covering of a surface. | Example: The artist achieved a stunning retessellation of the original design by rotating and reflecting the individual tiles.  = 艺术家通过旋转和反射单个瓷砖，实现了对原始设计的惊人重新镶嵌。 |近义词:retiling,  repaving (in specific contexts)|反义词:deconstruction (in a geometric sense)
