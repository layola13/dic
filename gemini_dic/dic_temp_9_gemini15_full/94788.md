impugnable: /ɪmˈpjuːɡnəbl/ | adj. | 可驳斥的；可攻击的；不能防御的 | Open to attack; vulnerable to criticism or refutation.  | The politician's claims were clearly impugnable and easily disproven.  这位政治家的说法显然是站不住脚的，很容易被驳倒。 | questionable, assailable, refutable, debatable | irrefutable, undeniable, incontestable, unassailable | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His argument is impugnable. = 他的论点是站不住脚的。
The evidence is impugnable. = 证据是可驳斥的。


一般过去时:
Her testimony was impugnable. = 她的证词是可驳斥的。
The theory proved impugnable. = 理论证明是站不住脚的。


现在完成时:
His reputation has been impugnable. = 他的名誉一直受到质疑。
The system has proven impugnable. = 该系统已被证明是存在漏洞的。


过去完成时:
The accusations had been impugnable. = 那些指控是站不住脚的。
The plan had been impugnable from the start. = 这计划从一开始就存在漏洞。


将来时:
The new policy will be impugnable. = 新政策将会受到批评。
His ideas will likely be impugnable. = 他的想法可能会受到质疑。


虚拟语气:
If his evidence were impugnable, the case would collapse. = 如果他的证据站不住脚，案件就会崩塌。
If the system were impugnable, it would need to be overhauled. = 如果系统存在漏洞，就需要进行彻底检修。


被动语态:
His claims were impugned by the opposition. = 他的说法受到了反对派的攻击。
The study's findings were impugned by several experts. = 这项研究的结果受到了几位专家的质疑。


疑问句:
Is his argument truly impugnable? = 他的论点真的站不住脚吗？
Are the conclusions of the report impugnable? = 报告的结论能否驳斥？


否定句:
His alibi was not impugnable. = 他的不在场证明是站得住脚的。
The data was not impugnable. = 数据是不可驳斥的。


PHRASE->
highly impugnable = 极易受到攻击的
seriously impugnable = 严重有问题的
easily impugnable = 容易驳斥的
open to impugnment = 易受攻击的
subject to impugnment = 容易受到质疑的

词源:
From Latin *impugnare (to fight against, attack), from pugna (fight, battle).  The prefix "im-" represents "in-", meaning "not" or "into," adding a sense of "against" to the core meaning.
