quatrain:/ˈkwɑːtreɪn/| n. |四行诗|A quatrain is a stanza or poem of four lines, usually with a specific rhyme scheme.  Quatrains are a very common poetic form, used in many different styles and traditions.  They can be found in sonnets (as part of a larger structure), limericks, and many free-verse poems.|Example:  The curfew tolls the knell of parting day, / The lowing herd wind slowly o'er the lea, / The ploughman homeward plods his weary way, / And leaves the world to darkness and to me. (Gray's Elegy)|近义词：stanza, verse|反义词：None specifically;  the antonym would relate to the length of the poem (e.g., long poem).
