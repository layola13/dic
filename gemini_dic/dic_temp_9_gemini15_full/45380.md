cunningman:/ˈkʌnɪŋmæn/| n. |江湖郎中，骗子，术士|A cunning man is a person, often in a rural area, who claims to have magical or supernatural powers and provides remedies or charms for illnesses or other problems.  They are often associated with folk magic and may use herbal remedies or other traditional practices, but their methods are not scientifically proven and can be deceptive.|例句：The villagers consulted the cunning man to cure their sick child. 村民们向江湖郎中求助以治愈他们生病的孩子。
The old cunning man claimed he could communicate with spirits.  那位老江湖郎中声称他能与灵魂沟通。|近义词：charlatan, quack, sorcerer, wizard, |反义词：physician, doctor, healer (in a legitimate sense)
