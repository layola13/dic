governesses:/ˈɡʌvənəsɪz/| n.|女家庭教师|Female teachers who provide private education to children, typically in a family's home.  They often provided a wide range of education and childcare. |例句：Many governesses in the 19th century came from middle-class families seeking employment.|近义词：tutors, private teachers|反义词：students, pupils|名词

词源:
From governess, from govern + -ess (feminine suffix).  The word "govern" has roots in Old French "governer" and ultimately in Latin "gubernāre" (to steer, govern).  The "-ess" suffix indicates a female role.
