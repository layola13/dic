microphyllous:/ˌmaɪkroʊˈfɪləs/| adj. | 微叶的 | Having small leaves; characteristic of certain primitive vascular plants.  This term describes plants with leaves that are small and relatively simple in structure, often lacking a complex system of veins.  These leaves are typically unbranched and narrow.  The term contrasts with megaphyllous, which refers to plants with larger, more complex leaves. | Example:  Many early land plants were microphyllous.  许多早期的陆地植物都是微叶的。  |近义词: small-leaved | 反义词: megaphyllous | 词性:形容词
