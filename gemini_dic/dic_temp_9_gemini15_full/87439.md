hesitated:/ˈhezɪteɪtɪd/| v.|犹豫，迟疑|To pause before saying or doing something, especially because of uncertainty or fear.|例句：He hesitated before answering the question. 他犹豫了一下才回答这个问题。|例句：She hesitated to accept the offer, fearing it was too good to be true. 她犹豫着是否接受这个提议，担心这太好了，不像是真的。|近义词：paused, wavered, faltered|反义词：decided, rushed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hesitates before speaking in public. 他在公开场合发言前会犹豫。
She hesitates to ask for help. 她犹豫着要不要寻求帮助。

一般过去时:
I hesitated to call him. 我犹豫着要不要给他打电话。
He hesitated before signing the contract. 他在签合同之前犹豫了。

现在完成时:
I have hesitated for too long. 我犹豫了太久了。
She has hesitated to make a decision. 她一直犹豫着要不要做决定。

过去完成时:
I had hesitated to tell him the truth. 我之前犹豫着要不要告诉他真相。
She had hesitated before accepting the job offer.  她之前犹豫着要不要接受这份工作。

将来时:
I will hesitate to do it again. 我将犹豫是否再次去做。
She will hesitate before speaking to her boss. 她将会在和她老板说话之前犹豫。

虚拟语气:
I would hesitate to jump to conclusions. 我会犹豫着要不要下结论。
If I were you, I would hesitate to trust him. 如果我是你，我会犹豫着要不要相信他。

被动语态:
The decision was hesitated over for weeks.  这个决定犹豫了几个星期。
His response was hesitated over before being given. 他的回答在给出之前犹豫了。

疑问句:
Do you hesitate to speak your mind? 你是否犹豫着要不要说出你的想法？
Does he hesitate to take risks? 他是否犹豫着要不要冒险？

否定句:
I didn't hesitate to help her. 我毫不犹豫地帮助了她。
He didn't hesitate to express his opinion. 他毫不犹豫地表达了自己的意见。
