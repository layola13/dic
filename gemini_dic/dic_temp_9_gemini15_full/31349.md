characterizable: /ˌkærəktəraɪzəbl/ | adj. | 可描述的，可刻画的，可表征的 | Capable of being characterized; possessing characteristics that can be described or identified.  | The experiment yielded results that were readily characterizable.  实验结果很容易描述。 | describable, definable, identifiable | undefinable, indescribable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This substance is characterizable by its high melting point. =  这种物质的特点是熔点高。
The painting is characterizable by its bold colours. = 这幅画的特点是颜色鲜艳。


一般过去时:
The symptoms were characterizable as flu-like. = 症状表现为类似流感的症状。
His personality was characterizable as shy and reserved. = 他的性格可以形容为害羞和内向。


现在完成时:
The new technology has been characterizable by its efficiency. = 新技术以其效率为特征。
The situation has been characterizable by uncertainty and confusion. = 这种情况的特点是不确定和混乱。


过去完成时:
The problem had been characterizable as unsolvable before the new discovery. = 在新的发现之前，这个问题曾被认为是无法解决的。
The relationship had been characterizable by mistrust and conflict. = 这段关系的特点是互不信任和冲突。


将来时:
The project will be characterizable by its ambitious goals. = 这个项目将以其宏伟的目标为特征。
The impact of the decision will be characterizable by its long-term consequences. = 该决定的影响将以其长期后果为特征。


虚拟语气:
If the data were more complete, the results would be more easily characterizable. = 如果数据更完整，结果就更容易描述。
If the situation were different, the response would be readily characterizable. = 如果情况不同，反应将很容易描述。


被动语态:
The data were characterizable by a clear trend. = 数据显示出明显的趋势。
The patient's condition was characterizable by several unusual symptoms. = 病人的情况表现为几种不寻常的症状。


疑问句:
Is this behaviour characterizable as normal? = 这种行为可以被认为是正常的吗？
Are the differences between the two groups characterizable by age? = 两组之间的差异是否可以用年龄来描述？


否定句:
The painting's style was not easily characterizable. = 这幅画的风格不容易描述。
The cause of the accident was not immediately characterizable. = 事故的原因无法立即确定。


PHRASE->
easily characterizable = 易于描述的
clearly characterizable = 清晰可辨的
uniquely characterizable = 独一无二的
difficult to characterize = 难以描述的
broadly characterizable = 广泛可描述的
