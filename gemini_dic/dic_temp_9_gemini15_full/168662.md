proconsul:/ˈproʊkɒnsəl/| n. |罗马行省总督，（殖民地）总督|A proconsul was a Roman governor of a province, especially one who held the office after his term as consul had ended.  They often governed provinces that were not considered as important as those governed by propraetors. |Example:  The proconsul ruled the province with an iron fist.  这位行省总督铁腕统治。
The proconsul oversaw the construction of new roads and aqueducts.这位行省总督负责监督新道路和渡槽的建设。|近义词：governor, administrator, prefect|反义词：subject, citizen
