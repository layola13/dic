judicable:/ˈdʒuːdɪkəbl/| adj.|可审判的；可判决的；可司法裁决的|Capable of being judged or decided in a court of law.  A judicable question is one that can be resolved by a court of law.|例句：The court determined that the matter was judicable. 法院认定此事可由其审判。

例句：The question of whether the government acted constitutionally is a judicable issue.  政府的行为是否符合宪法是一个可由法院裁决的问题。


近义词：justiciable, determinable, adjudicable|反义词：non-judicable, undeterminable


词源：源自拉丁语 *iudicābilis，由 *iūdex（法官）派生而来，后缀 -ābilis 表示“可…的”。  The word's history reflects its core meaning:  related to the ability of a court to make a judgment.

PHRASE->
There aren't common multi-word phrases directly using "judicable."  The word is used primarily in legal contexts and is generally used as a stand-alone adjective.  Instead of phrases, we often see "judicable question," "judicable issue," "judicable matter," etc.

judicable matter: 可司法裁决的事项
