consonancy:/ˈkɒnsənənsi/| n. |辅音和谐；和谐一致|The pleasing effect of the repetition or proximity of similar consonant sounds, particularly at the beginning of words.  It's a literary device used to create a sense of rhythm and euphony.  For example, the repetition of the "p" sound in "Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers" is a form of consonance.|The poem exhibits a remarkable consonance, enhancing its musicality. 这首诗展现了非凡的辅音和谐，增强了它的音乐性。|近义词：alliteration (though alliteration specifically refers to the repetition of initial consonant sounds), euphony, harmony|反义词：cacophony, dissonance
