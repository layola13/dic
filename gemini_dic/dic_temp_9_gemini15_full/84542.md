hamletism: /ˈhæmlɪtɪzəm/ | n. |哈姆雷特式性格；犹豫不决；优柔寡断 | The complex of emotions and actions characteristic of Hamlet, especially indecision and melancholy.  It often refers to a state of profound moral questioning and intellectual paralysis, often accompanied by procrastination and self-doubt. The term is frequently used to describe a character's or a situation's indecisiveness and brooding nature.  |例句：His hamletism prevented him from making a timely decision, leading to a missed opportunity.  他的优柔寡断使他错过了良机。


His prolonged hamletism regarding his career path left him feeling increasingly frustrated. 他在职业道路上的长期犹豫不决让他越来越沮丧。|近义词：indecisiveness, vacillation, procrastination, dilly-dallying |反义词： decisiveness, resoluteness, determination


词源:  The term derives from William Shakespeare's famous tragedy *Hamlet*, where the protagonist, Prince Hamlet, is famously indecisive and melancholic, grappling with moral dilemmas and delaying action.  The word is a relatively recent coinage, reflecting the enduring impact of Shakespeare's character on the cultural imagination.  The term's usage reflects the enduring fascination with Hamlet's psychological complexity and his struggle with inaction.
