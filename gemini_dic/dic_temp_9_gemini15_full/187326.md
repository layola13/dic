rockfall: /ˈrɒkfɔːl/ /ˈrɑːkfɔːl/ | n. |落石，山崩 | The sudden falling of rocks from a cliff or mountain.  A rockfall can be caused by various factors, including weathering, erosion, earthquakes, and human activity.  | 例句：The rockfall blocked the road.  落石阻塞了道路。

The rockfall injured several hikers. 落石使几名徒步旅行者受伤。| 近义词：landslide, rockslide, stoneslide | 反义词：None specifically.  (Antonyms would describe the opposite of the *event*, not the *noun* itself, e.g., stability, safety)
