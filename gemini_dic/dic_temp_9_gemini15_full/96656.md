industrialization: /ˌɪndʌstriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 工业化 | The process of developing industries in a country or region on a wide scale.  It involves the transformation of an economy from primarily agrarian to one dominated by manufacturing and industry.  This process often leads to significant social and environmental changes. | Example: The industrialization of Britain during the 18th and 19th centuries led to unprecedented economic growth but also significant social upheaval.  英国在18和19世纪的工业化导致了前所未有的经济增长，但也带来了重大的社会动荡。 | 近义词：industrial development, modernization | 反义词：deindustrialization, agrarianism | 名词
