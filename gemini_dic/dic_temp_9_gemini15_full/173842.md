quasi-promised:/ˈkwɑːzaɪˈprɒmɪst/ | adj. | 准许诺的，似已承诺的 |  A quasi-promise is a statement or action that strongly suggests a promise but lacks the full formality or commitment of a true promise. It implies a high likelihood of the promised action, but leaves some room for potential avoidance or non-fulfillment.  | Example:  He quasi-promised to help with the project, but I'm not entirely sure he'll follow through. 他似已承诺会帮忙这个项目，但我并不完全确定他会做到。|近义词: implied promise, virtual promise, near-promise | 反义词: explicit promise, firm commitment |
