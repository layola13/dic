cosmographically:/ˌkɒzməˈɡræfɪkli/ | adv. | 宇宙学地，宇宙论地 | In a manner relating to or characteristic of cosmography;  according to the principles of cosmography.  | Example: The universe was cosmographically mapped by the ancient astronomers.  古代天文学家对宇宙进行了宇宙学上的测绘。 | 近义词：cosmically, astronomically | 反义词：locally, terrestrially


USAGE->
This word is an adverb,  and therefore doesn't have verb conjugations like "is," "was," etc.  It describes *how* something is done or viewed.  Its usage depends on the context of the sentence.  Here are examples showcasing its use in different sentence structures:


Simple Sentence:
The ancient civilization viewed the world cosmographically.  古代文明以宇宙论的方式看待世界。


Complex Sentence:
Because they lacked advanced technology, their cosmographically-informed understanding of the universe was limited. 因为缺乏先进技术，他们基于宇宙学的宇宙观是有限的。


Compound Sentence:
The map depicted the constellations cosmographically; however, its accuracy was debatable. 这张地图以宇宙学的方式描绘了星座；然而，其准确性值得商榷。


PHRASE->
There aren't common phrases directly using "cosmographically," as it's a rather specialized adverb.  However,  phrases related to its meaning can be used:


from a cosmological perspective: 从宇宙学的角度来看
in a cosmic sense: 在宇宙意义上
according to astronomical principles: 根据天文原理
through a celestial lens: 通过天体的视角
with a universal viewpoint: 以宇宙的视角
