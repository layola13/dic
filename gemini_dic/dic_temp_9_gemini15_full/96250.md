/ɪnˈdɪkətɪvz/ | n. | 陈述语气；指示性词语 | Indicatives are verb forms that simply state a fact or opinion, without expressing mood or intention. They are the most common verb forms in a language.  They contrast with other moods like subjunctive or imperative. |  The dog barked loudly. (The sentence states a fact.)  I believe that the earth is round. (The sentence states an opinion.) | declarative sentences, assertions |  subjunctive, imperative | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He walks to school every day. = 他每天步行上学。
She plays the piano beautifully. = 她钢琴弹得真好。


一般过去时:
They went to the cinema last night. = 他们昨晚去看电影了。
He ate all the cookies. = 他把所有的饼干都吃光了。


现在完成时:
I have finished my homework. = 我已经完成了我的作业。
She has lived in London for five years. = 她已经在伦敦住了五年了。


过去完成时:
They had already left when I arrived. = 我到达的时候他们已经离开了。
She had finished her work before the deadline. = 她在截止日期前完成了工作。


将来时:
We will go to the beach tomorrow. = 我们明天要去海滩。
He will call you later. = 他稍后会给你打电话。


虚拟语气:
If I were rich, I would travel the world. = 如果我有钱，我会环游世界。(注意：虚拟语气中indicative的用法会根据上下文而变化，这个例句并非典型的indicative使用)
I wish I had more time. = 我希望我有更多的时间。(注意：虚拟语气中indicative的用法会根据上下文而变化，这个例句并非典型的indicative使用)


被动语态:
The car was stolen last night. = 汽车昨晚被偷了。
The cake is being baked now. = 蛋糕正在烘烤中。


疑问句:
Do you like coffee? = 你喜欢咖啡吗？ (疑问句本身不属于indicative的范畴，但疑问句可以使用indicative的动词形式)
Is she coming to the party? = 她会来参加派对吗？(疑问句本身不属于indicative的范畴，但疑问句可以使用indicative的动词形式)


否定句:
I don't like spinach. = 我不喜欢菠菜。
He didn't go to the party. = 他没有去参加派对。


PHRASE->
There is no common phrase specifically using "indicatives" as a noun.  The term is primarily used in grammatical discussions.  However,  phrases using indicative verbs are countless.  Examples below show the indicative mood in phrases:

It's raining cats and dogs = 倾盆大雨
He's been working hard = 他一直在努力工作
She's going to succeed = 她会成功的
