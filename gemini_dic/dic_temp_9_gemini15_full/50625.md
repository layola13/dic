derelicts:/ˈdɛrəlɪkts/| n. adj.|弃置物；被遗弃的人；破败的；颓废的|A derelict is something that has been abandoned and is in a state of disrepair.  It can refer to a building, a ship, or even a person who has become neglected and forgotten. As an adjective, it describes something abandoned and in a state of decay.|例句：The derelict ship was a hazard to navigation. 这艘废弃的船舶对航行构成危险。

The derelict building was eventually demolished.  那座破败的建筑最终被拆除了。


近义词：abandoned, neglected, ruined, dilapidated|反义词：maintained, cared-for, pristine, functional|词性：名词，形容词


词源：
The word "derelict" comes from the past participle of the Latin verb "dereliquere," meaning "to leave behind" or "to abandon."  The "de-" prefix means "away from," and the root "-relinquere" means "to leave." Therefore, the core meaning of "derelict" has always been related to abandonment.
