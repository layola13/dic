self-deprecatingly:/ˌsɛlfˈdɛprɪkeɪtɪŋli/| adv.|自谦地，自我贬低地|In a way that shows a lack of confidence or self-respect.  It often involves making humorous or modest comments about oneself.|例句：He self-deprecatingly admitted he wasn't the best player on the team. 他自谦地说自己不是队里最好的球员。

例句：She self-deprecatingly joked about her clumsy dance moves. 她自嘲地拿自己笨拙的舞姿开玩笑。


近义词：modestly, humbly, jokingly, ironically


反义词：boastfully, arrogantly, haughtily


词源:
* **self-**:  From Old English *self, *sylf, meaning "self, same."
* **deprecate**: From Latin *deprecārī, meaning "to pray against, avert by prayer, beg off."  The sense shifted to "express disapproval of" and then to "belittle, undervalue."
* **-ing**: Present participle suffix, forming an adverb.
* **-ly**: Adverbial suffix.
