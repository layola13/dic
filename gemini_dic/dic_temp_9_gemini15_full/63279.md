enthralment: /ɪnˈθrɔːlmənt/ | n. | 迷恋；着迷；吸引 | The state of being enthralled; captivating fascination or charm.  It suggests a complete absorption and intense interest, often accompanied by a sense of wonder or delight. |例句：The enthralment of the story kept me reading until dawn. 故事的魅力让我读到黎明。

The enthralment she felt for the charismatic leader was undeniable. 她对这位有魅力的领导人的迷恋是不可否认的。


近义词：captivation, fascination, enchantment, charm, allure

反义词：boredom, apathy, indifference


词源：源自动词enthral,  由en- (表示“使……”) + thrall (奴隶，束缚)构成。  最初含义是“使成为奴隶”，后来演变为“迷住，吸引”。
