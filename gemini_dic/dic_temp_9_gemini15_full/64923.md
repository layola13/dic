establishment:/ɪˈstæblɪʃmənt/ | n. | 建立；机构；当权派 | The act of establishing something, such as a business, system, or organization;  a well-known and respected institution or organization; the existing social and political system, often viewed with suspicion or disapproval by those outside it. |例句：The establishment of a new company requires careful planning.  建立一家新公司需要仔细的规划。 |例句：Harvard is a prestigious establishment.  哈佛大学是一所享有盛誉的机构。 |近义词：institution, organization, system, authorities |反义词：rebellion, revolution, overthrow


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government establishes new policies every year. = 政府每年都制定新的政策。
This company establishes a strong reputation in the market. = 这家公司在市场上建立了良好的声誉。


一般过去时:
They established a new branch office in London. = 他们在伦敦建立了一个新的分公司。
The treaty established peace between the two countries. = 该条约确立了两国之间的和平。


现在完成时:
They have established a successful business. = 他们已经建立了一家成功的企业。
The scientists have established a new theory. = 科学家们已经建立了一个新的理论。


过去完成时:
Before the war, they had established a strong economy. = 在战争爆发前，他们已经建立了一个强大的经济体系。
She had established her career before she got married. = 她结婚前已经建立了自己的事业。


将来时:
The company will establish a new factory next year. = 公司明年将建立一个新工厂。
They will establish a new research center. = 他们将建立一个新的研究中心。


虚拟语气:
If they had established better communication, the project would have been successful. = 如果他们建立了更好的沟通，项目就会成功。
I wish they had established clear guidelines. = 我希望他们当时已经制定了明确的指导方针。


被动语态:
The rules were established many years ago. = 这些规则是许多年前制定的。
The new system is being established gradually. = 新系统正在逐步建立。


疑问句:
When was the company established? = 这家公司是什么时候成立的？
Has the new policy been established yet? = 新政策已经制定了吗？


否定句:
They did not establish a clear timeline for the project. = 他们没有为项目制定明确的时间表。
The company has not yet established a presence in the Asian market. = 该公司尚未在亚洲市场建立业务。
