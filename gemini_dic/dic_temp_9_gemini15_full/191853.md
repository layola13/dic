sclerosis:/skləˈroʊsɪs/ | n. |硬化症，硬化；硬化作用 | A condition characterized by the hardening of body tissues, often due to the abnormal growth of fibrous tissue.  It can affect various parts of the body, including the brain, spinal cord, and arteries.  The term is often used in medical contexts to describe specific diseases. | Example: Multiple sclerosis (MS) is a chronic, autoimmune disease that affects the central nervous system. 多发性硬化症（MS）是一种影响中枢神经系统的慢性自身免疫性疾病。|近义词：hardening, induration |反义词：softening
