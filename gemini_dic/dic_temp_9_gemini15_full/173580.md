quasi-immortal: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ ɪˈmɔːrtəl/ | adj. | 近乎不朽的，几乎永生的 |  Nearly immortal; having a very long lifespan, exceeding normal expectations.  The term implies a lifespan significantly longer than that of a typical organism, but not truly immortal.  | Example:  Some scientists believe that certain species of jellyfish are quasi-immortal due to their ability to revert to a polyp stage.  某些科学家认为某些水母物种由于其恢复到息肉阶段的能力而近乎不朽。 |近义词: virtually immortal, nearly immortal, exceptionally long-lived | 反义词: mortal, short-lived | 词性: adjective
