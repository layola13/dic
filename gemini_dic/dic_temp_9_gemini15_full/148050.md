overrunning: /ˌoʊvərˈrʌnɪŋ/ | adj., v. | 泛滥的；超过；淹没；压倒 |  exceeding a limit or boundary; spreading uncontrollably; overwhelming. | 近义词： overflowing, inundating, overwhelming, exceeding | 反义词：underflowing, insufficient


英文解析：

The word "overrunning" can function as both an adjective and a verb.  As an adjective, it describes something that has exceeded its limits or boundaries, often implying a negative consequence, such as overcrowding or an excessive amount. As a verb, it means to spread beyond the intended limits, to exceed a limit, or to overwhelm.

As an adjective:

The overrunning weeds choked the garden.  = 泛滥的杂草扼杀了花园。

The overrunning budget forced the project to be cancelled. = 超支的预算迫使项目取消。


As a verb:

The river overran its banks after the heavy rain. = 大雨过后，河水泛滥成灾。

The army overran the enemy's position. = 军队攻占了敌人的阵地。

词源:

The word "overrunning" is derived from the verb "overrun," which is formed from the prefix "over-" (meaning "above," "beyond," or "excessively") and the verb "run," which in this context means to spread, extend, or exceed a limit.  The "-ing" suffix indicates the present participle or gerund form, functioning as an adjective or noun.
