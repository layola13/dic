non-Semitic: /ˌnɒn sɪˈmɪtɪk/ | adj. | 非闪米特的 | Not belonging to or characteristic of the Semitic peoples or their languages.  This term refers to languages and cultures not originating from the Semitic language family, which includes languages like Arabic, Hebrew, and Akkadian.  It's often used in linguistic and anthropological contexts. | Example:  Many non-Semitic languages are spoken in Europe.  许多欧洲语言是非闪米特语系语言。|近义词: non-Hebrew, non-Arabic (depending on context) | 反义词: Semitic | 词性: adjective
