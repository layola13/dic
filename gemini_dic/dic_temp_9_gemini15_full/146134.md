overemploying: /ˌoʊvərɪmˈplɔɪɪŋ/ | v. | 过度雇佣；雇用过多的人 |  The act of employing more people than are needed or productive. This can lead to inefficiencies and increased costs.  |  例句：The company is overemploying, leading to decreased productivity per employee. 公司过度雇佣，导致每位员工的生产力下降。 The project manager admitted to overemploying on the initial stages. 项目经理承认在初期阶段雇佣了过多的人员。| 近义词：overstaffing, overmanning | 反义词：understaffing, undermanning | 动词

The company overemploys workers during peak seasons. = 公司在旺季过度雇佣员工。
They are overemploying in the marketing department. = 他们在市场部雇佣了过多的人员。

The factory overemployed last year, resulting in losses. = 工厂去年过度雇佣，导致亏损。
