laryngealized:/ˌlærɪnˈdʒaɪzd/| adj.|带声门化的，声门化的|  Describes a sound produced with simultaneous vocal fold vibration and constriction in the larynx, resulting in a characteristic creaky or breathy quality.  This is often a feature of some languages' phonemes. | Example:  The singer produced a laryngealized vowel sound. 歌手发出了一个声门化元音。| 近义词：creaky, breathy| 反义词：clear, resonant
