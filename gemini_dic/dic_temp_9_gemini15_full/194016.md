self-farming:/selfˈfɑːrmɪŋ/| adj.| 自耕的；自给自足的农业 |Self-farming refers to agricultural practices where a farmer cultivates and manages their own land and resources without significant external inputs or reliance on external markets.  This often involves growing a wide variety of crops to ensure a diverse food supply and reducing reliance on purchased inputs. | Example: The community adopted self-farming techniques to increase food security and reduce their reliance on outside suppliers.  The self-farming system proved successful in providing enough food for the entire village.|近义词：subsistence farming, independent farming|反义词：industrial farming, commercial farming
