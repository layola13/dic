metaphor:/ˈmɛtəfɔːr/| n.|隐喻，比喻|A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable.  It involves an implicit comparison between two unlike things, highlighting their shared qualities.|Example:  "The world is a stage." (The world is compared to a stage to emphasize its transient and performative nature.)|近义词：simile, analogy, allegory|反义词：literal meaning
