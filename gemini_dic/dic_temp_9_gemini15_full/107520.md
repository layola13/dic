landed:/ˈlændɪd/| v. |着陆；降落；获得；使…着陆|To arrive on the ground after flying or falling.  To obtain or achieve something successfully.|例句：The plane landed safely. 飞机安全着陆了。

The job landed him a huge salary. 这份工作让他获得了巨额薪水。|近义词：arrived, touched down, secured, obtained|反义词：took off, departed, failed, missed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The plane lands at 3 pm every day. = 飞机每天下午3点降落。
He lands a new client every week. = 他每周都能争取到一个新客户。


一般过去时:
The bird landed gently on the branch. = 鸟儿轻轻地落在树枝上。
She landed the leading role in the play. = 她获得了该剧的主要角色。


现在完成时:
The pilot has landed the plane safely. = 飞行员已安全着陆。
He has landed several major contracts this year. = 他今年已经签订了几份重要合同。


过去完成时:
The plane had landed before the rescue team arrived. = 在救援队到达之前，飞机已经着陆了。
She had landed the job before she even knew the salary. = 她甚至在知道薪水之前就已经得到了这份工作。


将来时:
The spaceship will land on Mars next year. = 宇宙飞船将于明年登陆火星。
I think I will land a good job soon. = 我想我很快就会找到一份好工作。


虚拟语气:
If the plane had landed smoothly, we would have been on time. = 如果飞机平稳着陆，我们就会准时到达。
If she had landed the part, she would have been very happy. = 如果她得到了那个角色，她会非常高兴。


被动语态:
The plane was landed safely by the pilot. = 飞机被飞行员安全着陆。
The contract was landed by the sales team. = 合同是由销售团队争取到的。


疑问句:
Where did the plane land? = 飞机降落在哪里？
Did he land the job? = 他得到那份工作了吗？


否定句:
The plane didn't land on time. = 飞机没有准时着陆。
He didn't land the contract. = 他没有拿到合同。
