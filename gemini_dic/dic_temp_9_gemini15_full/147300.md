overmodifies:/ˌoʊvərˈmɑːdɪfaɪz/ | v. |过度修饰；矫枉过正|To modify excessively or to an inappropriate degree, often resulting in a negative effect.  It implies that the modifications obscure the original meaning or purpose, making it less effective or even counterproductive. |例句：The editor overmodified the manuscript, losing its original voice and impact. 编辑过度修改了稿件，使其失去了原有的风格和影响力。 The writer overmodified his prose, making it overly complex and convoluted. 作者过度修饰了他的散文，使其过于复杂和冗长。|近义词：overdecorate, overelaborate, embellish excessively, gild the lily|反义词：understate, simplify, streamline
