galliass:/ˈɡælɪæs/| n.|大帆船|A large sailing vessel of the 16th-18th centuries, typically having three or more masts and a high poop deck.  Galliasses were used for both war and commerce, and were known for their size and firepower (in the case of warships).|The Spanish Armada included numerous galliasses among its fleet.|近义词：galleon, carrack|反义词：无
