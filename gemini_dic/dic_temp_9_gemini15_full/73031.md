footglove: /ˈfʊtˌɡlʌv/ | n. | 脚套，脚趾套 | A footglove is a type of covering for the foot, similar to a glove for the hand, often used to protect the foot from cold, injury, or infection.  It can be made of various materials depending on its intended use. | Example:  The surgeon wore a sterile footglove during the operation.  外科医生在手术过程中戴着无菌脚套。  | 近义词: foot covering, sock (for some types), bootie (for some types) | 反义词:  None specifically, but the antonym would relate to its function, such as bare foot.
