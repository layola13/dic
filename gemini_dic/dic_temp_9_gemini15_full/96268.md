indictees:/ɪnˈdaɪktiːz/| n. |被起诉者，被告 |Those who have been formally accused of a crime and are awaiting trial.  The indictees pleaded not guilty. 被起诉者对指控表示不认罪。|近义词：defendants, accused|反义词：plaintiffs


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The grand jury indicts several individuals each year.  = 大陪审团每年都会起诉几个人。
Many indictees choose to accept plea bargains. = 许多被告选择接受认罪协议。


一般过去时:
The court indicted the suspect last week. = 法院上周对嫌疑人提出了起诉。
Several individuals were indicted for fraud. = 数人因欺诈被起诉。


现在完成时:
The prosecutor has indicted numerous individuals in this case. = 检察官在本案中已起诉了多人。
The judge has already indicted the defendant. = 法官已经对被告提起诉讼。


过去完成时:
The police had already indicted the criminal before the trial. = 在审判之前，警方已经对罪犯提起诉讼。
The lawyer had indicted the witness for perjury. = 律师已控告证人作伪证。


将来时:
The grand jury will indict more individuals next month. = 大陪审团下个月将对更多人提起诉讼。
They believe the court will indict the politician soon. = 他们相信法院很快就会对这位政治家提起诉讼。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, the judge would indict him immediately. = 如果证据更充分，法官会立即对他提起诉讼。
I wish the prosecutor would indict the corrupt official. = 我希望检察官能对这位腐败官员提起诉讼。


被动语态:
He was indicted on charges of bribery. = 他被指控犯有贿赂罪。
The case was indicted in the county court. = 此案在县法院受理。


疑问句:
Were any individuals indicted in the investigation? = 调查中是否有任何人被起诉？
Has the defendant been indicted yet? = 被告是否已被起诉？


否定句:
The suspect was not indicted due to lack of evidence. = 由于缺乏证据，嫌疑人未被起诉。
The jury decided not to indict the accused. = 陪审团决定不起诉被告。
