defaulters:/dɪˈfɔːltərz/ | n. | 拖欠债务者，违约者 | A defaulter is someone who fails to fulfill an obligation, especially a financial one, such as failing to repay a loan or meet a payment deadline.  They are characterized by their failure to meet their commitments. | The bank is taking legal action against its defaulters.  这家银行正在对拖欠债务者采取法律行动。  Many defaulters faced severe financial consequences. 许多拖欠债务者面临严重的财务后果。 |  debtors, delinquents |  payers,  prompt payers | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He defaults on his loan payments every month. = 他每个月都拖欠贷款。
They default on their credit card bills regularly. = 他们经常拖欠信用卡账单。

一般过去时:
The company defaulted on its bond obligations last year. = 该公司去年违反了其债券义务。
She defaulted on her mortgage payments, leading to foreclosure. = 她拖欠抵押贷款，导致房屋被收回。

现在完成时:
He has defaulted on his taxes for three years. = 他已经拖欠税款三年了。
The government has defaulted on its debt several times in history. = 政府历史上曾多次违约。

过去完成时:
The business had defaulted on its loan before the economic downturn. = 在经济衰退之前，这家企业就已经拖欠贷款了。
They had defaulted on their rent several times, resulting in eviction. = 他们已经几次拖欠房租，导致被驱逐。

将来时:
If he continues to miss payments, he will default on his loan. = 如果他继续错过付款，他将拖欠贷款。
The company is likely to default on its debt if interest rates rise further. = 如果利率进一步上升，该公司可能会违约。

虚拟语气:
If I had paid my bills on time, I wouldn't have defaulted on my loan. = 如果我按时支付账单，我就不会拖欠贷款了。
It's a shame he defaulted; if he had been more careful, things would have been different. = 他违约真是可惜；如果他更谨慎一些，情况就会不一样了。

被动语态:
The loan was defaulted on by several individuals. = 这笔贷款被几个人拖欠了。
The debt was defaulted on by the struggling company. = 这笔债务被这家挣扎的企业拖欠了。

疑问句:
Has the company defaulted on its obligations? = 这家公司是否违反了其义务？
Did he default on his credit card payments again? = 他是否再次拖欠信用卡付款？

否定句:
He did not default on his loan. = 他没有拖欠贷款。
The company has not defaulted on its payments this year. = 该公司今年没有拖欠付款。
