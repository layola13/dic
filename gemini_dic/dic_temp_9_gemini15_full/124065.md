morphologization:/ˌmɔːrfəˈlɒdʒɪzeɪʃən/| n. |形态化；形态学研究|The process by which a language develops its morphological system, or the study of this process.  It refers to the changes in word formation and structure within a language over time. This can involve the addition, loss, or alteration of morphemes (the smallest units of meaning in a language).|Example: The morphologization of English verbs over the past millennium has resulted in a simplification of inflectional morphology.  英语动词在过去千年中的形态化导致了屈折形态学的简化。|近义词：morphological development, grammaticalization|反义词：无
