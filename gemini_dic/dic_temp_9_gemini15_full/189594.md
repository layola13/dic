salient:/ˈseɪliənt/| adj.|显著的；突出的；重要的| Prominent or conspicuous; of notable significance.  The salient points of the argument were easily understood.  |The salient features of the landscape were the towering mountains and deep valleys.  = 这片景观最显著的特征是高耸的山脉和深邃的山谷。|prominent, important, significant, conspicuous|insignificant, unimportant, obscure|形容词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The salient points are clear. = 要点很清楚。
His salient features are his intelligence and humour. = 他的显著特点是聪明和幽默。


一般过去时:
The salient features of the report were overlooked. = 报告中的要点被忽视了。
The salient point of his argument was easily missed. = 他论点中的要点很容易被忽略。


现在完成时:
The salient facts have been presented. = 重要的事实已经被呈现出来了。
Several salient aspects have emerged from the study. = 这项研究中出现了一些显著的方面。


过去完成时:
The salient details had already been discussed. = 重要的细节已经被讨论过了。
By then, the salient differences had become apparent. = 到那时，显著的差异已经显而易见了。


将来时:
The salient issues will be addressed in the next meeting. = 重要的问题将在下一次会议上讨论。
His salient contribution will surely be remembered. = 他突出的贡献一定会被记住。


虚拟语气:
If the salient points were clearer, more people would understand. = 如果要点更清楚，更多的人就会理解。
I wish the salient features of the plan were more obvious. = 我希望计划中的显著特点更明显一些。


被动语态:
The salient features were highlighted in the presentation. = 这些显著的特征在演示文稿中得到了强调。
The salient points were carefully considered by the committee. = 委员会仔细考虑了要点。


疑问句:

否定句:
The report lacked salient details. = 报告缺乏重要的细节。
The plan failed to highlight the salient features. = 该计划未能突出重点。
