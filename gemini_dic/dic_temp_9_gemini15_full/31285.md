chaperonage:/ˈʃæpərəʊnɑːʒ/| n.|监护；陪伴；保护；|The act of chaperoning; the supervision or guardianship of someone, especially a young woman.  It often implies protection from impropriety or harm.|例句：The chaperonage provided by her aunt ensured a proper social debut.|近义词：supervision, guardianship, escort, protection|反义词：neglect, abandonment

The school provides chaperonage for all field trips. = 学校为所有实地考察提供监护。
Her parents insist on chaperonage for all her social events. = 她父母坚持对她所有的社交活动进行监护。


一般过去时:
Strict chaperonage was common in Victorian society. = 维多利亚时代的社会普遍实行严格的监护。
The chaperonage ensured the young ladies' safety. = 监护确保了年轻女士的安全。
