mid:/mɪd/| adj.|中间的，中部的| situated in the middle; average or intermediate.|The meeting is scheduled for mid-afternoon. 会议安排在下午中间时间。The mid-range price is acceptable. 中等价位是可以接受的。|近义词：central, medial, average|反义词：outer, peripheral, extreme


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ship is in mid-ocean.  = 船在中途洋面。
The test is mid-process. = 测试进行中。


一般过去时:
The plane crashed mid-flight. = 飞机在飞行途中坠毁。
The meeting ended mid-discussion. = 会议在讨论中途结束。


现在完成时:
They've been working on the project mid-summer. = 他们从仲夏就开始做这个项目了。
We have lived in this mid-size city for five years. = 我们在这个中等城市的居住了五年。


过去完成时:
He had arrived mid-morning. = 他在上午到达。
The negotiations had stalled mid-conversation. = 谈判在谈话中途陷入僵局。


将来时:
The concert will begin mid-evening. = 音乐会将在傍晚开始。
We will meet mid-week next week. = 我们下周会在周中见面。


虚拟语气:
If I were in mid-career, I would consider a change. = 如果我正处于职业生涯的中间阶段，我会考虑换工作。
I wish I were mid-way to my goal. = 我希望我已经完成了目标的一半。


被动语态:
The bridge was damaged mid-construction. = 桥在施工过程中受损。
The decision was made mid-debate. = 决定是在辩论中途做出的。


疑问句:
Is it mid-day already? = 已经是中午了吗？
Are we mid-way through the journey? = 我们旅程过半了吗？


否定句:
It's not mid-term yet. = 现在还不是期中考试。
The project is not mid-completion. = 项目尚未完成一半。
