ran-down: /ˌrænˈdaʊn/ | adj. | 破旧的；衰败的；疲惫不堪的 |

英文解析:  Describes something that is in a state of disrepair, decay, or exhaustion.  It can refer to physical objects (buildings, cars) or people.

例句:

The old house was completely ran-down. = 那栋老房子完全破败不堪了。

He looked completely ran-down after working all night. = 他通宵工作后看起来非常疲惫。


近义词: dilapidated, derelict, shabby, worn-out, exhausted, fatigued

反义词:  well-maintained, renovated, pristine, spick-and-span, energetic, vigorous


词性: 形容词
