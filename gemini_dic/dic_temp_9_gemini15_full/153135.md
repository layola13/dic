pelagius:/pəˈleɪdʒəs/| n. |伯拉纠|A British monk and theologian of the late 4th and early 5th centuries CE, whose teachings on free will and grace were condemned as heresy by the Catholic Church.|Pelagius argued that humans have free will and are not inherently sinful, a view that contradicted the prevailing Augustinian doctrine of original sin. His ideas sparked a major theological debate that shaped Christian thought for centuries.|例句：The Pelagian controversy profoundly impacted the development of Christian theology.  伯拉纠争议深刻影响了基督教神学的發展。|近义词：theologian, monk |反义词：N/A
