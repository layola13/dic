monophthongization: /ˌmɒnəˈfθɒŋɡaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | /ˌmɑːnəˈfθɒŋɡaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 单元音化 | The process by which a diphthong or a sequence of vowels becomes a monophthong.  This often happens through sound change over time in a language.  For example, the pronunciation of "fire" in some dialects has shifted from a diphthong to a monophthong. | The Great Vowel Shift in English involved monophthongization of some long vowels.  The pronunciation of "name" used to be a diphthong, but in modern English it's a monophthong in most dialects. | 近义词: vowel reduction, simplification | 反义词: diphthongization
