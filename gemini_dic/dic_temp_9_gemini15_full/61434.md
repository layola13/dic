embolus:/ˈembələs/| n. |栓子，血栓|A mass of solid, liquid, or gaseous material that travels through the bloodstream and lodges in a blood vessel, obstructing the flow of blood.|The blood clot acted as an embolus, blocking the artery and causing a stroke.|近义词：thrombus (if formed in situ), clot|反义词：None specifically, but terms like "clear passage" or "unobstructed flow" describe the opposite condition.


USAGE->

一般现在时:
An embolus blocks the artery. = 栓子阻塞了动脉。
The embolus travels through the bloodstream. = 栓子在血流中移动。

一般过去时:
A large embolus caused the heart attack. = 一个大的栓子导致了心脏病发作。
The doctor removed the embolus. = 医生清除了栓子。

现在完成时:
Several emboli have been found in his lungs. = 在他的肺部发现了几个栓子。
She has experienced several embolisms. = 她经历过几次栓塞。

过去完成时:
The patient had already experienced an embolus before the surgery. = 病人在手术前就已经经历过一次栓塞了。
The blockage had been caused by a traveling embolus. = 阻塞是由移动的栓子引起的。

将来时:
The surgeon will remove the embolus during the operation. = 外科医生将在手术中取出栓子。
A blood clot could potentially form an embolus in the future. = 血凝块将来可能会形成栓子。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:
