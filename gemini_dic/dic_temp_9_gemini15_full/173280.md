quasi-continuous:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ kənˈtɪnjuəs/| adj.|准连续的，近似连续的 |Approaching or resembling continuity; nearly continuous but not perfectly so.  It often describes a process or signal that is not strictly continuous but exhibits properties similar to a continuous one, such as a smooth variation over time.
