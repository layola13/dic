enthrones:/ɪnˈθroʊnz/| vt.|加冕；使登基；拥戴|To place or establish someone in a position of power or authority, especially as a monarch or a king or queen.|The king was enthroned in a magnificent ceremony. 国王在盛大的仪式上加冕。|近义词：crown, install, invest|反义词：dethrone, depose

一般现在时:
He enthrones a new king every year. 他每年都拥戴一位新国王。
The church enthrones its new bishop this Sunday. 教堂本周日将为新任主教举行就职典礼。


一般过去时:
They enthroned him as the new emperor. 他们拥戴他为新皇帝。
The ancient ritual enthroned the chosen leader.  古老的仪式使被选中的人登上了领导者的地位。


现在完成时:
The council has enthroned a new leader after much debate. 理事会经过长时间的辩论后拥戴了一位新的领导者。
They have enthroned him as the head of the company. 他们已拥戴他为公司负责人。


过去完成时:

The people had enthroned him as their savior. 人民将他拥戴为救世主。

虚拟语气:
If they had enthroned a wiser leader, the country would be more prosperous. 如果他们拥戴一位更明智的领导者，国家将会更加繁荣。
