preshadowed: /prɪˈʃædəʊd/ | adj. | 预示的，预兆的 |  To foreshadow is to indicate or suggest beforehand; to be a sign or warning of something that will happen later.  It implies a subtle or indirect indication, often one that is not immediately apparent. |


例句：

The dark clouds preshadowed the coming storm.  =  乌云预示着即将到来的暴风雨。

His nervous demeanor preshadowed the bad news he was about to deliver. = 他紧张的神态预示着他即将带来的坏消息。


近义词：foreshadowed, foreshadowing, prefigured, portended, augured


反义词：followed, succeeded


词性：形容词
