eavesdroppers:/ˈiːvzdrɒpərz/ | n. |窃听者，偷听者 | People who secretly listen to other people's conversations.  |The eavesdroppers hid behind the bushes, hoping to overhear the couple's secret. 窃听者躲在灌木丛后面，希望能偷听到这对夫妇的秘密。| listeners, spies |  speakers, announcers | 名词

Eavesdroppers often hide in shadows.  = 窃听者经常躲在阴影中。
They eavesdrop on their neighbors' conversations. = 他们偷听邻居的谈话。


一般过去时:
The police caught the eavesdroppers red-handed. = 警方当场抓获了窃听者。
He eavesdropped on their phone call last night. = 他昨晚偷听了他们的电话。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

被动语态:

词源:
The word "eavesdropper" is derived from "eaves," referring to the overhanging lower edge of a roof, and "drop," suggesting the act of dropping or letting something fall.  Originally, eavesdroppers would hide under the eaves of a house to overhear conversations within.
