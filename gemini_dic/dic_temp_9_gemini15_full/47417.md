deadlock:/ˈdedlɒk/ /ˈdedlɑːk/| n. v.|僵局；死锁；停滞 | A situation where no progress is possible, especially because of disagreement; a standstill.  |例句：Negotiations reached a deadlock.  谈判陷入了僵局。
The two sides are in a deadlock over the issue. 双方在这个问题上僵持不下。

近义词：impasse, stalemate, standstill, gridlock | 反义词：resolution, breakthrough, agreement | 词性: n. v.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The traffic deadlocks every afternoon. = 每一下午交通都会堵塞。
This project is deadlocking because of the budget issue. = 因为预算问题，这个项目陷入了僵局。

一般过去时:
The meeting deadlocked after hours of debate. = 会议经过数小时的辩论后陷入僵局。
The negotiations deadlocked, leaving both sides frustrated. = 谈判陷入僵局，使双方都感到沮丧。

现在完成时:
The talks have deadlocked, and a solution seems unlikely. = 会谈已经陷入僵局，解决方案似乎不太可能。
The committee has deadlocked on the proposed amendments. = 委员会在拟议的修正案上陷入了僵局。

过去完成时:
They had deadlocked on the previous point before moving on. = 在继续讨论之前，他们在前一点上已经僵持不下。
The project had deadlocked for months before a compromise was reached. = 在达成妥协之前，该项目已经僵持了数月。

将来时:

I fear the negotiations will deadlock without external mediation. = 我担心如果没有外部调解，谈判将会陷入僵局。

虚拟语气:

If they had been more flexible, they wouldn't have deadlocked. = 如果他们更灵活一些，他们就不会陷入僵局了。

疑问句:

否定句:
