schematism:/ˈskiːmətɪzəm/| n. | 模式化；图式；纲要；计划 | A schematism is a systematic arrangement or classification of ideas or concepts, often based on a particular principle or framework. It can refer to a formal system of representation or a simplified model of a complex process.  In philosophy, it often relates to Kant's concept of how our minds structure experience. | Example:  His approach to the problem was characterized by a rigid schematism, leading to an inflexible solution.  他的解决问题的方法具有僵化的模式化特征，导致了一个死板的解决方案。 |近义词:system, framework, model, plan, outline |反义词:randomness, chaos, disorder |名词
