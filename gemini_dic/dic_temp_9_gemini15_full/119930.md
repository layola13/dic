milkmaid:/ˈmɪlkeɪd/| n. |挤奶女工，牧羊女|A milkmaid is a woman who works on a farm, milking cows and doing other related tasks.  The term often evokes a romanticized image of rural life.|例句：The milkmaid hummed a cheerful tune as she carried the pails of fresh milk.  挤奶女工一边哼着愉快的曲子，一边提着装着鲜奶的桶。

The milkmaid's rosy cheeks suggested a life of healthy outdoor work.  挤奶女工红润的脸颊暗示着健康户外工作的生涯。|近义词：dairymaid, farmhand|反义词：None specifically;  related concepts like "city dweller" could be considered contrasting.

词源:
The word "milkmaid" is a compound word formed from "milk" and "maid."  "Milk" derives from Old English *meoluc,  and "maid" comes from Old English *mægden, meaning a young woman servant.  Therefore, "milkmaid" literally refers to a young woman whose job is related to milk (i.e., milking cows).
