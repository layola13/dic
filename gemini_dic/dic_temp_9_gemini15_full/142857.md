out-groups:/ˈaʊtɡruːps/| n. |外群体，非我族类|A social group toward which an individual feels a sense of opposition, rivalry, or competition.  Members of an in-group tend to perceive out-groups as being different or inferior.  The concept of out-groups is central to social identity theory. |例句：The study explored the biases that individuals hold against out-groups. 这项研究探讨了个人对外部群体的偏见。

例句：He felt a sense of alienation from the out-groups in his new community. 他感觉和新社区的外部群体疏远。


近义词：outsiders, others, non-members, competing groups|反义词：in-groups, insiders, own group|名词
