cringes:/krɪndʒ/| v.|畏缩；退缩；感到尴尬|To make a grimace or shrinking movement, typically in response to something unpleasant or embarrassing.  It can also refer to the feeling of embarrassment or discomfort itself.|例句：He cringed at the sound of his own voice. 他听到自己的声音感到很尴尬。|例句：She cringed when she saw the awful painting. 她看到那幅糟糕的画时感到畏缩。|近义词：shrink, recoil, flinch, wince|反义词：enjoy, relish


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He cringes at loud noises. = 他害怕大声的噪音。
She cringes every time she sees that video. = 每次看到那个视频她都感到很尴尬。

一般过去时:
I cringed when I heard his joke. = 听到他的笑话我感到很尴尬。
They cringed at the sight of the accident. = 他们看到事故现场感到畏缩。

现在完成时:
I have cringed so many times today. = 我今天已经无数次感到尴尬了。
She has cringed at his behaviour for years. = 多年来，她一直对他行为感到尴尬。

过去完成时:
I had cringed before he even started speaking. = 他还没开始说话我就已经感到尴尬了。
She had cringed many times before she finally spoke up. = 在她最终开口之前，她已经无数次感到尴尬了。

将来时:
I will cringe when I see her again. = 我再次见到她时会感到尴尬。
He will cringe if he sees that photo. = 如果他看到那张照片，他会感到尴尬。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would cringe at the thought. = 如果我是你，我会对这个想法感到尴尬。
I wish I hadn't said that; I'm cringing now. = 我真希望我没有说过那些话；我现在感到很尴尬。

被动语态:  (The verb "cringe" is rarely used passively.)

疑问句:
Does he cringe at the thought of public speaking? = 他想到公开演讲会感到尴尬吗？
Will she cringe when she hears the news? = 她听到这个消息会感到尴尬吗？

否定句:
He didn't cringe at the criticism. = 他对批评并不感到尴尬。
She doesn't cringe easily. = 她不容易感到尴尬。
