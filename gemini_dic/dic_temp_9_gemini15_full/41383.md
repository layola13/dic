cormophytes:/ˈkɔːrməfaɪts/| n. |具根植物；有根植物|Plants with a corm, a short, thickened underground stem that serves as a storage organ. Cormophytes are characterized by their well-developed root systems, stems, and leaves. They represent the vast majority of land plants, excluding non-vascular plants like mosses and liverworts.  |Example:  Many flowering plants are cormophytes, relying on their corms for survival during unfavorable conditions. 许多开花植物都是具根植物，依靠它们的块茎在不利条件下生存。|近义词：vascular plants, higher plants|反义词：bryophytes (non-vascular plants)
