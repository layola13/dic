reestablishing:/ˌriːɪˈstæblɪʃɪŋ/| v. |重建，重新建立，恢复|The act of establishing something again after it has been lost, damaged, or destroyed.  It implies a return to a previous state or condition.|例句：The government is reestablishing diplomatic relations with the neighboring country. 政府正在重建与邻国的外交关系。

例句：After the storm, they focused on reestablishing communication networks.  暴风雨过后，他们专注于重建通信网络。


近义词：restoring, renewing, reinstating, reviving

反义词：destroying, dismantling, abolishing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are reestablishing their old business partnership. = 他们正在重建他们过去的商业伙伴关系。
The company is reestablishing its market position. = 公司正在重建其市场地位。

一般过去时:
They reestablished the old family tradition. = 他们恢复了古老的家族传统。
The team reestablished its dominance in the league. = 这支球队重新确立了在联盟中的统治地位。

现在完成时:
We have reestablished contact with our lost friends. = 我们已经重新联系上了失散的朋友。
The organization has reestablished its credibility. = 该组织已经恢复了其信誉。

过去完成时:
They had reestablished the bridge before the flood came. = 洪水到来之前，他们已经重建了桥梁。
The country had reestablished its independence after many years of struggle. =  经过多年的斗争，这个国家已经恢复了独立。

将来时:
We will reestablish the old garden next spring. = 我们将在明年春天重建老花园。
The government will reestablish order in the city. = 政府将恢复城市的秩序。

虚拟语气:
If we had more resources, we could reestablish the program. = 如果我们有更多资源，我们就能恢复这个项目。
I wish we could reestablish contact with him. = 我希望我们能够重新和他取得联系。

被动语态:
The old building is being reestablished as a museum. = 这座旧建筑正在被改建成博物馆。
The relationship was reestablished by a sincere apology. =  真诚的道歉重建了关系。

疑问句:
Are they reestablishing the old factory? = 他们正在重建旧工厂吗？
Is the government reestablishing the old laws? = 政府正在恢复旧法律吗？

否定句:
They did not reestablish the business after the fire. = 大火之后，他们没有重建业务。
The company failed to reestablish its market share. = 公司未能恢复其市场份额。
