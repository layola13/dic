relativism: /ˈrɛlətɪvɪzəm/ | n. |相对主义|The philosophical view that truth and morality are relative to the individual, culture, or historical period, and not absolute.  It emphasizes the context-dependent nature of knowledge, values, and beliefs.  Relativism rejects universal standards and claims that what is considered true or right varies across different perspectives. |Example: Cultural relativism suggests that judging other cultures based on one's own values is inappropriate.  文化相对主义认为根据自身价值观来评判其他文化是不合适的。

Example:  Moral relativism argues there are no universally accepted moral principles. 道德相对主义认为没有普遍接受的道德原则。

近义词: subjectivism, contextualism |反义词: absolutism, objectivism
