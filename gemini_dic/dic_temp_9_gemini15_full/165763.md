prentice:/ˈprɛntɪs/| n.|学徒，新手 |A person who is learning a trade or craft by working under a skilled person.|例句：He served his apprenticeship as a carpenter. 他当了木匠学徒。 He is still a prentice in the art of diplomacy. 他在外交艺术上仍然是个新手。 |近义词：apprentice, novice, beginner |反义词：master, expert


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a prentice carpenter. = 他是一个木匠学徒。
She is a prentice teacher. = 她是一位实习教师。


一般过去时:
He was a prentice blacksmith for five years. = 他当了五年铁匠学徒。
She was a prentice lawyer before joining the firm. = 加入公司之前，她是一名实习律师。


现在完成时:
She has been a prentice chef for two years. = 她已经当了两年学徒厨师。
He has been a prentice writer for a decade. = 他已经当了十年的新手作家。


过去完成时:
He had been a prentice baker before opening his own shop. = 在开店之前，他做过面包师学徒。
She had been a prentice journalist for several years before she got her first job. = 在她得到第一份工作之前，她已经做了几年的实习记者。


将来时:
He will be a prentice electrician next year. = 他明年将成为一名电工学徒。
She will be a prentice programmer after graduation. = 毕业后她将成为一名实习程序员。


虚拟语气:
If he were a prentice, he would learn more quickly. = 如果他是学徒，他会学得更快。
If she had been a prentice, she would have gained more experience. = 如果她是学徒，她就会获得更多经验。


被动语态:
He was chosen as a prentice by the master craftsman. = 他被大师选为学徒。
She was accepted as a prentice at the design firm. = 她被设计公司录用为学徒。


疑问句:
Is he a prentice or a journeyman? = 他是学徒还是熟练工人？
Was she a prentice when she made that mistake? = 她犯那个错误的时候是学徒吗？


否定句:
He is not a prentice; he is a master craftsman. = 他不是学徒，他是一位技艺精湛的工匠。
She was not a prentice; she had already completed her training. = 她不是学徒；她已经完成了培训。
