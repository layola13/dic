positing:/ˈpɒzɪtɪŋ/ /ˈpɑːzɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 假设；提出；断定 | The act of putting forward an idea or theory; suggesting something as a basis for argument or action.  |  例句：Scientists are positing a new theory about the origin of the universe.  科学家们正在提出关于宇宙起源的新理论。

例句：He was positing a connection between poverty and crime. 他正在假设贫困与犯罪之间的联系。


近义词：suggesting, proposing, hypothesizing, assuming, advancing

反义词：refuting, denying, disproving


词源：源自拉丁语"ponere"，意为“放置，安置”。  通过古法语"poser"演变而来，最终发展成现代英语"posit"，再加"-ing"构成现在分词形式"positing"。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is positing a new solution to the problem. = 他正在提出解决这个问题的新方案。
They are positing a link between diet and health. = 他们正在假设饮食与健康之间的联系。


一般过去时:
She posited a compelling argument. = 她提出了一个令人信服的论点。
The researchers posited a new hypothesis. = 研究人员提出了一种新的假设。


现在完成时:
Scientists have posited several theories about black holes. = 科学家们已经提出了关于黑洞的几种理论。
They have posited a strong case for reform. = 他们已经为改革提出了强有力的理由。


过去完成时:
He had posited a different explanation before the new evidence emerged. = 在新的证据出现之前，他提出了不同的解释。
They had posited the existence of a new particle. = 他们曾假设存在一种新的粒子。


将来时:
She will posit a different perspective in her presentation. = 她将在她的演讲中提出不同的观点。
We will posit a question for discussion. = 我们将提出一个讨论的问题。


虚拟语气:
If I posited a different scenario, would the outcome be different? = 如果我假设不同的情况，结果会不同吗？
I wish I had posited a more cautious approach. = 我希望我采取了更谨慎的做法。


被动语态:
A new theory was posited by the researchers. = 研究人员提出了一种新的理论。(被动语态中，"positing"很少使用)
A connection between stress and illness was posited in the study. = 该研究假设压力和疾病之间存在联系。(被动语态中，"positing"很少使用)


疑问句:
What are you positing as the cause of the problem? = 你认为造成这个问题的原因是什么？
What hypothesis are they positing in their research? = 他们在研究中提出了什么假设？


否定句:
He did not posit a clear solution. = 他没有提出明确的解决方案。
The study did not posit a direct causal link. = 该研究没有假设直接的因果关系。
