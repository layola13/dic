half-madly: /ˈhæf ˈmædli/ | adv. | 半疯似的，近乎疯狂地 |  Describes an action or state performed or experienced with a degree of madness or craziness, suggesting a lack of control or reason.  It implies a partial loss of sanity or a wild, uncontrolled behavior. |例句：He laughed half-madly, his eyes gleaming with manic energy. 他半疯似地大笑，眼睛闪着狂热的光芒。

He ran half-madly through the streets, pursued by unseen terrors. 他半疯似地跑过街道，被看不见的恐惧追赶着。|近义词：frantically, wildly, dementedly, insanely |反义词：calmly, rationally, sanely, composedly

词源:
The word is a combination of "half," meaning partly or to a limited extent, and "madly," an adverb derived from the adjective "mad," meaning insane or crazy.  The "mad" part has its roots in Old English "mæd," meaning "angry, furious, insane."
