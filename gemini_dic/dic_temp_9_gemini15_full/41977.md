cost:/kɒst/ /kɔːst/| n. v.|成本；代价；费用；花费|The noun "cost" refers to the amount of money, time, or effort needed to obtain or achieve something.  As a verb, it means to require a certain amount of money or effort. |例句：The cost of living is increasing. 生活成本正在上升。 The project cost millions of dollars.  这个项目花费了数百万美元。|近义词：price, expense, expenditure, charge |反义词：profit, gain, benefit|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new car costs a lot of money. = 这辆新车花费很多钱。
This project costs us too much time. = 这个项目花费了我们太多时间。


一般过去时:
The concert cost $50 per ticket. = 音乐会的门票每张50美元。
The repairs cost more than we expected. = 修理费用比我们预期的要高。


现在完成时:
The renovations have cost us a fortune. = 装修已经花费了我们一大笔钱。
How much has this trip cost so far? = 到目前为止这次旅行花了多少钱？


过去完成时:
They had cost themselves a lot of money by investing in that company. = 他们投资那家公司已经让自己损失了很多钱。
By the end of the year, the project had cost over budget. = 到年底，该项目已经超支了。


将来时:
The new house will cost us a lot. = 新房子将花费我们很多。
These improvements will cost a considerable amount of money. = 这些改进将花费相当多的钱。


虚拟语气:
If the project had cost less, we would have continued. = 如果项目花费较少，我们就继续了。
I wish the repairs hadn't cost so much. = 我真希望修理费用没那么高。


被动语态:
The damage was costed at over $10,000. = 损失估算超过一万美元。
The project's total cost will be costed out next week. = 项目的总成本将在下周计算出来。


疑问句:
How much does it cost? = 它多少钱？
What did the repairs cost? = 修理费用是多少？


否定句:
The product doesn't cost much. = 这个产品不贵。
The trip didn't cost as much as I thought. = 这趟旅行没有我想象的那么贵。


PHRASE->
at all costs = 不惜任何代价
at the cost of = 以…为代价
the cost of living = 生活成本
no cost = 免费
hidden cost = 隐性成本
