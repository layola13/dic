excavation:/ˌɛkskəˈveɪʃən/ | n. |挖掘；发掘；挖掘出的东西 | The process of digging up something, especially something that is buried or hidden.  It can refer to the act of digging itself, or the hole or site that results from digging. |例句：The excavation of the ancient city revealed many artifacts.  古代城市的挖掘揭示了许多文物。

例句：The archaeologists are conducting an excavation at the site. 考古学家们正在该遗址进行挖掘工作。


近义词：digging, unearthing, exhumation | 反义词：burial, concealment, construction


词源:  From the Latin word "excavātiō," which is a combination of the prefix "ex-" (out) and "cavus" (hollow).  This reflects the act of making something hollow by digging.
