overlavishly: /ˌoʊvərˈlævɪʃli/ | adv. | 过分奢侈地，极其浪费地 |  Describes an action or state characterized by excessive extravagance or lavishness; going beyond what is reasonable or necessary in terms of spending or abundance. |  例句：They overlavishly decorated the wedding hall, spending far more than they could afford.  他们过分奢侈地装饰了婚礼大厅，花费远远超过了他们的承受能力。


例句：He overlavishly praised his own work, ignoring the flaws. 他过分地夸奖自己的作品，忽略了其中的缺陷。


近义词：excessively, extravagantly, prodigally, sumptuously


反义词：sparingly, frugally, economically, modestly


词源:  The word is formed from "over-" (meaning excessively or to an excessive degree) and "lavishly" (meaning in a lavish or extravagant manner).  "Lavish" itself originates from Old French "laviss," meaning "to wash," referring to the abundance of something, as if it were being poured out copiously, like washing something thoroughly.
