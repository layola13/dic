overcontract:/ˌoʊvərˈkɑːntrækt/ | v. | 超额承包；过度承诺 | To contract for more work or services than one can reasonably handle.  It implies taking on more responsibility or commitment than is realistically achievable, often leading to stress, delays, or failure to meet obligations. | Example: The company overcontracted for the project, leading to cost overruns and missed deadlines.  公司承接了超出能力范围的项目，导致成本超支和错过最后期限。|近义词：overcommit, overextend, overburden | 反义词：undercontract, undercommit
