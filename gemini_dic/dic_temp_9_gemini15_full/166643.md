presentiment:/ˌpreˈsɛntɪmənt/| n. |预感，预兆 |A feeling that something, especially something unpleasant, is going to happen.  |例句：She had a presentiment of disaster. 她有一种灾难将要来临的预感。|例句：A presentiment of doom hung over the city.  一股厄运的预感笼罩着这座城市。|近义词：foreboding, hunch, feeling, intuition |反义词：certainty, assurance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has a presentiment of trouble. = 他有一种麻烦将要发生的预感。
She often has presentiments about the future. = 她经常对未来有预感。


一般过去时:
I had a presentiment that something bad was going to happen. = 我预感到不好的事情即将发生。
She said she had a presentiment of his death. = 她说她预感到他要死了。


现在完成时:
I have had several presentiments this week. = 这周我已经有过几次预感了。
She has had a presentiment about this project from the start. = 从一开始她就对这个项目有一种预感。


过去完成时:
He had had a presentiment about the storm before it hit. = 在暴风雨来临之前，他已经预感到暴风雨要来了。
She realized she had had a presentiment of the accident. = 她意识到她之前对事故有过预感。


将来时:
I think I will have a presentiment about the exam results. = 我想我会对考试结果有所预感。
She believes she will have a presentiment of her child's arrival. = 她相信她会预感到孩子的到来。


虚拟语气:
If I had listened to my presentiment, I would have avoided the accident. = 如果我听了我的预感，我就能避免这场事故了。
I wish I hadn't ignored my presentiment. = 我真希望我没有忽视我的预感。


被动语态:
The disaster was foreshadowed by a general presentiment of unease. = 灾难被普遍存在的不安预感所预示。
His death was preceded by a strange presentiment. = 他的死之前出现了一种奇怪的预感。


疑问句:

否定句:
I didn't have any presentiment of the danger. = 我没有预感到危险。
She said she didn't have any presentiments about the trip. = 她说她对这次旅行没有任何预感。
