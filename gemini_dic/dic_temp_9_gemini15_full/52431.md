deduction: /dɪˈdʌkʃən/ | n. | 推论；演绎；扣除；减除 | The process of reaching a conclusion by reasoning from general principles; the drawing of inferences.  It also refers to the amount subtracted from something, such as a salary or a price. | 例句：The detective used deduction to solve the mystery. = 侦探用演绎法破案了。

His sharp deduction led him to the correct answer. = 他敏锐的推断让他得到了正确的答案。


近义词：inference, conclusion, reasoning, implication | 反义词：induction (归纳)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Deduction is a crucial skill in problem-solving. = 推理是解决问题的关键技能。
The company makes a deduction from my salary for taxes. = 公司从我的工资中扣除税款。


一般过去时:
The scientist made a deduction based on the experimental data. = 科学家根据实验数据做出了推论。

现在完成时:
We have made several deductions about the suspect's motives. = 我们对嫌疑人的动机做出了几个推断。
The accountant has already made the necessary deductions from the payroll. = 会计已经从工资单中扣除了必要的款项。


过去完成时:

The client had requested a deduction on their bill due to the delay. = 客户因延误要求在账单上打折。


将来时:

They will make a deduction for any returns. = 他们将对任何退货进行扣款。


虚拟语气:
