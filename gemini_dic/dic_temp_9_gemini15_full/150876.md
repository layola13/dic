parahormone:/ˌpærəˈhɔːrmoʊn/| n. |旁分泌激素，局部激素|A parahormone is a hormone-like substance that is produced by an organ or tissue and acts locally, affecting only nearby cells or tissues.  Unlike true hormones, parahormones don't travel through the bloodstream to reach distant target organs. |Example: Some researchers believe that certain substances produced in the gut act as parahormones, regulating local digestive processes.  某些研究人员认为肠道中产生的某些物质充当旁分泌激素，调节局部消化过程。|近义词：local hormone, autocrine substance|反义词：endocrine hormone|名词
