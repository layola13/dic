incriminators: /ɪnˈkrɪmɪneɪtərz/ | n. | 导致犯罪的人/物；控告者 | Those who or that which incriminates someone.  Evidence that can be used to accuse someone of a crime. |例句：The stolen documents served as incriminators against the suspect.  被盗的文件成了指控嫌疑人的证据。


The forged signature was the main incriminator in the case.伪造的签名是此案的主要罪证。


近义词：accusers,  evidences,  proofs | 反义词：exculpators,  justifications


词源:  From "incriminate" (to accuse or show to be guilty of a crime) + "-or" (agent suffix).  "Incriminate" itself comes from Latin "in-" (in, into) + "criminare" (to accuse).


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The stolen goods incriminate the thief. = 失窃的货物使小偷受到指控。
These fingerprints incriminate the suspect. = 这些指纹使嫌疑人受到指控。


一般过去时:
The witness's testimony incriminated the defendant. = 证人的证词使被告受到指控。
The broken window incriminated the child. = 打碎的窗户使孩子受到指控。


现在完成时:
The police have incriminated the gang leader. = 警方已经指控了该团伙的头目。
The new evidence has incriminated several more suspects. = 新证据已经使更多嫌疑人受到指控。


过去完成时:
The police had already incriminated him before he confessed. = 他认罪之前，警方已经指控他了。
The prosecutor had incriminated the defendant with strong evidence. = 检察官用强有力的证据指控了被告。


将来时:
The upcoming trial will incriminate the CEO. = 即将到来的审判将使首席执行官受到指控。
New evidence will incriminate the mayor. = 新证据将使市长受到指控。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, it would incriminate him more effectively. = 如果证据更充分，它就能更有效地指控他。
If I had known the truth, I would not have incriminated him. = 如果我知道真相，我就不会指控他了。


被动语态:
The suspect was incriminated by the eyewitness testimony. = 嫌疑人被目击证词指控。
He was incriminated by the circumstantial evidence. = 他被间接证据指控。


疑问句:
What incriminates the suspect? = 什么指控了嫌疑人？
Does the evidence incriminate the defendant? = 证据是否指控了被告？


否定句:
The alibi does not incriminate the suspect. = 不存在的不在场证明不能指控嫌疑人。
The investigation did not incriminate anyone. = 调查没有指控任何人。
