come under: /kʌm ˈʌndər/ | phrasal verb | 遭受；归类于；受到…的支配 | To experience or be subjected to something, often something negative; to be classified or categorized under something; to be under the control or authority of something. |

例句：

The company came under intense scrutiny after the scandal. 公司在丑闻之后受到了严格审查。

His work comes under the category of abstract art. 他的作品属于抽象艺术的范畴。


近义词：

suffer, experience, be subjected to, fall under, be classified as


反义词：

escape, avoid, evade, transcend


词性: phrasal verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project comes under heavy criticism. 这个项目受到了严厉批评。
His remarks come under the heading of irrelevant. 他的话归类为无关紧要。


一般过去时:
The building came under attack last night.  这座建筑昨晚遭到袭击。
The new policy came under immediate fire from opponents. 新政策立即遭到反对派的抨击。


现在完成时:
The government has come under pressure to act. 政府已面临采取行动的压力。
My theory has come under intense debate. 我的理论受到了激烈的争论。


过去完成时:
The old system had come under increasing strain before it collapsed. 旧体制在崩溃前承受着越来越大的压力。
The plan had come under review several times before its final approval. 该计划在最终批准前曾多次接受审查。


将来时:
The bridge will come under strain during the flood. 大桥将在洪水中承受压力。
The new product will come under intense marketing. 新产品将进行密集的市场营销。


虚拟语气:
If the company came under investigation, it would face serious consequences. 如果公司接受调查，将面临严重后果。
If the bill came under scrutiny, it would likely be rejected. 如果该法案受到仔细审查，很可能会被否决。


被动语态:
The proposal was to come under review by the board. 提案将由董事会进行审查。
The decision was to come under heavy criticism from the public. 这一决定将受到公众的强烈批评。


疑问句:
Does your work come under the jurisdiction of the mayor?  你的工作是否在市长的管辖范围之内？
Does this issue come under the purview of the committee?  这个问题是否属于委员会的职权范围？


否定句:
The proposal did not come under serious consideration. 该提案未得到认真考虑。
His actions did not come under the scrutiny of the law. 他的行为没有受到法律的审查。

词源:

The phrasal verb "come under" originates from the literal meaning of "to move or be placed underneath something."  Over time, its meaning evolved to encompass the figurative sense of being subjected to or categorized within something.  The "under" preposition implies subordination or subjection.
