impervious:/ɪmˈpɜːrvɪəs/ | adj. | 不受影响的；不可渗透的 | not affected or influenced by something | He seemed impervious to the cold. 他似乎不受寒冷的影响。 |  insensitive, resistant, unaffected | permeable, susceptible, vulnerable | 形容词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He remains impervious to criticism. 他对批评无动于衷。
The material is impervious to water.  这种材料不透水。


一般过去时:
She was impervious to his charms. 她对他魅力无动于衷。
The old walls were impervious to the rain.  古老的城墙不透雨。


现在完成时:
They have remained impervious to all attempts at persuasion. 他们对所有劝说尝试都无动于衷。
The dam has proven impervious to flooding. 这座水坝已被证明防洪。


过去完成时:
He had been impervious to the warnings. 他对警告一直无动于衷。
The fortress had remained impervious to siege for centuries. 这座堡垒几个世纪以来一直坚不可摧。


将来时:
The new coating will be impervious to scratches. 新涂层将防刮擦。
He will likely remain impervious to reason. 他很可能仍然不听劝。


虚拟语气:
If only he were impervious to stress!  如果他能够不受压力的影响就好了！
If the material were impervious to heat, it would be ideal. 如果这种材料耐热，那就理想了。


被动语态:
The city was rendered impervious to attack.  这座城市变得坚不可摧。
His heart seemed to be made impervious to sorrow. 他的心似乎变得对悲伤麻木不仁。


疑问句:
Is the fabric impervious to stains?  这种织物防污吗？
Is he really impervious to pain? 他真的感觉不到疼痛吗？


否定句:
The soil is not impervious to water. 土壤不防水。
He was not impervious to her beauty. 他并非对她美貌无动于衷。


PHRASE->
impervious to: 对…无动于衷的
completely impervious: 完全不受影响的
relatively impervious: 相对不透水的
largely impervious: 很大程度上不受影响的
seem impervious: 看起来不受影响的
