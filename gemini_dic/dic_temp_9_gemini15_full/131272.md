noncausations: /ˌnɒnkɔːˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. |非因果关系 |The absence of causal relationships; instances where events occur without a discernible cause-and-effect connection.  This term is often used in philosophy and science to describe phenomena that appear random or lack a clear explanation.  |例句：The study explored instances of noncausations in quantum mechanics.  这项研究探讨了量子力学中非因果关系的例子。

The apparent noncausations in the experiment were later explained by a hidden variable. 实验中看似非因果关系的现象后来被一个隐藏变量解释了。|近义词：randomness, chance, coincidence |反义词：causality, determinism |名词
