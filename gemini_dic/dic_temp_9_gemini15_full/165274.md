preintelligence:/ˌpriːɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns/| n. |前智能；前智力；预智能|The capacity or potential for intelligence that exists before full intelligence develops.  It refers to the foundational elements and processes that contribute to the emergence of intelligence.  This concept is often explored in the context of artificial intelligence and developmental psychology, referring to the initial stages or precursors of intelligence.|例句：Researchers are investigating the preintelligence of infants, focusing on their sensory processing and early learning abilities. 研究人员正在调查婴儿的前智能，重点关注他们的感觉处理和早期学习能力。|近义词：protointelligence, precognitive ability|反义词：intelligence (in the fully developed sense)
