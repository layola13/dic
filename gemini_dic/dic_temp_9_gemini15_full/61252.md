emancipated:/ɪˈmæn.sɪ.peɪ.tɪd/| adj.|解放的；被解放的；已获得自由的| freed from legal, social, or political restrictions; released or liberated.  The emancipated slaves built new lives for themselves.  |近义词：liberated, freed, released |反义词：enslaved, oppressed, bound


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The law emancipates slaves. = 法律解放奴隶。
She feels emancipated from her past. = 她感觉从过去解脱出来了。


一般过去时:
The slaves were emancipated in 1865. = 奴隶们在1865年被解放了。
He felt emancipated after leaving his abusive job. = 离开虐待他的工作后，他感觉解脱了。


现在完成时:
They have been emancipated from the constraints of tradition. = 他们已经从传统的束缚中解放出来。
Women have largely been emancipated in modern society. = 在现代社会，妇女在很大程度上已经获得了解放。


过去完成时:
The people had been emancipated before the revolution. = 在革命之前，人民就已经获得了解放。
She had felt emancipated after finishing her studies. = 完成学业后，她感觉解脱了。


将来时:
The prisoners will be emancipated soon. = 囚犯们很快就会被释放。
He hopes to be emancipated from debt by next year. = 他希望到明年摆脱债务。


虚拟语气:
If only I were emancipated from this tedious job! = 要是我能摆脱这份乏味的工作就好了！
I wish I were emancipated from my family's expectations. = 我希望我能摆脱家人的期望。


被动语态:
The slaves were emancipated by Lincoln's proclamation. = 奴隶们被林肯的宣言解放了。
She felt she was being emancipated from the burden of responsibility. = 她感觉自己正在摆脱责任的重担。


疑问句:
Are the children emancipated from their parents' control? = 孩子们摆脱了父母的控制吗？
Was she ever emancipated from her controlling husband? = 她曾经摆脱过她控制欲强的丈夫吗？


否定句:
The people were not yet emancipated from feudal rule. = 人民还没有从封建统治中解放出来。
She did not feel emancipated despite her new freedom. = 尽管获得了新的自由，但她并没有感到解脱。


PHRASE->
emancipated minor = 无需父母同意的未成年人
emancipated woman = 解放了的妇女
emancipated society = 解放了的社会
feel emancipated = 感到解放
become emancipated = 获得解放


词源:
emancipate源自拉丁语“emancipare”，由"e-" (out) + "manus" (hand) + "capere" (to take) 组成，字面意思是"to take out of hand"，即从控制中释放出来。
