jamestown:/ˈdʒeɪmzˌtaʊn/| n. |詹姆斯镇|Jamestown is the name of several places, most notably the first permanent English settlement in North America, established in 1607 in present-day Virginia.  It's a significant historical site representing the beginning of English colonization in the New World, albeit with a complex and often brutal history.|Example:  Jamestown played a crucial role in the early development of the United States. = 詹姆斯镇在美国早期发展中扮演了至关重要的角色。

Example:  Historians continue to debate the events and consequences of the founding of Jamestown. = 历史学家们仍在争论詹姆斯镇建立的事件和后果。
