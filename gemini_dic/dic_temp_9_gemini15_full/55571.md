disturbed's:/dɪˈstɜːrbd/ | adj. | 被扰乱的；不安的；心烦意乱的 |  Describes a state of being agitated, unsettled, or emotionally upset.  It's the past participle of the verb "disturb," used as an adjective.   | Example:  He felt disturbed by the news. 他被这个消息弄得心烦意乱。  She was visibly disturbed after the accident. 事故后她明显地很不安。|近义词:  troubled, agitated, upset, anxious | 反义词: calm, peaceful, tranquil, serene |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud music disturbs me. = 吵闹的音乐扰乱了我。
This problem disturbs many people. = 这个问题困扰着许多人。


一般过去时:
The noise disturbed my sleep. = 噪音扰乱了我的睡眠。
The sudden change disturbed the peace. = 突然的变化打破了平静。


现在完成时:
The news has disturbed me deeply. = 这个消息深深地扰乱了我。
Nothing has disturbed her composure. = 没有什么能扰乱她的镇静。


过去完成时:
He had been disturbed by the events of the previous day. = 他被前一天的事件扰乱了。
She had already been disturbed by similar reports. = 她已经被类似的报告扰乱了。


将来时:
The construction will disturb the neighborhood. = 施工将扰乱邻里。
The storm will disturb the peace of the countryside. = 暴风雨将扰乱乡村的平静。


虚拟语气:
If the dog hadn't barked, I wouldn't have been disturbed. = 如果狗不叫，我就不会被打扰了。
I wish I hadn't been disturbed by that phone call. = 我真希望我没有被那个电话打扰。


被动语态:
He was disturbed by the news. = 他被这个消息扰乱了。
The residents were disturbed by the late-night noise. = 居民们被深夜的噪音扰乱了。


疑问句:
Were you disturbed by the argument? = 你被争吵打扰了吗？
Is she disturbed by the changes? = 她被这些变化扰乱了吗？


否定句:
The news didn't disturb me at all. = 这个消息一点也没有扰乱我。
I wasn't disturbed by their behavior. = 我没有被他们的行为所打扰。


PHRASE->
deeply disturbed = 深受困扰
highly disturbed = 极其不安
mentally disturbed = 精神错乱
emotionally disturbed = 情绪失控
seriously disturbed = 严重不安
