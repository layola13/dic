kaiser:/ˈkaɪzər/| n.|凯撒，皇帝|The word "kaiser" is the German word for "emperor." It derives from the Roman title "Caesar," which was borne by Julius Caesar and later became a title for Roman emperors.  The term was adopted by the Holy Roman Emperors and later by the German Emperors. |例句：Wilhelm II was the last Kaiser of Germany.  威廉二世是德国的最后一位皇帝。|近义词：emperor, sovereign|反义词：subject, commoner
