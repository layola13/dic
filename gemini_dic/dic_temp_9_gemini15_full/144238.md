outsmart:/ˌaʊtˈsmɑːrt/ | vt. |智胜，比…更聪明 | To defeat or get the better of (someone) using greater intelligence or cunning.  |例句：The fox outsmart the hunter by leading him on a wild goose chase.  狐狸通过带领猎人进行一场徒劳无功的追逐而智胜了他。


例句：She outsmart her opponent in the chess match. 她在这场国际象棋比赛中智胜了对手。


近义词：outwit, outmaneuver,  trick, deceive


反义词：be outwitted by, be tricked by


词性：动词

词源:
The word "outsmart" is a combination of the prefix "out-" (meaning "to a greater extent than") and the word "smart" (meaning "clever" or "intelligent").  It emerged in the early 20th century as a natural extension of words like "outwit" and "outdo".
