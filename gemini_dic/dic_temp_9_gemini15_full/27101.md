词源:
The name "California" is derived from the Spanish novel *Las Sergas de Esplandián* (1510) by García Ordóñez de Montalvo.  In the novel, California is described as an island paradise inhabited by Amazons. The Spanish explorers later applied the name to the region they were exploring on the west coast of North America.  While the exact etymology is debated, the most widely accepted origin connects it to the fictional island.
