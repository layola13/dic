decentralists:/ˌdiːˈsɛntrəlɪsts/| n. | 分权主义者；地方分权论者 | Supporters of decentralization, believing that power and authority should be distributed away from a central authority to more local or regional levels. They advocate for distributing control and decision-making power among multiple entities rather than concentrating it in a single location.  | Example:  The decentralists argued for greater autonomy for the individual states. 分权主义者主张各州拥有更大的自治权。|近义词：localists, autonomists |反义词：centralists, authoritarians |名词
