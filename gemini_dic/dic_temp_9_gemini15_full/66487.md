exertment:/ɪɡˈzɜːrtmənt/ | n. |努力;尽力;运用;发挥|The exertion of effort is crucial to success.  努力至关重要。|近义词：effort, application, strain |反义词：laziness, idleness

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exerts himself in his studies. = 他努力学习。
She exerts her influence to help others. = 她运用自己的影响力帮助他人。


一般过去时:
He exerted himself to finish the project on time. = 他努力按时完成项目。
The team exerted great pressure to win the game. = 球队竭尽全力赢得比赛。


现在完成时:
She has exerted herself to reach this level. = 她努力才达到这个水平。
They have exerted considerable effort in this project. = 他们在这个项目上付出了相当大的努力。


过去完成时:
He had exerted all his strength before he collapsed. = 他竭尽全力之后才倒下。
They had exerted pressure to get the contract. = 他们施加压力以获得合同。


将来时:
She will exert all her energy to overcome this challenge. = 她将全力以赴克服这个挑战。
The government will exert its authority to maintain order. = 政府将运用其权力维持秩序。


虚拟语气:
If he exerted himself more, he would succeed. = 如果他更努力，他就会成功。
I wish I had exerted more effort in my studies. = 我希望我在学习中更努力一些。


被动语态:
Pressure was exerted on him to resign. = 他被迫辞职。
The law will be exerted to protect the environment. = 法律将被用来保护环境。


疑问句:
What effort will be exerted to solve this problem? = 将会付出什么努力来解决这个问题？
How much influence can she exert? = 她能有多大的影响力？


否定句:
He didn't exert himself enough to win the race. = 他没有足够努力赢得比赛。
She didn't exert any pressure on him. = 她没有对他施加任何压力。


PHRASE->
exert oneself = 努力，尽力

exert influence = 施加影响
exert control = 行使控制
exert force = 使用武力
