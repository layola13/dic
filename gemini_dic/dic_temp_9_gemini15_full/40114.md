contemp:/kənˈtɛmpt/| n.|轻蔑，蔑视|The feeling that someone or something is beneath consideration, worthless, or deserving scorn.|He looked at them with undisguised contempt.|近义词：scorn, disdain, derision|反义词：respect, admiration, esteem|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His contempt for the rules is evident. = 他对规则的轻蔑显而易见。
She shows open contempt for her rivals. = 她公开蔑视她的对手。


一般过去时:
Their contempt for authority led to their downfall. = 他们对权威的蔑视导致了他们的垮台。
He expressed his contempt for the politician's lies. = 他表达了他对这位政客谎言的蔑视。


现在完成时:
His actions have shown his contempt for the law. = 他的行为表明了他对法律的蔑视。
She has displayed contempt for those less fortunate than herself. = 她对那些不如她幸运的人表现出轻蔑。


过去完成时:
They had harbored a deep contempt for each other for years. = 他们多年来一直怀有深深的相互轻蔑。
I realized I had shown contempt for her feelings. = 我意识到我曾对她感情表现出轻蔑。


将来时:

Their behavior will surely attract contempt from the public. = 他们的行为肯定会招来公众的蔑视。


虚拟语气:
If he showed less contempt for others, he would have more friends. = 如果他对别人少一些轻蔑，他就会有更多的朋友。
I wish he didn't hold such contempt for my work. = 我希望他不那么蔑视我的工作。


被动语态:

疑问句:
