lavisher:/ˈlævɪʃər/| n. adj.|挥霍者；浪费者；奢侈的；丰富的 | One who spends lavishly; characterized by extravagance or profusion. |例句：He was a notorious lavisher of money. 他是一个臭名昭著的挥霍者。  The lavisher of praise was known for their generous compliments. 那个夸奖别人的人以慷慨的赞美而闻名。|近义词：spendthrift, prodigal, extravagant, abundant, profuse |反义词：miser, frugal, economical, scanty, meager


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He lavishes praise on his employees. = 他对他的员工赞不绝口。
She lavishes attention on her children. = 她对她的孩子关爱备至。


一般过去时:
They lavished gifts on their friends. = 他们给朋友们送了很多礼物。
He lavished affection on his pet dog. = 他非常疼爱他的宠物狗。


现在完成时:
She has lavished care on her garden. = 她精心照料她的花园。
They have lavished money on their new house. = 他们在新房子上花了大笔钱。


过去完成时:
He had lavished attention on the project before it was canceled. = 在项目取消之前，他一直非常关注这个项目。
They had lavished resources on the campaign, but it failed. = 他们在这场竞选中投入了大量资源，但最终失败了。


将来时:
They will lavish gifts on their grandchild. = 他们将给他们的孙子送很多礼物。
She will lavish praise on her students' work. = 她将对学生的作业赞不绝口。


虚拟语气:
If he were a lavisher, he would buy a yacht. = 如果他是个挥霍者，他会买一艘游艇。
I wish I could lavish more time on my hobbies. = 我希望我能有更多的时间来从事我的爱好。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

They did not lavish money on unnecessary things. = 他们没有在不必要的事情上花钱。
