preendorsements:/ˌpriːɪnˈdɔːrsmənts/| n.|预先背书，预先认可|The act of endorsing something before it is officially presented or released.  It often involves expressing support or approval in advance.  Preendorsements can be used in various contexts, such as politics, business, and publishing.|例句：The author received several preendorsements for her new novel before it was even published.  这位作者的新小说甚至在出版前就收到了几篇预先的推荐。

The candidate secured several high-profile preendorsements, boosting their campaign significantly.  这位候选人获得了几个高调的预先认可，极大地推动了他们的竞选活动。|近义词：advance approval, prior endorsement, preliminary support|反义词：post-release endorsement, disapproval
