impenitency: /ɪmˈpɛnɪtənsi/ | n. | 执迷不悟；不悔改；顽固不化 | The state of being impenitent; refusal to repent.  It implies a hardened heart and a lack of remorse for wrongdoing. | Example: His impenitency in the face of overwhelming evidence shocked the court.  他的执迷不悟，面对压倒性的证据，震惊了法庭。 |近义词: remorselessness, obduracy, contumacy | 反义词: repentance, remorse, contrition

词源:

Impenitency derives from the prefix "im-" (not) + "penitent" (repentant) + "-ency" (state or quality of).  "Penitent" itself comes from the Latin word "paenitentia," meaning "repentance."  Therefore, impenitency literally means "the state of not being repentant."
