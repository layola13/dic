lier:/ˈlaɪər/| n.|说谎者，骗子|A liar is someone who tells lies.  A person who habitually tells falsehoods.|He's a liar; I don't believe a word he says. 他是个骗子；我不相信他说的话。|She was caught in a lie and exposed as a liar. 她被发现说谎，暴露为骗子。|deceiver, fraud, fabulist|truth-teller, honest person|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He lies to his friends. = 他对他的朋友说谎。
She lies about her age. = 她对自己的年龄撒谎。

一般过去时:
He lied to the police. = 他对警察撒谎。
She lied about where she was. = 她撒谎隐瞒了自己的行踪。

现在完成时:
He has lied to me many times. = 他多次对我撒谎。
She has lied about her qualifications. = 她对自己的资历撒谎。

过去完成时:
He had lied before, so I didn't trust him. = 他以前就撒过谎，所以我才不相信他。
She had lied to her parents for years. = 她多年来一直在欺骗她的父母。

将来时:
He will lie again if given the chance. = 如果有机会，他会再次说谎。
She will lie to protect her secret. = 她会说谎来保护她的秘密。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't lied, we wouldn't be in this mess. = 如果他没有撒谎，我们也不会陷入这种困境。
I wish she hadn't lied to me. = 我真希望她没有对我撒谎。

被动语态:
Lies were told to cover up the truth. = 谎言被用来掩盖真相。
The truth was hidden by lies. = 真相被谎言掩盖了。

疑问句:
Is he a liar? = 他是个说谎者吗？
Was she lying to us? = 她当时是在骗我们吗？

否定句:
He is not a liar. = 他不是个说谎者。
She wasn't lying; she told the truth. = 她没有说谎；她说的是实话。


PHRASE->
pathalogical liar = 惯性说谎者
compulsive liar = 强迫性说谎者
habitual liar = 习惯性说谎者
professional liar = 职业说谎者
a born liar = 天生的骗子
