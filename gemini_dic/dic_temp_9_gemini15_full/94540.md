impost:/ɪmˈpoʊst/ | n. | 欺骗；骗局；税款；强加 | A deceptive act or statement; something imposed deceptively; a tax or duty imposed.  |例句：He was accused of impost. 他被指控欺诈。 |例句：The government imposed a new impost on imported goods. 政府对进口商品征收新税。|近义词：fraud, deception, imposture, tax, levy |反义词：truth, honesty, reality


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government imposes new taxes every year. = 政府每年都征收新税。
He often imposes his will on others. = 他经常把自己的意志强加于他人。


一般过去时:
They imposed a curfew after the riot. = 暴乱后他们实施了宵禁。
The king imposed his rule on the conquered lands. = 国王将他的统治强加于被征服的土地。


现在完成时:
The company has imposed strict new safety regulations. = 公司已经实施了严格的新安全条例。
She has imposed her views on the team. = 她已经把她的观点强加于团队。


过去完成时:
They had imposed a travel ban before the pandemic worsened. = 大流行恶化之前，他们已经实施了旅行禁令。
The dictator had imposed his ideology on the population for decades. = 独裁者几十年来一直将他的意识形态强加于人民。


将来时:
The authorities will impose stricter penalties for drunk driving. = 当局将对酒驾处以更严厉的惩罚。
They will impose a new dress code next month. = 他们下个月将实施新的着装规范。


虚拟语气:
If they imposed a higher tax, people would protest. = 如果他们征收更高的税，人们就会抗议。
I wish they hadn't imposed such harsh restrictions. = 我希望他们没有实施如此严厉的限制。


被动语态:
A new tax was imposed on luxury goods. = 对奢侈品征收了新税。
Strict rules were imposed to ensure safety. = 实施了严格的规则以确保安全。


疑问句:
What impost was levied on that import? = 对该进口商品征收了什么税？
Will a new tax be imposed next year? = 明年会征收新税吗？


否定句:
They did not impose any restrictions on the press. = 他们没有对新闻界施加任何限制。
The judge did not impose a harsh sentence. = 法官没有判处严厉的刑罚。


PHRASE->
impose on =  打扰；利用
impose oneself on =  强加于
impose a burden on =  给…带来负担
impose restrictions on =  对…施加限制
impose a fine on = 对…处以罚款
