rhinophore:/ˈraɪnəfɔːr/|/ˈraɪnəfɔːr/| n. |嗅角，鼻状突起 |A rhinophore is a sensory appendage found on the head of many sea slugs (nudibranchs). They are typically paired, finger-like or club-shaped structures covered in chemoreceptors that help the animal detect chemicals in the water, such as food or potential mates.  | Example: The nudibranch used its rhinophores to locate a patch of algae.  =  这只海蛞蝓用它的嗅角找到了一片海藻。  |  tentacle, antenna |  (无明确反义词) | 名词
