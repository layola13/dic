foundered:/ˈfaʊndərd/| v.| 沉没；失败；破产；衰落 | to sink; to fail; to collapse; to become unsuccessful |

The ship foundered in the storm.  = 这艘船在暴风雨中沉没了。

The business foundered due to poor management. = 由于管理不善，这家企业倒闭了。

近义词：sink, fail, collapse, flounder

反义词：succeed, thrive, flourish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ship sometimes founders in rough seas. = 船只在波涛汹涌的海面上有时会沉没。
His plans usually founder due to lack of funding. = 他的计划通常由于缺乏资金而失败。


一般过去时:
The small boat foundered quickly after hitting a rock. = 小船撞到岩石后很快就沉没了。
Their marriage foundered after years of conflict. = 他们的婚姻在多年的冲突后破裂了。

过去完成时:

The negotiations had foundered before a compromise could be reached. = 在达成妥协之前，谈判就已经破裂了。


将来时:

The economy may founder if inflation continues to rise. = 如果通货膨胀继续上升，经济可能会衰退。


虚拟语气:
If he had planned better, his business wouldn't have foundered. = 如果他计划得更好，他的生意就不会失败了。
I wish the negotiations hadn't foundered. = 我真希望谈判没有破裂。


被动语态:
The ship was foundered by a rogue wave. = 这艘船被一股异常巨浪击沉了。
The project was foundered by a lack of support. = 该项目由于缺乏支持而失败了。


疑问句:
Did the ship founder in the storm? = 这艘船在暴风雨中沉没了吗？
Will the new business founder due to competition? = 新企业会因为竞争而失败吗？


否定句:
The ship did not founder despite the heavy seas. = 尽管风浪很大，但船却没有沉没。
The company did not founder, it adapted to the changing market. = 公司没有倒闭，它适应了不断变化的市场。


PHRASE->
