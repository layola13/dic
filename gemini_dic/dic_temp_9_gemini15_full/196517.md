serving:/ˈsɜːrvɪŋ/ | n. v. | 服务；份；供应 | Serving as a noun refers to a portion of food or drink, or the act of providing service.  As a verb, it means to provide service or to supply something. |例句：The restaurant offers a large serving of pasta.  这家餐馆提供一大份意大利面。 He is serving in the army. 他在军队服役。|近义词：(n.) portion, helping, dish; (v.) assisting, supplying, providing |反义词：(v.) hindering, obstructing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He serves the community as a volunteer. = 他作为志愿者服务于社区。
The waitress serves the customers quickly. = 服务员快速地为顾客服务。

一般过去时:
She served us a delicious meal. = 她给我们做了一顿美味的饭菜。
The soldier served his country bravely. = 这名士兵勇敢地为国效力。

现在完成时:
I have served on this committee for five years. = 我在这个委员会工作了五年。
They have served the community for many years. = 他们为社区服务多年了。

过去完成时:
He had served in the military before he became a teacher. = 他在当老师之前曾在军队服役。
She had served the soup before the main course. = 在上主菜之前，她已经上了汤。

将来时:
I will serve you later. = 我稍后会为您服务。
The company will serve its customers better next year. = 公司明年将更好地为客户服务。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would serve the customers with a smile. = 如果我是你，我会面带微笑地为顾客服务。
I wish I had served them better. = 我希望我当时能更好地为他们服务。

被动语态:
The food was served promptly. = 食物很快就上来了。
Customers are served on a first-come, first-served basis. = 按先到先服务的原则为顾客服务。

疑问句:
What are you serving for dinner tonight? = 你今晚晚餐准备做什么？
Does this restaurant serve vegetarian dishes? = 这家餐馆供应素食吗？

否定句:
They did not serve alcohol at the event. = 他们在活动中没有供应酒水。
The waiter didn't serve us for a long time. = 服务员很久都没来为我们服务。
