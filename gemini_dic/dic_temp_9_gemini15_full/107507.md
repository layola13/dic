land:/lænd/| n. v.|土地，陆地；着陆；使…着陆|The word "land" has its origins in Proto-Germanic *landą, meaning "land, ground."  It's related to Old English land, Old High German lant, and Old Norse land.  As a noun, it refers to the part of the earth's surface that is not covered by water. As a verb, it means to arrive on land after traveling by air or sea, or to cause something to arrive on land. |例句：The plane landed safely. (飞机安全着陆了。)  The farmer owns a large tract of land. (这个农民拥有大片土地。) |近义词：ground, earth, soil, territory, alight, disembark |反义词：sea, ocean, air, water|名词，动词
