dysaesthesia:/ˌdɪsɛsˈθiːʒə/ | n. |感觉异常，异常感觉 | An unpleasant, abnormal sensation, whether spontaneous or evoked.  It can involve any sensory modality (touch, temperature, pain, etc.) and is often described as burning, tingling, prickling, or numbness.  The sensation may be localized or widespread. | Example: The patient reported dysaesthesia in her left leg, describing it as a constant burning sensation.  患者报告她的左腿有感觉异常，描述为持续的灼烧感。|近义词：paresthesia (if mild), neuralgia (if pain-focused) |反义词：normal sensation, analgesia (absence of pain) |名词
