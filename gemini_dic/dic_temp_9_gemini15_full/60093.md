eelpout:/ˈiːlpaʊt/| n. | 琵琶鱼；泥鳅；鳗鳚 | A type of fish, typically found in cold, freshwater habitats.  They are characterized by their long, slender bodies and often have a bottom-dwelling lifestyle.  Eelpouts are generally small, with some species reaching lengths of up to 30 inches. They are typically found in cold, freshwater habitats, often near the bottom of lakes and rivers. | Example: The fisherman caught a large eelpout near the riverbed.  这条渔夫在河床附近钓到了一条大鳗鳚。|近义词：burbot (in some contexts) |反义词：None readily available.
