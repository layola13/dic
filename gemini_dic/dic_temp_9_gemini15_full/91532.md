/haɪˈpælədʒi/ | n. | 换喻；颠倒词序 |  A figure of speech in which an adjective, noun, or other descriptive word is applied to a different noun than it seemingly should modify, resulting in a more striking or poetic effect.  It involves a transfer of epithet.  This is often used for emphasis or to create a vivid image. |  The weary miles stretched before them. (Here, "weary" modifies "miles," not the travelers, but implies their weariness.) |  metonymy, synecdoche |  (No direct antonym, but the opposite effect could be achieved through literal description) | n.
