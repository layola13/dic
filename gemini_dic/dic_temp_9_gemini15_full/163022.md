preclaim:/prɪˈkleɪm/| vt.|预告，预先声明|To announce or declare something in advance.|The company preclaimed its new product launch for next month. 公司预告了其新产品将于下个月发布。|announce, proclaim, declare|conceal, hide|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They preclaim their intentions every year. = 他们每年都预先声明他们的意图。
The government preclaims its policies before the election. = 政府在选举前预先公布其政策。


一般过去时:
She preclaimed her retirement from the company. = 她预先宣布她将从公司退休。
The mayor preclaimed a state of emergency. = 市长宣布进入紧急状态。


现在完成时:
They have preclaimed their support for the candidate. = 他们已经预先声明他们支持这位候选人。
The company has preclaimed its earnings for the quarter. = 公司已经预先公布了本季度的收益。


过去完成时:
He had preclaimed his arrival for the next day. = 他已经预先宣布他将在第二天到达。
They had preclaimed their opposition to the bill. = 他们已经预先声明他们反对该法案。


将来时:

The school will preclaim the holiday schedule. = 学校将预先公布假期安排。


虚拟语气:
If they preclaimed their intentions, we could prepare better. = 如果他们预先声明他们的意图，我们就能更好地准备。
I wish they had preclaimed the changes earlier. = 我希望他们早点预先宣布这些变化。


被动语态:

否定句:
