dropout: /ˈdrɑːpˌaʊt/ | n. v. |辍学，退学；辍学者，退学者 |

英文解析:  A dropout is someone who leaves a school or college course before finishing it.  As a verb, it means to leave a school or college course before finishing it.  The term can also be used more broadly to refer to someone who abandons a particular activity or pursuit before completing it.

例句:

He was a high school dropout. = 他是个高中辍学生。

She decided to dropout of college to pursue her dream. = 她决定辍学去追求她的梦想。


近义词:  leaver, quitter, deserter

反义词:  graduate, finisher, completer


词性: noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drops out of school every year. = 他每年都辍学。
She drops out of the marathon every year. = 她每年都中途退出马拉松比赛。


一般过去时:
He dropped out of college after his first year. = 他大学一年级后辍学了。
She dropped out of the competition early. = 她早早退出了比赛。


现在完成时:
He has dropped out of two schools already. = 他已经辍学两次了。
She has dropped out of several projects this year. = 今年她已经退出好几个项目了。


过去完成时:
He had dropped out of school before he turned 16. = 他在16岁之前就辍学了。
She had dropped out of the race before the final lap. = 她在最后一圈之前退出了比赛。


将来时:
He will drop out of the program if he doesn't improve his grades. = 如果他不提高成绩，他就会退出这个项目。
She will drop out of the competition if she feels unwell. = 如果她感觉不舒服，她就会退出比赛。


虚拟语气:
If he had not dropped out of school, he might have a better job now. = 如果他没有辍学，他现在可能会有更好的工作。
If she hadn't dropped out of the race, she might have won. = 如果她没有退出比赛，她可能就赢了。


被动语态:
He was dropped from the team for poor performance. = 由于表现不佳，他被踢出了队伍。(虽然不是完全的被动语态，但表达了类似的意思)
The project was dropped due to lack of funding. = 由于缺乏资金，该项目被取消了。(类似dropout的被动表达)


疑问句:

否定句:
He didn't drop out of school. = 他没有辍学。
She didn't drop out of the contest. = 她没有退出比赛。
