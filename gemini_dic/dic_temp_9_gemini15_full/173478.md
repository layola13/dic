quasi-famous: /ˈkwɑːziˌfeɪməs/ | adj. | 半有名的，小有名气的 |  Relatively or somewhat famous; known to a limited but significant extent.  It suggests a level of fame that is not widespread or truly significant, but still recognizable to some people. | 例句：The band achieved quasi-famous status after their song went viral on TikTok.  乐队在他们的歌曲在TikTok上爆红后获得了小有名气的地位。

例句：He's quasi-famous in the world of competitive eating. 他在竞技饮食界小有名气。


近义词： moderately famous, somewhat known, locally famous, relatively well-known | 反义词： unknown, obscure, infamous


词源:  The word "quasi" comes from Latin, meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."  Combined with "famous," it creates a word describing someone who possesses a degree of fame, but not to a significant or widespread extent.
