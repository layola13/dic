hydrometeor:/ˈhaɪdroʊˌmiːtɪər/| n. |水文气象；水成物 |A hydrometeor is any product of condensation or sublimation of atmospheric water vapor that is suspended in the air or falls from the air.  This includes things like rain, snow, hail, fog, and cloud.  It is a meteorological term used to describe atmospheric phenomena related to water in various states. |Example:  Rain, snow, and hail are all examples of hydrometeors that fall from the atmosphere.|近义词：precipitation |反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with atmospheric phenomena not involving water, such as dust storms.|
