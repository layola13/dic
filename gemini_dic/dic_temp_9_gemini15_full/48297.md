decreer:/dɪˈkriːər/| n. |法令发布者；裁决者|One who issues decrees or makes pronouncements with authority.|The decreer of the new law faced significant opposition.  新的法律发布者面临着巨大的反对。|ruler, legislator, judge|anarchist, lawbreaker|名词

一般现在时:
The king decrees new taxes every year. = 国王每年颁布新税。
She decrees that all meetings will start on time. = 她下令所有会议都准时开始。


一般过去时:
The emperor decreed the construction of a new temple. = 皇帝下令建造一座新的寺庙。
The judge decreed a fair sentence for the defendant. = 法官对被告判处了公平的判决。


现在完成时:
The council has decreed a ban on smoking in public places. = 理事会已下令禁止在公共场所吸烟。
The government has decreed new regulations for food safety. = 政府已经颁布了新的食品安全条例。


过去完成时:
The court had decreed his innocence before the new evidence emerged. = 在新的证据出现之前，法院已经判决他无罪。
They had decreed a state of emergency before the storm hit. = 在暴风雨来临之前，他们已经宣布进入紧急状态。


将来时:
The president will decree a national holiday next month. = 总统下个月将宣布全国假日。
The committee will decree the winner of the competition next week. = 委员会下周将宣布比赛的获胜者。


虚拟语气:
If the queen decreed a change in the law, it would be difficult to implement. = 如果女王下令修改法律，实施起来将会很困难。
I wish the government would decree a reduction in taxes. = 我希望政府能够下令减税。


被动语态:
A new law was decreed by the parliament. = 一部新的法律由议会颁布。
The punishment was decreed by the court. = 惩罚是由法院判决的。


疑问句:
Who decreed this unfair rule? = 谁颁布了这条不公平的规定？
What will be decreed by the council next? = 理事会接下来会颁布什么？


否定句:
The king did not decree any new laws this year. = 国王今年没有颁布任何新法律。
The judge did not decree a harsh sentence. = 法官没有判处严厉的刑罚。
