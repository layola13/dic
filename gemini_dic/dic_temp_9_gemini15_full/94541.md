imposter:/ɪmˈpɒstər/| n. |冒名顶替者，骗子 |A person who pretends to be someone else in order to deceive others.  An imposter is someone who falsely claims to be someone else, often to gain an advantage or to avoid responsibility. |例句：He was exposed as an imposter after years of pretending to be a doctor. 他冒充医生多年后被揭穿是骗子。

例句：The imposter skillfully imitated the CEO's signature.  这个冒名顶替者巧妙地模仿了CEO的签名。


近义词：fraud, charlatan, deceiver, sham, pretender |反义词：legitimate, genuine, authentic |词性：名词


词源:  The word "imposter" comes from the Old French word "imposteur," which in turn derives from the Latin word "impositus," meaning "imposed" or "placed upon."  This refers to the act of imposing oneself falsely upon others.
