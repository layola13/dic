duper:/ˈduːpər/| n. v.|骗子；欺骗者；欺诈者 |To deceive or trick someone.  A person who deceives or tricks someone. |例句：He's a duper; don't trust him. 他是个骗子；别相信他。
The con man duped the elderly woman out of her savings. 那个骗子骗走了老太太的积蓄。|近义词：imposter, deceiver, swindler, cheat |反义词：truth-teller, honest person


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dupes people out of their money. = 他骗取人们的钱财。
She dupes her friends into believing her lies. = 她欺骗她的朋友相信她的谎言。

一般过去时:
The magician duped the audience with his clever tricks. = 魔术师用他精湛的技巧欺骗了观众。
They duped me into signing that contract. = 他们骗我签了那份合同。

现在完成时:
He has duped many people over the years. = 多年来，他已经骗了许多人。
She has duped her way to the top of the company. = 她通过欺骗手段爬到了公司的高层。

过去完成时:
I realized that I had been duped after it was too late. = 等到太晚的时候我才意识到自己被骗了。
They had duped the investors before fleeing the country. = 他们在逃离该国之前骗取了投资者的钱财。

将来时:
He will dupe more people if he is not caught. = 如果他不被抓住，他会骗更多的人。
They will dupe you if you are not careful. = 如果你不小心，他们会骗你。

虚拟语气:
If I had known he was a duper, I would not have trusted him. = 如果我知道他是个骗子，我就不会相信他。
If he were not a duper, people would trust him. = 如果他不是骗子，人们会相信他。

被动语态:

The investors were duped out of millions of dollars. = 投资者被骗走了数百万美元。

疑问句:

否定句:

They did not dupe us; we knew what they were doing. = 他们没有骗我们；我们知道他们在做什么。
