honcho:/ˈhɑːntʃoʊ/ | n. | 头头；老大；领导 | A honcho is a leader or boss, often informally used.  It implies a position of authority, but not necessarily a formal title. | He's the honcho of the marketing department. 他是市场部的老大。| boss, leader, chief, manager | subordinate, follower | 名词

词源:

The word "honcho" is believed to be derived from the Japanese word "hanchō" (班長), meaning "section chief" or "squad leader."  It entered English slang during or after World War II, likely through American soldiers stationed in Japan.  The pronunciation and spelling adapted to fit English phonetics.
