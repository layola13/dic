high-mindedly: /ˈhaɪˈmaɪndɪdli/ | adv. | 高尚地；自以为是地 |  In a high-minded manner; with lofty ideals or principles.  This adverb suggests actions or behavior motivated by noble intentions, but can also imply a condescending or self-righteous attitude.


英文解析:The adverb "high-mindedly" describes actions or speech performed with a sense of moral superiority or with lofty ideals.  While it can describe genuinely noble behavior, it often carries a negative connotation, suggesting arrogance or a lack of empathy.


例句:

He spoke high-mindedly about the importance of charity, yet he never donated to any causes himself. = 他高谈阔论慈善的重要性，但他自己却从未捐过款。

She high-mindedly refused the bribe, earning the respect of her colleagues. = 她高尚地拒绝了贿赂，赢得了同事们的尊重。


近义词: nobly, virtuously, loftily,  pretentiously (negative connotation), sanctimoniously (negative connotation)


反义词:  selfishly, basely, meanly, cravenly


词性: 副词
