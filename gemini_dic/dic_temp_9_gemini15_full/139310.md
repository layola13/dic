obloquy:/ˈɒbləkwɪ/| n. |恶名，辱骂，诽谤 |Public condemnation resulting in disgrace; strong disapproval.  The politician faced intense obloquy for his scandalous behavior.  |近义词：disgrace, shame, opprobrium, reproach, censure |反义词：praise, commendation, acclaim

一般现在时:
The scandal brings obloquy to the family. = 这场丑闻给这个家庭带来了恶名。
His actions attract much obloquy. = 他的行为招致了很多辱骂。


一般过去时:

词源:
From Late Latin *obloquium, from ob- ("against") + loqui ("to speak").  It literally means "speaking against."
