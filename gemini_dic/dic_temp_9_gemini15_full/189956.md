sanctimony:/ˈsæŋktɪməni/| n. |假装虔诚；伪善；圣洁的样子 | Affected piety or righteousness.  It often suggests hypocrisy. |例句：His sanctimony was a thin veil for his greed.  他的伪善掩盖了他的贪婪。

例句：The politician's sanctimonious pronouncements rang hollow to the voters. 这位政客假惺惺的声明听起来对选民毫无说服力。


近义词：hypocrisy, piety (when used insincerely), self-righteousness, moralizing

反义词：genuineness, sincerity, humility


词源：
From Middle French *sanctimonie, from Late Latin sanctimonia, from Latin sanctus "holy".  The word's evolution reflects the shift from a genuine sense of holiness to a performative, often hypocritical, display of it.
