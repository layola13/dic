prefab:/ˈpriːfæb/| adj., n.|预制的；预制构件|A prefab is something that is made in sections beforehand and then assembled on site.  It is often used to describe buildings or building components that are manufactured off-site and then transported and assembled on-site, saving time and labor costs.  |例句：The houses were built using prefab panels.  这些房子是用预制板建造的。
例句：They ordered a prefab shed for their garden. 他们为他们的花园订购了一个预制棚屋。|近义词：prefabricated, ready-made, factory-built |反义词：custom-built, site-built
