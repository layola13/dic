no-dice:/noʊ daɪs/ | phrase | 不行；没戏；不行不通 | This phrase is an informal way of saying that something is not possible or will not happen.  It implies a definitive rejection or impossibility. The phrase originates from the game of dice, where "no dice" indicates a failed roll or an unsuccessful attempt. | Example: "I asked for a raise, but my boss said no dice."  = 我要求加薪，但是老板说不行。  "Can we postpone the meeting?" "No dice, it's already on the schedule." = “我们可以推迟会议吗？”“不行，会议已经安排好了。” | 近义词：no way, impossible, out of the question | 反义词：possible, feasible, okay
