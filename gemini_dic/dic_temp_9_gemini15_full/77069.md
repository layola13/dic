garnishment:/ˈɡɑːrnɪʃmənt/ | n. | 装饰；（工资）扣押；配菜 | The act of decorating or adorning something;  the legal process of seizing a portion of someone's wages to pay a debt; a small amount of food added to a dish for decoration or flavor. | Examples: The cake was beautifully garnished with fresh berries.  (装饰) The judge ordered garnishment of his wages to pay child support. (扣押) The chef added a garnish of parsley to the plate. (配菜) |近义词：decoration, ornament, embellishment, garnish (as a verb) | 反义词：simplicity, plainness
