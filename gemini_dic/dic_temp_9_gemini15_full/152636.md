peacocking:/ˈpiːkɔːkɪŋ/| n. v.|孔雀开屏；炫耀；卖弄；|The act of showing off one's attributes or accomplishments, often in a flamboyant or ostentatious manner.  It can refer to clothing, possessions, skills, or even personality traits.|例句：He was peacocking around the party, trying to impress everyone with his expensive watch. 他在派对上炫耀，试图用他昂贵的表给每个人留下深刻印象。|例句：Her peacocking on social media about her new job felt a bit excessive.她在社交媒体上炫耀她的新工作，感觉有点过分。|近义词：showing off, boasting, swaggering, flaunting|反义词：being humble, being modest, being self-effacing|名词，动词

词源:
The word "peacocking" is derived from the male peacock's habit of displaying its vibrant and elaborate tail feathers to attract mates.  This behavior is a prime example of ostentatious showing off, hence the metaphorical extension of the term to human behavior.
