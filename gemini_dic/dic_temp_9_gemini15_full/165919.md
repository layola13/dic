preordinate:/ˌpriːˈɔːrdɪneɪt/| vt. |预先注定；预先安排；预先决定 |To determine or decide beforehand; to predetermine.  Preordinate implies a prior decision or arrangement, often suggesting a divine or inevitable plan. |例句：The course of history seems preordained. 历史的进程似乎是预先注定的。

例句：It was preordained that they would meet again.  他们注定会再次相遇。


近义词：predestine, predetermine, foreordain

反义词：leave to chance, improvise


词性：动词
