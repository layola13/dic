downtrends:/ˈdaʊntrendz/| n.|下跌趋势；下降趋势|A downtrend refers to a general decline or decrease in value, price, or other measurable quantity over time.  It often represents a negative trend in a market, economy, or other system. |例句：The stock market is experiencing a sharp downtrend. 股票市场正在经历急剧下跌。
The company's profits have been in a downtrend for the past two quarters.  该公司过去两个季度的利润一直在下降。|近义词：decline, decrease, fall, drop, slump|反义词：uptrend, rise, increase, growth


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The price of oil shows a downtrend. = 油价显示出下跌趋势。
This region experiences a downtrend in population. = 这个地区人口呈下降趋势。

一般过去时:

There was a downtrend in unemployment rates throughout the 1990s. = 20世纪90年代的失业率呈下降趋势。

Stock prices have been in a downtrend for several months. = 股价已经下跌了好几个月了。

将来时:
