put-in:/ˈpʊtɪn/| n. v.|插入；添置；投入；安装|To place or insert something into something else.  It can also refer to a contribution or addition.|例句：The mechanic put in a new engine.  技工安装了一个新引擎。
例句：She put in a lot of effort to finish the project. 她为了完成项目付出了很多努力。|近义词：insert, install, add, contribute|反义词：remove, take out, delete


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They put in new windows every year. = 他们每年都安装新的窗户。
He puts in long hours at work. = 他在工作中投入很长时间。


一般过去时:
We put in a new kitchen last month. = 我们上个月安装了一个新的厨房。
She put in a request for a transfer. = 她提交了调动申请。


现在完成时:
They have put in a lot of hard work. = 他们已经付出了很多努力。
He has put in a complaint about the service. = 他已经投诉了服务。


过去完成时:
They had put in all the furniture before the party. = 他们在派对之前已经把所有的家具都摆放好了。
She had put in a bid for the contract. = 她已经对合同进行了投标。


将来时:
We will put in a new roof next spring. = 我们明年春天将安装一个新的屋顶。
He will put in a good word for you. = 他会为你美言几句。


虚拟语气:
If they put in more effort, they would succeed. = 如果他们付出更多努力，他们就会成功。
I wish I had put in more time to study. = 我希望我当时花更多时间学习。


被动语态:
A new air conditioner was put in last summer. = 去年夏天安装了一台新的空调。
The suggestion was put in by the committee. = 这个建议是由委员会提出的。


疑问句:
When will the new carpet be put in? = 新地毯什么时候安装？
Did you put in a request for leave? = 你提交了请假申请了吗？


否定句:
They didn't put in enough time to finish the project. = 他们没有投入足够的时间来完成项目。
She didn't put in a bid for the job. = 她没有申请这份工作。


PHRASE->
put in a good word for someone = 为某人说好话
put in an appearance = 出席
put in a claim = 提出索赔
put in for a job = 申请工作
put in overtime = 加班
