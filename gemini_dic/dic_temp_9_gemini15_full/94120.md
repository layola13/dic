impecunious:/ɪmˈpɛkjuːniəs/| adj. | 贫困的；没钱的 | lacking money; poor | The impecunious artist couldn't afford to buy new paints.  这位贫困的艺术家买不起新的颜料。|近义词：penniless, indigent, insolvent, destitute | 反义词：wealthy, affluent, rich


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is impecunious. = 他很穷。
She remains impecunious despite her hard work. = 尽管她努力工作，但她仍然很穷。


一般过去时:
They were impecunious during their early years. = 他们在早年很穷。
He was impecunious and unable to pay his debts. = 他很穷，无力偿还债务。


现在完成时:
They have been impecunious for many years. = 他们多年来一直很穷。
She has remained impecunious despite her efforts. = 尽管她付出了努力，但她仍然很穷。


过去完成时:
They had been impecunious before they won the lottery. = 在他们赢得彩票之前，他们一直很穷。
He had been impecunious for so long that he had given up hope. = 他穷了这么久，以至于已经放弃了希望。


将来时:
They will likely remain impecunious unless they find better jobs. = 除非他们找到更好的工作，否则他们很可能会继续贫困。
He will probably be impecunious for the foreseeable future. = 在可预见的未来，他可能都会很穷。


虚拟语气:
If he were not impecunious, he would travel the world. = 如果他不穷，他会周游世界。
I wish I weren't impecunious so I could buy that car. = 我希望我不穷，这样我就能买那辆车了。


被动语态:
His situation was considered impecunious by the bank. = 银行认为他的财务状况很糟糕。
(Impecunious is rarely used passively.)  (impecunious很少用被动语态)


疑问句:
Is he impecunious? = 他穷吗？
Are they impecunious enough to qualify for assistance? = 他们穷到可以获得援助的程度吗？


否定句:
He is not impecunious; he is quite wealthy. = 他不穷；他相当富有。
They are not impecunious; they simply choose a simple lifestyle. = 他们不穷；他们只是选择了一种简单的生活方式。


PHRASE->
become impecunious = 变得贫困
remain impecunious = 依然贫困
chronically impecunious = 习惯性贫困
utterly impecunious = 彻底贫困
dangerously impecunious = 危险地贫困


词源:
From Latin *impecuniōsus, from in- “not” + pecunia “money”.
