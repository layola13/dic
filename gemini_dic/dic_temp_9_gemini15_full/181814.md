reliquidation:/ˌriːlɪˈkwɪdeɪʃən/| n. | 再清算；重新变现 | The process of liquidating something again, often after an initial liquidation attempt has been insufficient.  This typically involves selling off remaining assets to repay debts or settle obligations.  Reliquidation might be necessary due to unforeseen circumstances or to address lingering liabilities. | Example:  The company underwent reliquidation after the initial sale of assets failed to cover all its debts. = 公司在最初的资产出售未能偿还所有债务后进行了再次清算。 |近义词：re-assessment, further liquidation, secondary liquidation | 反义词：accumulation, consolidation
