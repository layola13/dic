expressed:/ɪkˈsprest/ | v. | 表达；表示；表现 | To convey a thought, feeling, or idea through speech, writing, or action.  |  She expressed her gratitude for the gift. 她表达了她对礼物的感激之情。 He expressed his concern about the project. 他表达了他对这个项目的担忧。 | conveyed, communicated, articulated | suppressed, concealed, hidden | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He expresses his feelings openly. = 他公开表达自己的感受。
She expresses herself through art. = 她通过艺术来表达自己。

一般过去时:
They expressed their opinions at the meeting. = 他们在会议上表达了他们的意见。
He expressed a desire to travel. = 他表达了想旅行的愿望。

现在完成时:
She has expressed her interest in the job. = 她已经表达了她对这份工作的兴趣。
They have expressed their concerns to the management. = 他们已经向管理层表达了他们的担忧。

过去完成时:
He had expressed his reservations before the decision was made. = 在做出决定之前，他已经表达了他的保留意见。
She had expressed her disappointment several times. = 她已经多次表达了她的失望。

将来时:
I will express my thanks in person. = 我将亲自表达我的谢意。
They will express their support for the candidate. = 他们将表达他们对候选人的支持。

虚拟语气:
If I could express myself better, I would. = 如果我能更好地表达自己，我会的。
I wish I could express my love more effectively. = 我希望我能更有效地表达我的爱。

被动语态:
His concerns were expressed clearly. = 他的担忧被清晰地表达了出来。
The artist's emotions were expressed through vibrant colors. = 艺术家的情感通过鲜艳的色彩表达出来。

疑问句:
How did he express his anger? = 他是如何表达他的愤怒的？
What methods did she use to express her ideas? = 她用什么方法表达她的想法？

否定句:
He didn't express his feelings very well. = 他没有很好地表达自己的感受。
She failed to express her true intentions. = 她没能表达她真正的意图。
