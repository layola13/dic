morphines:/ˈmɔːrfiːnz/| n. |吗啡（复数）|Morphine is a highly addictive opioid pain reliever.  It is derived from the opium poppy and is used to treat severe pain.  The plural form, "morphines," is less common and usually only used in specific scientific or medical contexts when referring to different types or preparations of morphine.|例句：The doctor prescribed strong morphines to manage the patient's post-surgical pain. 医生开了强效吗啡来控制病人术后的疼痛。
