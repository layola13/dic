muttering:/ˈmʌtərɪŋ/ | v. |低声抱怨；咕哝；喃喃自语 | To speak in a low, indistinct tone, often in a complaining or grumbling way.  | Example: He was muttering to himself as he walked down the street. 他一边走一边自言自语。  |近义词：grumbling, murmuring, mumbling |反义词：shouting, yelling


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He mutters under his breath. = 他低声咕哝着。
She mutters complaints about her job. = 她低声抱怨她的工作。

一般过去时:
He muttered something about being tired. = 他咕哝着说累了。
She muttered an apology before leaving. = 离开前她低声说了句道歉。

现在完成时:
He has been muttering all afternoon. = 他整个下午都在低声抱怨。
She has muttered her displeasure to everyone. = 她已经向每个人抱怨过她的不满。

过去完成时:
He had muttered a warning before the accident. = 事故发生前他曾低声警告过。
She had muttered her concerns to her friend. = 她曾向她的朋友低声表达过她的担忧。

将来时:
He will mutter his disapproval at the meeting. = 他会在会议上低声表达他的不满。
She will mutter something about the weather. = 她会低声抱怨天气。

虚拟语气:
If he were here, he would mutter his usual complaints. = 如果他在这儿，他会像往常一样低声抱怨。
I wish she wouldn't mutter so much. = 我希望她不要那么多抱怨。

被动语态:
His complaints were muttered under his breath. = 他的抱怨是低声说的。
The concerns were muttered among the staff. = 这些担忧在员工中被低声议论。

疑问句:
What was he muttering about? = 他在咕哝些什么？
What are they muttering to each other? = 他们在互相低声说什么？

否定句:
He didn't mutter a word. = 他一句话也没说。
She didn't mutter any complaints. = 她没有抱怨。


PHRASE->
