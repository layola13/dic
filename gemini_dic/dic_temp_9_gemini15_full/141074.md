/ˌɒnəʊmætəˈpiːɪən/ /ˌɑːnəʊmætəˈpiːən/ | n. |拟声词；拟声的；摹声的 | A word that imitates the sound it represents.  Onomatopoeia is the formation of a word from a sound associated with what is named (e.g.,  *hiss*, *buzz*).  It can also refer to the use of such words in literature. | The cuckoo's song is a classic example of onomatopoeia.  The word "cuckoo" mimics the bird's call. |  拟声词，摹声词 |  There is no direct antonym.  Words like "descriptive" or "literal" might be considered contrasting in the sense that they describe words that *don't* imitate sounds. | noun
