prevenance: /prɪˈvɛnəns/ | n. | 出处；起源；来源 | The word "prevenance" is a relatively rare word, meaning the origin or source of something, particularly in a historical or scholarly context.  It emphasizes the place or circumstances from which something comes.  It is often used in discussions of historical artifacts, texts, or ideas. | Example:  The prevenance of the ancient manuscript was traced to a monastery in the Italian Alps.  古代手稿的出处被追溯到意大利阿尔卑斯山脉的一座修道院。 |近义词：origin, source, provenance, derivation | 反义词：destination, end, terminus | 词性:名词
