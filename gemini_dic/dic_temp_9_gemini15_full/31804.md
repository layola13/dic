checkout:/ˈtʃɛkaʊt/| n. v.|结账；办理退房手续；查看；检查|

英文解析:
As a noun, "checkout" refers to the process of paying for goods at a store or completing the departure formalities at a hotel. As a verb, it means to pay for goods and leave a store, to leave a hotel after paying the bill, or to examine something carefully.

例句:
The checkout line was long. = 收银台的队伍很长。

I'll go to checkout counter. = 我要去收银台。


近义词:
payment, departure, inspection, examination

反义词:
checkin (for hotels)


词性:
noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He checks out of the hotel every morning. = 他每天早上都办理酒店退房手续。
She checks out the new products at the store. = 她查看商店里的新产品。


一般过去时:
We checked out of the hotel yesterday. = 我们昨天退了房。
The manager checked out the problem carefully. = 经理仔细检查了这个问题。


现在完成时:
They have checked out of the hotel. = 他们已经退房了。
I have checked out the document thoroughly. = 我已经彻底检查了这份文件。


过去完成时:
She had checked out all the books before the library closed. = 图书馆关门前，她已经把所有的书都检查过了。
He had checked out of the hotel before the earthquake struck. = 地震发生前，他已经退了房。


将来时:
We will check out of the hotel tomorrow. = 我们明天将要退房。
I will check out the report later. = 我稍后会查看报告。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would check out the museum. = 如果我有更多时间，我会去参观博物馆。
I wish I had checked out the car more carefully before buying it. = 我真希望在买车之前更仔细地检查过那辆车。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->
check out (phrasal verb): to leave a hotel; to examine something; to investigate something
check out (informal): to look at someone or something attractive
check out of: to leave a hotel, etc. after paying
check someone out: to look at someone appraisingly
check out the competition: to see what your competitors are doing
