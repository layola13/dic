hydroxyl:/haɪˈdrɒksɪl/| n. |羟基|A hydroxyl group is a chemical group consisting of an oxygen atom and a hydrogen atom (-OH) bonded together. It is a functional group that is present in many organic compounds, including alcohols, phenols, and carboxylic acids. The presence of a hydroxyl group significantly affects the properties of the molecule.  For example, it makes alcohols polar and capable of forming hydrogen bonds. |Example: The hydroxyl group in ethanol makes it a polar molecule and allows it to dissolve in water. 羟基使乙醇成为极性分子并使其溶于水。|近义词:hydroxy|反义词:none
