industrialises: /ɪnˈdʌstriəlaɪzɪz/ | v. | 工业化 | To develop industries in (a country or region) on a large scale.  | 例句：The country is rapidly industrialising. = 这个国家正在迅速工业化。

例句：Many developing nations are striving to industrialise their economies. = 许多发展中国家都在努力使他们的经济工业化。


近义词：modernize, develop, mechanize | 反义词：deindustrialize, agrarianize

The government industrialises the agricultural sector. = 政府正在使农业部门工业化。
China industrialises at an incredible pace. = 中国以令人难以置信的速度工业化。

一般过去时:
The country industrialised rapidly after World War II. = 二战后，这个国家迅速工业化。
They industrialised the fishing industry, improving efficiency. = 他们使渔业工业化，提高了效率。

现在完成时:
The region has industrialised significantly over the past decade. = 在过去的十年里，这个地区已经实现了显著的工业化。
Many countries have industrialised their manufacturing processes. = 许多国家已经将其制造流程工业化。

过去完成时:
Before the reforms, the country had not industrialised significantly. = 在改革之前，这个国家还没有实现显著的工业化。
The nation had industrialised its textile industry before the war. = 这个国家在战争前已经将其纺织业工业化。

将来时:
The plan is to industrialise the entire province within twenty years. = 计划是在二十年内使整个省份工业化。
They will industrialise their energy sector using renewable sources. = 他们将利用可再生能源使他们的能源部门工业化。

虚拟语气:
If they industrialised more efficiently, they would have greater economic growth. = 如果他们更有效率地进行工业化，他们将获得更大的经济增长。

被动语态:
