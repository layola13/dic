dichloride:/ˈdaɪˌklɔːraɪd/| n. |二氯化物|A chemical compound containing two chlorine atoms.  Dichlorides are formed when two chlorine atoms bond with another element or group. They often exhibit ionic or covalent bonding depending on the electronegativity difference between chlorine and the other atom.|Example: Magnesium dichloride (MgCl2) is a common salt used in various applications.|近义词:  (depending on the specific dichloride) chloride, halide|反义词:  (No direct antonym, but compounds without chlorine could be considered opposite in terms of composition)|
