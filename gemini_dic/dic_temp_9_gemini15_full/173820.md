quasi-positively:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈpɒzətɪvli/| adj.|准正；近似正；接近正的|  Describes something that is almost or approximately positive, but not strictly or completely so. It often implies a weak or partial positivity, or a tendency towards positivity.  The term is commonly used in mathematical and scientific contexts, particularly in fields dealing with matrices, vectors, or other quantitative data. | Example: "The correlation between the two variables was quasi-positively correlated, suggesting a weak positive relationship."  = "这两个变量之间的相关性是准正相关的，这表明存在一种微弱的正相关关系。" |近义词: weakly positive, nearly positive, approximately positive |反义词: negative, strictly negative
