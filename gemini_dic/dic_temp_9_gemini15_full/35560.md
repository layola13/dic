coalition:/ˌkoʊəˈlɪʃən/ | n. |联盟，联合；结合 | A temporary alliance of distinct parties, people, or states for joint action, especially in politics.  |例句：The opposition parties formed a coalition to challenge the government. 反对党组成了一个联盟来挑战政府。

例句：A broad coalition of environmental groups is lobbying for stricter regulations.  一个广泛的环境组织联盟正在游说更严格的法规。


近义词：alliance, union, league, combination | 反义词：division, separation, discord


USAGE->
一般现在时:
A coalition governs the country. = 一个联盟政府统治着这个国家。
The groups form a coalition to achieve their goals. = 这些团体组成了一个联盟来实现他们的目标。


一般过去时:
A strong coalition won the election. = 一个强大的联盟赢得了选举。
Several parties joined a coalition government. = 几个政党加入了一个联合政府。


现在完成时:
The opposition has formed a coalition against the new law. = 反对派已经组成了一个反对新法的联盟。
They have built a powerful coalition of supporters. = 他们已经建立了一个强大的支持者联盟。


过去完成时:
The coalition had already collapsed before the crisis hit. = 在危机爆发之前，联盟就已经瓦解了。
The parties had formed a coalition several months prior to the election. = 各政党在选举前几个月组成了联盟。


将来时:
A new coalition will likely be formed after the election. = 选举后很可能会组建一个新的联盟。

虚拟语气:
