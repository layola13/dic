first: /fɜːrst/ /fɝst/ | adj., adv., n. | 第一的；首先；第一次 |  Describes something that comes before all others in a series or sequence; in a primary position.  | 例句：She was the first woman to win the award. = 她是第一个获得该奖项的女性。

例句：He arrived first at the meeting. = 他第一个到达会议现场。


近义词：initial, primary, foremost | 反义词：last, final


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The first step is to register. = 第一步是注册。
What is the first thing you do in the morning? = 你早上第一件事是什么？


一般过去时:
He finished first in the race. = 他在比赛中获得第一名。
We ate first, then we watched the movie. = 我们先吃饭，然后看电影。


现在完成时:
This is the first time I've been here. = 这是我第一次来这里。
She's the first person I've ever met who can do that. = 她是我遇到的第一个能做到这一点的人。


过去完成时:
He had already finished first before I even started. = 我甚至还没开始，他已经先完成了。
They had first tried to negotiate before resorting to force. = 在诉诸武力之前，他们首先尝试了谈判。


将来时:
I will be the first one there tomorrow. = 明天我将是第一个到那里的人。
Who will be first to finish the marathon? = 谁将第一个完成马拉松比赛？


虚拟语气:
If I were first, I would celebrate. = 如果我是第一名，我会庆祝。
I wish I had been first in the competition. = 我希望我在比赛中获得第一名。


被动语态:
The winner will be announced first. = 获胜者将首先被宣布。
The problem was first identified last year. = 这个问题首先是在去年被发现的。


疑问句:
Who came in first? = 谁是第一名？
What's the first thing on the agenda? = 议程上的第一件事是什么？


否定句:
I wasn't first in the race. = 我在比赛中不是第一名。
It wasn't the first time he had made such a mistake. = 这不是他第一次犯这样的错误。
