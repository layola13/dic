lock-in:/ˈlɒkɪn/| /ˈlɑːkɪn/| n., v.|锁定；保证；专心致志；拘留；(价格)固定；|

英文解析:
As a noun, "lock-in" refers to an event that keeps people confined to a particular place for a period of time, often involving entertainment or activities.  It can also refer to a situation where something is fixed or secured, or a commitment that is difficult to change.  As a verb, it means to secure or confine something or someone.  In finance, it can refer to a situation where prices are fixed or guaranteed.

例句:
The company held a lock-in for its employees to celebrate the year-end. = 公司为员工举办了一场年末庆祝活动，将他们留在了公司。

There's a lock-in at the university tonight; all students are invited. = 今晚大学里有一场锁定活动，所有学生都被邀请参加。


近义词:
confinement, commitment, retention, securement, fixation

反义词:
release, freedom, flexibility, unlock


词性:
Noun, Verb
