self-control: /ˌsɛlf kənˈtroʊl/ | n. | 自制力，自我控制 | The ability to control one's emotions and impulses.  It involves regulating one's thoughts, feelings, and behaviors in accordance with one's goals and values.  | 例句：She showed remarkable self-control in the face of adversity. 她面对逆境展现出非凡的自制力。

例句：He needs to develop more self-control to avoid making impulsive decisions. 他需要培养更多的自制力来避免冲动决策。


近义词：self-discipline, self-mastery, willpower, restraint | 反义词：impulsiveness, lack of restraint, indiscipline


词源:  The word "self-control" is a compound word. "Self" refers to oneself, and "control" means to exercise power or influence over.  The word developed naturally from the combination of these two existing words, reflecting the growing understanding and importance placed on the concept of managing one's own behavior and impulses.
