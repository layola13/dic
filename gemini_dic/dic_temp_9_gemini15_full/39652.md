conservatism: /kənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/ | n. | 保守主义 | The belief in preserving traditional institutions and practices, and opposing radical change.  It emphasizes the importance of order, stability, and tradition.  Conservatives generally favor gradual change and a cautious approach to social and political reforms. |  例句：His political views are rooted in conservatism. 他的政治观点根植于保守主义。

例句：The party's platform reflects a commitment to fiscal conservatism. 该党的纲领反映了对财政保守主义的承诺。


近义词：traditionalism, traditional values, reactionism (more extreme) | 反义词：radicalism, liberalism, progressivism


词源：The word "conservatism" is derived from the Latin word "conservare," meaning "to preserve" or "to keep safe."  It emerged as a distinct political ideology in the late 18th and early 19th centuries in reaction to the French Revolution and the rise of liberalism.
