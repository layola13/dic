orseille: /ɔːrˈseɪl/ | n. |胭脂红, 石蕊| A lichen, *Roccella tinctoria*, used to produce a purplish-red dye.  Orseille is also the name of the dye itself, which was historically important for dyeing textiles. |Example:  The ancient tapestry was dyed with orseille, giving it a rich, deep crimson hue.  古老的挂毯是用胭脂红染色的，使其呈现出浓郁深邃的深红色。|近义词:  cudbear, archil |反义词:  (no direct antonym, but colors like pale yellow or white could be considered opposites in terms of hue)|名词
