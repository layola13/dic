repulses:/rɪˈpʌlsɪz/ | n. | 击退；厌恶；反感 | The act of repelling or driving back; something that repels or disgusts.  |例句：The army's counterattack repulsed the enemy.  军队反攻击退了敌人。

The sight of the insects filled her with repulses.  看到那些昆虫让她感到厌恶。|近义词：rejection, defeat, disgust, aversion |反义词：attraction, appeal, acceptance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The smell repulses me. = 这个气味让我恶心。
His rudeness repulses many people. = 他的粗鲁让很多人反感。

一般过去时:
The enemy attack was repulsed. = 敌人的进攻被击退了。
Her proposal was repulsed by the board. = 她的提案被董事会否决了。

现在完成时:
The army has repulsed several attacks. = 军队已经击退了几次攻击。
His arrogance has repulsed many potential friends. = 他的傲慢已经得罪了许多潜在的朋友。

过去完成时:
They had repulsed several previous attempts. = 他们已经击退了几次之前的尝试。
She had been repulsed by his advances before. = 她之前就对他示好感到反感。

将来时:
We will repulse any further attacks. = 我们将击退任何进一步的攻击。
I am sure they will repulse the offer. = 我相信他们会拒绝这个提议。

虚拟语气:
If we had been better prepared, we would have repulsed the attack more easily. = 如果我们准备充分一些，就能更容易地击退攻击。
I wish my attempts to befriend him hadn't been repulsed. = 我真希望我的交友尝试没有被他拒绝。

被动语态:
The invasion was repulsed by the well-trained troops. = 训练有素的军队击退了入侵。
His advances were repulsed by her. = 他的示好被她拒绝了。

疑问句:
Was the attack repulsed successfully? = 进攻成功被击退了吗？
Will their offer be repulsed? = 他们的提议会被拒绝吗？

否定句:
The defense was not strong enough to repulse the attack. = 防御不够强大，无法击退进攻。
She did not repulse his compliments. = 她并没有拒绝他的赞美。
