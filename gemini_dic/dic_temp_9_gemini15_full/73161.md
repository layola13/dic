forbearers:/fɔːrˈbeərərz/ | n. |祖先，前辈 |  Those who came before in a family line; ancestors.  | Example:  My forbearers were mostly farmers. 我的祖先大多是农民。

The family's forbearers fought in the Civil War.  这个家族的祖先参加了内战。


近义词：ancestors, predecessors, forefathers, foremothers

反义词：descendants, posterity


词性：名词

词源:
The word "forbearers" is derived from the Old English "for-," meaning "before," and "beran," meaning "to bear" or "to carry."  Therefore, "forbearers" literally means those who "bore" or "carried" the family line before.  It is a compound word built upon older Germanic roots.
