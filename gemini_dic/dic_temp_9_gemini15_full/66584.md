exhorted:/ɪɡˈzɔːrtɪd/| v.| 规劝；力劝；激励 | To urge or strongly encourage (someone) to do something.  |例句：The coach exhorted his team to play harder. 教练力劝他的队员更努力地比赛。

例句：The speaker exhorted the audience to donate generously. 演讲者力劝听众慷慨捐款。


近义词：urge, encourage, implore, entreat

反义词：discourage, dissuade


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exhorts his students to study hard. 他规劝他的学生努力学习。
She exhorts her children to be kind to others. 她规劝她的孩子们要对别人友善。


一般过去时:
The general exhorted his troops to fight bravely. 将军力劝他的军队英勇作战。
The teacher exhorted the students to complete their assignments on time.老师规劝学生按时完成作业。


现在完成时:
He has exhorted his followers to remain faithful. 他一直规劝他的追随者保持忠诚。
She has exhorted us to act responsibly. 她一直规劝我们负责任地行动。


过去完成时:
The captain had exhorted his crew to stay calm before the storm. 船长在暴风雨来临之前曾力劝他的船员保持冷静。
They had exhorted him to reconsider his decision many times. 他们曾多次规劝他重新考虑他的决定。


将来时:
The president will exhort the nation to unity. 总统将呼吁全国团结一致。
She will exhort her colleagues to support the new project. 她将规劝她的同事支持新的项目。


虚拟语气:
If he had exhorted them earlier, they might have listened. 如果他早点规劝他们，他们也许会听。
I wish he had exhorted me to be more careful. 我真希望他早点规劝我更小心点。


被动语态:
The people were exhorted to support the government. 人民受到规劝去支持政府。
The students were exhorted to study diligently for the exam. 学生们受到规劝为考试努力学习。


疑问句:
Did he exhort you to join the club? 他有规劝你加入俱乐部吗？
Has she ever exhorted you to change your ways? 她曾经规劝你改变你的方式吗？


否定句:
He didn't exhort them to take any risks. 他没有规劝他们去冒险。
She didn't exhort her son to go to college. 她没有规劝她的儿子去上大学。

词源:
From Middle English exhorten, from Old French exhorter, from Latin exhortāre "to encourage, incite," from ex- "out, forth" + hortāri "to encourage," from hortus "garden, enclosure."  The root suggests the idea of "bringing forth" or "inspiring" from within, like nurturing a plant in a garden.
