quasi-scientific: /ˈkwɑːzaɪsaɪənˈtɪfɪk/ | adj. |似科学的；准科学的 | seeming scientific but not based on scientific method or evidence.  It often refers to theories or practices that claim to be scientific but lack rigorous testing and validation.  | 例句：His theories, while interesting, were considered quasi-scientific by the academic community.  他的理论虽然有趣，却被学术界认为是似是而非的科学。

例句：The study employed quasi-scientific methods, leading to questionable conclusions. 这项研究采用了似是而非的科学方法，导致结论值得怀疑。


近义词：pseudoscientific, para-scientific, unscientific


反义词：scientific, rigorous, evidence-based


词源:  The word is a combination of "quasi" (meaning "seemingly" or "apparently") and "scientific."  "Quasi" comes from the Latin word "quasi," meaning "as if," "as it were," or "almost." "Scientific" derives from the Latin word "scientia," meaning "knowledge."
