gonoblastidium: /ˌɡoʊnoʊˈblæstidiəm/ | n. | 胞芽体 | A gonoblastidium is a type of reproductive cell found in certain cnidarians (jellyfish, corals, etc.), particularly in the class Hydrozoa. It is a specialized cell that develops into a medusa (the free-swimming, bell-shaped stage) through asexual budding.  Gonoblastidia are often found clustered together in structures called gonophores. | 例句：The hydrozoan produced numerous gonoblastidia, each capable of developing into a medusa.  水螅纲动物产生了大量的胞芽体，每一个都能发育成水母。 The research focused on the morphology and development of gonoblastidia in this species. 这项研究的重点是该物种胞芽体的形态和发育。 | 近义词：gonophore (in some contexts, as it refers to the structure containing gonoblastidia) | 反义词：N/A
