paracelsian:/ˌpærəˈsɛlziən/| adj.|帕拉塞尔苏斯的；与帕拉塞尔苏斯相关的|Relating to or characteristic of Paracelsus, a 16th-century Swiss-German alchemist, physician, and astrologer.  His work emphasized the importance of observation and experience in medicine, and he is considered a founder of toxicology. |The paracelsian approach to medicine emphasized the use of minerals and other substances in treating illnesses.|近义词：alchemical, iatrochemical|反义词：conventional (in medicine)
