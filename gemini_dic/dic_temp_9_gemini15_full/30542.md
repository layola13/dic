cephalopoda:/ˌsefəˈlɒpədə/ | n. | 头足类动物 | Cephalopoda is a class of marine molluscs characterized by bilateral body symmetry, a prominent head, and a set of arms or tentacles modified from the foot.  They are highly intelligent and diverse, including octopuses, squid, cuttlefish, and nautiluses. | Example:  Octopuses, squid, and cuttlefish are all examples of cephalopods.  章鱼、鱿鱼和墨鱼都是头足类动物的例子。 | 近义词：mollusks (a broader category) | 反义词：None (no direct antonym exists)
