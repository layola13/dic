imide:/ˈɪmaɪd/ | n. |酰亚胺|A cyclic amide formed by the reaction of a dicarboxylic acid or its derivative with ammonia or an amine.  Imides are characterized by a carbonyl group bonded to a nitrogen atom, which is part of a ring structure.
