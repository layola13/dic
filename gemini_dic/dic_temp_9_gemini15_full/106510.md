krebs:/krɛbz/ | n. | 克雷布斯循环；柠檬酸循环 | The Krebs cycle is a series of chemical reactions used by all aerobic organisms to release stored energy through the oxidation of acetyl-CoA derived from carbohydrates, fats, and proteins into carbon dioxide and chemical energy in the form of ATP.  | The Krebs cycle is essential for cellular respiration. | citric acid cycle, tricarboxylic acid cycle (TCA cycle) |  (无明显反义词) | 名词
