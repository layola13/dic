dogbox: /ˈdɒɡbɒks/ | n. | 狗窝；（尤指）手动变速箱 | A dogbox is a colloquial term, primarily used in British English, referring to either a dog's kennel or, more commonly, a manual transmission in a car.  The term evokes the image of a clunky, noisy, and perhaps somewhat unpleasant experience, similar to dealing with a difficult dog. |  例句：1. The dog was happily curled up in its dogbox.  这只狗舒服地蜷缩在它的狗窝里。 2.  Driving that old car with its noisy dogbox was a real challenge. 开着那辆旧车，它那吵闹的手动变速箱真是一大挑战。| 近义词： (for kennel) dog house, (for manual transmission) manual gearbox, stick shift | 反义词： (for manual transmission) automatic transmission
