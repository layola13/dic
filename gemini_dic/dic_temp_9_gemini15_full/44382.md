cross-eyed:/krɒsˈaɪd/ | adj. |斗鸡眼的，斜视的 | Having eyes that turn inward or outward, resulting in a lack of proper convergence or parallelism.  This condition can be caused by various factors including muscle imbalances, refractive errors, or neurological issues.  |例句：The child was diagnosed with being cross-eyed and needed corrective glasses.  这个孩子被诊断为斗鸡眼，需要矫正眼镜。

The cat looked cross-eyed as it stared at the laser pointer.  那只猫盯着激光笔时看起来斗鸡眼。


近义词：wall-eyed, squint-eyed | 反义词：straight-eyed


词源:  The term "cross-eyed" is a descriptive compound word. "Cross" in this context refers to the eyes being misaligned, as if they are crossing over each other. "Eyed" is a simple adjective suffix indicating that the characteristic applies to the eyes.  The term's origins are straightforward and reflect its direct meaning.  It's a relatively old term, with its usage dating back to at least the 17th century.
