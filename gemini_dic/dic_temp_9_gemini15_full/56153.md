dod:/dɒd/| vt.|躲避，逃避；规避；闪避|to move quickly and suddenly to avoid being hit or caught|He dodged the blow. 他躲开了那一击。|She dodged the question. 她回避了这个问题。|近义词：evade, avoid, shun|反义词：confront, face


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He dodges the traffic skillfully. = 他熟练地躲避交通。
They dodge their responsibilities. = 他们逃避责任。


一般过去时:
She dodged the incoming ball. = 她躲开了飞来的球。
He dodged the police. = 他躲避了警察。


现在完成时:
He has dodged the issue for too long. = 他已经回避这个问题太久了。
She has dodged reporters all day. = 她整天都在躲避记者。


过去完成时:
He had dodged the question before the interview. = 他在面试前就回避了这个问题。
They had dodged the responsibility for years. = 他们多年来一直逃避责任。


将来时:
She will dodge the reporters again tomorrow. = 她明天还会躲避记者。
He will dodge the responsibility for his actions. = 他将会逃避为他行为负责。


虚拟语气:
If he had dodged the punch, he wouldn't be hurt. = 如果他躲开了那一拳，他就不会受伤了。
I wish I had dodged that speeding car. = 我真希望我躲开了那辆飞驰的汽车。


被动语态:
The question was dodged by the politician. = 这个问题被政客回避了。
The bullet was dodged skillfully by the soldier. = 士兵巧妙地躲开了子弹。


疑问句:
Did he dodge the question? = 他回避了这个问题吗？
Can she dodge the oncoming traffic? = 她能躲开迎面而来的车辆吗？


否定句:
He didn't dodge the issue. = 他没有回避这个问题。
She couldn't dodge the responsibility. = 她无法逃避责任。
