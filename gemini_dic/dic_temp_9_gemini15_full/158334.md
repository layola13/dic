playwright:/ˈpleɪˌraɪt/| n. |剧作家|A playwright is a person who writes plays.|例句：Shakespeare is a famous playwright. 莎士比亚是一位著名的剧作家。|近义词：dramatist, scriptwriter |反义词:actor, actress


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The playwright writes a new play. = 剧作家正在写一部新戏。
He writes plays for the theatre. = 他为剧院创作戏剧。


一般过去时:
The playwright wrote a successful play last year. = 剧作家去年写了一部成功的戏剧。
She wrote many plays during her career. = 在她的职业生涯中，她创作了许多戏剧。


现在完成时:
The playwright has written five plays this year. = 剧作家今年已经写了五部戏。
He has adapted several novels into plays. = 他已经将几部小说改编成戏剧。


过去完成时:
The playwright had already finished the first draft before the deadline. = 剧作家在截止日期前已经完成了初稿。
She had written several unsuccessful plays before finding success. = 在获得成功之前，她创作过几部不成功的戏剧。


将来时:
The playwright will write a historical drama next. = 剧作家接下来将创作一部历史剧。
They will be collaborating on a new play. = 他们将合作创作一部新戏。


虚拟语气:
If the playwright were alive today, he would be amazed by modern theatre. = 如果这位剧作家今天还活着，他会对现代剧院感到惊讶。
I wish the playwright had lived to see the success of his play. = 我希望这位剧作家能活到看到他的戏剧成功的那一天。


被动语态:

His plays are often performed in major theatres. = 他的戏剧经常在大剧院上演。


疑问句:
