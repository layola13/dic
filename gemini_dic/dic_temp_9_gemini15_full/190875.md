say-sos:/ˈseɪ soʊz/| n. |道歉；解释；辩解|A say-so is an explanation or statement that attempts to justify or excuse something.  It often implies a somewhat weak or unconvincing excuse.|例句：He gave a weak say-so about why he was late. 他对迟到的解释很无力。|近义词：apology, excuse, justification, explanation|反义词：confirmation, proof


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His say-so is usually accepted. = 他的解释通常会被接受。
Their say-so doesn't hold water. = 他们的解释站不住脚。

一般过去时:
Her say-so was unconvincing. = 她的解释令人难以信服。
His say-so didn't satisfy the manager. = 他的解释没有让经理满意。

现在完成时:
He has given several say-sos, but none have been satisfactory. = 他已经做了几次解释，但没有一个令人满意。
She has offered a say-so, but I'm still not convinced. = 她已经解释过了，但我仍然不相信。

过去完成时:
By the time he gave his say-so, it was too late. = 等他解释的时候，已经太晚了。
She had already given her say-so before the meeting started. = 会议开始之前，她已经解释过了。


将来时:
He will give a say-so tomorrow. = 他明天会解释。
They will need to provide a convincing say-so for their actions. = 他们需要为他们的行为提供令人信服的解释。


虚拟语气:
If he had a better say-so, I would believe him. = 如果他有一个更好的解释，我会相信他。
I wish he had a more believable say-so. = 我希望他有一个更可信的解释。


被动语态:
His say-so was questioned. = 他的解释受到了质疑。

疑问句:

否定句:

There was no say-so for his behavior. = 对他的行为没有任何解释。
