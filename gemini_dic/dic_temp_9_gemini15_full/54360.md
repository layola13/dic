dishonourable: /ˌdɪsˈɒnərəbl/ | adj. | 不光彩的；不名誉的；不诚实的 |  Dishonourable refers to something or someone lacking honor, integrity, or morality; behaving in a way that is shameful or disgraceful. It implies a violation of ethical principles or social norms. |


例句：His dishonourable actions led to his dismissal from the company.  他的不光彩行为导致他被公司解雇。

His dishonourable conduct was widely condemned. 他的不光彩行为受到了广泛谴责。


近义词：shameful, disgraceful, disreputable, ignominious, unscrupulous, unethical, immoral


反义词：honorable, respectable, upright, virtuous, ethical, moral


词源：The word "dishonourable" is formed from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") and the word "honourable."  "Honorable" comes from the Old French "honorable," ultimately deriving from the Latin "honor" (honor, respect, dignity).
