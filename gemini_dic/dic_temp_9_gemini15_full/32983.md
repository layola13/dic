choreographer:/ˌkɔːriˈɒɡrəfər/ | n. | 编舞者，舞蹈指导 | A choreographer is a person who creates and arranges dances and ballets.  They design the steps, movements, and patterns of a dance performance. | The choreographer created a stunning ballet that captivated the audience.  这位编舞者创作了一部令人惊叹的芭蕾舞剧，深深吸引了观众。  | dancer, dance director |  (No direct antonym, but perhaps "non-dancer" could be considered a contextual opposite) | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The choreographer designs the dance routines. = 编舞者设计舞蹈套路。
She choreographs for Broadway musicals. = 她为百老汇音乐剧编舞。

一般过去时:
He choreographed the entire show. = 他编排了整场演出。
The famous dancer choreographed her own solo. = 这位著名的舞蹈家编排了自己的独舞。


现在完成时:
She has choreographed numerous successful ballets. = 她已经编排了许多成功的芭蕾舞剧。
They have choreographed for many famous artists. = 他们为许多著名的艺术家编舞。

过去完成时:
Before the performance, she had choreographed every detail. = 演出之前，她已经编排好了每一个细节。
The director had already hired a choreographer for the play. = 导演已经为该剧聘请了一位编舞。


将来时:
He will choreograph a new dance piece next year. = 他明年将编排一部新的舞蹈作品。
She will be choreographing for the summer festival. = 她将为夏季音乐节编舞。


虚拟语气:
If she were a choreographer, she would create amazing dances. = 如果她是一位编舞家，她会创作出令人惊叹的舞蹈。
I wish I could choreograph as well as she can. = 我希望我能像她一样编舞。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
