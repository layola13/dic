coaxed:/koʊkst/| v.| 哄骗；劝诱；诱导 | To persuade or gently urge (someone) to do something. |例句：He coaxed the shy child to sing a song. 他哄着害羞的孩子唱了一首歌。

例句：She coaxed the information out of him. 她从他那里诱导出了信息。


近义词：persuade, urge, induce, entice, wheedle | 反义词：force, compel, coerce


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He coaxes his dog to sit. = 他哄他的狗坐下。
She coaxes her son to eat his vegetables. = 她哄她的儿子吃蔬菜。


一般过去时:
I coaxed him into going to the party. = 我劝他去参加派对。
They coaxed the information from the witness. = 他们从证人那里套取了信息。


现在完成时:
She has coaxed her daughter to practice the piano. = 她一直哄她的女儿练习钢琴。
He has coaxed a confession from the suspect. = 他已经从嫌疑人那里套取了供词。


过去完成时:
I had coaxed him to agree before the meeting. = 会议之前我已经说服他同意了。
She had coaxed the bird into her hand. = 她已经哄那只鸟飞到她手里了。


将来时:
I will coax my son to bed early tonight. = 今晚我要哄我的儿子早点睡觉。
They will coax the plant to grow taller. = 他们将会哄那棵植物长得更高。


虚拟语气:
If I coaxed him, he might agree. = 如果我去哄他，他可能会同意。
I wish I had coaxed her to stay longer. = 我真希望我当时哄她多待一会儿。


被动语态:
He was coaxed into making a deal. = 他被哄着做了一笔交易。
The information was coaxed out of him. = 信息是从他那里诱导出来的。


疑问句:
Can you coax him to come? = 你能哄他来吗？
How did you coax her to tell you the truth? = 你是怎么哄她说出真相的？


否定句:
I couldn't coax her to change her mind. = 我无法说服她改变主意。
They didn't coax him into participating. = 他们没有哄他参与。


PHRASE->
coax out = 诱导说出
coax into = 哄骗…做…
coax along = 慢慢地引导
coax from = 从…套取
coax up = 哄抬 (价格等)

词源:
The word "coax" originates from the Middle English word "cox," meaning "to cajole" or "to flatter."  This word ultimately derives from an Old French word, which further connects to related words in various Germanic languages. The modern spelling and usage evolved over time.
