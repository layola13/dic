puddlers:/ˈpʌd(ə)lər/| n. |搅炼工；（尤指）炼钢工人|A puddler is a worker, especially in a steelworks, who stirs molten metal in a puddling furnace.  The term refers specifically to the process of puddling, which involves stirring molten pig iron in a reverberatory furnace to remove impurities and produce wrought iron.|例句：The puddlers worked long hours in the sweltering heat of the furnace.  搅炼工在炉子的酷热中工作了很长时间。
The puddlers were skilled craftsmen, essential to the early iron industry. 炼钢工人是熟练的工匠，对早期的钢铁工业至关重要。|近义词：steelworker, ironworker|反义词：None specifically, but terms like "office worker" could be considered contrastive.
