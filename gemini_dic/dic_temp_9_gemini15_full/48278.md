decoupling:/ˈdiːkʌplɪŋ/| n. v.|解耦，脱钩|The process of separating two or more things that were previously interconnected.  It can refer to various contexts, including systems engineering, economics, and social science. |例句：The decoupling of the economy from the environment is a major challenge.  经济与环境的脱钩是一个重大挑战。
The software engineers worked hard on the decoupling of the modules. 软件工程师努力进行模块的解耦。|近义词：separation, disconnection, disentanglement |反义词：coupling, integration, interconnection


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company is decoupling its operations from the volatile market. = 公司正在将其运营与动荡的市场脱钩。
We decouple the variables to simplify the model. = 我们解耦变量以简化模型。

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

The engineers have successfully decoupled the power supply from the main system. = 工程师们已经成功地将电源与主系统解耦。
