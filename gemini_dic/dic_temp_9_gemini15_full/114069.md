magnetizing: /ˈmæɡnəˌtaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 磁化的；具有磁性的 |  Making or becoming magnetic.  The process of inducing magnetism. | 例句：The magnetizing field caused the iron to become a magnet. 磁化场使铁变成了磁铁。


The magnetizing current is crucial for the proper functioning of the electromagnet. 磁化电流对于电磁体的正常运行至关重要。


近义词：magnetic, attracting | 反义词：demagnetizing


词源:  From "magnetize" + "-ing".  "Magnetize" ultimately derives from the Greek word "magnēs" (μαγνήτης), meaning "magnet," which itself is likely of ancient Semitic origin, possibly referring to the region of Magnesia in Asia Minor, where lodestone (naturally magnetic iron ore) was found.
