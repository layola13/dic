entailments:/ɪnˈteɪlmənts/ | n. | 蕴涵; 推论; 必然结果 | The logical consequences or implications of something.  It refers to things that are necessarily implied or follow from something else.  The word often appears in the plural form. | Example: The entailments of his theory are far-reaching and require further investigation.  他的理论的必然结果影响深远，需要进一步调查。|近义词：implications, consequences, inferences |反义词： irrelevancies, unrelated aspects |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The decision entails significant risks. = 这个决定包含重大的风险。
His actions entail serious consequences. = 他的行为会导致严重的后果。


一般过去时:
The job entailed long hours and hard work. = 这份工作需要长时间工作和努力。
The project entailed a significant investment of time and resources. = 这个项目需要大量的时间和资源投入。


现在完成时:
The renovations have entailed unexpected expenses. = 装修带来了意想不到的开支。
The investigation has entailed interviewing numerous witnesses. = 调查需要采访众多证人。


过去完成时:
The accident had entailed a lengthy legal battle. = 事故导致了漫长的法律纠纷。
The delay had entailed considerable inconvenience for passengers. = 延误给乘客带来了相当大的不便。


将来时:
The new policy will entail changes to our procedures. = 新政策将需要改变我们的程序。
The expansion will entail hiring additional staff. = 扩张将需要招聘更多员工。


虚拟语气:
If we proceed, it would entail considerable expense. = 如果我们继续进行，那将需要相当大的开支。
If I accepted the offer, it would entail relocating to a new city. = 如果我接受这个提议，就需要搬到一个新的城市。


被动语态:
Significant changes were entailed by the new regulations. = 新规带来了重大的变化。
Further investigations are entailed by this discovery. = 这个发现需要进一步调查。


疑问句:
What does this decision entail? = 这个决定包含什么？
What will the new project entail? = 新项目包含什么？


否定句:
The task did not entail any special skills. = 这项任务不需要任何特殊技能。
The agreement does not entail any financial obligations. = 该协议不包含任何财务义务。


PHRASE->

semantic entailments = 语义蕴涵
entailment relations = 蕴涵关系
