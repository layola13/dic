promulgate:/ˈprɒmʌlɡeɪt/ /ˈprɑːmʌlɡeɪt/| vt.|颁布；公布；宣传|To make (a law, decree, etc.) known officially or publicly; to spread or promote (an idea, belief, etc.) widely.|例句：The government promulgated a new law to control pollution. 政府颁布了一项新的法律来控制污染。|例句：They tried to promulgate their new religious beliefs.他们试图传播他们的新宗教信仰。|近义词：announce, proclaim, disseminate, propagate|反义词：suppress, conceal, withhold


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government promulgates new laws regularly. = 政府定期颁布新法律。
The company promulgates its mission statement to its employees. = 公司向员工宣传其使命宣言。

一般过去时:
The king promulgated a decree. = 国王颁布了一道法令。
They promulgated their findings in a scientific journal. = 他们在一份科学期刊上公布了他们的研究结果。

现在完成时:
The organization has promulgated its principles globally. = 该组织已在全球范围内宣传其原则。
Scientists have promulgated the importance of vaccination. = 科学家们已经宣传了疫苗接种的重要性。

过去完成时:
The church had promulgated its doctrines for centuries. = 教会几个世纪以来一直在宣传其教义。
Before the war, they had promulgated a message of peace. = 在战争爆发前，他们曾宣传和平信息。

将来时:
The president will promulgate the new economic policy next week. = 总统下周将颁布新的经济政策。
The school will promulgate its new rules to the students. = 学校将向学生公布新的规章制度。

虚拟语气:
If the government promulgated stricter laws, pollution would decrease. = 如果政府颁布更严格的法律，污染就会减少。
It would be beneficial if the company promulgated its values more effectively. = 如果公司能更有效地宣传其价值观，将会有益。

被动语态:
The new law was promulgated yesterday. = 新法律昨天颁布了。
The company's vision was promulgated throughout the organization. = 公司的愿景在整个组织中得到宣传。

疑问句:
When will the new regulations be promulgated? = 新规章制度何时颁布？
How effectively was the message promulgated? = 信息传播得有多有效？

否定句:
The government did not promulgate the proposed changes. = 政府没有颁布拟议的变动。
The organization failed to promulgate its message clearly. = 该组织未能清晰地传播其信息。
