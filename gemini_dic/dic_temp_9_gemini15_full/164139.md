predisposed:/ˌpriːdɪˈspoʊzd/| adj.|易患…的；有…倾向的；事先安排好的 |Having a tendency or likelihood to suffer from a particular condition, hold a particular attitude, or act in a particular way.|例句：He's predisposed to heart disease. 他容易患心脏病。|近义词：susceptible, prone, inclined|反义词：immune, resistant


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is predisposed to anxiety. = 他容易焦虑。
She is predisposed to helping others. = 她倾向于帮助他人。


一般过去时:
He was predisposed to believe her lies. = 他倾向于相信她的谎言。
They were predisposed to making quick decisions. = 他们倾向于快速做决定。


现在完成时:
I have been predisposed to believe in the power of positive thinking. = 我一直倾向于相信积极思考的力量。
She has been predisposed to avoid conflict. = 她一直倾向于避免冲突。


过去完成时:
He had been predisposed to distrust authority figures. = 他一直不信任权威人物。
They had been predisposed to support the candidate. = 他们一直倾向于支持这位候选人。


将来时:
He will be predisposed to agree with anything she says. = 他将倾向于同意她说的任何话。
She will be predisposed to follow her heart. = 她将倾向于跟随自己的内心。


虚拟语气:
If I were predisposed to argue, I would have done so. = 如果我倾向于争论，我就会这么做了。
I wish I were not predisposed to procrastination. = 我希望我不那么容易拖延。


被动语态:
He was predisposed to depression by his difficult childhood. = 他艰难的童年使他容易患上抑郁症。（注意：此处被动语态强调童年经历的影响）
The patient was predisposed to infection due to a weakened immune system. = 由于免疫系统薄弱，病人容易感染。


疑问句:
Is she predisposed to anger easily? = 她容易生气吗？
Are they predisposed to taking risks? = 他们倾向于冒险吗？


否定句:
He is not predisposed to violence. = 他并不倾向于暴力。
She is not predisposed to giving up easily. = 她并不容易放弃。


PHRASE->
be predisposed to = 易于…；倾向于…
genetically predisposed = 基因易感
predisposed to illness = 易生病
predisposed to depression = 易患抑郁症
predisposed towards = 倾向于
