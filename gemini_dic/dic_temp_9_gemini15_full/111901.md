logy:/ˈlɒdʒi/ /ˈlɑːdʒi/| n. |学科，学说，论理|The suffix "-logy" is used to form nouns that refer to a particular branch of knowledge or study, often a scientific field. It comes from the Greek word "-logia," meaning "study of" or "speech."  Examples include biology (the study of life), geology (the study of Earth), psychology (the study of the mind), and sociology (the study of society).|Examples:  The study of rocks is called geology.  岩石的研究称为地质学。She is fascinated by the complexities of human psychology. 她对人类心理学的复杂性着迷。|近义词：-ology, study, science, field|反义词：None


USAGE->
This word does not have verb conjugations as it is a suffix, not a stand-alone verb.  It is used to create compound nouns.


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly using "logy" as a standalone word.  Its use is always within compound nouns.  Examples of compound nouns using "-logy" are numerous, including: biology, geology, psychology, sociology, anthropology, etc.
