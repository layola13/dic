joshes:/ˈdʒoʊʃɪz/ | v. | 开玩笑，戏弄 | To tease or make fun of someone playfully.  |例句：He was just joshing you; don't take it seriously. 他只是跟你开玩笑，别当真。 |近义词：tease, kid, joke, jest | 反义词：be serious, be sincere


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He joshes his friends frequently. = 他经常戏弄他的朋友们。
They josh each other good-naturedly. = 他们善意地互相开玩笑。

一般过去时:
She joshed him about his new haircut. = 她取笑他新剪的头发。
We joshed around all afternoon. = 我们整个下午都在开玩笑。

现在完成时:
He has joshed me about my driving skills before. = 他以前就拿我的驾驶技术开过玩笑。
They have joshed with each other for years. = 他们互相开玩笑已经好多年了。

过去完成时:
She had joshed him relentlessly before he finally cracked a smile. = 她没完没了地取笑他，直到他终于笑了。
They had joshed about the incident for weeks. = 他们已经就这件事开了几周的玩笑了。

将来时:
I will josh him about his upcoming exam. = 我会拿他即将到来的考试开玩笑。
We will josh around at the party. = 我们将在派对上开玩笑。

虚拟语气:
If I dared, I would josh the boss. = 如果我敢的话，我会取笑老板。
I wish I could josh her without hurting her feelings. = 我真希望我能和她开玩笑而不会伤到她的感情。

被动语态:
He was joshed mercilessly by his siblings. = 他被他的兄弟姐妹无情地取笑。
The jokes were joshed around the office all day. = 笑话整天都在办公室里被传开。


疑问句:
Are you joshing me? = 你在跟我开玩笑吗？
Was he joshing when he said that? = 他说那话的时候是在开玩笑吗？

否定句:
I wasn't joshing; I was serious. = 我不是在开玩笑；我是认真的。
They didn't josh her about her mistake. = 他们没有因为她的错误而取笑她。


PHRASE->
josh around = 开玩笑
josh somebody about something = 拿某事取笑某人
give somebody a josh =  戏弄某人
a good josh = 一个好笑的笑话
josh with someone = 和某人开玩笑
