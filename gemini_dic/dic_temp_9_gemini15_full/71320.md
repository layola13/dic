USAGE->

一般现在时:
The project often fizzles out before completion.  这个项目经常在完成之前就不了了之。
My enthusiasm for the task fizzles if I don’t see progress. 如果我看不到进展，我对这项工作的热情就会消退。


一般过去时:
Their attempt to start a business fizzled last year. 他们去年创业的尝试失败了。
The negotiations fizzled after several rounds of talks.  经过几轮会谈后，谈判破裂了。


现在完成时:
The excitement has fizzled since the initial announcement. 自从最初的公告以来，兴奋之情已经消退了。
My hopes for a promotion have fizzled after the recent restructuring.  最近的重组之后，我升职的希望破灭了。


过去完成时:
The protest had fizzled before the police arrived.  警察到达之前，抗议活动已经平息了。
Their romantic relationship had fizzled before the summer ended.  他们的恋爱关系在夏天结束之前就破裂了。


将来时:
I think the meeting will fizzle if we don't have a clear agenda. 我认为如果我们没有明确的议程，会议将会失败。
The company's new product launch is predicted to fizzle due to lack of marketing.  由于缺乏营销，该公司的新产品发布预计会失败。


虚拟语气:
If we don't address the issues, the project will fizzle. 如果我们不解决这些问题，项目就会失败。
I wish my efforts hadn't fizzled. 我真希望我的努力没有白费。


被动语态:
The negotiations were fizzled by disagreements between the parties.  各方之间的分歧导致谈判破裂。
The planned concert was fizzled due to unforeseen circumstances.  由于不可预见的情况，原定的音乐会取消了。


疑问句:
Will the new marketing campaign fizzle? 新的营销活动会失败吗？
Did their efforts fizzle out completely? 他们的努力完全失败了吗？


否定句:
The new product didn’t fizzle; it was a huge success.  新产品并没有失败；它取得了巨大的成功。
The party didn't fizzle; it was lively until dawn.  派对没有冷清下来；它一直热闹到黎明。
