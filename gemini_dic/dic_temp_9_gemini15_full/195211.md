semielevated: /ˌsemiˈɛləveɪtɪd/ | adj. | 半高架的，部分高架的 | Partially elevated; raised to a moderate height.  The semielevated highway runs above street level but not as high as a fully elevated structure. | 近义词：partially elevated, raised, lofty (in some contexts) | 反义词：ground-level, subterranean


例句：

The semielevated train tracks run through the city center. = 半高架的火车轨道穿过市中心。

The semielevated walkway offers a scenic view of the park below. = 半高架的人行道提供下方公园的优美景色。


词源:

* **semi-**:  A prefix meaning "half," "partly," or "partially."  Derived from Latin *semi*.

* **elevated**:  From the past participle of the verb "elevate," meaning "to raise to a higher position."  Ultimately derived from Latin *elevatus*, past participle of *elevare* ("to lift up").


用法说明：

semielevated 主要用作形容词，用来描述部分抬高的结构，例如道路、铁路、人行道等。它强调结构并非完全高架，而是介于地面和完全高架之间。


由于semielevated是一个相对较少见的词，它没有广泛的语法用法变化，在句中主要充当定语修饰名词。  以下例句仅供参考，因为semielevated本身并不涉及复杂的语法结构。
