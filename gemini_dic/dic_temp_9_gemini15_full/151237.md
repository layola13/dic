parenesis:/pəˈrenɪsɪs/ | n. | 劝告；训诫；恳求 | A figure of speech in which the speaker or writer breaks off to address a person or thing, often an absent person or abstraction.  It is a digression in the form of an address to the reader or listener, often to give advice or warning.  It can also be a fervent plea or appeal. | Example: "O, my friends,  let us not forget the sacrifices made for our freedom." (Here, the speaker directly addresses the audience, offering a plea for remembrance.) | 近义词：admonition, exhortation, advice, appeal | 反义词：command, order
