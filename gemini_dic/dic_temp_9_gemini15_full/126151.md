mutism: /ˈmjuːtɪzəm/ | n. |缄默症，沉默症 |The inability to speak, typically as a result of psychological trauma or disorder.  It's characterized by a persistent inability to speak in situations where speaking is expected.  This differs from selective mutism, where a person can speak in some situations but not others. |例句：The child's prolonged mutism was a cause for concern for his parents and teachers.  孩子的长期沉默让他的父母和老师都非常担心。|近义词：silence, taciturnity, aphonia (loss of voice)|反义词：loquacity, talkativeness|
