loxodromics:/ˈlɒksədrɒmɪks/| n.| 罗德斯线；恒向线 |The study of loxodromes, or rhumb lines, which are lines on a sphere that intersect all meridians at the same angle.  Loxodromics is a branch of navigation and cartography that deals with the mathematical properties and practical applications of these curves.  | Example:  Understanding loxodromics is crucial for long-distance navigation, especially for sailors and pilots.|近义词：rhumb line navigation |反义词：great circle navigation
