/ˈmɛrəsoʊm/| n. |染色体片段；染色体小体|A merosome is a fragment of a chromosome, often resulting from breakage or other chromosomal abnormalities.  It can be acentric (lacking a centromere) or monocentric (having one centromere). Merosomes are usually associated with aneuploidy and can lead to various genetic disorders.|Example: The cytogenetic analysis revealed the presence of several merosomes in the patient's karyotype, indicating chromosomal instability.|近义词：chromosome fragment|反义词：无|名词
