secularizers:/ˈsɛkjʊləraɪzərz/| n. |世俗化者，使世俗化的人或事物|People or things that promote secularization, the process of becoming less religious or more concerned with worldly matters. They may be individuals, groups, movements, or even societal trends that actively diminish the influence of religion in public life.  | Example: The rise of secularizers in the 17th century led to significant changes in social structures.  世俗化者在17世纪的兴起导致了社会结构的重大变化。|近义词：secularists, rationalists, modernizers |反义词：clericalists, traditionalists, fundamentalists |名词
