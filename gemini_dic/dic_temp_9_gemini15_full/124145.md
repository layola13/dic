mortify:/ˈmɔːrtɪfaɪ/ | vt. | 使羞愧；使蒙羞；使屈辱；克制（肉体欲望） | to cause (someone) to feel embarrassed, ashamed, or humiliated |  Her public criticism mortified her. 她当众受到批评，感到非常羞愧。
His failure to answer the question mortified him. 他没能回答这个问题，让他感到很羞愧。


近义词：humiliate, shame, embarrass, disgrace | 反义词：glorify, honor, exalt

词源:
From Middle English *mortifien, from Old French mortifier, from Latin mortificare "to kill, put to death," from mort-, mors "death" + -ficare "-fy".  The sense evolution is from "to subdue, kill (fleshly desires)" to "to cause shame or humiliation."
