rehonoring: /ˌriːˈɒnərɪŋ/ | v. | 重视；重新给予荣誉；再次表彰 | The act of honoring again; showing respect or admiration once more.  It implies a previous honoring that is being reaffirmed or repeated. |例句：The city is rehonoring its fallen heroes with a new memorial. = 城市正在用新的纪念碑来重新纪念牺牲的英雄。 The company is rehonoring its employees' hard work with bonuses. = 公司正在用奖金来重新表彰员工的辛勤工作。 |近义词：revering, celebrating, commemorating |反义词：dishonoring, disrespecting, ignoring |动词

词源:  The word is formed by adding the prefix "re-" (meaning "again") to the verb "honoring," which itself derives from the noun "honor."  The root of "honor" traces back to Old French *honor and ultimately to Latin *honōr-.
