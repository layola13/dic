publicises:/ˈpʌblɪsaɪz/| vt.|宣传，公布|To make something known publicly, especially by advertising or other means of publicity.|例句：The company publicises its products through television commercials. 公司通过电视广告宣传其产品。|例句：The event was widely publicised in the local newspapers.  这次活动在当地报纸上被广泛宣传。|近义词：advertise, promote, publicize|反义词：suppress, conceal, hide|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company publicises its new product line. = 公司正在宣传其新的产品线。
They publicise their services through social media. = 他们通过社交媒体宣传他们的服务。


一般过去时:
The band publicised their new album extensively. = 乐队广泛宣传了他们的新专辑。
She publicised her book through various channels. = 她通过各种渠道宣传她的书。


现在完成时:
They have publicised their findings in several scientific journals. = 他们已经在几家科学期刊上公布了他们的研究结果。
The government has publicised the new policy nationwide. = 政府已经在全国范围内公布了这项新政策。


过去完成时:
They had publicised the event for months before it took place. = 在活动举行之前，他们已经宣传了几个月。
The company had publicised the product launch for weeks leading up to it. = 公司在产品发布之前的几周内一直在宣传。


将来时:
They will publicise the concert on radio and television. = 他们将在广播和电视上宣传这场音乐会。
The museum will publicise the new exhibition through online platforms. = 博物馆将通过在线平台宣传新的展览。


虚拟语气:
If they publicised the event more effectively, more people would attend. = 如果他们更有效地宣传活动，会有更多人参加。
I wish they had publicised the change in schedule earlier. = 我希望他们早点公布时间安排的改变。


被动语态:
The product was publicised through a series of online ads. = 该产品通过一系列在线广告进行了宣传。
The news was widely publicised in the media. = 这条新闻在媒体上被广泛报道。


疑问句:
How will you publicise your new business? = 你将如何宣传你的新业务？
Has the company publicised its financial results yet? = 公司是否已经公布了其财务业绩？


否定句:
The company did not publicise the recall of the product quickly enough. = 公司没有及时公布产品的召回信息。
They didn't publicise the meeting effectively, so few people attended. = 他们没有有效地宣传会议，所以只有少数人参加。
