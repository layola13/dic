double-crossers:/ˈdʌbəlˌkrɔːsərz/ | n. | 背叛者，两面派 | People who betray someone's trust by secretly working against them or deceiving them.  They act as if they are loyal or friendly, but in reality they are working against the person's best interests. | Example: The police were suspicious of the informant, believing him to be one of the double-crossers. 警方对线人表示怀疑，认为他是一个两面派。  The double-crossers within the organization leaked confidential information to our competitors.  组织内部的叛徒将机密信息泄露给了我们的竞争对手。 | 近义词：traitors, betrayers, deceivers, backstabbers | 反义词：loyalists, allies, friends
