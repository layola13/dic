pasigraphy:/ˈpæzɪɡræfi/| n. |通用文字；世界语；意符文字 |A system of writing in which symbols represent ideas or concepts directly, rather than sounds or words.  It aims to create a universal language understood by people regardless of their native tongue.  The term often implies a system that is not yet fully realized or widely adopted. |Example:  Some early attempts at creating a pasigraphy involved using pictures to represent ideas, but these systems often lacked the precision and complexity needed for efficient communication.  一些早期创造通用文字的尝试涉及使用图片来表达思想，但这些系统往往缺乏有效沟通所需的精确性和复杂性。|近义词：universal language, world language, ideography|反义词：phonography (sound-based writing), logography (word-based writing)
