immobilization: /ɪˌmoʊbɪləˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 固定；固定化；不动化 | The act of making something immobile or motionless; the state of being immobile.  It often refers to the process of restricting movement, either physically or chemically. | 例句：The immobilization of the fractured leg required a cast.  =  骨折的腿需要石膏固定。

The immobilization of the enzyme reduced its activity. = 酶的固定降低了它的活性。


近义词：fixation, restraint, confinement, stabilization | 反义词：mobilization, movement, activation


词源:  From "immobilize," which is formed from the prefix "im-" (not) + "mobile" (movable) + "-ization" (the act of).  "Mobile" ultimately comes from the Latin word "mobilis," meaning "movable."
