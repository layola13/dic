confabulate:/kənˈfæbjuˌleɪt/ | v. | 虚构；编造；闲聊；（尤指非正式地）讨论 | To invent or fabricate stories, especially in a casual or informal way; to chat or converse casually.  | 例句：He confabulated a fanciful tale about his adventures in the Amazon. 他虚构了一个关于他在亚马逊冒险的奇异故事。

The witnesses confabulated amongst themselves before giving their testimony.  证人们在作证前私下交谈。


近义词：fabricate, invent, concoct, chat, gossip, discuss | 反义词：verify, confirm, corroborate


USAGE->

They confabulate every evening. = 他们每天晚上都会闲聊。
He confabulates stories to impress his friends. = 他编造故事来给朋友们留下深刻印象。


一般过去时:
They confabulated about the upcoming election. = 他们讨论了即将到来的选举。
She confabulated a believable excuse for her lateness. = 她编造了一个令人信服的迟到借口。

过去完成时:

否定句:

PHRASE->

词源:  From Latin *confabulari, from com- "together" + fabulari "to speak," from fabula "story," related to fari "to speak."  (字面意思是“一起说话”)
