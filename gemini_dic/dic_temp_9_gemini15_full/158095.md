plasticities:/plæˈstɪsətiz/| n.|可塑性；展性；适应性|The ability of a material to be molded or shaped.  It also refers to the capacity for adaptation or change.  The plural form of plasticity.|例句：The plasticities of clay allow for a wide range of sculptural forms. 粘土的可塑性允许制作各种各样的雕塑形式。

The remarkable plasticities of the human brain enable it to adapt to new challenges throughout life. 人类大脑的显著可塑性使其能够在一生中适应新的挑战。|近义词：flexibility, malleability, adaptability, ductility|反义词：rigidity, inflexibility, inelasticity

词源:
From plasticity + -ities (plural suffix).  Plasticity itself derives from the Greek word  πλαστός (plastos), meaning "molded," "formed," or "shaped," ultimately related to the verb πλάσσειν (plassein), meaning "to mold" or "to shape."  The suffix "-ity" indicates a state or quality.
