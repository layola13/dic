self-satirists: /ˈsɛlf ˈsætə(r)ɪsts/ | n. | 自我讽刺者 | People who use satire to critique or expose their own flaws, follies, or shortcomings. They often employ humor and irony to achieve this self-deprecating effect.  | Example: Many stand-up comedians are self-satirists, using their routines to poke fun at their own experiences and personalities.  许多单口喜剧演员都是自我讽刺者，他们利用自己的表演来取笑自己的经历和个性。 | 近义词: self-mocking comedians,  self-deprecating humorists | 反义词: self-aggrandizers,  braggarts
