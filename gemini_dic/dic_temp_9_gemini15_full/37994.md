complicities:/kəmˈplɪsɪtiz/ | n. |同谋；共犯；串通；牵连 | The plural form of "complicity," referring to instances or acts of involvement in wrongdoing.  It suggests a shared guilt or responsibility for a crime or misdeed. |例句：The investigation uncovered a web of complicities that extended far beyond the initial suspects.  调查揭露了一个远超最初嫌疑人的同谋网络。

The complicities between the government officials and the business executives led to a major scandal.  政府官员和企业高管之间的串通导致了一场重大丑闻。

|近义词：collusion, conspiracy, collaboration, involvement |反义词：innocence, non-involvement

词源:
From the word "complicity," which comes from the Old French *complicitee* and ultimately from the Latin *complicitus*, meaning "entangled, involved."  The Latin word itself is a combination of *com-* (together) and *plicitus* (folded, entangled).
