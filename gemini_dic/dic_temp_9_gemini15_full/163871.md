predestinarianism:/ˌpriːdɛstɪˈneəriənɪzəm/| n. |预定论，宿命论 |The theological doctrine that God has predetermined everything that will happen, including the salvation or damnation of individuals.  This belief often implies that human free will is limited or nonexistent. |例句：Predestinarianism is a complex theological concept that has been debated for centuries. 预定论是一个复杂的神学概念，几个世纪以来一直备受争议。

例句：Many religious scholars reject predestinarianism, arguing that it contradicts the idea of divine justice and human responsibility. 许多宗教学者拒绝预定论，认为它与神圣正义和人类责任的观念相矛盾。


近义词：determinism, fatalism |反义词：free will, voluntarism


词源：From "predestine" + "-arianism".  "Predestine" comes from the Old French *predestiner, ultimately from the Latin *praedestinare, a combination of *prae- ("before") and *destinare ("to determine"). "-arianism" is a suffix indicating a belief system or doctrine.
