logined:/ˈlɒɡɪnd/| adj.|已登录的，登录过的|The term "logined" is not a standard English word. It's an informal or non-standard past participle of "login," which itself is more commonly used as a verb.  It describes a state of being logged in or having successfully completed a login process. |Example:  He was logined, so he could access the system. 他已经登录，所以可以访问系统。|近义词：logged in, signed in|反义词：logged out, signed out


USAGE->
This word is not standard, so usage examples in standard grammatical tenses are not applicable.  Instead, here are examples using the standard verb "log in" and its past participle "logged in":

一般现在时:
I log in to my account every morning. = 我每天早上登录我的帐户。
They log in using their employee IDs. = 他们使用员工 ID 登录。

一般过去时:
I logged in yesterday without any problems. = 我昨天顺利登录了。
She logged in late because of traffic. = 由于交通堵塞，她登录晚了。

现在完成时:
I have logged in successfully. = 我已经成功登录了。
He has logged in several times today. = 他今天已经登录了好几次。

过去完成时:
I had logged in before the meeting started. = 会议开始前我已经登录了。
She had logged in earlier that day. = 她当天早些时候已经登录了。

将来时:
I will log in tomorrow morning. = 我明天早上会登录。
He will log in as soon as he gets to the office. = 他一到办公室就会登录。

虚拟语气:
If I logged in, I could see the report. = 如果我登录了，我就能看到报告。
I wish I had logged in earlier. = 我真希望我早点登录。

被动语态:
The account was logged in from an unknown location. = 该帐户是从未知位置登录的。（此句中"log in"作为被动语态较为少见，通常用其他表达）
My account is frequently logged in. = 我的帐户经常有人登录。

疑问句:
Have you logged in yet? = 你登录了吗？
Did he log in successfully? = 他成功登录了吗？

否定句:
I couldn't log in because I forgot my password. = 我无法登录，因为我忘记了密码。
She didn't log in this morning. = 她今天早上没有登录。
