non-equivalent:/ˌnɒnɪˈkwɪvələnt/| adj. |非等价的，不等值的|Not having the same value, meaning, or effect.  This term is often used in translation studies, linguistics, and mathematics to describe elements that do not correspond exactly in meaning or function. For example, a direct translation might not be a non-equivalent translation.  |例句：The two phrases, while seemingly similar, are non-equivalent in meaning. 这两个短语虽然表面相似，但在意义上却并不等价。

The direct translation of this idiom is non-equivalent to its original meaning.  这个习语的直译与它的本义不等价。|近义词：unequal, dissimilar, disparate, incomparable|反义词：equivalent, equal, identical
