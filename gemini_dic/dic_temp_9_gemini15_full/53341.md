disadvantageable: /ˌdɪsˌædˈvæntɪdʒəbəl/ | adj. | 不利的，有缺点的 |  Having disadvantages; putting one at a disadvantage.  This word is rarely used in modern English and is considered somewhat archaic or overly formal.  It suggests something inherently possessing disadvantages, rather than simply resulting in a disadvantage. |


例句：

The current economic climate is disadvantageable to small businesses. = 目前的经济环境对小型企业不利。

His lack of experience is disadvantageable in this highly competitive field. = 他缺乏经验，在这个竞争激烈的领域处于不利地位。


近义词：unfavorable, detrimental, disadvantageous, undesirable


反义词：advantageous, beneficial, favorable


词性:  形容词
