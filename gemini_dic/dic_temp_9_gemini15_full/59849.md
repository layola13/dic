ectopy:/ˈɛktoʊpi/ | n. |异位，异位妊娠|The displacement of an organ or other part from its normal position.  Ectopy most commonly refers to ectopic pregnancy, where a fertilized egg implants outside the uterus, typically in a fallopian tube.  |例句：The patient presented with symptoms consistent with an ectopic pregnancy.|近义词：displacement, malposition|反义词：normal position, in situ

PHRASE->
ectopic pregnancy (异位妊娠): The most common usage of the term.
