flycatcher: /ˈflaɪˌkætʃər/ | n. | 捕蝇器；捕蝇鸟 | A bird that catches and eats flies.  Flycatchers are characterized by their habit of perching and darting out to snatch insects in mid-air.  Many species have a wide gape and a flattened bill. | 例句：The flycatcher perched on a branch, waiting patiently for a fly.  这只捕蝇鸟栖息在树枝上，耐心地等待着苍蝇。

The spotted flycatcher is a common bird in Europe.  欧洲斑点捕蝇鸟是一种常见的鸟类。


近义词：insectivore (食虫动物) | 反义词：None (无明确反义词)


词源:  The word "flycatcher" is a compound word. "Fly" refers to the insect, and "catcher" refers to the act of catching.  It's a descriptive name reflecting the bird's primary feeding behavior.
