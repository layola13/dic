miasmology:/ˌmaɪæzˈmɒlədʒi/| n. |瘴气学，疫癘学 |The study of miasma, the belief that diseases were caused by bad air.  This theory, prevalent before the germ theory of disease, linked illness to foul-smelling air and decaying matter.  |Example:  Miasmology was a dominant theory in medicine until the late 19th century. = 瘴气学在19世纪后期之前是医学中占主导地位的理论。|近义词: (Historically) Contagion theory (in its early, less precise forms) |反义词: Germ theory, microbiology |词性: 名词
