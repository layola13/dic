grief:/ɡriːf/| n. |悲伤，悲痛 |The deep sorrow caused by someone's death.  Grief is a complex emotional response to loss, often involving sadness, anger, guilt, and confusion.  It's a natural and healthy process, though the intensity and duration can vary greatly from person to person. |例句：Her grief at losing her husband was overwhelming. 她失去丈夫的悲痛令人难以承受。

例句：The family is still grieving the loss of their child.  这个家庭仍在为失去孩子而悲痛。


近义词：sorrow, sadness, mourning, bereavement |反义词：joy, happiness, elation

He feels deep grief over his father's death. = 他为他父亲的去世感到深深的悲痛。
She expresses her grief through writing poetry. = 她通过写诗来表达她的悲伤。


一般过去时:
They grieved deeply after their pet died. = 他们的宠物死后，他们非常悲痛。

现在完成时:

They have been grieving the loss of their jobs. = 他们一直在为失去工作而悲伤。


过去完成时:

She had already grieved the loss of her friend before hearing about her cousin's death. = 在听说表弟去世之前，她已经为失去朋友而悲伤了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
