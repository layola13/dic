rigorous:/ˈrɪɡərəs/ | adj. | 严格的；严密的；缜密的；艰苦的 |  demanding strict attention to accuracy and detail; extremely thorough, exhaustive, or accurate.  |例句：The research involved rigorous testing and analysis. 这项研究涉及严格的测试和分析。|例句：She followed a rigorous training regime to prepare for the marathon. 她遵循严格的训练制度来为马拉松做准备。|近义词：strict, severe, demanding, thorough, painstaking | 反义词：lax, lenient, careless, sloppy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The professor makes rigorous demands on his students. = 教授对他的学生要求严格。
He maintains a rigorous schedule for his work. = 他保持严格的工作时间表。

一般过去时:
The police conducted a rigorous investigation into the crime. = 警方对这起犯罪进行了严格的调查。
She underwent a rigorous selection process for the job. = 她经历了严格的招聘流程。

现在完成时:
Scientists have conducted rigorous experiments to prove their theory. = 科学家们已经进行了严格的实验来证明他们的理论。
She has maintained a rigorous exercise routine for years. = 她多年来一直保持着严格的锻炼习惯。

过去完成时:
They had completed a rigorous review of the document before submitting it. = 他们在提交文件之前已经完成了严格的审查。
He had followed a rigorous diet for months before the competition. = 他在比赛前几个月一直坚持严格的节食。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
源自拉丁语 *rigor-, *rigēre "to be stiff, to be cold," related to *reg-, *rect- "straight, right."  The sense of "severity" developed from the idea of inflexible strictness.
