nazca: /ˈnɑːskɑː/ | n. |纳斯卡 | The Nazca Lines are a series of ancient geoglyphs located in the Nazca Desert in southern Peru.  They depict various figures, including animals, plants, and geometric shapes, and their purpose remains a mystery.  The Nazca culture, which flourished from approximately 100 BCE to 800 CE, is known for its sophisticated pottery, textiles, and intricate irrigation systems. The Nazca Lines are a significant archaeological site and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. | The Nazca Lines are a remarkable feat of engineering and artistry. |近义词: Nazca culture, Nazca Desert |反义词:  (None directly applicable)
