occultations:/ˌɒkəlˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 遮蔽；掩蔽；隐匿 | The act of obscuring or hiding something, often referring to astronomical events where one celestial body passes in front of another.  It can also refer to the deliberate concealment of knowledge or information. | Example: The occultation of the star by the moon was visible from many parts of the Earth. 月球遮蔽恒星的现象在地球的许多地方都能看到。  The occultation of the truth was a deliberate act by the government.  政府故意隐瞒真相。 | 近义词：eclipses, concealments, obscurations | 反义词：revelations, disclosures |
