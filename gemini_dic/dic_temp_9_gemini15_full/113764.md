macropod:/ˈmækrəpɒd/| n. |大型有袋动物，袋鼠|A macropod is a marsupial mammal belonging to the family Macropodidae, characterized by its large hind legs, strong tail, and hopping gait.  They are found primarily in Australia and New Guinea.  Examples include kangaroos, wallabies, and pademelons.|The kangaroo is a well-known macropod.|近义词：marsupial (as a broader category)|反义词：无
