firmaments:/ˈfɜːrməmənts/ | n. |天空，苍穹 |The expanse of the heavens; the sky, especially when regarded as a divinely created vault.  |例句：The stars twinkled in the vast firmaments.  群星在广阔的天空中闪烁。

例句：The ancient astronomers charted the movements of the celestial bodies across the firmaments. 古代天文学家绘制了天体在天空中的运行轨迹。


近义词：heavens, sky, celestial sphere | 反义词：earth, ground | 词性：名词


词源:

The word "firmament" comes from the Old French *firmanent*, and ultimately from the Latin *firmamentum*, a noun of action from *firmare* "to strengthen, make firm," related to *firmus* "firm, strong."  The Latin word was used to describe the solid, apparently unmoving expanse of the sky in ancient cosmology.


用法说明:

虽然现在我们理解天空不是坚固的穹顶，但"firmament"仍然用于诗意或文学的语境中，来表达天空的广阔和庄严之感。  它很少在科学或日常生活中使用。

例句的时态分析不在本词条的范围内，因为"firmament"本身是一个名词，不涉及动词的时态变化。  例句仅用于展示其在不同语境下的用法。
