imposture: /ɪmˈpɒstʃər/ /ɪmˈpɑːstʃər/ | n. | 冒名顶替；欺骗；假冒 | The act of imposing on others; deception by assuming a false identity or character.  It often involves fraudulent claims to skill, knowledge, or identity. | 例句：He was found guilty of imposture after claiming to be a qualified doctor. 他被发现犯有冒名顶替罪，因为他自称是合格的医生。

His entire career was built on imposture. 他的整个职业生涯都是建立在欺骗之上的。


近义词：fraud, deception, sham, hoax, masquerade, pretense | 反义词：genuineness, authenticity, truth


词源:  From Middle French *imposture, from Old French imposture (14c.), from Latin impostura "a deception, impost," from impostus, past participle of imponere "to impose," from in- "upon" + ponere "to place."
