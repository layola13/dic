noncommunicating: /ˌnɒnkəˈmjuːnɪkeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 不沟通的；不通话的 | Not communicating; unable to communicate.  This adjective describes a situation or a person who is incapable of or not engaging in communication.  It often implies a lack of interaction or exchange of information. |

例句：

The patient was in a noncommunicating state after the accident.  事故后病人处于无法沟通的状态。

The two groups remained noncommunicating, despite attempts at mediation.  尽管尝试调解，这两个团体仍然没有沟通。


近义词：uncommunicative, incommunicative, silent, unresponsive

反义词：communicative, talkative, articulate


词源:

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of"
* **communicating**:  present participle of the verb "communicate," meaning "to convey information or feelings."  "Communicate" itself derives from Latin "communicare," meaning "to share," "to make common."


用法说明：

This word is primarily used as an adjective to describe a state or condition.  It's often used in medical contexts to describe patients who are unable to communicate due to injury, illness, or other factors.  It can also be used in a more general sense to describe situations where communication is absent or broken down.  There is no verb form of "noncommunicating".  It's a descriptive adjective only.
