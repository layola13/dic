preenacted: /priːˈæktɪd/ | v. |预演；预先表演；预先制定 | To rehearse or practice beforehand; to enact or perform something in advance.  |例句：The play was carefully pre-enacted before the opening night.  = 这部戏在首映前进行了仔细的预演。


The students pre-enacted the historical scene for their history project. = 学生们为他们的历史项目预演了这个历史场景。| 近义词：rehearse, practice, simulate,  | 反义词：improvise, ad-lib | 动词

词源:
The word "preenacted" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before" or "in advance") and the verb "enacted" (meaning "to perform or represent (a play, etc.)").  The root of "enact" lies in the Old French "enacter," ultimately derived from Latin roots suggesting "to put into action."
