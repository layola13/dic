hardland:/ˈhɑːrdlænd/ | n. | 核心地区；内地；腹地 | A hardland is the central or most important part of a country, region, or area, often associated with its traditional culture and values. It's typically less urbanized than coastal regions or other peripheries.  | Example: The American heartland is often associated with farming and traditional values. 美国的心脏地带通常与农业和传统价值观联系在一起。 |近义词：hinterland, heartland, interior |反义词：periphery, outskirts, fringe
