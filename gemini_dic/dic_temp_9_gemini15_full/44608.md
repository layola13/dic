crowded:/ˈkraʊdɪd/ | adj. |拥挤的，人满为患的 |Full of people; very busy.  The adjective "crowded" describes a place or situation where there are too many people or things, resulting in a lack of space or comfort. |例句：The beach was crowded with tourists. 海滩挤满了游客。

The train was so crowded that I couldn't find a seat. 火车太拥挤了，我找不到座位。

|近义词：packed, congested, full, thronged |反义词：empty, sparse, uncrowded |词性：形容词

This restaurant is always crowded on weekends. = 这家餐厅周末总是很拥挤。


一般过去时:
The concert was extremely crowded last night. = 昨晚音乐会非常拥挤。
The bus was crowded when I got on it. = 我上车时公共汽车很拥挤。


现在完成时:
The city has become increasingly crowded in recent years. = 近年来，这座城市变得越来越拥挤。
The park has been crowded all day. = 公园里一整天都很拥挤。


过去完成时:
The stadium had already been crowded before the game started. = 比赛开始前体育场就已经很拥挤了。

将来时:
The airport will be crowded during the holiday season. = 假期期间机场将非常拥挤。
The roads will be crowded with commuters tomorrow morning. = 明天早上道路将挤满通勤者。


虚拟语气:
If the streets weren't so crowded, I would walk to work. = 如果街道不那么拥挤，我会步行上班。
I wish the train wasn't so crowded during peak hours. = 我希望高峰时段火车不要那么拥挤。


被动语态:
The room was crowded with furniture. = 房间里塞满了家具。(被动语态更常用于描述空间被物体填满)
The streets were crowded by the protestors. = 街道被抗议者挤满了。(被动语态描述空间被某种力量填满)


疑问句:

否定句:

The store isn't usually crowded on weekdays. = 工作日商店通常不拥挤。

词源:
The word "crowded" comes from the verb "crowd," which ultimately derives from the Old French word "crode," meaning "a crowd" or "a throng."  The suffix "-ed" transforms the verb into its adjectival form, indicating a state of being crowded.
