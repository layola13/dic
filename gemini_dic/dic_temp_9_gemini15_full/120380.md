minionship: /ˈmɪnjənʃɪp/ | n. | 奴性，奴才地位，依附 | The state or condition of being a minion; subservience; obsequiousness.  It implies a lack of independence and a willingness to blindly follow the orders of a superior.  The term often carries a negative connotation, suggesting a lack of integrity or self-respect. | Example:  His minionship was evident in his unwavering obedience to the tyrannical ruler.  他的奴性在他对暴君的坚定服从中显而易见。 | 近义词: subservience, servility, obsequiousness, fawning | 反义词: independence, autonomy, self-reliance | 名词
