queuing: /ˈkjuːɪŋ/ | n. | 排队；排队等候 | The act of forming or standing in a line, usually to wait for something.  | 例句：The queuing time at the airport was excessively long. 机场排队时间过长。

例句：There was a long queue for the new iPhone. 新款iPhone的队伍很长。


近义词：lining up, standing in line, waiting in line | 反义词：None (antonyms are contextual, e.g., bypassing, skipping) | 词性：名词


词源:  From the verb "queue," which ultimately derives from the Old French word "queue," meaning "tail."  This reflects the image of a line of people following one another like a tail.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
People are queuing for the bus. = 人们正在排队等候公共汽车。
We queue for coffee every morning. = 我们每天早上都排队买咖啡。

一般过去时:
They queued patiently for hours. = 他们耐心地排了好几个小时的队。
We queued outside the cinema. = 我们在电影院外面排队。

现在完成时:
They have queued for the concert all day. = 他们为了这场音乐会排队等候了一整天。
I have queued online for tickets. = 我已经在网上排队购票了。

过去完成时:
We had queued for an hour before the shop opened. = 商店开门前我们已经排了一个小时的队了。
They had queued all night to get tickets. = 为了拿到票，他们排队等候了一整夜。

将来时:
We will be queuing for hours tomorrow. = 我们明天将排队等候数小时。
They will queue early to get a good seat. = 他们会早点排队以获得一个好座位。

虚拟语气:
If we had queued earlier, we would have gotten better seats. = 如果我们早点排队，就能拿到更好的座位。
I wish I hadn't queued for so long. = 我真希望我没有排了这么久的队。

被动语态:
The customers are being queued efficiently. = 客户正在被有效地安排排队。
The tickets were queued for processing. = 票务正在排队等待处理。

疑问句:
Are you queuing for the movie? = 你在排队看电影吗？
How long have they been queuing? = 他们排队多久了？

否定句:
We didn't queue for the concert. = 我们没有排队去看音乐会。
They don't usually queue for buses. = 他们通常不排队等候公共汽车。
