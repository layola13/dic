consorts:/ˈkɒnsɔːts/| /ˈkɑːnsɔːrts/| n. v.| 配偶；伙伴；和谐相处 |  A consort is a spouse, especially a queen or prince's spouse.  It can also refer to a companion or associate, or to the act of associating harmoniously. |例句：The queen consort sat beside the king. 王后坐在国王身旁。

The musicians consorted to create a beautiful melody. 音乐家们协奏创造出一首优美的旋律。

|近义词：spouse, partner, companion, associate |反义词：enemy, opponent, adversary|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He consorts with the wealthy and powerful. 他与富人和权贵交往。
She consorts with her friends regularly. 她定期与朋友交往。


一般过去时:
They consorted together in secret. 他们秘密地在一起交往。
The king consorted with his advisors before making a decision. 国王在做决定之前与他的顾问们商议。


现在完成时:
She has consorted with many influential people throughout her career.  她职业生涯中结识了许多有影响力的人。
They have consorted to achieve their common goal. 他们为了达到共同的目标而合作。


过去完成时:
He had consorted with criminals before his arrest. 他在被捕前曾与罪犯交往。
The band had consorted for years before releasing their first album. 乐队在发行第一张专辑之前已经合作多年了。


将来时:
She will consort with the ambassador tomorrow. 她明天将与大使会面。
They will consort to plan their next move. 他们将协商计划他们的下一步行动。


虚拟语气:
If she consorted with him, she might gain an advantage. 如果她与他交往，她可能会获得优势。
If they had consorted earlier, they might have succeeded. 如果他们早点合作，他们也许会成功。


被动语态:
He was consorted with by many during his campaign. 在他的竞选期间，许多人与他交往。
The decision was made after the king had consorted with his advisors.  在国王与顾问商议后，做出了决定。


疑问句:
Does he consort with known criminals? 他是否与已知的罪犯交往？
Will they consort to solve the problem? 他们会合作解决这个问题吗？


否定句:
He does not consort with people he doesn't trust. 他不与他不信任的人交往。
They did not consort with the enemy. 他们没有与敌人勾结。


PHRASE->
consort with: 与...交往
royal consort: 王室配偶
consort ship:  （罕用）同伙关系；共谋
consort  together:  一起合作
consort to:  为了...而合作


词源:
源自古法语 *consorter，意为“聚集在一起”，来自拉丁语 consors，意为“伙伴，同伴”，由 com- (“together”) 和 sors (“lot, fate”) 组合而成。  因此，其核心含义是“分享命运”，延伸到“一起工作，交往”等含义。
