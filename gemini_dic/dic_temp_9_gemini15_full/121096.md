miscreancy: /ˈmɪskrɪənsi/ | n. | 恶行；不法行为；邪恶 | Wicked behavior; wrongdoing; villainy.  It refers to acts of wickedness or wrongdoing, often implying a disregard for moral principles or laws. | The miscreancy of the former CEO cost the company millions.  前首席执行官的恶行使公司损失数百万美元。


The miscreancy of the regime led to widespread unrest.  该政权的恶行导致了广泛的动荡。

近义词：wickedness, wrongdoing, villainy, depravity, iniquity

反义词：righteousness, virtue, goodness, rectitude


词源:  The word "miscreancy" is derived from the Old French word "mescreance," which itself comes from the Old French "mescreant," meaning "misbeliever" or "heretic."  The evolution reflects a shift from religious connotations (lack of faith) to a broader sense of moral wrongdoing.
