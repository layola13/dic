confounds:/kənˈfaʊndz/ | vt. |使困惑；使混淆；证明…是错误的 | to cause surprise or confusion to someone, or to make something more difficult to understand  | The complexity of the instructions confounded me.  指令的复杂性让我困惑。 | confuses, perplexes, baffles | clarifies, explains | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The noise confounds the students. = 噪音使学生们困惑。
This evidence confounds the theory. = 这证据驳斥了这个理论。

一般过去时:
The magician confounded the audience with his tricks. = 魔术师用他的戏法迷惑了观众。
The sudden change of plans confounded everyone. = 计划的突然改变使每个人都感到困惑。

现在完成时:
The new evidence has confounded the investigators. = 新证据使调查人员困惑不解。
The complexity of the problem has confounded experts for years. = 这个问题的复杂性多年来一直困扰着专家们。

过去完成时:
The unexpected results had confounded the scientists' predictions. = 出乎意料的结果使科学家的预测落空。
They had been confounded by the sheer scale of the problem. = 他们被问题的巨大规模所难倒。

将来时:
The new regulations will confound many businesses. = 新规章将使许多企业困惑。
The rapid technological advancements will confound many people.  快速的科技进步将使许多人困惑。

虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, it would not confound the jury. = 如果证据更充分，它就不会让陪审团困惑。
I wish this confusing information would not confound me so much. = 我希望这些令人困惑的信息不会让我如此困惑。

被动语态:
The students were confounded by the difficult exam. = 学生们被困难的考试难住了。
The theory was confounded by new research findings. = 该理论被新的研究结果推翻。

疑问句:
What confounded the detective in this case? = 在此案中是什么让侦探困惑？
Does the complexity of the instructions confound you? = 指令的复杂性让你困惑吗？

否定句:
The clear instructions did not confound the workers. = 清晰的指示并没有让工人们困惑。
The simple explanation did not confound the children. = 简洁的解释并没有让孩子们困惑。
