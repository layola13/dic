circumscribed:/ˈsɜːrkəmˌskraɪbd/ | adj. v. |限制的；限定的；在…周围画线；划定界限 |

英文解析:
As an adjective, circumscribed means restricted within limits; confined. As a verb, it means to draw a line around; to enclose; to limit or restrict.  The word often implies a constraint or limitation, suggesting something is smaller or less than it could potentially be.

例句:
The doctor's options were circumscribed by the patient's allergies. = 医生的选择受到病人过敏的限制。

The artist circumscribed the drawing with a bold black line. = 艺术家用粗黑线勾勒出图画的轮廓。


近义词:
limited, confined, restricted, bounded, delimited, defined, constrained

反义词:
unlimited, unrestricted, boundless, open-ended, expansive


词源:
From Middle English *circumscriben, from Latin circumscribere "to write around, to define the limits of," from circum- "around" + scribere "to write."  The original sense was literally to draw a line around something.  The figurative sense of limiting or restricting developed later.
