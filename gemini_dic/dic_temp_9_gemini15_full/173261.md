quasi-consequentially:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ kɒnsɪˈkwɛnʃəli/ | adv. | 几乎必然地；近乎必然地 |  Describes something that is almost certainly a consequence or result, but not definitively or directly. It implies a strong likelihood of a causal relationship but acknowledges a degree of uncertainty or indirectness. | Example: Quasi-consequentially, the rise in unemployment led to an increase in crime rates. 几乎必然地，失业率上升导致犯罪率上升。 | 近义词：virtually consequentially, almost inevitably, nearly certainly | 反义词：inconsequentially, coincidentally, unrelatedly


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't have tense conjugations like verbs.  Its usage is primarily in describing a consequence or outcome.  The tense of the main verb in the sentence determines the overall tense.

Example 1 (Present Tense): The company's poor management is quasi-consequentially leading to financial difficulties. 公司的糟糕管理几乎必然地导致了财务困难。

Example 2 (Past Tense): The heavy rain quasi-consequentially resulted in widespread flooding. 大雨几乎必然地导致了大范围洪水。

Example 3 (Future Tense):  The new regulations will quasi-consequentially reduce pollution levels. 新规几乎必然地会降低污染水平。


PHRASE->
There are no common set phrases using "quasi-consequentially". Its use is typically within a sentence to modify a verb.
