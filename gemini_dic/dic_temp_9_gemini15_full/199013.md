signal:/ˈsɪɡnəl/ | n., v. |信号；标志；示意；表明 | A signal is something that conveys information, a sign or indication.  It can be a sound, light, gesture, or other means of communication. As a verb, it means to communicate or indicate something using a signal. |例句：The traffic light signaled green. 交通信号灯显示绿灯。

The sailor signaled for help. 水手发出求救信号。

近义词：sign, indication, mark, symptom, cue |反义词：silence, obscurity, stillness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He signals his approval. = 他示意同意。
The light signals danger. = 灯光发出危险信号。


一般过去时:
She signaled to her friend. = 她向她的朋友示意。
The ship signaled its position. = 船只发出了位置信号。


现在完成时:
They have signaled their intention to leave. = 他们已经表示了要离开的意图。
He has signaled a change in policy. = 他已经暗示了政策的改变。


过去完成时:
The train had signaled its arrival before it stopped. = 火车在停下来之前已经发出到达信号了。
She had signaled her dissatisfaction earlier. = 她之前就已经表示了不满。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
