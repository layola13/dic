henries:/ˈhɛnriz/| n. |亨利（电感单位）|The henry (symbol: H) is the SI derived unit of electrical inductance.  It is named after Joseph Henry, who discovered self-inductance independently of Michael Faraday. One henry is defined as the inductance that induces one volt in a conductor when the current through the conductor changes at a rate of one ampere per second.|The inductor has an inductance of 10 henries. 这个电感器具有10亨利的电感。|farad, ohm|—|
