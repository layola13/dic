hyper:/ˈhaɪpər/ | adj. |超过的；极度的；活跃的；高血压的 | exceeding the usual, normal, or expected limits; excessively; characterized by abnormally high activity; relating to or affected by high blood pressure  |例句：He's hyper about the new game. 他对这款新游戏非常兴奋。  |近义词：excessive, extreme, over, heightened |反义词：subnormal, hypo-, low


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The child is hyperactive today. = 这个孩子今天特别好动。
He is hyper-sensitive to criticism. = 他对批评非常敏感。


一般过去时:
She was hyper focused on her work. = 她当时非常专注于她的工作。
The patient was hypertensive after the surgery. = 术后病人血压很高。


现在完成时:
He has been hyper since he drank that coffee. = 他自从喝了那杯咖啡后就一直很亢奋。
She has become hyper-aware of her surroundings. = 她对周围环境变得格外警觉。


过去完成时:
They had been hyper-vigilant throughout the night. = 他们整夜都高度警惕。
The pressure had been hyperbaric in the deep-sea submersible. = 深海潜水器内的压力一直是超压的。


将来时:
The market will be hyper-competitive next year. = 明年市场竞争将非常激烈。
He will be hyper-alert during the exam. = 考试期间他会高度警惕。


虚拟语气:
If he were hyper-focused, he would finish the project on time. = 如果他足够专注，他就能按时完成项目。
I wish I weren't so hyper right now. = 我希望我现在不要这么兴奋。


被动语态:
The data was hyper-analyzed by the research team. = 数据被研究团队进行了超详细的分析。
The patient is being hyper-oxygenated in the ICU. = 病人在重症监护室接受高氧治疗。


疑问句:

否定句:
He is not hyper today. = 他今天不亢奋。
The response was not hyper-reactive. = 反应不是过度活跃的。
