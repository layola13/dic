monolithic:/ˌmɒnəˈlɪθɪk/ /ˌmɑːnəˈlɪθɪk/| adj. |整体的；巨石的；庞大的；单一的|consisting of or formed into a single, massive, indivisible structure.  Often used to describe systems or organizations that are difficult to change or adapt because of their size and complexity.|The company's monolithic structure hindered innovation. 这家公司庞大的结构阻碍了创新。|近义词：unified, integral, indivisible, massive|反义词：fragmented, decentralized, diverse


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The system is monolithic. = 系统是整体的。
This organization remains monolithic in its approach. = 这个组织在方法上仍然是单一的。

一般过去时:
The software was monolithic and difficult to maintain. = 该软件是整体的，难以维护。
Their strategy proved to be monolithic and inflexible. = 他们的策略被证明是整体的且缺乏弹性。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
