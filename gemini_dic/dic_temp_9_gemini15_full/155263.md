phalarope:/ˈfæləroʊp/| n.|滨鹬|A small, slender shorebird of the genus *Phalaropus*, characterized by its distinctive swimming and feeding habits.  Phalaropes are known for their reversed sexual roles, with the females being more brightly colored and aggressively competing for mates. They are also skilled swimmers, often spinning on the water's surface to feed.|例句：The red phalarope is a beautiful bird with striking plumage.|近义词：sandpiper, stint|反义词:None.
