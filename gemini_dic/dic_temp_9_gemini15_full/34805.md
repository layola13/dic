climbing: /ˈklaɪmɪŋ/ | n. |攀登；攀爬 | The act of ascending, usually a steep surface.  It can refer to the activity itself or the sport of ascending mountains or rocks. |例句：Mountain climbing is a challenging but rewarding activity. 山地攀登是一项充满挑战但又令人欣慰的活动。

例句：He enjoys rock climbing as a hobby. 他喜欢攀岩作为一项爱好。


近义词：ascending, scaling, mounting | 反义词：descending


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He climbs mountains every weekend. 他每个周末都爬山。
She is climbing the ladder to success. 她正在攀登成功的阶梯。

一般过去时:
They climbed the highest peak last year. 他们去年攀登了最高峰。
He climbed the tree to get the ball. 他爬上树去拿球。

现在完成时:
I have climbed Mount Kilimanjaro. 我已经攀登了乞力马扎罗山。
She has been climbing for five years. 她已经攀岩五年了。

过去完成时:
They had climbed the mountain before the storm hit. 暴风雨来临之前他们已经爬上了山。
We had climbed halfway up the cliff when we realized it was too dangerous. 当我们意识到太危险时，我们已经爬到悬崖的一半了。

将来时:
We will climb the Matterhorn next summer. 我们明年夏天将攀登马特洪峰。
They will be climbing the rock face tomorrow. 他们明天将攀登岩壁。

虚拟语气:
If I were younger, I would climb that mountain. 如果我年轻一些，我会爬那座山。
I wish I could climb Everest. 我希望我能攀登珠穆朗玛峰。

被动语态:
The mountain was climbed by many adventurers. 那座山被许多冒险家攀登过。
The wall is being climbed by a skilled climber. 墙壁正在被一位熟练的攀登者攀登。

疑问句:
Are you climbing the stairs? 你在爬楼梯吗？
Is he climbing the rope? 他在爬绳子吗？

否定句:
They didn't climb the mountain. 他们没有爬山。
She doesn't climb rocks. 她不攀岩。
