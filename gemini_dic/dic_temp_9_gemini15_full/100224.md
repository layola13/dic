interrogatives:/ɪnˈtɛrəɡətɪvz/| n.|疑问词；疑问句|Interrogatives are words or phrases used to ask questions.  They are a crucial part of forming questions in various languages, including English.  They function as question words, determining the type of information sought.  Examples include "what," "who," "where," "when," "why," and "how."|The interrogatives in this sentence are "who" and "what". Who wrote this sentence? What is the meaning of this sentence?|近义词：question words, interrogatory words|反义词：declaratives (陈述句)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
What is your name? = 你叫什么名字？

Who are you? = 你是谁？


一般过去时:
What did you do yesterday? = 你昨天做了什么？

Where did you go? = 你去了哪里？


现在完成时:
What have you eaten today? = 你今天吃了什么？

Who has seen the movie? = 谁看过这部电影？


过去完成时:
What had you done before you called me? = 你在我打电话之前做了什么？

Where had they gone before the accident? = 事故发生前他们去了哪里？


将来时:
What will you do tomorrow? = 你明天打算做什么？

When will the meeting be held? = 会议什么时候举行？


虚拟语气:
If I knew what to say, I would say it. = 如果我知道该说什么，我会说的。

I wish I knew who had done it. = 我真希望我知道是谁干的。


被动语态:
What was said at the meeting? = 会议上说了什么？

Who was invited to the party? = 谁被邀请参加聚会了？


疑问句:
What time is it? = 现在几点？

Who is calling? = 谁在打电话？


否定句:
I don't know what to do. = 我不知道该做什么。

I didn't see who it was. = 我没看见是谁。


PHRASE->
what if = 如果…怎么样

who knows = 谁知道

wherever you go = 无论你去哪里

how about = ……怎么样

when possible =  如果可能的话
