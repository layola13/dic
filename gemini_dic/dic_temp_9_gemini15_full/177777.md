reced:/rɪˈsiːd/ | vi. | 退去；减弱；衰退 | To move back or away from a previous position; to become less intense or noticeable; to withdraw or retreat. |例句：The flood waters finally receded. 洪水最终退去了。The pain gradually receded. 疼痛逐渐减轻了。|近义词：withdraw, retreat, subside, abate |反义词：advance, increase, intensify


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The tide recedes twice a day. = 潮汐一天退去两次。
His influence is slowly receding. = 他的影响力正在慢慢减弱。


一般过去时:
The army receded from the border. = 军队从边境撤军了。
The symptoms receded after a few days. = 几天后症状消失了。


现在完成时:
The flood waters have receded. = 洪水已经退去了。
His anger has receded since then. = 从那以后他的怒气消退了。


过去完成时:
The pain had receded before the doctor arrived. = 在医生到达之前疼痛已经减轻了。
The army had receded before the reinforcements arrived. = 增援部队到达之前，军队已经撤退了。


将来时:
The swelling will recede in a few days. = 肿胀将在几天内消退。
Hopefully, the tension will recede after the meeting. = 希望会议结束后紧张气氛会缓和。


虚拟语气:
If the tide receded earlier, we could have gone to the beach. = 如果潮水早点退去，我们就可以去海滩了。
If the symptoms receded quickly, he would have recovered sooner. = 如果症状很快消退，他就会恢复得更快。


被动语态:
(This verb is rarely used in the passive voice because it is intransitive.)  The passive voice is grammatically possible but unnatural.


疑问句:
Is the flood receding yet? = 洪水退了吗？
Has the inflammation receded? = 炎症消退了吗？


否定句:
The flood didn't recede for several days. = 洪水好几天都没退。
The pain hasn't receded despite the medication. = 尽管服药了，疼痛也没有减轻。
