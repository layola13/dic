capex:/ˈkæpɛks/ | n. |资本支出 |Capital expenditure; the money a company spends on acquiring or upgrading physical assets such as property, plant, and equipment (PP&E).  This is in contrast to operational expenditure (OPEX), which covers day-to-day running costs. |The company's capex budget for next year is $10 million. 这家公司明年的资本支出预算为1000万美元。|OPEX, investment |operating expenditure |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Our company's capex is high this year. = 我们公司今年的资本支出很高。
Their capex plan includes new machinery. = 他们的资本支出计划包括购买新机器。


一般过去时:
Last year's capex was significantly lower. = 去年的资本支出明显较低。
The capex for the project exceeded expectations. = 该项目的资本支出超过预期。


现在完成时:
We have allocated significant funds to capex this quarter. = 本季度我们已拨出大量资金用于资本支出。
They have completed most of their planned capex. = 他们已经完成了大部分计划中的资本支出。


过去完成时:
Before the expansion, capex had been minimal. = 在扩张之前，资本支出一直很少。
By the end of the year, they had spent all their allocated capex. = 到年底，他们已经花光了所有分配的资本支出。


将来时:
Next year's capex will focus on infrastructure improvements. = 明年的资本支出将重点放在基础设施改善上。
We will increase our capex to support growth. = 我们将增加资本支出以支持增长。


虚拟语气:
If we had more capex, we could expand faster. = 如果我们有更多的资本支出，我们可以更快地扩张。
I wish we had more flexibility in our capex budget. = 我希望我们的资本支出预算有更大的灵活性。


被动语态:
The capex budget was approved by the board. = 资本支出预算已获得董事会批准。
A large portion of the capex is being used for R&D. = 大部分资本支出用于研发。


疑问句:
What is the total capex for this project? = 这个项目的总资本支出是多少？
How much capex will be needed for the new factory? = 新工厂需要多少资本支出？


否定句:
The company did not have sufficient capex for the expansion. = 公司没有足够的资本支出用于扩张。
There was no capex allocated for marketing this year. = 今年没有为市场营销分配资本支出。
