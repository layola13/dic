rouses: /ˈraʊzɪz/ | v. | 唤醒；激起；引起；鼓动 | To wake someone from sleep; to stir up or excite; to provoke or incite. |  例句：The loud noise roused me from my sleep.  = 嘈杂的声音把我从睡梦中吵醒。

The news roused public anger. = 这则新闻激起了公众的愤怒。


近义词：awaken, stir, excite, incite, awaken, arouse | 反义词：calm, soothe, quiet


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rooster rouses the farmer every morning. = 公鸡每天早上都叫醒农民。
The speech rouses the audience to action. = 这篇演讲激励听众采取行动。

一般过去时:
The sudden noise roused the sleeping child. = 突然的噪音吵醒了熟睡的孩子。
The detective's words roused suspicion in the witness. = 侦探的话在证人心中引起了怀疑。

现在完成时:
The scandal has roused much controversy. = 这起丑闻引发了很多争议。
The new policy has roused considerable public debate. = 新政策激起了大量的公众辩论。

过去完成时:
The ringing of the alarm clock had roused her from a deep sleep. = 闹钟的铃声把她从沉睡中吵醒。
The previous events had roused the community to protest. = 先前的事件激起了社区的抗议。

将来时:

The announcement will rouse public interest. = 这则公告将引起公众的兴趣。

虚拟语气:
If the music were louder, it would rouse the sleeping dancers. = 如果音乐再响亮一些，它就能唤醒沉睡的舞者。
I wish the speech had roused the audience more. = 我希望演讲能更能激励听众。

被动语态:
He was roused from his sleep by a loud bang. = 他被一声巨响吵醒。
The crowd was roused to fury by the injustice. = 大众被这非正义的行为激怒了。

疑问句:

否定句:
The gentle music did not rouse the baby from its sleep. = 轻柔的音乐并没有把婴儿从睡梦中吵醒。
Nothing could rouse him from his depression. = 什么也无法使他摆脱沮丧。
