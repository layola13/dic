muddle's:/ˈmʌd(ə)l/| n. v.|混乱；笨手笨脚地做事；使混乱；|英文解析：A muddle is a state of confusion or disorder.  As a verb, it means to mix things up in a confused way, or to do something in a clumsy or ineffective way.  The possessive 's' indicates belonging to or relating to a muddle. |例句：His desk was a complete muddle. 他的桌子乱七八糟。 He muddled his way through the exam. 他笨手笨脚地应付考试。|近义词：mess, jumble, confusion, disorder|反义词：order, organization, clarity
