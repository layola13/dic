rivers:/ˈrɪvərz/| n. |河流；江河 |Streams of water flowing in natural channels to the sea or to lakes. Rivers are vital for ecosystems, transportation, and human settlements.  |例句：The Amazon River is the largest river in the world. 亚马逊河是世界上最大的河流。

例句：Many ancient civilizations flourished along the banks of major rivers. 许多古代文明都繁荣于主要河流的河岸。


近义词：streams, currents


反义词：None (no direct antonym, but could consider "desert" as a contrasting geographical feature)


词性：名词

词源:
The word "river" comes from the Old English word "rīfere," which is of Germanic origin.  It's related to other Germanic words meaning "stream" or "current."  The ultimate origin is Proto-Germanic *rīwaz.
