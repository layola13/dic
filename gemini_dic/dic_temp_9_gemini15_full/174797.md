race:/reɪs/ /res/| n., v.|种族；比赛；速度；竞赛 |a group of people sharing the same culture, history, language, etc.; a contest of speed; to move quickly; to compete in a race|例句：The human race has a long history. 人类历史悠久。  He won the race. 他赢得了比赛。|近义词：competition, contest,  ethnicity,  group |反义词：walk, stroll


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He races every weekend. = 他每个周末都参加比赛。
They race cars for a living. = 他们靠赛车为生。


一般过去时:
She raced to the finish line. = 她冲向终点线。
We raced against the clock. = 我们争分夺秒。


现在完成时:
He has raced in many marathons. = 他参加过许多马拉松比赛。
They have raced against each other many times. = 他们彼此比赛过很多次。


过去完成时:
He had raced before, so he knew what to expect. = 他以前参加过比赛，所以他知道会发生什么。
She had raced all day, so she was exhausted. = 她整天都在比赛，所以她筋疲力尽了。


将来时:
We will race tomorrow. = 我们明天将比赛。
They will race against the best in the world. = 他们将与世界最佳选手比赛。


虚拟语气:
If I were faster, I would win the race. = 如果我更快，我就会赢得比赛。
I wish I had raced better. = 我希望我比赛表现更好。


被动语态:
The race was won by a newcomer. = 比赛由一位新人赢得。
The cars were raced on a challenging track. = 汽车在一条具有挑战性的赛道上比赛。


疑问句:
When will the race start? = 比赛什么时候开始？
Who won the race? = 谁赢得了比赛？


否定句:
He didn't race yesterday. = 他昨天没有比赛。
They don't race often. = 他们不经常比赛。

词源:
The word "race" ultimately derives from the Old French word "race," meaning "lineage," "breed," or "generation."  This in turn comes from the Latin word "radix," meaning "root."  The sense of "speed contest" developed later.
