kibbutz:/kɪˈbʊts/| n. |基布兹（以色列集体农场）|A kibbutz is a collective community in Israel, characterized by shared ownership of the means of production and communal living.  Members typically contribute their labor to the kibbutz and share in the profits.  Kibbutzim played a significant role in the development of Israel's agriculture and economy.|例句：Many kibbutzim have diversified their economies beyond agriculture, incorporating industrial and technological enterprises.|近义词：commune, collective|反义词：capitalist enterprise
