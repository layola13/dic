crass:/kræs/| adj.|粗俗的；粗鲁的；愚蠢的；粗糙的 |lacking sensitivity, refinement, or intelligence|His crass remarks offended many people. 他粗俗的言论冒犯了许多人。|近义词：vulgar, rude, insensitive, boorish|反义词：refined, cultured, sensitive, polite

USAGE->
一般现在时:
His behavior is crass. = 他的行为很粗俗。
The joke is crass and insensitive. = 这个笑话粗俗且缺乏同情心。

一般过去时:
Her comments were crass and inappropriate. = 她的评论粗俗且不恰当。
He made a crass mistake. = 他犯了一个愚蠢的错误。

现在完成时:
He has been crass in his dealings with others. = 他在与他人交往中一直很粗鲁。
They have shown crass disregard for the feelings of others. = 他们对别人的感受表现出极度的漠视。

过去完成时:
He had been crass in his previous interactions. = 他在之前的互动中一直很粗俗。
She had made a crass remark that embarrassed everyone. = 她说过一句粗俗的评论，让每个人都感到尴尬。

将来时:
He will likely be crass in his response. = 他很可能在回应中表现得很粗俗。
Their behavior will likely be crass and offensive. = 他们的行为很可能会粗俗和冒犯人。

虚拟语气:
If he were less crass, he'd have more friends. = 如果他不那么粗俗，他就会有更多朋友。
I wish he weren't so crass. = 我希望他不那么粗俗。

被动语态:
His actions were considered crass by many. = 许多人认为他的行为很粗俗。
His insensitivity was described as crass. = 他的不敏感被描述为粗俗的。

疑问句:
Is his behavior crass? = 他的行为粗俗吗？
Wasn't his comment rather crass? = 他的评论是不是相当粗俗？


否定句:
His behavior was not crass, just thoughtless. = 他的行为并非粗俗，只是欠考虑。
It was not a crass remark, but a simple misunderstanding. = 这不是粗俗的评论，而是一个简单的误会。

词源:
From Middle Dutch *craes, from Old Dutch *krās, from Proto-Germanic *kraz- (“gravel, grit”).  The meaning shifted from something rough and coarse in texture to something coarse in manners or behavior.
