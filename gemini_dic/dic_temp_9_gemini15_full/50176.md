deontological:/ˌdiːɒnˈtɒlədʒɪkəl/| adj.| 道义论的；本份论的 | Relating to or denoting the theory or system of ethics that judges the morality of an action by the action's adherence to rules and duties, regardless of its consequences.  | Example:  The deontological approach to ethics emphasizes duty and rules, irrespective of outcomes.  道义论方法强调责任和规则，而不考虑结果。

The deontological theory is often contrasted with consequentialism.  道义论理论通常与结果论形成对比。| 近义词：duty-based, rule-based | 反义词：consequentialist, teleological
