moraine:/məˈreɪn/| n. |冰碛；冰川沉积物 |A moraine is an accumulation of unconsolidated glacial debris (soil, rocks, etc.) that has been transported by a glacier and deposited along its margins or at its terminus.  Moraines can be various shapes and sizes, ranging from small mounds to massive ridges. They are valuable indicators of past glacial activity. | Example: The valley was filled with a large lateral moraine left behind by the retreating glacier.  这条山谷里堆满了冰川消退后留下的巨大的侧碛。|近义词：glacial deposit, till |反义词：无
