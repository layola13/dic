preobservations: /ˌpriːˌɒbzərˈveɪʃənz/ | n. | 预先观察；初步观察 | Observations made before a formal experiment or study begins.  They provide a baseline against which to compare later findings. | Example: The preobservations indicated a stable ecosystem before the introduction of the new species. 预先观察表明，在新物种引入之前，生态系统是稳定的。 | 近义词： preliminary observations, initial findings, prior assessments | 反义词： postobservations, final results | 名词
