quantum: /ˈkwɑːntəm/| n. |量子，量；一定数量| a discrete quantity of energy proportional in magnitude to the frequency of the radiation it represents.  It is the fundamental unit of energy in quantum physics. |例句：The theory of quantum mechanics revolutionized our understanding of the universe. 量子力学理论彻底改变了我们对宇宙的理解。  The experiment measured the quantum of energy emitted by the atom. 实验测量了原子发射的能量量子。|近义词：unit, amount, portion |反义词：continuum, whole

词源:

The word "quantum" derives from the Latin word "quantus," meaning "how much" or "how great."  Its usage in physics reflects this original meaning, referring to a discrete, measurable amount of something, specifically energy in the context of quantum mechanics.
