discovery:/dɪˈskʌvəri/ | n. |发现；发觉；被发现的事物 |The act of discovering something; something that has been discovered.  |例句：The discovery of penicillin revolutionized medicine. 青霉素的发现彻底改变了医学。|例句：Archaeologists made a significant discovery at the dig site. 考古学家在发掘现场有了重大发现。|近义词：invention, finding, uncovering |反义词：concealment, hiding, loss


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The scientist discovers new species. = 科学家发现了新的物种。
We discover new things every day. = 我们每天都会发现新事物。

一般过去时:
Columbus discovered America in 1492. = 哥伦布于1492年发现了美洲。
They discovered a hidden cave. = 他们发现了一个隐藏的洞穴。

现在完成时:
Scientists have discovered a new planet. = 科学家们发现了一颗新的行星。
She has discovered her talent for painting. = 她发现了自己绘画的天赋。

过去完成时:
They had discovered the truth before the trial. = 审判前他们就已经发现了真相。
I had discovered my mistake before submitting the report. = 我在提交报告之前就发现了自己的错误。

将来时:
We will discover many secrets of the universe. = 我们将发现宇宙的许多秘密。
They will discover the answer soon. = 他们很快就会发现答案。

虚拟语气:
If I had discovered the problem earlier, I could have fixed it. = 如果我早点发现这个问题，我就能解决了。
I wish I had discovered this sooner. = 我真希望我早点发现这个。

被动语态:
The truth was discovered by the police. = 真相被警方发现了。
A new species has been discovered in the Amazon rainforest. = 亚马逊雨林中发现了一种新的物种。

疑问句:
When was penicillin discovered? = 青霉素是什么时候被发现的？
What did they discover in the cave? = 他们在洞穴里发现了什么？

否定句:
They did not discover any evidence. = 他们没有发现任何证据。
We haven't discovered the cause of the problem yet. = 我们还没有发现问题的起因。
