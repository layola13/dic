seljuks:/ˈsɛldʒʊks/| n. |塞尔柱克人，塞尔柱克王朝|The Seljuks were a powerful Oghuz Turkic dynasty that played a significant role in the Islamic world during the 11th and 12th centuries.  They conquered vast territories across Persia, Anatolia, and Syria, establishing a major empire.  Their influence on art, architecture, and politics was substantial.|The Seljuk Empire significantly impacted the course of history in the Middle East.|近义词:Turks (in this historical context), Oghuz|反义词:None directly applicable.
