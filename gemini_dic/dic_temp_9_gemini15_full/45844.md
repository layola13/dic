cut:/kʌt/| vt., n.|切割，剪，砍；削减；伤口|To separate or divide something with a sharp-edged instrument or tool; a wound produced by cutting.|例句：He cut the bread with a knife. 他用刀切面包。  He got a deep cut on his arm. 他的手臂上被割了一道深口子。|近义词：sever, slice, trim, chop |反义词：join, mend, heal


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I cut the vegetables every day. = 我每天都切菜。
He cuts the grass on weekends. = 他周末割草。


一般过去时:
She cut the cake into eight pieces. = 她把蛋糕切成了八块。
They cut down the old tree. = 他们砍倒了那棵老树。


现在完成时:
I have cut myself while shaving. = 我刮胡子时割伤了自己。
She has cut her hair short. = 她把头发剪短了。


过去完成时:
He had cut the wire before the accident happened. = 事故发生前他已经剪断了电线。
They had cut the budget before the meeting. = 会议前他们已经削减了预算。


将来时:
I will cut the pizza into slices. = 我会把披萨切成片。
She will cut the ribbon at the opening ceremony. = 她将在开幕式上剪彩。


虚拟语气:
If I had a sharper knife, I would cut the meat more easily. = 如果我有一把更锋利的刀，我会更容易切肉。
I wish I hadn’t cut myself. = 我真希望我没有割伤自己。


被动语态:
The cake was cut into pieces. = 蛋糕被切成了几块。
The budget has been cut drastically. = 预算被大幅削减了。


疑问句:
Did you cut your finger? = 你割到手指了吗？
Have you ever cut your hair yourself? = 你有没有自己剪过头发？


否定句:
I didn't cut the cake. = 我没有切蛋糕。
He hasn't cut the grass yet. = 他还没割草。

词源:
The word "cut" comes from the Old English word *cutt, meaning "a cut, a piece cut off." This word has Germanic roots and is related to other words in Germanic languages with similar meanings.
