polypidom:/ˈpɒlɪpɪdɒm/| n. |群体珊瑚骨骼|A polypidom is the hard, skeletal structure formed by a colony of polyps, typically in corals or bryozoans.  It's the collective exoskeleton of the colony, often intricate and complex in shape.  The individual polyps live within or on this structure. |Example: The divers marveled at the vast, branching polypidom of the black coral.  潜水员们惊叹于黑珊瑚巨大的分枝状群体骨骼。 The delicate polypidom of the bryozoan colony was easily damaged.  苔藓虫群体的精致群体骨骼很容易被损坏。|近义词：colony skeleton, corallum (for corals)|反义词：N/A|名词
