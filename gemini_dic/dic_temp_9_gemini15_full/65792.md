evicting:/ɪˈvɪktɪŋ/| v. |驱逐，逐出 |The act of forcing someone to leave a property they are occupying, typically due to a breach of contract or non-payment of rent.  |例句：The landlord is evicting the tenants for non-payment of rent. 房东因租金未付而驱逐租户。|例句：They were evicted from their apartment after a dispute with the landlord. 他们在与房东发生争执后被赶出了公寓。|近义词：expelling, ousting, ejecting |反义词：housing, accommodating


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The landlord is evicting the tenants. = 房东正在驱逐房客。
They are evicting him from the building. = 他们正在将他从大楼里赶出去。

一般过去时:
The police evicted the protesters. = 警察驱逐了抗议者。
She was evicted from her home last week. = 她上周被赶出了家。

现在完成时:
The court has already evicted them. = 法院已经驱逐了他们。
They have been evicted multiple times. = 他们已经被驱逐多次了。

过去完成时:
He had been evicted before, so he knew what to expect. = 他以前被驱逐过，所以他知道该期待什么。
