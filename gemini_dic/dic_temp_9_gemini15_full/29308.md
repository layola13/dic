castellated:/ˌkæstɪˈleɪtɪd/| adj.| 城垛状的；有雉堞的 | Having battlements or turrets; resembling a castle.  The word evokes images of medieval castles with their characteristic crenellated walls.  |例句：The castellated walls of the old castle stood defiant against the ravages of time.  古老城堡的城垛状城墙抵挡住了时间的侵蚀。

例句：The architect designed a castellated building to evoke a sense of medieval grandeur. 建筑师设计了一座城垛状建筑，以唤起中世纪的宏伟感。


近义词：crenellated, turreted, fortified


反义词：plain, smooth, unwalled


词源：From Old French *castelé, past participle of casteler "to fortify," from castel "castle," from Vulgar Latin castellum, diminutive of Latin castrum "fort, camp."  The suffix "-ated" indicates a state or condition.
