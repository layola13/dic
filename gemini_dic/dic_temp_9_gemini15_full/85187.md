/ˈhærɪŋɪfɔːrm/| adj.| 鲱形目的；似鲱形的 | Relating to the herring-like fishes, characterized by a laterally compressed body and a single dorsal fin located far back on the body.  The term often refers to the order Clupeiformes, which includes herrings, sardines, and anchovies. | Example:  Many commercially important fish species are harengiform. 许多重要的商业鱼类都是鲱形目的。  The harengiform body shape is highly adapted for schooling. 鲱形目的体型非常适合群游。|近义词：clupeiform (more technical)|反义词：None specifically, but terms describing opposite body shapes could be used (e.g., elongated, robust).
