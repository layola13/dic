hoboed:/hoʊbd/| v. |流浪；漂泊；过流浪汉的生活|To live as a hobo; to travel from place to place without a permanent home or job.  The term often implies a life of poverty and hardship.|例句：He hoboed across the country for several years before settling down. 他在定居之前在全国各地流浪了好几年。 He was hoboing around Europe when he met her. 他在欧洲流浪时遇到了她。|近义词：wandered, travelled, tramped, bummed|反义词：settled, established


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hobos across the country. = 他在全国各地流浪。
They hobo around Europe. = 他们在欧洲流浪。


一般过去时:
He hoboed across the country last year. = 他去年在全国各地流浪。
She hoboed around South America for six months. = 她曾在南美洲流浪了六个月。


现在完成时:
He has hoboed through most of the states. = 他几乎走遍了大部分州。
She has hoboed for many years. = 她流浪了很多年。


过去完成时:
He had hoboed for years before he found a job. = 他流浪了很多年才找到工作。
They had hoboed all over the world before they settled down. = 他们在定居之前周游世界流浪。


将来时:
He will hobo across the country next year. = 他明年将在全国各地流浪。
They will hobo around Europe for a year. = 他们将在欧洲流浪一年。


虚拟语气:
If I had the courage, I would hobo around the world. = 如果我有勇气，我会周游世界流浪。
I wish I could hobo around Europe like I always wanted to. = 我真希望像我以前一直想的那样，能在欧洲流浪。


被动语态: (This verb is rarely used passively.)  The passive voice doesn't fit the context well.


疑问句:
Has he ever hoboed before? = 他以前流浪过吗？
Did she hobo around Europe? = 她曾在欧洲流浪吗？


否定句:
He didn't hobo around last summer. = 他去年夏天没有流浪。
She hasn't hoboed since she graduated. = 自从毕业后她就没再流浪了。



PHRASE->
There aren't common idiomatic phrases directly using "hoboed."  The word itself describes a complete action.
