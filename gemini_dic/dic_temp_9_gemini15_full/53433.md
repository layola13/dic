disappoint:/ˌdɪs.əˈpɔɪnt/ | vt. | 使失望；未能满足（期望） | To fail to fulfill the hopes or expectations of (someone).  |例句：The movie disappointed me.  这部电影让我失望了。 |例句：He was disappointed by the results of the test.  他对考试的结果感到失望。|近义词：frustrate, let down, disillusion |反义词：please, satisfy, delight |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news disappoints her. = 这个消息让她失望。
His performance disappoints the coach. = 他的表现让教练失望。

一般过去时:
The film disappointed the audience. = 这部电影让观众失望了。
The results disappointed everyone. = 结果让每个人都失望了。

现在完成时:
The team has disappointed its fans this season. = 这个赛季球队让球迷失望了。
She has been disappointed many times in her life. = 她一生中多次感到失望。

过去完成时:
I had been disappointed by his behavior before. = 我以前就对他那种行为感到失望。
They had already disappointed her once before. = 他们之前已经让她失望过一次了。

将来时:
The outcome may disappoint us. = 结果可能会让我们失望。
I think the new policy will disappoint many people. = 我认为这项新政策会让很多人失望。

虚拟语气:
I would be disappointed if he didn't come. = 如果他不来，我会很失望。
It would disappoint her if you didn't call. = 如果你不打电话，她会失望的。

被动语态:
The fans were disappointed by the team's performance. = 球迷们对球队的表现感到失望。
He was disappointed to learn the truth. = 他得知真相后感到失望。

疑问句:
Does this result disappoint you? = 这个结果让你失望吗？
Will the new rules disappoint the students? = 新规定会让学生失望吗？

否定句:
The ending didn't disappoint me. = 结局并没有让我失望。
The trip didn't disappoint us at all. = 这趟旅行一点也没有让我们失望。


PHRASE->
be disappointed in/with: 对...失望
deeply disappointed: 深感失望
bitterly disappointed: 非常失望
a disappointing result: 一个令人失望的结果
to one's disappointment: 令某人失望的是
