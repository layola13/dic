regimes:/reɪˈʒiːmz/ | n. | 政权；制度；养生法 | A regime is a system or method of government.  It can also refer to a system or plan of medical treatment, or a strict and organized way of living. | 例句：The new regime promised to improve the economy. 新政权承诺改善经济。  The doctor prescribed a strict regime of exercise and diet. 医生开了一套严格的运动和饮食疗法。 | 近义词：system, government, method, plan | 反义词：chaos, anarchy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The current regime focuses on economic growth. = 目前政权的重点是经济增长。
This exercise regime helps me stay fit. = 这种锻炼方法帮助我保持健康。


一般过去时:
The previous regime was overthrown in a coup. = 前政权在政变中被推翻。
His diet regime last year was very strict. = 他去年的饮食方案非常严格。


现在完成时:
The country has experienced several regimes since the revolution. = 自从革命以来，这个国家经历了几个政权。
She has maintained a healthy fitness regime for many years. = 她多年来一直保持着健康的健身方案。


过去完成时:
Before the new regime took power, the country was in chaos. = 在新政权上台之前，国家处于混乱之中。
By the time he finished his regime, he had lost a significant amount of weight. = 当他完成他的疗程时，他已经减掉了大量的体重。


将来时:
The next regime will face many challenges. = 下一个政权将面临许多挑战。
He will start a new exercise regime next month. = 他下个月将开始新的锻炼计划。


虚拟语气:
If the regime were more democratic, the people would have more freedom. = 如果这个政权更民主，人民就会有更多的自由。
If I had followed a stricter regime, I would have achieved better results. = 如果我遵循更严格的方案，我会取得更好的结果。


被动语态:

His strict weight-loss regime was recommended by his doctor.  = 他严格的减肥方案是他医生推荐的。


疑问句:

否定句:

He did not stick to his diet regime. = 他没有坚持他的饮食方案。
