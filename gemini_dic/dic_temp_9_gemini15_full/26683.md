cadaverously:/kəˈdævərəsli/| adj.| 死灰色的；死气沉沉的；瘦骨嶙峋的 | Extremely pale, gaunt, and thin, like a corpse.  The adjective suggests a state of extreme emaciation and pallor, often associated with illness or starvation. |例句：He looked cadaverously pale after his long illness. 他大病一场后脸色惨白。

例句：The starving children were cadaverously thin, their bones showing through their skin. 那些挨饿的孩子瘦得皮包骨，骨头都露出来了。


近义词： gaunt, ghastly, haggard, skeletal, emaciated

反义词： robust, healthy, plump, rosy-cheeked


词源:  From "cadaver" (corpse) + "-ously" (adverbial suffix).  The word ultimately derives from the Latin "cadāver," meaning "corpse."
