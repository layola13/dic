poonac: /ˈpuːnæk/ | n. | 糠麸，饼粕 |  Poonac refers to the byproduct left after oil is extracted from oilseeds, such as groundnut, cottonseed, or sunflower seeds. It's a residue rich in protein and fiber, often used as animal feed or fertilizer.  | Example:  The farmer used cottonseed poonac to enrich the soil.  农民用棉籽饼粕来改良土壤。 | 近义词：oil cake, seed cake, press cake | 反义词：N/A |  名词
