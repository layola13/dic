frown:/fraʊn/| v. n.|皱眉；不悦的表情|To contract the brows, typically to express displeasure, displeasure, or concentration.|例句：She frowned at the messy room. 她看着凌乱的房间皱起了眉头。 He frowned in concentration. 他皱着眉头全神贯注。|近义词：Scowl, glower|反义词：Smile, grin|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He frowns at the news. = 他对这个消息皱起了眉头。
She frowns when she's thinking hard. = 她认真思考时会皱眉。

一般过去时:
He frowned when he heard the bad news. = 他听到坏消息时皱起了眉头。
She frowned at the difficult problem. = 她对这个难题皱起了眉头。

现在完成时:
He has frowned all day. = 他整天都愁眉苦脸。
She has frowned since she received the letter. = 从收到这封信以来，她就一直愁眉苦脸。

过去完成时:
He had frowned before he spoke. = 他说话前就皱起了眉头。
She had frowned throughout the entire meeting. = 整个会议期间她都一直皱着眉头。

将来时:
He will frown if he sees the mess. = 如果他看到这乱七八糟的样子，他会皱眉的。
She will frown upon such behavior. = 她会反对这种行为。

虚拟语气:
If he knew the truth, he would frown. = 如果他知道真相，他会皱眉的。
I wish she wouldn't frown so much. = 我希望她不要总是皱眉。

被动语态:
The news was frowned upon by many people. = 这个消息受到了很多人的反对。
His suggestion was frowned upon by his boss. = 他的建议遭到了老板的反对。


疑问句:
Does he often frown? = 他经常皱眉吗？
Why is she frowning? = 她为什么皱眉？


否定句:
He didn't frown at all. = 他根本没有皱眉。
She didn't frown when she heard the joke. = 听到笑话时她并没有皱眉。



PHRASE->
frown upon: 反对，不赞成
frown at: 对...皱眉
wear a frown: 愁眉苦脸
frown on: 不赞成
a deep frown: 深深的皱纹
