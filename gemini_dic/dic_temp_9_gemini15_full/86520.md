heisting: /ˈheɪstɪŋ/ | v. | 提升；吊起；高举 | To raise or lift something, especially something heavy.  |例句：They were heisting the heavy beam into place. 他们正在把沉重的横梁吊到适当的位置。 |近义词：hoist, raise, lift, elevate |反义词：lower, drop, descend


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They hoist the flag every morning. = 他们每天早上都升旗。
He heists the heavy box onto the shelf. = 他把沉重的箱子搬到架子上。


一般过去时:
The workers hoisted the crate onto the truck. = 工人们把板条箱吊到卡车上。
She hoisted the sail and set off. = 她升起帆并启航。


现在完成时:
They have hoisted the sails. = 他们已经升起了帆。
The construction crew has hoisted the steel beams. = 建筑队已经吊起了钢梁。


过去完成时:
The workers had hoisted the equipment before the storm hit. = 工人们在暴风雨来临之前已经吊起了设备。
They had hoisted the banner high above the crowd. = 他们在人群上方高高地挂起了横幅。


将来时:
We will hoist the anchor tomorrow morning. = 我们明天早上将起锚。
They will hoist the new sign onto the building. = 他们将把新的标志安装到建筑物上。


虚拟语气:
If they had hoisted the sails earlier, they would have arrived sooner. = 如果他们早点升起帆，他们就会早点到达。
I wish they had hoisted the flag before the ceremony. = 我希望他们在仪式前已经升起了旗帜。


被动语态:
The heavy object was hoisted by a crane. = 沉重的物体由起重机吊起。
The flag was hoisted at sunrise. = 旗帜在日出时升起。


疑问句:
Will they hoist the bridge? = 他们会吊起桥梁吗？
Has the crane hoisted the load yet? = 起重机已经吊起重物了吗？


否定句:
They did not hoist the sails in time. = 他们没有及时升起帆。
The workers could not hoist the heavy stone. = 工人们无法吊起那块沉重的石头。


PHRASE->

to hoist oneself up = 往上拉自己
