provostship:/ˈprɒvəstʃɪp/| n. |教务长职位；校长职位；（大学的）管理职位|The position or office of a provost.  It typically refers to a high-ranking administrative position, often second in command to a president or chancellor in a university or college, responsible for academic affairs. In some contexts, it can also refer to the office or term of a provost.|例句：The provostship is a demanding but rewarding position. 教务长职位要求很高，但也很有回报。

例句：He was appointed to the provostship of the university. 他被任命为大学校长。


近义词：deanship, presidency (in some contexts), headship


反义词：subordinacy, low-ranking position


词性:名词
