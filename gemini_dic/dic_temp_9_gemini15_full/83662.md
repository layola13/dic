haemotachometry: /ˌhiːməʊtækˈɒmɪtri/ | n. | 血流速度测定法 | The measurement of blood flow velocity.  Haemotachometry involves techniques used to determine the speed at which blood is moving through vessels.  |  The study utilized haemotachometry to assess the impact of the new drug on blood flow in the coronary arteries.  血流速度测定法被用于评估新药对冠状动脉血流的影响。 | Doppler ultrasonography, laser Doppler velocimetry |  (no clear antonym exists, as it's a measurement technique) | 名词

词源:
haemo- (from Greek "haima," meaning blood) + tachy- (from Greek "tachys," meaning swift, quick, rapid) + -metry (from Greek "-metria," meaning measurement).  Therefore, haemotachometry literally means "the measurement of swift blood."
