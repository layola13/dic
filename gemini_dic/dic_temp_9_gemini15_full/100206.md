interrhymed:/ˌɪntərˈraɪmd/ | adj. | 内韵的 | Having internal rhyme; characterized by the use of rhyme within a single line or verse.  Internal rhyme occurs when two or more words rhyme within the same line of verse or sentence. This technique is often used in poetry and song lyrics to create a musical effect and add emphasis to certain words or phrases.  | Example:  The "silly, chilly" phrase from the song "Winter Wonderland" is an example of interrhymed words. | 近义词: internally rhymed | 反义词: unrhymed
