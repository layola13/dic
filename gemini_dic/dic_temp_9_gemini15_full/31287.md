chaperone:/ˈʃæpəroʊn/ | n. v. | 监护人；陪伴者；(尤指年轻女子)的伴侣；担任监护人；陪伴 |  A chaperone is a person who accompanies and supervises young people, especially on social occasions, to ensure their proper behavior.  The verb form means to act as a chaperone. |

例句：

Her aunt chaperoned her to the dance. = 她姑姑陪她去跳舞。

The teacher chaperoned the students on their field trip. = 老师在学生们的实地考察中担任监护人。


近义词：escort, companion, guardian, attendant

反义词：None directly opposite, but  "unsupervised" could be considered a contextual antonym.


词源：

From French *chaperon*, meaning "hood," originally referring to a hooded cloak worn by older women who would accompany younger women.  The protective and supervisory role of the cloak was transferred to the person wearing it.
