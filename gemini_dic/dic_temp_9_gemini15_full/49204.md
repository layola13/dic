delays:/dɪˈleɪz/ | n. | 耽搁；延误 | Delays are expected due to bad weather. 由于天气恶劣，预计会有延误。 | 近义词：postponements, postponing | 反义词：expeditions, accelerations


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Traffic delays are common during rush hour. = 高峰时段交通延误很常见。
The project delays are costing the company money. = 项目延误正在给公司造成损失。

一般过去时:
The flight delays caused many passengers to miss their connections. = 航班延误导致许多乘客错过了转机。
Unexpected delays forced us to reschedule the meeting. = 意外的延误迫使我们重新安排会议。

现在完成时:
The construction has experienced several delays. = 建设工程经历了几次延误。
We have had many delays this week. = 我们本周经历了很多延误。

过去完成时:
Because of the earlier delays, we arrived late. = 由于早些时候的延误，我们迟到了。
They had already experienced several delays before the final one. = 在最后一次延误之前，他们已经经历过几次延误了。

将来时:
Further delays are anticipated. = 预计还会有进一步的延误。
The report predicts significant delays next year. = 报告预测明年将出现严重的延误。

虚拟语气:
If there were no delays, we would arrive on time. = 如果没有延误，我们会准时到达。
I wish there were no delays. = 我希望没有延误。

被动语态:
The project was delayed by unforeseen circumstances. = 该项目因不可预见的情况而被推迟。
The shipment was delayed due to customs issues. = 货物由于海关问题而被延误。

疑问句:
Are there any delays on the train line? = 火车线路上有延误吗？
What caused the delays? = 延误的原因是什么？

否定句:
There were no delays on our journey. = 我们旅途中没有延误。
The project wasn't delayed. = 项目没有延误。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "delay" comes from the Old French word "delai," which means "putting off" or "deferment."  This in turn has roots in the Latin word "dilamen,"  meaning "to lengthen" or "to spread out."
