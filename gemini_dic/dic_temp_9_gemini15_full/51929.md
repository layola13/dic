diabetes:/ˌdaɪəˈbiːtiːz/| n. |糖尿病|A chronic metabolic disorder characterized by elevated levels of glucose in the blood. This is due to either the pancreas not producing enough insulin, or the body's cells not responding properly to the insulin produced.  There are several types of diabetes, including type 1, type 2, and gestational diabetes.|例句：My grandfather suffers from type 2 diabetes.  我的祖父患有2型糖尿病。
例句：She was diagnosed with gestational diabetes during her pregnancy. 她怀孕期间被诊断出患有妊娠糖尿病。|近义词：hyperglycemia |反义词：euglycemia

词源:

The word "diabetes" comes from the Greek word "διαβήτης" (diabētēs), meaning "siphon" or "to pass through." This refers to the excessive urination that is a characteristic symptom of the disease.  The suffix "-es" is a pluralization, though it is often used as a singular in modern English.
