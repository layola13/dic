hyperinnervation: /ˌhaɪpərˌɪnɜːrˈveɪʃən/ | n. |神经支配过多；神经纤维过度生长 | Excessive innervation; an abnormally large number of nerve fibers supplying a part.  This condition can lead to increased muscle tone, spasms, or other neurological issues.  It is often associated with nerve damage or other neurological conditions. | Example:  The patient presented with hyperinnervation in the affected limb, resulting in chronic pain and muscle spasms.  患者患肢出现神经支配过多，导致慢性疼痛和肌肉痉挛。 | 近义词:  overinnervation | 反义词:  hypoinnervation, denervation
