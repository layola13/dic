monodelph:/ˈmɒnəˌdɛlf/| n. | 单孔目动物；有袋类动物 | A monodelph is a mammal that has a single birth canal, as opposed to marsupials which have a pouch.  They are characterized by having a placenta during pregnancy.  The term is primarily used in a zoological classification context. | Example:  Kangaroos are not monodelphs; they are marsupials.  袋鼠不是单孔目动物；它们是有袋动物。|近义词：eutherian (although not a perfect synonym, as it refers to placental mammals in general) |反义词：marsupial, monotreme
