mischief-making:/ˈmɪstʃɪfˌmeɪkɪŋ/| adj.| 捣蛋的；爱恶作剧的 | Causing or involving mischief; playful but often troublesome.  |例句：The mischief-making children were constantly getting into trouble. 那些爱捣蛋的孩子总是惹麻烦。

The mischief-making cat knocked over the vase.  那只爱捣蛋的猫打翻了花瓶。


近义词：mischievous, prankish, troublesome, naughty

反义词：well-behaved, obedient, good, harmless


词源:

* **mischief:**  Old English *miscief*, from Old French *meschief*, from *mes-* (bad) + *chief* (thing, head).  The original meaning referred to an unfortunate event or mishap.  Over time, it evolved to mean playful troublemaking.

* **making:** Present participle of the verb "to make," indicating the act of causing or creating something.
