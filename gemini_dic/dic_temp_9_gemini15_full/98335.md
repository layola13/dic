insinuated:/ˈɪnsɪnjuˌeɪtɪd/| v.|暗示，暗指，逐渐渗透|To suggest or hint at something unpleasant or bad, often indirectly.  It implies a subtle and often malicious implication.|例句：He insinuated that I was responsible for the mistake. 他暗示这个错误是我的责任。
例句：The rumors insinuated that there was corruption in the government. 谣言暗示政府存在腐败。|近义词：imply, suggest, hint, intimate|反义词：state directly, declare openly|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He insinuates that I am lazy. = 他暗示我懒惰。
She insinuates problems exist in the company. = 她暗示公司存在问题。


一般过去时:
He insinuated that she was dishonest. = 他暗示她不诚实。
They insinuated a hidden agenda behind the proposal. = 他们暗示提案背后有隐藏的议程。


现在完成时:
She has insinuated her disapproval of the plan. = 她已经暗示她反对这个计划。
He has insinuated himself into the company's inner circle. = 他已经逐渐渗透到公司的核心圈子。


过去完成时:
They had insinuated that the project was doomed to fail. = 他们之前暗示这个项目注定要失败。
She had insinuated her way into a position of power. = 她已经设法获得了权力地位。


将来时:
He will insinuate that the evidence is unreliable. = 他将暗示证据不可靠。
She will insinuate herself into the conversation. = 她将会插进谈话中。


虚拟语气:
If he insinuated that, I would be very angry. = 如果他暗示那样，我会非常生气。
I wish he hadn't insinuated that I was incompetent. = 我真希望他没有暗示我无能。


被动语态:
It was insinuated that he had taken a bribe. = 暗示他受贿了。
The company's reputation was insinuated to be damaged. = 暗示公司的名誉受损。


疑问句:
Was he insinuating something against me? = 他是在暗示针对我的什么吗？
What are you insinuating? = 你在暗示什么？


否定句:
He didn't insinuate anything against her. = 他没有暗示任何针对她的事情。
She didn't insinuate that I was lying. = 她没有暗示我在说谎。


PHRASE->
insinuate oneself into = 逐渐渗透进…
insinuate a suggestion = 暗示一个建议
insinuate doubt = 暗示怀疑
insinuate a threat = 暗示威胁
insinuate corruption = 暗示腐败
