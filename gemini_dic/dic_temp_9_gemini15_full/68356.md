fallax:/ˈfælæks/| adj.|谬误的；欺骗性的；易错的 | Deceiving or misleading; likely to be false.  It often refers to an argument or statement that seems true but is actually false. | Example: His argument contained a fallacious assumption about human nature.  他的论点包含一个关于人性的谬误假设。


The claim that the earth is flat is fallacious.  地球是平的这一说法是错误的。|近义词：erroneous, deceptive, misleading, specious |反义词：true, accurate, valid, sound |词性：形容词


Etymology:  From Latin *fallax*, present participle of *fallere* "to deceive, trick, fail".  The word reflects a history of being easily misled or deceived.
