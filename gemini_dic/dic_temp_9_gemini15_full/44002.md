criminalized:/ˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪzd/| v. | 宣布...为犯罪；使犯罪化 | To declare something illegal or to make something a crime.  | The government criminalized the possession of certain drugs. 政府宣布持有某些毒品为犯罪行为。 | The new law criminalized downloading copyrighted material without permission. 新法律将未经许可下载受版权保护的材料定为犯罪。 | legalize, outlaw | legalize, permit |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They criminalize drug use in this country. = 这个国家将吸毒定为犯罪。
The government criminalizes certain forms of protest. = 政府将某些形式的抗议定为犯罪。


一般过去时:
They criminalized gambling in the 19th century. = 他们在19世纪将赌博定为犯罪。
The act criminalized the sale of unregistered firearms. = 该法案将销售未注册的枪支定为犯罪。


现在完成时:
Many countries have criminalized hate speech. = 许多国家已经将仇恨言论定为犯罪。
Society has increasingly criminalized certain behaviors. = 社会越来越将某些行为定为犯罪。


过去完成时:
Before the new law, they hadn't criminalized online harassment. = 在新法律颁布之前，他们还没有将网络骚扰定为犯罪。
The judge explained that the actions had already been criminalized under previous legislation. = 法官解释说，这些行为根据之前的立法已经构成犯罪。


将来时:
They will criminalize the use of AI in warfare. = 他们将把在战争中使用人工智能定为犯罪。
The proposed bill will criminalize certain types of data theft. = 拟议的法案将把某些类型的数据盗窃定为犯罪。


虚拟语气:
If they criminalized that behavior, there would be fewer incidents. = 如果他们将那种行为定为犯罪，就会减少此类事件。
I wish they would criminalize the spread of misinformation. = 我希望他们能将散布虚假信息定为犯罪。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
