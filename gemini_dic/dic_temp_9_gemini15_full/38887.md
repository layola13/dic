conferred:/kənˈfɜːrd/ | vt. |授予；给予；商讨；谈论 | To grant or bestow (a title, degree, right, etc.) formally.  To discuss something in detail, especially to exchange ideas or opinions. |例句：The university conferred an honorary degree upon him.  大学授予他荣誉学位。


The committee conferred about the best course of action. 委员会商讨了最佳行动方案。|近义词：bestow, grant, award, discuss, deliberate |反义词：take away, revoke, withhold


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The university confers honorary degrees annually. = 大学每年都授予荣誉学位。
They confer regularly to discuss company policy. = 他们定期开会讨论公司政策。


一般过去时:
The queen conferred knighthood on the general. = 女王授予将军爵士称号。
The scientists conferred for hours before reaching a conclusion. = 科学家们讨论了数小时才得出结论。


现在完成时:
The board has conferred numerous awards this year. = 董事会今年已颁发了无数奖项。
They have conferred on the matter several times. = 他们已经多次讨论过这件事。


过去完成时:
She had conferred with her lawyer before making a decision. = 她做出决定前已与律师商讨过。
They had conferred extensively before the final vote. = 他们在最终投票前进行了广泛的讨论。


将来时:
The president will confer with his advisors tomorrow. = 总统明天将与他的顾问们会商。
They will confer about the budget next week. = 他们下周将讨论预算。


虚拟语气:
If the committee had conferred earlier, the problem might have been avoided. = 如果委员会早点商讨，问题也许就能避免了。
I wish they had conferred before taking action. = 我希望他们在采取行动前能商讨一下。


被动语态:
An honorary doctorate was conferred upon her. = 她被授予荣誉博士学位。
The matter was conferred upon by the committee for hours. = 委员会讨论了这件事数小时。


疑问句:
Will they confer before making a decision? = 他们会在做决定之前商讨吗？
Have they conferred with the stakeholders yet? = 他们已经与利益相关者商讨过了吗？


否定句:
The university did not confer the degree this year. = 大学今年没有授予学位。
They did not confer sufficiently before making their decision. = 他们在做决定之前没有充分商讨。



PHRASE->
confer with someone = 与某人商讨
confer a title on someone = 授予某人一个称号
confer an honour on someone = 授予某人荣誉
confer benefits = 带来好处
confer authority = 授予权力


词源:
From Old French *conferrer, from Latin conferre "to bring together," from com- "together" + ferre "to bear, carry."  The sense development is from "bring together" (in discussion) to "bestow" (by bringing together the giver and receiver).
