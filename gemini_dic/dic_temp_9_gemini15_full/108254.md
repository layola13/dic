launched:/lɔːntʃt/| /lɑːntʃt/| vt.|发射；启动；开办；推出|To propel (something, especially a ship or spacecraft) into the water or into the air. To initiate or begin (a plan, project, etc.). To introduce (a new product or service) to the market.|例句：The company launched a new marketing campaign.  公司启动了一场新的营销活动。|例句：The rocket was successfully launched into space. 火箭成功发射到太空。|近义词：initiate, start, introduce, begin|反义词：conclude, end, terminate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They launch a new product every year. = 他们每年都会推出一个新产品。
The company launches its new marketing strategy next month. = 公司下个月将启动其新的营销策略。


一般过去时:
She launched her career in journalism. = 她开始了她的新闻事业。
The spaceship launched last night. = 宇宙飞船昨晚发射了。


现在完成时:
They have launched several successful projects this year. = 他们今年已经启动了几个成功的项目。
The government has launched a new initiative to combat poverty. = 政府已经启动了一项新的扶贫计划。


过去完成时:
They had launched the website before the conference. = 他们在会议之前已经上线了网站。
The team had launched the experiment when the power failed. = 团队在电力中断时已经启动了实验。


将来时:
We will launch the satellite next week. = 我们下周将发射卫星。
The company will launch its new app in the spring. = 公司将在春季推出其新的应用程序。


虚拟语气:
If they launched the product earlier, they would have had more sales. = 如果他们早点推出产品，就会有更多销量。
I wish they had launched the campaign sooner. = 我希望他们早点启动这场活动。


被动语态:
The new product was launched last month. = 新产品是上个月推出的。
The rocket will be launched tomorrow morning. = 火箭将于明天上午发射。


疑问句:
When was the product launched? = 产品是什么时候推出的？
Will the new service be launched soon? = 新的服务很快就会推出吗？


否定句:
They didn't launch the campaign on time. = 他们没有按时启动活动。
The project wasn't launched as planned. = 项目没有按计划启动。


PHRASE->
