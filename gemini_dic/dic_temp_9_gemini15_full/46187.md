The word's negative connotation stems from the association of Cyprus with Aphrodite, the goddess of love, leading to a long-standing stereotype.|例句：He met a Cyprian woman while on holiday in Cyprus. 他在塞浦路斯度假时遇到一位塞浦路斯女子。


例句：In some older literature, the term "Cyprian" is used as a derogatory term for a prostitute. 在一些较早的文学作品中，“塞浦路斯人”一词被用作妓女的贬义词。


近义词： (for the person): Cypriot; (for the prostitute): courtesan, harlot, strumpet


反义词： (for the prostitute): nun, virgin


词性: n. adj.


词源:  The word derives from Cyprus, the island in the Mediterranean Sea. The connection to prostitution is a historical association linked to the goddess Aphrodite, worshipped on Cyprus and associated with love and sexuality. This association gave rise to the pejorative usage of the term.
