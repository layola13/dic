hexastichon:/hekˈstæstɪkɒn/| n. |六行诗|A hexastichon is a poem of six lines.  It's a relatively short poetic form, often used as a stanza within a larger poem or as a complete poem in itself.  The rhyme scheme and meter can vary greatly depending on the poet's choice. | Example:  This poem, though short, is a hexastichon, / Its six lines holding meaning to a con. / A simple form, yet potent in its way, /  It speaks of things both serious and gay. / A miniature of art, a crafted piece, / A small perfection, bringing sweet release.  |近义词：six-line verse, six-line poem |反义词：None specifically, as it refers to a length.
