hold-off:/ˈhoʊld ɔːf/ | vt. n. | 延迟；阻止；坚持；(电压)保持 | To delay or postpone something; to prevent something from happening; to maintain a position or stance; in electronics, to maintain a voltage. |例句：The company decided to hold off on the launch of the new product until next quarter. 公司决定将新产品的发布推迟到下个季度。

例句：The police held off the rioters with tear gas. 警察用催泪弹阻止了暴乱者。

近义词：delay, postpone, prevent, restrain, defer

反义词：accelerate, expedite, hasten, proceed

词性:动词及名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He holds off his decision until next week. 他将决定推迟到下周。
They hold off the attackers with fierce resistance. 他们以猛烈的抵抗阻止了攻击者。


一般过去时:
She held off announcing her resignation until after the project was completed. 她直到项目完成后才宣布辞职。
The army held off the enemy's advance for several days.  军队阻止了敌人的进攻好几天。


现在完成时:
They have held off making any major changes to the company structure. 他们一直没有对公司结构做任何重大改变。
We have held off the deadline for submitting our report. 我们已经推迟了提交报告的截止日期。


过去完成时:
They had held off signing the contract until they had reviewed all the terms. 他们在审查完所有条款后才签署合同。
She had held off telling him the bad news for as long as she could. 她尽可能地拖延着告诉他坏消息。


将来时:
The company will hold off on hiring new employees until the economy improves. 公司将在经济好转之前推迟招聘新员工。
He will hold off on his decision until he has all the information. 他将在获得所有信息后再做决定。


虚拟语气:
If they had held off making a decision, they might have avoided the current crisis. 如果他们当时推迟做决定，他们也许就能避免目前的危机。
I wish I had held off buying that car; it's already broken down. 我真希望我没有买那辆车；它已经坏了。


被动语态:
The project was held off due to unforeseen circumstances.  由于不可预见的情况，项目被推迟了。
The attack was held off by the brave defenders.  勇敢的保卫者们阻止了攻击。


疑问句:
Will they hold off the product launch until next year? 他们会把产品发布推迟到明年吗？
Did they manage to hold off the enemy's attack? 他们成功地阻止了敌人的攻击吗？


否定句:
They did not hold off making a decision, even though they should have. 他们没有推迟做决定，即使他们应该这么做。
The troops couldn't hold off the enemy's advance for much longer. 部队再也无法长时间抵挡敌人的进攻了。
