indiscriminatenesses:/ɪnˌdɪskrɪmɪˈneɪtənəsɪz/ | n. |不加区别；不加选择；任意性 | The plural form of "indiscriminateness," referring to multiple instances of lacking discrimination or distinction.  It signifies a state of being indiscriminate in multiple ways or contexts.  The word highlights the repetitive or multifaceted nature of such behavior or condition. | Example: The indiscriminatenesses of the bombing campaign caused widespread civilian casualties.  = 轰炸行动的不加区别造成了广泛的平民伤亡。 The indiscriminatenesses in his spending habits led to his financial ruin. = 他花钱大手大脚，最终导致了经济破产。|近义词：lack of discrimination, partiality, bias |反义词：discrimination, selectivity, discernment


词源:

* **in-**: prefix indicating negation or reversal.
* **discriminate**:  to recognize or perceive the difference between things; to make a distinction.  This word originates from the Latin word "discriminare," meaning "to separate,"  from "dis-" (apart) + "cernere" (to separate, decide).
* **-ness**: suffix forming abstract nouns denoting state, quality, or condition.
* **-es**: plural suffix for nouns.


用法说明：

由于indiscriminateness本身就比较少见，其复数形式indiscriminatenesses更加罕见，几乎不会在日常口语或书面语中出现。  它主要用于强调多次或多方面的不加区别的行为或状态。  在使用时，需要确保语境能够充分体现其含义，避免造成歧义。  更常见的表达方式是使用"lack of discrimination," "indiscriminate actions/behavior,"  或者其他更简洁的表达。
