overtax:/ˌoʊvərˈtæks/ | vt. | 课税过重；使负担过重 | to tax excessively or to a degree that is debilitating or damaging.  |例句：The new tax laws overtaxed small businesses, leading to many closures. 新的税法对小型企业课税过重，导致许多企业倒闭。|例句：The constant demands of her job overtaxed her, resulting in burnout. 她工作的持续需求使她负担过重，导致倦怠。|近义词：overburden, overload, strain |反义词：undertax, underburden

词源:
The word "overtax" is a combination of the prefix "over-" (meaning "excessively" or "too much") and the verb "tax" (meaning "to impose a tax on").  The root of "tax" traces back to Old French "taxe," from Latin "taxare," meaning "to estimate, assess, or charge."
