sigillaria:/ˌsɪdʒɪˈleəriə/| n. |封印植物；鳞木|A genus of extinct, arborescent lycopsids that were abundant during the Carboniferous period.  They are characterized by their tall, tree-like form, and distinctive diamond-shaped leaf scars on their trunks. These scars are arranged in vertical rows, giving the trunks a distinctive appearance.|The sigillaria forests of the Carboniferous period were a major component of the ancient ecosystems.|近义词: Lepidodendron (another genus of extinct lycopsid)|反义词:  (No direct antonyms, as it refers to a specific extinct plant genus)|名词
