rash:/ræʃ/ | adj., n. | 鲁莽的；皮疹 |

英文解析:
As an adjective, "rash" means acting or done without careful consideration of the possible consequences; impetuous.  As a noun, it refers to a temporary eruption of small red spots on the skin.

例句:
It was a rash decision to quit your job without another one lined up. = 辞掉工作而没有找到另一份工作是一个鲁莽的决定。

He developed a rash after eating shellfish. = 吃海鲜后，他起了皮疹。


近义词:
(adj.) reckless, impetuous, hasty, precipitate, foolhardy
(n.) eruption, breakout


反义词:
(adj.) cautious, careful, deliberate, prudent


词性:
adjective, noun

词源:
Middle English: from Old French *rasche, of Germanic origin; related to Old High German *rasch ‘swift’.  The sense development is from ‘swift’ to ‘hasty’ to ‘rash’. The noun sense developed later.
