gizzards:/ˈɡɪzərdz/| n. |砂囊；肌胃|The gizzard is a muscular pouch in the digestive tract of birds, reptiles, and some fish that contains grit to grind food.  It's a vital part of their digestive system, helping to break down tough food items. |例句： Chickens have strong gizzards that help them digest grains and seeds. = 鸡有强壮的砂囊，帮助它们消化谷物和种子。

例句：The bird's gizzard was full of small stones. = 那只鸟的砂囊里充满了小石头。


近义词：muscular pouch, ventriculus |反义词：None  |词性：名词

词源:
The word "gizzard" comes from the Old French word "giselle," which ultimately derives from the Vulgar Latin *gisula.  The original meaning likely referred to the crop or pouch in the bird’s throat, but the word shifted to refer specifically to the muscular stomach.
