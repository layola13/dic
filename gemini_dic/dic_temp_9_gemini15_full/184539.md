restimulations:/ˌriːˌstɪmjʊˈleɪʃənz/| n. |再刺激；再次刺激|The process of stimulating something again, often after a period of inactivity or rest.  This term is commonly used in scientific contexts, particularly in biology and medicine, referring to the repeated application of a stimulus to elicit a response.  It can also refer to the repeated stimulation of a particular idea or emotion.|例句：The restimulations of the neurons caused a significant increase in activity.  再次刺激神经元导致活动显著增加。

The repeated restimulations led to desensitization.  重复的再刺激导致脱敏。


近义词：re-stimulation, repeated stimulation, re-excitation|反义词：inhibition, suppression|词性：名词
