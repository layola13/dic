reattracting: /ˌriːəˈtræktɪŋ/ | v. |重新吸引；再次吸引 | The act of attracting something or someone again.  It implies a previous attraction that had ceased or weakened, and now is being renewed. | 例句：The company is reattracting customers with a new marketing campaign. = 公司正在通过新的营销活动重新吸引顾客。

The museum is reattracting visitors with its new dinosaur exhibit. = 博物馆通过新的恐龙展览重新吸引游客。


近义词：re-engaging, recapturing, regaining, attracting anew


反义词：repelling, alienating, discouraging


词性: 动词

词源:  The word "reattracting" is formed by adding the prefix "re-" (meaning "again" or "back") to the present participle "attracting" (from the verb "attract").  "Attract" itself has Latin roots, derived from "ad-" (to) and "trahere" (to draw or pull).
