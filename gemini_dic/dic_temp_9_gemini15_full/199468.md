sinapism:/ˈsɪnəpɪzəm/| n. |芥末膏，芥子膏|A poultice or plaster made from mustard, used externally to relieve congestion, particularly in the chest.  It works by producing a counterirritant effect, drawing blood flow to the area and relieving underlying pain or inflammation.|Example: The doctor applied a sinapism to his chest to help relieve the congestion from his pneumonia.|近义词：mustard plaster, counterirritant|反义词：None specifically, but treatments that do not involve external irritation are antonyms in the sense of offering an alternative approach.|名词
