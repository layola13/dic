gruff:/ɡrʌf/| adj.|粗暴的，粗鲁的，严厉的|Rough, surly, and unfriendly in manner.|例句：He had a gruff voice and a grumpy expression. 他声音粗暴，表情暴躁。|例句：The old man gave a gruff reply. 老人粗鲁地回答。|近义词：surly, rough, harsh, brusque|反义词：gentle, kind, polite, courteous


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is gruff to strangers. = 他对陌生人很粗鲁。
She always speaks in a gruff manner. = 她说话总是很粗鲁。


一般过去时:
He was gruff with the customer. = 他对顾客很粗鲁。
The teacher's voice was gruff that day. = 老师那天声音很粗暴。


现在完成时:
He has been gruff all morning. = 他整个上午都很粗暴。
She has always been gruff with her employees. = 她一直对她员工都很粗鲁。


过去完成时:
He had been gruff before he softened up. = 他在变得温和之前一直很粗暴。
The manager had been gruff with her staff before he received the feedback. = 在收到反馈之前，经理对员工一直很粗鲁。


将来时:
He will be gruff to those who disagree with him. = 他会对那些不同意他的人很粗鲁。
She will probably be gruff if she doesn't get enough sleep. = 如果她睡不够，她可能会很粗鲁。


虚拟语气:
If he were not so gruff, people would like him better. = 如果他不那么粗鲁，人们会更喜欢他。
I wish he weren't so gruff to his children. = 我希望他不那么粗暴地对待他的孩子。


被动语态:
His manner is often perceived as gruff. = 他的举止常常被认为是粗鲁的。
His voice was described as gruff and unpleasant. = 他的声音被描述为粗鲁且令人不快。


疑问句:

否定句:
