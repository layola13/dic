preobservation:/ˌpriːˌɒbzərˈveɪʃən/ | n. | 预先观察；事先观察 | The act of observing something before it is formally studied or investigated.  It often involves a preliminary assessment to guide further investigation.  | Example: The preobservation of the patient's vital signs indicated a possible infection.  预先观察病人的生命体征表明可能感染。  |近义词：preliminary observation, initial observation, reconnaissance |反义词：postobservation
