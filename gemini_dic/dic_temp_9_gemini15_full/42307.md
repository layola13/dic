counter-passant: /ˌkaʊntərˈpæsənt/ | adj. | 对向而行的；相对而行的 |  Describes two pieces in chess that are positioned on squares diagonally opposite each other, so that one could capture the other on the next move.  It refers to the relative position of two chess pieces, not a specific movement. | Example: The black queen and the white bishop were counter-passant, each threatening the other. 黑后和白象相对而行，互相威胁着对方。| 近义词: opposing, facing | 反义词: aligned, parallel |  词性: adjective

词源:

The word "counter-passant" is a compound word.  "Counter" means "in opposition" or "against," and "passant" is from the French word "passant," meaning "passing" or "in passing."  In chess, it describes pieces that are "passing" each other in opposing directions on a diagonal.  The term reflects the relative positioning of the pieces rather than any specific move.
