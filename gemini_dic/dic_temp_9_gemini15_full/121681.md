mislead:/mɪsˈliːd/ | vt. | 误导；使…误入歧途 | To guide or lead someone wrongly or into error; to deceive. |例句：The misleading advertisement led many customers to buy the faulty product.  误导性的广告导致许多顾客购买了有缺陷的产品。|例句：Don't mislead the children into thinking that Santa Claus is real. 不要误导孩子们认为圣诞老人是真的。|近义词：deceive, delude, confuse, betray |反义词：guide correctly, enlighten, clarify


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He misleads his followers with false promises. = 他用虚假的承诺误导他的追随者。
The advertisement misleads consumers about the product's effectiveness. = 广告误导消费者相信该产品的效果。


一般过去时:
The map misled us, and we ended up lost in the woods. = 地图误导了我们，我们最终迷路在森林里。
She misled the police with her false testimony. = 她用虚假的证词误导了警察。


现在完成时:
The company has misled investors with its financial reports. = 公司用其财务报告误导了投资者。
He has misled me about his intentions all along. = 他一直都在欺骗我，隐瞒他的意图。


过去完成时:
They had misled the public about the true nature of the project. = 他们此前已就项目的真实性质误导了公众。
I realized that I had been misled by his charming demeanor. = 我意识到我一直被他迷人的举止所误导。


将来时:
They will mislead the public with their propaganda. = 他们将用他们的宣传来误导公众。
The campaign is likely to mislead voters with its biased information. = 这场运动可能会用其有偏见的信息误导选民。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't been misled, I would have made a different decision. = 如果我没有被误导，我会做出不同的决定。
I wish I hadn't been misled by his promises. = 我真希望我没有被他那些承诺所误导。


被动语态:
The customers were misled by the deceptive advertisement. = 顾客被具有欺骗性的广告误导了。
The public has been misled by the government's statements. = 公众被政府的声明误导了。


疑问句:
Were the jurors misled by the defense's arguments? = 陪审员是否被辩方的论点误导了？
Has the witness been misled by the police? = 证人是否被警方误导了？


否定句:
The instructions were clear; no one was misled. = 说明很清楚；没有人被误导。
He didn't intend to mislead anyone with his words. = 他无意用他的话来误导任何人。



PHRASE->
mislead someone into doing something = 诱使某人做某事
be misled by something = 被某事误导
mislead someone about something = 在某事上误导某人
mislead someone into believing something = 误导某人相信某事
intentionally mislead = 故意误导


词源:
mis- (错误地) + lead (引导)
