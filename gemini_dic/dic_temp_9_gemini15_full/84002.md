half-deservedly:/ˌhɑːf dɪˈzɜːrvdli/ | adv. | 部分应得地；一半应得地 |  The adjective "deserved" means earned or obtained through merit.  "Half-deservedly" modifies a verb or adjective, indicating that something is only partially justified or merited. It suggests a degree of deservingness that is incomplete or less than full.  | Example: He half-deservedly won the award, as his contribution was significant but not entirely pivotal. 他部分应得这个奖，因为他做出的贡献很大，但并非完全关键。 | 近义词: partly deserved, partially merited | 反义词: fully deserved, undeserved | 副词
