liberators: /ˈlɪbəreɪtərz/ | n. |解放者，解放军 | Those who liberate or set free.  People or forces that free a country or people from oppression or control. |例句：The Allied forces were the liberators of many European countries from Nazi occupation.  盟军是许多欧洲国家摆脱纳粹占领的解放者。

例句：The resistance fighters were celebrated as national liberators after the war.  战争结束后，抵抗战士被誉为民族解放者。


近义词：rescuers, deliverers, emancipators

反义词：oppressors, conquerors, enslavers

词性: 名词

词源:

The word "liberators" derives from the Latin verb "liberāre," meaning "to free" or "to set free."  This verb is related to the Latin word "liber," meaning "free."  The suffix "-ator" forms nouns denoting agents or doers of the action.  Therefore, "liberators" literally means "those who free."
