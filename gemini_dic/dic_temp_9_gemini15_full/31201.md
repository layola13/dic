changing: /ˈtʃeɪndʒɪŋ/ | adj. | 变化的；改变的 |  Describes something that is in the process of changing or is subject to change.  It emphasizes the ongoing nature of the transformation. | 例句：The changing seasons bring about dramatic shifts in the landscape.  四季的变化给景观带来了巨大的变化。

The changing political climate is causing uncertainty.  不断变化的政治气候正在造成不确定性。


近义词：shifting, altering, evolving, transforming, fluctuating | 反义词：static, unchanging, constant, stable | 词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The weather is changing rapidly. = 天气变化很快。
The situation is changing constantly. = 情况不断变化。


一般过去时:
The landscape changed dramatically after the flood. = 洪水过后，地貌发生了巨大的变化。
His attitude changed after the conversation. = 谈话之后他的态度变了。


现在完成时:
The company has changed its policy. = 公司已经改变了它的政策。
Everything has changed since I last saw you. = 自从我上次见到你以来，一切都变了。


过去完成时:
The situation had changed significantly by the time we arrived. = 我们到达时，情况已经发生了重大变化。
They had changed their minds before the meeting. = 会议前他们已经改变了主意。


将来时:
The technology will change significantly in the next decade. = 未来十年，这项技术将发生显著变化。
The weather forecast predicts that the weather will be changing tomorrow. = 天气预报预测明天的天气将会变化。


虚拟语气:
If the rules changed, we would have to adapt. = 如果规则变了，我们就必须适应。
I wish the situation were changing faster. = 我希望情况能更快地改变。


被动语态:
The rules are being changed. = 规则正在被修改。
The date of the meeting is being changed. = 会议日期正在被更改。


疑问句:
Is anything changing? = 有什么变化吗？
Are your plans changing? = 你的计划有变吗？


否定句:
Nothing is changing. = 什么也没有改变。
The situation hasn't changed at all. = 情况根本没有改变。
