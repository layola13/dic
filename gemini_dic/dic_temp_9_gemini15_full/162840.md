precarious:/prɪˈkeriəs/| adj.|不稳定的；危险的；不确定的|  likely to fall or collapse; unstable; dangerous; uncertain.  The precarious situation demanded immediate action.  |近义词：unstable, insecure, risky, hazardous, uncertain|反义词：stable, secure, safe, certain


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bridge is precarious. = 这座桥很不稳定。
His financial situation is precarious. = 他的经济状况很不稳定。


一般过去时:
The situation was precarious before the intervention. = 干预之前，情况很不稳定。
Her position in the company was precarious. = 她在那家公司的职位岌岌可危。


现在完成时:
The situation has become precarious. = 情况已经变得不稳定了。
His health has become precarious recently. = 他最近的健康状况变得不稳定了。


过去完成时:
The bridge had been precarious for months before it finally collapsed. = 这座桥在最终倒塌之前已经摇摇欲坠好几个月了。
The company's financial situation had been precarious for years. = 这家公司的财务状况多年来一直很不稳定。


将来时:
The situation will become even more precarious without intervention. = 如果不进行干预，情况将会变得更加不稳定。
The cliff edge looks precarious; we should not go near it. = 悬崖边看起来很危险；我们不应该靠近它。


虚拟语气:
If the situation were less precarious, we would proceed. = 如果情况不那么危险，我们就会继续进行。
I wish the situation were not so precarious. = 我希望情况不要那么危险。


被动语态:
The bridge was deemed precarious and closed to traffic. = 这座桥被认为不安全，并关闭了交通。
His future was considered precarious by many. = 很多人认为他的未来不确定。


疑问句:
Is the situation precarious? = 情况危急吗？
Is his job precarious? = 他的工作不稳定吗？


否定句:
The situation is not precarious. = 情况并不危急。
His position is not precarious at all. = 他的职位一点也不不稳定。


PHRASE->
a precarious position = 岌岌可危的地位
a precarious balance = 不稳定的平衡
a precarious livelihood = 不稳定的生计
precarious circumstances = 危险的境况
precarious existence = 艰难的生活
