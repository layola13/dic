re-defined:/ˌriːdɪˈfaɪnd/| v. |重新定义，改写定义 | To define something again or in a new way; to change the meaning or nature of something.  |例句：The company re-defined its brand identity to attract a younger demographic. 公司重新定义了其品牌形象以吸引更年轻的受众群体。
例句：The event re-defined the limits of human endurance.  这次活动重新定义了人类耐力的极限。|近义词：redefine, reinterpret, recast |反义词：define, establish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They re-define their goals every year. = 他们每年都会重新定义他们的目标。
We re-define success on a regular basis. = 我们定期重新定义成功。

一般过去时:
The scientists re-defined the concept of gravity. = 科学家们重新定义了引力的概念。
The artist re-defined the boundaries of his art form. = 艺术家重新定义了他艺术形式的界限。

现在完成时:
They have re-defined the rules of the game. = 他们已经重新定义了游戏规则。
The company has re-defined its marketing strategy. = 公司已经重新定义了它的营销策略。

过去完成时:
They had re-defined the terms of the agreement before signing. = 他们在签署协议之前已经重新定义了协议条款。
The team had re-defined its objectives after the initial setback. = 在最初的挫折之后，团队已经重新定义了其目标。

将来时:
They will re-define their approach to the project. = 他们将重新定义他们对项目的方法。
The government will re-define its environmental policies. = 政府将重新定义其环境政策。

虚拟语气:
If they re-defined their priorities, they would be more successful. = 如果他们重新定义他们的优先级，他们将会更加成功。
I wish they had re-defined their strategy earlier. = 我希望他们早点重新定义他们的战略。

被动语态:
The concept was re-defined by leading experts. = 这个概念是由领先的专家重新定义的。
The rules have been re-defined to address the new challenges. = 规则已经被重新定义以应对新的挑战。

否定句:
