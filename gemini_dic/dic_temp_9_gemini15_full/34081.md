civility:/sɪˈvɪləti/| n. |礼貌；文明；客气；文雅|Politeness and courtesy in behavior or speech.  It implies a respect for others and a willingness to treat them with consideration.|例句：The civility of the debate was remarkable, given the strong disagreements.  这场辩论的文明程度令人惊叹，考虑到双方意见分歧很大。

例句：He showed great civility to his guests, making them feel welcome and comfortable. 他对客人非常客气，让他们感到宾至如归。


近义词：courtesy, politeness, politeness, refinement, decorum|反义词：rudeness, discourtesy, incivility, barbarity


词源:  From Middle French *civilitee, from Latin civilis "civil," from civis "citizen."  The original sense referred to the qualities of a citizen in a well-ordered state, later evolving to encompass politeness and refinement in social interaction.
