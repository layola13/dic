pentadactyle:/ˌpentəˈdæktəl/ | adj. |五指的，五趾的|Having five digits on each hand or foot.  This term is typically used in zoology and comparative anatomy to describe the characteristic five-fingered or five-toed limb structure found in many vertebrates. |例句：Many mammals are pentadactyle, exhibiting five digits on each limb. 许多哺乳动物都是五指的，每肢都有五个指头。

The pentadactyle limb structure is a key feature in evolutionary biology, suggesting a common ancestor for many diverse species. 五指肢结构是进化生物学中的一个关键特征，表明许多不同物种的共同祖先。|近义词：five-fingered, five-toed |反义词：polydactylous (having more than five digits)


词源:
From Ancient Greek πέντε (pente, "five") + δάκτυλος (daktylos, "finger, toe").  The word reflects the common ancestry of many vertebrates, which share this basic five-digit limb plan.
