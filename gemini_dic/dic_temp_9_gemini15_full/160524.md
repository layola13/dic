poristic:/pɔːˈrɪstɪk/| adj.|可推论的；可由推论获得的；赘述的 |Relating to or characteristic of a porism;  a type of mathematical proposition that states that if certain conditions are met, then certain other conditions will necessarily follow.  The term often implies a certain elegance or inevitability in the logical deduction.  It can also describe something that is overly detailed or repetitive, adding little new information. |The proof of the theorem was remarkably poristic, each step following inevitably from the last.  The lecturer's explanation, while thorough, was somewhat poristic, dwelling excessively on minor details.|近义词：deductive, inferential, consequential|反义词：inconclusive, illogical, unproven
