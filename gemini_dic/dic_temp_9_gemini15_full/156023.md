photomagnetism: /ˌfoʊtoʊˈmæɡnəˌtɪzəm/ | n. |光磁性|The phenomenon where light affects the magnetic properties of a material, typically causing changes in magnetization. This effect arises from the interaction of light with the electronic spins and orbital angular momentum within the material.  | Example:  The study of photomagnetism in semiconductor nanostructures is an active area of research. 光磁性在半导体纳米结构中的研究是一个活跃的研究领域。|近义词:light-induced magnetism, optomagnetism |反义词:None (a specific phenomenon, not a polarity)|名词
