interlocutor: /ɪnˈtɜːrlɒkjʊtə(r)/ | n. | 对话者；谈话者 | A person who takes part in a conversation or dialogue.  | Example: The interviewer and the interviewee are interlocutors in a job interview.  面试官和面试者是求职面试中的对话者。|近义词：conversationalist, speaker, participant | 反义词：listener (in a specific context)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The interlocutor asks insightful questions. = 对话者问了一些有见地的问题。
My interlocutor is very friendly. = 我的对话者非常友好。


一般过去时:
The interlocutor explained the rules clearly. = 对话者清楚地解释了规则。
The interlocutor responded thoughtfully to my comments. = 对话者认真地回应了我的评论。


现在完成时:
The interlocutor has already stated their position. = 对话者已经表明了自己的立场。
The interlocutors have reached a consensus. = 对话者们达成了共识。


过去完成时:
The interlocutor had already finished speaking before I could respond. = 我还没来得及回应，对话者就已经说完了。
Before the argument began, the interlocutor had established common ground. = 在争论开始之前，对话者已经建立了共同点。


将来时:
The interlocutor will present their findings tomorrow. = 对话者将在明天陈述他们的发现。
The interlocutors will continue their discussion later. = 对话者们稍后将继续讨论。


虚拟语气:
If the interlocutor were more patient, the discussion would be more productive. = 如果对话者更有耐心，讨论会更有成效。
I wish the interlocutor had been more understanding. = 我希望对话者更能理解。


被动语态:
The interlocutor was interrupted several times. = 对话者被多次打断。
The questions were carefully chosen by the interlocutor. = 这些问题是由对话者仔细挑选的。


疑问句:
Who is the interlocutor in this conversation? = 这段对话中的对话者是谁？
Is the interlocutor prepared to compromise? = 对话者准备好妥协了吗？


否定句:
The interlocutor did not answer my question directly. = 对话者没有直接回答我的问题。
The interlocutor was not involved in the initial stages of the project. = 对话者没有参与项目的初期阶段。
