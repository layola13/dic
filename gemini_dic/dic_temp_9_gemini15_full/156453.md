pianet:/ˈplænɪt/| n. |行星|A celestial body moving in an elliptical orbit around a star.|例句：Earth is a planet. 地球是一颗行星。|近义词：world, celestial body|反义词:star, sun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The planets revolve around the sun. = 行星绕太阳旋转。
Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. = 木星是我们太阳系中最大的行星。


一般过去时:
Scientists discovered a new planet. = 科学家发现了一颗新的行星。
The ancient Greeks named the planets after their gods. = 古希腊人用他们的神来命名行星。


现在完成时:
Humans have explored several planets. = 人类已经探索了几颗行星。
Scientists have recently discovered many exoplanets. = 科学家最近发现了许多系外行星。


过去完成时:
Before the telescope was invented, people didn't know much about planets. = 在望远镜发明之前，人们对行星知之甚少。
They had already studied the movement of planets before they started their mission. = 在他们开始任务之前，他们已经研究了行星的运动。


将来时:
We will send a spacecraft to Mars. = 我们将向火星发射宇宙飞船。
Scientists predict that we will discover many more planets in the future. = 科学家预测我们将来会发现更多行星。
