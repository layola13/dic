ruminations:/ˌruːmɪˈneɪʃənz/| n. |沉思，冥想；反刍；反复思考 |The act of ruminating; deep or serious thought.  It often involves repeatedly considering something. |例句：Her ruminations on the meaning of life led her to a new philosophy.  她的关于人生意义的沉思让她产生了一种新的哲学。

例句：His late-night ruminations kept him awake. 他深夜的沉思让他睡不着觉。


近义词：contemplations, reflections, meditations, musings |反义词：superficial thoughts, impulsive decisions


词源:  From the verb "ruminate," ultimately from Latin *rumināre "to chew the cud," from rumen "paunch," "first stomach of a ruminant animal." The extended sense of "to ponder," "to meditate" developed in the 16th century.
