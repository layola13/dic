constricting:/kənˈstrɪktɪŋ/ | adj. | 紧缩的；约束的；令人窒息的 |  Describes something that squeezes, binds, or restricts movement or flow. It can refer to physical objects or situations that limit freedom or progress. |

例句：

The constricting bandages hindered blood circulation.  = 紧绷的绷带阻碍了血液循环。

The constricting atmosphere in the office stifled creativity. = 办公室里压抑的气氛扼杀了创造力。


近义词：tight, binding, restrictive, suffocating, oppressive, squeezing


反义词：loose, freeing, liberating, expansive, open


词性:形容词


词源:  Comes from the verb "constrict," which originates from the Latin word "constringere," meaning "to bind together" or "to draw together."  The prefix "con-" means "together," and "stringere" means "to bind" or "to draw tight."
