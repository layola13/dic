overmodifying: /ˌoʊvərˈmɒdəfaɪɪŋ/ | v. |过度修饰，矫枉过正 | The act of excessively modifying something, often to the point of diminishing its effectiveness or natural quality.  It can refer to writing, design, or any other form of creation. | Example: Overmodifying the sentence made it clunky and difficult to understand.  过度修饰这个句子使其变得笨拙且难以理解。  The architect's overmodifying of the building's facade detracted from its original elegance. 建筑师对建筑物外观的过度修饰削弱了其最初的优雅。 | 近义词：overdoing, embellishing excessively, over-elaborating | 反义词：simplifying, streamlining, minimizing
