sirens:/ˈsaɪrənz/| n.|塞壬，警报器，汽笛声|Mythological creatures whose enchanting songs lured sailors to their deaths;  a device that makes a loud, wailing sound as a warning.|例句：The sirens wailed, signaling the imminent danger. 汽笛声呜咽着，预示着迫在眉睫的危险。

The fire sirens screamed all night. 消防警报声整夜鸣叫。|近义词：alarms, whistles, howls|反义词：silence, quiet


USAGE->

The sirens wail frequently in this city.  = 这个城市里的警笛经常鸣叫。
The siren sounds every hour. = 警报每小时响一次。


一般过去时:
The sirens wailed loudly last night. = 昨晚警笛响得很厉害。

现在完成时:
