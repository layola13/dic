enfilade:/ˈɛn.fɪˈleɪd/ | n., v. | 齐射；（建筑物）纵向排列； |

英文解析:As a noun, enfilade refers to a line of fire directed along the length of a body of troops or a fortification, exposing them to a raking fire.  As a verb, it means to rake (a line of troops or a fortification) with enfilading fire. It can also describe the arrangement of rooms or buildings in a straight line, one opening into another.

例句：

The enemy's artillery was able to enfilade our position, causing significant casualties. = 敌军的炮火能够对我们的阵地进行齐射，造成重大伤亡。

The architect designed the house with a series of rooms en filade, creating a sense of spaciousness. = 建筑师设计了这栋房子，房间一字排开，营造出一种宽敞的感觉。


近义词: raking fire, flanking fire (as a noun); rake (as a verb)

反义词:  There isn't a direct antonym, but words like "crossfire" or "defending" could be considered contextual opposites depending on the usage.

词性: noun, verb
