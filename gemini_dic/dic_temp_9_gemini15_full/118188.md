mentor:/ˈmɛntɔːr/|/ˈmentɔːr/| n. |导师，指导者，顾问 |A mentor is a trusted advisor or counselor who provides guidance and support to a less experienced person.  Mentors often share their knowledge, skills, and experience to help their mentees grow and develop. |She found a mentor who helped her navigate her career. 她找到一位导师帮助她规划职业生涯。  He served as a mentor to many young artists. 他担任许多年轻艺术家的导师。|advisor, coach, guide, tutor |opponent, adversary|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He mentors young entrepreneurs. = 他指导年轻的企业家。
She mentors students in the writing program. = 她指导写作项目的学生。


一般过去时:
He mentored me throughout my career. = 他在我的职业生涯中一直指导我。
She mentored many aspiring musicians. = 她指导过许多有抱负的音乐家。


现在完成时:
I have mentored several students this year. = 今年我已经指导了几名学生。
She has mentored countless individuals throughout her career. = 在她的职业生涯中，她已经指导了无数的人。


过去完成时:
He had mentored her for five years before she started her own business. = 在她自己创业之前，他已经指导了她五年。
They had mentored the team for months before the competition. = 在比赛之前，他们已经指导了这个团队几个月。


将来时:
She will mentor a new group of interns next semester. = 下学期她将指导一组新的实习生。
He will mentor the next generation of leaders. = 他将指导下一代领导者。


虚拟语气:
If I had a mentor, I would be more successful. = 如果我有一个导师，我会更成功。
I wish I had had a mentor earlier in my career. = 我希望在我职业生涯的早期就有一个导师。


被动语态:
Many young people are mentored by experienced professionals. = 许多年轻人得到经验丰富的专业人士的指导。
The program is designed to mentor students from disadvantaged backgrounds. = 该项目旨在指导来自弱势背景的学生。


疑问句:
Do you have a mentor? = 你有导师吗？
Who mentors the new employees? = 谁指导新员工？


否定句:
He doesn't mentor anyone anymore. = 他不再指导任何人。
She didn't mentor him, but she gave him good advice. = 她没有指导他，但她给了他很好的建议。
