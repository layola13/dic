debenture:/dɪˈbentʃər/| n. |债券；公司债券| A debenture is a type of debt instrument, similar to a bond, that is issued by a company to raise capital.  It represents a loan made to the company by an investor, and typically pays interest at a fixed rate. Unlike secured debt, debentures are usually unsecured, meaning they are not backed by specific assets of the company.  If the company defaults, debenture holders are considered general creditors. |The company issued debentures to finance its expansion.  这家公司发行债券来资助其扩张。| bond, loan | secured loan, equity | 名词
