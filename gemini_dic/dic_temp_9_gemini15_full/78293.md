germinating: /ˈdʒɜːrmɪneɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 发芽；萌芽；开始发展 |  The act of a seed beginning to sprout and grow into a plant.  It also refers to the beginning development of an idea or plan. |

例句：

The seeds are germinating nicely in the warm soil. = 种子在温暖的土壤中长得很好。

His business idea is finally germinating. = 他的商业理念终于开始萌芽了。


近义词：sprouting, budding, developing, growing

反义词：dying, withering, decaying


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The seeds are germinating. = 种子正在发芽。
The plan is germinating slowly. = 计划正在缓慢发展。

一般过去时:
The seeds germinated quickly after the rain. = 雨后种子迅速发芽。
The idea germinated during his long walk. = 这个想法在他长时间散步时萌生了。

现在完成时:
The seeds have germinated. = 种子已经发芽了。
The project has been germinating for months. = 这个项目已经酝酿了好几个月了。

过去完成时:
The seeds had germinated before the frost arrived. = 霜冻来临之前，种子已经发芽了。
The concept had germinated in his mind before he shared it. = 在他分享这个概念之前，它就已经在他的脑海中萌生了。

将来时:
The seeds will germinate in a few days. = 种子几天后就会发芽。
The project will germinate once funding is secured. = 一旦资金到位，项目就会启动。


被动语态:
The seeds were germinated in a controlled environment. = 种子在受控环境中发芽。
The idea was germinated by a series of events. = 这个想法是一系列事件促成的。


PHRASE->

词源:

The word "germinating" comes from the Latin word "germinare," meaning "to sprout" or "to bud."  This in turn relates to the Latin word "germen," meaning "bud" or "sprout."
