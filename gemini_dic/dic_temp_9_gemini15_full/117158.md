meanness:/ˈminnəs/| n. |卑鄙；吝啬；刻薄；卑劣|The quality of being mean; unkindness, selfishness, or stinginess.  It refers to a lack of generosity, compassion, or kindness, often manifested in actions or behavior.  |例句：His meanness prevented him from donating to charity. 他的吝啬阻止了他向慈善机构捐款。

例句：The meanness of his words hurt her deeply. 他刻薄的话深深地伤害了她。


近义词：cruelty, unkindness, selfishness, stinginess, niggardliness, parsimony


反义词：kindness, generosity, compassion, benevolence, magnanimity


词源：源自古英语 *meannes，意为“卑鄙，吝啬”。  This ultimately derives from the Proto-Germanic *mainiz, meaning "evil, wicked."  The evolution reflects a shift in meaning from general wickedness to specifically mean-spirited behavior.
