polymaths:/ˈpɒliˌmæθs/ | n. |博学者；通才 | A polymath is a person of wide-ranging knowledge or learning.  They excel in multiple fields of study.  Polymaths are often characterized by their intellectual curiosity and their ability to connect seemingly disparate ideas. | Example: Leonardo da Vinci was a renowned polymath, excelling as a painter, sculptor, architect, musician, scientist, inventor, anatomist, geologist, cartographer, botanist, and writer.  达芬奇是一位著名的博学者，在画家、雕塑家、建筑师、音乐家、科学家、发明家、解剖学家、地质学家、制图师、植物学家和作家等多个领域都非常出色。 | 近义词: Renaissance person, universal scholar, intellectual, genius | 反义词: specialist, expert (in a single field)
