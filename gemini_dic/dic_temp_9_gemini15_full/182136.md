remitted:/rɪˈmɪtɪd/ | vt. |汇款；减轻；赦免；提交；宽恕 | to send money to someone; to reduce the severity of something; to pardon or forgive someone; to submit something; to remit a sentence


英文解析:

The verb "remit" has several meanings, often related to sending, reducing, or forgiving.  When referring to money, it means to send or transmit money, often as a payment.  In a legal context, it can mean to pardon or forgive a crime, or to reduce a sentence.  It can also mean to send something (like a document) to a higher authority or to lessen the intensity or severity of something, such as pain or a symptom.


例句:

1. He remitted $500 to his family in the Philippines. 他汇了500美元给菲律宾的家人。

2. The doctor prescribed medication to remit the pain. 医生开了药来减轻疼痛。


3. The judge remitted the defendant's sentence due to extenuating circumstances. 由于有减轻罪责的情况，法官减轻了被告的刑罚。


4.  The committee remitted the proposal to the board for final approval.  委员会将提案提交董事会最终批准。


5.  The governor remitted the prisoner's sentence. 州长赦免了囚犯的刑罚。


近义词:

transmit (send money), alleviate (reduce severity), pardon, forgive, submit, lessen, abate


反义词:

increase (opposite of reduce), aggravate (opposite of alleviate), condemn (opposite of pardon), retain (opposite of remit - in the sense of sending)


词性:

Verb (transitive)
