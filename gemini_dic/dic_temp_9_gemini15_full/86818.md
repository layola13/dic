hematocrystallin: /ˌhiːməʊˈkrɪstəlɪn/ | n. | 晶状体血红蛋白 | A protein found in the eye lens of certain animals, particularly birds and reptiles, that resembles hemoglobin in structure but lacks the ability to bind oxygen. It plays a role in maintaining the transparency and structural integrity of the lens.  |  The presence of hematocrystallin in bird lenses contributes to their exceptional visual acuity. | 近义词: lens protein | 反义词:  None specifically, but terms related to lens opacity could be considered antonyms in a functional sense.
