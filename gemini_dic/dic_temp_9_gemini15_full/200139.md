skilled:/skɪld/| adj. |熟练的，有技巧的 |Having or showing the knowledge and skill to do something well.  A skilled worker possesses expertise and proficiency in their craft or profession.  |例句：He is a skilled carpenter, capable of crafting intricate furniture. 他是一位熟练的木匠，能够制作精巧的家具。

例句：She is a skilled negotiator, always achieving favorable outcomes. 她是一位技巧娴熟的谈判者，总是能取得有利的结果。


近义词：proficient, expert, adept, accomplished, masterful

反义词：unskilled, inept, incompetent, clumsy, amateur


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a skilled musician. = 他是一位技艺精湛的音乐家。
She is skilled in many areas. = 她在许多领域都很熟练。

一般过去时:
He was a skilled craftsman. = 他是一位熟练的工匠。
She was skilled at problem-solving. = 她擅长解决问题。

现在完成时:
He has become a skilled programmer. = 他已经成为一名熟练的程序员。
She has been skilled at public speaking for years. = 多年来她一直擅长公开演讲。

过去完成时:
He had already become a skilled artist by the age of 20. = 他20岁时就已经成为一名技艺精湛的艺术家了。
She had been skilled in knitting since childhood. = 她从小就擅长编织。

将来时:
He will be a skilled surgeon one day. = 他将来会成为一名技艺精湛的外科医生。
She will become skilled at coding after this course. = 修完这门课程后，她将熟练掌握编码。

虚拟语气:
If he were more skilled, he would get the job. = 如果他更熟练的话，他会得到这份工作。
If she had been more skilled, she would have won the competition. = 如果她更熟练的话，她就会赢得比赛。

被动语态:
The work is best done by skilled hands. = 这项工作最好由熟练的人来做。
The project requires skilled workers to be successful. = 项目需要熟练的工人才能成功。

疑问句:

否定句:

She is unskilled in this area. = 她在这个领域不熟练。
