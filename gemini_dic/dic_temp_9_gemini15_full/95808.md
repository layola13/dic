incoordinate:/ˌɪnkoʊˈɔːrdɪnət/| adj.| 不协调的；不一致的；紊乱的 | lacking coordination or harmony; not coordinated; unorganized.  The incoordinate movements of the puppet were comical.  | 近义词：uncoordinated, disorganized, chaotic | 反义词：coordinated, harmonious, orderly


英文解析:
The word "incoordinate" describes something lacking coordination or harmony. It suggests a lack of order, system, or agreement among different parts or elements.  This can apply to physical movements, actions, or ideas.


例句:
The incoordinate movements of the dancer made her performance seem clumsy. = 舞者的不协调动作使她的表演显得笨拙。

His incoordinate efforts to solve the problem only made things worse. = 他不协调的解决问题的努力只会使事情变得更糟。


词源:
The word is formed with the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "coordinate," which comes from the Latin "coordinatus," meaning "arranged together."  Therefore, "incoordinate" literally means "not arranged together."
