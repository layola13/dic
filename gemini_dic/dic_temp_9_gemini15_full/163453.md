precontending:/ˌpriːkənˈtɛndɪŋ/| adj.| 预先竞争的，先期竞争的 |  Describes a situation where competition or rivalry exists before a formal contest or event begins.  It suggests an atmosphere of anticipation and preparation for a struggle.  The word is relatively rare and often used in more formal or literary contexts.|例句：The precontending atmosphere among the startups was palpable, each vying for early investor attention.  预先竞争的氛围在初创公司中是显而易见的，每个公司都在争夺早期投资者的关注。

The precontending phase of the election saw intense campaigning and mudslinging.  选举的预先竞争阶段见证了激烈的竞选和诽谤。|近义词：preliminary, anticipatory, preliminary competitive|反义词：post-contest, post-competition
