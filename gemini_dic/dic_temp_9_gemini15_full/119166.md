/ˈmɛksɪkənz/| n. |墨西哥人 |Mexicans are the people of Mexico.  The term refers to citizens of Mexico and people of Mexican descent, regardless of their current location.  It can encompass a diverse range of cultures and backgrounds within Mexico itself.  The term can be used in a neutral, positive, or negative way, depending on the context.| Example: Many Mexicans celebrate Cinco de Mayo.  许多墨西哥人庆祝五月份五号。

The term "Mexicans" is a collective noun referring to the people of Mexico.  It's derived from "Mexico," which itself originates from the Nahuatl word "Mēxihco," referring to the Mexica people (also known as the Aztecs), who were a dominant group in the region before the Spanish conquest.  The suffix "-ans" is a common way to form plural nouns denoting people of a specific nationality or origin.
