compluvium:/kəmˈpluːviəm/| n. |雨水槽；采光天井| A compluvium is an opening in the roof of an ancient Roman atrium, designed to collect rainwater and channel it into a cistern below.  The term also refers to the area within the atrium where the rainwater collected.  It was a crucial element in Roman domestic architecture, providing both natural light and a water source.  |Example: The compluvium of the Roman villa channeled rainwater into a cistern in the impluvium. 罗马别墅的雨水槽将雨水导入下方的雨水池。|近义词：atrium (in the context of rainwater collection), light well |反义词：None directly applicable.
