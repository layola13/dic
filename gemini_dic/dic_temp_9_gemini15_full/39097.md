confront:/kənˈfrʌnt/ | vt. | 对抗；面对；遭遇 | To meet (someone) face to face, especially as a challenge; to deal with (a problem or difficulty). |例句：He confronted the thief and demanded his money back. 他与小偷对质并要求他归还钱财。  |例句：The company is confronting serious financial problems. 这家公司正面临严重的财务问题。 |近义词：face, encounter, challenge |反义词：avoid, evade, shun |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He confronts his fears every day. = 他每天都直面自己的恐惧。
She confronts the challenges head-on. = 她迎难而上。

一般过去时:
The police confronted the suspect. = 警方与嫌疑犯对质。
He confronted his past mistakes. = 他直面过去犯下的错误。

现在完成时:
She has confronted many difficulties in her life. = 她一生中克服了许多困难。
They have confronted the issue of climate change. = 他们正面对气候变化的问题。

过去完成时:
He had confronted the bully before, so he wasn't scared. = 他以前就曾面对过那个恶霸，所以他不害怕。
She had confronted her boss about the unfair treatment. = 她曾就受到的不公平待遇向老板提出质疑。

将来时:
We will confront the problem together. = 我们将一起面对这个问题。
He will confront his accusers in court. = 他将在法庭上与指控者对质。

虚拟语气:
If I were to confront him, I would need a plan. = 如果我要和他对质，我需要一个计划。
I wish I had confronted him earlier. = 我真希望我早点儿和他对质。

被动语态:
The suspect was confronted by the police. = 嫌疑犯被警方逮捕。
The issue was confronted by the committee. = 委员会正面对这个问题。

疑问句:
Will you confront him about the issue? = 你会就此事和他对质吗？
How did he confront his fear of public speaking? = 他是如何克服对公开演讲的恐惧的？

否定句:
He didn't confront his problems. = 他没有面对他的问题。
She refused to confront the truth. = 她拒绝面对真相。
