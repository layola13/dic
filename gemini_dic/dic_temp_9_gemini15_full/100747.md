intransmissible: /ˌɪntrænˈzmɪsəbl/ | adj. | 不可传播的，不能传播的 |  Unable to be transmitted or communicated.  This adjective typically refers to diseases or information that cannot be passed from one person or thing to another. |

例句：

The disease is intransmissible through casual contact. = 这种疾病不会通过偶然接触传播。

This information is intransmissible due to security protocols. = 由于安全协议，此信息无法传播。


近义词：non-communicable, uncontagious, non-transmissible

反义词：transmissible, communicable, contagious


词源:

* **in-:**  A prefix meaning "not" or "without."
* **trans-:** A prefix meaning "across" or "through."
* **-missible:** A suffix derived from the verb "mittere" (Latin), meaning "to send" or "to let go."  This suffix often forms adjectives indicating the possibility of something being done or sent.

Therefore, "intransmissible" literally means "not able to be sent across" or "not able to be transmitted."


USAGE->
This word is primarily used as an adjective, so verb conjugations are not applicable.  The usage focuses on its descriptive function within sentences.

例如：

* **肯定句:** The virus is intransmissible. (病毒是不可传播的。)
* **否定句:** The information is not intransmissible; it can be shared securely. (信息并非不可传播；它可以安全地共享。)
* **疑问句:** Is the disease intransmissible? (这种疾病是不可传播的吗？)


PHRASE->

There are no common idiomatic phrases using "intransmissible."  Its usage is largely confined to scientific or technical contexts.
