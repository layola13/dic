nonproportioned:/ˌnɒnprəˈpɔːʃənd/| adj. |不成比例的，不成比例的；不相称的|Not having the correct or expected proportions; out of proportion.  It refers to something lacking balance or harmony in its parts.  The term often describes physical objects, designs, or even abstract concepts where the elements don't relate to each other in a balanced or harmonious way.|例句：The building's design is nonproportioned, with oddly sized windows and doors. 这座建筑的设计不成比例，窗户和门的大小都很奇怪。

词源:

The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "proportioned" (meaning "having the correct proportions").  "Proportioned" itself comes from the noun "proportion," ultimately deriving from the Old French word "proporcion" and the Latin word "proportio," meaning "due share, proportion."
