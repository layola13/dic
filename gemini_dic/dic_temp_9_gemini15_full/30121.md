cedars:/ˈsiːdɑːrz/| n.|雪松树；雪松树林|The word "cedars" refers to trees of the genus Cedrus, belonging to the pine family Pinaceae.  They are large evergreen conifers known for their aromatic wood and distinctive appearance. Cedars are native to the mountains of the Himalayas, the Mediterranean region, and the Atlas Mountains of North Africa.  They are often used in landscaping and for their timber.|Example: The wind whispered through the ancient cedars on the mountainside.  山坡上古老的雪松树林中风声低语。  The landscape architect specified several varieties of cedars for the park's new plantings. 园林设计师为公园的新种植指定了几种雪松。|近义词：conifers, evergreens|反义词：无
