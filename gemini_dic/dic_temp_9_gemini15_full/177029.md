reanchors:/ˌriˈæŋkərz/ | v. |重新固定，重新锚定 | To secure or fasten something again, especially after it has become loose or detached.  The term often implies a prior anchoring that has failed or needs reinforcement. | 例句：The divers reanchored the buoy after the storm.  潜水员在暴风雨后重新固定了浮标。


The ship's crew reanchored in a more sheltered bay. 船员在更安全的港湾重新抛锚。

近义词：refasten, secure, re-establish, reinforce | 反义词：unmoor, detach, loosen

词源:
The word "reanchors" is formed by adding the prefix "re-" (meaning "again" or "anew") to the verb "anchors."  "Anchor" itself comes from the Old French word "ancre," ultimately deriving from the Greek word "ankura."
