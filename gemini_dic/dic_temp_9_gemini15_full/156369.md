physiological:/ˌfɪziəˈlɒdʒɪkəl/|/ˌfɪzioˈlɑːdʒɪkəl/ | adj. |生理的；生理学的 | Relating to the branch of biology that deals with the normal functions of living organisms and their parts.  It refers to processes occurring in living organisms, particularly those relating to the functions and activities of their organs and tissues. | Example:  The experiment measured the physiological effects of the drug.  这个实验测量了药物的生理作用。

The physiological response to stress can vary greatly among individuals.  对压力的生理反应因人而异。

近义词：biological, bodily, somatic | 反义词：psychological, mental

词源:
From Late Latin *physiologicus, from Greek φυσιολογικός (physiologikós), from φύσις (phýsis) "nature" + λόγος (lógos) "word, reason".  The word essentially means "the study of nature".
