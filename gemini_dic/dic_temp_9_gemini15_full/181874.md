reluct:/rɪˈlʌkt/| adj.|不情愿的，勉强的| unwilling; hesitant; showing reluctance.  |例句：He reluctantly agreed to help. 他不情愿地同意帮忙。|例句：She gave a reluctant smile. 她勉强地笑了笑。|近义词：hesitant, unwilling, resistant, loath |反义词：willing, eager, enthusiastic


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is reluctant to admit his mistake. = 他不情愿承认自己的错误。
They are reluctant to share their resources. = 他们不愿分享他们的资源。


一般过去时:
She was reluctant to leave her friends. = 她不愿离开她的朋友们。
He was reluctant to accept the offer. = 他不愿接受这个提议。


现在完成时:
I have been reluctant to ask for help. = 我一直不愿寻求帮助。
They have been reluctant to invest in new technology. = 他们一直不愿投资新技术。


过去完成时:
He had been reluctant to tell the truth. = 他一直不愿说实话。
She had been reluctant to commit to a long-term relationship. = 她一直不愿承诺一段长期关系。


将来时:
She will be reluctant to go to the party. = 她将不愿去参加聚会。
He will be reluctant to accept responsibility. = 他将不愿承担责任。


虚拟语气:
I wish I weren't so reluctant to try new things. = 我希望我不那么抗拒尝试新事物。
If he weren't so reluctant, he would have already finished the project. = 如果他不那么抗拒，他早就完成项目了。


被动语态:
His participation was reluctantly accepted. = 他的参与勉强被接受了。
The decision was reluctantly implemented. = 这一决定勉强得到执行。


疑问句:
Are you reluctant to help? = 你不愿意帮忙吗？
Is he reluctant to explain? = 他不愿意解释吗？


否定句:
He is not reluctant to work hard. = 他不反对努力工作。
She is not reluctant to face challenges. = 她不害怕面对挑战。


PHRASE->
be reluctant to do sth. = 不情愿做某事
show reluctance = 表现出不情愿
reluctant agreement = 不情愿的同意
meet with reluctance = 勉强接受
overcome reluctance = 克服不情愿
