salmonella:/ˌsæl.məˈnɛ.lə/| n. |沙门氏菌|Salmonella is a genus of rod-shaped Gram-negative bacteria that causes salmonellosis.  These bacteria are commonly found in the intestines of animals, birds, and reptiles, and can contaminate food and water. Infection typically occurs through consumption of contaminated food or water. Symptoms of salmonellosis can range from mild to severe, including diarrhea, fever, and abdominal cramps. |Example:  The outbreak of salmonella was traced back to contaminated eggs. 沙门氏菌的爆发追溯到受污染的鸡蛋。

The restaurant was shut down after a case of salmonella poisoning was reported.  一家餐馆在发生一起沙门氏菌中毒事件后被关闭了。|近义词：bacteria, germ |反义词:无

词源:

The genus name *Salmonella* is derived from  Daniel Elmer Salmon (1850-1914), a prominent American veterinary pathologist and bacteriologist.  The species name *typhimurium*, for example, reflects the bacteria's association with typhoid fever (although not all salmonella cause typhoid).
