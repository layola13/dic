reposition's:/ˌriːpəˈzɪʃənz/ | n. |重新定位；重新安排位置 |The act of repositioning something.  It's the possessive form of the verb "reposition".|例句：The company's repositioning strategy aims to attract a younger demographic. 公司的重新定位策略旨在吸引更年轻的人群。 The marketing team is working on the product's repositioning. 营销团队正在致力于产品的重新定位。|近义词：realignment, relocation, readjustment |反义词：fixation, stabilization


USAGE->

The company repositions its products annually. = 公司每年都会重新定位其产品。
They reposition the furniture in the living room. = 他们重新安排客厅的家具。
