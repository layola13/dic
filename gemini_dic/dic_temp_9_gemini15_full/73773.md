formalisms:/ˈfɔːrməlɪzəm/| n.|形式主义；形式论；程式；约定俗成|The established conventions or procedures, especially in art, science, or philosophy.  It can also refer to the adherence to prescribed forms or rules, sometimes to the detriment of substance or meaning.|例句：The artist rejected the formalisms of traditional painting.  这位艺术家拒绝了传统绘画的形式主义。|例句：The excessive formalisms of the legal system often hinder justice. 法律体系中过多的形式主义常常阻碍正义。|近义词：convention, procedure, ritual, system|反义词：informality, naturalness, spontaneity
