going:/ˈɡoʊɪŋ/ | v. |进行中；即将发生；离开；(用于进行时态)| 英文解析：The present participle of "go," indicating an action in progress, a future event, departure, or used in continuous tenses. | 例句：The project is going well. 项目进展顺利。

The children are going to the park. 孩子们要去公园。


近义词：proceeding, progressing, departing, leaving | 反义词：stopping, ceasing, arriving


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I am going to the store. = 我要去商店。
He is going to school. = 他要去上学。


一般过去时:
I was going home when it started to rain. = 我回家的时候开始下雨了。
She was going to the library, but changed her mind. = 她要去图书馆，但是改变主意了。


现在完成时:
I have been going to that restaurant for years. = 我去那家餐馆很多年了。
They have been going on a trip every summer. = 他们每年夏天都去旅行。


过去完成时:
I had been going to the gym regularly before my injury. = 我受伤之前经常去健身房。
She had been going to school for five years before she graduated. = 她上学五年后才毕业。


将来时:
I am going to visit my parents next week. = 我下周要去看望我的父母。
They are going to have a party on Saturday. = 他们星期六要举行派对。


虚拟语气:
If I were going to the party, I would wear my new dress. = 如果我去参加聚会，我会穿上我的新衣服。
I wish I were going on vacation. = 我真希望我去度假。


被动语态:
The meeting is going to be held tomorrow. = 会议将在明天举行。
The project is going to be delayed. = 项目将被推迟。


疑问句:
Are you going to the cinema tonight? = 你今晚去看电影吗？
Is he going to the meeting? = 他要去参加会议吗？


否定句:
I am not going to the party. = 我不去参加聚会。
She is not going to school today. = 她今天不去上学。


PHRASE->
going on = 发生着
going to = 将要
going over = 复习
going out = 外出
going through = 经历


词源:
The word "going" originates from the Old English verb "gān," meaning "to go."  The present participle "-ing" ending is a standard grammatical formation in English.
