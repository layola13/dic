counter-disengagement: /ˈkaʊntərˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt/ | n. | 反向脱离；对抗性脱离 | The process of actively resisting or opposing a disengagement or withdrawal.  It implies a deliberate action to prevent or reverse a separation or disconnection.  This term is often used in political, military, or social contexts. | Example: The counter-disengagement strategy involved strengthening alliances and increasing military presence.  反向脱离战略包括加强联盟和增加军事存在。 |近义词:  opposition to disengagement,  resistance to withdrawal | 反义词:  engagement,  cooperation,  withdrawal,  retreat |  词性：名词
