moccasins:/ˈmɑːkəsɪnz/| n. |鹿皮鞋；软皮鞋 | Soft leather shoes, typically made from deerskin or similar material, and often without a heel.  They are traditionally associated with Native American footwear. |例句：She wore comfortable moccasins on the hike. 她远足时穿着一双舒适的鹿皮鞋。|例句：The museum displayed a collection of antique moccasins. 博物馆展出了一批古董鹿皮鞋。|近义词：slippers, loafers|反义词：high heels, boots

词源:
The word "moccasin" comes from the Algonquian word *makasin*, meaning "shoe".  Various Algonquian languages contributed to the word's development and eventual adoption into English.
