chaser:/ˈtʃeɪsər/| n. v.|追逐者；驱逐者；（尤指酒后喝的）解酒饮料；|英文解析：A person or thing that chases.  Also, a drink taken after another alcoholic beverage to relieve its effects. |例句：The police were in hot pursuit of the chaser. 警方向追捕者穷追不舍。 He drank a chaser of water after his whiskey. 他喝完威士忌后又喝了一杯水解酒。|近义词：pursuer, follower,  (for drink)  follow-up,  |反义词：chased, pursued


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He chases the ball. = 他追逐球。
The dog chases the cat. = 狗追逐猫。

一般过去时:
She chased the thief down the street. = 她追逐小偷跑下街。
They chased after the bus. = 他们追赶公共汽车。

现在完成时:
The police have chased the suspect for miles. = 警方已经追捕嫌疑犯数英里了。
I have chased my dreams for years. = 我多年来一直追逐我的梦想。


过去完成时:
He had chased the rabbit into the woods. = 他曾把兔子追到树林里。
They had chased the robbers before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，他们已经追捕了强盗。


将来时:
I will chase my goals relentlessly. = 我将不懈地追求我的目标。
We will chase the sunset on the beach. = 我们将在海滩上追逐日落。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would chase my dreams. = 如果我是你，我会追逐我的梦想。
I wish I had chased that opportunity. = 我真希望我当时抓住了那个机会。


被动语态:
The thief was chased by the police. = 小偷被警察追捕。
The ball was chased by the dog. = 球被狗追逐。


疑问句:
Who is chasing whom? = 谁在追谁？
What are you chasing? = 你在追什么？


否定句:
He didn't chase the thief. = 他没有追捕小偷。
She doesn't chase after fame. = 她不追求名利。
