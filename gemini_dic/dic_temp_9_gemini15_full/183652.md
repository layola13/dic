reregulating: /ˌriːˈreɡjʊleɪtɪŋ/ | v. |重新规范；再次调节 |  The act of regulating something again, often after a period of deregulation or insufficient regulation.  It implies a return to or strengthening of previous regulatory measures. | 例句：The government is reregulating the financial industry after the recent crisis. 政府在最近的危机之后正在重新规范金融业。


The government is reregulating the telecommunications market to promote competition. 政府正在重新规范电信市场以促进竞争。


近义词：re-regulating, readjusting, reforming, restructuring | 反义词：deregulating, liberalizing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government reregulates the market to protect consumers. = 政府重新规范市场以保护消费者。
They reregulate the industry to improve safety standards. = 他们重新规范该行业以提高安全标准。

一般过去时:
The agency reregulated the industry last year. = 该机构去年重新规范了该行业。
The country reregulated its banking sector after the financial crisis. = 金融危机后，该国重新规范了其银行业。


现在完成时:
They have reregulated the transportation sector to reduce emissions. = 他们已经重新规范了交通部门以减少排放。
The authorities have reregulated the water supply to ensure clean water. = 当局已经重新规范了供水以确保清洁用水。


过去完成时:
Before the new law, they had reregulated the fishing industry several times. = 在新法律颁布之前，他们已经多次重新规范了渔业。
The company had reregulated its internal procedures before the audit. = 在审计之前，该公司已经重新规范了其内部程序。


将来时:
The government will reregulate the energy sector next year. = 政府明年将重新规范能源部门。
They will reregulate the pharmaceutical market to control drug prices. = 他们将重新规范药品市场以控制药价。


虚拟语气:
If the government reregulated the market, prices might fall. = 如果政府重新规范市场，价格可能会下降。
It would be better if they reregulated the industry more strictly. = 如果他们更严格地重新规范该行业，那就更好了。


被动语态:
The industry was reregulated by the government. = 该行业由政府重新规范。
The rules were reregulated to address the safety concerns. = 规则被重新规范以解决安全问题。


疑问句:
Will the government reregulate the healthcare system? = 政府会重新规范医疗体系吗？
Has the company reregulated its operations following the scandal? = 该公司在丑闻发生后是否重新规范了其运营？


否定句:
The government did not reregulate the market immediately. = 政府没有立即重新规范市场。
They have not yet reregulated the transportation industry. = 他们尚未重新规范交通运输业。

词源:
re- (prefix indicating again or anew) + regulate (from Latin regula, meaning "rule" or "standard")
