detonation:/ˌdɛtəˈneɪʃən/| n. | 爆炸；爆破；引爆 | The sudden and violent explosion of a substance, especially an explosive.  It involves a rapid expansion of gases, producing a loud noise and often destructive force. |例句：The detonation of the bomb caused widespread destruction. 炸弹的爆炸造成了大范围的破坏。

例句：The accidental detonation of the fireworks led to injuries.  意外引爆的烟花导致了人员受伤。


近义词：explosion, blast, burst


反义词：implosion (内爆)


词源：From the word "detonate", which comes from the Latin "detōnāre" meaning "to thunder down".  This reflects the loud noise associated with detonations.
