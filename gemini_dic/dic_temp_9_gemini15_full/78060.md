geocells:/ˈdʒioʊsɛlz/| n. |地理单元格，地球单元格 |A geocell is a hierarchical spatial data structure that divides the Earth's surface into a grid of cells.  These cells are identified by a unique code, and their size varies depending on the level of detail required. Geocells are often used in location-based services and geographic information systems (GIS) to efficiently store, index, and query spatial data. They offer a balance between precision and storage efficiency, particularly useful for large datasets. |Example:  The application uses geocells to quickly find nearby restaurants.  该应用程序使用地理单元格快速查找附近的餐馆。|近义词：spatial index, grid cell |反义词：无
