low-profile: /ˈloʊ ˈproʊfaɪl/ | adj. | 低调的，不张扬的 |  Describes someone or something that avoids attracting attention.  It suggests a deliberate attempt to remain inconspicuous or unnoticed. | 例句：He maintains a low profile to avoid media scrutiny. 他保持低调以避免媒体审查。

He prefers a low-profile lifestyle, shunning public appearances. 他更喜欢低调的生活方式，避免公开露面。


近义词：unassuming, inconspicuous, modest, discreet | 反义词：high-profile, flamboyant, ostentatious, showy


词源:  The word "low-profile" is a compound adjective. "Low" means "not high" or "small,"  and "profile" in this context refers to a person's public image or the degree to which they are known or noticed.  The combination suggests a reduced or minimized public presence.  The use of "profile" in this figurative sense developed from its literal meaning referring to a side view or outline, particularly in architecture or photography.
