tremendous: /trəˈmɛndəs/ | adj. |巨大的，非常的，极好的 | extremely large or great; extraordinarily good. |例句：The tremendous size of the building is impressive. 这座建筑的巨大规模令人印象深刻。
例句：She showed tremendous courage in the face of danger. 面对危险，她表现出极大的勇气。|近义词：huge, immense, enormous, vast, fantastic, amazing |反义词：tiny, minuscule, insignificant, small, awful, terrible


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The task is tremendous. = 这项任务非常艰巨。
The view is tremendous. = 景色非常壮观。

一般过去时:
The storm was tremendous. = 那场暴风雨非常猛烈。
The success of the project was tremendous. = 项目的成功是巨大的。

现在完成时:
They have achieved tremendous progress. = 他们取得了巨大的进步。
We have had a tremendous time. = 我们玩得很开心。

过去完成时:
The damage had been tremendous after the earthquake. = 地震过后，损失惨重。
He had made a tremendous effort. = 他付出了巨大的努力。

将来时:
The impact will be tremendous. = 其影响将是巨大的。
The future possibilities are tremendous. = 未来的可能性是巨大的。

虚拟语气:
If we had more resources, the impact would be tremendous. = 如果我们有更多资源，影响将是巨大的。
It would be tremendous if the project were successful. = 如果项目成功，那将是巨大的成功。


被动语态:
The results were considered tremendous. = 结果被认为是巨大的。
His contribution was deemed tremendous. = 他的贡献被认为是巨大的。

疑问句:
Is the challenge tremendous? = 挑战巨大吗？
Was the effort tremendous? = 努力巨大吗？

否定句:
The effect was not tremendous. = 效果并不显著。
The difference was not tremendous. = 区别并不巨大。

词源:
tremendous源自于拉丁语词根“tremendus”，由“tremere”（震动，颤抖）和“-dus”（形容词后缀）构成。最初的意思是“令人恐惧的”，后来演变为形容事物规模或程度巨大，也指极好。
