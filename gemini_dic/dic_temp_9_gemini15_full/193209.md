seducible: /sɪˈdjuːsəbl/ | adj. | 易被诱惑的；易受影响的 | Easily influenced or persuaded to do something, especially something morally wrong.  |例句：He found her surprisingly seducible. 他发现她出乎意料地容易被诱惑。

例句：The young man, despite his better judgment, found himself seducible by the allure of easy money.  年轻男子尽管明知不可为，却发现自己被轻松赚钱的诱惑所吸引。


近义词：persuadable, susceptible, impressionable, vulnerable

反义词：resistant, immune, impervious, unyielding

词源:
From the verb "seduce," ultimately from Latin *sedūcĕre, from *sed- (aside) + dūcĕre (to lead).  Meaning "to lead astray" or "to entice." The adjective "seducible" thus means "capable of being led astray" or "easily enticed."
