compensation: /ˌkɒmpenˈseɪʃən/ /ˌkɑːmpənˈseɪʃən/ | n. | 补偿；赔偿；报酬 |

英文解析:Something, typically money, awarded to someone as a recompense for loss, injury, or suffering.  It can refer to payment for work or services, or to something that counterbalances or makes up for something else.

例句:

He received compensation for his injuries sustained in the accident. = 他因事故受伤而获得赔偿。

The company offers competitive compensation packages to attract top talent. = 公司提供具有竞争力的薪酬待遇以吸引顶尖人才。


近义词:remuneration, recompense, indemnity, redress, repayment

反义词:penalty, punishment, loss


USAGE->

What compensation do you receive for this work? = 你做这项工作获得什么报酬？

现在完成时:

The government has compensated farmers for the crop failure. = 政府已经赔偿了农民的歉收损失。


过去完成时:

词源:
From Middle English *compensacioun, from Old French *compensacion, from Latin compensatio, from compensare "to weigh together, recompense," from com- "together" + pensare "to weigh."
