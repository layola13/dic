canton's:/ˈkæntɒnz/| n.|广州的；广东的；|Canton's is a possessive form indicating belonging to Canton (广州) or Guangdong province (广东省). It's often used to describe something originating from or related to these regions in China.|例句：Canton's cuisine is renowned for its delicate flavors.  广州菜以其精致的味道而闻名。|近义词：Guangdong's, Guangzhou's|反义词：无


USAGE->
This word is a possessive form and doesn't have verb conjugations like "What".  It functions as an adjective modifying a noun.  Therefore, typical verb tense examples are not applicable.  It only shows up in noun phrases.


PHRASE->
Canton's silk, Canton's tea, Canton's opera, Canton's history, Canton's architecture
