quadrat:/ˈkwɒdræt/ /ˈkwɑːdræt/| n. |样方，方格 | A quadrat is a square frame of a known size, used in ecological studies to sample the distribution of organisms.  It allows researchers to count the number of individuals of a particular species within a defined area, providing data for population density estimations and community analyses. | Example:  The ecologist used a 1-meter quadrat to sample the plant species in the meadow. 生态学家使用1米见方的样方来采集草地上的植物种类样本。

Example: Researchers placed quadrats at regular intervals across the field to assess the distribution of wildflowers. 研究人员在田野中以规则的间隔放置样方，以评估野花的分布。
