dug-in:/dʌɡˈɪn/| adj.| 固执的；坚守阵地的；顽固的 | entrenched; firmly established; resistant to change;  determined to stay in a place or situation. | 例句：The dug-in soldiers refused to surrender. 坚守阵地的士兵拒绝投降。


The dug-in defense held despite the enemy's attack.  尽管敌人进攻，但坚固的防御工事仍然坚守。| 近义词：entrenched, steadfast, resolute, stubborn | 反义词：yielding, flexible, compromising, wavering | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The soldiers are dug in. = 士兵们坚守阵地。
He is dug in on his position. = 他坚持自己的立场。


一般过去时:
The army dug in for the night. = 部队扎营过夜。
They dug in their heels and refused to budge. = 他们固执己见，拒绝让步。


现在完成时:
They have dug in for the long haul. = 他们已经做好长期坚持的准备。
The company has dug in its heels and won't negotiate. = 公司固执己见，拒绝谈判。


过去完成时:
The troops had dug in before the attack began. = 部队在攻击开始之前就已构筑工事。
She had dug in her heels about the issue for weeks. = 她几周来一直对这个问题固执己见。


将来时:
The rebels will dig in and resist the government forces. = 反叛者将坚守阵地，抵抗政府军。
We will dig in and finish this project. = 我们将坚持到底，完成这个项目。


虚拟语气:
If they had dug in earlier, they would have survived the attack. = 如果他们早些构筑工事，他们就能在袭击中幸存下来。
If he dug in his heels, he would lose the argument. = 如果他固执己见，他会输掉这场争论。


被动语态:
The trenches were dug in by the soldiers. = 战壕是由士兵们挖掘的。
The position was dug in and well-protected. = 阵地坚固且防御良好。


疑问句:
Are the enemy dug in? = 敌人是否坚守阵地？
Is he dug in on that idea? = 他是否坚持那个想法？


否定句:
The soldiers were not dug in properly. = 士兵们没有 properly 构筑工事。
He wasn't dug in on his position. = 他对自己的立场并不坚决。
