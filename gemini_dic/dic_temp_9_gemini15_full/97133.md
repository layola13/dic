infiltrating:/ˈɪnfɪltreɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 渗透的；潜入的；浸润的 |  Describes something or someone that is gradually spreading or penetrating into something else, often secretly or subtly.  It can refer to physical substances, ideas, or people. | 例句：The enemy soldiers were infiltrating our defenses. = 敌军士兵正在渗透我们的防线。

The rain was infiltrating the old roof, causing leaks. = 雨水正在渗透老旧的屋顶，导致漏水。


近义词：penetrating, permeating, seeping, invading | 反义词：extruding, evacuating, exiting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The water is infiltrating the soil. = 水正在渗透土壤。
Cancer cells are infiltrating the surrounding tissue. = 癌细胞正在浸润周围组织。

一般过去时:
The rebels infiltrated the city at night. = 叛军在夜间潜入了城市。

现在完成时:

The new ideas have infiltrated the company culture. = 新理念已经渗透到公司文化中。

过去完成时:

The enemy had infiltrated our ranks before the attack. = 攻击之前，敌人已经渗透到我们的队伍中。

将来时:

They will infiltrate the organization and gather intelligence. = 他们将渗透到该组织并收集情报。

虚拟语气:

否定句:
