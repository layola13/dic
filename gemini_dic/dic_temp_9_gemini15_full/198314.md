shovelbill:/ˈʃʌvəlˌbɪl/| n. |铲嘴鸟|A wading bird of the family Plataleidae, characterized by a broad, spatulate bill used for foraging in shallow water.  They are found in various parts of the world, notably in the Americas and Australia.  The bill is used to sift through mud and water to find food such as insects, crustaceans, and small fish.  |例句：The shovelbill gracefully swept its bill through the shallows, searching for food. 铲嘴鸟优雅地用它的铲状喙在浅水中搜寻食物。  The yellow-billed shovelbill is a striking bird with a vibrant plumage. 黄嘴铲嘴鸟是一种有着鲜艳羽毛的引人注目的鸟类。|近义词：spoonbill (though not always a perfect synonym, as some spoonbills are not shovelbills)|反义词：无
