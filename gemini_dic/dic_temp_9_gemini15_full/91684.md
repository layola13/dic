hypercolumn:/ˈhaɪpərˌkɑːləm/ | n. |超列, 超柱 | A hypercolumn is a functional unit in the visual cortex of the brain that contains all the neural machinery necessary to analyze a small region of the visual field.  It receives input from a specific area of the retina and processes information about color, orientation, and motion within that area.  This allows for a comprehensive analysis of the visual scene. | Example:  Research on hypercolumns has significantly advanced our understanding of how the brain processes visual information.  = 关于超列的研究极大地促进了我们对大脑如何处理视觉信息的理解。 | 近义词: cortical column (皮质柱) | 反义词:  (无直接反义词)
