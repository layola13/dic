cabaret:/ˌkæbəˈreɪ/ | n. |歌舞表演；夜总会 | A cabaret is a type of entertainment featuring singing, dancing, and often comedy, typically performed in a nightclub or restaurant.  It often involves a variety of acts and a lively atmosphere. | Example:  We went to a cabaret show in Paris and were amazed by the performers. 我们去了巴黎看了一场歌舞表演，对表演者感到惊叹。 |近义词：nightclub, revue, show |反义词：None directly applicable.

词源:
The word "cabaret" comes from the French word "cabarets,"  which means "tavern" or "wine cellar."  It evolved to refer to places serving alcohol and offering entertainment, eventually becoming the term for the entertainment itself.
