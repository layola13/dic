quasi-genteel: /ˈkwɑːzi ˌdʒɛnˈtiːl/ | adj. |  准上流社会的；假装优雅的；半文雅的 |  Suggesting or attempting an appearance of gentility or refinement that is not genuine or fully achieved.  It often implies a slightly affected or pretentious quality. |


例句：

Her quasi-genteel mannerisms, such as her overly precise pronunciation, did little to hide her working-class background. = 她的准上流社会举止，例如她过于精确的发音，并不能掩盖她工人阶级的背景。

The novel portrays a quasi-genteel family struggling to maintain their social standing despite dwindling fortunes. = 这部小说描绘了一个准上流社会家庭，尽管家道中落，仍在努力维持他们的社会地位。


近义词：affected, pretentious,  posh (informal),  upper-crust (informal),  high-falutin' (informal)


反义词：unrefined, vulgar, common,  uncouth


词源：

* **quasi-**:  from Latin, meaning "as if," "seemingly," or "almost."

* **genteel**: from Old French *gentil*, from Latin *gentilis*, meaning "of noble birth," "refined," or "well-bred."
