prebuilding: /ˌpriːˈbɪldɪŋ/ | n. | 预制建筑；预制构件 | The process of constructing building components off-site before assembling them on the building site.  This method often involves the use of prefabricated materials and modules.  It aims to speed up construction, improve quality control, and reduce on-site disruption. | Example: The prebuilding of the wall sections significantly reduced the overall construction time. 预制墙体大大缩短了整体施工时间。| 近义词: prefabrication, modular construction | 反义词: on-site construction
