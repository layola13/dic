magnesia:/mæɡˈniːʒə/ | n. | 氧化镁；苦土 |  A white, earthy substance, magnesium oxide (MgO), used in medicine as an antacid and laxative, and in various industrial applications.  | Example:  The doctor prescribed magnesia for her indigestion.  医生给她开了氧化镁治消化不良。 |近义词：magnesium oxide | 反义词：无  | 名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate through verb tenses.  Therefore, tense examples are not applicable.


PHRASE->
milk of magnesia:  氧化镁乳剂
magnesia alba:  轻质氧化镁
burnt magnesia:  重质氧化镁
magnesia usta:  煅烧氧化镁
magnesium hydroxide: 氢氧化镁 (often used interchangeably with milk of magnesia in some contexts)


词源:
The word "magnesia" ultimately derives from the Greek word Μαγνησία (Magnēsia), referring to the ancient region of Magnesia in Thessaly, Greece, where the mineral was first discovered.  The name was later applied to the mineral itself, and subsequently to magnesium oxide.
