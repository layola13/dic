poised:/pɔɪzd/| adj.|摆好姿势的；准备好的；沉着的；优雅的|Ready to act; balanced; graceful and self-possessed.  The poised athlete waited for the starting gun.  |近义词：ready, prepared, composed, graceful, balanced|反义词：unprepared, nervous, awkward, clumsy


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is poised to strike. = 他准备好了要攻击。
She is always poised and elegant. = 她总是沉着优雅。


一般过去时:
The bird was poised on the branch. = 那只鸟停留在树枝上。
He was poised to answer the question. = 他准备好回答问题了。


现在完成时:
The company has been poised for growth. = 这家公司已经准备好发展了。
She has remained poised throughout the crisis. = 在整个危机中，她一直保持着镇定。


过去完成时:
He had been poised to jump when the whistle blew. = 哨子吹响时，他已经准备好跳了。
They had poised themselves for the difficult negotiation. = 他们已经准备好进行艰难的谈判了。


将来时:
The rocket will be poised for launch. = 火箭将准备发射。
She will be poised to take the lead. = 她将准备好领先。


虚拟语气:
If he were poised and calm, he would succeed. = 如果他沉着冷静，他就会成功。
If she had been poised, she would have handled the situation better. = 如果她当时镇定自若，她会更好地处理这种情况。


被动语态:
The camera was poised for the perfect shot. = 相机已准备好拍摄完美的镜头。
The plan was poised to be implemented. = 计划准备实施。


疑问句:
Is he poised to make a decision? = 他准备好做决定了吗？
Are you poised to accept the challenge? = 你准备好接受挑战了吗？


否定句:
He was not poised for such a sudden attack. = 他没有准备好应对如此突然的袭击。
She was not poised enough to deliver the speech. = 她不够镇定来发表演讲。
