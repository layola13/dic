jeered:/dʒɪərd/| v.|嘲笑，讥讽|To make fun of someone or something in a loud, unkind way.  It often involves shouting or making mocking sounds.|例句：The crowd jeered at the losing team.  观众嘲笑输掉的球队。
例句：They jeered and shouted insults at the speaker.  他们对演讲者发出嘲笑和辱骂。|近义词：mock, taunt, scoff, ridicule|反义词：praise, applaud


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They jeer at the homeless people. = 他们嘲笑无家可归的人。
The children jeer at the clumsy boy. = 孩子们嘲笑那个笨拙的男孩。


一般过去时:
The audience jeered at the comedian's jokes. = 观众嘲笑喜剧演员的笑话。
He jeered at her attempts to sing. = 他嘲笑她唱歌的尝试。


现在完成时:
The fans have jeered at the referee all game. = 球迷整场比赛都在嘲笑裁判。
She has jeered at his political views for years. = 她多年来一直嘲笑他的政治观点。


过去完成时:
The crowd had jeered at him before he even started speaking. = 他还没开始说话，人群就开始嘲笑他了。
They had jeered at his ideas long before he presented them. = 他们在很久以前，在他提出这些想法之前，就已经嘲笑它们了。


将来时:

I'm sure they will jeer at my new haircut. = 我确信他们会嘲笑我的新发型。


虚拟语气:
If they jeered at him again, he would walk off the stage. = 如果他们再次嘲笑他，他会下台。
I wish they hadn't jeered at my performance. = 我希望他们没有嘲笑我的表演。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
