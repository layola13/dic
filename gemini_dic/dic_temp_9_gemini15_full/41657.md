correlated:/ˈkɔːrəleɪtɪd/| adj.|相关的，互相关的|Existing or occurring together in a way that suggests a connection but does not necessarily imply cause and effect.  Correlation does not equal causation.|例句：Studies have shown a strong correlation between smoking and lung cancer. 研究表明吸烟和肺癌之间有很强的相关性。
例句：The two events are correlated, but one doesn't necessarily cause the other. 这两件事是相关的，但并不一定意味着一个导致另一个。|近义词：associated, connected, related|反义词：unrelated, unconnected, independent|词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The data points are correlated. = 数据点是相关的。
Smoking and lung cancer are correlated. = 吸烟和肺癌是相关的。

一般过去时:
The results were highly correlated. = 结果高度相关。
The two variables were correlated in the study. = 在这项研究中，这两个变量是相关的。

现在完成时:
Researchers have found that these factors have been correlated. = 研究人员发现这些因素一直相关。
Many studies have correlated stress with illness. = 许多研究都将压力与疾病联系起来。

过去完成时:
Previous studies had correlated similar factors. = 先前的研究已经将类似的因素联系起来。
It had been correlated that high blood pressure and heart disease are related.  = 高血压和心脏病相关这一点已经被关联起来。


将来时:

We expect that the new data will correlate with previous findings. = 我们预计新数据将与之前的发现相关。

虚拟语气:
