mollifyingly:/ˈmɒlɪfaɪɪŋli/ | adv. | 温和地；抚慰地；缓和地 | In a way that soothes or calms.  It describes an action or manner that is intended to reduce anger, anxiety, or distress. |例句：He spoke mollifyingly to the angry customer, eventually calming them down. 他温和地对生气顾客说话，最终使他们平静下来。

例句：She touched his arm mollifyingly, hoping to ease his worries. 她抚慰地碰了碰他的胳膊，希望能减轻他的担忧。


近义词：soothingly, placatingly, appeasingly, conciliatorily

反义词：aggressively, antagonistically, irritably, provokingly


词源:  Derived from the verb "mollify," which comes from the Late Latin *mollificare, meaning "to soften." This in turn comes from the Latin *mollis ("soft") + *facere ("to make").  The "-ingly" suffix transforms the adjective "mollifying" into an adverb.
