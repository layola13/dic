incarcerates:/ɪnˈkɑːrsəreɪts/| vt.|监禁，关押|To imprison or confine someone.|The government incarcerates political dissidents.|近义词：imprison, jail, confine, detain|反义词：release, liberate, free|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The prison incarcerates many criminals. = 监狱关押着许多罪犯。
He incarcerates his political opponents. = 他监禁他的政治对手。

一般过去时:
They incarcerated him for ten years. = 他们将他监禁了十年。
The dictator incarcerated thousands of people. = 独裁者监禁了成千上万人。

现在完成时:
The government has incarcerated many protesters. = 政府已经监禁了许多抗议者。
She has been incarcerated since last year. = 她从去年起就被监禁了。

过去完成时:
He had been incarcerated before, so he knew what to expect. = 他以前被监禁过，所以他知道该期待什么。
The police had incarcerated the suspect before the trial. = 警方在审判前已将嫌疑人监禁。

将来时:
They will incarcerate him if he doesn't pay the fine. = 如果他不付罚款，他们将监禁他。
The court will incarcerate the defendant for his crimes. = 法院将因其罪行而监禁被告。

虚拟语气:
If they incarcerated him, there would be a riot. = 如果他们监禁他，就会发生暴动。
I wish they hadn't incarcerated him so quickly. = 我希望他们没有那么快就将他监禁。

被动语态:
He was incarcerated for his crimes. = 他因犯罪而被监禁。
Many innocent people have been incarcerated unjustly. = 许多无辜的人被不公正地监禁。

疑问句:
Was he incarcerated for murder? = 他因谋杀而被监禁了吗？
Will they incarcerate the suspect? = 他们会监禁嫌疑犯吗？

否定句:
They did not incarcerate him immediately. = 他们没有立即监禁他。
The judge decided not to incarcerate the defendant. = 法官决定不监禁被告。


PHRASE->
be incarcerated for = 因…被监禁
incarcerate someone in = 将某人监禁于
incarcerate without trial = 未经审判就监禁
lengthy incarceration = 长期监禁
mass incarceration = 大规模监禁
