hyperdicrotic: /ˌhaɪpərdaɪˈkrɒtɪk/ | adj. | 双峰的；(脉搏)双收缩的 | Having or characterized by two dicrotic notches in the pulse wave.  This refers to a pulse with two distinct peaks following the main systole, often indicating a condition where the aortic valve closes slowly or incompletely. | Example: The patient's hyperdicrotic pulse suggested aortic regurgitation.  这位病人的双峰脉搏提示主动脉瓣反流。| 近义词: double-peaked (in the context of pulse) | 反义词: monocrotic (single-peaked pulse) |


词源:

* **hyper-**:  From Greek ὑπέρ (hupér), meaning "over," "above," "beyond."  It indicates an excessive or heightened degree.

* **dicrotic**: From Greek δίς (dís), meaning "twice," and κρóτος (krótos), meaning "a beating," "a striking."  It refers to a double beat or wave, specifically in the context of the pulse.

用法说明:

hyperdicrotic 主要用作形容词，修饰名词，通常指脉搏。它描述了一种脉搏的特征，即在一次心跳的收缩期之后，脉搏波形出现两个明显的峰值。这通常是由于主动脉瓣关闭不完全或关闭缓慢引起的，提示某些疾病，如主动脉瓣关闭不全。


例句:

The physician noted the patient's hyperdicrotic pulse, a sign of possible heart valve problems. 医生注意到患者的双峰脉搏，这是可能存在心脏瓣膜问题的迹象。

A hyperdicrotic pulse wave is characteristic of aortic insufficiency. 双峰脉搏波是主动脉关闭不全的特征。


注意：  由于 hyperdicrotic  是一个比较专业的医学术语，其用法主要局限于医学领域，在日常生活中很少用到。
