rise:/raɪz/| vi. n.|上升；上涨；起床；升起|to move upwards; to increase in amount, level, or intensity; to get out of bed; to come into view|The sun rises in the east. 太阳从东方升起。|The price of oil has risen sharply. 油价急剧上涨。|近义词：ascend, climb, increase, mount|反义词：fall, descend, decrease, sink|动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun rises in the east. = 太阳从东方升起。
The water level rises during high tide. = 高潮时水位上升。

一般过去时:
The balloon rose into the air. = 气球升上了天空。
She rose from her chair. = 她从椅子上站起来。

现在完成时:
The temperature has risen significantly. = 温度已经显著上升。
The sea level has risen over the past century. = 在过去的一个世纪里，海平面已经上升。

过去完成时:
The stock market had risen before the news broke. = 在消息传出之前，股市已经上涨。
By the time I arrived, the river had risen considerably. = 当我到达的时候，河水已经涨了不少。

将来时:
The price of bread will rise next month. = 面包的价格下个月会上涨。
The sun will rise tomorrow morning. = 明天早上太阳会升起。

虚拟语气:
If the temperature rose above 30 degrees, we would stay inside. = 如果温度超过30度，我们就待在室内。
I wish the water level didn't rise so quickly. = 我希望水位不要涨得这么快。

被动语态:
The flag was risen slowly. = 旗子慢慢地升起来了。(被动语态较少用于rise)
The matter was risen at the meeting. = 会议上提出了这个问题。(被动语态较少用于rise, 此处指"被提及")

疑问句:
Does the sun rise in the west? = 太阳是从西方升起吗？
Will the price of petrol rise again? = 汽油价格还会上涨吗？

否定句:
The plane didn't rise above the clouds. = 飞机没有飞到云层之上。
The bread didn't rise properly. = 面包没有好好发酵。


PHRASE->
rise and fall = 起伏；盛衰
rise to the occasion = 迎接挑战；应付紧急情况
rise above = 克服；超越
rise against = 反抗；起义
give rise to = 引起；导致
