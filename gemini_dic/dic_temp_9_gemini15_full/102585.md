jahvistic: /dʒæˈvɪstɪk/ | adj. | 雅威的；与雅威相关的 | Relating to or characteristic of Yahweh (the Hebrew name for God) as revealed in the Old Testament. The term often highlights the anthropomorphic and sometimes violent aspects of God's portrayal in certain biblical passages.  | The Jahvistic source is considered one of the four main sources of the Pentateuch. | 近义词：Yahwistic | 反义词：None specifically, but terms suggesting a different theological perspective could be considered contrasting.


USAGE->
This word is primarily used in academic discussions of the Old Testament/Hebrew Bible, specifically within the documentary hypothesis.  It doesn't have common usage in everyday conversation.

Example 1:  Scholars debate the extent to which the Jahvistic narratives reflect historical events.  学者们争论雅威叙事在多大程度上反映了历史事件。

Example 2: The Jahvistic portrayal of God is often characterized by its immediacy and emotional intensity. 雅威的描绘常常以其直接性和情感的强烈程度为特征。


PHRASE->
There aren't common phrases using "Jahvistic".  The word itself is used as an adjective to describe specific texts or aspects of biblical scholarship.
