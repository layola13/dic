/ˈmɑːrɡɪn/| n. |页边空白；利润；优势；差距 | A margin is the empty space around the edge of a page, or the difference between two figures.  It can also refer to a profit or an advantage. |例句：The printer left a wide margin on each page.  打印机在每一页都留出了宽阔的页边空白。|例句：The company made a healthy profit margin last year. 公司去年获得了可观的利润。|近义词：edge, border, profit, advantage, difference |反义词：center, core, loss, disadvantage


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The margin is too narrow. = 页边距太窄了。
What is the profit margin? = 利润是多少？


一般过去时:
The margin was sufficient. = 页边距足够了。
The company's profit margin fell last quarter. = 公司上季度的利润率下降了。


现在完成时:
They have increased the margin significantly. = 他们已经大幅增加了利润率。
The printer has added a wider margin to the document. = 打印机已在文档中添加了更宽的页边距。


过去完成时:
The margin had already been set before the printing started. = 在印刷开始之前页边距就已经设定好了。
The company had lost its market margin before the new product launched. = 在新产品推出之前，该公司已经失去了市场份额。


将来时:
We will reduce the margin next year. = 我们明年将减少利润率。
The new design will have a larger margin. = 新的设计将有更大的页边距。


虚拟语气:
If the margin were wider, it would look better. = 如果页边距更宽，看起来会更好。
I wish the profit margin were higher. = 我希望利润率更高。


被动语态:
The margin was adjusted by the designer. = 页边距由设计师调整。
The profit margin was heavily affected by the economic downturn. = 利润率受到经济衰退的严重影响。


疑问句:
What is the margin of error? = 误差范围是多少？
What is the margin on this product? = 此产品的利润是多少？


否定句:
There is no margin for error. = 没有犯错的余地。
The company did not achieve its target profit margin. = 公司没有达到目标利润率。
