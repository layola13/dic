girdling: /ˈɡɜːrdlɪŋ/ | v. | 环状剥皮；束缚；包围 |

英文解析:To girdle something means to encircle it completely, often with the intention of damaging or destroying it.  It most commonly refers to the practice of removing a ring of bark from the trunk of a tree, which prevents the flow of sap and ultimately kills the tree.  It can also be used figuratively to describe something that restricts or confines.

例句:

The farmer girdled the diseased tree to prevent it from spreading.  = 农民环状剥皮了那棵病树以防止其蔓延。

The tight corset girdled her waist. = 紧身胸衣束缚着她的腰。


近义词:  encircle, surround, constrict, bind, restrain

反义词:  unfasten, release, liberate
