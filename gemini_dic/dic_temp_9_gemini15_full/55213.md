disservices:/dɪsˈsɜːrvɪsɪz/ | n. | 不利服务；损害；伤害 | Acts or omissions that harm or disadvantage someone or something.  It often implies a failure to provide proper or expected service, resulting in negative consequences. | 例句：The government's inaction represents a grave disservice to the victims of the disaster.  政府的不作为是对灾难受害者的严重损害。

The company's poor customer service is a disservice to its loyal clients. 该公司的糟糕客户服务对其忠实客户是一种损害。


近义词：injuries, harms, wrongs, detriments, injustices | 反义词：services, benefits, advantages, improvements


词性:名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government's policies do a disservice to the poor. = 政府的政策对穷人有害。
His actions do a disservice to the cause he claims to support. = 他的行为损害了他声称支持的事业。


一般过去时:
Their negligence did a disservice to the entire project. = 他们的疏忽对整个项目造成了损害。
The misleading information did a disservice to the public. = 误导性信息损害了公众利益。


现在完成时:
The media's biased reporting has done a disservice to the truth. = 媒体的偏颇报道已经损害了真相。
Repeated delays have done a disservice to the project's timeline. = 反复的延误已经损害了项目的进度安排。


过去完成时:
The faulty design had done a disservice to the building's structural integrity. = 有缺陷的设计已经损害了建筑物的结构完整性。
Their initial misjudgment had done a disservice to the investigation. = 他们最初的误判已经损害了调查。


将来时:
Future cuts in funding will do a disservice to education. = 未来资金的削减将损害教育事业。
Ignoring this issue will do a disservice to future generations. = 忽视这个问题将损害后代的利益。


虚拟语气:
If they had acted sooner, they would not have done a disservice to the community. = 如果他们早点行动，就不会损害社区利益了。
I wish the company hadn't done a disservice to its customers. = 我希望公司没有损害其客户利益。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The report did not do a disservice to the facts. = 报告没有歪曲事实。
