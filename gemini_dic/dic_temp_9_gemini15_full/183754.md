rescinding:/rɪˈsɪndɪŋ/| v.|撤销；废除；取消|The act of making something invalid or ineffective; canceling or repealing something previously enacted or agreed upon.  It often refers to a contract, law, or decision.|例句：The company rescinded its offer of employment after discovering the candidate's false information. 公司在发现求职者提供虚假信息后撤回了录用通知。

例句：The government rescinded the new tax law after widespread public protests.  政府在民众广泛抗议后撤销了新的税法。


近义词：revoke, cancel, annul, repeal, void|反义词：ratify, confirm, enact, establish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rescinds the contract. = 他撤销合同。
The company rescinds its offer frequently. = 公司经常撤销其报价。


一般过去时:
They rescinded the agreement. = 他们取消了协议。
She rescinded her application. = 她撤回了申请。


现在完成时:
The court has rescinded the previous ruling. = 法院已经撤销了之前的裁决。
He has rescinded his support for the project. = 他已经撤回了对该项目的支持。


过去完成时:
They had rescinded the order before the mistake was discovered. = 在错误被发现之前，他们已经撤销了命令。
The company had rescinded its offer before he accepted it. =  在他接受之前，公司已经撤回了报价。


将来时:
They will rescind the policy next month. = 他们下个月将取消这项政策。
The government will likely rescind the new regulations. = 政府可能会撤销新的规章。


虚拟语气:
If they rescinded the contract, we would be in trouble. = 如果他们撤销合同，我们会陷入困境。
I wish they had rescinded the decision sooner. = 我希望他们早点撤销这个决定。


被动语态:
The contract was rescinded by the court. = 合同被法院撤销了。

疑问句:
Will the company rescind the contract? = 公司会撤销合同吗？

否定句:
The company did not rescind the contract. = 公司没有撤销合同。
They would not rescind their decision. = 他们不会撤销他们的决定。


PHRASE->
rescind a contract = 撤销合同
rescind an agreement = 撤销协议
rescind an order = 撤销命令
rescind a decision = 撤销决定
rescind a policy = 撤销政策

词源:
From the Latin *rescindere, from re- "back, again" + scindere "to cut, split."  The sense development is from "to cut back, undo" to the modern meaning of canceling or repealing something.
