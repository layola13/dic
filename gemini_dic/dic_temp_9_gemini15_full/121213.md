miseducating: /ˌmɪsˈɛdʒukeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 误教；教坏；错误教育 |  The act of teaching something incorrectly or improperly, leading to misunderstandings, misconceptions, or harmful beliefs.  It implies a negative impact on the learner's understanding or development. |

例句：

The curriculum miseducated students about the complexities of history, presenting a simplified and biased narrative. = 这套课程在历史的复杂性方面误导了学生，呈现了一种简化和有偏见的叙述。

The constant stream of misinformation online is miseducating a generation about climate change. =  网络上不断涌现的错误信息正在误导一代人对气候变化的认识。


近义词：misinstructing, misleading, misguiding, indoctrinating (with harmful ideas)


反义词：educating, enlightening, instructing properly


词性: 动词

词源:

The word "miseducating" is formed by adding the negative prefix "mis-" to the verb "educating."  "Mis-" indicates a wrong or bad action, while "educating" refers to the process of teaching or instructing. Therefore, "miseducating" means to teach incorrectly or to impart wrong or harmful information.
