preinhabitants: /ˌpriːˈɪnˌhæbɪtənts/ | n. |原住民，先住居民 |  People who inhabited a place before others.  This term often refers to indigenous populations or those who lived in a region before a significant historical event, such as colonization. |例句：The preinhabitants of the island were skilled navigators.  岛屿的原住民是熟练的航海家。  The archaeological evidence suggests that the preinhabitants of this valley were farmers. 考古证据表明，这个山谷的原住民是农民。|近义词：indigenous people, original inhabitants, earlier settlers |反义词：newcomers, immigrants, settlers
