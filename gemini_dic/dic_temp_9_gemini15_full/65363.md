eulogizing:/ˈjuːləˌdʒaɪzɪŋ/| v.|颂扬；赞美；悼词|The act of delivering a eulogy, a speech praising a deceased person.  It often involves highlighting the positive aspects of the person's life and character. |例句：The speaker eulogized the fallen soldiers, highlighting their bravery and sacrifice.  演讲者赞扬了阵亡的士兵，强调了他们的勇敢和牺牲。

例句：He delivered a heartfelt eulogy, eulogizing his grandfather's kindness and wisdom. 他发表了一篇感人的悼词，赞扬了他祖父的善良和智慧。


近义词：praising, commending, celebrating, extolling


反义词：criticizing, condemning, denouncing, vilifying


词源:  From Middle English *eulogien, from Old French *eulógiers, from Late Latin *eulogizāre, from Greek εὐλογία (eulogia), from εὖ (eu) "well" + λόγος (logos) "speech".  The word literally means "good speaking" or "praise".
