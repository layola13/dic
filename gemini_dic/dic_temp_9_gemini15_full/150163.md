pan-Turanianism:/ˌpænˌtʊˈreɪniənɪzəm/| n. |泛突厥主义|A political ideology advocating for the unification of Turkic-speaking peoples under a single state or confederation.  This ideology often draws upon shared linguistic and cultural heritage, sometimes incorporating historical narratives of a common ancestry and destiny. Pan-Turanianism has been associated with various nationalist and irredentist movements throughout history.|The rise of Pan-Turanianism in the early 20th century led to significant political upheaval in Central Asia. |近义词：Pan-Turkism, Turanism|反义词：None specifically, but concepts opposing unification or nationalism could be considered antonyms.
