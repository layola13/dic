dispensationalism:/ˌdɪspɛnˈseɪʃənəlɪzəm/| n. | 时代论；期盼论 | A system of theology that interprets the Bible in terms of distinct historical periods or dispensations, each with its own unique covenant and relationship with God.  This often emphasizes a literal interpretation of prophecy and a future role for Israel. | Example:  His understanding of biblical prophecy was deeply rooted in dispensationalism.  他的圣经预言理解深深植根于时代论。|近义词：covenantalism (部分重叠含义)|反义词：non-dispensationalism (非时代论)
