imido: /ˈɪmɪdoʊ/ | n. | 亚氨基 |  A divalent group =N- derived from ammonia by removal of two hydrogen atoms. It is often found as a ligand in coordination chemistry, bonding to a metal center through the nitrogen atom.  The term is also used to describe compounds containing this group. | Example:  The imido ligand is a crucial component in this organometallic catalyst.  亚氨基配体是这种有机金属催化剂的关键组成部分。| 近义词: nitrido (in some contexts)| 反义词:  None directly applicable, as it describes a specific chemical group.
