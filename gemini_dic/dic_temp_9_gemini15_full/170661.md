pseudo-presbyterians:/ˌsuːdoʊˌprɛzbɪˈtɪəriənz/| n. |伪长老会教徒；假装是长老会教徒的人 |  A pseudo-presbyterian is someone who claims to be a Presbyterian but does not genuinely adhere to the beliefs and practices of Presbyterianism. This term often implies hypocrisy or deception.  The term highlights a discrepancy between outward appearance (claiming to be Presbyterian) and inner reality (lack of genuine belief or practice). | Example:  Some historians argue that certain figures in the 17th century were pseudo-presbyterians, using the guise of Presbyterianism for political gain.  |近义词：pretender, imposter, hypocrite |反义词：genuine Presbyterian, true believer |名词
