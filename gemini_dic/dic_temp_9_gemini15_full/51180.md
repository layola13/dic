destabilise:/ˌdiːˈsteɪbəlaɪz/ | vt. |使不稳定；动摇；扰乱 |To make something unstable or unsteady; to cause something to lose its stability or equilibrium.  |例句：The political crisis destabilised the region. 政治危机使该地区陷入不稳定状态。

例句：The sudden influx of refugees destabilised the local economy.  难民的突然涌入扰乱了当地经济。


近义词：unsettle, disrupt, shake, undermine, weaken | 反义词：stabilize, strengthen, secure, reinforce | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant changes destabilise the workforce. = 持续不断的变化使员工队伍不稳定。
Economic sanctions destabilise the country's government. = 经济制裁动摇了该国的政府。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

词源:
The word "destabilise" is a combination of the prefix "de-" (meaning "to remove" or "to reverse") and the word "stabilise".  "Stabilise" itself comes from the Latin word "stabilis" meaning "stable" or "firm".  Therefore, "destabilise" literally means "to make unstable".
