gambrel:/ˈɡæmbrəl/| n.|  屋顶; 悬垂屋顶 | A gambrel roof is a type of roof with two slopes on each side, the lower slope being steeper than the upper slope. It is often found on barns and other agricultural buildings. |例句：The old barn had a distinctive gambrel roof. 这座老谷仓有一个独特的悬垂屋顶。|近义词：roof, gable roof |反义词：flat roof

词源:
The word "gambrel" ultimately derives from the Old French word "gambrel," meaning "hind leg of a horse."  The resemblance of the roof's curves to the shape of a horse's hind legs led to the adoption of this term for the roof style.
