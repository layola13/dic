lurid:/ˈlʊərɪd/| adj.|可怕的；令人毛骨悚然的；过于艳丽的；耸人听闻的 |  Describing something unpleasant and shocking, often in a way that is excessively graphic or sensational. It can also describe colors that are too bright and harsh. |例句：The lurid details of the crime were splashed across the front page.  犯罪的可怕细节占据了头版头条。

The novel contained lurid descriptions of violence. 这部小说包含了对暴力的可怕描写。


近义词：gory, gruesome, sensational, shocking, vivid (in a negative context) |反义词：pleasant, mild, subdued, tasteful


词源:源于古英语单词"luridus,"意思是"苍白的，黄色的"，后来引申为“可怕的”。  The word's evolution reflects a shift in meaning from a pale or sickly color to something shocking and unpleasant.
