preliquidating: /ˌpriːˈlɪkwɪdeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 预先清算的；提前清算的 |  Describing the process of selling assets or settling debts before the formal liquidation of a company or other entity.  It implies taking proactive steps to reduce liabilities or raise capital in anticipation of a more significant financial restructuring or dissolution. | The company is undergoing preliquidating activities, selling off non-core assets to improve its financial position before filing for bankruptcy.  | 近义词: preliminary liquidation, advance liquidation | 反义词: post-liquidation
