modernisms:/ˈmɒdərnɪzəm/| n. |现代主义；现代派作品|Modernisms refers to the movements in art, literature, architecture, and other cultural expressions that emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries as a reaction against traditional styles and values.  It emphasizes innovation, experimentation, and a break from the past.  Different artistic movements within modernism (such as Cubism, Surrealism, Expressionism) had their own specific characteristics, but they shared a common spirit of rebellion and a desire to create new forms of artistic expression.|The museum's collection showcases a wide range of modernisms, from abstract paintings to avant-garde sculptures. |Postmodernism, Traditionalism|Traditionalism, Classicism|名词
