nonsegmentally:/ˌnɒnˌseɡˈmentəli/ | adv. |非分节地，非音节地 |  Describes linguistic features that are not associated with individual segments (sounds) of speech, but rather with stretches of speech or suprasegmental aspects like intonation, stress, and rhythm.  It contrasts with segmental features, which are properties of individual phonemes. | Example:  The speaker expressed sarcasm nonsegmentally, through changes in intonation rather than specific word choices.  | 近义词: suprasegmentally | 反义词: segmentally


USAGE->
This word is primarily used in linguistic analysis and doesn't have common usage outside of that field.  Therefore, standard grammatical tenses are not applicable in the same way as for verbs like "to be" or "to go".  Examples will illustrate its usage within linguistic contexts.


Example 1:
The meaning of the utterance was conveyed nonsegmentally through the use of rising intonation. =  话语的意义是通过升调非分节地表达的。

Example 2:
Stress and rhythm, analysed nonsegmentally, reveal important aspects of the speaker's message. = 非分节地分析重音和节奏，揭示了说话者信息的重要方面。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases using "nonsegmentally".  Its usage is highly specific to linguistic discourse.
