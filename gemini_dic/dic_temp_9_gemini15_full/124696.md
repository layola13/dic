mow-down: /ˈmoʊdaʊn/ | v. | 碾压；扫荡；射杀；大量杀死 | To mow down means to cut down with a mowing machine, but it is more commonly used figuratively to describe the act of killing or defeating a large number of people or things quickly and violently.  | 例句：The soldiers mowed down the enemy with machine-gun fire.  士兵用机枪扫射敌人。 | The storm mowed down the trees.  暴风雨摧毁了树木。|近义词： massacre, slaughter, decimate, wipe out | 反义词：save, rescue, protect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He mows down the weeds every week. = 他每周都割除杂草。
The machine mows down the crops efficiently. = 这台机器高效地收割庄稼。


一般过去时:
The soldiers mowed down the enemy in the battle. = 士兵在战斗中消灭了敌人。
The hail storm mowed down the corn crop. = 冰雹摧毁了玉米作物。


现在完成时:
The farmer has mowed down all the wheat. = 农民已经收割了所有的麦子。
The disease has mowed down thousands of people. = 这种疾病已经夺去了成千上万人的生命。


过去完成时:
The army had mowed down the rebels before reinforcements arrived. = 增援部队到达之前，军队已经消灭了叛军。
The drought had mowed down the crops before the rain came. = 下雨之前，干旱已经摧毁了庄稼。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
