/ˌnɒnˌdaɪəˈlɛktɪks/ | adj. | 非辩证法的 | Relating to or characterized by a rejection of dialectical methods or principles.  It typically refers to a philosophy or approach that avoids or opposes the dialectical method, which involves the interplay of opposing ideas to arrive at a synthesis.  The term often implies a rejection of the concept of inherent contradictions driving historical and societal change. |  His nondialectical approach to history ignored the inherent conflicts shaping social structures.  |  anti-dialectical, undialectical | dialectical
