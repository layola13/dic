prosimiae: /proʊˈsɪmiˌeɪ/ | n. |原猴亚目|  A suborder of primates that includes lemurs, lorises, and bushbabies.  They are characterized by their relatively primitive features compared to monkeys and apes.  Prosimians are generally smaller than monkeys and apes, and they often have larger eyes and more specialized diets. |例句：Lemurs are a well-known example of prosimiae.  狐猴是原猴亚目一个众所周知的例子。|近义词：strepsirrhini (sometimes used interchangeably)|反义词：anthropoidea (monkeys, apes, humans) |名词
