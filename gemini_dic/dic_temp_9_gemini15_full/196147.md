septicity:/sɛpˈtɪsɪti/| n. |腐败；败坏；感染；脓毒症|The state of being septic; the presence of pathogenic microorganisms or their toxins in the blood or tissues.  Septicity refers to the condition of being infected with microorganisms, often leading to a serious systemic response. It is often associated with pus formation and a significant inflammatory reaction. |Example: The wound showed signs of septicity, requiring immediate medical attention.  伤口显示出败血症的迹象，需要立即就医。
The high level of septicity in the surgical site was a major concern. 手术部位的高度败血症令人担忧。|近义词：infection, sepsis, putrefaction |反义词：sterility, cleanliness
