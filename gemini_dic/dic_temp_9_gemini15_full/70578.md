fillibuster:/ˈfɪlɪbʌstər/ | n. v. |冗长演说；阻挠议事；海盗；冒险家 |A prolonged speech or series of speeches made to delay or obstruct legislative action.  Also, historically, a person engaged in unauthorized warfare or military adventure. |例句：Senators used a fillibuster to prevent the bill from being passed. 参议员们使用冗长演说来阻止该法案的通过。The pirate was a notorious fillibuster.  那个海盗是个臭名昭著的冒险家。 |近义词：obstruction, delay, filibustering (v.),  privateering (historical context)|反义词：expedite, facilitate, cooperation
