humanism:/ˈhjuːmənɪzəm/ | n. | 人文主义 | A system of thought that centers on human values and concerns, emphasizing reason, ethics, and justice, often rejecting supernatural or religious beliefs.  Humanism stresses the potential value and goodness of human beings, emphasizing common human needs and values. | Example: The Renaissance saw a revival of classical humanism, leading to significant advancements in art, literature, and philosophy.  文艺复兴时期见证了古典人文主义的复兴，这导致了艺术、文学和哲学的重大进步。 | 近义词：humanitarianism, secularism | 反义词：theocentrism, religious fundamentalism |

词源:
The word "humanism" comes from the Latin word "humanus," meaning "human," "cultured," or "civilized."  It emerged in the 15th century to describe the revival of classical learning and values that emphasized human potential and achievement rather than solely religious dogma.
