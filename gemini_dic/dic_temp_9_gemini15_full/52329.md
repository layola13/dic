dicotyledonous: /ˌdaɪˌkɒtɪˈliːdənəs/ | adj. | 双子叶的 | Having two cotyledons (seed leaves).  Dicotyledonous plants are characterized by having two seed leaves in the embryo, net-veined leaves, and usually flowers with parts in multiples of four or five. | Example: Oaks and roses are examples of dicotyledonous plants.  橡树和玫瑰是双子叶植物的例子。 | 近义词: dicot (informal) | 反义词: monocotyledonous


USAGE->

The dicotyledonous plant displayed vibrant flowers. = 这棵双子叶植物开出了鲜艳的花朵。
Many dicotyledonous species are important sources of food. = 许多双子叶植物物种是重要的食物来源。


否定句:
That plant is not dicotyledonous; it's a monocot. = 那株植物不是双子叶植物；它是单子叶植物。
This species is not considered truly dicotyledonous due to its atypical cotyledon structure. = 由于其非典型的子叶结构，该物种不被认为是真正的双子叶植物。

词源:
The word "dicotyledonous" is derived from the Greek words:

* **di-:**  meaning "two"
* **kotyledōn:** meaning "seed leaf" (cotyledon is a direct borrowing from this Greek term)
* **-ous:**  a suffix forming adjectives meaning "full of," "having," or "characterized by."

Therefore, "dicotyledonous" literally means "having two seed leaves."
