promise:/ˈprɒmɪs/ /ˈprɑːmɪs/| n. v.|承诺；诺言；允诺；有希望|

英文解析:A statement assuring someone that one will definitely do something or that something will definitely happen; a commitment or assurance.  As a verb, it means to make a promise to do something.

例句:
He promised to help me with my homework. 他答应帮我做作业。

She promised her daughter a new bicycle for her birthday. 她答应女儿生日送她一辆新自行车。


近义词:pledge, vow, undertaking, assurance, guarantee

反义词:break (a promise), renege, violate


词性:Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I promise to call you later. 我答应稍后给你打电话。
He promises to be there on time. 他保证会准时到。

一般过去时:
She promised to pay me back. 她答应还我钱。
They promised to keep it a secret. 他们答应保守秘密。

现在完成时:
I have promised to attend the meeting. 我已经答应参加会议。
She has promised to help us. 她已经答应帮助我们。

过去完成时:
He had promised to be there, but he was late. 他答应到场，但他迟到了。
They had promised to finish the project by Friday, but they didn't. 他们答应周五之前完成项目，但是没有完成。

将来时:
I will promise to support you. 我会答应支持你。
She will promise to do her best. 她会答应尽力而为。


虚拟语气:
If he promised to help, I would believe him. 如果他答应帮忙，我会相信他。
I wish he had promised to come. 我希望他曾答应来。


被动语态:
A promise was made to the investors. 对投资者做出了承诺。
The deadline was promised to be met. 承诺会按时完成。


疑问句:
Did he promise to come? 他答应来吗？
Will she promise to help us? 她会答应帮助我们吗？


否定句:
He didn't promise to help. 他没有答应帮忙。
She didn't promise to be there on time. 她没有答应准时到。
