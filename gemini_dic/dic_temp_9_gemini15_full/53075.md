dioxin:/ˈdaɪɒksɪn/ /ˈdaɪɒksɪn/| n. |戴奥辛|A group of highly toxic chemical compounds that are persistent organic pollutants.  They are formed as byproducts of various industrial processes, including the incineration of waste and the production of certain herbicides. Dioxins are highly resistant to breakdown in the environment and can accumulate in the food chain, posing significant risks to human health. |例句：Exposure to dioxins can cause severe health problems, including cancer and reproductive issues.  接触戴奥辛会导致严重的健康问题，包括癌症和生殖问题。

例句：The study found high levels of dioxins in the soil near the chemical plant. 研究发现化工厂附近的土壤中戴奥辛含量很高。


近义词：polychlorinated dibenzo-p-dioxins (PCDDs), polychlorinated dibenzofurans (PCDFs) |反义词：无
