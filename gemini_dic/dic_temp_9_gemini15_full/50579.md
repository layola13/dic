derail:/ˌdiːˈreɪl/| vt. |使脱轨；使失败；阻挠 |To cause (a train or vehicle) to leave the tracks; to disrupt or obstruct the progress of (a plan, system, or process).|例句：The train derailed after hitting a fallen tree. 火车撞到倒下的树后脱轨了。

The scandal derailed his political career.  丑闻毁了他的政治生涯。


近义词：disrupt, obstruct, upset, thwart |反义词：support, facilitate, advance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The storm derails many flights. = 暴风雨使许多航班脱轨。
He often derails the meeting. = 他经常扰乱会议。


一般过去时:
The unexpected rain derailed our picnic plans. = 突如其来的雨水打乱了我们的野餐计划。
The project was derailed by budget cuts. = 该项目因预算削减而搁浅。


现在完成时:
The accident has derailed the company's progress. = 事故已经阻碍了公司的发展。
Several setbacks have derailed my plans for the year. = 好几次挫折打乱了我今年的计划。


过去完成时:
The company had already derailed before the new CEO arrived. = 新任CEO到来之前，公司就已经走下坡路了。
They had derailed the negotiations by making unreasonable demands. = 他们提出不合理的要求，从而破坏了谈判。


将来时:
The strike will derail the production schedule. = 罢工将打乱生产计划。
If we don't get funding, the project will derail. = 如果我们得不到资金，项目就会失败。


虚拟语气:
If the negotiations had not been derailed, we would have reached an agreement. = 如果谈判没有破裂，我们就达成协议了。
I wish the unexpected event hadn't derailed my trip. = 我真希望意外事件没有打乱我的行程。


被动语态:
The train was derailed by a landslide. = 火车被山体滑坡冲出轨道。
His plans were derailed by unforeseen circumstances. = 他的计划因不可预见的情况而受阻。


疑问句:
Was the project derailed by a lack of funding? = 项目是因为缺乏资金而失败的吗？
Has the recent controversy derailed the election campaign? = 最近的争议是否破坏了竞选活动？


否定句:
The new strategy did not derail the company's growth. = 新战略并没有阻碍公司的增长。
The bad weather didn't derail our plans. = 恶劣天气并没有打乱我们的计划。
