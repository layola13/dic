rebeller:/rɪˈbɛlə(r)/| n. |反叛者，叛逆者| a person who rebels.  Someone who resists or opposes authority, control, or tradition.|The rebeller refused to conform to societal expectations.  这个反叛者拒绝遵守社会期望。|近义词：insurgent, rebel, mutineer |反义词：conformist, follower


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rebeller protests against the government. = 这个反叛者抗议政府。
He rebels against authority figures. = 他反抗权威人士。


一般过去时:
The rebeller fought against the regime. = 这个反叛者反抗政权。
She rebelled against her parents' wishes. = 她反抗父母的意愿。


现在完成时:
The rebeller has consistently challenged the system. = 这个反叛者一直挑战体制。
She has rebelled against many norms in her life. = 她一生中反抗过许多规范。


过去完成时:
The rebeller had already planned his uprising before he was captured. = 在被捕之前，这个反叛者已经计划了他的起义。
He had rebelled against the church before finding his own path. = 在找到自己的道路之前，他曾反抗过教会。


将来时:
The rebeller will continue his fight for freedom. = 这个反叛者将继续为自由而战。
She will rebel against the new rules if they are unfair. = 如果新规不公平，她将反抗。


虚拟语气:
If he were a rebeller, he would fight for justice. = 如果他是一个反叛者，他会为正义而战。
