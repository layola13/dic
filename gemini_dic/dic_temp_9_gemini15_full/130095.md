non-prohibition:/ˌnɒnˌprəʊhɪˈbɪʃən/| adj. |非禁止的；允许的|Lack of prohibition; permission or allowance.  It implies that something is not forbidden, but doesn't necessarily mean it's actively encouraged or approved.  |例句：The policy of non-prohibition allows for a certain level of experimentation.  该项非禁止政策允许一定程度的实验。

The company's non-prohibition approach to employee attire means casual dress is acceptable. 公司对员工着装采取非禁止的方式，这意味着休闲装是可以接受的。|近义词：permission, allowance, tolerance, acceptance|反义词：prohibition, ban, restriction, forbiddance


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms in common usage. Therefore, grammatical tenses and voice are not applicable in the same way as for verbs.  The usage focuses on how it modifies nouns.

Example sentences illustrating its usage:

A non-prohibition policy: 一项非禁止政策
A non-prohibition attitude: 一种非禁止的态度
A non-prohibition environment: 一个非禁止的环境
Non-prohibition of alcohol: 不禁止饮酒
Non-prohibition of certain activities: 不禁止某些活动


PHRASE->
Common phrases using "non-prohibition" are less frequent as it's not a commonly used word itself, but can be part of longer phrases:

Word Origin:

The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the noun "prohibition" (the act of forbidding something).  "Prohibition" itself comes from the Latin word "prohibere," meaning "to hold back" or "to forbid."
