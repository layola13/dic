/ˈlænddrɔːst/| n.|南非地区行政长官|A landdrost was a high-ranking official in the Cape Colony (South Africa) during the Dutch colonial period.  They were responsible for the administration of justice and general governance within a specific district. Their powers were extensive, encompassing both judicial and executive functions.|The landdrost of Stellenbosch was known for his fair judgment.|近义词：magistrate, administrator, governor|反义词：subject, subordinate
