recalculating:/ˌriːˈkælkjuleɪtɪŋ/ | v. |重新计算，再计算 |The process of computing something again, often to correct an error or to obtain a more precise result.  | Example: The GPS is recalculating the route after encountering unexpected traffic.  GPS在遇到意外交通状况后正在重新计算路线。 The computer is recalculating the figures to ensure accuracy.  电脑正在重新计算数据以确保准确性。|近义词：recomputing, recomputing, recalibrating |反义词：calculating (without the implication of re-doing)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The system recalculates the route every minute. = 系统每分钟重新计算路线。
We recalculate the budget annually. = 我们每年重新计算预算。


一般过去时:
The engineer recalculated the load-bearing capacity of the bridge. = 工程师重新计算了桥梁的承载能力。
She recalculated the results after discovering a mistake. = 她在发现错误后重新计算了结果。


现在完成时:

I have recalculated the figures several times, but I still can't find the error. = 我已经重新计算了几次数据，但我仍然找不到错误。


过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
