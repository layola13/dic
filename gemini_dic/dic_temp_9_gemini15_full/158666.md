ploddingness:/ˈplɒdɪŋnəs/| n. | 沉重缓慢；单调乏味；步履蹒跚 | The ploddingness of the work made him feel discouraged.  工作的沉闷乏味让他感到沮丧。|近义词：slowness, tediousness, monotony | 反义词：speed, briskness, liveliness


英文解析：Ploddingness refers to the quality or state of being plodding; characterized by slow, laborious, and monotonous movement or progress.  It suggests a lack of excitement or dynamism, often implying a tedious or burdensome nature.


例句：

The ploddingness of the journey across the desert tested their endurance.  穿越沙漠的旅程因其缓慢而单调，考验着他们的耐力。

His ploddingness in his studies eventually led to success, though it took longer than he had hoped.  他学习的缓慢最终导致了成功，尽管时间比他希望的要长。


词源:

Derived from the verb "plod," which itself originates from Middle English *plodden, possibly of imitative origin, suggesting the sound of heavy footsteps.  The suffix "-ness" transforms the verb into a noun, signifying the state or quality of plodding.
