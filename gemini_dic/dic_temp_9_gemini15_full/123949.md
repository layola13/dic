moribundity:/ˌmɔːrɪˈbʌndɪti/ | n. |垂死状态；衰败；没落 |The state of being moribund; a decline into death or ruin.  It refers to a state of decline, decay, or approaching death, applicable to institutions, systems, or even individuals.  |例句：The moribundity of the once-great empire was evident in its crumbling infrastructure and dwindling population.  =  曾经伟大的帝国的衰败在其不断崩塌的基础设施和日益减少的人口中显而易见。

The moribundity of the local economy led to high unemployment and widespread poverty. = 当地经济的衰败导致了高失业率和普遍贫困。|近义词：decline, decay, degeneration, deterioration, obsolescence |反义词：flourishing, thriving, prosperity, growth

词源:
