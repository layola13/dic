neocracy:/ˈniːoʊkrəsi/| n. |新统治；新政权；新兴的统治阶级|A neocracy is a form of government where power is held by a new or recently established ruling class.  This class often comes to power through revolution, coup, or a significant societal shift. The term emphasizes the novelty and often the untested nature of this new power structure.  It can also imply a lack of established traditions or norms. | Example: The country experienced a period of neocracy following the revolution, with a new elite controlling all aspects of life. |近义词：new regime, emerging power structure |反义词：old regime, established order, aristocracy
