ploughs:/plaʊz/ | n. v. | 犁；耕地 | A plough is a tool used in farming to turn over the soil before planting.  The farmer ploughs the field every spring. | 近义词：plow, till | 反义词：harvest


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The farmer ploughs the field every day. = 农民每天都耕地。
He ploughs the land before planting. = 他在播种前耕地。

一般过去时:
They ploughed the field last week. = 他们上周耕了地。
The farmer ploughed the land with his horse. = 农民用他的马耕地。

现在完成时:
He has ploughed the entire field. = 他已经耕完了整块地。
They have ploughed more land this year. = 他们今年耕了更多的土地。

过去完成时:
They had ploughed the field before the rain came. = 雨下之前他们已经耕好了地。
The farmer had ploughed the land before planting the seeds. = 农民在播种之前已经耕好了地。

将来时:
They will plough the field next week. = 他们下周将耕地。
He will plough the land after the rain stops. = 雨停后他会耕地。

虚拟语气:
If he had a plough, he would plough the field. = 如果他有一台犁，他会耕地。
I wish I had ploughed the field earlier. = 我真希望我早点耕地。

被动语态:
The field was ploughed yesterday. = 地昨天被耕了。
The land is being ploughed by the tractor. = 田地正在被拖拉机耕种。

疑问句:
Have you ploughed the field yet? = 你耕地了吗？
Will they plough the field tomorrow? = 他们明天会耕地吗？

否定句:
They didn't plough the field yesterday. = 他们昨天没有耕地。
He hasn't ploughed the land yet. = 他还没耕地。


PHRASE->

plough one's way: 艰难地前进


词源:
The word "plough" comes from the Old English word *plōh, which is related to the Proto-Germanic word *plokhō.  These words are ultimately derived from the Proto-Indo-European root *pleg-, meaning "to bend" or "to fold," reflecting the action of turning over the soil.
