rant:/rænt/| n., v.| 咆哮；怒骂；长篇抱怨；激昂的演说 | A rant is a long, angry speech or piece of writing, typically one that is full of complaints.  It can also refer to the act of giving such a speech. | 例句：He went on a rant about the unfairness of the system. 他开始喋喋不休地抱怨制度的不公平。

He launched into a furious rant against the government. 他开始愤怒地痛骂政府。


近义词：diatribe, tirade, harangue, outburst | 反义词：praise, compliment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rants about politics every day. = 他每天都对政治大发牢骚。
She often rants against social injustice. = 她经常痛斥社会不公。


一般过去时:
He ranted at the referee after the game. = 比赛结束后，他对裁判大发雷霆。
She ranted and raved about the poor service. = 她对糟糕的服务大发脾气。


现在完成时:
He has ranted about this issue for years. = 他多年来一直在抱怨这个问题。
She has never ranted at me like that before. = 她以前从未那样对我大发雷霆。


过去完成时:
He had ranted for an hour before anyone intervened. = 他已经抱怨了一个小时，才有人介入。
She had ranted and raved about the delay before they finally arrived. = 在他们最终到达之前，她已经因为延误而大发脾气。


将来时:
He will rant and rave if he doesn't get his way. = 如果他不遂心愿，他会大发雷霆。
She will probably rant about the traffic again. = 她可能会再次抱怨交通状况。


虚拟语气:
If he ranted less, people would listen more. = 如果他不那么大喊大叫，人们会更多地倾听。
I wish he wouldn't rant so much. = 我希望他不要抱怨那么多。


被动语态:
His complaints were often ranted about online. = 他的抱怨经常在网上被大肆宣扬。
The issue was ranted about by many people. = 这个问题被许多人强烈抱怨。


疑问句:
Why is he always ranting? = 他为什么总是大喊大叫？
Does she often rant about her job? = 她经常抱怨她的工作吗？


否定句:
He didn't rant at the meeting. = 他在会议上没有大发雷霆。
She didn't rant, but she was clearly upset. = 她没有大喊大叫，但她显然很生气。


PHRASE->
go on a rant = 开始大发牢骚
rant and rave = 大声抱怨；大发雷霆
a drunken rant = 酒后胡言乱语
a political rant = 政治方面的咆哮
a lengthy rant = 一长串的抱怨
