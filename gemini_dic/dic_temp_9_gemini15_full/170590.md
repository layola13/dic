pseudo-concept:/ˈsuːdoʊˌkɒnsept/| n. |伪概念，似是而非的概念 |A pseudo-concept is a term or idea that appears to be a well-defined concept but lacks the clear boundaries and consistent meaning of a true concept. It often relies on vague or ambiguous definitions, leading to confusion and misinterpretations.  Pseudo-concepts are often used in areas where precise terminology is lacking or deliberately avoided for rhetorical purposes. |The idea of "natural talent" is often criticized as a pseudo-concept, as success is usually the result of a complex interplay of innate abilities, hard work, and opportunity. |近义词：misconception, fallacy, illusion |反义词：well-defined concept, clear concept
