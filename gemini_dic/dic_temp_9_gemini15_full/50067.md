density:/ˈdɛnsɪti/ | n. |密度，浓度 |The density of a substance is its mass per unit volume.  密度是指物质单位体积的质量。|近义词：concentration, thickness |反义词：rarity, sparseness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The density of water is approximately 1 gram per cubic centimeter. = 水的密度大约是每立方厘米1克。
This area has a high population density. = 这个地区人口密度很高。

一般过去时:
The density of the fog was so high that visibility was very low. = 雾的浓度很高，能见度很低。
The air density at high altitudes is lower than at sea level. = 高海拔地区的空气密度低于海平面。

现在完成时:
Scientists have measured the density of various materials. = 科学家们已经测量了各种材料的密度。
The government has increased efforts to reduce population density in overcrowded cities. = 政府加大了努力，以降低人口过多的城市的密度。

过去完成时:

They had underestimated the density of the crowd at the concert. = 他们低估了音乐会人群的密度。

词源:
The word "density" comes from the Middle English word "densite," which in turn is derived from the Old French word "densité," ultimately tracing back to the Latin word "densitas," meaning "thickness."  The Latin word "densitas" is derived from "densus," meaning "thick, dense."
