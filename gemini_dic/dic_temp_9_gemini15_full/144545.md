outvotes:/ˌaʊtˈvoʊts/| vt.|超过票数，票数多于| To defeat or surpass in a vote.  This verb implies a process of voting where one side receives more votes than another.|The proposal was outvoted by a narrow margin.  这项提案以微弱优势被否决了。|The incumbent was outvoted in the election.  现任官员在选举中被击败了。|defeat, surpass, overcome|be defeated by, lose to|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bill outvotes the previous one. = 这项法案的票数超过了之前的法案。
He often outvotes his colleagues in the committee. = 他经常在委员会中以票数超过他的同事。


一般过去时:
The amendment was outvoted yesterday. = 修正案昨天被否决了。
The opposition party outvoted the ruling party on the key issue. = 反对党在关键问题上以票数超过执政党。


现在完成时:
The plan has been outvoted twice this year. = 这项计划今年已被否决了两次。
She has outvoted him on every important decision. = 她在每一个重要决定上都以票数超过了他。


过去完成时:
The resolution had been outvoted before the final vote. = 在最终投票之前，决议已经被否决了。
They had outvoted the proposal before the meeting even began. = 会议甚至还没开始，他们就已经否决了这项提案。


将来时:

I think they will outvote us on this matter. = 我认为他们在这一问题上会以票数超过我们。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
