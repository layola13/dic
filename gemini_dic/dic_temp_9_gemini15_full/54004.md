discriminative: /dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv/ | adj. | 有辨别力的；有鉴别力的；歧视性的 |

英文解析：Able to recognize and appreciate fine distinctions; showing good judgment or taste.  Also,  making or showing an unfair or prejudicial distinction between different categories of people or things, especially on the grounds of race, sex, or age.  The word has two distinct, yet related, meanings. One refers to the ability to discern differences, while the other refers to the act of unfairly treating different groups differently.

例句：

He has a highly discriminative palate. = 他拥有极高的味觉鉴别力。

The company was accused of discriminative hiring practices. = 这家公司被指控有歧视性的招聘行为。


近义词： discerning, perceptive, judicious (for the positive sense); prejudiced, biased, bigoted (for the negative sense)

反义词： undiscriminating, indiscriminate, impartial (for the positive sense); fair, unbiased, equitable (for the negative sense)

词性: adjective
