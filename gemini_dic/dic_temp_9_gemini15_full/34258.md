clapperboards:/ˈklæpərˌbɔːrdz/| n. |拍板，电影开拍板 |A clapperboard is a device used in filmmaking to synchronize picture and sound, and to identify each take. It's a slate with a hinged arm and a chalkboard surface that is typically held in front of the camera before each shot. The clapper is then snapped shut, creating a distinctive sound that is recorded and used to match the audio and video during post-production.  |Example: The sound recordist carefully marked the clapperboard before each take.  录音师在每次拍摄前仔细标记了拍板。|近义词：film slate, movie slate |反义词：无
