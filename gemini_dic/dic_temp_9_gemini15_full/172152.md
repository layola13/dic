pursership: /ˈpɜːrʃɪp/ | n. |合伙关系；伙伴关系|  A business relationship between two or more people who agree to share in the profits or losses of a business. It's a formal agreement, often involving a written contract, outlining the responsibilities, contributions, and profit-sharing arrangements of each partner.  | Example: The two entrepreneurs formed a pursership to launch their new tech startup.  两个企业家组建了一个合伙企业来启动他们的新科技公司。 | 近义词：partnership, joint venture | 反义词：sole proprietorship
