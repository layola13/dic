irascible:/ˈɪrəsəbl/| adj.|易怒的；暴躁的；脾气坏的|Easily provoked to anger; irritable.|He became irascible in his old age. 他上了年纪后变得易怒了。|近义词：irritable, choleric, testy, grumpy|反义词：calm, placid, patient


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is irascible. = 他脾气暴躁。
She is often irascible in the mornings. = 她早上经常脾气暴躁。

一般过去时:
He was irascible yesterday. = 他昨天脾气很坏。
The old man was irascible and difficult to please. = 那位老人脾气暴躁，难以取悦。

现在完成时:
He has become increasingly irascible lately. = 最近他变得越来越易怒了。
She has always been irascible, even as a child. = 她从小就脾气暴躁。

过去完成时:
He had been irascible all day, so we avoided him. = 他一整天都脾气暴躁，所以我们都躲着他。
She had been irascible before the meeting, but calmed down afterward. = 会议前她脾气很暴躁，但后来平静下来了。

将来时:
He will likely be irascible if you criticize his work. = 如果你批评他的工作，他可能会发脾气。
She will probably be irascible if she doesn't get enough sleep. = 如果她睡不够，她可能会脾气暴躁。

虚拟语气:
If he weren't so irascible, he would have more friends. = 如果他不那么易怒，他就会有更多朋友。
I wish he weren't so irascible; it makes it difficult to work with him. = 我希望他不那么易怒，这样和他一起工作就容易多了。

被动语态:  (This adjective is rarely used passively.)
His behavior was described as irascible. = 他的行为被描述为易怒的。
His irascible nature was well-known in the village. = 他易怒的性格在村子里是出了名的。(This focuses on the nature, not the passive state of being irascible)


PHRASE->
an irascible temper = 易怒的脾气
become irascible = 变得易怒
an irascible old man = 一个脾气暴躁的老人
irascible disposition = 易怒的性情
remarkably irascible = 非常易怒


词源:
From Latin *irascibilis, from irasci "to be angry," from ira "anger," from Proto-Italic *īsā.  Related to ire "to go," implying a rushing or moving forward in anger.
