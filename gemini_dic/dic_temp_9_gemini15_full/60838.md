electrosurface:/ɪˈlɛktrəʊˌsɜːrfɪs/| n. |电解表面，电极表面|The electrosurface is the interface between an electrode and an electrolyte solution, where electrochemical reactions occur.  It's a region characterized by a double layer of charges, influencing various electrochemical processes.|Example: The properties of the electrosurface significantly affect the efficiency of the electrochemical cell. 电极表面的特性显著影响电化学电池的效率。|近义词：electrode-electrolyte interface, double layer|反义词：bulk solution (体相溶液)
