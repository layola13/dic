intruding:/ɪnˈtruːdɪŋ/| v. | 侵入；打扰；闯入 |  The act of entering or intruding upon a place or situation without permission or welcome.  It implies an unwelcome or disruptive presence. |例句：I apologize for intruding on your privacy.  对不起，打扰您的隐私了。|例句：The noise from the party was intruding on my concentration. 派对的噪音扰乱了我的注意力。|近义词： invading, trespassing, encroaching, interrupting |反义词：welcoming, leaving, departing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is intruding on my time. = 他在占用我的时间。
They are intruding upon our privacy. = 他们正在侵犯我们的隐私。


一般过去时:
She intruded on the meeting without invitation. = 她未经邀请就闯入了会议。
The stranger intruded into our home. = 陌生人闯入了我们的家。


现在完成时:
They have intruded on our quiet life. = 他们扰乱了我们平静的生活。
He has intruded into our affairs too much. = 他过多的干涉了我们的事情。


过去完成时:
The thief had intruded into the house before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前，小偷已经闯入了房子。
She had intruded upon their conversation. = 她已经插进了他们的谈话。


将来时:
He will intrude on our conversation again. = 他还会再次插嘴我们的谈话。
They will intrude into our private lives. = 他们将会干涉我们的私生活。


虚拟语气:
If he intruded, I would be angry. = 如果他擅自闯入，我会很生气。
I wish he hadn't intruded on our meeting. = 我希望他不要插手我们的会议。


被动语态:
Our privacy was intruded upon. = 我们的隐私受到了侵犯。
The meeting was intruded on by a stranger. = 会议被一个陌生人打断了。


疑问句:
Is he intruding? = 他在打扰吗？
Are they intruding on our property? = 他们正在侵犯我们的财产吗？


否定句:
He didn't intrude on our conversation. = 他没有插手我们的谈话。
They did not intrude into the private room. = 他们没有闯入私人房间。
