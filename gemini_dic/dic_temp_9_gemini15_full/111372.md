liverwort: /ˈlɪvəwɜːrt/ | n. |苔类植物|A small, flowerless plant that grows in damp places and resembles a liver in shape (historically believed to cure liver ailments). Liverworts are non-vascular plants belonging to the division Marchantiophyta. They lack true roots, stems, and leaves, instead possessing rhizoids for anchorage and thalli or leafy structures for photosynthesis.  They reproduce both sexually and asexually. | Example:  Many species of liverwort thrive in the humid environment of the rainforest.  许多苔类植物在雨林潮湿的环境中茁壮成长。  |近义词: moss, hornwort |反义词: flowering plant
