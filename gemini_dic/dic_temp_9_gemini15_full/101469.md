irian:/ˈɪəriən/| n.| 伊里安（地名）| Irian is a name historically associated with the western half of the island of New Guinea, now part of Indonesia.  It's derived from the Malay word "Irian," meaning "crocodile."  The name was used in the context of Dutch New Guinea and later Indonesian West Irian.| Example:  The history of Irian Jaya is complex and involves Dutch colonization and Indonesian independence. = 伊里安查亚的历史复杂，涉及荷兰殖民和印尼独立。 |近义词：West Papua (though not a perfect synonym due to political context)|反义词：无
