faltered:/ˈfɔːltərd/ | /ˈfɒltəd/ | v. | 蹒跚；犹豫；衰弱；结巴；支吾 | To move or speak in an unsteady way, showing weakness or nervousness; to hesitate or stumble; to weaken or fail. |

例句：

His voice faltered as he tried to explain.  = 他试图解释时，声音颤抖了。

The negotiations faltered due to a lack of trust. =  由于缺乏信任，谈判陷入了僵局。


近义词：stumbled, hesitated, wavered, weakened, faltered


反义词：continued steadily, progressed smoothly, strengthened


USAGE->

He falters when he speaks in public. = 他在公共场合讲话时会结巴。
The engine falters under heavy load. = 发动机在重载下运转不稳。

词源:

The word "falter" comes from the Old English word *fealtarian, meaning "to stumble," "to hesitate," or "to waver."  It's related to the Old High German *faltarōn, with a similar meaning.  The modern English word retains the core sense of unsteady movement or wavering.
