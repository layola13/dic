perduellion:/ˌpɜːrduˈɛliən/| n. |叛国罪；通敌罪|The crime of treason or betrayal of one's country.  It specifically refers to the betrayal of one's country or sovereign, often involving acts of war or conspiracy against the state.  The term is less common in modern usage than "treason" but retains its historical significance.| Example:  The Roman emperor punished perduellion severely, often with death.  罗马皇帝严厉惩罚叛国罪，通常判处死刑。|近义词：treason, high treason, sedition|反义词：loyalty, patriotism|名词
