shortchanged:/ʃɔːrtˈtʃeɪndʒd/| v. |欺骗；少给；使…受亏待 |To give someone less than the correct amount of money or something else that is due to them.  It can also mean to treat someone unfairly or deprive them of something they deserve. |例句：The cashier shortchanged me by five dollars. = 收银员少给了我五美元。

The customer felt shortchanged by the poor quality of the product. = 顾客觉得产品质量差，受到了欺骗。|近义词：cheat, defraud, deprive, swindle |反义词：overpay, overcompensate
