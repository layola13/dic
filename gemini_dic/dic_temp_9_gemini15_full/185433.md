retuning:/riˈtjuːnɪŋ/ | v. | 重新调整；再调整；重调 | The act of adjusting something again, often to a previous or optimal state. It implies a prior adjustment that needs further refinement or correction.  | 例句：The technician is retuning the piano after the move. 技师正在搬家后重新调音。


The guitarist spent hours retuning his instrument before the concert. 吉他手在音乐会前花了数小时重新调音。


近义词： readjusting, readying, recalibrating, re-adjusting | 反义词： leaving as is, misadjusting

词源:  The word "retuning" is formed by adding the prefix "re-" (meaning "again" or "anew") to the word "tuning."  "Tuning" itself originates from the Old English word "tūnian," meaning "to attune or put in order."  Therefore, "retuning" literally means "to tune again" or "to adjust again."
