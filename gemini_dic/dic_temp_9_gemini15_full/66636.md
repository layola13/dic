existentialism:/ˌɛɡzɪˈstɛnʃəlɪzəm/| n. |存在主义|A philosophical theory or approach that emphasizes the existence of the individual person as a free and responsible agent determining their own development through acts of will.  Existentialism focuses on subjective experience, freedom, and responsibility, often questioning the meaning of life and the nature of existence. |Example:  Existentialism explores themes of freedom, responsibility, and the search for meaning in a seemingly meaningless universe. 存在主义探讨了自由、责任以及在一个看似毫无意义的宇宙中寻找意义的主题。

Example: Many works of literature, like Albert Camus' "The Stranger," are considered examples of existentialist thought. 许多文学作品，例如阿尔贝·加缪的《局外人》，都被认为是存在主义思想的例子。


近义词：existential philosophy, phenomenology (partially overlapping)


反义词：essentialism (a contrasting philosophical position)


词性:名词
