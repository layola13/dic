churchism:/ˈtʃɜːrtʃɪzəm/| n. |教会主义；教条主义|The rigid adherence to church doctrine and practices, often to the exclusion of other considerations.  It can refer to the excessive influence of religious institutions on social and political life, or to a narrow, dogmatic interpretation of religious beliefs.  The term often carries a negative connotation, suggesting intolerance and a lack of flexibility.|例句：The rise of churchism in the 16th century led to religious wars.|近义词：dogmatism, sectarianism, religious fundamentalism|反义词：ecumenism, tolerance, liberalism|
