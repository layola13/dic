in token of: /ɪn ˈtoʊkən əv/ | phrase |作为…的标志；作为…的表示 |This phrase indicates something is given or done as a symbol or sign of something else, typically a feeling or intention.  It often involves a gesture of goodwill, respect, or gratitude. |例句：I gave her a bouquet of flowers in token of my appreciation. 我送了她一束花以表达我的谢意。|例句：He sent a gift in token of his friendship. 他送了一份礼物以表示他的友谊。|近义词：as a sign of, as a mark of, in recognition of |反义词：in spite of, regardless of


USAGE->
This phrase is not a verb, noun, or adjective, so it doesn't conjugate or decline in the same way as other word classes.  It is a prepositional phrase and functions as an adverbial phrase, modifying the verb of the sentence.  Therefore, standard verb tenses don't apply directly to "in token of."  The tense is determined by the main verb in the sentence.


一般现在时:
He gives a gift in token of his respect. = 他送礼物以示敬意。
She offers her help in token of her friendship. = 她提供帮助以表示友谊。

虚拟语气:

I wish I could give her something in token of my admiration. = 我希望我能送她一些东西以表示我的钦佩。

否定句:
