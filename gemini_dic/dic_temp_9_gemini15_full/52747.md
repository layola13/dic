dilapidate:/dɪˈlæpɪdeɪt/| vt. adj.|使荒废；破旧的；衰败的|To fall into a state of disrepair; to become dilapidated.  The verb refers to the action of causing something to become dilapidated, while the adjective describes the state of being dilapidated.|例句：The old house had been allowed to dilapidate.  这栋老房子已经被允许荒废了。

The years of neglect had dilapidated the once-grand mansion.  多年的忽视使这座曾经宏伟的豪宅变得破败不堪。|近义词：decay, deteriorate, crumble, ruin|反义词：renovate, restore, repair, maintain|


USAGE->

The landlord neglects to maintain the property, and it slowly dilapidates. = 房东忽视了对房产的维护，它慢慢地破败了。
