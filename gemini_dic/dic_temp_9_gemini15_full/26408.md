bye-elections: /baɪ ɪˈlɛkʃənz/ | n. | 补选 |

英文解析:An election held to fill a vacancy that has occurred in an elective office before the next regular election.  These vacancies can be caused by death, resignation, or expulsion of the incumbent. Bye-elections are typically held at the local or regional level, unlike general elections which involve the entire country or region.

例句:
The recent bye-election saw a significant increase in voter turnout.  最近的补选中选民投票率大幅增加。

The ruling party lost the bye-election in a surprising upset.执政党在补选中意外落败。


近义词: supplementary election, special election

反义词: general election, regular election

词性: noun
