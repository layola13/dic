merited:/ˈmɛrɪtɪd/| adj.|应得的；值得的；合理的|Deserving or worthy of; justified.|The team merited their victory after a strong performance. 这支球队凭借精彩的表现赢得了胜利，实至名归。|近义词：deserved, earned, justified, warranted|反义词：undeserved, unwarranted, unjustified


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He merits a reward for his hard work. = 他勤奋工作，理应得到奖励。
Her kindness merits our respect. = 她的善良值得我们尊敬。


一般过去时:
The project merited further investigation. = 该项目值得进一步调查。
His actions merited severe punishment. = 他的行为应该受到严厉惩罚。


现在完成时:
His contributions have merited widespread recognition. = 他的贡献已获得广泛认可。
The situation has merited a careful review. = 这种情况值得仔细审查。


过去完成时:
The evidence had merited a thorough examination. = 证据需要彻底审查。
Their efforts had merited success. = 他们的努力理应获得成功。


将来时:
The case will merit further consideration. = 此案将需要进一步审议。
His dedication will merit promotion. = 他的奉献精神将会得到晋升。


虚拟语气:
If he had worked harder, he would have merited a promotion. = 如果他工作更努力，他本应得到晋升。
She deserved a better outcome, and she would have merited one, had she tried harder. = 她应该得到更好的结果，如果她更努力一些，她本来可以得到。


被动语态:
His efforts were merited by a significant increase in sales. = 他的努力带来了销售额的显著增长。
The award was merited by her outstanding achievements. = 她的杰出成就获得了该奖项。


疑问句:
Does his behavior merit such harsh criticism? = 他的行为值得如此严厉的批评吗？
Does this proposal merit our attention? = 这个提案值得我们关注吗？


否定句:
His complaints did not merit a response. = 他的抱怨不值得回应。
Their excuses did not merit consideration. = 他们的借口不值得考虑。
