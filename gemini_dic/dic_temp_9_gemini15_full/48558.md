defames:/dɪˈfeɪmz/| vt.|诽谤；中伤；损害名誉|To harm someone's reputation by saying or writing bad things about them that are not true.  |例句：He defamed his opponent by spreading false rumors about his past. 他通过散布关于他对手过去的谣言来诽谤他。|例句：The newspaper article defamed the company's image. 报纸上的文章损害了公司的形象。|近义词：slander, libel, vilify, calumniate|反义词：praise, commend, extol

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He defames his rivals regularly. = 他经常诽谤他的对手。
The tabloid magazine defames celebrities frequently. = 这本小报杂志经常诽谤名人。

一般过去时:
She defamed him in a public speech. = 她在公开演讲中诽谤了他。
The article defamed the politician's reputation. = 这篇文章损害了这位政治家的名誉。

现在完成时:
They have defamed the company's good name. = 他们已经损害了公司的名声。
He has defamed many people throughout his career. = 他在他职业生涯中诽谤过很多人。


过去完成时:
The lawyer argued that his client had been defamed before the trial. = 律师辩称他的当事人在审判前就被诽谤了。
She realized that her reputation had been defamed by a malicious rumour. = 她意识到她的名誉已经被一个恶意的谣言所损害。


将来时:
They will defame him in the upcoming election. = 他们将在即将到来的选举中诽谤他。
I fear he will defame me in the press. = 我担心他会向媒体诽谤我。


虚拟语气:
If he defamed me, I would sue him. = 如果他诽谤我，我会起诉他。
I wish he hadn't defamed my character. = 我真希望他没有诽谤我的品格。


被动语态:
He was defamed by the media. = 他被媒体诽谤了。
The company was defamed in a recent article. = 公司在一篇最近的文章中被诽谤了。


疑问句:
Did he defame her? = 他诽谤她了吗？
Were they defamed in the newspaper? = 他们在报纸上被诽谤了吗？


否定句:
He did not defame her, despite her accusations. = 他没有诽谤她，尽管她有指控。
They have not been defamed by the report. = 他们没有被这份报告诽谤。


PHRASE->
defame someone's character = 诽谤某人的品格
be defamed for something = 因某事而被诽谤
defame someone publicly = 公开诽谤某人
defame someone unjustly = 不公平地诽谤某人
defame someone intentionally = 故意诽谤某人

词源:
From Old French *defamer, from Latin diffamare (“to spread evil reports of”), from dis- (“apart”) + fama (“report, rumour”).
