/priːkʌlˈteɪʃənz/| n. |预培养；预先培养|The process of cultivating microorganisms or cells before their main use or experiment.  It involves growing them under specific conditions to achieve a desired state, such as a certain density or physiological condition, before transferring them to another environment.|Example:  The precultivations ensured the bacteria were in the optimal growth phase for the subsequent experiment. 预培养确保细菌在后续实验中处于最佳生长阶段。|近义词：preculture, preliminary cultivation|反义词：main culture, final cultivation|名词
