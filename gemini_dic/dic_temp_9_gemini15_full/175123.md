rafflesia:/ræˈfleɪʒə/| n. |大王花属植物|Rafflesia is a genus of parasitic flowering plants.  They are notable for producing the largest individual flowers in the world.  These flowers are foul-smelling, often described as smelling like rotting flesh, to attract pollinators such as carrion flies.|The Rafflesia arnoldii is famous for its gigantic flower.|近义词: giant flower (informal)|反义词: None (no direct antonym)|名词

词源:
The genus name *Rafflesia* honors Sir Stamford Raffles, the Lieutenant-Governor of Bencoolen (now Bengkulu) in Sumatra, who encountered the plant in 1818.  The specific epithets often refer to the discoverer or the location of the plant.
