disgrace's:/dɪsˈɡreɪsɪz/ | n. | 耻辱的；丢脸的 | The disgrace's stain on his reputation was difficult to remove. 他名誉上的污点很难消除。|近义词：shame, dishonor, scandal | 反义词：honor, glory


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His actions bring disgrace to his family. = 他的行为给他的家庭带来了耻辱。
The scandal is a disgrace to the city. = 这场丑闻是这座城市的耻辱。

一般过去时:
The incident brought disgrace upon the whole team. = 这件事给整个团队带来了耻辱。
His betrayal was a disgrace that he could never atone for. = 他的背叛是永远无法弥补的耻辱。

现在完成时:
This affair has brought disgrace to the government. = 这件事给政府带来了耻辱。
His actions have brought disgrace upon his family name. = 他的行为玷污了他的家族名誉。

过去完成时:
They had already fallen into disgrace before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，他们就已经声名狼藉了。
The company had already faced disgrace before the new CEO arrived. = 在新任CEO到来之前，公司就已经名誉扫地了。

将来时:
His upcoming trial will bring further disgrace upon his family. = 他即将进行的审判将给他的家人带来更大的耻辱。
This decision will bring disgrace to the entire organization. = 这个决定将给整个组织带来耻辱。

虚拟语气:
If he had not acted so foolishly, he would not have fallen into disgrace. = 如果他不那么愚蠢行事，就不会声名狼藉。
I wish this disgrace had never happened to our company. = 我希望这场耻辱从未发生在我们公司身上。

被动语态:
He was disgraced by his own actions. = 他因自己的行为而蒙羞。
The politician was publicly disgraced for his involvement in the scandal. = 这位政客因卷入丑闻而被公开羞辱。

疑问句:
What is the disgrace that he has brought upon himself? = 他给自己带来了什么耻辱？
Does his behavior bring any disgrace to his profession? = 他的行为给他的职业带来任何耻辱吗？

否定句:
His actions did not bring disgrace upon his family. = 他的行为并没有给他的家庭带来耻辱。
She did not fall into disgrace despite the accusations. = 尽管受到指控，她并没有声名狼藉。
