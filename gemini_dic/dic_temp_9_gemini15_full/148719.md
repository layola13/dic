overtrading:/ˌoʊvərˈtreɪdɪŋ/ | n. |过度交易，经营过度 |The act of a business undertaking more transactions than it can comfortably handle, often leading to financial difficulties.  This can involve buying too much inventory, extending credit too freely, or engaging in excessive borrowing. |例句：Overtrading led to the company's bankruptcy. 过度交易导致公司破产。|例句：The small business was struggling due to overtrading and a lack of cash flow. 这家小企业因过度交易和现金流不足而苦苦挣扎。|近义词：overextension, excessive trading, reckless trading |反义词：undertrading, cautious trading |名词
