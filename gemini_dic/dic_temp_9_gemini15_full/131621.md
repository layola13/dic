noncompositeness:/ˌnɒnkəmˈpɒzɪtnəs/| n. |非合成性，非复合性|The state or quality of not being composite; the state of being simple or uncompounded.  It refers to something that is not made up of separate parts or elements.  This term is commonly used in mathematics, especially in number theory, and in fields like chemistry and linguistics. | Example: The noncompositeness of the prime number 7 is evident in its indivisibility by any smaller integers except 1 and itself.  七的质数性（非合成性）体现在它除了1和它本身以外不能被任何较小的整数整除。|近义词：simplicity, indivisibility, primality |反义词：compositeness, complexity
