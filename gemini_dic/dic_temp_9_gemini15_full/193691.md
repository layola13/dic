self-compatibility:/ˌsɛlfkəmˌpætəˈbɪləti/| n. |自交亲和性；自合性|The ability of a plant or other organism to reproduce sexually by self-fertilization.  This means that the pollen from a flower can successfully fertilize the ovules of the same flower or another flower on the same plant. |Example:  Many cultivated varieties of plants are self-compatible, making propagation easier.|近义词：self-fertility|反义词：self-incompatibility
