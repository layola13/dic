/ˈɛkˌtʃiːtər/| n.|查没官，财务官|An escheator was a royal official in medieval England responsible for seizing lands and goods that escheated (fell to the Crown) due to lack of a legal heir.  They also dealt with the collection of royal revenues and the enforcement of royal rights.|The escheator investigated the property of the deceased to determine if it escheated to the crown.|近义词：revenue officer, tax collector|反义词：heir, inheritor
