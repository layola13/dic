nematophora:/nɛmætəˈfɔːrə/| n. |线形体；线状体|A nematophore is a specialized cell or structure found in certain organisms, particularly cnidarians (like jellyfish and corals), that produces and contains nematocysts (stinging cells).  They are often thread-like or filamentous in appearance, hence the name.  The nematophores may be used for defense or prey capture.|Example: The nematophores of the sea anemone glowed faintly in the dim light.  海葵的线形体在昏暗的光线下发出微弱的光芒。 The jellyfish's nematophores extended, ready to strike. 水母的线形体伸展开来，准备攻击。|近义词：cnidocyst (in some contexts)|反义词：无
