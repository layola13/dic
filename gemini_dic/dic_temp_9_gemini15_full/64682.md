escape:/ɪˈskeɪp/ | /ɪˈskeɪp/ | v. n. | 逃跑；逃脱；避免；逃避；泄漏；逸出 | to get away from a place or situation, especially one that is dangerous or unpleasant;  to avoid something unpleasant or unwanted | 近义词：flee, avoid, evade | 反义词：encounter, confront


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He escapes from prison every night in his dreams. = 他在梦里每晚都从监狱里逃出来。
The gas escapes from the damaged pipe. = 气体从损坏的管道中泄漏出来。


一般过去时:
The prisoner escaped from jail last night. = 囚犯昨晚越狱了。
They narrowly escaped serious injury in the accident. = 他们在事故中侥幸避免了重伤。


现在完成时:
She has escaped serious injury in the car accident. = 她在那次车祸中逃过一劫。
The details of the plan have escaped my memory. = 计划的细节我已经记不清了。


过去完成时:
They had escaped the burning building before the roof collapsed. = 房屋倒塌之前，他们已经逃出了燃烧的建筑物。
I had escaped noticing the mistake until it was too late. = 直到为时已晚，我才发现那个错误。


将来时:
He will escape punishment for his crime. = 他将逃脱对其罪行的惩罚。
I hope to escape the rush hour traffic tomorrow. = 我希望明天能避开早高峰交通。


虚拟语气:
If I had known the danger, I would have escaped. = 如果我知道危险，我就会逃跑了。
I wish I could escape this never-ending meeting. = 我真希望我能逃脱这个没完没了的会议。


被动语态:
The prisoner was escaped by the guards. = 囚犯被狱警放走了。(注意：此句被动语态较为不自然，更常用的表达是The prisoner escaped.)
The secret was escaped by someone in the company. = 公司里有人泄露了秘密。


疑问句:
Can he escape from the police? = 他能逃脱警方的追捕吗？
How did they escape the fire? = 他们是如何逃脱大火的？


否定句:
He could not escape his responsibilities. = 他无法逃避他的责任。
The truth could not escape our attention. = 事实无法逃过我们的注意。


PHRASE->
escape from = 从...逃脱
escape to = 逃到...
escape notice = 未被注意
a narrow escape = 九死一生
escape velocity = 逃逸速度
