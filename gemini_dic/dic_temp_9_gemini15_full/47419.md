deadlocking: /ˈdedˌlɒkɪŋ/ | v. |死锁；僵局；卡住 | The act of becoming deadlocked; a situation where two or more processes are blocked indefinitely, waiting for each other.  |例句：The negotiations are deadlocking, with neither side willing to compromise. 谈判陷入了僵局，双方都不愿妥协。

The software is deadlocking, preventing any further progress. 软件死锁，无法继续运行。|近义词：stalemate, impasse, standstill |反义词：resolution, breakthrough, progress |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The system deadlocks frequently. = 系统经常死锁。
The two parties are deadlocking on the key issue. = 双方在关键问题上僵持不下。


一般过去时:
The program deadlocked yesterday. = 程序昨天死锁了。
The negotiations deadlocked after hours of discussion. = 经过数小时的讨论后，谈判陷入了僵局。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

词源:
The word "deadlocking" comes from the combination of "dead" (meaning inactive or unable to proceed) and "locking" (meaning a state of being fixed or unable to move).  It describes a situation where processes are "locked" in a state of inactivity, unable to proceed further.
