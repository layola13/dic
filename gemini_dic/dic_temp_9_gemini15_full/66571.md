exhilarate:/ɪɡˈzɪləreɪt/| vt.|使兴奋；使高兴；使振奋|To make someone feel very happy and excited.|The news exhilarated her.|近义词：thrill, excite, invigorate|反义词：depress, discourage, dispirit|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The roller coaster exhilarates me. = 过山车让我兴奋。
The music exhilarates the crowd. = 音乐使人群兴奋。


一般过去时:
The victory exhilarated the team. = 胜利使球队兴奋。
The comedian's jokes exhilarated the audience. = 喜剧演员的笑话让观众很兴奋。


现在完成时:
The trip has exhilarated me. = 这趟旅行让我精神焕发。
Winning the competition has exhilarated her. = 赢得比赛让她兴奋不已。


过去完成时:
The good news had exhilarated him before he went to bed. = 好消息在他睡觉前让他兴奋不已。
She had been exhilarated by the prospect of the journey. = 她对旅程的前景感到兴奋。


将来时:
The concert will exhilarate the audience. = 音乐会将使观众兴奋。
I think the vacation will exhilarate us. = 我认为假期会让我们感到兴奋。


虚拟语气:
If I won the lottery, it would exhilarate me. = 如果我中了彩票，我会非常兴奋。
I wish the news would exhilarate my friend. = 我希望这个消息能让我朋友兴奋起来。


被动语态:
The audience was exhilarated by the performance. = 观众被演出所振奋。
He was exhilarated by the unexpected success. = 他因意外的成功而兴奋不已。


疑问句:
Does the prospect of a vacation exhilarate you? = 假期的前景是否让你兴奋？
What exhilarates you the most in life? =  生活中什么最让你兴奋？


否定句:
The bad weather didn't exhilarate them. = 恶劣的天气并没有让他们兴奋。
The boring lecture did not exhilarate the students. = 枯燥的讲座没有让学生们兴奋起来。


PHRASE->
be exhilarated by = 被…所振奋
feel exhilarated = 感到兴奋
become exhilarated = 变得兴奋
utterly exhilarated = 极其兴奋
deeply exhilarated = 深深地感到兴奋


词源:
From Middle English *exhilaraten, from Late Latin *exhilarāre, from Latin ex- ("out, from") + hilaris ("cheerful, merry").  The Latin root "hilaris" is related to the word "hilarious."
