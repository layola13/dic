feodatory:/ˈfiːədətɔːri/| n. |封臣；受封者；受领地者 | A person who holds land in fee or in feud from a superior lord; a vassal.  A feodatory owes allegiance and service to their lord in return for the land they hold. | Example: The knight was a feodatory of the king, bound to serve him in war.  这个骑士是国王的封臣，有义务为他作战。|近义词：vassal, liegeman |反义词：overlord, sovereign
