deflated:/dɪˈfleɪtɪd/ | adj. |泄气的；被放气的；被贬低的 |  Feeling disappointed and discouraged; lacking energy or enthusiasm; having had the air or gas removed.  |例句：After the loss, the team felt deflated. 比赛失利后，球队士气低落。

例句：The balloon was deflated after the party.派对结束后气球瘪了。


近义词：discouraged, disheartened, depressed, flat


反义词：inflated, encouraged, elated, buoyant


词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He feels deflated after the exam. 他考试后感到很泄气。
The tires are deflated. 轮胎瘪了。


一般过去时:
She was deflated by the criticism. 她被批评后很沮丧。
The basketball became deflated. 篮球瘪了。


现在完成时:
I have felt deflated since I lost my job. 自从失业后我就一直很沮丧。
The balloon has deflated. 气球瘪了。


过去完成时:
He had felt deflated before the interview. 他在面试前就已经很泄气了。
The tire had deflated before we started our journey.  在我们开始旅程之前轮胎就已经瘪了。


将来时:
She will feel deflated if she doesn't get the job. 如果她没得到这份工作，她会很沮丧。
The balloon will deflate soon. 气球很快就会瘪掉。


虚拟语气:
If I failed the test, I would feel deflated. 如果我考试不及格，我会感到很泄气。
I wish I hadn't felt so deflated after the presentation. 我真希望演讲后我没有那么沮丧。


被动语态:
His ego was deflated by the harsh review.  他那自大的态度被严厉的评论给打击了。
The tires were deflated by a sharp object. 轮胎被尖锐的物体扎破了。


疑问句:

Is the football deflated? 足球瘪了吗？


否定句:
