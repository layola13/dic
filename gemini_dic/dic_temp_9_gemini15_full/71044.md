first-degree:/ˌfɜːrstˈdiːɡriː/| adj.|一级；首要的；最严重的|Of the lowest or least advanced grade or level; primary or initial.  It also refers to the most serious degree of a crime, especially murder.|例句：He was charged with first-degree murder. 他被指控犯有一级谋杀罪。

例句：This is a first-degree burn. 这是一个一级烧伤。


近义词：primary, principal, initial, foremost, highest (in the context of crime severity) |反义词：second-degree, third-degree (in the context of crime severity), minor, subsequent


词源：The term "first-degree" comes from the historical grading of crimes and burns.  "First" indicates the highest or most serious level within a category.  "Degree" in this context refers to a level or rank.  The use in mathematics is a related concept, referring to the highest power of a variable in an equation.
