deterrence:/dɪˈtɜːrəns/| n. |威慑；震慑；制止|The action of discouraging an action or event through instilling doubt or fear of the consequences.|The threat of nuclear war acted as a deterrence to aggression during the Cold War. 核战争的威胁在冷战期间对侵略起到了威慑作用。|近义词：inhibition, restraint, discouragement|反义词：encouragement, incitement


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Deterrence plays a crucial role in international relations. 威慑在国际关系中扮演着至关重要的角色。
Nuclear weapons provide a powerful deterrence against attack. 核武器对攻击提供了强大的威慑力。


一般过去时:
The harsh punishment served as a deterrence to future crime. 严厉的惩罚对未来的犯罪起到了威慑作用。
His stern warning acted as a deterrence to further disobedience. 他严厉的警告阻止了进一步的违抗。


现在完成时:
The government's policies have not proven effective as a deterrence. 政府的政策并未证明具有威慑力。
Increased police presence has had a positive effect as a deterrence against crime. 增加的警力对犯罪起到了积极的威慑作用。


过去完成时:
The potential consequences had already served as a deterrence to his actions.  潜在的后果已经对他的行为起到了威慑作用。
They had hoped that the threat of punishment would act as a deterrence. 他们曾希望惩罚的威胁能够起到威慑作用。


将来时:
Stronger penalties will act as a greater deterrence against speeding. 更严厉的处罚将对超速行驶起到更大的威慑作用。
Increased security measures will hopefully provide a better deterrence in the future. 加强的安全措施有望在未来提供更好的威慑力。


虚拟语气:
If the penalties were harsher, they would be a greater deterrence. 如果惩罚更严厉，它们将起到更大的威慑作用。
I wish there were a more effective deterrence to cybercrime. 我希望有一种更有效的网络犯罪威慑措施。


被动语态:
Deterrence is often achieved through the threat of punishment. 威慑通常是通过惩罚的威胁来实现的。
The effectiveness of deterrence is constantly being debated. 威慑的有效性一直在被争论。


疑问句:
What measures can be taken to improve deterrence? 可以采取什么措施来改善威慑力？
Does the death penalty act as a sufficient deterrence? 死刑是否能起到足够的威慑作用？


否定句:
The current system does not provide adequate deterrence. 当前的制度没有提供足够的威慑力。
His actions were not deterred by the warnings. 他的行为没有被警告所阻止。
