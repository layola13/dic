pseudoconservative:/ˌsuːdoʊkənˈsɜːrvətɪv/| adj. |伪保守的，假保守的 |Appearing to be conservative but not truly holding conservative beliefs or principles; often used to describe policies or individuals that adopt conservative rhetoric or positions for strategic or opportunistic reasons, rather than out of genuine ideological conviction.  |Example: The politician's pseudoconservative stance on taxes was seen as a cynical ploy to win votes.  这位政治家在税收问题上的伪保守立场被视为一种赢得选票的卑鄙策略。|近义词：reactionary (in some contexts), opportunistic,  |反义词：liberal, progressive, genuinely conservative
