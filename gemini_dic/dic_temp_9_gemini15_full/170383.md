provocatively:/prəˈvɒkətɪvli/ | adv. | 煽动地；挑衅地；诱惑地 | In a way that is intended to provoke a reaction, especially anger or sexual excitement.  | 例句：She dressed provocatively to attract attention. 她穿着暴露以吸引注意。

例句：He spoke provocatively, aiming to ignite a debate. 他说话挑衅，旨在引发一场辩论。


近义词：stimulatingly, enticingly, alluringly, teasingly, defiantly


反义词：inoffensively, mildly, timidly, submissively


词源:  From provocative (adjective) + -ly (adverbial suffix).  "Provocative" itself comes from the verb "provoke," from Old French *provoquer, from Latin *provocare, from pro- ("forth") + vocare ("to call").  The sense evolution is from "to call forth" to "to incite" to "to stimulate (sexually)."
