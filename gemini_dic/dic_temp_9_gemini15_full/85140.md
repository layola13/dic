hardly:/ˈhɑːrdli/| adv.|几乎不，简直不 |Almost not; scarcely. It emphasizes the near absence of something.  |例句：I hardly knew him. 我几乎不认识他。

例句：She hardly ever eats meat. 她几乎不吃肉。


近义词：scarcely, barely, seldom, rarely |反义词：easily, readily, frequently


词源：源自古英语 *hardlice，由 hard（坚硬的，困难的）+ -lice（副词后缀）构成。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hardly ever goes to the gym. 他几乎不去健身房。
I hardly know what to say. 我几乎不知道该说什么。


一般过去时:
They hardly slept last night. 他们昨晚几乎没睡。
I hardly noticed the change. 我几乎没注意到变化。


现在完成时:
I have hardly seen him this year. 今年我几乎没见过他。
She has hardly ever traveled abroad. 她几乎从未出国旅行。


过去完成时:
I had hardly finished eating when the phone rang. 我刚吃完饭电话就响了。(强调紧接发生)
They had hardly started when it began to rain. 他们刚开始就下雨了。(强调紧接发生)


将来时:
I will hardly have time to finish the project. 我几乎没有时间完成这个项目。
We will hardly be able to afford the trip. 我们几乎负担不起这次旅行。


虚拟语气:
I would hardly expect him to agree. 我几乎不指望他会同意。
It would hardly be fair to punish him. 惩罚他几乎不公平。


被动语态:
The task was hardly completed on time. 任务几乎没有按时完成。
The problem is hardly ever discussed.  这个问题几乎从未被讨论过。


疑问句:
Hardly anyone came to the party?  几乎没有人来参加派对吗？
Hardly any of the food was left?  几乎没有食物剩下吗？


否定句:
It's not hardly cold enough to snow. 天气还不够冷到下雪。（口语中不推荐这种双重否定）
I don't hardly understand. 我几乎不明白。（口语中不推荐这种双重否定）


PHRASE->
hardly ever:几乎从不
hardly at all:几乎完全不
hardly anyone/anything:几乎没有人/物
hardly worth:几乎不值得
can hardly:几乎不能
