commensal:/ˈkɒmənˌsæl/ /ˈkɑːmənˌsæl/| adj. n.|共栖的；共栖生物| Relating to commensalism, a relationship between two organisms where one benefits and the other is neither harmed nor benefited.  A commensal organism benefits from another organism without harming or benefiting it. | Example:  Barnacles attached to a whale are a commensal relationship; the barnacles get a place to live and food, while the whale is largely unaffected.  The remora fish attaching to a shark is another example of a commensal relationship.|近义词：symbiotic (in some contexts)|反义词：parasitic, antagonistic|形容词，名词
