generalization:/ˌdʒɛnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |概括；普遍化；推广 |The process of forming a general idea, rule, or principle from specific examples.  It involves identifying common features and applying them to a broader context.  This can lead to useful insights but also to oversimplifications or inaccuracies if not done carefully. | Example:  The generalization that all cats are afraid of water is false.  所有猫都怕水的概括是错误的。 |近义词：summary, abstraction, generalization |反义词：specificity, detail, particularization


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He makes generalizations about people based on limited information. 他根据有限的信息对人进行概括。
This study avoids generalizations and focuses on specific cases. 这项研究避免了概括，而是关注具体案例。

一般过去时:
She made a hasty generalization about the entire group. 她对整个群体做出了草率的概括。
Scientists used to generalize about the behavior of animals. 科学家过去常常对动物的行为进行概括。

现在完成时:
Researchers have made significant generalizations about human behavior. 研究人员对人类行为做出了重大概括。
They have generalized their findings to a wider population. 他们已将他们的发现推广到更广泛的人群。

过去完成时:
Before the experiment, they had already made several generalizations. 在实验之前，他们已经做了几个概括。
He had generalized from a small sample size, leading to inaccurate conclusions. 他从一个小的样本量进行概括，导致了不准确的结论。

将来时:
She will generalize her research findings in her presentation. 她将在她的报告中概括她的研究结果。
Scientists will generalize the results to a broader range of applications. 科学家们将把结果推广到更广泛的应用领域。

虚拟语气:
If she had more data, she would not have made such a broad generalization. 如果她有更多的数据，她就不会做出如此宽泛的概括。
I wish I hadn't made such a sweeping generalization. 我真希望我没有做出如此笼统的概括。

被动语态:
The results were generalized to the entire population. 结果被推广到整个人群。
His theory was generalized by later researchers. 他的理论后来被研究人员推广。

疑问句:
Can we generalize these findings to other cultures? 我们能否将这些发现推广到其他文化？
What generalizations can be made about the data? 关于这些数据可以得出什么结论？

否定句:
We cannot generalize from this single case. 我们不能从这个单一案例中进行概括。
The study did not generalize its findings beyond the sample group. 这项研究没有将其研究结果推广到样本组之外。

词源:
The word "generalization" comes from the Latin word "generalis," meaning "general" or "of a general kind."  The suffix "-ization" indicates the process of making something general.
