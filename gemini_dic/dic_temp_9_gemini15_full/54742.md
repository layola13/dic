disparish: /dɪˈspærɪʃ/ | v. |轻视；贬低；诋毁 | To speak of or treat (someone or something) with contempt; to belittle. |例句：He disparaged her efforts, saying they were insignificant. 他轻视她的努力，说它们微不足道。  |例句：Don't disparage the achievements of others just because you feel inferior. 不要仅仅因为你感觉自己不如别人就贬低他人的成就。|近义词：belittle, denigrate, deprecate, slight, scorn |反义词：praise, commend, extol, laud


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disparages his competitors' products. = 他贬低竞争对手的产品。
She disparages the value of hard work. = 她轻视努力工作的价值。


一般过去时:
He disparaged her intelligence in front of everyone. = 他当着所有人的面贬低她的智力。
They disparaged the new policy as ineffective. = 他们贬低这项新政策，认为它无效。


现在完成时:
He has disparaged her accomplishments on numerous occasions. = 他多次贬低她的成就。
They have disparaged the importance of education. = 他们贬低了教育的重要性。


过去完成时:
She had disparaged his work before he even presented it. = 她在他甚至还没展示他的作品之前就贬低了他的作品。
They had disparaged the idea long before it was even considered. = 他们早在这个想法被考虑之前就贬低了它。


将来时:
He will disparage her efforts again if given the chance. = 如果有机会，他会再次贬低她的努力。
They will disparage the new rules if they don't benefit them. = 如果这些新规对他们没有好处，他们就会贬低这些新规。


虚拟语气:
If he disparaged her, she would be deeply hurt. = 如果他贬低她，她会非常伤心。
I wish he hadn't disparaged my work. = 我真希望他没贬低我的工作。


被动语态:
Her work was disparaged by her colleagues. = 她的作品被她的同事贬低了。
The new proposal was disparaged as unrealistic. = 这项新提案被贬低为不切实际。


疑问句:
Did he disparage your ideas? = 他贬低你的想法了吗？
Why did they disparage the efforts of the team? = 为什么他们贬低团队的努力？


否定句:
He did not disparage her abilities. = 他没有贬低她的能力。
They did not disparage the importance of the project. = 他们没有贬低这个项目的重要性。


PHRASE->
disparage openly = 公开贬低
disparage unfairly = 不公平地贬低
disparage subtly = 巧妙地贬低
disparage repeatedly = 反复贬低
disparage without cause = 无缘无故地贬低
