buz:/bʌz/| n. v.|嗡嗡声，忙碌，闲聊| a low humming sound, as of bees;  busy activity; to talk casually|例句：The bees were buzzing around the flowers. 蜜蜂嗡嗡地飞舞在花朵周围。|近义词：hum, drone, bustle, chatter|反义词：silence, quiet, stillness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bees buzz around the hive. = 蜜蜂在蜂巢周围嗡嗡叫。
He buzzes with energy. = 他精力充沛。


一般过去时:
The engine buzzed loudly. = 发动机嗡嗡作响。
They buzzed about the latest gossip. = 他们闲聊着最新的八卦。


现在完成时:
The news has buzzed through the town. = 这个消息已经在镇上传开了。
She has buzzed around all day. = 她整天都在忙碌。


过去完成时:
The phone had buzzed before I could answer. = 我还没来得及接电话，它就响了。
They had buzzed about the party for weeks. = 他们几个星期以来一直在谈论着派对。


将来时:
The alarm will buzz at 6 am. = 闹钟将在早上6点响。
They will buzz excitedly about the trip. = 他们会兴奋地谈论这次旅行。


虚拟语气:
If the phone buzzed, I would answer it. = 如果电话响了，我会接。
If only the rumour wouldn't buzz so much! = 但愿这个谣言不会传得这么厉害！


被动语态:
The room was buzzed with conversation. = 房间里充满了谈话声。
The news was buzzed around the office quickly. = 消息迅速在办公室里传开了。


疑问句:
Does the phone buzz often? = 电话经常响吗？
Is the city always buzzing with activity? = 这个城市总是那么热闹吗？


否定句:
The bees didn't buzz today. = 蜜蜂今天没有嗡嗡叫。
The party wasn't buzzing with excitement. = 派对并不令人兴奋。
