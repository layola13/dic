fetterer:/ˈfɛtərər/| n. | 束缚者；囚禁者 | A person or thing that fetters or restrains.  Someone or something that restricts freedom or progress. | Example: The oppressive regime was a fetterer of its people's aspirations.  这个压迫性政权束缚了人民的愿望。|近义词：restrainer, jailer, binder, oppressor |反义词：liberator, emancipator


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dictator fetters his people. = 独裁者束缚着他的子民。
He fetters the horse to the post. = 他把马拴在柱子上。


一般过去时:
They fettered the prisoner with chains. = 他们用锁链捆绑了囚犯。
The old law fettered the development of the economy. = 旧的法律束缚了经济的发展。


现在完成时:
The system has fettered progress for decades. = 这个制度几十年来一直阻碍着进步。
She has fettered herself with unnecessary commitments. = 她让自己承担了不必要的责任。


过去完成时:
They had fettered the suspect before the interrogation. = 审讯前他们已经把嫌疑犯绑住了。
The outdated rules had fettered innovation for years. = 过时的规章制度多年来一直阻碍着创新。


将来时:
The new regulations will fetter small businesses. = 新的规章制度将束缚小型企业。
They will fetter the criminal's movement. = 他们将会限制罪犯的行动。


虚拟语气:
If they hadn't fettered him, he would have escaped. = 如果他们没有捆绑他，他就会逃跑。
I wish they hadn't fettered the free press. = 我真希望他们没有束缚新闻自由。


被动语态:
The bird was fettered to the perch. = 鸟被绑在栖木上。
His creativity was fettered by the rigid rules. = 他的创造力被僵化的规则所束缚。


疑问句:
Were the prisoners fettered? = 囚犯们被束缚了吗？
What fettered their progress? = 是什么阻碍了他们的进步？


否定句:
The government did not fetter the media. = 政府没有束缚媒体。
They did not fetter themselves to tradition. = 他们没有让自己被传统束缚。
