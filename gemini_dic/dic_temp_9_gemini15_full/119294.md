micro-evolution:/ˌmaɪkroʊˌɛvəˈluʃən/ | n. |微进化 |The small-scale evolutionary changes that occur within a species or population over a relatively short period.  These changes are often driven by natural selection acting on variations within the gene pool.  Microevolutionary processes can lead to the emergence of new subspecies or varieties within a species, but not to the formation of entirely new species (that is macroevolution).| Example: The development of antibiotic resistance in bacteria is a classic example of microevolution.  细菌对抗生素产生耐药性是微进化的一个经典例子。|近义词:adaptation, genetic drift, speciation (in a limited sense)|反义词:macroevolution
