lamellirostres: /ˌlæməliˈrɒstreɪz/ | n. |  lamella-billed birds;  板状嘴类鸟 | Lamellirostres is a taxonomic group of birds characterized by their broad, flattened bills with lamellae (thin, parallel plates) along the edges.  These lamellae are used to filter food from water or mud.  The group includes ducks, geese, and swans.  | The lamellibill is an adaptation for filter feeding. | 近义词：暂无精确近义词，可根据语境用更具体的鸟类名称代替 | 反义词：暂无
