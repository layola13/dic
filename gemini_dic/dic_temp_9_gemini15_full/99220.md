intercolonization:/ˌɪntərˌkɒlənaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 殖民地间的殖民；互相殖民 | The process by which one colonized group establishes control over another colonized group, often within the same larger colonial structure.  This can involve economic exploitation, political domination, and cultural suppression.  It often occurs in contexts of overlapping or competing colonial powers. | Example: The study examined the intercolonization between different indigenous groups in the Americas under Spanish rule.  | 近义词:  internal colonization, sub-imperialism | 反义词: decolonization
