platitudinarian:/ˌplætɪˈtuːdɪnɛəriən/| n. |陈词滥调者，老生常谈者 | A person who habitually uses platitudes.  They often speak in clichés and generalities, lacking originality or depth of thought. |He's become a tiresome platitudinarian, constantly repeating the same empty phrases. 他已经变成了一个令人厌烦的老生常谈者，不断重复着同样的空话。|近义词：prosaist, cliché-monger |反义词：original thinker, innovator

词源:
