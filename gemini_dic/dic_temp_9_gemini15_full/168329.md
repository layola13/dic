pro-Republican: /ˌproʊ rɪˈpʌblɪkən/ | adj. | 支持共和党的；亲共和党的 |  Supporting or favoring the Republican Party.  This term describes individuals, policies, or actions that align with the Republican Party's platform and goals. | 例句：His pro-Republican stance was evident in his speeches. = 他在演讲中明显地表达了他支持共和党的立场。

The pro-Republican media outlets heavily criticized the Democratic candidate. = 支持共和党的媒体强烈批评了民主党候选人。


近义词：Republican-supporting, pro-GOP (GOP is an abbreviation for Grand Old Party, another name for the Republican Party)


反义词：pro-Democrat, anti-Republican


词源：The word comes from the combination of "pro-" (meaning "in favor of" or "supporting") and "Republican" (referring to the Republican Party).  The term's usage reflects the two-party system prevalent in the United States, where political affiliations are often strongly defined.


USAGE->
This word is primarily used as an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms in itself.  Its usage depends on the context of a sentence.  It can modify nouns like "stance", "media", "voter", "sentiment", etc.
