noninflectional: /ˌnɒnɪnˈflekʃənəl/ | adj. | 不屈折的；不变化的 | Not subject to inflection; not changing in form to express grammatical relationships such as tense, number, or case.  This term typically applies to words or parts of speech that do not undergo changes in form to indicate grammatical function.  For example, many adverbs and prepositions are noninflectional. | The preposition "in" is noninflectional; it doesn't change its form regardless of the context. |近义词：uninflected, invariable |反义词：inflectional
