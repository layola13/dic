rhizomes:/ˈraɪzoʊmz/| n. |根茎；地下茎|A rhizome is a continuously growing horizontal underground stem that sends out roots and shoots from its nodes.  It is a characteristic of many ferns and other plants. Rhizomes are also used in botany to describe similar structures in fungi and other organisms.| Example:  Ginger is a rhizome, and its lateral buds can develop into new plants. 姜是一种根茎，它的侧芽可以发育成新的植株。|近义词：rootstock, stolon |反义词：aerial stem, upright stem
