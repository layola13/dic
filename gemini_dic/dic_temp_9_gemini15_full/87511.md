heterodactylae:/ˌhɛtərəˈdæktɪliː/| adj.|异指的，对趾的|Having toes arranged in two groups, one pointing forward and one pointing backward, as in birds of prey. This arrangement allows for a strong grip on branches or prey.|Example:  Owls and parrots are examples of birds with heterodactyl feet.|近义词：zygodactyl (often used interchangeably, though technically zygodactyl refers to a specific 2-2 arrangement)|反义词：homodactyl
