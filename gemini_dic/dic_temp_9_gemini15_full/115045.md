mandore:/mænˈdɔːr/| n. |曼陀铃(一种拨弦乐器)|A mandore is a now-obsolete plucked stringed instrument of the lute family, typically having four or five courses of strings (a course is a pair of strings tuned in unison).  It was popular in Europe from the 16th to the 18th centuries.  Its sound was described as being somewhere between a lute and a guitar.|The mandore was a popular instrument in the baroque period. |近义词：lute, cittern|反义词：无
