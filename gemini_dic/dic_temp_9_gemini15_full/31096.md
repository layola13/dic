championed:/ˈtʃæmpiənd/| vt.|拥护；支持；倡导|To actively support or defend publicly.  The word implies a strong and often vocal advocacy.|例句：She championed the rights of the marginalized. 她拥护边缘群体的权利。

例句：He championed the cause of environmental protection. 他大力倡导环境保护事业。


近义词：advocated, supported, defended, promoted, espoused

反义词：opposed, resisted, condemned, denounced


词性：动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
She champions social justice. = 她拥护社会正义。
He champions the rights of workers. = 他维护工人的权利。

一般过去时:
They championed the new legislation. = 他们支持这项新法案。
She championed the cause of women's suffrage. = 她为妇女的选举权奔走呼号。

现在完成时:
He has championed environmental protection for many years. = 他多年来一直倡导环境保护。
She has championed the rights of refugees. = 她一直为难民的权利奔走呼号。

过去完成时:
They had championed the cause before the public outcry. = 在公众抗议之前，他们就已经为此奔走呼号了。
He had championed human rights throughout his career. = 他在整个职业生涯中都一直捍卫人权。

将来时:
She will champion the cause of the underprivileged. = 她将为弱势群体发声。
They will champion the new policy. = 他们将支持这项新政策。

虚拟语气:
If she championed the cause, more people would support it. = 如果她支持这项事业，更多的人会支持它。
I wish he had championed the idea earlier. = 我希望他早点支持这个想法。

被动语态:
The new policy was championed by the government. = 政府大力支持这项新政策。
His ideas were championed by many intellectuals. = 他的想法得到许多知识分子的支持。

疑问句:
Who championed this idea? = 谁支持了这个主意？
What causes did she champion in her lifetime? = 她一生中支持过哪些事业？

否定句:
He did not champion the controversial bill. = 他不支持那项有争议的法案。
She did not champion the cause of the opposition party. = 她不支持反对党的诉求。


PHRASE->
champion a cause = 倡导一项事业
champion the rights of = 捍卫…的权利
champion someone's efforts = 支持某人的努力
champion a new idea = 支持一个新想法
champion free speech = 捍卫言论自由
