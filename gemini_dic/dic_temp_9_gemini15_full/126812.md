napalm:/ˈneɪpɑːm/| n. |凝固汽油弹|A highly flammable substance used in incendiary bombs and weapons; a mixture of gasoline and a thickening agent such as polystyrene.  It adheres to targets and burns intensely, causing severe damage and casualties.|The napalm attack destroyed the village. 凝固汽油弹袭击摧毁了村庄。|incendiary bomb, firebomb|extinguisher, water.
