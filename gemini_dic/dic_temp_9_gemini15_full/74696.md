freehold:/ˈfriːhoʊld/| n. |完全所有权；永久业权|The right to own land or property without any time limit.  It implies absolute ownership and the right to dispose of the property as one sees fit.  This contrasts with leasehold, where ownership is for a specific period.|例句：He finally bought a freehold house after years of renting. 他在租房多年后终于买下了一栋拥有永久产权的房子。

例句：Owning a freehold property provides a sense of security and long-term investment.拥有永久产权的房产能带来安全感和长期的投资价值。


近义词：ownership, title, possession |反义词：leasehold, tenancy


词源：From Old English *frēohōld, from frēo "free" + hōld "holding".  The word reflects the historical distinction between freeholders (those with complete ownership) and tenants (those holding land under a lease).
