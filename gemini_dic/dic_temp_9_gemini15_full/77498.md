gazetteer: /ˌɡæzəˈtɪər/ | n. |地名词典，地名志 | A gazetteer is a geographical dictionary or directory. It provides information about places, such as their locations, populations, and historical significance.  Gazetteers can be general, covering a wide area, or specific, focusing on a particular region or topic.  They are often used by cartographers, researchers, and travelers. | Example:  The researcher consulted a gazetteer to verify the location of the ancient ruins.  = 研究人员查阅地名词典以核实古代遗址的位置。
