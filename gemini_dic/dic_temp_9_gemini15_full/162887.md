precensorship:/ˌpriːˈsɛnsərʃɪp/| n. |出版前审查；预先审查|The control of information before it is published or broadcast, often involving the suppression of material deemed objectionable.  Precensorship aims to prevent the dissemination of potentially harmful or offensive content.  It contrasts with postcensorship, where material is reviewed after publication.|例句：The government imposed strict precensorship on all media outlets. 政府对所有媒体实施了严格的出版前审查。

例句：Many argue that precensorship violates freedom of speech. 许多人认为出版前审查侵犯了言论自由。


近义词：prior restraint, prepublication review, censorship (before publication)

反义词：postcensorship, freedom of the press


词源：pre- (before) + censorship (the suppression of speech, public communication, or other information)
