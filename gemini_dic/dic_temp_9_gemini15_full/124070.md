morphophyly:/mɔːrfoʊˈfɪli/| n. |形态种系发生学|The study of the evolutionary relationships among organisms based on their morphology (form and structure).  It focuses on the shared derived characteristics (synapomorphies) that indicate common ancestry. Morphophyly can be contrasted with phylogenetics based on molecular data (molecular phylogenetics).|Example:  Morphophyly studies suggested a close relationship between these two species, a conclusion supported by subsequent molecular phylogenetic analyses.|近义词: morphological phylogeny, comparative morphology|反义词: molecular phylogeny
