preconization:/ˌpriːkɒnɪˈzeɪʃən/| n.|预先加冕；预先封圣|The act of preconizing someone, especially a cardinal or bishop, before the official ceremony.  This often involves the announcement of the appointment before the formal installation.|Example: The preconization of the new cardinal took place a week before the formal ceremony. 预先加冕新枢机主教的仪式在一周前的正式仪式之前举行。|近义词：pre-appointment, nomination|反义词：post-consecration
