buttonhole: /ˈbʌtənhoʊl/ | n., vt. | 钮扣眼；扣眼；(与人)攀谈 |

英文解析:
As a noun, a buttonhole is a slit or loop of fabric made to fasten a button.  As a verb, it means to stop someone and talk to them, often for a long time, whether they want to talk or not.

例句:
He carefully sewed the buttonhole on his shirt. = 他仔细地缝好了衬衫上的钮扣眼。

She buttonholed me at the party and talked for an hour. = 她在派对上拦住我，聊了一个小时。


近义词:
(n.) button loop, eyelet
(vt.) accost, detain, engage


反义词:
(vt.) ignore, avoid, dismiss


词性:
Noun, Verb
