minute:/ˈmɪnɪt/| n. adj.|分钟；一会儿；片刻；会议记录；微小的；详细的|A minute is a unit of time equal to 60 seconds.  It can also refer to a very short period of time.  As an adjective, it means extremely small or insignificant. |例句：The meeting lasted for 60 minutes.  会议持续了60分钟。|例句： I'll be with you in a minute. 我马上就来。|近义词：moment, instant, brief, tiny, slight|反义词：hour, long, extensive, huge


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The meeting lasts for one minute. = 会议持续一分钟。
A minute is a short period of time. = 一分钟是一段短暂的时间。

一般过去时:
The meeting lasted only five minutes. = 会议只持续了五分钟。
It took me a minute to understand. = 我花了一分钟才明白。

现在完成时:
It has been one minute since he left. = 他离开已经一分钟了。
We have waited for several minutes. = 我们已经等了几分钟了。


过去完成时:
The meeting had already started before we arrived. = 我们到达之前会议已经开始了。
He had been waiting for minutes. = 他已经等了数分钟了。


将来时:
The meeting will last about 30 minutes. = 会议将持续大约30分钟。
I will be there in a minute. = 我一会儿就到。


虚拟语气:
If I had a minute, I would call her. = 如果我有一分钟时间，我会给她打电话。
I wish I had a minute to rest. = 我真希望有一分钟时间休息一下。


被动语态:
The minutes of the meeting were recorded. = 会议记录被记录下来了。
The details were explained in minute detail. = 细节被详细解释了。


疑问句:
How many minutes are there in an hour? = 一小时有多少分钟？
Do you have a minute to talk? = 你有空闲时间谈谈吗？


否定句:
It didn't take a minute. = 这没花一分钟。
He doesn't have a minute to spare. = 他没有一点空闲时间。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "minute" comes from the Old French word "minue," meaning "small," which in turn derives from the Latin word "minutus," also meaning "small."  Its use as a unit of time comes from its being a "small" part of an hour.
