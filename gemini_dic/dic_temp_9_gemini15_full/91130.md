hybridism:/ˈhaɪbrɪdɪzəm/| n. |杂交；混合；混种现象 |The mixing of different things or characteristics to create something new.  It often refers to the crossbreeding of plants or animals of different varieties or species, resulting in offspring with mixed traits.  The term can also be used more broadly to describe the blending of different cultures, ideas, or styles.|例句：The study of plant hybridism has led to the development of many new and improved crop varieties. 植物杂交研究导致了许多新的和改良的作物品种的出现。

例句：The architect's design demonstrated a fascinating hybridism of modern and traditional styles. 建筑师的设计展现了现代和传统风格的迷人混合。


近义词：crossbreeding, hybridization, mixture, blending, intermingling |反义词：purity, homogeneity
