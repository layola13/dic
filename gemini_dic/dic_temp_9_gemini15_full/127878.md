neoconservative:/ˌniːoʊkənˈsɜːrvətɪv/| adj. n.| 新保守主义的；新保守主义者 |  A political ideology characterized by a strong belief in military strength, a largely unrestrained free market, and an emphasis on American exceptionalism.  Neoconservatives generally favor assertive foreign policy and a strong national defense.  They often advocate for the promotion of democracy and free markets globally. | Example:  The neoconservative commentators fiercely criticized the president's foreign policy. 新保守主义评论员强烈批评了总统的对外政策。 The rise of neoconservatism in the United States significantly influenced foreign policy decisions in the late 20th and early 21st centuries. 美国新保守主义的兴起极大地影响了 20 世纪末和 21 世纪初的对外政策决策。 | 近义词：conservative, right-wing | 反义词：liberal, leftist |  adj.
