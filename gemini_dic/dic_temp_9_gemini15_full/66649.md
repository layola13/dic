exit:/ˈɛɡzɪt/ | n., v. | 出口；离开；退出 |  A way out of a place; to go out of or leave a place; to stop being involved in something. | 例句：The exit is on the left.  出口在左边。

The meeting ended, and everyone made their exit.  会议结束了，每个人都离开了。

近义词：departure, egress, way out, leave, quit | 反义词：entrance, entry


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exits the building every day at 5 pm. = 他每天下午5点离开大楼。
The emergency exit is clearly marked. = 紧急出口标志清晰。


一般过去时:
She exited the theater quietly. = 她悄悄地离开了剧院。
The program exited unexpectedly. = 程序意外退出了。


现在完成时:
They have exited the competition. = 他们已经退出了比赛。
I have exited the game. = 我已经退出了游戏。


过去完成时:
He had exited the room before I arrived. = 我到达之前他已经离开了房间。
The system had exited due to an error. = 系统因错误而退出了。


将来时:
We will exit the highway at the next junction. = 我们将在下一个路口离开高速公路。
The train will exit the station shortly. = 火车不久将离开车站。


虚拟语气:
If I had known the situation, I would have exited earlier. = 如果我知道情况，我就会早点离开了。
I wish I had exited the deal before it went sour. = 我希望在交易变坏之前我就退出了。


被动语态:
The door was exited by the thief. = 门被小偷打开了。（离开）
The program is exited automatically after saving. = 保存后程序会自动退出。


疑问句:
Where is the nearest exit? = 最近的出口在哪里？
When does the train exit the station? = 火车什么时候离开车站？


否定句:
He did not exit the building. = 他没有离开大楼。
The software will not exit unless you save. = 除非你保存，否则软件不会退出。
