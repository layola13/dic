grandiloquent:/ɡrændˈɪləkwənt/ | adj. | 夸夸其谈的；言辞浮夸的；(风格)夸张华丽的 | Characterized by lofty and pompous language; bombastic.  It suggests an excessive use of impressive-sounding words, often to impress or conceal a lack of substance. | 例句：His grandiloquent speech was filled with impressive-sounding but ultimately meaningless phrases. 他那夸夸其谈的演讲充满了听起来很厉害但却毫无意义的词句。

例句：The politician's grandiloquent pronouncements failed to impress the skeptical audience.  这位政治家的夸夸其谈的声明未能打动怀疑的听众。


近义词：bombastic, pompous, inflated, high-flown, turgid, verbose


反义词：simple, modest, unpretentious, concise, plain


词源:  From  Middle French *grandiloquent, from Latin *grandiloquentia, from grandis "great" + loquentia "speech".
