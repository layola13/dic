referendum:/ˌrɛfəˈrɛndəm/| n. |全民公决；公投 | A referendum is a direct vote in which an entire electorate is invited to vote on a particular proposal and can be binding or non-binding.  A binding referendum requires the government to act according to the outcome, while a non-binding one serves as an indication of public opinion. | Example: The government held a referendum on the proposed new constitution.  政府就拟议的新宪法举行了公投。

The people voted in a referendum to change the law.  人民以公投的方式投票更改法律。|近义词：plebiscite, vote, ballot |反义词：N/A

词源:
Referendum comes from Latin *referendum, the neuter gerundive of referre ("to bring back, report").  The word entered English in the 19th century.
