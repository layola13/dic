nonfactually:/ˌnɒnˈfæktʃuəli/ | adv. |非事实地，不真实地 |  Describes something that is not based on facts or reality; untrue or inaccurate.  It emphasizes the lack of factual basis. | The article presented a nonfactually account of the events. 这篇文章对事件的描述不符合事实。  |近义词：inaccurately, unrealistically, falsely, incorrectly | 反义词：factually, truthfully, accurately


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and therefore doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  It modifies verbs, describing how an action is performed.  The tenses and voices of the verbs it modifies will determine the overall tense and voice of the sentence.
