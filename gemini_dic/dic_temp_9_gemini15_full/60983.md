elicits:/ɪˈlɪsɪts/| vt.|引出，诱出，探出|To evoke or draw out (a response, answer, or fact) from someone.|The teacher's question elicited a range of interesting answers from the students. 老师的问题从学生那里引出了各种有趣的答案。|The survey elicited a lot of useful information.  调查得出了许多有用的信息。|provoke, evoke, extract|suppress, conceal|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The interview elicits important details about the case. 采访引出了关于此案的重要细节。
His behavior elicits strong reactions from others. 他的行为引起了别人的强烈反应。

一般过去时:
The experiment elicited a surprising result. 实验得出了一个令人惊讶的结果。
Her speech elicited applause from the audience. 她的演讲赢得了观众的掌声。

现在完成时:
The new policy has elicited widespread criticism. 新政策引发了广泛的批评。
His work has elicited much praise from critics. 他的作品受到了评论家们的高度赞扬。

过去完成时:
The evidence had elicited a thorough investigation. 证据已经引发了一次彻底的调查。
The clues had elicited suspicion from the detective. 线索已引起侦探的怀疑。

将来时:
The upcoming election will elicit strong emotions from voters.  即将到来的选举将激起选民的强烈情绪。
The new product will elicit curiosity from consumers. 新产品将激起消费者的好奇心。

虚拟语气:
If the question elicited a different answer, we would have changed our strategy. 如果这个问题得到不同的答案，我们将改变策略。
I wish the interview had elicited more information about the suspect. 我希望采访能得到更多关于嫌疑人的信息。

被动语态:
Important information was elicited from the witness.  重要的信息是从证人那里获得的。
A confession was elicited under duress.  在压力下逼供出了一个认罪。

疑问句:
What kind of response does this question elicit?  这个问题会引发什么样的回应？
What evidence will elicit the truth? 什么证据能引出真相？

否定句:
The teacher's question failed to elicit a response. 老师的问题没有得到回应。
The advertisement did not elicit the expected sales. 广告没有产生预期的销售额。


PHRASE->
