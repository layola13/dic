propagandises:/ˈprɒpəɡændʌɪzɪz/| vt.|宣传；进行宣传|To spread ideas or beliefs, especially through the media, in order to influence people's opinions.|The government propagandises its policies through television and radio. 政府通过电视和广播宣传其政策。|近义词：promote, publicize, advertise|反义词：suppress, silence


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He propagandises his views on social media. 他在社交媒体上宣传他的观点。
The company propagandises its new product through various channels.  这家公司通过各种渠道宣传其新产品。


一般过去时:
They propagandised their ideology during the war. 他们在战争期间宣传他们的意识形态。
The dictator propagandised his regime's achievements relentlessly. 独裁者不遗余力地宣传其政权的成就。


现在完成时:
The party has propagandised its message effectively for decades. 这个党派几十年来有效地宣传了其信息。
They have propagandised against their political opponents. 他们一直在宣传反对他们的政治对手。


过去完成时:
Before the election, they had propagandised extensively in the rural areas.  选举前，他们在农村地区进行了广泛的宣传。
The group had propagandised their cause for many years before gaining recognition.  该组织在获得认可之前多年来一直在宣传他们的事业。


将来时:
They will propagandise their new policy aggressively next month.  他们下个月将积极宣传他们的新政策。
The government will propagandise the benefits of the new law. 政府将宣传新法律的好处。


虚拟语气:
If they propagandised more effectively, they would gain more support. 如果他们更有效地进行宣传，他们将获得更多支持。
I wish they hadn't propagandised so much misinformation. 我希望他们不要宣传那么多错误信息。


被动语态:

否定句:
