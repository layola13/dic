词源:
Inaffable comes from the Old French word "inaffable," meaning "unspeakable" or "inexpressible."  It's built from the prefix "in-" (not) and "affable," which itself derives from the Latin "affabilis" (easy to speak to, approachable).  Therefore, inaffable literally means "not easy to speak to."
