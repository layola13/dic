milords:/ˈmɪlɔːdz/ | n. |诸位老爷，各位勋爵 | A plural form of address used to refer to several lords, typically in a formal or archaic context.  It's a respectful and somewhat old-fashioned way of addressing a group of noblemen.  | Example: "Milords, I present to you the evidence."  诸位老爷，我向你们呈上证据。 |近义词：gentlemen (less formal), your honours (more formal)|反义词：无


USAGE->
This word is primarily a form of address and does not have typical verb conjugations like "what".  It's a noun used only in vocative case. Therefore, grammatical tenses do not apply.


PHRASE->
This word doesn't form common phrases in the same way as a word like "what".  Its usage is limited to formal address.
