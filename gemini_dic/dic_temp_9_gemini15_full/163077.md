precocity:/prɪˈkɒsəti/| n. |早熟；早慧；天赋；早产|The quality of being precocious; unusually advanced or mature in development, especially mental development.  It often refers to children exhibiting abilities or behaviors typically seen in older individuals. |例句：Her musical precocity was evident from a young age.  她的音乐天赋从小就显而易见。
例句：The child showed remarkable precocity in mathematics.  这个孩子在数学方面表现出了非凡的早慧。|近义词：precosity, maturity, giftedness|反义词：backwardness, retardation, immaturity

词源:
From Middle French *precocité, from Latin prōcōx, prōcocis ("early ripe," "precocious").  The Latin word is derived from prō- ("forward," "forth") + coquere ("to cook," "to ripen").  Therefore, the root meaning relates to something ripening or developing prematurely.
