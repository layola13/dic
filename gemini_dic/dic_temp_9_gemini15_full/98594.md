/ɪnˈstrʌkt/ | vt. |指示；命令；教导 |To give someone instructions or directions; to teach someone.  |The teacher instructed the students to write an essay.  老师指示学生们写一篇论文。| guide, direct, teach | disobey, ignore |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher instructs the students daily. = 老师每天都指导学生。
He instructs his team to finish the project on time. = 他指示他的团队按时完成项目。


一般过去时:
The captain instructed the crew to abandon ship. = 船长命令船员弃船。
She instructed me to turn left at the next corner. = 她指示我在下一个拐角处左转。


现在完成时:
The manager has instructed all staff to attend the meeting. = 经理已指示所有员工参加会议。
They have instructed me to prepare a presentation. = 他们已指示我准备一份演示文稿。


过去完成时:
He had instructed his assistant to prepare the documents before the meeting. = 他已指示他的助手在会议前准备好文件。
She had instructed them to wait for her call. = 她已指示他们等待她的电话。


将来时:
The professor will instruct the students on the new research method. = 教授将指导学生们学习新的研究方法。
They will instruct the workers on safety procedures. = 他们将指导工人进行安全程序操作。


虚拟语气:
If he had instructed them properly, the accident wouldn't have happened. = 如果他正确地指示他们，事故就不会发生了。
I wish he had instructed me more clearly. = 我希望他指示得更清楚些。


被动语态:
The students were instructed to complete the assignment by Friday. = 学生们被指示在星期五之前完成作业。
The workers were instructed on the new safety regulations. = 工人们接受了关于新的安全规章的指导。


疑问句:
Were you instructed to do that? = 你是被指示那样做的吗？
How were the students instructed to solve the problem? = 学生们是如何被指示解决这个问题的？


否定句:
He did not instruct them to leave. = 他没有指示他们离开。
She didn't instruct me to do anything. = 她没有指示我做任何事情。


PHRASE->
instruct someone to do something = 指示某人做某事
instruct someone in something = 在某方面指导某人
instruct someone on something = 就某事指导某人
clearly instruct = 清晰地指示
follow instructions = 遵循指示
