histamines: /ˈhɪstəmiːnz/ | n. | 组胺 | Histamines are organic nitrogenous compounds that are produced by the body in response to injury and allergic reactions. They are involved in a wide range of physiological processes, including inflammation, gastric acid secretion, and neurotransmission.  | Example:  The release of histamines causes the symptoms of an allergic reaction, such as sneezing and itching. 组胺的释放会导致过敏反应的症状，例如打喷嚏和瘙痒。


近义词:  (No perfect synonyms, as the effect is specific)  allergens (as they trigger histamine release)

反义词: (No direct antonyms, but antagonists can block histamine effects)  antihistamines


词性: 名词
