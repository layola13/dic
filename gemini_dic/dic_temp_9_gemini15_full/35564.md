coalitionism: /ˌkoʊəˈlɪʃənɪzəm/ | n. | 联合政府主义；联盟政治 | The belief in or practice of forming coalitions, especially in government.  It often implies a preference for governing through alliances rather than single-party rule.  | Example: The rise of coalitionism in the country's politics has led to more compromise and less ideological rigidity.  | 近义词：coalition politics, alliance politics | 反义词：single-party rule, authoritarianism |
