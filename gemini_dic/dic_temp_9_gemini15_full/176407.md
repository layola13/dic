re-honors:/ˌriːˈɒnərz/| v.|重新授予荣誉，再次表彰|To bestow honors again upon someone or something; to reinstate honors previously given.|例句：The university re-honored the alumnus for his outstanding contributions.  大学再次表彰这位校友为其杰出贡献。|例句：After a thorough review, the committee decided to re-honor the retired professor with a lifetime achievement award. 在彻底审查后，委员会决定再次授予退休教授终身成就奖。|近义词：re-award, reinstate, re-recognize|反义词：dishonor, disgrace, revoke


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They re-honor distinguished alumni annually. = 他们每年都重新表彰杰出的校友。
The company re-honors its top performers with bonuses. = 公司通过奖金再次表彰其表现最佳的员工。

现在完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:
