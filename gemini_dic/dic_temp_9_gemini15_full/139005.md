nymphs:/ˈnɪmfz/| n.|宁芙，仙女；少女|A nymph is a mythical being, typically a beautiful maiden inhabiting rivers, woods, or other locations in nature.  They are often associated with fertility and nature's bounty.  In some contexts, the term can also refer to the immature form of an insect. |例句：The poet wrote of nymphs dancing in the moonlight.  诗人写到月光下跳舞的仙女们。 The dragonfly nymph lived underwater. 蜻蜓若虫生活在水下。|近义词：fairy, sprite, naiad (water nymph)|反义词：None specifically, but "hag" or "crone" could be considered antonyms in a mythological context.
