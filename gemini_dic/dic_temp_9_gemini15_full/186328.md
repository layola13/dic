rhopalocera:/ˌroʊpəˈlɒsərə/| n. |锤角亚目昆虫(蝴蝶)|Butterflies, belonging to the order Lepidoptera, are characterized by their club-shaped antennae ("rhopala" meaning club and "cera" meaning horn). They undergo complete metamorphosis, transforming from egg to larva (caterpillar), pupa (chrysalis), and finally adult butterfly.  Rhopalocera is a taxonomic rank, specifically a suborder, used in older classifications. More recent classifications often treat butterflies as a superfamily (Papilionoidea) within the order Lepidoptera.|The rhopalocera are a fascinating group of insects, exhibiting a wide array of colours and patterns. |近义词：butterflies (informal)|反义词：none (it's a taxonomic group)|名词
