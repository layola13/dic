conjecturable: /kənˈdʒɛktʃərəbl/ | adj. | 可推测的，可猜测的 |  capable of being conjectured or guessed; possible to be inferred or deduced.  | The cause of the accident is conjecturable, but not definitively known. 事故的原因是可以推测的，但不是确切已知的。|  inferable, guessable, surmisable | certain, definite, proven |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The origin of the universe is conjecturable. 宇宙的起源是可以推测的。
This problem is conjecturable with further research. 通过进一步的研究，这个问题是可以推测的。


一般过去时:
The thief's motive was conjecturable from the evidence. 从证据来看，小偷的动机是可以推测的。
His next move was conjecturable based on his past behavior. 根据他过去的行為，他的下一步行动是可以推测的。


现在完成时:
The solution to the puzzle has been conjecturable for many years.  这个谜题的答案多年来一直是可以推测的。
Many theories have been conjecturable but none proven. 许多理论是可以推测的，但没有一个被证实。


过去完成时:
Before the discovery, the dinosaur's diet had been conjecturable. 在发现之前，恐龙的饮食是可以推测的。
The cause of the fire had been conjecturable based on the initial investigation.  根据初步调查，火灾的原因是可以推测的。


将来时:
The outcome of the election will be conjecturable until the votes are counted. 在计票之前，选举的结果是可以推测的。
The effects of climate change will be increasingly conjecturable in the coming decades. 在未来的几十年里，气候变化的影响将越来越容易推测。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, the culprit would be more easily conjecturable. 如果证据更充分，罪犯就更容易被推测出来。
If we had more information, the answer would be conjecturable. 如果我们有更多信息，答案是可以推测的。


被动语态:
The age of the artifact was conjecturable by experts. 文物的年代被专家推测出来。
The results of the experiment were conjecturable from the data. 实验的结果可以从数据中推测出来。


疑问句:
Is the suspect's whereabouts conjecturable? 嫌疑人的下落是可以推测的吗？
Is the future of this company conjecturable? 这家公司的未来是可以推测的吗？


否定句:
The murderer's identity was not conjecturable at that time. 当时凶手的身份是无法推测的。
The true meaning of the inscription was not easily conjecturable.  铭文的真正含义不容易推测。



PHRASE->
highly conjecturable:  极易推测的
somewhat conjecturable:  有点儿可推测的
reasonably conjecturable:  相当可以推测的
barely conjecturable:  几乎无法推测的
not conjecturable:  不可推测的


词源:
From Latin *conjectūra ‘a throwing together’, from conicere ‘to throw together’, from com- ‘together’ + jacere ‘to throw’.  The word ultimately relates to the idea of forming a guess or opinion by piecing together available information.
