disorientations:/ˌdɪsˌɔːriənˈteɪʃənz/ | n. |迷失方向；迷茫；错乱 |The state of being disoriented; a loss of bearings or sense of direction, often accompanied by confusion and disorganization.  It can also refer to a more general sense of confusion or disorganization. |例句：The sudden darkness caused a feeling of disorientation. 突然的黑暗导致了一种迷失方向的感觉。

The confusing information led to widespread disorientations among the voters.  令人困惑的信息导致选民普遍感到迷茫。|近义词：confusion, bewilderment, disorganization |反义词：orientation, clarity, organization
