jeopard:/ˈdʒɛpərd/| vt.|危及；使陷于危险|to put something or someone into a situation in which there is a risk of being harmed, damaged, or destroyed. |例句：Don't jeopard your health by smoking.  不要吸烟危害你的健康。|例句：The company's reputation was jeopardized by the scandal. 公司的声誉因丑闻而受损。|近义词：endanger, imperil, risk, threaten|反义词：protect, safeguard, secure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jeopardizes his career by being late to work every day. = 他每天上班迟到，危及自己的事业。
The new policy jeopardizes the company's future. = 新政策危及公司的未来。


一般过去时:
The reckless driver jeopardized the safety of others. = 鲁莽的司机危及了其他人的安全。
His careless actions jeopardized the entire project. = 他粗心大意的行为危及了整个项目。


现在完成时:
They have jeopardized their relationship by constant arguing. = 他们因为不断争吵而危及了彼此的关系。
The government has jeopardized public trust through its inaction. = 政府的不作为危及了公众的信任。


过去完成时:
He had jeopardized his chances of promotion by missing the deadline. = 他因为错过了截止日期而危及了他的晋升机会。
She had jeopardized her health by neglecting her diet. = 她因为忽视饮食而危及了她的健康。


将来时:
The upcoming storm will jeopardize the harvest. = 即将来临的风暴将危及收成。
His continued irresponsible behavior will jeopardize his future. = 他持续不负责任的行为将危及他的未来。


虚拟语气:
If he continued to gamble, he would jeopardize his family's financial security. = 如果他继续赌博，他会危及他家人的经济安全。
If she had been more careful, she wouldn't have jeopardized the experiment. = 如果她更小心一点，她就不会危及实验了。


被动语态:
The project was jeopardized by unforeseen circumstances. = 该项目因不可预见的情况而受损。
His reputation was jeopardized by the false accusations. = 他的声誉因虚假指控而受损。


疑问句:
Does this decision jeopardize our chances of success? = 这个决定是否会危及我们成功的几率？
Will the new regulations jeopardize small businesses? = 新规会不会危及小企业？


否定句:
The company took steps to ensure the project was not jeopardized. = 公司采取措施确保项目不受损害。
He tried to avoid actions that would jeopardize his position. = 他试图避免任何会危及他地位的行为。
