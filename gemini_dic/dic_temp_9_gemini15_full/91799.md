hyperfederalist's: /ˌhaɪpərˈfɛdərəlɪsts/ | n. | 超联邦主义者的 |  A hyperfederalist is an advocate for a system of government where regional or local governments hold significantly more power than the central or national government.  This is a more extreme form of federalism, pushing the balance of power heavily towards decentralization.  The term is relatively uncommon and often used in academic or political science contexts to describe a specific viewpoint on federal governance. |  The hyperfederalist's arguments centered on the need for greater autonomy for individual states. | 近义词: decentralist, states' rights advocate | 反义词: centralist, nationalist, unitarist |  名词
