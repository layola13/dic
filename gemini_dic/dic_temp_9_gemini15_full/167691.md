prided:/praɪdɪd/| v. |以…自豪，为…感到骄傲|  The past tense and past participle of "pride." It describes a state of feeling proud or having a sense of satisfaction or self-respect because of something one has done, achieved, or possesses. |例句：He prided himself on his honesty. 他以他的诚实为傲。

例句：She prided herself on her cooking skills. 她为自己的烹饪技巧感到骄傲。


近义词：boasted, gloried, vaunted | 反义词：ashamed, humbled, disgraced | 词性：动词


USAGE->

He prides himself on his work. = 他为自己的工作感到骄傲。
She prides herself on her intelligence. = 她为自己的聪明才智感到骄傲。


一般过去时:
He prided himself on his achievements. = 他为自己的成就感到骄傲。
She prided herself on her independence. = 她为自己的独立性感到骄傲。


现在完成时:
He has prided himself on his accomplishments throughout his career. = 他在整个职业生涯中都为自己的成就感到骄傲。
She has prided herself on maintaining a healthy lifestyle. = 她一直为保持健康的生活方式而自豪。


过去完成时:
He had prided himself on his quick wit before the unfortunate incident. = 在不幸事件发生之前，他为自己的机智感到骄傲。
She had prided herself on her ability to resolve conflicts peacefully. = 她曾为她和平解决冲突的能力感到自豪。


将来时:

She will pride herself on her resilience in the face of adversity. = 她将为自己在逆境中的韧性感到自豪。


虚拟语气:
If he were a better person, he would pride himself on his kindness. = 如果他是一个更好的人，他会为自己的善良而自豪。
If she had worked harder, she would have prided herself on her accomplishments. = 如果她工作更努力，她就会为自己的成就感到自豪。


被动语态:

否定句:
