impoverishing: /ɪmˈpɑːvərɪʃɪŋ/ | v. | 使贫困；使贫瘠；使枯竭 |  Making or becoming poor; depleting or ruining. The process or act of making someone or something poor or barren.  |  例句：The war is impoverishing the nation.  战争正在使这个国家贫困。

例句：Years of drought have impoverished the land.  多年的干旱使这片土地贫瘠。


近义词：ruining, depleting, bankrupting, destituting


反义词：enriching, enriching, prospering


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government's policies are impoverishing many families. = 政府的政策使许多家庭贫困。
This farming practice is impoverishing the soil. = 这种耕作方式正在使土壤贫瘠。


一般过去时:
The economic crisis impoverished many people. = 经济危机使许多人贫困。
The long war impoverished the entire region. = 长期的战争使整个地区贫困。


现在完成时:
Years of mismanagement have impoverished the company. = 多年的管理不善使公司陷入了困境。
The drought has impoverished the farmland. = 干旱使农田贫瘠。


过去完成时:
The previous regime had impoverished the country before the revolution. = 在革命之前，之前的政权已经使国家贫困。
Years of bad harvests had impoverished the farmers. = 多年的歉收使农民贫困。


将来时:
Without intervention, the ongoing conflict will impoverish the entire nation. = 如果不干预，持续的冲突将使整个国家贫困。
Uncontrolled deforestation will impoverish the soil and lead to desertification. = 不受控制的森林砍伐将使土壤贫瘠并导致沙漠化。


虚拟语气:
If we continue down this path, we will impoverish future generations. = 如果我们继续沿着这条路走下去，我们将使子孙后代贫困。
I wish the government hadn't implemented policies that impoverished so many. = 我真希望政府没有实施那些使很多人贫困的政策。


被动语态:
Many communities were impoverished by the natural disaster. = 许多社区因自然灾害而贫困。
The land was impoverished by years of intensive farming. = 这片土地因多年的集约化农业而贫瘠。


疑问句:
Is this economic system impoverishing the working class? = 这种经济制度是否正在使工人阶级贫困？
Are these agricultural practices impoverishing the land? = 这些农业实践是否正在使土地贫瘠？


否定句:
The new policies are not impoverishing the population. = 新的政策并没有使人民贫困。
Sustainable farming practices do not impoverish the soil. = 可持续的农业实践不会使土壤贫瘠。

词源:
Comes from the verb "impoverish," formed from the prefix "im-" (meaning "in" or "into") and the word "poverty."  The word ultimately derives from Old French "pauvrete" (poverty) and Latin "pauper" (poor).
