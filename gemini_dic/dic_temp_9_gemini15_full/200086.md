skewing:/ˈskjuːɪŋ/| v.|歪斜；曲解；偏向|To cause something to be no longer straight or aligned; to distort or misrepresent something.|The strong wind skewed the results of the experiment. 强风使实验结果失真。 The media skewed the facts to support their agenda. 媒体歪曲事实以支持他们的议程。|distorting, twisting, warping|straightening, aligning, rectifying|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The strong wind skews the data. 强风使数据失真。
He always skews the facts to his advantage. 他总是歪曲事实以利于自己。


一般过去时:
The accident skewed the traffic flow. 事故使交通流量混乱。
The painter deliberately skewed the perspective in his artwork. 画家故意在他的作品中歪曲了透视。


现在完成时:
Years of bias have skewed the public's perception of the issue. 多年的偏见扭曲了公众对这个问题的认知。
The media coverage has skewed the public's understanding of the situation. 媒体报道歪曲了公众对局势的理解。


过去完成时:
The faulty instrument had skewed all the previous readings. 有故障的仪器歪曲了之前的所有读数。
His prejudiced upbringing had skewed his judgment of others. 他偏见的教养歪曲了他对别人的判断。


将来时:
The upcoming election will likely skew the political landscape.  即将到来的选举可能会歪曲政治格局。
The new policy may skew the market equilibrium. 新政策可能会扭曲市场均衡。


虚拟语气:
If the data hadn't been skewed, the conclusion would have been different. 如果数据没有被歪曲，结论就会不同。
I wish the reporting hadn't skewed the public's opinion. 我希望报道没有歪曲公众的舆论。


被动语态:
The results were skewed by human error. 结果因人为错误而失真。
The survey was skewed by a biased sample. 调查由于样本偏差而失真。


疑问句:
Was the experiment skewed by outside factors? 实验是否受到外部因素的影响而失真？
Is the media intentionally skewing the information? 媒体是否故意歪曲信息？


否定句:
The results were not skewed by the experimental design. 结果未因实验设计而失真。
The evidence did not skew our judgment. 证据并没有歪曲我们的判断。
