diptych: /ˈdɪptɪk/ | n. |双联画；双幅画 | A painting, carving, or other work of art divided into two hinged sections, typically depicting the same subject or a continuous narrative.  | Example: The museum displayed a stunning diptych depicting scenes from the artist's childhood.  博物馆展出了一幅令人惊叹的双联画，描绘了艺术家童年时期的场景。 | 近义词: double panel, bifold | 反义词:monolith, single panel

词源:

The word "diptych" comes from Late Latin *diptychon, from Greek díptychon, literally meaning "two-folded."  The Greek word is a compound of di- ("two") and ptyx ("fold").
