hemitrope:/ˈhɛmɪtroʊp/| n. |半晶质体，半晶体|A hemitrope is a crystal that is twinned, meaning it consists of two or more individual crystals that are intergrown in a symmetrical way.  These crystals are mirror images of each other across a plane called the twin plane.  The twinning often results in unusual shapes and properties. |Example: The mineral calcite frequently forms hemitropes, exhibiting a characteristic "butterfly" or "swallowtail" shape.|近义词：twin crystal|反义词：无
