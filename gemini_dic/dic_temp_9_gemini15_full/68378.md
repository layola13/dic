falloff:/ˈfɔːlɒf/ /ˈfɔːlɔːf/| n. v.|衰减；下降；减少；（数量或质量的）减弱；逐渐减少|The gradual decline or reduction in something.  It can refer to a decrease in intensity, quantity, or quality.|例句：The falloff in sales was unexpected. 销售额的下降是出乎意料的。

The signal falloff was significant at that distance.  在那个距离上，信号衰减很显著。

近义词：decrease, decline, reduction, drop-off, diminution|反义词：increase, rise, growth, escalation

词源:
The word "falloff" is a compound word formed from the verb "fall" and the suffix "-off."  "Fall" originates from Old English *feallan, meaning "to fall." The "-off" suffix indicates a separation, detachment, or decline.  Therefore, "falloff" literally means "a falling off" or "a decline."
