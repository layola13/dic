enmeshing:/ɪnˈmɛʃɪŋ/| v. |使纠缠；使陷入；使卷入|The act of causing someone or something to become entangled or involved in a difficult or complicated situation.  It often implies a sense of being trapped or unable to escape easily.|例句：The scandal enmeshed the politician in a web of lies and deceit.  丑闻使这位政客陷入谎言和欺骗的泥潭中。

例句：Their lives became enmeshed with the lives of their neighbours. 他们的生活和邻居的生活交织在一起。

近义词：entangle, involve, implicate, ensnare|反义词：disentangle, extricate, liberate

虚拟语气:

词源:
The word "enmesh" comes from the Old English words "in" (meaning "in") and "masche" (meaning "mesh," as in a net).  Over time, the word evolved to describe the action of becoming caught or entangled, not just physically but also figuratively in complex situations.
