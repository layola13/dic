prequotation:/ˌpriːkwəˈteɪʃən/ | n. |预报价，预先报价 | A prequotation is a preliminary estimate of the cost of goods or services, provided before a formal quote is given.  It's often less detailed than a formal quote. | Example:  The supplier provided a prequotation for the project, allowing us to budget accordingly. = 供应商为该项目提供了预报价，使我们能够相应地进行预算。


The prequotation was significantly lower than the final quote. = 预报价远低于最终报价。


近义词：preliminary estimate, initial estimate, provisional quote | 反义词：final quote, confirmed quote
