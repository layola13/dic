overevaluations: /ˌoʊvərɪˌvæljuˈeɪʃənz/ | n. | 高估；估价过高 | The overevaluations of the assets led to significant losses.  资产估价过高导致了巨大的损失。 |近义词：overestimations, inflated valuations | 反义词：undervalutations, underestimations


英文解析:The act of placing too high a value on something; an instance of this.  It implies a misjudgment of worth or value, often leading to negative consequences.


例句:

The company's overevaluations of its inventory resulted in a large write-down. 公司对其库存的估价过高导致了大量的减记。

His overevaluations of his abilities led to his downfall. 他对自身能力的过高估计导致了他的失败。


词源:

* **over-**:  prefix indicating excess or above
* **evaluation**:  the act of determining or setting the value of something.  Derived from the verb "evaluate," which ultimately traces back to Latin roots ("valere," meaning "to be strong" or "to be worth").


用法说明:

overevaluations 通常作名词使用，表示对某事物价值的过高估计。  它可以指具体的财务估值，也可以指更广泛的判断上的错误。


PHRASE->
