fibr:/ˈfaɪbər/ | n. |纤维，纤维状物 | A fiber is a threadlike strand of a natural or synthetic substance, especially one that can be spun into yarn.  Fibers are often used in the manufacture of textiles, but also have applications in various other industries. | Examples: Cotton fibers are used to make clothing.  The carpet is made of synthetic fibers. |近义词: filament, thread, strand |反义词:  (无明确反义词，可考虑与“整体”相对) | 名词
