sellouts: /ˈsɛlˌaʊts/ | n. | 叛徒；背叛者；出卖原则的人 |  A person who betrays their principles or their group, especially for personal gain.  This term often carries a strong negative connotation, implying a lack of integrity and a willingness to compromise one's values for money, fame, or other benefits.  It can be applied to individuals or groups. |

例句：

He's considered a sellout by his former colleagues for working with the competition. = 他因为与竞争对手合作而被以前的同事视为叛徒。

Many fans felt betrayed when their favorite band released a pop album, calling them sellouts. = 当他们最喜欢的乐队发行了一张流行专辑时，许多歌迷感到被背叛了，称他们为叛徒。


近义词：traitor, betrayer, turncoat, quisling


反义词：loyal, true, faithful, steadfast


词性：名词
