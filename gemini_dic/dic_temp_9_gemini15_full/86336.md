heckle:/ˈhɛkl/ | vt. | 诘问；喝倒彩；起哄 | To interrupt a public speaker with questions, challenges, or abuse.  | The hecklers shouted insults at the politician during his speech.  演讲期间，起哄的人向这位政治家大声喊叫侮辱性的话。| interrupt, badger, jeer | praise, applaud | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He heckles the speaker every time he speaks. = 他每次演讲都会对他进行诘问。
They often heckle the comedian during his act. = 他们在喜剧演员表演期间经常对他喝倒彩。

一般过去时:
The audience heckled the performer relentlessly. = 观众无情地对表演者喝倒彩。
She heckled the politician with difficult questions. = 她用难题诘问这位政治家。

现在完成时:
The protestors have heckled the mayor at every public appearance. = 抗议者在市长每次公开露面时都对他进行过诘问。
He has heckled speakers at many rallies. = 他在许多集会上都对演讲者进行过喝倒彩。

The crowd had already heckled him several times before he even began his speech. = 在他开始演讲之前，人群已经多次对他进行过喝倒彩。
