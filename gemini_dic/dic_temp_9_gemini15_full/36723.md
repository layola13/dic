collocations:/ˌkɒləˈkeɪʃənz/| n. |搭配词，词语搭配 |Words that are often used together.  Collocations are important for sounding natural in a language. They show how words are typically used together in different contexts.  Understanding collocations can significantly improve your fluency and accuracy. |例句：The words "strong" and "coffee" are a common collocation.  强咖啡是一个常见的搭配词。

|例句：Learning common collocations is crucial for improving your English writing. 学习常见的搭配词对于提高你的英语写作至关重要。|近义词：word combinations, phrases, idioms (depending on the context) |反义词：None (the antonym would depend on the specific collocation being discussed)

词源:
The word "collocation" comes from the Latin prefix "col-" (together) and the verb "locare" (to place).  Therefore, it literally means "placed together."  The linguistic meaning evolved to describe the tendency of certain words to occur together frequently.
