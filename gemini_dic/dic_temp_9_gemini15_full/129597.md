non-compete:/ˌnɒn kəmˈpiːt/| n. |竞业禁止协议；竞业禁止条款| A non-compete agreement is a legal contract between an employer and employee (or other party) that restricts the employee from working for a competitor or starting a competing business for a certain period of time after the employment relationship ends.  It aims to protect the employer's trade secrets, confidential information, and customer relationships.| Example:  The company required all employees to sign a non-compete agreement upon hiring.  这家公司要求所有员工在雇佣时签署竞业禁止协议。


| Example: He was bound by a non-compete clause in his employment contract. 他受雇佣合同中竞业禁止条款的约束。|近义词：restrictive covenant, confidentiality agreement |反义词：non-disclosure agreement (in some contexts)|名词
