collocating:/ˈkɒləkeɪtɪŋ/ | v. |搭配；共现|The act of words appearing together frequently in a language.  It refers to the tendency of certain words to occur together more often than would be expected by chance. This is a crucial aspect of language learning and understanding natural language processing.  |Example:  The words "heavy rain" frequently collocates; you rarely hear "weighty rain".  搭配词语“heavy rain”（大雨）经常出现；你很少听到“weighty rain”（沉重的雨）|近义词：combining, associating|反义词：dissociating, separating|动词
