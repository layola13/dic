forwhy:/fɔːrwaɪ/| adv. |为什么；为了什么 |  This word is archaic and rarely used in modern English. It functions as an adverbial phrase, essentially meaning "for what reason" or "why."  It's a combination of "for" and "why," emphasizing the reason behind an action or event.  | He asked forwhy they had left. = 他问他们为什么离开。  They wondered forwhy the decision was made. = 他们想知道为什么做出这个决定。 | why, wherefore |  no direct antonym, as it expresses a question | adverb


USAGE->
This word is archaic and doesn't have common usage across different grammatical tenses.  The modern equivalent "why" is used instead.  Examples below illustrate how the equivalent "why" would be used:


一般现在时:
Why are you here? = 你为什么在这里？
Why does she like that? = 她为什么喜欢那个？


一般过去时:
Why did he go? = 他为什么走了？
Why did it happen? = 为什么会发生？


现在完成时:
Why have you done that? = 你为什么那样做？
Why has he left? = 他为什么离开了？


过去完成时:
Why had they left? = 他们为什么离开了？
Why had she screamed? = 她为什么尖叫了？


将来时:
Why will you go? = 你为什么要去？
Why will they come? = 他们为什么来？


虚拟语气:
I don't know why he would do that. = 我不知道他为什么那样做。
If I knew why, I would tell you. = 如果我知道为什么，我会告诉你的。


被动语态:
Why was it done? = 为什么这样做？
Why was the meeting cancelled? = 为什么会议取消了？


疑问句:
Why is this happening? = 为什么会发生这种情况？
Why are you crying? = 你为什么哭？


否定句:
I don't know why he did it. = 我不知道他为什么那样做。
She didn't say why she was late. = 她没说她为什么迟到。


PHRASE->
The word "forwhy" itself doesn't form common phrases.  However, phrases using "why" are numerous:

Why not? = 为什么不？
Why bother? = 何必麻烦？
Why so serious? = 为什么这么严肃？
Why me? = 为什么是我？
For what reason? = 为什么？ (a direct modern equivalent to "forwhy")
