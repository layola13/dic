persuasiveness:/pərˈsweɪsɪvnəs/ | n. | 说服力；劝说力 | The ability to persuade or convince someone to do or believe something.  It involves the skillful use of language, logic, and emotional appeal to influence someone's attitudes, beliefs, or actions.  |  例句：The persuasiveness of his argument was undeniable.  他的论点令人信服。

The persuasiveness of her presentation secured the deal. 她演讲的说服力确保了这笔交易。 | 近义词：influence, power to persuade, conviction, effectiveness | 反义词：ineffectiveness, unpersuasiveness, weakness


WORD ORIGIN->
The word "persuasiveness" is derived from the verb "persuade," which comes from the Late Latin word "persuadere," meaning "to make someone believe something."  "Persuadere" itself is a compound of "per-" (completely, thoroughly) and "suadere" (to advise, urge).  The suffix "-ness" is added to form the noun, indicating the quality or state of being persuasive.
