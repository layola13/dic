rehearses:/rɪˈhɜːrsɪz/| v.|排练，预演，复述|To practice or rehearse something, typically a play, a piece of music, or a speech, in preparation for a public performance.|例句：The actors rehearse the play every evening.  演员们每天晚上排练戏剧。|例句：She rehearsed her speech several times before the presentation. 她在演讲前排练了好几次。|近义词：practice, prepare, review|反义词：improvise, wing it

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rehearses his lines every day. = 他每天排练他的台词。
The band rehearses in the garage. = 乐队在车库排练。

一般过去时:
They rehearsed the scene until it was perfect. = 他们排练了这个场景直到完美为止。
She rehearsed her speech the night before. = 她在前一天晚上排练了她的演讲。

现在完成时:
The actors have rehearsed for weeks. = 演员们已经排练了好几个星期了。
We have rehearsed this song many times. = 我们这首歌排练过很多次了。

过去完成时:
They had rehearsed the entire play before the opening night. = 在首映夜之前，他们已经排练了整部戏。
She had rehearsed her lines so many times that she knew them by heart. = 她排练了那么多遍台词，以至于她已经烂熟于心了。

将来时:
We will rehearse the play next week. = 我们下周将排练这部戏。
They will rehearse the music before the concert. = 他们将在音乐会前排练音乐。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would rehearse more often. = 如果我有更多时间，我会更经常排练。
I wish I had rehearsed my presentation better. = 我希望我更好地排练了我的演示文稿。


被动语态:
The play was rehearsed meticulously. = 这出戏经过了细致的排练。
The song was rehearsed many times before the recording. = 这首歌在录音之前排练了很多次。


疑问句:
Have you rehearsed your part? = 你排练过你的角色了吗？
When will they rehearse again? = 他们什么时候再排练？


否定句:
They didn't rehearse the dance routine properly. = 他们没有好好排练舞蹈套路。
He hadn't rehearsed his lines, so he was nervous. = 他没有排练台词，所以他很紧张。


PHRASE->
rehearse a speech = 排练演讲
rehearse a play = 排练戏剧
rehearse a scene = 排练一场戏
rehearse a song = 排练一首歌曲
rehearse for a concert = 为音乐会排练
