illuminating: /ɪˈluːmɪneɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 启迪的；阐明的；令人印象深刻的 |  Giving light; enlightening; making something clear.  The explanation was illuminating, finally making the complex concept understandable. |  enlightening, clarifying, revealing, insightful |  obscure, confusing, unclear, perplexing | Adjective


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun illuminates the garden. = 太阳照亮了花园。
This example illuminates the problem. = 这个例子阐明了这个问题。


一般过去时:
The lecture illuminated the topic. = 讲座阐明了这个话题。
The artist illuminated the manuscript. = 艺术家为手稿绘制插图。


现在完成时:
His research has illuminated many aspects of the disease. = 他的研究阐明了该疾病的许多方面。
Recent studies have illuminated the causes of climate change. = 最近的研究阐明了气候变化的原因。


过去完成时:
The discovery had illuminated the mystery. = 这一发现阐明了这个谜团。
By then, the evidence had illuminated the truth. = 到那时，证据已经揭示了真相。


将来时:
This new data will illuminate the situation. = 这些新的数据将阐明情况。
Future research will illuminate the long-term effects. = 未来的研究将阐明长期影响。


虚拟语气:
If only the report illuminated the situation more clearly. = 但愿报告能更清楚地阐明情况。
I wish this article illuminated the subject better. = 我希望这篇文章能更好地阐明主题。


被动语态:
The painting was illuminated by a single spotlight. = 这幅画被一盏聚光灯照亮。
The mystery was illuminated by a new piece of evidence. = 这个谜团被新的证据阐明。


疑问句:

否定句:

The findings did not illuminate the underlying cause. = 研究结果并没有阐明根本原因。
