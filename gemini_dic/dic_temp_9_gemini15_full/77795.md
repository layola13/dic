generalizability: /ˌdʒɛnərəlˈaɪzəbɪləti/ | n. | 可推广性；普遍适用性 | The extent to which findings from a study can be generalized to other populations or settings.  It refers to the ability of a model, theory, or research finding to be applied to a wider range of situations or contexts than those in which it was originally developed.  | Example: The study's high generalizability suggests that its conclusions are applicable to a broader population.  这项研究的高可推广性表明其结论适用于更广泛的人群。|近义词：applicability, transferability |反义词：specificity, limitation |名词
