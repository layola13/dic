restrainment: /rɪˈstreɪntm(ə)nt/ | n. |克制；抑制；约束；限制 | The act of restraining or the state of being restrained.  It implies a holding back or controlling of impulses, emotions, actions, or forces. |例句：He showed remarkable restraint in the face of provocation. 他面对挑衅表现出非凡的克制力。


例句：The government imposed strict restraints on imports. 政府对进口商品实施了严格的限制。


近义词：control, self-control, constraint, limitation, restriction


反义词：abandon, indulgence, freedom, license


词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Restraint is important in many situations. = 克制在许多情况下都很重要。
His restraint prevents him from making rash decisions. = 他的克制阻止了他做出轻率的决定。


一般过去时:
Her restraint surprised everyone. = 她的克制让每个人都感到惊讶。
The police used restraint in handling the situation. = 警方在处理此事时表现克制。


现在完成时:
His years of self-restraint have paid off. = 他多年的自我克制终于得到了回报。
The company has shown considerable restraint in its spending. = 公司在支出方面表现出了相当的克制。


过去完成时:
They had exercised restraint until the last moment. = 他们直到最后一刻才克制住自己。
The situation had required a great deal of restraint from everyone involved. = 这种情况要求所有参与者都表现出极大的克制。


将来时:
More restraint will be needed to avoid conflict. = 将需要更多的克制来避免冲突。
We will exercise restraint in our response. = 我们将在回应中保持克制。


虚拟语气:
If he had shown more restraint, the argument wouldn't have happened. = 如果他表现出更多的克制，就不会发生争吵了。
I wish she had shown more restraint in her criticism. = 我希望她在批评中能更加克制。


被动语态:

His emotions were kept in check by self-restraint. = 他的情绪通过自我克制得到控制。


疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
restrain (v.) + -ment (n. suffix)  The verb "restrain" comes from Old French "restreindre," ultimately from Latin "restringere," meaning "to bind back, tighten."
