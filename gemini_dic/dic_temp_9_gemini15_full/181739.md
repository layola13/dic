relict:/ˈrɛlɪkt/| n. adj.|残余；遗迹；残留物；（动植物）孑遗；|A relict is something that has survived from a previous time or state, often something that is now rare or endangered.  It can refer to a surviving organism from an earlier geological period, a cultural practice that has persisted, or a physical object from the past.|例句：The ancient ruins are a relict of a once-great civilization.  这些古老的遗址是曾经伟大文明的遗迹。

The rare plant species is a relict from the Ice Age.  这种稀有的植物物种是冰河时代的孑遗。|近义词：remnant, vestige, survival|反义词：new, modern, recent

That ancient custom is a relict of their tribal history. = 那个古老的习俗是他们部落历史的残余。

现在完成时:

将来时:

Future generations will study these artifacts as relics of our time. = 后代将研究这些文物，作为我们时代的遗迹。
