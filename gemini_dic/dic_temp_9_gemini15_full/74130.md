found:/faʊnd/| /faʊnd/ | vt. | 发现；建立；创立；查明；基于；成立；找到；发现；认为；被发现 | To discover something or someone unexpectedly or after searching. To establish or create something, such as an organization or institution. To base something on a particular thing. |例句：The police found the missing child. 警方找到了失踪的孩子。
They found a new way to solve the problem.他们找到了一种解决问题的新方法。


例句：The archaeologists found ancient artifacts. 考古学家发现了古代文物。
They found the evidence convincing. 他们认为证据令人信服。

近义词：discover, establish, create, locate, determine, base

反义词：lose, miss, destroy, demolish

词源:
The word "found" originates from the Old English word "fundian," meaning "to provide, furnish, supply."  Over time, its meaning evolved to encompass the meanings of discovering something and establishing something.  The modern verb "found" retains both of these core senses.
