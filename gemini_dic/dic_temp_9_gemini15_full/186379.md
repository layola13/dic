rhythmometer:/rɪðˈmɒmɪtər/ | n. | 节奏计，韵律测量器 | A rhythmometer is an instrument used to measure the rhythm or tempo of music or other rhythmic phenomena.  It might record beats per minute (BPM) or other metrics of rhythmic regularity or irregularity. | Example: The conductor used a rhythmometer to ensure the orchestra maintained a consistent tempo throughout the performance.  The scientist employed a sophisticated rhythmometer to analyze the rhythmic patterns in the bird's song. | 近义词:  metronome (for musical tempo), tempo meter | 反义词:  None directly applicable.
