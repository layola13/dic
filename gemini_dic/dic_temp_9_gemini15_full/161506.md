potentiometers:/pəˌtɛnʃiˈɒmɪtərz/ | n. | 电位器，分压器 | A potentiometer is a three-terminal resistor with a sliding or rotating contact that forms an adjustable voltage divider.  It is used to control the voltage or current in a circuit. | Example: We used a potentiometer to adjust the volume of the amplifier. 我们用电位器调节了放大器的音量。 |近义词：variable resistor, rheostat |反义词：fixed resistor

词源:
The word "potentiometer" comes from the combination of "potential" (referring to electric potential or voltage) and "meter" (a measuring instrument).  It literally means an instrument for measuring potential difference.
