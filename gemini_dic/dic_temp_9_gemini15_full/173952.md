quasi-sincerely: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ sɪnˈsɪrli/ | adj. |似是而非地真诚地；不真诚地；半真半假地|  Seemingly sincere but actually not entirely genuine;  showing only a pretense of sincerity.  It implies a lack of complete honesty or earnestness, a feeling of insincerity masked by a superficial display of sincerity. | 例句：He offered a quasi-sincerely apology, leaving me feeling doubtful about his remorse. 他做了一个似是而非的道歉，让我对他是否悔恨感到怀疑。


He gave a quasi-sincerely promise, knowing he probably wouldn't keep it. 他做了一个似是而非的承诺，知道自己可能不会遵守。|近义词：halfheartedly, insincerely, superficially sincere, feigned sincerity |反义词：genuinely, sincerely, wholeheartedly, truthfully

词源:
The word is a combination of "quasi," meaning "seemingly," "apparently," or "to a certain extent,"  and "sincerely," meaning "in a genuine and honest way."  Therefore, "quasi-sincerely" describes something that appears sincere but lacks complete authenticity.
