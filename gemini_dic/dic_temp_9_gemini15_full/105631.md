kinetoscope:/ˈkaɪnɪtəskoʊp/| n. |活动电影机，活动视镜|A kinetoscope is a peephole device used for viewing moving pictures.  It was invented by Thomas Edison and William Kennedy Laurie Dickson in 1891. Unlike a projector, it was designed for individual use, not for a large audience.  |Example: Edison's kinetoscope allowed individuals to experience moving images for the first time. 爱迪生的活动电影机让人们第一次体验到了活动图像。|近义词：peep show (in a historical context)|反义词：projector (in terms of viewing method)|名词
