damper:/ˈdæmpər/| n. v.|减震器；抑制因素；阻尼器；使沮丧；使潮湿|A damper is something that reduces or prevents the intensity or effectiveness of something.  It can refer to a physical device that controls airflow or sound, or a metaphorical influence that lessens enthusiasm or excitement.|例句：The rain put a damper on our picnic.  雨水使我们的野餐扫兴了。|The heavy curtains acted as a damper on the noise. 厚重的窗帘起到了隔音的作用。|近义词：depressant, inhibitor, restrictor|反义词：stimulant, enhancer, boost|

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rain dampens the mood. = 雨水使气氛低沉。
This news dampens my spirits. = 这个消息让我沮丧。


一般过去时:
The bad weather dampened the festival. = 恶劣的天气使节日气氛低落。
His criticism dampened her enthusiasm. = 他的批评使她热情减退。
