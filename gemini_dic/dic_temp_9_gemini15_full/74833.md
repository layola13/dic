frenulum: /ˈfrɛnjʊləm/ | n. |小带；系带；缰绳 | A small fold or ridge of tissue that restrains or connects a part.  It is often found in the body, connecting a movable part to a fixed one.  For example, the frenulum linguae is the fold under the tongue. |例句：The baby's frenulum was clipped shortly after birth.  婴儿出生后不久就剪掉了系带。

The child has a short lingual frenulum, affecting his speech. 这个孩子的舌系带过短，影响了他的说话。


近义词：fold, band, ligament | 反义词：N/A


词源:  From Latin *frenulum, diminutive of frenum ("bridle," "rein").  The word reflects the anatomical structure's function of restraining or controlling movement, similar to how a bridle controls a horse.
