/priːv/ | v. | 赦免，暂缓执行（死刑） | To reprieve someone means to cancel or postpone their punishment, especially the death penalty.  It often implies a temporary delay rather than a permanent pardon. | The prisoner was reprieved at the last minute.  犯人最后一刻被赦免了。| pardon, respite | execute, punish | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The governor reprieves many prisoners each year. = 州长每年赦免许多囚犯。
He reprieves the death sentence. = 他暂缓死刑。


一般过去时:
The court reprieved the condemned man. = 法院赦免了那个被判死刑的人。
They reprieved him from execution. = 他们免除了他的死刑。


现在完成时:
The judge has reprieved him from the death sentence. = 法官已经免除了他的死刑。
They have reprieved several prisoners this month. = 这个月他们已经赦免了好几个囚犯。


过去完成时:
The lawyer had hoped the judge would reprieve his client. = 律师曾希望法官能赦免他的当事人。
The family had already prepared for the worst before the reprieve.  在赦免之前，家人已经做好了最坏的打算。


将来时:

I wish they had reprieved him. = 我希望他们赦免了他。


被动语态:
