rider:/ˈraɪdər/| n. | 骑手；附加条款；额外负担 | A person who rides an animal, especially a horse; an addition or amendment to a document, contract, or law; something that adds to an existing burden or difficulty. |例句：He is an experienced rider. 他是一位经验丰富的骑手。  The contract included a rider concerning confidentiality. 合同中包含一条关于保密性的附加条款。|近义词：horseman, rider, jockey, amendment, addition |反义词：driver(in some contexts)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rides his bike to work every day. = 他每天骑自行车上班。
She rides her horse in the park. = 她在公园里骑马。

一般过去时:
He rode his horse to the town. = 他骑马去了镇上。
She rode her bike to school yesterday. = 她昨天骑自行车去上学。

现在完成时:
He has ridden his bike across the country. = 他骑自行车横穿了全国。
She has ridden horses since she was a child. = 她从小就骑马。

过去完成时:
He had ridden his bike for hours before he stopped. = 他骑了几个小时的自行车才停下来。
She had ridden many horses before she found her favorite. = 在找到她最喜欢的马之前，她已经骑过很多马了。

将来时:
He will ride his bike to the beach tomorrow. = 他明天将骑自行车去海滩。
She will ride her horse in the competition next week. = 她下周将参加比赛骑马。

虚拟语气:
If I had a horse, I would ride it every day. = 如果我有一匹马，我每天都会骑它。
I wish I could ride a motorcycle. = 我真希望我能骑摩托车。

被动语态:
The horse was ridden by a young girl. = 这匹马被一个年轻女孩骑着。
The bicycle is being ridden by someone. = 自行车正被某人骑着。

疑问句:
Do you ride a bike? = 你骑自行车吗？
Does she ride horses often? = 她经常骑马吗？

否定句:
He doesn't ride a motorcycle. = 他不骑摩托车。
She didn't ride her bike today. = 她今天没骑自行车。
