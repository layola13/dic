fingerprint: /ˈfɪŋɡərprɪnt/ | n. | 指纹 | A unique pattern of ridges and valleys on the surface of a finger, used for identification.  | Example: The police found his fingerprint on the murder weapon.  警察在凶器上发现了他的指纹。|近义词：thumbprint, dactylogram | 反义词：None


USAGE->

The police use fingerprints to identify criminals. = 警察用指纹识别罪犯。
His fingerprint is on file with the police department. = 他的指纹已存入警局档案。

一般过去时:
They compared the fingerprints found at the scene. = 他们比对了在现场发现的指纹。
