sidled:/ˈsaɪdəld/| v.| 侧身移动；偷偷地走；| to move sideways or obliquely; to go stealthily or shyly|The cat sidled up to me, rubbing against my leg. 猫蹭着我的腿，偷偷地走到我身边。 He sidled out of the room, trying to avoid being noticed. 他偷偷地溜出了房间，试图避免被注意到。|近义词：inched, edged, slipped, crept|反义词：marched, strode


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He sidles up to the bar. = 他侧身走到酒吧。
She sidles past the group. = 她侧身穿过人群。

一般过去时:
He sidled into the room. = 他偷偷地溜进房间。
She sidled away from the argument. = 她悄悄地避开了争论。

现在完成时:
He has sidled up to the boss for a promotion. = 他为了升职而讨好老板。
She has sidled her way into the inner circle. = 她设法挤入了核心圈子。

过去完成时:
He had sidled away before I could speak. = 我还没来得及说话，他就偷偷溜走了。
She had sidled into the room unnoticed. = 她悄悄地溜进了房间，没有被人注意到。

将来时:
He will sidle up to her at the party. = 他将在派对上偷偷靠近她。
She will sidle out of the meeting early. = 她将早早地溜出会议。

虚拟语气:
If he sidled up to the witness, he might get some information. = 如果他偷偷接近证人，他可能会得到一些信息。
If she had sidled closer, she would have heard the conversation. = 如果她靠得更近一些，她就会听到谈话。

被动语态:
He was sidled aside by the security guard. = 他被保安人员推到一边。
She was sidled out of the conversation. = 她被排挤出了谈话。


疑问句:
Did he sidle out of the room? = 他是不是偷偷溜出了房间？
Why did she sidle away from him? = 她为什么躲着他？


否定句:
He didn't sidle up to her; he approached her directly. = 他没有偷偷靠近她；他是直接走向她的。
She didn't sidle away; she walked confidently. = 她没有偷偷溜走；她自信地走开了。


PHRASE->
sidle up to someone = 偷偷靠近某人
sidle into something = 偷偷进入某种情况
sidle out of something = 偷偷退出某种情况
sidle away from someone = 偷偷避开某人
sidle past someone = 侧身经过某人
