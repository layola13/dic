culminate:/ˈkʌlmɪneɪt/| v.|达到顶点；达到高潮|To reach a culminating point; to end, especially in a dramatic or important way.|The years of research culminated in a major discovery. 多年的研究最终取得了重大发现。|The festival culminated in a spectacular fireworks display.  节日以壮观的烟火表演达到高潮。|apex, climax, peak|anticlimax, nadir


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The project culminates in a final presentation. = 项目最终以最终演示文稿结束。
His efforts culminate in a successful business. = 他的努力最终成就了一家成功的企业。


一般过去时:
The party culminated in a lively dance. = 派对在热烈的舞蹈中达到高潮。
Their journey culminated in reaching the summit. = 他们的旅程最终到达了山顶。


现在完成时:
Years of hard work have culminated in this achievement. = 多年的辛勤工作最终成就了这一成就。
His career has culminated in a position of leadership. = 他的职业生涯最终达到了领导地位。


过去完成时:
The investigation had culminated in the arrest of the suspect. = 调查最终导致了嫌疑人的逮捕。
By the end of the year, their efforts had culminated in a successful product launch. = 到年底，他们的努力最终成功地推出了产品。


将来时:
The conference will culminate in a panel discussion. = 会议将以小组讨论结束。
The semester will culminate with final exams. = 学期将以期末考试结束。


虚拟语气:
If we worked harder, our efforts might culminate in success. = 如果我们更努力工作，我们的努力可能会最终取得成功。
I wish my efforts had culminated in a better outcome. = 我希望我的努力能有更好的结果。


被动语态:
The project is expected to culminate in a new product launch. = 预计该项目将最终推出新产品。
The investigation was culminated in the arrest of several individuals. = 调查最终导致了几人的逮捕。


疑问句:
When will the negotiations culminate? = 谈判什么时候结束？
How will the competition culminate? = 比赛将如何结束？


否定句:
His efforts did not culminate in success. = 他的努力没有取得成功。
The project did not culminate as planned. = 项目没有按计划结束。


PHRASE->

culminate in = 以…告终
culminate with = 以…结束
culminate a series of events =  一系列事件的最终结果
culminate in a victory = 以胜利告终
culminate in disaster = 以灾难告终
