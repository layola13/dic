connivance:/kəˈnaɪvəns/| n.|纵容；默许；共谋|The act of conniving; secretly allowing something bad to happen.  It often implies a shared understanding or agreement to overlook wrongdoing.  |例句：The success of the plan depended on the connivance of several officials.  这个计划的成功取决于几位官员的默许。

例句：Their silence was interpreted as connivance in the crime. 他们的沉默被解读为对犯罪的纵容。


近义词：collusion, complicity, tacit approval, consent, acquiescence|反义词：opposition, disapproval, protest, obstruction|词性：名词
