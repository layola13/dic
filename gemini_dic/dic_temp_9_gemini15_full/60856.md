elects:/ɪˈlɛkts/| vt.|选举，推选|To choose (someone) for a position, especially by voting.|The people elect their representatives.|近义词：choose, select, nominate|反义词：reject, dismiss


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The country elects a new president every four years. = 这个国家每四年选举一位新总统。
We elect our class representatives by secret ballot. = 我们通过无记名投票选举班代表。


一般过去时:
The committee elected him chairman. = 委员会选举他为主席。
They elected to postpone the meeting. = 他们决定推迟会议。


现在完成时:
The board has elected a new CEO. = 董事会已经选举了一位新的首席执行官。
She has been elected to the city council. = 她已被选入市议会。


过去完成时:
They had elected a new leader before the crisis. = 危机发生前他们已经选出了一位新领导人。
The party had already elected its candidate when the scandal broke. = 丑闻爆发时，该党已经选定了候选人。


将来时:
The voters will elect a new mayor next month. = 选民们下个月将选举一位新市长。
We will elect a new student council president next week. = 我们下周将选举一位新的学生会主席。


虚拟语气:
If they elected to cooperate, the project would succeed. = 如果他们选择合作，项目就会成功。
I wish they had elected a more experienced candidate. = 我真希望他们选了一位更有经验的候选人。


被动语态:
A new president was elected last year. = 去年选举了一位新总统。
The committee was elected by the members. = 委员会是由成员们选举产生的。


疑问句:
Who was elected to the Senate? = 谁当选参议员？
Will they elect a new leader soon? = 他们很快会选举一位新领导人吗？


否定句:
The party did not elect him as its leader. = 该党没有选举他为领导人。
They elected not to attend the meeting. = 他们决定不参加会议。


PHRASE->
elects to do something = 选择做某事
elects a representative = 选举代表
elects a committee = 选举委员会
elects a president = 选举总统
elects a leader = 选举领导人
