polycarpous:/ˌpɒliˈkɑːrpəs/| adj.|多核的，多心皮的|Having many carpels (the female reproductive organ of a flower).  This term is primarily used in botany to describe fruits or flowers that develop from multiple carpels.  For example, a raspberry is a polycarpous fruit because it's formed from many individual fruitlets, each developing from a separate carpel.|例句：The raspberry is a polycarpous fruit, unlike the peach, which is monocarpous.|近义词：multi-carpellate|反义词：monocarpous
