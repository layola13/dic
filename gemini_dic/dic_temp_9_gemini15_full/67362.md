extensionality's:/ˌɛkˌstɛnʃəˈnælətiz/| n. | 外延性；外延；外延性 |The property of a concept or expression being determined solely by its extension (the set of things it applies to), rather than its intension (the meaning or connotation).  It contrasts with intensionality, which focuses on the meaning and connotation. |Example:  The extensionality of set theory means that two sets are equal if and only if they have the same members, regardless of how they are described.|近义词：extensiveness|反义词：intensionality
