immovably:/ɪˈmuːvəbli/ | adj. |不动地；坚定地；坚决地 |  Unchangingly fixed in place or purpose; not capable of being moved or changed.  | The boulder sat immovably in the middle of the road. 巨石不动地躺在路中央。  The general remained immovably opposed to the peace treaty. 将军坚定地反对和平条约。| steadfastly, firmly, resolutely | movably, changeably


USAGE->

He stands immovably. 他一动不动地站着。
The statue remains immovably in the square. 雕像不动地矗立在广场上。


一般过去时:
The car immovably blocked the entrance. 车子纹丝不动地堵住了入口。

现在完成时:
The rock has remained immovably in place for centuries. 这块石头已经不动地在那里存在了几个世纪。

过去完成时:

将来时:
The mountain will stand immovably against the wind. 这座山将坚定地抵御风。

虚拟语气:
If the object were immovably fixed, it would not fall. 如果物体固定不动，它就不会掉下来。
I wish my belief in justice were immovably strong. 我希望我对正义的信念坚定如山。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
The situation was not immovably fixed; it could change.  情况并非一成不变；它可能会改变。
He was not immovably committed to the cause. 他并非坚定地致力于这项事业。
