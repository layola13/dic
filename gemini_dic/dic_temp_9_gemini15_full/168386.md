proactively:/proʊˈæktɪvli/ | adv. |主动地；积极地 |Acting in anticipation of future problems, needs, or changes.  It emphasizes taking initiative and preventing issues before they arise, rather than simply reacting to them. |例句：The company proactively addressed customer concerns before they escalated into major problems. 公司主动处理客户的顾虑，以免它们升级为重大问题。


例句：We need to proactively identify and mitigate potential risks. 我们需要主动识别和减轻潜在风险。


近义词：preemptively, anticipatorily, preventively


反义词：reactively, passively


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We proactively seek out new opportunities. = 我们主动寻求新的机会。
They proactively manage their time. = 他们积极管理时间。


一般过去时:
She proactively contacted the client. = 她主动联系了客户。
He proactively solved the problem. = 他主动解决了这个问题。


现在完成时:
We have proactively addressed all the issues. = 我们已经主动解决了所有问题。
They have proactively implemented new strategies. = 他们已经主动实施了新的策略。


过去完成时:
They had proactively prepared for the meeting. = 他们已经为会议做好了充分的准备。
She had proactively investigated the matter. = 她已经主动调查了此事。


将来时:
We will proactively monitor the situation. = 我们将主动监测形势。
They will proactively respond to any challenges. = 他们将积极应对任何挑战。


虚拟语气:
If we proactively addressed the issue, we would avoid further problems. = 如果我们主动处理这个问题，我们将避免进一步的问题。
I wish we had proactively planned for this eventuality. = 我希望我们已经为这种情况做了主动的计划。


被动语态:
The problem was proactively addressed by the team. = 问题由团队主动解决。
The risks were proactively mitigated by the company. = 风险由公司主动减轻。


疑问句:

否定句:
They did not proactively communicate the changes. = 他们没有主动沟通这些变化。
We did not proactively seek feedback. = 我们没有主动征求反馈。
