revolted:/rɪˈvəʊltɪd/| adj. | 反叛的；厌恶的；作呕的 | feeling or expressing disgust; having rebelled |The revolted citizens took to the streets to protest.  反叛的市民走上街头抗议。|  The smell revolted me.  这气味让我作呕。 | disgusted, nauseated, rebellious | content, pleased, accepting


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He revolts against authority. 他反抗权威。
She revolts at the sight of blood. 她看到血就反胃。


一般过去时:
The people revolted against the oppressive regime. 人民反抗压迫的政权。
The spoiled food revolted her.  变质的食物让她作呕。


现在完成时:
The citizens have revolted against the government’s policies.  公民们已经反抗政府的政策了。
The stench has revolted him for days. 这恶臭已经让他恶心了好几天了。


过去完成时:
They had revolted before the king even realized. 国王还没意识到他们就已经造反了。
She had revolted at the sight of the accident. 她看到事故后感到恶心。


将来时:
The people will revolt if their demands are not met. 如果他们的要求得不到满足，人民将会起义。
The thought of eating that makes me think I will revolt.  想到吃那个就让我觉得我会反胃。


虚拟语气:
If the people revolted, the government would fall. 如果人民起义，政府就会垮台。
If I had known it would revolt me, I wouldn't have tried it. 如果我知道它会让我作呕，我不会尝试的。


被动语态:
The government was revolted by the people's actions. 政府对人民的行为感到愤怒。
His stomach was revolted by the bad food. 他因为吃了坏的食物而胃不舒服。


疑问句:
Were the people revolted by the news? 人民对这个消息感到愤怒吗？
Is he revolted by the sight of insects? 他看到昆虫会恶心吗？


否定句:
The people did not revolt against the new law. 人民没有反抗新法律。
The taste didn't revolt her at all.  那个味道一点也没让她反胃。


PHRASE->

revolt against 反抗...
revolt at 对...感到厌恶
feel revolted 感到反感
be revolted by 被...所厌恶
open revolt 公开叛乱
