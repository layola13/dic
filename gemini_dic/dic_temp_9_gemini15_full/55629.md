ditch:/dɪtʃ/| n. vt.|沟渠；放弃；摆脱；|a long, narrow channel dug in the ground, usually at the side of a road or field, to carry away water.  To get rid of something or someone that is no longer wanted or needed.|The farmer dug a ditch to drain the water from his field.  农民挖了一条沟渠来排水。 They decided to ditch the old car and buy a new one.  他们决定抛弃旧车买一辆新车。|近义词：canal, trench, abandon, discard,  |反义词：keep, retain, preserve


USAGE->

He ditches his old shoes. = 他扔掉他的旧鞋子。
They ditch the plan. = 他们放弃了这个计划。
