lit:/lɪt/ | adj. |点燃的；照亮的；充满光亮的；兴奋的；醉醺醺的 | illuminated; shining brightly; filled with light; excited; intoxicated |

英文解析:  The adjective "lit" has two main meanings.  The first and more traditional meaning refers to something being illuminated or on fire. The second, more modern meaning, especially prevalent in informal contexts, describes something as exciting, vibrant, or exhilarating.  It can also colloquially mean intoxicated or drunk.

例句:

The room was lit by a single candle. =  房间里只有一支蜡烛照明。

The city at night was beautifully lit. = 夜晚的城市灯火辉煌。


The concert was absolutely lit! = 音乐会太棒了！

We were all lit after the party. = 派对后我们都兴奋极了。


近义词: (depending on the context) illuminated, bright, vibrant, exciting, thrilling, intoxicated, drunk


反义词: (depending on the context) dark, dim, dull, boring, sober


词源:  The word "lit" is the past participle of the verb "to light," meaning to ignite or illuminate.  Its modern informal usage evolved as slang, likely deriving from its association with the vibrant energy of a brightly lit place or event.
