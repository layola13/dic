hyperparathyroidism: /ˌhaɪpərˌpærəˈθaɪrɔɪdɪzəm/ | n. |甲状旁腺功能亢进| A condition characterized by an overproduction of parathyroid hormone (PTH), leading to high levels of calcium in the blood.  This can cause various symptoms, including bone pain, kidney stones, and fatigue.  It's often treated with surgery to remove the overactive parathyroid gland(s).| The patient presented with hyperparathyroidism, exhibiting symptoms such as bone pain and fatigue.|hypoparathyroidism|euthyroidism|名词
