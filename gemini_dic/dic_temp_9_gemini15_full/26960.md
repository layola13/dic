calcific: /kælˈsɪfɪk/ | adj. | 钙化的；含钙的 | Containing or undergoing the process of becoming calcified.  The term refers to the deposition of calcium salts in body tissues, often leading to hardening and dysfunction. | 例句：The X-ray showed calcific deposits in his arteries. X光显示他的动脉中有钙化沉积物。


The patient presented with calcific tendinitis in his shoulder. 病人出现肩部钙化性肌腱炎。| 近义词：calcified, ossified | 反义词：decalcified

词源:
From Latin *calcificāre, from calx "lime, limestone" + -ficāre "to make".  The suffix "-fic" indicates "making" or "causing."  Therefore, "calcific" means "making or relating to lime or calcium."
