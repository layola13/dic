humate:/ˈhjuːmeɪt/| n.|腐殖酸盐|Humate refers to the salts and esters of humic acid, a complex organic substance found in soil and water.  It's formed from the decomposition of plant and animal matter. Humates are characterized by their dark color, high molecular weight, and ability to bind to metals and other organic molecules.|The soil is rich in humates, making it fertile and productive.|近义词：humic substance|反义词：无
