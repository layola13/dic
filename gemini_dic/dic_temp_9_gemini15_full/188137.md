rouser:/ˈraʊzər/| n., v.| 唤醒者；激励者；煽动者 | A person or thing that rouses someone from sleep or inactivity; something that stirs up strong feelings or excitement. |例句：The early morning sun was a rouser, pulling me from my slumber.  清晨的阳光把我从睡梦中唤醒。


例句：His speech was a rouser, inspiring the crowd to action. 他的演讲很鼓舞人心，激励着人群采取行动。


近义词：awakener, stimulator, agitator, inciter |反义词：soother, calmer, pacifier

词性: 名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rouses his children early every morning. = 他每天早晨都早早地叫醒他的孩子们。
The strong wind rouses the leaves. = 强风吹动树叶。

一般过去时:
The alarm clock roused me at 6 a.m. = 闹钟在早上六点把我叫醒了。
The news roused public anger. = 这则新闻激起了公众的愤怒。

现在完成时:
The news has roused much debate. = 这则新闻引发了大量的辩论。
The storm has roused the sea. = 暴风雨激起了海浪。


过去完成时:
He had roused himself from his depression. = 他已从沮丧中振作起来。
The general had roused his troops for battle. = 将军已激励他的部队准备战斗。


将来时:
The speech will rouse the audience to action. = 这篇演讲将激励听众采取行动。
The coming election will rouse strong feelings. = 即将到来的选举将激起强烈的感情。


虚拟语气:
If the music were louder, it would rouse the sleeping child. = 如果音乐再响亮一些，它就能唤醒熟睡的孩子。
If I had known the truth, it would have roused my anger. = 如果我知道真相，它就会激怒我。


被动语态:
The sleeping child was roused by the loud music. = 熟睡的孩子被响亮的音乐吵醒了。
The audience was roused by the passionate speech. = 观众被热情洋溢的演讲所打动。


疑问句:
What roused you from your sleep? = 什么把你从睡梦中惊醒？
What will rouse the public's interest? = 什么才能引起公众的兴趣？


否定句:
Nothing could rouse him from his apathy. = 什么也无法让他从冷漠中振作起来。
The news did not rouse her from her daydream. = 这则新闻并没有把她从白日梦中惊醒。
