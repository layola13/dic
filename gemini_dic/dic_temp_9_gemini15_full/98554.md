instinct: /ˈɪnstɪŋkt/ | n. |本能，直觉 | An innate, typically fixed pattern of behavior in animals in response to certain stimuli.  It's a natural inclination or impulse. |例句：Birds have an instinct to build nests. 鸟类有筑巢的本能。|例句：She followed her instincts and chose the red dress. 她凭直觉选择了红裙子。|近义词：intuition, impulse, hunch |反义词：reason, deliberation


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My cat's instinct tells it to hunt mice. = 我家的猫有捕猎老鼠的本能。
Birds' instinct drives them to migrate south in winter. = 鸟类的本能驱使它们在冬天向南迁徙。


一般过去时:
He acted on instinct and saved the child. = 他凭本能行事，救了孩子。
My first instinct was to run away. = 我第一反应是逃跑。


现在完成时:
Her instincts have served her well throughout her career. = 直觉让她在事业上一直很成功。
I have followed my instincts and it has led me to success. = 我一直跟着直觉走，它让我走向成功。


过去完成时:
My instinct had told me something was wrong. = 我的直觉告诉我有什么不对劲。
I had a gut instinct that he was lying. = 我有一种强烈的感觉他是在撒谎。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish I had trusted my instincts. = 我真希望当时相信我的直觉。


被动语态:
His actions were guided by instinct. = 他的行为受本能支配。

疑问句:

否定句:
