glimmery:/ˈɡlɪməri/| adj. | 微光闪烁的；朦胧的；若隐若现的 |  Having a faint, shimmering, or flickering light; slightly luminous.  The description suggests a soft, unsteady light, often associated with stars, fireflies, or a faint light source. | 例句：The glimmery moonlight bathed the snow-covered landscape in an ethereal glow. 月光微弱地照亮了白雪皑皑的景色，呈现出一种超凡脱俗的光辉。

The glimmery lights of the distant city were barely visible through the fog. 远处城市微弱的灯光透过雾气几乎看不清。


近义词：shimmering, twinkling, sparkling, lustrous, faint | 反义词：bright, glaring, dazzling, brilliant


词性: 形容词

词源:
The word "glimmery" is derived from the verb "glimmer," which ultimately comes from Old English *glimerian, meaning "to shine faintly."  The suffix "-y" adds an adjectival quality, indicating something possessing the characteristic of glimmering.
