pacifist: /ˈpæsɪfɪst/ | n. adj. | 和平主义者；爱好和平的人；和平主义的 | A person who believes in pacifism and is opposed to war or violence.  |例句：Many pacifists actively work for peace and social justice. 许多和平主义者积极致力于和平与社会正义。
He is a staunch pacifist and refuses to participate in any military actions. 他是一个坚定的和平主义者，拒绝参与任何军事行动。|近义词：peacemaker, non-violent, anti-war |反义词：militarist, warmonger, bellicose

词源:
The word "pacifist" comes from the Latin word "pax," meaning "peace," combined with the suffix "-ist," indicating a person who believes in or practices something.  The term emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries as a way to identify individuals opposed to war and violence.
