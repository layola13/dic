maligned:/məˈlaɪnd/| vt.|诽谤；中伤；污蔑|to speak badly of someone or something, especially unfairly or falsely.|例句：He was maligned by the press for his controversial views.  他因其有争议的观点而受到媒体的诽谤。

例句：The politician's reputation was maligned by his opponents' relentless attacks.  这位政治家的声誉被对手无情的攻击所毁谤。


近义词：defame, slander, vilify, smear, denigrate|反义词：praise, commend, extol, laud


词源:
The word "maligned" comes from the Old French word "maligner," which means "to slander" or "to speak evil of."  This, in turn, derives from the prefix "mal-" (bad, ill) and the verb "ligner," related to the Latin "ligare" (to bind), suggesting the idea of binding someone with negative words.
