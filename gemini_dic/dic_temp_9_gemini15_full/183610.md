requirement:/rɪˈkwaɪərmənt/ | n. | 要求；必要条件；规定 | A requirement is something that is needed or necessary.  It can refer to a condition that must be met, a specification that must be followed, or a need that must be fulfilled. | Example:  The job requirement includes at least five years of experience. 这份工作的要求包括至少五年的经验。 |  近义词：need, demand, necessity, prerequisite | 反义词：option, choice


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project has several requirements. = 这个项目有几个要求。
This position requires excellent communication skills. = 这个职位需要优秀的沟通技巧。


一般过去时:
The previous version had different requirements. = 之前的版本有不同的要求。
The client required a quick turnaround time. = 客户要求快速周转时间。


现在完成时:
They have met all the requirements. = 他们已经满足了所有要求。
We have fulfilled the customer's requirements. = 我们已经满足了客户的要求。


过去完成时:
The software didn't meet the requirements that had been established. = 该软件没有满足既定的要求。
They had already discussed the requirements before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前就已经讨论过这些要求了。


将来时:
The new system will require significant investment. = 新系统将需要大量投资。
Future requirements will be addressed in the next phase. = 将来的需求将在下一阶段解决。


虚拟语气:
If only the requirements were clearer! = 要是要求更清晰就好了！
It is essential that all requirements be met. = 满足所有要求是必要的。


被动语态:
The requirements were clearly stated in the contract. = 合同中清楚地说明了要求。
The system is designed to meet the requirements of the users. = 该系统旨在满足用户的需求。


疑问句:
What are the requirements for this grant? = 申请这项拨款需要什么条件？
Are there any other requirements we need to consider? = 我们还需要考虑其他要求吗？


否定句:
The product doesn't meet the safety requirements. = 该产品不符合安全要求。
He failed to fulfill the necessary requirements. = 他未能满足必要的条件。


PHRASE->
