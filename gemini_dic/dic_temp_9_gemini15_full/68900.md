fasting:/ˈfæstɪŋ/| n. |禁食，斋戒 |The act of abstaining from all or some kinds of food or drink, especially for religious reasons or as a medical treatment.  禁食的行为，尤其出于宗教原因或作为一种医疗手段而戒绝所有或某些食物或饮料。|例句：Many religions involve periods of fasting. 许多宗教都包含禁食的时期。|例句：The doctor recommended fasting before the blood test. 医生建议在验血前禁食。|近义词：abstinence, starvation |反义词：gorging, feasting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fasts every Wednesday. = 他每周三都禁食。
She fasts for religious reasons. = 她因为宗教原因而禁食。

一般过去时:
I fasted for 24 hours. = 我禁食了24小时。
They fasted before the ceremony. = 他们在仪式前禁食。

现在完成时:
She has fasted intermittently for years. = 她多年来断断续续地禁食。
He has never fasted before. = 他以前从未禁食过。

过去完成时:
I had fasted for three days when I finally ate. = 当我最终进食时，我已经禁食了三天。
They had fasted all day before the feast. = 他们在盛宴前禁食了一整天。

将来时:
I will fast tomorrow. = 我明天要禁食。
We will be fasting for the next week. = 我们将在接下来的一个星期禁食。

虚拟语气:
If I were to fast, I would feel weak. = 如果我要禁食，我会感到虚弱。
I wish I had fasted before my medical examination. = 我希望我在体检前禁食过。

被动语态:  (很少用被动语态)
Fasting is often recommended before surgery. = 手术前常建议禁食。
The effects of fasting are being studied. = 禁食的效果正在被研究。

疑问句:
Are you fasting today? = 你今天禁食吗？
Does she often fast? = 她经常禁食吗？

否定句:
I don't fast regularly. = 我不定期禁食。
They didn't fast for the holiday. = 他们没有为假期禁食。
