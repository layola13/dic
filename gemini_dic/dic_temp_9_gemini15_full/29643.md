caterwaul: /ˈkætərˌwɔːl/ | v. |猫叫；发出尖厉刺耳的声音 | To make a harsh, discordant cry, like that of a cat; to make a loud, unpleasant noise.  |例句：The cats caterwauled all night long.  = 猫整夜都在猫叫。

The neighbors' loud argument caused a caterwaul that disturbed the peace. = 邻居们大声争吵发出的尖厉刺耳的声音扰乱了平静。


近义词：yowl, howl, shriek, screech, wail | 反义词：whisper, murmur

词源:
The word "caterwaul" is believed to be of imitative origin. The first part, "cater," may be related to the Old French word "cat," while "waul" is likely an alteration of "wail," reflecting the sound of a cat's cry.  The combination suggests the sound of cats wailing or crying.
