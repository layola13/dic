impulse:/ˈɪmpʌls/|/ˈɪmpʌls/| n. v.|冲动；脉冲；刺激|A sudden strong and unreflective urge or desire to act.  A short burst of energy. To give a sudden brief push to (something).|例句：He acted on impulse and regretted it later. 他一时冲动，后来后悔了。

He felt a sudden impulse to run away. 他感到一股突然想逃跑的冲动。


近义词：urge, whim, spur, thrust|反义词：deliberation, reflection, calmness


词源:
From Middle French impulsion, from Latin impulsus, past participle of impellere "to push, drive, urge," from in- "in, into" + pellere "to drive, push."


USAGE->
名词作主语：
An impulse caused her to shout. 一股冲动让她大喊起来。
The impulse to buy the dress was overwhelming.  购买这件衣服的冲动是难以抗拒的。

名词作宾语：
He resisted the impulse to interrupt. 他克制住了插嘴的冲动。
She acted on a sudden impulse. 她一时冲动行事。

动词作谓语：
The engine impulsed the car forward. 发动机推动汽车前进。
The news impulsed him to action. 这个消息促使他采取行动。


PHRASE->
on impulse: 一时冲动
nervous impulse: 神经冲动
impulse buying: 冲动购物
control one's impulses: 控制冲动
give an impulse to: 给予…推动力
