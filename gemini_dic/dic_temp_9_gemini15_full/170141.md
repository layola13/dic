protogalaxy:/ˈproʊtoʊˌɡæləksi/ | n. |原星系；原始星系 |A protogalaxy is a massive, gaseous cloud that is thought to be the precursor to a galaxy.  It is a large, diffuse cloud of gas and dust that has not yet collapsed to form a well-defined structure like a spiral or elliptical galaxy.  Protogalaxies are believed to have formed in the early universe and gradually evolved into the galaxies we see today. | Example:  Astronomers believe that our own Milky Way galaxy formed from the collapse of a protogalaxy billions of years ago.  天文学家认为我们自己的银河系是在数十亿年前由一个原星系坍缩而成的。| 近义词：pre-galaxy, early galaxy | 反义词：mature galaxy, fully formed galaxy
