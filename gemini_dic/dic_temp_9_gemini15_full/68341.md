fall-pipe: /ˈfɔːlpaɪp/ | n. | 落管；倾斜管道 | A fall pipe is a vertical or steeply inclined pipe used to convey materials, typically solids or slurries, downwards by gravity.  It is commonly used in mining, construction, and industrial processes. | 例句：The fall pipe transported the crushed ore from the mine to the processing plant. 落管将破碎矿石从矿井输送到加工厂。


The newly installed fall pipe significantly increased the efficiency of the concrete pouring operation. 新安装的落管大大提高了混凝土浇筑效率。

近义词：chute, downpipe, slide | 反义词：提升管 (There isn't a direct antonym, but an "uplift pipe" or similar would be the opposite function.)

词源:

The term "fall pipe" is a descriptive compound word.  "Fall" refers to the downward movement of the material, and "pipe" refers to the tubular structure used for conveyance.  The word's origin is thus straightforward and reflects its function.
