mudwort: /ˈmʌdˌwɔːrt/ | n. |泥花, 泥沼植物 | A small aquatic plant that grows in mud or shallow water, often with small, inconspicuous flowers.  Mudworts are typically found in wetlands and other damp habitats. They are often characterized by their ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions. | Example: The muddy bank was carpeted with a dense growth of mudwort.  泥泞的河岸上长满了茂密的泥花。


The tiny mudwort flowers were barely visible amongst the reeds.  芦苇丛中几乎看不见小小的泥花。


近义词: aquatic plant, marsh plant | 反义词:  (No direct antonyms, as it describes a specific type of plant)


词源:  The word "mudwort" is a compound word. "Mud" refers to the soft, wet earth, and "wort" is an Old English word meaning "herb" or "plant."  Therefore, mudwort literally means "mud plant".
