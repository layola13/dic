quasi-externally: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ ɪkˈstɜːrnəli/ | adj. |准外部地，类似外部地 |  Describes something that resembles or has characteristics of something external, but isn't fully or strictly external. It suggests a partial or indirect external influence or relationship.  | Example: The company's quasi-externally sourced components were subject to significant price fluctuations.  这家公司的准外部采购零部件价格波动很大。| 近义词: seemingly external, partially external, indirectly external | 反义词: internally, intrinsically


USAGE->
This word is primarily used as an adjective, and thus doesn't have verb conjugations.  The usage is primarily descriptive.  We can show examples in different sentence types.

肯定句:
The effects were quasi-externally imposed. = 影响是类似外部强加的。
His quasi-externally driven motivation was puzzling. = 他这种类似外部驱动的动力令人费解。


否定句:
The problem wasn't quasi-externally caused; it stemmed from internal issues. = 问题并非由准外部因素引起；它源于内部问题。
The decision wasn't influenced quasi-externally. = 这个决定没有受到类似外部的影响。


疑问句:
Were the factors quasi-externally determined? = 这些因素是准外部决定的吗？
Is the pressure quasi-externally applied? = 压力是准外部施加的吗？
