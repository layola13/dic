meltdowns:/ˈmɛltˌdaʊnz/| n.|崩溃；瓦解；熔毁|A meltdown is a complete loss of control, often involving extreme emotional distress. It can also refer to a catastrophic failure of a system, such as a nuclear reactor.  |例句：She had a complete meltdown after failing her exams. 她考试不及格后彻底崩溃了。


例句：The nuclear meltdown at Chernobyl caused widespread environmental damage. 切尔诺贝利的核反应堆熔毁造成了大范围的环境破坏。|近义词：breakdown, collapse, failure|反义词：calm, composure, stability|名词
