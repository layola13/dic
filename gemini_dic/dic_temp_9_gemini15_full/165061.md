preimages:/ˈpriːɪmɪdʒɪz/| n. |原像；逆像|The preimages are the points in the domain that map to a given point in the codomain under a function.  Preimages refer to the set of all input values that produce a specific output value when a function is applied.  For example, in the function f(x) = x², the preimages of 4 are 2 and -2, because f(2) = 4 and f(-2) = 4.|The preimages of 9 under the square root function are 3 and -3.|近义词：inverse images|反义词：images|名词
