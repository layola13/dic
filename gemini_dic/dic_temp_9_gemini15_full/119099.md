metro:/ˈmɛtroʊ/| n.|地铁，都市铁路系统|A metro is an underground railway system in a city.  It's a fast and efficient way to travel around a large urban area. |例句：I take the metro to work every day. 我每天乘地铁上班。|例句：The city's metro system is expanding to reach more suburbs.  城市的轨道交通系统正在扩展，以覆盖更多的郊区。|近义词：subway, underground, rapid transit|反义词：bus, taxi


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The metro runs every five minutes. = 地铁每五分钟运行一趟。
He takes the metro to school. = 他坐地铁去学校。

一般过去时:
I took the metro home last night. = 我昨晚坐地铁回家了。
The metro was delayed due to a signal failure. = 由于信号故障，地铁延误了。

现在完成时:
They have used the metro extensively during their stay. = 他们在逗留期间广泛使用了地铁。
We have never experienced such a crowded metro. = 我们从未体验过如此拥挤的地铁。

过去完成时:
I had already taken the metro before the announcement. = 在宣布之前我已经乘坐了地铁。
She had never ridden the metro before her trip to London. = 在去伦敦旅行之前，她从未乘坐过地铁。

将来时:
I will take the metro to the airport tomorrow. = 我明天将乘坐地铁去机场。
They will likely use the metro for their daily commute. = 他们很可能会使用地铁进行日常通勤。

虚拟语气:
If I had the metro pass, I would use it. = 如果我有地铁卡，我会使用它。
I wish I had taken the metro instead of driving. = 我真希望我坐地铁而不是开车。

被动语态:
The metro is being upgraded. = 地铁正在升级。
The metro line was closed for maintenance. = 地铁线路因维护而关闭。

疑问句:
Which metro line should I take? = 我应该乘坐哪条地铁线路？
Does the metro run late at night? = 地铁晚上运行很晚吗？

否定句:
I didn't take the metro; I walked. = 我没有坐地铁；我步行去的。
