dissimulates:/dɪˈsɪmjuleɪt/| vt.|掩饰；隐藏；伪装|To hide or disguise one's true feelings or intentions.|He dissimulated his anger, but I could see it in his eyes. 他掩饰了他的怒火，但我从他的眼睛里看出来了。|conceal, mask, feign|reveal, expose, show|动词

词源:
dis- (否定前缀) + simulare (拉丁语，意为“模仿，假装”)  The word ultimately derives from the Latin verb *simulare*, meaning "to imitate" or "to feign." The prefix "dis-" adds a sense of negation or reversal, leading to the meaning of "to hide" or "to disguise."
