re-embrace:/ˌriːɪmˈbreɪs/| vt.|重新拥抱，再次接受|To embrace again; to accept or adopt something again after a period of rejection or neglect.  |例句：After years of estrangement, the family decided to re-embrace their traditions.  多年疏远后，这家人决定重新接受他们的传统。

例句：The company decided to re-embrace its original business model after a failed attempt at diversification. 公司在多元化尝试失败后决定重新采用其最初的商业模式。|近义词：readopt, reaffirm, reinstate |反义词：reject, abandon, forsake |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They re-embrace their faith every Sunday. = 他们每个星期天都重新拥抱他们的信仰。
We re-embrace the opportunity to collaborate. = 我们再次拥抱合作的机会。


一般过去时:

过去完成时:
