immolate:/ˈɪməleɪt/ | vt. | 焚祭；牺牲；毁灭 | To kill or destroy (someone or something) by fire, especially as a sacrifice.  |例句：The ancient people immolated animals to appease the gods. 古代人焚祭动物以平息神灵。
The politician immolated his career by his controversial remarks.  这位政客因其有争议的言论而毁掉了自己的事业。|近义词：sacrifice, destroy, consume |反义词：save, preserve, protect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He immolates animals in a religious ceremony. 他在宗教仪式上焚祭动物。
She immolates her time to her work. 她把时间都奉献给了工作。


一般过去时:
They immolated the effigy of the dictator. 他们焚烧了独裁者的肖像。
The warrior immolated himself to save his people.  战士为了拯救他的子民而牺牲了自己。


现在完成时:
He has immolated himself on the altar of ambition. 他为了野心而牺牲了自己。
She has immolated her personal life for her career. 她为了事业牺牲了自己的个人生活。


过去完成时:
They had immolated their crops to prevent the spread of disease. 他们为了防止疾病蔓延而焚烧了庄稼。
The villagers had immolated the sacred animal before the ceremony. 村民们在仪式之前焚烧了神圣的动物。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he immolated his pride, he might have a better chance. 如果他能放下尊严，他可能会有更好的机会。
I wish she hadn't immolated her savings on that venture. 我真希望她没有把积蓄都投入那个冒险项目。


被动语态:
The sacrifice was immolated on the pyre. 祭品在火葬台上被焚烧。

疑问句:

否定句:
He did not immolate the sacred animal. 他没有焚烧神圣的动物。
She would not immolate her principles for political gain. 她不会为了政治利益而牺牲自己的原则。
