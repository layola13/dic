curses:/ˈkɜːrsɪz/| n. v.|咒骂；诅咒；祸害；灾难|The word "curses" can function as both a noun and a verb. As a noun, it refers to the act of cursing or the state of being cursed, or to misfortune or bad luck. As a verb, it means to invoke a curse upon someone or something.  |例句：His curses echoed through the hall. 他的咒骂声回荡在大厅里。  The old woman cursed the thief. 老妇人诅咒了小偷。|近义词：maledictions, imprecations, execrations,  misfortunes, calamities |反义词：blessings, good fortune, benedictions|名词，动词

词源:
The word "curse" ultimately derives from the Old English word *cursian*, meaning "to curse" or "to swear".  This in turn likely has roots in Proto-Germanic and further back in Proto-Indo-European.  The evolution reflects the long history of the concept of cursing and its expression in language across many cultures.
