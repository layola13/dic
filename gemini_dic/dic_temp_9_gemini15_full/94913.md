in-groups:/ˈɪnɡruːps/| n. |内群体，内部群体 |A social group to which a person psychologically identifies as being a member.  In-groups are characterized by a sense of belonging, loyalty, and mutual support among members.  They often contrast with out-groups, which are groups to which a person does not belong and may even feel hostility towards.|例句：The research showed a strong bias towards the in-group, with members consistently rating in-group members more favorably than out-group members.  这项研究表明，对内群体的偏见很强，成员始终对内群体成员的评价高于外群体成员。|近义词：inner circle, clique, insiders|反义词：out-groups
