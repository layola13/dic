enactor:/ɪˈnæktər/ | n. |制定者；颁布者；实施者 |A person or thing that enacts or puts something into effect, especially a law.  |The enactor of the new tax law faced much criticism. 新税法的制定者面临着许多批评。|近义词：legislator, implementer, executor |反义词：repealer, abolisher


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government enacts new laws every year. = 政府每年都制定新的法律。
He enacts the role of Hamlet with great passion. = 他充满激情地扮演哈姆雷特。


一般过去时:
Congress enacted the Civil Rights Act in 1964. = 国会在1964年通过了民权法案。
She enacted a scene from Shakespeare’s play. = 她演绎了莎士比亚戏剧中的一个场景。


现在完成时:
They have enacted several important reforms. = 他们已经实施了几项重要的改革。
The company has enacted a new code of conduct.  = 公司已经制定了一套新的行为准则。


过去完成时:
The court had enacted a ruling before the appeal. = 在上诉之前，法院已经做出了裁决。
Before the performance, they had enacted the scene many times. = 演出前，他们排练过很多次这个场景。


将来时:
The legislature will enact a new environmental protection law next year. = 立法机关将在明年制定一项新的环境保护法。
She will enact the role of Juliet in the upcoming play. = 她将在即将上演的戏剧中扮演朱丽叶。


虚拟语气:
If the president enacted this bill, it would cause controversy. = 如果总统签署这项法案，将会引起争议。
If they had enacted the plan earlier, the results would have been better. = 如果他们早些实施这项计划，结果会更好。


被动语态:
The law was enacted last month. = 这项法律是上个月颁布的。
The play was enacted with great skill. = 这出戏表演得很精彩。


疑问句:
Who enacted this law? = 谁制定了这项法律？
Who will enact the main role in the movie? = 谁将在电影中扮演主角？


否定句:
The government did not enact the proposed legislation. = 政府没有颁布拟议的法案。
He did not enact the character convincingly. = 他没有令人信服地扮演这个角色。
