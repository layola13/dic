hydriodide:/ˌhaɪdraɪˈaɪdaɪd/| n. |氢碘化物|A binary compound of iodine with another element or radical, especially a salt of hydroiodic acid.  Hydriodides are typically formed by the reaction of hydroiodic acid with a base or metal. They are often used in medicine and as reagents in chemical synthesis. |Example: Potassium hydriodide is a common source of iodide ions.  钾氢碘化物是碘离子的常见来源。|近义词：iodide (in some contexts)|反义词：N/A
