rambling:/ˈræmblɪŋ/| adj. | 冗长的；漫无目的的；杂乱无章的 | characterized by rambling; wandering from one subject to another; lengthy and unfocused;  unstructured and disorganized. |


例句：

His speech was rambling and difficult to follow. 他的演讲冗长且难以理解。

The rambling old house needed extensive repairs. 这座破旧的老房子需要大修。


近义词：wandering, meandering, rambling, discursive, diffuse, digressive


反义词：concise, succinct, focused, organized, coherent


词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The speaker is rambling again.  = 演讲者又开始跑题了。
His thoughts are rambling. = 他的思绪很混乱。


一般过去时:
The essay rambled and lacked focus. = 这篇文章冗长且缺乏重点。
He rambled on for hours. = 他滔滔不绝地讲了几个小时。


现在完成时:
She has rambled on for so long that everyone is bored. = 她已经说了很久，大家都厌烦了。
The conversation has rambled far from the original topic. = 谈话已经偏离了最初的话题。


过去完成时:
He had rambled for so long that he had lost his train of thought. = 他已经说了很久，以至于他忘记了自己要说的话。
They had rambled through the woods for hours before finding their way back. = 他们在树林里漫无目的地走了几个小时才找到回家的路。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish he wouldn't ramble so much. = 我希望他不要再那么罗嗦了。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The presentation wasn't rambling; it was well-structured. = 演示文稿并不冗长；它结构良好。
