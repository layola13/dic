pull:/pʊl/|vt. vi. n.|拉，拖，拔，吸引|to exert force on (something) so as to cause it to move towards oneself; to draw or haul (something) along a surface. |例句：He pulled the door open. 他拉开了门。

例句：The train pulled into the station. 火车进站了。

近义词：drag, haul, tug, attract|反义词：push


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I pull the weeds out of the garden every week. = 我每周都把花园里的杂草拔掉。
She pulls her hair back in a ponytail. = 她把头发扎成马尾。

一般过去时:
He pulled the trigger and fired the gun. = 他扣动了扳机，开枪了。
They pulled the heavy box across the floor. = 他们把沉重的箱子拖过地板。

现在完成时:
I have pulled all the weeds. = 我已经把所有的杂草都拔掉了。
She has pulled herself up from poverty. = 她已经摆脱了贫困。

过去完成时:
He had pulled the car over to the side of the road before the accident happened. = 事故发生前，他已经把车停在了路边。
They had pulled all-nighters to finish the project. = 为了完成项目，他们熬了好几个通宵。

将来时:
I will pull the car over to the side of the road if I see a police car. = 如果我看到警车，我会把车停在路边。
They will pull the heavy box up the stairs. = 他们会把沉重的箱子搬上楼梯。

虚拟语气:
If I pulled that lever, what would happen? = 如果我拉动那个杠杆，会发生什么？
I wish I could pull myself together. = 我希望我能振作起来。

被动语态:
The rope was pulled taut. = 绳子被拉紧了。
The car was pulled over by the police. = 车子被警察拦下了。

疑问句:
Can you pull this thorn out? = 你能把这根刺拔出来吗？
Will you pull the chair closer? = 你能把椅子拉近点吗？

否定句:
He couldn't pull the car out of the mud. = 他无法把车从泥里拉出来。
She didn't pull her weight on the project. = 她在这个项目中没有尽力。


PHRASE->
pull over: 靠边停车
pull through: 渡过难关
pull out: 撤出；拔出
pull off: 成功完成
pull a fast one: 耍花招
