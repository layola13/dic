seized: /siːzd/ | vt. | 抓住；夺取；查封；控制；突然感到（某种强烈情绪） | To take hold of suddenly and forcibly; to take possession of by force or authority; to affect suddenly and powerfully. | 近义词：grabbed, confiscated, captured, arrested | 反义词：released, relinquished, liberated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The police seize illegal drugs. = 警察查获非法毒品。
She seizes every opportunity to learn. = 她抓住每一个学习的机会。

一般过去时:
He seized the knife from the attacker. = 他从袭击者手中夺过刀子。
The government seized control of the bank. = 政府控制了该银行。

现在完成时:
The authorities have seized his assets. = 当局已查封了他的资产。
I have seized the chance to travel. = 我抓住了旅行的机会。


过去完成时:
They had seized the opportunity before anyone else did. = 他们在其他人之前抓住了机会。
The police had seized the evidence before the trial. = 警察在审判前已查获了证据。


将来时:
The police will seize the contraband. = 警察将查获走私货物。
He will seize the moment to confess his love. = 他将抓住时机表白他的爱意。


虚拟语气:
If I had seized the opportunity, I would have been successful. = 如果我抓住了机会，我就会成功。
I wish I had seized the chance to talk to him. = 我真希望我抓住了机会和他谈谈。


被动语态:
The stolen goods were seized by the police. = 赃物被警方查获。
His property was seized by the tax authorities. = 他的财产被税务机关查封。


疑问句:
Did the police seize anything suspicious? = 警察查获了什么可疑的东西吗？
Has the company seized the market share? = 公司是否已经占据了市场份额？


否定句:
The police didn't seize any weapons. = 警察没有查获任何武器。
He didn't seize the chance to explain. = 他没有抓住机会解释。


PHRASE->
seize control = 控制
seize the opportunity = 抓住机会
seize power = 夺取政权
seize upon = 抓住；利用
seize the initiative = 掌握主动权

词源:
From Old French *seisir, from Vulgar Latin *exquirere (from Latin ex- "out" + quaerere "to seek"), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kʷēr- "to turn, bend, seek".  The semantic shift from "to seek" to "to seize" likely involved the idea of actively pursuing and obtaining something.
