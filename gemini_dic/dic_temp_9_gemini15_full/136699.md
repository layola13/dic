nonsegmented: /ˌnɒnˈseɡmɛntɪd/ | adj. | 非分节的，不分段的 | Not divided into segments or sections.  This term is often used in linguistics to describe languages or words that lack clear morphological boundaries between morphemes (the smallest units of meaning).  It can also be used more broadly to describe things that are not divided into parts. | Example:  The nonsegmented nature of the language made it difficult to analyze.  非分节的语言性质使得对其进行分析变得困难。|近义词: undivided, unsegmented, whole | 反义词: segmented, divided, articulated
