dalton:/ˈdɔːltən/| n. |道尔顿；道尔顿单位|A dalton (symbol: Da) is a unit of mass used in biochemistry and molecular biology.  It is defined as one twelfth the mass of an unbound neutral atom of carbon-12.  It is approximately equal to the mass of one proton or one neutron.  The term is often used interchangeably with the atomic mass unit (amu).|例句：The protein has a molecular weight of 50,000 daltons.  这种蛋白质的分子量为50000道尔顿。|近义词：atomic mass unit (amu)|反义词:|
