facetiously:/fəˈsiːʃəsli/ | adj. |  戏谑地；开玩笑地；滑稽地 |  In a joking or playful manner, typically intending to be humorous or amusing, but sometimes seeming frivolous or disrespectful.  It implies a lack of seriousness. |


例句：

He facetiously suggested we all go skydiving.  = 他开玩笑地建议我们都去跳伞。

She facetiously remarked on the politician's questionable ethics. = 她戏谑地评论了这位政治家有问题的道德操守。


近义词：jokingly, humorously, playfully, ironically, satirically


反义词：seriously, solemnly, gravely, earnestly


词源:

From facetious (adjective), ultimately from Latin *facetiosus, from facetia "wit, pleasantry," related to facetus "witty, clever," from facere "to do, make."  The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective into an adverb.


USAGE->
(Since facetiously is an adverb, it modifies verbs, not nouns or other adverbs.  There is no verb conjugation to illustrate.)

Example sentences demonstrating its use in various contexts:

He spoke facetiously, intending to lighten the mood. = 他说得很开玩笑，是为了缓和气氛。

She laughed facetiously at his clumsy attempt. = 她对他的笨拙尝试哈哈大笑。

The news was delivered facetiously, though the situation was serious. = 消息是开玩笑说的，尽管情况很严重。


PHRASE->

There are no common idiomatic phrases directly incorporating "facetiously."  Its usage is primarily as a standalone adverb modifying a verb.
