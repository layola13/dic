knockabout:/ˈnɒkəbaʊt/| n. adj.|  嬉闹；打闹；临时拼凑的；不经久耐用的 |A knockabout is a rough and tumble type of play or performance, often characterized by slapstick humor and physical comedy.  It can also describe something that is makeshift, flimsy, or not built to last. |例句：The children engaged in a boisterous knockabout in the park. 孩子们在公园里进行了一场喧闹的打闹。

The old car was a real knockabout; it was constantly breaking down. 那辆旧车真是个破烂货，总是坏掉。|近义词：roughhouse, tussle, makeshift, ramshackle |反义词：refined, elegant, durable, sturdy
