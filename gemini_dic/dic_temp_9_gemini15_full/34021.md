citizens:/ˈsɪtɪzənz/ | n. | 公民，市民 | Citizens are the people who live in a particular city or country and have the rights and responsibilities of that place.  Citizens have the right to vote and participate in their government. | 例句：The citizens protested against the new law.  市民抗议新法律。

例句：All citizens are equal before the law. 所有公民在法律面前一律平等。


近义词：inhabitants, residents, nationals | 反义词：aliens, foreigners, non-residents | 词性：名词


词源:  The word "citizen" comes from the Old French word "citeain," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "civis," meaning "citizen" or "inhabitant of a city."  The suffix "-zen" reflects a later development in the French language.
