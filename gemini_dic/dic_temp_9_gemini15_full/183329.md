reprobating:/ˈrɛprəˌbeɪtɪŋ/| v.|谴责，斥责，反对|Expressing strong disapproval of; condemning.  The act of reprobating involves strongly criticizing or condemning something or someone. It implies a sense of moral judgment and often carries a connotation of harshness or severity.|例句：The teacher reprobated the student's disruptive behavior. 老师谴责了学生扰乱课堂的行为。

例句：The preacher reprobated the sins of the congregation. 牧师谴责了会众的罪过。


近义词：condemning, censuring, denouncing, disapproving, blaming

反义词：approving, praising, commending, endorsing


词性:动词

词源:
From Late Middle English *reprobaten, from Old French reprover, from Latin reprobare (“to disapprove, reject”), from re- (“again”) + probare (“to prove, test”).  The word's history reflects its meaning of strong disapproval, stemming from a process of testing or proving something to be wrong or unacceptable.
