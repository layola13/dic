paleontography:/ˌpeɪliɒnˈtɒɡrəfi/| n. |古生物地理学；古生物图谱|The study of the geographical distribution of fossils.  It involves mapping the locations of fossil finds to understand past environments and the evolution of life.  Paleontography helps reconstruct ancient ecosystems and track the movement of continents and species over time. | The paleontography of the region revealed a surprising diversity of marine life in the Jurassic period.  该地区的古生物地理学揭示了侏罗纪时期令人惊讶的海洋生物多样性。|近义词：paleobiogeography, fossil geography|反义词：无
