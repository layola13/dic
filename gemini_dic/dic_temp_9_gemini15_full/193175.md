seder:/ˈseɪdər/| n.|逾越节晚餐|The Seder is a Jewish ritual dinner that is held on the first two nights of Passover (or one night in some Reform Jewish communities). It's a symbolic reenactment of the Exodus story, where the Israelites were freed from slavery in Egypt.  The Seder involves the reading of the Haggadah, a text that tells the story of the Exodus, and the consumption of specific foods that symbolize different aspects of the story and the Jewish experience.|Example: We attended a traditional Seder with our family last Passover.  我们去年逾越节和家人一起参加了一个传统的逾越节晚餐。|近义词：Passover meal, ritual dinner|反义词：无
