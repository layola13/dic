idler:/ˈaɪdlər/| n. |懒汉；游手好闲的人；怠惰者；（机械）惰轮|A person who is lazy and avoids work.  An idler is someone who spends their time doing nothing useful or productive. In mechanics, an idler is a wheel or pulley that changes the direction of a belt or chain. |例句：He's become a complete idler since he retired. 他退休后成了一个十足的懒汉。

例句：The idler wheel allows the belt to turn in the opposite direction. 惰轮使皮带能够反向旋转。

近义词：loafer, sluggard, drone, parasite

反义词：worker, toiler, striver


词源:
The word "idler" comes from the Middle English word "idlere," meaning "lazy person," which in turn is derived from the Old English word "īdel," meaning "empty," "idle," or "useless."  The mechanical sense developed later, borrowing the sense of a non-productive but necessary component.
