half-Elizabethan: /ˌhɑːf ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən/ | adj. | 半伊丽莎白式 |  Descriptive of a style of architecture, literature, or other art form that blends elements of the Elizabethan era (roughly 1558-1603) with those of other periods, often later ones.  It suggests an incomplete or partial adoption of Elizabethan features.  It's not a strictly defined term and its meaning depends heavily on context. | The play exhibited a half-Elizabethan style, incorporating some of the era's poetic language but also modern dramatic techniques. | 近义词: partially Elizabethan, neo-Elizabethan (but with subtle differences in connotation) | 反义词: purely Elizabethan, entirely modern |
