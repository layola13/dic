gliadin:/ˈɡliːədɪn/| n. |麦醇溶蛋白|A group of proteins found in wheat flour that are responsible for the elasticity and extensibility of dough.  Gliadins are a component of gluten, along with glutenins.  They are also considered a major allergen in people with celiac disease.|Example:  The high gliadin content of this flour makes it ideal for bread baking. = 这种面粉的高麦醇溶蛋白含量使其非常适合烘焙面包。|近义词:gluten protein|反义词:None specifically, but substances lacking gluten proteins could be considered antonyms in context.
