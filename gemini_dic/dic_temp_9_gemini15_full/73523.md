foreshortening:/fɔːrˈʃɔːrt(ə)nɪŋ/| n.|透视缩短；近景透视；近大远小|The representation of an object or figure in a picture so that it appears shorter than it actually is because the object is angled away from the viewer.  This is a technique used to create the illusion of depth and three-dimensionality on a two-dimensional surface.|例句：The artist skillfully used foreshortening to create a sense of depth in the painting.  画家巧妙地运用透视缩短法在绘画中营造出深度的感觉。

The strong foreshortening of the figure made it appear to be rushing towards the viewer.  人物强烈的透视缩短效果使其看起来正冲向观看者。|近义词：perspective, shortening, compression|反义词:elongation, extension
