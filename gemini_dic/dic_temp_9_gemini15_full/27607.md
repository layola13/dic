cancellated: /ˈkænsəleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 取消的，作废的 |  Cancelled refers to something that has been officially called off or declared void.  It implies a formal decision to prevent something from happening. | Example: The flight was cancelled due to bad weather.  航班因恶劣天气而取消。  The meeting has been cancelled. 会议已被取消。 | 近义词：nullified, void, revoked, annulled | 反义词：confirmed, approved, validated |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The event is cancelled. = 活动取消了。
This ticket is cancelled. = 这张票作废了。

一般过去时:
They cancelled the order. = 他们取消了订单。
The game was cancelled because of rain. = 比赛因为下雨被取消了。

现在完成时:
The meeting has been cancelled. = 会议已经取消了。
My flight has been cancelled, so I'm stuck here. = 我的航班被取消了，所以我被困在这里了。


过去完成时:
They had cancelled the trip before we arrived. =在我们到达之前，他们已经取消了旅行。
The show had been cancelled due to low ticket sales. =演出因为门票销售不佳而被取消了。


将来时:
They will cancel the project if funding is insufficient. =如果资金不足，他们将取消该项目。
The concert may be cancelled if the artist falls ill. =如果艺术家生病，音乐会可能会被取消。


虚拟语气:
If they had cancelled the order earlier, we wouldn't have wasted so much time. =如果他们早点取消订单，我们就不会浪费那么多时间了。
I wish they had cancelled the meeting.  =我希望他们取消了会议。


被动语态:
The order was cancelled by the customer. = 订单被客户取消了。
The flight was cancelled because of the storm. =航班因为暴风雨而被取消了。


疑问句:
Has the meeting been cancelled? = 会议取消了吗？
Is the flight cancelled? = 航班取消了吗？


否定句:
The concert was not cancelled. = 音乐会没有取消。
The meeting has not been cancelled yet. = 会议还没有取消。
