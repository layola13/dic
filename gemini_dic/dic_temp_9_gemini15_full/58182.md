drowsed:/draʊzd/| v.|昏昏欲睡；打瞌睡；| to feel sleepy and tired, often causing you to fall asleep|The warm fire made me drowsy.|近义词：dozed, slumbered, nodded off|反义词：awake, alert|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drowses in the afternoon. = 他下午打瞌睡。
She often drowses during long lectures. = 她经常在冗长的讲座中打瞌睡。


一般过去时:
I drowsed off during the movie. = 我在看电影的时候睡着了。
He drowsed by the fireplace. = 他在壁炉旁打瞌睡。


现在完成时:
I have drowsed all afternoon. = 我整个下午都在打瞌睡。
She has never drowsed during a meeting before. = 她以前从未在会议上打瞌睡。


过去完成时:

被动语态:
(The verb "drowse" is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes a state of being rather than an action performed on someone or something.)
