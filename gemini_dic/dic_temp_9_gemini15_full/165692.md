premorse:/ˈpriːmɔːrs/| adj.| 预感；预兆；先兆|  Feeling or showing a sense of guilt or remorse before committing an act.  It suggests a preemptive feeling of guilt or regret, even before the action itself takes place.|  Example: He felt a premorse about lying to his parents, even before he opened his mouth.|近义词：foreboding, presentiment, misgiving|反义词：complacency, unconcern
