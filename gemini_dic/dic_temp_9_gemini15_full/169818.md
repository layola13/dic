proscript:/ˈproʊskrɪpt/| v.|禁止，剥夺…的权利；放逐|To prohibit or forbid something; to banish or exile someone.|The king proscribed the use of magic. 国王禁止使用魔法。|The dictator proscribed his political opponents. 独裁者放逐了他的政治对手。|近义词：ban, forbid, outlaw, exile|反义词：allow, permit, sanction


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The government proscribes the sale of illegal drugs. = 政府禁止销售非法毒品。
They proscribe certain activities in the park. = 他们在公园里禁止某些活动。


一般过去时:
The ancient Romans proscribed certain religious practices. = 古罗马人禁止某些宗教习俗。
The school proscribed the use of mobile phones during exams. = 学校在考试期间禁止使用手机。


现在完成时:
The authorities have proscribed several extremist groups. = 当局已经取缔了好几个极端组织。
They have proscribed the use of that chemical in food production. = 他们已经禁止在食品生产中使用那种化学物质。


过去完成时:
The council had proscribed the construction of high-rise buildings in that area. = 理事会此前已禁止在该地区建造高层建筑。
Before the war, they had proscribed the import of certain goods. = 在战争爆发前，他们已经禁止进口某些商品。


将来时:
The new law will proscribe the use of plastic bags. = 新法律将禁止使用塑料袋。
They will proscribe any form of violence at the protest. = 他们将在抗议活动中禁止任何形式的暴力。


虚拟语气:
If the king proscribed this activity, we would have to stop. = 如果国王禁止这项活动，我们就不得不停止。
I wish they hadn't proscribed my favorite book. = 我真希望他们没有禁止我最喜欢的书。


被动语态:
The use of fireworks was proscribed by the city council. = 城市议会禁止使用烟花。
Certain behaviors are proscribed by company policy. = 公司政策禁止某些行为。


疑问句:
Does the law proscribe the possession of firearms? = 法律是否禁止持有枪支？
What activities are proscribed in this area? = 在这个区域禁止哪些活动？


否定句:
The law does not proscribe free speech. = 法律并不禁止言论自由。
The school does not proscribe the use of dictionaries during exams. = 学校在考试期间并不禁止使用字典。
