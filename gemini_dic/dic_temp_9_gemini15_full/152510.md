payoffs:/ˈpeɪˌɔːfs/ | n. | 酬劳；回报；结果；（尤指）不正当利益 | The benefits, advantages, or rewards gained from a particular action, situation, or course of events.  This can include financial rewards, but also other positive outcomes.  It can also refer to the consequences, both positive and negative, of a particular action. |例句：The payoffs from investing in the stock market can be substantial.  投资股市的回报可能是巨大的。

The payoffs of hard work are often delayed.  努力工作的回报往往是滞后的。


近义词：rewards, benefits, returns, gains, consequences, outcomes | 反义词：losses, setbacks, penalties


词源:The word "payoff" comes from the verb "pay off," which means to pay someone or something completely, to settle a debt or to reward someone.  The noun form emerged from this meaning.  The "off" part is likely an adverbial particle, emphasizing the completeness or finality of the payment.
