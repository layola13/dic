prepracticing: /ˌpriːˈpræktɪsɪŋ/ | v. | 预先练习；提前练习 |  The act of practicing something beforehand.  It implies preparation and rehearsal before the actual performance or event.  This is not a standard dictionary word, but rather a newly coined word, combining "pre-" (before) and "practicing". | 例句：She spent hours prepracticing her presentation to ensure a smooth delivery. = 她花了几个小时预先练习她的演讲，以确保顺利进行。

She was prepracticing her lines for the play, hoping to reduce stage fright. = 她正在预先练习她的台词，希望能减少怯场。

近义词：rehearsing, preparing, drilling | 反义词：improvising, winging it | 词性：动词
