moor:/mʊər/|n. v.|荒野；沼泽；系泊；使停泊|an area of open uncultivated land, especially in upland regions, typically covered with heather or other low-growing vegetation.|The moor was covered in purple heather.  这片荒野覆盖着紫色的石南花。


The sheep grazed peacefully on the moor.  羊群平静地在这片荒野上吃草。


近义词：heath, bog, wasteland|反义词：cultivated land, city


系泊；使停泊|to secure (a ship, boat, etc.) in a particular place, typically by using ropes or anchors.|He moored his boat to the pier. 他把船系泊在码头上。


We moored the yacht for the night. 我们把游艇系泊了一夜。


词源: 古英语 *mōr  "荒野，沼泽"，与古高地德语 *mūr，古诺尔斯语 *mōr "沼泽"，以及古弗里西亚语 *mōr  "沼泽"相关联。  其词源可能与原始日耳曼语 *mōr-  "沼泽，黑暗的湿地"有关。
