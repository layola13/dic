parataxes:/ˈpærəˌtæksɪs/| n. |并列句法；并列结构 |The grammatical arrangement of clauses one after another, without subordination; coordination.  Parataxis is a rhetorical device used to create a sense of immediacy, speed, or urgency. It can also be used to create a sense of chaos or disorder.  |例句：The sun shone, the birds sang, the flowers bloomed. (阳光灿烂，鸟儿歌唱，鲜花盛开。)

The dog barked, the cat hissed, the baby cried. (狗吠叫，猫嘶嘶作响，婴儿哭泣。)

|近义词：coordination, juxtaposition |反义词：hypotaxis
