quasi-increased:/ˈkwɑːzaɪɪnˈkriːst/| adj.|准增加的，近似增加的 |  Describes a situation where a value or quantity shows a slight or seemingly insignificant increase, or an increase that doesn't fully meet the criteria of a true increase according to a strict definition.  It often implies a subtle or marginal growth, potentially lacking the consistency or significance of a genuine increase. | The company's profits showed a quasi-increased compared to last year, though the actual numbers were only marginally higher. 公司的利润与去年相比略有增加，尽管实际数字只略高一些。| 近义词：slightly increased, marginally increased, nominally increased | 反义词：decreased, significantly decreased
