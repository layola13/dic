drawled:/drɔːld/ | v. | 拉长声音说，慢吞吞地说 | To speak slowly with prolonged vowels, often in a lazy or relaxed manner.  | He drawled his answer, stretching out the syllables. 他慢吞吞地回答，拖长了音节。 |近义词：spoke slowly, drawled out,  slurred (in some contexts) | 反义词：rushed, blurted


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drawls his words. = 他拉长声音说话。
She always drawls when she's tired. = 她累了的时候总是慢吞吞地说话。

一般过去时:
He drawled a response. = 他慢吞吞地回答。
She drawled out the story. = 她慢悠悠地讲着故事。

现在完成时:
He has drawled through the entire meeting. = 他整个会议都在慢吞吞地说话。
She has drawled her way through the explanation. = 她慢吞吞地解释了一遍。

过去完成时:
He had drawled the news before anyone else. = 他在其他人之前慢吞吞地宣布了这个消息。
She had drawled the instructions so slowly that we missed some of it. = 她把指示说得那么慢，以至于我们错过了一些内容。

将来时:
He will drawl his way through the speech. = 他将慢吞吞地发表演讲。
She will likely drawl her answers to the questions. = 她可能会慢吞吞地回答问题。

虚拟语气:
If he were to speak, he would drawl. = 如果他要说话，他会慢吞吞地说。
I wish she wouldn't drawl so much. = 我希望她不要说得那么慢。

被动语态: (Not commonly used in passive voice)  The words were drawled out. =  字句被拖长了音调地说出。(罕见用法)
The information was drawled out slowly and deliberately. = 信息被慢吞吞、故意地拖长了音调地说出。(罕见用法)


疑问句:
Does he always drawl like that? = 他总是那样慢吞吞地说话吗？
Did she drawl her words on purpose? = 她故意拉长声音说话吗？


否定句:
He didn't drawl; he spoke clearly and quickly. = 他没有慢吞吞地说；他说话清晰而迅速。
She didn't drawl her words, but spoke with a normal pace. = 她没有拉长声音说话，而是以正常的速度说话。


PHRASE->
drawl out a story = 慢悠悠地讲故事
drawl an answer = 慢吞吞地回答
drawl one's words = 拉长声音说话
drawl something out = 拖长声音说某事
drawl a greeting = 慢吞吞地打招呼

词源:  The word "drawl" likely comes from an Old English word meaning "to drag" or "to draw out."  The connection to speech reflects the drawn-out quality of the vocalization.
