preadmonition:/ˌpriːædˈmɒnɪʃən/| n. |预兆，预警；先兆|A premonition is a feeling that something, typically something bad, will happen.  It's a warning or feeling of anticipation, often without a clear reason. |例句：She had a premonition of disaster. 她预感到将有灾难发生。|例句：His uneasy feeling proved to be a premonition of the storm. 他不安的感觉证明是暴风雨的预兆。|近义词：foreboding, presentiment, premonition|反义词：certainty, assurance|名词
