dunny:/ˈdʌni/| n.|厕所，盥洗室|A dunny is a colloquial term, primarily used in Australia and New Zealand, for a toilet.  It's a more informal and sometimes humorous word than "toilet" or "lavatory".|  例句：Where's the dunny? = 厕所在哪儿？

The old house only had an outside dunny. = 那栋旧房子只有一个外面的厕所。


近义词：toilet, lavatory, loo, restroom, bathroom|反义词：无|词性:名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun, and doesn't have verb forms or other grammatical variations in the same way as a verb like "to be" or "to go".  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  Its use is restricted to noun phrases.

词源：
The etymology of "dunny" is uncertain, but it's believed to be derived from either a shortening of "dunnekin," an older Scottish word for a privy or outdoor toilet, or possibly from the word "dung."  The exact origin remains debated among linguists.
