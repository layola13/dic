entend:/ɪnˈtɛnd/| vt.|打算，意图；想要；意味着|to have as one's purpose or intention; to mean or signify|例句：I intend to go to the park tomorrow. =我打算明天去公园。
I intended to call you, but I forgot. =我本来打算给你打电话，但我忘了。|近义词：plan, purpose, aim, mean|反义词：neglect, ignore.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I intend to finish this project by Friday. = 我打算在周五之前完成这个项目。
She intends to travel to Europe next year. = 她打算明年去欧洲旅行。

一般过去时:
He intended to buy a new car, but he changed his mind. = 他本来打算买一辆新车，但他改变了主意。
They intended to go to the beach, but it rained. = 他们本来打算去海滩，但是下雨了。

现在完成时:
I have intended to write to her, but I haven't had the time. = 我一直打算给她写信，但我一直没时间。
She has intended to quit her job for months, but she hasn't yet. = 她几个月来一直打算辞职，但还没有。

过去完成时:
I had intended to leave earlier, but I was delayed. = 我本来打算早点离开，但我被耽搁了。
They had intended to meet at the cafe, but he was late. = 他们本来打算在咖啡馆见面，但他迟到了。

将来时:
I will intend to help you whenever you need it. = 我将来会尽力帮助你，无论何时你需要。
She will intend to continue her studies after graduation. = 她毕业后打算继续深造。


虚拟语气:
I would intend to help you if I could. = 如果我能的话，我会打算帮助你。
If I had intended to go, I would have bought a ticket. = 如果我打算去，我就应该买票了。

被动语态:
It was intended that the project would be completed by the end of the year. = 项目原本计划在年底前完成。
The meeting was intended to address the concerns of the employees. = 会议旨在解决员工的担忧。

疑问句:
Do you intend to apply for the job? = 你打算申请这份工作吗？
What do you intend to do with the money? = 你打算怎么用这笔钱？

否定句:
I do not intend to participate in the discussion. = 我不打算参与讨论。
He did not intend to hurt her feelings. = 他并非有意伤害她的感情。


PHRASE->
intend to do sth = 打算做某事
be intended for = 旨在为...
with the intention of = 带着...的意图
have no intention of = 没有...的打算
intend to say = 打算说
