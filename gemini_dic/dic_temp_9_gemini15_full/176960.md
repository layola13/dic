reallocated:/ˌriːˈæləkeɪtɪd/ | v. |重新分配，重新配置 | To allocate (resources, assets, etc.) again or differently.  This often implies a previous allocation has been changed or that resources are being moved to a new purpose. | 例句：The company reallocated funds from marketing to research and development. 公司将资金从市场营销重新分配到研发部门。

The budget was reallocated to address the unexpected emergency. 预算被重新分配以应对突发的紧急情况。


近义词：reassign, redistribute, reallot | 反义词：allocate (in the original sense), retain


USAGE->

The government reallocates resources annually. 政府每年重新分配资源。
They reallocate funds based on need. 他们根据需要重新分配资金。


一般过去时:
The company reallocated its staff last quarter. 公司上个季度重新分配了员工。

词源:
The word "reallocate" is formed from the prefix "re-" (again) and the verb "allocate" (to distribute).  "Allocate" itself comes from the Latin word "allocare," meaning "to place, assign, or allot."  Therefore, "reallocate" literally means "to allocate again" or "to assign anew."
