illustrator: /ˈɪləstreɪtər/ | n. | 插图画家，绘图员 | A person who makes illustrations, especially for books.  |  The illustrator brought the children's story to life with her vibrant drawings.  这位插画家用她生动的图画使儿童故事栩栩如生。 | artist, draftsman, designer | photographer, writer | noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He illustrates children's books. 他为儿童书籍绘制插图。
She illustrates her points with examples. 她用例子来说明她的观点。


一般过去时:
He illustrated the novel with stunning images. 他用令人惊叹的图像为小说配图。
She illustrated the lecture with slides. 她用幻灯片为讲座配图。


现在完成时:
He has illustrated several best-selling novels. 他已经为几部畅销小说绘制插图。
She has illustrated the importance of education. 她已经阐明了教育的重要性。


过去完成时:
He had illustrated the book before the deadline. 他在截止日期前完成了这本书的插图。
She had illustrated her point before the argument ended.  在争论结束之前，她已经阐明了她的观点。


将来时:
He will illustrate the next chapter tomorrow. 他明天将绘制下一章的插图。
She will illustrate the concept with a diagram. 她将用图表来说明这个概念。


虚拟语气:
If he were an illustrator, he would have illustrated it better. 如果他是一位插画家，他会画得更好。
If she had illustrated the story, it would have been more appealing. 如果她为这个故事配图，它会更吸引人。


被动语态:
The book was illustrated by a famous artist. 这本书是由一位著名艺术家绘制插图的。
The importance of the topic was illustrated by the statistics.  这个主题的重要性由统计数据得到了说明。


疑问句:
Who illustrated this book? 谁为这本书绘制插图？
Does she illustrate her own books? 她自己为自己的书配图吗？


否定句:
He didn't illustrate the story properly. 他没有恰当地为故事配图。
She didn't illustrate her point clearly enough. 她没有足够清晰地说明她的观点。
