legalism:/ˈliːɡəlɪzəm/| n.|法律主义；法治主义；重形式轻实质|The strict and literal observance of the law, often to the neglect of its spirit or intent.  It can also refer to a political philosophy emphasizing strict adherence to law and legal codes. |例句：The judge's decision was criticized for its excessive legalism, ignoring the broader context of the case.  法官的判决因其过分的法律主义而受到批评，忽略了案件的更广泛背景。

例句：Blind adherence to legalism can hinder justice.  盲目遵守法律主义会阻碍正义。


近义词：formalism, rule-worship, literalism|反义词：equity, fairness, pragmatism

词源:  Derived from "legal" (relating to law) + "-ism" (a suffix indicating a doctrine, system, or practice).  The term emerged in the context of discussions about law, governance, and philosophy, particularly in ancient China and later Western legal thought.
