dihedron:/ˈdaɪˌhiːdrən/| n. |二面角；二面体|A dihedron is a geometric solid formed by two intersecting planes.  It is essentially an angle formed by two planes meeting at a line.  The dihedral angle is the angle between the two planes. |Example:  The dihedral angle between two faces of a cube is 90 degrees.  二面角是立方体两个面之间的夹角为90度。|近义词：angle (in some contexts)|反义词：None.


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "hello."  The grammatical usage focuses on how it is used in sentences.


PHRASE->
