obstructing: /əbˈstrʌktɪŋ/ | v. | 阻塞；妨碍；阻挠 |  The act of hindering or preventing movement, progress, or access. It implies a deliberate or unintentional act that creates an impediment. |

例句：

The fallen tree was obstructing the road. = 倒下的树木阻塞了道路。

The protesters were obstructing traffic. = 抗议者们正在阻碍交通。


近义词：impeding, hindering, blocking, hindering, obstructing, barricading


反义词：aiding, assisting, facilitating, clearing


USAGE->

They are obstructing the investigation. = 他们正在妨碍调查。

He obstructed justice by lying to the police. = 他对警察撒谎，妨碍了司法公正。

The snowstorm obstructed travel for days. = 暴风雪阻碍了多日的旅行。


现在完成时:

The heavy rain has obstructed our plans for a picnic. = 大雨打乱了我们野餐的计划。


过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish they hadn't obstructed my progress. = 我真希望他们没有阻碍我的进步。

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:

From Old French *obstruire, from Latin ob- "against" + struere "to build, pile up".  The sense development is from "to build up against" to "to block" or "obstruct".
