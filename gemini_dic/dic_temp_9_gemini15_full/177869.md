recess:/rɪˈsɛs/| n. v.| 休息；凹处；壁龛；休会；暂停 | A period of time when work or school is stopped for a short time; a small hollow or space in a surface; a niche in a wall; a temporary suspension of proceedings. |例句：The students have a recess after lunch.  学生们午饭后有休息时间。 The recess in the rock formed a shelter.  岩石上的凹处形成一个庇护所。|近义词：break, intermission, recess, interval, pause |反义词：continuation, resumption


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The school has a recess every afternoon. = 学校每天下午都有休息时间。
The judge orders a recess in the trial. = 法官下令休庭。


一般过去时:
We had a recess during the meeting. = 我们在会议期间休息了一下。
The court recessed until the next morning. = 法庭休庭至次日早晨。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:
They had already had a recess before the announcement. = 他们在宣布之前已经休息过了。
The case had recessed for the day before the jury arrived. = 在陪审团到达之前，案件已经休庭了。


将来时:
We will have a recess after the presentation. = 我们将在演示结束后休息。
The court will recess until tomorrow. = 法庭将休庭至明天。


虚拟语气:
If we had a recess, we would be more productive. = 如果我们有休息时间，我们会更有生产力。
I wish we could have a recess right now. = 我希望我们现在就能休息一下。


被动语态:

否定句:
