mortality:/mɔːˈtæləti/| n. |死亡率；必死性；死亡；死亡人数|The state of being mortal; death rate; the number of deaths in a particular time or place.  |例句：The mortality rate from this disease is high. 这疾病的死亡率很高。|例句：He accepted the mortality of all living things. 他接受了所有生物都会死亡的事实。|近义词：death rate, fatality rate, demise, mortality rate|反义词：immortality, immortality

一般现在时:
The mortality rate varies depending on the age group. = 死亡率因年龄段而异。
High mortality is a serious concern. = 高死亡率是一个严重的问题。


一般过去时:
The mortality rate from the flu was unusually high last winter. = 去年冬天流感的死亡率异常高。
Infant mortality decreased significantly in the past century. = 上个世纪婴儿死亡率显著下降。


现在完成时:
Mortality rates have improved dramatically thanks to medical advances. = 由于医学进步，死亡率已大大提高。
Many preventable deaths have been reduced due to improved public health. = 由于公共卫生状况的改善，许多可预防的死亡人数已减少。


过去完成时:

They had already experienced a high mortality rate among their troops. = 他们的军队中已经经历了很高的死亡率。


将来时:

Experts predict a rise in mortality due to climate change. = 专家预测气候变化将导致死亡率上升。
