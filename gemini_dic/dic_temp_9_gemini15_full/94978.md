in-the-chips:/ɪn ðə tʃɪps/| adj. |有钱的，富裕的|Having a lot of money; wealthy.|例句：After winning the lottery, he was really in the chips. 彩票中奖后，他真的很有钱了。|近义词：rich, wealthy, affluent, well-off |反义词：broke, penniless, impoverished


USAGE->
This phrase is primarily used as an adjective describing a person's financial state.  Therefore, grammatical tenses are applied to the subject of the sentence describing the person’s state, not directly to the phrase "in the chips."

Example sentences illustrating various tenses related to the state of being "in the chips":

一般现在时:
He is in the chips. = 他很有钱。
She is always in the chips. = 她总是很有钱。

一般过去时:
They were in the chips last year. = 他们去年很有钱。
He wasn't in the chips before he started his business. = 在他创业之前他并不富有。

现在完成时:
They have been in the chips for years. = 他们多年来一直很富有。
She has suddenly become in the chips. = 她突然变得很有钱了。

过去完成时:
They had been in the chips before the market crash. = 在市场崩盘之前他们很有钱。
We hadn't been in the chips long before we lost it all. = 我们富裕没多久就倾家荡产了.


将来时:
He will be in the chips once his business takes off. = 一旦他的生意起飞，他就会很有钱。
They won't be in the chips for much longer if they keep spending like that. = 如果他们继续这样挥霍，他们不会富有太久了。

PHRASE->
There are no common multi-word phrases directly using "in the chips" as a component.  The phrase itself is self-contained.  However, we can illustrate how it could be used within a larger phrase:

right now, he's really in the chips = 他现在真的很有钱
still in the chips despite the recession = 尽管经济衰退，他仍然富有
once in the chips, always in the chips = 一旦富有，就永远富有
trying to stay in the chips = 努力保持富有
worried about not being in the chips anymore = 担心不再富有了

词源:
The phrase "in the chips" originates from poker.  In poker, "chips" are tokens used to represent money.  Therefore, "in the chips" means to have a large number of chips, and consequently a lot of money.  The figurative use transferred to everyday language to describe financial prosperity.
