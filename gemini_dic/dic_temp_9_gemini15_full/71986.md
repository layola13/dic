flinches:/flɪntʃ/| v.|畏缩，退缩，退避|To make a sudden, slight movement of the body as a reaction to surprise, fear, or pain.  To shrink back involuntarily, especially from something unpleasant or frightening.|例句：He flinched at the loud noise. 他被巨大的噪音吓了一跳。

He flinched when the doctor touched his injured arm.  医生触碰他受伤的手臂时，他畏缩了一下。


近义词：shrink, recoil, wince, cower|反义词：stand firm, confront, face
