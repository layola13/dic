plea-bargainer:/ˈpliː ˈbɑːrɡənər/ | n. |认罪协商者，认罪交易者 | A person who pleads guilty to a lesser charge in exchange for a more lenient sentence.  This individual negotiates a deal with the prosecution to avoid a trial and potentially a harsher penalty.  They often admit guilt to a less serious crime than originally charged to reduce their potential prison time or fines. | Example: The plea-bargainer accepted a reduced sentence in exchange for testifying against his accomplices.  认罪协商者为了指证同伙而接受了减刑。  | 近义词：defendant (被告), convict (罪犯) | 反义词：无  |  词性：名词
