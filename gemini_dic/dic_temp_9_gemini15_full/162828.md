precanvassed:/ˌpriːˈkænvəst/| v. |预先调查；预先征求意见 | To have canvassed (sought opinions or votes from) beforehand.  It implies a thorough and systematic effort to gather information or opinions before a major event or decision. | Example: The marketing team precanvassed the target audience to gauge their interest in the new product.  市场团队预先调查了目标受众，以衡量他们对新产品的兴趣。| 近义词：pre-poll, pre-survey,  sound out | 反义词：neglect, ignore
