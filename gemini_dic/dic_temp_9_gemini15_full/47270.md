de-anglicizes: /ˌdiːˈæŋɡlɪsaɪzɪz/ | v. | 去英语化；使摆脱英语的影响 | To remove or reduce the influence of English language or culture from something.  The process involves replacing English words, phrases, or cultural elements with those of a different language or culture. | 例句：The government is attempting to de-anglicize the national curriculum. = 政府正试图使国家课程摆脱英语的影响。

The movement seeks to de-anglicize the country's media landscape. = 这场运动旨在使国家的媒体环境摆脱英语的影响。


近义词：de-anglicise (British spelling),  indigenize, nationalize (in certain contexts) | 反义词：anglicize, anglicise


词性：动词

词源:  The word is a combination of the prefix "de-" (meaning "to remove or reverse") and "anglicize" (meaning "to make English in character or form").  "Anglicize" itself comes from "Angle," an Old English word referring to the Angles, one of the Germanic tribes that migrated to Britain.
