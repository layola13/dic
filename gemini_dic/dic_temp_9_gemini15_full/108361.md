lavishing:/ˈlævɪʃɪŋ/| v. |挥霍；浪费；慷慨给予 | to give someone or something a large amount of something, especially money or praise |例句：They lavished praise on the young artist.  他们对这位年轻的艺术家赞不绝口。|例句：She lavished money on clothes and jewellery. 她把钱挥霍在衣服和首饰上。|近义词：squandering, spending freely, showering |反义词：stinting, economizing, saving


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They lavish attention on their children. = 他们对孩子们关爱有加。
She lavishes praise on her students. = 她对学生赞赏有加。

一般过去时:
He lavished gifts on his girlfriend. = 他给女朋友送了很多礼物。
They lavished money on their new house. = 他们在新房子上花了大笔钱。

现在完成时:
She has lavished care on her garden. = 她精心照料她的花园。
They have lavished resources on this project. = 他们在这个项目上投入了大量资源。

过去完成时:
He had lavished attention on his work before the deadline. = 截止日期前，他全力以赴工作。
She had lavished praise on him before the ceremony. = 典礼前，她对他赞赏有加。

将来时:
They will lavish attention on the new baby. = 他们将悉心照料新生儿。
She will lavish gifts on her friends for their birthdays. = 她将为朋友们的生日准备丰厚的礼物。


被动语态:
The team was lavished with praise after their victory. = 团队在获胜后受到了热烈的赞扬。
The project was lavished with funding by the government. = 该项目得到了政府的大量资金支持。

疑问句:
Are they lavishing too much money on their vacation? = 他们度假是不是花太多钱了？
Is she lavishing enough time on her studies? = 她学习是不是花足够的时间？

否定句:
They did not lavish attention on the details. = 他们没有关注细节。
She did not lavish praise on his performance. = 她对他的表演并没有赞赏有加。
