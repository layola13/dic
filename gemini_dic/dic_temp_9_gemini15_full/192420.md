scrooges:/ˈskruːdʒɪz/| n.|吝啬鬼，守财奴|A scrooge is a person who is unwilling to spend money or give anything to anyone.  The term is derived from Ebenezer Scrooge, the miserly protagonist in Charles Dickens's *A Christmas Carol*.|例句：He's such a scrooge; he never tips the waiters.|近义词：miser, tightwad, cheapskate|反义词：generous, philanthropist|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a scrooge. = 他是一个吝啬鬼。
They are scrooges with their money. = 他们对钱很吝啬。


一般过去时:
He was a scrooge in his youth. = 他年轻时是个吝啬鬼。
She was a scrooge until she retired. = 她退休前一直很吝啬。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:
He had been a scrooge all his life. = 他一辈子都是个吝啬鬼。
They had been scrooges before they won the lottery. = 他们中彩票之前一直很吝啬。


将来时:
