discreditable: /dɪˈskredɪtəbl/ | adj. | 令人不齿的；丢脸的；败坏名誉的 |  Bringing or deserving disgrace or shame; dishonorable.  | The politician's actions were discreditable and led to his resignation.  这位政治家的行为令人不齿，导致他辞职。  | shameful, disgraceful, dishonorable, ignominious | honorable, respectable, reputable | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His behavior is discreditable. = 他的行为令人不齿。
That company's practices are discreditable. = 那家公司的做法令人不齿。

一般过去时:
Her actions were discreditable to her family. = 她的行为给她的家庭带来了耻辱。
The scandal was discreditable to the entire organization. = 这场丑闻给整个组织带来了耻辱。

现在完成时:
His lies have proved discreditable to his character. = 他的谎言证明了他的品格卑劣。
The revelations have been discreditable to the government. = 这些揭露对政府来说是可耻的。

过去完成时:
The evidence had already proven his actions discreditable. = 证据已经证明了他的行为令人不齿。
They had already done things that were discreditable to their profession. = 他们已经做了有损他们职业的事情。


将来时:
His future actions will likely be discreditable. = 他未来的行为很可能令人不齿。
Such behavior will be discreditable to the whole team. = 这种行为将会给整个团队带来耻辱。


虚拟语气:
If he continued this way, his behavior would be discreditable. = 如果他继续这样下去，他的行为将是令人不齿的。
I wish his actions hadn't been so discreditable. = 我真希望他的行为没有那么令人不齿。

被动语态:
His conduct was deemed discreditable by the board. = 他的行为被董事会认为是令人不齿的。
The affair was considered discreditable to the company's image. = 这件事被认为有损公司的形象。

疑问句:
Is his behavior really discreditable? = 他的行为真的令人不齿吗？
Are these accusations discreditable? = 这些指控令人不齿吗？

否定句:
His actions were not discreditable; they were simply misguided. = 他的行为并非令人不齿；只是方向错误。
The situation was not discreditable, just unfortunate. = 局面并非令人不齿，只是不幸。



PHRASE->
a discreditable act  一件令人不齿的行为
discreditable conduct 不光彩的行为
a discreditable record  一份令人不齿的记录
discreditable rumors  令人不齿的谣言
a discreditable association  令人不齿的交往


词源:
discredit (v.) + -able (adj. suffix)  discredit 来自于  dis- (否定前缀) + credit (信任，名誉)
