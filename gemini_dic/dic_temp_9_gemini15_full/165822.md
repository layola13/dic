preobviously:/ˌpriːˈɒbviəsli/| adv.|显而易见地，预料之中地|This word is not a standard English word. It's a blend of "pre" (meaning before or in advance) and "obviously" (meaning clearly or evidently). It suggests something was evident even before it happened or was explicitly stated.  The use is informal and might be considered humorous or ironic, depending on context.|例句：Preobviously, the cake was going to be delicious, given the ingredients.  显而易见地，考虑到食材，蛋糕一定很好吃。|近义词：clearly, evidently, obviously, predictably|反义词：unexpectedly, surprisingly, surprisingly
