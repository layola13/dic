hydriodate:/ˈhaɪdraɪoʊdeɪt/ | n. | 氢碘酸盐 | A salt of hydriodic acid.  Hydriodates are compounds containing the iodide ion (I⁻) bonded to a cation. They are often formed by the reaction of a metal hydroxide or oxide with hydriodic acid. | Example: Potassium iodide (KI) is a common hydriodate used in medicine and photography. 钾碘化物(KI)是一种常用的用于医药和摄影的氢碘酸盐。| 近义词：iodide (more common term) | 反义词：N/A
