minicomputer:/ˈmɪnɪˌkɒmpjuːtə(r)/|/ˈmɪnɪˌkɑːmpjuːtər/| n. |小型计算机 |A minicomputer is a mid-sized computer that is more powerful than a microcomputer but less powerful than a mainframe computer. They were commonly used in the 1960s and 70s for tasks requiring more processing power than a microcomputer could handle, such as scientific research and industrial control.  |Example: In the 1970s, many businesses relied on minicomputers for their data processing needs.  在20世纪70年代，许多企业依靠小型计算机来满足其数据处理需求。|近义词：mid-range computer, small computer |反义词：mainframe computer, supercomputer |名词

词源:
The word "minicomputer" is a compound word formed from "mini-" (meaning "small") and "computer".  The term emerged in the 1960s to describe computers that were smaller and less expensive than the large mainframe computers of the time, but still significantly more powerful than the early microcomputers.
