plausibility:/ˌplɔːzəˈbɪləti/ | n. |似是而非；可能性；可信性 | The plausibility of his story is questionable.  他的故事的可信度令人怀疑。|近义词：credibility, likelihood, probability |反义词：implausibility, unlikelihood

英文解析:The quality of being plausible; appearing true, reasonable, or probable.  It refers to something that seems believable or likely to be true, even if it might not actually be so.  The degree of plausibility depends on the context and the evidence available.

例句:

The scientist questioned the plausibility of the theory. 科学家质疑了该理论的可能性。

Despite the lack of evidence, the police found the suspect's alibi to be reasonably plausible. 尽管缺乏证据，警方还是认为嫌疑人的不在场证明相当可信。


词源:From Middle French *plausibilité, from Latin plausibilis ("deserving applause, praiseworthy"), from plaudere ("to applaud").
