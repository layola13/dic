psychodramas:/ˈsaɪkəʊˌdrɑːməz/| n. |心理剧；心理戏剧|A form of psychotherapy in which patients role-play situations to gain insight into their behavior and feelings.  It involves enacting real-life conflicts and emotional experiences in a safe and controlled therapeutic setting, often with the guidance of a therapist.|Example:  The therapist used psychodrama to help the patient confront their fear of public speaking.  心理治疗师用心理剧帮助病人克服其对公开演讲的恐惧。|近义词：role-playing therapy, group therapy|反义词：individual therapy (in some contexts)
