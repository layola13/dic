coachload:/ˈkoʊtʃloʊd/| n.|  满载乘客的汽车；一车人 | A coachload refers to the number of people that a coach (a large bus) can carry, often used to describe a large group of people travelling together.  |Example: A coachload of tourists arrived at the museum this morning.  今天早上有一车游客到达博物馆。|近义词：busload, group, throng |反义词:  a few, handful


USAGE->
一般现在时:
A coachload of students travels to the competition every year. = 每年都有一车学生去参加比赛。
A coachload of passengers arrives at the airport. = 一车乘客到达机场。

一般过去时:
A coachload of fans left after the concert. = 音乐会结束后，有一车歌迷离开了。
A coachload of delegates attended the conference. = 一车代表参加了会议。

现在完成时:

Several coachloads of supporters have already arrived at the stadium. = 好几车支持者已经到达体育场了。

过去完成时:

A coachload of students had already left before the announcement. = 在宣布之前，已经有了一车学生离开了。

虚拟语气:
