lepidodendron:/ˌlɛpɪdəˈdɛndrən/| n. |鳞木属植物|An extinct genus of large, tree-like lycopsids (club mosses) that flourished during the Carboniferous period.  They are characterized by their diamond-shaped leaf scars on the trunk. |Fossil remains of Lepidodendron have been found in coal measures. 鳞木的化石遗迹在煤层中被发现。|  The abundance of Lepidodendron fossils indicates its prevalence in Carboniferous swamps. 鳞木化石的大量存在表明它在石炭纪沼泽中很普遍。|  Sigillaria (another extinct lycopsid genus) |  No direct antonyms exist, as it refers to a specific extinct plant.
