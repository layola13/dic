occasioned:/əˈkeɪʒənd/ | vt. | 导致；引起；促成 | to cause something to happen | The storm occasioned widespread power outages.  暴风雨导致了大范围的停电。The delay was occasioned by unforeseen circumstances. 延误是由不可预见的情况造成的。| caused, brought about, produced, resulted in | prevented, avoided, stopped | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud noise occasions a fright. = 响亮的噪音令人害怕。
This policy occasions many problems. = 这项政策带来了很多问题。


一般过去时:
The rain occasioned the cancellation of the picnic. =  雨导致野餐取消。
His carelessness occasioned the accident. = 他的粗心导致了事故。


现在完成时:
His actions have occasioned much controversy. = 他的行为引起了许多争议。
The new law has occasioned significant changes. = 新法律带来了重大的变化。


过去完成时:
The previous drought had occasioned a severe food shortage. = 先前的干旱导致了严重的粮食短缺。
The bad weather had occasioned the delay of the flight. = 恶劣天气导致航班延误。


将来时:
The upcoming election will occasion much debate. =  即将到来的选举将会引发许多辩论。
The new technology will occasion significant changes in the industry. = 新技术将给行业带来重大变化。


虚拟语气:
If the war had not occasioned it, we might have had a better future. = 如果没有战争，我们或许会有更好的未来。
I wish the misunderstanding hadn’t occasioned such a rift between them. = 我真希望误解没有导致他们之间如此大的裂痕。


被动语态:
Many problems were occasioned by the lack of planning. = 许多问题都是由于缺乏规划造成的。
The delay was occasioned by unexpected technical difficulties. = 延误是由意想不到的技术难题造成的。


疑问句:
What occasioned your sudden departure? = 什么导致你突然离开？
What factors occasioned the company's bankruptcy? = 哪些因素导致了公司的破产？


否定句:
The good weather did not occasion any delays. = 好天气没有造成任何延误。
Nothing occasioned his anger. = 没有什么激怒了他。
