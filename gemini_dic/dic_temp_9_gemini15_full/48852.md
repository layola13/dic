deforests:/ˌdiːˈfɒrɪsts/| v.|森林砍伐；使…失去森林|To clear forests from (an area).  The process of removing or clearing forests from a large area of land. |例句：Deforestation is a serious environmental problem. 森林砍伐是一个严重的环境问题。
例句：Illegal logging deforests vast tracts of rainforest every year. 每年非法采伐都会使大片雨林遭到砍伐。|近义词：defoliate, clear-cut, clear|反义词：reforest, afforest|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They deforest the land for agriculture. = 他们为了农业砍伐土地。
The company deforests large areas of rainforest. = 这家公司砍伐了大片雨林。


一般过去时:
They deforested the mountainside to build a road. = 他们砍伐了山坡以修建一条路。
The farmers deforested the land to create farmland. = 农民们砍伐了土地以创造农田。


现在完成时:
They have deforested much of the Amazon rainforest. = 他们已经砍伐了大部分亚马逊雨林。
Humans have deforested significant portions of the planet. = 人类已经砍伐了地球上相当一部分的森林。


过去完成时:
They had deforested the area before the environmentalists arrived. = 在环保主义者到达之前，他们已经砍伐了该地区。
The government had deforested the land before the law was passed. = 在法律通过之前，政府已经砍伐了这片土地。


将来时:
They will deforest more land if they continue with this project. = 如果他们继续这个项目，他们将砍伐更多的土地。
The company will deforest the area next year. = 公司明年将砍伐该地区。


虚拟语气:
If they deforested the entire forest, it would be a disaster. = 如果他们砍伐了整片森林，那将是一场灾难。
I wish they hadn't deforested that area. = 我真希望他们没有砍伐那个地区。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
They did not deforest the area. = 他们没有砍伐该地区。
The government has not deforested the protected forests. = 政府没有砍伐保护森林。
