hearest:/ˈhɪrəst/| v. |听到(hear的第二人称单数现在时)|The second person singular present tense of the verb "hear," meaning to perceive sound with the ear. It is an archaic form rarely used in modern English.  |Example: Thou hearest the words of wisdom. 你听到了智慧之言。|近义词：hear, listen to |反义词：ignore, disregard |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I hear you. = 我听到你了。
She hears the birds singing. = 她听到鸟儿在唱歌。


一般过去时:
I heard a noise. = 我听到一个声音。
He heard the news yesterday. = 他昨天听到了这个消息。


现在完成时:
I have heard that before. = 我以前听过这个。
She has heard many stories about him. = 她听过很多关于他的故事。


过去完成时:
I had heard the rumour before he told me. = 他告诉我之前我就听说了这个谣言。
They had heard the bell ring before they entered the church. = 他们在进教堂之前就听到铃响了。


将来时:
I will hear from you soon. = 我很快就会收到你的消息。
She will hear the results tomorrow. = 她明天会听到结果。


虚拟语气:
If I heard that, I would be shocked. = 如果我听到那个，我会很震惊。
I wish I had heard the concert. = 我真希望我听了那场音乐会。


被动语态:
The music was heard throughout the house. = 音乐声传遍了整个房子。
Your words were heard by many people. = 你的话被很多人听到了。


疑问句:
Do you hear me? = 你听到我说话了吗？
Can you hear the music? = 你能听到音乐吗？


否定句:
I didn't hear you. = 我没听到你说话。
She doesn't hear well. = 她听力不好。


PHRASE->
hear from = 收到…的来信/消息
hear of = 听说
hear out = 听完…的话
give ear to = 倾听
within earshot = 在听力范围内
