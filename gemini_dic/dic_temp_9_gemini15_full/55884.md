divisive:/dɪˈvaɪsɪv/ | adj. | 分裂的；引起分歧的 | causing disagreement or dissension | The issue of immigration is highly divisive in many countries.  许多国家的移民问题都极具争议性。 |  controversial, contentious, contentious | unifying, uniting, conciliatory | 形容词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The policy is divisive. = 这项政策引起分裂。
His remarks are divisive. = 他的言论引起分裂。


一般过去时:
The election was divisive. = 那次选举造成了分裂。
The debate proved to be divisive. = 这场辩论证明是引起分裂的。


现在完成时:
The issue has been divisive for years. = 这问题多年来一直存在争议。
His actions have proved divisive within the team. = 他的行为在团队内部造成了分裂。


过去完成时:
The decision had already been divisive before the announcement. = 在宣布之前，这个决定就已经引起了争议。
The comments had been divisive among the community. = 这些评论在社区中引起了分裂。


将来时:
The upcoming election will likely be divisive. = 即将到来的选举很可能会造成分裂。
The new law is expected to be divisive. = 这项新法案预计会引起争议。


虚拟语气:
If the proposal were less divisive, it might pass. = 如果提案不那么引起争议，它可能会通过。
I wish the issue weren't so divisive. = 我希望这个问题不那么引起争议。


被动语态:
The country was deeply divided by the divisive policies. = 这个国家被分裂的政策深深地分裂了。
The team was affected by divisive internal arguments. = 团队受到了内部争议的影响。


疑问句:
Is this topic divisive? = 这个话题有争议吗？
Are these proposals divisive amongst the team? = 这些提案在团队中是否有争议？


否定句:
The meeting wasn't divisive. = 会议没有引起分裂。
The new plan is not divisive. = 新计划没有引起争议。
