introverts:/ˈɪntrəvɜːrts/ | n. | 内向的人 | A person who is more interested in their own thoughts and feelings than in spending time with other people.  Introverts tend to be quiet and reserved, and they often prefer to spend time alone or with a small group of close friends. |  She's an introvert and prefers quiet evenings at home to noisy parties. 她是一个内向的人，比起吵闹的聚会更喜欢在家安静地度过夜晚。|  loners, solitaires | extroverts | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is an introvert. = 他是一个内向的人。
They are introverts. = 他们是内向的人。


一般过去时:
She was an introvert in her youth. = 她年轻时是一个内向的人。
We were introverts at school. = 我们上学时都是内向的人。


现在完成时:
He has always been an introvert. = 他一直都是一个内向的人。
She has become more of an introvert recently. = 她最近变得更内向了。


过去完成时:
He had been an introvert all his life. = 他一生都是一个内向的人。
They had never been introverts before moving to the city. = 在搬到城市之前，他们从来都不是内向的人。


将来时:
She will likely remain an introvert. = 她很可能仍然是一个内向的人。
He will probably become less of an introvert as he gets older. = 他随着年龄增长，可能会变得不那么内向了。


虚拟语气:
If he were an extrovert, he'd be more popular. = 如果他是外向的人，他会更受欢迎。
I wish I weren't such an introvert. = 我希望我不是这么内向。


被动语态:
(Introverts are rarely described in passive voice. The focus is usually on their actions or characteristics.)  内向者很少以被动语态来描述。重点通常是他们的行为或特征。


疑问句:
Is he an introvert? = 他是内向的人吗？
Are they introverts? = 他们是内向的人吗？


否定句:
He isn't an introvert; he's quite outgoing. = 他不是内向的人；他很外向。
They aren't introverts; they love socializing. = 他们不是内向的人；他们喜欢社交。



PHRASE->

introverted personality = 内向型人格
highly introverted = 极度内向
shy introvert = 害羞的内向者
social introvert = 社交内向者 (someone who is introverted but still participates in social situations)
introvert behavior = 内向行为


词源:

The word "introvert" is derived from the Latin prefix "intro-" meaning "inward" or "within," and the verb "vertere," meaning "to turn."  Thus, an introvert is literally someone who turns inward.  The term was popularized by Carl Jung's psychological theories.
