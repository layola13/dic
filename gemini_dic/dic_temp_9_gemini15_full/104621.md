/ˈkæpəz/ | n. |卡帕, 卡帕系数 |The kappas are a set of coefficients used in statistics to measure the inter-rater reliability of categorical data.  They represent the level of agreement between different raters or observers when assigning categories to the same items.  Higher kappa values indicate greater agreement. |Example: The kappa coefficient for the diagnosis of the disease was 0.8, indicating substantial agreement between the doctors.  卡帕系数用于诊断疾病的系数为0.8，表明医生之间存在实质性的一致性。|近义词：coefficient of concordance, correlation coefficient |反义词：None directly applicable.  |名词
