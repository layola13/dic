madelene:/ˌmædəˈleɪn/ | n. | 马德琳蛋糕 | A small, shell-shaped sponge cake, often with a slightly bumpy surface.  | Example:  She baked a batch of delicious madelenes for the tea party. 她为茶话会烤了一批美味的马德琳蛋糕。 | 近义词：shell-shaped cake, small sponge cake | 反义词：无


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  The usage focuses on its description and use in sentences.

词源:
The word "madeleine" is derived from the French word of the same spelling.  The origin of the name is debated, but it is likely connected to Saint Mary Magdalene,  possibly through the shell-like shape of the cake reminiscent of a scallop shell, a symbol often associated with the saint.
