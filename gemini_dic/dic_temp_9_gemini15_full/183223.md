represented:/ˌreprɪˈzɛntɪd/| v. |代表，象征，表现| To act on behalf of someone or something; to stand for or symbolize something; to portray or depict something. |例句：The red rose represented England. 红玫瑰代表英格兰。|例句：The painting represented a scene of rural life. 这幅画描绘了乡村生活的一幕。|近义词：symbolize, embody, portray, depict|反义词：misrepresent, distort


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The flag represents our nation. = 旗帜代表我们的国家。
This symbol represents peace. = 这个符号代表和平。


一般过去时:
He represented his country at the Olympics. = 他代表他的国家参加奥运会。
The artist represented the landscape beautifully. = 艺术家美丽地描绘了这片风景。


现在完成时:
She has represented her company at many conferences. = 她已代表她的公司参加过许多会议。
The evidence has represented a significant turning point in the case. = 证据代表了案件中的一个重要转折点。


过去完成时:
They had represented the company for over ten years. = 他们已经代表该公司工作了十多年。
The painting had represented the artist's inner turmoil. = 这幅画体现了艺术家内心的骚动。


将来时:
He will represent his district in parliament. = 他将代表他的选区进入议会。
The new logo will represent the company's modern image. = 新标志将代表公司现代的形象。


虚拟语气:
If he represented us better, we would win. = 如果他更好地代表我们，我们会赢。
I wish the painting represented my feelings more accurately. = 我希望这幅画更准确地表达我的感受。


被动语态:
Our interests were well represented at the meeting. = 我们的利益在会议上得到了很好的代表。
The situation was represented inaccurately in the media. = 媒体对这种情况的报道不准确。


疑问句:
Who represented your company at the negotiations? = 在谈判中谁代表你的公司？
Does this painting truly represent the artist's style? = 这幅画真的代表了艺术家的风格吗？


否定句:
He did not represent his views clearly. = 他没有清楚地表达他的观点。
The data did not accurately represent the true situation. = 数据并没有准确地反映真实情况。


PHRASE->
represent oneself = 自我介绍
represent a challenge = 构成挑战
represent a threat = 构成威胁
be well represented = 得到充分的代表
fairly represented = 公平地代表


词源:
来自古法语 *representer，源自拉丁语 repraesentare，由 re- ("again") 和 praesentare ("to present") 构成。
