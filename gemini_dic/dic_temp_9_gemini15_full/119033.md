methuselahs:/mɛθˈjuːzələz/| n. |므두셀라들(장수하는 사람들)|People who live exceptionally long lives;  often used figuratively to refer to very old things or institutions.  The term derives from Methuselah, the longest-lived person in the Bible.|例句：The company's methuselahs, many of whom had worked there for decades, were finally retiring.  这家公司的一些老员工，很多都在这里工作了几十年，终于退休了。|近义词：centenarians, elders, ancients|反义词：youths, youngsters
