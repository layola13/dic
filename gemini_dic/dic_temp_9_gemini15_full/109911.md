libation:/laɪˈbeɪʃən/| n.|奠酒；祭酒；酒宴|A libation is a pouring of a liquid offering to a deity or the spirits of the dead.  It is often performed as a ritual offering, typically of wine or other alcoholic beverages.  The act of making a libation is also sometimes referred to as a libation.|例句：The priest poured a libation of wine onto the altar.  祭司将一杯酒倒在祭坛上作为祭品。

The ancient Greeks made libations to their gods before battles. 古希腊人在战斗前会向他们的神明敬献奠酒。|近义词：offering, sacrifice, toast|反义词：None specifically
