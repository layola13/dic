coined:/kɔɪnd/| vt.|创造(新词);杜撰;铸造(硬币)|To invent or create a new word or phrase; to make coins.|例句：She coined a new term for her invention. 她为她的发明创造了一个新词。|例句：The mint coined millions of new dollars. 造币厂铸造了数百万美元的新硬币。|近义词：create, invent, forge|反义词：destroy, abolish

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He coins new words for his blog. 他为他的博客创造新词。
The mint coins money every day. 造币厂每天都在铸造货币。

一般过去时:
Shakespeare coined many words that we still use today. 莎士比亚创造了许多我们今天仍在使用的词语。
They coined a new slang term last year. 他们去年创造了一个新的俚语词。

现在完成时:
She has coined several successful marketing slogans. 她已经创造了几句成功的营销口号。
The government has coined new regulations. 政府已经制定了新的规章制度。


过去完成时:
Before the invention, the term had never been coined. 在发明之前，这个词从未被创造出来。
By the end of the war, they had coined a new type of currency. 在战争结束时，他们已经铸造了一种新型货币。


将来时:
He will coin a new phrase to describe the situation. 他将创造一个新的短语来描述这种情况。
The company will coin a new logo next year.  公司明年将设计一个新的标识。


虚拟语气:
If he coined a better name, the product would sell better. 如果他取个更好的名字，产品销量会更好。
I wish he had coined a more memorable slogan. 我希望他当初创造一个更令人难忘的口号。


被动语态:
The term was coined in the 19th century.  这个词是在19世纪创造出来的。
Millions of coins are coined daily at the mint. 造币厂每天铸造数百万枚硬币。


疑问句:
Who coined the term "artificial intelligence"? 谁创造了“人工智能”这个词？
Where are these commemorative coins coined? 这些纪念币是在哪里铸造的？


否定句:
He did not coin that phrase; it existed before him. 他并没有创造那个短语；它在他之前就存在了。
The company hasn't coined a new marketing strategy yet. 公司还没有制定新的营销策略。
