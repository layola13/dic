nonextenuating: /ˌnɒnɪkˈstɛnjuˌeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 不可宽恕的，无法解释的 | Not capable of being lessened or excused;  unjustifiable.  This adjective emphasizes the severity and lack of mitigating circumstances for an action or situation.  | 例句：The judge deemed the crime nonextenuating and handed down a harsh sentence.  法官认为罪行不可宽恕，并判处了重刑。


His nonextenuating behavior led to his dismissal. 他不可宽恕的行为导致了他的解雇。| 近义词：inexcusable, unforgivable, unjustifiable, aggravating | 反义词：excusable, justifiable, pardonable | 词性：形容词


词源:

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not."
* **extenuate**: Verb meaning "to lessen or try to lessen the seriousness or extent of (something)."  This word comes from the Latin *extenuare*, meaning "to make thin,"  ultimately from *tenuis*, meaning "thin."  The original sense was to make something physically thinner, but it evolved to mean making something less serious or weighty.


用法说明:

"Nonextenuating" is used to describe actions, circumstances, or offenses that cannot be excused or justified.  It implies a complete lack of mitigating factors.  The word is often used in formal contexts, such as legal or professional settings.  It carries a strong negative connotation.
