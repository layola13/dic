emotionalize:/ɪˈmoʊʃənəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使感情化；赋予感情；煽情 | To give emotional content or significance to something; to make something seem more emotional than it actually is.  | He emotionalized the simple disagreement into a major family feud. 他把简单的分歧煽情成了一场重大的家庭纠纷。 | dramatize, sensationalize | de-emotionalize, rationalize | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He emotionalizes every minor setback. = 他把每一个小小的挫折都夸大其词，赋予其情感色彩。
They emotionalize the political debate. = 他们使政治辩论变得情绪化。


一般过去时:
She emotionalized the story to make it more appealing. = 她使故事变得更煽情以使其更吸引人。
The media emotionalized the accident reports. = 媒体对事故报道进行了煽情处理。


现在完成时:
He has emotionalized his entire life story. = 他已经把他整个人生故事都渲染得充满情感。
They have emotionalized the situation unnecessarily. = 他们不必要地使情况变得情绪化。


过去完成时:
She had emotionalized the issue before the meeting even started. = 会议还没开始她就已将问题情绪化了。
The speaker had emotionalized his speech to sway the audience. = 演讲者为了影响听众，使他的演讲变得煽情。


将来时:
He will emotionalize the facts to support his argument. = 他会将事实情绪化以支持他的论点。
They will emotionalize the campaign to attract voters. = 他们会使竞选活动变得煽情以吸引选民。


虚拟语气:
If he emotionalized the situation less, it would be easier to solve. = 如果他少一些情绪化，问题就更容易解决了。
I wish she hadn't emotionalized the whole thing. = 我真希望她没有把整件事情都搞得这么情绪化。


被动语态:
The issue was emotionalized by the media. = 这个问题被媒体情绪化了。
The story has been emotionalized to appeal to a wider audience. = 故事被煽情化以吸引更广泛的观众。


疑问句:
Does he emotionalize every little problem? = 他是不是把每一个小问题都搞得很情绪化？
Why do they emotionalize the news so much? = 为什么他们要对新闻进行如此煽情的报道？


否定句:
He didn't emotionalize his presentation. = 他在陈述中并没有煽情。
She tried not to emotionalize the discussion. = 她努力避免使讨论变得情绪化。


PHRASE->
