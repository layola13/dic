pompous:/ˈpɒmpəs/| adj. | 傲慢的；自大的；浮夸的 | characterized by excessive self-importance; ostentatious.  | He delivered a pompous speech filled with self-congratulation. 他发表了一篇自吹自擂的傲慢演讲。|近义词： arrogant, haughty, pretentious, ostentatious | 反义词：humble, modest, unassuming


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is pompous and self-important. = 他傲慢自大。
She acts pompous when talking to her subordinates. = 她对下属说话时很傲慢。


一般过去时:
He was pompous in his manner. = 他举止傲慢。
The politician's speech sounded pompous. = 这位政治家的演讲听起来很浮夸。


现在完成时:
He has become quite pompous in his old age. = 他上了年纪后变得相当傲慢。
She has always been a little pompous. = 她一直有点自大。


过去完成时:
He had been pompous before, but now he seems humbled. = 他以前很傲慢，但现在似乎谦逊了。
They had been so pompous about their achievements. = 他们对自己的成就过于自夸。


将来时:
He will be pompous at the award ceremony. = 他将在颁奖典礼上表现得很傲慢。
She will likely be pompous in her acceptance speech. = 她很可能在获奖感言中表现得很自大。


虚拟语气:
If he weren't so pompous, people would like him more. = 如果他不那么傲慢，人们会更喜欢他。
I wish he wasn't so pompous. = 我希望他不那么傲慢。


被动语态:
His behavior was considered pompous by many. = 许多人认为他的行为傲慢。
His achievements were described in a pompous way. = 他的成就被夸大其词地描述了。


疑问句:
Is he always so pompous? = 他总是这么傲慢吗？
Wasn't his speech rather pompous? = 他的演讲是不是有点浮夸？


否定句:
He is not pompous at all; he's quite humble. = 他一点也不傲慢；他相当谦逊。
Her demeanor was not pompous, but rather gracious. = 她的举止并非傲慢，而是相当优雅。
