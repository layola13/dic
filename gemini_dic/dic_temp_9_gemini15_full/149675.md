paisleys:/ˈpeɪzliz/| n.|佩斯利图案；佩斯利纹|A paisley is a teardrop-shaped or curved pattern, often featuring intricate details and swirling designs.  It originated in Persia (modern-day Iran) and was popularized in the 18th and 19th centuries in Europe and beyond.  The name "paisley" comes from the Scottish town of Paisley, which became a major center for the production of shawls and textiles featuring this pattern.|The scarf is adorned with a beautiful paisley pattern.|近义词：damask, floral|反义词：plain, simple
