overdiscounting:/ˌoʊvərˈdɪskaʊntɪŋ/ | n. |过度贴现，过度低估|The act of assigning too much weight to recent events or information, leading to an underestimation of the probability of future events.  It's a cognitive bias where people overemphasize recent experiences, often negative ones, and consequently underestimate the likelihood of positive or more balanced outcomes. | Example:  Investors often exhibit overdiscounting, selling assets too quickly after a period of poor performance, even if the underlying fundamentals remain strong.  投资者经常表现出过度贴现，在业绩不佳的一段时间后过快地出售资产，即使基本面仍然强劲。|近义词：overestimation, undervaluation, misjudgment |反义词：overvaluation, underestimation
