retiringly:/rɪˈtaɪərɪŋli/ | adv. | 退休般地；羞怯地；含蓄地 | In a retiring or shy manner; modestly.  It describes an action or behavior characterized by shyness, reserve, or a tendency to withdraw from social interaction.  | 例句：She smiled retiringly and quickly left the room. 她羞怯地笑了笑，然后迅速离开了房间。

He spoke retiringly, almost whispering his words. 他说话很含蓄，几乎是耳语。


近义词：shyly, modestly, diffidently, timidly, unobtrusively

反义词：boldly, assertively, confidently, aggressively


词源:  Derived from the adjective "retiring," meaning tending to withdraw from company; shy; reserved.  The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective into an adverb.  The root is ultimately tied to the verb "retire," which originally meant "to withdraw" or "to retreat," and before that, from Old French "retraire" and Latin "re-" (back) + "trahere" (to draw).


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adverb and doesn't have tense variations like verbs. Its usage is descriptive, modifying a verb or an entire clause.


例句：
She answered retiringly. 她羞怯地回答。
He behaved retiringly at the party. 他在派对上表现得非常内敛。


PHRASE->
There aren't common set phrases using "retiringly" as it's a less frequently used word.  Phrases would be built around it describing a specific action, such as:  "spoke retiringly," "smiled retiringly," "acted retiringly."
