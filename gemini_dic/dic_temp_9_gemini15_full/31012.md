challenge:/ˈtʃælɪndʒ/| n./v.|挑战，质疑；向…挑战| A challenge is something that tests your ability or strength.  It can also be an objection or a call to compete. |例句：Climbing Mount Everest is a huge challenge.  攀登珠穆朗玛峰是一个巨大的挑战。

例句：He challenged my authority. 他质疑我的权威。|近义词：test,  trial,  contest,  defy|反义词：acceptance, surrender


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The task challenges my abilities. =这项任务挑战我的能力。
This problem challenges our assumptions. =这个问题挑战了我们的假设。


一般过去时:
She challenged him to a race. =她向他挑战赛跑。
They challenged the decision in court. =他们在法庭上对该决定提出质疑。


现在完成时:
He has challenged the status quo for years. =多年来，他一直在挑战现状。
Scientists have challenged the prevailing theory. =科学家们对流行的理论提出了质疑。


过去完成时:
He had challenged the rules before, but this time it was different. =他以前曾挑战过规则，但这次不一样。
They had challenged the system, but to no avail. =他们曾挑战过这个制度，但没有结果。


将来时:
I will challenge him to a debate. =我将向他挑战辩论。
She will challenge the results of the election. =她将挑战选举的结果。


虚拟语气:
If I were stronger, I would challenge him. =如果我更强壮，我会挑战他。
I wish I could challenge the unfair system. =我希望我能挑战不公平的制度。


被动语态:
His authority was challenged. =他的权威受到了挑战。
The theory is being challenged by new research. =该理论正在受到新的研究的挑战。


疑问句:
Does this task challenge you? =这项任务对你来说有挑战性吗？
What challenges do you face in your work? =你在工作中面临哪些挑战？


否定句:
He did not challenge the verdict. =他没有对判决提出异议。
The team didn't challenge the opponent's strategy.  =该团队没有挑战对手的策略。


PHRASE->

meet a challenge = 面对挑战
accept a challenge = 接受挑战
rise to a challenge = 迎接挑战
a serious challenge = 一项严峻的挑战
a global challenge = 全球性挑战
