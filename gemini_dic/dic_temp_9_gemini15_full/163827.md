predepreciate:/ˌpriːdɪˈpriːʃieɪt/ | v. | 预先贬值 | To decrease the value of (something) before it is officially or formally valued.  This typically happens in anticipation of a larger devaluation or decline in value.  | Example: The company predepreciated its assets to account for potential future losses. 公司预先贬值其资产以应对潜在的未来损失。 |近义词: undervalue, downplay,  discount | 反义词: overvalue, inflate, appreciate
