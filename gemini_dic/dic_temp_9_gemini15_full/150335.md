panic-stricken: /ˈpænɪkˌstrɪkən/ | adj. | 惊慌失措的，吓得魂飞魄散的 |  Describes someone overwhelmed by panic and fear.  It implies a state of intense fear and disorientation, often resulting in uncontrolled actions or reactions. |  The panic-stricken crowd rushed for the exits.  惊慌失措的人群冲向出口。  The panic-stricken child couldn't speak. 吓得魂飞魄散的孩子说不出话来。 | terrified, frightened, scared, distraught | calm, composed, serene |  形容词


USAGE->

He is panic-stricken by the sudden noise. = 他被突如其来的噪音吓坏了。
The audience is panic-stricken by the fire alarm. = 观众被火警警报吓得惊慌失措。

疑问句:

Were the passengers panic-stricken when the plane landed roughly? = 当飞机着陆颠簸时，乘客们惊慌失措了吗？


否定句:

She wasn't panic-stricken; she acted quickly and decisively. = 她没有惊慌失措；她行动迅速果断。

词源:
panic (n.) + stricken (past participle of strike)

panic 源自希腊语 "panikos," 指的是牧神潘恩，传说他出现时会引起惊慌。stricken 表示“受打击的，受折磨的”。  因此 panic-stricken 字面意思是“被恐慌击垮的”。
