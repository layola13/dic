sank:/sæŋk/| v.|沉没；下沉；下陷|To go down below the surface of water or another liquid; to fall to a lower level.|The ship sank rapidly after hitting the iceberg.  船撞上冰山后迅速沉没。
The ground sank under the weight of the heavy machinery.  地面在重型机械的重量下沉陷了。|sink, submerge, plummet|float, rise|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun sinks in the west. 太阳西沉。
He sinks into his chair. 他沉入椅子里。


一般过去时:
The Titanic sank in 1912.  泰坦尼克号于1912年沉没。
The price of oil sank dramatically. 油价暴跌。


现在完成时:
The ship has sunk to the bottom of the ocean. 船已沉入海底。
The value of the property has sunk considerably.  房产价值已大幅下跌。


过去完成时:
The ship had already sunk before the rescue team arrived.  救援队到达之前，船已经沉没了。
The stock market had sunk to its lowest point in years.  股市跌到了多年来的最低点。


将来时:
The ice will sink if you put enough weight on it. 如果你放足够的重量在冰上，它会沉下去。
The economy is expected to sink further next year.  预计明年经济将进一步下滑。


虚拟语气:
If the ship hadn't sunk, many lives would have been saved.  如果船没有沉没，许多生命就能得救。
I wish the ship hadn't sunk. 我真希望船没有沉没。


被动语态:
The ship was sunk by a torpedo. 船被鱼雷击沉。
The plan was sunk by opposition from the board.  该计划因董事会的反对而搁浅。


疑问句:
Did the ship sink? 船沉了吗？
Has the value of the house sunk? 房子的价值下跌了吗？


否定句:
The ship didn't sink immediately. 船没有立即沉没。
The proposal didn't sink; it was approved.  提案没有被否决；它被批准了。


PHRASE->
sink or swim:  成败自负
sink in:  逐渐理解
sink one's teeth into:  全力以赴
sink into depression: 陷入沮丧
sink into oblivion:  被遗忘
