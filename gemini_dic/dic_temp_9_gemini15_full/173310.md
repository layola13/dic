quasi-cynical: /ˈkwɑːsaɪˈsɪnɪkəl/ | adj. | 近乎愤世嫉俗的，略带愤世嫉俗的 |  Appearing slightly cynical or skeptical, but not entirely or completely so.  It suggests a hint of cynicism without being fully committed to it. |


例句：

He offered a quasi-cynical smile, suggesting a mixture of amusement and distrust. = 他报以一个近乎愤世嫉俗的微笑，暗示着一种娱乐和不信任的混合。

Her quasi-cynical remarks about the project hinted at her underlying doubts. = 她对该项目略带愤世嫉俗的评论暗示了她潜在的怀疑。


近义词：

slightly cynical, mildly skeptical, somewhat sarcastic,  ambivalent,  apathetic


反义词：

optimistic, idealistic, naive, trusting, credulous


词源：

* **quasi-**:  From Latin "quasi," meaning "as if," "as it were," or "almost."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation.

* **cynical**: From Greek "kynikos," meaning "like a dog," referring to the Cynic philosophers who emphasized virtue and self-sufficiency, often displaying an aloof and critical attitude toward society.
