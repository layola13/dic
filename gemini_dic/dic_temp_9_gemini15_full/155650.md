phlebotomization:/ˌflɛboʊtəˈmaɪzeɪʃən/| n. |放血疗法；静脉切开术|The process of removing blood from a vein, either therapeutically or for diagnostic purposes.  This can be done for various reasons, including blood donation, therapeutic phlebotomy (to reduce high iron levels), and obtaining blood samples for testing. | Example:  The patient underwent phlebotomization to reduce his dangerously high iron levels.  病人接受了放血疗法以降低其危险的高铁水平。|近义词：venesection, bloodletting |反义词：blood transfusion|名词
