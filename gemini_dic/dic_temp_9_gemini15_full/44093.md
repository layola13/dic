crippling: /ˈkrɪplɪŋ/ | adj. |严重的，使人衰弱的；严重损害的 | Extremely severe or damaging; causing serious impairment or destruction.  | 近义词：debilitating, devastating, paralyzing, disabling | 反义词：strengthening, empowering, invigorating


英文解析:  Describes something that causes severe damage, impairment, or destruction, often leaving something weak or unable to function properly.  It can be used to describe physical injuries, illnesses, financial problems, or emotional distress.


例句:

The crippling blow to the economy led to widespread unemployment.  经济遭受了沉重打击，导致了大范围的失业。

His crippling arthritis made even simple tasks difficult. 他严重的关节炎使即使是简单的任务也变得困难。


词源:

From "cripple," meaning a person who is lame or disabled.  The word "cripple" itself has origins in Middle English, possibly from Old Norse or Old French. Its evolution reflects a shift from simply describing physical disability to also encompassing metaphorical limitations.
