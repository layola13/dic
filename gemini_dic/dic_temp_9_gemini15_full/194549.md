self-sinking:/ˈsɛlfˌsɪŋkɪŋ/| adj. | 自沉的；自行下沉的 |  Describing something designed or tending to sink itself, often intentionally.  This typically refers to vessels or structures designed to sink under specific conditions, such as after fulfilling their purpose or in case of emergency. | Example: The self-sinking barge was used to create an artificial reef.  = 自沉驳船被用来创造一个人工暗礁。  The self-sinking mechanism failed, leaving the ship afloat. = 自沉机制失效，导致船只漂浮。| 近义词：self-destructing, submerging | 反义词：floating, buoyant |
