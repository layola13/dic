gatehouses: /ˈɡeɪtˌhaʊzɪz/ | n. | 门房；门卫室 | Small houses or buildings located at the entrance to a property, often providing security or control access.  They are typically found at the gates of estates, castles, or other large properties.  |


例句：

The gatehouses were manned by guards 24/7. = 门卫室全天候由警卫看守。

The ancient gatehouses were beautifully preserved. = 古老的门卫室保存得非常完好。


近义词：guardhouses, lodges, entrances

反义词：None directly applicable;  the antonym would depend on the context (e.g.,  open area instead of a gated area).

词性: 名词 (plural)


词源:

The word "gatehouse" is a compound word formed from "gate" and "house".  "Gate" comes from Old English "geat," meaning "opening, passage," related to "gap" and "yawning." "House" comes from Old English "hūs," meaning "house."  Therefore, "gatehouse" literally means "a house at the gate."
