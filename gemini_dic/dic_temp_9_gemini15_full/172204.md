push:/pʊʃ/| vt. vi. n.| 推，推动；强迫；敦促；坚持 | To exert force on (someone or something) so as to move it away from oneself, or to move (something) in a particular direction by using force.  |例句：He pushed the door open. 他推开了门。|例句：She pushed her way through the crowd. 她挤过人群。|近义词： shove, propel, urge, press |反义词：pull, draw


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pushes the cart. = 他推着手推车。
She pushes her limits. = 她挑战自己的极限。

一般过去时:
They pushed the button. = 他们按下了按钮。
He pushed the agenda forward. = 他推动了议程的进展。

现在完成时:
I have pushed myself too hard. = 我把自己逼得太紧了。
She has pushed for reform for years. = 她多年来一直争取改革。

过去完成时:
They had pushed the deadline back. = 他们已经把最后期限推迟了。
He had pushed the boundaries of his art. = 他已经突破了他艺术的界限。

将来时:
We will push the project to completion. = 我们将把项目推进到完成。
She will push him to succeed. = 她会督促他成功。

虚拟语气:
If I pushed harder, I might have succeeded. = 如果我更努力一些，我可能会成功。
I wish I had pushed him to study harder. = 我真希望我当时督促他更努力学习。

被动语态:
The button was pushed. = 按钮被按下了。
The reforms were pushed through by the government. = 改革被政府强推通过了。

疑问句:
Are you pushing me? = 你在推我吗？
Will they push for a change? = 他们会争取改变吗？

否定句:
He didn't push hard enough. = 他没有足够努力地推。
She doesn't push her children too hard. = 她不会把她的孩子逼得太紧。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "push" originates from the Middle English word "pushen," which is believed to be of Germanic origin, likely related to Old High German "puozan" and Old Saxon "pusian."  These words all share a common ancestor indicating a forceful movement.
