re-attributed: /ˌriːˈætrɪbjuːtɪd/ | v. |重新归属；重新归因 | To attribute something again or differently; to assign a different authorship or origin to something.  | The painting was re-attributed to a lesser-known artist after further research.  这幅画经过进一步研究后被重新归属于一位鲜为人知的艺术家。 |reassigned, reassigned, reallocated |  —


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They re-attribute the discovery to a different team. = 他们将这一发现重新归功于另一个团队。
The company re-attributes its losses to market volatility. = 公司将其损失重新归因于市场波动。


一般过去时:
Historians re-attributed the authorship of the poem to a different poet. = 历史学家将这首诗的作者重新归于一位不同的诗人。
The museum re-attributed the sculpture to a different period. = 博物馆将这尊雕塑重新归于不同的时期。


现在完成时:
Scholars have re-attributed many works to previously unknown artists. = 学者们已经将许多作品重新归于以前不为人知的艺术家。
The committee has re-attributed the responsibility to a new department. = 委员会已将责任重新分配给一个新的部门。


过去完成时:
They had re-attributed the blame to the contractor before the investigation concluded. = 在调查结束之前，他们已经将责任重新归咎于承包商。
The experts had re-attributed the cause of the accident after reviewing the evidence. = 专家们在审查证据后重新认定了事故的原因。


将来时:
The researchers will re-attribute the findings once the new data is analyzed. = 研究人员将在新数据分析后重新归纳研究结果。
The court will re-attribute the ownership of the property after the hearing. = 庭审结束后，法院将重新认定该房产的所有权。


虚拟语气:
If they had more evidence, they would re-attribute the authorship of the book. = 如果他们有更多证据，他们就会重新认定这本书的作者。
I wish they would re-attribute the credit where it truly belongs. = 我希望他们能将荣誉归于真正应得的人。


被动语态:
The painting was re-attributed to a different artist last year. = 这幅画去年被重新归于一位不同的艺术家。
The cause of the fire was re-attributed to accidental ignition. = 火灾原因被重新认定为意外点燃。


疑问句:
Will the authorship of the play be re-attributed? = 这部戏剧的作者身份会被重新认定吗？
Has the responsibility been re-attributed to someone else? = 责任是否已被重新分配给其他人？


否定句:
They did not re-attribute the failure to the manager. = 他们没有将失败归咎于经理。
The committee refused to re-attribute blame without further investigation. = 在没有进一步调查的情况下，委员会拒绝重新分配责任。
