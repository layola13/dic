prepledge:/ˈpriːplɛdʒ/| n.| 预先承诺；预先保证 | A prepledge is a commitment made in advance of a formal pledge or agreement.  It often serves as an indication of intent or a preliminary step towards a more binding arrangement.  | Example: The company made a prepledge to invest in renewable energy before signing the official contract.  公司在签署正式合同之前预先承诺投资可再生能源。 |近义词：commitment, undertaking, promise |反义词：repudiation, denial

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company prepledges significant funds to the project. = 公司预先承诺为该项目提供大量资金。
He prepledges his support for the candidate. = 他预先承诺支持该候选人。


一般过去时:
They prepledged their resources to the relief effort. = 他们预先承诺将资源用于救济工作。
She prepledged her loyalty to the cause. = 她预先承诺效忠于这一事业。

过去完成时:
