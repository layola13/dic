pay-offs:/ˈpeɪˌɔːfs/ | n. | 酬劳；回报；结果；贿赂 |  The benefits, advantages, or profits gained from a particular action, situation, or investment. It can also refer to the act of bribing someone. |例句：The pay-off for all their hard work was a successful product launch.  他们辛勤工作的回报是产品成功发布。

The company faced a huge pay-off to settle the lawsuit. 公司面临巨额赔偿以解决诉讼。


近义词：rewards, returns, benefits, compensation, bribe | 反义词：losses, penalties, setbacks


词源：pay (动词，支付) + off (副词，完全地；彻底地)  The original meaning relates to paying someone off completely, which later evolved to encompass the broader sense of rewards or consequences.
