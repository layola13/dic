preemption:/priˈɛmpʃən/| n.|优先权；优先购买权；先发制人；抢占；压制|The act of acquiring or taking something before someone else can;  the right to purchase something before others.  It can also refer to a government's action to prevent something from happening, often by force.|例句：The company exercised its right of preemption to buy the competitor's shares. 这家公司行使优先购买权购买了竞争对手的股份。|例句：The military intervention was a preemptive strike to prevent the enemy's attack.  这次军事干预是对敌方攻击的先发制人打击。|近义词：priority, precedence, preemption right, anticipation, forestalling|反义词：postponement, delay, subordination|名词
