electorate:/ɪˈlɛktərət/ | n. | 全体选民；选举区 | The body of persons entitled to vote in an election.  The electorate will decide the outcome of the election. 全体选民将决定选举的结果。|近义词：constituency, voters |反义词：N/A


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The electorate chooses its representatives. = 全体选民选择他们的代表。
The electorate overwhelmingly supports the current government. = 全体选民压倒性地支持现政府。


一般过去时:
The electorate voted overwhelmingly for the conservative party. = 全体选民压倒性地投票支持保守党。
Last year's electorate was significantly smaller than this year's. = 去年选民人数明显少于今年。


现在完成时:
The electorate has shown its dissatisfaction with the current policies. = 全体选民已经表达了对现行政策的不满。
The electorate has consistently supported this candidate. = 全体选民一直支持这位候选人。


过去完成时:
By the time the election concluded, the electorate had already made up its mind. = 到选举结束时，选民们已经做出了决定。
The electorate had previously rejected similar proposals. = 选民们此前曾拒绝过类似的提案。


将来时:
The electorate will decide the fate of the proposed legislation. = 全体选民将决定该提案的命运。
Next year's electorate will likely be very different. = 明年的选民很可能会大不相同。


虚拟语气:
If the electorate were more engaged, we might see better outcomes. = 如果选民更积极参与，我们可能会看到更好的结果。
I wish the electorate understood the complexities of the issue. = 我希望选民能够理解这个问题的复杂性。


被动语态:
The candidates are judged by the electorate. = 候选人由全体选民评判。

疑问句:
