scandalum:/ˈskændələm/| n.|丑闻，诽谤|A scandalum is a scandalous action or event, often involving a public figure or institution.  It can refer to an action or event that causes public outrage or disgrace, or it can refer to a false and defamatory statement about someone. |例句：The politician's affair became a major scandal.  这位政治家的婚外情成了一个重大丑闻。|例句：Spreading false rumors is a form of scandalum magnatum. 散播谣言是一种诽谤权贵的行为。|近义词：scandal, disgrace, infamy, shame|反义词：honor, glory, respectability|名词
