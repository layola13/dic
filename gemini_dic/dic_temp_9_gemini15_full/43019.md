cowboyed:/ˈkaʊbɔɪd/| v.|扮演牛仔；过牛仔般的生活|To act like or live as a cowboy.  The verb implies adopting the characteristics, lifestyle, or behavior associated with cowboys, often in a playful or romanticized way. It can also suggest recklessness or a disregard for rules.|例句：He cowboyed around the ranch all day, roping calves and singing country songs. 他整天在牧场扮演牛仔，套小牛犊并唱乡村歌曲。


He cowboyed his way through life, never settling down. 他过着牛仔般的生活，从未安定下来。|近义词：acted like a cowboy, played cowboy, lived like a cowboy|反义词：behaved civilly, acted responsibly
