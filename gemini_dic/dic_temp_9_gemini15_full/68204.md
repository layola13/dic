fair-traders:/ˈferˌtreɪdərz/| n. |公平贸易者，公平贸易支持者 |People or organizations that support and participate in fair trade practices, ensuring fair prices and working conditions for producers in developing countries.  They advocate for ethical and sustainable business models that prioritize the well-being of farmers and workers over profit maximization. |Example: Many fair-traders are actively involved in campaigning for better labor standards in coffee production. 许多公平贸易者积极参与倡导咖啡生产中更好的劳动标准。|近义词：ethical consumers, socially responsible businesses |反义词：exploiters, sweatshop owners
