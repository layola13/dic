sidle:/ˈsaɪdl/| vi.|侧身移动；偷偷摸摸地走|to walk in a shy or sneaky way, especially sideways|例句：He sidled up to the bar and ordered a drink. 他侧身走到吧台点了一杯酒。
例句：She sidled away from the group, trying to avoid being noticed. 她悄悄地离开人群，试图不被注意到。|近义词：edge, creep, slink|反义词：stride, march


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He sidles up to her. = 他侧身走到她身边。
They sidle through the crowd. = 他们侧身穿过人群。

一般过去时:
He sidled up to the bar and ordered a drink. = 他侧身走到吧台点了一杯酒。
She sidled away from the group, trying to avoid being noticed. = 她悄悄地离开人群，试图不被注意到。

现在完成时:
He has sidled into the conversation. = 他悄悄地加入了谈话。
She has sidled out of the room. = 她悄悄地离开了房间。

过去完成时:
He had sidled away before I could speak to him. = 我还没来得及和他说话，他就悄悄地走开了。
She had sidled into the shadows. = 她已经悄悄地躲进了阴影里。

将来时:
He will sidle up to her and ask her to dance. = 他会悄悄地走到她身边，邀请她跳舞。
She will sidle out of the meeting before anyone notices. = 她会在没人注意到之前悄悄地离开会议。

虚拟语气:
If he sidled up to me like that again, I would be angry. = 如果他再那样偷偷摸摸地靠近我，我会生气的。
I wish he hadn't sidled away so quickly. = 我真希望他没有那么快就悄悄地走开。


被动语态:
(Sidle is rarely used in the passive voice because it is an intransitive verb.)


疑问句:
Did he sidle away from the argument? = 他是不是偷偷地躲开了争吵？
Why did she sidle into the room so quietly? = 她为什么那么安静地偷偷溜进房间？


否定句:
He didn't sidle up to her; he walked straight towards her. = 他没有偷偷摸摸地走到她身边；他是直接朝她走去的。
She didn't sidle away; she left openly. = 她没有偷偷溜走；她是公开离开的。


PHRASE->
sidle into:  悄悄地进入
sidle up to: 悄悄地靠近
sidle away: 悄悄地走开
sidle past: 悄悄地走过
sidle out of: 悄悄地溜出


词源:
The word "sidle" likely originates from the Old English word "sidlian," meaning "to go sideways."  The exact etymology is uncertain, but the connection to side-stepping is clear.
