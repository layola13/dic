haploid:/ˈhæplɔɪd/| adj.| 单倍体的 | Having a single set of chromosomes, as opposed to the usual diploid number found in somatic cells.  A haploid cell or organism has only one complete set of chromosomes.  This is often denoted by "n".| Example: Gametes, such as sperm and egg cells, are haploid.  | 近义词：monoploid | 反义词：diploid, polyploid
