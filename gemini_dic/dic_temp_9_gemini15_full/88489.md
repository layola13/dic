historicists:/hɪˈstɔːrɪsɪsts/| n.|历史主义者，历史决定论者|Those who believe that historical events are determined by underlying forces and that understanding these forces is crucial to understanding the past and present.  They emphasize the importance of context and the interconnectedness of events.  They often reject universal truths and emphasize the relativity of historical interpretations.|Example:  Many historicists argue that social structures are shaped by historical forces, rather than individual actions. 许多历史主义者认为社会结构是由历史力量而非个体行为塑造的。|近义词: historical determinists, contextualists|反义词: ahistoricists, presentists
