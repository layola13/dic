ethic:/ˈɛθɪk/| n.|伦理；道德；伦理学|The principles of right and wrong that govern a person's behavior or the conducting of an activity.|例句：The company has a strong code of ethics. 这家公司有严格的道德准则。|例句：He questioned the ethics of the experiment. 他质疑了该实验的伦理道德。|近义词：morals, principles, values|反义词：immorality, wickedness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Ethics guide our decisions. = 伦理指导我们的决策。
What are the company's ethics? = 公司的伦理道德是什么？


一般过去时:
His actions violated professional ethics. = 他的行为违反了职业道德。
The committee debated the ethics of the project. = 委员会就该项目的伦理问题进行了辩论。


现在完成时:
We have discussed the ethical implications. = 我们已经讨论了伦理影响。
Society has increasingly focused on business ethics. = 社会越来越关注商业道德。


过去完成时:
They had already considered the ethical concerns. = 他们之前已经考虑过伦理问题。
She had never questioned her own ethics before. = 她以前从未质疑过自己的道德。


将来时:
The new policy will address ethical dilemmas. = 新政策将解决伦理困境。
We will explore the ethics of artificial intelligence. = 我们将探讨人工智能的伦理问题。


虚拟语气:
If I were in his position, I would act ethically. = 如果我处在他的位置，我会采取合乎道德的行为。
I wish the company had a stronger ethical code. = 我希望公司有更严格的道德准则。


被动语态:
Ethical guidelines were established by the organization. = 组织制定了伦理准则。
The matter is being reviewed for ethical violations. = 正在审查此事是否有违反道德行为。


疑问句:
What are the ethical considerations? = 伦理方面的考虑是什么？
Are these actions ethical? = 这些行为合乎道德吗？


否定句:
His behavior was not ethical. = 他的行为不道德。
The proposed solution is not ethically sound. = 提出的解决方案在伦理上站不住脚。
