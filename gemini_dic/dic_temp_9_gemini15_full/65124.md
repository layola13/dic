ethic:/ˈɛθɪk/| n.|伦理；道德；伦理学|The principles of right and wrong that govern a person's behavior or the conducting of an activity.|例句：The company has a strict ethic against bribery. 这家公司有严格的反贿赂道德准则。|例句：Medical ethics require doctors to protect patient confidentiality. 医学伦理要求医生保护病人的隐私。|近义词：moral, principle, morality|反义词：immorality, unethicalness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He adheres to a strict work ethic. = 他坚持严格的工作道德。
Their business ethics are questionable. = 他们的商业道德值得怀疑。


一般过去时:
The doctor's actions violated medical ethics. = 这位医生的行为违反了医学伦理。
The company's past unethical practices were exposed. = 该公司过去的不道德行为被揭露了。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:
