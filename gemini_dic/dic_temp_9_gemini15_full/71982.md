flinch:/flɪntʃ/| v.|畏缩；退缩；退避|to make a sudden, slight movement because of pain, surprise, or fear|例句：He flinched at the loud noise. 他被巨大的噪音吓了一跳。|近义词：shrink, recoil, wince|反义词：stand firm, brave


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flinches at loud noises. = 他害怕大声的噪音。
She flinches when she sees blood. = 她看到血就会畏缩。


一般过去时:
He flinched when the dog barked. = 狗叫的时候他畏缩了一下。
She flinched at the unexpected touch. = 她被突如其来的触摸吓了一跳。


现在完成时:
He has flinched from the challenge. = 他一直逃避挑战。
She has never flinched in the face of danger. = 她从未在危险面前退缩过。


过去完成时:
He had flinched before the blow landed. = 在打击到来之前，他已经畏缩了。
She had flinched many times before finally confronting her fears. = 在最终克服恐惧之前，她已经畏缩过很多次了。


将来时:
He will flinch when he hears the bad news. = 当他听到坏消息时他会畏缩。
She will not flinch from her duty. = 她不会逃避她的责任。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't flinch from telling the truth. = 如果我是你，我不会害怕说出真相。
I wish I hadn't flinched when he asked me that question. = 我真希望他问我那个问题的时候我没有畏缩。


被动语态:
(The verb "flinch" is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes a reflexive action.)


疑问句:
Did he flinch when you told him the truth? = 你告诉他真相的时候他畏缩了吗？
Does she flinch easily? = 她容易畏缩吗？


否定句:
He didn't flinch when the gun went off. = 枪响的时候他并没有畏缩。
She didn't flinch at the sight of the snake. = 她看到蛇并没有畏缩。


PHRASE->
flinch at: 对...畏缩
flinch from: 逃避...
flinch away: 畏缩躲开
without flinching: 毫不畏缩地
to flinch back: 退缩回去
