reconnoitre: /ˌrɛkəˈnɔɪtər/ | vt. |侦察；勘察；探查 | To make a preliminary survey of (an area or region), especially in order to gather military information.  | 例句：The soldiers reconnoitred the enemy's position before launching an attack.  士兵们在发动攻击前侦察了敌人的阵地。

The scouts reconnoitred the terrain to find a suitable campsite. 侦察兵勘察地形以寻找合适的营地。| 近义词：scout, survey, explore, investigate | 反义词：ignore, neglect | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The army reconnoitres the enemy's position regularly. = 军队定期侦察敌人的位置。
We reconnoitre the area before starting the construction. = 我们在开始建设之前勘察该地区。


一般过去时:

They had reconnoitred the enemy's defenses thoroughly before launching the assault. = 他们在发动攻击之前彻底侦察了敌人的防御工事。

词源:
From French *reconnoître*, from Old French *reconoistre*, from Vulgar Latin *re-cognoscere*, from Latin *cognoscere* ("to know").  The prefix *re-* means "again," so the word literally means "to know again" or "to learn about thoroughly."
