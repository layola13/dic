confederationists:/ˌkənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃənɪsts/| n. |邦联主义者；联邦主义者|Supporters of a confederation or federal system of government, advocating for a loose alliance of states or regions rather than a centralized authority.|The confederationists argued for a system where individual states retained significant autonomy.|近义词：federalists (in some contexts), decentralists|反义词：centralists, unitarians
