credible:/ˈkrɛdəbl/ | adj. | 可信的，可靠的 | believable; worthy of belief or trust.  | The evidence presented was credible enough to convict the defendant.  证据足以使被告定罪。 | believable, reliable, trustworthy, plausible | unbelievable, unreliable, untrustworthy, implausible

USAGE->
一般现在时:
His story sounds credible. = 他的故事听起来可信。
We find his explanation credible. = 我们认为他的解释可信。

一般过去时:
The witness's testimony was credible. = 目击证人的证词是可信的。
The alibi he provided was not credible. = 他提供的不在场证明不可信。

现在完成时:
The research has produced credible results. = 这项研究已经产生了可信的结果。
No credible evidence has emerged to support the claim. = 没有出现任何可信的证据来支持这一说法。

过去完成时:
By then, the rumors had become credible. = 到那时，谣言已经变得可信了。
They had presented no credible evidence before the trial. = 他们在审判前没有提供任何可信的证据。

将来时:
The new evidence will make the case more credible. = 新证据将使此案更加可信。
Future studies will determine if the findings are credible. = 未来的研究将确定这些发现是否可信。

虚拟语气:
If the evidence were more credible, the jury would convict. = 如果证据更可信，陪审团就会定罪。
I wish his explanation were more credible. = 我希望他的解释更可信一些。

被动语态:
His claims were deemed credible by the experts. = 他的说法被专家认为是可信的。
The theory is considered credible by many scientists. = 许多科学家认为这个理论是可信的。

疑问句:
Is the source of this information credible? = 这个信息的来源可靠吗？
Do you find his argument credible? = 你觉得他的论点可信吗？

否定句:
The suspect's alibi was not credible. = 嫌疑人的不在场证明不可信。
Her story lacked credibility and was not credible. = 她的故事缺乏可信度，不可信。


PHRASE->

a credible explanation = 可信的解释
