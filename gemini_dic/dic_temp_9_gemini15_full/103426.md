jittery:/ˈdʒɪtəri/| adj.|紧张不安的，颤抖的，|Nervous and unsteady; shaky.|She felt jittery before the interview. 她面试前感到紧张不安。He was jittery with excitement. 他兴奋得发抖。|nervous, shaky, anxious, fidgety|calm, relaxed, composed


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is jittery about the upcoming exam. 他对即将到来的考试感到紧张不安。
She is always jittery when she speaks in public.  她公开演讲时总是很紧张。


一般过去时:
He was jittery after drinking too much coffee. 他喝太多咖啡后感到紧张不安。
She was jittery all day because of the meeting.  因为会议，她整天都紧张不安。


现在完成时:
I have been jittery all week waiting for the results. 我等结果已经紧张了一周。
She has felt jittery ever since she received the bad news. 自从收到坏消息以来，她就一直感到紧张不安。


过去完成时:
He had been jittery all morning, so he decided to take a break. 他整个上午都很紧张，所以他决定休息一下。
She had been jittery before the presentation, but she calmed down afterward.  她在演示之前很紧张，但后来平静下来了。


将来时:
I will be jittery until I hear back from them. 在收到他们的回复之前，我会一直感到紧张。
She will probably be jittery on the day of the wedding. 她婚礼那天可能会很紧张。


虚拟语气:
If I weren't so jittery, I could give a better speech. 如果我不那么紧张，我就能做一个更好的演讲。
I wish I weren't so jittery during important meetings. 我希望在重要的会议上不要那么紧张。


被动语态:  (Jittery is rarely used in the passive voice.)  This adjective describes a state of being, not an action performed upon someone.


疑问句:

否定句:

She wasn't jittery; she was just excited. 她并不紧张，她只是兴奋。
