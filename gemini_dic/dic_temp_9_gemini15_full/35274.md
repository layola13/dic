clutched:/klʌtʃt/| v.|抓住；紧握；|To grasp or seize something tightly.|He clutched the steering wheel as the car skidded.|近义词：grasped, gripped, seized|反义词：released, let go


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He clutches his briefcase tightly. = 他紧紧抓住他的公文包。
She clutches her purse nervously. = 她紧张地抓着她的钱包。


一般过去时:
He clutched the rope for dear life. = 他紧紧抓住绳子，拼命地活下去。
She clutched her child to her chest. = 她把孩子紧紧抱在胸前。


现在完成时:
He has clutched at straws in his desperate search for a solution. = 在他绝望地寻找解决办法的过程中，他已经抓住了一根稻草。
She has clutched her beliefs despite the criticism. = 尽管受到批评，她仍然坚持自己的信念。


过去完成时:
He had clutched the money before he fell. = 他摔倒之前已经紧紧抓住钱了。
She had clutched her keys tightly, fearing she would lose them. = 她紧紧地攥着钥匙，害怕会弄丢。


将来时:

She will clutch her lucky charm during the exam. = 考试期间她会紧紧抓住她的幸运符。


虚拟语气:
If he clutched the railing, he might not have fallen. = 如果他抓住栏杆，他可能就不会摔倒了。
If she had clutched the bag more tightly, she wouldn't have lost it. = 如果她把包抓得更紧一些，她就不会丢了。


被动语态:

The lifeline was clutched desperately by the drowning man. = 溺水的人拼命抓住救生索。

否定句:
