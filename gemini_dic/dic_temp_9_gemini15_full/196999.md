shakerism:/ˈʃeɪkərɪzəm/| n. | 震动教；沙克教 | Shakerism is a religious movement that originated in England in the 17th century and later flourished in the United States.  Shakers are known for their simple, communal lifestyle, celibacy, and distinctive furniture and crafts. They emphasized spiritual equality and pacifism. | The Shaker community thrived in the 19th century, but its numbers dwindled significantly in the 20th century.  |近义词：Shakerism (The term itself is its own closest synonym, referring specifically to the Shaker religion and practices.) |反义词：There is no direct antonym, as it refers to a specific religious movement.  |名词
