croon:/kruːn/| vt. vi. n.|低吟浅唱，哼唱|to sing or hum in a low, soft voice, especially a song of a sentimental nature.|例句：She crooned a lullaby to her baby. 她低声哼唱摇篮曲哄孩子睡觉。  例句：He crooned softly to the melody. 他轻轻地哼着曲调。|近义词：hum, murmur, chant|反义词：shout, yell


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He croons every night. = 他每晚都哼唱。
She croons beautiful melodies. = 她哼唱优美的旋律。

一般过去时:
He crooned a love song last night. = 他昨晚哼唱了一首情歌。
She crooned a sweet tune to the sleeping child. = 她为熟睡的孩子哼唱了一首甜美的曲调。

现在完成时:
He has crooned that song many times. = 他已经唱过那首歌很多次了。
She has crooned to her baby all evening. = 她整个晚上都在哄她的宝宝睡觉。

过去完成时:
He had crooned the song before the concert began. = 音乐会开始之前，他已经哼唱过这首歌了。
She had crooned herself to sleep. = 她已经哼着歌睡着了。

将来时:
He will croon a new song at the concert. = 他将在音乐会上演唱一首新歌。
She will croon a lullaby for her baby tonight. = 她今晚将为她的宝宝唱摇篮曲。

虚拟语气:
If he crooned more often, she would be happier. = 如果他经常哼唱，她会更快乐。
I wish he would croon me a song. = 我希望他能为我哼唱一首歌。

被动语态:
The song was crooned beautifully. = 这首歌被优美地哼唱出来。
A lullaby was crooned softly to the baby. = 摇篮曲被轻轻地哼唱给婴儿听。

疑问句:

否定句:
