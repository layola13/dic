manequin:/ˈmænɪkɪn/| n. |人体模型；模特儿|A mannequin is a life-size model of the human body, typically used to display clothing in shops.  It can also refer to a person who acts as a model. |例句：The shop window was filled with mannequins wearing the latest fashions.  商店橱窗里摆满了穿着最新款服装的人体模型。


The department store used real mannequins in their advertising campaign. 这家百货公司在他们的广告宣传中使用了真人模特。|近义词：model, dummy, display figure|反义词：live person

词源:

The word "mannequin" comes from the French word "mannequin," which ultimately derives from the Italian word "manichino," a diminutive of "mano" (hand).  Originally, it referred to a small hand-sized model, and its meaning gradually expanded to include life-sized models.
