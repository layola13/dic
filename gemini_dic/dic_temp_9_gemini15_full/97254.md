inflict:/ɪnˈflɪkt/| vt.| 施加；使遭受；造成（痛苦、损伤等）| to cause (something unpleasant or painful) to be suffered by someone or something. |例句：The storm inflicted heavy damage on the coastal areas.暴风雨给沿海地区造成了严重的破坏。

例句：The government inflicted harsh penalties on the rebels. 政府对叛乱者施加了严厉的惩罚。


近义词：impose, deal, administer, cause


反义词：relieve, alleviate, ease


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He inflicts pain on others. 他对他人施加痛苦。
They inflict damage on the environment. 他们破坏环境。


一般过去时:
The earthquake inflicted significant damage to the city. 地震给城市造成了巨大的损失。
She inflicted a serious wound on her opponent. 她给对手造成了严重的伤口。


现在完成时:
The war has inflicted immeasurable suffering on the people.  战争给人民带来了无法估量的痛苦。
Years of drought have inflicted severe damage on the crops.  多年的干旱对庄稼造成了严重的损害。


过去完成时:
They had inflicted considerable losses on the enemy before the truce. 在休战前，他们已经给敌人造成了相当大的损失。
She had inflicted many injuries on herself through her reckless behaviour. 她由于鲁莽的行为给自己造成了许多伤害。


将来时:
The new policy will inflict further hardship on low-income families. 新政策将给低收入家庭带来更多困难。
The coming storm is likely to inflict significant damage to the power grid. 即将来临的风暴可能会对电网造成严重的损害。


虚拟语气:
If they inflicted that kind of damage on the property, they should be punished. 如果他们对财产造成那种破坏，他们应该受到惩罚。
I wish they hadn't inflicted so much pain on me. 我希望他们没有给我带来这么多的痛苦。


被动语态:
Severe damage was inflicted on the building by the fire. 建筑物遭受了火灾造成的严重损坏。
Great suffering was inflicted upon the refugees by the war. 难民遭受了战争带来的巨大痛苦。


疑问句:
What kind of harm did the accident inflict? 事故造成了什么伤害？
What punishment will be inflicted on the criminals? 罪犯将受到什么惩罚？


否定句:
The doctor made sure to inflict no further pain on the patient. 医生确保不会给病人带来进一步的痛苦。
The government did not inflict any additional taxes on the citizens. 政府没有对公民征收任何额外税款。


PHRASE->
inflict punishment  施加惩罚
inflict damage  造成损害
inflict injury  造成伤害
inflict suffering  造成痛苦
inflict a wound  造成伤口

词源:
From Old French *enflicter, from Latin infligere "to strike against, dash against," from in- "on, upon" + fligere "to strike."  The modern sense of "to cause suffering" developed later.
