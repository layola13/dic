predict:/prɪˈdɪkt/| vt. |预言；预测；预料|To say or estimate that (something) will happen in the future or will be a consequence of something.|The weather forecast predicts rain tomorrow.  天气预报预测明天会下雨。|The scientists predicted a major earthquake. 科学家预测将发生大地震。|forecast, foresee, anticipate|unpredict, miscalculate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Scientists predict the weather patterns. = 科学家预测天气模式。
I predict that the team will win. = 我预测球队会赢。

一般过去时:
They predicted a successful outcome. = 他们预测了一个成功的结果。
The fortune teller predicted my future. = 算命先生预测了我的未来。


现在完成时:
The experts have predicted a rise in inflation. = 专家们已经预测到通货膨胀将会上升。
She has predicted the winner of the race correctly. = 她准确地预测了比赛的获胜者。


过去完成时:
They had predicted the storm, but no one listened. = 他们已经预测到了暴风雨，但没有人听。
I had predicted that he would be late, and he was. = 我预测他会迟到，他果然迟到了。


将来时:
They will predict the next solar eclipse. = 他们将预测下一次日食。
We will predict the stock market trend. = 我们将预测股市趋势。


虚拟语气:
If I could predict the future, I would be rich. = 如果我能预测未来，我会很富有。
I wish I could predict the lottery numbers. = 我希望我能预测彩票号码。


被动语态:
The earthquake was predicted by scientists. = 地震被科学家预测到了。
The election results were accurately predicted. = 选举结果被准确地预测到了。


疑问句:
Can you predict the winner? = 你能预测赢家吗？
Do you predict rain tomorrow? = 你预测明天会下雨吗？


否定句:
I cannot predict the future. = 我无法预测未来。
The meteorologists did not predict the hurricane. = 气象学家没有预测到飓风。


PHRASE->
predict accurately = 准确预测
predict with certainty = 肯定预测

predict a trend = 预测趋势
