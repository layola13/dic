dyads:/ˈdaɪædz/ | n. |二元组；二元关系；两人一组 | A dyad is a group of two people or things, considered as a unit.  The term is often used in sociology, psychology, and other social sciences to describe a social unit consisting of two individuals.  It can refer to a pair of interacting individuals, or a pair of related concepts or things. | Example: The teacher divided the class into dyads for a collaborative project.  老师把班级分成两人一组进行合作项目。The study examined the communication patterns within dyads of close friends. 这项研究考察了亲密朋友二人组之间的交流模式。|近义词：pair, couple, duo |反义词：group (large group), multitude |名词
