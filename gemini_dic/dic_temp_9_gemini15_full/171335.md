public-spiritedness: /ˌpʌblɪkˈspɪrɪtɪdnəs/ | n. | 公益精神，社会责任感 |  The quality of being public-spirited; a disposition to serve the public good. It involves a concern for the welfare of others and a willingness to act for the benefit of the community. |  Her public-spiritedness led her to volunteer at the homeless shelter.  她的公益精神促使她去无家可归者收容所做志愿者。

The city council praised the public-spiritedness of its citizens in helping to clean up after the storm. 市议会赞扬了市民在风暴过后帮助清理的公益精神。


近义词：altruism, benevolence, philanthropy, civic duty, social responsibility

反义词：selfishness, egoism, self-interest


词源:

* **public:** From Latin *publicus*, meaning "of the people," from *populus*, "people."
* **spirited:** From Old English *spēdig*, meaning "successful, prosperous," ultimately from Proto-Germanic *speþijōz*.  The modern sense of "full of spirit" developed later.
* **-ness:** A suffix forming nouns denoting a quality, state, or condition.
