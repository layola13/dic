siphonophora:/ˌsaɪfəˈnɒrə/| n. | 僧帽水母；管水母；|A siphonophore is a marine hydrozoan characterized by its colony of specialized zooids, working together as a single organism.  They are often mistaken for jellyfish, but are actually complex colonies of individual polyps and medusoids.  They exhibit a remarkable range of forms and sizes, from small, inconspicuous creatures to massive, highly visible colonies. |Example: The Portuguese man-of-war is a well-known example of a siphonophore.  僧帽水母是管水母的一个著名例子。|近义词：hydrozoan (广义)|反义词：无
