evasion:/ɪˈveɪʒən/ | n. |逃避；规避；推诿；遁词 | The act of avoiding something or someone.  It often implies a deliberate attempt to avoid something unpleasant or undesirable, such as responsibility, a difficult question, or the law.  |例句：His evasion of the question made me suspicious. 他的避而不答让我起了疑心。

例句：The police were investigating his tax evasion. 警方正在调查他的逃税行为。


近义词：avoidance, dodging, shirking, sidestepping, circumvention | 反义词：confrontation, facing, acceptance


词源:  From Old French *evasioun, from Latin evasio, from evadere "to escape, evade".


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He evades his responsibilities. = 他逃避责任。
She evades taxes. = 她逃税。


一般过去时:
He evaded the police. = 他躲避了警察。
She evaded answering the question. = 她回避了这个问题。


现在完成时:
He has evaded capture for months. = 他已经潜逃数月了。
She has evaded the issue for too long. = 她对这个问题回避太久了。


过去完成时:
He had evaded the draft during the war. = 他在战争期间逃避了兵役。
She had evaded paying her debts for years. = 她多年来一直逃避偿还债务。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

否定句:
