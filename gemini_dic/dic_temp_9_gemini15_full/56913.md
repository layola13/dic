dotardly:/ˈdɑːtərdli/| adj.|昏庸的；老糊涂的；衰老的|  Showing the weakness or feebleness of old age; senile.  The adjective describes behavior or characteristics associated with senility or advanced age, often implying a loss of mental acuity and judgment. |例句：His dotardly pronouncements were increasingly ignored. 他的昏庸言论越来越被忽视。

例句：The dotardly king made foolish decisions that jeopardized his kingdom.  这位昏庸的国王做出了愚蠢的决定，危及了他的王国。


近义词：senile, doddering, feebleminded, decrepit, childish

反义词：youthful, vigorous, sharp-witted, astute


词源: From Middle English *dotard, from Old French *dotart, from Vulgar Latin *dotardus, from Latin *dotāre "to endow," "to give a dowry".  The connection lies in the idea that aging men, in a sense, become less capable and more reliant (as if endowed with the weakness of age).
