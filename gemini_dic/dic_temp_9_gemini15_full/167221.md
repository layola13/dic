pretentious:/prɪˈtɛnʃəs/| adj.|自以为是的；矫揉造作的；炫耀的|Affectedly and irritatingly grand, solemn, or self-important.  Pretentious behavior or language is intended to impress people, but it is usually considered to be silly or annoying.  |例句：His pretentious pronouncements on art were laughable.  他关于艺术的夸夸其谈令人发笑。

例句：She found his pretentious mannerisms irritating. 她发现他矫揉造作的举止令人恼火。


近义词：affected, ostentatious, pompous, showy, self-important

反义词：humble, modest, unassuming, simple

词源:
From Latin *praetendere ("to hold forth, allege, pretend"), from prae- ("before") + tendere ("to stretch").  The sense development is from "to put forward, claim" to "to make exaggerated claims," leading to the modern meaning of affectedly showy.
