reflexivities:/ˌriːflɛkˈsɪvɪtiz/ | n. | 自反性；反射性 | The study of reflexivities explores how social structures and individual actions mutually constitute and reinforce each other.  Reflexivities refer to the capacity for self-reference and self-awareness, particularly within systems or structures.  This concept is crucial in various fields like sociology, anthropology, and philosophy.  | Example: The researcher examined the reflexivities between individual choices and societal norms.  研究者考察了个体选择与社会规范之间的自反性。|近义词：self-referentiality, self-reflection | 反义词：objectivity, externality | 名词
