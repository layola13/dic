scaffoldage:/ˈskæfəldɪdʒ/| n. | 脚手架；支架；临时搭建的结构 | Temporary structure of poles, planks, etc., used for supporting workmen, materials, etc., when building or repairing; a temporary structure.  Scaffolding provides support during construction.  The scaffoldage was erected around the building. |近义词：scaffolding, staging |反义词：foundation, base


例句：

The builders erected a substantial scaffoldage around the building.
建筑工人围绕建筑物搭建了一个坚固的脚手架。


The artist used a simple scaffoldage to reach the high parts of the canvas.
艺术家用一个简单的脚手架来够到画布的高处。


词源：

From Middle English *scaffaldage, from Old French *eschafaude, from eschafaut "scaffold," from Old French eschaffer "to arrange, set up," from a Germanic root.


用法说明:


scaffoldage 主要指用于建筑或修理工作时支撑工人和材料的临时结构，强调其临时性和搭建的复杂性，通常规模较大，由多部分组成。与scaffolding相比，scaffoldage更强调整体结构，而scaffolding可以指单个的脚手架部件或简单的支架。
