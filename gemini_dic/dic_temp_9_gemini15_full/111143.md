lithagogue:/ˈlɪθəɡɔːɡ/| n.|利尿剂；通便剂|A lithagogue is a medicine or substance that promotes the excretion of stones from the body, particularly from the kidneys or gallbladder.  It often refers to substances that increase urine flow (diuretic) or bowel movements (purgative) to help expel stones.|例句：Some traditional herbal remedies are believed to have lithagogue properties.  一些传统的草药疗法被认为具有利尿通便的特性。|近义词：diuretic, purgative, cholagogue|反义词：None specifically, but substances that cause stone formation or retention could be considered antonyms.
