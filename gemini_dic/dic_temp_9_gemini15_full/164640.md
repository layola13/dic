/priːˌɛkspekˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 预期 |  Preexpectations refers to expectations formed *before* an experience, often shaping one's perception and interpretation of that experience.  It emphasizes the influence of prior knowledge, beliefs, and assumptions on subsequent encounters. This is distinct from expectations formed *during* or *after* an experience. |  My preexpectations of the concert were high, based on the band's previous album, but the actual performance fell short. 我对这场音乐会的预期很高，因为乐队的上一张专辑，但实际演出却差强人意。 | anticipations, preconceptions, forebodings |  (暂无明确反义词，可考虑:  unbiased perspectives,  open-mindedness) | 名词
