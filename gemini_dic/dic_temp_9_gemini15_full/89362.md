homonomous:/həˈmɒnɪməs/ | adj. | 同音异义词的 | Having the same sound but a different meaning.  Words that are homonomous are called homonyms.  | Example:  The words "bank" (financial institution) and "bank" (riverside) are homonomous.  | 近义词: homonymous | 反义词: synonymous


USAGE->
This word is an adjective, and thus doesn't have verb conjugations.  The usage focuses on its descriptive function.


Example 1: The words "bear" (the animal) and "bear" (to carry) are homonomous, creating potential for confusion.
= “bear”（动物）和“bear”（携带）这两个词是同音异义词，可能会造成混淆。

Example 2:  Many homonomous words in English have caused difficulties for non-native speakers.
= 英语中许多同音异义词给非母语人士带来了困难。

There are no common phrases directly using "homonomous."  The word is primarily used in linguistic discussions.
