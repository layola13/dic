shockwaves:/ˈʃɒkweɪvz/| n.|冲击波，震波|A shockwave is a sudden, powerful disturbance that spreads outwards from a point of impact or explosion.  It's characterized by a sharp increase in pressure, density, and temperature.  Shockwaves can be caused by various phenomena, including explosions, supersonic flight, and earthquakes.|例句：The explosion sent shockwaves through the city. 爆炸使冲击波席卷了整个城市。

The earthquake created powerful shockwaves that devastated the region. 地震产生了强大的冲击波，摧毁了该地区。|近义词：blast wave, pressure wave|反义词：ripple, gentle wave|名词
