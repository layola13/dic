muse:/mjuːz/| n. v.|缪斯女神；灵感；沉思；出神；|A muse is traditionally one of the nine goddesses in Greek mythology who preside over the arts and sciences.  More generally, a muse is a source of inspiration, especially in creative work. As a verb, it means to think deeply or be lost in thought.|例句：The artist found his muse in the beauty of the natural world. =这位艺术家在自然之美中找到了灵感。

The writer mused on the meaning of life. =这位作家沉思着生命的意义。|近义词：inspiration, source, thought, ponder|反义词：uninspired, distraction


USAGE->

一般现在时:
She muses on her past experiences. = 她沉思着她过去的经历。
He often muses about the future. = 他经常沉思未来。


一般过去时:
The poet mused over his next poem. = 诗人沉思着他的下一首诗。
She mused for hours on the beach. =她在海滩上沉思了几个小时。


现在完成时:
I have mused over this problem for days. = 我已经考虑这个问题好几天了。
She has mused on the implications of her discovery. = 她一直在思考她发现的意义。


过去完成时:
He had mused about the possibility for years. = 他多年来一直在思考这种可能性。
They had mused on the meaning of life before their trip. = 他们在旅行前思考过生命的意义。


将来时:

She will muse upon the subject for many weeks. = 她会思考这个主题好几个星期。


虚拟语气:

否定句:
