dripping:/ˈdrɪpɪŋ/| adj. v.|滴落的；湿透的；油腻的|Covered or saturated with liquid;  excessively wet;  (of food) containing or appearing to contain a lot of fat or oil.|例句：The dripping faucet kept me awake all night.  滴落的龙头让我整夜睡不着觉。 The rain left the streets dripping wet.  雨水使街道湿漉漉的。|近义词：soaking, saturated, drenched, oily, greasy|反义词：dry, arid


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The tap is dripping. = 水龙头在滴水。
The rain is dripping from the roof. = 雨水从屋顶滴落。

一般过去时:
The faucet dripped all night. = 水龙头滴了一整夜。
He dripped sweat after the run. = 他跑步后汗流浃背。

现在完成时:
The roof has been dripping since the storm began. = 从暴风雨开始以来，屋顶就一直在滴水。
The cake has dripped a lot of oil. = 蛋糕漏了很多油。

过去完成时:
The paint had dripped onto the floor before I noticed. = 我注意到之前，油漆已经滴到地板上了。
The ice cream had dripped down his chin. = 冰激凌已经滴到他的下巴上了。

将来时:
The melting snow will drip from the eaves. = 融化的雪将从屋檐滴落。
I think the roof will drip during the next rain. = 我认为下次下雨屋顶会漏水。

虚拟语气:
If the roof were dripping, I would call a repairman. = 如果屋顶在滴水，我会叫修理工。
I wish the dripping sound would stop. = 我希望滴水的声音能停止。

被动语态:
The floor was dripped with water. = 地板被水弄湿了。
The shirt was dripped with sweat. = 衬衫被汗水弄湿了。

疑问句:
Is the tap dripping again? = 水龙头又滴水了吗？
Are your clothes dripping wet? = 你的衣服湿透了吗？

否定句:
The tap isn't dripping now. = 水龙头现在不滴水了。
My clothes aren't dripping wet. = 我的衣服没有湿透。


PHRASE->

dripping in jewels = 珠宝满身


词源:

The word "dripping" originates from the Old English word "dryppan," meaning "to drip."  This root is shared with other Germanic languages, reflecting a common ancestor.  The "-ing" suffix denotes the present participle, originally indicating an ongoing action.
