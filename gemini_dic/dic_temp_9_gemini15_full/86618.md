heliotypy:/ˌhiːliˈɒtəpi/| n. |日光照相术，日光印画术|A photographic process using sunlight to create prints.  It is an early form of photomechanical reproduction.  The process involves exposing a sensitized plate to sunlight through a negative, then etching the plate to create a printing surface.|The museum displayed a collection of prints created using heliotypy.|photolithography, photoengraving| (no direct antonym,  as it describes a specific process)|名词
