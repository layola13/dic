murry:/ˈmʌri/|  adj.|  快乐的；愉快的 |  Merry means cheerful and happy. It is often used to describe a festive or celebratory atmosphere. |例句：They had a merry Christmas. 他们过了一个快乐的圣诞节。  |近义词：happy, joyful, cheerful, jolly |反义词：sad, unhappy, glum


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The children are merry today. = 孩子们今天很开心。
She is always merry and bright. = 她总是快乐而明亮。


一般过去时:
We were merry at the party. = 我们在派对上玩得很开心。
They were merry and carefree. = 他们快乐而无忧无虑。


现在完成时:
They have been merry all day. = 他们一整天都很开心。
We have had a merry time. = 我们玩得很开心。


过去完成时:
They had been merry before the news arrived. = 在消息传来之前，他们一直都很开心。
We had been merry until the storm broke. = 直到暴风雨来临之前，我们都很开心。


将来时:
We will be merry at the festival. = 我们在节日里会很开心。
They will be merry when they hear the good news. = 当他们听到好消息时，他们会很开心。


虚拟语气:
If they were merry, they would sing. = 如果他们快乐，他们就会唱歌。
If I were merry, I would dance. = 如果我快乐，我会跳舞。


被动语态:
(This adjective doesn't typically use passive voice.)


疑问句:
Are they merry? = 他们快乐吗？
Is she merry today? = 她今天快乐吗？


否定句:
They are not merry. = 他们不快乐。
She is not merry today. = 她今天不快乐。



PHRASE->
Merry Christmas! = 圣诞快乐！
Merry making = 狂欢
Merry heart = 快乐的心
Merry go round = 旋转木马
Make merry = 欢庆


词源:
The word "merry" comes from the Old English word "mærig," meaning "famous," "celebrated," or "joyful."  Over time, the meaning shifted to primarily focus on the sense of joyful celebration.
