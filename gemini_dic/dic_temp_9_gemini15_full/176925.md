realistic: /ˌriːəˈlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 现实的；实际的；逼真的 |

英文解析:  Conforming to reality; representing things as they really are.  Realistic descriptions or portrayals are accurate and detailed, not idealized or exaggerated.  A realistic goal is achievable and practical.

例句:

He gave a realistic assessment of the situation. 他对形势作出了现实的评估。

Her painting was remarkably realistic. 她的画作非常逼真。


近义词: practical, pragmatic, lifelike, true-to-life, believable, credible

反义词: unrealistic, idealistic, fanciful, imaginary, improbable


词源:

The word "realistic" is derived from the noun "realism," which ultimately comes from the Middle French word "réalisme," itself derived from the Latin word "realis," meaning "real" or "belonging to things."  The suffix "-istic" is added to form the adjective "realistic," indicating a characteristic or quality of realism.
