doubtless:/ˈdaʊtɪs/| adj.|无疑地，必定|Without a doubt; certainly.|The project is doubtless a success.|certainly, undoubtedly, surely|doubtful, uncertain|形容词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He doubtless knows the answer. = 他无疑知道答案。
She doubtless feels relieved. = 她无疑感到轻松。


一般过去时:
They doubtless understood the instructions. = 他们无疑理解了指示。
He doubtless regretted his actions. = 他无疑后悔了他的行为。


现在完成时:
We have doubtless made progress. = 我们无疑取得了进步。
She has doubtless learned a lot. = 她无疑学到了很多。


过去完成时:
They had doubtless anticipated the problem. = 他们无疑预料到了这个问题。
He had doubtless expected a better outcome. = 他无疑期望更好的结果。


将来时:
They will doubtless succeed. = 他们无疑会成功。
It will doubtless rain tomorrow. = 明天无疑会下雨。


虚拟语气:
If he were to try, he would doubtless succeed. = 如果他尝试，他无疑会成功。
Were she to apply, she would doubtless be accepted. = 如果她申请，她无疑会被录取。


被动语态:
The plan was doubtless well-received. = 该计划无疑受到了好评。
The decision was doubtless made carefully. = 该决定无疑是经过仔细考虑做出的。


疑问句:
Is it doubtless true? = 这无疑是真的吗？（疑问句中通常不直接使用doubtless）
Is it doubtless correct? = 这无疑是正确的吗？（疑问句中通常不直接使用doubtless）


否定句:
It is not doubtless that he will come. = 他是否会来并非毫无疑问。
It is not doubtless she will win. = 她是否会赢并非毫无疑问。


PHRASE->
doubtless true = 无疑是真的
doubtless the best = 无疑是最好的
doubtless a success = 无疑是成功的
doubtless a pleasure = 无疑是一种乐趣
doubtless important = 无疑很重要

词源:
doubtless源自"doubt" + "-less"。"doubt"源自古法语"douter"，意为“怀疑”，最终源自拉丁语"dubitare"，也有“怀疑”之意。"-less"是一个后缀，表示“没有”、“缺乏”。因此，doubtless的字面意思是“没有怀疑的”。
