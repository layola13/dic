ontologism:/ˌɒntəˈlɒdʒɪzəm/ | n. | 本体论论点；本体论主义 | The philosophical theory that being is the primary object of philosophical inquiry, and that all other concepts are derived from it.  It emphasizes the importance of understanding the nature of being and existence.  Ontology is the branch of metaphysics dealing with the nature of being. Ontologism often stresses the primacy of divine being or a supreme being. | Example:  Some critiques of ontologism argue that it neglects the importance of experience and empirical evidence in understanding reality.  | 近义词: metaphysical realism, essentialism | 反义词: empiricism, nominalism | 名词
