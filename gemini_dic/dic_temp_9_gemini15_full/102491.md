jacobian:/dʒəˈkoʊbiən/| n. |雅可比矩阵，雅可比行列式|The Jacobian matrix is a matrix of partial derivatives of a vector-valued function.  It describes the linear approximation of a function at a given point. The Jacobian determinant is the determinant of the Jacobian matrix, and it represents the scaling factor of volume transformation under the function.  |Example: In robotics, the Jacobian matrix is used to relate the joint velocities of a robot to the end-effector velocity.  The Jacobian determinant is used in change of variables in multiple integrals. |近义词：transformation matrix (in some contexts)|反义词：无
