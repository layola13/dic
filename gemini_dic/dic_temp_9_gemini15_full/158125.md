plateaued:/ˈplætoʊd/ | v. |进入高原期；达到稳定水平；停滞不前 | To reach a stage where further progress or development is slow or ceases.  | The company's profits plateaued after a period of rapid growth. 公司在快速增长一段时间后，利润增长进入高原期。|  level off, stabilize, stagnate | decline, decrease, fall


USAGE->

一般现在时:
Sales plateau this year.  = 今年销售额进入高原期。
Prices plateau at a high level. = 价格维持在高位。

一般过去时:
The project plateaued after six months. = 项目在六个月后进入停滞状态。
His career plateaued in his forties. = 他四十多岁时事业停滞不前。

现在完成时:
The economy has plateaued since the last quarter. = 自上季度以来，经济已进入平台期。
Progress on the negotiations has plateaued. = 谈判进展已陷入停滞。

过去完成时:
The researchers found that the data had plateaued before the experiment concluded. = 研究人员发现，实验结束前数据已进入平台期。
By the time they reached the summit, their enthusiasm had plateaued. = 当他们到达山顶时，他们的热情已经消退了。

将来时:
Analysts predict that inflation will plateau next year. = 分析师预测通货膨胀将在明年进入高原期。
We expect the project to plateau in the next few months. = 我们预计该项目将在未来几个月内进入平台期。

虚拟语气:
If the economy had not plateaued, we would have seen further growth. = 如果经济没有进入高原期，我们将看到进一步的增长。
I wish my writing skills hadn't plateaued so early in my career. = 我希望我的写作技能在我的职业生涯早期没有停滞不前。

被动语态:
The growth rate was plateaued by government intervention. = 增长率因政府干预而进入平台期。
The issue was plateaued by a lack of funding. = 由于缺乏资金，问题陷入停滞。

疑问句:
Has the number of applications plateaued? = 申请数量是否已达到饱和？
Did the stock price plateau last quarter? = 上季度股价是否进入平台期？

否定句:
The sales figures didn't plateau; they continued to rise. = 销售数据并没有进入高原期；它们仍在继续上升。
The disease didn't plateau; it continued to spread. = 疾病没有停滞，它继续蔓延。
