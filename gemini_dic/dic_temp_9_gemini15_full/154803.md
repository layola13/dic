perturbation:/ˌpɜːr.təˈbeɪ.ʃən/| n.|扰动；烦扰；微扰|A perturbation is a disturbance or deviation from a normal state or condition.  It can refer to a small change or variation, or a significant disruption.  The context determines the meaning.|例句：The unexpected rain caused a perturbation in their travel plans.  意外的雨水扰乱了他们的旅行计划。

The slight perturbation in the Earth's orbit is being studied by scientists. 科学家们正在研究地球轨道上的轻微扰动。|近义词：disturbance, disruption, upset, agitation|反义词：calm, peace, tranquility, stability|名词


USAGE->

The small change causes a perturbation in the system. = 细微的变化引起系统扰动。
This noise is a perturbation to my concentration. = 这种噪音扰乱了我的注意力。


一般过去时:
The earthquake caused a major perturbation in the city's infrastructure. = 地震对城市的基础设施造成了重大破坏。
Her sudden departure was a perturbation to the team. = 她突然离开让团队很不安。

词源:
Perturbation comes from the Latin word "perturbatio," meaning "disturbance" or "disorder."  It is formed from "per-" (thoroughly) and "turbare" (to disturb).
