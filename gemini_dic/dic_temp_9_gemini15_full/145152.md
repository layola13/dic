overassess:/ˌoʊvərəˈsɛs/| vt.|过高评估；高估|To assess something at too high a value or amount.|The committee overassessed the potential risks of the project.|近义词：overestimate, overvalue|反义词：underestimate, undervalue

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bank overassesses the value of many properties. = 银行对许多房产的估价过高。
They overassess the students' abilities in this exam. = 他们在这场考试中高估了学生的水平。

一般过去时:
The appraiser overassessed the antique chair. = 评估师高估了那把古董椅子的价值。
The teacher overassessed the difficulty of the assignment. = 老师高估了作业的难度。

现在完成时:
The government has overassessed the economic growth for the last quarter. = 政府高估了上一季度的经济增长。
They have overassessed the impact of the new policy. = 他们高估了新政策的影响。

过去完成时:
The investors had overassessed the company's potential before investing. = 投资者在投资前高估了公司的潜力。
The project manager had overassessed the time needed for completion. = 项目经理高估了完成项目所需的时间。

将来时:

The board expects that they will overassess the cost of the new building. = 董事会预计他们会高估新建筑的成本。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
