preemptive: /prɪˈɛmptɪv/ | adj. | 先发制人的；预防性的 |  Acting to prevent something from happening; anticipating and preventing a problem.  It refers to an action taken to prevent something else from happening. |

例句：

The government launched a preemptive strike against the rebels.  = 政府对叛军发起了先发制人的打击。

The doctor prescribed a preemptive medication to prevent the infection. = 医生开了预防性药物来预防感染。


近义词：proactive, preventative, anticipatory, forestalling


反义词：reactive, responsive, belated


词性：形容词
