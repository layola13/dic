overnationalization: /ˌoʊvərˌnæʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 超国家化 | The process of transferring power or authority from national governments to international or supranational organizations.  It often involves the delegation of certain governmental functions to international bodies, leading to a reduction in national sovereignty.  This can manifest in areas like economic policy, environmental regulations, or defense. | Example: The increasing overnationalization of environmental policy has led to greater international cooperation in addressing climate change.  The overnationalization of monetary policy through organizations like the European Central Bank is a significant aspect of European integration. |近义词: internationalization, supranationalization | 反义词: nationalism, nationalization
