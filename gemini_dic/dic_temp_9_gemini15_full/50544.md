deput:/ˈdɛpjʊt/| n.| 代理人，代表；副手 | A person appointed to act on behalf of another; a substitute or representative.  |The deputy manager handled the situation well in the manager's absence.  代理经理很好地处理了经理不在时的状况。|近义词：representative, substitute, delegate |反义词：principal, superior

一般现在时:
The deputy represents the mayor at the conference.  = 代理人代表市长参加会议。
He acts as a deputy for the head teacher. = 他担任校长助理。


一般过去时:
The deputy chaired the meeting yesterday. = 代理人昨天主持了会议。
She served as deputy to the director last year. = 去年她担任了主任的副手。


现在完成时:
The deputy has already addressed the concerns. = 代理人已经处理了这些问题。
He has successfully acted as deputy many times. = 他已经成功地担任过许多次代理人。


过去完成时:
The deputy had prepared the report before the meeting. = 代理人在会议之前已准备好报告。
She had served as deputy for five years before her promotion. = 在晋升之前，她已经担任了五年副手。


将来时:
The deputy will attend the negotiations tomorrow. = 代理人明天将参加谈判。
He will act as deputy during the manager's vacation. = 经理休假期间，他将担任代理人。


虚拟语气:
If he were deputy, he would have handled it differently. = 如果他是代理人，他会以不同的方式处理此事。
I wish the deputy had been more assertive. = 我希望代理人更果断一些。


被动语态:
The deputy was appointed by the mayor. = 代理人由市长任命。
The situation was handled well by the deputy. = 局势由代理人处理得很好。


疑问句:

否定句:
