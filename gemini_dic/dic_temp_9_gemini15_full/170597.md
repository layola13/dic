pseudo-Elizabethan:/ˌsuːdoʊɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən/| adj.|伪伊丽莎白式的；仿伊丽莎白时代的 |  Imitating or suggesting the style of the Elizabethan era (roughly 1558-1603) in England, often in a superficial or inaccurate way.  This term is typically applied to literature, architecture, fashion, or other artistic styles. | The play, while enjoyable, had a distinctly pseudo-Elizabethan feel, with its overblown language and contrived plots.  | 近义词：faux-Elizabethan, mock-Elizabethan | 反义词：authentically Elizabethan, genuine Elizabethan |  形容词

词源:

The word is a compound of "pseudo-" (from Greek *pseudēs*, meaning "false" or "sham") and "Elizabethan" (referring to the reign of Queen Elizabeth I of England).  Therefore, it literally means "falsely Elizabethan" or "imitation Elizabethan."
