leitmotif:/ˈlaɪtməʊtiːf/ | n. | 主导动机；反复出现的主题；领奏主题 | A leitmotif is a recurring theme throughout a musical or literary work, typically associated with a particular person, idea, or situation.  It helps to unify the work and develop its meaning.  | Example: The recurring image of the red rose served as a leitmotif throughout the novel, symbolizing the character's passionate but ultimately doomed love. | 近义词：recurring theme, motif, dominant idea | 反义词：None directly applicable.

词源:
The word "leitmotif" is a German loanword.  It combines "leit-" (meaning "leading" or "guiding") and "Motiv" (meaning "motif" or "theme").  It was popularized by Richard Wagner's use of the concept in his operas.
