preconcern:/ˌpriːkənˈsɜːrn/| n. |预先担忧；事先的顾虑 |A feeling of worry or anxiety about something that might happen in the future.  It suggests a concern that exists *before* the event or situation actually occurs. |例句：Her preconcern about the exam kept her awake all night.  她的考试焦虑让她整晚睡不着觉。

例句：The preconcern over potential budget cuts led to a series of emergency meetings. 对潜在预算削减的预先担忧导致了一系列紧急会议。


近义词：apprehension, anxiety, worry, misgiving, solicitude


反义词：confidence, assurance, equanimity


词性：名词
