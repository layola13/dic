parade:/pəˈreɪd/| n. v.|游行，阅兵；炫耀，夸示|A public procession, usually of people marching, often to celebrate something or to make a political statement.  It can also refer to the act of showing off something.|例句：The St. Patrick's Day parade was a huge success.  圣帕特里克节游行非常成功。

The soldiers paraded before the general. 士兵们在将军面前列队行进。|近义词：procession, demonstration, display|反义词：secrecy, concealment|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The band parades down Main Street every year.  乐队每年都在主街游行。
He parades his wealth ostentatiously. 他炫耀他的财富。


一般过去时:
Thousands participated in the victory parade. 数千人参加了胜利游行。
She paraded her new dress in front of her friends. 她在她朋友面前炫耀她的新裙子。


现在完成时:
They have paraded their achievements to the press. 他们向媒体炫耀了他们的成就。
The army has paraded its new weapons.  军队展示了它的新武器。


过去完成时:
The children had paraded their talents at the school fair. 孩子们在学校的集市上展示了他们的才能。
Before the war started, the country had paraded its military might. 在战争爆发前，这个国家曾展示过它的军事实力。


将来时:
We will parade through the city center tomorrow. 我们明天将在市中心游行。
The company will parade its new products at the trade show. 公司将在贸易展上展示其新产品。


虚拟语气:
If we had a parade, everyone would be happy. 如果我们举行游行，每个人都会很高兴。
I wish we could parade our success more often. 我希望我们能够更频繁地展示我们的成功。


被动语态:
The parade was watched by millions. 游行被数百万的人观看。
The new car was paraded in front of potential buyers. 这辆新车被展示在潜在买家面前。


疑问句:
Will there be a parade next year? 明年会有游行吗？
Is he parading his knowledge just to impress us? 他炫耀他的知识只是为了给我们留下印象吗？


否定句:
There wasn't a parade this year. 今年没有游行。
He didn't parade his accomplishments. 他没有炫耀他的成就。
