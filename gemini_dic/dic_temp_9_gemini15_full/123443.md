monopolizes:/məˈnɒpəlaɪzɪz/| vt.|垄断；独占；专营|To have or take control of something, especially so that other people cannot use or enjoy it.|例句：That company monopolizes the market for computer chips.  那家公司垄断了电脑芯片市场。|例句：He monopolizes the conversation, never letting anyone else speak. 他独占谈话，从不让别人说话。|近义词：dominate, control, corner,  grab|反义词：share, distribute, divide


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He monopolizes the conversation. = 他垄断了谈话。
The company monopolizes the market. = 这家公司垄断了市场。


一般过去时:
She monopolized the attention of the room. = 她独占了房间里所有人的注意力。
The government monopolized the sale of salt. = 政府垄断了食盐的销售。


现在完成时:
He has monopolized the discussion for too long. = 他已经独占讨论太久了。
The corporation has monopolized the industry for decades. = 这个公司几十年来一直垄断着这个行业。


过去完成时:
They had monopolized the resources before we arrived. = 我们到达之前，他们已经垄断了资源。
The family had monopolized the land for generations. = 这个家族世世代代垄断了这块土地。


将来时:
He will monopolize the spotlight at the awards ceremony. = 他将在颁奖典礼上独占风头。
The new technology will monopolize the market soon. = 新技术很快就会垄断市场。


虚拟语气:
If he monopolized the market, he would be very rich. = 如果他垄断了市场，他就会非常富有。
I wish he wouldn't monopolize the conversation. = 我希望他不至于独占谈话。


被动语态:
The market was monopolized by a single corporation. = 市场被一家公司垄断了。
The resources are being monopolized by a powerful group. = 资源正被一个强大的集团垄断。


疑问句:
Does he monopolize the conversation? = 他是不是垄断了谈话？
Is the company monopolizing the market? = 这家公司是不是正在垄断市场？


否定句:
He doesn't monopolize the conversation. = 他没有垄断谈话。
The company doesn't monopolize the market. = 这家公司没有垄断市场。


PHRASE->
