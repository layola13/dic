corporatism:/ˈkɔːrpərətɪzəm/| n. |公司主义；协商经济；集团主义 |A political or economic system in which representatives of businesses, labor, and the state work together to manage the economy and solve social problems.  This often involves the suppression of individual interests in favor of collective ones, often resulting in a close relationship between the government and major industries or corporations.  | Example:  The country's economic policy was heavily influenced by corporatism, with close collaboration between the government and major industrial groups. = 该国的经济政策受到公司主义的强烈影响，政府与主要工业集团之间密切合作。

The rise of corporatism in the 20th century led to significant changes in labor relations. = 20 世纪公司主义的兴起导致劳资关系发生了重大变化。|近义词：statism, collectivism |反义词：individualism, laissez-faire |名词
