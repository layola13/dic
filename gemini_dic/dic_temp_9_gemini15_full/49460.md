delude:/dɪˈluːd/| vt.|欺骗；迷惑；使产生错觉|To deceive or mislead someone, especially by making them believe something that is not true.  It often implies a more subtle or self-deceptive form of deception.|例句：He deluded himself into thinking he could win. 他自欺欺人地认为自己能赢。

例句：The advertisement deluded consumers into believing the product was superior. 这则广告欺骗消费者相信该产品是优质的。


近义词：deceive, mislead, trick, fool, beguile|反义词：enlighten, inform, reveal, unveil|词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deludes himself with false hopes. = 他用虚假的希望欺骗自己。
She deludes her followers with empty promises. = 她用空洞的承诺欺骗她的追随者。


一般过去时:
The magician deluded the audience with his tricks. = 魔术师用他的戏法迷惑了观众。
They deluded themselves into believing they were invincible. = 他们自欺欺人地认为自己是无敌的。


现在完成时:
He has deluded himself for years. = 他多年来一直自欺欺人。
She has deluded many people with her lies. = 她用谎言欺骗了许多人。


过去完成时:
They had deluded themselves into thinking they were safe. = 他们曾自欺欺人地认为自己是安全的。
He had deluded her into believing his story. = 他曾经欺骗她相信他的故事。


将来时:
He will delude himself again if he's not careful. = 如果他不小心，他还会再次自欺欺人。
The propaganda will delude many into supporting the war. = 这场宣传将会迷惑许多人去支持战争。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't been deluded, I would have acted differently. = 如果我没有被欺骗，我会采取不同的行动。
I wish I hadn't been deluded by his charm. = 我真希望我没有被他迷人的魅力所迷惑。


被动语态:
Many were deluded by the false promises. = 许多人被虚假的承诺所迷惑。
He was deluded into believing the impossible. = 他被欺骗相信不可能的事情。


疑问句:
Was he deluded by his own ambition? = 他是被自己的野心迷惑了吗？
Are they being deluded by the media? = 他们是被媒体欺骗了吗？


否定句:
He was not deluded by their flattery. = 他没有被他们的奉承所迷惑。
I am not deluded about the situation. = 我对形势没有产生错觉。


PHRASE->
be deluded by = 被……迷惑
delude oneself into believing = 自欺欺人地相信
delude sb into doing sth = 欺骗某人做某事
be deluded with = 被…迷惑
suffer from delusions = 患有妄想症

词源:
From Old French *deluder, from Latin ludere "to play".  The prefix "de-" implies "away from" or "out of," suggesting a sense of being led astray or away from the truth.
