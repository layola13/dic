quasi-equitably: /ˈkwɑːziˌekwɪtəbli/ | adj. | 准公平地；近似公平地 |

英文解析:  The word "quasi-equitably" describes a situation or action that is almost, but not completely, fair or just. It implies a degree of fairness but acknowledges a departure from perfect equity. The "quasi" prefix indicates something that resembles or has the characteristics of something else but is not entirely that thing.  It often suggests a compromise or an approximation of equitable treatment.

例句:

The inheritance was divided quasi-equitably, with the older sibling receiving a slightly larger share due to prior contributions to the family business. = 遗产分配得近似公平，哥哥因为之前对家族企业做出了贡献而获得略多一些的份额。


The judge attempted to resolve the dispute quasi-equitably, considering the unique circumstances of each party involved. = 法官试图近似公平地解决纠纷，考虑了每一方当事人的特殊情况。


近义词: fairly, reasonably, impartially, approximately equitably

反义词: inequitably, unfairly, unjustly, biasedly

词性: adjective (形容词)
