magian:/ˈmeɪdʒən/| n.|法师，术士，占星家|A magian is a person, especially a priest or religious leader, who practices magic or divination.  The term often refers to the wise men who visited the baby Jesus in the Christian nativity story.  It carries connotations of wisdom, mystical knowledge, and sometimes, sorcery.|The Magi brought gifts of gold, frankincense, and myrrh to the newborn Jesus.  |近义词：wizard, sorcerer, wise man|反义词：nonbeliever, skeptic
