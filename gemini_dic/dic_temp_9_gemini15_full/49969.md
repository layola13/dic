denigrated:/ˈdɛnɪɡreɪtɪd/| v.|贬低；诋毁；污蔑 | To attack the reputation or character of someone; to belittle or speak ill of.  |例句：He denigrated her work, claiming it was amateurish and lacking in originality. 他贬低了她的作品，声称它业余且缺乏原创性。

例句：The politician denigrated his opponent's policies in a series of televised debates.  这位政治家在一系列电视辩论中贬低了他的对手的政策。


近义词：belittle, disparage, defame, slander, vilify, traduce

反义词：praise, commend, exalt, laud, acclaim


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He denigrates his competitors' products. 他贬低竞争对手的产品。
They denigrate the value of hard work. 他们贬低努力工作的价值。


一般过去时:
She denigrated his achievements. 她贬低了他的成就。
The media denigrated the actor's performance. 媒体贬低了这位演员的表演。


现在完成时:
They have denigrated the reputation of the company. 他们已经损害了公司的声誉。
He has denigrated his own abilities. 他贬低了自己的能力。


过去完成时:
The critics had denigrated his earlier works. 批评家们贬低了他早期的作品。
She had denigrated her own contributions to the project. 她贬低了她自己对项目的贡献。


将来时:
They will denigrate the opposition's proposals. 他们将贬低反对派的提议。
He will denigrate his opponent in the upcoming election. 他将在即将到来的选举中贬低他的对手。


虚拟语气:
If he denigrated her work, she would be very upset. 如果他贬低她的作品，她会非常沮丧。
I wish they hadn't denigrated the efforts of the volunteers. 我希望他们没有贬低志愿者的努力。


被动语态:
His work was denigrated by the critics. 他的作品被评论家贬低了。
Her ideas were denigrated by the committee. 她的想法被委员会贬低了。


疑问句:
Did he denigrate her abilities? 他是否贬低了她的能力？
Why did they denigrate the importance of education? 为什么他们贬低教育的重要性？


否定句:
He did not denigrate her intelligence. 他没有贬低她的智力。
They did not denigrate the value of the research. 他们没有贬低这项研究的价值。


PHRASE->
denigrate someone's efforts  贬低某人的努力
denigrate someone's character 贬低某人的品格

systematically denigrate 系统性地贬低

词源:
From Latin *dēnigrāre, from dē- ("down, away") + nigrāre ("to blacken").  The original sense was to blacken or soil, which metaphorically extended to damaging reputation.
