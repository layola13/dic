nervewracking: /ˈnɜːrvˌrækiŋ/ | adj. | 令人紧张的，令人不安的 |  causing or characterized by great nervousness or anxiety.  |例句：The interview was incredibly nervewracking.  面试非常令人紧张。

例句：Waiting for the exam results was a nervewracking experience. 等待考试结果是一次令人紧张的经历。


近义词：stressful, anxiety-provoking, nerve-racking, unsettling, unnerving

反义词：relaxing, calming, reassuring, peaceful


词源:  The word is a compound of "nerve" and "wracking."  "Nerve" refers to the nervous system and the feelings associated with it,  while "wracking" means to cause great strain or distress. The combination suggests something that severely tests one's nerves.  "Wracking" itself comes from the verb "wrack," meaning to ruin or wreck.  In this context, it suggests a process of causing intense mental or emotional distress.
