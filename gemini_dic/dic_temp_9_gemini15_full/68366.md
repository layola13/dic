fallible:/ˈfæləbl/| adj.|易错的；会犯错误的|capable of making mistakes or being erroneous; imperfect.  |例句：Humans are fallible creatures. 人是容易犯错的生物。

例句：Even the most experienced surgeons are fallible.  即使是最有经验的外科医生也会犯错。


近义词：erroneous, imperfect, flawed, susceptible to error

反义词：infallible, perfect, impeccable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is fallible, just like everyone else. 他和其他人一样，是会犯错的。
Their judgment is often fallible. 他们的判断力往往不够准确。


一般过去时:
The system proved fallible in several key areas.  该系统在几个关键领域被证明是不可靠的。
His memory was fallible, and he often forgot important details. 他的记忆力不好，经常忘记重要的细节。


现在完成时:
We have learned that our initial assumptions have been fallible. 我们已经认识到我们最初的假设是错误的。
His methods have proven fallible over time. 他的方法随着时间的推移已被证明是不可靠的。


过去完成时:
They had realized their plan was fallible before it was too late. 他们在为时未晚之前就意识到他们的计划是不可靠的。
I had already suspected that my source of information was fallible. 我已经怀疑我的信息来源不可靠了。


将来时:
The new system will likely prove less fallible than the old one. 新系统可能会比旧系统更可靠。
Future research will show if these theories are fallible. 未来的研究将表明这些理论是否错误。


虚拟语气:
If only human beings weren't fallible! 如果人类不会犯错就好了！
I wish my memory wasn't so fallible. 我真希望我的记性不要这么差。


被动语态:
The argument was shown to be fallible.  论点被证明是错误的。
The assumptions underlying the theory have been found to be fallible. 该理论的基本假设已被证明是错误的。


疑问句:
Is this method fallible?  这种方法不可靠吗？
Are human judgments always fallible? 人类的判断总是错误的吗？


否定句:
The machine is not fallible; it operates with perfect precision. 这台机器不会出错；它运行精确无误。
His calculations were not fallible; they were meticulous and accurate. 他的计算没有错误；它们一丝不苟且准确无误。

词源:
From Late Latin *fallibilis, from Latin fallere "to deceive, fail, err."  最终源于拉丁语fallere，意为“欺骗，失败，犯错”。
