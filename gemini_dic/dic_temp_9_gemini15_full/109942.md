liberal/ˈlɪbərəl/ | adj. |自由主义的；慷慨的；宽容的 |  Characterized by or advocating for social, political, and economic freedom and reform; generous or tolerant.  |  He holds liberal views on social issues. 他对社会问题持有自由主义的观点。  The professor was known for her liberal grading policies.  这位教授以其宽松的评分标准而闻名。 | conservative, progressive | authoritarian, restrictive |  


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a liberal thinker. = 他是一位自由主义思想家。
She has liberal views on education. = 她对教育持有自由主义的观点。


一般过去时:
They were liberal with their donations. = 他们慷慨捐款。
The government adopted a more liberal immigration policy. = 政府采取了更为宽松的移民政策。


现在完成时:
The party has become more liberal over the years. = 这个政党多年来变得越来越自由主义。
Society has become more liberal in its attitudes towards homosexuality. = 社会对同性恋的态度变得更加宽容。


过去完成时:
The country had been more liberal before the war. = 战争爆发前，这个国家更加自由。
They had held liberal beliefs for many years. = 他们多年来一直持有自由主义的信念。


将来时:
The policy will become more liberal in the future. = 未来这项政策将会更加宽松。
We believe the country will become more liberal. = 我们相信这个国家将会变得更加自由。


虚拟语气:
If the government were more liberal, there would be less poverty. = 如果政府更自由一些，贫困人口就会更少。
I wish the society were more liberal. = 我希望社会更自由一些。


被动语态:
The laws were interpreted liberally by the courts. = 法律被法院自由解释。
His ideas were considered too liberal by some. = 他的想法被一些人认为过于自由。


疑问句:
Are you a liberal or a conservative? = 你是自由主义者还是保守主义者？
Is this a liberal interpretation of the law? = 这是对法律的自由解释吗？


否定句:
He is not a liberal in the traditional sense. = 他并非传统意义上的自由主义者。
Her views are not particularly liberal. = 她的观点并非特别自由。
