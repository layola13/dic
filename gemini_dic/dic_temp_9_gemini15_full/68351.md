fallaciously:/fəˈleɪʃəsli/ | adv. | 谬误地；虚假地 | In a way that is based on a mistaken belief.  It suggests that something is wrong or untrue because it is based on a faulty argument or a misunderstanding. | Example: He fallaciously argued that because some birds can fly, all birds must be able to fly. 他谬误地认为，因为有些鸟会飞，所以所有鸟都必须能够飞。 | 近义词：erroneously, falsely, wrongly, incorrectly | 反义词：correctly, accurately, truthfully, validly
