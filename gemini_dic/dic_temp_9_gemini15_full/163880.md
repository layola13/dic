predestined:/ˌpriːˈdɛstɪnd/| adj. |命中注定的，预定的 | predetermined by fate; inevitable.  It implies a lack of free will and an unavoidable outcome.| His predestined path led him to become a great leader.  他的命中注定之路使他成为了一位伟大的领导者。|近义词：fated, destined, preordained |反义词：accidental, coincidental, random


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This event is predestined to happen. = 这件事注定要发生。
The future is predestined. = 未来是注定的。


一般过去时:
The outcome was predestined from the start. = 结果从一开始就注定了。
They believed their meeting was predestined. = 他们相信他们的相遇是命中注定的。


现在完成时:
Everything has been predestined since the beginning of time. = 自古以来，一切都是注定的。
It has been predestined that they would meet again. = 他们注定会再次相遇。


过去完成时:
It was predestined that he would fail before he even started.  = 他甚至还没开始就注定要失败。
The decision had already been predestined. = 决定早已注定。


将来时:
The prophecy states that the hero's death is predestined. = 预言指出英雄的死是注定的。
Their marriage is predestined to be happy. = 他们的婚姻注定幸福。


虚拟语气:
If it were predestined, we couldn't change it. = 如果是命中注定的，我们就无法改变它。
I wish my future wasn't predestined. = 我希望我的未来不是注定的。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
