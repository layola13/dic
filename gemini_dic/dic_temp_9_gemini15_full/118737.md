metal:/ˈmɛtəl/| n.|金属|A solid material that is typically hard, shiny, and able to conduct electricity and heat. Metals are often malleable and ductile, meaning they can be shaped and drawn into wires.  Examples include iron, gold, and aluminum. |例句：Steel is a type of metal. 钢是一种金属。

The bridge is made of metal. 这座桥是用金属建造的。|近义词：mineral, alloy|反义词：nonmetal
