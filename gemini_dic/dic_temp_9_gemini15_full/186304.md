rhodopsin:/ˈroʊdəpsɪn/| n. |视紫红质|A light-sensitive pigment found in the rod cells of the retina, responsible for vision in low-light conditions.  It is a protein that changes shape when exposed to light, triggering a cascade of events that lead to the transmission of visual signals to the brain.|Example: Rhodopsin plays a crucial role in night vision. 视紫红质在夜视中起着至关重要的作用。|近义词：visual pigment|反义词：无
