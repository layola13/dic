catnapping:/ˈkætˌnæpɪŋ/| n. v.|小睡；打盹儿|The act of taking a short nap, usually unexpectedly or unintentionally.  It often implies a brief sleep that is not planned.|例句：She was caught catnapping at her desk. 她被发现趴在桌子上打盹儿。

例句：The meeting was so boring that I found myself catnapping. 会议太无聊了，我发现自己不知不觉睡着了。


近义词：dozing, napping, sleeping lightly, slumbering|反义词：being awake, being alert|词性:noun and verb

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He catnaps every afternoon. = 他每天下午都会小睡一会儿。
She often catnaps on the couch. = 她经常在沙发上打盹。

一般过去时:
I catnapped during the lecture. = 我在讲座期间打了个盹。
He catnapped for twenty minutes. = 他小睡了二十分钟。


现在完成时:
I have catnapped several times today. = 我今天已经打了好几次盹了。
She has catnapped through most of the movie. = 她几乎在电影的整个过程中都在打盹。


过去完成时:
I had catnapped before the boss came in. = 老板进来之前我打了个盹。
They had catnapped for hours before waking up. = 他们睡了好几个小时才醒来。


将来时:
I will catnap after lunch. = 我午餐后会小睡一会儿。
She will probably catnap on the plane. = 她可能在飞机上会打盹。


虚拟语气:
If I were tired, I would catnap. = 如果我累了，我会小睡一会儿。
I wish I could catnap right now. = 我真希望现在能打个盹。


被动语态:
The cat was often catnapped by the child.  = 那只猫经常被孩子抱去小睡。 (Note: This usage is less common for 'catnapping' itself, but illustrates the passive voice.)

否定句:
