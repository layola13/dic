outfoxing: /ˌaʊtˈfɑːksɪŋ/ | v. |  智胜，以计谋战胜 |  To outfox someone means to be more clever and cunning than them, often to gain an advantage or win a competition. It implies a degree of trickery or strategic thinking. | 例句：The detective outfoxed the cunning criminal by anticipating his every move.  侦探通过预料罪犯的每一个举动而智胜了狡猾的罪犯。


The clever fox outfoxed the hunter by leading him on a wild goose chase.  狡猾的狐狸通过把猎人带到一场徒劳无功的追逐中而智胜了他。


近义词：outsmart, outwit, outmaneuver,  trick, deceive


反义词：be outwitted, be tricked, be deceived


词性：动词
