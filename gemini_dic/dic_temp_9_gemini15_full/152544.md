pcm:/ˈpiːsiːɛm/|abbr.|脉冲编码调制|Pulse Code Modulation, a method used to convert analog signals into digital signals for transmission and storage.  It involves sampling the analog signal at regular intervals, quantizing the samples to discrete levels, and then encoding these levels into binary code. This allows for accurate and efficient transmission and storage of audio and video signals.|Example:  PCM is commonly used in digital audio recording and broadcasting.  PCM是常用的数字音频录制和广播技术。|近义词：digital audio, digital encoding|反义词：analog signal
