pantography:/pænˈtɒɡrəfi/| n. |泛影复制术，全景复制术|The process of making a copy of a drawing or map, typically on a larger or smaller scale, using a pantograph.  It's a mechanical device that uses levers and linkages to reproduce images proportionally. |Example:  The cartographer used pantography to create a large-scale map from a smaller original.  制图师用泛影复制术从较小的原图制作了一张大比例尺的地图。|近义词：copying, reproduction, duplication|反义词：original, unique|名词
