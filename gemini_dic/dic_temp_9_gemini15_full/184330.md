respectful:/rɪˈspektfəl/| adj.|恭敬的；尊敬的；尊重人的；有礼貌的 | Showing or feeling deference and respect.  It implies a considerate and polite manner towards others, often stemming from a genuine appreciation for their worth or position. |例句：He was always respectful to his elders. 他总是尊敬长辈。  She showed respectful attention during the lecture. 她在讲座中表现出恭敬的态度。|近义词：courteous, polite, deferential, considerate |反义词：disrespectful, rude, impolite, insolent


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is respectful to everyone he meets. = 他对遇到的每个人都很有礼貌。
They are respectful of their teachers’ opinions. = 他们尊重老师们的意见。


一般过去时:
She was respectful of the traditions of her culture. = 她尊重自己文化的传统。
He was respectful in his response to criticism. = 他对批评的回应很尊重。


现在完成时:
They have been respectful in their dealings with clients. = 他们与客户交往一直很尊重。
She has always been respectful of other people's feelings. = 她一直很尊重别人的感受。


过去完成时:
He had been respectful of the rules, so he was surprised by the punishment. = 他一直很尊重规则，所以他对惩罚感到惊讶。
They had been respectful throughout the negotiation. = 他们在谈判中一直都很尊重。


将来时:
We will be respectful of the environment in our new project. = 我们将在新的项目中尊重环境。
She will be respectful to the judges during the competition. = 她在比赛中会尊重评委。


虚拟语气:
If he were more respectful, people would like him better. = 如果他更有礼貌，人们会更喜欢他。
I wish he were more respectful towards his colleagues. = 我希望他对同事更尊重。


被动语态:
His opinions were respectfully considered by the committee. = 委员会恭敬地考虑了他的意见。
The rules should be respectfully followed. = 规则应该被恭敬地遵守。


疑问句:
Was he respectful during the interview? = 他在面试中是否有礼貌？
Are they respectful of the law? = 他们尊重法律吗？


否定句:
He wasn't respectful to his parents. = 他对父母不尊重。
Their behavior was not respectful of the occasion. = 他们的行为不符合当时的场合。


PHRASE->
