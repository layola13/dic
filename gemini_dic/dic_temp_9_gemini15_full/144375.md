outstolen:/ˌaʊtˈstoʊlən/| adj.| 被偷走的，被盗取的 |  Describes something that has been stolen, often implying a significant or noteworthy theft.  It emphasizes the completeness or thoroughness of the theft. | Example: The museum's most prized artifact was outstolen, leaving a gaping hole in its collection.  博物馆最珍贵的文物被盗了，留下了巨大的空缺。|近义词：stolen, pilfered, robbed, purloined |反义词：secured, safe, protected |
