demobilization: /ˌdiːməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 复员；遣散；解散 | The process of disbanding or releasing troops from military service; the act of returning soldiers to civilian life.  It also refers to the dismantling of a mobilized organization or system. | Example: The demobilization of the army after the war took several months.  战后军队复员花了几个月时间。

The demobilization process was slow and complex, causing significant social and economic challenges. 复员过程缓慢而复杂，造成了重大的社会和经济挑战。


近义词：discharge, disbandment, decommissioning, stand-down

反义词：mobilization


词源:  From "demobilize" + "-ation".  "Demobilize" itself is a combination of "de-" (meaning "to remove" or "reverse") and "mobilize" (from Latin "mobilis," meaning "movable").  Therefore, demobilization literally means "the act of removing mobilization."
