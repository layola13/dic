cohabitor:/koʊˈhæbɪtər/ | n. |同居者 | A person who lives with another person, especially a romantic partner, without being married.  |Example: They are cohabitors who have lived together for five years. 他们是一对同居五年了的伴侣。|近义词：partner, roommate (if not romantic), significant other |反义词：spouse (if married)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a cohabitor with his girlfriend. = 他和他的女朋友同居。
They are cohabitors and share household expenses. = 他们是同居者，共同承担家务支出。


一般过去时:
They were cohabitors before they got married. = 他们结婚前是同居关系。
He was a cohabitor for several years before finding a wife. = 在找到妻子之前，他同居了好几年。


现在完成时:
They have been cohabitors for over a decade. = 他们同居已经超过十年了。
We have been cohabitors since college. = 我们从大学时代就开始了同居生活。


过去完成时:
They had been cohabitors for two years when they decided to marry. = 当他们决定结婚时，他们已经同居了两年。
Before moving in together, they had been cohabitors for a short time. = 在搬到一起住之前，他们有过短暂的同居。


将来时:
They will be cohabitors until they get married. = 他们会在结婚前一直同居。
We will continue to be cohabitors even after the baby arrives. = 宝宝出生后我们还会继续同居。


虚拟语气:
If they were cohabitors, they would share expenses equally. = 如果他们是同居者，他们会平均分摊开支。
I wish I had been a cohabitor before marriage to better understand living together. = 我真希望在结婚前有过同居的经历，以便更好地了解一起生活。


被动语态:
The couple were not considered cohabitors by their families. = 这对夫妇没有被他们的家人视为同居者。
Cohabitors are often overlooked in legal documents. = 同居者在法律文件中经常被忽视。


疑问句:
Are you a cohabitor with your partner? = 你和你的伴侣是同居关系吗？
Were they cohabitors before their wedding? = 他们结婚前是同居关系吗？


否定句:
They are not cohabitors; they are just good friends. = 他们不是同居关系，只是好朋友。
We were not cohabitors; we just stayed at each other's places occasionally. = 我们不是同居关系，只是偶尔住在彼此家里。
