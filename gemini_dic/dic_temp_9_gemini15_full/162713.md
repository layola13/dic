prebankruptcy: /ˌpriːˈbæŋkrʌptsi/ | adj. | 破产前的，破产之前的 | Relating to the period of time immediately before a person or company declares bankruptcy.  This term describes the events, actions, or conditions leading up to a bankruptcy filing.  |  例句：The company's prebankruptcy financial statements showed significant losses.  这家公司破产前的财务报表显示了巨额亏损。

例句：Prebankruptcy negotiations with creditors proved unsuccessful. 破产前的债权人谈判以失败告终。


近义词：pre-insolvency, pre-failure


反义词：postbankruptcy, post-failure


词性：形容词
