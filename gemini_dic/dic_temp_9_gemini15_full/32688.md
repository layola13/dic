chlamydias:/klæˈmɪdiəz/| n. |衣原体(复数)|Chlamydias are a group of bacteria that can cause various infections in humans, including sexually transmitted infections (STIs). They are obligate intracellular parasites, meaning they can only survive and reproduce inside the cells of a host organism.|Example:  Chlamydias are responsible for a significant number of cases of pelvic inflammatory disease. 衣原体是许多盆腔炎病例的罪魁祸首。|近义词:  bacteria (广义)|反义词:  N/A|名词
