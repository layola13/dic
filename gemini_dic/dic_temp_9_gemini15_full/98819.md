intellectualize: /ɪnˌtɛləktʃuəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使理性化；过度理性地解释 | To explain or interpret something in an overly intellectual or abstract way, often to the point of ignoring or minimizing its emotional or practical aspects.  | 例句：He tried to intellectualize his grief by analyzing its psychological roots. 他试图通过分析悲伤的心理根源来使其理性化。

He intellectualized his feelings, avoiding genuine emotional expression. 他将自己的情感理性化，避免了真正的感情表达。


近义词：rationalize, overthink, analyze excessively, overintellectualize | 反义词：emotionalize, romanticize


词源:  From "intellectual" + "-ize" (to make or become).  "Intellectual" itself derives from the Latin word "intellectus," meaning "understanding" or "intellect."
