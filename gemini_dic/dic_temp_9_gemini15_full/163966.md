predicate:/ˈprɛdɪkət/| n. v.|谓语,断言|The part of a sentence or clause containing a verb and stating something about the subject (noun phrase).  A predicate describes what the subject does or is. |例句：In the sentence "The cat sat on the mat," "sat on the mat" is the predicate.  在句子“猫坐在垫子上”中，“坐在垫子上”是谓语。|近义词：assertion, affirmation, declaration|反义词：subject


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The boy plays the piano. = 男孩弹钢琴。
She sings beautifully. = 她唱得很好听。


一般过去时:
He ate dinner. = 他吃了晚饭。
They went to the park. = 他们去了公园。


现在完成时:
I have finished my work. = 我已经完成了我的工作。
She has lived here for ten years. = 她在这里住了十年了。


过去完成时:
They had already left when I arrived. = 当我到达时，他们已经离开了。
We had eaten before the movie started. = 电影开始之前我们已经吃过了。


将来时:
We will go to the beach tomorrow. = 我们明天要去海滩。
He will study hard for the exam. = 他将为考试努力学习。


虚拟语气:
If I were a bird, I would fly. = 如果我是一只鸟，我会飞。
I wish I had more time. = 我希望我有更多的时间。


被动语态:
The cake was eaten. = 蛋糕被吃掉了。
The book is being read. = 这本书正在被阅读。


疑问句:
Does he like pizza? = 他喜欢披萨吗？
Is she coming to the party? = 她来参加聚会吗？


否定句:
He does not like broccoli. = 他不喜欢西兰花。
She did not go to school today. = 她今天没去上学。
