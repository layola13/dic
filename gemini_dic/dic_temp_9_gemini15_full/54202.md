disfavor:/dɪsˈfeɪvər/ | n. | 不喜欢，反感，失宠 |  Disfavor is a noun referring to a lack of approval or liking;  a state of being disliked or out of favor. It can also refer to the act of showing disapproval. | The new policy fell into disfavor with the public.  新的政策不受公众欢迎。| He incurred the disfavor of his boss. 他惹恼了他的老板。 | favor, approval, acceptance | favor, approval, acceptance


USAGE->

The manager shows disfavor towards his lazy employee. = 经理对懒惰的员工表示不满。
Her proposal finds little disfavor among the board members. = 她的提议在董事会成员中几乎没有遭到反对。
