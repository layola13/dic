leg-up:/ˈlɛɡ ʌp/| n. |帮助，支持|A leg-up is assistance or support given to someone to help them achieve something.  It often implies a boost or a significant advantage.|例句：He gave me a leg-up into the truck. 他帮我爬上了卡车。

例句：Her family connections gave her a real leg-up in her career. 她的人脉关系给了她在事业上很大的帮助。


近义词：help, assistance, boost, advantage, support |反义词：hindrance, obstacle, impediment|词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gives me a leg-up whenever I need it. 他在我需要的时候都会帮助我。
She always provides a leg-up to her younger siblings. 她总是帮助她的弟弟妹妹们。


一般过去时:
My mentor gave me a leg-up in my early career. 我的导师在我职业生涯早期给了我很大帮助。
They gave him a leg-up over the fence. 他们帮他翻过了栅栏。


现在完成时:
This program has given many young people a leg-up in life. 这个项目帮助了许多年轻人改善生活。
The scholarship has given her a leg-up in her education. 这份奖学金帮助她更好地接受教育。


过去完成时:
He had given her a leg-up with the project before she even started.  在他开始之前，他已经帮她完成了项目的一部分。
They had given him a leg-up in his business before he became successful. 在他成功之前，他们已经帮助他的事业了。


将来时:
The new policy will give small businesses a leg-up. 新政策将扶持小型企业。
She will give her son a leg-up in his studies. 她将帮助她的儿子学习。


虚拟语气:
If I had had a leg-up, I would have succeeded earlier. 如果我当时得到帮助，我会更早成功。
I wish I had had a leg-up when I first started my business. 我真希望在我刚开始创业时能得到帮助。


被动语态:
He was given a leg-up by his uncle. 他得到了他叔叔的帮助。
She was given a leg-up into the prestigious university. 她得到了进入这所名牌大学的机会。


疑问句:
Did you get a leg-up with that project? 你在那个项目中得到帮助了吗？
Will she give him a leg-up in his career? 她会帮助他的职业生涯吗？


否定句:
He didn't get a leg-up from anyone. 他没有得到任何人的帮助。
She didn't receive a leg-up in her studies. 她的学习没有得到任何帮助。
