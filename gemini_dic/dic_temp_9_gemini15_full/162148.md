pre-Gothics: /ˌpriːˈɡɒθɪks/ | adj. | 前哥特式的 |  Referring to the architectural style prevalent in Europe before the emergence of the Gothic style, typically characterized by Romanesque features.  It encompasses a period of transition and stylistic evolution leading up to the fully developed Gothic. | Example: The church's pre-Gothic architecture displays strong Romanesque influences, particularly in its rounded arches and massive walls.  这教堂的前哥特式建筑展现出强烈的罗马式影响，尤其体现在其圆拱和厚重的墙壁上。| 近义词: Romanesque, proto-Gothic | 反义词: Gothic
