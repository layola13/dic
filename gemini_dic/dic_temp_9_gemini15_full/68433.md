falter:/ˈfɔːltər/ /ˈfɔːltɚ/ | vi.| 犹豫；蹒跚；衰弱；支吾其词 | To move unsteadily or walk with difficulty; to hesitate or waver.  |例句：His voice faltered as he tried to speak. 他说话时声音颤抖。

例句：The negotiations faltered due to a lack of trust.  由于缺乏信任，谈判陷入僵局。


近义词：hesitate, stammer, stumble, waver, weaken | 反义词：proceed smoothly, confidently advance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He falters when he tries to sing. = 他唱歌时会犹豫。
The old man falters as he walks. = 老人走路时步履蹒跚。

一般过去时:
My voice faltered during the presentation. = 我在演示期间声音颤抖了。
The project faltered due to funding issues. = 项目由于资金问题而停滞不前。

现在完成时:
The economy has faltered in recent months. = 近几个月来经济发展乏力。
The team has faltered in their attempts to win. = 该队在获胜的尝试中屡屡失败。

过去完成时:
The plan had faltered before it even started. = 计划甚至在开始之前就失败了。
She had faltered several times before finally speaking. = 她几次犹豫之后才最终开口。

将来时:
I fear my courage will falter in the face of danger. = 我害怕在危险面前我的勇气会动摇。
The negotiations will likely falter without compromise. = 如果没有妥协，谈判很可能会失败。


虚拟语气:
If my resolve faltered, I would fail. = 如果我的决心动摇，我就会失败。
I wish my voice hadn't faltered during the speech. = 我真希望我在演讲中声音没有颤抖。

被动语态:
The negotiations were faltered by internal disputes. = 谈判因内部纠纷而受阻。
His confidence was faltered by the criticism. = 他的信心因批评而动摇。

疑问句:
Will your courage falter under pressure? = 你在压力下会胆怯吗？
Does his voice falter when he's nervous? = 他紧张的时候声音会颤抖吗？

否定句:
His determination did not falter despite the setbacks. = 尽管遭遇挫折，他的决心没有动摇。
The economy did not falter even during the crisis. = 即使在危机期间，经济也没有衰退。
