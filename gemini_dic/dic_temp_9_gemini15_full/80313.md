goldbricked: /ˈɡoʊldˌbrɪkt/ | v. |偷懒; 逃避工作 | To avoid work or one's duties by pretending to be busy or working.  It implies deceptively avoiding responsibilities. |例句：He goldbricked all day, hoping nobody would notice his lack of productivity. 他整天偷懒，希望能没人注意到他的低效。

例句：The employees were caught goldbricking during working hours. 员工们被发现上班时间偷懒。


近义词：shirk, slack, malinger, goof off | 反义词：work diligently, be productive

词源:
The term "goldbricking" originates from the practice of using inferior, often gilded, bricks in construction. This practice was seen as a form of deception, much like avoiding work while appearing busy. The term therefore evolved to describe the act of deception and avoidance of duty.
