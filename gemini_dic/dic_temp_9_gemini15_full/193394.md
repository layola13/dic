seise:/ˈsiːz/| v.|没收；占有；掌握|To take possession of (something, especially by force or legal right).|The government seised the illegal drugs.|近义词：confiscate, seize, appropriate|反义词：release, relinquish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The police seize illegal weapons. = 警方没收非法武器。
She seises every opportunity to learn. = 她抓住每一个学习的机会。

一般过去时:
They seized the land from its rightful owners. = 他们从合法的所有者那里夺取了土地。
The rebels seized control of the city. = 反叛者夺取了该城市的控制权。

现在完成时:
The authorities have seized a large quantity of drugs. = 当局已经查获了大量的毒品。
He has seized the initiative in the negotiations. = 他在谈判中掌握了主动权。

过去完成时:
The police had seized the evidence before the suspect fled. = 嫌疑人逃跑前，警方已经查获了证据。
The army had seized power before the people realized what was happening. = 人民意识到发生了什么事之前，军队已经夺取了政权。

将来时:
The customs officers will seize any contraband goods. = 海关官员将没收任何违禁品。
She will seize the opportunity to present her ideas. = 她将抓住机会展示她的想法。

虚拟语气:
If the police had seized the drugs earlier, fewer people would have suffered. = 如果警方早点查获毒品，就不会有那么多人受害了。
I wish I had seized the chance to travel abroad. = 我真希望我抓住了出国旅行的机会。

被动语态:
The illegal goods were seized by the customs officials. = 违禁品被海关官员没收了。
The opportunity was seized by a rival company. = 机会被一家竞争公司抓住了。

疑问句:
Was the property seized legally? = 这处房产是否合法没收？
Will the stolen goods be seized? = 赃物会被没收吗？

否定句:
The police did not seize any weapons. = 警方没有缴获任何武器。
He did not seize the opportunity to speak. = 他没有抓住机会发言。


PHRASE->
