goofier:/ˈɡuːfiər/| adj.|更傻的，更滑稽的，更愚蠢的 | More foolish or silly; more clumsy or inept.  It is the comparative form of "goofy".| 例句：His goofier antics had everyone laughing. 他的滑稽举动逗得大家哈哈大笑。

His goofier behavior got him into trouble. 他更傻的行为让他惹上了麻烦。


近义词：sillier, more absurd, more ridiculous, more clownish | 反义词：serious, sensible, wise, clever


词源:  The word "goofy" itself has uncertain origins.  Some etymologists suggest it's a variant of "gauf," an archaic word meaning "stupid" or "foolish," while others trace it to a possible connection with the slang term "gaffe,"  referring to a social blunder.  The "-ier" suffix adds the comparative meaning, indicating a greater degree of goofiness.
