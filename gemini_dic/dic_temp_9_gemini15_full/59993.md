editor:/ˈɛdɪtər/ | n. |编辑，校订者 | A person who prepares written material for publication or broadcast, typically by correcting, revising, or adapting it.  | The editor carefully reviewed the manuscript before publication.  这位编辑在出版前仔细审查了稿件。 | publisher, reviser, proofreader | writer, author |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The editor edits the manuscript. = 编辑正在编辑稿件。
She edits articles for a magazine. = 她为杂志编辑文章。

一般过去时:
The editor edited the book last year. = 编辑去年编辑了这本书。
He edited the article before submitting it. = 他在提交文章之前编辑了它。

现在完成时:
The editor has edited many books. =这位编辑已经编辑过很多书了。
She has edited this article twice. = 她已经编辑过这篇文章两次了。

过去完成时:
The editor had edited the manuscript before the deadline. = 编辑在截止日期前已经编辑好了稿件。
They had edited the report before presenting it. = 他们在提交报告之前已经编辑过了。

将来时:
The editor will edit the next chapter tomorrow. = 编辑明天将编辑下一章。
She will edit the article after the meeting. = 会后她将编辑这篇文章。

虚拟语气:
If the editor were here, he would edit it immediately. = 如果编辑在这里，他会立即编辑它。
I wish the editor had edited my paper more carefully. = 我希望编辑能更仔细地编辑我的论文。

被动语态:
The manuscript was edited by the editor. = 稿件是由编辑编辑的。
The article is being edited now. = 文章正在被编辑。

疑问句:
Who is the editor of this magazine? = 这本杂志的编辑是谁？
Will the editor finish editing today? = 编辑今天能完成编辑吗？

否定句:
The editor did not edit the article. = 编辑没有编辑这篇文章。
She hasn't finished editing the book yet. = 她还没有完成这本书的编辑。
