sanction: /ˈsæŋkʃən/ | n., vt. | 制裁；批准；认可 |

英文解析:As a noun, sanction refers to an official permission or approval.  As a verb, it means to give official permission or approval to something, or to impose a penalty for violating a law or rule.  The meaning can vary depending on context; it can refer to both positive (approval) and negative (punishment) actions.

例句(noun):

The government gave its sanction to the new project. = 政府批准了这个新项目。

The UN imposed sanctions on the country for its human rights violations. = 联合国因该国侵犯人权而对其实施制裁。


例句(verb):

The school sanctioned the use of cell phones in classrooms. = 学校批准在教室里使用手机。(Positive sanction)

The company sanctioned the employee for violating company policy. = 公司因违反公司政策而对员工进行处罚。(Negative sanction)


近义词: (noun) approval, authorization, permission, penalty, punishment; (verb) authorize, approve, permit, punish, penalize


反义词: (noun) prohibition, disapproval, rejection; (verb) forbid, prohibit, disapprove


词性: noun, verb
