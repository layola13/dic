nonuniversally: /ˌnɑːnˌjuːnɪˈvɜːrsəli/ | adv. |并非普遍地;并非到处都;并非对所有人|Not universally; not in all cases or for all people. It implies that something is true or applicable in some instances but not in others.|


例句：

The practice is nonuniversally accepted.  = 这种做法并非普遍被接受。

This rule is nonuniversally applied. = 这条规则并非普遍适用。


近义词：partially, selectively, locally, regionally


反义词：universally, globally, completely, entirely


词源：

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of".
* **universally**: adverb meaning "in every case, everywhere, or by everyone".

The word is formed by combining the negative prefix "non-" with the adverb "universally," creating a word that expresses the opposite of universal application or acceptance.
