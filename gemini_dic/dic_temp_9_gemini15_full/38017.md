complot:/kəmˈplɒt/| n.|密谋；阴谋|A secret plan by a group of people to do something illegal or harmful.  A plot or conspiracy, especially one of a political nature.|例句：The police uncovered a complot to overthrow the government.  警方揭露了一个推翻政府的阴谋。|例句：They were accused of being involved in a complot to assassinate the president. 他们被指控参与暗杀总统的阴谋。|近义词：conspiracy, plot, scheme, intrigue|反义词：openness, transparency|名词

词源:
The word "complot" comes from the Old French word "complot," which means "a plot, a conspiracy."  It is ultimately derived from the Latin word "complector," meaning "I embrace, I entwine."  This reflects the idea of people coming together in a secret agreement.
