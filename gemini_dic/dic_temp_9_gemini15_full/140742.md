omniscience:/ˈɒmnɪsɪəns/| /ˈɑːmnɪsɪəns/ | n. |全知；无所不知 |The state of knowing everything; infinite awareness.  Omniscience is often attributed to God in religious contexts. |例句：The omniscience of God is a central tenet of many religions. = 上帝的全知是许多宗教的核心信条。

例句：His plan seemed to be based on an impossible omniscience of future events. = 他的计划似乎是基于对未来事件不可能的全知。


近义词：omnificence, prescience |反义词：ignorance, nescience


词源：From Latin *omniscient-, from omnis ‘all’ + sciēns, present participle of scire ‘to know’.
