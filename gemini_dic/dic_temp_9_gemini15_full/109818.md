/ˈlɛksɪmz/| n. |词素；词位|The word "lexemes" refers to the basic units of meaning in a language, which are often, but not always, individual words.  A lexeme is an abstract unit, representing a family of related word forms (inflections). For example, "run," "runs," "running," and "ran" are all forms of the same lexeme. |The linguist analyzed the lexemes of the ancient language to understand its grammatical structure.  语言学家分析了古代语言的词素以理解其语法结构。|近义词：morpheme, word (in some contexts)|反义词：N/A|名词
