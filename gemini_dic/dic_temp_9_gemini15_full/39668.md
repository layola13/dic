conservatorship:/ˌkɒnsɜːvəˈtɔːrʃɪp/ /ˌkɑːnsɜːrvətɔːrʃɪp/ | n. |监护；保管；保护；管理 |The legal right to manage the property and affairs of someone deemed incapable of doing so themselves.  This often involves financial matters but can extend to other aspects of a person's life.  A conservatorship is usually granted by a court. | 例句：The court appointed a conservator to manage the singer's finances during her illness. 法院任命了一名监护人管理这位歌手在生病期间的财务。

The conservatorship of Britney Spears became a highly publicized legal battle. 布兰妮·斯皮尔斯 的监护权成为一场备受瞩目的法律纠纷。| 近义词：guardianship, trusteeship, custodianship | 反义词：self-governance, autonomy
