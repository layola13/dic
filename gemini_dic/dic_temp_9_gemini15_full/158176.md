platitudes:/ˈplætɪtuːdz/| n. |陈词滥调；老生常谈；空话 |Banal, overused, and often insincere statements.  They lack originality and depth. |例句：His speech was full of platitudes about the importance of hard work and family values.  他的演讲充满了关于努力工作和家庭价值重要性的陈词滥调。


例句：The politician's speech was nothing but a string of platitudes.  这位政治家的演讲只不过是一连串的陈词滥调。


近义词：clichés, banalities, truisms, commonplaces


反义词：originality, novelty, insight, profundity


词性：名词

词源:
From Middle French *platitude, from plat "flat," from Latin plattus "flat."  The sense development is from "flatness" to "dullness" to "banality."
