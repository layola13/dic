exercise:/ˈɛksərsaɪz/ | n. v. |练习；运动；运用；实行 |  The act of using or engaging in physical activity for the purpose of improving fitness or health; the act of practicing or performing a skill or activity repeatedly to improve proficiency. It can also refer to the activity itself or a specific task designed for practice or training. |例句：I do exercise every morning. 我每天早上都锻炼。|例句：The teacher gave us an exercise in grammar. 老师给我们布置了一道语法练习题。|近义词：practice, training, drill, workout |反义词：inactivity, idleness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I exercise regularly. = 我定期锻炼。
He exercises his right to vote. = 他行使他的投票权。

一般过去时:
She exercised yesterday. = 她昨天锻炼了。
They exercised their authority. = 他们行使了他们的权力。

现在完成时:
I have exercised every day this week. = 我这周每天都锻炼了。
They have exercised great patience. = 他们表现出了极大的耐心。

过去完成时:
She had exercised for an hour before she felt tired. = 她锻炼了一个小时后才感到疲倦。
He had exercised his influence to get the job. = 他运用他的影响力得到了这份工作。

将来时:
I will exercise tomorrow morning. = 我明天早上会锻炼。
We will exercise our options carefully. = 我们会仔细权衡我们的选择。

虚拟语气:
If I had time, I would exercise more. = 如果我有时间，我会多锻炼。
I wish I exercised more often. = 我希望我能更经常地锻炼。

被动语态:
The muscles are exercised during the workout. = 肌肉在锻炼过程中得到锻炼。
His authority was exercised unfairly. = 他的权力被不公平地运用。

疑问句:

否定句:

He didn't exercise his right to appeal. = 他没有行使他的上诉权。
