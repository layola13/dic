mourning:/ˈmɔːrnɪŋ/| n. |哀悼，忧伤；服丧期 |The state of feeling sorrow or grief, especially for the death of someone.  It often involves expressing grief through rituals and customs.  |例句：The family is observing a period of mourning after the loss of their father.  这个家庭在父亲去世后正在服丧。

例句：Her mourning for her lost love lasted for many years. 她对失去的爱人的哀悼持续了很多年。


近义词：grief, sorrow, bereavement, lamentation |反义词：celebration, rejoicing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She is mourning the loss of her pet. = 她正在为失去宠物而悲伤。
They mourn the victims of the accident every year. = 他们每年都会为事故的受害者哀悼。


一般过去时:
He mourned the death of his grandfather. = 他为祖父的去世而悲伤。
The nation mourned the fallen soldiers. = 全国人民为阵亡的士兵哀悼。


现在完成时:
She has mourned the loss of her childhood home for years. = 多年来，她一直为失去童年故居而悲伤。
They have mourned the loss of their freedom. = 他们一直为失去自由而悲伤。


过去完成时:
They had mourned the passing of their leader for a week before the funeral. = 在葬礼前一周，他们已经为领导人的去世哀悼了一周。
She had mourned the loss of her innocence for many years before she started to heal. = 在开始疗伤之前，她已经为失去纯真悲伤了很多年。


将来时:
They will mourn the loss of their friend for many years to come. = 未来许多年，他们都会为失去朋友而悲伤。
The country will mourn the passing of its beloved queen. = 全国人民将为深受爱戴的女王的逝世而哀悼。


虚拟语气:
If I had known he was going to die, I would have mourned him properly. = 如果我知道他会死，我会好好为他哀悼。
I wish I hadn't lost my temper; I would not be mourning this broken vase now. = 我真希望我没有发脾气；我现在就不会为这个打碎的花瓶而悲伤了。


被动语态:
The loss of the great leader was mourned throughout the country. = 这位伟大领导人的逝世受到了全国的哀悼。
The death of the artist is being mourned by many people. = 这位艺术家的去世正在被许多人哀悼。


疑问句:
How long will you mourn the loss of your job? = 你会为失去工作而悲伤多久？
Are they still mourning the death of their mother? = 他们还在为母亲的去世而悲伤吗？


否定句:
He didn't mourn the loss of his old car. = 他并没有为他旧车的丢失而悲伤。
They did not mourn the end of the war. = 他们并没有为战争的结束而悲伤。
