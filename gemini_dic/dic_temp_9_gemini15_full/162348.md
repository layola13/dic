preachy:/ˈpriːtʃi/| adj.|说教的，教训人的|excessively moralizing or didactic.|His preachy tone annoyed everyone. 他说教的语气惹恼了所有人。|近义词：moralistic, didactic, sanctimonious|反义词：unassuming, humble, modest


USAGE->

一般现在时:
His speech is often preachy.  = 他的演讲经常是说教式的。
The teacher's tone sounds preachy to the students. = 老师的语气听起来对学生来说很说教。


一般过去时:
Her sermon was incredibly preachy. = 她的布道非常说教。
The lecture was too preachy for the audience. = 讲座对听众来说过于说教。


现在完成时:
He has become increasingly preachy in recent years. = 近年来，他变得越来越爱说教。
The media has been preachy about the importance of health. = 媒体一直在说教健康的重要性。


过去完成时:
The politician had been preachy about his policies before the election. = 在选举之前，这位政治家一直在他的政策上说教。
She had always been preachy, even as a child. = 她从小就一直爱说教。


将来时:
He will be preachy again at the next meeting. = 他下次会议还会再说教。
The book will likely be considered preachy by many readers. = 这本书很可能会被许多读者认为是说教的。


虚拟语气:
If he were less preachy, people would listen to him more. = 如果他不那么爱说教，人们就会更愿意听他的话。
I wish he weren't so preachy about his opinions. = 我希望他不那么固执己见地宣扬他的观点。


被动语态:
His views were considered preachy by most. = 他的观点被大多数人认为是说教的。
The novel was criticized for being too preachy. = 这部小说因过于说教而受到批评。


疑问句:
Is his advice always so preachy? = 他的建议总是那么说教吗？
Does the book come across as preachy? = 这本书听起来像是在说教吗？


否定句:
His style isn't preachy at all; it's insightful. = 他的风格一点也不说教，而是富有洞察力。
The article wasn't preachy; it simply presented the facts. = 这篇文章并非说教，它只是陈述了事实。
