preanticipated: /ˌpriːænˈtɪsɪpeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 预料中的，预期到的 |  Already anticipated; foreseen.  This word is less common and often considered informal or even awkward.  It suggests an anticipation that happened *before* the usual time of anticipation. |


例句：The preanticipated problems with the software launch were addressed before the release. = 软件发布前预料到的问题已得到解决。

The preanticipated snowfall caused the school to close early. = 预料中的降雪导致学校提前放学。


近义词：anticipated, expected, foreseen, foretold

反义词：unexpected, unforeseen, surprising


词性:形容词


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "What is/was/will be preanticipated?"  Its usage is limited to descriptive sentences.  It modifies nouns.  

Examples illustrating its use in different sentence structures:


肯定句:
The preanticipated delay was frustrating. = 预料之中的延误令人沮丧。
His preanticipated success was well deserved. = 他预料之中的成功是当之无愧的。


否定句:
The outcome wasn't preanticipated at all. = 结果完全出乎意料。
The project's failure was not preanticipated by the team. = 项目的失败并非团队预料之中。


疑问句:
Was the market crash preanticipated by the experts? = 专家们是否预料到了市场崩盘？
Is the high level of inflation preanticipated in the economic forecasts? = 经济预测中是否预料到了高通胀？


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases using "preanticipated".  Due to its infrequent use and somewhat awkward construction, it doesn't readily combine with other words to form established phrases.
