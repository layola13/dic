nonutilitarians:/ˌnɒnjuːtɪlɪˈteəriənz/| n. |非功利主义者 |Those who oppose utilitarianism, a moral philosophy that holds that the best action is the one that maximizes utility.  Nonutilitarians often prioritize other ethical principles, such as deontology (duty-based ethics) or virtue ethics. |Example:  Many nonutilitarians argue that consequences are not the sole determinant of moral worth.  许多非功利主义者认为结果并非道德价值的唯一决定因素。|近义词: deontologists, virtue ethicists |反义词: utilitarians


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It's used as a collective noun referring to people.  There is no tense variation.  The grammatical function changes depending on the sentence structure.

Example 1: The panel included several nonutilitarians.  小组中包括几位非功利主义者。(Subject of the sentence)

Example 2:  The arguments presented were persuasive to many nonutilitarians.  提出的论点让许多非功利主义者信服。(Object of the preposition "to")
