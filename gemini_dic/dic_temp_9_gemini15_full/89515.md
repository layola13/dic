honked:/hɑŋkt/| v.|鸣笛；按喇叭|To make a loud, harsh noise, typically with a horn.|The driver honked his horn at the pedestrian who almost walked into the street.|beeped, tooted|silent|

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He honks his horn every morning. = 他每天早上都按喇叭。
The geese honk loudly as they fly overhead. = 鹅在头顶飞过时大声鸣叫。

一般过去时:
The car honked angrily at the slow driver. = 汽车愤怒地对着慢速行驶的司机鸣笛。
She honked the horn to warn the cyclist. = 她按响喇叭警告骑自行车的人。

现在完成时:
The driver has honked his horn repeatedly. = 司机已经反复按响喇叭。
They have honked their horns in celebration. = 他们按响喇叭庆祝。

过去完成时:
He had honked the horn before the accident occurred. = 事故发生前他已经按响了喇叭。
The train had honked its whistle before entering the station. = 火车进站前已经鸣笛了。

将来时:
I will honk the horn if I see any danger. = 如果我看到任何危险，我会按喇叭。
The bus will honk loudly as it approaches the stop. = 公交车快到站时会大声鸣笛。

虚拟语气:
If I had seen him, I would have honked the horn. = 如果我看到他，我会按喇叭的。
I wish I hadn't honked the horn so loudly. = 我真希望我没有那么大声地按喇叭。

被动语态:
The horn was honked repeatedly. = 喇叭被反复按响。
The warning was given by a honked horn. = 警告是由鸣笛发出的。

疑问句:
Did he honk his horn? = 他按喇叭了吗？
Why did the car honk? = 为什么汽车鸣笛？

否定句:
He didn't honk his horn. = 他没有按喇叭。
The driver didn't honk at the pedestrian. = 司机没有朝行人鸣笛。
