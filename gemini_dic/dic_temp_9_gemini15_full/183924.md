reservedness:/rɪˈzɜːvdnəs/| n. |矜持；拘谨；沉默寡言 |The quality of being reserved; unwillingness or reluctance to express emotions or opinions freely.  It often manifests as a quiet demeanor and a cautious approach to social interaction.  |例句：Her reservedness made it difficult to get to know her.  她的矜持使得人们难以了解她。

例句：His reservedness at the party surprised everyone who knew him as a lively and outgoing person. 他在派对上的沉默寡言让所有认识他的人都感到惊讶，因为他们知道他是一个活泼外向的人。


近义词：shyness, reticence, taciturnity, introversion |反义词：extroversion, openness, sociability, loquacity


词源:  Derived from the adjective "reserved," which ultimately comes from the Old French word "reserver" meaning "to keep back, retain,"  from the Latin "reservare" (re- + servare, "to keep, preserve").  The "-ness" suffix indicates a state or quality.
