sarcoid:/ˈsɑːrkɔɪd/| n. |肉芽肿；肉瘤样病变|A sarcoid is a small, abnormal mass of tissue that forms in the body.  Sarcoids are often associated with inflammation and can occur in various organs.  They are usually benign but can sometimes cause serious problems depending on their location and size. |Example:  The biopsy revealed the presence of sarcoids in the lungs.  肺部活检显示存在肉芽肿。

The patient was diagnosed with sarcoidosis, a disease characterized by the formation of sarcoids in multiple organs. 病人被诊断为肉芽肿病，这是一种以多个器官形成肉芽肿为特征的疾病。|近义词：granuloma, nodule |反义词：None specifically, but "healthy tissue" could be considered an antonym in context.


Etymology:
The word "sarcoid" comes from the Greek words "sarx" (σαρξ), meaning "flesh," and "eidos" (εἶδος), meaning "form" or "appearance."  Therefore, it literally means "flesh-like form" or "flesh-like growth."
