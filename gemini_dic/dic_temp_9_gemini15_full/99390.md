interdictors: /ˌɪntərˈdɪktərz/ | n. | 干扰器；禁止者 | Devices or systems designed to impede or prevent the movement or operation of something, often used in a military or space-related context.  The term often refers to systems that disrupt enemy vessels or weapons. | Example: The enemy deployed powerful interdictors to prevent our ships from reaching the target.  敌方部署了强大的干扰器来阻止我们的舰船到达目标。  The government's new policies act as interdictors to free trade. 政府的新政策阻碍了自由贸易。 |近义词：jammers, disruptors, inhibitors |反义词：facilitators, enablers
