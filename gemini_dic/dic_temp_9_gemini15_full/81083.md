grallatorial:/ˌɡræleɪˈtɔːriəl/| adj.|涉禽类的；涉禽似的|Of or relating to birds that wade in shallow water, characterized by long legs and necks, and often long bills.  The term often implies a characteristic gait or posture.|例句：The grallatorial bird elegantly waded through the shallows. 这只涉禽优雅地涉水而过。|近义词：wading,  limb-long|反义词：flying (in the context of bird locomotion)
