limniad:/ˈlɪmniˌæd/| n. |湖泊生物; 湖泊生物群落|A limniad refers to the organisms living in a lake or other body of still freshwater.  It encompasses a diverse range of flora and fauna,  including plankton, fish, invertebrates, and aquatic plants,  all interconnected within the lake's ecosystem. The term often implies a focus on the community or assemblage of these organisms, rather than individual species.  |Example: The limniad of Lake Tahoe is renowned for its exceptional clarity and biodiversity.  太浩湖的湖泊生物群落以其非凡的清澈度和生物多样性而闻名。|近义词：lacustrine fauna, lake community |反义词：N/A
