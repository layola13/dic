counterstates:/ˈkaʊntərˌsteɪts/| n. |对抗性国家，对立国家|A counterstate is a political entity that opposes or challenges the authority of an established state, often through armed conflict or other forms of resistance.  It may or may not control a defined territory.  The term often implies a less formal or less internationally recognized entity than a fully sovereign state.|例句：The rebel group established a counterstate in the northern region, challenging the government's control.  反政府武装在北部地区建立了一个对抗性国家，挑战政府的控制。|近义词：rebel group, insurgent state, opposition movement|反义词：established state, legitimate government|名词
