redundance:/rɪˈdʌndənsi/ | n. |冗余；多余；重复 | The state of being redundant; superfluity.  It refers to something that is unnecessary because it duplicates something else.  This can apply to information, processes, or components.  For example, having two backup systems in place might be considered redundancy.  | Example: The report suffered from redundancy; many points were repeated unnecessarily.  = 报告冗余；许多要点被不必要地重复了。 The redundancy of the system ensured its reliability. = 系统的冗余确保了其可靠性。 |近义词：superfluity, superfluousness, excess, pleonasm |反义词：necessity, essentiality
