riprap: /ˈrɪpræp/ | n. | 块石护坡；块石；护岸石 | Loose stones, often of various sizes, used to protect a slope or bank from erosion.  It's commonly used in riverbanks, shorelines, and other areas prone to water damage. | The riverbank was reinforced with riprap to prevent erosion. 河岸用块石护坡加固以防止侵蚀。| 近义词：stonework, rubble, revetment | 反义词：None directly applicable, but "unprotected bank" could be considered an antonym in context.
