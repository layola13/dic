doze off:/doʊz ˈɔːf/ | v. |打瞌睡，睡着 |To fall asleep, especially lightly or unintentionally.  |例句：I often doze off during long lectures. 我经常在冗长的演讲中打瞌睡。
例句：He dozed off in front of the television. 他在电视机前睡着了。|近义词：nod off, drift off, drop off, fall asleep |反义词：stay awake, be alert


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I often doze off during boring meetings. = 我经常在无聊的会议上打瞌睡。
He sometimes dozes off while reading. = 他有时看书时会打瞌睡。

一般过去时:
I dozed off during the movie. = 我在看电影时睡着了。
She dozed off on the train. = 她火车上睡着了。

现在完成时:
I have dozed off several times today. = 我今天已经打瞌睡好几次了。
He has dozed off while working late. = 他加班时睡着了。

过去完成时:
I had dozed off before the speaker finished. = 演讲者还没讲完我就睡着了。
She had dozed off before the alarm went off. = 闹钟响之前她就睡着了。

将来时:
I think I will doze off if this meeting continues. = 如果这个会议继续下去，我想我会睡着的。
He might doze off during the long flight. = 他在漫长的飞行中可能会睡着。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't had so much coffee, I wouldn't have dozed off. = 如果我没有喝那么多咖啡，我就不会睡着了。
I wish I hadn't dozed off during the important announcement. = 我真希望在重要的宣布期间我没有睡着。

被动语态:  (Doze off is generally not used in the passive voice.)  This verb describes an action performed by the subject on themselves.

疑问句:
Did you doze off during the presentation? = 你在演示期间睡着了吗？
Does he often doze off in class? = 他经常在课堂上打瞌睡吗？

否定句:
I didn't doze off, I was just resting my eyes. = 我没有睡着，我只是闭目养神。
She tried to stay awake but she couldn't help dozing off. = 她试图保持清醒，但她忍不住睡着了。
