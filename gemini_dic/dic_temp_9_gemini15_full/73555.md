forestalled:/fɔːrˈstɔːld/| v. |阻止；先发制人；提前预防|To prevent or hinder something from happening; to anticipate and prevent (something undesirable)|The quick action of the firefighters forestalled a major fire.  消防员迅速行动阻止了一场大火。|They forestalled a potential crisis by negotiating with the union. 他们通过与工会谈判避免了一场潜在的危机。|prevented, avoided, averted|accelerated, promoted, encouraged|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He forestalls problems by planning ahead. = 他通过提前计划来预防问题。
She forestalls criticism by being meticulous. = 她通过一丝不苟来避免批评。


一般过去时:
The timely intervention forestalled a riot. = 及时的干预阻止了一场暴乱。
They forestalled the attack by strengthening their defenses. = 他们通过加强防御来阻止了袭击。


现在完成时:
We have forestalled a major financial loss. = 我们避免了一场重大的经济损失。
The government has forestalled the spread of the disease. = 政府阻止了疾病的蔓延。


过去完成时:
They had forestalled any potential objections by explaining the plan thoroughly. = 他们通过彻底解释计划来预先阻止了任何潜在的反对意见。
She had forestalled disaster by taking immediate action. = 她通过立即采取行动避免了灾难。


将来时:
We will forestall any attempts at sabotage. = 我们将阻止任何破坏活动。
The new policy will forestall future conflicts. = 新政策将防止未来的冲突。


虚拟语气:
If we had acted sooner, we could have forestalled the crisis. = 如果我们早点行动，就能避免这场危机。
I wish I had forestalled his anger. = 我真希望我当时能阻止他的怒火。


被动语态:
The strike was forestalled by negotiations. = 罢工通过谈判被避免了。
The accident was forestalled by a quick reaction. = 事故由于迅速的反应而被避免了。


疑问句:
Can we forestall further damage? = 我们能阻止进一步的损害吗？
How can we forestall this problem? = 我们如何才能避免这个问题？


否定句:
They did not forestall the inevitable. = 他们没有阻止不可避免的事情发生。
We failed to forestall the spread of rumors. = 我们未能阻止谣言的传播。



PHRASE->

forestall a crisis =  предотвратить кризис (prevent a crisis)
forestall an attack = предотвратить атаку (prevent an attack)
forestall disaster = предотвратить катастрофу (prevent disaster)
forestall objections = предотвратить возражения (prevent objections)
forestall problems = предотвратить проблемы (prevent problems)
