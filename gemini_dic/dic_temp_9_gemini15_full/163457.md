precontentment: /ˌpriːkənˈtɛntmənt/ | n. | 事先满足；预先满足 |  A feeling of satisfaction or contentment experienced before something is achieved or obtained.  It implies a sense of anticipation and quiet confidence that one will achieve a desired outcome. This word is not commonly used and is considered a neologism (newly coined word). | Example:  She felt a sense of precontentment knowing her hard work would eventually pay off. 她有一种预先满足的感觉，知道她的努力最终会得到回报。| 近义词: anticipation, expectation, forepleasure | 反义词: disappointment, anxiety, apprehension |  n.
