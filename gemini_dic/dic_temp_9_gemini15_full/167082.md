presuppose:/ˌpriːsəˈpəʊz/| vt.|预先假定；预设；以…为前提|To assume something to be true before it has been proved; to take something for granted.  It often implies a hidden or underlying assumption.|The argument presupposes a level of understanding that many students lack.|近义词：assume, presume, postulate |反义词：deny, refute


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He presupposes that everyone understands the rules. = 他预设每个人都理解规则。
The theory presupposes a stable environment. = 这一理论以稳定的环境为前提。


一般过去时:
She presupposed his agreement before even asking. = 她甚至还没问就预设他同意了。
The scientist presupposed the accuracy of his data. = 科学家预设了他的数据是准确的。


现在完成时:
We have presupposed the success of the project. = 我们已经预设了项目的成功。
They have presupposed a positive response from the audience. = 他们已经预设了观众的积极回应。


过去完成时:
They had presupposed the outcome of the election. = 他们已经预设了选举的结果。
She had presupposed his cooperation before starting the task. = 在开始任务之前，她已经预设了他的合作。


将来时:
We will presuppose their cooperation for the next phase. = 我们将在下一阶段预设他们的合作。
He will presuppose a good outcome given the current data. = 给定当前数据，他会预设一个好的结果。


虚拟语气:
If we presupposed a different scenario, the outcome might change. = 如果我们预设不同的情景，结果可能会改变。
I wish I hadn't presupposed his honesty. = 我真希望我没有预设他的诚实。


被动语态:
The success of the plan was presupposed by the management team. = 管理团队预设了计划的成功。
His innocence was presupposed until proven otherwise. = 在证明他有罪之前，预设他是无罪的。


疑问句:
Does this argument presuppose a complete understanding of physics? = 这个论点是否预设完全理解物理学？
Does this theory presuppose any specific conditions? = 这个理论是否预设任何特定条件？


否定句:
We don't presuppose any prior knowledge from the students. = 我们不预设学生有任何先验知识。
The judge didn't presuppose the defendant's guilt. = 法官没有预设被告有罪。


PHRASE->
presuppose something to be true = 预设某事为真
presuppose a certain level of knowledge = 预设一定的知识水平
presuppose the existence of something = 预设某事物的存在
presuppose a particular outcome = 预设特定结果
presuppose a particular interpretation = 预设某种解释
