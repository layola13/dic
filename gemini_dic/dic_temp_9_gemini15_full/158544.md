plesiohedrons:/ˌpliːzioʊˈhiːdrɒnz/| n. |拟似多面体|A plesiohedron is a convex polyhedron where all faces are congruent, but not necessarily regular.  Unlike a regular polyhedron,  a plesiohedron's faces may not be arranged symmetrically.  They are a generalization of regular polyhedra. |Example:  Some examples of plesiohedrons include certain types of truncated polyhedra and certain rhombic polyhedra.  There is no simple, universally accepted formula to calculate the number of plesiohedrons. |近义词：polyhedron (but less symmetrical)|反义词：regular polyhedron (more symmetrical)|名词
