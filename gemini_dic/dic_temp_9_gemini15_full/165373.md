prejudices:/ˈprɛdʒʊdɪsɪz/| n.|偏见；成见；歧视|Prejudices are preconceived judgments or opinions, often negative, about a person or group based on stereotypes rather than on individual merit or experience.  They can be based on race, religion, gender, nationality, or other characteristics.  Prejudices can lead to discrimination and unfair treatment.|例句：His prejudices against foreigners prevented him from seeing their positive qualities.  他的对外人的偏见使他无法看到他们的优点。|近义词：biases, preconceptions, stereotypes, bigotries|反义词：open-mindedness, tolerance, impartiality
