centrally:/ˈsentrəli/ | adv. | 中心地；集中地 | In the middle or most important part of something.  | The government controls the economy centrally. 政府集中控制经济。  |  centrally located, focused | peripherally, locally | 副词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company's operations are centrally managed. = 公司的运营是集中管理的。
Decisions are made centrally in this organization. = 在这个组织中，决策是由中心做出的。


一般过去时:
The project was centrally funded. = 这个项目是由中央资助的。
Power was centrally controlled during the dictatorship. = 在独裁统治期间，权力是集中控制的。


现在完成时:
The government has centrally planned the economy for decades. = 政府几十年来一直对经济进行中央计划。
They have centrally located their new office building. = 他们已将新办公楼设在中心位置。


过去完成时:
The company had centrally organized its resources before the expansion. = 公司在扩张之前已经集中组织了其资源。
The committee had centrally decided the policy before the announcement. = 委员会在宣布之前已经集中决定了政策。


将来时:
The resources will be centrally allocated next year. = 资源将在明年集中分配。
The new system will centrally manage all data. = 新系统将集中管理所有数据。


虚拟语气:
If the system were centrally managed, it would be more efficient. = 如果系统是集中管理的，它将更有效率。
I wish the resources were centrally allocated. = 我希望资源能够集中分配。


被动语态:
The decision was centrally made by the board. = 该决定是由董事会集中做出的。
The funds are centrally controlled by the government. = 资金由政府集中控制。


疑问句:
Is the system centrally controlled? = 系统是集中控制的吗？

否定句:

The system isn't centrally managed. = 该系统不是集中管理的。
