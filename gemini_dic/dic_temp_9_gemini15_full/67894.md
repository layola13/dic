/fæk/ | n. | 事实；真相 |  A fact is something that is known to be true, or a piece of information that is known to be true.  | The fact is, he lied. 事实是，他撒谎了。|  truth, reality, actuality | fiction, falsehood, lie |  名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This is a fact. = 这是一个事实。
The fact remains that he is guilty. = 事实仍然是他有罪。

一般过去时:
That was a surprising fact. = 那是一个令人惊讶的事实。
The fact remained that he was innocent. = 事实仍然是他无罪的。

现在完成时:
Many facts have emerged since the investigation began. = 自调查开始以来，已经出现了许多事实。
We have established the facts of the case. = 我们已经查明了案件的事实。

过去完成时:
The fact had been clear for many years. = 多年来，这个事实一直很清楚。
By then, the facts had already been presented. = 到那时，事实已经被摆出来了。

将来时:
The coming report will reveal many facts. = 即将来到的报告将揭示许多事实。
More facts will come to light soon. = 很快就会有更多的事实浮出水面。

虚拟语气:
If it were a fact, it would be important. = 如果这是事实，那将很重要。
If that fact had been known, the outcome would have been different. = 如果那个事实已为人知，结果就会不同。

被动语态:
The facts were carefully investigated. = 事实经过了仔细调查。
The truth of the facts is being debated.  = 事实的真相正在被争论。

疑问句:
What are the facts of the case? = 此案的事实是什么？
Is that really a fact? = 那真的是事实吗？

否定句:
That's not a fact. = 那不是事实。
It isn't a fact that he was there. = 他在那里并不是事实。
