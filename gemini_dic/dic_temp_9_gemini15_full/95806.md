inconvincibly:/ˌɪnkənˈvɪnsəbli/ | adj. |无法令人信服地，令人难以置信地 |  Unable to be persuaded; unconvincing.  This word describes something that fails to persuade or convince someone. It emphasizes the inability to change someone's mind or belief. | Example: He argued inconvincibly for his position, leaving the audience unconvinced. 他无法令人信服地为自己的立场辩护，使听众无法信服。  |近义词：unpersuasively, unconvincingly, persuasively |反义词：convincingly, persuasively |形容词


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, not a noun, verb, or interjection.  Therefore, it doesn't conjugate like verbs or decline like nouns.  It modifies verbs, describing how an action of persuading or arguing was performed.


Example Sentences illustrating its usage:

He presented his case inconvincibly, failing to sway the jury. 他无法令人信服地陈述了他的案情，未能说服陪审团。

She spoke inconvincibly, her arguments lacking both evidence and logic. 她说话令人难以信服，她的论点既缺乏证据又缺乏逻辑。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases using "inconvincibly".  Its usage is primarily as a standalone adverb modifying verbs of persuasion or argumentation.
