libertarianism:/ˌlɪbərˈtɛəriənɪzəm/| n. |自由主义；自由意志主义|A political philosophy that upholds liberty as a core principle.  It advocates for maximizing individual autonomy and minimizing the role of the state in people's lives. Libertarians generally support free markets, limited government, and individual rights, including the rights to free speech, freedom of religion, and the right to own property.  Different schools of libertarian thought exist, varying in their emphasis on specific principles and their proposed implementations.|The rise of libertarianism in the US is often attributed to increasing government intervention in the economy and social life. |近义词：individualism, liberalism (in some contexts) |反义词：authoritarianism, totalitarianism, collectivism
