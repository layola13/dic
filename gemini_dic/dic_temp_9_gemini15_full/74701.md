freelance:/ˈfriːlæns/| adj., n., v.|自由职业的；自由职业者；做自由职业|

英文解析:
As an adjective, "freelance" describes work or a worker who is not employed by a particular company but is hired for individual projects.  As a noun, it refers to a person who works independently, often on a project-by-project basis. As a verb, it means to work independently as a freelancer.


例句:
She's a freelance journalist. = 她是一位自由撰稿人。

He freelances as a web designer. = 他做自由职业网页设计师。

近义词:
independent contractor, self-employed, independent worker


反义词:
employed, salaried, staff


词性:
adj., n., v.

I freelanced as a writer for five years. = 我做过五年的自由撰稿人。

现在完成时:

She has freelanced successfully for many years. = 她多年来成功地从事自由职业。


过去完成时:
I had freelanced for a year before I got a full-time job. = 在我找到全职工作之前，我已经做过一年的自由职业。
They had freelanced together on several projects before. = 他们之前一起做过几个自由职业项目。

词源:
The word "freelance" originates from the Old French word "franc," meaning "free," and "lance," meaning "spear."  Originally, it referred to medieval knights who offered their services to various lords, hence "free lancers." The modern meaning evolved from this historical context.
