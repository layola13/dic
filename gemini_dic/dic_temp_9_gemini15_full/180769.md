regulate:/ˈreɡjuleɪt/| vt. |管理；调节；控制；规定|To control or maintain something, or to adjust something to a particular level or standard.|The government regulates the prices of essential goods. 政府管制生活必需品的價格。|The body regulates its temperature through sweating.  身体通过出汗来调节体温。|control, govern, adjust, modify |deregulate, disorganize|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government regulates the banking industry. = 政府监管银行业。
She regulates her diet carefully. = 她仔细地控制饮食。


一般过去时:
The committee regulated the flow of information. = 委员会规范了信息的流动。
He regulated his breathing before the race. = 他在比赛前调整呼吸。


现在完成时:
The new law has regulated the sale of firearms. = 新法律规范了枪支的销售。
She has regulated her spending habits. = 她已经规范了自己的消费习惯。


过去完成时:
They had regulated the water supply before the drought. = 干旱来临之前，他们已经调节了供水。
The company had regulated its production to meet demand. = 公司已经调整了生产以满足需求。


将来时:
The city will regulate traffic flow with new technology. = 城市将用新技术来调节交通流量。
They will regulate their expenses more strictly next year. = 他们明年将更严格地控制开支。


虚拟语气:
If the government regulated pollution more strictly, the air would be cleaner. = 如果政府更严格地控制污染，空气就会更清洁。
I wish they regulated the noise levels in the city. = 我希望他们能规范城市里的噪音水平。


被动语态:
The temperature is regulated by a thermostat. = 温度由恒温器控制。
The speed of the machine is regulated automatically. = 机器速度自动调节。


疑问句:
Does the government regulate the sale of alcohol? = 政府是否管制酒类的销售？
How do they regulate the quality of their products? = 他们如何控制产品质量？


否定句:
The company doesn't regulate its employees' working hours. = 公司不规定员工的工作时间。
He didn't regulate his emotions well during the interview. = 他在面试中没有很好地控制情绪。
