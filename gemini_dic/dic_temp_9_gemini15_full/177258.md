reasserted:/ˌriːəˈsɜːrtɪd/| v.|重申；再次断言；重新确定|To state or declare something again strongly and forcefully, often after it has been challenged or doubted.  |例句：The government reasserted its commitment to economic reform. 政府重申其致力于经济改革。|例句：He reasserted his dominance in the final race. 他在最后的比赛中再次确立了他的优势。|近义词：reaffirm, restate, reiterate, reaffirm, insist on|反义词：retract, deny, refute


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reasserts his authority every meeting. = 他每次会议都重申他的权威。
She reasserts her independence daily. = 她每天都重申她的独立性。

一般过去时:
The spokesperson reasserted the company's position on the issue. = 发言人重申了公司对该问题的立场。
He reasserted his innocence to the police. = 他向警方重申了他的清白。

现在完成时:
They have reasserted their control over the situation. = 他们已经重新控制了局面。
She has reasserted her right to privacy. = 她已经重申了她拥有隐私的权利。

过去完成时:
After the initial setback, he had reasserted his confidence. = 在最初的挫折之后，他已经重新树立了信心。
The team had reasserted its dominance before the final game. = 该队在决赛前已经重新确立了其统治地位。

将来时:
The president will reassert his power in the upcoming election. = 总统将在即将到来的选举中重申他的权力。
She will reassert her position in the next meeting. = 她将在下次会议上重申她的立场。

虚拟语气:
If he reasserted his claim, he might win the case. = 如果他重申他的说法，他可能会赢得官司。
I wish he had reasserted his authority earlier. = 我希望他早点重申他的权威。

被动语态:
His authority was reasserted by the board of directors. = 他的权威被董事会重新确立。
The company's position was reasserted in a press release. = 公司的立场在新闻稿中得到了重申。

疑问句:

Did he reassert his innocence after the trial? = 审判后他是否重申了他的清白？

否定句:
He did not reassert his dominance in the second half of the game. = 他在下半场比赛中没有再次确立他的统治地位。
The court refused to reassert the previous ruling. = 法院拒绝重申先前的裁决。
