insinuations: /ˌɪnsɪnjuˈeɪʃənz/ | n. | 暗示；影射；含沙射影 |

英文解析:The act of suggesting or hinting at something unpleasant or improper without explicitly stating it.  Insinuations are often indirect and can be difficult to prove. They rely on implication and innuendo to convey a negative message.

例句:

His insinuations about her honesty were deeply hurtful.  他的关于她诚实的暗示深深地伤害了她。

The newspaper article was full of insinuations about the politician's past. 这篇报纸文章充满了关于这位政治家过去的影射。


近义词: implications, hints, suggestions, innuendoes, aspersions

反义词:  direct statements, open accusations (Note:  There isn't a perfect antonym, as insinuations are defined by their indirect nature.)


词源:  From the verb "insinuate," which comes from the Latin word "insinuare," meaning "to wind in," "to creep in," or "to suggest subtly."  The original sense implied a gradual and indirect entry or approach.
