kisses:/ˈkɪsɪz/| n.|吻；亲吻|The act of pressing one's lips against another person or thing as a sign of affection, greeting, or farewell.  It can also refer to the sound of kissing. |例句：She gave her child a tender kiss. 她温柔地吻了她的孩子。  The sound of their kisses echoed in the night.  他们的亲吻声在夜里回荡。|近义词：kisses, smooches, pecks|反义词：slaps, punches


USAGE->

He kisses his wife every morning. = 他每天早上都吻他的妻子。
She kisses the baby goodnight. = 她亲吻婴儿道晚安。


一般过去时:

词源:
The word "kiss" originates from Old English *cyssan,  ultimately deriving from Proto-Germanic *kussijaną.  The meaning has remained consistent over time, referring to the act of pressing lips together.
