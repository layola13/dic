coanchored:/ˈkoʊæŋkərd/ | v. |共同主持，共同担任主持人 |To act as a host or presenter together with someone else.  This term is typically used in the context of television or radio programs, where two people share the hosting responsibilities.| Example:  The news program was coanchored by two experienced journalists.  新闻节目由两位经验丰富的记者共同主持。 |近义词：jointly hosted, presented together |反义词：solo anchored, individually hosted |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They coanchor the morning news show. = 他们共同主持早间新闻节目。
We coanchor this podcast every week. = 我们每周共同主持这个播客。


一般过去时:
They coanchored the awards ceremony last night. = 他们昨晚共同主持了颁奖典礼。
She and her colleague coanchored the debate. = 她和她的同事共同主持了这场辩论。


现在完成时:
They have coanchored this program for five years. = 他们已经共同主持这个节目五年了。
We have coanchored several successful events. = 我们已经共同主持了几场成功的活动。


过去完成时:
They had coanchored several shows before this one. = 在主持这个节目之前，他们已经共同主持过几个节目了。
Before the big event, they had coanchored many smaller ones. = 在大型活动之前，他们已经共同主持过许多小型活动。


将来时:
They will coanchor the next presidential debate. = 他们将共同主持下一场总统辩论。

虚拟语气:
If they were to coanchor the show, ratings would increase. = 如果他们共同主持这个节目，收视率将会提高。
I wish they would coanchor the event; they would be perfect together. = 我希望他们能共同主持这个活动；他们在一起会很完美。


被动语态:
The show was coanchored by two well-known personalities. = 这个节目由两位知名人士共同主持。
The event will be coanchored by a celebrity and a local politician. = 该活动将由一位名人与当地政治家共同主持。


疑问句:
Who coanchored the television special? = 谁共同主持了这个电视特别节目？
Will they coanchor the next episode? = 他们会共同主持下一集吗？


否定句:
They did not coanchor the show; one person hosted alone. = 他们并没有共同主持节目；只有一人单独主持。
She will not coanchor the event with him; they have a conflict. = 她不会和他一起主持这个活动；他们之间有冲突。
