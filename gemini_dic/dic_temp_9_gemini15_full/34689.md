词源:
Derived from "clerk" (itself from Old French "clerc," meaning "clergyman, scholar"), implying the qualities often associated with clerical work: meticulousness, routine, and adherence to procedure.  The "-liness" suffix creates a noun signifying the quality or state of being like a clerk.
