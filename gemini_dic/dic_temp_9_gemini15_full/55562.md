distrusts:/dɪsˈtrʌsts/ | v. |不信任；怀疑 |To have no trust in; suspect.  It implies a lack of confidence or belief in the honesty, reliability, or competence of someone or something. |例句：He distrusts politicians and their promises. 他不信任政客及其承诺。

例句：Many people distrust the information they read online. 很多人不信任他们在网上阅读的信息。


近义词：suspects, doubts, questions, misgives | 反义词：trusts, believes in, relies on | 词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He distrusts his neighbor's motives. = 他怀疑他邻居的动机。
She distrusts the company's claims. = 她不相信公司的说法。


一般过去时:
They distrusted the witness's testimony. = 他们不相信证人的证词。
I distrusted my own judgment in that situation. = 在那种情况下，我不相信自己的判断。


现在完成时:
We have distrusted him ever since the incident. = 从那件事起，我们就一直不信任他。
She has distrusted banks since the financial crisis. = 自金融危机以来，她就不再信任银行了。


过去完成时:
He had distrusted her for years before she finally betrayed him. = 在她最终背叛他之前，他已经不信任她多年了。
They had distrusted the project from the start. = 从一开始他们就不相信这个项目。


将来时:
I will distrust him until he proves his innocence. = 直到他证明自己的清白，我才会信任他。
They will distrust the new policy until it's proven effective. = 直到新政策被证明有效，他们才会信任它。


虚拟语气:
If I distrusted him, I wouldn't have told him my secret. = 如果我不信任他，我就不会告诉他我的秘密。
I wish I hadn't distrusted her advice; it would have saved me a lot of trouble. = 我真希望我没有不相信她的建议；那会省去我很多麻烦。


被动语态:
His judgment was distrusted by many. = 许多人不信任他的判断。
The politician's claims were distrusted by the public. = 公众不相信这位政客的说法。


疑问句:
Do you distrust his intentions? = 你怀疑他的意图吗？
Does she distrust her partner? = 她不信任她的伴侣吗？


否定句:
I don't distrust her; I simply need more evidence. = 我不怀疑她；我只是需要更多证据。
They didn't distrust the evidence; they simply misinterpreted it. = 他们不是不相信证据；他们只是误解了证据。


PHRASE->
