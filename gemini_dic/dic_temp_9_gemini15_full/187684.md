rookish: /ˈruːkɪʃ/ | adj. | 笨拙的；缺乏经验的；粗鲁的 |

英文解析：Rookish describes someone or something that is clumsy, inexperienced, or lacking in skill or judgment. It often implies a slightly mischievous or playful quality, but can also suggest a lack of sophistication or maturity.  The term originates from the image of a rook, a bird known for its sometimes clumsy behavior.

例句：

His rookish attempts at cooking resulted in a burnt dinner.  他的烹饪尝试很笨拙，结果晚餐烧焦了。

The rookish young soldier made several mistakes during his first battle.  这位缺乏经验的年轻士兵在第一次战斗中犯了好几个错误。


近义词：awkward, clumsy, inexperienced, unskilled, gauche, green

反义词：skilled, experienced, expert, polished, sophisticated


词性: 形容词
