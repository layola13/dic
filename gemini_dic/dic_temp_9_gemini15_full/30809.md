CFC:/ˌsiːˌɛfˈsiː/| n.| 氯氟烃 | Chlorofluorocarbons;  a family of chemical compounds containing carbon, fluorine, and chlorine, once widely used as refrigerants, propellants, and solvents, but now largely phased out due to their damaging effect on the ozone layer. | 例句：The Montreal Protocol aimed to phase out the production and consumption of CFCs.  《蒙特利尔议定书》旨在逐步淘汰氯氟烃的生产和消费。| 近义词：Freon (a specific brand of CFC) | 反义词：None specifically, but environmentally friendly refrigerants are alternatives. | 名词
