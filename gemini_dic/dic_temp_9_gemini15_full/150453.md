panted:/ˈpæn.tɪd/| v.|喘息，气喘吁吁地说话| to breathe quickly and loudly because you have been running or exercising, or because you are frightened, excited, or upset |例句：He panted, "I've run all the way from the station!" 他气喘吁吁地说：“我从车站一路跑过来的！”

例句：After climbing the hill, she panted for breath.  爬上山后，她气喘吁吁地喘着气。


近义词：gasped, breathed heavily, wheezed|反义词：breathed calmly, rested


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pants heavily after his workout. 他锻炼后呼吸急促。
She pants when she runs fast. 她跑步快的时候会喘气。

一般过去时:
The dog panted in the heat. 狗在热浪中喘气。
He panted with exhaustion after the race.  赛跑后，他累得喘不过气来。

现在完成时:
I have panted all the way up the stairs. 我一路爬楼梯都喘不过气来。
She has panted since she started running.  自从她开始跑步以来，她就一直气喘吁吁。

过去完成时:
He had panted before he finally reached the summit. 他到达山顶前就已经气喘吁吁了。
They had panted for hours before they found water. 他们在找到水之前已经喘了好几个小时了。

将来时:
He will pant when he sees the finish line. 他看到终点线时会喘气。
She will pant if she runs that fast. 如果她跑那么快，她会喘气。


虚拟语气:
If he ran a marathon, he would pant. 如果他跑马拉松，他会喘气。
I wish I hadn't panted so much; it made me feel worse. 我真希望我没有喘得那么厉害；那让我感觉更糟了。

被动语态: (很少用被动语态)
His words were panted out in a rush.  他的话气喘吁吁地脱口而出。(This example shows the result of panting, not panting itself being passive.)


疑问句:
Does he always pant like that? 他总是这样喘气吗？
Were they panting from exhaustion? 他们是因为筋疲力尽而喘气吗？

否定句:
He didn't pant; he walked calmly. 他没有喘气；他平静地走着。
She didn't pant during the walk.  她在散步过程中没有喘气。


PHRASE->
pant for breath: 喘不过气来
pant with excitement: 兴奋地喘息
pant and gasp: 喘息和倒吸气
panting dog: 喘气的狗
panting breaths: 急促的呼吸

词源:
The word "pant" comes from the Old French word "panter," meaning "to gasp" or "to pant."  This in turn likely derives from a Germanic root.
