lionises:/ˈlaɪənaɪz/| vt.| 崇拜；追捧；奉为偶像 | To treat someone as a very important and famous person. |例句：The media lionised the young actress after her successful film debut.  媒体在她的电影首秀成功后对她大加赞扬。

例句：The author was lionised for his groundbreaking novel.  这位作者因其开创性的作品而备受推崇。


近义词：celebrate, glorify, idolize, adore, exalt | 反义词：ignore, neglect, scorn, denigrate | 词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They lionise the pop star. = 他们崇拜这位流行歌星。
The city lionises its heroes. = 这个城市崇拜它的英雄。


一般过去时:
The town lionised the returning soldier. = 城镇的人们崇敬着归来的士兵。
The critics lionised her performance. = 批评家们赞扬了她的演出。


现在完成时:
The public has lionised him since his victory. = 自从他获胜以来，公众一直将他奉为偶像。
They have lionised her work for years. = 几年来，他们一直推崇她的作品。


过去完成时:
The media had already lionised him before the award ceremony. = 在颁奖典礼之前，媒体已经将他捧为偶像了。
The public had lionised her achievements for decades. = 几十年来，公众一直赞扬她的成就。


将来时:
They will lionise her for her contributions to science. = 他们将因她对科学的贡献而赞扬她。
The country will lionise its Olympic champions. = 全国人民将赞扬他们的奥运冠军。


虚拟语气:
If he wrote another best-seller, they would lionise him even more. = 如果他再写出一本畅销书，他们会更加崇拜他。
I wish they would lionise my work as they do his. = 我希望他们能像崇拜他的作品一样崇拜我的作品。


被动语态:
He was lionised by the press after his speech. = 演讲结束后，他受到了媒体的热捧。
Her paintings are lionised by art critics worldwide. = 她的画作受到世界各地艺术评论家的推崇。


疑问句:
Will they lionise him for his discovery? = 他们会因他的发现而赞扬他吗？
Are they going to lionise the new movie star? = 他们打算将这位新的电影明星捧为偶像吗？


否定句:
They didn't lionise him despite his talent. = 尽管他很有天赋，他们却没有崇拜他。
The critics did not lionise his latest novel. = 批评家们并没有赞扬他的最新小说。


PHRASE->
be lionised by = 被…所推崇
lionise someone for sth = 因某事而推崇某人
to be lionised as = 被奉为…
lionise someone to = 将某人推崇为…
widely lionised = 被广泛推崇
