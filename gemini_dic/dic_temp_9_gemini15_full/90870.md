hunker:/ˈhʌŋkər/ | v. | 蜷缩；蹲伏；坚持；顽强抵抗 | To crouch or settle down, usually for protection or warmth; to persist stubbornly. | 例句：The hikers hunkered down for the night in a cave.  徒步旅行者在一个山洞里过夜。

例句：She hunkered down in her chair to read. 她蜷缩在椅子上看书。


近义词：crouch, squat, snuggle, persevere, resist | 反义词：stand up, stretch out, give up

词源:
The word "hunker" likely originates from the Middle English word "honkeren," meaning "to crouch" or "to stoop."  The exact etymological path is unclear, but it shares similarities with words reflecting a bent or crouched posture.
