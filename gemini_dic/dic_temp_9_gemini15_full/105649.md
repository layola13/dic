kingfish:/ˈkɪŋˌfɪʃ/| n.|金枪鱼；大鱼；重要人物|A large fish, especially a kind of tuna.  The term can also refer to a person of great influence or importance.|例句：The kingfish is a prized catch for many anglers. = 金枪鱼是许多钓鱼爱好者梦寐以求的战利品。

例句：He's a kingfish in the business world. = 他是商界的大人物。


近义词：tuna, big shot, VIP, tycoon|反义词：small fry, nobody


词源:The word "kingfish" combines "king" (referring to its size and importance) and "fish".  The specific etymology traces back to its use in describing large tuna species in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, eventually extending to its metaphorical use for powerful individuals.
