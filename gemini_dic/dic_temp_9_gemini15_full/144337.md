outstanding:/ˌaʊtˈstændɪŋ/| adj.|杰出的，突出的，未解决的|Excellent; remarkably good; distinguished.  Something outstanding is superior or exceptional in quality, performance, or achievement.  It can also refer to something that remains unsettled or unpaid.|例句：Her performance was outstanding.  她的表演非常出色。


例句：There are several outstanding issues to be resolved.  还有几个悬而未决的问题需要解决。


近义词：excellent, exceptional, remarkable, prominent, distinguished, superior, unresolved


反义词：inferior, mediocre, ordinary, common, resolved


词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project is outstanding. = 这个项目很出色。
His work is outstanding. = 他的工作很出色。


一般过去时:
Her essay was outstanding. = 她的论文非常出色。
The performance was outstanding. = 表演非常出色。


现在完成时:
He has made an outstanding contribution. = 他做出了杰出的贡献。
She has achieved outstanding results. = 她取得了杰出的成绩。


过去完成时:
The problem had been outstanding for months. = 这个问题已经悬而未决好几个月了。
The debt had been outstanding since last year. = 债务自去年以来一直未偿还。


将来时:
The results will be outstanding. = 结果将会非常出色。
His future is outstanding. = 他的未来一片光明。


虚拟语气:
If his performance were outstanding, he would win the award. = 如果他的表现出色，他就会获奖。
If the design were outstanding, it would be adopted. = 如果设计很出色，它就会被采用。


被动语态:
The bill was outstanding for a long time. = 这张账单欠款很久了。
The problem is outstanding. = 问题未解决。


疑问句:
Is the quality of the work outstanding? = 这项工作的质量是否出色？
Is there any outstanding debt? = 是否有任何未偿还的债务？


否定句:
His work is not outstanding. = 他的工作并不出色。
The results were not outstanding. = 结果并不出色。
