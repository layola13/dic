individualising: /ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使个体化；个性化 |  The act of making something unique to a particular individual or group.  It emphasizes the distinct characteristics and needs of each person. | 例句：The teacher individualised the lesson plans to cater to the different learning styles of her students. = 老师个性化了教学计划以适应学生的不同学习风格。


The school is individualising its approach to student learning. = 学校正在个性化其学生学习方法。


近义词：personalizing, customizing, tailoring, differentiating | 反义词：standardizing, generalizing | 词性：动词


词源：源于individual (个体) + -izing (动词后缀，表示“使……化”)。  The suffix "-izing" transforms the noun "individual" into a verb, indicating the process of making something individual or unique.
