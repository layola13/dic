dragged:/dræɡd/ | v. | 拖；拉；拽；拖延 | To pull (someone or something) along forcefully, often with difficulty.  It also means to make something last longer than necessary. | 例句：He dragged the heavy box across the room. 他费力地把沉重的箱子拖过房间。

例句：The meeting dragged on for hours.  会议冗长地持续了好几个小时。


近义词：pulled, hauled, towed, delayed | 反义词：pushed, accelerated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drags the furniture across the floor. 他把家具拖过地板。
She drags her feet when it comes to doing her homework.  说到做作业，她总是拖拖拉拉。

一般过去时:
They dragged the boat to the shore. 他们把船拖到岸边。
He dragged the conversation on until everyone was tired. 他把谈话拖延到每个人都疲惫不堪。


现在完成时:
I have dragged myself out of bed this morning. 我今天早上费力地起了床。
She has dragged her feet on this project for weeks.  她这个项目已经拖延了几周了。


过去完成时:
He had dragged the heavy suitcase all the way from the airport. 他已经把沉重的行李箱从机场一路拖过来了。
They had dragged their feet on the decision, causing a delay in the project. 他们对这个决定拖延了很久，导致项目延期。


将来时:
I will drag the rubbish to the bin later. 我待会儿会把垃圾拖到垃圾桶里。
They will drag the negotiations out for as long as possible. 他们会尽可能地拖延谈判。


虚拟语气:
If I had known it was so heavy, I wouldn't have dragged it myself. 如果我知道这么重，我就不会自己拖了。
I wish I hadn't dragged my feet and started the project earlier. 我真希望我没有拖延，早点开始这个项目。


被动语态:
The heavy box was dragged across the room.  沉重的箱子被拖过房间。
The meeting was dragged out unnecessarily. 会议被不必要地拖长了。


疑问句:
Did you drag the car out of the ditch? 你把车从沟里拖出来了吗？
Why did they drag the process out so much? 他们为什么把这个过程拖得这么久？


否定句:
He didn't drag the chair into the room. 他没有把椅子拖进房间。
She didn't drag her feet on the project; she finished it ahead of schedule. 她并没有在这个项目上拖延；她提前完成了。


PHRASE->
drag one's feet: 拖延
drag someone down: 使某人消沉
drag oneself: 勉强，费力
drag something out: 拖延
drag race: 直线加速赛
