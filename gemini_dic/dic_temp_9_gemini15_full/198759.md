sickenings:/ˈsɪkənɪŋz/| adj.|令人作呕的；令人厌恶的；令人反感的 | Extremely unpleasant and causing nausea or disgust.  It describes something that causes a feeling of intense revulsion or nausea, both physically and emotionally. |例句：The smell of rotting meat was sickening.  腐烂肉的气味令人作呕。

The sight of the accident was sickening.  看到事故现场令人作呕。

近义词：disgusting, nauseating, repulsive, revolting, sickening

反义词：pleasant, delightful, appealing, agreeable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The smell sickens me. = 这气味让我恶心。
This behavior sickens many people. = 这种行为令许多人作呕。

一般过去时:
The news sickened her. = 这个消息让她恶心。
The sight of the blood sickened the onlookers. = 看到血，旁观者都感到恶心。

现在完成时:
The constant negativity has sickened me. =  持续的消极情绪让我感到厌烦。
This betrayal has sickened many of his supporters. = 这种背叛行为让他的许多支持者感到厌恶。

过去完成时:
I had already sickened of his constant complaints. = 我已经对他不断的抱怨感到厌烦了。
She had sickened of the endless routine before she quit her job. = 在她辞职之前，她已经厌倦了无尽的例行公事。


将来时:

That idea will sicken many people. = 那个主意会让很多人反感。


虚拟语气:
