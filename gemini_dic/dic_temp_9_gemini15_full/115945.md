marriable: /ˈmærɪəbl/ | adj. | 可结婚的；适于结婚的 | Suitable for marriage; of marriageable age.  |  例句：She's reached a marriageable age. 她到了适婚年龄。  | 近义词：eligible, suitable | 反义词：unmarriageable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is marriageable now. = 他现在到了适婚年龄。
She is considered marriageable by many. = 许多人认为她适婚。


一般过去时:
He was marriageable at 25. = 他25岁时到了适婚年龄。
She wasn't considered marriageable then. = 当时人们认为她还不适婚。


现在完成时:
He has become marriageable recently. = 他最近到了适婚年龄。
She has always been marriageable, but hasn't found the right person. = 她一直都适婚，但还没找到合适的人。


过去完成时:
He had been marriageable for years before he met her. = 在他遇到她之前，他已经适婚好几年了。
She had not been considered marriageable in her youth. = 她年轻时不被认为是适婚的。


将来时:
He will be marriageable in a few years. = 他几年后就到适婚年龄了。
She will likely be marriageable next year. = 她明年很可能就到适婚年龄了。


虚拟语气:
If she were marriageable, he would propose. = 如果她适婚，他会求婚。
If he had been marriageable earlier, they would have married sooner. = 如果他早些适婚，他们就会早些结婚。


被动语态:
He was considered marriageable by his family. = 他被家人认为是适婚的。
She wasn't considered marriageable by his parents. = 她不被他的父母认为是适婚的。


疑问句:
Is he marriageable yet? = 他到适婚年龄了吗？
Is she considered marriageable in this culture? = 在这种文化中，她被认为是适婚的吗？


否定句:
He is not yet marriageable. = 他还没到适婚年龄。
She was not considered marriageable due to her age. = 由于年龄原因，她不被认为是适婚的。

词源:
The word "marriageable" is derived from the word "marriage" + "-able," a suffix indicating capability or suitability.  "Marriage" itself has roots in Old French "mariage" and ultimately Latin "maritare" (to marry).
