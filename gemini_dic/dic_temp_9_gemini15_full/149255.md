oyster:/ˈɔɪstər/| n. |牡蛎，蚝|A marine bivalve mollusk with a rough, irregular shell.  Oysters are known for their edible flesh and pearls. They are filter feeders and play an important role in marine ecosystems.|例句：We had oysters for appetizers. 我们以牡蛎作为开胃菜。

例句：The oyster beds are a vital part of the coastal ecosystem.  牡蛎养殖场是沿海生态系统的重要组成部分。

词源:
The word "oyster" comes from the Old French "oistre," which in turn derives from the Latin "ostrea."  The ultimate origin is thought to be from the Proto-Indo-European root "*h₂ews-," meaning "shellfish."
