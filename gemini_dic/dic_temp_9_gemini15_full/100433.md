intertraded:/ˌɪntərˈtreɪdɪd/ | adj. | 互相交易的，相互贸易的 |  Describes goods or services that are traded between parties.  It implies a reciprocal exchange. | Example: The two countries engaged in extensive intertraded goods, particularly agricultural products and manufactured items.  两个国家进行了广泛的相互贸易，特别是农产品和制成品。 |近义词:mutually traded, reciprocally exchanged | 反义词: unilaterally traded, one-sided exchange |  词性:形容词


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "is," "are," "was," etc.  It describes the nature of the trade.  Therefore, tense and voice are determined by the verb used with it.

Intertraded services are a significant part of their economy. = 互相交易的服务是他们经济的重要组成部分。
