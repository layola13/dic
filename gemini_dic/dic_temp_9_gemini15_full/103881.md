jubilee:/ˈdʒuːbɪli/ | n. |禧年，庆祝会 | A jubilee is a period of celebration, typically marking a special anniversary.  It often involves public festivities and rejoicing.  | Example: The Queen's Golden Jubilee was a spectacular event.  女王的金禧庆典是一场盛大的活动。  |近义词：celebration, anniversary, festival |反义词：mourning, commemoration (depending on context)


indulgence:/ɪnˈdʌldʒəns/ | n. |放纵，沉溺；宽容 | Indulgence refers to the act of giving oneself over to pleasure or desire, often to excess. It can also refer to a lenient or permissive attitude. | Example: Her indulgence in chocolate cake led to a stomach ache. 她沉溺于巧克力蛋糕导致胃痛。  |近义词：pampering, gratification, leniency |反义词：self-denial, abstinence, restraint

**Indulgence:**

She indulges in long naps every afternoon. = 她每天下午都沉溺于长时间的午睡。
