renunciation:/rɪˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən/| n. |放弃；抛弃；弃权； отречение |The formal rejection or renunciation of something, typically a belief, claim, or right.  Renunciation often involves a conscious and deliberate act of giving something up.  It implies a turning away from or abandoning something previously held dear or considered important. |例句：His renunciation of violence surprised everyone.  他的放弃暴力举动让每个人都大吃一惊。

例句：The renunciation of his title was a surprise to the whole family. 他放弃头衔让全家人都很意外。


近义词：abandonment, relinquishment, surrender, abjuration |反义词：acceptance, embrace, retention, affirmation |词性：名词


词源：源自于拉丁语 "renuntiatio,"  由 "renuntiare" 派生而来，意为 "to renounce,"  由 "re-" (again) 和 "nuntiare" (to announce, declare) 组成。  因此，其字面意义是“再次宣布放弃”。


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He renounces his claim to the throne. = 他放弃了他对王位的要求。
She renounces her belief in that religion. = 她放弃了她对那种宗教的信仰。

一般过去时:
He renounced his citizenship. = 他放弃了他的国籍。
She renounced her inheritance. = 她放弃了她的继承权。


现在完成时:
He has renounced all worldly possessions. = 他已经放弃了所有世俗的财产。
She has renounced her former lifestyle. = 她已经放弃了她以前的生活方式。


过去完成时:
He had renounced his faith before joining the army. = 他在参军之前就放弃了他的信仰。
She had renounced her ambition before the disappointment. = 在失望之前，她已经放弃了她的雄心壮志。


将来时:
He will renounce his position next month. = 他下个月将放弃他的职位。
She will renounce her claim to the property. = 她将放弃她对该房产的要求。


虚拟语气:
If he renounced his title, he would be free. = 如果他放弃他的头衔，他将获得自由。
If she had renounced her beliefs earlier, she wouldn't be so conflicted now. = 如果她早点放弃她的信仰，她现在就不会如此矛盾了。


被动语态:
His claim was renounced by the court. = 他的要求被法院驳回了。
Her inheritance was renounced by her. = 她的遗产被她自己放弃了。


疑问句:
Will he renounce his position? = 他会放弃他的职位吗？
Has she renounced her citizenship? = 她放弃了她的国籍吗？


否定句:
He did not renounce his beliefs. = 他没有放弃他的信仰。
She will not renounce her claim to the money. = 她不会放弃她对这笔钱的要求。


PHRASE->

solemn renunciation = 庄严放弃
