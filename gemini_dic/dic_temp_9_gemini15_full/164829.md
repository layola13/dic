/ˈpriːfɪksɪz/ | n. | 前缀 | Prefixes are groups of letters added to the beginning of a word to modify its meaning. They are a common element in word formation, allowing for the creation of new words or the alteration of existing ones.  Prefixes often indicate negation, quantity, location, time, or other grammatical or semantic relationships. |  The prefix "un-" changes the meaning of "happy" to "unhappy". The prefix "re-" indicates repetition, as in "rewrite". | 近义词: word parts, affixes | 反义词: suffixes
