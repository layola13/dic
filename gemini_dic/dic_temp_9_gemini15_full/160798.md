positron:/ˈpɒzɪtrɒn/ /ˈpɑːzɪtrɑːn/| n. |正电子|A positron is the antiparticle or antimatter counterpart of the electron. It has the same mass as an electron but has a positive electric charge.|Example:  Positrons are used in PET scans to image the body's internal structures.|近义词:antielectron|反义词:electron|
