cutaway:/ˈkʌtəweɪ/ | n. adj. v. | 切除；特写镜头；剖面图；  |A cutaway is a drawing or model showing a cross-section, revealing the interior structure. It can also refer to a brief scene interrupting the main action of a film or television program, or to the act of cutting away something. |例句：The architect showed us a cutaway of the building's design. 建筑师向我们展示了建筑物设计的剖面图。

例句：The film used cutaways to show the characters' inner thoughts. 电影使用了特写镜头来展现人物的内心想法。


近义词：cross-section, schematic, excerpt, interruption |反义词：continuity, unbroken flow

词性：名词，形容词，动词

词源:
The word "cutaway" originates from the verb "cut away," meaning to remove or separate by cutting.  The noun and adjective usages developed from this meaning, referring to something that has been cut away to reveal an interior or to a scene that is cut away from the main narrative.
