estimated:/ˈɛstɪmeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 估计的，预计的 | Based on an estimation or calculation; approximate.  It suggests a degree of uncertainty. | The estimated cost of the project is $1 million.  该项目的估计成本为100万美元。 The estimated time of arrival is 3 PM. 预计到达时间是下午3点。 | calculated, projected, approximate, presumptive | exact, precise, definite


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The estimated cost is quite high. = 估计的成本相当高。
We estimated the number of attendees to be around 100. = 我们估计出席人数大约在100人左右。


一般过去时:
They estimated the damage to be significant. = 他们估计损失相当大。
The archaeologist estimated the age of the artifact. = 考古学家估计了文物的年代。


现在完成时:
We have estimated the project's completion date. = 我们已经估计了项目的完成日期。
Scientists have estimated the size of the asteroid. = 科学家们已经估计了小行星的大小。


过去完成时:
They had estimated the budget before the project started. = 项目开始前他们已经估计了预算。
She had estimated the travel time to be longer than it actually was. = 她估计的旅行时间比实际时间要长。


将来时:
We will estimate the repair costs after the inspection. = 我们将在检查后估算维修费用。
They will estimate the impact of the new policy. = 他们将评估新政策的影响。


虚拟语气:
If we estimated the risk accurately, we could have avoided it. = 如果我们准确地估计了风险，我们本可以避免它。
I wish we had estimated the time more carefully. = 我希望我们当时更仔细地估计时间。


被动语态:
The completion date was estimated to be in December. = 完工日期估计在12月。
The damage was estimated at several million dollars. = 损失估计达数百万美元。


疑问句:
What is the estimated completion time? = 预计完成时间是多少？
How was the population size estimated? = 人口规模是如何估计的？


否定句:
The cost was not accurately estimated. = 成本没有被准确地估计。
They did not accurately estimate the time required. = 他们没有准确地估计所需的时间。
