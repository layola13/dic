metazoan:/ˌmɛtəˈzoʊən/| n. |多细胞动物；後生动物 |A metazoan is a multicellular animal, as opposed to a protozoan (single-celled animal).  Metazoans are characterized by their complex organization, including specialized cells, tissues, organs, and organ systems. They exhibit a wide range of body plans and lifestyles. |Example: Sponges, jellyfish, insects, and mammals are all examples of metazoans.|近义词:multicellular animal|反义词:protozoan
