nixing:/ˈnɪksɪŋ/| v.|取消；否决；破坏；压制|To put an end to something; to veto or reject something; to suppress or destroy something.|例句：They nixed the plan to build a new highway. 他们否决了修建一条新高速公路的计划。|例句：The government nixed the proposal due to budget constraints. 政府由于预算限制而否决了该提案。|近义词：veto, reject, cancel, quash, scrap|反义词：approve, accept, endorse

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He nixes every new idea. = 他否决每一个新主意。
They nix the proposal. = 他们否决了提议。


一般过去时:
She nixed the deal at the last minute. = 她在最后一刻取消了交易。
The committee nixed the budget. = 委员会否决了预算。


现在完成时:
They have nixed several projects this year. = 今年他们已经否决了好几个项目。
She has nixed my plan again. = 她又一次否决了我的计划。


过去完成时:
He had nixed the idea before I could even present it. = 我还没来得及提出想法，他就已经否决了。
They had nixed the funding long ago. = 他们很久以前就否决了资金申请。


将来时:

She will probably nix the proposal. = 她可能会否决这项提案。


虚拟语气:

I wish they hadn't nixed the plan. = 我真希望他们没有否决这个计划。
