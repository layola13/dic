purine:/ˈpjʊəriːn/| n. |嘌呤|A heterocyclic aromatic organic compound that consists of a pyrimidine ring fused to an imidazole ring. It is a parent compound of many biologically important substances such as adenine and guanine which are components of DNA and RNA.  Purines are also involved in energy metabolism (ATP) and cell signaling.| Purines are found in many foods, including meat, poultry, fish, and beans.  High levels of purines in the body can lead to gout.|  近义词：None (it's a specific chemical compound)|反义词：None (no direct opposite)|名词
