comeuppance:/ˈkʌmʌpəns/| n. |报应；惩罚|The deserved punishment for a bad deed; retribution.|例句：He finally got his comeuppance for all his lies. 他最终为他的谎言付出了代价。|近义词：retribution, punishment, revenge|反义词：reward, recompense


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His bad behavior usually results in a comeuppance. = 他糟糕的行为通常会导致报应。
The villain’s comeuppance is often swift and brutal in this type of film. = 在这类电影中，恶棍的报应通常迅速而残酷。


一般过去时:
The bully received his comeuppance when his victim fought back. = 当他的受害者反击时，那个恶霸得到了报应。
Her comeuppance arrived unexpectedly in the form of a public humiliation. = 她的报应以公开羞辱的形式意外地到来。


现在完成时:
He has finally gotten his comeuppance for years of deceit. = 他终于为他多年的欺骗行为得到了报应。
The company has received its comeuppance for unethical business practices. = 这家公司因不道德的商业行为而受到了报应。


过去完成时:
By the time he confessed, his comeuppance had already begun. = 等他坦白的时候，他的报应已经开始了。
She realized that her earlier arrogance had already brought about her comeuppance. = 她意识到她之前的傲慢已经带来了她的报应。


将来时:

The consequences of their actions will surely bring them their just comeuppance. = 他们行为的后果肯定会给他们带来应有的报应。


虚拟语气:
