morosely:/məˈroʊsli/ | adv. | 郁闷地；阴沉地 | in a gloomy or sullen manner.  | He sat morosely by the window, watching the rain. 他郁闷地坐在窗边，看着雨。|  gloomily, sullenly, despondently | cheerfully, happily, brightly | 副词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He speaks morosely. = 他说话很郁闷。
She acts morosely. = 她行为举止很阴沉。


一般过去时:
He spoke morosely to his friend. = 他对他朋友说话很郁闷。
She acted morosely throughout the meeting. = 她在整个会议期间都表现得很阴沉。


现在完成时:
He has spoken morosely all day. = 他整天都说话很郁闷。
She has acted morosely since the accident. = 自从事故发生后，她一直表现得很阴沉。


过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

If she felt better, she wouldn't act so morosely. = 如果她感觉好些，她就不会表现得这么阴沉了。


被动语态: (This adverb doesn't typically take a passive voice.)  The situation was described morosely in the report.  报告中对情况的描述很悲观。  His mood was described morosely by the doctor. 医生对他的情绪描述得十分低落。


疑问句: (This adverb doesn't directly form questions.) Does he always speak so morosely? 他总是那么郁闷地说话吗？ Does she often act so morosely? 她经常表现得这么阴沉吗？


否定句:
He didn't speak morosely; he was actually quite cheerful. = 他并没有说话很郁闷；实际上他相当快乐。
She didn't act morosely; she was simply tired. = 她并没有表现得很阴沉；她只是累了。
