self-repellencies: /ˌsɛlf rɪˈpɛlənsiz/ | n. | 自排斥性；自相排斥 | The tendency of similar entities to repel or avoid each other.  This term is often used in physics, chemistry, and biology to describe phenomena where like components push away from each other.  For example, in colloid science, self-repellencies between particles can prevent aggregation. | Example: The self-repellencies of the charged particles prevented them from clumping together.  =带电粒子的自相排斥阻止了它们的聚集。 | 近义词: self-avoidance, mutual repulsion | 反义词: self-attraction, mutual attraction | 名词
