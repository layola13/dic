self-relishes: /ˌsɛlfˈrɛlɪʃɪz/ | v. | 自得其乐，自我陶醉 | To find great pleasure or satisfaction in oneself or one's own company.  | 例句：She self-relishes in her solitude, finding peace and contentment in her own company. 她独自一人自得其乐，在独处中找到平静和满足。


She self-relishes her accomplishments, often recounting her successes to anyone who will listen. 她为自己的成就而沾沾自喜，经常向任何愿意倾听的人讲述她的成功。


近义词：enjoys oneself, revels in, takes pleasure in, luxuriates in | 反义词：depresses oneself, feels miserable, is unhappy

词源:  The word is a relatively modern coinage, combining "self" with the verb "relish," which itself derives from Old French *releschir meaning "to lick again," suggesting a thorough enjoyment.  The "-ish" suffix adds an intensifying or somewhat informal tone.
