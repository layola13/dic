coadjuvant: /koʊˈædʒuːvənt/ | adj. |辅助的；佐剂的 |  A coadjuvant treatment or therapy is one that is given in addition to the main treatment to improve its effectiveness.  It often refers to therapies used alongside a primary treatment, such as chemotherapy or radiation therapy, to enhance the effects of the primary treatment.  | 例句：The study investigated the effectiveness of a coadjuvant chemotherapy regimen after surgery. 这项研究调查了手术后辅助化疗方案的有效性。

The coadjuvant therapy improved the patient's chances of survival.  辅助治疗提高了患者的生存几率。| 近义词：adjuvant, supplementary, auxiliary | 反义词：primary, principal

词源:
From French *coadjuvant* (16th century), from Latin *coadjuvare* "to help," from *co-*(combining form of *cum* "with") + *adjuvare* "to help," from *ad-* "to" + *juvare* "to help, assist."  The word ultimately traces back to the Proto-Indo-European root *yeu-*, meaning "to join, yoke."
