self-variation:/ˌsɛlfˌveəriˈeɪʃən/| n. |自我变异，自身变异 |The process by which something changes or develops independently, without external influence.  It often refers to the inherent capacity of a system or organism to change its own structure or behavior.  In biology, it can describe mutations or adaptations within a species without external selective pressure. In other fields, it may describe a system's internal adjustments or modifications. | Example:  The self-variation in the software's algorithms led to unexpected improvements in efficiency.  软件算法的自我变异导致效率意外提高。 |近义词: self-modification, internal change, autonomous variation |反义词: external influence, imposed change
