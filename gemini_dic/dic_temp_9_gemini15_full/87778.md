hiatuses:/haɪˈeɪtəsiːz/ | n. | 间歇；中断；缺口 |  A hiatus is a pause or break in continuity in a sequence or activity.  It can refer to a gap in a series, a break in time, or a pause in a process. |


例句：

There was a hiatus in the negotiations. = 谈判中断了一段时间。

The company experienced a long hiatus in production. = 公司经历了很长时间的停产。


近义词：break, interruption, gap, pause, intermission


反义词：continuity, continuation


词性: 名词


词源:  From Latin *hiatus, from hiare "to gape, yawn," related to Greek χάσκω (cháskō) "to gape."  The original sense referred to a physical gap or opening, which then extended to metaphorical gaps in time or activity.
