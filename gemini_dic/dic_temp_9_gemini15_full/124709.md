moxie:/ˈmɑːksi/| /ˈmɒksi/| n.|胆量；勇气；魄力|  A spirited and determined courage; nerve.  It implies a certain audacity and self-assurance.  |例句：She has the moxie to stand up to her boss. 她有胆量反抗她的老板。

例句：It takes real moxie to start your own business.  创业需要真正的魄力。


近义词：courage, nerve, guts, audacity, grit, spunk

反义词：cowardice, timidity, fear


词源：The word's origin is uncertain, but it likely comes from an alteration of the word "mox," a slang term for "muscular strength."  It then evolved through various slang uses before solidifying its modern meaning.  The etymology is not definitively traced.
