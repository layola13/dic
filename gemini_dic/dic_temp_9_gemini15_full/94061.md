impasse:/ˈɪmpæs/| n. |僵局；死胡同；困境 |A situation in which no progress is possible, especially because of disagreement; a deadlock.  |例句：Negotiations reached an impasse when neither side would compromise.  谈判陷入僵局，因为双方都不肯妥协。

例句：The two countries remain in an impasse over the territorial dispute.  这两个国家在领土争端上仍然僵持不下。


近义词：deadlock, stalemate, standstill, gridlock |反义词：breakthrough, resolution, solution |词性：名词


词源：

From French impasse (literally "impassable road"), from impos "impossible" + passer "to pass".  Ultimately derived from Latin impossibilis ("impossible") and passare ("to pass").
