handicap:/ˈhændikæp/| n. v.|障碍；残疾；不利条件；让步；使不利；妨碍|

英文解析:A handicap is a disadvantage that makes something difficult. It can refer to a physical disability, a disadvantage in a competition (like giving a better player a head start), or any other circumstance that makes something harder to achieve.  As a verb, it means to put someone or something at a disadvantage.

例句:
His blindness is a significant handicap in his daily life. = 他的失明严重妨碍了他的日常生活。

The team with the better record received a handicap in the tournament. = 拥有更好战绩的球队在锦标赛中获得了让步。


近义词:disadvantage, disability, impediment, drawback, obstacle, hindrance

反义词:advantage, benefit, assistance


词性:Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The steep hill handicaps the runners. =陡峭的山坡对跑步者造成了不利。
This new rule handicaps small businesses. = 这条新规不利于小型企业。

一般过去时:
The heavy rain handicapped the rescue efforts. = 大雨妨碍了救援工作。
His injury handicapped him in the competition. = 他的伤势在比赛中让他处于劣势。

现在完成时:
The lack of funding has handicapped the project. = 资金短缺已经阻碍了这个项目。
Years of illness have handicapped her. = 多年的疾病使她受到了很大的影响。

过去完成时:

The poor infrastructure had handicapped the country's development. = 糟糕的基础设施阻碍了国家的进步。

将来时:
The bad weather will handicap the flight schedule. = 恶劣天气将会影响航班时刻表。
The new regulations will handicap the company's expansion plans. = 新法规将阻碍公司的扩张计划。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't been handicapped by illness, he would have won the race. = 如果他没有因病而受阻，他就会赢得比赛。
If the team hadn't been handicapped by injuries, they would have done much better. = 如果球队没有因为伤病而受影响，他们会表现得更好。

被动语态:
The athletes were handicapped by the strong wind. = 运动员们受到了强风的阻碍。
The project was handicapped by a lack of resources. = 该项目因缺乏资源而受阻。

疑问句:
Does his disability handicap him significantly? = 他的残疾是否严重地影响了他？
Does the lack of funding handicap the research? = 资金不足是否阻碍了这项研究？

否定句:
His training did not handicap him in the competition. = 他的训练并没有在比赛中给他带来不利。
The new software does not handicap users who are not tech-savvy. = 新软件不会对那些不精通技术的用户造成不便。
