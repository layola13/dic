earthily:/ˈɜːrθɪli/ | adj. | 尘世般的；世俗的；朴实的；粗俗的 | Relating to or characteristic of the earth; down-to-earth; unrefined.  It suggests a connection to the physical world, often implying simplicity, lack of refinement, or even crudeness. | Example:  The painting depicted an earthily scene of farmers working in the field. 这幅画描绘了农民在地里劳作的朴实场景。


The novel's characters were portrayed in an earthily, realistic manner. 这部小说的角色以一种朴实、真实的方式被刻画出来。| 近义词：mundane, earthly, worldly, rustic, coarse | 反义词：heavenly, celestial, refined, sophisticated

词源:
The word "earthily" derives from the adjective "earthy," which itself comes from the Old English word "eorþe," meaning "earth."  The suffix "-ly" adds an adverbial or adjectival quality, indicating a manner or characteristic of something.
