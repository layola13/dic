monopolises:/məˈnɒpəlaɪzɪz/| vt.|垄断；独占；占据全部时间或注意力 |To control or possess something completely, especially to the exclusion of others.  |例句：The company monopolises the market for computer chips. 这家公司垄断了计算机芯片市场。|例句：He monopolises the conversation, never letting anyone else speak. 他独占了谈话，从不让别人说话。|近义词：dominate, control, corner, hoard |反义词：share, distribute, divide


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company monopolises the market. = 这家公司垄断了市场。
He monopolises the conversation. = 他垄断了谈话。

一般过去时:
The railroad company monopolised the transport industry. = 铁路公司垄断了运输业。
She monopolised his attention all evening. = 她整个晚上都吸引了他的注意力。

现在完成时:
That company has monopolised the software market for years. = 那家公司多年来一直垄断着软件市场。
He has monopolised the discussion since it started. = 从讨论开始他就一直在主导讨论。

过去完成时:
Before the new regulations, one company had monopolised the energy sector. = 在新的法规出台之前，一家公司垄断了能源行业。
She had monopolised her brother's time all summer. = 她整个夏天都霸占了哥哥的时间。

将来时:
They will monopolise the market if they succeed. = 如果他们成功了，他们将会垄断市场。
He will probably monopolise the meeting again. = 他可能会再次主导会议。

虚拟语气:
If the company monopolised the market, they would control prices. = 如果这家公司垄断了市场，他们就能控制价格。
I wish he wouldn't monopolise the conversation so much. = 我希望他不那么霸占谈话。

被动语态:
The market was monopolised by a single company. = 市场被一家公司垄断了。
The conversation was monopolised by the CEO. = 谈话被首席执行官所主导。

疑问句:
Does that company monopolise the market? = 那家公司是否垄断了市场？
Is he monopolising the discussion again? = 他又在主导讨论了吗？

否定句:
The company does not monopolise the market. = 这家公司并没有垄断市场。
She didn't monopolise the conversation. = 她并没有独占谈话。
