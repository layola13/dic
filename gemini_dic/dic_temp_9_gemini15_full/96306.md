indigently: /ˈɪndɪdʒəntlɪ/ | adv. | 贫困地；穷困地 | In a poor or needy manner; in poverty.  | The family lived indigently, struggling to make ends meet.  这个家庭生活贫困，努力维持生计。 | poorly, meagerly, despicably | affluently, opulently, richly | 副词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They live indigently. = 他们生活贫困。
He eats indigently. = 他吃得很差。


一般过去时:
They lived indigently in the past. = 他们过去生活贫困。
She ate indigently during her childhood. = 她童年时吃得很差。


现在完成时:
They have lived indigently for many years. = 他们多年来一直生活贫困。
She has always eaten indigently. = 她总是吃得很差。


过去完成时:
They had lived indigently before they received aid. = 他们获得援助之前一直生活贫困。
She had eaten indigently before she found a job. = 她找到工作之前一直吃得很差。


将来时:
They will continue to live indigently without help. = 没有帮助，他们将继续生活贫困。
He will likely eat indigently unless he gets a better job. = 除非他找到更好的工作，否则他很可能会吃得很差。


虚拟语气:
If they were not so indigent, they would live better. = 如果他们不那么贫困，他们会过得更好。
If she had not eaten so indigently, she would be healthier. = 如果她没有吃得那么差，她会更健康。


被动语态:
(This adverb is rarely used in the passive voice.  The action described by the adverb is not typically performed *on* someone.)


疑问句:
Do they live indigently? = 他们生活贫困吗？
Does he eat indigently? = 他吃得很差吗？


否定句:
They do not live indigently. = 他们不生活贫困。
He does not eat indigently. = 他不吃得很差。


PHRASE->
live indigently = 贫困地生活
exist indigently = 勉强维持生活
suffer indigently = 贫困地受苦
dress indigently = 衣着寒酸
work indigently = 勉强工作 (implying low pay or poor conditions)

词源:
From Middle English *indigent*, from Old French *indigent*, from Latin *indigēns*, present participle of *indīgēre* "to be in need," from *in-* "in" + *digēre* "to lack."
