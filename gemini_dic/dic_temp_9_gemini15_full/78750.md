gil:/ɡɪl/| n.|腮；鳃；峡谷|A gill is a respiratory organ found in many aquatic animals, used for extracting oxygen from water.  It can also refer to a narrow ravine or gorge.|例句：Fish breathe using their gills. 鱼用鳃呼吸。

The salmon swam upstream, its gills working hard.  鲑鱼逆流而上，鳃努力工作着。|近义词：branchia, fin (in some contexts)|反义词：lung (as an alternative respiratory organ)
