deduces:/dɪˈduːs/ | vt. |推断，演绎，推论 |To reach a conclusion from facts or evidence.  It involves reasoning from general principles to specific instances or from specific instances to general principles. |例句：From the footprints in the snow, we deduced that someone had passed by. 从雪地上的脚印，我们推断有人经过。

例句：The detective deduced the murderer's identity from the clues left at the scene.  侦探从案发现场留下的线索推断出凶手的身份。


近义词：infer, conclude, infer, gather, reason, surmise |反义词：assume, guess


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deduces the answer from the given data. 他从给定的数据中推断出答案。
She deduces the meaning from the context. 她从上下文推断出含义。


一般过去时:
The scientist deduced a new theory based on his experiments. 科学家根据他的实验推断出一个新理论。
The police deduced the suspect's location from his phone records. 警方根据他的电话记录推断出嫌疑人的位置。


现在完成时:
We have deduced that the problem lies in the software. 我们已经推断出问题出在软件上。
She has deduced the pattern of the numbers. 她已经推断出数字的模式。


过去完成时:
They had deduced the cause of the accident before the investigation began. 他们在调查开始之前就已经推断出事故的原因。
I had deduced the solution to the puzzle before I even read the instructions. 我甚至在阅读说明书之前就已经推断出谜题的答案。


将来时:
Scientists will deduce more about the universe using the new telescope. 科学家们将利用新的望远镜推断出更多关于宇宙的信息。
We will deduce the winning strategy from the competitor's moves. 我们将从竞争对手的举动中推断出获胜策略。


虚拟语气:
If we had more information, we could deduce the answer more easily. 如果我们有更多信息，我们就能更容易地推断出答案。
If I were a detective, I would deduce the truth from the evidence. 如果我是一个侦探，我会从证据中推断出真相。


被动语态:

疑问句:
What can be deduced from these facts?  从这些事实中可以推断出什么？
How did the detective deduce the killer's motive? 侦探是如何推断出凶手的动机的？


否定句:
We could not deduce the reason for the delay. 我们无法推断出延误的原因。
The police were unable to deduce the suspect's whereabouts. 警方无法推断出嫌疑人的下落。
