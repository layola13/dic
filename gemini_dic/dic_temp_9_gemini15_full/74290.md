fractals:/ˈfræktəlz/| n.|分形，碎形|Fractals are complex geometric shapes that are self-similar at different scales.  They are often created by repeating a simple process over and over again.  Examples include coastlines, snowflakes, and tree branches.|例句：The intricate patterns of a snowflake are a classic example of a fractal.|近义词：self-similar structures|反义词：Euclidean shapes
