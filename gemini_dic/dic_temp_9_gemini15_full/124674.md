/muːvd/ | adj. | 移动过的；搬迁过的 | moved describes something that has been changed in position or has undergone a transition.  It implies a past action resulting in a change of state.  | The furniture was moved to the new house. 家具被搬到了新房子。 The audience was moved to tears by the performance. 观众被演出感动得流泪。 | shifted, relocated, transferred | stationary, unmoved | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The box is moved every day. = 箱子每天都被移动。
The chair is moved to the window. = 椅子被移到窗边。

一般过去时:
The painting was moved yesterday. = 画昨天被移动了。
The table was moved to the other room. = 桌子被移到了另一个房间。

现在完成时:
The piano has been moved to the living room. = 钢琴已经被搬到客厅了。
The files have been moved to the cloud. = 文件已经被移动到云端了。

过去完成时:
The books had been moved before the cleaning. = 书在打扫之前已经被移动了。
The furniture had been moved before the party. = 家具在派对之前已经被移动了。

将来时:
The statue will be moved next week. = 雕像下周将被移动。
The plants will be moved to a sunnier spot. = 植物将被移到阳光更充足的地方。

虚拟语气:
If the desk were moved, the room would look bigger. = 如果书桌被移动，房间看起来会更大。
I wish the couch had been moved sooner. = 我希望沙发早点被移动。

被动语态:
The boxes were moved carefully. = 箱子被小心地移动了。
The heavy equipment is being moved now. = 重型设备现在正在被移动。

疑问句:
Was the car moved? = 车被移动了吗？
Has the meeting been moved? = 会议被改期了吗？

否定句:
The vase wasn't moved. = 花瓶没有被移动。
The project hasn't been moved yet. = 项目还没有被移动。
