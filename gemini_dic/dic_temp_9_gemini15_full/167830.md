/prɪm/ | adj. | 非常整洁的；一丝不苟的；呆板的；拘谨的 | Prim means excessively neat, precise, and formal in manner or appearance. It often suggests a stiffness and lack of spontaneity.  | She had a prim and proper demeanor, always impeccably dressed. 她举止一丝不苟，衣着总是无可挑剔。 |  precise, proper, meticulous |  sloppy, casual, informal | adj.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher is prim in her appearance. = 老师的穿着打扮非常整洁。
Her desk is always prim and tidy. = 她的桌子总是井井有条。


一般过去时:
She was prim and proper in her youth. = 她年轻时非常端庄。
The old woman was prim about her garden. = 那位老妇人对她的花园非常讲究。


现在完成时:
She has always been prim and proper. = 她一直都非常端庄。
He has remained prim despite his age. = 尽管上了年纪，他仍然一丝不苟。


过去完成时:

将来时:

He will remain prim, even in casual settings. = 他即使在非正式场合也会保持一丝不苟。
