re-phrasing:/ˌriːˈfeɪzɪŋ/| n. |重新措辞；改述；重新表达|The act of expressing something again in different words, often to clarify, improve, or adjust the meaning.  It involves restating ideas, sentences, or paragraphs to achieve better clarity, conciseness, or impact. |Example:  The teacher suggested a re-phrasing of the essay to improve its clarity.  老师建议修改作文以提高清晰度。|近义词：rewording, paraphrasing, reformulation|反义词：leaving unchanged, maintaining original wording|名词


USAGE->

The committee is re-phrasing the proposal. = 委员会正在修改提案。
He re-phrases his answers to sound more professional. = 他重新组织他的答案，听起来更专业。


一般过去时:
She re-phrased her question to make it clearer. = 她重新表达了她的问题，使其更清晰。
