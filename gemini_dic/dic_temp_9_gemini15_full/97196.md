inflames: /ɪnˈfleɪmz/ | vt. | 使发炎；激怒；煽动 | To cause (a part of the body) to become inflamed; to arouse strong feelings in (someone), especially anger or excitement; to incite or stir up (an undesirable feeling or situation).|

例句：

The infection inflamed his leg. = 感染使他的腿发炎。

His words inflamed the already tense situation. = 他的话使本已紧张的局势更加恶化。


近义词：irritate, aggravate, enrage, incite, exacerbate

反义词：soothe, calm, appease, pacify


USAGE->

The sun inflames his skin. = 太阳晒红了他的皮肤。

This issue inflames public opinion. = 这个问题激起了民愤。


一般过去时:

The wound inflamed quickly. = 伤口很快就发炎了。

现在完成时:

His actions have inflamed the conflict. = 他的行为加剧了冲突。


过去完成时:

The injury had inflamed before he sought medical attention. = 他就医前伤口就已经发炎了。

The media had inflamed the controversy before the facts were known. = 在事实真相大白之前，媒体已经煽动起了这场争议。

虚拟语气:

If you continued to scratch it, the wound would inflame. = 如果你继续抓挠它，伤口会发炎。

If he had spoken more carefully, he wouldn't have inflamed the audience. = 如果他说话更谨慎些，就不会激怒听众了。


被动语态:

His skin was inflamed by the sun. = 他的皮肤被太阳晒红了。

The public was inflamed by the politician's lies. = 公众被这位政治家的谎言激怒了。


疑问句:

Does the cut inflame easily? = 这伤口容易发炎吗？

Will this decision inflame tensions between the two countries? = 这个决定会加剧两国之间的紧张关系吗？


否定句:

The cream prevented the wound from inflaming. = 这种药膏防止了伤口发炎。

His calm response did not inflame the situation. = 他冷静的回应并没有激化局势。

词源:

From Old French *enflammer, from en- +flamme "flame," from Latin flamma.  The sense development is from "to set on fire" to "to excite or arouse strongly".
