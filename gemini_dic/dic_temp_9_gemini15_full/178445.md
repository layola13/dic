reconsolidation: /ˌriːkənˌsɑːlɪˈdeɪʃən/ | n. | 再巩固；记忆再巩固 | The process by which a memory is retrieved and then stored again.  This process can make the memory more resistant to forgetting or distortion, but it also makes it vulnerable to alteration.  | Example:  The reconsolidation of traumatic memories is a key area of research in PTSD treatment.  记忆的再巩固是创伤后应激障碍治疗中的一个关键研究领域。


Example:  Studies show that reconsolidation can be disrupted, leading to changes in the memory.  研究表明，记忆的再巩固可以被破坏，从而导致记忆的变化。| 近义词：restoration, reinforcement | 反义词：decay, fading |  名词
