fore-fending: /fɔːrˈfɛndɪŋ/ | v. | 预防；保护；抵御 |  The act of preventing something bad from happening; warding off.  This word is less common and often used in a more literary or archaic context. It suggests a proactive and anticipatory defense. |  例句：The sturdy ship was fore-fending against the heavy waves.  这艘坚固的船只抵御着汹涌的波浪。


The villagers fore-fended against the approaching storm by boarding up their windows. 村民们通过封住窗户来抵御即将到来的风暴。| 近义词：preventing, guarding, protecting, warding off | 反义词：allowing, permitting, encouraging

词源:
The word "fore-fending" combines "fore-" (meaning "before" or "in advance") and "fend" (meaning "to ward off," "to defend," or "to provide for").  The "fore-" prefix emphasizes the proactive nature of the action.  The root "fend" has Old English origins.
