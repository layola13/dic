monotremes:/ˈmɒnətriːmz/| n.|单孔目动物|Monotremes are mammals that lay eggs instead of giving birth to live young. They are characterized by their single opening (cloaca) for excretion and reproduction.|Example: The echidna and platypus are examples of monotremes.|近义词: egg-laying mammals|反义词: placental mammals, marsupials
