jiber:/ˈdʒaɪbər/ | v. | 嘲笑；挖苦；戏弄 | To speak or shout derisively; to jeer.  |  例句：The children jibed at the clumsy clown. 孩子们嘲笑那个笨拙的小丑。
例句：Don't jibe at his efforts, even if they seem foolish. 不要嘲笑他的努力，即使它们看起来很愚蠢。| 近义词：jeer, taunt, mock, scoff | 反义词：praise, commend, compliment | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jibes at his classmates often. = 他经常嘲笑他的同学们。
They jibe at the opposing team. = 他们嘲笑对方球队。


一般过去时:
She jibed at his new haircut. = 她嘲笑他的新发型。
The boys jibed at the girl for her mistake. = 男孩们因为她的错误而嘲笑那个女孩。


现在完成时:
He has jibed at me several times today. = 他今天已经好几次嘲笑我了。
They have jibed at her singing for years. = 他们多年来一直嘲笑她的歌声。


过去完成时:
She had jibed at him before he even began to speak. = 他还没开始说话她就嘲笑他了。
They had jibed at her performance before the critics did. = 批评家们还没评论之前，他们就已经嘲笑她的表演了。


将来时:
He will jibe at her if she makes another mistake. = 如果她再犯错，他会嘲笑她。
They will jibe at him for his poor choice of words. = 他们会因为他的词语选择不当而嘲笑他。


虚拟语气:
If he were to jibe at her, she would ignore him. = 如果他嘲笑她，她会不理他。
I wish they hadn't jibed at my presentation. = 我希望他们没有嘲笑我的演示。


被动语态:
His efforts were jibed at by his peers. = 他的努力受到了同龄人的嘲笑。
Her singing was jibed at relentlessly. = 她的歌声受到了无情的嘲笑。


疑问句:
Did he jibe at you? = 他嘲笑你了吗？
Were they jibing at her performance? = 他们当时是在嘲笑她的表演吗？


否定句:
He didn't jibe at her, he just offered constructive criticism. = 他没有嘲笑她，他只是提出了建设性的批评。
They didn't jibe at his idea, instead they supported him. = 他们没有嘲笑他的主意，反而支持了他。


PHRASE->
jibe at someone = 嘲笑某人
jibe about something =  就某事嘲笑
jibe with someone = （与某人）意见一致 (This is a less common usage, often confused with "jibe" meaning "to agree" in nautical context)
to jibe and jeer = 嘲笑和讥讽
constant jibing =  不断的嘲讽


词源:
The word "jibe" in this sense comes from the Middle English word "gibe,"  ultimately of uncertain origin, possibly related to words meaning "to strike" or "to make a sound."  Its meaning evolved from a general sense of mockery or ridicule to its current usage.
