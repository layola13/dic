cadging:/ˈkædʒɪŋ/| n. v.|乞讨；骗取；蹭吃蹭喝|The act of obtaining something, especially money or food, by begging or by making persistent requests.  It can also imply a less direct approach, such as subtly trying to get something without paying. |例句：He was cadging cigarettes from his friends. 他一直在向朋友们讨香烟。

例句：She spent the afternoon cadging rides from strangers. 她整个下午都在向陌生人蹭车。


近义词：begging, sponging, scrounging, mooching


反义词：giving, donating, contributing


词源：源自中古英语 *caggen，意为“捡拾，收集”。其词源与“cage”无关。


USAGE->

He is always cadging money from his parents. = 他总是向父母要钱。
They are cadging food from their neighbors. = 他们正在向邻居们蹭吃。


一般过去时:
He cadged a cigarette from me. = 他向我讨了一根香烟。
She cadged a lift home from her colleague. = 她向同事蹭了一程回家。


现在完成时:
He has cadged several meals this week. = 他这个星期已经蹭了好几顿饭了。
She has cadged rides from various people. = 她已经向不同的人蹭过车了。


过去完成时:
He had cadged money from his friends before he bought the ticket. = 他在买票之前已经向朋友们要过钱了。
She had cadged a ride before her car broke down. = 在她的车坏掉之前，她已经蹭过车了。


将来时:
He will cadge a ride to the party. = 他会蹭车去参加派对。
She will cadge some money from her brother. = 她会向她哥哥要一些钱。


虚拟语气:

I wish I hadn't cadged that money from him. = 我真希望我没有向他讨要那笔钱。
