established:/ɪˈstæblɪʃt/ | adj. | 已建立的；确立的；公认的 |  Something that is established is accepted, recognized, or well-known. It can also refer to something that has been created or set up. |例句：The company has an established reputation for quality. 这家公司以其高质量享有盛誉。

例句：He is an established author with many bestsellers. 他是一位享有盛誉的作家，有很多畅销书。


近义词：founded, instituted, recognized, confirmed, proven | 反义词：new, unproven, nascent, fledgling


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company establishes new branches every year. = 这家公司每年都建立新的分公司。
This rule establishes a clear guideline for conduct. = 这条规则为行为设定了明确的准则。


一般过去时:
They established a new research center last year. = 他们去年建立了一个新的研究中心。
The treaty established peace between the two countries. = 该条约确立了两国之间的和平。


现在完成时:
The government has established a new policy on immigration. = 政府已经制定了一项新的移民政策。
Scientists have established a link between smoking and lung cancer. = 科学家已经证实了吸烟与肺癌之间的联系。


过去完成时:
Before the war, they had established a strong trading relationship. = 在战争爆发前，他们已经建立了牢固的贸易关系。
By the time he retired, he had established himself as a leading expert. = 到他退休时，他已经成为一名领先的专家。


将来时:
They will establish a new department next month. = 他们下个月将建立一个新的部门。
The company plans to establish a presence in the Asian market. = 公司计划进军亚洲市场。


虚拟语气:
If they established a better system, the problem would be solved. = 如果他们建立一个更好的系统，问题就能得到解决。
I wish they had established clear guidelines earlier. = 我希望他们早些制定明确的指导方针。


被动语态:
A new school was established in the village last year. = 去年在村里建立了一所新学校。
The rules were established to maintain order. = 这些规则是为了维持秩序而制定的。


疑问句:
Has the committee established its objectives yet? = 委员会是否已经确定了其目标？
When was the company established? = 这家公司是什么时候成立的？


否定句:
The company hasn't established a strong online presence. = 这家公司还没有建立强大的在线影响力。
They didn't establish the facts before making a decision. = 他们在做决定之前没有查明事实。


PHRASE->
establish a fact  = 确立事实
establish a business = 创建企业
establish a relationship = 建立关系
establish a reputation = 建立声誉
establish oneself = 确立地位


词源:
From Old French establir, from Latin stabilire "to make firm, settle," from stabilis "firm, stable," from stare "to stand."
