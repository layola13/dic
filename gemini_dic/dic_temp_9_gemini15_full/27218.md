callowy:/ˈkæləʊi/| adj.|皮肤粗糙的，起茧的，无情的|Having hardened skin, especially on the hands or feet, often due to repeated friction or pressure.  It can also describe a person as being callous and insensitive.|例句：His hands were callowy from years of manual labor. 他多年的体力劳动使他的手变得粗糙。

The constant friction of the rope left his hands callowy and sore. 绳索的不断摩擦使他的手变得粗糙疼痛。|近义词：calloused, horny, toughened|反义词：soft, tender, delicate

词源:
The word "callowy" is derived from "callous," which ultimately comes from the Latin word "callus," meaning "hard skin" or "thickened skin."  The suffix "-y" is added to form the adjective "callowy," meaning having the characteristics of callus.
