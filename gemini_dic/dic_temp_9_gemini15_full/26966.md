calcify:/ˈkælsɪfaɪ/| vt. |使钙化；使硬化 |To become hardened or solidified by the deposition of calcium salts.  The process of calcification often occurs in tissues and organs, leading to a loss of flexibility and functionality.  |例句：The artery began to calcify due to high cholesterol.  动脉由于高胆固醇开始钙化。
The prolonged inflammation caused the tissue to calcify. 长期炎症导致组织钙化。|近义词：harden, solidify, petrify |反义词：soften

词源:

From Latin *calcificare, from calx (genitive calcis) "lime, limestone," + -ficare "to make."  The word reflects the role of calcium salts in the calcification process.
