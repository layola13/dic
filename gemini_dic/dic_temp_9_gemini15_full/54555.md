dislocating: /ˈdɪsloʊkeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 脱臼的；使错位的；扰乱的 |  Describing something that causes dislocation or is in a state of dislocation; causing disruption or confusion. | 例句：The dislocating experience of moving to a new country left her feeling unsettled.  搬到一个新的国家，这种颠覆性的经历让她感到不安。


例句：The dislocating effect of the earthquake was evident in the cracked pavements and damaged buildings. 地震的破坏性影响在开裂的人行道和损坏的建筑物中显而易见。


近义词：disruptive, unsettling, disorienting, jarring


反义词：stabilizing, comforting, reassuring, orderly


词性：形容词 (participle)


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The news is dislocating the market. = 这则新闻正在扰乱市场。
This constant change is dislocating the team's workflow. =  持续不断的变化正在扰乱团队的工作流程。


一般过去时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
