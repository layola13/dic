orthopoda: /ɔːrˈθɒpədə/ | n. | 直足类动物 |  A class of arthropods characterized by having uniramous (single-branched) appendages, typically legs adapted for walking or running.  They are mostly terrestrial and include insects, myriapods (centipedes and millipedes), and hexapods (insects). | Example:  Insects are a large and diverse group within the  *orthopoda*.  昆虫是直足类动物中一个庞大而多样的类群。 | 近义词:  Arthropoda (broader term) | 反义词:  N/A (no direct antonym, but  crustaceans, chelicerates, etc. are contrasting groups within Arthropoda)
