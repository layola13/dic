cocultivation:/ˌkoʊkʌlˈteɪʃən/| n. |共培养；联合培养|The process of growing two or more different types of cells or organisms together in the same culture medium.  This technique is used in various fields, including cell biology, microbiology, and plant science to study cell-cell interactions, symbiotic relationships, and other biological processes.|例句：Researchers used cocultivation to study the interaction between bacteria and human immune cells. 研究人员使用共培养来研究细菌和人类免疫细胞之间的相互作用。|例句：The cocultivation of plant roots with mycorrhizal fungi enhanced nutrient uptake. 植物根系与菌根真菌的共培养增强了养分吸收。|近义词：joint cultivation, mixed culture|反义词：monoculture
