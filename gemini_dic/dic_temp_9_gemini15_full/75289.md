fronted:/ˈfrʌntɪd/ | adj. v. | 位于前面的；以…开头；面对；(语法)前置的 | Placed at the front; beginning with; facing; (grammar) placed before its usual position. |例句：The house fronted onto a busy street. 这栋房子正面朝向一条繁忙的街道。|近义词：leading, preceding, facing,  headlined|反义词：rear, back, trailing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The building fronts the park. = 这栋建筑物面向公园。
She fronts the company's marketing team. = 她领导公司的市场营销团队。


一般过去时:
The soldiers fronted the enemy. = 士兵们面对敌人。
The article fronted a shocking revelation. = 这篇文章以一个令人震惊的揭露开头。


现在完成时:
The project has fronted significant challenges. = 这个项目已经面临了重大的挑战。
They have fronted the band for years. = 他们多年来一直是乐队的台前人物。


过去完成时:
He had fronted the company before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，他领导过这家公司。
The report had fronted numerous inaccuracies. = 这份报告此前曾出现过许多不准确之处。


将来时:
The new building will front the main road. = 新建筑将面向主干道。
She will front the campaign. = 她将领导这场运动。


虚拟语气:
If the building fronted the sea, it would be worth more. = 如果这栋建筑面向大海，它会更有价值。
If she had fronted the project earlier, it might have been more successful. = 如果她早些领导这个项目，它可能会更成功。


被动语态:
The parade was fronted by the marching band. = 游行队伍由军乐队领队。
The argument was fronted by a series of flawed premises. = 论点以一系列有缺陷的前提为基础。


疑问句:
Does the house front the river? = 这房子面向河流吗？
Who fronted the band's new album? = 谁是乐队新专辑的台前人物？


否定句:
The building doesn't front the park. = 这栋建筑物不面向公园。
He didn't front the meeting. = 他没有主持会议。



PHRASE->
front line: 前线
front door: 前门
front page: 头版
front and center: 突出地，显著地
in front of: 在…前面

词源:
From Old French *fronter (to border, confront), from Latin frons (forehead, front).  The sense development is from the physical "front" to a metaphorical "leading" or "facing" position.
