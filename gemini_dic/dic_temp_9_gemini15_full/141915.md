ordinalism:/ɔːrˈdɪnəlɪzəm/ | n. |序数主义；等级制；等级论|The philosophical theory that emphasizes the importance of order and rank in reality and knowledge.  Ordinalism rejects nominalism and emphasizes the reality of relations, especially serial relations. It is often contrasted with cardinalism, which focuses on quantity. | Example:  The study explored the implications of ordinalism in social hierarchy.  序数主义在社会等级制度中的意义。|近义词：rankism, hierarchy, serialism |反义词：nominalism, cardinalism |名词
