diandria: /daɪˈændriə/ | n. | 两雄蕊植物 | A plant having two stamens.  This term is primarily used in botanical classification systems, referring to the number of stamens (male reproductive organs) present in a flower.  It is a descriptive term used to categorize plants based on their floral morphology. | Example:  Many species in the family Proteaceae are characterized by diandria.  许多山龙眼科的物种具有两雄蕊的特征。 | 近义词:  (lacks direct synonyms, but related terms include "two-stamened," "bistaminate") | 反义词: polyandria (many stamens),  monadelphous (stamens united), diadelphous (stamens in two bundles) | 名词
