noninherently:/ˌnɒnɪnˈhɪərəntli/ | adv. |非固有地，非本质地 | Not inherently; not as an essential characteristic.  This adverb describes something that is not a fundamental or natural part of something else. It emphasizes that a quality or characteristic is acquired or added, not intrinsic. | Example: The danger is not noninherently present in the technology itself, but rather in how it is used.  这种危险并非技术本身固有，而是取决于它的使用方法。 |近义词:extrinsically, accidentally, incidentally |反义词:inherently, intrinsically, essentially
