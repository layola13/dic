house-broken: /ˈhaʊsˌbroʊkən/ | adj. |已训练好大小便的；已驯服的；规矩的|

英文解析:  House-broken refers to an animal, usually a dog or cat, that has been trained to relieve itself outside or in a designated area rather than indoors. It can also be used figuratively to describe a person who is well-behaved and follows social rules.

例句:

Our puppy is finally house-broken. = 我们的幼犬终于训练好大小便了。

He's a well-behaved child, completely house-broken. = 他是个行为良好的孩子，非常规矩。


近义词: trained, well-behaved, obedient, disciplined

反义词: untrained, unruly, undisciplined, ill-behaved


词性: adjective
