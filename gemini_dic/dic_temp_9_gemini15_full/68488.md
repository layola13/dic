famines:/ˈfæmɪnz/| n.|饥荒，灾荒|A widespread scarcity of food, caused by several factors such as war, inflation, crop failure, or population imbalance, resulting in widespread suffering and death.  |The Irish potato famine of the 1840s led to mass emigration.  爱尔兰19世纪40年代的土豆饥荒导致了大规模移民。  The current drought threatens to cause widespread famines across the region. 目前的干旱威胁着该地区将会发生大范围的饥荒。|starvation, dearth, drought|abundance, plenty|名词

词源:

The word "famine" originates from the Old French word "faim," meaning "hunger" or "famine."  This, in turn, comes from the Latin word "fames," also meaning "hunger" or "famine."
