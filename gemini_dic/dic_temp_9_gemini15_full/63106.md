enshrouding: /ɛnˈʃraʊdɪŋ/ | v. | 包裹；遮蔽；笼罩 |  The act of covering or surrounding something completely, often concealing or obscuring it.  It implies a sense of mystery or concealment. |例句：A thick fog was enshrouding the mountain.  浓雾笼罩着山峦。

例句：The ancient ruins were enshrouded in mystery.  古老的遗址笼罩着神秘的面纱。


近义词：covering, enveloping, concealing, veiling, shrouding


反义词：unveiling, revealing, exposing, disclosing


词性:动词 (现在分词)

词源:
The word "enshroud" comes from the Old English "scrūde," meaning "garment, covering." The prefix "en-" intensifies the meaning, signifying "to completely cover."  Thus, "enshrouding" means "completely covering or enveloping."
