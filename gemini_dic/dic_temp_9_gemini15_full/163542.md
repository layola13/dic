precorruptness:/ˌpriːkəˈrʌptnəs/| n. |预腐败；事前腐败；先期腐败 |The state or quality of being precorrupt;  the condition of being susceptible to or exhibiting signs of corruption before it is fully manifested.  It often refers to the actions or attitudes that precede overt acts of corruption, such as accepting questionable gifts or engaging in conflicts of interest. |The precorruptness of the officials was evident in their lavish lifestyles long before the scandal broke.  官员们在丑闻爆发前的奢华生活方式就表明了他们的预腐败行为。|近义词：susceptibility to corruption, predisposition to corruption, early signs of corruption|反义词：integrity, incorruptibility
