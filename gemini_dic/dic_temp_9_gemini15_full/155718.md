pholad:/ˈfoʊlæd/ | n. |凿船贝 |A marine bivalve mollusk that burrows into wood, rock, or clay.  Pholads are known for their ability to erode hard substrates using their powerful shells and specialized rasping structures. They are often found in intertidal zones and shallow waters. |例句：The pier pilings were riddled with the burrows of pholads.码头桩上布满了凿船贝的洞穴。
