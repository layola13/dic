incline:/ɪnˈklaɪn/| n. v.|倾斜；倾向；斜坡|

英文解析:As a noun, "incline" refers to a slope or a ramp. As a verb, it means to lean or bend, or to have a tendency or preference towards something.

例句:
The road inclines sharply to the right.  = 这条路向右急剧倾斜。

He inclined his head in greeting. = 他点头致意。

近义词:slope, slant, tilt, tend, lean, be prone to

反义词:straight, upright, vertical, level


词性:noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The road inclines sharply uphill. = 这条路向上急剧倾斜。
She inclines towards modern art. = 她倾向于现代艺术。

一般过去时:
He inclined his head in agreement. = 他点头表示同意。
The plane inclined to the left during takeoff. = 飞机起飞时向左倾斜。

现在完成时:
The evidence has inclined the jury towards a guilty verdict. = 证据已使陪审团倾向于有罪判决。
The tower has inclined slightly over the years. = 这座塔多年来略微倾斜。

过去完成时:
The tree had inclined towards the sun. = 这棵树已经向太阳倾斜。
He had inclined to believe her story. = 他曾倾向于相信她的故事。

将来时:
The tower will incline further with time. = 这座塔随着时间的推移会进一步倾斜。
She will incline to accept his offer. = 她将会倾向于接受他的提议。

虚拟语气:
If the ground inclined less steeply, the climb would be easier. = 如果地面倾斜度较小，攀登就会更容易。
I wish the building didn't incline so much. = 我希望这座建筑物不要倾斜得这么厉害。

被动语态:
The building is inclined at a dangerous angle. = 这座建筑物以危险的角度倾斜。
His opinion was inclined to be negative. = 他的意见倾向于消极。

疑问句:
Does the land incline steeply here? = 这儿的土地倾斜得很厉害吗？
Does she incline to agree with us? = 她倾向于同意我们吗？

否定句:
The road does not incline much. = 这条路不怎么倾斜。
He did not incline to violence. = 他并不倾向于暴力。


PHRASE->

词源:
From Old French encliner, from Latin inclinare "to bend, lean," from in- "in, into" + clinare "to bend," from PIE root *klei- "to bend."
