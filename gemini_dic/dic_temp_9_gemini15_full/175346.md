rake-offs: /ˈreɪkˌɔːfs/ | n. |回扣；分成；非法佣金 | A rake-off is a share of profits or money that is secretly given to someone, often illegally, in exchange for favors or assistance.  It often involves bribery or corruption. |例句：The politician was accused of taking rake-offs from construction companies.  这位政治家被指控收受建筑公司的回扣。

The mayor was investigated for rake-offs related to city contracts. 市长因与市政合同相关的回扣而接受调查。|近义词：kickback, bribe, payoff, backhander |反义词：legitimate payment, honest earnings |
