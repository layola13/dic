epithetically:/ˌɛpɪˈθɛtɪkli/| adv.|用称号地；以形容词的修辞手法|In a manner using epithets; descriptively using adjectives.|The poet described the hero epithetically, painting a vivid picture with carefully chosen words.|近义词：descriptively, figuratively|反义词：literally, factually


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It doesn't have tenses.  Its usage is consistent.

Example 1:
He referred to the king epithetically as "the wise." = 他称国王为“明智的”，使用了称号。

Example 2:
The painting was described epithetically in the catalogue. = 画册中对这幅画进行了形容词式的描述。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "epithetically".  Its usage is primarily within sentences describing how something is described.
