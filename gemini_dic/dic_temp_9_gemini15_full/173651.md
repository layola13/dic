quasi-irregular:/ˈkwɑːzaɪɪˈrɛɡjələr/ | adj. | 准不规则的，部分不规则的 |  Describes verbs or grammatical structures that exhibit some, but not all, characteristics of irregular verbs or patterns.  They show some irregularity in their conjugation or inflection, but not to the extent of fully irregular verbs.  | The verb "go" is irregular, while "drink" is quasi-irregular in its past tense form ("drank") differing from its base form. | 近义词: partially irregular, somewhat irregular | 反义词: regular, perfectly regular
