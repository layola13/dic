fair-traded:/ˈferˌtreɪdɪd/| adj.|公平贸易的|  Goods that are fair-traded are produced in a way that ensures fair prices and working conditions for producers, often in developing countries.  This typically involves certifications and standards that guarantee ethical sourcing and sustainable practices.|例句：Many consumers prefer to buy fair-traded coffee because they know the farmers are paid a living wage.  许多消费者更喜欢购买公平贸易咖啡，因为他们知道农民获得了生活工资。

例句：The chocolate bar is made with fair-traded cocoa beans, supporting sustainable farming practices. 这块巧克力棒是用公平贸易的可可豆制成的，支持可持续的农业实践。|近义词：ethically sourced, sustainably produced |反义词：exploitative, unfairly traded |词性:形容词
