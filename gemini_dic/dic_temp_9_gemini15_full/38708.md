condescend:/ˌkɒn.dɪˈsend/| /ˌkɑːn.dəˈsɛnd/ | adj. v.| 屈尊；俯就；故意贬低； снисходительный | To behave in a way that shows that you believe you are more important or intelligent than other people:  |例句：He condescended to answer my questions. 他屈尊回答了我的问题。

例句：She condescended to speak to us. 她屈尊跟我们说话。


近义词：deign, patronize, talk down to | 反义词：respect, elevate


词源：From Old French *condescendre, from Latin *condescendere "to yield, comply," from com- "together" + de- "down" + scandere "to climb."


用法说明：

condescend 作动词时，表示“屈尊；俯就；故意贬低”，通常带有轻蔑或傲慢的意味，暗示说话者认为自己优越于他人。  它强调的是说话者自认为地位或身份高于对方，所以才勉强去做某事。

condescend 作形容词时，意思是“屈尊的；俯就的”，同样带有轻蔑或傲慢的意味。


例句：

一般现在时:
He always condescended to his employees. = 他总是对他的员工摆出一副高高在上的样子。
She condescended to help the poor family. = 她屈尊纡贵地帮助了那个贫困的家庭。


一般过去时:
He condescended to explain the problem to me. = 他屈尊向我解释了这个问题。
She condescended to listen to his complaints. = 她勉强听了听他的抱怨。


现在完成时:
They have condescended to accept our invitation. = 他们屈尊接受了我们的邀请。
He has condescended to acknowledge my achievements. = 他勉强承认了我的成就。


过去完成时:
She had condescended to help him several times before. = 她以前曾多次屈尊帮助过他。
He had condescended to offer his assistance, but with a condescending tone. = 他曾屈尊提出帮助，但语气却很盛气凌人。


将来时:
She will condescend to answer our request. = 她将会屈尊回答我们的请求。
He will likely condescend to attend the meeting. = 他很可能会屈尊参加会议。


虚拟语气:
If he condescended to help, we would be very grateful. = 如果他肯帮忙，我们将不胜感激。
I wish he would condescend to give me some advice. = 我希望他能屈尊给我一些建议。


被动语态:
His request was condescended to by the manager. = 经理屈尊同意了他的请求。
Her offer was condescended to, albeit reluctantly. = 她的提议虽然勉强，但也得到了接受。


疑问句:
Would he condescend to attend the meeting? = 他会屈尊参加会议吗？
Will she condescend to explain her decision? = 她会屈尊解释她的决定吗？


否定句:
He did not condescend to speak to them. = 他不屑于和他们说话。
She refused to condescend to their level. = 她拒绝降低身份去迁就他们。


PHRASE->
condescend to explain = 屈尊解释
condescend to help = 屈尊帮助
condescend to answer = 屈尊回答
condescend to accept = 屈尊接受
condescending tone = 屈尊俯就的语气
