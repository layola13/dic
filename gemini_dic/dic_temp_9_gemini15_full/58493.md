duel:/ˈduːəl/| n., v.|决斗；竞争|A duel is a prearranged fight between two people, typically fought with swords or pistols.  It can also refer to a contest or struggle between two people or things.|例句：They fought a duel at dawn. 他们在黎明时分决斗了。

例句：The two companies are engaged in a fierce duel for market share. 这两家公司正在进行激烈的市场份额争夺战。|近义词：fight, contest, combat|反义词：cooperation, collaboration|名词，动词

词源:
The word "duel" comes from the Old French word "duel," meaning "a fight between two people," which in turn derives from the Latin word "duellum," a diminutive of "bellum," meaning "war."  The diminutive suggests a smaller, more contained conflict than a full-scale war.
