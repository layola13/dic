misleads:/ˌmɪsˈliːdz/| vt.| 误导，使误解 |To give someone the wrong idea or impression, causing them to believe something that is untrue.  |例句：The advertisement misleads consumers into believing the product is more effective than it actually is.  广告误导消费者，让他们相信该产品比实际效果更有效。

例句：His ambiguous statement misled many people into supporting the wrong candidate. 他模棱两可的声明误导了许多人支持了错误的候选人。


近义词：deceive, confuse, delude, mislead, betray

反义词：enlighten, clarify, inform, guide


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The advertisement misleads consumers. = 广告误导消费者。
He often misleads his friends with his jokes. = 他经常用笑话误导他的朋友们。


一般过去时:
The map misled us. = 地图误导了我们。
The politician misled the public about his past. = 这位政治家对他的过去误导了公众。


现在完成时:
The rumors have misled many people. = 谣言已经误导了许多人。
He has misled his investors for years. = 多年来，他一直在误导他的投资者。


过去完成时:
I realized that I had been misled. = 我意识到我被误导了。
They had misled the investigators for months. = 他们已经误导调查人员数月了。


将来时:
This information will mislead many people if it is released. = 如果发布这条信息，将会误导许多人。
I fear that the instructions will mislead the users. = 我担心这些说明会误导用户。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't been misled, I would have made a different decision. = 如果我没有被误导，我会做出不同的决定。
I wish I hadn't been misled by his promises. = 我真希望我没有被他那些承诺所误导。


被动语态:
Many people were misled by the false advertising. = 许多人被虚假广告误导了。
The students were misled by the teacher's explanation. = 学生们被老师的解释误导了。


疑问句:
Was he misled by the news report? = 他是被新闻报道误导了吗？
Were the investors misled by the company's financial statements? = 投资者是被公司的财务报表误导了吗？


否定句:
The instructions did not mislead me. = 说明书没有误导我。
I was careful not to be misled. = 我很小心，以免被误导。


PHRASE->
mislead someone into doing something = 误导某人做某事
be misled by = 被…误导
mislead someone about something = 在某事上误导某人
grossly mislead = 严重误导
intentionally mislead = 故意误导

词源:
mis- (错误的，坏的) + lead (引导)  The prefix "mis-" indicates a wrong or bad action, modifying the verb "lead" to mean to guide someone wrongly.
