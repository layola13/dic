declamation:/ˌdekləˈmeɪʃən/ | n. |雄辩；朗诵；慷慨激昂的演说 |The act of declaiming; a rhetorical speech delivered with studied emphasis and gestures.  It often involves passionate and expressive delivery. |Example:  His declamation of the poem was truly moving.  他的诗歌朗诵非常感人。

The student's declamation won first prize in the competition.  这位学生的朗诵在比赛中获得一等奖。|近义词：oration, recitation, speech |反义词：mumbling, whispering


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The actor declaims the lines with great passion. = 演员激情洋溢地朗诵台词。
He often declaims his political views. = 他经常慷慨激昂地表达他的政治观点。


一般过去时:
She declaimed the poem beautifully at the school event. = 她在学校活动上优美地朗诵了这首诗。
The orator declaimed against injustice. = 演说家慷慨激昂地谴责不公正。


现在完成时:
He has declaimed this speech many times. = 他已经多次发表过这个演讲。
She has never declaimed in public before. = 她以前从未在公众场合发表过演讲。


过去完成时:
They had declaimed their grievances before the king arrived. = 在国王到达之前，他们已经表达了他们的不满。
The protestors had declaimed against the government's policies for weeks. = 抗议者们已经连续几周谴责政府的政策。


将来时:
She will declaim her poem at the poetry slam. = 她将在诗歌朗诵会上朗诵她的诗歌。
He will declaim his views on the matter during the debate. = 他将在辩论中阐述他对这件事的看法。


虚拟语气:
If he declaimed with more feeling, the audience would be more engaged. = 如果他朗诵得更有感情，观众会更投入。
I wish I could declaim as eloquently as he does. = 我希望我能像他一样雄辩地朗诵。


被动语态:
The poem was declaimed with great skill. = 这首诗被技巧娴熟地朗诵出来。
His views were declaimed loudly by the opposition. = 他的观点被反对派大声地谴责。


疑问句:
Will he declaim the speech tomorrow? = 他明天会发表演讲吗？
Has she ever declaimed in front of such a large crowd? = 她曾在如此庞大的群体面前发表演讲吗？


否定句:
He did not declaim his views at the meeting. = 他在会议上没有表达自己的观点。
She will not declaim the poem unless she is well-prepared. = 除非她做好充分准备，否则她不会朗诵这首诗。


PHRASE->

a powerful declamation = 一次有力的演说
a passionate declamation = 一次充满激情的演说
a forceful declamation = 一次强有力的演说
a dramatic declamation = 一次戏剧性的演说
a memorized declamation = 一次背诵的演说


WORD ORIGIN->

From Late Latin *declamatio, from Latin declamare "to declaim," from de- "down, thoroughly" + clamare "to cry out, shout."  The word reflects the forceful and expressive nature of the act of declamation.
