nonsaturation: /ˌnɒnsætʃəˈreɪʃən/ | n. |非饱和，不饱和状态 | The state of not being saturated; lacking complete saturation.  This term often appears in chemistry and physics contexts, referring to a substance or solution that does not contain the maximum amount of a solute or component that it can dissolve or absorb under given conditions.  It can also be used more broadly to describe a lack of fullness or completeness. | Example: The nonsaturation of the soil led to poor crop yields.  土壤的不饱和导致作物产量低。 The nonsaturation of the market indicates there's room for more competitors. 市场的不饱和表明还有更多竞争者的空间。| 近义词: undersaturation, incompleteness | 反义词: saturation, completeness |  名词
