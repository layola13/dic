ebionitism:/ˈiːbaɪənɪtɪzəm/| n. |早期基督教派别埃比俄尼派|A form of early Christianity characterized by its adherence to Jewish practices and beliefs, often considered heretical by mainstream Christianity.  Ebionites believed Jesus was the Messiah but rejected the divinity of Christ. They emphasized the observance of Jewish law and rejected Paul's teachings. |The Ebionites were a significant early Christian sect, whose beliefs and practices differed significantly from those that eventually came to dominate Christianity.|近义词：early Christianity, Jewish Christianity|反义词：orthodox Christianity
