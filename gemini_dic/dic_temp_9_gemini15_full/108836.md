leaving:/ˈliːvɪŋ/ | v. |离开；留下；让；交付 |The act of departing from a place or situation.  The state of being left behind or remaining. |例句：He is leaving for London tomorrow. 他明天要去伦敦。
She left her keys on the table. 她把钥匙留在桌上了。|近义词：departing, going, abandoning |反义词：arriving, returning, staying |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He leaves for work at 7 am every day. 他每天早上7点去上班。
She leaves the lights on all night. 她整夜都开着灯。

一般过去时:
We left the party early. 我们早早离开了派对。
They left their luggage at the hotel. 他们把行李留在酒店了。

现在完成时:
He has left for the airport. 他已经去机场了。
She has left her mark on the world. 她给世界留下了自己的印记。

过去完成时:
I had left the office before the storm hit.暴风雨来之前我已经离开了办公室。
They had left the building before the fire alarm went off. 火灾警报响起之前他们已经离开了大楼。

将来时:
I will be leaving for vacation next week. 我下周就要去度假了。
They will have left by the time we arrive. 我们到达的时候他们已经离开了。

虚拟语气:
If I were leaving tomorrow, I would tell you. 如果我明天要走，我会告诉你的。
I wish I were leaving now. 我希望我现在就能离开。

被动语态:
The house was left empty. 房子空着。
The meeting was left unfinished. 会议没有完成。


疑问句:
Is he leaving soon? 他很快就要离开了吗？
When are you leaving? 你什么时候离开？

否定句:
He isn't leaving yet. 他还没离开。
She didn't leave any message. 她没有留下任何信息。


PHRASE->
leaving aside = 不考虑；撇开
leaving out = 省略；遗漏
leaving behind = 留下；遗留
leaving off = 停止
leaving it to = 交给…


词源:
Leaving originates from the Old English verb *lēfan*, meaning "to remain, stay behind," which also gave rise to the modern English verb "leave."  The meaning evolved to encompass the sense of departure, with the person departing leaving something or someone behind.
