indeterminate: /ɪnˈdɪtɜːrmɪnət/ | adj. | 不确定的；未决定的；无限的 | Uncertain; not precisely determined; indefinite.  The adjective "indeterminate" describes something that is not clearly defined or fixed; it lacks precision or certainty.  It can refer to a quantity, a period of time, a situation, or an outcome.


例句：

The future remains indeterminate.  = 未来依然不确定。

The treaty's effects are still indeterminate. = 条约的影响仍然不确定。


近义词：uncertain, vague, ambiguous, indefinite, unresolved, unspecified


反义词：certain, definite, specific, precise, determined


词性：形容词


词源：

The word "indeterminate" comes from the Latin prefix "in-" (meaning "not"), "determinare" (meaning "to determine, to bound, to limit"), and the suffix "-ate" (forming adjectives).  Therefore, it literally means "not determined" or "not bounded."
