sickly:/ˈsɪkli/| adj.|病态的；虚弱的；令人作呕的；甜腻的；不健康的 | Often used to describe someone who is weak and often ill, or something that is excessively sweet or sentimental.  It can also describe something that is unpleasant or unhealthy. |例句：The child looked sickly and pale.  那个孩子看起来病怏怏的，脸色苍白。


The flowers had a sickly sweet smell. 那些花散发着一种令人作呕的甜味。|近义词：weak, frail, delicate, unhealthy, cloying, nauseating |反义词：healthy, robust, strong, vigorous

词源:
The word "sickly" is derived from the Old English word "seoclic," which means "sickly," "weak," or "ailing."  It's a combination of "seoc" (sick) and a suffix "-lic" (like, resembling).  The modern spelling and usage evolved over time through Middle English.
