disquiets:/dɪsˈkwaɪəts/ | vt. | 使不安；使担忧 | To make someone feel worried or nervous.  | The news disquieted her greatly. 这则新闻让她非常不安。The uncertain future disquieted many people. 不确定的未来让许多人感到不安。|  trouble, worry, unsettle | calm, reassure | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news disquiets her. = 这则新闻让她不安。
His behavior disquiets his parents. = 他的行为让他的父母不安。


一般过去时:
The loud noise disquieted the baby. = 响亮的噪音让婴儿不安。
The unexpected results disquieted the researchers. = 出乎意料的结果让研究人员不安。


现在完成时:
The constant criticism has disquieted him. = 持续的批评让他不安。
Recent events have disquieted the market. = 最近的事件扰乱了市场。


过去完成时:
He had been disquieted by the strange letter. = 他一直被那封奇怪的信弄得不安。
She had disquieted herself unnecessarily. = 她不必要地让自己不安。


将来时:
The impending storm will disquiet the villagers. = 即将来临的风暴将使村民不安。
The announcement will disquiet many investors. = 这则公告将使许多投资者不安。


虚拟语气:

I wish that the news wouldn't disquiet my mother. = 我希望这个消息不会让我母亲不安。


被动语态:
He was disquieted by the news. = 他被这个消息弄得不安。
The public was disquieted by the government's actions. = 公众对政府的行动感到不安。


疑问句:
Does the thought of the exam disquiet you? = 考试的想法让你不安吗？
What disquiets you so much? = 什么事让你如此不安？


否定句:
The news did not disquiet him. = 这个消息没有让他不安。
Nothing disquieted her calm demeanor. = 没有什么能扰乱她平静的举止。


PHRASE->
be disquieted by = 被…弄得不安

seriously disquieted = 严重不安


词源:
The word "disquiet" comes from the Old French word "desquietir," which means "to disturb" or "to unsettle."  The prefix "dis-" adds a negative or opposite sense, intensifying the meaning of "quiet."  Therefore, "disquiet" literally means "to un-quiet" or "to disturb the peace."
