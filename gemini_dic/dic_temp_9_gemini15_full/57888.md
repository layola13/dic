drift:/drɪft/| n., v.|漂流；漂移；逐渐变化；趋势|

英文解析:
As a noun, "drift" refers to the act of being carried along by a current of water or air, or a gradual, unintentional change in direction or course. As a verb, it means to be carried along by a current, to move slowly and aimlessly, or to gradually change or develop in a particular direction.


例句:
The boat drifted slowly out to sea.  = 小船慢慢地漂流到海上。

The conversation drifted towards politics. =  谈话逐渐转向政治。


近义词:
float, wander, meander, trend, tendency


反义词:
stay put, remain stationary, remain unchanged


词性:
Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The leaves drift on the wind. = 叶子随风飘动。
The snow drifts down gently. = 雪轻轻地飘落。


一般过去时:
The ship drifted for days. = 船漂流了好几天。
The argument drifted into a shouting match. = 争论演变成一场喊叫。


现在完成时:
The boat has drifted miles from shore. = 船已经漂流到离岸数英里的地方。
The country has drifted towards authoritarianism. = 这个国家逐渐走向专制。


过去完成时:
The raft had drifted far out to sea before they were rescued. = 木筏在他们获救之前已经漂流到很远的海上。
The project had drifted off course due to lack of funding. = 由于缺乏资金，项目已经偏离了轨道。


将来时:
The ice floe will drift southward. = 浮冰将会漂向南方。
The company's market share will likely drift downwards. = 公司的市场份额可能会下降。


虚拟语气:
If the wind had been stronger, the boat would have drifted faster. = 如果风更大一些，船就会漂得更快。
If he had followed his original plan, his career wouldn't have drifted. = 如果他遵循最初的计划，他的事业就不会一蹶不振。


被动语态:
The boat was drifted by the current. = 船被水流冲走了。（注意：此被动语态用法较少见，更常用主动语态）
The conversation was drifted towards a more serious topic. = 谈话转向了更严肃的话题。（注意：此被动语态用法较少见，更常用主动语态）


疑问句:
Where did the balloon drift to? = 气球飘到哪里去了？
Which way is the river drifting? = 河水往哪个方向流？


否定句:
The boat didn't drift too far. = 船没有漂流太远。
The plan didn't drift away from its original goal. = 计划没有偏离其最初的目标。
