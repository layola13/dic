counter-coloured: /ˈkaʊntəˌkʌləd/ | adj. | 对比色调的，反色调的 | Having colors that contrast strongly.  This term describes a pattern where different parts of an organism have distinctly different colors, often for camouflage or warning.  The colors serve to break up the outline of the creature, making it harder to see against its background. |  例句：The butterfly's wings were beautifully counter-coloured, blending seamlessly with the surrounding foliage.  这只蝴蝶的翅膀颜色对比强烈，与周围的树叶完美融合。

The snake's counter-coloured pattern helped it hide amongst the rocks and shadows. 这条蛇的对比色图案帮助它隐藏在岩石和阴影中。| 近义词：contrasting, variegated,  bicoloured | 反义词：monochromatic, uniformly colored |
