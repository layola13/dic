quasi-alternatingly:/ˈkwɑːzi ˈɔːltəneɪtɪŋli/| adv. | 准交替地；近似交替地 |  Describes a process or sequence that is almost, but not perfectly, alternating. It suggests a pattern that approximates alternation but may have occasional deviations or irregularities. | Example: The lights flickered quasi-alternatingly, sometimes brighter on one side, sometimes the other. 灯光准交替地闪烁，有时一边亮一些，有时另一边亮一些。| 近义词：alternately (with deviations), intermittently, irregularly | 反义词：regularly, consistently, uniformly


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and its usage is primarily descriptive. It doesn't conjugate like verbs or decline like nouns.  Its use is restricted to describing the manner in which something happens.  Therefore, tense, voice, mood, etc., are not applicable.


PHRASE->

There are no common set phrases directly using "quasi-alternatingly". Its use is generally within a descriptive sentence structure.  For example:

The machine operated quasi-alternatingly.
The data points appeared quasi-alternatingly on the graph.
The birds sang quasi-alternatingly, with occasional pauses.
The lights flickered quasi-alternatingly.
The pattern was quasi-alternatingly arranged.
