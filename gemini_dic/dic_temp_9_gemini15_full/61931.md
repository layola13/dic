emulators:/ˈɛmjuleɪtərz/| n.|模拟器|A program that imitates the functions of a different system, allowing one system to run software or applications designed for another.| 例句：Many gamers use emulators to play classic video games on their modern computers. 许多游戏玩家使用模拟器在现代电脑上玩经典电子游戏。

例句：Android emulators allow developers to test their apps on various devices without needing physical hardware. 安卓模拟器允许开发者在各种设备上测试他们的应用程序，而无需使用物理硬件。


近义词：simulators, virtual machines|反义词：None|词性：名词


词源:  The word "emulator" comes from the Latin verb "emulari," meaning "to strive to equal or excel,"  "to imitate," or "to rival."  The suffix "-ator" forms nouns denoting an agent or instrument.  Therefore, an emulator is something that imitates or rivals another system.
