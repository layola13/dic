/ˌmɒnəˈlɪŋɡwəlɪzəm/ /ˌmɑːnəˈlɪŋɡwəlɪzəm/ | n. | 单语现象；只使用一种语言的现象 | Monolingualism refers to the use of only one language by an individual or a community.  It is the state of being monolingual.  This can be due to various factors, such as geographical isolation, limited exposure to other languages, or strong cultural preference for a single language.  |  The prevalence of monolingualism in the United States is often cited as a barrier to international understanding. | bilingualism, multilingualism | multilingualism | 名词
