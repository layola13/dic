danglers:/ˈdæŋɡlər/| n. |悬空词，悬垂修饰语|A dangler is a word or phrase that modifies a word not clearly stated in the sentence.  It creates grammatical ambiguity because the intended referent is missing or unclear. Danglers often appear as participial phrases or prepositional phrases at the beginning of a sentence.|例句：Walking to the store, the sun was shining brightly. (Incorrect - the sun wasn't walking)  Corrected: Walking to the store, I saw the sun shining brightly. |近义词：misplaced modifier, dangling modifier|反义词:  N/A
