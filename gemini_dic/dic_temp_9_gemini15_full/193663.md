self-chastisements:/ˌsɛlf ˈtʃæstɪzmənts/| n.|自我惩罚，自我责备|The act of punishing oneself, often mentally or emotionally, for perceived failings or mistakes.  It involves self-criticism and feelings of guilt or shame.|例句：Her self-chastisements over the missed deadline kept her awake at night.  她因为错过截止日期而自我责备，整晚都睡不着觉。|例句：He engaged in constant self-chastisements, believing himself unworthy of happiness. 他不断地自我惩罚，认为自己不配拥有幸福。|近义词：self-reproach, self-condemnation, self-flagellation, self-criticism|反义词：self-compassion, self-forgiveness, self-acceptance|名词

词源:

The word is a compound of "self-" (meaning oneself) and "chastisements" (the plural of "chastisement," meaning punishment).  "Chastisement" comes from the Old French "chastiement," ultimately deriving from the Latin "castigamentum," from "castigare" (to punish).
