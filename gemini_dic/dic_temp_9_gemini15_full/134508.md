nonlexical:/ˌnɒnˈlɛksɪkəl/| adj. |非词汇的；非词素的| Relating to or denoting elements of speech, such as intonation and stress, that are not represented by individual words.  These elements contribute to meaning but are not themselves words.  For example,  intonation can change the meaning of a sentence even without changing the words used.|The analysis revealed significant nonlexical features in the child's speech.|近义词：suprasegmental, prosodic |反义词：lexical
