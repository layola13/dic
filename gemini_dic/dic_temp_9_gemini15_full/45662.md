curtain:/ˈkɜːrtn/| n.|幕布，窗帘|A curtain is a piece of material, usually cloth, that hangs down to cover a window, doorway, or stage.|The curtain was drawn, revealing a breathtaking scene.  窗帘拉开了，展现出一幅令人叹为观止的景象。| drapes, blind, screen | open door | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The curtain hangs in the window. = 窗帘挂在窗户上。
The curtains match the wallpaper. = 窗帘和墙纸很搭。


一般过去时:
She closed the curtains before going to bed. = 她睡觉前拉上了窗帘。
He pulled the curtain across the stage. = 他拉上了舞台上的幕布。


现在完成时:
They have replaced the old curtains. = 他们已经换掉了旧窗帘。
We have drawn the curtains to block out the sunlight. = 我们拉上了窗帘挡住阳光。


过去完成时:
The play had already begun when the curtain rose. = 幕布升起时，戏剧已经开始了。
The audience had settled down before the curtain went up. = 在幕布升起之前，观众已经坐下了。


将来时:
I will close the curtains later. = 我晚点会拉上窗帘。
They will open the curtains for the performance. = 他们会在演出时拉开窗帘。


虚拟语气:
If I had a curtain, I would hang it up now. = 如果我有一块窗帘，我现在就会把它挂起来。
I wish the curtain were thicker to block out more noise. = 我希望窗帘更厚一些，能挡住更多噪音。


被动语态:
The curtains were drawn by the usher. = 窗帘是由引座员拉上的。
The curtain was suddenly raised. = 幕布突然升起。


疑问句:
Are the curtains clean? = 窗帘干净吗？
Should we close the curtains? = 我们应该拉上窗帘吗？


否定句:
The curtain doesn't reach the floor. = 窗帘没有垂到地板上。
They didn't open the curtains all day. = 他们一整天都没有拉开窗帘。


PHRASE->
