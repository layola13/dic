disassimilative: /ˌdɪsəˈsɪməleɪtɪv/ | adj. | 消同化的；异化的 |  Describing a process or change in which elements become less similar or different.  It often refers to linguistic changes where sounds become less alike, preventing the occurrence of identical sounds in adjacent syllables or morphemes.  | Example: The pronunciation shift is a disassimilative process, where the sounds /l/ and /r/ become differentiated.  消音变化是一个消同化过程，其中/l/和/r/的声音变得不同。| 近义词:differentiating, diversifying | 反义词:assimilative, unifying |  adj.
