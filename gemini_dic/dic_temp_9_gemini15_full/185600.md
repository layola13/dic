revendication:/ˌrɛvɛnˈdeɪʃən/| n. | 要求；索回；主张；恢复；平反 | The act of reclaiming or demanding something;  assertion of a right or claim.  It often implies a forceful or insistent demand, especially for something that has been unjustly taken or denied. |例句：The workers' revendication for better wages led to a strike. 工人们要求提高工资，导致了罢工。

例句：Her revendication of her stolen inheritance was successful after a long legal battle.  经过漫长的法律诉讼，她成功要回了被盗的遗产。


近义词：claim, demand, assertion, vindication, reclamation |反义词：concession, surrender, relinquishment |词性:名词

词源:
The word "revendication" comes from the Latin word "vindico," meaning "to claim," "to justify," or "to avenge."  The prefix "re-" adds the sense of "again" or "back," implying a reclaiming or re-assertion of something.  The word passed through Old French before entering English.
