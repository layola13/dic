preintentions:/ˌpriːɪnˈtɛnʃənz/| n. |预先打算，预先计划 |  A preintention is a plan or intention formed before a specific action or event.  It represents a mental state preceding conscious action, often involving unconscious or implicit biases or desires.  Unlike a conscious intention, a preintention might not be fully articulated or even acknowledged by the individual.  | Example:  Her preintentions to be helpful often led to unintended consequences.  她的好心预想常常导致意想不到的后果。  |近义词：forethought, predisposition, prior intention |反义词：impulse, spontaneity, afterthought |名词
