rhynchophora:/ˌrɪŋkəˈfɔːrə/| n. |吻喙类昆虫；象鼻虫类|The term "Rhynchophora" refers to a large superfamily of beetles characterized by their elongated snouts or rostrums.  These snouts house their mouthparts and are used for feeding, mating, and other functions.  They are incredibly diverse, encompassing a wide range of species with varying habits and appearances. Many are significant agricultural pests. | Example: The weevil, a member of the Rhynchophora, is known for its destructive feeding habits on crops.  象鼻虫，一种吻喙类昆虫，以其对农作物的破坏性取食习性而闻名。|近义词：Curculionidae (weevils) |反义词：无
