inflamers:/ɪnˈfleɪmərz/| n.|煽动者，激怒者|Someone or something that inflames or excites strong feelings, especially anger or violence.  |例句：The inflammatory speeches acted as inflamers, inciting the crowd to riot.  煽动性的演讲就像煽动者一样，煽动人群暴动。|例句：Hate speech online often serves as inflamers, fueling division and conflict. 网络上的仇恨言论通常充当煽动者，加剧分裂和冲突。|近义词：agitators, instigators, provocateurs |反义词：peacemakers, conciliators


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The politician's words inflame the public. = 这位政治家的言论激怒了公众。
These comments act as inflamers in the ongoing debate. = 这些评论在这个持续的辩论中充当煽动者的角色。


一般过去时:

The article served as an inflamer, causing widespread outrage. = 这篇文章充当了煽动者的角色，引起了广泛的愤怒。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

If only those comments hadn't acted as inflamers, the peace could have been preserved. = 如果那些评论没有充当煽动者的角色，和平本来是可以维持的。


被动语态:

The conflict was inflamed by several actors who acted as inflamers. = 冲突被几个充当煽动者的行为者激化了。


疑问句:
What inflamed the conflict? = 什么激化了这场冲突？

否定句:
His words did not inflame the audience. = 他的话并没有激怒听众。
The article did not act as an inflamer; instead, it promoted understanding. = 这篇文章并没有充当煽动者的角色；相反，它促进了理解。
