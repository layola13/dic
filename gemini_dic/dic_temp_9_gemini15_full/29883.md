causticities: /kɔːstɪˈsɪtiz/ | n. | 腐蚀性；苛刻；尖刻 | The plural form of causticity, referring to the quality of being caustic or corrosive, either literally or figuratively. It can describe the harshness of a substance that can burn or destroy organic tissue, or the biting, sarcastic, or severely critical nature of someone's remarks.  | Example: The causticities of the chemical spill were immediately apparent in the damaged vegetation.  The causticities of her criticisms left him feeling deeply wounded. | 近义词：corrosiveness, harshness, bitterness, acrimony, acerbity | 反义词：mildness, gentleness, kindness, sweetness
