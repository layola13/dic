met:/mɛt/| v.|遇见，相逢|To encounter or come across someone or something unexpectedly or by chance.  It's also the past tense of the verb "to meet".|例句：I met my friend at the mall yesterday. 我昨天在商场遇见了我的朋友。|例句：Have you met my sister? 你见过我姐姐吗？|近义词：encounter, run into, come across |反义词：part, separate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I meet my friends every week. = 我每周都见我的朋友们。
She meets with clients regularly. = 她定期会见客户。

一般过去时:
I met him at the party last night. = 我昨晚在派对上遇见了他。
They met for lunch yesterday. = 他们昨天一起吃午饭。

现在完成时:
I have met many interesting people in my life. = 我一生中遇到过许多有趣的人。
She has met the president. = 她见过总统。

过去完成时:
I had met him once before. = 我以前见过他一次。
They had met several times before the conference. = 会议之前他们见过好几次。

将来时:
I will meet you at the station tomorrow. = 我明天会在车站和你见面。
We will meet again soon. = 我们很快会再见。

虚拟语气:
If I had met her earlier, I would have asked her out. = 如果我早点遇到她，我会约她出去的。
I wish I had met him sooner. = 我希望我早点遇到他。

被动语态:
The requirements were met. = 要求得到了满足。
The challenge was met successfully. = 挑战成功地被克服了。

疑问句:
Have you met my parents? = 你见过我的父母吗？
Did you meet anyone interesting at the conference? = 你在会议上遇到什么有趣的人了吗？

否定句:
I haven't met her before. = 我以前没见过她。
They didn't meet their deadline. = 他们没有按时完成。


PHRASE->
meet the needs = 满足需求
meet the challenge = 面对挑战
meet the deadline = 按时完成
meet with success = 获得成功
meet one's fate = 迎接自己的命运
