planets:/ˈplænɪts/ | n. |行星 |Celestial bodies that orbit a star.  Planets are typically large enough to have become round due to their own gravity. They have cleared the neighborhood around their orbit. |Example:  The solar system contains eight planets.  太阳系包含八个行星。|近义词: celestial bodies |反义词: star, sun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The planets orbit the sun. = 行星绕太阳运行。
Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. = 木星是我们太阳系中最大的行星。

一般过去时:
Scientists discovered a new planet. = 科学家发现了一颗新的行星。
The planets were once thought to be the only celestial bodies. = 行星曾被认为是唯一的天体。

现在完成时:
Scientists have discovered many exoplanets. = 科学家们已经发现了许多系外行星。
We have learned a lot about the planets in our solar system. = 我们已经了解了很多关于我们太阳系中行星的知识。

过去完成时:
Before the telescope, people had only observed a few planets. = 在望远镜发明之前，人们只观测到几颗行星。
They had already mapped the orbits of the known planets before the mission. = 在任务开始之前，他们已经绘制了已知行星的轨道图。

将来时:
Scientists will continue to explore other planets. = 科学家们将继续探索其他行星。
We will likely find more planets in the future. = 我们将来可能会发现更多行星。

虚拟语气:
