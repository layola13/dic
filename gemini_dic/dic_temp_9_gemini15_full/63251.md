enters:/ˈɛntərz/ | v. |进入，参加，开始|To come or go into (a place).  To begin (an activity or period of time). To become a member or part of.|例句：He enters the room quietly. 他悄悄地走进房间。

The meeting enters its final stage. 会议进入最后阶段。|近义词：goes into, joins, commences, begins |反义词：exits, leaves, departs


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He enters the building every morning. 他每天早上都进入大楼。
She enters the competition every year. 她每年都参加比赛。


一般过去时:
They entered the contest last year. 他们去年参加了比赛。
He entered the room and sat down. 他走进房间坐了下来。


现在完成时:
She has entered three marathons this year. 她今年已经参加了三次马拉松比赛。
He has entered a new phase of his life. 他已经进入人生的新阶段。


过去完成时:
Before the show started, they had already entered the theatre. 演出开始前，他们已经进了剧院。
After he had entered the code, the door unlocked. 他输入密码后，门开了。


将来时:
I will enter the university next year. 我明年将进入大学。
They will enter the competition next month. 他们下个月将参加比赛。


虚拟语气:
If he entered the race, he might win. 如果他参加比赛，他可能会赢。
I wish I had entered the lottery. 我真希望我买了彩票。


被动语态:
The room was entered by a burglar. 房间被窃贼闯入了。
The new law will be entered into effect next week. 新法律将于下周生效。


疑问句:
Does he enter the building through the main door? 他是通过正门进入大楼的吗？
When does the competition enter its final round? 比赛什么时候进入决赛？


否定句:
He did not enter the competition. 他没有参加比赛。
She doesn't enter the room without knocking. 她不敲门就不进房间。


PHRASE->
enter into: 开始（谈判、讨论等）
enter upon: 开始（一段时期、一种活动等）
enter a plea: 提出请求
data entry: 数据录入
entertain: 款待；招待
