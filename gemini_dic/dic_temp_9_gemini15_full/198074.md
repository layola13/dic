shoguns:/ˈʃoʊɡən/| n.|幕府将军|A shogun (将гун) was the military dictator of Japan from 1192 to 1868.  The shogun held the real political power, even though the Emperor remained the symbolic head of state.  The shogunate was a period of feudal military government in Japan. |例句：The shogun ruled Japan with an iron fist. 幕府将军铁腕统治日本。|例句：The Tokugawa shogunate lasted for over 250 years. 德川幕府持续了超过250年。|近义词：military dictator, supreme commander|反义词：emperor (in terms of actual power), civilian leader
