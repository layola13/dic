irritate:/ˈɪrɪteɪt/| vt. |使恼怒；使烦躁；刺激；激怒 |to annoy someone, or to cause someone to feel angry or impatient|The constant noise irritated me.  不断的噪音让我很烦躁。|His remarks irritated many people in the audience. 他的话激怒了许多听众。|annoy, bother, vex |soothe, calm, please |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud music irritates me. = 吵闹的音乐让我心烦。
This rough fabric irritates my skin. = 这粗糙的布料刺激我的皮肤。


一般过去时:
The delay irritated the passengers. = 延误让乘客们很恼火。
His behavior irritated her greatly. = 他的行为让她非常生气。


现在完成时:
The constant interruptions have irritated me all day. =  不断的打断让我一整天都很烦躁。
His comments have irritated many people. = 他的评论激怒了许多人。


过去完成时:
The long wait had irritated him before the meeting even began. = 漫长的等待在他开会之前就已经让他很烦躁了。
She had been irritated by his constant complaining. = 她一直被他不断的抱怨所激怒。


将来时:
The traffic will irritate him if he is late again. = 如果他又迟到了，交通堵塞会让他很烦躁。
I think this new policy will irritate many customers. = 我认为这项新政策会激怒许多客户。


虚拟语气:
If the noise didn't irritate me, I would stay longer. = 如果噪音不让我烦躁，我会待久一点。
I wish this rash wouldn't irritate me so much. = 我希望这个皮疹不会这么刺激我。


被动语态:
He was irritated by her constant criticism. = 他被她不断的批评弄得很烦躁。
Many people were irritated by the change in policy. = 许多人对政策的改变感到恼火。


疑问句:
Does the smoke irritate your eyes? = 烟会不会刺激你的眼睛？
Does his attitude irritate you? = 他的态度让你生气吗？


否定句:
The soft music didn't irritate her. = 轻柔的音乐没有让她烦躁。
The change in plan didn't irritate him at all. = 计划的改变一点也没有让他生气。


PHRASE->
