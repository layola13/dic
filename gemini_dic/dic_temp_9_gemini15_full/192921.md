seawife: /ˈsiːwaɪf/ | n. | 海妻；水手妻子；海妖 | A seawife is traditionally a woman whose husband is a sailor, often implying a life of hardship and waiting for his return from sea.  The term can also refer to a mythical sea creature, similar to a mermaid, often depicted as dangerous and alluring. | Example:  She was a seawife, patiently awaiting her husband's return from his long voyage. = 她是一位海妻，耐心地等待着丈夫从漫长的航海中归来。  The legend spoke of a seawife who lured sailors to their doom with her enchanting song. = 传说中有一种海妖，用她迷人的歌声引诱水手走向死亡。 | 近义词: sailor's wife, mariner's wife (for the literal meaning); mermaid, siren (for the mythical meaning) | 反义词: landlubber's wife (for the literal meaning);  (no direct antonym for the mythical meaning)
