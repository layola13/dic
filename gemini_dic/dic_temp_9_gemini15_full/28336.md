caramelized:/ˈkærəmɛlaɪzd/ | adj. |焦糖化的；糖化的|  Describes food that has been browned and sweetened by the process of caramelization, where sugars are heated until they melt and turn brown.  |Example: The caramelized onions added a rich, sweet flavor to the soup.  焦糖洋葱为汤增添了浓郁的甜味。  The caramelized apples were a delicious topping for the ice cream. 焦糖苹果是冰淇淋的美味配料。|近义词：candied, browned, glazed |反义词：uncooked, raw

She caramelizes apples for her pie. = 她为她的馅饼焦糖化苹果。

词源:
From "caramel," ultimately from Spanish *caramelo*, from French *caramel*, possibly from Latin *calamellum* ("reed," or perhaps "sugar cane").  The -ized suffix indicates the process of making something into a caramel.
