pressurizing: /ˈprɛʃəraɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 加压；施压；使承受压力 |  The act of putting pressure on something or someone.  This can refer to increasing pressure in a physical system (like a container of gas) or applying psychological or social pressure to influence someone's actions or decisions. |


例句：

The engineers are pressurizing the fuel tanks before launch. = 工程师们在发射前正在给燃料箱加压。

The company is pressurizing its employees to meet unrealistic deadlines. = 公司正在向员工施压，要求他们完成不切实际的最后期限。


近义词：pressuring, stressing, coercing, forcing

反义词：releasing, relieving, supporting

词性：动词

词源:  The word "pressurizing" is derived from the verb "pressurize," which itself comes from the noun "pressure" + the suffix "-ize," meaning "to make or cause to be."  "Pressure" has roots in the Latin word "pressura," meaning "a pressing or squeezing."
