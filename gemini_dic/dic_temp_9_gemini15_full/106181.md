knocked:/nɒkt/ /nɑːkt/| vt.|敲打；撞击；击倒|To strike or hit something with force.  It can also refer to the sound made by such an action, or to the effect of such an action, such as causing someone to become unconscious.|例句：He knocked on the door. 他敲了敲门。

例句：The falling tree knocked down the power lines. 倒下的树木击倒了电线。


近义词：hit, struck, bumped, thumped|反义词：pushed, opened


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He knocks on the door every morning. 他每天早上都敲门。
She knocks over the vase accidentally. 她不小心碰倒了花瓶。


一般过去时:
I knocked on the door, but nobody answered. 我敲了门，但没有人应答。
The car knocked him to the ground. 汽车把他撞倒在地。


现在完成时:
The wind has knocked down several trees. 风刮倒了几棵树。
Someone has knocked over my milk.  有人打翻了我的牛奶。


过去完成时:
The thief had already knocked out the guard before escaping. 小偷逃脱前已经把守卫打昏了。
They had knocked down the old building before starting construction. 他们在开工前已经拆掉了旧建筑。


将来时:
The storm will likely knock down more trees. 暴风雨可能会刮倒更多的树木。
I will knock on the door politely. 我会礼貌地敲门。


虚拟语气:
If he had knocked louder, someone might have answered. 如果他敲得更响一点，可能有人会回答。
I wish I hadn't knocked over that glass of wine. 我真希望我没有打翻那杯葡萄酒。


被动语态:
The vase was knocked over by the cat. 花瓶被猫打翻了。
The door was knocked several times. 门被敲了好几次。


疑问句:
Did someone knock at the door? 有人敲门了吗？
What knocked that over? 是什么把那东西撞倒了？


否定句:
He didn't knock before entering. 他没有敲门就进来了。
She didn't knock the glass over intentionally. 她不是故意把杯子打翻的。


PHRASE->
knock down: 拆除
knock out: 打昏；淘汰
knock off: 下班；停止
knock over: 打翻
knock oneself out: 竭尽全力
