sheatfish: /ˈʃiːtˌfɪʃ/ | n. | 鲶鱼 | A bottom-dwelling, mostly nocturnal fish with long barbels around its mouth, belonging to the order Siluriformes.  They are known for their whisker-like barbels used to locate food in murky water. Many species are popular as food fish. | Example:  The fisherman caught a large sheatfish from the river.  渔夫从河里钓到一条大鲶鱼。
