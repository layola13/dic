counterpoints:/ˈkaʊntəpɔɪnts/ | n. | 对位点；对照；反驳；论点 | A counterpoint is a contrasting melody, idea, or element that provides a balance or contrast to another.  It can refer to music, argumentation, or design.  | Example: The simple melody was enhanced by a complex counterpoint.  这首简单的旋律被复杂的和声衬托得更加完美。  |近义词:contrast, opposition, foil | 反义词:harmony, agreement, concord | 名词

词源:
The word "counterpoint" comes from the Middle French *contrepoint*, combining "contre" (against) and "point" (point, note).  It originally referred to the musical technique of combining two or more independent melodies simultaneously.  The meaning has broadened to encompass any contrasting element.
