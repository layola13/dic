heterostylism:/ˌhɛtərəˈstaɪlɪzəm/| n. |异型花柱性|The presence of flowers with different styles (the part of the pistil that connects the stigma and ovary) within a population of the same species. This mechanism promotes cross-pollination by making it difficult or impossible for pollen from one flower to fertilize the stigma of another flower of the same style length.  It's a type of heteromorphism, specifically referring to differences in the lengths of styles and stamens. |Example:  Many species of primroses exhibit heterostylism, with some flowers having long styles and short stamens, and others having short styles and long stamens.  This ensures cross-pollination.  异型花柱性，例如报春花属的许多物种表现出异型花柱性，有些花具有长花柱和短雄蕊，而另一些花具有短花柱和长雄蕊。这确保了异花授粉。|近义词：heteromorphism, polymorphism (in a broader sense)|反义词：homostylism
