praefloration:/ˌpriːflɔːˈreɪʃən/| n. |花芽排列方式，花蕾的排列|The arrangement of floral parts in a flower bud before it opens.  This term is primarily used in botany and describes the relative positions of sepals, petals, and other floral organs within the unopened bud. Different types of praefloration, such as valvate, imbricate, and contorted, exist, each characterizing the unique pattern of overlapping or juxtaposition of floral parts.|Example: The praefloration of the tulip bud is imbricate, meaning that the petals overlap each other in a regular pattern.|近义词：floral arrangement, bud arrangement|反义词：无
