drools:/druːlz/| v.| 垂涎；流口水；|To drool means to let saliva flow from one's mouth, often because of sleepiness, illness, or the sight or smell of appealing food.  It can also be used figuratively to describe excessive enthusiasm or admiration.|例句：The baby drooled all over his bib.  婴儿把口水流了他围嘴上。

例句：He drooled over the pictures of the new car. 他垂涎于新车的照片。|近义词：slobber, dribble|反义词：无|词性：动词
