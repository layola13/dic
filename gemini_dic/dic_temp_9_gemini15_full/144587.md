outwave:/ˈaʊtweɪv/ | n. |涌出波,外波 |A wave that moves outward from a central point, often used in the context of electromagnetic radiation or other forms of energy.  It describes a wave that propagates away from its source. |例句：The outwave of the explosion sent shockwaves across the city. 爆炸的冲击波席卷了整个城市。|近义词：emanation, radiation, emission |反义词：inwave (less common, implies inward movement) |名词
