rebutter:/rɪˈbʌtər/| n. v.|反驳者；反驳|A rebutter is a person who rebuts, or a reply that refutes an argument.  It can also function as a verb, meaning to refute or disprove something. |例句：The lawyer presented a strong rebutter to the witness's testimony.  律师对证人的证词提出了强有力的反驳。


The judge listened carefully to the rebutter.  法官仔细倾听了反驳。

近义词：refuter, contradictor, opponent |反义词：supporter, endorser

词源:
The word "rebutter" comes from the Old French word "rebuter," meaning "to repulse, reject, or drive back."  This, in turn, derives from the Latin prefix "re-" (again) and "buttare" (to thrust, push).  Thus, the core meaning implies a forceful pushing back against something.
