equivocated:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪtɪd/ | v. |含糊其辞；模棱两可地说；支吾其词 | To use ambiguous language so as to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself.  |例句：He equivocated when asked about his involvement in the scandal.  他被问及卷入丑闻一事时含糊其辞。|例句：The politician equivocated on the issue, avoiding a direct answer.  这位政客对这个问题含糊其辞，避免直接回答。|近义词：prevaricate, hedge, waffle, obfuscate |反义词：clarify, elucidate, specify |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He equivocates to avoid answering difficult questions. = 他含糊其辞以避免回答难题。
She equivocates frequently in her speeches. = 她在演讲中经常含糊其辞。


一般过去时:
He equivocated during the interrogation. = 他在审讯中含糊其辞。
The witness equivocated under oath. = 证人在宣誓后含糊其辞。


现在完成时:
He has equivocated throughout the investigation. = 他在整个调查过程中都含糊其辞。
She has equivocated on this matter for years. = 她多年来一直在这个问题上含糊其辞。


过去完成时:
He had equivocated before finally admitting the truth. = 他在最终承认真相之前一直含糊其辞。
They had equivocated for so long that nobody believed them anymore. = 他们含糊其辞了这么久，以至于没有人再相信他们了。


将来时:
He will equivocate again if pressed for an answer. = 如果逼他回答，他还会再次含糊其辞。
She will likely equivocate when questioned about her past. = 当被问及她的过去时，她可能会含糊其辞。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't equivocated, the truth would have come out sooner. = 如果他没有含糊其辞，真相就会早点大白。
I wish he hadn't equivocated so much during the interview. = 我真希望他在采访中不要那么含糊其辞。


被动语态:
His involvement was equivocated upon by the media. = 媒体对他的参与含糊其辞。
The truth was cleverly equivocated by the spokesperson. = 发言人巧妙地含糊其辞了真相。


疑问句:
Did he equivocate during the testimony? = 他在证词中是否含糊其辞？
Why did she equivocate about her whereabouts? = 她为什么对自己的下落含糊其辞？


否定句:
He did not equivocate; he answered directly. = 他没有含糊其辞；他直接回答了。
She did not equivocate when asked about her intentions. = 当被问及她的意图时，她没有含糊其辞。


PHRASE->
equivocate on a matter = 对某事含糊其辞
equivocate about something = 对某事含糊其辞
deliberately equivocate =故意含糊其辞
to equivocate the truth =  含糊其辞真相
carefully equivocate = 小心谨慎地含糊其辞


词源:
From Middle French *équivoquer, from Latin aequus (“equal”) + vocāre (“to call”).  The original meaning referred to being called by two names, or having a double meaning.  The modern sense developed over time.
