/ˌoʊvərˈfriːkwənsiz/ | n. | 超频，过频 |  Overfrequencies refer to instances where a signal or system operates at a frequency higher than its intended or designed frequency. This can be intentional (e.g., overclocking a computer) or unintentional (e.g., due to interference or malfunction).  The term is often used in the context of radio frequencies, electronics, and signal processing.  | Example: The radio experienced overfrequencies due to atmospheric interference.  无线电由于大气干扰而出现超频现象。 The overclocking resulted in system overfrequencies, leading to instability. 超频导致系统超频，从而导致不稳定。 | 近义词: excessive frequencies, high frequencies | 反义词: underfrequencies, low frequencies | 名词
