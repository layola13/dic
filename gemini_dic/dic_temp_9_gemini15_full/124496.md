mouldy:/ˈmoʊldi/| adj. |发霉的，霉烂的 |Covered with or affected by mold.  The bread is mouldy and should be thrown away. 这面包发霉了，应该扔掉。|近义词：musty, mildewed, fusty |反义词：fresh, clean


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bread is mouldy. = 面包发霉了。
The basement smells mouldy. = 地下室闻起来有霉味。


一般过去时:
The cheese was mouldy. = 奶酪发霉了。
The fruit became mouldy after a week. = 水果一周后就发霉了。


现在完成时:
The clothes have become mouldy in the damp closet. = 这些衣服在潮湿的衣橱里发霉了。
The old book has gone mouldy. = 这本旧书发霉了。


过去完成时:
The food had gone mouldy before we could eat it. = 我们还没来得及吃，食物就已经发霉了。
The painting had become mouldy due to the leak in the roof. = 由于屋顶漏水，这幅画已经发霉了。


将来时:
The apples will be mouldy if we don't eat them soon. = 如果我们很快不吃掉这些苹果，它们就会发霉。
The leather will become mouldy in this humid weather. = 在潮湿的天气里，皮革会发霉。


虚拟语气:
If the bread weren't mouldy, I would eat it. = 如果面包没发霉，我会吃它。
I wish the shoes weren't mouldy. = 我真希望鞋子没发霉。


被动语态:
The food was found to be mouldy. = 发现食物发霉了。
The walls were left mouldy after the flood. = 水灾过后，墙壁上留下了霉斑。


疑问句:
Is the fruit mouldy? = 水果发霉了吗？
Are the books mouldy? = 书发霉了吗？


否定句:
The fruit isn't mouldy. = 水果没有发霉。
The bread is not mouldy. = 面包没有发霉。



PHRASE->
mouldy smell: 霉味

mouldy book: 发霉的书


词源:
From Middle English *moule, from Old French *mol, from Vulgar Latin *muldā, from Latin *muldō.  The word's evolution reflects the long history of human interaction with mold.
