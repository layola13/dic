calcination:/kælˈsɪneɪʃən/| n. |煅烧；焙烧|The process of heating a solid material to a high temperature, typically below its melting point, to bring about a physical or chemical change.  This often involves the removal of volatile substances like water or carbon dioxide. |例句：The calcination of limestone produces quicklime. 石灰石的煅烧产生生石灰。

例句：The high-temperature calcination process improved the material's reactivity. 高温煅烧过程提高了材料的反应活性。


近义词：roasting, firing, burning (in specific contexts) |反义词：None directly;  the opposite would involve processes like hydration or carbonation. |词性 n.
