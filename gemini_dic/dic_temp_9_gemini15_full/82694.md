guffaw:/ˈɡʌfɔː/| /ˈɡʌfɔː/| n., v.|  狂笑，哄堂大笑 | A loud, boisterous burst of laughter. |例句：A guffaw erupted from the audience as the comedian told his joke.  观众在喜剧演员讲笑话时爆发出阵阵哄堂大笑。

例句：He let out a hearty guffaw at the absurdity of the situation. 他对着这荒谬的处境放声大笑。


近义词：laugh, roar, chuckle, cackle | 反义词：sob, weep, cry

词源:
The word "guffaw" likely originates from a combination of onomatopoeia (imitating the sound of laughter) and possibly some older dialect words.  Its precise etymology is uncertain, but its imitative nature strongly suggests a connection to the sound of hearty laughter.
