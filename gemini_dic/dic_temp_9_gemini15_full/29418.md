catadrome:/ˈkætəˌdroʊm/ | n. |降河洄游的鱼类；降河洄游|A catadromous fish is a fish that migrates from freshwater to saltwater to breed.  Catadromy is the opposite of anadromy, where fish migrate from saltwater to freshwater to breed. |Example:  Eels are a well-known example of catadromous fish.|近义词：potadromous (for some species)|反义词：anadromous
