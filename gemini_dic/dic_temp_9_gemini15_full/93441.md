illuminatism: /ɪˌluːmɪˈneɪtɪzəm/ | n. |启蒙主义；光明主义|

英文解析:Illuminism refers to various philosophical and intellectual movements associated with the Enlightenment, particularly those emphasizing reason, individualism, and skepticism towards traditional authority.  The term can also be used, often pejoratively, to describe secret societies or conspiracies allegedly aiming to control the world through manipulation and deception.  The historical usage of the term is complex and nuanced, with different interpretations depending on context and perspective.

例句:
The Enlightenment was a period of great intellectual ferment, characterized by the rise of illuminism and its emphasis on reason. = 启蒙时代是一个伟大的思想活跃时期，其特点是启蒙主义及其对理性的强调。

Some historians argue that certain secret societies were influenced by, or even secretly part of, the broader movement of illuminism. = 一些历史学家认为，某些秘密社团受到更广泛的启蒙主义运动的影响，甚至秘密地参与其中。


近义词:Enlightenment, rationalism, freethinking

反义词:obscurantism, traditionalism, dogmatism


词性:名词
