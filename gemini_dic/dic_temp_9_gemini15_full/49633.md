demigroat:/ˈdɛmɪɡroʊt/| n. |半格罗特|A demigroat was a small English silver coin, half the value of a groat, worth two pence.  It ceased to be minted in the 16th century.  The term is now primarily of historical interest.|例句：The merchant accepted payment in both shillings and demigroats. = 商人接受先令和平格罗特作为付款。|近义词：halfpenny (in terms of value)|反义词：groat (double its value)
