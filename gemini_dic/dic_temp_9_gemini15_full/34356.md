classical:/ˈklæsɪkəl/ | adj. |古典的；经典的；传统的| Relating to the culture of classical antiquity, especially ancient Greece and Rome.  Characterized by qualities considered to be of the highest standard. |例句：He has a classical education. 他接受的是古典教育。

例句：The building is in the classical style. 这座建筑是古典风格的。


近义词：traditional, conventional, standard, orthodox | 反义词：modern, contemporary, avant-garde


词源:  From Late Latin *classicus (“belonging to the first class”), from Latin classis (“class,” “division,” “rank”). The original meaning referred to writers and artists of the highest rank, specifically those of ancient Greece and Rome.  The meaning then broadened to include anything considered to be of the highest quality or representing established standards of excellence.


用法说明:

The adjective "classical" has two main senses:

1. **Relating to classical antiquity:** This refers to the art, literature, philosophy, and culture of ancient Greece and Rome.  Examples include classical architecture, classical literature (like Homer's epics), and classical mythology.

2. **Of the highest quality or standard:**  This sense describes something considered to be exemplary or representing the best of its kind, often based on established traditions or principles.  This can apply to various fields, including music (classical music), art (classical painting), and literature (classical novels).


例句(按用法分类):

**用法一：指古希腊罗马的文化**

例句：He studied classical literature at university. 他在大学学习古典文学。

例句：The museum features a remarkable collection of classical sculptures.  博物馆展出了令人惊叹的古典雕塑收藏。


**用法二：指最高水准或标准**

例句：She prefers classical music to pop music. 她更喜欢古典音乐而不是流行音乐。

例句：His paintings are considered classical examples of Impressionism. 他的画作被认为是印象派绘画的经典范例。
