multiplet: /ˈmʌltɪplɛt/ | n. | 多重态；多重线；多峰信号 | A multiplet is a group of closely spaced spectral lines in a spectrum, arising from the interaction of nuclear spins in a molecule.  In NMR spectroscopy, it refers to a signal split into multiple peaks due to spin-spin coupling with neighboring nuclei.  In other contexts, it can refer to any system exhibiting multiple closely related components. | Example: The proton NMR spectrum showed a multiplet at 2.5 ppm due to the coupling of the protons on the adjacent carbon atom.  The hydrogen spectrum showed a complex multiplet. | 近义词: multiplet structure, cluster, group | 反义词: singlet (in spectroscopy)
