dish-antenna: /ˈdɪʃ ænˈtɛnə/ | n. | 碟形天线 | A dish antenna is a parabolic antenna that resembles a satellite dish. It's used to receive and transmit radio waves, particularly in satellite communication, broadcasting, and radio astronomy. The parabolic shape focuses the incoming radio waves onto a receiver located at the focal point.  | Example: The satellite TV company installed a large dish antenna on my roof.  卫星电视公司在我的屋顶上安装了一个大型碟形天线。 |近义词： parabolic antenna, satellite dish | 反义词： None (it's a type of antenna, not something with a direct opposite)

词源:
"Dish" refers to the shape of the antenna, resembling a dish.  "Antenna" comes from the Greek word "antennus," meaning "sail-yard," referring to the long, slender rod-like structures.  The use of "antenna" for radio reception evolved from the visual similarity to these structures.
