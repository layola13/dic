infuscate:/ɪnˈfʌskeɪt/| vt.|使模糊；使黯淡；使困惑|To make something unclear, obscure, or confusing; to darken or obscure.|The politician's speech infuscated the issue rather than clarifying it.  这位政治家的演讲使问题更加模糊，而不是澄清它。|  to obscure, obfuscate, cloud, bewilder, confuse | clarify, enlighten, illuminate, explain | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The fog infuscates the landscape. = 雾使风景模糊不清。
He tries to infuscate the truth. = 他试图掩盖真相。

一般过去时:
The smoke infuscated the air. = 烟雾使空气变得模糊。
The complex wording infuscated the meaning of the contract. = 复杂的措辞使合同的意思模糊不清。

现在完成时:
Years of neglect have infuscated the once-bright paintwork. = 多年的忽视使曾经明亮的油漆变得暗淡无光。
The scandal has infuscated the reputation of the company. = 丑闻玷污了公司的名誉。

过去完成时:
The heavy rain had infuscated the view from the window. = 大雨已经遮挡了窗外的景色。
They had infuscated the details of the plan to protect their interests. = 为了保护他们的利益，他们已经模糊了计划的细节。

将来时:

Further investigation will likely infuscate the already complex case. = 进一步调查可能会使已经很复杂的案件更加扑朔迷离。


虚拟语气:
If he continued to infuscate the facts, no one would believe him. = 如果他继续隐瞒事实，没有人会相信他。
I wish they hadn't infuscated the report with irrelevant details. = 我希望他们没有在报告中加入无关紧要的细节，使报告变得模糊。

被动语态:
The issue was infuscated by political maneuvering. = 这个问题被政治策略弄得模糊不清。
The truth was infuscated by deliberate misinformation. = 真相被蓄意的虚假信息所掩盖。

疑问句:
Does this policy infuscate the understanding of the process? = 这一政策是否使人们对这个过程的理解模糊不清？
What aspects of the debate were intentionally infuscated? = 辩论中哪些方面是被故意模糊处理的？

否定句:
The witness did not infuscate his testimony. = 证人没有模糊他的证词。
They attempted to infuscate the evidence, but failed. = 他们试图掩盖证据，但失败了。

词源:
From Latin *infuscare (to darken), from fuscus (dark brown, dusky).  The "in-" prefix adds a sense of intensifying or making something more of what the root word is.
