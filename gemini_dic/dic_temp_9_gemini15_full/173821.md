quasi-powerful:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈpaʊərfəl/| adj.|准强大的，似强大的|Approaching or resembling power; possessing some but not all the attributes of power.  It suggests a semblance of power, or power in a limited or specific context.  The term often implies a lack of genuine or complete power.|例句：The quasi-powerful CEO had influence but lacked real decision-making authority. 这位准强大的CEO有影响力，但缺乏真正的决策权。

The quasi-powerful nation exerted its influence through economic sanctions. 这个准强大的国家通过经济制裁来施加影响。|近义词：seemingly powerful, potentially powerful, influential, strong (in a limited sense)|反义词：powerless, weak, insignificant
