ord:/ɔːrd/ | /ɔːrd/ | n. |顺序；指令；圣职；命令；教令；法令|An ordinal number; a position in a numbered series.|The soldiers received their orders from the captain.|近义词：rank, sequence, command |反义词：chaos, disorder


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher calls the students in order. = 老师按顺序点名学生。
What order do you prefer? = 你喜欢什么顺序？


一般过去时:
He gave the order to attack. = 他下令进攻。
They followed the orders precisely. = 他们精确地执行了命令。


现在完成时:
The troops have received their orders. = 部队已经收到了命令。
We have completed the tasks in order of priority. = 我们已经按照优先顺序完成了任务。


过去完成时:
They had already given the order before the enemy arrived. = 敌人到达之前他们已经下达了命令。
She had placed the books in alphabetical order. = 她已经把书按字母顺序排列好了。


将来时:
He will give the order to proceed. = 他将下令继续前进。
We will arrange the items in numerical order. = 我们将把物品按数字顺序排列。


虚拟语气:
If I had given the order sooner, we would have won. = 如果我早点下令，我们就能赢了。
I wish I had known the correct order. = 我真希望我知道正确的顺序。


被动语态:
The orders were given clearly. = 命令清晰地传达下去了。
The books were arranged in chronological order. = 书籍按时间顺序排列。


疑问句:
What order should we follow? = 我们应该遵循什么顺序？
In what order should I put the ingredients? = 我应该按照什么顺序放配料？


否定句:
He didn't give the order on time. = 他没有按时下达命令。
The files were not in alphabetical order. = 文件没有按字母顺序排列。


PHRASE->
in order = 按顺序
out of order = 故障的；次序混乱的
in order to = 为了
word of order = 命令
order of magnitude =数量级
