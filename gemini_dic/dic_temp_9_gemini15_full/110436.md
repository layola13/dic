likely:/ˈlaɪkli/ | adj. | 可能的，大概的 | Likely means having a high probability of occurring or being true. It suggests a strong chance, but not a certainty.  | It's likely to rain tomorrow. = 明天可能会下雨。| The project is likely to succeed. = 这个项目很可能会成功。| probable, possible, plausible | unlikely, improbable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
It is likely that he will come. = 他很可能会来。
She is likely to be late. = 她很可能会迟到。


一般过去时:
It was likely that they had left. = 他们很可能已经离开了。
He was likely to win the race. = 他很可能赢得比赛。


现在完成时:
It's likely that they have finished the work. = 他们很可能已经完成了工作。
She is likely to have missed the bus. = 她很可能错过了公共汽车。


过去完成时:
It was likely that they had already eaten. = 他们很可能已经吃过了。
He was likely to have forgotten his keys. = 他很可能忘记带钥匙了。


将来时:
It is likely that it will snow tomorrow. = 明天很可能会下雪。
She is likely to be promoted next year. = 她很可能明年会被提升。


虚拟语气:
It is likely that he would have won if he had trained harder. = 如果他训练更努力，他很可能会赢。
It is likely that she would have accepted the offer. = 她很可能已经接受了这个提议。


被动语态:
The plan is likely to be approved. = 这个计划很可能会被批准。
The meeting is likely to be postponed. = 会议很可能会被推迟。


疑问句:
Is it likely to rain? = 会下雨吗？
Is she likely to succeed? = 她会成功吗？


否定句:
It's not likely to snow. = 不太可能会下雪。
He is not likely to agree. = 他不太可能同意。
