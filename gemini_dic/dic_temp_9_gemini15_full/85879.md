headborough:/ˈhedˌbʌrə/| n.|治安官，乡长|A headborough was a minor peace officer in England, historically responsible for keeping the peace within a specific area, often a village or township.  The role predates modern police forces.  Their duties included apprehending criminals and settling minor disputes.|Example: The headborough was responsible for maintaining order in the village.  乡长负责维持村庄的秩序。|近义词：constable, peace officer, village officer|反义词：criminal, lawbreaker|名词
