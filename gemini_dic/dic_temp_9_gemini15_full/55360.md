distancing:/ˈdɪstənsɪŋ/ | n. | 疏远；保持距离；拉开距离 | The act of creating or maintaining a physical or emotional space between oneself and others.  It can refer to physical distance to prevent the spread of illness, or emotional distance to protect oneself from harm or unwanted interactions. | Example: Social distancing is crucial to curb the spread of the virus. 社交距离对于控制病毒传播至关重要。  The couple's distancing was evident in their infrequent communication. 这对夫妇疏远的关系从他们不频繁的沟通中可以看出。 | 近义词：separation, remoteness, estrangement | 反义词：closeness, intimacy, proximity |


USAGE->

一般现在时:
Social distancing is important for public health. = 社交距离对公共健康很重要。
The increasing distancing between them worries me. = 他们之间日益疏远让我担心。


一般过去时:
The pandemic necessitated social distancing. = 大流行病使社交距离成为必要。
Their distancing began after a major disagreement. = 他们在发生重大分歧后开始疏远。


现在完成时:
We have practiced social distancing for months. = 我们已经进行了几个月的社交距离。
Their emotional distancing has damaged their relationship. = 他们的情感疏远损害了他们的关系。


过去完成时:
They had been distancing themselves from each other for years. = 他们多年来一直彼此疏远。
Before the argument, they hadn't shown any signs of distancing. = 在争吵之前，他们没有表现出任何疏远的迹象。


将来时:
We will continue social distancing until the virus is under control. = 在病毒得到控制之前，我们将继续保持社交距离。
I fear their distancing will only worsen in the future. = 我担心他们的疏远将来只会恶化。


虚拟语气:

I wish they hadn't distanced themselves so much. = 我真希望他们没有那么疏远。


被动语态:
