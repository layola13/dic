noncultivabilities: /ˌnɒnkʌltɪˈveɪbɪlətiz/ | n. |  不可培养性；难以培养性 | The inability of microorganisms to be cultivated in the laboratory.  This often refers to a situation where organisms are present but fail to grow under standard laboratory conditions.  This can be due to various factors including nutrient requirements, environmental sensitivities, or complex interactions within a microbial community. | Example: The study highlighted the challenges posed by the noncultivabilities of many environmental bacteria.  这项研究强调了许多环境细菌难以培养的挑战。  | 近义词：uncultivability, recalcitrance | 反义词：cultivability
