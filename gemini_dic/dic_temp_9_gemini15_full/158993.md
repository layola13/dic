plutons:/ˈpluːtɑːnz/ | n. |深成岩体，岩基 | A pluton is a large body of intrusive igneous rock that forms from cooled magma deep beneath the Earth's surface.  Plutons are characterized by their large size and often form batholiths, stocks, and laccoliths. | Example: The granite mountain range is composed of numerous plutons formed millions of years ago.  这座花岗岩山脉由数百万年前形成的许多深成岩体组成。|近义词：batholith, stock, laccolith |反义词：volcanic rock, extrusive rock |名词
