preidentification: /ˌpriːˌaɪdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 预先识别，预先鉴定 | The act of identifying something before it is fully known or understood.  It often involves making a preliminary assessment or judgment based on limited information. | Example: The preidentification of potential risks allowed the company to mitigate them effectively.  预先识别潜在风险使公司能够有效地减轻风险。


The police conducted a preidentification of the suspect from a blurry security camera image. 警方根据模糊的监控录像对嫌疑人进行了预先识别。 | 近义词：preliminary identification, advance identification, prior identification | 反义词：post-identification, subsequent identification
