edify:/ˈɛdəfaɪ/| vt.|教诲；启迪；熏陶|To instruct or improve (someone) morally or intellectually.  |例句：The teacher's lectures edified the students. 老师的讲座教诲了学生们。|例句：Reading good books can edify the mind. 阅读好书可以陶冶情操。|近义词：instruct, enlighten, improve, uplift|反义词：mislead, corrupt, debauch


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He edifies his students with interesting stories. = 他用有趣的故事来教诲他的学生们。
The teacher edifies the class with historical facts. = 老师用历史事实来启迪课堂。


一般过去时:
The sermon edified the congregation. = 布道教诲了会众。
The philosopher's writings edified many readers. = 哲学家的著作启迪了许多读者。


现在完成时:
This experience has edified me greatly. = 这段经历极大地教诲了我。
Reading this book has edified my understanding of the subject. = 阅读这本书提高了我对这个主题的理解。


过去完成时:
Having edified himself through years of study, he was ready to teach. = 通过多年的学习，他已经提升了自己，准备开始教学了。
She had edified her mind with various books before giving the speech. = 在发表演讲之前，她已经通过阅读各种书籍提升了自己的思想境界。

虚拟语气:
If I had edified myself more, I would have been better prepared. = 如果我当时更多地提升自己，我会准备得更好。
