generate:/ˈdʒɛnəreɪt/| vt.|产生；生成；引起；创造|To bring into existence; to produce or create something.|例句：The computer program generates random numbers.  这个电脑程序生成随机数。|例句：His speech generated a lot of excitement. 他的演讲引起了很大的兴奋。|近义词：produce, create, cause, make|反义词：destroy, eliminate, end


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The engine generates power. = 发动机产生动力。
The sun generates heat and light. = 太阳产生热和光。

一般过去时:
The storm generated high winds. = 暴风雨产生了强风。
The meeting generated several new ideas. = 会议产生了几个新想法。

现在完成时:
This company has generated significant profits this year. = 这家公司今年获得了可观的利润。
She has generated a lot of interest in her work. = 她对自己的工作产生了浓厚的兴趣。

过去完成时:
The drought had generated widespread food shortages. = 干旱已造成大范围的粮食短缺。
They had generated a lot of data before making a decision. = 他们在做决定之前已经生成了大量的数据。

将来时:
The new project will generate many job opportunities. = 新项目将创造许多就业机会。
We will generate a report based on the data. = 我们将根据数据生成一份报告。

虚拟语气:
If we invested more, we could generate more revenue. = 如果我们投资更多，我们可以创造更多收入。
I wish I could generate more ideas. = 我希望我能产生更多想法。

被动语态:
Electricity is generated by power plants. = 电力由发电厂产生。
New ideas were generated during the brainstorming session. = 在头脑风暴会议期间产生了新的想法。

疑问句:
How does the system generate reports? = 系统如何生成报告？
What factors generate consumer demand? = 哪些因素会产生消费者需求？

否定句:
The machine failed to generate the desired output. = 机器未能产生预期的输出。
The campaign did not generate enough support. = 这场运动没有获得足够的支持。
