psychophysics:/ˌsaɪkoʊˈfɪzɪks/ | n. |心理物理学|The branch of psychology that deals with the relationship between physical stimuli and subjective sensations.|Psychophysics studies how we perceive the world around us, focusing on the relationship between the physical properties of stimuli (like light, sound, or pressure) and our sensory experiences.  For example, it investigates how the intensity of a light affects our perception of its brightness. |近义词：sensory psychology, perception science |反义词：无
