declarations:/ˌdekləˈreɪʃənz/ | n. | 声明；宣告；申报 |  A formal statement or announcement.  Declarations can range from simple pronouncements to complex legal documents. | Example: The government issued a declaration of war.  政府发布了战争宣言。 | Example: She made a declaration of her love for him. 她宣告了她对他的爱。 |近义词：proclamations, pronouncements, announcements |反义词：secrecy, concealment |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company makes regular declarations of its profits. = 公司定期申报其利润。
He declares his income honestly every year. = 他每年都诚实地申报收入。

一般过去时:
The president declared a state of emergency. = 总统宣布进入紧急状态。
She declared her independence from her family. = 她宣布脱离家庭的束缚。

现在完成时:
They have declared their support for the candidate. = 他们已经宣布支持这位候选人。
The government has declared a public holiday. = 政府已经宣布放假一天。

过去完成时:
He had declared his intention to resign before the meeting. = 他在会议前已经宣布了辞职的意图。
She had declared bankruptcy several years ago. = 她几年前就宣布破产了。

将来时:
The court will declare the verdict next week. = 法院将在下周宣判。
They will declare their marriage publicly soon. = 他们很快就会公开宣布他们的婚姻。

虚拟语气:
If he declared his love, she might accept him. = 如果他表白，她可能会接受他。
I wish I had declared my feelings earlier. = 我真希望我早点表达我的感情。

被动语态:
The winner was declared at the end of the race. = 比赛结束后宣布了获胜者。
The law was declared unconstitutional. = 该法律被宣布违宪。

疑问句:
Has the company declared its dividends yet? = 公司是否已经宣布派息了？
Will the judge declare a mistrial? = 法官会宣布审判无效吗？

否定句:
He did not declare his assets to the tax authorities. = 他没有向税务机关申报他的资产。
The company has not yet declared a dividend for this year. = 公司尚未宣布今年的分红。
