carnification:/ˌkɑːrnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |肉质化；肉化；肉变|The process of becoming flesh-like or fleshy in texture.  This term is often used in medical contexts to describe the changes in tissue, especially lung tissue,  that result in a firm, red, and airless condition.  |例句：The autopsy revealed extensive carnification of the lungs, consistent with pneumonia.  尸检显示肺部广泛肉质化，与肺炎一致。|例句：The carnification of the affected tissue made it difficult to distinguish its original structure. 受影响组织的肉质化使得难以辨别其原始结构。|近义词：consolidation, induration, solidification |反义词：liquefaction, rarefaction

词源:
