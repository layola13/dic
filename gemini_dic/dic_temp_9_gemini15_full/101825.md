irritator:/ˈɪrɪteɪtər/| n. |刺激物；令人恼怒的人或事 | A thing or person that causes irritation or annoyance.  | The constant noise was a real irritator.  持续的噪音真让人烦。 | annoyance, nuisance, pest |calm, peace, solace|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant noise irritates me. = 持续的噪音让我烦躁。
That child is an irritator to his parents. = 那个孩子让他父母很烦。


一般过去时:
The loud music irritated her last night. = 昨晚大声的音乐让她很烦。
His behavior was an irritator that day. = 他那天表现得很令人恼火。


现在完成时:
His behavior has irritated me many times. = 他的行为已经让我多次感到恼火。
That loud music has been an irritator for weeks.  = 那吵闹的音乐已经烦了好几周了。


过去完成时:
The unfinished project had irritated him before the deadline. = 截止日期前未完成的项目让他感到恼火。
The constant complaints had been an irritator for months. = 持续的抱怨已经令人烦恼了好几个月了。


将来时:
The traffic will irritate me if I'm late for the meeting. = 如果我开会迟到，交通会让我很烦。
His attitude is likely to be an irritator in the future. = 他未来的态度可能会令人恼火。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't let that irritator bother me. = 如果我是你，我不会让那个令人恼火的事情烦扰我。
I wish that annoying noise wasn't such an irritator. = 我希望那个恼人的噪音不是那么令人烦躁。


被动语态:
He was irritated by the delay. = 他因为延误而感到恼火。
She was constantly irritated by her colleague's behavior. = 她经常被同事的行为激怒。


疑问句:
What is the biggest irritator in your life? = 你生活中最大的烦恼是什么？
Is that loud music an irritator for you? = 那种吵闹的音乐会让你感到烦躁吗？


否定句:
The quiet environment didn't irritate him. = 安静的环境没有让他感到烦躁。
The movie wasn't an irritator for her. = 那部电影并没有让她感到厌烦。


PHRASE->
major irritator: 主要刺激因素
pet irritator: 小烦恼，小刺激
chief irritator: 首要的令人恼火的事物
constant irritator: 持续的令人恼火的事物
primary irritator: 主要刺激物


词源:
irritator源自拉丁语动词irritare，意思是“激怒，刺激”。  它加上了“-tor”后缀，该后缀用于构成名词，表示“做某事的人或物”。
