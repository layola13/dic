neoconservatism:/ˌniːoʊkənˈsɜːrvətɪzəm/| n. |新保守主义|A political ideology characterized by a strong belief in military strength, a free market economy, and a cautious approach to social change.  Neoconservatives generally favor assertive foreign policy and a limited role for government in domestic affairs.  They often advocate for the promotion of democracy and free markets globally.|The rise of neoconservatism in the United States influenced foreign policy decisions in the early 2000s.  新保守主义在美国的兴起影响了21世纪初的对外政策决策。|conservatism, traditionalism|liberalism, progressivism|名词
