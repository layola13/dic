inleague: /ɪnˈliːɡ/ | adj. | 同盟的；勾结的 |  in league with sb. 指与某人狼狈为奸，暗中勾结；联合起来对付某人。通常指秘密的或不光彩的联盟。|例句：The two companies are in league to fix prices. 这两家公司串通一气抬高物价。


The police suspect that the two gangs are in league. 警方怀疑这两个帮派勾结在一起。

近义词：allied, united, confederate | 反义词：opposed, isolated, independent | 词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are in league against the government. = 他们联合起来反对政府。
The two companies are in league to control the market. = 这两家公司勾结起来控制市场。


一般过去时:
The rebels were in league with a foreign power. = 反叛者与外国势力勾结。
The two spies were in league against their own country. = 这两个间谍勾结起来反对自己的国家。


现在完成时:
They have been in league for many years. = 他们已经勾结多年了。
The evidence shows that they have been in league to defraud investors. = 证据表明他们长期以来串通一气欺骗投资者。


过去完成时:
It turned out that they had been in league before the scandal broke. = 事实证明，丑闻爆发之前他们就已经勾结了。
The investigation revealed that they had been in league to steal company secrets. = 调查显示他们曾串通一气窃取公司机密。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If they were in league, the consequences would be severe. = 如果他们串通一气，后果将不堪设想。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
They are not in league; they are simply cooperating. = 他们并没有勾结；他们只是在合作。
The investigation concluded that the suspects were not in league with each other. = 调查得出结论，嫌疑人之间并没有勾结。


PHRASE->
in league with: 与…勾结
go in league with:  与…联合
be in league against:  联合反对…

secret league: 秘密联盟

词源:  The phrase originates from the Middle English term "in league," which referred to a bond or alliance, often in a sporting or competitive context.  The modern usage, implying a clandestine or nefarious alliance, evolved over time.
