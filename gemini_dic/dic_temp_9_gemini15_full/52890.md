diminutives:/dɪˈmɪnjʊtɪvz/ | n. | 指小词，昵称，小东西 |  A diminutive is a shortened form of a name or word, often used to express affection or informality.  It can also refer to something small in size. | Examples:  "Mo" is a diminutive of "Mormon," "Liz" is a diminutive of "Elizabeth," and "kid" is a diminutive of "child."  The tiny kitten was a diminutive creature. |近义词：nickname, pet name, small version, miniature | 反义词：augmentative (enlarged form), full name, large
