rebelled:/rɪˈbɛld/| v.|反抗，造反|To act in defiance of authority or established norms; to engage in open resistance.|例句：The people rebelled against the oppressive regime.  人民反抗压迫的政权。|例句：The students rebelled against the new school rules. 学生们反抗新的校规。|近义词： revolted, mutinied, defied |反义词：obeyed, conformed, submitted


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rebels against authority. 他反抗权威。
They rebel against the status quo. 他们反抗现状。

一般过去时:
The colonists rebelled against British rule. 殖民者反抗英国统治。
She rebelled against her parents' wishes. 她反抗父母的意愿。

现在完成时:
The people have rebelled against the corrupt government. 人民已经反抗腐败的政府。
He has rebelled against societal norms his whole life. 他一生都在反抗社会规范。

过去完成时:
They had rebelled against the king before the war began. 战争开始前他们就已经反抗国王了。
She had rebelled against her family's expectations before moving to the city. 在搬到城市之前，她已经反抗了家人的期望。

将来时:
The people will rebel if their needs are not met. 如果他们的需求得不到满足，人民将会反抗。
He will likely rebel against any attempt to control him. 他很可能会反抗任何试图控制他的企图。

虚拟语气:
If they had rebelled sooner, things might have been different. 如果他们早点反抗，情况可能就不一样了。
I wish I had rebelled against my parents when I was younger. 我希望我年轻的时候能反抗我的父母。

被动语态:
The rebellion was brutally suppressed.  叛乱被残酷镇压。
The new laws were rebelled against by many citizens. 许多公民反抗新的法律。

疑问句:
Did the people rebel against the government? 人民反抗政府了吗？
Why did they rebel against the ruling class? 他们为什么反抗统治阶级？

否定句:
The people did not rebel against the new laws. 人民没有反抗新的法律。
He never rebelled against his family. 他从未反抗过他的家人。
