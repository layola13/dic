grumble:/ˈɡrʌmbəl/| v. n.|抱怨，咕哝|to complain or mutter in a low voice, typically in a grumpy manner.  |例句：He grumbled about the cold weather. 他抱怨天气寒冷。

例句：She grumbled to herself about the long line. 她自言自语地抱怨长长的队伍。


近义词：mutter, complain, murmur, grump

反义词：praise, compliment, commend

词性：动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grumbles about his job every day. = 他每天都抱怨他的工作。
They often grumble about the food. = 他们经常抱怨食物。


一般过去时:
He grumbled about the noise last night. = 他昨晚抱怨噪音。
She grumbled that the service was slow. = 她抱怨服务太慢。


现在完成时:
He has grumbled about this problem for weeks. = 他已经抱怨这个问题好几个星期了。
They have grumbled repeatedly about the unfair treatment. = 他们一再抱怨不公平的待遇。


过去完成时:
He had grumbled about the weather before the trip. = 旅行前他就抱怨天气。
She had grumbled many times about the lack of support. = 她多次抱怨缺乏支持。


将来时:
He will grumble if the food is not good. = 如果食物不好，他会抱怨。
They will grumble about the long hours. = 他们会抱怨长时间的工作。


虚拟语气:
If I were him, I wouldn't grumble so much. = 如果我是他，我就不会抱怨这么多。
I wish he wouldn't grumble all the time. = 我希望他不总是抱怨。


被动语态:
His complaints were grumbled about by everyone. = 他的抱怨被每个人都抱怨。 (This is less common)
The new policy was grumbled about by the employees. = 新政策遭到员工的抱怨。(This is less common)


疑问句:
What are you grumbling about? = 你在抱怨什么？
Why is he grumbling all the time? = 他为什么总是抱怨？


否定句:
He didn't grumble about the work. = 他没有抱怨工作。
She didn't grumble at all during the meeting. = 会议期间她一点也没有抱怨。


PHRASE->
a grumbling stomach = 咕咕叫的肚子
a grumbling voice = 低声抱怨的声音
to grumble to oneself = 自言自语地抱怨
grumbling discontent = 怨声载道的不满
grumbling complaints = 嘟囔的抱怨


词源:
The word "grumble" originates from the Middle English word "grumble," which itself likely derives from an imitative word representing the sound of low, rumbling speech.  The connection to sounds of discontent or complaining is inherent in its earliest usage.
