ramming:/ˈræmɪŋ/| v.|猛撞；填塞；强行塞入|The act of striking something with force, often repeatedly.  It can refer to physical impact or the forceful insertion of something.|例句：The car crashed into the wall, the sound of the ramming echoing through the street.  汽车撞上了墙，猛烈的撞击声回荡在街道上。

例句：He was ramming the documents into the briefcase, trying to close it before the meeting. 他正在把文件塞进公文包，试图在会议前把它关上。


近义词：crashing, smashing, jamming, stuffing, cramming|反义词：gently placing, delicately inserting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rams the door shut. = 他猛地把门关上了。
They ram the hay into the barn. = 他们把干草塞进谷仓。


一般过去时:
The ship rammed the iceberg. = 船撞上了冰山。
She rammed the key into the lock. = 她用力把钥匙插进锁孔。


现在完成时:
The workers have rammed the earth into the hole. = 工人们已经把土填进了洞里。
He has rammed the message home to his audience. = 他已经向听众强调了这个信息。


过去完成时:
They had rammed the gate before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前，他们已经撞开了大门。
The car had rammed into a tree.  = 汽车已经撞到了一棵树上。


将来时:
The protesters will ram the gates of the parliament. = 抗议者将冲撞议会的大门。
He will ram the facts down their throats. = 他会把事实强加给他们。


虚拟语气:
If he rammed the car, he would be arrested. = 如果他撞了车，他就会被逮捕。
I wish I hadn't rammed my foot into the table. = 我真希望我没有把脚撞到桌子上。


被动语态:
The gate was rammed open by the protesters. = 大门被抗议者撞开了。
The message was rammed home to the audience. = 信息被强加给听众了。


疑问句:
Did the car ram the wall? = 汽车撞上了墙吗？
Was the information rammed down his throat? = 信息是被强加给他的吗？


否定句:
He didn't ram the door. = 他没有猛撞门。
The message was not rammed home effectively. = 信息没有有效地被强调。


PHRASE->

ram into: 撞上；强行塞进
ram down someone's throat: 强迫某人接受
