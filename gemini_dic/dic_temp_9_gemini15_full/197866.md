shipman:/ˈʃɪpmən/ | n. |船员，水手 |A shipman is a sailor or a person who works on a ship.  The term is somewhat archaic and less common than "sailor" or "seaman" in modern usage. It often implies a less skilled or lower-ranking member of a ship's crew. |Example:  The shipman bravely battled the storm.  这位船员勇敢地与风暴搏斗。  The old shipman spun tales of his voyages.  这位老船员讲述了他航海的经历。|近义词：sailor, seaman, mariner |反义词：passenger, landlubber |名词

词源:
The word "shipman" is derived from the Old English "scipmann," a compound of "scip" (ship) and "mann" (man).  It literally means "ship-man."  The word evolved naturally through Middle English and into its modern form.
