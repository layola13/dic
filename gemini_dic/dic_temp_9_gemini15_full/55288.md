dissociates: /dɪˈsoʊʃieɪts/ | v. | 分离；解离；脱离；断绝关系 | To separate or become separated from something.  It can refer to a physical separation, a mental separation, or a social separation. | 例句：The chemical dissociates in water.  这种化学物质在水中分解。|  The trauma caused him to dissociate from his memories.  创伤导致他与自己的记忆脱节。| 近义词：separate, detach, disconnect, disengage | 反义词：associate, connect, unite, integrate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The salt dissociates in water. = 盐溶于水中。
He dissociates himself from the group's actions. = 他与该团体的行为撇清关系。

一般过去时:
The compound dissociated rapidly. = 该化合物迅速分解。
She dissociated herself from the controversial project. = 她与有争议的项目撇清关系。

现在完成时:
The molecule has dissociated into its constituent atoms. = 分子已分解成其组成原子。
He has dissociated himself from the organization since the scandal. = 自丑闻发生以来，他已与该组织脱离关系。

过去完成时:
The reaction had already dissociated before the analysis. = 在分析之前，反应已经分解了。
She had dissociated herself from the family business long ago. = 她很久以前就已与家族企业脱离关系了。

将来时:
The drug will dissociate in the bloodstream. = 该药物将在血液中分解。
He will dissociate himself from any involvement in the crime. = 他将与任何参与犯罪的行为脱钩。

虚拟语气:
If the solution were heated, the compound would dissociate. = 如果溶液被加热，化合物将会分解。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

He did not dissociate himself from his friend's actions. = 他没有与朋友的行为撇清关系。
