defibrillation:/ˌdiːfɪbrɪˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 除颤，电击除颤 | The process of using an electric shock to restore the heart's normal rhythm.  It's a life-saving procedure used to treat cardiac arrest. | Example:  The paramedics performed defibrillation on the patient immediately.  急救人员立即对病人进行了除颤。

The patient needed immediate defibrillation to restart his heart. 病人需要立即进行除颤以重启心脏。|近义词：cardioversion (if the heart is still beating but irregularly), resuscitation | 反义词：None directly opposite, but "allowing the heart to remain in fibrillation" could be considered an antonym. |名词

词源:
The word "defibrillation" is derived from "fibrillation" (the rapid, irregular contraction of the heart muscle) and the prefix "de-", meaning "to remove" or "to reverse".  Therefore, defibrillation literally means "to remove fibrillation".
