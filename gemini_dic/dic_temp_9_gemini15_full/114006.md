magistratically:/ˌmæʤɪˈstreɪtɪkli/| adj.|威严地；庄严地；有权威地 |In a manner suggesting the authority and dignity of a magistrate; with imposing dignity and authority.  |例句：The judge addressed the courtroom magistratically, silencing all murmurs.  法官威严地对着法庭讲话，使所有的窃窃私语都停止了。

例句：The mayor spoke magistratically about the city's future plans. 市长威严地谈论了城市未来的规划。


近义词：authoritatively, imperially, regally, officially, impressively

反义词： humbly, meekly, timidly


词源:  Derived from "magistrate" + "-ically".  "Magistrate" comes from Late Latin *magistratus, from Latin *magister (master).  The suffix "-ically" indicates "in the manner of".  Therefore, the word's root signifies "in the manner of a master or authority figure".
