preorganizes:/ˌpriːˈɔːrɡənaɪzɪz/| v.|预先组织；提前安排 |To organize or arrange something beforehand.|The committee preorganized the conference schedule to ensure a smooth event.  委员会预先安排了会议日程，以确保活动顺利进行。|近义词：plan, arrange, prepare|反义词：disorganize, haphazard


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He preorganizes his work schedule every Sunday. = 他每个星期天都会预先安排他的工作计划。
She preorganizes her presentations meticulously. = 她一丝不苟地预先准备她的演示文稿。


一般过去时:
They preorganized the team's travel arrangements. = 他们预先安排了团队的旅行安排。
The event planner preorganized all the logistics for the wedding. = 活动策划人预先安排了婚礼的所有后勤工作。


现在完成时:
We have preorganized the materials for the upcoming workshop. = 我们已经为即将到来的研讨会预先准备好了材料。
She has preorganized her entire wardrobe for her business trip. = 她已经为她的商务旅行预先准备好了全部衣物。


过去完成时:
He had preorganized the meeting agenda before the participants arrived. = 在与会者到达之前，他已经预先安排好了会议议程。
They had preorganized the seating chart for the banquet. = 他们在宴会之前已经预先安排好了座位表。


将来时:
I will preorganize my notes for the upcoming exam. = 我将为即将到来的考试预先整理好笔记。
She will preorganize the guest list for the party. = 她将为派对预先准备一份宾客名单。


虚拟语气:
If I had preorganized my time better, I would have finished the project on time. = 如果我更好地预先安排了时间，我就会按时完成项目。
I wish I had preorganized my packing for the vacation. = 我真希望我预先为假期打包好了行李。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
