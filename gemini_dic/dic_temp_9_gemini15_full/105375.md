kickstarting:/ˈkɪkˌstɑːrtɪŋ/| v.|启动；发起；激励；|to give something a strong start or boost, often referring to a project, business, or activity.|例句：We're kickstarting a new marketing campaign next month. 我们下个月将启动一个新的营销活动。|例句：The grant kickstarted the development of the new software. 这笔拨款启动了新软件的开发。|近义词：launching, initiating, beginning, commencing|反义词：stopping, halting, ending


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They kickstart a new project every quarter. = 他们每个季度都会启动一个新项目。
We kickstart our engines before driving. = 我们开车前启动发动机。


一般过去时:
The government kickstarted the economy with a new stimulus package. = 政府通过新的刺激计划来启动经济。
The entrepreneur kickstarted his business with a small loan. = 企业家通过一笔小额贷款启动了他的企业。


现在完成时:
They have kickstarted several successful businesses. = 他们已经启动了几家成功的企业。
The new initiative has kickstarted a wave of innovation. = 新的倡议引发了一波创新浪潮。


过去完成时:
They had kickstarted the project before the funding was secured. = 在资金到位之前，他们就已经启动了项目。
The team had kickstarted the campaign before the marketing director arrived. = 市场总监到达之前，团队就已经启动了活动。


将来时:
We will kickstart the fundraising campaign next week. = 我们下周将启动筹款活动。
They will kickstart their engines and drive off. = 他们将启动发动机然后开车离开。


虚拟语气:
If we had more funding, we could kickstart the project sooner. = 如果我们有更多资金，我们可以更快地启动项目。
I wish we could kickstart the process and get it done. = 我希望我们能够启动这个过程并完成它。


被动语态:
The project was kickstarted by a generous donation. = 该项目是由一笔慷慨的捐款启动的。
The engine was kickstarted using a jump starter. = 发动机是用启动器启动的。


疑问句:
When will they kickstart the new product launch? = 他们什么时候启动新产品的发布？
How do you kickstart your day? = 你如何开始你的一天？


否定句:
We couldn't kickstart the car; the battery was dead. = 我们无法启动汽车；电池没电了。
They didn't kickstart the project until they secured funding. = 直到他们获得资金后，他们才启动该项目。
