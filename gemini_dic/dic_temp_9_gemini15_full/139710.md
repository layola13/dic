ocellary:/oʊˈsɛləri/ | adj. | 单眼(的)；单眼斑(的) | Relating to or resembling an ocellus (a simple eye).  Ocellary refers to structures resembling simple eyes, often found in insects and other invertebrates. These structures may be light-sensitive but lack the complex structure of compound or camera eyes. | Example: The butterfly displayed prominent ocellary markings on its wings.  这只蝴蝶翅膀上有着明显的单眼斑。 |近义词：eyespots (in the context of markings) | 反义词：无
