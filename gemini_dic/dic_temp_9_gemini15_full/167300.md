pretorturing:/ˌpriːtɔːrˈtʃʊərɪŋ/ | v. | 预先折磨；在正式酷刑之前施加折磨 | Pretorturing refers to the act of inflicting pain or suffering on someone before subjecting them to official torture. It is often used to weaken a person's resolve or obtain information before more formalized methods of interrogation are employed.  This practice is considered a violation of human rights. | Example: The suspect alleged he was pretortured before his formal interrogation.  嫌疑人声称在正式审讯前受到了预先折磨。| 近义词：pre-interrogation abuse, preliminary torture, beforehand torment | 反义词：comfort, solace
