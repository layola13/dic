grangerism:/ˈɡreɪndʒərɪzəm/| n. |格兰杰主义；土地改革运动|A political movement in the United States in the late 19th century advocating for the interests of farmers, particularly focusing on land ownership and economic reform.  It involved various efforts to improve the social and economic conditions of farmers, often through cooperative action and political pressure.  The movement was named after the Patrons of Husbandry (also known as the Grange), a fraternal organization that played a key role in its development.|The Granger movement successfully lobbied for the Interstate Commerce Commission, which regulated railroad rates and aimed to prevent unfair practices against farmers. |近义词： agrarianism, populism |反义词： laissez-faire economics
