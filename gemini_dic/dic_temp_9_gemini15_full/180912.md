reidentifications:/ˌriːaɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/| n. |再识别；重新识别 |The process of identifying something or someone again, often after a period of time or after some change has occurred.  This often involves using new data or techniques to confirm or update previous identifications.  It's frequently used in contexts like facial recognition, data privacy, and security. |例句：The improved security system allowed for swift reidentifications of suspects.  改进后的安全系统允许快速重新识别嫌疑人。

例句：The research paper focuses on the challenges and ethical implications of reidentifications in large datasets. 这篇研究论文关注大型数据集再识别中的挑战和伦理问题。


近义词：reinspections, re-verifications, re-examinations

反义词：no further identification, initial identification


词性:名词
