flinched: /flɪntʃt/ | v. |畏缩；退缩；犹豫；|To make a sudden, involuntary movement as a reaction to surprise, fear, or pain.  It often implies a brief, slight movement, showing hesitation or nervousness.|例句：He flinched at the loud noise. 他被巨大的噪音吓了一跳。

He flinched when the doctor touched his wound.  医生触碰他的伤口时，他畏缩了一下。


近义词：shrink, recoil, wince, start, cower|反义词：brave, stand firm, stand one's ground|词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flinches at loud noises. = 他害怕大声的噪音。
She flinches when she sees blood. = 她看到血就会畏缩。


一般过去时:
He flinched when the dog barked. = 狗叫的时候他畏缩了一下。
She flinched at the sight of the spider. = 她看到蜘蛛时畏缩了一下。


现在完成时:
He has flinched from the challenge. = 他对挑战退缩了。
She has never flinched in the face of danger. = 面对危险她从未退缩。


过去完成时:
He had flinched before the blow landed. =  在被打之前他就已经畏缩了。
She had flinched many times before she finally faced her fears. = 在她最终面对恐惧之前，她已经畏缩过很多次了。


将来时:
He will flinch if you surprise him. = 如果你吓唬他，他会畏缩的。
She will probably flinch when she hears the news. = 当她听到这个消息时，她可能会畏缩。


虚拟语气:
If he had not flinched, he might have won the fight. = 如果他没有畏缩，他可能就赢得了这场战斗。
I wish I hadn't flinched when I saw the snake. = 我真希望看到蛇的时候我没有畏缩。


被动语态:
(The verb "flinch" is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes a reflexive action.)


疑问句:
Did he flinch when you told him the bad news? = 你告诉他坏消息的时候，他畏缩了吗？
Does she flinch easily? = 她容易畏缩吗？


否定句:
He didn't flinch when they threatened him. = 当他们威胁他时，他没有畏缩。
She didn't flinch even when she was injured. = 即使受伤了，她也没有畏缩。


PHRASE->
