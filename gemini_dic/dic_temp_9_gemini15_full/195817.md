send-up: /ˈsɛndʌp/ | n., v. |  模仿，戏仿；嘲弄，挖苦 |

英文解析:A send-up is a humorous imitation of someone or something, often exaggerating their characteristics for comedic effect.  It can be a performance, a piece of writing, or even just a casual remark intended to mock or satirize. As a verb, it means to create or perform such an imitation.

例句:

The comedian's send-up of the politician was hilarious.  喜剧演员对这位政治家的模仿非常滑稽。

The play was a send-up of classic Hollywood films. 这部戏是经典好莱坞电影的戏仿。


近义词:parody, satire, caricature, lampoon, mockery

反义词:serious imitation, tribute, homage


词源:The term likely emerged from the theatrical sense of "sending up" an actor or character, meaning to lift them up onto the stage—but with the ironic twist of implying an exaggerated or mocking portrayal.
