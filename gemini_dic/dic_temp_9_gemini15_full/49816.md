demonstrably:/ˈdɛmənstrəbli/| adv.|可证明地；明显地|Clearly and obviously demonstrable; capable of being shown or proved.  The experiment demonstrably proved the theory.|The results demonstrably show the effectiveness of the new drug. 结果明显地显示了新药的有效性。|provably, evidently, clearly, obviously|undemonstrably, inconclusively


USAGE->
一般现在时:
It is demonstrably true that the earth is round. = 地球是圆的，这是可以证明的。
The benefits of exercise are demonstrably clear. = 运动的好处是显而易见的。


一般过去时:
The experiment demonstrably proved his hypothesis. = 实验明显证明了他的假设。
His guilt was demonstrably established by the evidence. = 证据明显地证明了他的罪行。


现在完成时:
Scientists have demonstrably shown the link between smoking and cancer. = 科学家们已经明确地证明了吸烟与癌症之间的联系。
The success of the project has been demonstrably achieved. = 项目的成功已经得到了明确的证明。


过去完成时:
It had demonstrably been proven that the theory was flawed. = 事实已经清楚地证明该理论存在缺陷。
The evidence had demonstrably indicated his innocence. = 证据已经清楚地表明了他的清白。


将来时:
The new technology will demonstrably improve efficiency. = 新技术将明显提高效率。
Future studies will demonstrably confirm these findings. = 未来的研究将明显证实这些发现。


虚拟语气:
If the data were demonstrably accurate, the conclusion would be different. = 如果数据明显准确，结论就会不同。
I wish the benefits of this policy were demonstrably clear. = 我希望这项政策的好处能够明显地体现出来。


被动语态:
It was demonstrably shown that the drug was effective. = 该药物的有效性得到了明显的证明。
The effects of climate change are being demonstrably felt worldwide. = 气候变化的影响在全球范围内日益明显。


疑问句:
Is it demonstrably true that he committed the crime? = 他犯了罪，这是可以证明的吗？
Are the results demonstrably significant? = 结果明显显著吗？


否定句:
His claims are not demonstrably true. = 他的说法并非可以证明属实。
The connection between the two events is not demonstrably clear. = 这两件事之间的联系并不明显。


PHRASE->
demonstrably true = 可以证明是真实的
