chout:/tʃaʊt/| v.|大声喊叫，大声叫嚷 | to shout is to speak or cry out loudly.  It often implies anger, excitement, or a need to be heard over a distance or noise.|例句：He shouted at the top of his lungs. 他大声喊叫。|例句：She shouted with joy. 她高兴地大叫。|近义词：yell, scream, bellow, roar|反义词：whisper, murmur


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He shouts at the referee. = 他朝裁判大声喊叫。
They shout instructions to the workers. = 他们大声向工人下达指令。

一般过去时:
She shouted at the dog. = 她朝那条狗大声喊叫。
He shouted his answer across the room. = 他大声地把答案喊过房间。

现在完成时:
I have shouted myself hoarse. = 我喊得嗓子都哑了。
They have shouted for help many times. = 他们多次大声呼救。

过去完成时:
He had shouted warnings before the accident. = 事故发生前他已经大声发出警告。
She had shouted so much that her throat was sore. = 她喊得太多，嗓子都疼了。

将来时:
I will shout if I need help. = 如果需要帮助我会大声喊叫。
They will shout the news from the rooftops. = 他们会把这个消息广而告之。

虚拟语气:
If I shouted, would anyone hear me? = 如果我喊，有人会听到吗？
I wish I hadn't shouted at him. = 我真希望我没有朝他大声喊叫。

被动语态:
His name was shouted from the crowd. = 他的名字被人群大声喊叫出来。
The instructions were shouted over the noise. = 指令在大声喧哗声中被喊了出来。

疑问句:
Why did he shout? = 他为什么喊叫？
Who shouted that? = 谁喊的？

否定句:
I didn't shout. = 我没有喊叫。
She didn't shout at him. = 她没有朝他喊叫。
