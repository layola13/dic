frowns:/fraʊnz/| v.|皱眉，不悦|To make a facial expression in which the eyebrows are drawn together, typically expressing displeasure, disapproval, or concentration.  |She frowned at the messy room. 她皱着眉头看着凌乱的房间。 He frowned in concentration. 他皱着眉头思考。|smiles, scoffs|laughs, smiles|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
She frowns at the news. = 她对这个消息皱起了眉头。
He often frowns when he's thinking hard. = 他认真思考时经常皱眉。

一般过去时:
She frowned when she heard the bad news. = 当她听到坏消息时，她皱起了眉头。
He frowned at the difficult problem. = 他对那个难题皱起了眉头。

现在完成时:
She has frowned all day. = 她整天都愁眉苦脸的。
He has frowned since he received the letter. = 自从收到这封信以来，他就一直愁眉苦脸的。

过去完成时:
She had frowned before she spoke. = 她说话前就已皱起了眉头。
He had frowned all afternoon before finally giving up. = 他苦思冥想了一下午，最后才放弃。

将来时:
She will frown if she sees the mess. = 如果她看到这乱七八糟的样子，她会皱眉头的。
He will frown upon such behavior. = 他会反对这种行为的。

虚拟语气:
If she saw the mess, she would frown. = 如果她看到这乱七八糟的样子，她会皱眉头的。
I wish he wouldn't frown so much. = 我希望他不要总是皱眉。


被动语态:
(Frown is rarely used passively)  This action is not typically described passively.


疑问句:
Does she frown often? = 她经常皱眉吗？
Why is he frowning? = 他为什么皱眉？


否定句:
She didn't frown at all. = 她一点也不皱眉。
He doesn't frown easily. = 他不容易皱眉。


PHRASE->
frown upon = 不赞成
frown at = 对…皱眉
frown on = 反对
wear a frown = 愁眉苦脸
frown with displeasure = 不悦地皱眉

词源:
The word "frown" comes from Old English *frūnian, meaning "to wrinkle the brow,"  possibly related to Proto-Germanic *frū- "to wrinkle."  The modern English word developed through Middle English "frounen."
