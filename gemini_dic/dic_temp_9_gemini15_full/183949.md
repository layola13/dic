resettle:/ˌriːˈsɛtl/| vt.|重新安置；重新定居|To settle again or in a new place.  This often involves relocating people who have been displaced, such as refugees or those affected by natural disasters.|例句：The government is working to resettle the refugees in safe areas. 政府正在努力将难民安置在安全地区。|例句：After the flood, many families had to resettle in temporary housing. 洪水过后，许多家庭不得不搬到临时住所居住。|近义词：relocate, relocate, rehouse|反义词：displace, evict


USAGE->

The government resets the refugees in a new place. = 政府将难民安置在一个新的地方。
They resettle in a quiet village. = 他们在宁静的村庄重新定居。

一般过去时:
The family resettled in the countryside after the war. = 战争结束后，这家人在乡下重新定居。
The authorities resettled the displaced people in temporary shelters. = 当局将流离失所的人们安置在临时住所。

现在完成时:
Many refugees have resettled in other countries. = 许多难民已在其他国家重新定居。

过去完成时:

The community had resettled numerous times due to recurring floods. = 由于反复发生的洪水，这个社区已经多次搬迁。

将来时:
