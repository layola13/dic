preconsecrate:/ˌpriːˈkɒnsɪkreɪt/|/ˈpriːkɒnˌsɛkreɪt/| vt.|预先奉献；预先祝圣|To consecrate beforehand; to dedicate or sanctify in advance.|The church was preconsecrated before the official ceremony.|近义词：preordain, predetermine|反义词：desecrate, profane|

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They preconsecrate the altar before the main service. = 他们在主仪式之前预先奉献祭坛。
He preconsecrates the land for the new temple. = 他预先将这块地奉献给新的庙宇。


一般过去时:
The priest preconsecrated the chalice yesterday. = 牧师昨天预先祝圣了圣杯。
They preconsecrated the building last week. = 他们上周预先祝圣了这座建筑。


现在完成时:
The bishop has preconsecrated several new churches this year. = 主教今年已经预先祝圣了几座新教堂。
We have preconsecrated the grounds for the upcoming festival. = 我们已经预先为即将到来的节日奉献了场地。


过去完成时:
They had preconsecrated the site before the foundation stone was laid. = 在奠基石奠基之前，他们已经预先奉献了场地。
The villagers had preconsecrated the spring before the drought began. = 在旱灾开始之前，村民们已经预先奉献了泉水。


将来时:
The cardinal will preconsecrate the new cathedral next month. = 红衣主教下个月将预先祝圣新的教堂。
They will preconsecrate the land for the construction of the hospital. = 他们将为医院的建设预先奉献这块土地。


虚拟语气:
If they had preconsecrated the building, the ceremony would have been more meaningful. = 如果他们预先祝圣了这座建筑，仪式就会更有意义。
I wish they had preconsecrated the altar before the service started. = 我希望他们在仪式开始之前已经预先奉献了祭坛。


被动语态:
The altar was preconsecrated by the archbishop. = 祭坛是由大主教预先祝圣的。

疑问句:

否定句:
