ruption:/ˈrʌpʃən/| n.|中断，爆发，破裂|The sudden eruption of the volcano caused widespread damage. 火山的突然爆发造成了广泛的破坏。|近义词：burst, outbreak, disruption|反义词：calm, peace, continuity|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The volcano erupts regularly. = 这座火山定期喷发。
A disruption occurs in the network. = 网络中发生中断。


一般过去时:
The pipe ruptured and caused a flood. = 管子破裂导致洪水。
The meeting was disrupted by a sudden power outage. = 会议因突然停电而中断。


现在完成时:
Several eruptions have occurred this year. = 今年已经发生了几次火山爆发。
The project has been disrupted by unforeseen circumstances. = 该项目因不可预见的情况而受阻。


过去完成时:
The dam had already ruptured before the engineers arrived. = 工程师到达之前，水坝已经破裂了。
The negotiations had been disrupted by a lack of communication. = 谈判因缺乏沟通而中断。


将来时:
The scientists predict that the volcano will erupt soon. = 科学家预测火山很快将喷发。
The strike will disrupt train services for several days. = 罢工将使火车服务中断数天。


虚拟语气:
If the volcano erupted, it would cause significant damage. = 如果火山爆发，将会造成重大损失。
If the meeting hadn't been disrupted, we would have finished on time. = 如果会议没有中断，我们就能按时完成了。


被动语态:
The city was disrupted by the earthquake. = 城市因地震而受损。
The meeting was disrupted by protesters. = 会议被抗议者扰乱。


疑问句:
What caused the disruption of the service? = 什么导致了服务的瘫痪？
Has there been any recent volcanic eruption? = 最近有火山爆发吗？


否定句:
The eruption did not cause any casualties. = 火山爆发没有造成任何伤亡。
The protest did not disrupt the flow of traffic. = 抗议活动没有扰乱交通。


PHRASE->
eruption of violence  暴力爆发
sudden eruption 突然爆发
volcanic eruption 火山爆发
eruption of laughter 一阵大笑
disruptive eruption 破坏性爆发

词源:
rupture (v.) "to break, burst," from Latin *rumpere "to break," of unknown origin.  The noun eruption is a later development,  reflecting the forceful breaking out implied by the verb.
