pard:/pɑːrd/| n. |赦免；宽恕；原谅|A pardon is an official act of forgiveness for a crime.  It releases the offender from punishment.  The word often implies a formal, legal forgiveness, especially by a sovereign or authority.|例句：The king granted him a pardon. 国王赦免了他。|例句：She begged for his pardon. 她乞求他的原谅。|近义词：forgiveness, remission, absolution|反义词：conviction, condemnation, punishment|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pardons the criminal. = 他赦免了罪犯。
The judge pardons the defendant. = 法官赦免了被告。

一般过去时:
The king pardoned the traitor. = 国王赦免了叛徒。
She pardoned her son for his mistake. = 她原谅了她儿子的错误。

现在完成时:
The president has pardoned several prisoners. = 总统已经赦免了几名囚犯。
He has pardoned her for her lies. = 他已经原谅了她说谎。

过去完成时:
The governor had pardoned him before the news broke. = 在消息传出之前，州长已经赦免了他。
She had pardoned his rudeness earlier that day. = 那天早些时候，她已经原谅了他的粗鲁行为。

将来时:
The court will pardon him if he cooperates. = 如果他配合，法院将会赦免他。
I believe the jury will pardon her. = 我相信陪审团会原谅她。

虚拟语气:
If the judge pardoned him, he would be a free man. = 如果法官赦免了他，他将成为自由人。
I wish she had pardoned me. = 我希望她当时原谅了我。

被动语态:
He was pardoned by the queen. = 他被女王赦免了。
The criminal was pardoned for his crime. = 罪犯因其罪行而被赦免。

疑问句:
Will the president pardon him? = 总统会赦免他吗？
Has she been pardoned yet? = 她已经被赦免了吗？

否定句:
The king did not pardon the rebels. = 国王没有赦免叛乱者。
She would not pardon his betrayal. = 她不会原谅他的背叛。
