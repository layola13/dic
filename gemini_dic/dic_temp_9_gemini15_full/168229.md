pro-Germans: /ˌproʊˈdʒɜːrmənz/ | adj. | 亲德的；支持德国的 |  Supporting or favoring Germany, especially during wartime.  This term typically carries a negative connotation, implying collaboration or sympathy with the enemy.  | 例句：The pro-German sentiment in some parts of the country was a cause for concern during the war.  = 在战争期间，该国某些地区的亲德情绪令人担忧。

The pro-German newspapers were heavily censored. = 亲德报纸受到严格审查。


近义词：Germanophile (more neutral),  pro-Nazi (in the context of Nazi Germany) | 反义词：anti-German, pro-Allied (depending on context) | 词性：形容词


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms in standard usage.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  The usage is contextual, always modifying a noun.


PHRASE->
pro-German propaganda = 亲德宣传
pro-German sympathies = 亲德同情
pro-German activities = 亲德活动
pro-German sentiment = 亲德情绪
a pro-German stance = 亲德立场


词源:

The word is a compound of "pro-" (meaning "in favor of," "supporting") and "German" (referring to Germany or its people).  Its usage reflects historical periods, particularly times of conflict involving Germany, where the term acquired negative connotations due to associations with collaboration or treason.
