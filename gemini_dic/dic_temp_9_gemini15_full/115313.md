mannerists: /ˈmænərɪsts/ | n. | 风格主义者，矫饰派艺术家 |  Members of the Mannerist movement in art, characterized by artificiality, elegance, and exaggeration of form and style.  They reacted against the naturalism of the High Renaissance. | The Mannerists were known for their elongated figures and distorted perspectives.  风格主义者以其细长的身材和扭曲的视角而闻名。| 近义词：affected artists, stylistic artists | 反义词：naturalists, realists | 名词
