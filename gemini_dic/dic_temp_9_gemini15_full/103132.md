jeroboam:/ˈdʒɛrəboʊæm/ | n. |耶罗波安（酒瓶）；大酒瓶 | A jeroboam is a large bottle of wine, typically holding 3 liters (approximately 4 bottles of wine).  The term derives from King Jeroboam I of Israel, known for his lavish lifestyle. |例句：We opened a jeroboam of champagne to celebrate the wedding. 我们打开了一瓶耶罗波安香槟来庆祝婚礼。  The restaurant boasts a selection of wines available in jeroboam. 这家餐厅拥有各种可以按耶罗波安装瓶的葡萄酒。|近义词：rehoboam (larger bottle), magnum (smaller bottle) |反义词：None specifically, but smaller wine bottles like a standard bottle or half-bottle could be considered antonyms in terms of size. |名词
