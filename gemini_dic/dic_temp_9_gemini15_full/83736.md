hail-mary-passes:/ˈheɪlˌmɛriˈpæsɪz/| n.|绝望的尝试，孤注一掷|A hail Mary pass is a long, desperation pass in American football, typically thrown when a team is losing and time is running out.  It's a high-risk, high-reward play with a low probability of success. The term is now used metaphorically to describe any last-ditch effort to achieve something seemingly impossible.|例句：He threw a hail Mary pass in the final seconds of the game, but it fell incomplete. 他在比赛的最后几秒钟扔了一个绝望的传球，但没有成功。
The company made a hail Mary pass with their new product launch, hoping it would revive their failing business.  这家公司用他们的新产品发布做了一次孤注一掷的尝试，希望它能重振他们失败的业务。|近义词：last resort, long shot, desperate attempt|反义词：sure thing, certain victory, guaranteed success|名词
