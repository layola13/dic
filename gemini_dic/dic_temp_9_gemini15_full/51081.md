despatched:/dɪˈspætʃt/ | v. | 派遣；发送；迅速处理 | To send something or someone somewhere, especially in a quick or efficient way.  | The goods were dispatched yesterday. 货物昨天已发出。 |近义词：send, transmit, expedite | 反义词：receive, retrieve


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They dispatch the orders daily. = 他们每天都发出订单。
The company dispatches goods worldwide. = 这家公司向全球各地发货。


一般过去时:
She dispatched the letter immediately. = 她立即发了信。
The soldiers were dispatched to the front line. = 士兵们被派往前线。


现在完成时:
We have dispatched all the urgent orders. = 我们已经发出所有紧急订单。
The company has dispatched several teams to the disaster area. = 公司已经派出几支队伍前往灾区。


过去完成时:
They had dispatched the troops before the enemy arrived. = 敌军到达之前，他们已经派出了军队。
The manager had dispatched the memo before the meeting. = 会议开始前，经理已经发了备忘录。


将来时:
We will dispatch the shipment next week. = 我们下周将发货。
The government will dispatch aid to the affected region. = 政府将向受灾地区派遣援助。


虚拟语气:
If we dispatched the goods earlier, the customer would have received them by now. = 如果我们早点发货，客户现在已经收到了。
I wish I had dispatched the email sooner. = 我真希望我早点发了邮件。


被动语态:
The package was dispatched yesterday. = 包裹昨天被发出。
The troops were dispatched to quell the riot. = 部队被派遣去平息暴乱。


疑问句:
When will the order be dispatched? = 订单什么时候发出？
Has the package been dispatched yet? = 包裹已经发出去了吗？


否定句:
They did not dispatch the goods on time. = 他们没有按时发货。
The order has not yet been dispatched. = 订单尚未发出。
