glitzy:/ˈɡlɪtsi/| adj.|俗丽的；炫耀的；光鲜的 |Characterized by dazzling or showy brilliance or splendor, often to an excessive degree.  It implies an ostentatious display of wealth or luxury, sometimes lacking genuine substance. |例句：The glitzy awards ceremony was filled with celebrities and flashing cameras.  这场俗丽的颁奖典礼挤满了名人和闪光灯。

The new hotel boasts glitzy rooms and a lavish spa. 这家新酒店拥有奢华的客房和豪华的水疗中心。


近义词： flashy, glamorous, showy, ostentatious, flamboyant |反义词： understated, simple, plain, modest


词源:  The word "glitzy" likely originated as a slang term, probably emerging in the mid-20th century.  Its etymology isn't definitively traced to a single source, but it's reasonably inferred to be related to "glitter," suggesting a connection to shining, sparkling, and showy qualities. The "-zy" suffix adds an intensifying and somewhat informal tone.
