dadas:/ˈdɑːdɑːz/| n. |达达主义|Dadaism is an artistic and literary movement that began in Zurich, Switzerland during World War I.  It was characterized by its rejection of logic, reason, and traditional artistic values. Dada artists used absurd and nonsensical techniques to express their outrage at the horrors of war and the meaningless nature of modern society.  The movement embraced chance and spontaneity, often incorporating found objects and everyday materials into their works.  |Example: The Dada exhibition featured a collection of provocative and nonsensical art pieces.|近义词:Surrealism, Absurdism |反义词:Classicism, Realism |名词
