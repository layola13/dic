philosophe:/ˈfɪləˌsɒf/|/ˈfɪləˌsɔːf/| n. |哲学家；思想家|A philosopher is a person who studies or writes about philosophy.  Philosophes were influential intellectuals of the 18th-century Enlightenment. |Example:  Socrates is considered one of the greatest philosophers of ancient Greece. 苏格拉底被认为是古希腊最伟大的哲学家之一。

Example:  Many philosophes advocated for reason and individual liberty. 许多思想家提倡理性与个人自由。


近义词：thinker, intellectual, scholar, sage

反义词：ignorant, unlearned,  (no direct antonym, as it describes a profession/role)


词源:  From French *philosophe*, from Late Latin *philosophus*, from Ancient Greek *philosophos* (φιλόσοφος), from *philos* (φίλος) "loving" + *sophia* (σοφία) "wisdom".  The word ultimately derives from the Greek love of wisdom.
