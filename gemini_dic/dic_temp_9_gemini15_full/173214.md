quasi-colloquial: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˌkɒləˈkwiəl/ | adj. | 准口语的，介于口语和书面语之间的 |  Approaching colloquial style; informal but not entirely casual or slangy;  a style of language that is somewhat informal but not completely casual, falling between formal written language and everyday spoken language. It often uses contractions and less formal vocabulary than formal writing but avoids slang and highly informal expressions.  |例句：The author's quasi-colloquial tone made the academic text more accessible to a wider audience. 作者准口语的语气使学术文本更容易被更广泛的读者接受。  | 近义词：informal, semi-formal | 反义词：formal, literary
