pre-Celtic: /ˌpriːˈsɛltɪk/ | adj. | 前凯尔特人的；凯尔特人之前的 |  Relating to the period or cultures in Britain and Ireland before the arrival of the Celts.  This term often refers to the archaeological and linguistic evidence suggesting a non-Celtic population existed in these islands before Celtic influence became dominant.  The exact nature and extent of Pre-Celtic cultures remain a subject of ongoing research and debate. |  The discovery of Pre-Celtic artifacts suggests a rich and complex culture predating the arrival of the Celts.  | 近义词: pre-Celtic, proto-Celtic | 反义词: Celtic, post-Celtic |  形容词
