garrisoning:/ˈɡærɪsənɪŋ/| v.|驻军；防守|The act of providing a garrison to a place; the state of being garrisoned.  It implies the occupation of a place by military forces for defense or protection.|例句：The general ordered the garrisoning of the border towns.  将军下令驻守边境城镇。

例句：The strategic garrisoning of the island prevented invasion.  岛屿的战略性驻防阻止了入侵。


近义词：fortifying, defending, protecting, occupying

反义词：evacuating, abandoning, surrendering


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are garrisoning the fort. = 他们在驻守要塞。
The army is garrisoning the city. = 军队驻守着这座城市。


一般过去时:
They garrisoned the fort last year. = 他们去年驻守了要塞。
The troops garrisoned the town after the riot. = 骚乱后军队驻守了该镇。


现在完成时:
They have garrisoned the border for decades. = 他们几十年来一直在驻守边境。
The government has garrisoned the capital since the coup. = 自政变以来，政府一直驻守首都。


过去完成时:
They had garrisoned the city before the enemy attacked. = 敌人进攻之前，他们已经驻守了这座城市。
The soldiers had garrisoned the outpost for months. = 士兵们已经驻守了这个前哨站好几个月了。


将来时:
They will garrison the new base next month. = 他们下个月将驻守新的基地。
The troops will garrison the area to maintain peace. = 部队将驻守该地区以维持和平。


虚拟语气:
If they garrisoned the town, the rebels would not attack. = 如果他们驻守城镇，叛军就不会进攻。
I wish they had garrisoned the bridge before the fighting started. = 我真希望他们在战斗开始前就驻守了桥梁。


被动语态:
The fort was garrisoned by a thousand soldiers. = 要塞由一千名士兵驻守。
The city was garrisoned quickly after the earthquake. = 地震后，这座城市很快就被驻军了。


疑问句:
Was the town garrisoned during the war? = 战争期间城镇有驻军吗？
Are they garrisoning the strategically important island? = 他们正在驻守那个战略要岛吗？


否定句:
They did not garrison the outpost. = 他们没有驻守前哨。
The government has not garrisoned the area due to resource constraints. = 由于资源限制，政府没有驻守该地区。
