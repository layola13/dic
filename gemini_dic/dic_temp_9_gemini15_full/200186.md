skinflint:/ˈskɪnflɪnt/| n. |守财奴，吝啬鬼 | a person who is unwilling to spend money.  |例句：He's such a skinflint, he wouldn't even buy a cup of coffee for his friend.|近义词：miser, cheapskate, penny-pincher |反义词：generous, philanthropist, spendthrift |名词


词源:

The word "skinflint" is of uncertain origin, but likely a compound word combining "skin" and "flint."  The "flint" part refers to the hard, resistant nature of flint stone, suggesting the hardness and inflexibility of a person unwilling to part with their money. The "skin" element might suggest scraping or clinging to every bit of money, as if scraping the skin off something to get the last bit.  There's no definitive historical record pinpointing the exact origin and evolution of the word.


用法说明:

Skinflint is primarily used as a noun to describe a person who is extremely frugal to the point of being miserly. It carries a strong negative connotation, implying not just thriftiness but also meanness and unwillingness to share.


例句:


He is a notorious skinflint; he never tips and always haggles over prices. = 他是一个臭名昭著的守财奴；他从不给小费，总是讨价还价。

My uncle, a confirmed skinflint, left all his money to a cat charity. = 我的叔叔，一个十足的守财奴，把所有的钱都留给了猫的慈善机构。


近义词:


Miser:  A person who hoards wealth and spends as little as possible.
Cheapskate:  A less formal and slightly less harsh term than skinflint.
Penny-pincher:  Someone who is extremely frugal and saves money excessively.


反义词:


Generous: Willing to give and share freely.
Philanthropist: Someone who donates money to charitable causes.
Spendthrift:  Someone who spends money recklessly.
