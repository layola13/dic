predication:/ˌprɛdɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |断言；谓语；宣告|The act of predicating; the assertion of something as true.  It also refers to the part of a sentence that contains the verb and its complements, expressing what is said about the subject. |例句：His predication of doom was unfounded.  他的厄运预言毫无根据。

例句：The predication in the sentence "The cat sat on the mat" is "sat on the mat".  句子“猫坐在垫子上”中的谓语是“坐在垫子上”。


近义词：assertion, affirmation, declaration, statement|反义词：denial, refutation, contradiction|词性：名词


词源：源自于拉丁语praedicatio，由praedicare派生而来，意思是“宣告，断言”。  praedicare本身由prae-（向前）和dicare（说）构成。


用法说明:

predication 主要有两个含义：

1. **断言，宣告**:  指断定某事为真，或公开宣布某事。 例如：the predication of innocence (宣告无罪).

2. **谓语**:  在语法中，指句子中包含谓语动词及其宾语、补语等成分的部分，说明主语做了什么或是什么样的。


例句（断言）：

The professor's predication about the economic downturn proved accurate. 教授关于经济衰退的预言被证明是准确的。

Her predication of success was based on years of hard work. 她成功的预言是基于多年的努力工作。


例句（谓语）：

In the sentence "The dog chased the ball," "chased the ball" is the predication. 在句子“狗追逐球”中，“追逐球”是谓语。

The predication of the sentence needs to be clearer. 这句子的谓语需要更清晰。


PHRASE->

There is no common multi-word phrases directly using "predication"  as a core component.  The word is primarily used in a more formal or technical context.  However, phrases related to its meaning can be used:

make a predication = 做出一个断言
base a predication on = 基于...做出断言
a false predication = 一个错误的断言
the central predication = 主要的断言
support a predication = 支持一个断言
