hypnotises:/ˈhɪpnətaɪzɪz/ | vt. |催眠；迷惑；着迷 | To put (someone) into a hypnotic state; to fascinate or enthrall. |例句：The magician hypnotised the volunteer on stage.  魔术师催眠了舞台上的志愿者。

例句：The beauty of the sunset hypnotised her. 日落的美丽让她着迷。


近义词：mesmerize, enthrall, captivate, fascinate | 反义词：disenchant, disillusion


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hypnotises patients to treat their phobias. 他用催眠疗法治疗病人的恐惧症。
The music hypnotises me; I can't stop listening. 这音乐让我着迷，我停不下来。


一般过去时:
The therapist hypnotised the patient and helped her recall repressed memories. 治疗师催眠了病人，并帮助她回忆起被压抑的记忆。
The speaker hypnotised the audience with his powerful words. 演讲者用他富有感染力的语言征服了听众。


现在完成时:
She has hypnotised many people throughout her career. 在她的职业生涯中，她已经催眠过很多人。
The advertisement has hypnotised consumers into buying the product. 广告让消费者着迷，从而购买了产品。


过去完成时:
He had hypnotised the subject before the experiment began. 在实验开始之前，他已经催眠了受试者。
The performer had hypnotised the audience before revealing the grand illusion. 表演者在揭示重大幻象之前已经催眠了观众。


将来时:
The doctor will hypnotise the patient to alleviate pain. 医生将催眠病人以减轻疼痛。
I will hypnotise myself to relax before the exam. 我将在考试前催眠自己以放松身心。


虚拟语气:
If I could hypnotise people, I would help them overcome their fears. 如果我能催眠人，我会帮助他们克服恐惧。
I wish I could hypnotise myself to sleep. 我希望我能催眠自己入睡。


被动语态:
The patient was hypnotised by the therapist. 病人被治疗师催眠了。
The audience was hypnotised by the performer's magic show. 观众被表演者的魔术表演迷住了。


疑问句:
Can you hypnotise people? 你会催眠人吗？
Has the doctor hypnotised the patient yet? 医生已经催眠病人了吗？


否定句:
I cannot hypnotise people; I lack the skill. 我不会催眠人；我缺乏这项技能。
The magician did not hypnotise the audience successfully. 魔术师没有成功地催眠观众。

词源:
From Greek  hypnos ("sleep") + -ize (to make or cause to be).  The word ultimately reflects the association of hypnosis with a sleep-like state.
