lobbying:/ˈlɒbiɪŋ/ | n. |游说；说服；进行游说活动 | The act of attempting to influence decisions made by officials in a government or other institution.  This is often done by organized groups or individuals who try to persuade them to adopt a particular policy or take a specific action. | Example:  The environmental group engaged in intense lobbying to prevent the construction of the new dam.  环境保护组织进行了激烈的游说活动，以阻止新水坝的建设。

Example: The oil company’s lobbying efforts resulted in favorable tax legislation. 石油公司的游说努力导致了有利的税收立法。| 近义词：advocacy, persuasion, influence peddling | 反义词：opposition, resistance


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They lobby Congress regularly. = 他们定期游说国会。
The company lobbies for tax breaks. = 这家公司游说减税。


一般过去时:
They lobbied the mayor last week. = 他们上周游说了市长。
The union lobbied hard for better wages. = 工会为争取更好的工资而进行了大力游说。


现在完成时:
They have lobbied extensively on this issue. = 他们在这个问题上进行了广泛的游说。
The organization has lobbied for years to change the law. = 该组织多年来一直在游说改变法律。


过去完成时:
They had lobbied the committee before the vote. = 他们在投票前已经游说了委员会。
The group had lobbied extensively before the bill was passed. = 该组织在法案通过前进行了广泛的游说。


将来时:
They will lobby the government next month. = 他们下个月将游说政府。
The activists will lobby for stricter environmental regulations. = 这些活动家将游说更严格的环境法规。


虚拟语气:
If they lobbied harder, they might have succeeded. = 如果他们更努力游说，他们可能会成功。
I wish they had lobbied the senator more effectively. = 我希望他们能更有效地游说参议员。


被动语态:
The bill was lobbied against heavily. = 该法案受到了强烈的反对游说。
The proposal is being lobbied for by several groups. = 几个团体正在游说这项提案。


疑问句:
Are they lobbying for or against the bill? = 他们是在游说法案还是反对法案？
Who is lobbying the government on this issue? = 谁在这个问题上游说政府？


否定句:
They did not lobby the council. = 他们没有游说议会。
The company doesn't lobby politicians directly. = 这家公司不会直接游说政客。


PHRASE->
intense lobbying:  激烈的游说
grassroots lobbying: 草根游说
direct lobbying: 直接游说
indirect lobbying: 间接游说
corporate lobbying: 公司游说

词源:
From Middle English *lobyen, from Old French lobie, of obscure origin, possibly related to  Old French loge "lodge," "room."  The original sense referred to waiting in a room or area, often to meet or influence someone.  The modern sense developed over time, reflecting the practice of waiting near government buildings to speak with officials.
