shopaholics:/ˌʃɒpəˈhɒlɪks/ | n. |购物狂，购物成瘾者 | A person who is addicted to shopping.  They compulsively buy things, often beyond their means, leading to financial and emotional problems. |例句：Many shopaholics use shopping as a way to cope with stress or unhappiness. 许多购物狂用购物来应对压力或不快乐。

例句：She's a shopaholic; her closet is overflowing with clothes she never wears. 她是个购物狂；她的衣柜里塞满了她从不穿的衣服。


近义词：compulsive buyer, spending addict | 反义词：saver, frugal person

词源:

The word "shopaholic" is a blend of "shop" and "alcoholic."  It was coined by analogy to "alcoholic," reflecting the compulsive and often damaging nature of the behavior.  The suffix "-holic" is used to indicate an addiction or strong craving for something.
