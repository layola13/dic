hallucinator:/həˈluːsɪneɪtər/| n. |幻觉者；产生幻觉的事物|A person or thing that produces hallucinations.  A hallucinator can refer to a person experiencing hallucinations, or a system (like a large language model) that produces outputs that are not grounded in reality. |Example: The medication caused him to become a hallucinator, seeing things that weren't there.  The new AI model, while impressive, sometimes acts as a hallucinator, fabricating information. |近义词：visionary (in a negative context), fantasizer|反义词：realist
