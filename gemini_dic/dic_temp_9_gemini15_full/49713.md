democrat: /ˈdɛməkræt/ | n. | 民主主义者；民主党人 | A person who supports democracy or a member of the Democratic Party (especially in the US).  | Example:  She is a staunch democrat and believes in the power of the people. 她是一位坚定的民主主义者，相信人民的力量。  |近义词:  supporter of democracy, member of the Democratic Party | 反义词: autocrat, authoritarian


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a democrat. = 他是一个民主党人。
They support democratic principles. = 他们支持民主原则。


一般过去时:
She was a democrat in her younger years. = 她年轻时是民主党人。
They voted for the democratic candidate. = 他们投票支持民主党候选人。


现在完成时:
He has been a democrat all his life. = 他一生都是民主党人。
The party has championed democratic reforms. = 该党一直倡导民主改革。


过去完成时:
She had already been a registered democrat before the election. = 大选之前她就已经注册成为一名民主党人了。
They had long supported democratic ideals before the revolution. = 革命之前，他们长期支持民主理想。


将来时:
He will remain a democrat. = 他将继续是民主党人。
They will likely vote for the democratic nominee. = 他们很可能会投票给民主党的候选人。


虚拟语气:
If I were a democrat, I would vote differently. = 如果我是民主党人，我会投不同的票。
I wish he were a democrat so we could agree on policy. = 我希望他是民主党人，这样我们就能在政策上达成一致。


被动语态:
The candidate was supported by many democrats. = 许多民主党人支持这位候选人。
Democratic principles are often upheld by democrats. = 民主原则常常得到民主党人的维护。


疑问句:
Is he a democrat? = 他是民主党人吗？
Are they democrats? = 他们都是民主党人吗？


否定句:
