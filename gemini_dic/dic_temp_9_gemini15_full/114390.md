make a spectacle of oneself: /meɪk ə ˈspektəkəl əv wʌnˈsɛlf/ | v. phr. | 出丑；自取其辱；哗众取宠 | To behave in a very noticeable and embarrassing way, attracting unwanted attention. |


英文解析:This phrase describes actions that draw excessive attention to oneself in a negative or foolish manner.  It implies a display that is often embarrassing or ridiculous to the person involved and to observers.


例句:

He made a spectacle of himself at the party by dancing wildly and shouting obscenities. = 他在派对上疯狂跳舞并大喊粗口，出尽了洋相。

She made a spectacle of herself trying to climb the tree, only to fall down and scrape her knees. = 她试图爬树，结果却摔下来擦破了膝盖，真是自取其辱。


近义词:  embarrass oneself, make a fool of oneself, act the fool, cut a sorry figure, behave ridiculously


反义词:  behave discreetly, maintain composure, act with dignity, be inconspicuous


词性:  verb phrase (动词短语)

被动语态:  (This phrase is rarely used in the passive voice because it emphasizes the active role of the subject.)
