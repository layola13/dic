partisanship:/ˈpɑːrtɪzənʃɪp/| n. |党派性；偏袒；派系主义|Noun.  Strong support for a particular party, cause, or person, often without considering the facts objectively.  It often implies bias and a lack of neutrality.|例句：The intense partisanship in the political debate prevented any meaningful compromise.  激烈的党派之争阻碍了任何有意义的妥协。

例句：His partisanship was evident in his biased reporting. 他的偏袒在他的有偏见的报道中显而易见。


近义词：bias, prejudice, sectarianism, factionalism|反义词：neutrality, impartiality, objectivity|词性:名词
