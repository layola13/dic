liberties:/ˈlɪbərtiz/| n.|自由；特权；放肆；冒犯| freedoms granted by a government or by another authority, or freedoms of individual action and behavior.|例句：They took liberties with the facts. 他们歪曲了事实。
例句：He took the liberty of opening my letter. 他擅自拆开了我的信。|近义词：freedoms, rights, privileges|反义词：restrictions, constraints, limitations|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We enjoy many liberties in this country. = 我们在这个国家享有很多自由。
They often take liberties with the truth. = 他们经常歪曲事实。

一般过去时:
The prisoners were deprived of their liberties. = 囚犯被剥夺了自由。
He took liberties with the company car. = 他私自使用了公司的汽车。

现在完成时:
They have fought for their liberties for many years. = 他们为争取自由斗争多年了。
She has taken too many liberties with my patience. = 她太放肆了，已经耗尽了我的耐心。

过去完成时:
They had already lost many liberties before the revolution. = 在革命之前，他们就已经失去了许多自由。
He had taken such liberties before, so nobody was surprised. = 他以前就曾这么放肆过，所以没有人感到惊讶。

将来时:
They will fight for their liberties in the future. = 将来他们还会为争取自由而战。
She will regret taking such liberties with her boss. = 她会后悔对老板如此放肆的。

虚拟语气:
If we had more liberties, we would be happier. = 如果我们有更多的自由，我们会更快乐。
I wish I had the liberty to travel more often. = 我希望我有更多自由去旅行。

被动语态:
Their liberties were restricted during the war. = 战争期间他们的自由受到限制。
My feelings were hurt when he took those liberties. = 他那样放肆让我很受伤。

疑问句:
What liberties do we have as citizens? = 我们作为公民享有什么自由？
What liberties did he take with my property? = 他对我的财产做了什么过分的事？

否定句:
We don't have the liberty to do that. = 我们没有自由去做那件事。
They didn't take any liberties with the original text. = 他们没有随意改动原文。
