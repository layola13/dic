eke-out:/iːk aʊt/| vt.|勉强维持，使…勉强够用，补充|To supplement something; to make something last longer than it normally would.|They eked out a living on a small farm. 他们在小农场里勉强维持生计。|近义词：supplement, stretch, prolong |反义词：squander, waste


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They eke out a meager existence. = 他们勉强维持着贫困的生活。
We eke out our savings by taking on extra work. = 我们通过做额外的工作来勉强维持我们的储蓄。


一般过去时:
She eked out a living by selling handmade crafts. = 她靠卖手工制品勉强维持生计。
The family eked out their food supplies until the next harvest. = 这家人勉强维持他们的食物供应直到下一次收成。


现在完成时:
They have eked out a comfortable life for themselves. = 他们为自己创造了舒适的生活（虽然可能需要努力）。
We have eked out our resources carefully throughout the year. = 我们全年都谨慎地使用我们的资源。


过去完成时:
They had eked out a living on the land for generations. = 世世代代他们都在那片土地上艰难地生存。
She had eked out her small inheritance for several years. = 她已经勉强维持了她那点微薄的遗产好几年了。


将来时:
We will eke out a victory if we play strategically. = 如果我们有策略地比赛，我们将勉强取得胜利。
They will eke out a living in the city, despite the high cost of living. = 尽管生活成本很高，他们将在城市里勉强维持生计。


虚拟语气:
If we were more careful, we could eke out our resources for longer. = 如果我们更小心点，我们可以让我们的资源维持更长时间。
I wish we could eke out a better life for our children. = 我希望我们能为我们的孩子创造更好的生活。


被动语态:

Their resources were eked out carefully throughout the winter. = 他们的资源在整个冬天都被小心地使用。


疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "eke" comes from the Old English "ecian," meaning "to increase" or "to add to."  Over time, its meaning shifted to imply a struggling increase, a supplementing of something already meager.  The "out" in "eke out" emphasizes the act of extending or stretching something limited.
