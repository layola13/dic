essay:/ˈɛseɪ/ | n. | 论文；散文；尝试 | A short piece of writing on a particular subject.  Essays can be formal or informal, and they often present an argument or explore a topic in depth. | Example: She wrote an essay on the impact of social media. 她写了一篇关于社交媒体影响的论文。 |近义词：composition, treatise, paper |反义词：none


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He writes essays regularly. = 他定期写论文。
She usually submits her essays on time. = 她通常按时提交论文。

一般过去时:
I wrote an essay last night. = 我昨晚写了一篇论文。
They submitted their essays yesterday. = 他们昨天提交了论文。

现在完成时:
I have written three essays this week. = 我本周已经写了三篇论文。
She has never written an essay before. = 她以前从未写过论文。

过去完成时:
I had already written the essay before the deadline. = 我在截止日期之前就已经写完了论文。
They had finished their essays before the class started. = 课堂开始前他们就已经完成了论文。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish I had written a better essay. = 我希望我写了一篇更好的论文。

否定句:

词源:
The word "essay" comes from the French word "essai," meaning "attempt" or "trial."  It was first used in English in the 16th century by Michel de Montaigne, who popularized the essay form.  The word's origin reflects the exploratory and often tentative nature of the essay as a literary form.
