majorism:/ˈmeɪdʒərɪzəm/ | n. | 偏袒多数派；多数人统治；多数主义 | The belief in or practice of rule by the majority, often to the exclusion or detriment of minority interests.  It emphasizes the interests and perspectives of the majority, sometimes at the expense of minority rights and viewpoints. | Example: The government's policies showed a clear bias towards majorism, ignoring the concerns of marginalized communities.  政府的政策明显偏袒多数派，忽视了弱势群体的担忧。|近义词：majoritarianism, populism | 反义词：minority rights, pluralism
