scorpiodea:/ˌskɔːpiˈoʊdiə/| n. |蝎子类|The term "scorpiodea" refers to the order of arachnids that includes scorpions.  It encompasses a diverse group of predatory arthropods characterized by their segmented bodies, eight legs, and a venomous tail with a stinger.  They are found in various habitats worldwide, primarily in warm and arid regions. |Example:  The study focused on the venom composition of various species within the order Scorpiodea. 这项研究的重点是蝎子目各种物种的毒液成分。|近义词:scorpions (as a collective noun)|反义词: None specifically, but could contrast with non-arachnid predatory arthropods.|名词
