machaerodus: /ˌmækɪəˈroʊdəs/ | n. | 马氏剑齿虎 | An extinct genus of large predatory mammals belonging to the family Nimravidae, known for their prominent saber-like canine teeth.  Machaerodus species were similar in size and appearance to modern lions and tigers but possessed significantly longer and more curved canines. They were widespread across Eurasia and Africa during the Miocene and Pliocene epochs. |  The Machaerodus was a fearsome predator of its time. |  近义词：Smilodon (saber-toothed cat), Homotherium | 反义词：无  | 名词
