ropewalk:/ˈroʊpˌwɔːk/ | n. | 绳索厂；绳索制作；走钢丝 | A ropewalk is a long, narrow building where ropes and cables were traditionally made.  The process involved stretching the fibers along the length of the building to twist and ply them into rope.  The term can also refer to the act of walking a tightrope, a metaphorical challenge, or a difficult situation. | Example:  The old ropewalk, once a bustling hub of industry, now stands empty and derelict.  旧绳索厂，曾经是繁忙的工业中心，现在空无一人，破败不堪。 |The workers at the ropewalk skillfully twisted the hemp fibers into strong ropes. 绳索厂的工人熟练地将大麻纤维捻成结实的绳索。| 近义词：cordage factory, ropery | 反义词：无
