leads:/liːdz/ | n. v. |引导；领导；线索；开头；通向 |  The word "leads" can function as both a noun and a verb. As a noun, it refers to a path, clue, or advantage. As a verb, it means to guide or direct.  |例句：The detective followed the leads to find the criminal. (n.)  侦探顺着线索找到了罪犯。

The tour guide leads the group through the museum. (v.) 导游带领大家参观博物馆。


近义词:(n.) clues, hints, indications; (v.) guides, directs, manages |反义词:(v.) follows, misguides |词性:noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He leads the team to victory. 他带领团队取得胜利。
This road leads to the city center. 这条路通往市中心。

一般过去时:
She led the horse to the stable. 她把马带到马厩。
The path led to a hidden waterfall. 小路通向一个隐藏的瀑布。

现在完成时:
They have led a successful campaign. 他们已经领导了一场成功的运动。
This evidence has led to a new investigation.  这项证据导致了一项新的调查。

过去完成时:
He had led a difficult life before finding success.  在获得成功之前，他过着艰难的生活。
The clues had led them to a dead end. 线索把他们带到了一条死胡同。

将来时:
She will lead the meeting tomorrow. 她明天将主持会议。
This new technology will lead to many new opportunities. 这项新技术将带来许多新的机遇。

虚拟语气:
If he led the team, we would win. 如果他领导团队，我们会赢。
I wish I had led a more fulfilling life. 我希望我过着更有意义的生活。

被动语态:
The team was led to victory by their captain.  球队在队长的带领下取得了胜利。
The investigation is being led by a seasoned detective. 调查由一位经验丰富的侦探领导。

疑问句:
Who leads the project? 谁领导这个项目？
What leads to this conclusion?  是什么导致了这个结论？

否定句:

The evidence doesn't lead to a clear conclusion.  证据没有指向一个明确的结论。
