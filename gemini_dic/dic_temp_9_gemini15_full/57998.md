driver:/ˈdraɪvər/ | n. |司机，驾驶员；驱动程序；驱动力 | A person who drives a vehicle; a program that controls a device; a motivating force. |例句：He is a skilled driver. 他是一位熟练的司机。  |例句：The new printer driver is easy to install.  新的打印机驱动程序易于安装。|近义词：chauffeur, operator, motivator |反义词：passenger


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drives a taxi for a living. = 他靠开出租车为生。
She drives to work every day. = 她每天开车上班。

一般过去时:
He drove me to the airport. = 他开车送我去机场。
They drove through the countryside. = 他们开车穿过乡下。

现在完成时:
I have driven this car for five years. = 我开这辆车五年了。
She has driven thousands of miles. = 她已经开了数千英里。

过去完成时:
He had driven for hours before he stopped. = 他开了几个小时的车才停下来。
They had driven all night to get there. = 为了到达那里，他们开了整夜的车。

将来时:
I will drive you to the station tomorrow. = 我明天送你去车站。
He will drive the new car next week. = 他下周将驾驶新车。

虚拟语气:
If I had a car, I would drive to the beach. = 如果我有一辆车，我就会开车去海滩。
I wish I could drive a Formula 1 car. = 我希望我能开一级方程式赛车。

被动语态:
The car was driven into a tree. = 汽车撞到了一棵树上。
The truck is driven by a computer. = 卡车由计算机驱动。

疑问句:
Who drives this car? = 谁开的这辆车？
Does he drive a sports car? = 他开的是跑车吗？

否定句:
I don't drive. = 我不会开车。
He didn't drive fast enough. = 他开得不够快。
