galloper:/ˈɡæləpər/| n. |疾驰的人；快马；骑兵|A galloper is a person or horse that gallops, or a type of fast horse.  It can also refer to a type of fast-moving military unit.|例句：The cavalry galloper arrived with urgent news. 骑兵快马带来了紧急消息。
The racehorse was a powerful galloper. 这匹赛马是一匹强壮的快马。|近义词：rider, racer, horseman, cavalryman |反义词：walker, stroller


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gallops his horse across the field. 他骑着马飞奔过田野。
The horse gallops gracefully. 这匹马优雅地飞奔。

一般过去时:
The knight galloped into battle.  骑士纵马冲入战场。
She galloped along the beach. 她沿着海滩飞驰。

现在完成时:
He has galloped many miles today. 他今天已经跑了许多英里。
The children have galloped happily all afternoon. 孩子们快乐地飞奔了一下午。

过去完成时:
They had galloped for hours before reaching the village. 他们在到达村庄之前已经飞奔了几个小时。
The messenger had galloped across the plains before the storm broke. 信使在暴风雨来临之前已经飞奔过平原。

将来时:
She will gallop her horse in the race tomorrow. 她明天将骑马参加比赛。
The soldiers will gallop towards the enemy. 士兵们将向敌人飞奔。

虚拟语气:
If I had a horse, I would gallop across the countryside. 如果我有一匹马，我会飞奔穿越乡间。
I wish I could gallop like the wind. 我希望我能像风一样飞奔。

被动语态:
The horse was galloped hard by the rider.  这匹马被骑手猛烈地驱赶着飞奔。
(Note:  The passive voice is less common with "gallop".)


疑问句:
Does he gallop his horse every day? 他每天都骑马飞奔吗？
Will they gallop to victory? 他们会飞奔走向胜利吗？

否定句:
He does not gallop his horse carelessly. 他不会不小心地骑马飞奔。
The tired horse would not gallop any further.  疲惫的马不愿再飞奔了。
