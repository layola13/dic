pre-benefits:/ˌpriːˈbɛnɪfɪts/| n. | 预期利益；提前获得的利益 |  Pre-benefits refer to advantages or gains received before a particular event, action, or process is fully completed.  It often implies a reward or incentive offered in advance to encourage participation or performance.  | Example: The company offered pre-benefits to employees who completed the training program early.  公司为提前完成培训项目的员工提供提前获得的利益。|近义词：advance benefits, prior advantages, early rewards |反义词：post-benefits, delayed rewards, subsequent gains |名词
