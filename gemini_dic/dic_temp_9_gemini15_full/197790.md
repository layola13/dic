shine:/ʃaɪn/ | v., n. | 发光；照耀；擦亮 | To emit or reflect light; to make something bright by polishing. | 例句：The sun shines brightly today.  今天阳光灿烂。 The stars shine in the night sky. 星星在夜空中闪耀。 | 近义词：glitter, gleam, sparkle, polish | 反义词：dull, dim


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun shines every day. = 太阳每天都照耀。
The stars shine brightly at night. = 星星在夜晚闪耀。

一般过去时:
The sun shone brightly yesterday. = 昨天阳光灿烂。
She shone a light on the subject. = 她阐明了这个问题。

现在完成时:
The sun has shone all day. = 太阳一整天都在照耀。
He has shone a light on the issue. = 他已经阐明了这个问题。

过去完成时:
The sun had shone all morning before the rain started. = 下雨之前，太阳整个上午都在照耀。
She had shone the shoes before leaving. = 离开前她已经擦亮了鞋子。

将来时:
The sun will shine tomorrow. = 明天太阳会照耀。
I will shine my shoes before the interview. = 我会在面试前擦亮我的鞋子。

虚拟语气:
If the sun shone tomorrow, we would go to the beach. = 如果明天太阳照耀，我们就去海滩。
I wish the sun would shine more often. = 我希望太阳能经常照耀。

被动语态:
The floor was shone until it gleamed. = 地板被擦得闪闪发光。
The shoes were shone to a high gloss. = 鞋子被擦得非常亮。


疑问句:
Does the sun shine today? = 今天太阳照耀吗？
Does the metal shine after polishing? = 抛光后金属会发光吗？

否定句:
The sun doesn't shine today. = 今天太阳没有照耀。
The silver doesn't shine anymore. = 银子不再发光了。


PHRASE->
shine a light on = 阐明；揭示
shine through = 显露；透过…显现
take a shine to = 喜欢上；爱上
in the limelight = 成为众人瞩目的焦点
shine brightly = 闪耀；明亮
