slated:/ˈsleɪtɪd/ | adj., v. | 预定的；批评的；石板制的 |  Scheduled; criticized; made of slate. |


adj. 预定的; 计划中的;  批评的; 受批评的; 石板制的

The concert is slated for next Friday.  这场音乐会预定在下周五举行。

The new policy has been slated by critics.  这项新政策受到了评论家的批评。

The roof is made of slated tiles. 屋顶是用石板瓦做的。


v.  预定；计划；批评；攻击

The film is slated for release next month.  这部电影预定下个月发行。

The politician was slated for his handling of the crisis.  这位政治家因其处理危机的措施而受到批评。


近义词: adj. scheduled, planned, criticized; v. scheduled, planned, criticized, condemned


反义词: adj. unscheduled, unplanned, praised; v. praised, lauded


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The meeting is slated for tomorrow. = 会议预定在明天举行。
They are slated to appear in court next week. = 他们预定下周出庭。

一般过去时:
The project was slated for completion last year. = 该项目原计划去年完成。
The movie was slated by many critics. = 这部电影受到了许多影评人的批评。

现在完成时:
The book has been slated for publication. = 这本书已被预定出版。
The candidate has been slated by the opposition party. = 该候选人受到了反对党的批评。

过去完成时:
The event had been slated for a week earlier, but was postponed. = 活动原定在一周前举行，但被推迟了。
The article had been slated by the editor before publication. = 这篇文章在发表前已被编辑批评。

将来时:
The launch is slated to happen next quarter. = 产品发布计划在下个季度进行。
The show is slated to be a huge success. = 这场演出预计会大获成功。

虚拟语气:
If the project were slated for completion next month, we could meet the deadline. = 如果该项目计划在下个月完成，我们就能按时完成。
I wish the presentation hadn't been slated for so early in the morning. = 我希望演示文稿没有安排在早上这么早的时间。

被动语态:
The building is slated for demolition. = 这座建筑物将被拆除。
The new law was slated for immediate implementation. = 新法将立即实施。

疑问句:
Is the game slated for this weekend? = 这场比赛预定在本周末举行吗？
When is the book slated for release? = 这本书预定什么时候发行？

否定句:
The trip is not slated for this year. = 此次旅行今年没有计划。
The proposal was not slated for approval by the committee. = 该提案未获得委员会批准。
