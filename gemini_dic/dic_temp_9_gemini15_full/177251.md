reassembling:/ˌriːəˈsɛmblɪŋ/| v.|重新组装，重新聚集|The act of putting something back together again after it has been taken apart.  It can also refer to the gathering together of people or things that were previously separated.|例句：The mechanic is reassembling the engine after the repairs. 机械师正在修理后重新组装发动机。

例句：The scattered family members are reassembling for their annual reunion. 分散的家庭成员正在为他们的年度团聚而重新聚集。

近义词：reconstructing, putting back together, regrouping

反义词：disassembling, dismantling, scattering

词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reassemble the furniture every year. = 他们每年都重新组装家具。
The team reassembles after each game to discuss strategy. = 球队每次比赛后都会重新集会讨论策略。


一般过去时:
She reassembled the model airplane carefully. = 她仔细地重新组装了模型飞机。
The soldiers reassembled their ranks after the retreat. = 士兵们撤退后重新整队。


现在完成时:
They have reassembled the broken vase. = 他们已经重新组装了破碎的花瓶。
The committee has reassembled to discuss the new proposal. = 委员会已经重新集会讨论新的提案。


过去完成时:
He had reassembled the computer before I arrived. = 我到达之前他已经重新组装好了电脑。
The crowd had reassembled after the initial scare. = 初步恐慌之后人群重新聚集起来。


将来时:

The band will reassemble for their upcoming tour. = 乐队将为即将到来的巡演重新集结。


虚拟语气:
