catalyze:/ˈkætəlaɪz/ | vt. |催化；促成；激发 |To cause (a reaction or process) to occur more rapidly; to act as a catalyst.  | 例句：The discovery catalyzed further research in the field.  这项发现促进了该领域的进一步研究。 | 例句：The teacher's enthusiasm catalyzed the students' interest in science. 老师的热情激发了学生们对科学的兴趣。| 近义词：accelerate, expedite, trigger, spur | 反义词：inhibit, hinder, impede | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The enzyme catalyzes the reaction. = 这种酶催化该反应。
Heat catalyzes the chemical process. = 热量促进了化学过程。

一般过去时:
The event catalyzed significant social change. = 此事件促成了重大的社会变革。
The meeting catalyzed a new agreement. = 会议促成了一项新的协议。

现在完成时:
The new policy has catalyzed economic growth. = 新政策促进了经济增长。
His speech has catalyzed a national debate. = 他的演讲引发了一场全国性的辩论。

过去完成时:
The previous discovery had catalyzed the current research. = 此前的发现促成了当前的研究。
The initial investment had catalyzed the company's expansion. = 初始投资促进了公司的扩张。

将来时:
The new technology will catalyze a revolution in manufacturing. = 新技术将引发制造业的革命。
The project will catalyze collaboration between researchers. = 该项目将促进研究人员之间的合作。

虚拟语气:
If the government invested more, it would catalyze innovation. = 如果政府投入更多资金，它将促进创新。
I wish the new program would catalyze positive change. = 我希望新计划能够带来积极的改变。

被动语态:
The reaction was catalyzed by a specific enzyme. = 该反应是由一种特定的酶催化的。
Economic growth was catalyzed by government reforms. = 经济增长是由政府改革促成的。

疑问句:
What factors catalyzed the industrial revolution? = 哪些因素促成了工业革命？
What can we do to catalyze change in our community? = 我们能做些什么来促进我们社区的变革？

否定句:
The lack of funding did not catalyze research. = 缺乏资金并没有促进研究。
The program failed to catalyze any significant improvement. = 该计划未能带来任何重大改进。
