pecopteris:/pɛkˈɒptərɪs/ | n. |鱗木蕨屬|A genus of extinct ferns characterized by their pinnate (feather-like) fronds with small, closely spaced leaflets.  These fossils are common in Carboniferous and Permian rocks, providing valuable insights into ancient plant life and paleoclimates.|  Examples of Pecopteris fossils have been found in various locations across the globe.| 近义词:  (paleobotanical terms needed for true synonymy, as it refers to a specific genus - consider Alethopteris, Neuropteris for similar fossil fern types) | 反义词: (no direct antonym exists)| 名词
