deadlight:/ˈdedˌlaɪt/| n.|暗淡的光线；舷窗；防爆灯；|A deadlight is a strong, opaque, usually glazed cover fitted over a ship's porthole or window to prevent the entry of water or light.  It can also refer to a strong light that is not bright or cheerful, or a small, strong light used in an emergency.|例句：The storm raged, and the deadlights were secured to protect the ship from the waves.|近义词：skylight, portlight|反义词：bright light, sunlight
