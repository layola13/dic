cheeseburgers:/ˈtʃiːzbɜːrɡərz/ | n. |芝士汉堡(复数) | A cheeseburger is a hamburger topped with cheese.  It's a popular fast food item consisting of a patty of ground beef (or other meat substitute) cooked on a griddle, placed inside a bun, and topped with cheese, along with other condiments such as lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, and various sauces. |例句：I ordered two cheeseburgers and a large fries. 我点了两个芝士汉堡和一大份薯条。

例句：The restaurant is famous for its juicy cheeseburgers. 这家餐馆以其多汁的芝士汉堡而闻名。


近义词：burgers,  sandwiches (a broader category) | 反义词：None (it's a specific type of food) | 词性: 名词

词源:  The word "cheeseburger" is a simple combination of "cheese" and "hamburger."  "Hamburger" itself has a complex etymology, possibly originating from Hamburg, Germany, where a type of minced beef was popular. The addition of "cheese" simply specifies the inclusion of this topping.
