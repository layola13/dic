embolisms:/ˈɛmbəlɪzəm/| n.|栓塞；阻塞|The obstruction of a blood vessel by an embolus (a blood clot, air bubble, fat globule, etc.).  Embolisms can occur in arteries or veins and can lead to serious complications, such as stroke or heart attack, depending on the location and size of the blockage. |例句：A pulmonary embolism is a serious condition that occurs when a blood clot travels to the lungs. = 肺栓塞是一种严重的疾病，发生时血凝块会到达肺部。

The sudden blockage of a blood vessel by a foreign substance, such as a blood clot, fat globule, air bubble, or piece of tumor. =  由异物（如血凝块、脂肪球、气泡或肿瘤碎片）突然阻塞血管。


近义词：blockage, occlusion, obstruction|反义词：None specifically, but terms suggesting open flow could be considered opposites (e.g., perfusion).|词性:noun
