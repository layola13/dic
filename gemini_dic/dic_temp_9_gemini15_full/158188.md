platonicism:/pləˈtɑːnɪzəm/ | n. | 柏拉图主义 | The philosophical system developed by Plato and his followers, emphasizing the existence of abstract Forms or Ideas as the true objects of knowledge and the source of reality.  Platonic thought is characterized by its emphasis on reason, intellect, and the pursuit of knowledge as a path to understanding the true nature of reality. It contrasts with empiricism, which emphasizes experience and observation. |  Plato's theory of Forms is a central tenet of Platonicism.  His dialogues, such as *The Republic*, explore many aspects of Platonic thought. | idealism, rationalism | empiricism, materialism | 名词
