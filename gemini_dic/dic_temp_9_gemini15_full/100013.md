interpenetration:/ˌɪntərˌpɛnəˈtreɪʃən/| n. |相互渗透；互相影响|The complex interpenetration of cultures in the city creates a unique atmosphere. 城市中多种文化的相互渗透创造了一种独特的氛围。|近义词：intermingling, interaction, fusion|反义词：separation, isolation|名词


英文解析:The action or process of penetrating or being penetrated mutually; reciprocal penetration.  It suggests a two-way process where two or more things influence and affect each other deeply, often resulting in a blending or fusion of elements.


例句：

The interpenetration of art and technology is a defining characteristic of the 21st century.  艺术与技术的相互渗透是21世纪的标志性特征。


The essay explores the interpenetration of religious and political power in medieval Europe. 这篇文章探讨了中世纪欧洲宗教与政治权力之间的相互渗透。


词源:

* **inter-**:  prefix meaning "between," "among," or "mutually."
* **penetra-**: from Latin "penetrare," meaning "to penetrate," "to pierce."
* **-tion**: suffix forming nouns of action or process.


USAGE->

This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like a verb.  Therefore, tense considerations are not applicable.  However, it can be used in various grammatical structures depending on the sentence.
