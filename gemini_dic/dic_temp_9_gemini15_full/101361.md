ionising: /ˈaɪənaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 电离的，使电离的 | Causing or capable of causing ionization.  The process of ionization involves the removal or addition of electrons from atoms or molecules, resulting in the formation of ions. |  例句：Ionising radiation is a significant health hazard.  电离辐射是一种严重的健康危害。

例句：The experiment involved exposing the sample to ionising radiation. 实验包括将样品暴露于电离辐射。


近义词：ionogenic, radiating (in the context of radiation)

反义词：non-ionizing


词源:  From "ionize" + "-ing".  "Ionize" itself comes from "ion" (from Greek ἰόν, ion, "going," from the verb ἰέναι, ienai, "to go") + "-ize" (a suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become").  The suffix "-ing" creates the present participle, used adjectivally here.
