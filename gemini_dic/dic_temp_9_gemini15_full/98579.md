institutionalizes: /ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使制度化；使成为制度 | To establish something as a norm or custom within an institution or society.  |例句：The government institutionalized the new policy. 政府将新政策制度化了。 |例句：They institutionalized the practice of regular meetings. 他们将定期会议的惯例制度化了。|近义词：formalize, systematize, codify |反义词：informalize, abolish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company institutionalizes its ethical guidelines. = 公司将其实施道德准则制度化。
They institutionalize new technologies in their workflow. = 他们将其工作流程中的新技术制度化。


一般过去时:
The school institutionalized a new dress code. = 学校将新的着装规范制度化了。
The company institutionalized its environmental protection policies last year. = 公司去年将其实施环境保护政策制度化了。


现在完成时:
Society has institutionalized many discriminatory practices. = 社会已经将许多歧视性做法制度化了。
They have institutionalized a culture of innovation. = 他们已经将创新文化制度化了。

否定句:

词源:
The word "institutionalizes" derives from the noun "institution" (itself from Latin *instituere, meaning "to establish, set up")  and the suffix "-ize," indicating "to make or become."  Therefore, it literally means "to make into an institution" or "to establish as an institution."
