overgeneralization:/ˌoʊvərˌdʒɛnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 概括过度；过度泛化 | The act of drawing a conclusion that is too broad or general from specific instances; applying a rule or pattern too widely. It often involves making assumptions based on limited evidence or experience.  | Example:  He had a bad experience with one dog, leading to an overgeneralization that all dogs are aggressive. 他和一只狗有过不好的经历，导致他过度概括认为所有狗都很凶猛。 | 近义词: generalization, sweeping statement, oversimplification | 反义词:  specificity, nuance, precision
