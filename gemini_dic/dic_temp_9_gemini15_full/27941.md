/kɔːʃən/ | n. |警告；告诫；小心 | A caution is a warning about a possible danger or problem.  It advises someone to be careful. | Example: The doctor gave him a caution about the risks of smoking. 医生告诫他吸烟的危害。|近义词：warning, admonition, advice |反义词：encouragement, permission |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He issues a caution to the driver. = 他向司机发出了警告。
The sign displays a caution about the wet floor. = 标志显示地板湿滑的警告。

一般过去时:
She received a caution from her boss. = 她受到了老板的警告。
The police gave him a caution for speeding. = 警察因超速对他进行了警告。

现在完成时:
They have issued several cautions about the impending storm. = 他们已经发布了几次关于即将到来的风暴的警告。
The school has cautioned parents about the dangers of bullying. = 学校已经告诫家长们欺凌的危险。

过去完成时:
The teacher had cautioned the students about cheating on the exam. = 老师已经告诫学生不要在考试中作弊。
She had cautioned him against making hasty decisions. = 她曾告诫他不要草率做决定。

将来时:
The government will caution citizens about the health risks. = 政府将告诫公民注意健康风险。
They will caution against the use of unsafe chemicals. = 他们将告诫不要使用不安全的化学品。

虚拟语气:
If I had heeded the caution, I wouldn't have made that mistake. = 如果我听了警告，我就不会犯那个错误了。
I wish he had paid attention to the cautionary tale. = 我真希望他注意那个警示故事。

被动语态:
The workers were cautioned about the safety regulations. = 工人们受到了关于安全规章的警告。
The public was cautioned to stay indoors during the hurricane. = 公众被警告在飓风期间待在室内。

疑问句:
Did you receive a caution about the project deadline? = 你是否收到了关于项目截止日期的警告？
Has the company issued any cautions regarding the new policy? = 公司是否就新政策发布了任何警告？

否定句:
He didn't heed the caution and got into trouble. = 他没有理会警告，结果惹上了麻烦。
The driver ignored the caution and continued driving recklessly. = 司机无视警告，继续鲁莽驾驶。
