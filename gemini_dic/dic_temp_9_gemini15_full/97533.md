ingrain:/ɪnˈɡreɪn/| vt. adj.|  使根深蒂固；固有的； ingrained | to fix firmly and deeply | 例句：These values are ingrained in our culture. 这些价值观根深蒂固地融入我们的文化中。  |近义词：implant, inculcate, embed |反义词：eradicate, remove, uproot |动词，形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ingrains his lessons well. = 他把功课牢牢记住了。
They ingrain positive values in their children. = 他们把积极的价值观灌输给他们的孩子。


一般过去时:
She ingrained the poem in her memory. = 她把这首诗牢牢记在心里。
The experience ingrained a deep distrust in him. = 那次经历在他心中种下了深深的不信任。


现在完成时:
Years of practice have ingrained good habits in him. = 多年的实践在他身上养成了良好的习惯。
The message has been ingrained in the public consciousness. = 这条信息已经深入人心。


过去完成时:
He had ingrained the rules into his behavior before the competition. = 他在比赛前已将规则铭记于心。
They had ingrained the principles of honesty throughout their lives. = 他们一生都将诚实的原则铭刻于心。


将来时:
They will ingrain their knowledge into the next generation. = 他们将把他们的知识传承给下一代。
This experience will ingrain a sense of responsibility in him.  = 这段经历将会在他心中培养起责任感。


虚拟语气:
If they ingrained these values earlier, the children would be better. = 如果他们早点灌输这些价值观，孩子们就会更好。
If he had ingrained the lessons, he would have passed the exam. = 如果他把功课记牢了，他就通过考试了。


被动语态:
The principles are ingrained into the company culture. = 这些原则已经融入公司文化中。
The message was ingrained deeply in their minds. = 这条信息深深地印在他们的脑海里。


疑问句:
Is this belief ingrained in your culture? = 这种信仰在你们的文化中根深蒂固吗？
Were these values ingrained in you during your childhood? = 这些价值观是在你童年时期就根深蒂固的吗？


否定句:
He didn't ingrain the information well enough. = 他没有把信息记牢。
They failed to ingrain the importance of discipline. = 他们未能灌输纪律的重要性。
