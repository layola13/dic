philological:/ˌfɪləˈlɒdʒɪkəl/|/ˌfɪləˈlɑːdʒɪkəl/ | adj. |语言学的；语法的；文献学的| Relating to the study of language in its historical and cultural development.  The adjective philological describes something related to philology, the study of language in its historical and cultural context. This includes aspects of linguistics, literature, and the history of writing systems. |Example: His philological analysis revealed the ancient origins of the word.  他的语言学分析揭示了这个词的古老起源。

Example: The professor's philological research focused on the evolution of Old English grammar. 教授的语言学研究集中在古英语语法的演变上。


近义词：linguistic, grammatical, literary

反义词：None (no direct antonym, but opposites could be implied depending on context, e.g., "non-linguistic")


词性：形容词
