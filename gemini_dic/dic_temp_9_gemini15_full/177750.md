recarved:/ˌriːˈkɑːrvd/| v. |重新雕刻，再雕琢|To carve again; to carve anew.  This implies a previous carving that is being modified or improved upon.  It suggests a more refined or detailed process than simply carving.|例句：The sculptor recarved the statue's face, adding more detail to the expression.  雕塑家重新雕刻了雕像的脸，增加了更多的表情细节。
