lousewort:/ˈlaʊsˌwɜːrt/| n. | 咬舌头草；母草 | A plant of the genus Pedicularis, parasitic on the roots of other plants.  Louseworts are characterized by their oddly shaped flowers, often with a hooded appearance. They are known for their ability to absorb nutrients from their host plants. | The lousewort thrived in the damp meadow, clinging to the roots of nearby grasses.  咬舌头草在潮湿的草地上茁壮成长，依附在附近草的根部。 |  parasite,  root parasite |  (No direct antonym;  perhaps "host plant") | 名词
