slicing:/ˈslaɪsɪŋ/| n. v.|切片；切，割|The act of cutting something into slices;  the process of dividing something into portions.  It can also refer to the resulting slices. As a verb, it means to cut something into slices.|例句：The slicing of the tomatoes was quick and efficient.  西红柿切片很快很有效率。

The chef expertly sliced the roast.  厨师熟练地切了烤肉。|近义词：cutting, dicing, segmenting, portioning|反义词：joining, uniting, merging|词性:noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slices the bread thinly. = 他把面包切得很薄。
She slices the cake into eight pieces. = 她把蛋糕切成八块。

一般过去时:
I sliced the onions for the soup. = 我把洋葱切碎做汤。
He sliced his finger while chopping vegetables. = 他切菜时割破了手指。

现在完成时:
She has sliced all the vegetables for the salad. = 她已经把沙拉的所有蔬菜都切好了。
They have sliced the pizza into six slices. = 他们把披萨切成了六块。

过去完成时:
The chef had sliced the meat before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，厨师已经切好了肉。
She had sliced the bread before making the sandwiches. = 在做三明治之前，她已经切好了面包。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If I had a sharper knife, I would slice the bread more easily. = 如果我有把更锋利的刀，我会更容易地切面包。

被动语态:
The bread is sliced thinly. = 面包被切得很薄。

疑问句:
