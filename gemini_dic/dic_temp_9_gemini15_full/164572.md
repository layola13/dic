preexceptional: /ˌpriːɪkˈsɛpʃənəl/ | adj. | 异常前的；预示异常的 |  Describing something that occurs before an exceptional event, often foreshadowing or indicating its arrival.  It suggests a period or state preceding a significant departure from the norm.  The word combines "pre-" (before) and "exceptional" (unusual, outstanding). | Example:  The preexceptional tremors were a warning sign of the impending earthquake.  预兆地震的震颤是即将发生地震的预警信号。|近义词：antecedent, preliminary, preceding, precursory | 反义词：subsequent, following, ensuing |  词性：形容词
