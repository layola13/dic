quarkonium: /ˌkwɑːrˈkoʊniəm/ | n. |夸克偶素| A quarkonium is a meson composed of a quark and its antiquark.  These particles are bound together by the strong force, a fundamental force in nature.  Different types of quarkonium exist depending on the quark and antiquark involved (e.g., charmonium, bottomonium).  The study of quarkonium provides valuable insights into quantum chromodynamics (QCD), the theory describing the strong interaction. | Example:  The discovery of new quarkonium states helps refine our understanding of QCD.  夸克偶素的新发现有助于我们完善对量子色动力学的理解。| 近义词: meson (though not all mesons are quarkonia) | 反义词:  None directly, but baryons (composed of three quarks) are a contrasting particle type.
