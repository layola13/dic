causatively:/ˈkɔːzətɪvli/ | adv. | 导致地；引起地 | In a way that causes something to happen.  It describes the manner in which something produces a result. |  The rain causatively flooded the streets.  雨导致街道被淹。 The teacher causatively motivated the students to study harder. 老师促使学生们更努力学习。 |  effectively, resultantly | ineffectively, unproductively | 副词


USAGE->

This word is an adverb, so it doesn't have tenses like verbs.  Its usage depends on the verb it modifies.  Here are examples showing how it functions in different sentence structures:

Declarative Sentence:
The manager causatively implemented new policies. = 经理有效地实施了新政策。
The new law causatively reduced pollution levels. = 新法有效地降低了污染水平。


Interrogative Sentence:
Did the medicine causatively alleviate his symptoms? = 这药是否有效地缓解了他的症状？
Did the drought causatively impact crop yields? = 干旱是否对作物产量产生了影响？


Exclamatory Sentence:
How causatively effective the new marketing campaign was! = 新的营销活动多么有效！
How causatively devastating the storm was! = 暴风雨破坏力多么巨大！


Negative Sentence:
The advertisement did not causatively increase sales. = 广告并没有有效地增加销量。
The treatment did not causatively cure the disease. = 治疗并没有有效地治愈这种疾病。


Conditional Sentence:
If the government acted decisively, it could causatively improve the economy. = 如果政府果断行动，它就能有效地改善经济。
If we had invested more, we could have causatively increased profits. = 如果我们投资更多，我们本来可以有效地增加利润。


PHRASE->

There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "causatively."  Its usage is typically within a sentence modifying a verb.  However, we can illustrate its use with phrases:
