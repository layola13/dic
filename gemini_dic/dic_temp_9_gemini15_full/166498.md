presageful: /ˈprɛsədʒfʊl/ | adj. | 预兆的；预示不幸的 |


英文解析:  Suggesting or indicating something bad that is going to happen in the future; ominous.  It implies a feeling of foreboding or apprehension about a future event, often a negative one.


例句:

The presageful silence before the storm unnerved everyone.  暴风雨前的沉寂预示着不祥，让每个人都感到不安。

His presageful words proved tragically accurate. 他预言般的话语悲惨地应验了。


近义词: ominous, portentous, foreboding, threatening, inauspicious


反义词: auspicious, propitious, promising, encouraging


词源:  From "presage" (verb meaning to foretell or indicate) + "-ful" (suffix meaning "full of").  "Presage" itself comes from Old French *presager  (to foretell), ultimately from Latin *praesagire (to feel beforehand).
