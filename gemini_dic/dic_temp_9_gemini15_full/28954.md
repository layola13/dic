carry-on:/ˈkæriɔːn/|/ˈkæriɑːn/| n. adj. v.|随身携带的行李；继续；携带|

英文解析:As a noun, it refers to luggage that is small enough to be taken onto an aircraft or other form of public transport. As an adjective, it describes something that continues without interruption. As a verb, it means to transport or convey something.

例句:
n.  I only have a carry-on bag for this trip. = 我这次旅行只带了一个随身行李箱。
n.  The flight attendant asked passengers to check their carry-on baggage. = 空乘人员要求乘客托运他们的随身行李。

adj.  The meeting continued in a carry-on manner. = 会议持续进行，没有中断。
adj.  This is a carry-on project, we cannot stop it midway. = 这是一个持续进行的项目，我们不能中途停止。

v.  He carried on the family business. = 他继续经营家族企业。
v.  Please carry on with your work. = 请继续你的工作。


近义词:
n. hand luggage, personal baggage
adj. continuing, ongoing
v. continue, proceed, transport


反义词:
n. checked baggage
adj. discontinued, interrupted
v. cease, stop


词性:noun, adjective, verb
