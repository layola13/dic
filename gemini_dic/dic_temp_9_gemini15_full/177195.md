rearranged:/ˌriːəˈreɪndʒd/ | v. |重新安排，改组 | Past tense and past participle of "rearrange".  It means to change the position or order of things. |例句：The furniture was rearranged to make more space.  家具被重新摆放以腾出更多空间。
The schedule was rearranged after the unexpected snowstorm.  因为突如其来的暴风雪，日程安排被重新调整了。|近义词：reorganize, restructure, reorder |反义词：disorganize, jumble


USAGE->

They rearrange the furniture every year. = 他们每年都重新安排家具。
She rearranges her bookshelf regularly. = 她定期整理她的书架。

He rearranged the files on his desk. = 他重新整理了他桌上的文件。
