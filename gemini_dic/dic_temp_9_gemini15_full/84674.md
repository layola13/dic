handcuff: /ˈhændˌkʌf/| n. vt.| 手铐；用械铐住 | Metal restraints used to fasten a person's wrists together.  Handcuffs are typically used by law enforcement to restrain suspects. |例句：The police handcuffed the suspect. 警察给嫌疑犯戴上手铐。

The thief was found with handcuffs on. 小偷被发现戴着手铐。

近义词：shackles, restraints | 反义词：freedom, liberty | 词性: 名词，动词

词源:
The word "handcuff" is a compound word, combining "hand" and "cuff."  "Cuff" itself comes from the Middle English word "cuffe," meaning a blow or a blow with the hand, then later referring to a fastening around the wrist.  The combination thus suggests a restraint fastened around the hand.
