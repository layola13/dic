interview:/ˈɪntərvjuː/| n. vt.|面试；采访；会见|A formal meeting in which someone is asked questions to assess their suitability for a job or other position, or to collect information for a news story.  |例句：I have a job interview tomorrow. 我明天有一个工作面试。

例句：The journalist conducted an interview with the president.  记者对总统进行了采访。


近义词：meeting, audition, questioning|反义词：N/A|词性：n. vt.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He interviews candidates every Tuesday. 他每周二面试候选人。
She interviews witnesses for her stories. 她为她的报道采访证人。


一般过去时:
They interviewed me for the marketing position. 他们就市场营销职位面试了我。
The police interviewed several suspects.  警方讯问了几名嫌疑人。


现在完成时:
I have interviewed over 100 people for this role.  为了这个职位，我已经面试了100多人。
She has interviewed many famous actors. 她已经采访过许多著名演员。


过去完成时:
The reporter had interviewed the source before writing the article. 记者在撰写文章之前已经采访过消息来源。
They had interviewed all the applicants before making a decision. 他们在做出决定之前已经面试了所有申请人。


将来时:
I will interview the CEO next week. 我下周将采访首席执行官。
They will interview candidates throughout the month. 他们将在整个月面试候选人。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would prepare thoroughly for the interview. 如果我是你，我会为面试做好充分准备。
I wish I had interviewed for that position. 我真希望我申请过那个职位。


被动语态:
The candidates were interviewed by a panel of experts. 候选人由专家小组进行面试。
Many people were interviewed for the documentary. 许多人接受了这部纪录片的采访。


疑问句:
When will you be interviewed? 你什么时候面试？
Have you ever been interviewed for television? 你曾经接受过电视采访吗？


否定句:
They didn't interview her for the job. 他们没有让她做这份工作。
He hasn't been interviewed yet. 他还没有接受采访。


PHRASE->
job interview 工作面试
interview for a job 申请工作
conduct an interview 进行面试
panel interview 小组面试
prepare for an interview 准备面试
