reconsecrating: /ˌriːˈkɒnsɪkreɪtɪŋ/ | v. |重新奉献；再次祝圣 |The act of consecrating something again;  to dedicate something anew to sacred purposes.  This often involves a religious ceremony or ritual. |例句：The church was reconsecrated after extensive renovations.  教堂经过大规模翻修后重新进行了祝圣仪式。


The old altar was reconsecrated following its restoration. 修复后，古老的祭坛重新进行了祝圣。


近义词：redeicate, rededicate, rehallow


反义词：desecrate, profane


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reconsecrate the temple every year. = 他们每年都重新奉献神庙。
The priest reconsecrates the chalice after each mass. = 每场弥撒后，牧师都会重新祝圣圣杯。


一般过去时:
They reconsecrated the building after the fire. = 火灾之后，他们重新奉献了这座建筑物。
The bishop reconsecrated the land for religious use. = 主教将这块土地重新用于宗教用途。


现在完成时:
The church has reconsecrated its altar. = 教堂已经重新奉献了它的祭坛。
They have reconsecrated the statue to a new patron saint. = 他们已将雕像重新奉献给一位新的守护神。


过去完成时:
The priest had reconsecrated the church before the ceremony. = 牧师在仪式开始前已重新祝圣了教堂。
They had reconsecrated the land before building the new temple. = 他们在建造新庙宇之前已经重新奉献了这块土地。

虚拟语气:
