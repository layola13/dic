poison:/ˈpɔɪzən/|/ˈpɔɪzn/| n., v.|毒药；毒害；使中毒|a substance that is capable of causing the illness or death of a living organism when introduced or absorbed.|The snake injected its poison into its victim. 那条蛇将毒液注射到受害者体内。


He was poisoned by a deadly toxin. 他被一种剧毒物质毒死了。|toxin, venom, toxin, venom|antidote, cure|noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The plant poisons many insects.  这种植物毒害许多昆虫。
This chemical poisons the water supply.  这种化学物质污染了水源。


一般过去时:
The farmer poisoned the rats.  这位农民毒死了老鼠。
They poisoned the well. 他们毒害了井水。


现在完成时:
The contaminated food has poisoned several people. 受污染的食物已经毒害了几个人。
They have poisoned the river with industrial waste. 他们用工业废物污染了河流。


过去完成时:
She had poisoned the drink before he arrived. 她在他到达之前已经毒害了饮料。
The investigators discovered that he had been poisoned slowly over several weeks.  调查人员发现他数周来一直在慢慢中毒。


将来时:
They will poison the crops if we don't stop them.  如果我们不阻止他们，他们将毒害庄稼。
The terrorists plan to poison the city's water supply.  恐怖分子计划毒害城市的水源。


虚拟语气:
If I had known the berries were poisonous, I wouldn't have eaten them. 如果我知道浆果有毒，我就不会吃了。
If they poisoned the water, many people would die. 如果他们毒害了水，很多人都会死。


被动语态:

疑问句:
Was the food poisoned? 食物中毒了吗？

否定句:
The food wasn't poisoned. 食物没有中毒。
He didn't poison the dog. 他没有毒死那条狗。
