iambic:/aɪˈæmbɪk/| adj.|抑扬格的；抑扬格音步的| Relating to or characterized by iambs, metrical feet consisting of an unstressed syllable followed by a stressed syllable.  Iambic pentameter, for example, is a common metrical pattern in English poetry, consisting of five iambs per line. |Example: Shakespeare's sonnets are famous for their iambic pentameter.  莎士比亚的十四行诗以其抑扬格五音步而闻名。|近义词：trochaic (反义，指抑强格)|反义词：trochaic
