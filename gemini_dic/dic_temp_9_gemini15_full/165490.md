preloss:/ˈpriːlɒs/| n.|预损失，预期损失|Preloss refers to the estimated or projected losses that may occur before an insurance claim is filed. It is often used in the context of insurance and risk management to quantify potential financial harm before an event happens.|例句：The company conducted a preloss assessment to determine the potential impact of a hurricane.|近义词：anticipated loss, projected loss, estimated loss|反义词：postloss
