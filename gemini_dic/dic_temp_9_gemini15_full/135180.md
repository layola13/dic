nonoptionally:/ˌnɒnˈɒpʃənəli/ | adv. |非选择性地，强制性地 |  This adverb indicates that something is mandatory or required; it cannot be omitted or chosen otherwise.  It emphasizes the lack of choice or option. |  Attendance at the meeting is nonoptionally; everyone must be present.  会议出席是强制性的；每个人都必须到场。|近义词: mandatorily, compulsorily, necessarily |反义词: optionally, voluntarily


USAGE->
This word is an adverb and doesn't have tenses like verbs.  It only modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.  Its meaning remains consistent across different sentence structures.


例句1: He nonoptionally agreed to the terms. 他被迫同意了这些条款。(The lack of choice is emphasized)

例句2: The software update was nonoptionally installed. 软件更新是强制安装的。(It couldn't be avoided)

PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases using "nonoptionally".  Its usage is fairly straightforward and limited to conveying the mandatory nature of an action or condition.
