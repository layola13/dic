receivables: /rɪˈsiːvəbəlz/ | n. | 应收款 | Money owed to a business by its customers for goods or services sold on credit.  Receivables are considered assets on a company's balance sheet. |  The company's receivables increased significantly last quarter.  这家公司的应收账款上个季度显著增加。


The company is working to reduce its outstanding receivables. 这家公司正在努力减少未收回的应收账款。


|近义词:accounts receivable, outstanding debts |反义词:payables|词性:名词

词源:
The word "receivables" originates from the verb "receive," meaning to get or obtain something.  The suffix "-able" indicates something that can be received, and the "-s" denotes plurality.  Therefore, "receivables" refers to the total amount of money that is receivable, or owed to a business.
