nontolerably: /ˌnɒnˈtɒlərəbli/ | adv. |无法忍受地，极其|  extremely; to a degree that is difficult or impossible to tolerate. |例句：The heat was nontolerably high.  这热得让人无法忍受。|  The noise was nontolerably loud.  噪音大得无法忍受。|近义词：unbearably, intolerably, excessively| 反义词：tolerably, bearably


USAGE->
This word is an adverb and doesn't have verb conjugations like "What" in the example.  It describes the degree of something.


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "nontolerably."  Its use is primarily as a standalone adverb modifying adjectives or verbs describing unpleasant experiences.
