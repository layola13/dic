firebrand: /ˈfaɪərˌbrænd/ | n., adj. |煽动者；火爆分子；煽动性强的 |

英文解析: A firebrand is a person who is passionate and outspoken in their beliefs, often causing trouble or stirring up conflict. As an adjective, it describes something that is inflammatory or provocative.  The word originates from the literal meaning of a piece of burning wood used to start a fire, hence the association with igniting conflict or strong feelings.


例句:

He was a firebrand, always ready to start an argument. = 他是个煽动者，总是随时准备吵架。

The speech was a firebrand of nationalist sentiment. = 这篇演讲煽动了民族主义情绪。


近义词: agitator, incendiary, troublemaker, rabble-rouser, inflammatory


反义词: peacemaker, conciliator, appeaser, moderate


词性: noun, adjective
