interhybridized:/ˌɪntərˈhaɪbrɪdaɪzd/| v. | 杂交；互相杂交 | To interhybridize means to hybridize (crossbreed) two or more different species or varieties.  This process often results in offspring with traits from both parents. | Example: The researchers interhybridized two distinct strains of wheat to create a new, more resilient variety.  研究人员将两种不同的小麦品种杂交，培育出一种新的、更具抗性的品种。 |近义词：crossbreed, hybridize |反义词：separate, isolate |动词
