just:/dʒʌst/| adv. adj.|仅仅，刚才，只是；公平的，公正的|The word "just" is an adverb and sometimes an adjective. As an adverb, it has many meanings, including "only," "simply," "exactly," "recently," "barely," and "now." As an adjective, it means "fair" or "upright."|例句：I just saw him. (我刚刚看到他了。)  He's just a child. (他只是一个孩子。) |近义词：(adv.) merely, only, simply, exactly, recently; (adj.) fair, equitable|反义词：(adv.) completely, entirely, fully; (adj.) unfair, unjust


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I just want to help. = 我只想帮忙。
She just needs a little rest. = 她只需要稍微休息一下。


一般过去时:
I just saw him. = 我刚刚看见他了。
We just finished the project. = 我们刚刚完成了项目。


现在完成时:
I have just finished my work. = 我刚刚完成我的工作。
She has just arrived. = 她刚刚到达。


过去完成时:
I had just left when the phone rang. = 我刚离开电话就响了。
They had just eaten when the guests arrived. = 客人到达时他们刚吃完饭。


将来时:
I will just be a moment. = 我马上就来。
We will just go for a walk. = 我们只是去散散步。


虚拟语气:
If I just had more time, I would finish it. = 如果我再多一点时间，我就能完成它了。
I wish I just knew the answer. = 我真希望知道答案。


被动语态:
The work has just been finished. = 工作刚刚完成。

疑问句:

Are you just kidding? = 你只是开玩笑吗？


否定句:
It's not just a game. = 它不仅仅是一个游戏。
That's not just what I heard. = 那不是我听到的全部。
