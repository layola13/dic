prohibit:/prəˈhɪbɪt/ | vt. |禁止，阻止 | to forbid something, especially by law or official rule.  |例句：Smoking is prohibited in this building.  吸烟在此建筑物内禁止。
The law prohibits the sale of alcohol to minors. 法律禁止向未成年人出售酒精。|近义词：forbid, ban, prevent |反义词：allow, permit |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The school prohibits the use of cell phones. 学校禁止使用手机。
The city prohibits parking in this area.  城市禁止在这个区域停车。


一般过去时:
The government prohibited the import of certain goods. 政府禁止进口某些商品。
They prohibited me from entering the room. 他们禁止我进入房间。


现在完成时:
The company has prohibited the use of social media during work hours. 公司已禁止在工作时间使用社交媒体。
They have prohibited smoking in all public places. 他们已禁止在所有公共场所吸烟。


过去完成时:
The judge had prohibited the release of the defendant's name. 法官已禁止发布被告人的姓名。
They had prohibited access to the building before the investigation. 调查前他们已禁止进入大楼。


将来时:
The council will prohibit the construction of new buildings in this zone. 理事会将禁止在这个区域建造新建筑。
They will prohibit the use of drones in this area. 他们将禁止在这个区域使用无人机。


虚拟语气:
If they prohibited the event, many people would be disappointed. 如果他们禁止这场活动，很多人会失望。
I wish they prohibited the use of plastic bags. 我希望他们禁止使用塑料袋。


被动语态:
The sale of drugs is prohibited.  毒品销售是被禁止的。
The use of fireworks was prohibited during the festival. 节日期间禁止燃放烟花。


疑问句:
Does the law prohibit the possession of firearms?  法律是否禁止持有枪支？
Does the rule prohibit eating in the classroom? 规定是否禁止在教室里吃饭？


否定句:
The law does not prohibit free speech. 法律不禁止言论自由。
The policy does not prohibit employees from using their personal laptops. 该政策并不禁止员工使用个人笔记本电脑。



PHRASE->
prohibit from: 禁止...
prohibit the use of: 禁止使用...
strictly prohibit: 严格禁止
absolutely prohibit: 完全禁止
prohibit entry: 禁止进入
