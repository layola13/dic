misappoint:/ˌmɪs.əˈpɔɪnt/ | vt. | 使失望；未能实现（期望） | to fail to fulfill the hopes or expectations of (someone) | The movie seriously disappointed me.  这部电影严重让我失望了。|The government's failure to act disappointed many voters. 政府的不作为使许多选民感到失望。| disappoint, let down | satisfy, please | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often disappoints his parents. = 他经常让他的父母失望。
The weather disappoints us today. = 天气今天让我们失望了。


一般过去时:
The results disappointed the team. = 结果让团队失望了。
The film disappointed many viewers. = 这部电影让许多观众失望了。


现在完成时:
The news has disappointed her greatly. = 这个消息让她非常失望。
His behavior has disappointed everyone. = 他的行为让每个人都失望了。


过去完成时:
I had been disappointed by his previous work. = 我对他以前的作品感到失望。
They had been disappointed many times before. = 他们以前已经失望过很多次了。


将来时:
I fear the outcome will disappoint many people. = 我担心结果会让很多人失望。
The news might disappoint them. = 这个消息可能会让他们失望。


虚拟语气:
I would be disappointed if he failed the exam. = 如果他考试不及格，我会很失望。
If the project failed, we would be severely disappointed. = 如果项目失败，我们会非常失望。


被动语态:
Many people were disappointed by the announcement. = 许多人对这个公告感到失望。
The audience was disappointed by the performance. = 观众对这场演出感到失望。


疑问句:
Does this outcome disappoint you? = 这个结果让你失望了吗？
Would the result disappoint him? = 这个结果会让他失望吗？


否定句:
The party didn't disappoint me. = 派对没有让我失望。
The book did not disappoint its readers. = 这本书没有让它的读者失望。


PHRASE->
be disappointed in/with: 对……感到失望
deeply disappointed: 深感失望
bitterly disappointed: 非常失望
greatly disappointed: 非常失望
