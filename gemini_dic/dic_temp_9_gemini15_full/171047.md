pseudotribal:/ˌsuːdoʊˈtraɪbəl/| adj.|伪部落的；貌似部落的 |  Appearing to be tribal or having characteristics of a tribe, but not genuinely so; often used to describe groups that emulate tribal characteristics for social or political purposes, lacking the genuine historical and cultural depth of a true tribe.  | Example: The company fostered a pseudotribal culture among its employees, encouraging team bonding through rituals and shared symbols, but it lacked the deep-rooted history and interconnectedness of a real tribe. | 近义词：quasi-tribal, factitious, artificial | 反义词：genuine tribal, authentic, real
