chaired:/tʃeəd/| v. |主持；担任…的主席|To preside over a meeting, committee, or organization.|The meeting was chaired by the department head.  会议由系主任主持。|Professor Smith chaired the conference. 史密斯教授主持了会议。|presided, moderated, led|—


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He chairs the committee. 他担任委员会主席。
She chairs the meeting every week. 她每周主持会议。


一般过去时:
He chaired the meeting yesterday. 他昨天主持了会议。
She chaired the conference last year. 她去年主持了会议。


现在完成时:
He has chaired numerous conferences. 他主持过许多会议。
She has chaired the committee for five years. 她担任委员会主席五年了。


过去完成时:
He had chaired the committee before she joined. 她加入之前他已经担任了委员会主席。
They had chaired the debate for an hour before the break. 他们在休息前已经主持辩论一个小时了。


将来时:
He will chair the next meeting. 他将主持下一次会议。
She will chair the event next month. 她下个月将主持这个活动。


虚拟语气:
If he chaired the meeting, it would be more efficient. 如果他主持会议，效率会更高。
I wish she had chaired the debate; it would have been fairer. 我希望她主持了这场辩论；那样会更公平。


被动语态:
The meeting was chaired by the CEO.  会议由首席执行官主持。
The conference was chaired efficiently. 会议主持得很有效率。


疑问句:
Who chaired the meeting? 谁主持了会议？
Will she chair the next committee meeting? 她会主持下次委员会会议吗？


否定句:
He did not chair the meeting. 他没有主持会议。
She will not chair the conference this year. 她今年不会主持这个会议。


PHRASE->
