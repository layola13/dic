parenchyma:/pəˈrɛŋkɪmə/| n. |薄壁组织；实质；基本组织|The fundamental tissue of an organ, as opposed to the connective tissue or supporting structures.  It is composed of cells that perform the organ's specific function. For example, in the liver, parenchyma refers to the hepatocytes (liver cells) that carry out liver functions.  |例句：The liver parenchyma was severely damaged by the infection.  肝实质受到了感染的严重损伤。

例句：The lung parenchyma showed signs of inflammation. 肺实质显示出炎症迹象。


近义词：functional tissue, essential tissue|反义词：stroma (supporting tissue)|词性:名词


词源:  From Greek *παρέγχυμα (parénchyma), from παρά (pará) "beside" + ἔγχυμα (énchyma) "infusion, injection".  Originally referring to a substance filling the interstices of an organ.
