quasi-constructed:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ kənˈstrʌktɪd/ | adj. | 准人工构建的；半人工构建的 |  Describes languages or language features that exhibit characteristics of both natural and constructed languages.  They often arise from a mixture of planned features and natural evolution.  For example, a language might have a planned vocabulary but evolve its grammar naturally over time.  | Example:  Toki Pona, while designed, has undergone some degree of natural evolution among its speakers, making it a quasi-constructed language.  | 近义词：partially constructed, semi-artificial | 反义词：natural, completely artificial | 词性:形容词
