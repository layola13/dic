opioids:/ˈoʊpiɔɪdz/| n. |阿片类药物 |Opioids are a class of drugs that include natural and synthetic substances that bind to opioid receptors in the brain and nervous system, producing effects such as pain relief, euphoria, and sedation.  They are often used to treat moderate to severe pain, but can be highly addictive.|例句：The doctor prescribed opioids to manage his post-surgical pain.  医生开了阿片类药物来控制他的术后疼痛。|近义词：narcotics, analgesics|反义词：N/A
