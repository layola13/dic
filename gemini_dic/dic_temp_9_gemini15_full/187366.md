rodentia:/roʊˈdɛnʃə/| n. |啮齿动物|Rodentia is an order of mammals characterized by a single pair of continuously growing incisors in each of their upper and lower jaws.  These teeth require constant gnawing to prevent overgrowth.  Rodents are the most diverse and abundant order of mammals, comprising about 40% of all mammal species.|Example: Squirrels, mice, rats, and beavers are all examples of rodentia.|近义词:gnawers|反义词:None (no direct antonym, but could be contrasted with other mammal orders)|名词
