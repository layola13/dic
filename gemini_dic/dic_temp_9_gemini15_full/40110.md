contem: /kənˈtɛm/ | v. |轻蔑，鄙视 | To feel or show contempt for; despise.  |例句：He contemned their cowardly actions. 他鄙视他们的懦弱行为。|近义词：despise, scorn, disdain |反义词：admire, respect |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He contemns hypocrisy. = 他鄙视虚伪。
She contemns dishonesty in business. = 她鄙视商业上的不诚实。


一般过去时:
They contemned his arrogance. = 他们鄙视他的傲慢。
The villagers contemned the stranger. = 村民们鄙视那个陌生人。


现在完成时:
I have contemned such behavior for years. = 多年来我一直鄙视这种行为。
She has contemned his lies since the incident. = 自那件事以来，她一直鄙视他的谎言。


过去完成时:
He had contemned her suggestions before the meeting. = 他在会议之前就鄙视了她的建议。
They had contemned his ideas for years, ignoring his potential. = 他们多年来一直鄙视他的想法，忽视了他的潜力。


将来时:
They will contemn his betrayal. = 他们将鄙视他的背叛。
She will contemn his actions, regardless of the consequences. = 不管后果如何，她都会鄙视他的行为。


虚拟语气:
If I were in his shoes, I would contemn his approach. = 如果我是他，我会鄙视他的方法。
I wish I had contemned his advice earlier. = 我真希望我早点鄙视他的建议。


被动语态:
His ideas were contemned by the committee. = 他的想法被委员会所鄙视。
His efforts were contemned by his peers. = 他的努力被他的同龄人所鄙视。


疑问句:
Do you contemn his behavior? = 你鄙视他的行为吗？
Does she contemn his dishonesty? = 她鄙视他的不诚实吗？


否定句:
I do not contemn his efforts. = 我并不鄙视他的努力。
They did not contemn her ideas; they simply disagreed. = 他们并不鄙视她的想法；他们只是不同意。


PHRASE->
contemn with silence = 沉默以对（表示鄙视）
openly contemn = 公开鄙视
secretly contemn = 暗中鄙视
contemn someone's authority = 蔑视某人的权威
to contemn the world = 蔑视世界
