drunker:/ˈdrʌŋkər/| adj.|更醉的，更烂醉的|Comparative form of drunk, indicating a greater degree of intoxication.  It describes someone who is more intoxicated than someone else or than they were previously.|He got drunker than ever before at the party.|近义词：more intoxicated, tipsy, inebriated|反义词：sober


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is drunker than his friend. = 他比他的朋友更醉。
She is drunker now than she was an hour ago. = 她现在比一小时前更醉了。


一般过去时:
He was drunker last night than usual. = 他昨晚比平时更醉。
They were drunker at the end of the party. = 派对结束时他们更醉了。


现在完成时:
He has gotten drunker since he started drinking. = 自从他开始喝酒以来，他越来越醉了。
She has become drunker than I expected. = 她比我预料的更醉了。


过去完成时:
He had gotten drunker by the time the party ended. = 到派对结束时，他已经更醉了。
They had been drunker the previous night. = 他们前一天晚上更醉了。


将来时:
He will be drunker if he keeps drinking. = 如果他继续喝酒，他会更醉。
They will probably be drunker by the end of the evening. = 到晚上结束时，他们可能会更醉。


虚拟语气:
If he drank more, he would be drunker. = 如果他喝得更多，他会更醉。
I wish I hadn't gotten so drunker. = 我真希望我没有那么醉。


被动语态: (This adjective doesn't typically use passive voice)


疑问句:
Is he drunker than before? = 他比以前更醉了吗？
Was she drunker than her colleagues? = 她比她的同事更醉吗？


否定句:
He isn't drunker than you. = 他没有你醉。
She wasn't drunker than the others. = 她没有其他人醉。



PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "drunker".  The word is primarily used as an adjective to describe a state of intoxication.  Phrases using "drunk" could be adapted, for example:  "dead drunk", "stone drunk", "blind drunk", could be modified to "dead drunker", "stone drunker", "blind drunker" but these are uncommon and sound unnatural.
