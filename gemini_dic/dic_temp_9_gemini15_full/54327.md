disheartener:/dɪsˈhɑːrtənər/| n. |令人沮丧的事物；令人灰心的事物 |Something that discourages or disheartens.  It can refer to a person, event, or situation that causes a loss of hope or enthusiasm. |The constant rain was a disheartener.  持续的雨水令人沮丧。|近义词：discouragement, setback, disappointment |反义词：encouragement, inspiration, uplift


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bad news is a disheartener. = 这个坏消息令人沮丧。
That constant failure is a disheartener for him. = 持续的失败让他灰心。


一般过去时:
The exam result was a disheartener for many students. = 考试结果让许多学生感到沮丧。
His refusal to help was a disheartener. = 他拒绝帮忙令人灰心。


现在完成时:
The series of setbacks have been a disheartener for the team. = 一系列的挫折让球队士气低落。
Repeated failures have been a disheartener to her. = 反复的失败让她灰心丧气。


过去完成时:
The previous failures had been a disheartener, making them hesitant to try again. = 之前的失败让他们灰心丧气，不敢再次尝试。
Her past experiences had been a disheartener, making her cautious. = 她过去的经历让她灰心，变得谨慎。


将来时:
The upcoming challenges will be a disheartener if not properly addressed. = 如果不妥善处理，即将到来的挑战将会令人沮丧。
The potential loss of funding will be a disheartener for the project. = 资金可能损失将对项目构成打击。


虚拟语气:
If the continuous criticism were not a disheartener, he might have continued. = 如果持续的批评没有让他灰心，他也许会继续下去。
If the lack of support were not a disheartener, they would have persevered. = 如果缺乏支持没有让他们灰心，他们就会坚持下去。


被动语态:
The team was disheartened by the unexpected defeat. = 球队被意外的失败打击到了。
He was disheartened by her rejection. = 他被她的拒绝打击到了。


疑问句:
Is the lack of progress a disheartener for you? = 缺乏进展是否让你沮丧？
Was the negative feedback a disheartener for the artist? = 消极的反馈是否让艺术家感到沮丧？


否定句:
The positive feedback was not a disheartener; it encouraged him to continue. = 积极的反馈并没有让他灰心，反而鼓励他继续下去。
The supportive environment was not a disheartener for the students. = 支持性的环境并没有让学生们灰心。
