engagers:/ɪnˈɡeɪdʒərz/ | n. | 参与者，吸引者，订婚者 | People who actively participate in something or attract and retain the attention of others.  Engagers can refer to individuals involved in a specific activity, or to elements designed to captivate an audience. | Example:  The marketing campaign targeted young engagers on social media.  市场营销活动的目标是在社交媒体上吸引年轻的参与者。

The keynote speaker was a true engager, captivating the audience from start to finish.  主题演讲者是一位真正的吸引者，从头到尾都吸引着听众。


近义词：participants, attendees, captivators, enthrallers | 反义词：bystanders, onlookers, disinterest


词源:  Derived from the verb "engage," meaning to participate or attract. The suffix "-er" denotes a person or thing performing the action.  The word's evolution reflects the increasing importance of active participation and audience engagement in various fields.
