nonsyntactic:/ˌnɒnˈsɪntæktɪk/| adj. |非句法的，不合语法的|Not conforming to the rules of syntax; lacking grammatical structure.  This term is used to describe linguistic elements or structures that do not adhere to the grammatical rules of a language.  It often refers to sequences of words that are grammatically incorrect or meaningless.|Example: "The nonsyntactic phrase 'colorless green ideas sleep furiously' is a famous example used in linguistics to illustrate the distinction between grammaticality and meaningfulness."|近义词：ungrammatical, illogical, incoherent|反义词：syntactic, grammatical
