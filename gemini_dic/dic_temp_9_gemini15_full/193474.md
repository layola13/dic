selective:/sɪˈlɛktɪv/ | adj. | 选择性的；精选的；挑剔的 | Choosing or chosen carefully from a larger number.  It implies a careful consideration of options and a preference for particular qualities or characteristics. |例句：The college has a very selective admissions policy. 这所大学的招生政策非常严格。

例句：She is very selective about the people she chooses as friends. 她对选择朋友非常挑剔。


近义词：discriminating, discerning, choosy, picky


反义词：indiscriminate, uncritical, random


词源：源自中古英语 *selectif，来自拉丁语 *selectivus，来自动词 *selegere（选择）。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is selective about his food. 他对食物很挑剔。
The company is selective in its hiring process. 这家公司在招聘过程中非常挑剔。


一般过去时:
She was selective in her choice of words. 她选择词语很谨慎。
The museum was selective about the artifacts it displayed. 博物馆对展出的文物很挑剔。


现在完成时:
They have been selective about the information they release to the public. 他们对向公众发布的信息一直很谨慎。
The artist has been selective in choosing his subjects.  这位艺术家在选择他的主题方面一直很挑剔。


过去完成时:
The committee had been selective in its funding decisions. 委员会在资金分配决策上一直很谨慎。
She had been selective about her social engagements. 她一直对社交活动很挑剔。


将来时:
He will be selective in his choice of career. 他将会在选择职业方面很谨慎。
The school will be selective in admitting new students. 学校在录取新生方面将会很严格。


虚拟语气:
If he were more selective, he would have fewer problems. 如果他更挑剔一些，他就会少些麻烦。
I wish I had been more selective in my choice of friends. 我真希望我当初在选择朋友方面更挑剔一些。


被动语态:
Students are selectively chosen for the program. 学生们被有选择地选入该项目。
The best candidates are selectively recruited by the firm. 最好的候选人被公司有选择地招聘。


疑问句:
Is she selective in her reading choices? 她看书很挑剔吗？
Are the criteria for selection selective enough? 选择标准够严格吗？


否定句:
He is not selective about his friends. 他对朋友并不挑剔。
The process was not selective enough to ensure quality. 这个过程不够严格，无法保证质量。
