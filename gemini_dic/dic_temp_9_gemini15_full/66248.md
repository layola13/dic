exclude:/ɪkˈskluːd/| vt.|排除；不包括；除外|To prevent someone or something from being included in a group or activity.|The guest list excluded anyone under the age of 21.  宾客名单排除了所有21岁以下的人。|include, omit|include, admit|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We exclude certain foods from our diet. = 我们从饮食中排除某些食物。
The school excludes students with poor attendance. = 学校开除旷课的学生。

一般过去时:
They excluded him from the team. = 他们把他排除在团队之外。
The judge excluded that evidence from the trial. = 法官将该证据排除在审判之外。

现在完成时:
She has excluded herself from the competition. = 她已经把自己排除在竞争之外。
They have excluded several candidates from the interview process. = 他们已经将几位候选人排除在面试过程之外。

过去完成时:
He had excluded the possibility of an accident. = 他之前已经排除了事故的可能性。
They had excluded her from the meeting because she was late. = 因为她迟到了，他们已经把她排除在会议之外。

将来时:
They will exclude him from future projects. = 他们将把他排除在未来的项目之外。
We will exclude any duplicate entries from the contest. = 我们将把任何重复的参赛作品排除在比赛之外。

虚拟语气:
If they excluded him, the project would fail. = 如果他们把他排除在外，项目就会失败。
I wish they had excluded that embarrassing detail. = 我希望他们没有提到那个令人尴尬的细节。

被动语态:
He was excluded from the meeting. = 他被排除在会议之外。
The data was excluded from the analysis. = 数据被排除在分析之外。

疑问句:
Were any names excluded from the list? = 有任何名字被排除在名单之外吗？
Will this item be excluded from the sale? = 这件商品会被排除在促销之外吗？

否定句:
They did not exclude any possibility. = 他们没有排除任何可能性。
We did not exclude her from the group. = 我们没有把她排除在群体之外。


PHRASE->
exclude from = 排除在…之外
mutually exclusive = 互相排斥的
to the exclusion of = 排除…
exclude the possibility = 排除…的可能性
to be excluded = 被排除在外
