insectivores:/ɪnˈsɛktɪvɔːrz/| n. |食虫动物|Animals that feed primarily on insects. Insectivores are characterized by their specialized adaptations for capturing and consuming insects, such as sharp teeth, sticky tongues, or strong claws.  They play a vital role in regulating insect populations in their ecosystems.|Example:  Hedgehogs and shrews are common examples of insectivores. 刺猬和鼩鼱是常见的食虫动物。

Many insectivores are nocturnal, hunting insects under the cover of darkness. 许多食虫动物是夜行动物，在黑暗的掩护下捕猎昆虫。|近义词：insect-eaters|反义词：carnivores (for large prey), herbivores|名词
