preacquire: /ˌpriːəˈkwaɪər/ | vt. | 预先取得，提前获得 | To acquire something before it is needed or available to others.  This word combines the prefix "pre-" (meaning before) with the verb "acquire."  It emphasizes obtaining something in advance. |例句：The company preacquired the land to ensure future expansion.  公司预先收购了土地以确保未来的扩张。

The museum preacquired several rare artifacts before the auction.  博物馆在拍卖前预先收购了几件稀有文物。


近义词：secure beforehand, obtain in advance, get ahead of the curve

反义词：postpone, delay acquiring, relinquish


词性：动词
