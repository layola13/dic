outcourted: /ˌaʊtˈkɔːrtɪd/ | v. |  胜过（在球场上）；技高一筹 | To defeat decisively in a competition, especially a sporting event.  This word is a relatively rare and informal neologism formed by combining "out" (meaning to surpass) and "courted" (referencing competitive activities, often implying a romantic or strategic element). | The underdog team outcourted the favorites, winning the championship.  =  实力较弱的球队技高一筹，赢得了冠军。 |  outplayed, outmaneuvered, outclassed |  outmatched, defeated (less decisive) | 动词
