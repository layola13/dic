mudwall: /ˈmʌdˌwɔːl/ | n. | 泥墙 | A wall made of mud, clay, or a similar material.  Mud walls are a traditional building method used in many parts of the world, particularly in regions with limited access to other building materials. They can be surprisingly strong and durable, especially when properly constructed and maintained. | Example: The ancient village was surrounded by a high mudwall, offering protection from the elements and invaders.  The farmer repaired the mudwall in his garden after the recent storm damaged it. | 近义词：adobe wall, wattle and daub wall | 反义词：brick wall, stone wall
