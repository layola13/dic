half-acquainted:/ˌhɑːf əˈkweɪntɪd/| adj.| 半熟的，不太熟悉的 | Partially acquainted; having a slight or superficial knowledge of someone or something.  This word suggests a level of familiarity beyond a complete stranger, but less than a close friend or acquaintance. |例句：I felt half-acquainted with the city, having only visited a few key landmarks. 我觉得对这座城市不太熟悉，只参观了几个主要地标。

I'm half-acquainted with his work, having only read a couple of his articles. 我对他作品不太熟悉，只读过他几篇文章。


近义词：slightly familiar, somewhat known, superficially acquainted | 反义词：well-acquainted, intimately familiar, completely unknown

词性:形容词


USAGE->
This word is primarily used as an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms.  Therefore, tense conjugation, passive voice, etc., are not applicable.  Its usage is primarily descriptive.
