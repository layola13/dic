nonidentity: /ˌnɒn aɪˈdɛntɪti/ | n. | 非同一性，非同一；差异 | The lack of identity or sameness; difference or distinction.  This concept often arises in philosophy and ethics, particularly in discussions about personal identity and the implications of actions.  It contrasts with the idea of identity, which refers to sameness or oneness. | Example:  The nonidentity problem in ethics explores the possibility that harming someone in a way that causes the existence of a different person who is better off than the original person would still be morally wrong.  | 近义词：difference, distinction, disparity | 反义词：identity, sameness
