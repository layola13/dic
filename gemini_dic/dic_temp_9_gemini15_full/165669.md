premonitions:/ˌpriːməˈnɪʃənz/ | n. |预感，预兆 | A premonition is a feeling that something, especially something unpleasant, is going to happen.  It's a strong feeling of anticipation, often based on intuition rather than evidence. |例句：She had a premonition of disaster. 她有一种灾难将要发生的预感。

例句：He had a premonition that something bad was about to happen. 他预感到不好的事情即将发生。


近义词：foreboding, presentiment, hunch, feeling, intuition | 反义词：certainty, assurance


词源：from Old French *premonition, from Latin praemonitio, from praemonēre "to warn beforehand," from prae- "before" + monēre "to warn," related to  mens "mind."
