pluck-up:/plʌk ʌp/| v.|振作起来；鼓起勇气|to make a great effort to be brave or confident enough to do something|She plucked up the courage to ask him for a date. 她鼓起勇气约他出去。|近义词：summon, muster,  brace oneself|反义词: give up, despair


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He plucks up his courage every morning. = 他每天早晨都鼓起勇气。
She plucks up the strength to face her problems. = 她振作精神去面对她的问题。

一般过去时:
He plucked up the courage to speak in public. = 他鼓起勇气在公共场合发言。
She plucked up her spirits after the bad news. = 在听到坏消息后，她振作了精神。

现在完成时:
I have plucked up the courage to tell him the truth. = 我已经鼓起勇气告诉他真相了。
She has plucked herself up after a period of depression. = 她经历了一段抑郁期后，已经振作起来了。

过去完成时:
He had plucked up his courage before he went onstage. = 他上台前已经鼓起勇气了。
She had plucked herself together before the interview. = 面试前她已经振作起来了。

将来时:
I will pluck up the courage to confront him. = 我将鼓起勇气去面对他。
She will pluck up her spirits and try again. = 她会振作精神再试一次。

虚拟语气:
If I plucked up the courage, I would tell her how I feel. = 如果我鼓起勇气的话，我会告诉她我的感受。
I wish I had plucked up the courage to ask her out. = 我真希望我当时有勇气约她出去。

被动语态:  (pluck up is rarely used passively)  This tense is not naturally applicable to this verb.


疑问句:
Will you pluck up the courage to do it? = 你会鼓起勇气去做吗？
Did she pluck up her spirits after the accident? = 事故后她振作起来了吗？


否定句:
He couldn't pluck up the courage to leave. = 他鼓不起勇气离开。
She didn't pluck up the strength to finish the task. = 她没有力气完成任务。


PHRASE->
pluck up courage = 鼓起勇气
pluck up one's spirits = 振作精神
pluck up the heart = 鼓足勇气
pluck up one's resolution = 下定决心
pluck oneself together = 振作起来
