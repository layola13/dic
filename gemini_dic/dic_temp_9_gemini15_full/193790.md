self-denial:/ˌsɛlf dəˈnaɪəl/| n. |克己；自我克制；禁欲|The act of denying oneself something, especially a desire or pleasure, often for moral or religious reasons.  It involves suppressing one's own needs or wants for a greater good or for personal growth. |例句：Her self-denial in giving up her career to care for her ailing mother was admirable.  她的克己奉献，放弃事业去照顾生病的母亲，令人钦佩。

例句：The monk practiced rigorous self-denial as part of his spiritual discipline.  这位僧侣为了精神修行而严格克己。


近义词：abstinence, self-discipline, self-control, renunciation, asceticism|反义词：indulgence, self-indulgence, hedonism|词性:名词


词源:

* **self-**:  From Old English *self, *sylf, meaning "self, same."  This element is a prefix indicating something related to oneself.

* **denial**: From Old French *denier,  meaning "to deny," ultimately deriving from Latin *denegare,  "to deny, refuse."  This involves refusing to acknowledge or accept something.
