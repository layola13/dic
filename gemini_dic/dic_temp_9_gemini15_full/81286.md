granters:/ˈɡræn.tərz/ | n. | 授予者；批准者 | People or organizations that grant something, such as permission, money, or rights.  |The granters of the scholarship were impressed by the applicant's dedication. 奖学金的资助者对申请人的奉献精神印象深刻。  | donors, providers, approvers |  rejecters, deniers | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The university grants many scholarships every year. = 大学每年都会发放许多奖学金。
The government grants permits for construction projects. = 政府批准建筑项目的许可证。


一般过去时:
The committee granted the request for funding. = 委员会批准了资金申请。
She granted him permission to enter. = 她允许他进入。


现在完成时:
They have granted numerous awards to deserving individuals. = 他们已经向许多有功人士颁发了无数奖项。
The authorities have granted asylum to many refugees. = 当局已向许多难民提供庇护。


过去完成时:
The king had granted the land to his loyal subject. = 国王已将土地赐予了他忠诚的臣民。
They had granted him a loan before his business failed. = 他的生意失败前，他们曾给他贷款。


将来时:
The council will grant the building permit next week. = 理事会下周将批准建筑许可证。
The board will grant him the promotion he deserves. = 董事会将给他他应得的晋升。


虚拟语气:
If they granted my wish, I would be very happy. = 如果他们满足我的愿望，我会非常高兴。
I wish the bank had granted me a larger loan. = 我希望银行能给我更大的贷款。


被动语态:
The application was granted after a thorough review. = 申请经过仔细审查后获得批准。
Permission was granted to use the copyrighted material. = 已获准使用受版权保护的材料。


疑问句:
Will the committee grant our proposal? = 委员会会批准我们的提案吗？
Did they grant your request for leave? = 他们批准你的休假申请了吗？


否定句:
They did not grant him the visa. = 他们没有给他签证。
The judge refused to grant the motion. = 法官拒绝批准该动议。
