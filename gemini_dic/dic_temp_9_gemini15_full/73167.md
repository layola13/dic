forbiddal:/fərˈbɪdəl/| n. |禁止；阻止；禁令 |The forbiddal of smoking in public places is a good thing. 公共场所禁止吸烟是一件好事。|近义词：prohibition, ban, interdiction |反义词：permission, allowance|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The law forbids the sale of alcohol to minors. = 法律禁止向未成年人出售酒精。
This rule forbids using cell phones in class. = 这条规则禁止在课堂上使用手机。


一般过去时:
The king forbade the publication of the book. = 国王禁止出版这本书。
They forbade him from entering the building. = 他们禁止他进入大楼。


现在完成时:
The government has forbidden the import of certain goods. = 政府已经禁止进口某些商品。
She has forbidden her children from watching violent movies. = 她禁止她的孩子们观看暴力电影。


过去完成时:
He had forbidden her to see him again. = 他已经禁止她再次见他。
They had forbidden the use of the swimming pool. = 他们已经禁止使用游泳池。


将来时:
The council will forbid the construction of new buildings in this area. = 理事会将禁止在这个区域建造新建筑。
They will forbid access to the site until further notice. = 在另行通知之前，他们将禁止进入该场地。


虚拟语气:
If they forbade smoking here, it would be much better. = 如果他们禁止在这里吸烟，情况会好得多。
I wish they forbade the use of plastic bags. = 我希望他们禁止使用塑料袋。


被动语态:
Smoking is forbidden in this area. = 此区域禁止吸烟。
The use of cameras was forbidden at the event. = 活动中禁止使用相机。


疑问句:
Is smoking forbidden in this restaurant? = 这家餐厅禁止吸烟吗？
Are visitors forbidden from entering this building? = 游客禁止进入这座建筑物吗？


否定句:
The rule does not forbid the use of dictionaries. = 规则并不禁止使用字典。
They did not forbid us from leaving early. = 他们没有禁止我们早退。
