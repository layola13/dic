majors:/ˈmeɪdʒərz/ | n. | 主修科目；专业；大多数；主力 |  A major is a subject of study that a student chooses to specialize in at college or university.  It also refers to something that is important or significant. | 例句：What are your majors? = 你的主修科目是什么？

She's majoring in English literature. = 她主修英国文学。


近义词：specialties, fields of study, principal subjects | 反义词：minors

USAGE->
一般现在时:
What are your majors? = 你的主修科目是什么？
He majors in computer science. = 他主修计算机科学。


一般过去时:
What were her majors in college? = 她大学主修什么？
He majored in history. = 他主修历史。


现在完成时:
Have you decided on your majors yet? = 你已经决定你的主修科目了吗？
She has majored in biology for three years. = 她主修生物学三年了。


过去完成时:
Had he chosen his majors before the deadline? = 他在截止日期前是否已经选定了他的主修科目？
She had majored in physics before switching to engineering. = 在转到工程学之前，她主修过物理学。


将来时:
What will you major in next semester? = 下学期你将主修什么？
He will major in mathematics in the fall. = 秋天他将主修数学。


虚拟语气:
If I were to choose again, I would major in music. = 如果让我重新选择，我会主修音乐。
I wish I had majored in something more practical. = 我真希望我当时主修的是一些更实用的东西。


被动语态:
What majors are offered by this university?  = 这所大学开设哪些专业？
This subject is majored in by many students. = 许多学生都主修这门科目。（较少用被动语态）


疑问句:
What majors are you considering? = 你正在考虑哪些专业？
Which major is more challenging? = 哪个专业更具挑战性？


否定句:
I don't know what majors to choose. = 我不知道该选择什么专业。
He didn't major in engineering. = 他没有主修工程学。


PHRASE->
major in = 主修
a major problem = 一个主要问题
major league =  职业联赛（棒球）
a major breakthrough = 一个重大突破
major shareholder = 主要股东
