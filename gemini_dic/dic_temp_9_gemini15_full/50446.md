depreciated:/dɪˈpriːʃieɪtɪd/| adj.|贬值的；贬低的；不重视的|Having lost value, especially in monetary value.  The term also implies a decline in reputation or esteem.|例句：The value of the car has depreciated significantly over the years. 这辆车的价值多年来已大幅贬值。

例句：His work has been depreciated by critics. 他的作品受到了评论家的贬低。


近义词：devalued, diminished, lessened, undervalued, underestimated|反义词：appreciated, valued, enhanced, increased


词源:  From Latin *dēpreciātus, past participle of dēpreciāre "to lower the price of," from dē- "down" + pretium "price."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The currency depreciates steadily. = 货币稳步贬值。
The company depreciates its assets annually. = 公司每年对资产进行折旧。


一般过去时:
The dollar depreciated against the euro. = 美元兑欧元贬值。
The painting depreciated in value after the scandal. = 丑闻发生后，这幅画的价值下降了。


现在完成时:
The house has depreciated considerably since we bought it. = 自从我们买下这房子以来，它的价值已经大幅下降。
The brand has depreciated in the eyes of consumers. = 在消费者眼中，该品牌的地位已经下降。


过去完成时:
The stock had depreciated sharply before the market crash. = 在市场崩盘之前，股票已经大幅下跌。
The company's reputation had depreciated following the safety scandal. = 安全丑闻发生后，公司的声誉已经下降。


将来时:
The car will depreciate quickly in the first few years. = 这辆车头几年会很快贬值。
Experts predict the property will depreciate in the coming months. = 专家预测该房产将在未来几个月内贬值。


虚拟语气:
If the currency had not depreciated, we would have made a profit. = 如果货币没有贬值，我们就会盈利。
If the company had not depreciated its assets, its reported profits would have been higher. = 如果公司没有对资产进行折旧，其报告利润就会更高。


被动语态:
The equipment was depreciated over five years. = 设备在五年内被折旧。
The value of the shares was depreciated by the news. = 该消息导致股票价值贬值。


疑问句:
Has the value of your investment depreciated? = 你的投资价值贬值了吗？
How much has the car depreciated since you bought it? = 自从你买车以来，车价贬值了多少？


否定句:
The value of the land has not depreciated. = 土地的价值没有贬值。
The company's stock did not depreciate during the crisis. = 公司股票在危机期间没有贬值。
