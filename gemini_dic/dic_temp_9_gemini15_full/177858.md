receptivities:/ˌrɛsɛpˈtɪvɪtiz/| n. |接受能力；感受性；敏感性 |The capacity or willingness to receive or accept something, especially information, ideas, or experiences.  It often implies a positive response or openness to influence.  |例句：The teacher praised the students' receptivities to new learning methods.  老师称赞学生们对新学习方法的接受能力。

The receptivities of the audience were evident in their enthusiastic applause. 观众的接受度从他们热烈的掌声中可以看出。


近义词：openness, responsiveness, susceptibility, sensitivity |反义词：resistance, insensitivity, imperviousness, unresponsiveness

词源:  Derived from the word "receptive," which comes from the Latin "recipere" (to receive, take back). The suffix "-ivity" indicates a state or quality.
