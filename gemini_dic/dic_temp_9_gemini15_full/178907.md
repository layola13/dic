redden:/ˈrɛdn/| vt. vi.|使变红；变红|To become or turn red; to make something red.  The word comes from the Old English word "rēdan," meaning "to redden."|例句：Her cheeks reddened with embarrassment.  她的脸颊羞红了。|例句：The setting sun reddened the sky.  落日使天空变红。|近义词：blush, flush, color|反义词：pale, blanch


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The apples redden in the sun. = 苹果在阳光下变红了。
He reddens easily when he's embarrassed. = 他害羞的时候很容易脸红。

一般过去时:
Her face reddened after she heard the news. = 她听到这个消息后脸红了。
The sunset reddened the western sky. = 日落使西边的天空变红。

现在完成时:
His nose has reddened from the cold. = 他的鼻子冻红了。
The leaves have reddened beautifully in autumn. = 秋天的叶子变得非常漂亮地红了。

过去完成时:
Her cheeks had reddened before he even spoke. = 他还没开口说话，她的脸就已经红了。
The sky had reddened dramatically before the storm broke. = 暴风雨来临之前，天空已变得异常红。

将来时:
The tomatoes will redden as they ripen. = 西红柿成熟后会变红。
His face will redden with anger if you tease him. = 如果你取笑他，他的脸会气红了。

虚拟语气:
If I were embarrassed, I would redden. = 如果我尴尬的话，我会脸红。
If the apples had more sun, they would redden faster. = 如果苹果有更多的阳光，它们会更快变红。

被动语态:
The cheeks were reddened by the cold wind. = 脸颊被冷风吹红了。
The sky is reddened by the setting sun. = 天空被落日染红了。

疑问句:
Does your face redden easily? = 你的脸容易红吗？
Did the sunset redden the clouds? = 日落把云彩染红了吗？

否定句:
His face didn't redden at all. = 他的脸一点都没红。
The berries haven't reddened yet. = 浆果还没变红。
