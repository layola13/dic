deploring:/dɪˈplɔːrɪŋ/| v.|谴责，悲叹，对…感到遗憾|expressing strong disapproval of something; feeling or expressing great sorrow or distress|She deplored the violence. 她谴责了暴力行为。|He deplored the lack of funding for the project. 他对该项目缺乏资金感到遗憾。|praising, approving, celebrating|condoning, celebrating


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They deplore the government's actions. = 他们谴责政府的行动。
We deplore the state of the environment. = 我们对环境的状况感到痛心。

一般过去时:
She deplored the decision to close the school. = 她对关闭学校的决定表示遗憾。
He deplored the loss of so many lives. = 他对如此多的生命损失感到悲痛。

现在完成时:
They have deplored the recent increase in crime. = 他们一直谴责最近犯罪的增加。
We have deplored the lack of progress for years. = 多年来，我们一直对缺乏进展感到遗憾。

过去完成时:
She had deplored the situation for months before taking action. = 在采取行动之前，她已经对这种情况感到遗憾了好几个月了。
They had deplored the injustice for years before it was finally addressed. = 在这个问题最终得到解决之前，他们多年来一直谴责这种不公正行为。

将来时:
They will deplore the outcome of the election. = 他们将谴责选举的结果。
We will deplore any attempt to undermine the peace process. = 我们将谴责任何破坏和平进程的企图。

虚拟语气:
I would deplore it if they made such a decision. = 如果他们做出这样的决定，我会感到非常遗憾。
She would have deplored the consequences if she had known. = 如果她知道的话，她会对后果感到非常遗憾。

被动语态:
The government's actions were deplored by many. = 政府的行动受到了许多人的谴责。
The lack of funding was deplored by the researchers. = 研究人员对缺乏资金表示遗憾。

疑问句:
Do they deplore the new policy? = 他们是否谴责这项新政策？
Does he deplore the current situation? = 他是否对当前局势感到遗憾？

否定句:
They do not deplore the changes. = 他们并不谴责这些改变。
He did not deplore her decision. = 他并不谴责她的决定。
