railed:/reɪld/| v.| 责骂；抱怨；(以栏杆)围住；|To express strong disapproval or criticism of someone or something.|The politician railed against the government's policies.  这位政客痛斥政府的政策。|He railed at the injustice of it all. 他痛骂这一切的不公正。|近义词：scold, berate, denounce, lambaste|反义词：praise, commend


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He rails against the unfair system. 他抨击不公平的制度。
She rails at the lack of opportunity. 她抱怨缺乏机会。


一般过去时:
They railed against the new regulations. 他们反对新的规章制度。
He railed at his bad luck. 他抱怨自己的运气不好。


现在完成时:
She has railed against the injustice for years. 她多年来一直抗议这种不公正。
He has railed at the government's inaction. 他一直强烈谴责政府的不作为。


过去完成时:
They had railed against the decision before it was even announced.  他们在决定宣布之前就已强烈反对。
She had railed at her fate for many years. 她多年来一直抱怨自己的命运。


将来时:
He will rail against the proposed changes. 他将强烈反对拟议的变革。
They will rail at the incompetence of the management. 他们将痛斥管理层的无能。


虚拟语气:
If he knew the truth, he would rail against it. 如果他知道真相，他会强烈反对。
I wish I hadn't railed at him so harshly. 我真希望我当时没有对他那样严厉地斥责。


被动语态:
The government's policies were railed against by many citizens. 许多公民强烈反对政府的政策。
His incompetence was railed at by everyone.  他的无能被所有人痛斥。


疑问句:
Why did he rail against the plan? 他为什么强烈反对这个计划？
Does she often rail at her colleagues? 她经常斥责她的同事吗？


否定句:
He didn't rail against the decision, surprisingly.  令人惊讶的是，他没有反对这项决定。
She didn't rail at her misfortunes; she accepted them. 她没有抱怨自己的不幸，而是接受了它们。


PHRASE->
rail against something:  强烈反对某事
rail at someone:  痛斥某人
rail about something:  喋喋不休地抱怨某事
rail with anger:  愤怒地斥责
rail at the injustice:  痛斥不公
