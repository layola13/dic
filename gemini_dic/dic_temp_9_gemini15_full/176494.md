re-presentation:/ˌriːˌprezenˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 再现；重新呈现；代表；演示 | The act of presenting something again; a second showing or performance; the act of representing something or someone.  It can also refer to a formal presentation or a depiction. | Example: The re-presentation of the data clarified the initial findings.  再次呈现数据阐明了最初的发现。 The museum's re-presentation of the ancient artifacts was stunning. 博物馆对古代文物的再次展示令人惊叹。|近义词：representation, presentation, re-enactment, reproduction |反义词：absence, omission |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company gives a re-presentation of its new product every week. = 公司每周都会重新展示它的新产品。
His re-presentation of the historical events is quite controversial. = 他对历史事件的重新诠释颇具争议。


一般过去时:
The professor gave a re-presentation of the lecture last night. = 教授昨晚重新讲授了这节课。
