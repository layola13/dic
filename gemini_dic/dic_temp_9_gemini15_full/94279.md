impersonations:/ˌɪmpɜːrsəˈneɪʃənz/| n. |模仿；扮演；假冒|The act of imitating someone's voice, mannerisms, or behavior, often for entertainment or deception.  It can refer to a single instance or multiple instances of such imitation. |例句：His impersonations of famous politicians were hilarious.  他的政治家模仿表演非常搞笑。

例句：The police investigated several impersonations of the CEO to defraud investors. 警方调查了数起冒充首席执行官以欺骗投资者的事件。


近义词：mimicry, imitations, enactments, portrayals|反义词：authenticity, genuineness|词性:n.


词源:

From the verb "impersonate," which combines the prefix "im-" (meaning "into" or "upon") with "personate," derived from the Latin word "persona" (meaning "mask," "character," or "person").  The suffix "-ation" forms a noun, indicating the act or process of impersonating.
