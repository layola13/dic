objectification:/ˌɒbdʒektɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | /ˌɑːbdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 客体化；物化；将…视为客体 | The act of treating someone as an object rather than a person. This involves reducing a person's worth or value to their physical attributes or sexual appeal, disregarding their personality, feelings, and autonomy.  | Example: The objectification of women in advertising is a persistent problem.  广告中对女性的物化是一个持续存在的问题。The objectification of workers in the factory led to low morale and high turnover. 工厂中对工人的物化导致士气低落和高流动率。| 近义词：dehumanization, commodification, instrumentalization | 反义词：humanization, respect, valuing


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The media often objectifies women. = 媒体经常将女性物化。
He objectifies his employees, treating them as mere tools. 他将他的员工物化，把他们当作单纯的工具。

一般过去时:
The film objectified women to sell tickets. = 这部电影物化女性以售卖电影票。
The company's advertising campaign objectified its female consumers. 该公司的广告活动将女性消费者物化了。

现在完成时:
The fashion industry has objectified women for decades. = 时尚行业几十年来一直在物化女性。
Society has objectified people based on race and gender. 社会根据种族和性别对人进行了物化。

过去完成时:
Before the #MeToo movement, the entertainment industry had long objectified women. 在#MeToo运动之前，娱乐行业长期以来一直在物化女性。
They had objectified the workers for years before the union intervened. 在工会介入之前，他们多年来一直在物化工人。

将来时:
Future advertising campaigns will continue to objectify women unless there is significant change. 除非发生重大变化，未来的广告活动将继续物化女性。
The company plans to stop objectifying its employees in its marketing materials. 公司计划停止在其营销材料中物化其员工。

虚拟语气:
