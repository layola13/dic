droll:/drɒl/| adj. |滑稽的，古怪有趣的 | Amusingly odd or peculiar; whimsically humorous.  The droll humor in his stories often relies on unexpected twists and wordplay.  | 近义词：funny, comical, humorous, whimsical, quirky | 反义词：serious, dull, boring, somber


例句：

His droll remarks kept the audience entertained throughout the lecture. = 他滑稽的评论让观众在整个讲座过程中都保持着娱乐性。

The painting, with its droll depiction of a cat playing the violin, was a conversation starter. = 这幅画以滑稽的方式描绘了一只猫拉小提琴，成为了一个谈话的开端。


词源:

The word "droll" ultimately derives from the Middle Dutch word *drol*, meaning "buffoon" or "jester".  It entered English in the 16th century, initially referring to a person who is amusingly strange or eccentric. Over time, its meaning shifted to describe the quality of humor itself.


用法说明：

droll通常用来形容一种幽默感，这种幽默感是古怪的、奇特的，甚至是略带讽刺的。它并不总是指直接的、大声的笑话，而是更倾向于一种微妙、含蓄的幽默，往往需要一些理解才能欣赏。
