prespeculation:/ˌpriːˌspɛkjuˈleɪʃən/| n. |预先推测；事前猜测 |The act of speculating or forming opinions beforehand, often without sufficient evidence.  It involves anticipating future events or trends based on limited information.  This can be applied to various fields, including finance, business, and scientific research. |例句：The prespeculation about the merger led to considerable market volatility.  事前关于合并的推测导致市场剧烈波动。

例句：Her prespeculation on the outcome of the election proved inaccurate. 她对选举结果的预先推测被证明是不准确的。


近义词：anticipation, conjecture, speculation, prediction, forecast

反义词：certainty, proof, fact


词性：名词
