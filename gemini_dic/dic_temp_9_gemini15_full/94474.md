impose:/ɪmˈpoʊz/ | vt. |强加；征税；施加；强迫接受 | To force someone to accept or put up with something, especially a belief, rule, or tax.  |例句：The government imposed a new tax on alcohol. 政府对酒类征收新税。|例句：Don't impose your beliefs on others. 不要把你的信仰强加于他人。|近义词：enact, inflict, levy |反义词：remove, abolish

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He imposes his will on everyone. = 他把他的意志强加于每个人。
The government imposes strict regulations on businesses. = 政府对企业施加严格的规定。

一般过去时:
They imposed a curfew after the riot. = 暴乱后他们实施了宵禁。
The judge imposed a heavy fine on the defendant. = 法官对被告处以巨额罚款。

现在完成时:
The new law has imposed significant burdens on small businesses. = 新法律给小型企业带来了沉重的负担。
She has imposed her ideas on the project team. = 她把她的想法强加于项目团队。

过去完成时:
The king had imposed his rule over the entire kingdom. = 国王已将其统治强加于整个王国。
Before the meeting, they had imposed a strict dress code. = 会议前，他们已经规定了严格的着装规范。

将来时:
The company will impose stricter quality control measures. = 公司将实施更严格的质量控制措施。
They will impose sanctions on the country for its human rights abuses. = 他们将因该国侵犯人权而对其实施制裁。

虚拟语气:
If they imposed stricter rules, the situation would improve. = 如果他们实施更严格的规定，情况就会好转。
I wish they hadn't imposed such a high price on the tickets. = 我真希望他们没有对门票定价这么高。

被动语态:
A new tax was imposed on luxury goods. = 对奢侈品征收了新税。
New rules were imposed to improve safety. = 实施了新的规章以提高安全性。

疑问句:
What restrictions will be imposed on travelers? = 对旅客将施加什么限制？
What penalties are imposed for violating the rules? = 违反规定会受到什么处罚？

否定句:
The government did not impose any new restrictions. = 政府没有实施任何新的限制。
They did not impose their will on their children. = 他们没有把他们的意志强加于他们的孩子。


PHRASE->
impose on =  打扰；麻烦
impose oneself on =  强加于；强迫自己接受
impose a burden on =  给…带来负担
impose a penalty on =  对…处以罚款
impose restrictions on = 对…施加限制
