hepatorenal: /ˌhɛpətoʊˈriːnəl/ | adj. | 肝肾的，肝肾相关的 | Relating to both the liver and the kidneys.  The term describes conditions, diseases, or processes that affect both organs. |The patient presented with hepatorenal syndrome, a serious complication of liver disease.  病人出现了肝肾综合征，这是肝病的严重并发症。 |近义词：hepato-renal (less common)|反义词：None specifically, but antonyms could be related to terms describing the *absence* of liver or kidney involvement in a specific condition.

hepatorenal insufficiency (肝肾功能不全): reduced function of both the liver and kidneys.
hepatorenal fibrosis (肝肾纤维化): scarring of both the liver and kidneys.
hepatorenal dysfunction (肝肾功能障碍): impaired function of both the liver and kidneys.


词源:
The word "hepatorenal" is a combination of two combining forms:

* **Hepato-**:  From Greek "hēpar," meaning "liver."
* **-renal**: From Latin "renalis," meaning "of or pertaining to the kidneys."

Thus, "hepatorenal" literally means "relating to both the liver and kidneys."
