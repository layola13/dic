encephalopathy: /ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɑːpəθi/ | n. |脑病|A disease of the brain.  Encephalopathy is a general term for any disease or disorder of the brain. It is not a specific disease itself, but rather a symptom of an underlying condition that affects brain function.  This can include a wide range of conditions, from infections and injuries to metabolic disorders and toxins.  Symptoms can vary greatly depending on the underlying cause and may include confusion, disorientation, memory loss, seizures, changes in personality, and altered levels of consciousness. |The patient presented with encephalopathy following a severe head injury. 患者头部严重受伤后出现了脑病。| hepatic encephalopathy, metabolic encephalopathy, toxic encephalopathy |healthy brain function|
