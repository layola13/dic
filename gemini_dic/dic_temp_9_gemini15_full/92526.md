hypovitaminosis:/ˌhaɪpoʊˌvaɪtəməˈnoʊsɪs/| n. |维生素缺乏症|A deficiency of one or more vitamins in the body, leading to various health problems.  This condition can manifest in various ways depending on the specific vitamin deficiency.|  Examples include scurvy (vitamin C deficiency) and rickets (vitamin D deficiency).  The symptoms can range from mild fatigue to severe organ damage, depending on the severity and type of deficiency.|近义词：vitamin deficiency, avitaminosis|反义词：hypervitaminosis
