extemporised:/ɪkˈstempəraɪzd/| v. |即兴创作；临时准备 |To speak or perform without preparation; to improvise.  |例句：She extemporised a speech on the importance of education. 她即兴发表了关于教育重要性的演讲。

例句：He extemporised a song for his friend's birthday. 他为朋友的生日即兴创作了一首歌。


近义词：improvise, ad-lib, impromptu, off-the-cuff |反义词：rehearse, prepare, plan


词源:  The word "extemporised" comes from the Latin word "ex tempore," meaning "out of time" or "on the spur of the moment."  The prefix "ex-" means "out of" or "from," and "tempore" means "time."  The word evolved through Middle French and eventually into English.
