photomezzotype:/ˌfoʊtoʊˈmɛzətaɪp/ | n. |照相半色调版 |A photomezzotype is a type of photomechanical printing process used to create high-quality reproductions of photographs and other artwork. It involves exposing a sensitized gelatin plate to light, creating a relief image that is then used to print. The result is a continuous-tone image with a wide range of tonal values, similar to a mezzotint but produced photographically.| Example: The museum displayed a stunning photomezzotype of a 19th-century landscape.  |近义词：photogravure, collotype |反义词：None (No direct antonym exists)|名词
