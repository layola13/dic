preaffirmed:/ˌpriːəˈfɜːrmd/| v.|预先确认；事先肯定|To affirm something beforehand; to confirm something in advance.  This is a relatively rare word, formed by combining "pre-" (before) and "affirmed" (stated positively). It implies a strong and confident assertion made before an event or action.|例句：The judge preaffirmed the verdict before the jury even reached a decision. = 法官在陪审团甚至做出裁决之前就预先确认了判决。  The company preaffirmed its commitment to sustainability in its annual report. = 公司在其年度报告中预先肯定了其对可持续发展的承诺。|近义词：preconfirm, reaffirm, confirm beforehand|反义词：deny, contradict, refute|动词
