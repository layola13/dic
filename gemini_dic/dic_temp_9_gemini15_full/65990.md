exalters:/ɪɡˈzɔːltərz/| v.| 赞扬；提升；使得意；| To raise in rank, power, or importance; to praise highly.  |The church exalters God's glory. 教堂赞扬上帝的荣耀。|近义词：elevate, praise, glorify, magnify|反义词：degrade, humiliate, belittle


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exalts the virtues of hard work. 他赞扬努力工作的优点。
She exalts her children's achievements. 她赞扬她孩子的成就。


一般过去时:
The king exalted his loyal subjects. 国王提升了他忠诚的臣民。
They exalted the hero after the victory. 胜利后，他们赞扬了这位英雄。


现在完成时:
The media has exalted the athlete's performance. 媒体已经赞扬了这位运动员的出色表现。
She has exalted her position in the company. 她已经提升了她在公司的地位。


过去完成时:
They had exalted him to the position of CEO. 他们已经将他提升为首席执行官。
The crowd had exalted the winning team. 人群已经赞扬了获胜的球队。


将来时:
The company will exalt its new product. 公司将大力宣传其新产品。
He will exalt his family's legacy. 他将提升他家族的遗产。


虚拟语气:
If they exalted him, he would be very happy. 如果他们赞扬他，他会非常高兴。
I wish they would exalt her contributions. 我希望他们能赞扬她的贡献。


被动语态:
His achievements were exalted by his peers. 他的成就受到了同行的赞扬。
The new policy was exalted as a great success. 新政策被誉为巨大的成功。


疑问句:
Does the company exalt its employees? 公司是否赞扬其员工？
Will they exalt the winner of the competition? 他们会赞扬比赛的获胜者吗？


否定句:
They did not exalt his efforts. 他们没有赞扬他的努力。
The government did not exalt the opposition party's views. 政府没有赞扬反对党的观点。
