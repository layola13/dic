interviewer:/ˈɪntərˌvjuːər/ | n. | 采访者；面试官 | A person who conducts an interview, typically to assess a candidate for a job or to gather information for a news story. | Example: The interviewer asked me about my experience.  面试官问我关于我的经验。 |近义词：questioner, inquisitor | 反义词：interviewee


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The interviewer asks insightful questions. = 面试官问了一些有见地的的问题。
The interviewer assesses the candidate's skills. = 面试官评估求职者的技能。


一般过去时:
The interviewer asked me about my salary expectations. = 面试官问我关于我的薪资期望。
The interviewer praised my qualifications. = 面试官称赞了我的资历。


现在完成时:
The interviewer has already interviewed five candidates. = 面试官已经面试了五个候选人。
The interviewer has asked many difficult questions. = 面试官已经问了很多难题。


过去完成时:
The interviewer had reviewed my resume before the interview. = 面试官在面试前已经审查过我的简历。
The interviewer had already formed an opinion before I spoke. = 在我开口之前，面试官就已经形成了自己的看法。


将来时:
The interviewer will contact me next week. = 面试官下周会联系我。
The interviewer will ask about my strengths and weaknesses. = 面试官会询问我的优点和缺点。


虚拟语气:
If the interviewer were more friendly, I would feel more relaxed. = 如果面试官更友善一些，我会感觉更放松。
I wish the interviewer had asked more challenging questions. = 我希望面试官能问一些更有挑战性的问题。


被动语态:
The candidates were interviewed by the hiring manager. = 候选人由招聘经理进行面试。
The questions were carefully prepared by the interviewer. = 问题是由面试官仔细准备的。


疑问句:
What does the interviewer want to know? = 面试官想知道什么？
Who is the interviewer for this position? = 这个职位的采访者是谁？


否定句:
The interviewer didn't ask me about my weaknesses. = 面试官没有问我我的缺点。
The interviewer didn't seem impressed with my answers. = 面试官似乎对我的回答并不印象深刻。
