damningness:/ˈdæmɪŋnəs/| n. | 令人谴责的程度；罪证确凿的程度；毁灭性；有害性 | The damningness of the evidence sealed his fate.  证据的罪证确凿的程度决定了他的命运。|近义词：condemnation, culpability, incrimination |反义词：exoneration, vindication


英文解析:The quality or state of being damning; the extent to which something is clearly incriminating or harmful.  It refers to the degree to which something provides strong evidence of guilt or wrongdoing, or the severity of its negative consequences.


例句:

The damningness of the leaked emails led to the politician's resignation.  泄露的电子邮件的毁灭性导致了这位政治家的辞职。

The damningness of the situation was undeniable; the company faced potential bankruptcy.  局势的严重性是不容否认的；公司面临着潜在的破产。


词源:  Derived from the adjective "damning," which ultimately comes from the verb "damn," meaning to condemn or to send to hell.  The suffix "-ness" transforms the adjective into a noun, indicating a state or quality.
