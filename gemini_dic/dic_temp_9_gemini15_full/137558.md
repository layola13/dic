nontransitive:/ˌnɒnˈtrænzɪtɪv/| adj.|非及物的|Describing a verb that does not take a direct object.  A nontransitive verb expresses an action or state of being that does not directly affect another noun or pronoun.  For example, "sleep" is a nontransitive verb because one cannot "sleep something".|The dog sleeps soundly.  这只狗睡得很香。
The sun shone brightly.|太阳明亮地照耀着。|intransitive, non-objective|transitive, objective|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sleeps eight hours a day. = 他每天睡八个小时。
She sings beautifully. = 她唱歌很美。


一般过去时:
They walked in the park yesterday. = 他们昨天在公园散步。
The bird flew away. = 鸟飞走了。


现在完成时:
I have slept well. = 我睡得很香。
She has sung in many concerts. = 她已在许多音乐会上演唱过。


过去完成时:
He had walked miles before he reached the village. = 他走了几英里才到达村庄。
The rain had stopped before we arrived. = 我们到达之前雨已经停了。


将来时:
They will sleep in the hotel tonight. = 他们今晚将在酒店睡觉。
She will sing at the wedding. = 她将在婚礼上唱歌。


虚拟语气:
If I slept more, I would feel better. = 如果我睡得更多，我会感觉更好。
I wish I had sung better. = 我希望我唱得更好。


被动语态:
(Nontransitive verbs generally cannot be used in the passive voice.)  非及物动词通常不能用于被动语态。
