pectoriloquy:/ˌpɛktərɪˈlɒkwi/| n. | 肺语症，胸部语音 | The transmission of sounds from the lungs or bronchi to the surface of the chest, making it possible to hear the patient's voice through the stethoscope.  This is a sign of consolidation or cavity formation in the lung. | Example: The doctor detected pectoriloquy during the auscultation, suggesting a possible lung problem.  医生在听诊时发现了肺语症，这表明可能有肺部问题。|近义词:bronchophony, whispered pectoriloquy |反义词:no significant antonyms, but the opposite would be a normal, clear lung sound. |名词
