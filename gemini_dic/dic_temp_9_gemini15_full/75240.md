frolicsome:/ˈfrɑːləksəm/| adj. |嬉戏的；欢乐的；顽皮的 |Full of playful gaiety; merry.  Characterized by or given to frolicking. |例句：The frolicsome puppies tumbled over each other in the park.  公园里嬉戏的小狗们互相翻滚在一起。

The children were in a frolicsome mood, running and laughing in the garden. 孩子们兴高采烈，在花园里跑来跑去，欢笑着。|近义词：playful, merry, sportive, mischievous |反义词：serious, solemn, subdued, glum

词源:
From Middle English *frolyksome, from frolic + -some.  The word "frolic" itself has uncertain origins, possibly related to Old French *frole, meaning "to graze" or "to rub."  The suffix "-some" adds the sense of "full of" or "characterized by."
