reorientating: /ˌriːˈɔːriənˌteɪtɪŋ/ | v. |重新定向；重新定位；使改观 |  The act of changing someone's or something's orientation;  to change the direction or focus of something.  |例句：The company is reorientating its marketing strategy to target a younger demographic. 公司正在调整其营销策略以瞄准更年轻的人群。

例句：The navigation system helped reorientate the lost hikers. 导航系统帮助迷路的徒步旅行者重新定位方向。


近义词：redirect, refocus, reposition, realign | 反义词：misalign, disorient


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are reorientating their business model. = 他们正在调整他们的商业模式。
We reorientate our goals every quarter. = 我们每个季度都会重新调整我们的目标。


一般过去时:
The teacher reorientated the students' attention to the board. = 老师重新将学生的注意力吸引到黑板上。
She reorientated her career path after having children. = 她生孩子后重新规划了职业道路。


现在完成时:
The company has reorientated its resources towards sustainability. = 公司已经将其资源重新定向到可持续发展方面。
They have reorientated their thinking about the problem. = 他们已经改变了对这个问题的看法。


过去完成时:
The pilot had reorientated the plane before landing. = 飞行员在降落前已经重新调整了飞机的方向。
He had reorientated his life after the accident. = 事故后，他重新规划了自己的生活。


将来时:
The government will reorientate its foreign policy. = 政府将重新调整其外交政策。
We will reorientate the furniture in the living room. = 我们将重新布置客厅的家具。


虚拟语气:
If we reorientated our priorities, we could achieve more. = 如果我们重新调整优先级，就能取得更多成就。
I wish I had reorientated my career earlier. = 我希望我早点重新规划职业道路。


被动语态:
The map was reorientated to show the new highway. = 地图被重新定向以显示新的高速公路。
The company's strategy is being reorientated to improve profitability. = 公司的战略正在被调整以提高盈利能力。


疑问句:

How will the company reorientate its operations? = 公司将如何调整其运营？


否定句:
The project was not reorientated despite the setbacks. = 尽管遭遇挫折，项目并未重新定向。
They did not reorientate their efforts towards the new goal. = 他们没有将精力重新转向新的目标。
