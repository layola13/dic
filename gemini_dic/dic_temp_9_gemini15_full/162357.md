preacknowledging: /ˌpriːækˈnɒlɪdʒɪŋ/ | v. | 预先承认；预先告知 |  The act of acknowledging something before it officially happens or is formally announced.  It implies a prior awareness or anticipation of an event or fact.  This word is rarely used and is often considered overly formal or even awkward.  | 例句：Preacknowledging the potential risks, the company developed a comprehensive safety plan. = 预先承认潜在风险，公司制定了全面的安全计划。
The team’s preacknowledging of the project’s challenges led to a more efficient approach.=团队预先承认项目挑战，从而采取了更高效的方法。 | 近义词：anticipating, foreseeing, foreshadowing | 反义词：ignoring, overlooking, disregarding |
