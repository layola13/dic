re-estimation: /ˌriːˌɛstɪˈmeɪʃən/ | n. |重新估计，重新评估 | The act of estimating something again, often after obtaining new information or correcting previous errors.  It involves revising an initial assessment or calculation. |例句：The re-estimation of the project's cost was necessary after the discovery of unforeseen complications.  项目在发现不可预见的复杂情况后，需要重新评估成本。


The re-estimation of the damage caused by the hurricane is still ongoing.  飓风造成的损失的重新评估仍在进行中。|近义词： reassessment, recalculation, reappraisal |反义词： initial estimation, first assessment

一般现在时:
The team performs a re-estimation of the project's timeline every week. = 团队每周都会对项目的进度进行重新评估。
We regularly re-estimate the budget to ensure accuracy. = 我们定期重新评估预算以确保准确性。

否定句:
