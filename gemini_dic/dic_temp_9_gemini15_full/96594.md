induction's: /ɪnˈdʌkʃənz/ | n. | 归纳法；感应；就职典礼；引入；启蒙 | The process of inferring a general law or principle from the observation of particular instances.  It also refers to the process by which an object becomes electrically charged or a magnetic field is produced. In a more figurative sense, it can mean the act of introducing someone to a new job or position. |例句：

The scientist used induction to arrive at a theory about the behavior of light.  = 科学家用归纳法得出了关于光行为的理论。

The process of induction involves creating an electric current in a coil of wire by changing the magnetic field near it. = 感应过程包括通过改变其附近的磁场在电线线圈中产生电流。


近义词：deduction, inference, initiation, introduction | 反义词：deduction (in the context of reasoning)
