rebounds:/ˈriːbaʊndz/| n.|反弹；篮板球；恢复；反作用力|The act of bouncing back;  a recovery from setback; in basketball, a ball that bounces off the rim or backboard.  |例句：The basketball player grabbed the rebound.  这个篮球运动员抓住了篮板球。
The company is experiencing a strong rebound after the economic downturn. 公司在经济低迷后正在强劲反弹。|近义词：recovery, resurgence, rally|反义词：decline, downturn, collapse|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The ball rebounds off the wall. = 球从墙上反弹回来。
He rebounds from every setback. = 他从每一次挫折中恢复过来。

一般过去时:
The price rebounded sharply after the announcement. = 公告发布后，价格大幅反弹。
She rebounded quickly from her illness. = 她很快从病中恢复过来。


现在完成时:
The economy has rebounded significantly this year. = 今年经济已大幅反弹。
The team has rebounded from its earlier losses. = 球队已经从早些时候的失利中恢复过来。


过去完成时:
The stock market had rebounded before the news broke. = 在消息传出之前，股市已经反弹了。
He had rebounded from numerous failures before achieving success. = 在获得成功之前，他经历过无数次失败并从中恢复过来。


将来时:
The market is expected to rebound next quarter. = 预计市场将在下个季度反弹。

虚拟语气:
