dryad:/ˈdraɪæd/| n. |树 нимфа,树精|A dryad is a tree nymph or spirit in Greek mythology, closely associated with and often identified with a particular tree.  They are believed to live and die with their tree.  Dryads are often depicted as beautiful women who are guardians of the forest and its inhabitants.|Example:  In the ancient Greek myth, the dryad was inseparable from the oak tree she inhabited.  在古希腊神话中，树 нимфа 与她居住的橡树密不可分。|近义词: wood nymph, tree spirit, hamadryad |反义词:  N/A
