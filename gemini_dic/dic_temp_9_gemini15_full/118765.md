metalliferous:/ˌmɛtəlˈɪfərəs/| adj.|含金属的；产金属的|Containing or producing metal.  The term refers to rocks, ores, or regions containing significant amounts of metallic minerals.|例句：The metalliferous region yielded a rich harvest of copper and zinc.  这个含金属的地区出产了大量的铜和锌。
The geologist identified the rock as metalliferous based on its high iron content. 地质学家根据岩石的高铁含量将其鉴定为含金属的岩石。|近义词：metal-bearing, mineral-rich|反义词：non-metalliferous


词源:

* **metal-:**  From Old French *metal*, from Latin *metallum* ("mine," "metal").
* **-ferous:** From Latin *-fer* ("bearing," "carrying"), a suffix used to form adjectives indicating the capacity to produce or bear something.  This suffix is also seen in words like "pestiferous" (disease-bearing) and "coniferous" (cone-bearing).
