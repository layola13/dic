relative:/ˈrelətɪv/| adj., n.|亲戚的；相对的；相关的| Relating to, connected with, or belonging to something else.  A person connected by blood or marriage.|例句：He is a relative newcomer to the field. 他是这个领域的相对新人。


例句：My relatives live in different parts of the country. 我的亲戚们住在全国各地。


近义词：kin, relation, comparative, relevant|反义词：absolute, unrelated, unconnected|词性:形容词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is relative to the problem. = 他与这个问题有关。
The price is relative to the quality. = 价格与质量相关。

一般过去时:
The two events were relatively close together in time. = 这两件事发生的时间比较接近。
The results were relative to the experiment’s conditions. = 结果与实验条件相关。

现在完成时:
She has been relatively successful in her career. = 她在事业上取得了相对的成功。
The changes have been relative to the previous year’s figures. = 这些变化与去年的数据有关。

过去完成时:
The decision had been relative to the available resources. = 这一决定与可用的资源有关。
The damage had been relatively minor compared to the initial expectations. = 与最初的预期相比，损失相对较小。

将来时:
The outcome will be relative to the efforts put in. = 结果将取决于付出的努力。
The new policy will be relatively easy to implement. = 新政策将相对容易实施。

虚拟语气:
If the cost were relative to the benefits, we would proceed. = 如果成本与收益相关，我们将继续进行。
If the information were more readily relative, the decision would be easier. = 如果信息更容易获得且相关，决策就会更容易。

被动语态:
The success is relative to many factors. = 成功取决于许多因素。
The findings were relative to the hypotheses tested. = 研究结果与所检验的假设相关。

疑问句:
Is the cost relative to the benefit? = 成本与效益相关吗？
Is your decision relative to the current situation? = 你的决定与当前形势相关吗？

否定句:
The two concepts are not relative. = 这两个概念无关。
His success was not relative to his effort. = 他的成功与其努力无关。
