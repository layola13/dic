nullify:/ˈnʌlɪfaɪ/| vt. |使无效；取消；废除 |to make something legally or officially ineffective or invalid|The contract was nullified by a court order. 法院命令使合同无效。|近义词：invalidate, annul, cancel, abolish |反义词：validate, ratify, confirm |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:

He nullifies his previous statement. = 他推翻了他之前的声明。


一般过去时:
The court nullified the agreement. = 法院宣布该协议无效。
She nullified her own argument. = 她推翻了自己的论点。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->

nullify an effect: 消除影响
nullify someone's efforts:  使某人的努力付诸东流


词源:
The word "nullify" comes from the Latin word "nullificare," which is a combination of "nullus" (meaning "nothing" or "no") and "facere" (meaning "to make" or "to do").  Therefore, "nullificare" literally means "to make nothing" or "to make void."  The word entered English in the 16th century.
