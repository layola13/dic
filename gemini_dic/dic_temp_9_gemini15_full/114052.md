magnetised:/ˈmæɡnɪtaɪzd/|/ˈmæɡnətaɪzd/| adj. | 磁化的；有磁性的 |  Magnetised refers to something that has become magnetic, either naturally or artificially.  This means it has acquired the properties of a magnet, attracting or repelling other magnetic materials. | Example: The needle was magnetised after being placed near a strong magnet.  磁铁附近放置后，针被磁化了。 The iron filings became magnetised when exposed to the magnetic field.  铁屑暴露在磁场中后被磁化了。|近义词：magnetic, magnetized, attracted |反义词：demagnetized, unmagnetized
