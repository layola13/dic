felony:/ˈfɛləni/| n. |重罪|A felony is a serious crime, typically one involving violence, that is punishable by imprisonment for more than one year or by death.  Felonies are distinguished from misdemeanors, which are less serious crimes.|例句：Murder is a felony. 谋杀是重罪。

He was charged with a felony. 他被指控犯有重罪。


近义词：grave crime, serious offense|反义词：misdemeanor
