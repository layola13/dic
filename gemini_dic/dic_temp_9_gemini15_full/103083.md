jeoparder:/ˈdʒɛpərdi/| vt.|危及；使陷于危险|to put something or someone into a dangerous situation|例句：His reckless driving jeopardized the safety of others. 他鲁莽的驾驶危及了其他人的安全。

例句：Don't jeopardize your health by smoking. 不要吸烟损害你的健康。


近义词：endanger, risk, imperil, threaten|反义词：protect, safeguard, secure|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jeopardizes his career by being late to work. = 他上班迟到，危及了他的职业生涯。
The company jeopardizes its reputation by releasing faulty products. = 公司发布有缺陷的产品，危及了它的声誉。

一般过去时:
His careless actions jeopardized the entire project. = 他粗心的行为危及了整个项目。
The storm jeopardized the safety of the climbers. = 暴风雨危及了登山者的安全。

现在完成时:
His irresponsible behavior has jeopardized his relationship with his family. = 他不负责任的行为已经危及了他与家人的关系。
The scandal has jeopardized the politician's career. = 这场丑闻已经危及了这位政治家的职业生涯。

过去完成时:
They had jeopardized their chances of success by not preparing adequately. = 他们因为没有充分准备而危及了他们成功的几率。
The company had jeopardized its financial stability by making poor investments. = 公司因进行不良投资而危及了其财务稳定性。

将来时:
The delay will jeopardize the deadline. = 延误将危及截止日期。
Further cuts in funding could jeopardize the project. = 进一步削减资金可能会危及该项目。

虚拟语气:
If he continued to drink, he would jeopardize his health. = 如果他继续喝酒，他会危及他的健康。
I wish I hadn't jeopardized my chances by being so arrogant. = 我真希望我不要因为如此傲慢而危及我的机会。

被动语态:
The mission was jeopardized by the unexpected storm. = 任务因突如其来的风暴而受阻。
His reputation was jeopardized by the false accusations. = 他的名誉因不实的指控而受损。

疑问句:
Does this decision jeopardize our future prospects? = 这个决定会危及我们的未来前景吗？
Will this action jeopardize the peace talks? = 这项行动会危及和平谈判吗？

否定句:
We will not jeopardize our principles for the sake of profit. = 我们不会为了利润而危及我们的原则。
The company took steps to ensure that the project would not be jeopardized. = 公司采取措施以确保项目不会受阻。
