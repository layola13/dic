hydrogenide: /ˈhaɪdrədʒənaɪd/ | n. |氢化物 | A compound of hydrogen with another element, especially a metal.  Hydrogenides are formed when hydrogen reacts with other elements, often metals, resulting in the formation of ionic or covalent bonds.  | Example: Sodium hydride (NaH) is a common example of a hydrogenide used in organic chemistry.  钠氢化物(NaH)是常用在有机化学中的氢化物的一个例子。|近义词: hydride (more common)|反义词:  None specifically, but the opposite concept would be a compound lacking hydrogen.
