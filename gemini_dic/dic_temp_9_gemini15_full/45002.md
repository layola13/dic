crystallography:/ˌkrɪstəˈlɒɡrəfi/ | n. |晶体学|The scientific study of the arrangement of atoms in crystalline solids.  It involves determining the structure of crystals, which are highly ordered solids with repeating patterns of atoms, ions, or molecules.  Techniques used include X-ray diffraction, neutron diffraction, and electron diffraction.|例句：X-ray crystallography is a powerful technique used to determine the three-dimensional structure of molecules. X射线晶体学是一种强大的技术，用于确定分子的三维结构。

例句：Advances in crystallography have revolutionized our understanding of materials science. 晶体学的发展彻底改变了我们对材料科学的理解。|近义词：crystal structure analysis |反义词：amorphous study (非晶体研究)
