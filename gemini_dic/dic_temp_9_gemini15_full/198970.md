sightful: /ˈsaɪtfʊl/ | adj. | 有见识的；有洞察力的；有眼光的 | Having or showing good judgment, insight, or perception.  The word is rarely used in modern English and is considered archaic or obsolete.  It implies a keen understanding or perception, often beyond the ordinary. |  例句：He possessed a sightful mind, able to grasp complex issues quickly. 他拥有一个有洞察力的头脑，能够迅速掌握复杂的问题。


近义词：perceptive, insightful, discerning, perspicacious, sagacious | 反义词：blind, unperceptive, obtuse, unseeing

词性: 形容词

USAGE->

词源: sight (视觉，景象) + -ful (形容词后缀，表示“充满…的”， “具有…性质的”)  The word combines the idea of sight with the implication of keen understanding, suggesting that a sightful person sees beyond the surface.
