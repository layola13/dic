coerces:/kəʊˈɜːrs/ | vt. | 强迫；胁迫；迫使 | To persuade (an unwilling person) to do something by using force or threats.  | The government coerced the opposition party into accepting the new law. 政府强迫反对党接受新法律。|  compel, force, pressure | persuade, encourage | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He coerces his employees into working overtime. 他强迫他的员工加班。
The dictator coerces his people into obedience. 独裁者强迫人民服从。


一般过去时:
She coerced him into signing the contract. 她强迫他签署合同。
The bully coerced the younger children into giving him their lunch money. 恶霸强迫年纪较小的孩子给他他们的午餐钱。


现在完成时:
They have coerced the witness into changing his testimony. 他们已经强迫证人改变他的证词。
The regime has coerced its citizens into silence. 该政权迫使公民保持沉默。


过去完成时:
The thief had coerced the shopkeeper into opening the safe. 小偷已经强迫店主打开保险箱。
She had coerced her son into attending the family gathering. 她已经强迫她的儿子参加家庭聚会。


将来时:
The company will coerce its suppliers into lowering their prices. 公司将强迫其供应商降低价格。
He will coerce his friend into helping him move. 他将强迫他的朋友帮他搬家。


虚拟语气:
If he coerced her, she would have reported him to the police. 如果他强迫她，她会向警方举报他。
I wish he hadn't coerced me into doing that. 我真希望他没有强迫我那样做。


被动语态:
The workers were coerced into accepting lower wages. 工人们被迫接受较低的工资。
He felt coerced into making a decision he didn't agree with. 他感到被迫做出一个他不赞成的决定。


疑问句:

否定句:
He did not coerce anyone into doing anything. 他没有强迫任何人做任何事。
They didn't coerce her into revealing her secrets. 他们没有强迫她泄露她的秘密。
