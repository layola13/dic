overbooking:/ˈoʊvərˌbʊkɪŋ/ | n. | 超额预订 | The practice of selling or booking more tickets, rooms, or seats than are actually available.  This is often done by businesses to account for cancellations.  | Example: The airline apologized for the overbooking and offered passengers compensation.  航空公司为超额预订道歉，并向乘客提供赔偿。

Example: The hotel experienced an overbooking due to a large conference in town.  这家酒店因为镇上举行大型会议而出现了超额预订的情况。|近义词：overscheduling, oversubscription | 反义词：undersubscription, underbooking |  词性：名词

The hotel often overbooks during peak season. = 酒店在旺季经常超额预订。
They usually overbook flights to compensate for cancellations. = 他们通常会超额预订航班以弥补取消的情况。


一般过去时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:
