predisplacing: /ˌpriːdɪˈspleɪsɪŋ/ | v. | 预先移动，提前安置 |  The act of moving something beforehand; to displace something before something else.  It often implies preparing or arranging something in advance. |


例句：

The construction company predisplaced the utilities before beginning demolition. = 建设公司在开始拆除工作之前预先迁移了公用设施。

They predisplaced the furniture to make room for the party. = 他们提前移动家具，为聚会腾出空间。


近义词：prelocate, relocate beforehand,  preposition


反义词：postplacing (Not a standard word, but conveys the opposite meaning)


词性：动词
