deconstructed:/ˌdiːkənˈstrʌktɪd/ | adj. | 解构的；被解构的 |  Describes something that has been analyzed by breaking it down into its constituent parts to reveal its underlying structure or meaning, often to expose contradictions or inconsistencies.  It often implies a critical or subversive approach to the subject matter. | Example: The deconstructed essay challenged traditional notions of narrative structure.  解构的论文挑战了传统的叙事结构观念。The deconstructed building revealed its intricate inner workings. 被解构的建筑展现了其错综复杂的内部运作。 |近义词:analyzed, dissected, broken down, interpreted | 反义词:constructed, synthesized, unified | 词性:形容词
