generator:/ˈdʒɛnəreɪtər/ | n. | 发电机；发生器；生产者 | A generator is a machine that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy.  It is also something that produces or creates something. | Example: The power plant uses a large generator to produce electricity.  这家发电厂使用大型发电机发电。 The writer is a generator of new ideas.  这位作家是新思想的产生者。 |近义词：producer, creator, engine |反义词：consumer


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The generator produces electricity. = 发电机发电。
This machine generates heat. = 这台机器产生热量。

一般过去时:
The generator produced enough power for the whole town. = 发电机为全镇提供了足够的电力。
The experiment generated surprising results. = 实验产生了令人惊讶的结果。

现在完成时:
The company has generated significant profits this year. = 公司今年获得了可观的利润。
We have generated a lot of new leads this month. = 我们这个月已经开发了很多新的潜在客户。

过去完成时:
They had generated a lot of waste before the new regulations were implemented. = 新规实施之前，他们已经产生了大量的废物。
The scientist had generated a hypothesis before starting the experiment. = 科学家在开始实验之前已经提出了一个假设。


将来时:
The new wind turbine will generate clean energy. = 新的风力涡轮机将产生清洁能源。
The project will generate many job opportunities. = 该项目将创造许多就业机会。

虚拟语气:
If the generator worked, we would have power. = 如果发电机运转，我们就会有电力。
If the idea generated interest, we would pursue it further. = 如果这个想法引起了兴趣，我们会进一步研究。

被动语态:
Electricity is generated by the generator. = 电力是由发电机产生的。
The report was generated automatically by the system. = 报告是由系统自动生成的。

疑问句:
What generates this sound? = 什么产生了这种声音？
How much electricity does the generator generate? = 发电机产生多少电力？

否定句:
The generator didn't produce enough power. = 发电机没有产生足够的电力。
The campaign didn't generate much interest. = 这场活动没有引起多大兴趣。
