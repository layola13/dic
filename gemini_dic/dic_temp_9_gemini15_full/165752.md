prenostic:/ˌprɛˈnɒstɪk/| n. |预兆，预感，预言|A prenostic is a sign or indication of something that will happen in the future; a prediction or prophecy.  It often implies an intuitive or mystical sense of foreknowledge rather than a reasoned prediction.|例句：The dark clouds were a prenostic of the approaching storm.  乌云是即将到来的暴风雨的预兆。

The unsettling silence was a prenostic of trouble.  令人不安的寂静预示着麻烦。|近义词：omen, portent, presage, augury, prediction, prophecy|反义词：certainty (in the sense of a sure absence of a future event), reality (when the prenostic is false)|名词
