reliance: /rɪˈlaɪəns/ | n. |依赖，依靠，信赖 | Dependence or trust in someone or something.  |例句：My reliance on public transport is absolute.  我的生活完全依赖公共交通。|例句：Their success is based on their reliance on each other. 他们的成功基于他们彼此间的信赖。|近义词：dependence, trust, confidence |反义词：independence, self-reliance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He relies on his parents for financial support. = 他依靠父母提供经济支持。
She relies heavily on her intuition. = 她非常依赖自己的直觉。

一般过去时:
They relied on outdated information. = 他们依赖过时信息。
I relied on him to finish the project. = 我信赖他会完成项目。

现在完成时:
We have relied on this system for years. = 我们多年来一直依赖这个系统。
She has relied on her friends for emotional support. = 她一直依靠朋友给予情感支持。

过去完成时:
He had relied on his brother's help before. = 他以前曾依赖他兄弟的帮助。
They had relied on the weather forecast, which proved inaccurate. = 他们依赖天气预报，结果证明是不准确的。


将来时:
We will rely on new technology to solve the problem. = 我们将依靠新技术来解决这个问题。
She will rely on her experience to guide her decision. = 她将依靠经验来指导她的决定。

虚拟语气:
If I could, I would rely on my own judgment. = 如果可以，我会依靠自己的判断。
I wish I didn't have to rely on others so much. = 我希望我不必那么依赖别人。

被动语态:
The company is heavily relied upon by its clients. = 该公司受到客户的高度依赖。
This method is rarely relied upon in modern practice. = 这种方法在现代实践中很少被依赖。

疑问句:
Can we rely on the accuracy of this data? = 我们能依赖这些数据的准确性吗？
Do you rely on anyone for emotional support? = 你依赖任何人来寻求情感支持吗？

否定句:
He doesn't rely on anyone for help. = 他不依赖任何人帮助。
We cannot rely on luck alone to succeed. = 我们不能仅仅依靠运气来成功。


PHRASE->
