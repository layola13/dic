keymaps:/ˈkiːmæps/| n. |按键映射，键盘映射|A keymap is a configuration that defines the relationship between physical keys on a keyboard and the characters or commands they produce.  It's essentially a translation table. For example, a keymap might specify that pressing the 'A' key produces the letter 'a', while pressing 'Shift' + '2' produces the symbol '@'.  Different operating systems and applications may use different keymaps to accommodate various keyboard layouts and languages.|Example: The new keymap allows for easier typing of accented characters.  新的键盘映射使得打出带重音符号的字符更容易了。|近义词：keyboard layout, key assignment|反义词：无
