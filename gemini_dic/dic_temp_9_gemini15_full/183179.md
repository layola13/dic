reprecipitate:/ˌriːpriˈsɪpɪteɪt/| v.|再沉淀；重新沉淀|To precipitate again; to cause (a substance) to separate from solution as a solid.|The impurities were removed by reprecipitation.|近义词：re-precipitate|反义词：dissolve


USAGE->

The chemist reprecipitates the solution to purify it. = 化学家重新沉淀溶液以使其纯化。
The experiment reprecipitates the protein multiple times. = 实验多次重新沉淀蛋白质。

They reprecipitated the sample to ensure its purity. = 他们重新沉淀样品以确保其纯度。

PHRASE->

reprecipitate a substance = 重新沉淀一种物质
reprecipitate the protein = 重新沉淀蛋白质
reprecipitate the precipitate = 再沉淀沉淀物
reprecipitate to purify = 为了纯化而再沉淀


词源:
re- (prefix indicating repetition) + precipitate (from Latin praecipitare, meaning "to throw headlong," "to hasten,"  referring to the rapid separation of a solid from a solution).
