metafictional:/ˌmɛtəˈfɪkʃənəl/| adj. |元小说(的)，超小说(的) | Relating to or characteristic of metafiction; self-consciously drawing attention to its own artificiality as a literary construct.  Metafictional works often blur the lines between fiction and reality, and the author may directly address the reader or comment on the writing process itself. | Example:  The novel's metafictional elements, such as the author's frequent asides to the reader, disrupted the traditional narrative flow.  这部小说的元小说元素，例如作者经常对读者的旁白，扰乱了传统的叙事流程。|近义词：self-reflexive, self-aware |反义词：realistic, straightforward
