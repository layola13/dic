disputable:/dɪˈspjuːtəbl/ | adj. | 有争议的；可争论的 | open to question or discussion; debatable.  | The date of the painting is disputable. 这幅画的创作日期是有争议的。| debatable, questionable, contentious | undeniable, indisputable, certain


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The evidence presented is disputable. = 提交的证据是有争议的。
His claims are disputable. = 他的说法是有争议的。

一般过去时:
The results of the election were disputable. = 选举结果是有争议的。
The historian's interpretation of the event was disputable. = 这位历史学家对该事件的解读是有争议的。

现在完成时:
The authorship of the book has been disputable for years. = 这本书的作者身份多年来一直存在争议。
Many aspects of the theory have been disputable. = 理论的许多方面一直存在争议。

过去完成时:
Before the new evidence emerged, the verdict had been disputable. = 在新的证据出现之前，判决一直是有争议的。
By the time the trial concluded, the defendant's guilt had been disputable. = 到审判结束时，被告的罪责一直是有争议的。

将来时:
The outcome of the negotiations will likely be disputable. = 谈判的结果很可能是有争议的。
The new policy will undoubtedly be disputable. = 新政策无疑将是有争议的。

虚拟语气:
If the evidence were more conclusive, the case wouldn't be disputable. = 如果证据更确凿，案件就不会有争议了。
Were the facts different, the decision would not be disputable. = 如果事实不同，决定就不会有争议了。

被动语态:
The decision was widely disputed. = 这个决定受到了广泛的争议。
The findings of the study were disputed by many experts. = 该研究的结果受到了许多专家的质疑。

疑问句:
Is the claim disputable? = 这个说法有争议吗？
Are the results of the experiment disputable? = 实验的结果有争议吗？

否定句:
The facts are not disputable. = 事实是无可辩驳的。
Her expertise in the field is not disputable. = 她在这个领域的专业知识是无可置疑的。
