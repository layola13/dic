词源:

From *latitude* + *-al*.  "Latitude" ultimately derives from the Latin *latitudo*, from *latus* ("wide").  The suffix *-al* is an adjectival suffix.  Therefore, "latitudinal" literally means "relating to width" in a geographic context, referring to the distance from the equator.
