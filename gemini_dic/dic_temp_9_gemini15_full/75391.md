frozen:/ˈfroʊzn/ /ˈfrəʊzn/ | adj. | 冷冻的；冻僵的；凝固的 |  Frozen describes something that has been subjected to freezing temperatures, resulting in a solid state. It can refer to food, water, or even people who are extremely cold.  |例句：The lake is frozen solid.  湖面结冰了。

The meat is frozen. 肉是冷冻的。


近义词：icy, frigid, congealed | 反义词：melted, thawed, liquid | 词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The water is frozen. = 水冻住了。
This food is frozen. = 这食物是冷冻的。


一般过去时:
The river froze overnight. = 河水一夜之间结冰了。
The pie froze solid in the freezer. = 派在冰箱里冻得硬邦邦的。


现在完成时:
The pipes have frozen again. = 水管又冻住了。
We have frozen all the berries for the winter. = 我们把所有的浆果都冷冻起来过冬。


过去完成时:
The food had frozen before we could eat it. = 我们还没来得及吃，食物就冻住了。
The lake had frozen over by the time we arrived. = 我们到达的时候，湖面已经结冰了。


将来时:
The temperature will fall below freezing and the water will freeze. = 气温将降至冰点以下，水会结冰。
We will freeze the leftovers for later. = 我们会把剩菜冷冻起来以后再吃。


虚拟语气:
If the temperature dropped below zero, the lake would freeze. = 如果温度降到零度以下，湖面就会结冰。
I wish the ice cream hadn't frozen so hard. = 我真希望冰淇淋没有冻得这么硬。


被动语态:
The food was frozen quickly. = 食物被迅速冷冻。
The vegetables are being frozen for later use. = 蔬菜正在被冷冻起来以备后用。


疑问句:
Is the soup frozen yet? = 汤冻好了吗？
Are the strawberries frozen? = 草莓冻了吗？


否定句:
The water isn't frozen yet. = 水还没冻。
The ice cream wasn't frozen hard enough. = 冰淇淋没有冻硬。
