recession:/rɪˈseʃən/ | n. |经济衰退，衰退期 | A period of temporary economic decline during which trade and industrial activity are reduced, generally identified by a fall in GDP in two successive quarters.  |例句：The country is experiencing a deep recession. 这个国家正在经历严重的经济衰退。|例句：The recession led to widespread unemployment. 经济衰退导致了大范围的失业。|近义词：depression, downturn, slump |反义词：boom, expansion


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The economy is in recession. = 经济处于衰退之中。
A recession often causes job losses. = 经济衰退通常会导致失业。


一般过去时:
The Great Depression was a severe recession. = 大萧条是一次严重的经济衰退。
The country recovered quickly from the recession. = 该国迅速从经济衰退中恢复过来。


现在完成时:
We have experienced several recessions in the past decade. = 在过去的十年中，我们经历了几次经济衰退。
The company has not fully recovered from the last recession. =  该公司尚未完全从上次经济衰退中恢复过来。


过去完成时:
By the time the government acted, the recession had already deepened. = 当政府采取行动时，经济衰退已经加剧了。
They had predicted the recession months in advance. = 他们几个月前就预测到了经济衰退。


将来时:
Experts predict a recession will occur next year. = 专家预测明年将发生经济衰退。
The government hopes to avoid a recession through its policies. = 政府希望通过其政策避免经济衰退。


虚拟语气:
If we had acted sooner, we might have avoided the recession. = 如果我们早点采取行动，我们也许就能避免经济衰退。
I wish we hadn't entered this recession. = 我真希望我们没有进入这次经济衰退。


被动语态:
