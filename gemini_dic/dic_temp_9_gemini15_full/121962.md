missions:/ˈmɪʃənz/ | n. | 任务；使命；使团 |  A mission is a task or objective that a person or group is given to achieve.  It can also refer to a group of people sent on a special assignment, such as a diplomatic mission. |例句：The astronauts' mission was to explore the moon. 宇航员的任务是探索月球。

The peacekeepers were sent on a mission to the war-torn country. 维和部队被派往饱受战争蹂躏的国家执行任务。|近义词：task, assignment, objective, undertaking |反义词：leisure, recreation


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Our team has several missions this quarter. = 我们团队本季度有几个任务。
Their mission is to help the poor. = 他们的使命是帮助穷人。


一般过去时:
The mission was completed successfully. = 任务圆满完成。
The diplomats completed their mission in peace. = 外交官们和平地完成了他们的使命。


现在完成时:
We have accomplished all our missions this year. = 我们今年完成了所有任务。
They have successfully finished their mission abroad. = 他们已成功完成海外任务。


过去完成时:
The mission had already started before we arrived. = 我们到达之前任务已经开始了。
They had completed the initial phase of the mission. = 他们已经完成了任务的初步阶段。


将来时:
We will launch a new mission next month. = 我们下个月将启动一项新任务。
The team will begin its mission tomorrow. = 该团队明天将开始执行任务。


虚拟语气:
If we had more resources, we could accomplish the mission more quickly. = 如果我们有更多资源，我们可以更快地完成任务。
I wish we could have finished the mission on time. = 我希望我们能按时完成任务。


被动语态:
The mission was delayed by bad weather. = 任务因恶劣天气而延误。
The mission is being carried out by a special team. = 这项任务由一个特别小组执行。


疑问句:
What is the main mission of this organization? = 这个组织的主要任务是什么？
What are the missions for the next fiscal year? = 下一个财政年度的任务是什么？


否定句:
The mission was not a success. = 任务没有成功。
They did not accomplish their mission. = 他们没有完成他们的任务。
