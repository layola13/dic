pandora:/pænˈdɔːrə/| n.|潘多拉；祸端；灾难的来源|The name Pandora comes from Greek mythology, referring to the first woman created by Hephaestus on the orders of Zeus.  She was given a jar (often mistakenly called a box) containing all the evils of the world, which she unleashed upon humanity. The word now symbolizes the release of evils or troubles, often unforeseen consequences. |例句：Opening that Pandora's box unleashed a torrent of problems. 打开那个潘多拉魔盒释放了一连串的问题。

例句：The new policy proved to be a Pandora's box, creating unforeseen complications. 这项新政策证明是一个潘多拉魔盒，造成了不可预见的复杂问题。

近义词：calamity, disaster, catastrophe|反义词：blessing, boon, good fortune|名词
