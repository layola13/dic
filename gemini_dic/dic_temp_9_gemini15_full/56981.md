double-cross:/ˈdʌbəlkrɒs/ | vt. n. |背叛；出卖；欺骗 |To betray or deceive someone, especially by secretly helping their enemy.  It can refer to the act of betraying someone or the person who does the betraying. |例句：He was accused of double-crossing his partners in the deal. 他被指控在交易中背叛了他的伙伴们。

例句：The double-cross led to the collapse of the entire operation.  这次背叛导致了整个行动的崩溃。


近义词：betray, deceive, cheat, mislead, undermine


反义词：support, help, assist, be loyal to


词性：动词和名词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He double-crosses his friends regularly. = 他经常背叛他的朋友。
She double-crosses her opponents in business. = 她在生意场上欺骗她的对手。


一般过去时:
They double-crossed their allies during the war. = 他们在战争期间背叛了他们的盟友。
He double-crossed me and took all the credit. = 他出卖了我，并窃取了所有的功劳。


现在完成时:
He has double-crossed me more than once. = 他不止一次出卖过我。
She has double-crossed many people in her career.  = 她在职业生涯中出卖过很多人。


过去完成时:
I realized he had double-crossed me after the deal was done. =  交易完成后我才意识到他出卖了我。
They discovered that their partner had double-crossed them months ago. = 他们发现他们的合伙人几个月前就背叛了他们。


将来时:
I fear he will double-cross us if we give him too much power. = 我担心如果我们给他太多的权力，他会背叛我们。
They will likely double-cross their competitors to gain an advantage. = 他们很可能会出卖他们的竞争对手以获得优势。


虚拟语气:
If he had not double-crossed us, we would have succeeded. = 如果他没有背叛我们，我们就会成功。
I wish he hadn't double-crossed me; I trusted him implicitly. = 我真希望他没有背叛我；我完全信任他。


被动语态:
The businessman was double-crossed by his own employee. =  这位商人被自己的员工出卖了。
The plan was double-crossed, leading to its failure. = 计划被破坏了，导致计划失败。


疑问句:
Do you think he'll double-cross us? = 你认为他会背叛我们吗？
Has she ever double-crossed anyone before? = 她以前背叛过任何人吗？


否定句:
He would never double-cross his family. = 他永远不会背叛他的家人。
She didn't double-cross her partner in the end. = 她最终没有背叛她的伙伴。
