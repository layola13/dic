judahite:/ˈdʒuːdeɪt/ | n. |犹大人；犹太人 | A Judahite is a person from Judah, an ancient kingdom in the southern part of Canaan, later part of the Kingdom of Israel.  The term often refers to the inhabitants of the Kingdom of Judah after the division of the united Israelite kingdom.  In modern usage, it's often used interchangeably with "Jew," but historically, it has a more specific geographical and political connotation. | The archaeological evidence suggests that the Judahite kingdom was a significant power in the region. |近义词：Jew (in some contexts), Israelite |反义词：None directly applicable, but could be contrasted with terms like "Samaritan" or "Israelite" from the northern kingdom. |
