choreographer:/ˌkɔːriˈɒɡrəfər/ | n. | 编舞者 | A choreographer is a person who creates and arranges dances and ballets.  They plan and design the steps, movements, and patterns of a dance performance. | The choreographer created a stunning dance sequence for the musical.  这位编舞者为音乐剧创作了一个令人惊叹的舞蹈片段。|  dance director, movement director |  (无明显的反义词) | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The choreographer designs the dance moves. = 编舞者设计舞蹈动作。
She choreographs the ballet for the company. = 她为剧团编排芭蕾舞。


一般过去时:
He choreographed a beautiful contemporary dance. = 他编排了一支美丽的现代舞。
The famous choreographer designed the show's opening number. = 著名的编舞者设计了该节目的开场舞。


现在完成时:
She has choreographed numerous award-winning performances. = 她已经编排了许多获奖演出。
They have choreographed several musicals on Broadway. = 他们已经为百老汇编排了几部音乐剧。


过去完成时:
The dancers had already learned the choreography before the rehearsal. = 舞蹈演员在排练前已经学会了舞蹈编排。
He had choreographed the piece for many years before he finally put it on stage. = 他在最终将作品搬上舞台之前已经编排了多年。


将来时:
She will choreograph the next big dance production. = 她将编排下一部大型舞蹈作品。
They will be choreographing a new piece for the festival. = 他们将为节日编排一个新的作品。


虚拟语气:
If I were a choreographer, I would create innovative and groundbreaking dances. = 如果我是编舞者，我会创作创新和突破性的舞蹈。
I wish I could choreograph as well as he can. = 我希望我能像他一样擅长编舞。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
