obumbrant:/əˈbʌmbrənt/| adj.|阴暗的，遮蔽的，忧郁的 |shadowy; darkened; gloomy; suggestive of gloom or shadow. The obumbrant forest hid many secrets within its depths.  |近义词：shadowy, gloomy, dark, somber, depressing |反义词：bright, sunny, cheerful, light, luminous


The word "obumbrant" is relatively uncommon. It suggests a state of being darkened or overshadowed, either literally or figuratively.  The sense of gloom or melancholy is often present.


词源:

The word derives from the Latin "umbra," meaning "shadow."  The prefix "ob-" intensifies the meaning,  similar to how "obscure" intensifies "obscurus" (dark).


用法说明:

The word is primarily used as an adjective to describe something that is dark, gloomy, or suggestive of shadow.  It can be used to describe physical environments (a dark forest, a shadowy room) or emotional states (a gloomy mood, a depressing atmosphere).


例句:

The obumbrant sky threatened rain. =阴沉的天空预示着要下雨。

His obumbrant expression revealed his deep sorrow. =他阴郁的表情显露出了他深深的悲伤。
