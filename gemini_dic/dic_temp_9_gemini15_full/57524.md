dragooner:/ˌdræɡənˈɪər/| n. |龙骑兵；强行征募者|A dragoon was originally a soldier who served on horseback, often armed with a carbine and a sword, and known for their aggressive tactics. The term later came to refer to anyone who forcefully recruits or enlists others, often against their will. |例句：The dragoon regiment was famous for its swift cavalry charges. 龙骑兵团以其快速的骑兵冲锋而闻名。  The sergeant acted like a dragoon, forcing reluctant recruits into the army. 军士像个强行征募者一样，强迫不愿参军的士兵入伍。|近义词：horse soldier, cavalryman, conscript, press-ganger |反义词：volunteer
