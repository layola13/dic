say-so:/ˈseɪˌsoʊ/| n.|发言权；决定权；说了算|The right to decide or to have one's opinion accepted; authority.  It often implies an arbitrary or unsupported claim to authority.|例句：He had the say-so on all matters relating to the project. 他对项目的所有事务都有决定权。

例句：The boss has the final say-so on hiring decisions.  老板对招聘决定拥有最终决定权。


近义词：authority, control, power, final word

反义词：powerlessness, lack of control, influence


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has the say-so in this matter. = 他在这件事上有决定权。
She doesn't have much say-so in the company. = 她在这家公司里没有多少发言权。


一般过去时:
They had the say-so in choosing the location. = 他们有权选择地点。
He didn't have the say-so about the budget. = 他无权决定预算。


现在完成时:
They have had the say-so for many years. = 他们多年来一直拥有决定权。
She hasn't had much say-so in recent decisions. = 她最近在决策中没有多少发言权。


过去完成时:
They had had the say-so until the new manager arrived. = 直到新经理到来之前，他们一直拥有决定权。
She hadn't had the say-so before the promotion. = 升职之前，她没有发言权。


将来时:
He will have the say-so once the project starts. = 项目启动后，他将拥有决定权。
They will not have the say-so in the future. = 他们将来将没有发言权。


虚拟语气:
If he had the say-so, he would have done things differently. = 如果他有决定权，他会采取不同的做法。
If she had had the say-so, the outcome would have been better. = 如果她有发言权，结果会更好。


被动语态:
The decision was made without her say-so. = 她的意见未被采纳就做出了决定。
The project's direction is determined by his say-so. = 项目的方向由他的决定来决定。


疑问句:
Does he have any say-so in this? = 他在这件事上有发言权吗？
Who has the final say-so? = 谁有最终决定权？


否定句:
She had no say-so in the matter. = 她在这件事上没有发言权。
They didn't have the say-so over the budget. = 他们无权支配预算。


PHRASE->
have the say-so = 有决定权

give someone the say-so = 赋予某人决定权
hold the say-so = 掌握决定权
