英文解析:The plural form of profligacy, referring to instances or examples of reckless extravagance or immoral behavior.  It denotes multiple acts or displays of profligate conduct.


例句:

His profligatenesses finally caught up with him, leading to financial ruin. 他的挥霍最终导致了他经济上的破产。

The king's profligatenesses were legendary, contributing to the decline of his kingdom. 国王的放荡行为是出了名的，这导致了他的王国衰落。


词源:

From "profligate" (adjective and noun), meaning recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources; immoral or dissipated.  The suffix "-ness" denotes a state or quality.  Ultimately derived from the Latin "profligare" (to cast down, overthrow, ruin), suggesting a connection to the idea of squandering resources and ruining oneself through excess.
