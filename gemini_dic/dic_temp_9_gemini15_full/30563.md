ceramic:/səˈræmɪk/ | n. |陶瓷制品；陶瓷的 |  A ceramic is a non-metallic material made from inorganic compounds that are formed and solidified through the action of heat.  Ceramics are typically hard, brittle, and resistant to high temperatures and chemical attack. They are used in a wide variety of applications, from tableware to industrial components. | Example:  The artist crafted a beautiful ceramic vase.  这位艺术家制作了一个美丽的陶瓷花瓶。


The ancient Greeks and Romans used ceramic vessels for storage and cooking. 古希腊和古罗马人使用陶瓷器皿进行储存和烹饪。


近义词：pottery, earthenware, porcelain | 反义词：metallic, plastic

词源:

The word "ceramic" ultimately derives from the Greek word "keramikos" (κεραμικός), meaning "of pottery," which in turn comes from "keramos" (κέραμος), meaning "potter's clay" or "burnt clay."  The word entered English via the French word "céramique."
