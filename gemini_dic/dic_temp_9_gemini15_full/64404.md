/ˈɜːrɡæstər/| n. |能人(古人类)|Homo ergaster is an extinct species of hominin that lived in eastern and southern Africa during the early Pleistocene, roughly 1.9 to 1.4 million years ago.  It is considered a direct ancestor of Homo erectus and possibly modern humans.  The name "ergaster" means "worker" in Greek, reflecting the species' presumed advanced tool-making abilities.| The discovery of Homo ergaster fossils has significantly advanced our understanding of human evolution.  |近义词：Homo erectus (部分特征重叠)|反义词：无
