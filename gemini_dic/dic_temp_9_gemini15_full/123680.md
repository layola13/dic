moodle:/ˈmuːdl/| n.|心境；情绪；脾气；坏脾气|A temporary state of mind or feeling; a fit of bad temper.|例句：He was in a bad mood after the argument. 争吵后，他心情很糟糕。|例句：Her mood changed quickly.她的情绪变化很快。|近义词：temper, humor, disposition|反义词：good spirits, cheerfulness


USAGE->

一般现在时:
His mood is generally good. = 他通常心情很好。
What is your mood today? = 你今天的心情如何？


一般过去时:
Her mood was terrible yesterday. = 她昨天心情很糟糕。
What was his mood like before the meeting? = 会议前他的心情如何？


现在完成时:
My mood has improved since I had coffee. = 我喝了咖啡后心情好多了。
His mood has been low all week. = 他整个星期心情都很低落。


过去完成时:
I had been in a bad mood all day before she called. = 在她打电话之前，我整天心情都很糟糕。
He had felt angry before the apology. = 在道歉之前，他一直很生气。


将来时:
I think my mood will improve after a good night's sleep. = 我想睡个好觉后我的心情会好转。
What will your mood be like tomorrow? = 你明天的心情会怎么样？


虚拟语气:
If I were in a better mood, I would go out. = 如果我的心情更好，我会出去。
I wish I were in a better mood. = 我希望我的心情更好。


被动语态:
His mood is easily affected by the weather. = 他的情绪很容易受天气影响。(被动语态较少用于描述心情)
The mood of the meeting was largely determined by the chairman. = 会议的气氛很大程度上取决于主席。(被动语态在此处指气氛)


疑问句:
How is your mood today? = 你今天心情如何？
What's your mood? = 你心情怎么样？


否定句:
I am not in a good mood today. = 我今天心情不好。
He wasn't in the mood to talk. = 他没有心情说话。
