flashmobs:/ˈflæʃmɒbz/ | n. |快闪族；快闪活动 | A flash mob is a large group of people who suddenly appear in a public place, perform a brief, unexpected act (often a dance, song, or other performance), and then quickly disperse.  Flash mobs are often organized using social media and cell phones. | Example:  A flash mob spontaneously broke out in the town square, surprising everyone with a choreographed dance routine.  一个快闪族自发地在广场爆发，用精心编排的舞蹈表演令所有人惊讶。|近义词：surprise performance, impromptu gathering |反义词：planned event, scheduled performance
