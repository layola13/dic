habitude:/ˈhæbɪtjuːd/| n. |习惯，习性|A settled or regular tendency or practice, especially one that is hard to give up.|例句：He has a habit of biting his nails. 他有咬指甲的习惯。|近义词：habit, custom, routine|反义词：unfamiliarity, novelty


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  The usage will focus on how the noun "habitude" is used in different sentence structures.


Simple Sentence:
Her habitudes were quite different from ours. = 她的习惯和我们的很不相同。
His habitudes are very difficult to break. = 他的习惯很难改掉。


Compound Sentence:
He maintained his old habitudes, and this caused conflict. = 他保持着旧习惯，这导致了冲突。
She tried to change her habitudes, but found it challenging. = 她试图改变自己的习惯，但发现这很有挑战性。


Complex Sentence:
Because of his ingrained habitudes, he struggled to adapt. = 由于根深蒂固的习惯，他难以适应。
Although she tried, her ingrained habitudes persisted. = 虽然她尝试了，但她根深蒂固的习惯仍然存在。


Sentence with a prepositional phrase:
His habitudes in regard to punctuality were impeccable. = 他关于准时的习惯无可挑剔。
Despite her good intentions, her habitudes of procrastination remained. = 尽管她有良好的意图，但她拖延的习惯依然存在。


Passive Voice:

Her habitudes were criticized by her peers. = 她的习惯受到同龄人的批评。
