partialities:/ˌpɑːrʃiˈælətiz/ | n. |偏见；偏爱；偏袒 | noun.  A partial preference or liking for someone or something. It suggests a favorable bias, often overlooking shortcomings or flaws.  Partialities can be conscious or unconscious, and they can influence decisions and judgments. |


例句：

His partialities towards his own family were evident to everyone.  他的偏袒自己家人的行为对每个人来说都显而易见。

Her partialities for classic literature led her to a career as a librarian. 她对古典文学的偏爱让她成为了一名图书管理员。


近义词：biases, preferences, prejudices, favoritism, predilections


反义词：impartiality, objectivity, fairness, neutrality


词性: 名词


词源:  Derived from the word "partial," meaning incomplete or biased.  "Partial" itself comes from the Latin word "partialis,"  relating to a part or fraction.  The suffix "-ities" denotes a plural or a state of being.
