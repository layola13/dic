dogship: /ˈdɒɡʃɪp/ | n. |狗船；（尤指）劣质船；无能的领导 | A dogship is a ship considered to be of poor quality or in bad condition, often implying inefficiency or incompetence.  The term can also be used figuratively to refer to a poorly led organization or a leadership that is incompetent. |例句：The old dogship was barely seaworthy. 这艘旧船勉强还能航行。The company was run like a dogship, with no clear direction. 这家公司管理混乱，缺乏明确的方向。 |近义词：ramshackle vessel, poorly managed organization |反义词：flagship, well-managed organization
