pizzicatos:/ˌpɪt͡siˈkɑːtoʊz/| n. |拨弦演奏；拨弦音|The act of plucking a stringed instrument to produce a short, detached sound, or the sounds produced in this way.  It often refers to a musical notation indicating this technique. |例句：The violinist played a series of rapid pizzicatos.  小提琴家演奏了一系列快速的拨弦音。

The cellist's pizzicatos were crisp and clear.  大提琴家的拨弦音清晰而干脆。


近义词：plucked notes, snapped notes |反义词：legato (连奏)


词源：Italian *pizzicato*, past participle of *pizzicare* "to pluck", from *pizzica* "a pinch".  Ultimately from Proto-Italic *pik-*,  of uncertain origin.
