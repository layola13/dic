paras:/ˈpærəs/ | n. | 段落；节 | A paragraph is a self-contained unit of writing, typically consisting of several sentences that develop a central idea or topic.  It's a fundamental building block of longer pieces of writing. | Example: The first paragraph of the essay introduced the main argument.  这篇文章的第一段介绍了主要论点。|近义词：section, passage, stanza | 反义词： N/A


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The book has many long paragraphs. = 这本书有很多长段落。
This paragraph explains the concept clearly. = 这段话清楚地解释了这个概念。

一般过去时:
The author revised the last paragraph. = 作者修改了最后一段。
The previous paragraph discussed the history. = 前一段讨论了历史。

现在完成时:
I have read several paragraphs of this article. = 我已经读了这篇文章的几段。
She has written many interesting paragraphs in her book. = 她在这本书中写了很多有趣的段落。

过去完成时:
I had already read the first paragraph before the meeting. = 会议前我已经读了第一段。
They had finished writing the opening paragraph before the deadline. = 他们在大限前就完成了开篇段落的写作。

将来时:
I will write a new paragraph tomorrow. = 我明天将写一个新的段落。
She will add a concluding paragraph to her essay. = 她将在她的论文中添加一个结论段。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would write a longer paragraph. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会写一个更长的段落。
I wish I had written a clearer paragraph. = 我希望我写了一个更清晰的段落。

被动语态:
The paragraph was written in a concise style. = 这段话是用简洁的风格写的。
The paragraph is being edited by the professor. = 这段话正在由教授编辑。

疑问句:
How many paragraphs are there in this chapter? = 这一章有多少段？
What is the main idea of this paragraph? = 这段话的主要思想是什么？

否定句:
The paragraph does not make sense. = 这段话没有意义。
He didn't understand the paragraph. = 他不明白这段话。


PHRASE->
a paragraph of text = 一段文字
the opening paragraph = 开篇段落
the concluding paragraph = 结论段落
the next paragraph = 下一段
a long paragraph = 一段长文字
