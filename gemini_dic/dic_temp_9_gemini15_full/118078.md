amendment: /əˈmɛndmənt/ | n. | 修正案；修改；改进 | An amendment is a change or addition to a law or document.  It can also refer to the process of making such a change. |

例句：

The Senate passed an amendment to the tax bill.  参议院通过了税收法案的修正案。

They made several amendments to the contract before signing it.  他们在签署合同之前对其进行了几处修改。


近义词：alteration, modification, revision, correction

反义词：origination, inception


词源：

From Old French *amendment, from amender "to amend," from Latin emendare "to correct, improve," from ex- "out, from" + mendare "to entrust, commit" (related to manus "hand").  The sense evolution is from "to entrust for correction" to "to correct."

The government proposes amendments to the constitution regularly. 政府定期提出宪法修正案。
This bill requires several amendments before it becomes law. 这项法案在成为法律之前需要几项修改。

一般过去时:
The committee amended the report before submitting it. 委员会在提交报告之前修改了报告。
They amended the rules to include new members. 他们修改了规则以纳入新成员。

现在完成时:
The legislature has amended the law to address the issue. 立法机关已经修改了法律以解决这个问题。
Congress has amended the bill several times. 国会已经多次修改了该法案。

过去完成时:
They had amended the document before the meeting began. 他们在会议开始之前已经修改了文件。
The company had amended its policies in response to the crisis.  该公司已根据危机修改了其政策。

将来时:

疑问句:

否定句:
