certifiably:/ˈsɜːrtɪfaɪəbli/ | adj. | 可证明地；确实地；肯定地 |  The word "certifiably" means in a way that can be officially confirmed or verified. It implies a high degree of certainty and often suggests an extreme or unusual characteristic.  |  He is certifiably insane. 他肯定是疯了。 |  undeniably, definitely, incontrovertibly |  uncertain, questionable, doubtful |  形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is certifiably insane. = 他确实疯了。
This product is certifiably organic. = 这个产品确实是有机的。


一般过去时:
The doctor certifiably declared him unfit for work. = 医生确实宣布他不能工作。
The evidence certifiably proved his guilt. = 证据确实证明了他的罪行。


现在完成时:
They have certifiably improved their performance. = 他们确实提高了他们的表现。
The project has certifiably exceeded expectations. = 项目确实超出了预期。


过去完成时:
It had certifiably been the worst day of his life. = 那确实是他生命中最糟糕的一天。
The company had certifiably lost a significant amount of money. = 公司确实损失了一大笔钱。


将来时:
This technology will certifiably revolutionize the industry. =这项技术肯定能彻底改变这个行业。
The new policy will certifiably benefit the citizens. = 新政策肯定会使市民受益。


虚拟语气:
If he were certifiably insane, he wouldn't be allowed to drive. = 如果他确实疯了，就不会被允许开车。
If the evidence were certifiably false, the case would be dismissed. = 如果证据确实虚假，案件就会被驳回。


被动语态:
He was certifiably declared unfit for duty. = 他被正式宣布不适合服役。
The painting was certifiably authenticated as a genuine masterpiece. = 这幅画被确认为真迹杰作。


疑问句:
Is he certifiably insane? = 他确实疯了吗？
Is this product certifiably safe? = 这个产品确实安全吗？


否定句:
He is not certifiably insane. = 他并非真的疯了。
The claim is not certifiably true. = 这一说法并非确实属实。
