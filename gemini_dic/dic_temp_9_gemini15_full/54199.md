disfame:/dɪsˈfeɪm/| vt.|诽谤；败坏名声 |to damage the reputation of someone or something.  |例句：The newspaper article disfamed the politician, causing irreparable damage to his career.  这篇报纸文章诽谤了这位政治家，给他的事业造成了无法弥补的损害。|近义词：defame, slander, vilify |反义词：praise, glorify |动词

一般现在时:
He disfames his rivals constantly. = 他不断诽谤他的竞争对手。
The company disfames its competitors through false advertising. = 该公司通过虚假广告诽谤其竞争对手。


一般过去时:
They disfamed the professor with false accusations. = 他们用虚假的指控诽谤了这位教授。
The tabloid disfamed the actress with a scandalous story. = 这家小报用一个丑闻故事诽谤了这位女演员。


现在完成时:
He has disfamed many people throughout his career. = 他在他的职业生涯中诽谤过许多人。
The media has disfamed the company with biased reporting. = 媒体以有偏见的报道诽谤了这家公司。


过去完成时:
They had disfamed her before she even knew about the accusations. = 在她甚至知道这些指控之前，他们就已经诽谤她了。
The website had disfamed the artist before the apology was issued. = 在道歉发布之前，该网站已经诽谤了这位艺术家。


将来时:
They will disfame him if he continues his illegal activities. = 如果他继续从事非法活动，他们将会诽谤他。
The press will disfame the organization if the evidence is revealed. = 如果证据被揭露，媒体将会诽谤这个组织。


虚拟语气:
If they disfamed him, he would sue them. = 如果他们诽谤他，他会起诉他们。
I wish they hadn't disfamed her reputation. = 我希望他们没有败坏她的名声。


被动语态:
He was disfamed by a rival company. = 他被一家竞争对手公司诽谤了。
The politician was disfamed in the media. = 这位政治家在媒体上受到了诽谤。


疑问句:
Were they disfamed by the article? = 他们被这篇文章诽谤了吗？
Has he ever been disfamed before? = 他以前曾被诽谤过吗？


否定句:
They did not disfame him, despite the rumors. = 尽管有谣言，他们并没有诽谤他。
She would never disfame her friends. = 她永远不会诽谤她的朋友。
