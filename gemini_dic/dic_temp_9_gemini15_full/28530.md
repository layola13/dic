cardinal:/ˈkɑːrdɪnəl/|adj., n.|主要的；枢机主教；深红色的|

英文解析:As an adjective, "cardinal" means of primary importance; fundamental.  As a noun, it refers to a high-ranking clergyman in the Roman Catholic Church or a bright red color.

例句:
The cardinal rule is to always be honest.  = 最重要的规则是永远诚实。

His cardinal sin was betraying his friends. = 他的大罪是背叛了他的朋友。


The cardinal flew into the garden. = 枢机主教飞进了花园。(This uses "cardinal" in a less common, figurative sense referring to a bird.)

She wore a cardinal red dress. = 她穿着一件深红色的连衣裙。


近义词:
(adj.) chief, principal, main, essential, primary, fundamental
(n.) prelate, dignitary


反义词:
(adj.) minor, secondary, insignificant, unimportant
(n.) layman


词源:
From Old French *cardinal, from Latin cardinalis "principal, chief," from cardo "hinge."  The connection is that something cardinal is like a hinge, on which something else pivots.  The use for the bird and the church official are later derivations.
