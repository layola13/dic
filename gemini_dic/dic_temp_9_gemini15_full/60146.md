effectuated:/ɪˈfɛktʃueɪtɪd/ | v. | 使生效；使实现；完成 | To put into effect; to accomplish; to bring about.  | The plan was finally effectuated after years of planning and discussion.  这项计划经过多年的计划和讨论后终于得以实施。 | implemented, accomplished, realized | frustrated, thwarted, failed |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They effectuate changes regularly. = 他们定期实施变革。
The company effectuates its strategies effectively. = 公司有效地执行其战略。

一般过去时:
He effectuated the sale of the property. = 他完成了房产的出售。
The government effectuated new policies to improve the economy. = 政府实施了新的政策以改善经济。

现在完成时:
They have effectuated significant improvements in the process. = 他们已经在这个过程中实现了重大改进。
The team has effectuated a successful product launch. = 团队已经成功地推出了产品。

过去完成时:

The committee had effectuated the changes prior to the meeting. = 委员会在会议之前已经完成了更改。

将来时:
They will effectuate the plan next month. = 他们下个月将执行该计划。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
The changes were effectuated quickly and efficiently. = 这些变化迅速而有效地得到了实施。

疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->
effectuate a change = 实施改变

词源:
Effectuate comes from the Latin word "effectuare," meaning "to bring about," "to accomplish," or "to make effective."  The prefix "ef-" is a variant of "ex-," meaning "out" or "from," while "fectuare" is related to "facere," meaning "to do" or "to make."
