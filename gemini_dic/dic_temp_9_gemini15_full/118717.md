metafictionist:/ˌmɛtəfɪkʃənɪst/ | n. | 元小说家 | A writer of metafiction.  Metafictionists are authors who self-consciously write about the process of writing or the nature of fiction itself, often blurring the lines between fiction and reality within their works. They may incorporate elements like authorial intrusion, unreliable narrators, and direct address to the reader. | Example:  Many consider Italo Calvino to be a prominent metafictionist.  许多人认为伊塔洛·卡尔维诺是一位杰出的元小说家。 |近义词: experimental novelist, postmodern writer | 反义词: realist writer
