foxhole:/ˈfɑːkshoʊl/ /ˈfɒkshoʊl/| n. |掩体；狐穴| A shallow pit or trench dug in the ground as a shelter, especially by soldiers in combat.  It is typically narrow and only deep enough to allow a person to sit or crouch in for protection. The term is derived from the resemblance of the pit to a fox's den.|例句：The soldiers dug foxholes to protect themselves from enemy fire. 士兵们挖了掩体以保护自己免受敌人的炮火。

The exhausted soldiers found refuge in their foxholes during the bombardment.  筋疲力尽的士兵在轰炸期间在掩体中找到了避难所。|近义词：trench, shelter, dugout, hideout |反义词：exposure, open space
