debar:/dɪˈbɑːr/ | vt. |禁止；阻止；排斥 | To exclude or prevent from; to shut out.  |例句：He was debarred from holding public office. 他被禁止担任公职。

例句：The judge debarred the lawyer from practicing law.  法官禁止该律师执业。


近义词：exclude, prohibit, prevent, bar, forbid | 反义词：allow, permit, admit


词源:  From Old French *desbarrer, from des- (from Latin dis-) + barrer "to bar".  The sense evolution is from "to bar" to "to exclude by barring".


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The club debarrs members who violate the rules. =  俱乐部禁止违反规定的会员。
They debar anyone with a criminal record. = 他们禁止任何有犯罪记录的人。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:
They had been debarred from voting before the reform. = 改革之前，他们已被禁止投票。
The organization had debarred him long before the scandal broke. = 早在丑闻爆发之前，该组织就已经禁止他了。

虚拟语气:
