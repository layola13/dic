impugnment:/ɪmˈpjuːn.mən t/| n. | 质疑；攻击；责难 | The act of calling something into question; an attack on the truth, validity, or integrity of something.  |例句：His testimony was met with strong impugnment from the defense.  他的证词遭到了被告方的强烈质疑。

例句：The impugnment of the witness's credibility damaged the prosecution's case.  对证人可信度的质疑损害了检方的案情。


近义词：challenge, attack, criticism, questioning, denial, refutation |反义词：confirmation, verification, validation, endorsement


词源：From Old French *empugnement, from empugner "to fight, attack," from Vulgar Latin *im-pugnare, from Latin pugnare "to fight."  The "im-" prefix indicates intensification or opposition.

一般现在时:
The lawyer impugns the witness's testimony. = 律师质疑证人的证词。
They impugn the validity of the contract. = 他们质疑合同的有效性。


一般过去时:
The judge impugned the defendant's character. = 法官质疑了被告的品格。
He impugned the accuracy of the report. = 他质疑了报告的准确性。


现在完成时:
The media has impugned the politician's integrity. = 媒体已经质疑了这位政治家的正直。
They have impugned the findings of the study. = 他们已经质疑了这项研究的结果。


过去完成时:
The defense had impugned the evidence before the trial. = 在审判之前，辩方已经质疑了证据。
She had impugned his motives for years. = 她多年来一直质疑他的动机。


将来时:
The opposition will impugn the government's policies. = 反对派将质疑政府的政策。
He will impugn the accuracy of the data. = 他将质疑数据的准确性。


虚拟语气:
If they impugned the evidence, the case would collapse. = 如果他们质疑证据，案件就会崩溃。
I wish they hadn't impugned my honesty. = 我真希望他们没有质疑我的诚实。


被动语态:
His claims were impugned by several experts. = 他的说法被几位专家质疑。
The report's findings were impugned by the opposition party. = 报告的调查结果被反对党质疑。


疑问句:
Was his character impugned during the trial? = 审判期间他的品格是否受到了质疑？
Will his reputation be impugned by this scandal? = 他的名誉会因为这个丑闻而受损吗？


否定句:
The court did not impugn the witness's testimony. = 法院没有质疑证人的证词。
The study did not impugn the effectiveness of the treatment. = 这项研究并没有质疑治疗的有效性。
