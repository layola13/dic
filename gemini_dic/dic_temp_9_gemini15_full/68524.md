fancies:/ˈfænsiz/| n. v.|幻想；爱好；设想；认为|

英文解析:
As a noun, "fancies" refers to a pleasant or whimsical idea or notion; an inclination or liking; a capricious notion or desire.  As a verb, it means to imagine or believe something that is not true; to have a liking or fondness for something.


例句：
(Noun)
He had fancies of becoming a famous musician. 他梦想成为一名著名的音乐家。

She has a fancy for antique furniture. 她喜欢古董家具。


(Verb)
I fancy I heard a noise. 我觉得我听到一个声音。

Do you fancy going to the cinema tonight? 你今晚想去看电影吗？


近义词:
(Noun)  whims, notions, ideas, imaginations, fancies, caprices, desires
(Verb)  imagine, suppose, believe, think, assume, like, enjoy


反义词:
(Noun) realities, facts, truths
(Verb)  disbelieve, doubt, dislike, abhor


词性:
Noun, Verb

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fancies himself a great artist. 他自认为是一位伟大的艺术家。
She fancies chocolate more than vanilla. 她更喜欢巧克力而不是香草。

一般过去时:
I fancied I saw a ghost last night. 我昨晚好像看到鬼了。
They fancied a walk in the park. 他们想在公园里散步。

现在完成时:
He has fancied himself a writer for years. 多年来他一直自认为是个作家。
She has fancied that dress since she first saw it. 自从第一次看到那件衣服以来她就一直很喜欢它。

过去完成时:
I had fancied that we would be friends forever. 我曾以为我们会永远是朋友。
She had fancied him for a long time before he finally noticed her. 在他最终注意到她之前，她已经暗恋他很久了。

将来时:
I fancy I will enjoy the concert. 我想我会喜欢这场音乐会的。
They fancy they will win the game. 他们认为他们会赢得比赛。

虚拟语气:
If I fancied myself a painter, I would start painting. 如果我认为自己是个画家，我就会开始作画。
I wish I fancied traveling more often. 我希望我能更频繁地旅行。

被动语态:
(Not commonly used in passive voice for the verb "fancy")
His work is often fancied to be groundbreaking. 他的作品经常被认为是开创性的。(使用被动语态但对象是work)

疑问句:
Do you fancy a cup of tea? 你想喝杯茶吗？
What do you fancy doing this weekend? 你这个周末想做什么？

否定句:
I don't fancy going out tonight. 我今晚不想出去。
She didn't fancy the idea at all. 她一点也不喜欢这个主意。


PHRASE->
take a fancy to = 喜欢上
have a fancy for = 喜欢
catch one's fancy = 吸引某人的注意
a flight of fancy = 一时的幻想
more than fancy = 不只是喜欢
