competing:/kəmˈpiːtɪŋ/| adj. |竞争的，对抗的 |Engaging in rivalry or competition.  Describes something that is involved in a contest or struggle against others.|The competing teams showed great sportsmanship. |近义词：rivaling, contending, vying |反义词：cooperating, collaborating


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The two companies are competing for market share. = 两家公司正在争夺市场份额。
These athletes are competing in the Olympics. = 这些运动员正在参加奥运会。

一般过去时:
They competed fiercely for the prize. = 他们为了奖品而激烈竞争。
We competed in a singing contest last night. = 我们昨晚参加了一场歌唱比赛。

现在完成时:
She has competed in many marathons. = 她参加过许多马拉松比赛。
The company has been competing in the global market for years. = 这家公司多年来一直在全球市场竞争。

过去完成时:
They had competed several times before the final match. = 在决赛之前他们已经比赛过几次了。
The team had already competed in several qualifying rounds. = 该队已经参加了几轮资格赛。

将来时:
They will be competing in the next tournament. = 他们将在下一届锦标赛中参赛。
We will compete against the best teams in the world. = 我们将与世界上最好的球队竞争。

虚拟语气:
If I were competing, I would train harder. = 如果我参赛，我会更加努力训练。
I wish I were competing in the national championship. = 我希望我能参加全国锦标赛。

被动语态:
The election was dominated by competing interests. = 选举被相互竞争的利益集团所主导。
The market is being flooded by competing products. = 市场被竞争性产品充斥着。

疑问句:
Are these two products competing against each other? = 这两种产品是否相互竞争？
Who are you competing with in this project? = 在这个项目中你与谁竞争？

否定句:
The two companies are not competing directly. = 这两家公司并没有直接竞争。
He wasn't competing for the top spot. = 他并没有争夺第一名。


PHRASE->
competing interests = 竞争利益

competing priorities = 优先顺序冲突
