decomposed:/ˌdiːkəmˈpoʊzd/ | adj. v. | 分解的；腐烂的 |  To separate or be separated into simpler substances, typically as a result of decay or chemical action.  | 例句：The decomposed body was found in the woods.  =  尸体在树林里被发现，已经腐烂了。


The leaves decomposed into rich soil. = 叶子分解成肥沃的土壤。


近义词：decayed, rotted, disintegrated


反义词：intact, whole, preserved


词性: Verb (transitive and intransitive), Adjective


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bacteria decompose organic matter. = 细菌分解有机物。
The compost pile is decomposing slowly. = 堆肥正在缓慢分解。


一般过去时:
The scientist decomposed the sample in the lab. = 科学家在实验室里分解了样品。
The fruit decomposed quickly in the heat. = 水果在高温下迅速腐烂。


现在完成时:
The body has decomposed significantly. = 尸体已经严重腐烂了。
The leaves have decomposed into humus. = 叶子已经分解成腐殖质。


过去完成时:
The food had decomposed before we could eat it. = 我们还没来得及吃，食物就已经腐烂了。
The evidence had decomposed beyond recognition. = 证据已经腐烂得无法辨认了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->

词源:
From de- + compose (to put together), ultimately from Latin *componere (com- + ponere, to place).  The prefix "de-" indicates reversal or removal. Thus, "decompose" means to "un-compose" or break down.
