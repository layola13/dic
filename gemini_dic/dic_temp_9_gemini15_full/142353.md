orthology:/ɔːrˈθɒlədʒi/ | n. |直系同源性，直系同源 |The study of orthologous genes.  It refers to the relationship between genes in different species that arose from a common ancestor through speciation. Orthologs typically retain the same function in the descendant species. |Example:  The study of orthology helps us understand evolutionary relationships and functional conservation across species.  直系同源性研究帮助我们理解物种间的进化关系和功能保守性。 |近义词：homology (更广义的同源性) |反义词：paralogy (旁系同源性) |名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily used in the context of biology and evolutionary genetics.  Therefore, typical grammatical tenses and structures associated with everyday language are not directly applicable in the same manner. The following examples illustrate its use within scientific writing and discussions:

Present Simple: This research focuses on the orthology of genes involved in metabolic processes. 这项研究关注参与代谢过程的基因的直系同源性。

Present Perfect: Researchers have identified several orthologous genes between humans and chimpanzees. 研究人员已经鉴定出人类和黑猩猩之间几个直系同源基因。


Past Simple:  The previous study demonstrated a high degree of orthology between the two species. 先前的研究证明了这两个物种之间高度的直系同源性。

Past Perfect: Scientists had already established the orthology of these genes before undertaking this comparative analysis. 科学家们在进行这项比较分析之前，已经确定了这些基因的直系同源性。


Future Simple: Future studies will likely explore the orthology of genes related to disease resistance. 未来研究可能会探索与抗病性相关的基因的直系同源性。

Conditional: If the genes are orthologous, we can expect similar functional roles. 如果这些基因是直系同源的，我们可以预期它们具有相似的功能作用。


Passive Voice: The orthology of the genes was confirmed by phylogenetic analysis.  基因的直系同源性通过系统发育分析得到证实。
