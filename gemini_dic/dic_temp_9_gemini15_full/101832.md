irrigate:/ˈɪrɪɡeɪt/| vt.|灌溉；冲洗；冲净|To supply (land) with water artificially.  This usually involves a system of canals, pipes, or ditches to distribute water to crops.  It can also refer to flushing or washing out a bodily cavity or wound.|例句：Farmers irrigate their fields to ensure a good harvest. 农民灌溉他们的田地以确保获得好收成。

例句：The doctor irrigated the wound to remove debris. 医生冲洗伤口以清除碎屑。|近义词：water, flush, rinse|反义词：dehydrate, dry out|动词

词源:
From Latin *irrigāre "to water," from *ir- "in" + *rigāre "to water," from *rigus "stiff, erect," from PIE *h₂reǵ- "to stretch, straighten, make stiff."  The connection lies in the idea of making the land "stiff" with water.
