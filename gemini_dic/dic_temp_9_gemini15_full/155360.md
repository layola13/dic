pharmacopoeias:/ˌfɑːrməkəˈpiːəz/| n. |药典（复数）|A pharmacopoeia is an official book containing a list of medicinal drugs with their effects and directions for their use.  It is a comprehensive collection of standards for the identity, purity, strength, and quality of medications.  Pharmacopoeias are typically published by national or international organizations and serve as authoritative references for healthcare professionals and regulatory agencies.|The United States Pharmacopeia (USP) sets standards for drug quality. = 美国药典 (USP) 制定药物质量标准。

The European Pharmacopoeia is widely used across Europe. = 欧洲药典在欧洲广泛使用。|近义词： formulary, compendium |反义词：无
