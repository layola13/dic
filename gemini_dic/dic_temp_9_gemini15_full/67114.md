explosives:/ɪkˈsploʊsɪvz/| n.|炸药，爆炸物|Substances that explode violently when ignited or detonated.  They are used in mining, demolition, and unfortunately, warfare.  The power of explosives comes from their rapid expansion upon detonation, releasing tremendous energy.|例句：The bomb squad carefully neutralized the explosives found near the school.  = 爆炸物处理小组小心地中和了在学校附近发现的炸药。

例句：Improvised explosive devices (IEDs) pose a significant threat in conflict zones. = 简易爆炸装置（IED）在冲突地区构成重大威胁。


近义词：high explosives, detonators, blasts

反义词：None directly opposite, but one could consider "dampeners" or "stabilizers" as contrasting concepts.
