hegelianism:/ˌhɛɡəˈliənɪzəm/| n. |黑格尔主义|The philosophical system of Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel, emphasizing the dialectical process of thesis, antithesis, and synthesis in the development of ideas and history.  Hegelianism sees history as a progressive unfolding of Geist (spirit or mind), culminating in absolute knowledge.  It's often characterized by its focus on the interconnectedness of things and the idea that contradictions drive progress.|The professor's lecture focused on the complexities of Hegelianism and its influence on 19th-century thought.|Idealism, dialectical materialism|Pragmatism, positivism|名词
