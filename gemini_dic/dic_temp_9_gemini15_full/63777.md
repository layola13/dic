epicurean:/ˌɛpɪˈkjuəriən/| adj. n.|享乐主义的；美食家的；| relating to or characteristic of Epicurus or Epicureanism; devoted to pleasure;  fond of good food and drink.  |例句：He led an epicurean lifestyle, indulging in fine wines and gourmet meals. 他过着享乐主义的生活，沉迷于美酒佳肴。

He is known for his epicurean tastes and his refined palate. 他以其美食家的品味和精致的味觉而闻名。


近义词：hedonistic, pleasure-seeking, luxurious, gourmet |反义词：ascetic, austere, abstemious


词源:From Epicurus, a Greek philosopher who advocated for pleasure as the highest good.  The word derives from the name of the ancient Greek philosopher Epicurus (341-270 BC), who believed that pleasure, understood as the absence of pain and mental disturbance, was the highest good.
