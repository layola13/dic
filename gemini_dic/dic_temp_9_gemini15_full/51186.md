destabilize:/ˌdiːˈsteɪbəlaɪz/| vt.|使不稳定；动摇；扰乱|To make something unstable or insecure.  To disturb the stability of a system, situation, or organization.|例句：The economic crisis destabilized the government. 经济危机使政府动荡不安。|例句：The sudden influx of refugees destabilized the region. 难民的突然涌入使该地区陷入不稳定状态。|近义词：unsettle, disrupt, undermine, shake|反义词：stabilize, strengthen, secure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant changes destabilize the workforce. =  不断的变化使员工队伍不稳定。
The rumors destabilize the market. = 谣言扰乱了市场。

一般过去时:
The earthquake destabilized the building's foundation. = 地震使建筑物的基础不稳。
His actions destabilized the peace negotiations. = 他的行为扰乱了和平谈判。

现在完成时:
The political unrest has destabilized the entire country. = 政治动荡已经使整个国家陷入不稳定。
Years of conflict have destabilized the region. = 多年的冲突使该地区陷入不稳定。

过去完成时:
The company had destabilized the market before the merger. = 在合并之前，该公司已经扰乱了市场。
The drought had destabilized the agricultural sector. = 干旱已经使农业部门陷入不稳定。

将来时:
The new policy will destabilize the economy. = 新政策将扰乱经济。
Further military action will destabilize the region. = 进一步的军事行动将使该地区陷入不稳定。

虚拟语气:
If they continued this behavior, it would destabilize the team. = 如果他们继续这种行为，就会使团队不稳定。

疑问句:

否定句:
