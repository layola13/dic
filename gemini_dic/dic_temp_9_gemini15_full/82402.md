grudged:/ɡrʌdʒd/| v.|怨恨，嫉妒，勉强给予|To feel or show resentment; to be unwilling to give or admit.  |例句：He grudged paying so much for the tickets. 他很不情愿花那么多钱买票。|例句：She grudged him his success. 她嫉妒他的成功。|近义词：resent, begrudge, envy|反义词：accept, welcome, appreciate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grudges me my happiness. = 他嫉妒我的幸福。
They grudge paying extra taxes. = 他们不愿多交税。


一般过去时:
She grudged him the extra time off. = 她不情愿给他额外的假期。
I grudged admitting I was wrong. = 我很不情愿承认我错了。


现在完成时:
I have grudged him this promotion for years. = 多年来我一直嫉妒他升职。
They have grudged paying their fair share. = 他们一直不愿意支付他们应付的份额。


过去完成时:
She had grudged her sister's beauty since childhood. = 从小她就嫉妒她姐姐的美貌。
He had grudged giving up his free time. = 他一直不情愿放弃他的空闲时间。


将来时:
I will grudge paying such a high price. = 我将不情愿支付这么高的价格。
They will grudge losing their position. = 他们将不情愿失去他们的职位。


虚拟语气:
I would grudge giving up my weekend. = 我不愿意放弃我的周末。
If I had known, I would have grudged lending him the money. = 如果我知道的话，我就不愿意借给他钱了。


被动语态:
The extra work was grudged by many employees. = 许多员工都不愿意做额外的工作。
His success was grudged by his rivals. = 他的成功遭到了对手的嫉妒。


疑问句:
Do you grudge spending money on yourself? = 你吝啬给自己花钱吗？
Does he grudge giving his time to charity? = 他不愿意把时间捐给慈善机构吗？


否定句:
I don't grudge helping those in need. = 我不介意帮助需要帮助的人。
He didn't grudge sharing his knowledge. = 他不吝啬分享他的知识。


PHRASE->
grudge a favor = 勉强帮忙
grudge someone something = 嫉妒某人拥有某物
bear a grudge = 心怀怨恨
hold a grudge = 怀恨在心
grudge against = 对…怀恨
