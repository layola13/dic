iconoclast:/ˈaɪkənəˌklæst/ | n. |偶像破坏者；反传统者 |A person who attacks cherished beliefs or institutions.  Someone who challenges or attacks traditional beliefs, ideas, or institutions. | 例句：He was an iconoclast who challenged the authority of the church. 他是一位挑战教会权威的偶像破坏者。

例句：The artist's work was considered iconoclastic for its bold rejection of traditional techniques.  这位艺术家的作品因大胆摒弃传统技法而被认为是反传统的。


近义词：heretic, rebel, dissenter, revolutionary, nonconformist

反义词：conformist, traditionalist, conservative


词源：From Late Latin *iconoclastes, from Greek eikonoklastēs, from eikon "image" + klastēs "breaker".  The term originated in the Byzantine Empire during the Iconoclastic Controversy (8th-9th centuries), when the destruction of religious images was a major issue.
