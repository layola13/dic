fugitiveness:/ˈfjuːdʒɪtɪvnəs/ | n. | 逃亡；易逝；短暂 | The fugitiveness of youth is a common theme in literature. 青年时代的短暂是文学中常见的主题。|近义词：transitoriness, ephemerality, fleetingness | 反义词：permanence, durability


词源:

源自拉丁语 *fugitivus，意思是“逃跑的”，由 *fugere（“逃跑”）派生而来。  "-ness" 是一个英语后缀，表示性质或状态。


英文解析:

Fugitiveness refers to the quality or state of being fleeting, transient, or elusive;  the tendency to escape or avoid capture. It can describe something that is quickly disappearing, lasting only a short time, or something that is difficult to grasp or pin down.


例句:

The fugitiveness of beauty is a source of both sorrow and inspiration.  美丽的易逝是悲伤和灵感的源泉。


The police were frustrated by the suspect's fugitiveness. 警方对嫌疑人的逃亡感到沮丧。


近义词:

transitoriness, ephemerality, fleetingness, elusiveness, evanescence


反义词:

permanence, durability, stability, steadfastness

名词
