condescends:/ˌkɒn.dɪˈsɛndz/ /ˌkɑːn.dəˈsɛndz/ | v. | 屈尊；俯就；故意显示优越 | To behave in a way that shows that you believe you are superior to someone else.  | He condescended to answer my questions. 他屈尊回答了我的问题。|近义词：deign, patronize, talk down to |反义词：respect, treat equally


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He condescends to his employees. = 他对他的员工摆出一副高高在上的样子。
She condescends to those less fortunate than herself. = 她对那些不如她幸运的人总是摆出一副优越感。

一般过去时:
He condescended to help me with my project. = 他屈尊帮我完成了我的项目。
She condescended to listen to my complaints. = 她屈尊听取了我的抱怨。

现在完成时:
He has condescended to speak to me only once. = 他只屈尊跟我说过一次话。
She has condescended to accept our invitation. = 她屈尊接受了我们的邀请。

过去完成时:
He had condescended to give me some advice before leaving. = 他在离开前屈尊给了我一些建议。
She had condescended to attend the meeting, though reluctantly. = 尽管不情愿，但她还是屈尊参加了会议。

将来时:
He will condescend to explain the matter again. = 他将会屈尊再次解释这件事。
She will condescend to visit us next week. = 她下周将会屈尊来拜访我们。

虚拟语气:
If he condescended to help, we could finish the task earlier. = 如果他肯帮忙，我们就能早点完成任务。
I wish she would condescend to give me a second chance. = 我希望她能屈尊再给我一次机会。

被动语态:
His advice was condescended to by his colleagues. = 他的建议受到了同事们的轻视（被动语态更强调被轻视）。
Her opinion was condescended to, making her feel unheard. = 她的意见被轻视了，让她感到不被重视。（被动语态更强调被轻视）。

疑问句:
Does he often condescend to others? = 他经常对别人摆出一副高高在上的样子吗？
Will she condescend to attend the ceremony? = 她会屈尊参加典礼吗？

否定句:
He did not condescend to engage in trivial conversation. = 他不屑于进行琐碎的谈话。
She never condescends to those less fortunate than her.  = 她从不对那些不如她幸运的人摆出一副优越感。


PHRASE->
condescend to help = 屈尊帮忙

condescend to listen = 屈尊倾听
condescend to accept = 屈尊接受
condescending tone = 屈尊俯就的语气


词源:
Comes from the Latin "condescendĕre," meaning "to come down."  The prefix "con-" means "together" or "with," and "scandere" means "to climb."  Therefore, the original meaning implied a descent from a higher position.
