indispose:/ˌɪndɪsˈpoʊz/| vt. adj.|使不适；使倾向于；使(某人)不愿做某事；使…不适宜；使…无资格|to make someone feel slightly ill; to make someone unwilling to do something; to make something unsuitable. |例句：The bad weather indisposed me to go out.  恶劣的天气使我不想外出。  The doctor indisposed him for several weeks. 医生使他卧床数周。|近义词：ail, upset, predispose|反义词：dispose, cure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bad weather indisposes many people to exercise. = 恶劣的天气使许多人不愿锻炼。
This medicine indisposes some patients to nausea. = 这种药使一些病人恶心。


一般过去时:
The news indisposed him to work that day. = 这个消息使他那天不想工作。
The loud noise indisposed the baby to sleep. = 巨大的噪音使婴儿无法入睡。


现在完成时:
The long journey has indisposed me to further travel. = 漫长的旅程使我不愿再旅行。
This illness has indisposed her for weeks. = 这场病使她卧床数周。


过去完成时:
The argument had indisposed them to cooperate further. = 那场争论使他们不愿进一步合作。
His previous experience had indisposed him to trust strangers. = 他之前的经历使他不信任陌生人。


将来时:
The heat will indispose many people to attend the outdoor concert. = 炎热的天气将使许多人不想参加户外音乐会。
I think this news will indispose him to accept the job offer. = 我认为这个消息会使他不愿接受这份工作。


虚拟语气:
If the weather were better, it would not indispose me to go for a walk. = 如果天气好一点，我就不至于不想去散步了。
If I hadn't been indisposed, I would have attended the party. = 如果我没有不舒服，我会参加聚会的。


被动语态:
He was indisposed by the strong medicine. = 他被烈性药弄得不舒服。
She was indisposed to the idea from the beginning. = 她从一开始就对这个想法不感兴趣。


疑问句:
Is he indisposed to answer my questions? = 他不愿意回答我的问题吗？
Was she indisposed by the heat? = 她中暑了吗？


否定句:
The doctor said that the patient was not indisposed. = 医生说病人没有不舒服。
I am not indisposed to helping you. = 我不反对帮助你。



PHRASE->
indisposed to: 不愿
indisposed for: 不适合于
slightly indisposed: 有点不舒服
feel indisposed: 感到不适
indisposed towards: 对...不感兴趣
