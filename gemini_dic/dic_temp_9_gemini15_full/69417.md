feeler-gauges: /ˈfiːlər ɡeɪdʒɪz/ | n. | 塞尺 |  Feeler gauges are a set of thin, graduated blades used to measure small gaps or clearances, typically in mechanical engineering and automotive repair.  They are used to check the gap between parts such as spark plugs, piston rings, and valve clearances. | Example: The mechanic used feeler gauges to check the valve clearance in the engine.  The technician carefully inserted the feeler gauge into the gap to measure the clearance. | 近义词: thickness gauge, gap gauge | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, the opposite would be a measurement device for large distances)
