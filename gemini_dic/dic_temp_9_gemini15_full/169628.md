propitiative: /prəˈpɪʃieɪtɪv/ | adj. | 讨好的；抚慰的；劝解的 | Appeasing; intended to soothe or conciliate.  It suggests an attempt to win favor or prevent anger or displeasure. |

例句：

He offered a propitiative smile to the angry customer. = 他对愤怒的顾客露出了一个讨好的笑容。

Her propitiative tone helped to calm the situation. = 她讨好的语气帮助平息了局势。


近义词：conciliatory, appeasing, placating, mollifying, pacific


反义词：antagonistic, provocative, offensive, insulting, infuriating


词源:

From Middle English *propiciatiif, from Old French *propiciatif, from Latin propitiat- (stem of propitiatus), past participle of propitiare "to appease, conciliate," from propitius "favorable, gracious."
