redounding:/rɪˈdaʊndɪŋ/| v.|回馈；导致；产生积极影响 | to have a significant effect, usually a positive one, as a consequence of something |His efforts redounded to the benefit of the entire community. 他的努力使整个社区受益。|近义词：resulting, contributing, leading to |反义词：detracting from, harming


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The success of the project redounds to the team's credit. = 项目的成功归功于团队。
Her kindness redounds to her own happiness. = 她的善良让她自己感到快乐。


一般过去时:
The decision redounded to the company's advantage. = 这个决定对公司有利。
His hard work redounded to his eventual promotion. = 他的努力最终促使他升职。


现在完成时:
Their efforts have redounded to the improvement of the environment. = 他们的努力已经改善了环境。
The charity's work has redounded to the benefit of many families. = 慈善机构的工作使许多家庭受益。


过去完成时:
The positive reviews had redounded to the author's reputation. = 积极的评论提高了作者的名声。
The success of the previous project had redounded to the team's confidence. = 上一个项目的成功增强了团队的信心。


将来时:
This new policy will redound to the benefit of small businesses. = 这项新政策将使小型企业受益。
Increased investment will redound to the growth of the economy. = 增加投资将促进经济增长。


虚拟语气:
If we worked harder, our efforts would redound to our success. = 如果我们更努力工作，我们的努力就会导致成功。
I wish my actions would redound to the greater good. = 我希望我的行为能造福大众。


被动语态:
The benefits of the program are redounded to the participants. = 该项目的益处惠及参与者。
The credit for the success is redounded to the entire team. = 成功应归功于整个团队。


疑问句:
Will this decision redound to our advantage? = 这个决定对我们有利吗？
Does his behavior redound to his credit? = 他的行为值得称赞吗？


否定句:
The poor management did not redound to the company's success. = 糟糕的管理并没有使公司成功。
His actions did not redound to his reputation. = 他的行为没有给他带来好名声。



PHRASE->
redound to someone's credit/benefit/advantage = 对某人有利/有益/有好处
redound to someone's honor/glory = 为某人增光
redound to the benefit of = 使…受益
redound to the glory of = 使…光荣
redound upon someone = 反映在某人身上


词源:
The word "redounding" comes from the Old French word "redonder," meaning "to overflow, abound, or surplus."  This, in turn, derives from the Latin "redundare," which has a similar meaning.  The sense of "resulting in" or "having a consequence" developed over time.
