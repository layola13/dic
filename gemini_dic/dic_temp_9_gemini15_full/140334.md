oh-so:/ˈoʊˌsoʊ/| adj.|多么，非常，极其|Emphasizes the degree or extent of something.  It's often used informally and adds a slightly ironic or sarcastic tone.|例句：That was an oh-so-charming comment. = 那真是个多么迷人的评论。(讽刺意味)|近义词：very, extremely, incredibly, terribly|反义词：slightly, barely, hardly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dress is oh-so beautiful. = 这件裙子真是太漂亮了。
This cake is oh-so delicious. = 这个蛋糕真是太好吃了。


一般过去时:
The party was oh-so fun. = 派对真是太有趣了。
The movie was oh-so boring. = 这部电影真是太无聊了。


现在完成时:
She has been oh-so patient. = 她一直非常有耐心。
He has become oh-so successful. = 他已经变得非常成功了。


过去完成时:
I had been oh-so tired. = 我那时非常疲惫。
They had felt oh-so relieved. = 他们当时感到非常轻松。


将来时:
It will be oh-so exciting. = 这将非常令人兴奋。
The trip will be oh-so relaxing. = 这趟旅行将会非常轻松。


虚拟语气:
If I had an oh-so-clever plan, I would tell you. = 如果我有一个非常巧妙的计划，我会告诉你的。
I wish I had an oh-so-easy solution. = 我真希望我有一个非常简单的解决方案。


被动语态:
The problem was made oh-so clear. = 问题被解释得非常清楚。
The situation is being made oh-so complicated. = 局势正变得非常复杂。


疑问句:
Is the weather oh-so beautiful today? = 今天天气是不是非常漂亮？
Isn't the view oh-so stunning? = 景色是不是非常令人惊叹？


否定句:
It wasn't oh-so bad. = 它并没有那么糟糕。
The situation isn't oh-so simple. = 情况并非那么简单。
