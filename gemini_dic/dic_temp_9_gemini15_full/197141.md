shanghaiing:/ʃæŋˈhaɪɪŋ/| v. | 诱拐；强迫上船；（尤指）强迫某人当水手 | The act of kidnapping someone, especially to force them into working on a ship.  This often involved drugging or otherwise incapacitating the victim. | 例句：He was shanghaiied into working on a whaling ship. 他被诱拐去捕鲸船上工作了。

例句：The sailors were known to shanghai unsuspecting men from the docks. 水手们以从码头上诱拐毫无戒心的男人而闻名。


近义词：kidnapping, pressganging, impressing


反义词：releasing, freeing


词性:动词

词源:
The term "shanghaiing" originates from the port city of Shanghai, China, in the 19th century.  During this period, Shanghai was a major port and a hub for international trade, and it was notorious for its prevalence of kidnapping sailors and forcing them onto ships.  The practice became so associated with Shanghai that the term "shanghaiing" came to describe the act itself.
