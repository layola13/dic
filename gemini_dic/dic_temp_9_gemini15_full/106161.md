knock:/nɒk/ /kɑːk/| vt. n.|敲，击；碰撞；批评|To strike a surface, especially a door or window, repeatedly with the hand or fist;  to criticize sharply.|例句：He knocked loudly on the door. 他大声敲门。

例句：The storm knocked down the trees.暴风雨刮倒了树木。


近义词：rap, tap, strike, criticize|反义词：push, pull


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He knocks on the door every morning. 他每天早上都敲门。
She knocks over the vase accidentally. 她不小心打翻了花瓶。


一般过去时:
I knocked on the door but nobody answered. 我敲了门，但没有人应答。
The car knocked into a tree. 车撞到了一棵树上。


现在完成时:
He has knocked on every door in the street. 他敲了这条街上的每一扇门。
The wind has knocked down several trees. 风刮倒了几棵树。


过去完成时:
I had knocked several times before she finally opened the door. 我敲了好几次门，她才最终开门。
The earthquake had knocked down many buildings. 地震已经摧毁了许多建筑物。


将来时:
I will knock on your door tomorrow morning. 我明天早上会敲你的门。
The strong winds will likely knock down the power lines. 强风可能会刮倒电线。


虚拟语气:
If I knocked, would anyone answer? 如果我敲门，会有人应答吗？
I wish I hadn't knocked so hard. 我真希望我当时没有敲那么大力。


被动语态:
The door was knocked loudly. 门被大声敲响了。
The vase was knocked over by the cat. 花瓶被猫打翻了。


疑问句:
Who knocked at the door? 谁在敲门？
Did someone knock? 有人敲门吗？


否定句:
He didn't knock before entering the room. 他没有敲门就进了房间。
She didn't knock the glass over. 她没有打翻玻璃杯。


PHRASE->
knock down: 撞倒；拆除
knock out:  击倒；使失去知觉
knock off:  下班；停止工作
knock on: 敲（门等）
knock about: 漂泊；四处奔波

词源:
The word "knock" originates from the Old English word "cnocian," meaning "to strike" or "to beat."  It's related to other Germanic words with similar meanings.
