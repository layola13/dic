enticer:/ɪnˈtaɪsər/| n. |诱惑者，引诱者 |A person or thing that entices or tempts.  Someone or something that attracts or charms. |例句：The advertisement was a clever enticer, drawing in many unsuspecting customers.  广告是一个巧妙的诱惑者，吸引了许多毫无戒心的顾客。

The sweet smell of freshly baked bread was a powerful enticer. 新鲜烘焙面包的香味是一种强大的诱惑。


近义词：tempter, seducer, allurement, lure |反义词：deterrent, repellent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The salesman acts as an enticer to sell products. = 销售员扮演诱惑者的角色来销售产品。
That shiny new car is an enticer for many people. = 那辆闪亮的新车对许多人来说是一种诱惑。

一般过去时:
The siren song acted as an enticer, leading sailors to their doom. = 塞壬的歌声起到了诱惑的作用，导致水手们走向灭亡。
The promise of easy money was a powerful enticer for the young man. = 轻而易举就能赚钱的承诺对这个年轻人来说是一个强烈的诱惑。

现在完成时:

The beautiful scenery has been an enticer for tourists for years. = 多年来，美丽的风景一直吸引着游客。

过去完成时:
The delicious aroma had been an enticer, drawing the guests to the dining room. = 美味的香气一直是一种诱惑，吸引着客人们来到餐厅。
The rumors of treasure had been a powerful enticer, leading them on a perilous journey. = 关于宝藏的谣言一直是一个强大的诱惑，引领他们踏上了一段危险的旅程。

将来时:
The new marketing campaign will be an enticer, attracting a new customer base. = 新的营销活动将成为一个诱饵，吸引新的客户群体。
The prospect of a vacation will be a great enticer for many people. = 度假的预期将对许多人来说是一个巨大的诱惑。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
