mocking:/ˈmɒkɪŋ/| /ˈmɑːkɪŋ/ | adj., v. | 嘲笑的；模仿的；戏弄的 |  Making fun of someone or something in a cruel or unkind way; imitating someone or something in a way that is intended to be amusing or contemptuous. |


例句：

His mocking laughter was unbearable. 他的嘲笑声令人难以忍受。

She gave a mocking imitation of the teacher's voice. 她模仿老师的声音，带着嘲讽的意味。


近义词：derisive, scornful, satirical, mimicking, taunting


反义词：respectful, complimentary, admiring, serious


词性：形容词，动词


词源:

The word "mocking" comes from the Old French word "moquer," meaning "to mock" or "to make fun of."  This ultimately derives from a Vulgar Latin word, possibly from a Germanic source.
