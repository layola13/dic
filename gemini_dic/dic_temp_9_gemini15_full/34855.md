clinks:/klɪŋks/| v.|叮当作响，碰撞声|The sound made by two hard objects hitting each other, often metal.|The glasses clinked as we toasted.|近义词：tinkles, chinks|反义词：silence


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The glasses clink together. = 玻璃杯碰撞在一起。
The coins clink in his pocket. = 硬币在他的口袋里叮当作响。


一般过去时:
The bells clinked merrily. = 铃铛快乐地叮当作响。
The ice clinked in the glass. = 冰块在玻璃杯里叮当作响。


现在完成时:
The wind chimes have clinked all night. = 风铃整夜都在叮当作响。
She has clinked her glass to mine many times. = 她已经多次与我碰杯了。


过去完成时:
The blacksmith had clinked his hammer all day. = 铁匠整天都在叮叮当当地敲打他的锤子。
The children had clinked their toys together before bed. = 孩子们在睡觉前把他们的玩具碰撞在一起。


将来时:
The glasses will clink at midnight. = 玻璃杯将在午夜时分碰撞。
The silverware will clink during the wedding feast. = 银器将在婚礼宴席上发出叮当声。


虚拟语气:
If only the glasses would clink together and seal our friendship. = 但愿酒杯能碰在一起，巩固我们的友谊。
I wish the wind chimes would clink more softly. = 我希望风铃能轻一些地响。


被动语态:
The glasses were clinked together. = 玻璃杯被碰在一起。
The coins were clinked together in his hand. = 硬币在他手中被碰撞在一起。


疑问句:
Did you hear the glasses clink? = 你听到玻璃杯碰撞的声音了吗？
Do you like the sound of coins clinking? = 你喜欢硬币叮当作响的声音吗？


否定句:
The glasses didn't clink. = 玻璃杯没有碰撞。
The bells didn't clink this time. = 铃铛这次没有响。
