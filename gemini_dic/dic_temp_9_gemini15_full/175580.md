randomize:/ˈrændəmaɪz/| vt.|使随机化；随机分配|To make something random; to arrange or select something in a random order.|例句：The experiment was designed to randomize the participants. 实验旨在对参与者进行随机分配。|例句：The software randomizes the questions each time you take the test. 软件每次测试时都会随机排列问题。|近义词：shuffle, jumble, scramble|反义词：order, organize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They randomize the test questions. = 他们将测试问题随机化。
We randomize the participant assignment. = 我们随机分配参与者。


一般过去时:
The researcher randomized the subjects into two groups. = 研究人员将受试者随机分为两组。
The computer randomized the order of the names. = 电脑随机排列了名字的顺序。


现在完成时:
The scientists have randomized the data for analysis. = 科学家们已经将数据随机化以进行分析。
They have randomized the selection process to ensure fairness. = 他们已经将选择过程随机化以确保公平。


过去完成时:
The team had randomized the samples before the experiment began. = 在实验开始之前，团队已经将样本随机化了。
She had randomized the playlist before starting her workout. = 在开始锻炼之前，她已经将播放列表随机化了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If we randomized the data, the results would be more reliable. = 如果我们对数据进行随机化，结果将更可靠。
It would be better if they randomized the participant selection. = 如果他们对参与者选择进行随机化，那就更好了。


被动语态:

否定句:
