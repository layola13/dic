prepositional:/ˌprɛpəˈzɪʃənəl/| adj. |介词的；用介词构成的| Relating to or characterized by prepositions.  A prepositional phrase is a group of words that begins with a preposition and ends with a noun or pronoun (its object).  Prepositional phrases modify nouns, verbs, or entire clauses. |例句：The book is on the table. (The phrase "on the table" is prepositional and modifies the verb "is".)  The bird flew over the house. (The phrase "over the house" is prepositional and modifies the verb "flew".)|近义词：prepositional phrase|反义词：N/A

The cat sits under the table. = 猫坐在桌子底下。
The dog sleeps in the house. = 狗在房子里睡觉。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:
