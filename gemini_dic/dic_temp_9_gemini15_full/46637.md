damned:/dæmd/ | adj., adv., v. |该死的，可恶的；非常；诅咒 |  The word "damned" is a strong adjective and adverb, often used as an intensifier or to express anger, frustration, or condemnation. It derives from the past participle of the verb "damn," which originally meant to condemn to hell.  Its usage can be informal and even vulgar depending on context. |例句：That's a damned good idea! = 那是个该死的绝妙主意！
This damned rain is ruining my picnic! = 这该死的雨把我的野餐都毁了！|近义词：cursed, blasted, confounded, infernal |反义词：blessed, holy, sacred


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is damned if he does and damned if he doesn't. = 他怎么做都会被骂。
She is damned to repeat this mistake. = 她注定要重蹈覆辙。


一般过去时:
They damned him for his actions. = 他们因他的行为而谴责他。
I damned the situation as hopeless. = 我认为情况毫无希望。


现在完成时:
I've been damned to a life of poverty. = 我被判处贫困的生活。
He has damned himself with his lies. = 他因谎言而自毁前程。


过去完成时:
They had damned the project before even starting it. = 他们在项目开始之前就否定了它。
She had damned herself to failure by her lack of preparation. = 她因缺乏准备而注定失败。


将来时:
He will be damned if he doesn't apologize. = 如果他不道歉，他将受到谴责。
They will be damned to eternal suffering. = 他们将遭受永恒的痛苦。


虚拟语气:
If he had listened, he wouldn't have been damned. = 如果他听了劝告，他就不会被谴责。
I wish I hadn't damned him so harshly. = 我真希望我没有那样严厉地谴责他。


被动语态:
He was damned by the public for his corruption. = 他因腐败而被公众谴责。
The plan was damned as unrealistic. = 该计划被认为不切实际。


疑问句:
Is he damned to failure? = 他注定要失败吗？
Am I damned if I try to help? = 如果我试图帮忙，我会遭殃吗？


否定句:
He wasn't damned for his beliefs. = 他并没有因为他的信仰而受谴责。
She wasn't damned to an unhappy life. = 她并没有注定过不幸的生活。
