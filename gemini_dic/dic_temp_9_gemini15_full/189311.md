safavids:/ˈsæfəvɪdz/| n. |萨非王朝；萨菲家族|The Safavids were a powerful dynasty that ruled Persia (modern-day Iran) from the early 16th to the early 18th centuries.  They are known for establishing Twelver Shia Islam as the state religion of Persia, significantly impacting the religious and cultural landscape of the region. |The Safavid dynasty's influence extended throughout Persia and beyond.|近义词: Qajars (a later Persian dynasty)|反义词: None
