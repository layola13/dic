mantled:/ˈmæntld/| v. adj. n.|披着斗篷的；覆盖的；覆盖物|To cover or envelop, as with a mantle;  to conceal or obscure.|例句：The mountain was mantled in snow.  山被雪覆盖着。|近义词：covered, shrouded, enveloped, cloaked|反义词：uncovered, exposed, bare


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The snow mantles the mountain. = 雪覆盖着山。
He mantles his feelings. = 他掩饰自己的感情。


一般过去时:
The old castle was mantled in ivy. = 古堡被常春藤覆盖着。
She mantled her disappointment. = 她掩饰了自己的失望。


现在完成时:
The hills have been mantled in mist all morning. = 整天早上山丘都被薄雾笼罩着。
He has mantled his true intentions. = 他一直掩盖着他真正的意图。


过去完成时:
The village had been mantled in darkness before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，村庄已被黑暗笼罩。
She had mantled her fear for years. = 她多年来一直掩饰着自己的恐惧。


将来时:

He will mantle his anger. = 他会掩饰他的怒火。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->
mantle of snow = 一层雪
mantle of secrecy = 一层神秘的面纱

词源:
From Old French *mantel, from Latin mantellum, diminutive of mantilla, "small mantle," of uncertain origin, possibly related to Latin manus, "hand".  The word's evolution reflects its historical use as a type of cloak or covering.
