dittying:/ˈdɪtiɪŋ/| v.|哼小曲；随意吟唱|The act of singing a ditty or short song, often in a lighthearted or casual manner.  It implies a simple, informal tune, not a formal or complex musical piece.|例句：She was dittying a cheerful tune as she worked in the garden. 她一边在花园里工作，一边哼着欢快的曲子。|例句：The children were dittying along to the radio. 孩子们跟着收音机哼唱。|近义词：humming, singing, warbling|反义词：belting (singing powerfully), chanting (singing in a monotone)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ditties a tune every morning. = 他每天早上都哼唱一首小曲。
She ditties while she cooks. = 她做饭时哼着小曲。

一般过去时:
He dittied a love song yesterday. = 他昨天哼唱了一首情歌。
They dittied happily on their way home. = 他们高兴地哼着歌回家。

现在完成时:
She has dittied that same song all day. = 她整天都在哼唱那同一首歌。
He has dittied many songs in his life. = 他一生中哼唱过许多歌曲。

过去完成时:
She had dittied the tune before I entered the room. = 我进房间之前，她就一直在哼唱那支曲子。
They had dittied a lullaby to their baby. = 他们已经给他们的宝宝哼唱了摇篮曲。


将来时:
I will ditty a song for you. = 我会为你唱首歌。
She will ditty while she walks in the park. = 她会在公园散步时哼歌。

虚拟语气:
If I could ditty, I would sing all day. = 如果我会哼唱，我会整天唱歌。
I wish I could ditty as beautifully as she does. = 我希望我能像她一样优美地哼唱。

被动语态:  (The verb "ditty" is rarely used passively.)
A cheerful tune was being dittied in the distance. = 远处传来一首欢快的曲调。 (This is unusual phrasing)
The song was dittied softly by the child. = 这首歌被孩子轻轻地哼唱着。(This is less common than active voice)


疑问句:
Are you dittying a song? = 你在哼歌吗？
Was she dittying when you arrived? = 你到达时她在哼歌吗？

否定句:
He doesn't ditty very often. = 他不经常哼歌。
She didn't ditty during the meeting. = 会议期间她没有哼歌。
