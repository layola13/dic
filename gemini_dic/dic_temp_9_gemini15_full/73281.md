forebode:/fɔːˈboʊd/|/fɔːˈbəʊd/ | v. |预兆，预感；预示…的发生 |To have a strong feeling that something bad is going to happen; to be a sign that something bad will happen. |The dark clouds foreboded a storm.  乌云预示着一场暴风雨。
The ominous silence foreboded trouble.  不祥的寂静预示着麻烦。| portend, presage, augur |  - | v.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dark clouds forebode rain. = 乌云预示着下雨。
She forebodes trouble for the company. = 她预感到公司会有麻烦。

一般过去时:
The old woman foreboded disaster. = 老妇人预感到了灾难。
The strange noises foreboded danger. = 奇怪的声音预示着危险。

现在完成时:
The recent events have foreboded a difficult future. = 最近发生的事件预示着未来将充满挑战。
Scientists have foreboded a potential environmental crisis. = 科学家们预言了一场潜在的环境危机。


过去完成时:

将来时:
Experts forebode a sharp increase in energy prices next year. = 专家们预言明年能源价格将大幅上涨。
Many people forebode a recession in the coming months. = 许多人预感未来几个月将出现经济衰退。

虚拟语气:
If I had listened to my gut, I would have foreboded the accident. = 如果我听了我的直觉，我本可以预感到这场事故。
I wish I had foreboded the consequences of my actions. = 我希望我早就能预见到我行为的后果。

被动语态:
The storm was foreboded by the darkening sky. = 乌云密布预示着暴风雨的来临。
The economic crisis was foreboded by many experts. = 许多专家都预言了这场经济危机。

疑问句:
Does the eerie silence forebode anything sinister? = 诡异的寂静预示着什么不祥之兆吗？
Do these signs forebode a change in the weather? = 这些迹象预示着天气变化吗？

否定句:
Nothing foreboded the sudden change of events. = 没有什么预示着事情的突然变化。
The initial signs did not forebode such a catastrophic outcome. = 最初的迹象并没有预示着如此灾难性的结果。
