nugatory:/ˈnjuːɡətəri/| adj. | 无价值的；无效的；微不足道的 | Having little or no value or importance.  | The argument was nugatory and easily refuted.  论点毫无价值，很容易驳斥。  His efforts proved nugatory. 他的努力证明是徒劳的。| insignificant, trifling, worthless | significant, important, valuable |


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The contract is nugatory. = 这份合同无效。
His objections are nugatory. = 他的反对意见毫无意义。


一般过去时:
The agreement was nugatory from the start. = 这项协议从一开始就无效。
Her complaints were nugatory and ignored. = 她的抱怨微不足道，被忽略了。


现在完成时:
The court has declared the contract nugatory. = 法院已宣布该合同无效。
His attempts have proven nugatory. = 他的尝试已被证明是徒劳的。


过去完成时:
It turned out that the evidence had been nugatory. = 事实证明证据毫无价值。
The lawyer realised the testimony had been nugatory. = 律师意识到证词毫无价值。


将来时:
The judge will likely declare the agreement nugatory. = 法官可能会宣布该协议无效。
Their efforts will likely be nugatory. = 他们的努力很可能会徒劳无功。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were nugatory, the case would collapse. = 如果证据无效，案件就会崩溃。
I wish his arguments were not so nugatory. = 我希望他的论点不要那么无力。


被动语态:
The law was deemed nugatory. = 这项法律被认为无效。
His efforts were rendered nugatory by unforeseen circumstances. = 他的努力因不可预见的情况而付诸东流。


疑问句:
Is his contribution to the project nugatory? = 他对项目的贡献是否微不足道？
Are these regulations nugatory and unenforceable? = 这些规章制度是否无效且无法执行？


否定句:
The evidence is not nugatory; it's crucial. = 证据并非毫无价值；它至关重要。
His work is not nugatory; it's highly significant. = 他的工作并非毫无价值；它非常重要。


PHRASE->
a nugatory argument = 无效的论点
a nugatory sum = 微不足道的数目
a nugatory effort = 无效的努力
a nugatory claim = 无效的主张
nugatory considerations = 无关紧要的考虑


词源:
From Latin *nugatorius, from nugæ "trifles."
