imagists:/ˈɪmɪdʒɪsts/| n. |意象派诗人（或画家）|Imagists were a group of poets who wrote in the early 20th century.  They emphasized the use of clear, precise imagery and free verse.  Their work often focused on concrete details and avoided abstract or overly sentimental language.|Example: Ezra Pound and Amy Lowell were prominent Imagists.|近义词：modernist poets|反义词：None specifically.  The opposite would be poets who prioritize abstract concepts or sentimental language.
