maces:/ˈmeɪsiz/| n.|权杖；球棒；(某些植物的)果穗|A mace is a ceremonial staff of office, often made of metal and ornately decorated, carried as a symbol of authority.  It can also refer to a heavy club or a type of spice. |例句：The mayor carried the mace of office during the ceremony. 市长在仪式上携带了权杖。

例句：The police officer used his mace to break up the fight. 警官用警棍制止了打斗。


近义词：scepter, club, staff, baton

反义词：None directly applicable


词源:From Old French *mace, from Late Latin *macia "club, mace," of uncertain origin possibly related to Old High German maz "club". The sense of the spice comes from the resemblance of its flower spikes to the club-like weapon.
