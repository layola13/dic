pouter:/ˈpaʊtər/| v.|撅嘴，生气地嘟嘴|To push out one's lips in a expression of annoyance, displeasure, or sulkiness.|The child pouted when he didn't get the toy he wanted.  孩子没得到想要的玩具，就撅起了嘴。|pout, sulk|smile, grin|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pouts when he is unhappy. = 他不开心的时候会撅嘴。
She often pouts to get what she wants. = 她经常撅嘴来得到她想要的东西。

一般过去时:
He pouted all afternoon after the argument. = 争吵后，他整个下午都撅着嘴。
The little girl pouted when her ice cream melted. = 小女孩的冰淇淋融化了，她撅起了嘴。

现在完成时:
He has pouted ever since he lost the game. = 自从输掉比赛后，他一直撅着嘴。
She has pouted for hours, refusing to speak to anyone. = 她已经撅嘴好几个小时了，拒绝和任何人说话。

过去完成时:
He had pouted for days before finally apologizing. = 他撅嘴好几天后才最终道歉。
She had pouted all morning before her mother finally gave in. = 她早上一直撅着嘴，直到她母亲最终让步。

将来时:
He will pout if he doesn't get his way. = 如果他不顺心，他会撅嘴。
She will probably pout when she finds out the bad news. = 当她发现坏消息时，她可能会撅嘴。

虚拟语气:
If he were happier, he wouldn't pout so much. = 如果他更快乐，他就不会那么撅嘴了。
I wish she wouldn't pout all the time. = 我希望她不要总是撅嘴。

被动语态:  (很少用被动语态)
His displeasure was pouted at the audience. = 他的不满向观众表达了出来（用pout作为名词）。
His unhappiness was shown by his pouting. = 他的不开心通过他的撅嘴表现了出来。

疑问句:
Does he often pout when he's angry? = 他生气的时候经常撅嘴吗？
Is she pouting because she didn't get the candy? = 她是不是因为没有得到糖果而撅嘴？

否定句:
He didn't pout when he heard the news. = 他听到这个消息时并没有撅嘴。
She doesn't usually pout; she's quite cheerful. = 她通常不会撅嘴；她很开朗。
