polylingualism:/ˌpɒliˈlɪŋɡwəlɪzəm/| n. |多语言能力；使用多种语言的现象|The ability to speak, read, and write in multiple languages. It refers to the state or condition of being able to communicate effectively in more than two languages.  This can encompass a range of proficiency levels in each language.  The term is often used in the fields of linguistics, education, and sociology. |例句：The school promotes polylingualism by offering classes in various languages. 这所学校通过提供各种语言课程来推广多语言能力。

例句：Her polylingualism allowed her to easily navigate international business dealings. 她的多语言能力使她能够轻松地处理国际商务往来。


近义词：multilingualism


反义词：monolingualism


词源:  The word "polylingualism" is derived from the Greek words "poly" (πολύς), meaning "many," and "lingua" (lingua), meaning "tongue" or "language." The suffix "-ism" denotes a state, condition, or practice.  Therefore, polylingualism literally means "the state of having many languages."
