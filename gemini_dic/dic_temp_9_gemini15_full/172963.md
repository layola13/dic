quandaries:/ˈkwɑːndəriːz/| n. |困境，难题，疑难问题 |A quandary is a state of perplexity or uncertainty over what to do in a difficult situation.  It implies a feeling of being trapped between equally undesirable options. |例句：He was in a quandary about whether to tell the truth or lie. 他左右为难，不知是说实话还是撒谎。

例句：The conflicting evidence left the jury in a quandary.  相互矛盾的证据使陪审团陷入困境。


近义词：dilemmas, predicaments, problems, puzzles |反义词：certainty, clarity, solution |词性：名词

词源:
The word "quandary" ultimately derives from the Middle English word "quadre," meaning "a square," which then evolved into "quadrary," implying a state of being "four-square," or fixed in a specific position, often one of difficulty.  The sense of being stuck or perplexed developed over time.
