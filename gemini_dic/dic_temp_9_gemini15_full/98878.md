intender:/ɪnˈtɛndər/ | vt. | 打算；想要；企图 | to have a plan or intention to do something |  He intends to travel to Europe next year. 他打算明年去欧洲旅行。 |  plan, purpose, aim |  forget, neglect, disregard | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He intends to finish the project by Friday. = 他打算在星期五之前完成这个项目。
She intends to buy a new car. = 她打算买一辆新车。

一般过去时:
They intended to go to the beach, but it rained. = 他们本来打算去海滩，但是下雨了。
He intended to call her, but he forgot. = 他本来打算给她打电话，但是他忘了。

现在完成时:
I have intended to write to him, but I haven't had time. = 我一直打算给他写信，但我一直没时间。
She has intended to quit her job for months. = 她几个月来一直打算辞职。

过去完成时:
They had intended to leave early, but they were delayed. = 他们本来打算早点离开，但是他们耽误了。
I had intended to visit my grandmother, but she passed away. = 我本来打算去看望我的祖母，但她去世了。

将来时:
We will intend to support the new initiative. = 我们将打算支持这项新的倡议。
They will intend to move to a bigger house. = 他们将打算搬到更大的房子里。

虚拟语气:
If I had intended to hurt you, I wouldn't have done it. = 如果我本来打算伤害你，我就不会这么做了。
I wish I had intended to study harder for the exam. = 我希望我本来打算为考试更努力学习。

被动语态:
The meeting was intended to discuss the budget. = 会议的目的是讨论预算。
The project was intended to improve the community. = 该项目的目的是改善社区。

疑问句:
Do you intend to go to the party? = 你打算去参加聚会吗？
What do they intend to do next? = 他们接下来打算做什么？

否定句:
I don't intend to answer his questions. = 我不打算回答他的问题。
She didn't intend to cause any trouble. = 她无意造成任何麻烦。


PHRASE->
intend to do something = 打算做某事
intend for = 打算给……用
be intended for = 专为……而设计
intend on doing something = 打算做某事
with the intention of = 打算；目的
