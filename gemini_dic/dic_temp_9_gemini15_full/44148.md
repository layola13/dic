critic:/ˈkrɪtɪk/| n.|评论家，批评者|A person who judges the merits of literary, artistic, or musical works, or the like.|The critic praised the novel's intricate plot.  这位评论家称赞了这部小说的复杂情节。|reviewer, commentator|supporter, admirer|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He criticizes the government's policies. 他批评政府的政策。
The critic reviews the new movie. 这位评论家评论这部新电影。


一般过去时:
She criticized his performance. 她批评了他的表演。
The critics panned the play. 批评家们狠批了这部戏。


现在完成时:
He has criticized the company's actions. 他已经批评了公司的行为。
The public has criticized the decision. 公众已经批评了这个决定。


过去完成时:
They had criticized the plan before the meeting. 他们在会议前就批评了这个计划。
The newspaper had already criticized the proposal. 报纸已经批评了这个提议。


将来时:
She will criticize his work again. 她会再次批评他的作品。
The reviewers will criticize the new album harshly. 评论家们会严厉批评这张新专辑。


虚拟语气:
If I were a critic, I would give it a bad review. 如果我是个评论家，我会给它一个差评。
I wish I hadn't criticized him so harshly. 我真希望我没有那么严厉地批评他。


被动语态:
His work was criticized by many. 他的作品受到了许多人的批评。
The movie was criticized for its poor acting. 这部电影因其糟糕的演技而受到批评。


疑问句:
Does the critic like the painting? 这位评论家喜欢这幅画吗？
What did the critics say about the concert? 评论家们对这场音乐会说了什么？


否定句:
He didn't criticize her writing. 他没有批评她的写作。
The critics did not find any faults in the performance. 评论家们没有发现表演中的任何错误。
