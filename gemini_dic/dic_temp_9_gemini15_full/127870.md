neocolonialization: /ˌniːoʊˌkəloʊniəlɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |新殖民主义，新殖民化 | The practice of using economic, political, cultural, or other pressures to control or influence a country or region, similar to the methods used during the historical colonial era, but without direct political control.  It often involves exploiting resources, influencing government policies, and maintaining economic dependence.  | Example:  The critics argued that the multinational corporation's practices constituted neocolonialization of the developing nation.  批评人士认为，这家跨国公司的行为构成了对发展中国家的新殖民主义。


|近义词:  economic imperialism, indirect rule, exploitation, domination |反义词:  self-determination, independence, liberation |词性: noun
