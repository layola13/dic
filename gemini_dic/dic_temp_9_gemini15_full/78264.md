germanicism:/ˈdʒɜːrmənɪsɪzəm/| n. |日耳曼语源词；日耳曼习语|A word or expression in English that is derived from German.  It refers to the influence of the German language on English, especially during periods of significant cultural or political interaction.  This influence can be seen in vocabulary, grammar, and even pronunciation.|Example:  The word "rucksack" is a germanicism in English.|近义词：German loanword, Germanism|反义词：N/A
