darning:/ˈdɑːrnɪŋ/| n. |缝补；修补 |The act of mending a hole or tear in fabric by weaving new threads through the damaged area.  |Example:  The darning on her socks was almost invisible. 她袜子上的缝补几乎看不出来。|近义词：mending, patching, repairing |反义词：tearing, ripping


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She darning socks every night. = 她每晚都在缝补袜子。
He darning the hole in his trousers. = 他正在缝补裤子上的洞。


一般过去时:
Grandma darned my sweater after I ripped it. = 祖母在我撕破毛衣后缝补了它。
She darned the hole in her jeans. = 她缝补了她牛仔裤上的洞。


现在完成时:
I have darned all the socks. = 我已经缝补好了所有的袜子。
She has darned several holes in her favorite dress. = 她已经缝补了她最喜欢的裙子上的几个洞。


过去完成时:
I had darned the socks before going to bed. = 我睡觉前已经缝补好了袜子。
She had darned the hole before she wore the dress. = 她穿裙子之前已经缝补好了洞。


将来时:
I will darn this hole tomorrow. = 我明天会缝补这个洞。
She will darn the socks after dinner. = 她晚饭后会缝补袜子。


虚拟语气:
If I had time, I would darn my socks. = 如果我有时间，我会缝补我的袜子。
I wish I had darned the hole earlier. = 我真希望我早点缝补了这个洞。


被动语态:
The socks were darned by my grandmother. = 袜子是我祖母缝补的。
The hole in the sweater was darned carefully. = 毛衣上的洞被仔细地缝补了。


疑问句:
Have you darned the trousers yet? = 你缝补好裤子了吗？
Did she darn the socks herself? = 她自己缝补的袜子吗？


否定句:
I didn't darn the socks. = 我没有缝补袜子。
She hasn't darned the sweater yet. = 她还没有缝补毛衣。
