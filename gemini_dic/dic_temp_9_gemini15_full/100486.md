interveined:/ˌɪntərˈveɪnd/| v.|介入，插入；(血管等) 交错分布 |To intervene is to become involved in a situation to try to change what is happening.  Interveined, as a past participle, often describes a state where things are interwoven or interconnected.  |例句：The government intervened to prevent a strike. 政府介入以阻止罢工。 The delicate veins interveined the leaf's surface.  纤细的叶脉交错分布在叶面上。|近义词：interfere, interpose, mediate |反义词：ignore, neglect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He intervenes in every family dispute. 他介入每一次家庭纠纷。
The river intervenes between the two towns. 这条河位于这两个城镇之间。


一般过去时:
She intervened to stop the fight. 她介入阻止了这场争斗。
The doctor intervened immediately. 医生立即进行了干预。


现在完成时:
They have intervened several times this year. 他们今年已经介入过好几次了。
The government has intervened in the economy. 政府已经介入经济事务。


过去完成时:
The police had intervened before the situation escalated. 在局势升级之前，警方已经介入。
He had intervened on behalf of his friend. 他已经代表他的朋友进行了干预。


将来时:
I will intervene if necessary. 如果必要，我会介入。
The army will intervene if the peace talks fail. 如果和平谈判失败，军队将会介入。


虚拟语气:
If he had intervened earlier, the outcome might have been different. 如果他早点介入，结果可能会有所不同。
I wish I had intervened sooner. 我希望我早点介入。


被动语态:
The dispute was intervened by the mediator. 纠纷由调解人进行了调解。
The situation was intervened in by the authorities.  情况受到了当局的干预。


疑问句:
Did anyone intervene during the argument?  在争吵期间有人介入吗？
Will the United Nations intervene in the conflict? 联合国将会介入冲突吗？


否定句:
He did not intervene in the conflict. 他没有介入冲突。
Nobody intervened to help her.  没有人介入帮助她。


PHRASE->

intervene to (do something): 为了做某事而干预
