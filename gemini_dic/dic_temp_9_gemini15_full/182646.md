reorganized:/ˌriːˈɔːrɡənaɪzd/| v. |重新组织；改组；改编|To organize something again, often in a different way.  This implies a restructuring or rearrangement of existing elements.|例句：The company reorganized its sales department to improve efficiency. 公司为了提高效率，重组了销售部门。|例句：The novel was reorganized to improve the plot flow. 这部小说被重新组织以改善情节流程。|近义词：restructured, rearranged, revamped|反义词：disorganized, disordered


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reorganize the office every year. = 他们每年都会重新组织办公室。
We reorganize our files regularly. = 我们定期整理我们的文件。

一般过去时:
The team reorganized its strategy after the loss. = 团队在输球后重新调整了战略。
She reorganized her closet to make more space. = 她重新整理了衣橱以腾出更多空间。

现在完成时:
They have reorganized the entire department. = 他们已经重组了整个部门。
The company has reorganized its marketing efforts. = 公司已经重新调整了其市场营销工作。

过去完成时:
They had reorganized the schedule before the announcement. = 他们在宣布之前已经重新安排了日程。
She had reorganized her notes before the exam. = 她在考试前已经重新整理了笔记。

将来时:
We will reorganize the library next month. = 我们下个月将重新整理图书馆。

虚拟语气:
