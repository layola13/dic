hyperinsulinaemia: /ˌhaɪpərɪnsʊlɪˈniːmiə/ | n. | 高胰岛素血症 |  An abnormally high level of insulin in the blood. This condition is often associated with insulin resistance, where the body's cells do not respond properly to insulin, leading to elevated blood sugar levels.  |  The doctor suspects hyperinsulinaemia as a contributing factor to the patient's weight gain. |近义词:hyperinsulinism|反义词:hypoinsulinaemia
