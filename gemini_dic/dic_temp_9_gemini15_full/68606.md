fantasm:/ˈfæntæzəm/| n. |幻想；幽灵；空想|A figment of the imagination; a ghost or phantom; an illusion.  The play was filled with fantasms of love and betrayal. 这出戏充满了爱与背叛的幻想。|近义词：illusion, hallucination, apparition, chimera|反义词：reality, truth


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The child fantasizes about flying. = 孩子幻想飞行。
He often fantasizes about his ideal life. = 他经常幻想他理想的生活。


一般过去时:
She fantasized about her childhood home. = 她幻想她童年的家。
They fantasized about traveling the world. = 他们幻想环游世界。


现在完成时:
I have fantasized about this moment for years. = 我多年来一直幻想这一刻。
She has fantasized about becoming a famous actress. = 她一直幻想成为一名著名的女演员。


过去完成时:
He had fantasized about winning the lottery before he actually won. = 在他真正中奖之前，他曾幻想过中彩票。
Before the interview, she had fantasized about acing it. = 面试前，她曾幻想自己会表现出色。


将来时:
I will fantasize about my future career. = 我将会幻想我未来的职业。
They will fantasize about their honeymoon. = 他们将会幻想他们的蜜月旅行。


虚拟语气:
If I could, I would fantasize about escaping to a tropical island. = 如果可以，我会幻想逃到一个热带岛屿。
I wish I could fantasize about something more positive. = 我希望我能幻想一些更积极的事情。


被动语态:
His dreams were fantasized by many. = 他的梦想被许多人幻想过。
The story is often fantasized by readers. = 这个故事经常被读者幻想。


疑问句:
What do you fantasize about? = 你幻想什么？
What are they fantasizing about now? = 他们现在幻想什么？


否定句:
He doesn't fantasize much. = 他不太幻想。
She didn't fantasize about having children. = 她从不幻想拥有孩子。
