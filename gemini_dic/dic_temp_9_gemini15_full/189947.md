sanctify:/ˈsæŋktɪfaɪ/ | vt. | 使神圣；奉为圣物；使纯洁 | To make or declare something sacred or holy.  To purify or cleanse spiritually. |例句：The church was sanctified by the priest. 教堂由牧师进行了圣化仪式。

The ancient ritual sanctified the land.  古老的仪式使这片土地神圣化。


近义词：consecrate, hallow, bless | 反义词：desecrate, profane


USAGE->

The priest sanctifies the bread and wine. = 牧师将面包和葡萄酒圣化。
She sanctifies her daily routine with prayer. = 她以祈祷使她的日常生活变得神圣。

They sanctified the temple with a special ceremony. = 他们以特别的仪式使神庙神圣化。

过去完成时:

They had sanctified the agreement with a solemn oath. = 他们以庄严的誓言使协议神圣化。

虚拟语气:

词源:
From Old French *sanctifier, from Latin sanctificāre "to make holy," from sanctus "holy" + -ficāre, suffix used to form verbs meaning "to make."  The ultimate root is likely Proto-Indo-European *sek- "to cut, separate," referring to the idea of setting something apart as holy or sacred.
