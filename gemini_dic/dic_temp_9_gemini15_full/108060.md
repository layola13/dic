latency:/ˈleɪtənsi/ | n. |延迟，潜伏期 |The time delay between a cause and effect, or between the stimulus and response.  It often refers to the time it takes for a system to respond to a request.  |例句：The latency of the network connection was causing problems. 网络连接的延迟导致了问题。

例句：High latency can make online gaming frustrating. 高延迟会使网络游戏令人沮丧。


近义词：delay, lag, dormancy |反义词：promptness, immediacy


词源：From Latin *latēns, present participle of latēre "to lie hidden".  The word evolved through the Old French latence.
