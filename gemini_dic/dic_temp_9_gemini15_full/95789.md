incontrovertibly: /ˌɪnkɒntrəˈvɜːtəbli/ | adv. |无可辩驳地，无可争议地 |  Beyond doubt or dispute; undeniably.  |例句：The evidence incontrovertibly proves his guilt.  证据无可辩驳地证明了他的罪行。


The results of the experiment incontrovertibly demonstrate the effectiveness of the new drug.  实验结果无可辩驳地证明了这种新药的有效性。


近义词：undeniably, undeniably, irrefutably, indisputably, undeniably


反义词：arguably, debatably, questionably, disputably


词源:

* **in-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "without"
* **controvert-**:  from Latin *controvertere*, meaning "to turn against," "to dispute"
* **-ible**: suffix making an adjective, meaning "capable of"
* **-ly**: suffix turning an adjective into an adverb


USAGE->
(Since 'incontrovertibly' is an adverb,  it doesn't have tenses like verbs.  The usage examples show how it modifies verbs and entire clauses.)


Example Sentences illustrating its use:

He incontrovertibly won the debate. 他无可辩驳地赢得了辩论。

The data incontrovertibly supports the theory. 数据无可辩驳地支持了这个理论。

The witness's testimony was incontrovertibly true. 证人的证词是无可辩驳的真实的。

It was incontrovertibly clear that she was lying.  很明显她是在撒谎（这是无可辩驳的）。

The evidence incontrovertibly linked him to the crime. 证据无可辩驳地将他与犯罪联系起来。


PHRASE->

(Adverbs like 'incontrovertibly' don't typically form common phrases in the same way verbs or nouns do. Its meaning is self-contained.)
