hypochondriacal: /ˌhaɪpəˈkɒndrɪəkəl/ | adj. | 疑病的；臆想病的 |

英文解析:  Characterized by or exhibiting hypochondria; excessively concerned about one's health and prone to imagining oneself suffering from various diseases.  It implies an unwarranted fear of illness, often based on misinterpretation of normal bodily sensations.


例句:

He's become increasingly hypochondriacal lately, convinced that every ache and pain signals a serious illness.  = 他最近越来越疑病，确信每一次疼痛都预示着严重的疾病。

Her hypochondriacal tendencies led her to seek medical attention for minor ailments that were easily treatable. = 她疑病的倾向让她去寻求医疗帮助，即使只是轻微且容易治疗的小毛病。


近义词:  anxious, apprehensive, worried, neurotic, obsessive


反义词:  healthy, unconcerned, stoic, robust


词性: adjective (形容词)


词源:  From "hypochondria," which comes from Greek "hypo-" (under) and "chondros" (cartilage), referring to the ancient belief that diseases originated in the hypochondriac region (the area under the ribs).  This belief, though inaccurate, gave rise to the term.
