conscripted:/kənˈskrɪptɪd/| v. adj.|征募；被征募的| To compel someone to serve in the armed forces.  The act of conscripting someone, or the state of being conscripted.| 例句：Many young men were conscripted into the army during the war.  战争期间许多年轻男子被征入伍。

The government conscripted doctors and nurses to help in the pandemic. 政府征召医生和护士来帮助应对疫情。


近义词：drafted, enrolled, called up| 反义词：volunteered, discharged


词性:动词(过去分词形式)  形容词(过去分词作形容词)


词源:  Comes from the Latin word "conscribere," meaning "to write together," originally referring to the act of writing down the names of those selected for military service.  The prefix "con-" means "together," and "scribere" means "to write."
