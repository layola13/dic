monopolist:/məˈnɒpəlɪst/|/ˈmɑːnəpəlɪst/| n. |垄断者，独占者 |A monopolist controls the supply of a particular commodity or service.  垄断者控制着某种特定商品或服务的供应。|The company was accused of being a monopolist in the market. 这家公司被指控在市场上拥有垄断地位。|monopolist,  trust |competitor,  rival |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The monopolist controls the market. = 垄断者控制着市场。
He monopolizes the conversation. = 他垄断了谈话。


一般过去时:
The monopolist controlled the industry for decades. = 垄断者控制了这个行业几十年。
She monopolized the attention of everyone in the room. = 她吸引了房间里所有人的注意力。


现在完成时:
The monopolist has stifled competition. = 垄断者扼杀了竞争。
He has monopolized the best seats. = 他占有了最好的座位。


过去完成时:
The monopolist had already established his dominance before the new regulations were introduced. = 在新的规章制度出台之前，垄断者已经确立了他的统治地位。
She had monopolized the discussion for the entire meeting. = 她在整个会议上都垄断了讨论。


将来时:
The monopolist will likely face legal challenges. = 垄断者很可能会面临法律挑战。
He will monopolize the stage for his entire performance. = 他将在他的整个表演中占据舞台。


虚拟语气:
If he were a monopolist, he would control the prices. = 如果他是垄断者，他就会控制价格。
I wish I hadn't monopolized the conversation. = 我真希望我没有垄断谈话。


被动语态:
The market was monopolized by a single company. = 市场被一家公司垄断。
The conversation was monopolized by him. = 谈话被他垄断了。


疑问句:
Is he a monopolist? = 他是垄断者吗？
Did she monopolize the discussion? = 她垄断了讨论吗？


否定句:
He is not a monopolist. = 他不是垄断者。
She did not monopolize the conversation. = 她没有垄断谈话。



PHRASE->

monopoly monopolist 垄断性垄断者
