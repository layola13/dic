incentive:/ɪnˈsɛntɪv/| n. |刺激；鼓励；动机|Something that motivates or encourages someone to do something.|The company offered incentives to its employees to improve productivity. 公司为了提高生产力而为员工提供了奖励。|motivation, encouragement, inducement|disincentive, deterrent|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
What incentive do you have to work harder? = 你有什么动力要更努力工作？
The bonus serves as a strong incentive for employees. = 奖金是激励员工的一个强大动力。


一般过去时:
What incentive did they offer for early completion? = 他们为了提前完成提供了什么奖励？
Tax breaks served as a powerful incentive in the past. = 税收优惠在过去曾是一个强大的激励因素。


现在完成时:
What incentives have they introduced recently? = 他们最近引入了哪些激励措施？
The new policy has provided significant incentives for investment. = 新政策为投资提供了巨大的激励。


过去完成时:

They had already offered substantial incentives to attract talent. = 他们已经提供了大量的激励来吸引人才。


将来时:
What incentives will be offered to attract new customers? = 将会提供什么奖励来吸引新客户？
The government will introduce new incentives to boost the economy. = 政府将推出新的激励措施以刺激经济。


虚拟语气:
If there were better incentives, more people would participate. = 如果有更好的激励措施，更多的人会参与。
I wish there were stronger incentives for environmental protection. = 我希望有更强有力的激励措施来保护环境。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
