promulgation:/ˌprɒmʌlˈɡeɪʃən/| n. |颁布；公布；宣传 |The official promulgation of the new law caused widespread discussion.  官方颁布新法引起广泛讨论。|The government's promulgation of its new policies was met with mixed reactions. 政府颁布新政策受到了褒贬不一的反应。| dissemination, publication, declaration | suppression, concealment | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The government promulgates new laws regularly. = 政府定期颁布新法律。
This company promulgates its mission statement every year. = 这家公司每年都会公布其使命宣言。


一般过去时:
The king promulgated a new decree. = 国王颁布了一道新法令。
They promulgated the results of the election. = 他们公布了选举结果。


现在完成时:
The organization has promulgated several important policy changes. = 该组织已经颁布了几项重要的政策变更。
The news has been promulgated throughout the country. = 这条新闻已经在全国公布。


过去完成时:
Before the war, they had promulgated a series of warnings. = 在战争爆发前，他们已经发布了一系列警告。
The company had promulgated its new marketing strategy before the launch. = 公司在产品发布前已经公布了新的营销策略。


将来时:
The president will promulgate the new economic plan next week. = 总统下周将公布新的经济计划。
They will promulgate the findings of their research soon. = 他们很快就会公布他们的研究结果。


虚拟语气:
If the government promulgated stricter regulations, the problem might be solved. = 如果政府颁布更严格的法规，这个问题或许就能解决。
I wish the school had promulgated the new rules earlier. = 我希望学校早点公布新规。


被动语态:
The new law was promulgated last month. = 新法是上个月颁布的。
The decision was promulgated to all employees. = 该决定已通知所有员工。


疑问句:
When will the new rules be promulgated? = 新规什么时候会公布？
Has the company promulgated its financial statements yet? = 公司是否已经公布财务报表了？


否定句:
The government did not promulgate the new policy on time. = 政府没有按时颁布新政策。
The company has not yet promulgated its annual report. = 公司尚未公布年度报告。
