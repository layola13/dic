nonconcentrical: /ˌnɒnkənˈsentrɪkl/ | adj. | 非同心的；不同中心的 | Not having a common center; not concentric.  The term describes two or more circles, spheres, or other figures that do not share the same center point.  | Example: The target's rings were nonconcentrical, making it difficult to score accurately.  目标环是非同心的，这使得准确得分变得困难。| 近义词: eccentric, off-center | 反义词: concentric

Word Origin:

The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not"), "con-" (meaning "together" or "with"), "centr-" (from Latin "centrum," meaning "center"), and the suffix "-ical" (forming an adjective).  Therefore, it literally means "not having a common center."
