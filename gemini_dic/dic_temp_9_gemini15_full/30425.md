centralisms:/ˈsɛntrəlɪzəm/| n.|中央集权制度；中央集权主义|The concentration of power and control in a central authority, often in government or an organization.  It can refer to political systems where the central government holds supreme authority, or to organizational structures where decision-making is highly centralized.|例句：The country suffered under decades of rigid centralism.  这个国家在数十年的严格中央集权统治下受苦。|例句：The company's centralism hindered innovation and responsiveness to local market needs. 这家公司的中央集权阻碍了创新和对当地市场需求的响应。|近义词：centralization, concentration of power|反义词：decentralization, federalism, autonomy|名词
