extrapolates:/ɪkˈstræpəleɪt/ | vt. |推断；推测；外推|To estimate, infer, or conclude something by extending or projecting known information.|The scientists extrapolated from the data to predict future trends.|近义词：infer, deduce, project|反义词：interpolate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He extrapolates the data to create a model. = 他根据数据推断出一个模型。
Scientists extrapolate from existing research. = 科学家从现有研究中推断。


一般过去时:
She extrapolated the results of the survey. = 她推断出调查的结果。
They extrapolated the findings to a larger population. = 他们将研究结果推断到更大的人群。


现在完成时:
Researchers have extrapolated the impact of climate change. = 研究人员已经推断出气候变化的影响。
We have extrapolated the sales figures for next year. = 我们已经推断出明年的销售额。


过去完成时:
They had extrapolated the data before drawing conclusions. = 他们在得出结论之前已经推断出数据。
The analyst had extrapolated the market trends before presenting the report. = 分析师在提交报告之前已经推断出市场趋势。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
