obtruding:/əbˈtruːdɪŋ/ | adj. | 冒犯的；突出的；强加的 |  Something that is obtruding is noticeable because it is inappropriately prominent or is forcing itself on one's attention. It suggests an unwelcome intrusion or imposition. | 例句：His loud laughter was obtruding in the quiet library.  他的大声笑声在安静的图书馆里很刺耳。

例句：Her constant questions were obtruding on our conversation. 她不断的问题打断了我们的谈话。


近义词：intrusive, imposing, prominent, interfering | 反义词：subtle, unobtrusive, discreet


词源:  Comes from the verb "obtrude," which is from the Latin "obtrudere," meaning "to thrust or push against."  The prefix "ob-" means "against" or "toward," and "trudere" means "to thrust."

一般现在时:
The loud music is obtruding on my concentration. = 吵闹的音乐扰乱了我的注意力。
His opinions are constantly obtruding themselves into the discussion. = 他的意见不断地强加到讨论中。

一般过去时:
The bright sunlight obtruded into the room. = 刺眼的阳光照进了房间。
His rude comments obtruded upon the peaceful atmosphere. = 他粗鲁的评论扰乱了宁静的气氛。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
