representative: /ˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv/ | n. | 代表，代理人 | A person who speaks or acts on behalf of another person or group.  | The representative spoke on behalf of the workers' union.  这位代表代表工会发言。 | delegate, agent, spokesperson |  unrepresentative, atypical | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He represents the company at the conference. 他在会议上代表公司。
She represents her constituents in parliament. 她代表她的选民在议会工作。


一般过去时:
He represented his country in the Olympics. 他代表他的国家参加奥运会。
She represented the firm at the trade show. 她代表公司参加了贸易展。


现在完成时:
They have represented this company for many years. 他们已经代表这家公司很多年了。
She has successfully represented numerous clients in court. 她已成功地在法庭上代表众多客户。


过去完成时:
He had represented the company before the merger.  在合并之前，他曾代表过这家公司。
They had represented the interests of the community for decades. 他们几十年来一直代表着社区的利益。


将来时:
She will represent the school at the debate. 她将代表学校参加辩论。
He will represent the firm in negotiations. 他将代表公司进行谈判。


虚拟语气:
If he were to represent the company, he would do a great job. 如果他代表公司，他会做得很好。
I wish she had represented us more effectively. 我希望她能更有效地代表我们。


被动语态:
The company is represented by a skilled lawyer. 公司由一位经验丰富的律师代表。
The interests of the workers were represented by the union. 工人的利益由工会代表。


疑问句:
Who represents your company? 谁代表你的公司？
Does anyone represent the interests of the consumers? 有没有人代表消费者的利益？


否定句:
He does not represent the views of the majority. 他并不代表大多数人的观点。
She did not represent her clients well in court. 她没有在法庭上很好地代表她的客户。
