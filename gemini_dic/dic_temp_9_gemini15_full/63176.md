entailing: /ɪnˈteɪlɪŋ/ | v.ing | 牵涉；包含；意味着 |  Involving something as a necessary part or consequence.  | 例句：The job entails a lot of travel. 这份工作需要大量的差旅。  | 例句：His decision entails considerable risk. 他的决定包含相当大的风险。 | 近义词：involving, including, implying, necessitating | 反义词：excluding, omitting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project entails extensive research. = 这个项目需要大量的研究。
This decision entails significant risks. = 这个决定包含巨大的风险。


一般过去时:
The experiment entailed many unexpected difficulties. = 实验带来了许多意想不到的困难。
The renovation entailed more work than we had anticipated. = 装修工程比我们预期的工作量更大。


现在完成时:
The changes have entailed significant costs. = 这些改变带来了巨大的成本。
Our actions have entailed unforeseen consequences. = 我们的行为带来了无法预料的后果。


过去完成时:
The investigation had entailed countless hours of work. = 调查耗费了无数个小时的工作。
The negotiations had entailed compromises from both sides. = 谈判双方都做出了让步。


将来时:
The new policy will entail significant adjustments. = 新政策将需要做出重大调整。
The merger will entail job losses. = 合并将导致失业。


虚拟语气:
If the plan entailed such risks, I would reconsider. = 如果计划包含如此大的风险，我会重新考虑。
I wish the project didn't entail so much paperwork. = 我希望这个项目不需要这么多文书工作。


被动语态:
Extensive research is entailed by the project. = 这个项目需要大量的研究。
Significant changes are entailed by this decision. = 这个决定需要重大的改变。


疑问句:
Does this position entail international travel? = 这个职位需要出国旅行吗？
What exactly does this plan entail? = 这个计划究竟包含什么？


否定句:
The job does not entail any overtime. = 这份工作不需要加班。
The agreement did not entail any legal obligations. = 这份协议不包含任何法律义务。



PHRASE->
entail upon  = 使承担
entail a cost = 导致成本
entail a responsibility = 承担责任
entail consequences = 导致后果
entail risks = 蕴含风险
