constellation:/ˌkɒnstəˈleɪʃən/| /ˌkɑːnstəˈleɪʃən/ | n. |星座，星群；一群杰出人物 |A constellation is a group of stars that form a recognizable pattern in the night sky.  Constellations are used in astronomy to help navigate and identify celestial objects. |例句：The Big Dipper is a well-known constellation.  北斗七星是一个众所周知的星座。

例句：The cast of that movie was a constellation of talented actors. 那部电影的演员阵容是一群才华横溢的演员。


近义词：asterism, configuration


反义词：None (no direct antonym)


词性：名词

一般现在时:
The constellation Orion is visible in the winter sky. = 冬天的夜空中可以看到猎户座。
Many constellations are named after mythological figures. = 许多星座是以神话人物命名的。


一般过去时:
Ancient astronomers identified many constellations. = 古代天文学家识别出许多星座。
They charted the positions of the constellations. = 他们绘制了星座的位置图。


现在完成时:
Scientists have cataloged thousands of constellations. = 科学家们已经编目了数千个星座。
Astronomers have discovered new stars within known constellations. = 天文学家们在已知的星座中发现了新的恒星。


过去完成时:
Before the invention of the telescope, people had only identified a few constellations. = 在望远镜发明之前，人们只识别出少数几个星座。
They had already mapped the major constellations centuries ago. = 几个世纪前，他们就已经绘制了主要星座的地图。


将来时:
We will observe the constellation Ursa Major tonight. = 我们今晚将观测大熊星座。
Future astronomers will likely discover new constellations. = 未来的天文学家可能会发现新的星座。


虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:
