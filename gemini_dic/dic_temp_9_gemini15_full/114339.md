majestic:/məˈdʒɛstɪk/ | adj. | 庄严的；宏伟的；威严的 |  Inspiring awe or admiration because of its grandeur, power, or beauty.  It suggests something impressive and commanding, often associated with royalty, nature, or great works of art or architecture. | Example: The majestic mountains towered over the valley.  = 雄伟的山峰耸立在山谷之上。 The majestic lion surveyed its kingdom. = 威严的狮子俯视着它的领地。 | 近义词: grand, magnificent, splendid, imposing, regal | 反义词: humble, insignificant, mean, paltry


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The mountains are majestic. = 山脉是雄伟的。
The castle appears majestic from afar. = 从远处看，城堡显得雄伟壮观。


一般过去时:
The king's entrance was majestic. = 国王的进场非常庄严。
The waterfall looked majestic in the sunlight. = 瀑布在阳光下显得非常壮观。


现在完成时:
The building has remained majestic for centuries. = 这座建筑物几个世纪以来一直保持着雄伟壮丽。
The view has always been majestic from this spot. = 从这个地方看到的景色一直都很壮观。


过去完成时:
The palace had been majestic even before the renovations. =  即使在翻修之前，宫殿就已经非常雄伟了。
The empire had once been majestic, but now lay in ruins. = 这个帝国曾经辉煌过，但现在却沦为废墟。


将来时:

The completed sculpture will look majestic. = 完成后的雕塑将会显得非常壮观。


虚拟语气:
If the building were majestic, it would attract more tourists. = 如果这座建筑物雄伟壮观，它会吸引更多游客。

被动语态:

疑问句:
