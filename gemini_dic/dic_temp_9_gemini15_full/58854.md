duplicitous:/djuːˈplɪsɪtəs/ | adj. |  欺骗的；两面派的；奸诈的 |  Characterized by deceitfulness; intending to deceive;  given to or characterized by duplicity.  It implies a deliberate and often cunning intention to mislead or betray. |  His duplicitous behavior led to the downfall of the company.  他的欺骗行为导致公司倒闭。

The politician's duplicitous promises angered the voters.  这位政治家的欺骗性承诺激怒了选民们。


近义词：deceitful, deceptive, treacherous, two-faced, dishonest, underhanded

反义词：honest, truthful, sincere, straightforward, candid


词源：From Middle English *duplecite, from Old French *duplecite, from Latin duplicitas "double dealing," from duplex "double," from duo "two" + -plex, combining form of plicare "to fold."  The suffix "-ous" indicates an adjective meaning "full of" or "characterized by."
