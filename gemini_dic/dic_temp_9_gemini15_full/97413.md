infringing:/ɪnˈfrɪndʒɪŋ/| v.|侵犯，违反|The act of infringing upon someone's rights or property.  It involves violating a law, rule, or agreement.|例句：Downloading copyrighted music without permission is infringing on copyright laws. 下载未经许可的版权音乐侵犯了版权法。

例句：Their expansion into new markets is infringing on our territory. 他们向新市场的扩张侵犯了我们的领地。

近义词：violating, trespassing, encroaching, contravening

反义词：respecting, upholding, obeying

词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are infringing on my privacy. = 他们侵犯了我的隐私。
He infringes the rules regularly. = 他经常违反规定。


一般过去时:
The company infringed the patent. = 公司侵犯了专利。
She infringed on his rights. = 她侵犯了他的权利。


现在完成时:
They have infringed my copyright. = 他们侵犯了我的版权。
He has infringed the law several times. = 他已经违反法律好几次了。


过去完成时:
They had infringed the agreement before we noticed. = 我们注意到之前，他们就已经违反了协议。
She had infringed upon his property for years. = 她多年来一直侵犯他的财产。


将来时:
They will infringe on our intellectual property. = 他们将侵犯我们的知识产权。
He will infringe the contract if he continues this behavior. = 如果他继续这种行为，他将违反合同。


虚拟语气:
If they infringed the law, they would face consequences. = 如果他们违反了法律，他们将面临后果。
I wish they hadn't infringed my trust. = 我真希望他们没有背叛我的信任。


被动语态:
His rights were infringed. = 他的权利受到了侵犯。
The agreement was infringed upon. = 协议被违反了。


疑问句:
Are they infringing on our rights? = 他们是否侵犯了我们的权利？
Did he infringe the law? = 他是否违反了法律？


否定句:
They did not infringe the contract. = 他们没有违反合同。

词源:
The word "infringing" comes from the Old French word "enfringre," which means "to break" or "to violate."  The prefix "in-" intensified the meaning, and "-ing" is the present participle suffix.  It ultimately traces back to the Latin word "frangere," meaning "to break."
