revalorizing:/ˌriˈvæljʊəraɪzɪŋ/| v.|重新评估；重新评价；赋予新的价值|The act of re-evaluating something and assigning it a new or renewed value, often in a positive or improved sense. It implies a reassessment that leads to a higher regard or appreciation.  |例句：The museum is revalorizing its collection of 19th-century paintings by showcasing them in a new, more modern context.  博物馆正在通过在新的、更现代的环境中展示其19世纪绘画收藏来重新评估其价值。

The government is revalorizing its social programs to better address the needs of the population. 政府正在重新评估其社会项目，以更好地满足民众的需求。|近义词：reassess, reappraise, rehabilitate, revitalize |反义词：devalue, depreciate, dismiss
