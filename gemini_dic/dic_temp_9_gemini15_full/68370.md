falling: /ˈfɔːlɪŋ/ | /ˈfɔːlɪŋ/ | v. adj. | 下落；下降；跌倒；衰落 |  The act or process of something dropping or descending; the state of something declining or deteriorating. | 近义词：dropping, descending, tumbling, declining | 反义词：rising, ascending, climbing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Leaves are falling from the trees. = 叶子从树上掉下来。
The temperature is falling rapidly. = 气温迅速下降。


一般过去时:
The vase fell and broke. = 花瓶掉下来摔碎了。
He fell down the stairs. = 他从楼梯上摔下来了。


现在完成时:
The stock market has been falling for weeks. = 股市已经下跌了好几个星期了。
She has fallen in love. = 她坠入爱河了。


过去完成时:
The rain had been falling steadily all day. = 雨一整天都在不停地下着。
Before I arrived, the snow had fallen heavily. = 我到达之前，雪下得很厉害。


将来时:
The price of oil will be falling next year. = 明年油价将会下跌。
I think it will be falling soon. = 我认为它很快就会掉下来。


虚拟语气:
If the price of gasoline fell, people would drive more. = 如果汽油价格下降，人们就会多开车。
If it were to fall, I'd catch it. = 如果它掉下来，我会接住它。


被动语态:
The tree was felled by the storm. (Note: "felled" is the past participle of "fell", often used passively) = 这棵树被风暴刮倒了。
The plan was falling apart. = 计划正在瓦解。


疑问句:
Is the rain still falling? = 雨还在下吗？
Is the temperature falling? = 气温下降了吗？


否定句:
The leaves are not falling yet. = 叶子还没掉下来。
The sales are not falling. = 销售额没有下降。


PHRASE->
falling in love: 坠入爱河
falling apart: 瓦解；崩溃
falling behind: 落后
falling short: 未达到；不足
falling down: 倒塌；跌倒
