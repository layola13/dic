packed-in: /pæktˈɪn/ | adj. | 包装好的；塞满的；拥挤的 |  Describes something that is tightly filled or densely packed, often implying a lack of space or a large quantity within a confined area.  It can refer to physical objects or people. | 例句：The suitcase was packed-in with clothes. = 行李箱塞满了衣服。

The concert venue was packed-in with enthusiastic fans. = 音乐会场挤满了热情的粉丝。|近义词：crowded, crammed, full, congested | 反义词：empty, sparse, uncrowded | 词性:形容词
