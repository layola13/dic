interdependability:/ˌɪntərˌdɪˈpɛndəbɪləti/| n. |相互依赖性，互相依存|The state of being mutually dependent; the reliance of two or more things on each other.  Interdependability highlights the interconnectedness of various elements within a system, where the success or failure of one component affects the others. |The success of the project relies on the interdependability of all team members.  这个项目的成功依赖于所有团队成员的相互依赖。


The global economy is characterized by high levels of interdependability between nations. 全球经济的特点是国家之间高度的相互依赖。


近义词：mutual dependence, interdependence, interconnectedness | 反义词：independence, self-sufficiency

词源:

* **inter-**:  prefix meaning "between," "among," or "mutually"
* **depend**: verb meaning "to rely on," "to be contingent upon"
* **-ability**: suffix forming nouns denoting capacity or capability


The word "interdependability" is a relatively modern coinage, a blend of the prefix "inter-" and the noun "dependability," itself derived from the verb "depend."  Its usage reflects the increasing awareness of interconnected systems in various fields, including ecology, economics, and sociology.
