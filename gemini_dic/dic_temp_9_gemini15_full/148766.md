overtwisting: /ˌoʊvərˈtwɪstɪŋ/ | v. |过度扭曲；过度旋转| The act of twisting something too much, beyond its normal or intended limit, often resulting in damage or distortion.  It can refer to physical objects or abstract concepts. |例句：Overtwisting the wire caused it to snap.  过度扭曲电线导致它断裂。

The excessive twisting of the rope led to its fraying.  绳子过度扭曲导致它磨损。


近义词：overtorquing, overrotating, distorting, warping, contorting | 反义词：untwisting, straightening, relaxing


词源:  The word is a combination of "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") and "twisting" (the present participle of the verb "to twist").  "Twist" originates from Old English *twiscian, meaning "to twine, braid."
