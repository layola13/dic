overworked:/ˌoʊvərˈwɜːrkt/ | adj. | 过度劳累的，筋疲力尽的 |  Describes someone who has been working too much, often to the point of exhaustion.  It implies a state of being tired and stressed due to excessive work. | Example:  She's overworked and stressed from juggling a demanding job and family responsibilities. 她因为要同时兼顾一份要求很高的工作和家庭责任而过度劳累和压力很大。


Example: He was overworked and needed a vacation. 他工作过度，需要休假。


Near Synonyms:  exhausted, fatigued, burned out, stressed, jaded


Antonyms: rested, relaxed, refreshed


Word Origin:  The word is formed by combining the prefix "over-" (meaning excessively or too much) with the past participle "worked" (from the verb "to work").  The combination directly indicates the meaning of excessive work.
