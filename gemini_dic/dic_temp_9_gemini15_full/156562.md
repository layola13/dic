picket:/ˈpɪkɪt/| n., v.|纠察；巡逻；罢工纠察；尖桩；木桩|

英文解析:
As a noun, a picket refers to a person stationed at a place to protest or demonstrate, often during a strike.  It can also refer to a pointed stake or post, typically of wood. As a verb, it means to patrol or demonstrate at a place, often as a form of protest.

例句:
The workers set up a picket line outside the factory.  = 工人们在工厂外设置了纠察线。

The farmer used pickets to build a fence. = 农民用尖桩来建篱笆。


近义词:
Noun: protestor, demonstrator, strikebreaker (for the opposite of picketing)
Verb: patrol, demonstrate, protest


反义词:
None directly oppose the entire meaning, but "negotiate" or "compromise" could be considered opposites in the context of labor disputes.


词性:
Noun, Verb
