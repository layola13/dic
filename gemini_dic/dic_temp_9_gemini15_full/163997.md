prediminished: /ˌpriːdɪˈmɪnɪʃt/ | adj. | 减弱的，预先减弱的 |  Describes a chord that is diminished before it is used in a musical progression.  It is created by lowering the third of a diminished chord by a half step. This results in a chord with a characteristically dissonant and unstable sound.  It's often used to create tension or unexpected harmonic movement. |  The prediminished chord added a surprising twist to the otherwise predictable melody. | 近义词：altered, dissonant | 反义词：consonant, stable
