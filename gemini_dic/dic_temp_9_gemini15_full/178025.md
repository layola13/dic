reckoned:/ˈrɛkənd/| vt. n.|认为，估计，计算|To believe something to be true or likely; to calculate or estimate something.|例句：I reckoned he would be late. 我认为他会迟到。|例句：The distance was reckoned at about two miles. 距离估计大约两英里。|近义词：believe, estimate, calculate, compute|反义词：disbelieve, underestimate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I reckon that's the best solution. = 我认为那是最好的解决办法。
They reckon the project will finish next month. = 他们估计项目下个月完成。


一般过去时:
She reckoned she had enough money. = 她认为她有足够的钱。
We reckoned the journey would take five hours. = 我们估计旅程需要五个小时。


现在完成时:
They have reckoned the cost of the project. = 他们已经计算出项目的成本。
I have reckoned without the cost of travel. = 我没有考虑到旅行的费用。


过去完成时:
I had reckoned that I would have enough time. = 我原本以为我有足够的时间。
She had reckoned without his help. = 她没有料到他的帮助。


将来时:
I reckon I'll be home by six. = 我估计六点之前能到家。
They reckon the storm will hit tomorrow. = 他们估计暴风雨明天来临。


虚拟语气:
If I reckoned correctly, we should arrive on time. = 如果我计算正确，我们应该准时到达。
I wish I had reckoned the risks more carefully. = 我真希望我当时更仔细地考虑过风险。


被动语态:
The distance was reckoned to be ten miles. = 距离被估算为十英里。
The cost is reckoned at about $10,000. = 成本估计约为1万美元。


疑问句:
Do you reckon he'll come? = 你认为他会来吗？
What do you reckon the problem is? = 你认为问题是什么？


否定句:
I don't reckon he'll be happy about it. = 我认为他不会高兴。
They didn't reckon on such a large crowd. = 他们没有预料到会有这么大的规模的人群。


PHRASE->
reckon with:  处理，对付 (to deal with or consider something)
reckon on:  依赖，指望 (to depend on or expect something)
reckon without:  没有考虑到 (to fail to consider something)
reckon up:  计算，总结 (to calculate or add up)
reckon among:  将…算作 (to count or include someone or something among others)
