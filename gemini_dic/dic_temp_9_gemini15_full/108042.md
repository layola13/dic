latch-onto:/lætʃ ˈɒnˌtuː/|/læʧ ˈɑːnˌtuː/| v. |抓住；理解；依赖；坚持|To grasp or seize something firmly. To understand or grasp an idea or concept. To become dependent on something or someone. To cling to or persist in something.|例句：The baby latched onto its mother's finger.  婴儿紧紧抓住母亲的手指。|例句：I finally latched onto the meaning of the poem. 我终于理解了这首诗的意思。|近义词：grab, grasp, comprehend, cling to, depend on |反义词：release, let go of, ignore, abandon


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He latches onto new ideas quickly. = 他很快就能抓住新的想法。
She latches onto her beliefs strongly. = 她坚定地坚持自己的信仰。

一般过去时:
The dog latched onto the bone fiercely. = 那条狗凶猛地咬住骨头。
He latched onto that opportunity immediately. = 他立即抓住了那个机会。

现在完成时:
She has latched onto a new diet plan. = 她开始坚持一项新的饮食计划。
They have latched onto this trend for years. = 他们多年来一直坚持这一趋势。

过去完成时:
He had latched onto the theory before anyone else. = 他比任何人都早地抓住了这个理论。
She had latched onto that belief since childhood. = 她从小就坚信这一点。

将来时:
I think he will latch onto this project enthusiastically. = 我认为他会热情地投入这个项目。
She will latch onto any chance to improve her skills. = 她会抓住任何机会提高自己的技能。

虚拟语气:

I wish she had latched onto the opportunity sooner. = 我希望她早点抓住这个机会。

被动语态:
The idea was latched onto by many people. = 许多人接受了这个想法。
The solution was quickly latched onto by the team. = 团队很快接受了这个解决方案。

疑问句:
