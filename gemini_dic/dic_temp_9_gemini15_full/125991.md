muskogees:/ˈmʌskoʊɡiːz/ | n. |马斯科吉人（复数） | The Muskogees are a group of indigenous peoples of the Southeastern Woodlands of North America, historically inhabiting a vast area encompassing parts of present-day Alabama, Georgia, Florida, Mississippi, and Louisiana. They are known for their rich cultural heritage and complex social structures.  | The Muskogees were skilled farmers and warriors, and their language, Muscogee, is still spoken by many of their descendants.  | Creek, Seminole |  (no direct antonym) |名词
