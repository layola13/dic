myriarch:/ˈmɪriɑːrk/| n. |万主，统治无数者|A myriarch is a ruler or sovereign of countless numbers;  a powerful leader or potentate over a vast multitude. The term evokes images of immense power and dominion over a seemingly limitless population or territory. It is rarely used in modern English and carries a strong literary or archaic feel.|例句：The ancient texts spoke of a myriarch whose empire spanned continents.  古代文献中提到一位帝国横跨大陆的万主。  The myriarch ruled with an iron fist, feared by all.  万主铁腕统治，人人惧怕。|近义词：sovereign, potentate, monarch, emperor, ruler|反义词：subject, subordinate, vassal
