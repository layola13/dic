liberalizing: /ˈlɪbərəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 自由化；开放化 | The act of making something more liberal; removing or relaxing restrictions or controls.  It often refers to economic policies, social attitudes, or political systems. | 例句：The government is liberalizing its trade policies to attract foreign investment.  政府正在放宽贸易政策以吸引外国投资。

The government is liberalizing its immigration laws to address labor shortages.  政府正在放宽移民法以解决劳动力短缺问题。

近义词：deregulating, freeing, opening up, reforming | 反义词：restricting, controlling, tightening, suppressing | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government is liberalizing its economic policies. = 政府正在放宽其经济政策。
They are liberalizing their views on social issues. = 他们正在放宽对社会问题的看法。


一般过去时:
The country liberalized its media laws in the 1990s. = 该国在20世纪90年代放宽了其媒体法律。
The reforms liberalized the education system. = 改革使教育体系更加自由化。


现在完成时:
The country has liberalized its trade significantly in recent years. = 近年来，该国已大幅度放宽了贸易。
They have liberalized their stance on same-sex marriage. = 他们已经放宽了对同性婚姻的立场。


过去完成时:
Before the revolution, the country had not liberalized its political system. = 在革命之前，该国尚未放宽其政治制度。
They had already liberalized their visa requirements before the agreement. = 在达成协议之前，他们已经放宽了签证要求。


将来时:
The government will liberalize its immigration policy next year. = 政府将在明年放宽其移民政策。
We expect the company to liberalize its hiring practices. = 我们预计该公司将放宽其招聘做法。


虚拟语气:
If they liberalized the market, more businesses would thrive. = 如果他们放宽市场，更多企业将蓬勃发展。
It would be beneficial if the government liberalized its regulations. = 如果政府放宽其规章制度，将是有益的。


被动语态:
The market was liberalized by the government reforms. = 市场因政府改革而自由化。
The trade barriers have been liberalized through international agreements. = 通过国际协议，贸易壁垒已被放宽。


疑问句:
Will the government liberalize its censorship laws? = 政府会放宽审查制度吗？
Has the company liberalized its dress code? = 公司已经放宽着装规定了吗？


否定句:
The government did not liberalize its trade policy as quickly as expected. = 政府没有像预期的那样迅速地放宽其贸易政策。
They haven't liberalized their social policies enough. = 他们没有充分放宽其社会政策。

词源:  From "liberal" + "-izing" (a suffix indicating the act of making something).  "Liberal" ultimately derives from the Latin word "liberalis," meaning "pertaining to a free man," from "liber," meaning "free."
