preinaugurated:/ˌpriːɪˈnɔːɡjʊreɪtɪd/| adj.| 就职前的；就职典礼前的 |  Done or occurring before an inauguration.  This word describes something that happens in the period leading up to a formal inauguration ceremony, often involving preparations or preliminary actions. | 例句：The preinaugurated celebrations included a parade through the city. = 就职典礼前的庆祝活动包括一场穿过城市的游行。

The preinaugurated meetings focused on policy discussions. = 就职典礼前的会议集中讨论政策。


近义词：anteinaugural, preliminary (to inauguration), prior (to inauguration) | 反义词：postinaugural


词源:

* **pre-**:  prefix meaning "before" or "in advance of"
* **inaugurate**: verb meaning "to formally begin or introduce (something); to install (someone) in office with a ceremony"

The word is formed by combining the prefix "pre-" with the past participle of "inaugurate".  Therefore, it literally means "before the inauguration".
