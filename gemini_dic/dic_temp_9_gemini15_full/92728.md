ichnolithology:/ˌɪknoʊlɪˈθɑːlədʒi/| n. |古生物遗迹学|The branch of paleontology that studies fossil traces of animal activity, such as footprints, burrows, and coprolites.  It helps reconstruct ancient environments and ecosystems by analyzing the behavior and interactions of extinct organisms.|The ichnolithology of the site revealed a diverse community of dinosaurs, including herbivores and carnivores.|近义词：paleontology (broader term)|反义词：无
