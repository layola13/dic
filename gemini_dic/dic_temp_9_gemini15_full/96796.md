inerrability: /ˌɪnɛrəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 无谬性；不会犯错的能力 | The quality of being incapable of making mistakes or being wrong; infallibility.  It often refers to the supposed inability of certain authorities, like religious texts or leaders, to err. | Example:  The church's claim of inerrancy for its scriptures is a matter of ongoing debate. 教会对其经文无谬性的说法是一个持续争论的问题。 | 近义词：infallibility, impeccability | 反义词：fallibility, error-proneness | 词性:名词
