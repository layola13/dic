panic-buttons: /ˈpænɪk ˌbʌtənz/ | n. | 恐慌按钮；紧急按钮 |  A panic button is a mechanism, typically a button or switch, designed to quickly trigger an alarm or emergency response in a situation of danger or distress. It is often used in situations where immediate action is needed to prevent harm or injury.  | Example:  The employee pressed the panic button when the intruder entered the building. = 当入侵者进入大楼时，员工按下了紧急按钮。

The security guard activated the panic buttons during the robbery. =保安在抢劫案中启动了紧急按钮。

| 近义词：emergency button, alarm button, distress signal | 反义词：None |  词性：名词
