linkage:/ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ/| n. |联系；关联；遗传连锁|The connection or relationship between things.|Genetic linkage refers to the tendency of genes located close together on a chromosome to be inherited together. 遗传连锁是指染色体上紧密相连的基因一起遗传的趋势。|近义词：connection, association, relationship|反义词：separation, disconnection


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The linkage between the two events is clear. = 这两个事件之间的联系很清楚。
This study explores the linkage between diet and disease. = 这项研究探讨了饮食与疾病之间的联系。


一般过去时:
The police investigated the linkage between the two crimes. = 警方调查了这两起犯罪之间的关联。
Scientists discovered a new linkage in the human genome. = 科学家们在人类基因组中发现了一种新的联系。


现在完成时:
Researchers have established a strong linkage between smoking and lung cancer. = 研究人员已经确定了吸烟和肺癌之间的强烈联系。
We have found a surprising linkage between music and memory. = 我们发现音乐和记忆之间存在令人惊讶的联系。


过去完成时:
Before the discovery, there had been little understanding of the linkage between genes and behavior. = 在这项发现之前，人们对基因和行为之间联系的了解很少。
They had already established the linkage between the two companies before the merger. = 在合并之前，他们已经建立了两家公司之间的联系。


将来时:
Further research will uncover more linkages between genes and diseases. = 进一步的研究将揭示基因和疾病之间更多的联系。
Scientists predict that future studies will reveal important linkages in brain function. = 科学家预测，未来的研究将揭示大脑功能中重要的联系。


虚拟语气:
If there were no linkage between the two variables, the results would be different. = 如果这两个变量之间没有联系，结果就会不同。
If the linkage had been stronger, the outcome might have been better. = 如果联系更强，结果可能会更好。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
