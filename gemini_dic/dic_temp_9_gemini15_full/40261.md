contingential:/kənˈtɪndʒəntəl/| adj.| 或然的；意外的；可能的；取决于情况的 | Dependent on or subject to a condition or contingency; possible but not certain.  It emphasizes the uncertainty and dependence on external factors. | The contingent plan will be implemented if the primary strategy fails.  应急计划将在主要策略失败时实施。| possible, dependent, conditional, eventual | certain, definite, inevitable, assured


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The outcome is contingent on several factors. = 结果取决于几个因素。
This plan is contingent upon market conditions. = 这个计划取决于市场状况。


一般过去时:
The success of the project was contingent on securing funding. = 项目的成功取决于获得资金。
His attendance was contingent upon his health. = 他的出席取决于他的健康状况。


现在完成时:
The decision has been contingent on the results of the survey. = 决定一直取决于调查结果。
Everything has been contingent on the weather. = 一切都取决于天气。


过去完成时:
The plan had been contingent on the availability of resources. = 该计划一直取决于资源的可获得性。
The success of the mission had been contingent on the cooperation of locals. = 任务的成功取决于当地人的合作。


将来时:
The trip will be contingent on the weather forecast. = 旅行将取决于天气预报。
Our plans will be contingent on the availability of tickets. = 我们的计划将取决于门票的可获得性。


虚拟语气:
If the weather were favorable, the event would not be contingent on anything else. = 如果天气良好，活动将不会取决于其他任何因素。
I wish the outcome were not contingent on such unpredictable factors. = 我希望结果不会取决于如此不可预测的因素。


被动语态:
The decision was contingent on many things. = 这个决定取决于许多事情。
The success of the project is contingent on several factors. = 项目的成功取决于几个因素。


疑问句:
Is the decision contingent on external approval? = 该决定是否取决于外部批准？
Is the plan contingent on the outcome of the negotiations? = 该计划是否取决于谈判的结果？


否定句:
The outcome is not contingent on luck. = 结果不取决于运气。
The success is not contingent on a single factor. = 成功不取决于单一因素。
