/ˌɪntərˌmjuˈteɪʃən/| n. |交互突变，互变异构|The alteration of two or more genes by reciprocal exchange of segments of DNA.  This process is common in many organisms and plays a significant role in genetic diversity.  Intermutation can lead to new combinations of alleles and contribute to evolutionary change.|Example:  The study investigated the role of intermutation in the evolution of antibiotic resistance in bacteria.  这项研究调查了交互突变在细菌抗生素耐药性进化中的作用。|近义词: recombination, exchange, translocation|反义词: mutation (in the sense of a single gene change)|名词
