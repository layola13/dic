dusking:/ˈdʌskɪŋ/| n. |黄昏；傍晚；渐暗 |The process of becoming dark; the period of twilight.  It often refers to the atmospheric conditions during twilight. |例句：The dusking sky painted the clouds in hues of orange and purple.  黄昏的天空将橙色和紫色的色调涂抹在云朵上。

例句：We enjoyed a romantic walk during the dusking hours. 我们在黄昏时分享受了一次浪漫的散步。


近义词：twilight, nightfall, gloaming, evening


反义词：dawn, daybreak, sunrise


词性:名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun is dusking.  太阳正在西沉。
Dusking often occurs around 6 PM in the winter. 冬天黄昏经常发生在下午六点左右。

一般过去时:
The dusking began early that day. 那天黄昏开始得很早。
We watched the dusking from the hilltop. 我们从山顶上观赏黄昏。

现在完成时:
It has been dusking for an hour. 黄昏已经持续了一个小时了。
The sky has already dusked. 天空已经黄昏了。

过去完成时:
It had dusked before we reached the cabin. 我们到达小屋前天已经黑了。
The birds had settled down before the dusking.  鸟儿们在黄昏前就安顿下来了。


将来时:
It will be dusking soon.  很快就要黄昏了。
We will watch the dusking from the beach. 我们将在海滩上观看黄昏。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
The process of dusking is beautifully serene. 黄昏的过程是宁静而美丽的。 (This is less common because "dusking" is a natural process, not an action performed on something.)
