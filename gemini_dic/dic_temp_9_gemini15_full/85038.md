harboring:/ˈhɑːrbərɪŋ/ | v. |窝藏；怀有（感情、想法等）|To give shelter or refuge to, especially someone fleeing from the law; to contain or conceal (something undesirable).|例句：The farmer was accused of harboring fugitive slaves. = 这位农民被指控窝藏逃亡的奴隶。

例句：She harbors deep resentment towards her former boss. = 她对她的前任老板怀有深深的怨恨。

近义词：sheltering, concealing, hiding, nurturing, cherishing

反义词：revealing, exposing, disclosing


USAGE->

They harbor resentment towards their rivals. = 他们对他们的竞争对手怀有怨恨。
The old house harbors many secrets. = 这座老房子隐藏着许多秘密。

一般过去时:
The ship harbored in the bay for the night. = 船在海湾停泊了一夜。

词源:
The word "harbor" comes from the Old English word "heafod," meaning "head" or "headland," referring to a sheltered place where ships could anchor. Over time, its meaning broadened to encompass the idea of providing shelter and refuge, thus leading to the verb "harboring."
