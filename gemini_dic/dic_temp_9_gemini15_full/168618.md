proclamation:/ˌprɒkləˈmeɪʃən/| n. |公告；宣言；声明 |A public and official announcement, especially one declaring something.  |例句：The king issued a proclamation of war. 国王发布了战争宣言。|例句：The mayor's proclamation declared the city a disaster area. 市长的公告宣布该城市为灾区。|近义词：announcement, declaration, pronouncement |反义词：concealment, secrecy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government issues many proclamations each year. = 政府每年发布许多公告。
This company makes a proclamation of its new product every quarter. = 这家公司每个季度都会发布其新产品的公告。


一般过去时:
The president proclaimed the independence of the nation. = 总统宣布国家独立。
They proclaimed their love for each other. = 他们宣告彼此相爱。


现在完成时:
The authorities have proclaimed a state of emergency. = 当局已宣布进入紧急状态。
The school has proclaimed its commitment to diversity and inclusion. = 学校已经宣布其致力于多元化和包容性。


过去完成时:
After the war had ended, they proclaimed peace. = 战争结束后，他们宣布和平。
The company had proclaimed its success before the official results were released. = 在官方结果发布之前，该公司已经宣布其成功。


将来时:
The governor will proclaim a day of mourning. = 州长将宣布哀悼日。
They will proclaim the winner of the competition tomorrow. = 他们明天将宣布比赛的获胜者。


虚拟语气:
If the king proclaimed a new law, the people would have to obey. = 如果国王颁布新法，人民就必须服从。
I wish the government would proclaim a solution to this problem. = 我希望政府能宣布解决这个问题的方案。


被动语态:
A state of emergency was proclaimed by the government. = 紧急状态由政府宣布。
The winner of the election will be proclaimed later tonight. = 选举获胜者将在今晚晚些时候宣布。


疑问句:
Has the government proclaimed any new policies recently? = 最近政府是否宣布了任何新政策？
What will be proclaimed at the ceremony? = 典礼上将宣布什么？


否定句:
The government did not proclaim a new law. = 政府没有颁布新法。
The judge did not proclaim a verdict yet. = 法官还没有宣布判决。
