comparatives:/kəmˈpærətivz/ | n. |比较级（复数）|Comparative forms are adjectives or adverbs that show a difference in degree or quality between two things.  They are often formed by adding "-er" to the adjective or adverb, or by using "more" or "less" before the adjective or adverb. For example, "bigger" is the comparative form of "big," and "more beautiful" is the comparative form of "beautiful".|The teacher explained the rules of forming comparatives and superlatives. 老师解释了构成比较级和最高级的规则。
