quasi-equally:/ˈkwɑːziˈiːkwəli/ | adv. | 近乎相等地，大致相等地 |  Approximating equality; almost equally. It implies a degree of inequality, but the difference is relatively small or insignificant in the context. |例句：The two groups were quasi-equally represented in the study.  = 这两组在研究中几乎平等地被代表。

The results were quasi-equally distributed across the different categories. = 结果在不同的类别中几乎平均分布。| 近义词：approximately equally, nearly equally, roughly equally | 反义词：unequally, disproportionately


WORD ORIGIN->
The word "quasi-equally" is a compound word.  "Quasi" is derived from the Latin word "quasi," meaning "as if," "as it were," or "almost." "Equally" is an adverb derived from the adjective "equal," ultimately from the Latin word "aequalis," meaning "equal," "even," or "uniform."  Therefore, "quasi-equally" literally means "almost equally" or "approximately equally."


USAGE->
This word is primarily used in academic or technical writing, particularly in contexts involving statistical analysis, data representation, or comparisons where perfect equality is not achieved but a close approximation exists.  It's less common in everyday conversation.  There isn't a specific tense conjugation for "quasi-equally" as it's an adverb. Its usage is consistent across different tenses.

Example Sentences demonstrating its use in different contexts:


In a descriptive sentence:
The resources were quasi-equally allocated among the three departments. = 三个部门的资源分配大致相同。


In a comparative sentence:
The performance of the two algorithms was quasi-equally efficient. = 两种算法的性能大致相同。


In a statistical context:
The study found that the participants' responses were quasi-equally distributed across the age groups.  = 研究发现，参与者的回应在各个年龄组之间大致平均分布。
