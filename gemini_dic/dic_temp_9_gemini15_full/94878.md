in case:/ɪn keɪs/ | conj. | 万一，以防 |  In case expresses a possibility or contingency. It introduces a clause describing a situation that might happen, and the main clause explains what action should be taken in response. It is often used to express caution or preparedness. | 例句：Take an umbrella in case it rains. = 万一下雨，带把伞。

I'll bring extra food in case we run out. = 万一食物不够，我会多带一些。


近义词：if, lest, for fear that | 反义词：certainly, definitely | 词性:连词
