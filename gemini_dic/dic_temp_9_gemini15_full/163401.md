preconsolidating: /ˌpriːkənˈsɒlɪdeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 预先巩固；预先整合 |  The act of strengthening or unifying something in advance.  It implies preparing for a future consolidation or merger. This is typically done to improve efficiency, stability, or competitive advantage before a larger, more formal process. |

例句：

The company is preconsolidating its assets in preparation for a major merger next quarter. = 公司正在预先整合其资产，为下个季度的大规模并购做准备。

The government is preconsolidating its resources to better respond to the impending natural disaster. = 政府正在预先整合资源，以便更好地应对即将到来的自然灾害。


近义词：preparing, consolidating, unifying, integrating, strengthening

反义词：disintegrating, fragmenting, weakening, dividing, separating


词性：动词
