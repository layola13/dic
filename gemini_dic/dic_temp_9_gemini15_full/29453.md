catalyse: /ˈkætəlaɪz/ | vt. |催化；促成| To accelerate or cause (a reaction, process, or event) to happen faster or sooner, typically by acting as a catalyst.|例句：The discovery catalysed further research in the field.  这个发现促进了该领域进一步的研究。|近义词：accelerate, expedite, facilitate, trigger|反义词：inhibit, hinder, impede, obstruct|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The enzyme catalyses the reaction. = 酶催化该反应。
Heat catalyses the chemical process. = 热量促进了化学过程。


一般过去时:
The catalyst catalysed the breakdown of the substance. = 催化剂促进了物质的分解。
The event catalysed a significant shift in public opinion. = 此事件促成了公众舆论的重大转变。


现在完成时:
The new policy has catalysed economic growth. = 新政策促进了经济增长。
His speech has catalysed a national debate. = 他的演讲引发了全国性的辩论。


过去完成时:
The prior investment had catalysed the company's expansion. = 先前的投资促成了公司的扩张。
The initial success had catalysed further investment in the project. = 初期成功促进了对该项目的进一步投资。


将来时:
The new technology will catalyse a revolution in manufacturing. = 新技术将催化制造业的革命。
The upcoming conference will catalyse discussions on climate change. = 即将召开的会议将促进关于气候变化的讨论。


虚拟语气:
If we had a better catalyst, the reaction would catalyse more quickly. = 如果我们有更好的催化剂，反应会更快地催化。
If the government implemented this policy, it would catalyse economic recovery. = 如果政府实施这项政策，它将促进经济复苏。


被动语态:
The reaction was catalysed by the enzyme. = 该反应是由酶催化的。
The process was catalysed by the addition of a catalyst. = 该过程是通过添加催化剂来催化的。


疑问句:
What will catalyse this change? = 什么会催化这种变化？
What factors catalysed the industrial revolution? = 哪些因素催化了工业革命？


否定句:
The inhibitor did not catalyse the reaction; instead, it slowed it down. = 抑制剂并没有催化反应；相反，它减缓了反应。
The lack of funding did not catalyse the research project. = 资金不足并没有促进研究项目。
