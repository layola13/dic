sentimentalises:/ˌsɛntɪmənˈtaɪzɪz/ | v. | 使感伤；使多愁善感；过分强调情感 | To make something or someone sentimental; to excessively focus on or exaggerate emotions, often in a way that is considered overly sweet or mawkish.  |  He sentimentalises the past, ignoring its harsh realities. 他过分怀念过去，忽略了它残酷的现实。  |  romanticize, idealize |  de-sentimentalize, objectify | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sentimentalises his childhood. = 他对他的童年过于怀旧。
She sentimentalises every little thing. = 她把每一件小事都看得过于感伤。

一般过去时:
The author sentimentalized the characters' relationship. = 作者过分渲染了人物之间的关系。
The movie sentimentalized the war experience. = 这部电影过分美化了战争经历。

现在完成时:
He has sentimentalized his memories to such an extent that they're unrealistic. = 他已经把他的回忆美化到不切实际的程度。
She has sentimentalized her relationship with her grandfather. = 她已经对她与祖父的关系过于感伤。

过去完成时:

将来时:
She will sentimentalize this moment forever. = 她将永远怀念这一刻。

虚拟语气:

I wish he wouldn't sentimentalize every small event. = 我希望他不必对每一个小事件都过于感伤。

被动语态:
