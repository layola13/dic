scowlers:/ˈskaʊlərz/| n.|怒视的人，皱眉的人|People who habitually scowl; those who frown.  The scowlers in the audience made the speaker uncomfortable.|近义词：glowerers, frowners|反义词：smilers


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The children scowl at each other. = 孩子们互相怒视。
He scowls when he's angry. = 他生气的时候会皱眉。

一般过去时:
She scowled at the rude comment. = 她对粗鲁的评论皱起了眉头。
They scowled at the injustice. = 他们对不公正的行为怒目而视。

现在完成时:
He has scowled all day. = 他整天都愁眉苦脸的。
She has scowled at me since the argument. = 自从那次争吵后，她一直对我怒目而视。

过去完成时:

They had scowled throughout the entire meeting. = 他们在整个会议期间一直愁眉苦脸。

否定句:

词源:
The word "scowl" comes from the Middle English word *sculen*, meaning "to frown," which is related to Old Norse *skúla*, also meaning "to frown."  The ultimate origins are likely in Proto-Germanic, but the exact pathway is uncertain.
