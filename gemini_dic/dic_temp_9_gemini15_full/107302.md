lamarckism:/ləˈmɑːrkɪzəm/| n. |拉马克主义|The theory of inheritance of acquired characteristics,  proposed by Jean-Baptiste Lamarck, stating that changes acquired during an organism's lifetime can be passed on to its offspring.  This theory is now largely discredited in the face of modern evolutionary biology.|The giraffe's long neck is often cited as an example of Lamarckism;  Lamarck suggested that giraffes stretched their necks to reach higher leaves, and this stretching was inherited by their offspring.|Darwinism, neo-Darwinism,  evolution|Mendelism, Darwinism|名词
