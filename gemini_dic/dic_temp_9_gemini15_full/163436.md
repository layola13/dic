precontact: /ˌpriːˈkɑːntækt/ | adj. |接触前；接触前的| Relating to the period before contact between two groups of people, especially between indigenous populations and colonizers.  This term often refers to the period before significant European contact with the Americas, but can apply to other similar historical situations. |例句：Precontact societies in the Pacific Northwest relied heavily on fishing and foraging.  接触前的太平洋西北部社会严重依赖捕鱼和觅食。


The precontact archaeological record reveals much about their social structures.  接触前的考古记录揭示了他们社会结构的许多信息。|近义词：antecedent, prior, preceding |反义词：postcontact
