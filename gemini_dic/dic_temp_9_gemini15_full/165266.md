preinsurances:/ˌpriːɪnˈʃʊərənsɪz/| n. |预先保险；预付保险|Insurance purchased in advance, often to cover a specific period or event before a primary insurance policy takes effect.  It might provide temporary coverage until the main policy is active or offer supplemental protection not covered by the main policy.|例句：The company offered preinsurances for the equipment during shipping to mitigate potential losses before the main policy began.|近义词：advance insurance, interim insurance|反义词：post-insurance (not a common term)
