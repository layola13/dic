hurtling:/ˈhɜːrtlɪŋ/ | v. |猛冲，飞驰，急速前进 |Moving extremely fast, in a way that seems uncontrolled.  |例句：The car hurtled down the hill.  这辆车飞速冲下山坡。|例句：The runaway train was hurtling towards the bridge.  失控的火车正朝桥飞驰而去。|近义词：racing, dashing, speeding, rushing |反义词：creeping, crawling, inching

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bird hurtles through the air. = 鸟儿在空中飞驰。
He hurtles towards the finish line. = 他猛冲向终点线。

一般过去时:
The rollercoaster hurtled down the track. = 过山车飞速冲下轨道。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

He had hurtled through the forest before he realized he was lost. = 他在意识到自己迷路之前就已经冲过森林了。

疑问句:

否定句:
The plane was not hurtling; it was flying smoothly. = 飞机没有猛冲；它飞行平稳。

词源:
The word "hurtling" comes from the Old Norse word "hyrta," meaning "to push, to strike."  Over time, the word evolved to describe forceful, rapid movement.
