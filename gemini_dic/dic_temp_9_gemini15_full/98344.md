insinuators:/ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪtərz/| n. |暗指者；暗示者 |A person who insinuates or hints at something unpleasant.  Those who subtly imply something negative or harmful without stating it directly.  |例句：The insinuators spread rumors that damaged his reputation. 暗指者散布谣言，损害了他的名声。
The insinuators in the office created a hostile work environment. 办公室里的暗指者制造了充满敌意的工作环境。|近义词：whisperers, backbiters, gossips |反义词：truth-tellers, supporters
