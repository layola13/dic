preacquisitively:/ˌpriːækwɪˈzɪtɪvli/ | adv. |  贪婪地；渴望地；急切地 |  In a manner showing eagerness to acquire something; greedily.  It implies a strong desire to obtain something, often to the point of being excessive or unscrupulous. |例句：He eyed the antique jewelry preacquisitively. 他贪婪地盯着那些古董珠宝。

例句：She preacquisitively snatched the last cookie from the plate. 她急切地从盘子里抓走了最后一块饼干。


近义词：avidly, greedily, covetously, eagerly, ravenously


反义词：uninterestedly, disinterestedly, nonchalantly


词性：副词
