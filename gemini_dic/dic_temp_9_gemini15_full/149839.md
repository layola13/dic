paleotheroid:/ˌpeɪliːˈθɪərɔɪd/| n. |古兽类|Paleotheroid refers to a superfamily of extinct mammals belonging to the order Perissodactyla, which includes modern-day horses, rhinoceroses, and tapirs.  They were herbivores and inhabited various parts of the world during the Eocene and Oligocene epochs.  Their characteristics include long, low skulls, and relatively long legs.  They are considered ancestors of modern perissodactyls but are not directly ancestral to any extant group. | Example:  The discovery of new paleotheroid fossils in Wyoming sheds light on the evolution of perissodactyls.  怀俄明州发现的新的古兽类化石揭示了奇蹄类动物的进化过程。|近义词：extinct perissodactyl (已灭绝的奇蹄类动物)|反义词：暂无|名词
