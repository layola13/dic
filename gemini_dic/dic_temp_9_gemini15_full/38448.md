concession:/kənˈseʃən/| n. |让步；特许；承认；特许权 | The act of conceding or yielding, a thing conceded; a grant or privilege. It can refer to something given up in negotiation, an acknowledgment of a point, or a right or privilege granted. |例句：The company made a concession to the workers and increased their wages. 公司向工人做了让步，提高了他们的工资。

例句：He made a concession to his opponent's argument. 他对对手的论点做出了让步。


近义词：compromise, yield, grant, allowance |反义词：refusal, denial, insistence


词源：源自于古法语“concession”，意为“让步，授予”，最终来源于拉丁语“concedere”，意思是“给予，让步”。  “con-”是加强前缀，“cedere”意为“to yield, to go”。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government makes concessions to appease protesters. = 政府做出让步以平息抗议者。
She rarely makes concessions in negotiations. = 她很少在谈判中做出让步。

一般过去时:
The union made significant concessions during the strike. = 罢工期间工会做出了重大让步。
He conceded defeat after the election results. = 选举结果出来后，他承认了失败。


现在完成时:
Both sides have made concessions in the peace talks. = 双方在和平谈判中都做出了让步。
The company has conceded to the employees' demands. = 公司已经同意了员工的要求。


过去完成时:
They had already made several concessions before the final agreement. = 在最终协议达成之前，他们已经做出了几次让步。
The government had conceded to pressure from the opposition. = 政府已经屈服于反对派的压力。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
