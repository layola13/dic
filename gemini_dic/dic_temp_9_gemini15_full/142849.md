out-glooms:/ˌaʊtˈɡluːmz/| v.|黯然失色，胜过…的阴暗|To surpass in gloom or darkness; to exceed in melancholy or sadness.|例句：The oppressive atmosphere of the old house out-gloomed even the stormy night outside.  这栋老房子的压抑气氛甚至超过了外面的暴风雨之夜。|例句：His despair out-gloomed any sorrow I'd ever witnessed. 他的绝望超过了我见过的任何悲伤。|近义词：outshines, surpasses, overshadows|反义词：brightens, illuminates, cheers


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dark clouds out-gloom the setting sun. = 黑云遮蔽了落日。
His sadness out-glooms her joy. = 他的悲伤胜过了她的喜悦。

一般过去时:
The storm out-gloomed the previous night's darkness. = 暴风雨比前一天晚上更黑暗。
Yesterday's news out-gloomed all expectations. = 昨天的消息比所有预期都更令人沮丧。

现在完成时:
The recent events have out-gloomed any previous crisis. = 最近发生的事件比以往任何危机都更令人沮丧。
This winter has out-gloomed all others in recent memory. = 这个冬天比最近记忆中的任何一个冬天都更阴暗。

过去完成时:
The previous tragedy had out-gloomed anything they'd ever experienced. = 之前的悲剧比他们经历过的任何事情都更令人沮丧。
The old castle had out-gloomed all other buildings in the village. = 这座古老的城堡比村里其他所有建筑物都更阴暗。

将来时:
The coming winter will out-gloom the last one. = 即将来临的冬天将比上一个冬天更阴暗。
The projected economic downturn will out-gloom current predictions. = 预计的经济衰退将比目前的预测更糟糕。

虚拟语气:
If the clouds were thicker, they would out-gloom the night. = 如果云层更厚，它们将会比夜晚更阴暗。
If he had more troubles, his despair would out-gloom even this. = 如果他遇到更多麻烦，他的绝望甚至会超过这个。

被动语态:
The previous happiness was out-gloomed by the recent tragedy. = 之前的快乐被最近的悲剧所掩盖。
The bright sunshine was out-gloomed by the sudden storm.  明亮的阳光被突如其来的暴风雨所遮蔽。

疑问句:
Does his grief out-gloom her sorrow? = 他的悲伤是否超过了她的悲伤？
Will the coming winter out-gloom the previous one? = 即将来临的冬天会比前一个冬天更阴暗吗？

否定句:
The sunshine did not out-gloom the shadows. = 阳光没有遮蔽阴影。
The pleasant atmosphere did not out-gloom the happy occasion. = 愉快的氛围没有使快乐的场合黯然失色。
