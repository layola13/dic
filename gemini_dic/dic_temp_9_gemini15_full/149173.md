oxyacid:/ˈɒksiˌæsɪd/| n.|含氧酸|An oxyacid is an acid that contains oxygen.  It is typically formed from a nonmetal oxide reacting with water. The general formula is HₓAOᵧ, where A is a nonmetal atom.  Examples include sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄), nitric acid (HNO₃), and phosphoric acid (H₃PO₄).|Sulfuric acid is a strong oxyacid used in many industrial processes.|近义词：oxoacid|反义词：无
