词源:

The word "missorted" is a combination of the prefix "mis-" (meaning "badly," "wrongly," or "incorrectly") and the past participle of the verb "to sort" (meaning "to arrange or categorize").  Therefore, it literally means "incorrectly sorted".  The verb "to sort" has Old English roots.


用法说明：

"Missorted" is primarily used as an adjective to describe something that has been incorrectly categorized or arranged. It often refers to tangible items like files, data, or physical objects, but can also be applied to abstract concepts if they are organized into categories.  The implication is that a proper sorting process was attempted but failed to achieve the desired result due to error or mishap.  There is no verb form of "missorted".  It's always used as an adjective describing a noun.
