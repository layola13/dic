interposed:/ˌɪntərˈpoʊzd/| vt.|插入；介入；打断|To place or insert between other things; to interrupt or come between.  |例句：He interposed himself between the fighting men. 他介入争斗的双方之间。|例句：She interposed a question, interrupting the speaker. 她插话提问，打断了发言者。|近义词：insert, intervene, interrupt|反义词：remove, withdraw, continue


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He interposes his opinion frequently. = 他经常插话发表意见。
She interposes a comment in the discussion. = 她在讨论中插了一句嘴。


一般过去时:
The judge interposed to stop the argument. = 法官介入阻止了争论。
He interposed his hand to shield her from the blow. = 他伸出手来保护她免受打击。


现在完成时:
He has interposed himself in many business deals. = 他已介入过许多商业交易。
She has interposed objections at every step. = 她每一步都提出了异议。


过去完成时:
Before I could speak, he had interposed a remark. = 我还没来得及说话，他就插了一句嘴。
They had interposed a delay in the project's launch. = 他们推迟了项目的启动。


将来时:
He will interpose if necessary. = 必要时他会介入。
She will interpose an objection to the proposal. = 她将对该提案提出异议。


虚拟语气:
If he had interposed earlier, the fight might have been avoided. = 如果他早点介入，这场争斗也许就能避免了。
I wish he had not interposed his opinion. = 我真希望他没有插话发表意见。


被动语态:
His opinion was interposed at an inappropriate time. = 他的意见在不恰当的时候被插入。
A new paragraph was interposed into the report. = 报告中插入了一个新的段落。


疑问句:
Will he interpose himself in this matter? = 他会介入这件事吗？
Did she interpose any objections during the meeting? = 会议期间她有提出异议吗？


否定句:
He did not interpose in the dispute. = 他没有介入这场争端。
She did not interpose any further remarks. = 她没有再做任何评论。


PHRASE->
interpose an objection = 提出异议
interpose a question = 插话提问
interpose oneself = 介入；干预
interpose a remark = 插嘴；插话
interpose a plea = 提出请求
