diluting: /daɪˈluːtɪŋ/ | v. | 稀释；冲淡；减弱 | To make a liquid weaker by adding water or another liquid to it; to make something weaker or less effective. | 例句：He diluted the paint with water to make it less thick. 他用水稀释油漆使其不那么浓稠。


例句：The company is diluting its brand by expanding into unrelated markets.  这家公司通过拓展与自身业务无关的市场来稀释其品牌影响力。


近义词：weakening, thinning, weakening, reducing, lessening | 反义词：concentrating, strengthening, intensifying | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dilutes the juice with water every morning. = 他每天早上都用水稀释果汁。
They dilute the cleaning solution before using it. = 他们在使用清洁剂之前先将其稀释。


一般过去时:
She diluted the acid carefully. = 她小心地稀释了酸。
The rain diluted the strength of the chemicals in the soil. = 雨水冲淡了土壤中化学物质的强度。


现在完成时:
We have diluted the stock solution to the required concentration. = 我们已将原液稀释到所需的浓度。
The government has diluted its efforts to control inflation. = 政府已经削弱了控制通货膨胀的努力。


过去完成时:
They had diluted the paint before applying it to the walls. = 他们在将油漆涂到墙上之前已经将其稀释了。
The company had diluted its market share by failing to innovate. = 这家公司由于缺乏创新而稀释了其市场份额。


将来时:
She will dilute the syrup with sparkling water. = 她将用苏打水稀释糖浆。
The doctor will dilute the medicine before giving it to the patient. = 医生会在给病人服药之前稀释药物。


虚拟语气:
If I diluted the solution further, it would be too weak. = 如果我进一步稀释溶液，它就会太稀了。
I wish I had diluted the paint more before painting the walls. = 我真希望我在粉刷墙壁之前能把油漆稀释得更多一些。


被动语态:
The solution was diluted with distilled water. = 该溶液是用蒸馏水稀释的。
The brand's message is being diluted by inconsistent marketing. = 品牌信息由于营销不一致而被削弱。


疑问句:
How much water should I dilute the concentrate with? = 我应该用多少水稀释浓缩液？
Has the company diluted its commitment to environmental sustainability? = 公司是否削弱了其对环境可持续性的承诺？


否定句:
He did not dilute the bleach before using it. = 他在使用漂白剂之前没有稀释它。
The company did not dilute its core values despite the pressure. = 尽管面临压力，该公司并没有淡化其核心价值观。


PHRASE->
dilute with = 用...稀释
dilute the effect of = 削弱...的影响
dilute the message = 冲淡信息
dilute the power = 削弱权力
dilute the stock = 稀释股票


词源:  Comes from the Latin word *diluere*, meaning "to wash away," "to rinse," or "to flood."  This reflects the original sense of diluting as washing something away or making it less strong.
