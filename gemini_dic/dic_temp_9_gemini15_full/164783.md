prefertilize: /ˌpriːˈfɜːrtɪlaɪz/ | v. | 预先施肥 | To fertilize (land, etc.) before the usual time or before planting.  This term is primarily used in agriculture and horticulture. It refers to the application of fertilizer to soil in advance of the normal planting season or before the main crop is sown or planted, often to improve soil conditions or to provide nutrients for a future crop.  | Example: The farmer decided to prefertilize the field to ensure a bountiful harvest in the spring.  农民决定预先施肥，以确保春天丰收。 | 近义词: pre-fertilize, pre-treat (in some contexts) | 反义词:  post-fertilize (a less common term) |
