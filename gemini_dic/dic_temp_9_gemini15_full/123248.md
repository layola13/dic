monocyte:/ˈmɒnəsaɪt/|/ˈmɑːnəsaɪt/| n. |单核细胞|A type of white blood cell that is part of the immune system and plays a role in fighting infections. Monocytes are the largest type of white blood cell and are characterized by their large, single nucleus. They circulate in the blood and can migrate into tissues, where they differentiate into macrophages and dendritic cells, which are important for phagocytosis (engulfing and destroying pathogens) and antigen presentation (presenting antigens to T cells to initiate an adaptive immune response).|例句：Monocytes are crucial in the body's defense against bacterial infections. 单核细胞对机体防御细菌感染至关重要。|例句：The number of monocytes in the blood can be an indicator of infection or inflammation. 血液中单核细胞的数量可能是感染或炎症的指标。|近义词：leukocyte, white blood cell, macrophage (after differentiation)|反义词：N/A
