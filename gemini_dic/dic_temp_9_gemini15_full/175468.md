rampage:/ˈræmpeɪdʒ/| n. v.|狂暴；暴行；横冲直撞|A rampage is a period of uncontrolled and violent behavior.  It can refer to a person's actions or a widespread destructive event.|例句：The protesters went on a rampage, smashing windows and setting cars on fire.  抗议者们开始暴乱，砸碎窗户并纵火烧车。


The angry mob went on a rampage through the streets.愤怒的暴徒在街上横冲直撞。|近义词：frenzy, outburst, riot, spree|反义词：calm, peace, tranquility|词性:noun, verb


USAGE->

词源:
The word "rampage" originates from the Middle Dutch word "rampen," meaning "to rush violently" or "to act violently."  The word evolved through various forms in Middle English and ultimately settled into its modern form.
