confronted:/kənˈfrʌntɪd/| v.|面临；对抗；使面对；质问|to meet (someone) face to face, especially as a challenge; to present (someone) with something unwelcome or difficult to deal with|例句：He was confronted by a masked man. 他被一个蒙面人拦住了。

例句：She was confronted with the evidence of her own guilt. 她被迫面对她自己有罪的证据。


近义词：faced, encountered, challenged, confronted with


反义词：avoided, ignored, evaded


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He confronts his fears every day. = 他每天都直面自己的恐惧。
They confront the challenges head-on. = 他们迎难而上。


一般过去时:
I confronted him about his lies. = 我就他的谎言质问了他。
The police confronted the suspect. = 警察与嫌疑犯对峙。


现在完成时:
She has confronted many obstacles in her life. = 她一生中克服了许多障碍。
We have confronted the problem and found a solution. = 我们直面了这个问题并找到了解决方法。


过去完成时:
He had confronted the bully before, but this time it was different. = 他以前曾与欺凌者对抗过，但这次情况不同。
They had confronted the issue many times, but no resolution was reached. = 他们多次讨论过这个问题，但没有达成解决方案。


将来时:
I will confront him tomorrow. = 我明天将要和他对质。
They will confront the difficult decision soon. = 他们很快就会面对这个艰难的决定。


虚拟语气:
If I confronted him, what would happen? = 如果我去质问他，会发生什么？
I wish I had confronted him earlier. = 我真希望我早点和他对质。


被动语态:
He was confronted by the unexpected news. = 他被突如其来的消息震惊了。
The issue was confronted by the committee. = 委员会正面对这个问题。


疑问句:
How did you confront the situation? = 你是如何应对这种情况的？
Will you confront your boss about the unfair treatment? = 你会因为不公平待遇而与老板对质吗？


否定句:
He didn't confront his problems. = 他没有面对自己的问题。
She didn't confront the difficult truth. = 她没有面对残酷的真相。


PHRASE->
confront a problem = 面对一个问题
confront a challenge = 面对一个挑战
confront a crisis = 面对一场危机
confront one's fears = 面对恐惧
confront the enemy = 与敌人对抗


词源:
From Old French confronter, from Latin con- ("together") + frons, frontis ("forehead, face").  The original meaning emphasized the act of facing someone directly.
