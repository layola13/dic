legalized:/ˈliːɡəlaɪzd/ | vt. | 使合法化；准许 | To make something legal.  |The government legalized marijuana in several states.  政府在几个州将大麻合法化了。|  authorized, permitted, sanctioned | prohibited, outlawed, banned | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They legalize certain drugs in some countries. = 在一些国家，他们将某些药物合法化。
The city council legalizes the construction of new buildings. = 市议会批准了新建建筑物的施工。

一般过去时:
The country legalized same-sex marriage last year. = 该国去年将同性婚姻合法化。
They legalized gambling in the state a decade ago. = 他们十年前在该州将赌博合法化。

现在完成时:
Many countries have legalized abortion. = 许多国家已经将堕胎合法化。
They have legalized online gambling recently. = 他们最近将在线赌博合法化了。

过去完成时:
Before the reform, they had legalized many previously illegal activities. = 改革之前，他们已经将许多以前非法的活动合法化。
The government had legalized the import of certain goods before the trade agreement. = 在贸易协定之前，政府已经将某些商品的进口合法化。


将来时:
The government will legalize the sale of alcohol in the near future. = 政府将在不久的将来将酒类销售合法化。
They will likely legalize assisted suicide soon. = 他们可能会很快将辅助自杀合法化。


被动语态:
Marijuana was legalized in California in 2016. = 大麻于2016年在加利福尼亚州合法化。
The new law will see many previously illegal activities legalized. = 新法律将使许多以前非法的活动合法化。


疑问句:
Will they legalize euthanasia in the future? = 他们将来会将安乐死合法化吗？
Has the government legalized the use of drones yet? = 政府是否已经将无人机的使用合法化？


否定句:
The government did not legalize that practice. = 政府没有将那种做法合法化。
They haven't yet legalized the new technology. = 他们尚未将这项新技术合法化。
