causativenesses:/ˌkɔːzəˈtɪvnəsɪz/ | n. |致因性；导致性（复数）|The plural form of "causativeness," referring to multiple instances or aspects of the ability or tendency to cause something.  It's a relatively uncommon word used in specialized contexts, particularly in linguistics and philosophy, discussing the nature of causation.  The word highlights the multifaceted nature of causality, implying diverse or multiple causal factors or effects. |例句：The researchers explored the various causativenesses at play in the complex social phenomenon. 研究人员探讨了复杂社会现象中起作用的各种致因性。 The multiple causativenesses of the economic crisis were debated extensively. 经济危机的多种致因性受到了广泛的讨论。|近义词：causalities, causes, effects (in a reversed sense)|反义词：non-causality, chance, accident|名词
