fand:/fænd/| v.|发现，找到|to find something or someone unexpectedly or by chance|例句：I fanned out the papers on the table. 我把纸摊在桌子上。|近义词：discover, unearth, locate|反义词：lose, misplace|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I find my keys every day. = 我每天都找到我的钥匙。
She finds her work challenging. = 她觉得她的工作很有挑战性。

一般过去时:
They found a lost dog yesterday. = 他们昨天找到了一只走失的狗。
He found the answer to the riddle. = 他找到了谜语的答案。

现在完成时:
I have found my lost wallet. = 我找到了我丢失的钱包。
She has found a new job. = 她找到了一份新工作。

过去完成时:
They had found the treasure before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前他们已经找到了宝藏。
He had found the evidence before the trial began. = 在审判开始之前他已经找到了证据。

将来时:
I will find a solution to this problem. = 我会找到解决这个问题的方法。
They will find a way to get there. = 他们会找到到达那里的方法。

虚拟语气:
If I found a million dollars, I would travel the world. = 如果我找到一百万美元，我会周游世界。
I wish I had found a better job. = 我希望我找到一份更好的工作。

被动语态:
The lost child was found safe and sound. = 走失的孩子被安全地找到了。
The solution to the mystery was finally found. = 谜底最终被解开。

疑问句:
Did you find your glasses? = 你找到你的眼镜了吗？
Where did they find the missing painting? = 他们在哪里找到丢失的画作？

否定句:
I couldn't find my phone anywhere. = 我到处都找不到我的手机。
They didn't find any evidence. = 他们没有找到任何证据。


PHRASE->
find out = 发现；查明
find one's way = 找到路
find oneself = 发现自己
find fault with = 吹毛求疵
find it difficult to = 发现...很难
