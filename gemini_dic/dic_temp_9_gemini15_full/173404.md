quasi-eloquent: /ˈkwɑːziˈɛləkwənt/ | adj. | 几乎雄辩的；似雄辩的 |  Approaching eloquence; somewhat eloquent but lacking the full force or polish of true eloquence.  It suggests a style that is impressive but perhaps slightly flawed, lacking the complete mastery of language or persuasive power of truly eloquent speech.  |


例句：

His quasi-eloquent speech, while passionate, lacked the precise phrasing of a true orator. = 他的演讲虽然充满激情，但缺乏真正演说家那种精准的措辞。

The quasi-eloquent plea for mercy moved the judge, though not enough to overturn the sentence. =  这番几乎可以说是雄辩的求饶之词打动了法官，但不足以推翻判决。


近义词：articulate, persuasive, expressive, fluent

反义词：inarticulate, ineloquent, halting, rambling


词性:形容词

词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "as it were," or "almost."  This prefix indicates a resemblance or approximation to something.

* **eloquent**: From Latin *eloquens*, present participle of *eloqui*, meaning "to speak out," "to express oneself."  This stems from *e-* (out) + *loqui* (to speak).
