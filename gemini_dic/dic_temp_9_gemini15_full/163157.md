precommunicated:/ˌpriːkəˈmjuːnɪkeɪtɪd/| adj.|预先沟通的，预先告知的 | Having been communicated beforehand; previously announced or informed.  |例句：The precommunicated agenda allowed the meeting to proceed efficiently.  预先沟通的议程使会议得以高效进行。
The team was well-prepared because the strategy had been precommunicated. 因为策略已经预先告知，团队准备充分。|近义词：preannounced, forewarned, previously informed |反义词：unannounced, unforeseen, unexpected


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company precommunicates its strategies to its employees. = 公司向员工预先沟通其战略。
He precommunicates his intentions clearly. = 他清楚地预先告知他的意图。

一般过去时:
The organizers precommunicated the event schedule to all attendees. = 组织者预先向所有与会者告知了活动日程。
She precommunicated her travel plans to her family. = 她提前告知家人她的旅行计划。

现在完成时:
They have precommunicated the changes in policy to the public. = 他们已向公众预先告知了政策变化。
We have precommunicated our decision to the board. = 我们已向董事会预先告知了我们的决定。


过去完成时:

They had precommunicated their concerns to the management. = 他们已预先向管理层表达了他们的担忧。

虚拟语气:
