preemergencies:/ˌpriːɪˈmɜːrdʒənsiz/| n.| 紧急情况的预兆；即将发生的紧急情况 | Events or situations that foreshadow or indicate an impending emergency.  Preemergencies are warning signs that a larger crisis might soon occur, allowing for proactive measures. | 例句：The unusually high water levels were a preemergency, signaling the potential for a major flood.  异常高的水位是紧急情况的预兆，预示着可能发生大洪水。


The increasing number of small wildfires served as preemergencies for a larger, devastating fire.  越来越多的小型野火是更大规模破坏性火灾的预兆。| 近义词：warning signs, early indicators, precursors, forerunners | 反义词：aftermath, consequences, resolutions
