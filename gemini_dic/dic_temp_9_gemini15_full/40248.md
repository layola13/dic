continent:/ˈkɒntɪnənt/ /ˈkɑːntɪnənt/| n. |大陆，洲 |A large continuous area of land, separated from other land areas by oceans, seas, or other significant geographical features.  Continents are typically characterized by their unique geological formations, climate, and ecosystems. |Example: Asia is the largest continent in the world.  亚洲是世界上最大的洲。

The seven continents are generally considered to be Africa, Antarctica, Asia, Australia/Oceania, Europe, North America, and South America.  这七大洲通常被认为是非洲、南极洲、亚洲、大洋洲/澳洲、欧洲、北美洲和南美洲。


|近义词:mainland|反义词:island, ocean|词性:名词


WORD ORIGIN->
From Old French *continent*, from Latin *continēns*, present participle of *contīnēre* "to hold together," from *com-* "together" + *tenēre* "to hold."  The original meaning referred to a continuous, unbroken expanse of land.
