rekindled:/ˌriˈkɪndl/| vt.|重新点燃；复燃；重温；|To revive or reawaken something, especially a feeling, memory, or relationship.  It implies a previous existence of the thing being rekindled.|例句：The old flame was rekindled when they met again after many years.  多年后再次相遇，旧情复燃。

例句：They rekindled their friendship after a long silence.  他们打破了长时间的沉默，重新燃起了友谊。


近义词：reignite, revive, renew, reawaken|反义词：extinguish, quench, suppress|词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rekindles the fire every morning. = 他每天早上都重新点燃火。
She rekindles old memories by looking at her photos. = 她通过看照片来重温旧日回忆。


一般过去时:
They rekindled their romance after years apart. = 分开多年后，他们重燃爱火。
The news rekindled his hopes for the future. = 这个消息重新燃起了他对未来的希望。


现在完成时:
I have rekindled my passion for painting. = 我重新燃起了我对绘画的热情。
She has rekindled her interest in learning a new language. = 她重新燃起了学习一门外语的兴趣。


过去完成时:
He had rekindled the fire before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，他已经重新燃起了火。
They had rekindled their friendship before the tragedy occurred. = 在悲剧发生之前，他们已经重拾友谊。


将来时:
I will rekindle the fire when it gets cold. = 天冷的时候我会重新生火。
They will rekindle their relationship after resolving their issues. = 解决问题后，他们将重修旧好。


虚拟语气:
If I could rekindle my youth, I would travel the world. = 如果我能重返青春，我就会环游世界。
I wish I could rekindle the passion we once shared. = 我真希望我们能重燃曾经的激情。


被动语态:

Their romance was rekindled by a chance encounter. = 他们的爱情因一次偶然的相遇而重燃。


疑问句:

否定句:
