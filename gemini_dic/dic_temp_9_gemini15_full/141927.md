ordinariness:/ˌɔːrdɪˈnærɪnəs/| n. |平凡；普通；平庸 |The state or quality of being ordinary; lack of distinction or unusualness.  It refers to the quality of being typical, common, or unexceptional.  |例句：The ordinariness of her life was a source of both comfort and discontent.  她的生活平淡无奇，这既让她感到舒适，又让她感到不满。

例句：He found beauty in the ordinariness of everyday life. 他在日常生活的平凡中发现了美。


近义词：commonness, commonplace, banality, mediocrity

反义词：exceptionality, uniqueness, extraordinariness, peculiarity


词源:  Derived from the adjective "ordinary," which comes from the Old French word "ordinarie," meaning "of the rank or order of."  This ultimately traces back to the Latin word "ordinarius," meaning "belonging to an order" or "regular."


用法说明:

ordinariness 通常作不可数名词使用，表示一种状态或性质。它可以用来形容事物、经历或生活方式。


例句：

The ordinariness of the food was disappointing.  (食物的平淡无奇令人失望。)


The ordinariness of their relationship gradually wore them down. (他们关系的平淡无奇逐渐磨损了他们。)


The speaker challenged the notion that ordinariness is synonymous with mediocrity. (演讲者挑战了平凡与平庸同义的观念。)


由于ordinariness本身就表达了缺乏特殊性或与众不同之处，因此它在语法中的运用相对简单。它主要用在陈述句中，很少出现在疑问句或祈使句中。  它可以与形容词或副词搭配使用，以增强表达效果。例如：


The surprising ordinariness of the event. (事件的出乎意料的平凡)


The almost unbearable ordinariness of his daily routine. (他日常生活的几乎令人难以忍受的平凡)


PHRASE->

There are not many common phrases directly using "ordinariness."  The word is more often used in descriptive sentences than in phrasal constructions. However, we can construct examples:


a life of ordinariness (平凡的生活)
the ordinariness of daily life (日常生活的平凡)
escape the ordinariness (逃离平凡)
accept the ordinariness (接受平凡)
embracing the ordinariness (拥抱平凡)


注意：由于ordinariness本身的含义，它很少与其他词语形成固定搭配的短语。  其用法主要在于修饰和描述。
