inveighed:/ɪnˈveɪd/| v. |猛烈抨击；强烈抗议 |To protest strongly or attack someone verbally.  |The senator inveighed against the proposed tax cuts. 参议员强烈反对拟议的减税措施。|近义词：denounce, denounce, rail against, fulminate against |反义词：praise, commend, applaud


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He inveighs against the government's policies. 他猛烈抨击政府的政策。
She inveighs against injustice wherever she finds it.  她发现哪里有不正义就强烈抗议。


一般过去时:
The speaker inveighed against the evils of war. 演讲者强烈谴责战争的罪恶。
They inveighed against the unfair treatment they had received. 他们强烈抗议他们受到的不公平待遇。


现在完成时:
He has inveighed against this issue for years.  多年来他一直强烈反对这个问题。
She has inveighed against corruption throughout her career.  她职业生涯中一直强烈反对腐败。


过去完成时:
They had inveighed against the new rules before the meeting even started.  会议还没开始他们就已强烈反对新规了。
He had inveighed against the company's practices for months before he finally resigned. 他在最终辞职前几个月就一直强烈反对公司的做法。


将来时:
She will inveigh against the decision at the next meeting.  她将在下次会议上强烈反对这个决定。

虚拟语气:
If he were here, he would inveigh against this injustice. 如果他在这儿，他会强烈反对这种不公正行为。
I wish he would inveigh against the corruption more openly. 我希望他能更公开地强烈反对腐败。


被动语态:
The proposed changes were inveighed against by many people.  许多人强烈反对拟议的改变。
His policies were inveighed against by the opposition party.  他的政策遭到反对党的强烈抨击。


疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "inveighed" comes from the Old French word "enveier," meaning "to urge on, incite."  This in turn derives from the Latin word "invectare," meaning "to carry in, bring in," which evolved to mean "to attack or abuse verbally."
