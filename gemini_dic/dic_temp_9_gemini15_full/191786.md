scious: /ˈʃəs/ | adj. | 有意识的；有知觉的 | Possessing consciousness; aware of and responding to one's surroundings.  | The patient regained consciousness after the surgery.  病人手术后恢复了意识。| conscious, aware | unconscious, unaware | 形容词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is conscious of his mistakes. = 他意识到自己的错误。
She is conscious of the time. = 她注意到了时间。


一般过去时:
He was conscious of the danger. = 他意识到了危险。
She was conscious that she was being watched. = 她意识到自己被人监视。


现在完成时:
I have been conscious of a problem for a while. = 我已经意识到这个问题有一段时间了。
They have been conscious of the need for change. = 他们一直意识到需要改变。


过去完成时:
She had been conscious of his presence before he spoke. = 在他说话之前，她就意识到了他的存在。
He had been conscious of his limitations. = 他一直意识到自己的局限性。


将来时:
I will be conscious of my words. = 我会注意我的言辞。
They will be conscious of the impact of their actions. = 他们将会意识到自己行为的影响。


虚拟语气:
If I were conscious of the risk, I would not have done it. = 如果我意识到了风险，我就不会那样做了。
I wish I had been more conscious of the consequences. = 我真希望我当时更清楚地意识到后果。


被动语态:
His mistakes were made conscious to him by his teacher. = 他的老师让他意识到自己的错误。
The need for change was made conscious to the public through the media. = 通过媒体让公众意识到了改变的必要性。


疑问句:
Are you conscious of your surroundings? = 你意识到你周围的环境吗？
Is he conscious of the problem? = 他意识到这个问题吗？


否定句:
He was unconscious of his surroundings. = 他没有意识到周围的环境。
She was unconscious of the danger. = 她没有意识到危险。


PHRASE->
conscious effort = 有意识的努力
conscious decision = 有意识的决定
conscious mind = 意识
highly conscious = 高度警觉的
self-conscious = 害羞的; 自卑的
