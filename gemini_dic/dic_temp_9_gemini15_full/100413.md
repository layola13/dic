intertextuality:/ˌɪntərˌtekstʃuˈæləti/| n. |互文性|The relationship between texts, especially literary texts.  It refers to the way that texts are interwoven with each other, borrowing from, referencing, and responding to other texts. This can be seen in allusions, quotations, parodies, and other forms of textual interaction.  Intertextuality highlights the interconnectedness of literary works and demonstrates that meaning is not inherent in a single text but is constructed through its relationship to other texts. |Example:  The allusions to Shakespeare in many contemporary novels demonstrate intertextuality.  莎士比亚在许多当代小说中的典故体现了互文性。|近义词：textual referencing, cross-referencing|反义词：textual isolation, self-contained text|名词
