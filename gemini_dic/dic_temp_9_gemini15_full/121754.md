mispurchase:/ˌmɪsˈpɜːrtʃəs/| n. |误购，错买|The act of buying something unsuitable, unnecessary, or faulty.  It implies a mistake in judgment or a lack of careful consideration before the purchase.|例句：The mispurchase of that expensive software proved to be a costly mistake.  这套昂贵软件的误购证明是一个代价高昂的错误。

例句：His mispurchase of a second-hand car led to numerous repair bills. 他买了一辆二手车的误购导致了大量的修理费。


近义词：bad buy, wrong purchase, ill-advised purchase | 反义词：wise purchase, good buy, sound investment | 词性：名词
