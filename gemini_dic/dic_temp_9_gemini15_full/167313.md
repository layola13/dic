pretransactions:/ˌpriːtrænzˈækʃənz/| n. |预交易；交易前活动|Pretransactions refer to the activities and processes that occur before a transaction is formally executed.  This can include planning, negotiation, preparation, and other steps leading up to the final transaction.  For example, in a business context, pretransactions might involve market research, contract drafting, and due diligence.|例句：The pretransactions for the merger took several months and involved extensive due diligence.  合并前的准备工作耗时数月，并进行了广泛的尽职调查。|近义词：preparations, preliminaries, groundwork|反义词：post-transactions, aftermath
