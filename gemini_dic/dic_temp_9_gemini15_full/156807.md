pigeonholes: /ˈpɪdʒənhəʊlz/ | n. v. |鸽舍；分类；把…分别归类 |

英文解析:
Noun (n.):  A small compartment in a desk, cabinet, or other piece of furniture, typically one of a series.  Figuratively, it refers to a category or niche into which someone or something is placed.

Verb (v.): To put someone or something into a pigeonhole; to categorize or classify someone or something too rigidly.


例句:
Noun:
The office had rows of pigeonholes for storing files. = 办公室里有一排排的鸽舍用来存放文件。

The journalist was pigeonholed as a political commentator. = 这位记者被归类为政治评论员。


Verb:
Don't pigeonhole him as just a poet; he's also a talented painter. = 不要把他仅仅归类为诗人；他也是个有天赋的画家。

They tried to pigeonhole the new ideas into existing frameworks, but they didn't quite fit. = 他们试图将新的想法归入现有的框架，但它们并不完全合适。


近义词:
Noun: category, compartment, niche, slot
Verb: categorize, classify, typecast, compartmentalize


反义词:
Noun:  (no direct antonyms, but opposites in meaning might include "open space," "broad spectrum")
Verb:  generalize, integrate, unify


词性:
Noun and Verb
