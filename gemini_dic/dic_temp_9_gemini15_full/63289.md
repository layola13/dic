enthronize:/ɪnˈθroʊn/| vt.|加冕；使就职；拥戴|To place or establish (someone) on or as if on a throne; to invest with regal power or authority; to establish firmly in a position of power or authority.|例句：The king was enthroned in a grand ceremony. 国王在盛大的仪式上加冕。|例句：The new CEO was enthroned after a successful campaign. 新任CEO在成功竞选后就职。|近义词：crown, install, inaugurate|反义词：dethrone, oust, depose


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They enthrone a new king every year. = 他们每年都拥戴一位新国王。
The company enthrones innovation as its core value. = 这家公司将创新作为其核心价值。


一般过去时:
The queen was enthroned in 1952. = 女王于1952年加冕。
He was enthroned as the chairman of the board. = 他被拥戴为董事会主席。


现在完成时:
They have enthroned a new leader after the revolution. = 革命后，他们拥戴了一位新的领导人。
The company has enthroned sustainability as a key principle. = 该公司已将可持续性作为一项重要原则。


过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
