mandingos:/mænˈdɪŋɡoʊz/| n. |曼丁哥人；曼丁哥语|A group of West African people, primarily located in Gambia, Senegal, Guinea-Bissau, and Mali, known for their rich cultural heritage and language.  The term can also refer to their language.|例句：The Mandingos are known for their intricate weaving techniques. 曼丁哥人以其复杂的编织技术而闻名。


The Mandingos maintained a strong cultural identity throughout centuries of contact with Europeans. 曼丁哥人在与欧洲人几个世纪的接触中保持了强大的文化认同。|近义词：Mande (broader term)|反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it refers to a specific ethnic group)|名词
