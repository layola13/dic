mold:/moʊld/ /məʊld/| n., v.| 模具；霉菌；塑造；(使)成形 |  A shape or form into which something is cast or molded; a fungus that grows in damp places; to shape or form something. | 例句：The baker used a mold to shape the bread. = 面包师用模具来塑造面包。

The cheese was covered in mold. = 奶酪上长满了霉菌。

近义词：shape, form, mildew, fungus | 反义词：destroy, ruin


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He molds the clay into a beautiful vase. = 他把粘土塑造成一个美丽的瓶子。
The baker molds the dough into loaves. = 面包师把面团塑造成面包。


一般过去时:
She molded the plastic into a toy car. = 她把塑料塑造成一辆玩具车。
The artist molded the sculpture from clay. = 艺术家用粘土塑造了雕塑。


现在完成时:
They have molded the metal into intricate designs. = 他们已将金属铸造成复杂的设计。
The company has molded its products to meet customer demand. = 公司已调整其产品以满足客户需求。


过去完成时:
He had molded the wax before adding the color. = 他在添加颜色之前已经塑造了蜡。
The chef had molded the chocolate before serving it. = 厨师在端上巧克力之前已经把它塑造成型了。

词源:
Old English *mōld, from Proto-Germanic *muldō, from Proto-Indo-European *(g)mel- (“earth, soil”).  The meaning shifted over time to include the sense of "soft earth" and then to the shapes made in earth or other materials, leading to its modern uses.
