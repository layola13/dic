pacer:/ˈpeɪsər/ | n. |起搏器；踱步者；（赛马的）配速马 |A pacer is someone or something that sets the pace, especially in a race. It can refer to a person who walks at a steady pace, a device that regulates heartbeat, or a horse used to set the pace in a race. |例句：He was a steady pacer, maintaining a consistent speed throughout the marathon. 他是一位稳定的配速者，在马拉松比赛中始终保持一致的速度。


例句：The doctor implanted a cardiac pacer to regulate her heartbeat. 医生植入了一个心脏起搏器来调节她的心跳。


近义词：pacemaker, regulator, setter |反义词：dawdler, laggard


词源：源自古法语“pacer”,意为“步伐”，最终来自拉丁语“passus”，意为“步伐，步子”。

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The horse paces steadily around the track. = 这匹马稳步地绕着跑道踱步。
He paces back and forth in his office. = 他在他办公室里来回踱步。


一般过去时:
She paced nervously before the exam. = 考试前她紧张地踱步。
The runner paced himself carefully during the race. = 跑步者在比赛中小心地控制着自己的节奏。


现在完成时:
He has paced the floor for hours, worrying about the test. = 他已经踱步几个小时了，担心考试。
She has paced herself well throughout the competition. = 在整个比赛中，她都很好地控制了自己的节奏。


过去完成时:
He had paced the room for an hour before the phone rang. = 电话响铃前，他已经在房间里踱步了一个小时。
They had paced themselves too quickly in the first half of the race. = 他们在比赛的前半段节奏太快了。


将来时:
I will pace myself during the marathon to avoid exhaustion. = 我将在马拉松比赛中控制自己的节奏，以避免筋疲力尽。
She will pace the room while waiting for the news. = 她将在等待消息时在房间里踱步。


虚拟语气:
If I were to pace myself better, I might have finished the race. = 如果我能够更好地控制节奏，我可能会完成比赛。
I wish I had paced myself more wisely during the marathon. = 我希望我在马拉松比赛中能够更明智地控制自己的节奏。


被动语态:
The race was paced by a leading runner. = 比赛是由一名领先的跑步者来控制节奏的。
The marathon is paced to challenge even the most experienced runners. = 马拉松的节奏是为了挑战即使是最有经验的跑步者。


疑问句:
Does he always pace when he is stressed? = 他在压力大的时候总是踱步吗？
How does she pace herself during long runs? = 她长跑时是如何控制节奏的？


否定句:
He didn't pace himself during the race. = 他在比赛中没有控制好自己的节奏。
She didn't pace the room; she sat quietly. = 她没有在房间里踱步；她静静地坐着。
