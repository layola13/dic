self-injurious: /ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdʒʊəriəs/ | adj. | 自伤的，自我伤害的 |  Describing behavior that intentionally causes harm to oneself.  This term is often used in clinical settings to describe behaviors such as cutting, burning, hitting, or other acts of self-harm.  | Example:  The patient exhibited self-injurious behavior, requiring immediate intervention.  病人表现出自残行为，需要立即干预。


The therapist noted several self-injurious tendencies in the patient's history.  治疗师注意到病人的病史中有一些自残倾向。| Near synonyms: self-harming, self-destructive, masochistic (in some contexts) | Antonyms: self-preserving, self-caring, life-affirming |

词源:
The word "self-injurious" is a compound adjective.  "Self" is a prefix meaning "of or relating to oneself," "injurious" is an adjective derived from the noun "injury," meaning "harmful" or "causing damage."  The combination describes actions that cause harm to oneself.
