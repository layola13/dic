romp:/rɒmp/| /rɑːmp/ | n., v. |嬉戏；喧闹；轻快地跑；轻松获胜 |A boisterous or playful activity; to play boisterously; to move in a lively, energetic way; to win easily.  |例句：The children were having a romp in the park.孩子们在公园里尽情嬉戏。The horse romped to victory. 这匹马轻松获胜。|近义词：frolic, play, gambol, scamper, canter |反义词：crawl, creep, drag


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The children romp in the garden every afternoon. = 孩子们每天下午都在花园里嬉戏。
He romps through his work, finishing it quickly. = 他轻轻松松地完成了工作。


一般过去时:
They romped through the fields, laughing and shouting. = 他们在大田里嬉戏，又笑又叫。
The team romped to victory in the final match. = 这支球队在决赛中轻松获胜。


现在完成时:
The puppies have romped all day and are now fast asleep. = 小狗们玩了一整天，现在睡得很香。
She has romped through her exams, achieving top marks. = 她轻松地通过了考试，取得了最高分。


过去完成时:

The children had romped around the house all day before their parents got home. = 孩子们在父母回家之前一整天都在房子里嬉戏。

虚拟语气:

疑问句:
Do the children often romp in the playground? = 孩子们经常在操场上嬉戏吗？
Did the team romp to victory in the last game? = 这支球队在上一场比赛中轻松获胜了吗？


否定句:

词源:
The word "romp" ultimately derives from the Middle Dutch word "rompen," meaning "to make a noise," "to brawl," or "to roughhouse."  It evolved through Middle English, maintaining its connotations of boisterous play and energetic movement.
