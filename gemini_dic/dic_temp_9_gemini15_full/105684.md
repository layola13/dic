kingtruss: /ˈkɪŋtrʌs/ | n. |桁架桥, 王式桁架 | A king post truss is a type of truss used in architecture and engineering. It's characterized by its simple design, consisting of a vertical post (king post) connecting the top chord (usually a horizontal beam) to the bottom chord (another horizontal beam), with diagonal struts bracing the structure.  It's typically used for relatively short spans and lighter loads. | The old barn was supported by a sturdy king post truss.  旧谷仓由坚固的王式桁架支撑。 The bridge's king truss design proved remarkably stable. 这座桥的王式桁架设计证明非常稳定。 | truss, frame | (no direct antonym, but less stable structures could be considered opposites conceptually) | 名词
