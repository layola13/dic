muddied:/ˈmʌdid/ | adj. | 污浊的；混乱的；含糊不清的 | Covered or mixed with mud; unclear or confused.  |例句：The river was muddied after the heavy rain.  暴雨过后，河水变得浑浊。

例句：The explanation was muddied and difficult to understand. 解释含糊不清，难以理解。

近义词：soiled, dirty, murky, unclear, confused

反义词：clear, clean, lucid, transparent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rain muddies the stream. 雨水使小溪变得浑浊。
This issue muddies the waters of the negotiation. 这个问题使谈判变得复杂。


一般过去时:
The flood muddied the streets.  洪水使街道变得泥泞。
He muddied the waters with his misleading statements. 他用误导性言论使情况变得混乱。


现在完成时:
The recent scandal has muddied the company's reputation. 最近的丑闻玷污了公司的声誉。
Years of neglect have muddied the historical record. 多年的忽视使历史记录变得混乱。


过去完成时:
The evidence had been muddied by the passage of time.  证据随着时间的推移变得模糊不清。
They had muddied the issue with irrelevant details. 他们用无关紧要的细节使问题变得复杂。


将来时:
The upcoming election will muddie the political landscape.  即将到来的选举将使政治局势复杂化。
Further investigation will likely muddie the waters even more.  进一步的调查可能会使情况更加混乱。


虚拟语气:
If you muddied the water, you would have to clean it. 如果你弄脏了水，你必须把它清理干净。
I wish I hadn't muddied the issue with my comments. 我真希望我没有因为我的评论而使问题复杂化。


被动语态:
The stream was muddied by the heavy rain. 小溪被大雨冲刷得浑浊不堪。

疑问句:

否定句:

The witness did not muddie the issue with unnecessary details. 证人没有用不必要的细节使问题复杂化。
