prone:/proʊn/| adj.|倾向于…的；易于…的；俯卧的|Likely to suffer from or be affected by something undesirable; lying flat.  |例句：He is prone to colds. 他容易感冒。|例句：The patient was lying prone on the operating table. 病人俯卧在手术台上。|近义词：susceptible, liable, predisposed|反义词：immune, resistant, upright


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is prone to accidents. = 他容易发生事故。
She is prone to exaggeration. = 她容易夸大其词。

一般过去时:
The area was prone to flooding. = 这个地区容易发生洪水。
He was prone to making rash decisions. = 他以前容易做出草率的决定。

现在完成时:
The company has been prone to financial difficulties. = 这家公司一直容易遇到财务困难。
I have been prone to migraines lately. = 我最近容易偏头痛。

过去完成时:
The bridge had been prone to collapse for years. = 那座桥多年来一直容易坍塌。
He had been prone to anxiety throughout his childhood. = 他童年时期一直容易焦虑。

将来时:
The region will be prone to droughts this summer. = 今年夏天该地区将容易发生干旱。
She will likely be prone to stress in her new job. = 她在这份新工作中可能会容易感到压力。

虚拟语气:
If he were prone to anger, the situation would be worse. = 如果他容易生气，情况会更糟。
I wish I weren't so prone to procrastination. = 我希望我不那么容易拖延。

被动语态:
The system is prone to being hacked. = 该系统容易被黑客攻击。
The building was prone to being damaged by storms. = 这座建筑容易被风暴损坏。

疑问句:
Is he prone to depression? = 他容易抑郁吗？
Are these plants prone to disease? = 这些植物容易生病吗？

否定句:
He is not prone to violence. = 他不容易暴力。
The material is not prone to shrinking. = 这种材料不容易缩水。
