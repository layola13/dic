lionize:/ˈlaɪənaɪz/| vt.| 崇拜；将…视为重要人物；赞扬 | To treat (someone) as a celebrity; to praise highly.  |例句：The town lionized the local hero after his Olympic win.  这个小镇在他奥运夺冠后将他视为英雄般崇拜。

例句：After the publication of his bestselling novel, the author was lionized by the media.  在他畅销小说出版后，这位作家受到了媒体的盛赞。


近义词：celebrate, glorify, honor, idolize


反义词：ignore, neglect, disregard


词性：动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They lionize the successful entrepreneur. = 他们崇拜这位成功的企业家。
The media lionizes famous actors. = 媒体崇拜着著名的演员。


一般过去时:
The city lionized the returning soldiers. = 该城市将回国的士兵视为英雄。
The critics lionized her performance. = 批评家们盛赞她的演出。


现在完成时:
The public has lionized her for years. = 公众多年来一直崇拜她。
The company has lionized its founder's legacy. = 公司一直尊崇其创始人的遗产。


过去完成时:
They had lionized him before his scandal. = 在他丑闻爆发之前，人们就已崇拜他。
The town had lionized the mayor for decades. = 这个镇几十年来一直将市长视为重要人物。


将来时:
They will lionize the Nobel laureate. = 他们将崇拜这位诺贝尔奖获得者。
The press will lionize the new champion. = 媒体将赞扬这位新冠军。


虚拟语气:
If he won the award, they would lionize him. = 如果他获奖，他们就会崇拜他。
I wish they would lionize my work. = 我希望他们能欣赏我的作品。


被动语态:
He was lionized by the crowd. = 他受到了人群的崇拜。
Her achievements were lionized by the academic community. = 她的成就受到了学术界的赞扬。


疑问句:
Will they lionize the new scientist? = 他们会崇拜这位新的科学家吗？
Was he lionized for his bravery? = 他因为勇敢而受到赞扬吗？


否定句:
They did not lionize the unpopular politician. = 他们没有崇拜这位不受欢迎的政治家。
The critics did not lionize his latest film. = 批评家们并没有赞扬他的最新电影。


PHRASE->
be lionized = 受人崇拜
lionize somebody for something = 因某事而受人赞扬
lionize somebody as = 将某人视为…
to be lionized by the press = 受媒体追捧
lionized figure = 受人尊敬的人物
