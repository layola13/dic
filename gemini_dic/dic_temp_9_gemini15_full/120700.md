misaffection:/ˌmɪsæˈfɛkʃən/| n.|厌恶；反感；不喜爱|Lack of affection; dislike; ill will.  It often implies a feeling of resentment or displeasure, going beyond simple indifference.|例句：His misaffection for his stepfather stemmed from years of neglect.  他的继父多年来的忽视导致了他对继父的反感。

例句：The misaffection between the two brothers was evident in their strained conversations.  两兄弟之间的厌恶感在他们紧张的谈话中显而易见。


近义词：dislike, aversion, antipathy, animosity, resentment

反义词：affection, love, fondness, liking


词性:名词


词源:  The word "misaffection" is composed of the prefix "mis-" (meaning wrongly or badly) and the noun "affection" (meaning fondness or liking).  Therefore, misaffection literally means "wrong affection" or "bad affection".  The word's roots trace back to the Old French word "affection" and ultimately to the Latin word "affectus," meaning "a state of being affected or influenced."
