harkener's: /ˈhɑːkənərz/ | n. | 倾听者；注意者 | A harkener is someone who listens attentively or heeds a call or warning.  The word is archaic and rarely used in modern English. It suggests a more deliberate and focused listening than simply "listener".  | Example: The old seer's warnings went unheeded by most, but a few harkeners escaped the coming disaster.  老先知的警告大多数人都没理会，但少数几个倾听者逃过了即将到来的灾难。 | 近义词: listener, hearer, attender | 反义词:ignoramus, inattentive person | 名词
