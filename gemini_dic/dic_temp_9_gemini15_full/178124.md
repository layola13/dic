recognise:/ˈrekəɡnaɪz/ | /ˈrɛkəɡnaɪz/ | vt. | 认出；识别；承认；认可 | To identify someone or something from previous knowledge or experience.  To acknowledge the existence, validity, or legality of something. |例句：I recognised him from his photograph. = 我从照片上认出了他。|例句：The government recognises the need for reform. = 政府认识到改革的必要性。|近义词：identify, acknowledge, appreciate, discern |反义词：misidentify, ignore, disregard | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I recognise her face. = 我认出了她的脸。
They recognise the importance of education. = 他们认识到教育的重要性。

一般过去时:
I recognised my old friend across the street. = 我认出了街对面我的老朋友。
The court recognised the validity of the contract. = 法院承认了合同的有效性。

现在完成时:
I have recognised the pattern in the data. = 我已经识别出数据中的模式。
The company has recognised her outstanding contribution. = 公司已经认可了她杰出的贡献。

过去完成时:
I had recognised his voice before he spoke. = 他还没说话我就认出了他的声音。
They had recognised the danger before it was too late. = 他们早在为时已晚之前就意识到了危险。

将来时:
I will recognise him when I see him. = 我见到他就会认出他。
The government will recognise the new state. = 政府将承认这个新的国家。

虚拟语气:
If I recognised him, I would say hello. = 如果我认出他，我会向他打招呼。
I wish I had recognised the signs earlier. = 我希望我早点认出这些迹象。

被动语态:
He was recognised for his bravery. = 他因勇敢而受到表彰。
The new law was recognised internationally. = 新法律得到国际认可。

疑问句:
Do you recognise this song? = 你认得这首歌吗？
Does the company recognise your qualifications? = 公司认可你的资历吗？

否定句:
I didn't recognise her at first. = 我一开始没认出她。
The government does not recognise that regime. = 政府不承认那个政权。


PHRASE->

词源:
recognise 源自古法语 "reconnoistre"，意为“再次知道”，由拉丁语 "re-" (再次) 和 "cognoscere" (知道) 组合而成。  因此，其核心含义是“再次认出”或“重新认识”。
