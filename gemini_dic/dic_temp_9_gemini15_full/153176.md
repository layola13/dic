pellagra: /pɛˈleɪɡrə/ | n. | 糙皮病 | A severe vitamin deficiency disease, especially of niacin (vitamin B3), characterized by dermatitis, diarrhea, and dementia.  It is caused by a deficiency of niacin (vitamin B3), often due to a diet lacking in protein and tryptophan (an amino acid the body converts to niacin). Symptoms include scaly skin rashes, especially on sun-exposed areas, digestive problems (diarrhea), and neurological problems (dementia). If left untreated, it can be fatal. |  The farmer developed pellagra due to a diet consisting mainly of corn.  这个农民因为主要吃玉米而患上了糙皮病。|  None explicitly; related conditions include other vitamin deficiency diseases. |  None explicitly; good nutrition is the opposite. | noun
