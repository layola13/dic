self-dominions:/ˌsɛlfˈdɒmɪnjənz/| n. | 自治领土；自主领域 |  Refers to territories or areas governed independently, often within a larger political entity.  It emphasizes self-rule and autonomy.  The term often implies a degree of freedom from external control, although ultimate sovereignty might reside elsewhere. |Example: The island nation, despite being part of the commonwealth, enjoyed considerable self-dominions in its internal affairs.  岛国尽管是英联邦的一部分，但在内政方面享有相当大的自治权。|近义词：autonomous regions, self-governing territories, independent states (in a limited sense) |反义词：colonies, protectorates, dependencies |名词
