post-apostolic:/ˌpoʊstəˈpɑːstɑːlɪk/| adj. |使徒时代以后的；使徒继承人的|Post-apostolic refers to the period in the history of early Christianity that followed the deaths of the apostles, generally considered to be from the late 1st century to the mid-2nd century. This era saw the development and dissemination of Christian doctrines and practices, often with varied interpretations and debates.  It was a time of significant growth and expansion of the Christian faith, but also one of internal struggles and external persecution.|The post-apostolic fathers wrote extensively on the developing Christian theology.|近义词：post-apostolic period, early Christian, patristic|反义词：apostolic
