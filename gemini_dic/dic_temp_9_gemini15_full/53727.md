discodactylia:/ˌdɪskoʊdækˈtɪliə/| n. | 并指畸形，分指畸形 | A congenital condition characterized by the fusion or splitting of fingers or toes.  It's a rare developmental anomaly affecting the hands and/or feet, resulting in malformation of digits.  The severity varies greatly, ranging from minor webbing to complete fusion. | Example: The child was born with discodactylia, requiring surgical intervention to separate the fused fingers.  孩子天生患有并指畸形，需要手术来分开融合的指头。|近义词：syndactyly (more common term)|反义词：polydactyly (extra digits)
