parch:/pɑːrtʃ/| vt.|使干枯；烤焦；使焦渴|to make or become dry from heat; to make thirsty|The sun parched the earth.  太阳晒干了土地。|The long hike parched us. 漫长的徒步旅行使我们口干舌燥。|dry, scorch, desiccate|hydrate, moisten|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun parches the land. = 太阳晒干了土地。
He parches the beans before grinding them. = 他在磨碎豆子之前先把它们烤干。

一般过去时:
The drought parched the crops. = 干旱使庄稼干枯了。
The heat parched my throat. = 炎热使我的喉咙干渴。

现在完成时:
The sun has parched the fields. = 太阳已经晒干了田地。
The long journey has parched me. = 漫长的旅程使我口干舌燥。

过去完成时:
The plants had parched before the rain came. = 雨来之前，植物已经干枯了。
They had parched their throats with several bottles of water.  = 他们喝了几瓶水才解渴。

将来时:
The intense heat will parch the land. = 强烈的热量将使土地干旱。
Without water, we will parch in the desert. = 没有水，我们将在沙漠中干渴而死。

虚拟语气:
If it continued this hot, the crops would parch. = 如果持续这么热，庄稼就会干枯。
I wish my throat wouldn't parch so quickly. = 我希望我的喉咙不会这么快就干渴。

被动语态:
The land was parched by the sun. = 土地被太阳晒干了。
His throat was parched by the dry air. = 他的喉咙被干燥的空气弄干了。

疑问句:
Will the intense heat parch the crops? = 强烈的热量会使庄稼干枯吗？
Does the desert climate parch the skin? = 沙漠气候会使皮肤干燥吗？

否定句:
The rain prevented the land from parching. = 雨水阻止了土地干涸。
The regular watering prevented the plants from parching. = 定期浇水防止了植物干枯。
