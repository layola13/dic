pushing:/ˈpʊʃɪŋ/ | v. | 推，推动；力推；催促 |  The act of applying force to something in order to move it away from you.  It can also refer to promoting something or someone forcefully. | 例句：He was pushing the cart uphill. 他正在推着手推车上坡。


例句：She is pushing for a promotion. 她正在争取晋升。


近义词：shoving, propelling, urging, promoting | 反义词：pulling, drawing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pushes the door open. = 他推开门。
They are pushing the project forward. = 他们正在推进这个项目。


一般过去时:
She pushed the button. = 她按下了按钮。
He pushed his way through the crowd. = 他挤过人群。


现在完成时:
They have been pushing for change for years. = 他们多年来一直在争取变革。
She has pushed herself too hard. = 她把自己逼得太紧了。


过去完成时:
He had pushed the table aside before he sat down. = 他坐下之前把桌子推到一边了。
They had been pushing for a solution all week. = 他们整个星期都在努力寻找解决办法。


将来时:
I will push the button when I'm ready. = 我准备好后就会按下按钮。
They will be pushing for higher wages next year. = 明年他们将争取更高的工资。


虚拟语气:
If he pushed harder, he might succeed. = 如果他更努力地推，他可能会成功。
I wish I had pushed myself more in school. = 我真希望我上学时能更努力一些。


被动语态:
The door was pushed open. = 门被推开了。

疑问句:

What are they pushing for? = 他们在争取什么？


否定句:
He didn't push hard enough. = 他用力不够。
They are not pushing the issue. = 他们没有强求这个问题。

词源:
The word "pushing" comes from the Old English word "pūsian," meaning "to push" or "to thrust."  This word has Germanic roots, related to words with similar meanings in other Germanic languages.
