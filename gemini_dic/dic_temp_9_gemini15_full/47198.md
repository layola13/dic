daydreams: /ˈdeɪdriːmz/ | n. | 白日梦，幻想 |  Pleasant thoughts or fantasies that distract one from the present. Daydreams are often associated with a state of relaxed wakefulness and can involve images, scenarios, or desires.  | 例句：She often loses herself in daydreams of traveling the world. 她经常沉迷于环游世界的白日梦中。


例句：His daydreams were interrupted by the ringing of the telephone.  他的白日梦被电话铃声打断了。


近义词：fantasies, reveries, visions | 反义词：reality, actuality


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He daydreams during boring lectures. = 他在上无聊的课时做白日梦。
She daydreams about her future career. = 她幻想她未来的职业。


一般过去时:
I daydreamed about my childhood home yesterday. = 我昨天做梦梦到了我的童年家园。
He daydreamed throughout the entire meeting. = 他在整个会议期间都在做白日梦。


现在完成时:
She has daydreamed about this moment for years. = 她多年来一直梦想着这一刻。
He has daydreamed of becoming a famous writer. = 他一直梦想着成为一名著名的作家。


过去完成时:
I had daydreamed of winning the lottery before I bought the ticket. = 在我买彩票之前，我一直梦想着中彩票。
She had daydreamed about her wedding day for months. = 她几个月来一直梦想着她的婚礼。


将来时:
I will daydream about my vacation all week. = 我整个星期都会幻想我的假期。
They will daydream less once the project begins. = 项目开始后，他们做白日梦的时间会减少。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would daydream more often. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会更频繁地做白日梦。
I wish I could daydream without feeling guilty. = 我希望我可以不做白日梦而不会感到内疚。


被动语态: (很少用被动语态)
His attention was lost in daydreams. = 他的注意力迷失在白日梦中。
(被动语态的例子有限，因为daydream 通常是主动进行的动作。)


疑问句:
Do you often daydream? = 你经常做白日梦吗？
What do you usually daydream about? = 你通常会幻想什么？


否定句:
He doesn't daydream very often. = 他不经常做白日梦。
She didn't daydream during the presentation. = 她在演示过程中没有做白日梦。


PHRASE->
indulge in daydreams = 沉溺于白日梦
lost in daydreams = 迷失在白日梦中
fleeting daydreams = 短暂的白日梦
vivid daydreams = 生动的白日梦
daydreams of grandeur = 奢华的白日梦


词源:
The word "daydream" is a compound word formed from "day" and "dream."  "Day" refers to the daytime, suggesting the activity occurs while awake. "Dream" refers to the imaginative activity of the mind. The word emerged in the late 19th century as a combination of these two existing words.
