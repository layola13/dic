overcareful: /ˌoʊvərˈkerfəl/ | adj. | 过分小心的；过于谨慎的 | Excessively careful; showing too much caution.  |近义词: overly cautious, meticulous, scrupulous | 反义词: careless, reckless, negligent


例句：

He was overcareful in checking his work, and it took him much longer than necessary. 他检查工作时过于谨慎，花费的时间比必要的多得多。

Her overcareful approach to the project actually hindered its progress. 她对这个项目过于谨慎的态度实际上阻碍了它的进展。


词源:

The word "overcareful" is a compound word formed from the prefix "over-" and the adjective "careful".  "Over-" indicates an excess or to a greater degree than is necessary. "Careful" derives from Middle English *carefu(l), from Old English *cærfūl "anxious, worried," from *cæran "to worry, grieve" + *fūl "full."


用法说明：

Overcareful is primarily used as an adjective to describe someone who is excessively cautious or meticulous to the point of hindering progress or efficiency. It often implies that the level of care is unnecessary or counterproductive.  It carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting that the person is being overly cautious and perhaps wasting time or energy on unnecessary details.
