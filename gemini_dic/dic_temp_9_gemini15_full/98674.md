insulations:/ˌɪnsʊˈleɪʃənz/| n. |绝缘层；隔热层；隔音层 | The process or result of insulating something.  Insulations are materials used to prevent the transfer of heat, electricity, or sound. | 例句：The house's excellent insulations kept it warm in winter and cool in summer. 这房子的优良隔热层使它冬天温暖夏天凉爽。

The building's insulations were inadequate, leading to high energy bills. 这栋建筑的隔热层不足，导致能源账单很高。|近义词：isolation, protection |反义词：conduction, transmission |

词源:

The word "insulations" comes from the verb "insulate," which in turn derives from the Latin word "insulare," meaning "to make into an island."  This reflects the function of insulation: to isolate something from its surroundings.
