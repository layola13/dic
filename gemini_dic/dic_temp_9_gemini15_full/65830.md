evinced:/ɪˈvɛnst/| vt.|表明；显示；证明|To reveal the presence of (a quality or feeling); to show clearly.  It implies a clear and often undeniable demonstration.|例句：His trembling hands evinced his nervousness.  他的颤抖的双手表明了他的紧张。

例句：Her smile evinced her happiness.  她的笑容显示了她的快乐。


近义词：showed, demonstrated, revealed, manifested, indicated

反义词：concealed, hid, masked, obscured


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His behavior evinces a lack of respect. = 他的行为表现出缺乏尊重。
The data evinces a clear trend. = 数据显示出一个明显的趋势。

一般过去时:
Her silence evinced her disapproval. = 她的沉默表明了她的反对。
The results evinced the effectiveness of the treatment. = 结果证明了治疗的有效性。

现在完成时:
The evidence has evinced the defendant's guilt. = 证据已经证明了被告的罪行。
His actions have evinced a change in attitude. = 他的行为表明了他的态度转变。

过去完成时:
The preliminary findings had evinced a need for further research. = 初步调查结果表明需要进一步研究。
The earlier symptoms had evinced the onset of the disease. = 早期的症状已经显示出疾病的开始。

将来时:
The upcoming election will evince the public's opinion on the issue. = 即将到来的选举将显示公众对该问题的意见。
Further investigation will evince the truth. = 进一步调查将证明真相。

虚拟语气:
If his actions evinced remorse, he might receive a lighter sentence. = 如果他的行为表现出悔恨，他可能会受到较轻的判决。
If the study had evinced different results, we would have drawn different conclusions. = 如果研究显示不同的结果，我们将得出不同的结论。

被动语态:
His guilt was evinced by the evidence. = 他的罪行由证据所证明。
The effectiveness of the new drug was evinced by the clinical trials. = 新药的有效性已通过临床试验得到证明。

疑问句:
Does his behavior evince any signs of guilt? = 他的行为是否表现出任何内疚的迹象？
What does this data evince about the market? = 这些数据表明了关于市场什么信息？

否定句:
His actions did not evince any sign of remorse. = 他的行为没有表现出任何悔恨的迹象。
The study did not evince a statistically significant difference. = 这项研究没有显示出统计学上的显著差异。


PHRASE->
evince one's gratitude  = 表达感激之情
evince a willingness to = 表现出…的意愿
evince great interest in = 对…表现出极大的兴趣
evince a clear preference for = 表明对…的明显偏好
evince little concern for = 对…不太关心


词源:
From Latin *ēvincō "to overcome, conquer," from *ē- "out" + vincō "conquer."  The sense development is from "overcome" to "prove conclusively" to "show clearly."
