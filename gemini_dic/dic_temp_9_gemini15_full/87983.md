high-mindedness:/ˈhaɪˈmaɪndɪdnəs/| n. |高尚，崇高；高傲；清高|The quality of having or showing lofty ideals; moral excellence.  It can also imply a disdain for the mundane or practical. |例句：His high-mindedness prevented him from compromising with his opponents.  他的高尚阻止了他与对手妥协。

例句：Her actions, though seemingly impractical, stemmed from a deep-seated high-mindedness.  她的行为，虽然看似不切实际，却源于一种根深蒂固的高尚情操。


近义词：noble-mindedness, idealism, magnanimity, elevation

反义词：meanness, pettiness, selfishness, baseness, cynicism


词源：high (high) + minded (having a particular disposition or outlook) + -ness (suffix forming nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality)  The word evolved from the concept of having a "high mind," implying superior moral or intellectual qualities.
