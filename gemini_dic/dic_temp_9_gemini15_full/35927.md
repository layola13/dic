coculture:/ˈkoʊkʌltʃər/ | n. | 共同培养；共培养；混合培养 | The growth of two or more different types of cells or organisms in the same culture medium.  This technique is used in various fields, such as cell biology and microbiology, to study cell-cell interactions, synergistic effects, or competitive interactions between different species.  | Example: The researchers conducted a coculture of human cells and bacteria to investigate the impact of the microbiome on human health.  研究人员对人体细胞和细菌进行了共培养，以研究微生物组对人体健康的影响。 |近义词：mixed culture, dual culture |反义词：monoculture (单一培养)
