cram-down:/kræmˈdaʊn/| vt.|强行推行，强迫接受|To force (something, especially a plan or agreement) on someone, despite their objections.|例句：The judge crammed down the settlement on the reluctant parties.  法官强迫双方接受和解方案。|例句：The company tried to cram down its new policy on its employees, leading to widespread discontent. 公司试图强迫员工接受新政策，导致了广泛的不满。|近义词：impose, force, thrust|反义词：negotiate, compromise, persuade


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They cram down new rules on the students. = 他们强迫学生接受新的规章制度。
The government crams down unpopular policies. = 政府强行推行不受欢迎的政策。

一般过去时:
The court crammed down a harsh sentence. = 法院强行判处了严厉的刑罚。
He crammed down the food quickly. = 他迅速地把食物塞进嘴里。

现在完成时:
They have crammed down the new curriculum. = 他们已经强行推行了新的课程。
The company has crammed down its restructuring plan. = 公司已经强行推行了重组计划。

过去完成时:
The judge had crammed down the verdict before the lawyers could object. = 在律师们提出异议之前，法官已经强行宣判了。
They had crammed down the unpopular decision despite protests. = 尽管有抗议，他们还是强行推行了不受欢迎的决定。

将来时:
They will cram down the new regulations next year. = 他们明年将强行推行新的规章。
The administration will cram down its austerity measures. = 政府将强行推行紧缩措施。

虚拟语气:
If they crammed down the proposal, there would be a riot. = 如果他们强行推行这项提案，就会爆发骚乱。
I wish they hadn't crammed down that unfair decision. = 我真希望他们没有强行推行那个不公平的决定。

被动语态:
The new regulations were crammed down on the public. = 新规章被强行推行给公众。
The unpopular decision was crammed down on the employees. = 不受欢迎的决定被强加给员工。

疑问句:

否定句:
They did not cram down the proposal. = 他们没有强行推行这项提案。
The management didn’t cram down the unpopular changes. = 管理层没有强行推行不受欢迎的改变。
