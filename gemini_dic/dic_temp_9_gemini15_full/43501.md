词源:
The word "cravat" entered English in the 17th century, ultimately derived from the Croatian word "hrvat," meaning "Croatian."  Croatian mercenaries in the French army popularized a particular style of neckcloth, leading to the adoption of the term "cravat" to describe this type of neckwear.
