sipper:/ˈsɪpər/| n. |小口喝的人；吸管；小口啜饮器 |A person who sips; a device for sipping.  A sipper is someone who drinks slowly and delicately, taking small sips. It can also refer to a drinking utensil, often with a straw or spout, designed for sipping beverages. |例句：He is a slow sipper of his tea. 他慢慢地品着茶。 She used a sipper to drink her juice. 她用吸管喝果汁。|近义词：drinker, imbiber |反义词：gulper, quaffing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sips his coffee slowly. = 他慢慢地啜饮咖啡。
She sips water throughout the day. = 她整天都在喝水。

一般过去时:
He sipped his wine thoughtfully. = 他若有所思地啜饮着葡萄酒。
She sipped the tea cautiously. = 她小心翼翼地啜着茶。

现在完成时:
I have sipped all the juice. = 我已经把所有的果汁都喝完了。
She has sipped her drink several times. = 她已经啜饮了几次她的饮料。


过去完成时:
He had sipped his tea before the meeting. = 他在会议前已经喝了茶。
She had sipped the champagne before the toast. = 她在祝酒前已经喝了香槟。

将来时:
I will sip my wine while watching the sunset. = 我将在看日落时啜饮我的葡萄酒。
She will sip the herbal tea before bed. = 她将在睡前喝草药茶。

虚拟语气:
If I had a sipper, I would enjoy my smoothie more. = 如果我有吸管，我会更享受我的冰沙。
I wish I had sipped my coffee more slowly. = 我希望我喝咖啡喝得慢一点。

被动语态:
The juice was sipped delicately by the child. = 果汁被孩子细细地啜饮着。
The drink is being sipped slowly. = 饮料正被慢慢地啜饮着。

疑问句:
Does he sip his tea every morning? = 他每天早上都喝茶吗？
Will she sip the cocktail slowly? = 她会慢慢地啜饮鸡尾酒吗？

否定句:
He doesn't sip his coffee; he gulps it. = 他不啜饮咖啡；他大口喝。
She didn't sip the wine; she poured it down her throat. = 她没有啜饮葡萄酒；她把酒灌了下去。
