patronized:/ˈpeɪtrənaɪzd/| vt.| 惠顾；赞助；以高人一等的态度对待；屈尊俯就 |To be a regular customer of a shop or business.  To treat (someone) as if they were inferior. To support or sponsor.|例句：He patronized the local coffee shop every morning. 他每天早上都惠顾当地的咖啡店。  She felt patronized by his condescending tone. 她觉得他的居高临下的语气让她很受不了。|近义词：support, sponsor, condescend to, talk down to |反义词：ignore, neglect, respect, treat equally


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He patronizes the local bookstore. = 他经常惠顾当地书店。
She patronizes several charities. = 她资助几家慈善机构。


一般过去时:
They patronized that restaurant for years. = 他们在那家餐馆光顾了很多年。
The company patronized the arts. = 这家公司赞助艺术事业。


现在完成时:
I have patronized this cafe since it opened. = 自从这家咖啡馆开业以来，我一直是它的顾客。
She has patronized many artists throughout her career. = 在她的职业生涯中，她资助过许多艺术家。


过去完成时:
They had patronized the same tailor for decades. = 他们几十年来一直都找同一位裁缝。
The museum had been patronized by wealthy donors for years. = 这家博物馆多年来一直受到富有的捐赠者的资助。


将来时:
We will patronize that new restaurant next week. = 我们下周将去那家新餐馆吃饭。
The government will patronize the development of renewable energy. = 政府将资助可再生能源的开发。


虚拟语气:
If I had more money, I would patronize that expensive restaurant. = 如果我有更多钱，我会去那家昂贵的餐馆吃饭。
If they patronized local businesses more, the economy would improve. = 如果他们更多地惠顾当地企业，经济就会好转。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
