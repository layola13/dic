deejaying:/ˈdiːdʒeɪɪŋ/ | n. | DJing, 打碟；放音乐 | The act of playing recorded music for an audience, typically using turntables and a mixing desk to blend tracks together and create a continuous flow of music.  It often involves skilled manipulation of the music, such as beatmatching, scratching, and looping. | Example:  Deejaying is a demanding skill requiring both technical proficiency and musical creativity.  打碟是一项要求很高技巧，需要技术熟练和音乐创造力。

| Example: The party was fantastic; the deejaying was superb. 派对很棒；打碟非常棒。 | 近义词：disc jockeying, mixing music | 反义词：None (no direct antonym) | 词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deejays at several clubs every week. 他每周在几家俱乐部打碟。
She deejays at local events and private parties. 她在当地活动和私人派对上打碟。


一般过去时:

She deejayed at my birthday party last year. 她去年在我的生日派对上打碟。


现在完成时:
He has deejayed at that club for five years. 他在那家俱乐部打碟五年了。
She has deejayed at many different venues. 她已经在许多不同的场所打碟了。


过去完成时:
