mumbles:/ˈmʌmbəlz/| v.|含糊地说，咕哝|To speak indistinctly and quietly, often in a way that is difficult to understand.|He mumbled something about being tired and went to bed. 他含糊地说自己累了就上床睡觉了。|近义词：mutter, murmur, grumble|反义词：shout, articulate

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He mumbles his words. = 他含糊不清地说。
She mumbles when she's nervous. = 她紧张的时候说话含糊不清。

一般过去时:
He mumbled a response. = 他含糊地回答。
She mumbled something under her breath. = 她低声咕哝着什么。

现在完成时:
He has mumbled his way through the entire speech. = 他含糊不清地完成了整个演讲。
She has mumbled the answers to all the questions. = 她含糊地回答了所有问题。

过去完成时:
He had mumbled the instructions before I could understand them. = 我还没听懂他就已经含糊不清地交代了指示。
She had mumbled her apologies before running away. = 她含糊地道歉后就跑开了。

将来时:
He will mumble his way through the presentation. = 他将含糊不清地完成演示。
She will mumble the answers during the exam. = 考试时她会含糊地回答问题。


虚拟语气:
If he mumbled less, people would understand him better. = 如果他不那么含糊其辞，人们就会更好地理解他。
I wish she wouldn't mumble so much. = 我希望她不要那么多含糊不清的话。

被动语态:
His words were mumbled indistinctly. = 他的话含糊不清。
The answer was mumbled so low that nobody heard it. = 回答声音太低，没有人听到。

疑问句:
Was he mumbling something? = 他在咕哝什么吗？
Is she mumbling again? = 她又在含糊不清地说吗？

否定句:
He didn't mumble; he spoke clearly. = 他没有含糊不清地说；他说话很清楚。
She didn't mumble a word. = 她一句话也没说。


PHRASE->
mumble something to oneself = 自言自语
mumble incoherently = 语无伦次地咕哝
mumble an apology = 含糊地道歉
mumble a greeting = 含糊地打招呼
mumble under one's breath = 低声咕哝
