incorporated:/ɪnˈkɔːrpəreɪtɪd/| adj. v. | 合并的；组成公司的；被纳入的 |  To combine or unite a thing with something else already in existence.  To form into a corporation. |


例句：
The small company was incorporated into a larger one. = 这家小公司并入了一家更大的公司。

The newly incorporated company is expanding rapidly. = 这家新成立的公司正在迅速扩张。


近义词：integrated, merged, united, consolidated


反义词：separated, divided, disintegrated


词性：形容词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company incorporates new technologies into its products. = 这家公司在其产品中整合新技术。
They incorporate feedback from customers into their design process. = 他们将客户的反馈纳入他们的设计流程。


一般过去时:
The artist incorporated various styles into his artwork. = 这位艺术家在他的作品中融合了各种风格。
The town incorporated a new park last year. = 去年，小镇新建了一个公园。


现在完成时:
The company has incorporated several smaller firms. = 这家公司已经并购了几家小型公司。
She has incorporated many new ideas into her presentation. = 她已将许多新想法融入她的演示文稿中。


过去完成时:
Before the merger, the company had incorporated several subsidiaries. = 在合并之前，该公司已经并购了几家子公司。
They had incorporated the latest research findings into their report. = 他们已将最新的研究成果纳入他们的报告中。


将来时:
The project will incorporate elements of both traditional and modern design. = 该项目将融合传统和现代设计的元素。

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
Comes from the Latin "incorporatus," the past participle of "incorporare," meaning "to embody," "to make into a body,"  from "in-" (into) + "corpus" (body).  The evolution reflects the sense of combining separate elements into a unified whole.
