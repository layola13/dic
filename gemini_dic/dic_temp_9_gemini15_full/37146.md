come-to-a-head:/ˌkʌm tu ə ˈhed/| v. |达到高潮；到达关键时刻；最终解决|To reach a critical point or a climax; to culminate.  The situation has come to a head.  Things have come to a head.|近义词：culminate, climax, peak, reach a crisis|反义词：begin, start, commence


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The problem comes to a head every spring. = 这个问题每年春天都会达到高潮。
Tensions often come to a head during family gatherings. = 家庭聚会期间，紧张关系往往会达到顶点。


一般过去时:
The argument came to a head during the meeting. = 争论在会议期间达到了高潮。
Their differences came to a head last week. = 他们的分歧在上周达到了顶点。


现在完成时:
The situation has come to a head, and we need to act quickly. = 局势已经到了关键时刻，我们需要迅速采取行动。
The conflict has come to a head, requiring immediate intervention. = 冲突已经达到顶点，需要立即干预。


过去完成时:
By the time the police arrived, the situation had already come to a head. = 等警察到达时，情况已经到了无法收拾的地步。
The dispute had come to a head before the negotiations even began. = 在谈判开始之前，争端就已经达到了白热化。


将来时:
The negotiations are expected to come to a head next week. = 预计谈判将在下周达到高潮。
I think the conflict will come to a head soon. = 我认为冲突很快就会达到顶点。


虚拟语气:
If the situation had come to a head earlier, we might have avoided the crisis. = 如果情况早些达到高潮，我们或许可以避免这场危机。
If only the issue had come to a head sooner, we could have acted more effectively. = 要是问题早点达到高潮就好了，我们可以采取更有效的行动。


被动语态:
The issue was allowed to come to a head before any action was taken. = 在采取任何行动之前，这个问题被允许发展到关键时刻。
The matter was deliberately allowed to come to a head. = 这件事被故意放任发展到无法收拾的地步。


疑问句:
When will the crisis come to a head? = 危机何时会达到高潮？
Has the situation come to a head yet? = 情况已经达到高潮了吗？


否定句:
The issue hasn't come to a head yet. = 这个问题还没有达到高潮。
The situation did not come to a head until much later. = 直到很久以后，情况才达到高潮。


PHRASE->
allow something to come to a head = 放任某事发展到无法收拾的地步
bring something to a head = 使某事达到高潮
let something come to a head = 放任某事发展到顶点
the situation has come to a head = 情况已经到了无法收拾的地步
things have come to a head = 事情已经到了关键时刻
