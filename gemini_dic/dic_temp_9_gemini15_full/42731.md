countess:/ˈkaʊntəs/| n. |女伯爵|A countess is a woman who holds the title of countess by birth or marriage.  This title is usually given to the wife of a count or earl, or a woman who holds the title in her own right. |例句：The countess hosted a lavish ball at her castle. 女伯爵在她的城堡里举办了一个奢华的舞会。

例句：She inherited the title of countess from her mother. 她从母亲那里继承了女伯爵的头衔。


词源:
The word "countess" comes from the Old French word "contesse," which in turn derives from the Latin word "comitissa," the feminine form of "comes," meaning "companion" or "count."  The title originally denoted a woman of high social standing associated with a count.


近义词：noblewoman, lady (informal)


反义词：commoner


词性：名词
