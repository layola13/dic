disillusioning: /ˌdɪsɪˈluʒənɪŋ/ | adj. |令人清醒的；令人幻灭的；使人梦想破灭的 |  causing someone to lose their illusions or beliefs; disappointing.  | The experience was disillusioning, shattering her idealistic view of the world.  经历令人清醒，粉碎了她对世界的理想化看法。 | disappointing, disheartening, disenchanting |  encouraging, inspiring, uplifting |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news is disillusioning to many people. = 这个消息让很多人感到幻灭。
This job is more disillusioning than I expected. = 这份工作比我预期的更令人失望。


一般过去时:
The results of the election were deeply disillusioning. = 大选的结果令人非常失望。
His experience in the city was disillusioning. = 他在城市里的经历令人清醒。


现在完成时:
The past few years have been disillusioning for many young people. = 过去几年对许多年轻人来说都令人幻灭。
Recent events have disillusioned many voters. = 最近发生的事件使许多选民感到幻灭。


过去完成时:

She had been disillusioned by repeated failures. = 她因屡屡失败而感到灰心。

虚拟语气:
