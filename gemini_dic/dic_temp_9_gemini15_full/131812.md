nonconsequences:/ˌnɒnkɒnsɪkwɛnsɪz/ | n. |非后果，不重要的事 |  Things or events that have no significant or lasting impact; trivial matters.  The term often implies a disregard for potential repercussions. |Example:  He treated the warnings as mere nonconsequences, leading to disastrous results. 他把警告当成无关紧要的事，最终导致了灾难性的后果。|近义词：trivialities, insignificances, irrelevancies |反义词：consequences, repercussions, ramifications

USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun, and therefore doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It doesn't have tenses. The usage will vary depending on the sentence structure.

Example 1 (Simple sentence): The project's minor flaws were deemed nonconsequences.  这个项目的轻微缺陷被认为无关紧要。

Example 2 (Complex sentence):  Despite the initial concerns, the committee decided the potential risks were ultimately nonconsequences. 尽管最初有一些担忧，但委员会最终决定潜在风险无关紧要。
