slipstream:/ˈslɪpstriːm/| n. v.|滑流；顺流而行；搭便车|The air current behind a moving object, especially an aircraft or vehicle, which can be used to reduce drag.|例句：The cyclist took advantage of the slipstream to conserve energy.  骑车人利用滑流节省体力。

例句：The smaller boat tried to slipstream the larger vessel. 小船试图利用大船的滑流航行。


近义词：wake, draught, current|反义词：headwind


词源:  The word "slipstream" is a compound word combining "slip" and "stream."  "Slip" here refers to the smooth, gliding movement, and "stream" refers to the current of air or water.  The term originated in aviation, describing the area of reduced air pressure behind a moving aircraft.


用法说明:

名词用法:

一般现在时:
The slipstream provided a significant advantage. 滑流提供了显著的优势。
The race car benefited from the slipstream of the car in front. 赛车受益于前面赛车的滑流。


一般过去时:
The pilot expertly used the slipstream to his advantage. 飞行员熟练地利用滑流为自己争取优势。
The boat found shelter in the slipstream of the larger ship. 小船在大船的滑流中找到了庇护。


动词用法:

一般现在时:
The small plane slipstreams the larger one. 小飞机紧跟在大飞机后面飞行，利用其滑流。
He slipstreams the leading cyclist to conserve energy. 他紧跟在领先的骑车人后面，以节省体力。


一般过去时:
The young athlete slipstreamed the more experienced runner. 年轻运动员紧跟经验丰富的跑步者，利用其滑流。
They carefully slipstreamed the faster boat to avoid excessive exertion. 他们小心地跟在速度更快的船后面，以避免过度用力。


PHRASE->
in the slipstream of:  跟随…的潮流/趋势
to slipstream behind:  紧跟在…后面
to take advantage of the slipstream: 利用滑流
to be in the slipstream:  处于…的顺流中
to cut through the slipstream:  冲破滑流


注意事项:

slipstream作为名词时，主要指空气或水流的滑流，尤其在航空和赛车领域。作为动词时，指利用滑流，通常指车辆或飞机紧跟在另一辆车或飞机后面以减少阻力。
