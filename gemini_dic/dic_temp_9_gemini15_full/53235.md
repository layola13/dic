director:/dəˈrektər/| n. |导演；主管；董事|A director is a person who manages or controls something, especially a company or organization, or a person who directs a film or play.  |例句：Steven Spielberg is a famous film director. 史蒂文·斯皮尔伯格是一位著名的电影导演。|例句：The marketing director is responsible for the company's advertising campaigns.市场总监负责公司的广告宣传活动。|近义词：manager, supervisor, superintendent, administrator |反义词：subordinate, follower


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The director oversees the entire project. = 导演负责整个项目。
She directs the marketing team. = 她指导市场团队。


一般过去时:
He directed a successful film last year. = 他去年执导了一部成功的电影。
The board of directors met yesterday. = 董事会昨天开会了。


现在完成时:
She has directed several award-winning films. = 她执导过几部获奖电影。
The company has appointed a new director of finance. = 公司任命了一位新的财务总监。


过去完成时:
They had already chosen a director before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前已经选好了导演。
The company had appointed a new director before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，公司已经任命了一位新的主管。


将来时:
He will direct his next film in Hollywood. = 他将在好莱坞执导他的下一部电影。
The board of directors will meet next week. = 董事会将在下周开会。


虚拟语气:
If he were the director, he would have done things differently. = 如果他是导演，他会采取不同的做法。
I wish I were a director; I could make my own movies. = 我希望我是一个导演；我可以制作我自己的电影。


被动语态:
The film was directed by a talented newcomer. = 这部电影是由一位有才华的新人导演的。
The company's finances are directed by a very experienced team. = 公司的财务是由一个经验丰富的团队管理的。


疑问句:
Who directed that movie? = 那部电影是谁导演的？
Is she the director of this department? = 她是这个部门的负责人吗？


否定句:
He is not the director of this project. = 他不是这个项目的负责人。
The board of directors did not approve the budget. = 董事会没有批准预算。


PHRASE->
film director 电影导演
managing director 常务董事
executive director 执行董事
artistic director 艺术总监
creative director 创意总监

词源:
The word "director" comes from the Latin word "dirigere," meaning "to direct, to guide, to manage."  It entered English through the Old French word "direteur."  The modern meaning encompassing film and theatre direction developed later.
