parrhesia:/pəˈreɪziə/| n. |坦率；直言不讳；大胆发言|The act of speaking truth to power; frank and fearless expression of one's opinions.  It involves a moral responsibility to speak the truth, even when it is dangerous or unpopular.  It is not simply bluntness or rudeness, but a courageous and ethically driven form of speech.|Example:  The journalist showed parrhesia in her reporting, despite the threats she received.  记者在报道中表现出坦率，尽管她受到了威胁。

|近义词：frankness, candor, outspokenness, forthrightness|反义词：secrecy, reticence, evasion, hypocrisy|

词源:
From Ancient Greek παρρησία (parrēsia), meaning "frankness," "freedom of speech," "boldness."  It combines παρά (para), meaning "beside," "beyond," and ῥῆσις (rhēsis), meaning "speech," "utterance."  The word carries a strong connotation of speaking truth to power, even at personal risk.
