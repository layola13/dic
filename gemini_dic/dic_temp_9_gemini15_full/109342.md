lenin:/ˈlɛnɪn/| n. |列宁|Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov (1870-1924), the leader of the Bolshevik Revolution in Russia and founder of the Soviet state.  He was a key figure in the development of Marxist theory and the establishment of communism in the Soviet Union. His ideas and actions had a profound and lasting impact on the 20th century.|Lenin led the Bolsheviks to victory in the Russian Revolution, establishing the world's first communist state. His theories on revolution and communism influenced many socialist movements worldwide. |近义词：Bolshevik leader, communist revolutionary|反义词：Tsar (in the context of Russian opposition)|名词
