preallocate: /ˌpriːˈæləkeɪt/ | vt. |预先分配，预先配置 | To allocate (resources, memory, etc.) in advance, before they are actually needed.  This often improves performance by avoiding delays associated with allocation at runtime. | 例句：The operating system preallocates memory for each process when it starts. = 操作系统在每个进程启动时预先分配内存。

The program preallocates a large buffer to store the incoming data. = 程序预先分配一个大的缓冲区来存储传入的数据。

近义词：reserve, allocate beforehand, assign in advance | 反义词：deallocate, release
