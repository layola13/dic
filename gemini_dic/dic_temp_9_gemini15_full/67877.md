fabricators:/ˈfæbrɪkeɪtərz/| n.|制造商；伪造者|Those who construct, create, or manufacture something, often from raw materials.  It can also refer to someone who fabricates a lie or story. |例句：Many small fabricators supply parts to the automotive industry. 许多小型制造商为汽车行业提供零部件。|例句：He was accused of fabricating evidence to support his claims. 他被指控伪造证据来支持他的说法。|近义词：manufacturers, producers, constructors, falsifiers|反义词：destroyers, deconstructors


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company fabricates metal components. = 这家公司制造金属部件。
They fabricate custom furniture. = 他们制造定制家具。

一般过去时:
The artisans fabricated intricate jewelry. = 工匠们制作了复杂的珠宝。
She fabricated a story to explain her lateness. = 她编造了一个故事来解释她的迟到。

现在完成时:
We have fabricated several prototypes this year. = 我们今年已经制造了几种原型。
He has fabricated numerous lies over the years. = 他多年来编造了许多谎言。

过去完成时:
They had fabricated the entire structure before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期前已经建造了整个结构。
She had fabricated a convincing alibi before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，她已经编造了一个令人信服的不在场证明。

将来时:
They will fabricate the new bridge next year. = 他们明年将建造这座新桥。
He will fabricate a response for the press conference. = 他将为新闻发布会准备一份答复。

虚拟语气:
If they fabricated the parts themselves, it would be cheaper. = 如果他们自己制造零件，成本会更低。
If she had not fabricated the evidence, she wouldn't be in trouble. = 如果她没有伪造证据，她就不会有麻烦。

被动语态:
The parts are fabricated using advanced technology. = 零件是用先进技术制造的。
A false story was fabricated to discredit him. = 一个虚假的故事被编造出来以败坏他的名誉。

疑问句:

否定句:
