psychogeography:/ˌsaɪkoʊdʒiˈɑːɡrəfi/| n. |心理地理学|The study of the effects of geographical settings, especially in urban areas, on the emotions and behavior of individuals.  It involves exploring the psychological impact of the environment and how it shapes our experiences and perceptions. Psychogeography often involves dérives, unplanned journeys through a city or other environment, to discover and document these effects.|例句：The artist's work is a form of psychogeography, exploring the hidden emotional landscapes of the city.  这位艺术家的作品是一种心理地理学形式，探索城市中隐藏的情感景观。

例句：Many psychogeography practitioners use maps and photography to record their observations and findings. 许多心理地理学实践者使用地图和摄影来记录他们的观察和发现。
