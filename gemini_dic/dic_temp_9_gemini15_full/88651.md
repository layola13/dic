hoarders:/ˈhɔːrdərz/| n.|囤积者，收藏家|People who compulsively collect and store large quantities of items, often to the point of becoming a health or safety hazard.  They struggle to discard possessions, even those of little or no value.|例句：The house was filled with the possessions of hoarders, making it barely habitable.  这栋房子里堆满了囤积者的物品，几乎无法居住。


The documentary showed the lives of several hoarders and their families. 这部纪录片展现了几位囤积者及其家人的生活。|近义词：collectors (in a less negative context), accumulators|反义词：minimalists, declutterers

一般现在时:
Hoarders often accumulate unnecessary items. = 囤积者经常囤积不必要的物品。
He hoards old newspapers and magazines. = 他囤积旧报纸和杂志。


一般过去时:
She hoarded money throughout her life. = 她一生都在囤积钱财。
They hoarded food during the war. = 战争期间他们囤积食物。


现在完成时:
He has hoarded so much stuff that he can barely move. = 他囤积了如此多的东西以至于几乎无法动弹。
They have hoarded supplies for a potential emergency. = 他们已经为潜在的紧急情况囤积了物资。


过去完成时:
They had hoarded all the available resources before the storm. = 暴风雨来临之前他们已经囤积了所有可用的资源。
She had hoarded cash for years, unbeknownst to her family. = 她多年来一直在偷偷囤积现金，她的家人毫不知情。


将来时:
He will hoard his inheritance for his children. = 他会为他的孩子们囤积他的遗产。
They will hoard water if the drought continues. = 如果干旱持续下去，他们将会囤积水。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't hoarded all that money, he wouldn't be so rich. = 如果他没有囤积那么多钱，他就不会这么富有。
I wish I hadn't hoarded so many old clothes. = 我真希望我没有囤积这么多旧衣服。


被动语态:

否定句:
