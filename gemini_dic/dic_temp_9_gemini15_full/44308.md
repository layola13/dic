crooned:/kruːnd/| v.|低吟浅唱，哼唱|to sing or hum in a low, soft voice, especially a song of a sentimental nature. |例句：She crooned a lullaby to her baby. 她低声哼着摇篮曲哄她的孩子睡觉。|例句：The singer crooned a soulful ballad. 歌手低沉地唱着一首深情的歌谣。|近义词：hummed, murmured, whispered, chanted|反义词：shouted, bellowed, screamed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He croons softly to himself. = 他轻声自言自语地哼唱。
She croons beautiful melodies. = 她哼唱着优美的旋律。


一般过去时:
The singer crooned a beautiful song. = 歌手唱了一首优美的歌曲。
He crooned a love song to his girlfriend. = 他对着女朋友哼唱了一首情歌。


现在完成时:
She has crooned that song many times. = 她已经唱过那首歌很多次了。
He has crooned his way into the hearts of millions. = 他凭借他的歌声赢得了数百万人的心。


过去完成时:
She had crooned the song before, but this time it felt different. = 她以前唱过这首歌，但这次感觉不同。
They had crooned the ballad at the wedding. = 他们在婚礼上哼唱过这首歌谣。


将来时:
He will croon a lullaby to his child tonight. = 他今晚将为他的孩子哼唱摇篮曲。
She will croon a song at the karaoke bar. = 她将在卡拉OK酒吧唱歌。


虚拟语气:
If I could sing, I would croon romantic ballads. = 如果我会唱歌，我会哼唱浪漫的歌谣。
I wish I could croon like Frank Sinatra. = 我希望我能像弗兰克·辛纳屈那样唱歌。


被动语态:
The song was crooned beautifully. = 这首歌被优美地演唱了。
The lullaby was crooned softly to the baby. = 摇篮曲被轻声哼唱给婴儿听。


疑问句:
Did he croon the song for you? = 他为你唱那首歌了吗？
Will she croon at the party? = 她会在派对上唱歌吗？


否定句:
He didn't croon the entire song. = 他没有把整首歌都唱完。
She didn't croon loudly; she sang softly. = 她没有大声哼唱；她轻声细语地唱着。


PHRASE->
croon a song = 哼唱一首歌
croon a tune = 哼唱一首曲子
croon a lullaby = 哼唱摇篮曲

croon to someone = 对某人哼唱


词源:
The word "croon" likely originated in the early 20th century, possibly from an imitative origin, representing the sound of a low, murmuring voice.  There isn't a definitive etymological source tracing it to an older word.  Its usage became widespread in the context of jazz and popular music singers.
