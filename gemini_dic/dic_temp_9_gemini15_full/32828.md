choking-off: /ˈtʃoʊkɪŋ ɒf/ | v. | 掐断；扼杀；阻止；使窒息 | To stop something from continuing or developing; to suppress or stifle something.  | The government's new regulations are choking off innovation in the tech sector. 政府的新规正在扼杀科技行业的创新。|近义词：suffocate, stifle, suppress, strangle, impede | 反义词：promote, encourage, stimulate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The drought is choking off the crops. = 干旱正在扼杀庄稼。
He is choking off the conversation. = 他正在打断谈话。


一般过去时:
The sudden storm choked off communication. = 突如其来的暴风雨切断了通讯。
The company choked off funding for the project. = 公司切断了该项目的资金。


现在完成时:
The regulations have choked off competition in the market. = 法规扼杀了市场竞争。
Years of neglect have choked off economic growth. = 多年的忽视扼杀了经济增长。


过去完成时:
The unexpected event had choked off their plans for expansion. = 意想不到的事件打乱了他们的扩张计划。
He had choked off his emotions until he could no longer contain them. = 他一直压抑自己的情绪，直到再也无法控制。


将来时:
The new law will choke off illegal activities. = 新法将扼杀非法活动。
The rising interest rates will choke off investment. = 持续上升的利率将抑制投资。


虚拟语气:
If they had not choked off the funding, the project would have been successful. = 如果他们没有切断资金，项目就会成功。
I wish they hadn't choked off the discussion so abruptly. = 我真希望他们不要如此突然地打断讨论。


被动语态:
Innovation was choked off by excessive regulation. = 过度的监管扼杀了创新。
The project was choked off due to lack of funding. = 该项目由于缺乏资金而被搁置。


疑问句:

Are these new rules choking off free speech? = 这些新规是在扼杀言论自由吗？


否定句:
The company did not choke off investment in research and development. = 公司没有扼杀研发投资。
They didn't choke off the opportunity to negotiate a better deal. = 他们没有扼杀达成更好协议的机会。
