haruspice:/ˈhærəspaɪs/| n. |占卜师，内脏占卜者|A haruspice was a Roman religious official who practiced haruspicy, a form of divination that involved examining the entrails of sacrificed animals to predict the future.  They were highly skilled in interpreting the liver, lungs, and other organs, believing that their condition and appearance revealed divine messages.  Their pronouncements held significant weight in Roman society, influencing political decisions and military campaigns.|The haruspices examined the entrails of the sacrificed animal to predict the outcome of the battle. = 占卜师检查了祭祀动物的内脏，以预测战斗的结果。|augur, soothsayer, diviner|—|名词
