constitute:/ˈkɒnstɪtjuːt/ /ˈkɑːnstɪtuːt/ | vt. |构成，组成；设立，建立；相当于；被认为是 | To form or make up (something) : to be the parts or elements of.  To establish or create (something) by law or agreement. To be considered or regarded as. |

例句：

These houses constitute a village. = 这些房子构成一个村庄。

The committee was constituted in 1990. = 该委员会成立于1990年。


近义词：compose, form, make up, establish, create

反义词：destroy, abolish, dismantle


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The elements constitute the earth. = 元素构成地球。
These actions constitute a crime. = 这些行为构成犯罪。

一般过去时:
The treaty constituted a new era of peace. = 这项条约构成了一个新的和平时代。
The judge constituted a jury. = 法官组建了一个陪审团。

现在完成时:
These events have constituted a turning point in history. = 这些事件已经构成历史上的转折点。
They have constituted a new company. = 他们已经成立了一家新公司。

过去完成时:
The evidence had constituted a strong case against him. = 证据已经构成对他强有力的指控。
Before the meeting, they had constituted a plan. = 会议之前，他们已经制定了一个计划。

将来时:
The new law will constitute a major challenge. = 新法律将构成一项重大挑战。
They will constitute a new government. = 他们将组成一个新的政府。

虚拟语气:
If these actions constituted a crime, he would be arrested. = 如果这些行为构成犯罪，他就会被逮捕。
If the documents constituted sufficient evidence, the case would be closed. = 如果文件构成充分证据，案件就会结案。

被动语态:
The committee was constituted by the president. = 委员会是由总统组建的。
The new rules were constituted to improve safety. = 制定新规是为了提高安全性。

疑问句:
Does this behavior constitute harassment? = 这种行为构成骚扰吗？
What constitutes a fair trial? = 什么才算公平审判？

否定句:
His actions did not constitute a threat. = 他的行为并不构成威胁。
The evidence did not constitute proof of guilt. = 证据不足以证明有罪。

词源:
From Latin *constituere, from con- "together" + statuere "to set, place, establish."  The sense development is from "to set up, establish" to "to compose, make up".
