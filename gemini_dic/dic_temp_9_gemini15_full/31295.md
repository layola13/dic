chaplain:/ˈtʃæplɪn/| n. |牧师，随军牧师|A chaplain is a member of the clergy, especially a Christian minister, attached to a particular institution (such as a hospital, prison, or military unit) or community to provide religious guidance and services.  |The chaplain offered comfort to the grieving family.  随军牧师安慰了悲痛的家庭。|priest, minister, clergyman | secular, non-religious
