crowbarring:/ˈkraʊbɑːrɪŋ/| n. v.|撬动；强行插入；勉强塞入|The act of forcing something in or using excessive force to achieve a result. It often implies a lack of finesse or elegance.  |例句：He crowbarred the window open. 他用撬棍撬开了窗户。  The new policy was crowbarred into the existing system. 新政策被强行塞入现有体系中。|近义词：forcing, jamming, wedging, ramming|反义词：gently inserting, smoothly integrating|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He crowbars the door open. = 他撬开了门。
They crowbar new ideas into the curriculum. = 他们强行把新想法塞进课程大纲里。

一般过去时:
She crowbarred the lid off the jar. = 她撬开了罐子的盖子。
The protesters crowbarred their way into the building. = 抗议者强行闯入了大楼。

现在完成时:
He has crowbarred his way to the top. = 他不择手段地爬到了顶峰。
They have crowbarred the information from unwilling sources. = 他们从不愿透露消息的来源处强行获得了信息。

过去完成时:
She had crowbarred the window open before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，她已经撬开了窗户。
They had crowbarred their way into the meeting before anyone noticed. = 他们在任何人注意到之前就已经强行闯入了会议。

将来时:
He will crowbar the old machine apart to repair it. = 他将会把旧机器撬开进行修理。
They will crowbar their demands into the negotiations. = 他们会在谈判中强行提出他们的要求。

虚拟语气:
If he crowbarred the safe open, he would have stolen the money. = 如果他撬开了保险箱，他就会偷走钱。
I wish I hadn't crowbarred that information into the conversation. = 我真希望我没有把那条信息强行塞进谈话里。

被动语态:
The door was crowbarred open by a thief. = 门被小偷撬开了。
The new regulations were crowbarred into the legislation. = 新规被强行塞进了立法中。

疑问句:
Was the window crowbarred open? = 窗户是被撬开的吗？
How did they crowbar their way through the security system? = 他们是如何突破安全系统的？

否定句:
He didn't crowbar the box open; he used a key. = 他没有撬开箱子；他用的是钥匙。
They didn't crowbar their opinions into the discussion; they presented them respectfully. = 他们没有强行表达他们的意见；他们尊重地提出了意见。


PHRASE->
crowbar something in/into = 强行加入某物
crowbar something open = 撬开某物
crowbar oneself into = 强行挤进
crowbar one's way into = 设法挤进
crowbar a solution = 强行解决
