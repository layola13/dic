palatalism:/ˈpælətəlɪzəm/| n. |颚音化；腭化|The process by which a consonant sound is modified by raising the body of the tongue towards the hard palate.  This often results in a change in the articulation of the consonant, creating a palatalized sound.  For example, the English /j/ sound in "yes" is a palatalized /j/.|The palatalization of /k/ in some dialects of English leads to the pronunciation of "key" as /kiːj/.|近义词：palatalization|反义词：N/A
