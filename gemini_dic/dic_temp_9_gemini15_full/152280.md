patsy:/ˈpætsi/ | n. | 傻瓜；容易受骗的人；替罪羊 | A patsy is someone who is easily tricked or manipulated.  They are often naive, gullible, or unsuspecting.  The term can be used in a derogatory way. | 例句：He was used as a patsy in the robbery. 他在抢劫案中被当作替罪羊。 They made him a patsy for their scheme. 他们把他当作他们计划中的替罪羊。| 近义词：fool, dupe, sucker, victim | 反义词：smart, clever, shrewd


USAGE->

词源:

The origin of "patsy" is uncertain, but it's likely derived from the name "Patsy," a diminutive of "Patrick," possibly associated with the idea of a naive or easily fooled person.  The slang usage likely emerged in the early 20th century.
