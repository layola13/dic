ministryship: /ˈmɪnɪstriʃɪp/ | n. | 部长职位；部长任期；神职；牧师职位 | The state or position of being a minister; the office or term of a minister.  It can also refer to the work or duties of a minister, or the period during which someone holds the office of minister.  In a religious context, it can refer to the office or ministry of a pastor or priest. | 例句：His ministryship was marked by significant reforms. 他的部长任期以重大改革为标志。

The new minister's ministryship is already facing challenges. 新任部长的部长职位已经面临挑战。


近义词：ministry, office, pastorate (in religious context) | 反义词：None directly applicable.  The antonym would depend on the context. For example, if referring to a period of leadership, the opposite might be a period of inactivity or absence of leadership.


词性：名词
