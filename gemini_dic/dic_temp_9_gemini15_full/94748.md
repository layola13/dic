improvisate:/ˈɪmprəvaɪzeɪt/ | vt. |即兴创作；临时应付 |To create and perform something spontaneously or without preparation.  It often involves music, drama, or speaking. |例句：The band decided to improvisate a new song during their concert.乐队决定在音乐会上即兴创作一首新歌。
例句：She had to improvise a solution when her original plan fell through.  当她的最初计划落空时，她不得不临时想出一个解决方案。|近义词：extemporize, ad-lib, improvise |反义词：rehearse, plan, prepare |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He improvises a lot during his stand-up routines. = 他在脱口秀表演中经常即兴发挥。
They improvise new dance moves every week. = 他们每周即兴创作新的舞蹈动作。

一般过去时:
The jazz musicians improvised a beautiful melody. = 爵士乐手即兴创作了一首优美的旋律。
She improvised a speech when the speaker failed to show up. = 当演讲者没有出现时，她即兴发表了演讲。

现在完成时:
The chef has improvised many dishes over the years. = 这位厨师多年来已经即兴创作了许多菜肴。
They have improvised a solution to the problem. = 他们已经为这个问题即兴想出了一个解决方案。

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:
