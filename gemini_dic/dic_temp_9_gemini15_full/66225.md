excites:/ɪkˈsaɪts/ | vt. |使兴奋；使激动 | To cause someone or something to feel excitement or strong emotion.  |例句：The news excites me greatly.  这个消息让我非常兴奋。

例句：The prospect of travel excites her. 旅行的前景让她兴奋不已。


近义词：thrills, stimulates, energizes | 反义词：bores, depresses, discourages | 词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The roller coaster excites the children. = 过山车让孩子们兴奋。
This new technology excites investors. = 这项新技术让投资者兴奋。


一般过去时:
The concert excited the audience. = 音乐会让观众兴奋。
The surprise party excited her immensely. = 惊喜派对让她非常兴奋。


现在完成时:
The news has excited everyone in the family. = 这个消息让全家人都兴奋不已。
His success has excited his friends and family. = 他的成功让他的朋友和家人兴奋。


过去完成时:
I had never been so excited before. = 我以前从未如此兴奋过。
They had already been excited by the rumors. = 他们已经被谣言激起了兴奋。


将来时:
The announcement will excite many people. = 这个公告将使许多人兴奋。
The new game will excite gamers worldwide. = 这款新游戏将让全世界的游戏玩家兴奋。


虚拟语气:
If the news were true, it would excite many people. = 如果这个消息是真的，它会让很多人兴奋。
I wish the trip would excite him more. = 我希望这次旅行能让他更兴奋。


被动语态:
The crowd was excited by the performance. = 观众被表演所吸引而兴奋。
She was excited to hear the good news.  她听到好消息很兴奋。(被动语态，但更常见的表达方式是主动语态)


疑问句:
Does this idea excite you? = 这个主意让你兴奋吗？
What excites you about this project? = 你对这个项目什么地方感到兴奋？


否定句:
The movie didn't excite me at all. = 这部电影一点也不让我兴奋。
The presentation didn't excite the potential investors. = 演示并没有让潜在的投资者兴奋。



PHRASE->
excite someone's interest = 激起某人的兴趣
excite curiosity = 激起好奇心
excite controversy = 引起争议
excite passion = 激起热情
excite the imagination = 激发想象力
