inductee:/ɪnˌdʌkˈtiː/ | n. | 入会者；被吸收者；新成员 | A person who has been inducted into a group or organization, especially one that requires an initiation ceremony.  | Example:  The inductees were honored at a special ceremony.  入会者在一个特别的仪式上受到表彰。 |近义词: recruit, initiate, member | 反义词:  graduate (in the context of leaving an organization)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The inductee receives a certificate. = 入会者收到一张证书。
He is an inductee in the hall of fame. = 他是名人堂的入会者。

一般过去时:
She was an inductee last year. = 她去年是入会者。
They inducted many new inductees last night. = 他们昨晚吸收了许多新成员。


现在完成时:
He has been an inductee for five years. = 他已经成为入会者五年了。
Many talented people have been inducted into the academy. = 许多有才华的人已被吸收进入学院。

过去完成时:
Before the ceremony, he had already been informed that he was an inductee. = 在仪式之前，他已经获知自己是入会者。
They had already inducted all the inductees before the dinner. = 他们在晚宴之前已经吸收了所有的入会者。


将来时:
She will be an inductee next year. = 她明年将成为入会者。
They will induct several new inductees at the upcoming ceremony. = 他们将在即将举行的仪式上吸收几名新成员。

虚拟语气:
If he were an inductee, he would have a special pass. = 如果他是入会者，他将会有一个特殊的通行证。
I wish I were an inductee in that prestigious club. = 我希望我是那个著名俱乐部的入会者。


被动语态:
The inductee was carefully selected. = 入会者经过仔细挑选。
Many candidates were inducted into the society. = 许多候选人被吸收进入协会。

疑问句:
Is he an inductee? = 他是入会者吗？
Were they all inductees? = 他们都是入会者吗？

否定句:
He is not an inductee yet. = 他还不是入会者。
She was not an inductee last year. = 她去年不是入会者。
