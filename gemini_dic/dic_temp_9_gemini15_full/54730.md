disparagement:/dɪˈspærɪdʒmənt/| n. |轻蔑；贬低；诋毁 |The act of speaking about someone or something in a way that shows a lack of respect or approval; belittling or slighting.  |例句：His constant disparagement of her work made her feel undervalued. 他不断贬低她的工作，让她感到自己的价值被低估了。
例句：The disparagement of women in the media is a serious problem. 媒体对女性的贬低是一个严重的问题。|近义词：belittling, denigration, derogation, slighting |反义词：praise, commendation, exaltation |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disparages his opponent's ideas. = 他贬低对手的观点。
They disparage the efforts of the volunteers. = 他们贬低志愿者的努力。


一般过去时:
She disparaged his achievements in the meeting. = 她在会议上贬低了他的成就。
The critic disparaged the new movie. = 批评家贬低了这部新电影。


现在完成时:
He has disparaged her work on several occasions. = 他多次贬低她的工作。
The company has disparaged its competitors' products. = 这家公司贬低了其竞争对手的产品。


过去完成时:
They had disparaged his efforts before he even started. = 他甚至还没开始，他们就已经贬低了他的努力。
She had disparaged the plan before anyone had a chance to explain it. = 在任何人有机会解释之前，她就贬低了这个计划。


将来时:
He will disparage her qualifications in the interview. = 他将在面试中贬低她的资历。
They will disparage the opposition party's policies. = 他们将会贬低反对党的政策。


虚拟语气:
If he were to disparage her again, I would intervene. = 如果他再次贬低她，我会介入。
I wish he hadn't disparaged my efforts. = 我真希望他没有贬低我的努力。


被动语态:
His work was disparaged by the critics. = 他的作品受到了评论家的贬低。
The proposal was disparaged by the committee. = 这个提案受到了委员会的贬低。


疑问句:
Did they disparage your ideas? = 他们贬低了你的想法吗？
Does he often disparage other people's opinions? = 他经常贬低别人的意见吗？


否定句:
They did not disparage her contributions. = 他们没有贬低她的贡献。
He would never disparage someone's hard work. = 他永远不会贬低别人的辛勤工作。


PHRASE->
a campaign of disparagement = 一场诽谤运动
public disparagement = 公开贬低
without disparagement = 并非贬低的意思
show disparagement = 表现出轻蔑
subject to disparagement = 受到贬低

词源:
disparagement源于古法语"desparagier"，意思是“使…失去价值”。  "des-"是加强否定意义的前缀，"parage" 指的是平等，地位，因此字面意思是“使…失去平等的地位”。 经过漫长的演变最终形成了现代英语的disparagement.
