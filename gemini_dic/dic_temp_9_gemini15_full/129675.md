non-departmental: /ˌnɒnˌdɪˈpɑːtmɛntəl/ | adj. | 非部门的，非部属的 |  An adjective describing an organization, body, or public authority that is not part of a government department but works with or for it.  It often implies a degree of independence from direct departmental control, though still ultimately accountable to the government.  | Example:  The agency is a non-departmental public body. 这个机构是一个非部门公共机构。 | 近义词：independent, autonomous | 反义词：departmental


USAGE->
There is no verb conjugation for an adjective.  Adjectives describe nouns.  The usage of "non-departmental" is always modifying a noun.

Example 1: The non-departmental public body held its annual meeting.  这个非部门公共机构举行了年度会议。
Example 2:  Several non-departmental organizations provide vital services.  几个非部门组织提供重要服务。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "non-departmental" as its core element due to its descriptive nature.  Phrases would involve the noun it modifies.  For example:

Example 1: Non-departmental public body funding 非部门公共机构的资金

Example 5:  Structure of non-departmental public bodies 非部门公共机构的结构


词源:

The word is a compound of:

* **non-**:  A prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of".
* **departmental**:  An adjective derived from "department," referring to a division or section of an organization, especially a government one.  "Departmental" itself comes from the Old French word "departemant," meaning "parting," "division," or "separation."
