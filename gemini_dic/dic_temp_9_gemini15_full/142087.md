orient:/ˈɔːriɛnt/ | /ˈɔːriənt/ | vt. n. adj. | 使适应；使朝向；东方；亚洲；定向的 | to adjust or become adjusted to a new situation, environment, or set of circumstances; to position or align something in a particular direction; the East, especially East Asia; characterized by or relating to the East | 例句：The map is oriented north.  地图是北向的。| 例句：He quickly oriented himself to the new job. 他很快适应了新工作。 |近义词：adapt, adjust, align, position | 反义词：disorient, confuse


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She orients herself to the new culture. = 她使自己适应新的文化。
The building is oriented towards the south. = 这栋建筑朝南。

一般过去时:
They oriented the new employees. = 他们使新员工适应环境。
The captain oriented the ship towards the harbor. = 船长将船驶向港口。

现在完成时:
We have oriented ourselves to the changing market. = 我们已经适应了变化的市场。
The company has oriented its products towards younger consumers. = 公司已将产品定位于年轻消费者。

过去完成时:

将来时:
We will orient the new recruits next week. = 我们下周将对新兵进行适应性训练。
The architect will orient the house to maximize sunlight. = 建筑师将设计房屋以最大限度地利用阳光。

虚拟语气:

否定句:
