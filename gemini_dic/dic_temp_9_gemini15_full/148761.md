overturner:/ˌoʊvərˈtɜːrnər/ | n. | 推翻者；颠覆者；倾覆物 | A person or thing that overturns something.  It can refer to a person who causes a system or regime to collapse, or a mechanism that causes something to turn over. | Example: The protesters were considered overturners of the existing government.  抗议者被认为是推翻现有政府的人。 The heavy truck acted as an overturner, tipping the small car onto its side.  重型卡车像一个倾覆物一样，把小汽车弄翻了。 | 近义词：subverter, revolutionary, disruptor | 反义词：supporter, upholder, stabilizer |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The machine overturns the soil efficiently. = 这台机器高效地翻土。
He overturns the established rules frequently. = 他经常推翻既定的规则。


一般过去时:
The revolution overturned the monarchy. = 革命推翻了君主制。
The strong wind overturned the table. = 大风把桌子吹翻了。


现在完成时:
The recent events have overturned public opinion. = 最近发生的事件已经改变了公众舆论。
The scandal has overturned his career. = 这场丑闻毁了他的事业。


过去完成时:
They had overturned the previous agreement before the new one was signed. = 在签署新协议之前，他们已经推翻了之前的协议。
The storm had overturned several boats before the rescue team arrived. = 在救援队到达之前，暴风雨已经弄翻了几艘船。


将来时:
The new policy will overturn the current system. = 新政策将推翻现行制度。
The upcoming election might overturn the results of the last one. = 即将到来的选举可能会推翻上次选举的结果。


虚拟语气:

I wish the government would overturn this unfair law. = 我希望政府能推翻这项不公平的法律。


被动语态:

The car was overturned in the accident. = 汽车在事故中被撞翻了。


疑问句:

否定句:
The argument did not overturn his belief. = 这一论点并没有动摇他的信念。
They failed to overturn the government's decision. = 他们未能推翻政府的决定。
