predetermines:/ˌpriːdɪˈtɜːrmɪnz/|/ˈpriːdɪˈtɜːrmɪnz/| vt. |预先决定；预定；预先安排|To decide or determine something in advance.  Predetermines implies a decision made before the event or situation occurs, often suggesting a lack of flexibility or spontaneity.|The committee predetermines the agenda for each meeting. |委员会预先决定每次会议的议程。


The company predetermines its production schedule for the next quarter. |公司预先确定下个季度的生产计划。


近义词： predetermine, preordain, prearrange, fix, settle


反义词： improvise, leave to chance



USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government predetermines the tax rates. = 政府预先确定税率。
They predetermine the outcome of the election. = 他们预先决定了选举的结果。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

He had predetermined his response to any questions. = 他已经预先确定了对任何问题的回答。
