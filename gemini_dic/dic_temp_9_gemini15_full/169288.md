promised:/ˈprɒmɪst/ /ˈprɑːmɪst/| v.|承诺，允诺|to make a statement that one will definitely do something or that something will definitely happen|He promised to help me with my homework. 他答应帮我做作业。
She promised me a new dress for my birthday. 她答应我生日送我一件新裙子。|pledged, vowed, guaranteed|broken, unfulfilled|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I promise to call you later. = 我保证稍后给你打电话。
He promises to be there on time. = 他保证会准时到。

一般过去时:
She promised to help me. = 她答应帮助我。
They promised to return the book. = 他们答应归还这本书。

现在完成时:
I have promised to donate to the charity. = 我已经承诺向慈善机构捐款。
She has promised to keep my secret. = 她已承诺保守我的秘密。

过去完成时:
He had promised to finish the project by Friday. = 他已承诺在周五之前完成项目。
They had promised to meet us at the station. = 他们已答应在车站与我们会面。

将来时:
I will promise to do my best. = 我将承诺尽我所能。
She will promise to support the cause. = 她将承诺支持这项事业。


虚拟语气:
I wish he had promised to come. = 我真希望他答应来。
If he had promised to help, I wouldn't be in this situation. = 如果他答应帮忙，我不会陷入这种境地。

被动语态:
It was promised that the project would be completed on time. = 项目被承诺会准时完成。
A reward was promised to whoever solved the mystery. = 任何解开谜团的人都会得到奖励。

疑问句:
Did he promise to attend the meeting? = 他承诺参加会议了吗？
Has she promised to pay you back? = 她答应还钱了吗？


否定句:
He didn't promise to help. = 他没有答应帮忙。
She promised not to tell anyone. = 她承诺不告诉任何人。


PHRASE->
break a promise = 违背诺言

a solemn promise = 庄严的承诺
