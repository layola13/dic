earthworks:/ˈɜːθwɜːks/ | n. | 土方工程；夯土建筑 | Large-scale movements of earth, typically for construction or landscaping purposes.  Earthworks can include embankments, excavations, and other ground-shaping activities.  They are often associated with ancient civilizations and their monumental structures. | Example: The ancient Egyptians were masters of earthworks, creating massive pyramids and temples.  古代埃及人是土方工程的大师，建造了巨大的金字塔和寺庙。


The construction of the new highway involved extensive earthworks. 新高速公路的建设涉及大量的土方工程。|近义词：groundworks, excavations, embankments |反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "earthworks" is a compound word formed from "earth" and "works".  "Earth" refers to the ground or soil, while "works" refers to structures or constructions.  The term dates back to at least the 18th century, reflecting the increasing scale and complexity of engineered land modifications.
