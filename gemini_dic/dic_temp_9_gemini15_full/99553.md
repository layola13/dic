interhalogen:/ˌɪntərˈhælədʒən/| n. |互卤化物|A binary compound between two different halogen elements.  Interhalogens are typically formed between a less electronegative halogen and a more electronegative halogen. They exhibit a variety of structures and properties depending on the halogens involved.  For example, chlorine trifluoride (ClF3) and iodine monochloride (ICl) are both interhalogens.|Example: Iodine monochloride (ICl) is a common example of an interhalogen used in chemical synthesis.|近义词: halogen compound |反义词:  None specifically, but could be contrasted with homonuclear diatomic halogens (like Cl2).|名词
