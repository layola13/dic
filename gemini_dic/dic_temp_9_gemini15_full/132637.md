nondisclosure: /ˌnɒn.dɪˈskloʊʒər/ | n. |保密；不公开；隐瞒 |The act of not revealing something; keeping something secret.  It often refers to a legal agreement or obligation. |例句：The nondisclosure agreement prevented him from talking about the project. 保密协议阻止了他谈论这个项目。
The company enforced strict nondisclosure on its new product. 公司对其新产品实施了严格的保密措施。 |近义词：secrecy, confidentiality, concealment |反义词：disclosure, revelation |名词

词源:
The word "nondisclosure" is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") + "disclosure" (the act of revealing something).  "Disclosure" itself comes from the Old French "desclore" meaning "to uncover" or "to reveal," ultimately deriving from Latin "discludere" with the same meaning.
