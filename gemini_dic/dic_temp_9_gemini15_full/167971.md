prioritizer: /ˈpraɪɔːrɪtaɪzər/ | n. | 优先考虑者，优先级设定者 | A prioritizer is someone who determines the order of importance of tasks or items.  They are skilled at identifying and focusing on the most crucial things. | Example:  She's a skilled prioritizer, always managing to complete the most important tasks first.  她是一位熟练的优先级设定者，总是能够先完成最重要的任务。 |近义词: organizer, planner, scheduler | 反义词: procrastinator, dawdler


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project manager prioritizes tasks based on deadlines. = 项目经理根据截止日期安排任务的优先级。
He prioritizes his family above all else. = 他把家人放在首位。


一般过去时:
She prioritized her studies over her social life. = 她把学习放在社交生活之上。
The team prioritized fixing the bug over adding new features. = 团队优先修复错误而不是添加新功能。


现在完成时:
They have prioritized efficiency in their new workflow. = 他们在新的工作流程中优先考虑效率。
She has prioritized her health since her illness. = 自从生病以来，她一直把健康放在首位。


过去完成时:
He had prioritized his career before his family, and now regrets it. = 他以前把事业放在家庭之前，现在后悔了。
The company had prioritized profit over environmental concerns. = 公司以前把利润放在环境问题之上。


将来时:
We will prioritize customer satisfaction in the coming year. = 我们将在来年优先考虑客户满意度。
They will prioritize research and development in their new strategy. = 他们将在新的战略中优先考虑研发。


虚拟语气:
If I were a prioritizer, I would finish the most important task first. = 如果我是个优先级设定者，我会先完成最重要的任务。
I wish I had prioritized my time better last semester. = 我希望上学期能更好地安排时间。


被动语态:
Tasks are prioritized by the project manager. = 任务由项目经理安排优先级。
Customer needs are prioritized in our business. = 在我们的业务中优先考虑客户需求。


疑问句:
How do you prioritize your tasks? = 你如何安排任务的优先级？
What factors do you consider when prioritizing projects? = 在安排项目优先级时，你考虑哪些因素？


否定句:
He doesn't prioritize his health enough. = 他不够重视自己的健康。
The company didn't prioritize employee well-being. = 公司没有优先考虑员工的福祉。
