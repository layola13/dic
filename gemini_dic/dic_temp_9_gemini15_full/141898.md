ordered:/ˈɔːdəd/ | adj. | 有序的；整齐的；被命令的 | Arranged or placed in a particular order;  given as a command or instruction. |例句：The books were ordered alphabetically on the shelves. 书籍按字母顺序排列在书架上。

The soldiers obeyed the ordered retreat. 士兵们服从了撤退命令。|近义词：arranged, systematic, methodical, commanded |反义词：chaotic, disordered, random, disorganized |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The items are ordered by size. = 这些物品按大小排列。
The manager ordered a new report. = 经理下令撰写一份新的报告。


一般过去时:
The students ordered their supplies. = 学生们整理好了他们的学习用品。
My boss ordered me to leave early. = 我的老板命令我早点离开。


现在完成时:
We have ordered the new furniture. = 我们已经订购了新的家具。
The court has ordered a new trial. = 法庭已下令进行新的审判。


过去完成时:
They had ordered the cake before the party started. = 他们在派对开始之前就订好了蛋糕。
The general had ordered the troops to advance. = 将军已经下令部队前进。


将来时:
I will order a pizza for dinner. = 我将点一份披萨作为晚餐。
She will order the flowers tomorrow. = 她明天将订购鲜花。


虚拟语气:
If I had ordered the tickets earlier, we wouldn't have missed the show. = 如果我早点订票，我们就不会错过演出。
I wish I had ordered more food. = 我真希望我多点了些食物。


被动语态:
The books were ordered by author. = 书籍按作者顺序排列。
The meeting was ordered to be adjourned. = 会议被命令休会。


疑问句:
Are the files ordered correctly? = 文件是否按正确的顺序排列？
Were the troops ordered to attack? = 部队是否接到进攻命令？


否定句:
The documents were not ordered chronologically. = 文件没有按时间顺序排列。
He did not order the necessary supplies. = 他没有订购必要的用品。
