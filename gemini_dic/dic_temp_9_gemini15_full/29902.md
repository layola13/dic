caution:/ˈkɔːʃən/| n. v.|谨慎；警告；小心 |Careful attention to possible danger or trouble; a warning; to warn someone. |例句：He urged caution in interpreting the results. 他告诫大家在解读结果时要谨慎。

例句：The police issued a caution to the driver. 警方对司机发出了警告。


近义词：care, heed, warning,  attention |反义词：recklessness, negligence, carelessness |词性: noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cautions against hasty decisions. = 他告诫不要草率做决定。
She always cautions her children about strangers. = 她总是告诫孩子们要小心陌生人。


一般过去时:
The teacher cautioned the students about the dangers of fire. = 老师告诫学生们火灾的危险。
He cautioned me to be careful. = 他告诫我要小心。


现在完成时:
They have cautioned us repeatedly about the risks. = 他们反复警告过我们风险。
She has cautioned him against drinking too much. = 她告诫他不要喝太多酒。


过去完成时:
I had cautioned him about the slippery road, but he didn't listen. = 我已经警告过他路滑，但他没听。
She had cautioned me against trusting him. = 她曾警告我不要相信他。


将来时:
I will caution him before he goes. = 他走之前我会警告他。
They will caution the public about the storm. = 他们将警告公众暴风雨来临。


虚拟语气:
I would caution you to think before you act. = 我要提醒你三思而后行。
If I were you, I would caution against making such a decision. = 如果我是你，我会谨慎考虑是否做这个决定。


被动语态:
We were cautioned about the dangers of the experiment. = 我们被警告了实验的危险。
He was cautioned by the police for speeding. = 他因超速驾驶而受到警方的警告。


疑问句:
Did he caution you about anything? = 他有没有警告你任何事？
Should we caution the children about the dog? = 我们应该警告孩子们小心那条狗吗？


否定句:
He did not caution me about the risks. = 他没有警告我风险。
They didn't caution him enough about the danger. = 他们没有充分警告他危险。


PHRASE->
exercise caution = 谨慎行事
a word of caution = 一句忠告
cautionary tale = 警示故事
with caution = 小心地
without caution = 鲁莽地
