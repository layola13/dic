re-boarding:/ˌriːˈbɔːrdɪŋ/ | v. | 重新登机；再次登机 | The act of getting back onto an aircraft, typically after a delay or disruption.  | Example: After a two-hour delay, passengers were finally allowed to re-board the plane.  重新登机两小时后，乘客们终于获准重新登机。 |近义词：re-embarking |反义词：disembarking


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They re-board the plane after the delay. = 延误后他们重新登机。
Passengers re-board frequently during long flights. = 在长途航班中，乘客经常重新登机。


一般过去时:
We re-boarded the flight after the emergency landing. = 紧急着陆后我们重新登机。
The passengers re-boarded the plane after the security check. = 安检后乘客重新登机。


现在完成时:
They have re-boarded the aircraft. = 他们已经重新登机了。
The airline has re-boarded all passengers. = 航空公司已经让所有乘客重新登机了。


过去完成时:
The captain had already announced that passengers could re-board. = 机长已经宣布乘客可以重新登机了。
We had re-boarded before the storm hit. = 在暴风雨来临之前，我们已经重新登机了。


将来时:
They will re-board the plane in ten minutes. = 他们十分钟后将重新登机。
The passengers will re-board once the problem is solved. = 一旦问题解决，乘客们就会重新登机。


虚拟语气:
If the weather improved, we would re-board immediately. = 如果天气好转，我们将立即重新登机。
I wish we had re-boarded sooner. = 我希望我们早点重新登机。


被动语态:
The passengers were re-boarded after the mechanical issue was fixed. = 机械故障修复后，乘客被重新安排登机。
The plane was re-boarded quickly and efficiently. = 飞机迅速而有效地重新登机。


疑问句:
Will we be able to re-board the flight? = 我们能够重新登机吗？
When will the passengers re-board the aircraft? = 乘客们什么时候重新登机？


否定句:
They did not re-board the plane until the fault was repaired. = 直到故障修复后，他们才重新登机。
The passengers were not allowed to re-board until the investigation was completed. = 直到调查结束，乘客才被允许重新登机。
