preraphaelism:/ˌpriːræˈfeɪlɪzəm/| n. |前拉斐尔主义|A 19th-century British art movement that rejected the academic art of the time and sought inspiration in the art of the period before Raphael (roughly 1300-1520).  The Pre-Raphaelites emphasized a more realistic depiction of nature, intense emotion, and moral and spiritual themes.  Their work often featured bright, symbolic colors, and detailed depictions of nature and human figures.|The Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood's paintings often featured idealized women and lush natural settings.|近义词：Pre-Raphaelitism|反义词：Academic art (of the time)|名词
