cenotaph:/ˈsɛnəˌtæf/| n. |空冢，纪念碑 |A cenotaph is a monument erected in memory of a person or group of people whose remains are elsewhere.  It is often a symbolic tomb or memorial. |例句：The cenotaph in the town square commemorates the soldiers who died in the war. 镇中心广场上的空冢是为了纪念在战争中牺牲的士兵。|例句：A grand cenotaph was built to honor the victims of the disaster.一座宏伟的纪念碑被建造来纪念灾难的受害者。|近义词：monument, memorial, tomb |反义词：grave (containing remains)
