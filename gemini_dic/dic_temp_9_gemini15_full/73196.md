force-feeding: /fɔːrsˈfiːdɪŋ/ | n. | 强迫喂食 | The act of feeding someone or something against their will.  It is often associated with situations of cruelty, neglect, or medical necessity (though ethically debated in the latter). | Example: The activists protested against the force-feeding of prisoners on hunger strike.  = 激进分子抗议对绝食囚犯进行强迫喂食。 The doctor ordered force-feeding for the patient who was refusing to eat due to their illness. = 医生下令对因病拒绝进食的病人进行强迫喂食。 | 近义词：compulsory feeding, involuntary feeding | 反义词：voluntary feeding, self-feeding | 名词
