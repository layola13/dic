holophrastic:/ˌhɒləˈfræstɪk/| adj.| 单词句的，一个词表达完整句意的 |  Relating to or denoting a stage of language development in which a single word conveys a complex meaning that would require a whole phrase or sentence in a more mature language.  It's characteristic of early child language. | Example: A child saying "milk" to express the desire for a glass of milk. |近义词：one-word sentence, single-word utterance|反义词：multi-word, complex sentence


USAGE->
There is no verb conjugation for the adjective "holophrastic".  The word itself describes a linguistic phenomenon, not an action. Therefore, grammatical tenses like past, present, future, etc., are not applicable.  The usage is primarily descriptive.

Example 1: The child's speech was largely holophrastic, with single words conveying complex meanings.  孩子的语言主要是一词多义的，一个词表达复杂的意思。

Example 2:  Researchers observed holophrastic utterances in the early stages of language acquisition. 研究人员在语言习得的早期阶段观察到了一词多义的表达。
