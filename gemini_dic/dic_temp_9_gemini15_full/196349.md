seriffed:/ˈsɛrɪft/| adj.|衬线(字体)的|Having serifs, small decorative strokes at the ends of letterforms.  Serif fonts are traditionally considered more readable in large blocks of text.|例句：Times New Roman is a seriffed typeface.  Times New Roman 是一种衬线字体。

例句：The poster used a seriffed font for the title. 海报标题使用了衬线字体。


近义词：with serifs|反义词：sans-serif


词源：From "serif," ultimately from the Latin word "seriphus," meaning "a small piece of wood."  The term likely originated from the small decorative strokes resembling small pieces of wood added to the ends of letters in early printing.
