libelling:/ˈlɪbəlɪŋ/ | v. |诽谤；中伤 |The act of making a false and damaging statement about someone.  It involves written or printed defamation. |例句：He was arrested for libelling his former employer. 他因诽谤他的前雇主而被捕。

例句：The newspaper was sued for libelling the politician.  这家报纸因诽谤这位政治家而被起诉。


近义词：slandering, defaming, calumniating, vilifying

反义词：praising, complimenting, extolling


词性：动词 (verb)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is libelling his neighbor online. = 他正在网上诽谤他的邻居。
They libel people regularly in their blog. = 他们在博客上经常诽谤他人。


一般过去时:
She libelled the company in her book. = 她在她书中诽谤了那家公司。
The journalist libelled the mayor in his article. = 这名记者在他的文章中诽谤了市长。


现在完成时:
He has libelled many people throughout his career. = 他在他的职业生涯中诽谤过很多人。
They have libelled the organization repeatedly. = 他们一再诽谤该组织。


过去完成时:
She had libelled him before he even knew her. = 在他认识她之前，她就诽谤过他了。
The newspaper had libelled the actress in several previous articles. = 这家报纸之前在几篇文章中诽谤过这位女演员。


将来时:
He will libel her again if given the chance. = 如果有机会，他会再次诽谤她。
They will libel the company to damage its reputation. = 他们会诽谤这家公司以损害其声誉。


虚拟语气:
If he libelled her, he would face legal consequences. = 如果他诽谤了她，他将面临法律后果。
I wish they hadn't libelled my family. = 我真希望他们没有诽谤我的家人。


被动语态:
He was libelled in a national newspaper. = 他在一个全国性报纸上被诽谤了。
The company was libelled by a rival firm. = 这家公司被一家竞争对手公司诽谤了。


疑问句:
Was he libelled by the article? = 他被这篇文章诽谤了吗？
Were they libelled in the comments section? = 他们在评论区被诽谤了吗？


否定句:
He did not libel her, despite the rumors. = 尽管有谣言，但他并没有诽谤她。
They did not libel the politician intentionally. = 他们并非故意诽谤这位政治家。


PHRASE->
be libelled for = 因...而被诽谤
a libel suit = 诽谤诉讼
to libel someone = 诽谤某人
action for libel = 诽谤诉讼
a case of libel = 一宗诽谤案


词源:
From the Old French word *libel*, meaning "a little book," ultimately from the Latin word *libellus*, a diminutive of *liber*, meaning "book."  The sense shifted from a written document to a defamatory written document.
