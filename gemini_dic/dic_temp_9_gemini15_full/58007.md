driving:/ˈdraɪvɪŋ/| v. n. adj.|驾驶；驱使；动力；强烈的|the act of operating a motor vehicle; a strong motivating force; compelling; forceful|例句：He enjoys driving his new car. 他喜欢开他的新车。
例句：The driving force behind the project was her determination.  这个项目背后的驱动力是她坚定的决心。

近义词：operating, propelling, compelling, forceful|反义词：stopping, halting, passive, weak|词性:verb, noun, adjective


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drives to work every day. = 他每天开车上班。
They are driving across the country. = 他们正在驾车横穿全国。

一般过去时:
She drove to the airport yesterday. = 她昨天开车去了机场。
We drove for hours without stopping. = 我们开了几个小时的车，没有停下来。

现在完成时:
I have driven this car for five years. = 我已经开了这辆车五年了。
They have driven over 1,000 miles. = 他们已经开了1000多英里。

过去完成时:
He had driven for hours before he finally stopped. = 他开了几个小时的车才终于停下来。
We had driven all night to get there on time. = 我们开了一整夜的车才能准时到达那里。

将来时:
She will drive to the meeting tomorrow. = 她明天将开车去参加会议。
They will be driving a new car next year. = 他们明年将开一辆新车。

虚拟语气:
If I had a car, I would drive to the beach. = 如果我有一辆车，我就会开车去海滩。
I wish I could drive a sports car. = 我真希望我能开一辆跑车。

被动语态:
The car was driven by a young woman. = 这辆车是由一位年轻女子开的。
The truck was being driven recklessly. = 那辆卡车正在鲁莽驾驶。

疑问句:
Do you drive? = 你会开车吗？
Where are they driving? = 他们开车去哪里？

否定句:
He doesn't drive. = 他不会开车。
She didn't drive to the party. = 她没有开车去参加聚会。

词源:
The word "driving" originates from the Old English verb "drīfan," meaning "to drive, push, impel."  This verb has Germanic roots and is related to other words in various Germanic languages with similar meanings.  Over time, its usage expanded to encompass the act of operating vehicles.
