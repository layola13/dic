ironies:/ˈaɪrəniːz/ | n. | 反语；具有讽刺意味的情况 |  The plural form of "irony," referring to instances of irony.  It describes situations, statements, or events that are contrary to what is expected.  Ironies can be situational, verbal, or dramatic. |例句：The ironies of life often leave us speechless.  生活的讽刺之处常常让我们说不出话来。  The play is full of dramatic ironies that keep the audience on the edge of their seats. 这部戏充满了戏剧性的反讽，让观众始终屏息以待。|近义词：paradoxes, contradictions, incongruities |反义词： congruities, consistencies |名词


USAGE->
This word is a noun and does not have verb conjugations.  Therefore, tenses like past, present, future, etc., are not applicable.  It can, however, be used in various sentence structures.

Example sentences demonstrating different usages:

Simple sentence:  The ironies in his speech were quite subtle. 他演讲中的讽刺意味相当微妙。

Complex sentence:  Because of the many ironies surrounding the situation, the investigation was prolonged. 由于情况中存在许多具有讽刺意味的情况，调查被延长了。

Sentence with a prepositional phrase: The report highlighted several key ironies in the data. 报告强调了数据中的几个关键性反讽之处。


PHRASE->
There aren't common phrases specifically using "ironies" as a core component.  The word is typically used independently to describe multiple instances of irony.  Instead, phrases use the singular "irony":

irony of fate:  命运的讽刺
the cruel irony:  残酷的讽刺
a twist of irony:  出人意料的反转（讽刺）
black irony: 黑色幽默
dramatic irony: 戏剧性反讽


The word "ironies" itself doesn't readily lend itself to phrasal constructions in the same way that words like "what" do.  It functions primarily as a noun describing multiple instances of ironic situations or statements.
