sibilance:/ˈsɪbɪləns/| n. |咝音，摩擦音 |The sound produced by the friction of breath in the articulation of certain consonants, such as /s/, /z/, /ʃ/, /ʒ/, /tʃ/, and /dʒ/.  It is often used in poetry and literature to create a specific effect or mood.|例句：The poem's sibilance created a sense of mystery. 这首诗的咝音营造了一种神秘感。The sibilance of the snake's hiss was unnerving. 蛇嘶嘶作响的咝音令人不安。|近义词：hissing, whispering|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with plosives or vowels.|名词
