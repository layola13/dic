/ˈdɜːrli/|n.|达利（姓氏）|Durley is a surname of English origin.  It is believed to be a locational name from places called Durley in Hampshire and in Dorset. These places derive their names from the Old English elements "durh" meaning "thoroughfare" or "passage" and "lēah" meaning "woodland clearing". Therefore, Durley originally meant "clearing in the woodland by a thoroughfare".|The Durleys were a prominent family in the area.|近义词:|反义词:|n.
