non-igneous:/ˌnɒnɪˈɡniːəs/ | adj. | 非火成的；非岩浆的 | Not formed by the action of fire or volcanoes; relating to rocks formed by processes other than volcanic activity.  These rocks are typically sedimentary or metamorphic. | Example: The geologist identified the sample as a non-igneous rock, likely sedimentary in origin.  地质学家确定该样本是非火成岩，可能是沉积岩。|近义词：sedimentary, metamorphic | 反义词：igneous

词源:

The word "non-igneous" is a combination of the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "igneous" (derived from the Latin word "igneus," meaning "fiery" or "of fire").  Therefore, non-igneous literally means "not fiery" or "not formed by fire" in the context of rock formation.
