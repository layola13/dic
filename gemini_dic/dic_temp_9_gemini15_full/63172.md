entailable: /ɪnˈteɪləbl/ | adj. | 可蕴含的，可推论的 |  capable of being entailed; logically implied or necessarily following from something else.  This term is primarily used in logic and linguistics, referring to the relationship between sentences where one sentence logically implies another.  For example, the sentence "John is a bachelor" entails the sentence "John is unmarried." |  The sentence "The vase broke" entails that "Something caused the vase to break". |近义词：implied, inferable, deducible |反义词：unentailable, independent
