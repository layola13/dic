graptolites:/ˈɡræptəlaɪts/| n. |笔石，笔石类动物|Extinct colonial marine animals of the Paleozoic Era, characterized by a branching or linear structure composed of numerous tiny cups or thecae, often preserved as fossils.  Graptolites are important index fossils used in dating Paleozoic rocks.| 例句：The discovery of graptolites in the rock formation helped geologists date the strata to the Ordovician period.  发现岩石构造中的笔石帮助地质学家将地层年代确定为奥陶纪。
