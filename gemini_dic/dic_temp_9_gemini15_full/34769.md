clim:/klaɪm/| v.|攀登，爬；上升|to go up or over something, especially a mountain or hill; to increase gradually|例句：He climbed the mountain in three hours. 他在三个小时内爬上了这座山。|例句：The temperature climbed steadily throughout the day. 温度全天都在稳步上升。|近义词：ascend, mount, scale|反义词：descend, drop

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He climbs mountains every weekend. = 他每个周末都爬山。
The price of oil is climbing. = 油价正在上涨。

一般过去时:
She climbed the tree to get the cat. = 她爬上树去救猫。
The temperature climbed to 30 degrees yesterday. = 昨天的温度上升到了30度。

现在完成时:
They have climbed Mount Everest. = 他们已经攀登了珠穆朗玛峰。
The company's profits have climbed significantly this year. = 公司的利润今年大幅增长。

过去完成时:
He had climbed several mountains before attempting Everest. = 在尝试攀登珠穆朗玛峰之前，他已经攀登过几座山了。
The water level had climbed dangerously high before the dam broke. = 大坝决堤之前，水位已经上升到危险的高度。

将来时:
We will climb the highest peak next summer. = 我们明年夏天将攀登最高峰。
The inflation rate is expected to climb further next year. = 预计通货膨胀率明年将进一步上升。

虚拟语气:
If I were younger, I would climb that mountain. = 如果我年轻一些，我会爬那座山。
I wish I had climbed that mountain when I had the chance. = 我真希望我有机会爬那座山。

被动语态:
The mountain was climbed by many people last year. = 去年这座山被很多人攀登过。
The temperature is expected to be climbed by 2 degrees tomorrow. = 预计明天的温度将上升2度。

疑问句:
Did he climb the mountain alone? = 他独自爬山了吗？
Will the stock price climb again tomorrow? = 股价明天还会上涨吗？

否定句:
He didn't climb the mountain because of the bad weather. = 由于天气不好，他没有爬山。
The sales didn't climb as much as expected this quarter. = 本季度销售额增长不及预期。


PHRASE->
climb the ladder =  晋升
climb the wall =  发疯，抓狂
climb down =  让步，妥协
climb aboard =  上车，登机
climb out of = 摆脱（困境）
