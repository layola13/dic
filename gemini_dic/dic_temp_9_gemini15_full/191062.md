scandals:/ˈskændəlz/| n. |丑闻，诽谤 |A scandal is an event or action that causes public outrage or disgust.  It often involves wrongdoing or impropriety by a person or organization in a position of power or authority.  Scandals can damage reputations, lead to investigations, and have significant social and political consequences. |Example: The politician's affair became a major scandal that rocked the nation.  这位政治家的婚外情成为震惊全国的一大丑闻。

The recent accounting scandal led to the company's bankruptcy. 最近的会计丑闻导致公司破产。|近义词：controversy, disgrace, shame, scandal. |反义词：honor, integrity, respectability.
