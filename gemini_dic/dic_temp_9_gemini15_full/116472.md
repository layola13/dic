materialise:/məˈtɪəriəlaɪz/ | v. | 实现；具体化；显现 | To become real or visible, or to cause something to become real or visible.  | The long-awaited project finally materialised.  长期期待的项目最终实现了。 |  realize, manifest, appear | disappear, vanish | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His ideas rarely materialise into concrete plans. = 他的想法很少具体化为具体的计划。
The symptoms of the disease usually materialise slowly. = 这种疾病的症状通常缓慢显现。


一般过去时:
Her dreams materialised when she got the job. = 当她得到这份工作时，她的梦想实现了。
The ghost materialised before our eyes. = 鬼魂在我们眼前显现。


现在完成时:
His predictions have materialised with alarming accuracy. = 他的预言以惊人的准确性实现了。
Several opportunities have materialised recently. = 最近出现了一些机会。


过去完成时:
By the time help arrived, the situation had materialised into a full-blown crisis. = 等到帮助到来时，情况已经演变成一场严重的危机。
The plan had materialised exactly as we had hoped. = 计划正如我们所希望的那样实现了。


将来时:
Hopefully, the new technology will materialise within the next five years. = 希望这项新技术将在未来五年内实现。
We expect the results to materialise soon. = 我们预计结果很快就会显现。


虚拟语气:
If only my plans would materialise! = 但愿我的计划能够实现！
I wish my dreams would materialise. = 我希望我的梦想能够实现。


被动语态:
The plan was materialised through the collective effort of the team.  = 这个计划是通过团队的集体努力实现的。
The vision was materialised into a successful company. = 愿景变成了一个成功的公司。


疑问句:
Will his ambitions ever materialise? = 他的雄心壮志最终会实现吗？
When will the promised improvements materialise? = 承诺的改进何时才能实现？


否定句:
His hopes did not materialise. = 他的希望没有实现。
The project failed to materialise. = 项目未能实现。


PHRASE->
materialise out of thin air =凭空出现
materialise into reality =变为现实
materialise unexpectedly =意外出现
materialise from nothing =从无到有
materialise before one's eyes = 在眼前出现
