grand-daddy's:/ˈɡrændˌdædiz/| n.|爷爷的；（非正式）非常棒的；极好的|The possessive form of "grand-daddy," indicating ownership or belonging to a grandfather.  It's often used informally to express something of exceptional quality or magnitude.|例句：That's a grand-daddy of a cake! 那是块超大的蛋糕！|近义词：grandfather's, awesome, fantastic|反义词：terrible, awful

This is grand-daddy's favorite chair. = 这是爷爷最喜欢的椅子。
