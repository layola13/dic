communalisms:/kəˈmjuːnəlɪzəm/| n. |集体主义；公社制度；公共所有制|The word "communalisms" refers to the principles or practices of communal living, often emphasizing shared resources, responsibilities, and decision-making.  It can also refer to the specific beliefs and ideologies supporting such systems.  The term often carries a connotation of shared ownership of property and resources,  a strong sense of community, and a collaborative approach to life. However, the exact meaning can vary depending on the context.|The kibbutzim in Israel are a well-known example of communalisms in practice.  The residents share resources and responsibilities, working together for the collective good.|collectivism, socialism, cooperation|individualism, capitalism, privatization|名词
