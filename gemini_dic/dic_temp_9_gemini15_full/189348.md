sagamore:/ˈsæɡəmɔːr/| n. |萨加莫尔（印第安部落首领）|A sagamore was a Native American leader, particularly of the Algonquian peoples of New England.  The term's exact meaning varied slightly depending on the specific tribe, but generally referred to a chief or sub-chief, often with significant influence but not always the highest authority. |The sagamore led his tribe in negotiations with the colonists.  萨加莫尔带领他的部落与殖民者进行谈判。  The historical records mention several powerful sagamores who shaped the region's early history.  历史记录中提到了几位影响该地区早期历史的强大萨加莫尔。|chief, chieftain, leader |subject, follower
