counterpole:/ˈkaʊntərpəʊl/| n. |对立面，对极；对照物|A counterpole is something that is opposite or contrasting to something else.  It represents a directly opposing force or influence.  The term often implies a significant and fundamental opposition, not just a simple difference.|Example: The company's aggressive marketing campaign became a counterpole to its previous cautious approach.  这家公司的积极营销活动与其之前谨慎的做法形成了鲜明对比。|近义词：opposite, antithesis, converse, counterpart |反义词：complement, counterpart (in a complementary sense)|名词
