expanded:/ɪkˈspændɪd/ | adj. | 扩展的；详尽的；扩大的 |  Made or become larger or more extensive.  |例句：The company has expanded its operations into new markets. 公司已将其业务扩展到新的市场。  |例句：She gave an expanded version of the story. 她讲述了一个更详尽的故事版本。|近义词：enlarged, extended, amplified, detailed |反义词：contracted, reduced, shortened, concise


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company expands its business globally. = 公司在全球范围内扩张业务。
This metal expands when heated. =  这种金属受热会膨胀。

一般过去时:
The city expanded rapidly during the industrial revolution. = 工业革命期间，这座城市迅速扩张。
They expanded their product line last year. = 他们去年扩大了产品线。

现在完成时:
The universe has expanded since the Big Bang. = 自大爆炸以来，宇宙一直在膨胀。
We have expanded our research team recently. = 我们最近扩大了研究团队。

过去完成时:
The army had expanded its forces before the battle. = 军队在战斗前扩大了兵力。
They had expanded their influence throughout the region. = 他们在该地区扩大了影响力。

将来时:
The company will expand its operations next year. = 公司明年将扩大业务。
The population is expected to expand significantly in the coming decades. = 预计未来几十年人口将大幅增长。

虚拟语气:
If the budget were expanded, we could hire more staff. = 如果预算增加，我们可以雇佣更多员工。
I wish the company had expanded into Europe earlier. = 我希望公司早些进军欧洲市场。

被动语态:
The building was expanded to accommodate more people. = 这座建筑物进行了扩建以容纳更多的人。
The project has been expanded to include new features. = 该项目已扩展以包含新功能。

疑问句:
Has the company expanded its market share? = 公司是否扩大了市场份额？
Will the team be expanded next quarter? = 下个季度团队会扩大吗？

否定句:
The business did not expand as quickly as expected. = 这项业务并没有像预期的那样快速扩张。
The government hasn't expanded its social welfare programs. = 政府没有扩大社会福利计划。
