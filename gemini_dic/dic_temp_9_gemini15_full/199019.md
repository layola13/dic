signalize:/ˈsɪɡnəlaɪz/ | vt. | 标识；标志；表明；庆祝 | To be a sign of; to mark or indicate.  To celebrate or commemorate. |例句：The red light signalizes danger.  红灯标志着危险。

The fireworks signalized the victory.  烟花庆祝了胜利。|近义词：indicate, mark, denote, celebrate, commemorate |反义词：obscure, conceal, hide


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The flashing lights signalize an emergency. = 闪烁的灯光标志着紧急情况。
The rising sun signalizes a new beginning. = 冉冉升起的太阳标志着新的开始。

一般过去时:
The bell signalized the end of class. = 铃声标志着下课。
The treaty signalized a new era of peace. = 条约标志着和平新时代的到来。

现在完成时:
The discovery has signalized a major breakthrough in science. = 这一发现标志着科学上的一大突破。
Her success has signalized her dedication and hard work. = 她的成功标志着她的奉献和努力。

过去完成时:
The storm clouds had signalized the approaching tempest. = 乌云预示着暴风雨即将来临。(过去完成时较少用在此词)
The ancient ruins had signalized a lost civilization. = 古代遗址标志着一个失落的文明。(过去完成时较少用在此词)

将来时:
The upcoming election will signalize a change in government. = 即将到来的选举将标志着政府的变革。
The new technology will signalize a revolution in the industry. = 新技术将标志着该行业的革命。

虚拟语气:
If the sirens signalized a fire, we would evacuate immediately. = 如果警报器发出火警信号，我们会立即撤离。
If the treaty had signalized genuine peace, we would be celebrating now. = 如果条约标志着真正的和平，我们现在就会庆祝了。


被动语态:
The importance of the event was signalized by a large celebration. =  这次活动的重要性通过大型庆祝活动得到了体现。
The danger was signalized by the loud alarm. =  危险是由响亮的警报标志出来的。


疑问句:
Does the smoke signalize a problem? = 烟雾是否表明存在问题？
Does this symbol signalize anything significant? = 这个符号是否标志着什么重要的东西？


否定句:
The silence did not signalize agreement. = 沉默并不表示同意。
The lack of response did not signalize indifference. = 缺乏回应并不表示冷漠。


PHRASE->
signalize a change = 宣告改变
signalize the beginning = 标志着开始
signalize an end = 标志着结束
signalize one's arrival = 宣告某人的到来
signalize one's intent = 表明某人的意图


词源:
From "signal" + "-ize," a suffix forming verbs.  The root is from Old French *signaler, ultimately from Latin *signāre "to mark, sign."
