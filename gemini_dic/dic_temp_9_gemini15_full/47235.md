dazzles:/ˈdæzəl/| vt.|使眼花缭乱；使惊叹|To impress greatly; to overwhelm with brilliance or splendor.  It can also refer to a sudden, intense brightness that temporarily affects vision.|例句：The fireworks display dazzled the crowd.  烟花表演使人群眼花缭乱。

The diamond necklace dazzled her with its brilliance.  钻石项链以其璀璨夺目令她惊叹。|近义词：amaze, astound, impress, overwhelm|反义词：bore, disappoint, underwhelm|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bright lights dazzle me. = 耀眼的光线让我眼花缭乱。
The singer's performance dazzles the audience. = 歌手的表演让观众惊叹。

一般过去时:
The magician's trick dazzled the children. = 魔术师的戏法让孩子们眼花缭乱。
The sunset dazzled us with its beauty. = 日落的美景让我们惊叹。

现在完成时:
The city lights have dazzled me since I arrived. = 自从我到达以来，城市灯火一直让我眼花缭乱。
His success has dazzled everyone. = 他的成功让每个人都惊叹。

过去完成时:
The spectacle had dazzled the audience before the final act. = 在最后的表演之前，这场盛大的表演就已经让观众惊叹不已。
I had never been dazzled by such beauty before. = 我以前从未被如此美丽的事物所震撼。


将来时:
The new technology will dazzle the world. = 新技术将使世界惊叹。
The fireworks display will dazzle the spectators. = 烟花表演将使观众眼花缭乱。

虚拟语气:
If the lights were dimmer, they wouldn't dazzle me so much. = 如果灯光暗一些，它们就不会让我这么眼花缭乱。
I wish the performance had dazzled me more. = 我希望这场表演能让我更加惊叹。

被动语态:
We were dazzled by the beauty of the scenery. = 我们被风景的美景所震撼。
The audience was dazzled by the singer's voice. = 观众被歌手的歌声所倾倒。

疑问句:
Does the bright sunshine dazzle you? = 刺眼的阳光是否让你眼花缭乱？
Will the new product dazzle consumers? = 新产品会让消费者惊叹吗？

否定句:
The performance didn't dazzle me as much as I expected. = 这场表演并没有像我预期的那样让我惊叹。
The movie didn't dazzle me; it was quite boring. = 这部电影并没有让我眼花缭乱；它相当无聊。
