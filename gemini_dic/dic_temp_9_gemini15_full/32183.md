chiasm:/ˈkaɪæzəm/| n.|交叉；交叉结构；(修辞)对句；(解剖)交叉|A chiasm is a rhetorical or literary figure of speech in which two or more clauses are balanced against each other by the reversal of their structures in order to produce an artistic effect.  It can also refer to a cross-shaped structure or an anatomical crossing of nerves or fibers.|例句：The sentence "Fair is foul, and foul is fair" is a classic example of a chiasm. 这句话“公平就是邪恶，邪恶就是公平”就是一个经典的交叉结构的例子。


The optic chiasm is where the optic nerves from each eye cross over.  视交叉是大脑中来自双眼的光神经交叉的地方。|近义词：interchange, crossing, crisscross|反义词：parallel, linearity
