intern:/ˈɪntɜːrn/|/ˈɪntɜːrn/| n. v.|实习生；实习；拘留|A person who works in an organization, often for a short time, in order to gain work experience.  The act of serving as an intern; a period of internship. To confine or imprison someone.|例句：She's interning at a law firm this summer. 她今年夏天在一个律师事务所实习。  He was interned during the war. 他在战争期间被拘留。|近义词：trainee, resident, apprentice|反义词：employer, supervisor


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He interns at a hospital. = 他在一家医院实习。
She interns at a design firm. = 她在一家设计公司实习。


一般过去时:
I interned at Google last year. = 我去年在谷歌实习。
He interned at a bank before graduating. = 他毕业前在银行实习过。


现在完成时:
She has interned at several companies. = 她曾在几家公司实习过。
They have not interned before this opportunity. = 他们在此之前从未实习过。


过去完成时:
He had interned for six months before he got the job. = 他实习了六个月后才找到这份工作。
She had already interned at that company before applying again. = 在再次申请之前，她已经在那个公司实习过。


将来时:
I will intern at a tech startup next summer. = 我明年夏天将在一家科技创业公司实习。
He will have interned for a year by next June. = 到明年六月，他将实习一年了。


虚拟语气:
If I had interned at that company, I might have gotten the job. = 如果我在那家公司实习过，我可能会得到这份工作。
I wish I could intern abroad next year. = 我希望明年能够在国外实习。


被动语态:
Many students are interned by large corporations. = 许多学生被大公司接收实习。
He was interned in a prison camp during the war.  = 他在战争期间被关押在战俘营里。


疑问句:
Where do you intern? = 你在哪里实习？
Have you ever interned before? = 你以前实习过吗？


否定句:
I don't intern anywhere at the moment. = 我目前没有任何实习。
She didn't intern at that company. = 她没有在那家公司实习。
