词源:
From Middle English *hakneien, from Old North French haquenée, a riding horse, from haque, a nag.  The sense of "worn-out" developed from the association with frequently used horses.  The modern sense of "computer hacking" is a figurative extension of the sense of "to cut or chop roughly."
