nonhabitableness: /ˌnɒnˌhæbɪtəbəlˈnəs/ | n. |不宜居住；不适宜居住；无法居住 | The state or quality of being uninhabitable; lacking the qualities necessary for comfortable or safe living.  It often refers to places unsuitable for human habitation due to environmental factors or structural issues. | Example: The nonhabitableness of the building was due to severe structural damage and the presence of asbestos.  这栋建筑物不宜居住是由于严重的结构性损坏和石棉的存在。 The extreme cold and lack of resources contributed to the nonhabitableness of the Antarctic region. 极度寒冷和资源匮乏导致南极洲无法居住。 | 近义词：uninhabitability, unsuitability, inhabitability | 反义词：habitability, suitability
