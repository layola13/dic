nondiffusible:/ˌnɒn.dɪˈfjuːzəbl/ | adj. |不可扩散的，不能扩散的 | Not able to spread or be diffused through a membrane or other material.  This term often refers to molecules that are too large or too polar to pass through cell membranes without assistance. | Example:  Large proteins are often nondiffusible across cell membranes.  大型蛋白质通常不能穿过细胞膜。


| Example: The nondiffusible components of the blood plasma contribute to its osmotic pressure. 血液血浆中不可扩散的成分有助于其渗透压。| 近义词：impermeable, nonpermeable | 反义词：diffusible, permeable |


词源:

* **non-**:  A prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of".
* **diffus-**: From Latin *diffundere*, meaning "to pour out, spread, scatter". This root is also related to the word "diffuse".
* **-ible**: A suffix forming adjectives, meaning "capable of being" or "able to be".


因此，"nondiffusible" 字面意思就是 "不能被扩散的"。
