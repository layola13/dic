quasilegislative: /ˌkwɑːziˈlɛdʒɪslətɪv/ | adj. |准立法；类似立法的；具有立法性质的 |  Relating to or having the characteristics of legislation, but not possessing full legal force.  It describes actions or bodies that perform functions similar to a legislature but lack the formal authority of a true legislature.  For example, a regulatory agency might have quasilegislative powers to create rules and regulations, but these rules are subject to judicial review and can be overturned. |The agency exercised quasilegislative powers in setting industry standards. 该机构在制定行业标准方面行使了准立法权。 |近义词：quasi-legislative, semi-legislative |反义词：legislative, fully legislative


词源：

* **quasi-**:  From Latin, meaning "as if," "seemingly," or "apparently."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation but not a complete equivalence.

* **legislative**: From Latin *legislātīvus*, from *legislāre* "to enact laws," from *lex* (genitive *lēgis*) "law" + *lāre* "to bring."


用法说明：

该词主要用作形容词，修饰名词，描述具有类似立法机构功能但不具备其全部法律效力的实体或行为。  它经常用于描述行政机构或其他非立法机构的规则制定权力。


例句：

The committee's actions, though lacking formal legislative authority, were effectively quasilegislative in their impact.  委员会的行动虽然缺乏正式的立法权，但在影响上却实际上具有准立法性质。


The regulatory body possesses quasilegislative powers to create binding rules for the industry.  该监管机构拥有准立法权，可以为该行业制定具有约束力的规则。


近义词和反义词的补充说明:

近义词：semi-legislative (半立法性质的)  也表示类似但并非完全的立法权力。

反义词：legislative (立法的) 指的是拥有正式立法权的机构或行为；fully legislative (完全立法的) 进一步强调完全的立法权力。  与quasilegislative形成对比，后者强调部分或类似的立法功能，而非完整的权力。
