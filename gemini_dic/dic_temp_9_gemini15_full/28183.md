capping:/ˈkæpɪŋ/| n. v.|覆盖；封顶；限制；|the act of putting a cap on something;  the action of limiting or restricting something. |例句：The capping of the well prevented further oil spills.  封井防止了进一步的漏油。|例句：The government imposed a capping on tuition fees. 政府对学费进行了限制。|近义词：covering, topping, limiting, restricting|反义词：uncovering, opening, unlimited


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company is capping its investment in this project. = 公司正在限制对该项目的投资。
He caps the bottle tightly before leaving. = 他离开前紧紧地盖上了瓶子。


一般过去时:
They capped the building with a dome. = 他们用圆顶覆盖了建筑物。
She capped the pen after writing. = 她写完字后盖上了笔帽。


现在完成时:
They have capped the oil well successfully. = 他们已经成功地封住了油井。
We have capped our expenses this month. = 这个月我们已经限制了支出。


过去完成时:
They had capped the bottle before the journey began. = 在旅程开始之前，他们已经盖上了瓶子。
The government had capped the price before inflation spiked. = 在通货膨胀飙升之前，政府已经限制了价格。


将来时:
They will cap the water main tomorrow. = 他们明天将封住自来水管。
The company will cap its expenditure at $1 million. = 公司将把支出限制在100万美元。


虚拟语气:
If they capped the well sooner, the damage would have been less. = 如果他们早点封住油井，损失就会小一些。
If the government had capped the prices earlier, the crisis could have been prevented. = 如果政府早点限制价格，这场危机本来是可以避免的。


被动语态:
The well was capped by the emergency crew. = 油井由应急人员封堵。
The budget was capped at five million dollars. = 预算被限制在500万美元。


疑问句:
Is the project budget capped? = 项目预算是否有限制？
Will the water supply be capped off tonight? = 今晚会切断供水吗？


否定句:
The well was not capped in time. = 油井没有及时封堵。
The company did not cap its spending. = 公司没有限制支出。
