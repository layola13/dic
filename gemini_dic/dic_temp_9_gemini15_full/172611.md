pyrotechnician:/ˌpaɪrəʊtekˈnɪʃən/| n. |烟火技师，烟火专家|A pyrotechnician is a person who designs, manufactures, or sets off fireworks.  They are skilled in the handling and manipulation of explosive materials and pyrotechnic devices. |例句：The pyrotechnician meticulously crafted the fireworks display for the Independence Day celebration.  烟火技师精心制作了独立日庆祝活动的烟火表演。

The experienced pyrotechnician ensured the safety of the audience during the spectacular firework show.  经验丰富的烟火技师确保了观众在精彩的烟火表演中的安全。|近义词：fireworks expert, fireworks technician|反义词：None specifically, but someone unskilled in handling explosives could be considered an antonym in a contextual sense.

词源:
The word "pyrotechnician" is derived from the Greek words "pyr" (πῦρ), meaning "fire," and "techne" (τέχνη), meaning "art" or "skill," combined with the English suffix "-ician," indicating an expert or practitioner.  Therefore, it literally means "one skilled in the art of fire."
