galliot:/ˈɡæliət/| n.|盖轮船，轻型帆船|A galliot is a type of small, flat-bottomed sailing vessel, typically with two or three masts and a relatively shallow draft.  They were commonly used for coastal trading and fishing in the 17th and 18th centuries. |Example: The merchant traded spices on a small galliot along the coast.  商人驾驶一艘小型盖轮船沿海贸易香料。|近义词：schooner, brigantine, ketch|反义词：None specifically, but  large ocean-going vessels are its opposite in size and capability.|名词
