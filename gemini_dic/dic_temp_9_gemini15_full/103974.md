judgement:/ˈdʒʌdʒmənt/ | n. | 判断，判决，意见 | The act of judging or forming an opinion; a decision made or given on a matter.  It can also refer to the ability to make sound judgments. |例句：The judge's judgement was fair and impartial.  法官的判决公正无私。

The jury delivered their judgement after a long deliberation. 陪审团经过长时间的商议后做出了判决。


近义词：decision, verdict, opinion, assessment, appraisal | 反义词：inconclusiveness, uncertainty


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His judgement is always sound. = 他的判断总是很可靠。
Her judgement on art is impeccable. = 她对艺术的鉴赏力无可挑剔。


一般过去时:
The judge's judgement surprised everyone. = 法官的判决让所有人都大吃一惊。
My quick judgement led to a mistake. = 我草率的判断导致了一个错误。


现在完成时:
He has shown poor judgement in his business dealings. = 他在商业交易中表现出糟糕的判断力。
We have reached a judgement on the matter. = 我们对这件事做出了判断。


过去完成时:
I had already formed my judgement before he spoke. = 他说话之前我已经形成了自己的判断。
They had made their judgement based on incomplete information. = 他们根据不完整的信息做出了判断。


将来时:
The jury will deliver their judgement tomorrow. = 陪审团将于明天做出判决。

虚拟语气:
If I had better judgement, I wouldn't have made that mistake. = 如果我判断力更好，我就不会犯那个错误了。
I wish I had shown better judgement. = 我希望我当时表现出更好的判断力。


被动语态:
The case will be judged by the Supreme Court. = 此案将由最高法院审理。
The matter was judged fairly. = 这件事处理得很公平。


疑问句:
What is your judgement on this matter? = 你对这件事的看法是什么？
What was the court's judgement? = 法庭的判决是什么？


否定句:
His judgement was flawed. = 他的判断有缺陷。
I don't agree with your judgement. = 我不同意你的判断。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "judgement" comes from the Old French word "juggement," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "iudicium," meaning "judgment, judicial decision, trial."  The Latin word is related to "iudex," meaning "judge."
