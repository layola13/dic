liberalised: /ˈlɪbərəlaɪzd/ | v. | 自由化；放宽管制 | To make something less strict or more liberal.  | The government liberalised the economy, leading to increased foreign investment.  政府对经济进行了自由化改革，导致外国投资增加。 | deregulate, relax, loosen | restrict, tighten, control | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government liberalises its immigration policy. = 政府正在放宽其移民政策。
They liberalise their views on social issues. = 他们在社会问题上的观点更加自由。


一般过去时:
The country liberalised its trade laws in the 1990s. = 这个国家在20世纪90年代放宽了贸易法。
They liberalised the market after years of strict regulation. = 在多年的严格管制之后，他们对市场进行了自由化改革。


现在完成时:
The reforms have liberalised the financial sector. = 改革已经使金融部门自由化了。
Many countries have liberalised their telecommunications industries. = 许多国家已经将其电信行业自由化了。


过去完成时:
They had liberalised the media before the election. = 他们在选举前已经放宽了媒体管制。
The company had liberalised its dress code after employee feedback. = 在收到员工反馈后，公司已经放宽了着装规定。


将来时:
The government will liberalise the visa application process. = 政府将简化签证申请流程。
They will liberalise the education system to improve access. = 他们将使教育体系更加自由化，以改善入学机会。


虚拟语气:
If they liberalised the market, the economy would grow faster. = 如果他们对市场进行自由化改革，经济增长速度将会更快。
I wish the government had liberalised the laws sooner. = 我希望政府早点放宽法律。


被动语态:
The market was liberalised by the government. = 市场由政府进行了自由化改革。
The regulations were liberalised to stimulate economic growth. = 为了刺激经济增长，法规得到了放宽。


疑问句:
Has the government liberalised its trade policies yet? = 政府是否已经放宽了贸易政策？
Will the bank liberalise its lending criteria? = 银行会放宽贷款标准吗？


否定句:
The government did not liberalise the healthcare system. = 政府没有对医疗体系进行自由化改革。
They have not yet liberalised their immigration laws. = 他们尚未放宽其移民法。



PHRASE->
liberalised trade = 自由贸易
liberalised economy = 自由经济
liberalised market = 自由市场
liberalised society = 自由社会
liberalised approach = 开明的做法


词源:
From "liberal," ultimately from Latin *liberalis ("of or pertaining to a freeman," from liber ("free")).  The "-ised" suffix indicates a process of making something more liberal.
