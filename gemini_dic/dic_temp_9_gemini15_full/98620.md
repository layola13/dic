instrumentalism:/ˌɪnstrʊmənˈtælɪzəm/| n. |工具主义；实用主义|The philosophy that the value of an idea lies solely in its practical application.  It emphasizes the practical consequences of beliefs and actions rather than their intrinsic truth or moral value.  Instrumentalism views scientific theories, for example, as useful tools for predicting and controlling phenomena, rather than as accurate descriptions of reality.|Example:  His approach to art was purely instrumentalist; he cared only about its marketability, not its aesthetic merit.  他的艺术方法纯粹是工具主义的；他只关心它的市场价值，而不是它的美学价值。|近义词：pragmatism, utilitarianism|反义词：idealism, absolutism
