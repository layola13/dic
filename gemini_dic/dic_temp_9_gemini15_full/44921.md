cryptobranchiate:/ˌkrɪptoʊˈbræŋkieɪt/ | adj. | 具隐鳃的；隐鳃类的 |  Having concealed gills, characteristic of certain amphibians.  The term refers to amphibians that retain gills throughout their larval stage, or even into adulthood. These gills are often internal or covered by skin, unlike the external gills of some other aquatic animals. | Example:  The cryptobranchiate salamanders are a fascinating group of amphibians.  具隐鳃的蝾螈是一类令人着迷的两栖动物。 |近义词:  (No direct synonyms, but related terms include "neotenous," referring to the retention of juvenile characteristics in adulthood) |反义词:  (No direct antonyms, but the opposite would be an amphibian that loses its gills during development) |词性:形容词
