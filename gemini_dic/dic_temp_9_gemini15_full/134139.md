nonimplication:/ˌnɒnɪmplɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 非暗示；不蕴涵；非必然结果 | The author's silence on the matter does not necessarily constitute a nonimplication of guilt.  作者对这件事的沉默并不一定意味着他不构成犯罪。|近义词：lack of implication, absence of inference | 反义词：implication


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  The grammatical functions relate to how it's used in a sentence as a noun.

Examples using "nonimplication" in different sentence structures:

Declarative Sentence:
The study's nonimplication of a causal link surprised the researchers. = 这项研究没有暗示因果关系，这令研究人员感到惊讶。

Interrogative Sentence:
Is the nonimplication of the suspect in the police report intentional? = 警报中对嫌疑人的不暗示是故意的吗？

Negative Sentence:
The lack of evidence does not equate to a complete nonimplication of guilt. = 缺乏证据并不等于完全没有暗示有罪。


PHRASE->
There aren't common set phrases directly using "nonimplication".  The usage is more within a sentence structure to describe the absence of implied meaning.  Phrases built around the concept:


absence of implication = 暗示的缺乏
lack of suggestion = 建议的缺乏
no inference = 没有推论
no hidden meaning = 没有隐含意义
clear and direct statement = 清晰直接的陈述
