devalued:/ˌdiːˈvæljuːd/ | adj. v. |贬值的；使贬值 |Reduced in value.  The word describes something that has lost its worth, either in monetary terms or in a more general sense. |例句：The currency has been devalued by 10%.  = 货币贬值了10%。

The government's policies have devalued the importance of education. = 政府的政策贬低了教育的重要性。|近义词：depreciated, diminished, lessened |反义词：revalued, appreciated, enhanced


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The government devalues the currency regularly. = 政府定期贬值货币。
This policy devalues the contributions of women in the workplace. =这项政策贬低了女性在工作场所的贡献。


一般过去时:
The company devalued its assets last year. =该公司去年对资产进行了减值。
The falling demand devalued the painting significantly. =需求下降使这幅画的价值大大降低。


现在完成时:
Inflation has devalued savings significantly. =通货膨胀已经使储蓄大大贬值。
The recent scandal has devalued the company's reputation. =最近的丑闻损害了公司的声誉。


过去完成时:
The stock market crash had devalued their investments dramatically. =股市崩盘已使他们的投资大幅贬值。
The prolonged drought had devalued the farmland's worth. =长期干旱使农田的价值贬低。


将来时:
The new tax law will devalue property in the city. =新的税法将使该市的房产贬值。
Economists predict that the currency will devalue further next year. =经济学家预测，明年货币将进一步贬值。


虚拟语气:
If they had not devalued the currency, the economy might have been stronger. =如果他们没有贬值货币，经济可能会更强劲。
I wish they hadn't devalued my efforts. =我希望他们没有贬低我的努力。


被动语态:
The currency was devalued to boost exports. =贬值货币是为了促进出口。
The company's image was devalued by negative publicity. =公司的形象因负面宣传而贬值。


疑问句:
Has the dollar been devalued recently? =美元最近是否贬值了？
Will the government devalue the currency again? =政府还会再次贬值货币吗？


否定句:
The central bank did not devalue the currency. =中央银行没有贬值货币。
The company’s actions did not devalue its brand. =公司的行为并没有贬低其品牌。


PHRASE->

devalue the currency = 贬值货币
devalue the assets = 贬值资产
devalue the brand = 贬低品牌
devalue the experience = 贬低经验
devalue the work = 贬低工作


词源:

The word "devalued" comes from the verb "devalue," which is formed by adding the prefix "de-" (meaning "to reduce" or "to remove") to the noun "value."  The ultimate origin of "value" traces back to Old French and Latin roots related to strength and worth.
