enfeebling: /ɪnˈfiːblɪŋ/ | adj. | 使虚弱的；使衰弱的 | Weakening; making feeble.  The enfeebling effects of the disease were evident in his weakened state. |  The long illness had an enfeebling effect on him.  他久病体弱。  | weakening, debilitating, devitalizing | strengthening, invigorating | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The illness is enfeebling him. = 这场病正在削弱他。
This constant stress is enfeebling my spirit. = 这种持续的压力正在削弱我的精神。


一般过去时:
The heat enfeebling the soldiers. = 炎热使士兵虚弱。
The long journey enfeebled the travelers. = 漫长的旅程使旅行者疲惫不堪。


现在完成时:
The drought has enfeebled the crops. = 干旱已经使庄稼枯萎。
Years of overwork have enfeebled his health. = 多年的过度劳累已经损害了他的健康。


过去完成时:
The prolonged famine had enfeebled the entire population. = 长期的饥荒已经使整个人口虚弱。
He had been enfeebled by years of illness. = 他多年患病，身体虚弱。


将来时:
Lack of sleep will enfeebling his performance. = 睡眠不足将会削弱他的表现。
The ongoing conflict will enfeebling the nation's economy. = 持续的冲突将会削弱国家的经济。


虚拟语气:
If the medication had not been administered, the condition would have enfeebled him further. = 如果没有服用药物，病情会进一步削弱他。
I wish the poor diet hadn't enfeebled him so much. = 我真希望糟糕的饮食没有让他如此虚弱。


被动语态:
He was enfeebled by the disease. = 他被疾病削弱了。
The army was enfeebled by lack of supplies. = 军队因缺乏补给而虚弱。


疑问句:

Are these policies enfeebling the nation's economy? = 这些政策是否正在削弱国家的经济？


否定句:
The treatment did not enfeebling him as much as expected. = 治疗并没有像预期的那样削弱他。
The exercise program did not enfeebling him; rather, it strengthened him. = 运动计划并没有削弱他，反而使他增强了体质。
