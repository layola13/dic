/sɪˈzeɪʒərəl/| n. | 抑扬格；诗歌中的停顿 | A caesura is a pause or break in a line of verse, often marked by punctuation.  It's a rhythmic device used to create emphasis or a change in pace. The pause can be within a foot or between feet, and its length can vary. | Example:  Sing, Muse, the anger of Peleus' son, Achilles. (The comma after "Sing, Muse" indicates a caesura.)  |近义词：pause, break, interruption |反义词：continuity, flow |名词
