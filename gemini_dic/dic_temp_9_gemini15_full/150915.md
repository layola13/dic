parallelist: /ˈpærəlˌlɪst/ | n. | 平行论者；并行主义者 | A parallelist is someone who advocates for or believes in parallelism, a rhetorical device or literary style using parallel structures of words or phrases.  They might focus on the structural similarities in literature, rhetoric, or even programming. | Example:  The professor is a parallelist, often highlighting the parallel structures in Shakespeare's sonnets.  这位教授是一位平行论者，经常强调莎士比亚十四行诗中的平行结构。 | 近义词:  comparatist,  analogist | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but could be contrasted with someone who emphasizes dissimilarity or asymmetry)  |  n.
