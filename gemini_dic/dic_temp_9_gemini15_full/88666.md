hoarseness:/ˈhɔːrsnəs/| n. |嘶哑，声音沙哑 |A roughness or huskiness of the voice.  Hoarseness is often a symptom of a respiratory infection or overuse of the vocal cords. |Example:  He developed hoarseness after shouting at the football game. 他在足球比赛中大声喊叫后声音嘶哑了。

The persistent hoarseness prompted him to see a doctor.  持续的嘶哑促使他去看医生。|近义词：rawness, roughness, huskiness |反义词：clarity, smoothness

The word "hoarseness" comes from the Middle English word "hors," meaning "rough" or "harsh," combined with the suffix "-ness," indicating a state or condition.  The root is related to the Old English word "hars," meaning "rough" or "shaggy".
