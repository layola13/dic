pose:/poʊz/| vt. vi. n.|摆姿势；提出；假装；难题|to assume a particular posture or attitude, typically one that is considered to be striking or impressive; to put forward or suggest something for consideration; to pretend to be or have something.|例句：She posed for a photograph. 她摆好姿势拍照。 He posed the question to the audience. 他向观众提出了这个问题。|近义词：strike, present, feign|反义词：ignore, neglect,  disguise

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He poses for the camera every day. = 他每天都摆姿势拍照。
She poses a difficult question. = 她提出了一个难题。


一般过去时:
The model posed gracefully for the artist. = 模特优雅地为艺术家摆姿势。
He posed as a doctor to gain entry. = 他假扮医生以进入。


现在完成时:
She has posed for many famous photographers. = 她为许多著名的摄影师摆过姿势。
The scientist has posed a new theory. = 科学家提出了一种新的理论。


过去完成时:
He had posed the question before the meeting began. = 会议开始前他就提出了这个问题。
The suspect had posed as a police officer. = 嫌疑人之前假扮警官。


将来时:
The artist will pose the model tomorrow. = 明天艺术家将为模特摆姿势。
She will pose a challenge to the authorities. = 她将向当局提出挑战。


虚拟语气:
If I were to pose for a portrait, I would want a skilled artist. = 如果我要拍肖像画，我会想要一位技艺娴熟的艺术家。

被动语态:
