nanostructures:/ˈnænəʊˌstrʌktʃərz/ | n. |纳米结构 |Structures with dimensions on the nanometer scale (1-100 nm).  These structures exhibit unique physical and chemical properties due to their small size and high surface area-to-volume ratio. They are used in various applications, including electronics, medicine, and materials science. | Example:  Researchers are developing new nanostructures for efficient solar energy conversion.  研究人员正在开发用于高效太阳能转换的新型纳米结构。 |近义词：nanomaterials, nanoscale structures |反义词：macroscopic structures
