rusted:/ˈrʌstɪd/| adj.|生锈的，腐蚀的|Covered or affected by rust.  Describes a surface that has been corroded by oxidation, typically iron or steel.|例句：The old gate was rusted and barely usable.  那扇旧门生锈了，几乎不能用了。

例句：The rusted car sat abandoned in the field. 那辆生锈的汽车被遗弃在田野里。


近义词：corroded, oxidized, weathered, decayed|反义词：shiny, polished, new, bright


词源:  From the verb "rust," ultimately from Old English *rust, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch roest and German Rost.  The suffix "-ed" indicates the past participle form, used here adjectivally.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The metal is rusting quickly in this humid climate. = 这潮湿的气候下金属很快生锈。
The old tools are always rusting. = 那些旧工具总是生锈。


一般过去时:

The car body rusted over the years. = 车身多年来生锈了。


现在完成时:
The fence has rusted significantly since last winter. = 自从去年冬天以来，栅栏已经严重生锈了。
The pipe has rusted through in several places. = 管子在几处已经锈穿了。


过去完成时:
The ship's hull had rusted badly before they found it. = 在他们发现之前，船体已经严重生锈了。
The engine had rusted solid before we could repair it. = 在我们能修理之前，发动机已经锈死了。


将来时:
The car will rust if you don't protect it. = 如果你不保护它，汽车会生锈。
The iron railing will probably rust within a few years. = 铁栏杆可能几年内就会生锈。


虚拟语气:

I wish the bicycle hadn't rusted so badly. = 我真希望自行车没有那么严重地生锈。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
