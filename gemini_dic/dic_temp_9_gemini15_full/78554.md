ghostwriter:/ˈɡoʊstˌraɪtər/ | n. | 捉刀人；代笔人 | A ghostwriter is a person who writes books, articles, or other texts that are officially credited to another person.  They work behind the scenes, often for celebrities, busy executives, or individuals who lack the time or writing skills to produce a work themselves. | Example:  Many famous celebrities use ghostwriters to help them write their autobiographies.  许多著名的名人请捉刀人来帮忙撰写他们的自传。 | 近义词：writer for hire, uncredited author | 反义词：credited author

He ghostwrites for several high-profile clients. = 他为几位知名客户代笔。
She ghostwrites articles for a popular magazine. = 她为一本流行杂志撰写文章（代笔）。


一般过去时:
