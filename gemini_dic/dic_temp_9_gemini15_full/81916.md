grillage:/ɡrɪˈlɑːʒ/| n. |格栅；钢筋网；井字梁 |A grillage is a structure of interwoven beams or bars, often used as a foundation for heavy loads or to distribute weight evenly.  It's commonly made of steel or timber. |例句：The bridge was supported by a massive grillage of steel beams. 这座桥由巨大的钢梁格栅支撑。|例句：The foundation of the building consisted of a reinforced concrete grillage. 这座建筑的地基由钢筋混凝土格栅构成。|近义词：lattice, framework, grating |反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "grillage" comes from the French word "grillage," meaning "grating" or "lattice."  It ultimately derives from the verb "griller," meaning "to grill" or "to broil," referring to the criss-cross pattern resembling a grill.
