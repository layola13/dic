cold-war:/ˈkoʊld wɔːr/| n. |冷战|The Cold War was a period of geopolitical tension between the United States and the Soviet Union and their respective allies, the Western Bloc and the Eastern Bloc, after World War II.  It was characterized by an arms race, proxy wars, and ideological conflict, but without direct military confrontation between the two superpowers. |Example: The Cold War ended with the collapse of the Soviet Union in 1991.  冷战于1991年苏联解体时结束。|近义词：ideological conflict, geopolitical tension, arms race|反义词：hot war, peace, cooperation|名词
