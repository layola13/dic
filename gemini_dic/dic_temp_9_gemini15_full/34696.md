clerstory:/ˈklɜːrstɔːri/| n. |走马楼，窗格间壁|A clerestory is a section of wall, typically above eye level, that contains windows.  It's often found in churches, cathedrals, and other buildings with high roofs, allowing light to penetrate the interior.  The windows are typically placed high in the wall, above the roofline of a lower section, to provide illumination without obstructing the lower level. |The cathedral's clerestory windows bathed the nave in sunlight.|近义词：high window, upper window|反义词：basement, ground floor.
