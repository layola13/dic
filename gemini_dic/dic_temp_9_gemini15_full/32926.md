choosy:/ˈtʃuːzi/| adj.|挑剔的，难以取悦的|Someone who is choosy is very careful in choosing things and is not easily pleased.  They have high standards and are often unwilling to accept anything that does not meet those standards. |例句：She's very choosy about her clothes. 她对衣服很挑剔。

She's so choosy; she refused all the job offers. 她太挑剔了，拒绝了所有的工作机会。|近义词：fastidious, picky, particular, discerning|反义词：undemanding, uncritical, easygoing

词源:
The word "choosy" comes from the verb "choose," meaning to select or make a decision from a range of options.  The suffix "-y" transforms the verb into an adjective, indicating a characteristic or tendency.  Its origin traces back to Old English "ceosan," meaning "to choose."
