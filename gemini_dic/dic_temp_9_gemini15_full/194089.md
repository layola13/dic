self-ignorant: /ˌsɛlfˈɪɡnərənt/ | adj. | 自我无知的，对自身缺乏了解的 | Lacking self-awareness; unaware of one's own limitations, flaws, or shortcomings.  |  He was self-ignorant of his own biases, leading to insensitive comments. 他对自己存在的偏见毫无自知之明，导致了他发表了一些不敏感的言论。 |近义词: unaware, oblivious, unselfconscious | 反义词: self-aware, insightful, perceptive

PHRASE->
There are not many common phrases directly using "self-ignorant". The meaning is usually conveyed through other phrases describing lack of self-awareness.  Examples of related phrases might include:

blissfully unaware = 幸福地一无所知
lack of self-awareness = 缺乏自我意识
willful ignorance = 故意无知
in denial = 否认
out of touch with reality = 脱离现实


词源:

The word is a modern compound.  "Self" is a common prefix indicating something related to oneself. "Ignorant" derives from Old French *ignorant, from Latin *ignorāre "to be unaware," from *in- "not" + *gnōscere "to know," related to Greek *gignōskein.
