jeopardised: /ˈdʒɛpədaɪzd/ | v. | 危及；使处于危险境地 | To put something or someone at risk of being harmed or damaged. | The project was jeopardised by a lack of funding.  这个项目因缺乏资金而危及。|The delay jeopardised our chances of success.  延误危及了我们成功的几率。| endanger, threaten, imperil | secure, protect, safeguard | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The reckless driver jeopardizes the safety of others. = 鲁莽的司机危及他人的安全。
He jeopardizes his career by making such a poor decision. = 他因做出如此糟糕的决定而危及自己的职业生涯。


一般过去时:
The storm jeopardised the harvest. = 暴风雨危及了收成。
His careless actions jeopardised the entire operation. = 他粗心大意的行为危及了整个行动。


现在完成时:
His actions have jeopardised the company's reputation. = 他的行为已经危及了公司的声誉。
The recent scandal has jeopardised the politician's career. = 最近的丑闻已经危及了这位政治家的职业生涯。


过去完成时:

We had jeopardised the deal by failing to meet the deadline. = 我们因未能按时完成而危及了这笔交易。


将来时:
The proposed changes will jeopardise the stability of the economy. = 提议的变更将危及经济的稳定性。
Further delays will jeopardise the success of the mission. = 进一步的延误将危及任务的成功。


虚拟语气:
If he continued to drink, he would jeopardise his health. = 如果他继续喝酒，他将危及他的健康。
If we hadn't acted quickly, we would have jeopardised the entire project. = 如果我们没有迅速行动，我们就会危及整个项目。


被动语态:

疑问句:

Would the new regulations jeopardise the company's future? = 新的法规会危及公司的未来吗？


否定句:
