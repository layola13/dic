lockdown:/ˈlɒkdaʊn/ /ˈlɑːkdaʊn/| n. v.|封锁；禁闭；停课|The act of restricting access to an area or facility, often in response to a crisis or emergency.  It can refer to a period of confinement or restrictions on movement imposed by authorities.|例句：The city was placed under lockdown after the terrorist attack.  城市在恐怖袭击后被封锁。|例句：The school implemented a lockdown due to a security threat. 学校因安全威胁而实施了封锁。|近义词：confinement, quarantine, closure, curfew|反义词：opening, freedom, release|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government locks down the city. = 政府封锁了这座城市。
They lockdown the school during the fire drill. = 他们在消防演习期间封锁了学校。


一般过去时:
The police locked down the area after the accident. = 事故发生后，警方封锁了该区域。
They locked down the building for several hours. = 他们封锁了大楼数小时。


现在完成时:
The authorities have locked down the entire region. = 当局已经封锁了整个地区。
They have locked down the borders to prevent the spread of the virus. = 他们已经封锁了边境以防止病毒蔓延。


过去完成时:
They had locked down the hospital before the outbreak became severe. = 在疫情严重爆发之前，他们已经封锁了医院。
The city had locked down for weeks before the restrictions were eased. = 在限制措施放宽之前，这座城市已经封锁了数周。


将来时:
The government will lockdown the capital if the situation worsens. = 如果情况恶化，政府将封锁首都。
They will lockdown the entire state if the virus spreads further. = 如果病毒进一步蔓延，他们将封锁整个州。


虚拟语气:
If the threat were real, they would lockdown the airport immediately. = 如果威胁是真实的，他们会立即封锁机场。
I wish they had locked down the area sooner. = 我希望他们早点封锁该区域。


被动语态:
The area was locked down by the police. = 该区域被警方封锁。
The school was locked down due to a bomb threat. = 学校因炸弹威胁而被封锁。


疑问句:
Will the city be locked down? = 城市会被封锁吗？
Was the school locked down yesterday? = 学校昨天被封锁了吗？


否定句:
The authorities did not lockdown the city. = 当局没有封锁城市。
They did not lockdown the school despite the threat. = 尽管有威胁，他们也没有封锁学校。
