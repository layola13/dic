buttonholing: /ˈbʌtənˌhoʊlɪŋ/ | v. | 抓住（某人）并强行与之交谈；纠缠；盘问 | To stop and detain someone in order to talk to them, especially in a way that is not welcome. | 例句：He kept buttonholing me at the party, asking about my work. 他在派对上一直抓住我不放，问我工作的事情。  | 例句：She was buttonholed by a journalist who wanted an interview. 她被一位想采访她的记者拦住了。 | 近义词：accost, detain, interrupt,  | 反义词：ignore, avoid


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He buttonholes people at every conference. = 他在每次会议上都抓住人们不放。
She buttonholes her colleagues to discuss the project. = 她抓住同事们讨论项目。


一般过去时:
He buttonholed me in the hallway yesterday. = 他昨天在走廊里拦住了我。
She buttonholed the CEO after the meeting. = 会议后她拦住了CEO。


现在完成时:
He has buttonholed me several times this week. = 他这周已经几次拦住我了。
She has buttonholed many potential clients. = 她已经拦住了许多潜在客户。


过去完成时:
He had buttonholed the professor before the lecture. = 他在讲座前拦住了教授。
She had buttonholed him several times before he agreed. = 她在他同意之前已经几次拦住他了。


将来时:
He will buttonhole the minister at the reception. = 他将在招待会上拦住部长。
She will buttonhole him again tomorrow. = 她明天还会再次拦住他。


虚拟语气:
If I saw him, I would buttonhole him. = 如果我看到他，我会拦住他。
I wish I hadn't buttonholed him yesterday. = 我真希望昨天没有拦住他。


被动语态:
He was buttonholed by a reporter. = 他被一个记者拦住了。
She was buttonholed for hours. = 她被拦住了好几个小时。


疑问句:
Was he buttonholed by the police? = 他被警察拦住了吗？

否定句:
