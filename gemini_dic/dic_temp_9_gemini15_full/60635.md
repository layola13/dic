elect:/ɪˈlɛkt/ | vt. |选择；选举；推选 | To choose someone or something for a position or task, usually by voting. |例句：The committee elected a new chairman. 委员会选举了一位新主席。|例句：They elected him president. 他们选他当总统。|近义词：choose, select, nominate |反义词：reject, dismiss


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We elect a new president every four years. = 我们每四年选举一位新总统。
The school elects a student council each year. = 学校每年都选举学生会。


一般过去时:
The citizens elected him mayor. = 市民们选他当市长。
She was elected class president. = 她被选为班长。


现在完成时:
They have elected a new leader. = 他们已经选出了新领导。
We have never elected a woman president before. = 我们以前从未选举过女总统。


过去完成时:
They had elected him before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，他们已经选了他。
The committee had already elected a chairperson when I arrived. = 我到达时，委员会已经选出了主席。


将来时:
The people will elect a new government next month. = 人民下个月将选举新政府。
We will elect our representatives in November. = 我们将在十一月选举我们的代表。


虚拟语气:
If we elected a better leader, things might improve. = 如果我们选出一个更好的领导人，情况可能会好转。
I wish they had elected a more experienced candidate. = 我希望他们选了一个更有经验的候选人。


被动语态:
He was elected to the board of directors. = 他被选入董事会。
A new president will be elected next year. = 明年将选举一位新总统。


疑问句:
Who will be elected as the next senator? = 谁将当选下一任参议员？
When are the elections to be held? = 选举什么时候举行？


否定句:
They did not elect him to the committee. = 他们没有选他进入委员会。
She was not elected to the position. = 她没有当选这个职位。


PHRASE->
elect to do something = 选择做某事
be elected to = 被选为…
elect a representative = 选举代表
elect a committee = 选举委员会
get elected = 当选


词源:
From Old French *elegir, from Latin *eligere "to pick out, choose," from ex- "out" + legere "to gather, read, choose."  The sense development reflects the original meaning of picking out a candidate from many.
