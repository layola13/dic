contrabass:/ˈkɒntrəbeɪs/ /ˈkɑːntrəbeɪs/| n. |倍大提琴；低音提琴|A contrabass is a large stringed instrument, the lowest-pitched member of the violin family. It provides the bass line in orchestral and other musical ensembles.|The orchestra's contrabass player delivered a powerful, resonant tone.  乐队的低音提琴演奏家演奏出浑厚有力的音色。|bass, double bass|treble, violin|名词

词源:
The word "contrabass" comes from the Italian "contrabasso," a combination of "contra" (meaning "against" or "counter") and "basso" (meaning "bass" or "low").  It refers to its function as a counterpoint to the bass.  The word entered English through Italian musical terminology.
