overnormalizations:/ˌoʊvərˈnɔːrməlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. |过度规范化；过度标准化 | The act of excessively standardizing or simplifying something, often resulting in the loss of nuance or complexity.  This term is often used in linguistics, particularly in the context of language acquisition and second language learning, where learners might overapply grammatical rules. | Example:  The student's overnormalizations in their writing led to some awkward sentences.  学生的写作中过度规范化导致一些句子很别扭。 | 近义词: oversimplification, overregulation | 反义词:  underregulation, naturalization
