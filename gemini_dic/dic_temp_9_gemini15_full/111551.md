lobbish:/ˈlɒbɪʃ/| v.|游说，说服|To try to persuade an official or a group of officials to support or reject a particular policy.|The environmental groups lobbied the government to pass stricter environmental regulations.  环境组织游说政府通过更严格的环境法规。|They lobbied hard for the new law.他们大力游说争取新法案通过。| advocate, persuade, influence | oppose, resist |动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They lobby the government regularly. = 他们定期游说政府。
He lobbies for stricter gun control laws. = 他游说更严格的枪支管制法。

一般过去时:
She lobbied the committee for weeks. = 她游说了委员会好几个星期。
They lobbied Congress unsuccessfully. = 他们游说国会未果。

现在完成时:
They have lobbied extensively on this issue. = 他们在这个问题上进行了广泛的游说。
We have lobbied for years to get this changed. = 我们多年来一直在游说改变这一点。

过去完成时:
They had lobbied the mayor before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前已经游说了市长。
She had lobbied hard but to no avail. = 她曾努力游说，但没有结果。

将来时:
They will lobby the council next week. = 他们下周将游说议会。
We will continue to lobby for change. = 我们将继续游说以求改变。

虚拟语气:
If they lobbied harder, they might succeed. = 如果他们更加努力游说，他们可能会成功。
I wish they had lobbied more effectively. = 我希望他们游说的更有效率。

被动语态:
The government was lobbied by several groups. = 政府受到了几个团体的游说。
The bill was being lobbied against heavily. = 这项法案正遭到强烈的反对游说。

疑问句:
Who are they lobbying? = 他们在游说谁？
Are they lobbying for or against the proposal? = 他们在游说支持还是反对该提案？

否定句:
They did not lobby the senators. = 他们没有游说参议员们。
She didn't lobby for the bill. = 她没有为这项法案游说。


PHRASE->

lobby for: 为…游说
lobby against: 反对…游说
intense lobbying: 强烈的游说
effective lobbying: 有效的游说
behind-the-scenes lobbying: 幕后游说
