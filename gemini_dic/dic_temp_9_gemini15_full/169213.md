prologged:/ˈprɒlɒɡd/ /ˈproʊlɒɡd/| adj.|  带有序言的；有开场白的；冗长的 |  Having a prologue;  characterized by a long and often tedious introduction.  The word implies a lengthy or drawn-out nature, often in a negative context. | 例句：The professor's lecture was prologged with a lengthy historical overview. = 教授的讲座以冗长的历史概述作为开场白。

The novel had a prologged introduction that delayed the main plot. = 这部小说有一个冗长的引言，拖延了主要情节。


近义词：prefaced, introduced, extended, lengthy, protracted | 反义词：abbreviated, concise, brief, succinct | 词性：形容词

词源:
From "prologue" + "-ed" (past participle suffix).  "Prologue" itself comes from the Late Latin *prologus, from Greek πρόλογος (prólogos), from πρό (pró, "before") + λόγος (lógos, "word, speech").
