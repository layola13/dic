quadrituberculate:/ˌkwɒdrɪˈtjuːbərˌkjuːleɪt/| adj. |具四个瘤的；四结节的 |Having four tubercles.  The term is primarily used in zoology and botany to describe structures with four small, rounded projections or bumps. |Example: The quadrituberculate molars of the extinct primate were a key feature in its identification.  灭绝灵长类动物的四个结节的磨牙是其鉴定的一个关键特征。|近义词: four-tubercled, tetratuberculate |反义词:tuberculate(having one tubercle)
