bustles:/ˈbʌsəlz/| n. v.|喧嚣；忙碌；裙撑；(使)忙碌 | A bustle is a state of energetic activity and movement.  It can also refer to a padded structure worn under a woman's skirt to give it a full appearance. As a verb, it means to move or act in a busy and energetic way.|例句：The market was a bustle of activity. 市场一片繁忙景象。 The children bustled around, getting ready for school. 孩子们忙着准备上学。|近义词：bustle about, flurry, rush, stir, flurry of activity|反义词：calm, quiet, stillness


USAGE->

She bustles about the kitchen preparing dinner. = 她忙忙碌碌地在厨房准备晚餐。
