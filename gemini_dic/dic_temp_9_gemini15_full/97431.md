infuriate:/ɪnˈfjʊəriˌeɪt/ | vt. |使愤怒；激怒 | To make someone extremely angry. |例句：His constant interruptions infuriated me.  他的不断打断让我非常生气。|例句：The traffic jam infuriated the commuters. 交通堵塞激怒了通勤者。|近义词：enrage, anger, irritate, exasperate |反义词：calm, soothe, appease


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news infuriates him. = 这个消息让他很生气。
His behavior infuriates everyone. = 他的行为惹恼了所有人。


一般过去时:
The delay infuriated the passengers. = 延误激怒了乘客们。
Her comments infuriated her boss. = 她的评论激怒了她的老板。


现在完成时:
His rudeness has infuriated me many times. = 他的粗鲁已经多次激怒了我。
The constant noise has infuriated the neighbors. = 持续的噪音已经激怒了邻居们。


过去完成时:
I had been infuriated by his lies before I confronted him. = 在我与他对质之前，我已经被他谎话激怒了。
The team had been infuriated by the unfair decision. = 球队对不公平的决定感到非常愤怒。


将来时:
The announcement will infuriate many people. = 这个公告将会激怒很多人。
His actions will surely infuriate his parents. = 他的行为肯定会激怒他的父母。


虚拟语气:
If he knew the truth, it would infuriate him. = 如果他知道真相，这会让他很生气。
I wish that wouldn't infuriate him so much. = 我希望这不会让他如此生气。


被动语态:
He was infuriated by their behavior. = 他被他们的行为激怒了。
Many people were infuriated by the politician's speech. = 许多人被这位政治家的演讲激怒了。


疑问句:
Does his attitude infuriate you? = 他的态度让你生气吗？
What infuriates you the most about this situation? = 在这种情况下，什么事情最让你生气？


否定句:
The news didn't infuriate her as much as she expected. = 这个消息并没有像她预期的那样让她生气。
The explanation didn't infuriate him; it just confused him. = 解释并没有让他生气；它只是让他困惑。


PHRASE->
be infuriated by = 被……激怒
infuriated with = 对……感到愤怒
infuriatingly = 令人恼火地
absolutely infuriated = 非常生气
become infuriated = 变得愤怒
