preclosures: /ˈpriːˌkloʊʒərz/ | n. | 预先关闭；预先封锁；预防性关闭 |  The actions taken to prevent something from happening, often involving shutting down or stopping a process before a problem occurs.  Preclosures are often implemented as a preventative measure to mitigate risks. | 例句：The bank implemented preclosures on several accounts suspected of fraudulent activity. = 银行对几个涉嫌欺诈活动的账户采取了预先关闭措施。


The company initiated preclosures of its factories in anticipation of the hurricane. = 公司预料到飓风来临，提前关闭了工厂。


近义词： preventative measures, safeguards, precautions | 反义词：post-event actions, reactive measures |  词性：名词

词源：
