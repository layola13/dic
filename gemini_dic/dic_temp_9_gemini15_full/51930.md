diabetes insipidus: /ˌdaɪəˈbiːtiːz ɪnˈsɪpɪdəs/ | n. |尿崩症|A condition characterized by the excretion of large amounts of dilute urine, resulting from a deficiency of vasopressin (antidiuretic hormone) or the kidney's inability to respond to it.  It is characterized by excessive thirst and the production of large volumes of very dilute urine. Unlike diabetes mellitus, it is not related to blood sugar levels.|The patient presented with polyuria and polydipsia, consistent with diabetes insipidus.  病人出现多尿和多饮，与尿崩症相符。  His diabetes insipidus was treated with desmopressin. 他的尿崩症用去氨加压素治疗。|diabetes mellitus (a different type of diabetes),  polyuria (frequent urination)|normal urine production|
