janissary:/ˈdʒænɪsəri/| n. | 耶尼切里，禁卫军 | A member of an elite corps of infantry in the Ottoman army, recruited as boys, and noted for their military skill and loyalty.  They were originally Christian slaves, but later included Muslims. | The janissaries were a powerful force in the Ottoman Empire for centuries.  耶尼切里几个世纪以来一直是奥斯曼帝国的一支强大的力量。|近义词：soldier, guardsman, mercenary |反义词：civilian
