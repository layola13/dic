mohawks:/ˈmoʊhɔːks/ | n. | 莫霍克族人；莫霍克发型 |  A member of a Native American people originally inhabiting the Mohawk Valley in New York State.  The term also refers to a hairstyle characterized by a strip of hair left standing upright in the center of the head, often shaved or closely cropped on the sides. |例句：The Mohawk warriors were known for their ferocity in battle. 莫霍克族战士以其战斗中的凶猛而闻名。  He sported a daring mohawk at the concert. 他在音乐会上梳了一个大胆的莫霍克发型。|近义词：Iroquois (for the people),  fauxhawk (for the hairstyle) |反义词：buzz cut (for the hairstyle, opposite in style)
