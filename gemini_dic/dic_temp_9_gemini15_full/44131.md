crisscrossed:/ˈkrɪskrɒst/| adj. v.| 交叉的；纵横交错的 |  Describes something that is marked or arranged in a pattern of intersecting lines.  It can be used to describe both physical objects and abstract concepts. |例句：The city streets were crisscrossed by canals.  城市的街道被运河纵横交错地分割开来。 The teacher crisscrossed the names on the list to pair students for the project. 老师在名单上交叉勾选名字，为项目将学生配对。|近义词：intertwined, interwoven, intersecting|反义词：parallel, straight, unentangled|形容词，动词

词源:
The word "crisscross" is a reduplication of "cross," meaning to intersect or go across.  The repetition emphasizes the multiple intersecting lines.  The origin of "cross" itself goes back to Old English "cris," meaning "cross" or "a mark shaped like a cross."
