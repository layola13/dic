disdainful: /dɪsˈdeɪnfəl/ | adj. | 轻蔑的，鄙视的 | Showing contempt or lack of respect.  |例句：He gave her a disdainful look. 他给了她一个轻蔑的眼神。|例句：Her disdainful attitude towards her colleagues made her unpopular. 她对同事轻蔑的态度让她不受欢迎。|近义词：contemptuous, scornful, supercilious |反义词：respectful, admiring, deferential


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is disdainful of his rivals. = 他对他的对手不屑一顾。
She is always disdainful towards newcomers. = 她总是对新人轻蔑。

一般过去时:
He was disdainful of their efforts. = 他对他们的努力不屑一顾。
She was disdainful of his apologies. = 她对他的道歉不屑一顾。

现在完成时:
He has been disdainful of authority his whole life. = 他一生都对权威不屑一顾。
She has always been disdainful of gossip. = 她一直对流言蜚语不屑一顾。

过去完成时:

She had been disdainful of his advances for months. = 她几个月来一直对他示好不屑一顾。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "disdainful" comes from the Old French word "desdaigneus," which itself derives from the verb "desdaigner," meaning "to disdain."  The ultimate origin traces back to the Vulgar Latin *desdenare, a compound of *de- ("from, away from") and *sdenare ("to scorn, despise").
