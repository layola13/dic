reconceptualizing: /ˌriːkənˈsɛptʃuəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |重新概念化，重新构想 | The process of reconsidering and redefining existing concepts or ideas, often to create a new understanding or framework.  It involves challenging assumptions and developing alternative perspectives. | 例句：The researchers are reconceptualizing the nature of consciousness.  研究人员正在重新思考意识的本质。

例句：The company is reconceptualizing its marketing strategy to better reach younger consumers. 公司正在重新构想其营销策略，以更好地接触年轻消费者。


近义词：rethinking, reimagining, reformulating, revising, reinterpreting | 反义词：accepting (uncritically), maintaining (status quo) | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are reconceptualizing the curriculum. = 他们正在重新构想课程体系。
We reconceptualize the problem every day. = 我们每天都在重新思考这个问题。


一般过去时:
She reconceptualized her approach to the project. = 她重新构想了她处理项目的方法。
They reconceptualized the entire system. = 他们重新构想了解整个系统。


现在完成时:
We have reconceptualized our understanding of gravity. = 我们已经重新构建了我们对引力的理解。
They have reconceptualized the role of education in society. = 他们已经重新定义了教育在社会中的作用。


过去完成时:
Before the presentation, he had reconceptualized his argument. = 在演示文稿之前，他已经重新构想了他的论点。
They had reconceptualized their business model several times. = 他们已经多次重新构想了他们的商业模式。


将来时:
We will reconceptualize the design next week. = 我们下周将重新设计。
She will reconceptualize her research plan after the conference. = 会议后，她将重新构想她的研究计划。


虚拟语气:
If we reconceptualized the problem, we might find a solution. = 如果我们重新构想这个问题，我们可能会找到解决方案。
I wish they had reconceptualized the project earlier. = 我希望他们早点重新构想这个项目。


被动语态:
The theory is being reconceptualized by the scientists. = 这一理论正在被科学家们重新构想。
The marketing strategy was reconceptualized last year. = 营销策略去年被重新构想。


疑问句:
Are they reconceptualizing their product line? = 他们是否正在重新构想他们的产品线？
How will we reconceptualize the approach to this challenge? = 我们将如何重新构想应对这一挑战的方法？


否定句:
They did not reconceptualize the existing framework. = 他们没有重新构想现有的框架。
We will not reconceptualize the plan without further data. = 没有进一步的数据，我们将不会重新构想这个计划。

词源:  re- (again) + conceptualize (to form a concept in the mind)  来自拉丁语“conceptus” (something conceived),  最终源于“concipere” (to conceive, take in).
