commensurable: /kəˈmɛnzərəbəl/ | adj. | 可比較的；相稱的；可通約的 |

英文解析: Capable of being measured by the same standard; comparable.  Two quantities are commensurable if their ratio is a rational number.  In a broader sense, it refers to things that can be compared or measured against each other in a meaningful way.

例句:

The two projects are commensurable in terms of their complexity. = 这两个项目的复杂程度是可以比较的。

The success of the two companies is commensurable, both having experienced significant growth. = 两家公司的成功是可比的，两者都经历了显著的增长。


近义词: comparable, equivalent, proportionate, measurable

反义词: incommensurable, incomparable, disproportionate, immeasurable


词源:  From Latin *commensurābilis, from com- "together" + mensurābilis "measurable," from mensūra "measure."
