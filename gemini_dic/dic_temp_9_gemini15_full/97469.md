ingender:/ɪnˈdʒɛndər/ | vt. | 产生；引起；造成 | To cause or give rise to (a feeling, situation, or condition). | 例句：His policies engendered much controversy. 他的政策引起了许多争议。

例句：The war engendered widespread suffering.  这场战争带来了广泛的苦难。


近义词：cause, produce, generate, create, bring about | 反义词：destroy, eliminate, prevent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new law engenders hope for a better future. = 这项新法律带来了对更美好未来的希望。
His actions engender distrust among his colleagues. = 他的行为在同事中造成了不信任。


一般过去时:
The speech engendered a sense of unity among the audience. = 这篇演讲在听众中产生了一种团结感。
The accident engendered significant damage to the property. = 事故给财产造成了重大损失。


现在完成时:
The reforms have engendered positive changes in society. = 改革已经给社会带来了积极的变化。
Her efforts have engendered respect from her peers. = 她的努力赢得了同行的尊重。


过去完成时:
The previous government's policies had engendered economic instability. = 前政府的政策已经导致了经济不稳定。
Years of neglect had engendered a sense of hopelessness in the community. = 多年的忽视已经在社区中造成了绝望感。


将来时:
The new technology will engender many new job opportunities. = 新技术将带来许多新的就业机会。
The proposed changes will engender significant debate. = 提议的改变将引起激烈的辩论。


虚拟语气:
If the project were successful, it would engender significant economic growth. = 如果项目成功，它将带来显著的经济增长。
I wish my actions had not engendered such negativity. = 我真希望我的行为没有引起如此负面的影响。


被动语态:
Hope was engendered by the announcement of the peace treaty. = 和平条约的宣布带来了希望。
Much criticism was engendered by the company's decision. = 该公司的决定引起了很多批评。


疑问句:
What will this new policy engender? =这项新政策会产生什么影响？
Does this behavior engender trust? = 这种行为会带来信任吗？


否定句:
The meeting did not engender any productive solutions. = 会议没有产生任何有效的解决方案。
His words did not engender any sympathy. = 他的话没有引起任何同情。
