slavering: /ˈslævərɪŋ/ | adj. |垂涎欲滴的；口水直流的 | excessively eager or greedy; covered with saliva |

英文解析:  The word "slavering" describes someone or something that is excessively eager or greedy, often to the point of being unpleasant or repulsive.  It can also literally describe something covered in saliva.

例句:

The dog, slavering at the mouth, watched intently as I prepared its dinner. = 那条狗嘴边流着口水，专心地看着我准备它的晚餐。

His slavering ambition led him to betray his friends. = 他贪婪的野心导致他背叛了他的朋友。


近义词: drooling, salivating, ravenous, greedy, avaricious

反义词:  indifferent, apathetic, unconcerned, disinterested


词源:  Comes from the verb "slaver," meaning to drool or dribble saliva. This, in turn, originates from the Old French word "slaver," meaning to slaver or spit.  The ultimate origin is likely onomatopoeic, imitating the sound of saliva.
