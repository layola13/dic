enervate:/ɪˈnɜːrveɪt/ | vt. |使衰弱；使无力；使疲惫 |To weaken or destroy the strength or vitality of.  |The long journey enervated the travelers. 长途旅行使旅行者精疲力竭。|近义词：weaken, debilitate, exhaust |反义词：invigorate, strengthen


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The heat enervates me. = 炎热的天气使我感到虚弱。
This medicine enervates the patient. = 这药使病人虚弱。


一般过去时:
The illness enervated him. = 这场病使他虚弱。
The long speech enervated the audience. = 漫长的演讲使听众感到疲惫。


现在完成时:
The drought has enervated the crops. = 干旱已经损害了庄稼。
The stress has enervated her. = 压力使她精疲力竭。


过去完成时:
He had been enervated by weeks of illness. = 他几周的病痛已经让他虚弱不堪。
The army had been enervated by the long campaign. = 长期的战役使军队精疲力竭。


将来时:
The heat will enervate us. = 炎热的天气会让我们感到虚弱。
Lack of sleep will enervate him for the exam. = 睡眠不足会让他考试时状态不佳。


虚拟语气:
If I had not rested, the journey would have enervated me completely. = 如果我没有休息，旅途会让我完全精疲力竭。
If he continued working, the pressure would enervate him. = 如果他继续工作，压力会使他衰弱。


被动语态:
He was enervated by the heat. = 他被炎热的天气弄得精疲力竭。
The soldiers were enervated by the long march. = 士兵们被长途行军累垮了。


疑问句:
Does this climate enervate you? = 这种气候会让你感到虚弱吗？
Will the long flight enervate the children? = 长途飞行会让孩子们感到疲惫吗？


否定句:
The cool breeze did not enervate them. = 凉爽的微风并没有使他们感到虚弱。
The exercise did not enervate her, but energized her. = 运动并没有使她虚弱，反而使她精力充沛。
