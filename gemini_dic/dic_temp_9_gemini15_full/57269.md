downplays: /ˈdaʊnpleɪz/ | vt. |轻描淡写，贬低；不重视 | To represent or treat (something) as less important than it really is.  |例句：The government downplayed the severity of the crisis. 政府轻描淡写了危机的严重性。

例句：He downplayed his role in the project. 他轻描淡写他在项目中的作用。


近义词：understate, minimize, belittle, underplay | 反义词：exaggerate, overstate, emphasize, highlight | 词性:动词

词源:
The word "downplay" is a relatively recent coinage,  formed by combining "down" (meaning to reduce or lessen) and "play" (in the sense of "treat" or "deal with").  Its usage reflects a shift in how we communicate, emphasizing the act of diminishing or minimizing something's importance.
