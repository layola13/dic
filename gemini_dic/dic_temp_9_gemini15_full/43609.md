crease:/kriːs/ | n., v.| 折痕；皱纹；褶；弄皱；起皱 | A crease is a line or mark made by folding or crumpling something.  It can refer to a fold in cloth, paper, or skin. As a verb, it means to make a crease in something or to become creased. | Examples:  The trousers had a sharp crease down the front. (这条裤子前面有一条清晰的折痕。)  He creased the paper in half. (他把纸对折。) |近义词：fold, wrinkle, rumple |反义词：smooth, ironed


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The iron removes creases from the shirt. = 熨斗能去除衬衫上的褶皱。
That fabric creases easily. = 那块布料很容易起皱。


一般过去时:
She creased the letter before putting it in the envelope. = 她把信折好后才放进信封。
The accident creased the car's fender. = 事故弄坏了汽车的挡泥板。


现在完成时:
I have creased my new trousers already! = 我已经把我的新裤子弄皱了！
The sun has creased her face over the years. = 岁月在她的脸上留下了皱纹。


过去完成时:
He had creased the map so many times it was almost torn. = 他把地图折了那么多次，几乎要撕破了。
She had creased the fabric before realizing it was silk. = 她把布料弄皱了才意识到那是丝绸。


将来时:
I will crease the paper carefully. = 我会小心地把纸折好。
The heat will crease the plastic. = 热量会使塑料起皱。


虚拟语气:
If I had been more careful, I wouldn't have creased the dress. = 如果我更小心一点，我就不会把衣服弄皱了。
I wish I hadn't creased the book cover. = 我真希望我没有弄皱书皮。


被动语态:
The fabric was creased by the heavy weight. = 布料被重物压皱了。
The photograph was carefully creased and folded. = 照片被仔细地折叠起来。


疑问句:
Has the heat creased the paint? = 热量把油漆弄皱了吗？
Did you crease the paper intentionally? = 你是故意把纸弄皱的吗？


否定句:
The material doesn't crease easily. = 这种材料不容易起皱。
He didn't crease the document. = 他没有弄皱文件。

词源:

The word "crease" comes from the Middle English word "cresen," meaning "to bend, curve."  This, in turn, likely derives from a Proto-Germanic root related to words meaning "to bend" or "to curve."
