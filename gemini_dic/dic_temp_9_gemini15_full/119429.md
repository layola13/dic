micrographic:/ˌmaɪkrəˈɡræfɪk/| adj.|显微摄影的；显微镜的；微型照相术的|Relating to or employing microphotography.  The term often refers to the process of reducing documents to a very small size for storage or transmission, or to the resulting images.  It can also describe things visible only under a microscope.|例句：The micrographic images were incredibly detailed. 显微照片细节非常丰富。

例句：The library uses a micrographic system to store its vast collection of historical documents. 图书馆使用缩微胶片系统来存储其大量的历史文献。


近义词：microphotographic, miniature


反义词：macroscopic, large-scale


词源:  From "micro-" (small) + "-graphic" (relating to writing or drawing).  The root "graph" comes from the Greek "graphein" (to write).
