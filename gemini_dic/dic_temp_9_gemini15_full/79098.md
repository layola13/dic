glad-handed: /ˈɡlædˌhændɪd/ | adj. | 虚情假意的；逢迎讨好的 | excessively and insincerely friendly and polite, especially to someone in power or someone who might be useful.  |  例句：He was glad-handed by the politicians hoping to secure his vote. 他被那些希望获得他选票的政客们虚情假意地奉承着。|近义词：insincere, obsequious, fawning, sycophantic |反义词：sincere, genuine, forthright

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The politician glad-hands the voters at every opportunity. = 这位政客抓住每一个机会逢迎选民。
She glad-hands her superiors to get ahead. = 她逢迎上司以求升迁。


一般过去时:
He glad-handed the CEO, hoping for a promotion. = 他逢迎首席执行官，希望能得到晋升。
The candidate glad-handed everyone in the room. = 候选人对屋里的每个人都虚情假意地表示友好。


现在完成时:
He has glad-handed his way to the top of the company. = 他通过逢迎拍马一路爬到了公司的高层。
She has glad-handed numerous clients to secure contracts. = 她逢迎了许多客户以确保合同。


过去完成时:
He had glad-handed the committee members before presenting his proposal. = 在提交提案之前，他已经对委员会成员们进行了逢迎。
They had glad-handed the investors, hoping to secure funding. = 他们之前已经逢迎了投资者，希望能获得资金。


将来时:
She will glad-hand the potential investors at the conference. = 她将在会议上逢迎潜在投资者。
He will glad-hand his way into the exclusive club. = 他将通过逢迎拍马的方式进入那个高级俱乐部。


虚拟语气:
If he glad-handed the board, maybe they'd approve the project. = 如果他逢迎董事会，也许他们会批准这个项目。
I wish he hadn't glad-handed his way to success. = 我真希望他没有通过逢迎拍马的方式获得成功。


被动语态:
The new employee was glad-handed by the entire team. = 新员工受到了整个团队的逢迎。
The proposal was presented after the speaker had glad-handed the audience. = 在演讲者逢迎听众之后，提案才被提交。


疑问句:

否定句:
He didn't glad-hand the voters; he spoke honestly. = 他没有逢迎选民；他说了实话。
She doesn't glad-hand her colleagues; she values genuine relationships. = 她不逢迎同事；她重视真诚的关系。
