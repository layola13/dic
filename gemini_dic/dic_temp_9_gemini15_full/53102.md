diphthongizations:/ˌdɪpθɒŋɡaɪˈzeɪʃənz/| n. |双元音化；变成双元音|The process by which a simple vowel sound becomes a diphthong.  This often occurs through phonetic changes over time,  where a single vowel sound develops into a glide from one vowel to another.  For example, the Great Vowel Shift in English involved many diphthongizations.|The pronunciation of "price" in modern English demonstrates a diphthongization from a historical monophthong.|近义词：vowel shift, diphthong formation|反义词：monophthongization
