haggard:/ˈhæɡərd/| adj.|憔悴的，消瘦的，(尤指因担忧或疲劳)|having a tired or worn appearance, especially from worry, lack of sleep, or illness.|例句：She looked haggard after weeks of sleepless nights.  她连续几周睡不好觉，看起来很憔悴。|例句：The long journey left him looking haggard and exhausted. 漫长的旅程使他看起来憔悴而疲惫。|近义词：gaunt, drawn, weary, emaciated|反义词：radiant, healthy, rested


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He looks haggard. = 他看起来很憔悴。
She always appears haggard after a long day at work. = 她每天下班后总是看起来很憔悴。

一般过去时:
He looked haggard after the accident. = 事故后他看起来很憔悴。
She appeared haggard when she heard the news. = 当她听到这个消息时，她看起来很憔悴。

现在完成时:
He has looked haggard for weeks. = 他已经憔悴了好几周了。
She has become haggard since the loss of her job. = 自从失业以来，她变得憔悴了。

过去完成时:
He had looked haggard before the operation. = 手术前他已经看起来很憔悴了。
She had become haggard before she finally sought help. = 在她最终寻求帮助之前，她已经变得很憔悴了。

将来时:
He will look haggard if he continues to work so hard. = 如果他继续这么努力工作，他会看起来很憔悴的。
She will probably look haggard after the marathon. = 马拉松比赛后，她可能会看起来很憔悴。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't worked so hard, he wouldn't look so haggard. = 如果他不那么努力工作，他就不会看起来这么憔悴了。
If she had slept more, she wouldn't have looked so haggard this morning. = 如果她睡得更多，今天早上她就不会看起来这么憔悴了。


被动语态: (haggard本身很少用被动语态)
His face was described as haggard in the newspaper report. = 报纸报道中形容他的脸很憔悴。
Her appearance was noted as haggard by everyone. = 每个人都注意到她的外表很憔悴。


疑问句:
Does he look haggard? = 他看起来很憔悴吗？
Does she appear haggard to you? = 她看起来很憔悴吗？


否定句:
He doesn't look haggard at all. = 他一点也不憔悴。
She didn't look haggard after her holiday. = 假期后她看起来并不憔悴。
