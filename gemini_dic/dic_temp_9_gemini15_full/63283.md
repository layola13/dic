enthroned:/ɪnˈθroʊnd/| vt. |加冕；使登基；使就位；|to place or establish someone in a position of power or authority, especially a throne; to elevate to a position of great importance or prominence.|例句：The new king was enthroned in a magnificent ceremony.  新的国王在一个盛大的仪式上加冕。|例句：Her talent and hard work have enthroned her as a leader in the field. 她的天赋和努力使她成为该领域的领导者。|近义词：install, inaugurate, invest, elevate |反义词：dethrone, depose

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They enthrone a new king every year. = 他们每年都拥立一位新国王。
The company enthrones customer satisfaction as its top priority. = 公司将客户满意度作为其首要任务。


一般过去时:
The queen was enthroned in 1970. = 女王于1970年加冕。
He was enthroned as the CEO last month. = 他上个月被任命为首席执行官。


现在完成时:

She has been enthroned as the undisputed champion. = 她已被确立为无可争议的冠军。


过去完成时:

They had enthroned him as their leader before the war broke out. = 在战争爆发之前，他们已经拥立他为他们的领导者。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

The principle of customer satisfaction has been enthroned as the cornerstone of our business. = 客户满意度的原则已被确立为我们业务的基石。
