constraint:/kənˈstreɪnt/| n. |约束；限制；强制 |A constraint is something that limits or restricts something or someone.  It can refer to a limitation on freedom of movement, action, or thought.  |例句：Budget constraints forced us to cut back on our spending. 预算限制迫使我们削减开支。

例句：The time constraint meant we had to rush the project. 时间限制意味着我们必须赶进度。


近义词：restriction, limitation, confinement, restraint |反义词：freedom, liberty, openness


词源：源自古法语 *constraint，来自拉丁语 *constringere，意为“拉紧，束缚”，由 *con-（一起）和 *stringere（拉紧）构成。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rules constrain our behaviour. = 规则约束着我们的行为。
Financial constraints limit our options. = 财政限制限制了我们的选择。


一般过去时:
The heavy rain constrained our outdoor activities. = 大雨限制了我们的户外活动。
Lack of resources constrained the company's growth. = 资源匮乏限制了公司的发展。


现在完成时:
Budget cuts have constrained our research efforts. = 预算削减已经限制了我们的研究工作。
Regulations have constrained the industry's practices. = 法规已经约束了该行业的惯例。


过去完成时:
The previous government's policies had constrained economic growth. = 前政府的政策已经限制了经济增长。
A lack of funding had constrained their ability to complete the project. = 资金不足已经限制了他们完成项目的能力。


将来时:
Increased regulations will constrain business expansion. = 加强的法规将限制企业扩张。
Future budget cuts will constrain our ability to hire new staff. = 未来预算削减将限制我们招聘新员工的能力。


虚拟语气:
If there were no constraints, we could achieve more. = 如果没有限制，我们可以取得更多成就。
I wish there were fewer constraints on our creativity. = 我希望我们创造力的限制更少。


被动语态:
Our progress was constrained by unforeseen circumstances. = 我们的进展受到不可预见的情况的限制。
The project was constrained by a lack of funding. = 该项目因缺乏资金而受限。


疑问句:
What constraints are there on this project? = 这个项目有什么限制？
Are there any constraints we should be aware of? = 我们应该注意哪些限制？


否定句:
There are no constraints on the use of this software. = 使用此软件没有任何限制。
The company is not constrained by traditional methods. = 公司不受传统方法的约束。
