manpower: /ˈmænˌpaʊər/ | n. |人力，劳动力 |The company's manpower is its most valuable asset.  这家公司的人力资源是其最宝贵的资产。|近义词：workforce, personnel, labor |反义词：automation

英文解析: Manpower refers to the available supply of people who can be used to do work. It often refers to the total number of people available for work within an organization or a country.  The term emphasizes the human element in production and services.

例句：

The project requires a significant manpower increase. 这个项目需要大幅增加人力。

We need to boost our manpower to meet the deadline. 我们需要增加人力以赶上最后期限。


词源:  The word is a compound of "man" and "power," reflecting the idea of human beings as a source of power or capability.  Its usage developed in the early 20th century, reflecting the increasing industrialization and the need to quantify the workforce.
