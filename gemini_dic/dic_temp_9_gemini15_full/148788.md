overvaliantly: /ˌoʊvərˈvæliəntli/ | adv. | 过于勇敢地，过于英勇地 | Excessively or boastfully valiant; with excessive bravery.  It implies a show of bravery that might be excessive or even foolhardy. | 例句：He charged overvaliantly into the enemy lines, almost single-handedly. 他过于勇敢地冲进敌阵，几乎是单枪匹马。


The knight fought overvaliantly, but his reckless courage led to his downfall.  这位骑士战斗得过于英勇，但他的鲁莽勇气导致了他的失败。| 近义词：bravely, courageously, gallantly, recklessly, foolhardily | 反义词：cowardly, timidly, cautiously


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't have verb conjugations like the example "what".  It modifies verbs, describing how an action is performed.

例句：

He acted overvaliantly in the face of danger. 他面对危险时表现得过于勇敢。

She charged overvaliantly towards the burning building to rescue the cat. 她过于勇敢地冲向燃烧的建筑物去救那只猫。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "overvaliantly".  Its usage is generally as a single adverb modifying a verb describing an action.
