hackberry:/ˈhækˌbɛri/ | n. | 构树；美国朴树 | A hackberry is a tree of the genus Celtis, which includes various species of deciduous trees found in temperate regions.  They are known for their rough bark, small, sweet berries, and relatively fast growth.  The wood is often used for inexpensive items. | Example: The birds loved the hackberries growing in the park.  鸟儿喜欢公园里生长的构树。

The old fence post was made of hackberry.  那根旧栅栏柱是用构树做的。


近义词：Celtis (genus name), nettle tree | 反义词：无


词源:  The name "hackberry" is believed to be of uncertain origin. Some etymologists suggest a connection to the Old English word "hac," meaning "hook," referring to the hooked branches of some species.  Others propose a connection to "hack," referring to the rough bark. The "-berry" suffix clearly indicates the tree's fruit.
