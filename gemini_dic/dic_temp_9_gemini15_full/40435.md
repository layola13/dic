contrast:/ˈkɒntrɑːst/ /ˈkɑːntræst/| n., v.|对比，对照；使对比；与…形成对比|

英文解析:
As a noun, "contrast" refers to a noticeable difference between people, things, or ideas, especially when they are placed close together or compared. As a verb, "contrast" means to compare two or more things in order to show their differences, or to show a difference between things.

例句:
The contrast between their two personalities was striking. = 他们两人性格的差异非常显著。

There is a sharp contrast between rich and poor in this city. = 这个城市贫富差距悬殊。


The artist skillfully contrasted light and shadow in the painting. = 艺术家在绘画中巧妙地对比了光影。

His behaviour contrasted sharply with his usual politeness. = 他的行为与他平时彬彬有礼的举止形成鲜明对比。


近义词:difference, opposition, disparity, comparison


反义词:similarity, resemblance, likeness


词性:Noun, Verb
