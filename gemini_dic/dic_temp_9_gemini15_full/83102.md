gust:/ɡʌst/ | n. |一阵风，一阵强风；一阵（感情等）| A sudden, strong rush of wind; a sudden outburst of emotion or activity. |例句：A gust of wind blew my hat off.一阵风吹掉了我的帽子。

例句：A sudden gust of anger swept over him.一阵怒火涌上心头。

近义词：blast, flurry, squall | 反义词：calm, breeze


词源:

源自古法语 *gust,  最终来自日耳曼语系，与古高地德语 *gust "吹，冲击"有关。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
A gust of wind blows the leaves around. = 一阵风吹得树叶四处飞舞。
The wind gusts frequently in this area. = 这个地区经常刮大风。

一般过去时:
A strong gust of wind knocked down a tree. = 一阵强风吹倒了一棵树。
A sudden gust of emotion overwhelmed her. = 她被突如其来的情绪所淹没。

现在完成时:
Strong gusts of wind have damaged the crops. = 强风已经损坏了庄稼。
Several gusts of anger have erupted throughout the meeting. = 会议期间爆发了几次愤怒。


过去完成时:
Before the storm arrived, several gusts of wind had already rattled the windows. = 暴风雨来临之前，几阵狂风已经震动了窗户。
By the time the argument ended, several gusts of frustration had passed between them. = 争吵结束时，他们之间已经爆发了几次沮丧。


将来时:
The forecast predicts strong gusts of wind tomorrow. = 预报明天将有强风。
I anticipate that gusts of excitement will follow the announcement. = 我预料宣布后将会有阵阵兴奋。


虚拟语气:
If there had been a stronger gust of wind, the boat would have capsized. = 如果风再大一些，船就会翻了。
If I had known she was so sensitive, I wouldn't have let such a gust of criticism out. = 如果我知道她这么敏感，我不会说出那么尖刻的批评。

被动语态:
The small boat was overturned by a sudden gust of wind. = 小船被一阵突如其来的强风掀翻了。
His reputation was damaged by a gust of negative publicity. = 他的名声被一阵负面宣传所损害。


疑问句:
Was that a gust of wind I felt? = 我刚才感觉到的是一阵风吗？
Was that a gust of emotion, or was I simply imagining things? = 那是一阵情绪波动，还是我仅仅是胡思乱想？


否定句:
There wasn't a gust of wind all day. = 一整天都没有一阵风。
There wasn't a gust of anger in his voice. = 他的声音里没有一丝怒气。
