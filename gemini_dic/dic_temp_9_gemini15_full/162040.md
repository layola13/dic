pre-adjective:/priːˈædʒɪktɪv/| adj.|前置形容词|A pre-adjective is a word that comes before an adjective to modify or intensify its meaning.  It often functions as an adverb but is placed before the adjective rather than the verb.  Pre-adjectives are not a formally defined grammatical category in the same way as adjectives or adverbs, but are rather a descriptive term for words used in this way.|例句：The extremely talented artist painted a stunning masterpiece. (extremely modifies the adjective talented)|近义词：modifying adverb, intensifying word|反义词：N/A

虚拟语气:
If he were truly honest, he would admit his mistake. = 如果他真的诚实，他会承认自己的错误。(truly modifies the adjective honest)
I wish she were more openly supportive of my efforts. = 我希望她能更公开地支持我的努力。(more modifies the adjective openly supportive)


被动语态:
The project was exceptionally well-managed. = 这个项目管理得非常好。(exceptionally modifies the adjective well-managed)
The painting was beautifully restored. = 这幅画被修复得非常漂亮。(beautifully modifies the adjective restored)


疑问句:

否定句:

She was not overly concerned about the delay. = 她对延误并不太担心。(overly modifies the adjective concerned)
