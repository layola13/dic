inducing:/ɪnˈduːsɪŋ/ | v. | 引起；导致；诱导 | To persuade or influence (someone) to do something. To bring about or cause (a particular reaction, state, or phenomenon). |例句：The doctor induced labor to help deliver the baby. 医生催产以帮助分娩。

The warm weather induced a sense of relaxation.  温暖的天气带来一种轻松的感觉。

近义词：cause, bring about, provoke, stimulate, instigate | 反义词：prevent, hinder, deter, discourage


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The medicine induces sleep. = 这药催眠。
Stress induces heart problems. = 压力会导致心脏问题。


一般过去时:
The advertisement induced many people to buy the product. = 这则广告诱使许多人购买该产品。
The evidence induced the jury to convict the defendant. = 证据促使陪审团判定被告有罪。


现在完成时:
His speech has induced a change in public opinion. = 他的演讲已引起公众舆论的变化。
The new policy has induced a significant increase in productivity. = 新政策已导致生产力的显著提高。


过去完成时:
The earlier events had induced a feeling of unease among the participants. = 早期事件已在参与者中引起不安感。
The previous drought had induced a severe water shortage. = 先前的干旱已造成严重缺水。


将来时:
The upcoming election will induce significant political changes. = 即将到来的选举将带来重大的政治变化。
The new technology will induce a revolution in the industry. = 新技术将引发该行业的革命。


虚拟语气:
If the experiment induced the desired result, we would publish the findings. = 如果实验产生了预期的结果，我们将发表研究结果。
I wish the treatment had induced a faster recovery. = 我希望治疗能更快地恢复。


被动语态:
Sleep was induced by the medication. = 睡眠是由药物引起的。
A sense of calm was induced by the music. = 音乐带来一种平静的感觉。


疑问句:
What induced you to make that decision? = 什么促使你做出那个决定？
What factors induced the economic downturn? = 哪些因素导致了经济衰退？


否定句:
The treatment did not induce any noticeable improvement. = 该治疗没有引起任何明显的改善。
The advertisement failed to induce sales. = 广告未能促进销售。


PHRASE->

induce labor = 催产

induce current = 感应电流
induce hypnosis = 催眠
