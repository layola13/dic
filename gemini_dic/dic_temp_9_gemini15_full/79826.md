glycosuria:/ˌɡlaɪkoʊˈsjʊəriə/| n. |糖尿；尿糖症|The presence of glucose in the urine, typically indicative of diabetes mellitus.  It is a symptom, not a disease itself.  The condition can be caused by various factors, including uncontrolled diabetes, pancreatic disease, hormonal imbalances, pregnancy, or kidney damage.| Example:  The doctor ordered a urine test to check for glycosuria. 医生开了尿检来检查是否有尿糖。


The patient's high blood sugar levels led to glycosuria. 病人的高血糖导致了尿糖。|hyperglycemia,glucosuria|normoglycemia|名词
