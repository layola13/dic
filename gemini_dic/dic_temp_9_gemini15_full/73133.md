foragers:/ˈfɒrɪdʒəz/|/ˈfɔːrɪdʒərz/| n. |觅食者，采集者 |People or animals that search for food.  Foragers actively seek out and gather food, often over a wide area, rather than cultivating or raising it. They play a crucial role in ecosystems. |例句：Many bird species are foragers, constantly searching for insects and seeds. 许多鸟类都是觅食者，不断寻找昆虫和种子。

例句：The early humans were skilled foragers, adapting their diets to the available resources. 早期人类是熟练的觅食者，他们根据可获得的资源调整饮食。


近义词：gatherers, hunters, scavengers |反义词：farmers, cultivators

词源:
From Old French *forager, from Old North French forre "fodder, forage," from Vulgar Latin *fodrum ("fodder").  The meaning evolved from "to feed (animals)" to "to search for food."
