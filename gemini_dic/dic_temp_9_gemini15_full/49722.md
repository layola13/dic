democratising: /dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzɪŋ/ | /dɪˈmɑːkrətaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使民主化；普及；大众化 | The process of making something accessible to a wider range of people, typically by reducing costs or removing barriers.  | 例句：The internet is democratising access to information.  互联网正在使信息获取民主化。

例句：The government's policies are aimed at democratising education. 政府的政策旨在使教育民主化。


近义词：popularising, spreading, equalising, liberalising

反义词：centralising, concentrating, restricting, elitising

词性：动词(现在分词)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government is democratising the healthcare system. = 政府正在使医疗体系民主化。
This technology is democratising access to education. = 这项技术正在使教育普及化。

一般过去时:
The reforms democratised the political process. = 改革使政治进程民主化。
The invention of the printing press democratised access to books. = 印刷术的发明使书籍普及化。

现在完成时:
The internet has democratised communication. = 互联网已经使交流民主化。
Technological advancements have democratised many aspects of life. = 技术进步已经使生活的许多方面民主化。

过去完成时:
Before the reforms, the education system hadn't been democratised. = 在改革之前，教育体系没有实现民主化。
They hadn't realised how much the internet had democratised information access. = 他们没有意识到互联网已经使信息获取多么民主化。

将来时:
This new policy will democratise access to healthcare. = 这项新政策将使医疗保健普及化。
Future technological advances will further democratise information. = 未来技术进步将进一步使信息民主化。

虚拟语气:
If they implemented this policy, it would democratise the legal system. = 如果他们实施这项政策，它将使法律体系民主化。
I wish they would democratise the process of applying for visas. = 我希望他们能使签证申请流程民主化。

被动语态:
The education system is being democratised through new initiatives. = 教育体系正在通过新的举措实现民主化。
Access to information is being democratised by the widespread use of the internet. = 通过互联网的广泛使用，信息获取正在民主化。

疑问句:
How can we democratise the decision-making process? = 我们如何才能使决策过程民主化？
Is the government really committed to democratising education? = 政府真的致力于使教育民主化吗？

否定句:
The current system does not democratise access to resources. = 当前的系统并未使资源获取民主化。
The government failed to democratise the legal system effectively. = 政府未能有效地使法律体系民主化。
