sale-leaseback:/ˈseɪlˌliːsˌbæk/| n. | 售后回租 | A financial transaction where a company sells an asset (usually property or equipment) to an investor and simultaneously leases it back from the investor.  This allows the company to raise capital by freeing up the asset's value while retaining the use of the asset. | Example: The company used a sale-leaseback arrangement to raise funds for expansion, selling its headquarters building and leasing it back for a period of 10 years. | 近义词: leveraged lease, asset sale with leaseback | 反义词:  direct purchase, outright sale
