screen-tested:/ˈskriːnˌtɛstɪd/| adj.|试镜过的；通过试镜的|Having undergone a screen test, typically for a film or television role.  This adjective describes someone who has participated in a screen test, a process used to evaluate an actor's suitability for a role.|例句：She was screen-tested for the lead role but didn't get the part. 她试镜了女主角的角色，但没得到这个角色。|例句：The director was impressed by the screen-tested actors’ performances. 导演对试镜演员的表现印象深刻。|近义词：auditioned, tested|反义词：untested, unauditioned


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is screen-tested for the new movie. 他正在为新电影试镜。
They are screen-tested every week. 他们每周都进行试镜。

一般过去时:
She was screen-tested last week. 她上周参加了试镜。
He was screen-tested for the role of Hamlet. 他试镜了哈姆雷特的角色。

现在完成时:
They have been screen-tested for several roles. 他们已经为几个角色试镜了。
She has been screen-tested by many directors. 她已经被许多导演试镜了。

过去完成时:
He had been screen-tested before the final decision was made. 在最终决定做出之前，他已经试镜过了。
She had been screen-tested numerous times, without success. 她已经试镜过很多次了，但都没有成功。

将来时:
They will be screen-tested next month.他们下个月将进行试镜。
She will be screen-tested for the upcoming drama. 她将为即将上映的电视剧试镜。


虚拟语气:
If he were screen-tested, he would get the part. 如果他试镜的话，他会得到这个角色。(与事实相反)
If she had been screen-tested earlier, she might have got the role. 如果她早点试镜的话，也许会得到这个角色。(与过去事实相反)

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

They haven't been screen-tested this year. 他们今年还没有试镜。
