nonfundamentalist: /ˌnɑːnˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪst/ | adj. |非原教旨主义的|  Describing someone or something that does not adhere to strict or literal interpretations of religious doctrine, particularly in Christianity, but applicable to other religions as well. It suggests a more moderate, liberal, or progressive approach to faith. |例句：The nonfundamentalist preacher emphasized compassion and social justice.  非原教旨主义的牧师强调同情和社会正义。

The nonfundamentalist approach to biblical interpretation allows for a wider range of perspectives.  非原教旨主义的圣经解释方法允许更广泛的视角。


近义词：liberal, moderate, progressive, ecumenical, broad-minded |反义词：fundamentalist, literalist, orthodox, conservative


词源:  The word is a combination of "non-" (meaning "not") and "fundamentalist."  "Fundamentalist" originates from "fundamental," meaning "basic" or "essential," referring to a belief system based on a literal interpretation of fundamental religious texts.  The suffix "-ist" denotes a person or adherent to a particular belief system.  Therefore, "nonfundamentalist" signifies someone who does not adhere to such a literal or strict interpretation.
