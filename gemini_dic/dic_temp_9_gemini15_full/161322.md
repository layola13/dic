postponing: /pəˈspoʊnɪŋ/ | v. | 推迟，延期 |  The act of delaying or deferring something to a later time.  It is the present participle of the verb "postpone". |

例句：
They are postponing the meeting until next week. = 他们将会议推迟到下周。

The game was postponed due to bad weather. = 比赛由于恶劣天气而被推迟。


近义词：deferring, delaying, putting off, adjourning

反义词：expediting, hastening, accelerating


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We are postponing the trip. = 我们正在推迟旅行。
They postpone meetings frequently. = 他们经常推迟会议。


一般过去时:
We postponed the party. = 我们推迟了派对。
He postponed his decision. = 他推迟了他的决定。


现在完成时:
We have postponed the launch several times. = 我们已经推迟了产品发布几次。
They have postponed the project indefinitely. = 他们无限期地推迟了这个项目。


过去完成时:
We had postponed the meeting before the email arrived. = 在邮件到达之前，我们已经推迟了会议。
They had postponed their vacation due to the pandemic. = 由于疫情，他们已经推迟了他们的假期。


将来时:
We will postpone the concert if it rains. = 如果下雨，我们将推迟音乐会。
They will postpone the announcement until Monday. = 他们将把公告推迟到星期一。


虚拟语气:
If I had known, I would have postponed the appointment. = 如果我知道的话，我就已经推迟了预约。
I wish I had postponed my travel plans. = 我真希望我推迟了我的旅行计划。


被动语态:
The meeting was postponed. = 会议被推迟了。
The project is being postponed. = 项目正在被推迟。


疑问句:
Is the deadline being postponed? = 截止日期会被推迟吗？
Will the game be postponed? = 比赛会被推迟吗？


否定句:
We did not postpone the event. = 我们没有推迟活动。
They will not postpone their departure. = 他们不会推迟他们的离开。


PHRASE->
postponing the inevitable = 推迟不可避免的事情
postponing a decision = 推迟决定
postponing a meeting = 推迟会议
postponing a trip = 推迟旅行
postponing action = 推迟行动


词源:
From post- + pone (to put).  The prefix "post-" indicates "after" or "behind," and "pone" is from Latin, meaning "to put" or "to place."  Therefore, "postpone" literally means "to put after."
