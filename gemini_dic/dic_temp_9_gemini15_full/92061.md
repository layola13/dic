/ˌhaɪpəʊˌprɒlæktɪˈniːmiə/ | n. | 高泌乳素血症 | Hyperprolactinaemia is a condition characterized by abnormally high levels of prolactin in the blood.  It can be caused by various factors, including pituitary tumors, medications, and certain medical conditions. Symptoms can include irregular menstrual cycles, infertility, galactorrhea (breast milk production in non-breastfeeding individuals), and decreased libido.  |  The patient presented with hyperprolactinaemia, leading to a thorough investigation of her pituitary gland.  |  Prolactinoma,  |  Hypoprolactinemia | 名词
