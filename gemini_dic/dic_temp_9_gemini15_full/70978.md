firer:/ˈfaɪərər/ | n. | 开除者；解雇者；点火者 | A person who fires someone from a job or dismisses someone from a position; someone who starts a fire. |例句：The firer was accused of unfair dismissal.  开除者被指控不公平解雇。 |例句：The firer quickly extinguished the small fire he'd started.  点火者迅速扑灭了他点燃的小火。 |近义词：dismisser, dismissal, employer (in context of firing), igniter |反义词：employee, hiree


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The manager fires employees who are consistently late. = 经理解雇那些总是迟到的员工。
The arsonist fires buildings in the dead of night. = 纵火犯在深夜纵火烧毁建筑物。


一般过去时:
He fired his assistant for incompetence. = 他因为不称职而解雇了他的助理。
She fired the gun and missed her target.  = 她开枪了，但没打中目标。


现在完成时:
The company has fired several employees this year. = 公司今年已经解雇了几名员工。
They have fired several warning shots before opening fire. = 在开火之前，他们已经鸣了几枪示警。


过去完成时:
He had fired the gun before anyone realized what was happening. = 在任何人意识到发生了什么事之前，他已经开枪了。
The factory had fired its entire workforce before the bankruptcy. = 破产之前，工厂已经解雇了所有员工。


将来时:
She will fire him if he continues to be disruptive. = 如果他继续捣乱，她就会解雇他。
The soldiers will fire upon the enemy at dawn. = 士兵们将在黎明时向敌人开火。


虚拟语气:
If I were the boss, I would fire him immediately. = 如果我是老板，我会立即解雇他。
If he had fired sooner, the damage would have been less. = 如果他早点开火，损失就会小一些。


被动语态:
He was fired for stealing company property. = 他因偷窃公司财产而被解雇。
The gun was fired accidentally. = 枪是意外走火的。


疑问句:
Who fired the gun? = 谁开的枪？
Will the company fire more workers? = 公司还会解雇更多工人吗？


否定句:
The manager didn't fire anyone this month. = 经理这个月没有解雇任何人。
He didn't fire the gun intentionally. = 他不是故意开枪的。


PHRASE->
fire someone = 解雇某人
fire a gun = 开枪
fire up = 激励；点燃（热情）
fire away = 开始吧；尽管说
under fire = 受到攻击；批评
