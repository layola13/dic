misadaptations:/ˌmɪsˌædəpˈteɪʃənz/| n. |不良适应，适应不良|Features that are disadvantageous to an organism's survival and reproduction, often arising from genetic mutations or environmental changes. These can lead to reduced fitness and potentially extinction.  |Example: The peppered moth's dark coloration, initially an advantage in polluted environments, became a misadaptation when pollution levels decreased.  The dark moths were easier for predators to spot against lighter tree bark. |近义词：maladaptations, evolutionary disadvantages |反义词：adaptations
