cryohydrate:/ˈkraɪoʊhaɪdreɪt/| n. |低温水合物|A cryohydrate is an ice-like substance formed when water freezes in the presence of another substance, typically a gas or small molecule, which becomes trapped within the ice crystal lattice.  These substances are also known as clathrate hydrates. They are stable at low temperatures and high pressures, and are often found in permafrost regions and deep ocean sediments.|例句：Methane cryohydrates are a potential source of energy, but their extraction poses significant challenges. 甲烷低温水合物是一种潜在的能源，但其开采面临巨大挑战。
The formation of cryohydrates in pipelines can lead to blockages. 管道中低温水合物的形成会导致堵塞。|近义词：clathrate hydrate, gas hydrate|反义词：无
