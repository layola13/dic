fantasise:/ˈfæntəsaɪz/| v.|幻想；梦想；凭空想象|To imagine or daydream about something, especially something pleasant or exciting.  It often implies a degree of escapism or a lack of grounding in reality.|例句：I sometimes fantasise about winning the lottery. 我有时会幻想中彩票。

例句：She spent hours fantasising about her ideal wedding. 她花了好几个小时幻想她理想中的婚礼。


近义词：dream, daydream, imagine, envision

反义词：realize, achieve, accomplish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fantasises about travelling the world. 他幻想环游世界。
I fantasise about living in a big house. 我幻想住在一栋大房子里。


一般过去时:
She fantasised about becoming a famous actress. 她曾幻想成为一名著名的女演员。
They fantasised about escaping to a tropical island. 他们曾幻想逃到一个热带岛屿。


现在完成时:
I have fantasised about this moment for years. 我多年来一直幻想这一刻。
She has fantasised about meeting her favourite celebrity. 她一直幻想见到她最喜欢的名人。


过去完成时:
He had fantasised about quitting his job for months. 他几个月来一直幻想辞职。
They had fantasised about owning a yacht before they won the lottery. 在他们中彩票之前，他们一直幻想拥有一艘游艇。


将来时:
I will fantasise about my future career. 我将幻想我未来的职业。
She will fantasise about her upcoming vacation. 她将幻想她即将到来的假期。


虚拟语气:
If I could fantasise about anything, I would choose to fly. 如果我可以幻想任何事情，我会选择飞行。
I wish I could fantasise more freely. 我希望我能更自由地幻想。


被动语态:  (Fantasise is rarely used passively)
The scenario was fantasised about by many people.  (This is awkward and unnatural)  许多人对这个场景进行了幻想。（这句话很别扭，不自然）
The possibilities were often fantasised about by the children.  孩子们常常幻想各种可能性。（这句话也略显不自然）


疑问句:
Do you often fantasise about your future? 你经常幻想你的未来吗？
What do you fantasise about most often? 你最常幻想什么？


否定句:
He doesn't fantasise about unrealistic things. 他不会幻想不切实际的事情。
I don't fantasise about things that are impossible. 我不会幻想不可能的事情。



PHRASE->
fantasise about: 幻想…
fantasise wildly: 胡思乱想
fantasise endlessly:没完没了地幻想
fantasise freely: 自由地幻想
daydream and fantasise: 白日梦和幻想


词源:
The word "fantasise" comes from the French word "fantasie," meaning "fantasy," which in turn comes from the Italian word "fantasia."  Ultimately, its roots trace back to the Greek word "phantasia," meaning "imagination" or "apparition."
