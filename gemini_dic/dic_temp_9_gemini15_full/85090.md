hardballing:/ˈhɑːrdbɔːlɪŋ/| v. |强硬谈判；强硬手段；强硬作风|To engage in aggressive and uncompromising negotiations or tactics.  It often involves using threats or intimidation to achieve one's goals.  |例句：The union is hardballing in the contract negotiations. 工会在合同谈判中采取强硬手段。

例句：He was accused of hardballing his opponents during the election campaign. 他被指控在竞选期间对对手采取强硬手段。


近义词：strong-arming, bullying, tough negotiating, playing hardball


反义词：compromising, negotiating fairly, appeasing


词性:动词
