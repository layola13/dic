drenchers:/ˈdrɛntʃərz/| n.| 浸湿者；浇灌者 |Someone or something that drenches.  The term is rarely used alone and usually appears in phrases like "heavy drenchers" to describe something that causes heavy soaking. |例句：The heavy drenchers of the monsoon season flooded the low-lying areas.  季风季节的大雨淹没了低洼地区。  |近义词：soakers, saturators |反义词：driers

一般现在时:
The sprinklers drench the lawn every morning. = 喷水器每天早上都会浇灌草坪。
Heavy rain drenchers the crops. = 大雨浇灌着庄稼。

一般过去时:
The storm drenched the city. = 暴风雨使城市浸透了。
The farmer drenched the field with water. = 农民用大量的水浇灌了田地。

现在完成时:
The continuous rain has drenched the ground. = 持续的降雨已经浸透了地面。
The heavy showers have drenched my clothes. = 倾盆大雨把我的衣服都淋湿了。

过去完成时:
The ground was already drenched because it had drenched the previous night. = 地面已经湿透了，因为前一天晚上就已经下过大雨了。
The clothes were drenched because it had drenched earlier. = 衣服湿透了，因为之前下过大雨。

将来时:
The forecast predicts that heavy rain will drench the region tomorrow. = 预报预测明天该地区将会有大雨倾盆而下。
I think the upcoming storm will drench the entire city. = 我想即将到来的暴风雨会把整个城市都淋湿。

虚拟语气:
If it drenched tomorrow, we would cancel the picnic. = 如果明天下雨，我们将取消野餐。
I wish it hadn't drenched so much yesterday. = 我希望昨天不要下这么大的雨。

被动语态:
The field was drenched by the rain. = 田地被雨水淋湿了。
The city was drenched by the unexpected downpour. = 城市被突如其来的暴雨淋湿了。

疑问句:
Was the garden drenched by the rain? = 花园被雨淋湿了吗？
Were the hikers drenched by the sudden shower? = 登山者被突如其来的阵雨淋湿了吗？

否定句:
The rain didn't drench the garden. = 雨没有淋湿花园。
The sun didn't drench the field. = 太阳没有把田地晒干。
