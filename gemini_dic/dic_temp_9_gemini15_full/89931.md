horseflies: /ˈhɔːrsflaɪz/ | n. | 厩蝇；牛虻 | Large flies that bite horses and other animals, and sometimes humans.  They are known for their painful bites. |  例句：The horseflies were swarming around the horses in the pasture.  厩蝇在牧场里围绕着马群嗡嗡飞舞。

例句：We had to wear protective clothing to avoid the horseflies during our hike.  我们在徒步旅行中不得不穿上防护服以避免厩蝇叮咬。


近义词：gadflies, breeze flies


反义词：None directly applicable. (The antonym would relate to a creature that doesn't bite, which is too broad.)


词性：名词

词源:
The word "horsefly" is a compound word.  "Horse" refers to the animal they often bite, and "fly" refers to the insect.  The etymology of "fly" traces back to Old English *flēoge, from Proto-Germanic *fleugō.  The meaning and usage have remained consistent over time.
