cormorant: /ˈkɔːrmərænt/ | n. | 鸬鹚 | A large, dark-colored bird with a long neck, hooked bill, and webbed feet, that is a skilled swimmer and diver, often found near the sea. Cormorants are known for their ability to catch fish underwater. |  Example:  The cormorant plunged into the water and emerged with a wriggling fish in its beak.  这条鸬鹚潜入水中，叼着一条扭动的鱼浮出水面。  |  近义词: shag, darter | 反义词: None specifically applicable.
