flip-flop:/ˈflɪpˌflɑːp/| n. v.|人字拖；轻微的移动或改变；突然改变主意；|A flip-flop is a type of sandal with a thong between the big toe and the second toe.  It can also refer to a sudden or unexpected change, especially of opinion or policy.|例句：I bought a new pair of flip-flops for the beach. 我买了一双新的海滩人字拖。
He flip-flopped on his decision to run for office. 他改变了竞选的决定。|近义词：sandal, shift, change, vacillate|反义词：consistency, steadfastness|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flips his flip-flops while walking. = 他走路时啪嗒啪嗒地响。
They often flip-flop between different opinions. = 他们经常在不同的意见之间摇摆不定。


一般过去时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
