dipsy:/ˈdɪpsi/| adj. n.| 傻乎乎的；笨手笨脚的；（尤指）醉醺醺的 |  Describes someone who is clumsy, silly, or drunk, often in a playful or endearing way.  The word often implies a slight, endearing silliness rather than outright stupidity. |例句：He was a bit dipsy after too much beer. 他喝太多啤酒后有点傻乎乎的。  The puppy was dipsy-doodle, tumbling over its own paws. 小狗笨手笨脚的，跌倒在自己的爪子上。 |近义词：clumsy, goofy, tipsy, silly |反义词：clever, graceful, sober


USAGE->
This word doesn't have a verb form, and it's primarily an adjective used informally.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  The usage is primarily descriptive.

例句1: The dipsy dancer stumbled on the stage.  (傻乎乎的舞者在舞台上绊倒了。)
例句2: She gave a dipsy smile. (她露出了傻乎乎的笑容。)


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "dipsy".  However, "dipsy-doodle," while not strictly a phrase, is used informally as a playful intensifier to describe clumsiness or silliness.

例句1:  The children were being dipsy-doodle. (孩子们很调皮。)
例句2: He acted dipsy-doodle all afternoon. (他整个下午都表现得很笨拙。)
