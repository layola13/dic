pye-dog:/paɪdɒɡ/| n. |派狗，牧羊犬|A pye-dog is a type of dog, often a mixed breed, historically used for herding sheep and other livestock.  The term is primarily used in dialects of England, particularly in the southwest.  The etymology is uncertain, but it might be related to the piebald coloring of some such dogs. |The farmer used his trusty pye-dog to keep the sheep from straying.  那个农民用他忠实的派狗来防止羊群走散。|近义词：sheepdog, herding dog |反义词：None specifically; could be contrasted with dogs not used for herding.
