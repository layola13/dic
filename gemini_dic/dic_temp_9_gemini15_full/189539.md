salarymen: /ˈsælərimen/ | n. | 工薪阶层男性 | A salaryman is a man who works for a company and receives a regular salary.  The term is commonly used in East Asia, particularly Japan, to describe men who work long hours and dedicate their lives to their careers, often at the expense of their personal lives.  They are typically employed in white-collar jobs and are often perceived as being somewhat robotic or lacking individuality due to the pressures of their jobs. |  He's a typical salaryman, working long hours and commuting every day. 他是个典型的上班族，每天工作很长时间，每天上下班通勤。 Many salarymen in Japan work overtime regularly.  许多在日本的上班族经常加班。| businessman, office worker, white-collar worker | freelancer, entrepreneur, self-employed
