mandibles:/ˈmændɪbəlz/ | n. |上颚；下颚；（昆虫的）大颚 | paired mouthparts of an insect or similar arthropod, typically used for seizing and manipulating food.  The term can also refer to the jawbone in vertebrates. |例句：The praying mantis uses its powerful mandibles to capture its prey.  螳螂用它强壮的大颚捕捉猎物。

The ant's mandibles are strong enough to lift objects many times its own weight.  蚂蚁的大颚足够强壮，可以举起比自身重许多倍的物体。


近义词：jaws, pincers, forceps |反义词：None (antonym depends on the context;  for example, if referring to a specific function, the antonym would describe the opposite function, such as "proboscis" for sucking instead of biting).
