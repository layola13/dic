obstructionist:/əbˈstrʌkʃənɪst/| adj. n. |阻挠者；妨碍者；故意阻挠的|One who deliberately hinders or obstructs progress, especially in politics or legislation.  An obstructionist tactic is one designed to delay or prevent action. |例句：The senator was accused of being an obstructionist, delaying crucial legislation.  参议员被指控为阻挠者，拖延了关键立法。

The opposition party employed obstructionist tactics to prevent the bill from passing. 反对党采取了阻挠策略，阻止该法案通过。|近义词：blocker, hinderer, impediment, saboteur |反义词：facilitator, supporter, collaborator

词源:

From "obstruct" + "-ionist".  "Obstruct" comes from the Latin *obstruere, from *ob- ("against") + *struere ("to build, pile up").  The suffix "-ionist" indicates a person who performs or is associated with a particular action.
