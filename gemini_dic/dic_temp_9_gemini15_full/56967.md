double-blind: /ˈdʌbəl blaɪnd/ | adj. | 双盲的，双盲试验的 |  Describes an experiment or trial in which neither the participants nor the researchers know who is receiving the treatment and who is receiving a placebo until after the results are analyzed. This helps to eliminate bias. | Example: The drug trial was conducted as a double-blind study to minimize bias.  双盲试验旨在最大限度地减少偏差。 |近义词：controlled, unbiased | 反义词：open, single-blind
