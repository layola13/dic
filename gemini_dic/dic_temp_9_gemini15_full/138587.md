nubble:/ˈnʌbəl/ | n. |小瘤；小块；粗糙部分 | A small lump or protuberance on a surface; a knob.  The term often refers to small, hard bumps, particularly on a relatively smooth surface.  It can also describe a small, undeveloped or immature growth. |例句：The surface of the fabric had tiny nubbules of texture.  = 这块布料的表面有很多细小的纹理小疙瘩。

The sculptor carefully smoothed away the nubbules on the clay figure. = 雕塑家仔细地将粘土塑像上的小瘤磨平。


近义词：knob, nodule, bump, protuberance | 反义词：smoothness, flatness | 词性:noun


词源:  The word "nubble" likely originates from the Middle English word "nob," meaning "a lump or knot," which itself may have roots in Old English or a related Germanic language. The suffix "-le" added a diminutive sense, making "nubble" specifically refer to a small lump.
