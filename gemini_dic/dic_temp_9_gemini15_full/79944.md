gnosticism:/ˈnɒstɪsɪzəm/ | n. |诺斯替主义|A collection of religious ideas and beliefs that flourished in the late 1st and early 2nd centuries CE, emphasizing secret knowledge (gnosis) and a dualistic worldview where the material world is seen as evil and the spiritual world as good.  Gnostics believed in a supreme, unknowable God, and that the material world was created by a lesser, flawed deity.  Salvation was achieved through gaining gnosis, which allowed one to escape the material world and reunite with the divine.|The influence of Gnosticism on early Christianity is a subject of ongoing debate among scholars.|近义词：heresy, mysticism|反义词：orthodox Christianity
