sagum:/ˈseɪɡəm/| n.|短斗篷，军袍|A sagum was a type of short cloak or military mantle worn by Roman soldiers and citizens.  It was typically made of wool and was often worn over other garments. Unlike the toga, which was associated with civilian life, the sagum was a practical garment suited for military campaigns and other strenuous activities. |Example: The Roman legionary wore a sagum for protection from the elements during his campaigns.|近义词：cloak, mantle, cape|反义词：toga (in the context of Roman attire)
