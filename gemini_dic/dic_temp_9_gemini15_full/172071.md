purloins:/ˈpɜːrloɪnz/| vt.| 偷窃；窃取 | To steal something in a secretive or sly way.  It implies a less violent or forceful theft than robbery. |例句：He purloined a cookie from the jar while no one was looking. 他趁没人注意的时候偷偷从罐子里拿了一块饼干。
例句：The thief purloined valuable jewels from the museum.  小偷从博物馆偷走了贵重的珠宝。|近义词：steal, pilfer, filch, swipe|反义词：give, donate, bestow|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He purloins documents from the office. 他从办公室偷窃文件。
She purloins ideas from her colleagues. 她剽窃同事的创意。

一般过去时:
The thief purloined a valuable painting. 小偷偷走了一幅名贵的画。

They have purloined several important files. 他们已经窃取了几份重要的文件。
She has purloined information from the government database. 她已经从政府数据库窃取了信息。

过去完成时:

将来时:
