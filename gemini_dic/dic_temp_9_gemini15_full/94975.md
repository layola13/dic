in-the-bud: /ɪn ðə bʌd/ | adj. |含苞待放的；初露头角的；处于萌芽阶段的 | In its initial stage of development;  not yet fully developed or realized.  |例句：Her talent was still in the bud.  她的才能才刚刚显露。

The rose is still in the bud. 这朵玫瑰花还是含苞待放的。


近义词： nascent, embryonic, incipient, undeveloped

反义词： mature, full-blown, developed


词源:

"In the bud" is a figurative expression.  "Bud" refers to the undeveloped flower or shoot of a plant. The "in" indicates a state of being within that early stage of development. The phrase’s meaning extends metaphorically to describe anything in its early stages of growth or development.  The root of "bud" is from Old English *budde,  of uncertain origin, possibly related to words meaning "to swell" or "to sprout".
