canonicity:/ˌkænəˈnɪsəti/ | n. | 正典性；规范性 | The state of being accepted as part of a canon, especially a religious or literary canon.  It refers to the authoritative status of a text or work within a specific tradition or body of work.  | Example: The canonicity of the Gospel of Thomas is still debated among scholars.  托马斯福音的正典性仍在学者中争论不休。 |近义词:authenticity, authority, legitimacy |反义词:apocryphality, illegitimacy |名词
