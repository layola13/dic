entoptic: /ɛnˈtɒptɪk/ | adj. | 内感视的，内视的 | Relating to or perceived within the eye itself, rather than originating from an external source.  Entoptic phenomena are visual effects caused by structures within the eye itself, such as floaters or light scattering. | Example:  The shimmering lights I see are entoptic phenomena, caused by the movement of cells within my vitreous humor.  内眼看到的闪烁的光芒是内感视现象，由我的玻璃体液中的细胞运动引起。| 近义词:  intraocular | 反义词:  exogenous
