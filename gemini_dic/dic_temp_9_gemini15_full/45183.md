cuirassier:/ˌkwɪrəˈsɪər/| n. | cuirassier | 重装骑兵；胸甲骑兵 | A cuirassier is a cavalry soldier who wears a cuirass (a piece of armor protecting the chest and back).  Historically, cuirassiers were heavily armored cavalry, known for their shock tactics and role in decisive battles.  Today, the term may refer to a type of cavalry regiment or its members. | The cuirassiers charged into battle, their armor gleaming in the sun.  重装骑兵冲锋陷阵，他们的盔甲在阳光下闪闪发光。| cavalryman, horseman, trooper | infantryman, foot soldier | 名词
