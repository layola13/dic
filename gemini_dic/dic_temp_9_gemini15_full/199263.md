silva:/ˈsɪlvə/| n.|森林；树林；银（古时指森林）|From Latin silva, meaning "forest", "woods", or "grove".  The word's usage has broadened over time, sometimes referring to a large collection of something, similar to a forest. In some contexts, it also refers to silver, particularly in older texts and contexts where the connection between the metal and the forest's silvery appearance is implied.|The ancient silva was a place of mystery and wonder. = 古代的森林是一个神秘而奇幻的地方。|woods, forest, woodland|desert, plain|名词
