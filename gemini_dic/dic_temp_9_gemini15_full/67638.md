extricated:/ˈɛkstrɪkeɪtɪd/| v.|解救；使摆脱；脱困 | To free (someone or something) from a constraint or difficulty.  |例句：The firefighters extricated the passengers from the wrecked car. 消防队员将乘客从撞毁的汽车中救了出来。|例句：She extricated herself from a difficult situation. 她摆脱了困境。|近义词：liberate, rescue, release, disentangle |反义词：entangle, trap, ensnare


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The firefighters extricate people from burning buildings. = 消防员将人们从燃烧的建筑物中救出来。
He extricates himself from difficult conversations. = 他让自己摆脱困境的谈话。


一般过去时:
They extricated the dog from the thorny bushes. = 他们把狗从荆棘丛中解救出来。
She extricated herself from the complex legal battle. = 她摆脱了复杂的法律纠纷。


现在完成时:
The police have extricated the hostages safely. = 警方已安全解救了人质。
He has extricated his company from financial trouble. = 他已使公司摆脱了财务困境。


过去完成时:
They had extricated themselves from the sinking ship before it disappeared. = 在船沉没之前，他们已成功脱险。
She had extricated the truth from a web of lies. = 她从谎言的网络中挖掘出了真相。


将来时:
We will extricate the trapped miners as soon as possible. = 我们将尽快将被困的矿工救出来。
They will extricate themselves from this messy situation eventually. = 他们最终会摆脱这种混乱局面。


虚拟语气:
If we had more resources, we could have extricated them more quickly. = 如果我们有更多资源，我们可以更快地解救他们。
I wish I could extricate myself from this commitment. = 我希望我能摆脱这个承诺。


被动语态:
The victim was quickly extricated from the wreckage. = 受害者很快就被从残骸中救了出来。
The truth will eventually be extricated from the evidence. = 真相最终会从证据中被揭露出来。


疑问句:
Can we extricate the car from the ditch? = 我们能把车从沟里弄出来吗？
How will they extricate themselves from this crisis? = 他们将如何摆脱这场危机？


否定句:
They couldn't extricate the child from the mud. = 他们无法将孩子从泥里救出来。
He failed to extricate himself from the scandal. = 他未能摆脱丑闻。
