remunerated:/rɪˈmjuːnəreɪtɪd/ | adj. | 酬劳的；有报酬的 | Paid for services rendered; compensated.  | The work was remunerated at a fair rate. 这项工作得到了公平的报酬。|近义词：compensated, paid, salaried | 反义词：unpaid, voluntary


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company remunerates its employees fairly. = 公司公平地补偿其员工。
They are remunerated for their hard work. = 他们因辛勤工作而获得报酬。


一般过去时:
The musicians were remunerated for their performance. = 音乐家们因为他们的演出获得了报酬。
He was remunerated handsomely for his consulting work. = 他因为他的咨询工作获得了丰厚的报酬。


现在完成时:
She has been remunerated for her services. = 她已经因为她的服务得到了报酬。
They have not yet been remunerated for the extra work. = 他们还没有因为额外的工作得到报酬。


过去完成时:
The contractors had been remunerated before the project was completed. = 在项目完成之前，承包商已经获得了报酬。
The workers had not been remunerated for their overtime. = 工人们没有因为他们的加班而获得报酬。


将来时:
The freelance writer will be remunerated upon completion of the article. = 自由撰稿人将在文章完成后获得报酬。
We will be remunerated for our time and effort. = 我们将因为我们的时间和精力而获得报酬。


虚拟语气:
If I were remunerated fairly, I would work harder. = 如果我的报酬公平，我会更努力工作。
I wish I were remunerated for my contributions. = 我希望我的贡献得到报酬。


被动语态:
Employees are remunerated monthly. = 员工每月获得报酬。
The project was remunerated from government funds. = 该项目由政府资金支付报酬。


疑问句:
Are the employees adequately remunerated? = 员工的报酬是否足够？
How are the consultants remunerated for their services? = 顾问们如何获得服务的报酬？


否定句:
The interns were not remunerated for their work. = 实习生没有因为他们的工作获得报酬。
The volunteers were not remunerated for their time. = 志愿者没有因为他们的时间获得报酬。


PHRASE->
fully remunerated = 全额报酬
adequately remunerated = 足够的报酬
fairly remunerated = 公平的报酬
poorly remunerated = 报酬低的
remunerated position = 有偿职位


词源:
From Latin *remunerātus, past participle of remunerāre "to reward, compensate," from re- "again" + munus, muneris "gift, duty, service."  The sense evolution reflects the original meaning of "to give a gift in return for service."
