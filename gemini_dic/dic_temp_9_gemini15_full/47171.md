dawns:/dɔːnz/| n.|黎明；开始；曙光|The beginning of something, especially the beginning of a period of time or a new stage in life.  It can also refer to the time when daylight first appears in the morning.|例句：Dawn broke over the mountains. 黎明时分，阳光洒在山峦之上。

例句：The dawn of a new era in technology is upon us.  科技新时代的曙光已经来临。

近义词：sunrise, daybreak, beginning, start

反义词：sunset, dusk, twilight, end


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The sun dawns every morning. = 太阳每天早上升起。
A new understanding dawns on him. = 他开始明白一个新的道理。


一般过去时:
Dawn broke just as we reached the summit. = 我们到达山顶时，黎明刚刚破晓。
It dawned on me that I had forgotten my keys. = 我突然意识到我忘记带钥匙了。


现在完成时:
It has dawned on me that I need to change my lifestyle. = 我已经意识到我需要改变我的生活方式。
A new era has dawned for the company. = 公司迎来了一个新时代。


过去完成时:
It had dawned on him that he was in serious trouble. = 他已经意识到自己遇到了大麻烦。
By the time dawn had broken, the storm had passed. = 黎明到来时，风暴已经过去了。


将来时:
Dawn will break soon. = 天快亮了。
It will dawn on him eventually. = 他最终会明白的。


虚拟语气:
If dawn broke earlier, we could have left sooner. = 如果黎明来得早一点，我们就能早点出发了。
I wish dawn would break quickly. = 我希望天快点亮。


被动语态:
(The verb "dawn" is rarely used in the passive voice because it's intransitive.)


疑问句:
When will dawn break tomorrow? = 明天几点钟天亮？
Has it dawned on you yet? = 你明白了吗？


否定句:
Dawn did not break until late that morning. = 那天早上很晚才天亮。
It didn't dawn on her until much later. = 直到很久以后她才明白过来。


PHRASE->
the dawn of a new era = 新时代的开始

until dawn = 直到黎明
