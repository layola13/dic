good-old-boys:/ˌɡʊdˈoʊldbɔɪz/| n. |好老哥们儿，老资格，（贬义）男性统治集团| A term referring to a group of men, typically white and older, who hold significant power and influence within a particular social or professional setting, often characterized by cronyism and exclusion of outsiders.  The term often carries a negative connotation, suggesting a lack of transparency, fairness, or meritocracy.  |例句：The good-old-boys network in the city hall made it nearly impossible for outsiders to get contracts.  市政厅里的老资格们使得局外人几乎不可能拿到合同。

The good-old-boys club controlled the local politics for decades.  这个老资格俱乐部控制了当地的政治几十年。|近义词：old boys' network, establishment, elite, insiders|反义词：outsiders, newcomers, reformers
