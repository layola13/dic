performance:/pərˈfɔːrməns/ | n. | 表演；性能；表现；工作效率 | The act of performing a task or function; the manner in which something functions or operates; an entertainment presented to an audience. | 例句：Her performance in the play was outstanding.  她的戏剧表演非常出色。 | 例句：The car's performance was impressive. 这辆车的性能令人印象深刻。 | 近义词：execution, achievement, output, functioning | 反义词：failure, malfunction


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The engine performs well. = 这台引擎运转良好。
His performance consistently exceeds expectations. = 他的表现始终超出预期。

一般过去时:
She performed beautifully at the concert. = 她在音乐会上表演得很精彩。
The computer performed poorly during the stress test. = 这台电脑在压力测试中表现不佳。

现在完成时:
The company has performed well this year. = 公司今年表现良好。
He has performed many successful surgeries. = 他已经成功进行了许多手术。

过去完成时:
The team had performed brilliantly before the final game. = 在决赛之前，球队表现出色。
They had performed the experiment several times before achieving success. = 他们成功之前已经做过几次实验。

将来时:
The orchestra will perform Beethoven's Fifth Symphony. = 乐团将演奏贝多芬的第五交响曲。
The new software is expected to perform more efficiently. = 新软件预计会更高效地运行。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
