kushites:/ˈkuʃaɪts/| n. |库施人；库施语使用者|The Kushites were a group of ancient peoples inhabiting the region of Kush, located in present-day Sudan and Ethiopia.  They are known for their advanced civilization, including their impressive pyramids and contributions to art and culture.  The term often refers to speakers of Cushitic languages, a branch of the Afro-Asiatic language family.|例句：The ancient Kushites built magnificent pyramids along the Nile River. 古代库施人沿尼罗河建造了宏伟的金字塔。
