helter-skelter: /ˈhɛltər ˈskɛltər/ | adj. | 杂乱的；慌乱的；仓促的 |

英文解析:In a hurried and disorganized way; pell-mell.  The word suggests a chaotic and uncontrolled rush or movement.

例句:
The children ran helter-skelter down the hill. = 孩子们慌慌张张地跑下山。

The papers were scattered helter-skelter across the desk. = 文件杂乱地散落在桌子上。


近义词: disorderly, chaotic, haphazard, pell-mell, tumultuous, frantic

反义词: orderly, organized, systematic, methodical


词源:  The word is a compound of two Old English words:

* **Helter:**  Derived from an Old English word meaning "to roll" or "to slide."  It's related to the word "halter," referring to a rope used to restrain an animal,  suggesting a tumbling or uncontrolled movement.

* **Skelter:**  Derived from an Old English word  meaning "to scatter" or "to run quickly." It suggests a rapid and uncontrolled dispersal.
