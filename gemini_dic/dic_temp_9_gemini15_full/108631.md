leadings:/ˈliːdɪŋz/| n.|领先地位；主要方面；主要人物|Leading positions in the industry are highly competitive.  行业中的领先地位竞争非常激烈。|The leading causes of death are heart disease and cancer. 导致死亡的主要原因是心脏病和癌症。|近义词：foremost, primary, chief|反义词：lagging, trailing


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The leading company in the market holds a significant advantage.  市场上的领先公司拥有显著优势。
Leading indicators suggest a strong economic recovery.  领先指标表明经济将强劲复苏。


一般过去时:
The leading candidate lost the election.  领先的候选人输掉了选举。
Leading scientists presented their findings at the conference.  主要的科学家们在会议上展示了他们的研究成果。


现在完成时:
The leading team has won all its matches this season.  领先的队伍在本赛季赢得了所有比赛。
Leading economists have predicted a recession.  主要的经济学家们预测了经济衰退。


过去完成时:
The leading company had already secured a major contract before the merger.  在合并之前，领先的公司已经获得了重要的合同。
The leading researchers had completed their experiments before publishing their findings. 领先的研究人员在发表研究结果之前已经完成了他们的实验。


将来时:
The leading candidate is expected to win the election.  预计领先的候选人将赢得选举。
The leading experts will discuss the latest developments at the summit.  主要的专家们将在峰会上讨论最新的发展。


虚拟语气:
If our team were leading, we would be celebrating. 如果我们的团队领先，我们会庆祝。
If the leading party had lost, the political landscape would have changed dramatically. 如果领先的政党输了，政治格局将会发生巨大变化。


被动语态:
The leading role was played by a famous actor.  主角由一位著名演员扮演。
The leading cause of the accident was determined to be driver error. 事故的主要原因被认定为驾驶员错误。


疑问句:
Is this company leading in its sector? 这家公司在其行业中处于领先地位吗？
Who are the leading experts in this field?  在这个领域的主要专家是谁？


否定句:
This company is not leading in innovation. 这家公司在创新方面并非领先。
The team was not leading at halftime.  球队在上半场并没有领先。
