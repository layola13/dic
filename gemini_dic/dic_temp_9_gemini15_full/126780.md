nanometres:/ˈnænəˌmiːtərz/| n. |纳米|A nanometre (nm) is a unit of length in the metric system, equal to one billionth of a metre (10⁻⁹ m). It's commonly used to measure extremely small things, such as the wavelengths of light or the dimensions of atoms and molecules.|The wavelength of visible light is typically between 400 and 700 nanometres.|近义词：billionth of a metre|反义词:kilometre, metre
