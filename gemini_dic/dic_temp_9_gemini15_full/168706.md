procrustes:/ˈprɒkrʌstiːz/| n. |普罗克鲁斯忒斯（希腊神话人物）|Procrustes was a figure in Greek mythology who owned an inn and would adjust his guests' bodies to fit his bed, either by stretching them or cutting off their limbs. He represents the act of forcing something to conform to a pre-determined standard, regardless of the consequences. The term is often used metaphorically to describe such actions.|The company's Procrustean approach to budgeting forced departments to cut essential programs.|近义词：tyrant, oppressor|反义词：flexible, adaptable
