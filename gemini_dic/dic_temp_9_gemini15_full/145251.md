/ˌoʊvərˈblaɪnd/ | adj. | 过分盲目；过于轻信的 | Excessively blind; lacking in discernment or judgment; too trusting.  It implies a lack of critical thinking and an excessive reliance on others' opinions or superficial information. |  He was overblind to the dangers of the situation, rushing headlong into it without a second thought. 他对情况的危险过于盲目，不假思索地一头扎了进去。  She was overblind in her faith, accepting everything her guru said without question. 她对自己的信仰过于盲目，毫不怀疑地接受了她的导师所说的所有话。 |  gullible, naive, credulous | discerning, skeptical, shrewd | adj.
