out-of-town:/ˌaʊt əvˈtaʊn/ | adj. | 外地的；外乡的；来自城外的 |  Referring to something or someone from a place outside of the immediate town or city.  This typically implies a distance that requires travel, often suggesting a less frequent or less familiar connection. |  例句：He's an out-of-town visitor. 他是一位外地游客。


例句：We're expecting a lot of out-of-town guests for the wedding. 我们期待着许多外地宾客来参加婚礼。


近义词：foreign, provincial, non-local


反义词：local, in-town, nearby


词性：形容词
