disturbance:/dɪˈstɜːrbəns/ | n. | 干扰；扰乱；骚动 | a disruption or interruption of an event, activity, system, etc.  | The construction work caused a major disturbance.  施工造成了严重的干扰。 | interruption, disruption, commotion | peace, calm, quiet | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The noise causes a disturbance. = 噪音造成干扰。
A small disturbance occurs during the meeting. = 会议期间发生了一点小骚动。

一般过去时:
The storm caused a great disturbance. = 暴风雨造成了很大的扰乱。
There was a disturbance in the street last night. = 昨晚街上发生了一起骚乱。

现在完成时:
The renovations have caused considerable disturbance. = 装修造成了相当大的干扰。
There have been several disturbances in the city recently. = 最近城市里发生了几起骚乱。

过去完成时:
The argument had caused a significant disturbance before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，争吵已经造成了很大的骚乱。
Before the announcement, there had been a lot of disturbance among the staff. = 在宣布之前，员工中有很多骚动。

将来时:
The protest will likely cause a major disturbance. = 抗议活动可能会造成严重的骚乱。
The upcoming election is expected to cause a lot of disturbance. = 预计即将到来的选举将会造成很大的骚动。

虚拟语气:
If there were no disturbance, the concert would be perfect. = 如果没有干扰，音乐会就会完美无缺。
I wish there had been less disturbance during the lecture. = 我希望讲座期间干扰少一些。

被动语态:
The meeting was disturbed by a sudden noise. = 会议被突然的噪音打断了。
Order was restored after the disturbance had been quelled. = 骚乱被平息后秩序恢复了。

疑问句:
Was there a disturbance during the night? = 夜里有什么骚动吗？
What caused the disturbance? = 什么引起了骚乱？

否定句:
There was no disturbance during the performance. = 演出期间没有干扰。
The police reported that there had been no significant disturbance. = 警方报告说没有发生重大的骚乱。
