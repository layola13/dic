prebudgets:/ˌpriːˈbʌdʒɪts/| n.| 预算编制前的；预算法案|  The term "prebudgets" refers to the period or processes leading up to the creation and presentation of a budget.  It encompasses activities like planning, forecasting, data gathering, and initial discussions that inform the final budget.  It's not a standard dictionary word but a contextual term used in financial and governmental contexts.|The prebudget discussions were intense, with many departments vying for funds.  预算法案前的讨论非常激烈，许多部门都在争夺资金。|budgetary planning, financial planning, fiscal planning|postbudget,  actual budget|名词
