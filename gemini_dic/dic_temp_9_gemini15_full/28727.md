carnalism:/ˈkɑːrnəlɪzəm/| n. |肉欲；肉体主义；纵欲；性行为|The excessive indulgence in sexual activity;  the belief that physical pleasures, especially sexual ones, are the most important things in life.  It can also refer to the practice of eating human flesh (cannibalism). However, in most modern usage, it primarily refers to unrestrained sexual indulgence. |例句：The novel explored themes of carnalism and its consequences. 这部小说探讨了肉欲及其后果。

例句：His life was characterized by a pursuit of carnalism, neglecting spiritual and intellectual growth. 他的生活以追求肉欲为特征，忽视了精神和智力的发展。


近义词：sensuality, hedonism, libertinism, debauchery

反义词：asceticism, chastity, abstinence


词源：From Middle English *carnalisme, from Old French *carnalisme, from Latin *carnalis (“of the flesh”) + -ismus.  The Latin root "caro, carnis" means "flesh".
