posse:/ˈpɒsi/| n.|  团伙，随从，一帮人 | A posse is a group of people, often armed, assembled for a particular purpose, especially to pursue a criminal.  It can also refer to a group of followers or supporters. |例句：The sheriff gathered a posse to search for the escaped convict.  警长召集了一队人马搜捕逃犯。

例句：The rock star traveled with a large posse of bodyguards and assistants.  这位摇滚巨星带着一大群保镖和助手出行。


近义词：group, gang, retinue, entourage

反义词：individual, lone person


词源:  From Old French *posse, from Latin posse "to be able".  The original meaning was "power" or "ability," but it evolved to refer to a group having such power or ability.
