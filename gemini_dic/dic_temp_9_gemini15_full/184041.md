residentiaryship: /ˌrezɪˈdɛnʃɪp/ | n. |居住权；居住资格；住院医师职位 | The state or fact of being a resident; the period of time spent as a resident.  It can also refer to the position of a resident physician in a hospital. | 例句：He applied for residency and secured a residentiaryship in a prestigious hospital. 他申请了住院医师职位并在一家著名的医院获得了住院医师职位。

例句：The residentiaryship program provided valuable experience for the young doctors. 住院医师项目为年轻医生提供了宝贵的经验。


近义词：residency, residentship (less formal)


反义词：non-residency


词源:  The word is a combination of "resident" (someone who lives in a particular place) and "-ship," a suffix indicating status, condition, or skill.  "Resident" itself comes from the Latin "residentem," the present participle of "residēre," meaning "to sit back," "to remain," or "to dwell."  The "-ship" suffix has Germanic origins.
