ratite:/ˈreɪtaɪt/| n. |平胸鸟，无翼鸟|A ratite is a flightless bird characterized by a flat breastbone (sternum) lacking a keel, which is the point of attachment for flight muscles in flying birds.  Ratites are large, strong birds with powerful legs and are found on various continents.  They are believed to have evolved from flying ancestors. |Example: Ostriches, emus, and kiwis are all examples of ratites.  鸵鸟、鸸鹋和几维鸟都是平胸鸟的例子。|近义词：flightless bird,  无翼鸟|反义词：flying bird, 有翼鸟
