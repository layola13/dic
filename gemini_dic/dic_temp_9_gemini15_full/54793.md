dispensatively: /dɪˈspɛnsətɪvli/ | adv. | 分配地；特许地；豁免地 | In a dispensing or distributive manner; by way of dispensation or exemption.  The word implies an action or process that distributes something, grants an exception, or allows something to be done outside normal rules. | 例句：The judge ruled dispensatively, granting the defendant a reprieve. = 法官特许地判决，给予被告缓刑。 The resources were distributed dispensatively among the needy. = 资源被分配给需要的人。| 近义词：distributively, ex officio, exceptionally | 反义词：restrictively, mandatorily


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and therefore does not have typical verb conjugations as shown in your example.  The usage depends entirely on the context of the sentence.  It describes *how* an action is performed.


PHRASE->

This word is rarely used in phrases. Its use is generally limited to formal writing and legal contexts.  There aren't common idiomatic phrases with "dispensatively."
