leader:/ˈliːdər/ | n. |领导者，领导，领袖 | A person who leads or commands a group, organization, or country.  A leader guides and influences others to achieve a common goal. |例句：She is a strong leader who inspires her team. 她是一位激励团队的强有力的领导者。|例句：The leader of the expedition guided them through the jungle. 探险队的领导人带领他们穿过丛林。|近义词：head, chief, boss, manager |反义词：follower, subordinate


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The team leader guides the project. = 团队领导指导这个项目。
She leads the marketing department. = 她领导市场部。


一般过去时:
He led the company to success. = 他带领公司走向成功。
The captain led his team to victory. = 船长带领他的团队取得了胜利。


现在完成时:
She has led the company for ten years. = 她领导这家公司十年了。
They have led the industry in innovation. = 他们一直引领行业创新。


过去完成时:
He had led the orchestra for many years before retiring. = 他退休前领导乐团多年。
They had led the charge before the enemy surrendered. = 敌人投降之前，他们已经领导了冲锋。


将来时:
She will lead the meeting tomorrow. = 她明天将主持会议。
He will lead the research team on this project. = 他将领导这个项目的科研团队。


虚拟语气:
If she were the leader, things would be different. = 如果她是领导，情况就会不同。
I wish I were a leader like her. = 我希望我能像她一样成为领导。


被动语态:
The project is being led by a very experienced manager. = 这个项目由一位经验丰富的经理领导。
The team was led to victory by their courageous captain. = 他们的英勇船长带领团队取得了胜利。


疑问句:
Who is leading the project? = 谁在领导这个项目？
Who will lead the team next year? = 明年谁将领导这个团队？


否定句:
He didn't lead the team effectively. = 他没有有效地领导团队。
She does not lead by example. = 她没有以身作则。
