outshouting: /ˌaʊtˈʃaʊtɪŋ/ | v. | 大声喊叫以压过；大声喧哗以盖过 | To shout louder than someone else in order to be heard; to drown out someone's voice by shouting. |例句：He was outshouting everyone in the room, trying to make his point. 他在房间里大声喊叫，试图让别人听到他的观点。

The children were outshouting each other, making it impossible to have a conversation. 孩子们互相大声喊叫，根本无法进行对话。


近义词：drowning out, overpowering, shouting down | 反义词：whispering, murmuring


词源:

The word "outshouting" is a verb formed from the prefix "out-" and the verb "shouting".  "Out-" is a prefix that indicates exceeding or surpassing something, often in a competitive sense. "Shouting" is the gerund form of the verb "to shout," meaning to cry out loudly.  Therefore, "outshouting" literally means "to shout louder than" or "to surpass in shouting."  Its formation is straightforward and follows common English word-building patterns.
