quasi-legitimately: /ˈkwɑːzi leˈdʒɪtɪmətli/ | adv. |似是而非地合法地；表面上合法地 |  Apparently or seemingly legitimate, but lacking true or complete legitimacy.  It suggests an action or situation that has some appearance of legality or justification, but ultimately falls short of genuine legitimacy.  | 例句：The company operated quasi-legitimately, skirting the law through technicalities. 公司似是而非地合法经营，通过技术手段规避法律。 The regime maintained power quasi-legitimately through a carefully orchestrated election. 该政权通过精心策划的选举表面上合法地维持权力。 | 近义词：apparently legitimately, seemingly legitimately, nominally legitimately | 反义词：illegitimately, unlawfully, illegally |
