get-ones-irish-up: /ˌɡɛt wʌnz ˈaɪrɪʃ ʌp/ | phrasal verb | 使某人恼火；激怒某人 | To make someone angry or annoyed.  |  例句：His constant complaining really gets my Irish up.  他不断的抱怨真让我火大。

例句：The rude waiter really got my Irish up.  那个粗鲁的服务员真让我生气。


近义词：annoy, irritate, infuriate, provoke, incense

反义词：soothe, appease, calm, pacify


词源：The origin is uncertain, but it likely arose in the late 19th or early 20th century.  The "Irish" element doesn't necessarily refer to the Irish people themselves, but rather uses "Irish" as a colloquial intensifier, similar to how other nationalities might be used in similar idioms. The phrase taps into stereotypes of the Irish temperament, implying a quickness to anger.  The exact etymology remains debated.
