scrofula:/ˈskrɒfjʊlə/| n. |瘰疬，颈淋巴结结核|A form of tuberculosis affecting the lymph nodes, especially those in the neck.  It is characterized by swollen, inflamed lymph nodes that may suppurate (form pus). Scrofula was once a common and feared disease, particularly among children.  The term is now largely obsolete, as effective treatments for tuberculosis are available.| The child suffered from scrofula, resulting in a large, unsightly swelling on his neck.  |Tuberculosis, lymphadenitis|healthy lymph nodes|
