sargon:/ˈsɑːrɡɒn/|/ˈsɑːrɡɑːn/| n. |萨尔贡|Sargon refers to several ancient Mesopotamian kings, most famously Sargon of Akkad (circa 2334–2284 BCE), who founded the Akkadian Empire.  He is known for his military conquests and his establishment of a centralized government.  The name itself likely derives from a Sumerian word, though its precise meaning remains debated. The name is also associated with other rulers in different periods and regions.|The reign of Sargon of Akkad marked a significant turning point in Mesopotamian history.|近义词:Akkadian emperor, Mesopotamian ruler|反义词:None directly applicable.
