marshlands:/ˈmɑːrʃlændz/ | n. |沼泽地；湿地 | Areas of low-lying land that are saturated with water, often characterized by vegetation adapted to wet conditions, such as reeds, grasses, and shrubs. Marshlands play a crucial role in ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife and filtering pollutants from water.  | Example: The birds build their nests in the marshlands.  鸟儿在沼泽地筑巢。  The researchers studied the plant life of the marshlands. 研究人员研究了沼泽地的植物。 | 近义词：wetlands, swamps, bogs | 反义词：drylands, uplands
