coauthored:/koʊˈɔːθərd/ | v. | 合著；共同撰写 |  To write or create something (such as a book, article, or report) with one or more other people. | Example:  They coauthored a groundbreaking paper on climate change.  他们合著了一篇关于气候变化的开创性论文。 | 近义词：collaborated, jointly authored | 反义词：solo authored, single-authored

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They coauthor several papers each year. = 他们每年合著几篇论文。
We coauthor a book about history. = 我们合著一本关于历史的书。


一般过去时:
We coauthored a book last year. = 我们去年合著了一本书。
The two scientists coauthored a groundbreaking article. = 这两位科学家合著了一篇具有开创性的文章。


现在完成时:
They have coauthored numerous articles on the subject. = 他们已经就这个主题合著了许多文章。
She has coauthored three books with her sister. = 她和她姐姐合著了三本书。


过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->

词源:
The word "coauthored" is a combination of "co-" (meaning "together" or "jointly") and "authored" (from "author," meaning "the writer of a book, article, or other text").  Therefore, it literally means "written together."
