membrane:/ˈmembreɪn/| n. |膜；薄膜|A thin, pliable sheet of tissue that covers or lines an organ or cavity, or separates one part of a body from another.  It often acts as a barrier or filter. For example, cell membranes regulate what enters and exits a cell.|The cell membrane protects the cell's internal environment.  细胞膜保护细胞的内部环境。|The mucous membrane lines the nasal passages. 粘膜衬里鼻腔。|film, layer, sheet |thick skin, solid surface |名词
