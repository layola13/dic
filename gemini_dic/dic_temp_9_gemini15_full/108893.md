lecturing:/ˈlɛktʃərɪŋ/| v.|讲课；训斥|The act of delivering a lecture or a formal speech; also, the act of scolding or reprimanding someone.  |例句：The professor is lecturing on the history of art. 教授正在讲授艺术史。 |例句：Stop lecturing me! 不要再教训我了！|近义词：teaching, instructing, reprimanding, scolding |反义词：listening, obeying


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is lecturing on Shakespeare. = 他正在讲授莎士比亚。
She lectures at the university. = 她在大学里讲课。

一般过去时:
He lectured on economics last semester. = 上学期他讲授经济学。
She lectured the children about their behavior. = 她训斥了孩子们的不良行为。

现在完成时:
He has lectured at this university for ten years. = 他在这所大学讲课十年了。
She has lectured extensively on the topic of climate change. = 她已就气候变化问题做了广泛的演讲。

过去完成时:
He had lectured at Oxford before moving to Cambridge. = 他在去剑桥之前曾在牛津大学讲课。
She had lectured the students on the importance of punctuality. = 她之前已经告诫学生们准时的重要性。

将来时:
He will lecture on modern philosophy next term. = 下学期他将讲授现代哲学。
She will be lecturing at the conference next week. = 她下周将在会议上发表演讲。

虚拟语气:
If he lectured more clearly, the students would understand better. = 如果他讲课更清晰些，学生们就会更好地理解。
I wish she hadn't lectured me so harshly. = 我真希望她没有那样严厉地训斥我。

被动语态:
The topic was lectured on by several experts. = 这个主题由几位专家进行了讲解。
The students were lectured about the importance of hard work. = 学生们被告诫要努力工作的重要性。

疑问句:
Is he lecturing today? = 他今天讲课吗？
Who is lecturing in this class? = 这节课是谁在讲课？

否定句:
He isn't lecturing this semester. = 这个学期他没在讲课。
She didn't lecture the students on that topic. = 她没有就那个话题给学生们讲课。
