common markets:/ˈkɑːmən ˈmɑːrkɪts/ | n. |共同市场，公共市场 |A common market is a type of trade bloc which is composed of a free trade area with common external tariffs.  This means that member countries can trade freely amongst themselves without tariffs or other trade barriers, and they also agree on a common set of tariffs to be applied to goods imported from outside the bloc.  | Example: The European Union is a well-known example of a common market.  欧盟是一个著名的共同市场例子。|近义词：free trade area, customs union |反义词：protectionist economy, closed market
