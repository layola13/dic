geodes:/ˈdʒiːoʊdz/| n. |晶洞；结核|A geode is a hollow, roughly spherical rock that is lined with crystals.  Geodes are formed when minerals precipitate from water within a cavity in a rock. They are often found in sedimentary and volcanic rocks.  |Example: The geologist carefully cracked open the geode, revealing a dazzling array of amethyst crystals.  地质学家小心地敲开了晶洞，露出了令人眼花缭乱的紫水晶。|近义词：nodule, concretion|反义词：无
