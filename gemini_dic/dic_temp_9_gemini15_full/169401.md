pronominalization:/ˌprəˌnɑːmɪnəlɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |代词化；代词置换|The process of replacing a noun phrase with a pronoun.  This is a common linguistic phenomenon used to avoid repetition and make sentences more concise and fluent.  It involves understanding the referent of the pronoun (the noun phrase it replaces).|Example: "John went to the store. He bought milk."  Here, "He" is a pronoun replacing the noun phrase "John".|近义词：pronoun substitution, anaphora|反义词：nominalization
