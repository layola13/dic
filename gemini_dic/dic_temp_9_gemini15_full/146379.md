/ˌoʊvərˈflɔːrɪdnəs/ | n. | 过分华丽；过于繁复 | Excessive ornamentation or elaboration; being overly ornate.  The overfloridness of the baroque style is sometimes criticized for being too excessive.  | 近义词：excessiveness, extravagance, ostentation | 反义词：simplicity, plainness, austerity |


例句：

The overfloridness of the palace's interior design was overwhelming. = 宫殿内部设计的过度华丽令人难以招架。

The overfloridness of her language made it difficult to understand her main point. = 她语言的过于繁复使得难以理解她的要点。


词源:

* **over-**:  prefix indicating excess or to a great degree.
* **florid**: adjective meaning adorned, embellished, or excessively ornate.  It derives from the Latin word *floridus*, meaning "flowering," "blooming," or "flourishing."


用法说明：

Overfloridness is primarily used to describe something that is excessively ornate or embellished to the point of being overwhelming or distracting. It's often applied to artistic styles, writing, or speech.  It carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting that the ornamentation is excessive and detracts from the overall effect.  The word is relatively uncommon in everyday conversation.
