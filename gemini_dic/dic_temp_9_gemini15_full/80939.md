grab:/ɡræb/ | vt. n. |抓住；夺取；吸引；少量；一把 |To seize or take hold of suddenly and roughly; to snatch.  To attract or capture the attention or interest of someone. A small amount or quantity. |例句：He grabbed the phone and answered it. 他一把抓起电话并接听了。  The new design grabbed everyone's attention. 新设计吸引了所有人的注意。近义词：seize, snatch, catch, attract |反义词：release, let go, ignore


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grabs the newspaper every morning. 他每天早上都拿报纸。
She grabs a quick lunch before work. 她在上班前快速吃午餐。

一般过去时:
I grabbed my coat and ran out the door. 我抓起外套跑出门外。
They grabbed the opportunity to travel. 他们抓住了旅行的机会。

现在完成时:
She has grabbed the top spot in the rankings. 她已经占据了排行榜的首位。
He has grabbed a few minutes to relax. 他抽出几分钟时间放松一下。

过去完成时:
I had grabbed my bag before leaving the house. 我离开家之前已经拿好我的包了。
They had grabbed the last two tickets before we arrived. 我们到达之前他们已经抢到了最后两张票。

将来时:
I will grab a bite to eat later. 我晚点会吃点东西。
He will grab the chance to speak to the manager. 他将会抓住机会和经理谈话。


虚拟语气:
If I could grab some sleep, I would feel better. 如果我能睡一会儿，我会感觉好些。
I wish I could grab a coffee right now. 我真希望现在能喝杯咖啡。


被动语态:
The phone was grabbed by a thief. 电话被小偷抢走了。
The opportunity was grabbed by a competitor. 机会被竞争对手抓住了。


疑问句:
Did you grab your keys? 你拿钥匙了吗？
Will you grab some milk on your way home? 你回家的时候能买些牛奶吗？


否定句:
He didn't grab the chance to explain. 他没有抓住机会解释。
I didn't grab the book from the shelf. 我没有从书架上拿书。
