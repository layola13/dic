preconspires: /ˌpriːkənˈspaɪərz/ | v. |预谋；共谋；密谋 | To plan or plot something secretly, often with others.  The word combines "pre-" (before) and "conspire" (to plot secretly). It emphasizes the planning stage before the actual act of conspiracy. |例句：They preconspired to overthrow the government. = 他们预谋推翻政府。

They preconspired to steal the jewels and frame an innocent man. = 他们预谋偷窃珠宝并嫁祸给一个无辜的人。


近义词：plot, scheme, collude, intrigue | 反义词：cooperate openly, act honestly


词性：动词
