ression:/ɪˈprɛʃən/| n.|压迫；压制；表达；印象|The act of pressing or being pressed; the state of being pressed or squeezed.  It can also refer to the act of expressing thoughts or feelings, or the effect or impact something has on someone.|例句：The government's repression of dissent was met with widespread protests.  政府压制异见的行为引发了大规模抗议。|例句：Her painting made a strong impression on me. 她的画给我留下了深刻的印象。|近义词：suppression, constraint, impact, effect|反义词：freedom, liberation, release, expression

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government represses dissent. = 政府压制异见。
She expresses her feelings openly. = 她公开表达自己的感受。

一般过去时:
They repressed the rebellion swiftly. = 他们迅速镇压了叛乱。
He expressed his gratitude for the help. = 他表达了对帮助的感激之情。

现在完成时:
The regime has repressed any opposition for years. = 该政权多年来压制了任何反对声音。
She has expressed her concerns to the management. = 她已向管理层表达了她的担忧。

过去完成时:
The dictator had repressed all forms of freedom before the revolution. = 在革命之前，独裁者已经压制了所有形式的自由。
He had expressed his doubts before the meeting. = 会议之前他已经表达了他的怀疑。

将来时:
They will repress any attempt at a coup. = 他们将压制任何政变企图。
She will express her opinions at the meeting. = 她将在会议上表达她的意见。

虚拟语气:
If the government did not repress free speech, there would be more diversity of views. = 如果政府不压制言论自由，就会有更多样化的观点。
I wish I had expressed my feelings more clearly. = 我希望我当时能更清楚地表达我的感受。

被动语态:
Dissent was repressed by the authorities. = 异见被当局压制了。
His concerns were expressed to the committee. = 他的担忧已向委员会表达。

疑问句:
How is the government repressing the opposition? = 政府是如何压制反对派的？
How did she express her anger? = 她是如何表达她的愤怒的？

否定句:
The government does not repress freedom of religion. = 政府不压制宗教自由。
He did not express any interest in the proposal. = 他对这项提案没有表示任何兴趣。
