journalists:/ˈdʒɜːrnəlɪsts/ | n. |记者们，新闻工作者们 |People who write news stories for newspapers, magazines, or websites, or who report news on television or radio.  |The journalists rushed to the scene of the accident. 记者们迅速赶往事故现场。  Many journalists were present at the press conference. 许多记者出席了新闻发布会。| reporters, newsmen, correspondents |  (无明确反义词，可考虑 anti-establishment media  作为语义上的反义) | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Journalists report the news. = 记者报道新闻。
Journalists often work long hours. = 记者经常工作很长时间。

一般过去时:
Journalists covered the election extensively. = 记者广泛报道了选举。
Journalists interviewed the witnesses. = 记者采访了证人。

现在完成时:
Journalists have exposed many scandals. = 记者们已经揭露了许多丑闻。
Journalists have worked hard to get the story. = 记者们为了得到这个故事而努力工作。

过去完成时:
Journalists had already filed their stories before the deadline. = 记者们在截止日期之前已经提交了他们的稿件。
Journalists had investigated the case for months. = 记者们已经调查了这个案子几个月了。

将来时:
Journalists will cover the summit meeting. = 记者们将报道这次峰会。
Journalists will be reporting live from the scene. = 记者们将从现场进行直播报道。

虚拟语气:
If journalists were more careful, there would be fewer errors. = 如果记者们更细心，错误就会更少。
I wish journalists would focus more on factual reporting. = 我希望记者们更关注事实报道。

被动语态:
The story was written by several journalists. = 这个故事是由几位记者写的。
The journalists were criticized for their biased reporting. = 记者们因其有偏见的报道而受到批评。

疑问句:
Are the journalists reliable? = 这些记者可靠吗？
How many journalists are covering the story? = 有多少记者在报道这个故事？

否定句:
The journalists didn't get all the facts. = 记者们没有得到所有的事实。
Journalists could not access the information. = 记者们无法获取信息。
