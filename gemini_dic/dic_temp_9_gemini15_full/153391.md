penicillins:/ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪnz/| n.|青霉素类抗生素|A group of antibiotics derived from the Penicillium mold, used to treat bacterial infections.  They inhibit bacterial cell wall synthesis.|例句：Penicillins are effective against many types of bacteria. 青霉素对许多类型的细菌有效。

例句：The doctor prescribed penicillin for her strep throat. 医生给她开了青霉素治疗链球菌性咽喉炎。

近义词：antibiotics, cephalosporins|反义词：None (as a class of drugs)|词性：n.

词源:
The name "penicillin" is derived from the genus of fungi, *Penicillium*, from which the antibiotic is obtained.  The name was coined by Alexander Fleming, who discovered penicillin in 1928.  The suffix "-cillin" is a chemical designation indicating a specific type of beta-lactam antibiotic.
