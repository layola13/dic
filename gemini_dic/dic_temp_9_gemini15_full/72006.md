flinting: /ˈflɪntɪŋ/ | v. |  打火石；使变硬；使坚硬 |  The act of striking flint to create a spark; to make something hard or firm.  |例句：He was flinting the steel to start a fire. 他正在用打火石打火生火。 |近义词：striking, hardening | 反义词：softening


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flints his lighter to light a cigarette. = 他用打火机点燃香烟。
They flint the stones to create sparks for their fire. = 他们用石头打出火花来生火。

一般过去时:
She flinted the steel against the flint to make a spark. = 她用钢对着燧石打出火花。
The blacksmith flinted the metal to make it harder. = 铁匠打铁使金属变硬。


现在完成时:
He has flinted the stones many times to start fires in the wilderness. = 他在野外已经用石头打火很多次了。
They have flinted the steel, but still haven't managed to get a fire going. = 他们已经用钢打火了，但仍然没有成功生火。


过去完成时:
The pioneer had flinted his flint and steel many times before successfully starting a fire. = 拓荒者在成功生火之前已经用打火石和钢多次打火了。
Before the match, the athlete had flinted his resolve to win.  = 在比赛前，运动员已经下定决心要赢。


将来时:
He will flint the lighter to light the candles. = 他将用打火机点燃蜡烛。
They will flint the metal to strengthen it for the construction. = 他们将打铁以增强金属的强度用于建筑。


虚拟语气:
If he flinted the steel harder, he might have started a fire. = 如果他用力敲打钢材，他也许就能生火了。
If I had flinted my resolve earlier, I would have achieved more. = 如果我早点下定决心，我会取得更多成就。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

They didn't flint the metal properly, so it broke easily. = 他们没有正确地打铁，所以它很容易断裂。
