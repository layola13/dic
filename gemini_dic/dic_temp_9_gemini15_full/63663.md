epanaphora:/ɪˌpænəˈfɔːrə/| n. | 首语重复 |  The stylistic device of repeating the same word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences. It is a form of anaphora, emphasizing the repeated element and creating a sense of rhythm and impact.  | Example:  "We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds, we shall fight in the fields and in the streets, we shall fight in the hills; we shall never surrender." (Winston Churchill) This sentence uses epanaphora to emphasize the unwavering resolve to fight. |近义词：anaphora, repetition |反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with techniques avoiding repetition.


USAGE->
This word is a literary term and doesn't have grammatical tenses or voice like verbs. It describes a stylistic choice in writing.  There's no conjugation. The usage is solely in the analysis of text.

Example 1:  "Every day, every hour, every minute, I work to achieve my goals."  (首语重复加强了努力的程度)
Example 2:  "Slowly, slowly, the sun crept over the horizon." (首语重复描绘了太阳升起的缓慢过程)


PHRASE->
Epanaphora is not used in phrases in the way a verb or noun would be.  It describes a rhetorical technique found within phrases and sentences.  Therefore, providing phrase examples as requested in the model is not applicable.
