nova:/ˈnoʊvə/| n.|新星；突发事件|A nova is a sudden, significant increase in the brightness of a star, caused by a thermonuclear runaway on the surface of a white dwarf star in a binary system.  This results in a temporary increase in luminosity.  The term can also refer to any sudden, significant event or development, especially one that is unexpected.|例句：The discovery of the nova was a major breakthrough in astronomy.  天文学家发现这颗新星是重大突破。

The sudden increase in stock prices was described as a financial nova. 股票价格的突然上涨被形容为金融新星。|近义词：supernova, outburst, flare-up|反义词：dimness, decline
