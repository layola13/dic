/koʊɪnˈsaɪdər/ | n. | 同时发生的事物；巧合；同时发生的人 | A coincider refers to something or someone that happens or exists at the same time as something else, often implying a connection or unexpected similarity. It can also refer to a device or system that detects coincidences.  | The simultaneous eruption of two volcanoes was a remarkable coincider.  两个火山同时喷发是一件非凡的巧合。The timing of their arrival and the availability of rooms was a happy coincider. 他们到达的时间和房间的空置是一个令人高兴的巧合。 | coincidence, concurrence, simultaneity | discrepancy, difference, divergence | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The two events coincide. = 这两件事同时发生。
This device coincides the signals. = 此设备使信号同时发生。


一般过去时:
Their birthdays coincided. = 他们的生日是同一天。
The two lines coincided at the point. = 两条线在这一点上相交。


现在完成时:
Many coincidences have coincided in my life. = 我生命中发生了许多巧合。
The data from different sources have coincided remarkably well. = 来自不同来源的数据惊人地吻合。


过去完成时:
The two events had coincided before. = 这两件事以前就同时发生过。
By the time I arrived, the meeting had already coincided with the presentation. = 我到达时，会议已经与演示同时进行了。


将来时:
The concert will coincide with the festival. = 音乐会将与节日同时举行。
Our schedules will coincide next week. = 我们下周的时间安排将一致。


虚拟语气:
If their schedules coincided, they could work together. = 如果他们的时间安排一致，他们就可以一起工作。
I wish the presentation had coincided with the meeting. = 我希望演示与会议同时进行。


被动语态:
The two events were found to coincide. = 发现这两件事同时发生。
The data points were made to coincide by adjusting the measurements. = 通过调整测量结果使数据点一致。


疑问句:
Do the data points coincide? = 数据点是否一致？
Does this event coincide with anything else? = 此事件与其他任何事件同时发生吗？


否定句:
The two events did not coincide. = 这两件事没有同时发生。
The results of the two experiments did not coincide. = 两次实验的结果不一致。


PHRASE->
