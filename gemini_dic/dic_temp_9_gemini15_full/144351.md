outstanding: /aʊtˈstændɪŋ/ | adj. |杰出的，突出的，显著的 | Extremely good; excellent; remarkable.  |例句：Her performance was outstanding. 她的表演非常出色。

例句：His achievements in the field are outstanding. 他在该领域的成就非常突出。


近义词：excellent, exceptional, remarkable, superb, prominent, distinguished

反义词：inferior, mediocre, ordinary, poor, insignificant


词源:  The word "outstanding" comes from the Old English "ūtstandan," meaning "to stand out."  The prefix "out-" signifies "beyond" or "surpassing," while "stand" refers to position or prominence. Over time, the meaning evolved to describe something that is superior or remarkable because it stands out from the rest.


用法说明:

一般现在时:
The project is outstanding. = 这个项目很出色。
His work is outstanding. = 他的工作很出色。


一般过去时:
The performance was outstanding. = 表演非常出色。
The results were outstanding. = 结果非常出色。


现在完成时:
They have produced outstanding results. = 他们已经取得了杰出的成果。
She has had an outstanding career. = 她拥有杰出的职业生涯。


过去完成时:
The problem had been outstanding for months. = 这个问题已经存在好几个月了。(此处outstanding指未解决的，未完成的)
He had done outstanding work before the deadline. = 在截止日期之前他完成了出色的工作。


将来时:
The results will be outstanding. = 结果将会非常出色。
The company expects its future performance to be outstanding. = 公司期望其未来的业绩将非常出色。


虚拟语气:
If the product were outstanding, it would sell well. = 如果产品很出色，它就会卖得好。
I wish my presentation had been more outstanding. = 我希望我的演讲更出色一些。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
