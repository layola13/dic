pentasepalous: /ˌpɛntəˈsɛpələs/| adj. |五萼的；具有五个萼片的|Having five sepals.  A sepal is one of the modified leaves that enclose and protect a flower bud before it opens.  The term is primarily used in botany to describe the floral structure of certain plants.|例句：The flower is pentasepalous, with each sepal a vibrant green. 这朵花是五萼的，每个萼片都是鲜绿色。  The pentasepalous condition is a key characteristic of this species. 五萼状态是该物种的主要特征。|近义词：pentamerous (referring to five parts in general)|反义词：tetrasepalous (four sepals), trisepalous (three sepals), etc.|


词源:  From "penta-" (five) + "sepalous" (having sepals). "Sepal" comes from the Late Latin *sepālum, a diminutive of *sēpēs, "hedge," referring to the protective function of sepals.
