canty:/ˈkænti/| adj.|活泼的；愉快的；轻快的|Characterized by a lively and cheerful disposition; brisk and spirited.  The word suggests a quick-witted, energetic, and perhaps slightly mischievous personality.|例句：She had a canty spirit and a ready laugh. 她性格活泼，爱笑。|例句：The canty music filled the air. 轻快的音乐充满了空气。|近义词：lively, cheerful, spirited, brisk, merry|反义词：gloomy, morose, sluggish, lethargic


USAGE->

This word is primarily an adjective, so verb conjugations, passive voice, etc., are not applicable.  The usage focuses on describing a noun (person, music, atmosphere etc.)

PHRASE->

a canty laugh:  爽朗的笑声

a canty step: 轻快的步伐

词源:

The word "canty" originates from the Scots language.  Its etymology is uncertain, but it's likely related to words suggesting alertness, briskness, or sharpness of mind.  The connection to "cant" (meaning slang or jargon) is unlikely.  It reflects a long tradition of lively and spirited expression within Scots culture.
