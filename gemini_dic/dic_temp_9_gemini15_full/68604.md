fantasizes:/ˈfæntəsaɪzɪz/| v.|幻想；空想；编造|To imagine or create something in one's mind, often something pleasant or exciting, that is not real or likely to happen.  It often involves daydreaming or creating scenarios. |例句：She fantasizes about living in a tropical paradise. 她幻想住在热带天堂。

例句：He often fantasizes about winning the lottery. 他经常幻想赢得彩票。


近义词：dreams, imagines, daydreams, envisions|反义词：realizes, actualizes


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fantasizes about his future career. = 他幻想他未来的职业。
She fantasizes about traveling the world. = 她幻想环游世界。


一般过去时:
I fantasized about being a famous musician. = 我曾经幻想成为一名著名的音乐家。
They fantasized about winning the competition. = 他们曾经幻想赢得比赛。


现在完成时:
She has fantasized about this moment for years. = 她多年来一直幻想这一刻。
He has fantasized about escaping his mundane life. = 他一直幻想逃离他平凡的生活。


过去完成时:
I had fantasized about meeting her before we actually met. = 在我们真正见面之前，我曾幻想过和她见面。
They had fantasized about their wedding day for months. = 他们几个月来一直幻想他们的婚礼。


将来时:
She will fantasize about her vacation while she's working. = 工作时，她会幻想她的假期。
He will fantasize about retirement once he's older. = 他老了以后会幻想退休生活。


虚拟语气:
If I could, I would fantasize about anything I wanted. = 如果可以，我会幻想任何我想要的东西。
I wish I could fantasize about something positive. = 我希望我能幻想一些积极的事情。


被动语态:  (The verb "fantasize" is rarely used in the passive voice because it's usually an intransitive verb and focuses on the subject's mental activity.)  A more appropriate passive construction might use a related noun:  His escape was fantasized about by many people. = 许多人幻想他的逃脱。

疑问句:
Does he often fantasize about unrealistic scenarios? = 他是否经常幻想不切实际的情景？
What does she fantasize about most often? = 她最常幻想什么？


否定句:
He doesn't fantasize about violence. = 他不幻想暴力。
She doesn't fantasize about things that are impossible. = 她不幻想不可能的事情。
