preindulging: /ˌpriːɪnˈdʌldʒɪŋ/ | v. | 预先纵容；提前放纵 |  Engaging in indulgence before it's appropriate or necessary.  This word is a relatively rare neologism, formed by combining "pre-" (before) and "indulging" (giving way to desires). It implies an act of self-permission to enjoy something before one has earned it or before the proper time. |  例句：He was preindulging in the celebratory cake before the race even started. 他甚至在比赛开始之前就提前享用庆祝蛋糕了。


例句：She was preindulging in expensive wines, anticipating her bonus. 她提前享用昂贵的葡萄酒，期待着奖金的发放。


近义词：preempting (satisfaction), anticipating (pleasure),  overindulging (in advance)


反义词：restraining,  deferring,  denying


词性:动词
