physiophyly:/ˌfɪzioʊˈfɪli/| n. | 生理亲缘关系 | The evolutionary relationship between organisms based on their physiological characteristics.  It refers to the grouping of organisms based on similarities in their physiological functions and processes, rather than solely on their genetic relationships.  Physiophyly can be used in conjunction with or as an alternative to phylogenetic classification based on genetic data.  | Example:  Studies of physiophyly in marine invertebrates revealed unexpected relationships between seemingly disparate species. = 海洋无脊椎动物的生理亲缘关系研究揭示了看似不同的物种之间意想不到的关系。|近义词：physiological homology, functional similarity |反义词：phylogenetic divergence, physiological dissimilarity
