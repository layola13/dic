debatable:/dɪˈbeɪtəbl/ | adj. | 可争论的，有争议的 | Open to discussion or argument; not certain or agreed upon.  | The cause of the accident is still debatable. 事故的起因仍在争论中。 | disputable, questionable, contentious | certain, undeniable, undisputed | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The issue is debatable. = 这个问题是有争议的。
His claim is debatable. = 他的说法是值得商榷的。

一般过去时:
The theory was debatable at the time. = 当时那个理论是有争议的。
The results of the experiment were highly debatable. = 实验的结果非常有争议。

现在完成时:
The matter has been debatable for years. = 这件事多年来一直存在争议。
The effectiveness of the treatment has remained debatable. = 该疗法的有效性一直存在争议。

过去完成时:
The decision had been highly debatable before the final vote. = 在最终投票之前，这个决定一直存在很大争议。
The plan had been debatable among the team members. = 这个计划在团队成员中一直存在争议。

将来时:
The outcome of the election will likely be debatable for some time. = 大选的结果很可能在一段时间内都存在争议。
The new policy will undoubtedly be debatable. = 新政策无疑将引起争议。

虚拟语气:
If the evidence were more conclusive, the verdict wouldn't be so debatable. = 如果证据更确凿，判决就不会有这么大的争议。
If the facts were different, the situation would not be so debatable. = 如果事实不同，情况就不会那么有争议。

被动语态:
The issue is being debated. = 这个问题正在被讨论。
The plan was debated extensively. = 该计划被广泛讨论。

疑问句:
Is the statement debatable? = 这种说法有争议吗？
Is the conclusion truly debatable? = 结论真的有争议吗？

否定句:
The fact is not debatable. = 事实不容置疑。
The evidence is not debatable. = 证据是无可辩驳的。


PHRASE->
highly debatable = 非常有争议的
open to debate = 可以讨论的
a debatable point = 有争议的一点
subject to debate = 容易引起争议的
a matter of debate = 有争议的问题

词源:
From "debate" + "-able".  "Debate" itself comes from Old French *debat, from *debatre "to fight, struggle," from Latin *battuere "to beat, fight."  The "-able" suffix indicates something capable of being done.
