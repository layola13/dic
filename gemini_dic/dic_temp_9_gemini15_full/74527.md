fraternising: /ˌfrætərˈnaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 友好相处；与…兄弟般相处；(尤指士兵)与敌军士兵友好相处 |

英文解析:To fraternize is to associate or be friendly with someone, especially someone who is on the opposite side in a conflict.  It often implies an inappropriate level of friendliness or familiarity, particularly in a context where such behavior is forbidden or discouraged, such as between soldiers from opposing armies.


例句:

The soldiers were court-martialed for fraternising with the enemy.  =  那些士兵因与敌人友好相处而被军事法庭审判。

It's against regulations for officers to fraternize with enlisted men in that way. = 军官那样与士兵友好相处是违反规定的。


近义词: socialize, associate, mingle, hobnob


反义词:  estrange, alienate, isolate


词性: Verb (present participle)
