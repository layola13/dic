semicolon:/ˈsɛmɪˌkoʊlən/| n. |分号|A semicolon is a punctuation mark (;) used to connect two closely related independent clauses or to separate items in a list when those items already contain commas.  It indicates a closer relationship between the clauses than a period but a more distinct separation than a comma.|Example: The rain was pouring; the wind was howling.|近义词：comma (in some contexts), colon|反义词：period, comma (in other contexts)
