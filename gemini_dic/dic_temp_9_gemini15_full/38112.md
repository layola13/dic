compradors:/kɒmˈpɹeɪdɔːrz/| n.|买办；代理商|A comprador is a native of a country who acts as an intermediary between foreign companies or governments and the local population, often serving as an agent or interpreter.  They frequently handle trade and financial transactions.  Historically, compradors held positions of power and influence, often mediating between colonial powers and the colonized. The term often carries a negative connotation, implying exploitation or collaboration with oppressive forces.|例句：The British East India Company relied heavily on compradors to manage its trade in China. = 英国东印度公司非常依赖买办来管理其在中国的贸易。

The rise of compradors in Southeast Asia facilitated the expansion of European colonial powers. = 东南亚买办的兴起促进了欧洲殖民势力的扩张。|近义词：agent, intermediary, broker, middleman|反义词：producer, independent entrepreneur
