let-on:/letˈɒn/| v.|泄露，透露，假装|To reveal or disclose something; to pretend or feign.|例句：Don't let on that you know about the surprise party. = 不要泄露你知道惊喜派对的事。  例句：He let on that he was very busy. = 他假装自己很忙。|近义词：reveal, disclose, pretend, feign|反义词：conceal, hide, keep secret


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He lets on that he's rich. = 他假装自己很有钱。
She lets on to know everything. = 她假装什么都知道。

一般过去时:
They let on that they were lost. = 他们假装迷路了。
He let on to have seen the movie. = 他假装看过那部电影。

现在完成时:
She has let on to several people about the secret. = 她已经向好几个人透露了这个秘密。
Have they let on about their plans? = 他们有没有透露他们的计划？


过去完成时:
He had let on that he was leaving town before the news broke. = 在消息传开之前，他已经透露他要离开城镇了。
Had she let on about her feelings before? = 她以前有没有透露过她的感受？


将来时:
I will not let on about your surprise. = 我不会透露你的惊喜。
Will you let on to your parents about your grades? = 你会向你父母透露你的成绩吗？


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't let on about the surprise, it would have been perfect. = 如果他没有透露惊喜，那就完美了。
I wish I hadn't let on about my concerns. = 我真希望我没有透露我的担忧。


被动语态:
The secret was let on to the press. = 这个秘密被泄露给了媒体。
The truth was let on unintentionally. = 事实无意中被透露了。


疑问句:
Did he let on about the problem? = 他有没有透露这个问题？
Will she let on to anyone else? = 她会告诉其他人吗？


否定句:
I didn't let on that I was worried. = 我没有表现出我很担心。
They didn't let on to know anything about it. = 他们假装对此一无所知。


PHRASE->
let on to somebody about something  = 向某人透露某事
let on to be something = 假装是...
let somebody on to something = 让某人知道某事
let it be known = 让大家知道
don't let on = 不要泄露秘密
