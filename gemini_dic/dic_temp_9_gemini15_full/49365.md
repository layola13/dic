deliquescence:/ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛsəns/| n. |潮解；溶解；衰败 |The process by which a substance absorbs moisture from the atmosphere until it dissolves in the absorbed water.  This typically occurs with certain salts.  It's different from hygroscopy, where a substance absorbs moisture but doesn't dissolve. |例句：The deliquescence of the salt caused it to become a liquid.  盐的潮解使其变成液体。

The deliquescence of the moral fabric of society is a worrying trend. 社会道德结构的衰败是一个令人担忧的趋势。|近义词：dissolution, liquefaction |反义词：efflorescence |词性 n.
