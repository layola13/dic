hellbound: /ˈhɛlbaʊnd/ | adj. |地狱束缚的；注定要下地狱的；|doomed to hell; determined to go to hell; completely committed to a course of action, often a reckless or destructive one.|例句：The hellbound driver ignored all warnings.  地狱般驾驶员无视所有警告。|例句：He was hellbound on revenge. 他一心要报仇。|近义词：doomed, damned, condemned, obsessed|反义词：saved, redeemed, blessed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is hellbound on his mission. 他一心一意要完成他的任务。
The car is hellbound for destruction. 这辆车注定要毁坏。


一般过去时:
She was hellbound on getting the promotion. 她一心想要晋升。
They were hellbound for a fight. 他们势必会打一架。


现在完成时:
He has been hellbound on this project for years. 他多年来一直致力于这个项目。
They have been hellbound to their destructive habits. 他们一直沉迷于他们具有破坏性的习惯。


过去完成时:
He had been hellbound on revenge since his brother's death. 自从他兄弟去世后，他就一心想要报仇。
She had been hellbound on proving herself right. 她一直致力于证明自己是正确的。


将来时:
He will be hellbound on success. 他将全力以赴追求成功。
They will be hellbound to fail. 他们注定要失败。


虚拟语气:
If he were hellbound on revenge, he would have acted sooner. 如果他一心想要报仇，他本应该早点采取行动。
If she had been hellbound on her goal, she would have achieved it. 如果她一心一意追求她的目标，她本可以实现它。


被动语态:
(This adjective is rarely used passively.)


疑问句:
Is he hellbound on destroying the evidence? 他一心想要销毁证据吗？
Are they hellbound for disaster? 他们注定要遭遇灾难吗？


否定句:
He was not hellbound on winning at all costs. 他并非不惜一切代价都要赢。
They were not hellbound to follow the rules. 他们并非一定要遵守规则。
