interpellation:/ɪnˌtɜːrpəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 质询；盘问；(尤指政治上的)  interpellation;  | The act of questioning or challenging someone, especially in a formal or official way, often with the intention of exposing something.  This term is frequently used in political and legal contexts, but can also apply to other forms of questioning or challenge.  | Example: The opposition party's interpellation of the minister forced him to reveal the truth.  反对党对部长的质询迫使他透露了真相。 |近义词：questioning, interrogation, inquiry, challenge | 反义词：confirmation, endorsement
