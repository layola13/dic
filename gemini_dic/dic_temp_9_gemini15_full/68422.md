falsified:/ˈfɔːlsɪfaɪd/ | v. |伪造；篡改；歪曲 | To falsify something means to make it false or incorrect, often in a deliberate way.  This can involve changing documents, data, or statements to make them appear different from the truth. |例句：He falsified the documents to support his claim. 他伪造文件以支持他的说法。

例句：The scientist was accused of falsifying his research data.  这位科学家被指控伪造研究数据。


近义词：counterfeit, forge, fabricate, adulterate, doctor


反义词：verify, authenticate, validate


词性：动词
