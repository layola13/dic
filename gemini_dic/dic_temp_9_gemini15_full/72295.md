flounder:/ˈflaʊndər/| n., v.|比目鱼；挣扎；蹒跚而行；笨拙地行动 |A flatfish; to struggle or move clumsily; to fail or falter.|The flounder is a delicious fish. 比目鱼是一种美味的鱼。|He floundered in the mud. 他在泥里挣扎。|近义词：struggle, stumble, falter |反义词：succeed, thrive, excel


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flounders in his speech. = 他说话结结巴巴的。
The fish flounder on the seabed. = 鱼在海底游动。

一般过去时:
She floundered in the deep water. = 她在大水里挣扎。
The project floundered due to lack of funding. = 项目因缺乏资金而失败。


现在完成时:
The company has floundered since the CEO left. = 自从首席执行官离开后，公司一直经营不善。
He has floundered for years trying to find a job. = 他找工作多年，一直很不顺利。


过去完成时:
The ship had floundered before help arrived. = 在救援到来之前，船已经搁浅了。
The negotiations had floundered before they even began. = 谈判甚至还没开始就破裂了。


将来时:
The economy will flounder if there is no government intervention. = 如果政府不干预，经济将会衰退。
I think the team will flounder without their captain. = 我认为没有队长，球队将会一败涂地。


虚拟语气:
If he had not floundered, he would have won the race. = 如果他没有挣扎，他就会赢得比赛。
I wish I hadn't floundered so badly in the interview. = 我真希望我在面试中不要表现得那么糟糕。


被动语态:
The boat was floundered by the storm. = 船在暴风雨中搁浅了。
The plans were floundered by unexpected problems. = 计划由于意外问题而失败了。


疑问句:
Did he flounder during his presentation? = 他在演讲中是否挣扎？
Will the company flounder without its leader? = 没有领导，公司会失败吗？


否定句:
He didn't flounder in the exam. = 他考试没有失败。
The business didn't flounder despite the recession. = 尽管经济衰退，这家企业也没有倒闭。


PHRASE->
flounder around = 笨拙地移动
flounder about = 挣扎
flounder in the dark = 摸索前进
to flounder in one's speech = 说话结巴
flounder on = 继续艰难地进行


词源:
The word "flounder" ultimately comes from the Old English word *flunder, meaning "flatfish".  This shares a Germanic root with words related to flatness.  The verb sense, meaning "to struggle," developed later.
