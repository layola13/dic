keloid:/ˈkiːlɔɪd/| n. |瘢痕疙瘩，蟹足肿|An abnormally raised and often nodular scar that extends beyond the original wound boundary.  Keloids are caused by an overgrowth of collagen during the wound healing process. They are more common in people with darker skin. |例句：The burn left a large, unsightly keloid.  烧伤留下一个又大又难看的瘢痕疙瘩。|例句：He developed a keloid scar after surgery.  手术后他长了一个瘢痕疙瘩。|近义词：hypertrophic scar, raised scar |反义词：flat scar
