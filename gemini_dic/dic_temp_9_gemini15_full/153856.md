perch:/pɜːrtʃ/|/pɝtʃ/| n., v.|栖息处；鲈鱼；高处；（使）栖息|

英文解析：As a noun, "perch" refers to a place where a bird or other animal rests, a type of fish (the perch), or a high position or place. As a verb, it means to settle or alight on something, typically a bird on a branch.

例句：
The bird perched on the branch.  = 鸟栖息在树枝上。

The fisherman caught a large perch. = 渔夫钓到了一条大鲈鱼。

近义词： roost, rest, seat,  (for fish) bass

反义词：  (no direct antonyms, depending on context.  For example,  "fall" might be an antonym for the verb sense.)


词性：名词，动词

词源:
The word "perch" comes from the Old English word *perc, meaning "perch" (the fish).  The bird-resting sense is a later development, likely a metaphorical extension of the image of a fish resting on the bottom.
