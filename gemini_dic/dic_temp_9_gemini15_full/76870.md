ganglions: /ˈɡæŋɡliən/ | n. |神经节；淋巴结 | A ganglion is a group of nerve cell bodies located outside the central nervous system.  Ganglia often serve as relay points, connecting sensory neurons to motor neurons. They are also involved in various autonomic functions.  | Example: The doctor detected an enlarged ganglion in his wrist, suggesting carpal tunnel syndrome.  医生在他的手腕上发现了一个肿大的神经节，这表明是腕管综合征。 |近义词：node, nucleus |反义词：无
