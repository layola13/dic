implacental: /ˌɪmˈplæsɛntəl/ | adj. | 无胎盘的 |  Relating to or characteristic of animals that do not possess a placenta;  lacking a placenta.  The term is primarily used in zoology and comparative anatomy to describe the reproductive strategies of certain animals, particularly marsupials and monotremes. These animals give birth to relatively undeveloped young, often relying on a pouch or other means of external development. | Example:  The platypus is an implacental mammal. = 鸭嘴兽是一种无胎盘哺乳动物。 | 近义词: aplacental | 反义词: placental
