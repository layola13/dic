catalyzes:/ˈkætəlaɪzɪz/ | v. | 催化；促成 | To cause or accelerate (a reaction, process, or change) | The enzyme catalyzes the breakdown of sugars. 这种酶催化糖的分解。 The discovery catalyzed a surge in research.  这一发现促使了研究的激增。 | accelerates, facilitates, triggers | inhibits, hinders, obstructs | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The enzyme catalyzes the reaction. = 这种酶催化该反应。
Heat catalyzes the chemical process. = 热量催化化学过程。


一般过去时:
The catalyst catalyzed the reaction quickly. = 催化剂迅速催化了反应。
The event catalyzed a significant shift in public opinion. = 此事件促成了公众舆论的重大转变。


现在完成时:
The new policy has catalyzed economic growth. = 新政策促进了经济增长。
This discovery has catalyzed further research in this field. = 这一发现推动了该领域的进一步研究。


过去完成时:
The previous reforms had catalyzed the economic boom. = 先前的改革促成了经济繁荣。
The initial investment had catalyzed the expansion of the company. = 初始投资促使了公司的扩张。


将来时:
The upcoming conference will catalyze collaboration between researchers. = 即将举行的会议将促进研究人员之间的合作。
The new technology will catalyze a revolution in the industry. = 新技术将催化该行业的革命。


虚拟语气:
If we had a better catalyst, the reaction would catalyze faster. = 如果我们有更好的催化剂，反应就会更快。
If the government implemented the right policies, it would catalyze economic recovery. = 如果政府实施正确的政策，它将促进经济复苏。


被动语态:
The reaction was catalyzed by the enzyme. = 该反应是由酶催化的。
Economic growth was catalyzed by government investment. = 经济增长是由政府投资促成的。


疑问句:
What factors catalyzed the change? = 哪些因素促成了这种变化？
What will catalyze the next technological breakthrough? = 什么将催化下一次技术突破？


否定句:
The lack of funding did not catalyze the project. = 资金不足并没有促进该项目。
The current situation does not catalyze innovation. = 当前形势并没有促进创新。


PHRASE->
catalyze a reaction: 催化反应
catalyze change: 促成改变
catalyze growth: 促进增长
catalyze innovation: 促进创新
catalyze development: 促进发展
