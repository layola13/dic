microscopes:/ˈmaɪkrəskoʊps/| n. |显微镜|An instrument used to see objects that are too small to be seen by the naked eye.  Microscopes use lenses to magnify the image of an object, allowing for detailed observation of its structure and composition.|Example: Scientists use microscopes to study cells and microorganisms. 科学家使用显微镜来研究细胞和微生物。

Example: The electron microscope allows us to see even smaller details than a light microscope. 电子显微镜让我们能够看到比光学显微镜更小的细节。


近义词：magnifier, enlarger | 反义词：None (no direct antonym)|名词
