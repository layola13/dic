dingdong: /ˈdɪŋdɒŋ/ | int. | 叮当声，门铃声 |  The sound of a bell, typically a doorbell or a similar device.  It is often used onomatopoeically to represent the sound itself. | Example: The doorbell went dingdong. 门铃响了叮当声。 |近义词：clang, chime, peal | 反义词: silence


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The doorbell dings frequently. = 门铃经常响。
It dings whenever someone rings the bell. = 每当有人按门铃时它就响。


一般过去时:
The bell dinged loudly. = 铃声响得很响亮。
It dinged three times before he answered. = 它响了三次他才开门。


现在完成时:
The doorbell has dinged several times this morning. = 今早门铃已经响了好几次了。
The bell has dinged, announcing a visitor. = 门铃响了，宣布有访客到来。


过去完成时:
The bell had dinged before he even reached the door. =  在他到达门口之前，门铃就已经响了。
The bell had dinged repeatedly, but no one answered. = 门铃反复响过，但没有人应答。


将来时:
The bell will ding when the package arrives. =  包裹到达时铃声会响。
It will ding again soon, I’m sure. = 我肯定它很快会再次响起。


虚拟语气:
If the bell dinged, I would answer it. = 如果门铃响了，我会去开门。
I wish the bell dinged to signal the end of the meeting. = 我希望铃声响起以表示会议结束。


被动语态:
The bell is rarely dinged these days. =  这些天门铃很少响。
The bell was dinged incessantly throughout the night. =  整个晚上门铃不停地响。


疑问句:
Did the bell ding? = 门铃响了吗？
Has the bell dinged yet? = 门铃响了吗？


否定句:
The bell didn't ding. = 门铃没有响。
The bell hasn't dinged yet. = 门铃还没响。


PHRASE->
ding-dong ditch (恶作剧): A prank where someone rings a doorbell and runs away. 恶作剧：有人按门铃然后跑掉。
ding-dong bell (门铃): A bell that rings when someone presses a button on a doorbell. 门铃：有人按门铃上的按钮时会响的铃。
a ding-dong battle (激烈的争论): A long and noisy argument. 激烈的争论：一场冗长而嘈杂的争论。
There was a ding-dong battle over the issue. 关于这个问题，双方展开了激烈的争论。
The ding-dong of the bells. 铃铛的叮当声。
