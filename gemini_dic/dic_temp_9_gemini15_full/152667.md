peaking:/ˈpiːkɪŋ/| v.|达到顶峰；达到最高点；|Reaching the highest point or level; the act of reaching a peak.|例句：The economy is peaking.  经济正在达到顶峰。
例句：The mountain peak is peaking above the clouds. 山峰高耸于云端之上。|近义词：climaxing, culminating, topping out|反义词：bottoming out, declining, falling


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sales are peaking this quarter. = 本季度销售额达到顶峰。
The athlete is peaking in his performance. = 这名运动员的竞技状态达到巅峰。


一般过去时:
The stock market peaked last year. = 股市去年达到顶峰。
The river peaked after the heavy rain. = 大雨过后，河水水位达到最高点。


现在完成时:
The project has peaked in terms of funding. = 项目的资金投入已达到顶峰。
The band has peaked in popularity. = 乐队的人气已达到顶峰。


过去完成时:
The population had peaked before the war. = 在战争爆发前，人口数量已达到顶峰。
The temperature had peaked by noon. = 中午时分温度已达到最高点。


将来时:
The demand will peak next month. = 下个月需求将达到顶峰。
The sun will peak around noon. = 太阳将在中午左右达到最高点。


虚拟语气:
If the economy peaked earlier, we would have seen different results. = 如果经济早些达到顶峰，我们将看到不同的结果。
I wish the performance had peaked sooner. = 我希望演出早些达到高潮。


被动语态:
The demand is expected to be peaked next month. = 预计下个月需求将达到顶峰。
The event was peaked by a spectacular firework display. = 活动以壮观的烟火表演达到高潮。


疑问句:
Has the traffic peaked yet? = 交通拥堵达到高峰了吗？
When did the sales peak? = 销售额何时达到顶峰？


否定句:
The fever didn't peak until late evening. = 直到傍晚体温才达到最高点。
The project hasn't peaked yet. = 项目尚未达到顶峰。
