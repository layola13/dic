over-optimistic: /ˌoʊvərˌɑːptɪˈmɪstɪk/ | adj. | 过度乐观的；过于乐观 | Excessively optimistic; expecting things to turn out better than is likely.  It suggests an optimism that is unrealistic or naive. | 例句：He was over-optimistic about the chances of success. 他对成功的几率过于乐观。


He's being over-optimistic about the project's deadline; it's unlikely to be finished on time. 他对项目的截止日期过于乐观；不太可能按时完成。


近义词：unrealistically optimistic, excessively hopeful, overly sanguine, too positive | 反义词：pessimistic, cynical, realistic, skeptical


词源：over- (prefix indicating excess) + optimistic (from Latin optimus "best" + -istic, a suffix forming adjectives)  The word "optimistic" itself reflects a philosophical stance emphasizing the best possible outcome.
