chill-out:/tʃɪlˈaʊt/ | v. |放松，冷静下来 | to relax completely; to calm down | 例句：I need to chill out after a long day at work. 我需要在辛苦工作一天后放松一下。  例句：Just chill out, everything will be alright. 放松点，一切都会好起来的。 | 近义词：relax, unwind, de-stress | 反义词：stress, tense up


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He chills out by listening to music. = 他通过听音乐来放松。
They chill out at the beach every weekend. = 他们每个周末都在海滩上放松。

一般过去时:
I chilled out after finishing my exams. = 我考试结束后放松了一下。
We chilled out watching a movie last night. = 我们昨晚看电影放松了。

现在完成时:
She has chilled out significantly since starting yoga. = 自从开始练习瑜伽以来，她已经放松了很多。
They have chilled out a lot after their vacation. = 他们度假后放松了很多。

过去完成时:
He had chilled out before the important meeting. = 在重要的会议之前，他已经放松了下来。
We had chilled out by the pool all afternoon. = 我们整个下午都在泳池边放松。

将来时:
I will chill out by the lake this weekend. = 我这个周末将在湖边放松。
We will chill out and play some games later. = 我们稍后会放松一下，玩一些游戏。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would chill out more often. = 如果我有更多时间，我会更经常地放松。
I wish I could chill out and not worry about work. = 我希望我能放松下来，别为工作担心。

被动语态: (Chill out is typically not used in the passive voice because it's an intransitive verb.)


疑问句:
How do you chill out after a stressful day? = 你如何在压力大的一天后放松？
Where do you usually chill out? = 你通常在哪里放松？


否定句:
I couldn't chill out because of the noise. = 因为噪音我无法放松。
He didn't chill out even after taking a long break. = 即使休息了很长时间，他也没有放松。
