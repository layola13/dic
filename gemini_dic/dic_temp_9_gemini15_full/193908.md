self-duplicating: /ˌsɛlfˈdjuːplɪkeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 自我复制的，自我繁殖的 | Able to create exact copies of itself.  This term often applies to biological entities like cells and viruses, but can also be used metaphorically for ideas, systems, or processes.  | Example:  The self-duplicating nature of the virus made it extremely difficult to contain. 自我复制的病毒特性使其极难控制。


The self-duplicating code caused the computer system to crash. 自我复制的代码导致计算机系统崩溃。


近义词: self-replicating, reproducing, proliferating | 反义词:  unique, singular (in the sense of not being able to replicate)


词性: adjective

词源:
The word is a compound of "self-" (meaning "of, by, or for oneself") and "duplicating" (from "duplicate," meaning "to make an exact copy").  The term's usage became prominent with the understanding of cellular reproduction and the rise of computer science and artificial intelligence.
