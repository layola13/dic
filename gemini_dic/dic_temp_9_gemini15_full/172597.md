pyrophosphate:/ˌpaɪroʊˈfɒsfet/ | n. |焦磷酸盐|Pyrophosphate is a salt or ester of pyrophosphoric acid. It is formed by the condensation of two phosphate groups, resulting in a P-O-P bond.  Pyrophosphates are used in various applications, including food additives, detergents, and in some biological processes.|Example:  Sodium pyrophosphate is commonly used as a food additive to prevent discoloration and improve texture.|近义词:diphosphate|反义词:None
