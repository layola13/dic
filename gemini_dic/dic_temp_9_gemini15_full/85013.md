haranguing: /həˈræŋɡɪŋ/ | v. | 滔滔不绝地训斥；长篇大论地责骂 |  The act of delivering a long, passionate, and typically angry speech.  It often implies a forceful and often aggressive attempt to persuade or influence the audience. | 例句：The politician spent an hour haranguing the crowd about the dangers of the proposed legislation.  政治家花了一个小时向人群滔滔不绝地训斥拟议立法法的危险性。

The angry professor harangued his students for their poor performance on the exam.  愤怒的教授因学生考试成绩差而长篇大论地责骂他们。


近义词：tirade, diatribe, rant, lecture, sermon | 反义词：praise, commend, compliment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He harangues the crowd every Sunday. = 他每个星期天都向人群发表长篇大论。
She harangues her employees about punctuality. = 她总是训斥她的员工不准时。


一般过去时:
The speaker harangued the audience for two hours. = 演讲者向听众发表了两个小时的长篇大论。
He harangued the crowd about the importance of voting. = 他向人群慷慨激昂地演讲投票的重要性。


现在完成时:
The mayor has harangued the public about the need for new taxes. = 市长一直就征收新税的必要性向公众发表长篇大论。
She has harangued her children about the importance of education. = 她一直告诫她的孩子们教育的重要性。


过去完成时:
He had harangued the committee for weeks before they finally agreed. = 在委员会最终同意之前，他已经向他们发表了数周的长篇大论。
They had harangued the board about the budget cuts. = 他们曾就预算削减问题向董事会发表过长篇大论。


将来时:
He will harangue the protestors at the rally. = 他将在集会上向抗议者发表长篇大论。
She will harangue the students about the importance of studying. = 她将向学生们发表关于学习重要性的长篇大论。


虚拟语气:
If he harangued them more politely, they might listen. = 如果他更礼貌地向他们训斥，他们可能会听。
I wish he hadn't harangued me so harshly. = 我真希望他没有那么严厉地训斥我。


被动语态:
The crowd was harangued by the speaker for hours. = 听众被演讲者训斥了好几个小时。
The students were harangued about their poor grades. = 学生们因为成绩差而受到训斥。


疑问句:
Was he haranguing the crowd about politics? = 他是在向人群发表关于政治的长篇大论吗？
Is she still haranguing the board? = 她还在向董事会发表长篇大论吗？


否定句:
He did not harangue the crowd. = 他没有向人群发表长篇大论。
She did not harangue her employees today. = 她今天没有训斥她的员工。


PHRASE->
harangue against = 反对…发表长篇大论
harangue someone about = 就…对某人发表长篇大论
harangue on = 就…发表长篇大论
a fiery harangue = 一场激烈的训斥
a political harangue = 一场政治性的训斥


词源:
From Middle French *harangue, from Old French harangue ("speech, address"), of unknown origin, possibly related to the Old High German hrūgan ("to shout, cry out").  The word's evolution reflects its historical association with public speaking, often in a contentious or impassioned manner.
