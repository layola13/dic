enclaves:/ˈɛŋkleɪvz/| n. |飞地；被包围的领土；（文化或社会方面的）聚集地 | A geographically isolated area or group, especially one surrounded by a foreign territory or population.  An enclave is a distinct area or group within a larger area or group.  This can refer to a geographical location, a cultural group, or a social community. |例句：Tibet is geographically an enclave of China.  西藏在地理上是中国的飞地。

The Italian community in New York forms a vibrant cultural enclave.  纽约的意大利裔社区形成了一个充满活力的文化聚集地。|近义词：pocket, island, community |反义词：mainland, metropolis
