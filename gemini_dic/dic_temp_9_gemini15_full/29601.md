categorematic:/ˌkætɪɡəˈrɛmætɪk/| adj.|范畴的，能指称的|  A categorematic term is a word or phrase that can stand alone as the subject or predicate of a proposition, unlike a syncategorematic term, which only has meaning in conjunction with other terms.  It refers to a word that has a meaning in and of itself, independent of context.|Example:  "Dog" is a categorematic term because it refers to a specific type of animal.  In the sentence "The dog barked," "dog" is the subject and has independent meaning.|近义词：descriptive, significant|反义词：syncategorematic
