sawbuck:/ˈsɔːbæk/| n. |十美元；支架|A sawbuck is slang for ten dollars,  originating from the resemblance of a sawhorse (a wooden frame used to support lumber while sawing) to the Roman numeral X (ten).  It can also refer to the sawhorse itself.|例句：He paid ten bucks, a sawbuck, for the tickets. 他花了十美元（一个“十美元”）买了票。|近义词：tenner, ten dollars|反义词:  None specifically, but could be contrasted with larger sums of money.|名词
