commission:/kəˈmɪʃən/ | n. vt. |佣金；委员会；委托；犯罪；授权 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "commission" refers to a percentage or fixed fee paid to an agent or employee for their services, often in sales. It can also refer to a group of people officially appointed to investigate or perform a specific task (a committee or commission).  Furthermore, it can mean the act of giving someone an official task or job, or the act of committing a crime or other wrongdoing.

As a verb, "commission" means to formally request or authorize the creation of something, such as a work of art or a report. It can also mean to appoint someone to a job or task, or to commit a crime.


例句:
The salesperson earned a 10% commission on each sale.  = 销售员每笔销售赚取10%的佣金。

The government set up a commission to investigate the accident. = 政府成立了一个委员会来调查事故。

The artist was commissioned to paint a portrait of the queen. = 该艺术家受委托为女王画一幅肖像。

He was commissioned as a lieutenant in the army. = 他被任命为陆军中尉。

反义词:
