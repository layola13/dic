pangenesis:/ˌpænˈdʒɛnɪsɪs/| n. |泛生论|A now-discredited theory of heredity proposing that all body parts contribute to the germ cells (reproductive cells) that transmit hereditary characteristics.|The theory of pangenesis, proposed by Darwin, suggested that gemmules from all parts of the body were collected in the reproductive organs and transmitted to offspring.  This theory is no longer accepted.|近义词：暂无 |反义词：暂无
