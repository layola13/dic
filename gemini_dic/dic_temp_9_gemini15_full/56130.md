docudramatist: /ˌdɒkjuːˈdræmətɪst/ | n. |纪实剧作家| A docudramatist is a writer who creates docudramas, which are films or television programs that blend documentary techniques with dramatic storytelling.  They use factual events and characters as a basis, but often add fictional elements to create a more compelling narrative.  The line between fact and fiction can sometimes be blurred in docudramas, requiring careful consideration by the docudramatist. | Example:  The docudramatist skillfully intertwined real historical accounts with imagined conversations to create a captivating portrayal of the event.  纪实剧作家巧妙地将真实的史实与虚构的对话交织在一起，创造了一个引人入胜的事件描绘。  |近义词: screenwriter, playwright (in the context of docudrama)|反义词:  fiction writer (in a sense that they primarily focus on fictional narratives)|词性:名词
