martins:/ˈmɑːrtɪnz/| n. |马丁（复数）, 燕子（复数）|The word "martins" is the plural of "martin," referring to several species of swallows in the family Hirundinidae.  It is most commonly used to refer to purple martins (Progne subis), a large North American swallow known for its social nesting habits.  The term can also refer to other species of swallows collectively.| Example:  Purple martins are known for their impressive aerial acrobatics. 紫燕以其令人印象深刻的空中特技而闻名。  Many different species of martins were observed during the birdwatching trip. 在观鸟旅行中观察到许多不同种类的燕子。|近义词: swallows (general term), birds|反义词:  (no direct antonyms, context-dependent)|名词
