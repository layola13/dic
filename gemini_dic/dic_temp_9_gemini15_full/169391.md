prones:/proʊnz/| adj.|倾向于…的；易于…的；俯卧的 | Likely to suffer from or be affected by (a disease or ailment);disposed or inclined to do something. |例句：He is prone to headaches. 他容易头痛。|例句：She's prone to exaggeration. 她爱夸张。|近义词：susceptible, liable, inclined, disposed|反义词：resistant, immune


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is prone to accidents. = 他容易发生事故。
The area is prone to flooding. = 这个地区容易发生洪水。

一般过去时:
She was prone to illness as a child. = 她小时候容易生病。
The old bridge was prone to collapse. = 那座旧桥容易坍塌。

现在完成时:
He has been prone to depression since the accident. = 自从事故发生以来，他一直容易抑郁。
The company has been prone to financial difficulties in recent years. = 近年来，该公司一直容易面临财务困难。

过去完成时:
The building had been prone to leaks for years. = 这座建筑多年来一直容易漏水。
They had been prone to arguing before the divorce. = 离婚前他们就容易吵架。


将来时:
The new dam will be prone to cracking under pressure. = 新水坝在压力下容易开裂。
He will be prone to making mistakes if he's tired. = 如果他累了，他就会容易犯错。


虚拟语气:
If he were prone to anger, he would have reacted differently. = 如果他容易生气，他会以不同的方式反应。
I wish I weren't so prone to procrastination. = 我希望我不那么容易拖延。


被动语态:
The region is prone to being hit by hurricanes. = 该地区容易遭受飓风的袭击。
The system is prone to being hacked. = 该系统容易被黑客攻击。


疑问句:
Is he prone to getting colds? = 他容易感冒吗？
Is this type of wood prone to warping? = 这种木材容易变形吗？


否定句:
He is not prone to violence. = 他不容易暴力。
This material is not prone to staining. = 这种材料不容易染色。



PHRASE->
