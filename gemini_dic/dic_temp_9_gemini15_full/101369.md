ionizes:/ˈaɪənaɪzɪz/| v. |电离；使电离|To ionize something means to convert it into ions, which are electrically charged atoms or molecules. This usually involves adding or removing electrons.  Ionization is a fundamental process in many areas of science and technology, including chemistry, physics, and biology.|Example 1:  High-energy radiation, such as X-rays, ionizes atoms in the air.  = 高能辐射，例如X射线，会使空气中的原子电离。|Example 2: The strong electric field in a lightning strike ionizes the air molecules. = 雷击中强大的电场使空气分子电离。|近义词：ionize, electrify|反义词：neutralize
