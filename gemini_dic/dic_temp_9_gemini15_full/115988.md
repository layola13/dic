marshal:/ˈmɑːrʃəl/ | n., vt. | 元帅；警官；整理；引导 | To arrange or assemble (troops, equipment, etc.) in order; to lead or guide; a high-ranking military officer; a police officer. |例句：The general marshaled his troops for the attack.  将军们整顿军队准备进攻。|例句：The police marshaled the crowd away from the scene. 警察引导人群离开现场。|近义词：arrange, organize, lead, guide |反义词：disorganize, scatter

词源:
The word "marshal" ultimately derives from Old French "maréchal," meaning "horse servant," "groom," or "farrier."  It evolved through the Middle English "marischal,"  referring to an officer in charge of horses and later military forces.  The modern meanings evolved from this military context.
