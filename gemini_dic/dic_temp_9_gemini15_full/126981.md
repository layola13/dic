narwhal:/ˈnɑːrwəl/ | n. |独角鲸 |A narwhal is an Arctic whale known for its long, spiraled tusk, which is actually an elongated tooth.  Narwhals are social animals, often found in pods. They feed primarily on fish and squid. |Example: The narwhal's unique tusk is a remarkable adaptation to its environment.  独角鲸独特的长牙是其对环境的显著适应。  |近义词: Arctic whale |反义词:  (No direct antonym)

词源:
The word "narwhal" comes from Old Norse *narhvalr, a compound of *nar-, meaning "corpse," and *hvalr, meaning "whale." The etymology is debated, with some theories suggesting the "corpse" element refers to the whale's pale colour.
