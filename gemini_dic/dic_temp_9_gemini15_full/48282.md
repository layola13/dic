decoy:/dɪˈkɔɪ/| v. n.|诱饵；诱骗；欺骗|To lure or entice someone or something into a trap or unfavorable situation, often by means of deception.  It can also refer to something used to lure or distract.|例句：The police used a decoy car to catch the robbers. = 警方用诱饵车抓捕了劫匪。

The hunters used a decoy to attract the ducks. = 猎人用诱饵来吸引鸭子。|近义词：lure, bait, trap, entice, mislead|反义词：protect, safeguard, defend

词源:

The word "decoy" ultimately derives from the Dutch word "kooi," meaning "cage" or "coop."  Over time, the word evolved through Middle Dutch and Middle French, with its meaning shifting from a cage used to trap birds to the act of luring birds (and later, people) into a trap.
