disard:/dɪˈskɑːrd/ | vt. |丢弃；抛弃；放弃 |To throw something away or get rid of something because you no longer want or need it.  |例句：He discarded the old shoes. 他扔掉了旧鞋子。|例句：She discarded her initial plan and tried a new approach. 她放弃了最初的计划，尝试了一种新的方法。|近义词：throw away, abandon, reject, relinquish |反义词：keep, retain, preserve, cherish |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He discards old newspapers every week. = 他每周都丢弃旧报纸。
She discards her worn-out clothes. = 她丢弃了她穿坏的衣服。

一般过去时:
They discarded the broken chair. = 他们丢弃了那把坏椅子。
He discarded his old habits. = 他改掉了他的旧习惯。

现在完成时:
I have discarded all my old letters. = 我已经扔掉了我所有的旧信。
She has discarded her old beliefs. = 她已经放弃了她旧有的信仰。

过去完成时:
They had discarded the evidence before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前，他们已经销毁了证据。
She had discarded her dreams before she met him. = 在遇到他之前，她已经放弃了她的梦想。

将来时:
We will discard this outdated technology soon. = 我们很快就会淘汰这项过时的技术。
He will discard his old phone after buying a new one. = 他买新手机后会扔掉旧手机。


被动语态:
The old furniture was discarded. = 旧家具被丢弃了。
The plan was discarded due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，该计划被放弃了。

疑问句:
Will you discard the empty bottles? = 你会丢弃空瓶子吗？
Should we discard this broken appliance? = 我们应该丢弃这个坏掉的电器吗？

否定句:
He did not discard his valuable collection. = 他没有丢弃他珍贵的收藏。
She refused to discard her cherished memories. = 她拒绝放弃她珍爱的回忆。


PHRASE->
discard a theory = 抛弃一种理论
discard a plan = 放弃一个计划
discard an idea = 放弃一个主意
discard evidence = 销毁证据
discard one's pride = 放下自尊
