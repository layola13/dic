词源:

* **Manganese:**  From Latin *magnes*, possibly referring to a magnet due to some ores' magnetic properties, or from Greek *μαγνήτης* (magnētēs), meaning "magnet".  The connection is due to early confusion of manganese ores with magnets.

* **Dioxide:** From "di-" (two) + "oxide" (a compound of oxygen).  "Oxide" ultimately derives from Greek *ὀξύς* (oxús), meaning "sharp, acid," reflecting the acidic properties of many oxides.


Therefore, "manganese dioxide" literally means "a compound containing two oxygen atoms per atom of manganese".
