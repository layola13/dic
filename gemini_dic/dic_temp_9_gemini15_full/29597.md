catechumenate:/ˌkætɪˈkjumənət/| n.|教理问答学习时期；教理问答班；受洗前的教导时期|The period of instruction given to catechumens before baptism; the institution or system of such instruction.  It refers to the period of instruction and preparation for Christian baptism, particularly in the early Church. The catechumenate involved a process of spiritual formation and education, encompassing moral instruction, liturgical training, and community engagement.|The early church had a formal catechumenate that lasted for several years.|近义词：instruction, preparation, training|反义词：baptism (in the sense that it precedes baptism)
