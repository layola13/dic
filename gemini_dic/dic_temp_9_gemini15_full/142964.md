outawed:/ˈaʊtˌlɔːd/| adj.|被逐出的；被放逐的；被宣布为非法的|Declared illegal or forbidden; banished or outlawed.  The practice of dueling was outlawed centuries ago.|近义词：illegal, prohibited, forbidden, banished|反义词：legal, permitted, allowed


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The government outlaws the sale of certain drugs. = 政府禁止销售某些毒品。
This activity is outlawed in many countries. = 这种活动在许多国家是被禁止的。

一般过去时:
They outlawed slavery in the 19th century. = 他们在19世纪废除了奴隶制。
The king outlawed the use of certain weapons. = 国王禁止使用某些武器。

现在完成时:
Many harmful substances have been outlawed. = 许多有害物质已被禁止。
Smoking has been outlawed in many public places. = 许多公共场所已禁止吸烟。

过去完成时:
Before the war, the practice had been outlawed for many years.  = 战争之前，这项活动已经被禁止多年了。
The substance had been outlawed long before its dangers were fully understood. = 在其危险被完全了解之前很久，这种物质就已经被禁止了。

将来时:
They will likely outlaw the use of this technology soon. = 他们很可能会很快禁止使用这项技术。
The new law will outlaw the sale of this product. = 新法将禁止销售该产品。


虚拟语气:
If they outlawed that activity, crime would decrease. = 如果他们禁止这项活动，犯罪就会减少。
I wish they outlawed the use of plastic bags. = 我希望他们禁止使用塑料袋。


被动语态:
The sale of the product was outlawed last year. = 该产品的销售于去年被禁止。
That practice has been outlawed for decades. = 那项活动已被禁止了几十年。


疑问句:
Was that activity ever outlawed? = 那项活动曾经被禁止过吗？
Will this substance be outlawed in the future? = 未来这种物质会被禁止吗？


否定句:
The activity was not outlawed until recently. = 这项活动直到最近才被禁止。
That practice has not yet been outlawed. = 那项活动尚未被禁止。


PHRASE->

outlawed substance: 被禁止的物质
outlawed activity: 被禁止的活动
outlawed practice: 被禁止的做法
outlawed weapon: 被禁止的武器
being outlawed: 正在被取缔
