quasi-empty:/ˈkwɑːziˈɛmpi/| adj.|几乎空的；近乎空的；|The word "quasi-empty" means nearly empty, but not completely so. It suggests a state where there is a very small amount of something remaining.  It implies a degree of emptiness that is close to, but not quite, total emptiness.|例句：The almost empty bottle still contained a quasi-empty amount of wine. 这几乎空了的瓶子里还剩下少量葡萄酒。 The theater was quasi-empty; only a handful of people attended the play. 剧院几乎空了；只有少数人观看了演出。|近义词：nearly empty, almost empty, sparsely populated|反义词：full, crowded, replete
