retired:/rɪˈtaɪərd/ | adj. |退休的；退役的 |  A retired person is someone who has stopped working, typically because of age or ill health.  The adjective describes the state of being retired.  |  My grandfather is retired and enjoys gardening. 我的祖父退休了，喜欢园艺。 He is a retired teacher. 他是一位退休教师。|  pensioned,  ex- | working, employed |


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is retired. = 他退休了。
She is retired from her job as a doctor. = 她从医生的工作中退休了。


一般过去时:
He retired last year. = 他去年退休了。
She retired from teaching at the age of 65. = 她65岁时从教书工作中退休了。


现在完成时:
He has been retired for five years. = 他退休五年了。
She has retired from the company after 30 years of service. = 她为公司服务了30年后退休了。


过去完成时:
He had retired before the company went bankrupt. = 公司破产之前，他已经退休了。
She had retired before her health problems became severe. = 在她的健康问题变得严重之前，她就已经退休了。


将来时:
He will retire next year. = 他明年将退休。
She will retire when she turns 60. = 她60岁时将退休。


虚拟语气:
If I were retired, I would travel the world. = 如果我退休了，我会周游世界。
I wish I were retired and could relax more. = 我希望我已经退休了，可以更放松。


被动语态:
(The term "retired" is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes a state of being, not an action performed on someone.)


疑问句:
Is he retired? = 他退休了吗？
Are you retired yet? = 你退休了吗？


否定句:
He is not retired yet. = 他还没退休。
She is not retired; she still works full-time. = 她没有退休；她仍然全职工作。
