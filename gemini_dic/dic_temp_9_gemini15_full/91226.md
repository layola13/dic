词源:
The word "hydrocarburet" is a combination of "hydro-" (from Greek "ὕδωρ" - hydōr, meaning "water") referring to hydrogen, and "carburet" (from French "carbure," ultimately from Latin "carbo" meaning "charcoal" or "carbon"), referring to carbon.  It indicates a compound made of hydrogen and carbon.  The more modern and widely accepted term is "hydrocarbon".
