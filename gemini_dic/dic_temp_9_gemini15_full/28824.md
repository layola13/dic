carousing:/kəˈraʊzɪŋ/| v.|狂欢；寻欢作乐；喧闹|Engaging in boisterous, drunken merrymaking.|例句：The students were carousing in the streets after the football game. 学生们在足球赛后在街上狂欢。|例句：Their carousing lasted well into the night. 他们的狂欢持续到深夜。|近义词：reveling, partying, celebrating, merrymaking|反义词：sobriety, quietude, stillness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They carouse every weekend. = 他们每个周末都狂欢。
We often carouse at the pub. = 我们经常在酒吧狂欢。

一般过去时:
They caroused all night long. = 他们整夜狂欢。
We caroused until the early hours. = 我们狂欢到凌晨。

现在完成时:
They have caroused too much lately. = 他们最近狂欢过头了。
We have caroused at many different locations. = 我们在许多不同的地点狂欢过。

过去完成时:
They had caroused before the accident happened. = 事故发生前他们就已经狂欢了。
We had caroused for hours before we went home. = 我们回家前已经狂欢了好几个小时。

将来时:
They will carouse again next weekend. = 他们下周末还会再次狂欢。
We will carouse to celebrate our victory. = 我们将狂欢来庆祝我们的胜利。

虚拟语气:
If they weren't so tired, they would carouse. = 如果他们不那么累，他们就会去狂欢。
I wish I had caroused more when I was younger. = 我希望我年轻时能多狂欢一些。

被动语态: (很少用被动语态)
The streets were caroused in after the parade. (街道在游行后被狂欢的人群占据)  This is an unusual usage.
The party was marked by excessive carousing. (派对以过度狂欢为特征)


疑问句:
Were they carousing last night? = 他们昨晚在狂欢吗？
Will they be carousing this weekend? = 他们这个周末会狂欢吗？

否定句:
They did not carouse last night. = 他们昨晚没有狂欢。
We will not carouse tonight. = 我们今晚不会狂欢。
