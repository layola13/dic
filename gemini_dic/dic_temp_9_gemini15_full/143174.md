outclimb:/ˌaʊtˈklaɪm/| vt.|超越…攀登；胜过…|To climb higher or further than (someone or something).  It implies surpassing someone in climbing or achieving something better than someone else.|例句：He outclimbed all the other competitors. 他超越了所有其他竞争者。
例句：The new skyscraper outclimbed all the existing buildings in the city. 这座新摩天大楼的高度超过了城市里所有现有的建筑物。|近义词：surpass, exceed, outdo |反义词：underclimb (not a common word)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He outclimbs everyone on the team. = 他在团队中攀登能力超过所有人。
They outclimb their rivals in sales this year. = 今年他们在销售额上超过了竞争对手。

一般过去时:
She outclimbed her brother to the top of the mountain. = 她攀登到山顶，超过了她的兄弟。
The experienced climber outclimbed the novice with ease. = 经验丰富的登山者轻松地超过了新手。

现在完成时:
He has outclimbed all previous records. = 他已经超越了所有之前的记录。
She has outclimbed her previous best time on this route. = 她在这条路线上的时间已经超过了她之前的最好成绩。

过去完成时:
By the time I reached the summit, she had already outclimbed me. = 当我到达山顶时，她已经超过我了。
He had outclimbed his fear and reached the top. = 他克服了恐惧，到达了顶峰。

将来时:
She will outclimb her rivals in the upcoming competition. = 在即将到来的比赛中，她将超越她的对手。
We believe he will outclimb the challenges ahead. = 我们相信他会克服未来的挑战。


虚拟语气:
If he trained harder, he might outclimb his competitors. = 如果他训练更努力，他可能会超过他的竞争对手。
I wish I could outclimb my limitations. = 我希望我能克服我的局限性。

被动语态:
No one has ever been outclimbed on this route before. = 在这条路线上前所未有的人被超越。
His record was outclimbed last year. = 他的记录在去年被打破了。

疑问句:
Can he outclimb that difficult section? = 他能攀登过那段困难的路段吗？
Will she be outclimbed by her younger sister? = 她会被她妹妹超越吗？

否定句:
He didn't outclimb his opponent in the final round. = 他在决赛中没有超过他的对手。
They were unable to outclimb the sheer rock face. = 他们无法攀登那陡峭的岩壁。
