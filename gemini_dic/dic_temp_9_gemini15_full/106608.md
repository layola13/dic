/kʊ/ | int. |  咳，咳咳 | An involuntary sound made in the throat, often to clear it or to express mild pain or surprise.  | Example: He coughed politely before speaking. 他在说话前礼貌地咳嗽了一声。| 近义词：hack, clear one's throat | 反义词：无


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He coughs frequently. = 他经常咳嗽。
She coughs a lot during allergy season. = 过敏季节她咳嗽得很厉害。


一般过去时:
He coughed all night. = 他整夜都在咳嗽。
She coughed up some blood. = 她咳出了血。


现在完成时:
He has coughed all day. = 他咳了一整天了。
She has coughed so much that she's hoarse. = 她咳得太多了，嗓子都哑了。


过去完成时:
He had coughed for weeks before he saw a doctor. = 他咳嗽了好几个星期才去看医生。
She had coughed so violently that she felt dizzy. = 她剧烈地咳嗽，感觉头晕。


将来时:
He will cough again if he smokes. = 如果他抽烟，他还会咳嗽。
She will probably cough less after taking the medicine. = 服药后她可能会少咳嗽一些。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't coughed so much, he wouldn't be so tired. = 如果他不那么咳嗽，他就不会这么累了。
I wish I hadn't coughed during the presentation. = 我真希望我在演示过程中没有咳嗽。


被动语态:
(This is not a common usage for "cough" as it's generally an intransitive verb)  A cough can't be passively done.  咳嗽不能被被动地进行。


疑问句:
Are you coughing? = 你在咳嗽吗？
Why is he coughing so hard? = 他为什么咳得这么厉害？


否定句:
He doesn't cough often. = 他不经常咳嗽。
She didn't cough at all during the performance. = 演出期间她根本没有咳嗽。


PHRASE->
cough up (phrasal verb):  to produce something with difficulty, especially money  勉强拿出（尤指钱）
a hacking cough:  a harsh, dry cough  干咳
a dry cough:  a cough without phlegm  干咳
a fit of coughing:  a sudden bout of coughing  一阵咳嗽
cough it out:  to forcefully expel something from the lungs  用力咳出
