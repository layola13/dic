ross:/rɒs/|ˈrɑːs/| n. |罗斯（男子名）；罗斯（地名）；沼泽地；荒地| A common given name of Scottish origin, often associated with the Scottish Highlands and its rugged landscape.  It can also refer to a type of marshy or boggy area. |例句：His name is Ross. 他的名字是罗斯。

例句：The Ross family owns a large estate on the coast.罗斯家族在海岸拥有一个大型庄园。


近义词：moor, marsh, bog, fen |反义词： cultivated land, farmland


词源:  The name Ross has a rich history, deriving from the Gaelic word "ros," meaning "promontory" or "headland."  This reflects the geographical features common in Scotland, where many places named "Ross" are situated on headlands or peninsulas. The name's popularity spread beyond Scotland, becoming common in other parts of the British Isles and beyond.
