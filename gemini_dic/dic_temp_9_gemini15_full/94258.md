impermeability:/ˌɪmpɜːrmɪəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 不渗透性；不透水性 | The inability of a material to allow fluids to pass through it.  This property is crucial in many applications, from building materials to biological membranes. | Example: The impermeability of the clay layer prevented the water from seeping into the foundation.  粘土层的不可渗透性阻止了水渗入地基。 The impermeability of the skin protects us from infection. 皮肤的不可渗透性保护我们免受感染。|近义词：impenetrability, watertightness | 反义词：permeability
