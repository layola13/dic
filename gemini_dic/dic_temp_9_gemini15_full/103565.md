john-bullishness: /ˈdʒɒnˈbʊlɪʃnəs/ | /ˈdʒɑːnˈbʊlɪʃnəs/ | n. |约翰牛式作风；英国人的固执和偏见；粗鲁无礼 |

英文解析：John Bullishness refers to the stereotypical characteristics often associated with the English, particularly their perceived stubbornness, insularity, complacency, and sometimes aggressive patriotism.  It often carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting an outdated or unreasonable clinging to traditional values and a lack of understanding or tolerance for other cultures.  The term is derived from the personification of England as "John Bull," a stout, boisterous, and somewhat jingoistic figure.


例句：

His unwavering belief in the superiority of British tea displayed a certain degree of john-bullishness.  他的坚定信念，认为英国茶是优越的，显示出某种程度的约翰牛式作风。

The politician's john-bullishness alienated many international partners.  这位政治家的约翰牛式作风疏远了许多国际伙伴。


近义词：chauvinism, jingoism, insularism, parochialism, stubbornness, complacency


反义词：cosmopolitanism, open-mindedness, tolerance, flexibility


词性：名词
