crashed:/kræʃt/| v. adj. n.|坠毁；撞坏；(市场)暴跌；崩溃的；失败的 |To collide violently; to fail suddenly and completely;  a sudden and dramatic fall in value. |例句：The plane crashed into the mountain.飞机撞上了山。 The stock market crashed.股市暴跌了。The party crashed after midnight.派对午夜后就垮了。|近义词：collided, plummeted, failed, collapsed|反义词：succeeded, soared, thrived


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The plane crashes every time. = 飞机每次都坠毁。
He crashes his car frequently. = 他经常撞坏他的车。


一般过去时:
The stock market crashed yesterday. = 股市昨天暴跌了。
My computer crashed during the presentation. = 我电脑在演示过程中崩溃了。


现在完成时:
The price of oil has crashed this year. = 油价今年暴跌了。
The company has crashed and burned. = 公司彻底失败了。


过去完成时:
The system had crashed before I could save my work. = 系统在我能够保存我的工作之前就崩溃了。
The economy had crashed before the new government took office. = 新政府上任之前，经济已经崩溃了。


将来时:
The server will crash if it is overloaded. = 如果服务器超载，它就会崩溃。
I think the project will crash due to lack of funding. = 我认为由于缺乏资金，这个项目将会失败。


虚拟语气:
If the software had not crashed, I would have finished my report on time. = 如果软件没有崩溃，我就能按时完成我的报告了。
If the bridge had crashed, many people would have been hurt. = 如果桥梁坍塌，很多人都会受伤。


被动语态:
The system was crashed by a virus. = 系统被病毒破坏了。
The car was crashed in the accident. = 汽车在事故中被撞坏了。


疑问句:
Did the computer crash? = 电脑崩溃了吗？
Has the stock market crashed again? = 股市又崩盘了吗？


否定句:
The car didn't crash. = 车没有撞坏。
The website didn't crash during the peak hours. = 网站在高峰期没有崩溃。
