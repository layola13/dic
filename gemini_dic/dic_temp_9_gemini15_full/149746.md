palatalisms:/ˈpælətəlɪzəm/| n.|腭化现象，腭音化|The modification of a consonant sound by raising the body of the tongue towards the hard palate during articulation.  This often results in a shift in the sound's place of articulation, creating sounds that are palatalized or semi-palatalized.  The process can be observed across many languages, both historically and synchronically.|Example: The English "y" sound is a palatalization of a consonant.|近义词：palatalization|反义词：None specifically, but the opposite would be a lack of palatalization.
