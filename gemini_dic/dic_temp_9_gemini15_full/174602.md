quislingist:/ˈkwɪzlɪŋɪst/| n. |通敌者；卖国贼| A quislingist is a person who betrays their country by collaborating with an enemy occupying force.  They are characterized by their treachery and collaboration with the enemy, often for personal gain or to avoid punishment.  The term is derived from the name of Vidkun Quisling, a Norwegian politician who collaborated with Nazi Germany during World War II. |Example:  Many Norwegians viewed Quisling as a quislingist and traitor to his nation.|近义词：traitor, collaborator, betrayer|反义词：patriot, loyalist
