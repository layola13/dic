predramatic: /ˌpriːˈdræˈmætɪk/ | adj. |戏剧之前的；预示戏剧的；戏剧性之前的 |  Describing something that occurs before a dramatic event, often foreshadowing or hinting at the drama to come.  It suggests an anticipation or build-up to a more significant, dramatic occurrence.  The word is relatively uncommon and often used in a literary or artistic context. |


例句：

The predramatic silence was more unsettling than any scream. = 戏剧之前的沉默比任何尖叫声都更令人不安。

The predramatic tension in the room was palpable. = 房间里戏剧性的紧张气氛是显而易见的。


近义词：antecedent, preliminary, preparatory, precursory


反义词：postdramatic, subsequent, following


词性:形容词
