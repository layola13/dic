grand:/ɡrænd/ | adj., n. |宏伟的；重大的；主要的；祖母（祖父）的；一千（美元）|

英文解析:As an adjective, "grand" means impressive in size, scope, or extent.  It can also describe something magnificent or splendid. As a noun, it refers to a thousand dollars (informal) or a grandparent.

例句:
The grand hotel overlooked the ocean. = 这家宏伟的酒店俯瞰着大海。

The grand total of our expenses exceeded our budget. = 我们的总支出超过了预算。


近义词: magnificent, impressive, splendid, majestic, superb, imposing, important, chief, principal

反义词: insignificant, unimportant, petty, small, humble, modest

词性: adjective, noun

词源:
The word "grand" comes from the Old French word "grant," meaning "great," "large," or "important."  This Old French word ultimately derives from the Latin word "grandis," with the same meaning.
