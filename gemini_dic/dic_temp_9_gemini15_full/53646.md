discharge: /dɪsˈtʃɑːrdʒ/ | n., vt. | 排放；释放；解雇；履行；出院 |

英文解析:As a noun, discharge refers to the act of releasing something, such as a liquid, gas, or person. As a verb, it means to release, dismiss, or fulfill an obligation.  The specific meaning depends heavily on context.

例句:

(Noun):

The discharge of pollutants into the river is illegal.  = 向河里排放污染物是违法的。

The hospital discharge papers confirmed his recovery. = 医院的出院证明证实了他的康复。


(Verb):

The factory discharged toxic waste into the nearby lake. = 工厂将有毒废物排放到附近的湖中。

The company discharged him for misconduct. = 公司因行为不端而解雇了他。

The judge discharged the jury. = 法官解散了陪审团。


近义词: (depending on context) release, expel, dismiss, fire, fulfill, let go, liberate, acquit, emanate

反义词: (depending on context) retain, keep, employ, hire,  retain,  arrest,  charge,  condemn
