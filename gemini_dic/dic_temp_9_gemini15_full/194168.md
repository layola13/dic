self-interrupting:/ˌsɛlf ɪntəˈrʌptɪŋ/ | adj. | 自中断的，自我中断的 |  Describing a process or system that interrupts itself, often as a means of control or error handling.  This term is commonly used in computer science and engineering contexts. | Example: The program exhibited self-interrupting behavior when it encountered an invalid input.  程序在遇到无效输入时表现出自我中断的行为。 |近义词：self-terminating (in some contexts) | 反义词：continuous, uninterrupted
