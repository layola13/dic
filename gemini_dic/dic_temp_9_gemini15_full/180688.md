regressively:/rɪˈɡrɛsɪvli/| adv. | 累退地；逆行地；退步地 | In a way that is regressive; moving backward or reverting to a previous state.  It often describes a system, policy, or tax that disproportionately affects lower-income individuals or groups. | Example: The new tax policy is regressively structured, placing a heavier burden on lower-income earners.  新的税收政策结构具有累退性，对低收入者造成更大的负担。 |近义词：retroactively, inversely |反义词：progressively
