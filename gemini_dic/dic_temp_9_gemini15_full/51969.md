diacritical:/ˌdaɪəˈkrɪtɪkəl/| adj.|附加符号的；区分的；有区别的|  A diacritical mark is a symbol added to a letter or other character to indicate a difference in pronunciation or meaning.  It is used to distinguish between similar-looking characters that represent different sounds or have different meanings. For example, the acute accent (´) in café changes the pronunciation of the 'e'.|The acute accent in "café" is a diacritical mark.|近义词：distinctive, differentiating|反义词：undifferentiated, indistinct
