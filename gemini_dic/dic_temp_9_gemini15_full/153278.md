penalties:/ˈpɛnəltiz/| n.|惩罚；处罚；不利因素|The penalties for speeding are quite severe.超速的处罚相当严厉。|近义词：punishments, sanctions, fines|反义词：rewards, benefits


英文解析：Penalties refer to punishments imposed for wrongdoing or failure to comply with rules or laws.  They can range from fines and imprisonment to less formal consequences like loss of points or privileges.  The term can also refer to disadvantages or drawbacks in a situation.


例句：

He faced severe penalties for his crime. 他因犯罪面临严厉的惩罚。

The team incurred penalties for repeated fouls.  该队因多次犯规而受到处罚。


词源：

The word "penalty" comes from the Late Latin word "poena," meaning "punishment" or "pain."  This in turn has roots in the Proto-Indo-European root "*kʷeh₁-," meaning "to kill" or "to strike."  The evolution reflects the originally harsh nature of punishments.
