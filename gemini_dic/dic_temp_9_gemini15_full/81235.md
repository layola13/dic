grandstand: /ˈɡrændˌstænd/ | n. |看台；观众席；炫耀；卖弄|

英文解析:A grandstand is a raised structure in a sports stadium or other venue from which spectators can watch an event. It can also refer to an action or display intended to impress onlookers.

例句:
The crowd roared their approval from the grandstand.  观众从看台上发出震耳欲聋的欢呼声。

The politician's speech felt like a grandstand play to win votes. 这位政治家的演讲感觉像是为了赢得选票而进行的卖弄。


近义词: stands, bleachers, platform, show off, ostentation

反义词: modesty, humility

词源:
The word "grandstand" originated in the late 19th century.  It combines "grand," meaning large and impressive, with "stand," referring to a raised platform for spectators.  The original meaning was solely the physical structure. The figurative sense of "showy display" developed later.
