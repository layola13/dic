glorify:/ˈɡlɔːrɪfaɪ/| vt.| 赞美；颂扬；使…显得光荣 | To praise and celebrate something or someone; to make something or someone seem more glorious or impressive. | 例句：They glorified the achievements of their ancestors. 他们赞美了他们祖先的成就。| 例句：The film glorified violence.  这部电影美化了暴力。| 近义词：exalt, praise, celebrate, magnify | 反义词：denigrate, belittle, disparage, condemn | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We glorify God in our worship. = 我们在崇拜中荣耀上帝。
They glorify their achievements excessively. = 他们过分地夸耀他们的成就。

一般过去时:
The media glorified the athlete's victory. = 媒体赞扬了这位运动员的胜利。
The artist glorified the beauty of nature in his paintings. = 艺术家在他的画作中歌颂了大自然的美丽。

现在完成时:
She has glorified her life through acts of kindness. = 她通过善举使自己的人生更加光彩。
They have glorified their country's history in numerous books. = 他们在许多书中歌颂了他们国家的历史。

过去完成时:
The king had glorified his reign with lavish celebrations. = 国王用奢华的庆祝活动来美化他的统治。
They had glorified the past, ignoring present problems. = 他们美化了过去，忽略了当前的问题。

将来时:
We will glorify God with our lives. = 我们将用我们的生命来荣耀上帝。
The company will glorify its new product with a massive marketing campaign. = 公司将通过大规模的营销活动来宣传其新产品。

虚拟语气:
If they glorified their failures, they might learn from them. = 如果他们能正视自己的失败，他们或许就能从中吸取教训。(注意：此句中glorify的用法略微不同，表示“正视，重视”，而非单纯的赞美)
I wish they would glorify their community's contributions more. = 我希望他们能更多地赞扬他们社区的贡献。

被动语态:
His achievements were glorified in the newspaper. = 他的成就被报纸大肆宣传。
The event was glorified by the media, exaggerating its significance. = 该事件被媒体夸大其词地宣传，夸大了它的意义。

疑问句:
Do they glorify violence in their songs? = 他们的歌里是否美化了暴力？
How do they glorify their culture? = 他们如何弘扬他们的文化？

否定句:
We do not glorify war; we condemn it. = 我们不美化战争；我们谴责它。
They did not glorify their success, remaining humble. = 他们没有夸耀自己的成功，而是保持谦逊。
