philippic:/fɪˈlɪpɪk/| n.|谴责性演说；痛斥；猛烈抨击|a strong verbal attack against someone or something.  A philippic is a long and angry speech full of criticism. |例句：He launched a furious philippic against the government's economic policies. 他对政府的经济政策发起了猛烈的抨击。

例句：Her philippic against the injustices of the system was met with applause from the crowd. 她对制度不公正的强烈谴责赢得了人群的掌声。


近义词：diatribe, invective, harangue, tirade|反义词：praise, commendation, eulogy

词源:
The word "philippic" comes from the Greek orator Demosthenes's speeches against Philip II of Macedon.  These speeches were known for their passionate and vehement condemnation, and thus the term "philippic" came to refer to any similarly forceful and critical oration.
