parataxises:/ˌpærəˈtæksɪsiːz/| n. |并列句法；并列结构|The use of coordination rather than subordination in the grammatical structure of a sentence; the juxtaposition of clauses or phrases without the use of conjunctions or other connecting words.  Parataxis is a stylistic device that creates a sense of immediacy and speed, often reflecting the flow of thought or speech. It can also emphasize the equal importance of the elements being juxtaposed.|例句：The sun rose. The birds sang. (Simple example of parataxis)  太阳升起了。鸟儿歌唱了。(并列句法的简单例子)|近义词：juxtaposition, coordination|反义词：hypotaxis
