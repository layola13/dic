howitzer:/ˈhaʊɪtsər/| n. |榴弹炮|A howitzer is a type of artillery gun that fires projectiles at a high angle, typically for indirect fire.  Unlike cannons, which fire at flatter trajectories, howitzers are designed to lob shells over obstacles and into enemy positions. They are known for their versatility and ability to hit targets beyond direct line of sight.|The army used howitzers to bombard the enemy's position from a distance.|cannon, mortar|rifle|名词
