para:/ˈpærə/ | n. | 段落；节；一部分 | A paragraph is a self-contained unit of text, typically consisting of several sentences that develop a specific idea or aspect of a larger topic.  It is usually marked by indentation or a blank line. | Example: The first paragraph of the essay introduced the main argument.  这篇论文的第一段介绍了主要论点。 |近义词：section, passage, portion | 反义词：whole, entirety


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This paragraph explains the main points. = 这段话解释了要点。
The paragraph contains a lot of useful information. = 这段话包含许多有用的信息。


一般过去时:
The last paragraph summarized the key findings. = 最后一段总结了主要发现。
He read the paragraph carefully before answering. = 他仔细阅读了这段话后才回答。


现在完成时:
I have written several paragraphs about this topic. = 我已经写了几段关于这个话题的文章。
She has finished reading the first paragraph. = 她已经读完了第一段。


过去完成时:
I had already read that paragraph before the meeting. = 我在会议之前已经读过那段话了。
They had discussed that paragraph extensively before reaching a conclusion. = 他们对那段话进行了广泛的讨论才得出结论。


将来时:
I will write a new paragraph tomorrow. = 我明天会写一段新的话。
She will read the next paragraph in a moment. = 她一会儿会读下一段。


虚拟语气:
If I had written a better paragraph, I would have received a better grade. = 如果我写得更好一些，我会得到更高的分数。
I wish I had written a clearer paragraph. = 我希望我写得更清楚一些。


被动语态:
The paragraph was written by a renowned author. = 这段话是一位著名作家写的。
This paragraph is being revised by the editor. = 这段话正在由编辑修改。


疑问句:
What does this paragraph mean? = 这段话是什么意思？
What is the main idea of this paragraph? = 这段话的主要思想是什么？


否定句:
This paragraph does not contain any new information. = 这段话不包含任何新信息。
The paragraph did not explain the concept clearly. = 这段话没有清楚地解释这个概念。
