metaphony:/ˈmɛtəfəni/| n. |元音同化；变音；变调|A phenomenon in linguistics where a vowel's quality changes due to the influence of a neighboring sound, typically a following vowel.  This change often involves a raising or lowering of the vowel's position in the mouth.  It's a type of assimilation. |Example: In some dialects of English, the vowel in "goat" can be affected by a following vowel, leading to pronunciation variations.  |近义词：vowel assimilation, sound change|反义词：N/A
