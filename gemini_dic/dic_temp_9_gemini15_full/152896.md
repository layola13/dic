peddler:/ˈpedlər/| n.|小贩，流动商贩|A peddler is a person who travels from place to place selling small goods.  They often sell their wares on the street or from door to door.|例句：The peddler sold colorful scarves and trinkets.  这个小贩出售色彩鲜艳的围巾和小饰品。|例句：A traveling peddler stopped by our farm yesterday. 昨天一个流动小贩停在了我们的农场。|近义词：hawker, vendor, itinerant merchant|反义词：retailer (in a fixed location)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The peddler sells his goods in the market. = 小贩在市场上出售他的商品。
He peddles his wares from town to town. = 他挨家挨户地兜售他的货物。


一般过去时:
The peddler sold all his goods yesterday. = 小贩昨天卖掉了所有的货物。
She peddled her wares on the street corner. = 她在街角兜售她的商品。

过去完成时:

She had peddled her wares in many different towns. = 她曾在许多不同的城镇兜售过她的商品。

词源:
The word "peddler" comes from the Middle English word "pedlere," which in turn is derived from the Old French word "pedler," meaning "to travel on foot."  This ultimately relates to the Latin word "pes," meaning "foot."
