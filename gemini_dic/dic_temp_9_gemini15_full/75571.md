fud:/fʌd/| n.|胡说八道，废话，虚假信息 |  False or misleading information, often presented as fact. It can also refer to deliberately deceptive or misleading statements, especially in the context of politics or finance. | Example:  "Don't believe that fud; it's completely untrue."  "The politician's speech was full of fud." | 近义词：nonsense, balderdash, hogwash, misinformation, disinformation | 反义词：truth, fact, reality


USAGE->
This word does not have typical verb conjugations like "What" because it's a noun.  The grammatical usage revolves around how it functions as a noun in sentences.

Example 1 (Simple Sentence): That report is full of fud.  (这份报告充满了谎言。)
Example 2 (Complex Sentence):  Despite the fud circulating online, the company maintained its position. (尽管网上流传着虚假信息，该公司仍然坚持其立场。)


PHRASE->
There are no common set phrases specifically using "fud" as it's relatively informal and niche slang. The usage is primarily as a standalone noun.
