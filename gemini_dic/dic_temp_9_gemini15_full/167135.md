presympathies:/ˌpriːˈsɪmpəθiz/| n. |预先同情，先发制人式的同情|  A feeling of sympathy expressed or felt before an event has happened, often implying a patronizing or insincere attitude.  It suggests a preemptive expression of sympathy that may not be genuinely felt or appropriately timed.  | Example: Her presympathies for the victims felt hollow and unconvincing.  她的预先同情受害者的举动听起来空洞且缺乏说服力。|近义词：preemptive compassion, anticipatory sympathy|反义词：indifference, apathy|名词
