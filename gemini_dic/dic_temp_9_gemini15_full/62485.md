endoss:/ɛnˈdɔːs/ | vt. |背书；认可；签署；|To endorse something means to publicly declare your support for it or to sign a document.  This word is primarily used in the context of financial instruments like checks or legal documents. |The celebrity endorsed the new product.|近义词：approve, support, sanction, ratify |反义词：reject, disapprove, condemn


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He endorses the candidate. = 他支持这位候选人。
She endorses the new policy. = 她认可这项新政策。

一般过去时:
They endorsed the bill. = 他们批准了该法案。
He endorsed the check. = 他在支票上签了名。


现在完成时:
She has endorsed several products this year. = 她今年已经代言了好几种产品。
The bank has endorsed the transaction. = 银行已经批准了这笔交易。


过去完成时:
He had endorsed the check before leaving the bank. = 他在离开银行之前已经签署了支票。
They had endorsed the agreement before the meeting. = 他们会议前已经批准了该协议。


将来时:
She will endorse the book next month. = 她下个月将为这本书作序。
They will endorse the project after reviewing the proposal. = 他们在审核提案后将批准该项目。


虚拟语气:
If he endorsed the plan, it would succeed. = 如果他支持这个计划，它就会成功。
I wish she had endorsed my application. = 我希望她已经批准了我的申请。


被动语态:
The check was endorsed by the payee. = 支票由收款人背书。
The proposal was endorsed by the committee. = 该提案得到了委员会的批准。


疑问句:
Did he endorse the contract? = 他是否签署了合同？
Will they endorse the initiative? = 他们会支持这项倡议吗？


否定句:
He did not endorse the unethical practices. = 他没有认可那些不道德的行为。
She refused to endorse the document. = 她拒绝签署该文件。


PHRASE->
endorse a check = 背书支票

endorse a product = 代言产品
endorse a loan = 担保贷款
endorse a view = 赞同观点
