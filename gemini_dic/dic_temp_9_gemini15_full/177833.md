recentralizations:/ˌriːsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃənz/ | n. | 再中心化；重新集中化 | The process of returning power or functions to a central authority after a period of decentralization.  This often involves reversing previous policies that aimed to distribute power more broadly.  The term often refers to political or administrative systems, but can also apply to other contexts like business organizations. | Example: The recentralizations of power after the regional uprisings led to increased political stability but also stifled local initiatives.  The recentralizations of the company's operations resulted in higher efficiency but reduced employee morale in the branch offices. | 近义词：re-centralization, centralization, concentration | 反义词：decentralization, devolution
