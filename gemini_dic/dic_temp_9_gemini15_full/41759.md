corrupter:/kəˈrʌptər/ | n. | 腐蚀者；败坏者；行贿者 | A corrupter is someone who corrupts or spoils something, especially someone's morals or integrity. They might bribe officials, spread misinformation, or otherwise undermine ethical behavior. | Example:  The corrupter offered the politician a large sum of money in exchange for his vote. 腐蚀者向政客提供巨款以换取他的选票。


近义词：corruptor, demoralizer, debaucher, perverter

反义词：moralizer, reformer, purifier

词源:
The word "corrupter" is derived from the Latin word "corruptor," which means "one who corrupts."  The Latin verb "corrumpere" means "to break, destroy, spoil, or bribe."  The English word "corrupt" evolved from this Latin root, and "corrupter" is its agent noun, indicating the person who performs the action of corrupting.
