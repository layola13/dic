perisperm:/ˈpɛrɪspɜːrm/| n. |胚乳状组织，外胚乳|A nutritive tissue in some seeds, formed from the nucellus (the tissue surrounding the ovule) rather than from the endosperm.  It is similar in function to the endosperm, providing nourishment to the developing embryo.|The perisperm of the seed provides sustenance for the developing seedling.|近义词：endosperm (though originating from different tissue)|反义词：None specifically, but terms describing seed lacking nutritive tissue could be considered antonyms.
