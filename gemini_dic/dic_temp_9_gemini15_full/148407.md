overstates:/ˌoʊvərˈsteɪts/| vt.|夸大；过分强调|To represent (something) as being larger, better, or worse than it really is.|The advertisement overstates the effectiveness of the product.  广告夸大了产品的功效。|近义词：exaggerate, magnify, hyperbolize|反义词：understate, downplay


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He overstates his accomplishments in interviews. = 他在面试中夸大了自己的成就。
She often overstates the seriousness of her problems. = 她经常夸大自己问题的严重性。

一般过去时:
The media overstated the impact of the storm. = 媒体夸大了暴风雨的影响。
He overstated his case in the debate. = 他在辩论中夸大了自己的论点。

现在完成时:
The report has overstated the economic growth of the country. = 报告夸大了该国的经济增长。
She has overstated her qualifications on her resume. = 她在简历上夸大了自己的资历。

过去完成时:
They had overstated their expectations for the project. = 他们对项目的期望过高了。
The politician had overstated the benefits of his proposed policy. = 该政治家夸大了其提议政策的好处。

将来时:
He will overstate his abilities during the job application. = 他将在求职过程中夸大自己的能力。
The news will likely overstate the significance of the event. = 新闻可能会夸大事件的重要性。

虚拟语气:
If he overstated his qualifications, he might not get the job. = 如果他夸大了自己的资格，他可能得不到这份工作。
I wish he hadn't overstated his involvement in the project. = 我希望他没有夸大他在项目中的参与程度。

被动语态:
The danger of the situation was overstated by the media. = 媒体夸大了局势的危险性。
His contributions to the team were often overstated by his manager. = 他的经理经常夸大他对团队的贡献。

疑问句:
Does he often overstate his achievements? = 他经常夸大自己的成就吗？
Did the newspaper overstate the number of casualties? = 报纸是否夸大了伤亡人数？

否定句:
He didn't overstate his capabilities; he was simply confident. = 他并没有夸大自己的能力；他只是自信。
The study didn't overstate the findings; the results were clear. = 研究并没有夸大研究结果；结果很明确。
