canonises:/ˈkænənaɪzɪz/ | vt. | 承认…为圣徒；尊崇；奉为经典 | To declare (a deceased person) to be a saint; to regard as sacred or authoritative.  | The Church canonised her last year.  教会去年将她封为圣徒。|  The book has been canonised as a classic. 这本书已被奉为经典之作。 | sanctify, glorify, enshrine |  desecrate, condemn | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The Church canonises saints after a rigorous process. = 教会经过严格的程序之后才会封圣。
He canonises his teachers' wisdom in his writings. = 他在他的著作中尊崇老师的智慧。


一般过去时:
The Pope canonised several new saints last year. = 教宗去年封了几个新圣徒。
They canonised the author's works as essential reading. = 他们将这位作者的作品列为必读书籍。


现在完成时:
The Catholic Church has canonised many figures throughout history. = 天主教教会历史上已封圣了许多人物。
She has canonised the principles of her grandmother in her life. = 她一生都奉行她祖母的原则。


过去完成时:

By the end of the century, his works had been canonised as masterpieces. = 到世纪末，他的作品已被奉为杰作。

虚拟语气:
If they canonised him, it would be a great honor. = 如果他们封他为圣徒，那将是莫大的荣幸。

被动语态:
