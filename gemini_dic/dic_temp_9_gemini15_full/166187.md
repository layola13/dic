prepsychology:/ˌpriːsaɪˈkɒlədʒi/| n. |前心理学，心理学前奏|The study of the psychological factors that precede and contribute to the development of a particular psychological condition or behavior.  It explores the antecedents of mental states and actions, often focusing on developmental, biological, and environmental influences.  It's a relatively new and evolving field, drawing from various disciplines to understand the roots of psychological phenomena before they manifest as diagnosable conditions.|Example: Research in prepsychology might examine the early childhood experiences that contribute to the later development of anxiety disorders.|近义词:propedeutics of psychology, developmental psychopathology|反义词: N/A
