prevalence:/ˈprɛvələns/| n. |流行；普遍；盛行|The prevalence of a disease refers to how common it is in a particular population.  It indicates the proportion of individuals within a group who have the disease at a specific time.  Prevalence is often expressed as a rate, such as the number of cases per 100,000 people.  Understanding the prevalence of a disease is crucial for public health planning and resource allocation.|The prevalence of heart disease is increasing in many developed countries.|近义词：commonness, frequency, incidence (though incidence refers to new cases)|反义词：rarity, infrequency
