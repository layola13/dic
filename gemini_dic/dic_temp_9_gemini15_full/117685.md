meitnerium:/ˈmaɪtnərɪəm/| n. |镄|A synthetic chemical element, symbol Mt, atomic number 109.  It is a highly radioactive, metallic element that is not found in nature and is created artificially in particle accelerators. It is named after Lise Meitner, a physicist who made significant contributions to the discovery of nuclear fission.|Example:  Scientists synthesized meitnerium in a laboratory. 科学家在实验室合成了镄。|近义词: transactinide element|反义词:  (no direct antonym, as it's a specific element)|名词
