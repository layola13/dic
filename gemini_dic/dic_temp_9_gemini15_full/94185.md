imperativism:/ɪmˈpærətɪvɪzəm/| n. |命令主义；强制主义；命令式|The philosophical stance that emphasizes the importance of commands and imperatives in shaping morality and action.  It contrasts with other ethical theories that prioritize reason, consequences, or virtue.  Imperativism often features prominently in deontological ethics. | Example:  His approach to management could be described as imperativism; he issued directives and expected unquestioning obedience.  他的管理方法可以说是命令主义；他发布指令并期望无条件服从。 | 近义词：authoritarianism, commandism | 反义词：pragmatism, consequentialism
