ghostwrite:/ˈɡoʊstˌraɪt/| vt.|代笔，捉刀|To write something, such as a book or article, for another person, who is then credited with the work.  This often involves creating content based on the client's ideas, research, and style preferences.|例句：She ghostwrote several bestselling novels for famous celebrities. 她为几位著名人士代笔创作了几部畅销小说。

例句：The politician paid a ghostwriter to pen his autobiography.  这位政治家付钱给一位代笔作家撰写他的自传。


近义词：write for, compose for, author for


反义词：self-write,  (no direct antonym, the opposite would imply writing one's own work)


词性：动词


USAGE->

He ghostwrites for many famous authors. 他为许多著名作家代笔。
They ghostwrite speeches for politicians. 他们为政客代写演讲稿。
