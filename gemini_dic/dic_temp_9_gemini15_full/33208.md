chronical:/ˈkrɒnɪkl/| adj.|编年史的；逐年记载的；冗长的；漫长的|Chronicling events in order of occurrence, often detailed and extensive.  It can also describe something that is persistent or long-lasting, often in a negative way.|例句：He kept a chronical record of his travels. 他详细记录了他的旅行历程。|例句：She suffers from a chronical illness. 她患有慢性病。|近义词：detailed, lengthy, protracted, persistent, ongoing|反义词：brief, short, fleeting, temporary


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The historian chronicles the events of the war. = 历史学家记录了战争的事件。
He chronicles his daily life in a journal. = 他在日记中记录他的日常生活。


一般过去时:
She chronicled the rise and fall of the Roman Empire. = 她记述了罗马帝国的兴衰。
The journalist chronicled the events of the protest. = 记者记录了抗议活动的事件。


现在完成时:
He has chronicled the history of his family for generations. = 他已经记述了他家族几代人的历史。
The author has chronicled the lives of many famous people. = 作者已经记述了许多名人的生活。


过去完成时:
By the end of the book, the author had chronicled the entire saga. = 到书的结尾，作者已经记述了整个传奇故事。
They had chronicled the events leading up to the revolution. = 他们已经记述了导致革命的事件。


将来时:
She will chronicle the expedition to the Amazon. = 她将记录亚马逊探险的经历。
The reporter will chronicle the upcoming election. = 记者将报道即将到来的选举。


虚拟语气:
If I had the time, I would chronicle my childhood memories. = 如果我有时间，我会记录我的童年记忆。
I wish I could chronicle every interesting event in my life. = 我希望我能记录我生命中每一个有趣的事件。


被动语态:
The events of the revolution were chronicled in detail. = 革命的事件被详细地记录下来了。
The history of the city is being chronicled by a team of historians. = 城市的历史正由一个历史学家团队编纂。


疑问句:
Will the book chronicle the lives of ordinary people? = 这本书会记录普通人的生活吗？
Does this chronicle accurately reflect the events? = 这部编年史准确地反映了这些事件吗？


否定句:
The journalist did not chronicle the less important details. = 记者没有记录不太重要的细节。
The book does not chronicle the political aspects of the period. = 这本书没有记录那个时期的政治方面。
