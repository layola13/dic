pan-presbyterian: /ˌpænˌprɛzbɪˈtɪəriən/ | adj. |泛长老会的；全体长老会的 | Relating to or supporting a system of church government uniting Presbyterian churches of various denominations.  It suggests a broader, more inclusive Presbyterianism than that of a single denomination. |例句：The pan-Presbyterian movement aimed to foster unity among different Presbyterian churches. 泛长老会运动旨在促进不同长老会教会之间的团结。


The term is a compound word formed from:

* **pan-**:  A prefix meaning "all," "every," "whole," or "entire."  It comes from the Greek word "pan," meaning "all."

* **Presbyterian**:  Relating to the Presbyterian system of church government, which emphasizes the rule of elders (presbyters) in church affairs.  This word's etymology traces back to the Greek word "presbyteros," meaning "elder."


近义词： ecumenical (in the context of church unity), interdenominational


反义词： denominational (referring to a single Presbyterian denomination, lacking the broader scope)


词性：形容词 (adjective)
