deluge's:/ˈdɛljuːdʒ/| n.| 洪水；暴雨；泛滥；大量涌入 | A deluge is an overwhelming amount of something, typically water. It can refer to a flood, a heavy downpour, or a large quantity of something arriving suddenly.  |例句：The heavy rain caused a deluge in the city streets.  这场大雨导致城市街道被洪水淹没。The company received a deluge of applications for the job.  公司收到了大量的工作申请。|近义词：flood, inundation, torrent, spate |反义词：drought, shortage

词源:
The word "deluge" comes from the Old French word "deluge," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "diluvium," meaning "a flood."  The Latin word is related to the verb "diluere," meaning "to wash away."
