predischarging: /ˌpriːdɪsˈtʃɑːrdʒɪŋ/ | v. | 预先放电；预先充电 | The act of discharging something before its normal or intended use, often to prevent an unwanted discharge or buildup of charge.  This can refer to electrical systems, capacitors, or even emotional tension. | 例句：The technician predischarged the capacitor to prevent a short circuit. 技术人员预先放电电容器以防止短路。


The battery was predischarged before being installed in the device. 电池在安装到设备之前进行了预先放电。 | 近义词：pre-discharging, pre-releasing (in some contexts) | 反义词：overcharging, overloading (in electrical contexts)
