non-drinker:/ˈnɒnˌdrɪŋkər/ | n. | 不喝酒的人 | A person who does not drink alcoholic beverages.  | He's a non-drinker, so he'll have a soda. 他不喝酒，所以他要一杯苏打水。| abstainer, teetotaler | drinker, alcoholic


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense, voice, and mood are not applicable.  Examples will demonstrate its usage in different sentence structures.


例句1:  Many non-drinkers enjoy social gatherings without feeling pressured to consume alcohol. 许多不喝酒的人都乐于参加社交聚会，而不会感到有压力要喝酒。

例句2: The restaurant caters to both drinkers and non-drinkers, offering a wide selection of non-alcoholic beverages. 这家餐厅既照顾喝酒的人，也照顾不喝酒的人，提供各种各样的不含酒精的饮料。

例句3:  She's a lifelong non-drinker, preferring herbal teas and juices. 她一辈子都不喝酒，更喜欢喝花草茶和果汁。

例句4:  For some non-drinkers, avoiding alcohol is a matter of personal preference, while for others it's a health decision. 对一些不喝酒的人来说，避免饮酒是个人喜好问题，而对另一些人来说，则是一个健康决定。

例句5:  The study showed a significant increase in the number of young non-drinkers. 研究表明，年轻人不喝酒的人数显著增加。
