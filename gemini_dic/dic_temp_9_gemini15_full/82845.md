gulfs:/ɡʌlf/| n.|海湾；差距；深渊|A gulf is a large area of sea that is partly enclosed by land. It can also refer to a large difference between two things or people, or a deep and seemingly bottomless chasm.|例句：The Persian Gulf is a major source of oil. 波斯湾是重要的石油产地。  The gulf between the rich and the poor is widening. 贫富差距正在扩大。|近义词：bay, chasm, abyss|反义词：shallow, bridge, connection

A wide gulf exists between their opinions. = 他们的意见存在很大的分歧。

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

Technological advancements have bridged the gulf between the two countries. = 技术进步弥合了两国之间的差距。

过去完成时:
A huge gulf had already existed between their cultures before the conflict. = 在冲突之前，他们的文化之间就已经存在巨大的差异。
The gulf had deepened over many years of misunderstandings. = 多年来的误解使隔阂加深了。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
