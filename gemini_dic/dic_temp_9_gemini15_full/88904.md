hold-a-brief-for:/hoʊld ə briːf fɔːr/| v.| 为…辩护，支持…| to support or defend someone or something, especially in a court of law | 例句：He's holding a brief for the defendant. 他正在为被告辩护。| 例句：She's holding a brief for stricter gun control. 她正在为更严格的枪支管制辩护。| 近义词：defend, support, advocate for | 反义词：oppose, condemn, denounce | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They hold a brief for the new policy. = 他们支持这项新政策。
He holds a brief for environmental protection. = 他支持环境保护。


一般过去时:
The lawyer held a brief for the accused. = 律师为被告辩护。
She held a brief for the reform movement. = 她支持改革运动。


现在完成时:
They have held a brief for this cause for many years. = 多年来，他们一直支持这一事业。
He has held a brief for stricter regulations. = 他一直支持更严格的法规。


过去完成时:
The lawyer had held a brief for the company before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，律师曾为该公司辩护。
She had held a brief for the candidate throughout the campaign. = 在整个竞选期间，她一直支持这位候选人。


将来时:
They will hold a brief for the victims in the upcoming trial. = 他们在即将到来的审判中将为受害者辩护。
He will hold a brief for the proposed changes. = 他将支持拟议的变更。


虚拟语气:
If he held a brief for the opposition, he would have a different perspective. = 如果他支持反对派，他会采取不同的视角。
If she had held a brief for the environmental group, the outcome might have been different. = 如果她支持环保组织，结果可能会有所不同。


被动语态:
A brief was held for the defendant by a prominent lawyer. = 一位著名的律师为被告辩护。
The new initiative was held a brief for by several influential figures. = 几个有影响力的人物支持这项新举措。


疑问句:
Does he hold a brief for the government's new plan? = 他支持政府的新计划吗？
Will they hold a brief for the accused in court? = 他们会在法庭上为被告辩护吗？


否定句:
He does not hold a brief for that political party. = 他不支持那个政党。
They did not hold a brief for the controversial project. = 他们不支持有争议的项目。


PHRASE->
hold a brief for someone/something = 为某人/某事辩护
to hold a brief =  担任律师
brief for = 为……辩护
hold a strong brief for = 坚决支持
to hold no brief for = 不支持


词源:
The phrase originates from the legal profession.  A "brief" is a written document summarizing the facts and legal arguments of a case.  "Holding a brief" originally meant representing a client in court.  The meaning has expanded to imply supporting or defending something more generally.
