rupturing: /ˈrʌptʃərɪŋ/ | v. | 破裂；断裂；撕裂 | The act of breaking or bursting something.  It often describes a sudden and forceful separation of something. |例句：The earthquake caused the rupturing of several gas pipelines. 地震导致几条天然气管道破裂。

The old pipe is in danger of rupturing. 老旧的管道有破裂的危险。


近义词：breaking, bursting, splitting, tearing, severing | 反义词：repairing, mending, joining, uniting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The pressure is rupturing the container. = 压力正在使容器破裂。
The machine is constantly rupturing under heavy loads. = 机器在重载下不断破裂。


一般过去时:
The balloon ruptured with a loud bang. = 气球砰的一声破裂了。
The dam ruptured, releasing a torrent of water. = 水坝决堤了，释放出一股洪流。


现在完成时:
The pipe has ruptured, causing a major water leak. = 管道破裂了，导致大规模漏水。
The negotiations have ruptured, leaving little hope for a solution. = 谈判破裂了，几乎没有解决的希望。


过去完成时:
The artery had ruptured before the paramedics arrived. = 医生到达之前动脉已经破裂了。
The deal had ruptured due to disagreements over pricing. = 由于价格分歧，交易已经破裂。


将来时:
The overloaded bridge will likely rupture under the weight. = 超负荷的桥梁很可能会在重量下断裂。
If the pressure continues to increase, the tank will rupture. = 如果压力继续增加，油箱将破裂。


虚拟语气:
If the boiler had ruptured, there would have been a serious accident. = 如果锅炉破裂了，就会发生严重事故。
I wish the contract hadn't ruptured.  = 我真希望合同没有破裂。


被动语态:
The water main was ruptured by the earthquake. = 自来水干线被地震震裂了。
The agreement was ruptured by a sudden disagreement. = 协议因突然的分歧而破裂。


疑问句:
Has the pipe ruptured? = 管道破裂了吗？
Did the negotiations rupture? = 谈判破裂了吗？


否定句:
The container did not rupture under the pressure. = 容器在压力下没有破裂。
The relationship has not ruptured despite the challenges. = 尽管面临挑战，但关系并没有破裂。
