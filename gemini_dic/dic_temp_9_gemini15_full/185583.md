revelatory: /ˌrevəˈleɪtəri/ | adj. |  启示的；揭露的；展现真相的 |  serving to reveal something; bringing something to light.  It implies a significant and often surprising unveiling of truth or information, often previously hidden or unknown. |


例句：

The new research findings were revelatory, completely changing our understanding of the disease. =  新的研究发现具有启示意义，彻底改变了我们对这种疾病的理解。

His testimony provided revelatory insights into the inner workings of the organization. = 他的证词提供了对该组织内部运作的启示性见解。


近义词：revealing, disclosing, illuminating, enlightening, insightful

反义词：concealing, hiding, obscuring, masking


词源:

From "reveal," ultimately from Old French *reveiller  "to unveil, disclose" (from Latin *reuelāre, from *re- "again" + *uelāre "to uncover"). The suffix "-atory" denotes something that tends to or is capable of doing the action of the verb.
