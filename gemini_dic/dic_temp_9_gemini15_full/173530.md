quasi-gloriously: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈɡlɔːriəsli/ | adj. | 貌似光荣地；近乎辉煌地 |  The word combines "quasi," meaning seemingly or apparently, with "gloriously," meaning in a glorious manner. It describes something that appears glorious or magnificent but may lack true glory or substance. It often implies a slightly ironic or understated tone.  | 例句：He quasi-gloriously recounted his supposed adventures in the Amazon. 他貌似光荣地讲述了他所谓的亚马逊探险经历。


The band's quasi-gloriously loud performance was ultimately undermined by poor sound mixing. 乐队近乎辉煌的响亮表演最终因糟糕的混音而逊色。


近义词：seemingly glorious, apparently magnificent, superficially splendid | 反义词：ingloriously, shamefully, disgracefully

词性：形容词
