funiculus: /fjuːˈnɪkjʊləs/ | n. | 索；束；精索 | A small cord or bundle of fibers, especially a nerve fiber or vascular bundle.  The term is often used in anatomy and botany.  | Example: The spermatic cord, also known as the funiculus spermaticus, contains the vas deferens, blood vessels, and nerves.  精索，也称为精索索，包含输精管、血管和神经。 | 近义词：cord, strand, bundle | 反义词：N/A

PHRASE->
spermatic funiculus (精索): The cord-like structure containing the vas deferens, blood vessels, and nerves that runs from the abdomen to the testis.
umbilical funiculus (脐带): The cord connecting the fetus to the placenta.
funiculus of the seed (种子的索): The structure that attaches the seed to the placenta.
spinal funiculus (脊髓索): A longitudinal column of white matter in the spinal cord.
vascular funiculus (血管束): A bundle of blood vessels.
