mugged:/ˈmʌɡd/| v.| 抢劫；袭击 | To attack and rob someone, typically in a public place. | Example: He was mugged in broad daylight. 他在光天化日之下被抢劫了。 |近义词：robbed, assaulted, attacked | 反义词：None


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They mug people regularly in this park. = 他们在公园里经常抢劫人们。
He mugs tourists near the train station. = 他在火车站附近抢劫游客。

一般过去时:
I was mugged last night. = 我昨晚被抢劫了。
She was mugged on her way home from work. = 她下班回家的路上被抢劫了。

现在完成时:
He has been mugged twice this year. = 他今年已经被抢劫过两次了。
They have never been mugged before. = 他们以前从未被抢劫过。

过去完成时:
I had been mugged before, so I was more cautious. = 我以前被抢劫过，所以我更加谨慎。
She realized she had been mugged only after she got home. = 她回家后才意识到自己被抢劫了。

将来时:
I will call the police if I am mugged. = 如果我被抢劫，我会报警。
He thinks he will be mugged again soon. = 他认为自己很快就会再次被抢劫。

虚拟语气:
If I were mugged, I would scream for help. = 如果我被抢劫，我会大声呼救。
I wish I hadn't been mugged last week. = 我真希望上周我没有被抢劫。

被动语态:
Many people are mugged in this city every year. = 每年都有许多人在这个城市被抢劫。
The victim was mugged and seriously injured. = 受害者被抢劫并严重受伤。

疑问句:
Were you mugged last night? = 你昨晚被抢劫了吗？
Has he ever been mugged before? = 他以前被抢劫过吗？

否定句:
I wasn't mugged; I just lost my wallet. = 我没有被抢劫；我只是丢了钱包。
He hasn't been mugged yet, but he's worried it will happen. = 他还没被抢劫过，但他担心会发生。
