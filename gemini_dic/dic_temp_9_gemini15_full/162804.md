preburlesque:/ˌpriːbərˈlɛsk/| adj.| 预先滑稽剧的；滑稽剧之前的 | Preburlesque refers to the period or style of entertainment that preceded the fully developed form of burlesque.  It often involved elements of comedy, satire, and parody, but lacked the explicitly sexualized nature that later characterized burlesque.  It might include musical acts, sketches, and comedic routines. |The preburlesque shows were less scandalous than their successors.|近义词：proto-burlesque, early burlesque|反义词：high burlesque, classic burlesque
