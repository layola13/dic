nondoctrinaire: /ˌnɒnˌdɒktrɪˈneər/ | adj. |非教条的；不墨守成规的 | Not bound by or following a doctrine or dogma; open-minded and flexible.  It implies a willingness to consider different perspectives and approaches without rigid adherence to established beliefs or principles. |The professor's approach to teaching was nondoctrinaire, encouraging students to question and explore different viewpoints. 教授的教学方法是非教条式的，鼓励学生质疑和探索不同的观点。|近义词：open-minded, flexible, tolerant, pragmatic |反义词：dogmatic, doctrinaire, rigid, inflexible


词源:

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not" or "without".
* **doctrinaire**:  Adjective meaning "relating to or characteristic of a doctrine or dogma; dogmatic".  It originates from the French word "doctrinaire,"  ultimately from the Latin "doctrina" meaning "teaching" or "doctrine".

用法说明:

The word "nondoctrinaire" is primarily used as an adjective to describe a person, approach, or system that is not rigidly bound by a particular set of beliefs or principles.  It emphasizes flexibility, open-mindedness, and a willingness to adapt.  It often contrasts with "doctrinaire," which implies a strict adherence to a particular ideology or doctrine, often to the exclusion of other perspectives.


例句:

He took a nondoctrinaire approach to the problem, considering various solutions before making a decision. 他采取了一种非教条的方式来解决这个问题，在做出决定之前考虑了各种解决方案。

Her nondoctrinaire leadership style fostered a collaborative and inclusive work environment. 她不墨守成规的领导风格营造了合作和包容的工作环境。
