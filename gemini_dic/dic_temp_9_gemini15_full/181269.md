reinstitutionalization: /ˌriːˌɪnstɪtjuːʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 重新机构化；再次收容；重新制度化 | The process of returning individuals or groups to institutional settings, often after a period of community-based care or deinstitutionalization.  This can refer to various settings, such as hospitals, prisons, or mental health facilities.  It often carries a negative connotation, suggesting a failure of community-based support systems or a relapse in an individual's condition. |

例句：The rise in homelessness has led to concerns about the reinstitutionalization of mentally ill individuals.  = 流浪汉的增加导致人们担心精神病患者会被重新收容。

例句：The reinstitutionalization of juvenile offenders is a complex issue with various contributing factors. = 青少年罪犯的重新收容是一个复杂的问题，有多种因素促成。


近义词：reinstatement, re-confinement, recommitment

反义词：deinstitutionalization


词性：名词
