grossing:/ˈɡroʊsɪŋ/ | v. | 总收入，总收入达 |  earning a specified amount of money as gross revenue.  The verb "grossing" refers to the total revenue generated before deductions for expenses.  | Example: The movie is grossing over $100 million worldwide. 这部电影在全球的总收入超过1亿美元。 |近义词：earning, taking in, raking in | 反义词：losing, netting (after expenses)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The film is grossing millions. = 这部电影正在赚取数百万美元。
The company is grossing over $1 billion annually. = 这家公司每年总收入超过10亿美元。


一般过去时:
The concert grossed $50,000. = 这场音乐会的总收入为5万美元。
The play grossed over $1 million during its run. = 这部戏在演出期间总收入超过100万美元。


现在完成时:
The new restaurant has grossed over $100,000 in its first month. = 这家新餐厅在第一个月总收入超过10万美元。
The business has grossed significantly more this year than last year. = 今年这家企业的总收入比去年显著增加。


过去完成时:
By the end of the year, the company had grossed $2 billion. = 到年底，该公司总收入达到20亿美元。
Before the renovations, the hotel had grossed significantly less. = 在装修之前，酒店的总收入要少得多。


将来时:
The new product line is expected to gross $50 million next year. = 预计明年新产品线的总收入将达到5000万美元。
Analysts predict the company will gross over $10 billion in the next decade. = 分析师预测该公司在未来十年内的总收入将超过100亿美元。


虚拟语气:
If the film grossed more, the studio would be happier. = 如果电影票房更高，制片厂会更高兴。
I wish the business had grossed more last quarter. = 我希望上季度业务总收入更高。


被动语态:
Millions of dollars were grossed by the blockbuster movie. = 这部卖座电影赚取了数百万美元。
Over $1 billion was grossed by the company last year. = 该公司去年总收入超过10亿美元。


疑问句:
How much did the event gross? = 这场活动总收入多少？
What was the total gross for the year? = 这年的总收入是多少？


否定句:
The small business didn't gross enough to cover its expenses. = 这家小企业赚的钱不够支付其开支。
The play didn't gross as much as expected. = 这部戏的票房不如预期。
