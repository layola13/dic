self-estimations: /ˌsɛlfˌɛstɪˈmeɪʃənz/ | n. | 自我评价，自我估量 |  The act of judging or assessing one's own abilities, qualities, or worth.  It involves forming opinions about oneself based on one's own perceptions and experiences. Self-estimations can be accurate or inaccurate, positive or negative, and can significantly impact one's self-esteem and behavior. |


例句：

His self-estimations were wildly inflated. = 他的自我评价高得离谱。

Her self-estimations were surprisingly accurate. = 她的自我评价出奇地准确。


近义词：self-assessments, self-appraisals, self-judgments

反义词：external evaluations (外部评价)


词性：名词
