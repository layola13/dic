non-receivable: /ˌnɒn rɪˈsiːvəbl/ | adj. | 不可收回的；坏账 |  Uncollectible;  referring to debts or accounts that are unlikely to be paid.  This term is often used in accounting and finance. | 例句：The company wrote off several non-receivable accounts.  这家公司注销了几笔坏账。

The non-receivable debts led to a significant loss for the business.  这些无法收回的债务给企业带来了重大损失。


近义词：uncollectible, bad debt, irrecoverable

反义词：receivable, collectible

词性: adjective (形容词)

词源:  The word is a combination of "non-" (meaning "not") and "receivable" (referring to something that can be received, particularly money owed).  "Receivable" derives from the verb "receive," ultimately from Old French "receivre" and Latin "recipere" (to take back).
