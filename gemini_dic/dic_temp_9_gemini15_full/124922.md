mudslide:/ˈmʌdˌslaɪd/ | n. |泥石流 | A mudslide is a mass of mud and other debris that flows down a slope, often after heavy rain.  Mudslides can be very destructive, burying homes and causing significant damage. | Example: The heavy rains triggered a massive mudslide that destroyed several homes in the valley.  这次暴雨引发了巨大的泥石流，摧毁了山谷中几座房屋。


近义词：landslide, debris flow, mudflow | 反义词：None (It's a phenomenon, not something with a direct opposite)
