downtrodden:/ˈdaʊnˌtrɒdn/ | adj. | 被压迫的；受虐待的；受欺凌的 |  Oppressed; treated unjustly and cruelly.  The downtrodden people finally rose up against their oppressors. 被压迫的人民最终反抗了他们的压迫者。 | 近义词：oppressed, suppressed, subjugated, persecuted | 反义词：privileged, empowered, liberated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The workers are downtrodden and overworked. 工人们被压迫并且工作过度。
She feels downtrodden and ignored. 她感到被压迫和被忽视。


一般过去时:

词源:
The word "downtrodden" comes from the verb "tread," meaning to step or walk.  The prefix "down" adds the sense of being repeatedly stepped on or oppressed.  Therefore, "downtrodden" literally means "trodden down,"  describing someone who is constantly subjected to oppression and hardship.
