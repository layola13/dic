hyperbolical: /ˌhaɪpərˈbɒlɪkəl/ | adj. |  夸张的，夸大的 | Exaggerated; not literal; characterized by exaggeration.  | The hyperbolical claims made by the salesman were easily disproven.  销售员的夸大其词很容易被驳倒。 | exaggerated, inflated, overstated | understated, literal, realistic |  形容词

He speaks in a hyperbolical manner. = 他说话方式夸张。
Her descriptions are often hyperbolical. = 她的描述常常很夸张。


一般过去时:
His speech was highly hyperbolical. = 他的演讲非常夸张。
She used hyperbolical language to describe her experience. = 她用夸张的语言来描述她的经历。


现在完成时:

She has made several hyperbolical statements about her achievements. = 她对自己的成就作了几次夸大的陈述。


过去完成时:

She had used hyperbolical language in her previous speeches. = 在她之前的演讲中她使用了夸张的语言。


将来时:

She will probably use hyperbolical language to impress the audience. = 她可能会使用夸张的语言来给观众留下深刻印象。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't used such hyperbolical language, people would have believed him. = 如果他没有使用如此夸张的语言，人们就会相信他。
I wish she hadn't used such hyperbolical descriptions in her report. = 我希望她在报告中没有使用如此夸张的描述。


被动语态:
His achievements were hyperbolically described in the press. = 他的成就被媒体夸大其词地描述了。
The situation was hyperbolically presented to the public. = 情况被夸大其词地呈现在公众面前。


疑问句:
Is his statement truly hyperbolical or just a figure of speech? = 他的说法真的是夸张的吗，还是仅仅是一种比喻？
Are her claims hyperbolical or based on facts? = 她的说法是夸张的还是基于事实的？


否定句:
His account of the event was not hyperbolical; it was quite accurate. = 他对事件的描述并非夸大其词；相当准确。
Her descriptions were not hyperbolical, but rather understated. = 她的描述并非夸张，而是轻描淡写。

词源:
From  hyperbole (itself from Greek ὑπερβολή (hyperbolḗ), meaning "excess, exaggeration").  The suffix "-ical" indicates an adjective form.
