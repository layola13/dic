mosaism:/moʊˈzeɪzəm/ | n. |镶嵌图案；马赛克；嵌合体 |The presence of two or more genetically distinct cell populations within an individual. This can result from mutations during development, resulting in patches of tissue with different genetic makeup.  Mosaicism can affect any tissue or organ, and its severity varies greatly depending on the type and extent of the genetic difference and the tissues involved. |例句：The artist created a beautiful mosaic of colored glass. 艺术家创作了一幅美丽的彩色玻璃马赛克作品。


The patient's genetic testing revealed mosaicism for a specific gene mutation. 患者的基因检测显示特定基因突变的嵌合体现象。|近义词：patchwork, variegation |反义词：uniformity, homogeneity
