logical-empiricism: /ˌlɒdʒɪkəlɪmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ | n. |逻辑实证主义|A philosophical movement that combines empiricism (the idea that all knowledge comes from sensory experience) and logic (the study of valid reasoning) to analyze and understand the world.  Logical empiricists emphasize verifiable statements and reject metaphysical speculation.  They sought to create a unified scientific worldview based on observation and logical analysis. | The Vienna Circle, a group of philosophers and scientists, was central to the development of logical empiricism.  Their work significantly influenced the philosophy of science and language. |近义词：positivism, logical positivism |反义词：metaphysics, idealism
