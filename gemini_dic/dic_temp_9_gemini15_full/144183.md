outsidedness: /ˌaʊtˈsaɪdədnəs/ | n. | 外来性；外部性；局外人的地位 | The state or quality of being outside; externality; the condition of being an outsider.  It can refer to a physical location, social standing, or a mental state of detachment. |

例句：

The outsidedness of the small village made it feel isolated from the rest of the world.  小村庄的偏远使其感觉与世界其他地方隔绝。

Her outsidedness in the company stemmed from her reluctance to participate in office politics.  她在公司里的局外人地位源于她不愿参与办公室政治。


近义词：externality, alienation, detachment, isolation, separateness

反义词：inclusion, integration, involvement, belonging, internality


词源:

The word "outsidedness" is derived from the adjective "outside" + the suffix "-ness," which is a common suffix used to form nouns that denote a state, quality, or condition.  "Outside" itself has Germanic roots, ultimately related to Old English *ūt ("out").  The "-ness" suffix is of Old English origin as well.


用法说明:


虽然outsidedness是一个相对较少使用的词，但它在学术写作和社会科学讨论中可以找到。 它常常用来描述一种状态或感觉，强调与某事物或某群体的分离或疏远感。  它可以指：

* **地理上的位置:**  例如，一个偏远地区的“outsidedness”。
* **社会地位:** 例如，一个社会群体中的“outsidedness”，表示被排斥或边缘化。
* **心理状态:** 例如，一个人的“outsidedness”，表示感觉与周围环境或他人脱节。


需要注意的是，“outsidedness” 通常暗示着一种消极的体验，表示缺乏归属感或参与感。
