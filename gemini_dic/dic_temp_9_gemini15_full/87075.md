heptarchic:/hepˈtɑːrkɪk/| adj.|七国统治的；七分统治的|Of or relating to a heptarchy, a state or territory ruled by seven kings or chieftains.  The term is most commonly associated with the historical period in Anglo-Saxon England.|Example:  The heptarchic period of Anglo-Saxon England was marked by constant conflict and shifting alliances among the seven kingdoms.|近义词：septempartite (if referring to a division into seven parts)|反义词：unified, centralized
