driftwind:/ˈdrɪftwɪnd/| n.|偏流风，侧风|A driftwind is a wind that blows at an angle to the intended course of a ship or aircraft, causing it to drift off course.  It is often caused by complex interactions of wind patterns and terrain.|例句：The pilot had to correct for the strong driftwind to maintain his heading. 飞行员必须校正强烈的偏流风以保持航向。


The strong driftwind pushed the sailboat far off course. 强烈的偏流风使帆船偏离航道很远。|近义词：crosswind, sidewind|反义词：headwind, tailwind|名词
