cain:/keɪn/ | n. | 该隐 | The name of Adam and Eve's first son, who murdered his brother Abel.  Cain is often used as a symbol of evil, jealousy, and fratricide.  | Example: The story of Cain and Abel is a cautionary tale about the consequences of envy. 该隐和亚伯的故事是一个关于嫉妒后果的警示故事。| 近义词：murderer, fratricide | 反义词：Abel (in the context of the biblical story) | 名词
