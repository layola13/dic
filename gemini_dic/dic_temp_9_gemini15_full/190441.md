sashays:/səˈʃeɪz/ | v. |轻快地走；扭动着走；(指人或物)轻快地移动 | To walk in a smooth, graceful, and often ostentatious way.  It often implies a confident and self-assured manner. |例句：She sashayed down the runway in a stunning gown. 她身穿一件令人惊艳的长袍，轻盈地走过T台。

She sashayed into the room, attracting everyone's attention. 她扭动着腰肢走进房间，吸引了所有人的注意。|近义词：strut, glide, swagger, promenade |反义词：shuffle, lumber, trudge

词源:
The word "sashay" is believed to have originated in the early 20th century. Its etymology is uncertain, but it's likely an onomatopoeic word, meaning it imitates the sound of a smooth, gliding movement.  Some theories link it to French words related to movement or style, but no definitive connection has been established.
