catalyzed:/ˈkætəlaɪzd/| v.|催化的；促成的|To accelerate or bring about (a process or reaction) by acting as a catalyst.|The addition of a small amount of catalyst catalyzed the reaction.|近义词：accelerated, expedited, facilitated|反义词：hindered, impeded, obstructed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The enzyme catalyzes the reaction. = 这种酶催化该反应。
Heat catalyzes the chemical process. = 热量促进了化学过程。

一般过去时:
The discovery catalyzed a wave of new research. = 这一发现引发了一波新的研究。
The meeting catalyzed important discussions. = 会议促成了重要的讨论。

现在完成时:
This innovation has catalyzed significant economic growth. = 这一创新已经促进了显著的经济增长。
The new policy has catalyzed a shift in public opinion. = 新政策促使了公众舆论的转变。

过去完成时:
The previous events had catalyzed the current crisis. = 先前的事件已经导致了当前的危机。
His speech had catalyzed the movement's growth. = 他的演讲促进了该运动的发展。

将来时:
The new technology will catalyze further advancements in the field. = 新技术将进一步推动该领域的进步。
The investment will catalyze the development of the project. = 这项投资将促进项目的开发。

虚拟语气:
If we had a better catalyst, the reaction would be catalyzed more quickly. = 如果我们有更好的催化剂，反应将会更快地被催化。
If the government had acted sooner, it might have catalyzed a faster recovery. = 如果政府早点采取行动，它可能会促使更快的复苏。

被动语态:
The reaction was catalyzed by the enzyme. = 该反应是由酶催化的。
The economic growth was catalyzed by government investment. = 经济增长是由政府投资促成的。

疑问句:
What catalyzed the change in policy? = 什么促成了政策的改变？
What factors catalyzed the company's success? = 哪些因素促成了公司的成功？

否定句:
The lack of funding did not catalyze the research project. = 缺乏资金并没有促进研究项目。
The slow response did not catalyze any significant change. = 缓慢的反应并没有促成任何重大变化。
