exaggerates:/ɪɡˈzædʒəreɪts/| v.|夸大；夸张；言过其实|To represent (something) as being larger, better, or worse than it really is.  |例句：He exaggerates his achievements. 他夸大了他的成就。|例句：Don't exaggerate the danger; it's not that serious. 不要夸大危险，没那么严重。|近义词：overstate, inflate, magnify|反义词：understate, downplay


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exaggerates his abilities. = 他夸大了自己的能力。
She exaggerates her problems. = 她夸大了她的问题。

一般过去时:
He exaggerated the size of the fish he caught. = 他夸大了他钓到的鱼的大小。
She exaggerated the difficulties she faced. = 她夸大了她面临的困难。

现在完成时:
He has exaggerated his role in the project. = 他夸大了他在项目中的作用。
She has exaggerated her contribution to the team. = 她夸大了她对团队的贡献。

过去完成时:
He had exaggerated his experience in the interview. = 他在面试中夸大了他的经验。
She had exaggerated the seriousness of the situation. = 她夸大了局势的严重性。

将来时:
He will exaggerate his success in the presentation. = 他将在演示中夸大他的成功。
She will exaggerate the impact of the news. = 她将夸大新闻的影响。

虚拟语气:
If he exaggerated, people wouldn't believe him. = 如果他夸大其词，人们就不会相信他。
I wish she hadn't exaggerated the situation. = 我希望她没有夸大其词。

被动语态:
His achievements were exaggerated by the media. = 他的成就被媒体夸大了。
The dangers were exaggerated to scare people. = 危险被夸大以恐吓人们。

疑问句:
Does he often exaggerate? = 他经常夸大其词吗？
Is she exaggerating the truth? = 她是在夸大真相吗？

否定句:
He doesn't exaggerate his accomplishments. = 他不会夸大他的成就。
She didn't exaggerate the details of the accident. = 她没有夸大事故的细节。
