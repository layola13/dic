reindulge:/ˌriːɪnˈdʌldʒ/| vt.|再次沉溺；再次放纵|To indulge again in something pleasurable, especially after a period of abstinence or self-denial.  It implies a return to a previous habit or behavior.|例句：After a month of strict dieting, she decided to reindulge in her favorite chocolate cake.  一个月严格节食后，她决定再次享用她最喜欢的巧克力蛋糕。
例句：He promised himself he wouldn't reindulge in his gambling habit, but the lure proved too strong. 他发誓不会再次沉迷于赌博，但诱惑力太大了。|近义词：overindulge,  yield to temptation|反义词：abstain, deny oneself|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reindulges in his old habits every weekend. = 他每个周末都会重蹈覆辙。
They reindulge their sweet tooth after a long diet. = 他们经过长时间的节食后又开始吃甜食了。


一般过去时:
She reindulged in a long bath after a stressful day. = 压力重重的一天后，她又好好泡了个澡。
He reindulged his passion for collecting stamps. = 他重拾了他对集邮的热情。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish I could reindulge in that delicious meal again. = 我真希望还能再吃一次那顿美味佳肴。


被动语态:
