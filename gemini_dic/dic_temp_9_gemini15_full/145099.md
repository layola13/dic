overappraising: /ˌoʊvərəˈpreɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 过高评价；估价过高 |  To estimate the value of something too highly.  This involves placing a higher value on something than it is objectively worth, often due to bias, emotional attachment, or lack of accurate information. |


例句：

The investor was accused of overappraising the company's potential, leading to significant losses. = 投资者被指控高估了公司的潜力，导致了巨大的损失。

He overappraised his own abilities and consequently failed to meet the deadline. = 他高估了自己的能力，结果错过了最后期限。


近义词：overestimating, overvaluing, inflating, exaggerating


反义词：undervaluing, underestimating


词性：动词
