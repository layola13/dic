imposement: /ɪmˈpoʊzmənt/ | n. |强加；施加；征收 | The act of imposing something, typically a burden, tax, or rule, on someone or something.  It often implies an unwelcome or unfair imposition. |例句：The imposement of new taxes angered many citizens.  新的税收的征收激怒了许多公民。

The imposement of strict rules stifled creativity. 严格规定的强加扼杀了创造力。


近义词：enforcement, imposition, infliction | 反义词：removal, lifting, abolition | 词性: noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government imposes new regulations frequently. = 政府经常实施新的规章制度。
This company imposes strict deadlines on its employees. = 这家公司对员工施加严格的期限。


一般过去时:
They imposed a curfew after the riot. = 骚乱后他们实施了宵禁。
The judge imposed a heavy fine on the defendant. = 法官对被告处以巨额罚款。


现在完成时:
The new law has imposed significant restrictions on businesses. = 新法律对企业施加了重大限制。
The company has imposed a hiring freeze due to budget cuts. = 由于预算削减，公司已经暂停招聘。


过去完成时:
They had imposed a travel ban before the outbreak worsened. = 在疫情恶化之前，他们已经实施了旅行禁令。
The school had imposed stricter dress codes after several incidents. = 在发生几起事件后，学校已经实施了更严格的着装规定。


将来时:
The council will impose higher parking fees next year. = 理事会明年将提高停车费。
The company will impose sanctions on employees who violate the policy. = 公司将对违反规定的员工处以制裁。


虚拟语气:
If they imposed stricter regulations, the problem might be solved. = 如果他们实施更严格的法规，这个问题可能会得到解决。
I wish they hadn't imposed such a harsh penalty. = 我真希望他们没有处以如此严厉的惩罚。


被动语态:
New taxes were imposed on the public without warning. = 新税收在没有警告的情况下强加于公众。
A strict dress code was imposed by the school. = 学校强制执行了严格的着装规定。


疑问句:
What restrictions will be imposed on data usage? = 将对数据使用施加什么限制？
Will new tariffs be imposed on imported goods? = 对进口商品会征收新的关税吗？


否定句:
The government did not impose any new taxes this year. = 政府今年没有征收任何新税。
The school did not impose any restrictions on student activities. = 学校没有对学生的活动施加任何限制。



PHRASE->
impose on someone =  麻烦某人
impose a burden on = 给...带来负担
impose a penalty/fine = 处罚/罚款
impose restrictions on = 对...施加限制
impose one's will on = 将自己的意志强加于


词源:  From Old French *imposer ("to impose," literally "to put upon"), from Latin imponere ("to put upon"), from in- ("on, upon") + ponere ("to put").
