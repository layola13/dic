plowgate:/ˈplaʊɡeɪt/| n. |犁沟；犁耕地带|A plowgate is a furrow or strip of land turned over by a plow in a single operation.  It refers to the width of land a plow can cover in one pass. The term is also sometimes used figuratively to describe a path or course of action.|The farmer carefully followed the plowgate as he plowed the field.|furrow, swathe| (No direct antonym; perhaps "unturned land")|名词
