/ˈreɪʃəlɪzəm/ | n. | 种族主义 |  Racialism refers to the belief that a particular race is superior to others, leading to prejudice and discrimination.  It often involves the assertion of the inherent superiority of one's own race and the inferiority of others, sometimes manifesting in systemic oppression.  The term is frequently used interchangeably with racism, although some argue that racialism emphasizes the belief system itself more than the actions resulting from it. |  The rise of racialism in the 19th century led to many injustices. | 近义词：racism, prejudice, bigotry | 反义词：equality, tolerance, inclusivity | 名词
