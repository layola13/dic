changan:/ˈtʃæŋ.æn/| n. |长安|Chang'an is a Chinese word referring to the ancient capital city,  often translated as "Eternal Peace" or "Long Peace".  It has been the name of several capital cities throughout Chinese history, most notably during the Han, Sui, and Tang dynasties.  The name evokes a sense of stability, prosperity, and imperial grandeur.  Today, the name is also used for a modern Chinese automotive brand.|  The ancient city of Chang'an was a major center of trade and culture on the Silk Road. |近义词: Xi'an (modern name for the area), capital |反义词:  None directly applicable.
