slashing:/ˈslæʃɪŋ/| adj., v.|猛烈的；削减；砍伐|The word "slashing" can be used as an adjective or a verb. As an adjective, it means very forceful, severe, or intense. As a verb, it means to cut something with a sharp instrument, often in a forceful or violent way.  |例句：The slashing wind whipped through the streets. 猛烈的风席卷街道。  The company announced slashing cuts to its workforce. 公司宣布大幅裁员。|近义词：(adj.) fierce, intense, severe; (v.) cutting, hacking, slashing|反义词：(adj.) gentle, mild, slight; (v.) patching, mending, repairing|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slashes the price. 他砍价。
She slashes the vegetables. 她切菜。

一般过去时:
They slashed the budget. 他们削减了预算。
He slashed the tires of the car. 他砍破了汽车的轮胎。

现在完成时:
The government has slashed taxes. 政府已经减税了。
She has slashed her way through the undergrowth. 她已经砍出一条路穿过灌木丛。

过去完成时:
They had slashed the prices before the sale. 他们在促销前已经降价了。
He had slashed his wrist. 他曾经割腕。

将来时:
They will slash the interest rates. 他们将会降低利率。
She will slash the fabric for her dress. 她将剪裁布料做她的衣服。

虚拟语气:
If they slashed the prices, more people would buy. 如果他们降价，更多的人会购买。
I wish they had slashed the budget sooner. 我希望他们早点削减预算。

被动语态:
The budget was slashed by the government. 预算被政府削减了。
The tires were slashed by someone. 轮胎被某人划破了。

疑问句:
Did they slash the prices? 他们降价了吗？
Will they slash the workforce? 他们会裁员吗？

否定句:
They didn't slash the budget. 他们没有削减预算。
She didn't slash the meat properly. 她没有把肉切好。
