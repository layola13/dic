gestalt:/ɡəˈʃtɑːlt/|/ˈʃtɑːlt/| n. |格式塔，整体|The Gestalt principle emphasizes that the whole is greater than the sum of its parts.  格式塔原理强调整体大于部分之和。|holism, totality, configuration|analysis, detail|名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun, and thus doesn't conjugate through verb tenses.  However, it can be used in various sentence structures.

Example sentences illustrating different usages:

Declarative sentences:

The artist's work demonstrates a strong sense of gestalt. = 艺术家的作品展现出很强的整体感。
We need to consider the gestalt of the problem, not just the individual components. = 我们需要考虑问题的整体，而不仅仅是各个组成部分。

Interrogative sentences:

Does the design have a clear gestalt? = 这个设计有清晰的整体感吗？
What is the gestalt of the situation? =  局势的整体情况是什么？

Exclamatory sentences:

What a gestalt! = 多么完美的整体！
That's a powerful gestalt! = 这是一个强大的整体！


PHRASE->
There are no common phrasal verbs using "gestalt."  The word is typically used as a noun on its own, or as part of established phrases like:

Gestalt therapy: 格式塔疗法
Gestalt psychology: 格式塔心理学
Gestalt principle: 格式塔原理
Gestalt switch: 格式塔转换
Law of Prägnanz (Gestalt):  格式塔的完形法则
