convener:/kənˈviːnə(r)/| n. |召集人，组织者|A person who calls a meeting together or arranges for it to take place.  |The convener of the meeting opened the session with a brief welcome.  会议召集人以简短的欢迎词开场。|organizer, chairman, chairperson|disbander, disorganizer


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He convenes the team meetings every Monday. 他每周一召集团队会议。
She convenes a meeting to discuss the project. 她召集会议讨论项目。

一般过去时:
The committee convener called the meeting to order. 委员会召集人宣布会议开始。
He convened a press conference to address the rumors. 他召开新闻发布会来回应谣言。

现在完成时:
The board has convened several times this year. 董事会今年已经召开过几次会议。
She has convened many successful workshops. 她已经主持过许多成功的研讨会。

过去完成时:
They had convened several meetings before reaching a decision. 他们在做出决定之前已经召开过几次会议。
The organizer had convened the group before the event started. 组织者在活动开始前已经召集了小组。

将来时:
The mayor will convene a town hall meeting next week. 市长下周将召开市民大会。
They will convene a special meeting to discuss the crisis. 他们将召开特别会议讨论危机。

虚拟语气:
If he were the convener, he would have handled the situation differently. 如果他是召集人，他会以不同的方式处理这种情况。
I wish the convener had been more organized. 我希望召集人更有条理。

被动语态:
The meeting was convened by the chairperson. 会议由主席召集。
A special meeting will be convened to address the concerns. 将会召开特别会议来解决这些担忧。

疑问句:
Who convened the meeting? 谁召集了会议？
When will the committee be convened? 委员会什么时候召开？

否定句:
He did not convene the meeting on time. 他没有按时召开会议。
The meeting was not convened due to unforeseen circumstances. 由于不可预见的情况，会议没有召开。
