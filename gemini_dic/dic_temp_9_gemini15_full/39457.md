conned:/kɒnd/| vt.|欺骗，诈骗|To trick or deceive someone, typically in order to gain something.|例句：He conned me out of my money. 他骗走了我的钱。|近义词：cheated, defrauded, swindled|反义词：helped, assisted


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cons his friends out of money regularly. = 他经常骗朋友的钱。
They con people into buying fake products. = 他们骗人买假货。


一般过去时:
The scammer conned the elderly woman out of her life savings. = 骗子骗走了这位老太太一生的积蓄。
He conned me into believing his lies. = 他骗我相信他的谎话。


现在完成时:
The company has conned thousands of customers. = 这家公司已经骗了成千上万的顾客。
He has conned his way to the top of the company. = 他靠欺骗手段爬到了公司的高层。


过去完成时:
They had conned the investors before the scandal broke. = 丑闻爆发前，他们已经骗取了投资者的钱。
I realised I had been conned after I signed the contract. = 我签了合同后才意识到自己被骗了。


将来时:
They will con more people if they are not caught. = 如果他们不被抓住，他们将会骗更多的人。
She will probably con him out of his inheritance. = 她可能会骗走他的遗产。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't conned me, I would have more money. = 如果他没有骗我，我就会有更多的钱。
I wish I hadn't been conned by that salesman. = 我真希望我没有被那个推销员骗。


被动语态:
Many people were conned by the pyramid scheme. = 很多人被传销骗了。
He was conned out of his valuable antique. = 他被骗走了他珍贵的古董。


疑问句:
Were you conned into buying that car? = 你是被骗着买了那辆车吗？
Has anyone ever conned you before? = 有人曾经骗过你吗？


否定句:
I wasn't conned; I knew exactly what I was doing. = 我没有被骗；我清楚自己在做什么。
They didn't con me; I saw through their trick. = 他们没有骗我；我识破了他们的诡计。


PHRASE->
con someone out of something = 骗取某人某物
con someone into doing something = 骗某人做某事
be conned = 被骗
con artist = 骗子
con game = 骗局
