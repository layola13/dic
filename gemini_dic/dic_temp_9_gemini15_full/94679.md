imprison:/ɪmˈprɪzn/| vt.|监禁，关押|To confine or keep someone in prison or a similar place.|例句：He was imprisoned for ten years. 他被监禁了十年。|例句：They imprisoned him without trial. 他们未经审判就将他监禁了。|近义词：jail, incarcerate, confine|反义词：release, liberate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government imprisons political opponents. = 政府监禁政治对手。
He imprisons his anger. = 他压抑着怒火。


一般过去时:
They imprisoned him for his crimes. = 他们因为他的罪行而将他监禁。
The dictator imprisoned thousands of people. = 独裁者监禁了成千上万的人。


现在完成时:
They have imprisoned him without a fair trial. = 他们未经公平审判就将他监禁了。
The country has imprisoned many innocent people. = 这个国家监禁了许多无辜的人。


过去完成时:
They had imprisoned him before the evidence was found. = 在证据被发现之前，他们已经将他监禁了。
The police had imprisoned the suspect for questioning. = 警方已将嫌疑犯拘留进行审问。


将来时:
They will imprison him if he breaks the law again. = 如果他再次违法，他们将会将他监禁。
The judge believes they will imprison the defendant. = 法官相信他们会将被告监禁。


虚拟语气:

I wish they hadn't imprisoned him so harshly. = 我希望他们没有那样严厉地将他监禁。
