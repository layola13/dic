push-to-the-wall: /pʊʃ tu ðə wɔːl/ | adj. | 危急的；走投无路的；逼到绝境的 |  Describes a situation where someone is facing extreme pressure or a critical point, often requiring immediate action or a desperate solution. It implies a situation of urgency and high stakes. |


例句：

The company was pushed to the wall by its debt. = 这家公司被债务逼到了绝境。

He was pushed to the wall and had to make a difficult decision. = 他被逼到了绝境，不得不做出一个艰难的决定。


近义词：desperate, critical, urgent, dire, extreme

反义词：easy, comfortable, relaxed, secure

词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The pressure pushes them to the wall. = 压力使他们走投无路。
This situation is pushing us to the wall. = 这种情况让我们走投无路。

一般过去时:
The deadline pushed them to the wall. = 截止日期使他们走投无路。
The economic downturn pushed many businesses to the wall. = 经济衰退使许多企业走投无路。

现在完成时:
The constant demands have pushed them to the wall. = 持续不断的需求使他们走投无路。
Years of neglect have pushed the infrastructure to the wall. = 多年的忽视使基础设施到了崩溃的边缘。

过去完成时:
They had been pushed to the wall before they finally agreed. = 在他们最终同意之前，他们已经被逼到了绝境。
The company had been pushed to the wall before the merger. = 在合并之前，这家公司已经被逼到了绝境。

将来时:
The upcoming election will push the government to the wall. = 即将到来的选举将使政府面临巨大压力。
Increasing competition will push many small businesses to the wall. = 日益激烈的竞争将使许多小型企业面临倒闭的风险。

虚拟语气:
If we were pushed to the wall, we would fight back. = 如果我们被逼到绝境，我们会反击。
I wish we weren't pushed to the wall like this. = 我希望我们不会像这样被逼到绝境。

被动语态:
The team was pushed to the wall by the unexpected setback. = 该团队因意外挫折而面临巨大压力。
The project was pushed to the wall due to budget cuts. = 由于预算削减，该项目面临巨大压力。

疑问句:

否定句:
