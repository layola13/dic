conceptionist:/kənˈsɛpʃənɪst/| n. | 观念论者；概念主义者 | A conceptionist is someone who emphasizes the role of concepts or ideas in understanding the world, often in contrast to those who emphasize sensory experience or empirical evidence. They might prioritize abstract thought and theoretical frameworks in their approach to knowledge.  |  Many philosophers are considered conceptionists, prioritizing reason and abstract thought over direct observation. | 近义词：idealist, rationalist | 反义词：empiricist, materialist | 名词
