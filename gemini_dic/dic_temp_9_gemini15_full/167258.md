/prɛˈtɛstɪfaɪ/| v.| 预先作证；提前作证 |To give testimony or evidence before an event officially occurs.  This is typically done to provide information in advance of a trial or hearing, often to support a particular claim or position.  The term is not standard English and is likely a blend of "pre-" and "testify."  It is not found in standard dictionaries. | Example: The witness pretestified to ensure his statement was recorded before the trial began. = 证人提前作证以确保他的证词在审判开始前被记录下来。|近义词:pre-emptively testify, give advance testimony|反义词:testify later, withhold testimony|动词
