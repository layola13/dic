semibureaucratic: /ˌsɛmiˌbjʊərəˈkrætɪk/ | adj. | 半官僚的；部分官僚化的 |  Partially or somewhat bureaucratic; exhibiting characteristics of a bureaucracy but not fully so.  It suggests a system or organization that has some bureaucratic features, such as hierarchy and formalized procedures, but also retains elements of flexibility or informality. |


例句：

The company's structure is semibureaucratic, with some departments operating more formally than others.  = 公司的结构是半官僚化的，有些部门运作比其他部门更正式。

The university's administration, while aiming for efficiency, retains a semibureaucratic approach to avoid stifling creativity. =  大学的行政管理虽然以效率为目标，但仍保留着半官僚化的做法，以避免扼杀创造力。


近义词：partially bureaucratic, quasi-bureaucratic, somewhat formalized


反义词：completely informal, entirely decentralized, anarchic


词性: adjective


词源:

* **semi-**:  A prefix meaning "half," "partly," or "partially."  Derived from Latin.

* **bureaucratic**:  Relating to or characteristic of a bureaucracy (a system of government in which most of the important decisions are taken by state officials rather than by elected representatives).  Derived from "bureau" (office) and "-cratic" (relating to power or rule).  Ultimately rooted in French and Greek.
