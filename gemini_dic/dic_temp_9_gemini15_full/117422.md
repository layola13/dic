medievalism:/ˌmidiˈiːvəlɪzəm/ | n. |中世纪主义；中世纪复兴 | The revival of medieval styles, values, or attitudes in a later period.  It often involves a romanticized or idealized view of the Middle Ages. |例句：The Gothic revival in architecture was a significant manifestation of medievalism.  哥特式建筑的复兴是中世纪主义的重要表现。

例句：Her novels are full of medievalism, featuring knights, castles, and courtly love.  她的小说充满了中世纪主义，以骑士、城堡和宫廷爱情为特色。


近义词：medieval revival, retrospection (of the Middle Ages)

反义词：modernism, futurism


词源:  The word "medievalism" is derived from "medieval" + "-ism". "Medieval" comes from the Latin "medius" (middle) + "aevum" (age), literally meaning "middle ages". The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, system, or practice.  Therefore, medievalism represents a specific doctrine or practice related to the Middle Ages.
