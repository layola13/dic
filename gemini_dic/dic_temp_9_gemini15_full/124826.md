muckerer:/ˈmʌkərər/| n. |爱搬弄是非的人，捣蛋鬼，弄脏者| A muckerer is someone who is habitually messy or untidy, or who interferes in other people's affairs in a troublesome way. They might be someone who creates disorder, spreads gossip, or generally causes trouble.  The term often carries a negative connotation. |The office muckerer left his dirty coffee cup on my desk again.|近义词：busybody, meddler, troublemaker, mischief-maker |反义词：neat freak, orderly person

词源:
The word "mucker" originates from the Scots word "muk," meaning "dung" or "manure."  The sense of "messy" or "untidy" is a direct development from this.  "Muckerer" is simply the agentive form, indicating the person who performs the action of mucking.
