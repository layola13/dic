scintillate:/ˈsɪntɪleɪt/| v.|闪耀；闪烁；才华横溢 |To sparkle; to shine brightly with flashes of light; to be brilliantly and excitingly clever or skillful.|The diamonds scintillated under the spotlight. 钻石在聚光灯下闪闪发光。  His wit scintillated throughout the evening. 他整晚都妙语连珠。|glitter, sparkle, gleam|dull, dim|动词

一般现在时:
The stars scintillate in the night sky. = 星星在夜空中闪耀。
The city lights scintillate in the distance. = 城市的灯光在远处闪烁。

一般过去时:

现在完成时:
The diamond has scintillated for centuries. = 这颗钻石已经闪耀了几个世纪。
His career has scintillated with success. = 他的事业辉煌成功。

过去完成时:
The chandelier had scintillated brightly before the power went out. = 在停电之前，枝形吊灯曾明亮地闪耀。
The debate had scintillated with insightful arguments before it degenerated. = 辩论在恶化之前曾因深刻的论点而精彩纷呈。

将来时:

The new play is expected to scintillate with humor and wit. = 这部新剧有望充满幽默和智慧。

虚拟语气:
If the diamonds scintillated more, they would be worth more. = 如果钻石闪耀得更多，它们就会更有价值。
I wish my writing could scintillate with such brilliance. = 我希望我的写作也能如此精彩。

被动语态:

疑问句:
Do the stars scintillate tonight? = 今晚星星闪耀吗？
Does his writing scintillate with originality? = 他的写作是否独具匠心？

否定句:
The dull gems did not scintillate. = 这些暗淡的宝石没有闪耀。
His speech failed to scintillate; it was quite boring. = 他的演讲没有精彩之处；相当乏味。
