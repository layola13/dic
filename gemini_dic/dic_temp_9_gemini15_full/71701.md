flavor:/ˈfleɪvər/| n.|味道，风味，香气，特色|The distinctive taste of a food or drink, or the distinctive quality of something.|The chocolate cake had a rich, delicious flavor. 这块巧克力蛋糕有着浓郁美味的味道。The new car has a sporty flavor. 这辆新车有一种运动风格。|taste, scent, aroma, character |tasteless, bland|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The cake has a strong chocolate flavor. = 这蛋糕有浓厚的巧克力味。
This wine possesses a fruity flavor. = 这葡萄酒带有果香味。


一般过去时:
The soup lacked flavor yesterday. = 昨天的汤缺乏味道。
The pie had a unique lemon flavor. = 这馅饼有独特的柠檬味。


现在完成时:
This chef has developed many unique flavors. =这位厨师已经开发了许多独特的味道。
We have experimented with many different flavors this year. = 今年我们尝试了许多不同的口味。


过去完成时:
The dessert had lost its flavor by the time we ate it. = 等我们吃的时候，甜点已经失去了味道。
She had never tasted such a strange flavor before. = 她以前从未尝过这么奇怪的味道。


将来时:
The new restaurant will offer a wide variety of flavors. = 这家新餐厅将提供各种各样的口味。
This dish will have a spicy flavor. = 这道菜会有辣味。


虚拟语气:
If this sauce had more flavor, it would be perfect. = 如果这个酱汁更有味道，那就完美了。
I wish this coffee had a stronger flavor. = 我希望这咖啡味道更浓一些。


被动语态:
The food is flavored with herbs and spices. = 食物用香草和香料调味。
The ice cream is known for its unique flavors. = 这冰淇淋以其独特的味道而闻名。


疑问句:
What is the flavor of this tea? = 这茶是什么味道？
Does this dish have a strong flavor? = 这道菜味道浓吗？


否定句:
The food had no flavor at all. = 这食物一点味道都没有。
The drink lacked any discernible flavor. = 这饮料没有任何可辨别的味道。


PHRASE->

a flavor of: 一丝…的味道;略带…的味道
full of flavor: 味道浓郁的
lose flavor: 走味；失去味道
a hint of flavor: 一丝味道
flavor enhancer: 调味剂
