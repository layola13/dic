chicanery:/ʃɪˈkeɪnəri/ | n. |欺骗；诡计；狡诈 |  The use of tricks to deceive someone (usually in a legal or political context). It implies cunning and deception, often involving elaborate schemes or underhanded methods.  | 例句：The lawyer was accused of using chicanery to win the case.  律师被指控使用欺骗手段赢得了这场官司。

The politician's campaign was marred by chicanery and false promises.  这位政治家的竞选活动充满了欺骗和虚假承诺。

| 近义词：trickery, deception, fraud, subterfuge, guile, artifice  | 反义词：honesty, truthfulness, straightforwardness | 名词
