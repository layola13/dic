pre-taste:/priːˈteɪst/| n. | 预先品尝；初步体验 | A preliminary taste or experience of something that will be fully experienced later.  It suggests a brief, anticipatory encounter, offering a hint of what's to come. | 例句：The pre-taste of the wine promised a full-bodied experience. 这款葡萄酒的预先品尝预示着一种浓郁的体验。

The pre-taste of the upcoming concert, through the snippets of music released online, was electrifying. 通过在线发布的音乐片段，即将到来的音乐会的预先体验令人兴奋。


近义词：preview, foretaste, anticipation | 反义词：aftertaste, review
