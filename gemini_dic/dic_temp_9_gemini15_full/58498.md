dueling:/ˈduːəlɪŋ/| n. v.|决斗；竞争|The act of fighting a duel; a contest or competition.|例句：Duelling was once common among gentlemen.  决斗曾经在绅士中很常见。  The two companies are engaged in a fierce dueling for market share. 两家公司正在进行激烈的市场份额争夺战。|近义词：combat, contest, rivalry|反义词：cooperation, collaboration


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are dueling over the inheritance. = 他们正在为遗产而争斗。
We duel regularly in online games. = 我们经常在网络游戏中对决。

一般过去时:
He dueled his rival in a field outside town. = 他在城外的一片田野里与他的对手决斗。
The two teams dueled for the championship. = 这两支球队为冠军而战。

现在完成时:
They have dueled many times before. = 他们以前决斗过很多次。
She has dueled with her opponent for years. = 她与对手对抗多年。

过去完成时:
He had dueled before, but never with such a skilled opponent. = 他以前曾决斗过，但从未与如此技艺高超的对手交手。
The two knights had dueled fiercely before the king arrived. = 两位骑士在国王到来之前进行了激烈的决斗。

将来时:
They will duel at dawn tomorrow. = 他们明天黎明将决斗。
The two debaters will duel on the topic of climate change. = 这两位辩论者将在气候变化的话题上进行辩论。

虚拟语气:
If he had dueled more wisely, he might have won. = 如果他更明智地决斗，他可能会赢。
I wish I had dueled him before he became so powerful. = 我真希望在他变得如此强大之前就和他决斗。

被动语态:
The duel was fought under the cover of darkness. = 决斗是在黑暗的掩护下进行的。
The matter was dueled out in the court of law. = 这件事在法庭上得到了解决（暗指激烈争辩）。

疑问句:
Will they duel again? = 他们还会再次决斗吗？
Did they duel fairly? = 他们的决斗公平吗？

否定句:
They did not duel; they settled their differences peacefully. = 他们没有决斗；他们和平地解决了分歧。
He would not duel anyone who wasn't a worthy opponent. = 他不会与任何不配为对手的人决斗。
