gravitate:/ˈɡrævɪteɪt/| vi.|趋向，倾向，被吸引|To move towards or be drawn to something.|People tend to gravitate towards jobs that offer better pay and benefits. 人们倾向于选择薪水和福利更好的工作。|近义词：tend, incline, lean|反义词：avoid, shun|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The iron filings gravitate towards the magnet.  铁屑被吸引到磁铁上。
People naturally gravitate towards those who share their interests. 人们自然而然地被志趣相投的人吸引。


一般过去时:
The discussion gravitated towards the issue of funding. 讨论逐渐转向资金问题。
He gravitated towards the quiet corner of the room. 他走向房间的安静角落。


现在完成时:
Over time, the company has gravitated towards a more sustainable business model.  随着时间的推移，公司已经转向更可持续的商业模式。
My interests have gravitated towards history and archaeology. 我的兴趣已转向历史和考古学。


过去完成时:
Before the meeting, the conversation had gravitated towards a resolution. 会议之前，谈话已趋向于达成解决方案。
By the end of the evening, the guests had gravitated towards the dance floor.  到晚上结束时，客人们都聚集到舞池。


将来时:
I think the conversation will gravitate towards politics soon. 我认为谈话很快就会转向政治。
The focus of the research will gravitate towards renewable energy. 研究重点将转向可再生能源。


虚拟语气:
If she were here, she would gravitate towards the books. 如果她在，她会倾向于看书。
I wish my attention would gravitate towards my studies more often. 我希望我的注意力能更经常地集中在学习上。


被动语态:
The debate was gravitated towards a compromise. 辩论最终达成妥协。(Note:  "Gravitate" is rarely used in the passive voice; this example shows a possible, albeit less natural, construction)
The conversation is being gravitated towards a solution. 谈话正在朝着解决方案的方向发展。(Note:  This is also an unnatural use of the passive voice)


疑问句:
Why do people gravitate towards large cities? 为什么人们会向大城市迁移？
Where do your interests gravitate? 你的兴趣爱好是什么？


否定句:
He didn't gravitate towards the challenges; he avoided them. 他没有迎难而上，而是避开了它们。
The project didn't gravitate towards a successful conclusion. 项目没有朝着成功的方向发展。


PHRASE->
gravitate towards: 趋向于…
gravitate to: 被吸引到…
gravitate around:  围绕…展开
gravitate to a decision:  最终决定
gravitate to a conclusion: 得出结论


词源:
The word "gravitate" comes from the Latin word "gravitas," meaning "weight" or "heaviness."  The scientific concept of gravity gave rise to the figurative meaning of being drawn towards something.
