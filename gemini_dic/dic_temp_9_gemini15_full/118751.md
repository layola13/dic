metalinguist:/ˌmɛtəlɪŋˈɡwɪst/| n. |元语言学家，超语言学家|A metalinguist is someone who studies the use of language to discuss language itself. They analyze language's structure, function, and use in a way that is separate from the content of the language being used.  This often involves analyzing things like grammar, syntax, semantics, and pragmatics.  |Example: The metalinguist presented a paper on the evolution of grammatical structures in English. 元语言学家发表了一篇关于英语语法结构演变的论文。|近义词：linguist, language scholar|反义词：N/A

词源:

The word "metalinguistics" (the study itself) is a combination of "meta-" (meaning "beyond" or "about") and "linguistics" (the study of language).  "Metalinguist" is simply the person who engages in metalinguistics.
