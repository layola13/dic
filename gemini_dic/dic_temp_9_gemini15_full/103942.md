judicata:/ˌdʒuːdɪˈkeɪtə/| n.|已判决之事；既判力|A judicata is a matter that has been adjudicated, meaning it has been decided by a court of law and is therefore binding.  It refers to a legal principle that prevents the same issue from being litigated twice.  This principle is designed to promote finality and efficiency in the legal system.|例句：The court ruled that the matter was res judicata, preventing the plaintiff from filing another lawsuit on the same issue. 法院裁定此事已成既判事实，阻止原告就同一问题提起另一起诉讼。|近义词：precedent, prior judgment, settled matter|反义词：pending case, open question
