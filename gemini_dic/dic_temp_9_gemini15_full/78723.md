gigging:/ˈɡɪɡɪŋ/| n. v.|演出；用叉捕鱼|The act of performing gigs (musical performances) or the act of catching fish with a gig.|例句：He's got a gigging tonight. 他今晚有一场演出。|近义词：performing, fishing, spearing|反义词：rehearsing (for v.), netting (for n.)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gigs regularly at that club. = 他定期在那家俱乐部演出。
She gigs in various pubs around town. = 她在城里不同的酒吧演出。

一般过去时:
They gigged all summer long. = 他们整个夏天都在演出。
He gigged for a small audience last night. = 他昨晚为少数观众演出。

现在完成时:
I have gigged in many different countries. = 我在许多不同的国家演出过。
She has gigged with some famous musicians. = 她曾与一些著名音乐家一起演出。

过去完成时:
They had gigged extensively before signing a record deal. = 在签署唱片合约之前，他们已经广泛地演出过。
We had gigged at that venue several times before. = 我们以前在那家场馆演出过好几次。

将来时:
We will gig at the festival next month. = 我们下个月将在音乐节上演出。
They will be gigging in London next week. = 他们下周将在伦敦演出。

虚拟语气:
If I could gig every night, I would. = 如果我每晚都能演出，我会的。
I wish I could gig at a bigger venue. = 我希望我能在一个更大的场馆演出。

被动语态:
The band was gigged extensively last year. = 乐队去年进行了广泛的演出。(被动语态在此处用得较少，更常见的是主动语态。)
The fish was gigged and quickly dispatched. = 鱼被叉到并迅速处理掉了。

疑问句:
Are they gigging tonight? = 他们今晚演出吗？
Where are you gigging this weekend? = 你这个周末在哪里演出？

否定句:
We didn't gig last weekend. = 我们上周末没有演出。
He doesn't gig very often. = 他不经常演出。


PHRASE->
gigging opportunities: 演出机会
going gigging: 去演出
a successful gig: 一场成功的演出
a grueling gigging schedule: 繁重的演出行程
a promising gig: 一个有前途的演出
