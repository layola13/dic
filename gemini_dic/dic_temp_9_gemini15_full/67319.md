extemporaneous:/ˌɛkspʌmˈteɪniəs/| adj.|即席的；无准备的；临时的|Spoken or done without notes or preparation; impromptu.|The speaker delivered an extemporaneous speech that captivated the audience.  演讲者发表了一篇精彩的即兴演讲，吸引了观众。|impromptu, improvised, off-the-cuff|premeditated, planned, rehearsed|

词源:
From Latin *extemporāneus, from ex tempore "out of time," from ex "out of" + tempore "time."  The original meaning referred to something done "on the spur of the moment," implying a lack of premeditation.
