post-Augustan: /ˌpoʊstɔːˈɡʌstən/ | adj. | 奧古斯都時期以後的；後奧古斯都時期的 | Relating to the period in Roman literature and culture following the reign of Emperor Augustus (27 BC – 14 AD).  This period is generally characterized by a decline in the classical ideals and styles associated with the Augustan Age, often marked by increased moral laxity and stylistic experimentation. | Example: Post-Augustan poetry often shows a departure from the formal elegance of Virgil and Horace. 後奧古斯都時期的詩歌往往偏離了維吉爾和賀拉斯的正式優雅風格。 | 近义词: post-classical, late classical | 反义词: Augustan, classical
