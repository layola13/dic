phatic:/ˈfeɪtɪk/| adj.|寒暄的；社交的；社交性语言的| relating to or denoting language used to maintain social relationships rather than convey information.  Phatic communication is a type of speech that serves a social function rather than a purely informational one.  It's used to establish, maintain, or end social contact. Examples include greetings, farewells, and small talk. |例句：The seemingly meaningless pleasantries exchanged at the start of a meeting are examples of phatic communication.  会议开始时交换的看似毫无意义的客套话是寒暄式交流的例子。|近义词：social, conversational, ritualistic|反义词：informative, direct, factual
