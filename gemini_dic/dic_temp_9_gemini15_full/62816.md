enharmonic:/ɛnˈhɑːrmɒnɪk/| adj.|同音异名的，等音的|Relating to or denoting notes or chords that sound the same but are written differently.  Enharmonic notes are notes that share the same pitch but have different names. For example, C# and Db are enharmonic equivalents.  The term is commonly used in music theory.|例句：C sharp and D flat are enharmonic equivalents.  C升和D降是同音异名的。|近义词：equivalent, synonymous (in the context of pitch)|反义词：dissonant (in the context of sound, not spelling)|无
