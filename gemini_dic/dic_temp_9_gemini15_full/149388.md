pacinian:/pəˈsɪniən/| adj.|帕西尼氏的；（指）帕西尼氏小体|Pacinian refers to Pacinian corpuscles, a type of mechanoreceptor found in the skin and other tissues.  These receptors are sensitive to pressure and vibration.  The term is derived from the name of Filippo Pacini, the Italian anatomist who first described them.|例句：Pacinian corpuscles are responsible for detecting rapid vibrations and pressure changes.  帕西尼氏小体负责检测快速振动和压力变化。|The term doesn't have direct synonyms or antonyms as it's a specific anatomical term.  |adj.
