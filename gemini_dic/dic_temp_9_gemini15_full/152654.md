peak:/piːk/| n., v.|顶峰；高峰；达到顶点|The highest point of something, such as a mountain or a graph; to reach the highest point or level. |例句：The mountain peak was covered in snow.  这座山峰覆盖着白雪。

例句：The company's profits peaked last year.  公司利润去年达到顶峰。|summit, apex, zenith|valley, bottom, nadir|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The mountain peaks at 10,000 feet. = 这座山峰高达一万英尺。
Sales usually peak during the holiday season. = 销售额通常在节日期间达到顶峰。

一般过去时:
The stock market peaked in 2021. = 股市在2021年达到顶峰。
He peaked in his early thirties. = 他在三十出头的时候达到巅峰。

现在完成时:
The river has peaked and is now receding. = 河水已达到峰值，现在正在退去。
We have peaked in our productivity this quarter. = 我们本季度的生产力达到了顶峰。

过去完成时:
The temperature had peaked before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前温度已经达到最高点。
The athlete had peaked before the Olympics. = 运动员在奥运会之前就已经达到巅峰状态。

将来时:
The flu season will peak in January. = 流感季节将在1月份达到高峰。
We anticipate that demand will peak next spring. = 我们预计需求将在明年春天达到顶峰。

虚拟语气:
If the project had peaked earlier, we could have started the next one sooner. = 如果项目早点达到顶峰，我们就可以早点开始下一个项目了。
I wish my career had peaked later. = 我希望我的事业巅峰期能晚一些到来。

被动语态:
The demand is expected to be peaked next year. = 预计明年需求将达到峰值。
The mountain peak is often peaked by climbers. = 登山者经常攀登这座山峰。

疑问句:
When did the sales peak? = 销售额在什么时候达到顶峰？
Has the project peaked yet? = 项目达到顶峰了吗？

否定句:
The performance didn't peak until the third act. = 直到第三幕表演才达到高潮。
The economy hasn't peaked yet. = 经济还没有达到顶峰。
