phoebus:/ˈfiːbəs/| n. |阿波罗（太阳神）; 太阳|The name Phoebus is a poetic and literary term for the sun, derived from the Greek god Apollo, who was often associated with the sun and light.|例句：The poet described the phoebus as a fiery chariot across the sky.  诗人将太阳描述为横跨天空的火热战车。  The setting phoebus cast long shadows across the land.  落日的余晖在地面上投下长长的影子。|近义词：sun, Apollo, Helios|反义词：moon, night
