connivance:/kəˈnɪvəns/| n. |纵容；默许；共谋|The secret agreement or cooperation between people to do something wrong, especially to deceive or cheat someone.  It implies a tacit agreement or willful blindness to wrongdoing. |例句：The success of the fraud was due to the connivance of several senior employees.  (这场欺诈的成功是由于几名高级雇员的纵容造成的。)

The police suspected connivance between the criminals and some corrupt officials. (警方怀疑罪犯和一些腐败官员之间存在勾结。)


近义词：collusion, conspiracy, complicity, collusion, tacit agreement |反义词：opposition, resistance, protest


词源:  From Middle French *connivence, from Latin connivere "to wink, close the eyes," from con- "together" +  nivere "to wink, close the eyes". The original sense implied turning a blind eye to wrongdoing.
