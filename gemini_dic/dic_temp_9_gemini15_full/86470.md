hegiras:/hɪˈɡaɪrəz/ | n. |  逃亡；迁徙；大规模移民 | A hegira is a flight or emigration, especially of a large number of people.  It often implies a journey undertaken to escape persecution or oppression. The term is often used in reference to the journey of the Prophet Muhammad from Mecca to Medina in 622 AD. | Example: The hegira of the refugees from the war-torn country was a tragic but necessary event.  逃离饱受战争蹂躏的国家的难民的迁徙是一件悲惨但必要的事件。 |近义词: exodus, migration, flight, emigration | 反义词: immigration, return, settlement | 名词
