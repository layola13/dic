scandalises: /ˈskændəlaɪzɪz/ | v. | 使震惊；使愤慨；使丑闻缠身 | To shock or offend someone by doing something considered morally wrong or improper.  | 例句：His behavior scandalized the entire community.  他的行为震惊了整个社区。


The actions of the politician scandalized the public.  这位政治家的行为激怒了公众。


近义词：outrage, shock, appall, offend | 反义词：please, delight, soothe


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His actions scandalise many people. = 他的行为令许多人震惊。
The news scandalises the public. = 这则新闻令公众感到震惊。

一般过去时:
The scandal scandalised the whole nation. = 这场丑闻震惊了全国。
His words scandalised his mother. = 他的话令他母亲感到震惊。

现在完成时:
The revelation has scandalised the entire country. = 这项揭露已经震惊了全国。
This behavior has scandalised the family. = 这种行为令全家蒙羞。

过去完成时:
They had scandalised the town before they left. = 他们在离开之前就震惊了整个城镇。
I had never been so scandalised in my life. = 我一生中从未如此震惊。

将来时:
His confession will scandalise everyone. = 他的供词将会震惊所有人。
The truth will scandalise the world. = 真相将会震惊世界。

虚拟语气:
If he knew, he would be scandalised. = 如果他知道，他会很震惊。
I would be scandalised if that were true. = 如果那是真的，我会非常震惊。

被动语态:
The public was scandalised by the news. = 公众被这则新闻震惊了。
The community was scandalised by the mayor's actions. = 社区被市长的行为震惊了。

疑问句:
Would you be scandalised by this behaviour? = 你会被这种行为震惊吗？
What would scandalise you the most? = 什么事情最能让你感到震惊？

否定句:
The news didn't scandalise me. = 这则新闻并没有让我震惊。
His actions did not scandalise everyone. = 他的行为并没有震惊所有人。


PHRASE->
be scandalised by = 被...震惊
deeply scandalised = 深感震惊
utterly scandalised = 极其震惊
feel scandalised = 感到震惊
become scandalised = 变得震惊

词源:
From the noun "scandal" (a disgraceful event),  + "-ise" (verb suffix indicating to make or become).  Ultimately derived from the Greek word "skandalon" (a trap or stumbling block).
