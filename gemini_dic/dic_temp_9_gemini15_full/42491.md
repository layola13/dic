counterhypotheses:/ˌkaʊntərhaɪˈpɒθɪsiːz/ | n. |反假设，对立假设 | Alternative hypotheses that contradict or challenge the main hypothesis.  They are used to test the robustness of the main hypothesis by considering alternative explanations for the observed phenomena.  Counterhypotheses are crucial in scientific research to avoid confirmation bias and ensure the validity of conclusions. | Example: The researchers formulated several counterhypotheses to their initial theory about the origin of the species, exploring alternative evolutionary pathways. | 近义词：alternative hypotheses, competing hypotheses | 反义词：supporting hypotheses, confirming hypotheses
