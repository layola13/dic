lurches:/lɜːrtʃɪz/| v.| 蹒跚；摇晃；突然倾斜；|To move suddenly and awkwardly from side to side or forward.  It often implies a lack of control or balance. |例句：The old car lurches violently when accelerating. 这辆旧车加速时剧烈地摇晃。

The drunken man lurches down the street.  那个醉汉摇摇晃晃地走在街上。|近义词：stagger, sway, tilt, wobble|反义词：glide, flow, smoothly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The old bus lurches around corners. = 这辆旧公共汽车拐弯时摇摇晃晃。
He lurches when he walks. = 他走路时摇摇晃晃的。


一般过去时:
The ship lurches violently in the storm. = 船在暴风雨中剧烈摇晃。
The building lurches before collapsing. = 建筑物摇晃了一下然后倒塌了。


现在完成时:
The train has lurched several times during the journey. = 火车在旅途中已经摇晃了好几次。
The economy has lurched from one crisis to another. = 经济已经从一个危机摇摆到另一个危机。


过去完成时:
The car had lurched violently before coming to a stop. = 汽车在停下之前剧烈地摇晃了一下。
The plane had lurched dangerously before the pilot regained control. = 飞机在飞行员重新获得控制之前危险地摇晃了一下。


将来时:
The boat will lurch as it turns. = 船在转弯时会摇晃。
I think the building will lurch and fall down soon. = 我认为这座建筑物很快就会摇晃并倒塌。


虚拟语气:
If the bridge were to lurch, it would be catastrophic. = 如果桥摇晃起来，那将是灾难性的。
I wish the rollercoaster wouldn't lurch so much. = 我希望过山车不要晃动得这么厉害。


被动语态:
The passengers were lurched forward by the sudden braking. = 乘客由于突然刹车而向前猛冲。
The small boat was lurched about by the waves. = 小船被海浪颠簸得厉害。


疑问句:
Does the car always lurch like that? = 这辆车总是这样摇晃吗？
Did the building lurch before it collapsed? = 建筑物倒塌前是否摇晃过？


否定句:
The car didn't lurch at all during the journey. = 汽车在旅途中根本没有摇晃。
The ship didn't lurch despite the rough seas. = 尽管风浪很大，船却没有摇晃。


PHRASE->
lurch forward = 向前猛冲
lurch to one side = 向一边倾斜
lurch into action = 突然行动起来
lurch from one crisis to another = 从一个危机摇摆到另一个危机
a sudden lurch = 突然的倾斜


词源:
The word "lurch" ultimately derives from the Old French word "lorche," meaning "a trick" or "a deceit."  However, its evolution into its current meaning of a sudden, unsteady movement is not fully clear, likely involving a semantic shift over time.
