experience:/ɪkˈspɪəriəns/ | n. v. |经验；体验；经历 |  The process of personally observing, encountering, or undergoing something.  It can refer to the knowledge or skill acquired through such processes, or the event itself. |例句：My experience in customer service has been invaluable.  我的客户服务经验非常宝贵。

例句：I had a wonderful experience traveling through Europe. 我在欧洲旅行的经历很棒。


近义词：event, incident, adventure, expertise, knowledge | 反义词：inexperience, naivety


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He experiences difficulties in his work. 他在工作中遇到困难。
She experiences joy in helping others. 她从帮助他人中获得快乐。


一般过去时:
I experienced a lot of challenges during my college years. 我在大学期间经历了很多挑战。
They experienced a power outage last night. 他们昨晚经历了停电。


现在完成时:
She has experienced many ups and downs in her career.  她在职业生涯中经历了许多起伏。
We have experienced significant growth in the past year. 我们在过去一年中取得了显著的增长。


过去完成时:
He had experienced similar problems before. 他以前遇到过类似的问题。
By the time she arrived, she had experienced a long and tiring journey.  在她到达时，她已经经历了一段漫长而疲惫的旅程。


将来时:
I will experience the thrill of skydiving next week. 我下周将体验跳伞的刺激。
They believe they will experience success with their new project. 他们相信他们的新项目将会成功。


虚拟语气:
If I had experienced this before, I would have reacted differently. 如果我以前经历过这种情况，我会采取不同的反应。
I wish I had experienced more travel opportunities in my youth. 我希望我年轻时有更多旅行机会。


被动语态:
The new product is being experienced by many customers. 许多顾客正在体验这款新产品。
The difficulties were experienced by the whole team.  整个团队都经历了这些困难。


疑问句:
Have you ever experienced anything like this before? 你以前有没有经历过这样的事情？
What kind of experiences have shaped your worldview?  什么样的经历塑造了你的世界观？


否定句:
He has not experienced any major setbacks in his business. 他的生意没有经历过任何重大挫折。
I have never experienced such a beautiful sunset. 我从未见过如此美丽的日落。
