out-boxed:/ˌaʊtˈbɑːkst/ | v. | 超出…的范围；胜过；打败 | To surpass or exceed the capabilities or performance of something or someone; to defeat someone in a competition. |例句：The new model out-boxed its predecessor in terms of speed and efficiency. 新型号在速度和效率方面都超过了其前代产品。 |例句：Our team out-boxed their team in the final round. 我们队在决赛中击败了他们队。 |近义词：surpass, exceed, outdo, defeat, beat |反义词：underperform, lose


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The new phone out-boxes the old one in terms of battery life. = 新手机的电池续航能力超过了旧手机。
This strategy out-boxes the competitor's approach. = 这一策略胜过竞争对手的方法。


一般过去时:
Their arguments out-boxed our initial concerns. = 他们的论点驳倒了我们最初的担忧。
Last year's sales figures out-boxed our predictions. = 去年的销售额超过了我们的预测。


现在完成时:
The company has out-boxed its rivals in the market. = 这家公司在市场上已经击败了竞争对手。
Technological advancements have out-boxed many traditional methods. = 技术进步已经超越了许多传统方法。


过去完成时:
By the end of the match, he had out-boxed his opponent. = 比赛结束时，他已经击败了他的对手。
The team had out-boxed their expectations, achieving a remarkable victory. = 该队超出了他们的预期，取得了显著的胜利。


将来时:
We expect the new product to out-box the competition. = 我们预计新产品将胜过竞争对手。
Their innovative design will likely out-box all others in the market. = 他们的创新设计很可能在市场上胜过所有其他设计。


虚拟语气:
If they had trained harder, they might have out-boxed their opponents. = 如果他们训练更努力，他们可能会击败对手。
I wish our team had out-boxed the other team. = 我希望我们的队伍能够击败另一支队伍。


被动语态:
The previous record was out-boxed by the new athlete. = 先前的记录被新的运动员打破了。

疑问句:

否定句:
