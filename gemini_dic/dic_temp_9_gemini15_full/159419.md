polarizes:/ˈpoʊləraɪzɪz/ | v. | 使两极分化；使偏振 | To cause (something) to become divided into two opposing groups.  | The issue of immigration polarizes public opinion.  移民问题使公众意见两极分化。 | divide, split, fracture | unite, unify, consolidate | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The debate polarizes the community. = 这场辩论使社区两极分化。
This issue polarizes voters. = 这个问题使选民两极分化。


一般过去时:
The election polarized the country. = 那次选举使国家两极分化。
His comments polarized the audience. = 他的评论使观众两极分化。


现在完成时:
The media has polarized public discourse. = 媒体已经使公众言论两极分化。
The conflict has polarized the society. = 冲突已经使社会两极分化。


过去完成时:
The previous government had already polarized the nation before the crisis. = 在危机爆发之前，前政府就已经使国家两极分化了。
The discussions had polarized the team before a decision was reached. = 在做出决定之前，讨论已经使团队两极分化了。


将来时:
This policy will likely polarize voters further. = 这项政策可能会进一步使选民两极分化。
The upcoming election will probably polarize the nation. = 即将到来的选举可能会使国家两极分化。


虚拟语气:
If the government hadn't acted, the situation would have polarized even more. = 如果政府没有采取行动，情况就会更加两极分化。
I wish the media wouldn't polarize the public on this issue. = 我希望媒体不要在这个问题上使公众两极分化。


被动语态:
Public opinion was polarized by the president's speech. = 总统的讲话使公众舆论两极分化。

疑问句:

否定句:
The leader attempted to avoid polarizing the public. = 领导人试图避免使公众两极分化。
The aim was not to polarize the community but to unite it. = 目的不是使社区两极分化，而是团结它。
