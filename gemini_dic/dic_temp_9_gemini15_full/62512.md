endozoa:/ˌɛndoʊˈzoʊə/| n. |内寄生虫|Endozoa are animals that live inside the bodies of other animals, deriving nourishment and shelter from their hosts.  They are a paraphyletic group, meaning they do not include all descendants of their common ancestor.  Many endozoa are parasites, causing harm to their hosts. |Example: Tapeworms are a common example of endozoa. 绦虫是内寄生虫的一个常见例子。|近义词：internal parasites, endoparasites|反义词：ectozoa, exoparasites|名词
