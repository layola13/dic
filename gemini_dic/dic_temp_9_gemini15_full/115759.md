marinades:/ˌmærɪˈneɪdz/| n. |腌料；腌渍 |A marinade is a mixture of liquids, such as oil, vinegar, wine, or soy sauce, often with herbs and spices, used to flavor and tenderize meat, fish, or vegetables before cooking.  Marinades can also add moisture and enhance the color of the food. |Example:  I marinated the chicken in a lemon-herb marinade for two hours before grilling it. 我在烤之前用柠檬草药腌料腌制鸡肉两个小时。|近义词：seasoning, brine |反义词：N/A
