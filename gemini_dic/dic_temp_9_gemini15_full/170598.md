pseudo-Elizabethans:/ˌsuːdoʊɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθənz/| adj. |假伊丽莎白时代的；模仿伊丽莎白时代的|Adjective describing works of art, literature, or architecture that imitate the style of the Elizabethan era (roughly 1558-1603) in England, but are not genuinely from that period.  These imitations might intentionally evoke the style for artistic effect or unintentionally reflect a later artist's understanding of the period. |例句：The play, while enjoyable, was criticized for its pseudo-Elizabethan language, which felt forced and unconvincing. 这部戏虽然引人入胜，但其矫揉造作、缺乏说服力的假伊丽莎白时代语言受到了批评。

例句：That building's pseudo-Elizabethan architecture is a striking contrast to the surrounding modern structures. 那座建筑的假伊丽莎白式建筑风格与周围的现代建筑形成了鲜明的对比。


近义词：faux-Elizabethan, imitation Elizabethan, mock-Elizabethan

反义词：authentic Elizabethan, genuine Elizabethan


词性：形容词
