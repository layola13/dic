not-a-little:/ˌnɑːt ə ˈlɪtl/| adj.|相当多；不少；非常|The phrase "not a little" is an idiom that means "to a considerable extent" or "very much".  It emphasizes the degree or extent of something, often suggesting a surprising or significant amount. It's a slightly more emphatic way of saying "quite a lot" or "considerably".|He was not a little surprised by the news. 他对这个消息相当惊讶。

She was not a little upset about the delay. 她对延误非常生气。

近义词：considerably, quite a lot, very much, substantially

反义词：a little, slightly, hardly at all

词性：形容词
