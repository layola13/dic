diocletian:/ˌdaɪəˈkliːʃən/| n. |戴克里先|The Roman emperor Diocletian (244/245 – 311/312 AD) implemented significant reforms, including the Tetrarchy, a system of four co-emperors to rule the vast empire more efficiently.
