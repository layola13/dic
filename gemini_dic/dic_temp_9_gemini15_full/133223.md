/ˌnɒnɪkˌstʃeɪnʒəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 不可交换性 | The inability of two or more things to be exchanged or substituted for one another without altering the overall outcome or value.  This often arises in contexts where order matters, or where items have unique properties that cannot be replicated.  For example, in some statistical models, the order of observations is important and they cannot be freely permuted.  | The nonexchangeability of the data points makes standard statistical methods inappropriate.  数据的不可交换性使得标准统计方法不适用。
