preafflict:/ˌpriːəˈflɪkt/| v.|预先折磨；预先使痛苦|To inflict suffering or hardship on someone or something beforehand or in anticipation of something else.  The word is rare and largely found in literary contexts.|例句：The thought of the upcoming surgery preafflicted her with anxiety.  = 即将到来的手术让她提前感到焦虑。
The impending storm preafflicted the coastal towns with fear. = 即将来临的风暴让沿海城镇提前感到恐惧。|近义词：preoccupy, pre torment, foreshadow |反义词：soothe, comfort, alleviate |动词
