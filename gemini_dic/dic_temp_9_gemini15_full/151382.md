parliament:/ˈpɑːləmənt/ /ˈpɑːrləmənt/| n. |议会，国会|A parliament is a body of representatives that makes laws for a country or region.  It typically consists of elected members who debate and vote on legislation.  The structure and powers of a parliament vary significantly depending on the country's political system.|Example: The British Parliament is made up of the House of Commons and the House of Lords.  英国议会由下议院和上议院组成。|近义词：congress, legislature, assembly|反义词：monarchy (as a contrasting system)|名词
