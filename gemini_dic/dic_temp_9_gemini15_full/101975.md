isocephalism:/ˌaɪsoʊˈsɛfəlɪzəm/| n. |等头性，等头现象|The state or quality of having heads of equal size or height, especially in a group of plants or animals.  Isocephalism is often used in botany to describe a plant community where the dominant plants are of similar height. |Example: The even distribution of tree heights in the forest exhibited a remarkable degree of isocephalism.|近义词：uniformity (in height), evenness|反义词：heterophylly (uneven leaf shape), unevenness
