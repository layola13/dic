jugging:/ˈdʒʌɡɪŋ/ | n. |慢跑；慢跑运动 | The act of running at a steady, moderate pace, usually for exercise or training.  It's a popular form of cardiovascular exercise. |例句：He goes jogging every morning before work. 他每天早上上班前都慢跑。

例句：She enjoys jogging in the park on weekends. 她喜欢周末在公园慢跑。


近义词：running, jogging, trotting | 反义词：sprinting, racing


词性：名词 (n.)

词源:
The word "jogging" is derived from the verb "jog," which means to run at a steady, gentle pace.  The verb "jog" itself has a long history, with roots tracing back to Old English words associated with movement and jolting.  Its modern usage as a form of exercise became popular in the late 20th century.
