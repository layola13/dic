shabby:/ˈʃæbi/| adj. |破旧的；寒酸的；卑鄙的|  Of poor quality, worn-out, or in disrepair; appearing neglected or impoverished. It can also describe behavior that is mean or unfair. |例句：The old house was shabby and needed repairs. 这栋旧房子破旧不堪，需要修理。

The politician's shabby treatment of his constituents angered many people.  这位政客对选民的卑劣待遇激怒了许多人。|近义词：worn, dilapidated, ragged, mean, paltry |反义词：smart, elegant, luxurious, generous, splendid
