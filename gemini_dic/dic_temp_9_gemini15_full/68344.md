fall-through: /ˈfɔːlθruː/ | adj. | 默认的，直接执行的；（计划等）失败的，没有成功的 |  Describes a situation where, in the absence of a specific condition being met, a default action or outcome occurs.  It often refers to a situation in programming where a case in a switch statement or similar construct is executed if no other case matches.  It can also describe a plan or project that fails to achieve its intended goal. | 例句：If the first condition isn't met, it will fall-through to the next one. = 如果第一个条件不满足，它将直接执行下一个条件。


The project fell through due to a lack of funding. = 由于缺乏资金，该项目失败了。| 近义词：default,  predetermined, unsuccessful | 反义词：successful, planned, intentional | 词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The program falls through to the default case if no match is found. = 如果找不到匹配项，程序将直接执行默认情况。
This plan usually falls through because of unexpected issues. = 这个计划通常因为意外问题而失败。


一般过去时:
The negotiation fell through because both sides couldn't reach an agreement. = 谈判破裂了，因为双方都无法达成协议。
The deal fell through at the last minute. = 这笔交易在最后一刻失败了。


现在完成时:
Several projects have fallen through this year due to the economic downturn. = 由于经济衰退，今年已经有几个项目失败了。
The attempt to climb the mountain has fallen through because of the bad weather. = 由于恶劣天气，攀登这座山的尝试失败了。


过去完成时:
The backup plan was already in place in case the primary plan had fallen through. = 如果主要计划失败了，备用计划已经到位了。
They realized that the original strategy had fallen through before they even started. = 甚至在他们开始之前，他们就意识到最初的策略失败了。


将来时:

I fear the deal will fall through unless we compromise. = 我担心除非我们妥协，否则这笔交易将会失败。


虚拟语气:

If the negotiations hadn't fallen through, we would have a contract by now. = 如果谈判没有失败，我们现在应该已经有合同了。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
