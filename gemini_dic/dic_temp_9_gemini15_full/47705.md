debuting:/dɛˈbjuːɪŋ/ | v. | 首次公演；首次亮相；初次登场 |  The act of making a first public appearance or performance.  It implies a formal introduction or presentation, often of a significant nature, such as a new product, artist, or play. | 例句：The band is debuting their new album next month.乐队下个月将发行他们的新专辑。 The young actress is debuting on Broadway.这位年轻女演员将在百老汇首演。 | 近义词：launching, introducing, unveiling, premiering | 反义词：retiring, concluding, ending | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The band is debuting a new song tonight. = 乐队今晚将首次演唱一首新歌。
She is debuting her new collection at Paris Fashion Week. = 她将在巴黎时装周上首次发布她的新系列。

一般过去时:
The film debuted to rave reviews. = 这部电影首映获得了好评如潮。
He debuted his new novel at a literary festival. = 他在文学节上首发了他的新小说。

现在完成时:
The artist has debuted several successful albums. = 这位艺术家已经发行了几张成功的专辑。
She has debuted in several films since graduating from acting school. = 自从戏剧学校毕业以来，她已经出演了几部电影。

过去完成时:
By the time the play debuted, the critics had already written their reviews. = 在戏剧首演之前，评论家们已经写好了他们的评论。
The singer had debuted several singles before releasing her first album. = 在发行她的第一张专辑之前，这位歌手已经发行了几首单曲。

将来时:
The company will be debuting its new product line next quarter. = 公司将在下个季度推出其新的产品线。
They will debut their new dance routine at the competition. = 他们将在比赛中首次表演他们的新舞蹈。

虚拟语气:
If they debuted their song earlier, they might have won the award. = 如果他们早点发布他们的歌，他们可能就获奖了。
I wish I had been there to see her debut performance. = 我真希望我能在那儿看到她的首秀。

被动语态:
The new car was debuted at the auto show. = 新车在车展上首次亮相。
The painting was debuted at a private gallery. = 这幅画在一间私人画廊首次展出。

疑问句:
When is the new product debuting? = 新产品什么时候首发？

否定句:
The play did not debut as planned. = 这部戏没有按计划首演。
They haven't debuted their new single yet. = 他们还没有发行他们的新单曲。
