captivated:/ˈkæptɪveɪtɪd/| v. (过去分词)|迷住的；被吸引的；被俘虏的|To hold the attention of someone completely; to fascinate.  |例句：I was captivated by her beauty.  我被她的美貌迷住了。

例句：The audience was captivated by the magician's performance. 观众被魔术师的表演迷住了。


近义词：fascinated, charmed, enthralled, enchanted

反义词：bored, uninterested, indifferent

词性：动词（过去分词）


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I captivate the audience with my stories. = 我用我的故事迷住了观众。
The scenery captivates many tourists every year. =  每年都有许多游客被风景迷住。


一般过去时:
The novel captivated me from beginning to end. = 这本小说从头到尾都吸引着我。
Her singing captivated the entire room. = 她的歌声迷住了整个房间的人。


现在完成时:
The museum has captivated millions of visitors. = 这个博物馆已经吸引了数百万游客。
His performance has captivated audiences worldwide. = 他的表演已经迷住了全世界的观众。


过去完成时:
I had been captivated by his charm before I even met him. = 在我见到他之前，我就已经被他的魅力迷住了。
She had been captivated by the story for hours before she realized it. = 她被故事迷住了几个小时才意识到。


将来时:
The new exhibit will captivate visitors with its interactive displays. = 新展览将以其互动展示吸引游客。
This movie will captivate you with its amazing plot. = 这部电影将会以其精彩的情节吸引你。


虚拟语气:
If the movie were better, it would captivate more people. = 如果电影更好，它会吸引更多的人。
I wish this book could captivate me as much as the last one did. = 我希望这本书能像上一本那样吸引我。


被动语态:
The audience was captivated by the speaker's charisma. = 观众被演讲者的魅力所吸引。
Many people were captivated by the beauty of the sunset. = 许多人被日落的美丽所吸引。


疑问句:
Was the crowd captivated by the concert? = 观众被音乐会迷住了吗？
Are you captivated by the story? = 你被这个故事迷住了吗？


否定句:
The movie didn't captivate me at all. = 这部电影一点也没有吸引我。
The lecture failed to captivate the students. = 讲座未能吸引学生。


PHRASE->
be captivated by = 被…迷住
utterly captivated = 完全被迷住
instantly captivated = 一下子就被迷住了
become captivated = 开始被迷住
remain captivated = 保持着被迷住的状态


词源:
The word "captivated" comes from the Latin word "captivāre," meaning "to capture" or "to take captive."  This reflects the sense of being completely "taken" or held by something fascinating.
