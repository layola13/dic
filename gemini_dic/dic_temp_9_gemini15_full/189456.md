saint:/seɪnt/ | n. |圣人，圣徒 | A person acknowledged as holy or virtuous and regarded in the Christian faith as being in heaven after death.  |例句：Many saints are revered for their acts of charity and compassion. 许多圣徒因其慈善和同情行为而受到尊敬。|例句：She dedicated her life to helping others, truly living the life of a saint. 她将自己的一生奉献给帮助他人，真正活出了圣人的生活。|近义词：holy person, righteous person, blessed one |反义词：sinner, villain, wicked person

一般现在时:
He is a saint in my eyes. = 在我眼中他是个圣人。
She is considered a saint by many. = 许多人认为她是个圣人。

一般过去时:
He was known as a saint in his community. = 他在他所在的社区被称为圣人。
They revered him as a saint after his death. = 他死后，人们将他尊为圣人。

现在完成时:
She has become a saint in the eyes of her followers. = 在她追随者眼中，她已经成为圣人。
He has been canonized as a saint by the Church. = 他已被教会封为圣人。

过去完成时:
They had already considered him a saint before his death. = 在他死前，人们就已经将他视为圣人。
She had long been regarded as a saint by the local people. = 当地居民很久以前就将她视为圣人。

将来时:
He will be remembered as a saint for generations to come. = 后代将会永远铭记他是一位圣人。
She will likely be canonized as a saint in the future. = 她将来很可能会被封为圣人。

虚拟语气:
If he were a saint, he would not have done that. = 如果他是圣人，他就不会那样做了。
I wish I were a saint and could help everyone in need. = 我希望我是一个圣人，可以帮助所有需要帮助的人。

被动语态:
He was declared a saint by the Pope. = 他被教皇宣布为圣人。
She is being considered for sainthood. = 她正在被考虑封圣。

疑问句:

Was she canonized as a saint? = 她被封为圣人了吗？

否定句:
