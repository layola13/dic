preportraying: /ˌpriːpɔːrˈtreɪɪŋ/ | v. | 预先描绘；预先刻画 |  To depict or represent something beforehand; to foreshadow.  This word is a less common, more formal, and somewhat archaic-sounding version of "portraying" with the addition of "pre-" emphasizing the action happening before something else. |


例句：The author's prologue preportrayed the tragic events to come.  = 作者的序言预示了即将发生的悲剧事件。

The artist preportrayed the scene on a small sketch before beginning the large mural. = 艺术家在开始大型壁画之前，先在一个小的素描上预先描绘了场景。


近义词：foreshadow, prefigure, anticipate, preface, prelude


反义词：postscript, epilogue, afterthought


词性：动词
