centonism:/ˈsɛntənɪzəm/| n. |拼缀诗歌；拼缀法|The practice of creating a new literary work by patching together fragments from existing works.  It involves assembling passages from various sources to form a coherent, albeit derivative, whole.  This technique was particularly common in late antiquity. |Example:  The poem is a striking example of centonism, drawing liberally from the works of Virgil and Ovid. 这首诗是拼缀法的突出例子，大量借鉴了维吉尔和奥维德的作品。|近义词：patchwork, collage, pastiche |反义词：originality, innovation
