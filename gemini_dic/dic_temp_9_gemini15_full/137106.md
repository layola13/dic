nonsubjugable: /ˌnɒnˈsʌbdʒuɡəbl/ | adj. | 不可征服的，不可压制的 |  Not capable of being subjugated or conquered; unconquerable.  The word highlights an inherent resistance to being controlled or dominated. It emphasizes a stubborn refusal to yield or submit to authority or force. | 例句：The nonsubjugable spirit of the people fueled the revolution.  人民不可征服的精神推动了革命。

The nonsubjugable mountain range proved a formidable obstacle to the expedition.  不可征服的山脉证明了探险队面前的一个巨大的障碍。


近义词：unconquerable, invincible, indomitable, insuperable | 反义词：subjugable, conquerable, subdued, submissive


词源：

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not"
* **subjug-**: from Latin "subjugare," meaning "to bring under the yoke," "to subdue."  This reflects the historical context of conquest and control.
* **-able**: suffix indicating capability or possibility.


USAGE->

由于nonsubjugable是形容词，它本身不具备时态变化。  以下例句展示了其在不同句法结构中的用法：

否定句:
The enemy was not nonsubjugable; they eventually surrendered. = 敌人并非不可征服；他们最终投降了。
Their spirit, though strong, was not entirely nonsubjugable.  = 他们的精神虽然强大，但并非完全不可征服。


疑问句:
Is the problem nonsubjugable, or is there a solution? = 这个问题是不可克服的吗，还是有解决办法？
Was the fortress truly nonsubjugable, or was it simply well-defended? =  这座堡垒真的是不可征服的吗，还是仅仅防御良好？
