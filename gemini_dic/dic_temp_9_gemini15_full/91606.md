hyperaltruism: /ˌhaɪpərˈæltruɪzəm/ | n. | 超利他主义 | The behavior of acting altruistically in a way that reduces one's own fitness and increases the fitness of others, even to the point of self-sacrifice, exceeding what would be expected from kin selection or reciprocal altruism.  This often involves helping unrelated individuals. | Example:  Donating a kidney to a stranger exemplifies hyperaltruism.  超利他行为的一个例子是向陌生人捐献肾脏。|近义词: extreme altruism, selfless dedication, self-sacrifice | 反义词: selfishness, egoism, self-interest |
