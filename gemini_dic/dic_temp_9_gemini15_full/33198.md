chromosomes:/ˈkroʊməˌsoʊmz/ | n. | 染色体 | Thread-like structures located inside the nucleus of animal and plant cells.  They carry genetic information in the form of genes.  | Example:  Human cells contain 23 pairs of chromosomes. 人类细胞包含23对染色体。  | Example: The study of chromosomes is crucial to understanding heredity. 染色体研究对于理解遗传至关重要。|近义词：chromatin,  gene carrier | 反义词： (no direct antonym)

词源:
The word "chromosome" comes from the Greek words "chroma" (χρώμα), meaning "color," and "soma" (σῶμα), meaning "body."  This is because chromosomes are readily stained with dyes, making them easily visible under a microscope.  The term was coined in the late 19th century.
