expected:/ɪkˈspektɪd/ | adj. | 预期的，期待的 |  Something that is expected is thought likely to happen or be true.  | The expected outcome is a win for the home team. 预期的结果是主队获胜。  The rain was expected, so we took umbrellas.  预计会下雨，所以我们带了伞。 | anticipated, foreseen, predicted | unexpected, unforeseen, surprising | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
We expect good weather this weekend. = 我们预计这个周末天气晴好。
They expect to arrive before dark. = 他们预计在天黑前到达。


一般过去时:
I expected him to call. = 我期待他打电话。
She expected better results. = 她期待更好的结果。


现在完成时:
We have expected a rise in interest rates. = 我们一直预期利率会上涨。
They haven't expected such a strong reaction. = 他们没有预料到这么强烈的反应。


过去完成时:
I had expected to finish the work by now. = 我原本以为现在已经完成工作了。
She had expected a more formal event. = 她原本以为会是一个更正式的场合。


将来时:
We will expect a lot of visitors next week. = 我们预计下周会有很多访客。
He will expect you to help. = 他会指望你帮忙。


虚拟语气:
I expected that she would be happy, but she wasn't. = 我以为她会很开心，但她并没有。
If I had expected this, I would have prepared differently. = 如果我预料到这一点，我会做不同的准备。


被动语态:
A lot of rain is expected tomorrow. = 明天预计会有很多雨。
The project is expected to be completed next month. = 该项目预计下个月完成。


疑问句:
Is rain expected tomorrow? = 明天会下雨吗？
What is expected of me? = 我应该做什么？


否定句:
The results were not as expected. = 结果并非预期的那样。
I didn't expect him to say that. = 我没想到他会这么说。


PHRASE->
as expected = 正如预期
less than expected = 少于预期
more than expected = 多于预期
beyond expectation = 超出预期
as expected  =不出所料
