semicretins:/ˌsemiˈkriːtɪnz/| n. |半分泌物|A term used in older medical literature referring to substances in the body that have some, but not all, of the characteristics of secretions.  It's now largely obsolete and not used in modern medical terminology. The term suggests something partially secreted or having incomplete secretory function.  |Example:  The research paper discussed the presence of semicretins in the thyroid gland. (This would be an example from older literature.)|近义词: partial secretions (in a historical context)|反义词: secretions, excretions (in a comparative sense)|名词
