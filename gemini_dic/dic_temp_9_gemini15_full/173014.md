quarantines:/ˈkwɒrəntiːnz/ | n. |隔离期；检疫；隔离措施 |The act of isolating individuals or groups to prevent the spread of disease or other harmful elements.  It refers to a period of enforced isolation. |例句：The government imposed strict quarantines to contain the virus. = 政府实施严格的隔离措施以控制病毒蔓延。

例句：  Passengers arriving from affected areas were subject to mandatory quarantines. = 来自受影响地区的乘客必须接受强制隔离。


近义词：isolation, confinement, seclusion, quarantine period

反义词：freedom, release, liberty


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government quarantines infected individuals. = 政府隔离感染者。
This hotel quarantines guests from high-risk areas. = 这家酒店隔离来自高风险地区的客人。


一般过去时:
They quarantined the city after the outbreak. = 爆发后，他们对这座城市进行了隔离。
The ship was quarantined in the harbor. = 这艘船在港口被隔离。


现在完成时:
The country has quarantined several regions to prevent the spread of the disease. = 该国已隔离了几个地区以防止疾病蔓延。
They have quarantined the infected animals. = 他们已经隔离了受感染的动物。


过去完成时:
They had quarantined the village before the disease spread further. = 在疾病进一步蔓延之前，他们已经隔离了村庄。
The authorities had quarantined the suspect before questioning him. = 当局在审问嫌疑人之前已将其隔离。


将来时:
The authorities will quarantine anyone showing symptoms. = 当局将隔离任何出现症状的人。
The airline will quarantine passengers who have been in contact with infected individuals. = 航空公司将隔离与感染者有过接触的乘客。


虚拟语气:
If we had quarantined the city earlier, the outbreak might have been contained more effectively. = 如果我们早点隔离城市，疫情本来可以得到更有效的控制。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The government did not quarantine the region immediately. = 政府没有立即隔离该地区。
