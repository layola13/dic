chloroplasts:/ˈklɔːrəˌplæsts/ | n. |叶绿体 |  Organelles found in plant cells and some other organisms that conduct photosynthesis. They contain chlorophyll, a green pigment that absorbs light energy to convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose (sugar) and oxygen.  | Example:  Chloroplasts are the sites of photosynthesis in plant cells. 叶绿体是植物细胞中进行光合作用的场所。

The leaves’ chloroplasts are responsible for the plant’s green color. 叶子的叶绿体赋予植物绿色。|近义词: plastids (更广义的细胞器)|反义词:  (无直接反义词，可以考虑与不含叶绿体的细胞器对比，如 mitochondria 线粒体)|词性:名词
