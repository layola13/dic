presid:/ˈprɛzɪd/| v.|主持，担任主席；主导|To preside means to act as the chair or leader of a meeting, organization, or ceremony.  It implies authority and control over the proceedings.|例句：The judge presided over the trial.  法官主持了审判。|例句：The chairman presided at the meeting. 主席主持了会议。|近义词：chair, govern, lead, conduct|反义词：follow, obey, submit


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The judge presides over the court. = 法官主持法庭。
The chairperson presides over the meeting. = 主席主持会议。

一般过去时:
The mayor presided at the ceremony. = 市长主持了仪式。
She presided over the committee for five years. = 她主持了该委员会五年。

现在完成时:
He has presided over the company's growth for decades. = 他几十年来一直主持着公司的发展。
The judge has presided over many important cases. = 法官审理过许多重要案件。


过去完成时:
Before the new CEO arrived, she had presided over the company for ten years. = 在新任首席执行官到来之前，她已经主持公司十年了。
He had presided over several similar projects before this one. = 在这个项目之前，他已经主持过几个类似的项目。

将来时:
The chief justice will preside over the Supreme Court hearing. = 首席法官将主持最高法院的听证会。
She will preside at the next board meeting. = 她将主持下一次董事会会议。

虚拟语气:
If he were to preside, the meeting would be more orderly. = 如果他来主持，会议将会更有秩序。
I wish the experienced lawyer had presided over the case. = 我希望经验丰富的律师能主持这个案子。

被动语态:
The meeting was presided over by the vice president. = 副总裁主持了会议。
The trial was presided over by a fair and impartial judge. = 审判由一位公正无私的法官主持。

疑问句:

Who presided at yesterday's meeting? = 昨天是谁主持的会议？

否定句:
He did not preside over the committee. = 他没有主持委员会。
The inexperienced lawyer did not preside over the important case. = 没有经验的律师没有主持这个重要的案件。
