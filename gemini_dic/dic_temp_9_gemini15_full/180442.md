regarded:/rɪˈɡɑːrdɪd/| v. |认为，看待，重视| considered, viewed, deemed, held to be;  to think of or consider in a specified way.|例句：His work is highly regarded in the scientific community.  他的作品在科学界享有盛誉。|例句：She regarded him with suspicion. 她怀疑地看着他。|近义词：considered, deemed, viewed, esteemed|反义词：disregarded, overlooked, ignored|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He regards her as a friend. = 他把她当作朋友。
They regard the project as successful. = 他们认为这个项目是成功的。

一般过去时:
She regarded him with suspicion. = 她怀疑地看着他。
The painting was regarded as a masterpiece. = 这幅画被认为是杰作。

现在完成时:
His contributions have been highly regarded. = 他的贡献一直备受推崇。
We have regarded this matter as urgent. = 我们一直认为这件事很紧急。

过去完成时:
They had regarded him as a hero. = 他们曾经把他当作英雄。
The decision had been regarded as controversial. = 这个决定曾被认为是有争议的。

将来时:
They will regard this as a major breakthrough. = 他们将会把这看作一项重大突破。
The new policy will be regarded as unfair. = 新政策将会被认为是不公平的。

虚拟语气:
If they regarded the situation differently, they would act differently. = 如果他们对形势有不同的看法，他们就会采取不同的行动。
I wish they regarded my opinion more seriously. = 我希望他们能更认真地对待我的意见。

被动语态:
His work is regarded as innovative. = 他的作品被认为是创新的。
The proposal was regarded with skepticism. = 该提案受到了怀疑。

疑问句:
How is his work regarded in your field? = 他的作品在你们领域里是如何评价的？
How was the treaty regarded by the public? = 公众是如何看待这项条约的？

否定句:
They did not regard his suggestion seriously. = 他们没有认真对待他的建议。
She did not regard him as a threat. = 她不认为他是一个威胁。


PHRASE->
be regarded as = 被认为是
highly regarded = 高度评价的
generally regarded = 一般认为
widely regarded = 广泛认为
well regarded = 受好评的
