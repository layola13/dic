levitate:/ˈlɛvɪteɪt/| vt. vi.|使……漂浮；漂浮|To rise or cause to rise and hover in the air, especially by supernatural means.  The magician levitated a small child.  |近义词：float, hover, suspend|反义词：fall, sink, plummet|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The magician levitates a rabbit. = 魔术师让一只兔子漂浮起来。
He levitates effortlessly. = 他毫不费力地漂浮起来。


一般过去时:
The monk levitated during meditation. = 僧人在冥想时漂浮起来。
She levitated the table with her mind. = 她用意念使桌子漂浮起来。


现在完成时:
He has levitated objects many times. = 他多次使物体漂浮起来。
They have never levitated before. = 他们以前从未漂浮过。


过去完成时:
The audience gasped as the performer had levitated. = 当表演者漂浮起来时，观众倒吸了一口气。
Before the trick, he had never levitated. = 在表演之前，他从未漂浮过。


将来时:
The scientist will levitate the object using magnetic fields. = 科学家将利用磁场使物体漂浮起来。
I believe I will one day levitate. = 我相信有一天我会漂浮起来。


虚拟语气:
If I could levitate, I would explore the world from above. = 如果我能漂浮，我将从高处探索世界。
I wish I could levitate like a bird. = 我希望我能像鸟一样漂浮。


被动语态:
The object was levitated by the powerful magnets. = 物体被强大的磁铁漂浮起来。
The table was levitated using a hidden mechanism. = 桌子是用隐藏的装置漂浮起来的。


疑问句:
Can he levitate? = 他能漂浮吗？
Does she know how to levitate? = 她知道如何漂浮吗？


否定句:
The object did not levitate. = 物体没有漂浮起来。
I cannot levitate, unfortunately. = 不幸的是，我不能漂浮。


PHRASE->
levitate objects = 使物体漂浮
levitate in the air = 在空中漂浮
levitate through the air = 穿过空中漂浮
levitate with ease = 轻松地漂浮
levitate effortlessly = 毫不费力地漂浮

词源:
From the Latin word "levis" (light) + "are" (to raise).  It literally means "to make light," referring to the apparent lightness of something that levitates.
