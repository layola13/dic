count-down:/ˈkaʊntdaʊn/| n. |倒计时，倒数|A countdown is a sequence of numbers that decrease, typically announcing the approach of an event.|例句：The countdown to the launch began. 倒计时发射开始了。|The countdown to the new year is exciting. 新年倒计时令人兴奋。|近义词：reverse count, timer|反义词：count-up


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The countdown begins at ten. = 倒计时从十开始。
We count down the seconds until the fireworks. = 我们倒数秒数直到烟花燃放。


一般过去时:
The countdown ended at zero. = 倒计时在零结束。
They counted down from ten to one. = 他们从十倒数到一。


现在完成时:
We have counted down to the launch. = 我们已经倒数到发射了。
They have completed the countdown. = 他们已经完成了倒计时。


过去完成时:
The crowd had already begun the countdown before the announcement. = 在宣布之前，人群已经开始倒计时了。
The astronauts had completed the final countdown before liftoff. = 宇航员在发射前完成了最后一次倒计时。


将来时:
We will count down to the new year tonight. = 我们今晚将倒数新年。
The announcer will begin the countdown in five minutes. = 播音员将在五分钟后开始倒计时。


虚拟语气:
If we had more time, we could have done a longer countdown. = 如果我们有更多时间，我们可以进行更长时间的倒计时。
I wish we had started the countdown earlier. = 我希望我们早点开始倒计时。


被动语态:
The countdown was announced over the loudspeaker. = 倒计时通过扩音器宣布。
The countdown is being displayed on the large screen. = 倒计时正在大屏幕上显示。


疑问句:
When will the countdown begin? = 倒计时什么时候开始？
How long is the countdown? = 倒计时有多长？


否定句:
The countdown did not start on time. = 倒计时没有准时开始。
They did not complete the countdown. = 他们没有完成倒计时。
