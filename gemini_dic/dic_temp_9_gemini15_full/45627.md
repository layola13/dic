cursers:/ˈkɜːrsərz/| n.|咒骂者，诅咒者|A curser is a person who curses or uses profane language.|例句：The angry curser hurled insults at the referee.|近义词：swearer, blasphemer|反义词：blesser


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He curses frequently. = 他经常咒骂。
She curses under her breath. = 她低声咒骂。


一般过去时:
He cursed at the broken machine. = 他对着坏掉的机器咒骂。
She cursed her bad luck. = 她咒骂自己的厄运。


现在完成时:
He has cursed his fate many times. = 他多次诅咒自己的命运。
She has cursed at the traffic jam for hours. = 她对着堵车咒骂了几个小时。


过去完成时:
He had cursed the day he met her. = 他曾诅咒过他遇到她的那天。
She had cursed her own foolishness before realizing her mistake. = 在意识到自己的错误之前，她已经咒骂过自己的愚蠢了。


将来时:
He will curse his enemies if they cross him again. = 如果他的敌人再次与他作对，他会诅咒他们。
She will curse the day this project started. = 她将诅咒这个项目开始的那一天。


虚拟语气:
If I were him, I wouldn't curse so much. = 如果我是他，我不会那么咒骂。
I wish I hadn't cursed at my boss. = 我真希望我没有对我的老板咒骂。


被动语态:
His actions were cursed by many. = 他的行为被许多人诅咒。
The project was cursed from the beginning. = 这个项目从一开始就受到了诅咒。


疑问句:
Does he curse often? = 他经常咒骂吗？
Why did she curse him? = 她为什么诅咒他？


否定句:
He doesn't curse in front of children. = 他不在孩子们面前咒骂。
She didn't curse at all during the meeting. = 在会议期间她根本没有咒骂。


PHRASE->
