quasi-civil:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈsɪvəl/ | adj. |准民事的，类民事的 |  Relating to or resembling civil law or procedure, but not fully or strictly so;  having some, but not all, characteristics of civil matters.  It often refers to disputes or actions that are not purely civil in nature but share some similarities. | Example: The quasi-civil dispute arose from a breach of contract but involved aspects of criminal negligence.  该准民事纠纷源于合同违反，但涉及刑事过失方面。 | 近义词:  semilegal, quasi-judicial | 反义词: purely civil, strictly criminal
