obeisances:/əˈbeɪs(ə)nsɪz/ | n. | 敬礼；鞠躬；(表示敬意的) 行礼 |  A formal act of reverence or respect, typically a bow or curtsy.  The word often refers to multiple acts of obeisance. | 例句：The courtiers performed their obeisances before the king.  朝臣们在国王面前行礼。
The dancers ended their performance with a series of elegant obeisances. 舞蹈演员以一系列优雅的鞠躬结束了他们的表演。| 近义词：bows, curtsies, prostrations, homages | 反义词：insults, affronts, discourtesies | 名词

词源:
From Old French *obeissance, from Latin *obēdientia, from obēdīre "to obey," from ob "towards" + audīre "to hear."  The shift in meaning from obedience to a physical act of reverence reflects a change in social customs and the way respect was shown.
