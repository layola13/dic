paramorph:/ˈpærəˌmɔrf/ | n. |异形体，变形体 |A paramorph is a mineral that has the same chemical composition as another mineral but a different crystal structure.  This difference in structure leads to different physical properties. For example, graphite and diamond are paramorphs because they both consist entirely of carbon, but they have different crystal structures and vastly different properties. |The diamond and graphite are paramorphs of carbon.  金刚石和石墨是碳的异形体。|近义词：polymorph, allotrope |反义词：isomorph
