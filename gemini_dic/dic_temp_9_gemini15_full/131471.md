WORD ORIGIN->
The word "noncohesive" is formed by adding the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") to the adjective "cohesive."  "Cohesive" comes from the verb "cohere," which originates from the Latin word "cohaerēre," meaning "to stick together," "to cling together."  The prefix "co-" is from the Latin "com-," meaning "together," and "-here" relates to the verb "haerere," meaning "to stick" or "to adhere."
