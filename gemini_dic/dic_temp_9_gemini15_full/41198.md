coquet:/kəˈkɛt/| n. v.| 卖弄风情的女子；(尤指女子) 卖弄风情，矫揉造作 | A coquet is a woman who flirts a lot, often playfully and charmingly.  The verb "to coquet" means to behave in a flirtatious manner. |例句：She was a notorious coquet, always attracting attention with her playful glances. 她是一个臭名昭著的卖弄风情的女子，总是用她俏皮的眼神吸引人们的注意。  He coquetted with the idea of starting his own business. 他玩弄着创业的想法。(暗指对创业的兴趣，但并不认真) |近义词：flirt, tease, vamp |反义词：prude, modest

词源:

The word "coquet" comes from the French word "coquette," meaning "a flirtatious woman."  The French word ultimately derives from the Old French word "coquer," meaning "to strut" or "to show off," which is related to the Latin word "caucārius," meaning "a proud person."
