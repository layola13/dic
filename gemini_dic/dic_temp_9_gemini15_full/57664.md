drawdowns:/ˈdrɔːdaʊnz/| n.|回撤，缩减，撤资|A drawdown refers to a decline in the value of an asset or portfolio from its peak to a trough.  It's often used in finance to describe the decrease in the value of an investment.  |例句：The recent market downturn caused significant drawdowns in many investors' portfolios. 近期市场低迷导致许多投资者的投资组合出现大幅回撤。
The fund experienced a 15% drawdown during the crisis. 该基金在危机期间经历了15%的回撤。|近义词：decline, decrease, drop, slump|反义词：increase, growth, upswing|名词
