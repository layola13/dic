overassured:/ˌoʊvərəˈʃʊrd/ | adj. | 过分自信的，自负的 | excessively confident or self-assured; presumptuous.  |  He was overassured about his chances of winning the election. 他对赢得选举过于自信。  | 近义词：cocky, arrogant, presumptuous, self-important | 反义词：humble, modest, diffident, unsure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is overassured about his abilities. = 他对自己的能力过于自信。
She is overassured in her judgment. = 她对自己的判断过于自信。


一般过去时:
He was overassured in his predictions. = 他对自己的预测过于自信。
She was overassured during the interview. = 她在面试中过于自信。


现在完成时:
He has been overassured since his promotion. = 自从升职以来，他一直过于自信。
She has become overassured after winning the competition. = 赢得比赛后，她变得过于自信。


过去完成时:
He had been overassured for years before his downfall. = 在他垮台之前，他已经自负了很多年。
She had been overassured, and that's why she failed. = 她一直过于自信，所以她失败了。


将来时:
He will be overassured if he wins the lottery. = 如果他赢得彩票，他会过于自信。
She will likely be overassured after completing her PhD. = 完成博士学位后，她可能会过于自信。


虚拟语气:
If he were less overassured, he might be more successful. = 如果他不那么自负，他可能会更成功。
I wish she weren't so overassured; it's off-putting. = 我希望她不要那么自负；这让人反感。


被动语态:
His behavior was seen as overassured by many. = 许多人认为他的行为过于自信。
Her claims were considered overassured and unfounded. = 她的说法被认为是过于自信且毫无根据的。


疑问句:

否定句:
He wasn't overassured, just confident. = 他不是过于自信，只是自信。
She wasn't overassured; she was simply stating facts. = 她并没有过于自信；她只是陈述事实。
