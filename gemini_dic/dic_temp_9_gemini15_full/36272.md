cohabited:/koʊˈhæbɪtɪd/ | v. | 同居 | To live together and have a sexual relationship, without being married.  | Example: They cohabited for five years before getting married. 他们同居五年后结婚了。 | 近义词: lived together, shacked up | 反义词: lived separately, lived apart


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They cohabit. = 他们同居。
We cohabit peacefully. = 我们和平地同居。

一般过去时:
They cohabited for several years. = 他们同居了好几年。
We cohabited before marriage. = 我们婚前同居。

现在完成时:
They have cohabited since college. = 他们从大学时代就开始同居了。
We have cohabited for five years. = 我们同居五年了。

过去完成时:
They had cohabited for two years before they decided to marry. = 他们同居了两年后才决定结婚。
We had cohabited briefly before breaking up. = 我们短暂地同居过然后分手了。

将来时:
They will cohabit after graduation. = 他们毕业后将会同居。
We will cohabit until we can afford a house. = 我们会一直同居直到我们买得起房子。

虚拟语气:
If they cohabited, they would save money on rent. = 如果他们同居，他们就能省下房租钱。
I wish we had cohabited sooner. = 我真希望我们早点同居。

被动语态:  (被动语态在此词语中不常用)
The apartment was cohabited in by three students. = 这套公寓住着三个学生（同居）。
This house has never been cohabited in. = 这所房子从未有人同居过。

疑问句:
Do they cohabit? = 他们同居吗？
Have they ever cohabited before? = 他们以前同居过吗？

否定句:
They do not cohabit. = 他们没有同居。
They have never cohabited. = 他们从未同居过。
