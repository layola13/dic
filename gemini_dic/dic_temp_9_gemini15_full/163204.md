precomplications:/ˌpriːkɒmplɪˈkeɪʃənz/| n. |并发症，预先存在的并发症|Complications that exist before the onset of a disease or condition.  They can worsen the prognosis and increase the severity of the primary condition. |例句：The patient's precomplications, including diabetes and heart disease, significantly increased the risk of post-surgical complications.|近义词：preexisting conditions, prior complications|反义词：postcomplications
