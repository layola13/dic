pseudo-instruction:/ˈsuːdoʊɪnˈstrʌkʃən/| n. |伪指令|A pseudo-instruction is an instruction in an assembly language that is not directly executed by the computer's central processing unit (CPU) but is translated into one or more actual machine instructions by the assembler.|Example:  The assembly language instruction `EQU` (equate) is a pseudo-instruction; it doesn't correspond to a machine instruction but directs the assembler to assign a value to a symbol.|近义词：Assembler directive, macro|反义词：Machine instruction


USAGE->
无。伪指令本身不参与运行时语法分析，其作用发生在汇编阶段。


PHRASE->
无。伪指令是单个指令，不形成短语。
