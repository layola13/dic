plaudit:/ˈplɔːdɪt/| n. |赞扬；喝彩；夸奖 | a demonstration of approval; enthusiastic praise or applause.  |The actress received a plaudit for her performance.  这位女演员因其表演而受到赞扬。|praise, commendation, applause, acclaim |criticism, condemnation, disapproval, censure |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The audience gives the singer a loud plaudit. = 观众给歌手热烈的喝彩。
He receives many plaudits for his work. = 他因他的工作而受到许多赞扬。


一般过去时:
The speaker received a plaudit for his speech. = 演讲者因其演讲而受到赞扬。
The athlete's performance earned him a well-deserved plaudit. = 运动员的出色表现为他赢得了当之无愧的赞扬。


现在完成时:
The author has received numerous plaudits for her latest novel. = 这名作者因其最新小说而获得了无数赞扬。
The film has garnered considerable plaudits from critics. = 这部电影获得了评论家的大量好评。


过去完成时:
By the end of the concert, the band had received a thunderous plaudit. = 音乐会结束时，乐队获得了雷鸣般的喝彩。
The chef had already received several plaudits before the awards ceremony. = 在颁奖典礼之前，这位厨师已经获得了几项赞扬。


将来时:
The new product will undoubtedly receive many plaudits. = 新产品无疑会获得许多赞扬。
His groundbreaking research will earn him considerable plaudits in the scientific community. = 他突破性的研究将在科学界为他赢得相当多的赞扬。


虚拟语气:
If he had finished the project, he would have received a deserved plaudit. = 如果他完成了项目，他将获得应得的赞扬。
I wish my work had earned me more plaudits. = 我希望我的工作能赢得更多赞扬。


被动语态:
His work was lauded with a great plaudit. = 他的作品受到了高度赞扬。
The painting was given a deserved plaudit by art critics. = 这幅画受到了艺术评论家的应得赞扬。


疑问句:
Did the performance receive a plaudit? = 这场演出获得了赞扬吗？
Will his new book earn him a plaudit? = 他的新书会为他赢得赞扬吗？


否定句:
His efforts did not receive the plaudit they deserved.  他的努力没有得到应有的赞扬。
The project failed to receive any plaudit from the client. = 该项目未能获得客户的任何赞扬。


PHRASE->
a round of plaudits = 一阵喝彩
a storm of plaudits = 一阵热烈的喝彩
earn plaudits = 获得赞扬
deserve a plaudit = 值得赞扬
win plaudits = 获得赞扬


词源:
From Late Latin *plausum, from Latin plaudere "to clap".  The 't' was added later, perhaps influenced by words like "audit".
