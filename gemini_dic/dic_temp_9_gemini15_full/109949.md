liberalize:/ˈlɪbərəlaɪz/| vt.|使自由化；放宽管制|To make something less strict or more open:  to make (laws, regulations, etc.) less strict; to remove or relax restrictions on something.|例句：The government is trying to liberalize the economy.  政府正试图使经济自由化。|例句：The country has recently liberalized its immigration policies. 这个国家最近放宽了移民政策。|近义词：deregulate, loosen, relax|反义词：restrict, tighten, control|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government liberalizes trade policies. = 政府正在放宽贸易政策。
They liberalize their views on social issues. = 他们正在放宽对社会问题的看法。


一般过去时:
The reforms liberalized the banking sector. = 改革使银行业自由化。
They liberalized the censorship laws. = 他们放宽了审查制度。


现在完成时:
The country has liberalized its media laws. = 这个国家已经放宽了媒体法律。
They have liberalized their approach to education. = 他们已经放宽了教育方法。


过去完成时:
They had liberalized the market before the crisis hit. = 危机爆发前，他们已经使市场自由化了。
The company had liberalized its hiring practices. = 公司已经放宽了招聘制度。


将来时:
The government will liberalize the visa requirements. = 政府将放宽签证要求。
They will liberalize the import regulations. = 他们将放宽进口规定。


虚拟语气:
If they liberalized the laws, it would boost the economy. = 如果他们放宽法律，将会促进经济发展。
I wish they would liberalize the immigration policies. = 我希望他们能放宽移民政策。


被动语态:
Trade policies were liberalized last year. = 贸易政策去年被放宽了。
The regulations are being liberalized gradually. = 这些规章制度正在逐步放宽。


疑问句:

Have they liberalized the internet access? = 他们是否放宽了互联网访问？


否定句:
The government did not liberalize the labor laws. = 政府没有放宽劳动法。
