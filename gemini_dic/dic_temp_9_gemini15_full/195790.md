sempiternity:/ˌsɛmpɪˈtɜːrnɪti/| n. |永恒；永久 |Eternal existence; endless duration.  The concept often implies a timeless, unchanging state.  It suggests a quality beyond mere longevity or immortality. |例句：The poem speaks of sempiternity and the endless cycle of life and death. 这首诗歌吟诵着永恒以及生命与死亡的无尽循环。

例句：Some believe in the sempiternity of the soul. 一些人相信灵魂的永恒。


近义词：eternity, perpetuity, immortality, timelessness |反义词：mortality, transience, ephemerality |词性：名词


词源:

The word "sempiternity" comes from the Late Latin word *sempiternitas*, which is a combination of:

* **semper:**  Always, ever, perpetually (from Proto-Italic *semper, ultimately related to the Sanskrit word *sā́mprat*, meaning "together").
* **aeternitas:** Eternity (from Latin *aeternus*, meaning "eternal").

Therefore, *sempiternitas* literally means "always eternity" or "eternal always," emphasizing the continuous and unending nature of the concept.  The English word "sempiternity" directly derives from this Late Latin form.
