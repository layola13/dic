inaffability:/ˌɪnæfæˈbɪləti/| adj.|不友善，冷漠，难以亲近|  Inaffability refers to the quality or state of being unsociable, unfriendly, or not inclined to talk; a lack of affability. It suggests a coldness or reserve that makes it difficult to connect with someone.  | Example:  His inaffability made it difficult to get to know him.  他的冷漠使人难以了解他。

The inaffability of the professor was legendary amongst his students. 教授的冷漠在学生中是出了名的。|近义词：unfriendliness, coldness, aloofness, standoffishness, unsociableness|反义词：affability, friendliness, sociability, warmth, approachability|词性：形容词

词源:
The word "inaffability" comes from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") and the word "affability."  "Affability" derives from the Latin word "affabilis," which means "easy to speak to" or "approachable."  Therefore, "inaffability" literally means "not easy to speak to" or "unapproachable."
