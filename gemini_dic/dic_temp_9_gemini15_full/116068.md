martingale:/ˈmɑːrɪŋɡeɪl/| n.|马丁格尔；套索；（赌博中的）加倍下注法|A martingale is a type of harness for a horse, consisting of straps that run from the horse's chest to the bit. It is used to control the horse's head and neck.  In gambling, a martingale is a betting system where you double your bet after every loss, hoping to win back your losses and make a profit.  It can also refer to a type of statistical process.|例句：The rider used a martingale to control his horse during the jump.  骑手在跳跃时用马丁格尔控制他的马。

The gambler employed a martingale system, doubling his bet after each loss. 赌徒采用了马丁格尔系统，每次输后都加倍赌注。


近义词： (for harness) bridle, reins; (for betting system) doubling-up system |反义词： (no direct antonyms exist for either meaning)|词性:名词
