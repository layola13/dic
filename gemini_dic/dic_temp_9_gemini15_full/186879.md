rinky:/ˈrɪŋki/| adj.|破旧的；劣质的；不靠谱的|Describes something that is of poor quality, flimsy, or unreliable; often implies a slightly comical or quaint aspect.  It suggests something is slightly shabby or run-down, but not necessarily broken.|例句：The rinky-dink car sputtered and died halfway up the hill. 这辆破旧不堪的车在半山腰就熄火了。 The rinky concert hall had peeling paint and uncomfortable seats.  这个破旧的音乐厅油漆剥落，座位不舒服。|近义词：shabby, ramshackle, dilapidated, flimsy, rickety|反义词：sturdy, robust, reliable, high-quality

词源:

The origin of "rinky" is uncertain, but it's likely an intensifying word with onomatopoeic qualities, echoing a sound associated with something flimsy or shaky.  The added "-dink" further amplifies this effect.  The word is informal and primarily used in North American English.
