paralleled:/ˈpærəleld/| v. | 平行的；比得上；与…相似 | To be equal or similar to something; to be compared to something.  It can also mean to run parallel to something. |例句：Her success was paralleled by her sister's achievements.  她的成功与她姐姐的成就旗鼓相当。

例句：The two roads paralleled each other for miles. 这两条路平行延伸了好几英里。


近义词：matched, equaled, corresponded, resembled

反义词：differed, contrasted


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The lines parallel each other. = 这几条线互相平行。
His talent is paralleled by his hard work. = 他的天赋与他的勤奋相媲美。

一般过去时:
The two events paralleled each other in many ways. = 这两件事在很多方面都很相似。
His career was paralleled by a steady rise in his social standing. = 他的职业生涯伴随着他社会地位的稳步提升。


现在完成时:
Nothing has paralleled the impact of the internet. = 没有什么能与互联网的影响相比。
Their success has been paralleled by increased investment. = 他们的成功伴随着投资的增加。


过去完成时:
The earlier discoveries had been paralleled by similar findings in other countries. = 早期发现与其他国家的类似发现相呼应。
Little had paralleled the sheer scale of the disaster. = 几乎没有什么能与这场灾难的巨大规模相提并论。


将来时:
Future developments will likely parallel those of the last decade. = 未来的发展很可能与过去十年相似。
This trend will be paralleled by a similar one in other industries. = 这一趋势将在其他行业中出现类似的趋势。


虚拟语气:
If his efforts were paralleled by others, we could achieve more. = 如果他的努力得到其他人的配合，我们就能取得更大的成就。
I wish my skills were paralleled by my experience. = 我希望我的技能能与我的经验相匹配。


被动语态:
His achievements were paralleled by few others. = 他的成就鲜有人能比。
The study's findings were paralleled by those of a previous research. = 这项研究的结果与之前的研究结果相符。


疑问句:
Can his achievements be paralleled? = 他的成就能够与之相比吗？
Are the two approaches paralleled in their methodology? = 这两种方法在方法论上是否相似？


否定句:
His actions were not paralleled by his words. = 他的行动与他的话语并不相符。
The results did not parallel the predictions. = 结果与预测不符。


PHRASE->
paralleled by = 与…相提并论
paralleled in = 在…方面相似
paralleled with = 与…平行
closely paralleled = 非常相似
roughly paralleled = 大致相似


词源:
The word "paralleled" comes from the word "parallel," which itself originates from the Greek words "para" (beside) and "allēlos" (one another).  Therefore, the original meaning referred to lines running side-by-side.  The extended meaning of being comparable or similar developed later.
