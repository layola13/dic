goliath:/ɡəˈlaɪəθ/| n. |歌利亚，巨人；庞然大物 | A giant, especially the Philistine champion slain by David.  The word is often used figuratively to refer to something of immense size or power. |例句：David slew Goliath with a slingshot. 大卫用弹弓打死了歌利亚。

例句：The new skyscraper is a veritable Goliath among the surrounding buildings. 这座新摩天大楼在周围的建筑物中宛如一座名副其实的巨人。


近义词：giant, giantess, behemoth, colossus


反义词：dwarf, midget, pygmy


词源:  The name comes from the Bible, specifically the story of David and Goliath in 1 Samuel 17.  The name is of Hebrew origin and its meaning is uncertain, though some scholars suggest it might relate to "exile" or "overflowing."  Its use has expanded beyond its biblical context to describe anything exceptionally large or powerful.
