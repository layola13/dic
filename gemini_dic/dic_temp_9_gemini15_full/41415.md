corner:/ˈkɔːrnər/| n. v.|角落，拐角；困境；市场份额；逼迫；使陷入困境 |A place where two lines or surfaces meet to form an angle.  A position or situation that is difficult or unpleasant.|例句：The cat sat in the corner of the room. 猫坐在房间的角落里。 He backed himself into a corner with his lies. 他因谎言而使自己陷入困境。 |近义词：angle, edge, predicament |反义词：center, middle


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The store is on the corner. = 商店在街角。
We always meet at the corner cafe. = 我们总是在街角的咖啡馆见面。


一般过去时:
He turned the corner and saw his house. = 他拐过弯，看到了他的房子。
They painted the corner of the room. = 他们粉刷了房间的角落。


现在完成时:
I've turned the corner in my recovery. = 我的康复已取得了进展。
They have cornered the market on organic coffee. = 他们垄断了有机咖啡市场。


过去完成时:
She had already rounded the corner before he saw her. = 他看到她之前，她已经拐过弯了。
They had cornered the suspect before the police arrived. = 警察到达之前，他们已经控制住了嫌疑人。


将来时:
We will meet at the corner tomorrow. = 我们明天将在街角见面。
They will corner the market in the coming years. = 他们将在未来几年垄断市场。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would avoid that corner. = 如果我是你，我会避开那个角落。
If they had cornered the market sooner, they would have made more profit. = 如果他们早点垄断市场，他们就会赚更多钱。


被动语态:
The room was painted in the corner. = 房间的角落被粉刷了。
The market was cornered by a large corporation. = 市场被一家大公司垄断了。


疑问句:
Is the library on the corner? = 图书馆在街角吗？
Have they cornered the suspect yet? = 他们已经控制住嫌疑犯了吗？


否定句:
The house isn't on the corner. = 房子不在街角。
They haven't cornered the market yet. = 他们还没有垄断市场。
