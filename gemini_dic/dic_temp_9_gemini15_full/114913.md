mandate: /ˈmænˌdeɪt/ | n. | 命令，指令；授权；委托 |  A mandate is an official order or commission to do something. It can also refer to the authority granted to someone to do something, often by election.  |例句：The government has a mandate to reduce taxes. 政府有减税的授权。The election gave the party a clear mandate to reform the healthcare system.  选举给了该党改革医疗体系的明确授权。|近义词：order, command, authorization, commission |反义词：prohibition, ban, veto| 名词
