deictically:/daɪˈɪktɪkli/ | adv. | 指示地，指示性地 |  Deictically refers to the way language points to or indicates something in the context of its use. It involves words or phrases whose meaning depends on the context of their utterance, such as pronouns ("I," "you," "here," "now"), demonstratives ("this," "that," "these," "those"), and adverbs of place and time.  |  The speaker pointed deictically to the chair, saying, "Sit there."  | 近义词：indicatively, demonstratively | 反义词：abstractly, generally
