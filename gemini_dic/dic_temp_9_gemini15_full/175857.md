rat-a-tat-tat: /ˌræt əˈtæt ˌtæt/ | int. | 叩叩叩（连续敲击声） |  Onomatopoeia representing a rapid series of sharp knocks or taps, typically on a door or window.  | Example:  We heard a rat-a-tat-tat at the door. 我们听到一阵急促的敲门声。 | 近义词：knock-knock-knock, tap-tap-tap | 反义词:  (无直接反义词，可考虑与表示安静或无声的词相对)  | 词性:  拟声词


USAGE->
This word is an onomatopoeia and doesn't have typical grammatical tenses like verbs.  It primarily functions as an interjection.  Therefore, traditional tense analysis isn't applicable.  The usage is descriptive.


PHRASE->
There aren't common phrases directly using "rat-a-tat-tat," as it's primarily used to describe a sound.  However, it can be part of a descriptive phrase:

Example 1:  The rat-a-tat-tat of the rain on the window kept me awake. 雨点敲打窗户的声音“叩叩叩”让我睡不着。
Example 2: A rat-a-tat-tat signaled the arrival of the postman.  “叩叩叩”的声音预示着邮递员到了。
