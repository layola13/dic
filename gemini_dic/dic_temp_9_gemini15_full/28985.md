cartel:/kɑːrˈtel/ | n. | 卡特尔；垄断集团 | A cartel is an association of manufacturers or suppliers with the purpose of maintaining prices at a high level and restricting competition.  Cartels often operate secretly and illegally to control supply and prices of a product or service.  They are typically found in industries with limited competition. | Example: The oil cartel manipulated global oil prices for years.  这个石油卡特尔多年来操纵着全球石油价格。 | 近义词：trust, syndicate, combine, monopoly | 反义词：competition, free market
