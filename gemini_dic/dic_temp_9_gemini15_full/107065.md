lacwork:/ˈlækˌwɜːrk/ | n. | 漆器；漆器工艺 | Lacquerware is a type of decorative artwork made by applying layers of lacquer to a base material, often wood, and then decorating it with various techniques.  The process can involve intricate designs and multiple layers of lacquer, resulting in highly polished and durable pieces. | Example: The museum showcased a stunning collection of Japanese lacwork, each piece a masterpiece of artistry.  |近义词：lacquerware,  varnishing |反义词：None (lack of a direct antonym)
