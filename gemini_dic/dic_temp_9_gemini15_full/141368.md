operativeness:/ˌɒpərəˈtɪvnəs/| /ˌɑːpəˈreɪtɪvnəs/| n. |有效性；可操作性；运作能力|The state or quality of being operative; the ability to function or work effectively.  It refers to the capacity of something to be put into effect or to operate successfully. This could apply to a system, a plan, a machine, or a law. |例句：The operativeness of the new software is being tested.  新的软件的有效性正在测试中。

The operativeness of the emergency plan was crucial during the crisis.  在危机期间，应急计划的有效性至关重要。

近义词：effectiveness, efficiency, functionality, efficacy |反义词：ineffectiveness, inoperability, malfunction


词源:  Derived from the adjective "operative," which comes from the verb "operate" (Old French *operer, from Latin operari "to work," from opus "work").  The suffix "-ness" adds the quality or state of being.
