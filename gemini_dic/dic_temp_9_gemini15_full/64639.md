erythrocyte:/ɪˈrɪθrəsaɪt/| n. |红细胞|A erythrocyte is a red blood cell that contains hemoglobin and carries oxygen throughout the body.  Erythrocytes are the most common type of blood cell and are responsible for giving blood its red color.|Example:  The number of erythrocytes in the blood can indicate health problems.|近义词：red blood cell |反义词：无
