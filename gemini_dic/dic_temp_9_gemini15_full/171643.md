pulsars:/ˈpʌlsɑːrz/ | n. |脉冲星|A pulsar is a highly magnetized, rotating neutron star that emits beams of electromagnetic radiation out of its magnetic poles.  These beams sweep across the sky like a lighthouse beam, and are only detected when they point towards Earth, resulting in the characteristic pulsed signal. Pulsars are formed from the remnants of massive stars that have undergone supernova explosions. |Example:  Scientists have discovered a new millisecond pulsar in the Milky Way galaxy. 科学家们在银河系中发现了一颗新的毫秒脉冲星。|The Crab Pulsar is a well-known example. 蟹状脉冲星是一个著名的例子。|近义词：neutron star (though not all neutron stars are pulsars) 中子星（但并非所有中子星都是脉冲星）|反义词：无
