militarism:/ˈmɪlɪtərɪzəm/| n. |军国主义；尚武精神|The belief or desire of a government or people that a country should maintain a strong military capability and be prepared to use it aggressively to defend or promote national interests.  It often involves the extensive military preparedness and the glorification of military power.|例句：The rise of militarism in the early 20th century contributed to the outbreak of World War I.  20世纪初军国主义的兴起导致了第一次世界大战的爆发。

例句：His views on militarism are extreme and dangerous. 他关于军国主义的观点极端且危险。


近义词：jingoism, bellicosity, warmongering|反义词：pacifism, antimilitarism


词源:  The word "militarism" comes from the French word "militarisme," which itself derives from the Latin word "militaris," meaning "military."  The suffix "-ism" denotes a doctrine, system, or practice.
