paralyse: /ˈpærəlaɪz/ | vt. |使瘫痪；使麻痹；使无力；使停顿 | To cause (a person, animal, or part of the body) to become paralyzed; to make (something) ineffective or unable to function. |例句：The accident paralysed him from the waist down. 事故使他从腰部以下瘫痪。

The news paralysed her with fear.  这个消息使她吓得动弹不得。


近义词：cripple, incapacitate, disable, immobilize | 反义词：activate, empower, enable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The cold weather paralyses the plant's growth.  寒冷的天气使植物生长停滞。
Fear paralyses many people in crisis situations. 恐惧在危机情况下使许多人瘫痪。


一般过去时:
The strike paralysed the city's transportation system. 罢工使该市的交通系统瘫痪。
The sudden news paralysed her with shock.  突如其来的消息让她震惊得说不出话来。


现在完成时:
The government's policies have paralysed the economy. 政府的政策已经使经济瘫痪。
Years of conflict have paralysed the country's development. 多年的冲突已经使这个国家的进步停滞不前。


过去完成时:
The previous administration had paralysed efforts to reform the healthcare system.  前任政府已经使医疗改革的努力停滞不前。
Heavy snow had paralysed the entire region before the rescue team arrived. 在救援队到达之前，大雪已经使整个地区瘫痪。


将来时:
A prolonged power outage will paralyse the city. 长时间的停电将使城市瘫痪。
The upcoming protests could paralyse the government's operations.  即将到来的抗议活动可能会使政府的运作瘫痪。


虚拟语气:
If the virus had paralysed the communication systems, the rescue would have been even more difficult. 如果病毒使通信系统瘫痪，救援将会更加困难。
I wish the fear hadn't paralysed me during the interview.  我希望面试时恐惧没有使我瘫痪。


被动语态:
The city was paralysed by the blizzard. 城市被暴风雪瘫痪了。
The negotiations were paralysed by disagreements.  谈判因分歧而陷入僵局。


疑问句:
Will the strike paralyse the whole country?  罢工会使整个国家瘫痪吗？
Has the heavy rain paralysed transportation in the area?  大雨是否使该地区的交通瘫痪？


否定句:
The cold weather didn't paralyse the plants completely.  寒冷的天气并没有完全使植物瘫痪。
The news, thankfully, didn't paralyse her completely.  谢天谢地，这个消息并没有让她完全瘫痪。

词源:
From Late Latin *paralys-, from Greek παράλυσις (parálysis), from παράλυτος (parálytos) "struck with paralysis," from παρά (pará) "beside" + λύειν (lýein) "to loosen."  Essentially, it means "to loosen beside," referring to the loosening or loss of control over a limb or function.
