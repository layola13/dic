radicalism:/ˈrædɪkəlɪzəm/ | n. | 激进主义；激进；极端主义 | The belief in or practice of radical social or political change.  It often involves challenging established norms and institutions.  The extent to which radicalism is considered positive or negative depends heavily on context and perspective. Some consider it essential for progress, while others view it as disruptive and dangerous. | Example: The student movement of the 1960s was characterized by its radicalism.  二十世纪六十年代的学生运动以其激进主义为特征。 | 近义词：extremism, revolutionism, progressivism | 反义词：conservatism, traditionalism, moderation
