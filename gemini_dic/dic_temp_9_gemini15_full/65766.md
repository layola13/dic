/ˈɛvriˌbɑːdiz/ | pron. | 每个人，大家 | Everybody loves a winner.  每个人都喜欢赢家。|近义词：everyone, all, each person | 反义词：no one, nobody


Everybody refers to all the people in a particular group or place.  It is a pronoun and functions grammatically like a singular noun, even though it refers to multiple individuals.  It's often used informally.


例句：

Everybody is invited to the party.  = 大家都被邀请参加派对。

Everybody knows that smoking is harmful. = 每个人都知道吸烟有害健康。


词源:

The word is a contraction of "every body," where "every" means "each and all" and "body" is a shortened form of "person" or "people".  The combination signifies the entirety of a group.


用法说明：

Everybody is usually followed by a singular verb.  This is a grammatical convention, even though it refers to multiple people.


例句：

Everybody agrees with the proposal.  = 大家都同意这个提议。

Everybody is responsible for their own actions. = 每个人都要为自己的行为负责。


There is no specific tense conjugation for "everybody" as it is a pronoun,  but the verb it governs will conjugate according to the tense of the sentence.

一般现在时:
Everybody goes to the park every Sunday. = 每周日每个人都去公园。
Everybody needs love and support. = 每个人都需要爱和支持。

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:
Everybody had left before the storm arrived. = 在暴风雨来临之前，大家都离开了。
Everybody had eaten dinner by eight o'clock. = 八点钟之前，大家都吃过晚饭了。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:
