词源:
The word "candy" has a complex etymology, ultimately deriving from the Arabic word  "qandi," meaning "candied," referring to crystallized sugar.  This term entered various European languages, evolving into the modern English "candy."  The evolution involved several intermediary forms and variations in different languages before settling into its current form.
