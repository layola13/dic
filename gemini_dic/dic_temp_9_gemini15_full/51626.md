developmentalist:/dɪˌvɛləpˈmɛntəlɪst/ | n. |发展论者，发展心理学家 | A developmentalist is a person who studies human development, typically focusing on the psychological, cognitive, social, and emotional aspects of growth and change across the lifespan.  They might research topics such as child development, adolescent development, or adult development.  Their work often informs educational practices, parenting strategies, and social policies. | Example: The developmentalist presented compelling evidence that early childhood experiences significantly impact later life outcomes.  发展论者提出了令人信服的证据，证明早年经历会显著影响后来的生活结果。|近义词：developmental psychologist, child psychologist, lifespan researcher |反义词：None directly applicable, but could be contrasted with someone who focuses solely on a specific stage of life or a particular aspect of development.
