preexciting: /ˌpriːɪkˈsaɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 预先兴奋的，提前兴奋的 |  Describing a feeling of excitement that occurs before an anticipated event.  It implies anticipation and a heightened state of emotional readiness.  This word is relatively uncommon and often considered informal. | 例句：The preexciting buzz before the concert was palpable. = 音乐会前的兴奋气氛是显而易见的。

The preexciting feeling of anticipation was almost unbearable. =  难以忍受的期待和兴奋感。

近义词：anticipatory, expectant, thrilled (in anticipation) | 反义词：calm, apathetic, indifferent | 词性:形容词
