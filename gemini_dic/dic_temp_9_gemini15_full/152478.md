payably:/ˈpeɪəbli/| adj.|应付的，应付账款的 |  Relating to amounts owed by a business;  due to be paid.  | Example:  The company's payables increased significantly last quarter.  这家公司的应付账款上个季度大幅增加。The payables are due next week.  应付账款将于下周到期。|近义词：due, owing, outstanding | 反义词：receivable, payable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company's payables are high this month. = 这个月公司的应付账款很高。
Our payables are carefully monitored. = 我们的应付账款受到仔细监控。


一般过去时:
Their payables were lower last year. = 他们去年的应付账款较低。
Payables were a major concern for the board. = 应付账款是董事会的主要担忧。


现在完成时:
Payables have increased substantially. = 应付账款已大幅增加。
We have cleared most of our payables. = 我们已经结清了大部分应付账款。


过去完成时:
The company had paid off its payables before the audit. = 公司在审计之前已经还清了应付账款。
They had anticipated higher payables. = 他们预料到应付账款会更高。


将来时:
Our payables will likely increase next month. = 我们的应付账款下个月可能会增加。
We will pay our payables on time. = 我们将按时支付应付账款。


虚拟语气:

I wish our payables were not so high. = 我希望我们的应付账款不要这么高。
