delirious:/dɪˈlɪriəs/| adj.| 精神错乱的；妄想的；兴奋的；极度兴奋的 |Experiencing a state of extreme mental confusion and excitement, often accompanied by hallucinations.  Characterized by incoherent speech, disorientation, and an inability to think clearly.|例句：The fever left him delirious and talking nonsense.  发烧使他神志不清，胡言乱语。


例句：She became delirious with joy after hearing the good news. 她听到好消息后欣喜若狂。


近义词：raving, frenzied, manic, deranged, insane


反义词：lucid, rational, sane, composed, calm

词源:
From Middle French *delirer, from Latin delirare "to go out of the furrow," "to be mad," from de- "off, away from" + lira "furrow."  The original sense referred to a farmer's plow going off course, metaphorically extended to a mind straying from reason.
