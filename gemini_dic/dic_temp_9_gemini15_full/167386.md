preunderstand:/ˌpriːˌʌndərˈstænd/| v.|预先理解，预先明白|To understand something before it is fully explained or experienced.  It implies a prior knowledge or anticipation of the subject.|例句：I preunderstand the basic concepts, so the lecture was easier to follow. 我预先理解了基本概念，所以讲座更容易理解。|例句：Because of her prior experience, she could preunderstand the challenges of the project.  由于她之前的经验，她能够预先理解这个项目的挑战。|近义词：anticipate, foresee, prefigure|反义词：misunderstand, misinterpret|动词
