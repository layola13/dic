jotting:/ˈdʒɒtɪŋ/ | n. |笔记，摘要 | A brief note or sketch.  It's usually a quick record of something, not a detailed account. | Example: I made a jotting of the meeting's key points. 我记下了会议要点。  |  近义词：note, memorandum, annotation | 反义词：detailed account, comprehensive report |  词性: 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is jotting down the address. = 他正在记下地址。
She jots down ideas for her novel. = 她记下她小说的一些想法。


一般过去时:
I jotted down the phone number. = 我记下了电话号码。
They jotted down important information during the lecture. = 他们在讲座期间记下了重要的信息。


现在完成时:
I have jotted down all the important details. = 我已经记下了所有重要的细节。
She has jotted down a few ideas for the project. = 她已经为这个项目记下了一些想法。


过去完成时:
I had jotted down the address before I left the house. = 我在离开家之前已经记下了地址。
She had jotted down some notes before the meeting started. = 会议开始之前，她已经记下了一些笔记。


将来时:
I will jot down the instructions. = 我会记下指示。
She will jot down the recipe before she goes to the market. = 她去市场之前会记下食谱。


虚拟语气:
If I had time, I would jot down the recipe. = 如果我有时间，我会记下食谱。
I wish I had jotted down his phone number. = 我真希望我记下了他的电话号码。


被动语态:
The key points were jotted down by the secretary. = 要点由秘书记下了。
The instructions should be jotted down carefully. = 指示应该仔细记下。


疑问句:
Did you jot down the address? = 你记下地址了吗？
Have you jotted down the main points of the discussion? = 你记下了讨论要点了吗？


否定句:
I didn't jot down the information. = 我没有记下信息。
He didn't jot down any notes during the presentation. = 演示期间他没有记任何笔记。


PHRASE->
jot down = quickly write something
jot something up = to write something quickly
make a jotting of = to make a brief note of
a jot of = a small amount of
jotting pad = a small notebook for quick notes
