eco-geographical:/ˌekoʊˌdʒiːəˈɡræfɪkəl/ | adj. |生态地理的 | Relating to the interaction between ecological and geographical factors.  This term encompasses the study of how geographical features (climate, topography, etc.) influence the distribution and characteristics of living organisms and ecosystems, and vice versa.  | Example: The eco-geographical distribution of this plant species is limited by its specific climate requirements.  这个植物物种的生态地理分布受其特定的气候条件限制。 | 近义词: biogeographical, ecological | 反义词: N/A
