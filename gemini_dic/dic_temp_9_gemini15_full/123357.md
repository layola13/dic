monologued:/ˈmɒnəlɒɡd/| v.|独白；自言自语|To deliver a monologue; to speak at length in a self-absorbed way.|例句：The actor monologued for five minutes, captivating the audience.  演员独白了五分钟，迷住了观众。
例句：She monologued about her problems for hours, ignoring everyone else. 她自言自语地抱怨她的问题几个小时，忽略了其他人。|近义词：soliloquized, declaimed|反义词：dialogued, conversed

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He monologues every morning. = 他每天早上都自言自语。
She monologues about her work. = 她喋喋不休地谈论她的工作。

一般过去时:
He monologued for an hour. = 他独白了一个小时。
She monologued about her childhood. = 她滔滔不绝地讲述她的童年。

现在完成时:
He has monologued throughout the play. = 他在整部戏中都在独白。
She has monologued about this issue repeatedly. = 她反复地就这个问题进行独白。

过去完成时:
He had monologued before the audience arrived. = 在观众到达之前，他已经独白了。
She had monologued extensively before the question and answer session. = 在问答环节之前，她已经做了大量的独白。

将来时:
He will monologue at the conference. = 他将在会议上进行独白。
She will monologue about her research findings. = 她将就她的研究成果进行独白。

虚拟语气:
If he monologued less, people would listen more. = 如果他少说一些独白，人们就会多听一些。
I wish she hadn't monologued so much. = 我真希望她不要说得那么多。

被动语态:
The play was monologued by a single actor. = 这部戏是由一个演员独白完成的。
Her ideas were monologued to the committee. = 她的想法被独白地呈现在委员会面前。


疑问句:
Did he monologue at the meeting? = 他在会议上独白了吗？
Will she monologue at the party? = 她会在派对上独白吗？

否定句:
He didn't monologue during the performance. = 他在演出期间没有独白。
She doesn't usually monologue. = 她通常不会自言自语。


PHRASE->
