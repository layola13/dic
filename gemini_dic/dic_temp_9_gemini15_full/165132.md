preindebtednesses:/ˌpriːɪnˈdɛtɪdnəsɪz/| n. |预先负债；预先欠款；先前的债务|  A preindebtedness refers to debts or obligations incurred before a specific point in time. It emphasizes the prior existence of the debt, often implying a continuing impact on the present situation.  The term is less common than "prior debts" or "existing liabilities," but clarifies that the debts existed *before* a particular event or circumstance. |The company's preindebtednesses significantly hampered its ability to secure new loans. 公司先前的债务严重阻碍了它获得新贷款的能力。
The restructuring plan failed to address the company's preindebtednesses, leading to further financial difficulties. 重组计划未能解决公司的预先负债，导致进一步的财务困难。| prior debts, existing liabilities | assets, equity |名词
