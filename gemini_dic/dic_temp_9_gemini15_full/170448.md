prudent:/ˈpruːdənt/| adj. |谨慎的；明智的；节俭的 |Showing care for the future; cautious and avoiding risks.  It suggests a wise and careful approach, prioritizing long-term benefits over immediate gratification. |例句：It's prudent to save some money for a rainy day.  = 未雨绸缪，存点钱以备不时之需。

It is prudent to consult a doctor before taking any new medication. = 在服用任何新药之前，咨询医生是谨慎的做法。|近义词：cautious, careful, wise, sensible, judicious |反义词：reckless, imprudent, rash, careless, foolish


USAGE->

一般现在时:
It is prudent to save money. = 存钱是明智的。
He is prudent in his investments. = 他在投资方面很谨慎。


一般过去时:
She was prudent in her spending. = 她花钱很节俭。
They were prudent to avoid the risk. = 他们避免风险的做法是明智的。


现在完成时:
We have been prudent with our resources. = 我们一直谨慎地使用我们的资源。
They have been prudent in their decision-making. = 他们在决策方面一直很谨慎。


过去完成时:
He had been prudent and saved enough money for retirement. = 他以前很谨慎，存够了退休金。
She had been prudent to avoid that dangerous situation. = 她很明智地避免了那种危险的情况。


将来时:
It will be prudent to wait for more information. = 等到更多信息会比较明智。
They will be prudent to proceed cautiously. = 他们谨慎行事会比较明智。


虚拟语气:
It would be prudent to seek professional advice. = 寻求专业建议是明智的。
If I were you, I would be prudent and wait. = 如果我是你，我会谨慎地等待。


被动语态:
Prudence is often advised in financial matters. = 在财务问题上，通常建议谨慎。
Caution is considered prudent in high-risk situations. = 在高风险情况下，谨慎被认为是明智的。


疑问句:
Is it prudent to invest all our savings? = 把所有积蓄都投资进去明智吗？
Wasn't it prudent to check the weather forecast? = 检查一下天气预报是不是很明智？


否定句:
It is not prudent to make hasty decisions. = 匆忙做决定是不明智的。
He was not prudent in his choice of words. = 他在措辞方面不够谨慎。

词源:

The word "prudent" comes from the Latin word "prūdens,"  meaning "foreseeing,"  "careful," or "skillful."  This, in turn, is derived from "prōvidēre," meaning "to foresee" or "to provide."  The evolution reflects the core meaning of careful planning and foresight associated with prudence.
