flamed:/fleɪmd/| v.|点燃；燃烧；激怒|to burn or be burned with flames; to become intensely angry or excited|例句：The fire flamed brightly. 火焰熊熊燃烧。The criticism flamed his anger. 批评激怒了他。|近义词：ignited, burned, infuriated|反义词：extinguished, calmed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The candle flames brightly. = 蜡烛燃烧得很明亮。
He flames with anger. = 他怒火中烧。


一般过去时:
The fire flamed fiercely last night. = 昨晚大火猛烈燃烧。
His comments flamed her temper. = 他的评论激怒了她。


现在完成时:
The sun has flamed all day. = 太阳整天都炙热地照耀着。
She has flamed with passion for her work. = 她对她的工作充满了热情。


过去完成时:
The house had flamed before the firefighters arrived. = 消防员到达之前，房子就已经着火了。
He had flamed with rage before he realized his mistake. = 他在意识到自己的错误之前就已经怒火冲天了。


将来时:
The bonfire will flame brightly tonight. = 今晚篝火将燃烧得很旺。
The situation will flame into a crisis. = 局势将会恶化为危机。


虚拟语气:
If the fire flamed higher, we would have to leave. = 如果火势更大，我们就得离开了。
If he had flamed with anger, the meeting would have ended badly. = 如果他怒火中烧，会议就会以糟糕的方式结束。


被动语态:
The wood was flamed by the hot coals. = 木柴被烧红的煤炭点燃了。
His reputation was flamed by the scandal. = 他的名誉因丑闻而受损。


疑问句:
Does the fire still flame? = 火还燃烧着吗？
Will his words flame her anger? = 他的话会激怒她吗？


否定句:
The fire didn't flame very high. = 火没有烧得很高。
His words didn't flame her temper. = 他的话没有激怒她。


PHRASE->
flame out = 突然熄灭；爆发；筋疲力尽
flame up = 突然燃烧；突然发怒
burst into flame = 突然燃烧起来
in flames = 燃烧着；着火
go up in flames = 焚毁；化为灰烬
