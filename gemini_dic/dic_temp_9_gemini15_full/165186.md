preinfluences:/ˌpriːˈɪnflʊənsɪz/ | n. | 先期影响；预先影响 | Influences that precede and shape later developments or events.  Preinfluences are factors that contribute to the formation of something before its main influences take effect.  They often lay the groundwork for future events or trends. | Example:  The artist's early childhood experiences served as powerful preinfluences on their later work.  这位艺术家的早期童年经历对其后来的作品产生了强大的先期影响。 | 近义词：antecedents, precursors, forerunners | 反义词：aftereffects, consequences, results | 名词
