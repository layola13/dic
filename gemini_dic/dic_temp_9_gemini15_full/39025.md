conflagrations:/ˌkɒnfləˈɡreɪʃənz/| n. |大火，火灾，骚乱 |A conflagration is a large destructive fire.  The term can also refer to a violent and widespread conflict. |例句：The forest fire quickly escalated into a massive conflagration. 森林大火迅速演变成一场巨大的火灾。

The city was ravaged by a conflagration that destroyed many buildings. 这座城市被一场摧毁了许多建筑物的火灾所摧毁。

近义词：fire, inferno, blaze, combustion, upheaval |反义词：calm, peace, tranquility |词性:noun

词源:
The word "conflagration" comes from the Late Latin word *conflagratio, from the verb *conflagrare, meaning "to burn together,"  combining the prefix *con- ("together") and *flagrare ("to burn").  The ultimate root is the Proto-Indo-European root *gʷleh₂- ("to burn").
