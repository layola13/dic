normalisation: /ˌnɔːrməlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |标准化；规范化；正常化| The process of making something conform to a standard or norm.  It involves reducing or eliminating variations and inconsistencies to achieve uniformity and consistency.  This process can apply to various fields, including data, databases, language, and social behavior. |例句：The normalisation of the database improved data retrieval speed. 数据库的标准化提高了数据检索速度。


The normalization of blood pressure is crucial for heart health. 血压的正常化对心脏健康至关重要。|近义词：standardization, regulation, regularization |反义词：irregularity, abnormality, chaos |名词

词源:
The word "normalization" comes from the word "norm," meaning a standard or rule.  The suffix "-ization" indicates the process of making something conform to a norm.  Its roots trace back to Latin "norma" (carpenter's square, rule, pattern).
