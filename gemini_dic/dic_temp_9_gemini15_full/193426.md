seizes:/ˈsiːzɪz/ | vt. |抓住；夺取；控制；理解；利用|To take hold of something suddenly and firmly; to take control of something by force; to understand or grasp an idea or opportunity. |例句：The police seized the stolen goods.  警察查获了赃物。

The eagle seized its prey in its talons. 老鹰用爪子抓住了猎物。


近义词：grab, snatch, capture, apprehend, grasp, commandeer | 反义词：release, relinquish, surrender


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He seizes every opportunity. 他抓住每一个机会。
She seizes the microphone and starts to speak. 她抓起麦克风开始讲话。

一般过去时:
The army seized the city.  军队占领了这座城市。
He seized the chance to escape. 他抓住了逃跑的机会。


现在完成时:
The government has seized control of the situation. 政府已经控制住了局面。
She has seized the initiative in the negotiations.  她在谈判中掌握了主动权。


过去完成时:
They had seized the building before the police arrived. 他们在警察到达之前已经占领了那座建筑。
He had seized the opportunity to speak before the meeting ended. 会议结束前他抓住了发言的机会。


将来时:
The rebels will seize power. 反叛者将夺取政权。
I will seize the moment and ask her out. 我将抓住时机约她出去。


虚拟语气:
If he seized the opportunity, he would be successful. 如果他抓住机会，他就会成功。
I wish I had seized the chance to travel. 我真希望我抓住了旅行的机会。


被动语态:
The city was seized by the rebels.  这座城市被叛军占领了。

疑问句:

否定句:
