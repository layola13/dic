prewarranting:/ˌpriːˈwɒrəntɪŋ/| v.|预先担保；预先保证；提前授权|The act of giving a guarantee or assurance in advance;  to authorize or sanction something beforehand.  This is not a common word and is often used in legal or highly formal contexts. It implies a proactive measure to ensure something will happen or be acceptable.|例句：The company prewarranted the quality of their product before its release. 公司在产品发布之前预先担保了其质量。|The board prewarranted the expenditure of funds for the project. 董事会预先批准了该项目的资金支出。|近义词：pre-approving, guaranteeing, assuring, sanctioning|反义词：disapproving, rejecting, refusing
