kinsman:/ˈkɪnzˌmæn/| n.|亲属，亲戚|A male relative; a man related by blood or marriage.|例句：He is a distant kinsman of mine. 他是我的远房亲戚。|例句：Many of his kinsmen attended the funeral. 他的许多亲属都参加了他的葬礼。|近义词：relative, relation, family member|反义词：stranger


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is my kinsman. = 他是我的亲戚。
My kinsmen live nearby. = 我的亲戚住在附近。


一般过去时:
He was a close kinsman of the king. = 他是国王的近亲。
Many kinsmen attended the ceremony. = 许多亲戚参加了仪式。


现在完成时:
I have met several of his kinsmen. = 我见过他的一些亲戚。
She has always been close to her kinsmen. = 她一直和她的亲戚很亲近。


过去完成时:
His kinsmen had already arrived before the wedding. = 婚礼前他的亲戚们已经到了。
We had never met any of his kinsmen before. = 我们以前从未见过他的任何亲戚。


将来时:
I will visit my kinsmen next week. = 我下周将拜访我的亲戚们。
Many kinsmen will gather for the holiday. = 许多亲戚将在假期聚集在一起。


虚拟语气:
If he were my kinsman, I would help him. = 如果他是我的亲戚，我会帮助他。
I wish I knew more about my kinsmen. = 我希望我能更多地了解我的亲戚们。


被动语态:

疑问句:
Are they your kinsmen? = 他们是你的亲戚吗？
Who are your closest kinsmen? = 你最亲近的亲戚是谁？


否定句:
He is not my kinsman. = 他不是我的亲戚。
They were not close kinsmen. = 他们不是近亲。
