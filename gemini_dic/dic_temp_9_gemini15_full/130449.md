nonabsolutists:/ˌnɒnˌæbsəˈluːtɪsts/| n. |非绝对主义者|Those who reject absolutism, believing that there are no absolute truths or moral principles. They tend to embrace relativism or contextualism.  |Example: Many contemporary ethicists are nonabsolutists, arguing that moral judgments depend on cultural or situational factors.|近义词: relativists, contextualists |反义词: absolutists
