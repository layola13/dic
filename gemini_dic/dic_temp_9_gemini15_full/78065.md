geochemistry:/ˌdʒioʊˈkɛmɪstri/| n. |地球化学|The science that uses the principles and methods of chemistry to explain the composition, structure, processes, and history of the Earth.  It examines the chemical composition of rocks, minerals, soils, waters, and the atmosphere, and how these components interact. |Geochemistry helps us understand the formation of planets, the evolution of life, and the environmental impact of human activities. |近义词：geological chemistry |反义词：无
