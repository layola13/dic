innovating:/ˈɪnəveɪtɪŋ/| v. |创新；革新|The act of creating new methods, ideas, or products.  It implies a process of change and improvement.|例句：The company is innovating in the field of sustainable energy. 这家公司正在可持续能源领域进行创新。|例句：They are constantly innovating to stay ahead of the competition. 他们不断创新以保持竞争优势。|近义词：inventing, pioneering, developing, creating|反义词：stagnating, maintaining the status quo, copying


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are innovating constantly. = 他们在不断创新。
The company is innovating new products. = 公司正在研发新产品。


一般过去时:
They innovated a new system. = 他们创新了一个新系统。
The engineers innovated a solution to the problem. = 工程师们创新性地解决了这个问题。


现在完成时:
The company has innovated significantly in recent years. = 近年来，该公司取得了显著的创新成果。
They have innovated a new approach to marketing. = 他们创新了一种新的市场营销方法。


过去完成时:
They had innovated several products before the merger. = 在合并之前，他们已经创新了几种产品。
The team had innovated a solution, but it was rejected. = 团队已经创新了一个解决方案，但是被否决了。


将来时:
They will innovate new technologies in the future. = 他们将来会创新新的技术。
The company will continue innovating to meet customer needs. = 公司将继续创新以满足客户需求。


虚拟语气:
If they innovated more, they would be more successful. = 如果他们能更多地创新，他们会更成功。
I wish they had innovated a better solution. = 我希望他们能想出一个更好的解决方案。


被动语态:
New methods were innovated by the team. = 新方法是由团队创新的。
A new approach to problem-solving is being innovated. = 正在创新一种新的解决问题的方法。


疑问句:
Are they innovating in the right direction? = 他们的创新方向对吗？
What are they innovating? = 他们在创新什么？


否定句:
They are not innovating enough. = 他们的创新还不够。
The company didn't innovate quickly enough to stay competitive. = 公司的创新速度不够快，无法保持竞争力。


PHRASE->
