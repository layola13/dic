hemophilia:/ˌhiːməˈfɪliə/| n. |血友病|A group of hereditary genetic disorders that impair the body's ability to control blood clotting or coagulation, which is the process that stops bleeding.  Hemophilia is characterized by prolonged or excessive bleeding after injury or surgery, or even spontaneous bleeding.  There are different types of hemophilia, the most common being hemophilia A and hemophilia B.|The patient was diagnosed with hemophilia A, requiring regular infusions of clotting factor.|近义词：bleeding disorder|反义词：none
