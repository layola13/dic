hurdle:/ˈhɜːdl/ /ˈhɜːr.dl/| n. v.|障碍；跨栏；克服|A hurdle is a problem or difficulty that must be overcome.  It can also refer to a fence or barrier in a race. |例句：The company faces many hurdles in the new market. 这家公司在新市场面临许多障碍。

The athlete cleared the hurdle with ease.  这位运动员轻松地越过了栏架。


近义词：obstacle, obstruction, barrier, impediment |反义词：ease, solution, aid


词性：名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hurdles the fence easily. = 他轻松地越过栅栏。
She faces many hurdles in her career. = 她在事业上面临许多障碍。


一般过去时:
The horse easily hurdled the fence. = 马轻松地越过栅栏。
We hurdled the many obstacles in our path. = 我们克服了我们道路上的许多障碍。


现在完成时:
She has hurdled many obstacles to get where she is today. = 她克服了许多障碍才到达今天的位置。
They have successfully hurdled the regulatory hurdles. = 他们已经成功地克服了监管障碍。


过去完成时:
He had already hurdled the biggest obstacle before the final challenge. = 在最后的挑战之前，他已经克服了最大的障碍。
They had hurdled many difficulties before achieving success. = 他们在取得成功之前克服了许多困难。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

If only I had hurdled that hurdle earlier! = 早知道我早点克服那个障碍就好了！


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->

词源:
The word "hurdle" ultimately derives from the Old English word "hyrdle," meaning a "small gate" or "wattled fence."  This evolved through Middle English forms before arriving at its modern meaning, encompassing both the physical barrier and the metaphorical obstacle.
