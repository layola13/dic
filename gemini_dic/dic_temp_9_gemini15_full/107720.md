lanyards:/ˈlænjərdz/ | n. | 绳索；系带；挂绳 | A lanyard is a cord, often made of fabric, plastic, or other material, worn around the neck or wrist to hold an ID card, keys, or other small items.  It can also refer to a short rope used in climbing or sailing. | Example:  She wore a lanyard around her neck to hold her employee ID. 她脖子上戴着一条挂绳，用来挂员工证。
