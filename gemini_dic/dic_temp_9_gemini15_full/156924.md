piledriving: /ˈpaɪldraɪvɪŋ/ | n.  |打桩；打桩工程|The act of driving piles (large posts or beams) into the ground, typically for constructing foundations.  This involves using heavy machinery to force the piles deep into the earth, providing a stable base for buildings, bridges, or other structures. |例句：The piledriving for the new bridge is expected to last several weeks. 新桥的打桩工程预计将持续数周。|近义词：pile driving, foundation construction|反义词：pile extraction, demolition
