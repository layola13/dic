promulgating: /ˈprɒmʌlɡeɪtɪŋ/ | v. |颁布；公布；宣传 |  The act of making something (especially a law or doctrine) known publicly or officially.  It emphasizes the dissemination of information, often with an authority or official capacity. | 例句：The government is promulgating new regulations on data privacy. 政府正在颁布关于数据隐私的新规章。

The university is promulgating its new research findings to the scientific community. 大学正在向科学界公布其新的研究成果。


近义词：disseminate, publish, proclaim, announce, broadcast, spread

反义词：suppress, conceal, hide, withhold


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government promulgates new laws every year. = 政府每年颁布新法律。
They are promulgating their ideas through social media. = 他们正在通过社交媒体宣传他们的想法。


一般过去时:
The king promulgated a new decree. = 国王颁布了一道新法令。
The company promulgated its new marketing strategy. = 公司公布了新的营销策略。


现在完成时:
They have promulgated several important policies this year. = 今年他们已经颁布了几项重要政策。
The organization has promulgated its mission statement to its members. = 该组织已向其成员公布了其使命宣言。


过去完成时:
Before the war, they had promulgated a peace treaty. = 在战争爆发前，他们已经颁布了一项和平条约。
The church had promulgated its doctrines for centuries. = 教会几个世纪以来一直在宣传其教义。


将来时:
The government will promulgate new environmental protection laws next year. = 政府明年将颁布新的环境保护法。
The organization will promulgate its findings in a scientific journal. = 该组织将在科学期刊上公布其研究结果。


虚拟语气:
If the government promulgated stricter rules, the situation might improve. = 如果政府颁布更严格的规定，情况可能会改善。
I wish they would promulgate clearer guidelines. = 我希望他们能公布更清晰的指导方针。


被动语态:
New regulations were promulgated by the committee. = 新规章是由委员会颁布的。
The company's new policies are being promulgated to employees. = 公司的新政策正在向员工公布。


疑问句:
When will the new regulations be promulgated? = 新规章何时颁布？
Has the court promulgated its decision yet? = 法院是否已经公布了判决？


否定句:
The government did not promulgate the new law until next year. = 政府直到明年才颁布新的法律。
They did not promulgate their findings widely enough. = 他们没有充分地公布他们的研究结果。

词源:
From Latin *promulgare, from pro- ("forth") + vulgare ("to make public").  The word's history reflects its core meaning: making something public or known.
