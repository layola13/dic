overfaithful: /ˌoʊvərˈfeɪθfəl/ | adj. | 过于忠诚的，盲目忠诚的 | Excessively or blindly faithful; characterized by excessive loyalty or devotion, often to a fault.  It implies a degree of naiveté or lack of critical judgment in one's loyalty. | 例句：His overfaithful devotion to the company led him to accept unreasonable demands.  他的过度忠诚导致他接受了不合理的要求。


The overfaithful knight followed his king into a reckless battle.  这位过于忠诚的骑士跟随国王参加了一场鲁莽的战斗。|近义词： excessively loyal, blindly devoted, unquestioningly obedient | 反义词： disloyal, treacherous, rebellious |


USAGE->
This word is an adjective, so it doesn't conjugate like a verb.  It doesn't have tenses.  However, it can be used in various sentence structures:

Affirmative Sentence:
He was overfaithful to a fault. 他过于忠诚，到了过分的程度。
Her overfaithful nature made her easily manipulated. 她过于忠诚的天性让她很容易被操纵。


Negative Sentence:
He wasn't overfaithful; he simply trusted his friend. 他并非过于忠诚；他只是信任他的朋友。
She wasn't overfaithful, she was just cautious. 她并非过于忠诚，她只是谨慎。


Interrogative Sentence:
Was he overfaithful in his support? 他在支持方面是否过于忠诚？
Is she overfaithful to a point of detriment? 她过于忠诚到了有害的程度吗？

词源:

The word "overfaithful" is a compound word formed by combining the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much," "excessively," or "beyond") with the adjective "faithful" (meaning "loyal," "true," or "trustworthy").  The word's meaning is therefore a straightforward combination of its constituent parts.  Both "over" and "faithful" have long histories in the English language with roots tracing back to Old English and beyond.
