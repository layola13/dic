semi-intelligently:/ˌsɛmiɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəntli/ | adv. | 半智能地；不太聪明地 |  Acting or functioning with only partial intelligence; showing a limited degree of intelligence.  |例句：The robot moved semi-intelligently, sometimes bumping into obstacles. 机器人行动不太灵巧，有时会撞到障碍物。

例句：The software responded semi-intelligently to complex queries, sometimes providing inaccurate information.  该软件对复杂的查询做出了不太智能的回应，有时会提供不准确的信息。


近义词：unintelligently, clumsily, awkwardly, ineptly

反义词：intelligently, cleverly, skillfully, brilliantly


词源:

* **semi-**:  A prefix meaning "half," "partly," or "partially."  Derived from Latin *semi*.

* **intelligent**: Adjective meaning "having or showing intelligence."  Derived from Latin *intelligens*, present participle of *intelligere* ("to understand").  *Intelligere* is formed from *inter* ("between") and *legere* ("to choose, select, gather").


用法说明:

This adverb describes actions or processes that show some level of intelligence but are not fully intelligent or efficient.  It suggests a lack of complete understanding or skill.  It often implies a degree of imperfection or clumsiness in the performance of a task.
