self-division:/ˌsɛlfdɪˈvɪʒən/| n. | 自我分裂；自我分割 | The process by which a single entity divides into two or more similar entities.  This term is often used in biology to describe cell division (mitosis or meiosis), but can also be used metaphorically to describe the splitting of a group, organization, or even a concept. | Example: The rapid self-division of the bacteria led to a massive infection. 快速的细菌自我分裂导致了严重的感染。The political party experienced a painful self-division over the issue of immigration.  在移民问题上，该政党经历了痛苦的自我分裂。|近义词：fission, splitting, fragmentation, bifurcation |反义词：unification, merger, consolidation |名词
