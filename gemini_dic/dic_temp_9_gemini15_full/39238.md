congratulating:/kənˈɡrædʒuleɪtɪŋ/| v. |祝贺；庆贺 |The act of expressing joy or pleasure at someone's success or good fortune.  It involves conveying warm wishes and positive feelings. |例句：He was congratulating his friend on his promotion. 他正在祝贺朋友升职。|例句：We were congratulating her on her wedding. 我们正在祝贺她的婚礼。|近义词：celebrating, felicitating |反义词：condemning, criticizing |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I am congratulating you on your success. = 我祝贺你成功。
They are congratulating the team on their victory. = 他们祝贺球队获胜。

一般过去时:
I congratulated him on his graduation. = 我祝贺他毕业。
She congratulated me on my new job. = 她祝贺我找到新工作。

现在完成时:
I have congratulated her many times on her achievements. = 我已经多次祝贺她取得的成就。
They have congratulated the winners on their performance. = 他们已经祝贺获奖者们的表现。

过去完成时:
I had already congratulated him before he even knew he won. = 在他甚至知道自己赢之前，我已经祝贺他了。
She had congratulated them on their engagement before the party. = 在派对之前，她已经祝贺他们订婚了。

将来时:
I will congratulate her when I see her. = 我见到她时会祝贺她。
We will congratulate them on their anniversary. = 我们将在他们的周年纪念日祝贺他们。

虚拟语气:
If I saw him, I would congratulate him. = 如果我看到他，我会祝贺他。
I wish I could congratulate them in person. = 我希望我能亲自祝贺他们。

被动语态:
He was congratulated by his colleagues on his achievement. = 他因其成就而受到同事们的祝贺。
The team was congratulated on their outstanding performance. = 球队因其杰出的表现而受到祝贺。

疑问句:
Are you congratulating him on his new car? = 你在祝贺他买新车吗？
Have you congratulated her yet? = 你已经祝贺她了吗？

否定句:
I didn't congratulate him on his birthday. = 我没有祝贺他的生日。
They didn't congratulate the winner. = 他们没有祝贺获胜者。


PHRASE->
congratulate someone on something = 祝贺某人某事
offer congratulations = 表示祝贺
extend congratulations = 致以祝贺
warm congratulations = 热烈的祝贺
hearty congratulations = 衷心的祝贺


词源:
The word "congratulating" comes from the Latin word "congratulor," which is a combination of "con-" (together) and "gratulor" (I rejoice).  Thus, it literally means "to rejoice with."  The "-ing" suffix indicates the present participle form, describing an ongoing action.
