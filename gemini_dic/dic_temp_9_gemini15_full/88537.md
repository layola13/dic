hit the ceiling:/hɪt ðə ˈsiːlɪŋ/| phrasal verb| 勃然大怒；怒不可遏 |To become extremely angry.  |例句：He hit the ceiling when he saw the bill. 他看到账单后勃然大怒。|例句：She hit the ceiling when she found out her son had crashed her car. 她发现儿子撞坏了她的车后，怒不可遏。|近义词：blow up, fly off the handle, go ballistic, lose it|反义词：remain calm, keep cool


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hits the ceiling when he's stressed. = 他压力大的时候就会勃然大怒。
She hits the ceiling if you're late. = 如果你迟到了，她会大发雷霆。


一般过去时:
He hit the ceiling when he saw the damage. = 他看到损坏后勃然大怒。
She hit the ceiling after she received the bad news. = 她收到坏消息后怒不可遏。


现在完成时:
He has hit the ceiling several times this week. = 他这周已经好几次大发雷霆了。
She has never hit the ceiling like that before. = 她以前从未如此生气过。


过去完成时:
He had hit the ceiling before I even started to explain. = 我还没开始解释，他就已经勃然大怒了。
She had hit the ceiling already when he finally arrived. = 等他终于到了的时候，她已经怒火中烧了。


将来时:
He will hit the ceiling if you don't finish the project on time. = 如果你不按时完成项目，他会勃然大怒。
She will probably hit the ceiling when she finds out the truth. = 当她发现真相时，她可能会勃然大怒。


虚拟语气:
If I told him the truth, he would hit the ceiling. = 如果我告诉他真相，他会勃然大怒。
I wish I hadn't told him, he would have hit the ceiling. = 我真希望我没有告诉他，他会勃然大怒的。


被动语态: (This phrase is rarely used in the passive voice)
*Not applicable*


疑问句:
Will he hit the ceiling if he finds out? = 如果他发现会勃然大怒吗？
Did she hit the ceiling when you told her? = 你告诉她的时候她勃然大怒了吗？


否定句:
He didn't hit the ceiling, he just sighed. = 他没有勃然大怒，只是叹了口气。
She didn't hit the ceiling; instead, she calmly explained the problem. = 她没有勃然大怒，而是平静地解释了问题。
