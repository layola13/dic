enantiomorphous:/ɪˌnæntiˈmɔːrfəs/ | adj. | 对映异构的；互为镜像的 |  Describing objects or structures that are mirror images of each other but cannot be superimposed.  This term is commonly used in chemistry and crystallography to describe molecules or crystals that are non-superimposable mirror images. | Example: The two enantiomorphous crystals rotated polarized light in opposite directions.  两个对映异构的晶体使偏振光向相反方向旋转。| 近义词：chiral, mirror-image | 反义词：achiral, superimposable |


USAGE->
This word is primarily used in a descriptive way and doesn't have typical verb conjugations like "hello."  The usage focuses on describing the relationship between two objects or molecules.


Example Sentences:


These two molecules are enantiomorphous, exhibiting optical activity. 这两个分子是对映异构体，表现出旋光性。

The crystals were determined to be enantiomorphous based on their X-ray diffraction patterns. 根据它们的X射线衍射图谱，确定这些晶体是互为镜像的。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly using "enantiomorphous." The term is used within specific scientific contexts.
