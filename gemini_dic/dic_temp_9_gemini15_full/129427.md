nomineeism:/nɒmɪˈniːɪzəm/ | n. |提名主义；任命主义|The practice or tendency of focusing on the selection of candidates for office rather than on policy issues or the qualifications of the candidates.  It often implies a prioritizing of the process of nomination over substantive considerations.  This can lead to situations where individuals are chosen for their political connections or fundraising abilities rather than their competence or suitability for the role. |The recent election was marred by nomineeism, with candidates chosen more for their fundraising prowess than their policy platforms.  最近的选举因提名主义而蒙上阴影，候选人的选择更看重他们的筹款能力而不是他们的政策平台。| 近义词：patronage, favoritism | 反义词：meritocracy
