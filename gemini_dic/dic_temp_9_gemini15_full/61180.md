eluded:/ɪˈluːdɪd/ | v. | 躲避；逃避；未被察觉；未被理解 | to escape from someone or something, especially by being clever or quick; to avoid someone or something | 近义词：evade, avoid, escape | 反义词：encounter, confront


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The thief eludes the police. = 小偷躲避了警察。
The answer eludes me. = 我找不到答案。


一般过去时:
The criminal eluded capture for many years. = 罪犯多年来一直逍遥法外。
The solution to the puzzle eluded us. = 我们找不到这个谜题的答案。


现在完成时:
He has eluded capture for years. = 他多年来一直逃脱追捕。
The meaning of the poem has eluded many readers. = 这首诗的意思让许多读者难以理解。


过去完成时:
The suspect had eluded the police before. = 嫌疑犯之前就躲避过警方。
The problem had eluded a solution for months. = 这个问题几个月来一直没有解决办法。


将来时:
The criminal will likely elude capture again. = 这个罪犯很可能会再次逃脱。
I doubt the answer will elude us for much longer. = 我怀疑答案不会让我们困惑太久。


虚拟语气:
If only I could elude this responsibility. = 要是我能逃避这份责任就好了。
I wish I could elude the inevitable. = 我希望我能逃避不可避免的事情。


被动语态:
The police were eluded by the cunning criminal. = 狡猾的罪犯躲过了警察。
The truth was eluded by the deceptive statements. = 真相被欺骗性的陈述所掩盖。


疑问句:
Did the suspect elude the police? = 嫌疑犯躲避了警察吗？
Will the solution elude us forever? = 我们会永远找不到答案吗？


否定句:
The criminal did not elude capture. = 罪犯没有逃脱抓捕。
The meaning of the text did not elude the professor. = 教授理解了文本的意思。
