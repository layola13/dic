laughed:/lɑːft/| /læft/ | v. |笑了，发笑|To make the sound or sounds expressing mirth, amusement, or derision.  It implies a vocal expression of amusement or joy. |例句：She laughed at the funny joke. 她听了那个笑话哈哈大笑。

例句：They laughed until their sides hurt.  他们笑得肚子都疼了。


近义词：chuckled, giggled, guffawed, snickered |反义词：cried, wept


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He laughs at every joke. = 他对每个笑话都哈哈大笑。
She laughs easily. = 她很容易笑。


一般过去时:
I laughed so hard last night. = 我昨晚笑得很厉害。
He laughed heartily at the comedian's performance. = 他对喜剧演员的表演报以热烈的笑声。


现在完成时:
I have laughed until I cried. = 我笑到哭了。
She has laughed at my jokes before. = 她以前就笑过我的笑话。


过去完成时:
I had laughed so much that I had tears in my eyes. = 我笑得太多了，眼睛都流泪了。
They had laughed themselves silly before the show started. = 在演出开始之前，他们已经笑得前仰后合了。


将来时:
We will laugh again soon. = 我们很快会再笑。
They will laugh when they hear the news. = 他们听到这个消息后会哈哈大笑。


虚拟语气:
If I had known it was so funny, I would have laughed harder. = 如果我知道这么好笑，我会笑得更厉害。
I wish I had laughed more when I was younger. = 我希望我年轻的时候能笑得更多。


被动语态:
(The joke) was laughed at by everyone. = (这个笑话)被每个人都笑了。
(His story) wasn't laughed at. = (他的故事)没有人笑。


疑问句:
Did you laugh at the movie? = 你看电影的时候笑了吗？
Why are you laughing? = 你为什么笑？


否定句:
I didn't laugh at his joke. = 我没有笑他的笑话。
They didn't laugh at all. = 他们一点也没笑。


PHRASE->
laugh at =  嘲笑
laugh off =  一笑置之
laugh out loud =  大声笑
burst out laughing =  突然大笑起来
have a good laugh =  开怀大笑
