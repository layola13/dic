jehad:/dʒɪˈhɑːd/ | n. |圣战；努力；奋斗 |  A jehad is a religious duty of Muslims to maintain their religion.  It can refer to a holy war against infidels, or a personal struggle against sin. The term is often misused and misunderstood in the West.  | Example: Some groups interpret jehad as a call to violence, while others view it as a spiritual struggle for self-improvement.  一些团体将圣战解释为暴力号召，而另一些团体则将其视为自我完善的精神斗争。 |近义词：struggle, striving, crusade |反义词：peace, complacency

lesser jehad (外圣战):  The external military struggle against enemies of Islam. 对抗伊斯兰教敌人的外部军事斗争。
a jehad for social justice：为社会正义而战的斗争
a jehad against ignorance：反对无知的斗争
