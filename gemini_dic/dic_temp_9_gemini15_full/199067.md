signifying:/ˈsɪɡnɪfaɪɪŋ/| v.|具有…意义；象征；表示|To be an indication of; to represent; to mean.|The dark clouds signifying an approaching storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨即将来临。|The red light signifies danger. 红灯表示危险。|indicate, represent, denote, symbolize|unimportant, meaningless|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The symbol signifies peace. = 这个符号象征和平。
His silence signifies his disapproval. = 他的沉默表示他的反对。


一般过去时:
The painting signified a new era in art. = 这幅画标志着艺术新纪元的到来。
The old building signified the town's history. = 这座老建筑象征着这个城镇的历史。


现在完成时:
These events have signified a major shift in public opinion. = 这些事件标志着公众舆论的重大转变。
Her actions have signified a change in her attitude. = 她的行为表明她的态度发生了变化。


过去完成时:
The old flag had signified the country's independence for many years. = 那面旧旗帜多年来象征着国家的独立。
The discovery had signified a breakthrough in scientific research. = 这一发现标志着科学研究的突破。


将来时:
The new law will signify a major change in policy. = 新法律将标志着政策的重大变化。
The election results will signify the direction of the country. = 选举结果将表明国家的走向。


虚拟语气:
If the data signified a problem, we would have addressed it. = 如果数据表明存在问题，我们会解决它。
I wish the results signified a better outcome. = 我希望结果能表明更好的结果。


被动语态:
Peace is signified by the dove. =和平由鸽子象征。
The importance of education is signified by many factors. = 教育的重要性由许多因素体现。


疑问句:
What does this gesture signify? = 这个手势代表什么？
Does this symbol signify anything particular? = 这个符号有什么特别的含义吗？


否定句:
The data does not signify a trend. = 数据并不表明一种趋势。
His words did not signify his agreement. = 他的话并不表示他同意。


PHRASE->
signify something to someone  = 向某人表示…
signify one's approval/disapproval = 表示赞成/反对
signify one's intention = 表示意图
signify one's willingness = 表示愿意
significantly important = 非常重要
