/ɪmˈbrɒθəl/ | v. | 使堕落；使腐败；使败坏 | To corrupt morally; to debase; to make vicious or wicked. The scandal embroiled the politician, damaging his reputation and career.  | 近义词：corrupt, debase, deprave, vitiate | 反义词：purify, elevate, ennoble |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The scandal embroils the politician. = 这场丑闻使这位政客陷入困境。
He embroils himself in every argument. = 他总是卷入每一场争论。


一般过去时:
The war embroiled many nations. = 那场战争使许多国家陷入其中。
The incident embroiled the two families in a bitter feud. = 这件事使这两个家庭陷入激烈的世仇中。


现在完成时:
His actions have embroiled him in controversy. = 他的行为使他卷入了争议。
The company has embroiled itself in a legal battle. = 这家公司已卷入一场法律纠纷。


过去完成时:
They had embroiled themselves in a complex web of lies. = 他们已经把自己卷入了一个复杂的谎言网络。
The situation had embroiled the entire community. = 局势已经使整个社区陷入困境。


将来时:
This decision will embroil us in further difficulties. = 这个决定将使我们陷入进一步的困境。
The upcoming election will likely embroil the country in political debate. = 即将来临的选举可能会使该国陷入政治辩论。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't interfered, he wouldn't have embroiled himself in the matter. = 如果他没有干预，他就不会卷入此事。
I wish I hadn't embroiled myself in this mess. = 我真希望我没有卷入这团糟。


被动语态:
Many innocent people were embroiled in the conflict. = 许多无辜的人卷入了冲突。
The city was embroiled in a fierce debate over the new development plan. = 该市就新的发展计划展开了激烈的辩论。


疑问句:
Will this decision embroil us in legal problems? = 这个决定会使我们陷入法律问题吗？
Did his actions embroil him in any trouble? = 他的行为是否给他带来了任何麻烦？


否定句:
He did not want to embroil himself in their argument. = 他不想卷入他们的争论。
The government tried to avoid being embroiled in the international crisis. = 政府试图避免卷入国际危机。
