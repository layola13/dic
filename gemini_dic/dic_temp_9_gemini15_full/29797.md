caucuses:/ˈkɔːkəsɪz/| n.|党团会议；预备会议；核心小组会议|A caucus is a meeting of members of a political party, typically to select candidates or decide policy.  It can also refer to a smaller, informal group within a larger organization.|例句：The Democratic caucus met to discuss their strategy for the upcoming election.  民主党核心小组开会讨论即将到来的选举策略。

The senators held a caucus to decide on their next move.参议员们举行了一次党团会议以决定下一步行动。|近义词：meeting, conference, convention|反义词：None


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The party caucuses regularly to discuss policy. = 该党定期举行党团会议讨论政策。
Our team caucuses before every game to strategize. = 我们团队在每场比赛前都会举行会议制定策略。


一般过去时:
The caucus decided to endorse the candidate. = 党团会议决定支持该候选人。
They caucused late into the night to reach a consensus. = 他们彻夜开会以达成共识。


现在完成时:
The committee has caucused several times this month. = 委员会本月已经开过几次会了。
They have caucused on the issue for weeks. = 他们就这个问题已经商讨了几个星期。


过去完成时:
They had caucused before the official meeting began. = 在正式会议开始之前，他们已经开过会了。
The party had caucused extensively on the platform before the convention. = 在大会之前，该党已经就纲领进行了广泛的讨论。


将来时:
The party will caucus again next week to review the results. = 该党下周将再次举行党团会议以审查结果。
They will caucus to determine their next steps. = 他们将举行会议以决定下一步行动。


虚拟语气:
If they had caucused earlier, they might have avoided the conflict. = 如果他们早点开会，他们也许就能避免冲突。
I wish we had caucused before making the decision. = 我希望我们在做决定之前开过会。


被动语态:
The issues were discussed in the caucus. = 这些问题在党团会议上进行了讨论。
A decision was reached after a lengthy caucus. = 经过长时间的党团会议后，达成了决定。


疑问句:

否定句:

The party did not caucus on that particular issue. = 该党没有就那个特定问题举行会议。

词源:
The word "caucus" originates from the Algonquian word "caucus," meaning "to talk."  It entered English in the 18th century through its use in Boston, where it described informal political gatherings.  The spelling and meaning evolved over time, solidifying its association with formal political meetings.
