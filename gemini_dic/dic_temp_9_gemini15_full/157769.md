placeman:/ˈpleɪsmən/| n. | 安置人员；（尤指）由上级任命的官员；受雇人员 | A person appointed to a position, especially a government position, often through patronage rather than merit.  A placeman is someone who holds a position of authority or influence due to connections or favoritism rather than ability or qualifications. | Example: The new governor appointed several placemen to key positions in his administration.  新的州长任命了几名亲信担任他政府中的关键职位。|近义词： appointee, officeholder, incumbent | 反义词：meritocrat
