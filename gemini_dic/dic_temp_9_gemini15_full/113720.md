macrocosm:/ˈmækrəʊkɒzəm/ | n. |宏观世界；宇宙；大千世界 | The macrocosm refers to the entire universe or the totality of things, often contrasted with the microcosm (a smaller, representative system).  It emphasizes the vastness and complexity of the universe as a whole, often in philosophical or theological contexts.  | Example:  The human body is often seen as a microcosm reflecting the macrocosm of the universe. 人体常常被看作是反映宇宙宏观世界的一个微观世界。|近义词：universe, cosmos, totality |反义词：microcosm, detail
