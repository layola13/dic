indicted:/ɪnˈdaɪtɪd/ | v. | 控告；起诉 |  To formally accuse someone of a crime.  | The man was indicted on charges of fraud.  这个人被控欺诈罪。 | accused, charged | acquitted, exonerated | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The grand jury indicts suspects of serious crimes. = 大陪审团起诉严重犯罪的嫌疑人。
He regularly indicts the government's policies. = 他经常谴责政府的政策。


一般过去时:
The court indicted him for theft. = 法院以盗窃罪起诉他。
She was indicted last year on tax evasion charges. = 她去年被控逃税。


现在完成时:
The prosecutor has indicted several key figures in the scandal. = 检察官已经起诉了这起丑闻中的几个关键人物。
They have indicted him on multiple counts of murder. = 他们以多项谋杀罪名起诉了他。


过去完成时:
Before the trial, the defendant had already been indicted. = 在审判之前，被告已经被起诉了。
The judge had indicted the lawyer for contempt of court. = 法官已经以藐视法庭罪起诉了这位律师。


将来时:
The authorities will indict him if they find sufficient evidence. = 如果当局找到足够的证据，他们将起诉他。
The case will likely go to trial after he is indicted. = 他被起诉后，案件很可能会开庭审理。


虚拟语气:
If they had more evidence, they would have indicted him sooner. = 如果他们有更多证据，他们就会早点起诉他。
I wish the court had indicted the corrupt officials years ago. = 我希望法院几年前就起诉那些腐败官员。


被动语态:
He was indicted on multiple charges. = 他被控多项罪名。
The suspect was indicted by the grand jury. = 嫌疑人被大陪审团起诉。


疑问句:
Has the suspect been indicted yet? = 嫌疑人已经被起诉了吗？
Will he be indicted for perjury? = 他会被控伪证罪吗？


否定句:
The grand jury did not indict the defendant. = 大陪审团没有起诉被告。
The evidence was insufficient to indict him. = 证据不足以起诉他。


PHRASE->
be indicted on charges of... = 被控...罪名
to be formally indicted = 被正式起诉
indicted for… = 因...被起诉
a true bill of indictment = 正式起诉书
to indict someone for a crime = 因犯罪起诉某人

词源:
The word "indicted" comes from the Old French word "endite," meaning "to dictate," "to compose," or "to write."  It evolved through the Middle English word "endite" and eventually took on its modern legal meaning of formally accusing someone of a crime.
