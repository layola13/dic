quasi-investigated: /ˈkwɑːziɪnˈvɛstɪɡeɪtɪd/ | adj. |  准调查的；似是而非地调查的 |  Implying an investigation that is incomplete, superficial, or lacking the rigor of a formal investigation. It suggests an inquiry that may have been undertaken but fell short of a thorough or systematic examination. | 例句：The quasi-investigated report lacked the necessary evidence to support its conclusions.  = 这份似是而非的调查报告缺乏支持其结论的必要证据。


The police conducted a quasi-investigated inquiry into the incident, failing to interview key witnesses. = 警方对该事件进行了粗略的调查，没有采访关键证人。


近义词：partially investigated, superficially examined, informally probed | 反义词：thoroughly investigated, fully examined, rigorously scrutinized | 词性：形容词


词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin "quasi," meaning "as if," "as it were," or "almost."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation to something but not a complete or exact equivalence.

* **investigated**:  Past participle of "investigate," derived from Latin "investigare" meaning "to track down," "to search out," or "to inquire into."
