preelective: /ˌpriːɪˈlɛktɪv/ | adj. | 预先选举的；选举前的 | Relating to or occurring before an election.  It typically refers to activities or events that take place in the period leading up to an election, such as campaigning, candidate selection, or voter registration. |


例句：

The preelective period saw intense campaigning by both candidates.  = 选举前夕，两位候选人都进行了激烈的竞选活动。

Preelective polls indicated a close race. = 选举前的民调显示这是一场势均力敌的竞赛。


近义词：pre-election, ante-election


反义词：postelective (post-election)


词性：形容词
