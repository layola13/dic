polygraphing: /pɒˈlɪɡræfɪŋ/ | v. |测谎；测谎仪测试 | The act of using a polygraph (lie detector) to test someone's truthfulness.  It involves asking a series of questions while monitoring physiological responses such as heart rate, respiration, and skin conductivity. | Example: The suspect was subjected to polygraphing as part of the investigation.  嫌疑犯作为调查的一部分接受了测谎仪测试。 | 近义词: lie detection, truth verification | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but concepts like deception or lying could be considered opposites of the *purpose* of polygraphing.)
