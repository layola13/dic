endogamy:/ɛnˈdɒɡəmi/ | n. | 族内婚配；近亲结婚 | The practice of marrying within one's own group, clan, or tribe.  This is often dictated by social customs, religious beliefs, or a desire to maintain lineage purity. | Example: Endogamy was common in many ancient societies, leading to a high degree of genetic homogeneity within those groups.  族内婚配在许多古代社会很常见，导致这些群体内部高度的基因同质性。|近义词：inbreeding, consanguinity | 反义词：exogamy
