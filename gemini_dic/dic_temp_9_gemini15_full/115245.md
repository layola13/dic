maniple:/ˈmænɪpəl/| n.|小队，一把，少量|A maniple is a small group of soldiers, typically 60-120 men, forming a tactical unit in the Roman army.  It could also refer to a small bunch or handful of something. |例句：The Roman legion was composed of several maniples. 罗马军团由几个小队组成。

The general ordered his maniples to advance. 将军命令他的小队前进。


近义词：squad, detachment, handful, bunch|反义词：army, legion (in terms of size)|词性：名词

词源:
