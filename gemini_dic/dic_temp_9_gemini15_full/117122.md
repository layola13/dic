meanderer:/miˈændərər/| n. |蜿蜒而行的人或物；漫游者 | A meanderer is a person or thing that follows a winding course; someone who wanders aimlessly.  It often implies a lack of direction or purpose in the movement. |例句：The river is a meanderer, twisting and turning through the valley.  这条河蜿蜒曲折地流经山谷。

The hikers, true meanderers, had no set destination, simply enjoying the journey. 这些徒步旅行者，真正的漫游者，没有固定的目的地，只是享受旅程。|近义词：wanderer, rambler, rover, stroller |反义词：straightforward traveler, direct route

The river meanders through the valley. = 河水蜿蜒流过山谷。
He meanders through life without a clear goal. = 他漫无目的地生活，没有明确的目标。

一般过去时:

She meandered through the shops, browsing without buying. = 她漫无目的地逛商店，看看而不买。

Before settling down, she had meandered through various careers. = 在安定下来之前，她漫无目的地尝试过各种职业。

词源:

The word "meanderer" is derived from the Greek word "Maiandros," which was the name of a winding river in ancient Caria (modern-day Turkey).  The river's sinuous course gave rise to the verb "meander," meaning to wind or turn in its course, and subsequently the noun "meanderer."
