/ˌɪntərˈvɛntər/| n. |干涉者；调解人；介入者 | A person who intervenes, especially in a dispute or conflict.  An intervenor is someone who becomes involved in a situation to try to influence the outcome, often in a legal or political context. They might be trying to help resolve a conflict, protect their own interests, or add new information to the proceedings. | Example: The mediator acted as an intervenor in the labor dispute, helping both sides reach a compromise.  调解员在劳资纠纷中充当调解人的角色，帮助双方达成妥协。  |近义词：mediator, arbitrator, negotiator |反义词：bystander, onlooker | 名词
