pass:/pɑːs/ /pæs/| v. n. adj.| 通过，及格；经过；传递；去世；通行证；合格的；|To go by or move past something; to succeed in a test or exam; to transfer something to someone. |例句：The bill passed through Congress. 这项法案通过了国会。

The runner passed the baton to his teammate. 赛跑运动员将接力棒传给了他的队友。

近义词：succeed, approve, transmit,  expire|反义词：fail, reject,  retain,  live


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He passes the ball to his teammate. 他把球传给他的队友。
She passes the test with flying colors. 她以优异的成绩通过了考试。


一般过去时:
I passed the exam last week. 我上周通过了考试。
The car passed us on the highway. 车子在高速公路上超过了我们。


现在完成时:
She has passed her driving test. 她已经通过了驾照考试。
Many years have passed since we last met. 自从我们上次见面以来，已经过去很多年了。


过去完成时:
The train had passed before we arrived at the station. 我们到达车站之前，火车已经开走了。
He had already passed the exam before he knew the results. 他在知道结果之前就已经通过考试了。


将来时:
The bill will pass the Senate next week. 该法案将于下周通过参议院。
I think she will pass her exams easily. 我认为她会轻松通过考试。


虚拟语气:
If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam. 如果我学习更努力，我就通过考试了。
I wish I had passed the exam. 我希望我通过了考试。


被动语态:
The bill was passed by Congress. 这项法案由国会通过。
The message was passed along to the manager. 消息被传递给了经理。


疑问句:
Did you pass the exam? 你考试及格了吗？
Will the bill pass? 这项法案会通过吗？


否定句:
He didn't pass the test. 他考试不及格。
The bill did not pass the House. 这项法案没有通过众议院。
