carotid: /kəˈrɒtɪd/ | /kəˈrɑːtɪd/ | n. |颈动脉|The carotid arteries are the major arteries that supply blood to the head and neck.  They are crucial for brain function and overall health.  Damage to the carotid arteries can lead to serious consequences, including stroke.|近义词：arteries (in the context of supplying the head and neck)|反义词：N/A


英文解析：The carotid arteries are paired blood vessels located on either side of the neck.  There are internal and external carotid arteries. The internal carotid arteries supply blood to the brain, while the external carotid arteries supply blood to the face and neck.


例句：

The surgeon carefully examined the patient's carotid arteries before the operation.
外科医生在手术前仔细检查了病人的颈动脉。

A blockage in the carotid artery can lead to a stroke.
颈动脉阻塞会导致中风。


词源：From Late Latin *carōtīdēs, from Greek καρωτίδες (karōtídes), plural of καρωτὶς (karōtís), from καροῦν (karoûn) "to put to sleep," from κάρα (kára) "head."  The name likely refers to the effect of pressure on the carotid artery causing temporary unconsciousness.
