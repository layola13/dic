helioscope:/ˈhiːliəʊskoʊp/| n. |日晷；日像仪|A helioscope is an instrument used to observe the sun without causing eye damage.  It typically uses a lens or mirror system to project an image of the sun onto a screen.  Early versions were used to study sunspots.  Modern versions might incorporate filters to reduce the intensity of sunlight. | Example:  Early astronomers used a helioscope to safely observe sunspots and other solar phenomena. 早期天文学家使用日像仪安全地观测太阳黑子和其它太阳现象。|近义词：solar telescope, sun viewer|反义词：无
