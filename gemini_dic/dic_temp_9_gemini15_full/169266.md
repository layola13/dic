prometheus:/proʊˈmiːθiːəs/| n. |普罗米修斯|A Titan in Greek mythology who stole fire from the gods and gave it to humankind.  He is often depicted as a champion of humanity and a symbol of defiance against authority.  The name Prometheus is often used to refer to things that bring enlightenment or progress, even if risky.|Example: The scientist's groundbreaking research was hailed as a Promethean act of daring.  普罗米修斯式的勇敢行为。 The new technology was considered a Promethean gift to society. 这项新技术被认为是给社会的一份普罗米修斯式的礼物。|近义词：rebel, innovator, benefactor |反义词：conformist, oppressor
