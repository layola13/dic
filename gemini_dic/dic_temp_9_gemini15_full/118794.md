metals:/ˈmɛtəlz/| n.|金属|Metals are chemical elements that are typically hard, shiny, good conductors of heat and electricity, and malleable and ductile. They are often found in the earth's crust and are used in a wide variety of applications.|例句：Iron, copper, and gold are examples of metals.  铁、铜和金是金属的例子。|例句：Many modern technologies rely heavily on the use of rare earth metals. 许多现代科技都严重依赖稀土金属的使用。|近义词：elements (in a chemical sense), ores|反义词：nonmetals
