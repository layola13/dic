heliograph:/ˈheliəɡræf/| n. |日光电报机；日光信号器|A heliograph is a device that uses mirrors to reflect sunlight to communicate over long distances, typically used for signaling. It's an early form of long-distance communication that predates radio and other modern technologies.  The device works by precisely aiming a mirror to reflect sunlight at a distant receiver, who interprets the flashes as a code. |The soldiers used a heliograph to send messages across the valley.  士兵们用日光电报机在山谷间发送信息。
