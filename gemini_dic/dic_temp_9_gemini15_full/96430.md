indistinct:/ˌɪnˈdɪstɪŋkt/ | adj. | 模糊不清的，难以辨认的 | Not clear or distinct to the sight, hearing, or mind.  |例句：The photograph was indistinct, making it difficult to identify the people.照片模糊不清，难以辨认照片中的人。

例句：His speech was indistinct due to his heavy cold. 因为他重感冒，他的话语含糊不清。

近义词：blurred, unclear, faint, vague, obscure | 反义词：clear, distinct, sharp, precise, lucid


词源:  The word "indistinct" comes from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") and the word "distinct," which itself comes from the Latin word "distinctus," meaning "separated, distinguished."  Therefore, "indistinct" literally means "not separated" or "not clearly distinguished."
