draw a line in the sand: /drɔː ə laɪn ɪn ðə sænd/ | phrasal verb |划清界限；设定不可逾越的底线| To establish a limit or boundary beyond which one will not go; to set a point of no return.  This idiom evokes the image of drawing a line in the sand to mark a boundary.  |例句：The government drew a line in the sand regarding tax increases, stating they would not compromise further. 政府在税收增加问题上划定了底线，表示不会进一步妥协。


The company drew a line in the sand, refusing to negotiate further reductions in employee salaries. 公司划定了底线，拒绝进一步协商降低员工薪资。|近义词：set a limit, establish a boundary, ultimatum,  |反义词：compromise, negotiate, concede
