redrafting:/ˌriːˈdræftɪŋ/| v.|重新起草；修改；改写|The act of revising or rewriting something, typically a written document or piece of writing, to improve it or make it more accurate.  It involves making changes to the original text, such as adding, deleting, or rearranging content.|例句：The editor suggested some redrafting of the article before publication. 编辑建议在发表前修改一下这篇文章。  The lawyer is redrafting the contract to reflect the new agreement. 律师正在修改合同以反映新的协议。|近义词：rewriting, revising, editing, amending|反义词：finalizing, completing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is redrafting the proposal. = 他正在修改提案。
She redrafts her essays multiple times. = 她多次修改她的论文。

一般过去时:
They redrafted the report after receiving feedback. = 他们在收到反馈后重新起草了报告。
He redrafted the letter to make it more polite. = 他重写了这封信，使其更礼貌。

现在完成时:
I have redrafted the contract three times. = 我已经修改了合同三次。
She has redrafted the document to include the latest data. = 她已经修改了文件，以包含最新的数据。

过去完成时:

She had redrafted the manuscript several times before submitting it. = 在提交之前，她已经修改了手稿好几次。
