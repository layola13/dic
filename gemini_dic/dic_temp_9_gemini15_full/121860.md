misrepresents:/ˌmɪsrɛprɪˈzɛnts/| vt.|歪曲；误传；不实地描述|To give a false or misleading representation of; to misstate or distort the facts.|The advertisement misrepresents the product's capabilities.  广告歪曲了产品的性能。|The politician misrepresented his position on the issue to gain votes. 这位政治家歪曲了他在这个问题上的立场以赢得选票。|represents, portrays, describes|accurately represents, truthfully describes


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He misrepresents the facts in his report. = 他在他的报告中歪曲了事实。
The company misrepresents its environmental record. = 这家公司歪曲了它的环保记录。

一般过去时:
She misrepresented her qualifications to get the job. = 她为了得到这份工作而谎报了自己的资历。
The journalist misrepresented the events of the meeting. = 记者歪曲了会议的事件。

现在完成时:
They have misrepresented the situation to the public. = 他们向公众歪曲了情况。
The media has misrepresented the findings of the study. = 媒体歪曲了这项研究的结果。

过去完成时:
He had misrepresented his intentions from the start. = 他从一开始就歪曲了他的意图。
The company had misrepresented its financial position for years. = 这家公司多年来一直歪曲其财务状况。

将来时:
She will misrepresent the data to support her argument. = 她将歪曲数据以支持她的论点。
The government will likely misrepresent the economic situation. = 政府可能会歪曲经济形势。

虚拟语气:

I wish he hadn't misrepresented my ideas. = 我真希望他没有歪曲我的想法。

被动语态:
