denigrative:/ˈdɛnɪɡreɪtɪv/| adj. |贬低的；毁谤的；轻蔑的 |  expressing contempt or disapproval; belittling.  It is used to describe words, remarks, or actions that express a negative opinion of someone or something, often unfairly or unjustly. |例句：His denigrative remarks about her abilities were unacceptable. 他对她能力的贬低言论是不可接受的。

例句：The article contained several denigrative comments about the opposing political party. 这篇文章包含了几条关于反对党的贬低性评论。


近义词：derogatory, disparaging, belittling, pejorative, contemptuous

反义词：laudatory, complimentary, praising, flattering

词源:  The word "denigrative" comes from the Latin word "denigrare," which means "to blacken" or "to make black."  This reflects the idea of sullying someone's reputation or making them appear bad.  The suffix "-ative" indicates that it functions as an adjective.
