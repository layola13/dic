prytaneum:/praɪˈteɪniəm/| n. |普里塔尼翁（古希腊公共建筑）|A prytaneum was a public building in ancient Greek city-states, serving as a meeting place for the council, a dining hall for officials, and a place to honor the city's gods and heroes.  It often housed a sacred hearth fire that was never allowed to go out. |The prytaneum in Athens was a significant center of civic life.|近义词：town hall, civic center|反义词：private residence
