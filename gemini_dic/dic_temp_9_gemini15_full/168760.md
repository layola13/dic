prodigal:/ˈprɒdɪɡəl/|adj.|挥霍的；浪费的；放荡的|Characterized by extravagance and wasteful expenditure; recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources.|He lived a prodigal lifestyle, squandering his inheritance in a few short years. 他过着挥霍的生活，几年内就挥霍掉了他的遗产。|extravagant, wasteful, spendthrift|frugal, thrifty, economical|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is prodigal with his money. = 他花钱大手大脚。
She is prodigal in her praise. = 她赞扬过度。


一般过去时:
He was prodigal in his youth. = 他年轻时很挥霍。
They were prodigal with their resources. = 他们浪费资源。


现在完成时:
He has been prodigal with his time. = 他浪费了时间。
She has been prodigal in her spending. = 她花钱大手大脚。


过去完成时:
He had been prodigal with his inheritance before he went bankrupt. = 破产前，他挥霍了他的遗产。
They had been prodigal with their efforts, achieving little. = 他们努力过度，却收获甚微。


将来时:
He will be prodigal with his new fortune. = 他将挥霍他的新财富。
She will be prodigal in her celebration. = 她将大肆庆祝。


虚拟语气:
If he were not so prodigal, he would be rich. = 如果他不那么挥霍，他会很富有。
I wish he weren't so prodigal with his time. = 我希望他不那么浪费时间。


被动语态:
His wealth was prodigally spent. = 他的财富被挥霍掉了。
Resources were prodigally used in the project. = 资源在这个项目中被浪费了。


疑问句:
Is he prodigal with his money? = 他花钱大手大脚吗？
Are they prodigal in their promises? = 他们的承诺是否过于夸大？


否定句:
He is not prodigal with his words. = 他说话不多。
She is not prodigal in her affections. = 她感情不随便。
