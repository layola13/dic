rush:/rʌʃ/| /rʌʃ/| n., v.| 仓促；冲；涌入 | To move or act with great speed or haste; a sudden strong movement or flow of people or things. |例句：He rushed to catch the train. 他匆忙赶火车。  |例句：There was a rush of people towards the exit.  人群涌向出口。|近义词：hurry, hasten, scramble, surge |反义词：dawdle, delay, procrastinate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rushes to work every morning. = 他每天早上都匆忙上班。
They rush through their meals. = 他们匆匆忙忙地吃饭。

一般过去时:
I rushed to the hospital. = 我赶往医院。
She rushed to finish her work before the deadline. = 她赶在截止日期前完成了工作。

现在完成时:
He has rushed through three projects this month. = 这个月他已经匆匆完成了三个项目。
We have rushed into a decision we regret. = 我们仓促做出了一个让我们后悔的决定。

过去完成时:
They had rushed to the airport, but missed their flight. = 他们匆忙赶到机场，但是错过了航班。
She had rushed through her presentation and made several mistakes. = 她匆匆忙忙地做了演示，犯了好几个错误。

将来时:
I will rush to help you if you need it. = 如果你需要，我会赶紧去帮助你。
They will rush to complete the task before the meeting. = 他们将在会议前赶完任务。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I wouldn't rush. = 如果我有更多时间，我就不会匆忙了。
I wish I hadn't rushed into buying that car. = 我真希望我当初没有匆忙买那辆车。

被动语态:
The project was rushed to completion. = 项目被仓促完成。
The decision was rushed through without proper consideration. = 决定在没有充分考虑的情况下仓促做出。

疑问句:
Are you rushing? = 你在赶时间吗？
Why are you rushing so much? = 你为什么这么着急？

否定句:
Don't rush! = 别着急！
I didn't rush my work, I took my time. = 我没有赶工，我从容不迫地完成了工作。
