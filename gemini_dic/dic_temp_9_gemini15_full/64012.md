epitaph:/ˈɛpɪtæf/| n. |墓志铭，墓碑铭文|An inscription on a tomb or monument in memory of the person buried there.  It often summarizes the deceased's life or character. |Example:  Her epitaph simply read, "Beloved wife and mother."  她的墓志铭简单地写着：“亲爱的妻子和母亲”。|近义词：inscription, eulogy, memorial|反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "epitaph" comes from the Greek word "epitaphion," which means "tomb-inscription."  The Greek word is a compound of "epi," meaning "upon," and "taphos," meaning "tomb."  Therefore, literally, an epitaph is something written "upon" a tomb.
