macrolinguistics:/ˌmækroʊlɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks/| n. |宏观语言学|The branch of linguistics that studies the relationship between language and society, culture, and other aspects of human life.  It examines the broader social and cultural context in which language operates, going beyond the study of individual sentences or utterances.| Example: Macrolinguistics explores how language use reflects and shapes social power dynamics.  宏观语言学探讨了语言使用如何反映和塑造社会权力动态。|近义词:sociolinguistics, anthropological linguistics|反义词:microlinguistics
