palter:/ˈpɔːltər/ | v. |含糊其辞；支吾其词；敷衍；欺骗 | To speak evasively or misleadingly; to act insincerely or deceitfully.  |例句：He paltered with the truth, avoiding a direct answer. 他含糊其辞，避免直接回答。|例句：Don't palter with me; tell me the truth. 不要敷衍我；告诉我真相。|近义词：equivocate, prevaricate, hedge, temporize |反义词：be straightforward, be honest

词源:
The word "palter" ultimately derives from Middle English *palteren, possibly related to Old French *palterer, meaning "to stammer, to stutter."  Its meaning evolved to encompass the sense of evasiveness and deception associated with unclear or misleading speech.
