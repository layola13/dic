phylum:/ˈfaɪləm/| n. |门（生物分类单位）|A phylum is the highest taxonomic rank below kingdom and above class.  It is used in the classification of organisms, grouping together organisms with similar body plans and evolutionary histories. For example, Chordata is the phylum that includes vertebrates. |Example:  The phylum Arthropoda includes insects, spiders, and crustaceans.  节肢动物门包括昆虫、蜘蛛和甲壳类动物。|近义词:division (in botany)|反义词:N/A|名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily used in a biological context and doesn't have typical verb conjugations like "to be" or "to do".  Grammatical usage focuses on its function as a noun within sentences.

Example 2: The classification of this newly discovered organism is currently under debate, specifically regarding its phylum.  这个新发现的生物的分类目前正在争论中，特别是关于它的门类。
