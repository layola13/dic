ride-off:/ˈraɪd ɔːf/| n. v.| 骑马离开；驱逐；强行带走 | A ride-off is an act of riding away, often implying a hasty or dramatic departure.  It can also refer to the act of forcibly removing someone or something. |例句：The cowboys rode off into the sunset.  牛仔们骑马奔向夕阳。  The sheriff rode off the troublemakers. 警长把闹事者赶走了。 |近义词：departure, expulsion, removal |反义词：arrival, return


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rides off every morning. = 他每天早上骑马离开。
They ride off to the mountains every weekend. = 他们每个周末都骑马去山里。

一般过去时:
He rode off in a hurry. = 他匆匆骑马离开了。
The thieves rode off with the stolen goods. = 小偷骑马带着赃物逃走了。

现在完成时:
He has ridden off without saying goodbye. = 他没说再见就骑马走了。
They have ridden off into the wilderness. = 他们骑马进入荒野了。

过去完成时:
He had ridden off before the sun rose. = 太阳升起之前他就骑马离开了。
They had ridden off before we could stop them. = 在我们阻止他们之前，他们就已经骑马离开了。

将来时:
He will ride off at dawn. = 他将在黎明时分骑马离开。
They will ride off as soon as they are ready. = 他们准备好了就骑马离开。

虚拟语气:
If he were here, he would ride off with us. = 如果他在这儿，他会和我们一起骑马离开。
I wish I could ride off and escape all my problems. = 我真希望我能骑马离开，逃避我所有的问题。

被动语态:
The horses were ridden off into the forest. = 马被骑到森林里去了。（比较少见，语境受限）
The bandits were ridden off by the police.  （语境受限，更常用被动语态表达驱逐）歹徒被警察赶走了。


疑问句:
Will he ride off alone? = 他会独自骑马离开吗？
When will they ride off? = 他们什么时候骑马离开？

否定句:
He didn't ride off. = 他没有骑马离开。
They wouldn't ride off without me. = 他们不会不带我一起骑马离开。


PHRASE->
ride off into the sunset: 骑马奔向夕阳 (象征着浪漫的结束或新的开始)
ride off into the night: 骑马消失在夜色中 (象征着神秘的逃离)
ride off a problem: 解决问题（非正式说法）
ride off into the distance: 骑马消失在远处
ride off a horse: 骑马离开（强调骑乘行为）
