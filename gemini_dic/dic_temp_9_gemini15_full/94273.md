impersonals: /ɪmˈpɜːrsənəlz/ | n. | 不及物动词；无人称动词 |  Impersonals refer to verbs that lack a grammatical subject, or whose subject is implied rather than explicitly stated. They often describe actions or states of being that are not directly attributed to any particular person or thing.  | 例句：It is raining. (下雨了。)  It snows in winter. (冬天会下雪。) | 近义词： impersonal verbs,  intransitive verbs (in some contexts) | 反义词： personal verbs


USAGE->
一般现在时:
It rains a lot in Seattle. = 西雅图雨水很多。
It is cold today. = 今天很冷。

一般过去时:
It snowed heavily last night. = 昨晚下了很大的雪。
It rained cats and dogs yesterday. = 昨天下了倾盆大雨。

现在完成时:
It has rained all day. = 一整天都在下雨。
It has been cold for weeks. = 好几个星期以来一直很冷。

过去完成时:
It had rained for days before the flood. = 在洪水来临之前已经下了好几天雨了。
It had been sunny all week until yesterday. = 一周以来一直阳光灿烂，直到昨天。

将来时:
It will rain tomorrow. = 明天会下雨。
It will be hot this summer. = 今年夏天会很热。

虚拟语气:
If it were to snow, we would stay home. = 如果下雪的话，我们就待在家里。
It's time we went home. = 我们该回家了。


被动语态:
It is said that he is a genius. = 据说他是个天才。(虽然形式上是被动，但实际意义上是无人称句)
It was announced that the meeting was postponed. = 宣布会议延期。(虽然形式上是被动，但实际意义上是无人称句)


疑问句:
Is it raining? = 下雨了吗？
Is it far to the station? = 到车站远吗？


否定句:
It isn't raining. = 没有下雨。
It wasn't cold yesterday. = 昨天不冷。


PHRASE->
It's a pity that... = 很可惜...
It goes without saying that... = 不用说...
It depends on... = 取决于...
It's up to you. = 由你决定。
It's no use... = 没用...
