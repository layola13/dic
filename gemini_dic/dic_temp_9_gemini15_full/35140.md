clout:/klaʊt/ | n. | 影响力；权力；打击 |  The power to influence or affect people or events.  It can refer to political influence, social influence, or even physical force. |例句：The senator wields considerable clout in Washington.  这位参议员在华盛顿拥有相当大的影响力。|例句：He used his clout to get the job. 他利用自己的影响力得到了这份工作。|近义词：influence, power, authority, sway |反义词：powerlessness, weakness


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has a lot of clout in the company. = 他在公司很有影响力。
That politician holds significant clout in the government. = 那个政客在政府中拥有相当大的影响力。


一般过去时:
The mayor used his clout to get the project approved. = 市长利用他的影响力使项目获得批准。
She lost her clout after the scandal. = 丑闻过后，她失去了影响力。

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
