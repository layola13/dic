inscribing:/ɪnˈskraɪbɪŋ/| v.|刻写；题写；铭刻|The act of writing or carving words or symbols onto a surface.  It implies a permanent or lasting record.|例句：He was inscribing his name on the trophy. 他正在奖杯上刻写他的名字。

例句：The artist inscribed a dedication on the painting.艺术家在画上题写了题词。


近义词：engraving, carving, etching, writing


反义词：erasing, removing, obliterating


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He inscribes his name on every book he owns. 他在他的每一本书上都刻上他的名字。
She inscribes inspirational quotes on her journal. 她会在日记本上写下励志格言。


一般过去时:
They inscribed a message on the time capsule. 他们把信息刻在了时间胶囊上。
The craftsman inscribed the customer's name on the silver plate. 工匠在银盘上刻上了顾客的名字。


现在完成时:
She has inscribed her name on the wall of fame. 她已经把名字刻在了名人墙上。
He has inscribed many poems in his lifetime. 他一生中刻写过许多诗歌。


过去完成时:
They had inscribed the date on the cornerstone before the ceremony. 仪式开始前，他们已将日期刻在了奠基石上。
She had inscribed her wishes in a bottle before throwing it into the sea. 她把愿望写在瓶子里，然后扔进了大海。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If I could inscribe a message to my future self, I would write "be patient". 如果我能给未来的自己留下信息，我会写“要有耐心”。
I wish I had inscribed my grandmother's recipe before she passed away. 我真希望在她去世之前，我把祖母的食谱抄写下来。


被动语态:

否定句:
