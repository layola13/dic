camail:/kəˈmeɪl/| n.|头巾，兜帽|A camail is a large, often richly ornamented, cape-like hood, typically worn by medieval ecclesiastics or knights.  It was usually attached to a cloak or other outer garment and often fell down over the shoulders and back.|Example: The knight’s camail, embroidered with his family crest, flowed behind him as he rode.|近义词：hood, cowl, cape|反义词：None specifically.
