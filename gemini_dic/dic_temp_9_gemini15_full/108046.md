latching: /ˈlætʃɪŋ/ | v. n. adj. | 闩上；锁上；抓住；卡住 | The act of fastening something securely, typically with a latch.  |  例句：He was latching the gate when I arrived. 他在我到达时正在闩门。

例句：The child latched onto his mother's leg.  孩子紧紧抓住他母亲的腿。


近义词：fastening, securing, catching, gripping | 反义词：unlocking, unfastening, releasing


词性:动词(v.) 名词(n.) 形容词(adj.)

The word "latch" comes from the Old English word *læccan*, meaning "to catch" or "to seize."  This word is related to other Germanic words with similar meanings.  The "-ing" suffix forms the present participle or gerund, indicating the action of latching.
