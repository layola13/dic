renotarizing: /ˌriːˈnoʊtəraɪzɪŋ/ | v. |重新公证；再次公证 | The act of notarizing something again.  This typically involves a document that needs to be re-verified by a notary public.  It might be necessary due to errors, omissions, or changes made to the original document after it was first notarized. | Example:  Renotarizing the contract was necessary after the buyer requested a change to the purchase price. = 买家要求更改购买价格后，需要重新公证合同。


The process of renotarizing a document is required after an amendment or correction to the original notarized document. = 在对原始公证文件进行修改或更正后，需要重新进行公证流程。


近义词: re-notarization, recertification

反义词:  There isn't a direct antonym, but words like "invalidating" or "canceling" could be considered opposites in the context of a document's legal status.
