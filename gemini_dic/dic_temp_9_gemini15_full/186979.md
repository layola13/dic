risk-averse:/ˈrɪsk əˈvɜːrs/| adj. |规避风险的，厌恶风险的 |Someone who is risk-averse is unwilling to take risks. They prefer to avoid situations that could lead to losses, even if those situations also offer the potential for gains.  This preference often stems from a desire for stability and security. |The company's risk-averse approach to investment led to slow growth. 这家公司规避风险的投资方法导致增长缓慢。  He is risk-averse and prefers to save his money rather than invest it in the stock market. 他很厌恶风险，宁愿存钱也不愿投资股市。|risk-taking, adventurous |risk-seeking, adventurous


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is risk-averse in his financial decisions. = 他在财务决策方面很厌恶风险。
She is risk-averse and always chooses the safest option. = 她很厌恶风险，总是选择最安全的选择。

一般过去时:
He was risk-averse when he was younger. = 他年轻的时候很厌恶风险。
The company was risk-averse before the new CEO took over. = 在新任首席执行官上任之前，该公司一直很厌恶风险。

现在完成时:
They have been risk-averse throughout their careers. = 他们职业生涯中一直很厌恶风险。
She has remained risk-averse despite many opportunities. = 尽管有很多机会，她仍然很厌恶风险。

过去完成时:
He had been risk-averse until he started his own business. = 直到他开始自己的生意，他才不再厌恶风险。
The investors had been risk-averse before the market crash. = 在市场崩盘之前，投资者一直很厌恶风险。

将来时:
They will likely remain risk-averse in the future. = 他们将来很可能会继续规避风险。
The company will be risk-averse in its expansion plans. = 公司在扩张计划中将采取规避风险的策略。

虚拟语气:
If he weren't so risk-averse, he would have invested in that startup. = 如果他不那么厌恶风险，他就会投资那家初创公司。
I wish I weren't so risk-averse; I could have made a lot of money. = 我希望我不那么厌恶风险；我本来可以赚很多钱。

被动语态:
His investment strategy is considered risk-averse. = 他的投资策略被认为是规避风险的。
The decision was criticized for being too risk-averse. = 这一决定因过于规避风险而受到批评。

疑问句:
Is he risk-averse when it comes to investments? = 在投资方面，他是否规避风险？
Are they risk-averse in their business dealings? = 他们在商业交易中是否规避风险？

否定句:
He is not risk-averse; he often takes calculated risks. = 他并不厌恶风险；他经常承担有计划的风险。
They are not risk-averse; they embrace challenges. = 他们并不厌恶风险；他们勇于迎接挑战。
