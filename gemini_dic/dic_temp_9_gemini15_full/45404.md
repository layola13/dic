cupids:/ˈkjuːpɪdz/ | n. |丘比特（复数）, 爱神们 |  The plural of Cupid, the Roman god of love.  Cupids are often depicted as winged cherubs with bows and arrows, symbolizing love and desire.  They represent the playful and sometimes mischievous aspects of love. | Example: Paintings often feature cupids playfully shooting arrows at unsuspecting lovers.  油画中经常描绘丘比特顽皮地向毫无戒心的恋人射箭。


近义词: cherubs (in the context of love), love gods

反义词:  (No direct antonyms, as cupids represent a specific concept)
