gougers:/ˈɡaʊdʒərz/| n.|敲竹杠的人，漫天要价者 |A gouger is someone who charges excessively high prices, especially for goods or services that are in demand or difficult to obtain.  They exploit a buyer's vulnerability or lack of options. |Example: The taxi driver was a real gouger, charging me double the fare.  出租车司机是个十足的敲竹杠的人，向我收取双倍的车费。|近义词：extortionist, price-gouger, swindler |反义词：fair dealer, honest merchant |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gouges his customers regularly. = 他经常敲诈他的顾客。
That company gouges its customers on repair costs. = 那家公司在维修费用上敲诈顾客。


一般过去时:
The mechanic gouged me on the repair bill. = 修理工在我的修理账单上敲诈了我。
They gouged tourists during the peak season. = 他们在旅游旺季敲诈游客。


现在完成时:
They have gouged consumers for years with their overpriced products. = 多年来，他们以高价产品敲诈消费者。
He has gouged me out of hundreds of dollars. = 他敲诈了我几百美元。


过去完成时:
The landlord had gouged them on the rent for months before they moved out. = 在他们搬出去之前，房东几个月来一直敲诈他们的租金。
The company had gouged its investors before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，该公司已经敲诈了投资者。


将来时:
They will gouge the public with this new tax. = 他们将用这种新税来敲诈公众。
The airline will gouge passengers during the holiday rush. = 航空公司将在假期高峰期敲诈乘客。


虚拟语气:
If they knew they would be caught, they wouldn't gouge their customers. = 如果他们知道会被抓住，他们就不会敲诈顾客了。
I wish the hotel hadn't gouged me on the price of the room. = 我真希望酒店没有敲诈我的房间价格。


被动语态:
Customers are often gouged by unscrupulous businesses. = 顾客经常被不择手段的企业敲诈。
Tourists are easily gouged in unfamiliar places. = 游客在不熟悉的地方很容易被敲诈。


疑问句:
Are they gouging their customers again? = 他们又在敲诈顾客了吗？
Was the customer gouged by the seller? = 卖家是否敲诈了顾客？


否定句:
He didn't gouge anyone; he charged a fair price. = 他没有敲诈任何人；他收取了合理的价格。
We won't let them gouge us. = 我们不会让他们敲诈我们。
