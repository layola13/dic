crayfish:/ˈkreɪfɪʃ/| n. |小龙虾，螯虾|A crayfish is a freshwater crustacean with a long body, a hard shell, and large claws. They are often eaten as food.  |例句：We caught several crayfish in the stream. 我们在小溪里抓到几只小龙虾。|例句：The crayfish are a popular delicacy in many parts of the world. 小龙虾在世界许多地方都是受欢迎的美味佳肴。|近义词：crawfish, crawdad|反义词:  (无直接反义词)|名词

词源:
The word "crayfish" is of Old English origin, derived from "crevis," which itself likely comes from a Germanic source related to words meaning "crab" or "claw."  The spelling variations "crawfish" and "crawdad" are also common, reflecting regional dialect and evolving pronunciation.
