drawback:/ˈdrɔːbæk/| n.|缺点，不足之处|A drawback is a disadvantage or problem that makes something less good. It refers to something that hinders progress or is otherwise undesirable. |例句：The main drawback of this product is its high price.  这个产品的最大缺点是价格高。

例句：A major drawback of living in the city is the noise.  住在城市的主要缺点是噪音。


近义词：disadvantage, shortcoming, defect, flaw, impediment|反义词：advantage, benefit, plus


词源:  The word "drawback" originally meant "a deduction or rebate," stemming from the practice of drawing back (refunding) taxes or duties. Over time, its meaning shifted to encompass any disadvantage or impediment.  The "draw" part relates to the act of withdrawing or taking away something.
