nonamenability:/ˌnɒnˌeɪməˈbɪləti/ | n. | 非可几性，不可约性 | The inability of a group or structure to be amenable (easily persuaded or controlled).  This often refers to the failure of a group to possess a certain property, such as possessing an invariant mean.  In mathematics, particularly in the study of groups and algebras, it indicates a lack of a certain averaging property. | Example: The nonamenability of the free group on two generators is a well-known result in group theory.  非自由群在两个生成元上的非可约性是群论中一个众所周知的结论。 | 近义词: non-amenability,  inamenability | 反义词: amenability
