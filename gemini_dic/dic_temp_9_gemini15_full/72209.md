floras:/ˈflɔːrəz/| n. |植物群，植物区系|The floras of the Amazon rainforest are incredibly diverse. 亚马逊雨林的植物群极其多样化。|近义词：vegetation, plant life|反义词：fauna (动物群)

英文解析:
Floras refers to the plant life of a particular region, habitat, or geological period.  It encompasses all the species of plants found in that specific area, considering their diversity and interactions.  The term is often used in scientific contexts, such as botany and ecology, to describe and categorize plant communities.

例句:
The alpine floras are adapted to harsh conditions.高山植物群适应了恶劣的环境。

The unique floras of the Galapagos Islands fascinated Darwin. 加拉帕戈斯群岛独特的植物群深深吸引了达尔文。


词源:
The word "flora" originates from the Roman goddess of flowers and spring, Flora.  The term's usage in botany to describe plant life emerged later, reflecting the association between the goddess and the abundance and variety of plant life.
