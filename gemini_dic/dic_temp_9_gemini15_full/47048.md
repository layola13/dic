datadriven:/ˈdeɪtəˌdrɪvən/ | adj. | 数据驱动的 |  Data-driven describes something that is based on the analysis of large amounts of data.  It implies decisions and actions are guided by data rather than intuition or guesswork.  This approach is common in many fields, including business, marketing, and science. | 例句：The company uses a data-driven approach to marketing, targeting its advertisements based on customer behavior. 公司采用数据驱动的方法进行营销，根据客户行为定位其广告。

The company's decision to launch a new product was data-driven, based on extensive market research. 公司推出新产品的决定是数据驱动的，基于广泛的市场调查。


近义词：evidence-based, fact-based, empirical | 反义词：intuition-based, guesswork-based, subjective


词源:  The term is a compound word combining "data" and "driven."  "Data" originates from the plural of the Latin word "datum," meaning "something given." "Driven" comes from the verb "to drive," suggesting being propelled or guided by something.  The combination signifies that the subject is propelled or guided by data.
