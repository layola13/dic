gripe:/ɡraɪp/| n., v.|牢骚，抱怨；抓住；紧紧握住|A gripe is a complaint or a strong feeling of annoyance.  It can also mean to hold or grasp something firmly. |例句：He had a gripe about the long hours. 他对长时间工作感到不满。


He gripped the steering wheel tightly. 他紧紧抓住方向盘。|近义词：complaint, grievance, grumble, complain, grasp, clutch|反义词：praise, compliment, satisfaction


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gripes about the weather every day. = 他每天都抱怨天气。
She gripes to her friends about her boss. = 她向朋友们抱怨她的老板。


一般过去时:
He griped about the unfair treatment. = 他抱怨不公平的待遇。
She griped to her mother about her brother. = 她向她母亲抱怨她的兄弟。


现在完成时:
He has griped about this problem for weeks. = 他已经抱怨这个问题几周了。
She has griped repeatedly about the noise. = 她反复抱怨噪音。


过去完成时:
He had griped about the situation before the meeting. = 他在会议前就抱怨过这种情况。
She had griped constantly about her workload before quitting. = 在辞职之前，她一直在不断地抱怨她的工作量。


将来时:
He will gripe about the new rules. = 他会抱怨新规定的。
She will gripe again if the problem isn't solved. = 如果问题没有解决，她还会再次抱怨。


虚拟语气:
If he knew the consequences, he wouldn't gripe so much. = 如果他知道后果，他就不会抱怨这么多了。
I wish he wouldn't gripe all the time. = 我希望他不总是抱怨。


被动语态:
His complaints were often griped about by his colleagues. = 他的抱怨经常被他的同事们所抱怨。  (This usage is less common)
The issue has been griped about for years. =这个问题已经被抱怨多年了。(This usage is less common)


疑问句:
What are you griping about? = 你在抱怨什么？
Why is he griping so much today? = 他今天为什么抱怨那么多？


否定句:
He didn't gripe about the food. = 他没有抱怨食物。
She doesn't usually gripe about her job. = 她通常不抱怨她的工作。
