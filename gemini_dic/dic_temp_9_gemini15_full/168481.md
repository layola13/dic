probed:/proʊbd/| v.|探查；探究；调查|To explore or examine something carefully and thoroughly, often in order to find information or evidence.|例句：The police probed the circumstances surrounding the accident.  警方调查了事故发生的相关情况。

例句：Scientists probed the depths of the ocean to study marine life. 科学家探测海洋深处以研究海洋生物。


近义词：investigate, explore, examine, scrutinize|反义词：ignore, overlook


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They probe the market for new opportunities. = 他们探寻市场以寻找新的机遇。
She probes her students' understanding of the material. = 她探究学生们对材料的理解程度。


一般过去时:
The detective probed the suspect's alibi. = 侦探调查了嫌疑人的不在场证明。
The journalist probed the politician's past. = 记者调查了这位政治家的过去。


现在完成时:
Scientists have probed the mysteries of the universe for centuries. = 科学家们几个世纪以来一直在探索宇宙的奥秘。
They have probed deeply into the causes of the conflict. = 他们深入探究了冲突的根源。


过去完成时:
The investigators had probed the scene before the police arrived. = 调查人员在警方到达之前已经勘察过现场。
They had probed the issue thoroughly before making a decision. = 他们在做出决定之前已经彻底调查过这个问题。


将来时:
The team will probe the wreckage for clues. = 该小组将探查残骸寻找线索。
We will probe further into this matter next week. = 我们下周将进一步调查此事。


虚拟语气:
If they probed deeper, they might find the truth. = 如果他们更深入地调查，他们可能会发现真相。
I wish they had probed the situation more carefully. = 我希望他们更仔细地调查过这种情况。


被动语态:
The case was probed by the FBI. = 此案由联邦调查局调查。
The matter is being probed by a special committee. = 此事正由一个特别委员会调查。


疑问句:

否定句:
