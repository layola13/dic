digress:/daɪˈɡres/| vi.|离题；偏离主题；扯远了|To stray from the main subject in speaking or writing.|The lecturer digressed from his prepared speech to answer a question from the audience.  讲师从准备好的演讲中离题，回答了听众的一个问题。|近义词：deviate, wander, stray|反义词：focus, concentrate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often digresses during his lectures. = 他在讲课时经常离题。
She digresses frequently when she tells stories. = 她讲故事时经常跑题。


一般过去时:
The speaker digressed from his main point. = 演讲者偏离了他的要点。
He digressed into a discussion of ancient history. = 他扯到对古代历史的讨论上。


现在完成时:
He has digressed so much that we've lost track of the topic. = 他已经跑题太多，以至于我们都忘了主题。
She has digressed several times during her presentation. = 在她的演示过程中，她已经几次离题了。


过去完成时:
He had digressed several times before he finally returned to the main point. = 他在最终回到要点之前已经多次离题了。
They had digressed so far that nobody remembered the original question. = 他们已经离题太远，以至于没有人记得最初的问题了。


将来时:
He will likely digress again during the meeting. = 他很可能在会议期间再次离题。
She will probably digress if she sees an opportunity. = 如果她看到机会，她可能会离题。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't digressed, the meeting would have ended earlier. = 如果他没有离题，会议就会早点结束。
I wish he hadn't digressed so much. = 我希望他没有离题那么多。


被动语态:
The topic was digressed from repeatedly during the debate. = 在辩论中，话题被反复地离题。
The conversation was digressed from by the unexpected phone call. = 意外的电话打断了谈话。


疑问句:
Did he digress during his speech? = 他在演讲中离题了吗？
Does she often digress when speaking? = 她说话时经常离题吗？


否定句:
He didn't digress at all during his presentation. = 他在演讲中根本没有离题。
She didn't digress from the main topic. = 她没有偏离主题。


PHRASE->
