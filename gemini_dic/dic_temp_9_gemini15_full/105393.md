kiddishness: /ˈkɪdɪʃnəs/ | n. |孩子气；幼稚；天真 |The state or quality of being childish; silliness or immaturity.  |例句：Her kiddishness was endearing at times, but it also got her into trouble.  她的孩子气有时很可爱，但也让她惹上了麻烦。


例句：His constant kiddishness prevented him from taking his responsibilities seriously. 他持续的孩子气让他无法认真对待责任。


近义词：childishness, immaturity, silliness, puerility


反义词：maturity, adulthood, seriousness, gravitas


词源:  Derived from the word "kid," meaning a young goat or child, plus the suffix "-ishness," which creates nouns indicating a quality or state.  The word evolved to describe the behavior and characteristics associated with children.
