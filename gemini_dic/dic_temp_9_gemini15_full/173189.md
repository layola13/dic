quasi-brilliant:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈbrɪljənt/| adj.| 准杰出的；差强人意的；略显辉煌的 |Almost brilliant; showing some but not all of the qualities of brilliance.  It suggests a level of accomplishment or intelligence that falls just short of true brilliance. |例句：His quasi-brilliant plan almost worked, but ultimately fell apart due to a lack of detail.他的计划几乎成功了，但最终由于细节不足而失败了。

例句：The quasi-brilliant performance, while entertaining, lacked the depth and originality of a true masterpiece.这场演出虽然引人入胜，但却缺乏真正杰作的深度和独创性。


近义词：fairly brilliant, moderately brilliant, somewhat brilliant, exceptionally good

反义词：dull, mediocre, uninspired, poor


词源：The word "quasi" is derived from the Latin word "quasi," meaning "as if," "almost," or "somewhat."  "Brilliant" comes from the French word "brillant," meaning "shining," "bright," or "clever."
