flaring:/ˈfleərɪŋ/| adj. v.| 闪耀的；突然加剧的；突然爆发；|Suddenly burning brightly; increasing rapidly in intensity or extent.|The fire flared up suddenly.|近义词： blazing, blazing, flaring up, intensifying |反义词：diminishing, fading, waning


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun flares brightly today. = 太阳今天闪耀夺目。
The argument flares up again. = 争论再次爆发。


一般过去时:
The candle flared briefly before going out. = 蜡烛短暂地闪耀了一下然后熄灭了。
His anger flared when he heard the news. = 当他听到这个消息时，他的怒火爆发了。


现在完成时:
The situation has flared up unexpectedly. = 局势出乎意料地恶化了。
The disease has flared again in several areas. = 这疾病在几个地区再次爆发。


过去完成时:
The conflict had flared before the peace talks began. = 在和平谈判开始之前，冲突就已经爆发了。
Tensions had flared between the two countries. = 这两个国家之间的紧张关系已经加剧了。


将来时:
The volcano will flare again soon. = 火山很快会再次喷发。
I think the conflict will flare up next week. = 我认为冲突下周会爆发。


虚拟语气:
If the fire flared, we would have to evacuate. = 如果火势蔓延，我们就必须撤离。
If the economy flared, we would have lots of jobs. = 如果经济繁荣，我们将有很多工作机会。


被动语态:
The flames were flared by the strong wind. = 强风助长了火焰。
The conflict was flared up by political interference. = 冲突因政治干预而加剧。


疑问句:
Did the fire flare up last night? = 火灾昨晚是否加剧了？
Is the situation flaring up again? = 局势是否再次恶化？


否定句:
The fire didn't flare up, so we were safe. = 火势没有蔓延，所以我们很安全。
The argument didn't flare up, thankfully. = 谢天谢地，争论没有爆发。
