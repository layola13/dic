mutualism:/ˈmjuːtʃuəlɪzəm/ | n. | 互利共生；互惠互利 | A relationship between two species of organisms in which each organism benefits.  This symbiotic relationship is essential for the survival and prosperity of both species involved. For example, bees receive nectar from flowers, while simultaneously pollinating them.  | Example: The relationship between bees and flowers is a classic example of mutualism.  蜜蜂和花朵之间的关系是互利共生的典型例子。|近义词：symbiosis, cooperation | 反义词：parasitism, competition
