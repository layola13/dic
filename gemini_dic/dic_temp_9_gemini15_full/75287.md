frontbencher:/ˈfrʌntˌbɛntʃər/ | n. |（英国议会）前排议员，要员 | A member of parliament who sits on the front benches of the House of Commons or Lords, typically a senior member of the government or the opposition.  They are usually involved in policy making and debates. | Example:  The frontbenchers engaged in a heated debate about the new economic policy. 前排议员就新的经济政策展开了激烈的辩论。 |近义词：leading member, senior politician | 反义词：backbencher
