/sɪˈlɪkjʊlə/| n. |短角果|A silicula is a type of fruit in botany, specifically a short, broad silique (a type of dry fruit that splits open along two sutures).  It is typically less than three times as long as it is wide, distinguishing it from a silique which is longer than wide.  The seeds are attached to a central partition called the replum.|Example:  Many plants in the Brassicaceae family, such as some species of Arabidopsis, produce siliculas.|近义词：silique (but shorter and broader)|反义词：None specifically, but it's the opposite of a long silique.
