pauperdoms:/ˈpɔːpərdʌmz/| n. |赤贫阶层；穷人区；贫民窟 |The state of being a pauper; extreme poverty; a place inhabited by paupers.  It often refers to a system or condition of widespread poverty and social inequality.  |例句：The novel depicts the harsh realities of life in the pauperdoms of 19th-century London. 这部小说描绘了19世纪伦敦贫民窟中残酷的现实生活。


例句：The rapid industrialization led to the growth of sprawling pauperdoms around the factories.  快速的工业化导致工厂周围出现了大片的贫民窟。


近义词：slums, ghettos, shantytowns, impoverished areas


反义词：affluence, opulence, wealth, prosperity


词源:  Derived from "pauper" (a very poor person), adding the suffix "-doms" to indicate a state or condition, or a place characterized by that state.  The word ultimately traces back to the Latin word "pauper," meaning "poor" or "needy."
