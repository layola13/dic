precelebrating:/ˌpriːˈsɛləˌbreɪtɪŋ/| v. |庆祝（即将发生的事情）|To celebrate something before it actually happens.  This often implies an excessive or premature celebration. |例句：They were precelebrating their team's likely victory before the final game even began.  他们甚至在决赛开始之前就庆祝他们球队很可能会获胜。

例句：The company was precelebrating the launch of their new product, even though it hadn't been fully tested yet. 公司在他们的新产品完全测试之前就提前庆祝了新产品的发布。


近义词：anticipating, foreshadowing, preempting (in the sense of celebrating something before it's appropriate)


反义词：postponing, delaying, mourning (in the context of an event that doesn't happen)


词性：动词
