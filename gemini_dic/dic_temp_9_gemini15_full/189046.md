sabicu: /ˈsæbɪkuː/ | n. |萨比库木|A very hard, durable, and heavy wood from a tree (Lysiloma sabicu) native to the Caribbean.  It is prized for its strength and resistance to decay, making it suitable for construction, furniture, and other applications where durability is crucial. The wood is typically a reddish-brown color. |The sabicu wood was used to build the bridge's supports because of its exceptional strength and resistance to rot. |近义词： lignum vitae (similar hardness and durability)|反义词：balsa (light and soft wood)
