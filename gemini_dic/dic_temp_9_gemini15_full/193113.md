secularists:/ˈsɛkjʊlərɪsts/ | n. | 世俗主义者 |  People who advocate for the separation of religion from the affairs of the state and public life, emphasizing reason and science over religious dogma. They often promote a society where individuals are free to practice any religion or no religion without government interference. |  Many secularists believe that the state should remain neutral on matters of faith. | 近义词：nonbelievers, atheists (in some contexts), humanists | 反义词：clericalists, religious fundamentalists | 名词
