malthusianism:/mælˈθuːʒənɪzəm/| n. |马尔萨斯主义|The theory that population growth will always tend to outrun the food supply and that unless it is checked by moral restraint or disaster (such as war, famine, or disease) widespread poverty and misery are inevitable.|例句：His views on overpopulation reflect a strict adherence to Malthusianism. 他的关于人口过剩的观点反映了他对马尔萨斯主义的严格遵守。|近义词：population control, demographic theory|反义词：unrestricted population growth
