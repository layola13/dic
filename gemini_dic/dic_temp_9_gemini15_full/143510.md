outgo:/ˈaʊtɡoʊ/ | n. |支出，开支 |Expenditures; money paid out.  |例句：Our monthly outgo is quite high due to the mortgage and car payments.  我们的月支出很高，因为有抵押贷款和汽车贷款。|例句：We need to carefully track our outgo to manage our finances effectively. 我们需要仔细追踪我们的支出以有效地管理我们的财务。|近义词：expenditure, expense, outlay |反义词：income, revenue, inflow


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The outgo for this project is significant. = 这个项目的支出很大。
Our monthly outgo usually includes rent and utilities. = 我们的月支出通常包括房租和水电费。


一般过去时:
Last month's outgo exceeded our budget. = 上个月的支出超过了我们的预算。
The outgo for the conference was higher than anticipated. = 会议的支出比预期的要高。


现在完成时:
Our total outgo this year has already surpassed last year's. = 我们今年的总支出已经超过了去年。
They have significantly reduced their outgo by cutting unnecessary expenses. = 他们通过削减不必要的开支大大减少了支出。


过去完成时:
By the end of the year, our outgo had reached an unsustainable level. = 到年底，我们的支出已经达到了不可持续的水平。
Before the renovations, their outgo had been much lower. = 在装修之前，他们的支出要低得多。


将来时:
Next quarter's outgo is projected to be even higher. = 下季度的支出预计会更高。
We anticipate that our outgo will increase next year. = 我们预计明年的支出将会增加。


虚拟语气:
If our outgo were lower, we could save more money. = 如果我们的支出更低，我们就能省更多钱。
I wish our outgo were less unpredictable. = 我希望我们的支出不那么难以预测。


被动语态:

The outgo for the event was fully covered by sponsorships. = 活动的支出完全由赞助商承担。


疑问句:

否定句:
