reckon:/ˈrɛkən/ | vt. vi. n.| 认为；估计；计算；猜想 | To believe or suppose something to be true; to calculate or estimate something. | 例句：I reckon he'll be late. 我认为他会迟到。 | 例句：We reckoned the journey would take about three hours. 我们估计旅程大约需要三个小时。| 近义词：believe, suppose, estimate, calculate, figure | 反义词：know, be certain


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I reckon that's the best option. = 我认为那是最好的选择。
They reckon the project will finish on time. = 他们认为项目会按时完成。

一般过去时:
She reckoned she had enough money. = 她认为她有足够的钱。
We reckoned the distance incorrectly. = 我们错误地计算了距离。

现在完成时:
I've reckoned the total cost. = 我已经计算出了总成本。
They've reckoned without the cost of materials. = 他们没有计算材料的成本。

过去完成时:
He had reckoned on a larger crowd. = 他预料会有更多的人。
She had reckoned without the traffic. = 她没有考虑到交通状况。

将来时:
I reckon I'll be tired tomorrow. = 我认为我明天会很累。
We reckon the repairs will be expensive. = 我们估计维修费用会很高。

虚拟语气:
I would reckon it's a good deal if the price were lower. = 如果价格低一些，我认为这是一个好交易。
If I had reckoned correctly, I would have arrived earlier. = 如果我计算正确，我本可以早点到达。

被动语态:
The distance was reckoned to be ten miles. = 距离被估计为十英里。
The cost of the project is being reckoned now. = 项目的成本正在计算中。

疑问句:
Do you reckon he'll come? = 你认为他会来吗？
What do you reckon the weather will be like tomorrow? = 你认为明天的天气会怎么样？

否定句:
I don't reckon that's true. = 我认为那不是真的。
They didn't reckon with the opposition. = 他们没有考虑到反对意见。


PHRASE->
reckon with:  处理；对付；考虑到
reckon on: 依赖；指望；预期
reckon without: 没有考虑到；忽略
reckon up: 计算；总结
reckon something to be: 认为某事是


词源:
The word "reckon" comes from the Old Norse word "rekna," meaning "to calculate" or "to count."  It entered Middle English and evolved into its modern form.  The shift in meaning from purely numerical calculation to including estimation and belief is a natural semantic development.
