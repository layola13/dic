over-talked:/ˌoʊvərˈtɔːkt/ | v. | 说的太多；喋喋不休 | To talk excessively or for too long; to dominate a conversation.  |  例句：I over-talked at the meeting and didn't let others contribute. 我在会议上说了太多话，没有让其他人参与。


例句：He over-talked during the presentation, losing the audience's attention. 他在演示过程中说得太多，失去了听众的注意力。


近义词：monopolize the conversation, dominate the conversation, gabble, chatter excessively, ramble


反义词：be silent, be brief, listen attentively


词性：动词

词源:
The word "over-talked" is formed by combining the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") with the past tense of the verb "talk."  The root "talk" comes from Old English *talian, meaning "to speak".  The prefix "over-" has Germanic roots, also appearing in other languages.
