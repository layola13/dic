siestas:/siˈestɑːz/ | n. |午睡；小睡 | A siesta is a midday nap, especially one taken in a hot climate.  It's a period of rest or sleep taken during the hottest part of the day, typically in the afternoon. | 例句：Many people in Spain take siestas during the hottest part of the day.  许多西班牙人在一天中最热的时候午睡。

例句：The afternoon siesta left her feeling refreshed. 午睡让她感觉神清气爽。


近义词：nap, rest, doze | 反义词：insomnia, wakefulness


词源:  The word "siesta" comes from the Latin word "sexta," meaning "sixth," referring to the sixth hour of the day (around noon) when the siesta was traditionally taken in ancient Rome.  Over time, the word evolved through Spanish and other Romance languages to its current form.
