infinitive:/ɪnˈfɪnətɪv/ | adj. n. |不定式 |The infinitive is the base form of a verb, usually preceded by "to" (e.g., to run, to eat, to be).  It functions as a noun, adjective, or adverb. |例句： I want to go home. (noun)  He has a lot of work to do. (adjective) She is too tired to walk. (adverb)|近义词：verb, base form |反义词：finite verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I want to eat dinner. = 我想吃晚饭。
They plan to travel to Europe. = 他们计划去欧洲旅行。

一般过去时:
She decided to leave early. = 她决定早点离开。
He hoped to win the race. = 他希望赢得比赛。

现在完成时:
I have tried to contact him. = 我试着联系他了。
They have promised to help us. = 他们答应帮助我们。

过去完成时:
She wanted to have finished the report before the meeting. = 她想在会议前完成报告。
He intended to have gone to the party. = 他本来打算去参加派对。

将来时:
We are going to visit our grandparents. = 我们打算去看望我们的祖父母。
They will try to finish the project on time. = 他们将会努力按时完成项目。

虚拟语气:
It is important to remember your promise. = 记住你的承诺很重要。
It would be nice to have a vacation. = 有个假期会很棒。

被动语态:
The book is difficult to understand. = 这本书很难理解。
The problem needs to be solved immediately. = 这个问题需要立即解决。

疑问句:
What do you want to do? = 你想做什么？
Where did you plan to go? = 你计划去哪里？

否定句:
I don't want to go to the party. = 我不想去参加派对。
He decided not to tell her the truth. = 他决定不告诉她真相。
