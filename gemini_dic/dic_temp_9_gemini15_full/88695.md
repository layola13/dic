hobbist:/ˈhɒbɪst/| n. |业余爱好者 |A person who engages in a hobby.  Someone who pursues a pastime or avocation, often with enthusiasm and dedication, but not professionally. |He's a keen bird-watching hobbiest. 他是一位热衷观鸟的业余爱好者。|enthusiast, amateur, devotee |professional, expert


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a skilled model airplane hobbiest. = 他是一位技艺精湛的模型飞机爱好者。
She collects stamps as a hobby. = 她把集邮当作爱好。


一般过去时:
He was a serious stamp collector in his youth. = 他年轻时是个认真的集邮者。
She participated in many craft fairs as a hobbyist. = 她作为一名业余爱好者参加了许多工艺品展览会。


现在完成时:
He has been a dedicated woodcarver for many years. = 他多年来一直是一位专注的木雕爱好者。
She has developed exceptional skills in knitting. = 她已经练就了精湛的编织技巧。


过去完成时:
Before he retired, he had been a passionate photographer for decades. = 在他退休之前，他几十年来一直是一位充满激情的摄影师。
She had already completed several intricate embroidery projects before starting on her latest one. = 在开始她的最新作品之前，她已经完成了几个复杂的刺绣项目。


将来时:
He will continue his pottery hobby in retirement. = 他将在退休后继续他的陶艺爱好。
She will attend a painting workshop to improve her skills. = 她将参加绘画工作坊来提高她的技能。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would be a more enthusiastic photographer. = 如果我有更多时间，我会成为一个更热情的摄影家。
I wish I could dedicate more time to my painting hobby. = 我希望我能将更多时间投入到我的绘画爱好中。


被动语态:
His latest woodworking project was admired by many. = 他的最新木工作品受到许多人的赞赏。
Her intricate crochet work is often featured in local craft shows. = 她的复杂的钩针编织作品经常在当地的工艺品展览会上展出。


疑问句:

否定句:

She does not have much time for her hobbies these days. = 她现在没有太多时间用于她的爱好。
