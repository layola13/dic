inebriate:/ɪnˈbriːeɪt/ | vt. n. adj.| 使醉；醉汉；醉的 | To make drunk; a person who is drunk; intoxicated. |例句：The party inebriated many of the guests.  派对让许多客人喝醉了。

The strong wine inebriated him quickly.  烈酒很快让他喝醉了。


近义词：intoxicate, inebriate, besot, fuddle | 反义词：sober


词性:动词(vt.)，名词(n.)，形容词(adj.)


词源:  The word "inebriate" comes from the Latin word "inebriare," which means "to intoxicate" or "to make drunk."  It's ultimately derived from the prefix "in-" (in, into) and "ebrius," meaning "drunk."


用法说明:

一般现在时:
The wine inebriates him easily. 酒很容易让他醉。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:
They had been inebriated before the accident. 事故发生前他们已经喝醉了。
She had inebriated herself with champagne. 她已经用香槟把自己灌醉了。

The inebriate driver caused the accident. 醉酒司机导致了这场事故。
