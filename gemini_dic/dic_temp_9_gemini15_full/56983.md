double-crosser:/ˈdʌbəlkrɒsər/ | n. |背叛者，两面派 | A person who betrays someone who trusts them.  This word emphasizes the act of betrayal, often involving deception and a breach of trust.  The "double" aspect suggests a betrayal on two fronts, or a betrayal of a betrayal. | Example: He was a double-crosser, betraying both his allies and his enemies. 他是个两面派，既背叛了他的盟友，也背叛了他的敌人。|近义词：traitor, betrayer, deceiver, backstabber |反义词：loyal friend, trustworthy person |名词
