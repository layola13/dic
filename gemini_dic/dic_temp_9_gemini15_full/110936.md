lippiest: /ˈlɪpiːst/ | adj. | 最厚嘴唇的；最爱说闲话的 | Most characterized by full lips; most given to gossiping or idle talk. |  例句：She was the lippiest girl in the whole school, always spreading rumors. 她全校最爱嚼舌根，总是到处散布谣言。


例句：His lippiest remark of the evening was about the boss's new haircut. 他当晚最刻薄的一句话是关于老板的新发型的。


近义词：gossipiest, chattiest, most talkative | 反义词：most taciturn, most silent


词源:  Derived from "lip" (meaning the fleshy border of the mouth) + "-iest" (superlative suffix).  The meaning evolved from simply having full lips to implying talkativeness, often associated with gossiping.  This is a similar semantic shift as seen with words like "mouthy".
