legislator:/ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪtər/| n. |立法者；议员|A legislator is a person who makes laws, typically a member of a parliament, congress, or other legislative body.  Legislators are responsible for creating, debating, and voting on laws that govern a country, state, or region. They represent the interests of their constituents and work to shape public policy.|Example: The legislators debated the new environmental protection bill for hours. 立法者就新的环境保护法案进行了数小时的辩论。 The state legislators voted to increase the minimum wage. 州议员投票决定提高最低工资。|近义词：lawmaker, representative, congressman, senator, assemblyman|反义词：constituent (in the sense of being the one governed, not the one governing)
