ebonics:/ˈɛbəˌnɪks/| n. |非标准黑人英语|A variety of English spoken by some African Americans, characterized by its distinctive phonological, grammatical, and lexical features.  It is often viewed as a dialect rather than a separate language.  The term itself is controversial, with some finding it pejorative.|例句：The study analyzed the grammatical structures found in ebonics. 这项研究分析了非标准黑人英语中的语法结构。|近义词：African American Vernacular English (AAVE), Black English Vernacular (BEV)|反义词：Standard English
