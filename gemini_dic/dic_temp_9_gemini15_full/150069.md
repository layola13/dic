palsy:/ˈpælsɪ/ | n. |麻痹；瘫痪 | A loss of muscle function, often affecting one side of the body.  It can be caused by various neurological conditions.  The term often implies weakness and lack of coordination rather than complete paralysis. | Example: The stroke left him with a slight palsy in his left arm.  中风导致他的左臂轻微瘫痪。|近义词：paralysis, paresis |反义词：full motor function
