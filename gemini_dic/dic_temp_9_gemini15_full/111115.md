literally:/ˈlɪtərəli/ | adv. |确实地；逐字地；字面上地 |The word literally means "in a literal manner,"  implying that something is true without exaggeration or figurative language.  It can also emphasize the truth of a statement, sometimes ironically. |例句：He literally jumped for joy. 他欣喜若狂。（字面意思）
例句：I'm literally starving. 我真的饿死了。（夸张用法）|近义词：exactly, precisely, actually |反义词：figuratively, metaphorically

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He literally runs a marathon every weekend. 他确实每周都跑马拉松。
She literally shines when she's on stage.  她站在舞台上时真的闪闪发光。


一般过去时:
I literally fell off my chair when I heard the news. 我听到这个消息时真的从椅子上摔了下来。
He literally froze in fear. 他真的吓得僵住了。


现在完成时:
I have literally eaten everything on the plate. 我把盘子里的东西都吃光了。
She has literally worked herself to the bone. 她真的累坏了。


过去完成时:
I had literally never seen anything like it before. 我以前真的从未见过这样的事情。
They had literally just left when the storm hit. 暴风雨来临时，他们才刚刚离开。


将来时:
I will literally faint if I see a spider. 如果我看到蜘蛛，我肯定会晕倒。
We will literally be working around the clock. 我们将彻夜工作。


虚拟语气:
If I literally fell into a money pit, I would be rich! 如果我真掉进一个装满钱的坑里，我就富了！
I wish I literally had a time machine. 我真希望我有一台时光机。


被动语态:
The book was literally translated from French. 这本书是逐字翻译自法语的。
The instructions were literally followed, and the machine still didn't work. 说明书上的指示是逐字照做的，但机器还是没修好。


疑问句:
Did you literally mean what you said? 你刚才说的是字面意思吗？
Does he literally believe that? 他真的相信吗？


否定句:
It wasn't literally raining cats and dogs.  天上并没有真的下猫下狗。
I didn't literally mean to insult you. 我并非有意要侮辱你。


PHRASE->
literally true = 确实属实
literally speaking = 严格来说
take something literally =  认真对待某事
literally impossible =  绝对不可能
literally blown away =  彻底震惊
