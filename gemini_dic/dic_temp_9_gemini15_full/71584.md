flashmobbing:/ˈflæʃˌmɒbɪŋ/| n. |快闪族活动，快闪行动| A flash mob is a large group of people who assemble suddenly in a public place, perform an unusual and pre-arranged action for a brief period, and then quickly disperse.|例句：The flash mob performed a synchronized dance routine in the town square. 快闪族在城镇广场表演了同步舞蹈。
例句：Organizers used social media to coordinate the flash mob.组织者用社交媒体来协调快闪活动。|近义词：sudden gathering, surprise performance |反义词：planned event, long-term activity
