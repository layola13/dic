settling: /ˈsɛtlɪŋ/ | v. n. adj. | 解决；定居；沉淀；沉降；安顿 |

英文解析:
As a verb, "settling" means to come to rest or to reach an agreement or conclusion.  As a noun, it refers to the act of settling or the state of being settled. As an adjective, it describes something that is settled or in the process of settling.

例句:
Verb (解决):
The dispute was finally settled after weeks of negotiation. = 经过几周的谈判，争端终于解决了。

The dust settled slowly after the explosion. = 爆炸后灰尘慢慢沉降。


Verb (定居):
They are settling in a new country. = 他们正在一个新的国家定居。

My family settled in this town many years ago. = 我的家人多年前就定居在这个镇上了。


Noun (解决):
The settling of the boundary dispute was a major accomplishment. = 解决边界争端是一项重大成就。

The quick settling of the account was appreciated. = 帐户快速结清深受好评。


Noun (沉淀):
The settling of the sediment is crucial to the winemaking process. = 沉淀物的沉降对酿酒过程至关重要。

There's a lot of settling at the bottom of the tank. = 水箱底部有很多沉淀物。


Adjective (安顿好的):
The newlyweds are happily settled in their new home. = 新婚夫妇幸福地安顿在新家。

After a long journey, they were glad to be settled for the night. = 长途跋涉之后，他们很高兴能安顿下来过夜。


近义词:
Verb: resolving, resolving, establishing, establishing,  subside, precipitate (for the noun sense of settling)
Noun: resolution, agreement,  sediment (for the noun sense of settling)
Adjective: established, fixed, stable


反义词:
Verb:  disrupting, agitating, unsettling, rising (for the opposite of settling down)
Noun:  conflict, unrest, agitation
Adjective: unsettled, unstable, turbulent
