inferencing:/ˈɪnfərənsɪŋ/| n.| 推理，推论 |The process of drawing conclusions based on evidence and reasoning.  It involves using available information to make educated guesses or predictions. |Example:  The detective used inferencing to determine the suspect's whereabouts.  侦探运用推理来确定嫌疑人的下落。|近义词：reasoning, deduction, conclusion, implication |反义词：fact, certainty


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She is inferencing the meaning from the context. = 她正在根据上下文推断意思。
We are inferencing the author's intentions. = 我们正在推断作者的意图。

一般过去时:
He inferred the answer from the clues. = 他从线索中推断出答案。
They inferred the cause of the accident. = 他们推断出事故的原因。

现在完成时:
She has inferred that he is lying. = 她已经推断出他在撒谎。
We have inferred a pattern from the data. = 我们已经从数据中推断出一个模式。

过去完成时:
They had inferred that the project would fail. = 他们已经推断出该项目将会失败。
She had inferred the truth from his hesitant reply. = 她从他犹豫的回答中推断出了真相。

将来时:
He will infer the meaning from the nonverbal cues. = 他将从非语言线索中推断出意思。
We will infer the results from the experiment. = 我们将从实验中推断结果。


虚拟语气:
If I had more information, I would infer the solution more easily. = 如果我有更多信息，我会更容易推断出解决方案。
I wish I could infer the future. = 我希望我能推断未来。

被动语态:
The meaning was inferred from the context. = 意思是从上下文中推断出来的。
The author's intentions were inferred by the readers. = 作者的意图被读者推断出来。

疑问句:
What can you infer from these facts? = 你能从这些事实中推断出什么？
How did she infer the solution? = 她是怎么推断出解决方案的？

否定句:
I cannot infer any meaning from this passage. = 我无法从这段文字中推断出任何意思。
He didn't infer the correct answer. = 他没有推断出正确的答案。
