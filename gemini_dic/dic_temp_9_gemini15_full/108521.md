layship: /ˈleɪʃɪp/ | n. | 敷设舰；铺设船 | A ship designed and equipped to lay submarine cables or pipelines.  A layship is a specialized vessel used for the underwater installation of cables, pipelines, or other infrastructure.  It typically carries and deploys large reels of cable or pipe, and has sophisticated equipment for controlling the laying process. | Example: The layship carefully maneuvered into position to begin laying the new fiber optic cable.  敷设船小心地驶入位置，开始铺设新的光纤电缆。| 近义词：cable-laying ship, pipeline-laying vessel | 反义词：无
