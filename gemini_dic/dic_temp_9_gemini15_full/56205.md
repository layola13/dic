dodging:/ˈdɒdʒɪŋ/ /ˈdɑːdʒɪŋ/| v.|躲避；回避；闪躲 |To move quickly and suddenly to one side in order to avoid being hit by something.  |例句：He was dodging cars in the street. 他在街上躲避车辆。


例句：The boxer skillfully dodged his opponent's punch. 拳击手巧妙地躲过了对手的拳击。


近义词：evading, avoiding, escaping, sidestepping


反义词：facing, confronting, encountering


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dodges questions skillfully. 他巧妙地回避问题。
She dodges the traffic. 她躲避交通。


一般过去时:
He dodged the falling brick. 他躲开了掉落的砖头。
She dodged a speeding car. 她躲开了一辆飞驰的汽车。


现在完成时:
He has dodged the issue for too long. 他回避这个问题太久了。
She has dodged responsibility for her actions. 她一直逃避对她行为的责任。


过去完成时:
He had dodged the reporters all day. 他一整天都在躲避记者。
She had dodged the bullet, luckily.  幸运的是，她躲过了一劫。


将来时:
He will dodge the responsibility. 他将会逃避责任。
She will dodge the incoming attack. 她将会躲避即将到来的攻击。


虚拟语气:
If I had been quicker, I could have dodged the blow. 如果我更快一点，我就能躲开那一击。
I wish I had dodged that question. 我真希望我当时回避了那个问题。


被动语态:
The question was dodged by the politician. 这个问题被政治家回避了。
The ball was dodged successfully. 球被成功地躲开了。


疑问句:
Did he dodge the blow? 他躲开了那一击吗？
Can you dodge the incoming projectile? 你能躲开飞来的炮弹吗？


否定句:
He didn't dodge the question. 他没有回避这个问题。
She couldn't dodge the responsibility. 她无法逃避责任。
