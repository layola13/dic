homographic:/ˌhoʊməˈɡræfɪk/ | adj. | 同形异义的 |  Having the same spelling but different meanings and often different origins.  Words that are homographic are called homographs.  The term often implies that the words also have different pronunciations. |  Example: The word "bank" can refer to a financial institution or the land alongside a river. These two meanings of "bank" are homographic. | 近义词:polysemous (but polysemous implies related meanings) | 反义词:  N/A (the opposite would be words with different spellings and meanings)
