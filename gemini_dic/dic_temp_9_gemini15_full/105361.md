kicking-off:/ˈkɪkɪŋ ɔːf/| v. |开始，启动|To begin or start something, often with energy and enthusiasm.  It often implies a forceful or lively beginning.|例句：The festival is kicking off with a spectacular fireworks display.  节日以壮观的烟火表演拉开序幕。

The project is kicking off next Monday.  项目将于下周一启动。

|近义词：starting, commencing, initiating, launching|反义词：ending, concluding, finishing|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The band is kicking off their tour in London. = 乐队将在伦敦开始他们的巡演。
We kick off the meeting with a prayer every Monday. = 我们每周一都以祈祷开始会议。


一般过去时:
The game kicked off at 3 p.m. = 比赛在下午3点开始。
They kicked off the celebrations with a speech. = 他们以一篇演讲拉开了庆祝活动的序幕。


现在完成时:
The renovations have kicked off, and the house is already looking different. = 装修已经开始了，房子看起来已经不一样了。
They have kicked off a new marketing campaign. = 他们已经启动了一个新的营销活动。


过去完成时:
The director had kicked off the meeting before I arrived. = 我到达之前，导演已经开始了会议。
By the time we got there, the party had already kicked off. = 当我们到达那里时，派对已经开始了。


将来时:
The concert will kick off at 8 p.m. sharp. = 音乐会将于晚上8点准时开始。
We will kick off the fundraising campaign next month. = 我们下个月将启动筹款活动。


虚拟语气:
If we kicked off the project earlier, we might have finished by now. = 如果我们早点开始项目，现在也许就完成了。
I wish we had kicked off the meeting on time. = 我真希望我们按时开始会议。


被动语态:
The meeting was kicked off by the CEO. = 会议由首席执行官启动。
The project was kicked off with great fanfare. = 该项目在盛大的宣传中启动。


疑问句:
When does the sale kick off? = 促销活动什么时候开始？
What time does the marathon kick off? = 马拉松什么时候开始？


否定句:
The negotiations didn't kick off until late afternoon. = 谈判直到下午晚些时候才开始。
The meeting didn't kick off because of the delay. = 由于延误，会议没有开始。
