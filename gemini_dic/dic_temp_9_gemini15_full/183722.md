resampling:/ˌriːˈsæmplɪŋ/ | n. | 重采样，再抽样 | The process of repeatedly drawing samples from a dataset to obtain a more stable or robust estimate of a statistic or to assess the variability of a statistic.  It's commonly used in statistical inference and machine learning to reduce bias and variance, improve the accuracy of models, and create more reliable results. | Example:  Resampling techniques like bootstrapping are frequently used to estimate the confidence intervals of a parameter.  重采样技术如自助法常用于估计参数的置信区间。


Example: We used resampling to improve the accuracy of our model by reducing overfitting. 我们使用重采样来提高模型的准确性，减少过拟合。

近义词: bootstrapping, jackknifing, cross-validation | 反义词:  N/A (no direct antonym, but the opposite would be using a single sample without any resampling) | 词性: 名词
