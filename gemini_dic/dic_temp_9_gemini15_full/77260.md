gastropoda:/ɡæsˈtrɒpədə/| n. |腹足类动物|Gastropods are a large class of mollusks that includes snails, slugs, and limpets. They are characterized by a single shell (in most cases) and a muscular foot used for locomotion.  They are found in a wide variety of habitats, from terrestrial to marine environments.|Example:  Snails and slugs are examples of gastropods.|近义词:mollusk (a broader term)|反义词:None specifically, but could be contrasted with other mollusk classes like bivalves or cephalopods.
