factionalism: /ˈfækʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. |派系主义；派系斗争 |The existence of factions within a group or organization, often leading to conflict and division.  Factionalism can hinder progress and effectiveness.  It often involves competing for power, resources, or influence within the group. |The rise of factionalism within the party led to its eventual downfall.  派系主义的兴起导致了该党的最终垮台。 |partisanship, sectarianism, division |unity, harmony, cooperation |名词
