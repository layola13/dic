prespeculations:/ˌpriːˌspekjʊˈleɪʃənz/ | n. | 预先推测；事先猜测 | Speculations made before having sufficient evidence or information.  It often involves anticipating future events or outcomes based on incomplete data or assumptions.  | Example:  The prespeculations about the upcoming election were rife with partisan bias.  这次即将到来的选举的预先推测充满了党派偏见。 | 近义词：preconceptions, anticipations, forecasts, predictions | 反义词：postulations (based on evidence), certainties | 名词
