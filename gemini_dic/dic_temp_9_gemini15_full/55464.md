distort:/dɪˈstɔːrt/| vt.|扭曲；歪曲；曲解|To change the shape, appearance, or sound of something so that it is strange or unnatural.|The funhouse mirrors distorted our reflections.  哈哈镜扭曲了我们的倒影。|The media often distorts the truth.媒体经常歪曲事实。|twist, warp, falsify|straighten, clarify, correct|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He distorts the facts to support his argument. 他歪曲事实来支持他的论点。
The heat distorts the image on the screen. 热量使屏幕上的图像变形。

一般过去时:
She distorted the meaning of the poem. 她曲解了这首诗的意思。
The accident distorted the car's frame. 事故扭曲了汽车的车架。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:
