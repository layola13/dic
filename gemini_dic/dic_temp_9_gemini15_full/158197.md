platooned:/pləˈtuːnd/| v.|编队行进，排成纵队|To move or be arranged in a platoon or platoons.  This word often describes military formations, but can also be used more generally to describe things moving in a coordinated, linear fashion.|例句：The soldiers platooned through the narrow street. 士兵们排成纵队穿过狭窄的街道。
The cyclists platooned along the highway.  骑自行车的人们在高速公路上排成纵队行驶。|近义词：formed, lined up, arranged|反义词：scattered, dispersed|


USAGE->

The soldiers platoon through the streets every morning. = 士兵们每天早上都排队穿过街道。
The cars platoon along the highway during rush hour. = 在高峰时间，汽车在高速公路上排成一列。

词源:
From "platoon," a military unit, ultimately from French *peloton, a diminutive of *pelote, a ball of thread, referring to a small, closely knit group.
