hakas:/ˈhɑːkɑːs/| n.|哈卡舞|A haka is a traditional Māori war cry or dance performed by the indigenous Māori people of New Zealand.  It is a powerful and expressive display, often used in ceremonial contexts, such as welcoming guests or celebrating victories.  The haka involves vigorous movements, chanting, and facial expressions.  |Example: The All Blacks performed a powerful haka before the rugby match.  新西兰全黑队在橄榄球比赛前表演了强有力的哈卡舞。|近义词：war dance, ceremonial dance|反义词：None
