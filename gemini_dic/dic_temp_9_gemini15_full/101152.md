inveterate:/ɪnˈvɛtəreɪt/| adj.|根深蒂固的；积习难改的；习惯性的| firmly established and difficult or impossible to change; habitual.  |例句：He was an inveterate gambler and lost a fortune. 他是个积习难改的赌徒，输掉了家产。

例句：Her inveterate optimism never failed her, even in the face of adversity.  她根深蒂固的乐观主义从未让她失望，即使面对逆境也是如此。


近义词：habitual, chronic, ingrained, confirmed, dyed-in-the-wool


反义词：occasional, infrequent, sporadic, transient


词源：From Late Latin *inveteratus, past participle of inveterare "to make old," from in- "in" + veterāre "to grow old."  The original sense was "very old, long-established," which evolved to its current meaning.
