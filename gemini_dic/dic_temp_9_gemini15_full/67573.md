extrapolators:/ɪkˈstræpəleɪtərz/| n. |推断者；外推法|Those who or that which extrapolates; a method or technique for estimating values beyond the range of known data.  Extrapolation is the process of inferring unknown values from known values. Extrapolators use existing data to make predictions about future trends or conditions. |Example:  The scientists used sophisticated extrapolators to predict the future climate change based on current data. 科学家们利用复杂的推断方法，根据现有数据预测未来的气候变化。


Example: Statistical extrapolators are frequently used in financial modeling to forecast future market behavior. 统计推断法经常用于金融建模以预测未来的市场行为。


近义词: predictors, forecasters, projectionists, estimators|反义词: interpolators (focuses on values within known data range)|词性:noun
