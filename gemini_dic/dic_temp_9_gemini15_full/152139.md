patiences: /ˈpeɪʃəns/ | n. |耐心；忍耐力 | The ability to accept delay or suffering without complaint, or to continue in the face of obstacles.  |例句：He showed remarkable patience in dealing with the difficult children. 他在处理那些难缠的孩子时表现出了非凡的耐心。

例句：Her patience finally wore thin after waiting for three hours.  在她等了三个小时后，她的耐心终于耗尽了。


近义词：tolerance, forbearance, endurance, perseverance | 反义词：impatience, intolerance


词源:  From Old French *pacience, from Latin patientia, from pati "to suffer".


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has great patience. = 他很有耐心。
She shows patience with her students. = 她对学生很有耐心。

一般过去时:

They had the patience to finish the project. = 他们有耐心完成这个项目。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:
He had already lost his patience before she arrived. = 在她到达之前他已经失去了耐心。
They had patiently waited for hours. = 他们耐心地等了好几个小时。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
