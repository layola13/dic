lieutenants:/ˈluːtɛnənts/| n. |中尉，副官|A lieutenant is a commissioned officer in the armed forces, ranking below a captain.  They often lead platoons or companies and are responsible for the training and welfare of their troops.  The term can also refer to a person deputized to act on behalf of another, especially in a position of authority. |例句：He was promoted to lieutenant after two years of exemplary service. 他在服役两年表现优异后被晋升为中尉。

例句：The general's lieutenant handled the press conference in his absence.  将军的副官在他缺席的情况下主持了新闻发布会。


近义词：second-in-command, deputy, officer


反义词：commander, superior


词性: noun
