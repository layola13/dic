oblige:/əˈblaɪdʒ/ | vt. | 迫使；施恩惠于；使感激 | To force or require (someone) to do something; to do a kindness or service for (someone). | 例句：The law obliges companies to provide pension schemes. 法律迫使公司提供养老金计划。
The store obliged me by accepting my return. 商店好心地接受了我的退货。


近义词：compel, force, require, favor | 反义词：refuse, deny


词源:
源自古法语 *obliger，最终源自拉丁语 obligāre，意思是“绑定”，由ob-（“在...之上”）和ligāre（“绑定”）构成。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rules oblige us to wear uniforms. = 规定要求我们穿制服。
Does the contract oblige you to pay the full amount? = 合同是否迫使你支付全部款项？


一般过去时:
The judge obliged the defendant to testify. = 法官迫使被告作证。
He obliged her with a ride home. = 他好心地送她回家。


现在完成时:
The new law has obliged many businesses to change their practices. = 新法律已迫使许多企业改变其做法。
She has obliged me with her help countless times.  = 她无数次地帮助过我。


过去完成时:
The court had obliged him to pay a large fine. = 法院已迫使他支付巨额罚款。
They had obliged him with a loan in the past. = 他们过去曾借钱给他。


将来时:
The government will oblige all citizens to register. = 政府将迫使所有公民登记。
I will oblige you with an answer tomorrow. = 我明天会给你答复。


虚拟语气:
If the situation obliged, I would resign. = 如果情况所迫，我会辞职。
I wish he would oblige me with a song. = 我希望他能为我唱首歌。


被动语态:
We were obliged to leave early. = 我们被迫早退。
He was obliged to accept the offer. = 他被迫接受了这个提议。


疑问句:
Are we obliged to follow these instructions? = 我们必须遵守这些指示吗？
Is she obliged to attend the meeting? = 她必须参加会议吗？


否定句:
I am not obliged to help him. = 我没有义务帮助他。
They were not obliged to pay any extra fees. = 他们不需要支付任何额外费用。


PHRASE->
oblige somebody to do something: 迫使某人做某事
be obliged to somebody: 感激某人
feel obliged to do something: 觉得有义务做某事
under an obligation to do something: 有义务做某事
much obliged: 非常感谢
