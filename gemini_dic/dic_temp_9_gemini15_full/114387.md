make a hole in: /meɪk ə hoʊl ɪn/ | phrasal verb | 在…上打洞；对…造成损害 | To create a hole in something, or to significantly damage or impair something. | 例句：The kids made a hole in the fence with a stick. 孩子们用棍子在栅栏上打了个洞。

The storm made a hole in the roof.  暴风雨在屋顶上砸了个洞。


近义词：pierce, puncture, damage, impair | 反义词：repair, mend, fix


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They make a hole in the wall every year for the festival. 他们每年都在墙上凿一个洞来庆祝节日。
The constant rain makes a hole in my budget.  持续的雨水使我的预算出现了缺口。


一般过去时:
He made a hole in his savings account to buy a new car. 他掏空了储蓄账户买了一辆新车。
The burglars made a hole in the bank vault.  窃贼在银行金库上凿了个洞。


现在完成时:
The termites have made a hole in the floor joists. 白蚁蛀蚀了地板梁。
The drought has made a hole in the farmer's profits. 干旱使农民的利润大受损失。


过去完成时:
The mice had made a hole in the bag of flour before we noticed.  在我们注意到之前，老鼠已经在面粉袋上打了个洞了。
The company had made a hole in its reputation before the CEO stepped down. 在CEO下台之前，公司已经声誉受损。


将来时:
They will make a hole in the ground to plant the tree.  他们会在土里挖个坑种树。
The new project will make a hole in our schedule. 新项目会打乱我们的日程安排。


虚拟语气:
If he made a hole in that wall, he would be in big trouble. 如果他在这堵墙上打洞，他会惹上大麻烦。
