guardrails:/ˈɡɑːrdreɪlz/ | n. |防护栏，安全措施 |Protective barriers, typically along the edge of a road, platform, or other elevated area, designed to prevent falls or accidents.  They can also refer to rules or guidelines designed to prevent negative outcomes.|例句：The guardrails along the cliff prevented the car from falling.  悬崖边的防护栏阻止了汽车坠落。

The company implemented new guardrails to prevent financial fraud.  公司实施了新的防范措施以防止财务欺诈。|近义词：barriers, railings, safeguards, protections |反义词：hazards, risks, dangers |词性:名词
