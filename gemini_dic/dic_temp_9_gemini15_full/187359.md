词源:
The word "rod-like" is a compound adjective formed from the noun "rod" and the suffix "-like," indicating resemblance.  "Rod" itself has Old English origins, ultimately stemming from Proto-Germanic roots referring to a pole or stick.  The "-like" suffix is a common English formation creating adjectives signifying similarity.
