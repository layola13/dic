insatiableness:/ˌɪnˈseɪʃəbəlnəs/| n. |贪得无厌；不知足；无法满足|The insatiableness of his ambition led to his downfall.  他的野心贪得无厌，最终导致了他的垮台。|近义词：insatiability, voracity, greed|反义词：satisfaction, contentment, satiety|


英文解析:The state or quality of being insatiate; an inability or unwillingness to be satisfied.  It refers to an extreme and unending desire or appetite, often for something that cannot be fully obtained.

例句:

His insatiableness for power was evident in his relentless pursuit of the presidency. 他对权力的贪得无厌在他对总统职位的执着追求中显而易见。

The insatiableness of the human appetite for knowledge is a driving force behind scientific progress. 人类对知识的无尽渴望是科学进步的驱动力。


词源:

* **in-**:  prefix indicating negation or lack of. (否定前缀)
* **satis-**: from Latin *satis*, meaning "enough." (来自拉丁语satis，意思是“足够”)
* **-able**: suffix making adjectives. (形容词后缀)
* **-ness**: suffix forming nouns from adjectives, indicating a state or quality. (名词后缀，表示状态或品质)


因此，insatiableness字面意思是“缺乏满足的状态”。
