mumbling:/ˈmʌmblɪŋ/| n. v.|含糊不清地说；咕哝；低声抱怨 |The act of speaking indistinctly and unclearly, often in a low voice. It can also refer to the sound of such speech. |例句：His mumbling made it difficult to understand him.  他的咕哝声使人难以理解他。

His constant mumbling annoyed everyone in the room. 他不断的咕哝惹恼了房间里的每个人。


近义词：murmuring, grumbling, muttering |反义词：articulating, enunciating|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He mumbles his words. = 他含糊不清地说。
She mumbles to herself. = 她自言自语。


一般过去时:
He mumbled a reply. = 他含糊地回答。
She mumbled something under her breath. = 她低声咕哝着什么。


现在完成时:
He has mumbled his way through the entire speech. = 他含糊不清地完成了整个演讲。
She has mumbled apologies all day. = 她整天都在含糊地道歉。


过去完成时:
He had mumbled his agreement before realizing his mistake. = 他在意识到自己的错误之前含糊地表示同意。
She had mumbled the answer, making it hard to hear. = 她含糊地回答，让人难以听清。
