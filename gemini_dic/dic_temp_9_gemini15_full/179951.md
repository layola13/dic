referendums:/ˌrɛfəˈrɛndəmz/| n. |全民公决；公投| A referendum is a direct vote in which an entire electorate is invited to vote on a particular proposal.  This is typically used to make a decision on a specific policy question.  Referendums can be binding, meaning the government must act according to the result, or non-binding, offering guidance but not obligating action. |The government held a referendum on the proposed new constitution.  政府就拟议的新宪法举行了公投。

The citizens voted in favor of the referendum to increase the minimum wage. 公民投票赞成提高最低工资的公投。|plebiscite, plebiscites|election, vote|n.
