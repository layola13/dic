USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, grammatical tenses (present, past, future, etc.) and voice (active/passive) are not applicable.  The word itself expresses a quality or state.


PHRASE->
a compendium of knowledge:  知识的概要
a compendious history:  简明的历史
a compendious account:  简要的叙述
compendious in style:  风格简洁的
lacking compendiousness: 缺乏简洁性


词源:
The word "compendiousness" derives from the Latin word "compendium," meaning "summary" or "abridgment."  The suffix "-ness" is added to form the noun signifying the quality of being compendious.
