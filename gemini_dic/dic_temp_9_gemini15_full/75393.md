frozenness:/ˈfroʊzənəs/| n. |冻结状态；僵硬；呆滞 |The state of being frozen;  a feeling of stiffness, rigidity, or numbness, either physically or emotionally. It can refer to a lack of movement, expression, or feeling.  |例句：The frozenness of her expression revealed her shock.  她的表情僵硬，显示了她受到了惊吓。

例句：The frozenness in the air made it difficult to breathe.  空气中的寒气让人难以呼吸。


近义词：rigidity, stiffness, numbness, immobility, inertia


反义词：fluidity, flexibility, mobility, animation, liveliness


词源:源自动词freeze的派生词，加上名词后缀-ness。freeze本身来源于古英语的 *frēosan，意思是“冻结”。


USAGE->
(由于frozenness本身是名词，没有动词变化，以下列举名词的用法)

单数名词：
The frozenness of the lake was impressive.  湖面的冰冻景象令人印象深刻。
Her frozenness was a barrier to communication. 她的冷漠阻碍了沟通。


复数名词(罕见，通常指多次出现或不同类型的冻结状态):
The various frozennesses of the different regions were studied. 不同地区的各种冰冻状态都得到了研究。(此例相对生僻)


所有格：
The frozenness of the situation worried everyone.  局势的僵局让每个人都担心。


介词短语：
Because of the frozenness of the ground, planting was delayed. 由于地面的冻结，种植工作被推迟了。


PHRASE->
(由于frozenness本身词义比较特殊，以下列举一些与“冰冷，僵硬”相关的短语，并非frozenness的直接搭配)
a sense of frozenness: 一种冰冷的感觉
break the frozenness:打破僵局
overcome the frozenness: 克服僵硬
emotional frozenness:情感上的麻木
physical frozenness:身体上的僵硬
