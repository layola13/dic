costs:/kɒsts/ /kɔːsts/ | n. | 成本，代价 | The costs of the project were higher than expected.  这个项目的成本高于预期。| expenses, expenditures, price | benefits, profits | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The project costs a lot of money. = 这个项目花费很多钱。
What does this dress cost? = 这件衣服多少钱？


一般过去时:
The tickets cost $50 each. = 每张票价50美元。
The renovation cost us a fortune. = 装修花掉了我们一大笔钱。


现在完成时:
The project has cost us more than we anticipated. = 这个项目已经花费了我们比预期更多的钱。
How much has this trip cost so far? = 到目前为止这次旅行花了多少钱？


过去完成时:
By the time we finished, the project had cost us far more than we'd budgeted. = 等我们完成时，这个项目已经花费了比我们预算多得多的钱。
They had cost themselves a lot of time and resources. = 他们已经浪费了大量的时间和资源。


将来时:
The new car will cost a lot of money. = 新车将会花费很多钱。
The repairs will cost more than we initially thought. = 维修费用将比我们最初想的要多。


虚拟语气:
If the project cost less, we would have been able to proceed. = 如果项目成本更低，我们就能继续进行。
I wish the renovations hadn't cost so much. = 我真希望装修费用没有这么高。


被动语态:
The expenses were carefully cost-analyzed. = 成本被仔细分析过。
The total cost was miscalculated. = 总成本被误算了。


疑问句:
How much do these items cost? = 这些东西多少钱？
What are the total costs involved? = 总成本是多少？


否定句:
The project didn't cost as much as we thought. = 这个项目没有我们想的那么多钱。
The repairs didn't cost anything. = 维修什么也没花。


PHRASE->

cost-effective = 成本效益高的
opportunity cost = 机会成本
hidden costs = 隐性成本
the cost of living = 生活成本
marginal cost = 边际成本
