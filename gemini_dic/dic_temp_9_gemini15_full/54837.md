This word is an adjective and thus does not have verb conjugations.  The following examples illustrate its use in different sentence structures.

肯定句:

否定句:

PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly using "dispiteous". The word itself is relatively uncommon in modern English.  It's more likely to be found in older literature.

词源:
The word "dispiteous" is derived from the Old French word "despiteux," which combines the prefix "de-" (meaning "away from" or "without") with "piteous," meaning "full of pity."  Therefore, "dispiteous" literally means "without pity."
