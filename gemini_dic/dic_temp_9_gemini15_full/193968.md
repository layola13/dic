self-exaggerations:/ˌsɛlf ɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃənz/ | n. | 自夸；自我吹嘘；言过其实 |  Self-exaggerations are statements or actions that inflate one's own importance, abilities, or accomplishments beyond reality.  They often stem from insecurity or a desire for admiration.  | Example: His self-exaggerations about his business success were quickly exposed as lies. 他关于自己事业成功的夸夸其谈很快就被揭穿是谎言。 | 近义词：boasting, braggadocio, hyperbole, overstatement | 反义词：understatement, modesty, humility | 名词
