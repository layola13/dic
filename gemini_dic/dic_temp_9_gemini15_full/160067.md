polyxenic: /ˌpɒliˈzɛnɪk/ | /ˌpɑːliˈzɛnɪk/ | adj. | 多样的；多种生物共存的 |  Relating to or characterized by the presence of many different kinds of organisms or species, especially in a particular environment or habitat.  It often implies a complex and interacting community. | 例句：The polyxenic culture of the soil supported a wide range of invertebrates.  土壤的多样性培养支持了各种各样的无脊椎动物。


The polyxenic nature of the rainforest ecosystem makes it incredibly biodiverse. 雨林生态系统的多样性使其生物多样性极其丰富。

近义词：diverse, heterogeneous, multifarious | 反义词：monoculture, uniform, homogenous | 词性：adjective


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms commonly used.  Therefore, tense conjugations, active/passive voice, etc., are not applicable in the same way as with verbs.  The usage largely revolves around describing the diversity of a system.


例句：

一般陈述句:  The meadow exhibited a polyxenic community of plants and insects.  这片草地展现出多种植物和昆虫组成的群落。
The researcher studied the polyxenic microbial community in the gut. 研究人员研究了肠道中多种微生物群落。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly incorporating "polyxenic".  Its usage is primarily scientific or ecological.

词源:
From Greek πολύς (polys, "many") + ξένος (xenos, "stranger, guest, foreigner"), referring to many different kinds of organisms living together.
