invisigothic: /ˌɪnvɪzɪˈɡɒθɪk/ | adj. |  隐秘哥特式的；隐晦哥特式的 |  A term coined to describe works that exhibit characteristics of Gothic literature or art, but in a subtle or understated way, often masked by other genres or styles.  It suggests a hidden or underlying Gothic influence, rather than an overt display. | Example: The novel, while ostensibly a romance, possesses an invisigothic undercurrent of dread and suspense. 这部小说表面上是浪漫的，但却暗藏着令人恐惧和悬念的哥特式暗流。 | 近义词: subtly Gothic, understatedly Gothic | 反义词: overtly Gothic, explicitly Gothic
