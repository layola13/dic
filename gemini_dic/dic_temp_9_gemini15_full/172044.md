puritan:/ˈpjʊərɪtən/| n. adj.|清教徒；清教徒的；极端严谨的|A Puritan is a member of a group of English Protestants of the late 16th and 17th centuries who regarded the Reformation of the Church of England under Elizabeth as incomplete and sought to simplify and regulate forms of worship.  They emphasized a strict moral code and believed in predestination. As an adjective, it describes something relating to or characteristic of Puritans, often implying strictness, austerity, or moral severity. |例句：The Puritans fled England to escape religious persecution. 清教徒逃离英格兰以逃避宗教迫害。

The Puritan ethic influenced American culture for generations. 清教徒伦理影响了美国文化几代人。

近义词：Protestant, ascetic, strict, austere|反义词：libertine, hedonist, lax, indulgent
