shy:/ʃaɪ/| adj.|害羞的，腼腆的；躲避的|Feeling or showing nervousness or timidity in the company of other people.  It can also describe something that is difficult to see or find.|例句：She was too shy to speak to him. 她太害羞，不敢和他说话。

She was shy about showing her paintings. 她不好意思展示她的画作。|近义词：bashful, timid, reserved, coy|反义词：outgoing, bold, confident, uninhibited


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is shy around strangers. = 他在陌生人面前很害羞。
She is shy of making new friends. = 她不太敢交新朋友。


一般过去时:
He was shy as a child. = 他小时候很害羞。
She was shy to ask for help. = 她不好意思寻求帮助。


现在完成时:
He has always been shy. = 他一直都很害羞。
She has become less shy since joining the drama club. = 自从加入戏剧社后，她变得不那么害羞了。


过去完成时:
He had been shy all his life until he met her. = 他一生都很害羞，直到遇到她。
She had been shy about her talent before the competition. = 比赛前，她对自己的才能感到很害羞。


将来时:
He will be shy at the party. = 他在派对上会很害羞。
She will likely be shy when she gives the presentation. = 她做演示的时候可能会很害羞。


虚拟语气:
If he weren't shy, he would have asked her out. = 如果他不那么害羞，他就会约她出去了。
I wish I weren't so shy when meeting new people. = 我希望在遇到新人的时候不要那么害羞。


被动语态:  (Shy is rarely used passively)  This aspect is not applicable for this adjective.


疑问句:
Is he shy? = 他害羞吗？
Are you shy of commitment? = 你害怕承诺吗？


否定句:
He is not shy at all. = 他一点也不害羞。
She is not shy about expressing her opinions. = 她毫不羞于表达自己的意见。


PHRASE->
shy away from = 躲避；回避
too shy to = 太害羞而不能
feel shy about = 对…感到害羞
be shy of = 缺乏；不足
shy glance = 偷偷一瞥


词源:

The word "shy" comes from Old English *scēo "shy, timid," of uncertain origin.  Some etymologists suggest a connection to Old Norse *skjuga "to hide," implying a sense of hiding or withdrawing from others.  However, a definitive etymology is still debated.
