dam:/dæm/ | n. vt. | 水坝；阻拦；抑制 | A dam is a barrier constructed to contain or control the flow of water.  It can be used to create reservoirs for water storage, generate hydroelectric power, or prevent flooding.  As a verb, it means to obstruct or restrain. |


例句：
The Hoover Dam is a massive concrete structure. 胡佛水坝是一个巨大的混凝土结构。

They dammed the river to create a lake. 他们筑坝拦河，形成一个湖泊。


近义词：barrier, dike, weir, obstruct, hinder
反义词：open, release, free


词性: 名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dam holds back the river.  水坝拦住了河流。
They dam the river every year. 他们每年都筑坝拦河。


一般过去时:
They built a dam last year. 他们去年建了一座水坝。
The old dam broke during the storm.  暴风雨期间旧水坝破裂了。


现在完成时:
They have built many dams in this region. 他们在该地区建造了许多水坝。
The dam has held back the floodwaters. 水坝阻挡了洪水。


过去完成时:
The dam had already broken before the rescue team arrived.  救援队到达之前，水坝已经决堤了。

将来时:
They will build a new dam next year.  他们明年将建造一座新的水坝。
The dam will likely fail if the rainfall continues.  如果降雨持续，水坝很可能会失效。


虚拟语气:
If they had built a stronger dam, the flood would not have been so bad. 如果他们建造一个更坚固的水坝，洪水就不会那么严重了。
I wish they had dammed the river sooner.  我希望他们早点拦河筑坝。


被动语态:

否定句:
