prepersuasion: /ˌpriːpərˈsweɪʒən/ | n. | 预先劝说；事前劝服 | The act of influencing someone's attitudes or beliefs before they are exposed to a persuasive message.  It aims to create a receptive audience for a subsequent persuasive attempt.  It involves techniques to prepare the ground for later persuasion, making the target more likely to accept the main message. | Example: The marketing campaign used prepersuasion techniques to create positive associations with the brand before launching the main advertising campaign.  The company sent out free samples and positive reviews prior to launch. | 近义词:  priming, predisposition, conditioning | 反义词: dissuasion, counter-persuasion |  词性: 名词
