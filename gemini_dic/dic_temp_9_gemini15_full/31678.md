chauffer:/ʃoʊˈfɜːr/| n. v.|司机；（尤指专职）驾驶员；为…开车；|A chauffeur is a person employed to drive a private car for someone.  The word often implies a high level of skill and professionalism. |例句：He hired a chauffeur to drive him to the airport. 他雇了一个司机开车送他去机场。

例句：The chauffeur expertly navigated the busy city streets. 司机熟练地驾车穿过繁忙的城市街道。


近义词：driver,  personal driver|反义词：passenger|词性:noun and verb

词源:
The word "chauffeur" comes from the French word "chauffer," meaning "to heat." This is because early automobiles were powered by steam, and the driver's job often involved managing the steam engine's boiler.  The word was adopted into English in the early 20th century.
