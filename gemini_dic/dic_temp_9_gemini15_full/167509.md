previolate:/ˌpriːˈvaɪəleɪt/ | v. | 侵犯；违反；亵渎 | To violate beforehand or in anticipation; to preempt or infringe upon something before it happens.  It implies a proactive or anticipatory violation, rather than a reaction to an existing situation.  The word is relatively rare and often used in legal or highly formal contexts. | 例句：The company was accused of previolating the patent by starting production before the patent was officially granted. 公司被指控在专利正式批准前就开始生产，从而侵犯了专利权。

The government's actions were seen as a previolation of citizens' rights. 政府的行为被视为侵犯公民权利的行为。


近义词：anticipate (in a negative sense), preempt, infringe upon, encroach upon | 反义词：respect, uphold, protect
