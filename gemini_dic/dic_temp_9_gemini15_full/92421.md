hypomagnesaemia:/ˌhaɪpəʊˌmæɡniːˈsiːmiə/| n. |低镁血症 |A condition characterized by abnormally low levels of magnesium in the blood.  This can be caused by various factors, including malnutrition, malabsorption, alcoholism, and certain medications. Symptoms can range from mild muscle cramps to more serious cardiac arrhythmias. | The patient presented with hypomagnesaemia, manifesting as muscle weakness and tremors.  这位病人出现低镁血症，表现为肌肉无力和震颤。|Magnesium deficiency, hypomagnesemia |Hypermagnesaemia
