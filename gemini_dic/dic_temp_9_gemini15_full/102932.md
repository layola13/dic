jaywalks:/ˈdʒeɪwɔːks/| v.|非法穿越马路，闯红灯|To cross a road illegally, without using a pedestrian crossing.  This usually implies disregarding traffic signals or pedestrian crossing rules.|例句：He jaywalked across the busy street, narrowly avoiding a car. 他在繁忙的街道上非法穿越马路，险些被车撞到。


例句：It's dangerous to jaywalk, especially in heavy traffic. 在车流密集的路段非法穿越马路很危险。


近义词：cross illegally, disregard traffic signals,  dash across the road


反义词：use pedestrian crossing, obey traffic laws


词性:动词

词源:
The word "jaywalk" is a blend of "jay" (meaning a foolish or stupid person) and "walk."  It emerged in the early 20th century as automobiles became more common, highlighting the perceived foolishness of pedestrians ignoring traffic rules.
