showmanship: /ˈʃoʊmənʃɪp/ | n. | 演艺才能；表演技巧；炫技；卖弄；哗众取宠 | The skill and artistry of a performer or entertainer, often involving impressive displays and a captivating stage presence.  It can also refer to the skill in presenting something effectively, even if it's not a performance. |

例句：

His showmanship was undeniable; the audience was captivated from beginning to end. = 他的表演才能毋庸置疑；观众从头到尾都被迷住了。

The politician's showmanship helped him win over the crowd. = 这位政治家的表演技巧帮助他赢得了群众的支持。


近义词：performance, artistry, flair, spectacle, presentation

反义词：amateurishness, clumsiness, ineptitude


词源:

The word "showmanship" is a compound word formed from "showman" + "-ship".

"Showman" itself combines "show" (meaning a public display or performance) and "-man" (a suffix indicating a person who does something).

"-ship" is a suffix that denotes skill, condition, or quality, often indicating a certain level of expertise or mastery.
