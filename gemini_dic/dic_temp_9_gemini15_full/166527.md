presbyopics:/ˌprɛzbiˈɒpɪks/ | n. | 老视眼；老花眼 |  Presbyopics refers to the condition of presbyopia, a gradual loss of your eyes' ability to focus on nearby objects.  It's a natural part of aging, usually starting around age 40. |  Many people with presbyopics need reading glasses to see clearly up close. | 近义词：presbyopia, age-related farsightedness | 反义词：None (it's a condition, not something with a direct opposite) | 名词
