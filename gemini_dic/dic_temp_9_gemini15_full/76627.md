galloping:/ˈɡæləpɪŋ/ | adj. v. |飞奔的；疾驰的；迅速的 |  Describing something moving or happening at a very fast pace, often in a forceful or uncontrolled manner.  It can refer to the gait of a horse, but also metaphorically to the rapid progress or development of something. | 例句：The horse was galloping across the field.  这匹马正在田野里飞奔。

The economy is experiencing galloping inflation. 经济正在经历飞速上涨的通货膨胀。 | 近义词：racing, surging, rapid, swift | 反义词：slow, sluggish, creeping


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gallops his horse across the plains. = 他骑着马在平原上飞奔。
The inflation rate is galloping. = 通货膨胀率正在飞速上涨。


一般过去时:
The soldiers galloped into battle. = 士兵们飞奔着冲向战场。
The news of her success galloped through the town. = 她成功的消息迅速传遍了小镇。


现在完成时:
The horse has galloped many miles today. = 这匹马今天已经跑了许多英里。
Inflation has galloped out of control. = 通货膨胀已经失控。


过去完成时:
They had galloped ahead of the others. = 他们已经跑到其他人前面了。
The company had galloped to success in just a few years. = 这家公司在短短几年内就取得了巨大的成功。


将来时:
He will gallop his horse to victory. = 他将骑着马奔向胜利。
The price of oil will gallop upward. = 油价将会飞涨。


虚拟语气:
If I had a horse, I would gallop across the countryside. = 如果我有一匹马，我会在乡间飞奔。
If the economy continued galloping, we would face a crisis. = 如果经济继续飞速发展，我们将面临危机。


被动语态:
The news was galloped to every corner of the world. = 这个消息传遍了世界的每一个角落。 (Note: This usage is less common)
The project was galloped through without proper planning. = 这个项目仓促完成，没有进行适当的规划。(Note: This usage is less common and implies a rushed and potentially negative connotation)


疑问句:
Is the horse still galloping? = 马还在飞奔吗？
Is the inflation rate galloping uncontrollably? = 通货膨胀率是否在不受控制地飞涨？


否定句:
The horse did not gallop; it walked slowly. = 马没有飞奔；它慢悠悠地走着。
The negotiations did not gallop forward as planned. = 谈判没有像计划的那样顺利进行。

词源:
From the Middle English word *galopen*, ultimately from the Old Norse word *galopa*, meaning "to run".  The modern English word retains the sense of swift, energetic movement, often associated with the distinctive gait of a horse.
