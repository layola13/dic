nonmovably:/ˌnɒnˈmuːvəbli/ | adj. |不可移动地，固定地 |  Incapable of being moved; fixed in place.  This describes something that is firmly attached and cannot be easily shifted or relocated.  |例句：The monument was nonmovably fixed to its pedestal. 这座纪念碑牢固地固定在基座上。

The heavy machinery was nonmovably installed in the factory floor. 这台重型机械固定安装在工厂车间的地板上。|近义词：immovably, fixedly, steadfastly |反义词：movably, loosely, detachably |词性:形容词


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and doesn't have tenses like verbs.  It describes the state of being unmovable.  Therefore, typical verb tenses are not applicable.  Instead, we can show its use in different sentence structures.


Declarative Sentences:
The boulder was nonmovably lodged in the mountain crevice.  巨石不可移动地卡在山缝里。

The bridge's central pillar is nonmovably anchored to bedrock. 桥梁的中心柱牢固地锚定在基岩上。


Interrogative Sentences:
Is the equipment nonmovably installed? 设备是固定安装的吗？

Are these components nonmovably attached? 这些组件是不可移动地连接的吗？


Negative Sentences:
The sign wasn't nonmovably fixed; it could easily be moved. 这个标志没有固定好；很容易移动。

The satellite dish is not nonmovably positioned; it's adjustable. 卫星天线不是固定定位的；它是可调节的。


PHRASE->
There aren't common phrases directly using "nonmovably."  Its usage is typically within a descriptive sentence.  However, we can show example phrases where the concept of immovability is expressed:


firmly fixed: 牢固固定

词源:
The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adverb "movably" (from "movable," meaning "capable of being moved").  Therefore, "nonmovably" literally means "not movably."
