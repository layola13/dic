exterminating: /ɪkˈstɜːrmɪneɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 消灭；根除；灭绝 |  The act of destroying something completely, especially pests or a particular group of people.  This is the present participle form of the verb "exterminate." |

例句：

The government is implementing a new program for exterminating rats in the city. = 政府正在实施一项新的计划来消灭城市里的老鼠。

The farmer is exterminating weeds in his field. = 农民正在清除田里的杂草。


近义词：annihilating, eradicating, destroying, eliminating, wiping out

反义词：preserving, conserving, protecting, fostering


词性：动词 (Verb)

词源:

The word "exterminating" comes from the Latin words "ex-" (out, from) and "terminus" (end, boundary, limit).  The combination suggests the complete removal or ending of something.  The "-ating" suffix indicates the present participle, showing an ongoing action.
