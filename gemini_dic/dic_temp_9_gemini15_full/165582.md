premeditation:/ˌpriːˌmɛdɪˈteɪʃən/| n. |预谋，预先考虑|The act of planning or plotting a crime or other wrongful act in advance.  It implies a deliberate and conscious decision to commit the act, with time taken to consider the consequences.|例句：The murder was committed with premeditation, making it a more serious crime.  这起谋杀案是蓄谋已久的，因此罪行更为严重。

例句：The judge considered the premeditation in sentencing the defendant.  法官在判决被告时考虑到了预谋因素。


近义词：forethought, planning, plotting, deliberation |反义词：impulsiveness, spontaneity
