interventionists:/ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃənɪsts/| n. |干涉主义者，介入主义者 |Those who favor intervention, especially in the affairs of other countries.  People who believe in or advocate for intervention, particularly in political or economic matters. |例句：The interventionists argued for military action to prevent the humanitarian crisis. 干涉主义者主张采取军事行动以阻止人道主义危机。

例句：Many interventionists believe that international organizations should play a larger role in resolving global conflicts. 许多干涉主义者认为国际组织应该在解决全球冲突中发挥更大的作用。


近义词：interferers, meddlers, busybodies |反义词：non-interventionists, isolationists


词源:
From intervention (n.) + -ist (suffix denoting a person who does something).  "Intervention" itself comes from the Latin *intervenire*, meaning "to come between," from *inter* ("between") + *venire* ("to come").
