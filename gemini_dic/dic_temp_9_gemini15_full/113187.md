lures:/lʊərz/| n. |诱饵；诱惑物；吸引人的事物 |Something that attracts or entices someone or something, often to a place or situation that is dangerous or harmful.  It can be a physical object used in fishing or hunting, or a more metaphorical enticement. |例句：The fisherman used shiny lures to attract the fish. 渔夫用闪亮的诱饵来吸引鱼。

The bright lights of the city were lures for the young people from the countryside. 城市的明亮灯光吸引着来自农村的年轻人。|近义词：bait, temptation, attraction, enticement |反义词：repellent, deterrent


USAGE->

虚拟语气:

词源:
The word "lure" ultimately derives from the Old French word "leurre," meaning "decoy" or "bait."  This, in turn, has roots in the Proto-Germanic word *lauzō, meaning something like "to lie in wait" or "ambush."  The connection lies in the idea of something used to trap or attract prey.
