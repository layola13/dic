neolocally: /ˌniːoʊˈloʊkəli/ | adv. | 新居地；近居地 |  Adverb describing a residential pattern where a married couple establishes their home in a location near the wife's family. This is in contrast to patrilocality (near the husband's family) or bilocality (near both families).  | Example:  The couple chose to live neolocally, settling in a town near the wife's parents.  这对夫妇选择住在新居地，在一个靠近妻子父母的城镇定居。 | 近义词： uxorilocally (near the wife's family) | 反义词： patrilocally (near the husband's family) | 副词
