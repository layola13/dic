constitutionalisms:/ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. |宪政主义；立宪制度 | The study and practice of constitutionalism, encompassing the principles, structures, and processes of government based on a constitution.  Constitutionalism emphasizes the rule of law, separation of powers, protection of individual rights, and limitations on government power. | The rise of constitutionalism in the 18th century marked a significant shift in political thought.  | 近义词：constitutional government, limited government | 反义词：authoritarianism, totalitarianism
