miss:/mɪs/| n. vt.|想念，错过，小姐|英文解析：As a noun, it refers to a young unmarried woman. As a verb, it means to fail to hit, catch, or reach something or someone, or to fail to attend something. |例句：I miss my family. 我想念我的家人。 I missed the bus. 我错过了公共汽车。|近义词：noun: young woman, girl; verb: lose, fail to catch, omit |反义词：noun: madam, Mrs., missus; verb: hit, catch, attend


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I miss my friends. = 我想念我的朋友们。
He misses his family. = 他想念他的家人。


一般过去时:
I missed the train yesterday. = 我昨天错过了火车。
She missed the meeting last week. = 她上周错过了会议。


现在完成时:
I have missed several opportunities. = 我错过了好几个机会。
She has missed many chances. = 她错过了很多机会。


过去完成时:
I had missed the deadline before I realized it. = 我在意识到之前就已经错过了截止日期。
They had missed the turning before they noticed it. = 他们在注意到之前就已经错过了转弯处。


将来时:
I will miss the concert if I don't hurry. = 如果我不快点，我会错过音乐会。
We will miss our flight if we don't leave now. = 如果我们现在不走，我们会错过航班。


虚拟语气:
I wish I hadn't missed the party. = 我真希望我没有错过派对。
If I hadn't missed the bus, I would have been on time. = 如果我没有错过公共汽车，我就不会迟到了。


被动语态:
The opportunity was missed. = 机会错过了。
The meeting was missed by many people. = 很多人错过了会议。


疑问句:
Did you miss the game? = 你错过了比赛吗？
Did she miss her flight? = 她错过了航班吗？


否定句:
I didn't miss the train. = 我没有错过火车。
He didn't miss a single detail. = 他没有错过任何细节。
