gehenna:/ˈɡɛhɛnə/| n. |地狱，苦难之地| Gehenna refers to a valley in Jerusalem, traditionally associated with the fires of hell in Jewish and Christian traditions.  It symbolizes a place of punishment, suffering, and destruction. The word is often used figuratively to describe a place or situation of intense suffering and wickedness. |The fires of Gehenna await the wicked.  = 地狱的烈火在等待着恶人。


The term's use in the New Testament emphasizes eternal punishment.  |The rich man in the parable of Lazarus feared Gehenna. = 喇撒路比喻中的富人害怕地狱。|  hell, abyss, inferno, Tartarus |heaven, paradise | n.
