honky-tonk:/ˈhɑːŋkiˌtɑːŋkər/| n.|喧闹的乡村酒吧；乡村民谣音乐|A honky-tonk is a type of bar, typically found in the American South and Midwest, that features country music, often with a rowdy and lively atmosphere.  It is characterized by its simple, sometimes rough-around-the-edges, decor and its focus on providing a place for people to drink, dance, and listen to music. The term can also refer to the type of country music played in these establishments, often characterized by its upbeat tempo, simple instrumentation, and themes of love, loss, and working-class life.|The old honky-tonk was always packed on Friday nights.|近义词：dive bar, roadhouse, juke joint|反义词：upscale bar, elegant lounge|
