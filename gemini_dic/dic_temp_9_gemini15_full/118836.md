metaphor:/ˈmɛtəfɔːr/ | n. |隐喻，比喻 |A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable.  It involves an implicit comparison between two unlike things without using words such as "like" or "as".|Example:  He is a lion in battle. (He is brave and fierce like a lion.)|近义词：simile, analogy, allegory |反义词：literal meaning


例句:

The world is a stage, and all the men and women merely players.  = 世界是一座舞台，所有男男女女都只是演员。

Her laughter was music to his ears. = 她的笑声对他来说是天籁之音。

词源:

From Middle French *metaphore, from Latin metaphora, from Greek metaphora ("carrying over, transference"), from metapherein ("to transfer, carry over, carry from one place to another"), from meta- ("over, beyond, across") + pherein ("to bear, carry").
