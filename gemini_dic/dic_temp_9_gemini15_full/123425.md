monopoler:/məˈnɒpələr/ | n. |垄断者，独占者 | A monopoler is a person or entity that holds a monopoly, controlling the supply or trade of a commodity or service.  They often have significant market power and can influence prices and availability. | Example:  The company became a monopoler in the software market after acquiring its main competitor. 这家公司在收购主要竞争对手后，成为软件市场的垄断者。|近义词：monopolist,  trust, cartel |反义词：competitor,  rival


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company monopolizes the market. = 这家公司垄断了市场。
He tries to monopolize the conversation. = 他试图垄断谈话。


一般过去时:
Standard Oil monopolized the oil industry for many years. = 标准石油公司垄断了石油工业多年。
She monopolized her father's attention. = 她独占了她父亲的注意力。


现在完成时:
They have monopolized the technology for decades. = 他们几十年来一直垄断着这项技术。
He has monopolized the discussion since he arrived. = 自从他到达以来，他一直主导着讨论。


过去完成时:
Before the antitrust lawsuit, the company had monopolized the market. = 在反垄断诉讼之前，该公司已经垄断了市场。
She had monopolized the conversation before I could get a word in. = 我还没来得及插话，她就垄断了谈话。


将来时:
Will this company monopolize the future of AI? = 这家公司会垄断人工智能的未来吗？
They will monopolize the resources if we don't act quickly. = 如果我们不迅速行动，他们将会垄断资源。


虚拟语气:
If they monopolized the market, prices would rise sharply. = 如果他们垄断了市场，价格将会急剧上涨。
I wish they hadn't monopolized the resources. = 我真希望他们没有垄断资源。


被动语态:
The market was monopolized by a single corporation. = 市场被一家公司垄断了。
The conversation was monopolized by the speaker. = 谈话被演讲者垄断了。


疑问句:
Does this company monopolize the industry? = 这家公司垄断了这个行业吗？
Who monopolized the resources? = 谁垄断了资源？


否定句:
This company does not monopolize the market. = 这家公司没有垄断市场。
She did not monopolize the conversation. = 她没有垄断谈话。

monopoly profit: 垄断利润
