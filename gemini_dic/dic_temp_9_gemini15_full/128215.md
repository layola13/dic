neurochemical:/ˌnʊroʊˈkɛmɪkl/| n. |神经化学物质|A neurochemical is a chemical substance that is produced and used by the nervous system to transmit messages between neurons or from neurons to other cells.  Neurochemicals are crucial for various physiological processes, including mood regulation, cognition, and behavior.|Example: Dopamine is a key neurochemical involved in reward and motivation. 多巴胺是一种参与奖赏和动机的重要神经化学物质。|近义词：neurotransmitter, neuromodulator |反义词：无
