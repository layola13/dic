moved-in: /ˌmuːvdˈɪn/ | adj. | 搬入的；刚搬进来的 |  Describes someone or something that has recently moved into a place.  It's a past participle used as an adjective. |例句：The moved-in family are still unpacking their boxes. 刚搬进来的这家人还在整理箱子。 The moved-in furniture looks brand new. 刚搬进来的家具看起来崭新。|近义词：newly settled, recently arrived |反义词：moved-out


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are moved-in to their new house. = 他们搬进了新房子。(This is less common and would be better expressed as "They have moved into...")
The furniture is moved-in. = 家具已经搬进来了。(This is less common and usually implies something is already in place)


一般过去时:
They moved in yesterday. = 他们昨天搬进来了。
The family moved into the apartment last week. = 这家人上周搬进了公寓。


现在完成时:
They have moved in. = 他们已经搬进来了。
She has moved into her new apartment. = 她已经搬进她的新公寓了。


过去完成时:
They had moved in before the party. = 他们在派对前就搬进来了。
She had moved into the house by the time her parents arrived. = 当她父母到达时，她已经搬进了房子。


将来时:
They will move in next month. = 他们下个月将搬进来。
We will have moved in by Christmas. = 我们会在圣诞节前搬进去。


虚拟语气:
If they had moved in earlier, they would have attended the neighborhood barbecue. = 如果他们早点搬进来，他们就会参加社区烧烤了。
I wish I had moved in sooner. = 我真希望我早点搬进来。


被动语态:
The furniture was moved in by the movers. = 家具是由搬家公司搬进来的。
The new tenants were moved in last night. = 新租户昨晚搬进来了。


疑问句:
Have they moved in yet? = 他们搬进来了吗？
When will they move in? = 他们什么时候搬进来？


否定句:
They haven't moved in yet. = 他们还没搬进来。
She didn't move in until the next day. = 直到第二天她才搬进来。


PHRASE->
moved-in date: 搬入日期
newly moved-in: 刚搬进来的
just moved in: 刚搬进来
moved in next door: 搬到隔壁
moved in with: 和...一起搬进来
