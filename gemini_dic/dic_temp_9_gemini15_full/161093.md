postdiluvian:/ˌpoʊstdaɪˈluːviən/| adj.|大洪水以后的；洪水过后的；久远的；陈旧的|Relating to or denoting the period after the biblical Deluge.  Often used figuratively to describe something old-fashioned or outdated.|例句：The postdiluvian landscape was scarred and barren. = 洪水过后的景象伤痕累累，荒凉不堪。

例句：His ideas about marketing seem rather postdiluvian in today's digital age. = 他关于营销的想法在当今的数字时代显得相当过时。


近义词：antediluvian, archaic, ancient, old-fashioned

反义词：modern, contemporary, new, recent

词性：形容词
