This word is German, not English.  There is no English word "gesellschaft".  The following analysis is for the German word *Gesellschaft*.

/ɡəˈzɛlʃaft/ | n. | 社会；公司；社团 | Gesellschaft refers to a group of people associated together for a common purpose or sharing a common characteristic.  It can refer to society as a whole, or to a specific organization or group. | Examples: Die deutsche Gesellschaft ist sehr divers. (German society is very diverse.)  Er arbeitet für eine große Gesellschaft. (He works for a large company.) | 近义词: Gemeinschaft (community), Verein (association), Firma (firm) | 反义词: Isolation, Einzelgängertum (solitude, being a loner) |  词性: Noun


USAGE->
Nominative singular: Die Gesellschaft ist modern. (Society is modern.)
Nominative plural: Die Gesellschaften sind unterschiedlich. (Societies are different.)
Accusative singular: Ich sehe die Gesellschaft. (I see society.)
Accusative plural:  Er unterstützt die Gesellschaften. (He supports the societies/companies.)
Dative singular: Ich helfe der Gesellschaft. (I help society.)
Dative plural: Er gibt den Gesellschaften Geld. (He gives money to the societies/companies.)
Genitive singular: Das Problem der Gesellschaft ist groß. (The problem of society is big.)
Genitive plural: Die Strukturen der Gesellschaften sind komplex. (The structures of the societies are complex.)

ETYMOLOGY->

The word "Gesellschaft" comes from Middle High German *geselleschaft*,  a compound of *geselle* ("companion," "fellow") and *-schaft* (a suffix denoting a group or association).  *Geselle* itself has roots in Old High German *gesello*, ultimately related to the Proto-Germanic *ga-salijō*, implying a collective or fellowship.  The suffix *-schaft* is productive in German, creating nouns denoting groups or associations (e.g., Freundschaft - friendship).  Therefore, the original meaning points to a group of companions or a fellowship.  The word's meaning has broadened over time to encompass the broader concepts of society and company.
