post-arrest:/ˌpoʊst əˈrɛst/ | adj. |逮捕后；拘留后 | Describing the period of time following an arrest.  This term is used in legal and law enforcement contexts to refer to events, procedures, or actions that occur after someone has been taken into custody. |例句：Post-arrest interrogation is a critical stage in the criminal justice process. 逮捕后的审讯是刑事司法程序中的一个关键阶段。
The suspect's post-arrest statement contradicted his earlier testimony.  嫌疑人在逮捕后的陈述与他之前的证词相矛盾。|近义词：post-detention, after-arrest |反义词：pre-arrest, before-arrest

词源:

The term "post-arrest" is a compound word formed from two elements:

* **post-**: This prefix means "after" or "following."  It's derived from Latin "post," meaning "after" or "behind."

* **arrest**: This noun refers to the act of seizing someone and taking them into custody. It originates from the Old French "arrest," meaning "to stop" or "to detain."
