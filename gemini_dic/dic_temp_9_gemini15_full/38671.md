condemnations:/ˌkɒndemˈneɪʃənz/| n.|谴责；定罪；判刑|The act of condemning something or someone; strong disapproval.  It often refers to official pronouncements of disapproval, but can also describe public outcry.|例句：International condemnations followed the terrorist attack. 国际社会对恐怖袭击事件表示谴责。
例句：The court issued a condemnation of his actions. 法院对其行为作出了谴责。|近义词：denunciations, censures, reproaches|反义词：approvals, endorsements, praises


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government issues condemnations of human rights abuses. = 政府谴责侵犯人权的行为。
Many people express their condemnations of the war. = 许多人表达了他们对战争的谴责。


一般过去时:
The judge delivered a condemnation of the defendant's actions. = 法官对被告的行为进行了谴责。
The media issued strong condemnations of the politician's speech. = 媒体对这位政治家的讲话发出了强烈谴责。


现在完成时:
The international community has issued numerous condemnations of the violence. = 国际社会已经对暴力事件发出了多次谴责。
They have received many condemnations for their unethical practices. = 他们因为不道德的行为受到了许多谴责。


过去完成时:

The company had faced numerous condemnations for its environmental damage. = 这家公司因其造成的环境破坏而面临无数谴责。

虚拟语气:

否定句:
