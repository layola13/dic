/ˈkɑːdɪtʃ/ | n. | 犹太教的悼词 | A kadish is a Jewish prayer recited in mourning for the dead.  It is a prayer of sanctification, praising God and acknowledging His sovereignty even in the face of grief.  The specific wording varies slightly depending on the context (e.g., said immediately after burial, recited at specific times during the year of mourning, etc.). | Example: The mourners recited the kadish after the burial.  哀悼者在葬礼后诵读卡迪什。 | 近义词：mourning prayer, eulogy (though not a direct synonym as a eulogy is more of a speech) | 反义词：celebratory prayer, hymn of joy |  n.
