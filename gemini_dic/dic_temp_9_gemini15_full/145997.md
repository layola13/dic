/ˌoʊvərˈdoʊmɛstɪkeɪts/ | vt. |过度驯化；过分家养|To domesticate to an excessive degree, often resulting in negative consequences for the animal or plant.  This can include loss of natural behaviors, increased susceptibility to disease, or reduced genetic diversity. |The farmer overdomesticated his chickens, making them reliant on human care and unable to survive in the wild. |驯化过度，过度家养|  underdomesticate |wild
