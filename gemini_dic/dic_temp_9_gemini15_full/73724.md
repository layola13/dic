forked:/fɔːrkt/| adj. v. n.|分叉的；岔开的；有叉的|Having two or more branches or prongs.  A fork in the road is a point where the road splits into two directions. |例句：The road forked sharply to the left.  路急转弯向左分叉。

The river forked into two smaller streams. 河流分成了两条较小的溪流。|近义词：branched, divided, split|反义词：straight, undivided, single


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The road forks ahead. = 道路在前面分叉。
He forks his food with a knife and fork. = 他用刀叉吃东西。

一般过去时:
The path forked to the left. = 小路向左分叉。
She forked the potatoes onto her plate.  = 她用叉子把土豆叉到她的盘子里。

现在完成时:
The river has forked several times. = 这条河已经分叉好几次了。
She has forked over the money. = 她已经交出了钱（俚语）。

过去完成时:
The road had forked before we realized it. = 在我们意识到之前，路就已经分叉了。
He had forked out a lot of money for the car. = 他已经为这辆车花了很多钱（俚语）。

将来时:
The trail will fork soon. = 小路很快就会分叉。
They will fork over the evidence.  = 他们将会交出证据（俚语）。


虚拟语气:
If the road forked earlier, we would have taken the other path. = 如果路早点分叉，我们就会走另一条路。
If he had forked over the money sooner, he wouldn't be in this trouble. = 如果他早点把钱交出来，他就不会有现在的麻烦了。


被动语态:
The road was forked by the construction. = 道路被施工分叉了。
The money was forked over to the authorities. = 钱被交给当局了（俚语）。


疑问句:
Does the path fork here? = 小路在这里分叉吗？
Where does the river fork? = 河流在哪里分叉？


否定句:
The road doesn't fork until further down. = 这条路直到更远的地方才分叉。
He didn't fork over the information. = 他没有提供信息。
