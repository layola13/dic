crenel:/krəˈnel/| n.|垛口，雉堞|A crenel is an opening in a battlement, typically a regularly spaced embrasure in a parapet, allowing archers or other defenders to fire upon attackers without exposing themselves.  Crenels are characteristic features of medieval fortifications.|The castle walls were topped with a series of crenels, providing the defenders with ample firing positions.|近义词：embrasure, loophole|反义词：None specifically, but the opposite concept would be a solid, unbroken wall.
