norovirus:/ˈnɔːrəʊˌvaɪrəs/ | n. |诺如病毒 | A genus of viruses that cause gastroenteritis (inflammation of the stomach and intestines).  Noroviruses are highly contagious and can spread easily through contaminated food, water, or surfaces. Symptoms typically include vomiting, diarrhea, nausea, and stomach cramps. | Example: The outbreak of norovirus at the school led to the closure of several classrooms. 学校爆发诺如病毒导致几间教室关闭。|近义词：calicivirus |反义词：无
