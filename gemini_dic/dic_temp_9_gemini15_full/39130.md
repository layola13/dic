confusedly:/kənˈfjuːzɪdli/ | adv. | 困惑地，茫然地 | in a confused manner; in a way that shows confusion or bewilderment.  | He looked around confusedly. 他困惑地环顾四周。 | bewilderedly, disorientedly | clearly, understandably |


例句：

He mumbled confusedly, unable to articulate his thoughts clearly. 他含糊不清地说，无法清晰地表达他的想法。

She stared confusedly at the complex instructions. 她困惑地盯着复杂的说明。


词源：

Derived from the adjective "confused," which comes from the past participle of the verb "confuse" (to bewilder or perplex).  The suffix "-ly" is added to create the adverbial form.  The root of "confuse" is Latin "confundere," meaning "to pour together,"  implying a mixing up or jumbling of ideas or perceptions.


用法说明:

Confusedly is an adverb that modifies a verb, describing how an action is performed. It indicates a state of mental disarray or uncertainty.  It's often used to describe actions performed in a state of confusion or bewilderment.  The adverb can be used to describe a person's demeanor, speech, or actions.
