preshaping: /ˈpriːʃeɪpɪŋ/ | v. | 预成形；预先成形 |  The act of giving something a preliminary shape or form before further processing or shaping.  It often involves preparing a material for a later, more refined shaping process. |


例句：

The preshaping of the clay ensures that the final product is uniform and consistent. = 粘土的预成形确保最终产品均匀一致。

The metal underwent preshaping before being stamped into its final form. = 金属在被冲压成最终形状之前进行了预成形。


近义词：preforming, pre-forming, preliminary shaping


反义词：finishing, final shaping


词性：动词
