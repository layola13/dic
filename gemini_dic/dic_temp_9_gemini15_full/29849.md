causative:/ˈkɔːzətɪv/| adj.| 使役的；导致的 |  relating to or denoting a verb that expresses the causing of an action or state.  A causative verb indicates that the subject of the sentence causes something to happen.  |Example:  She had her hair cut. (She caused her hair to be cut.) |近义词：effective, efficient, influential | 反义词：ineffective, powerless


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He makes his children study hard. = 他让他的孩子们努力学习。
The teacher has the students read aloud. = 老师让学生们大声朗读。

一般过去时:
She had her car repaired. = 她让人修理了她的车。
They made him confess his crime. = 他们迫使他承认了他的罪行。


现在完成时:
He has had his house painted. = 他已经让人粉刷了他的房子。
They have made significant progress. = 他们已经取得了显著的进展。(This example is less directly causative, but shows the resulting effect)

过去完成时:
She had had her book published before the conference. = 在会议之前，她已经出版了她的书。
The manager had made the decision before the meeting. = 会议之前经理已经做出了决定。(Similar to the above, the causative is less direct)

将来时:
I will have my car serviced next week. = 下周我将让人保养我的车。
They will make him apologize. = 他们会让他道歉。


虚拟语气:
If I had had more time, I would have had my essay proofread. = 如果我有更多时间，我会让人校对我的论文。
If they had made a different decision, the outcome would have been different. = 如果他们做出不同的决定，结果就会不同。(Causative is indirect in this example)


被动语态:
The work was made easy by the new machine. = 新机器使这项工作变得容易。
The children were made to clean their rooms. = 孩子们被迫打扫他们的房间。


疑问句:
Who had the house painted? = 谁让人粉刷的房子？
What made you change your mind? = 什么让你改变主意了？


否定句:
I didn't have my car washed. = 我没有让人洗我的车。
They didn't make him work overtime. = 他们没有让他加班。


PHRASE->
have something done  = 让人做某事
make someone do something = 使某人做某事
get someone to do something = 使某人做某事
cause something to happen = 导致某事发生
let someone do something = 让某人做某事
