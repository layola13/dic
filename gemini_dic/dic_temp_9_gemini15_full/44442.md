cross-saddle:/krɒsˈsædl/| n. |交叉鞍座，跨鞍|A type of saddle with a design that allows the rider's legs to be positioned on either side of the horse's body, rather than one side.  It is often used in side-saddle riding, particularly in women's equestrian events.|Example:  The cross-saddle provided a more secure and balanced riding position.  交叉鞍座提供了更安全和平衡的骑乘姿势。|近义词:side-saddle (though not exactly synonymous)|反义词:None specifically, but contrasts with a regular, "center" saddle.
