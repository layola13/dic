hyoid:/ˈhaɪɔɪd/ | n. |舌骨|The hyoid bone is a horseshoe-shaped bone situated in the anterior midline of the neck between the chin and the thyroid cartilage. It is unique because it is the only bone in the human body that does not articulate directly with any other bone; instead, it is suspended by muscles and ligaments.  It plays a crucial role in swallowing, speech, and respiration. | The hyoid bone supports the tongue and is essential for swallowing.  |近义词：None (as it's a specific bone)|反义词：None (as it's a specific bone)|名词
