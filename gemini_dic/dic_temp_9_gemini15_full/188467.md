rudists:/ˈruːdɪsts/| n. |双壳类生物（一种已灭绝的牡蛎）|Extinct, large, mostly conical, marine bivalve mollusks of the Mesozoic Era, characterized by their unusual asymmetrical shells.  They formed extensive reefs in warm, shallow seas. |例句：The Cretaceous period saw the flourishing of rudist reefs in many parts of the world.  |近义词：reef-building bivalves,  (in a broader sense) mollusks|反义词：None directly applicable.
