criticising:/ˈkrɪtɪsaɪzɪŋ/| v. |批评，指责 |The act of expressing disapproval of someone or something.  It involves pointing out faults or shortcomings. |例句：He was criticising the government's handling of the crisis. 他正在批评政府处理危机的措施。|例句：She spent the whole evening criticising her friend's new haircut. 她整个晚上都在批评她朋友的新发型。|近义词：condemning, rebuking, censuring |反义词：praising, commending, applauding |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is criticising the new policy. = 他正在批评这项新政策。
They are constantly criticising each other. = 他们不断地互相批评。

一般过去时:
She criticised his performance. = 她批评了他的表现。
We criticised the restaurant's service. = 我们批评了这家餐馆的服务。

现在完成时:
I have criticised his work several times. = 我已经批评过他的工作好几次了。
They have been criticising the company's ethics. = 他们一直在批评公司的道德规范。

过去完成时:
He had criticised her before, so she was not surprised. = 他以前就批评过她，所以她并不惊讶。
They had already criticised the plan, so there was no point in discussing it again. = 他们已经批评过这个计划了，所以再讨论也没有意义。

将来时:
She will criticise him again if he doesn't improve. = 如果他不改进，她还会再次批评他。
They will be criticising the movie all night. = 他们今晚会一直批评这部电影。

虚拟语气:
If I criticised him, he would be angry. = 如果我批评他，他会生气的。
I wish I hadn't criticised her so harshly. = 我真希望我没有那么严厉地批评她。

被动语态:
His work was criticised by the boss. = 他的工作受到了老板的批评。
The new law is being criticised by many people. = 这项新法律正受到许多人的批评。

疑问句:
Are you criticising my work? = 你在批评我的工作吗？
Why are they criticising the project? = 他们为什么批评这个项目？

否定句:
I am not criticising your ideas. = 我不是在批评你的想法。
He did not criticise her directly. = 他没有直接批评她。
