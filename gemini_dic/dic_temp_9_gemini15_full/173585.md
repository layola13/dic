quasi-importantly:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ɪmˈpɔːrtəntli/ | adv. | 几乎重要地；似是而非地重要地 |  The adverb "quasi-importantly" suggests something is presented or treated as important, but in a way that is not entirely genuine or significant. It implies a pretense of importance or a superficial display of seriousness.  | 例句：He cleared his throat and announced, quasi-importantly, the results of the meeting. 他清了清嗓子，似是而非地宣布了会议结果。  He spoke quasi-importantly about his new project, but it was just a minor task. 他一本正经地谈论他的新项目，但这只是一项小任务。 | 近义词：seemingly importantly, ostensibly importantly, superficially importantly | 反义词：unimportantly, insignificantly, trivially | 副词
