rock:/rɒk/ /rɑːk/| n., v.| 岩石；摇滚乐；石头；困境 | A hard, solid mineral substance forming part of the surface of the earth and other similar planets.| The music genre characterized by electric guitars, bass guitar, drums, and vocals.| A difficult or awkward situation. |  近义词：stone, boulder,  bramble, predicament | 反义词：soft, smooth, easy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Rocks are made of minerals. = 岩石由矿物质构成。
He rocks the baby to sleep. = 他摇晃着婴儿使其入睡。


一般过去时:
The earthquake rocked the city. = 地震震动了这座城市。
The band rocked the stadium. = 乐队在体育场进行了精彩的摇滚演出。


现在完成时:
The climbers have reached the rocky summit. = 登山者已经到达了岩石顶峰。
She has rocked the boat with her recent actions. = 她最近的行为引起了轩然大波。


过去完成时:
The house had been rocked by the explosion. = 房子已被爆炸震动了。
They had rocked the boat for weeks before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发前的几周里，他们一直制造麻烦。


将来时:
The volcano will rock the island. = 火山将震动该岛屿。
The new band will rock the charts. = 新乐队将席卷排行榜。


虚拟语气:
If I were a rock, I would be unmoved. = 如果我是一块石头，我将不动如山。
I wish I hadn't rocked the boat. = 我真希望我没有制造麻烦。


被动语态:
The boat was rocked by the waves. = 船被海浪摇晃。
The city was rocked by the news of the earthquake. = 该市被地震的消息震惊了。


疑问句:
Does this rock contain any valuable minerals? = 这块岩石含有任何有价值的矿物吗？
Will their new album rock the world? = 他们的新专辑会风靡全球吗？


否定句:
This ground is not rocky. = 这块地不是岩石地。
The music didn't rock me. = 这音乐没有打动我。
