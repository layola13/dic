high-grading:/ˈhaɪˌɡreɪdɪŋ/ | n. |高品位选择；优选；挑选优质产品 |The act of selectively choosing and removing the highest-quality items from a batch or group, often leaving behind lower-quality items. This practice is common in various industries, such as mining, where the best ore is selected first.  | 例句：The miners were accused of high-grading the best ore, leaving behind the lower-grade material.  矿工被指控挑选最好的矿石，留下低品位的矿石。

The company's policy of high-grading its products resulted in a higher profit margin. 公司挑选优质产品的政策带来了更高的利润率。| 近义词：cherry-picking, cream-skimming, selective extraction | 反义词：low-grading, random selection |  词性：名词
