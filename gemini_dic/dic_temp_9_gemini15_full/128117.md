nestorianism:/nɛˈstɔːriənɪzəm/| n. |聂斯脱里派,聂斯脱里主义|A Christological doctrine that distinguished between the divine and human natures of Christ, asserting that he possessed two distinct persons.|The Nestorian Church flourished in Persia and throughout Asia for centuries, spreading its influence to China and beyond.|近义词：monophysitism (反义词)|无
