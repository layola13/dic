intestable: /ɪnˈtɛstəbəl/ | adj. | 不可测试的，无法检验的 |  Impossible to test; incapable of being subjected to a test. This often refers to a hypothesis or theory that cannot be empirically verified or falsified.  | The theory is currently intestable due to a lack of appropriate technology.  这个理论目前由于缺乏合适的技术而无法测试。  | testable, verifiable | provable, demonstrable | 形容词


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "what".  Therefore, tense variations (present, past, future, etc.) are not applicable.  The word itself expresses a state of being.


例句1:The hypothesis is intestable because it relies on unobservable phenomena.  这个假设是不可检验的，因为它依赖于不可观察的现象。

例句2:  Many philosophical concepts are considered intestable by the scientific method.  许多哲学概念被认为是科学方法无法检验的。
