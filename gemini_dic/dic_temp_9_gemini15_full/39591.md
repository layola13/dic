consecrating: /ˈkɒnsɪkreɪtɪŋ/ | /ˈkɑːnsɪkreɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 奉献；祝圣；使神圣 | To make or declare sacred; dedicate formally to religious or sacred use.  |例句：The bishop consecrated the new church. 主教为新教堂举行了祝圣仪式。

The priest consecrated the bread and wine. 牧师将面包和葡萄酒祝圣。


近义词：dedicating, sanctifying, hallowing | 反义词：desecrating, profaning


词源:  From Old French *consecrer, from Latin consecrare, from con- "together" + sacrare "to make sacred".


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They consecrate the new building. = 他们为新建筑举行奉献仪式。
He consecrates his life to serving others. = 他将一生奉献于服务他人。


一般过去时:
The king consecrated the temple. = 国王为神庙举行了祝圣仪式。
She consecrated her time to her family. = 她把时间都奉献给了她的家人。


现在完成时:
They have consecrated the land for a park. = 他们已将这块土地奉献为公园。
He has consecrated himself to the cause of peace. = 他已将自己献身于和平事业。


过去完成时:

She had consecrated many years to her research. = 她多年来一直致力于她的研究。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
