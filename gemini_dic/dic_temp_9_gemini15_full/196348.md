serifed:/ˈsɛrɪft/| adj.|衬线体(的)|Having serifs, small decorative strokes at the ends of letterforms.  Serifed fonts are often considered more traditional and easier to read in large blocks of text.|Example: Times New Roman is a classic serifed typeface.  Times New Roman 是一种经典的衬线字体。|近义词：with serifs|反义词：sans-serif


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective describing fonts, and doesn't have verb or noun forms in common usage.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.
