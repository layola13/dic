candidest: /ˈkændɪdəst/ | adj. | 最坦率的；最直率的 | Most frank and honest; revealing the truth without reservation. |例句：He gave the candidest account of the events. 他对事件做了最坦率的描述。

例句：Her candidest criticism was surprisingly helpful. 她最坦率的批评出乎意料地很有帮助。

近义词：most frank, most open, most honest, most forthright, most straightforward

反义词：most dishonest, most deceitful, most evasive, most secretive, most disingenuous


词源:  Comes from the Latin word "candidus," meaning "shining white," "pure," or "innocent."  Over time, the meaning shifted to imply sincerity and openness, likely due to the association of white with purity and truthfulness.


用法说明:

candidest作为candid的最高级形容词，通常用于强调某人或某事达到了最坦率、最真诚的程度。它通常修饰名词，例如人、评论、陈述、照片等。


注意:  虽然"candid"本身可以作为最高级使用(例如，"the most candid"),  "candidest"在实际使用中不太常见，"most candid" 更为普遍。
