indulgent: /ɪnˈdʌldʒənt/ | adj. | 纵容的；溺爱的；放纵的 | Characterized by or given to indulgence; lenient; permissive.  Showing or characterized by a readiness or overreadiness to indulge oneself or another. |例句：Her indulgent parents let her have whatever she wanted.  她那溺爱的父母让她想得到什么就得到什么。

例句：He indulged in a rich chocolate cake after his diet. 他在节食后放纵地吃了一块丰富的巧克力蛋糕。


近义词：permissive, lenient, tolerant, complaisant, liberal | 反义词：strict, austere, severe, rigorous, demanding


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is indulgent towards his children. = 他对他的孩子很纵容。
She is always indulgent with her sweet tooth. = 她总是对自己的甜食很纵容。


一般过去时:
My parents were very indulgent when I was young. = 我小的时候，我的父母很溺爱我。
The teacher was indulgent to the late student. = 老师对迟到的学生很宽容。


现在完成时:
She has been too indulgent with her spending habits. = 她对自己的消费习惯过于放纵了。
He has been indulgent to his employees for too long. = 他对他的员工纵容太久了。


过去完成时:
They had been indulgent with their son, leading to his spoiled behavior. = 他们过去一直很溺爱他们的儿子，导致了他被宠坏的行为。
The company had been indulgent in its risk assessment, resulting in significant losses. = 公司在风险评估方面过于宽松，导致了巨大的损失。


将来时:
I will not be indulgent to his mistakes anymore. = 我不会再纵容他的错误了。
They will be more indulgent towards their staff during the holiday season. = 节日期间，他们会对员工更加宽容。


虚拟语气:
If I were more indulgent, I would let them go. = 如果我更纵容一些，我会让他们去的。
I wish I had been more indulgent during my childhood. = 我希望我童年时能更放纵一些。


被动语态:
His requests were always indulged. = 他的要求总是被满足。
The children were indulged by their grandparents. = 孩子们被他们的祖父母宠坏了。


疑问句:
Is she being too indulgent with the children? = 她对孩子们是不是太纵容了？
Are you being too indulgent with yourself? = 你对自己是不是太放纵了？


否定句:
He is not indulgent towards his employees. = 他对他的员工并不纵容。
The judge was not indulgent to the defendant. = 法官对被告并不宽容。


PHRASE->
indulgent parent = 溺爱的父母
indulgent behaviour = 放纵的行为
indulgent attitude = 纵容的态度
indulgent spending = 挥霍的消费
indulgent lifestyle = 放纵的生活方式


词源:
From Middle English *indulgent, from Old French *indulgent, from Latin *indulgent-, present participle of *indulgere (to be kind, yield, comply) from *in- (in, into) + *dulcere (to soothe, to sweeten).  The sense development reflects the idea of yielding to someone's desires or needs.
