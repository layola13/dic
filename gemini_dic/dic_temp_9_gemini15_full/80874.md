govern:/ˈɡʌvən/ | vt.  |统治；管理；支配；控制 | To rule or control a country, area, people, etc.  |The country is governed by a president.  这个国家由总统统治。|  rule, control, regulate | anarchy, chaos | verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The queen governs the country. = 女王统治这个国家。
The rules govern our behavior. = 规则规范着我们的行为。

一般过去时:
The emperor governed wisely. = 皇帝英明地统治着。
The committee governed the decisions. = 委员会决定了这些事宜。

现在完成时:
She has governed the province for ten years. = 她已经统治这个省份十年了。
They have governed the company successfully. = 他们成功地管理了这家公司。

过去完成时:
He had governed the city before the war. = 他在战争前统治过这座城市。
The board had governed the company for many years. = 董事会管理这家公司多年了。

将来时:
She will govern with fairness and justice. = 她将公平公正地执政。
They will govern the country effectively. = 他们将有效地治理国家。

虚拟语气:
If he governed fairly, the people would be happier. = 如果他公平地统治，人民会更幸福。
If the committee governed better, the outcome would be different. = 如果委员会管理得更好，结果就会不一样。

被动语态:
The country is governed by a democratically elected government. = 这个国家由民选政府统治。
The company is governed by its board of directors. = 公司由董事会管理。

疑问句:
Who governs this land? = 谁统治这片土地？
How is the company governed? = 这家公司是如何管理的？

否定句:
He did not govern well. = 他治理得不好。
The rules do not govern this situation. = 这些规则不适用于这种情况。



PHRASE->

govern oneself = 自我约束
