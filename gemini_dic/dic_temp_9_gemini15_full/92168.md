hypersophisticatedly: /ˌhaɪpərˌsɒfɪˈstɪkeɪtɪdli/ | adv. | 过分精巧地；极其复杂地 |  In an excessively sophisticated or complex manner.  The argument was presented hypersophisticatedly, making it difficult for the average person to understand.  | 近义词：elaborately, intricately, complexly | 反义词：simply, plainly, crudely


例句：

The painting was hypersophisticatedly rendered, showcasing the artist's mastery of technique.  = 这幅画的绘制极其精巧，展现了艺术家精湛的技法。


The software's interface was hypersophisticatedly designed, making it difficult for new users to navigate. = 该软件的界面设计过于复杂，使得新用户难以操作。


词源：

* **hyper-**:  prefix meaning "excessive," "over," or "beyond."  源自希腊语 ὑπέρ (hupér)，意为“超过”、“之上”。

* **sophisticated**:  adjective meaning "highly developed or complex."  最终源自拉丁语 *sophisticus*，与希腊语 σοφιστής (sophístēs)  有关，意为“智者”、“诡辩家”。  This word's evolution reflects a shift in meaning, initially implying cleverness or skill, but later gaining connotations of artificiality or complexity.


用法说明：

`hypersophisticatedly` is an adverb formed by adding the suffix "-ly" to the adjective "hypersophisticated." It describes how an action is performed, emphasizing the excessive complexity or sophistication involved.  It is not a frequently used word and often suggests an overly complex or pretentious approach.  The word carries a slightly negative connotation, implying that the complexity may be unnecessary or even detrimental.
