napping:/ˈnæpɪŋ/| v. |小睡；打盹儿|To take a short sleep, especially during the day.  |例句：I'm going to take a nap after lunch. 我午餐后要小睡一会儿。|例句：The baby napped for two hours this afternoon. 宝宝今天下午睡了两个小时。|近义词：dozing, slumbering, sleeping|反义词：being awake, staying up


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He naps every afternoon. 他每天下午都打盹。
She often naps on the sofa. 她经常在沙发上打盹。

一般过去时:
I napped for an hour yesterday. 我昨天睡了一个小时。
The children napped after their playtime. 孩子们玩耍后睡着了。

现在完成时:
I haven't napped today. 我今天还没打盹。
She has napped twice this week. 她这周已经打盹两次了。

过去完成时:
I had napped before the meeting started. 会议开始前我打了个盹。
They had napped earlier, so they were alert. 他们早些时候睡过觉了，所以很清醒。

将来时:
I will nap after work. 我下班后要打个盹。
She will nap in the car on the long drive. 长途开车时她会在车里打盹。

虚拟语气:
If I had time, I would nap. 如果我有时间，我会打个盹。
I wish I could nap right now. 我真希望现在能打个盹。

被动语态:  (Napping doesn't typically use passive voice)


疑问句:
Do you often nap? 你经常打盹吗？
Does he nap during the day? 他白天打盹吗？

否定句:
I don't usually nap. 我通常不打盹。
She didn't nap yesterday. 她昨天没打盹。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "napping" comes from the Old English word "hnappian," meaning "to snatch," "to seize," or "to take quickly."  Over time, the meaning shifted to refer to a short, quick sleep.
