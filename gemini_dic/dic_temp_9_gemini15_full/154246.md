periscopes:/ˈpɛrɪskoʊps/| n. |潜望镜|An optical instrument for observing objects which are above the level of an obstruction, such as the horizon or a wall. It uses a system of mirrors or prisms to reflect the image.  |The submarine used its periscopes to observe enemy ships.  潜艇用潜望镜观察敌舰。

The soldiers used periscopes to see over the wall. 士兵们用潜望镜越过墙观察。|近义词：sighting device, observation instrument|反义词：无|名词

词源:
The word "periscope" comes from the Greek words "peri," meaning "around," and "skopein," meaning "to look" or "to see."  Therefore, it literally means "to look around."
