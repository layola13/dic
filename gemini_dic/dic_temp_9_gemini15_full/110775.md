linguistics: /lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks/ | n. | 语言学 | The scientific study of language, including its structure, history, and social context.  Linguistics explores various aspects of language, such as phonetics, phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics, and pragmatics. | Example:  She is majoring in linguistics at university. 她大学主修语言学。

| Example:  The professor's lecture covered various branches of linguistics. 教授的讲座涵盖了语言学的各个分支。| 近义词: philology (more focused on historical aspects)| 反义词: N/A | 词性: 名词

词源:  The word "linguistics" is derived from the Latin word "lingua," meaning "tongue" or "language,"  combined with the suffix "-istics," indicating a field of study or science.  Therefore, linguistics literally means "the science of language."
