put-on:/pʊtˈɑːn/| n., v.|虚饰；伪装；戏弄；穿上；上演|

英文解析：
As a noun, "put-on" refers to something artificial or pretended, a deliberate act of deception or a display intended to impress.  As a verb, it means to place something on, such as clothing, or to stage or perform a play or show. It can also mean to deceive or trick someone.

例句：

名词用法:
His whole story was a put-on; I didn't believe a word he said. = 他的整个故事都是虚构的；我一个字也不信。

His extravagant lifestyle was just a put-on to impress his friends. = 他奢侈的生活方式只是为了给朋友们留下印象的虚张声势。


动词用法:
Please put on your coat; it's cold outside. = 请穿上你的外套；外面很冷。

The students put on a fantastic play for the school festival. = 学生们为学校节日上演了一出精彩的戏剧。


近义词:
(noun) affectation, pretense, sham, hoax, deception
(verb) don, wear, stage, perform, deceive, trick


反义词:
(noun) genuineness, authenticity, reality
(verb) take off, remove


词性:
Noun, Verb
