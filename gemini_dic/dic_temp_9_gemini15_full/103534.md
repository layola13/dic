jog:/dʒɑːɡ/ | /dʒɒɡ/ | v., n. |慢跑；轻推；轻撞；(使)轻微晃动 | to run at a steady gentle pace, especially for exercise; to push or move something gently; to knock or bump gently |例句：I jog every morning. 我每天早上慢跑。  I jogged his elbow to get his attention. 我轻轻碰了碰他的肘部以引起他的注意。|近义词：run, trot, nudge, jostle |反义词：sprint, dash


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I jog every morning. = 我每天早上慢跑。
He jogs in the park. = 他在公园慢跑。

一般过去时:
I jogged five kilometers yesterday. = 昨天我慢跑了五公里。
She jogged to the store. = 她慢跑到商店。

现在完成时:
I have jogged regularly for years. = 我多年来一直定期慢跑。
He has jogged a marathon before. = 他以前跑过马拉松。

过去完成时:
I had jogged for an hour before I felt tired. = 我慢跑了一个小时后才感到疲倦。
They had jogged ten miles when it started to rain. = 下雨时他们已经慢跑了十英里。

将来时:
I will jog tomorrow morning. = 我明天早上会慢跑。
She will jog with her friend. = 她会和她的朋友一起慢跑。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would jog more often. = 如果我有更多时间，我会更经常慢跑。
I wish I could jog a marathon. = 我希望我能跑马拉松。

被动语态: (Jog is rarely used passively)
The memory was jogged by the old photograph. = 这张旧照片唤起了他的记忆。(被动语态，但强调记忆被唤醒)
His confidence was jogged by the criticism. = 批评动摇了他的信心。(被动语态，但强调信心被动摇)


疑问句:
Do you jog regularly? = 你经常慢跑吗？
Does he jog in the mornings? = 他早上慢跑吗？

否定句:
I don't jog very often. = 我不经常慢跑。
She didn't jog yesterday. = 她昨天没有慢跑。


PHRASE->
jog one's memory = 唤起某人的记忆
jog along = 慢慢地走/进行
jog the memory = 刺激记忆
a gentle jog = 轻微的撞击
to jog somebody's elbow = 轻推某人的肘部

词源:
The word "jog" ultimately derives from Old Norse *jǫkka, meaning "to shake" or "to jolt."  This word evolved through Middle English, retaining the sense of a gentle push or movement.  The modern sense of running at a gentle pace developed later.
