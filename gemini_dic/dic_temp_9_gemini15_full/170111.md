proto-scientific:/ˌproʊtoʊsaɪənˈtɪfɪk/ | adj. | 原科学的；似科学的 |  Relating to or characteristic of a stage of development in a field of study that precedes the establishment of rigorous scientific methods and standards.  It often implies a lack of systematic methodology, verifiable evidence, or a coherent theoretical framework, but possesses some elements resembling scientific inquiry. | Example:  His theories, while interesting, were considered proto-scientific due to their lack of empirical support.  他的理论虽然很有趣，但由于缺乏经验证据，被认为是原科学的。 | 近义词：pre-scientific, quasi-scientific, para-scientific | 反义词：scientific

The early alchemists' work, while not strictly scientific, can be seen as proto-scientific in its attempts to understand matter. 早期炼金术士的工作，虽然并非严格意义上的科学，但在试图理解物质方面可以看作是原科学的。

Comparative:
Compared to modern physics, the ancient Greek's understanding of the cosmos was proto-scientific. 与现代物理学相比，古希腊人对宇宙的理解是原科学的。
