compunction:/kəmˈpʌŋkʃən/| n. |良心不安；悔恨；内疚 | A feeling of guilt or moral scruple that prevents or follows the doing of something.  It often involves regret for a past action or a sense of responsibility for something wrong. |例句：He felt no compunction about lying to the police. 他对向警方撒谎毫无愧疚感。

例句：She acted without compunction, knowing it was wrong. 她明知不对，却毫不犹豫地做了。


近义词：remorse, regret, guilt, penitence, scruple

反义词：remorselessness, unconcern, apathy


词源：Middle English: from Old French *compunction, from Latin *compunctio, from compungere ‘to prick, sting’, from com- ‘together’ + pungere ‘to prick’.
