equivocacy:/ɪˈkwɪvəki/| n. |含糊其辞；模棱两可；歧义|The use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself;  uncertainty or ambiguity of meaning.|例句：His response was full of equivocacy, leaving me unsure of his intentions.他的回答含糊其辞，让我不确定他的意图。
例句：The equivocacy of the contract led to a lengthy legal battle. 合同的含糊不清导致了漫长的法律纠纷。|近义词：ambiguity, vagueness, obscurity, uncertainty|反义词：clarity, precision, directness, explicitness|词性:名词

词源:
From Late Latin *equivocatio, from Latin equivocus ("ambiguous").  The Latin root *aequi- means "equal," and *vox means "voice". Thus the original meaning implied a voice that sounds the same way for different things.
