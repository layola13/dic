contests:/ˈkɒntests/| /ˈkɑːntests/| n.|竞赛，比赛；争夺 |A contest is an event in which people compete for victory.  It can involve skill, strength, knowledge, or other qualities. |例句：The school held a singing contest. 学校举办了一场歌唱比赛。

例句：They are in a contest for the championship. 他们在争夺冠军。


近义词：competition, rivalry, match, tournament |反义词：agreement, cooperation


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The school holds a spelling contest every year. = 学校每年都举行一次拼写比赛。
Many athletes participate in various contests. = 许多运动员参加各种比赛。


一般过去时:
She won the baking contest last year. = 她去年赢得了烘焙比赛。
The team participated in a robotics contest. = 这支队伍参加了一场机器人比赛。


现在完成时:
He has entered several photography contests. = 他已经参加了几次摄影比赛。
They have never won a national contest before. = 他们以前从未赢得过全国比赛。


过去完成时:
Before the final round, she had already won two contests. = 在决赛之前，她已经赢得了两场比赛。
By the time the event concluded, they had lost several contests. = 到活动结束时，他们已经输掉了几场比赛。


将来时:
They will participate in a dance contest next month. = 他们下个月将参加一场舞蹈比赛。
The company will sponsor a creative writing contest. = 公司将赞助一场创意写作比赛。


虚拟语气:
If I were better at chess, I would enter the contest. = 如果我的象棋水平更好，我会参加比赛。
I wish I had entered the science contest. = 我真希望我参加了科学竞赛。


被动语态:
The contest was judged by three experts. = 比赛由三位专家评判。
The winner of the contest will be announced soon. = 比赛获胜者将很快公布。


疑问句:
What kind of contest is this? = 这是什么类型的比赛？
When will the art contest be held? = 美术比赛什么时候举行？


否定句:
