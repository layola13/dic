duelings:/ˈduːəlɪŋz/| n.|决斗；互相争斗|The practice or act of engaging in duels; a series of duels.  It can also refer to a figurative contest or struggle.|例句：The duelings between the two knights were legendary.  = 这两位骑士之间的决斗是传奇般的。  The political duelings dominated the news cycle. = 政治上的互相争斗主导了新闻周期。|近义词：fights, combats, contests, skirmishes|反义词：peace, harmony, agreement


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The knights are dueling fiercely. = 骑士们正在激烈地决斗。
The politicians are constantly dueling for votes. = 政客们为了选票不断地互相争斗。


一般过去时:
They dueled at dawn. = 他们在黎明时分决斗。
The two companies dueled for market share. = 这两家公司为了市场份额而竞争。


现在完成时:
They have dueled many times before. = 他们以前决斗过很多次。
The rivals have been dueling for years over the inheritance. = 这两个竞争对手为了遗产已经争斗多年。


过去完成时:
He had dueled several times before the final encounter. = 在最后一次遭遇之前，他已经决斗过几次了。
The parties had been dueling over policy for months. = 各方就政策问题已经争论了几个月。


将来时:
They will duel at high noon tomorrow. = 他们明天中午将决斗。
The two companies will continue dueling for market dominance. = 这两家公司将继续争夺市场主导地位。


虚拟语气:
If they had dueled fairly, the outcome might have been different. = 如果他们公平决斗，结果可能会有所不同。
I wish they would stop dueling and find a peaceful solution. = 我希望他们能停止争斗并找到和平的解决办法。


被动语态:
The duel was witnessed by many. = 决斗被许多人目睹。
The argument was seen as a metaphorical dueling between the two parties. = 这次争论被视为双方之间的象征性决斗。


疑问句:

否定句:
They did not duel yesterday. = 他们昨天没有决斗。
The two parties did not duel openly, but their actions spoke volumes. = 这两方没有公开决斗，但他们的行动说明了一切。
