randomized:/ˈrændəmaɪzd/ | adj. |随机的，随机化的 |  Adjective describing something that has been subjected to randomization, a process of making something random or unpredictable.  It implies a deliberate act of introducing randomness, often to eliminate bias or increase the objectivity of a process or experiment. | Example: The participants were randomized into two groups for the study.  实验参与者被随机分成两组。  The randomized algorithm ensures fairness in the selection process. 随机算法确保选择过程的公平性。 |近义词：random, haphazard, arbitrary |反义词：deterministic, planned, systematic |


USAGE->

The computer program randomized the data. = 电脑程序对数据进行了随机化处理。
