gastrocnemius: /ˌɡæstroʊˈniːmiəs/ | n. |腓肠肌 |The gastrocnemius is a superficial two-headed muscle in the back of the lower leg. It is the most superficial muscle of the posterior compartment of the leg, and it plays a significant role in plantarflexion of the foot (pointing the toes downwards) and flexion of the knee.  The gastrocnemius muscle originates from the medial and lateral condyles of the femur (thigh bone) and inserts into the calcaneus (heel bone) via the Achilles tendon. |Example:  The runner strained his gastrocnemius during the marathon.  = 跑步者在马拉松比赛中拉伤了他的腓肠肌。

The injury to her gastrocnemius required several weeks of physical therapy. = 她腓肠肌的损伤需要几周的物理治疗。
