messing-up: /ˈmɛsɪŋ ʌp/ | v. | 搞砸，弄糟，搞乱 | To spoil or ruin something; to make a mistake; to cause disorder or confusion. |例句：He messed up the project by missing the deadline. 他因为错过最后期限而搞砸了项目。 |近义词：ruining, spoiling, botching, screwing up | 反义词：improving, fixing, perfecting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is messing up his room. = 他正在把他的房间弄乱。
They are messing up the experiment. = 他们在搞砸实验。


一般过去时:
I messed up the recipe. = 我把食谱搞砸了。
She messed up the presentation. = 她把演示搞砸了。


现在完成时:
He has messed up his life. = 他把自己的生活搞砸了。
We have messed up the garden. = 我们把花园弄糟了。


过去完成时:
I had messed up the interview before I even started. = 我甚至还没开始面试就已把事情搞砸了。
They had messed up the calculations, leading to an incorrect result. = 他们把计算搞砸了，导致结果错误。


将来时:
I will mess up the exam if I don't study. = 如果我不学习，我会把考试搞砸。
She will mess up the painting if she's not careful. = 如果她不小心，她会把画弄坏。


虚拟语气:
If I had studied harder, I wouldn't have messed up the test. = 如果我学习更努力，我就不会考试不及格了。
I wish I hadn't messed up the opportunity. = 我真希望我没有错过这次机会。


被动语态:
The project was messed up by the unexpected delay. = 项目因意外延误而被搞砸了。
The data was messed up due to a software error. = 数据由于软件错误而被搞乱了。


疑问句:
Did you mess up the assignment? = 你把作业搞砸了吗？
What did you mess up this time? = 你这次又把什么搞砸了？


否定句:
I didn't mess up the instructions. = 我没有弄错说明。
She didn't mess up the arrangement. = 她没有弄乱安排。
