communicable: /kəˈmjuːnɪkəbl/ | adj. | 可传播的；传染性的 |  Able to be transmitted from one person or organism to another, especially through direct contact or indirect means.  | 例句：Influenza is a highly communicable disease.  流感是一种高度传染性疾病。

The disease is communicable through contaminated water.  这种疾病可以通过受污染的水传播。


近义词：contagious, infectious, transmissible | 反义词：non-communicable, non-contagious


词源:  Comes from the verb "communicate," meaning "to share or exchange information," which itself originates from the Latin word "communicare," meaning "to share, to make common." The "-able" suffix indicates capability or possibility.  Therefore, "communicable" literally means "able to be communicated" or "able to be spread."
