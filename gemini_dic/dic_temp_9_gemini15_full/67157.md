expositionally:/ˌɛkspəˈzɪʃənəli/ | adv. | 解说性地；阐述性地 | In a manner that explains or sets forth; clearly and systematically.  | The professor explained the complex theory expositionally, ensuring all students understood. 教授以清晰系统的方式阐述了复杂的理论，确保所有学生都理解。 | clearly, explicitly, systematically | obscurely, vaguely, cryptically | 无


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and thus doesn't follow typical verb conjugation patterns like "what."  Its usage is primarily concerned with *how* something is explained or presented.  There are no tenses associated with its use.  The following examples show its usage in different sentence structures:

Affirmative Sentence:
The artist's statement was expositionally presented. = 艺术家的陈述被清晰地阐述了。
He explained the concept expositionally. = 他阐述了这个概念。


Negative Sentence:
The report was not expositionally written; it was confusing. = 报告写得不够清晰，令人困惑。
She didn't explain the situation expositionally. = 她没有清晰地解释情况。


Interrogative Sentence:
Was the lecture presented expositionally? = 讲座是清晰地讲解的吗？
Is this information presented expositionally? = 这个信息是清晰地呈现的吗？



PHRASE->
There are no common set phrases directly using "expositionally."  However, it can modify phrases:

Expositionally presented evidence:  清晰地呈现的证据
Expositionally clear explanation: 清晰易懂的解释
An expositionally rich narrative:  叙述详尽的故事
Expositionally detailed report:  细节详尽的报告
Expositionally sound argument:  论证严谨的论点
