fantasied:/ˈfæntəsaɪzd/| v. |幻想；梦想；凭空想象 |To have imagined or created something in one's mind, often in a vivid or detailed way.  It often implies a degree of unreality or escapism. |例句：She fantasied about living in a tropical paradise. 她幻想住在热带天堂。

She fantasied about winning the lottery. 她幻想中了彩票。


近义词：dreamed, imagined, envisioned, daydreamed |反义词：realized, experienced


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He fantasizes about becoming a famous musician. = 他幻想成为一名著名的音乐家。
She fantasizes about traveling the world. = 她幻想环游世界。

一般过去时:
I fantasized about flying when I was a child. = 我小时候幻想过飞行。
They fantasized about winning the game. = 他们幻想赢得比赛。

现在完成时:
She has fantasized about this moment for years. = 她多年来一直幻想这一刻。
He has fantasized about living in a castle. = 他一直幻想住在城堡里。

过去完成时:
I had fantasized about meeting him before I actually did. = 在我真正见到他之前，我一直幻想过和他见面。
She had fantasized about her wedding day since she was a little girl. = 从小女孩开始，她就一直幻想过她的婚礼。

将来时:
I will fantasize about my vacation all week. = 我整个星期都会幻想我的假期。
They will fantasize about their future together. = 他们会幻想他们共同的未来。

虚拟语气:
If I could, I would fantasize more often. = 如果可以的话，我会更频繁地幻想。
I wish I could fantasize as vividly as she does. = 我希望我能像她一样生动地幻想。

被动语态:
This scene was fantasized by the author. = 这个场景是作者虚构的。(This is less common as "fantasize" is usually active.)
The story was often fantasized about by the children. = 孩子们经常幻想这个故事。(This is less common as "fantasize" is usually active.)

疑问句:
What do you fantasize about? = 你在幻想什么？
What did she fantasize about last night? = 她昨晚在幻想什么？

否定句:
He doesn't fantasize much. = 他不太幻想。
She didn't fantasize about anything negative. = 她没有幻想任何负面的东西。


PHRASE->
fantasize about = 幻想关于...

fantasize incessantly = 不停地幻想
