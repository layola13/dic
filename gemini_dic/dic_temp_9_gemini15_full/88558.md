hitcher:/ˈhɪtʃər/| n.|搭便车的人|A person who travels by getting free rides in vehicles, especially by standing by the roadside and thumbing for rides.|例句：He was a lonely hitcher, traveling across the country with nothing but a backpack. 他是一个孤独的搭便车的人，带着一个背包穿越全国。|例句：The police warned against picking up hitchhikers at night. 警方警告不要在晚上搭载搭便车的人。|近义词：free rider, stowaway|反义词：driver, passenger (paying)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hitches rides regularly. = 他经常搭便车。
She hitches a ride to work every day. = 她每天搭车去上班。


一般过去时:
He hitched a ride to the city yesterday. = 他昨天搭车去了城里。
They hitched a ride with a friendly trucker. = 他们搭了一位友好的卡车司机的车。


现在完成时:
I have hitched rides across three states. = 我已经搭车穿越了三个州。
She has never hitched a ride before. = 她以前从未搭过便车。


过去完成时:
He had hitched several rides before reaching the town. = 在到达城镇之前，他已经搭了好几次便车了。
They had hitched a ride from a passing car. = 他们搭乘了一辆经过的汽车。


将来时:
I will hitch a ride to the concert tomorrow. = 我明天将搭车去音乐会。
She will probably hitch a ride home. = 她可能会搭车回家。


虚拟语气:
If I didn't have a car, I would hitchhike. = 如果我没有车，我会搭便车。
I wish I could hitch a ride right now. = 我真希望我现在能搭便车。


被动语态:
Rides are often hitched on this highway. = 这条高速公路上经常有人搭便车。
A ride was hitched by the stranded traveler. = 被困的旅行者搭上了一辆车。


疑问句:
Do you often hitch rides? = 你经常搭便车吗？
Has he ever hitched a ride before? = 他以前搭过便车吗？


否定句:
He doesn't usually hitch rides. = 他通常不搭便车。
They didn't hitch a ride yesterday. = 他们昨天没有搭便车。
