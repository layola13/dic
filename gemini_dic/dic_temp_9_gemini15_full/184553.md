restitutionist: /ˌrɛstɪˈtuːʃənɪst/ | n. | 恢复论者；主张恢复论者 | A person who advocates for restitution, especially in the context of criminal justice or social policy.  Restitutionists believe that the focus of the justice system should be on repairing the harm caused by crime, rather than solely on punishment.  | Example:  The restitutionist argued that the offender should be required to pay for the damages caused by their actions, in addition to serving a prison sentence.  恢复论者认为，罪犯除了服刑外，还应该赔偿其行为造成的损失。|近义词：restorative justice advocate, reparative justice proponent | 反义词：punitive justice advocate, retributivist
