mortars:/ˈmɔːrtərz/ | n. | 臼，研钵；迫击炮；灰泥 | A mortar is a bowl-shaped vessel in which substances are ground or crushed with a pestle.  It can also refer to a type of short-barreled artillery weapon that fires projectiles at a high angle. In construction, mortar is a mixture of cement, sand, and water used to bind bricks or stones together. | 例句：She used a mortar and pestle to crush the spices. 她用研钵和杵来研磨香料。

The soldiers fired their mortars at the enemy positions. 士兵们用迫击炮向敌军阵地发射炮弹。

The bricklayer mixed the mortar carefully before applying it to the wall.  泥瓦匠在将灰泥涂抹到墙上之前仔细地混合了它。

| 近义词：pestle (for the grinding tool), howitzer (similar artillery), cement (for the building material) | 反义词：N/A | 名词
