fish-out-of-water: /ˈfɪʃˌaʊtəvˈwɔːtər/ | adj. n. |格格不入的人；感到不自在的人 |A person who feels uncomfortable or out of place in a particular situation or environment.  They feel awkward and do not belong. This idiom often describes someone who is in an unfamiliar or inappropriate setting,  making them feel strange and uncomfortable.  |例句：He felt like a fish out of water at the formal dinner party, dressed in his casual clothes. 他在正式的晚宴上穿着休闲装，感觉像一条离开水的鱼。

He was a fish out of water in the high-powered corporate world, preferring a more relaxed environment. 在高压力的企业界，他像一条离开水的鱼，更喜欢轻松的环境。|近义词：alienated, uncomfortable, misplaced, out of place |反义词：comfortable, at ease, at home
