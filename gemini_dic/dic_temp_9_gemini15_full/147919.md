overregulation: /ˌoʊvərˌreɡjuˈleɪʃən/ | n. |过度管制；管制过严| Excessive regulation; the imposition of more rules and regulations than are necessary or desirable.  It often leads to inefficiency, stifles innovation, and increases costs. |The overregulation of the financial industry contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.  过度管制金融行业导致了2008年的金融危机。  |近义词：excessive regulation, overcontrol, red tape |反义词：deregulation, laissez-faire
