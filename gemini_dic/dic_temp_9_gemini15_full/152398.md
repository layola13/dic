pavilion:/pəˈvɪljən/| n. |凉亭；展馆；大帐篷|A pavilion is a light, usually open building, used for shelter or exhibitions.  It can also refer to a large tent.  The word often suggests a temporary or decorative structure. |例句：The park featured a beautiful wooden pavilion overlooking the lake.  公园里有一个美丽的木制凉亭俯瞰着湖泊。

The exhibition was held in a large glass pavilion.  展览在一个大型玻璃展馆举行。|近义词：kiosk, booth, tent, gazebo |反义词：mansion, palace

词源:
The word "pavilion" comes from the Old French word "pavillon," meaning "tent," "pavilion," or "banner."  This ultimately derives from the Latin word "papilio," meaning "butterfly," likely due to the resemblance of a tent's shape to a butterfly's wings.
