sidesteps: /ˈsaɪdˌstɛps/ | v. | 避开；回避；巧妙地避开 | To move to one side in order to avoid something.  To avoid dealing with something directly. | 例句：He sidestepped the question. 他回避了这个问题。

He skillfully sidestepped the puddle. 他巧妙地避开了水坑。

近义词：avoid, evade, circumvent, dodge, bypass | 反义词：confront, face, tackle


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sidesteps the issue. 他回避这个问题。
She sidesteps the difficult questions in the interview. 她在采访中回避了那些棘手的问题。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:
She has sidestepped responsibility for the mistake. 她一直逃避对这个错误的责任。

词源:
The word "sidestep" is a compound word formed from "side" and "step".  "Side" meaning to the side, and "step" meaning to move one's foot.  The combination signifies a movement to the side to avoid something.  The word developed naturally from the physical action to the metaphorical sense of avoiding a difficult situation or topic.
