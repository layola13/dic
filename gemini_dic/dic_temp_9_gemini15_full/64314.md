equivocations:/ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. |含糊其辞；模棱两可；支吾其词 | The use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or to avoid committing oneself; a statement or expression that is capable of being interpreted in two or more ways.  |例句：His equivocations about the deal made the investors nervous.  他的关于这笔交易的含糊其辞让投资者紧张不安。

例句：The politician's response was full of equivocations, avoiding a direct answer to the question.  这位政治家的回答充满了含糊其辞，避免了对问题的直接回答。


近义词：ambiguity, vagueness, ambiguity, prevarication, obfuscation |反义词：clarity, frankness, directness


词源：来自拉丁语 *aequus (“equal”) + *vox (“voice”)  The word evolved through Old French *equivocacion and Middle English *equivocacioun.  The core meaning reflects the idea of a statement having multiple, equally valid interpretations.
