re-canceling: /ˌriːˈkænsəlɪŋ/ | v. | 再次取消；重新取消 |  To cancel something that has already been canceled.  This often implies a further action taken to ensure the cancellation is definitive or to address a previous incomplete cancellation. |

例句：

The flight was canceled due to bad weather, and then re-canceling was announced due to a further deterioration of conditions. = 由于恶劣天气航班被取消，之后又宣布再次取消，因为天气进一步恶化。

They initially canceled the meeting, but due to unforeseen circumstances, they are re-canceling it again, this time permanently. = 他们最初取消了会议，但由于不可预见的情况，他们再次取消了会议，这次是永久性的。


近义词：countermanding (implies a superior authority), voiding, annulling


反义词：confirming, reinstating, scheduling


词性:动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They re-cancel the order every week. = 他们每周都重新取消订单。
The company re-cancels the project regularly. = 公司定期重新取消项目。

They re-canceled the contract last month. = 他们上个月重新取消了合同。

现在完成时:
They have re-canceled the event several times. = 他们已经几次重新取消了活动。
We have re-canceled our subscription. = 我们已经重新取消了订阅。

过去完成时:
They had re-canceled the booking before I arrived. = 我到达之前他们已经重新取消了预订。
She had re-canceled the appointment before he called. = 他打电话之前她已经重新取消了预约。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

否定句:
