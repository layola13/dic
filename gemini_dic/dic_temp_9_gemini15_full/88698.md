hobble:/ˈhɒbəl/ /ˈhɑːbəl/| v. n.|蹒跚而行；跛行；阻碍；使困难；|To walk with difficulty because of a limp or injury; to restrict the movement of; to hamper or impede.|The old woman hobbled down the street.  老妇人蹒跚地走下街道。|He hobbled along on his crutches. 他拄着拐杖一瘸一拐地走着。|limp, limp along, shuffle|run smoothly, flow freely|动词
USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hobbles down the street every morning. 他每天早上都一瘸一拐地走下街道。
She hobbles when she walks. 她走路的时候一瘸一拐的。

一般过去时:
He hobbled home after the accident. 事故后他一瘸一拐地回家了。
She hobbled awkwardly across the room. 她笨拙地一瘸一拐地穿过房间。

现在完成时:
He has hobbled around for weeks since the injury. 受伤几周后，他一直一瘸一拐地走动。
She has hobbled since her surgery. 她手术后就一直一瘸一拐的。

过去完成时:
He had hobbled for miles before he found help. 他走了几英里路才找到帮助。
She had hobbled through the entire race. 她一瘸一拐地跑完了整场比赛。

将来时:
He will hobble to the finish line. 他将一瘸一拐地走到终点线。
She will hobble if she doesn't rest her ankle. 如果她不休息脚踝，她就会一瘸一拐的。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't injured his leg, he wouldn't hobble. 如果他没有伤到腿，他就不会一瘸一拐的。
If she had rested, she wouldn't have hobbled so much. 如果她休息了，她就不会这么一瘸一拐的了。

被动语态:
His progress was hobbled by the difficult terrain. 艰难的地形阻碍了他的前进。
Their plans were hobbled by a lack of funding. 他们的计划由于缺乏资金而受阻。

疑问句:
Does he hobble because of his injured knee? 他的膝盖受伤，所以他一瘸一拐的吗？
Does she always hobble when walking? 她走路总是瘸着腿吗？

否定句:
He doesn't hobble anymore after his physiotherapy. 物理治疗后，他不再一瘸一拐了。
She doesn't hobble easily; she runs swiftly. 她不瘸，她跑得很快。
