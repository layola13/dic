clambered:/ˈklæmbərd/| v.|攀爬；攀登；挣扎着前进 |To climb or move with difficulty, often using hands and feet.|例句：The children clambered over the rocks. 孩子们艰难地爬过岩石。

He clambered into the attic. 他费力地爬进阁楼。


近义词：climb, scramble, ascend| 反义词：descend, slide


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They clamber up the mountain every weekend. 他们每个周末都爬山。
Birds clamber on the branches to find food. 鸟儿在树枝上攀爬寻找食物。


一般过去时:
He clambered over the wall to escape. 他翻墙逃跑了。
She clambered into the car, relieved to be inside. 她爬进车里，感到松了一口气。


现在完成时:
They have clambered to the top of the mountain. 他们已经爬到了山顶。
The cat has clambered onto the bookshelf. 猫已经爬上了书架。


过去完成时:
He had clambered halfway up the tree when he fell. 他爬到树 halfway 时掉了下来。
Before the police arrived, the thieves had clambered out of the window. 在警察到达之前，小偷们已经从窗户爬了出去。


将来时:
They will clamber up the cliff tomorrow. 他们明天将攀登悬崖。
The monkeys will clamber through the trees to reach the fruit. 猴子们将穿过树林去摘水果。


虚拟语气:
If I were stronger, I would clamber up that rock face. 如果我更强壮，我会爬上那块岩石。
I wish I could clamber over those mountains. 我希望我能翻越那些山。


被动语态:
The rocks were clambered over by the children. 孩子们爬过了岩石。(被动语态使用较少)
The wall was clambered over by the thief. 窃贼翻过了墙。(被动语态使用较少)


疑问句:
Did they clamber up the hill? 他们爬上了小山吗？
Can you clamber over this fence? 你能翻过这个栅栏吗？


否定句:
He didn't clamber over the fence, he went around it. 他没有翻过栅栏，而是绕过去了。
They couldn't clamber up the steep slope. 他们爬不上陡峭的斜坡。


PHRASE->
clamber over: 翻越
clamber up: 爬上
clamber down: 爬下
clamber onto: 爬到...上面
clamber through: 爬过


词源:
The word "clamber" comes from the Old French "clamberer," which meant "to climb with difficulty."  The word's origins possibly lie in the Old Norse "klumbra," meaning "to climb."
