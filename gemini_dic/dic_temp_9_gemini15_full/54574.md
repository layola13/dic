disloyalty: /dɪsˈlɔɪəlti/ | n. | 不忠；背叛 |  The state or instance of being disloyal; treachery.  It implies a breach of trust or allegiance, often involving betrayal of someone's confidence or commitment. |

例句：

His disloyalty to his friends cost him their trust.  他的不忠使他失去了朋友们的信任。

The general's disloyalty led to a military coup.  将军的背叛导致了军事政变。


近义词：treachery, betrayal, infidelity, faithlessness, perfidy


反义词：loyalty, faithfulness, fidelity, allegiance


词性：名词
