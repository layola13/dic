/nɪˈkəʊtɪn/ | n. | 尼古丁 | Nicotine is a highly addictive chemical found in tobacco products. It is a stimulant that affects the central nervous system, causing increased heart rate, blood pressure, and alertness.  Long-term use can lead to serious health problems, including heart disease, lung cancer, and other respiratory illnesses. | Example: Smoking cigarettes exposes you to high levels of nicotine. 吸烟会使你接触到高浓度的尼古丁。

The effects of nicotine on the brain are complex and multifaceted. 尼古丁对大脑的影响是复杂且多方面的。 | 近义词:  tobacco alkaloid (烟碱) | 反义词:  N/A
