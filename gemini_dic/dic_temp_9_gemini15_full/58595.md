dull:/dʌl/ | adj. | 沉闷的；乏味的；迟钝的；暗淡的 |  Lacking brightness, interest, or sharpness.  It can refer to colors, sounds, light, or even people's personalities or minds. |

例句：The movie was dull and predictable.  这部电影沉闷且可预测。

例句：He felt dull and listless after his illness. 他病后感到沉闷和倦怠。


近义词：boring, tedious, monotonous, uninteresting, dim, blunt, stupid

反义词：bright, interesting, exciting, vibrant, sharp, intelligent, stimulating


词源：源自古英语 *dull,  含义为“迟钝的，愚笨的”。  其含义随着时间的推移扩展到描述颜色、声音和整体缺乏活力的事物。


USAGE->

The lecture is dull. = 这场讲座很乏味。

一般过去时:
The party was dull. = 派对很沉闷。
The colors seemed dull in the dim light. = 在昏暗的灯光下，颜色显得暗淡。

现在完成时:
The weather has been dull all week. = 这周的天气一直阴沉。

过去完成时:

将来时:
The meeting will be dull. = 会议将会很沉闷。
I think the future looks dull without any plans. = 我认为没有计划的未来看起来很黯淡。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
