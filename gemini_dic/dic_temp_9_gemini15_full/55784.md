divert:/daɪˈvɜːrt/| /daɪˈvɜːt/ | vt. n. |转移；使转向；消遣 |To change the course or direction of something; to distract someone's attention; something that amuses or entertains. |例句：The river was diverted to irrigate the fields. 河水被改道用于灌溉农田。|例句：He diverted his attention from his work by listening to music. 他通过听音乐来转移对工作的注意力。|近义词：redirect, deflect, distract, entertain |反义词：maintain, focus, concentrate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They divert the traffic during the parade. = 他们在游行期间改道交通。
The company diverts its resources to new projects. = 公司将资源转移到新的项目上。

一般过去时:
The police diverted the traffic away from the accident. = 警察将交通引离事故现场。
She diverted her attention from the boring meeting. = 她将注意力从枯燥的会议上转移开。

现在完成时:
They have diverted the funds to a more worthy cause. = 他们已将资金转移到更有价值的事业上。
The government has diverted resources to fight the epidemic. = 政府已将资源转移到抗击疫情上。

过去完成时:
They had diverted the river before the flood. = 他们在洪水到来之前就改道了河流。
She had diverted her attention from the problem for too long. = 她已经太久没有关注这个问题了。

将来时:
They will divert the flight to a nearby airport. = 他们将把航班改到附近的机场。
He will divert his energy to his studies. = 他将把精力投入到学习中。

虚拟语气:
If they had diverted the funds earlier, the project would have succeeded. = 如果他们早点转移资金，项目就会成功。
I wish they had diverted more resources to research. = 我希望他们能把更多资源投入到研究中。

被动语态:

His attention was diverted by a loud noise. = 他的注意力被一阵巨响分散了。

疑问句:
