quasi-guilty:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈɡɪlti/| adj.|似有罪的；貌似有罪的；近乎有罪的| seemingly guilty; having some of the characteristics of guilt, but not fully guilty.  The term often implies a feeling of guilt or responsibility without a clear legal or moral transgression.|例句：He felt quasi-guilty about leaving his friend behind, even though it was necessary.|近义词：partly guilty, somewhat culpable,  responsible in part|反义词：innocent, blameless, guiltless
