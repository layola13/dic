inclin:/ɪnˈklaɪn/| v. |倾斜；倾向；使倾斜 |To lean or bend; to be disposed or tend (toward something).|例句：The tower inclines slightly to the south. 塔略微向南倾斜。

例句：He inclines towards the opinion that we should wait. 他倾向于我们应该等待的意见。

近义词：slope, slant, tilt, tend, be prone to |反义词：straighten, upright


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The tower inclines slightly. = 塔略微倾斜。
She inclines towards vegetarianism. = 她倾向于素食。

一般过去时:
The tree inclined in the wind. = 树在风中倾斜。
He inclined his head in agreement. = 他点头表示同意。

现在完成时:
The building has inclined further since the earthquake. = 自地震以来，建筑物倾斜得更厉害了。
Public opinion has inclined towards supporting the new policy. = 公众舆论已倾向于支持这项新政策。

过去完成时:
The road had inclined sharply before reaching the summit. = 道路在到达山顶前急剧倾斜。
She had inclined to believe him, until she heard the truth. = 在她听到真相之前，她倾向于相信他。

将来时:
The sun will incline towards the horizon. = 太阳将向地平线倾斜。
I think the government will incline to compromise. = 我认为政府会倾向于妥协。


虚拟语气:
If the building inclined any further, it would collapse. = 如果建筑物再倾斜一点，它就会倒塌。
I wish my neighbor wouldn't incline to gossip so much. = 我希望我的邻居不要那么爱说闲话。


被动语态:
The monument was inclined by the earthquake. = 纪念碑在地震中倾斜了。
The decision is inclined towards a more conservative approach. = 决定倾向于采取更保守的方法。


疑问句:
Does the leaning tower of Pisa still incline? = 比萨斜塔还在倾斜吗？
Does she incline to your suggestion? = 她倾向于你的建议吗？


否定句:
The road doesn't incline steeply here. = 这条路在这里不陡峭。
He didn't incline to accept the offer. = 他不愿意接受这个提议。
