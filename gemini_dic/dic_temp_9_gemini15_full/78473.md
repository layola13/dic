geysers:/ˈɡaɪzərz/| n.|间歇泉|A geyser is a hot spring that erupts periodically, shooting a column of water and steam high into the air.  Geysers are formed by underground water heated by geothermal activity. The pressure builds up until it forces the water to erupt.|Example:  The Yellowstone National Park is famous for its geysers. 黄石国家公园以其间歇泉而闻名。

The geyser erupted with a spectacular display of water and steam.  间歇泉喷发了壮观的水和蒸汽。|近义词：hot spring, fountain|反义词：无
