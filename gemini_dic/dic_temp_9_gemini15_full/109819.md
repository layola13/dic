lexemes:/ˈliːksiːmz/| n. |词素，词位|The smallest units of meaning in a language, which can be words or parts of words.  They are often considered the building blocks of vocabulary.  For example, "unbreakable" contains three lexemes: "un-", "break", and "-able".|The linguist analyzed the lexemes of the ancient language to understand its structure.|近义词：morphemes, words (in some contexts)|反义词：N/A
