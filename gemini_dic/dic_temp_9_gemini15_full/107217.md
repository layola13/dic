laid-in:/leɪdˈɪn/| adj. |预先准备好的；内置的；事先安排好的|  Describes something that has been previously prepared, installed, or arranged.  It often implies a degree of planning or incorporation into a larger system or structure.|例句：The house had many laid-in features, including built-in bookshelves and a state-of-the-art sound system.  这房子有很多预先准备好的设施，包括内置书架和最先进的音响系统。


The company's laid-in supply of materials ensured that production wouldn't be disrupted. 公司预先准备好的物料确保生产不会中断。|近义词：built-in, pre-installed, pre-arranged, incorporated |反义词：improvised, ad-hoc, impromptu|形容词
