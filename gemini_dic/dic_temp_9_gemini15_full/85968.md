headroom:/ˈhɛdruːm/| n. |净空高度，余地，空间|The available space above something, or a margin for manoeuvre.|The low bridge forced them to reduce their speed to gain enough headroom. 低矮的桥迫使他们减速以获得足够的净空高度。  The project has plenty of headroom in the budget. 这个项目预算还有很大的余地。| clearance, space, allowance | constraint, limitation | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
There is plenty of headroom in the budget. = 预算还有很大的空间。
The truck needs more headroom to pass under the bridge. = 这辆卡车需要更大的净空高度才能通过桥下。


一般过去时:
The plane had sufficient headroom to avoid collision. = 飞机有足够的净空高度避免了碰撞。
We had little headroom for negotiation. = 我们几乎没有谈判的余地。

They have used up all their headroom for this year's expenses. = 他们已经用完了今年的所有预算空间。
The company has achieved significant headroom in market share. = 公司在市场份额方面取得了显著的领先优势。(这里headroom指优势，领先空间)


过去完成时:

We had already consumed most of our headroom in the project by that time. = 到那时，我们在这个项目中已经消耗了大部分的余地。


将来时:
We will need more headroom in the future for expansion. = 我们将来需要更大的扩展空间。
The new design will provide more headroom for passengers. = 新的设计将为乘客提供更大的空间。


虚拟语气:
If we had more headroom, we could afford a bigger house. = 如果我们有更多空间，我们就能买得起更大的房子。
I wish we had more headroom in the schedule. = 我希望我们在日程安排上有更多余地。
