professing:/prəˈfɛsɪŋ/ | v. |公开宣称；声称；自称 |  To declare openly; to affirm; to claim; to avow.  | 例句：He was professing his innocence. 他公开宣称自己是无辜的。

例句：She spent years professing her love for art. 她多年来一直公开表达她对艺术的热爱。


近义词：declaring, avowing, asserting, claiming, maintaining

反义词：denying, refuting, contradicting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He professes his faith daily. = 他每天都宣称他的信仰。
They profess to know everything. = 他们自称无所不知。


一般过去时:
She professed her love for him. = 她向他表达了爱意。
He professed ignorance of the matter. = 他声称对这件事一无所知。


现在完成时:
They have professed their allegiance to the king. = 他们已经宣誓效忠国王。
She has professed her commitment to the cause. = 她已经表明她对事业的承诺。


过去完成时:
He had professed his belief in God since childhood. = 他从小就宣称相信上帝。
They had professed their innocence before the trial. = 他们曾在审判前宣称自己是无辜的。


将来时:
She will profess her love in public. = 她将在公众场合表达爱意。
He will profess his support for the candidate. = 他将公开表示支持该候选人。


虚拟语气:
If he professed remorse, I might forgive him. = 如果他表示悔恨，我可能会原谅他。
I wish he professed a little more humility. = 我希望他能谦虚一点。


被动语态:
His innocence was professed by many witnesses. = 许多证人都宣称他是无辜的。
The doctrine was professed by the early Christians. = 这项教义是由早期基督徒宣扬的。


疑问句:
Does he profess any religious beliefs? = 他信奉任何宗教信仰吗？
What political views does she profess? = 她宣扬什么政治观点？


否定句:
He did not profess to be an expert. = 他并没有自称是专家。
She did not profess any interest in the subject. = 她对这个话题没有表现出任何兴趣。


PHRASE->
professing faith = 宣称信仰
professing ignorance = 宣称无知

professing loyalty = 表达忠诚

词源:
From Old French *professer, from Latin *professus, past participle of profiteri "to declare publicly, to profess," from pro- "forth" + fateri "to confess."
