flamen:/ˈfleɪmən/ | n. |祭司；火祭司|A flamen was a priest or priestess in ancient Rome, dedicated to a particular deity.  They performed religious rites and ceremonies.  The term often refers to a Flamen Dialis, the highest-ranking priest dedicated to Jupiter.| Example: The flamen diligently performed the sacred rituals.  火焰祭司勤奋地执行着神圣的仪式。|近义词:priest, priestess|反义词:layperson
