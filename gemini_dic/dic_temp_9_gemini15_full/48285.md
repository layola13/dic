decoys:/ˈdiːkɔɪz/| n.|诱饵；诱骗物；圈套|Something used to lure or trap someone or something.  A decoy is typically something that appears attractive or desirable, but which serves to distract from a hidden danger or objective.|例句：The police used a decoy car to catch the robbers. = 警方用诱饵车来抓捕强盗。

The hunters set out decoys to attract the ducks. = 猎人放置诱饵来吸引鸭子。

近义词：lure, bait, trap, snare |反义词：target, objective

词源:
From the Middle French word "décoy," ultimately from the Dutch word "de kooi," meaning "from the cage."  Originally, it referred to a type of bird trap.  The meaning gradually broadened to encompass any lure or stratagem used for deception.
