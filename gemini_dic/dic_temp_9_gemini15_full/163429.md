preconsumed:/ˌpriːkənˈsjuːmd/ | adj. | 预先消费的；已被预先消费的 | Already consumed or used before a particular time or event.  This word is relatively rare and primarily used in specialized contexts, particularly related to economics and resource management. It implies consumption that has happened prior to a point of reference, often impacting future availability or analysis. | Example:  The preconsumed resources left little for the next generation.  预先消费的资源给下一代留下了很少的东西。| 近义词:  pre-used, previously consumed | 反义词: unconsumed, remaining, available
