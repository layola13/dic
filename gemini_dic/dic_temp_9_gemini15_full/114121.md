magus:/ˈmeɪɡəs/| n.|法师，术士，占星家|A magus is a wise man, especially one who practices magic or divination.  The term often refers to a priestly figure with mystical or supernatural powers, often associated with ancient Persia and Zoroastrianism.  The word has also been used in fantasy literature to describe powerful wizards or sorcerers.|例句：The ancient magus foretold the coming of a great king.  古代法师预言一位伟大的国王即将到来。

The three Magi brought gifts to the baby Jesus.  三位东方博士给婴儿耶稣带来了礼物。|近义词：wizard, sorcerer, warlock, sage|反义词：layperson, novice
