rearrangement:/ˌriːəˈreɪndʒmənt/ | n. |重新安排；改组；改编 |The act or process of rearranging something.  It refers to the changing of the arrangement or order of elements within a system or structure. This could involve physical objects, data, or abstract concepts. |例句：The rearrangement of the furniture improved the flow of the room. = 家具的重新摆放改善了房间的动线。

The rearrangement of the schedule caused some confusion. = 日程的重新安排引起了一些混乱。|近义词：reorganization, restructuring, reordering, adjustment |反义词：disarrangement, disorder
