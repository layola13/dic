embroil:/ɪmˈbrɔɪl/ | vt. |使卷入；使陷入；使混乱 | To involve someone or something in a difficult or complicated situation, especially a conflict or argument.  |例句：The scandal embroiled several prominent politicians.  这个丑闻牵涉到几位政界要人。

例句：The two countries were embroiled in a bitter trade dispute. 这两个国家卷入了一场激烈的贸易纠纷。


近义词：involve, entangle, implicate | 反义词：extricate, disentangle | 词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The conflict embroils many innocent civilians.  冲突使许多无辜平民卷入其中。
He constantly embroils himself in unnecessary arguments. 他不断地卷入不必要的争论中。


一般过去时:
The scandal embroiled the company in a legal battle.  丑闻使公司卷入了一场法律诉讼。
The war embroiled the entire region.  战争使整个地区陷入混乱。


现在完成时:
The ongoing investigation has embroiled several high-profile figures.  正在进行的调查已经牵涉到几位知名人士。
Years of conflict have embroiled the nation.  多年的冲突使这个国家陷入混乱。


过去完成时:
They had already been embroiled in a dispute before the meeting began. 会议开始之前，他们就已经卷入了一场争端。
The company had been embroiled in several lawsuits before it finally collapsed.  该公司在最终倒闭之前已经卷入了多起诉讼。


将来时:
The upcoming election will likely embroil the country in further political division.  即将到来的选举很可能会使国家进一步陷入政治分裂。
This decision could embroil us in serious trouble.  这个决定可能会使我们陷入严重的麻烦。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't interfered, he wouldn't have been embroiled in this mess. 如果他没有干预，他就不会卷入这场混乱。
I wish I hadn't been embroiled in that argument. 我真希望我没有卷入那场争论。


被动语态:
The politician was embroiled in a corruption scandal.  这位政治家卷入了一起腐败丑闻。
Many innocent people were embroiled in the conflict.  许多无辜的人卷入了冲突。


疑问句:
Is he embroiled in any legal troubles? 他是否卷入了任何法律纠纷？

否定句:
He wasn't embroiled in the dispute. 他没有卷入这场争端。
The government attempted to avoid being embroiled in the crisis.  政府试图避免卷入这场危机。
