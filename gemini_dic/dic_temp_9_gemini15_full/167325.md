pretranslation:/priːˌtrænzˈleɪʃən/ | n. |预翻译，预先翻译 |The process of translating a text before it is officially translated, often to provide a preliminary understanding or to aid in the main translation process.  It might involve creating a rough draft or identifying key concepts and terminology.  Pre-translation is often used in large-scale translation projects to improve efficiency and accuracy. |Example: The pre-translation of the legal document helped the team identify potential challenges before the official translation began. =  法律文件的预翻译帮助团队在正式翻译开始前发现了潜在的挑战。|近义词： preliminary translation, advance translation |反义词：post-translation, final translation |名词
