sacrum:/ˈseɪkrəm/| n. |骶骨|The sacrum is a large, triangular bone at the base of the spine, formed by the fusion of five vertebrae. It is located between the fifth lumbar vertebra and the coccyx.  The sacrum plays a crucial role in supporting the weight of the upper body and transferring it to the pelvis. It also forms part of the posterior wall of the pelvis, contributing to its stability.  |The sacrum is a crucial bone in the human anatomy.|近义词：pelvic bone|反义词：无
