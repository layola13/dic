copublisher:/ˈkoʊpʌblɪʃər/ | n. | 合著者；合作出版商 | A copublisher is a publishing house that collaborates with another to produce and distribute a book or other publication.  They share the costs, risks, and profits.  | Example:  Oxford University Press and Yale University Press are copublishers of this scholarly work. 牛津大学出版社和耶鲁大学出版社是这部学术著作的合作出版商。 |近义词: joint publisher, collaborating publisher |反义词: sole publisher
