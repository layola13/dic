left:/lɛft/| adj., n., v.|左边的；左边；离开|

英文解析:
As an adjective, "left" describes something situated on the side of the body opposite to the right. As a noun, "left" refers to the left side or hand. As a verb, "left" signifies the act of departing from a place or situation.

例句:
The car turned left at the intersection. = 汽车在十字路口左转。

He left his keys on the table. = 他把钥匙留在桌上了。


近义词:
(adj.)  left-hand, port (nautical), sinister (figurative)
(n.)  port (nautical)
(v.) departed, went away, abandoned


反义词:
(adj.) right
(v.) arrived, stayed, remained


词性:
adjective, noun, verb


词源:
The word "left" originates from Old English *lyft, meaning "left (side)".  It's related to other Germanic words for "left", reflecting a common ancestor in Proto-Germanic *leftiz. The association of "left" with negative connotations in some cultures is not reflected in its etymological roots, but rather developed later in history.
