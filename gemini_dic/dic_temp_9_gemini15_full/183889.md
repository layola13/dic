resent:/rɪˈzent/| vt.|怨恨，愤恨；不满|To feel bitterness or indignation at (a circumstance, action, or person).  It implies a feeling of injustice or unfairness. |例句：He resented being treated like a child. 他讨厌被人当小孩对待。|例句：She resented his interference in her affairs. 她讨厌他对她的事情指手画脚。|近义词：dislike, hate, begrudge,  |反义词：appreciate, welcome, enjoy|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He resents my success. = 他嫉妒我的成功。
They resent the new rules. = 他们对新规不满。

一般过去时:
She resented his criticism. = 她对他的批评感到愤恨。
I resented having to work overtime. = 我讨厌不得不加班。

现在完成时:
I have resented his behavior for years. = 我多年来一直对他这种行为感到愤恨。
They have resented the lack of opportunity. = 他们一直对缺乏机会感到不满。

过去完成时:
He had resented her promotion for months. = 他对她的升职已经不满了好几个月了。
We had resented the unfair treatment for a long time. = 我们很久以来就对这种不公平待遇感到不满。

将来时:
She will resent his betrayal. = 她将对她背叛感到愤怒。
They will resent any attempt to control them. = 他们将抵制任何试图控制他们的企图。

虚拟语气:
I would resent it if you told my secrets. = 如果你说出我的秘密，我会生气的。
He would have resented the delay even more if he had known the reason. = 如果他知道原因的话，他会更讨厌这个延误。

被动语态:
His actions were resented by many. = 他的行为受到了许多人的怨恨。
The new policy is being resented by the employees. = 员工们对这项新政策感到不满。

疑问句:
Do you resent his interference? = 你讨厌他的干涉吗？
Does she resent being overlooked? = 她对被忽视感到不满吗？

否定句:
He doesn't resent her help. = 他并不讨厌她的帮助。
They don't resent the extra work. = 他们并不讨厌额外的工作。


PHRASE->
resent doing something: 讨厌做某事
resent someone's actions: 讨厌某人的行为
resent being treated unfairly: 讨厌被不公平对待
deeply resent: 深深地怨恨
secretly resent: 暗中怨恨
