post-romantic:/ˌpoʊst roʊˈmænɪk/| adj.| 后浪漫主义的 | Post-romantic refers to a period or style in art, literature, or music that followed Romanticism, often characterized by a reaction against or a modification of Romantic ideals. It encompasses diverse movements and approaches, but generally involves a shift away from intense emotionality and subjective experience towards a more objective, analytical, or ironic perspective.  | Example:  The painting shows a post-romantic sensibility, with its muted colors and restrained emotional expression.  这幅画展现了一种后浪漫主义的情感，色彩柔和，情感表达克制。 |近义词: late Romantic, neo-classical (in some contexts) |反义词: Romantic
