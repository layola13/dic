microscopically:/ˌmaɪkrəˈskɑːpɪkli/ | adv. | 显微镜地；微观地 | In a way that is too small to be seen without a microscope.  It describes something that is so small it can only be observed using a microscope. | Example: The bacteria were microscopically small.  细菌小到只能用显微镜观察到。 The damage to the tissue was only microscopically visible. 组织损伤只有在显微镜下才能看到。 | 近义词: minutely, invisibly, imperceptibly | 反义词: macroscopically, visibly
