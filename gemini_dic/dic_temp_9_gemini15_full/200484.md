slandering: /ˈslændərɪŋ/ | v. | 诽谤；中伤；诋毁 |  To make false and damaging statements about someone.  | 例句：He was slandering his opponent in the election. 他在选举中诽谤他的对手。

例句：She was slandered by the tabloids. 她被小报诽谤了。


近义词：defaming, calumniating, vilifying, traducing, smearing


反义词：praising, commending, extolling


词源：源自中古英语 *slanderen，来自古法语 eslandrer，源自古高地德语 skandalōn（“丑闻”），最终源自希腊语 skandalon（“绊脚石；陷阱；丑闻”）。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slanders his colleagues regularly. = 他经常诽谤他的同事。
They slander their competitors to gain an advantage. = 他们诽谤竞争对手以获得优势。


一般过去时:
She slandered him to the police. = 她向警方诽谤了他。
The newspaper slandered the politician with false accusations. = 报纸用虚假指控诽谤了这位政治家。


现在完成时:
He has slandered many people throughout his career. = 他在他职业生涯中诽谤过很多人。
The company has been slandered by a rival firm. = 这家公司被竞争对手公司诽谤了。


过去完成时:
They had slandered her reputation before she even knew it. = 在她甚至知道之前，他们就已经诽谤了她的名誉。
The media had slandered the actor for years before the truth came out. = 在真相大白之前，媒体诽谤了这位演员多年。


将来时:
She will slander him again if given the chance. = 如果有机会，她还会再次诽谤他。
They will likely slander the company in the coming weeks. = 他们很可能会在接下来的几周内诽谤这家公司。


虚拟语气:
If he slandered her, he would face legal consequences. = 如果他诽谤了她，他将面临法律后果。
I wish he hadn't slandered my family. = 我真希望他没有诽谤我的家人。


被动语态:
He was slandered in the press. = 他在媒体上受到了诽谤。
The politician was slandered by his opponents. = 这位政治家被他的对手诽谤了。


疑问句:
Was he slandering her intentionally? = 他是有意诽谤她吗？
Are they slandering the company to damage its reputation? = 他们是在诽谤这家公司以损害其声誉吗？


否定句:
He did not slander her; he simply stated the facts. = 他没有诽谤她；他只是陈述了事实。
They denied slandering their neighbor. = 他们否认诽谤他们的邻居。
