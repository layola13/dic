geminately:/ˈdʒɛmɪnətli/| adv.|成对地，双生地|In botany, geminately refers to the arrangement of paired structures, such as leaves or flowers, arising from a single point.  It implies a twinned or double nature.|例句：The leaves were arranged geminately along the stem. 叶片成对地排列在茎上。 The flowers bloomed geminately, creating a beautiful symmetrical display. 花朵成对开放，形成美丽的对称图案。|近义词：in pairs, doubly, twinned|反义词：singly, individually, solitarily|副词
