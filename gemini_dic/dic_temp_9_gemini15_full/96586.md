inducted:/ɪnˈdʌktɪd/| v.|正式介绍；使就职；接纳；引入；归纳|To formally admit someone into a group or organization, especially with a ceremony.  Also, to install someone in an office or position.  It can also mean to introduce something new or lead something into a system or process. |例句：He was inducted into the Hall of Fame last year. 他去年被引进名人堂。

例句：The new members were inducted into the society with a formal ceremony. 新成员们在一次正式仪式上被接纳进入社团。


近义词：initiated, admitted, installed, introduced, inaugurated

反义词：expelled, removed, dismissed, ousted


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They induct new members every year. = 他们每年都吸收新成员。
The company inducts new employees with a detailed training program. = 公司对新员工进行详细的培训项目。


一般过去时:
She was inducted into the army last month. = 她上个月入伍了。
He was inducted into the club after a rigorous application process. = 在经过严格的申请流程后，他被接纳进入俱乐部。


现在完成时:
They have inducted several prominent figures into the organization. = 他们已经将几位杰出人物吸纳到组织中。
She has been inducted into the national honor society. = 她已被吸纳进入国家荣誉学会。


过去完成时:
Before the ceremony, they had inducted all the new officers. = 在仪式之前，他们已经引进了所有新官员。
The company had inducted hundreds of employees before the expansion. = 在扩张之前，公司已经引进了数百名员工。


将来时:
They will induct the new president next week. = 他们下周将新任总统就职。
The university will induct several distinguished alumni into its hall of fame. = 大学将把几位杰出的校友引进其名人堂。


虚拟语气:
If they inducted him, he would be very honored. = 如果他们接纳他，他会非常荣幸。
I wish they had inducted her sooner. = 我希望他们早点接纳她。


被动语态:
He was inducted into the team after a successful trial. = 在成功试训后，他被招入球队。
New members are inducted after a background check. = 新成员在背景调查后被接纳。


疑问句:
When will the new recruits be inducted? = 新兵什么时候入伍？
Were all the nominees inducted into the academy? = 所有被提名的人都被吸纳进学院了吗？


否定句:
They did not induct him because of his past record. = 由于他的过去记录，他们没有接纳他。
She was not inducted into the society due to a lack of qualifications. = 由于缺乏资格，她没有被接纳进入协会。


PHRASE->
be inducted into = 被接纳进入…
inducted as = 被任命为…
inducted member = 入会会员
formally inducted = 正式就职
inducted into office = 就职


词源:
The word "inducted" comes from the Latin word "inducere," meaning "to lead in," "to introduce," or "to bring in."  The prefix "in-" means "in" or "into," and the root "-ducere" is related to the word "duce," meaning "to lead."
