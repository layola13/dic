gigs:/ɡɪɡz/| n.|演出；零工；工作；大量|a single performance by a musician or a group of musicians, especially one that is not part of a longer tour; a short period of work, especially one that is temporary.|例句：The band played a great gig last night. 乐队昨晚演出很棒。|近义词：job, performance, concert, show|反义词：permanent position, full-time job|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often plays gigs in small clubs. 他经常在小型俱乐部演出。
She usually picks up gigs on weekends. 她通常在周末接零活。


一般过去时:
They played a memorable gig at the festival. 他们在音乐节上进行了一场令人难忘的演出。
I did several gigs during the summer. 我夏天做了一些零工。


现在完成时:
She has played many gigs since she started her career. 自从她开始职业生涯以来，她已经演出过很多次了。
He has taken on a few gigs to make ends meet. 他接了一些零工来维持生计。


过去完成时:
The band had already played several gigs before their album release. 乐队在专辑发行前已经演出过好几次了。
I had finished all my gigs before the holiday. 假期前我已经完成了所有工作。


将来时:
They will play a gig in London next month. 他们下个月将在伦敦演出。
I will be taking on more gigs next year. 明年我将接更多零工。


虚拟语气:
If I had the chance, I would play more gigs. 如果我有机会，我会参加更多演出。
I wish I could get more gigs. 我希望我能得到更多演出。


被动语态:
The gig was canceled due to the storm. 演出因暴风雨而取消。
Many gigs are being offered online these days. 如今网上提供了许多零工。


疑问句:
Do you have any gigs coming up? 你接下来有什么演出吗？
Are there any gigs available this week? 本周有哪些工作机会？


否定句:
He doesn't have any gigs scheduled for next week. 他下周没有安排任何演出。
She didn't get the gig she applied for. 她没有得到她申请的那份工作。
