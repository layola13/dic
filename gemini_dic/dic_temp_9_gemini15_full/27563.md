canaliculus:/ˌkænəˈlɪkjʊləs/| n. |小管；细管；小沟|A canaliculus is a small channel or duct, especially in bone or other tissues.  It refers to a tiny tube-like structure.  Examples include the canaliculi of bone, which connect lacunae (spaces containing bone cells), and the canaliculi of the liver, which transport bile. |The canaliculi of bone allow for the passage of nutrients and waste products between osteocytes.  骨骼的小管允许营养物质和废物在骨细胞之间通行。|duct, tubule, channel| (no direct antonym, but could consider "large vessel" as a contrasting term)|名词
