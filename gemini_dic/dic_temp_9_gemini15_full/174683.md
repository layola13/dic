quorums:/ˈkwɔːrəmz/| n. |法定人数；足够人数| A quorum is the minimum number of members of an assembly or society that must be present at a meeting for the proceedings to be valid.  It ensures that decisions are made with sufficient representation and legitimacy.  The specific number required for a quorum varies depending on the organization's rules and bylaws.|Example:  The meeting couldn't proceed because they lacked a quorum.  会议因为缺乏法定人数而无法继续进行。|近义词：majority, sufficient number |反义词：minority, insufficient number
