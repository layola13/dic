kiss-off:/ˈkɪs ɒf/| n., v.| 拒绝；解雇；抛弃 | A decisive rejection or dismissal;  a peremptory dismissal from a job.  It can also refer to a casual or dismissive rejection of a person or idea. |例句：He gave me the kiss-off after our first date. = 他在我们的第一次约会后就拒绝了我。  The company gave him the kiss-off last week. = 公司上周解雇了他。|近义词：rejection, dismissal, sacking, dumping |反义词：acceptance, embrace, hiring


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gives everyone the kiss-off. = 他拒绝所有人。
She gives the project the kiss-off. = 她放弃了这个项目。

一般过去时:
They gave him the kiss-off. = 他们解雇了他。
She gave the idea the kiss-off. = 她否决了那个想法。

现在完成时:
He has given many people the kiss-off. = 他已经拒绝过很多人了。
The company has given him the kiss-off. = 公司已经解雇了他。


过去完成时:
She had already given him the kiss-off before he proposed. = 在他求婚之前，她已经拒绝了他。
They had given the project the kiss-off before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期之前就已经放弃了这个项目。

将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish he hadn't given me the kiss-off. = 我真希望他没有拒绝我。

被动语态:

否定句:
