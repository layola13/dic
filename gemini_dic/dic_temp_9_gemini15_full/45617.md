curse:/kɜːrs/|ˈkɝːs/| n./v.|诅咒；祸害；灾难 | A curse is a spoken word or wish intended to bring harm or bad luck to someone or something.  It can also refer to something that causes great suffering or misfortune. |例句：He uttered a curse on his enemies. 他诅咒他的敌人。|  The drought was a curse upon the land. 干旱是这片土地上的灾难。|近义词：malediction, imprecation, hex, affliction |反义词：blessing, boon


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He curses his bad luck. = 他诅咒他的坏运气。
She curses under her breath. = 她低声咒骂。


一般过去时:
The witch cursed the prince. = 女巫诅咒了王子。
They cursed the day they met. = 他们诅咒他们相遇的那一天。


现在完成时:
I have cursed this machine a hundred times. = 我已经咒骂这台机器一百遍了。
She has cursed her fate. = 她一直诅咒自己的命运。


过去完成时:
He had cursed his foolishness before he acted. = 他在行动之前就诅咒过自己的愚蠢。
They had cursed the rain before it stopped. = 在雨停之前，他们一直在咒骂这场雨。


将来时:
I will curse you if you do that again. = 如果你再这样做，我会诅咒你。
He will curse the day he was born if this happens. = 如果发生这种情况，他会诅咒他出生的那一天。


虚拟语气:
If I cursed him, I would feel bad. = 如果我诅咒他，我会感觉很糟糕。
I wish I hadn't cursed at him. = 我真希望我没有对他咒骂。


被动语态:
The land was cursed by the gods. = 这片土地受到了神灵的诅咒。
He felt cursed by his own actions. = 他觉得自己被自己的行为所诅咒。


疑问句:
Did he curse you? = 他诅咒你了吗？
What did he curse about? = 他在咒骂什么？


否定句:
I didn't curse him, I just shouted. = 我没有诅咒他，我只是大声喊叫。
She didn't curse at the driver. = 她没有咒骂司机。


PHRASE->
a curse on sb/sth 对某人/某事的一种诅咒
a curse of sth 某事的一种祸害
under a curse 受到诅咒
lift a curse 解除诅咒
a family curse 家族诅咒
