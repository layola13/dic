commercialised: /ˌkəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 商业化的；商品化的 |  transformed into a commodity or product for sale;  marked by or characteristic of commerce | The increasing commercialisation of healthcare is a growing concern.  医疗保健日益商业化令人担忧。 |近义词：commercialized, marketised, monetised |反义词：non-commercial, uncommercial


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company commercialises its new technology. = 这家公司将它的新技术商业化。
They commercialise their ideas into successful products. = 他们将其想法商业化为成功的产品。


一般过去时:
The government commercialised the state-owned enterprises. = 政府将国有企业商业化。
The artist resisted attempts to commercialise his work. = 这位艺术家抵制了将他的作品商业化的企图。


现在完成时:
They have commercialised many aspects of daily life. = 他们已经将日常生活的许多方面商业化。
The internet has commercialised communication in unprecedented ways. = 互联网以空前的方式将沟通商业化。


过去完成时:
Before the reforms, the industry had not been commercialised. = 在改革之前，该行业尚未商业化。
The company had already commercialised the product in other countries. = 这家公司已经在其他国家将该产品商业化。


将来时:
They will commercialise their research findings next year. = 他们将在明年将他们的研究成果商业化。
The company plans to commercialise a new drug in the near future. = 该公司计划在不久的将来将一种新药商业化。


虚拟语气:
If they commercialised their invention, they would become very wealthy. = 如果他们将他们的发明商业化，他们将变得非常富有。
I wish they hadn't commercialised that beautiful piece of art. = 我真希望他们没有将那件美丽的艺术品商业化。


被动语态:
The software was commercialised by a large corporation. = 该软件由一家大公司商业化。
The process of commercialisation is being closely monitored. = 商业化过程正在受到密切监控。


疑问句:
Has the technology been fully commercialised? = 这项技术是否已完全商业化？
When will the new product be commercialised? = 新产品何时商业化？


否定句:
The company has not yet commercialised its new product. = 这家公司尚未将新产品商业化。
They chose not to commercialise their research. = 他们选择不将他们的研究商业化。

词源:
commercialise is derived from "commercial" + "-ise" (a suffix forming verbs).  "Commercial" comes from the Middle French *comercial, ultimately from Latin *commercium ("commerce," "trade").
