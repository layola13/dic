cirripedia:/sɪrɪˈpiːdiə/| n. |藤壶类动物|Cirripedia is a subclass of crustaceans that includes barnacles and other related organisms. They are characterized by their sessile lifestyle, meaning they are attached to a substrate and do not move around freely.  They have feathery appendages called cirri that they use to filter feed. | Example:  The rocks were covered in a thick layer of cirripedia.  岩石上覆盖着一层厚厚的藤壶。  The study focused on the reproductive strategies of various cirripedia species.  这项研究的重点是各种藤壶物种的繁殖策略。|近义词：barnacles (更具体的例子)|反义词：无

词源:
The word "cirripedia" is derived from the Latin words "cirrus" (a curl of hair or feather) and "pes" (foot).  This refers to the feathery appendages (cirri) that these crustaceans use for feeding, which resemble feet in function.  The "-pedia" suffix indicates a class or group of animals.
