exempt:/ɪɡˈzɛmpt/ | adj. | 免除的；豁免的 | Free from an obligation or liability imposed on others.  | Example:  He is exempt from military service. 他免服兵役。

The company is exempt from paying taxes this year.  这家公司今年免交税款。|近义词：excluded, excused, freed |反义词：liable, subject, obligated |词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is exempt from the new regulations. 他免受新规定的约束。
The students are exempt from the final exam. 学生们免考期末考试。

一般过去时:
She was exempt from jury duty. 她免除了陪审员的职责。
They were exempt from the usual fees. 他们免除了通常的费用。

现在完成时:
He has been exempt from taxes for several years. 他多年来一直免税。
The organization has been exempt from certain regulations. 该组织一直免除某些规章制度。

过去完成时:
She had been exempt from the previous rules. 她已经免除了之前的规则。
They had been exempt from paying the tax before the new law.  在新的法律出台之前，他们一直免交税款。

将来时:
He will be exempt from the upcoming assessment. 他将免除即将到来的评估。
They will be exempt from the new policy. 他们将免除新的政策。

虚拟语气:
If he were exempt, he would be very happy. 如果他免除，他会非常高兴。
I wish I were exempt from this tedious work. 我希望我能免除这项繁琐的工作。

被动语态:
He was exempted from the fee. 他被免除了费用。
They were exempted from the requirements. 他们被免除了要求。

疑问句:
Is he exempt from the rule? 他是否免除该规则？
Are they exempt from the penalty? 他们是否免除处罚？

否定句:
He is not exempt from the law. 他没有免除法律。
They are not exempt from the consequences. 他们没有免除后果。


PHRASE->

词源:
The word "exempt" comes from the Latin word "exemplum," meaning "example" or "model."  The sense shifted over time to "taken out" or "removed," leading to its modern meaning of being freed from an obligation.
