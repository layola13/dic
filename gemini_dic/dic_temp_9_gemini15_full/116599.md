matriculators:/məˈtrɪkjʊleɪtərz/ | n. |注册入学的学生；入学者|Those who have enrolled at a college or university, especially those newly enrolled.  The term often implies a formal process of matriculation has been completed.|  例句：The matriculators were given a welcome address by the university president.  入学新生受到了大学校长的欢迎致辞。

例句：This year's class of matriculators is the largest in the university's history.今年入学的新生人数是该大学历史上最多的。


近义词：enrollees, registrants, freshmen (for first-year students) | 反义词：graduates, alumni


词源:  The word "matriculators" derives from the verb "matriculate," which in turn comes from the Latin word "matriculāri."  "Matriculāri"  means "to enroll in a register," referring to the formal enrollment of students in a university's register or roll.  The suffix "-ors" indicates the people performing the action.
