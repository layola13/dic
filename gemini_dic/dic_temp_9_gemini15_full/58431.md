duck:/dʌk/| n. vi. vt.|鸭子；迅速低下头或躲藏；逃避；|a web-footed bird|例句：The ducks swam gracefully on the pond. 鸭子优雅地在池塘里游泳。|近义词：bird,fowl|反义词:无


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ducks swim in the lake. = 鸭子在湖里游泳。
He ducks his head to avoid the ball. = 他低下头躲避球。


一般过去时:
The duck quacked loudly yesterday. = 昨天鸭子大声嘎嘎叫。
She ducked quickly to avoid being seen. = 她迅速低下头以免被看见。


现在完成时:
The children have fed the ducks. = 孩子们已经喂了鸭子。
I have ducked several difficult questions. = 我已经回避了几个棘手的问题。


过去完成时:
The hunter had already shot the duck before I arrived. = 我到达之前猎人已经射杀了那只鸭子。
She had ducked behind the tree before the storm hit. =暴风雨来临之前，她已经躲到树后了。


将来时:
We will watch the ducks tomorrow. = 我们明天将去看鸭子。
He will duck the question again. = 他将再次回避这个问题。


虚拟语气:
If I were a duck, I would swim in the lake. = 如果我是一只鸭子，我就会在湖里游泳。
I wish I had ducked sooner to avoid the rain. = 我真希望我早点躲起来避雨。


被动语态:
The duck was chased by a fox. = 那只鸭子被狐狸追赶。
The question was ducked by the politician. = 这个问题被政客回避了。


疑问句:
Are those ducks wild? = 那些鸭子是野生的吗？
Did he duck the punch? = 他躲开了那拳吗？


否定句:
The ducks didn't swim today. = 鸭子今天没有游泳。
He didn't duck the responsibility. = 他没有逃避责任。

词源:
The word "duck" originates from the Old English word "dūce," which itself likely derives from a Proto-Germanic word.  The exact Proto-Germanic root is debated, but it's generally considered onomatopoeic, mimicking the sound the bird makes.
