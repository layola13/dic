regard:/rɪˈɡɑːrd/| n., vt.| 考虑；尊敬；关系；问候 | to consider or think of in a specified way; to show respect or admiration for; a feeling of respect or admiration; a particular way of considering or thinking about something; greetings |例句：I regard him as a friend. 我把他当朋友看待。|例句：He is held in high regard. 他受到高度尊重。|近义词：consider, respect, esteem, view, regards |反义词：disregard, disrespect, scorn


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I regard him as a genius. = 我认为他是个天才。
They regard this matter seriously. = 他们认真对待这件事。


一般过去时:
She regarded him with suspicion. = 她怀疑地看着他。
We regarded their proposal favorably. = 我们对他们的提议表示赞同。


现在完成时:
I have always regarded you highly. = 我一直很尊重你。
They have regarded this issue as unimportant. = 他们一直认为这个问题不重要。


过去完成时:
He had regarded her as a sister. = 他把她当作妹妹看待。
They had regarded the situation as hopeless. = 他们之前认为情况毫无希望。


将来时:
I will regard your advice carefully. = 我会仔细考虑你的建议。
They will regard this matter as confidential. = 他们将把此事视为机密。


虚拟语气:
If I regarded his words differently, I would have acted differently. = 如果我对他的话有不同的看法，我会采取不同的行动。
I wish I had regarded his warning more seriously. = 我真希望我当时更认真地对待他的警告。


被动语态:
His work is regarded as groundbreaking. = 他的作品被认为是开创性的。
The proposal was regarded with skepticism. = 这个提议受到了怀疑。


疑问句:
How do you regard this situation? = 你如何看待这种情况？
What do they regard as success? = 他们认为成功是什么？


否定句:
I do not regard him as a threat. = 我不认为他是一个威胁。
They did not regard her opinion seriously. = 他们没有认真对待她的意见。



PHRASE->
