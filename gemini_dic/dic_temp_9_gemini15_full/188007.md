rough-and-ready: /ˌrʌf ænd ˈredi/ | adj. |粗制滥造的；简陋但有效的；|粗糙的，简陋的，但足够实用；指东西或人快速制作或准备的，可能不完美但足够好用。它强调实用性而非精致。 |例句：He gave a rough-and-ready explanation of the situation. 他对情况作了粗略的解释。


The plan was rough-and-ready, but it worked. 计划很粗糙，但它成功了。|近义词： makeshift, improvised, provisional, ad hoc |反义词： refined, polished, sophisticated, elaborate


词源：

The term "rough-and-ready" combines two separate words:

* **rough:**  Meaning "not smooth, uneven, or unrefined."  Its origins trace back to Old English *rūh*, meaning "rough, harsh."

* **ready:** Meaning "prepared, willing, or quick." Its origins trace back to Old English *rāde*, meaning "ready, prepared."

The combination emphasizes the immediacy and practicality of something that might lack refinement or polish.
