colonialism:/kəˈloʊniəlɪzəm/ | n. |殖民主义 |The policy or practice of acquiring full or partial political control over another country, occupying it with settlers, and exploiting it economically.  Colonialism involves the subjugation of one people by another, often resulting in the displacement, oppression, and cultural destruction of the colonized population. |例句：The legacy of colonialism continues to affect many parts of the world. 殖民主义的遗产继续影响着世界许多地区。

例句：European colonialism profoundly shaped the political and economic landscapes of Africa and Asia. 欧洲殖民主义深刻地塑造了非洲和亚洲的政治和经济格局。


近义词：imperialism, dominion, subjugation |反义词：independence, self-governance, liberation |词性：名词


Etymology:

The word "colonialism" is derived from the word "colony," which in turn comes from the Latin word "colonia," meaning "a settlement of citizens in a new territory."  The suffix "-ism" denotes a system, practice, or ideology.  Thus, colonialism refers to the system or practice of establishing and maintaining colonies.
