equitancy:/ˈɛkwɪtənsi/| n.|公平；公正；正义|The state or quality of being equitable; fairness; justice.  It refers to the inherent fairness of a situation or action, often in legal or moral contexts.  |例句：The equitancy of the judge's decision was questioned by many. = 许多人质疑法官判决的公平性。


例句：The company strives for equitancy in its hiring practices. = 这家公司努力在其招聘实践中做到公平。


近义词：fairness, justice, impartiality, equity


反义词：injustice, unfairness, bias, partiality


词源:  Derived from the adjective "equitable," which ultimately comes from the Latin word "aequus," meaning "equal" or "level."  The suffix "-ancy" denotes a state or quality.
