preguessing: /ˌpriːˈɡɛsɪŋ/ | n. | 预先猜测，事先推测 | The act of guessing something before it happens or before having all the information.  It often involves making predictions based on limited evidence or prior knowledge. |  例句：The preguessing of the election results was wildly inaccurate.  选举结果的预先猜测是极其不准确的。


例句：His preguessing about the exam questions proved helpful. 他对考试题目的预先猜测证明是有帮助的。


近义词：prediction, anticipation, forecasting, speculation


反义词：hindsight (事后诸葛亮)


词性: 名词
