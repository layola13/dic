diegesis:/daɪˈdʒiːsɪs/| n.|情节，叙事，故事背景|The totality of what is known about the world of the narrative.  It includes the setting, characters, events, and the overall narrative structure. It refers to the story itself, as opposed to the way it is told (narration).|Example: The diegesis of the novel includes a detailed description of the Victorian-era London setting and its impact on the characters' lives.  这个小说的情节包括对维多利亚时代伦敦环境及其对人物生活的影响的详细描述。|近义词：narrative, story, plot|反义词：mimesis (showing), narration (telling)
