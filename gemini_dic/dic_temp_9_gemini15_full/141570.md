oppositionist: /ˌɒpəˈzɪʃənɪst/ | n. | 反对者，对抗者 | A person who opposes something, especially a political system or government.  | The oppositionist voiced strong objections to the new policy.  这位反对者强烈反对这项新政策。| opponent, dissident, protester | supporter, conformist | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is an oppositionist. = 他是一个反对者。
The oppositionist speaks out against the government. = 反对者公开反对政府。


一般过去时:
He was a fierce oppositionist during the revolution. = 他在革命期间是一个激烈的反对者。
The oppositionist protested against the unfair laws. = 反对者抗议不公平的法律。


现在完成时:
She has been a consistent oppositionist throughout her career. = 她一生都是一个始终如一的反对者。
The oppositionist has voiced their concerns repeatedly. = 反对者一再表达他们的担忧。


过去完成时:
He had been an oppositionist long before the current leader came to power. = 在现任领导人上台很久以前，他就是一个反对者。
The oppositionist had already prepared their arguments before the debate. = 在辩论之前，反对者已经准备好他们的论据。


将来时:
He will remain an oppositionist until the government changes its policies. = 他将继续成为反对者，直到政府改变其政策。
The oppositionist will continue to fight for their cause. = 反对者将继续为他们的事业而战。


虚拟语气:
If he were an oppositionist, he would speak up. = 如果他是一个反对者，他会大声疾呼。
I wish I were not an oppositionist, but I have to speak the truth. = 我希望我不是一个反对者，但我必须说实话。


被动语态:
The oppositionist was arrested for his protests. = 反对者因抗议而被捕。
The government's policies are opposed by many oppositionists. = 政府的政策受到许多反对者的反对。


疑问句:
Is he an oppositionist? = 他是一个反对者吗？
Are there many oppositionists in this country? = 这个国家有很多反对者吗？


否定句:
He is not an oppositionist. = 他不是一个反对者。
She was not an oppositionist; she simply disagreed with the policy. = 她不是反对者；她只是不同意这项政策。
