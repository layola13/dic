marshbanker:/ˈmɑːʃˌbæŋkər/| n. |沼泽银行家；（指）黑背鸥|A marshbanker is a colloquial name, primarily used in North America, for the black-backed gull ( *Larus marinus*).  It refers to its habit of nesting and foraging near marshes and banks.  The name suggests a connection to banks (of rivers or marshes) and perhaps implies a predatory nature, similar to how a banker might be seen as accumulating resources.|Example:  I saw a marshbanker swoop down and snatch a fish from the shallows. 我看到一只黑背鸥俯冲下来从浅滩抓到一条鱼。|近义词：black-backed gull, great black-backed gull|反义词：无
