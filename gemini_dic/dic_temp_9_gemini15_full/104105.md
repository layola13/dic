jump-with-both-feet: /dʒʌmp wɪð boʊθ fiːt/ | phrasal verb | 全力以赴，义无反顾地投入 | To commit oneself completely and enthusiastically to something, without hesitation or reservation.  | 例句：She jumped with both feet into her new career. 她全身心地投入到她的新事业中。  He jumped with both feet into the project, working long hours to meet the deadline. 他全力以赴投入到这个项目中，加班加点地赶工期。 | 近义词： wholeheartedly, fully commit, plunge in, dive in | 反义词：hesitate, waver, procrastinate

被动语态: (This phrase is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes a voluntary action.)


疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->
jump in with both feet = 全力投入
jump into something with both feet = 义无反顾地投入某事
jump feetfirst into something = 勇敢地投入某事
throw oneself into something with both feet = 全身心地投入某事
dive in headfirst = 义无反顾地投入


词源:  The phrase combines the literal meaning of "jump" (a sudden movement) with the figurative emphasis of "both feet" to signify complete and unwavering commitment.  There's no single historical event or text that pinpoints its origin, but its use reflects a common human experience of wholeheartedly embracing challenges or opportunities.
