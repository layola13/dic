civilized:/ˈsɪvəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 文明的；开化的；有教养的 |  Refers to a state of society or an individual characterized by advanced culture, refinement, and a high level of social and moral development. It implies a level of societal organization beyond mere survival, including things like established laws, social norms, and complex institutions.  |  例句：Many consider the invention of writing a crucial step in the development of civilized society.  许多人认为文字的发明是文明社会发展中的一个关键步骤。

例句：He was a civilized man, always polite and considerate of others. 他是一位文明的人，总是礼貌体贴他人。


近义词：cultured, refined, cultured, sophisticated, advanced | 反义词：barbaric, primitive, savage, uncultured, uncouth


词源:  Derived from the Old French word "civilise," ultimately from the Latin "civilis," meaning "civil," "of a citizen," "relating to citizens."  The root "civ-" comes from the Latin "civis," meaning "citizen."  The concept of "civilized" evolved from the idea of a well-ordered society of citizens living under established laws and customs.
