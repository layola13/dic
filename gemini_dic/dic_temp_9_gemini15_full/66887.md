expedience:/ɪkˈspiːdiəns/| n. |权宜之计；方便；便利；|The suitability of something as a means to an end, especially when it's convenient but not necessarily morally right.  Expedience often implies a pragmatic approach prioritizing immediate benefit over long-term consequences or ethical considerations.|例句：He acted out of expediency, not principle. 他出于权宜之计行事，而非出于原则。|例句：The expediency of the decision was questioned.  这个决定的权宜性受到了质疑。|近义词：convenience, practicality, opportunism |反义词：principle, morality, ethics
