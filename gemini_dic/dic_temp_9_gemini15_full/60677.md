electric-blues: /ɪˌlɛktrɪkˈbluːz/ | n. | 电蓝调 | A genre of blues music characterized by the use of electric guitars, amplification, and often a stronger rhythmic drive than acoustic blues.  It emerged in the 1930s and 1940s, evolving from the earlier Delta blues style.  | Example:  Many consider B.B. King a pioneer of electric blues.  许多人认为B.B. 金是电蓝调的先驱。 | 近义词: electric blues music, amplified blues | 反义词: acoustic blues
