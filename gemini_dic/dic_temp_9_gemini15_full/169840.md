proselyte:/ˈprɒsəlaɪt/| /ˈprɑːsəlaɪt/| n. |皈依者；改宗者 |A proselyte is a person who has converted from one religion or belief to another.  The term often carries a connotation of recent conversion and sometimes implies a less than fully committed or sincere belief. |例句：Many proselytes joined the new religious movement.  许多皈依者加入了新的宗教运动。

例句：He was a recent proselyte to Christianity. 他是最近才改信基督教的。


近义词：convert, adherent, disciple |反义词：apostate (叛教者)


词源:  From Late Latin *proselytus, from Greek prosēlytos (προσήλυτος), from pros- ("to," "towards") + ēlytos ("come," "having come").  The Greek word originally meant "a newcomer," "stranger," or "one who has come from elsewhere," but it came to refer to converts to Judaism, and later to other religions.
