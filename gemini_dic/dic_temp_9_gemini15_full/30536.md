cephalomere:/ˈsɛfələˌmɪər/| n. |头节，头部体节 |A cephalomere is a segment of the head of an arthropod, especially a crustacean.  It is a structural unit often associated with specific appendages and sensory organs. The term is less commonly used than other descriptors of head segmentation.  | Example:  The study focused on the development and evolution of cephalomeres in different crustacean species.  这项研究关注不同甲壳类物种头节的发育和进化。 |近义词：head segment, protocerebrum (in some contexts) |反义词：N/A |名词
