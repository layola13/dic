caliper:/ˈkælɪpər/ | n. |卡尺；测径器 | A caliper is a device used to measure the diameter or thickness of an object.  It typically consists of two legs with jaws that can be adjusted to fit the object being measured.  Calipers can be used for a variety of purposes, from woodworking and engineering to medicine and jewelry making. | Example:  The machinist used a caliper to precisely measure the diameter of the bolt.  机械师用卡尺精确测量螺栓的直径。  The doctor used a caliper to measure the patient's skin fold thickness. 医生用卡尺测量病人的皮褶厚度。|近义词：gauge, gauge, dividers |反义词：None.
