sensationalises: /ˌsɛnˈseɪʃənəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使轰动；夸大其词；耸人听闻地报道 | To present (something) in a sensational manner, often exaggerating or distorting facts to create excitement or interest.  | The media sensationalised the story, focusing on the most dramatic aspects and ignoring the underlying complexities.  媒体夸大了这个故事，专注于最戏剧性的方面，而忽略了潜在的复杂性。 | exaggerate, dramatize, hype | downplay, understate, minimize | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The tabloid media sensationalises every minor incident. = 小报媒体将每一个小事件都夸大其词。
He sensationalises his achievements to impress others. = 他夸大自己的成就以给他人留下深刻印象。


一般过去时:
The newspaper sensationalised the trial, causing public outrage. = 报纸夸大了审判，引起了公众的愤怒。
They sensationalised the accident to sell more papers. = 他们夸大这起事故以卖出更多报纸。


现在完成时:
The media has sensationalised the issue, creating widespread panic. = 媒体已经夸大了这个问题，造成了广泛的恐慌。
The politician has sensationalised his political stance to garner votes. = 这位政治家夸大了他的政治立场以争取选票。


过去完成时:
The press had sensationalised the story before the truth was revealed. = 在真相大白之前，媒体已经夸大了这个故事。
The public had already been sensationalised by the early reports. = 公众已经被早期的报道所误导。


将来时:
The media will sensationalise the upcoming election. = 媒体将会夸大即将到来的选举。
They will likely sensationalise the details to increase viewership. = 他们可能会夸大细节以增加收视率。


虚拟语气:
If they sensationalised the report, public trust would be lost. = 如果他们夸大报道，公众信任就会丧失。
I wish they hadn't sensationalised the situation so much. = 我希望他们没有如此夸大其词。


被动语态:
The incident was sensationalised by the press. = 这件事被媒体夸大了。
The story was sensationalised to boost sales. = 这个故事被夸大以提高销量。


疑问句:
Was the story sensationalised by the media? = 这个故事是被媒体夸大的吗？
Did they sensationalise the findings to generate more interest? = 他们是否夸大了调查结果以引起更多兴趣？


否定句:
The reporter did not sensationalise the facts in his article. = 记者在他的文章中没有夸大事实。
They refused to sensationalise the situation, opting for a balanced approach. = 他们拒绝夸大其词，选择了一种平衡的方式。


PHRASE->
sensationalise the news = 夸大新闻
sensationalise a crime = 夸大犯罪
sensationalise the truth = 夸大真相
sensationalise the issue = 夸大问题
sensationalise the event = 夸大事件


词源:
From "sensational" (causing great excitement or interest) + "-ise" (to make or become).  "Sensational" itself derives from "sensation" (a feeling or perception).
