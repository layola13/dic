officiation:/ˌɒfɪʃiˈeɪʃən/| n. |主持；行使职权；正式批准|The act of officiating; the performance of duties or ceremonies.  It often refers to the formal conducting of a ceremony, such as a wedding or a sporting event.|例句：The officiant pronounced them husband and wife. 主持人宣布他们成为夫妻。|例句：The officiant's role is crucial in ensuring the smooth conduct of the ceremony. 主持人的作用对于确保仪式顺利进行至关重要。|近义词：administration, conduct, performance, execution|反义词：neglect, disregard, abstention|名词

词源:
officiation comes from the verb "officiate," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "officium," meaning "duty," "office," or "service."  The suffix "-ation" is added to create a noun indicating the action or process of officiating.
