irritable:/ˈɪrɪtəbl/| adj.|易怒的，烦躁的|easily annoyed or angered.|He's become increasingly irritable since his illness. 他自从生病后变得越来越易怒了。|She was irritable and short-tempered. 她易怒且脾气暴躁。|calm, patient|good-tempered


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is irritable today. = 他今天很易怒。
She is always irritable in the mornings. = 她早上总是很烦躁。


一般过去时:
He was irritable yesterday. = 他昨天很易怒。
She was irritable because she was tired. = 她因为疲倦而易怒。


现在完成时:
He has been irritable all week. = 他整个星期都很易怒。
She has become more irritable recently. = 她最近变得更易怒了。


过去完成时:
He had been irritable for days before he finally snapped. = 在他最终爆发之前，他已经易怒了好几天了。
She had been irritable all day, so I didn't bother her. = 她整天都很烦躁，所以我没有打扰她。


将来时:
He will be irritable if he doesn't get enough sleep. = 如果他睡不够，他会变得易怒。
She will probably be irritable after the long flight. = 长途飞行后，她可能会很烦躁。


虚拟语气:
If he weren't so irritable, we could get along better. = 如果他不那么易怒，我们相处会更好。
I wish he weren't so irritable. = 我希望他不那么易怒。


被动语态:  (Irritable itself is rarely used passively.)


疑问句:
Is he irritable today? = 他今天易怒吗？
Was she irritable yesterday? = 她昨天易怒吗？


否定句:
He is not usually irritable. = 他通常并不易怒。
She wasn't irritable at all. = 她一点也不易怒。
