infinitesimals:/ˌɪnfɪnɪˈtɛsɪməlz/| n.|无穷小量|Extremely small quantities, approaching zero but not necessarily equal to zero.  They are often used in calculus and other branches of mathematics to represent quantities that are smaller than any assignable positive number. |Example: In differential calculus, infinitesimals are used to define derivatives.  在微积分中，无穷小量用于定义导数。|近义词：minute quantities, tiny amounts|反义词：infinite quantities, large amounts|名词
