hypersplenism:/ˌhaɪpərˈspliːnɪzəm/| n. |脾功能亢进|An enlargement of the spleen that results in increased destruction of blood cells.  This leads to low counts of red blood cells (anemia), white blood cells (leukopenia), and platelets (thrombocytopenia).|Example:  The patient presented with symptoms consistent with hypersplenism, including fatigue, pallor, and easy bruising.|近义词：splenomegaly (if referring to the enlarged spleen itself), cytopenia (if referring to the low blood cell counts)|反义词：There isn't a direct antonym, but a normal spleen function could be considered the opposite.|名词
