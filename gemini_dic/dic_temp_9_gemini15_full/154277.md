perissodactyl:/ˌpɛrɪsəˈdæktɪl/| n. |奇蹄类动物|A perissodactyl is a hoofed mammal with an odd number of toes on each foot, typically one or three.  The most notable characteristic is the weight-bearing axis passing through the third toe. This group includes horses, rhinoceroses, and tapirs.| Example: Horses, rhinoceroses, and tapirs are all examples of perissodactyls.  奇蹄目动物，例如马、犀牛和貘，都是奇蹄类动物。|近义词：ungulate (if referring to the hoofed characteristic)|反义词：artiodactyl (even-toed ungulates)
