/ˌfoʊnoʊtækˈtɪkəl/ | adj. | 音韵的；音节的 | Relating to phonotactics, the study of the permitted combinations of phonemes in a particular language.  Phonotactic constraints govern which sounds can occur together and in what order within a syllable or word. For example, English allows "str" at the beginning of a word (street), but not "rts" (this is phonotactically impossible).| The research focuses on the phonotactic constraints of the language. 这项研究侧重于该语言的音韵约束。 | 近义词：phonemic, phonetic | 反义词：无
