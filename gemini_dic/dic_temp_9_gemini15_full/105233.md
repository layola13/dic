英文解析:The KGB (Komitet Gosudarstvennoy Bezopasnosti), or Committee for State Security, was the main security agency for the Soviet Union from 1954 until its dissolution in 1991.  It was responsible for foreign intelligence, counter-intelligence, and domestic security. The KGB was known for its extensive surveillance networks, its use of informants, and its suppression of dissent.  Its operations often involved intimidation, assassination, and other extrajudicial actions.

Many feared the power and reach of the KGB during the Cold War.  在冷战期间，许多人都害怕克格勃的权力和影响力。


词源:  The acronym KGB comes from the Russian words Komitet Gosudarstvennoy Bezopasnosti (Комитет государственной безопасности).  Komitet means "committee," Gosudarstvennoy means "state," Bezopasnosti means "security."
