ophiolatry:/ˌɒfiəʊˈleɪtri/ | n. | 蛇崇拜 | The worship of snakes, often associated with ancient religions and cultures.  Ophiolatry is a form of zoolatry, the worship of animals.  Many cultures throughout history have revered snakes as symbols of power, healing, or fertility.  | Example:  The ancient Egyptians practiced ophiolatry, venerating snakes as embodiments of deities like Wadjet.  古代埃及人奉行蛇崇拜，将蛇视为瓦吉特等神灵的化身。 | 近义词: serpent worship, herpetolatry | 反义词:  None specifically, but concepts opposing religious animal veneration could be considered antonyms. | 名词
