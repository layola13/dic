self-punishers: /ˈsɛlf ˈpʌnɪʃərz/ | n. | 自我惩罚者 | Individuals who engage in self-destructive behaviors as a form of punishment for perceived failings or wrongdoing.  They may inflict physical or emotional harm upon themselves as a coping mechanism or a way to alleviate guilt or shame. The term often implies a cyclical pattern of behavior where self-punishment leads to further self-criticism and more self-destructive acts. | Example:  Some studies suggest that individuals with borderline personality disorder are more likely to be self-punishers. 一些研究表明，患有边缘性人格障碍的人更有可能成为自我惩罚者。


近义词: self-destructors, self-saboteurs, masochists (in extreme cases)

反义词: self-care practitioners, self-compassionate individuals


词性：名词 (plural)
