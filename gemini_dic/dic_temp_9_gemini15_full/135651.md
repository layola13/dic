nonpredicatively:/ˌnɒnprɪˈdɪkətɪvli/ | adv. |非谓语地|  The adverb "nonpredicatively" describes the use of a word, phrase, or clause that does not function as a predicate in a sentence.  It indicates that the element in question is not making an assertion or statement about the subject.  Instead, it might be acting as a modifier, complement, or part of a different grammatical structure.  The term is primarily used in linguistics and grammatical analysis. | The adjective "beautiful" is used nonpredicatively in the phrase "a beautiful flower." | 近义词:  non-predicatively | 反义词: predicatively
