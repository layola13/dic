generalises:/ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪzɪz/| v.|概括；推广；普遍化 | To make a general statement about something; to draw general conclusions from particular instances; to apply something widely or generally. | 例句：The study generalises the findings to the entire population.  这项研究将调查结果推广到整个人群。

The professor generalised about the students' laziness. 教授概括了学生们的懒惰。


近义词：summarize, extend, apply widely, generalize (美式拼写) | 反义词：specify, detail, particularize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He generalises about politics too much. 他对政治的概括太多了。
She generalises her experiences to others. 她将自己的经验推广到其他人身上。


一般过去时:
The scientist generalised his results. 科学家概括了他的研究结果。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

I wish she hadn't generalised so broadly about the situation. 我希望她不要对这种情况进行如此广泛的概括。


被动语态:
The results were generalised to the whole sample group. 结果被推广到整个样本组。
The findings were generalised inappropriately. 这些发现被不恰当地概括了。


疑问句:
Does he often generalise about people's behavior? 他经常对人们的行为进行概括吗？

否定句:
He doesn't generalise easily about complex situations. 他不会轻易地对复杂的情况进行概括。
She didn't generalise her observations to other contexts. 她没有将她的观察结果推广到其他语境。

词源:
From the verb "generalize" (American spelling), ultimately from Latin *generalis ("general"), from genus ("kind, race").  The "-ise" ending is a common British English suffix for verb formation.
