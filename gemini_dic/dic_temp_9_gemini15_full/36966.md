colporter:/kɒlˈpɔːtə(r)/| n. |小贩；（尤指）宗教书籍小贩|A colporter is a person who travels around selling goods, especially religious books or pamphlets.  They often sell their wares door-to-door or at public gatherings.  The term carries a historical connotation, suggesting a more itinerant and less established form of commerce than a typical shopkeeper.|The colporter sold Bibles and religious tracts in the small villages.|近义词：peddler, hawker, vendor|反义词：retailer (in a fixed location)
