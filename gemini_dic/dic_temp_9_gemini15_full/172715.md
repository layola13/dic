quacked:/kwækt/| v.|嘎嘎叫；吹嘘；说谎 |To make the characteristic sound of a duck or similar bird.  It can also mean to speak in a boastful or insincere manner, or to utter something false or deceptive.|例句：The ducks quacked loudly in the pond.  鸭子在池塘里大声嘎嘎叫。The salesman quacked about the amazing features of the product.  推销员夸夸其谈地介绍了产品的惊人功能。|近义词：gobble, quacking, croaked|反义词：whispered, mumbled


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ducks quack every morning. = 鸭子每天早上都嘎嘎叫。
He often quacks about his achievements. = 他经常吹嘘自己的成就。

一般过去时:
The duck quacked before it flew away. = 鸭子飞走之前嘎嘎叫了一声。
He quacked a whole story about his trip. = 他吹嘘了一大堆关于他旅行的故事。

现在完成时:
The ducks have quacked all afternoon. = 鸭子整个下午都在嘎嘎叫。
He has quacked so much that nobody believes him anymore. = 他吹嘘了这么多，以至于没有人再相信他了。

过去完成时:
The ducks had quacked before I even woke up. = 我还没醒，鸭子就已经嘎嘎叫了。
He had quacked about his wealth before anyone knew him. = 在任何人认识他之前，他就已经吹嘘他的财富了。

将来时:
The ducks will quack when they see food. = 鸭子看到食物就会嘎嘎叫。
He will quack about his new car all day long. = 他会整天吹嘘他的新车。

虚拟语气:
If the ducks quacked, I would know they were there. = 如果鸭子嘎嘎叫，我就知道它们在那里。
If he hadn't quacked so much, people would have believed him. = 如果他不那么夸夸其谈，人们就会相信他了。

被动语态:
The sound of the quacking was heard across the lake. = 鸭子的叫声传遍了整个湖面。(This is a less common usage for "quack")
His lies were quickly discovered even though he quacked about them. = 尽管他夸夸其谈，他的谎言很快就被揭穿了。

疑问句:
Did the ducks quack this morning? = 鸭子今天早上嘎嘎叫了吗？
Did he quack about his success again? = 他又吹嘘他的成功了吗？

否定句:
The ducks didn't quack last night. = 鸭子昨晚没有嘎嘎叫。
He didn't quack about his new job at all. = 他根本没有吹嘘他的新工作。
