counterclaimed:/ˌkaʊntə(r)ˈkleɪmd/| v.|反驳；反诉；提出反请求|To make a counterclaim is to present a claim in opposition to another claim, often in a legal context.  It involves asserting a different version of events or a different set of facts to refute the original claim.  |例句：The defendant counterclaimed that the plaintiff had breached the contract.  被告反驳说原告违反了合同。

例句：After the plaintiff sued for damages, the defendant counterclaimed for breach of contract. 原告起诉索赔后，被告反诉合同违约。


近义词：rebut, refute, contest, oppose|反义词： concede, admit, accept


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He counterclaims that the evidence is fabricated. 他反驳说证据是伪造的。
She counterclaims in the divorce proceedings. 她在离婚诉讼中提出反诉。


一般过去时:
The lawyer counterclaimed against the opposing party. 律师对对方提出了反诉。
They counterclaimed that they were not responsible for the accident. 他们反驳说他们不应对事故负责。


现在完成时:
The company has counterclaimed, seeking compensation for lost profits. 公司已经提出反诉，寻求赔偿损失的利润。
He has counterclaimed several times during the trial. 他在审判期间多次提出反诉。


过去完成时:
Before the judge ruled, the defense had counterclaimed for damages. 在法官作出裁决之前，被告方已经提出反诉要求赔偿。
The plaintiff had counterclaimed before the defendant could present their evidence. 原告在被告方提出证据之前已经提出反诉。
