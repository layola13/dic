retirer:/rəˈtɑːr/|/rɪˈtaɪər/| v.|退休；撤退；收回；解雇 |To withdraw from one's position or profession, especially upon reaching a certain age or after a long period of service.  Also to withdraw or remove something. |例句：He retired from the army at the age of 60. 他60岁从军队退休了。|例句：The general ordered his troops to retire from the battlefield. 将军命令他的部队从战场撤退。|近义词：retire, withdraw, retreat, resign|反义词：begin, start, join, enlist


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He retires next year. = 他明年退休。
She retires early every day. = 她每天都早退。

一般过去时:
He retired from his job last year. = 他去年从工作中退休了。
They retired to their cabin for the weekend. = 他们周末退回到他们的乡间小屋。

现在完成时:
He has retired from professional football. = 他已经退出了职业足球。
She has retired to her room to read. = 她已经退回到房间看书了。

过去完成时:
He had retired before his health deteriorated. = 他的健康恶化之前他已经退休了。
They had retired to bed when the fire alarm sounded. = 火灾警报响起时，他们已经上床睡觉了。

将来时:
He will retire next month. = 他下个月将退休。
She will retire to her seaside cottage. = 她将退到她海边的别墅。

虚拟语气:
If he retired, he would have more free time. = 如果他退休了，他就会有更多空闲时间。
I wish I could retire early. = 我希望我能早点退休。

被动语态:
He was retired from the company. = 他被公司解雇了。
The proposal was retired from consideration. = 该提案已被撤回。

疑问句:
When will you retire? = 你什么时候退休？
Has he retired yet? = 他退休了吗？

否定句:
He didn't retire until he was 70. = 直到70岁他才退休。
She hasn't retired yet. = 她还没退休。


PHRASE->
retire from: 从...退休

retire a debt: 偿还债务
retire a product: 下架产品
retire hurt: 受伤退场
