prefigurement: /priːˈfɪɡjərment/ | n. | 预兆；预示；先兆 | The act of prefiguring; something that prefigures something else.  It suggests a foreshadowing or symbolizing of a future event or state. |例句：The ancient rituals served as a prefigurement of the later religious ceremonies. 古代仪式预示了后来的宗教仪式。

The unsettling dreams were a prefigurement of the difficult times to come.  令人不安的梦预示着即将到来的艰难时期。|近义词：foreshadowing, premonition, portent, omen |反义词：aftermath, consequence, result
