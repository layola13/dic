lineated:/ˈlaɪnieɪtɪd/| adj.| 有线条的；有行线的；排列成行的 | Marked with or divided into lines.  The poem was beautifully lineated, with each line contributing to the overall rhythm and meaning.  |近义词：striped, striated, ruled,  lined | 反义词：unlined, blank


The poem was beautifully lineated, with each line contributing to the overall rhythm and meaning. = 这首诗的排版很漂亮，每一行都对整体的节奏和意义做出了贡献。

The architect created a lineated design for the building's facade. = 建筑师为建筑物的正面设计了一个有线条的设计。


词源:  From Late Latin *lineātus, past participle of lineāre "to mark with lines," from līnea "line."  Ultimately derived from Proto-Indo-European *leig- "to lie, to be situated," reflecting the concept of lines lying across a surface.
