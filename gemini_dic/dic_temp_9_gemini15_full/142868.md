out-measuring: /ˌaʊtˈmɛʒərɪŋ/ | v. | 超过…的测量；胜过…的测量 |  To surpass in measurement; to exceed in measurement.  This is a relatively rare word, formed by combining "out-" (meaning exceeding or surpassing) and "measuring" (the act of determining size, quantity, or extent).  It implies a comparison where one thing is greater in size or quantity than another. | The new bridge out-measures the old one in length by a considerable margin. = 新桥的长度大大超过了旧桥。


The architect's design out-measures the initial specifications in terms of space utilization. = 建筑师的设计在空间利用方面超过了最初的规格。


近义词: surpassing, exceeding, outstripping, outdoing


反义词: undermeasuring, falling short of


词性: Verb (present participle)
