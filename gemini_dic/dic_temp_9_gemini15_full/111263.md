WORD ORIGIN->

The word "litigiousness" is derived from the adjective "litigious," which comes from the Latin word "litigiosus," meaning "quarrelsome," "contentious," or "inclined to litigation."  "-ious" is a suffix forming adjectives, and "-ness" is a suffix creating nouns signifying a quality or state. Therefore, litigiousness directly reflects the state of being litigious.
