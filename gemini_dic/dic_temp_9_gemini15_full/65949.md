exactitude:/ɪɡˈzæktɪtjuːd/| n. |精确性；准确性 |The exactitude of the measurements was crucial to the experiment's success.  测量结果的精确性对实验的成功至关重要。| precision, accuracy, correctness | approximation, vagueness, inaccuracy | 名词


近义词: precision, accuracy, correctness
反义词: approximation, vagueness, inaccuracy


词源:  From Middle French *exactitude, from Latin *exactitudo, from exactus (past participle of exigere) "to demand, require, drive out," from ex- "out" + agere "to drive, do."  The meaning evolved from the sense of "demanding exactness" to the quality of exactness itself.


用法说明:

exactitude 主要指精确性和准确性，强调结果与标准或预期的一致性。它通常用于描述测量、计算、陈述或行为的精确程度。与 precision (精确度) 和 accuracy (准确度) 类似，但 exactitude 更强调整体的精确性，包含了对细节的关注和对错误的容忍度极低。


例句:

1. The architect insisted on the utmost exactitude in the construction plans. 建筑师坚持在施工图纸中做到最高的精确性。

2.  The historian's exactitude in citing sources was commendable. 这位历史学家在引用资料方面的精确性值得称赞。

3. Scientific research demands a high degree of exactitude. 科研工作需要高度的精确性。

4.  He was known for his exactitude in his work, leaving no room for error. 他以工作精确而闻名，不容许任何错误。

5. The committee demanded greater exactitude in the financial reporting. 委员会要求财务报告更加精确。


注意：exactitude 通常不用于描述人的性格或行为方式，而更侧重于客观的事物或结果。  使用时应注意其正式的语体风格。
