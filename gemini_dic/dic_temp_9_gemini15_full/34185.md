clamoured:/ˈklæməd/| v.|喧闹，吵闹，大声要求|To make a loud confused noise, especially of many people speaking or shouting at the same time; to demand something loudly.|The crowd clamoured for his resignation.  人群大声要求他辞职。|The children clamoured for attention. 孩子们吵着要引起注意。|shouted, yelled, protested|silent, quiet|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The crowd clamours for change. = 人群大声要求改变。
They clamour for better working conditions. = 他们大声要求更好的工作条件。

一般过去时:
The protesters clamoured outside the parliament building. = 抗议者在议会大厦外大声抗议。
The audience clamoured for an encore. = 观众大声要求再来一首。

现在完成时:
The public has clamoured for answers to this scandal. = 公众一直在大声要求对这起丑闻给出答案。
They have clamoured for justice for many years. = 他们多年来一直大声要求正义。

过去完成时:
The people had clamoured for reform for decades before the revolution. = 在革命之前，人民几十年来一直大声要求改革。
She had clamoured for attention her entire life. = 她一生都在大声要求关注。

将来时:
The students will clamour for more resources next semester. = 下学期学生们将大声要求更多资源。
The workers will clamour for a pay rise. = 工人们将大声要求加薪。

虚拟语气:
If they clamoured louder, they might have gotten what they wanted. = 如果他们喊得更大声，他们可能会得到他们想要的东西。
I wish they hadn't clamoured so much. = 我真希望他们不要吵吵闹闹这么多。

被动语态:
Their demands were clamoured for by the majority. = 他们的要求得到了大多数人的强烈要求。
Changes were clamoured for by the public. = 公众强烈要求改变。


疑问句:
Were they clamouring for a better future? = 他们是在大声要求更好的未来吗？
Is the crowd clamouring for a new leader? = 人群是在大声要求一位新的领导人吗？


否定句:
The people did not clamour for war. = 人民没有大声要求战争。
They didn't clamour for his dismissal. = 他们没有大声要求解雇他。


PHRASE->
clamour for something = 大声要求某事物
clamour against something = 大声反对某事物
clamour to do something = 吵着要做什么事
clamour with something = 用某物喧闹
a clamour of voices = 一片喧闹声
