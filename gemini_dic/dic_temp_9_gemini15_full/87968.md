high-graded:/ˈhaɪˌɡreɪdɪd/ | adj. | 高品位的；优质的；精选的 |  Describes something of superior quality or grade, often implying selection of the best parts or materials.  It often refers to ore, but can also apply to other products or materials. |

例句：
The mine produced high-graded ore, resulting in higher profits.  这家矿山生产高品位矿石，从而获得更高的利润。

The company only uses high-graded components in its products. 这家公司在其产品中只使用高等级的组件。


近义词：superior, prime, top-quality, choice, select

反义词：low-grade, inferior, substandard

词源:

The word is a compound of "high" and "graded".  "High" indicates a superior level or quality. "Graded" refers to being sorted or classified according to quality or size. The combination describes something that has been selected for its superior quality after a grading process.
