holometabola: /ˌhɒləʊmɛˈtæbələr/ /ˌhoʊləʊmɛˈtæbəlɑː/ | n. | 完全变态昆虫 |  Insects undergoing complete metamorphosis, characterized by four distinct life stages: egg, larva, pupa, and adult.  This contrasts with incomplete metamorphosis (hemimetabola), where the insect gradually develops through nymph stages.  Examples include butterflies, beetles, and flies. | The butterfly exhibits holometabolous development, transforming from a larva (caterpillar) to a pupa (chrysalis) before emerging as an adult.  |近义词：complete metamorphosis | 反义词：hemimetabola (incomplete metamorphosis)
