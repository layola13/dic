labyrinthodon:/ˌlæbəˈrɪnθədɒn/| n. |迷齿类动物|An extinct group of amphibians characterized by their labyrinthine tooth structure.  These animals were large, often reaching several meters in length, and were prevalent during the Paleozoic Era.  They possessed a sprawling gait and were likely semi-aquatic. | Example: The discovery of a new labyrinthodont fossil sheds light on the evolution of early amphibians.  迷齿类动物新化石的发现揭示了早期两栖动物的进化。|近义词: temnospondyl (a specific type of labyrinthodont)|反义词: N/A
