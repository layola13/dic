respit:/ˈrɛspɪt/| n. v.| 喘息；暂缓；缓刑；延期 | a short period of rest or relief from something difficult or unpleasant | The ceasefire gave both sides a much-needed respite. | 近义词：rest, pause, reprieve | 反义词：continuation, persistence | 名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gives himself a short respite after work. = 他下班后给自己短暂的休息时间。
The soldiers need a respite from the constant fighting. = 士兵们需要从持续的战斗中得到喘息。


一般过去时:
The rain gave us a brief respite from the heat. = 雨给我们带来了短暂的凉爽。
The judge granted the defendant a respite from his sentence. = 法官给予被告缓刑。


现在完成时:
We have had no respite from the noise all day. = 我们一整天都没有从噪音中得到解脱。
The company has finally found respite from its financial difficulties. = 公司终于摆脱了财务困境。


过去完成时:
They had enjoyed a brief respite before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们享受了一段短暂的平静。
The soldiers had found little respite during the long campaign. = 在漫长的战役中，士兵们几乎没有得到喘息。


将来时:
We will have a respite from work next week. = 下周我们将休息一下。
The doctor hopes the new medication will provide some respite from the pain. = 医生希望新药能缓解一些疼痛。


虚拟语气:
If only we could have a respite from this endless pressure. = 要是我们能从这无休止的压力中解脱出来就好了。
I wish I could find a respite from my worries. = 我希望我能摆脱我的担忧。


被动语态:
A respite was granted to the prisoners. = 囚犯们得到了缓刑。
Some respite was offered to the exhausted workers. = 精疲力尽的工人们得到了一些喘息的机会。


疑问句:
Will there be any respite from the heatwave? = 热浪会有缓解吗？
Is there any respite in sight for the struggling economy? = 对于挣扎的经济来说，有什么缓解的迹象吗？


否定句:
There was no respite from the relentless attacks. = 没有从无情的攻击中解脱出来。
She found no respite from her grief. = 她没有从悲伤中解脱出来。


PHRASE->
give respite = 给予喘息的机会
offer respite = 提供喘息的机会
a brief respite = 短暂的喘息
much-needed respite = 急需的喘息
a period of respite = 一段喘息的时间
