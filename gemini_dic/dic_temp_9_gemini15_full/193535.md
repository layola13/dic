self-actualization: /ˌsɛlfˌæktʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |自我实现，自我实现的倾向 | The realization or fulfillment of one's talents and potentialities, especially considered as a drive or need present in everyone.  It refers to the process of becoming the best possible version of oneself. | 例句：He found self-actualization through painting. 他通过绘画找到了自我实现。


例句：Through years of dedicated practice, she achieved self-actualization in her musical career. 通过多年的刻苦练习，她在音乐事业上实现了自我实现。


近义词：self-realization, fulfillment, self-development | 反义词：self-destruction, stagnation, unfulfillment | 词性: 名词

词源:  The word is a combination of "self" and "actualization."  "Actualization" comes from the verb "actualize," meaning "to make real or actual," which in turn derives from the Latin word "actuālis" (actual).  Therefore, self-actualization literally means "the making real of oneself."
