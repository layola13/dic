microfracturing: /ˌmaɪkroʊˈfrækʃərɪŋ/ | n. | 微裂 | The process of forming numerous small cracks or fractures in a material, often due to stress or fatigue.  It is typically invisible to the naked eye and can weaken the material significantly. |例句：The rock experienced microfracturing due to the tectonic pressure.  岩石由于构造压力而发生了微裂。

例句：Extensive microfracturing was observed in the concrete after the earthquake. 地震后在混凝土中观察到广泛的微裂。


近义词：cracking, fissuring, fracturing (on a smaller scale)


反义词：consolidation, strengthening, healing


词性: 名词
