regretter:/rɛɡrɛtˈeɪ/|/rɛɡrɛtˈeɪ/| vt.|后悔，遗憾|To feel sorry about something that has happened or something that you have done.  It often implies a sense of sadness or wishing things were different.|例句：I regret telling him the truth. 我后悔告诉他真相。|例句：She deeply regretted missing the concert. 她非常后悔错过了这场音乐会。|近义词：rue, repent, be sorry for|反义词：rejoice, celebrate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I regret my decision. = 我后悔我的决定。
We regret to inform you that... = 我们很遗憾地通知你...

一般过去时:
He regretted his actions. = 他后悔他的行为。
They regretted missing the opportunity. = 他们后悔错过了这个机会。

现在完成时:
I have regretted this ever since. = 从那以后我一直后悔。
She has regretted her hasty words. = 她一直后悔自己说过的话。

过去完成时:
I had regretted my choice before I even finished the project. = 我在项目完成之前就后悔我的选择了。
He had regretted his decision long before the consequences became apparent. = 他在后果显现之前很久就后悔他的决定了。


将来时:
I will regret this if I don't act now. = 如果我现在不采取行动，我会后悔的。
They will regret their inaction later. = 他们以后会后悔自己的不作为。


虚拟语气:
I would regret not trying. = 我会后悔没有尝试。
I wish I hadn’t made that mistake; I regret it deeply. = 我真希望我没有犯那个错误；我非常后悔。

被动语态:
His absence was deeply regretted by all. = 大家都非常遗憾他的缺席。
The decision was later regretted. = 这个决定后来被后悔了。

疑问句:
Do you regret anything? = 你后悔什么吗？
Will you regret this decision in the future? = 你将来会后悔这个决定吗？

否定句:
I don't regret a single thing. = 我一点也不后悔。
She doesn't regret her choices. = 她不后悔自己的选择。


PHRASE->
regret to say = 遗憾地说
regret to inform you = 遗憾地通知你
deeply regret = 深深后悔
have no regrets = 毫无悔意
I regret that... = 我遗憾的是...
