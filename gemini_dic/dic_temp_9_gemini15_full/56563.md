dominicans:/dɒmɪˈnɪkənz/ | n. | 多米尼加人；多米尼加教团成员 |  Dominicans refers to people from the Dominican Republic or members of the Dominican Order, a Roman Catholic order founded by Saint Dominic.  The word can be used as a plural noun referring to multiple people or members.


例句：

Many Dominicans migrated to the United States in search of better opportunities.  许多多米尼加人为了寻求更好的机会而移居美国。

The Dominican friars played a significant role in the spread of Christianity. 多米尼加修士在基督教的传播中发挥了重要作用。


近义词：Dominican Republic residents (for people from the Dominican Republic),  Order of Preachers (for members of the Dominican Order)


反义词：无直接反义词，需根据语境而定 (例如，若指多米尼加人，则反义词可以是其他国家的人民)


词性：名词
