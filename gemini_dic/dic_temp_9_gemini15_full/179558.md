reeling:/ˈriːlɪŋ/| v. n. adj.| 旋转；蹒跚；摇晃； reeling from (震惊，打击等)|  to move unsteadily, as if about to fall; to feel dizzy or disoriented; to wind on a reel;  a series of events, typically unpleasant ones|近义词：stagger, sway, totter, spin |反义词：steady, stable, balanced

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is reeling from the news. = 他正被这个消息震惊得不知所措。
The dancer is reeling gracefully across the stage. = 舞者优雅地在舞台上旋转。


一般过去时:
She reeled back in shock. = 她震惊地向后踉跄。
The fisherman reeled in his line. = 渔夫收起他的鱼线。


现在完成时:
I have been reeling since I heard the bad news. = 自从听到这个坏消息后，我一直感到晕头转向。
They have reeled in a huge catch of fish. = 他们已经捕获了大量的鱼。


过去完成时:
He had reeled from the blow before he fell to the ground. = 他在倒地之前已经被打击得东倒西歪了。
The film had already reeled before we arrived. = 我们到达之前电影已经开始播放了。


将来时:
She will reel from the impact of the accident. = 她将会因为事故的影响而感到震惊。
They will reel the cable back into the ship. = 他们将会把电缆收回到船上。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't drunk so much, I wouldn't be reeling now. = 如果我没有喝那么多酒，我现在就不会这么晕了。
If he had reeled in the fish sooner, it wouldn't have escaped. = 如果他早点收线，鱼就不会跑了。


被动语态:
The film was reeled off the projector. = 胶片从放映机上放下来了。
The cable is being reeled out slowly. = 电缆正在慢慢地放出来。


疑问句:
Is he still reeling from the loss? = 他还在为失去的东西而感到震惊吗？
Are you reeling the fishing line in? = 你在收鱼线吗？


否定句:
He didn't reel from the punch. = 他没有被那一拳打倒。
She didn't reel the yarn into a ball. = 她没有把纱线卷成球。


PHRASE->
reel in: 收线；吸引
reel off: 快速地说出；连续不断地做
reel back: 向后仰；震惊地后退
reel under: 承受不住；被压垮
reel it in: 控制自己；冷静下来
