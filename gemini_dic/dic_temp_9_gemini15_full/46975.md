/dɑːrˈvɑːzɑː/ | n. | 达尔瓦扎 | Darvaza refers to the Darvaza gas crater, also known as the "Door to Hell," a natural gas field in Turkmenistan that collapsed into a large crater in 1971.  Geologists set it afire to prevent the spread of methane gas, and it has been burning continuously ever since.  The name itself is Turkmen, meaning "gate" or "door." | The Darvaza gas crater is a popular tourist attraction, despite its remote location. | 近义词: gas crater, burning pit | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but could consider terms like "extinguished fire" or "sealed well") |  名词
