praefoliation:/ˌpriːfoʊliˈeɪʃən/| n. |叶的预先形成；芽的发生|The arrangement of leaves in a bud before they unfold.  It refers to the way leaves are packed within a bud before they emerge and expand.  This arrangement can vary greatly depending on the species of plant.|Example: The praefoliation of the oak bud is imbricate, meaning the leaves overlap like shingles on a roof.  橡树芽的叶序是覆瓦状的，这意味着叶子像屋顶上的瓦片一样重叠。|近义词：vernation (more common term)|反义词：无
