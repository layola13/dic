love-ins: /ˈlʌvɪnz/ | n. |爱情示威，示爱活动 | A love-in is a gathering of people, often hippies in the 1960s, expressing their love and affection for one another, often through music, dancing, and other forms of free expression.  It's a demonstration of peace and love, often in contrast to the social and political tensions of the time. |  The 1960s saw many love-ins across the United States as a way to promote peace and counter the Vietnam War.  | 近义词：peace rally, demonstration of affection | 反义词：protest, riot
