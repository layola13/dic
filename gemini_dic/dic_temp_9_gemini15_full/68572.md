fanning:/ˈfænɪŋ/| v.|扇动；煽动；吹拂|The act of waving something, such as a hand or a fan, to create a current of air; to stir up or encourage something, often negative.|例句：She was fanning herself with a palm leaf. 她用棕榈叶扇着自己。|例句：They accused him of fanning the flames of racial hatred. 他们指责他煽动种族仇恨。|近义词：stirring, agitating, blowing|反义词：stilling, calming, suppressing|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fans the fire. = 他扇火。
The wind fans the flames. = 风扇动着火焰。

一般过去时:
She fanned herself with a newspaper. = 她用报纸扇自己。
The breeze fanned the leaves. = 微风吹拂着树叶。

现在完成时:
The activists have fanned the protests across the country. = 激进分子已经在全国范围内煽动抗议活动。
He has fanned the embers of the fire to keep it going. = 他一直在煽动余烬以保持火势。

过去完成时:
They had fanned the flames of discontent for months before the riot. = 暴乱发生前几个月，他们一直在煽动不满情绪。
She had fanned the flames of the fire before the firefighters arrived. = 在消防员到达之前，她已经扇动了火焰。

将来时:
He will fan the embers of the fire. = 他将扇动余烬。
They will fan the flames of rebellion. = 他们将煽动叛乱。

虚拟语气:
If he fanned the fire harder, it would burn brighter. = 如果他更用力地扇火，火就会烧得更旺。
I wish I hadn't fanned the flames of the argument. = 我真希望我没有煽动这场争论。

被动语态:
The fire was fanned by the wind. = 火被风扇动了。
The protests were fanned by social media. = 抗议活动被社交媒体煽动。

疑问句:
Is she fanning the fire? = 她是在扇火吗？

否定句:
He didn't fan the flames of the controversy. = 他没有煽动这场争议。
She didn't fan herself because it wasn't hot. = 她没有扇自己，因为不热。
