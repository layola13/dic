facing:/ˈfeɪsɪŋ/| v. adj. |面对；朝向；对抗|To be positioned or directed towards something; to confront or deal with something.  |例句：The house is facing the sea.  房子面向大海。|例句：She's facing a difficult challenge. 她正面临一个艰难的挑战。|近义词：confronting, encountering, opposing |反义词：avoiding, ignoring, shunning


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is facing the music. = 他正在面对现实。
The building is facing south. = 这栋建筑物朝南。


一般过去时:
They faced many difficulties. = 他们面临许多困难。
I faced my fears. = 我直面我的恐惧。


现在完成时:
She has faced numerous challenges in her career. = 她在她的事业中面临无数挑战。
We have faced similar problems before. = 我们以前也面临过类似的问题。


过去完成时:
He had faced bankruptcy before starting his own business. = 在创业之前，他曾面临破产。
They had faced numerous setbacks before achieving success. = 在取得成功之前，他们经历了无数的挫折。


将来时:
We will face many obstacles in the future. = 我们将来会面临许多障碍。
She will face a tough decision tomorrow. = 她明天将面临一个艰难的决定。


虚拟语气:
If I were facing the same situation, I would do the same. = 如果我面对同样的情况，我会做同样的事。
I wish I weren't facing this problem. = 我真希望我没有面临这个问题。


被动语态:
The problem is being faced by the government. = 这个问题正在由政府处理。
The issue was faced by the committee last week. = 上周委员会讨论了这个问题。


疑问句:
What challenges are you facing? = 你正在面临什么挑战？
Is he facing any difficulties? = 他面临任何困难吗？


否定句:
They are not facing any serious problems. = 他们没有面临任何严重的问题。
She didn't face the consequences of her actions. = 她没有面对她行为的后果。


PHRASE->
face to face = 面对面
face the facts = 面对现实
in the face of = 面对
lose face = 丢脸
save face = 保全面子
