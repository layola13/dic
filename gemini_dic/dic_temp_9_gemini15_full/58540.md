dug-up:/dʌɡ ʌp/| v.| 挖掘出；发现；揭露 | To remove something from the ground by digging; to discover something hidden or forgotten; to bring something to light, often something unpleasant or scandalous. |例句：The archaeologists dug up ancient artifacts. 考古学家挖掘出了古代文物。

例句：The journalist dug up a scandalous story about the politician.  记者挖出了关于这位政治家的丑闻。


近义词：unearth, excavate, uncover, discover, reveal

反义词：bury, conceal, hide


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They dig up the garden every spring. = 他们每年春天都翻耕花园。
We dig up potatoes every year. = 我们每年都挖土豆。


一般过去时:
The dog dug up the bone. = 狗挖出了骨头。
The police dug up evidence of the crime. = 警方挖出了犯罪证据。


现在完成时:
They have dug up all the potatoes. = 他们已经挖出了所有的土豆。
The team has dug up some interesting information. =  团队已经挖掘出一些有趣的信息。


过去完成时:
They had dug up the garden before the rain started. = 下雨前他们已经翻耕了花园。
The detectives had dug up crucial information before the trial. = 侦探在审判前已经挖掘出关键信息。


将来时:
We will dig up the garden next week. = 我们下周将要翻耕花园。
They will dig up the buried treasure. = 他们将要挖掘出埋藏的宝藏。


虚拟语气:
If they dug up the evidence, they would prove his guilt. = 如果他们挖出证据，他们就能证明他有罪。
I wish they had dug up more information. = 我希望他们能挖掘出更多信息。


被动语态:
The garden was dug up yesterday. = 花园昨天被翻耕了。
The truth was dug up by the reporter. = 真相被记者揭露了。


疑问句:
Did they dig up anything interesting? = 他们挖到什么有趣的东西了吗？
Have they dug up all the evidence yet? = 他们已经挖出所有证据了吗？


否定句:
They didn't dig up anything valuable. = 他们没有挖到任何有价值的东西。
The police didn't dig up enough evidence to convict him. = 警方没有挖到足够的证据来定他的罪。


PHRASE->
dig up dirt on someone = 挖出某人的丑闻
dig up old memories = 挖掘旧的回忆
dig up the past = 挖掘过去
dig up information = 挖掘信息
dig up roots = 挖根



词源:
The word "dug" is the past tense of the verb "dig," which comes from Old English *dician, meaning "to dig" or "to ditch."  The "up" is an adverb that emphasizes the upward direction of the digging action.
