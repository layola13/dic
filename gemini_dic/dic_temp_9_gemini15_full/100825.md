introducing: /ˌɪntrəˈduːsɪŋ/ | v. |介绍，引进，提出 | Introducing a new product to the market requires careful planning. 将新产品推向市场需要仔细的规划。 | 近义词：presenting, unveiling, launching | 反义词：concluding, finishing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is introducing a new bill to Congress. 他正在向国会提出一个新法案。
The company is introducing a new line of products. 公司正在推出一个新的产品系列。


一般过去时:
She introduced me to her parents. 她把我介绍给了她的父母。
The speaker introduced the main topic of his presentation. 演讲者介绍了他演讲的主要主题。


现在完成时:
They have introduced several new features to the software. 他们已经为软件引入了几个新功能。
The government has introduced new regulations to protect the environment. 政府已经出台了新的法规来保护环境。


过去完成时:
Before the meeting, he had introduced himself to all the participants. 会议之前，他已经向所有与会者做了自我介绍。
She had introduced the concept in her previous presentation.  她在之前的演讲中已经介绍了这个概念。


将来时:
They will be introducing a new mobile app next month. 他们下个月将推出一个新的移动应用程序。
The professor will introduce the key concepts of the course in the first lecture. 教授将在第一堂课上介绍课程的关键概念。


虚拟语气:
If they introduced a better system, things would run more smoothly. 如果他们引进一个更好的系统，事情就会运行得更顺畅。
I wish they had introduced the new policy sooner. 我希望他们早点推出这项新政策。


被动语态:
The new technology was introduced to the public last year.这项新技术是去年向公众推出的。
A new law was introduced to combat crime. 一项新的法律被引入以打击犯罪。


疑问句:
Who introduced you to this job? 是谁把你介绍到这份工作的？
What new policy will the government be introducing? 政府将要推出什么新的政策？


否定句:
They didn't introduce the changes until after the deadline. 直到截止日期之后，他们才引进这些变化。
The company hasn't introduced any new products this year. 公司今年还没有推出任何新产品。


PHRASE->
introducing oneself 自我介绍
introducing a topic  介绍一个话题
introducing evidence 提出证据
introducing a bill  提出法案
introducing a speaker 介绍演讲者


词源:
introduce源自古法语 *introduire,  由拉丁语 *introducere演变而来，由 *in- (进入) + *ducere (引导) 构成。因此，其本义是“引导进入”。
