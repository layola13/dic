semi-intelligibly:/ˌsɛmiɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəbli/| adj.| 半懂不懂地；含糊不清地 |  Difficult to understand; partly intelligible.  The speaker mumbled semi-intelligibly, making it hard to follow his presentation.  | 近义词：unclearly, indistinctly, vaguely | 反义词：clearly, distinctly, intelligibly

例句：

He spoke semi-intelligibly, his words slurred and indistinct. 他含糊不清地说着话，字句含糊不清。

The instructions were written semi-intelligibly, leading to much confusion.  说明书写得含糊不清，导致很多混乱。


词源:

* **semi-**:  A prefix meaning "half," "partly," or "partially."  Derived from Latin *semi*.

* **intelligibly**: From "intelligible," meaning "capable of being understood." This in turn derives from Latin *intellegibilis*, from *intellegere* ("to understand"), combining *inter* ("between") and *legere* ("to gather, choose, read").


USAGE->

(Since "semi-intelligibly" is an adverb, it doesn't have tenses like verbs.  The usage examples below show how it modifies verbs.)

Present Tense:
He spoke semi-intelligibly. 他含糊不清地说。
She mumbled semi-intelligibly to herself. 她自言自语，含糊不清。

Past Tense:
He spoke semi-intelligibly yesterday. 他昨天含糊不清地说。
She whispered semi-intelligibly in his ear. 她在他耳边含糊不清地低语。

Future Tense:
He will speak semi-intelligibly at the meeting. 他将在会议上含糊不清地发言。
She will likely mumble semi-intelligibly during the presentation.  她很可能会在演示过程中含糊不清地咕哝。
