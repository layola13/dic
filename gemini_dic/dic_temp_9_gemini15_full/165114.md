preinaugurate:/ˌpriːɪˈnɔːɡjʊreɪt/| vt.|  预先就职；提前举行就职典礼 | To perform the ceremonies or duties of an office before the official inauguration.  This term is relatively rare and often used in a formal or official context, suggesting an action taken before the formal installation into office. | 例句：The newly elected mayor preinaugurated several key initiatives, aiming to hit the ground running on day one.  新当选的市长提前启动了几项重要举措，旨在第一天就全力以赴。


例句：The committee decided to preinaugurate the new park by hosting a community picnic a week before the official opening ceremony. 委员会决定在正式开幕式一周前举办社区野餐，以提前启用新的公园。

近义词：precede, anticipate, foreshadow

反义词：postpone, delay
