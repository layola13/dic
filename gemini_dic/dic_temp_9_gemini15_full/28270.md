captivates:/ˈkæptɪveɪts/| vt.|迷住；吸引；俘获|To fascinate or enchant someone, holding their attention completely.|The breathtaking scenery captivated us.|近义词：enthrall, fascinate, charm, enchant|反义词：bore, repel


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The music captivates the audience. = 音乐迷住了观众。
Her beauty captivates everyone she meets. = 她的美貌迷住了她遇到的每一个人。

一般过去时:
The magician's performance captivated the children. = 魔术师的表演迷住了孩子们。
The story captivated me from beginning to end. = 这个故事从头到尾都吸引着我。

现在完成时:
The artist's work has captivated art lovers for decades. = 艺术家的作品几十年来一直吸引着艺术爱好者。
This novel has captivated me completely. = 这部小说完全迷住了我。

过去完成时:
The ancient city's history had captivated her imagination for years. = 古城的历史多年来一直吸引着她的想象力。
I had been captivated by his charm before I even knew his name. = 在我甚至不知道他的名字之前，我就被他迷住了。


将来时:
The new exhibit will surely captivate visitors. = 新展览肯定会吸引游客。
I think that the sunset will captivate you. = 我想日落会迷住你。

虚拟语气:
If the painting were displayed, it would captivate the viewers. = 如果这幅画被展出，它会吸引观众。
I wish the play had captivated the audience more. = 我希望这出戏能更吸引观众。

被动语态:
The audience was captivated by the performance. = 观众被表演迷住了。
We were captivated by the stunning view. = 我们被令人惊叹的景色迷住了。

疑问句:
Does the story captivate you? = 故事吸引你吗？
What captivates you about this painting? = 这幅画的什么地方吸引了你？

否定句:
The movie didn't captivate me. = 这部电影没有吸引我。
The lecture failed to captivate the students. = 讲座未能吸引学生。


PHRASE->
be captivated by = 被…迷住
totally captivated = 完全着迷
captivated audience = 被迷住的观众
utterly captivated = 完全被迷住
deeply captivated = 深深地被迷住
