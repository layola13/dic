rheme:/riːm/| n. |述题，述谓|The rheme is the part of a sentence that gives new information about the theme.  It's what the sentence is primarily about and often answers the question "What about the theme?". For example, in the sentence "The cat sat on the mat," "the cat" is the theme and "sat on the mat" is the rheme.  The rheme provides the new information, the comment, or the predicate of the sentence.|The rheme of the lecture was the importance of critical thinking.  述职报告的主题是批判性思维的重要性。|comment, predicate, focus|topic, subject|名词
