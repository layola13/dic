misfortuned:/ˌmɪsˈfɔːrtʃənd/| adj.| 不幸的，遭遇不幸的 | Having experienced misfortune; unlucky.  |The misfortuned family lost their home in the fire.  不幸的家庭在火灾中失去了他们的家。| unfortunate, unlucky, ill-fated | fortunate, lucky, blessed | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is misfortuned. 他是不幸的。
She is misfortuned in love. 她在爱情上是不幸的。

一般过去时:
He was misfortuned in business. 他在生意上是不幸的。
They were misfortuned by the storm. 他们遭遇了暴风雨的不幸。


现在完成时:
He has been misfortuned throughout his life. 他一生都遭遇不幸。
She has been misfortuned by a series of setbacks. 她遭遇了一系列挫折的不幸。


过去完成时:
He had been misfortuned before he met her. 在遇到她之前，他一直很不走运。
They had been misfortuned many times before they finally succeeded. 在他们最终成功之前，他们已经遭遇了很多次不幸。


将来时:
He will be misfortuned if he continues down this path. 如果他继续走这条路，他会很不幸。
They will be misfortuned unless they change their strategy. 除非他们改变策略，否则他们将会遭遇不幸。


虚拟语气:
If he were misfortuned, he would need our support. 如果他不幸，他会需要我们的支持。
I wish I were not so misfortuned. 我希望我不那么不幸。


被动语态:
(This word is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes a state, not an action.)  这个词很少用于被动语态，因为它描述的是一种状态，而不是一个动作。
He was considered misfortuned by many.  许多人认为他是不幸的。(被动，但并非直接对misfortuned进行被动)


疑问句:
Is he misfortuned? 他不幸吗？
Are they misfortuned in their business ventures? 他们的商业冒险是不幸的吗？


否定句:
He is not misfortuned; he's quite lucky. 他并不不幸；他相当幸运。
They were not misfortuned; they were simply unlucky. 他们并非遭遇不幸；他们只是运气不好。
