schoolman:/ˈskuːlmæn/| n. |经院哲学家；学者；学校教师|A schoolman is a medieval scholar, especially one who taught or wrote on scholastic philosophy.  The term often carries a connotation of being overly pedantic or scholastic in approach. |Example:  The schoolmen of the 13th century debated the nature of God for hours.  十三世纪的经院哲学家们就上帝的本质争论了数小时。|近义词：scholar, theologian, philosopher |反义词：ignoramus, layman
