pelorus:/ˈpɛlərəs/| n.|方位盘，罗经盘|A pelorus is a navigational instrument used to measure bearings.  It's a type of non-magnetic compass that uses a rotating dial with markings to indicate directions. Unlike a magnetic compass, a pelorus doesn't rely on Earth's magnetic field and is therefore less susceptible to interference. It is primarily used in conjunction with other navigational tools to determine a vessel's position.|The captain used the pelorus to take bearings on prominent landmarks. 船长用方位盘测量了重要地标的方位。|gyrocompass, bearing circle|magnetic compass|名词
