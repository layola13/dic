plunging:/ˈplʌndʒɪŋ/| adj. |突然下降的；骤降的；猛冲的；投入的|Suddenly decreasing sharply;  moving rapidly downward or forward with force;  deeply involved or engaged.|The plunging stock market worried investors.  股市暴跌让投资者担忧。The bird plunged into the water.  鸟儿一头扎进水里。|近义词：diving, plummeting, dropping, steep|反义词：rising, ascending, climbing, gradual


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The temperature is plunging. =气温正在骤降。
He is plunging into his work. = 他正全身心地投入工作。


一般过去时:
The plane plunged into the ocean. =飞机坠入大海。
She plunged her hand into the icy water. = 她把手伸进冰冷的水里。


现在完成时:
The value of the currency has plunged. = 货币价值已经暴跌。
He has plunged himself into debt. = 他已陷入债务危机。


过去完成时:
The stock market had plunged before the news broke. = 在消息传出之前，股市已经暴跌。
They had plunged into the project without a second thought. = 他们毫不犹豫地投入了这个项目。


将来时:
The economy will plunge into recession. = 经济将陷入衰退。
They will plunge into the icy lake. = 他们将跳入冰冷的湖中。


虚拟语气:
If the price plunged, we would lose a lot of money. = 如果价格暴跌，我们将损失很多钱。
I wish the temperature wouldn’t plunge so suddenly. = 我希望气温不要骤降得这么突然。


被动语态:
The city was plunged into darkness. = 城市陷入黑暗。
The country was plunged into war. = 国家陷入战争。


疑问句:
Is the stock market plunging again? = 股市又在暴跌吗？
Are they plunging into the unknown? = 他们正在冒险吗？


否定句:
The sales figures are not plunging. = 销售额没有下降。
He didn’t plunge into the argument. = 他没有卷入争论。


PHRASE->
plunge into: 投入；突然开始
plunge forward: 向前猛冲
plunge headlong: 头脑发热地投入
take the plunge: 冒险一试
plunge oneself into: 埋头于；全身心地投入
