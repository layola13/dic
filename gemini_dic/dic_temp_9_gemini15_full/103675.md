jolter:/ˈdʒɒltər/ | n. v. |颠簸者；震动；使颠簸；使震惊 |A jolter is something that causes a sudden jolt or shake, or the act of jolting itself.  It can also be a person or thing that causes a shock or surprise. |例句：The bumpy road jolted the car violently. = 颠簸的路面剧烈地颠簸着汽车。
The news jolted him to his core. = 这个消息让他震惊。|近义词：jounce, shake, shock, surprise |反义词：smooth, calm, soothe


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bumpy road jolts the car. = 颠簸的路面颠簸着汽车。
The news jolts him. = 这个消息使他震惊。


一般过去时:
The earthquake jolted the city. = 地震震动了这座城市。
The sudden noise jolted me awake. = 突然的噪音把我震醒了。


现在完成时:
The ride has jolted me badly. = 这趟旅程把我颠簸得很厉害。
The news has jolted the markets. = 这个消息震动了市场。


过去完成时:

He had been jolted by the unexpected news. = 他被突如其来的消息震惊了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
