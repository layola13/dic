galenic:/ɡəˈliːnɪk/| adj.|盖伦的；药剂学的|Relating to Galen, an ancient Greek physician, or to his system of medicine, which was based on the four humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile).  The term is often used to describe traditional pharmacy or the preparation of medicines.|The galenic method of preparing medicines was once common.|近义词：pharmaceutical, medicinal|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with "modern," "synthetic," or "allopathic."
