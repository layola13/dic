publicizing: /ˈpʌblɪsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 宣传；公布；推广 | To make something known publicly; to advertise or promote something. |

例句：The company is publicizing its new product through television commercials.  公司正在通过电视广告宣传其新产品。

例句：The government is publicizing the dangers of smoking. 政府正在宣传吸烟的危害。


近义词：advertising, promoting, marketing, announcing, broadcasting


反义词：suppressing, concealing, hiding, keeping secret


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They publicize their products on social media. = 他们在社交媒体上宣传他们的产品。
The school publicizes its upcoming events on its website. = 学校在网站上宣传即将举行的活动。

一般过去时:
The band publicized their new album through a concert tour. = 乐队通过巡回演唱会宣传了他们的新专辑。

现在完成时:
They have publicized their findings in several scientific journals. = 他们已在几家科学期刊上公布了他们的研究结果。
The company has publicized its commitment to sustainability. = 公司已经公布了其对可持续发展的承诺。

过去完成时:
The company had publicized the product launch before the actual date. = 公司在实际发布日期之前就已宣传了产品发布。
They had publicized the event extensively, yet the turnout was disappointing. = 他们已经广泛宣传了这次活动，但出席人数却令人失望。

虚拟语气:
If they publicized their product more effectively, they would sell more. = 如果他们更有效地宣传他们的产品，他们会卖出更多。
