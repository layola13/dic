disgust:/dɪsˈɡʌst/| n. v.|厌恶，恶心；使厌恶|Feeling of intense aversion or repugnance; to cause this feeling.|The smell of rotting garbage filled me with disgust.  腐烂垃圾的气味让我感到恶心。|The sight of the accident disgusted him. 事故的景象让他感到厌恶。|repulsion, abhorrence, loathing|delight, pleasure|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He disgusts me with his behavior. = 他的行为让我恶心。
This food disgusts many people. = 这食物让很多人恶心。


一般过去时:
The movie disgusted the audience. = 这部电影让观众感到恶心。
His actions disgusted her deeply. = 他的行为让她深感厌恶。


现在完成时:
His dishonesty has disgusted everyone. = 他的不诚实已经让每个人都感到厌恶。
The news has disgusted me to the core. = 这个消息让我深恶痛绝。


过去完成时:
I had already been disgusted by his rudeness before he even spoke. = 他还没开口我就已经被他的粗鲁行为激怒了。
They had been disgusted by the conditions in the refugee camp. = 他们对难民营里的状况感到非常厌恶。


将来时:
That smell will disgust you. = 那气味会让你作呕。
I think this presentation will disgust the investors.  = 我认为这份报告会让投资者感到厌恶。


虚拟语气:
If I had known about this, I would have been disgusted. = 如果我知道这件事，我会感到非常恶心。
I wish I hadn't seen that; it disgusted me. = 我真希望我没看到那个，它让我恶心。


被动语态:
He was disgusted by the sight. = 他被眼前的景象弄恶心了。
Many people are disgusted by his actions. = 很多人对他行为感到厌恶。


疑问句:
Does this smell disgust you? = 这气味让你恶心吗？
What disgusted you about the movie? = 你对这部电影的什么感到厌恶？


否定句:
The food didn't disgust me. = 这食物并没有让我恶心。
I wasn't disgusted by his behavior. = 我并没有被他行为恶心到。
