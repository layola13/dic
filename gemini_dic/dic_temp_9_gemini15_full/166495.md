presacrificing: /ˌpriːˈsækrɪfaɪsɪŋ/ | v. | 牺牲前的；祭祀前的；预先牺牲的 |  The act of preparing something or someone for sacrifice; the period or process immediately preceding a sacrifice. This word is relatively rare and often used in specialized contexts, particularly in religious studies or discussions of ritual practices.  It implies actions taken in preparation for a sacrifice, whether literal or metaphorical. | 例句：The presacrificing rituals involved elaborate cleansing ceremonies. = 牺牲前的仪式包括精细的净化仪式。The presacrificing prayers were recited by the high priest. = 牺牲前的祈祷由大祭司诵读。| 近义词：preliminary, preparatory, antecedent | 反义词：post-sacrificial
