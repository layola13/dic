palp:/pælp/| n. v.|触须；触角；轻触；摸索|The word "palp" refers to a slender, jointed appendage near the mouth of many invertebrates, such as insects and crustaceans, used for sensing or manipulating objects.  As a verb, it means to touch or feel something gently or tentatively.|例句：The butterfly used its palps to taste the nectar. 蝴蝶用触须品尝花蜜。

The spider gently palpated the web for vibrations. 蜘蛛轻轻地触碰蛛网以感知震动。|近义词：antenna, feeler, tentacle (n.); touch, feel (v.)|反义词：N/A (for noun);  N/A (for verb)|

词源:
The word "palp" comes from the Latin word "palpare," meaning "to touch" or "to stroke."  This reflects the sensory and manipulative function of the appendage in many creatures.
