micromanager:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌmænɪdʒər/ | n. | 微观管理者，事无巨细的管理者 | A micromanager is a manager who controls every detail of their employees' work, often to an excessive degree, hindering their autonomy and creativity.  They tend to excessively interfere in the work of their subordinates. | Example:  He's a notorious micromanager; he checks every email and edits every report. 他是一个臭名昭著的微观管理者；他检查每一封邮件并编辑每一份报告。

Example: The project suffered because the micromanager stifled creativity and innovation. 项目失败是因为微观管理者扼杀了创造力和创新。


近义词: overmanager, control freak, taskmaster | 反义词: delegator, laissez-faire manager

词源:
The word "micromanager" is a relatively recent coinage, a blend of "micro" (meaning very small or detailed) and "manager."  It reflects the perception of such managers as excessively involved in the minutiae of their employees' tasks, rather than focusing on the broader strategic picture.
