quasi-exceptional: /ˈkwɑːzi ɪkˈsɛpʃənəl/ | adj. | 准例外的；近乎例外的 |  Almost but not quite exceptional; possessing some but not all the characteristics of an exception.  It suggests a condition or object that displays some features of an exception but falls short of being fully exceptional. |  The quasi-exceptional circumstances surrounding the case led to a delay in the trial.  (案件周围近乎例外的环境导致审判延迟。)  The observed phenomenon is quasi-exceptional, exhibiting some, but not all, traits of previously categorized exceptions. (观察到的现象是准例外的，表现出一些，但不是全部，先前分类例外的特征。) | near-exceptional, borderline exceptional | typical, normal, ordinary |  形容词


USAGE->
(由于quasi-exceptional是一个形容词，以下例句展示了其在不同句法中的用法，而不是动词的时态变化。)

肯定句:
The company's success is quasi-exceptional in this struggling market. = 在这个苦苦挣扎的市场中，这家公司的成功几乎是例外的。
His artistic talent is quasi-exceptional, showing promise but needing further refinement. = 他的艺术天赋近乎例外，显示出希望，但需要进一步完善。

否定句:
The situation is not quasi-exceptional; it's entirely commonplace. = 情况并非近乎例外；它完全是司空见惯的。
His performance, while good, wasn't quasi-exceptional enough to warrant special recognition. = 他的表现虽然不错，但还不够接近例外，不足以获得特别表彰。

疑问句:
Is the student's achievement quasi-exceptional, or does it simply reflect diligent effort? = 学生的成就几乎是例外的吗？还是仅仅反映了勤奋的努力？
Is this a quasi-exceptional case that requires special handling? = 这是一个需要特殊处理的准例外案例吗？


PHRASE->
(由于quasi-exceptional本身就是一个完整的词，没有常见的词组搭配。)

词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi* meaning "as if," "almost," "like."
* **exceptional**: From Latin *exceptio* meaning "exception," derived from *excipere* "to except, take out."

因此，quasi-exceptional字面意思是“几乎是例外的”，表示某种情况或事物接近于例外，但并非完全符合例外的所有标准。
