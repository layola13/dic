eventuated:/ɪˈvɛntʃueɪt/| v.|最终发生，结果是|To happen or occur as a result or consequence; to come to pass.|The long negotiations eventually eventuated in a compromise. 长时间的谈判最终以妥协告终。|近义词：result, culminate, conclude, terminate|反义词：begin, commence, initiate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His hard work eventually eventuates in success. = 他的努力最终导致成功。
The plan eventuates in a more efficient system. = 该计划最终导致了一个更高效的系统。


一般过去时:
The meeting eventuated in a unanimous decision. = 会议最终达成了一致决定。
Their efforts eventuated in a successful product launch. = 他们的努力最终导致了产品成功发布。


现在完成时:
The investigation has eventuated in several arrests. = 调查已经导致了几起逮捕。
The dispute has eventuated in a costly lawsuit. = 纠纷已导致代价高昂的诉讼。


过去完成时:
It had eventuated that the project was over budget. = 结果是该项目超支了。
The argument had eventuated in a complete breakdown of communication. = 争论最终导致了沟通完全中断。


将来时:
The negotiations will eventually eventuate in an agreement. = 谈判最终将达成协议。
We believe this research will eventuate in a groundbreaking discovery. = 我们相信这项研究最终将导致一项突破性的发现。


虚拟语气:
If the plan had been followed, it would have eventuated in a better outcome. = 如果按照计划执行，结果会更好。
I wish the situation had eventuated differently. = 我希望情况有所不同。


被动语态:
A compromise was eventuated after lengthy negotiations. =经过长时间的谈判后，最终达成了妥协。
The project was eventuated in a successful completion. = 项目最终成功完成。


疑问句:
Will the current situation eventuate in a war? = 目前的情况会最终导致战争吗？
What will eventuate from this experiment? = 这个实验最终会产生什么结果？


否定句:
The talks did not eventuate in an agreement. = 会谈没有达成协议。
The plan did not eventuate as expected. = 计划的结果并非预期那样。
