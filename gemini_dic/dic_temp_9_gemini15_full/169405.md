pronounce:/prəˈnaʊns/|/ˈprɒnəʊns/| vt.|发音，宣告，断言|To pronounce a word or phrase means to say it clearly and correctly.  To pronounce a judgment or a decision means to announce it formally.|例句：She pronounced the word perfectly. 她把这个单词发音很完美。|例句：The judge pronounced sentence on the criminal. 法官宣判了罪犯的刑罚。|近义词：articulate, enunciate, declare, announce|反义词：mispronounce, mumble


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pronounces every word carefully. 他每个单词都仔细地发音。
She pronounces her opinion clearly. 她清晰地表达了自己的观点。

一般过去时:
The judge pronounced the defendant guilty.  法官宣判被告有罪。
He pronounced the speech eloquently. 他雄辩地发表了演讲。

现在完成时:
She has pronounced the verdict. 她已经宣判了判决。
They have pronounced their support for the project. 他们已经表示支持该项目。

过去完成时:
He had pronounced the sentence before the lawyer could object.  在律师提出异议之前，他已经宣判了。
She had pronounced the words so clearly that everyone understood. 她把话说的如此清楚，以至于每个人都理解了。

将来时:
The judge will pronounce the sentence tomorrow. 法官将在明天宣判。
The speaker will pronounce the winner's name soon. 演讲者很快就会宣布获胜者的名字。

虚拟语气:
If she pronounced the words correctly, she would have passed the test. 如果她把单词发音正确，她就能通过测试。
I wish he had pronounced his name more clearly. 我希望他把名字说得更清楚一些。

被动语态:
The words were pronounced incorrectly. 这些单词发音不正确。
The verdict will be pronounced next week. 判决将于下周宣布。

疑问句:
How do you pronounce this word? 你怎么发音这个词？
Will the judge pronounce a guilty verdict? 法官会宣判有罪吗？

否定句:
He did not pronounce the name correctly. 他没有正确地说出这个名字。
She wouldn't pronounce the verdict until all the evidence was presented. 在所有证据提交之前，她不会宣判。
