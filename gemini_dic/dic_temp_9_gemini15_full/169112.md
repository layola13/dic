prohibitory:/prəˈhɪbɪtəri/| adj.|禁止的，阻止的|Prohibitory refers to something that forbids or prevents something else.  It implies a strong restriction or ban.|The prohibitory laws against drug trafficking are strictly enforced.|近义词：restrictive, forbidding, prohibitive|反义词：permissive, allowing

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The law prohibits smoking in public places. = 法律禁止在公共场所吸烟。
This sign prohibits entry without authorization. = 这个标志禁止未经授权进入。


一般过去时:
The government prohibited the import of certain goods. = 政府禁止进口某些商品。
They prohibited him from attending the meeting. = 他们禁止他参加会议。


现在完成时:
The city has prohibited the use of fireworks. = 该市已禁止使用烟花。
They have prohibited access to the building. = 他们已经禁止进入该建筑物。


过去完成时:
They had prohibited the sale of alcohol before the event. = 他们在活动之前就禁止了酒类的销售。
The school had prohibited students from using mobile phones. = 学校之前就禁止学生使用手机。


将来时:
The council will prohibit parking in this area. = 理事会将禁止在该区域停车。
They will prohibit the construction of new buildings. = 他们将禁止建造新的建筑物。


虚拟语气:
If they prohibited the use of cars, the air would be cleaner. = 如果他们禁止使用汽车，空气会更清洁。
I wish they prohibited smoking in restaurants. = 我希望他们在餐馆里禁止吸烟。


被动语态:
The use of drones is prohibited in this airspace. = 在这个空域禁止使用无人机。
Access to the classified documents was prohibited. = 禁止访问机密文件。


疑问句:
Is the sale of alcohol prohibited here? = 这里禁止销售酒精吗？
Are dogs prohibited in this park? = 这个公园禁止带狗吗？


否定句:
The rule doesn't prohibit the use of cameras. = 这条规则并不禁止使用相机。
He wasn't prohibited from leaving the country. = 他没有被禁止离开国家。


PHRASE->
prohibitory measures: 禁止措施
prohibitory regulations: 禁止条例
prohibitory order: 禁止令
prohibitory price: 过高的价格（高到足以阻止购买）
prohibitory tariff: 禁止性关税
