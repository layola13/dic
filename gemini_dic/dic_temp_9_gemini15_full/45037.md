/kənˈtroʊl/ | n. v. |控制，管理 | control refers to the power to influence or direct people's behavior or the course of events.  It can be a noun referring to the power itself or a verb describing the act of exercising that power. |The government has strict controls on the sale of weapons. 政府对武器销售有严格的控制。|He controls the company's finances. 他控制着公司的财务。|regulation, management, command |chaos, anarchy, freedom


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The system controls the temperature automatically. = 系统自动控制温度。
She controls her emotions well. = 她很好地控制了自己的情绪。


一般过去时:
He controlled the car with great skill. = 他熟练地控制着汽车。
The pilot controlled the plane through the storm. = 飞行员驾驭飞机度过了暴风雨。


现在完成时:
They have controlled the spread of the disease effectively. = 他们有效地控制了疾病的传播。
She has controlled her weight for years. = 她多年来一直控制着体重。


过去完成时:
He had controlled his anger before speaking. = 他在说话之前已经控制住了怒火。
The authorities had controlled the riot before it escalated. = 当局在暴乱升级之前就控制住了它。


将来时:
We will control the robots remotely. = 我们将远程控制机器人。
The new software will control the factory's production. = 新软件将控制工厂的生产。


虚拟语气:
If I had controlled my spending, I wouldn't be in debt. = 如果我控制好我的支出，我就不会负债了。
I wish I had controlled my temper yesterday. = 我真希望昨天我能控制住我的脾气。


被动语态:
The temperature is controlled by a thermostat. = 温度由恒温器控制。
The project is controlled by a central committee. = 项目由中央委员会控制。


疑问句:
Who controls the company? = 谁控制着这家公司？
How do you control your stress levels? = 你如何控制你的压力水平？


否定句:
He couldn't control his laughter. = 他忍不住笑了。
I can't control what others think. = 我无法控制别人的想法。
