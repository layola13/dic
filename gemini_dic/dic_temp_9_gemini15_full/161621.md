poultice:/ˈpʊltɪs/| n. |药膏；热敷剂 |A soft, moist mass of material, typically of herbs, applied to the body to relieve pain or inflammation.  |例句：The doctor applied a warm poultice to the injured area. 医生将温热的药膏敷在受伤部位。|例句：She made a poultice of herbs to soothe the burn. 她用草药做成药膏来缓解烧伤。|近义词：compress, cataplasm |反义词：None specifically, but "treatment without a poultice" could be considered an antonym in context. |名词

词源:
From Middle English *pultys, from Old French *poultice, from Vulgar Latin *pulsaticum, diminutive of Latin puls, "porridge."  The evolution reflects the original consistency of poultices as a soft, mushy mass.
