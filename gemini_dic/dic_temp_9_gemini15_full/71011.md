firlot:/ˈfɜːrlɒt/| n. |弗尔洛特（苏格兰干量单位）|A firlot is a former Scottish dry measure of capacity, varying in size according to locality and the commodity measured.  It was typically used for grain. The exact size varied regionally, but it was generally equivalent to about 1/4 of a boll.  It's now obsolete.|The farmer measured his barley harvest in firlots.|近义词：boll (in some contexts), peck (approximate equivalent in other systems)|反义词：None (as it's a unit of measurement, not a concept with an inherent opposite).
