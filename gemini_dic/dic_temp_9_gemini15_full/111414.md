lixiviation:/ˌlɪksɪˈveɪʃən/ | n. | 浸出；淋洗；（矿物）溶滤 | The process of extracting a soluble substance from a solid by dissolving it in a liquid.  This is often used in contexts like leaching minerals from ore or removing unwanted substances from a material. | Example: The lixiviation of gold from its ore using cyanide is a controversial process. 金矿石用氰化物浸出黄金是一个有争议的过程。 |近义词：leaching, extraction, elution | 反义词：precipitation, deposition
