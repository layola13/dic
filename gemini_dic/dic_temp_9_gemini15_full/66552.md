exhibited:/ɪɡˈzɪbɪtɪd/ | v. | 展览；展示；表现 | To show or display something publicly.  | The museum exhibited a collection of ancient artifacts.  博物馆展出了一批古代文物。 | displayed, showcased, presented | concealed, hidden | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The museum exhibits a wide range of art. = 博物馆展出各种各样的艺术品。
He exhibits great talent in painting. = 他在绘画方面展现出极高的天赋。


一般过去时:
They exhibited their work at the art fair. = 他们在艺术博览会上展出了他们的作品。
She exhibited remarkable courage in the face of danger. = 面对危险，她表现出非凡的勇气。


现在完成时:
The artist has exhibited his paintings in several galleries. =这位艺术家已经在几个画廊展出过他的画作。
She has exhibited a strong determination to succeed. = 她表现出强烈的成功决心。


过去完成时:
Before the accident, he had exhibited no signs of illness. = 事故发生前，他没有任何生病的迹象。
They had exhibited their paintings many times before this show. = 在这次展览之前，他们已经多次展出过他们的绘画作品。


将来时:
The gallery will exhibit the works of a famous sculptor next month. = 画廊下个月将展出著名雕塑家的作品。
He will exhibit his new invention at the science fair. = 他将在科学展览会上展示他的新发明。


虚拟语气:
If he exhibited more confidence, he would be more successful. = 如果他表现出更多自信，他会更成功。
I wish he had exhibited more patience. = 我希望他当时表现出更多耐心。


被动语态:
The paintings were exhibited in a prestigious gallery. = 这些画作在一个著名的画廊展出。
His talents were exhibited through his outstanding performance. = 他的才能通过他杰出的表现得以展现。


疑问句:
Will the paintings be exhibited at the gallery? = 这些画作会在画廊展出吗？
Have they exhibited their products at the trade show? = 他们在贸易展上展出他们的产品了吗？


否定句:
The museum did not exhibit modern art. = 博物馆没有展出现代艺术。
He didn't exhibit any signs of nervousness. = 他没有表现出任何紧张的迹象。
