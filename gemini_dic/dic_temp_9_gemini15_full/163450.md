precontemporaneously:/ˌpriːkənˈtɛmpəreɪniəsli/| adv.|先前同时地，预先同时地|The word "precontemporaneously" means occurring at the same time as something else, but before it in terms of precedence or importance.  It suggests a prior action or event that is simultaneous with, but conceptually or causally precedes, another event.  This word is extremely rare and its usage is largely found in highly specialized or academic contexts.  It is often better replaced by clearer, more concise language. |例句：The author's precontemporaneous notes provided crucial context for understanding the later manuscript. 作者的先前同时的笔记为理解后面的手稿提供了重要的背景。The contract's precontemporaneous clauses were critical to resolving the dispute. 合同中先前同时的条款对于解决争议至关重要。|近义词：simultaneously, concurrently, coincidentally (but with emphasis on prior importance)|反义词：subsequently, later, afterward
