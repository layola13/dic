sciurus:/ˈsaɪərəs/| n. |松鼠|A sciurus is a genus of tree squirrels in the family Sciuridae.  They are characterized by their bushy tails, agile movements, and ability to climb trees.  Sciurus species are found in various habitats across the globe,  with many species exhibiting distinct characteristics depending on their geographic location and environment.  Examples include the eastern gray squirrel, the red squirrel, and the fox squirrel.|The red squirrel (Sciurus vulgaris) is a common sight in many European forests.  =  在许多欧洲森林中，红松鼠（Sciurus vulgaris）是很常见的景象。
