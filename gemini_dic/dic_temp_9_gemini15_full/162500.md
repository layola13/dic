preafflictions:/ˌpriːæˈflɪkʃənz/| n.| 预先的苦难，先前的苦难 |  Suffering or misfortune that occurs before something else, often serving as a prelude or preparation for a greater trial.  The word is a relatively rare and somewhat archaic combination of "pre-" (before) and "afflictions" (sufferings).  It emphasizes the prior nature of the hardships. |例句：His preafflictions, though significant, paled in comparison to the trials he faced later in life. = 他之前的苦难，虽然很大，但与他后来生活中面临的磨难相比就显得微不足道了。


例句：The numerous preafflictions experienced by the pioneers strengthened their resolve. = 拓荒者们经历的诸多苦难增强了他们的决心。


近义词：prior hardships, previous sufferings, antecedent troubles |反义词：post-afflictions, subsequent blessings, later joys |词性：名词
