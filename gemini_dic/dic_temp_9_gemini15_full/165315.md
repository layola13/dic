preinventories:/ˌpriːɪnˈvɛntəriːz/| n. | 预先盘点，预先库存 |  A preinventory is a count of inventory taken before a physical inventory is conducted, often used to estimate the amount of stock and identify discrepancies.  This helps in streamlining the physical inventory process and improving accuracy. | Example: The company conducted preinventories in each warehouse to prepare for the year-end stocktake.  公司在每个仓库进行了预先盘点，为年底盘点做准备。|近义词： preliminary inventory, advance inventory check|反义词：post-inventory|名词
