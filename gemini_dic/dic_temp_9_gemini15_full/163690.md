predeclaring: /ˌpriːdɪˈklɛərɪŋ/ | v. |预先声明；预先宣布 |  The act of declaring something beforehand.  It implies making a statement or announcement before something else happens or is done, often to prepare or inform others. |例句：The company predeclared its intention to merge with a competitor. 公司预先声明了其与竞争对手合并的意图。

The developers predeclared all variables before using them in the function. 开发人员在函数中使用变量之前预先声明了所有变量。


近义词：preannouncing, announcing in advance, giving prior notice  | 反义词：postdeclaring (not a common word) | 词性：动词
