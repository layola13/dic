ghostwriters:/ˈɡoʊstˌraɪtərz/ | n. |代笔作家|A ghostwriter is a person who writes books, articles, or other texts that are officially credited to another person.  They work behind the scenes, often for celebrities, busy executives, or those who lack the time or writing skills to produce a piece of work themselves. | Example:  Many famous celebrities use ghostwriters to help them write their autobiographies.  许多著名的名人使用代笔作家来帮助他们撰写自传。

|近义词:  shadow writer, uncredited writer |反义词:  credited author |词性: noun
