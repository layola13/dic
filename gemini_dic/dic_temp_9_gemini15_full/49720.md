democratised:/dɪˈmɒkrətaɪzd/ | v. | 使民主化；普及 | To make something accessible to or controlled by more people.  | The internet has democratised access to information.  互联网使信息获取民主化了。 |  popularise, equalise |  oligarchise, centralise | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They democratise the education system. = 他们使教育制度民主化。
The company democratizes its decision-making process. = 公司使其决策过程民主化。

一般过去时:
The government democratised the media. = 政府使媒体民主化了。
The reforms democratised the political system. = 改革使政治制度民主化了。

现在完成时:
Technology has democratised communication. = 技术使交流民主化了。
The movement has democratised access to healthcare. = 该运动使医疗保健更容易获得。

过去完成时:
They had democratised the voting system before the election. = 他们在选举前已使投票制度民主化了。
The country had democratised its economy after the revolution. = 该国在革命后使其经济民主化了。

将来时:
They will democratise the energy sector. = 他们将使能源部门民主化。
The new policy will democratise access to housing. = 新政策将使住房更容易获得。

虚拟语气:
If they democratised the legal system, it would be fairer. = 如果他们使法律制度民主化，它将更加公平。
I wish they had democratised the internet sooner. = 我希望他们早点使互联网民主化。

被动语态:
The education system was democratised by the reforms. = 教育制度因改革而民主化。
Access to information is being democratised by the internet. = 互联网正在使信息获取民主化。

疑问句:
Has the company democratised its hiring process? = 公司是否使其招聘流程民主化？
How can we democratise the distribution of wealth? = 我们如何使财富分配民主化？

否定句:
They did not democratise the healthcare system. = 他们没有使医疗保健系统民主化。
The government has not democratised the media effectively. = 政府尚未有效地使媒体民主化。
