cankerworm: /ˈkæŋkərˌwɜːrm/ | n. | 卷叶虫；食叶害虫 | A moth larva that feeds on the leaves of trees, often causing significant damage.  The name derives from the canker-like lesions they leave on foliage. | Example: The cankerworms defoliated the entire orchard. 卷叶虫使整个果园的树叶都脱落了。 |近义词：leaf-eater, defoliator |反义词：beneficial insect
