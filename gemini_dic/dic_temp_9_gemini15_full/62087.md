encephalopathies:/ɛnˌsɛfəˈlæpəθiz/| n. |脑病| Diseases affecting the brain;  a general term encompassing various conditions that impair brain function.  Encephalopathies can be caused by a wide range of factors, including infections, metabolic disorders, toxins, and genetic abnormalities.  Symptoms vary widely depending on the underlying cause and can include confusion, seizures, altered consciousness, and cognitive impairment.|The patient presented with symptoms consistent with hepatic encephalopathy.|近义词：brain disease, encephalopathy (singular)|反义词：healthy brain function
