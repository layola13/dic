rail: /reɪl/ | n. v. | 铁路，栏杆；抱怨；责备 |  A railway track; a horizontal bar; to complain or scold. |  The train derailed and crashed. 火车脱轨并坠毁了。 He railed against the injustice. 他痛斥这种不公正。 | track, line, scold, complain | praise, compliment |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rails against the government's policies. 他反对政府的政策。
The birds perch on the rail of the balcony. 鸟儿栖息在阳台的栏杆上。


一般过去时:
She railed at her son for his bad behavior. 她因儿子的不良行为而责骂他。
The old wooden rail had rotted away. 旧木栏杆已经腐烂了。


现在完成时:
They have railed against the new regulations for months. 他们已经数月来反对新的规章制度。
The farmer has built a new rail fence around his property. 这位农民在他的房产周围建了一道新的栅栏。


过去完成时:
He had railed against the unfair treatment for years before he finally took action. 在他最终采取行动之前，他已经多年来反对这种不公平的待遇。
The old rail had already been damaged before the storm hit.  暴风雨来临之前，那旧栏杆就已经受损了。


将来时:
She will rail against the decision at the next meeting. 她将在下次会议上反对这项决定。
They will install a new safety rail along the cliff edge. 他们将在悬崖边安装新的安全栏杆。


虚拟语气:
If he had railed against the injustice sooner, perhaps things would have been different. 如果他早点反对这种不公正，也许情况会有所不同。
I wish I hadn't railed at him so harshly. 我真希望我没有对他那么严厉地斥责。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The bridge did not have a safety rail. 这座桥没有安全栏杆。
