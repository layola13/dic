hexadactylous:/ˌhɛksəˈdæktɪləs/| adj. |六指的，六趾的|Having six fingers or toes on each hand or foot.  This term is used to describe animals or, less commonly, humans with this characteristic.  The condition is often caused by genetic mutations or other developmental abnormalities.|例句：The cat was hexadactylous, possessing six toes on each paw.  这只猫是六趾的，每只爪子上都有六个脚趾。

Etymology:

* **hexa-**: From Greek *hex*, meaning "six."
* **-dactyl-**: From Greek *daktylos*, meaning "finger" or "toe."
* **-ous**:  A suffix forming adjectives, meaning "full of" or "having the character of."


USAGE->
This word is primarily used as an adjective, so verb conjugations are not applicable.  The usage focuses on describing a subject as having six fingers or toes.
