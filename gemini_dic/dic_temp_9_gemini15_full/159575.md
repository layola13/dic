pollard:/ˈpɒlərd/ /ˈpɑːlərd/| n. v.|截枝，去枝，嫩枝；矮树桩|A pollard is a tree that has had its branches regularly cut back, usually to prevent it from growing too tall.  The term can also refer to the branches that have been cut off.  As a verb, it means to cut off the branches of a tree in this way.|例句：The pollarded willows provided a striking visual feature in the park.  公园里修剪过的柳树构成了一道引人注目的风景线。

The farmer pollarded the apple trees to encourage fruit production.  农民修剪苹果树以促进果实生产。|近义词：lop, prune, top|反义词：let grow, leave uncut|词性：n. v.

词源:
The word "pollard" comes from the Old French word "polard," meaning "a young shoot" or "a branch."  This, in turn, likely derives from the Proto-Germanic word "*pulō," meaning "a sprout" or "young growth." The meaning evolved to refer specifically to trees that are regularly cut back.
