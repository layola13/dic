prerogative:/ˈprɛrəɡətɪv/| n. |特权，权利，优先权 |The prerogative of the president to pardon criminals. 总统赦免罪犯的特权。|近义词：privilege, right, entitlement |反义词：duty, obligation


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The king has many prerogatives. = 国王拥有许多特权。
This is my prerogative. = 这是我的特权。


一般过去时:
The emperor held many prerogatives. = 皇帝拥有许多特权。
She exercised her prerogative to veto the bill. = 她行使否决法案的特权。


现在完成时:
They have enjoyed certain prerogatives for centuries. = 他们几个世纪以来一直享有某些特权。
The company has lost its prerogative in the market. = 公司失去了在市场上的优先权。


过去完成时:
He had already used his prerogative to dismiss the employee. = 他已经行使了辞退员工的特权。
The aristocracy had held many prerogatives before the revolution. = 在革命之前，贵族拥有许多特权。


将来时:
The new law will limit the company's prerogatives. = 新法律将限制公司的特权。
She will certainly exercise her prerogative to choose her own path. = 她一定会行使选择自己道路的特权。


虚拟语气:
If I had the prerogative, I would change the rules. = 如果我有特权，我会改变规则。
I wish I had the prerogative to make that decision. = 我希望我有权做出那个决定。


被动语态:
His prerogatives were questioned by the public. = 他的特权受到了公众的质疑。
The decision was made using the prerogative of the CEO. = 该决定是利用首席执行官的特权做出的。


疑问句:
What are the prerogatives of the position? = 这个职位的特权是什么？
Does the CEO have the prerogative to make such a decision? = 首席执行官有权做出这样的决定吗？


否定句:
The workers do not have the prerogative to strike. = 工人们没有罢工的特权。
He didn't have the prerogative to interfere in their affairs. = 他无权干涉他们的事务。
