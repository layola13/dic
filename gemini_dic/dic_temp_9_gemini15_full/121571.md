misnavigation:/ˌmɪsˌnævɪˈɡeɪʃən/| n. |错误导航；迷航；误导|The act of navigating incorrectly or getting lost;  a wrong direction or course taken, often resulting in unintended consequences. It can refer to literal navigation (like getting lost at sea) or figurative navigation (making poor decisions leading to undesirable outcomes).|例句：The ship's misnavigation led to its running aground. 这艘船的错误导航导致它搁浅了。

The company's misnavigation of the market led to significant financial losses. 公司对市场的错误判断导致了巨大的经济损失。|近义词：wrong navigation, lost, misdirection, error in navigation|反义词：accurate navigation, correct guidance, proper direction
