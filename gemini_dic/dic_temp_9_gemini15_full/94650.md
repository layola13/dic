impressionism: /ɪmˈprɛʃənɪzəm/ | n. |印象派；印象主义 | Impressionism is a 19th-century art movement characterized by relatively small, thin, yet visible brush strokes,  open composition, emphasis on accurate depiction of light in its changing qualities (often accentuating the effects of the passage of time), ordinary subject matter, and unusual visual angles.  | Example:  The museum's collection boasts several masterpieces of Impressionism, including Monet's "Water Lilies". = 博物馆的收藏拥有几幅印象派的杰作，包括莫奈的《睡莲》。  | 近义词: Post-Impressionism,  Realism (in contrast to its emphasis on light and subjective experience) | 反义词:  Classicism (in contrast to its rejection of strict academic rules)
