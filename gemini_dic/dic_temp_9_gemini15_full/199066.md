signify:/ˈsɪɡnɪfaɪ/ | vt. |表示；意味着；象征；预示|To be an indication of; to mean; to represent symbolically; to be a sign of something.|The dark clouds signify rain.  乌云预示着下雨。|The dove signifies peace.  鸽子象征和平。|convey, represent, denote, indicate|ignore, disregard|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The symbol signifies love. = 这个符号代表爱。
Her silence signifies agreement. = 她的沉默表示同意。


一般过去时:
The red light signified danger. = 红灯表示危险。
His frown signified his displeasure. = 他的皱眉表示他的不满。


现在完成时:
The discovery has signified a breakthrough in the research. = 这一发现标志着研究取得了突破。
Recent events have signified a shift in public opinion. = 最近的事件表明公众舆论发生了转变。


过去完成时:
The old building had signified prosperity in the past. = 这座旧建筑过去象征着繁荣。
The letter had signified the end of their relationship. = 这封信预示着他们关系的结束。


将来时:
The upcoming election will signify a new era. = 即将到来的选举将标志着一个新时代的开始。
The results of the experiment will signify whether the hypothesis is correct. = 实验的结果将表明假设是否正确。


虚拟语气:
If this sign signified danger, we should leave. = 如果这个标志表示危险，我们就应该离开。
If only the smoke signified a minor fire! = 但愿烟只是表示小火！


被动语态:
Love is signified by a red heart. = 爱用红心来表示。
The importance of the event was signified by the large crowd. = 大批人群的出席标志着此次活动的重大意义。


疑问句:
What does this gesture signify? = 这个手势是什么意思？
Does this symbol signify anything special? = 这个符号有什么特别的含义吗？


否定句:
The gesture didn't signify anything important. = 这个手势没有什么重要的含义。
The silence didn't signify disapproval. = 沉默并不表示反对。


PHRASE->
signify one's approval/disapproval = 表示赞成/反对
signify one's intention = 表示意图
signify one's consent = 表示同意
signify one's willingness = 表示愿意
signify something to somebody = 向某人表示某事
