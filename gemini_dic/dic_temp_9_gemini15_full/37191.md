comedowns: /ˈkʌmdaʊnz/ | n. | 令人沮丧的经历；衰落；下降；药效消退 | A comedown is an unpleasant experience that follows a period of excitement or success.  It can also refer to a decline or fall from a higher position or state. In the context of drugs, it refers to the unpleasant physical and psychological effects that follow the use of a drug. |例句：After the excitement of the party, the comedown was quite depressing.派对的兴奋过后，那种低落感相当令人沮丧。


The sudden comedown in the stock market caused widespread panic. 股票市场的突然暴跌引起了普遍恐慌。


近义词：letdown, disappointment, decline, slump, crash |反义词：high, peak, uplift, improvement


词源：The word "comedown" is a compound word.  "Come" is from Old English *cuman, meaning "to come," and "down" is from Old English *dun, meaning "hill" or "valley," indicating a movement downwards. The figurative sense developed over time to signify a decline in status, fortune, or mood.
