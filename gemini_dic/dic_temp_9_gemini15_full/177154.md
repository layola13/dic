rearbitrating: /ˌriːˈɑːrbɪtreɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 再仲裁，重新仲裁 | The act of arbitrating again; submitting a dispute to arbitration a second time, often because the initial arbitration was unsatisfactory or incomplete.  | Example:  The parties agreed to rearbitrate the dispute after the initial award was deemed unfair. = 双方同意在最初的裁决被认为不公平后重新仲裁该争议。 | 近义词: re-arbitration, rehearing, reconsideration | 反义词: final judgment, conclusive decision |
