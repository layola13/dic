fluoroscopy:/ˌflʊərˈɒskəpi/ | n. | 荧光透视术 | The use of X-rays to obtain real-time moving images of the internal structures of a body.  It involves passing a continuous X-ray beam through the body part and projecting the image onto a fluorescent screen. | Example: Fluoroscopy is commonly used during angioplasty to visualize the blood vessels.  荧光透视术通常用于血管成形术中以观察血管。  The doctor used fluoroscopy to guide the insertion of the catheter. 医生使用荧光透视术来引导导管的插入。|近义词：X-ray imaging, real-time imaging | 反义词：None |  名词

词源:

From *fluoro-* (relating to fluorescence) + *-scopy* (relating to viewing or examination).  The term combines the process of fluorescence (the emission of light by a substance after absorbing radiation) with the act of viewing or examining.
