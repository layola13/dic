innocent: /ˈɪnəsnt/ | adj. | 无辜的；天真的；清白的 | free from guilt or sin; lacking experience or knowledge of the world; pure. | 例句：The jury found the defendant innocent. 陪审团裁定被告无罪。


The child's innocent question made us laugh.  孩子的无邪问题让我们发笑。


近义词：guiltless, blameless, pure, naive, unsophisticated | 反义词：guilty, culpable, wicked, sophisticated, experienced


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is innocent of the crime. 他对这起罪行无罪。
She seems innocent and naive. 她看起来天真无邪。


一般过去时:
The judge declared him innocent. 法官宣布他无罪。
They believed her to be innocent. 他们相信她是无辜的。


现在完成时:
He has been proven innocent. 他已被证明无罪。
She has always appeared innocent. 她一直显得天真无邪。


过去完成时:
The court had already declared him innocent. 法院已经宣布他无罪。
They had believed her to be innocent before the evidence emerged. 在证据出现之前，他们都相信她是无辜的。


将来时:
The jury will likely find him innocent. 陪审团可能会判他无罪。
I believe she will remain innocent in everyone's eyes. 我相信在每个人的眼中她都会保持清白。


虚拟语气:
If he were innocent, he wouldn't be in jail. 如果他是无辜的，他就不会在监狱里。
I wish I could believe he was innocent. 我希望我能相信他是无辜的。


被动语态:
He was declared innocent by the court. 他被法院宣告无罪。
Innocence is often mistaken for guilt. 清白常常被误认为有罪。


疑问句:
Is he truly innocent? 他真的无辜吗？
Are you sure she's innocent? 你确定她是无辜的吗？


否定句:
He is not innocent; he committed the crime. 他并非无辜；他犯了罪。
She is far from innocent in this matter. 在这件事上她远非无辜。


PHRASE->
innocent bystander 无辜的旁观者
innocent until proven guilty 除非罪名成立，否则视为无辜
innocent abroad  涉世未深的年轻人 (常指出国旅行)
plead innocent  认罪
prove innocent 证明清白


词源:
The word "innocent" comes from the Old French word "innocent," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "innocens," meaning "harmless," or "not guilty."  The Latin word is a combination of "in-" (not) and "nocens" (harmful, guilty).
