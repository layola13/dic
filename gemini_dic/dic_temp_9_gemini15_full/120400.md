ministering:/ˈmɪnɪstərɪŋ/| v.| 侍奉；照料；施予；执行(职责)|To give help or care to someone or something.|例句：She devoted her life to ministering to the sick and needy. 她毕生致力于照顾病人和穷人。|例句：The priest ministered to the congregation. 牧师为会众服务。|近义词：serving, attending, caring for |反义词：neglecting, ignoring


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She ministers to the poor. = 她侍奉穷人。
He ministers to his sick mother. = 他照顾生病的母亲。


一般过去时:
The nurse ministered to the wounded soldiers. = 护士照顾受伤的士兵。
They ministered to the needs of the community. = 他们满足了社区的需求。


现在完成时:
She has ministered to the homeless for many years. = 她多年来一直在帮助无家可归的人。
The church has ministered to the spiritual needs of the community. = 教堂满足了社区的精神需求。


过去完成时:
The doctor had ministered to the patient before he died. = 医生在病人死前已经照顾过他了。
They had ministered to the victims of the disaster. = 他们已经帮助了灾难的受害者。


将来时:
She will minister to the sick in the hospital. = 她将在医院照顾病人。
He will minister to the needs of his family. = 他将满足家人的需求。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would minister to the needs of the less fortunate. = 如果我有更多时间，我会帮助那些不幸的人。
I wish I could minister to the suffering of the world. = 我希望我能减轻世界的痛苦。


被动语态:
The sick were ministered to by the kind nurses. = 病人们受到了善良护士的照顾。
The needs of the community are ministered to by various organizations. = 社区需求由各种组织来满足。


疑问句:
Who is ministering to the patients? = 谁在照顾病人？
What services are being ministered to the public? = 提供给公众哪些服务？


否定句:
He did not minister to the needs of his family. = 他没有满足家人的需求。
They failed to minister to the suffering of the victims. = 他们未能帮助受害者的痛苦。
