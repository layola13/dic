polymorphous:/pɒliˈmɔːfəs/ | adj. | 多形的；多态的；多种形式的 | Having or assuming many forms; exhibiting polymorphism.  The term often describes something with multiple appearances or manifestations,  especially in biological or crystalline contexts.  | Example: The virus is polymorphous, making it difficult to identify and treat.  病毒是多形的，这使得它难以识别和治疗。

The crystal exhibits polymorphous structures under different conditions.  这种晶体在不同条件下表现出多晶型结构。 | 近义词：multiform, multifaceted, versatile | 反义词：monomorphic, uniform

WORD ORIGIN->
The word "polymorphous" is derived from the Greek words "poly" (πολύς, meaning "many") and "morph" (μορφή, meaning "form" or "shape").  The suffix "-ous" indicates an adjective.  Therefore, "polymorphous" literally means "having many forms."
