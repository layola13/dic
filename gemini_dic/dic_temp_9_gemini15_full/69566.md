felonies:/ˈfɛləniz/| n.|重罪，重罪的罪行|Serious crimes, typically involving violence or a threat of violence, and punishable by imprisonment for more than one year.  Felonies are more serious than misdemeanors.|例句：He was convicted of multiple felonies, including armed robbery and assault. 他被判犯有多项重罪，包括武装抢劫和袭击。

例句：The judge sentenced him to twenty years for the felony of grand theft auto. 法官判处他因犯下盗窃汽车重罪而入狱20年。


近义词：grave crimes, serious offenses, major crimes|反义词：misdemeanors


词源：From Old French *felonie, from Old French felonie "treachery, wickedness, felony," from fel, felun "wicked, traitorous," of Germanic origin; related to Old High German felō "wicked, treacherous," Old English feol "wicked, cruel."  The modern sense developed from the medieval legal meaning.
