semisocialistically:/ˌsɛmiˌsoʊʃəlˈɪstɪkli/| adv. | 半社会主义地，部分社会主义地 |  The term describes policies or systems that incorporate some socialist principles but not fully embracing a socialist ideology.  It suggests a blend of socialist and non-socialist elements. | Example: The country's economy operated semisocialistically, with some industries nationalized and others privately owned.  这个国家的经济实行半社会主义制度，一些产业国有化，另一些产业私有化。| 近义词: partially socialistically, quasi-socialistically | 反义词: fully socialistically, capitalistically


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and therefore doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Its usage is descriptive and modifies verbs or entire clauses.  There are no tenses to illustrate.

Example 1: The government attempted to manage the healthcare system semisocialistically.  政府试图半社会主义地管理医疗系统。

Example 2:  They argued that the reforms were implemented semisocialistically, leading to a mixed outcome. 他们认为改革是半社会主义地实施的，导致了混合的结果。


PHRASE->
This word is not typically used in common phrases.  Its usage is largely confined to academic or political discussions.
