emulsibility:/ˌɛmjuːlˈsɪbɪləti/| n. |乳化性，乳化能力|The ability of a substance to form an emulsion.  This refers to the ease with which a substance, usually a liquid, can be dispersed into another liquid with which it is normally immiscible, forming a stable emulsion.  The process often requires the use of an emulsifier.|The emulsibility of oil in water is significantly improved by the addition of a surfactant.|易乳化性，良好的乳化性能|immiscibility, non-emulsibility|
