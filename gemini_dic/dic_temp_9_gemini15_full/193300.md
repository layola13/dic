seems:/siːmz/| v.|似乎，好像|It appears to be true or probable.  The verb "seem" expresses an opinion or impression based on evidence or observation, rather than a definitive statement of fact.|例句：It seems that he is happy.  他似乎很快乐。|例句：The future seems bright. 未来看起来一片光明。|近义词：appears, looks, appears to be|反义词：is certain, is definite


USAGE->
一般现在时:
It seems he's tired. = 他似乎很累了。
It seems like it will rain. = 看起来要下雨了。

一般过去时:
It seemed that she was nervous. = 她似乎很紧张。
It seemed like a good idea at the time. = 那时候看起来是个好主意。

现在完成时:
It has seemed unfair to many people. = 对许多人来说，这似乎是不公平的。
It has seemed to me that he's hiding something. = 我觉得他好像在隐瞒什么。

过去完成时:
It had seemed impossible at first. = 起初这似乎是不可能的。
It had seemed to her that he was lying. = 她觉得他好像在撒谎。

将来时:
It will seem strange at first. = 起初这会显得奇怪。
It will seem easier once you understand it. = 一旦你理解了，它看起来就会容易多了。

虚拟语气:
It would seem that he is innocent. = 他似乎是无辜的。
It would seem more reasonable to wait. = 等一等似乎更合理。

被动语态:
It is seemed that the project failed. (不自然，避免此用法)  The project seemed to have failed. 项目似乎失败了。
It is seemed that the results were inconclusive. (不自然，避免此用法) The results seemed to be inconclusive. 结果似乎并不确定。

疑问句:
Does it seem possible? = 这看起来可能吗？
Does it seem right to you? = 你觉得这合理吗？

否定句:
It doesn't seem likely. = 这看起来不太可能。
It doesn't seem to matter. = 这似乎无关紧要。
