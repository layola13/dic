replantable:/ˌriˈplæntəbəl/| adj. | 可重新种植的，可再种植的 | Able to be planted again; capable of being replanted.  | Example:  The nursery sells a wide variety of replantable seedlings.  苗圃出售各种可重新种植的幼苗。  The farmer practices sustainable agriculture, ensuring all crops are replantable. 农民实行可持续农业，确保所有农作物都可再种植。|近义词：replantable, transplantable |反义词：non-replantable, disposable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This plant is replantable. = 这棵植物可以重新种植。
These seeds are replantable after the first harvest. = 这些种子第一次收割后可以重新种植。


一般过去时:
The farmer replanted the damaged seedlings. = 农民重新种植了受损的幼苗。
The gardener replanted the flowers after the frost. = 园丁在霜冻后重新种植了花朵。


现在完成时:
We have replanted all the trees in the park. = 我们已经重新种植了公园里所有的树木。
The city has replanted thousands of trees along the riverbank. = 城市已经在河岸边重新种植了数千棵树木。


过去完成时:

The farmers had replanted the damaged crops by the end of the week. = 农民们在本周末前已经重新种植了受损的农作物。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If the seedlings were replantable, we would have more success. = 如果幼苗可以重新种植，我们会获得更大的成功。
