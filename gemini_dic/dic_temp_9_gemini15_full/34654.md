clerestories:/ˈklɛrɪstɔːriz/| n.| clerestory窗户，采光窗|A clerestory is a high section of wall, typically in a church or other large building, containing windows that allow light to enter.  It is usually placed above a lower wall or arcade.  The term derives from the clear story, meaning "clear story" or "a story that is clear,"  referring to the unobstructed view and light it provides.|例句：The cathedral's clerestories filled the nave with a glorious light. 这座大教堂的采光窗让中殿充满了辉煌的光芒。

The church's clerestories were beautifully decorated with stained glass. 这座教堂的采光窗上装饰着美丽的彩色玻璃。|近义词：high windows, upper windows|反义词：None specifically, but the opposite concept would be a building without high windows for light.|名词
