moneybags: /ˈmʌniˌbæɡz/ | n. |有钱人，暴发户 | A person who has a lot of money, often implying ostentation or lack of taste.  |例句：He's a real moneybags, driving around in his fancy new car. 他是个十足的暴发户，开着他的新款豪车到处炫耀。

例句：Despite his moneybags image, he's surprisingly generous to charity. 尽管他看起来像个暴发户，但他对慈善事业却出奇的大方。


近义词：millionaire, tycoon, plutocrat, wealthy person

反义词：pauper, beggar, indigent


词源:  The word is a compound of "money" and "bags," literally meaning "bags of money."  The implication of "bags" suggests a large, possibly excessive, amount of money, often acquired in a way considered vulgar or ostentatious.  The term's origins trace back to the late 19th century, reflecting the growing wealth disparity of that era and the social perceptions associated with newly acquired fortunes.
