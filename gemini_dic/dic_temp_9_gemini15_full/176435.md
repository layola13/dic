re-interrupting:/ˌriːɪntəˈrʌptɪŋ/| v.|再次打断，重新中断|The act of interrupting again, or starting an interruption anew.  It implies a previous interruption has already occurred. |例句：He kept re-interrupting the speaker, making it difficult to follow the presentation. 他不断地打断演讲者，使得难以理解演讲内容。
例句：The internet connection re-interrupted our video call after a brief moment of restoration. 网络连接在短暂恢复后再次中断我们的视频通话。|近义词：interrupting again, restarting the interruption|反义词：continuing uninterruptedly, allowing to proceed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He re-interrupts the meeting frequently. = 他频繁地再次打断会议。
They re-interrupt the conversation with silly jokes. = 他们用愚蠢的笑话再次打断谈话。


一般过去时:
She re-interrupted him mid-sentence. = 她在他说话中途再次打断了他。
The power re-interrupted our work several times yesterday. = 昨天电力几次中断我们的工作。

过去完成时:
