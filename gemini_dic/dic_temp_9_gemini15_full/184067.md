resign:/rɪˈzaɪn/| vt. & vi.|辞职；放弃；顺从；委托；|to give up a job or position by formally telling your employer that you are leaving|He resigned from his position as CEO. 他辞去了首席执行官的职务。|quit, abandon, surrender|continue, persist|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He resigns from his job every year. 他每年都辞职。
She resigns herself to her fate. 她听天由命。

一般过去时:
He resigned from his post last month. 他上个月辞职了。
She resigned herself to a life of poverty. 她认命过穷日子。

现在完成时:
He has resigned from the company. 他已经从公司辞职了。
She has resigned herself to the inevitable. 她已经接受了不可避免的事实。

过去完成时:
He had resigned before the scandal broke. 丑闻爆发前他就辞职了。
She had resigned herself to failure before even trying.  她甚至还没尝试就认定自己会失败。

将来时:
He will resign next week. 他下周将辞职。
She will resign herself to whatever happens. 她将顺其自然。

虚拟语气:
If he resigned, the company would be in trouble. 如果他辞职，公司就会陷入困境。
I wish I had resigned sooner. 我真希望我早点辞职。

被动语态:
His resignation was accepted. 他的辞职请求被接受了。
The matter was resigned to fate. 这事听天由命。

疑问句:
Will he resign? 他会辞职吗？
Has she resigned herself to the situation? 她是否已经接受了现状？

否定句:
He did not resign. 他没有辞职。
She did not resign herself to defeat. 她不甘心失败。
