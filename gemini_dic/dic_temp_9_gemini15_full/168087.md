prize:/praɪz/ | n. |奖品，奖赏，值得重视的事物 | A prize is something valuable given as a reward for winning a competition or achieving something excellent.  It can also refer to something highly valued or desirable. |  She won first prize in the science fair. 她获得了科学展览会的一等奖。|The prize for winning the lottery was a million dollars. 彩票中奖的奖金是一百万美元。| reward, award, accolade | punishment, penalty | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The prize is a new car. = 奖品是一辆新车。
This painting is a prized possession. = 这幅画是一件珍贵的藏品。

一般过去时:
He received a prize for his essay. = 他因他的论文获得了奖项。
She won the first prize at the competition. = 她在比赛中获得了一等奖。

现在完成时:
She has won several prizes this year. = 她今年已经赢得了几个奖项。
He has never received such a valuable prize before. = 他以前从未获得过如此珍贵的奖品。

过去完成时:
They had already decided who would receive the prize before the announcement. = 他们在宣布之前就已经决定了谁将获得奖品。
She had won many prizes before she became famous. = 在她成名之前，她已经赢得了许多奖项。

将来时:
He will receive the prize next week. = 他下周将获得奖项。
The winner will receive a prize of 1000 dollars. = 获胜者将获得1000美元的奖金。

虚拟语气:
If I won the lottery, I would buy a big prize. = 如果我中了彩票，我会买一个大奖品。
I wish I had entered the competition; the prize was amazing. = 我真希望我参加了比赛；奖品太棒了。

被动语态:
The prize was awarded to the best student. = 奖品颁发给了最好的学生。
The prizes are being prepared for the ceremony. = 奖品正在为仪式准备。

疑问句:
What is the prize for winning? = 获胜的奖品是什么？
What prize did he win? = 他赢得了什么奖？

否定句:
He didn't win any prizes. = 他没有赢得任何奖项。
She didn't receive the prize she expected. = 她没有收到她期望的奖品。


PHRASE->
