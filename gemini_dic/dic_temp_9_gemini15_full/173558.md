quasi-historic:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ hɪˈstɔːrɪk/| adj. |似历史的；准历史的；具有历史色彩的 |Seemingly or partly historical; having the characteristics of history but not entirely accurate or authentic.  It suggests a blend of factual elements with fictional or imaginative components, often used to describe narratives, settings, or events that draw inspiration from the past but are not strictly bound by historical accuracy.|The novel presents a quasi-historic account of the French Revolution, blending real events with fictional characters.  这部小说对法国大革命作了似是而非的描述，将真实事件与虚构人物混合在一起。|近义词：historical-fiction, partly historical, pseudo-historical|反义词：factual, entirely accurate
