overseasoned:/ˌoʊvərˈsiːzənd/ | adj. | 加调料过多的；调味过浓的 | Having too much seasoning added.  The dish was overseasoned, making it too salty. |近义词：overspiced, too salty, too flavorful | 反义词：underseasoned, bland

词源:
The word "overseasoned" is a compound word formed by combining the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") with the past participle "seasoned" (from the verb "to season").  "Season" in this culinary context means to add spices, herbs, or salt to food to enhance its flavor.
