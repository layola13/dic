prediagnostic: /ˌpriːdaɪæɡˈnɒstɪk/ | adj. |诊断前的；诊断之前的 | Relating to or occurring before a diagnosis is made.  The term describes something that happens or is done in the period before a formal medical diagnosis is given. This could include symptoms, tests, or assessments. | 例句：Prediagnostic symptoms can be subtle and easily overlooked. 诊断前的症状可能很细微，很容易被忽视。  The prediagnostic period can be a time of significant anxiety for patients. 诊断前的时期可能是患者焦虑的重要时期。|近义词：antecedent, preliminary, prior |反义词：postdiagnostic
