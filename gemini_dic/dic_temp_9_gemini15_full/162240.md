pre-sold:/ˌpriːˈsoʊld/| adj.|预售的，售出的|Already sold before being officially released or available for purchase.  预先售出的，在正式发布或上市前就已售出。|Example: The concert tickets were pre-sold within hours.  演唱会门票在几个小时内就预售一空了。|近义词：advance-sold, presold|反义词：unsold


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company pre-sells a large portion of its products each year. = 公司每年预售大量产品。
These tickets are pre-sold, so you can't buy them now. = 这些票已经预售了，所以你现在买不到。


一般过去时:
They pre-sold all the merchandise before the show even began. = 他们在演出开始之前就预售了所有商品。
The developer pre-sold half the apartments before construction started. = 开发商在开工前就预售了一半的公寓。


现在完成时:
We have pre-sold over 500 copies of the book. = 我们已经预售了超过500本的书。
The artist has pre-sold all the paintings in the exhibition. = 艺术家已经预售了展览中的所有绘画作品。


过去完成时:
They had pre-sold most of the tickets by the time the announcement was made. = 在公告发布时，他们已经预售了大部分门票。
The company had pre-sold all the limited edition items before the official release. = 公司在正式发行前就预售了所有限量版商品。


将来时:
They will pre-sell the new album online next week. = 他们下周将在网上预售新专辑。
The organizers expect to pre-sell a significant number of tickets for the event. = 主办方预计将预售大量活动的票务。


虚拟语气:
If they had pre-sold more tickets, they would have made more profit. = 如果他们预售更多门票，他们就会赚更多钱。
I wish I had pre-sold my shares before the market crashed. = 我真希望我在市场崩盘前就预售了我的股票。


被动语态:
A large number of tickets were pre-sold to VIP customers. = 大量门票预售给了VIP客户。
The new game is being pre-sold on several online platforms. = 新游戏正在多个在线平台上预售。


疑问句:
Were the concert tickets pre-sold? = 演唱会门票预售了吗？
Have the albums been pre-sold yet? = 专辑预售了吗？


否定句:
The tickets weren't pre-sold, so you can still buy them. = 门票没有预售，所以你仍然可以购买。
The product wasn't pre-sold, which resulted in lower initial sales. = 产品没有预售，导致最初销售额较低。
