co-optative: /koʊˈɑːptətɪv/ | adj. | 合作的；联合的；兼并的 |

英文解析:  Relating to or characterized by co-optation; involving the adoption of ideas or practices from another source.  It often implies a subtle or indirect appropriation, sometimes without full acknowledgement.  The term can also refer to the act of incorporating someone into a group or organization, often to neutralize opposition or gain their support.

例句:

The company adopted a co-optative strategy, incorporating elements of its competitor's successful marketing campaign.  = 公司采取了一种合作策略，吸收了竞争对手成功营销活动中的元素。

Their approach to conflict resolution was distinctly co-optative, seeking common ground rather than confrontation. = 他们解决冲突的方法明显是合作的，寻求共同点而不是对抗。


近义词:  collaborative, cooperative, inclusive, assimilative, appropriative

反义词:  exclusive, antagonistic, adversarial, divisive


词性:  adjective (形容词)
