counter-questioning:/ˈkaʊntərˌkwɛstʃənɪŋ/| n. |反问；反诘；质疑|The act of responding to a question with a question, often to challenge the original question's assumptions or implications.  It's a technique used in various contexts, including legal cross-examination, debates, and everyday conversations. |例句：The lawyer used counter-questioning to expose inconsistencies in the witness's testimony. 律师用反问来揭露证人证词中的矛盾之处。|例句：Her counter-questioning revealed the hidden agenda behind his seemingly innocent proposal. 她的反问揭示了他看似天真无邪的提议背后的隐藏议程。|近义词：questioning, challenging, interrogation|反义词：affirmation, agreement

Effective counter-questioning requires careful planning. = 有效的反驳性提问需要仔细的计划。
