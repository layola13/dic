quasi-realistic: /ˈkwɑːziˌriːəˈlɪstɪk/ | adj. | 拟真的，似真的 |  Approximating reality; having a semblance of reality but not being truly realistic.  It often implies a degree of artificiality or simplification. |例句：The video game offered a quasi-realistic depiction of medieval warfare. 这款电子游戏对中世纪战争进行了拟真的描绘。


The simulation, while impressive, was only quasi-realistic;  many details were simplified or omitted for efficiency.  尽管令人印象深刻，但模拟只是似真的；为了效率，许多细节被简化或省略了。|近义词：semi-realistic, verisimilar, lifelike (in some aspects) |反义词：unrealistic, fantastical, imaginary

词源:
The word "quasi-realistic" is a combination of "quasi-" (meaning "seemingly," "apparently," or "to a certain extent") and "realistic" (meaning "closely resembling reality").  Therefore, "quasi-realistic" describes something that appears realistic but isn't entirely so.
