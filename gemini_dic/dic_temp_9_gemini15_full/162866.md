precedency: /ˈprɛsɪdənsi/ | n. |优先权，优先次序 | The right or privilege of preceding or being given precedence.  It refers to the order of importance or priority given to something. |例句：The precedency of the tasks was determined by their deadlines. 任务的优先顺序由它们的截止日期决定。

例句：  The precedency given to the CEO's requests often delayed other projects.  首席执行官的要求享有优先权，这常常导致其他项目被延误。


近义词：priority, precedence, preference, seniority, preeminence

反义词：inferiority, subordination


词源:  From Middle French *précédence, from Latin *praecedentia, from praecedere "to precede".


用法说明:

precedency 通常指按照重要性或优先级排列的顺序。它可以指各种情况下的优先权，例如：

* **社会地位的优先权:**  In formal settings, precedency dictates the order in which people are seated or addressed. 在正式场合，优先顺序决定了人们就座或被称呼的顺序。

* **任务的优先权:**  Project managers often establish precedency to manage workloads efficiently. 项目经理通常会确定优先顺序以有效管理工作量。

* **法律程序的优先权:**  In some legal systems, certain cases may have precedency over others. 在某些法律体系中，某些案件可能优先于其他案件。


注意：precedency 通常是不可数名词，很少用复数形式。  有时也用作 "precedence"，两者意思相同。
