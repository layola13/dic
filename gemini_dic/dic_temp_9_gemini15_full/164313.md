preeducationally: /ˌpriːˌɛdʒʊˈkeɪʃənəli/ | adv. | 教育之前地；在教育之前 |  This word describes something that happens or is done before formal education begins. It implies a state or condition existing prior to the influence of formal schooling.  There are few examples of the word being used in common language because it is quite specific. | The child exhibited preeducationally acquired language skills.  孩子在接受正规教育之前就展现出了语言能力。 |  pre-school, pre-literacy | post-educationally | adv.


USAGE->
This word is an adverb and therefore doesn't have the same range of grammatical usage as a verb or noun.  It primarily modifies verbs and occasionally adjectives.


General usage example:
He behaved preeducationally, instinctively reacting to danger. 他在接受教育之前就本能地对危险做出了反应。
The skills were developed preeducationally, through interactions with family.  这些技能是在接受正规教育之前，通过与家人的互动而习得的。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases using "preeducationally."  The word itself is quite rare and specialized.
