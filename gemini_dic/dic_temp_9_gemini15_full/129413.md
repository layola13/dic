nominate:/ˈnɒmɪneɪt/ /ˈnɑːmɪneɪt/| vt.|提名，推荐，任命|To officially suggest someone for a position or award.|The committee will nominate a candidate for the presidency.  委员会将提名一位总统候选人。


The academy nominated her for best actress.  学院提名她为最佳女演员。


近义词：propose, suggest, appoint


反义词：reject, dismiss


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They nominate a new CEO every year. = 他们每年都会提名一位新的首席执行官。
The board nominates candidates for the open positions. = 董事会提名空缺职位的候选人。


一般过去时:
The party nominated him for the senate. = 该党提名他为参议员。
She was nominated for an Oscar last year. = 她去年获得了奥斯卡提名。


现在完成时:
He has been nominated for several awards. = 他已被提名获得多个奖项。
The committee has nominated three candidates. = 委员会已提名三名候选人。


过去完成时:
They had already nominated him before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前就已经提名他了。
She had been nominated twice before winning the award. = 在获得该奖项之前，她已经被提名过两次。


将来时:
The committee will nominate a chairperson next week. = 委员会下周将提名一位主席。
They will be nominated for the upcoming election. = 他们将被提名参加即将到来的选举。


虚拟语气:
If they nominated him, he would accept. = 如果他们提名他，他会接受。
I wish they had nominated her for the prize. = 我希望他们已经提名她获得该奖项。


被动语态:
He was nominated for the position. = 他被提名为该职位。
She was nominated for an award. = 她被提名获得一个奖项。


疑问句:
Who did they nominate for the job? = 他们提名谁担任这份工作？
Has she been nominated for any awards? = 她是否被提名获得任何奖项？


否定句:
They did not nominate him for the post. = 他们没有提名他担任这个职位。
She wasn't nominated for the prize. = 她没有被提名获得这个奖项。


PHRASE->
nominate for: 提名...
be nominated for: 被提名...
self-nominate: 自荐
nominate somebody as: 提名某人为...
nominate somebody to: 提名某人担任...
