psychoacoustically: /ˌsaɪkoʊəˈkuːstɪkli/ | adv. | 心理声学地 |  Adverb describing something related to the study of the perception of sound and its psychological effects.  It refers to the way in which sounds are perceived and processed by the mind, considering both the physical properties of the sound and the listener's psychological state. | Example:  The music was psychoacoustically designed to evoke feelings of calm and relaxation. 音乐在心理声学上经过设计，以唤起平静和放松的感觉。|近义词:  auditorily, perceptually  |反义词:  physically (in the context of sound production, not perception)


USAGE->
This word is an adverb and doesn't have verb conjugations like "What" in the example.  It modifies verbs, describing *how* an action is performed in relation to psychoacoustics.


Example Sentences demonstrating adverbial use:

The experiment was conducted psychoacoustically to minimize bias. 实验在心理声学上进行，以最大限度地减少偏差。

The sound system was psychoacoustically calibrated for optimal listening experience.  音响系统在心理声学上经过校准，以获得最佳的收听体验。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "psychoacoustically".  The word is used in a more technical and academic context.
