lithodome:/ˈlɪθədoʊm/| n. |凿石动物；凿穴动物|A marine organism that bores into rocks or other hard substrates to create a cavity for shelter.  Lithodomes are often bivalves or other mollusks. |Example:  Many lithodomes, such as certain types of clams, are found in intertidal zones.  许多凿石动物，例如某些类型的蛤类，都存在于潮间带。|近义词：rock borer, burrower |反义词：None specifically, but organisms that live freely in the open water could be considered opposites.
