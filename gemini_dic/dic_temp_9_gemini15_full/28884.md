carpooling:/ˈkɑːrpuːlɪŋ/| n. |拼车；合乘|The act of sharing a car ride with others, typically to commute to work or school, to save money or reduce traffic congestion.  |例句：Carpooling is a great way to reduce your carbon footprint. 拼车是减少碳足迹的好方法。
例句：Many employees participate in carpooling programs to reduce traffic congestion during rush hour. 许多员工参加拼车计划以减少高峰时段的交通拥堵。|近义词：ride-sharing, shared ride|反义词：driving alone, solo driving|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They carpool to work every day. = 他们每天拼车上班。
We carpool with our neighbors to save on gas. = 我们和邻居拼车以节省汽油。

一般过去时:
She carpooled with her colleagues last week. = 她上周和同事们拼车。
They carpooled to the concert last night. = 他们昨晚拼车去音乐会。

现在完成时:
We have carpooled to school for three years. = 我们已经拼车去学校三年了。
They have carpooled to the office since the new policy was implemented. = 自从实施新政策以来，他们一直拼车去办公室。

过去完成时:
He had carpooled with them several times before the accident. = 事故发生前，他曾多次和他们拼车。
She had never carpooled before moving to the city. = 在搬到城市之前，她从未拼车过。

将来时:
We will carpool to the airport tomorrow. = 我们明天将拼车去机场。
They will be carpooling to the conference next month. = 他们下个月将拼车去参加会议。

虚拟语气:
If we lived closer, we would carpool. = 如果我们住得近些，我们会拼车。
I wish I could carpool, but my schedule is too unpredictable. = 我希望我能拼车，但是我的时间安排太不可预测了。

被动语态:
Carpooling is encouraged by the company. = 公司鼓励拼车。
The carpool lane is often used by those who carpool. = 拼车道经常被拼车的人使用。

疑问句:

否定句:
