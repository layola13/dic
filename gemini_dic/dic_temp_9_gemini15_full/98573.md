institutionalises: /ɪnˌstɪtjuːʃənəlaɪz/ | vt. | 使制度化；使成为制度 | To establish something as a norm or standard practice within an institution or society.  |例句：The government institutionalized the practice of mandatory retirement at age 65. 政府将65岁强制退休的惯例制度化了。
|例句：They are attempting to institutionalize equality and fairness in the workplace. 他们正试图在工作场所制度化平等和公平。|近义词：formalize, systematize, codify |反义词：informalize, abolish, dismantle |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company institutionalizes its values through regular training. = 公司通过定期培训将价值观制度化。
They institutionalize new policies to improve efficiency. = 他们将新政策制度化以提高效率。


一般过去时:
The school institutionalized a new curriculum last year. = 学校去年实施了新的课程。
The country institutionalized human rights protections in its constitution. = 该国在其宪法中将人权保障制度化。


现在完成时:
The organization has institutionalized a system of checks and balances. = 该组织已经建立了一个制衡系统。
Society has institutionalized the use of social media. = 社会已经将社交媒体的使用制度化了。


过去完成时:

They had institutionalized the process long before I joined the team. = 我加入团队很久以前，他们就已经将这个流程制度化了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
