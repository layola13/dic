penalizing: /ˈpiːnəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |惩罚；处罚；不利于 |  To impose a penalty on; to punish.  The act of penalizing involves inflicting a punishment or disadvantage as a consequence of wrongdoing or non-compliance. |

例句：

The judge penalized the team for unsportsmanlike conduct. = 裁判因不文明行为而惩罚了该队。

Excessive speed limits penalizes drivers by increasing fuel consumption. = 超速限制会通过增加燃油消耗来惩罚驾驶员。


近义词：punishing, sanctioning, chastising, punishing, fining

反义词：rewarding, encouraging, benefiting


词性：动词

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
Penalizing comes from the Latin word "poena," meaning "penalty" or "punishment."  The suffix "-izing" indicates the process of making something into a penalty or inflicting a punishment.
