pilgrim:/ˈpɪlɡrɪm/| n.|朝圣者，香客；旅行者|A pilgrim is a person who journeys to a sacred place for religious reasons.  It can also refer to someone who travels a long distance, often for a specific purpose.|例句：Many pilgrims travel to Mecca each year. 每年都有许多朝圣者前往麦加。

The pilgrims walked for days to reach the holy city. 朝圣者走了好几天才到达圣城。


近义词：traveler, wanderer, wayfarer|反义词：stay-at-home


词源:
The word "pilgrim" comes from the Old French "pelerin," which itself is derived from the Latin "peregrinus," meaning "foreigner" or "stranger."  The original meaning was simply someone who traveled to a foreign land. Over time, the meaning evolved to specifically refer to someone making a religious journey.
