downfallen: /ˈdaʊnfɔːlən/ | adj. | 失败的；衰落的；被推翻的 |

英文解析:  Describes something or someone that has experienced a significant loss of power, status, or success; fallen from a previous high position or state.  It implies a decline from a formerly prominent or successful position.

例句:

The once-powerful empire is now downfallen.  曾经强大的帝国现在已经衰落了。

His downfallen reputation made it difficult to find a new job. 他声名扫地，很难找到新工作。


近义词: ruined, defeated, fallen, disgraced, overthrown,  bankrupt (in a metaphorical sense)

反义词: successful, triumphant, prosperous, flourishing, ascendant


词性: Adjective


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The economy is downfallen. = 经济正在衰退。
His career is downfallen. = 他的事业已经衰落。


一般过去时:
The kingdom was downfallen. = 王国灭亡了。
The company was downfallen. = 公司倒闭了。


现在完成时:
The project has been downfallen. = 项目已经失败了。
His reputation has been downfallen. = 他的名声已经毁了。


过去完成时:
The empire had been downfallen before the invasion. = 在入侵之前，帝国就已经衰落了。
The business had been downfallen before the new manager arrived. = 新经理到来之前，公司就已经倒闭了。


将来时:
The government will be downfallen. = 政府将会垮台。
The building will be downfallen. = 这座建筑物将会倒塌。(Note: this usage is less common for 'downfallen' and 'collapse' would be more appropriate)


虚拟语气:
If he continued this way, his business would be downfallen. = 如果他继续这样下去，他的生意将会倒闭。
If the king had not made those mistakes, his kingdom would not have been downfallen. = 如果国王没有犯那些错误，他的王国就不会灭亡。


被动语态:
The dictator was downfallen by a revolution. = 独裁者被革命推翻了。
The old system was downfallen by new technology. = 旧体制被新技术取代了。(Note: 'replaced' would be more common than 'downfallen' in this context)


疑问句:

否定句:
The project is not downfallen. = 项目没有失败。
The empire was not yet downfallen. = 帝国还没有灭亡。
