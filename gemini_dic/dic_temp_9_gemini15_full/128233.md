neurofibrilla: /ˌnʊroʊfaɪˈbrɪlə/ | n. | 神经原纤维 | A very fine fibril found in nerve cells.  They are composed of proteins, primarily neurofilaments and microtubules, and play a crucial role in maintaining the structural integrity and function of neurons.  Neurofibrils are particularly important for axonal transport, the process of moving materials along the length of a neuron. Damage or abnormalities in neurofibrils are implicated in various neurological disorders. | Example:  The researchers observed significant damage to the neurofibrils in the affected neurons. 研究人员观察到受损神经元中的神经原纤维受到严重破坏。 | 近义词: nerve fiber, axon filament | 反义词: (None directly opposite;  terms describing absence or dysfunction might be used instead,  e.g.,  neurofibrillary degeneration) |  名词
