opalotype: /ˈoʊpəlˌoʊtaɪp/ | n. | 宝石印刷术；蛋白石照相术 | A photographic process, similar to the Woodburytype, using a gelatine relief produced from a photographic negative.  The relief is inked and then printed onto paper.  The resulting print has a characteristic iridescence, resembling the play of color in an opal. | The opalotype process was known for its fine detail and beautiful, almost jewel-like, quality. | 近义词：Woodburytype, photogravure | 反义词：无
