disconsolate:/ˈdɪskɒnsəleɪt/ | adj. | 沮丧的；极度悲伤的 | extremely unhappy and without hope; dejected.  | Example: She was utterly disconsolate after the death of her beloved pet.  她心爱的宠物死后，她非常沮丧。  |近义词:dejected,despondent,devastated | 反义词: cheerful, joyful, elated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is disconsolate about the lost opportunity. 他对失去的机会感到沮丧。
She remains disconsolate despite our efforts to cheer her up. 尽管我们努力让她振作起来，她仍然沮丧。


一般过去时:
He was disconsolate after failing the exam.  考试不及格后，他很沮丧。
She felt disconsolate when her plans fell through. 当她的计划落空时，她感到沮丧。


现在完成时:
He has been disconsolate ever since he lost his job. 自从失业以来，他一直很沮丧。
She has remained disconsolate for weeks following the accident. 事故发生后几周，她一直很沮丧。


过去完成时:
He had been disconsolate for months before he finally sought help. 在他最终寻求帮助之前，他已经沮丧了几个月。
She had been disconsolate all day, so we took her out for a treat. 她整天都很沮丧，所以我们带她出去吃好吃的。


将来时:
He will likely be disconsolate if his team loses the game. 如果他的球队输了比赛，他很可能会沮丧。
She will probably be disconsolate if she doesn't get the promotion. 如果她没有得到晋升，她可能会很沮丧。


虚拟语气:
If he knew the truth, he would be disconsolate. 如果他知道真相，他会非常沮丧。
I wish I weren't so disconsolate about the situation. 我希望我对这种情况不要如此沮丧。


被动语态:
(This adjective is rarely used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
Is she disconsolate about the news? 她对这个消息感到沮丧吗？
Are you disconsolate about the outcome? 你对结果感到沮丧吗？


否定句:
He was not disconsolate; he was relieved. 他并不沮丧；他松了一口气。
She is not disconsolate; she is actually quite optimistic. 她并不沮丧；事实上她相当乐观。


PHRASE->

词源:
dis- (否定前缀) + consolare (拉丁语，意为“安慰”)  The word evolved through Old French *desconsoler.
