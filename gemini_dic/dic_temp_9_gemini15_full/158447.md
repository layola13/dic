plebiscites:/ˈplɛbɪsaɪts/| n. |公民投票；全民公决| A plebiscite is a vote in which the entire electorate is invited to vote on a particular issue.  It's often used to gauge public opinion on a specific proposal or policy.  Unlike a general election, a plebiscite doesn't usually result in a change of government. |Example: The government held a plebiscite on the issue of constitutional reform.  政府就宪法改革问题举行了全民公决。

The country held a plebiscite to determine whether to join the European Union. 这个国家举行全民公决以决定是否加入欧盟。


近义词：referendum, poll, vote|反义词：dictatorship, autocracy|词性：noun
