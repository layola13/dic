prognosticate:/prɑːɡˈnɒstɪkeɪt/| /prɑːɡˈnɑːstɪkeɪt/| vt.|预言；预测；预示|To foretell or predict something, especially on the basis of present indications or knowledge.  The verb suggests a more formal and considered prediction, often based on expertise or careful analysis.|例句：The doctor prognosticated a long and difficult recovery.  医生预言将会有一个漫长而艰难的康复过程。

例句：Experts prognosticate a rise in interest rates next year.专家预测明年利率将会上升。


近义词：predict, forecast, foretell, foresee


反义词：None directly opposite, but words suggesting uncertainty or denial of prediction could be considered indirect opposites, such as doubt, question, deny.


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The economist prognosticates a recession. = 经济学家预测经济衰退。
She prognosticates the future of the company. = 她预测公司的未来。


一般过去时:
The fortune teller prognosticated a happy marriage. = 算命先生预言了一场幸福的婚姻。
He prognosticated a successful outcome to the surgery. = 他预言手术会有一个成功的结果。


现在完成时:
Scientists have prognosticated the effects of climate change for decades. = 科学家几十年来一直在预测气候变化的影响。
They have prognosticated a significant increase in tourism. = 他们预测旅游业将大幅增长。


过去完成时:
Experts had prognosticated a stock market crash before it happened. = 专家在股市崩盘之前就预测到了。
Archaeologists had prognosticated the discovery of ancient artifacts. = 考古学家曾预测到古代文物的发现。


将来时:
Analysts will prognosticate the impact of the new law. = 分析师将预测新法律的影响。
Weather forecasters will prognosticate a severe storm. = 气象预报员将预测一场严重的暴风雨。


虚拟语气:
If I could prognosticate the future, I would be rich. = 如果我能预测未来，我会很富有。
I wish I could prognosticate the winner of the election. = 我希望我能预测选举的获胜者。


被动语态:
A long recovery was prognosticated by the doctor. = 医生预言了漫长的恢复期。
The economic downturn was prognosticated by many experts. = 许多专家都预测到了经济低迷。


疑问句:
Can you prognosticate the outcome of this experiment? = 你能预测这个实验的结果吗？
Does the report prognosticate a successful product launch? = 这份报告是否预测产品发布成功？


否定句:
The weatherman did not prognosticate rain. = 气象员没有预报下雨。
Experts were unable to prognosticate the severity of the earthquake. = 专家无法预测地震的严重程度。

词源:
