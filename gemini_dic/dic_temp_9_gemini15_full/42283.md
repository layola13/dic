counter-evidence:/ˈkaʊntərˌevɪdəns/| n.|反证，反驳证据|Evidence that contradicts or refutes other evidence.  It challenges the validity or accuracy of existing evidence.|The defense presented counter-evidence to disprove the prosecution's claims.  辩方提交了反证以驳斥检方的说法。|contrary evidence, opposing evidence|supporting evidence

一般现在时:
The defense presents counter-evidence in court. = 辩方在法庭上提交反证。
Counter-evidence often challenges the initial findings. = 反证通常会挑战最初的发现。


一般过去时:
The lawyer presented strong counter-evidence during the trial. = 律师在审判期间提供了强有力的反证。
They found counter-evidence that contradicted the initial report. = 他们发现了与初步报告相矛盾的反证。


现在完成时:
Scientists have found counter-evidence to the existing theory. = 科学家们已经发现了与现有理论相矛盾的反证。
The research team has accumulated substantial counter-evidence. = 研究团队已经积累了大量反证。


过去完成时:
By then, they had already gathered enough counter-evidence to refute the hypothesis. = 到那时，他们已经收集了足够的反证来驳斥该假设。
The investigators had discovered counter-evidence before the conclusion of the case. = 调查人员在案件结束前发现了反证。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If they had presented more counter-evidence, the verdict might have been different. = 如果他们提供了更多反证，判决可能会有所不同。
I wish I had found stronger counter-evidence earlier. = 我希望我早点找到更有力的反证。


被动语态:

疑问句:
What counter-evidence can you offer to support your claim? = 你能提供什么反证来支持你的说法？
Is there any counter-evidence to support this controversial opinion? = 有任何反证来支持这种有争议的观点吗？


否定句:
They couldn't find any counter-evidence to disprove the theory. = 他们找不到任何反证来驳斥该理论。
The absence of counter-evidence doesn't necessarily prove the claim. = 反证的缺乏并不一定能证明该说法。
