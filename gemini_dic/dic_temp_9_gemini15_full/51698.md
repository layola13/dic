deviously:/ˈdiːviəsli/ | adj. | 狡猾地；阴险地；偷偷摸摸地 |  In a cunning or deceitful way; sneakily.  It implies a sense of hidden intention or manipulation. | 例句：He deviously manipulated the situation to his advantage. 他狡猾地操纵局势以利于自己。

He deviously avoided answering the direct question. 他巧妙地回避了直接的提问。


近义词：cunningly, slyly, craftily, subtly, insidiously | 反义词：honestly, openly, frankly, straightforwardly, directly

词源:  Derived from the word "devious," which comes from the Old French word "devie," meaning "to stray from the way," ultimately from the Latin "de" (away from) + "via" (way, path).  The "-ly" suffix transforms the adjective "devious" into an adverb.
