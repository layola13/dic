showpiece:/ˈʃoʊpiːs/ | n. | 杰作；展览品；引人注目之物 | A showpiece is an object, building, or event that is very impressive and is intended to attract attention. It is often the best or most impressive example of its kind. |例句：The new stadium is a showpiece of modern architecture.  这座新体育馆是现代建筑的杰作。


例句：The museum's showpiece is a priceless diamond necklace. 博物馆的镇馆之宝是一条无价的钻石项链。


近义词：masterpiece, highlight, centerpiece, attraction | 反义词：dull, unimpressive, ordinary


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new building is a showpiece of modern design. = 这座新建筑是现代设计的典范。
That painting is a showpiece of his early work. = 那幅画是他早期作品的杰作。


一般过去时:

Her latest novel was a showpiece of her writing skills. = 她最新的那部小说是她写作技巧的典范。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:
The castle had been a showpiece of royal power for centuries. = 这座城堡几个世纪以来一直是王权的象征。
His previous work had already established him as a showpiece artist. = 他以前的作品已将他确立为一位杰出的艺术家。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

词源:
The word "showpiece" combines "show," meaning a public display or exhibition, and "piece," referring to an object or item.  Its meaning developed naturally from the idea of a prominent item displayed as part of a show or exhibition.
