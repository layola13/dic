coheirs:/ˈkoʊhɛrz/ | n. |共同继承人 |People who inherit something jointly.  Coheirs inherit property or other assets together, sharing the inheritance equally or according to a specified division outlined in a will or legal document. |Example: After their father's death, the two brothers became coheirs to his vast estate.  他们父亲去世后，两兄弟成为了他巨额遗产的共同继承人。


Example:  The three cousins are coheirs to their grandmother's antique collection. 三个表兄弟姐妹是他们祖母古董收藏的共同继承人。

近义词: joint heirs, fellow heirs

反义词: sole heir, only heir


词性: 名词

词源:
The word "coheir" is a combination of "co-" (meaning "together" or "jointly") and "heir" (meaning "a person who inherits something").  It's a relatively straightforward compound word reflecting its meaning.
