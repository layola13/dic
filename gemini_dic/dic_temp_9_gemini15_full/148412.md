overstays:/ˌoʊvərˈsteɪz/ | v. |逾期逗留；滞留 | To remain beyond the permitted or expected time.  |例句：He overstayed his visa by three months. 他逾期逗留三个月。 |例句：The guests overstayed their welcome. 客人们待的时间过长，让人感到不受欢迎。|近义词：outstay, remain beyond the allotted time |反义词：depart on time, leave promptly |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He overstays his welcome at parties. = 他在派对上待的时间过长，让人感到不受欢迎。
They often overstay their visas. = 他们经常逾期居留。


一般过去时:
She overstayed her visa and was fined. = 她逾期居留并被罚款。
We overstayed at the restaurant last night. = 我们昨晚在餐厅待的时间过长。


现在完成时:
He has overstayed his welcome again. = 他又一次待的时间过长，让人感到不受欢迎。
They have overstayed their visas multiple times. = 他们多次逾期居留。


过去完成时:
I realized I had overstayed my welcome when everyone started yawning. = 当每个人都开始打哈欠时，我意识到我待的时间过长了。
She discovered she had overstayed her visa after trying to re-enter the country. = 她试图重新进入该国后才发现自己逾期居留了。


将来时:
If we don't leave soon, we will overstay our welcome. = 如果我们不马上离开，我们就会待的时间过长，让人感到不受欢迎。
I'm afraid we'll overstay our allotted time for the presentation. = 我担心我们将超过演讲的规定时间。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't overstayed, I wouldn't have missed my flight. = 如果我没有逾期逗留，我就不会错过我的航班。
I wish I hadn't overstayed at the library; I missed my appointment. = 我真希望我没有在图书馆待的时间过长；我错过了我的约会。


被动语态:
His visa was overstayed, resulting in deportation. = 他的签证逾期，导致被驱逐出境。(This is a less common usage, as "overstay" is usually active.)
The welcome was overstayed by the boisterous guests. = 热情的款待被喧闹的客人拖延了。(This is a less common and more figurative usage.)


疑问句:
Did he overstay his visa? = 他是否逾期居留？
Have they overstayed their welcome? = 他们是否待的时间过长，让人感到不受欢迎？


否定句:
We didn't overstay our welcome at the party. = 我们在派对上并没有待的时间过长，让人感到不受欢迎。
She made sure not to overstay her visa. = 她确保自己没有逾期居留。



PHRASE->
overstay a visa = 逾期居留
overstay one's welcome = 待的时间过长，让人感到不受欢迎
overstay a deadline = 超过截止日期
overstay one's leave = 擅自延长休假
overstay one's visit = 访问时间过长


词源:
The word "overstay" is a combination of the prefix "over-" (meaning "beyond" or "excessively") and the verb "stay" (meaning "to remain").  It's a relatively straightforward formation reflecting its meaning.
