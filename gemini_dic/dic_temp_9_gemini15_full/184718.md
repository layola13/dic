resubscribing:/ˌriːsəbˈskraɪbɪŋ/ | v. | 重新订阅；再次订阅 | The act of subscribing again to a service or publication after a lapse in subscription.  It implies a previous subscription that was discontinued and is now being renewed. | Example: I'm resubscribing to the streaming service after a month's break. 我在一个月的休息后重新订阅了流媒体服务。  |近义词: renewing, re-enrolling |反义词: unsubscribing, cancelling


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is resubscribing to the magazine. = 他正在重新订阅杂志。
They are resubscribing to the gym membership. = 他们正在重新订阅健身房会员资格。


一般过去时:
I resubscribed to the newspaper after a year. = 我在一年后重新订阅了报纸。
She resubscribed to the online course last week. = 她上周重新订阅了在线课程。


现在完成时:
We have resubscribed to Netflix. = 我们已经重新订阅了奈飞。
They have resubscribed to the streaming service for the third time. = 他们已经第三次重新订阅了流媒体服务。


过去完成时:
He had resubscribed to the journal before the new issue came out. = 新期刊发行前，他已经重新订阅了该期刊。
She had resubscribed to the yoga classes before the new schedule started. = 在新的课程表开始之前，她已经重新订阅了瑜伽课程。


将来时:
I will resubscribe to the software next month. = 我下个月将重新订阅该软件。
They will resubscribe to the magazine after their holiday. = 他们度假后将重新订阅杂志。


虚拟语气:
If I had more money, I would resubscribe to the premium service. = 如果我有更多钱，我会重新订阅高级服务。
I wish I could resubscribe to that amazing podcast. = 我真希望我能重新订阅那个精彩的播客。


被动语态:

否定句:
