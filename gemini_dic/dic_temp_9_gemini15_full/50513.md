deprivement:/dɪˈpraɪvmənt/ | n. |剥夺；丧失；缺乏 |The state of being deprived of something; loss or lack of something considered essential or desirable.  Deprivation often refers to the lack of basic necessities like food, shelter, or education. |例句：The long-term deprivation of sleep led to health problems. 长期睡眠剥夺导致健康问题。  The children suffered from severe emotional deprivation. 孩子们遭受了严重的感情剥夺。|近义词：deprivation, loss, lack, want, absence |反义词：abundance, plenty, provision, enrichment

词源:
The word "deprivement" is derived from the verb "deprive," which comes from the Old French word "depriver," meaning "to take away from, to rob."  The suffix "-ment" indicates a state or condition.  The ultimate roots trace back to Latin "privare," meaning "to deprive."
