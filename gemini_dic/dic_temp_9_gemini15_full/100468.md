interval:/ˈɪntərvəl/| n.|间隔，间歇；区间；幕间休息|A period of time between events or things.|The train arrives at intervals of 10 minutes. 火车每隔十分钟到达一次。|There was a long interval between the two world wars. 两次世界大战之间有一段很长的间隔。|近义词：gap, space, pause|反义词：continuity, continuation


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The train arrives at intervals of ten minutes. = 火车每隔十分钟到达一次。
She takes a short interval between each exercise. = 她在每个练习之间休息一会儿。


一般过去时:
There was a long interval between the two acts. = 两幕之间有很长的间隔。
He worked without interval for hours. = 他连续工作了好几个小时。


现在完成时:
There have been several intervals of silence during the meeting. = 会议期间有好几次沉默的间隙。
We have had many intervals of sunshine this week. = 这周我们有很多阳光明媚的间隙。


过去完成时:
There had been several intervals of rain before the storm broke. = 在暴风雨来临之前，已经下过几次阵雨了。
By the time I got there, a significant interval had passed. = 当我到达那里时，已经过了一段相当长的时间。


将来时:
There will be a 15-minute interval after the first act. = 第一幕结束后将有15分钟的休息时间。
We will have regular intervals for rest during the hike. = 在徒步旅行中，我们将定期休息。


虚拟语气:
If there were a shorter interval between trains, more people would use them. = 如果火车间隔时间更短，更多的人会乘坐火车。
I wish there were more intervals for reflection in my busy schedule. = 我希望在我忙碌的日程安排中能有更多的时间进行反思。


被动语态:
The performance was interrupted by several intervals of noise. = 表演被几次噪音打断。
The intervals between songs were filled with announcements. = 歌曲之间的间隙被公告填满了。


疑问句:
What is the interval between the two meetings? = 两次会议之间的时间间隔是多少？
How long is the interval? = 间隔时间有多长？


否定句:
There was no interval between the two speeches. = 两次演讲之间没有间隔。
The work continued without interval. = 工作持续进行，没有间歇。
