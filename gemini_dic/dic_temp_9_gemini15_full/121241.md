词源：
From Latin *miserēre, infinitive of misereri "to feel pity, to have compassion," from miser "wretched, unhappy" (see misery) + -ēri, a verb-forming suffix.  It's primarily known as the name of Psalm 51 (the 51st Psalm in the Vulgate), a penitential psalm.  The word's usage expanded to include musical settings of this psalm and, more broadly, to any expression of deep sorrow or penitence.
