/kæˈsiːmiə/ | n. | 喀什米尔病 |  Cachexia is a complex syndrome characterized by severe weight loss, muscle wasting, and weakness. It is often associated with chronic diseases such as cancer, AIDS, and heart failure. The exact cause of cachexia is not fully understood, but it is believed to be a result of a combination of factors, including inflammation, hormonal imbalances, and metabolic changes.  Treatment focuses on addressing the underlying disease and improving nutritional intake. | The patient's cachexia was a significant concern, impacting their ability to participate in treatment. | 近义词：wasting, emaciation, atrophy | 反义词：robustness, health, vigor | 名词
