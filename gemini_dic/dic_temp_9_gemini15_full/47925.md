deceptivity:/dɪˈsɛptɪvɪti/| n. |欺骗性，迷惑性|The deceptivity of the illusion made it difficult to discern reality from fantasy. 错觉的迷惑性使得难以分辨现实与幻想。|近义词：deception, trickery, guile|反义词：truthfulness, honesty, frankness|名词


英文解析:The quality or state of being deceptive; the tendency to deceive or mislead.  It refers to the act or practice of causing someone to accept as true or valid what is false or invalid.  This can be intentional or unintentional, depending on the context.


例句:

The deceptivity of his smile hid a cruel heart. 他笑容的迷惑性掩盖了一颗残酷的心。

The deceptivity of the advertisement led many consumers to buy the product. 广告的欺骗性导致许多消费者购买了该产品。
