deludes:/dɪˈluːdz/| vt.|欺骗；迷惑；使产生错觉|To deceive someone into believing something that is not true.  To fool or mislead someone.|例句：He deluded himself into thinking he could win. 他自欺欺人地认为自己能赢。

例句：The magician deluded the audience with his clever tricks. 魔术师用他精湛的技巧欺骗了观众。


近义词：deceive, mislead, trick, fool, hoodwink

反义词：enlighten, inform, reveal, clarify

词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deludes himself with false hopes. 他抱着虚假的希望自欺欺人。
She deludes her followers with empty promises. 她用空洞的承诺欺骗她的追随者。


一般过去时:
The advertisement deluded many consumers into buying the product. 广告欺骗了许多消费者购买该产品。
He deluded himself into believing he was important. 他自欺欺人地认为自己很重要。


现在完成时:
They have deluded the public for years. 他们多年来一直在欺骗公众。
She has deluded herself into thinking she's invincible. 她自欺欺人地认为自己是无敌的。


过去完成时:
He had deluded himself into believing he was talented. 他曾经自欺欺人地认为自己很有天赋。
They had deluded the investors with fraudulent schemes. 他们曾用欺诈计划欺骗投资者。


将来时:
The campaign will delude voters with promises it cannot keep. 这场竞选活动将用无法兑现的承诺来欺骗选民。
He will delude himself again if he doesn't change his ways. 如果他不改变自己的方式，他还会再次自欺欺人。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't been deluded by his charm, I wouldn't have trusted him. 如果我没有被他迷人的魅力所迷惑，我就不会相信他。
I wish I hadn't been deluded by his lies. 我真希望我没有被他谎言所欺骗。


被动语态:
Many people were deluded by the propaganda. 许多人被宣传所迷惑。
The public has been deluded by false claims. 公众被虚假说法所欺骗。


疑问句:
Was he deluded by her beauty? 他是被她的美貌所迷惑吗？
Are you deluded into thinking you can succeed without hard work? 你是不是自欺欺人地认为不努力也能成功？


否定句:
He was not deluded by their flattery. 他没有被他们的奉承所迷惑。
I am not deluded about the difficulties ahead. 我并没有对未来的困难抱有幻想。


PHRASE->

词源:
源自中古英语 *deluden，来自古法语 *deluder，来自拉丁语 *de- + *ludere（玩耍，欺骗）。
