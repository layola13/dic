fair:/fer/| adj.|公平的，合理的，美丽的，相当大的|Fair means just, impartial, and equitable.  It can also describe something beautiful or of considerable size.  |例句：The judge ensured a fair trial.  法官确保了一场公正的审判。
例句：She has fair skin and golden hair. 她有着白皙的皮肤和金色的头发。|近义词：just, equitable, impartial, lovely, pretty, considerable|反义词：unjust, unfair, inequitable, biased, ugly, insignificant|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The judge is fair. = 法官是公平的。
The weather is fair today. = 今天天气晴朗。


一般过去时:
The game was fair and square. = 比赛公平公正。
The weather was fair yesterday. = 昨天天气晴朗。


现在完成时:
We have had a fair share of problems. = 我们已经遇到过相当多的问题。
The team has played fairly this season. = 这个赛季球队比赛一直很公平。


过去完成时:
They had already had a fair amount of success. = 他们已经取得了相当大的成功。
She had played fair throughout the competition. = 在整个比赛中她都表现得很公平。


将来时:
The weather will be fair tomorrow. = 明天天气会晴朗。
I hope the judge will be fair. = 我希望法官会公平。


虚拟语气:
If the judge were fair, the result would be different. = 如果法官公平，结果就会不一样。
I wish the weather were fair for our picnic. = 我希望天气晴朗以便我们野餐。


被动语态:
The case was judged fairly. = 此案得到了公平的审判。
The decision was deemed fair by all parties. = 所有各方都认为该决定是公平的。


疑问句:
Is the deal fair? = 这笔交易公平吗？
Is the price fair? = 价格合理吗？


否定句:
The game wasn't fair. = 比赛不公平。
The judge wasn't fair to the defendant. = 法官对被告不公平。
