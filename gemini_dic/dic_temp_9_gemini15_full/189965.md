sanctioning: /ˈsæŋkʃənɪŋ/ | v. | 制裁；批准；认可 | The act of imposing a penalty or restriction, or the act of giving official approval or permission.  | 例句：The UN is sanctioning the country for its human rights abuses. 联合国因该国侵犯人权而对其进行制裁。  The government sanctioned the new building plans. 政府批准了新的建筑计划。 | 近义词：approving, authorizing, penalizing, punishing | 反义词：approving, supporting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The council is sanctioning the new development. = 理事会正在批准新的发展项目。
The government sanctions trade with enemy nations. = 政府制裁与敌对国家的贸易。

一般过去时:
The committee sanctioned the proposal. = 委员会批准了该提案。
The court sanctioned the defendant for contempt. = 法庭因藐视法庭而对被告处以罚款。


现在完成时:
The international community has sanctioned several countries for their actions. = 国际社会已经因为这些国家的行为而对它们实施制裁。
Several countries have sanctioned Russia for its invasion of Ukraine. =好几个国家已经因为俄罗斯入侵乌克兰而对其实施制裁。


过去完成时:
The company had sanctioned the project before the funding was secured. = 在资金到位之前，公司已经批准了该项目。
The government had sanctioned the use of force before the conflict began. = 在冲突开始之前，政府已经批准使用武力。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If the committee sanctioned the proposal, we could proceed. = 如果委员会批准该提案，我们就可以继续进行。
