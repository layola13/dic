pole:/poʊl/| n. |杆，柱，极点 |A long, slender, rounded piece of wood or metal, often used as a support or for a specific purpose.  Examples include flagpoles, tent poles, and the North Pole.  The word also refers to the Earth's axis or a magnetic pole.|例句：He planted a pole to mark the boundary. 他竖起一根杆子来标记边界。

The South Pole is very cold. 南极很冷。|近义词：post, stake, shaft, rod |反义词：equator

词源:
The word "pole" comes from the Old English word "pólu," which itself is derived from the Latin word "polus," meaning "axis."  The meaning has expanded over time to encompass various long, slender objects used as supports or markers.
