preservatives:/prɪˈzɜːrveɪtɪvz/| n.|防腐剂|Substances added to food to prevent spoilage and extend shelf life.  They inhibit the growth of microorganisms like bacteria, yeasts, and molds, and can also prevent enzymatic reactions that cause food to deteriorate.|Example: Many processed foods contain preservatives to maintain their quality and safety.|近义词：food additives, stabilizers|反义词：None (antonyms are more accurately described as actions that oppose preservation, such as spoilage or decay)|名词
