cockneyism:/ˈkɒkniˌɪzəm/| n.|考克尼方言特色|A characteristic feature of Cockney speech, especially a distinctive pronunciation or a particular word or phrase.  This often involves rhyming slang, dropping the 'h' sound, or using unique vocabulary. | Example:  "He's got a 'dog and bone' " (meaning "He's got a telephone"). This is a Cockney rhyming slang.  | 近义词：Cockney slang, London slang | 反义词：Standard English
