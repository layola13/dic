propellers:/prəˈpɛlərz/| n. |螺旋桨；推进器 |Rotating blades that push a vessel or aircraft through a fluid (water or air).  Propellers convert rotational motion into thrust. |The airplane's propellers whirred as it took off.  飞机的螺旋桨在起飞时嗡嗡作响。

The ship's propellers churned the water.  船的螺旋桨搅动着水。|  screw, rotor |sail, oars |名词

词源:
From Middle English propellere, from Old French propeler, from Latin propellere, from pro- ("forward") + pellere ("to push, drive").  The modern sense of a rotating blade device for propulsion developed later.
