expressness: /ɪkˈsprɛsnɪs/ | n. | 表达力；明确性；坦率 | The quality of being expressive; the ability to convey meaning or feeling effectively.  It can also refer to the clarity and directness of something. |例句：Her paintings are remarkable for their expressness and emotional depth.  她的画作以其表达力和情感深度而著称。

The speech lacked expressness; the speaker's message was unclear.  演讲缺乏表达力；演讲者的信息不明确。|近义词：expressiveness, clarity, directness |反义词：vagueness, ambiguity, indirectness


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, typical verb tenses are not applicable.  However, we can show how it's used in different sentence structures.


In a declarative sentence:
The artist's expressness was evident in her bold brushstrokes. = 艺术家的表达力在她大胆的笔触中显而易见。
His writing lacked expressness, making it difficult to understand his intentions. = 他的写作缺乏表达力，使得难以理解他的意图。


In an interrogative sentence:
Was there enough expressness in his letter to convey his feelings? = 他的信中表达力是否足够来传达他的感情？
Did the speaker's expressness leave any room for misinterpretation? = 演讲者的表达力是否留有误解的空间？


In an exclamatory sentence:
What expressness!  The way she conveyed her emotions was truly remarkable. = 多么强的表达力！她表达情感的方式真是非凡。
Such expressness in a short poem! = 短诗中表达力如此之强！
