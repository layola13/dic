drowning:/ˈdraʊnɪŋ/ | v. n. adj.| 溺水；淹没；使沉没；充满；沉浸 |  The act of being submerged in water and unable to breathe, resulting in death or near-death.  It can also refer to being overwhelmed by something. |例句：The child was drowning in the river.  孩子在河里溺水。 The city was drowning in a deluge of rain. 城市被暴雨淹没了。 | 近义词：submerging, inundating, overwhelming | 反义词：saving, rescuing, surfacing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drowns his sorrows in alcohol. = 他借酒消愁。
She drowns her worries in work. = 她埋头苦干以忘记烦恼。


一般过去时:
The boat capsized and several passengers drowned. = 船翻了，几名乘客溺水身亡。
He almost drowned in the strong current. = 他差点被急流冲走溺水。


现在完成时:
Three people have drowned in that lake this year. = 今年已经有三人在那湖中溺亡。
Many animals have drowned in the recent floods. = 近期洪水中许多动物溺亡。


过去完成时:
The rescuers discovered that the man had drowned hours earlier.  = 救援人员发现该男子数小时前就已经溺亡了。
She realized she had almost drowned when she regained consciousness. = 她恢复意识后才意识到自己差点溺水。


将来时:
If he falls in, he will drown. = 如果他掉下去，他会溺水。
The heavy rain will drown the crops. = 大雨会淹没庄稼。


虚拟语气:
If I had swum further, I might have drowned. = 如果我游得更远，我可能会溺水。
I wish I hadn't gone into the water; I almost drowned. = 我真希望我没有下水；我差点溺水。


被动语态:
Many people were drowned in the tsunami. = 海啸中许多人被淹死。
The field was drowned by the unexpected rain. = 田地被突如其来的雨水淹没了。


疑问句:
Did anyone drown in the accident? = 事故中有人溺水吗？
How many people drowned in the storm? = 暴风雨中溺水身亡的人有多少？


否定句:
He didn't drown, he was rescued. = 他没有溺水，他被救了。
The boat didn't drown, it floated. = 船没有沉，它漂浮着。
