nonsalinity:/ˌnɒn.səˈlɪnəti/| n. |非盐度；淡水度|The absence of salinity; the state of being fresh or not salty.  Nonsalinity is a characteristic of freshwater environments, distinguishing them from saline or brackish waters.  It refers to the low concentration of dissolved salts in water. |Example: The high nonsalinity of the river made it unsuitable for certain species of fish.  这条河的高淡水度使得某些鱼类不适合在其中生存。|近义词：freshness, lack of salinity|反义词：salinity, saltiness
