hyperreactivities: /ˌhaɪpərˌriˌæktɪˈvɪtiz/ | n. |过敏反应性，过度反应性 | Excessive reactivity or responsiveness, often to stimuli that would not normally cause such a strong reaction. This term is often used in medical contexts to describe heightened sensitivity or exaggerated responses in various systems of the body, such as the immune system, nervous system, or endocrine system.  | Example:  The patient's hyperreactivities to common allergens necessitated a change in treatment. 患者对常见过敏原的过度反应需要改变治疗方案。  |近义词: hypersensitivity, overresponsiveness, exaggerated response |反义词: hyporeactivity, underresponsiveness, blunted response
