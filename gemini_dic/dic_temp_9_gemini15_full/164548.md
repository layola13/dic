preevaporating: /ˌpriːɪˈvæpəreɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 预先蒸发 |  The act of evaporating something before another process.  It's a less common word, often used in technical contexts describing processes where evaporation is a preparatory step. For example, in some chemical processes, a substance might be preevaporated to remove water before further reactions. | The technician preevaporated the solvent before adding the catalyst. 技师在添加催化剂之前预先蒸发了溶剂。|  pre-evaporating (no direct synonyms, the meaning needs to be conveyed contextually) | non-evaporating |  v.
