ruminant:/ˈruːmɪnənt/| n. |反刍动物|An even-toed ungulate mammal that chews the cud.  Ruminants have a four-chambered stomach.  They regurgitate their food to further break it down before digestion.|例句：Cows, sheep, and goats are all ruminants. 奶牛、绵羊和山羊都是反刍动物。
|例句：The rancher's herd consisted mainly of ruminants.  这位牧场主的畜群主要由反刍动物组成。|近义词：cud-chewer|反义词：monogastric (单胃动物)|名词

词源:
From Latin *rūmināre "to chew the cud," from rumen "throat, gullet."  The word ultimately derives from the Proto-Indo-European root *reu-, meaning "to roar." This is linked to the sounds made by animals during rumination.
