contracted:/kənˈtræktɪd/ | adj. v. | 缩小的；已订婚的；感染的；(肌肉)收缩的 | Reduced in size; engaged to be married; infected; (of muscles) shortened and tightened. |

例句：

The contracted muscles caused him intense pain.  =  收缩的肌肉让他疼痛难忍。

The company contracted its operations to save money. = 公司缩减业务以节省开支。


近义词：shortened, reduced, compressed, engaged, infected

反义词：expanded, enlarged, extended, disengaged, healthy


词源:

From Middle English *contracted, from Old French contracter, from Latin contractus, past participle of contrahere "to draw together," from com- "together" + trahere "to draw."

用法说明：

作为形容词:

* **Reduced in size:**  This refers to something that has become smaller.  For example, a contracted muscle, a contracted economy, or a contracted version of a document.

* **Engaged to be married:** This is a less common usage, referring to a couple who are formally engaged.

* **Infected (with a disease):** This describes someone who has caught a disease.

* **(of muscles) shortened and tightened:** This is a specific medical or anatomical term.


作为动词:

* **To make or become smaller:** This is the most common usage.  It can be used transitively (contracting something) or intransitively (something contracting).  Examples include contracting a muscle, contracting a business, contracting a disease.

* **To enter into an agreement:**  This refers to formally agreeing to something, such as contracting a service or contracting a loan.  This usage often involves a formal written agreement.

* **To reduce the size or scope of something:** To lessen or shorten, in size or duration.
