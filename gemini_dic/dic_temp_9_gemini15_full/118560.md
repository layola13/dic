mesopodium:/ˌmɛsoˈpoʊdiəm/| n. |中足|The mesopodium is the middle part of a limb, specifically referring to the region between the carpus (wrist) or tarsus (ankle) and the digits (fingers or toes) in certain animals.  It is less commonly used than terms like "metacarpus" (hand) or "metatarsus" (foot) in mammals, but more relevant to describing the limb structure of certain invertebrates. |The insect's mesopodium was unusually long and slender.  昆虫的中足特别长而细。|近义词:metacarpus, metatarsus (in mammals)|反义词:propodium, epipodium (depending on context)|名词
