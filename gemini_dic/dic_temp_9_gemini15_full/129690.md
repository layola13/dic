non-disjunction:/ˌnɒn dɪsˈdʒʌŋkʃən/ | n. | 染色体不分离 | The failure of homologous chromosomes or sister chromatids to separate during cell division.  This results in an abnormal number of chromosomes in the daughter cells.  Non-disjunction can occur during meiosis (resulting in gametes with an abnormal number of chromosomes) or mitosis (resulting in somatic cells with an abnormal number of chromosomes). | Example: Non-disjunction during meiosis I can lead to Down syndrome. 染色体I期不分离会导致唐氏综合症。


Example: Non-disjunction of chromosome 21 is a common cause of Down syndrome. 21号染色体的非分离是唐氏综合症的常见原因。


近义词：chromosome segregation error, meiotic error, mitotic error | 反义词：disjunction | 词性：名词
