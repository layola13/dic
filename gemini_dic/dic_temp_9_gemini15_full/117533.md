meeting:/ˈmiːtɪŋ/| n.|会议，集会|A meeting is a gathering of people for a particular purpose, such as a business discussion, a social event, or a political rally.  It can be formal or informal.|例句：The board meeting is scheduled for tomorrow morning.  董事会会议定于明天上午举行。|例句：We had a casual meeting at the coffee shop.我们在咖啡店进行了一次非正式的会面。|近义词：conference, assembly, gathering, session|反义词：separation, parting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The team has a meeting every Monday. = 团队每周一开会。
We hold a staff meeting twice a month. = 我们每月举行两次员工会议。

一般过去时:
The meeting ended at 5 pm. = 会议在下午5点结束。
They had a productive meeting yesterday. = 他们昨天开了一个富有成效的会议。

现在完成时:
We have already had three meetings this week. = 我们本周已经开了三次会了。
They have just finished the meeting. = 他们刚刚结束会议。

过去完成时:
The decision had been made before the meeting started. = 会议开始之前就已经做出了决定。
We had already discussed the proposal before the meeting. = 会议之前我们已经讨论过这个提案了。

将来时:
The meeting will be held in the conference room. = 会议将在会议室举行。
We will have a meeting next week to discuss the project. = 我们下周将举行会议讨论这个项目。

虚拟语气:
If we had had more time, the meeting would have been more productive. = 如果我们有更多时间，会议会更有效率。
I wish the meeting hadn't been so long. = 我希望会议不要这么长。

被动语态:
The meeting was chaired by the CEO. = 会议由首席执行官主持。
The agenda for the meeting was distributed beforehand. = 会议议程事先已分发。

疑问句:
What time is the meeting? = 会议几点钟？
Where is the meeting being held? = 会议在哪里举行？

否定句:
There was no meeting today. = 今天没有会议。
We didn't attend the meeting. = 我们没有参加会议。
