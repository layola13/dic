doodler:/ˈduːdlər/| n. |涂鸦者，随意乱画者 | A person who doodles.  Someone who draws or scribbles absentmindedly. | Example: My son is a doodler; he fills his notebooks with strange creatures.  我的儿子是个涂鸦者；他把他的笔记本里画满了奇怪的生物。|近义词：scribbler, scrawler |反义词：precise artist, meticulous draftsman


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He doodles during boring meetings. = 他在无聊的会议期间涂鸦。
She doodles intricate patterns. = 她涂鸦着复杂的图案。


一般过去时:
He doodled a funny face in his notebook. = 他在他的笔记本上涂鸦了一个滑稽的脸。
She doodled all through the lecture. = 她在整个讲座期间都在涂鸦。


现在完成时:
He has doodled all over his textbook. = 他在他的教科书上到处涂鸦。
She has doodled a whole sketchbook. = 她已经涂鸦了一整本速写本。


过去完成时:
He had doodled a map on the napkin before the waiter came. = 服务员来之前，他已经在餐巾纸上涂鸦了一张地图。
She had doodled several designs before she found one she liked. = 在她找到一个她喜欢的设计之前，她已经涂鸦了好几个设计。


将来时:
He will doodle something interesting during the meeting. = 他将在会议期间涂鸦一些有趣的东西。
She will doodle on her tablet during the flight. = 她将在飞行期间在她的平板电脑上涂鸦。


虚拟语气:
If I were bored, I would doodle. = 如果我无聊，我会涂鸦。
I wish I could doodle as well as he does. = 我希望我能像他一样擅长涂鸦。


被动语态:
The margins of the book were doodled on. = 书的页边被涂鸦了。
A strange pattern was doodled on the wall. = 一个奇怪的图案被涂鸦在墙上。


疑问句:
Does he often doodle? = 他经常涂鸦吗？
What is she doodling? = 她正在涂鸦什么？


否定句:
He doesn't doodle very often. = 他不经常涂鸦。
She didn't doodle at all during the meeting. = 会议期间她根本没有涂鸦。
