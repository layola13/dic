/ɪˈlɛktər/ | n. | 选民；选举人 | A person who has the right to vote in an election.  | The electors chose the president. 选举人选择了总统。 | voter, electorial |  non-voter


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The electors vote in the presidential election. = 选民在总统选举中投票。
She is an elector in her local constituency. = 她是当地选区的一名选民。

一般过去时:
The electors voted for the candidate they preferred. = 选民投票给了他们更喜欢的候选人。
He was an elector in the last general election. = 他是上次大选的选民。

现在完成时:
The electors have already cast their votes. = 选民们已经投票了。
She has been an elector for many years. = 她多年来一直是选民。

过去完成时:
The electors had already chosen their candidate before the debate. = 在辩论之前，选举人已经选出了他们的候选人。
He had never been an elector before that election. = 在那次选举之前，他从未做过选举人。

将来时:
The electors will cast their votes next week. = 选民们下周将投票。
She will be an elector in the upcoming elections. = 她将在即将到来的选举中成为选民。

虚拟语气:
If the electors were to change their minds, the outcome would be different. = 如果选民改变主意，结果将会不同。
I wish the electors had chosen a different candidate. = 我希望选举人选了一个不同的候选人。

被动语态:
The president is elected by the electors. = 总统是由选举人选举产生的。
The votes were counted by the electors. = 选票是由选举人统计的。

疑问句:
Are you an elector in this election? = 你是这次选举的选民吗？
How many electors are there in this constituency? = 这个选区有多少选民？

否定句:
He is not an elector in this district. = 他不是这个地区的选民。
She did not vote in the last election, as she was not an elector then. = 她上次选举没有投票，因为她当时不是选民。
