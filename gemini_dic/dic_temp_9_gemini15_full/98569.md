institutio:/ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃəʊ/ | n. | 建立；制度；教育；训导 | The act of establishing or founding; an established institution or organization; the process of teaching or training.  | Example: The institutio of the Roman Empire was a long and complex process. 罗马帝国的建立是一个漫长而复杂的过程。  The institutio of a new school requires careful planning. 建立一所新学校需要仔细的规划。 |近义词：establishment, foundation, organization, education, instruction | 反义词：destruction, abolition, dismantling


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The university institutes new programs every year. = 这所大学每年都设立新的课程。
This company institutes strict quality control measures. = 这家公司实行严格的质量控制措施。


一般过去时:
The government instituted new tax laws last month. = 政府上个月实施了新的税法。
They instituted a new system for managing their finances. = 他们建立了一个新的财务管理系统。


现在完成时:
They have instituted many reforms since the revolution. = 自从革命以来，他们已经进行了许多改革。
The school has instituted a new dress code. = 学校已经实施了新的着装规范。


过去完成时:
Before the war, they had instituted a system of rationing. = 在战争爆发前，他们已经实行配给制度。
By the time the project started, they had already instituted a detailed plan. = 项目启动时，他们已经制定了一个详细的计划。


将来时:
The company will institute a new marketing strategy next quarter. = 公司将在下个季度实施新的营销策略。
They will institute legal proceedings against the offender. = 他们将对违规者提起诉讼。


虚拟语气:
If they instituted stricter rules, there would be less crime. = 如果他们实施更严格的规定，犯罪就会减少。
I wish they had instituted a better system of communication. = 我希望他们已经建立了一个更好的沟通系统。


被动语态:
New regulations were instituted by the government. = 新规章由政府制定。
A new policy was instituted to address the problem. = 实施了一项新政策来解决这个问题。


疑问句:
What measures will be instituted to address the crisis? = 将采取哪些措施来应对这场危机？
When was this system instituted? = 这个系统是什么时候建立的？


否定句:
The committee did not institute any changes to the policy. = 委员会没有对政策进行任何更改。
They did not institute the new program on time. = 他们没有按时启动新的项目。
