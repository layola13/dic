co-regency:/ˌkoʊˈriːdʒənsi/ | n. | 共同摄政；联合摄政 | The rule of a country by two or more rulers simultaneously, often members of the same family, usually during the minority or incapacity of a monarch.  | Example: The co-regency of Mary and William III marked a significant shift in English politics.  共同摄政玛丽和威廉三世标志着英国政治的重大转变。 |近义词：joint reign, shared rule | 反义词：sole reign, absolute rule
