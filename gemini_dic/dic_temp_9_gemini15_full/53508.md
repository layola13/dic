disassimilation: /ˌdɪsəˌsɪməˈleɪʃən/ | n. |异化；不同化；脱同化|The process by which sounds become less alike, typically involving the change of one sound to make it less similar to an adjacent sound. This is often a phonological process that simplifies pronunciation.  |Example:  The dissimilation of /p/ to /b/ in the word "cupboard" (originally "cupbord").  杯橱中/p/到/b/的异化。|近义词: differentiation, divergence |反义词: assimilation
