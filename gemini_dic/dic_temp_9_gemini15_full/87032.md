hepatization:/ˌhɛpətaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |肝化；肝样变|The process by which a tissue or organ takes on the characteristics of liver tissue, typically becoming firm and reddish-brown.  This often occurs as a result of inflammation or disease. |Example:  The lung showed signs of hepatization due to pneumonia. 肺部因肺炎而显示出肝化的迹象。|近义词：liverification|反义词：无
