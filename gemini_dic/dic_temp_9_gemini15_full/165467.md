preliquidate:/ˌpriːˈlɪkwɪdeɪt/| vt.| 预先清算；提前清算 |To settle (debts, accounts, etc.) before the usual or expected time.  This term is primarily used in financial contexts, often referring to the early repayment of debts or the early sale of assets to generate cash. |例句：The company decided to preliquidate its assets to avoid potential bankruptcy.  公司决定提前清算资产以避免潜在的破产。

The company preliquidated its inventory to make room for the new product line. 公司提前清算库存为新产品线腾出空间。|近义词：prepay, settle early, liquidate in advance |反义词：delay liquidation, postpone settlement |动词
