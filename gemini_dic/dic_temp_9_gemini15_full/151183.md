parched:/pɑːrtʃt/ | adj. | 干旱的；焦渴的；枯萎的 | extremely dry, especially from heat.  |例句：The land was parched after months without rain.  几个月没下雨，土地干旱了。

The desert was parched and barren.  沙漠干旱而贫瘠。


近义词：arid, dry, dehydrated, withered | 反义词：moist, wet, saturated | 词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The land is parched. = 这片土地干旱。
The flowers are parched from the sun. = 花儿被太阳晒枯萎了。


一般过去时:
The fields were parched last summer. = 去年夏天田地干涸了。
His throat was parched after the long run. = 长跑后他的喉咙干渴了。


现在完成时:
The drought has parched the crops. = 干旱使庄稼枯萎了。
The sun has parched the earth. = 太阳晒干了大地。


过去完成时:
The riverbed had been parched for years. = 河床多年干涸了。
She had been parched before she found water. = 她在找到水之前已经非常口渴了。


将来时:
The heat will parch the land. = 炎热会使土地干旱。
If it doesn't rain soon, the plants will be parched. = 如果不久不下雨，植物就会干枯。


虚拟语气:
If it hadn't rained, the land would have been parched. = 如果不下雨，土地就会干旱。
I wish the plants weren't so parched. = 我希望植物不要这么干枯。


被动语态:
The fields were parched by the sun. = 田地被太阳晒干了。
The land is being parched by the ongoing drought. = 这片土地正遭受持续干旱的侵袭而干涸。


疑问句:
Is the soil parched? = 土壤干旱吗？
Are your lips parched? = 你的嘴唇干裂了吗？


否定句:
The land isn't parched yet. = 这片土地还没干旱。
His throat wasn't parched, he just felt thirsty. = 他的喉咙并不干渴，只是感觉口渴。

词源:
From Old English *pearced, past participle of *pearcian "to pierce, prick," from Proto-Germanic *þerskaną.  The semantic shift is likely from the idea of a "pierced" or "punctured" dryness, referring to the appearance of cracked, dry earth.
