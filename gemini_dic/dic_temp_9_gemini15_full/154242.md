periscii: /pɛrɪˈskiːaɪ/ | n. | 周日圈居民；极圈内的人 | Inhabitants of the frigid zones, those who see the sun circle around them without setting.  The term refers to people living in regions within the Arctic or Antarctic Circles where the sun remains above the horizon for at least 24 hours during summer and below the horizon for at least 24 hours during winter. | Example:  The ancient Greeks, lacking a complete understanding of the Earth’s shape, imagined the periscii as living in a bizarre and uncharted land. = 古希腊人由于对地球形状缺乏完整的理解，想象中的周日圈居民生活在一个奇异而未知的土地上。  | 近义词:  Arctic inhabitants, Antarctic inhabitants | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but could be contrasted with people living in temperate zones) | 名词
