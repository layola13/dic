jeopardises:/ˈdʒepədaɪzɪz/| vt.|危及；使…处于危险境地|To put something or someone at risk of being harmed, damaged, or destroyed.|例句：His reckless driving jeopardises not only his own life but also the lives of others.  他的鲁莽驾驶不仅危及他自己的生命，也危及他人的生命。

例句：The company's poor management jeopardises its future success. 公司的糟糕管理危及了它的未来成功。


近义词：endanger, risk, threaten, imperil|反义词：protect, safeguard, secure|词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jeopardises his health by smoking. = 他吸烟危害了他的健康。
The new policy jeopardises the company's reputation. = 新政策危及公司的声誉。

一般过去时:
The storm jeopardised the harvest. = 暴风雨危及了收成。
His careless mistake jeopardised the entire project. = 他粗心的错误危及了整个项目。

现在完成时:
His actions have jeopardised the peace talks. = 他的行为已经危及了和平谈判。
The scandal has jeopardised her career. = 这场丑闻已经危及了她的事业。

过去完成时:
They had jeopardised their position by making that decision. = 他们做出那个决定已经危及了他们的地位。
The company had jeopardised its future by neglecting its employees. = 公司忽视员工已经危及了它的未来。

将来时:
The upcoming election will jeopardise the country's stability. = 即将到来的选举将危及国家的稳定。
Further delays will jeopardise the project's completion. = 进一步的延误将危及项目的完成。

虚拟语气:
If he continued to drink, he would jeopardise his health. = 如果他继续喝酒，他会危及他的健康。
I wish he hadn’t jeopardised the deal. = 我真希望他没有危及这笔交易。

被动语态:
The peace talks were jeopardised by his actions. = 和平谈判因他的行为而受到危及。
The project was jeopardised by a lack of funding. = 该项目因缺乏资金而受到危及。

疑问句:
Does this decision jeopardise our chances of success? = 这个决定是否危及我们成功的几率？
Will the new regulations jeopardise the industry? = 新规会危及这个行业吗？

否定句:
The company did not jeopardise its reputation. = 公司没有危及它的声誉。
He didn't jeopardise his job by speaking out. = 他没有因为直言不讳而危及他的工作。

词源:
The word "jeopardise" comes from the Old French "jeu partir," meaning "to play a game" or "to stake."  The modern meaning evolved from the idea of putting something at risk, like a gambler risking their stake in a game.  The "j" sound is a later development.
