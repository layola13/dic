hyperconstitutional:/ˌhaɪpərˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃənəl/| adj.| 超宪法的；高于宪法的 |Beyond or above the constitution; exceeding the limits of constitutional power.  This term describes actions, laws, or powers that go beyond the authority granted by a constitution.|Example: The president's actions were deemed hyperconstitutional by the opposition party, exceeding the powers outlined in the constitution.  总统的行动被反对党认为是超宪法的，超过了宪法中规定的权力。|近义词：ultraconstitutional, unconstitutional (in the sense of exceeding limits, not simply violating)|反义词：constitutional


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective,  and therefore doesn't have verb conjugations like "what".  The usage would be within a sentence describing something as "hyperconstitutional". Examples will illustrate its use in different sentence structures.

Declarative Sentences:
The Supreme Court ruled the decree hyperconstitutional. 最高法院裁定该法令超宪法。
His actions were clearly hyperconstitutional and therefore invalid. 他的行为显然是超宪法的，因此无效。


Interrogative Sentences:
Is the new law hyperconstitutional? 新法律是超宪法的吗？
Could the proposed amendment be considered hyperconstitutional?  拟议的修正案会被认为是超宪法的吗？


Negative Sentences:
The government's response was not hyperconstitutional, but rather within the bounds of the law. 政府的回应并非超宪法的，而是在法律范围内。
The judges found the legislation not to be hyperconstitutional.  法官们认定该立法并非超宪法的。
