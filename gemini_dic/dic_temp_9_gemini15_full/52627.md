digged-up: /ˈdɪɡd ʌp/ | adj. | 挖出来的；被发现的；被揭露的 |  Describes something that has been unearthed or discovered, often implying something hidden or previously unknown has been brought to light.  It can also refer to something that has been revived or re-examined after being forgotten or neglected. |

例句：

The digged-up artifacts revealed a lost civilization.  挖出来的文物揭示了一个失落的文明。

The digged-up scandal damaged his reputation.  被揭露的丑闻损害了他的名誉。


近义词：unearthed, discovered, revealed, exposed, exhumed

反义词：buried, hidden, concealed, obscured


词性：形容词 (It's an adjective formed from the past participle of the verb "dig up".)


USAGE->

一般现在时:

The archeologists dig up artifacts. 考古学家挖掘文物。
They dig up old memories. 他们挖掘旧的回忆。

一般过去时:

The dog dug up the bone. 狗挖出了骨头。
They dug up the garden. 他们翻了花园的土。

现在完成时:

They have dug up all the potatoes. 他们已经挖出了所有的土豆。
She has dug up some interesting information. 她挖到了一些有趣的信息。


过去完成时:

They had dug up the treasure before the police arrived. 警察到达之前他们已经挖出了宝藏。
The gardener had dug up the weeds before the rain started.  下雨之前园丁已经除掉了杂草。

将来时:

They will dig up the foundations tomorrow. 他们明天将挖掘地基。
We will dig up the truth eventually. 我们最终会查明真相。

虚拟语气:

If they had dug up the evidence sooner, the trial would have been different. 如果他们早点挖出证据，审判结果就会不同。
I wish I had dug up more information before the meeting. 我希望我在会议前能多找些资料。

被动语态:

The garden was dug up last week. 花园上周被翻土了。
The truth was eventually dug up. 真相最终被揭露了。

疑问句:

Have they dug up anything interesting? 他们挖到什么有趣的东西了吗？
Did they dig up the whole garden? 他们把整个花园都翻了吗？

否定句:

They didn't dig up anything valuable. 他们没有挖到任何有价值的东西。
We haven't dug up enough evidence yet. 我们还没有挖到足够的证据。
