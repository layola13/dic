respiting:/rɪˈspaɪtɪŋ/| v. |暂缓；使缓和；暂时中止 |The act of respiting something, usually a punishment or difficult task; granting a temporary delay or reprieve.  |例句：The judge respited the sentence, giving the defendant more time to prepare his defense. = 法官暂缓了判决，给被告更多时间准备辩护。

例句：The rain respited for a while, allowing us to finish our picnic. = 雨暂时停了，让我们完成了野餐。

近义词：postpone, delay, defer, suspend, stay

反义词：accelerate, expedite, hasten


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He respites the execution. = 他暂缓处决。
She respites her work for a few days. = 她把工作暂缓几天。


一般过去时:
The king respited the prisoner. = 国王赦免了囚犯。
They respited their journey due to the bad weather. = 由于天气恶劣，他们推迟了行程。


现在完成时:
The judge has respited the sentence until next week. = 法官已将判决暂缓至下周。
We have respited our decision until we have more information. = 我们已将决定暂缓，直到获得更多信息。


过去完成时:
He had respited his decision until after the meeting. = 他把决定暂缓到会议之后。
They had respited the project until funding was secured. = 他们已将项目暂缓，直到资金到位。


将来时:
The court will respite the case until further evidence is presented. = 法院将暂缓此案，直到提交进一步证据。
She will respite her travel plans until the pandemic is over. = 她将暂缓她的旅行计划，直到疫情结束。


虚拟语气:
If the judge respited the sentence, he would be free. = 如果法官暂缓判决，他就会自由。
I wish the storm would respite. = 我希望暴风雨能停歇。


被动语态:
The sentence was respited by the judge. = 判决被法官暂缓了。
The project was respited due to lack of funds. = 项目因资金不足而被暂缓。


疑问句:
Will the judge respite the sentence? = 法官会暂缓判决吗？
Can we respite the meeting until tomorrow? = 我们能否将会议暂缓到明天？


否定句:
The judge did not respite the sentence. = 法官没有暂缓判决。
They will not respite their plans. = 他们不会暂缓他们的计划。


PHRASE->
respite from = 来自…的喘息机会
respite of =  …的暂缓
a brief respite =  短暂的喘息
a much-needed respite =  非常需要的喘息
grant a respite =  给予缓刑


词源:
From Old French *respit, from respiter "to grant a delay", from Latin respectus "regard, consideration".  The modern sense of temporary delay or rest developed later.
