nonpromiscuous:/ˌnɒnprəˈskjuːəs/| adj. |非滥交的，专一的，选择性的|Not promiscuous; selective in choosing partners.  This term is typically used in biology to describe organisms that do not mate indiscriminately.  It can also be used figuratively to describe individuals who are selective in their relationships or choices.|例句：The study showed that this species of bird is nonpromiscuous, forming strong pair bonds for life. 这项研究表明，这种鸟类是专一的，会终生形成牢固的配偶关系。

例句：In contrast to some of its relatives, this plant demonstrates nonpromiscuous pollination, relying on specific pollinators. 与一些近亲相比，这种植物表现出选择性的授粉，依靠特定的传粉媒介。


近义词：selective, monogamous, faithful, discerning|反义词：promiscuous, indiscriminate, unfaithful


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "promiscuous," which comes from the Latin "promiscuus," meaning "mixed," "common," or "indiscriminate."  The Latin root is "miscere," meaning "to mix."
