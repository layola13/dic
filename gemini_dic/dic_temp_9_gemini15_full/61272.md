emasculation: /ɪˌmæs.kjʊˈleɪ.ʃən/ | n. | 阉割；使丧失男子气概；削弱；使无效 | The act of castrating a male; the deprivation or loss of masculine characteristics; the weakening or undermining of something. | 例句：The emasculation of the male lead in the film was a controversial choice.  电影中男主角被弱化是一个有争议的选择。


The emasculation of the opposition party left them powerless. 反对党的势力被削弱，使他们无力回天。


近义词：castration, weakening, undermining, neutralization | 反义词：empowerment, strengthening, invigoration


词源:  From Middle French *emasculacion, from Latin emasculare "to castrate," from e- "out" + masculare "to make male," from masculus "male."  The modern English word reflects the evolution of the meaning from the literal act of castration to the metaphorical sense of weakening or depriving of power.
