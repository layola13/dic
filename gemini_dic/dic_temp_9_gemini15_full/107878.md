largiloquent:/lɑːrdʒɪˈloʊkwənt/| adj. |夸夸其谈的；雄辩的；冗长的|Characterized by excessive talkativeness; verbose; long-winded.  The professor's largiloquent lecture on the intricacies of quantum physics went on for three hours.|近义词：verbose, loquacious, voluble, long-winded|反义词：laconic, taciturn, concise


Etymology:  The word "largiloquent" combines the Latin prefix "largi-" (meaning "abundant," "ample," or "copious") with the Latin word "loquens" (the present participle of "loqui," meaning "to speak").  Therefore, its literal meaning is "abundantly speaking."
