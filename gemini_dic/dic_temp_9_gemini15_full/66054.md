exasperates: /ɪɡˈzæs.pə.reɪts/ | vt. | 使恼怒；使生气；使厌烦 | To irritate intensely; to annoy greatly.  | The constant interruptions exasperated the speaker.  持续不断的打断使演讲者非常恼火。|  annoys, irritates, frustrates | pleases, soothes


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His constant complaining exasperates me. = 他不断的抱怨让我很生气。
The traffic jam exasperates drivers every day. = 交通堵塞每天都让司机们很恼火。


一般过去时:
The child's tantrum exasperated his parents. = 孩子耍脾气让他的父母很生气。
The delay exasperated the already stressed passengers. = 延误让本来就压力很大的乘客更加恼火。


现在完成时:
His behavior has exasperated everyone in the office. = 他的行为已经激怒了办公室里的每个人。
The endless paperwork has exasperated her to the point of exhaustion. = 无尽的文书工作让她厌烦到了极点。


过去完成时:
I had been exasperated by his incompetence before I finally quit. = 在我最终辞职之前，我已经被他无能的表现激怒了。
They had been exasperated by the lack of progress on the project. = 项目进展缓慢已经让他们非常恼火了。


将来时:

This situation will undoubtedly exasperate him further. = 这种情况无疑会让他更加生气。


虚拟语气:
If he continued to behave like that, it would exasperate me even more. = 如果他继续那样表现，那会让我更生气。
I wish that these constant delays wouldn't exasperate me so much. = 我希望这些持续的延误不会让我这么生气。


被动语态:
The audience was exasperated by the long and boring speech. = 听众被冗长乏味的演讲激怒了。
He was exasperated by the lack of support from his team. = 他因为缺乏团队的支持而感到恼火。

否定句:
