silents:/ˈsaɪlənts/ | n. |寂静，沉默 |The silents refer to the era of filmmaking before the advent of sound films, approximately from 1895 to 1929.  This period is characterized by the absence of synchronized dialogue, relying instead on intertitles, music, and visual storytelling.| 例句：The silents were a remarkable period in cinematic history.  无声电影是电影史上一个非凡的时期。 The audience was captivated by the silents' expressive performances. 观众被无声电影的富有表现力的表演所吸引。|近义词：quiet, hush, stillness |反义词：noise, clamor, sound
