prededicating:/ˌpriːˈdɛdɪkeɪtɪŋ/| v.|预先确定；预先规定；预先宣告|The act of establishing or declaring something beforehand, especially a belief or principle.  It often implies a commitment or assumption made before a full understanding or evidence is available.|例句：Prededicating a positive outcome can lead to overlooking potential problems. 预先认定积极的结果可能会导致忽视潜在的问题。|例句：The research team prededicated their analysis to a specific hypothesis. 研究小组预先将他们的分析限定在一个具体的假设上。|近义词：predetermining, predefining, preassuming|反义词：postponing, delaying, leaving open
