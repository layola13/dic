convictions:/kənˈvɪkʃənz/| n. |信仰；信念；定罪 |Strong convictions are important in life.  坚定的信念在生活中很重要。 His convictions were based on years of experience. 他的信念是基于多年的经验。|近义词：beliefs, principles, faiths |反义词：doubts, uncertainties |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His convictions guide his actions. = 他的信念指导他的行为。
Their strong convictions shape their decisions. = 他们坚定的信念塑造了他们的决定。


一般过去时:
Her convictions were tested during difficult times. = 她在困难时期受到了信念的考验。
His convictions led him to fight for justice. = 他的信念促使他为正义而战。


现在完成时:
My convictions have remained unchanged throughout my life. = 我毕生的信念始终未变。
His convictions have strengthened over the years. = 他多年的信念更加坚定。


过去完成时:
By then, his convictions had already been formed. = 到那时，他的信念已经形成。
She had held those convictions for many years. = 她多年来一直坚持这些信念。


将来时:
His convictions will remain steadfast. = 他的信念将坚定不移。
Her convictions will guide her future actions. = 她的信念将指导她未来的行动。


虚拟语气:
If he had stronger convictions, he would have acted differently. = 如果他更有信念，他会采取不同的行动。
I wish I had the convictions to stand up for what I believe in. = 我希望我有勇气为我所信仰的东西挺身而出。


被动语态:
His convictions were challenged in court. = 他的信念在法庭上受到了质疑。
The prisoner's convictions were overturned on appeal. = 囚犯的定罪在上诉中被推翻。


疑问句:
What are your deepest convictions? = 你最深切的信念是什么？
Do you have any strong convictions about this issue? = 你对这个问题有什么坚定的信念吗？


否定句:
He lacked the convictions to follow through. = 他缺乏坚持到底的信念。
She had no convictions about the matter. = 她对这件事没有任何信念。



PHRASE->
strong convictions: 坚定的信念
deep convictions: 深切的信念
moral convictions: 道德信念
religious convictions: 宗教信念
political convictions: 政治信念

词源:
源自拉丁语convincere，意思是“to overcome, conquer, persuade”。  最初是指说服或征服某人，后来演变成指内心的确信和信念。  "Conviction" as a legal term (定罪) developed later, reflecting the firm belief of a jury or judge in the guilt of the accused.
