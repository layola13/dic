masses:/ˈmæsɪz/ | n. | 大多数；群众；民众；质量 | The word "masses" refers to a large number of people, especially the common people of a country or society, as distinct from the elite. It can also refer to a large amount of something, often in a physical sense.  | 例句：The masses protested against the government's policies.  群众抗议政府的政策。

The masses are demanding better living conditions.  民众要求改善生活条件。


近义词：majority, multitude, crowd, populace, public

反义词：minority, elite, few, select


词源:  From Old French *masse, from Latin massa ("lump, mass").  The meaning shifted from a physical mass to a large group of people.
