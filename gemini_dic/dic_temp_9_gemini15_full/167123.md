presuspicion:/ˌpriːsəˈspɪʃən/| n. |预先怀疑，事先猜疑|A feeling of suspicion that exists before there is evidence to support it.  It refers to a suspicion that arises before any concrete evidence or indication of wrongdoing is available.  This preemptive suspicion is often based on intuition, prior experience, or a general sense of unease.|例句：The police acted on presuspicion, stopping the car before any crime had been committed. = 警方根据事先的怀疑，在任何犯罪行为发生之前就拦截了汽车。
The presuspicion surrounding the new CEO led to increased scrutiny of their activities. =  围绕新任首席执行官的预先怀疑导致对其活动的审查更加严格。|近义词：premonition, foreboding, apprehension|反义词：trust, confidence, certainty|名词
