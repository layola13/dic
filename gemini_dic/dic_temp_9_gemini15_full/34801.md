climbdown:/ˈklaɪmdaʊn/| n. v.|妥协；让步；放弃立场|The act of climbing down from a high place or position; a retreat from a previously held position or statement, often under pressure.|例句：The government was forced into a climbdown over its unpopular policies. 政府被迫在其不受欢迎的政策上做出让步。例句：After much protest, the company made a climbdown and apologized for its actions.  在多次抗议之后，该公司做出了让步并为其行为道歉。|近义词：retreat, concession, compromise, backdown|反义词：escalation, persistence, defiance|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He often makes a climbdown to avoid conflict. = 他经常妥协以避免冲突。
The company performs a climbdown on its new product. = 公司对其新产品进行了让步。

一般过去时:
She made a climbdown after realizing her mistake. = 她意识到自己的错误后做出了让步。
The politician did a climbdown on his controversial statement. = 这位政治家对他有争议的言论做出了让步。

现在完成时:
They have made several climbdowns this year. = 他们今年已经做了几次让步。
The government has finally done a climbdown on the tax increase. = 政府最终对增税做出了让步。

过去完成时:
He had made a climbdown before the negotiations began. = 在谈判开始之前，他已经做出了让步。
The company had made a climbdown before the public outcry. = 在公众抗议之前，该公司已经做出了让步。

将来时:
They will make a climbdown if the pressure continues. = 如果压力持续下去，他们将会让步。
The CEO will likely do a climbdown to appease shareholders. = 首席执行官可能会做出让步以安抚股东。

虚拟语气:
If they had made a climbdown earlier, the situation would be better. = 如果他们早些时候做出让步，情况就会更好。
I wish they would make a climbdown and admit their fault. = 我希望他们能做出让步并承认他们的错误。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The company didn't make a climbdown on its decision. = 公司没有对其决定让步。
