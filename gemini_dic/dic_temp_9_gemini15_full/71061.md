first-worlds: /ˌfɜːrst ˈwɜːrldz/ | n. | 第一世界国家（复数） | The term "first-worlds" refers to the highly developed, industrialized nations of the world, primarily those in North America, Western Europe, and parts of East Asia.  It's a term often used in the context of geopolitical discussions and comparisons with the developing world. The term is now largely considered outdated and potentially problematic due to its inherent biases and oversimplification of global complexities. | Example:  The first-worlds enjoyed unprecedented economic growth in the post-war era.  第一世界国家在战后时期享有空前的经济增长。 |近义词: developed countries, industrialized nations |反义词: developing countries, third-world countries |名词
