doffer:/ˈdɒfər/ | n., v. |脱衣工；脱掉（衣服等）的人；去除…的人或物；去除；脱掉 | A person whose job is to remove something, especially cotton from a spinning machine.  The word also refers to the act of removing something.  |例句：The doffer removed the full bobbins from the spinning mule. 脱衣工从骡机上取下满纱锭。


The doffer carefully removed the old bandages. 脱衣工小心地取下了旧绷带。

近义词：remover, taker-off, shedder |反义词：adder, applier


词源: 源自中古英语 *doff,  意思为“脱下”，是动词 *do off 的缩写。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He doffs his hat to the queen. 他向女王脱帽致敬。
The machine doffs the finished product. 机器卸下成品。


一般过去时:
She doffed her coat and sat down. 她脱掉外套坐了下来。
The workers doffed their hats as the manager passed. 当经理走过时，工人们脱帽致敬。


现在完成时:
He has doffed his disguise. 他已经脱掉了伪装。
The factory has doffed its old equipment. 工厂已经淘汰了旧设备。


过去完成时:
She had doffed her shoes before entering the house. 她在进屋前已经脱掉了鞋子。
They had doffed their uniforms before going home. 他们回家前已经脱掉了制服。


将来时:
He will doff his mask at the end of the play. 他将在剧终时摘下面具。
The company will doff its old practices. 公司将摒弃旧的作法。


虚拟语气:
If he doffed his arrogance, he would be more likeable. 如果他放下傲慢，他会更讨人喜欢。
I wish he would doff that ridiculous hat. 我希望他能脱掉那顶可笑的帽子。


被动语态:
His hat was doffed in respect.  他的帽子被脱下以示敬意。
The old system was doffed in favor of a new one. 旧制度被废除，取而代之的是新制度。

否定句:
