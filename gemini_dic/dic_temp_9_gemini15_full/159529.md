politicalness: /pəˈlɪtɪkəlnəs/ | n. |政治性，政治色彩|The quality or state of being political; the extent to which something is influenced by or related to politics.  It can refer to the involvement of political considerations, motivations, or strategies in a situation, action, or decision.|例句：The politicalness of the debate prevented a rational discussion of the issue.  这场辩论的政治性阻碍了对该问题的理性讨论。The judge stressed the importance of maintaining impartiality and avoiding any appearance of politicalness in the courtroom.  法官强调在法庭上保持公正，避免任何政治色彩的重要性。|近义词：politicality, politicization |反义词：apoliticalness, nonpartisanship


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense does not apply.  However, it can be used in various sentence structures.

Example Sentences demonstrating different uses:

Declarative Sentence:  The politicalness of the campaign was evident in the negative advertising.  这场竞选的政治性在负面广告中显而易见。

Interrogative Sentence: What is the degree of politicalness involved in this decision?  这个决定中涉及的政治性程度有多大？

Exclamatory Sentence: The politicalness of it all is astounding!  这一切的政治性令人震惊！

Negative Sentence:  There was a lack of politicalness in the student government's approach to the issue. 学生会处理这个问题的方式缺乏政治性。
