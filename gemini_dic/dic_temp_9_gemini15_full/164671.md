preexploded: /ˌpriːɪkˈspləʊdɪd/ | adj. | 预先爆炸的，预先引爆的 |  Describes something that has been exploded or detonated beforehand, typically as a controlled measure.  This term is primarily used in technical contexts, particularly in relation to demolition, military operations, or industrial processes. |


例句：
The building was preexploded to ensure a controlled collapse. = 这栋建筑物被预先引爆以确保受控倒塌。

The demolition crew preexploded the charges to minimize risk. = 拆除队预先引爆了炸药以最大限度地降低风险。


近义词：pre-detonated, pre-triggered


反义词：unexplained, intact


词性：形容词
