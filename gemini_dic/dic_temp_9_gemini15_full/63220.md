entering:/ˈɛntəriŋ/ | v. |进入；参加；开始|The act of going into or becoming a part of something; the process of beginning something.|例句：He is entering the competition. 他正在参加比赛。

例句：The sun is entering the clouds. 太阳正在进入云层。


近义词：entering, commencing, initiating, beginning, joining


反义词：exiting, leaving, departing, quitting


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I am entering the building. = 我正在进入大楼。
She is entering a new phase of her life. = 她正在进入人生的新阶段。


一般过去时:
He entered the room quietly. = 他悄悄地走进了房间。
They entered the competition last year. = 他们去年参加了比赛。


现在完成时:
I have entered the race. = 我已经报名参加了比赛。
She has entered a new era of her career. = 她已经进入职业生涯的新纪元。


过去完成时:
He had entered the house before the rain started. = 下雨开始之前他已经进了屋。
They had entered the agreement before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期之前就已经签订了协议。


将来时:
I will be entering the university next year. = 我明年将进入大学。
They will enter the market with a new product. = 他们将带着新产品进入市场。


虚拟语气:
If I were entering the contest, I would win. = 如果我参加比赛，我会赢。
I wish I were entering the competition. = 我真希望我参加了比赛。


被动语态:
The building is being entered illegally. = 大楼正在被非法进入。
The new policy was entered into effect last month. = 新政策上个月生效了。


疑问句:
Are you entering the contest? = 你要参加比赛吗？
Is she entering the building? = 她正在进入大楼吗？


否定句:
I am not entering the competition. = 我不参加比赛。
He did not enter the room. = 他没有进入房间。


PHRASE->
entering into: 开始从事; 缔结(协议等)
entering data: 输入数据
entering a plea: 提出答辩
entering a new market: 进入新市场
entering the workforce: 进入劳动力市场

词源:
The word "entering" comes from the verb "enter," which has Old French origins ("entrer") stemming ultimately from Latin "intrare," meaning "to go in."  The "-ing" suffix indicates the present participle, denoting an ongoing action.
