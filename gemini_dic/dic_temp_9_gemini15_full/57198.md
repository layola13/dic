Down syndrome: /daʊn ˈsɪn droʊm/ | n. |唐氏综合征| A genetic disorder caused by the presence of all or part of a third copy of chromosome 21.  It is characterized by various degrees of intellectual disability, distinctive facial features, and other physical abnormalities. | Example:  Early diagnosis of Down syndrome allows for early intervention and support.  早期诊断唐氏综合征可以进行早期干预和支持。|近义词: Trisomy 21 |反义词:  None (it's a condition, not something with a direct opposite)|名词
