green-light:/ɡriːnˈlaɪt/ | v. |批准，通过，许可 |To give permission or approval for something to proceed.  |例句：The committee green-lighted the project after careful consideration. 委员会在仔细考虑后批准了该项目。|例句：The film was finally green-lighted after years of development hell.  这部电影经过多年的开发困境后终于获批。|近义词：approve, authorize, sanction |反义词：reject, veto, deny


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They green-light new projects every month. = 他们每个月都会批准新的项目。
The board green-lights most proposals. = 董事会批准大多数提案。

一般过去时:
The CEO green-lighted the marketing campaign. = 首席执行官批准了营销活动。
They green-lighted the construction last year. = 他们去年批准了这项建设。

现在完成时:
They have green-lighted three projects this year. = 今年他们已经批准了三个项目。
We have green-lighted the budget increase. = 我们已经批准了预算增加。

过去完成时:
They had green-lighted the funding before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前已经批准了资金。
The committee had green-lighted the plan a week prior. = 委员会在一周前就批准了该计划。

将来时:
They will green-light the expansion next quarter. = 他们将在下个季度批准扩张。
The government will green-light the new highway soon. = 政府很快就会批准新的高速公路。

虚拟语气:
If they green-lighted the proposal, we could start immediately. = 如果他们批准了提案，我们可以立即开始。
I wish they would green-light my funding request. = 我希望他们能批准我的资金申请。

被动语态:
The project was green-lighted by the board. = 该项目得到了董事会的批准。
The proposal was green-lighted after a long debate. = 经过长时间的辩论后，该提案获得了批准。

疑问句:
Will they green-light our application? = 他们会批准我们的申请吗？
Has the budget been green-lighted yet? = 预算已经批准了吗？

否定句:
They didn't green-light the idea. = 他们没有批准这个想法。
The proposal wasn't green-lighted due to budget constraints. = 由于预算限制，该提案未获批准。
