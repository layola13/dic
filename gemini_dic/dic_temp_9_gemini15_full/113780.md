macrosociology:/ˌmækroʊˌsoʊsiˈɑːlədʒi/| n. |宏观社会学|The study of large-scale social structures and processes.  It examines broad social patterns and trends, rather than focusing on individual interactions.  Macrosociology often uses quantitative methods to analyze data from large populations. |Example:  Macrosociology analyzes societal shifts like globalization and urbanization.  宏观社会学分析诸如全球化和城市化之类的社会转变。|近义词：large-scale sociology, societal analysis|反义词：microsociology
