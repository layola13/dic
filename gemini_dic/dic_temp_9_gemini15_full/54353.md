dishonesty: /dɪsˈɒnɪsti/ | n. | 不诚实；欺诈 | The act or practice of being dishonest; lack of honesty; deceit.  It refers to a lack of integrity, trustworthiness, or truthfulness.  This can manifest in various forms, including lying, cheating, stealing, or other deceptive behaviors. | Example:  His dishonesty cost him his job and his reputation.  他的不诚实让他失去了工作和名誉。

The company's dishonesty in its financial reporting led to a major scandal.  该公司财务报告中的不诚实导致了一场重大丑闻。


近义词：deceit, fraud, deception, duplicity, treachery, chicanery, dishonesty, perfidy

反义词：honesty, integrity, truthfulness, veracity


词源:  The word "dishonesty" is derived from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of") and the noun "honesty."  "Honesty" ultimately comes from the Old English word "honest," meaning "honor," "goodness," or "decency."  Thus, "dishonesty" literally means "the state of not being honest."
