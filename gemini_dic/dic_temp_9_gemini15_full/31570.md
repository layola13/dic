chasidism:/ˈhæsɪdɪzəm/| n. |哈西德主义|A Jewish mystical movement characterized by its emphasis on ecstatic prayer, religious enthusiasm, and the study of Kabbalah.  It originated in Eastern Europe in the 18th century and continues to be a significant branch of Judaism. |The rise of Hasidism provided a powerful counterpoint to the more intellectual and rationalistic approaches to Judaism prevalent at the time. |近义词：Jewish mysticism, pietism|反义词：secularism
