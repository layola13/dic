melopoeia:/ˌmɛləˈpoʊiə/| n. |乐句，乐音诗|The art or quality of beautiful or expressive sound in speech or writing, especially poetry.  It refers to the musicality of language, achieved through the skillful use of sound devices such as assonance, alliteration, consonance, rhythm, and rhyme.|例句：The poem's melopoeia is captivating, creating a hypnotic effect on the reader. 这首诗的乐音诗令人着迷，对读者产生了催眠效果。|近义词：euphony, musicality |反义词：cacophony, dissonance


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, grammatical tenses (present, past, future, etc.) and voice (active/passive) are not applicable.  The word itself describes a characteristic of language, not an action.
