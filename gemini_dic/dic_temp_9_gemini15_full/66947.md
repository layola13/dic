experience:/ɪkˈspɪəriəns/ | n. v. |经验，经历；体验；感受 | 英文解析：The noun "experience" refers to practical contact with and observation of facts or events.  As a verb, it means to have practical contact with something. | 例句：His experience in the army was invaluable.  他的军队经历非常宝贵。

He experienced a lot of hardship during his travels. 他在旅行中经历了许多苦难。 | 近义词：experiment, encounter, event | 反义词：inexperience, ignorance | 词性：n. v.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I experience great joy in my work. = 我在我的工作中体验到极大的快乐。
She experiences difficulty breathing. = 她呼吸困难。

一般过去时:
We experienced a power outage last night. = 我们昨晚经历了停电。
He experienced a profound sense of loss. = 他经历了深刻的失落感。


现在完成时:
I have experienced many challenges in my life. = 我一生经历过许多挑战。
She has experienced significant growth as a leader. = 她作为领导者取得了显著的进步。


过去完成时:
They had experienced similar problems before. = 他们以前也遇到过类似的问题。
I had never experienced such kindness before. = 我以前从未体验过这样的善良。


将来时:
We will experience some delays due to the weather. = 由于天气原因，我们将经历一些延误。
They will experience a cultural shock when they arrive. = 他们到达后会经历文化冲击。


虚拟语气:
If I had experienced this before, I would have been better prepared. = 如果我以前经历过这种情况，我会准备得更好。
I wish I had experienced more travel in my younger years. = 我希望我年轻时能多旅行。


被动语态:
The product is designed to be experienced by all age groups. = 该产品的设计理念是让所有年龄段的人都能体验。
Many challenges were experienced during the project. = 项目期间遇到了许多挑战。


疑问句:
Have you experienced anything like this before? = 你以前经历过这样的事情吗？
What kind of experiences are you looking for? = 你在寻找什么样的体验？


否定句:
I have not experienced such a thing before. = 我以前从未经历过这样的事情。
She didn't experience any problems during her flight. = 她在飞行过程中没有遇到任何问题。
