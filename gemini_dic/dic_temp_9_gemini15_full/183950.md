resettled:/ˌriːˈsɛtld/| v. |重新安置；重新定居|To settle again, especially in a new place or situation after being displaced.|The refugees were resettled in a new country.|近义词：relocate, reestablish, reinstate|反义词：displace, evict


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They resettle refugees in safe zones. = 他们将难民重新安置在安全区域。
The company resets its servers regularly. = 公司定期重置其服务器。


一般过去时:
The government resettled the farmers after the flood. = 政府在洪水之后重新安置了农民。
She resettled her family in a new town. = 她在一个新的城镇重新安置了她的家人。


现在完成时:
Many people have been resettled since the war. = 自战争以来，许多人已被重新安置。
The company has resettled its accounts. = 公司已经重新调整了其账户。


过去完成时:
They had resettled before the winter arrived. = 他们早在冬天到来之前就已重新定居。
The system had resettled itself before the technician arrived. = 在技术人员到达之前，系统已经自行重置。


将来时:
We will resettle in a quieter neighborhood next year. = 我们明年将搬到一个更安静的社区居住。
The government will resettle the displaced population. = 政府将重新安置流离失所的人口。


虚拟语气:
If they had more resources, they could resettle more families. = 如果他们有更多资源，他们就能重新安置更多的家庭。
I wish I could resettle my life in a tropical country. = 我希望我能在一个热带国家重新开始我的生活。


被动语态:

疑问句:
Were the families resettled successfully? = 家庭是否成功地重新安置？
Has the software been resettled properly? = 软件是否已正确重置？


否定句:
