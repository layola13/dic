kited:/ˈkaɪtɪd/ | v. |放风筝；诈骗；(非正式)兴奋；|

英文解析:The verb "kite" has several meanings.  It primarily refers to flying a kite, a light frame with thin material stretched over it, flown in the wind.  Less commonly, it can mean to defraud someone, often by writing bad checks.  In informal contexts, it can also mean to be very excited or exhilarated.

例句:

He kited his new kite high above the beach. = 他把他的新风筝放得很高，飞过海滩的上空。

The con artist kited several checks to cover his debts. = 那个骗子用几张空头支票来偿还他的债务。


近义词:fly (a kite), defraud, swindle, cheat, excite


反义词:ground (a kite), be calm, be depressed


词性:动词

词源:

The word "kite" ultimately derives from Old English "cyta," meaning a bird of prey, referring to the shape and flight of the toy.  The sense of "fraud" is a much later development, possibly stemming from the way a fraudulent check "flies" through the banking system undetected.
