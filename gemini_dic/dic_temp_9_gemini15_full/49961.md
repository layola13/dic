denialist:/dɪˈnaɪəlɪst/| n. |否认者，否定者|A person who denies the reality or seriousness of a problem, especially a scientific consensus such as climate change or the Holocaust.  Someone who refuses to accept the truth of something, often despite overwhelming evidence. |Example: Climate change denialists often cite anecdotal evidence to refute scientific findings. 气候变化否认者经常引用轶事证据来反驳科学发现。|近义词：skeptic, doubter, disbeliever |反义词：believer, supporter, advocate
