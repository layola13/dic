jolt:/dʒoʊlt/| n., vt.|猛推；震动；电击；震惊|a sudden violent shake or bump; a sudden shock or surprise; to give a sudden violent shake or bump to something; to shock or surprise someone suddenly|例句：The car jolted violently over the pothole.  汽车猛地颠簸了一下，碾过了那个坑洞。|例句：The news jolted her out of her complacency.  这个消息使她大吃一惊，打破了她的自满情绪。|近义词：shake, shock, jar, jolt|反义词：calm, soothe


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The earthquake jolts the city. = 地震震动了这座城市。
He jolts his coffee every morning. = 他每天早上猛烈地摇晃他的咖啡。


一般过去时:
The sudden noise jolted me. = 突然的噪音把我吓了一跳。
The car jolted violently when it hit the curb. = 汽车撞上路缘时剧烈震动。


现在完成时:
The news has jolted the market. = 这则新闻震动了市场。
Nothing has jolted him out of his routine. = 没有什么能让他改变他的日常习惯。


过去完成时:
The loud bang had jolted him awake. = 巨响把他震醒了。
The previous day's events had jolted her confidence. = 前一天发生的事情动摇了她的信心。


将来时:
The explosion will jolt the building. = 爆炸会震动这座建筑物。
The announcement will jolt the public. = 这一声明将震惊公众。


虚拟语气:
If the earthquake were stronger, it would jolt the whole city. = 如果地震更强烈，它将会震动整个城市。
If I had known the truth, it would have jolted me. = 如果我知道真相，那会让我大吃一惊。


被动语态:
The building was jolted by the earthquake. = 这座建筑物被地震震动了。
He was jolted awake by the loud noise. = 他被巨大的噪音震醒了。


疑问句:
Did the news jolt you? = 这个消息让你震惊了吗？
Will the announcement jolt the investors? = 这一声明会让投资者震惊吗？


否定句:
The news didn't jolt her at all. = 这个消息根本没有让她震惊。
The bumpy road didn't jolt the car too much. = 颠簸的路并没有让汽车震动太多。
