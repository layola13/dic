lockout:/ˈlɒkaʊt/ /ˈlɑːkaʊt/| n., v.|停工；封锁；拒之门外|The action of an employer to prevent employees from entering their place of work during a labor dispute.  It is a type of industrial action, typically used by employers in response to a strike by workers.  As a verb, it means to prevent someone from entering a place. |例句：The factory workers went on strike, leading to a company lockout. 工厂工人罢工，导致公司停工。


The management locked out the employees after they refused to accept the new contract. 管理层在员工拒绝接受新合同后将他们拒之门外。|近义词：shutdown, strike (as a result), exclusion|反义词：unlock, open, admission
