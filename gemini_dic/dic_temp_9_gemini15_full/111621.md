localises:/ˈloʊkəlaɪzɪz/|/ˈləʊkəlaɪzɪz/| v. | 使局部化；使地方化 | To adapt (something) to a particular place or locality; to confine to a specific location or area. | The company localises its software for different markets.  这家公司将其软件本地化以适应不同的市场。


The game has been localised for several countries.  这款游戏已针对多个国家进行了本地化。


近义词：adapt, regionalize, customize | 反义词：generalize, globalize

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company localises its software for different regions. = 公司为不同的地区本地化其软件。
He localises his teaching materials to suit the students' needs. = 他将教学材料本地化以适应学生的需要。

一般过去时:
They localised the website for the Brazilian market. = 他们为巴西市场本地化了网站。
The team localised the training manual for the new hires. = 团队为新员工本地化了培训手册。

现在完成时:
We have localised all our marketing materials. = 我们已经将所有营销材料本地化了。
The firm has localised its operations in several countries. = 这家公司已在多个国家本地化了业务。

过去完成时:
They had localised the game before its release in Japan. = 他们曾在日本发行前将游戏本地化。
The translators had localised the document by the deadline. = 翻译人员已在截止日期前将文件本地化。

将来时:
We will localise the app for the Asian market next year. = 我们明年将为亚洲市场本地化该应用程序。
The developers will localise the software for different platforms. = 开发人员将为不同的平台本地化软件。


虚拟语气:
It would be beneficial if we localised our products for the local market. = 如果我们为当地市场本地化产品，将会有益。
I wish we had localised the website earlier. = 我希望我们早点将网站本地化。


被动语态:
The software is localised by a professional team. = 该软件由专业团队本地化。
The game was localised for multiple languages. = 该游戏被本地化为多种语言。


疑问句:
Has the document been localised yet? = 文件是否已本地化？
Will the app be localised for the Chinese market? = 该应用程序是否会针对中国市场进行本地化？


否定句:
The company did not localise its product for the European market. = 公司没有为欧洲市场本地化其产品。
They haven't localised the website for the French market. = 他们尚未为法国市场本地化网站。


PHRASE->
localise for = 为...本地化
localise to = 本地化到
fully localised = 完全本地化
partially localised = 部分本地化
localised version = 本地化版本


词源:
From the verb "localise" (to make local), which itself is formed from the adjective "local" + the suffix "-ise" (a suffix used to form verbs).  The ultimate origin is from the Latin word "locus" meaning "place".
