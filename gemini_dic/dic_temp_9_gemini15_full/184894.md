retained:/rɪˈteɪnd/ | vt. adj. | 保留；记住；雇用 | to keep something; to continue to employ someone; to remember something. |例句：The company retained its market share. 公司保持了它的市场份额。 |近义词：kept, preserved, employed, remembered |反义词：released, dismissed, forgotten


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company retains its top employees. = 公司保留其顶尖员工。
He retains a lot of information from his studies. = 他记住很多学习内容。


一般过去时:
She retained the lawyer to represent her. = 她聘请了律师代表她。
They retained all the profits for themselves. = 他们将所有利润都留给了自己。


现在完成时:
The school has retained its excellent reputation. = 这所学校保持了其优秀的声誉。
I have retained the information for future reference. = 我已经保留了这些信息以备将来参考。


过去完成时:
He had retained the documents for many years. = 他保留了这些文件很多年。
She had retained all the details of the meeting. = 她记住了会议的所有细节。


将来时:
The firm will retain its current pricing strategy. = 公司将保留其目前的定价策略。
They will retain the services of a consultant. = 他们将继续聘用一位顾问。


虚拟语气:
If they retained more staff, they would be more efficient. = 如果他们保留更多员工，效率就会更高。
It would be better if you retained a copy of the contract. = 最好你保留一份合同副本。


被动语态:
The information was retained for future analysis. = 信息被保留以供将来分析。
The lawyer was retained by the company. = 律师被公司聘请。


疑问句:
Did they retain any evidence? = 他们保留了任何证据吗？
Will the company retain its position in the market? = 公司会保持其市场地位吗？


否定句:
The company did not retain any of the profits. = 公司没有保留任何利润。
He failed to retain the information he had learned. = 他未能记住他所学到的信息。
