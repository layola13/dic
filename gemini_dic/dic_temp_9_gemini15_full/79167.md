glamorize:/ˈɡlæməraɪz/ | vt. |使…具有魅力；美化；渲染；使…浪漫化 | To make something seem more attractive, exciting, or interesting than it really is. |例句：The film glamorizes the life of a gangster. 这部电影美化了黑帮的生活。
例句：The advertisement glamorizes the use of cigarettes. 这则广告渲染了吸烟的乐趣。


近义词：romanticize, idealize, romanticise


反义词：denigrate, disparage, criticize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They glamorize their lifestyle on social media. = 他们在社交媒体上美化他们的生活方式。
The magazine glamorizes unrealistic beauty standards. = 这本杂志美化了不切实际的审美标准。


一般过去时:
The director glamorized the historical events in his movie. = 导演在他的电影中美化了历史事件。
The media glamorized the celebrity's controversial behavior. = 媒体美化了这位名人的争议行为。


现在完成时:
The fashion industry has glamorized thinness for too long. = 时尚界长期以来一直美化瘦弱的身材。
He has glamorized his past experiences in his autobiography. = 他在他的自传中美化了他过去的经历。


过去完成时:
The writer had glamorized the hero's actions in the previous chapter. = 作者在上一章中美化了英雄的行为。
They had glamorized the dangers of their job to impress their friends. = 为了给朋友们留下深刻印象，他们美化了他们工作的危险性。


将来时:
The upcoming movie will glamorize the life of a famous artist. = 即将上映的电影将美化一位著名艺术家的生活。
She will glamorize her new product through a series of clever advertisements. = 她将通过一系列巧妙的广告来美化她的新产品。


虚拟语气:
If they glamorized their work more, they might attract more customers. = 如果他们更多地美化他们的工作，他们可能会吸引更多客户。
I wish they hadn't glamorized violence in that video game. = 我真希望他们在那个电子游戏中没有美化暴力。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

The author avoided glamorizing the villain's actions. = 作者避免了美化反派的行为。
