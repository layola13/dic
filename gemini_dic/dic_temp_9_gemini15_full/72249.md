florilege:/ˈflɔːrɪlɪdʒ/| n. |选集；文选；花集|A florilege is a collection of the best or most beautiful passages from different literary works or a collection of flowers.  It is often characterized by its selectivity and focus on quality or beauty. |例句：The anthology is a florilege of 19th-century poetry. 这部选集是19世纪诗歌的文选。

例句：She created a beautiful florilege of wildflowers from her garden. 她用花园里的野花制作了一束美丽的花束。

近义词：anthology, selection, bouquet, compilation

反义词：None (No direct antonym, but words implying randomness or poor quality could be considered opposites in context)

词性:名词

PHRASE->

词源:
From Late Latin *florilegium, from Latin flos (genitive floris) "flower" + legere "to gather, collect".  The original sense was "a gathering of flowers," which extended metaphorically to collections of literary passages.
