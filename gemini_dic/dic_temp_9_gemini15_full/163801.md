predemonstrative: /ˌpriːdɛmənˈstreɪtɪv/ | adj. | 预示的；先兆的 |  Describing something that serves as a precursor or indication of something else that is about to happen or be demonstrated. It suggests an early sign or hint of a future event or action, often implying a more significant event is to follow.  | The unusually warm weather in February was a predemonstrative sign of an early spring.  二月异常温暖的天气是早春的预兆。 |  indicative, foreshadowing, precursory |  unpredictable, unexpected |  形容词
