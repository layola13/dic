indign:/ɪnˈdɪɡn/| adj.|愤怒的；愤慨的；不公平的|Feeling or showing anger or annoyance at what is perceived as unfair treatment.|The indignity of being forced to apologize for something he didn't do made him furious.  他被迫为一件自己没做的事道歉，这让他感到非常愤怒。|近义词：angry, resentful, outraged, affronted|反义词：calm, pleased, content


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He feels indignantly about the unfair decision. 他对这个不公平的决定感到愤怒。
She is indignant at the injustice she witnessed. 她对她目睹的不公正感到愤慨。


一般过去时:
He was indignant at the way he was treated. 他对他受到的待遇感到愤怒。
She felt indignant after hearing the news. 听到这个消息后，她感到愤怒。


现在完成时:
He has been indignant ever since the incident. 自从那件事发生以来，他就一直很生气。
She has remained indignant about the lack of transparency. 她一直对缺乏透明度感到愤慨。


过去完成时:
He had been indignant for days before he finally spoke out. 他生气了好几天才最终说出来。
She had felt indignant about the situation long before it escalated. 在情况升级很久之前，她就对这种情况感到愤怒了。


将来时:

She will likely be indignant at the outcome. 她很可能会对结果感到愤怒。


虚拟语气:
