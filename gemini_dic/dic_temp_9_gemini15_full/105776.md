kirpan:/ˈkɪrpɑːn/| n. |短劍，彎刀|A kirpan is a curved, single-edged dagger traditionally worn by Sikhs as a symbol of faith and defense.  It is one of the five Ks (kesh, kangha, kara, kachera, kirpan) that represent Sikh identity and commitment to their faith. The size and style of the kirpan can vary, with some being small enough to be concealed and others larger and more prominently displayed.|Example:  Many Sikhs wear a kirpan as a symbol of their faith and commitment to their religious principles.  许多锡克教徒佩戴短剑作为其信仰和对宗教原则承诺的象征。|近义词：dagger, knife, sword|反义词：N/A
