favoured:/ˈfeɪvəd/ | adj. | 受宠爱的；偏爱的；有利的 |  Showing preference or partiality; enjoying special favor or advantage.  |例句：He was the favoured son. 他是受宠爱的儿子。

例句：The weather favoured our plans for a picnic. 天气有利于我们的野餐计划。


近义词：preferred, favored, preferred, chosen, selected | 反义词：disfavored, disliked, unpopular


词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The teacher favoured the student with good grades. = 老师偏爱成绩好的学生。
The wind favoured the sailors. = 风向对水手们有利。


一般过去时:
The judges favoured the younger contestant. = 评委们偏爱年轻的参赛者。
The company favoured a new marketing strategy. = 公司偏爱新的营销策略。


现在完成时:
The government has favoured certain industries with tax breaks. = 政府一直通过减税来偏袒某些行业。
He has always favoured his eldest daughter. = 他总是偏爱他的大女儿。


过去完成时:
She had favoured the red dress over the blue one. = 她之前更喜欢那件红裙子而不是蓝裙子。
The court had favoured the defendant in previous rulings. = 法院在之前的裁决中偏袒被告。


将来时:
The forecast favours clear skies tomorrow. = 预报显示明天天气晴朗。
They will likely favour the proposal that saves the most money. = 他们很可能会支持最省钱的提案。


虚拟语气:
If the wind favoured us, we would have arrived earlier. = 如果风向有利于我们，我们本可以早点到达。
I wish the judges had favoured my performance. = 我希望评委们喜欢我的表演。


被动语态:
The proposal was favoured by the majority of the board members. = 该提案得到了大多数董事会成员的赞同。
His application was favoured over others. = 他的申请比其他申请更受青睐。


疑问句:
Does the current evidence favour one hypothesis over the other? = 目前的证据是否支持一种假设而不是另一种？
Which candidate are the voters favouring? = 选民们支持哪位候选人？


否定句:
The decision was not favoured by all the stakeholders. = 并非所有利益相关者都赞同这一决定。
The judge did not favour either side in the dispute. = 法官在争端中没有偏袒任何一方。



PHRASE->
be favoured with = 受到…的青睐
in favour of = 支持；赞成
favour someone with something = 给予某人…
find favour with = 受…的欢迎
out of favour = 失宠；不受欢迎

词源:
From Old French *favourer, from Latin *favor-, stem of favor "to be kind, to grant favor," from *favere "to be kind, to favor," of uncertain origin.  The modern spelling reflects the influence of the noun "favor".
