hole:/hoʊl/| n.|洞，孔，穴|A hole is an opening in a solid object.  It can be small or large, and it can be natural or man-made. |例句：There's a hole in my sock. 我的袜子上有个洞。

例句：The rabbit disappeared into its hole. 兔子钻进了它的洞穴。|近义词：opening, cavity, gap|反义词：solid, whole


USAGE->
一般现在时:
There is a hole in the wall. = 墙上有个洞。
The mouse lives in a hole. = 老鼠住在洞里。

一般过去时:
He dug a hole in the ground. = 他在地上挖了个洞。
She found a hole in her sweater. = 她发现她的毛衣上有个洞。

现在完成时:
They have filled the hole with cement. = 他们已经用水泥填补了那个洞。
I have noticed a hole in the roof. = 我注意到屋顶上有个洞。

过去完成时:
The water had leaked through the hole in the roof before we noticed it. =在我们注意到之前，水已经从屋顶上的洞漏进来了。
He had already dug the hole before I arrived. =在我到达之前，他已经挖好了洞。

将来时:
We will dig a hole for the new tree. = 我们将为新树挖个坑。
They will repair the hole in the road tomorrow. = 他们明天将修补路上的坑洞。

虚拟语气:
If there were a hole in the bucket, it would leak. = 如果桶上有个洞，它就会漏水。
I wish there weren't so many holes in my clothes. = 我真希望我的衣服上没有这么多洞。

被动语态:
The hole was filled with dirt. = 洞被土填满了。
The hole is being repaired. = 洞正在被修补。

疑问句:
Is there a hole in your pocket? =你的口袋破了吗？
Where is the hole in the fence? =栅栏上的洞在哪儿？

否定句:
There is no hole in the wall. = 墙上没有洞。
He didn't make a hole in the fence. = 他没有在栅栏上弄个洞。
