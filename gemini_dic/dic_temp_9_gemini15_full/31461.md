charlatans:/ˈʃɑːrlətənz/| n.|江湖骗子，骗子|A charlatan is a person who pretends to have special knowledge or skill, especially in medicine or science, in order to deceive people.  They often use impressive-sounding but meaningless language to impress their audience and gain their trust.|例句：He was exposed as a charlatan after his "miracle cure" was proven to be ineffective. 他被揭露为骗子，因为他所谓的“奇迹疗法”被证明无效。|近义词：fraud, imposter, quack, mountebank|反义词：expert, authority, genuine
