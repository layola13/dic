inwardnesses:/ˈɪnwədnəsɪz/ | n. | 内心世界；隐秘；本质 | The inwardnesses of human nature are complex and often contradictory. 人性的内心世界复杂且往往自相矛盾。 | 近义词：innermost thoughts, secrets, depths, essentials | 反义词：externals, superficialities


英文解析：The plural noun "inwardnesses" refers to the inner thoughts, feelings, and experiences of a person or thing that are not readily apparent or easily understood.  It suggests a deeper, more hidden level of reality than what is immediately visible. It often implies complexity, mystery, or even secrecy.

例句：

Exploring the inwardnesses of a character is crucial for creating a believable fictional persona.  探索一个人物的内心世界对于创造一个可信的小说人物至关重要。

The artist sought to capture the inwardnesses of the human spirit in her paintings.  这位艺术家试图在她的画作中捕捉人类精神的内心世界。


词源：Derived from the adjective "inward," meaning "situated inside or within," and the suffix "-ness," which forms nouns indicating a state, quality, or condition.  Ultimately, it traces back to Old English "inward."
