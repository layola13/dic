dwindler: /ˈdwɪndlə(r)/ | n. | 逐渐减少的人或事物；减少者 | A person or thing that is gradually decreasing in size, amount, or importance.  It can also refer to someone who is becoming less significant or influential. |例句：The dwindling number of participants forced the organizers to cancel the event.  参与者人数的减少迫使组织者取消了活动。


The dwindling supply of food led to widespread famine.  食物供应的减少导致了大范围的饥荒。

近义词：diminisher, reducer, decliner | 反义词：augmenter, increaser, grower | 词性: Noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The population of the town dwindles every year. = 这个小镇的人口每年都在减少。
His influence is dwindling rapidly. = 他的影响力正在迅速减弱。

一般过去时:
The company's profits dwindled last quarter. = 公司上季度的利润减少了。
His energy dwindled as the day wore on. = 随着一天的过去，他的精力也逐渐减少了。

现在完成时:
The number of students has dwindled since the new policy was implemented. = 自从新政策实施以来，学生人数减少了。
His savings have dwindled considerably over the years. = 多年来，他的积蓄已经大大减少了。

过去完成时:
By the end of the journey, their supplies had dwindled to almost nothing. = 到旅程结束时，他们的补给几乎已经消耗殆尽。
The army's strength had dwindled significantly before the battle. = 在战斗开始之前，军队的力量已经大大减弱了。

将来时:
The supplies will dwindle if we don't get more soon. = 如果我们很快得不到更多补给，现有的补给就会减少。
His patience will dwindle if you don't stop bothering him. = 如果你不停地烦他，他的耐心就会耗尽。

虚拟语气:
If the funding dwindled, the project would be in jeopardy. = 如果资金减少，项目就会处于危险之中。
I wish my enthusiasm didn't dwindle so quickly. = 我希望我的热情不会这么快就消退。

被动语态:
The resources were dwindled by overuse. = 资源因过度使用而减少了。
The team's morale was dwindled by a series of defeats. = 一系列的失败使团队士气低落。

疑问句:
Are your funds dwindling? = 你的资金是不是快用完了？
Is his support dwindling? = 他的支持是不是在减少？

否定句:
The resources did not dwindle despite the increased demand. = 尽管需求增加，资源并没有减少。
His confidence did not dwindle even after the setback. = 即使在挫折之后，他的信心也没有减弱。

词源:
The word "dwindle" comes from the Old English word "dwīnian," meaning "to grow thin or weak."  It reflects a gradual decrease or decline.
