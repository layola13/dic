moots:/muːts/| v.| 提出讨论；争论；提出异议 | To raise (a point) for discussion or debate; to discuss or debate (a point).  | The lawyer mooted the possibility of a plea bargain. 律师提出了认罪协商的可能性。 |  discuss, debate, raise, propose |  ignore, dismiss, accept


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He moots a new theory every week. 他每周都提出一个新理论。
They moot the idea during the meeting. 他们在会议上提出了这个想法。


一般过去时:
The committee mooted several solutions. 委员会提出了几种解决方案。
She mooted the idea of a fundraiser. 她提出了举办募捐活动的建议。


现在完成时:
They have mooted several options for the project. 他们已经为该项目提出了几个方案。
The council has mooted a new traffic plan. 理事会已经提出了一个新的交通方案。


过去完成时:
He had mooted the proposal before the meeting. 他在会议前已经提出了这个提案。
They had mooted the possibility of a strike. 他们曾讨论过罢工的可能性。


将来时:
We will moot the topic at the next meeting. 我们将在下次会议上讨论这个话题。
She will moot a new approach to the problem. 她将提出解决这个问题的新方法。


虚拟语气:
If they mooted the issue earlier, the problem might have been avoided. 如果他们早点提出这个问题，问题可能就避免了。
I wish they had mooted that suggestion sooner. 我希望他们早点提出那个建议。


被动语态:
The idea was mooted by the chairman. 这个想法是由主席提出的。
The proposal was mooted in the last meeting.  这个提案在上一次会议上被提出。


疑问句:
Was the proposal mooted at the meeting?  这个提案在会议上被提出过吗？
Will the new policy be mooted at the next meeting? 下次会议会讨论新政策吗?


否定句:
The judge did not moot the possibility of an appeal. 法官没有提出上诉的可能性。
They did not moot any objections. 他们没有提出任何异议。


PHRASE->

moot point:  一个无足轻重的问题 (a point that is irrelevant or already decided)
moot court: 模拟法庭 (a simulated court proceeding for law students)
moot question:  一个有争议的问题 (a question open to discussion or debate)
to moot a suggestion: 提出建议 (to suggest something for discussion)
moot court case: 模拟法庭案件 (a case used in a moot court)
