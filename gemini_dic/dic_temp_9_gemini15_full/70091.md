fever:/ˈfiːvər/ | n. | 发烧，发热 | A raised body temperature; a feeling of being hot and unwell, typically associated with an infection.  |  He had a high fever and felt weak. 他高烧不退，感觉很虚弱。  She woke up with a fever and chills. 她醒来时发烧并伴有寒战。 |  high temperature, pyrexia |  normal temperature,  health | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has a fever. = 他发烧。
She often gets a fever when she catches a cold. = 她感冒时经常发烧。

一般过去时:
He had a fever yesterday. = 他昨天发烧了。
The doctor said she had a high fever. = 医生说她高烧。

现在完成时:
She has had a fever for three days. = 她发烧三天了。
He has never had such a high fever before. = 他以前从未发过这么高的烧。

过去完成时:
He had had a fever for a week before he went to the doctor. = 他在去看医生之前已经发烧了一个星期了。
They had already had their fevers treated before the outbreak. = 在疫情爆发之前，他们已经接受了退烧治疗。

将来时:
He will probably have a fever tomorrow. = 他明天可能还会发烧。
If she doesn't rest, she will have a fever. = 如果她不休息，她就会发烧。

虚拟语气:

I wish I didn't have a fever. = 我希望我不发烧。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
