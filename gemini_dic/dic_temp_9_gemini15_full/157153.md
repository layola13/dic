pinedrops:/ˈpaɪnˌdrɒps/| n. |鹿蹄草属植物|Pinedrops are parasitic plants that lack chlorophyll and obtain nutrients from the roots of other plants. They are typically found in coniferous forests and have reddish-brown or purplish stems and bell-shaped flowers.|例句：The hiker spotted a cluster of pinedrops growing near the base of an old pine tree.  徒步旅行者在老松树底部附近发现了一簇鹿蹄草。|例句：Pinedrops are fascinating examples of myco-heterotrophic plants. 鹿蹄草是菌异养植物的迷人例子。|近义词：Indian pipes (similar appearance)|反义词:  (No direct antonym, as it describes a specific plant)|名词
