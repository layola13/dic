dsl:/dʒiːɛsˈɛl/| n. |领域特定语言|Domain-Specific Language.  A programming language or notation designed for a specific class of problems or tasks, rather than for general-purpose programming. DSLs are often more concise and easier to use for their intended purpose than general-purpose languages.  They may be embedded within a general-purpose language or be standalone languages.|例句：Many CAD programs use a DSL for describing the shapes and properties of objects. 许多CAD程序使用DSL来描述物体的形状和属性。|近义词：specialized language, niche language |反义词：general-purpose language
