quasi-disgraced: /ˈkwɑːzaɪdɪsˈɡreɪst/ | adj. | 几乎失宠的；名声受损的 |  Almost disgraced; having suffered a partial loss of reputation or favor. The term combines "quasi" (meaning seemingly, apparently, or to a certain extent) with "disgraced" (meaning having lost the respect and approval of others). It suggests a situation where someone's reputation has been tarnished but not completely destroyed.  | The politician, though not formally charged, was quasi-disgraced after the scandal broke.  这位政客虽然没有被正式指控，但在丑闻爆发后名声受损。 | disgraced, discredited, dishonored | honored, respected, esteemed | adj.
