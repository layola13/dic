obscurantism: /ɒbˈskjʊərənˌtɪzəm/ | n. | 蒙昧主义；故意晦涩；故弄玄虚 | The deliberate act of making something obscure, unclear, or difficult to understand, often to conceal or mislead.  It can refer to policies, ideas, or practices that deliberately prevent the spread of knowledge or understanding. | Example: The government's policy of obscurantism regarding its financial dealings led to widespread distrust.  政府对其财务往来的故意隐瞒政策导致了广泛的不信任。


Example: The professor's obscurantism, using overly complex jargon, made it difficult for students to grasp the core concepts. 教授故弄玄虚，使用了过于复杂的专业术语，使学生难以理解核心概念。


近义词：mystery, enigma, concealment, obfuscation, mystification


反义词：clarity, transparency, illumination, enlightenment, lucidity


词源:  From "obscure" (meaning dark, unclear) + "-ism" (a suffix denoting a doctrine, system, or practice). The word's roots trace back to Latin "obscurus" (dark, hidden).
