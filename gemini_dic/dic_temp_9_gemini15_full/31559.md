chary:/ˈtʃɛri/| adj.|谨慎的；迟疑的；小心的|Hesitant or cautious; wary.  The investor was chary of investing in the volatile market.|近义词：cautious, wary, hesitant, suspicious|反义词：reckless, careless, bold


例句：

He was chary of making promises he couldn't keep. 他谨慎地避免做出无法兑现的承诺。

She was chary about revealing her personal information online. 她对在网上透露个人信息很谨慎。


词源:

The word "chary" ultimately derives from the Old English word "cērig," meaning "careful" or "cautious."  Over time, the meaning shifted slightly to emphasize a hesitant or wary approach.


用法说明：

Chary is primarily used as an adjective to describe someone who is hesitant or cautious about something, often due to potential risks or uncertainties. It suggests a degree of reluctance or apprehension.  It's often used with a preposition like "of" or "about" to specify what the person is cautious about.  The word has a slightly formal tone and is less common in everyday speech than words like "cautious" or "wary."
