self-mockeries: /ˈsɛlf ˈmɑːkəriːz/ | n. | 自嘲；自我调侃 |  The act of mocking or making fun of oneself; self-deprecating humor.  It often involves acknowledging one's flaws or shortcomings in a humorous way. |

例句：

His self-mockeries were a way to deflect criticism and make himself more approachable.  他的自嘲是一种化解批评、使自己更容易接近的方式。

Her stand-up routine was filled with self-mockeries about her dating life.  她的脱口秀节目充满了关于她约会生活的自嘲。


近义词：self-deprecation, self-ridicule, irony, sarcasm (when directed at oneself)

反义词：self-aggrandizement, self-importance, boasting, bragging

词性：名词 (plural)
