expecto:/ɛkˈspɛktoʊ/| v.|期待，预料，期望|to look forward to something; to anticipate something; to expect something. |例句：I expecto a positive response to my proposal. 我期待我的提案得到积极回应。|例句：She expectoed her guests to arrive at 7 pm. 她预计客人晚上7点到达。|近义词：anticipate, await, expect|反义词：dread, fear


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I expect a call from him. = 我期待他的电话。
They expect to win the game. = 他们期望赢得比赛。

一般过去时:
I expected him to be here by now. = 我预计他现在应该到了。
We expected better results. = 我们期待更好的结果。

现在完成时:
I have been expecting your email. = 我一直在等你的邮件。
They have expected this outcome for weeks. = 他们几周来一直预料到这个结果。

过去完成时:
I had expected the meeting to be shorter. = 我原以为会议会短一些。
She had expected him to call, but he didn't. = 她原以为他会打电话，但他没有。

将来时:
I will expect to see you tomorrow. = 我明天期待见到你。
We will expect a large turnout at the event. = 我们预计活动会有很多人参加。

虚拟语气:
I would expect him to apologize. = 我希望他会道歉。
If I were you, I would expect more from him. = 如果我是你，我会对他有更高的期望。

被动语态:
The results were expected to be released today. = 预计结果今天公布。
The change is expected to be implemented soon. = 预计这项改变很快就会实施。

疑问句:
Do you expect me to believe that? = 你期望我相信吗？
What do you expect to achieve? = 你期望取得什么成就？

否定句:
I don't expect any problems. = 我预计不会有任何问题。
They didn't expect such a strong reaction. = 他们没有预料到如此强烈的反应。


PHRASE->
expectantly: 期待地
expect nothing less than: 至少期待
expect great things: 期待美好的事物
to be expected: 预料之中
expect the worst: 预料最坏的情况
