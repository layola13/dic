reimposing:/ˌriːɪmˈpoʊzɪŋ/| v.|重新实施，再次强加|The act of imposing something again; to impose something again.  It implies a previous imposition that was perhaps removed or ceased, and is now being reinstated.|例句：The government is reimposing restrictions on public gatherings. 政府正在重新实施对公众集会的限制。

例句：After a brief period of relaxation, they are reimposing the curfew.  短暂的放松后，他们又重新实施了宵禁。


近义词：reinstate, reintroduce, renew


反义词：remove, abolish, repeal


词性：动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reimpose the tax every year. = 他们每年都重新征税。
The school reimposes the uniform policy. = 学校重新实施了校服政策。

一般过去时:
The governor reimposed martial law. = 州长重新实施了戒严令。
They reimposed the travel restrictions last week. = 他们上周重新实施了旅行限制。

现在完成时:
The company has reimposed its dress code. = 公司已经重新实施了着装规范。
They have reimposed the fines for late submissions. = 他们已经重新实施了逾期提交的罚款。

过去完成时:
The court had reimposed the sentence after the appeal failed. = 上诉失败后，法院已重新判刑。
Before the incident, they had reimposed stricter security measures. = 事故发生前，他们已经重新实施了更严格的安全措施。

将来时:
The government will reimpose the lockdown if the situation worsens. = 如果情况恶化，政府将重新实施封锁。
The council will reimpose parking restrictions next month. = 理事会下个月将重新实施停车限制。

虚拟语气:
If the virus spread, they would reimpose travel bans. = 如果病毒蔓延，他们将重新实施旅行禁令。
I wish they wouldn't reimpose the tax. = 我希望他们不要重新征税。

被动语态:
The restrictions were reimposed due to rising infection rates. = 由于感染率上升，限制措施被重新实施。
New rules will be reimposed if necessary. =如有必要，将重新实施新的规定。

疑问句:
Will the government reimpose the mask mandate? = 政府会重新强制戴口罩吗？
Have they reimposed the quarantine measures? = 他们是否重新实施了隔离措施？

否定句:
The committee did not reimpose the sanctions. = 委员会没有重新实施制裁。
They have not reimposed the restrictions yet. = 他们还没有重新实施限制。


PHRASE->
reimpose sanctions = 重新实施制裁
reimpose restrictions = 重新实施限制
reimpose a ban = 重新实施禁令
reimpose control = 重新控制
reimpose order = 重新建立秩序
