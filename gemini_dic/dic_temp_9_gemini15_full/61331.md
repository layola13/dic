embattail:/ɛmˈbætəl/| vt.|列阵；整队|To arrange (troops) in battle order; to draw up in battle array.  The verb implies a specific tactical arrangement for fighting.|例句：The general embattled his troops before the anticipated attack. 将军在预料中的攻击前将他的军队列阵。  The soldiers were embattled along the ridge. 士兵们沿着山脊列阵。|近义词：array, deploy, marshal, muster|反义词：disperse, scatter|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The general embattles his troops before every battle. = 将军在每次战斗前都将他的部队列阵。
He embattles his arguments with meticulous detail. = 他用细致的细节来组织他的论点。


一般过去时:
The commander embattled his forces along the riverbank. = 司令官把他的部队沿河岸列阵。
They embattled their defenses against the approaching enemy. = 他们布置防御工事以对抗逼近的敌人。


现在完成时:
The army has embattled its forces for the upcoming offensive. = 军队已经为即将到来的进攻列阵。
She has embattled her ideas in a powerful and persuasive essay.  = 她在一篇有力且有说服力的文章中阐述了她的观点。


过去完成时:
The troops had embattled themselves before the enemy's surprise attack. = 在敌人的突然袭击之前，部队已经列阵。
He had carefully embattled his arguments before presenting them to the committee. = 在向委员会陈述他的论点之前，他已经仔细地组织好了它们。

虚拟语气:

否定句:
The general did not embattle his troops in time. = 将军没有及时将他的部队列阵。

PHRASE->
embattail one's forces = 列阵部队
embattail one's arguments = 有条理地组织论点
embattail oneself =  (较为罕见)做好战斗准备
embattail for battle = 为战斗做好准备
embattail against the enemy = 对抗敌人列阵


词源:
From Old French *embattailer, from bataille "battle".  The prefix "em-" is an intensifying prefix common in Old French.
