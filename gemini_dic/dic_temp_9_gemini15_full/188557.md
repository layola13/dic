ruinated:/ˈruːɪneɪtɪd/ | adj. | 毁坏的；破败的；衰败的 |  destroyed or severely damaged; in a state of disrepair; having lost all one's money or possessions | The earthquake ruined the city. 地震毁了这座城市。 The abandoned house was ruined.  那座废弃的房子破败不堪。 |近义词：destroyed, devastated, spoiled, wrecked | 反义词：restored, repaired, improved


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rain ruins the picnic. 雨毁了野餐。
This bad habit ruins his health. 这个坏习惯毁了他的健康。

一般过去时:
The storm ruined the crops. 暴风雨毁了庄稼。
The scandal ruined his reputation. 丑闻毁了他的名誉。

现在完成时:
The flood has ruined many homes. 洪水已经毁了许多房屋。
Years of neglect have ruined the building. 多年的忽视毁了这座建筑物。

过去完成时:
They had ruined their chances of winning. 他们已经毁掉了获胜的机会。
The company had been ruined by bad management. 公司因管理不善而破产。

将来时:
The new law will ruin small businesses. 新法将毁掉小型企业。
If we continue like this, we will ruin our relationship. 如果我们继续这样下去，我们会毁掉我们的关系。

虚拟语气:
If he had studied harder, he wouldn't have ruined his future. 如果他学习更努力，他就不会毁掉他的未来。
I wish I hadn't ruined the surprise. 我真希望我没有破坏惊喜。

被动语态:
The castle was ruined by fire. 城堡被大火烧毁了。
His career was ruined by a single mistake. 他的事业因一个错误而毁于一旦。

疑问句:
Was the project ruined by the budget cuts? 项目是否因预算削减而失败？
Has the storm ruined the harvest? 暴风雨是否毁掉了收成？

否定句:
The rain didn't ruin the party. 雨并没有毁掉聚会。
The accident didn't ruin his life. 事故并没有毁掉他的生活。
