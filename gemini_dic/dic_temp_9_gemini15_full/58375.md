dualists:/ˈduːəlɪsts/| n. |二元论者；二元论的支持者|A dualist is someone who believes in dualism, a philosophical theory that posits two fundamental principles or entities, such as mind and matter, or good and evil.  Dualists often believe these principles are distinct and irreducible to each other.|Example: Many religious thinkers are dualists, believing in the separation of body and soul.  许多宗教思想家都是二元论者，相信身心分离。|近义词：dichotomists,  binary thinkers|反义词：monists,  reductionists|名词
