/ˈsuːdədɒks/| n. |似是而非的学说；伪说；谬说 |A pseudodox is a belief or opinion that is false or unfounded, often presented as true or correct. It is a statement or doctrine that is superficially plausible but ultimately false.  Pseudodoxes can be based on misinformation, logical fallacies, or wishful thinking.  They often spread through repetition and lack of critical examination. |Example: The idea that the Earth is flat is a pseudodox. 地球是平的这种说法是一种谬说。|近义词：fallacy, delusion, misconception, error |反义词：truth, fact, verity
