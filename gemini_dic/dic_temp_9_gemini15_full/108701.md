lean: /liːn/ | v., adj., n. | 倾斜；依赖；瘦的；精简的 |  to incline or bend from a vertical position; to rely on for support or help; thin; not fat; free from unnecessary things | 近义词：incline, rely, slender, slim, spare | 反义词：plump, stout, obese, bulky


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He leans against the wall. = 他靠在墙上。
She leans heavily on her family for support. = 她非常依赖她的家人支持。


一般过去时:
The tower leaned precariously. = 塔楼摇摇欲坠地倾斜着。
He leaned forward to hear better. = 他向前倾听以听得更清楚。


现在完成时:
The building has leaned significantly over the years. = 这栋建筑多年来已经严重倾斜了。
She has leaned on her faith during difficult times. = 在困难时期，她一直依靠她的信仰。


过去完成时:
The tree had leaned so far it almost touched the ground. = 那棵树已经倾斜到几乎碰到地面的程度了。
They had leaned on each other for years. = 他们互相依靠了很多年。


将来时:
The old house will lean further with the next storm. = 老房子在下一场暴风雨中会倾斜得更厉害。
He will lean on his experience to succeed. = 他将依靠他的经验来取得成功。


虚拟语气:
If the tower leaned any further, it would collapse. = 如果塔倾斜得更厉害，它就会倒塌。
I wish I hadn't leaned so heavily on his advice. = 我真希望我没有那么依赖他的建议。


被动语态:
The picture was leaned against the fireplace. = 图片被靠在壁炉旁。
The decision was heavily leaned on the expert's opinion. = 这个决定很大程度上依赖于专家的意见。


疑问句:
Is the table leaning? = 桌子倾斜了吗？
Does he lean towards optimism or pessimism? = 他倾向于乐观还是悲观？


否定句:
The fence doesn't lean. = 栅栏没有倾斜。
He doesn't lean on anyone for support. = 他不依赖任何人支持。


PHRASE->

lean meat: 瘦肉

lean-to: 临时棚屋


WORD ORIGIN->

The word "lean" comes from the Old English word *hlēan*, meaning "to incline" or "to rest."  This is related to other Germanic words with similar meanings. The modern English word retains many of the original meanings and has expanded to encompass notions of thinness and efficiency.
