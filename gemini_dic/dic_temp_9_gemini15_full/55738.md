divel:/daɪv/ |v.| 潜水；猛冲；突然下降； |to plunge or descend rapidly, especially into water; to immerse oneself in something.|例句：He dived into the pool. 他跳入游泳池。 He dived into his work. 他埋头工作。|近义词：plunge, immerse, submerge|反义词：ascend, surface


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dives into the ocean every summer. = 他每年夏天都潜入大海。
She dives into her books every evening. = 她每天晚上都埋头苦读。


一般过去时:
They dived into the deep water. = 他们潜入深水。
He dived headfirst into the project. = 他一头扎进了这个项目。


现在完成时:
I have dived in the sea many times. = 我多次在海里潜水。
She has dived deep into the research. = 她深入研究了这项课题。


过去完成时:
He had dived before, so he wasn't scared. = 他以前潜过水，所以他不害怕。
They had dived into the problem before finding a solution. = 他们在找到解决方案之前已经深入研究过这个问题了。


将来时:
We will dive into the coral reef tomorrow. = 我们明天将潜入珊瑚礁。
They will dive into the investigation next week. = 他们下周将深入调查。


虚拟语气:
If I could dive, I would visit the Great Barrier Reef. = 如果我会潜水，我会去参观大堡礁。
I wish I could dive as deep as a professional diver. = 我希望我能像专业潜水员一样潜得深。


被动语态:
The treasure was dived for by the experienced diver. = 经验丰富的潜水员寻找到了宝藏。（被动，强调宝藏被找到）
The problem will be dived into by the committee. = 委员会将深入研究这个问题。（被动，强调问题将被研究）


疑问句:
Will you dive with us? = 你会和我们一起潜水吗？
Have you ever dived before? = 你以前潜过水吗？


否定句:
I don't dive because I am afraid of water. = 我不潜水因为我怕水。
He didn't dive into the discussion. = 他没有深入讨论。
