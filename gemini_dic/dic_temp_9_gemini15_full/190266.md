saprophagan:/ˈsæprəfeɪɡən/| n. |腐生生物|An organism that feeds on decaying organic matter.  Saprophages play a crucial role in nutrient cycling within ecosystems. They break down dead plants and animals, releasing nutrients back into the environment.  Examples include fungi, bacteria, and some invertebrates. |Example: Fungi are important saprophagans in forest ecosystems, breaking down dead wood and leaves.  真菌是森林生态系统中重要的腐生生物，分解枯木和落叶。|近义词：decomposer, detritivore |反义词：producer, autotroph
