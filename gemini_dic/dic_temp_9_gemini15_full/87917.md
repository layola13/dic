hierographic:/ˌhaɪəroʊˈɡræfɪk/ | adj. | 象形文字的；图画文字的 | Relating to or denoting a system of writing in which pictorial symbols represent words or ideas.  Hieroglyphic writing uses pictures to represent words or concepts.  The ancient Egyptians used a hieroglyphic system. | 近义词：pictographic, ideographic | 反义词：alphabetic, phonetic


例句：

The Rosetta Stone helped decipher ancient Egyptian hieroglyphic inscriptions. = 罗塞塔石碑帮助破译了古埃及的象形文字铭文。

Many ancient civilizations used hierographic systems before developing alphabets. = 许多古代文明在发展字母表之前都使用过象形文字系统。


词源:

From  hieroglyphic + -ic.  "Hieroglyphic" itself comes from Greek *hieroglyphikós*, from *hierós* "sacred" + *glýphein* "to carve".  The original meaning referred to the sacred carvings of ancient Egypt, later extended to similar writing systems.
