conversionism:/kənˈvɜːʒənɪzəm/ | n. |皈依主义；改宗主义 |The belief that religious conversion is essential for salvation.  It emphasizes a sudden and dramatic change in religious belief and practice.  This can manifest in various ways, from individual experiences of spiritual transformation to large-scale religious movements focused on converting others. |Example:  The Great Awakening was a period marked by widespread conversionism in the American colonies.  美国殖民地的大觉醒时期，以广泛的皈依主义为特征。|近义词：conversion, proselytism |反义词：agnosticism, secularism
