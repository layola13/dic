hydroxylamine:/haɪˈdrɒksɪləmiːn/ | n. | 羟胺 | Hydroxylamine is a chemical compound with the formula NH2OH.  It is a weak base and a reducing agent. It exists as colorless crystals that are hygroscopic and readily soluble in water and alcohol. Hydroxylamine is used in various industrial applications, including the production of pharmaceuticals, pesticides, and photographic developers. It's also used in the synthesis of other organic compounds.  | Example:  Hydroxylamine is a crucial intermediate in the synthesis of many important drugs. 羟胺是许多重要药物合成中的关键中间体。

词源:
The name "hydroxylamine" is a combination of "hydroxy" (referring to the hydroxyl group, -OH) and "amine" (referring to the amine group, -NH2).  It reflects the chemical structure of the compound, which contains both a hydroxyl group and an amine group.
