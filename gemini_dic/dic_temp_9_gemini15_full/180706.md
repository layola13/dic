regretted:/rɪˈɡretɪd/| v.|后悔，遗憾|To feel sorry about something that has happened or something that one has done.|I regretted missing the concert. 我后悔错过了这场音乐会。|I regretted telling him the truth. 我后悔告诉了他真相。|近义词：rue, repent, bemoan|反义词：rejoice, celebrate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He regrets his decision. = 他后悔他的决定。
She regrets her hasty words. = 她后悔说了那些话。


一般过去时:
I regretted my actions immediately. = 我立刻后悔了我的行为。
They regretted not attending the party. = 他们后悔没有参加聚会。


现在完成时:
He has regretted his choice ever since. = 从那以后他就一直后悔他的选择。
She has regretted not studying harder for the exam. = 她一直后悔没有为考试更努力学习。


过去完成时:
I had regretted missing the deadline before I even received the email. = 我甚至在收到邮件之前就已经后悔错过了截止日期。
They had regretted their impulsive purchase long before they got home. = 他们早就后悔冲动购物了，还没到家就已经后悔了。


将来时:
I will regret this someday. = 我总有一天会后悔的。
They will regret their decision to quit their jobs. = 他们会后悔他们辞职的决定。


虚拟语气:
I wish I hadn't said that; I regret saying it. = 我真希望我没说过那些话；我后悔说了那些话。
If I had studied harder, I wouldn't regret it now. = 如果我当时学习更努力，现在就不会后悔了。


被动语态:
His actions were deeply regretted by everyone. = 他的行为受到了所有人的深深遗憾。
The missed opportunity was greatly regretted. = 错失的机会令人非常惋惜。


疑问句:
Do you regret your choice? = 你后悔你的选择吗？
Does she regret what she did? = 她后悔她所做的事情吗？


否定句:
I don't regret my decision at all. = 我一点也不后悔我的决定。
He didn't regret helping her, even though it was difficult. = 他不后悔帮助她，即使那很难。


PHRASE->
regret to inform you = 遗憾地通知你
deeply regret = 深深遗憾
regrettably = 遗憾的是
without regret = 毫不后悔
have no regrets = 毫无遗憾
