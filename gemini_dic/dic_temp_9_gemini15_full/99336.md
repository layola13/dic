interdeal:/ˌɪntərˈdiːl/| n.|中间交易；中间协议|An interdeal is a transaction or agreement that takes place between two parties, often involving a third party or intermediary.  It typically refers to a deal that is not directly between the primary buyers and sellers, but facilitated through a middleman or another entity.|Example: The interdeal between the manufacturer and the retailer involved a significant markup.|近义词：mediation, intermediary agreement|反义词：direct deal
