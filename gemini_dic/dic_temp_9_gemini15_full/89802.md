horn:/hɔːrn/| n. v.|角；喇叭；号角；（汽车等的）喇叭；[乐]号角；(动词)吹号；发出喇叭声|a hard, pointed, usually curved structure growing from the head of an animal, such as a cow or a goat; a device that makes a loud noise, used on vehicles to warn other people; a musical instrument made of horn|The bull charged, its horns lowered. 公牛冲了过来，低下头。The car horn blared loudly. 汽车喇叭响亮地鸣叫。|近义词：trumpet, bugle, toot|反义词:none


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He blows the horn every morning. 他每天早上都吹喇叭。
The car horn honks continuously. 汽车喇叭一直在响。


一般过去时:
She blew the horn to warn the driver. 她吹响喇叭警告司机。
The train horn sounded in the distance. 火车鸣笛声从远处传来。


现在完成时:
They have blown the horn several times. 他们已经吹响了喇叭好几次。
The car has been honking for ten minutes. 这辆车已经鸣笛十分钟了。


过去完成时:
He had blown the horn before the accident. 事故发生前他已经吹响了喇叭。
The driver had already honked before the collision. 碰撞之前司机已经按响了喇叭。


将来时:
I will blow the horn when I arrive. 我到达时会吹响喇叭。
The ship will sound its horn as it enters the harbor. 船进入港口时会鸣笛。


虚拟语气:
If I had a horn, I would warn them. 如果我有喇叭，我会警告他们。
I wish I hadn't blown the horn so loudly. 我真希望我没有这么大声地吹喇叭。


被动语态:
The horn was blown loudly to signal the start. 喇叭被大声吹响以示开始。
