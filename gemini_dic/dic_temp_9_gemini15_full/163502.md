preconviction: /ˌpriːkənˈvɪkʃən/ | n. | 前科；先前定罪 | A preconviction refers to a criminal conviction that occurred before a more recent one.  It is often relevant in sentencing for subsequent offenses, as it can indicate a pattern of criminal behavior.  | Example: His preconviction for assault significantly impacted his sentencing for the robbery.  他的攻击罪前科严重影响了他抢劫案的判决。 | 近义词: prior conviction, previous conviction | 反义词: postconviction |  词性: noun
