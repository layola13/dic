janizary:/ˈdʒænɪzəri/| n. | 耶尼切里| A member of an elite corps of infantry soldiers in the Ottoman army, known for their military skill and loyalty.  They were originally composed of Christian slave boys, converted to Islam.  Their power and influence waned over time, and they were eventually abolished. |例句：The Janissaries were renowned for their discipline and effectiveness in battle. 耶尼切里以其纪律严明和战斗力强而闻名。The sultan feared the Janissaries' growing power and influence. 苏丹害怕耶尼切里日益增长的权力和影响力。|近义词：soldier, mercenary, guardsman |反义词：civilian
