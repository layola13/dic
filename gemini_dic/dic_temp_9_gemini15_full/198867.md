sidestep:/ˈsaɪdstep/| vt. & vi.| 避开；回避；侧身避开 | To move to the side in order to avoid something.  To avoid (a problem or question) | 例句：He sidestepped the question about his past. 他回避了关于他过去的问题。  She sidestepped the puddle. 她侧身避开了水坑。 | 近义词：avoid, evade, circumvent | 反义词：confront, face, address | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sidesteps all difficult questions. = 他回避所有难题。
They sidestep the issue of funding. = 他们回避了资金问题。


一般过去时:
She sidestepped the reporters. = 她躲避了记者。
He skillfully sidestepped the obstacle. = 他巧妙地避开了障碍物。


现在完成时:
The company has sidestepped many controversies. = 这家公司已经避开了许多争议。
He has sidestepped responsibility for years. = 他多年来一直逃避责任。


过去完成时:
They had sidestepped the problem before it became serious. = 他们在问题变得严重之前就避开了它。
She had sidestepped the issue during the debate. = 她在辩论中回避了这个问题。


将来时:
He will sidestep the issue in the next debate. = 他将在下一次辩论中回避这个问题。
She will sidestep the puddle by jumping over it. = 她将跳过水坑来避开它。


虚拟语气:
If he had sidestepped the issue, the consequences would have been less severe. = 如果他当时回避了这个问题，后果就不会那么严重。
I wish I had sidestepped that argument. = 我真希望我当时避开了那场争论。


被动语态:
The question was sidestepped by the politician. = 这个问题被政客回避了。
The responsibility cannot be sidestepped. = 责任无法推卸。


疑问句:
Did he sidestep the issue intentionally? = 他是故意回避这个问题的吗？
How did she sidestep the falling object? = 她是怎么躲避掉落物体的？


否定句:
He didn't sidestep the challenge. = 他没有回避挑战。
She didn't sidestep the difficult conversation. = 她没有回避那场艰难的谈话。
