quasi-sufficiently:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ səˈfɪʃəntli/ | adv. | 准充分地，近乎足够地 |  Sufficiently but not completely or perfectly; almost enough.  This adverb combines "quasi" (meaning seemingly, apparently, or to a certain extent) with "sufficiently" (meaning to a sufficient degree).  It indicates a condition that meets the minimum requirements but falls short of being fully satisfactory or complete. | Example: The evidence was quasi-sufficiently to convict him, but the judge wanted more concrete proof.  证据几乎足以定他的罪，但法官想要更确凿的证据。| 近义词: almost sufficiently, nearly sufficiently, practically sufficiently | 反义词: insufficiently, inadequately, completely insufficiently |  词性: 副词
